Rick Hubbard’s Summary

Because:

•ProServ is hard—Systematic thinking makes it easier;
•PS is essential for customer satisfaction—moreso for success—purposeful innovation can vault ProServ from aftersale afterthought to presales strategic significance; and
•PS is a balancing act—needing constant refinement among PS Strategy, Marketing, Sales, Engineering, Execution and PS Operations…

…is why for two decades I’ve sought to understand, develop and enhance the Professional Services Solutions Framework (PSSF). The PSSF is a unity of structure, methods and lightweight tools which form attractive, profitable Services offerings.

This work has led to contributions to the craft which solve critical problems such as:

•Seizing control during the Critical First 5%. Displacing chaos with clarity with the “OP3” (One-Page Project Plan). The OP3 has stopped scope creep cold;
•Avoiding technical solutions which result in failed implementations due to overlooked non-technical factors with the Whole Solution Framework (WSF) approach;
•Overcoming forces which thwart technology firms moving from vendor to solution provider;
•Rapidly obtaining requirements with the Accelerated Requirements Method (ARM). In use worldwide, in 1,000s of cases, ARM yields more & better requirements in less than a fifth the time...

…as well as: gaining active client engagement participation via the Whole Solutions Roadmap (WSR); relaxing tension between Sales and PS by creating streams of incremental revenues; undertaking higher-margin, fixed-price engagements using Accelerated Project Estimating (APE); discovery opportunities for Sales with the “PS Lead Detective” program; helping customer’s clarify purpose and rationale in a fifth of the time with BRIEFCASE—The IT/Business Case method; ensure client’s receive a positive “experiential outcome” in addition to a satisfactory technical outcome via “Consulting Charm School”; and simplifying the myriad PS processes with the “Six Functions of Professional Services."
