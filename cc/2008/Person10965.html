Jeff Cobb’s Summary

Jeff has spent more than a decade on the leading edge of Web technology and online learning. He was a co-founder of Isoph, a leading provider of online learning technologies and content to major national associations and charitable organizations. His clients have included the Society for Human Resource Management, the American Red Cross, the American Nurses Credentialing Center, the Alzheimer’s Association, and many other well-known organizations.

Before launching Isoph, he served as senior vice president of business development for Quisic, a developer of high-quality online management education. At Quisic, Jeff oversaw all business development for the company, as well as relationships with major partners, including the National Center on Education and the Economy, PBS, IBM, Pearson, the London Business School, Insead, the Tuck School at Dartmouth, the Marshall School of Business (USC), and the Kenan-Flagler Business School (UNC-Chapel Hill).

Earlier in his career, Jeff taught at UNC-Chapel Hill, where he received an undergraduate teaching award, and was a research analyst in the corporate governance division of the Investor Responsibility Research Center, a prominent think tank. He founded and has served as executive director and chairman of BoysCamp, a nonprofit corporation serving at-risk youths.

Jeff earned his bachelor’s degree at the University of Virginia and his master’s degree at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill. He currently serves on the Professional Development Council Section of the American Society of Association Executives as well as on the advisory board for Philantech, provider of the PhilanTrack™ online grant proposal, reporting and management system. He has previously served on the research committee of the eLearning Guild as well as on the editorial board of Innovate, a leading resource for information about technology and education.
