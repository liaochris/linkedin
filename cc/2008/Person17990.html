Miki Saxon’s Summary

As CEO of RampUp Solutions, I provide founders and startup executives with affordable, out-of-the-box ways to improve their bottom line through employee motivation, communication, incentives and culture.

I'm a dedicated simplifier – verbally direct, but with a relaxed, humorous style that saves time and money while reducing client stress. During more than 25 years of successful recruiting I developed an in-depth understanding of the bottom line impact of employees who are turned on—and off, and I now have 10 years of experience turning that knowledge into solutions for startups and their leaders.

In 1993, I wrote The Swamp and the Alligators: a (slightly irreverent) guide to career planning and the search process and was named Author-in-Residence at San Francisco State University's Career Center.

I was born/raised in Denver (CO), spent 25 years in San Francisco (CA) and currently live with 2 cats in Washougal (WA) across the river from Portland (OR). I'm very much a cat person; love reading and conversation; and mess around the garden. My license plates are “Miki MOU” (Master Of the Universe)—I have the Miki down pat and am working on the MOU.

Currently, I'm especially interested in meeting startup executives looking to fairly and equitably allocate and manage their stock option plan as beta testers for our latest product, OptionSanity™.
