Jason Leonard’s Experience

  • Director of Staffing

    Citigroup

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; C; Financial Services industry)

    June 2005 — September 2008 (3 years 4 months)

    Citigroup, founded in 1812, is the world’s largest Financial Services company, with over 360,000 employees and some 220+ million customer accounts in more than 140 countries.

    • Responsible for leading the Recruiting function for Citigroup's Consumer Lending businesses (mortgage, auto financing, educational lending & home equity) consisting of 16,000+ active employees and hiring 3,500+ new employees per year.
    • Integrated the Staffing function for five separate Citigroup business units into one Recruiting team consisting of 4 managers and 49 staffing professionals.
    • Redesigned and introduced consistent metrics and reporting across supported businesses.
    • Moved business away from a legacy job board posting methodology by introducing and cultivating a variety of non-traditional sourcing tactics.
    • Reduced average Time-to-Fill from 58 days in 2005 to 42 days in 2006 and 36 days in 2007.
    • 2007 Citi Presidents Club award recipient.
    • 2006 Citi Quality Excellence award recipient.

  • Director of Talent Acquisition

    Mosaic

    (Retail industry)

    July 2001 — June 2005 (4 years)

    Mosaic is a $200M revenue, privately owned, outsourced sales, merchandising and data collection solutions provider at the retail level. They represent world class brands such as Wal-Mart, Procter & Gamble, American Express, Microsoft, Disney, Reebok, Best Buy, AT&T, Bank of America, Nike, Epson, Revlon and Labatt Breweries.

    • Responsible for leading the Recruiting function for a 13,000+ employee company, while hiring 9,000+ new employees per year.
    • Managed 11 direct reports and a fluctuating base of an additional 28 to 67 immediate indirect reports.
    • Successfully led the restructuring of the Talent Acquisition function from a decentralized, generalist model to a more cost effective and efficient centralized, specialist model. This shift combined with other strategic changes directly drove the cost-per-hire across the organization from $650/hire to $390/hire in two years.
    • Led the vendor analysis, selection, integration and implementation of a new ATS (Taleo).
