Alicia Parrish’s Summary

Alicia Parrish founded P Inc Events in October 2006. The company specializes in producing, planning and promoting. She recently completed a position for the inaugural SPI Music Fest as the Festival Business Partner and Volunteer Coordinator.

She was also the marketing manager for the TBS "My Boys" TV premier. She co-produces the Entertainment and Production Pavilion at G2E, the world's largest casino gaming conference, attracting more than 26,000 attendees, and continues to produce EventPro Forum, THE conference for event professionals.

For nine years, she was the vice president of marketing and special events for CFB Productions, Inc., which produces two festivals and manages variety and corporate entertainers and television personalities.

Alicia worked as an editor and conference coordinator for PERFORMANCE magazine, production coordinator for the Nashville Summer Lights Music Festival, Lollapalooza in Miami and with Philip Morris/JoMo Productions — touring nationally with the Dixie Chicks and producing concerts for the Marlboro Military Tour in Colorado and the Marlboro 500 in California. Her writing has been published in several magazines, including "Celebrated Living," American Airlines’ first-class in-flight publication.

A passionate golfer, she founded the Armadillo Golf Players, the premier co-ed golf league in Austin, Texas, where she currently resides.

Alicia chaired the fifth annual Umlauf Garden Party to benefit the Umlauf Sculpture Garden & Museum with honorary chair Ian Moore. She was recognized as a leader in the Arts & Entertainment division for AustinUnder40, which honors excellence in professional achievement and community service.

A Delta Zeta alumna, Alicia graduated with a Bachelor of Arts degree in Journalism from Texas A & M University. She also attended the Université de Americaine in Montpelier, France.
