Mike Calma’s Summary

Mike Calma’s passion is on providing leadership and managerial expertise in establishing and growing new technology-based businesses.

Mike recently joined NEC Philippines as Business Support Development Manager. His mission is to enter new markets and innovate on new revenue generators.

He has 6 action-packed years of business experience and has been instrumental in several tech start-ups. Prior to joining NEC, he has worked as Business Development Manager for GeoSpatial Solutions, Inc. (pioneered Offshore Outsourcing Practice), General Manager for LinkSphere IT Solutions (Self-financed/first failed IT start-up) and Business Development Manager for General Instrument Corporation (Family-financed Engineering Consulting start-up).

He has an MBA from the University of the Philippines, Diliman (2008) as well as a Bachelor’s Degree in Computer Science from the University of the Philippines, Los Baños (2003). He is a member of the Upsilon Sigma Phi (www.upsilon.com ) and the UPLB Computer Science Society (www.uplbcoss.org ).

He blogs (www.mikecalma.com) to share some of his lucid (sometimes myopic) moments. He is starting work on his first book with the working title of: "Entrepreneurial Agitation: Starting Trouble to Create Value".

  • Owner and General Manager

    LinkSphere IT Solutions/BridgeFort Technologies, Inc.

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Information Technology and Services industry)

    May 2004 — December 2006 (2 years 8 months)

    LinkSphere came about when I was starting to earn well from freelance IT work. My first break came when I closed a US project worth $55k. This was a serious amount for a then 23 year old fresh grad and his team of geeks. We used the earnings to beef-up our education. However, our inexperience caught up with us. The company folded up late 2006. While it was a very disappointing time for me, I believe that this is one of my best learning experiences.
    Key Results:
    • Secured Key Offshore Accounts on the first 4 months of operations.
    • Headed a crisis management team to resolve client emergencies
    • Bootstraped Standardization and Documentation of Business Methods and Processes for specialized service lines (Systems Migration and Network Security) based on Best Practices to enhance competitiveness
    • Dissolved the corporation in good terms with each partner better educated and each getting a fair amount of cash. I buried my dead company well! :)

    General Instrument Corp.

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Oil & Energy industry)

    October 2002 — May 2004 (1 year 8 months)

    This is a venture I stared with my Dad when I was an undergrad. My Dad at that time quit his stable job to scratch his entrepreneurial itch. GIC is primarily an engineering consultancy firm. It has distinguished itself by solving the toughest problems in rotating machinery and back-up power. GIC primarily caters to power plants. My Dad is the principal consultant and I was business manager, salesman and all around assistant. When the business stabilized and was already profitable, I realized that I too needed to scratch my entrepreneurial itch with my own company.
    Key Results:

    • Managed Project teams for Power Industry accounts
    • Developed sales and marketing materials on battery monitoring technologies for mission critical applications.
    • Participated in on-site battery testing activities and authored reports and recommendations
    • Performed market research and prospective client correspondence
