Krzysztof Urbanowicz (urba@mignon-media.com)’s Summary

Krzysztof Urbanowicz is a principal and director of 5W Mignon Media Eastern Europe. He is also CEO and Founder of Mediapolis, an information design and media consulting company, based in Warsaw, Poland. He established Mediapolis in 1998 and Jeff Mignon of 5W Mignon Media has helped to develop the company.

His expertise is in newsroom organization, editorial strategy, media training and business strategy. He has extensive experience with newspapers, magazines, book publishing and corporate communications.

The client list includes more than 160 print publications and over 350 corporations. He has worked on the relaunch or the rethinking of about 20 Polish newspapers.

Mr. Urbanowicz was also the deputy editor-in-chief for Super Express, a Polish daily newspaper.

He started his career as a journalist in France in Le Quotidien de Paris and Le Figaro. He wrote for daily publications, such as La Croix, Info-Matin, Le Parisien and also for magazines, such as L'Express or Le Moniteur du Commerce International.

In addition, he was the director of a French-Polish journalism school based in Warsaw, Poland. He has also been an advisory member of the French-Polish Foundation for Journalism in charge of media training and consulting.

He created the Press Academy, Local Press Academy alongside the Polish Editors Association and the Academy of Corporate Media.

He wrote the book L'EMPIRE NUCLÉAIRE ÉCLATÉ (Editions Michalon, Paris 1994), a description of the nuclear question in the collapse of the Soviet Union.

He also co-authored the book KIOSK, GUIDE DE L'ACTUALITÉ MONDIALE DE L'ANNÉE 1994 (Editions- de l'Atelier/Editions Ouvrieres, Paris 1993.

He is Polish and French citizen.
