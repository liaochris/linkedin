Craig Strachan’s Summary

I wrote my first computer program at the age of 11 on my father’s Apple 2 computer. This led me into an IT career, during which time I realized that poor communication within organizations was costing business. While people were working towards common objectives, the lack of effective communication was killing business. This was when I understood a simple fact: The key to successful business is good communication.

I joined Toastmasters International in 1999 with the goal of learning to give better business presentations. I soon found that I was really enjoying public speaking. Speaking as often as I could, I was soon elected into Toastmasters leadership roles. I have since spoken and trained all over Southern Africa, as well as leading over 500 members to Presidents Distinguished status – the highest recognition. I currently runs the marketing portfolio for Toastmasters Southern Africa, representing nine countries.

I combine my knowledge of communication and experience in organizations to facilitate effective communication. I do this through training seminars, keynote speeches, and individual coaching and mentoring. With the right assistance, everybody can be an effective communicator.

I received the Toastmaster of the Year award from his Toastmasters club, and I have been recognized in Southern Africa for Division Governor of the Year. I have also received service excellence awards in corporations.

I am an expert author at Ezinearticles.com, who have published several of my articles. My free ebook “Putting the Power back into PowerPoint” can be downloaded off my website.

I play several musical instruments, and I have been played on South African and International radio.

Having a blind wife has led me to be aware of our differences, strengths and weaknesses. My wife, Lois, taught me that disability is not in the body – disability is in the way we think. I cycles 109km every year to raise funds for mentally and physically disabled people.
