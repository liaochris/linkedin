Karen Commins’s Summary

I'm a voice-over actor who collaborates with my clients so that your communication can be delivered -- and remembered -- in this frenzied world.

As a voice-over artist, I produce audio recordings of:

* commercials
* narrations for corporate, Internet and televised videos
* podcasts
* e-learning modules
* audiobooks

Past projects have included commercials, narrations, audiobooks, games and phone systems. Credits and clients include Pepsi Cola, ADP, Popeyes Chicken and Biscuits, Delta Airlines, Electronic Arts, BlueCielo ECM, Verizon Wireless, Georgia Department of Transportation, Turner South and NorthStar Audio Books.

My custom-built, soundproof studio is outfitted with industry-standard equipment found in top recording facilities:

Neumann TLM 103 microphone
Focusrite Platinum Voicemaster pre-amp/proc.
Macbook Pro
Pro Tools LE
Telos Zephyr ISDN Transceiver
Panasonic 3700 DAT recorder

You can see pictures of my stunning studio at this page on my web site:

http://www.AVOICEAboveTheCrowd.com/tour.shtml

I can quickly record your script and provide it to you in your choice of file formats, either on-line or on CD or DAT. Many clients receive the final product within hours.

I am available on short notice to record for you in any location throughout the metro Atlanta, GA area. I am a non-union voice talent and can be booked either freelance or through one of my agents. Depending on the length and nature of the project, I also am willing and able to travel to other cities.

Karen Commins’s Specialties:

You will discover that I'm:

** an energetic voice talent with proven ability to perform to direction

** an outstanding writer and editor who assists with script revisions

** a self-directed producer with professional home studio capable of recording and delivering clear, edited digital audio on CD, DAT or on-line in file formats including .MP3, .WAV, and .RA

  • Information Technology (IT) Specialist

    Internal Revenue Service (IRS)

    (Government Agency; 10,001 or more employees; Government Administration industry)

    1913 — 1913 (less than a year)

    When I started a part-time job at everyone's favorite government agency during high school, I never dreamed I would work there for 30 years! My dream was always to become a voice-over artist, so it was even more surprising that I worked in information systems (IS) positions. I worked as a programmer, programmer analyst, supervisor to the programming staff and finally a local area network (LAN ) / wide area network (WAN) / e-mail administrator.

    My intense technical expertise has served me well in my voice-over career. I research, buy, install, maintain and operate the many pieces of equipment in my stunning studio. My clients love that I have an IT background as I am able to perform scripts aimed at IT audiences with complete credibility and authenticity!

    For privacy purposes, I did not state the years of my actual employment. Trivia buffs will know that 1913 is the first year that Form 1040 was used. :)
