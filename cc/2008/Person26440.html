Valinda Lee’s Summary

Valinda is a career counselor with experience in both public and private higher education. Working in community colleges, graduate schools, and now at a women’s liberal arts college, she understands the importance of listening to and partnering with clients to help them through their career journey, wherever they happen to be. She is a certified administrator of the Myers Briggs Type Indicator (MBTI) and the Strong Interest Inventory (SII).

Preparing presentations for her students as well as for her colleagues is something that Valinda enjoys and she recently presented at the Mountain Pacific Association of Colleges and Employers (MPACE) conference on the topic of using new media with millennial students in university career centers. She has given other workshops focusing on giving parents the skills to nurture their children’s career interests and has spoken at La Sierra University to undergraduate psychology students about the use of career assessments in counseling.

In her current work, Valinda has acquired knowledge about careers with nonprofit organizations and is partnering with Idealist.org to help both college students and mid-life career changers effectively use technology in their nonprofit career search. She is also developing a career podcast series for college students.

For further information, you may contact Valinda at valinda.lee [at] gmail [dot] com.
