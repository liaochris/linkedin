Don Citarella’s Summary

A native of Kalamazoo, Michigan, Don graduated with a Bachelor of Science in Visual Communication from Ferris State University. At FSU, he began as a Media Production student worker and rose to become the campus's interim graphic designer. He founded the Visual Communication program's "Design Works" organization to better prepare design students for their careers. Before graduating, Don had already won a number of design awards, including the GRAAF's Addy Award.

Prior to graduation, Don was hired to work for Performancebase, and other online businesses in New York City during the dot-com surge. It was at these businesses that he realized the problems with existing design groups and began writing the business plan for a new type of studio, soon to be era//404.

In August of 2001, Don left the dot-com world to pursue these endeavors. The company grew quickly—already a network of 22 contractors—and two years later, he asked his brothers to join the team.

Don currently lives in New York City with his cat, Floyd.
