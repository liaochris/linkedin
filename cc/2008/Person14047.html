Mats Kallmyr’s Summary

I was implementing an Ericsson business strategically sourcing solution in all 12 SSC (globally).

My goal was to work abroad, in a multicultural environment. So in April 2005 I came back from an assignment from abroad, Riyadh (Saudi) and Dubai (United Arab Emirates). I worked in Middle East for over 2 years time as a Financial Controller, for the 3 branch offices Ericsson had their. During this time I worked very close to Project Control and Business Control.

I am very interested in Asian cultures, and it has always been my goal to work in Singapore, for at least 3-4 more years (or even more). My wife comes from Shanghai, China, and we have an apartment here since 1996. We use to visit Shanghai every year.

  • Gällöfsta

    pass, FUGL (FIRO etc), 2007 — 2007

    The UGL program was first developed for the Swedish National Defense Academy in 1981. The program is structured around Will Schutz’s FIRO-theory (Fundamental Interpersonal Relationship Orientation). The systematical approach of this theory gives the participants the opportunity to experience, and follow, the leadership.

    This FUGL program differs significantly from “traditional” leadership development programs. The pedagogical approach of the UGL program is based on “Experiential Learning”, which means that new learning is acquired through the following four steps; experiencing, reflecting, generalizing and testing new behavior.

    By attending the FUGL- program you will receive the essential tools necessary for effective leadership, such as:
    • Group Maturity Assessment
    • Conflict Resolution
    • Effective Communication
    • Situational Leadership
    • Increased Self-awareness
    • Active Listening

    UGL need to be done before you do the FUGL-Program
