Mike Borella’s Summary

Michael Borella is technical advisor and patent agent at a high-tech law firm, specializing in patent prosecution and litigation advice for telecommunications, engineering, and software companies.

Previously, he was Senior Vice President of Engineering at Fastmobile, Inc., where he ran multiple development teams in the US and China totaling over 60 staff members. His role included management of server and mobile handset software development, architecture, testing, program management and intellectual property administration. After successfully launching five major messaging services from Europe to Asia and the US, the company was sold to Research in Motion (RIM).

Previously to that, he was Director of R&D in the UTStarcom CDMA business unit, where he managed all R&D functions within this group. His staff totaled over 75 engineers and managers in the US and India. His other responsibilities include product and feature definition, standards, intellectual property development, business development, and customer interaction. In addition to UTStarcom, he has held positions at 3Com , IBM and ROLM, and has consulted to various high-tech companies and served as an expert witness.

Mike has taught at a number of universities, including Northwestern University in Evanston, Illinois, where he was an adjunct professor in the Masters of Information Technology Program (MITP). He has published numerous technical papers and been granted over 65 patents. He has been recognized numerous times within UTStarcom and 3Com for his contributions to their respective intellectual property development programs, including being named “Inventor of the Year” by 3Com in 2000.

Mike Borella’s Specialties:

3G wireless / CDMA2000 technology expert, years of experience with technical management, international standards and intellectual property.
