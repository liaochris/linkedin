  • Chairman

    East London and Lee Valley Mutual Guarantee Society

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Banking industry)

    January 1997 — July 1998 (1 year 7 months)

    This was an unusual project - for the UK that is. A mutual guarantee society is basically a credit union for businesses. Established as a co-operative, each member business would aim to save a regular sum and then, after a qualifying period, be able to borrow a multiplied amount against their savings. A number of societies were established in the UK but, unfortunately, the UK banking laws prevented them from operating as they should. It was a shame and no credit goes to the Treasury or (at the time) the Bank of England for not enabling them to work. In Portugal, a law was passed enabling MGSs to operate. Some of the largest banks in Europe (especially France) began as MGSs.
    Anyway, I was one of the instigators of this one, bringing a founding membership of 21 businesses if I remember rightly. It was a fun project and quite a disappointment to some of the members, although we did help several with finance elsewhere.
