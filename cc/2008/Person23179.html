Joe Rozsa’s Summary

My background lies in several years of e-commerce and web strategy management as well as leading strategic web business plans. I have significant experience in leading web site development from conceptualization to successful and very large implementations. Because of my experience in the web industry and search strategy, I regularly speak at technology conferences and seminars, including the recent Search Engine Strategies 2007 Chicago conference.

Joe Rozsa’s Experience

  • Founder / President

    SEO Columbus LLC

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Internet industry)

    January 2004 — Present (4 years 7 months)

    Consulting in search strategy for large and medium sized web businesses, including Microsoft Live, Marriott, Lowe's, and several other smaller e-commerce businesses. Twelve years of high profile search engine strategic planning with great success has allowed SEO Columbus to be "considered among the most respected search related experts in the midwest and one of the rising stars on a national stage", according to SEO Pro magazine. SEO Columbus coined the phrase "Advocate Generated Link Building" as a primary search engine optimization strategy, based on generating goodwill and publicity through independent blogger networks and other high profile, but free, promotional medium. This search engine optimization strategy alone has generated millions of dollars in recurring revenue for clients of SEO Columbus, and the transformation to this modern search strategy away from more traditional web (as well as offline) marketing campaigns is what makes a successful e-commerce business.

  • IT Manager

    Nationwide Insurance

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; NFS; Insurance industry)

    April 2006 — January 2008 (1 year 10 months)

    I managed a terrific group of five project managers, establishing consistent best practices and process improvements for the team in an effort to improve service to business partners as well as save money for the organization. A major improvement was molding Nationwide's inefficient and outdated Enterprise Solution Delivery methodology to make sense for managing the projects typically handled by our department, delivering projects faster and at a higher rate of quality than was present before taking the position.

    Simultaneously managed a group of five systems analysts that were responsible for defining business requirements for property insurance rate adjustment projects.
