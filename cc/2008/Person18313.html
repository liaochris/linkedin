Mō Mosaicthump’s Summary

Before I begin you need to know two things. Number one, the definition of my catchphrase, “ain’t nuthin’ neo about soul” and number two the definition of the type of music you will find at Mosaicthump.

“ain’t nuthin’ neo about soul“, should be self explanatory but it is not to most people. The term neo-soul is industry created. It seems that they are always trying to manufacture a way to make more money. The fact is, soul music is experiencing a resurgence these days. Our generation was beginning to miss music reminiscent of our parents favorites like Sly and the Family Stone, Earth Wind and Fire, Rufus, Donnie Hathaway, Stevie Wonder, Phyllis Hyman and Maze; just to name a few. The truth is, what they are calling neo-soul is, “a spiritual revival of decades old underground funk“. Did that look familiar? It should, I have adopted it as the best definition of music in this genre. Now you are ready to go on.

  • Carmen Rodgers & Geno Young with special guest L’Renee and introducing Tiaybe

    Mosaicthump Online Inc.

    January 2007 — January 2007 (1 month)

    Carmen Rodgers has a “home girl next door” charm resonating through a flow of words that seems to invade your most private thoughts. It’s the delivery of those words that is refreshingly calm and patient, making you want to scream. Her “sweet jazzy soul” sounds gently massages the ears of listeners with every lyric.

    Geno Young is one of the most versatile artists in soul music today; Geno is bound to write new chapters in the history of soul artistry. He is a singer in the mold of the classic soul singers of our time, fusing passion with precision and old soul with new style.

    L’Renee is jazz, soul, R&B, and hip-hop; she’s alternative. Detroit’s own L’Renee has got a special song just for you. When L’Renee sings there’s passion, there’s soul, there’s something that soothes – and makes you “wanna holla” at the same time. She’s definitely something special.

  • Nicolay Featuring DJ Dez

    Mosaicthump Online Inc.

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Entertainment industry)

    September 2006 — September 2006 (1 month)

    A record release party for internationally known producer Nicolay, musical architect of the classic Hip-Hop Duo Foreign Exchange.

    Nicolay is world renowned for his ability to use classic soul and hip-hop in a phenomenal and cohesive mix that can only be called genius. As one half of the now classic Foreign Exchange, he was instrumental in taking the hip-hop world by storm and upping the ante for every producer in the business. The resulting CD from that historic union with Phonte, “Connected,” will definitely go down as one of the must have CDs of our generation.

    Nicolay has worked with the kings and queens of underground soul and hip-hop, such as Alice Smith, Kenn Starr, 6th Sense, Roy Ayers, Cesar Comanche, Little Brother, Darien Brockington, Black Spade, Yazarah, and Sy Smith just to name a few. His newest effort entitled “Here” will most certainly solidify his position in the industry, while providing insight to this master’s diverse repertoire.
