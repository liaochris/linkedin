Eriks Goodwin’s Summary

I have a proven track record of causing business growth, either directly or indirectly. My focus is on growth-related consulting projects, public speaking, and the development of new materials related to business management, entrepreneurship, business networking and technology implementation.

I am a firm believer in adopting strengths-based organizational practices and goals. Innovation, tenacity and leadership are among my primary methods and I seek out equally ambitious clients with a desire to achieve rapid profit and business growth.

  • President

    UniMatrix xSP

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Internet industry)

    July 2003 — Present (4 years 11 months)

    Several of my clients at UniMatrix International insisted I continue working with them after my sale of the company, so I founded UniMatrix xSP in 2003.

    While the company provided both development and hosting services originally, the company's focus is now on providing carrier-class hosting solutions for web sites, eMail, and web-based applications. UniMatrix xSP actively seeks out professional web and application developers with whom to work.

    I chose to base all servers and applications provided by UniMatrix xSP on open source Linux software. Through implementation of bleeding edge technology, I was able to greatly reduce spam and other afflictions common to the Internet.

    I have taken the company into a growth mode and am seeking qualified professionals to work with on this endeavour.

  • President & CEO

    UniMatrix International Corporation

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Information Technology and Services industry)

    March 1994 — July 2003 (9 years 5 months)

    I founded the company in 1994. UniMatrix International specialized in business automation, web development, telecommunications and technological infrastructure.

    Partnering with a variety of companies, including IBM, Lotus, Sceptre, iEnterprises, and Comdial, UniMatrix provided top-notch enterprise-grade solutions to its local and national clients. UniMatrix worked with clients ranging from start-ups to multi-city law firms to multinational manufacturing companies.

    Specializations included web-based application development and hosting, infrastructure installation and deployment, CRM (Client Relationship Management), ERP (Enterprise Resource Planning), and workflow automation.

    Preferred software platforms included IBM Lotus Notes and Domino, iExtensions CRM, Red Hat Linux, Remark!, and others. Substantial supporter of open source development projects and technology.

    Expanded the company to 3 cities and 26 full-time employees before selling the company in 2003.
