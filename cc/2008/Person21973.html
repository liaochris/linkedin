Rolf Akker’s Summary

In 1996 I started working as a IT Professional for Pink Elephant. As was normal in those days I started at the helpdesk and gradually I worked my 'way up' to System Manager. The following assignment was as a system engineer but I quickly became involved in ITIL Change Management. From 1998 on I was involved in several ITIL processes up to the year 2000.

In 2000 I started working as Manager Remote Management for Netwatch. This did not last long, because of a takeover by Centric. Therefor I started working for Bureau Hoving & Van Bon in the end of 2000, holding a position as a consultant and as an editor. During the five years I worked for BHVB I learned a lot about IT (Service) Management and IT Governance and I was able to practice this at several companies.

In the latter part of my career at BHVB I became involved in ITGA, the association for IT Governance. ITGA consisted of several companies all sharing the idea to establish one single framework for IT Governance. This was when I realised that IT Governance was something I liked a lot.

In 2005 I became aware it was time to change and so I started working as a Consultant IT Governance for Atos Origin. During my short stay at Atos Origin I was able to explore other aspects of IT Management as well. So, I became involved in Bid Management and Crisis Management.

In september 2006 I made the move to the NV Nederlandse Gasunie as an ICT Strategist. At Gasunie I am responsible for the Gasunie Information Plan, The Strategic ICT Policies, The Gasunie Logical Data Model, and several other strategic issues.

Rolf Akker’s Specialties:

ICT Strategy, Information Planning, IT Governance, Demand Management, IT Service Management, IT Management, Crisis Management, Bid Management, Service Level Management, Change Management.
