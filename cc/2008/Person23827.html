Sophie Lagace’s Summary

I'm a professional engineer licensed in Washington, British Columbia and Quebec, working in environmental remediation, restoration, and impact assessment.

I'm versatile, and my strength lies in combining information from various sources to solve practical problems in environmental and civil engineering, appropriate technologies, and environmental health management.

Sophie Lagace’s Specialties:

Environmental impact assessment, environmental data management, water quality, environmental investigation, constructed wetlands, sustainability, solid waste management, containment systems.

Sophie Lagace’s Experience

  • Environmental engineer

    RIDOLFI Inc.

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Environmental Services industry)

    May 1999 — May 2008 (9 years 1 month)

    Much of my work involved environmental impact assessment and the preparation of support documents for permitting processes under the National Environmental Policy Act (NEPA), the Endangered Species Act (ESA), the Washington State Environmental Policy Act (SEPA), the California Environmental Quality Act (CEQA), and similar regulations. I also provided clients with data management expertise, from the creation of dedicated database applications using a variety of software and platforms, to complex data drilling and cross-linking, to information sharing and public outreach via Web sites.

  • Research Assistant/Technical Writer

    Hydro Resources International

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Environmental Services industry)

    January 1997 — April 1999 (2 years 4 months)

    Data collection and field and laboratory testing of water quality parameters and macrophyte production. Research, preparation and editing of technical reports and journal articles on watershed restoration, wastewater treatment, and constructed wetlands. Preparation of spreadsheet models (e.g., evaluation of constituent removal in wetlands, projection of peri-urban sanitation costs, watershed water balance, etc.)

  • Lecturer/Teaching Assistant

Teaching assistant, laboratory preparation and supervision, and grading for courses on water quality analysis, water and wastewater treatment, constructed wetlands design, environmental impact assessment, and watershed restoration.

  • Senior Staff Engineer

Preparation of customized quality control and quality assurance programs, and of seminars.
