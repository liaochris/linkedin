Jessica Margolin’s Summary

I am the kind of person who keeps organizations healthy while they undergo stress, or who fixes broken projects, programs and organizations. Because of my broad background, I relate well to most people, and my non-judgmental, problem-solving attitude leads to drastically improved results.

For the past several years I have focused on understanding tools to facilitate decision making based on what I have learned intuitively. It has led me to build some knowledge in the area of understanding how to measure intangible assets, which are the assets that predict future value in a company, or can be used to measure outcomes in a non-profit. I blog on this and other topics.

I'm now in a position to seek a more formal role in a consulting firm, financial firm, corporation or social venture that is interested in moving beyond "diversity programs" and "triple bottom line" and incorporate gathering this type of knowledge in such a way as to benefit everything from morale to financial return to risk management. (It won't cure baldness, though.)
