Leon Ho’s Summary

Leon Ho has a decade of experience in technology and the Internet. As manager of Software Engineering at Red Hat, Inc., Leon led an international team of software engineers to add international support -- including 22 languages -- to Red Hat's Enterprise Linux. In 2005, he founded lifehack.org, a blog committed to sharing what Leon had learned about productivity and personal development as the manager of a team of professionals spanning several continents.ﾠ

In 2007, Leon left Red Hat to launch Stepcase as an umbrella for both lifehack.org -- by then one of the most popular blogs in the world, ranked in the top half of Technorati's Top 100 Blogs list -- and his new endeavor, Stepcase Apps. Leon's commitment to increasing productivity and individual happiness by leveraging the power of the Internet to make us more efficient, better connected, and more mobile has won him the #4 spot in BusinessWeek's Top 24 Young Asian Entrepreneurs.

何樂頌(Leon Ho)曾在跨國軟件公司Red Hat擔任程式開發主管，統領不同地方的同事建立Linux 多種語言系統。由於長期的工作訓練，令他對「如何提升生產力」這課題加深了認識，他覺察到沒有很專門的網站能提供全面的改善生產力方案，於是在 2005年成立了lifehack.org，一個專為管理人而設計的有關生產力及個人改革的網誌，時至今日，Lifehack已成為全球100名最受歡迎網誌之一。

直至 2007年Leon正式全身投入自己的事業，創立了Stepcase公司，將Lifehack及應用程式結合起來。Leon非常洞悉互聯網的趨勢，冀透過創新的程式及流程，將每個人的工作能力提升，Leon亦因此於2007年被BusinessWeek選為「全亞洲年青企業家」（Young Asian Entrepreneurs）第四名。
