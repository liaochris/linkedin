Corin Goodwin’s Summary

My goal is to help families recognize the unique intellectual and social-emotional needs of their child(ren) and assist them in finding or creating an educational environment in which the child can thrive, and which works for the family as a whole.

In addition to serving as executive director of the non-profit Gifted Homeschoolers Forum, I write articles, give presentations and workshops for small groups and at conferences.

I joined the board of Josephine Community Libraries so that I can bring my expertise to bear on the need to have open, functioning libraries in the county in which I am raising my children.

Corin Goodwin’s Specialties:

Non-profit management. Policy analysis. Government and public relations strategy. Providing support, guidance and advocacy to families of gifted or 2e children. Public speaker, author, consultant.

Corin Goodwin’s Experience

  • Vice President

    Josephine Community Libraries

    (Non-Profit; 51-200 employees; Libraries industry)

    November 2007 — Present (9 months)

    JCLI is a non-profit organization formed to reopen and operate the library system in Josephine County, OR. This position involves management, business and political strategy, public education, community building, grant writing and a great deal of fundraising.

  • Founder & Director

    Gifted Homeschoolers Forum

    (Non-Profit; 11-50 employees; Education Management industry)

    November 2004 — Present (3 years 9 months)

    Founded Gifted Homeschoolers Forum at the urging of clients and colleagues who saw that the population of families with gifted or gifted/ld children who were choosing alternative education was increasing rapidly. The growth in numbers necessitated a focus that was impractical for any organization focusing on either gifted *or* homeschooling to provide. GHF has grown to become a major resource for families and professionals worldwide who are interested in giftedness and in the wide array of educational options beyond the traditional classroom. GHF provides education, support, and outreach through a variety of methods.

    HomeSchool Association of California

    (Non-Profit; 11-50 employees; Education Management industry)

    September 2002 — May 2008 (5 years 9 months)

    Government relations at state and national levels, including networking related organizations and coordinating public relations strategy. Responsible for monitoring legislation and court decisions pertaining to homeschoolers in California, performing analysis to determine if any such legislation is beneficial or harmful to HSC members, and advocating as appropriate.

    (Non-Profit; 11-50 employees; Education Management industry)

    September 2002 — November 2004 (2 years 3 months)

    Created this position to provide advice and assistance for families seeking guidance in educating their differently abled children. HSC is a statewide association supporting families choosing alternative methods of education.
