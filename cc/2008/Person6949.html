Earon Davis ’s Summary

Earon has a broad, varied background, from years as a writer and environmental health lawyer and consultant, to nonprofit management, integrative bodywork and holistic healing. He is completing a book that integrates much of what he's learned in his life and career. "Divine Primates: Hope for Our Stressed-Out Species" presents a unique view of human nature that gives new hope as we attempt to change our collective awareness in ways that foster sustainability. Divine Primates helps us re-define our "Systems Thinking" so that we can re-direct some of our culture's diffuse energies towards sustainability and peace.

Earon's current professional goals include developing his practice at the Integrative Medicine Program at Evanston (Northwestern) Healthcare, teaching, getting his book published, and possibly finding an academic or leadership position that combines his talents and interests.

His networking value at this time is varied and Earon is open to change. He can provide professional services to linked-in members (and their organizations) interested in exploring systems thinking, bodywork and energy work and/or interspirituality as a modality of spiritual and traditional healing and transformation - as a way to understand and access the inner voice and strength. He could also be involved with projects in which massage and spirituality are used for clients/employees as a stress-reduction, health education, teambuilding experience. (He's had marvelous results in 15 minute fully clothed massages at the workplace or classroom.)

Through expanding systems thinking, bodywork, energywork and breathwork, Earon helps people find their "reset button" so they can envision better futures. His premise is that it isn't chiefly our logical minds that hold us back - but rather our unconscious minds - which can often be opened through creativity, touch, body awareness, relaxation, breath work and imagination.

Earon Davis ’s Specialties:

Earon performs individual and group healing work, teaching and strategic/systems analysis, based upon his education (B.A. in Sociology, Master's degree in Public Health and a Law degree), in-depth study of spirituality and wisdom traditions, experience in nonprofit management, environmental advocacy, teaching, holistic healing and a wide range of publications. He is a licensed massage therapist, journalist and futurist. The recommendations below provide a glimpse of Earon's effectiveness.
