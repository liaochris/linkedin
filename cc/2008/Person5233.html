Dave Aiello’s Summary

David E. Aiello is the lead developer of RinkAtlas.com, a Google Maps mashup that provides directions to over 2,000 ice rink facilities in North America. Regarding RinkAtlas, Guy Kawasaki said, "If you ever need to find a rink, this is the site to use. It’s the first mashup that I’ve found useful."

In October 2007, Dave joined US Auto Parts as a senior software engineer. He maintained two e-commerce websites that sell swimming pool and outdoor products. He left the company in February 2008.

In May 2007, Dave joined Billtrust as a senior developer. He rewrote Billtrust’s customer service web application to add CRM features. He left the company in September 2007.

In June 2006, Dave developed and launched RinkAtlas.com.

In November 2003, Dave founded OperationGadget.com, an electronic gadgets weblog. He is currently serving as editor. Operation Gadget is a Movable Type-based weblog that uses Amazon Web Services to provide purchase information about products discussed on it.

Dave founded Chatham Township Data Corporation, an Internet application development company. CTDATA develops complex web sites that contain relational databases.

Dave has performed dozens of consulting projects at large and small corporations and non-profit organizations. Clients that he has served on behalf of CTDATA include Bear Stearns, J.P. Morgan Chase, ADP, Schering-Plough, Sybase, and L.L. Bean. His non-profit experience includes work with USA Hockey and Teach for America.

Dave also founded the web publishing consulting practice called WeblogImprovement.com which focuses on the Movable Type publishing platform.

Dave is an NCAA Ice Hockey Official at the Division III level, a USA Hockey Level 4 Official, and member of the National Ice Hockey Officials Association.

Dave received his B.S. in management from Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute. He is married to Kathleen Aiello, MD, a pediatrician in private practice in Ambler, PA.

Dave Aiello’s Specialties:

web publishing systems, the Movable Type web publishing system, Google Maps mashups, Amazon.com E-commerce Services, web application development with PHP or Perl
