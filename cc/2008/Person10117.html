  • Jack Davis Sr. "School of Business", Lifetime Automotive Dealer

    Expertise, Automotive Industry

    I bought my first car wholesale when I was 12 years old using money I had saved up from various odd jobs around the dealership and Christmas gift monies. I still remember collecting my silver dollars and scraping together change to take to the auction with me.

    The car, a 1963 Chevy Nova, was $425 (including the $25 auction fee). I took it home that night with help from my dad, and spent the next week getting it cleaned up, detailed, rebuilt and ready. You know, I sold that car in less than two weeks and made over $200. Not bad for kiddie money.

    I immediately rolled the profit and some quickly earned extra elbow grease money into a dark Green 1969 Opel GT and a 1966 Pontiac Catalina (a four speed manual, red, with a white top and white interior).

    Though I was not old enough to enjoy driving these cars, the rest, as they say, is dealer history.

    Activities and Societies:
    I started working on my father's horse farm when I was 8; the first things I learned to drive and maintain were tractors and large machinery; I learned to drive a Volvo inside a dirt track horse ring; once I turned 16 I began hot rod racing; I learned to customize my cars because my dad used to own a race team and his chief mechanic used to help me; I attended private grade school as a child so I would learn the appropriate etiquette for business; I've been around trading and racing everything.
