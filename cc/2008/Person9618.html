Holly Setter’s Summary

I just completed an internship in Washington, D.C., working for GalleryWatch, an online legislative tracking company. While there, I covered Congressional hearings (with a focus on the Senate) for GalleryWatch, as well as its sister publication CongressNow. I also contributed to Roll Call newspaper, gathered testimony from hearings to be posted on the Web and assisted with GalleryWatch's Web site maintenence.

Prior to that, I had interned at The Lapeer County Press, where I covered a variety of stories that ran on the news, features, community, business, and sports pages.

The bulk of my journalistic experience has been at my college paper, The Pleiad. I've held the positions of staff writer, senior writer, news editor, Web editor and I will be serving as Editor-in-Chief of the paper this fall. In these positions, I have been responsible for covering the Student Senate and administration beat, managing as many as ten writers, editing stories, page lineups and layouts, Web design and guiding the entire staff of the paper.

While working for The Pleiad, I have won numerous awards, including the Roxanna Sayre Endowed Scholarship in Environmental Journalism and two Michigan Press Association College awards (first place investigative reporting in a Division II paper, second place column in a Division II paper).

I intend to work as a government reporter upon graduation. Ideally, I would love to return to Washington, D.C., to continue reporting on Congress at either a newspaper or an online publication.

Holly Setter’s Specialties:

Governmental reporting, editing, AP style, Adobe InDesign, Adobe Photoshop, Web design, typing speed of 70 wpm
