Jeff Hunter’s Summary

Accomplished innovation strategist, technologist, author, speaker and entrepreneur focused on creating systemic changes that inspire sustainable growth by innovating in the areas of talent, learning and technology. Articles / quotes published in Fortune’s Business Innovation Insider, Wall Street Journal, ERE, the CRL Journal, Recruiting.com, and WetFeet. Most recent speaking engagement at OECD's Innovation Summit in Copenhagen, Denmark entitled "Creating an Innovation Ecosystem" and "Innovating for Innovators." I have applied for two patent in the areas of search and online community.

Systemic Innovation, Strategic Planning, Learning Systems, Business Process Outsourcing, Writing / Speaking, Talent Acquisition and Management, Human Resources Information Technology, Agile Development Methodologies, Sales Strategy and Execution, Marketing and New Markets Strategy, Contract Negotiation, Corporate Development and Business Development / Partnership Management

  • Founder

    Talent Unconference

    (Educational Institution; Myself Only; Human Resources industry)

    January 2007 — Present (1 year 7 months)

    The Talent Unconference was first held in January 2007. The purpose of the Talent Unconference (http://www.taluncon.com/) is to assemble leaders from diverse disciplines associated with fields that strategize, operationalize, educate and invest in human talent. These fields include business, government, academia, education and NGOs.

    The primary purpose of Taluncon is to establish and forward the proposition that in the 21st century talent is more valuable than capital and that systems, language, leading measures and infrastructure should be developed and strengthened to motivate and capitalize on individual's talents.

    The Unconference arose from my writings on my blog "Talentism" (http://www.talentism.com/). These writings explore tactics and strategies for actualizing the power and potential of talent in business environments in order to maximize value creation in sustainable economic systems.

  • Co-Founder / CEO & Chairman

    Euphorion

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Computer Software industry)

    April 1997 — March 2002 (5 years)

    Euphorion was based on the prediction that the Internet was going to require “adaptive, flexible enterprise systems” and “service-oriented architectures” to become a useful long-term solution to the IT departments. Euphorion developed and implemented unique software frameworks and methodologies called e-blox that reduced the “risk of rapid change” associated with deploying large-scale, Internet-facing enterprise applications. E-blox was one of the first development architectures based on XML and dynamic data exchange amongst distributed services.

    Using e-blox Euphorion became a leader in the development of custom recruitment management solutions, including developing, deploying and supporting Talent Alliance’s Talent Management solution and Heidrick & Struggles Leaders On Line. Euphorion was purchased by Catenas, Inc. in 2001.
