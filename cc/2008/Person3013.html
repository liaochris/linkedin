Robert James DeGemmis’s Summary

My professional experience began in the fall of 1999 after being released from the Asheville Smoke of the United Hockey League. Since then I have had professional endeavors in both politics and hockey. Both continue today.

After the 2002 Farm Bill I began working with the American Nursery & Landscape Association. At ANLA I have primarily managed our legislative conferences, developed issue messages, organized and trained stakeholders and handled lobbying and political finance disclosure.

During this same period of time I began coaching on volunteer basis for the University of Maryland Women’s ACHA Club. The U Maryland experience led to paying positions with the Montgomery Youth Hockey Association (MD) and the Towson University Men’s team (2003-2006).

In 2006 I began Red Line Hockey. RLH is custom goaltender instruction, training and analysis. I am now putting my hockey energy toward this endeavor while looking forward to opportunities surrounding the 2008 elections.

In the near future developing RLH alongside a consulting business handling administrative and advocacy needs of trade associations, non-profits and private firms is a possibility.
