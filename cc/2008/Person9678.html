Harold Shaw’s Summary

I enlisted in the U.S. Coast Guard eleven days after graduating from Nokomis Regional High School in 1975. What a way to enter into the world, but it was actually the best thing I ever did for myself.

After finishing boot camp, I was assigned to the USCGC SPAR out of South Portland and learned that I really didn't enjoy life on the ocean, but that I was really good with paperwork. That is the career path that I choose and it landed me in several interesting places:

USCG Air Station Cape Cod - Otis ANGB, MA
USCG Marine Inspection Office - St. Ignace, MI
USCG Headquarters - Washington, D.C.
USCG Research and Development Center, Groton, CT
USCG Resident Inspectors Office - 378 FRAM Project, - Bath, ME
USCG Group Sandy Hook - Highlands NJ
USCG First Coast Guard District, Boston, MA

I then had a choice of going into the computer technology field or working with children when I left the military. I choose kids, it was the best decision I could have made, except in the pocket book, but it has rewards that go far beyond the monetary aspects.

I worked in Good Will-Hinckley's cottage and boys regroup program for 2 1/2 years after retiring. I then changed jobs for what I thought was a promotion in the social services field and found it to have it is own challenges, which I did not enjoy as much, then I wandered for a couple of years, at one point becoming an Office Manager for a Pharmaceutical Company, I was offered an opportunity to go back to working with kids at Good Will-Hinckley and found that I really loved teaching and eventually became a certified Special Education Teacher.

What am best at: getting things done, being organized, completing paperwork and working with people. I love to learn new things and enjoy using technology, even when it doesn't always make my life easier.

My goals are to be ready for opportunities that may appear, but to enjoy what I already have.
