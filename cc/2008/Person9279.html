Richard Haimann’s Summary

During the last 250 years of the industrial revolution, we have innovated beyond belief to drive more economic productivity, more value, from each individual.

In this next century the greatest economic value will come from those innovations that squeeze more and more productivity out of our natural resources and environment, rather than out of people.

This is my mission - to drive innovation around environmental productivity - to create products, services, and systems that continuously reduce the natural resources and environmental impacts each person needs to increase their personal productivity.

I've been consulting in the environmental space for 18 years, drawing together disparate technologies and integrating them to solve environmental problems, constantly innovating to create value for my clients, help them reduce their environmental impact footprint, comply with regulations, and find wealth-creating opportunities within their own systems and processes that, through small modification can reduce their overall environmental costs.
