William Kappele’s Summary

I am a chemist with extensive experience in Design of Experiments. My goal is to improve my customers' lives by making it easy for them to learn and use practical, objective Design of Experiments in their daily experimentation.

William Kappele’s Specialties:

Design of Experiments, Gage Repeatability and Reproducibilty Studies

William Kappele’s Experience

  • Owner

    Objective Design of Experiments

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Professional Training & Coaching industry)

    August 1998 — Present (9 years 10 months)

    Objective Design of Experiments provides you with educational workshops to help you study the complexities of nature thoroughly, confidently, and easily. You can learn more at http://www.ObjectiveDOE.com

  • President

    The Experiment Strategies Foundation

    (Non-Profit; 1-10 employees; Professional Training & Coaching industry)

    December 1997 — September 1998 (10 months)

    I was responsible for the operation of The Experiment Strategies Foundation, a non-profit Foundation whose mission is to help people of all ages develop understanding and confidence in the use of numbers and math. The Foundation specializes in teaching statistical experiment design (design of experiments or DOE) to scientists and engineers. The emphasis is always on practical product improvement. I also had responsibility as an instructor for the DOE classes.

  • Research and Development Chemist

    Lexmark International

    (Public Company; 501-1000 employees; Computer Hardware industry)

    March 1995 — December 1997 (2 years 10 months)

    I was responsible for the design and development of pigmented thermal ink-jet inks. I co-invented the co-solvent systems used in the pigmented black inks sold in Lexmark printers, including the Lexmark 7000. I also co-invented a pigmented color ink set which was displayed at the 1996 Summer Olympics in Atlanta. I performed extensive internal consulting on the design and analysis of experiments.

  • Research and Development Chemist

    Hach Company

    (Public Company; Chemicals industry)

    1992 — 1995 (3 years)

  • Printed Circuit Board Plating Engineer

    Hewlett-Packard

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; HPQ; Information Technology and Services industry)

    1990 — 1992 (2 years)

    I was responsible for the printed circuit board electroplating line. I also had responsibility for the photoresist stripping line, the copper etching line, the tin etching line, and the electroless copper plating line.

  • Research and Development Chemist

    Hewlett-Packard

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; HPQ; Information Technology and Services industry)

    1986 — 1990 (4 years)

    I was responsible for the design and development of dye-based thermal ink-jet inks. I invented the improved ink set released with the Paintjet printer. I also had responsibility for providing chemical support for all thermal ink-jet projects. I trained the chemical technicians in the chemistry lab and helped to select equipment suitable for the measurement of ink physical properties.
