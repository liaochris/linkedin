Nathaniel Palmer’s Summary

* Helped lead the transformation of a small services business into a global enterprise across five continents, ultimately selling to $2.0 Billion, publicly-traded firm

* Founder of a market leading business advisory firm and chairman of the “Transformation and Innovation” conference series

* Recognized thought leader with multiple studies published on business technology and marketing

* Author of "The X-Economy", a critically-acclaimed text on the post-dotcom economy

* Lead sponsor/investor in nearly two-dozen commercial real estate transactions totaling over 300 single and multiple family unit sales


Nathaniel Palmer is an active advisor, investor and entrepreneur. Founder of Transformation+Innovation, he was previously Director, Business Consulting for Perot Systems Corp, and for over a decade was Vice President and Chief Analyst of Delphi Group where he built one of the fastest growing and highest performing business units.

The author of over 200 research studies and published articles as well as the “The X-Economy” (Texere, May 2001) Nathaniel has been featured in numerous media ranging from Fortune to The New York Times.

He has authored dozens of articles in IT publications such as CIO and InformationWeek as well as monthly columns in Business Transformation Journal, BPM.com, and Business Integration Journal. He has also been featured as a guest expert on National Public Radio and World Business Review. His regular column “Open for Business” is featured in each issue of the Enterprise Open Source Journal.

Frequently cited for his work on business process management, Nathaniel was the first individual to receive the award of "Laureate in Workflow," an internationally recognized distinction today held by over 200 business process pioneers.

Consisting of over 300 members worldwide, the Coalition is the primary standards body for this significant software market.
