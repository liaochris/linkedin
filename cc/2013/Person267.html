James Crosson's Experience

API and Client Integration Manager, Credit Technology

Intercontinental Exchange

Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; ICE; Financial Services industry

April 2011 – Present (2 years 2 months) New York, NY

Am responsible for management of API-based services and client integrations for ICE's credit execution business, Creditex.

Upon joining ICE in 2011, led the initiative to launch the company's first API based trading platform with a major swaps dealer. Today, based on our competitive advantage in technology and the evolving market structure of the swaps business, API-based trading is quickly becoming the de-facto approach for both fixed income and derivatives.

In 2012, Creditex became the first market in our industry to support algorithmic trading over our API. These efforts support the existing brokerage business and contribute to several new strategic opportunities to expand to new markets. This year, Brazil's largest clearinghouse, Cetip, will launch an electronic bond trading platform utilizing Creditex's software and API.

A multi-disciplinary role that bridges business and technology, am responsible for maintaining relationships with client technical teams to ensure the competitiveness of our service, representing the company on industry groups ranging from ISDA to FIX Protocol, and coordinating API-based specifications and development internally. Am also responsible for defining Creditex's business practices around client on-boarding and support.

Managed the firm's strategic initiatives, most lucrative software deliveries, and represented the company on numerous industry groups. Supported international clients and collaborated with international teams for engineering and support. Role included a substantial amount of time on-site with clients.

Director, Technical Support and Quality Assurance

CecilRep LLC

May 2004 – August 2008 (4 years 4 months)

CecilRep was a software firm focused on trade and regulatory reporting initiatives. Ahead of its time, CecilRep pre-dated the arena of "big-data" in the financial services industry. The software utilized search technology to analyze trade and reporting data in a manner not before seen in the industry. The user interface allowed trade and operations personnel to find information and generate reports without knowledge of data structure or complex languages such as SQL.

Real-time reporting tools for traders, a new innovation at the time, was an early glimpse of the soon-to-be industry obsession with data analytics; helping to enable efficient high speed trading.

The firm was started in 2004. The software and operations of the company were licensed to FIX Flyer in 2008. Was the company’s lead client advocate; responsible for the overall technical quality of software and services. Joining the company in its infancy, helped start operations of the business and launch the original platform.
