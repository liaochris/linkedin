Dubtitled Entertainment has worked with such seminal companies as the Grammy Award winning record labels Tommy Boy Records and Downtown Records, as well as the phenomenally successful Rockstar Games, best known for creating the Grand Theft Auto video games & soundtracks.

"State of Emergency" aka ( S.O.E.) is a controversial action and adventure video game that won the prize for 'Best Video Game' at the Electronic Entertainment Expo aka ( E3 ) after being initially developed by the Scottish firm VIS Entertainment & released by Rockstar Games worldwide. All of the voice over artists for this game were cast by Dubtitled Entertainment founder creator, Mic Neumann. The "State of Emergency" aka ( S.O.E.) game has military-style violence, including political assassinations and was denounced by some Washington state politicians for its similarity to the real-life 1999 World Trade Organization riots & protests in Seattle which caused $3 million in damages. The game features a fictional 'American Trade Organization' as the antagonistic establishment and it made many media 'Top Ten Lists', including a list of the "Most Controversial Video Games of All Time".

Tommy Boy Entertainment (formally known as Tommy Boy Records) is a record label started in 1981 by Tom Silverman. The label is widely recognized for significant contribution to the development of hip hop music, dance music, and electronica.

In 2001, Tom Silverman, in collaboration with producer-director Mic Neumann and his company Dubtitled Entertainment, launched an independent television and film division known as Tommy Boy Films, LLC.

In 2003, Tommy Boy Films’ first project, a half-hour television series titled Kung Faux was released on DVD after first being licensed in dozens of countries around the world to television networks such as STAR Asia, FOX Latin America, & CANAL Plus Europe. Kung Faux has now been seen in over 150 countries worldwide. ( http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kung_Faux )
