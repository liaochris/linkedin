Aaron Wissner's Summary

Aaron Wissner is a public school teacher for Wayland Union Schools; co-president of the Wayland Union Education Association; and founder of the Local Future educational non-profit.

Wissner is a public school teacher with twenty years of experience. He taught students from Kindergarten through twelfth grade, in subject areas including: mathematics, science, computers, video production, and student leadership. He is active at all levels of the National Education Association (national, state, local) and co-president of the Wayland Union Education Association

Wissner founded Local Future, a non-profit organization which helps communities envision positive, sustainable futures. As president, he organizes and directs various events including The International Conference on Sustainability, Transition and Culture Change: Vision – Action – Leadership. The education channel on YouTube features over 200 free educational videos, with over one million views.

Wissner holds a Bachelor of Science in Mathematics and a minor in Physical Science from the University of Michigan in Ann Arbor; with additional course work from Michigan State University and Grand Valley State University. He lives in Michigan with his wife and two children.
