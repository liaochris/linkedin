Mark Manning's Summary

Community Organizer, Director, Teacher, Gardener, Performance Artist, Actor, Writer, Radio Producer & Host, Stage Manager, Engineer, Activist, Mentor, Designer, Board Member, Secretary, Environmentalist, Progressive, GLBT Organizer and Speaker. Mark Manning has worked in Kansas City Performing Arts for over 25 years. His work in professional theatre has led him to collaborations in radio, television, film, mural projects, not-for-profit service organizations, AIDS education and activism, first amendment and free speech issues, inner-city schools, children and education, mentoring, organic gardening, nutrition, and various social programs. Mark's collaborations have opened new career paths, permitting his passions to push himself into new territories, harvesting the fruit of life's experience. Mark has unique capabilities working inside groups of people with diverse backgrounds and points of view, to achieve objectives to serve a greater good. Mark has the ability to stay with projects through difficult times to insure they deliver to those in need. Mark's work as a stage manager, project coordinator and producer on hundreds of programs, give him special abilities to get the job done. Mark brings creativity, energy, and humor to each project. Mark has collaborated with many not-for-profit organizations including: Many professional theatres, KKFI Kansas City Community Radio, Kansas City Community Gardens, The Lesbian & Gay Community Center, KCK Public Schools, Passages, PROMO, ACLU, KC Free Speech Coalition, Willow Productions, UMKC, The Storytellers Inc., Crosscurrents Culture, and many others.

Specialties

Mark builds sustainable programs from small resources. He developed garden curriculum for schools. He helped create bylaws & policy manuals for several not-for-profits. He supervised building organic gardens at public schools with teams of 700 students. He created budgets, productions, fundraisers, programs. He is skilled in publicity, marketing, promotion, grant writing, education, program building, supervision, management of staff, computer systems, community outreach, writing, production.

KCK Community Gardens Project

September 1999 – Present (12 years 6 months)

The KCK Organic Teaching Gardens, an Initiative of The University of Kansas School of Medicine, Office of Cultural Enhancement and Diversity, Health Careers Pathways Program, K-12. Mark works with 1000 - 2000 students per year, between K through 8th grades, with organic, "raised bed" gardens, built directly on the school grounds of 3 Middle Schools and 4 Elementary Schools in the inner city of the Kansas City, Kansas. Mark conducts over 300 workshops annually in classrooms at these schools. Workshops include: planting, harvesting, cooking homegrown produce, parts of the seed, parts of the plant, worms & worm bins, salsa, and other subjects. Mark started the project under the guidance of Marcia Pomeroy, in 1999. The KCK Ortganic Teaching Gardens has been financially supported through grants from The Kauffman Foundation, and The University of Kansas Medical Center and has been recognized by The National Gardening Association, The Kansas City Star, The Green Bliss Festival, KACEE and the PBS television program America's Harvest.

Interests:

As a young child, Mark learned about gardening from his grandmother Edna Jacobsen on the family's McCool Junction, Nebraska farm. His grandmother raised a huge garden, with chickens, sheep and cattle. She preserved apples, wild berry jams, beets and virtually everything she grew was canned for consumption in winter months. Edna raised seven children with no running water. As a child, she lived in a sod house. Mark's passion for gardening with kids is fueled by the reality that the lessons he learned from his grandmother don't seem to be passed down to kids today. Young people deserve to know where their food comes from, especially with the rise of diabetes, obesity, and environmental concerns. We can all learn from our gardens how to treat ourselves and the world better.
