Matt A. Getz's Publications

  • Moving Beyond Football: A multi-image analysis of the 1969 Salvadoran invasion of Honduras

    • Publius
    • February 2011
    Authors: Matt A. Getz

    This paper presents the underlying causes of the 1969 Honduras-El Salvador conflict, including oligarchic resource allocation, domestic politics, the security dilemma and preemptive warfare. This paper seeks to understand the populational and domestic factors at the heart of conflicts and to demonstrate how these types of conflicts may reoccur in the future.

  • Acknowleding the Americas: Charting a New Course for American Foreign Policy

    • Columbia Political Review
    • October 24, 2011
    Authors: Matt A. Getz

    Despite Obama's intention to refocus U.S. policy toward Latin America, he has thus far failed to create a consistent or coherent policy in the hemisphere. This article discusses the sources of this deficiency—most notably, a debilitating lack of understanding about new political actors in Latin America—and identifies areas where Obama can push for progress.

  • The Chile Winter: Education Reform and Lessons for Global Protests

    • Columbia Political Review
    • December 19, 2011
    Authors: Matt A. Getz

    The ongoing student protests in Chile represent the most sustained social mobilization in Chile in over a decade. In this article I discuss the state of education in Chile, the reasons for the movement's contingent success and its prospects for the future.
