Project Manager, App Development

The Bodleian Library, University of Oxford

Higher Education industry

September 2010 – September 2011 (1 year 1 month) Oxford, United Kingdom

I was appointed to develop a new line of mobile and online products at the Bodleian, University Oxford - one of the oldest libraries in Europe and the largest University library system in the UK. I reported to the executive team and successfully project managed two major initiatives, designed to invigorate the library's digital identity:

(1) the launch of a new series of exhibition apps for iOS and Android, including 'The Making of the King James Bible' and 'Treasures'

(2) testing and development of a new web-based e-reader application, which will incorporate content from across the library (including Google book scans from the 17th-19th centuries).

I was responsible for defining the product roadmap and overseeing content management, user interface design, software development, marketing and launch.

Career Development Fellow

Centre for the Environment, University of Oxford

May 2006 – April 2010 (4 years) Oxford, United Kingdom

I served as the youngest member of faculty and held a prestigious University career development fellowship. I taught economics and human geography, covering topics related to creativity, innovation, publishing and intellectual property. My Doctoral research into the economics of digital media (and its impact on the US motion picture industry in particular) was published in a variety of international peer-reviewed journals and books.

Research Associate

Reuters Institute, University of Oxford

January 2008 – January 2010 (2 years 1 month) Oxford, United Kingdom

I was appointed to lead multiple research projects investigating the impact of digital media on news publishing in the UK. The work led to a published book, Whats Happening to our News, in addition to academic papers and presentations. In the course of the appointment, I networked extensively and eventually interviewed 70 senior media executives, journalists, politicians and industry experts. The book attracted international media coverage.

Teaching Associate

St Catharines College, University of Cambridge

October 2003 – February 2005 (1 year 5 months)

I was responsible for tutoring undergraduate students, developing a course related to economic geography, and assisting with field research. I performed these duties in parallel with my Doctoral research.

Research Analyst

Research Assistant
