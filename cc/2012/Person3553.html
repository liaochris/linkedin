Jerry Weinstock's Summary

Jerry Weinstock, President and CEO, has the energy and vision of a true entrepreneur. His 20+ years’ experience includes a broad mix of business development, sales and sales management, hardware and software technical competency, and marketing know how. Jerry has successfully launched three companies based on new economy principles.

• In 2010 Jerry was recognized for his contributions to the the Microsoft Dynamics CRM community and has been recognized as a Microsoft MVP for Dynamics CRM.

• In 2008 he became the President of the Kansas City Chapter of the International Association of Microsoft Channel Partners, an international organization. Jerry is one of the founding members of the chapter.

• In 2004 he formed the Kansas City Chief Marketing Officers Group which is based on exchanging ideas and driving results. The KCCMO has now gone web 3.0 and is a Linkedin group.

• In 2002 Jerry became the first and ultimately only Kansas resident to successfully file 12 lawsuits against bulk mailers that spammed iBizInitiatives with unsolicited mail. To chronicle the successful lawsuits and educate other people, he created the Web site – KansasNoSpam.com.

• Jerry has been recognized for his professional contributions and served on the Board of Directors of the American Marketing Association - Kansas City Chapter as the President and VP of Interactive Services from 2001 through 2005.

• At the height of the dot-com craze in 2000, he sold a permission-based e-mail newsletter business (AutoIdeas.com) for a niche market for the rate of $33 per name – a rate that was previously unthinkable.

• In 1996 Jerry was one of the first to develop a for-profit HTML based e-mail newsletter targeting a niche marketplace.

  • Microsoft Dynamics CRM "Building Consistency into Free Form Text Fields" - September 9, 2011

    • Microsoft CRM Team Blog
    • September 9, 2011
    Authors: Jerry Weinstock

    In Microsoft Dynamics CRM there are in a few core entities where the Primary Attribute field is a nvarchar Text field that the user must complete. These fields are system required and you are unable to edit them to change their type. Additionally, since they are required fields the record cannot be saved until it contains some data. The fields do not lend themselves to a consistent data entry format due to the nature of them being free form text fields.
