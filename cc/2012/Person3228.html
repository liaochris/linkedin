Lesley (Chase) Barton's Summary

An Activist & former Political Consultant for Congressmen & Campaigns Chase (Lesley) Barton created a positive empowered international community A United Journey For Humanity (www.myspace.com/desertstarlight8) in late 2007 to create awareness, recognize, & honor the love, compassion, & humanity that still exists & flourishes. It is a united front of 6,000+ members & has a skyrocketing circulation. A UJFH runs bi-weekly series, "My Heroes, Our Heroes, Humanity's Heroes," "The Gifted Artists You Should Know," & "Creative Innovators Advocating A Global Difference." The site features individuals/causes, creative artists/innovators & spotlights the positive impact their extraordinary actions & gifts are making for the world. June 2008, Chase guest radio hosted on "Dare To Dream" (www.universal7radio.com/Home.php). 2012 is the launch of the main online magazine (www.humanityunite.com) & radio show to shine the light even further.

A UJFH motto “If united we dare to envision it there is no reason that united we cannot achieve it."

Chase is also a violent crime survivor who helps bring survivors out of the shadows of shame & blame by writing a featured op-ed in the Chicago Tribune in 2007, as well as, openly speaking out in interviews in the Tribune & People Magazine's Heroes Among Us (www.voicesandfaces.org/article_people.asp) series in March 2006 Oscars Edition as a Survivor Participant in The Voices & Faces Project, founded by Anne Ream. Chase is Chief Ambassador & Co-Lead Contact for Nobel Peace Nominee and internationally revered humanitarian/poet/satellite radio host, Bryant McGill's, Goodwill Treaty For World Peace (www.goodwilltreaty.org). Chase was also interviewed by the “Italian Oprah” Judyth Piazza for her cutting edge radio show “The American Perspective” (www.thesop.org/article.php?id=10083) where she passionately speaks of these endeavors more in depth.
