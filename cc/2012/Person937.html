Brock Ray's Summary

I lead marketing strategy, interactive, and business development for Circadian, a Twin Cities-based creative agency specializing in brand development, management & advocacy. I am passionate about the effective communication of my clients’ marketing message and creating solutions that deliver results.

I bring a wide range of skills to bear in solving the unique communications challenges of Circadian’s clients. I am well-versed in marketing communications, design, market research, business planning, interactive & social media strategy, and marketing ROI. In addition, I also have extensive experience in recruiting, training and developing creative talent – essential skills when Circadian is pulling together a unique team of professionals to support our clients’ needs.

In addition to Circadian’s current client list, I have had the opportunity to develop strategic communications efforts for brands like 3M, ADT Home Security, Piper Jaffray, Sears, and Wilson’s Leather.

Originally hailing from Washington, D.C., I also spent nearly a decade in central Florida, and had enough of the heat. I now live in New Hope with my wife, Jennifer, and our son, Collin. When I’m not immersed in solving client problems, I can be found brewing beer and delving into life's deep philosophical issues.

Brock Ray's Experience

Marketing Principal

Circadian

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Marketing and Advertising industry

January 2010 – Present (2 years 2 months)

Circadian is a Twin Cities-based creative agency, specializing in brand development, management & advocacy. I am responsible for helping clients develop marketing strategy tied to their unique value proposition and designed to achieve results. On any given day, you can find me conducting competitive research, developing strategic communications plans, or writing creative marketing messages. I also lead Circadian's interactive efforts, helping clients design online solutions that engage users and motivate action. I take an analytical approach to my work, defining each stage and working through issues in a methodical fashion. At every step of the process, my goal is to ensure our clients understand exactly what we’re doing and why we’re doing it.

Chief Creative Officer

Brock Ray Creative

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Marketing and Advertising industry

August 2004 – January 2010 (5 years 6 months)

Brock Ray Creative focused on using marketing principles to inform intelligent design decisions, becoming a go-to resource for marketing and creative solutions. I provided strategic consulting services that helped clients achieve concrete business results. I delivered branding, websites, e-mail marketing, advertising, brochures, point of purchase, social media strategy and more for a variety of local & national clients. More than merely pretty pictures, I implemented the effective communication of my clients' marketing messages. In 2010, Brock Ray Creative merged with Swanson Creative to form Circadian.

Senior Recruiter

Celarity

Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Staffing and Recruiting industry

December 2007 – May 2009 (1 year 6 months)

Celarity specializes in connecting marketing, creative & interactive professionals to job opportunities with Minneapolis and St. Paul’s top employers. As a senior recruiter, I used my background in marketing and design to connect talented creative professionals with the employers who needed their services. I collaborated with clients to help identify, find & place the right talent for their unique needs. I managed Celarity’s employees as they worked on each project, ensuring positive results and satisfied clients.

Director of Programming

AIGA Minnesota

Nonprofit; 11-50 employees; Design industry

May 2007 – May 2009 (2 years 1 month)

AIGA Minnesota is the first place to turn for inspiration, professional development, and excellence in design in Minnesota. As the Director of Programming, I oversaw the successful development and implementation of all chapter programming, including DesignCamp®, the largest regional design conference in the country. This position required the considerable application of negotiation and interpersonal skills to ensure that programs were produced on tight timelines and even tighter budgets.

Graphic Designer

Privately Held; 201-500 employees; Marketing and Advertising industry

October 2005 – November 2007 (2 years 2 months)

Skyline is the premier source for remarkable face-to-face marketing experiences, offering trade show and event exhibits to match any size and budget, ranging from tabletop displays and portable displays to modular inline exhibits and large-scale island exhibits. I was responsible for planning, creating and producing integrated graphic design solutions for exhibits and marketing materials. I was also responsible for organizing and implementing training and professional development sessions for Skyline’s 150 graphic and exhibit designers nationwide.
