Christian Heinrich's Summary

Last Updated: 30 April 2012

Christian Heinrich has over sixteen years (since 1996) of professional experience and formal training in diverse disciplines of Security ranging from Locksmith, Shot Firer, Body Guard, IT Engineer and Chief Security Officer.

Christian Heinrich has been promoted several times within the Australian Government based on his performance within projects for the Defence and Intelligence Services. By reputation, this lead to several more projects within the three Major Banks of Australia and other NSW Government Critical Infrastructure and is regarded as the "Thought Leader" for Security within the Media and Entertainment industries.

Christian Heinrich has presented at a number of industry forums:
• OWASP Conferences in Australia, Europe and USA.
• ToorCon (USA)
• Shmoocon (USA)
• SecTor (Canada)
• CONFidence (Europe)
• Hack In The Box (Europe)
• SyScan (Singapore)
• RUXCON (Australia)
• AusCERT (Australia)

Christian Heinrich has been quoted in numerous security articles in “The Australian”, "The Guardian", etc newspapers and online at ZDNet Australia, Computerworld and Hacker News Network (HNN).

Christian Heinrich is the “Chair” of the Snort User Group (SUG) for Australia and New Zealand and previous "Chair" of the Microsoft Security Interchange for Sydney and has been nominated to Chair the “Australian Information Security Association” (AISA).

Christian Heinrich has also been selected for the Australian Attorney General’s Trusted Information Sharing Network for Critical Infrastructure Protection – Computer Network Vulnerability Assessment Program (CNVA) since its inception in 2004.

Christian Heinrich has delivered Professional Services for the NSW Government awarded under Contract 2319 and 2020 and presented to their internal Security Management Forum on PCI DSS.

Christian Heinrich was nominated for the "Individual" SCAwards 2009 and ISC2 2009 Asia-Pacific Information Security Leadership Achievements (ISLA) representing Australia.

OWASP Project Leader - OWASP "Google Hacking" Project

OWASP

Nonprofit; 10,001+ employees; Computer & Network Security industry

July 2008 – Present (3 years 11 months)

The OWASP "Google Hacking" Project entered Alpha Status Project in July 2008.

"Download Indexed Cache", the deliverable of this OWASP Project, implements the Google SOAP Search API to retrieve content indexed within the Google Cache and supports the "Search Engine Reconnaissance" section of the OWASP Testing Guide v3 which is superior to the Google Hacking Database (GHDB).

Christian Heinrich has presented this OWASP Project at the OWASP Australian (Feb 2009), European (May 2009) and USA (New York) (Sept 2008) Conferences, SecTor in Toronto, Canada (Oct 2008), SyScan'09 in Singapore (July 2009) and RUXCON 2K8 in Sydney (November 2008).

The OWASP "Google Hacking" Project was marked as inactive in September 2009 due to http://googlecode.blogspot.com/2009/08/well-earned-retirement-for-soap-search.html

The port to Microsoft's Search Engine, Bing, commenced in September 2010 and is available from https://dic.codeplex.com/

Project Manager

TCP Input Text

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; GOOG; Internet industry

February 2008 – Present (4 years 4 months)

"TCP Input Text" implements the Google SOAP Search API to extract TCP Ports and Fully Qualified Domain Names (FQDN) from Google Search Results into a .csv file and individual shell scripts for nmap and nc aka netcat to provide assurance of a listening TCP service since the time that has past of the last crawl performed by the "GoogleBot".

The port to the Microsoft's Search Engine, Bing, commenced in September 2010.
