Amaan Penang's Summary

Born to a family of entrepreneurs, from a very young age, Amaan assisted his father in many day to day operations of Cliff Worldwide. At the age of 13 he developed an inventory management system for internal use in the garment manufacturing department of Cliff in India. In his high school years, Amaan partnered up with local cellphone wholesalers in the Houston area to move their catalog online in order to boost sales by reaching a wider audience.

In the Fall of 2006, he started his undergraduate work at The University of Texas at Austin where he pursued a double major in Computer Science and Mathematics along with the Texas Business Foundations Program at UT's top-rated McCombs School of Business. During his first semester at UT, he joined Delta Epsilon Psi, a South Asian interest social and service fraternity, where he held roles such as academic and technical chair. He also assisted in logistical planning for his fraternity's annual signature events such as Project Come Together and Sugar Free Bowl.

As a senior he undertook an undergraduate research project comparing various design patterns seen in conventional software engineering to those seen on emerging mobile platforms. He finished his Bachelor of Science in Computer Science in the Fall of 2010 after which he did a brief stint as a freelancer for Austin area tech companies such as campus2careers.com and booksonboard.com to help automate the process of finding potential customers. He recently started working full time with a fantastic team of Scientists and Engineers at Ravel as a Software Engineer. His current focus is on programming for distributed systems to analyze big data using open source solutions.
