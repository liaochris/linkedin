  • Photographer

    The Great Peace March

    (Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    March 1986 — November 1986 (9 months)

    300+ people walked across the United States... leaving from Los Angeles, CA on March 1st 1986, arriving in Washington DC on Nov. 15th 1986.

    I shot about 500 rolls of film, as the quote unquote "official photographer" of the Great Peace March. One of my photos was featured on the book cover written by Franklin Folsom detailing the story of the GPM as well as numerous photos inside.

    Even though I was still in college at the time (taking off the 2 semesters to participate in the GPM) and having left my job waiting tables at a Pizza Hut in Kent, OH, I still treated this as a professional job, as if I was a photojournalist, and feel that I became a photojournalist.

    I made some beautiful images on the March, and as a result I had an interview with Tom Kennedy, Director of Photography at National Geographic in 1987.

    It was an amazing once in a lifetime experience.

  • Paper Boy

    Akron Beacon Journal

    (Public Company; KRI; Newspapers industry)

    1976 — 1979 (3 years )

    Large paper route, about 100 homes, I delivered the daily paper to several neighborhoods around the area I lived in.

    On Sunday we had more than 200 to deliver, so my mom would drive me and my brothers, who also had routes of their own, to deliver the Sunday papers, it was a big job, we would put together sections on Saturday night.

    I also had to collect, going door to door with the punch cards, I didn't enjoy that part of the job as much, but it taught me the importance of keeping current with responsibilities, even at a young age.
