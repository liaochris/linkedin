Fer Isella’s Summary

Fer Isella is an Argentinean music producer, entrepreneur, composer and performer artist based in Buenos Aires and New York City. He has produced music for record albums, live performances, films, spots and theatre. His work combines South American music, avant-garde jazz, post-rock, electronica, and free experimental improvisation in contemporary musical frameworks.

After being awarded with a Fulbright Scholarship to study at Berklee College of Music, Fer worked for SONY as a music producer selling more than 2 million records and achieving more than 15 platinum and gold albums.

As a keyboard player he's participated in the New York downtown scene, playing at venues like Tonic, Nublu, Bowery Ballroom with artists like Miho Hatori from Cibo Matto, Joy Askew (Peter Gabriel, David Bowie), Karina Zeviani (Thievery Corporation) among others.

Currently an active cultural entrepreneur:

Limbo Music, Indy Record Label 2.0 specialized in the best latin american music.

Limbo Fest, first Independent Music Festival in Buenos Aires.

Folklorear, the First Latin American Cultural Social Network.

Poly Music, music supervision for commerical spots in the US hispanic and general market.

Limbo Digital, a digital agency specialized in marketing online for creative industries and entertainment, with clients like Lily Allen in Argentina, etc.
