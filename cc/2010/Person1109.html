Ed Hale’s Summary

Ed Hale is writer, blogger, singer/songwriter, rockstar, activist, entrepreneur, occasionally controversial YouTube star and one hell of a hard man to keep up with -- who happens to love working on multiple projects with people who share a similar passion for group oriented achievement. Formerly known as Eddie Darling from the band Broken Spectacles, he is currently the lead singer of the popular Britpop/modern rock group Transcendence. He also performs, records and releases solo albums. He is currently signed to the Dying Van Gogh record label. Hale is known internationally to friends and fans as "The Ambassador." The nickname was given to him by friend and video gaming industry maven Michael Nichols due to Hale's love of travel, exploring other cultures, and learning foreign languages.

Current interests for LinkedIn: Making new contacts, sharing resources, or new or ongoing cooperative projects in: All areas of the music business, literary and publishing industry, writing and blogging projects and opportunities, film screenwriting projects, the health, wellness, and anti-aging industry, business consulting opportunities, the television industry, social and political activism projects, civilian diplomacy missions, spiritual or enlightened art or entertainment, new business ideation and development, marketing and branding, consciousness expansion projects, internet and web 2.0 projects, real estate investment and contrarian investing, technology and sociology research and projects - especially ones focused on current and future trends.

    Optum Consulting Health

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Health, Wellness and Fitness industry)

    January 2006 — Present (4 years 1 month)

    What started out as a side business of offering nutritional supplement advice to friends due to being in the health and wellness industry for many years soon turned into a full time job due to word of mouth. The idea was simple enough: answer people's questions and offer advice on what vitamin and nutritional supplements they should take based on their specific needs, age, gender, and health concerns on a per-minute fee basis - not hourly - with no minimum and no appointment or consultation necessary.

    They receive only the information they want or need, they don't have to spend an initial "consultation fee" of two to three hundred dollars. They email or call as often as they need and only get charged for the amount of time it takes me to answer their questions or concerns. If it takes me five minutes to answer someone's question about calcium, or arthritis, or weight loss, or what have you, then I only bill them for five minutes. They get the information they need and can move on.

  • Founder and President

    Sebastians Smoothies Juice Bars

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; JUICE; Restaurants industry)

    January 1996 — October 1998 (2 years 10 months)

    Hale founded the Sebastians Smoothies Juice Bar chain in 1996 in FL with 2 partners due to their shared passion for juicing fresh fruits and vegetables and concocting healthy and delicious smoothies. Two years yielded much fun and learning but the juice bar business is not a high-profit-margin business model. A lot of waste, high employee turn-over due to the necessity for low wages, and Smoothie King and Jamba Juice were about to enter into a fierce competition with one another.
    So with a touch of sadness and much relief we closed the stores after two years. A good call. Jamba Juice got a truckload of venture capital less than two years later and started becoming the behemoth that it is today.

    I am still asked on occasion by friends and clients about their desire to start a juice bar. My advice is always the same. If you want to run it yourself all day and night and make it a little mom and pop, you might be able to squeeze a living out of it. But you aren't going to get rich.
