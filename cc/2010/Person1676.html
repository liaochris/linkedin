Igi Fischer’s Experience

  • Owner and General Manager

    Igi's TV Network

    (Internet industry)

    2009 — Present (1 year )

    Project developer, website/graphic designer, social media network administrator, seo/ smo programmer, programming director, marketer, webmaster.

    * Igi's TV (IgisTV) Network provides recent news videos of over sixty different YouTube channels (such as Associated Press, Reuters, CBS, CNN, Foxsports, AFP, TVGuide, Google, Howcast and many others) bundled into seven different video streams such as News, Economy, Sports, Showbiz, Lifestyle, Travel and Tips. On the contrary to other websites, Igi's Network is managed just from a one page navigation site and all content is distributed across a network of third party services such as blogs, micro blogs, social networks, online shops and other web pages.

    To ease up finding specific pages with complicate URLs, Igi's TV Network uses a defined URL standard - I named it sub-domain navigation system. For this, the user enters a certain keyword as sub-domain in addition to '.igistv.com' (e.g. 'sports.igistv.com', 'shop.igistv.com' or 'privacypolicy.igistv.com') into the browser's text bar (no need to use Google) and gets accordingly redirected either to the most recent sports videos of Igi's TV Sports (at Friendfeed), to Igi's TV Shop (at Amazon) or to Igi's TV Privacy Policy (posted on a Tumblr blog).

    Igi's TV Network is designed being a multimedia news sharing showcase, a concept for integral Web 2.0 system design focusing on search engine optimized linking of social networks, profile pages, blogs, micro blogs, online shops and any other kind of websites and pages. The user shall have the impression to stay permanently on the very same website, even though browsing across various domains of different providers and services throughout the entire Internet.

    "My most recent baby, can be managed by one person, a smooth and easy-to-use Web 2.0 innovation/ concept/ showcase and besides, probably one of the broadest and best structured resources of recent news videos on the entire Internet."
