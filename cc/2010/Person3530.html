Scott Noon’s Summary

Scott Noon is Vice President of Marketing and Business Development at Teachscape, Inc. As the marketing leader at Teachscape, Scott drives the product marketing strategy, builds lead generation systems, manages online marketing and mar-com initiatives, and offers strategic vision to the company's positioning, branding, and online presence. Teachscape's recent merger with Edgenuity now provides the opportunity to combine exceptional instructional effectiveness systems with best practices in professional learning solutions to offer districts meaningful school improvement strategies.

In his business development role, he actively consults with superintendents of large urban school districts to develop custom teacher professional development solutions. He also works with other business leaders in the education space to create mutually beneficial partnerships that enhance customer solutions, leverage both companies' marketing opportunities, and expand revenue.

Previously, Scott was the Regional Vice President of Sales for Riverside Publishing Company, the leader in assessment services for a variety of markets. As a sales manager, Scott led the northeast sales team producing about $15m in annual sales (moving them from 4th of 4 regional teams to 2nd in just 5 months). Prior to being RVP, Scott was the Vice President for Strategic Initiatives at Edusoft, a leading provider of assessment solutions for low- to mid-stakes assessments in K-12 school districts.

Prior to his work in assessment, Scott was the Vice Present for Teaching and Learning Initiatives at Classroom Connect. In that role, Scott consulted with state and federal agencies, as well as national grant-making organizations, to assist in the development of large-scale professional development initiatives. Regarded as a leader in distance learning for teacher professional development, Scott developed Connected University, Classroom Connect’s online staff development product.

Scott Noon’s Specialties:

Online marketing strategies, automated lead generation systems, branding and identity, online marketing, formative assessment practices in large urban school districts, national sales, sales management, teacher professional development, online product development in education, business development, strategic alliances in the educational marketplace.
