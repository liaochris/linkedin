Background

Summary

My mission is to collaboratively hack the $7 trillion (annual) building industry using innovative technologies to bridge design, fabrication and community. Technology has enabled massive disruption and efficiency in many industries and it is increasingly impacting the construction industry and creating a golden era for those willing to embrace new and innovative building systems and technologies. My work combines my 20 years of experience as the owner of a residential building company and 10 years of experience as a technology entrepreneur.

I "build change" by creating and working with global networks of industry experts from robotics, fabrication, green building, gaming, software, manufacturing, building science, etc. Although I am a passionate about technology and innovation, ultimately I believe real innovation and change is the result of working with the right people and projects to accelerate change by demonstrating what is possible by building the right teams and projects.

Join us in our work to hack the building industry at our AEC Hackathons, the Digital Fabrication Network and by collaborating with us to build change.

Digital Fabrication Network
June 2011 – Present (3 years 5 months)International

dFab Net (Digital Fabrication Network) is a rapidly growing and innovative organization designed to create a strong community of design professionals, researchers, digital fabricators, and software/ hardware tool providers discussing and exploring Better Ways of Building through Digital Fabrication. We are raising the general understanding and acceptance of Digital Fabrication techniques, and providing people access to resources and knowledge.

dFab Net is a non-for-profit organization which aims to promote information technology, research and innovation in manufacturing for its members and their customers. Our objectives are to:

1. Identify and promote the benefits of Digital Fabrication
2. Address issues & obstacles encountered by the use of digital information and tools
3. Research, identify and address opportunities to improve the effectiveness and efficiency of digital manufacturing
4. Promote the general adoption of Digital Fabrication
5. Foster cooperation and understanding between designers, fabricators and software/hardware suppliers
6. Encourage innovation through cross-industry collaboration

The following key stakeholders are already officially represented: Autodesk, Cut My Timber, IDEAbuilder, Kreysler & Associates, Matsys, Robert McNeel & Associates, Zahner, California College of the Arts, University of Texas Schools, and Institute for Computational Design at the University of Stuttgart. We invite other interested and relevant parties to participate by becoming a member.
