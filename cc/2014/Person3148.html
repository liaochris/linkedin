  • Senior Design Project - Multispectral Camera

    • January 2013 to Present

    Modern cameras are consistently improving with more pixels being added to each new generation to improve their resolution. However, higher resolution imagery is not the only way to get superior and detailed image quality. The goal of designing and building a multi-spectral camera is to expand on the capabilities of the common camera by capturing selected visible spectrum ranges in order to obtain more information from the image.

    Multi-spectral cameras capture images through special optical filters, essentially band-pass filters, which allow only a certain range of wavelengths through while blocking the rest. By carefully selecting these optical filters, the small range of wavelengths that were allowed through can show certain optical characteristics within the target image. These optical characteristics can indicate what substances and even concentrations of substances that are present within the image . The multi-spectral camera will be operated by a microcontroller system that will position an individual band-pass interference-type filter in front of a monochrome camera. The system will allow an image to be captured and displayed to the operator on an external monitor with the ability to store the image on an removable storage device.
