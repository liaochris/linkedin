Steve Outing's Experience

Writer | futurist | researcher | author | consultant | advisor | speaker | educator

Media Disruptus

January 1994 – Present (20 years 10 months) Boulder, CO

Over the years, I've done a wide range of independent work within the digital/online news field:
• Research reports, book chapters, books (on online newspapers, e-mail publishing, online classifieds, etc.).
• Freelance writing and columns on a range of online-media topics. (The Guardian, Harvard's Nieman Reports, Writer's Digest, Ad Age, and many others in the U.S. and overseas.)
• Advisor/consultant to media companies on digital-media strategy.
• Speaker at numerous conferences and seminars: student groups, journalism associations, media executives, journalism academics, public lectures.
• Regularly interviewed by reporters from media organizations worldwide, book authors, and scholars on topics relating to many aspects of digital media, social media, and news innovation.
• Judge for online-news and journalism competitions (Online Journalism Awards, EPpies, SND.ies, National Society of Newspaper Columnists, etc.). Founder of EPpies.

Founder, program director, instructor

Digital News Test Kitchen

Educational Institution; 1-10 employees; Internet industry

November 2009 – May 2013 (3 years 7 months) Boulder CO

Digital-innovation specialist within CU-Boulder Journalism program:
• Recruited and supervised student and team research projects (news for smartphones; incivility in user comments; rhetoric of popular photographs; news gaming; community news/info curation; disruptive-media tools; etc.).
• Taught Digital-Media Trends and Innovative Digital Storytelling for News.
• Co-founded dLab, bringing digital- and social-media training to classrooms and workshop series.
• Invited and hosted a series of digital-news/media expert speakers for workshops.
• Assisted student-media editors and advisor on innovation.
• Frequent guest lecturer in classrooms on digital, future-of-news topics.
• Introduced social-media data to university as needed new area of study and research. (Will become part of planned new College of Communication, Media, and Information.)
• Brought innovative new tools to Journalism program for student and faculty use (Public Insight Network, DocumentCloud, Geofeedia, mobile reporting hardware, experimental/beta tools and services).
• Partnered with media and technology companies for university-private news coverage, research projects.
• Organized workshop on Funding for Non-profit News Organizations.
• Mentored individual students interested in digital future of journalism.
• Researched and blogged about new trends affecting journalism.

Columnist

Editor & Publisher

My regular column covered the intersection of the Internet and news media, with emphasis on newspapers but covering all sorts of interactive media, social media, journalism-innovation, and industry issues.

Senior editor

Poynter Institute

Nonprofit; 11-50 employees; Education Management industry

September 2001 – February 2006 (4 years 6 months)

I did mostly writing and research for Poynter on topics relating to the Internet and news media. Established, wrote, and edited E-Media Tidbits, a group blog of online-media experts from across the globe. Favorite Poynter project: Eyetrack III study of broadband-era news websites and multimedia editorial content; findings published, September 2004 at http://www.poynter.org/eyetrack/

Graphics editor, graphic artist, features copy editor

San Francisco Chronicle

Public Company; 501-1000 employees; Newspapers industry

May 1986 – December 1993 (7 years 8 months)

While at the Chronicle, I bounced around various editorial jobs -- editing for the features department, trying my hand at being an infographics artist, and managing the news graphics operation. Also was involved in the (very) early days of SFGate.com, when we were playing around with BBS's. (I left before it became a web site.)
