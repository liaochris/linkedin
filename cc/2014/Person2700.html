David Carson's Summary

A renowned Design and Creative Director, David Carson has a long history of working with some of the worlds largest agencies on a wide variety of projects. These to include Airlines, Automakers, Hi-Tech companies, fashion brands and start ups. He worked as the worldwide Creative Director for Bose corporation in Boston where he managed a creative team of over 100 people, executing campaigns for digital, tv and print.

Often asked to speak to a broad range of industries, (be it automakers, agencies to creative groups),
David shares his experiences and how he continues to push the envelop. David knows how to inspire and what it takes to build an effective team. He's humble, engaged and willing to listen.

In 2014, Apple computers selected him as one of their top 30 innovators in their 30 year history, calling David a "Pioneer with profound impact". Creativity magazine listed him as one of the worlds most influential creatives, while the AIGA/NYC, awarded David their highest honor in 2014, the Gold Medal for his creativity and design work. He's also been awarded "best designer" working with photography by the International Centre for Photography in NYC as well as countless awards from the ADC, TDC and SPD. David was also selected as a keynote speaker at the 2014 FUSE Branding Conference in Chicago and has been a guest speaker at TED. David was called "Art Director of the era" by Londons Creative Review magazine. Recently he spoke to sold out audiences at the Type Directors Club in NYC and other creative conferences in Berlin ,and Oslo.
Creative talks are coming up this fall in Helsinki,Spain +Portugal. David remains an extremely hands on designer +creative, and is the subject of a cover story for the intl. design mag. CASA, out late 2014.

Today, David's looking forward to the next phase of his career.
Ideally, David's looking to be part of a great creative team. His greatest desire is to lead, mentor and become the benchmark by which all creative teams are judged.

  • Ortlos. Architecture of the NetWORKS

    • Hatje Cantz Publishers
    • November 15, 2005
    Authors: David Carson, Ivan Redi, Andrea Redi

    Designer David Carson pronounced the "End of Print" in response to the virtual world's ever-increasing influence on design. And if Ortlos: Architecture of the Networks is any indication, it seems that the group will soon pronounce the "End of Architecture" (as we know it). Ortlos--which means "placeless" in German--is a virtual office (or platform) that deals with architectural topics, urban planning issues and interface design. Its members include a global network of architects, web designers, media theorists, net artists and IT specialists, all of whom are experts in their various fields and professions, and all of whom can collaborate on online projects that are either of interest to them, or specific to their locales. "Ortlos" also describes the virtual office's basic working method: not being dependent on a specific physical space to work, but located instead in networks and anywhere where an Internet connection is provided. The firm aims to generate advanced instruments for architectural and urban design, a sort of platform, a creative pool supported with information and databases: It is a virtual office that never sleeps. Designed and written by the firm's "partners," Architecture of the Networks documents Ortlos' projects from 2000 to 2003. Each of the 12 projects shown and explained here by Ortlos incorporates various approaches to the fields of architecture, urban planning, media theory, product design, installation art and interface design. This new and fresh way of addressing contemporary architecture also includes a research project about network collaborations, and Carson's interpretation of Ortlos' architectural work through the specific language of graphic design.
