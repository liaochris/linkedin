Dave Scriven-Young's Summary

Dave Scriven-Young is a senior litigation associate primarily in the Environmental Law, Construction, and Commercial Litigation Groups at Peckar & Abramson. His environmental expertise includes handling Superfund cases and wrongful death, personal injury, and property damage claims arising out of underground storage tank leaks and chemical releases. He represents clients before the Environmental Protection Agency in enforcement matters. He counsels clients on compliance and permitting issues under environmental laws and regulations. He helps clients take advantage of state and federal audit policies. He advises clients on mergers and acquisitions concerning environmental liability and permitting issues, and conducts due diligence and prepares representations and warranties for corporate transactions.

His construction expertise includes prosecution and defense of mechanic’s lien actions, payment and performance bond claims, breach-of-contract actions, breach-of-warranty claims, delay and inefficiency claims, contract terminations, and other matters. He also defends clients against actions involving personal injuries at job sites.

He regularly speaks and publishes on environmental and construction law issues. He is the author of the Illinois Environmental Law blog (http://illinoisenvironmentallaw.com), which provides news and updates on environmental cases, laws, and regulations.

He holds several leadership positions, including: Member of the Chicago Bar Association Board of Managers; Chair of the CBA Environmental Law Committee; officer of the American Bar Association Young Lawyers Division, serving as Assembly Speaker; Member of the ABA Standing Committee on Technology and Information Systems; and Member of the Board of Directors of the Public Interest Law Initiative.

He is accepting new clients and appreciate any referrals. To set up an initial consultation, please contact his office by phone at (312) 239-9722 or e-mail at dscriven-young@pecklaw.com.

At P&A, I litigate commercial litigation matters, including cases involving fraud, breach of contract, RICO, and employment cases brought by Equal Employment Opportunity Commission and Illinois Department of Human Rights. I help prepare clients’ key personnel for deposition and cross-examination at trial, manage discovery, including paper and electronic production and privilege logs, and supervise junior associates and paralegals during document production.

I also act as outside general counsel to corporate clients in the construction and manufacturing industries on construction, environmental, and employment issues. Concerning environmental matters, I handle Superfund cases and wrongful death, personal injury, and property damage claims arising out of underground storage tank leaks, spills of chlorinated solvents, and other chemical releases. I represent clients before the Environmental Protection Agency and state environmental agencies in enforcement matters. I counsel clients on compliance and/or permitting issues under the Clean Air Act, Clean Water Act, Emergency Planning and Community Right-to-Know Act, Resource Conservation and Recovery Act, and Toxic Substances Control Act. I also help clients take advantage of state and federal audit policies. I advise clients on mergers and acquisitions concerning environmental liability and permitting issues, and conduct due diligence and prepare representations and warranties for corporate transactions.

On construction issues, I regularly prosecute and defend mechanics lien actions, payment and performance bond claims, breach-of-contract actions, breach-of-warranty claims, delay and inefficiency claims, contract terminations, and other matters. I also defend clients against actions involving personal injuries at job sites.

Public Interest Law Initiative (PILI)

Nonprofit; 1-10 employees; Legal Services industry

2014 – Present (less than a year) Chicago Chicago Area

PILI’s mission is to cultivate a lifelong commitment to public interest law and pro bono service within the Illinois legal community to expand the availability of legal services for people, families and communities in need.

We envision a legal community with a deeply rooted culture of service, where law students, lawyers and legal professionals at all stages of their careers engage in public interest law or pro bono work, and remain committed to addressing the unmet legal needs of the poor and underrepresented.

PILI’s Law Student Internship and Graduate Fellowship Programs place law students and recent law school graduates at public interest law organizations, with PILI providing supplemental educational, networking and mentoring opportunities.

Our Alumni Network builds and maintains the connection between past PILI Interns and Fellows and the public interest law community through educational, networking, leadership, and service opportunities.

Through our Pro Bono Initiative Program, PILI enhances the scope and quantity of pro bono legal assistance in Illinois for those who lack access to justice by offering a range of pro bono programming and resources, by developing innovative projects and cultivating best practices, and by celebrating ongoing pro bono contributions.
