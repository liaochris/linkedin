NOAA National Weather Service (NWS) Broadcast Message Handler (BMH) Independent Cost Estimate

February 2014

Developed an independent cost estimate for the National Weather Service Broadcast Message Handler, a software program used to send and receive weather products across the country. Employed function point analysis based on client requirements and utilized PRICE Systems’ TruePlanning parametric cost model, which allowed for rapid estimate development. The analysis indicated the BMH development team lacked several aspects of a mature development organization. This lead to acquisition management training specifically dealing with the implementation of formal software development methodologies and tools, an Independent Verification and Validation framework (IV&V), and project management and oversight paradigm for Iterative and Agile development.

Provided acquisition management support and oversaw the IV&V process for the BMH program. After increased interaction, the BMH development team learned that NOAA did not have an IV&V process that included formal test procedures. Observations showed that during Phase 1 testing, troubleshooting and code fixes were performed when problems were uncovered, forcing a “code/fix as you test’ paradigm with no paper trail. In result, we provided the IV&V team training and support in building out the testing process and the development of test artifacts.

Developed a hybrid software development approach that included 1) incorporating agile planning tools, 2) a metric collection process for portfolio management, and 3) the garner of support from senior executives. The BMH project manager noted that defining and implementing a standard software development methodology across the program will have significant benefits worth pursuing – such as improved technical team processes, rapid detection of development challenges, and flexible workaround planning. Additionally, it provided the flexibility to effectively management BMH and future software development projects.
