Michael Blonsky's Summary

Michael is a veteran technologist who has 20+ years of operations, business development, software sales, software architecture, software development and consulting experience with firms like VMware, Grant Thornton and Accenture.

Currently, Michael is the CTO at the Family Independence Initiative. Prior to joining Fii, Michael was consulting with a number of companies (both startups and established businesses) to provide strategic and technical consulting (including designing and developing iOS apps using Objective-C, web apps written in Python for Google App Engine, and web apps / sites using standard LAMP frameworks and WordPress).

Michael is a veteran of several Silicon Valley software companies and startups. His experience includes directing the efforts around building out the Virtual Appliances initiative for VMware (NASDAQ: VMW). By developing a successful ISV outreach program, Michael helped define a new category of software solutions. Prior to the last 11 years in the San Francisco Bay Area, Michael spent 8 years in technology consulting with firms like Accenture and Grant Thornton.Michael holds a M.S. in Business and Technology from Johns Hopkins University.

Specialties: Objective C, Python, Django, Google App Engine, PHP, Flourish PHP Framework, Smarty Template Engine, LAMP, HTML 5, CSS3, jQuery, AJAX, C#, ASP.net, Visual Basic, Java, JBoss, Weblogic, MailChimp, SilverPop (and the list goes on and on).

Perkvine

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Internet industry

2010 – 2011 (1 year) San Francisco Bay Area

Perkvine is a rewards network that was established to give restaurants, caterers and other merchants access to new, high-margin consumers and reward those consumers for their patronage. Simplistically, you can think of Perkvine as a "frequent flyer program" for people who dine out or cater in frequently for work.

Perkvine was conceived to combat the negative experiences merchants were having with the existing crop of Review, Rewards, and Deal-of-the-Day websites. After months of research, we discovered that the highest-margin patrons were not being properly targeted, so we created Perkvine to fill this need. Merchants can now realize both an increase in revenue and, for once, an equal or larger increase in profit margins. In short, Merchants get to Pay-For-Performance by investing in a rewards program that brings high-margin patronage into their establishments at the right price and without crippling their cash flow. The real value of the Perkvine model is our focus on professionals who dine out or cater in as part of their business. These professionals are highly networked, share with their peers, and are highly competitive. The Perkvine Rewards Program targets all of these behaviors and puts them to work for Perkvine Merchants.

earthfaves has been in business since 2007, was conceived and bootstrapped by a group of ex-SAP talent that has been quietly building an incredible new platform that adds a useful layer to the social networks that are currently exploding around the globe.

earthfaves is a platform that allows businesses to effectively build a community of promoters. earthfaves plugs into existing social networks and allows people to recommend their favorite places (restaurants, hotels, taverns, parks, virtually anything) to their network of friends. earthfaves allows people to perform "social searches" and find things to do, places to go, and amazing adventures that their network of trusted friends have favored. This social search delivers banded results that show earthfaves of a person's friends, friends-of-friends, subject matter experts, and then general Google search results. Instead of getting millions of results that were ranked by a third-party, users of earthfaves get trusted search results that leverage the collective intelligence and similar tastes of the people in their social networks.
