Background

Summary

Mark Stout is a senior energy professional with over 15 years in solar power, utility regulatory, and high tech engineering positions.

Mr. Stout stays current on PV solar technology and maintains industry-wide procurement relationships. He applies years of experience with solar project capital cost estimating, from distributed generation to utility-scale. Mr. Stout is an expert on renewable resource data in US, and played a lead role in defining resource measurement standards for Meridian Energy USA's meteorological monitoring equipment.

During graduate work at the UC Berkeley Energy and Resources Group, Mr. Stout interned at the Union of Concerned Scientists, campaigning for the California Renewable Power Portfolio Standard, which was signed into law in 2002. He later worked as a Public Utilities Regulatory Analyst with the CPUC Office of Ratepayer Advocates.

Mr. Stout began his career in computer hardware, firmware, and software development. As an R&D Engineer at Compaq Computer, he played a leading role in the cost reduction and specification of a single-chip, high-fidelity audio codec, which attracted multiple manufacturers and has been widely deployed. As a Senior Systems Software Design Engineer at Crystal Semiconductor, he then went on to develop the device drivers to bring this audio codec to market.

Mr. Stout graduated Summa Cum Laude in Electrical Engineering from the University of Illinois - Urbana, and earned his Master's degree in Energy and Resources from UC Berkeley.
