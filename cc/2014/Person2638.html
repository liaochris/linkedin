Interactive Constultant & Developer

Comedy Central

Public Company; 201-500 employees; Entertainment industry

2006 – 2011 (5 years) New York

One of the most long running and successful projects I have managed and produced with my team at Scine is an online media sharing and approval application that has been essential to all production efforts within Comedy Central for the past five years. Internal departments interface directly with vendors and design studios to produce all marketing and digital collateral for the company with this site. Over the years we have been contracted to upgrade and add new functionality to the core system. The result is a fully branded user friendly experience where producers, project managers, designers, and animators can keep track of every project's history visually while approving and sharing a variety of digital media online.

Scine also built a Flash application that displays Twitter feeds in real time over live broadcast TV so the audience can tweet as they watch the show and see their comments displayed on the lower third of the screen. Tweets are fed into a content management system where traffic monitors can screen for content approval before being animated in Flash and composited over the live broadcast. The web meets TV in a unique and successful way.

Head of Interactive

Verb! Media

January 2002 – March 2006 (4 years 3 months) New York, NY

Verb! was one of the the most recognized boutique motion graphics studios in New York City and produced innovative award winning work for clients like HBO, Hewlett Packard, RSCG, TBWA\Chiat\Day. As they expanded more clients requested interactive projects outside of Verb!'s core abilities and they brought me on full time to head up their growing Interactive department.

I worked on a wide range of projects from interactive & animated data driven Flash web sites to building a custom After Effects rendering application in Apple's Xcode studio that allowed HBO to easily render customized components of the channel package Verb! designed without ever opening After Effects!

My biggest accomplishment at Verb! was creating the company's intranet / extranet: a browser based client and project management system which became instrumental in overhauling internal workflow. The internal and external web sites minimized day to day production tasks that formerly consumed time and resources, increased efficiency and created a central digital archive of their work.

I left Verb! in 2006 to start my company Scine. Our first job was designing and building an elegant interactive Flash web site that defined Verb's online presence. Connected to the intranet and extranet, the new site seamlessly provided sales with features giving them the ability to create secure and personalized online portfolios of selected Verb! projects for pitches, RFP's, and lead generation.

Since Verb! closed their doors I've keep the site I designed here for posterity: http://verb.scine.com/
