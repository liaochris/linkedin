Dick Lee Ph. D.'s Summary

I am a CEO, an author, inspirational speaker, recognized international expert in innovation and value innovation management. I have had P&L responsibility for divisions of Fortune 500 companies and led their R&D organizations. I have generated successful outcomes in the building materials, oil and gas, electronics, energy storage and medical products industries

I am the CEO & Chief Innovation Officer of Value Innovations, Inc. (Vi). We focus on Value Innovation Management, helping clients "Deliver exceptional value to the Most Important Customers in their Value Chain(s)"). We offer consulting services (>70 clients in Asia, NA and EU), workshops (>180 organizations: e.g., American Vanguard, Bekaert, Chevron, P&G, Singapore Telecom), webinars (>300 organizations) & software.

I have been a keynoter on value innovation management around the globe. I share experiences working in corporate America, my 28 years with the Industrial Research Institute, innovation management, and working with over 300 clients. Whether it's Kim and Mauborgne's Blue Ocean Strategy- Value Innovation, or Eric von Hippel's customer centric innovation or Clay Christensen's disruptive innovation, Henry Chesbrough's Open Innovation, I share my thoughts and observations. All large companies and small businesses must tailor their innovation management process to its culture to manage innovation successfully.

I was the VP and general manager of the Portable Battery Division (Gould Inc.) and Elgar (McGraw-Edison); the VP R&D for Pharmaseal (American Hospital Supply); and the VP strategic business operations at Johns Manville. I started my career as an inorganic chemist at UOP's R&D Center in Chicago, IL. I have a Ph. D. in inorganic chemistry from the University of London. I am a Fulbright scholar (UK to US) and today I'm a member of The Fulbright Academy.

  • Value Innovation: Passport to Wealth Creation

    • Research*Technology Management
    • March 2005
    Authors: Dick Lee Ph. D., Thomas A. Dillon, David Matheson

    OVERVIEW: Although most managers will agree that innovation can be the passport to wealth creation, many of their actual business practices and behaviors fail to support the activities crucial to value innovation. In particular, many companies over-emphasize technology innovation and R&D, which, when used in isolation, are insufficient to create new wealth. Through literature review and interviews with value innovators, a subcommittee of the Industrial Research Institute’s Research on-
    Research Committee has defined a value innovation process, providing a framework to develop a new product, service or business model that will have a strong, positive effect on enterprise value.

    The subcommittee also created a Value IQ instrument to help companies understand their ability to value-innovate and identify those areas where changes in behavior and company culture may be required.

    The Value IQ Instrument provided great insights into a company's culture but it took 45 minutes to complete. The Value Innovation Process Assessment Tool was developed to provide an instrument that could be completed on line in <10 minutes.

    KEY CONCEPTS: value creation, value innovation process, enterprise value, company culture, corporate culture, stakeholder behavior, capability and capacity to innovate.

  • Identify Your Innovation Enablers and Inhibitors

    • Research*Technology Management
    • November 2008
    Authors: Dick Lee Ph. D., Thomas J. Balsano, Nina F. Goodrich, John W. Miley, Terri F. Morse and David A. Roberts

    OVERVIEW: Air Products and Chemicals, Alcan Global Pharmaceutical Packaging, Boeing, Milliken & Co. and Solvay Advanced Polymers have used the Value Innovation Potential Assessment Tool (VIPAT) to identify their Value Innovation enablers and inhibitors. Value Innovation is defined as delivering exceptional value to the most important customer in the value chain.

    The information obtained from the VIPAT allows managers to understand the cultural drivers for innovation within their organization and guide the creation of a more effective Value Innovation culture.

    KEY CONCEPTS: innovation assessment, value innovation, value innovation tools, organizational culture.
