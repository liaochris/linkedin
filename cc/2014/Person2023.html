Ally Noormohamed's Summary

I have been crafting digital experiences since 2006. Today I work as the Lead UI/UX Designer at Ritchie Bros. Auctioneers. One of my many responsibilities is to design, evolve and maintain the usability of digital products from websites, website applications to internal tools for desktop, kiosk, television, tablet, and mobile devices.

My primary responsibility is to design for our responsive B2B/B2C ecommerce website: rbauction.com. I work with the research team to improve and iterate on designs using agile surveys and analytics. I combine this data with customer feedback, personas and guerrilla research tactics to validate solutions.

Storyboards, sketches, interactive wireframe prototypes and visual mockups are some of the methods I use to communicate design ideas and solutions. I work with the design team to craft the information architecture and interactions of every solution. I have created style guides and visual artifacts to establish design standards and align our branding across multiple digital properties.

My excellent interpersonal, communication and leadership skills help me to effectively collaborate with marketing strategists, business analysts, systems analysts, department leaders, front-end engineers and partner services to ensure consistency across all design phases and business strategy.

Freelancing has helped to further develop my creative abilities by building elegant user-centric experiences. I merge innovation with modern design methodologies and technologies to craft every client solution. Continuous learning through local meetup groups, web conferences and online sources helps me elevate every new project. Most of all continue to evolve as a senior design professional.

Ally Noormohamed's Projects

  • Ritchie Bros. Auctioneers

    • January 2007 to Present
    Team Members: Ally Noormohamed

    UI design lead for rbauction.com and Ritchie Bros. Auctioneers digital properties. Retrofitted for responsive design to provide a richer mobile experience for users. This meant we had to move away from our mobile site developed for predated usage patterns.

    The best approach would have been to start from the ground up but we had recently redesigned our site which involved substantial time, money and resources. It would have been challenging to convince our leadership team that we had to start again. We had to find an alternative because our contract with our mobile vendor was soon expiring. We decided to retrofit our desktop site to create an optimized experience across multiple devices.

    Retrofitting a desktop site is not the ideal solution but not only did it deliver an optimized experience for our users, we gained a competitive advantage in our online marketplace without breaking the bank.
