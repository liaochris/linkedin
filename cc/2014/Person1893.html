Mark Goldman's Summary

Extensive experience in building, growing and managing start-up firms and high-performing integrated marketing programs for many of the most respected and highly-valued consumer and B-to-B brands such as: American Express, Campbell Soup, Cadillac/GM, Citi Group, The Seagram Company, Lever Brothers, Marriott, Intercontinental Hotels (Holiday Inn and Crowne Plaza), Days Inns, Arby’s, Applebee’s, Miller Brewing Company, Procter & Gamble, BellSouth, The Coca-Cola Company, The Home Depot, Georgia Pacific, UPS, RJR/Nabisco, Sun Oil Company, Viking Range Corporation, Longines Watch Company and Trans World Airlines.
Co-Founded an Atlanta-based advertising and marketing firm (360thinc). Sold this business to Grey Global Group (WPP) in 2002.

Specialties: Brand strategy & development, marketing innovation, digital/social media strategy, integrated marketing strategy, operations management, new business development, corporate governance and leadership. Accomplished at creating and executing innovative marketing solutions, managing strategic alliances and identifying marketplace and brand innovation opportunities to grow market share – in short, a big picture, creative thinker skilled at driving brand growth.

Companies he has worked with include Wells, Rich, Greene; Ogilvy/NY; Whittle Communications; Babbit & Reiman.

Mark Goldman is Managing Partner of Goldman Consulting Group -- an Atlanta-based interim CMO consultancy for private equity groups and company owners to manage the marketing departments of start-up and mid-size companies. He was the President and CEO of Atlanta-based Housewarmers II, LLC - franchisor of a personal, direct-to-home delivery and greeting service. During his tenure, the company had 47 franchise territories - including 7 corporate-owned territories - operating in Texas, Oklahoma and North Carolina. More recently, he filled the CMO position for Viking Range corporation, the preeminent manufacturer of premium kitchen appliances. Currently, he serves as COO of Scout Marketing.

In 1994 Goldman co-founded an integrated marketing services firm called 360 (that earned the distinction of Inc Magazine's Top 200 fastest growing US companies in 1996). In 2002, he sold the 8 year-old agency to Grey Worldwide.
