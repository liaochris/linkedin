Background

Summary

Dr. Peter Millett (970-479-5871) currently oversees the Shoulder Division at the Steadman Clinic in Vail, CO. He is an orthopedic shoulder and sports medicine specialist and a leading expert in the field of orthopedics. Selected by his peers as one of the “Best Doctors in America,“ he strives for excellence in shoulder, elbow and knee reconstruction so that his patients can resume normal activities and maintain healthy and active lifestyles. Dr. Millett is highly involved with research, implementation and peer training to develop innovative surgical techniques for complex shoulder injuries. These procedures restore damaged ligaments, bones & joints.

He has treated athletes from the NFL, MLB, NBA, X-games, and the Olympics, and has also served as a consultant to the US Ski Team and the Montreal Canadians. He proudly served as an associate physician for the Denver Broncos professional football team and the Colorado Rockies Major League Baseball team. He often treats patients who suffer from traumatic injuries, as well as those who have long-time degenerative problems.

A native of Pennsylvania, he received his undergraduate degree from the University of Scranton and his medical degree from Dartmouth Medical School in Hanover, New Hampshire. He had the honor of studying as a research scholar at the University of Cambridge in England, where he was awarded a master’s degree in science (M.Sc.) for work in skeletal biology. Dr. Millett performed his orthopedic residency training at the Hospital for Special Surgery in NYC and sub-specialty, fellowship training in sports medicine, knee and shoulder surgery at the coveted Steadman Hawkins Sports Medicine Fellowship in Vail, Colorado.

Orthopedics is his life-long passion. He constantly strives to continue to advance the industry with dedicated orthopedic research, orthopedic surgical technique using the latest technologies, excellence in service and a passion to help patients recover to the best of his ability.

Shoulder Replacement, Shoulder Impingement Syndrome Surgery, Rotator Cuff Treatment using Double-Row Arthroscopic Surgery, Shoulder Stabilization Surgery, Sports Medicine, Tendon Transfers for Tendon Injuries, Arthroscopic Knee Surgery
