Background

Summary

Kelly Ehlers has built her reputation as a leader and expert in social media for the salon and beauty industry on three solid pillars. Having literally grown up in the industry – her mother was a successful salon owner for 40 years – Kelly took that passion, added in more than a decade of experience building brands for clients ranging from grassroots start-ups to multi-billion dollar businesses, and topped it off with a keen sense of innovation and intuition on digital connectivity and technology.

An award-winning, nationally recognized keynote speaker and social media educator, Ehlers leads a team at Evoke Brands that specializes in providing innovative digital solutions for beauty brands looking to make meaningful connections with their customers in today's 24/7 technology-driven world. Among dozens of other clients Evoke counts major Fortune 50 company Procter & Gamble as well as Elizabeth Arden Red Door Spas among it’s roster.

Previously named one of 10 up-and-coming Women to Watch by the Phoenix Business Journal, in 2011 she was honored as one of the publication’s Top 25 Women in Business. Also in 2011, Kelly formed a strategic partnership with Modern Salon Media as their resident expert blogger and is a regular contributor for The Levo League, a global forum for Gen Y career women.

Every brand has a story, an emotional, underlying reason as to why it exists. In today’s hyper-connected, socially driven world, how is your story being told? That’s where we come in.

At Ideas That Evoke™ we create, narrate and manage brand experiences in the social space. We develop content that connects people to brands and evokes an emotional response in an organic way…but to us, that’s just the beginning.

Our agile, highly responsive team works as an extension of your brand, engaging with and managing your social communities in real-time. We believe social media is a science, one that deserves a high attention to detail and constant, consistent nurturing. It can be fun, interactive and downright silly, but at the end of the day, we know social moves the needle.
