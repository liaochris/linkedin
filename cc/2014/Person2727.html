Background

Summary

David Steele is a Certified Management Consultant, Six Sigma Black Belt and a five year member of the Board of Examiners for the Malcolm Baldrige National Quality Award. He has also completed executive education for The Shingo Prize. David has over 30 years of leadership experience, 16 of which has been in senior corporate officer roles; two of these assignments included international experience.

David is also a successful entrepreneur. In venture roles, he has led two successful early stage ventures and one stage II venture with an international liquidity event involving a group of blue chip private equity investors in New York and London. His international experience includes Europe, Canada and India with teams ranging from small ventures of less than 100 to over 1200 under his leadership. His management consulting includes both Fortune 100 and 500 companies.

David is an award winning faculty member at Indiana University's Kelley School of Business with over 17 years of classroom success, and a decorated veteran of the US Army Intelligence Corps.

Board of Examiners

Malcolm Baldrige National Quality Award
March 2009 – Present (5 years 7 months)

The Baldrige National Quality Award, created by public law in 1987, is the highest level of national recognition for performance excellence that a U.S. organization can receive. Selection to the Board of Examiners is made from a national competitive process. This one-year appointment by the US Department of Commerce involves comprehensive training and certification as an examiner at the National Institute of Standards (NIST) in Washington, D.C. Examiners complete extensive independent and consensus review of award application and site visit; examiners also involve themselves as ambassadors for the Malcolm Baldrige National Quality Award.

I was appointed in 2009, and have been reappointed in 2010, 2011, 2012 and 2013 to serve as a member of the Board of Examiners by Dr. Patrick Gallagher, US Department of Commerce, National Institute of Standards (NIST).
