Halsey and Alice North's Summary

Halsey and Alice North jointly head The North Group Inc. which, since 1987, has been assisting nonprofit theaters, performing arts centers, arts councils, and arts service organizations across the country with fundraising feasibility studies, capital and endowment campaigns, annual operating campaigns, solicitor training, strategic planning, board development and retreats, cultural planning, organizational assessments, business plans, and workshops on fundraising, board development, and planning. They wrote the chapter on fundraising for Fundamentals of Arts Management in cooperation with the Arts Extension Service, University of Massachusetts. Both are members of the Juilliard School Drama Council and the Board of Directors of the League of Historic American Theatres.

Specialties

Fundraising, Planning, Community Planning and Workshops in the Performing Arts Industry

Halsey and Alice North's Projects

  • ArtsQuest at SteelStacks

    • August 2007 to Present
    Team Members: Halsey and Alice North, Darlene (Dee) Smolik, Jeffrey Parks, Ron Unger, Doug Banghart, Joel Cohn, CPA, Walt Keiper, Bob Oster

    State of the Art Performing Arts Center, by ArtsQuest in Bethlehem, PA, on the largest brownfield in America, as part of this nonprofit’s mission to bring Arts to the region. This “State of the Art” facility has allowed for significant expansion of the Musikfest 10 day community festival, Christkindlmarkt, and First Night events held throughout Bethlehem under the management of ArtsQuest, as well as now allowing ArtsQuest to bring in artists year round for new festivals and indoor/outdoor events and education opportunities with local schools and colleges. Independent Cinema, tourist destination activities, and a vast array of opportunities to bring people together in the "Town Square" of the region now exists. This was accomplished with NMTC participation by US Bank, CitiFirst, and Commonwealth Cornerstone Group as well assponsor financing with Lafayette Ambassador and TD Banks. Consideration is now being given to additional adaptive redevelopment of the Turn and Grind Shop Facility on the SteelStacks campus to accommodate more indoor community and artist events "off season."

  • Multiple Historic Theatre Rehabilitations

    • June 2005 to Present
    Team Members: Halsey and Alice North, Darlene (Dee) Smolik

    Several Historic Theatre rehabilitations using both “20%” and “10%” HTCs and NMTCs, as applicable. Clients have included/currently include: Clemens Center in Elmira, New York; Proctors Theater in Schenectady, New York; Fox Theater in Spokane, Washington; Adler Theater in Davenport, Iowa; Stanley Theatre in Utica, New York; Hudson Opera House in Hudson, New York; Colony Theatre in Marietta, Ohio; Capitol Theatre in Rome, New York; Goodwill Theatre in Johnson City, New York and several other independent one day consults and training sessions with Historic Theatres throughout the United States
