Rory Bowman's Experience

Freelance Sophist

Self-Unemployed

January 2014 – Present (10 months)

Sophists were the consultants of classical Greece, providing subject-matter expertise and general counsel across a variety of topics to best meet the needs of their clients. After shutting down Thrasymachus Corporation in August, I am currently working as a sole proprietor with a few of my former consulting clients and taking on other temporary and short-term projects as I work on a sabbatical of study and service for 2014.

Executive Director

ManKind Project Northwest

Nonprofit; 201-500 employees; Professional Training & Coaching industry

August 2012 – December 2013 (1 year 5 months) Portland, Oregon Area

Executive director for a fifteen-year-old nonprofit with primary responsibility for six-figure annual budget and all operations during major restructuring. Co-created and streamlined a centralized communication plan with regular status meetings. Used "lean" principles to narrow focus on fundamental tasks and "open book" techniques to simplify and convey budget process more cleanly. Helped reduce personnel expenses by nearly half, mobilizing dozens of volunteer staff, coordinated across two states. Was part of a dynamic outreach team, whose volunteers tripled the number of outreach events to completely fill almost all of our flagship trainings while shepherding the organization toward final merger into a larger, national structure.

Macintosh Consultant

MacRory.Com

August 1998 – August 2013 (15 years 1 month)

Founded own business as a contract technical writer in 1998, just as release of the iMac and OS X created opportunities for someone with Macintosh and Unix experience. Changed company name from RoryBowman.com to MacRory.com, then incorporated in 2003 as Thrasymachus Corporation to bring on employees and function as a small-business incubator. Specializing in integration of systems for non-profits, individuals, small and micro-businesses, my primary job was to serve as IT department and small business advisor to people using Mac OS computers. I helped make sure that hardware, software, people and business processes all worked together to increase pleasure, decrease frustration and improve productivity.

Unhappy with changes in the direction of the industry, I gave one year's notice and chose to shut down my corporation fifteen years to the day after my first business license, having supported thousands of users at hundreds of locations. In all that time I never lied to or hurt anyone for money, and never turned anyone away for inability to pay. It was an honor to have served and had the trust of so many kind and interesting people.

Contract Project Manager

Go.com

October 1999 – February 2000 (5 months)

Contract technical writer and project manager, covering key accounts for woman on maternity leave during transition from InfoSeek to Go.Com after purchase by Disney corporation, primarily in Content Services.

Teacher

Special emphasis on Visual Math program and Junior Great Books, with an interest in hands-on science, cross-disciplinary social studies and integration of the writing process across the curriculum.
