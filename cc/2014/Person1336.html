Background

Summary

Professional freelance writer, content creator and motivational speaker with over 4 years of writing and blogging experience. Specialized in pet blogging, most specifically speaking about the ways you can take care of your dogs through an all-natural lifestyle. Owner of the popular blog Keep the Tail Wagging, and regular writer at Pet360 and Pets Teach Us So Much on TPPC.tv.

As a dog mommy, tech-savvy and wordsmith, I have established wonderful connections with fellow pet lovers all over the world, and I have been able to use my social media and content marketing skills for positive causes. For example, I wrote an e-book on social media tips for animal rescue groups, so these organizations can share their achievements and inspire others in an inexpensive and engaging way. I have spoken at several blogging events all over the US, including Bloggy Boot Camp Vegas 2012, BlogPaws 2013 and 2014, Edmonds Pet Business Group 2013 and WordCamp Seattle 2014.

Keep the Tail Wagging has been featured on Coupon Audit's Top 100 Pet Blogs to Follow in 2013, SheKnows.com's 5 Pet Bloggers You Need to Know About!, nominated for a 2013 and 2014 Petties Blog Aware, and nominated by Toot Sweet for Two for a Liebster Blog Award.

Besides my regular outlets, I have written guest posts for worldwide-renowned websites such as ProBlogger, Girl Power Hour and The SITS girls.

Away from my dogs and my computer, I work as a Senior GL Accountant at Era Living. I have a Bachelor's Degree in Business Administration from Seattle University, and I have been involved in professional corporate accounting for the last 15 years. Nowadays, I manage 4 assisted living communities, as well as the corporate office. Just like in blogging, I enjoy sharing complex information in a simple, enjoyable and understandable way.

Liebster Blog Award

Nominated by Toot Sweet for Two
January 2013

This is an award given by bloggers to up and coming bloggers, most of whom have less than 200 followers or have been blogging for less than 6 months. It is to show newer bloggers that they are appreciated, and to help spread the word about new blogs. It was created to promote appreciation and recognition among the blog world. Liebster translates to “dearest” (or favorite/best) in German. It is also known as the Love Blog Award.

Note to Kimberly Gauthier: My niece, Tiffany, and I met you at Bloggy Boot Camp LV. We thoroughly enjoyed your talk and learned so much from you. Thanks for your inspiration and positive attitude.
