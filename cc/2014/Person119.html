Background

Summary

Specialized in advanced image processing and image segmentation in the medical domain, I've been developing in the medical imaging domain since 2002.

I have extensive experience programming mainly in C++ under Windows and Linux as well as in project management and defining software architecture.

The following are some of the modules I've recently created:
- semi-automatic carotid vessel segmentation
- automatic aortic vessel segmentation
- automatic coronary vessel segmentation
- automatic colon segmentation with centerline and cleansing
- colon unfolding
- automatic bone masking
- an image processing SDK

Subjects:
- 3D tissue segmentation algorithms
- haptic interfaces for 3D volumes
- memory and speed optimization
- CT, MRI, DICOM data
- efficient architecture and documentation
- skeletonization and 3D centerlines, level-sets, morphological operators

Patents

Adaptative hit-or-miss region growing for vessel segmentation in medical imaging(Link)

Europe EP2357609
Issued June 27, 2012

The general field of the invention relates to, without being limited to, medical imaging. It finds applications for the segmentation and identification of complex structures such as vessels structures in volumetric images (3D images) generated by any suitable imaging technique such as computed tomography, resonance magnetic imaging or the like.

Inventors:
  • Christoph Spuhler,
  • Frederic Banegas

Artifact-free filet view for virtual endoscopy of lumen structures

Europe EP 2339538 A1
Filed December 23, 2009

The present invention concerns a method for performing virtual endoscopy on a volumetric image of a lumen-defining structure having an elongated lumen, comprising steps of determining an unfolding axis substantially inside the lumen, defining sampling planes substantially perpendicular to and intersecting said unfolding axis at sampling positions, selecting ray casting directions in said sampling planes, performing ray casting to define pixel values, and arranging said pixel values into an unfolded view, wherein said ray casting directions are selected so that the angle increment between them is substantially inversely proportional to the ray propagation distance into the lumen, from the unfolding axis to the structure.

Inventors:
  • Christoph Spuhler,
  • Marc Moulis,
  • Frederic Banegas,
  • Charles Caderas de Kerleau
