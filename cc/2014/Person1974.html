StatusPimp

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Internet industry

September 2009 – July 2011 (1 year 11 months) Timis County, Romania

Status Pimp is the first IM status messages advertising network. It works on revenue shared model where the users (called pimpers) would get up to 0.07€ for each click from their friends. It launched in May 2009 using only Twitter and in less than 24 hours the first 1,000 beta accounts were already filled. It quickly gained traction with advertisers also, attracting brands like Coca-Cola, Wrigley's, Raiffeisen, BRD Societe Generale, PokerStars and Vichy.

I was the first employee and my day to day responsibilities included communication, account management and customer care.
Branding and communication were our first priorities as users would first associate the company with the usual "make money online" scams. We separated ourselves from this by creating live action videos explaining the registration process, writing creative copy that would exactly target the demographic or even setting up a flash mob in front of Bucharest's biggest mall with a professional team of street dancers.

As things moved on and using the insights I gained by interacting with both users and advertisers I focused on establishing the marketing and product strategy and prepare for the international launch.

Alen-Jelco Todorov's Projects

  • UTM.to

    • September 2010 to Present
    Team Members: Alen-Jelco Todorov, Stefan Szakal

    UTM.to is an url shortener targeted towards marketing professionals who use Google Analytics UTM's codes to track referral attribution.

    As more and more websites and services start defaulting to https (https does not send referral to a non https website) and the web is accessed through a myriad of devices and desktop clients, referral attribution can't rely solely on headers sent by the browsers. This is where UTM.to comes along and ensures that data driven decisions are based on reliable referral sources.

  • inspiră-mă.te-rog.eu

    • December 2009 to Present
    Team Members: Alen-Jelco Todorov, Ionut Botizan

    Translated from Romanian, it means "inspire me, please" and the idea was to help writers rewrite their texts by translating the input into Japanese and then back to Romanian. By doing this back and forth about 30 times, the text would've lost the original meaning but instead gain a whole new interpretation, yielding unexpected results.

    What started as an idea at 9 PM went online the next morning, and after a single tweet, it quickly became viral creating almost 10,000 "inspirations". It even got featured on a national radio station.
