Ed Terpening's Summary

Proven thought leader with a long history of success and firsts at companies as diverse as Ford, Apple, CNET, Wells Fargo and Altimeter Group. Combines strategic thinking with strong business results.

VP Social Media Strategy

Wells Fargo

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; WFC; Financial Services industry

March 2006 – October 2012 (6 years 8 months)

Responsible for social media strategy, development/operations, policy and organizational strategy. Developed and executed channel strategies for social platforms, including blogs, Facebook, Wikipedia, YouTube, MySpace and LinkedIN.

Launched the first blog of any major US bank ("Best of the Web" award, 2006) and built their first dedicated social media team from the ground up—the first of any major financial institution.

Wrote first social media policies governing team member participation and business use. Designed and taught first social media workshops. Lead partnerships across the company in the launch of more than 25 sites/programs. Success measured in increased brand reputation, sales, and cost-savings.

Led the first company-wide Social Media Vision/Strategy team. Developed social media strategic framework and process to safely scale social media across the company, with a focus on channel alignment, integration and risk management. Served on the Corporate Communications Steering Committee, and as a trusted partner, provided leadership for successfully addressing PR fires during the Financial Crisis.

Wells Fargo is consistently named as the leader in social media among US banks, including “Highest-regarded Bank in Social Media” (2010 Serendio Research).

Founding member of SocialMedia.org. Represent Wells Fargo with the press and public speaking, including Forrester Finance Forum, BlogWell, Community 2.0, and Finance Tech Innovation (keynote, Sydney). Quoted in press as diverse as American Banker and BrandWeek, to USA Today. Named one of 6 “Social Media Revolutionaries” by leading social media author Charlene Li. Interviewed and quoted in social media books, including Groundswell, Open Leadership and WikiBrands.

Frequent community partner, including work for The Veteran’s Health Research Network, The National Hispanic Council and the Red Cross.
