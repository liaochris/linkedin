Seth Faison's Summary

Head of Communications at the Global Fund to Fight AIDS, TB and Malaria, an international financing organization devoted to treating and preventing these three highly infectious diseases.

Supervise and manage a department of professionals in media relations, on-line operations, special projects and events, marketing and branding, audio-visual, translations and publications. Guide the Global Fund's overall communications operations and strategy. Manage day-to-day communications issues. Advise senior staff on communications matters. Serve on the Global Fund's Management Executive Committee.

In the past, served as a strategic communications director, leader and manager at Sitrick and Company, a top-ranked communications consultant. Skilled writer and editor. Experienced adviser on crisis communications.

Trained as a journalist. Shanghai Bureau Chief for the New York Times. Lived and worked in China for 12 years, first as a student of the Chinese language and then as a journalist in Hong Kong and Beijing. Covered the 1989 student uprising in Tiananmen Square. Also worked as a journalist in New York, covering Wall Street, Manhattan Criminal Court, the subways, and Asian Organized Crime.
