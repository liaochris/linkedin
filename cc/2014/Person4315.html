Michael Benami Doyle's Summary

Wide-ranging strategic planning, project management, stakeholder communications, and development experience in the non-profit and public sectors. Skilled at brand ambassadorship, strategic messaging, event management, fund development and donor management, full-spectrum media relations, and production and maintenance of organizational websites and newsletters. Sensitive to issues of social justice, faith, and interfaith relations.

Michael Benami Doyle's Experience

Manager of Economic Development Initiatives

Alliance for Regional Development | Chicago Central Area Committee (CCAC)

January 2014 – Present (9 months) Chicago, IL

A dual role for sister organizations with common staff.

For the Alliance, focus and guide the cross-jurisdictional collaborative activities of four working teams comprised of more than 100 high-level governmental, commercial, and institutional leaders committed to improving the Chicago region’s tri-state economy in the areas of workforce development, innovation, transportation and logistics, and green growth, as part of a regional planning pilot project funded by the U.S. Department of Commerce Economic Development Administration and regional sponsors in response to the recommendations of an OECD Territorial Review of the Chicago metropolitan area. Assisted in securing more than $100,000 in delayed matching funds in first eight months, and responsible for implementing 2014 sponsorship campaign. Shared responsibility for planning of December 2014 Second Annual Summit on Regional Competitiveness with partner Federal Reserve Bank of Chicago.

For the CCAC, provide strategy and program support for Chicago’s historic growth coalition comprised of more than 100 civic leaders from the domains of architecture, planning, development, government, cultural affairs, and academia. Assisted in securing more than $50,000 in delayed membership payments in first eight months. Responsible for planning and managing monthly member luncheons with high-level guest speakers. Co-organizer of highly successful 2014 Central Area Transportation Workshop which moved the Chicago Loop Connector Project forward for the first time in 20 years.

For both organizations, perform membership and donor development and management activities, write major grant applications for public and private funders, plan and manage major monthly and annual events, produce press materials, electronic newsletters, and organizational websites, track and report on budgets and billing, develop and administer organizational databases and cloud-server systems, and provide administrative support.
