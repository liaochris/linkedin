Dan Pope's Publications

  • Utah's Weather and Climate

    • Publishers Press, Salt Lake City, Utah
    • August 1, 1996
    Authors: Dan Pope, Clayton Brough

    Utah's geology and geography have varied significantly in the past 200 million years, so looking at these many changes and covering this history in some depth, the book looks at why changes in the weather are perfectly normal. Droughts, floods, extreme snow events, heat and cold are what we have seen and will see going forward. Safety for lightning, flash floods, avalanches and other life threatening events are covered in some depth; in hopes of saving lives. Historical color (some black and white) pictures of significant weather events in Utah are in the middle of the book, while a daily almanac of historical events are categorized just before the pictures. The last sections cover 50 cities historical weather, averages for temperature, precipitation and snowfall. The final section here has the most detailed weather history for St. George and Salt Lake City that was available at the time of printing.
