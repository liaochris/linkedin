Background

Summary

In 2013, I left the wonderful world of being a SALT consultant in public accounting to begin my solo career. I immediately began utilizing my almost 20 years of state tax experience and network of contacts to determine what that solo career would look like. My favorite part of working in the multistate taxation field has always been conducting research to answer complex questions and helping clients develop positions and support when the answers aren’t quite clear. Writing audit protests and memos, outlining and defending a client’s position is the type of work I find enjoyable – I like to call it – “research with a purpose.”

I also enjoy following the ever-changing world of multistate taxation on a daily basis and writing blog posts, articles, alerts, etc. attempting to explain complex matters in an easy-to-understand format while identifying the issues and potential action steps clients should take.

You might say that the initial steps of starting my solo career began back in 2009 when I started my state and local tax blog, “LEVERAGE SALT.” It was my creative outlet, my way to voice my thoughts, concerns, and ideas regarding multistate taxation. It was also my way of creating connections with CFOs, Controllers, and other tax professionals all across the country. (I am one of the first (if not the first) state and local tax blogs, now with over 700 posts on a variety of state tax topics).

I provide Fortune 500 and middle-market companies across the U.S. with a multistate tax resource not bound by independence restrictions or firm association, and without the overhead and large fees. I also work with CPA and law firms as an independent / subcontractor to provide them with an additional resource when needed.

I am “your state and local tax partner.”
