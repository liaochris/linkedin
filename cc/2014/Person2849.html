Dominic Carone, PhD, ABPP-CN's Summary

Goal is to expand my professional lecturing services to a paid-for-service business focuing on topics related to neuropsychology, particularly symptom validity assessment, effort testing, mild traumatic brain injury, and what some refer to as "post concussion syndrome."

Another goal is to develop a private legal consulting business offering professional opinions on neuropsychology, such as case vaiability and review of records to assist counsel.

For a summary of professional experience, see http://medfriendly.com/history.html

Specialties

Professional lecturing and speaking, legal consulting, neuropsychologcal assessment, neuropsychology

Dominic Carone, PhD, ABPP-CN's Experience

Neuropsychologist and Coordinator of Neuropsychology Assessment Program

SUNY Upstate Medical University

Educational Institution; 5001-10,000 employees; Hospital & Health Care industry

September 2005 – Present (9 years 1 month)

Provides outpatient neuropsychological services to adults and children, beginning at age 5. Diagnoses include traumatic brain injury, dementia, developmental disorders, multiple sclerosis, stroke, & psychiatric illness. Also responsible for training and supervising psychology interns in clinical neuropsychology as well as supervising and training undergraduate and graduate research assistants. Lecturing responsibilities includes hospital in-services, presentations to local colleges (i.e., Syracuse University, Le Moyne College) on clinical neuropsychology, coordinating a monthly journal club on mild traumatic brain injury, providing monthly lectures about mild traumatic brain injury, and coordinating a monthly neuropsychology case conference series on diverse neurological diagnoses in adults and children. Other duties include coordinating the clinical and business aspects of the neuropsychology service, developing and maintaining a statistical database, and initiating research studies.

Founder and Webmaster

MedFriendly

October 2001 – Present (13 years)

Created and currently maintains a medical website that explains medical terminology in an easy to understand, jargon-free manner. Many of the 2500+ medical terms are related to the neurosciences. MedFriendly is listed as a resource by the Center for Disease Control and Prevention, was used to define medical terms in a U.S. court case, and has been featured in metropolitan newspapers and on a local NBC news affiliate.
