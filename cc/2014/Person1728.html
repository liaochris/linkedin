Dr. Simon R. R. Atkins, MBA, PhD, DSc (AM)'s Summary

*** FIRM SUMMARY ***

CEO, Advanced Forecasting Corporation.

D.Sc. A.M. in Planetary Electromagnetics: IBAM, Kolkata, India
Ph.D. in Biometeoelectromagnetics; Energy Medicine: CCNH, USA
iMBA in Macro-Economics: Helsinki School of Economics, Finland
M.Sc. in Oceanography (coursework only): University of East Anglia, UK
B.Sc. in Atmospheric Sciences: Cornell University, USA

Advanced Forecasting Corporation [AFC] specializes in Meteorological Intelligence, Planetary Risk Management, and VARM (Vulnerability Assessment and Risk Modeling) in connection with business continuity & contingency planning. AFC provides advanced quantitative details in weather risk prognostications, threat-based impact prediction, & any forecast of both nature-based and human-caused disasters from hourly (such as electricity demand based on atmospheric parameterizations) to long-term of 10-50 years ahead (such as for investment and planning purposes). AFC serves any industry that is negatively affected or impacted by climate change, weather volatility and planetary disasters. AFC provides a wealth of strategy & risk management services and products, all customized to meet the exact needs of the client at the time. AFC clients receive an edge in managing risk and optimize their ROI and minimize asset loss from damage-related events in their investment portfolios. AFC conveys industry-specific strategic expertise in global risk scenarios.

*** PERSONAL EXPERTISE ***

Simon has extensive knowledge in applications in planetary risk and climate change asset risk management and global vulnerability analysis for many different industries. Simon is a strategic consultant with exceptional interpersonal & tri-lingual communication skills. Simon's experience provides a strength in his ability to conceive, translate, market, debate & teach corporate & strategic management ideas in risk management, and then promote & adapt them into practical, business solutions.

Specialties

Simon is an analytical, energetic & entrepreneurial spirit as well as an ‘international citizen’ with vision, creativity, drive & innovation, with a commitment to ‘real-world’ interactive thinking and empirical research.

Dr. Simon R. R. Atkins, MBA, PhD, DSc (AM)'s Publications

  • When Weather Risk Becomes Inevitable Or Obligatory

    • The 2011 Euromoney Risk Management Handbook
    • January 15, 2011
    Authors: Dr. Simon R. R. Atkins, MBA, PhD, DSc (AM)

    Let’s face it: Mother Nature is becoming a big(ger) player
    nowadays, and as an atmospheric scientist focused on
    business continuity and vulnerability assessments, I have
    never been busier attending to different industrial sectors’,
    companies’ and individuals’ unique investment needs that
    are being more regularly impacted by the rather telling
    increase in extreme natural events around the world.
    Obviously, weather has most likely played a rather
    significant role in risk management in decades past, at least
    on a cyclical basis. But today, the revenue and profits of
    about 83% of all companies are directly affected by weather
    hazards, and not only is that number going up and becoming
    more inclusive, but the severity of losses are swelling too.

  • Solar Flares And Potential Impacts On Global Markets

    • The 2012 Euromoney Risk Management Handbook
    • January 15, 2012
    Authors: Dr. Simon R. R. Atkins, MBA, PhD, DSc (AM)

    2012 and 2013 are likely to be volatile years for potentially many reasons,
    but not withstanding, this will be a solar cycle max. As electrical and
    magnetic energy from the sun hits Earth, have you ever thought about
    what could happen during a larger solar flare, or even how it might
    affect your market portfolio? Sounds far-fetched, but think again.
