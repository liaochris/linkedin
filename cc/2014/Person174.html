Background

Summary

Davin Hutchins is an award-winning journalist, digital strategist, strategic communicator specializing in the Middle East, nonprofit communications, international social issues, social media engagement, digital products and agile project management. He has a solid track record in defining digital strategy, product launches and overseeing restructuring of legacy organizations. He is also passionate about the environment and climate change issues.

Currently, Hutchins is Director, Digital at MBN which includes Alhurra and Radio Sawa. In the past two years, Hutchins supervised a new digital division of more than 30 employees including restructuring and talent acquisition. He served as team lead on a news app, music app, website redesigns as well as the overall content strategy. He increased website and app visits by more than 400% and helped grow Facebook communities from 900,000 to 10,000,000 fans. He regularly speaks in D.C. on U.S. communications strategy in the Arab world to the BBG Board, State Department, National Counterterrorism Center and U.S. Senate and House of Representatives.

Before this, Hutchins was Managing Editor of Middle East Voices at Voice of America / BBG where he conceived of an Arab Spring social journalism site covering grass-roots democratization in the Middle East. The site won the Topical Reporting category in the 2012 Online Journalism Awards (ONA) beating CNN and the New York Times.

In 2009-10, he was Director of IndiesLab at PBS and ITVS helping documentary filmmakers earn revenue and audience from iTunes, Amazon, Netflix and Hulu. Hutchins has worked for and with CNN, CNN International, Headline News, TechTV, Internews and Huffington Post. His projects have taken him across the Middle East and Africa. Among career highlights, Hutchins directed The Art Of Flight a feature documentary which debuted at AFI Fest, International Documentary Festival Amsterdam (IDFA) and Bangkok International Film Festival.
