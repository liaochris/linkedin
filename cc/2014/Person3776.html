Katrina Stevens' Summary

Ms. Stevens has extensive experience designing curricula, conducting professional development, designing, reviewing and integrating education technology, writing and managing grants, project and program management, and developing teachers and leaders in both public and independent school settings, in addition to over twenty years teaching experience. Recent clients include Baltimore County Public Schools, MSDE, Maryland Public Television, Health Teacher, MICA, Johns Hopkins University, Conceptua Math, Sylvan Learning, LearnIt Systems, and Calvert Education Services.

She also writes regularly for Edsurge, SmartBlog for Education, and for her own blog, Education Matters, and she co-founded and co-moderates #edtechchat. Katrina serves as a Startup Weekend EDU facilitator as well.

In her position as the English Language Arts Supervisor for STEM in Baltimore County, Ms. Stevens coordinated multiple curricular, professional development and implementation programs to prepare teachers and the 108,000 students in the district for the Common Core State Standards and Next Generation Science Standards. She also served on the MSDE committees to design the new Maryland curriculum framework and the Maryland STEM Standards of Practice. Prior to Baltimore County, Ms. Stevens served as Deputy Director for Johns Hopkins University Center for Talented Youth in Bermuda where she designed and implemented an island-wide acceleration and enrichment program and laboratory school for academically talented students. Previous to CTY Bermuda, Ms. Stevens worked in independent schools as a department chair, dorm dean and teacher.
