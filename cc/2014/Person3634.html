  • Workgroup AOCG Security Threat Model

    • January 2014 to June 2014
    Team Members: Joram Teusink, Henk Van Gestel, Paul Oor, Jaap Pinkster, Peter Retera, Ytzen de Vries

    AOCG stands for Atos Outsourcing Contact Group and is a representative organization for customers of Atos that have outsourced (parts of) their IT-department. AOCG requested to start a workgroup to address Information Security and its relevance to the customers of Atos and Atos itself.

    The starting point of this workgroup was the report about the developments of Information Security according to the National Cyber Security Center of the Dutch government. From there the team worked to make a connection between the developments and actors mentioned in the report and the key threats that are relevant for the participating AOCG members.

    From all this the "AOCG Security Threat Model" emerged. This model gives a global overview and relation between the business cases of the (potential) attacks, the actual threats including impact and risks and the mitigating actions that can be taken. This model can be used to explain needed Security improvements with managers and decision makers. It is a tool to help the Security specialist to better explain the world of Information Security.

    My role in this workgroup was to take the lead in the modeling of all the information we found important into one view (the actual model). The entire workgroup worked together to make sure all key information was represented in the model. The end result of this model was presented and explained by me to all AOCG members.
