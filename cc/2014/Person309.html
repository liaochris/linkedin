JD MEDIA, LLC
September 2007 – Present (7 years 3 months)

JD MEDIA, LLC, is a media and public relations consulting business in Washington, DC. This boutique firm is founded by Joann Donnellan, a media relations specialist with 20 years experience in television, non-profit organizations and public affairs. JD MEDIA specializes in media relations strategy and outreach, cause marketing, multi-media advocacy campaigns and special events.

Client work includes: Department of Energy - National Commission on the BP Deepwater Horizon Oil Spill and Offshore Drilling, Ad Council, Bipartisan Policy Center, University of Miami, National Center for Missing & Exploited Children, National Rehabilitation Hospital, Brookings Institution, UNAIDS, Enough Project, Malaria No More, National Academies of Practice, the International Early Lung Cancer Action Program and Home Front Communications.

President's Commission on Care for America's Returning Wounded Warriors
April 2007 – August 2007 (5 months)

Created on March 6, 2007 by President George Bush, the nine-member Commission, co-chaired by former U.S. Senator Robert J. Dole and former U.S. Secretary of Health and Human Services Donna E. Shalala, was tasked with completing a comprehensive review of the military’s healthcare system by the end of July. After eight public meetings, 23 site visits to military and VA centers across the nation, 50 briefings by medical experts and agency officials and the completion of a first-of-its-kind survey of injured service members, the commission approved six recommendations that make sweeping changes in the delivery of military health care and services.

The Commission also created a “YouTube” video of its recommendations which features Commissioners Jose Ramos, Marc Giammatteo, and Tammy Edwards. Ramos and Giammatteo were both wounded in Iraq, as was Tammy’s husband, Chris Edwards. The video can be viewed at www.youtube.com.

Lipstick & Liquor: Secrets in the Suburbs(Link)

February 2010 – Present

Independent documentary. A Cold Winter's Day. A Mystery Unfolds. Lipstick & Liquor explores the secret in the suburbs and confronts the stigma surrounding women who drink. A growing number of women are abusing alcohol. DUI arrests of women are up 30% from a decade ago, while arrests for men are down. Binge drinking among women is also on the rise. Lipstick & Liquor includes expert commentary from researchers, addiction specialists and authors who shed light on the conditions impacting the increase in alcohol use and abuse among American women.
