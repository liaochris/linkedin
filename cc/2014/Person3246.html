Heather Rafter's Summary

For most of my legal career, I've had the pleasure of representing a wide variety of music technology companies, including the top audio and digital media companies. Representative clients (past and present) include: Digidesign, M-Audio, Sibelius, Universal Audio, Euphonix,Flite, PreSonus, Creative Allies, BrightTALK, Imagine Research, Sixense, Spin Media, the Slate companies and others.

My career began as a litigator at a large multi-national law firm, where I worked on cutting-edge cases involving software and intellectual property law. I then served as the General Counsel of Digidesign for fourteen years, where I started as Digidesign's first lawyer and helped grow not only a legal department but also a family of technology companies, working closely with the business development team and third-party partners.

As of January, 2009, I launched a law firm, RafterMarsh US, which keeps me connected to the world of hardware and software technology but also expands my scope to companies focused on the interplay between media and technology, live events and ecommerce. RafterMarsh provides general advice for companies looking for part-time "general counsel." We specialize in handling licensing and IP matters for technology companies. If European and music licensing expertise is need, I work closely with a UK colleague, Terry Marsh, of RafterMarsh UK. I also recently passed the UK Solicitor exam (but haven't activated my license yet).

Specialties: Intellectual property law, technology licensing, ecommerce/online contracting (terms and conditions, privacy policies, etc.), contract manufacturing (U.S. and Asia), software and hardware development agreements, mergers and acquisitions (asset deals. due diligence), employment law, leases and dispute resolution. Prior to my job as General Counsel of Digidesign, I was a litigator focusing on intellectual property, complex litigation and business disputes.
