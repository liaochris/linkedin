David Snyder's Summary

David Snyder is an independent consultant helping organizations implement innovative technologies. His experience covers healthcare systems, data security, Internet business, credit card issuing & transaction processing, contactless payments, customer support, and environmental engineering. He has an MBA and is a California-registered engineer.

Member of Board of Directors of Health Information Management Systems Society (HIMSS) Northern California Chapter.

At SVForum, organizes speaker presentations and panels on hot topics in healthcare IT and related areas for the Healthcare IT Leadership Special Interest Group. Also produced the Mobile Health Track for the GMIC 2013 conference at Moscone Center in San Francisco (sv.thegmic.com/m-health).

Member of 2013 Grand Prize winning team at Stanford University Medical Center StartX Med Innovation Challenge (www.startxmed.com).

Mr. Snyder helped Kaiser Permanente develop and pilot an enhanced membership ID card and enable its payments switch with direct network connections to American Express and a check processing service. For a large payments processor, he provided strategic consulting to help understand the merchant's point of view on alternative payment systems for international e-commerce.

Prior to the credit card industry, Mr. Snyder was an environmental engineer working with energy facilities, hazardous materials, and hazardous waste remediation.

Before that, he was a Respiratory Therapy Technician in Intensive Care Units.  

Mr. Snyder is a registered civil engineer in California with a B.S. in Civil Engineering & Engineering and Public Policy from Carnegie-Mellon University and an MBA in Technology Management from the University of Phoenix.

Specialties: healthcare IT; mobile health; medical devices; data security; electronic payments; credit cards; contactless payments; NFC; networking; Internet; e-commerce; mobile payments; product management; environmental engineering
