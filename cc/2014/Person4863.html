Russell Gifford's Experience

Instructional Designer and Guest Instructor, Lifelong Learning

Western Iowa Tech Community College

Educational Institution; 501-1000 employees; Higher Education industry

January 2004 – Present (10 years 9 months) Sioux City, Iowa Area

Multi-media presentations and seminars on subjects surrounding American History.

My tenth year with WIT included an incredibly successful 15 part examination of "The Great War: 1914-1918", a detailed look at the disaster that set the course for the 20th Century. The fall term followed the "Founding Fathers" from 1750 to 1800, through the Revolution, the Constitutional crisis, and the beginning of the new nation. Both series were funded by a generous grant from the Kind World Foundation. Other highly successful series during my past ten years include "Iowa During the American Civil War"; "Khrushchev in Iowa: 1959"; (both funded by Humanities Iowa grants); "Picturing America" (funded in part by a National Endowment for the Arts grant);"The Roots of the Civil Rights Movement"; "JFK and the Campaign that Changed America"; (Submitted for Iowa Lifelong Learning grant); "When the Walls Came Down: Examining the History and the Impact of the Berlin Wall"; and nearly 100 others.

Interactive delivery with extensive background provided by historical documents, my 20th century programs include use of period media as well. The goal is to shine a light on areas of history that continue to shape America today, and to get the participant to view it as the people of the time saw events unfold. When we understand why particular decisions were made, and the results, we can truly judge what the options were.
