Leanne Coffman's Summary

Throughout her career, Leanne Coffman has instructed more than six thousand individuals in government required trainings for general industry and developed numerous site-specific compliance programs for clients. Along with workplace safety and health, Coffman specializes in human resource management that fits clients’ unique criteria and facility organization. Through the decade Ms. Coffman has spent in consulting, casework routinely exposes her to a wide variety of industries, from manufacturing to healthcare facilities. Professionally, these experiences continually augment her knowledge of complex HR and workplace safety issues.

As a consultant, Coffman assesses workplace conditions and delivers training programs and policies for clients on topics relating to diversity, anti-discrimination laws, violence prevention, substance abuse and occupational safety and health compliance. Additionally, she has participated in more than twenty-five separate OSHA inspections and subsequent formal and informal conferences. Post-accident investigations, disaster preparedness, and hazard abatement are other arenas of dedicated expertise.

Ms. Coffman serves as an expert witness for two northern Indiana law firms representing client litigation. In 2009, she was an invited presenter on topics of combustible dust and hazard mitigation for the Tri-State Safety Council sponsored, Construction Safety Day in Cincinnati, Ohio.

Also a prolific author and content writer, Coffman’s published works include medical and mental health articles, as well as on topics regarding workplace safety, OSHA compliance, personal and public health, human resource, legal aspects of employment and disaster response.

More than 1,000 articles have been published by Coffman throughout her writing career.

She has authored or collaborated on various books and training manuals, including portions of a comprehensive ergonomics training program for a leading HR source.
