Background

Summary

• Manage an award-winning news website.
• Developed a social media strategy to increase digital and mobile visibility for the newspaper. The plan is continuously updated as new technologies emerge, and adjusted to the paper's goals and needs.
• Conduct weekly workshops with news and sports staff to help them improve their social media skills.
• Lead a monthly social media group discussion and write a weekly column on social media and digital/mobile/tablet developments. Give presentations on social media in journalism courses, and to business and non-profit organizations.
• Contribute to weekly companywide discussions on social media engagement, new tools and apps, with social media managers across the country.
• Participate in weekly discussions on Twitter with digital and social media journalists across the country.
• Use search engine optimization to boost visibility and engagement.
• Use analytical tools to measure engagement. Provide reports to news, advertising and circulation departments to outline growth and areas that need improvement.
• Developed and maintained niche websites for the newspaper's specialty products and microsites.
• Have a working knowledge of basic web-building/design language including HTML, Java, CSS, SQL, etc.
• Use photo editing and design programs; trained in audio and video recording and editing.
• Manage the newspaper's opinion page, write some editorials, vet letters to the editor and work with 30 community columnists.
• As life editor, revamped the features department, which at one point grew to include 10 full- and part-time employees and more than a dozen freelance writers.
• Launched a monthly outdoors magazine, a quarterly family magazine and a weekly community news section, which included coverage of local government, current events and features.
• Started a weekly entertainment guide, which covered events in four states.
• Bachelor’s degrees in journalism and French; cum laude, Distinguished Service Award.
