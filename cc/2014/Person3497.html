Jim Burgess' Summary

Trial attorney who represents businesses in all phases of litigation. Specializes in class actions and cases under the Unfair Competition Law (Business and Professions Code Section 17200). Has significant experience representing insurers in claims handling, underwriting, coverage and premium disputes. Also represents clients in disputes concerning merger and asset purchase agreements. Cases typically involve allegations of breach of contract, unfair competition and commercial torts such as insurance bad faith, fraud and interference with contract. Also handles cases under the Americans with Disabilities Act and California's Unruh Civil Rights Act, as well as intellectual property disputes, including trade secret, copyright and trademark cases, and product defect/breach of warranty disputes. Represents clients in a broad range of industries, including entertainment, health care, insurance, real estate, manufacturing, retail, technology and internet companies.

Admitted in all courts in the State of California, the US Court of Appeals for the Ninth Circuit, the US District Courts for the Central, Northern, Eastern and Southern Districts of California, the Eastern District of Pennsylvania (pro hac vice), and the Southern District of Texas (pro hac vice).

Published cases include Advanced Building Maintenance v. State Compensation Insurance Fund (1996) 49 Cal. App. 4th 1388, Scheffield Medical Group, Inc. v. Workers' Compensation Appeals Board (1999) 70 Cal. App. 4th 868, Conversive, Inc. v. Conversagent, Inc. (C.D. Cal. 2006) 433 F.Supp.2d 1079, 79 U.S.P.Q.2d 1284, Mitan v. Feeney (C.D.Cal. 2007) 497 F.Supp.2d 1113, 68 Fed.R.Serv.3d 809, Koszdin v. State Compensation Ins. Fund (2010) 186 Cal. App. 4th 480, Fenton v. Balick (E.D.Pa. 2011) 821 F. Supp.2d 755, 2011 WL 5248140, and unpublished but citable cases include Stern v. Sony Corp. (C.D.Cal. 2010) 2010 WL 8022226; affirmed in Stern v. Sony Corp. (9th Cir. 2011) 2011 WL 5854299.

Specialties

Trial attorney, class actions, insurance bad faith, trade secrets, unfair competition, 17200, Unruh Civil Rights Act, Americans with Disabilities Act, breach of warranty, product defect, breach of contract, interference with contract, fraud, litigation, real estate litigation, corporate litigation, antitrust, idea submission, copyright and trademark infringement.
