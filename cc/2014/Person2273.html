Binaebi Akah's Experience

Sr. User Experience Designer

OCLC

Nonprofit; 1001-5000 employees; Information Technology and Services industry

May 2013 – Present (1 year 5 months) Columbus, Ohio Area

I integrate closely with developers, business analysts, visual designers, and product owners to create interaction patterns and annotated wireframes for cloud-based library cataloging systems, which librarians, researchers, and patrons will use.

Discussing wireframes exposes technical constraints, interaction gaps, and additional business requirements before developers touch code. Responsibilities include leading content-first sessions with multiple stakeholders, designing templates for inter-library loan print outs for librarians, determining user flows for new, requested functionality, and maintaining consistency across the app and related products. I use Axure, AxShare, and Excel to create wireframes, prototypes, and tabular templates. I love post-its and sharpies for facilitating brainstorming sessions.

User Experience Design Associate

Privately Held; 201-500 employees; Architecture & Planning industry

June 2011 – May 2013 (2 years) Columbus, Ohio Area

As the first user experience designer in the company, I help bridge the communication gap between the client and the design, and the design and the developer. I do this by translating research insights into actionable designs and specifications for digital solutions. Client industries include consumer product goods, retail, and restaurants.

Managed small design teams to meet digital menu board project deadlines under budget. Created sitemaps, user flows, and annotated wireframes to facilitate conversations between the client, designer, and developer. Sketched storyboards and digital concepts in retail contexts. Developed a standard document to compile annotated sitemap, wireframes, and creative designs to hand to contracted developers and designers. Interfaced with the architecture/industrial design teams to ensure digital solutions worked within proposed client's environment.

Clients include: Office Depot, Peter Piper Pizza, Vail Resorts, X-ACTO, The Home Depot, Chiquita, Nationwide Insurance, Zoe's Kitchen, Pottery Barn.

Binaebi Akah's Publications

  • Beatrice Learns to Dance

    • Bright Bird Press
    • August 2013
    Authors: Binaebi Akah, Jorge Sanclemente

    Beatrice the little girl robot wants to dance as well as her neighbor Melvin, but every time she tries, something happens! Read this book with your child for a funny, encouraging story about finding your own way to learn new things.

  • Sketchnotes Field Guide for the Busy Yet Inspired Professional

    • Bright Bird Press
    • January 2012
    Authors: Binaebi Akah, Charlene McBride

    32 pages of helpful instruction and exercises to help you break into sketchnoting your next team meeting, conference talk, or big idea.

    This field guide aims to empower your “live” sketchnoting and visual thinking abilities by providing explanations, examples, and exercises.

  • Empowering Products: Personal Identity through the Act of Creative Appropriation

    • Extended Abstracts of CHI 2010
    • April 2010
    Authors: Binaebi Akah, Shaowen Bardzell

    This paper explores the relationship between personal identity and the act of appropriating digital objects in the home--specifically do-it-yourself--to inform the design of empowering products. It reports ongoing research and provides a preliminary analysis of the Steampunk movement as a case study for personal appropriation. Appropriation-identity design guidelines are provided as a result of the data analysis.
