Background

Summary

Rosie X, founded the seminal web publication geekgirl(r) in 1993 that became part of a catchcry of an online feminist revolution.This 'internet-influencer' is interested in Arts, Science & (biomimicry, AI, robots), Electronics, Technology, Social Justice and Animal Welfare.

She has written for: New Matilda online, Internet.AU, Newton Science Magazine, Eworld, MyCareer, Vive, Wired, The Mag, Syte (The Australian), Icon (Sydney Morning Herald) 21c and Hyper Magazine. Interviewed Noam Chomsky, Donna Haraway et al

Exhibitions include: The New National Museum of Australia (Canberra), Powerhouse Museum (Sydney), Virtual Society and Multimedia Conference '98 (Gifu, Japan), The Pandora Project - National Library of Australia, State Library of NSW, Scienceworks Museum (VIC), The New Museum of Contemporary Art (NY), Comdex (US/Japan) and archived by the Andy Warhol Museum Pennsylvania (USA).

Featured in more than 100 National and International publications: Fantasies of Fetishism: From Decadence to the Post-Human, New York Library Feminist Press – NY, Processed Lives (Gender and Technology in Everyday Life), Nattering on the Net, Net Chick, Hypervoices, Net Travellers (Japan), How to Mutate and take over the World, DIY Feminism, Postcards from the Net, Australia the Clever Country, The Riotgrrl Guide to Designing Webzines, The Transit Lounge – 21C, Suffragettes to She-Devils, and Jamming the Media.

As a Project Manager, Producer and Consultant, clients have included: ABC (Radio), World Vision Australia, Dept of Human Services, Sensis, Telstra, BigPond, Intel, Toyota, Qantas, eBay, Office of Fair Trade, Victorian WorkCover, Nine MSN, Worldschool, Dept of Education & Training, University of Technology Sydney (Institute of Multimedia & Learning), Deakin Uni, Swinburne Uni and Melbourne City Mission.

"Happiness never decreases by being shared" Dalai Lama

Specialties: Staying calm, being creative, teaching, producing, sharing skills, and mentoring.
