Cathy Allen's Publications

  • Benefits of Effective Quality Control Systems in Accounting Firms: Seeing the Big Picture

    • The CPA Journal
    • January 2011
    Authors: Cathy Allen

    Based on surveys of CPA firm personnel conducted by the author, this article describes the larger benefits (i.e. besides compliance with professional standards) -- such as greater competitiveness, adaptability, and profitability that accrue to firms that have effective systems in place to assure high quality work.

  • Comparing the Ethics Codes: AICPA and IFAC

    • Journal of Accountancy
    • October 2010
    Authors: Cathy Allen

    This article provides an indepth comparison between the Ethics Codes of the American Institute of CPAs (AICPA) and the International Federation of Accountants (IFAC), describes how the AICPA Code is converging with the IFAC Code, and the AICPA's ethics codification project.

  • A Global Standard for Professional Ethics

    • Journal of Accountancy
    • May 2008
    Authors: Cathy Allen, Robert Bunting

    This article focuses on the international ethics standards—including auditor independence—promulgated by the International Federation of Accountants (IFAC) and how U.S. practitioners may be affected by them. (Co-author Robert Bunting was deputy president of IFAC at the time the article was developed.)

  • Improving the Code of Professional Conduct

    • Journal of Accountancy / AICPA
    • June 1, 2011
    Authors: Cathy Allen

    This article provides background on the AICPA's ethics codification project and explains the conceptual framework approach and how it is being incorporated into the Code.
