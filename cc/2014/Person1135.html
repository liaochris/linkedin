Background

Summary

Glenn Llopis (pronounced ‘yō-pēs) is an author, speaker and business consultant best known for what he calls "the immigrant perspective.” Most of his writings, speaking engagements, and consulting assignments focus on leadership, Hispanics talent and business development, career advancement, marketing, diversity management, entrepreneurship, and turn around operations.

He focuses on the idea that if people embrace the natural skills and characteristics that are influenced by their culture, they enable an immigrant mentality that gives them a distinct advantage to propel success and significance in ones career, business and life.

Glenn is the Chairman of Glenn Llopis Group, LLC and the Founder/President of the Center for Hispanic Leadership.

Llopis currently is a contributing writer at Forbes, Huffington Post and Harvard Business Review. He is the author of Earning Serendipity: 4 Skills for Creating and Sustaining Good Fortune in Your Work; The Six Reasons Why Hispanic Leadership Will Save America's Corporations; and Why a Personal Employee Brand Will Save Your Career and Your Workplace.

The son of Cuban immigrants who were victims of Castro's revolution, Glenn combines an immigrant's perspective, and a UCLA education, with fast-tracked years at the Gallo Wine Company and Sunkist where he became the youngest senior executive in the company's 100-year history.

Mr. Llopis has been featured on CNN, Fox News, Univision, ABC, NBC and CBS. Glenn is a board member for the Society & Business Lab at the Marshall School of Business at the University of Southern California. Mr. Llopis also serves as a mentor for Junior Achievement.

Specialties: Thought-Leadership,Consumer Packaged Goods, Innovation, Leadership Development, Workplace Transformation, Performance Development, Branding, Business Strategy

Seeing with circular vision: Broaden your observation beyond what you seek and beyond the obvious details before you, and enlarge your field of opportunities; search within conversations and adverse circumstances for possibilities.

Sowing entrepreneurial seeds: When good vision is met with consistent, hand-dirtying execution every day, the result is a stable, growing fortune; focus on proper timing and proper depth.

Sharing the harvest: Focus on meeting others’ needs to improve personal good fortune; make generosity part of your purpose, an integral part of the DNA of your career or company.


Those who master this quartet - tap into a power most never reach: the power to create and sustain a momentum of good fortune. This ability to earn serendipity can elevate your career or company quicker than any single force. And with the skills outlined in this book, you will be catapulted onto the path of prosperity.
