Russ Nelson's Summary

I'm a doctoral candidate in marketing at the University of California, Irvine.

I'm a "quantitative economic sociologist" who applies empirical models to social phenomena within the arenas of market creation and financial decision-making.

Supported by a Dissertation Fellowship from the Kauffman Foundation, my dissertation research uses a unique dataset of 1.2 million Twitter messages and state-of-the-art social network techniques to bridge the competitive dynamics and market evolution literatures. Within the empirical context of the Southern California gourmet food truck market, I examine the decisions that entrepreneurs make in deciding how to interact with competitors, how to differentiate their product offerings, and where to locate their mobile firms.

I taught a UC Irvine class on Brand Management and gourmet food trucks:
merage.uci.edu/deans-quarterly/spring2014/the-truck-stops-here.html

More about my research and teaching here: http://russnelson.flavors.me/

I have over 5 years of industry experience, having worked as a marketing and strategy consultant on new product development, marketing strategy, branding, and process improvement projects within the US and internationally for clients including Yahoo!, McDonald’s, SC Johnson, Saatchi & Saatchi, Merck, Proctor & Gamble, Kaiser Permanente, Unilever, MasterCard, and Intuit.
