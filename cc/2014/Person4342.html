Michael Jaynes' Summary

Michael Jaynes is a Director within the Expert Services practice area of Ocean Tomo’s Chicago office. Michael provides a unique and comprehensive finance and accounting perspective to each of his engagements along with expertise in intellectual property and commercial litigation issues.

Michael provides key perspective on intellectual property issues, including valuation, licensing, and calculation of economic damages.

In addition to his role at Ocean Tomo, Michael is an Adjunct Professor at the Illinois Institute of Technology’s Master of Intellectual Property Management and Markets Program. He is also an expert speaker and leads seminars and lectures on patent damages, intellectual property valuation, licensing, economic damages, and other topics. Michael also is extensively involved with the mock trial program at the University of Notre Dame where he writes both case materials and acts as a mock expert witness.
Michael has experience in several key industries, including: pharmaceuticals, software, biogenetics, consumer goods, and other technologies.

Michael Jaynes' Publications

  • Design in three dimensions: choices and challenges for legal protection

    • IAM® Intellectual Asset Management Magazine, Issue 66
    • June 2014
    Authors: Michael Jaynes

    The question of how best to protect non-functional 3D designs or design elements from an IP perspective is becoming increasingly important with the advent and rising popularity of 3D printing...In the United States and the European Union, such design aspects may be protected through copyrights, design patents (in the United States) or registered designs (elsewhere), trademarks/trade dress or, in some cases, a combination of these. Each of these forms of IP protection has certain advantages and disadvantages in terms of the degree of protection, cost, time to obtain, damages potentially available in the event of infringement and hurdles to proving such damages.
