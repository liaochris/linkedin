Background

Summary

If you are looking for the best DUI trial lawyer in Pennsylvania, look no further than Attorney Justin J. McShane of http://themcshanefirm.com The McShane Firm. Based in Harrisburg, Attorney McShane is the most highly qualified Pennsylvania DUI (http://themcshanefirm.com/practice/dui) and criminal defense attorney. Justin is a nationally renowned forensic science expert who successfully uses his superior knowledge, experience, and guts to defend citizens accused of crimes ranging from DUI to underage drinking, from drug possession to assault, and even capital homicide. Widely respected for his encyclopedic knowledge of all forms of forensic science, Attorney McShane has lectured at national forensic and DUI seminars all over the country and has even lectured before the American Chemical Society.

Please visit our DUI blog to learn more about PA DUI: http://paduiblog.com.
For more information about Justin's Qualifications as a Harrisburg DUI Lawyer: http://themcshanefirm.com.

Specialties:DUI Defense, Criminal Law, Litigation

Experience

Chairman/CEO

The McShane Firm, LLC
January 2003 – Present (11 years 9 months)

After buying out his partner, Attorney McShane immediately increased the firm to include 5 attorneys and 11 support staff. Attorney McShane operates, maintains and manages a thriving law firm. Attorney McShane has limited his practice to criminal law specializing in traffic offenses and DUI. Immediately arranged to have all attorneys certified in accident scene reconstruction, Evidentiary Breath Alcohol Technician, and Standardized Field Sobriety Testing Instructors, Drugs that Impair Driving, DRE and advanced issues in forensic blood and urine testing. Maintains the Dauphin County contract for representing those who are subject to involuntary mental health commitments. Maintains the Dauphin County contract representing those who claim they have received the ineffective assistance of counsel and those seeking to assert their actual innocence using DNA (the NACDL effort known as "The Innocence Project").

Assistant Public Defender

County of Dauphin
December 1999 – January 2003 (3 years 2 months)

I was a certified legal intern when I was in law school. I tried six (6) cases to full verdict before I graduated from law school. While in the Public Defender's Office, I defended thousands of cases in every stage of litigation including a wide-range of cases from a triple homicide death penalty case, other murders, sex offenses all the way to ungraded misdemeanors. I represented clients at involuntary civil commitments (i.e., mental health hearings) and Children and Youth matters.
