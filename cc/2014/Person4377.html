Michael Turner's Summary

Financially independent accredited investor with homes in Spain, Hawai'i, and South Beach, I am active in philanthropic endeavours and a vocal advocate of education, wildlife, and social causes around the globe. Additionally, I consider myself a technology geek, global gastronome, news-junkie, European football fanatic, stock market guru, and philomath of the Arts and Sciences.

Born in Beverly Hills, California with both British and American citizenships, I spent my twenties and thirties living and working in some of the world's greatest cities: Boston, Paris, Montreal, Honolulu, Minneapolis, and Manhattan.

In spite of the adventures I had hitherto experienced, at the age of 37, I found myself caught up in what was ultimately an unbalanced and unrewarding corporate lifestyle in Manhattan. Wanting less of that, and more of everything else, I hung-up my dress shirts, suits, and ties and retired completely from working.

Fast-forward to today.

Never in the same country for more than a month or two, I spend the majority of my time with my wife/BFF driving, flying, and sailing around the world, timing our travels to experience local festivals, events, and seasons: eating, exploring, and enjoying life!

Pleasurable pursuits aside, occasionally I still make time to write on topics such as education, investment strategy, cultural anthropology, and food & travel. I am proud to have had articles published in Bon Appétit and Food & Wine Magazine. Some of my other pieces have been republished on news outlets such as cbsnews.com, worldnews.com, seekingalpha.com and other content-specific news sites.
