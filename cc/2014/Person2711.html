David H. Fink's Summary

David Fink, a native of the Detroit area, is an experienced civil litigator, whose practice has focused on commercial litigation. He handles both individual and class actions and currently serves as court-appointed interim lead counsel in the Eastern District of Michigan in the Refrigerant Compressors Antitrust Litigation, where the Court has preliminarily approved a settlement, with Defendants committing to pay up to $48.5 million. Mr. Fink is also interim co-lead counsel in the Enbridge Oil Spill litigation in the Western District of Michigan.Mr. Fink has handled civil lawsuits in courts throughout the Greater Detroit area and the State of Michigan, winning important victories not only at the State and Federal trial court level, but also in the Michigan Court of Appeals, the Michigan Supreme Court and the Sixth Circuit Court of Appeals.

After serving in Governor Granholm's Cabinet, Mr. Fink was appointed by Governor Granholm to mediate a complex dispute between the Detroit Medical Center and the Wayne State University Medical School. In 2010, Mr. Fink was appointed to serve on the Michigan State Officers Compensation Commission for a term expiring in 2014. He also serves on the Michigan State Bar Committee on Judicial Qualifications. Mr. Fink previously served on the Judicial Advisory Committee, appointed by Senator Carl Levin and Senator Debbie Stabenow, to assist in evaluating candidates for appointment to the United States District Court for the Eastern District of Michigan. In 2013, Mr. Fink was appointed by the Judges of the Eastern District of Michigan to serve as Chair of their Magistrate Judge Merit Selection Panel.

David H. Fink's Experience

Managing Partner

Fink + Associates Law

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Law Practice industry

January 2011 – Present (3 years 9 months)

Fink + Associates Law is a law firm focused on commercial litigation, including individual and class actions, as well as derivative litigation. Practice areas include securities fraud (including securities fraud class actions), consumer fraud (including consumer fraud class actions), antitrust (including antitrust class action litigation), Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA) (including FLSA collective actions) and environmental matters. The firm is also involved in litigation on matters involving insurance coverage, intergovernmental issues, construction contract disputes, eminent domain (condemnation), property tax assessment challenges, probate disputes, partnership dissolution and shareholder oppression claims. The firm also litigates derivative claims on behalf of disaffected shareholders.
