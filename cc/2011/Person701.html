Scott Simpson Cohen's Summary

I am a recidivist entrepreneur and 20-year veteran of Internet and new-media startups. I have started three companies, sold two of them, and have funded and advised innovative companies in Boston, Chicago, Israel, and elsewhere. I was involved in the founding of The Nantucket Conference on Innovation and Entrepreneurship, now in its 13th year, and work to support entrepreneurialism and innovation in the Greater Boston area. I can be reached via email: info at scottcohen.com

Scott Simpson Cohen's Experience

Founder

FanTab Corp.

January 2011 – Present (1 year 1 month) Boston, MA

FanTab tracks sports fan confidence in real-time. The platform enables sports fans to contribute their sentiment or confidence regarding their favorite sports teams; that sentiment is then aggregated to create real-time, chartable indexes of fan confidence. Available on 400+ sports sites, team sites, and media sites. Try it at http://www.fantab.com

Founder

Financial Media Holdings Group

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Publishing industry

April 2002 – December 2010 (8 years 9 months)

Founded and sold FMHG, which publishes Compliance Week, an information service for the officers and boards of global public companies. Compliance Week was acquired by London-based Haymarket Media in July 2008.

Founder

Newmediary

January 1998 – March 2002 (4 years 3 months)

Co-founded and sold this venture-backed company, which built and patented a directory platform that was deployed by dozens of publishers, including The Economist, Forbes, Fortune, Business Week and others. It was acquired by CNET Networks, which is now part of CBS Interactive.

Founding Team Member and Director

Boston.com

Public Company; 201-500 employees; NYT; Online Media industry

January 1993 – August 1998 (5 years 8 months)

Was on the founding team of The Boston Globe’s electronic publishing subsidiary, which developed the pioneering and award-winning Boston.com. Oversaw all content and editorial operations at Boston.com, which at that time was part of The New York Times Company’s “Times Company Digital.”

Director

Fidelity Capital

July 1992 – December 1993 (1 year 6 months)

Got my start in the publishing business at a chain of newspapers that was acquired by Fidelity Capital, the private-equity arm of Fidelity Investments. Prior to the acquisition, I was recruited by Fidelity to help with their central editorial strategy. Afterwards, I attempted to launch an early Internet healthcare startup called Patient.com. Let's just say it was too early.
