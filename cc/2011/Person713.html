*What and where is Suriname?
Suriname's 161.470 km² are situated on the northeast coast of South America, bordering Brazil in the south. The semi-official language in this former Dutch colony is ... Dutch. Its population consists of descendants of the original inhabitants (Amerindians), plus descendants of imported African slaves, descendants of former East Indian and Indonesian contract laborers, and also descendants of Chinese, Lebanese, Portuguese, Jewish and other immigrants. Any unsuspecting tourist feels pleasantly surprised by the exquisite local cuisine, a fascinating intact nature, the mainly wooden and red bricked historical architecture (UNESCO Heritage Listed) in the Capital City, lovely tropical weather and a delicate blend of witty, spontaneous and also friendly people. Local currency is SRD, the Surinamese dollar. Dominant foreign currencies: US$ and euro.

Editor Sranan Dialect

Spectrum Prisma Sranan Tongo Dictionary

2004 – 2004 (less than a year)

Sranan is the main dialect in Suriname. As one out of three locals*, I too was privileged to be part of a wonderful experience: (the making of) the Prisma Sranan Tongo Dictionary.

Editing the original database in assistance to both writers (Juliënne Blanker and Jaap Dubbeldam) was a true, true honor.

About the book:
This overview of Sranan words and verbal expressions was the first selection ever translated into Dutch ánd vice versa. The dictionary was published by Spectrum, a Dutch firm. Its first edition in 2005 sold out in weeks in the Netherlands.

About the publisher:
Spectrum is also the publisher of the Winkler Prins encyclopedia. A Microsoft/Spectrum collaboration resulted in the 2004 Winkler Prins Encarta.
