Amy Webb’s Summary

Simply put, I help organizations innovate. I am an author, educator, speaker, consultant and thought leader who adapts current and emerging technologies to solve problems in mainstream journalism and journalism education. I also run the Webbmedia Group, a strategic, digital product and business consultancy to online media companies and other organizations.

As I consultant, I've worked with large companies evaluate and implement new cms solutions, monetize content, develop community editorial partnerships and launch new digital publications.

Recent projects include: CMS selection for a large newspaper company; digital strategy for a major TV network; digital strategy for an international magazine brand; development of a university journalism program curriculum; training module development for a large international media organization.

Our full client list can be viewed at http://www.webbmediagroup.com. Email me directly at amy@webbmediagroup.com.

Amy Webb’s Specialties:

• Creating and implementing training modules for media organizations around the world
• Selection and implementation of content management systems and other third party applications
• Innovating within a mobile framework: location-aware services, geospatial web, stand-alone apps
• Converting single-platform content into multiple-platform for monetization and growth
• Working with web teams to harness and use social networking tools
• Digital strategy for current/ future monetization

  • Founder & Editor In Chief

    Dragonfire

    (Non-Profit; 11-50 employees; Online Media industry)

    January 2005 — January 2007 (2 years 1 month)

    I am the founder and former publisher of Dragonfire (http://www.dfire.org), an online magazine of news and culture. Under my direction, we went from a one-person office to a staff of more than 25. Our traffic spiked from 4,000 unique weekly visitors in our first few issues to more than 80,000 uniques every day from around the world.

    I established Dragonfire as a nonprofit and spent two years creating relationships with external stakeholders to ensure a properly-funded budget for my organization. Under my direction, everyone on the Dragonfire staff worked collaboratively: Editors developed stories alongside programmers, freelance journalists were cross-trained on audio production, staff reporters all learned how to code in Flash. I believe very strongly that the development of a converged media organization depends on cross-training as well as cooperation. (Dragonfire went dark in August 2007.)
