Jan Husdal’s Summary

Jan Husdal has since 2005 been a research scientist with the Transport Economics Research Group at Møreforsking Molde/Molde Research Institute in Molde, Norway. His main scope of work is transport modelling, impact analysis, and cost-benefit evaluations of infrastructure projects.

Prior to his current engagement, he served as an adviser in vulnerability assessment, safe community planning and crisis management with several Norwegian government authorities for more than 15 years.

Jan Husdal holds a BSc in Civil Engineering from Norway (1985) and an MSc in GIS (2000) from the University of Leicester, UK, focusing on least-cost paths and transportation network analysis.

Since 2004 he has served as a member of the program committee on European Policy and Research with the Association for European Transport, AET.

His personal research interests and publications are centred on topics related to

* supply chain risk management
* transportation network vulnerability

He has also served as a referee for articles submitted to the following journals:

* Transportation Science
* Transport Policy
