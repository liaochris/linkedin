Fulvio Pasculli’s Summary

From 1996 until 2008 I was responsible in Italy for the entire application development, maintenance, infrastructure, telecommunications (voice and data), IT security and Data Management areas
I managed the relationships with external vendors in the context of Pfizer’s high reliance on outsourcing, and managed the largest part of the BT department’s 10 million € budget.
In this role I coordinated a staff of around 7 projects managers and indirectly 60 external consultants employed both on projects and ongoing services.
I managed the relationships with Pfizer’s international teams for the areas of my responsibility. I was a member of Pfizer’s European Application Architecture Community, which had the objective of addressing the selection and the integration of new application platforms, in order to support the company’s aims of standardisation and globalisation.
I was responsible for the company’s IT infrastructure (30 Unix servers, 30 Intel servers, 2 SAN, an IBM backup library), delivering services to about 3000 users (1000 campus and 2000 remote). In this capacity I was responsible for IT Security and Business Continuity and Disaster Recovery.
In 2008 I worked for a relevant European Application Razionalization Project with the objective to consolidate the Local European applications into Regional/global Solutions. In April 2009 I was appointed Service Lead Southern Europe with the responsability to support the design of the European Service Strategy and Governance Model. My main task is to oversee in the Region (Southern Europe) the service delivery performances against SLA's from services providers (Global Shared Services organization or third party vendors) and Continual Service Improvement.

Fulvio Pasculli’s Specialties:

Integration of a large pharmaceutical multinational company in terms of different processes and systems, outsourcing processes, Sap platform, Siebel platform , Data Warehouse environment, BPR and organizational projects, CRM systems, Internet strategy for the pharmaceutical market, SOX and security management, Infrastructure and telecomms management, VOIP and mobile phone services, Service strategy and governance model design.
