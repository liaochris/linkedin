  • EVP of Technology

    New Road Health, LLC

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Internet industry)

    January 2007 — February 2008 (1 year 2 months)

    The company intended to build an Internet-based product that is focused on the university health market. The product was designed to: a) store lifetime personal health records, b) connect patients with their medical providers, c) provide highly personalized access to medical/health information and self-help tools, and d) run on popular student social networking sites to make it easy to remember and fun to use. A high degree of customer focus would enable the product to be highly differentiated: a) special emphasis on student-to-health center connectivity and convenience, and b) tailored to the unique health concerns and educational needs of young adults who are just beginning to make their own health and medical choices. The company was unable to fund in the Alpha-phase.

  • Director of Technology

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Medical Practice industry)

    October 2005 — December 2006 (1 year 3 months)

    This startup company has created Catalis Accelerator™, a new graphical medical record product designed for physicians in private practice. It is used to manage patient clinical encounters and diagnostic testing data. The application is designed to be intuitive, easy to use and enable the rapid input/use of complex clinical data. This automates the core activities of the clinic, so security, high availability and performance are critical to success. The technology is packaged to be managed by Catalis on a turn-key remote basis. The company is leaving beta-phase with 15 customer sites and plans for exponential growth.
