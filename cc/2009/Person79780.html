Pietro Paganini’s Summary

Pietro helps firms to build a proactive and more competitive market environment, so they can perform more successfully and gain a leadership. He provides companies with the right strategies to break down monopolies and market barriers, as well as to overcome social hostilities. He combines communications skills with market knowledge and management capabilities. He has a good knowledge of the U.S. and EU political environment.

His first book “Innovation: from Theory to Territory: Paradigm and Strategies in the Information Society” was published in January 2006. He is also the author of several articles in various Italian and international papers and journals such as the WSJ, in which he has continuously advocated free market solutions.

Beside working at Competere, Pietro serves as Adjunct Professor at John Cabot University (Rome, Italy) and as lecturer at LUMSA (Rome, Italy). He was visiting lecturer at Karlstad University (Sweden).

He holds a Doctorate in Communication and Complex Organizations, a Master in Multimedia and a Bachelor in Business Communication.

Pietro's partner at Competere are Henrik Rasmussen and Nico Wirtz.

Pietro is board member at: Istituto Italiano Privacy (Italian Institute for Privacy) Baia - Network Italian Chapter - la Storia nel Futuro.

Pietro wrote among others in the WSJ, Il Sole 24 Ore, Libero Mercato, and he appeared on RAI 1, RAI 3, LA7, Al Jazeera, Radio RAI 2, Radio 24, Eco Radio, Il Velino Radio, Decidere Radio

Pietro Paganini’s Specialties:

Pietro works with ICT firms, pharmaceutical and medical industries, finance and banks, venture, business angels, private equities and start-ups. He also works with philanthropic organizations.
Pietro has a wide network of policy makers in Europe and in the US, he works with EU and US think-tanks, grassroots and institutes.
Specialities: grassroots, lobbying, public affairs, public relations, network building, government relations, geopolitical management, scenario building, com. strategies.
