Will Stevenson’s Summary

Mr. Will Stevenson is a graduate of SUNY Institute of Technology at Utica/Rome in Information Systems. He also holds a Masters Certification from Oracle Corporation in Financial Systems Management.

In cooperation with General Alexander Haig, Maurice Strong and the Ministry of Health to the People's Republic of China, Will most recently developed one of China's premier Pharmaceutical/Medical Surgical Logistics & Distribution e-Commerce platforms to a live pilot in the Haidian District of Beijing.

In the fortune 500 space, Will held the highest Technology Leadership position at a Malcolm Baldrige award winning company. Will's corporate track record there included maintaining 100% employee retention throughout the .com technology industry boom, positive budgetary variance and "raving fans" for customers for over 7 years at 6 tenths of one percent (.6%) of annual revenue.

His leadership and experience in the Technology Industry has been requested in the local community as a Board Member of CPEx (Colorado Performance Excellence). CPEx state-wide programs are dedicated to enabling organizations to succeed through the effective application of the practices embodied in the Baldrige Criteria for Performance Excellence.

In his career, Will has been certified to act as CIO by META Group Corporation, a Gartner Company focusing on information technology and business transformation strategies.

Will is also certified in "Values-Centered Leadership" as expressed by his graduation from The Saltwater Institute; where he learned processes and tools to create a new style of management that capitalizes on the energy and creativity of people by integrating personal values within the core mission of an organizational system.

Will’s experience include a term as CTO for Lifeline Therapeutics. Lifeline launched their premier product "Protandim", on national television programs 'Extra' and 'Primetime' to a valuation of $500MM.

Will Stevenson’s Specialties:

Quality as a Business Strategy, Linkage of Processes, Creating IT Market Differentiation, Data Standardization and Taxonomies, Intellectual Property Rights, Software Requirements
