Jason Loftis’s Summary

Jason Loftis received his undergraduate degree in education with an emphasis in history and social studies. In addition to teaching, Jason spent a decade in Christian ministry both in US churches and on the mission field overseas. Along the way, a growing passion for visual communication and web technology led Jason to pursue a career as a visual designer.

In 2008, Jason completed a Master of Arts in Theology at Fuller Theological Seminary in Pasadena, California. The degree program is unique in its effort to blend the study of theology with an exploration of art and culture. Students are expected to maintain an art portfolio in concert with their theological studies. During this season as a full-time student, Jason freely took on freelance graphic and web design projects. These projects gave him the opportunity to build his portfolio and provided the challenges he needed to expand his abilities as a self-taught visual designer.

After completing graduate school, Jason took a visual design position with Slacker.com, an internet radio company in San Diego. This was a valuable learning experience, and he enjoyed working in a collaborative, creative environment.

Unfortunately, Jason was laid off in December of 2008 due to the economic recession that took its toll on so many industries. Presently, he is pursuing design work as a freelancer.
