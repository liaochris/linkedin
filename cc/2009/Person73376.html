Neil Robinson’s Summary

Are you looking for the best technologies and direction for your business, but also want to know that your hard-earned money or your department's budget is spent in the most productive way?

Well, maybe hiring the usual big names may not be the way to go.

I think most people have had enough of corporate waste, malpractice and overcharging global consultancies. The fact is, there are many individuals who can provide truly innovative, original and high quality ideas for your business that deliver the bang per buck your company deserves.

What is LANZen?

LANZen is my technology and strategy consultancy. Technology has been my life since college; from NCR through to Olivetti, Wang Global, ICL, then on my own with LANZen from 2000.

LANZen provides great strategy ideas to a range of businesses, from City banks at exec level to local start-ups. I set up celebrity chef Jamie Oliver and could do the same for you, too.

My LinkedIn Network

My contacts here on LinkedIn extend the quality of business services that LANZen offers. These are people who are experts in their field and not just random names built up with no relevance to what I do.

I charge per day, but the majority of my clients have used me for over 3 years. So they must be seeing the results they were looking for.

Why not give me a call and see how your business could benefit from some impartial, informed advice?

Neil Robinson’s Experience

  • strategy consultant

    LANZen Limited

    (Information Technology and Services industry)

    February 2000 — Present (9 years 10 months)

    I started out as an IT engineer - fixing things that stopped people working.

    That gave me a unique approach to IT strategy and design. How can I fix what doesn't work and make what does work, work better?

    That worked. I found I was designing corporate infrastructures in City banks, national service centres and even helping prestige clients like Jamie Oliver.

    But after years of designing enterprise level infrastructures around Microsoft architectures, I've realised the Internet is changing everything.

    What used to cost a great deal of money to build in a data centre has been built much better in "The Cloud" - the Internet.

    So, that's what LANZen is about today. Tomorrow, who knows what we'll be able to do. I just know that I'll be keeping LANZen looking forward.

    I work on HOW to do it and let my clients concentrate on WHAT they want to do.
