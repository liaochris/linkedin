Noelle Chun’s Summary

Noelle Chun is a writer with additional specialties in reporting and research, news and internet aggregation, audio and video editing, blogging, and social media. She is experienced in bizarrely wide range of news media—from newspapers to magazines, from TV news to radio, from blogs to magazine web sites. She’s written for such sites as Newsweek International, Newsweek.com, VentureBeat, Guy Kawasaki’s blog, The Honolulu Advertiser, Honolulu magazine, and The Honolulu Star-Bulletin, among others.

Noelle currently serves Alltop, working closely with founder Guy Kawasaki and Chief Evangelist Neenz Falafine to think about ways to revolutionize web browsing and create dynamic new marketing techniques using Twitter, Facebook and blogs.

In the past, Noelle worked as a mid-level manager, directly supervising a staff of six people, three buildings, three hall boards, and more than 330 students at Northwestern University.

Two of Noelle’s favorite articles include the story she pitched and reported on for Newsweek.com’s lead following the Virginia Tech Shootings, as well as her coverage of life in a Chinese orphanage for Newsweek.com and Newsweek International.

Noelle Chun’s Specialties:

Internet content, writing, editing, reporting, page design, photography, producing video and audio, aggregation, multimedia journalism.
