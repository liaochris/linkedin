Kevin Dayhoff’s Summary

Kevin is a Westminster MD native. He has been a self-employed businessperson, artist; and free-lance journalist since 1971. He has been exhibiting art since 1981.

He has written for “The Tentacle,” an on-line columnist collective, since June 9, 2005. Since June 2, 2005, he has written a weekly community events and history column for the Westminster Eagle, a weekly newspaper publication of Patuxent Publishing Company, a subsidiary of Tribune; and The Sunday Carroll Eagle – in the Sunday Carroll County section of the Baltimore Sun since October 28, 2007.

He has also authored an arts, history, community events, and political blog, www.kevindayhoff.net since December 2005.

He was first elected to the Westminster City Council in May of 1999 and served as the Mayor of Westminster from May 2001 until May 16th, 2005.

Kevin served on the Maryland Municipal League Board of Directors annually for five consecutive years, from June 2000 to May 2005.

He is retired from being self-employed as a landscape designer, horticulturist and nursery stock farmer for 25 years (1974-1999).

Kevin has taught design and horticulture classes as an Adjunct Faculty Member: Carroll Community College (Branch of Catonsville Community College) (1988 to 1991).

He has served on a number of state and county advisory boards including; the Carroll County Landscape Manual Committee (1987 – 1989), University of Maryland Cooperative Extension Service Agriculture Advisory Committees (1980-1989) and the Carroll County Environmental Affairs Advisory Board (1994 – 1999).

He is a member of Grace Lutheran Church (10/25/1998), and the Carroll County Chapter of the NAACP (01/2001).

He is a volunteer with the 4-H Therapeutic Riding Program of Carroll County (1995)

Kevin attended Carroll County Public Schools from 1959 through graduation from Westminster Senior High School in 1971. He served in the United States Marine Corp Reserve (1971-1973).
