Marco Santiago’s Summary

I began my filmmaking journey in July 2003 with a feature length documentary currently in post-production titled “Into the Border”. My very first narrative film, "El Trafico", was an award winning short film, which premiered at the 2004 Filmstock International Film Festival in the UK and was exhibited at the 2005 Scottsdale International Film Festival and the 5th annual Phoenix International Film Festival. "El Trafico" won Best of Show, Best Dramatic Short, Best Directing, Best Cinematography, and 2nd place for Sound Design and Editing at the 24th annual SCC MP/TV Film Festival in Phoenix. I’ve since directed other shorts films such as "Pecan Run", "OnceUpon a Time in the Desert", and “Diablita”, which stars Jeff Fahey (“Grindhouse”, “Wyatt Earp”, “Lawnmower Man”, “Silverdo”, “Delta Force” ).

I'm a fellow of the 2006 writers lab of the National Association of Latino Independent Producers (NALIP), where my first feature length screenplay, "86", was selected for development. "86" was also selected into the prestigious NALIP Producers Academy for 2006 and again for 2007, where I attanded as a directing fellow. The project was also selected for final consideration to the Sundance Writers Lab, 2007. Most recently, my second feature film project, based on the recently acquired novel, “How to Cope with Suburban Stress”, was selected for support in the 2007 NALIP Writers Lab. The project is undergoing further development at UCLA. "How to Cope with Suburban Stress" has also been selected as a finalist for consideration to the Sundance Writers Lab for 2008.

Modeled after the Sundance writers and feature film labs, the NALIP writers lab are supported by HBO, Time Warner, MTV Networks, ABC/Walt Disney New Talent Development Program, FOX, Cox Communications, UCLA, ASU, UofA, the Academy Foundation, POV, HFPA Hollywood Foreign PressAssociation, the Ford Foundation, The Rockefeller Foundation, the John D. and Catherine T. MacArthur Foundation, and the WGA East/West.
