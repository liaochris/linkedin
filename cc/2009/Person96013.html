Susan G. Ippoliti, RP’s Summary

With 15 years of experience in the legal services industry, a Registered Paralegal (RP) and subject matter expert in electronic discovery, litigation support and litigation readiness, my experience extends to overseeing litigation support and legal technology services, and consulting with a variety of clients on best practices related to E-Discovery.

I have worked as the VP of Litigation Support & E-Discovery Services with a computer forensics firm, where I consulted with law firms and corporations on proper document management, electronic discovery processes, practice and procedures, litigation support, regulatory compliance, document retention policies and routine document destruction practices. My experience also includes sales of services and account management.

I have been responsible for managing the E-Discovery Lifecycle on the national and international level and co-chaired the EDRM Code of Conduct working group. I am a national speaker on E-Discovery and Continuing Legal Education.

Additionally, I am a member of the adjunct faculty of a two local community colleges in their ABA-approved paralegal programs. I teach Introduction to Legal Practice and Litigation Support Technology to students who, upon graduation, will become valuable members of the legal services team and advance the operations of a law office and litigation practice.

Currently, I am the Vice President & Director of Profession Development for the National Federation of Paralegal Associations (NFPA) and have been on the NFPA Board since 2004. My years of experience in the legal industry as well as my involvement in the paralegal profession on the local, state and national level has rewarded me with experience, education and leadership and has helped me build strong relationships both personal and professional.

Susan G. Ippoliti, RP’s Specialties:

Function5 provides comprehensive digital forensic services including: identification, preservation, collection and analysis of electronically stored information (ESI) recovery of deleted files and file fragments from virtually any operating system
    or storage device detection of evidence of unauthorized use or transfer of intellectual property, investigation of internal employee matters relating to malfeasance, theft or harassment, and in-court testimony by certified analysts.

    E-discovery refers to discovery in civil litigation dealing with information in electronic form. Electronic form is the representation of information as binary numbers. This is different from paper information because of its intangible form, volume, transience, and persistence.

    ESI can be stored in every type of electronic media including hard drives, thumb drives, computers, handheld devices, backup tapes, and optical disks and includes e-mails, voice-mails, instant messages, text messages, documents, spreadsheets, databases, file fragments, metadata, digital images, and digital diagrams. Evidence of destruction failure to produce ESI during E-Discovery can lead to costly penalties, sanctions, and potential loss of the lawsuit through declaratory judgment.
