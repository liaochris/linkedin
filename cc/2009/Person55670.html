Kim Phillips Sasso’s Summary

My husband and I own and operate a rapidly growing restaurant in Coweta County, Georgia. We are currently renovating and expanding our facilities, with the intent to add a second dining room and a broader range of catering and special events services.

We recently expanded into real estate, purchasing the six-unit commercial complex housing our restaurant. The purchase is an essential link to between the restaurant venture and our broader goal of contributing to the economic revival our local downtown district.

We are active members of the local Chamber of Commerce; I have recently joined the leadership team for Entrepreneurs Thrive in Coweta, a small business initiative within the chamber. I frequently work with new and prospective entrepreneurs, providing everything from informal consulting to editing and ghostwriting services.

Outside of these core professional activities, I have founded an organization focused on civic engagement, heritage preservation and community development. In this capacity I founded and continue to organize an annual spring "celebration of citizenship and community" and a winter concert, parade and banquet. In addition to creating and maintaining our restaurant website, I have developed a community website serving my small west Georgia town. Both sites include active blogs, and I maintain an online archive of blog entries and newsletters.
