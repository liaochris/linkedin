Dinesh Katre’s Summary

Presently, Dinesh Katre heads the Human-Centred Design & Computing Group at C-DAC, Pune. In this responsibility, he manages multiple teams and coordinates between diverse technology development groups.

During past 16 years at C-DAC, he has conceptualized and implemented many R&D projects that deal with e-Learning, Game Design, Digital Library, Museum Informatics, Multimedia Authoring and Content Creation. He is the principle designer of many software, content and training products. He has published around 25 research papers in international conferences and journals. India's first Conference on Advances in Usability Engineering (CAUE 2008) was organized under his mentorship.

Professional bodies like Computer Society of India (CSI), Usability Practitioners Association (UPA), Usability Matters Organization (UMO), Project Management Institute (PMI) and many other institutes of international repute have organized his lectures and workshops on Human-Computer Interaction (HCI). He is invited by universities from USA, UK and Denmark to share his expertise in the area of HCI and Usability. During past 2 years, he has delivered over 70 invited talks on wide ranging topics related to HCI and usability at multinational IT companies, research and academic institutes.

He is passionately interested in enriching the HCI/Usability domain and has set the following goals-

1. Create usable products and technologies
2. Define new methods for user study, design evaluation and usability testing
3. Design usability engineering process adaptable to different work cultures
4. Evolve multi-disciplinary teaching curriculum on HCI/Usability
5. Propagate the usability culture
