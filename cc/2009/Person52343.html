Julie Unger’s Summary

Through networking, I began working for a retained executive search firm, Step Hire. Through this experience, it became clear that I enjoyed consulting others in their careers. Once again through networking, I discovered a position at the Jewish Vocational Service in Boston as a Career Networks and Mentoring Specialist. At JVS, I work with both young and seasoned Jewish women professionals with networking, skill building and mentoring opportunities. I also manage the Career Advisory Network (CAN), which consists of volunteers who provide Career Moves' clients with career advice, industry expertise and connections.

Julie Unger’s Experience

    Jewish Vocational Service (JVS)

    (Non-Profit; 51-200 employees; Computer Software industry)

    May 2007 — Present (2 years 7 months)

    I work in Career Moves, a department within JVS, which provides career coaching, career counseling and job search assistance to professionals in the greater Boston area. Our clients range from recent grads to senior level execs. I manage two career networking efforts in Career Moves; The JVS/CJP Jewish Women's Career Network (JWCN), which aims to support and enhance career development for greater Boston Jewish women of all ages through programs, leadership development, networking and mentoring opportunities.

    I also manage the Career Advisory Network (CAN), which consists of industry experts, who as volunteers provide invaluable career advice and networking opportunities to our clients. I have been fortunate to work with such a dedicated and experienced team; they have created an environment that welcomes change and fresh new ideas.
