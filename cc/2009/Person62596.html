Margaret Hampton’s Summary

Family Caregiver, Internet Marketer, Author, and Speaker with goal to provide caregiver support, books & media, home health care products, and extensive resources to relieve caregiver stress, help prevent caregiver burnout, and promote caregiver health and wellness.

Compassionate Minister to abused, battered, traumatized and otherwise hurting women, co-dependents and people pleasers, to help prevent these situations, assist people trapped in them, and restore women afflicted by damaging after-effects.

Resourceful, results-oriented Executive and Consultant with proven leadership, strategic vision, market opportunity identification, corporate planning, finance, fundraising, and communication skills applied in corporations, new ventures, and national health charities.

Creative thinker, e.g., one prong of the two high-profit-potential market segments she identified in 1986 with strategies and implementation plans in the highly decentralized, rapidly growing "pre-recorded videocassette" industry - for tertiary markets and superstores - was applied in the creation of Blockbuster Video.

Noted for identifying problems & opportunities, developing responsive strategies & teams, Margaret's asset/liability management computer model was used by the Regional Comptroller's Office as the model for banks (1978-82). The youngest female Senior Vice President in Southeastern Regional Banking in 1981, her corporate planning process was emulated (with photos) by Ernst & Whinney/Young's National Bank Consulting practice. Her fall 1985 turnaround plan was dubbed by Merrill Lynch Hubbard's CFO (Merrill Lynch's real property syndication arm) as "the best money we have ever spent."

Margaret was offered the position of Senior Advisor to the U.S. Comptroller of the Currency in 1984 after being informed she came in third for Nancy Teeter's replacement on the Federal Reserve Board.

Margaret Hampton’s Specialties:
