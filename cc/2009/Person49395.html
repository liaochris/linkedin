John Michitson’s Experience

  • Project Leader

    MITRE

    (Non-Profit; Information Technology and Services industry)

    August 1981 — Present (28 years 4 months)

    Served 20+ years as an engineer, project leader and resource manager at MITRE.

    I am currently leading a cross-functional team applying acquisition program and technical guidance across several programs to ensure interoperability. I am currently spearheading two internal research proposals that leverage open innovation and prediction markets for Federal Government markets.

    I was Project Leader for the USAF Net-centric Enterprise Solutions for Interoperability (NESI) team with a focus on net-centric IT and networking technical guidance for the warfighter’s tactical edge.

    I led an effort that identified the key functional requirements for the joint aerial layer network to serve as DoD’s airborne network backbone.

    I was the project leader for the Transformational Satellite Communications System (TSAT,) a major program to extend the Global information Grid (GIG) to space by adapting commercial networking protocols to meet the needs of highly mobile users in an unstable networking environment. I led a corporate-wide assessment of the TSAT network architecture.

    I also served as project leader for the Royal Saudi Air Force (RSAF) Command, Control, Communications, Computers and Intelligence (C4I) modernization study. The highlight of the study was definition of the kingdom-wide network architecture which introduced the RSAF to net-centric warfare.

    Upon returning to MITRE in 2002 after six years in the telecommunications industry, I helped to define the architecture for the Objective Gateway (OG), a key system needed to integrate existing data links (e.g. Link 16) with IP-based networks for net-centric operations.

    I also served as a resource manager for a large group of engineers.

  • Product Manager

    Lucent Technologies

    (Public Company; LU; Telecommunications industry)

    November 1997 — June 2002 (4 years 8 months)

    As a networking product manager in marketing and a system engineer in Bell Labs’ R&D organization, I gained extensive commercial networking experience at Lucent (now Alcatel-Lucent.) I helped to formulate and drive the integrated roadmap for Lucent’s portfolio of core and edge multi-service switches and led development of Lucent’s next generation WaveStar broadband network management and auto-discovery system.
