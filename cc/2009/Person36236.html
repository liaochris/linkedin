Graeme Gibson’s Summary

Graeme Gibson, CCP, MCSE, MCP+Internet, is president and CEO of Computers and Tele-Comm, Inc, (CTC) a leader in design of wireless networks and a well-respected and profitable Wireless ISP with years of experience. Gibson was trained at Duke University and the University of New York at Albany . In addition to his 25 years experience in the computer industry he also is formally trained in RF technologies, radio and television broadcasting. He is probably best known for his Books How to Build a Clone Computer and The Computer Repair Handbook . Those conversant with Microsoft's MCSE program will recognize him for his work on Windows NT, Tests Tricks & Technique , an Audio book covering test basics and best practices. Gibson is also well known for his 6-year run as the Host of the syndicated TV show Computers Today, as an actor, radio announcer and director, and as a contributing author to Computer User Magazine, Sysop News, FaxNews and The Kansas City Small Business Monthly.

Graeme Gibson’s Specialties:

Specialties include: Computers, Windows Servers, Network Design, Hosting, WiFi, WiMAX, RF Engineering, Aviation, Video and Photography.
