Laura Francis’s Summary

About me:
10 years experience in delivering web projects
Passionate about accessibility, usability and user experience
Proactive, able to take initiative to come up with creative, innovative solutions.
Experience of working within Prince2 environments with agile practices whilst satisfying clients requirements for Prince2 deliverables.
Excellent writing and editing skills with the drive and ambition to deliver
Understanding and empathy with client and user requirements

Laura has over 10 years experience designing and delivering digital projects. Most recently she has focussed on User Centred Design, Information Architecture, Interface Design, Usability and Accessibility on the web. Laura started out in marketing after gaining a degree in Business Studies in 1999, and found that digital channels were playing an increasingly important role in marketing at that time. This led her to focus on designing and delivering digital projects from websites and DVD's to interactive CD-ROM's.

Over the last 10 years Laura has mainly worked as an independent consultant with clients ranging from BECTA (British Educational Communications and Technology Agency) to Oxford University Press and from Future Publishing to CABI (a not-for-profit science-based development and information organization), specialising in working with publishers, educational organisations and not-for-profits.

Also experienced in making agile and user-centred methods work in a commerical environment, Laura has worked with clients to help them refine their internal processes, increase profitability and deliver to targets.

Laura Francis’s Specialties:

Usability, User Experience Design, User Interface Design, User Centred Design, persona creation, workshop facilitation, Information Architecture, project management, production management, agile, SCRUM, XP, XHTML, CSS, Accessibility, Web Standards, Marketing strategy and planning, advertising buying, print production.
