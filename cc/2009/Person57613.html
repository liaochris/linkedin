Lars Leafblad’s Summary

Lars joined KeyStone in 2007 after serving as Director of Development for the Humphrey Institute of Public Affairs at the University of Minnesota. He began his career in a leadership development program with GE Capital and subsequently spent two years helping launch Four51.com, a high-tech start-up, before returning to his alma mater, St. Olaf College, as interim assistant director of alumni and parent relations. He also spent two summers in college working as a member of the National Touring BBQ Team for Famous Dave's of America competing in BBQ competitions across the upper Midwest.

Lars earned a BA in Economics from St. Olaf College, and his MBA from the University of Minnesota's Carlson School of Management. There he was recognized with a "President's Student Leadership and Service Award" for his leadership contributions to the MBA student community. In May 2007, Lars was also recognized with a "40 Under 40" award from The Minneapolis-St. Paul Business Journal.

Lars serves on the board of directors of two Minnesota-based non-profits: CaringBridge (www.caringbridge.org) and The Salvation Army - Twin Cities (www.thesalarmy.org). He is also on the host committee of TEDxTC (www.tedxtc.com).

He is a member of the Citizens League (www.citizensleague.org), RESOLVE: The National Infertility Association (www.resolve.org), and participated in the 2008-09 Humphrey Institute Policy Fellows program at the University of Minnesota (www.tinyurl.com/hhhpolicyfellows).

Lars makes his home in Roseville with his wife Maren and their three children. In his free time, he enjoys spending time with his family, reading, creative writing, poker, cookin' up some mean barbeque and trying to become slightly less unhandy.

KeyStone Search is a Minneapolis-based retained executive search firm emphasizing cultural fit when filling executive level positions. In 2009, KeyStone was selected as a recipient of a Minnesota Business Ethics Award in the small business category.
