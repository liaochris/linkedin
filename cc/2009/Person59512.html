Lisa Sinclair’s Summary

Lisa is a professional web designer, with skills in CSS, HTML and Flash which she uses to create great sites. Her role as web designer for Tall Poppy Digital has also helped her to hone her graphic design skills, and she is proficient in the use of Adobe Photoshop.

Her skills in Copywriting have been put to great use in a recent AdWords campaign, and she is currently working on a website landing page for a Victorian small business.

Lisa also has experience in Copyediting, most notably on three issues of an Australian niche magazine, and also acted as editor, writer and copy editor of her own street magazine during 2004 and 2005.

In the Technical Writing sphere, Lisa has ten years experience, thus giving her a firm grounding in the technical and non-technical world. Lisa specialises in the creation and design of easy to use, user-friendly procedural materials, and has additional skills in the creation of all aspects of document creation and design, including print manuals, specification documents, business requirements and design documentation. She is well versed in the creation and setup of WIKI systems and also has expert knowledge of MS Office products.

Analysis could be said to be one of Lisa’s strongest skills, and her abilities in the breakdown of complex systems into easy to comprehend portions is evident in the documentation and diagrams she produces. These are all designed with a top-down philosophy, taking the whole -- the bigger picture -- and segmenting it into smaller, easier to understand pieces.

Past roles include a 9 month stint at Telstra during 2007 for the Transformation Project, 3 months at Sensis in 2006 for the disaster recovery project, and shorter-term contracts at DST International in 2006 and at MYOB in 2005.

She worked at Quest Software, as Systems Tester and then Technical Writer on various projects from 1999 to 2004.

She has an Australian and EU passport, allowing her to work both here in Australia and in Europe.
