Mitch Pirtle’s Summary

I started my career in hardware engineering at Apple Computer and then Sun Microsystems, and in the late 80s I became enamored with the challenges that occurred when you combined people with technology. As such I entered into software development and interface design, with a focus on emerging technologies - I wanted to have every possible avenue available to produce the best systems available. As soon as Web technologies were available, I'd completely walked away from the fat-client world of development and have focused on web application development ever since, including some high traffic sites such as ClassMates.com.

From the technical perspective I am most skilled and experienced working with high-traffic sites, with a strong development discipline that centers on sound implementation practices, performance and massive scaling, security, and code reuse.

I've since branched out to also cover both business and strategic areas, such as online marketing, brand strategy and product development.

However my real focus at present is bringing all of this varied experience together to promote emerging technologies to the non-technical, while still keeping my hands dirty in the implementation of said technologies. I enjoy the challenges of setting up development teams and starting companies.

Web technologies, database technologies, open source (industry and technologies), information security, product development, enterprise architecture planning, technologies/tools evaluation and research.
