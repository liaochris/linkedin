Brian Ritchey’s Summary

Brian actively practiced law for 7 years in a firm in Birmingham Alabama. He was recruited away from his practice early in his career to join a leading case management company and spent a year working as a liaison between attorneys and software developers to provide customized case management solutions to clients. He went back to private practice in 2000, but was lured away again to join the Juris team in 2005 where Brian used his knowledge of law firm dynamics to help partners, directors, shareholders and legal administrators manage their firms' practice through the utilization of technology, strategy and process. When Juris was purchased by LexisNexis, Brian accepted a position as chief contributing editor for the financial management blog More Partner Income. He also wrote and edited the practice management newsletter Managing Partner Advocate. Brian left LexisNexis in July, 2008 to start his own consulting company.

Brian is a speaker for the Managing Partner Forum, writes for several legal publications, and provides tailored benchmarking presentations and strategic planning guidance to Juris clients.

Brian obtained his J.D. from Cumberland School of Law after attending the University of Alabama for his undergraduate degree in English. He was admitted to the Alabama Bar in 1997, is licensed and in good standing with the Alabama Bar, the U.S. District Courts of Alabama for the Northern, Middle and Southern Districts as well as the 11th Circuit Court of Appeals.
