Corrado Giustozzi’s Summary

Official expert of the Criminal Court of Justice of Rome for computer crimes.

Member of the Scientific Committee of the UACI (Special Unit for the Analysis of Computer Crimes) of the italian Police.

Member of the Scientific Committee of the "Master ICT Security" at Scuola Superiore G. Reiss Romoli (l'Aquila).

Information security consultant for italian police forces and law-enforcing agencies such as:
- Comando Generale (General Headquarter) and Raggruppamento Operativo Speciale (Special Operations Group) of the Carabinieri (Military Police);
- Gruppo Anti Crimine Tecnologico (Hi-tech Crime Group) of the Guardia di Finanza;
- Polizia delle Telecomunicazioni (Computer-crime branch of italian Police).

Has taught “Information security” and “Technointelligence” in the italian Police Officers School at Nettuno (Roma).

As a professional scientific journalist he has always been active in popularizing the issues of computer security and digital citizenship. He has been featured in many radio and television programmes, and is a regular columnist on the magazine “Internet Magazine”. In the late ‘90s he has been founder and editor-in-chief of the italian edition of BYTE magazine (McGraw-Hill, USA).
