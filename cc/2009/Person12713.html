Bradford Frank’s Summary

I graduated from the University of Colorado with a BA in Chemistry and my MD degree. I completed two residencies, one in Family Practice and one in Psychiatry. I have a Master of Public Health (MPH) from Yale, where I studied infectious diseases, and an MBA from Northwestern's Kellogg School of Management.

I am board certified in Psychiatry, and am licensed to practice medicine in New York and Pennsylvania.

I am a Clinical Assistant Professor at the University of Buffalo School of Medicine and Biomedical Sciences, Buffalo, NY,

I am the author of numerous scientific articles. My latest articles are Psychotropic Medications and Informed Consent: A Review, in Annals of Clinical Psychiatry (2008), and A Review of Antioxidants and Alzheimer's Disease, published in the October-December 2005 issue of Annals of Clinical Psychiatry.

I am the author of numerous books, including: Rising Star: How You Can Become An Extremely Successful Person; Terror Unleashed: The Coming Bird Flu, Oil, Terrorist, and Financial Crises; and Bird Flu: Expert Advice to Help You and Your Family Stay Safe.

My latest book is, Mega Options For Physicians: How Doctors Can Achieve Their Dreams By Moving Into Non-Clinical Careers Or Becoming Entrepreneurs (2008).

I am a life-long learner and confirmed entrepreneur, and started TheDoctorZone.com in 2007 to help physicians who want to switch from clinical medicine into non-clinical careers or start new businesses. This is a membership-only Web site that features an interactive forum, interviews with both physician and non-physician experts, and a wide array of products and services.

My latest venture is investing in real estate.
