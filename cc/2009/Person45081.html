Jean-Florent Rérolle’s Summary

Jean-Florent Rérolle is a Managing Director at Houlihan Lokey, co-head of Financial Advisory Services in Europe (fairness opinions, valuation, board advisory services).
He is also on the finance experts' list of the Paris Court of Appeal (Expert près la Cour d'Appel de Paris en Finance).
From 1995 to 2004, he was a partner at Ernst & Young which he joined in 1991. Specializing in corporate finance, he assisted international companies in their M&A operations and performed complex business valuations and fairness opinions with an increasing emphasis on risk and value-based management and financial marketing. His last position at Ernst & Young was head of corporate governance services.
He is one of the founders and member of the board of the Institut Français des Administrateurs and of the Société Française des Evaluateurs ( French Society of Valuation Experts). He is a member of the International Valuation Profession Board (IVPB). He was a member of the AMF working group on fairness opinions.
Since 1994, Jean-Florent Rérolle has lectured in two major French Business schools (HEC and ESCP-EAP) on Corporate Finance and related topics (valuation and VBM). He is responsible for a Corporate Governance seminar at the Institut d'Etudes Politiques de Paris. He is also an author of many articles where he dealt with new valuations techniques, valuation in the connected economy, corporate governance and value based management issues. He co-authored the IFA "vade-mecum for board members".
He received his education at the Institut d'Etudes Politiques de Paris (1981) with an emphasis in Public Administration, Law and Economics. He got a Pre-doctoral degree in Defense Policy, at Paris I-Sorbonne (1982) and he holds the MBA of HEC Group (1991).
