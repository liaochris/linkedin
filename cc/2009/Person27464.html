Don Hamilton’s Summary

Don is Principle and General Manager of Nevastreet.com, Which provides custom management tools, corporate turnaround, moral building and coaching. See list of clients at donaldhamilton.com.

Don was President of Bridon Enterprises Inc. He managed and was VP and COO of Glabman Furniture. (http://glabman.com) He was voted by the Glabman family to the Board of Directors Glabman Furniture

He was also COO Richard MacDonald Fine Art. Where he built a custom Web based CMS (Customer Management System) eliminating costly VPN and fixed accounting and production problems
http://richardmacdonald.com

He was Founder of WWWiz corp., Publisher and Editor-in-Chief WWWiz magazine.

He was also Founder and Editor-in-Chief Microtimes Magazine

He founded Hamilton Multimedia Systems a maker of training system. International projects included training companies in Mexico that assembled airbags for most car models

Experience includes NATO Executive Secretary, 10 years weapons system development and interchangeability testing: expert in weapon systems and tactics

Producer and host of an hour-long weekly radio show on KUCI in Southern California, where movers and shakers in Internet-related businesses were interviewed. Guest expert and co-host for the television broadcast of “The Next Wave” with host, Leonard Nimoy - where high technology corporate guests were interviewed about their business ventures.

He has written 100s' of articles on many subjects like: Quantum Computing and Communication http://wwwiz.com/issue46/html/cover.html

He attended high school Verdun France. His Math and Physics education is from California Polytechnic State University Pomona, West Coast University and has a BS in Business Administration from University of La Verne.

Don Hamilton’s Specialties:

Successful real manager with experience in large companies and entrepreneurial startups. Wide spectrum experience with diverse cultures and businesses as diverse as art, furniture, publishing, engineering ...and NATO executive secretary.
