Bill Brenner’s Summary

I'm an experienced journalist, family man and history buff looking to use my writing and editing skills for the betterment of humanity. I'm driven by a passion to write stories that will help security professionals do their jobs, and consider myself blessed to be doing this work.

Along the way, I've gotten to work with outstanding, gifted writers, editors and bloggers: Derek Slater, Anne Saita, Jim Malone, Rob Westervelt, Dennis Fisher, Mike Mimoso, Gretchen Putnam and Jack Daniel, to name a few.

When I'm not obsessing about the next story, I immerse myself in family: my wife, Erin, our boys, Sean and Duncan, our church community (born Jewish, became Catholic), and history. I love traveling to Washington DC and anywhere else with historical significance. I'm also interested in health and wellness matters, especially the fight against mental illness.

If you want to share ideas for security stories or get in touch for no particular reason, please do.

Bill Brenner’s Specialties:

Writing, editing, management, design, public presentations/speaking, organizational expertise and accumulating many security and media contacts

Bill Brenner’s Experience

  • Senior Editor

    CSOonline and Magazine, part of IDG Enterprise

    (Online Media industry)

    April 2008 — Present (1 year 4 months)

    I've joined the team at CSO magazine and CSO online for a simple reason: The group churns out superb content that helps security professionals understand the risks and opportunities around them so they can do their jobs more effectively. I am thrilled to be here.

  • Senior News Writer

    TechTarget Security Media Group

    (Public Company; 501-1000 employees; Online Media industry)

    May 2004 — April 2008 (4 years)

    As senior news writer I focused on breaking news, in-depth analysis and multimedia projects for SearchSecurity.com and Information Security magazine. In this role, I wrote regular series and produced weekly podcasts dealing with cyber threats, be it terrorism or data thievery, computer vulnerabilities the bad guys are trying to exploit and technologies/policies the good guys use to fight back.

  • Night Editor

    The Eagle-Tribune

    (Newspapers industry)

    November 1999 — May 2004 (4 years 7 months)

    As night editor I was responsible for managing night-time newsroom operations. This involved sending reporters and photographers to the scene of breaking news, editing news that was filed in the evening and helping to plan coverage for the following days and weeks.

  • Editor/reporter

    Community Newspaper Company

    (Newspapers industry)

    May 1995 — November 1999 (4 years 7 months)

    I started as a reporter covering the Swampscott, Mass., school system for The Swampscott Reporter and went on to cover crime, government and community news for the Stoneham Sun. Then came a year of covering Lynn city politics for the Lynn Sunday Post and North Shore Sunday, followed by more than two years as editor of the Billerica Minuteman.
