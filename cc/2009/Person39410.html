Helvy Tiana Rosa’s Summary

I have been writing 40 book titles, mostly in Indonesia but some are translated into English, Arabic, Japan, German, France and Swedish language.

Books:
- Bukavu (Short Stories, Lingkar Pena Publishing House, 2008)
- Tanah Perempuan (Land of Women), a drama, Lapena, 2007.
- Risalah Cinta (Message of Love), inspiring stories, Lingkar Pena Publishing House, 2005.
- Lelaki, Kabut dan Boneka (the Man, Mist and the Dolls), a bilingual compilation of my selected short stories, Syaamil, 2002.
- Segenggam Gumam, Syaamil,2003.
- Wanita yang Mengalahkan Setan (the Woman who Defeated the Demons): analyzing the role of the demons in the works of Taufiq el-Hakim, an important contemporary Egypt writer, Tambora, 2002.
- Pelangi Nurani (a Rainbow of Hearts), compiled actual, inspiring stories, Syaamil
- Akira no Seisen/ Akira: Muslim wa Tashiwa, Syaamil, 2000.
- Pangeranku (My Prince), a compilation of stories for children, Syaamil, 2000.
- Manusia-Manusia Langit (the Men from Heaven), a compilation of selected short stories, published by Syaamil, 2000
- Titian Pelangi, a compilation of short stories, published by Mizan, 2000.
- Nyanyian Perjalanan (the Songs of the Journey), compilation of short stories, Syaamil, 1999.
- Hingga Batu Bicara (Until the Stone Talks), a compilation of short stories about the Palestinian struggle, 1999.
- Kembara Kasih (a Journey of Love), a novel published by Pustaka Annida, 1999
- Sebab Sastra yang Merenggutku dari Pasrah, a compilation of selected poems and short stories, Gunung Jati, 1999.
- Ketika Mas Gagah Pergi (When Brother Gagah Went Away). Compilation of short stories. First publication by Pustaka Annida, 1997
- Mc Alliester, published by Moslem Press, London, 1996, a fiction novel about how of a former CIA agent turned to be to a Muslim has to confront his own friends from Agency and also his old enemies.

  • Member

    Majelis Sastra Asia Tenggara (Mastera) - The South East Asia Literature Assembly

    (Writing and Editing industry)

    2005 — Present (4 years)

    The Assembly is a government-to-government cooperation to promote the development of literary works in Melayu language in Indonesia, Malaysia, Brunei and Singapore.

    To achieve its goal, the assembly is regularly giving award to promising writers as well as conduction writing workshops in the member countries.

  • Chairperson of the Board of Writers

    Forum Lingkar Pena (FLP)

    (Writing and Editing industry)

    2005 — Present (4 years)

    Forum Lingkar Pena is a non-governmental organization to help and drive people, especially teenagers as well as young men and women, to write.

    The forum is facilitating writing workshops, giving awards and also helping promising writers to find the publishers for their works.

    The active members are now more than 5,000 people across Indonesia as well as in some cities abroad.

    Dewan Kesenian Jakarta ( The Jakarta Arts Council)

    (Non-Profit; 11-50 employees; Arts and Crafts industry)

    2003 — 2006 (3 years)

    Dewan Kesenian Jakarta (The Jakarta Arts Council) is a non-profit organisation and partner of the Regional Government (DKI Jakarta) whose aim is to preserve, develop and give guidance and direction to the programs of the Jakarta Arts Centre (Taman Ismail Marzuki). The Jakarta Arts Council is the artistic and programming board of the Taman Ismail Marzuki (TIM), and expected to evaluate and present works by well-known and promising artists, including experimental works from all art forms.

  • Founder and General Chairperson

    Forum Lingkar Pena (FLP)

    (Non-Profit; 5001-10,000 employees; Writing and Editing industry)

    February 1997 — February 2005 (8 years 1 month)

    Forum Lingkar Pena (FLP) is a writers group who have more than 5,000 active members (2005) and have published more than 600 book titles together with about 40 publishers. FLP has branches in more than 125 cities across Indonesia and abroad.

  • Founder and Caretaker

    Rumah Cahaya- House of Light

    (Non-Profit; 51-200 employees; Libraries industry)

    2002 — 2005 (3 years)

    Rumah Cahaya is a community based library, where people, especially the poor ones, can read and learn to write. Cahaya stands for ‘baCA dan HAsilkan karYA’ means: read and create writing. Rumah Cahaya has branches in more than 20 cities across Indonesia.

  • Founder, Scriptwriter and Director

    Teater Bening

    (Non-Profit; 51-200 employees; Arts and Crafts industry)

    April 1990 — April 2003 (13 years 1 month)

    Teater Bening is the first all-female Islamic theatre Group in Indonesia. Until 2003 has performed 25 productions. Usually performs at University of Indonesia, Taman Ismail Marzuki Jakarta Arts Center and Jakarta Arts Building.

  • Chief Editor of 'Annida' teen magazine

    PT. Kimus

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Publishing industry)

    1998 — 2001 (3 years)

    Annida is a famous Islamic teen magazine in Indonesia. Almost all of its content are fiction (compilation of short story). I worked there since 1991 as a senior editor, and In 1998 as a Chief Editor.
