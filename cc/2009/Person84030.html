Rick Schroeder’s Summary

Rick has a wide depth and breadth of experience in Business Technology within the Insurance industry. He started in the development of applications then progressed to productivity and developer utilities (writing such tools as email and change control systems). Rick quickly moved into senior system design and analysis roles. For the last 15 years he has been working in various architecture roles including technology architecture, application architecture, and Enterprise Architecture (EA is concerned with the strategic alignment of IT activities to business goals).

Rick firmly believes that all significant IT activity should be strategically aligned to long-term business objectives, even when under pressure for short-term value delivery. Rick has extensive experience acting as IT Project architect / Solutions Architect, and working closely with project managers and sponsors to develop and implement projects that are indeed strategic, well implemented, and meet tactical objectives, in order to provide competitive business value.

Rick enjoys hands-on opportunities where he can use his experience and skills in the design and implementation of superior IT solutions. He has also contributed successfully in the roles of Solutions Architecture, Technology Architecture, and IT Project Architecture or leadership.

Strategy, Enterprise Content Management (ECM), Enterprise Records Management (ERM), Business Process Management (BPM), Systems Management, Technical Architect, Application Architect, Solutions Architect, Enterprise Architect, Solutions Design, IT Governance, Collaboration, Facilitation, Metrics, International experience.

    August 2001 — February 2009 (7 years 7 months)

    He designed and implemented the global IT Governance process, using BPM technology. This review process ensures the quality delivery of IT solutions and their alignment to approved architectures, compliance, security, and infrastructure technology.

    Rick has had many opportunities to work at project level as well as the strategic level. Opportunity to not only function on tactical technical and consultative support level, but to also develop methodologies, best practices and frameworks for other architects to consume. The rich experience gained in content management, collaboration, and records management projects and tools will be a strong asset for the future.
