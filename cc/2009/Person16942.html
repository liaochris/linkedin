Chuck Hansen’s Summary

Currently managing diversified investment portfolio of stocks, bonds, hedge funds, real estate, venture capital, and private equity. Former Chairman & CEO, Hansen Information Technologies, sold to Infor Global, a portfolio company of Golden Gate Capital, in June 2007.

Chuck Hansen’s Specialties:

Distressed Real Estate, Private Equity, Venture Capital, Digital Music.

Chuck Hansen’s Experience

  • Chairman & CEO

    Hansen Investment Holdings LLC

    (Venture Capital & Private Equity industry)

    June 2007 — Present (2 years 6 months)

    Manages portfolio of stocks, bonds, real estate, private equity, hedge funds, and venture capital investments.

  • Chairman & CEO

    Hansen Information Technologies

    (Privately Held; Computer Software industry)

    September 1986 — June 2007 (20 years 10 months)

    Founded in August 1983 by Robert, Scott, and Charles Hansen.

    Designed, developed, and marketed software products for state & local government markets. Offices in Auckland (New Zealand), Melbourne (Australia), London, Mississauga (Canada), with joint ventures in Japan and South Africa. Products including Asset Management, Building Permit, Property Tax, and Utility Billing, with major customers including the cities of New York City, Los Angeles, Chicago, Dallas, San Antonio, Seattle, Austin, Montgomery County, Fairfax County, Riverside County, and Caltrans.

    Acquired transportation sofware developer, Spear Technologies, in May 2006, to add LA Metro, NJ Turnpike Authority, NY Transit Authority immediately following private equity investment in April 2006 by Golden Gate Capital (San Francisco, CA).

    Acquired by Atlanta-based Infor Global in June 2007.

  • CFO

    Via Video

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; PLCM; Telecommunications industry)

    July 1984 — August 1986 (2 years 2 months)

    Computer animation and graphic processing. Sold to E.I. DuPont in 1986. Investors included Brentwood Associates, InterWest, John Hancock Venture Capital, Menlo Ventures, and Saugatuck Capital.

    MCORP

    (Public Company; 51-200 employees; Information Technology and Services industry)

    September 1982 — May 1984 (1 year 9 months)
