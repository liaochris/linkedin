Susan Tull’s Summary

High energy, masters-degreed broadcast journalist turned award-winning, strategic communications professional with proven ability to develop and direct integrated programs to establish industry leadership, accelerate revenue growth, influence public policy and/or protect organizational reputation. Experienced serving Fortune 500s, start-ups, professional service firms and nonprofits. Skilled advisor to senior executives. Strength in mentoring and building motivated teams that produce results.

Susan Tull’s Specialties:

Strategic planning, branding, message development, media relations, media training, social media, speaking platforms, industry analyst relations, executive communications, crisis communications, internal communications, business development, magazine publishing, Internet marketing, video production.

  • Founder and Principal

    Susan Tull Communications

    (Public Relations and Communications industry)

    July 2007 — Present (2 years 5 months)

  • Vice President of Marketing and Communications

    Orange Leap

    (Computer Software industry)

    March 2008 — March 2009 (1 year 1 month)

    Launched Orange Leap’s first in-house marketing communications and public relations function to reposition company in the nonprofit sector – from a proprietary, constituent relationship management (CRM) software vendor serving the Christian ministry vertical to a leading CRM provider for all nonprofits, offering the only mature, nonprofit-specific open source solution.

    Co-managed a complete company rebranding that included development and rollout of new name, logo, messaging and Web site. Also introduced several major new products. Executed integrated program of traditional and online media relations, speaking platforms, advertising and executive communications to support these efforts.

    In only 12 months, cultivated relationships with top tier trade journalists and bloggers through frequent issuance of news releases, one–on-one briefings and ongoing pitches, resulting in nearly 80 published stories, including two cover stories and seven contributed articles. Within first few months, company was regularly spotlighted in trade media round-up coverage as a leading nonprofit CRM software vendor. Also placed company executives and clients as thought-leading speakers at eight nonprofit conferences and Webinars hosted by the industry’s most influential, third party, professional associations and media organizations.

    Oversaw development and implementation of two print and online trade media advertising campaigns executed by outside agencies.

  • Vice President, Corporate Communciations

    Convio

    (Privately Held; Computer Software industry)

    April 2001 — September 2006 (5 years 6 months)

    As management team member, helped drive growth of venture-funded, on-demand CRM software company for nonprofits – from $2.4M in ‘02 revenues to $13.3M in ‘05 (~450 percent increase). Profiled by PR Week on successful, 18-month, publicity project spotlighting company's work with Howard Dean's presidential nomination campaign, http://budurl.com/PRWeek.

    Elevated Convio’s profile with integrated communications programs showcasing technology innovation, thought leadership and corporate viability. Led team in annually generating: 200 media stories; ~36 contributed articles; and 30-plus speaking engagements, including keynotes. News coverage included stories by AP, BBC, Bloomberg Markets, Business Week, Forbes, NPR, New York Times, Time, Wall Street Journal, Washington Post, Wired.com and key trade media.

    Worked closely with CXO-level executives and legal counsel on crisis communications to reassure customers and protect corporate reputation during work force reductions, litigation and public criticism of controversial customer relationships. Conceived of and launched successful industry coalition to thwart litigious competitor’s patent strategy.

  • National Director of Public Relations

    Mothers Against Drunk Driving

    (Non-Profit; Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    August 1997 — September 1999 (2 years 2 months)

Campaigns often involved working with White House, congressional leaders, federal and state agencies, and celebrities.

    Directed production of publications and collateral, including annual reports and brochures. Provided oversight to cross-functional team handling multi-media PSA campaigns (TV, radio, Internet). Launched and was editor-in-chief of Driven, MADD's semi-annual magazine (circ.: 1,000,000). Ensured consistent branding, messaging and graphic standards in all materials.

    Led project to update MADD’s logo for first time in the organization's 20-year history to strengthen branding and reflect growing emphasis on youth and ethnic markets.

    Supervised team in generating ongoing positive coverage by national media including Associated Press, Reuters, United Press International, New York Times (see Sunday Magazine cover story at http://budurl.com/vwee), Washington Post, Wall Street Journal, Time, ABC, NBC, CBS, CNN, CNBC and MSNBC.

    Counseled and provided tactical support to MADD’s national president, executive director, board members and 600+ local affiliates.
