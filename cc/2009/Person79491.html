Philip Redding’s Experience

  • Managing Consultant

    Endor

    (Privately Held; Information Technology and Services industry)

    March 2009 — Present (5 months)

    Endor are experts in finding people and forming relationships with them, and have 4 service lines:

    1. Network services
    We form nice communities and bring the members of these communities together in order that they develop valuable relationships based on trust and respect. Our recruitment team (see below) often turn to these networks for assistance in finding elusive candidates, and we offer sponsorship packages to organisations wishing to gain introduction to our network communities.

    Our controlled network service includes a ‘Study Tour’ for IT Managers, where small groups are introduced over a 2 day period to numerous senior CIO’s in a study tour arrangement, experiencing intimate and formal presentations on structure; business alignment; outsourcing; and the general management of a corporate IT function.

    2. Recruitment services
    We concentrate on positions that cannot often be filled via advertising on Internet job boards, as we believe that our clients can save large sums by filling some positions themselves. We rarely advertise ourselves, and prefer a rifle shooting approach to proactively identifying candidates via networking (see above) and search.

    3. Payroll Service.
    As we believe that organisations can now address a large percentage of their recruitment needs directly, we offer payroll services either to organisations who require ‘distance’ between their company and an external contractor, or to individuals who have secured a contractual engagement with an organisation and require a payroll service. Our services in this area are supplied via our association with an established global payroll organisation.

    4. Business Communication Training for IT professionals
    Via an association with an established NLP Master Practitioner, we facilitate and provide either customised corporate training programs or pay-per-head public programs, which improve the business communication skills of IT professionals.
