  • Validation Engineer

    CIMA Labs

    (Public Company; 201-500 employees; Pharmaceuticals industry)

    October 2003 — Present (5 years 10 months)

    I write, execute, and report on equipment qualifications (lab and manufacturing) as well as cleaning, software, and process validations. Our Validations group does everything but analytical method validation. Process validations are my favorite; I like seeing all the pieces come together and my production experience serves me well. I held the title of Associate Validation Engineer from May '06 to December '07.

    I held the positions of Operator and then Lead Operator from October '03 to April '06. As a Lead Operator I lead a group of operators and temporaries in keeping a production line humming along. Trained them to do their jobs better and to work better as a team. I learned to trust my gut and I learned to balance trusting others' training with overestimating their common sense.

    CIMA Labs specializes in orally disintegrating tablet (ODT) and oral transmucosal (OTM) drug delivery technologies. We make tablets melt in your mouth, not in your stomach.

    http://cinna.mn - "32 flavors of Minnesota (and then some)"
    I created cinna.mn to be a place for discussing the sometimes strange experience of being a person of color in schizophrenically progressive - but still very white - Minnesota and other minority experiences. cinna.mn debuted in October 2008.

    The Dish, established in 2001, became a part of cinna.mn in December 2008. The Dish is a free, grassroots service to provide information about things of interest for queer women, especially queer women of color and friends, and to build community and connections. http://cinna.mn/dish

    http://fresh.mn - "twincityblogging"
    Writing on life in the Twin Cities. A continuation of my 3+ years of work at the Minneapolis Metblog. fresh.mn debuted in November 2008.
