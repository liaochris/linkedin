Oliver Kirchhoff’s Experience

  • Camera TD

    Scanline VFX GmbH

    (Privately Held; Motion Pictures and Film industry)

    January 2009 — Present (7 months)

    Live action footage has to be tracked for the perfect matchmove. This includes water surfaces from a boat, helicopter shots, green screen actor shots and object trackings.

  • Teacher

    The Animation Workshop

    (Educational Institution; Animation industry)

    May 2009 — May 2009 (1 month)

    Some students here at "The Animation Workshop" need to learn how to light a scene in Maya, render it out in passes and compose it with a live action footage. These guys are not absolut beginners. They already know how to model, texture and rig a creature. But they don't know the tricks. I'll show 'em.

  • 3D Artist

    Fish Blowing Bubbles

    (Graphic Design industry)

    August 2008 — September 2008 (2 months)

    The Toyota IQ mini car had to be visualized photorealistic for a convention presetation. I got a CAD model which I textured, shaded, lit, animated and rendered out in many passes so the compositor have maximum flexibility...

  • 3D Generalist

    ARRI Digital

    (Motion Pictures and Film industry)

    March 2008 — July 2008 (5 months)

    I worked on two feature films. "Killing Is My Business, Honey" is a comedy with extensive use of virtual stunts. "The Baader Meinhof Complex" has more subtle effects. In both I did mainly particle effects with 3ds max and ParticleFlow.

  • Lighting / Shading TD

    Trixter

    (Privately Held; Animation industry)

    December 2006 — March 2008 (1 year 4 months)

    I introduced Pixar's Photorealistic Renderman to the render pipeline of Trixter. Me and my collegues had to develop tools and scripts to streamline the workflow with RMan. At the end we could render all needed passes with the click of a button.
    I also did the lighting of many shots besides my coding work.
    Escape Studios have released an article about my work, which you can find here: http://www.escapestudios.com/en_GB/technology/news/trixter-renderman.html . The topmost picture at that web page was also lit by me :-)

  • Technical Director

    Trixter Film GmbH

    (Privately Held; Animation industry)

    2006 — 2007 (1 year)

    I this period I worked on many smaller project like commercials, but also on the feature film "The Secret of Loch Ness" with a CG main actor called "Oki". For that project I developted a lighting and rendering tool for Maya to streamline the, otherwise very time consuming render passes creation process.

  • 3D Designer and TD

    Plazamedia

    (Privately Held; Media Production industry)

    Plazamedia is a big player in the german tv business. They develop and support the on-air effects for many tv stations. I worked as 3d and compositing consultant for several projects. One bigger project was the creation of a whole company presentation DVD with movies, animated 3d charts and animated DVD menus.

  • Lighting TD

    CA Scanline

    (Privately Held; Motion Pictures and Film industry)

    April 2006 — April 2006 (1 month)

    Hui Buh is a semitransparent CG cartoon ghost character. He had to be integrated into live action footage. Since he was the main actor in this movie there were many shots to do. I worked in the lighting department for this project.

  • VFX Supervisor

    Trixter

    (Privately Held; Animation industry)

    December 2005 — March 2006 (4 months)

    Well, at least the VFX were really nice. Besides supervision I also modeled and textured half of the main character. I corrected some animations and some compositings and matchmoved all shots.

  • Camera TD

    Weta Digital

    (Privately Held; Motion Pictures and Film industry)

    May 2005 — October 2005 (6 months)

    King Kong 2005 was the project, and I worked in Weta's camera department. There we did matchmovings, motion capturing, Kuper motion control simulations and roto animations. We used 3D-Equalizer for matchmoving.

  • 3D Artist

    ARRI Digital

    (Privately Held; Motion Pictures and Film industry)

    November 2004 — January 2005 (3 months)

    I did a full CG shot for the feature film "Sophie Scholl" and some scene addons for other shots. This shot showed the Nazi headquater in Munich in which Sophie Scholl was murdered. That building was totally destroyed by the allies bombing. So I had to recreate it and it's surrounding in 3d.

  • 3D Artist

    Das Werk

    (Privately Held; Motion Pictures and Film industry)

For "Das Wunder von Bern" I learned Maya which has become my main tool by now.
