    Robert Allen Mansfield (born January 28th, 1971) is an American success story in the making.

    Robert is currently serving in the Pennsylvania Army National Guard as a Transportation Sergeant, and is running as a Republican Candidate for Governor for the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania 2010.

    Robert Mansfield upholds the traditions and patriotism that his oath into the military bestowed on him; to serve with dignity, to care for and offer solace and comfort to the downtrodden and those in despair, to confront tyranny, despotism, discrimination and prejudice and to live a life of faith, honesty and fairness to all with whom he comes into contact with.

    Surviving years of childhood poverty and abuses, foster and state domiciles and youth homelessness, Robert pushed himself through early education to receive a GED and join the military. Quickly rising through the enlisted ranks, Robert was deployed to Iraq in December 2006 as a Non-Commissioned Officer in Transportation. One year later, Robert was diagnosed with type II diabetes and subsequently with cancerous kidney tumors.

    Surviving operations and treatment, Robert remains proud of his service in uniform, of those who supported him during his trials and of his country he so proudly served and continues to serve.

    Once again, Robert is heeding a call to duty as a Constitutional Traditionalist, a Stout Conservative and a lifelong supporter, defender and member of the Republican Party
