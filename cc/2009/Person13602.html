Brian Kurth’s Summary

Brian Kurth (www.briankurth.com) is the founder of VocationVacations (www.vocationvacations.com) and is a nationally-recognized career coach/strategist, professional speaker and author. He has appeared on CNBC, CNN, Fox News, Microsoft's Small Business Summit (live), MSNBC and NBC’s TODAY Show (live). Brian's career advice has been featured in articles in BusinessWeek, Entrepreneur Magazine, Fast Company Magazine, Fortune Magazine, Men's Journal, The New York Times; O, The Oprah Magazine; and The Wall Street Journal, just to name a few.

After a successful corporate career in Chicago, he moved to Portland, Oregon and founded VocationVacations, the only company of its kind that allows people to experience their dream job for 2-3 days. Brian is the author of “Test-Drive Your Dream Job – A Step-By-Step Guide to Finding and Creating the Work You Love” (Hachette, 2008) and is a sought-after speaker.

The concept for VocationVacations was born on Chicago's Kennedy Expressway in 1999 during one of Brian’s long, daily commutes. He wondered what it would be like to be a wine maker, a tour guide or a dog trainer. A business idea was born. VocationVacations was launched in January 2004.

Before launching VocationVacations, Brian spent more than a decade in the corporate world which included living and working abroad in Tallinn, Estonia and Budapest, Hungary; working his way up in product marketing within the telecommunications industry with AT&T and Ameritech; and finally becoming the director of business development for a dot-com.
