Nadine B. Hack’s Summary

beCause President Nadine B. Hack is recognized internationally for her proven expertise in effective strategic action plans, creative problem solving, innovative partnerships, insightful policy analysis and politically sensitive negotiations. For over three decades Hack has advanced the mission and values of individuals and organizations from all sectors, marrying vision with concrete actions. Her specialties include developing complex multi-sector strategic alliances in a globalized world; managing reputations in a socially-conscious society; and forging new presences in specific communities, whether by region or market segmentation.

Nadine B. Hack’s Experience

  • President and CEO

    beCause Global Consulting

    (Management Consulting industry)

    1997 — Present (12 years )

    beCause Global Consulting provides advisory services for corporations, foundations, governments, nonprofits and individuals on:
    • Cause-related strategies;
    • Philanthropic initiatives;
    • Transformative organizational leadership;
    All work is designed to advance organizations' mission and values.

  • Professor

    NYU Wagner Graduate School of Public Affairs

    (Higher Education industry)

    May 2002 — July 2009 (7 years 3 months)

    At request of university administrators created and teaches annual graduate course "Global Development Strategies: Internal and External Capacity Building" initially offered to international program degree students and then expanded to graduate students university-wide

  • The Commissioner

    NYC Commission for United Nations, Consular Corps & International Business

    (Government Administration industry)

    January 1992 — April 1994 (2 years 4 months)

    Served as NYC's senior foreign service officer and official liaison with the world's largest international diplomatic and business communities. Provided strategic policy direction, liaison functions and direct services to these constituencies, as well as to city, state and federal government, businesses and individuals affected by their presence; managing myriad complex dynamics at the interstices.

  • Contributing participant

    U.S. Presidential Foreign Policy Transition Team

    (Government Relations industry)

    November 1992 — April 1993 (6 months)

    Created a series of briefing memos for the incoming U.S. President and his senior foreign policy team on the United Nations system globally, with particular focus on agencies - and key positions within them - of strategic value to the US. Focused on the U.S. Mission to the UN, the U.S. State Department's Office of International Organizations, the UN Secretariat, special UN Commissions, Specialized UN Agencies and other UN entities globally where there was strategic value to have a U.S. presence.

  • President

    Sister City Program, City of New York

    (Government Agency; 201-500 employees; Government Administration industry)

    January 1991 — December 1991 (1 year )

    Created and maintained mutually beneficial exchange programs - technical, cultural, economic, educational and sports - between New York and its sister cities worldwide, among them Beijing, Budapest, Cairo, Jerusalem, Johanessburg, Madrid, Rome, Santo Domingo, San Juan and Tokyo.

  • Consultant

    Freelance

    (Management Consulting industry)

    January 1975 — December 1990 (16 years )

    Has advised three UN Secretaries-General, Heads of State including Nelson Mandela and Corazon Aquino as well as numerous senior government officials in the U.S. and abroad. Corporations worked with include Aprica Kassai Industries, Cisco Systems, Coca-Cola, Chevron Texaco, Deutsche Telecom, Fujitsi Siemens and Reebok International. Foundations and other civil society organizations worked with include Doctors of the World, Ford Foundation, Lawyers Committee for Human Rights, Ford Foundation, Global Citizens Circle and Ms Foundation.
