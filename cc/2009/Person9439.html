Barbara Walters Price’s Summary

I currently serve as the Senior Vice President of Marketing of Mercer Capital. I am also a member of the firm’s board of directors, the only non-fee earner on the board.

Mercer Capital is an employee-owned business valuation and investment banking firm serving a national and international client base. We are one of the largest business valuation firms in the nation serving both private and public companies, as well as high net-worth families. In addition, we are the premier valuation firm for financial institutions. Services provided include valuation for financial reporting, gift & estate tax valuation, litigation support, corporate valuation, ESOP valuation, as well as transaction advisory services, among others. Visit www.mercercapital.com for more information..

My career began with Mercer Capital and I have been with the firm for over 20 years. I have helped the firm grow from two people (me and the owner) to one of the most respected firms in our profession.

I am responsible for the firm's marketing strategy and for overseeing all marketing functions of the firm, including corporate communications, web site development, speaking engagements, product development, newsletters, e-newsletters, direct marketing, web and live seminars, and business development, among others.

In addition to my marketing responsibilities, I led the firm in developing a strategic plan in 2007 and currently oversee the implementation of that strategic plan.

I publish and speak frequently on the topic of professional services marketing and leadership, with a specialty in firms providing business valuation services, and provide other professional service firms with marketing and product development advice.

For more detailed information, see the detail in the Experience section below.

I live in Memphis, Tennessee with my husband and two daughters.

    1991 — 1998 (7 years )

    During these years as Director of Marketing, we formalized the marketing function of the firm. I led or supported all marketing activities. Marketing and business development was concentrated on newsletter communications, direct mail, website, personal calling, speaking, publishing in external publications, and e-newsletters, among others.
