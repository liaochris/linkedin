Amit Garg’s Summary

Building a career at the intersection of technology, medicine and business. Deeply committed towards service.

[professional] Google, Online Operations Business Analyst, Apr 06 - today
· Trusted advisor to key teams at Google. I help managers understand the business and my work directly impacts ~50% of core revenue (currently at ~$11 billion).
· Leading various reporting initiatives in our business intelligence platform, which is used by ~1,000 people.
· Supported a major reorganization of North American unit which affected ~600 people. My role was wide-encompassing and went from defining scope to actually executing.
· Received a Gold Award twice recognizing my contributions.

[professional] Google, Associate Product Manager, Jun 04 - Apr 06
· Led a team of engineers to launch a new index for Google Images with over a billion entries, making it the largest free online repository of images.
· Worked with a cross-functional team to take various of our products to international markets. One highlight is taking our photo-sharing software from 1 language to 39 in less than a year.
· In my spare time made Google into a registrar so we could improve search results. This made headlines in the news and was recognized in front of the company.

[leadership]
· Co-founded BookBox, whose mission is “a book for every child in every language”, after winning a social entrepreneurship challenge at Stanford.
· Worked in orphanages in Brazil, taught in rural India, trained volunteers in subsequent years, raised funds to build a library and a laboratory in a village.
· Worked for over 2 years in residential staff teams for college and high school students. Recipient of the James Lyons Award for leadership and service to the Stanford community.
· Co-founded a program to teach computer skills to disadvantaged high-schoolers. We graduated ~30 students.
· As a Claude Buss Fellow hosted ~60 East Asian students at Stanford, being their residential and academic mentor.
