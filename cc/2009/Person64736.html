    The Municipal Employees' Retirement System (MERS) is a statewide Michigan retirement plan and tax-qualified trust that municipalities may adopt for their employees. MERS offers employee benefit programs: defined benefit, defined contribution and hybrid plans, and group insurance products. MERS serves cities, counties, hospitals, libraries, medical care facilities, road commissions, townships, villages, and similar units of local government.

    MERS operated under state government from 1945 until 1996, when MERS became an independent non-profit public corporation. MERS members total approximately 700 municipalities and more than 75,000 individual members and retirees
