Dorothy Wetzel’s Summary

Dorothy Wetzel has been innovating in the consumer health care arena for over 25 years.

As VP, Consumer Marketing at Pfizer Pharmaceuticals, she worked on blockbuster brands such as Lipitor, Viagra, Zoloft, and Celebrex. She also launched several enterprise-wide platforms, including the RM program “Pfizer for Living,” which is featured in a Kellogg School of Management case study. Additionally, she drove the development of the award-winning Hispanic health program, Sana La Rana and the widely acclaimed Pfizer Health Literacy initiative.

Joining the Agency world in 2006, Ms. Wetzel served as the Senior Vice President, Management Supervisor on AstraZeneca for Saatchi & Saatchi Healthcare Communications, as well as the Publicis Healthcare Communications Group’s AstraZeneca Navigator. Staying within Publicis Healthcare, she took the helm of Glow Worm, a consumer healthcare agency as EVP, Managing Director.

She has recently co-founded Extrovertic, a new kind of Marketing and Communications agency with Mark Drossman, formerly Glow Worm ECD. Extrovertic helps clients strategically infuse social media and other non-traditional solutions into their marketing mix. Extrovertic offers integrated solutions such as "Amped Advocacy" a "pharma safe" social media program that builds strong brand advocates.

Ms. Wetzel was named one of the 20 Most Influential People in DTC by Medical Marketing & Media, and was inducted into the Hall of PhAME in 2004.
