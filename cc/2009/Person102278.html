Vaughn Bullard’s Summary

I'm a sort of Jack-of-all-trades. I've been in basically every type of development and IT management position over the past 18+ years. I've actually been programming professionally and non-professionally since the early 80s. I've had exposure to basically all modern and "ancient" development methodologies, languages and operating systems. In addition, I write and edit technical books and articles in English, German and Italian.

I'm also a Mac OS fanatic, "eco-Freak", Standards editor, Open Source supporter and contributor. I'm an "idea man" and a quick study on most things technical. I've got an entrepreneurial spirit as well-I was the kid with the lemonade stand, lawn care business and ran two successful businesses as an adult.

I grew up in Europe, but graduated high school in Chicago. This was because I was a military brat, raised by an US Army Officer father and DoD civilian accountant mother, in Germany. I come from a long lineage of military men. I still have relatives in Germany that I visit often. As a result, I have learned many languages and experienced many cultures. I have traveled extensively across the world (My passport is actually full!).

My somewhat offbeat and atypical background affords me an ability to approach problem solving unconventionally. I have never been a "Yes Man" and always give my clients an honest opinion, whether it is the popular opinion or not. Although quite introverted and shy in my personal life, I am outgoing professionally with no problems speaking to small groups or in front of hundreds of people.

In my spare time (with what little I have :) ), I enjoy playing the guitar, piano, shooting firearms, landscape architecture, watching movies and cooking fanciful dishes. I enjoy posting articles about technology on my blog as well. I have a strong commitment to fitness and nutrition, running an average of 35 miles per week.

The Summit of the 8 brings the heads of state of the world’s largest economies together to set global economic policy. EUROWEB allowed analysts in the field to get up-to-the-minute information using Web-based technologies.
