Keith Teare’s Summary

I am a British and US dual Citizen with homes in Palo Alto, California and in Cape Town, South Africa, the land of my wife, Gené. I spend significant time in the US, Europe and Africa each year.

I have founded or co-founded several companies since the early 1980s. Up until 1998 these were UK based entities. Since then they have been Silicon Valley based ventures. My resume chronicles these.

I have built teams that created companies that were valued by others at more than $1 billion (EasyNet and RealNames);

I have been involved in client based companies that are small but remain consistent in their support of their clients to this day(cScape and NetNames);

I have innovated (CYBERIA - the world's first Internet Cafe and franchised or expanded to Thailand, Japan, France, Ireland, The Philippines and elsewhere; RealNames, the first multi-lingual addressing system for the Internet; TechCrunch, the worlds first and biggest technology blog).

I have failed (edgeio - the first aggregator and syndicator of Classified Ads on the Internet - sold to LookSmart in 2007; ultimately RealNames, when Microsoft discontinued service to boost short term revenues).

I am proud of my successes and my failures. I learned a great deal from each.

I have had to deal with partners, customers; investors; bankers; Governments; Organizations and Institutions.

I have served on many boards (cScape, EasyNet, CYBERIA, NetNames, NetBenefit, RealNames, SnapNames, edgeio, TechCrunch).

I have managed small teams of 2 or 3 and large teams of over 300, with zero revenues, or with revenues over $30m a year.

But, above all else, I have been privileged to work with great people whilst doing what I love - making change a positive experience.
