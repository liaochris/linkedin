Eric Vyncke’s Experience

  • Distinguished System Engineer

    Cisco Systems

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; CSCO; Computer Networking industry)

    January 1997 — Present (12 years 11 months)

    Eric works for Cisco by helping customers with security design and by assisting product design (notably security). He participates regularly at the IETF (author of RFC 3585) as well as other conferences like RSA.
    He wrote the 'LAN Security' book in Cisco Press
    He holds a CISSP certification.

  • Guest Professor

    HEC

    (Educational Institution; 10,001 or more employees; Education Management industry)

    September 1986 — Present (23 years 3 months)

    In 2006-2007, two lectures:
    1) Outils d'Analyse des Bases et Banques de Données: SQL, PHP, security, multi-media
    2) Sécurité de l'Information: security from physical to network including risk management

  • Project Manager

    Siemens Nixdorf

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; Computer Networking industry)

    1992 — December 1996 (4 years )

    Acting both as a consultant (mainly for European Commission) as well as development manager/project manager/architect of several projects including: subscriber management for a mobile operator, secure military message handling system (smart card, X.400, ACP.127 gateway), call center, firewall

  • R&D Supervisor

    NRB

    (Privately Held; 501-1000 employees; Computer Networking industry)

    September 1986 — 1992 (6 years )

    Eric worked as the head of R&D with projects like developing an automated network management system with the help of Artificial Intelligence, application gateway between different protocols (like Digital LAT over Ethernet to Token Ring). He also managed to team who was the first to deploy a multi-protocol network (DECnet, bridging, IP and AppleTalk) in Belgium with Cisco routers running 8.3.

  • Research Assistant

    Université de Liège

    (Educational Institution; 10,001 or more employees; Education Management industry)

    September 1983 — August 1986 (3 years )

    Eric worked on a European Commission funded project called ESPRIT BWN. He developped and simulated a new data link layer which pre-dates the FDDI.
    He also developped an Ethernet interface (based on existing hardware) for a Digital TOPS-20 mainframe including device driver, kernel routines and XNS stack.
