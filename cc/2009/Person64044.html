Martin Duval’s Summary

Currently CEO of bluenove (www.bluenove.com) and still supports the 'Orange Start Up Programme' (Orange Group) he has created in 2006 to develop partnerships with start-ups and venture capital funds with focus on WEB 2.0 and convergence (web and mobile)
From 2001 to 2006 at Orange, he headed different management positions in the areas of innovation and business development. For the las 3 years as Bus.Dev. Director, he launched various projects most of them being Woldwide or European premieres such as the emergency geolocation services for Senior customers (eg. Alzheimer bracelet from Medical Intelligence), the M-coaching quit-smoking sms program, the sms Juke Box or the Bluetooth interactive windows implemented in the Orange stores.
In addition since end of 2004, he managed the fast growing Mobile Music activities within Orange France, including the launch of the Music Portal and the Full Track Dowload services, thus developing strong relationships and partnerships with the Majors.
From 2001 to 2003, he managed the Orange owned early-stage investment fund (Inventmobile) focusing on innovative mobile services and technical solutions: among them was Mobivillage valued from 1,5 M€ in 2001 to 15M€ when acquired by the japanese company For Side in 2004.
He has been in the area of high-tech early-stage financing since the beginning of 2000: first as COO of a French early-stage fund and then as Managing Director the Swedish fund Speed Ventures in France.
Prior to that, he spent 3 years in Consulting as Senior Project Manager in the Management Consulting division of Cambridge Technology Partners in France.
From 1996 to 1997, he was International Business Development Manager for the Accor Group.
He started his carrier in 1990 in the aerospace industry with Eurocopter (EADS Group) first in Purchasing in the USA (Dallas) and later as Area Sales Manager of Commercial helicopters in Northern Europe before to start-up his own business in aircraft financing services.
