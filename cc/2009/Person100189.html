Tom Weishaar’s Summary

As a serial entrepreneur, I've successfully managed small companies through all phases of the life cycle from start up to shut down.

As a corporate professional, I've worked with executives to provide leadership support in the areas of internal communications, strategy development, and both management and new-hire training. I have internalized the continuous improvment process and know how to evaluate and improve initatives and processes. I've managed startup workgroups and I have successfully managed workgroups remotely.

Tom Weishaar’s Specialties:

All forms of printed, spoken, and online communications; continuous improvement initiatives; development and implementation of strategic plans; process improvement; development of frontline managers; management of fast-growing startup groups.

    Home By Myself

    (Writing and Editing industry)

    May 1981 — December 1984 (3 years 8 months)

    I wrote software for the Apple II computer that was published by Beagle Bros (Frame-Up and ProntoDOS) and worked as a freelance consultant and writer.

  • Columnist

    Softalk magazine, Softalk Publishing Inc.

    (Program Development industry)

    1983 — August 1984 (1 year)

    I wrote SoftTalk's DOStalk column, which covered the Apple II's operating systems, DOS 3.3 and ProDOS, in fine detail. Columns were anthologized in two books, The DOStalk Scrapbook (written with Bert Kersey) and ProDOS Inside and Out (written with Dennis Doms).

  • Managing Editor

    Commodity News Service

    (Online Media industry)

    November 1976 — April 1981 (4 years 6 months)

    CNS published commodity prices and news using modems and a nationwide phone network before there was such a thing as the internet.
