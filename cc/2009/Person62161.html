Marcia Stepanek’s Summary

An award-winning, multimedia news journalist with byline credits in national consumer, specialty, and business magazines, online publications, and radio/TV--including NPR, PBS, Hearst, McGraw-Hill, msnbc.com, and Salon.com. Also the founding editor-in-chief of Contribute Media, a magazine, Web site, and events/conferences series, "New Conversations for Change," that covers social entrepreneurs and the new philanthropy movement; also creator of Cause Global, a news blog that covers the use of social media to affect social change globally that appears regularly on JustMeans.org and SSIR.org, the website of the Stanford Social Innovation Review. Distinguished honors include: Finalist/Pulitzer Prize for Public Service; George Polk Award for Consumer Writing; National Press Club Award for Washington Correspondence; New York Newswomen's Club Front Page Award for Internet Commentary, Clarion Award for Investigative Reporting, American Business Press Magazine Editor's Gold Awards for Editing in 2003 and 2004, among others. Also was a 1995-96 John S. Knight Fellow at Stanford University and a Jefferson Fellow in Asian Studies at the East-West Center at the University of Hawaii, specializing in Japan and China.

Marcia Stepanek’s Specialties:

Specialist in journalism with an investigative bent on stories about innovation—chiefly social media, information technology, philanthropy, business, the work place, and change management.
