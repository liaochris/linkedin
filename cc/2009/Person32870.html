Federico Bordonaro, Ph.D.’s Summary

I am a geopolitical analyst with solid expertise in the following areas:

- European security and the ESDP ;
- European energy security;
- The Black Sea-Caspian region, with a particular focus on energy security, energy policy of regional states, frozen conflicts, and the geopolitical and social dynamics in Ukraine, Moldova, Georgia, and Azerbaijan ;
- Russo-Western strategic relations
- The Darfur conflict and the geopolitics of Sudan and Chad, as well as its implications for US and European security strategies ;
- Pakistan's geopolitics and the security issues affecting the Indian Ocean region and the Afghan-Pakistani border..

***********************************************************************
SEE my latest publication here: http://tinyurl.com/geopolitica
***********************************************************************

I regularly teach geopolitical analysis at COESPU's programme for peacekeeping studies (http://www.carabinieri.it/Internet/Coespu/), with special focus on Sudan and the Darfur question, European security, Afghanistan, Iraq, and Asymmetrical threats.

I teach geopolitics at Rome's university "La Sapienza" in the framework of the Master programme in "Geopolitica e Sicurezza globale", see http://www.uniroma1.it/studenti/laureati/master/scheda.php?cod=05242&fac=340

I am lecturer of East European history at Rome's university "La Sapienza" for the academic year 2009-2010.

Federico Bordonaro, Ph.D.’s Specialties:

Geopolitical analysis, country risk analysis, conflict analysis, energy security, Russo-Western strategic relations, teaching.
