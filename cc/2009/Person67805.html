Michael Weisel’s Summary

My legal practice concentrates on corporate finance, mergers, acquisitions and divestitures, commercial real estate, venture capital, business and corporate law, government relations, administrative and regulatory law, campaign finance and election law, and securities arbitration matters.

For nearly 20 years, with firms such as Kemper (Zurich) Financial Services and Wells Fargo Bank, I was a pension fund advisor and investment manager in Chicago and Washington, D.C.

Working with pension funds, domestic and foreign commercial banks, investment and merchant banks, insurance companies and municipalities, I invested and managed over $1.25 billion in assets.

During the course of my career, I also gained extensive experience in turnarounds, crisis management, workouts, bankruptcy, DIP lending and litigation management.

Returning to North Carolina, I served on numerous regulatory and administrative bodies and as general counsel to an occupational licensing board with my prior law firm. I joined Bailey & Dixon, LLP in 2007 after serving as general counsel to the North Carolina House Speaker Pro Tempore Richard Morgan.

Most importantly, my business clients get practical advice on making a good deal work - not excuses or roadblocks on why a deal can’t work. I believe clients need clear, concise and practical counsel from their lawyer – not a 40-page legal memorandum.

When representing corporations, financial institutions, insurance companies, associations and individuals before the N.C. General Assembly, U.S. Congress, agencies and regulators, I am able to bring a unique blend of legal, legislative, business and political skills to successfully navigate clients through any governmental squalls.
