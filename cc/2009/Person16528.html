  • Chief architect

    Albumprinter

    (Privately Held; Computer Software industry)

    June 2006 — August 2009 (3 years 3 months)

    For 3 years, I have been working as chief architect for Albumprinter. In Europe, Albumprinter is one of the largest providers of services to create personalized photo books. The company prints over a million books a year, mainly for large retailers and its own brand Albelli.

    • My responsibilities were the architecture vision, strategy, innovation and business development.

    • The functional areas were the internet presence, consumer software, storage facility, order platform and plant automation.

    • As part of the IT management team, I worked with about 30 developers and architects, including an outsourced team in Delhi, India - using a distributed SCRUM approach.

    The unprecedented growth of 22,000% in five years led to several honors. In 2008, Albumprinter was the winner of the Deloitte Fast 50 for fastest growing technology companies in The Netherlands. The company also took a grand second place in the Deloitte EMEA Fast 500 that year.

    Such scalability requires innovative technology. In 2007, we were launching customer for Amazon's Simple Storage Service (S3) in Europe. Shortly after that, we released early Adobe AIR based software in partnerships with Photobucket and others. Lately, we have been using Amazon EC2, SQS and Cloudfront to create and distribute OpenSocial widgets.

  • Owner

    Centrical

    (Information Technology and Services industry)

    May 2004 — June 2006 (2 years 2 months)

    As a consultant, I serviced several international companies. I dealt with architectures, technical management and strategy. Customers were Akzo Nobel, UMC, Hogeschool Utrecht, HKZ, Albumprinter, Evident Interactive and more.

    For example, Akzo Nobel hired me to lead the redesign of their worldwide internet platform - based on a service oriented architecture using Flex, Microsoft Content Management Server and BizTalk Server. Its first website (akzonobel.com itself) received the Macromedia Site of the Day award in October 2005.

However, the jury called the architecture very clear, used the phrase "the Internet Platform is state-of-the-art" and also praised the methodology description.

    Other stuff: during my Centrical time, I wrote over 40 publications - mostly Dutch content for Infoworld - and presented at international KCUK and European Networking Group conferences.

  • Geophysicist

    Geophysics.nl

    (Information Technology and Services industry)

    September 1998 — June 2000 (1 year 10 months)

    In an early attempt to do something commercial with the internet, this little venture was built around the result of our thesis.

    In order to prevent multiple visits to an exploration site, a web application was developed around an inversion algorithm for the processing of electro-magnetic field data. Using the internet, the application could be accessed at the remote location, thereby allowing for better targeting of the research goal.

    The results were presented at the EEGS conference in Budapest, Hungary (1999).

    We tried to market the application under the name Geophysics.nl, but we couldn't really make it a commercial succes. Eventually, the faculty of Earth Sciences of Utrecht University continued the development of the software - using it for scientific research only.
