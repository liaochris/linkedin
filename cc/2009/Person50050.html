Jonathan Midgley’s Summary

Since completing an honors degree in Computer Science in '92, I have been delivering large scale business critical solutions to a client list that includes UBS, Standard Life, Barclays Bank, RBS, Zurich, Royal Skandia and CACI.

I began my career leading development teams for large global finance companies, Zurich & Royal Skandia; started my own company in the 90's; and am now focused on technology start-ups.

My experience covers both global infrastructure and large scale application development, as well as operational support, and business start-up and change.

In 2006 I co-founded a software house in the City of London called Evaluate Technologies Ltd., specialising in delivering turnkey solutions to the Finance Sector, with clients including GMAC-RFC & Charcol. I have helped grow the business from a start-up to a team of over 50 people.

In early 2009 I founded Comment Technologies, a business which combines social media with market research, and am currently launching BandPie, a unique social media platform for music, and Thick Cut Recordings, a record label.

  • Interim CTO

    Utilyx

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Utilities industry)

    January 2005 — July 2006 (1 year 7 months)

    I joined Utilyx during a time of crisis as Interim CTO, to help out the co-founder, Scott Bradley, a friend. The company had expanded rapidly, and the bespoke technology, infrastructure and teams were failing to keep up.

    The company has an in-house trading desk, as well as a number of proprietary business critical trading systems, all of which were built and supported in-house.

    The trading desk were struggling under a lack of systems, and the core proposition, the online reverse auction, was not scaling to meet customers demands. Customers included some of the UK's largest companies, Tesco, Cemex, Royal Mail, AllianceBoots, Diageo, O2, Yorkshire Water and AstraZeneca.

    The reverse auction system had to be scaled through decommissioning and fundamental re-structuring of the infrastructure, and re-engineering of much of the architecture. I took the system from circa 60% reliability to 100%.
