Brad Selby’s Summary

I love building excellent products and working with great people. I have a background in product marketing and product management. My passion is consumer product development.

Metrics are my best friends but sometimes intuition and experience is my greatest asset. I like to think strategically but act tactically, iteratively, quickly. I'm fascinated by media and technology, and I like to think about where we're headed.
I'm like to get my head around new concepts and find connections between seemingly disparate ideas and phenomena.

I look forward to doing something crazy like starting my own thing. In the meantime, I’m happiest as a leader on super-collaborative teams of smart people making stuff that touches people’s daily lives. I have a strong aversion to TPS reports, mild aversion to dark suits.

Brad Selby’s Experience

  • Sr. Product Manager, Flixster.com

    Flixster

    (Privately Held; Internet industry)

    September 2007 — Present (2 years 4 months)

    I'm the lead for Flixster.com, the world's largest social network for moviegoers (45M registered users). We’re a small, agile company that does some pretty cool stuff with a tiny group of smart, motivated folks. I have a lot of fun going from high-level strategy and planning down to sleeves-rolled page mock ups. Data shows us the way, so after I start with my gut, talk to users, and collaborate with my awesome colleagues, we do tons of optimization iteration and testing.

    Some things I’ve been heading up recently:

    Bring people in - generate traffic
    * Generate tons of long-term sustainable traffic through features to appeal to moviegoers, social networkers, and game players.
    * Generate tons of traffic with viral features.

    Bring people together - social graph building
    * Design social features to introduce users to each other and get them to build real online connections.
    * Use graph building technologies (Facebook Connect, MySpaceID, Rapleaf, Social Graph API, etc.) to increase users’ social graphs.

    I hear this "Google" thing is big - SEO
    * Build and optimize UGC-generating features (photos, videos, actor wikis).
    * Make all content very indexable.
    * Link building strategies (widgets, outreach to movie vertical influencers, publishing + Digg/Reddit/Stumble).
    * Optimize anon experience for SE.

    Make people happy and safe - community management
    * Develop and oversee the maintenance of community-building programs (mostly for reactivation and content generation).
    * Create policies that keep a large movie community happy with very low overhead (ToS, problem user remediation, COPPA)
    * Kill spam in its many, many forms.

    We love your inbox - email business
    * Create, test, optimize, and productize content emails to our large opted-in userbase.
    * Create and tune transactional emails.

    C.R.E.A.M. - support ad sales, ad ops, and create revenue-generating ad features.
