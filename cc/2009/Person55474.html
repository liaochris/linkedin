Kieron Smith’s Experience

  • Managing Director

    The Book Depository

    (Privately Held; Retail industry)

    August 2008 — Present (1 year)

    The Book Depository is the UK's largest dedicated online bookseller - offering the largest range of titles in the world, available for dispatch within 48 hours. Founded in 2004 to make 'All Books To All' they focus on selling 'less of more' rather than 'more of less', differentiating itself from other retailers who increasingly focus on bestsellers. Having forged unique publisher and supplier relationships, they have the ability to dispatch over 2.3 million titles within 48 hours, with free worldwide delivery, enabling customers to find the books they want. As the world's fastest growing bookseller, The Book Depository saw the last 12 months' sales topping £40m, despatching up to 100,000 parcels a week worldwide.

  • Managing Director

    Retail8 Limited / BookRabbit.com

    (Retail industry)

    September 2007 — August 2008 (1 year)

    ArgentVive PLC was established in July 2007 after an entrepreneur took a majority stake in the small AIM listed bookseller Methven’s/Samedaybooks. It was renamed and the business refocused on acquiring and developing technology oriented businesses. Kieron was approached to join from Waterstone’s in order to start a new online consumer brand; that became BookRabbit.com, and to look after the remaining bookshops in the chain.

  • Head of eCommerce

    Waterstone's Booksellers

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; Retail industry)

    July 2006 — September 2007 (1 year 3 months)

    Kieron approached Waterstone’s in 2006 and offered to come on board to help co-ordinate the launch of Waterstones.com, previously handled by Amazon.co.uk. Responsible for the marketing, operations and production of the site, he was instrumental in the successful launch online and in establishing the business unit.

  • Head of Online Operations

    GAME Stores Group

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; Retail industry)

    January 2003 — July 2006 (3 years 7 months)

    When Kieron joined GAME in 2003 the online business was not commercially focused and had little online presence. Through the development of an online strategy, a complete reorganisation of the team and clear integrated marketing communication, GAME.co.uk was grown to be one of the most significant online retail properties in the UK.

  • Head of Operations

    Methven's Booksellers

    (Retail industry)

    October 2001 — January 2003 (1 year 4 months)

    Development of Corporate Strategy and Balanced Scorecard for the business.
    Management of further integration of web site with stores and site re-launch.
    Responsible for delivery of exceptional customer service across all stores and the website.
    HR Management for 11 bookshops (105 staff) – including store closure and redundancy.
    Introduction of company-wide performance management system.
    Co-ordination and analysis of customer research, to inform future strategy decisions.

  • Head of Online Operations

    BCA / Bertelsmann Media Group (BMG)

    (Privately Held; 501-1000 employees; Retail industry)

    January 2000 — November 2001 (1 year 11 months)

    Established online sites for all bookclubs in the UK, and grew many to be a significant share of overall business. Worked with offline direct mail on integration, measurement and optimisation.
