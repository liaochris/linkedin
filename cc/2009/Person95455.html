Stuart Adams’s Summary

Have been licensed to practice law in Kentucky, Massachusetts, Indiana, and admitted to practice before the Supreme Court of the United States.

Served as: This I Believe, Inc., Chairperson; Louisville Third Century, Inc., Chairperson; The Entrepreneur Society, President; Bluegrass Inventors Guild co-founder; The Venture Club of Louisville, board; Kentucky Network of Information Technology Executives (KNITE) board; Technology Network of Greater Louisville (TeN), board; Intellas Group, LLC, co-founder and Executive Committee member.

Principal of a business consulting company, concentrating on management issues, competitive intelligence, integration of technology solutions, e-commerce, disaster preparation and recovery, and executive coaching.

Founding equity member and executive committee member of Intellas Group, LLC, a multi-disciplinary technology and business management consulting company.

Lectures: entrepreneurship, business litigation, and business strategies, technology problems and solutions, disaster preparation & recovery, e-commerce and business management issues, electronic information management, discovery and disclosure.

Publications on business issues, include the Courier Journal, Business First, and TechRepublic, monthly column for Louisville Computer News on legal issues related to the computer industry and currently writing a book, on how to start an E-commerce business in tough economic times.

Created and served as volunteer instructor for adult education public school course: "Entrepreneuring" and starting a small business. Volunteer counselor with Louisville chapter of SCORE, a resource partner of the United States Small Business Administration.

Currently serve as Chairperson of the Norton Commons® neighborhood association, The Commons Community Council and as a member of the board of directors of the Norton Commons Residential Village Association, Inc.

Stuart Adams’s Specialties:

Have practiced at all levels of the court system. Have represented private businesses and been legal counsel for local economic development agencies in the public sector. A major area of law practice involves representation of entrepreneurs and small to medium-sized businesses, which are embarking on, or engaged in, issues relating to electronic commerce.

Have been trained as a mediator, concentrating in resolution of business disputes related to technology and the computer industry.
