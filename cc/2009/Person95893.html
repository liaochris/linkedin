Lars Soeftestad’s Summary

Anthropologist with 25 years of experience in operations and policy work for, among others, GTZ, IFAD, IFC, ILO, Norad, Sida, UNDP, UNOPS, and World Bank; in Europe (Bulgaria, Hungary, Portugal, Serbia, Slovenia), Middle East (Yemen), South Asia (Bangladesh, India, Nepal, Sri Lanka), Southeast Asia (Viet Nam), and Sub-Saharan Africa (Cameroon, Central African Republic, Chad, Ethiopia, Ghana, Mauritania, Niger, Nigeria).
Focus on project management (incl. preparation, appraisal, implementation, supervision and evaluation), social and institutional analysis/development, knowledge management, strategic communication, and capacity-building.
Substantial experience with World Bank policies and projects in several areas and sectors, incl. coastal zone management, education, fisheries, minorities / indigenous peoples, involuntary resettlement, and social funds.
Vision: Analysis, management, production, dissemination and use of relevant knowledge, with local people, for local people and by local people, towards addressing local needs and perceived problems at local, regional and global levels, with the aim of fostering public participation and community empowerment, and through this contribute to social and environmental sustainable development.
Language skills in Bengali, English, French, and German. Citizenship: Norway.

Lars Soeftestad’s Specialties:

Biodiversity conservation, capacity building, community-based natural resource management (CBNRM), conflict management, democratization, GIS, ICT and development (ICT4D), inter-disciplinary collaboration, knowledge management, land management, land rights, legal reform, networking, participation / participatory approaches, poverty analysis, social analysis / assessment, social entrepreneurship, stakeholder analysis, research methodologies, strategic communication, and Web 2.0.
