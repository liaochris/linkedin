Enrique Martínez Luque’s Summary

Enrique Martínez Luque is a 33-year-old Argentine professional, specialized in subjects related with Media, Sports and Management. He is currently working as Institutional Relations Manager for the Argentine Professional Golf Tour (TPG Tour). He was born on August 6th, 1976 in Tucumán and completed primary and secondary education at St. Patrick School (83-94). He holds a Bachelor’s degree in Tourism Administration from the Santo Tomás de Aquino North University (class of 2001), and an MBA specializing in Sports Management obtained jointly from the EAE Business School and the Universitat Politècnica de Catalunya, both in Spain (class of 2006). Similarly, in 2000 he completed studies in the United States at the University of Texas, the Lyndon B. Johnson School of Public Affairs and the Department of State. He was Director of Tiempo de Golf magazine (00-03), specialized golf journalist at La Gaceta newspaper, Tucumán (00-05) and Press Chief of many golf tournaments. Between 2003 and 2006 he was coach of professional golfers competing at the European Tour. During this period, he was General Manager at Las Yungas Country Club (05-08). Also of note he was President of the Youthful Commission at the Jockey Club Tucumán (95-96) and auditor at the Argentine Northwest Golf Federation (07-09). Since 1999 he is member of Fundación Universitaria del Río de la Plata. He holds a Scholarship from Fundación Carolina (05-06) and he also won a scholarship from the United States Embassy in Argentina (04) and from the International Republican Institute (04). In addition, he was Honored Guest of the State of Texas in 2000. In 2004, he was selected to represent his country at the Americas Leadership Summit, held in Quito, Ecuador. He also participated as International Observer of the United States 2008 elections, program organized by the Center for Strategic and International Studies. He was Mentor of Fundación Impulsar (07-08) and he is Planning Consulting of COPLAN, Perú since 2004.
