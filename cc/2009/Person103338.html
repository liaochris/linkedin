Vladimir Campos’s Summary

My interests in technology started since very young with my first computer, an Apple II. In 1999 when I purchased a Palm IIIx and because of the potential I sow in that device, I've started a company to develop mobile solutions and also a website about the Palm platform with tutorials, downloads, tips, articles and a huge amount of information for end users. A partnership with Zip.Net (later acquired by UOL.com.br) brought revenue from advertisement and the website popularity growth helped selling more PDA solution for the corporate market.

Since I was the responsible for writing all the website content I also started writing for several American and Brazilian specialized magazines like PocketPCMag.com, ComputingUnplugged.com, Webinsider.com.br and others. The next natural step was covering other platforms such as Windows Mobile and Symbian. And after that my company also started covering and developing corporate solutions for mobile phones in general.

With the increase of the demand for mobile corporate solutions, I became Director of Business Development in Brazil for LatinGo, an AvantGo partner for Latin America. This move helped my company develop better solutions and also reach the market of bigger companies.

Imagining the mobile phone market would grow fester then the PDA market I started working in the sales department of Claro, a Brazilian GSM carrier. Because of my skills and knowledge I was promoted and became Consultant for Products & Services, the same position I latter accepted in Brasil Telecom GSM, a GSM start up. In both companies I worked with product development, sales consulting and sales force training. I also worked for GVT a Brazilian landline company, and nowadays I work at TIM with contracts and auctions for the brazilian federal government.
