Gregg Costanzo’s Experience

  • Co. of Arts, Cultural, Entertainment & Intellectual Programming

    Eastern Michigan University

    (Educational Institution; 1001-5000 employees; Higher Education industry)

    November 2006 — Present (3 years 1 month)

    Currently I develop, plan, implement, and evaluate multifaceted arts and entertainment programs, services, and activities. I am responsible for strategic planning, budgets, promotions, technical operations, and student staff supervision. I participate in the planning and development of diverse educational arts-related, cultural and entertainment activities including but not limited to: performing arts series, lecture series, excursions, films, concerts, union programs, etc. I manage legal contracts and coordinate technical and house management areas, in addition to departmental marketing activities including marketing research, website, relevant E-groups, and media relations. I hire, train, schedule, supervise and evaluate undergraduate and graduate student staff, including the department webmaster, graphic designers, marketing and program coordinators. I also ensure cultural diversity and multicultural dimensions integral to student activities programs.

  • Director of Campus Ministry

    Holy Trinity Student Parish at EMU

    (Religious Institutions industry)

    July 2003 — November 2006 (3 years 5 months)

    I served as the Director of Catholic Campus Ministry at Holy Trinity Student Parish. Holy Trinity is comprised of the Catholic faculty, staff, students and alumni of Eastern Michigan University. My duties included the administration of the chapel and its campus mission by maintaining and supporting a Catholic presence on campus, providing counseling and spiritual guidance to the university community who sought assistance at Holy Trinity, developing and implementing educational, social and religious programs geared toward college students, and fostering an inclusive environment. Our crowning achievement during these three years was the renovation and expansion of the Catholic Student Center.

  • Theology Department, Chair

    Elyria Catholic High School

    (Education Management industry)

    August 1998 — June 2003 (4 years 11 months)

    My duties included counseling, service and justice programs, liturgy planning, retreats, faculty formation, peer ministry, music ministry, prayer groups, dialogue with surrounding parishes, providing learning opportunities for parents, relevant field trips, web page design, etc. As chair of the Theology Department, I revised the curriculum, made our service learning program more dynamic, fostered relationships with Catholic universities, promoted and fostered faculty certification and created a resource network for the entire staff allowing them access to current church trends and teachings. I received a Provisional Catechesis Certificate from the Diocese of Cleveland in January 1999.
