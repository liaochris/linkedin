Shaun Dakin’s Summary

Currently the CEO and Founder of The National Political Do Not Contact Registry, a program by Citizens for Civil Discourse.

Shaun Dakin took an idea from start up to launch to Saturday Night Live.

In less than a year, Mr. Dakin launched StopPoliticalCalls.org and grew membership to over 85,000. During this time Mr. Dakin became the most recognized expert in the nation working on protecting voters from the scourge that is modern political campaigning: robo calls or automated political calls.

In addition to appearing on CNN, MSNBC and the local affiliates of ABC, CBS, NBC and Fox as well as NPR and XM radio he testified at the US Senate on behalf of the Robocall Privacy Act (Sen. Feinstein, D-CA sponsor) and published several op-eds in leading national newspapers including the Washington Post.

During the last weeks of the 2008 election cycle, Mr. Dakin and his members were at the center of providing the audio of campaign robo calls that led to Colin Powell calling out robo calls in his endorsement of Barack Obama for President. Saturday Night Live created a "robo call" character. The audio and real time reporting of robo calls allowed Talking Points Memo, the Huffington Post and the Politico to report robo calls in real time.

Shaun Dakin lives in Falls Church, VA and is married to Dr. Saleena Dakin. He and Dr. Dakin have one son, Joseph.

Awards:

StopPoliticalCalls.org won the Non-Profit category in the Mashable Open Web Awards (http://tinyurl.com/WeWon)

In recognition of the work of StopPoliticalCalls.org during the election, Mashable.com considered work done by Mr. Dakin as one of the top 16 great Twitter moments of the year.
