Peter Hoyt’s Summary

Goals:
- To grow a renowned research program in basic and applied science of cell biology, using cross-disciplinary methodology. Combine Biology, and Informatics with the most advanced technologies available, and push the envelope of technology through novel applications.
- To bring technology advancement to the academic community through partnerships with industry using mutually beneficial intellectual property agreements.
- To teach at an academic institution both basic and applied science.

Experience:
- Academic training includes developmental biology of mice, mouse molecular genetics, regulation of gene expression, genomic expression analyses (arrays), bioinformatics, low dose radiation, and cancer biology/toxicology.
- Technological/Industrial experience includes facility production at Oak Ridge National Laboratory, including core facilities in transgenic mouse production, gene targeted "knock-out" facilities, DNA and RNA high-throughput production for DNA microarrays, and high-throughput protein purification and complex isolation laboratories.
-Computers/Informatics experience includes directing a bioinformatics core facility, upgrading the computer systems using Linux and Windows OS. Building servers. Systems administration. Scripting languages. emphasis on open-source bioinformatics tools and interactions within cell metabolism.

Peter Hoyt’s Specialties:

I specialize in cross disciplinary programs and as a liaison between academic Biologists and technologists. Trained as an academic, I have repeatedly implemented complex biological core facilities and now high-throughput biological sample preparation. 18 years experience bringing new instrumentation to genetics, genomics, bioinformatics, and cell biology.
