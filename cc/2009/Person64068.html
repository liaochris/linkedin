Martin Finn’s Summary

I have always been results orientated, and get a kick out of setting a target and blowing it away. I have found it an even greater kick to inspire others to do the same.

In recent years I have concentrated upon how to use internet technology to help sales and marketing become more successful, particularly for large value sales.

I'm not talking about CRM, that done very well by others. I am talking about how to get sales/marketing/technical teams to collaborate better, prepare better, present better, react quicker to change and ultimately to win.
See www.thepowerofinsight.com as an example.

I still consult to small group of Blue Chip IT clients; creating market positioning, marketing & sales campaigns, creative writing and sometimes the real fun of blue sky thinking and making some big ideas - with actions to turn them into reality.

  • Commercial and Marketing Director

    Hamilton Rentals

    (Internet industry)

    1991 — 2001 (10 years)

    We were a successful reseller of Digital (DEC) mid range systems - VAX, Unix and also PC. But growth was limited. I created a new division focused upon Networking (and won reseller of the year in our first year).
    I then forced fundamental change and made new Divisions for other brands - HP, Sun, IBM and Oracle. These were turning over more than £100M pa within 2 years - and are the back bone of the business even today.

  • Group Marketing Director

    Compel Group

    (Public Company; Information Technology and Services industry)

    Whilst having a day job running the groups most profitable business I was also responsible for the group marketing, and created a number of new business divisions selling high-end IT solutions to corporations. Setting up specialist divisions for HP, SUN, IBM and Oracle amongst others - these ultimately became the backbone of the group.

  • Director - Hamilton Rentals

    Compel Group

    (Public Company; 201-500 employees; Information Technology and Services industry)

    May 1992 — December 2000 (8 years 8 months)

    I took over the management of Hamilton Rentals, a well respected but languishing computer rental supplier. In 4 years I took the business from laggard in the UK to be the leader in Europe. We smashed our competitors with a mix of increased investement, improved products, massively enhanced services, innovative marketing and great sales. It was paid for by higher prices and greater sales. We multipled profits 5 fold..

  • National Sales Manager

    Northamber plc

    (Marketing and Advertising industry)

  • Product Marketing

    Texas Instruments

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; TXN; Semiconductors industry)

    1981 — 1986 (5 years)

    I joined TI straight from University. My engineering degree had got me the interview, but I was the only new gradute who was not an straight A electronics engineer. The reason was that the guys who wanted me, wanted by ballsy approach and they wanted a businessman too. I was the protege of Irfam Salim - my boss. I joined the Consumer electronic dvision, selling calculators and educational toys and home computers. On my first day I was writing copy for catalogue sales. Within 3 weeks I was responsible for TV and Radio ads.
    Then I spent 4 months and made a new 'English' version of my premium product Speak & Spell. I had to make it myself as there had never been a speaking product that did not have a US voice, and no-one outside the US knew how to do it.

    I then became the youngest ever sales person in the division and outsold all others for Home Computers (I was closer to the user group age and saw the sales wave before the other guys).
