Based on my background in communication and marketing I'm interested in the dialogue. The dialogue between a company and their surrounding, but also when people meet in person. Dialogue often gets mistaken for a discussion but I prefer to look at it as a mutual exchange of information. As a result I'm a firm believer that information gets more valuable when it gets shared. The Internet allows us to share information in an increasing number of ways. But sharing information isn't just using a website, sharing on You Tube or having a good conversation with someone who might be a potential business opportunity. It's about combining those efforts to get the most from the interaction between a company and its stakeholder.

Business wise this comes to practice through supporting companies with advice on how to improve their website. As a Web Analytics and SEO consultant I'm helping companies improving their website and supporting them with knowledge about how to turn the Internet in to an effective communication channel.

In my spare time I am also interested in the dialog. My broad interests for corporate communication, marketing, reputation management, internal communication, web2.0/social web and website findability/usability/credibility amongst others show this.

On a regularly base I have wrote articles about this and other subjects within my field of interest.
