DJ Disco Wiz’s Summary

DJ Disco Wiz (Luis Cedeño) was born in Bronx New York on August 11, 1961. he is credited as being the first Latino Hip-Hop DJ, His contributions have paved the way for Latin DJs everywhere to make their mark in the industry. Back in the day, Dj Disco Wiz was known for his aggressive battle style of DJing, and together with his partner and childhood friend Grandmaster Caz, then known as DJ Casanova Fly of the Mighty Force Crew throughout the mid- to late-70s. They are legendary for their DJ battles in the streets of the South Bronx; DJ Disco Wiz is also credited for being the first DJ to make a "mixed plate" in 1977, In the years since, DJ Disco Wiz has openly contributed to the education and recognition of the early years of hip-hop. In the millennium issue of The Source magazine he candidly shared his experience in being the first Latino DJ in the evolution of hip-hop. Dj Disco Wiz was also a major contributor in the opening of the Experience Music Project in Seattle in 2000. His contribution and donation of original hip-hop flyers, solely for the preservation of hip-hop can be seen as part of the archives. He was also instrumental in the making of Jim Fricke and Charlie Ahearn’s rendition of the early years of hip-hop entitled Yes, Yes Y’all. DJ Disco Wiz poem entitled “The Land Before The Rhyme” was performed at the Apollo Theater, the landmark Nuyorican Poet Cafe and also at poet venues through out New York City, and can be seen in the book Born in the Bronx, He is also featured in ALL OUT WAR a B-Boy Story. and The Emmy Nominated Vh1 Rock Doc "NY77: The Coolest Year In Hell" He Co-Author with Ivan Sanchez based on his life - It’s Just Begun: The Epic Journey of DJ Disco Wiz, Hip-Hop’s First Latino DJ. Miss Rosen Editions/powerhouse Books 2009. Wiz is also a board member of the Universal Federation for the Preservation of Hip Hop Culture.and founder, creator, director of The Hip-Hop Meets Spoken Wordz fundraiser for the homeless.

    History:
    Hip Hop has been an integral part of the people’s movement for decades. Unfortunately, the old school pioneers had not found a connection to the new school, yet they remained joined eternally through the art form. It became specifically important to regain the voice of the people that the art was founded in. This is what prompted us to collaborate with young, up in coming artists from the hip hop and spoken word communities. Though the mainstream does not always reflect our needs, we could still hear the voice of the disenfranchised and the muted in the underground circuit. This message needed to be heard and so the event, Hip Hop meets Spoken Wordz was born in January 2006.
