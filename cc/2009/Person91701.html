Shilpa Shukla’s Summary

I am a professional User Experience Designer with nearly 15 years of experience working with leading IT companies including Hewlett Packard, SightSpeed (a service of Logitech), Microsoft, J.D.Edwards (a former Peoplesoft company), Nokia, and Apple.

I hold a B.S. and M.S. in Information and Computer Science with a focus in Human Computer Interaction, Software Engineering, and Artificial Intelligence.

My goal is to holistically apply quality design fundaments to inform software developers on how to create an optimal user experience while factoring in the organization's business model, the end-user's context and general design priciples such as interaction, visual, information, and usability concepts. My depth of knowledge in software engineering, human computer interaction and user-centered design combined with my artistic design knowledge has severed as a strong foundation for my career.

Responsibilities:
• I am the liaison which helps IT organizations bridge communication between internal and external stakeholders to ensure requirements are being effectively translated to most efficiently build the right product.
• I am the unifying voice that leads IT organizations to achieve the envisioned goals through application of pragmatic validation methods including contextual studies, concept testing, and usability testing, as well as through proactive heuristic, competitive, and market research.
• I serve IT organizations in driving the User Centered Design process through task page flows, mockups, design pattern guidelines, and application overview sitemaps.

Shilpa Shukla’s Specialties:

Team Leadership, Group Vision, Project ideation and management, Coordination of overall creative process, Design strategy and advocacy, Resource and personal management, Hands on creation of deliverables, Innovation in product conceptualization & design, Communication with software development teams, Computer Science skills to realistically assess feasibility goals, Interaction Design, User Experience Research, Quantitative and Qualitative Methods, Ethnography, Technical Writing.
