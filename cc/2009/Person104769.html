Bill Staley’s Summary

• Tax planning and general business law for closely-held businesses and nonprofit organizations; often in the role of primary outside counsel, coordinating the prevention and resolution of legal and tax problems of all kinds; often working closely with other attorneys, accountants and trusted advisors
• Structuring, negotiating and documenting business deals: start-ups, reorganizations, buying and selling businesses, redemptions, corporate liquidations, partnership transactions and property transfers
• Advising attorneys and accountants concerning high-stake tax issues
• Business succession planning: income, estate, gift and property tax issues; restructuring businesses and designing incentive plans for management
• Obtaining rulings, advisory opinions and determination letters on tax issues, usually for a business transaction or a family transaction involving business assets
• See www.staleylaw.com

Bill Staley’s Specialties:

• Selling or buying a business
• Buy-sell agreements among owners
• Incorporating, reorganizing and dissolving business and nonprofit entities
• Incentive compensation and issuing stock to employees
• Succession planning – transferring a business to the next generation – legal and tax issues with a special sensitivity to family issues
• Tax-exempt nonprofit entities – private foundations, charitable organizations and trade associations – legal and tax issues
