John Bishop’s Summary

Accent On Success is a nonprofit organization dedicated to helping students succeed in school and in life. Our first book, Goal Setting for Students®, has won three national parenting book awards.

The book teaches students (grades 5 -12) how to use the goal setting principles in the classroom to take more responsibility for their education.

Ryan Chase, a student who used the program said: “… I learned to never give up. It is important to have pride in yourself and to try your hardest to achieve all your goals.”


For a comparison to the SMART goals program please hit this link.

http://www.goalsetting4students.com/benefitsmoreinfo.html


What Will Students Learn?

1. Don't Find a Fault; Find a Solution.
2. To get ahead in life - learn more, do more.
3. No Vision = No Direction.
4. Minimizing the Bummer Words: no, can't, won't, never, maybe, and if.
5. How to use the goal setting principles in the classroom.
6. The importance of using the "YES! Count On Me" words: yes, I can, and I will.
7. Eliminating excuses.
8. Ask yourself everyday: "Did I give my best effort to today's activities?"
9. The importance of help others.
10. How to take action on your dreams.
