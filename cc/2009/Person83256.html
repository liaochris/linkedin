Rand Fitzpatrick’s Summary

I am passionate about information and interaction. From French to Ruby, linguistics to game theory, and API design to UI development, interacting information systems inspire me.

Brevity and clarity are high art forms, and simplicity should be well respected. The acts of design, optimization and management should be acts of simplification, removing of obstacles and facilitation of communication.

Throughout my professional and personal life, I continue to learn, to strive for excellence, and to apply a constant cycle of reflection and review upon my experience in order to improve what I do.

Here are a few of my core principles that I use in guiding projects and products through conception, development and release.

• You can't know how effective you're being if you don't know what your goals are. - It is critical to understand how to define requirements in the domain of the client, and to be able map those requirements to the technical execution process. Real metrics are the way to drive change.

• Change is the only constant. Knowing how to adapt is the key to successful projects. - It is important to understand the likely sources of change, to build variability into the scope of a project, and to control the outcome.

• Multidisciplinary excellence is the road to true happiness. - Being able to communicate effectively and with native competence in the creative, technical and business realms is central to managing a modern product.

• Focused design is incredibly effective. - Attention is finite, for both developers and users. Keeping an application focused on its most salient elements will deliver a more effective and compelling product.
