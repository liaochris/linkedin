  • Senior Vice President, IT Services USA

    Hudson Global Resources (TMP Worldwide)

    (Staffing and Recruiting industry)

    February 2002 — October 2003 (1 year 9 months)

    IT Services business with 700+ technical contractors on assignment, generating $90M in annual revenue. Craig managed 4 regional vice presidents and was in charge of 17 field sales offices.

    Hudson was spun off from TMP Worldwide (the parent company of Monster.com) in April 2003. Craig was responsible for sales and marketing over the U.S. IT Services Division. He designed and implemented a new brand positioning, direct marketing program, collateral material, public relations and advertising campaign to increase visibility and generate new business.

  • Executive Vice President Sales / Chief Marketing Officer

    Hall Kinion and Associates

    (Public Company; HAKI; Staffing and Recruiting industry)

    March 1996 — September 2001 (5 years 7 months)

    Craig was hired to replace the co-founder of the company and was given operational responsibility over the 30 person Recruiting Division. Over the next 4 years he opened 44 new sales offices, hired 400 technical sales recruiters and increased sales revenue from $3M to $70M annually. In addition to his sales management & operations responsibility he was also the CMO and headed up all advertising, marketing, public relations, and training efforts. In 1999, Craig spearheaded the project to re-brand the company as "The Talent Source for the Internet Economy" that led to a $100M market cap increase within 30 days of release.

    During his time with the company Hall Kinion went public with an IPO in August of 1997, acquired 5 other companies, increased annual revenues from $25M to $296M, had a very successful secondary public offering, was added to the S&P Small Cap Index and was listed by Forbes magazine as one of the Top 25 Best Companies to work for in America.

  • Vice President, Sales

    Strategic Mapping

    (Computer Software industry)

    February 1989 — February 1996 (7 years 1 month)

    Craig built the sales team from a start-up to 65 people strong as he took reveunes from $1M to $25M a year. He helped raise 2 rounds of venture funding from Mayfield Fund, Venrock, D&B Enterprises, JMI, and Information Partners.

    Craig worked closely with Marketing and Product Development to create unique products and programs to increase sales revenue. Under his leadership SMI built a large VAR network of 300 partners to develop custom applications for niche markets. Craig spearheaded the consulting division to work on custom application development projects for key clients such as CATMAN - Catastrophe Management application for Continental Insurance. He recruited, hired and trained all sales staff, and aided in due diligence on multiple M&A projects. When SMI acquired Donnelly Marketing Information Services from Dunn & Bradstreet, Craig managed the post merger sales transition team.
