Daniel Freeman’s Summary

I'm an AS3/Flex/AIR/RIA expert, with well-regarded accomplishments in the field. Seeking contracts or possible permanent position. Worldwide. Development, Technical leadership, Research or University lecturer. A role that ignites my enthusiasm and makes good use of my talents.

1980: Designed and built, from scratch, my first computer. A four bit machine, programmed in binary, with lights and switches on the front.

1986: Graduated from the University of Manchester (UK). Joined BT Laboratories. Became involved in the ‘Skyphone’ development.

1988: Skyphone beat four worldwide competitors to become an AEEC aeronautical standard.

1989: Involved in standardisation for the first Pan-European Digital Mobile (GSM) standard and CCITT standards committees in the USA.

1989: My Voice Activity Detector for mobile telephony use which became a European Standard. This algorithm is still implemented in every GSM mobile phone worldwide. (There are over 3.3 billion phones, and most are GSM).

1989: Awarded the ‘IPU4+ Inventors Prize’ by the Intellectual Property Unit of BT for patented inventions. I was the youngest person ever to receive this award.

1993: Obtained an MSc in Telecommunications from the University of Essex.

1994: Transferred to the Cybernetics Group, working on Artificial Intelligence and Genetic Algorithms.

1996: Studied fine arts and design. Got into film-making.

1997: Set up multimedia training business.

1998: Consultant and trainer for Signals Media Arts Centre in Colchester UK.

1999: Set up Creative Technology Co-Op ltd.

2000: Awarded a commission to produce Video Art for the Millennium Dome.

2001: Focussed on web development technologies.

2003: Developed RIAs using AJAX, Central, and Flex.

2004: Several advanced applications were being sold online.

2006: Taught in Asia, while exploring the continent.

2007: Developed applications for Adobe AIR.

2008: Lectured Computer Science in Beijing.
