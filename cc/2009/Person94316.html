  • VP Music Publishing/Music Supervision at Pearson (Fremantle) Television

    Pearson (Fremantle) Television

    (Entertainment industry)

    November 1997 — January 2000 (2 years 3 months)

    Responsibilities for worldwide creation & administration of all music amongst production of almost 40 programs in 150 countries, with P&L responsibility for music operation. Programs included "Family Feud" and "Match Game", "Baywatch" & "First Wave. Later under retainer with Pearson to re-license approximately 1200 sync and master use song licenses for third party music used in "Baywatch" including digital rights. It is significant to have been perhaps the first to negotiate digital rights en masse. This exercise provided the foundation for new clearance concepts. On "First Wave", was acknowledged as the first media professional to procure music for a television series or motion picture exclusively from the Internet. Accomplished through an innovative agreement negotiated with MP3.com. Other successes involved the selection of composers, negotiation of their agreements and creative and budgetary supervision of the scores to many of Pearson Televisions production slate.
