Daniel D. Moses’s Summary

Daniel D Moses is a London based Freelance Photographer and Filmmaker with project experience in the various fields of film, documentary, performance, corporate, event and charity.

He is able to work at any level of production, with director, producer, photographer, writer, editor and designer to add to his credits. From scripting, to shoot to edit he completes a production to a high quality standard in any photography, filmmaking or design project; his work across these mediums is often in a documentary or ‘reportage’ style, which result in innovative productions that manages to entertain and inform.

His excellent grasp of using multi media as effective marketing and campaigning tools across different platforms has led to his work showcased as part of in- house programmes, during events, in publications and online. Types of projects that have benefited from his marketing include short and feature films, musicals, charity and corporate projects.

Daniel has directed and edited several original short films, documentaries and showreels, and has several shorts in development in 2009. Recent projects include his original short film The Rabbi, which won 3 categories in the MySpace Short Film of the Month Film Festival in August 2008; marketing 2 feature films, and producing a series of campaigning short films for the charity Scope.

He loves talking about himself in third person. Especially when writing his own bio......

http://www.danielmoses.com

    (Non-Profit; Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    September 2007 — Present (1 year 11 months)

    Scope is the disability organisation in England and Wales whose focus is people with cerebral palsy. Their aim is that disabled people achieve equality: a society in which they are as valued and have the same human and civil rights as everyone else.

    "No Voice, No Choice" is a campaign running between 2007- 2010 to ensure that all those who need Alternative and Augmentative Communication (AAC) get the equipment and support they need.

    Sponsored by the BT Better World Campaign, the brief is to create a series of short films over several themes affecting the lives of those who need AAC, to be used as campaigning resources. The final videos will be filmed in partnership with and distributed via various networks and organisations including BT, Scope,1Voice, ACE Centre.

    Themes include Children and young people and Human Rights. Under each theme will be different strands including case studies, an original narrtive short and a music video
