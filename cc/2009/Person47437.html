Jim Luce’s Summary

Jim Luce brings 25 years management experience within investment banking and the non-profit sector. As Founder & President of Orphans International Worldwide, associated with the United Nations Department of Public Information, he is working with a strong network of committed professionals to build interfaith, interracial, Internet-connected orphanages and family-care centers in Sulawesi (Indonesia), Sumatera (Indonesia), Haiti, Guyana, the Philippines, Ghana, Sri Lanka,and Togo; and exploring new projects in China (Henan), India, Nigeria, Cambodia, Guatemala, and the Dominican Republic.

In pursuit of a better world he has left corporate America – twice, testified in Congress, and helped raise over $2.5 million in cash, services and gifts-in-kind for the unfortunate. Jim holds an East Asian Studies degree from the College of Wooster, and studied at Waseda University, Tokyo, Centro de Estudiar Colombino-Americano (Bogotá), and through AFS at Max-Plank Gymnasium (Bielefeld, Germany). He serves as past president of his local Toastmasters chapter and has received awards for his work with orphans, HIV & AIDS prevention, and the homeless. He has served as a New York State-licensed foster father and an officer of his local PTA. He is a member of St. Bart’s Episcopal Church, but has also attended mosque and studies the Torah with his Jewish neighbors, associated with Central Synagogue. He was awarded the Certificate of Congressional Recognition for his work. He writes for the New York Times and the Huffington Post.
