James M. Haile’s Summary

Original, independent, creative thinker that can stimulate team efforts toward problem solving. Ability to adjust problem-solving strategies to find new insights and lines of attack. Preference for whole-system analyses rather than piece wise quick-fixes.

Adept at finding and identifying underlying causes and sources of problems, developing a plan of attack, uncovering secondary issues, and following through to resolution.

Advanced ability to digest highly technical material and convey the essence in a form that is understandable to a target audience.

Comfortable in cross-disciplinary environments, including physicists, chemists, engineers, mathematicians, computer scientists, educational psychologists, artists.

Expert in thermodynamics, classical statistical mechanics, molecular dynamics simulation, Monte Carlo simulation, molecular theory, solution theories, thermophysical properties of fluids, applied numerical methods, numerical analysis, data analysis, technical writing, technical editing, technical illustration.
