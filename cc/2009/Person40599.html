Hui Ying Yang’s Summary

Research collaboration is welcomed!

RESEARCH INTERESTS

Investigate the room temperature transition mechanism between excitonic and electron-hole plasma stimulated emission at high excitation level.

Design and fabricate quantum confinement structure of Zinc Oxide (ZnO) thin film to sustain room temperature excitonic gain at high excitation level.

Optimize the ZnO thin film by using the industrial filtered cathodic vacuum arc (FCVA) system.

Demonstrate a low excitation threshold and high power continuous wave ultraviolet (UV) lasing using the proposed ZnO thin film.

Hui Ying Yang’s Specialties:

TECHNICAL SKILLS
- Skillfully operate JEOL 2010, 2010F TEMs. Rich working experience on the methods of diffraction contrast, CBED, HREM, and XEDS (EDX). Practical experience on preparing planar, cross section, and grinded samples.
- X-Ray Diffractometer (XRD).
- Atomic Force Microscopy (AFM).
- Scanning Electron Microscopy (SEM).
- Photoluminescence (PL) measurement by Nd: YAG (355 nm) and He-Cd (325 nm) laser pumping.
- Filtered cathodic vacuum arc system.
