Stefan Koopmanschap’s Summary

After learning myself PHP as the first programming language during my study, when I started with PHP3, I've gone through PHP4 to currently PHP5. I've done anything from "simple" websites to full-blown Content Management Systems to intranet-systems to an XML server that talks to a desktop software application.

I feel a strong commitment to the PHP and Open Source community. I am a user of a lot of Open Source applications and libraries, and try to give something back as well, be it in the form of documentation, bug reports, modifications, or even applications.

Currently, I am specialized in PHP5 development, especially with the Symfony framework. As such, I am also occasionally available (through my employer) as PHP5 or Symfony consultant or developer.

    Myphp-busters: symfony framework
    The symfony web application framework is a great framework to work with, as most symfony-experienced developers will tell you. It is very reliable and flexible, and allows for fast and structured development, yet those who haven't quite looked at the framework but only know the basics will often tell you symfony is very slow, extremely coupled, and overengineered. So who is telling the truth?

    This talk will attempt to bust some myphps (pronounce: 'myths') around the symfony framework. Stefan will go into the most common prejudices against symfony, and show you that symfony is actually a bit different than what those people might think.
