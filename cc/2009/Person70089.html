Steve Moretti’s Experience

  • Co-Owner

    SymPop Productions™

    (Music industry)

    April 2008 — Present (1 year 4 months)

    Founded by Steve Moretti and Charles Dixon, SymPop Productions™ is a unique company that produces new and innovative symphonic pops programs for symphony orchestras across the country. We also offer a consulting division, evaluating and restructuring symphonic pops programming. We at SymPops can assist your orchestra in coming up with ways to capitalize on your local market as well as reaching new markets. Our goal is to provide the highest artistic quality for your pops program with new and creative programs that will draw in new audiences while sustaining your current subscribers.

  • Owner & Producer

    Morettiman Music™ (Self-employed)

    (Self-Employed; Myself Only; Music industry)

    August 1996 — Present (13 years)

    Morettiman Music™ is a production company geared to offering new, creative and artistic insight to the highest production quality of recorded music as well as making an active contribution to music education and community outreach by teaching innovative programs, masterclasses, and clinics to prospective students of music at the middle school, high school, and college levels.

  • Professional Freelance Drummer & Percussionist

    Steve Moretti Music

    (Music industry)

    January 1991 — Present (18 years 7 months)

    Touring, Recording, Performing in all genres of music, ranging from Classical, Latin, Brazilian, Big Band, Funk to Rock. Excellent sight reading skills and able to adapt to any style of music. Well seasoned in the studio as well as the road. Endorsed by Pearl Drums, Paiste Cymbals, Aquarian Drum Heads, Vic Firth Sticks, Audix Microphones, and use Futuresonic in-ear monitors. Garnered two Grammy® nominations.

  • Co-Founder

    Bay Area Music, Inc.

    (Music industry)

    June 2002 — June 2007 (5 years 1 month)

    Bay Area Music, Inc, is a week long, intense summer chamber music workshop for young musicians from ages 12-college in the Tampa Bay Area. These young musicians get to work closely with Principal players from the Florida Orchestra, Florida West Coast Symphony and faculty from USF and Stetson University, among other special guest artists.

  • Artistic Director

    The Palladium Theater

    (Non-Profit; 1-10 employees; Music industry)

    April 2002 — April 2004 (2 years 1 month)

    In charge of the artistic vision for the theater, raising the level of programming, and implementing new ways to reach a wider range of audiences. Served on the Artistic Advisory Committee of the Encore Chamber Music Series, which is still a major part of the theater today. Also installed and redesigned the entire sound system.

  • Drummer

    Big Kahuna and The Copa Cat Pack

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Music industry)

    January 1998 — July 2003 (5 years 7 months)

    Drummer for this high energy, Concord recording artist group. Released 5 records on the Concord Music Group label. Shared the stage with: Rosemary Clooney, Michael Feinstein, Linda Eder, Toni Tennille, Mourine McGovern, Ann Hampton Callaway, The Count Basie Orchestra, Lionel Hampton, DeeDee Bridgewater, among others.
