Barry Threw’s Summary

I work to leverage technology to catalyze new aesthetics for the creation, performance, and presentation of forward looking artistic works. In particular, I am involved with the development and support of systems and tools for rich immersive media experience; combining surround sound, video, network, and audience interaction. I excel at the integration of disparate hardware and software technologies.

Additionally, I work with content creation as an audio engineer, sound designer, composer, and electronic musician. My aesthetics are focused on visceral, tangible sound, ambiance, and creating a sense of space for the listener.

Barry Threw’s Specialties:

Media Technology Solutions, Systems Integration, Audio and Video Engineering Services, Programming, Aesthetic Consulting

Barry Threw’s Experience

  • Software Director

    Keith McMillen Instruments

    (Music industry)

    November 2006 — Present (2 years 9 months)

    Keith McMillen Instruments makes innovative technology for string instruments. Our products let musicians that are familiar with traditional string instruments (violin, viola, cello, bass, or guitar) use the computer as an extension of their current playing ability. New technology inspires new art, and we hope that our products will enable performers to take their music in directions never before possible.

    Our first two flagship products are the K-Bow, the first commercially available wireless sensor bow, and StringPort, a polyphonic string-to-USB 2.0 converter with a unique, powerful, and extensible software suite that greatly expands the synthesis and processing palette of guitarists, violinists, bassists, and other stringed instrument players.

  • Systems Engineer

    Recombinant Media Labs

    (Media Production industry)

    June 2006 — Present (3 years 2 months)

    Recombinant Media Labs is an immersive media research, development, and presentation space. We explore the emerging medium of "spatial media synthesis", the merging of surround sound and surround video, and their placement in space.

    • System design and programming of specialized tools for surround cinema content creation and support.
    • Video engineering for real-time and prerecorded works in a 10-screen, 360-degree, surround cinema space.
    • Audio engineering for 16-channel surround sound space, and traditional record production facility.

Using enhanced instruments coupled through an intelligent computer network, we create new music that is reactive, introspective, and fluid; resulting in surprising complexity and intimacy.
