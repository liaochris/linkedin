Dr Mariann Hardey’s Summary

You can classify me as one of the few authorities on social media, innovation and creativity.

I have already advised to business and leading companies around the world on potential social media innovation strategies and drawn on these experiences in my PhD thesis 'Seriously social: making connections in the Information Age' funded by the Economic Social Research Council (ESRC) UK. My research charts the rise of digital social networks and associated media in the lives of young people. The study draws attention to the increasingly participatory nature of technology, and characterises how such resources have become ubiquitous and are seen as 'everyday' essential hubs of information. Facebook is just one of my 'case studies' and distraction activities.

As well as advising to a wide range of organisations about social media including the BBC, British Telecom BT, Channel 4, Coutts Bank, Natwest, Post Office, Royal Bank of Scotland RBS and Sky TV, I have offered a critical evaluation about social media resources to various Web 2.0 Start-ups including WhatTalent.com and Moonri.se.com, the corporate and education sector as well as UK Government based agencies.

As a long-time student of the social theory, politics and literature I am both a keen observer and participant of social networks and new technology. My research represents one of the leading studies about the impact of social networks, social media, SNSs and Web 2.0. Working at both an academic and professional level my work has already provided a conceptual guide to understand the consequences of social media that motivate social action and facilitate digital communication.
