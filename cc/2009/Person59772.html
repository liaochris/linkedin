Liz Ryan’s Summary

Twitter: asklizryan
Ask Liz Ryan Online Community: http://www.asklizryan.com/group
NING Site: http://asklizryanhr.ning.com


I'm a Human Resources strategist and commentator. I consult with employers on their acquisition and retention of smart people. I deliver keynote presentations in the US and abroad. I help individuals with their job-search and career-change issues. I write about the workplace, HR leadership, and online community for Yahoo!, Business Week, AllBusiness.com, and LifeMeetsWork.com, and provide radio commentary for BBC Radio and NPR.

You can join the Ask Liz Ryan online discussion community at http://www.yahoogroups.com/group/asklizryan
There are 25,000 of us sharing business, careers and "life" advice there!

My background: I was a Fortune 500 Human Resources leader (U.S. Robotics) and then the co-founder of the venture-backed startup Ucentric Systems, now a division of Motorola. In 1999 I launched the email group ChicWIT, which over time became WorldWIT, the world's largest online network for professional women. In April 2007 I launched Ask Liz Ryan, an HR think tank and consultancy, as well as the global Ask Liz Ryan online community.

I'm a keynote speaker, consultant and author on workplace issues, networking and work/life management.

I write a weekly workplace column for Business Week online, and am the Yahoo!HotJobs networking expert. I give on-air Human Resources and workplace commentary for BBC Radio and NPR's "Morning Edition." I am the Careers Expert for LifeMeetsWork.com. I consult with organizations on their people-management, leadership and HR issues. In 2006 I wrote Happy About Online Networking: The Virtual-ly Simple Guide to Building Business Relationships. I also work with a small number of career-changers and professionals looking for career guidance.

I live in Boulder, Colorado with my husband and five children, and sing opera and musical theatre for fun. My website is www.asklizryan.com

Liz Ryan’s Specialties:

Workplace columnist for Business Week online, on-air workplace commentator for NPR's "Morning Edition" and BBC Radio and for CNN, CNBC, MSNBC et al. Expertise in Human Resources and the changing workplace; Diversity and Inclusion; networking, particularly online networking; work-life management; and women in entrepreneurism and corporate leadership. Writer and speaker on post-millennium workplace issues and work/family conflict.
