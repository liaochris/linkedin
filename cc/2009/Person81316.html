Rachel Martin’s Summary

Rachel Martin Design is an eco-friendly graphic design studio producing creative solutions to help businesses and non-profits build unique brand messages with positive social and environmental impacts. The studio is committed to high quality and sustainable design, which is evident in its attention to detail for innovative and intelligent communication materials.

Rachel Martin, Principal, is a senior graphic designer with over a decade of design and communications experience. As a green advocate, she collaborates with eco-conscious leaders on a national level in marketing, packaging, product design, promotions, web development, architecture and more. She believes designers have the power to lead the way in communicating the importance of positive socially and ecologically responsible messages and implements these values into her projects and everyday life.

Before relocating to Charlotte, NC, Rachel was the senior designer and project manager of Corporate Communications in New York City for Louis Dreyfus, an international group of diversified businesses that is one of the largest privately held companies in the world. She had the honor of working with Tom Wood of award-winning firm, Wood Design, who collaborated with such talents as Richard Saul Wurman, Massimo Vignelli and Tibor Kalman. Her work has been honored with awards from Applied Arts, Creativity, HOW, I.D. and Print Magazine. Rachel began her career in advertising as a graphic designer at BBDO Worldwide. She graduated from Hofstra University with a Bachelor of Science degree in Fine Arts and Graphic Design.

Rachel is the Membership Director of the Charlotte chapter of AIGA, the professional association for design, an adopter of the Designers Accord, member of Design Can Change and DESIGN 21: Social Design Network and actively involved with the AIGA Center for Sustainable Design.
