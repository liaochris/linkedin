Christopher Dean’s Summary

- Have worked in the field of application and data integration and web technologies for the last 10 years utilizing technology such as Java/JSP, Adobe Flex, Actionscript 3, ColdFusion, XML, HTML, Ajax, and relational databases.

- Have a U.S. patent and several publications in these areas.

- A firm believer in test-driven/automated development processes and Agile Development methodologies and has extensive knowledge of such tools including Ant, xUnit, Subversion, Eclipse, ColdSpring, and Mach-II.

- Currently the Software Architect and Technology Analyst for the Enterprise Software and Data Services (ESDS) group of the Engineering and Science Contract Group (ESCG) for the NASA Johnson Space Center and the Senior Architect for Lighthouse Data Services, LLC.

- Two important projects include the Lunar Sample Database web application (using Mach-II, Coldfusion, Coldspring, and CFUnit) charged with managing a U.S. National Treasure and the XIM (eXtensible Information Model) schema-less database program for modeling all the systems of the next generation Lunar Lander (dubbed Altair) using Adobe Flex and ColdFusion.

- Previously was the VP of Engineering for Kiboga Systems Inc. and the manager and lead engineer on both the patented RIDE (Real-time Integration and Data Exploration) and DILE (Data Integration for Law Enforcement) systems with RIDE being a java agent-based, domain-independent, integration platform and DILE, a web-based law enforcement integration platform currently being used by over 1000 officers at the Dallas Police Department and other departments in the surrounding cities.

- Was the lead engineer on the Integrated Resource Management System, a Java/C++/CORBA integration platform, developed under the Launch Operations Support Contract for the Cape Canaveral Air Force Station.

- Three years of experience in advanced distributed simulation and computer generated forces research for the U.S. Army.
