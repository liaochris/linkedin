Morten Blaabjerg’s Summary

I've been working with film, theatre, video and digital media platforms for the last 10 years.

On the side, I've managed to complete two university degrees, in History and Applied Visual Communications respectively. I've built wikis, board and computer games and abstract academic systems. I've juggled with texts, code, images, video and other kinds of material in a plethora of genres, setups and technological platforms. Last, but not least, I've written a thesis on the introduction of the Maxim Gun in colonial Africa.

All of these activities have given me one particular asset more than any other : an intimate knowledge of how the internet works. In part from the perspective of someone looking for very particular and special information, and for niche products of all sorts. And not the least from the point of view of someone producing niche products, looking for an audience, downloaders, buyers and opportunities to make a living of producing something spectacular, but economically limited, at least in the traditional sense. But the internet is changing this - and in our new company Kaplak, we're building peer production tools which will help individual producers and companies sell products on the slim end of the long tail.

Morten Blaabjerg’s Experience

  • Founder and CEO

    Kaplak

    (Partnership; 1-10 employees; Internet industry)

    May 2007 — Present (2 years 3 months)

    In Kaplak we're building internet distribution tools for cultural products on the slim end of the long tail, and making a sound business out of it too. We're building and strengthening peer production tools in order to create better ways to match production and demand, than has hitherto been possible. This creates great value for millions of people, who would otherwise have few or difficult ways to make their products available, or find the products they need. Otherwise unsuccessful queries, made every day on web search engines and indexes, and on p2p networks, is a huge business opportunity.

    Crewscut.com

    (Privately Held; Myself Only; Internet industry)

    November 2004 — Present (4 years 9 months)

    Crewscut.com is a wiki dedicated to cultural projects, which I've lived with for a couple of years now, and learned a lot from. The wiki is first and foremost a great personal tool for it's users, to keep track of projects, thoughts, interesting links, texts, images and details of obscure information, which doesn't fit anywhere else. The wiki is mainly written and administrated in Danish, and only to a minor degree in English.

  • Archival Assistant

    Landsarkivet for Fyn, Statens Arkiver

    (Government Agency; 11-50 employees; Information Services industry)

    September 2006 — December 2006 (4 months)

    Landsarkivet for Fyn, the regional archives institution under the National Danish Archives (Statens Arkiver) received a greater than usual amount of material in 2006, as a result of the re-organization of the Danish regional structure and bureaucracy. Additional funding were secured to bring in extra manpower to secure, sort and package the archives safely, knowledgeably and speedily. It was a great team to work with, a mixture of historians, student aids and archival experts, and a welcome refreshing change from research on my thesis.

  • Editor-in-Chief

    Ude af fokus, Odense Studenterradio

    (Non-Profit; 1-10 employees; Broadcast Media industry)

    August 2004 — August 2005 (1 year 1 month)

    "Ude af fokus" (literally meaning "out of focus") was a weekly academic radio talkshow featured on Odense Studenterradio, the grassroot's radio station for students in Odense. The program typically featured one or more guests, invited to discuss a particular topic of the week, from an academic angle. It was a lot of fun and also a lot of hard work to be inventive enough to do a live radio show every week for a complete season, and I/we learned a lot from it too.

  • Website Admin

    History Dept., University of Southern Denmark

    (Educational Institution; 51-200 employees; Education Management industry)

    August 2002 — August 2004 (2 years 1 month)

    The Dept. of History at University of Southern Denmark was in need of a website, which combined the daily need of the staff to communicate with students and the students' need for online communication of events, seminars, social activities, new research, jobs & career opportunities etc. to other students. I merged two old existing websites into one and created a new and lighter approach, which worked well for a few years. Sadly, the site became a tad neglected as funds ran thin, other admins carried on the work, and the "local sites" of faculties and departments collided with wishes of the central administration for a grand, general IT and "branding" strategy. But the site continues to be in use.

Rubicon provides a spirited and academically highly vigorous and ambitious environment for scholars and students alike, so much so that an "official" historical periodical based at the History dept. at the university does not exist.

  • Founder and CEO

    Hard Joy PD

    (Privately Held; Myself Only; Computer Software industry)

    June 1993 — December 1994 (1 year 7 months)

    Hard Joy PD was a mail order software library. It contained thousands of public domain and shareware titles on floppy disks for the Amiga computer, which were labouriously manually copied and distributed to customers in packed envelopes. In the days before the internet made software like this accessible to everyone, there was a thriving business model in making this type of software available via mail order.
