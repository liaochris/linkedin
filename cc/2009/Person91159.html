Nigel Shardlow’s Summary

I like trying to do things that nobody has quite worked out how to do yet: this, more than anything else, defines my career path.

I started out as a professional philosopher, but figured out pretty soon that the solitary life of an academic wasn't for me. I switched to thinking about how the internet and digital media could be used to support marketing communications and other business processes. That was fourteen years ago now. I knew nothing about business, but learned fast. This line of thought took me from a little web design start-up to Orange, the telco, where I made a lot of noise getting 'web stuff' onto the corporate radar. It paid off, in the end.

Lately, I've been doing a lot of new product development work, which usually means thinking about customers, technology, processes and commercial models. I have also been engaged on a number of consulting assignments, both directly and with other consulting businesses. Recent projects have included clients such as Unilever, Apple, and a couple of technology start-up businesses.

I set up a business with a colleague back in 1999 to provide companies with reporting tools that their people might actually want to use. We called it Vanilla Internet, 'internet' being a buzzword at the time. A lot of what we do now has little to do with the internet.

I am also the co-founder of another business, Zygo Communications Ltd, which has developed an incredibly powerful and usable group-based mobile communications product, available now at www.zygohubs.com.
