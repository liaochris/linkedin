Jason Shinn’s Summary

Jason M. Shinn is an attorney licensed to practice law in Michigan and is also admitted to the Federal District Courts for the Eastern and Western District of Michigan, Northern District of Ohio, and the Bankruptcy Court for the Eastern and Western Districts of Michigan.
Jason counsels companies regarding legal matters related to their general business operations, including addressing:
• Trade secret protection, and other non-patent intellectual property issues;
• Cyberlaw and e-commerce;
• Contract drafting, review, and negotiation;
• Legal and regulatory obligations concerning the protection of employee and customer confidential information;
• Preference claims under bankruptcy law;
• Drafting non-compete/restrictive covenants, record retention policies, and technology policies; and
• Issues arising under the Federal Computer Fraud & Abuse Act.

Mr. Shinn is also an experienced trial attorney available to litigate these issues and other business torts, if necessary. Jason's litigation practice is further focused on defending professional liability claims, including those against technology professionals, directors and officers, and claims involving employment matters.

Since 2001, Mr. Shinn has focused on addressing the interplay between legal and technology issues in response to meeting increasing legal, judicial, and regulatory obligations. This combined legal/technology experience – often referred to as “e-discovery” – provides the foundation to highlight a client’s litigation risks, opportunities, and options. At the end of the day, Mr. Shinn’s e-discovery experience provides a road map for implementing cost-saving and risk reduction strategies for companies involved in litigation.

Jason Shinn’s Specialties:

Contract drafting and negotiation, electronic discovery management, risk reduction, risk reduction, defending against request for direct access to company databases and/or computer systems, Computer Fraud and Abuse Act claims, establishing trade secret programs and litigating such issues.
