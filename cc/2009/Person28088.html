Shara Cohen’s Summary

Dr Cohen has an Applied Biology Degree (1985) and a doctorate in Immunology (1989). She has worked as a scientist in several prestigious laboratories around the world [including Addenbrookes' Hospital (Cambridge), The Kennedy Institute of Rheumatology (London), The Mount Sinai Hospital (New York) and The Royal Postgraduate Medical School (London)]. During her academic career she taught and supervised medical students, scientists and clinicians at BSc, MSc, MRCPath and PhD levels. She has been a senior scientist, lecturer and head of research groups. During her long research career she established international collaborations, was awarded UK and international grants and published a large number of scientific articles. She has been a senior scientist, university lecturer (UCL) and head of a research group.

Dr Cohen continues to
-present science for non scientists
-write peer reviewed publications for scientific journals and -presents her work at international scientific meetings
-edit and contribute to scientific books
-write scientific reviews

Additional work includes being an Independent Scientific Advisor for example she is on the Scientific Advisory Board for Cordlife Pte Ltd, Singapore (for advisory board click here). She is also a Mentor to women science undergraduates in the US and is on the UK Database of Women Experts in Science, Engineering and Technology and is listed in the Whos' Who in Medicine and Healthcare and The International Who's Who of Professionals.

Dr Cohen is a
-Fellow of the Royal College of Pathologists
-Fellow of the World Innovation Foundation
-Fellow of the Royal Society of Medicine
-Chartered Biologist and Member of the Institute of Biology
-Member of the Royal Institution of Great Britain
-Member of the British Association for the Advancement of Science
