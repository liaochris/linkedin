John James O'Brien, CRM’s Summary

25+ years combining multi-sectoral internal leadership and external consulting & education roles. Seasoned executive/directorate manager of enterprise-wide services with expertise in knowledge, change & records management. Experienced in:

* finance & admin: budgeting incl. operations / projects to 42 mil. CAD; business continuity planning; presentation to Cabinet committees; management of multiple responsibility centers; outcome-based evaluation;

* human resources: competency-based hiring / termination; performance planning / review; conflict resolution; litigation support; talent development; FTE justification / allocation; negotiating union - management agreement to establish a new classification;

* information & records management: policy; multimedia, Web 2.0, intellectual capital mapping; IT / User interface / functional reqs / classification systems; retention, storage, vital records / disaster planning, manuals, micrographics, media conversion;

* facilities: relocations, mergers, 'built-to-suit' projects for climate controlled, high security repositories; disaster recovery;

* equipment & supplies: saving 4+ million CAD in annual recurrent costs by establishing horizontal collaboration, implementing records retention plans and negotiating procurement credit for release of non-standard equipment to off-set replacement cost; region-wide deployment;

* program renewal:
- created comprehensive, integrated records management branch serving multi-disciplinary, sensitive program areas;
- analyzed content management needs;
- restructured Hong Kong’s Government Records Service for better positioning toward electronic records management;

* facilitation / presentation services (undergraduate, graduate, vocational education; conferences, corporate event speaker);

* compliance evaluation (legislation, policy, standards including ISO 9000, ISO 15489); access, privacy & ethics; conflict resolution; strategic / tactical project management.

    (Government Administration industry)

    November 2002 — October 2005 (3 years )

    Selected from among international candidates and brought to Hong Kong to lead a transition away from traditional paper-based archives and records management to undertake the challenge of technological change and electronic records management (ERM). Achievements include:
