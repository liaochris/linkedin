Tobias Thornblad’s Experience

  • Management Consultant

    CIP Professional Services AB

    (Management Consulting industry)

    June 2009 — Present (6 months)

    CIP Professional Services AB is research-based development and business consultancy company. CIP PS is a subsidiary of Chalmers Industrial Technology Foundation and the operative branch of Center for Intellectual Property (CIP) at Chalmers University of Technology and University of Gothenburg. Drawing upon a background of over 15 years of interdisciplinary research CIP PS works as a development partner or consultant helping companies and other technology-intensive organizations to build, manage and govern technology-based business.

  • Academic position

    Center for Intellectual Property (CIP)

    (Education Management industry)

    June 2009 — Present (6 months)

    CIP is a joint center between University of Gothenburg and Chalmers University with the mission to drive and facilitate a shift to a knowledge-based economy. Specifically, CIP shall work to support industry, academia and society as a whole to build wealth and welfare from early-stage innovation. The aim is thus to serve the global knowledge economy by coordinating and providing competencies, networks and research projects locally as well as internationally.

    CIP is interfacing with a wide range of industry and academic partners to set the future agenda of knowledge-based business. The visions are implemented practically through coordination of various development projects, events and initiatives.

    More about CIP: http://www.cip.chalmers.se/

    The Institute for Innovation and Entrepreneurship is an initiative from the University of Gothenburg supported by the Stena foundation. The institute will be a hub for research, education and collaboration in the field of innovation and entrepreneurship. The institute will be an integrated part of the Gothenburg University innovation system by supporting innovation and entrepreneurship processes.

    More about IIE: http://www.hgu.gu.se/item.aspx?id=17007

  • Intellectual Capital Manager (Intern)

    Dow AgroSciences

    (Public Company; DOW; Chemicals industry)

    June 2008 — May 2009 (1 year )

    This position comprised two internships working in DAS' ICM group where I participated in a number of projects. Most of the biotech projects were related to management of intellectual property in plant genomics, genetic engineering, gene therapy, trait management, and identification of external technologies. Some of the typical tasks included technology mapping, IP landscape visualizations, plant variety right strategizing, IP due diligence, identification of external capabilities, recommendation of strategic fits with R&D objectives, etc.. Other projects included the development of a range of IA/IP tools and templates to be used for day-to-day IC management as well as to be included in an internal training program.

    January 2008 — January 2009 (1 year 1 month)

    ICM Advice is a non-profit student run consulting organization primarily aiming to support scientists with strategic and practical advice in the field of intellectual property rights, commercialization of research results and business ideas. The main objective of the organization is to assist researchers and inventors while applying the acquired knowledge and skills gained from the School of Intellectual Capital Management (ICM) to improve and develop the integration between the local research community and the existing innovation system.
