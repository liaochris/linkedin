Fran Battaglia’s Summary

Currently I'm working full-time for Solid (www.solidla.com) in Santa Monica. I've worked as a freelance Flame Artist between various projects since 1997. Visual effects work includes national commercials; network studio image campaigns, Upfront and on-air promos; independent and feature films.

Global Shift Entertainment is a Joint Venture with Golden Roads, LLC. Plans include creating digital media for the planet's first sustainable live music venue, a renovated landmark with a 40-year live music history called State Bridge. This state-of-the-art, outdoor venue along the Colorado River and Rocky Mountains will be a benefit concert every show.

In 2008, I traveled to Liberia and Kenya documenting new and existing humanitarian and fair trade projects (food, water, education, and micro-loan programs). Earlier in 2008 I produced, directed and edited HUB's 11-disc Global Empowerment Series, featuring speakers Kimmie Weeks, Michael Beckwith and Charlie Gay.

Education, work and life experience includes documenting surfers riding giant waves in the middle of the ocean and creating big wave surf films, producing live awards programs (XXL and Surfer Magazine Video Awards), shooting internationally (TV / Film production on the WCT pro surfing tour), and editing (Sublime's documentary, Quiksilver's Surfers of Fortune, TV programs).

I'm grateful to have worked with 2006 Nobel Peace Prize winner / Grameen Bank founder Dr. Muhammad Yunus, Historian / Author Dr. Howard Zinn and many others, including athletes, artists, designers, inventors and humble effective leaders. While in college, I played varsity soccer, worked as a newspaper reporter and radio broadcaster. I also studied abroad in Costa Rica and completed an independent study of bias in the news media using communications research methodology. I've won awards as a writer, producer, cameraman, editor and visual effects artist (latest project news at www.nomads-cafe.com).

Fran Battaglia’s Specialties:

Integrating forward-thinking projects with digital platforms. Bridging the gap between technical and creative. Managing artists, clients and administrators. Writing, producing and editing original programs. Flame visual effects compositing. Color-correction. Directing multi-camera HD productions; web video and DVD Production.

  • Director | Big Wave Filmmaker

    Surfline.com

    (Privately Held; Sports industry)

    January 2000 — December 2003 (4 years)

    Surfline.com merged with and then split from Swell.com around 2002. Located in Huntington Beach, Surfline.com is the most popular wave forecasting company on the planet, serving surfers, boaters, lifeguards and coast guards on every coastline in the world. On January 19, 2001, perfect conditions arrived at an offshore big wave spot called the Cortes Bank, which had been monitored for over 10 years. Documenting the first 60ft+ surf session at Cortes Bank 100 miles west of San Diego was a once in a lifetime experience that landed on the front page of the LA Times the next day. The footage was also utilized to make two documentaries, the first for Swell.com with Jay Moriarity (R.I.P.), Flea, Gerlach, Snips and Laird. The second combined action, music and science for Surfline and a target high school library audience, "Making The Call: Big Waves of the North Pacific". (http://www.surfline.com/mtc/04/) The footage was also licensed to NBC Dateline and Activision.
