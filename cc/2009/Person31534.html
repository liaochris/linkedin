Erik Schwartz’s Summary

I've been doing new media for a long time, the serial port controlled laser disc age is when I started. Before CD-ROMs were invented. I still think hypercard was one of the most underrated tools of all time.

I was the first Yahoo! hired to work in Entertainment. I managed the initial creation of the core suite of Y!'s entertainment products. Y! Movies, Y! TV, Y! Music, Y! Radio

I was also the one who talked Jerry, David, Tim and Jeff into getting Yahoo! into the games business, the result of which is http://games.yahoo.com.


After a few years of being a dad, two solo transpacific races, a little consulting and mostly retirement, I'm back in the game with our VC funded start up called Foneshow.

Erik Schwartz’s Experience

  • CEO/Co-Founder

    Foneshow Inc

    (Privately Held; Wireless industry)

    August 2006 — Present (3 years)

    Foneshow mobilizes the radio industry and changes it from a broadcast model to a on-demand narrowcast model.

    Foneshow has built a telephony-based distribution platform for short-form audio (primarily news/talk radio and podcast programming). The platform leverages the cellular telephone network and enables users to subscribe to, access, publish, share, and consume short-form audio programming immediately from virtually any cell phone. The system features the very rapid propagation of programming from the creator to the consumer. Patents are pending on aspects of our technology.

    Stephanie Miller, Allen Hunt, and Bill Press, amongst many others use Foneshow to reach their audience.

    In 2009 we have been pushing into sports media. We've added NASCAR, Tony Bruno, RotoWire, and others. Many more to come.

    CEI Community Ventures, Masthead Venture Partners, and the Small Enterprise Growth Fund of Maine are investors in Foneshow.
