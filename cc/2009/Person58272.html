Didier Lebon’s Summary

Didier Lebon is a French Avocat admitted to the Bar in 1987. He was a partner in a French law firm based in Lille, Paris and London. He set up his own practice in July 2006 and represents foreign clients in a broad range of international commercial and corporate matters in France and in Europe.

Didier Lebon was educated in France and in England. After obtaining his law degree (licence en droit) at the University of Lille, he received a Maitrise en Droit in 1982. Then he obtained a postgraduate diploma at Lille University (Diplôme d’Etudes Approfondies in Private Law, 1983) which included research at the University of Warwick, England. After two years teaching law in the Ivory Coast, he received a second postgraduate degree at the University of Paris Sorbonne (Diplôme d’Etudes Approfondies in Private International Law and International Trade Law, 1986).

He has written academic works in private international law: "The applicable Law to Divorce in English and French Private International Law", 1983; "The Remarriage of the Divorced Spouse in English Private International Law", 1985; “Forum and Applicable law in internet transactions” (published in “Lawyering in the International market”, Transnational Publishers, Inc, New York, 1999). He has been a lecturer in this field at the University of Lille from 1986 to 1994.

He currently teaches International Trade Law at the EDHEC Business School (Lille, France) and at the MDI Alger Business School (Algiers, Algeria) and leds a seminar on 'Drating legal documents in English" at the University of Lille (School of Law).

Didier Lebon’s Specialties:

Contracts, distribution agreements, franchising law, agency law, due diligence and acquisition, arbitration, employment law, dispute resolution, commercial litigation, private international law, international trade law,
