Alex Chompff’s Summary

My first job is to be a good father and husband, and mostly this entails being a reasonable human being.

In order to accomplish this first job, I have to earn a living.

This leads to my day job. As far as that goes, my real specialty is solving business problems. To do this, I generally employ business process design and process reengineering techniques, with a strong focus on developing and delivering practical solutions. It happens that the solutions are usually rooted in project management philosophies and delivered hand in glove with technology systems.

I come from a business operations background, with emphasis in high tech, venture capital, biotech and most recently, State government. While I am a capable technologist from the perspective of your everyday business person, I'm not a technical engineer.

It's very important to ask questions, to learn from those who have gone before, to listen to what's being said, and to think deeply on the problem being examined.

Solutions have to be practical, grounded in reality and executable. Otherwise, they're just good ideas.
