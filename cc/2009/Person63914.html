  • Chair of the Newspaper and Periodicals Working Group

    IFRRO

    (Publishing industry)

    February 2009 — Present (11 months)

    The Working Group provides relevant information to all IFRRO members on the analogue and digital licensing of material from newspapers and periodicals. This includes information on requirements for digital services in the news and journalistic media, and how these can be monitised for the rightholders of original content; and how the development of digital media business models can be sustained.

    The International Federation of Reproduction Rights Organisations (IFRRO) is an independent organisation established on the basis of the fundamental international copyright principles embodied in the Berne and Universal Copyright Conventions. Its purpose is to facilitate, on an international basis, the collective management of reproduction and other rights relevant to copyrighted works through the co-operation of national Reproduction Rights Organisations (RROs). Collective or centralised rights management is preferable where individual exercise of rights is impractical.

  • Project Manager Licensing Business Sector

    Cedar BV

    (Privately Held; Publishing industry)

    September 2002 — December 2005 (3 years 4 months)

    In 2003 legislation made it mandatory for the Dutch business sector to pay authors a fee through Stichting Reprorecht for photocopying copyright protected works.
    It was initially decided to ensure that the payment model complied as closely as possible with the relevant law; a payment structure relating to usage. In this respect it was important to know the percentage of all of the photocopies made within a business in respect of which reprographic reproduction fees are payable. This was determined in the case of each industry based on research carried out by TNS and Veldkamp.

    However, many businesses appeared to doubt the accuracy of the rates determined for their particular sector. Industrial associations approached the Minister of Justice, because they wanted a different scheme.
    A new, simplified scheme was introduced at the request of the business sector and the various industrial associations. On 18 March 2004 an agreement was signed by representatives of VNO-NCW [Confederation of Industry and Employers], MKB Nederland [SME Association] and Stichting Reprorecht under the watchful eye of the Minister of Justice.

    Implementation of the new scheme commenced immediately and more than 250,000 businesses had received a proposal for payment by the autumn of 2004. The number of problems specific to implementation remained limited and were largely resolved by a monitoring team. The Regeling Reprorecht Bedrijfsleven [Business Sector Reprographic Reproduction Rights Plan] got off to a successful start.
    Cedar BV is a service company for copyright organisations in the Netherlands.
