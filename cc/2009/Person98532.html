Thom Tillis’s Summary

Thom has 27 years of work experience with the last 18 years at Price Waterhouse/IBM. Thom has led a number of large-scale, complex projects involving business process reengineering and the use of advanced technology. Thom has directly managed more than two million project hours of work. Thom has a track record for leading practices that spanned the United States.

Thom is well versed in industry issues and priorities in financial services, media & entertainment, energy & utilities, State government, and telecommunications. He has helped clients with solving complex problems related to optimizing organization structures and improving performance. Thom has significant experience with customer relationship management (CRM), financial management, and supply chain/operations management.

Thom has served on the senior leadership team of a $1B business unit and he has had direct P&L responsibility for a $200M annual business operation. He has led a number of successful business growth initiatives, and he has managed the difficult task of downsizing in the face of a challenging economic climate. Thom has demonstrated a strong ability to manage expenses, drive revenue and oversee administrative processes in large organizations.

In 2006 Thom was elected to the North Carolina House of Representatives and he was re-elected in 2008. In 2008, Thom was elected by the Republican Caucus members to be the House Republican Whip, one of only two leadership positions in the caucus. Thom has demonstrated the value of his management skills in a highly complex, dynamic environment. Thom's business expertise has facilitated his appointment to key House Standing Committees and Select Committees including:

House Financial Institutions
House Science and Technology
House Ways and Means / Broadband (Vice Chair)
House & Senate Joint Select Committee on Emergency and Disaster Preparedness
House & Senate Joint Select Committee on Economic Recovery
