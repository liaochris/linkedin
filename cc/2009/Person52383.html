  • Chairman

    Atlanta Fulton Public Library Board of Trustees

    (Government Relations industry)

    June 2005 — June 2007 (2 years 1 month)

    Chairman of the Board of Trustees for the Atlanta-Fulton Public Library System that included the internationally renowned Auburn Research Library (ARL) on Civil Rights; lead meetings of an 11-member board and Executive Director in approving policy and implementing strategic facility plans for the library system (comprised of 34 branches with over 400 staff, 2.3 million holdings and approximately a half a million patrons) and overseeing a 34-million-dollar annual budget.

  • Board Member

    Kids Voting USA

    (Media Production industry)

    1991 — 2005 (14 years )

    Board Member mission to guide and preserve the democratic process by providing resources for teaching in classrooms and conducting first field experiences of the power and meaning of voting for millions of students k-12 at polling precincts. Helped formulate public awareness campaign. I and others participated in direct outreach to members of Congress for funding, successfully to the tune of $2-million. Personally wrote the published John S. and James L. Knights Foundation 50th Anniversary Annual Report overview of the KVUSA project.

  • Executive Producer CNN Student News

    CNN

    (Public Company; TWX; Broadcast Media industry)

    2000 — 2002 (2 years )

    Creator/Developer and Executive Producer (1989-1998, 2000-2002)
    Creator/Developer of CNN Newsroom (CNN Student News), daily program (with teaching guide) that positioned CNN in 30-thousand middle and high school classrooms in the United States. As Creator/Developer, guided the program brand, which was the centerpiece of TBS’s government affairs outreach and the cornerstone of Cable in the Classroom Initiatives, and earned the George Foster Peabody award for coverage and comprehensive study guide materials of the 1991 Soviet Coup. In 2001, as Executive Producer, helmed CNN’s Emmy Award coverage of the 9/11 World Trade Center Attacks and aftermath and launched online counterpart CNN Student News.

  • Senior Vice President News Features

    CNN

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; TWX; Broadcast Media industry)

    January 1991 — January 1998 (7 years 1 month)

    Senior Vice President/Executive Producer for Cable News Network (CNN) the first 24-hour broadcast television news channel with unparalleled coverage, reaching 2 billion people in more than 200 countries and territories. Lead ten award-winning production units consisting of 75 staff members responsible for the development of new innovative documentary specials and other target marketing programs, which garners multi-million dollar revenue streams for the Company. Direct report EVP, Vice Chairman, President/Chairman.
