Michael Riggs’s Summary

A key member of FAO’s global team to enhance the positive impact of information and communication technology (ICT) in agricultural and rural development, and increase availability, accessibility and application of good practices.

The lead facilitator of the e-Agriculture community of practice, with over 5,600 individuals from more than 150 countries, who are focused on improving ICT4D.

Focused on issues of people, processes and content in ICT.

Engaging in active knowledge sharing, through traditional and new methods and tools, to improve our understanding and performance in solving complex, multi-disciplinary challenges.

Prior to joining FAO, experienced in the private sector with responsibility for technology transfer, project development and coordination of information flows between international partners.

Educated in both economics and organizational management, with extensive on the job experience in cross-cultural management and communication.

Michael Riggs’s Specialties:

knowledge sharing methods and tools, catalyzing communities of practice and international collaboration, conceptualizing and communicating new ideas, information surveys, building individual and organizational capacity in ICT4D
