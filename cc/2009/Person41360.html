Anand Sharma’s Summary

After spending more than three years pursuing dual Masters degree from Arizona State in Information Systems (MSE) and Computer Science (MS) and having spent more than a decade working in IT for Fortune 500 companies like Cisco Systems, Lucent Technologies and Avaya, it's amazingly clear things that I like and don't like.

I like to read and learn and equally enjoy sharing what I have learnt. I enjoy giving talks and presentations to large audiences. Not surprisingly, I like to teach, although I have yet to formally do anything on that front. I enjoy solving problems, a trait that started when I was exposed to solving Trigonometric equations. I try to live by the maxim that context matters, that big picture viewpoint is necessary to better understand things. I pray at the altar of knowledge and worship intellect and insight when I see it. Hence, even after a decade in IT departments of large enterprises, my shangri-la continues to be an Ivy league University.

I like technologies that simplify interactions - be it between human-to-human, human-to-system or system-to-system and the infrastructure that makes it all possible. My experience and current interests includes designing and developing web applications using SOA/WOA paradigm and continue to evangelize the importance of Social Computing/Enterprise Web 2.0 tools in large global organizations. I typically code applications in Java/JEE liberally using frameworks like Spring/Struts in my professional work environment. However, in my own virtual fiefdom, I prefer dynamic languages/MVC frameworks like Python/Djangowith sprinkle of PHP and Perl. I also enjoy hacking away on Linux-based systems, preferably Fedora and Ubuntu. Lately, I have been involved in Dynamic/Adaptive Infrastructure initiatives. Specifically, my focus is around understanding the inner workings of Data Center, DC Virtualization and Distributed Computing.

I am passionate, pragmatist and consider work to be much more than just "work"!
