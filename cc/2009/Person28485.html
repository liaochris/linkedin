Dave Sifry’s Summary

Dave has been building and running software and internet companies for over 20 years. He has started and built 5 companies, varying from enterprise software, linux services, wireless, blog search, to his most recent endeavor, Offbeat Guides, which is making absurdly simple personalized travel guides for over 30,000 destinations around the world. He is founder and Chairman of Technorati, a conversation search engine at http://www.technorati.com/ . He was CEO there for the first 5 years of Technorati's lifetime. He was co-founder and CTO of Sputnik, where he directed the engineering of all Sputnik products, including its new WiFi security and management products, the AP120 and Sputnik Central Control. As co-founder, CTO, and VP of Engineering at Linuxcare, Inc., Dave built Linuxcare's services infrastructure, including its managed services business, a Linux-based, managed-server technology and service for xSPs. Dave is a recognized expert on Leadership development, blogs and the massive changes in the digital media environment, Open Source development, and the Linux operating system. He's created a bunch of moderately interesting websites like Projectdocs, Hoosgot, and myne.ws. He served on the board of Linux International, the advisory board of the National White Collar Crime Center, and the technical advisory board of the National Cybercrime Training Partnership for law enforcement. He has a B.S. in Computer Science from Johns Hopkins University. Dave lived and worked in Kobe, Japan for Mitsubishi Electric, and speaks Japanese in a rusty kansai-ben. He now lives in foggy San Francisco, CA.

Dave Sifry’s Specialties:

Building a high-tech business, Venture Capital funding, technology due diligence, building scalable service architectures, building and leading great teams, doing whatever it takes to get the job done.
