Terry Anderson’s Summary

A writer and editor in hematology, HIV/AIDS, malaria, neurology, oncology, orphan diseases, tuberculosis, pharmacology, drug safety, and drug-drug interaction.

I write and edit for the President’s Emergency Plan for AIDS Relief (PEPFAR), the President’s Advisory Council on HIV and AIDS (PACHA), Auburn Theological Seminary, the World Bank, and the U.S. Food and Drug Administration, and in previous years, for at least 30 other government and nongovernment clients.

A strong believer in community service, I'm a Communities Delegate to the Board of Directors of the Global Fund to Fight AIDS, Tuberculosis and Malaria (i.e., I represent persons living with HIV, TB, and malaria to the Global Fund); a consumer advocate for the National Association of People with AIDS; a weed warrior for Montgomery County, Maryland (i.e., a specialist in invasive plants); a docent at the Smithsonian Institution’s National Museum of Natural History; a tenor in the Cathedral Voices choir at the Washington National Cathedral; Vice President of my homeowners association; and Chairman of the Board of Trustees of Friends of Liberia, a small nonprofit organization dedicated to aiding Liberia.

Terry Anderson’s Specialties:

Proposal and grant writing, human interest articles, volunteerism, international public health.

Terry Anderson’s Experience

    Grant Management Solutions Project

    (International Affairs industry)

    August 2007 — Present (2 years 4 months)

    I work with the President's Emergency Plan for AIDS Relief (PEPFAR) providing technical assistance to countries to better manage their grants from the Global Fund to Fight AIDS, Tuberculosis and Malaria. I travel overseas at least once a month. I write and edit documents of all sorts and work directly with international officials to find the most efficient ways of getting life-saving drugs to the people who need them. My work often involves ensuring that grants in excess of $30 million are being implemented properly.

  • Writer/Editor

    Self-employed

    (Self-Employed; Myself Only; Writing and Editing industry)

    January 1992 — Present (17 years 11 months)

    A writer and editor in the sciences and macroeconomics, my writing and editing skills encompass scientific papers, human interest articles, proposals, and conference proceedings in hematology; HIV/AIDS; malaria; neurology; oncology; orphan diseases; tuberculosis; and pharmacology, drug safety, and drug-drug interaction (particularly antiepileptics, NSAIDS, anticholinergics, anticoagulants, anticonvulsants, benzodiazepines, beta-blockers, chemotherapeutic agents, contraceptives, corticosteroids, and GABAa agonists).
