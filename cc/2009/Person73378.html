Neil Ruston’s Summary

Windows Infrastructure and Directory Services architecture and design, specifically Active Directory design, encompassing security and delegation design as well as NT-AD and AD-AD migrations.

Extensive experience with various 3rd party toolsets - especially those offered by Quest - such as Group Policy Manager, QMM & DMW (Migrations), Intrust (Security Monitoring) and RMAD (Recovery).

Directory synchronisation, involving data analysis, process engineering and Microsoft ILM-based solutions.

I have written and editied several technical publications for Syngress and New Riders, relating to Windows 2000/2003, DNS and Active Directory.

Neil Ruston’s Specialties:

Areas of speciality include: Active Directory infrastructure architecture and design; Active Directory network architecture and design; Security, policy and delegation design; DNS design and integration; user migrations; management tools, such as monitoring and auditing tools; Disparate directory sync; Project management (PRINCE2); Platform governance, service design & delivery and operating models (ITIL).
