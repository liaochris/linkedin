John Nawn’s Summary

I am a business professional who helps organizations grow revenues or increase market share using learning for a competitive advantage.

Some of my WOW projects include:
• Developed a distance learning strategy for the American Medical Association, enabling them to effectively and affordably deliver continuing education to primary care physicians
• Created programs for both clinicians and administrators at the Center for Disease Control, insuring that front line healthcare workers are providing the best possible care, with optimal support and resources, in the most challenging of treatment environments
• Built a state-of-the-art wet lab and a comprehensive curriculum for a medical software company, helping them promote industry standards while driving product and practice innovation

In addition, I’ve served as adjunct faculty at several Chicago-area medical schools including the University of Chicago, Northwestern, and Loyola.

I worked at Northwestern Memorial Hospital’s Institute of Psychiatry on short- and long-term care units, as well as adolescent, geriatric, and substance abuse units.

This was great training for all the corporate work I did subsequently.

Finally, I helped launch an online wellness company that enables employers to reduce their healthcare costs.

John Nawn’s Specialties:

The common thread which weaves throughout all my work is providing:

• the information people need to know
• when they need to know it
• in the manner which is most convenient for them

While I’m obviously passionate about learning, perhaps my greatest talent is my knack for asking good questions. And as more than one really smart person has noted, questions are far more important than answers (and some answers are pretty darn important).
