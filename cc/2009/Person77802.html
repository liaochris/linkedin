Paul Papadimitriou’s Summary

Paul Papadimitriou is a social web otaku or a social evangelist, as he's nicknamed.

Enthusing in the real-time fast-paced web environment, he is the owner of socialevangeli.st, a digital intelligence agency and the official Poken Japan evangelist.

Understanding the mindset shift that digital social marketing is bringing, he specializes in digital influence, social web strategies and their implications in traditional business development.

He is also the co-organizer of Tokyo 2.0, the co-founder of Mobile in Japan and dedicates part of his time & work to non-profit organizations, such as Beers For Books or TEDx Tokyo.

Paul has been working in the business consulting and digital/analog lobby & advocacy field for over ten years. He now lives in Manila and commutes between Tokyo and Geneva. His thoughts reside on the cloud, all linked from paulpapadimitriou.com

Paul holds a master's degree in International Affairs from the Graduate Institute of International Studies in Geneva, Switzerland, speaks several languages, loves the Helvetica font type, his iPhone and photography.



"There's no internet. There are internets. Being from Greek and Finn descent, raised in Switzerland, having lived in Japan and now based in the Philippines, I can see how culture and language affect the way people use technology. It's not only about making these internets connect, it's about creating a platform that allows people to be on the same page when they say hello."
