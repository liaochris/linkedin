Sara Goldberger’s Summary

Having enjoyed life in Eindhoven, The Netherlands where I was contracting as Senior Communications Manager for two months, I now have landed in Paris. This does not only mean a new city and a new country, it also means new job, new industry, new colleagues. In other words - new and fascinating challenges!

Prior to that I was the interim PR & Communications Manager with the AeroSpace and Defence Industries Association of Europe. I am now looking for a new position. I cannot imagine more interesting tasks than PR & Communications so I am looking to stay within this category of work, but am of course ready to change sector.

During my 18 months with ASD Europe, I substantially raised its profile, internally as well as externally. I did this by e.g. launching an advertisement financed bi-annual magazine, ASD Focus, in order to exploit and show ASD's expertise and thought leadership externally.The readership is international decision makers, industry leaders and members. I have established better press and media contacts.

As people manager I have a helicopter view of the work of my direct reports, in practise this means I set targets with the staff for which I am responsible after which I combine regular follow-ups with trust. I have found this is a very efficient way of working; it builds well-organised projects and entities, empowers the staff and encourage professional growth. It also means that the identified goals are achieved on time and within budget.

I have lived and worked in four European countries. I have worked with and managed multinational teams. I see the common cultural and market factors, while at the same time understanding their differences. After an initial "study and thinking phase" I provide creative solutions that take into account corporate traditions. I have found this way of combining the new and inventive with "old ways" a very efficient way of achieving results and targets.
