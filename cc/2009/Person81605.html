Raines Cohen’s Summary

I worked at Lotus when it was 5 people in a basement. Then I went to High School, and started the Newton Apple Kids club. From that experience, I helped start BMUG when I came out to Berkeley, which gave me the connections and background to work in the MacWEEK newsroom, then User Group Connection (an Apple spinoff), Editor of NetProfessional magazine, then launched my own consulting business, Communitarian Visions. I help people build community, whether its coworking shared offices, cohousing neighborhoods, or "Aging In Community" service systems that help keep people in their homes.
