Mirko Vidovic’s Summary

I have been using computers from 1982 and I always kept my motivation when it came to dealing with new technologies. I remember browsing the Usenet comp.* newsgroups as soon as 1992. The following year, I installed Linux for the first time (this was a Sunsite/FTP mirror on a CD and I had to rawrite it onto enough floppies to get it ready for installation). I began designing web sites as soon as 1993 in HTML in 1993, and in Java as soon as 1995. I also worked as a computing teacher and in the print industry. I also have a strong background in Lingo which I taught to young professionals in 1996. In 1997, I migrated the Ecco website to reflect its new Adecco brand. In 1998, I was working in the very Alcatel Alsthom Research group which announced DSL. I then left to Germany where I worked in the performance team optimizing Mannesmann's Arcor billing system before leaving to Switzerland where I now work as System Administrator, Troubleshooter and Network Services integrator. In parallel, I work as a journalist, as a movie maker and as a musician with both small Gregorian vocal ensemble, rock bands, electronic acts or massive orchesters. I now speak 3 languages, have lived and worked in 4 countries and just couldn't live without an highly dynamic work environment: No matter the stress, the routine is worse.
