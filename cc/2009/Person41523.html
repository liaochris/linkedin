  • Volunteer

    Homeless Shelter "Catharijnehuis"

    (Non-Profit; 51-200 employees; Philanthropy industry)

    July 2007 — January 2008 (7 months)

    One day a week I help out in a daytime-only homeless shelter for non-drug addicts. I help out in the soup kitchen, talk/listen to clients and make them feel at home.

    The shelter's philosophy is based on the Theory of Presence. This theory (by Dutch Professor Baart) provides a new perspective on care. Presence is based on involvement and proximity; it is carefully slow and general instead of specialised; attention is a keyword. By criticising detachment, the Theory of Presence strips managers of many tried and tested methods. Authority, firmness and speed should be replaced by attention. Management and market jargon, just like performance contracts, is regarded as taboo.
