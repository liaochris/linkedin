  • Founder

    Community Commerce Centers Initiative

    (Information Technology and Services industry)

    December 2007 — Present (1 year 8 months)

    The Community Commerce Centers Initiative redefines the workplace for the 21st century in a way that enables virtually any office worker with the ability to have a maximum 3-5 mile commute regardless of where they live or their employer.

    The Community Commerce Centers provide the means for workers to save up to 50% and in some cases more on their normal fuel expense. In addition, each Community Commerce Center qualifies as a Carbon Project under the additionality test, which means participating employers receive Carbon Credits for each employee working at a center.

    This initiative provides not only a way to achieve energy independence but also a stimulus for sluggish economies around the world.
