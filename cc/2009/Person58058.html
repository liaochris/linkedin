  • CEO

    Apartment Home Living

    (Privately Held; Marketing and Advertising industry)

    March 2006 — January 2009 (2 years 11 months)

    Mr. Cotter was the CEO and President for Capitol Media Group ("CMG"), the parent company of ApartmentHomeLiving,com, an online social media apartment website. At CMG, Mr. Cotter successfully transitioned Apartment Home Living from a high-cost local print catalog to a high-margin and profitable national online brand focused on the Apartment Lifestyle, local content and social media; steered overall company direction; led team of 60 employees including the direct management of sales, marketing and business development; sourced, identified, and negotiated sale to Classified Ventures.

  • CEO

    Sandbox

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Internet industry)

    November 1997 — September 2004 (6 years 11 months)

    Mr. Cotter was the Founder, CEO and President of Sandbox.com, an online Fantasy Sports provider. At Sandbox, Mr. Cotter sourced, negotiated and raised more than $25 million in venture capital from Paul Allen’s Vulcan Ventures, Time Warner, Next Generation Fund and others; steered overall company direction; led team of 110 employees including the direct management of sales, marketing and business development, which resulted in more than 8 million members and $20 million in online direct marketing revenue; and sourced, identified and negotiated asset sale to All Star Stats and NBC Universal.

  • Director of Business Development and Franchising

    Moovies, Inc

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; moov; Entertainment industry)

    January 1995 — October 1997 (2 years 10 months)

    Mr. Cotter was the Director of Business Development and Franchising at Moovies, Inc. where he performed financial analysis and modeling, supporting the company’s overall strategy, resulting in a $3.0 million venture financing and a $36 million initial public offering; identified, negotiated and closed 18 acquisitions representing over 180 video store locations, 1.4 million members and $60 million in annual revenue; acquired and managed 53 franchise locations representing over 400,000 members and $10 million in annual revenue; and identified and facilitated negotiations with Video Update resulting in the sale of the company.
