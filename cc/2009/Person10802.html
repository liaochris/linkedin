Bessie A. Winn-Afeku’s Summary

Beauty, eloquence, parity, and community chieftain are just a few words to describe Bessie Akuba Winn. A Georgia Peach by way of Ghana West Africa, Bessie moved to Atlanta at the age of 7. The exceedingly well rounded Winn holds a B.A. in Political Science from Georgia State University with a minor in Sociology. With an extensive background in beauty and pageantry, Bessie was a contestant in the 2003 Miss Georgia USA pageant and a finalist in the Today's Black Women Magazine Beauty Competition. In April of 2006, Ms. Winn was crowned Miss Black Georgia USA 2006. The philanthropic beauty used her reign as a vehicle to create awareness about teenage domestic violence, as well as an empowerment tool for past survivors. Ms.Winn devotes her time to the non-profit "BreakFree", an organization she co-founded that aims to promote and educate the nation about teenage domestic violence. Bessie Winn is currently working on a self help work book for teens that will be used not only as a self empowerment tool, but will also touch on teen dating violence, positive self esteem and self image, and the importance of pursuing your dreams. Bessie has been featured in periodicals such as VIBE Vixen, the Atlanta Voice, Rolling Out, and was selected and featured in the July 2007 issue of EBONY magazine as one of the nations top Bachelorettes. Bessie Winn is a professionally trained actor that has studied atThe Alliance Theatre, and the Premier Actors Network. The love for acting that Bessie has, has landed her roles in Feature films, commercials, and local theatre productions. Winn serves as a role model utilizing her gifts of public speaking, mentoring, life coaching, acting, and singing as an afflatus to the world.
