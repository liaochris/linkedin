David Berkey’s Summary

Printing experience is extensive. Managed the print production of Reader's Digest for a Rand McNally print plant in Mexico City. Represented a variety of Seattle print and graphic arts industry companies, such as Forward Press (mid-sized color printer, Rono Graphics (large Portland based printer), Rainier Color (1st waterless press) and Digital Image (prepress company w/digital image library capability). Clients included ad agencies, graphic designers and corporations. A few would be Cole & Weber, Elgin Syferd, John Hornall Design, Image Ink Studios, Esterline, Boeing, Fluke and Intermec. After some time away from the printing industry, restructuring a private label, repackaging company, consulting for a national herbal company and being the director of sales for a data center, I started dberkeyGroup, utilizing past sales experience and vendor contacts to to create print communication solutions, including the management of producing all graphics related materials. An example would be the total production management for the Pacific Science Center's outreach project based on PBS's "Origins" 4-part TV series. It involved balancing creative, graphic design, content input from a variety of client managers, teachers and scientists to be put into one voice, and the printing of displays, manuals, various teaching aides, CD/DVD covers, plus producing the disks, while keeping colors and branding consistent.

David Berkey’s Specialties:

Vendor relations, employee relations, customer relations, graphic design communications, applying new technologies to marketing efforts, whether that means use of digital or offset presses or prepress software to create digital libraries. Currently, I can help clients produce banners, brochures, annual reports, direct mail packages, CD wraps, manuals, binders, postcards and variable data output.
