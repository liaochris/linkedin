    NVU Productions

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Entertainment industry)

    January 2002 — October 2004 (2 years 10 months)

    NVU Productions was an Entertainment Brand Management Firm. NVU conceived, designed and produced exclusive visual content for Print, Interactive and Multimedia touchpoints. NVU’s mission was to maximize the brand impact on behalf of it's celebrity and entertainment clientele. As one of several talented Art Directors, I worked on projects ranging from packaging for Britney Spears to calendars for Aerosmith. I even can lay claim to designing the Charlotte Bobcats logo. It was a unique and exciting company, which eventually opened the door for my current position at Avenue A | Razorfish.

  • Senior Designer

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Publishing industry)

    July 1999 — December 2001 (2 years 6 months)

    Rare Air Media was the brainchild of 3 very talented and creative individuals. Best known for their high-end visual books (Michael Jordan: For the Love of the Game), Rare Air conceives, designs and produces some of the more beautiful published products on the market. As a Senior Designer, I was fortunate enough to work with such brands as the NBA, ABC, Hard Rock Cafe and more. I honed my skills learning from Creative Director, mentor and all-around-good-guy John Vieceli.

  • Designer

    Harbauer Bruce Nelson Design LTD

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Design industry)

    January 1999 — July 1999 (7 months)

    HBN is a package design and branding studio with such clients as Quaker, Borden's and Everfresh. This was my first job out of college and a great opportunity to work with some fun and engaging brands. While I really liked the management and staff at HBN, I left the company in less than a year to eagerly pursue an opportunity at Rare Air Media. I enjoyed package design, but I was much more interested in designing high-end published products.
