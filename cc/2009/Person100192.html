Tom White’s Summary

Author William Gibson wrote, “the future is here. It’s just not evenly distributed yet.” This is especially true in healthcare and clinical research, where the cycle time between new ideas and disseminated best practices is often measured in decades. Having seen too many loved ones suffer from those delays, my passion is finding ways to reduce that cycle time.

My Bureau of Mental Health Informatics serves as both an R&D shop and a national advocate for improving clinical care and research using healthcare IT. We won a national innovation award for PSYCKES, a combined clinical decision support + business intelligence + disease management system for improving alignment with clinical guidelines and streamlining services and outcomes research. Dialogix, a system for rapidly designing, deploying, and analyzing research studies, has enabled cutting-edge epidemiological and clinical research studies. These platforms solved major challenges in data capture, visualization, and analytics for mental health. On the advocacy side, we ensure that the interests of behavioral health are incorporated into national Healthcare IT and standards activities.

Through consulting for the federal government and coordination of efforts across several national standards organizations, I lead efforts to get extensions to Dialogix adopted as the national standard for interoperable assessment instruments. This will help speed the standardization of two critical content areas – mental health and federal assessment instruments.

Prior successes included the development of best-of-breed systems for neurophysiology and brain imaging NIMH (CORTEX), and Memorial Sloan Kettering Cancer Center (fMRI); plus a novel content management system for a national insurance regulator (NCCI).

    (Government Agency; Public Policy industry)

    November 2005 — June 2007 (1 year 8 months)

    As Consultant to the Office of the Assistant Secretary of Planning and Evaluation (ASPE), I architected and championed a solution to Medicaid/Medicare’s (CMS)'s and Social Security (SSA)'s challenge of making their federally required assessment forms compliant with interoperable Health Information Technology standards.
