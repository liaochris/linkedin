Jonathan Sullivan’s Summary

I hold a BA in theology and pastoral ministry from Quincy University and a MA from Aquinas Institute of Theology. I work to strengthen diocesan catechetical programs, including the delivery of catechist training and the implementation of the National Directory for Catechesis.

I have made a number of presentations over the years on such topics as using technology in ministry, Catholics and the Bible, and the moral world of Harry Potter. I have also published several columns on healthcare ethics in the Michigan Catholic, the newspaper of the Archdiocese of Detroit.

Jonathan Sullivan’s Specialties:

My interests include the use of technology in ministry; faith development in the family and across generations; healthcare ethics; and promoting an authentically Catholic understanding of the Scriptures.

During this year I participated in Trinity Health's Foundations of Leadership program for emerging leaders; compiled a community needs assessment of Clinton County, Iowa; assisted with the 2008-2011 strategic plan; and conducted a mission assessment of the hospital.

  • Trinity Health Mission Fellow

    St. Joseph's Healthcare

    (Non-Profit; 1001-5000 employees; Hospital & Health Care industry)

    June 2006 — June 2007 (1 year 1 month)

    My first year in the Trinity Health fellowship program was served at the former St. Joseph's Healthcare (now Henry Ford Macomb Hospital), a 435-bed facility in Macomb County, Michigan.

    Working closely with the hospital's leadership team I served as Chair of the Mission Assessment Steering Committee and prepared the hospital's mission assessment report; prepared an overview of the hospital for the local bishop; participated in the hospital's Acute Care Ethics Committee and the Archdiocese of Detroit's Advisory Committee on Healthcare Ethics; and assisted with the hospital's diversity, patient satisfaction and culture initiatives.

    In preparation for a transfer of ownership to a non-Catholic system, I also conducted an inventory of all religious artwork and artifacts and participated in the Mission and Values Transition Team.

  • Director of Ministry in the Mountains/Admissions Associate
