Craig Scroggie’s Summary

Craig Scroggie is Vice President and Managing Director and serves as the senior leader for the overall Symantec business in Pacific. A dynamic, entrepreneurial leadership strategist with 18+ year record of achievement & demonstrable success driving multimillion-dollar business growth while providing award-winning leadership in highly competitive technology markets. Adept at driving growth of company revenues, profits and improving performance through disciplined methodology, rigorous planning & focussed execution. An exceptional mentor & coach for leaders in business, government & NFP. He is tenacious in building new business, securing customer loyalty, forging strong relationships with external business partners, a dedicated networker and committed to life long learning and coaching.

Craig is a Graduate & Fellow of the Australian Institute of Company Directors, Fellow of the Aust Institute of Management, Fellow of the Australian Sales & Marketing Institute and serves on their Board & National Advisory Committee, Advisory Board for Computers Off Aust, Trustee for the Committee for Economic Development of Australia, Member of the International CEO Forum, Chairman & Non Executive Director of the Storage Networking Industry Association & Advisory Committee for Super Leaders of Australia. Craig is a Graduate of the University of Southern Queensland and holds post graduate qualifications in Information Technology and Management. Currently undertaking MBA / DBA program. A regular key note speaker in Asia Pacific he is recognised as an industry thought leader on business transformation & leadership.

    September 2008 — Present (11 months)

    Computers Off Australia is a not-for-profit organisation and Green IT labelling initiative designed to educate and recognise organisations that are doing their part to reduce their carbon footprint through the implementation of power management and virtualisation technology.

    We all agree that computers are a great invention, and can be used to unlock all sorts of social, economic and environmental benefits - including reducing the carbon impact of our modern lifestyle. But when they're left idling for hours on end, they're contributing to the greenhouse gas problem. For most of us, its an easy way to reduce the carbon impact of our computers by a third, a half or even more.
    The Computers Off campaign is a simple but important initiative designed to educate home, business and government users on how to reduce our carbon footprint by enabling power management on our home and work computers.
