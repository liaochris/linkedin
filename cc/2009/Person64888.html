Matt Grest’s Summary

An experienced and passionate Internet industry senior professional with a broad and mature cross-functional skill-set

I have worked at the cutting edge of Internet development for 10 years. I was part of the core team that took Plusnet (a UK Internet Service Provider) from £6m turnover in 1999 to an eventual £67m acquisition by BT in January 2007.

I played a lead role in the design and implementation of the UK's most sophisticated ISP provisioning and CRM platform. A methodology of automation and customer self-serve created a platform that made Plusnet the most profitable major ISP in the UK market on a per customer basis.

I come from a technical background with a broad knowledge of software technologies, specifically LAMP, .NET and SharePoint. I have successfully managed multi-million pound project and departmental teams on a range of internal and customer facing projects using waterfall and agile methodologies.

I have also successfully completed several offshore transformation projects over the past few years at Plusnet, 3T Productions and LocallyCompared; taking existing UK based processes and skills and moving the work-flow to newly created offshore teams in India and Poland. As a result of this particular work I have extensive contacts around the world in this area.

I am presently Head of Software Development at NHS Choices where I am responsible for delivery of all new software development on the NHS flagship website, NHS Choices - http://nhs.uk. Receiving over 1 million visitors per week, the site is one of the highest profile websites in the UK and is the largest SharePoint publishing platform in Europe.

I have developed a “can do” reputation, with the ability to solve a business problem with the appropriate technology and process.

I'm happy to discuss new opportunities and provide my experience where it's best needed.
