Barbara Saunders’s Summary

I am an award-winning writer and editor with strong ability to communicate complex ideas or information in language comprehensible to diverse audiences.

Representative work:
• Wrote Ivan Pavlov: Exploring the Mysteries of Behavior (Enslow, 2006), which in 2007 was recognized on Science Books and Films' "Best List" in the junior high/young adult category
• Published anthology entry, "Tribe", in Skeleton Key: a Dictionary for Deadheads (Doubleday, 1994)
• Edited technical manuals for customers and developed ISO-9000 audit preparation training materials for colleagues
• Wrote outreach materials targeted to utility customers including home owners, plant managers, building trade professionals, building designers and engineers, and decision makers in business
• Wrote recruiting posts, scripts, and other outreach materials for a company seeking attorneys, project managers, and linguists with advanced degrees for a novel information management service
• Wrote grant proposals, marketing, communications, and public relations texts for nonprofit organizations
• Developed persuasive copy to sell services ranging from business-to-business consulting on supply chain and logistics management to alternative veterinary care
• Edited manuscripts for new and experienced authors and graduate students

• Licensed Facilitator of C.J. Hayden's Get Clients Now marketing program for independent professionals: coach and consultant
