Charles Kramer’s Summary

My over 15 years of experience as a corporate and
intellectual property attorney includes:

- working as General Counsel of a software company
in New Jersey where I:

-- I negotiated and drafted all of their contracts,
including toolkit, enterprise, and other complex
licenses to customers around the world, and long
-term, milestone-based services agreements. The work
required frequent interaction with the company's sales
department and management; and

-- was responsible for basic corporate and employment
matters.

- Managing a private law practice where I:

-- negotiated and drafted many agreements involving
computer games (including publishing agreements,
engine licenses, shareware licenses, and agreements
providing for the exploitation of fictional characters)
during a time when the technology and related legal
issues were new;

-- filed and prosecuted many trademark applications;
and

-- led a successful royalty audit against a major game
publisher.

My background also includes:

- obtaining an LL.M. (post-JD graduate law degree)
that required study of intellectual property and
antitrust law;

- publishing Articles on copyright, trademark, and licensing
issues, including in Miller Freeman's InterActivity
Magazine;

- speaking at conferences on legal issues, including at the
Game Developers' Conference, the U. of Texas School of
Law's Computer Law Conference, and Miller Freeman's Digital
Video Conference;

- Contributing to CSC Consulting's Leading Edge
Forum paper "Open Source: Open for Business."
www.csc.com/features/2004/uploads/LEF_OPENSOURCE.pdf;
and

- Forming and representing trade associations including
STAR ("Shareware Trade Association and Resources"),
IGDN ("International Game Developers' Network") and
The ASN.1 Consortium.

Charles Kramer’s Specialties:

Licensing, with particular experience in connection with software generally and computer games and ASN.1 in particular.
