Anthony Rotolo’s Summary

I'm an enthusiastic, forward-thinking designer, creative services manager, and commercial writer serving the publishing and education industries. I partner with clients in a highly collaborative way to uncover the themes that drive great projects. An experienced mentor to creative staff, I know how to allocate the right mix of talent and align them to a shared vision.

Recent Highlights:

Built diverse in-house creative department for e-learning firm, and a portfolio of interactive learning products that generated continuous, large follow-on contracts. The success of any organization lies in its people--the true "capital investment." As department head, I developed and retained the best talent, fostering high organizational stability and quality.

Produced, directed, co-wrote the documentary, Army Nurses of World War I, for the U.S. Army Heritage Center, now airing on PBS stations, and companion book, available at http://www.armynursesmovie.com/trailer.html.

Produced the print and audio release of "Who Goes There?" (the story that became THE THING films), e-commerce blog and outreach media for RocketRideBooks.com. The new edition features an Introduction and never-before-published Screen Treatment by William F. Nolan (Logan's Run).

Host of the popular podcast, Design Guy, available in iTunes. An editorial pick by Apple, it was featured in their "Design Culture" showcase at the iTunes Store. The program has garnered excellent reviews from its user community and enjoys a growing subscriber base.

Anthony Rotolo’s Specialties:

Leadership, management, design (experience, usability, interaction), book design and packaging, small press publishing, commercial writing, new media, flash animation, branding and identity, marketing, sales..
