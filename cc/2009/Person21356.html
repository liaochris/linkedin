Curt Hopkins’s Summary

Deep knowledge of communications technologies, messaging, understanding and communicating with a target audience, staff leadership and solid management skills, as well as strong social media marketing experience.

I have led and implemented projects, developed and supervised teams and content departments for Ask.com, Visa, Trimble (Tripod Data Systems), AboutUs, PBwiki, the Oregon Shakespeare Festival, Entopia, Autoweb, eLance and others. I have assessed communication needs for clients and employers, drafted written strategy, assembled personnel, set dates and work budgets, led meetings and brainstorming sessions, written style guides, press releases and taglines, kept senior management up to date, solicited and elicited messaging ideas from stakeholders, placed press and supervised the work of staffers and freelancers.

I am also an experienced journalist with credits in Newsweek, San Francisco Chronicle, San Francisco Bay Guardian, Salon, Los Angeles Times, National Post, New Times, Seattle Times, Reuters and others.

Finally, I have extensive experience in social media. As the Marketing Communications Manager for TDS, I am in charge of their social network and instituted the use of Twitter, Delicious and YouTube. Formerly, as the Social Media Manager for GarageGames, I developed their corporate social media marketing strategy and promoted their social network for gamers. I also developed an extensive "conversation metric" to measure the success of their social media initiatives. I am a blogger at Morpheme Tales. I am the founder and director of the non-profit The Committee to Protect Bloggers and have served as the project director of Spirit of America’s Anonymous Blogging Campaign, among other projects. I have been interviewed, sourced, quoted and invited to write on blogging, employment and human rights issues by national and international print, online and broadcast media.

Curt Hopkins’s Specialties:

Marketing communications: Writing, editing, message creation, social media marketing, and developing and implementing communications strategies.

Social media: Strategy and implementation of conversational marketing; conversation metrics and analysis; writing and message development via social media tools.

Journalism: Identifying compelling stories, research, interviewing, securing access to sources, cultivating sources over time and writing.
