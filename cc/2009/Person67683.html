Michael Samstag’s Summary

Documentary films.
TV Production.
Interactive Media.
Freelance Producer/Director/Camera Operator.

I have recently begun to produce short, focused mini-documentaries for non-profit groups. These videos focus on fund raising and volunteer recruitment while telling a moving story and the vision of the organization. It's rewarding to see how far we can stretch a small budget to create meaningful fiscal and emotional results.

I'm the President and Founder of KnoxvilleFilms.com which is a cutting edge organization specializing in promoting the collaborative film arts in East Tennessee. Through our new partnership with the Scripps Howard owned Metro Pulse, we are also creating almost a dozen new episodic shows for the web. Details on www.knoxvillefilms.com.

Michael Samstag’s Experience

  • Executive Producer

    Samstag Productions

    (Privately Held; Myself Only; Motion Pictures and Film industry)

    February 1999 — Present (10 years 6 months)

    Documentary films. Special Features. Broadcast television. Video for the web.

    Best known for his work directing and producing special features for the first three Harry Potter DVDs and the Van Helsing DVD, Samstags' work on Van Helsing was featured in an article by American Cinematographer Magazine and the making of WAR & TRUTH was recently featured in the Metro Pulse.

    His documentary feature film, WAR & TRUTH, won Best Documentary at the Annapolis Film Festival in November 2005 and the Director's Choice Award at the Secret City Film Festival in 2007. In 2005, he directed 30 shows for the Telly Award winning Backyard Habitat series for Discovery Network ‘s Animal Planet Channel. Samstags' second documentary, BOMBS OVER BEN HAI is currently in pre-production.

    A 1989 graduate of Hampshire College, Samstag has worked with blockbuster budgets but also prides himself with making the most of smaller budgets for non-profit organizations.

  • Producer/Director, iPIX Movies

    IPIX

    (Public Company; 51-200 employees; IPIX; Computer Software industry)

    June 2000 — September 2001 (1 year 4 months)

    Responsible for producing and directing all iPIX Movies, utilizing both 180 and 360 capture systems. Production experience shooting on 35mm film using the AATON 35 III, High Definition video using the SONY HDW-F900, and various DV cameras. Post-production experience includes extensive After Effects, iPIX proprietary software and Non-Linear High Def editing and streaming video for the Internet. Additional responsibilities include producing demo projects from script to post-production as well as managing all aspects of the iPIX Movies process.

  • Manager of Media Deveoplment

Additional duties included writing and creating the management training program for three restaurant concepts.
