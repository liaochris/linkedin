Chris Burrett’s Experience

  • Head of Sales and Marketing

    LBS Accessories Ltd.

    (Consumer Electronics industry)

    February 2008 — March 2009 (1 year 2 months)

    LBSA was a consumer electronics start-up launched in November 2007, and closed in March 2009, with a range of own-brand (rxs: redshift) and 3rd party branded products aimed at the mobile lifestyle including, LCD pico projectors, Bluetooth enabled accessories and speakers mainly sourced from China.

  • Head of Marketing

    InComm Europe

    (Financial Services industry)

    June 2005 — February 2008 (2 years 9 months)

    InComm Europe was a start-up that became a wholly owned subsidiary of InComm, the world’s largest provider of prepayment products and services including debit cards, gift cards, and mobile phone top-up.

  • Head of Wholesale Value Added Services

    Virgin Mobile

    (Public Company; Telecommunications industry)

    May 2004 — May 2005 (1 year 1 month)

    In this role I was responsible for Virgin Mobile’s revenue from content and services delivered through 3rd party providers. Key responsibilities were:

    • The management of the commercial relationships with circa 30 partners made up of content providers, application providers, service providers, and service aggregators.

    • The management of services accessible through premium SMS and voice shortcodes, and WAP.

    • Responsibility for the relationships with industry bodies including ICSTIS, IMCB, Mobile Data Association, Mobile Broadband Group, and the DTI.

  • Channel Marketing Manager

    Virgin Mobile

    (Public Company; Telecommunications industry)

    January 2001 — April 2004 (3 years 4 months)

    Whilst in this role I managed the marketing of Virgin Mobile's proposition in our largest accounts including Carphone Warehouse, Dixons Stores Group, Tesco, and Argos. This included:


    • Leading the development and implementation of marketing programmes with the retailers.

    • Responsibility for the positioning of the Virgin Mobile brand within all retail related media.

    • Communicating Virgin Mobile strategies and developments to the retail channel.

    • Managing a channel marketing budget of over £6 million per annum.

    • Project managing the launch of Virgin Retail’s electronic top-up solution.

  • Senior Product/Category Manager

    DSGi

    (Public Company; DXNS; Retail industry)

    April 1997 — December 2001 (4 years 9 months)

    Joining as an Assistant Product Manager in PC Peripherals I progressed through the ranks to make Senior Category Product Manager by June 2000 before being asked to join the short lived Jakarta/gameplay.com chain to develop the proposition and oversee the change of brand. My main responsibilities and achievements included:

    • Managing PC add-ons and upgrades; accounting for 17% of PC World's turnover (> £200m per annum).

    • Instigating and delivering a project looking at the viability of increasing PC World's range of products and services in order to better service the medium sized enterprise and corporate sectors.

  • Account Executive

    AC Nielsen

    (Public Company; VNU; Market Research industry)

    April 1995 — March 1997 (2 years)

    Working as an Account Executive on the Holiday Booking Audit I managed the relationships with a range of clients including tourist boards, tour operators, travel agents, ferry companies and Eurotunnel.
