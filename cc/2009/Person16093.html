Cathy Brooks’s Summary

I'm a story teller who has spent most of her career telling other people's stories for them.

It wasn't until I went through the process of learning to tell my own story (which is harder than you would think) that I found out I not only liked doing it, but that I have a knack for teaching others how to do it too.

So that's what I do.

It manifests in all sorts of different ways - from workshops and seminars to bespoke influencer engagement programs and media training.

A few of the recent resume items include heading up business development for Loic Le Meur's Seesmic (December 2007 - December 2008) and during that time I also helped Loic develop and produce the program for his LeWeb conference in Paris.

Immediately prior to working with Loic, I spent some time with Guidewire Group, a market intelligence firm focused wholly on emerging markets, technologies and companies - anyone and everyone who's involved in the innovation ecosystem. My role was developing and producing two events for this organizations (Innovate!Europe and Leadership Forum), as well as an ongoing podcast - I of Innovation - an in-depth interview series that profiled the entrepreneurs who build companies and the people who invest in them.

Immediately prior to this I had the perfect job for polishing my psychological analysis skills - heading Business Development in the Bay Area for one of the largest PR firms in the US. I've spent some other hands-on time in PR and marketing, but mostly my career is comprised by time in newsrooms in Philadelphia, Chicago, Miami and San Francisco. Primarily a broadcaster, I have a degree from the Medill School of Journalism at Northwestern University.

I'm interested in talking with people of all ilk - especially if they have an interesting story to tell ... or maybe they need helping telling it. :)

Cathy Brooks’s Specialties:

Writing, personality profiling, behavioral trend spotting in business, making people laugh, starting conversations, unraveling meaningless marketing blather, dog behavior & training (skills that I often wish transferred to the business world).
