Stuart Harris’s Summary

Stuart is a highly skilled and dynamic (C#, .Net) Developer, Technical Lead and Architect with more than 10 years commercial experience in Object- (and Service-) Oriented software design (20 years in IT). He is creative and quality-driven, implementing appropriate design patterns using agile methodologies, domain-driven design (DDD) and test/behaviour-driven development (TDD/BDD). He leads and mentors in all aspects of the agile software development life cycle.
For the past six years, Stuart has been leading large teams of developers, consistently delivering robust solutions on time and within budget. His strong leadership, coaching abilities and personal approach have allowed him to build capable teams with a culture of learning, productivity, quality and consistency.
Stuart has expert skills in modern enterprise software design and architecture. He constantly studies to keep abreast of emerging technologies giving him a keen ability to build solutions that are current and compatible with future innovation. This direction, together with his attention to detail and quality, ensure that the solutions he delivers exceed expectations and provide a good return on investment.
Stuart has worked with Microsoft at their Technology Centre in Reading to build two PoC projects in WPF – one for London Underground and the Windows Vista launch (Nov 2006), and one for Bob Geldof and the BBC (Dictionary of Man Project – Aug 2007).
