Giuseppe Genna’s Summary

Actually, although i'm in search for a during job in creative and/or online company, i'm an author for Mondadori.
This is the official c.v:

Giuseppe Genna (Milan, 1969) is one of the most acclaimed Italian writers of the new generation. After working, when still very young, for the magazine 'Poesia', he occupied important consulting positions in the italian Parliament. While holding that office he had the opportunity to access and study the proceedings of the government committee dealing with the main politic mysteries of Italy. His literary work, eclectic and resisting the established poetic of genre, reveals itself in the opening of 'Assalto a un tempo devastato e vile', a mixage of open stories and essay comments on political-civil and metaphysical issues; the thriller 'Catrame' marks the birth of inspector Lopez as a character, who continues in the metaphysical thriller trilogy 'Nel nome di Ishmael', 'Non toccare la pelle del drago', 'Grande Madre Rossa', all published by Mondadori and translated in many Countries (U.S., for Talk Miramax Books; not only in US but also published in UK with Atlantic Books, and France by Grasset, Germany by Diogenes and DTV, Spain by Grijalbo, Russia, Holland, Belgium, Bulgaria and Japan). The german national broadcasting successfully programmed a radiodrama which drew on the metaphysical thriller 'Ishmael', while the tv series Suor Jo, directed by Gilberto Squizzato and broadcasted by the Italian national network Rai, drew on Lopez, the serial character created by Genna.
Genna’s most recent works are represented respectively by the anti-novel 'L’Anno Luce' and 'Dies Irae' saga. Here the thriller pattern is vanished and stylistic elements and frames usually characterizing the classic epic, are used from modern and updated viewpoint, à la DeLillo. Giuseppe Genna has been in charge of the website Clarence, among the most striking case histories of the Italian net.
