Kyle Dreier’s Summary

For nearly my entire life and career I have been involved in small business ventures with an out-of-the-box mind-set.

Turn the clock back to the early 70s and you would see me capitalizing on the sale and distribution of Pop Rocks prior to their arrival to the 48 contiguous states. While I was marketing them during recess at my elementary school I knew my target audience and my message was clear ... “Listen to these pop in your mouth!”

Fast forward twenty years and after business school at Baylor University and design school at the University of North Texas I found myself in the prestigious position of Art Director for American Way Magazine, the in-flight magazine for American Airlines. My additional involvement there at American Airlines in marketing and product development provided an excellent foundation from which I then launched my own design communication business in Dallas, Texas.

While operating my own design firm in Dallas I segued from “designer” to “illustrator” with assignments for clients such as United Airlines, IBM, Better Homes and Gardens and countless other corporate and editorial clients worldwide.

After making the move to Tennessee and with the emergence of the Internet I started a web hosting company named DogBark.com which now provides services to over 2,000 customers and recently celebrated its ninth year in business.

Today, I am able to combine my skills, my experience and my passion in commercial photography. I enjoy shooting a variety of subject matter for a variety of clientele. However, somehow, my passion for photography seems to always come back to food. :)

I am an out-of-the-box thinker and recognize that not everyone’s box is the same size. I continue to push the confines of my own box. People come to me for my ability to question the status quo. If asked to change a light bulb my response is “does it have to be a light bulb.” Additionally, when asked to “think outside the box” my response is “Your box or mine?”
