Scott Rains’s Summary

My passion is "social change with an IT toolkit." My professional experience spans two domains:

- Management in non-profits and higher education
- Consulting in travel and disability

Non-Profit & Education Management

Following years of direct service in the non-profit field I directed a co-curricular program on social justice education at Santa Clara University, directed University Ministry at Benedictine University, managed Computer-Assisted Education for Eden Housing, and directed Programs & Services for SeniorNet. I am a Fellow of the Zero Divide Foundation, the Graduate Theological Foundation, and have been nominated as candidate for the Ashoka Fellowship.

My work as an educator has involved elementary students to seniors - usually with innovative pilot programs designed to test a concept, model, or scalablity.


Travel & Disability

I publish daily news notes and commentary in the Rolling Rains Report on Universal Design, Travel & Disability at http://www.RollingRains.com

Presentations I have given since 2005 include at the Rhode Island School of Design and as conference keynoter in Australia, Brazil, Japan, Korea, Thailand, and Taiwan.

Academic work includes guest editing the Travel & Disability issue of the journal Review of Disability Studies and contributions to the journals such as Disability Studies, Design for All India. Study of Current Rehabilitation (Japan).

Scott Rains’s Specialties:

non-profit management, fundraising, and program design, technology education for underserved populations

research, speaking, and writing on the adoption of Universal Design by the worldwide travel & hospitality industry.
