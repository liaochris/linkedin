Tom Lennon’s Summary

Thomas Lennon is the Managing Partner of Watermark Capital Corporation, a private investment and strategic advisory firm providing investment and corporate development services in the areas of product & organization development, business planning and modeling, strategic partnerships, capital acquisition, corporate restructuring and M&A. During his 21 year corporate and entrepreneurial career, Mr. Lennon has developed an extensive network of vc/investment firms, traditional mezzanine and non-traditional lenders as well as c-suite executives across multiple industries. In addition to working with companies in various development and operating stages, he has founded several startups in a variety of industries including real estate development, construction, technology, etc. He remains a shareholder, consultant and advisor to several different companies in high security printing, consumer products, next-generation technology platform solutions, branded entertainment, interactive and alternative outdoor advertising, integrated marketing and distribution, CRM, telecommunications, optical networks, optical media, internet marketing, online video creation/aggregation/distribution, healthcare, travel and investment banking. He attended the University of Georgia where he received his B.S. in genetics and biochemistry.

Tom Lennon’s Specialties:

Debt and equity investment banking, mergers & acquisitions (corporate consolidations, restructurings & divestitures), strategic planning, business & financial modeling, intellectual property strategy & planning, new technology development, sales & marketing strategy, c-suite corporate and strategic partnership development, new product and brand development, new media strategy, organization development and recruiting, extensive commercial/residential real estate development & construction.

  • Director

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Venture Capital & Private Equity industry)

    March 2000 — Present (9 years 9 months)

  • Interim EVP & CFO

    Darkstrand, Inc.

    (Privately Held; Information Technology and Services industry)

    April 2009 — October 2009 (7 months)

    Tom was brought in to augment the management team and was responsible for corporate finance, corporate development and strategic planning on an interim basis at Darkstrand. During that time his firm Watermark Capital Partners worked on the Company's first Series A financing which ultimately led to an offer to buy the Company. Darkstrand was formed to commercialize the 15,000-mile next generation hyper-speed, supercomputing optical network known as the National LambdaRail (“NLR” - www.nlr.net). NLR was formed by a consortium of U.S. universities, scientific organizations and regional operating networks (RONs) that deployed this infrastructure to advance networking research and, in the process, enable next-generation applications in science, engineering and medicine. This network now facilitates project work for NASA, the U.S. Department of Defense and the worldwide scientific community. NLR and Darkstrand Share a new vision that Tom helped to create of being 100% focused on commercial collaborations which transcend traditional telecommunication models to advanced unified communications integrated with business, information and scientific platforms, workflows, processes and analytics. This next-gen optical network - the only one of its kind on earth through its direct connection to approximately 280 national labs and academic research institutions. The NLR is not theoretical; it is fully operational and in high demand by Fortune and Global 1000 companies in major industries.

SCT’s mission is to re-define the product security and brand protection industry through innovation in overt/covert security technologies, manufacturing and security program methodologies for its customers.

Through internally developed hosted applications as well as co-branded and private labeled ASP services from its affiliate partners, the company provided professionally enhanced corporate template drive websites, member company intranets with permission based social networking, collaboration, project management, online bidding, material procurement, equipment rental, employee tracking and much more. The main focus of the portal was to provide a one-stop shop for construction based business-to-business e-commerce and construction management.

MedAscend concepted and created the first interactive training tutorials for minimally invasive surgical procedures on CDROM for CME as well as other healthcare information products (Therapeutic Online, etc.). This physician-centric company acquired DotMD, LLC which controlled the rights for the ccTLD ".md". Through Park Avenue Capital, LLC., a successor venture formed by the founding principals of MedAscend/DotMD with three former NY investment bankers, MAX.md (www.max.md) DotMD's efforts to create an application platform and internet domain for the healthcare industry continue.

  • Director

    Chamber Edit, LLC

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Internet industry)

    2000 — 2002 (2 years )

    Founded in early 2001, the Company developed a proprietary system (Chamber Economic Development Internet Technologies or “Chamber EDIT”) that addressed a significant number of problems encountered by as well as providing several enhanced revenue programs to Chambers of Commerce businesses and their executives. By providing customized hosted solutions and a call center, the Company helped Chambers of Commerce drive new membership, membership retention, membership management and the management and implementation of non-dues revenue programs. The Chamber “EDIT” system was deployed in several Chamber areas across the Southeastern U.S. before being sold in 2002.

  • Chairman & CEO

    The Lennon Companies

    (Real Estate industry)

    1986 — 2000 (14 years )

LCCI was the first of several companies that would be under the umbrella of The Lennon Companies. Other companies under the umbrella were Lennon Development Group, Inc., Lennon Properties, Inc., LenCor Corporation and Riverside Senior Living, LLC. Through these varies entities Tom Lennon developed residential subdivisions, completed high end custom homes and renovations, built commercial buildings, condominiums, restaurants and retain centers and built assisted living centers. Proceeds from these ventures allowed Tom to build an active investing platform which is now Watermark Capital Partners, LLC.
