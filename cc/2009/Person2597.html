Alan Johnson’s Summary

Before starting a family and getting back into the full time workforce, I founded a fixed-wireless Internet Service Provider, FINOWEN, which I grew to about 250 broadband customers before moving on. Right out of college, I started doing software development on a consulting basis, first as an employee of a consulting company, and then as an independent while I transitioned into the wireless data communications business that evolved into FINOWEN.

I am interested in just about anything geeky, but I am really driven by technologies that improve quality of life, especially any technology that enables people to improve their own quality of life. This is why I started FINOWEN: once you have practically instant access to the virtually limitless information that is the Internet, your quality of life is limited only by your desire to change it.

The following is a list of skills and technologies for which I have at least some experience and interest.

SYSTEM AND SOFTWARE ENGINEERING: eXtreme Programming; Object-oriented software development; Component based and functional design; Data structures and associated algorithms; Classifier systems; MVC; Java; RMI; JDBC; JavaMail; SQL; Bash, Perl; Eclipse

WORLD WIDE WEB: HTML; JSP; Servlets; CGI; SSI; JavaScript

SYSTEMS AND SERVERS: PC and server hardware; Red Hat, CentOS, Debian, and Ubuntu Linux; MySQL admin and performance tuning; Windows 98, NT4, 2000, XP, Server 2003; Apache Tomcat and HTTP Server; Exchange and MS Domain administration; Veritas

NETWORKING: Internet networking and related technologies; Wireless LANs and WANs; IEEE 802.11 standards; Ethernet LAN, basic wired WAN, firewall installation and admin including SonicWall; General knowledge of hundreds of products, technologies, and standards

BUSINESS MANAGEMENT: Human capital management; Financial modeling; QuickBooks; Billing processes and automation; Customer support and feedback management; Bootstrapping; Financing/fundraising; Sales and marketing
