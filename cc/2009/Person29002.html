Pawel Siwczak’s Summary

- Early keyboard specialist (harpsichord, fortepiano, clavichord, virginals, chamber organ), freelancing soloist and chamber musician based in London, performing both in the UK and abroad.
- Teacher at the Royal Academy of Music in London
- Junior Fellow at the Royal Welsh College of Music & Drama in Cardiff
- Award winner (including 8th Broadwood Harpsichord Competition 2007, Musica Britannica's Sir Anthony Lewis Memorial Prize 2006, DipRAM for an outstanding final recital 2008)

Completed a Master of Music in Performance (MMus) degree with distinction, researching Wanda Landowska's expressive playing and combination of the value of individual interpretation with the historically informed manner of performing early music to contemporary audiences.

For more details, including concert invitations, latest news, contact details,
see http://www.pawelsiwczak.com

Pawel Siwczak’s Specialties:

Performing early keyboard music - late renaissance & baroque as well as early classical, chamber music from 16th, 17th and 18th centuries, solo and with chamber ensembles.
