    Founded MarketOutsider.com with one other business partner. MarketOutsider is a company dedicated to extracting actionable, meaningful data hidden inside plain text published to the Internet.   • Senior Software Engineer

    The Search and Content is the core Search Technology team at Whitepages.com, INC, a leader in the online people-search industry. My responsibilities as a member of that team include maintaining and extending the search engine and database storage system that drives the entire company. The engine is comprised of tens of thousands of lines of advanced Object-Oriented Perl, and uses a variety of database engines for content storage and delivery.

  • Systems Analyst/Programmer

    Fred Hutchinson Cancer Research Center

    (Non-Profit; 1001-5000 employees; Computer Software industry)

    January 2004 — August 2006 (2 years 8 months)

    The SCHARP organization at the Fred Hutchinson Cancer Research Center focuses on HIV/AIDS prevention trials. I was in a core position supporting the statisticians and other programmers in the group by developing and maintaining tools and databases for them. My main project was a safety reporting system that effectively did real-time monitoring of thousands of trial participants for safety in a variety of parameters. This was a highly critical system, written in Object-Oriented Perl, heavily relying on a PostgreSQL database with numerous stored procedures. I designed and built the system from the ground-up. I eventually presented a piece of this work at the DataFax Users Group conference in 2006.

    Additionally I was involved in a variety of other key projects in the group, and was frequently involved in working with industry standards such as MedDRA, CDISC, and other controlled vocabulary systems.

  • Contract Programmer

    Forrest Pruzan Creative

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Computer Games industry)

    December 2004 — March 2005 (4 months)

    Worked with a partner to create an automated play-testing system for the collectible game Zypods. We created a web-based system that would pit various AI opponents against each other with different components and store batch history in a MySQL database. I was primarily responsible for the AI and statistics engines, written in Object-Oriented Perl.

  • Research Technician

    Fred Hutchinson Cancer Research Center

    (Non-Profit; 1001-5000 employees; Biotechnology industry)

    November 2000 — December 2003 (3 years 2 months)

    The Heimfeld Research Lab at the Fred Hutchinson Cancer Research Center focuses on supplying hematapoietic stem cells to researchers nationwide. This enabled me to become very knowledgeable in various immunology procedures such as Flow Cytometry and Magnetic Cell Separation. We were one of, if not the highest throughput lab for cell separation in the Nation, and thus became experts at it.

    As the position progressed, I was able to apply more of my programming skills to the lab to build tools to enable us to more efficiently serve our purpose. These included Perl systems to aid in analysis and cataloging of flow cytometry specimens, databases to store the information to provide to other researchers, and machine interfaces to automatically download and parse information from blood analyzers.
