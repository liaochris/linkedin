Rob Williams’s Summary

Self motivated and innovative software engineer experienced in tools design and development, test automation, test execution and documentation. High level of expertise in performance, stability, feature and network testing of telecommunications infrastructure software. Strengths include excellent problem solving skills, technical leadership and an ability to visualize new processes and procedures to improve quality and efficiency. Effective working individually or in a team.

I am actively seeking a temporary position in the USA as I plan to move to the UK in September/October 2009.

Rob Williams’s Specialties:

Software system test, software performance test, UNIX, Linux, TCL/TK/Expect, GUI, Perl, C, MS Windows, TCP/IP, SS7 and other Common Channel Signalling protocols, MS Word, Excel, Access, SQL, HTML, CGI, CATTgen(proprietary development system for test automation equipment from Catapult Communications, Inc)

Rob Williams’s Experience

  • Senior Staff Engineer

    Motorola

    (Public Company; MOT; Telecommunications industry)

    September 1993 — March 2009 (15 years 7 months)

    Systems Integration and Test Dept., Schaumburg, IL (2006 to 2009)
    Software installation and network level testing of 2 major infrastructure subsystems (Network Fault Management and Fire Station Alerting) in communication product for emergency services.

    Cellular Infrastructure Performance Tools Team, Arlington Heights, IL (2004 to 2006)
    Technical lead in development and test of call generator state-machine applications using CATTgen development environment. The applications were used for background call load, stability, performance and stress testing of Motorola Network products including Electronic Mobile Exchange (EMX2500 and EMX5000), Motorola Soft Switch (MSS) and MR Message Register (MR).

    Cellular Infrastructure Performance Modeling Group, Arlington Heights, IL (1996 to 2004)
    Technical lead of EMX performance test team.
    Performed performance and stability testing of many EMX releases. Developed and documented EMX lab databases for load testing and designed test tools.

    Cellular Infrastructure Software Lab Support Group, Arlington Heights, IL (1993 to 1994 as a contractor and 1994 to 1996 as a permanent staff member)
    Responsible for load bring up activities in several EMX labs and for maintaining a suite of Perl programs used to auto-generate command files which were executed on the EMX lab switches to populate their databases
