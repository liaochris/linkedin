Jay Fraser’s Summary

As President/CEO of Tracer Detection Technology my responsibilities are strategic direction, operations, managing our programs with federal agencies & National Labs, negotiating licensing and R&D agreements, and dealing with potential commercial partners and customers. We are involved in counterfeit detection and covert surveillance technologies. I've also coordinated International “special projects.”

I believe that solid strategic thinking is at the core of any successful business or product. My roots are on Madison Ave. where I worked on some of the toughest client assignments and was taught by some of the top strategic minds in the country (the best, IMO, still advises me as my strategic collaborator).

In the late 80s I began consulting, applying my strategic and marketing skills to high-tech companies. In 1990 I was introduced to the Dean of Engineering at SUNY Farmingdale. I have since focused on transitioning technologies from the R&D environment to commercialization and end-user adoption.

I enjoy speaking to large groups. My presentations have covered topics such as technology transfer, bioterrorism, homeland security and entrepreneurship. In May 2008 I presented a paper on my company's anti-counterfeit solution at the IEEE International Conference on Technologies for Homeland Security. I was the Keynote speaker at the meeting of the Federal Laboratory Consortium on the subject of “A Small Business Perspective on Technology Transfer" (http://tinyurl.com/dg3g7l). I recently spoke on the "Strategic Management of IP."

I have knowledge and insight into a range of homeland security and law enforcement issues. In June 2008, I received a certification in “Enhanced Threats and Risk Assessment” from Texas A&M's Engineering Extension Service. I also write about issues related to homeland security, policy implications and technologies used in the Global War on Terror for ThreatsWatch.Org, the web-based publication of the Center for Threat Awareness (CTA).
