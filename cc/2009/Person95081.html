Steven Ericsson-Zenith’s Summary

In 2002, after many years in industry, I returned to research and development, to science. I continue to apply the results of that effort to problems I know well in high technology.

Our work at IASE promises a new foundation to logic and the science of apprehension that will enable both an explanation of what is happening in biology and the means to engineer motile machines that experience.

We challenge a number of current expectations in technology development. For example, according to our models the fundamental problems of recognition cannot be solved using software and silicon for reasons that are fundamental to the world. The Singularity is not near.

IASE is a 501(c)3 non-profit science organization with California charity status. I am fundraising and recruiting for this defining commitment.

The scope of our work is broad, it includes physical engineering, systems operation and high order systems-of-systems behavior (as seen in our work on reasoning about behavior in social networks). We deal with fundamental issues related to the communication of difficult ideas and the development of human understanding.

IASE undertakes select intellectual property development projects with industry that can apply our ideas and promise to provide some new empirical insight in our problem space.
