Herb Morreale’s Summary

Whether a founder, CEO, CTO, investor, consultant, or board member, my goal is to help organizations achieve significant growth. I'm drawn to working with teams and organizations with a strong "social glue" - where the culture offers every employee an opportunity to learn & grow, take pride in their work, live a balanced life, have fun, and be compensated fairly.

  • Chief Technology Officer

    When I joined Me.dium, there was a founding management team and one engineer with a prototype.   • Chief Technology Officer

    Gold Systems, Inc.

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Computer Software industry)

    September 2004 — April 2006 (1 year 8 months)

    In 2004, I joined Gold Systems, Inc. as Chief Technology Officer. Founded in 1991, Gold Systems develops speech recognition applications for Global 1000 clients. The company has won several industry awards and is nationally recognized as a leader in voice automated self-service. As CTO, I was responsible new product development, strategic partnerships, corporate marketing, and overall technology vision. Prior to leaving Gold Systems I led the company’s efforts to expand into VOIP unified communications via its partnership with Microsoft.

  • Managing Partner

    Kalos Strategy Group

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Management Consulting industry)

    March 2001 — September 2004 (3 years 7 months)

    In 2001, I started Kalos Strategy Group, a consulting firm that worked with senior management of high-growth organizations. Kalos developed a propriety methodology that links strategy, leadership, and execution with key performance metrics. By 2003, Kalos was listed as one of Denver’s top consulting firms. Through its partnership with Gazelles International, Kalos twice hosted local events with Jim Collins, NY Times Best Selling Author of “Good to Great.” Before disbanding to explore new opportunities, the firm had grown to six partners and senior advisors whom had previously held top executive positions in high tech, telecommunications, manufacturing, education, and military.

  • CEO/President

    XOR Inc.

    (Privately Held; 201-500 employees; Computer Networking industry)

    January 1991 — November 2000 (9 years 11 months)

    I co-founded XOR Inc., serving as President/CEO from 1991-1999, then Chief Strategist until November 2000. We grew XOR into a full-service ebusiness outsourcing firm with approximately 500 employees, with seven offices throughout the U.S., and revenues in excess of $50 million. I was proud to lead the company as it became the sixth fastest growing technology company in Colorado (Ernst & Young Colorado Fast 50) and was included on Inc. Magazine's list of the country's 500 fastest growing private companies (#109).
