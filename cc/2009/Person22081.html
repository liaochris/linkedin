Daniel Fowler’s Summary

Professional Experience

I built my current employer's social media presence from the ground up. Unfortunately, in higher education, there are substantial regulatory barriers that prevent tapping into the potential power of marketing via social media. I need a wider pasture to cultivate the power of these networks.

I come from a development background. I have learned valuable best practices and web standards, and I can learn any programming language quickly and understand them immediately. But my mind is wired for analysis. I people-watch. I evaluate behaviors, trends, and results unintentionally because it is just second nature to me. I always question the metrics of success and whether or not they are accurately measuring it.

My thought processes are complex beyond description. I tap into the psychology behind social networks. I have been on the Internet for over six years... long before being on the Internet was a positive thing for one's social life. So I know all the ins and outs and can effectively leverage social media to maximize brand value. It's all about niche audiences.

Goals

My ideal job title right now would be "social media strategist." Down the road I would like to be in a senior- or director-level position dealing with corporate web strategy.

I am expecting to relocate when an employer finds me and recognizes my qualifications. I am not bound geographically. Atlanta, New York, Los Angeles: it's all the same to me. And I hate self-glorification, so please try to limit requests for me to "talk myself up" more than I have already done here.

Cheers,
Daniel
