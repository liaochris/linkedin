David vanBlaricom II’s Summary

For a history lesson, we have to go back to the early 80’s when the personal computer was born. I often stayed up late at night fixing and editing applications for my Tandy 1000 to make it faster and easier. At the same time, I am the son of an Interior Designer and an Architect. Design is in my blood. I attended Design Camp at N.C. State University when I was a teenager, where I embraced the technical artistry of the web. I grew up with a PC in front of me, merging from the Tandy 1000 to the IBM 286, 386, 486, and the revolution of the Pentium Chip. When the web was available to the consumer, I was at Virginia Tech in my dorm room using the ROLM modem to access music and web sites. I attended VCU for film, music and traditional art and moved on to the School of Communication Arts in Raleigh, NC where I studied 3D animation and digital media. I then started Nadami Digital Media, LLC with some friends from VCU in Richmond, VA doing 3D renderings for architects and interior designers along with websites and graphical presentations. I moved into the client side working for Baskervill in Richmond, VA as a project designer, and then on to marketing media specialist where I built Baskervill’s web presence and intranet. The next step was a marketing media specialist for Getloaded.com where I rebranded the company and all of its subsidiaries. I moved up the ladder to take on the marketing department myself as Marketing Manager. There I soaked up all I could learn about, not only marketing a national web-based application, but how the client looks at vendors and their internal needs for budget and time.

Today, I help companies grow. I develop sites that the user can manage, and change as they need it changed without hiring a web developer every time. I continue to use my past to inject value, personality and perception into the brand and its online presence.
