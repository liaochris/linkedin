Jaimie VanSickle’s Summary

I am owner of J VanSickle Virtual Assistant. I was once asked a question from a potential client: "What is it that you can do for me, that I myself don't want to do?"

In response to such a great question, I could very easily go on about my 15-years professional business experience, and all my skills and advanced competency levels. But instead, I simply will state what is special and unique about me.

But first, I quite simply want to put all my focus on my client's needs. Tell me about you and your business so we can determine the best administrative solutions to your dilemma.

And that specialness about me is my perseverance and the ambitious drive I have in pursuing new experiences to better advance myself in my professional career and personal life.

So I guess the overall answer to such a question is: I would love to take on any task that would benefit your advancement for you and/or your business. No task is too big or too small.

And I will represent you and your business, with the utmost form of professionalism.

I also launched a blog called The Virtual Industry Hub. The sole focus of this division is to offer free resources, advice and tools to other virtual professionals. These references will also be valuable to business professionals seeking a better understanding of the Virtual Assistant Industry.

If you should desire to learn more about my business and the services I offer and how J VanSickle Virtual Assistant can help you, please take a look at my website.

If you want more information on this new industry, please visit my blog, The Virtual Industry Hub for guides, resources, and tools that will explain everything in more detail.

  • Owner

    My Spa Products

    (Cosmetics industry)

    December 2006 — December 2009 (3 years 1 month)

    In December of 2006 I initiated my first entrepreneurial adventure and started an online ecommerce site called My Spa Products that specialized in organic, 100% natural products made from dead sea minerals, essential oils, and other natural products. I manufactured these products on my business site and sold the inventory through online avenues such as eBay and the main domain website.

    This business started out on a fast track. The websites received a high volume of traffic and the inventory sold quickly. But the investment, both in time and money, to continue to operate the business became more of burden than I anticipated. It would require more staff, more production space, and an extensive more amount of time to manage everything. It was all a great sign of growth, and future success, but it became evident that this business did not fit the focus I was seeking.

    So in December 2009 I sold off the last of the inventory and moved onto something that was more my specialty and which has become the perfect niche for me. I am still a business owner, but now I am a Virtual Assistant.

    Operating this company was such an exciting, and challenging event. I love to seek challenges, to reach new heights, and to take an innovative and creative approach to my work. And it was with this company that I developed my website and internet experience which led me to the savvy technical professional I am today.

  • Staff Accountant

    Jewish Community Center

    (Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    January 2004 — December 2006 (3 years )

    Jewish Community Center is a nonprofit center focused on the life, education, and health of the Louisville community.

    I was a staff accountant whose primary roles were accounts payable and cash management. I was responsible for all the cash that was required to run the day-to-day operations of the facility. Each department had cash registers and end-of-day balancing and hourly management was required to maintain the registers. I was also responsible for the full-charge accounts payable process along with membership registrations and program registrations.

    I found this position to be quite gratifying. The work environment and my colleagues were such a pleasure to work with. However, I felt this position did not offer the challenges and growth that I desired in my career. I am always a person who requires new and challenging experiences.

  • Accounts Payable Specialist

    Alcan Packaging Pharma Center Shelbyville

    (Pharmaceuticals industry)

    January 2002 — December 2004 (3 years )

    Alcan Packaging a Worldwide Corporation, which at the time was headquartered in Germany, are experts in packaging for pharmaceutical companies (and more) worldwide.

    I was the full-charge accounts payable specialist. I was responsible for managing all the bills from the time the product reached the site. I worked extensively with the logistics department to maintain proper document and work flow between all associated staff. I was responsible for month-end close, check runs, and capital project document management.

    Advanced spreadsheets and analytical skills were just a couple of the necessary skills required to manage this position. I managed foreign currency issues and was required to understand metric units to properly receive inventory and manage the payment side. I also had the privilege to maintain customer service relationships with vendors and banks from all over the world.

    This job was such a challenge and I thoroughly enjoyed my time with this company. I moved from this position to the Jewish Community Center so I could slow things down a little bit and not have such a drastic commute to work. (I had a 45-mile drive through the city.)

    (Printing industry)

    This is where I developed my administrative management skills. I worked directly with the President of the company. He started me out as a customer service representative to his largest account, with the intention from the start to move me into the Administrative Manager position. Shortly after he hired me, I received that promotion and I was then responsible in overseeing the customer service department while managing all other administrative tasks. Accounting, human resources, customer service, industry knowledge and sales support were just some of my responsibilities when working with this company.
