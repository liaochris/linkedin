Fortune McLemore’s Summary

I am undergoing a career transition, from being a freelance photographer, to a counseling psychologist. I am working on both a BS degree in psychology, via distance education, at Upper Iowa University in Fayette, IA, and an associate degree in mental health at Montgomery College in Silver Spring, MD. This associate degree program is excellent preparation for a master's degree, because it teaches subjects such as counseling theory and technique, and systems theory, which are normally taught at the master's level. This program also offers two practicums of two hundred hours each, so a student graduates with four hundred hours of experience already in place.

I plan to obtain a master's degree in counseling at Johns Hopkins University in Baltimore, then pursue a doctorate in counseling. I have volunteered with SPAN USA, Suicide Prevention Action Network as a Field Liaison for the 4th Congressional district in Maryland, and with CrisisLink as well, as a writer/researcher. I also write mental health articles for Examiner.com, as a DC Mental Health Examiner. I've written on such diverse topics as social anxiety disorder, mental illness within families, Traumatic Brain Injury, and personality theory. I usually write a "series" of articles on a particular topic, and I am always looking for new ideas for articles.

I would like to obtain an administrative assistant type job in the Washington, DC area in the mental health area. I have learned so much about this field from working at organizations like the American Psychiatric Association, where I was always reading their journals, the American Psychological Association, where I learned about things like issues with HMOs, and the International Association of Counseling Services, where I learned about the importance of accreditation for counseling centers. I have been very lucky to work in those places, and I would love to work in another organization where I could learn more about the field.

Fortune McLemore’s Specialties:

I'd like to work in an employee assistance program. As a freelance photographer, I did temporary office work to supplement my income, and I've seen how employees' personal and work lives overlap. Among my interests are substance abuse, eating disorders, grief counseling, and personality disorders. I am also interested in veterans' mental health issues. I would love to hear from professionals in any of those areas, but anyone is welcome!
