May Deng’s Summary

I have been interested in working on Environmental Issues and Activism for a long time. I started learning about Environmental Justice as a ninth grader. I participated in Toxic Tours conducted by LEJ (Literacy for Environmental Justice) in Bayview Hunters Point in San Francisco and Environmental Activism. As an Earth Corps Member and High School Student, I talked to Staff Members of my High School and San Francisco Environment to help start a Recycling Program. I received an Environmental Service Award from San Francisco Environment. As a result, I was voted for Most Environmentally Conscious by my English Teacher, Ms. Kohler.

I gained experience through multiple Environmental Issues and Justice related opportunities. I worked as an Intern for a Non-Profit Environmental Justice Organization called Global Community Monitor during the Summer of 2008. I was a Compost Campaign Volunteer Canvasser at the San Francisco Department of the Environment. I talked to San Francisco Residents to help encourage them to compost and recycle. I am interested in Natural Resources Conservation, Waste Management, Environmental Injustice, Geographic Information Systems, and Environmental Planning. My goal is to work with organizations dedicated to solving and alleviating environmental problems, to teach others how to become advocates for solving our environment problems, and to pursue environmental education for everyone.

I am also interested in becoming an Elementary School Teacher. I enjoy working with Elementary School students and helping them learn. I have experience with working in a Fourth Grade Classroom in North Berkeley. I am currently working as a Paraeducator in Elementary School After School Programs in San Mateo. I am currently studying for the CSET for applying for Teaching Schools.
