Brad Miskell’s Experience

  • Freelance Writer

    WEB: Contributing writer of satire, reviews, profiles, book adaptations and web copy for sites incl. Huffington Post, Artnet Magazine, Journal of AIDS Care (IAPAC).

  • Chief Instigator

    DogtownInk.com

    (Online Media industry)

    2008 — 2008 (less than a year)

    ON HIATUS Publisher, Editor-in-Chief, Chief Technology Officer for satire, counterculture and local interest site, Dogtown Ink. Responsible for site design, code customizing, SEO, SMO and administration. Create vast majority of content, including video, photo and editorial. Oversee contributors.

  • Creator/Host

    Yahoo! News Underground

    (Public Company; YHOO; Internet industry)

    August 2006 — August 2007 (1 year 1 month)

    Created, hosted and created all content for Yahoo! News Underground—a unique portal dedicated to American subculture featuring video, written and photographic reportage—for Yahoo! News (w/ 40 million unique monthly readers).

    Researched subjects and reported from the field; shot, edited, wrote scripts for and narrated all video shorts; wrote and performed clip soundtracks—often narrative songs—in subculture-specific musical styles; wrote all feature articles and blog posts; photographed, composed and wrote copy for slide shows; performed all copywriting.

    Published features comprised of multiple videos, articles, blog posts and slide shows bi-monthly. Blogged (vlogged) about community members between feature publication.

    Oversaw Underground social networks at Flickr, Jumpcut, Yahoo Video, Upcoming and MySpace.

    Designed and built site prototype.

    Reported to Yahoo! News managing editor, publisher and executive producer of video.

  • Editor

    AMEX Custom Publ., Smart & Strong, Fishdrum, Inc., Gruner + Jahr Publ.

    (Writing and Editing industry)

    1998 — 2006 (8 years )

    Consulting/contributing editor, creating edit plans and TOC of proposed publications for major corporate clients including Jet Blue, Target, G+J.

    Longtime contributing editor to literary magazine Fishdrum.

  • Non-profit/Corporate Writing/Design

    Gill Foundation, CAPE (Equality California), MCI, MedSN, SureScripts, Poets In Need

    (Non-Profit; Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    2000 — 2005 (5 years )

    Wrote annual reports, public addresses, press releases, articles, web copy, biz plans, PPT presos, correspondence, etc.

    Designed websites, PPT presos, print ads, one sheets, logos, etc.

  • Writer/Screenwriter

    Various Company

    (Marketing and Advertising industry)

    1994 — 2004 (10 years )

    Created and developed original programming under contract for entertainment companies including Paramount TV, Viacom TV, MTV Films, Broadway Video, NBC TV, Modern Cartoons, Sunshine TV.

    Consulting writer on Rudy Guiliani documentary Rudyland for HBO/Downtown Productions.

  • Fine Artist

    International Institutions

    (Fine Art industry)

    1993 — 1998 (5 years )

    Created multimedia art works for major exhibitions at international institutions including New York Museum of Modern Art, Boymanes Museum (Rotterdam Intl. Film Festival) and Wexner Center for the Arts. Created works shown at galleries including Yale Art Gallery, Thread Waxing Space (NYC) and American Fine Arts (NYC).

    Wrote conceptual pieces for exhibitions at galleries and institutions including Unité d’Habitation, Firminy, France and Museum of Contemporary Art, Antwerp, and for Social Text 36 (Duke University Press). Curated and mixed music for the exhibition alt.youth.media at New Museum of Contemporary Art (NYC).

  • Performing Artist

    Theater, Film & TV

    (Performing Arts industry)

    1978 — 1994 (16 years )

    Appeared in five Broadway musicals including 1986 Tony Award-winning Best Musical The Mystery of Edwin Drood (original cast member) as well as in films and TV including A Chorus Line (Columbia Pictures) and The Best of Broadway Dances (Showtime) and music videos for Def Leppard, Nile Rodgers, Jim Steadman, etc.

    Played regular gigs at CBGB and Bitter End in early ‘90’s. Developed by Geffen Music and Atlantic Records. Produced by Grammy Award winner John Leventhal, Wharton Tiers and others. Published songwriter and member ASCAP.
