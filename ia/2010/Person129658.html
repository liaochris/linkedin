Marion MacKenzie Pyle’s Summary

Communications professional, Marion Mackenzie Pyle is known for wanting to change the world.

As Vice President and Director of Latino Strategies at The Rogers Group, she spearheads multicultural social marketing about childhood development issues such as autism, anti-smoking and childhood obesity for First 5 California. For Warner Brothers Interactive Entertainment, she spotlights Sesame Street’s new video games so toddlers get an early start with reading and math skills. Her non-profit work includes advancing efforts for the LA Regional Foodbank, the Institute of Myeloma and Bone Cancer Research and CORO.

As writer, producer and co-host of Metropolitan Water District’s video news magazine “Straight From The Tap” Marion passionately pursued a sustainable use of our state’s natural resources. The series proved to be one of the most strategic and successful public education vehicles in MWD’s recent history reaching a statewide cable television audience of more than 17 million and garnering numerous Telly, PRism,PCLA Aurora, and other public communication honors.

As copywriter and producer, Marion’s advertising experience includes Clio and Belding-award-winning work for accounts such as the Mexican Ministry of Tourism, Kraft Foods USA and La Opinion. She has helped advance philanthropic causes for World Vision and also championed memorable campaigns for the health, banking and automotive industries.

Marion has a B.A. in Mass Media and Communications from the Anahuac University in Mexico City and an M.A. in Communication Management from the Annenberg School of Communications. Affiliations include the Southern California Water Committee, the Producer's Guild of America, the Association of Television and Radio Artists, and the Screen Actor’s Guild of America.
