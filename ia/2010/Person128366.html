Luca Candela’s Summary

A good way to define me would be: energetic, hard working problem solver with multiple years of experience in launching new sites and web applications, defining requirements, analyzing competition, creating on-line strategies, driving revenue growth and bringing projects in on time and on budget.

But apart of all the marketing, keyword ridden fluff, what sets me apart is the ability to see through complexities and find the core of a problem, then solve it in the most elegant way available to me.

I'm a constant stream of new ideas, and I like to work with equally energetic people to test the boundaries of what can be done instead of playing it safe.

The great passion of my life is product design: here my Italian upbringing really gets the center stage. Every Italian loves to be comfortable, to have and use nice things, to be surrounded by beauty and elegance, and that's what I strive for in my life and in my work.

The elegance of a beautiful object serving a purpose is the highest expression of human ingenuity, being a Ferrari Italia, a Parker Fly or a Alessi 9090.

With work experience in Italy, Spain and in the United States. As the result of that, I have a solid international experience and understanding of both the interactive markets on both sides of the Atlantic, and I speak Italian, English, Spanish and a little bit of German!

You can reach me at info@luckymethod.com

  • Liceo Scientifico

    1992 — 1997

    Liceo scientifico (in Italian, Scientific Lyceum) is a secondary school type in Italy. It lasts 5 years (students of about 14-19 years of age).

    Students follow a specific pattern of courses that covers a large range of disciplines: Italian language and literature, history, philosophy, Latin, mathematics, physics, foreign language (usually English, German or French, but many schools offer different courses), life sciences, art history and chemistry.

    It offers a complete and widespread range of knowledge, allowing its students to attend most University courses.

    A Liceo scientifico course is divided between the biennio (the first two school years) and the triennio (the remaining three), which is characterised by higher skill requirements and different subjects. The minimum days of lessons per year are 200.
