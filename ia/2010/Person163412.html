Philip Kearney’s Experience

  • President & Co-Founder

    Trailing Zee Productions, LLC.

    (Privately Held; Computer Software industry)

    December 2008 — Present (1 year 7 months)

    Development of mobile application solutions for the Apple iPhone, iPod touch and other smartphone platforms. Creating the best, most innovative mobile platform applications and games to meet client needs. Leading a team that develops back-end foundational technology and software that makes a client company's blog or online portal accessible to their users, as well as daily downloads of their content. With these solutions, a client can deepen their marketing impact while reaching out to new customers in ways not previously available without creating and dedicating an internal application development team to such an effort.

  • Director of Engineering & Product Development, Data Networking Technologies

    Apple, Inc.

    (Computer Hardware industry)

    January 2002 — December 2008 (7 years )

    Directed a team of over 70 software, hardware, embedded firmware and QA engineers responsible for developing the data networking technologies deployed in Macintosh computers as well as the AirPort Base Station product line.

    Managed every aspect of the design & manufacture of all hardware modules, device drivers and associated MacOS X user interface software for all of the Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, Ethernet, dial-up modem and 3G WWAN technology used in Macintosh computers. Duties included day to day management of internal engineering teams, driving relations with silicon vendors worldwide and contract manufacturers in Asia, developing short & long term product and technology roadmaps, managing capital, spending & human resource plans/budgets.

    Visionary behind every aspect of the development of Apple’s AirPort Base Station line of Wi-Fi access point products including four different models of the AirPort Extreme, two different models of the AirPort Express as well as the Time Capsule product. Grew the Base Station business over tenfold in revenues to over $500M annually.

    Chief strategist behind Apple’s innovation and standards development in these technologies, I also managed a Standards & Architecture group that sends representatives to organizations like IEEE, Wi-Fi Alliance, Bluetooth SIG and PCI SIG. Apple’s agenda and objectives were worked into these groups to form new standards and drive innovation of these technologies to meet Apple’s needs. Co-authored a number of patents for Apple in the AirPort Base Station products in areas such as AirTunes, Wi-Fi Direct and use of Link Local networking.

    Apple’s board member on the Board of Directors of the Wi-Fi Alliance where all Wi-Fi policy and standards coordination and interoperability compliance is driven. Successfully interacted with many executives from many of the member companies to help represent their interests at the board level of this organization.

  • Sr. Manager of Engineering, Wireless Communications & Networking Division

    Agere Systems

    (Public Company; LSI; Semiconductors industry)

    January 2001 — January 2002 (1 year 1 month)

    Built the team responsible for the specification, design, implementation, integration, and testing of a variety of Wi-Fi (802.11) products including wireless client software for Linux, Macintosh, and Windows as well as embedded Wi-Fi access point products covering Enterprise, Secure Enterprise, and Small Office Home Office (SOHO) market spaces.

    Delivered multiple products under the labels of Apple, Avaya, Compaq, Dell, HP, IBM, Lucent, Toshiba & others.

    [The Wireless Communications and Networking Division of Lucent, among many others, were spun out of Lucent Technologies and became Agere Systems.]

  • Engineering Manager, Wireless Communications & Networking Division

    Lucent Technologies

    (Public Company; LU; Telecommunications industry)

    June 1999 — January 2001 (1 year 8 months)

    One of the people responsible for Apple’s market-defining first generation AirPort base station product after leading the development of the world’s first modern wireless home router, the Lucent BG-2000. Lead a team of software engineers and a team of QA engineers who designed and implemented wireless client software solutions for the WaveLAN and Orinoco line of 802.11 products. This included driver and configuration interface development for the Windows, MacOS and Unix platforms. The team also developed all client software for the Lucent secure 802.11 product line called the AS-1000.

    After leading my team in integrating all of the embedded VxWorks code used in Lucent’s different WaveLAN 802.11 access point products for the Enterprise, Secure Enterprise, SOHO and Outdoor markets, I was promoted by the Director of the division to Sr. Manager and tasked with the duties described above.

    About six months after this promotion, Lucent spun it’s semiconductor division out into a new company called Agere Systems and the entire Lucent Wireless Division was moved into that new company.

    [Ascend Communications was bought by Lucent Technologies.]

  • Senior Software Engineer

    Ascend Communications

    (Public Company; ASND; Telecommunications industry)

    March 1997 — June 1999 (2 years 4 months)

    Worked on the design and implementation of embedded data networking software solutions at Ascend and eventually was promoted to manager of my team. Team wrote low level embedded networking software technologies for products like the Ascend Max 6000 dial-up concentrator as well as the TNT and PPPoE concentrator devices and Pipeline broadband modems. Also wrote and debugged TCP/IP client software products like TCP/Connect4 and NFS/Share.

    [InterCon, a subsidiary of PSINet, was bought by Ascend Communications]

  • Senior Software Engineer

    InterCon Systems

    (Privately Held; Computer Software industry)

    October 1992 — March 1997 (4 years 6 months)

    Responsible for specification, design, implementation, and testing of IETF standards-based TCP/IP client software for Macintosh and Windows platforms. Shipped over a dozen commercially successful networking software products including AOL’s first web browser for the Macintosh, TCP/Connect, NFS/Share, Networker, InterPrint, InterPPP and InterSLIP among others.

    InterCon Systems was purchased by PSINet in late 1995 who held the company as a wholly owned subsidiary until early 1997 when they sold the engineering assets of InterCon to Ascend Communications.

As a software engineer I worked on internal software systems for the company to run on their Macintosh computers to aid in sales management and supply chain management.
