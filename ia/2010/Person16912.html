Art Silverman’s Experience

  • Founder and President

    Op-Ad Media

    (Marketing and Advertising industry)

    2005 — 2009 (4 years )

    • Owner of a creative services agency specializing in issue and advocacy communications.
    • Produced print, video and interactive media content; provided conceptual, editorial and design services.
    • Focus on climate change, clean energy technology and public health issues.
    • Sample Clients: Council of State Governments, Environmental Defense Fund, Google, Human Rights First, National Center for Women’s Health, National Council of Churches, Solar Energy Industries Association, Turner Strategies

  • Senior VP & Creative Director

    Fenton Communications

    (Privately Held; Public Relations and Communications industry)

    1996 — 2004 (8 years )

    • As Fenton's founding Creative Director, built and managed the agency's in-house advertising and design division. Produced groundbreaking public awareness and advocacy campaigns on controversial public policy issues.
    • As Senior VP, managed a team providing media relations, public affairs and editorial services to agency clients, including legislative, regulatory, litigation, and public awareness campaigns
    • Played a significant role in agency growth from 11 to 65 professional staff, one to three cities, and $1 million to $10 million in annual revenue.

  • Director of Communications

    Center for Science in the Public Interest

    (Public Policy industry)

    1993 — 1996 (3 years )

    • Helped CSPI cement its reputation as America’s premiere consumer group focused on food and nutrition.
    • Generated multiple stories in virtually every top U.S. media outlet.
    • Placed CSPI experts on Today, Good Morning America, Oprah, ET, even a Doonesbury mention and Letterman “Top 10” list.
    • Publicized CSPI's nutritional investigations into Chinese, Mexican and Italian restaurant foods, movie theater popcorn, dietary supplements, Olestra, etc.
    • Helped increase CSPI’s paid membership to more than 1 million.

  • Deputy Press Secretary to the Mayor

    San Francisco, CA

    (Government Agency; Government Administration industry)

    1990 — 1992 (2 years )

    • Official spokesperson for San Francisco Mayor Art Agnos; worked closely with local, national and international journalists.
    • Produced press conferences and large media events; accompanied the Mayor to public appearances; prepared briefing memos, press releases, position papers and op-ed columns.
    • Liaison with departmental communications staff including police, fire, public transit, EMT, hospital and mental health facilities, seaport, airport, stadium, etc.

  • Founding Editor

    SF Weekly

    (Privately Held; Newspapers industry)

    1986 — 1989 (3 years )

    • Helped establish the city’s first successful alternative newsweekly in 20 years (currently owned by Village Voice Publications).
    • Supervised editorial operations including news planning, assignments, story editing, headlines and covers.
    • Wrote 30+ cover stories, 18 months of weekly editorials and a weekly media & politics column.
