Jody Lusher’s Summary

Jody Lusher served his time with BP carrying out a modern apprenticeship with a mechanical bias. Once through his time he transferred offshore to work for Apache North Sea Ltd as an Operations Mechanic, during this time he carried out maintenance tasks on all types of rotating equipment as well as vessels, valves and heat exchangers. A short time was then spent in Apaches offices’ as there TAR Co-ordinator before he decided to look for a more technical role. This technical role took the form of reliability engineering with SKF in Aberdeen where Jody progressed from an assistant engineer to a consultant engineer and moved from carrying out routine cm tasks to ad-hoc work involving performance monitoring and thermo graphic imagery. He then moved across to the maintenance engineering team where he carried out Tag Hierarchy builds, template creation for equipment and routines and also did ECA, RCA, RCM and RAM Modelling. After 2 years at SKF in Aberdeen he then progressed his career by moving to SulzerWood as a contracts/project engineer where he spent a further 2 years developing his contractual and commercial awareness managing a number of key client accounts for the company. Jody decided to rejoin SKF AMS as an international consultant as he had always enjoyed the technical work, and felt with this role at SKF there would be a great many opportunities to develop within the company and at a personal level.
Moved on from SKF to take up a role as maintenance focal point working for Wood Group on the TAQA contract.
