Gary Goltz’s Summary

I assist companies in all aspects of sales including, new business development, national accounts, sales management, recruiting, training, marketing, and advertising. This is based upon my broad range of industry experience that spans bio medical devices, corporate compliance, databases, disease management, enterprise software solutions, home healthcare, radiology, and more. My experience as a judo leader and working with law enforcement often helps in building relationships with senior executives.

Gary Goltz’s Specialties:

I am known as the consummate networker whose perseverance has resulted in successful strategic alliances, mergers, and acquisitions with group purchasing organizations, hospital systems, insurance companies, and managed care payers on behalf of my clients.

  • President of Home Care Division

    Comprehensive Pharmacy Services

    (Privately Held; 501-1000 employees; Hospital & Health Care industry)

    March 1989 — July 1995 (6 years 5 months)

    Built this start-up into a market leader using disruptive technology based on a strategic decision to target managed care several years ahead of the competition. Curaflex Health Services acquired Comprehensive in July of 1993 for a record 7X earnings. Afterwards, I continued to serve as a senior executive up and through the company's merger with several of its national rivals to form Coram Healthcare in July of 1994. Both Foster and Coram today are now part of Apria, the largest home healthcare company in the nation.

  • Vice President

    (Hospital & Health Care industry)

    July 1977 — February 1989 (11 years 8 months)

    Lead company's highest revenue and most profitable region. Developed national sales training program and groomed a team of managers who continued to hold key leadership roles in the industry long after Abbey Medical (now Apria) acquired Foster.
