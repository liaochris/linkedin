Charles D. Toy’s Summary

Managing Director of PCG International LLC; Chief Compliance Officer of Pangaea Trading Corporation (2005-); Executive Vice President and Managing Member of Pangaea Holdings, LLC (2002-); Partner at Wilmer, Cutler & Pickering (now Wilmer Cutler Pickering Hale and Dorr LLP) (2001-2002); Vice President and General Counsel, Vice President for Investment Funds, and Vice President for Finance at Overseas Private Investment Corporation (1993-2001); Partner at Kaye, Scholer, Fierman, Hays & Handler (now Kaye Scholer LLP) (1984-1993); Associate at Milbank, Tweed, Hadley & McCloy (1980-1984).

Charles D. Toy’s Specialties:

International private equity; asset management; trade finance; international project finance; political risk insurance (partial risk guaranties); foreign direct investment; global private equity.

Charles D. Toy’s Experience

  • Managing Director

    PCGI

    (Partnership; Venture Capital & Private Equity industry)

    2007 — Present (3 years )

    PCGI is manager of international private equity funds of funds, including PCGI Global Opportunities Fund, LLC, PCGI Global Opportunities Fund 2 (CalPERS), LLC, and PCGI/NYSCRF Emerging Europe Investment Program, L.P. The funds manage assets for institutional investors from the public pension, foundation, and family office communities, and invest on a discretionary basis in a diversified portfolio of private equity and co-investment opportunities located outside of the United States, Canada, and Western Europe. Total assets under management are over $1.4 billion.

  • Executive Vice President

    Pangaea Holdings, LLC

    (Financial Services industry)

    September 2002 — December 2006 (4 years 4 months)

    Pangaea Holdings, LLC was a principal owner of Pangaea Trading Corporation, a private, non-bank provider of trade and other financing and related services to small- and medium-sized exporters of advanced technology products, from factoring, forfaiting, and other receivables financing to supply chain solutions (inventory management and inventory procurement services, including third-party logistics support). Compensation for such venture lending included warrants in the borrowers as well as interest income.

  • Partner

    (Partnership; 1001-5000 employees; Legal Services industry)

    April 2001 — August 2002 (1 year 5 months)

    Partner in Business Transactions Section.
    General representation of, U.S., European, and Asian multinational corporate clients, including preparation and negotiation of documentation for: (a) project and other international financings, (b) equity and other joint ventures with private and public-sector enterprises, and (c) establishment of operations in multiple jurisdictions, and advice and assistance with respect thereto.

  • Vice President and General Counsel

    Overseas Private Investment Corporation

    (Government Agency; 51-200 employees; International Trade and Development industry)

    September 1993 — February 2001 (7 years 6 months)

    Senior manager in profitable international finance and insurance agency with foreign policy and domestic economic mandates. Headed 40-person legal department providing advice for all project, internal management, and external relations matters. Vice President for Investment Funds managing support for over $3.25 billion in privately managed and financed funds making direct equity investments around the world. Vice President for Finance managing 50 in all facets of international lending program (loan production and operations, credit administration, and portfolio, risk, and process management). Negotiated government agreements for agency programs in South Africa, Gaza and the West Bank, Vietnam, Pakistan, India, Turkey, and Zimbabwe. During tenure, annual finance commitments grew from $415 million to a high of over $2.25 billion (over $9 billion in seven years), and annual insurance issued grew from $2.8 billion to a high of over $16.5 billion ($45 billion in seven years).

  • Adjunct Professor of Law

    The George Washington University, National Law Center

    (Educational Institution; 501-1000 employees; Higher Education industry)

    September 1994 — January 1995 (5 months)

    Instructor for course on “Foreign Direct Investment,” examining the legal, business, and financial problems involved in investing across national borders. Focused on the strategies and techniques for structuring and financing such investments. Analysis covered U.S. regulation of foreign investors, different kinds of foreign regulation of U.S. investments, and international controls on domestic regulation of foreign investment through treaties and conventions.

  • Partner

    Kaye, Scholer, Fierman, Hays & Handler

    (Partnership; 501-1000 employees; Legal Services industry)

  • Associate
