Tim Calder’s Summary

I am currently involved with a range of different organisations which are largely based here in Dunedin.

My education took me from the family farm in the Bay of Plenty to St Peter's School in Cambridge, Waikato, and then to the University of Otago in Dunedin. It is from there, during my studies, I came into contact with some great Otago business people, whom I now share offices with.

Since basing myself in Dunedin I have developed strong ties with local community groups that include The Distiller, University Rugby Club, Our Stadium Supporters Club, and the Big Buddy program.

Having now been based in Dunedin for the best part of a decade, when asked if I now call Dunedin home I reply "I will alway love, live and work from Dunedin, but heart and home will always be on the farm in Pikowai"

Tim Calder’s Specialties:

Management Consulting, Financial Planning, Project Managment, Strategic Business Development

Tim Calder’s Experience

  • Co-founder

    ParkHawk

    (Internet industry)

    October 2009 — Present (8 months)

    ParkHawk is a web based parking management system designed to help provide the most efficient car parking solutions for both large scale parking buildings as well as individual parking spaces. ParkHawk uses a web based platform to enable the ability to view a live feed of car parks available in any given area while allowing users to book them for their required time periods.

  • Co-founder

    The Distiller

    (Internet industry)

    August 2008 — Present (1 year 10 months)

    The Distiller is a network of IT Entrepreneurs which was formed to provide a supportive community for networking, collaboration, and to provide investment opportunities. The Distiller is also aiming to create a large enough network that would support the creation of New Zealands first complete IT incubation residence similar to the Y-Combinator in the US.

  • Club Secretary

    Our Stadium Visionaries Club

    (Sports industry)

    February 2007 — Present (3 years 4 months)

    Tim is a founding member of the Our Stadium Visionaries Club which is a supporter’s club formed to promote the benefits of the proposed multi-purpose stadium at Awatea Street, and to assist the Carisbrook Stadium Trust which is the authority responsible for the development of this project. Tim holds the position of secretary on the committee which includes prominent New Zealand sports, business and community representatives.

  • Club Manager

    Otago University Rugby Club

    (Sports industry)

    February 2006 — Present (4 years 4 months)

    Tim is the Manager for the Otago University Rugby Club. Tim oversees the operations of the rugby club while promoting the club as the greatest University Rugby Club in the country. The Otago University Rugby Club lays claim to producing the highest number of All Blacks out of any other club in New Zealand. Since recently retiring from Rugby in 2007, Tim is also a member of the Light Blues which is an Association of non-players that are dedicated to assisting with University rugby and promoting the club through its network of past players around the world.

  • General Manager

    EZY STOR Self Storage

    (Construction industry)

    August 2005 — Present (4 years 10 months)

    Tim is the General Manager of EZY STOR Self Storage a rapidly growing self storage company established in 2003 which now has five locations across Dunedin totaling over 300 garages. EZY STOR has developed a reputation for high quality storage spaces as all of our facilities comprise of purpose built concrete garages with power points and lights. EZY STOR is riding the high growth of the storage industry within New Zealand and Australia. EZY STOR is a certified member of the Self Storage Association of Australasia.

  • General Manager

    Willowbank Quarter

    (Real Estate industry)

    May 2005 — Present (5 years 1 month)

    Willowbank Quarter is a 31 apartment, 150 bedroom accommodation development that consists of high quality student and executive accommodation. Willowbank Quarter was developed by John Farry, Tony and Max Clear, from the old Wilson Whiskey Distillery which closed in 2002. The development also included a commercial property which is now home to IT firm Enabling Technology.

  • Managing Director

    Carswell Calder Limited

    (Management Consulting industry)

    2004 — Present (6 years )

    Carswell Calder Limited was the first company I created to commence my contract management career. The first contracts I received while in my second year of University were: The Otago Language Centre, Gardens Tavern, Allied Security, and New Zealand Duty Free Stores. These contracts later brought me to to the attention of Dunedin's top property developers and business people whom now this company largely consults for.

Tim Calder’s Education

  • University of Otago

    MBA , Business Administration , 2007 — 2009

    The Otago MBA is New Zealands highest ranked MBA, it is in the top 3 in Australasia, and the top 100 in the world (out of over 30,000 MBA courses). I was fortunate to be accepted into this prestigious course as a 23 year old considering I had only just received the required real life business experience required from the management consulting firm established previously. I am honored to follow such successful business people as Graeme Hart (billionaire owner of Carter Holt Harvey, Burns Philip, etc) and Theresa Gattung (CEO of Telecom New Zealand) who have completed an Otago MBA.

    Activities and Societies:
    The Distiller - IT Web Entrepreneurs Network

  • University of Otago

    M Entr , Entrepreneurship , 2006 — 2007

    The Master of Entrepreneurship course was in it's forth year when I was accepted. This is a revolutionary new university course as it deals with the practical development of high growth businesses. Everyone accepted in this course was activity working on their business with the clear expectation of taking it global. In this course I was fortunate to meet a range of very talented business owners, many of which I have gone on to work with on different projects. This course introduced us to many inspiring local and international entrepreneurs as well as accountants, lawyers, venture capitalists, and other successful business people.

    Activities and Societies:
    Chamber of Commerce, The Dunedin Club, Lunch Speak Toastmasters, Our Stadium Supporters Club

  • University of Otago

    B Com , Management, Accounting , 2002 — 2006

    I studied a wide range of business subjects in my first year which included Legal Studies, Business Law, Economics, Computer Science, Accounting, and Management. I intended to complete a minor in Economics under a Bachelor of Arts but moved on to my Masters early. It was in these early years when I got a taste for property as I purchased my first property at the age of 18, operating it as an International Students boarding hostel. For the first three years I was very focused on my Rugby career, it wasn't until I could no longer afford the intense practice time required to compete with the other professional players as well as the downtime experienced after a tough physical game.

    Activities and Societies:
    Selwyn College, Otago Property Investors Association, University Rugby Colts Blues (Captain), Otago Under 19s

  • St Peter's School, Cambridge

    1997 — 2001

    Set in a beautiful rural, boarding environment St Peter's School, Cambridge, was a very enjoyable 5 years of considerable personal growth. Over this time I was introduced to my first experience with management and leadership at both student, staff, sporting, academic and administration levels. As a Head Prefect I was invited to join both the Hamilton City and Waipa District Youth Councils' where I received my first insight into how larger organisations operate. I excelled in Accounting, Business Studies and Mathematics academically and rugby, equestrian, and long distance running sportingly.

    Activities and Societies:
    Head Boy, Head of House (Boarding), Head of Sport, Captain of the 1st XV, Co-captain of Equestrian.
