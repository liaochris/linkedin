Michael Capiraso’s Summary

Michael Capiraso is a General Manager, Marketing and Strategy exec focused on bringing high-profile, traditional brands to new audiences. He has led Marketing, Communications and product development for blue-chip brands including the National Football League and Calvin Klein. Equally at home in strategic/operations and CMO roles, Michael brings deep vertical background in the consumer, sports, fashion, entertainment and media sectors. His strong suit is re-imagining brand potential for interactive, community-seeking markets to drive profitability.

Most recently Michael was CMO at Cole Haan, renovating the brand and product set to tap the high-end denim/leisure market with the successful development of the Cole, Rood and Haan sub-brand, and building Cole Haan’s first celebrity endorsement/product development deal with Maria Sharapova. Prior to Cole Haan, Michael was CEO of PRISM North America, a WPP Sports & Entertainment agency advising AT&T, Coca-Cola Bottling Co., Xerox, Rolex, PGA, Dolphins Enterprises and Ferrari on strategy, marketing and PR.

As VP Marketing/Executive Creative Director at the National Football League, Michael brought new energy to the NFL’s brand via youth and Hispanic sites and redesigned NFLshop and NFL.com sites to suit a changing audience profile. Overseeing $200M in media assets, he led a marketing integration across SBUs and a partner roster including Coors, Visa, IBM, Burger King, FedEx, Sprint, Pepsi and Reebok. He created the Super Bowl XL “Road to Forty” platform, which led to $300 million in activation, the highest in NFL history.

At Major League Baseball, Michael consulted on marketing and strategy. Earlier, as a VP at Calvin Klein, he enacted some of the most recognized advertising and PR campaigns for all Calvin Klein brands including cK one, Escape, Eternity, and Calvin Klein Jeans.

Michael has an MBA and studied Film at New York University, and has recently completed his 19th consecutive NYC Marathon.
