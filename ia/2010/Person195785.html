  • Chief Architect Quicken/Quicken Health

    Intuit

    (Public Company; 5001-10,000 employees; INTU; Computer Software industry)

    November 2004 — November 2008 (4 years 1 month)

    In this role I served to define the technical architecture, development process and strategy for the product lines. Having started from its onset, I worked to define the initial Quicken Health product including partnering with Ingenix, Hewitt, United Health, Optima and Cigna. Moving the group to an Agile process, in a startup environment, I worked to define the initial product and architecture including designs, put in place the design process and built out the Quicken Health business and technical roadmap. I also served to work with customers supporting the initial product rollout.

    During that time, I invented and coauthored the OMX (Open Medical Exchange) standard, a standard for retrieving consumer medical information from Health Plans. Building of HIPPA and HL7, OMX extends the range of information available to consumers. Working with Ingenix, I defined the technical architecture for the Quicken Health site including automatic claim and benefit plan information download from plans via OMX. Quicken Health was architected using a combination of SSIS, SQL, Java and ASP.net. The architecture provided full fault tolerance, scalability to (initial design supporting a ramp to 10 million in three years).

    Prior to this, I established the initial architecture for what would become the Intuit Bills and Cash Manager product ultimately leading to the acquisition of Digital Insight. Bills and Cash Manager was designed as an enterprise application deployed within a bank allowing the bank to provide similar functionality as that of Quicken and QuickBooks to its consumer and small business customers.

    Additionally, I architected the Quicken Home Inventory product, a consumer productivity tool for personal inventory management which recently won a Codie award for "Best Consumer Productivity Solution".
