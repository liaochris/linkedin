  • Director of Photography

    National Geographic Society

    (Non-Profit; 1001-5000 employees; Publishing industry)

    March 1987 — December 1997 (10 years 10 months)

    Directed operation of the Photographic Division that produced all still photography for the Society, with the primary focus on National Geographic Magazine. Set and managed an annual budget of approximately $6.5 M. Selected photographers to execute photo assignments, negotiating all necessary contracts and arranging for appropriate logistical support. In addition, developed story ideas with photographers and served on the Editor’s advisory board for content creation. Reviewed policy and contract issues annually, making recommendations to the Executive Management Committee on necessary changes.

Tom Kennedy’s Honors:

Under my editorial leadership, washingtonpost.com's documentary video team earned national and local Emmys, four consecutive Murrow awards for excellence in broadcast journalism in our category, and a Peabody Award. Overall collaborations between video journalists and multimedia editors have also done work that has earned two consecutive Scripps Howard National Journalism awards for Web Reporting.

While at the National Geographic, we were nominated eight times in ten years for excellence in photography in the American Society of Magazine Editors annual National Magazine Awards competition, winning the award five times.

While at the Philadelphia Inquirer, I directed and edited photo projects that won two Pulitzer Prizes for Feature Photography as well as two Robert F. Kennedy prizes.

While at the Gainesville Sun, I had a photographer win the National Press Photographers Association "Photographer of the Year Award".
