Dick Kegels’s Summary

I am comfortable in my role as coach, specifically when it comes to career and business. Over the years I gained a pretty good insight into what makes people tick, and in that process, learned a bit about myself as well.
Contrary to the popular belief I do not think that we have to find our true inner self in order to get the job that was made for us. I rather think that we have an extraordinary capacity for fulfilling a great many different roles that suit our personality. Do you know which roles you fit?

A lot of people simply did not have a higher calling to become doctor, nurse, policeman or a fire-fighter. However though, most people do experience unease in their professional lives from time to time.
But, all too often they do not take action and guess what?
Before you know the company they work for work is downsizing and his or her job is being shipped overseas. Currently here in Holland in Oss, some 2000+ jobs are in jeopardy thanks to Big Pharma.

I was struck by the book of Tom Peters called ‘The Brand You’ wherein Tom delves into to the productivity of white collar workers and claimed that ‘90 percent of the jobs will be either transformed or lost within the next ten years’. Tom wrote that around 2000. Well, you know, he was right and then the economy did the rest. Basically, what he says is this: you owe it to yourself to make the most of your professional life and you better start working on it now. And also: it can be done!

You are a talented, gifted human being with ideas and skills that you carry with you all day but some of us leave at the door in the morning when going to work.
So, in order to stay in our professional lives we have to be good at what we are doing. But you only can become good at what you do if it is the thing that you really want to do. And you can only do what you really want to do by bringing your whole being to work. Simple, but not easy.
