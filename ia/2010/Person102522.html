John Loken’s Summary

I’m a conservatory-trained musician who “got religion” halfway through UC Berkeley and decided to pursue a career on the other side of the desk – as a record label executive. After graduation I moved to LA and climbed the corporate ladder, first at Warner Bros. Records, then PolyGram International, coordinating international album launches for everyone from Madonna to Stevie Wonder. This was brand management bootcamp, where I learned that an artist’s attitude and actions are as important as the music they make.

In the mid 90s I got off the corporate treadmill for a bit and ran indie labels China Records (home to Morcheeba) and Ultimatum Music (Sugarcult). Leveraging the success of Ultimatum, a joint venture with the William Morris Agency, my team sourced and managed acquisitions including Fearless Records (at the drive in, Plain White T’s), social commerce site Smartpunk.com, and an artist management division. By 2006, when I was recruited by Live Nation to oversee their tour marketing department, it had become clear that the technological and social forces disrupting the music business were irreversible.

Rather than sit idle, I checked into business school to fine tune my quant skills. I studied strategy development and value creation, immersing myself in the world of venture capital. I also co-founded a creative agency that helped brands use music as a means to engage and retain customers. Our thesis was that corporations are modern day "Medicis", funding the creation and distribution of entertainment content. Subsequently, our shop, BPM(brands+music), partnered with numerous ad agencies to become one of the earliest purveyors of music-as-a-service.

Today I lead the performance marketing initiatives at Goldstar.com, the #1 publisher of discount offers to live entertainment. I speak regularly at industry conferences, participate in the occasional Triathlon, and blog and tweet about digital culture, disruptive business models, and brand strategy.
