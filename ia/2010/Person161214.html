Paulo Barthelmess’s Summary

My primary focus is on multimodal collaborative systems and applications. I have explored alternative approaches that leverage natural group communication to provide pro-active support. In particular, I have looked at issues involving the integration of speech, handwriting, sketch and gesture recognizers as sources of information driving automated system support features.

As part of this focus on supporting natural communication and practices, I have been exploring tangible interfaces using digital paper technology to support collaboration in environments in which computers are not a good fit, for example in meeting rooms and in the field.

I have also an ongoing interest in process-oriented systems research, and have an extensive background working on software development and training in the industry.

Paulo Barthelmess’s Specialties:

Computer mediated human interaction and collaboration; intelligent systems; multimodal and perceptual systems; tangible and mobile interaction. Process-oriented software systems, workflow systems.
