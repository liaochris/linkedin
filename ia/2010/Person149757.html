  • Graduate Student

    Princeton University

    (Institución educativa; Enseñanza superior industry)

    2000 — 2005 (5 years )

    "Ab-initio molecular dynamics via maximally localized Wannier functions: Theory, implementation and applications to the vibrational and dielectric properties of liquid water and ice"

    Implemented novel numerical techniques within an ab-initio molecular dynamics code (Car-Parrinello Molecular Dynamics) to do molecular dynamics based on localized orbitals (Maximally Localized Wannier Function) within a plane wave Density Functional Theory (DFT) formalism.

    Calculated the dielectric constant of water and ice and demonstrated that the origin of the anomalously large dielectric constant lies in its local tetrahedral co-ordination and hydrogen bonding.
