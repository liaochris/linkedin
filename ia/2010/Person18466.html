August Craanen’s Summary

Formerly with the Dutch Merchant Marine. His interest in energy conservation began when he launched his career during the energy crisis of the 1970’s.
In the hospitality industry, Mr. Craanen was overseeing engineering, architectural design, and construction services for the development, renovation, and maintenance of major hotel operations.
In addition to a number of impressive hotel industry affiliations, Mr. Craanen is an active member of the Association of Energy Engineers; the American Institute of Plant Engineers; the American Society of Heating, Refrigeration, and Air Conditioning Engineers; the Association for Facility Engineering; and the National Fire Protection Association.
From 1994-1995, Mr. Craanen served on the board for the NYC Hotel Engineers Association, and from 2003 – 2005, he served as Chair of the Engineering and Environmental Committee for the American Hotel & Lodging Association. Within the American Hotel & Lodging Association, Mr. Craanen was named as a Certified Hotel Administrator (C.H.A.), and as a Certified Engineering Operation Executive (C.E.O.E.). He also holds a certificate in Certified Plant Engineering (C.P.E) from the Association of Facility Engineers.
Mr. Craanen’s vast experience includes guest lectures at NYU’s School of Hospitality Facility Management, and he has been a panel member for topics ranging from “Alternative Energy Management Solutions” to “Indoor Air Quality” at the National Hotel Show (NYC).

Mr. Craanen has assisted in developing the “Energy Management and Conservation Guide” published by the A.H.&L.A. in November 2001, and in developing the “Maintenance Employee Guide” as a part of the Hospitality Skills training Series produced by the Educational Institute of the AH&LA. He was a key contributor to the “renovation” chapter of the Hospitality Facility Management and Design handbook published by the AH&LA and has contributed to various monthly publications in hospitality Industry.
