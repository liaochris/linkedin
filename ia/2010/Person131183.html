  • Postdoctoral Fellow

    Medical College of Wisconsin

    (Educational Institution; Research industry)

    May 2007 — September 2008 (1 year 5 months)

    Human and Murin Epilepsy. (see www.marcchevalier.fr)

    Keywords: Temporal Lobe Epilepsy, Seizures, Persistent Sodium current, Network plasticity, Intrinsic Pacemaker properties, Neocortical neurons.

    Projects associated with research among epilepsy are various and mostly concentrated on the determination of the mechanisms modified in epileptic brain. Here, we try to define the mechanisms associated with the onset of epilepsy. If we can determine the changes of intrinsic properties and network relashionships between neurons, we hypothesize that it could be easier to define a treatment. We work on mouse to screen the different hypotheses and then we have the opportunity to verify them on brain slices isolated by surgery on children with intractable epilepsy.

  • PhD Thesis - President of "Reseau BIOTechno"

    CNRS UMR 5017

    (Research industry)

    October 2003 — October 2007 (4 years 1 month)

    (see www.marcchevalier.fr)

    Keywords: Excitability, Pacemaker, Burst Firing, Epilepsy, Thalamus, Channel Regulation

    Techniques: Patch-Clamp (Current-clamp, Voltage-Clamp), Epifluorescence (Indo-1), Confocal Microscopy (Fluo-3, GFP-tagged protein), Molecular Biology (RT-PCR), Cell culture.

    This work was performed in order to better understand the role of T-type voltage-activated calcium channel in the regulation of exitability. From this fundamental research, many hypotheses have highlighted the major role of T-type calcium channels in rhythm genesis.

    References:

    Traboulsie A, Chemin J, Chevalier M, Quignard JF, Nargeot J, Lory P.
