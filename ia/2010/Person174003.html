Roger London’s Summary

Mr. London is the Chairman of the nation’s largest security competition, the non-profit American Security Challenge, a platform that encourages new relationships between emerging security technology companies’ with public and private entities to utilize new technology for our nation’s defense. The Challenge has grown exponentially in just two short years with over $10M in teaming agreements and investor term sheets in 2009. The Challenge is the most innovative mechanism in the country that seeks to secure the nation by matching the most cutting edge entrepreneurial technologies with both defense and homeland security applications/customers.

Mr. London is also the Innovation Program Director for the Chesapeake Crescent Initiative, a task force established by/for Maryland Governor O’Malley, Virginia Governor Kaine and Mayor Fenty of Washington DC. Leveraging his experience as venture capitalist, successful entrepreneur, merchant banker, security incubator manager, defense and intelligence community technology scout, his role is to design a regional strategic plan to commercialize more technology in the Chesapeake Crescent region.

Roger London’s Specialties:

venture capital, technology scouting, innovation commercialization, security technology, early stage companies, incubation, mergers and acquisitions

Roger London’s Experience

  • Chairman

    American Security Challenge

    (Defense & Space industry)

    September 2007 — Present (2 years 10 months)

    The American Security Challenge, America's first emerging security technology competition devoted to our nation's long-term security, is a platform that enables emerging companies to obtain precious investment capital and customer contract revenue, helping to ensure the latest technology finds its way to the market to secure our nation.

    In conjunction with the 2009 Homeland Security S&T Stakeholders Conference East, presented by the National Defense Industrial Association (NDIA) with subject matter support provided by the Science & Technology Directorate (S&T) of the U.S. Department of Homeland Security (DHS), the American Security Challenge hosted its second annual event May 21, 2009 at the Ronald Reagan Building and International Trade Center in Washington, D.C.

    The 2009 Challenge obtained $2.5M of investment capital for two growing small technology companies as well as identified four companies to team with a large defense contractor. Its Investor Partner Chart Ventures provided the investment capital, and the Industry Partner Alion Science and Technology Corporation provided the teaming agreements, which led to contracts and revenue.

    2008 Patriot Award winner Governor Tom Ridge, former Secretary Of Department of Homeland Security was on hand to introduce and award the 2009 Patriot Award to Admiral James Loy. See short video at http://www.momuxd.com/video2.php

  • Innovation Director

    Chesapeake Crescent Initiative

    (Non-Profit; Civic & Social Organization industry)

    August 2007 — Present (2 years 11 months)

    Mr. London is currently the Innovation Program Director for the Chesapeake Crescent Initiative, a task force established by/for Maryland Governor O’Malley, Virginia Governor Kaine and Mayor Fenty of Washington DC. His role is to design a regional strategic plan to commercialize more technology in the Chesapeake Crescent region (MD, DC, VA) to ensure the region is a global leader in the energy, national security and life science sectors. This goal is realized by enabling or improving partnerships between technologists, investors, entrepreneurs and customer groups.

    Current activities include:

    1. The creation and support of the nation’s first multi-state university consortium the Chesapeake Crescent Innovation Alliance (CCIA) composed of Johns Hopkins University, University System of Maryland, Georgetown University, George Washington University, George Mason University, University of Virginia, James Madison University and Virginia Tech whose charter is to collaborate in new ways to generate more research funding, increase technology transfer and spin out more viable technology companies.

    2. The establishment and leading of an advisory commercialization working group for the Department of Homeland Security (DHS) and the District of Columbia regarding the new DHS headquarters at the St. Elizabeths site. DHS is consolidating over 60 offices onto a single campus. This $3B+ site is the largest GSA construction project in history (larger than the Pentagon). The commercialization working group is recommending programs and facilities to be incorporated into the new design that will help DHS better identify and procure cutting edge technologies from stable emerging companies.

    3. The design of a holistic regional commercialization plan to leverage over $100B of local federal research to support startups + young companies (net job creation key) by leveraging the huge buying capabilities of the world’s largest consumer of technology, the US government.

  • Director of Technology Scouting

    Chesapeake Innovation Center

    (Defense & Space industry)

    September 2004 — November 2007 (3 years 3 months)

    Mr. London was the Chesapeake Innovation Center’s (CIC) Director of Technology Scouting. Mr. London successfully led the development of the TechBridge Program for the NSA, productized it for sale to other members of the security sector, and perfected the unique pull strategy of commercialization used successfully by the CIC. Rather than push innovative technology into the market hoping/guessing customers will adopt it, Mr. London’s pull strategy gathers criteria from the customers and scouts for matching technology. As these requested innovations have a higher likelihood of finding their way successfully into the marketplace, resources invested in the commercialization process have a significantly higher return.

    Mr. London’s scouting and screening activities provided the CIC with viable technology companies to match criteria of its Partners (The Boeing Company, Northrop Grumman, ARINC, NSA, Athlone Global Security and Next Century). While the scouting could identify companies/technologies for sub-contracting, acquisistion or internal deployment, most scouting was for the purpose of identifying technology to procure into a larger system. Eleven technologies/companies were contracted with as a result of these scouting efforts. Because of this success, a DOD customer asked for Mr. London by name to lead specific project technology scouting efforts that address particular critical and/or highly visible problems.

    Under his watch and assistance, CIC Member companies generated customer contracts/awards over $300M, raised over $100M in venture capital and corporate finance and enjoyed public offerings and mergers of approximately $100M. Customers of CIC Members are the some of the largest companies and government agencies in the world including DHS, DOD, NSA, NIH, UK Ministry of Defence, BAE, Boeing, GlaxoSmithKline, Heineken, Northrop Grumman, Ford Motor Corp, and others.

  • Sourcing Principal

    Nokia Innovent Ventures

    (Telecommunications industry)

    September 2003 — September 2004 (1 year 1 month)

    Innovent, an early stage corporate venture arm of the Finnish communication giant Nokia, expanding from its US headquarters in California, desired an East Coast presence, and recruited Mr. London to lead/open its East Coast operations as Sourcing Principal. Mr. London’s responsibility was to build a strategic presence in the Mid-Atlantic, recruit technical analysts and build a pipeline of early stage companies with technology applicable to Nokia products. Innovent was a very early stage investor of only $200,000-500,000, but this investment “bought” access and insight into technologies developed outside of Nokia’s global research centers and assisted new product direction. Technologies of most interest were wireless security, location based technology, battery technology, mesh networking, and other edge of network innovations.

    In conjunction with the NSA, Innovent was the co-founding sponsor of the country’s first security incubator, the Chesapeake Innovation Center in Annapolis Maryland. In 2004, Nokia opened a large new office in White Plains and determined to relocate Innovent East from Annapolis to New York.

  • President

    TrustOne Capital Services

    (Investment Banking industry)

    1991 — 2003 (12 years )

    Mr. London founded TrustOne Capital in 1990 to acquire assets from the Resolution Trust Corporation (RTC), the organization formed by Congress to bail out the failed Savings and Loan banks of the late 1980’s (TARP's predecessor). TrustOne bought many loan and asset portfolios to repackage and sell to the commercial sector. Upon RTC closure in 1993, TrustOne continued to buy assets on the secondary market as well as act as financial advisor to clients seeking disposition of their portfolios. Mr. London shifted TrustOne’s focus from the banking and real estate to the technology sector in 1995 to help small technology company founders develop their business models, raise capital and sell their businesses. TrustOne acted as principal advisor to multiple corporate finance, equity investments, mergers and acquisitions.

    Following 9/11, Mr. London felt compelled to focus on homeland security, intelligence and defense applications and initiated a new program called ACTION (Assisting Corporate Technology in Our Nation) which provided security oriented technology companies with significant fee discounts for financial and strategic business services.
