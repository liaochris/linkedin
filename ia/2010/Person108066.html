Julia Margaret Murphy’s Summary

Recipient of the MS Leadership Scholar Award as an honoree in the National Multiple Sclerosis Society Leadership Class of 2009, Julia subscribes to this quote attributed to Ms. Alana Shepherd, founder, Shepherd Center: “A person who aspires to great leadership also champions community and social responsiveness." (www.nationalmssociety.org)

Raised in Atlanta, Julia has served as an executive assistant, project manager, office manager, program administrator and state governing board professional. Her career has included time in the industries of insurance, finance, governmental affairs, intermodal transportation, live and film event production, radio broadcasting, volunteer management, higher education administration and homelessness and domestic violence advocacy. Recognizing that her heart’s desire is to continue bridge building in a meaningful vocation, she returned to college as an adult, earned a BA in Religious Studies from the College of Mount Saint Joseph and is currently pursuing a Masters in Theological Studies at Spring Hill College in Mobile.

Hobbies include community service volunteerism, reading, travel, study, skydiving, skiing, cycling and exploring the mind-body-spirit connection in wellness and wellbeing.

The sky is not the limit.
