Marissa Levin’s Summary

As Founder/CEO of Information Experts, Marissa leads the effort to define, shape, and evangelize IE's values, mission, vision, growth strategy, brand, & culture. IE helps companies & agencies fulfill their missions through compellng communications & educational programs.
Marissa has led the organization’s entry into the federal market to achieve a presence in 17 agencies. Information Experts was named as a USAID small business success story, has won more than 70 creativity & leadership awards, & was named to the Inc. 5000 List of America’s Fastest Growing Companies for 2009 & 2010.
Ms. Levin is known throughout the national business community as a leader & visionary. She is the national Women’s Entrepreneurship columnist for the DC Examiner, & a regular columnist for The Washington Post.
Ms. Levin was named as a 2009 & 2010 SmartCEO Smart 100 participant by SmartCEO Magazine, which recognizes the top 100 CEOs in the region, & was named as a 2008 BRAVO Award winner, which honors the region’s 25 most influential women CEOs. She was also named as one of Washington’s Top 100 Technology Titans for 2009 by Washingtonian Magazine. She was a finalist for the 2002 Ernst & Young Entrepreneur of the Year Award & for the 2004 Women in Technology Entrepreneur Leadership Award.
Marissa was selected as one of greater Washington’s “Women Who Mean Business” by the Washington Business Journal, which honors the region’s “most influential and powerful” women executives, and was nominated for the Women President’s Organization (WPEO) Outstanding Women's Business Advocate Award.
Marissa’s heartfelt passion is mentoring other business owners, and providing a path of insight & inspiration for those that are also committed to fulfilling their entrepreneurial dreams. She is a highly sought-after speaker as an expert in what it takes to successfully build a multi-million dollar government contracting business, and how to build a thriving business while living a fulfilling life.
