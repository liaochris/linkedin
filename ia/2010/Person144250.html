Michelle Frey (Schutters)’s Summary

New to CCA and hoping to make a difference in the actual academic design experience as it applies the work world for young designers in partnership with faculty.

Notable contributions as Design Director, UC Berkeley: "The Campaign for Berkeley" suite of materials across media used by campus units (http://identity.berkeley.edu/guidelines/), "Thanks to Berkeley..." photography book; "The Builders of Berkeley" identity program, granite/bronze wall in front of Doe Library, Book, and gift award; Reestablishment of UC Berkeley Design Alliance, meetings and blog; completion of the recreation of UC seal and implementation of its use and along with Public Affairs assisted in the digital creation of the University's historic font by Frederick Goudy, digitized by Richard Beatty; New structure, layout and art direction of the "Promise of Berkeley" magazine, and annual philanthropy reports; CASE Gold and Silver awards for the above projects.

Motivated to keep the design process intact even in daunting in-house environments. Inspired by new design directions, implementation strategies/templates to comply or co-brand, as well as project tracking systems to streamline process. Strong ability to translate executive verbal wishes to visual ideas.

Michelle Frey (Schutters)’s Experience

  • Design Manager

    California College of the Arts

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    December 2009 — Present (5 months)

    Michelle now manages Sputnik, CCA’s in-house undergraduate design studio. It is a wonderful opportunity for Graphic Design students to extend their training beyond the traditional classroom environment and deal with real projects, real deadlines, and real limitations (of time, money, and resources).

    Sputnik produces 80 to 100 print and multimedia projects for the college each year—everything from CCA’s 48-page magazine, Glance, to websites, multimedia presentations, video, and holiday cards. Under the direction of staff and faculty advisors, the students design each piece from concept to production, meeting with clients, presenting ideas, and press checking. The experience is a terrific example of the college’s motto: theory and practice.

    Every semester, the Graphic Design faculty nominates third- and fourth-year students to interview for Sputnik. Eight to 12 are selected. Sputnik may be taken as an upper-level elective or for internship credit.
