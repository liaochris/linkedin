Burnet D. Brown’s Experience

  • Principal

    RNV Ventures

    (Renewables & Environment industry)

    1996 — Present (14 years )

    Specialist in research, analysis, strategy development, program management, marketing and accelerating commercialization and market penetration of Green tech, cleantech, green building and sustainable technologies and best practices. Private sector and public policy expertise is energy with emphasis on renewable energy and technology transfer. Experience working with Fortune 500 companies developing and directing marketing strategies from conception through implementation. Broad experience writing proposals, conducting research and analysis, working with new technologies and developing programs to accelerate market penetration.   • Financial Analyst, Policy Analyst

    California Energy Commission

    (Government Agency; Public Policy industry)

    September 1981 — October 1984 (3 years 2 months)

    Worked in the Office of Policy & Program Evaluation, Sacramento, CA. Involved in technology evaluation and the development of recommendations to CEC policymakers regarding funding of RD&D projects for renewable energy, energy efficiency and fossil fuel technologies. Authored a report entitled "Assessment of capitalization and expensing accounting treatment for utility Residential Conservation Service (RCS) expenditures (Southern California Edison)." Analysis in this report showed (1) expensing of RCS for tax and book purposes would be inequitable because there would be no matching of RCS costs with benefits, (2) expensing for tax and capitalization for book purposes yielded the lowest cost to ratepayers on a present value basis. Testimony presented to the California Energy Commission.
