Catherine Schenck-Yglesias’s Summary

Ms. Schenck-Yglesias is a health informatics expert and has contributed her technical expertise for 15 years to the global health field; her current interest is in the design, implementation and evaluation of clinical and public health information and communications systems using appropriate technology.

She has served as a senior strategic information/informatics advisor to multilateral, federal and non-governmental organization health programs supported by US foreign assistance funding -- in sub-Saharan Africa, Latin America & the Caribbean, and Asia, including two post-conflict countries: the Islamic Republic of Afghanistan and Timor Leste. English is her native language, Spanish her second language, and she has studied basic Hindi-Urdu.

While at USAID, Ms. Schenck-Yglesias served on the Afghanistan Health Team and as Senior Health Informatics Advisor to the President’s Emergency Plan for AIDS Relief (PEPFAR). She co-chaired an interagency technical working group on health management information systems, advising the International Health Facility Assessment Network (IHFAN), the Routine Health Information Network (RHINO) and the PEPFAR Geographic Information Systems (GIS) Working Group. She represented USAID to the Partnership for Quality Medical Donations (PQMD) and advised the Office of the Global AIDS Coordinator (OGAC) on health informatics public-private partnerships. She and her fellow Strategic Information Advisor colleagues earned the USAID Global Health Bureau Teamwork Award.

Ms. Schenck-Yglesias designed and co-instructed “Health Informatics Applications of Geographic Information Systems (GIS): From Data Visualization to Spatial Analysis” at a triennial World Congress on Medical and Health Informatics (Medinfo) and has given presentations at several conferences and courses.

Catherine Schenck-Yglesias’s Specialties:
