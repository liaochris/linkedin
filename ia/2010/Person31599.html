Cameron Norris’s Summary

Have worked in sports media and management since 2002 at age 15 as a sideline reporter for high school football state championships. Worked in high school sports from 2002-08 as a broadcaster with the FHSAA as play-by-play, color, and sideline broadcaster, as well as producer role and later network coordinator for the FHSAA Radio Network.

Began with ESPN 1410 (WHBT-AM, Tallahassee) in 2006 as a show producer and fill-in host for the station's flagship sports talk call-in show. Created and hosted a weekly high school sports talk show during 2006-07 academic year, responsible for all show imaging, content, and advertising - sold over $10,000 in advertising for show.

Currently working as a scoreboard/dashboard operator for ESPN 3D and associate producer for ESPN Regional Television - started in sports television in 2007 at age 20, and have worked for nearly a dozen different broadcast outlets in a freelance role.

My goal is to eventually be a live sports television broadcaster, producer, or director. Collectively logged around 3,000 hours of on-air time and sold over $15,000 in advertising inventory.
