Francois Painchaud’s Summary

As Partner in charge of the business law group of Robic, LLP ("Robic"), I specialise in the preparation and negotiation of licensing agreements, technology transfers and in the fields of commercial and corporate laws such as joint venture and merges and acquisitions.

I was President, until October 2009, of the Licensing Executives Society (U.S.A. & Canada), Inc. (“LES US/C”) of which I have been a trustee since 2001. I am Director and Corporate Secretary of the Canadian Foundation Louis Pasteur. I am a member of the Canadian Bar Association (“CBA”) where I served on the Executive Committee - Business Law Section (Quebec Division) and am a former President of the Executive Committee - Telecommunication, Information and Intellectual Property Law Section (Quebec Division). I also served, until march 2008, on the Board of Directors of Axcan Pharma Inc. and presently sreve on various technology driven private companies.

At the academic level, I was a seasonal lecturer at McGill University in charge of the course entitled "Complex Legal Transactions - Trade Secrets Law and Technology Transfers". I am also a regular lecturer in the areas of technology transfers and intellectual property in commercial transactions to professional and business associations. He also regularly publishes articles in his field of specialisation.

    (Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    CLP, Inc. administers the Certified Licensing Professional (CLP) program for professionals in the fields of licensing, business development and commercialization of intellectual property. More than 700 individuals involved in patenting, marketing, valuation, IP law, negotiation, and intellectual asset management have earned the CLP, Inc. certification. The mission of CLP is to elevate the licensing profession through knowledge and standards.

  • Member of the Board of Directors & Secretary

    CANADIAN LOUIS PASTEUR FOUNDATION

    (Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    October 2007 — Present (2 years 6 months)

    The Canadian Louis Pasteur Foundation focuses on research on infectious diseases that impact human organs and on other scientific applications, such as drug testing, coronary disease detection, etc. To this end, the Canadian Louis Pasteur Foundation provides scholarships to Canadian students interested in continuing post-doctoral studies at the Pasteur Institute in Paris as well as to foreign students wishing to continue their post-doctoral studies in Canadian universities or laboratories.

    Although the Canadian Louis Pasteur Foundation is headquartered in Montreal, its activities are Canada-wide; a second office is planned for Toronto in the near future.

  • Member of the Board of Trustees and immediate Past-President

    LES USA & CANADA

    (Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    1994 — Present (16 years )

    Established in 1965, the Licensing Executives Society (U.S.A. and Canada), Inc. (LES USA Canada) is a professional society comprised of about 6,000 members engaged in the transfer, use, development, manufacture and marketing of intellectual property.

    The LES USA Canada membership includes a wide range of professionals, including business executives, lawyers, licensing consultants, engineers, academicians, scientists and government officials. Many large corporations, professional firms, and universities comprise the Society's membership.

    LES USA Canada is a member society of the Licensing Executives Society International, Inc. (LESI), with a worldwide membership of over 12,000 members in 30 national societies, representing over 80 countries.
