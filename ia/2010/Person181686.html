Sandip Nallani Chakravartula’s Experience

  • Analog IC Designer

    Intel

    (Public Company; INTC; Semiconductors industry)

    December 2008 — Present (2 years )

    I am currently working as an Analog Design Engineer and am responsible for ramping up and designing basic analog building blocks in Power Regulation & Clocking domain. Here I have worked on designing the following
    •Digital to Analog Converter: The DAC was a crucial element in providing a stable reference supply for power regulation. The DAC output voltage ranged from 0.6V to 1.0V with an LSB step size of +/-1.8mV and consumed less than 50uW power across process, voltage and temperature skews.
    •Ring Oscillator: This seven stage ring oscillator circuit provided all the necessary ranging from 1GHz to 1.5MHz required by the various blocks involved in power regulation and consumed less than 100uW of power across all process, voltage and temperature skews.
    •DLL: I characterized and improved the performance of a DLL used in the clocking circuitary. It operated at 2.5GHz and 4GHz with DNL less than +/-0.5LSB, Duty Cycle error less than 1% , and power consumption of less than 20uW across all process voltage and temperature skews.
    •My role also involves working closely with Mask Designers to ensure that the post-layout simulations for all of the above closely matched the pre-layout circuit simulations and to ensure that the analog layout guidelines were being closely followed.

  • Product Development and Test Engineer

    Texas Instruments

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; TXN; Semiconductors industry)

    April 2008 — December 2008 (9 months)

    My responsibilities included designing final test solutions for high precision Digital to Analog Data Converters. I was trained to design hardware interface boards and writing test routines for the ETS-364 Testers (Eagle Test Systems).

My role also involved sustaining the yield of data converter products already out in production, to accommodate for process variations.
