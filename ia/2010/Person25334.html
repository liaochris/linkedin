Bob Clark’s Summary

Accomplished and respected results-producing photographer. Creates images that inspire, motivate and call for emotional engagement with the viewer. Versatile professional who captures the needs of the client while using innovation to meld artistic expression with the client branding and story. Outstanding collaboration and communication and computer skills.

I bought my first 35mm camera, a Minolta, in the early 70s. By 1977, I was ready to upgrade to a Nikon and purchased a Nikon FE. I continued to add lenses and equipment, eventually upgrading to an 8008s when autofocus became dominant. My daughter is now using an FE in her design work in College. I discovered the darkroom in about 1980 and spent many hours working with my black and white negatives and print. I eventually started developing E-6 slide film and still have 1000s of slides that I developed and mounted.

Now we have a whole new era. I went digital when the Nikon D70 came out, and have upgraded my way with a D90 and D700. It amazes me that now I have a $2,500 camera and $2,000 lens, when back in the days of the FE I had a $200 camera and a $200 lens. My how times changed!
