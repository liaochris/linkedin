Ken Adelson’s Summary

Ken Adelson is an Emmy award-winning sports media industry leader and innovator with over 28 years of broadcasting and digital media expertise in media management, launching broadcast/multimedia networks, production, programming, operations, anchoring and reporting.

As Senior Vice President of Production, Operations, and Planning for NBA Entertainment Adelson launched NBA TV in 1999, the first cable/satellite network created by a sports league, was instrumental in the NBA's push into digital content creation and distribution, pioneered one of the nation's first all digital newsrooms, led the development of one of the most advanced digital media management systems in the world, was influential in the NBA's initial development of 3D Broadcasting and re-engineered NBAE's business operations.

Before joining NBAE, he worked for 10 years as a sports anchor, reporter, producer and writer at network television affiliates in Phoenix, AZ, Hartford, CT, Salt Lake City, UT and Wilmington, NC.

In 2008, Adelson launched Thunder Broadcasting in less than two months, an unprecedented undertaking. As Senior Vice President and Executive Producer, he managed the daily operations for the NBA's newest franchise overseeing high definition television and radio game broadcasts, Spanish language radio broadcasts and the organizations digital production facilities. He is adept at integrating viewer interactive elements across platforms and taking advantage of emerging social media applications.

A highly regarded executive with ability to evaluate operational needs and implement strategies that improve productivity, enhance visibility and brand awareness, ensure quality, and increase cost effectiveness.
