Dave Woodall’s Summary

Nobody needs another t-shirt.

Yet we freely accept them anytime they're offered. Our drawers are stuffed full of tees. Maybe it's because they remind us of past memories we don't want to discard.

Being in the t-shirt biz, my objective is to get someone to think of your t-shirt next time they go to the closet. Only 5 or so have that position in their minds. Let's make yours be one of them.

    AMB3R {custom tees & hoodies}

    (Privately Held; Apparel & Fashion industry)

    April 2008 — Present (2 years 4 months)

    AMB3R (pronounced “Amber”) is a custom fashion t-shirt company.

    AMB3R's view is that our customers want t-shirts in order to raise money and/or awareness about their cause, event, company, etc.

    But the person who actually wears the shirt doesn't care about those goals. People who wear shirts want one that feels, fits, and looks great!

    Our service is making sure both parties win.

  • Product Developer

    Futureshirts.com

    (Apparel & Fashion industry)

    December 2004 — August 2008 (3 years 9 months)

    While building my first company as a trendy brand, I was often approached by bands asking for help with their tour merchandise. I developed a small side service to provide design and production assistance for those clients. That service began to take market share from Futureshirts so they offered me a sales position.

    At Futureshirts I was responsible for acquiring new business and managing the design and production of projects in progress. I started several marketing initiatives including a catalog division and trade-show presence. My largest single sale was $186,000 for 32,000 tees. (The average company order was 144 tees.)

  • Founder

    Church Clothes

    (Apparel & Fashion industry)

    October 2000 — December 2004 (4 years 3 months)

    Throughout my undergrad I was required to wear a tie to class so the last thing I wanted to do on Sundays was dress up. I printed some tees shirts that simply stated “Church Clothes” in a tongue and cheek vibe. In total, I sold roughly 15,000 tees through 100 retailers, two mall kiosks, and summer festivals.

    To market the brand I produced a seven city music tour to play concerts near the retail stores that carried the line. The tour consisted of identifying three rock bands, negotiating with their management, enlisting a booking agency, coordinating with local promoters, and supplying promotional materials.

    Other projects include producing and distributing 10,000 promotional DVDs at youth events; Marketing, booth design, and logistical details for trade-shows; Designing the line twice a year; Production management once orders were placed.
