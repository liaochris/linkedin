Tom Patterson’s Summary

I'm a long time security guy, with hardware, software, product, service, enterprise, R&D, global, Federal, and CSO experience. I've also got an entrepreneurial streak that has helped me start Carlyle-venture backed company, and buy/sell 7 more. I've served on two public boards, and provided governance to several early stage companies, and written a well-published book on global security. I'm currently a chief security officer (CSO) in the financial sector, leading the effort to wipe out counterfeit payment card fraud worldwide.

For the technology part of my background, I was a key member of the MCC teams that developed the first secure web browsers and servers, first directory and search engine, and first commercial certificate authority, and now leverage identity management and authentication in the payments world. I've also built industry-leading hardware and software in the infosec space while at Centel and Tradewave.

For the security part, I've helped secure projects as diverse as a space shuttle launch (STS-37) and the largest United Way telethon in history. I've built products (HW & SW), led one of the largest Infosec consultancies in the world (Deloitte- EMEA), and currently advise Federal law enforcement and the intelligence community.

For the vision part, I was IBM's chief strategist for eCommerce, and conducted regular briefings with the White House on the NII and Information Superhighway.

I saw eCommerce coming back in 93, and helped get IBM into it in a big way. I saw InfoSec coming in 1999, and led a public company's CA business, wrote a pretty good book about global security (www.MappingSecurity.com ), and led Deloitte's infosec practice in Europe, middle east, and Africa. I saw next generation internet coming in 2005, and founded and ran the largest IPv6 company ( www.CommandInformation.com )in America, with lead investment from the Carlyle Group. Now is the time to put all of that together!
