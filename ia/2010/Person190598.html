  • Co-Instructor for Character Animation

    University of Massachusetts Amherst

    (Educational Institution; 1001-5000 employees; Higher Education industry)

    September 2008 — May 2009 (9 months)

    As a senior at UMass Amherst, I was offered to instruct the character animation class in the computer science department. My main responsibility was to teach students the fundamentals of 3d Character animation.

    To accomplish this goal the class was broken up into different parts.
    Lecture: This is where we would go over the classic rules of animation and watch examples of what we were studying that week.
    Demonstration: This I where I would animate something in front of class for students to see how to apply what we had discussed in class to their own animations. This time was also used to help instruct student how to use Maya software as an animation tool.
    Assignments: Each assignment would be an animation where the student had to demonstrate they could effectively apply the topic we discussed in lectures to their own animation.
    In Class Critique: This time was used to watch the student's animations and discuss what was working in each of them. I would then give them points on what they could improve and a chance to resubmit the animation if they choose.
