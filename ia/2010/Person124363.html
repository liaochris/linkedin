Lester A. Myers, Ph.D., J.D., CPA, CFF’s Summary

Les Myers provides advisory services in organizational integrity and responsibility to directors and officers of corporations, exempt organizations, and other entities. He helps them assess risks of fraud and misconduct and assists them in evaluating, designing, and implementing ethics and compliance programs and other anti-fraud programs and controls to prevent, detect, and respond to such risks within the context of leading frameworks for organizational governance and internal control. His services in corporate citizenship and responsibility include stakeholder and public affairs matters involving environmental sustainability, labor standards, consumer protection, and philanthropic engagement. He has 28 years of experience in public accounting, SEC reporting, university management, university teaching and research, and management advisory services, particularly for the financial services, environmental, exempt organization, and defense sectors.

Les designed and earned the first Ph.D. in business ethics at Georgetown University. While earning his J.D. at Georgetown, he served as associate editor for and contributor to the American Criminal Law Review. He is a fellow of the Caux Round Table, serves on the advisory board for Georgetown's Corporate Counsel Institute, and has served on the committee for the Mid-Atlantic Anti-Money Laundering Conference. He also is a faculty member for Board Advisory Services for the National Association of Corporate Directors and a member of the CSR working group of the Business Civic Leadership Center at the United States Chamber of Commerce.

Les teaches business ethics, law, and Catholic social thought at Georgetown University and previously taught at the Jesuit University of San Francisco. He has published in scholarly and popular venues and he speaks and advises for scholarly and professional societies and organizations. His recent work includes multiple entries in the Encyclopedia of Business Ethics and Society.
