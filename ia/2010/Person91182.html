John Tucker’s Summary

John received his PhD in chemistry in the research group of Nobel Laureate Donald Cram in 1987 and his MBA from the Anderson School of Management at UCLA in 2009. He currently works as a consultant in biomedical technology assessment.

His research career spans 16 years performing drug discovery research in metabolic diseases, cancer, HIV infection, antibacterials, and Alzheimer's disease. John led the medicinal chemistry effort leading to the discovery of the CBLC102 series of anticancer compounds, currently the basis of an $18M joint development venture between Cleveland BioLabs and Bioprocess Capital Ventures. He has authored 20 peer-reviewed scientific articles, and is listed as an inventor on 19 granted and 12 pending U.S. patents.
