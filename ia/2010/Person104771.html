Jordan Furlong’s Summary

I'm a partner with Edge International, providing consulting services to law firms on strategic planning and tactical matters. I'm also a Senior Consultant with Stem Legal and principal of its Media Strategy consulting service. Finally, I'm an award-winning blogger who chronicles the extraordinary changes underway in the practice of law at "Law21: Dispatches from a Legal Profession on the Brink."

After graduating from Queen’s University Faculty of Law in 1993, I articled with Blake, Cassels & Graydon LLP in 1994 before beginning a career in legal journalism. I spent more than a dozen years leading three top Canadian legal periodicals: I served as Editor-in-Chief of the Canadian Bar Association’s flagship National magazine for a full decade (1999-2009), during which time it earned nine awards for journalistic excellence. I also founded and served as Executive Editor of the Canadian Corporate Counsel Association’s CCCA Magazine (2007-2009), and I held the position of Managing Editor with The Lawyers Weekly newspaper (1997-1998).

I'm an Honourary Fellow of the College of Law Practice Management, and I serve as Chair of the College's InnovAction Awards, which recognize and reward creativity and innovation in legal services delivery. I've spoken about the challenges facing the legal profession at numerous conferences, including for the Law Society of Upper Canada and the National Association of Law Placement. My written work has been published in a wide variety of periodicals aimed at both lawyers and clients. I live in Ottawa, Canada, with my wife and two children.
