Robert Wheatley’s Summary

My role is to help clients dig deeper into their business challenges and growth opportunities. We have great appreciation not only for the power of brands and the emotional connections that can be created underneath them, but also for the seismic changes that have taken place in how consumers relate to and engage in relationships with the brands that matter to them.

In some respects I'm an agent for building brand relevancy, engagement and trust. I have spent the last 30 years in the agency business, working for some of the most recognized brands, big and small, in refining their go-to-market strategies and helping them grow their business.

I am reminded of David Ogilvy's famous remark about the agency he built: "We sell. Or else." This mantra has followed me throughout my career. And it is at the forefront of what we do in our firm. There is a formula now that sits at the edge of success in brand building: we call it the higher purpose or strategic mission. You can read about it in more detail here: http://www.wheatleytimmons.com/pages/error_page/58.php

Brands today are built on a foundation of lifestyle connection. Thus the difference from being a commodity bought on price, vs one that is sought after and enjoyed by its user -- this has much to do with the ability of businesses to align themselves and help enable the passions, interests and desires of those they wish to sell to.

Call it "permission" based brand relationships grounded in relevancy. Purchase decisions spring from emotional judgments. We know the keys to unlocking those connections.

This means consumer insight research is vital to designing communications and public relations strategies that work.

Clients get more from us than great creative and ideas. They also find a resource in helping them map and plan their brand strategy, new product and channel decisions, too. In short, we help sell. Or else.

Robert Wheatley’s Specialties:

Brand strategy development, public relations, social and digital media, branded events and experiences and crisis counsel for consumer branded businesses.

  • President and Principal

    Wheatley Blair, Inc.

    (Public Relations and Communications industry)

    1990 — 2000 (10 years )

    Focused on food, beverage, household and health/beauty aids brands. We started the business in 1990 with Sara Lee as our founding client at a time when the venerable brand was in need of triage. Our revival campaign centered around the "First International Symposia on Dessert" staged in Vienna, Austria (dessert capitol of the world) in front of the top food journalists from the US and Canada. It was a spectacular success. In combination with other programs focused on high brand development indexed markets, the business results turned around in 9 months time.

    We also represented First Alert home safety products. And in collaboration with then client, Rich Timmons (yes, the same person), we launched the first residential carbon monoxide alarm product category via the "Silent Killer" public education campaign. A huge success by any definition: $100 million in sales within 15 months of launch on the back of a comprehensive PR campaign.

    We also represented Labatt USA's portfolio of beers, Hoover vacuum products, Bosch appliances, Progresso soups, H20 skin care products, Kaukauna Cheese, projects for Sears, Starbucks and others.

    My role centered in strategic planning and creative campaign development. It was my first foray into owning an agency. I learned a lot --- starting from scratch with no safety net except wits and determination, we built a successful agency by doing great work that consistently delivered sales and market share growth.

    Cole & Weber, headquartered in Seattle, is a wholly-owned but autonomous subsidiary of Ogilvy & Mather. An unusual company in its day as a combination of three distinct businesses: an advertising agency, dominant in the Pacific Northwest by a very wide margin; a PR firm that grew to become the largest in the region; and a market research operation.

    I ran the PR company. I built an office network that included businesses in Seattle, Portland and San Francisco. I had over-sight of PR operations also in Calgary and Edmonton, Canada. My vision was a West Coast powerhouse. This plan eventually led to a merger with the parent company's PR division and creation of O&M/West.

    Our largest client was Westin Hotels & Resorts, based in Seattle. I was immersed in that business on a number of levels. As well as Weyerhaeuser Company, Washington Apple Commission, Chateau Ste. Michelle wines, GNA Financial products, Roman Meal bread, Nalley's Foods and others.

    I was an early believer in integrated communications plans and worked hard to bring PR thinking to the strategic table at the CMO level with client organizations. I had ideas that would cross platforms and routinely found myself in planning meetings that bridged various disciplines of communication.
