Chris Martin’s Summary

(May 2010 - completed an 18 month assignment) With 13 years of Wall Street and Big 4 Management Consulting experience, I reside in Manhattan as a Contract Consultant for Bank of America Merrill Lynch. I grew up in Reading, PA and graduated from Penn State in 1997 with an Industrial Engineering degree. My travels took me to San Francisco’s dot-com boom as an ETL developer at startup WeddingChannel.com. Working in the fast-paced Systems Integration Team environment, I obtained a technical foundation for database integration, programming languages, and website functionality.

After discussions with friends at Deloitte, I moved to Chicago to apply my experience to consulting. From 2000-2005 I worked on client engagements across the US in a range of industries and businesses, both technically and strategically. Following years of travel, in 2005 I accepted an exciting offer with Citi’s Investment Bank in NYC. My role allowed me to further expand my business analysis and project management experience within Citi’s global BASEL II implementation.

In May 2007 I was hired by Goldman Sachs as a PM responsible for managing multiple initiatives for new systems and process improvements. As a liaison between Senior Management, Technology, and User Community stakeholders, my responsibilities involved requirements gathering, status communication, tactical and strategic decision-making, issue and risk identification, and budget tracking.

In December 2008, I decided to pursue a long-term career strategy of applying my skills to specialized contract consulting. Accepting an offer from Bank of America, I’m currently a Lead Business Analyst in the Global Risk Investment and Treasury Technology Division focusing on the bank’s integration for BASEL II.

Future career interests include furthering my financial consulting, or expanding into healthcare or media. As a passionate sports fan, my dream-job is to work for the likes of ESPN or NBC Sports utilizing my career skillset.
