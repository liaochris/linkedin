Kristian Merenda’s Summary

Kristian Darigan Merenda is a global corporate citizenship, cause branding, marketing strategy and fundraising specialist. She advises Fortune 500 companies and leading NGOs in their efforts to develop, manage and communicate programs that deliver mutually beneficial solutions for their business and society.

Merenda is a futurist for social marketing trends. She has pioneered approaches to creating global corporate citizenship platforms, nonprofit signature programs and the next-generation cause branding. In her career, she led the creation of many critically acclaimed programs for organizations including Alticor, American Heart Association, American Red Cross, Dell, Gillette, Make-A-Wish Foundation, Microsoft, PNC Bank, Sprint PCS, Thrivent, Wal-Mart, Washington Mutual, Wells Fargo and Western Union.

For nearly a decade in partnership with Carol Cone, she played a critical role in developing the cause branding practice at Cone, Inc, which was then widely recognized as the leading cause branding practice in the USA. Prior to her tenure at Cone, Merenda worked in the Public Affairs Group at GTECH Corporation, the world’s leading supplier of lottery and gaming solutions; at Firebrand LLC, a marketing strategy-consulting firm servicing Fortune 1000 companies; and the YMCA.

She recently traveled to China to research CSR and published her findings in the Journal of Corporate Citizenship. She is currently completing a book Breakthrough Nonprofit Branding: The Seven Principles To Power Extraordinary Growth with Jocelyn Daw, Carol Cone and Anne Erhard, which is due to be published in late 2010.

She received a Bachelor of Science degree in Marketing, with a specialty in computer science, from Providence College, where she graduated Magna Cum Laude and was named to the honorary Tau Pi Phi Business Society. She completed her MBA at Boston University, where she graduated with Honors. She resides in Canton, MA with her husband, Justin and son, Liam.

Kristian Merenda’s Specialties:

Global corporate citizenship and cause branding, thought leadership and issues management, fundraising and strategic philanthropy, new media and social engagement, as well as sponsorship and integrated marketing communications.
