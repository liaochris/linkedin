Sean Perkins’s Summary

I joined the United States Marine Corps September 10th 1996 and was screened into the Yankee White Presidential Support program. I was granted a Top Secret/SCI clearance and I learned about leadership, service management, security, and project work flow. I also deployed to the Mediterranean Sea for 6 months.

Honorably discharged from the Marines October 10th 2000 I started going to college while working. My first job post military was at CompUSA which required me to perform rolling inventory audits broken up by department. I also worked as a computer technician and in sales.

At Kroger I ran the seafood department and ordering seafood inventory on a daily basis. I participated in the regional sales meetings and ensured that my store complied with regional department standards. I learned a lot about customer service working in this position due to the complexity of customer issues.

At Yamaha Motor Corporation, USA I was a Regional Technical Adviser (RTA). The primary focus of this position was to act as a help line for the 2000+ marine dealers in the US. Averaging 25 calls a day we used proprietary CRM software to log our activities and customer information. This position focused on analytical skills and customer service.

As an Assistant Accessories Coordinator for watercraft/marine products I was exposed to the Yamalube care product line. This line had been neglected for several years and I quickly advised my manager that this line needed some one running the show.

As the Yamalube Product Line Coordinator I was charged with the creation of the engine oils and care products. This included developing new formulations as well as new packaging and POP. I completed a complete relaunch of the entire product line or more than 40 unique products. The final results are visible at any Yamaha dealership. This product line is responsible for approximately $30 million dollars in sales annually and we created a plan to grow that to $35 million.
