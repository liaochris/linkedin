Carlos R. Hernandez’s Summary

I thoroughly enjoy being a “Social Media Catalyst, Trainer and Connector" to people desiring to be Web 2.0 savvy.

My mission since the fall of 2007 has been to help people —especially fellow boomers who feel intimidated by the emerging social media technologies.

Three years have yielded successfully working with dozens of individuals and businesses to leverage Web 2.0 technology in their sales and marketing initiatives. I have shown many professionals how to develop integrated and cross-promotional networks and connections via LinkedIn, Facebook, and Twitter.

I feel blessed to bring my talents in public speaking, problem solving, and teaching to my work as an “agent of conscious social media change.” I spent 28 years working for Westinghouse Electric & Eaton Corporations in technical-solution sales, marketing, and project management after studying Civil Engineering at Stanford University (Class of 1979).

I promote building community through various leadership roles in the Phi Kappa Psi Fraternity, the Catholic Professional Business Club, and the Grace Cathedral. Time and energy is volunteered at Graceworks, a program for those in job and career transition sponsored by the Cathedral.
