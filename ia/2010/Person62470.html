Ed Zschau’s Summary

I provide retained search services for frugal entrepreneurs and resourceful investors. I've completed 70 searches in eight years of executive search.

Principles of my search practice:

Know my client’s business – I invest a lot of time to internalize my client’s business, market, product, team, etc. I translate that knowledge into a story for candidates that is compelling and factual. That knowledge helps me get hard to find candidates interested in the role.

Cast a wide net – I leverage my candidate network to start a search, but 75% of the people I place come from original research. I typically contact 200-300 candidates on a search and get a 70% response rate.

Keep fees reasonable and tied to results – My retained search fees are 25-30% below market. My fees have been constant the last 3 years and will not go up when the market gets busy again. I put 2/3rds of my fee based upon a successful hire and I will take equity as part of the fee.

Guarantee the hire – I offer a one-year guarantee on my retained search placements. If a candidate does not work out, I will re-execute the search. I have only had to re-execute one search in eight years. Ask me about it.

Build strong client relationships – I work with many of my clients on multiple searches. My new clients are referrals from existing clients or VCs. I will not recruit from my clients for 5 years after completing a search.

Vette candidates thoroughly – I’m putting my guarantee on someone so I check the person out thoroughly. I am willing to dismiss a finalist candidate based upon negative, corroborated feedback.

Don’t give up – A search can move quickly, but some take longer to complete. My average time to complete a search is 67 days. I don’t give up on my clients and stay with the assignment, particularly when things gets difficult.
