Chris Amico’s Summary

I'm a multi-platform journalist with international experience and an itch to experiment. I’m fascinated by the way things fit together and love a good story. I'm comfortable writing Python or prose.

I have a talent for dissecting large organizations: Businesses. Non-profits. Web sites. Bureaucracy. Anything with people and moving parts. I like to see how it works and explain it in a way that makes sense.

I've worked at newspapers and TV, blogged, founded a local news and community web site. I'm interested in finding new ways to get and share information and expand news organizations' offerings.

  • Co-founder

    DalianDalian.com

    (Online Media industry)

    January 2007 — Present (3 years 5 months)

    When I moved to Dalian in 2006, I had to hunt for useful information in a mix of local blogs, forums and rudimentary government websites. Most of what I learned came from word of mouth.

    DalianDalian is an effort to bring together many disparate pieces of information about Dalian, a city in Northeast China with a growing expat population and a thriving tech sector.

    The site relies on existing channels to let users contribute without having to learn a new system. Local bloggers can import posts with RSS; photographers push pictures to the site through Flickr. The site's open source CMS (Drupal) organizes the information automatically, allowing people to share information in useful and intelligent ways.

    I helped found the site with Alex Bowman (an economist and web developer) and Rick Martin (a tech writer and trainer), who also lived in the city.
