Jim Carlini’s Summary

Skilled RF Engineer with a strong motivation to excel at high frequency and analog electronics. Lead the R&D microwave efforts at Bosch’s Fairport facility. Teaching Adjutant for electro-magnetic-microwave lab at the Rochester Institute of Technology. Consultant on synthesizer circuitry. Strong experience in developing designs from prototype thru production, with product quantities ranging from sample runs to over 200K units.
Design experience:
RF-Active: (PLLs-Vco's (low_noise - wideband)), (amplifiers: LNAs, med-power and wide-band). mixers, rf-switches.
IF-Analog: LDOs, Op_Amps, comparators, differential-Amps, and VGAs to ADCs.
Planar: couplers, filters, dividers -combiners, antennae. Lumped: filters, noise suppression, wire antenna, matching.
Performed RF system design-Architecture cascaded, spurious, image, ½ IF, size, cost, EMI-noise, DC efficiency.
Wide ranging experience from Baseband to 10.7 GHz, lowpass to 31.5 GHz. Experience with discrete design techniques and RFIC’s using low noise RF control - interface circuitry to drive RF circuits through all phase of the design cycle.. Strong experience and expertise in the development of RF/Microwave circuits used in a wide variety of products. Ranging from low cost, low current transceivers, receivers and transmitters used in the commercial industry to exciters, receivers, hand held radios and radar receivers used for the military. Simulation tools used include: HFSS, Microwave-Office, Ansoft-Designer, Genesys-Spectrasys, MathCAD and Spice. Strong familiarity with test equipment: Spectrum Analyzers, VNAs, Signal Source Analyzers, Signal Generators, Power Meters, Oscilloscopes, Anechoic Chamber. Results-oriented and proficient in creation of schematics, test reports, parts lists, and analysis reports which are clear, concise, and accurate. Simulation and troubleshooting at concept – prototype level. Have written numerous proposals and technical documents. Enjoys working in a team environment.
