Mark Hannah’s Summary

At Edelman, Mark’s key client engagements have included the American Petroleum Institute (API) and Yale University. He has also conducted account work for Dannon, Edison Schools, New York Presbyterian Hospital and MetLife.

Before joining Edelman, Mark worked for v-Fluence Interactive Public Relations where he provided strategic counsel and online issues management services to such clients as Anheuser-Busch, Hilton Hotels, the Monsanto Company, the NCAA, and AstraZeneca. Mark was active with v-Fluence’s stakeholder research and analysis as well as social media outreach and content development.

Mark worked on the 2004 Kerry-Edwards campaign for two years. After starting in Senator Kerry’s press office in Washington, D.C., he traveled nationally for the campaign as a member of the national advance staff. His on-site work included the presidential announcement event, the Democratic National Convention, two of the three presidential debate preps and the senator’s residence on election night. Afterward, Mark accepted a temporary position on the creative development team of the television show, The Apprentice.

He conducted research with an Annenberg Public Policy Center project, which analyzed the network news treatment of terrorist threats in the wake of the 9/11 attacks.

An active member of PRSA, Mark served as a judge for its national 2007 and 2008 Bateman Case Study competitions. He is a 2008 fellow with the Society for New Communications Research (SNCR), where he's conducted primary research into online media's impact on public opinion formation. He sits on the SNCR Best Practices Committee.

Mark is currently enrolled in a Columbia University master’s degree program in strategic communications. He graduated with honors from the University of Pennsylvania, with a double major in communications and philosophy. While at Penn, Mark also completed significant coursework at the Wharton School of Business.
