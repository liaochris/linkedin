Alessandro Nadalin’s Summary

As years go by I developed a fast-growing SEO/benchmark culture, so I've been mainly involved in writing tests, benchmarking apps and optimizing webpages in order to make them fit perfectly on SERPs.

Starting from December, 2009, I moved to Rome in order to join DNSEE, as external consultant/developer, having the possibility to be a team leader for the PHP-oriented projects of the agency, under a former JAVA coder CTO which, in total agree with my principles, started to spread agile methods and enterprise tools in the agency.

I've also wrote, with the collaboration of some external testers and developers, Qredd, a SEO-oriented content management system, which is a CMS with lots of native features which basically make it search engine friendly. Due to the lack of time I decided to migrate Qredd from pure custom PHP to Symfony 1.4/2.0 in 2010.

From January 2010 I started to use Magento ( and, subsquentially, the Zend Framework ) developing REALLY custom and kick-ass modules for the e-commerce platforms.

At last ( but not least ) I've decided that 2010 will be the year of the migration to Symfony ( from Joomla! ), because I really love the enteprise approach of the framework and I've been disappointed about some decisions of OSM ( J!'s fathers ).
