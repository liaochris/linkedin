Richard Granville’s Summary

With over 20 years of new technology development, sales and marketing experience, Granville possesses real world knowledge, a senior executive acumen and the drive to execute objectives effectively. Granville’s core fundamental business management ideals are derived from a broad range of industries for which he has served in many capacities successfully. Granville understands the value of people and that management bears the responsibility for their success. Years of closing major contracts, capital raises, as well as multiple merger and acquisitions successes round out this balanced and polished front line commander.

  • Chairman and CEO

    Grace Development DBA: Avana Communications

    (Telecommunications industry)

    June 1998 — February 2000 (1 year 9 months)

    Grace Development, Inc. (NASDAQ: GCDV), a holding company for a wholly owned subsidiary Avana Communications, was created by Granville. Grace Development was taken public 18 months from inception and Avana became a formidable competitor in the post Telco deregulation period.
    Granville developed Avana Communications as the first integrated communications provider (ICP) operating as a competitive local exchange carrier (CLEC), with the last mile capabilities of a Regional Bell Operating Company (RBOC). Avana was the second largest regional Internet services provider (ISP) behind Mind Spring and delivered network services to businesses and residential customers primarily in the southeastern United States. Avana was the innovator of bundled network services. Avana’s slogan was, “One company – One Solution – One Account”.

    Granville’s advanced “last mile” RF network designs, marketing and sales initiatives enabled Avana to gain traction through pricing and bundled service offerings such as local and long distance (“pre-VOIP”), frame relay, ATM (Asynchronous Transfer Mode), dedicated private line VPNs ( Virtual private networks) and calling card distribution. Avana pioneered, “big city” microwave technologies, providing the first to market affordable high-speed Internet with speeds exceeding 256K in 1998 coupled to “the first operational” Voice over IP solution for business customers. Bellsouth, AT&T and MCI did recognize Avana for its innovative approach to bundled network services. Avana was the first to achieve last mile end run in the Bell South operating territory.

    Granville lost voting control of his board and at the pinnacle of his tenor was forced to resign and gracefully usher in a more experienced executive who had just resigned as the President of Bellsouth International.

  • National Sales Manager
