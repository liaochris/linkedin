  • Electrical Engineer

    Naval Undersea Warfare Center

    (Government Agency; Defense & Space industry)

    January 1992 — August 1994 (2 years 8 months)

    In support of the Microelectronics Branch provided support in electronic circuit design, analysis, fabrication, testing, and documentation for the New Sonar Intercept System ( NSIS ), a high speed data acquisition system. Responsible for the design and development of high speed (20 MHz - 1 GHz) communication cards.

    Developed components for the New Sonar Intercept System (NSIS), a Submarine Defensive Warfare System (SDWS) to improve the effectiveness and survivability of all classes of US submarines. Efforts in this program consisted of threat detection systems and command and control systems (C2). Specific NSIS project tasking consisted of:

    - A new acoustic threat intercept system that had sonar and torpedo recognition capability for early threat detection, classification, and tracking.
    - Electronic circuit design, analysis, fabrication, testing, and documentation for a high speed data acquisition system.
    - Design and development of high speed (20 MHz - 1 GHz) communication cards, which interfaced processed transducer data with a high speed multi-channel Kodak data recorder.
    - Design and assembly of VME bus (ANSI/IEEE 1014-1987) circuit cards in conformance to MIL-STD-2000 for functions such as A/D & D/A conversion, DSP, and Communication.

    System level component development for the AN/BQR-23A Sonar Receiving Set, a towed array sonar narrowband detection and tracking system. The AN/BQR-23A Sonar Receiving Set processed towed array data from a range of submarine towed arrays for detection and display of narrowband signatures.

    - Conducted noise testing of analog hybrid preamplifiers utilizing spectrum analyzers.
    - Assembly of hybrid microelectronic circuits such as amplifiers, preamplifiers, and telemetry circuits.
    - Encapsulation of hybrid microelectronic circuit dies utilizing "glop top" epoxies.
    - Time and frequency domain DSP algorithms including median filter spectral equalization.

  • Electrical Engineer II

    Naval Undersea Warfare Center

    (Government Agency; Defense & Space industry)

    February 1990 — December 1991 (1 year 11 months)

    Design, development, and testing of analog circuits to control the deployment of towed arrays by counting the occurrence and rate of change of magnetic pulses from deployment winches. Circuits took in a series of inputs and adjusted the speed of the winch depending on how much of the array was deployed and the speed of the vessel, to alleviate stress on array and ensure the array was not stressed to the point of failure.

    - Use of Laboratory equipment such as oscilloscopes, arbitrary waveform generators, function generators, spectrum analyzers, and multimeters to develop analog circuits and perform acceptance testing on circuit cards.
    - Assembly of Electronic Circuit Cards in conformance to MIL-STD-2000.
    - Performed "burn in" testing of assembled electronic circuits at an elevated temperature for a specified number of hours (MIL-STD-883, Method 1015: 160 hrs @ 125 °C), with bias and an electrical load applied, in an attempt to stress all elements within the circuit card at maximum rated operating conditions in order to reveal all stress and time dependent failures (aka infant mortality).

    Assisted in the setup and execution for numerous experiments to test the transient response of structures and materials to vibration, shock, noise, and acceleration including:

    - MIL-STD 810F Method 513.5 Acceleration, MIL-STD 810F Method 514.5 Vibration, MIL-STD 810F Method 515.5 Acoustic Noise, MIL-STD 810F Method 516.5 Shock.
    - MIL-STD-1540C - one third octave power spectrum.
    - Collected data for numerous experiments in areas such as vibration, resonance, noise, shock, and calculation of the natural frequency.
    - Developed programs to simulate the vibration of solid enclosures using the finite element method, and to decimate shock test data samples.
    - Wrote programs to automate plotting of test data from a Hewlett Packard spectrum analyzer over an HP-IB Bus.
