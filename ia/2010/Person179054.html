Rusty Citron’s Summary

Rusty Citron, is a respected leader and innovator in global marketing for entertainment, online digital media, consumer products.

Rusty created and founded The Actors Hall of Fame; a non-profit foundation honoring lifetime achievement in film, television and theatre that dedicated to restoring dramatic arts education in schools.

His 30+ years of experience covers global entertainment, media production, online advertising and merchandising, as well as traditional marketing publicity and promotion campaigns for Fortune 500 clients including: Berkshire Hathaway, US West, Time-Warner, Microsoft, Melvin Simon, McDonalds, Disney, MGM, SONY Pictures, NBC, ABC, and Columbia Pictures to name a few.

Serving as Worldwide Senior VP at New World Entertainment, he led the team that revitalized Marvel Comics and the franchise of classic characters developed by Stan Lee; in addition to marketing the release of 30 motion pictures and 8 TV series.

His feature film credits includes work with all the major studios on hundreds of major motion pictures including; Return of the Jedi, Dances with Wolves, and Rocky V. As a personal manager, his association with Mel Brooks, Anne Bancroft, Dom DeLuise, Larry Gelbart, and Sha Na Na cover every aspect of the industry.

His television production and marketing credits encompass over 30 major television series and specials.

More recently, he developed the marketing and promotion of digital high defintion media for online and video on demand distribution. He led a team of designers and sales people who executed online merchandising programs generating $200k in daily revenue.

He was the founding chairman of the entertainment industry's first 'product placement' council, and he created and started one of the first online entertainment merchandising services in partnership with NBC.

He lives in Seattle and Los Angeles with his wife Jill and daughters Rachel and Stacy.
