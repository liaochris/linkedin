Doug Richards’s Summary

I first started working with web technology in 1994 as an early adopter of the now legendary Netscape (Mozilla) tool set. Through the 90's I financed my life as a part-time rock star by designing and developing websites and graphics for punk and grunge bands and restaurants. In 1997 I received an invitation to teach English at the University of Guadalajara in Mexico, an adventure that lasted three wonderful years. While in Mexico I developed and implemented a 1st gen web portal system for restaurants and tourist destinations located in Puerto Vallarta, Acapulco and Mazatlan.

Upon my return to the states in early 2000 I continued to push the boundaries of online development for the Sprint University of Excellence creating multimedia applications for distance learning. During my tenure I became an evangelist for Flash through my work with first gen action-script applications and streaming video displayed via the Flash player. In 2003 I presented "Selling the Value of In-House Design" to the HOW Design conference to illustrate the power social design as a training tool. In 2004 I left Sprint and spent the next few years on the launch teams of start-ups or as a sales and process consultant. Over those 5 years I've spent thousands of hours researching social media trends and I now apply that accumulated knowledge to my role as a social media educator at Centriq Training.

As a social media educator I train people how and companies why they need to use social media for business. We are the only state (Kansas) Board of Regents certified social media certification program in the country. Focusing on three key areas, Demand Generation, Sales Enablement and Customer Experience, our program utilizes a blended approach to learning; instructor led, facilitated and on-demand to make sure your team has access to 'just in time' training when you need it. If your company is serious about social media success in 2010 consider Centriq Training 2.0 as your education partner.
