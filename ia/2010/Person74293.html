Mr. Flanigan has worked on industrial machines for over forty years. Presently Mr. Flanigan is displaying a Car Seat Restraint that will save over 50,000 lives a year. This new computer controlled machine will eventually replace the airbags & seat belt/harnesses used in cars today. Here is how it works. A computer controlled net is buckled in when you sit on a car seat; instead of a seatbelt and harness. This net is attached to a machine that can be built into the seat or attached to the seat. The net allows the speed and the -pressure of the passenger, pushing against the net, to be controlled after a car crash. This new car seat restraint can be used by a 400 lbs man and a 5 lbs baby. 5 of these restraints can be installed for less than the cost of 2 airbags, In every auto accident there are 3 collisions: The car crashing into something, the passenger colliding within the car and the internal organs of the passenger colliding with the body frame
