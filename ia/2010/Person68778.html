Brian McCarthy’s Summary

The Evolution of FACE

It would be fair to say that FACE originated out of frustration. After many years in the corporate world and having experienced using various personality profiling and communication tools, Mike Francis observed that knowing how those tools work and making good use of them were two very different things.

“As a manager, I was often frustrated at the lack of practical application of these tools, especially at the coalface – in sales and with customer/client relations. Some organizations spend a small fortune on ways to improve the performance of their people, only to have their investment wasted because the tool was placed in the ‘Too Hard’ basket.” (Mike Francis)

There are many personality profiling tools in the marketplace. These tools are primarily designed to help us understand ourselves better; and they can also improve our understanding of other people and how to communicate more effectively with them. These tools are widely used for management, recruitment and in sales and marketing environments.

However, the effectiveness of any of these tools lies in their ease of use and translation to market; and it is on this measure where many of these tools fail to have any lasting impact. In simple terms, if the masses find the tool too difficult, complicated or time-consuming to use, it will be destined to gather dust on an office shelf somewhere.

Therefore, the development of FACE focused on 3 key parameters: effectiveness, easy to use and FUN!

Another unique element of FACE that separates it from other personality profiling tools actually occurred without a specific intent. This unique element is that FACE is also Visual. The FACE characters draw attention and are easily and clearly recognizable.

FACE was launched in 2004, and the feedback and results have proven that the 3 key parameters have all been achieved, with the Visual element as a bonus.

Brian McCarthy’s Specialties:

1) FACE needs to be Effective. It has to be a tool which can reliably define the various personality profiles of humans and one which is entirely credible to the end-user.
2) FACE needs to be Simple. To ensure that the majority of people will use this tool, it must be simple to understand and easy to apply.
3) FACE needs to be Fun. Children learn best when playing, laughing or singing; truth is, adults are exactly the same.
