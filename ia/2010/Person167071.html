Rey Gutierrez’s Summary

At the age of 22, Rey Gutierrez stood in New York City’s famous Times Square watching his video for Morisson Poe’s Glitter Girl, which helped launch the Xbox 360, play on MTV’s 44 1/2-foot screen. “It made me feel like it’s about damn time that my work and the people I work with get the recognition that it deserves,” Gutierrez explained. “I spent my teenage years working my ass off teaching myself something that no one else could teach me---to nurture my talent.” Gutierrez’s video for Glitter Girl was part of Perfect Dark Zero, the titled that helped launch the initial release of the Xbox 360. “When I got the word that I would be shooting a music video for a game from Rare, one of my favorite developers, I was ecstatic,” he explained. “A lot of my ideas and foundation come from video games and not from books or movies, like other directors."

For Gutierrez, video games are more about storytelling and experience, as well as being immersed in the world. Storytelling and cinematic experience in a video game come first and he has gained much inspiration from them. Gutierrez merged his ideas from video games and music with his commercial work, creating a unique blend of visually stimulating work with an edge. His way of working is unorthodox; he shoots, directs and edits all of his work, giving him complete control over the final product. Gutierrez’s projects have been seen on more than 50 major online and broadcast networks, including MTV, Music Choice, Much Music, Fuse, ESPN and GameTrailers.com. The response to Gutierrez’s hardcore edge in the rap and pop world has been overwhelmingly positive since he has begun to merge his vision with the more mainstream commercial world. “It’s a great feeling that people enjoy my work and unorthodox ways,” Gutierrez said. “Hopefully I won’t piss them off."
