Sharon Melnick, Ph.D.’s Summary

1. Clients preparing for greater leadership responsibilities whose success depends on executive presence, confidence, and influencing effectively in up and down communications.

Clients shift from tactical responsibilities to strategic thinking, focus despite distractions, and assert ideas persuasively yet respectfully with senior management. Clients get clarity on career direction and take control of navigating next career steps.

2. Clients ready to balance their hard driving, results orientation with respectful communication and relationship effectiveness.

Clients move beyond a potentially career limiting style by sharpening listening skills and discovering a tone and approach that motivates others to high standards. Clients create positive culture change on their teams.

3. Clients who want a competitive edge by having practical business focused techniques to be calm under pressure, energize when tired, remain rational instead of reactive, and clear thinking when overwhelmed.

4. Clients who want to quickly resolve personal situations that are distracting them at work.
