Jonathan L. Raper’s Summary

Growing up in the electronics and telecom industry, I began assisting my father's company at an early age installing voice, data, and other systems. Having been in this environment for so long, I've been exposed to more (technologically speaking) than most people 10 years my senior. In my early teens I was proficient in printed circuit board design utilizing a CAD system; in my later teens, I was the principal CAD designer for machine frame design projects. I've done just about everything from pulling cable to assisting in a sales presentation to a customer.

In 1981 I began programming in BASIC on a Commodore 64. From that experience in my early years, my interest in computers grew as a hobby until I accepted the position I now hold with Eagle Physicians. Since 1999 I have been instrumental in the growth of the Eagle IT Department from 1 FTE to 7 FTEs. I have coordinated two major network upgrades, and implemented cutting edge wireless technologies (Free Space Optics in 2001 and Non Line of Sight RF in early 2006). Also in early 2006 I coordinated the implementation of two separate Mitel VoIP phone systems, built a new small data center, and implemented Windows 2003 Terminal Services. In late 2006 I coordinated the implementation of ProVation, an Electronic Procedural Record system for the purpose of documenting procedures at our Endoscopy Center in order to speed up documentation as well as meet regulatory compliance guidelines. In early 2008 we began the virtualization of our data center utilizing VMware and completed our AD migration to Windows 2003.

In 2009 we completed a successful migration from Exchange 2000 to 2007, implementation of a new Gig-E FSO link, EMC Avamar, Citrix Xen App, and Cisco 802.11n wireless. We also started the roll-out of a new EMR/PM system eClinicalWorks.

In 2010, we'll be completing the roll-out of our EMR, implementing 3 new Mitel VoIP phone systems, changing phone service providers, and installing a second Gig-E FSO Link.
