Dave Isbell’s Summary

The only thing Dave Isbell ever wanted to be when he grew up was a rock star. However, he played in enough bars to realize that he disliked watching drunken bar fights, sleeping in sweaty vans, and inevitable lung cancer by proxy. So he eventually shoved his Bass Guitar in a closet and got a job working for "the Man." Fortunately for him, he was laid off during a merger and found himself in a career helping other people to manage their careers.

More than ten years later, he is still passionate about his work and continues to be motivated by his vision of a world that is socially-just, and spiritually-enlightened, where people are free to be who they were created to be without pain or grief because of their decision to be themselves. To that end, he has found his purpose is to help adults find and understand their unique vision, purpose, passions, and competencies that aid them in constructing a well-balanced, meaningful life while also making a contribution to society.

If you are tired of working for “the Man,” just to pay the bills, then you will find Dave’s compassionate, relationship-oriented career coaching strategies helpful. He clearly has a hunger for knowledge and wisdom and the strong desire to pass it on to others. This hunger is not only evident in his coaching strategies, but also within the personal enrichment and career development classes that he teaches.

If you are content to work for “the Man,” Dave will happily share his knowledge and experience of human resource management and current hiring practices to help you to speak “the Man’s” language in a more clear, concise and consistent manner.

If you are “the Man,” Dave will gratefully help you to connect with employees that will be very happy to work for you!

If you are an artist looking to collaborate, sadly, all he can offer is conversation about the arts and an honest critique of your work until he frees up time by finishing his MSW.
