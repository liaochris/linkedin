Eriko Grill’s Summary

Thank you! We are very glad to see you are interested in purchasing ModeEsty products. We want you to wear our shirts with a sense of pride knowing that you made a difference in someone’s life with the profit from your purchase that will supports sustainable employment in sub-Saharan Africa.

Furthermore, if you have traveled to those countries before, we are confident to say that you have received much more than you have given from those whom you have met— a life-changing experience!

The ModeEsty designer, Eriko Grill is so excited to keep designing a new line of clothing that sends positive and powerful messages that can be carried through the products and of course, those who are wearing our ModeEsty products. She hopes that one message on your clothing will find someone’s life a little brighter than it was found before. Positivity is contagious. Spread the LOVE that never fails!

ModeEsty has established a partnership with EDUN LIVE clothing line to pursue its vision and mission that are matched with the mission of EDUN LIVE, bringing sustainable employment to sub-Saharan Africa.

"EDUN LIVE is a new line of t-shirts created for merchandising purposes. Headquartered in Ireland, it is the sub-brand of EDUN – the socially conscious clothing company launched in the Spring of 2005 by Ali Hewson and Bono with New York clothing designer Rogan Gregory. The EDUN LIVE’s mission is to drive sustainable employment in sub-Saharan Africa through high-volume sales. From the African cotton we source, to the spinning and knitting process, all the way to the manufacturer, EDUN LIVE t-shirts are 100% African - or as we like to say, “grower-to-sewer” African. This is a very special and unique position as very few, if any, businesses are doing the same."

Eriko Grill’s Specialties:

"EDUN LIVE aims to put forth a business model that is a proof of concept to other companies. In other words, we want to demonstrate that you can do business (and do it very well) with developing countries like those in sub-Saharan Africa. It is our hope that others will replicate the EDUN LIVE business model and grow the trade and employment opportunities available in these impoverished regions."
