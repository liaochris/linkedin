  • CTO/Vice-President of Engineering

    Newbury Networks

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Computer Networking industry)

    March 2001 — August 2002 (1 year 6 months)

    I was the founding executive of Newbury Networks, recruited by idealab Boston to build up a company in the "Bluetooth middleware space". Within a few months, we shifted focus to wireless networking (WLAN). The core technology developed revolved around the use of the wireless network essentially as "in-building GPS", detecting the spatial location of WLAN client devices with high-accuracy and enabling location-based services and functionality.

    The key product out of this technology is "Wifi Watchdog", which adds a location-based access control to a wireless network. This helps sysadmins find rogue access point as well as impose "virtual boundaries" on the extent of the WLAN, a sort of location-based wireless VLAN.

    Newbury Networks won a DEMO God Award at DEMO 2002 and at DEMO Mobile 2002.

    Newbury Networks continues to be financed by idealab.

  • Founder/CTO

    Virtual Ink

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Computer Hardware industry)

    September 1996 — March 2001 (4 years 7 months)

    Inventor of "mimio" and co-founder of Virtual Ink. Mimio is a marker-pen tracking system that turns a regular whiteboard into a digital writing surface, capturing handwritten information in color and in real-time for storage or sharing with others over the Internet.

    Over 120,000 units have been sold worldwide since mimio hit the market in September 1999. Key markets continue to include complementing video conferencing with ad-hoc data conferencing and distance-learning for corporate and educational purposes.

    I aggressively pursued the development of a strong intellectual property portfolio. Virtual Ink currently holds over a dozen US Patents and several patents in the EU and Asia.

    Virtual Ink was financed by angel investors, CMGI, IDG Ventures, Bear Stearns, and Intel Capital.
