Sarah Kelley’s Experience

  • Information Technology Ombudsman

    DPR Construction, Inc.

    (Privately Held; Construction industry)

    June 2009 — Present (9 months)

    The Information Technology Ombudsman is a designated internal customer advocate in the Information Technology team whose duty is to assist with customer service concerns, track problem areas, and recommend changes to policies, procedures, and processes to improve organizational efficiency and effectiveness.

    This role is all about communication. For any organization that provides mission critical services and support, it is almost impossible to have too much communication when it comes to important problems and related issues.

    The Ombudsman is also charged with establishing and maintaining cooperation with and understanding of the goals and priorities of the various business units and departments within the business. Devoted specifically to help interface with the business, the role requires a combination of technical and business understanding, public relations, and triage skills. One important function is to be aware of and monitor “new things” – that is, relevant concepts, trends, challenges and innovations that are new to the organization. This is particularly important if the “new thing” is disruptive in nature, in the sense of requiring the organization to review and improve policies, procedures, and processes.
