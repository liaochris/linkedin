Becky Ebenkamp’s Summary

SNAPSHOT: It all started in kindergarten. Some misguided but well-meaning teacher gave this natural lefty a pair of right-handed scissors and said, "Cut." Fearing corporal punishment I quickly switched sides. I've been running with scissors ever since. (To see what I've been up to, click the "portfolio" link and read my Boston Herald profile.)

And so goes the lobes. In a world full of people who are classified as right- or left-brained, creative or logical, I'm ambidextrous. This combo of arts + smarts has its advantages. I've worked as a journalist and a graphic designer and find I'm a quick study on most any subject. As platforms for delivering media evolve, I'm becoming drawn to generating ideas--for new food products, urban design, ad campaigns, iPhone apps, entertainment content and more--and exploring ways to communicate their relevance to people.

I love to collaborate on projects and am a valuable asset to a think tank or brainstorming unit. As the token artsy brainiac on the project, I have the ability to see from everyone's perspective and to get the artistic and the analytical speaking one language. I spark creativity when discussions turn dull and strategize "what if?" scenarios when members are settling into the deadly habit of groupthink.

WRITING & EDITING: I'm an expert textpert who would gladly go back to a writing gig at the right publication. Freelance assignments are welcome, too. I'm trying to be realistic about finding work in my chosen profession because the self-supporting print journalist is becoming extinct. I define myself broadly as a storyteller/creative communicator, so my resume is expanding to comedy writing, branded content, documentary filmmaking and other specialties. I am passionately interested in progressive causes and pop culture, especially, music.

SPEAKING OF MUSIC: I also host, curate and engineer my radio show, Bubblegum & Other Delights, which airs live on KillRadio.org every other Tuesday, 7-9 p.m. (PST)

Becky Ebenkamp’s Specialties:

Analysis of pop culture trends (TV interview experience), copy editing (graduated from Dow Jones editing program), headline writing (never met a spit-take-funny pun I didn't like). My most recent day job involved analyzing consumer marketing and advertising, while most freelance work is music-related and a labor of love. My wide range of interests and expertise allows me to report on and regurgitate just about any subject worth writing about.
