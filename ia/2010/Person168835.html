Robert Andosca’s Summary

I have worked in the semiconductor and MEMS (microelectromechanical systems) industries for the last 20+ years. This has included operations and general management to project management roles, business development, and lead engineering positions. The breadth of my experience has been leading teams in design/development, where in several cases the developed products and fabrication processes have been transferred to manufacturing with sustained high yield.

Robert Andosca’s Specialties:

Operations to technical engineering and program management roles. Business development, including forming strategic alliances. MEMS design and modeling, MEMS and IC process design, development and manufacturing. Development to manufacturing transfer. Design for manufacturability (DFM), and Six Sigma statistical process control experience.

Robert Andosca’s Experience

  • Founder, President and CTO

    MicroGen Systems, LLC

    (Privately Held; Nanotechnology industry)

    February 2007 — Present (3 years 8 months)

    MicroGen is developing a patent-pending MicroElectroMechanical Systems (MEMS) based Piezoelectric Vibrational Energy Harvester (PZ-EH). MicroGen's low-cost, long lifetime BOLT(tm) family of micro-generators/rechargers will produce power levels at 60/120 Hz (or other specified vibration frequency) from a minimum of 100 uWatts at 0.1 g acceleration to as much as 1.0 mWatt at 1.0 g. A "self-charging battery" is formed when integrated with EH-boards with voltage regulating electronics and power storage (advanced thin-film batteries and/or ultra-capacitors) that are available from our commercial partners. The intent is to power mesh and non-mesh wireless sensor network (WSN) applications primarily for the industrial and aerospace/military markets, and later for the automobile industry. The MEMS-based device is under development at the Cornell NanoScale Science and Technology Facility (www.cnf.cornell.edu). MicroGen plans to release its Alpha-level product at in the fall of 2010.
