Colin Ude-Lewis’s Summary

Writer of Wisdom Notes "school of life" manuscripts containing the wisdom from ancient philosophers and modern day thinkers to help you succeed in life.

Past - I had the formidable task of launching a start-up dotcom company and running it through a stock listing in 2000. Faced with these pressures, I learnt that it is essential to be a good strategist and planner: to set goals, to build clarity and consensus around them, and to connect the short-term goals with long-term results through excellent execution. I also learnt, through the experience, the most successful executives are also those who can morph to the market conditions, be they changing capital requirements or capital supply. Being the CEO of any company is difficult, but being the CEO of a stock listed 'start-up' is like being hit by a train every day.

Experience shows that you get hired for your competencies and fired for your behaviour. Recognising a coach can help clients to change their behaviours, I use my experience by working with organisations that see development as an opportunity and know that they must take their individuals and the whole organisation to the next level.

As an Executive Coach - my style typically involved guiding clients to reflect on their values, visions, and develop new habits and behaviours that demonstrate improved everyday behaviours. Acting with increasing integrity, and being driven by values has been shown in research to be a characteristic of the ‘best’ companies. Developing more ethical practices has also been correlated with better financial performance.

Coaching is focused on improving Executives decision making styles, interpersonal and intrapersonal skills and helping management teams create and execute on strategic plans.
