Kengwei Lu’s Summary

Kengwei Lu was born in Taipei, Taiwan where he studies mass media. In 1996, he moved to New York where he attended the world famous Pratt Institute where he studied in Computer Graphics and interactive media. He earned scholarship from the Art institute of Chicago. After graduation, he joined MarchFirst as a designer in Austin, Texas, USA.

In 2001, he joined the renowned, award-winning Taipei-based DMC (www.d-m-c.com.tw), where he guided the pitch process, as well as developed creative ideas for such brands as Nike, Unilever, and Roche. While in DMC, he co-host and participated in Tomato creative workshop in Taipei. He moved to Toronto in 2003 to be an Art Director and flash designer at ADEO communication, where he worked primarily on the Nissan and Infinity brands.

While at Critical Mass, Lu spearheaded visual concepts for Citibank Online, the ThankYou Network, Citi Cards, Cablevision, and Alienware. As an Art Director, he has managed a team of 3 to 5 designers, and worked directly with research, experience, and technology teams. He also provided creative strategies/solutions to satisfy client's business requirements. Most importantly, he won trust from Citibank for Critical Mass and played an essential role for Critical Mass Toronto's success.

He has a passion for branding and creative and regularly reads blogs about physical computing/arts. "I think interactivity is the key to interest people in so many different ways," he says, "the day will come in our life time and we all get to experience it." Lu's dream is to invite the creative minds of the world to come and play with him and create interactive art. "I love to experience. There are so many ideas there. The question is how big your dreams are and how could you make it happen".
