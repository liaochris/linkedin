Mike Baldus’s Summary

Mike helps clients solve problems that keep them awake at night. He generates positive returns on the investment of effort by:
Providing specific, attainable action items
Improving personal and business effectiveness
Increasing profitability and decreasing costs

He uses a mentoring process that includes:
Refining the vision and goals of the business
Identifying strengths as well as areas for improvement
Developing action plans specific to the needs of the business and its owner(s)

RESULTS: Many owners find that their stress levels decrease dramatically because they are able to focus on specific do-able steps toward realizing their vision. Quite often, family harmony also improves as cash flow increases. His clients find renewed enthusiasm for their business with the successes that are achieved through Mike’s mentoring.

Mike is recognized for his skills in insightful questioning, understandable explanations, and collaborative team building.

Mike Baldus’s Specialties:

Over twenty-five years experience providing personalized solutions for business owners in professional services, agriculture and construction.

Wide range of leadership positions in various community organizations, including Toastmasters, Rotary, Boy Scouts, Redwood Empire Cursillo, 4-H, Redwood Empire Estate Planners, and Redwood Empire Remodelers Association.

My collaborative leadership skills help small business owners build better teams on their way to increased profitability.

Mike Baldus’s Experience

  • Owner

    M. Baldus & Associates

    (Professional Training & Coaching industry)

    1988 — Present (22 years )

    After the “tax simplification act of 1987” I decided to pursue my personal interest of helping small business owners succeed in their dreams of creating a successful source of family income. I began my mentoring practice before “coaching” became a mainstream improvement tool.

    I am a small business owner, committed to that segment of our economy’s success. I have worked with many family-owned businesses, especially helping husband-wife/father-son owners, to resolve work-related issues and increase profitability. Usually my clients make money on their investment in my services – if they follow our agreed upon action steps.

  • Treasurer

    Sonoma County Grape Growers Association

    (Farming industry)

    1983 — Present (27 years )

    I am a founding member of this trade association, which we formed to promote Sonoma County wine grapes back when Napa was the only recognized California brand name. As CFO/Treasurer, I established a balanced budget process and initiated the member services program which substantially increased our membership to almost 800 paid members. The increased funding supported more extensive marketing and member services programs. I was awarded the Sonoma County Viticultural Award of Excellence in 2004.

    I was a key member of the team that conducted the referendum resulting in the successful approval of the Sonoma County Winegrape Commission. I helped with the transition to a commission structure, including developing database and accounting systems to meet the needs of the commission. The effect of the commission was to triple the funds available for marketing programs.

  • Division Governor

    Toastmasters International

    (Non-Profit; Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    2007 — 2009 (2 years )

    As Division Governor of a geographical area from Cotati to Eureka in the north and east to Lake County, I developed a cohesive club structure, promoted a spirit of cooperation and support, and mentored the five Area Governors. I introduced an extensive program of club officer training in the division, as well as advanced leadership training through monthly “Fireside” presentations.

    In recognition of my efforts, I received District 57’s Division Governor of the Year in 2007-2008 as well as the President’s Distinguished Division Governor in 2008-09. My wife and I also received the District’s “Dynamic Duo” Award in 2009.

    I have given presentations at District 57’s Training Leadership Institutes, the Winter Conference, as well as at our local “Fireside” leadership series. I have served two terms as my club’s president and am currently the Secretary-Treasurer for the club. In the past year, I have successfully chartered two new Toastmasters clubs in Santa Rosa. Currently, I am in the final stage of completing all the requirements for my Distinguished Toastmaster (DTM) award.

  • Adjunct faculty member--Ag & Natural Resources

    Santa Rosa Junior College

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    1989 — 1992 (3 years )

    Obtained lifetime teaching credential for California Community Colleges. Taught Ag Finance with an emphasis on practical business planning and profitability analysis.

  • Partner

    Pisenti & Brinker LLP

    (Partnership; Accounting industry)

    1976 — 1988 (12 years )

    Managing tax partner 1978-1988

    I structured the first formally-organized department within the firm, initiated a streamlined process for preparing tax returns, and was on the cutting edge of technology at the time in transitioning from exclusive use of an outside service bureau to more extensive use of computers within the office. In my 12 years there, I built the tax department by attracting both clients and talented staff. When I left in 1988 to begin my mentoring practice, we had a unique regional tax practice that had attracted the attention of the then “big eight” accounting firms.
