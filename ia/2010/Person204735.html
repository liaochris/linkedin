Tirumalai Kamala’s Experience

  • Research Associate (Kelly contractor)

    NIH

    (Government Agency; Research industry)

    February 2007 — December 2009 (2 years 11 months)

    Successful Scientific Collaborations

    1. I collaborated with Dr. Navreet Nanda, an expert in the field of MHC class II antigen processing and presentation, to show that the accessory antigen processing molecule, HLA-DM, plays a critical role in the susceptibility of BALB/c mice to leishmania infection. In recent years, cytokines have been the major focus of immunological investigations into disease causation and susceptibility. In contrast, our research highlights the hitherto ignored and potentially critical disease modulating role of antigen processing and presentation molecules. Results of our study were published in the Journal of Immunology in April 2009.
    2. I collaborated with Dr. David Usharauli, an expert in cytotoxic T cell effector function differentiation, in a study comparing effector function of CD8 T cells following brief or prolonged stimulation. This study, in which I was the last author, was published in the Journal of Immunology in April 2008.

  • Immunological Innovator

    NIH

    (Government Agency; Research industry)

    2003 — 2008 (5 years )

    Independent Immunological Innovations

    1. I developed HOCK IMMUNIZATION, a novel immunization method
    • Novel & humane alternative to mouse footpad injection
    • Non-load-bearing site, minimizing unrelieved pain & distress
    • Drains to same lymph nodes, & induces equivalent immune responses
    • Included in the NIH Office of Animal Care and Use (OACU) Guidelines for the Research Use of Adjuvants (revised 11/14/2007)
    • Published (single author) in the Journal of Immunological Methods in December 2007
    2. I developed a Novel Modular Lymphocyte Purification Protocol
    • Uses unique antibody cocktails & negative selection
    • Easily adaptable to purify untouched CD4+, CD8+ or B cells
    • Very high purity (comparable to cell sorting)
    • Very high recovery (higher than with other methodologies)
    • Published (single author) in the Scandinavian Journal of Immunology in March 2008
    3. Extensive Immunoassay development experience
    • Novel sandwich ELISA for all 7 mouse antibody isotypes using a single capture antibody
    • Novel sandwich ELISAs for sheep antibody isotypes
    • Novel ELISAs for alpha lactalbumin, a milk protein, in sheep & human milk
    • Expertise in mouse cytokine detection using my own ELISAs, not kits.

    Solicited feedback from colleagues make my protocols more user-friendly
    Deconstructing my protocols helped me identify the elements critical for reproducible results
    Hope to someday write or contribute to a manual of cellular immunology

  • Co-founder, Founder Chair (2003-2007), NIHVFC

    NIH

    (Government Agency; Research industry)

    2001 — June 2007 (6 years )

    Organizational Aptitude

    Under the NIH Visiting Fellow Award Program, at any given time, ~3000 non-US post-doctoral fellows receive research training. In fact, approximately 60% of NIH Fellows are visiting scientists (NIH Catalyst, Vol.17, Mar-Apr 2009).

    Brief History & my role in the NIHVFC (NIH Visiting Fellows Committee):
    • Involved (2001-2003) in creating NIHVFC, officially inaugurated on July 1, 2003
    • Unique grassroots self-governing resource for NIH Visiting Fellows
    • Addresses post-training transition to independent research careers
    • Served as founder chair from July 2003 - June 2007
    • Voluntarily resigned, to make way for new blood & to focus on other aspects of my career

    As Chair, I was instrumental in creating:
    • Distinctive NIHVFC logo: different colored hands reaching towards each other across the globe, symbolizing the global makeup of NIH Visiting Fellows, working together towards the common goal of betterment of human health
    • NIH Alumni Associations outside the US
    • Two programs to help NIH Visiting Fellows successfully transition to independent careers:
    A. Annual Career Fair, the International Opportunities Expo, launched in 2004
    • Provides information on global job opportunities to NIH Visiting Fellows
    • NIHVFC co-founder Valeria de Mello Coelho & I helped Donna Vogel organize 1st Career Fair
    • Primary organizer & contact person for NIHVFC Career Fairs in 2005, 2006 and 2007
    B. Science Voices from Home
    • Since 2006 this program helps NIH Visiting Fellows re-connect with their scientific brethren back home through one-to-one interaction with prominent scientists & high level government officials visiting NIH from their home country or region

    NIHVFC and its programs continue to flourish after I & other co-founders left it. It has a robust & growing presence on LinkedIn. This is testament to the genuine need it serves in the NIH post-doc community.
