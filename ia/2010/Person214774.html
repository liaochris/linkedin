William Pence’s Experience

  • EVP and CTO

    WebMD

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; WBMD; Internet industry)

    2007 — Present (3 years )

  • SVP and CTO

    Napster, Inc.

    (Public Company; 51-200 employees; NAPS; Internet industry)

    May 2003 — October 2007 (4 years 6 months)

    Bill was instrumental in relaunching the Napster service as a licensed, paid music service in 2003. As part of the service rollout, Bill led the creation of an online media delivery platform that supports a range of business models (a la carte purchases, subscription) and media types (audio, video). From 2003 to present, the service has grown to over $120M in revenue, has more than 6 million registered users and nearly 1 million premium subscribers. The service is now offered in five territories: the US, Canada, UK, Germany, and Japan. Over the years, Bill has been involved in pioneering efforts to remake the digital music market as a legitimate business, and has led the integration and adaptation of Digital Rights Management into real-world systems. Napster's current platform is highly scalable and supports robust e-commerce capabilities (micropayments, advertising, and subscription management).

  • SVP and CTO

    pressplay

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Online Media industry)

    March 2000 — May 2003 (3 years 3 months)

    pressplay was a pioneering online music service, launched in 2001 in response to rampant piracy and chaos in the music industry. The service was the first online music subscription service of its kind, and formed the basis for the relaunch of the Napster brand in 2003. As part of pressplay, Bill helped to staff the organization and build a platform and product set from the ground up.

  • Director, Digital Media Solutions

    IBM Software Group

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; IBM; Information Technology and Services industry)

    September 1998 — March 2000 (1 year 7 months)

    Bill helped to create the IBM Intenet Media Group in 1997. This organization helped to develop and promote internet media technologies across a range of IBM products, including Lotus Notes/Domino and Websphere. From its inception, Bill was instrumental in staffing the organization and working with IBM lines of business to identify opportunities to incorporate digital media for a variety of applicaiton areas, including corporate communitcations, distance learning, interactive advertising, and collaboration. Several product features were introduced across IBM and Lotus products as a result of this initiative.

  • Director, Digital Media Research

    IBM Research Division

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; IBM; Computer Software industry)

    1997 — 1998 (1 year )

    Managed a 50+ person team of researchers focused on applications of digital media: video parsing (scene detection, feature extraction), pattern matching (protein folding and bioinformatics), biometrics (fingerprint and other identity technologies), and natural language (language translation). Several technologies were commercialized through IBM divisions (web page translation for websphere, fingerprint system for government applications).

  • Senior Director, Communication Technology

    IBM Research Division

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; IBM; Computer Hardware industry)

    1993 — 1997 (4 years )

    Managed group of researchers focused on networking and communications hardware. Led pioneering work in 802.11 (wifi) and infrared technologies, resulting in commercialization of some of the first wireless adapters for PCs and several generations of high-speed infrared communication ICs. Helped to commercialize research in intra-computer and Fibre Channel optical interconnects, and demonstrate the viabilty of several low-end IC technologies for RF applications, including CMOS and BiCMOS, as well as the viability of emerging technolgies such as SiGe for high-frequency devices.
