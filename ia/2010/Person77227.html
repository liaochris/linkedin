  • National Advertising Manager

    Australian Consolidated Press (ACP)

    (Privately Held; Publishing industry)

    2000 — 2005 (5 years )

    Lynette Rochford employed me at ACP. It was a bit of a risk for her given that I had no experience in media, however both Lynette and her publisher believed that given I had self-published numerous books, I deserved a go.

    Work commenced with me and one other bloke on a floor of over 40 women. The other bloke was selling Bride to Be and all the others were either working in sales or editorial/publishing on Bride to Be or my publications - The ACP Kids Titles.

    I quickly learnt that kids titles were in a state of disarray. Pacific Publications had been publishing Disney Adventures and soon as they learnt they were losing the license to ACP, they basically ran it into the ground in preparation for launch of KZONE.

    With next to no forward revenue booked, I had no other option than to get creative and make loads of contacts.

    The interesting thing was that no-one knew much about the kids market at the time. The term 'Tweens' had only just started and their influence on the weekly family investment bill was becoming more regularly publicised.

    What developed was:
    Initially increasing advertising rates - when competitors were highly discounting theirs - based on Disney family values
    Researching our readers - and kids love to provide feedback
    I launched a enews to all the contacts I had been making - starting at CEO level for all the major businesses interested in promoting to kids
    Then started the cross-sell. There was no way we could compete with the circulation numbers reported by PacPubs. However when I added publications to proposals like:
    Mothers: Womens Weekly/Day, Take 5
    Teens: Dolly etc
    Digital: ninemsn/disney
    Events and entertainment characters
    Marketing and sampling, etc
    Our footprint expanded for every client

    Throughout my time at ACP, I was an anomaly - unlike everyone who reported to the sales hierarchy, my team communicated with editorial and publishing. The results lead to the launch of new publications and $millions in revenue...

  • Director & Founder

    Youth by Design

    (Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    1997 — 2001 (4 years )

    In early 2007 my cousin Tomas came to me with a scenario requiring a solution.

    A friend of his was establishing a new Pizza joint in Caringbah and needed something special.

    We had an initial meeting and after some brainstorming, decided to look for some graffiti artists to decorate his wall.

    YouthbyDesign was born.

    The wall ended up being decorated by a highly experienced airbrush artist we met - though the journey in finding that artist led to:

    Removable graffiti art displayed throughout the shire
    Discovery of a network of over 300 creative aerosol artists
    Discovery of over 200 artists from many artistic fields
    One of the first companies providing contractual employment and promotion of youth artistic work through local and national media
    The first aerosol and airbrush Tafe program and some of the first local community and government funding training programs for aerosol artists
    Many community and business funded aerosol designs that still exist today.

    What an inspirational time this was - the youth provided the inspiration and the community encouraged it.

    I remember arguing with John Laws about the only way to manage graffiti, was to make it compulsory - then pay those who were successful at it.

    There were many successes - like front window displays on Oxford street, Window displays with manikin heads for Sceats comp, Artists works rotated within restaurants for sale, Walls of remembrance at PCYC locations, YBDevious days sponsored by Pepsi, Nando's and many others...

    so much fun... and the young ones benefitted...

  • Contracted Sales Executive

    Larson-Juhl

    (Privately Held; Wholesale industry)

    1999 — 2000 (1 year )

    Have you ever wished to learn more about the industry of 'Art & Memorabilia'?

    Through my experience with YouthbyDesign, my interest in this area was developed significantly.

    After spending a week working with my cousin Tomas on the bell tower overlooking the SCG at Fox Studios (One of the places I re-inforced my fear of heights - stepping out 10 stories up onto a skimpy scaffold with sheeting 3mtrs long and 5foot high flexing in the wind that promoted FOX) - I finished with an interview with the MD at Larson Juhl and took the job.

    What a interesting time

    I was taken through the archives and background tunnels of some of Australia's leading art galleries, museums, conservation centres and picture framing galleries - gathering knowledge on international and local history that covered centuries of knowledge.

    Everything from mass production of your footy memorabilia in your cellar to high brow artists works that never see the light of day.

    I sold the framing materials and machinery that are internationally regarded as the best in the world, whilst also having in the back of my mind that many of the framing materials were using high quality timber from ever depleting forests across the planet.

    I comforted those thoughts with the knowledge that as I travelled between picture framers, they provided me with the cut-outs of their mat boards and off cuts of their framing that helped me produce the hand made publications that I later sold through galleries and book retailers to help raise money for various charitable projects.

    It really is a funny world we live in, and the conservation of our history I found through this work and see in peoples memories framed in every home I visit never ceases to remind me of that.
