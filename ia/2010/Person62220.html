Edvard Toth’s Summary

★ Seasoned creative professional with an unusually rounded, exotic multi-disciplinary skillset, and a deep insight into multiple facets of interactive entertainment production on a variety of platforms.

★ In more recent years has been focusing on Flash development and design, social games and online media, but has more than 17 years of past history working on “traditional” videogames as well, including a string of high-profile, multi-million selling console titles.

★ Extensive experience creating, improving and managing nearly all forms of digital art ranging from pixel graphics to digital paintings to 3D models to vector illustration.

  • Flash Developer | Designer | Technical Art Director

    Flektor [Fox Interactive Media]

    (Public Company; NWS; Internet industry)

    January 2007 — January 2009 (2 years 1 month)

    During this foray into online media worked on real-time online mashup-editing technology and developed markedly visual, presentation-driven Flash applications and widgets for Flektor, MySpace, Photobucket, TinyPic and strategic projects like MyDebates.org, the official site of the 2008 Presidential Debates.

    This colorful process involved everything from extensive ActionScript3 programming to conceptualization, design and art/animation production using Flash, Flex, Illustrator, PhotoShop, AfterEffects, Papervision3D and Maya.

    In May 2007 Flektor got acquired by Fox Interactive Media.

  • Art Director

    Nival Interactive

    (Privately Held; Computer Games industry)

    June 2006 — October 2006 (5 months)

    Provided art direction - which often included hands-on involvement - to various overseas (primarily Chinese and Russian) outsourcing teams on several high-profile projects for clients like Electronic Arts or Sony.

    Created and developed concept art, illustrations, pre-visualization assets and presentation materials for original next-gen and handheld properties.

  • Senior Environment | Technical Artist

    Naughty Dog [Sony Computer Entertainment America]

    (Public Company; Computer Games industry)

    March 2004 — June 2006 (2 years 4 months)

    Environmental modeling, tools development and MEL scripting on Jak3 and JakX:Combat Racing. Modeled and designed the look of numerous highly complex, expansive environments.

    Created various tools, most notably several placement-utilities to aid the procedural distribution of instanced geometry, and the Maya portion of a "virtual motion-capture" system to apply motion-data recorded from physics-driven ingame vehicles back into hand-animated cinematics to achieve more realistic vehicular movement.

    Worked on the pre-production team for Uncharted:Drake's Fortune.

  • Principal Artist

    Heavy Iron Studios [THQ]

    (Public Company; Computer Games industry)

    July 2002 — March 2004 (1 year 9 months)

    Setting artistic and technical standards, environment and character modeling, texturing, lighting, special effects R&D, performance optimization, art-pipeline improvements, tools development / MEL scripting and art-staff training on The Incredibles (based on the Pixar feature) and Spongebob: Battle for Bikini Bottom.

  • Senior Artist

    Luxoflux [Activision]

    (Public Company; ATVI; Computer Games industry)

    January 1998 — July 2002 (4 years 7 months)

    Texturing and modeling environments, characters, vehicles, plus R&D, special effects and user-interface work on True Crime:Streets of L.A., Ghostworld (unpublished prototype), Star Wars: Demolition, Vigilante 8: Second Offense and Vigilante 8.
    Developed several incarnations of the company's website.

  • Managing Director

  • Managing | Creative Director

    Morbid Visions

    (Computer Games industry)

    1991 — 1994 (3 years )

    Concept, story, design, all artwork, art direction and project-management on the cult classic role-playing game Perihelion for the Commodore Amiga. The game was published by Psygnosis.

    Started working on this project at the tender age of 18, while still in school. The game eventually climbed to #9 on Amiga Action magazine's benchmark "Top 50 RPGs" list, and has an active, dedicated following to this day.
