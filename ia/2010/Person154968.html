Most people are pretty clear about what a copywriter does, less so with that of a Strategic Planner. For me, one is a subset of the other, and both need to know an awful lot about a client before they can do their job properly.

In the role of Strategic Planner I aim to solve any number of business and brand challenges for my clients. This always begins with listening, asking questions and independent research to ensure I fully understand their business, the category, competitors and landscape in which they currently or intend to operate in.

I then aim to find the business focus, identify core values, competitive positioning, ideal audiences, key messaging and optimum channels of communication. In addition to this I develop a written brief that brings together the attitudes and interests of both client and consumer, and provides the creative team with a platform of insight from which to seed their thinking.

Whether it is planning or writing, much of what I do is simply searching for the clues that will solve a client challenge, be it increasing sales, raising awareness, enhancing prestige, creating jobs, cutting costs or benefiting society or the environment.
