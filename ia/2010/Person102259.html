John Holmes’s Summary

I am one of the true pioneers of online sports media, having worked continuously on big-time golf Web sites since 1994, when I was one of the founders of golf.com. In fact, no one has worked in the online golf space longer than me. Since 2002, I have been with Turner Sports Interactive, a division of Time Warner that produces the official sites for the PGA Tour, Champions Tour, Nationwide Tour, the PGA of America, NASCAR and the NBA. I focus exclusively on the golf properties.

My background is journalism, but throughout my years in the online world I have really enjoyed exploring all the various methods of better reaching and serving our audiences beyond merely posting stories. At golf.com, for example, we created the first online golf shop, developed the first regularly scheduled online radio programming for golf, and even created the first major old media/news media partnership in golf through our at-the-time-groundbreaking equity partnerships with Golf Digest and NBC Sports.

News, analysis and columns remain a big part of what we do at PGATOUR.COM and PGA.COM – along, of course, with video highights and features, extensive audio programming, and more – but these days I am most excited about our ventures into social media. In early 2008, for example, I created the Twitter accounts for both PGATOUR.COM and PGA.COM, making these two of the very first major sports organizations to embrace this new outlet, and we also have developed a robust presence on both Facebook and YouTube. I’m eager to see where we go from here!

John Holmes’s Specialties:

Cool, quick content creation and production on deadline. Creating “big picture” editorial and production plans while handling day-to-day chores. Supervising diverse staff of writers and producers.

The semi-annual promotional magazine won several major national marketing and promotion awards, including 1987 Cable Television Advertising and Marketing Grand Prize, beating out such competitors as HBO and ESPN, and helped the start-up HTS network grow to more than 2 million subscribers.

  • Director of Editorial and Programming

    PGA Interactive

    (Online Media industry)

    1999 — 2002 (3 years )

    When I joined PGA Interactive in early 1999, its PGA.com Web site was essentially a start-up, and I played a major role in growing it into one of the Web’s biggest golf sites, with several million dollars in advertising, syndication and e-commerce revenue annually.

    I directed all aspects of our daily editorial operations as well as the editorial and multimedia coverage for the official sites of the PGA Championship and the Ryder Cup, which rank among the largest in sports. In addition, I worked with PGA event partners such as AOL, IBM and CBS to develop and coordinate content, technology and promotion for our major event sites.

    I also worked with our other directors and PGA officials to identify and develop business and content relationships, and to direct strategy and policy for advertising, marketing and public relations. In addition, I worked with our advertising director and clients such as Buick, Delta and Titleist to conceive, develop and create advertising campaigns.

  • Vice President/Editorial Director/Equity Partner

    Golf.Com

    (Online Media industry)

    1994 — 1999 (5 years )

    Literally founded in a basement in Arlington, Va., in 1994, golf.com was one of the first major sites on the Web and the only big golf site that remained independent and profitable until its sale in 1999. Among our many accomplishments were creating the first major old media/new media partnership in golf with NBC Sports and Golf Digest; producing the first official webcast of a major golf event for the 1995 Ryder Cup; and pioneering the online golf shop.

    As the site’s editor, I directed all aspects of our editorial coverage from long-range planning to daily operations for the multi-award-winning site that was the home of NBC’s golf coverage and Golf Digest and Golf World magazines. I also worked with Golf.com President Alex Miceli and our partners to develop and execute our strategy and policy for advertising, marketing, public relations and media relations.
