Sebastien Wagner’s Summary

My academic background is based on a deep knowledge in Applied Mathematics and Numerical Modelling, Computer Programming, and Computational Physics. After my graduation as an Ingénieur at the Institut des Sciences de l’Ingénieur de Toulon-Var, I oriented my work experience towards the design and the implementation of industrial numerical algorithms dedicated to Environmental Engineering and Geophysics.
I have been working on atmospheric processes since the year 2000. Thanks to my initial academic background, I was able to develop an expertise both in atmospheric modelling and remote sensing. I first joined the European Commission’s Joint Research Centre as a scientific officer within the Climate Change Unit where I worked for almost five years on air quality modelling. Since September 2004, I have been working at EUMETSAT, as a consultant in numerical algorithm development. I am currently in charge of developing and validating an aerosol product using the MSG/SEVIRI observations. This project benefits from the expertise I acquired as an atmospheric chemistry modeller, as I could make the link between the satellite products and their use by the modellers community. In parallel, I have initiated many interactions with potential users and with other scientific groups working on the retrieval of aerosol characteristics.
Looking at the future, this project is foreseen to be part of the core developments for the Meteosat Third Generation Flexible Combined Imager Mission.
Through my work experience I have acquired a solid experience in various fields of Earth Sciences and in particular in Atmospheric Sciences, together with a strong expertise in algorithm development in operational and industrial environments. This multifaceted expertise allowed me to bring people from different communities to work together and to create synergies. At last, my experience abroad, and in international environments enabled me to develop excellent communication and inter-personal skills.

Sebastien Wagner’s Specialties:

Numerical code development, applied to environmental modelling and remote sensing. Mathematical inversion and optimization methods, applied to geosciences. Resolution of partial differential equations using discretization methods (finite elements, finite volumes, finite differences).
UNIX/Linux environments, C, F90, IDL, Perl, parallel computing.

    (Government Agency; 201-500 employees; Government Administration industry)

    February 2000 — August 2004 (4 years 7 months)

    In the Climate Change / Emissions and Health Units, in charge of studying and developing methods of mesh embedding applied to the multiscale modelling of atmospheric processes. A multiscale air quality model dealing with gas chemistry and aerosol thermodynamics has been developed in order to couple regional scale with urban and local scales. The model has been applied to the ESCOMPTE area (Marseille - Etang de Berre). The formation and the destruction of tropospheric ozone was studied as well as the impact of anthropogenic emissions. The aim of such a work is to set abatement strategie to reduce emissions (European directive on ozone - Kyoto protocol).
    In the framework of a model co-development with the Climate Change Unit, I have been involved in the validation of an air quality model using optical thickness as a parameter to compare remote sensing data (retireved from MISR and MODIS instruments), AERONET data, and model results over the area of Milan and the Po Valley.
