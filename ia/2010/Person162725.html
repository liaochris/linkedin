Peter Frerichs’s Summary

I co-founded Pole to Pole Consulting, an international development and design firm combining professional research, writing, and program evaluation services with graphic design and corporate branding all under one roof.

I am also a Program Manager at the Boulder Institute of Microfinance. We are an executive education training institute for professionals working in the field of microfinance. We hold trainings around the world and count on the representation of faculty and participants from over 100 countries.

Peter Frerichs’s Specialties:

Project/Program Evaluation, Strategic Planning, Graphic/Web Design, Microfinance, Grant Writing, Market Research, Corporate Branding, Copywriting

  • Development Director

    Pole to Pole Consulting

    (Program Development industry)

    January 2008 — Present (2 years 5 months)

    As Development Director I supervise all grant-writing, market research, and project/program evaluation projects. Our grant-writing is geared towards helping non-profits leverage public and private funding to aid operations. We work with a range of non-profits, but specialize with U.S. based non-profits with international programming.

    Our market research and project/program evaluation services span the public and private sector as well. Recent on-going contracts have been with Yale University examining financial literacy, present-bias, and health and savings outcomes in Chilean Social Protection Programs and research and investigation for a microfinance training organization in Chile. We take on contracts around the world, and have a particular interest in Latin America as we are fluent in Spanish with considerable experience in the region.

    Our design department handles all graphic and web design as well as corporate branding projects. Our head designer, Sandra Busta, has over twelve years of experience working with private sector businesses primarily, and is a trained fine artist by trade.

  • Program Supervisor

We worked closely with city, state, and county government officials as well as local print, radio, and T.V. outlets.
