Conna Craig’s Summary

Fifteen years experience in the public policy arena, leading a think tank that advised media and policy makers of two administrations at The White House.

Researched and wrote policy blueprints for state and national leaders. Developed messaging that helped change how the media approach the issues of foster care and adoption. A seasoned public speaker, often key-note, invited to speak before a wide array of audiences in the U.S. and U.K.

Contributing author to academic books; guest columnist for major newspapers; appeared on local and national television as commentator and spokesperson.

Conna Craig’s Honors:

Profiled in The Leader Within You, by Robert Danzig (Lifetime Books, 1998) as one of 34 Americans – including Nelson Rockefeller, Lee Iacocca, Beverly Sills, John F. (Jack) Welch, Jr. – “who, together, define leadership.”

Awarded first annual Salvatori Prize for American Citizenship, recognizing “extraordinary efforts by American citizens who are helping their communities solve problems the government has been unable to solve” (1995).

Awarded honorary doctorate in social services, Caldwell College (2001).

Named one of “The New Social Architects,” including Millard Fuller who founded Habitat for Humanity and Phillip Martin, chief of the Choctaw Indians, in Policy Review (1997).
