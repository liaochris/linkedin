Robert Rosenstein’s Education

  • Technische Universität München

    Postdoctoral , Theoretical Chemistry , 1970 — 1972

    Activities and Societies:
    Researched chemical dynamics and molecular energy transfer. Published results, contributed to seminar presentation and organization, attended scientific conferences, and assisted Ph.D. candidates with thesis work.

  • Northwestern University

    Ph.D. , Physical Theoretical Chemistry , 1965 — 1970

    I have found during my adult life a dichotomy between theoretical and applied chemistry. I met past ACS President Dr. Catherine Hunt, at the March, 2007 National ACS Meeting Chicago, and later in Feb., 2008 at NU Dept.Chemistry Charles Hurd Lecture Series. Dr. Hunt enunciated specific goals I fully share as one component of my career focus. Viz., elimination of this dichotomy to prevent stagnation in industrial chemistry/chemical engineering and advancing American competitiveness in the global economy.

    The Annual NU Lecture Series honors the late Professor Hurd, well known to me, for his recognition of a perspective, not widely shared at that time. That perspective highlighted the need for feedback between indusry and academe. Having in 2008 Dr.Catherine Hunt as a lecturer was a fitting tribute to Hurd's memory.

    Past American Physical Society President Dr. Cherry Murray enunciated at a NU nanotechnology forum 2008 identical goals for the discipline of physics.

    Activities and Societies:
    NIH Predoctoral Fellow due to purported application of thesis to stability of genetic code. Graduate work focused on physical chemistry, physics,and applied mathematics. Thesis modeled proton transfer dynamics in condensed matter. Thesis and subsequent publication were cited in the literature for over twenty years. Other activities included presentation of physical chemistry seminars, instruction assistance, candidacy and the final oral examination in defense of the thesis project.

  • Western Reserve University

    Admitted to doctoral program , Physical and Inorganic Chemistry , 1961 — 1964

    In one of the classes I took there at Western Reserve in statistical thermodynamics, the faculty member lamented that there was then no microscopic perspective on the transition state theory of chemical reactions rates. Later, I accomplished at Northwestern and Technical University Muenchen ,through focus on non-equilibrium statistical mechanics, the goal of developing that microscopic framework for the transition state theory.
    Berichte Bunsengesellschaft fuer Physikalische Chemie, 77, 493(1973).

    The earlier inorganic chemistry instruction at Western Reserve gave me first exposure to storage of hydrogen in condensed matter in the form of metallic hydrides. This issue has interested me for decades especially now in the context of the need to develop renewable energy in transportation applications to supplant the gasoline engines and mitigate against carbon dioxide emissions that impact adversely global climate resiliency.

    Activities and Societies:
    Took classes in evening while working full time in aerospace technology at NASA
    Cleveland,Ohio.

  • University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign

    B.S. , LAS with focus on chemistry, physics, mathematics , 1956 — 1961

    Financed 50% of undergraduate expenses. Graduated with honors in LAS (liberal arts and sciences). The positive undergraduate experiences I had with the three disciplines chemistry, physics, and mathematics motivated me to pursue later a Ph.D. program in physical theoretical chemistry.

    Returned to campus in 2002 as volunteer on a committee assisting set up the Noyes Lab Centennial Celebration . Attended later that year the Centennial.
