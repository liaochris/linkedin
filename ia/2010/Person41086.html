Clarence Harris’s Summary

Clarence B. Harris is also the President and CEO of Diversity International Television Network of Atlanta, Georgia. Bishop Harris has been an educator and administrator in several of our nation’s public school systems and served as an organizational sales manager for the Southwestern Publishing, Company in Nashville, Tennessee from 1976 to 1980. He was Miami-Dade County Florida's Mainstream Teacher-of-the-Year in 1991 and has taught Psychology, Sociology, Religion, History, and Television Production in both Florida and Georgia. He has been a non-denominational pastor training church leaders globally. Bishop Harris is an established international speaker, civil rights leader, and community activist for over thirty years. He is the founder of Alpha Omega Productions, Inc. (www.alphaomegapro.org), a non-profit Christian organization specializing in video documentaries, publications, and international family-based and multicultural seminars.

Bishop Harris is a graduate of IBI CEO Space of Los Angeles, California (2007) and the founder of Christian Fellowship Assemblies (in 1982). As a graduate of the University of Miami (Florida), Bishop Harris majored in History and Religion and minored in Biology and Chemistry. He served as a chaplain at the South Dade Federal Corrections Prison, of Miami, Florida in 1979. In 1980 he serves as a Chaplain for the University of the District of Columbia and Howard University in Washington DC.. He also founded the Open Bible Institute for Christian Apologetics in Coral Hills, Maryland in 1984. Bishop Harris has authored five books which address how anti-Semitism and racism has effected church theology and Western interpretations of Bible Prophecy.

His long term goals include the increase of literacy in America and in Third-World countries in order to develop critical thinking skill for multicultural global community.
