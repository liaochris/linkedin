Edward Sax’s Summary

Edward Sax has been a marketing executive for over 25 years. His career began with General Electric’s Semiconductor Division and has since spanned several technical and non-technical industries, including electronic components, semi-custom integrated circuits, software, comedy entertainment, children’s edu-tainment, and most recently, mobile marketing. He has an electrical engineering degree from Lafayette College.

Mr. Sax has been married since 1986 and has two daughters, ages 20 and 12, one of whom seems to enjoy changing her major. He has traveled extensively, enjoys duplicate bridge, softball and volleyball, and moonlights as a professional comedy juggler.

After several years with GE at Research Triangle Park, Mr. Sax relocated to Honolulu to become the founder, owner and operator of a chain of stand-up comedy nightclubs in the Hawaiian Islands. To this day it is still regarded the longest running and most successful comedy operation in the history of the state. After eight years, the business sold and he moved back to the U.S. Mainland where he took some (well-deserved) time off and then accepted a series of offers from both technical and non-technical companies in a variety of industries. Included are marketing positions with MoldWorks - a SolidWorks-based Computer-Aided 3D Mold & Die Design software, and WaveStation - radio automation and audio production software from Broadcast Software International.

Mr. Sax came through General Electric’s Technical Marketing Program and has completed courses and seminars in Negotiating Skills, Public Speaking, Marketing Management, Management Logic, and Advanced Leadership Strategies. He is originally from Syosset, Long Island, a suburb of New York City, and is currently home based, in northeast Mississippi near Memphis, TN.
