Renee White’s Summary

Renee has held leadership roles >25yrs in medicine, finance and business with expertise identifying disruptive life science technologies for commercialization.
• Previously dual-careered licensed in both medicine and finance
• Works closely with venture capital investors, device engineers, M.D./Ph.D. entrepreneurs and inventors.
• Strengths include executive operations, strategic commercialization planning, detailed project management, financial portfolio management and investment decisions, and business development.

Her medical career began as part of the early artificial heart research team affiliated with the Texas Heart Institute; Graduated from Baylor College of Medicine as a P.A. and practiced general Internal Medicine in Houston, Texas - with her own clinic of 650 patients, managed a multi-year FDA clinical research trial and directed construction of a rural health clinic in Needville, Texas. In 1998 attended The University of Texas’ IC2 Institute, receiving a MSc degree in Science & Technology Commercialization.

Renee also has life-long sophisticated financial market and professional money management expertise. Founded a NASD registered firm Emerald Street Market Advisors, LLC for “fee-and-success-based” portfolio management of private client funds and trust accounts. She was the first female financial writer 1-3x/wk for an online website teaching technical analysis, trading skills, options, and evaluating corporate and economic news.

In 2005, she founded Caduceus Technology Partners, LLC a life science commercialization management and product development firm providing contract services for medical device technologies and international clients who seek assistance expanding into the USA.

Renee has been a guest Judge and panel member at national and international venture capital competitions and sits on the Life Science Review Committee for the Gulf Coast regional division of the $200M Texas Emerging Technology Fund.
