Roger Cunningham’s Summary

Roger leads the Business Intelligence, Process Management, Global Value Chain and Supply Chain Strategy work at Idhasoft, Ltd. His work focuses on Source Based and Demand Based consumer products and service companies in Asia, Europe, Middle East and Americas. In his tenure he has developed and implemented operations strategies for over a third of the nation's top 500 retailers, manufacturers and wholesalers. As an operations and IT management consultancy, Idhasoft obviously excels at change; but moreover, Roger's team believes that landmark change needs to occur in the Value Chain disciplines.

Supply Chains are not about the trucks, boxes, facilities containers and ships employed in logistics networks. To that end, as a first business priority, Supply Chain is not about efficiency, but rather is about selecting that service and cost channel which lends support to the greatest achievable Net Margin, Markdown Avoidance, and Brand Strength position. This margin based Value Chain alignment does not always involve the cheapest channel of operations, but rather is an orchestrated decision set targeting maximum business effectiveness, not efficiency. Today, CPG companies are broaching the understanding that margin optimized value chains are wholly different than efficiency based supply chains.

In the previous decades we as Supply Chain practitioners almost "efficiently" put middle tier retail out of business; recommending consumer products companies adopt poor business practices in order to save on expenses. In many cases not even realizing, that in reality the "savings" were simply a repositioning of costs, or could even endanger the business. The net result has been both an over-shift of profitability offshore, and a massive middle tier CG wealth transfer into the hands of a few sourcing nations.

Our academic and consulting partners were complicit in this mis-direction.

Our clients say: "Idhasoft gets it"
