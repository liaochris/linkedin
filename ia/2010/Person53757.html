Debra A. Harkins’s Summary

Debra Harkins is an executive coach and consultant, specializing in diversity, leadership development, women’s growth and non-profit human service organizations. In 2007, she founded Leading Change, a leadership development coaching and consulting firm. Debra’s leadership model includes five basic areas: empowerment, vision and systems thinking, emotional intelligence, strength based communication, and relationship building. She has worked with C-suite exectuives, supervisors, managers, and directors — both men and women — from small to large organizations.

A skilled consultant and workshop leader, Debra has led diversity, leadership, communication, customer service, strategic restructuring, management, negotiation workshops and retreats for many organizations including: Coalition for a United Nations, Organizational Development Learning Group, American Psychological Association, Executive Service Corp of New England, Battered Women’s shelters, American Red Cross, YMCA, Women at Work, Healthcare of Southeastern Massachusetts, Riverside Community Care, Homeless Empowerment Program, Melville Candy Company, Candy.com, Massachusetts Down Syndrome Congress, La Allianza Hispana, Winchester Multicultural Network, Emerald Necklace Conservancy, and Neighborhood Action.

During her career she co-founded a manufacturing business, has held management positions in academia and worked in nonprofit organizations. She is an Associate professor of psychology at Suffolk University and is a licensed psychologist in Massachusetts. Debra is a Certified Empowerment Coach, a social justice mediator and a board member of Oasis Coalition of Boston.
