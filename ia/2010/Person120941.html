Landon Hall’s Summary

I have more than 14 years' experience in journalism, including nearly a decade at The Associated Press, where I was a legislative reporter, sports writer and online editor. I'm now seeking to transfer my skills to the public relations industry in the Bay Area. I want to keep writing, learning and educating about the important issues of the day, and I'm particularly interested in government affairs, technology and the environment.

Landon Hall’s Specialties:

Writing about a variety of subjects, including sports, entertainment, international issues and science and medicine; media relations; interviewing; translating complex ideas into easy-to-understand precepts; collaborating on a creative team.

Landon Hall’s Experience

  • Online Editor

    Associated Press

    (Non-Profit; Writing and Editing industry)

    June 2003 — October 2006 (3 years 5 months)

    This was the best job I've ever had, because I worked with a very talented, fun-loving crew, and we took pride in delivering breaking news to online users faster and in a more comprehensive way than any other news organization. Essentially, we took copy that moved on the print wire and jazz it up for Internet readers, adding photos, video, graphics, audio and rewriting the headlines to make them sing. We'd manage and update various queues that had the top stories of the day separated by subject -- U.S. news, politics, international, sports, entertainment, business, technology and health. Often we would have to rewrite the tops of stories to make them more palatable for an online audience, or combine multiple stories into a single cohesive one, or craft a new lede based on the info we had. My specialty was international news. Every day was a different adventure. I worked with the best reporters, editors, graphic artists and photographers around the world.

  • Sports Writer

    Associated Press

    (Non-Profit; Writing and Editing industry)

    June 1998 — June 2003 (5 years 1 month)

    I was promoted to Sports Editor in the Portland, Ore., bureau of AP when Bob Baum left to take over in Phoenix. My main focus was the never-dull Portland Trail Blazers, who required almost daily coverage. I also covered Oregon and Oregon State, particularly football and basketball. I went to two Fiesta Bowls, following the Beavers' 11-1 season in 2000 and the Ducks' 11-1 campaign in 2001 -- a one-two punch Oregonians may never see again. I loved covering different sports, such as women's soccer and basketball, track and field, auto racing and golf -- and interviewing athletes and telling their stories. I also covered news, including courts, the environment, political races and state government. The most fun story I covered was a Hillsboro man who bought an old jetliner and had it moved to the woods so he could convert it into his new home. "Space-age bachelor pad," I called it. Working in this great AP bureau taught me the basics of how to be a seasoned journalist.

Landon Hall’s Education

  • Columbia University in the City of New York

    MFA , Writing/Nonfiction , 2003 — 2008

    The Columbia Writing Program was a great experience for me, and undoubtedly helped me become a better writer and editor. The two-year curriculum is grueling: Not only workshops but reading a book a week in three different classes. Profs like Richard Locke, Lis Harris, Patty O'Toole, Stephen O'Connor, Michael Scammell and Mark Slouka are brilliant, and they opened up a world of literature and non-sentimental writing that was previously unknown to me.

    Activities and Societies:
    I was working part-time at AP while I was in grad school, so it was a very busy, yet exhilarating, time. I enjoyed writing my submissions for workshop, and talking about the craft of writing -- as well as art and culture -- with my classmates.

  • University of Wyoming

    BA , Journalism , 1987 — 1992

    With the exception of the bitter cold, I'd recommend the U. of Wyoming to anyone. I often refer to Laramie as the Berkeley of Wyoming, because of its vibrant cultural and artisan scene. One of my favorite things to do was attend foreign and indie films at the Wyo Theater every Sunday night. Although the journalism program was on the wane then, some of the best teachers I've ever had came in other classes, like beginning acting, philosophy and American lit. Thanks, Chris Durer, for introducing me to Jack London's "Martin Eden."

    Activities and Societies:
    I spent most of my time at the school paper, the Branding Iron, where I worked as a reporter, features editor and arts writer for four years. I was also a proud member of the Pi Kappa Alpha fraternity, which provided a social element that I needed, having been a shy, awkward kid from a small Wyoming town.
