John Czwartacki’s Summary

As an executive who -- for 20-plus years -- has been successfully leading teams through some of the most demanding communications and public affairs challenges, I pride myself on an expertise that delivers valuable outcomes. Whether briefing C-Suite executives on strategy, managing a media crisis within a reporter "scrum" in the White House driveway, or bringing ahead-of-the-curve tactics to a 100 year-old Fortune 500 company, I’ve built a record of results.

Recent accomplishments:
• Proposed and built one of the nation’s first official corporate blogs and Twitter beachheads, after identifying the then-still emerging importance of social media.
• Prepared for the lowered barriers to consumer produced visual communication by creating active and low cost video channels for company engagement.
• Recognized the end of one-way, monolithic communications and earned a widely-respected reputation as someone who intuitively understands the right way to navigate this new era of on-line influencers and highly connected communities.

I’m also a frequent panelist and speaker before industry groups and government agencies, such as the National Governors Association, the State of the Net Conference, the National Security Agency, and multinationals like Vodaphone, and have been a featured guest on CNN, Fox News Channel, and C-SPAN.

John Czwartacki’s Specialties:

Crisis communications, social media, on-line engagements, comunications campaigns, stakeholder management, blogs and mirco-blogging, on-line video, reputation management, public policy and communications.

John Czwartacki’s Experience

  • Executive Director -- External Communications

    Verizon

    (Public Company; VZ; Telecommunications industry)

    January 2003 — Present (7 years 10 months)

    Currently, I am an executive director for external communications at Verizon, America's leading broadband and communications company. There, I lead a number of social media efforts, including overseeing the blog I proposed, designed, and built, PolicyBlog, one of the first official blogs by a leading Fortune 500 company. I explore and explain changing communications landscape to senior executives, as well as expertly use these new communications channels, such as blogging, micro-blogging, and video, to further company goals.

  • Adjunct Professor, School of Media and Public Affairs

    George Washington University

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    January 2002 — December 2008 (7 years )

    Taught, once each academic year, a course titled "Government Information and Public Affairs" to undergraduates studying political communications.

  • Director, Public Affairs

    Federal Emergency Management Agency

    (Government Agency; Government Administration industry)

    September 2001 — December 2002 (1 year 4 months)

    After a stint in the private sector at Greener and Hook public service called again, this time on September 11, 2001.

    I was asked to return to the government the week of the attacks, and became the Director of Public Affairs for the agency charged with leading America’s domestic response and recovery from the attacks.

    After a year of anthrax, “undisclosed locations,” and managing media stories from “ground zero,” the Pentagon, and Shanksville, PA, I left the agency.

  • Partner

    Greener & Hook

    (Public Relations and Communications industry)

    November 2000 — September 2001 (11 months)

    As partner, I helped this highly regarded boutique communications and public affairs firm assist clients in sectors spanning the adult beverage industry to banking. From media training to strategic communications counsel, to new business development, I touched it all.

  • Press Secretary

    Office of the Senate Majority Leader

    (Public Policy industry)

    March 1998 — November 2000 (2 years 9 months)

    In 1998 I was tapped to cross the Capitol Rotunda and become the Senate Majority Leader’s press secretary. The defining challenge? Managing the media covering the historic impeachment trial of President Bill Clinton. In addition to regular duties of the Senate’s top press job, I was charged with the helping shape one of the biggest political stories of the 1990’s.

  • Commications Director/National Spokesman

    U.S. House of Representatives, Rep. Bill Paxon

    (Government Agency; Legislative Office industry)

    February 1997 — March 1998 (1 year 2 months)

    Named as a top advisor and communications director for Rep. Bill Paxon in 1998, the congressman soon found himself at the center of an alleged “coup attempt” against a then-sitting House Speaker Newt Gingrich. As his chief press guy I helped lead Rep. Paxon’s improbable rise to become the odds-on favorite to be a future Speaker of the House.

  • Director, State Media

    Dole-Kemp 1996

    (Public Policy industry)

    September 1996 — November 1996 (3 months)

    Served as liaison to and between each of 1996 GOP presidential ticket's state and regional press operations and its national headquarters-based leadership team.

  • Press Secretary, Republican Conference

    U.S. House of Representatives

    (Government Agency; Legislative Office industry)

    January 1995 — August 1996 (1 year 8 months)

    By 1995, I was still with Rep. Boehner but was now serving as the spokesman for the Republican Conference he now chaired. There I was responsible for assisting my fellow press secretaries, many of them new to the job in the GOP’s 1st congressional majority in 40 years, with message and media strategy.

  • Press Secretary

    U.S. Representative John Boehner (OH)

    (Public Policy industry)

    April 1992 — December 1994 (2 years 9 months)

    I joined then-freshman rising star, Rep. John Boehner, as the then-youngest press secretary on Capitol Hill. There, I helped shape some early moments of his early DC career, including his effort to successfully add the XXVII Amendment to the US Constitution, which placed limits congressional pay rasies, in 1992.

  • Public Affairs Specialist

    U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Develpoment

    (Government Agency; Government Administration industry)

    April 1989 — March 1992 (3 years )

    Working for a childhood hero while attending college, I served in the press office as a paid staffer for the late-HUD Secretary Jack Kemp’s successful navigation of the housing department’s clean-up from its scandals of the late 1980s, and the administration’s response to the 1991 LA riots.
