Jens Jonason’s Summary

I am a User Experience Designer who has been working professionally in the field for over ten years. As a dedicated team player, I am invested in the design, development, and business success of interactive products. My professional goal is to design great user experiences that are usable, efficient, and enjoyable.

Jens Jonason’s Specialties:

Brainstorm Facilitation, Design Rationale, Agile Processes (Scrum), Remote Collaboration, Web Analytics.

Visio, OmniGraffle, Illustrator, Photoshop, DreamWeaver, InDesign, wiki (TWiki), Adobe Connect, Skype, HTML, JavaScript.

Bilingual: English, Swedish.

Jens Jonason’s Experience

  • Director of Product Design

    Loud3r Inc.

    (Privately Held; Internet industry)

    February 2010 — Present (1 month)

  • Staff Interaction Designer

    Yahoo!

    (Public Company; YHOO; Internet industry)

    July 2005 — January 2010 (4 years 7 months)

    Lead interaction designer for a data-intensive, global Yahoo! Sports site currently in development.

    Created interaction design for celebrity news site omg! (omg.yahoo.com). The innovative, simplified, and patented design allowed for fast development within a 6-month time to market window. The design afforded new advertising integrations that helped the sales team exceed annual revenue goals. U.S. Patent D596,644 "Graphical user interface for a display screen", issued July 2009.

    Created interaction design for a modular blogging platform that enabled the sales team to create higher-value, custom advertising packages. Helped create a content management system that simplified editors’ workflows and increased their productivity.

    Redesigned Yahoo! TV site using Rapid Iterative Testing and Evaluation (RITE) techniques that enabled the early exploration and validation of design concepts.

    Pioneered Wiki-based, modular design documentation, leading to faster revision turnarounds for designers, and easier-to-use design specifications for developers.

    Hosted public User Experience events at the Santa Monica office, showcasing the company and its involvement with the Los Angeles talent pool of User Experience Designers.

    Promoted from Senior Interaction Designer to Staff Interaction Designer. Managed three designers on the Entertainment team.

  • Experience Architect

    Zentropy Partners / MRM Worldwide

    (Privately Held; Internet industry)

    July 2003 — July 2005 (2 years 1 month)

    Created functional requirements, site maps, process flows, and wireframes for both consumer-facing experiences and content management systems for clients such as Microsoft, WestCoast Hospitality Corp., and NADA Guides.

    Collaborated with account managers and account planners to improve the definition of project scope and product features. As a result, clients received more accurate estimates, the team had achievable expectations, and the company saved money by not exceeding the billable hours for specific projects.

    Created a transparent change management system to mitigate the project risks of design change requests made by clients and internal stakeholders. Made the requests, requestor, and rationale transparent to the entire team, which drove up the relevance of requests and made it easier to prioritize important design changes.

  • Interaction desinger (independent contractor)

    (Online Media industry)

    Created process flows, wireframes, and design style-guide for the user registration process of a proposed web site for 24 Hour Fitness. Updated design specifications after a mid-project requirements gathering workshop while meeting the original project deadline.

    Created process flows, wireframes, and design style-guide for the U.S.-version of a Japanese horoscope site – Charanavi – as part of a distributed, virtual team of contractors.

    Designed a user interface for a point-of-sale system for Teleflora.

    Relocated from Sweden to United States as a permanent resident (green card holder; authorized to work in the United States for any employer).

  • Principal Interaction Designer

    Spray / Lycos Europe

    (Online Media industry)

    June 1999 — July 2002 (3 years 2 months)

    Designed User Experience for several market-leading, consumer-facing products including webmail, classified ads, and home page builder application.

    Built an in-house usability test lab for rapid, iterative design and prototyping. The usability lab allowed for more frequent and informal testing of interactive products and applications at a lower cost than external labs.

    Edited the company-wide development process documentation in addition to authoring the User Experience process section. Titled “Organic,” the development process established a shared framework for the connection between User Experience design activities and engineering development. As a result, inter-disciplinary collaboration was smoother and project plans were more accurate.

    Coached twenty interaction designers in the Stockholm, Copenhagen and Gütersloh offices to make design quality more consistent across multi-site, international projects.

  • Writer

    Datamedia

    1994 — 1996 (2 years )
