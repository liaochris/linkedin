John Speweik’s Summary

John Speweik, CSI is a historic masonry preservation specialist based in Chicago. He is an author, traditional building investigator, and brick mason, from a family of masons, dating back to 1870 in Posen, Prussia.

John works with architects, engineers building owners, and government agencies as a building investigator and architectural adviser to the Secretary of the Interior's Standards for Rehabilitation. Speweik has written a book and many publications on historic masonry. He is the co-author of "Preservation Briefs 2:"Repointing Mortar Joints in Historic Masonry Buildings," published by the National Park Service, Historic Preservation Services in Washington DC. He is an active member of ASTM and recently announced the new ASTM C1713 Standard Specification for Mortars for the Repair of Historic Masonry.

John Speweik’s Specialties:

historic lime putty mortar on-site training programs, project specific historic preservation technical support, ASTM material testing, project specification reviews, condition assessments, building investigations, building repair, building restoration, building preservation, architectural design, construction materials, historic material matching, material testing, onsite investigations, project inspections, building code compliance, stone reconstruction, brick masonry restoration

  • Historic Masonry Preservation Specialist, VP, Co-Founder

    U.S. Heritage Group, Inc.

    (Building Materials industry)

    March 1997 — February 2009 (12 years )

    Was responsible for architectural support services and hands-on traditional masonry workshop programs. Worked primarily as a fee-based consultant to city, state and federal government architects in the restoration of historic masonry buildings constructed prior to 1932. My work involved collaboration between building owner and architect in the pre-planning phase with initial building investigations to determine the scope of work and proposed budgets. Mortar, plaster, stucco, and brick and stone masonry and terra cotta matching was integrated into these services as a turn key approach to quality control. Workmanship quality was established during the specified onsite training when the field mock up(s) were applied or installed. During the construction phase the mason contractor was re-trained, if needed, when in-progress inspections were carried out with the architect or building owner or government agency. Active in ASTM committees, industry groups and conference speaker and author.
