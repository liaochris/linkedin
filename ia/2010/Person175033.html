Robert Mustard’s Summary

I’ve begun a new career in Biology, including doing extensive field and lab work and participating in analyzing results and writing research papers, as well as teaching college-level biology courses to new Biology majors. This new path in biology and the environmental sciences is built upon a foundation of over 20 years’ experience in Information Technologies, creating and implementing new applications, developing and fulfilling sales and consulting opportunities, and hiring and developing key sales-support and consulting services personnel.

Robert Mustard’s Experience

  • Adjunct Professor

    Palomar College

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    September 2007 — Present (2 years 7 months)

    I teach one of the sections of Biology 201, Foundations of Biology II. I am responsible for developing all course materials (lecture and lab), both electronic and hardcopy resources, as well as knitting together support materials from other, online sources (e.g., scientific journals, other universities, etc.). I find the experience of working with individual students to be very rewarding; helping someone to make sense of very abstract concepts -- as well as being able to apply these concepts to contemporary issues -- is critical to the development of these young Biology majors.

  • Research Assistant

    California State University, San Marcos

    (Higher Education industry)

    June 2004 — August 2007 (3 years 3 months)

    This position was in support of NSA grant to Dr. George Vourlitis of California State University, San Marcos. Duties included gathering soil and plant samples from research sites in Southern California native plant communities; using instruments in the field to measure soil respiration and temperature; running experiments in the lab to assess soil chemistry (pH, total carbon and nitrogen, N taken up in plants and soil bacteria). Also, modified described procedures to measure soil moisture, water holding capacity and microbial biomass metrics. Produced documentation for standard practices in the lab, including Kjeldahl methods and assessment of soil moisture, microbial biomass, and use of the elemental (CHN) analyzer.

  • Lab Instructor

    California State University, San Marcos

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    September 2004 — May 2005 (9 months)

    As a graduate student, I was responsible for teaching two semesters of the lab section of Biology 210 (Introduction to Cellular and Molecular Biology), working alongside Dr. Thomas Wahlund. I contributed to portions of later editions of Dr. Wahlund's text for the course, and also developed the Lab Instructor's Guide for the course.

  • Program Manager

    Software AG of North America, Inc.

    (Public Company; WEBM; Computer Software industry)

    October 1983 — September 1999 (16 years )

    Performed a variety of roles in my career with Software AG, building successful relationships with new-name accounts as well as with existing customers. Technologies include Software AG's core products (Data Management and Application Engineering), and emerging technologies (Middleware, Distributed Systems, and Data Warehouse and Enterprise Integration solutions).

Robert Mustard’s Education

    Received my Masters of Science degree for my studies in Microbial Ecology. Title of thesis: Responses of Nitrifying Microbes in Southern California Native Plant Communities to Additions of Nitrogen.
    GRE Biology subject test taken in November 2004. Score: 830; Percentile: 94th.

  • DePaul University

    BA , Environmental Studies , 1999 — 2001

    Received Bachelor's of Arts degree in June 2001.
    Research Seminar: An exploration of the characteristics of environmentally-friendly residential developments (“conservation communities”), and the reasons why people would want to live in those carefully-controlled communities.
    Externship: An investigation of the methods and incentives for restoring damaged and discarded industrial land (“brownfields”), helping it to be reclaimed for use as commercial or residential space, public parkland, or for agriculture.
    Capstone: An examination of the potential difficulties ahead for a booming economy (the Republic of Ireland) in a geographically-constrained, energy-poor environment – with proposals for development of alternate energy sources.
    Major Piece: Creation of a “white paper” recommending measures for preventing the creation of distressed properties, maintaining the land for positive, productive and beneficial use in the community. Winner of 2001 DePaul University Award for Excellence.
