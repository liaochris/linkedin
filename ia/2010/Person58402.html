Donald Newell’s Summary

• Sr. Principal Engineer and Director of SoC Platform Architecture and Datacenter Networking Architecture groups. Talented at building and retaining strong, highly motivated teams. Management leadership scores regularly some of the highest at Intel.

• Proven ability to take research all the way to product intercept. This includes driving SoC innovations, IOAT, a server networking innovation, and QoS technologies from initial research to product intercept.

• Author on 60+ refereed publications including HPCA, MICRO and other first tier conferences, 20+ patents filed. Invited keynotes and seminars given at ISPASS, Stanford, etc. Directed team that published 100+ papers, including seminal papers in QoS, and filed dozens of patents over the last 8 years.

• Intel On-line Services (IOS) was Intel’s data center hosting service. Developed the technical requirements and led the initial development of AppChoice, the most successful service sold by IOS.

• Data-talks-loudest management style. Demonstrated capability of finding the right people to work with regardless of level.

• Offered significant strategic and technical mentorship or directly managed teams in California, China, Germany, Mexico and Russia. Was key to developing technical programs with these geos that led to products such as Intel’s Quick Assist. Managed teams in Oregon, California, Russia and Mexico.

• 7 Division Recognition Awards in time at Intel.
