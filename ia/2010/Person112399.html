Katie Fairchild’s Summary

Since I was in my early twenties, I have always been in sales. I started off selling insurance supplements to Medicare, then worked for NCOA (Non Commissioned Officers Association) explaining military benefits and selling their Life Insurance Program. After being with NCOA only a few years, I was promoted to Manager.

I also sold new and used cars for awhile. After that, I bought a Property and Casualty Insurance Agency, which was only bringing in a profit of $20,000 a year. After 2 years, the profit of the Agency went up to $120,000 a year.

When my mother became ill in 2000, I became involved in care giving, and realizing how important this is, continued my role, yet in more specialized areas.
