Mark Button’s Summary

Over 15 years in PR in both in-house and agency roles for well-known brands (e.g. BTopenworld, HP, Pirelli, Caterpillar, Eastern Electricity, SAFC/Sigma-Aldrich) as well as start-ups and not-for-profit organizations.

Every employee of a company - from the CEO down - communicates an image of that organization, and so can influence choice and preference. Everyone has a stake. In an ultra-competitive age when many products are ubiquitous, the choice is usually vast and the amount and type of information bombarding audiences ever increasing, what REALLY makes one brand preferential to another?

The simple answer is - image, perception and reputation.

Classic example: Apple. In my mind, an absolute genius of a company. Not only desirable with a great 'cool' factor, but one of the very few companies that actually continues to define its own markets and invent new ones. But we all can't be Apple, so we have to get creative. It's all very well having being the best thing since sliced bread, but if you cannot communicate this effectively to your target audiences, there's really no point being in business!

A solid PR program dovetails with countless other spokes on the marketing wheel and becomes a true extension of a client's business. It's a two-way understanding so also key is encouraging clients to 'think PR' and develop an appreciation for what PR is, how it operates and how it is fed. Where good PR really earns its money? By taking a client and packaging what it has to say in such a way that it's of interest to selected audiences. It's also where key relationships come into play. Know your market and the editors who cover it.

PR 2.0 and social media define a new way to market as the dissemination of information quickens via multiple channels. The challenge facing businesses now is how to get a handle on these new routes to customers and leverage them in such as way as to add value to the brand. PR must keep in step with this.
