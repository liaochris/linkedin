Eric Riback’s Summary

Versatile sales professional with broad business skills. Experienced in map and book publishing, outdoor recreation industry and broadcasting.

Eric Riback’s Specialties:

B2B sales and sales management, marketing communications, channel marketing, business development, proposals, product development, packaging, competitive intelligence. Knowledgeable about finance and operations.

Eric Riback’s Experience

  • Independent Sales Representative and Consultant

    Riback & Company

    (Privately Held; Consumer Goods industry)

    June 2009 — Present (10 months)

    Independent sales representative in the map, book, gift and outdoor channels. Consultant to book and map publishers on sales, marketing and product development.

  • Vice President, Sales

    National Geographic Maps

    (Publishing industry)

    January 2001 — June 2009 (8 years 6 months)

    This renowned division of the National Geographic Society publishes wall maps, outdoor recreation maps and map software. As Director of Sales, I significantly increased sales of maps to other divisions of NGS, developed original and customized products for them, and more than doubled profit margins on those sales. I revamped and expanded our international distribution and licensing. Promoted to Vice President, in addition to $2 million in personal sales, I managed a team of 5 selling into the retail channel.

  • Director of Sales & Marketing

    MapQuest

    (Publishing industry)

    January 1997 — March 2001 (4 years 3 months)

    Before the launch of its well-known web site, Mapquest was a producer of custom maps and geographic data. In 1996 it partnered with the National Geographic Society (for which I wrote the proposal) to distribute their maps and create a line of branded map products. I played a key role in launching the National Geographic line worldwide, including product development, marketing communication and operations.

    I built distribution in niche retail, mass market and newsstand outlets; created a successful sales program in the promotional products industry; proposed and launched the company’s online map store. I managed a team of 5 that had reached $4 million in annual sales when Mapquest exited the publishing business.

  • Consultant

    Riback & Company

    (Marketing and Advertising industry)

    April 1995 — December 1996 (1 year 9 months)

    I authored the successful proposal for Mapquest to partner with the National Geographic Society; performed market research for Thomas Brothers Maps; helped clients evaluate potential acquisitions; created a customer survey for a retailer; conducted competitive shopping research that addressed shortcomings in a spiral staircase manufacturer’s sales process.

  • Director of Sales & Marketing

    DeLorme

    (Privately Held; Computer Software industry)

    March 1980 — March 1995 (15 years 1 month)

    Starting as a one-person sales and marketing department for a regional Maine publisher, I built a team of 18 as we expanded nationally and grew sales 40x during my time there. I established distribution in all mass market, office supply, outdoor sports and bookstore chains; created a national telesales program selling to independent stores (each rep’s annual sales average: $1 million); launched a direct-store-delivery program with 500 accounts in our home market; opened and managed DeLorme’s retail store.

    As Director of Marketing for the combined print and digital products groups, I led an in-house publicity staff of 2 that delivered results worthy of a national agency and developed trade promotion programs for national accounts. I authored the business plan for the first product linking digital maps to GPS navigation satellites in real-time.

  • Account Manager

    Portland, Maine Radio Stations

    (Broadcast Media industry)

    1977 — 1980 (3 years )

    Sold local radio advertising to independent retailers. Depending on the customer, used demographics, station affinity and spec spots to win buys. Wrote and produced most of my own commercials.

  • General Manager

    Progressive Radio Network

    (Broadcast Media industry)

    1975 — 1977 (2 years )

    Managed sales and operations for this producer of syndicated radio features. Tripled sales in the first year.
