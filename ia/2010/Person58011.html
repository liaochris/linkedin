Donald Porter’s Summary

In spite of my time spend in management, as an instructor, or as an author, I still think of myself as a "network guy". I consider myself a "hands on" manager and remain facinated by the technology. Early in my career, I thought that if I could "see" the packets on the wire I would better understand how the applications were working. Imagine my joy at discovering the Sniffer, I still have my first protocols poster hanging in the basement! After all, this was not art or biology, this was engineering. Everything could be explained and understood. Everything was deliberate (except the bugs of course, which were accidental and could be fixed). Every one and zero meant something specific. Throughout my career, I have never lost that interest in the technical details.
This interest has grown into a desire to understand how large, complex, dynamic systems can be effectively managed. I began to understand how financial, business, and even human dynamics affect technical systems. I don't think I ever stopped thinking like an engineer (I'm not sure I ever will at this point), but I do see that there are many more aspects to making good business decisions than just the technical ones. I've been an advocate of process engineering for some time now, and am excited, intrigued, amused, and even occasionally frustrated as I watch large organizations try to adopt frameworks such as ITIL. I can't imagine a more exciting thing to do, and (meetings aside) am occasionally a little surprised I get paid to do something so fun....
