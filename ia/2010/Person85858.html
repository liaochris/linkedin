What I believe in Project Management...

* The project manager is the person ultimately responsible for the outcome of the project, formally empowered to use organizational resources, in control of the project, authorized to spend the project's budget and authorized to make decisions for the project.
Because the project manager is in charge of the project, most of the project problems and responsibilities belongs to him. It's typically a bad idea to escalate a problem to someone else.

* All disputes should be resolved in favor of the customer

* The project manager should plan twice and execute once.

* The project manager must work proactively to influence the factors that cause change.

* There is a big difference between regulations and standards, compliance with a regulation is mandatory, and compliance with a standard is not mandatory but maybe helpful, the PMBOK guide provides a standard for project management.

* Those who passing the PMP exam without truly understanding the driving philosophy and reasons behind the project management are doing themselves and the project management profession a great disservice. "quotes by Andy Crowe"
