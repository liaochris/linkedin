Vivek Tuljapurkar’s Summary

Lived and worked in the US for 20+ years. Currently living in Pune, India. Extensive global experience in Mobile Wireless Technology and Management; have worked with telecom companies in over 50 countries.

Most of my experience is related to:
- Developing new technologies or world-class technology products, and taking them to market globally.
- Managing large product portfolios on a global scale.
- Consulting: Helping solve strategy, process, organizational, and technology problems.

I have been an advisor or consultant to the governments of USA (via CSPP), India (VSNL), New Zealand, Saudi Arabia, Brazil, and to Exxon-Mobil's Corporate Strategy Group.

I have taught at Case Western Reserve University, Drexel University, and Indian Institute of Management.

I was a key contributor to twelve "firsts" in technology:
1) First ever digital satellite networks - Inmarsat M/B; American Mobile Satellite Corp.
2) First ever “switch in the sky” - NASA’s Advanced Communications Technology Satellite
3) First ever Push-to-Talk over satellite - American Mobile Satellite Corp.
4) First ever satellite packet-data network - Telesat Mobile Inc., Canada
5) First ever hierarchical cell structure for voice - American Mobile Satellite Corp.
6) First ever hierarchical cell structure for packet data - Eaton FleetComm
7) One of the earliest (possibly first) MVNOs in the world - Eaton FleetComm
8) First ever digital satellite packet data terminal, ruggedized to US Military specifications and with fully integrated GPS (for Eaton FleetAdvisor)
9) One of the earliest (possibly first) attempts at flow-through provisioning (VVIS project for Dutch PTT)
10) Possibly the first ad-hoc network for battlefield communications - US Army’s UMS
11) First ever artificial intelligence application for attitude control of in-orbit communications satellites - Intelsat
12) Low-earth orbit satellite communications for US military use (US Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency)
