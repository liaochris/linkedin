John Erickson’s Summary

A rare gift is what say. Others have called it is a rare quality that one person can take a limited amount of resources asking for very little else and turn it into something valuable. Tell me the rules, show me the game and we will find a way to win. It has been terribly difficult for a guy like me to admit that I have been wrong for more than twenty five years. Since everyone has always called me John the Alarm Guy I just figured that I was John the Alarm Guy selling and designing security and home automation systems. Why not? I have a vast knowledge of technology and security and I lived and breathed it day and night. What else would they call me? Heck I love technology.

When I walked into that 500 square foot, one man office, with an IBM type writer, sitting on a metal desk and a radio shack phone with several of the buttons missing on it; I did not see a beat up office. I saw potential. I did not see that he had one employee; himself and only clients were his friends and family. I saw that there were file cabinets to be filled with new customers. I saw opportunity. So loving a challenge and not ever considering failure I took on what some called as impossible.

I embraced failure as a lesson to success. The more I failed the more I became determined and tenacious I became to achieve. Driven by goals and fueled by creativity I never stopped. After twenty five years I was given an opportunity to look back and we had taken a $5000 initial investment and built into a multi-million dollar company.

With reflection, I figured it out I was not only John the Alarm Guy because it did not matter that it was security systems. In reality, I created opportunity, trust and made the business grow. These are not just words to me they are the foundation I work from in both my professional and personal life; trust, community, honor, quality, creativity, respect. Some might call that the American Dream.
