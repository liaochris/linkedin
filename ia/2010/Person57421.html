  • Manager

    Information Systems Meade Instruments

    (Machinery industry)

    January 1993 — January 1995 (2 years 1 month)

    I have a vast background in technology and back in 1990 we were still using mainframes to manage data for mid-size companies like Meade.

    I came into this position much like the planner position because of the leaving of an excellent Material Manager. He gets the credit for introducing the technology. I get the credit for maintaining it, and keeping the company connected. When Meade moved to a new facility, I connected all the terminals to the mainframe, wiring them and maintaining the physical plant. I did minor programing to the ASK manman software and produced reports for management. It was after this move we upgraded to a new mainframe. I managed its purchased and oversaw its install and brought it online within a day or two.

    My other responsibility was the purchasing and maintaining of personal computers. As 486 machines became inexpensive, Meade began to use more and more of them and I purchased them, created small networking systems not connected to the mainframe, purchased software for the company and trained people on the use of that software.

  • Machine Shop Supervisor

    Meade Instruments

    (Machinery industry)

    January 1985 — January 1995 (10 years 1 month)

    This was my longest assignment at Meade.

    I supervised the daily tasks and projects of up to 60 employees during five shifts. including the manual machining department and the CNC deparment. I had 7 department supervisors reporting to me.

    Oversaw all aspects of plant management, which included the repair of all machine shop machines and CNC machines to facilitate ongoing production. We repaired almost all of our machines with only a few exceptions requiring outside service providers.

    I was an change agent here as well, introducing computers for data transfer to the CNCs, that alone improved accuracy and reduced setup times significantly. It also made CNC tapes obsolete. During my tenure as our schedule became more demanding with more product, we began to find ways of reducing setup times and increasing productivity. We standardized mill setups and utilized a central tooling center to preset all setups, reducing setup times by 2 hours per set up. At this time we introduced additional tools that allowed the quality of the product to be checked by the operator at the machine, reducing scrape and improving quality significantly.

    Overall, this small shop that went fairly large did a great job of incorporating change and staying clean as we practiced the 5s of Lean without even knowing we were doing so. Everything in its place, and a place for everything.

    I learned so much when I was here, and took that into my future experiences. I still love the smell of a machine shop, love the sound of a lathe winding up to make a cut.
