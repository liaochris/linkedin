Margaret F. Bonafide’s Summary

My professional experience combines an unusual skill set which includes both technical and creative careers. I have worked as a computer technician with a Secret Clearance for a government contractor and later returned to school and with the initial intent to become a technical writer.

I interned in a newsroom as part of a journalism class and found an intense interest in story telling. I remained in the news field and have delighted in seeing it embrace the technology with which I was already familiar.
The morphing of the journalistic product from paper to the Internet has been particularly favorable for me with my background. I enjoy working with digital media. Much of the technology I use today, I had worked with in my previous career.
I shoot digital photographs and video and can edit in Avid Express Pro.

Margaret F. Bonafide’s Honors:

I am part of an Oscar winning documentary which won the 2008 Documentary Short category for a film called Freeheld. I am interviewed in the film by the director Cynthia Wade. I wrote the stories that brought world wide attention to the plight of a police lieutenent who was dying of cancer and wanted to leave her pension to her domestic partner. The Ocean County Freeholders turned Laurel Hester down because she was gay saying it would "Violate the sanctity of marriage.'' With relentless coverage of all the issues, the Freeholders changed their minds to treat domestic partners equally as heterosexual employees. As a result, the matter drew the attention of Wade who made the film which is now available on DVD. For more information see Freeheld.com.
