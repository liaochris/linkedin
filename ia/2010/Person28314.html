Brian Murphy’s Summary

Throughout my professional career in IT, my experience has ranged from Network Engineer to Director to Enterprise Architect to my present day position as leader of two teams “Application Support” and “Application Accessibility”. My former positions have been fast-paced, challenging and broad in scope and have required strong technical and strategic planning capability. I have been instrumental in the improvement, streamlining and cost savings of these organizations and have progressed quickly as a result.

I am privileged, for more than eleven years now I’ve had the opportunity to assume multiple roles; my roles range from Team Manager, Salesman, Technology Promoter, Leader, Mentor, Negotiator, Conflict Resolution Specialist, Project Manager, Business Relationship Manager, and Enterprise Architect. Depending on the situation or project I might assume some or all of these responsibilities simultaneously. I will assume whatever roles are necessary to exceed the expectations of my leadership, my team, my customers and my organization.

Implementing cutting edge technology can be difficult and there are certain risks, as an architect, which you must be willing to endure. I’ve endured those risks and created real change in organizations with implementation of technologies to support VDI (Virtual Desktop Infrastructure), SBC (Server Based Computing) and ultimately I foresee companies leveraging “internal private cloud architecture” where capacity is dynamically allocated so as to maximize the economies of scale for the data center. This technology and methodology is just one of many visionary examples that have allowed me to streamline operations, improve end-user experience, and drive down costs.
