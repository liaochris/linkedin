Jeanne C Meister’s Summary

Jeanne C. Meister is an internationally recognized expert in corporate learning and development whose name is synonymous with the establishment and institutionalization of corporate universities and customer education programs among FORTUNE 1000 firms and agencies of the federal government. Jeanne is skilled working at top executive levels. She is a leading edge thinker, researcher and author. Jeanne has written two books on the corporate universities which have helped to accelerate the global corporate university movement.

In 2007, Jeanne was voted by her peers on www.trainingindustry.com as one of the 20 top influential training professionals. Jeanne has consulted with over 250 enterprises on launching and managing a corporate university. Jeanne is the author of Corporate Quality Universities, and Corporate Universities: Lessons in Building a World-Class Work Force. In addition, Jeanne’s research has been profiled in Chronicle of Higher Education, CLO Magazine, Harvard Business Review Japan, Financial Times, Journal of Business Strategy, TRAINING, T&D magazine, Outlook, and Workforce Management.

Jeanne writes a monthly column for CLO magazine and she is on the Editorial Board of CLO Magazine. Jeanne has also been appointed a faculty member at Indian School Of Business teaching a program on Innovations In Learning & Development. Jeanne is on the Advisory Board of the Executive Program in Work-Based Learning Leadership developed by Wharton and University of Pennsylvania School of Education. Jeanne lives in New York City and Columbia County, New York with her husband, daughter and havanese dog. She is an active fund raiser and on the board of directors of American Diabetes Association as well as a member of the Columbia County Land Conservancy. Jeanne is a graduate of University of Connecticut as well as Boston University.
