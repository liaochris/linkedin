Jennifer Romano’s Summary

My research through the years has primarily focused on successful cognitive aging; that is, what factors allow our minds to age better, in terms of learning and memory. My current work consists of more applied human factors, such as using eye tracking in usability testing of various Census Web sites. As a post doc researcher at the Census Bureau, I am examining age-related differences in Web site performance and ways to improve Web sites and surveys for all users, including older adults.

As an independent consultant, I teach about usability and accessibility. I conduct training and hands-on workshops to teach you how to improve your website. I also conduct usability evaluations on your website and make recommendations to improve it.

In addition, I teach older adults how to use the internet. I am interested in how learning improves and sustains cognition in old age, and so some of my applied, consulting work is also a part of my research.
