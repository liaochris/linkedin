Simon Brise’s Summary

I have worked in several areas - from Broadcast Hire & Engineering to IT, Electronics Design, Radio comms, and as a Cameraman & Video Editor. With my teaching (of Video Editing, Web Design & Emerging Technology) I enjoy understanding technology & being able to help others understand it. In broadcast hire for example I respond to customer technical questions.

I began my career in 1982 as a BBC Broadcast Engineer working in News & Current affairs at Lime Grove & TV Centre [special facilties, studio, MCR / Satellite, computer graphics, VTR, telecine, lighting & racks operations & maintenance, PSC / OB news truck] , changed in 1988 to being self employed & designed engine management systems for racing engines. In 1997 I moved to a Radio Comms / IT User Support role at DHL. I then moved to VMI broadcast hire as principle maintenance engineer in 2000 . Going creative, I then moved into Studio Camera work & Video Editing for Sky and channel 4 (2002). After a brief period at Express shopping Channel, I moved into media & creative teaching work in 2005 at Ravensbourne College, CCCMedia, Richmond (RACC) & UCCA (research work). A qualified teacher in 2008, I delivered courses at Richmond Business School, in video editing & web design using ‘moodle’. I also take on special effects projects such as (dec 2008) rotating camera assemblies for prodigy concert at the Brixton academy. I love to be creative, to design & understand technology.

Simon Brise’s Specialties:

Electronics Design & Special Effects Engineer, Freelance Sessional Lecturer in Emerging Technology & Trends in the Creative Sector, Post Production and Web Design. News and Sport Video Editor, Broadcast Engineer, Studio Lighting Cameraman, Graphic Designer, Web Designer, CAD design.
