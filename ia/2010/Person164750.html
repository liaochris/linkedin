Phil Harrell’s Summary

Phil Harrell spent nearly 10 years at Akamai Technologies, where he was a key contributor to Akamai's explosive growth from its pre-IPO beginnings as an early stage start up to its current position as a leading Internet company with more than $800M in revenue. Harrell launched several successful initiatives for Akamai, including leading the company's entry into the online video market, building its New York office, reorganizing its Eastern US Sales Division, and developing and expanding Akamai's second generation application acceleration business worldwide as VP of Sales, Application Performance Solutions (APS). After launching APS, Harrell was made Vice President, Channel Sales, where he spearheaded efforts to create a new class of channel partnerships to accelerate customer acquisition and growth.

Before joining Akamai, Mr. Harrell held sales positions at Pegasystems, where he was responsible for commercializing the company’s Business Process Management solutions in the telecommunication market. He also co-founded Summa Systems, a company designed to provide targeted Internet advertisements and content to consumers via in-store terminals.

Mr. Harrell holds a B.A. from Duke University in Public Policy and Slavic Languages and is a graduate of MIT Sloan School's Greater Boston Executive Program.
