Mike Edwards’s Summary

Mike Edwards is a 26-year retail veteran who has led brand transformations and business turnarounds for some of the leading names in the industry. He joined Borders Group in September 2009 as Executive Vice President, Chief Merchandising Officer, and was appointed President and Chief Executive Officer in June 2010.

Mike brings a demonstrated track record of success to Borders, having recently served as President and CEO of lucy activewear, where he led the reinvention of the sportswear retailer, taking it from just 10 stores and no e-commerce capability to over 60 stores and a successful web business. The International Council of Shopping Centers recognized Mike’s leadership at lucy by naming it 2007 “Brand of the Year.” Lucy was sold in August 2007 to VF Corporation (NYSE: VCF)., a $7.6 billion apparel powerhouse.

Earlier in his career, Mike was part of a turnaround team that moved fabric and craft retailer Jo-Ann Stores Inc. (NYSE: JAS) from near bankruptcy to a highly profitable company with $2 billion in annual sales. During Mike’s tenure at Jo-Ann, where he served for three years as Executive Vice President, Operations, the stock price increased from $3 per share to $29 per share. His contributions there included a transformation of operating performance, which was a key factor in increasing earnings, sales and customer satisfaction.

In addition to lucy and Jo-Ann, Mike was a integral part of enhancing performance and driving business results at other organizations including UIB Capital-owned Ellington Leather, where he most recently served as President and CEO; West Marine (NASDAQ: WMAR) and CompUSA, where he helped grow the company from annual sales of $100 million to more than $5 billion in eight years and developed partnerships with Apple and Microsoft. He began his career with merchandising positions at The May Department Store Company and Target Corporation.
