Maria Ripoll’s Summary

I have over 20 years in many areas of IT. I started in telecommunications installing smart terminals at grocery store branches for Great American First Savings (later became Wells Fargo), upon graduating U of A went to work at IBM as a software support specialist, working on the SMS family of products. I then became an IT Director at TRAC Media Services, combining Oracle database data with internal products including Microsoft SQL and Corel's Paradox. It was here I became much more interested in the hardware of PCs, the advancements made in software to use the PC, and their capabilities when networked together. So I pursued Microsoft certifications in network and ventured into business ownership where I could better manage the services I provided. It was also at TRAC that I realized I love teaching,which became part of my services as a consultant.

Summary:
Systems Architect including Network designs and Administration, IT Director, Instructor and Paperless Office Consultant. Focused on getting the most and best use out of the technology already in place and doing so future expansion is seamless.

Application Support configuring and tailoring specialty programs of small to mid-sized businesses. (You how to use your software for your business, let me teach you how to manage that software.)

Project Manager in a wide variety of business applications including non-profit.

CompTIA A+ and MCP with MCSE education and experience. Currently pursuing MCITP.

The Dream for the business:
Grow and expand Computer Medics 911 to help clients get the most of their technology, while providing internships to those just completing their IT education.
My personal goal:
I want to share my knowledge and build seminars for larger user communities to learn how to enjoy their computers beyond just e-mail and gaming.
