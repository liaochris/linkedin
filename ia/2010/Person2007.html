Andy Coppock’s Summary

I have owned and operated PFM Engineering since January of 2000. I provide consulting and manufacturing services specializing in laser cutting, shape setting / heat treating and electropolishing Nitinol components for the biomedical industry.

PFM Engineering also specializes in metallurgical analysis of exotic materials. We design and build custom laser manufacturing centers used to create neurovascular stents, cardiovascular stents and artificial heart valve components.

I specialize in the fabrication of Nitinol (Nickel Titanium alloy) components from sheet stock, bar stock and tubing. We manufacture precision, large bore Nitinol tubing with diameters up to 2 inches. My research has developed Nitinol processing technology that renders components with unsurpassed corrosion resistance and dimensional conformity.

I also function as an expert witness for materials science and hydrology / valve related issues for the United States Department of Interior, Minerals Management Division.

Recently I’ve been involved creating specialized instrumentation for paranormal research as seen on SyFy's Ghost Hunters, Ghost Hunters International, History Channel Mystery Quest and Travel Channel Ghost Adventures.

These devices are also now being used for experimental communication with autistic children and also in the treatment of seizure disorders demonstrating real world applications for this new found technology.

I have also appeared on Travel Channel's Mystery Quest series "Return of the Amityville Horror" episode as well as Travel Channels Ghost Adventures, “Wolfe Manor” episode.
