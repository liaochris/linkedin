Michael Otto, MA, LPC’s Summary

Licensed Professional Counselor. Practice primarily from two theoretical pillars; Adlerian and Existential.  Focus is aimed at identifying social influences on thinking, feeling, and behaving; increasing responsibility, genuineness, and creating meaning.  I also have roots in Solution-Focused Brief Therapy as well as Multi-Systemic Therapy; both are techniques that are rooted in research on what is effective in therapy.  What I pull from these approaches depends on each individual’s needs in each session.  Sessions can be directive and confrontational or non-directive and completely patient driven.  Identifying future goals and the intermediary goals in order to reach them are always addressed.  I also strive to get as much of the patient’s social systems involved in order for the patient to have social supports in place when psychotherapeutic intervention is no longer needed.

Spent 5 years working with at-risk adolescents in the child welfare system in a County Funded Transitional Living program in the Allentown, Bethlehem, and Easton Area. Assisted these youth who were "aging out of care" to learn the necessary life-skills in order to live successfully and independently while still working towards educational and employment goals.

1 year of Master's Level Therapy Internship at Muhlenberg College's Counseling Services Center. Worked with young adults in the areas of depression, anxiety, relationship issues, transitional adjustment, consultation and coaching for students with concerns about peers, and some trauma work.

Future goals include the pursuit of a Doctoral Degree (Psy. D) with potential research in the cathartic effects of Hardcore/Heavy Metal music in people with aggressive impulses.

Michael Otto, MA, LPC’s Specialties:

Work experience has been focused on older adolescents and young adults from a diversity of ethnic and financial backgrounds. Most recently, my therapeutic focus has leaned towards abnormal psychology: antisocial adolescents and families who are repeat offenders with the justice system, adolescents in psychiatric crisis that require hospitalization, and young adults - elderly schizophrenics; primarily paranoid type.
