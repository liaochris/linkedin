Joanna Clay’s Summary

I'm a journalist who has contributed to the Los Angeles Times, NBC Los Angeles, Access Hollywood and Orange County Local News among others.

As of right now, I'm a stringer for the Orange County bureau of the Los Angeles Times, primarily contributing to the the Daily Pilot. The Pilot is based in Newport Beach and Costa Mesa and has over 50,000 weekend readers.

Besides traditional journalism, I also work in new media by managing a luxury lifestyle website called LadyLUX.com. As senior editor, I contribute a majority of content, which consists of event reporting, interviews and style posts. Besides writing, I also manage the back-end (cms system) and oversee freelancers and interns.

A recent college grad, you could easily call me a media junkie. During my four years at UCI, I started a progressive journalism magazine called Incite (sometimes compared to Mother Jones), which received funding from the Center for American Progress in Washington D.C. I also had two radio shows at KUCI, one was an off-beat news program with my friend Robbie and another was a solo indie pop show called "Cup of Jo" that aired on Sunday mornings.

While at UCI I earned two bachelors degrees in social-cultural anthropology and Spanish. I thoroughly enjoyed the study of both and think they’ve given me a unique and invaluable perspective on the world we live in. I also took the opportunity to study both disciplines at the Universitat de Barcelona in Barcelona, Spain from July '07 to July '08.

I’m always open to learning about new opportunities, meeting new people, and growing more as a creative professional. I hope you enjoy my site and feel free to contact me!
