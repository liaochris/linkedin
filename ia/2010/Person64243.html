  • Senior Fantasy Writer

    (Public Company; CBS; Online Media industry)

    November 2004 — September 2010 (5 years 11 months)

    I was the No. 1 baseball player analyst and the most innovative and senior member of a 12-man writing and editorial staff that included some of the industry's biggest names. I led by example and was the driving force in all aspects of content -- news, projections, rankings, live video, radio and podcasts. I was also active during spring training in Florida, interviewing players for stories and online videos.

    I conceptualized and executed Fantasy sports projects like roster trends (ie. most-owned and most-viewed), two-start pitchers presentation, NFL injury report automation, injury report tools, live draft parties/Fantasy conventions, depth charts, probable pitchers report, mock drafts, average-draft position, multiple-player-update process, sticky updates and social media strategies.

    These projects helped streamline player news and analysis to maximize the staff's time and make the site as timely, informative and well-displayed as any in the industry. The world's best in Fantasy play there because I was steps ahead.

    Not only was I a horse behind the scenes, but I have developed a presence in front of the camera and with a microphone as well. I performed live on Fantasy Baseball Today and in daily video reports, online shows and podcasts with a lively flair. I have also been featured prominently on live cable TV (notably MASN in Baltimore) and sports talk radio shows around the country.

    I was also the lead writer/analyst and executive editor of the annual Fantasy Baseball Draft Guide and Owners Manual from 2005-09.

    And, because of CBSSports.com's recognition of me as a top mind in the realm of baseball specifically, I wrote the website's MLB Power Rankings from January 2005-August 2008. It was the website's most hotly debated feature.
