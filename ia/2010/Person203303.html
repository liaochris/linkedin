Tammy House’s Summary

I have been in and out of 3M for most of my adult life. I started as a file clerk while going to college and like most people in this area, decided that 3M was for me.

I was working in the 3M International Distribution center when I got my first PC. I worked myself right out of a job there and moved into IT support.

I left 3M for a short while when my daughter was born and came back as a professional service consultant when she started school.

I was part of a team of desktop support analysts and played a role in developing the support structure for the 3M Helpdesk mobile teams. I led a team of support analysts in various divisions of 3M, provided client training for desktop tools, did 3M TV productions to provide training for remote locations, and trained new support analysts coming in to our group.

I moved within 3M from the IT support "Wiseguys" (as we were called) to provide Business Analyst support to Lab IT providing support for hardware planning and acquision as well as basic IT software and hardware support .

For the next 4 years I worked on my own as a business consultant providing network planning and implementation, hardware and software consulting and support, backup and disaster planning, and web development to a variety of small business clients.

I am now back at 3M as a professional service consultant providing support for the rollout efforts of web 2.0 collaboration tools going on with my focus mainly on the Research and Development area.

My goal is to bridge the communication gap between lab personnel here and around the globe and to continue my growth as a SharePoint Developer.

Tammy House’s Specialties:

Microsoft SharePoint, Desktop support, Software training and support, Web Development, Training and software development mostly with collaboration web 2.0 tools.
