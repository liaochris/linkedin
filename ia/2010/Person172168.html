Richard "Doc" Gooding’s Summary

Richard Gooding founded Strategic Advantage in 1995 after leaving Arizona State University where he was a Professor in the College of Business.

Over the last 15 years, Doc, as his clients call him, has helped over fifty entrepreneurial companies grow their business, develop their internal systems and build their teams. He understands the unique needs of entrepreneurs and the challenges they face in bringing their vision to life.

The entrepreneur’s business idea is of little value if it isn’t operationalized into a plan that is implemented by the team. Doc provides the process and advice that helps entrepreneurs make their dreams come true.

His clients come to see him as an integral part of their team, a confidant they can trust to always do the right thing for them and their company.

Doc’s clients have included Cold Stone Creamery, Insight Enterprises, VIZIO, China Mist Brands, Fairy Tale Brownies, Great American Marketing and Events, Canterra Homes, The Weitz Company, Precision Science, Company Nurse, iMemories, Forensic Consulting, I-ology, Science Care, Sonic, Unitus, Harmon Solar & Electric, Valutek, and OpenWorks.

    Responsible for designing, organizing, automating and implementing Avnet’s Global Strategic Planning and Management System worldwide. Approximately 150 SBUs are engaged in the planning process between Americas, Asia and Europe.

    Managed Global professional staff of four who support strategic planning functions in each of Avnet’s operating groups. Worked with the CEO, Senior Executives and Group Presidents to continuously evolve and improve the strategic planning process within Avnet.

  • Assistant Professor, College of Business

    Arizona State University

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    August 1989 — October 1995 (6 years 3 months)

    Taught undergraduate and graduate courses in Strategic Planning, Managerial Decisions Making, and Organizational Theory. Received excellent performance ratings in all courses.

    Conducted and supervised the following research studies:

    -- Strategies in the Plastics Industry: Survey of 360 CEOs in the plastics industry with 36% response rate. Report provided information about performance standards and competitive strategies in the plastics industry that participating companies used as benchmarks.

    -- Merger and Acquisition Success: Survey of top-management teams in 98 companies that made acquisitions between 1990 and 1992. Report for each firm showed how it compared with companies that made successful acquisitions.

    -- Organizational Performance: Study used a national database on 64 industries to identify factors that influence firm performance.

    -- Strategic Alliances in High-Technology Industries: Survey of 303 top managers of SBUs in high-technology companies. Study was the first to examine the types of strategic alliances companies form and how those alliances influence competition.

    Published articles in top strategic management journals and presented papers at national conferences. Awarded College of Business Faculty Research Development Award.
