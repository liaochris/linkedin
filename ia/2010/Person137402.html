Matthew Leingang’s Summary

Ivy League college mathematics instructor (Ph.D.), eight years of experience with undergraduates of all levels. Manage large (hundreds of students) courses taught in dozens of sections, with graduate student/postdoc/faculty instructors, graduate student teaching assistants, and undergraduate graders. Train, place, and mentor graduate student and postdoctorate undergraduate teaching. Teach content knowledge to area middle- and high-school teachers.

Along with mathematics and math education research, interested in new technologies in the classroom and for teaching and course management online. Extensive programming. Experienced technical writer and subject matter expert for mathematics textbooks, e-learning resources, and computer language books.

Matthew Leingang’s Specialties:

Teaching single- and multivariable calculus and linear algebra. Inquiry-Based Learning techniques in the classroom, including personal response systems and nontraditional teaching methods (the Moore Method). Programming in PHP, Perl, and Python; also use XML and XSLT. Approximately 15 years of LaTeX use and programming.
