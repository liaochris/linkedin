Murali Krishna Devarakonda’s Summary

I have an insatiable desire to leverage software to build products and services that solve end-user problems.

I am a die-hard entrepreneur & an activist at heart, a software engineer & a product manager in my soul, and a technology consumer & an informed citizen in mind.

As someone who cares as much about the journey as the destination, I coach "under-privileged" entrepreneurs working on hi-tech product startups at Agile Entrepreneurs, a social network for entrepreneurs.

I worked on RideStation.com from 1999 till the 2001 collapse, and revived it in 2005 as a product of Crystal Ball Corp.I have learned several invaluable lessons in bootstrapping and the challenges of building a hi-tech product based business, including managing teams across multiple timezones on two continents and 5 cities,

I setup the India operations of Crystal Ball in Hyderabad, hiring and remotely leading an engineering team in India that built RideStation from scratch, using Agile methods (Scrum & XP).

Previously, I was also jointly responsible for one of the major successes of the internet in 2000/2001 - Immigrants Support Network, a national not-for-profit volunteer-only organization dedicated to helping legal immigrants fix a system full of "bugs". ISN had over 20 thousand registered members and over a quarter million supporters. Our efforts resulted in several legislative and regulatory reforms starting with the passage of the American Competitiveness in the 21st Century Act (AC21) in October 2000.

I am an active member/volunteer member of organizations like TiE, SDForum, SVASE & SVPMA, and a recurring volunteer of TiECon (www.TiECon.org), the world's largest conference on entrepreneurship. I have served on the 'Entrepreneur Committee' within the Board of Advisors of SVASE, and have contested a board seat on the Agile Alliance in 2007.
