    Dr. Santin, CEO and President of Saravanti Educational Advisors, is an educational consultant providing services to colleges and universities in the area of accreditation and licensing, developing and sustaining online learning enterprises within both for profit and not for profit sectors. She is particularly recognized for her ability and expertise in assisting institutions broaden their base of operations or product lines through blending technology with innovative programs. Her consulting clientele reaches from Phoenix to NY, Ohio, Chicago and Oregon and are comprised of small to medium sized universities, educational associations and entrepreneurs beginning start up educational initiatives or expanding their base of operation or product lines.

  • President/Provost

    Northcentral University

    (Educational Institution; E-Learning industry)

    March 2004 — October 2007 (3 years 8 months)

    After serving on the NCU Board of Directors for three years, she was appointed the first and founding Dean of the School of Education in 2004.

    Her extensive experience in developing and marketing online programs and bringing them through the regional accreditation process resulted in the NCU being approved to offer an Ed.D., Ph.D. and M.Ed. within six months from her appointment. Enrollments grew from 24 students to 1700 students in a two year period.

    She was promoted to Academic Vice President and Provost and in 2006, appointed President by the Board. During her tenure she at NCU, she led the academic, operations, marketing, enrollment and IT functions. She developed articulation agreements with academic institutions in Vietnam, People’s Republic of China, the FBI Academy, several community colleges, e.g. Rio Salado, and numerous professional associations. Her leadership resulted in NCU’s business school becoming the first 100% online graduate program to achieve accreditation by the Association of Collegiate Business Schools and Programs (ACBSP); and the School of Education becoming the first 100% online institution to be awarded a Kappa Delta Pi (KDP) chapter for the School of Education.

    Prior to NCU, Dr. Santin was at Nova Southeastern University (NSU). She served as Dean of Leadership Studies at the Fischler School of Education from 2001-2004. While at the Huizenga School of Business at NSU, she served in increasing leadership positions; e.g. Associate Dean, Assistant Dean, Director of Marketing and Enrollment.
