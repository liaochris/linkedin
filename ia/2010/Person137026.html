Matt Grigsby’s Education

  • Rhode Island School of Design

    BFA , Industrial Design , 2002 — 2005

    Rhode Island School of Design (RISD), founded in 1877 in Providence, Rhode Island, is a vibrant community of designers and artists. RISD includes 2,200 students from around the world, approximately 350 faculty and curators, and 400 staff members. Each year more than 200 prominent artists, critics, authors and philosophers visit the school. The mission of the Rhode Island School of Design, through its college and museum, is to educate its students and the public in the creation and appreciation of works of art and design, to discover and transmit knowledge and to make lasting contributions to a global society through critical thinking, scholarship and innovation.

    Activities and Societies:

  • Strate Collège Designers

    Transportation Design 2003 — 2004

    Strate Collège Designers is a place for discovering, encountering and exchanging. The school blends creative passion with real world design solutions. Their priority is to teach drawing, it is the designer’s first means of expression, along with the methodology and the strategy necessary to integrate design with industrial manufacturing processes. From the beginning, students are trained to work in groups, and they are given many opportunities to discover the world. Strate provides short work experience opportunities, design internships in France or abroad, international exchanges with foreign schools, study trips, and research or prospective projects in the framework of partnerships with companies. 80 professionals teach every year, from designers to philosophers, from engineers to poets, helping students progressively acquire a personal and unique vision of what design is.
