Kate Seely’s Summary

I am continuing my interest in the food and farming sector through working for FarmsReach, a start-up focused on supporting effective regional food systems through an online marketplace. After three years in philanthropy, I also consult with Emerging Practitioners in Philanthropy, an organization focused on creating networks and professional development opportunities for emerging foundation staff with a social justice lens.

Before FarmsReach and EPIP, I worked with non-profit and philanthropic organizations focused on providing alternatives to Latin American migration and enhancing equity and access to services for Latin American immigrants in the U.S. I founded and directed a non-profit in Oaxaca, Mexico, that advances food sovereignty and the well-being of rural Oaxacan communities through the promotion of amaranth, a native grain that all but disappeared from Mesoamerica after the Spanish conquest. My work in Mexico helped me understand many of the core forces of emigration and consequently to work with a broader focus in the field of philanthropy. I spent three years working with Hispanics in Philanthropy, where she gained valuable insight on the role of philanthropy in development.

Bottom line - I want to make ours a more equitable world starting with the communities outside our back door. My interest in food and farming pervades that work.
