Stephen Silveri’s Experience

  • Partner: Real Estate | Estate Planning | Probate Attorneys

    Silveri & Wilson, LLC

    (Law Practice industry)

    April 2004 — Present (6 years 8 months)

    Attorney Stephen D. Silveri focuses his efforts in the areas of Real Estate, Estate Planning, and Probate. His practice specializes in residential purchases, sales, condominium conversions, and real estate closings for some of the top lenders in Massachusetts, Rhode Island, and New Hampshire. He also works with clients of all ages and income levels to prepare their Wills, Trusts, Powers of Attorney, and Healthcare Proxies and assist them through the probate process.

    He earned his Bachelor of Arts in English Literature, with a concentration in Professional Writing and Technical Communication, from the University of Massachusetts at Amherst. After making his mark in the high-technology sector for several years, he then pursued his Juris Doctor at Suffolk University Law School where he was the recipient of the coveted "Best Advocate Award" at the National Tax Trial competition in St. Petersburg, Florida.

    He is a frequent speaker at Suffolk University Law School, the Massachusetts Bar Association, the Boston Bar Association, Greater Boston Real Estate Board and the Tri-County Board of Realtors. In 2007, he was recognized as one of Boston's "brightest" lawyers by the President of the Boston Bar Association when he was selected to be a part of its prestigious Public Interest Leadership Program (PILP).

    He also has a long-standing commitment to the community which is evident from his work as a volunteer lecturer, a project leader for a local volunteer organization called Boston Cares, and his fundraising efforts in connection with eight Boston Marathons where he has raised more than $30,000 for a variety of charities. For two of the five years he ran with the Leukemia and Lymphoma Society’s Marathon Team (“TNT”), he also served as a team mentor to less experienced marathon runners. In the 2010 Boston Marathon, he ran a fast enough time to enter the 2011 Boston Marathon as an official entrant.
