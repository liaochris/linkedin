Laine Cunningham’s Summary

Laine works with independent authors to ghostwrite, revise and edit book-length fiction and nonfiction manuscripts. She also writes book proposals and query letters, the primary tools authors need to approach agents and publishers.
In July of 2007, CNN Money interviewed her as a publishing industry consultant to get her views on current publishing trends. Around the same time, Canada's Business News Network (BNN) requested her appearance on their live show for a segment on publishing trends.
Projects Laine has worked on have received attention from the largest publishers in the United States, including Random House, Simon & Schuster, Harper-Collins, Doubleday, Viking, Penguin-Putnam, Bantam, Scribner, Ballantine, and Little Brown, among others. Book proposals she has written have netted contracts with premium agents, and movie deals are currently in the works for a select handful of her clients.
To date, she has ghostwritten fourteen manuscripts in a variety of genres. She has also restructured, developed and rewritten over a hundred more for clients ranging from thrillers and crime novels to self-help, business and medical books. Her background includes consulting and freelancing work for the Oakland Museum, boldexec.com, Gracer Medical Group, Lakeview Cattle Station in Queensland, Australia, the Learning Center for Supportive Care, and a California-based monthly newspaper.

Laine Cunningham’s Specialties:

Ghostwriting thrillers, crime novels, self-help and business books, mind-body-spirit books, and memoirs, rewriting existing manuscripts from many categories, restructuring existing books, writing book proposals, consulting, editing
