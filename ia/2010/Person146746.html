Miranda Ko’s Summary

Miranda was born and raised in Hong Kong before moving to Tokyo at the age of thirteen. Her immersion of the three cultures has allowed her to travel fluidly in Asia and the Americas.

Miranda is now pursuing a degree in Management Science at the University of California, San Diego, and Marine Science at Scripps Institution of Oceanography. She also studied English at Stanford University.

Since fifteen, Miranda has been an easily recognized model in Hong Kong and Tokyo whose work included Intel, Northface, Neutrogena, Adidas, and many more.

She also finds time for another passion of hers, the ocean. She is now serving as the youngest Docent at the Birch Aquarium, Scripps Institution of Oceanography - where she leads exclusive tours for visitors and train new volunteers. She has also served as a Rules Judge for the National Ocean Science Bowl, and hosts bi-monthly beach clean-ups as a San Diego Coastkeeper. Miranda currently holds an internship for Climate Change at the California Center for Sustainable Energy, where she learns to incorporate the topic of energy into climate change. She has also conducted field work research with Brown University's Environmental Marine Biology Lab to come up with possible solutions to eliminate invasive plant species in Kona, Hawaii.
