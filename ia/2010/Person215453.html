  • Founder and Sales Director

    Stoody

    (Internet industry)

    March 1999 — March 2001 (2 years 1 month)

    Stoody was an early form of Social Media.

    We operated as an Internet Startup and got caught out in the dotcom bubble.

    The idea though was brilliant. We operated on the student market and had a vision very similar to what we see is happening with facebook today.

    Yet technology wasn't ready and we dabbled from one verion of the website to another without really getting a fluid version out.

    We started purchasing copy shops in front of the main universities that we viewed as hubs for students to meet and share and for companies to come and either interview or simply communicate witht them.

    In itself, the project was very good, we lifted €2M, yet our investors did not take their place on the board, and we clearly did not have the experience to run this operation through. With too many employees and the product not generating revenue, we needed more time and finances. March 2001 happened and our second round financing never took place.

    Live and Learn !

  • Area Sales Manager

    Ericsson

    (Public Company; ERIC; Telecommunications industry)

    September 1995 — March 1999 (3 years 7 months)

    I love Sweden. I really did then and I still do now.

    When I finished my studies in 1995 out of Ecole Centrale, I could see the potential of mobile technology. The internet had just started, and there was barely a 1% penetration of mobile phones in France for a staggering 8% in Sweden.

    I applied for a position in France Telecom in Stockholm first, where I did an internship, and then got a final posting with Ericsson in Gothenburgh, then got transferred back to Stockholm.

    I actually worked in a division called Ericsson Hewlett Packard Telecommunications, also known as EHPT. It was owed 60% by Ericsson and 40% by Hewlett-Packard.

    We produced alert management, traffic management, war room systems, as well as crm and billing software. Mostly we were very successful selling this platform as an add on to Ericsson AXE10 clients on the fixed and mobile sides, and developed very innovative open software to manage switches of other manufacturers too.

    EHPT taught me a lot about company procedures and how to operate the chain of command. Ericsson provided an excellent work envinronment where employees were very well looked atfer. I felt very welcome within the team and was able to deliver my best for the company.

    Area Manager for Australia New Zealand at age 25, I did a memorable business trip to Sydney with my great boss, Gerard Ponce de Leon. I learned a lot from him dunring this trip and we had fantastic fun. This remains to date the highest point of my career in telecoms.

    I then got transferred to operate the Russian market, which was more...cold, and subsquently finished with a year back in France to look after South Europe. I will keep excellent memories of my career with Ericsson and Hewlett Packard. the urge to create my own company was too strong and I left the company full of hope, but with a little pinch in my heart.
