Brian Alderman’s Summary

In my current position I am a Microsoft Certified Trainer allowing me to utilize my numerous MS certifications and PMP certification to deliver training internationally. In this role I am a subject matter expert (SME), which includes developing and presenting Microsoft technology training events on several Microsoft courses including; Windows Server 2003, XP, Vista, Active Directory, SQL Server 2000/2005, and SharePoint 2007 as well as a PMP Exam Preparation course.

While at NETg, I also managed a team of nine mentors who provided 24x7x365 international support to students via chat and email for our entire NETg course library. This new business began with two mentors in January of 2006 and grew to nine mentors providing a multi-million dollar service. During the growth of this department I implemented the following technologies; LMS platform-independent access, open source client/server web-based chats, incident tracking system, reporting capabilities for NETg and our customers, third-party QA evaluations, web-based scheduling system, and mentor-to-student screen sharing capabilities.

Also while working with Thomson NETg I managed the BPD team including the development and implementation of the processes used to build and deliver our BPD courses. Within this rold I recruited, trained, and mentored new instructors while defining course development requirements and processes necessary to implement a successful BPD offering.

I worked closely with Thomson Corporate to design, build, and present a custom course on the new compensation planning tool, attended by over 4,000 Thomson managers corporate wide.

Outside of Thomson Learning I have Co-Authored two books; Windows 2000 Professional Onsite and The SQL Server 2000 as well as tech-edited technical books, and have written a series of Microsoft practice exams for testing companies.
