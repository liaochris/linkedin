Colten Rowland’s Summary

I have worked in the fine art profession most of my life. A business that survives by creating product to satisfy the palate of less than one tenth of the population. Yet it is more diverse than the world itself because of the limitless imaginations of artists. I have seen the unforgiving nature of marketing to this niche. Its important to know your footing in this business to avoid financially and professionally damaging mistakes. I've seen successes that have lasted lifetimes, and others fail before the first six months. It is very easy become a casualty in the midst of changes in trend or technology. Web development has a similar nature. The internet is saturated with false claims, bad work and financial traps. Unless you've had experience developing and experience with those who have fallen victim to these mistakes. You would never know. If your site is adaptable, changeable, scalable and built with technologies that will support its longevity. It will be a good investment in cultivating new relationships and retaining old ones. Don't become a repeat casualty of financial loss on the web. I've made incredible amount of money for many people. I certainly would like the opportunity to do the same for you.

  • Director of On Demand Fine Art Printing

    Black Box Collotype

    (Printing industry)

    June 1998 — June 1999 (1 year 1 month)

    These were my early years in digital printmaking and large format digital photography. Some of the product I created at Black Box is currently retailing for $15,000.00 per sheet. Iris machines were the "choice" print on demand devices at the time. During my time there I was privileged to work with the Chicago Field Museum and their rare book collections. That experience includes reproducing pages from the Audubon Elephant Pholio. One of the rarest and most sought after antique books. This was one of only two complete and intact volumes left in the world. Pages from other books have made their way to the auction block and fetched as much $175,000.00 per sheet. The reproductions I produced were made available through Kenyon Oppenheimer Gallery on Michigain Avenue in Chicago and are still being sold there today.

    September 1995 — 1997 (2 years )

    During my college years I worked with Carlton Brown as his part-time, assistant videographer. While working for Carlton his talent for hand-held videography techniques had rubbed off on me and served me well. I was always complimented on my camera techniques by professors and other students at Columbia. I am very grateful to have had the experience. The talents I acquired there are reflected in my web development projects.
