christian benedetto jr’s Summary

Hopkins, Sampson and Brown Real Estate Advisory Services, LLC was initially formed in 2006 and located in Bluffton, South Carolina to fill a need, nationally, in order to create, execute and deliver favorable results to private, corporate and institutional clients in real estate markets across the United States.

Company founder, Christian M. Benedetto, Jr. has a 15 year track record in providing real estate, financial and economic solutions to clients, leading to more than 300 closed transactions. This dedication, knowledge and insight have now led him to the position of Director of Real Estate Services in the newly opened Newark, New Jersey office of Hopkins, Sampson & Brown Real Estate Advisory Services, LLC.

The Broker of Record for Hopkins, Sampson & Brown Real Estate Advisory Services, LLC, Suzanne Kiall Benedetto continues her successful 10-plus year career in the New Jersey commercial real estate market and oversees the day to day operations of the firm.

Hopkins, Sampson & Brown Real Estate Advisory Services, LLC, with its decades of experience, shall provide unparalleled service in the following areas:

Investment Property Division
Urban Renewal Division
Tenant Representation Division
Health & Wellness Division
Land Division
Rooftop & Media Division

Whether it is acquiring, selling, leasing or a question, Hopkins, Sampson & Brown Real Estate Advisory Services, LLC is specialized in confidentiality and will provide service, execution and discretion with a broad base of real estate expertise.

Hopkins, Sampson & Brown Real Estate Advisory Services, LLC is here to help its clients realize their real estate goals.

In my late 30’s I started giving most those things up, well except for the cars. Apparently watching the House marathon or the History channel while emailing clients off my blackberry did not qualify, nor did reading my son the Bill of Rights or teaching the dog to catch food in her mouth from the table.

    She wanted me to get an actual hobby – I thought who has time for such things. It’s like taking a long walk, you start out here and end up back here. I did plenty of that in the Marine Corp. A hobby made no sense to me, but since my wife is right 99.99% of the time I figured it was just easier to get a hobby now, then figure out why I needed one later on my own.

    I considered my options, Tennis? seems too much like work. Golf? too hot. Bare Knuckle boxing? I was too old. While I enjoy reading, but that’s really not a hobby I also enjoy writing so she suggested I put together a fifth book which would include the poems I wrote for our son. And although my wife, mom and mother-in-law love them, I was not sure if they would sell.
