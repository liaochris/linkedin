Brian Link’s Summary

Brian is a software technology executive, passionate technologist and creative, lateral thinker who loves building things. With over 17 years working in technology and consulting, he was most recently the technology executive for Digg.com, driving the growth in the company from 200 thousand to 3.5 million users, from 40 million to 250 million pageviews a month, from 10 to 80 employees and to over 35 million unique visitors a month, making it one of the most highly trafficked websites in the world.

At Digg, Brian led the engineering team and grew it from 3 to 20 developers and became an expert in adapting process changes for a rapidly evolving technical team. He crafted a custom Agile/Scrum software methodology that scales both the work and the organization itself going forward. Additionally, Brian established and built the QA and R&D teams; creating test-driven processes to integrate QA into the software lifecycle; and building an R&D team that is actively involved in the product roadmap, contributing complex algorithms and prototyping exploratory features that become part of Digg.

Prior to Digg, Brian spent most of his career at Cambridge Technology Partners where he delivered over 20 full life-cycle custom software development projects. His experience includes architectural leadership, project and program management and five years in client delivery management where he was ultimately responsible for all of the projects and accounts in the Midwest.
