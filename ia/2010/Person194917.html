    Runge

    (Public Company; Mining & Metals industry)

    May 2008 — November 2009 (1 year 7 months)

    Runge provides consultancy services to the mining industry. Runge has offices in; USA, Canada, Central America, Europe, Africa, Asia and Australia. Head office is in Brisbane, with a staff of around 500 globally. Runge's primary products are; consultancy services, software applications and training.   • Support & Training Manager

    Leica Geosystems

    (Public Company; Electrical/Electronic Manufacturing industry)

    January 2005 — April 2008 (3 years 4 months)

    Leica Geosystems designs and manufactures machine automation products for the mining, construction and agriculture industries. Leica’s major clients are: BHP (BMA), Rio Tinto & Anglo.
    Products consist of embedded applications running on a combination of propriety and “off the shelf” hardware, which is installed on mine site machines to collect data on production rates and cycle times. Mine site engineers utilize this data for both KPI analysis, mine planning and production reports.

    Responsibilities
    Management of a $5M pa ‘support’ profit centre
    Smooth implementation and maintenance of our systems on-site (hardware, firmware & software), during the warranty and maintenance contract periods
    Support remote client sites in both Australia and internationally
    Maintain client data integrity sent via either 900mhz telemetry or 802.11 network back to a SQL database
    Manage a team of seventeen support staff, throughout Australia, and a full-time trainer

    Achievements
    Recruited, developed and retained 10 additional team members, during a challenging mining boom period where good staff were difficult to acquire and retain.
    Recognized need for, and implemented, a 24/7 call centre. This provided clients with a reliable, single point of contact, offering consistent response and resolution times. Resolution times reduced from an average of 24 hours to 4 hours and close to 100% of issues were resolved within the 24 hour KPI compared to 90% previously.
    Development of a helpdesk package (Teamtrack) to better track, report and improve the support process. This helped to lift the KPI responses by a further 10%, and was critical to the full & proper SLA reporting required by a number of clients.
    Development and implementation of structured client training courses which resulted in a reduction of calls to the support team by 25%. This then allowed expansion of product sales without an increase in support staff.
