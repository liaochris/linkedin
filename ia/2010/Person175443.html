Rob Geiger’s Summary

I started my career as a disc jockey at Houston rock-n-roll powerhouse KLOL-FM. After four years, I made the jump to the sports department of KRLD-AM in Dallas and began another four-year stint as a beat reporter for the Dallas Cowboys and Dallas Stars.

Eventually, the newspaper gene handed down from my father became too strong to ignore and I shifted my career to capitalize on my passion for writing. My first job in the business was as sports editor at The Baytown Sun and within two years I had earned three AP awards for writing and was named Sportswriter of the Year by the Texas High School Coaches Association.

While covering a National Hot Rod Association (NHRA) drag race for the Sun, a representative of Pennzoil approached me about handling their race team's public relations. The timing was right so I took the job. Within months, I picked up several additional racing clients including the NHRA itself.

Two years into the drag racing portion of my career, I contractually agreed to write all of the original content for the organization’s website, NHRA.com. Eventually, my job duties expanded to include compiling and verifying the statistics generated by the tour as well as the formulation, writing, and editing of the 24 national event souvenir programs sold at each race. Additionally, I wrote all the AP stories.

In 2007, I published my first book, Darrell Russell: Broad Smiles, Quarter-Miles, a biography of drag racer Darrell Russell, who was killed in a tragic on-track accident. It has won two awards to date.

In the spring of 2008, I ventured back out on my own and started my own drag racing news Web site -- go2geiger.com. I also took a position writing all the drag racing news for AutoWeek magazine and became a contributor to USA Today.

I'm also a regular guest on numerous radio programs.
