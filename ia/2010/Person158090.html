Óscar Curros’s Summary

Óscar Curros is an experienced internacionalization & communication consultant, localization manager, Spanish & Galician native translator, and multilingual journalist & editor.

He started offering freelance services as a professional journalist in Galicia, Spain, in 2000. Moved to Brazil in 2005, where he founded his first consulting company, Comletras Text Services, in 2007. Left this partnership to work full-time as the head of Óscar Curros Consulting, which he founded in 2008. This company provides top-quality internationalization and translation services for various kinds of organizations, such as world-class institutions, Fortune 500 companies, international publishers, and leading universities.

Óscar Curros holds a B.A. in Journalism from University of Santiago de Compostela (Galicia, Spain), as well as several M.S. degrees: in International Cooperation, from University of Santiago de Compostela; in Advanced Studies on Latin America, from Complutense University of Madrid; and in Corporate Communications and Public Relations Strategic Management from University of São Paulo. He is currently pursuing a M.S. in Translation at Gama Filho University.

Óscar Curros was awarded the Johán Carballeira Prize (Journalism) for the interview "Manuel Rivas: The Unknown Poet", published in monthly magazine Tempos Novos. He also received the CCCB Best Informative Program Award for "Maria Galena's Stories", a mix of information, documentary and entertainment radio program.

Language expertise:
- Native in Spanish & Galician
- Fluent in English & Portuguese
- Intermediate in French
- Basic in Catalan & Italian
- Also studied Latin & Greek
