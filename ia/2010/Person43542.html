Corvus Elrod’s Summary

I am a storyteller with over 20 years of professional experience. I have always searched for ways to craft stories that are ever more meaningful to my audience, and worked to help people learn how to tell their own stories--not in a traditional educational environment, but through the storytelling itself.

In 1990, I began running RPG sessions using my own system. I have revised my technique, giving players greater and greater control over the gaming experience. This has led to powerfully emotional game sessions for both myself and the players, whom I consider fellow storytellers.

Eventually I experimented with incorporating gameplay into my performances. I realized that not only was it possible for the audience to become an integral part of the storytelling process when it was treated like a game, but that it was possible to communicate meaning via the gameplay itself.

As an IT professional, I've worked with clients to help them better understand their technology. Using my storytelling skills, I specialized in teaching others how to tell better stories about their use of technology. This approach has proven to be invaluable in maintaining healthy hardware and software systems.

The confluence of tech, story, and game design led me to an exploration of video games as narrative. I found more evidence that game mechanics are indeed powerful storytelling tools, even when used unwittingly towards that purpose. While writing and speaking about the storytelling potential of video games, I began developing a participatory storytelling model, a critical framework for examining the capacity of any media to share the storytelling experience with its audience.

I now work with the enthusiast, academic, and professional communities in the video game industry, to further clarify and formalize my participatory storytelling model. I am in the process of designing a free and open source software platform called the HoneyComb Engine that will demonstrate my theories.
