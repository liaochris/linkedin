Frank Hitzert’s Summary

Frank Hitzert started his career as newspaper journalist. He wrote prize-winning stories about Hurricane Hugo (during his honeymoon in Florida, USA in 1989), the military coup in Gambia (1996 – “Please, before you shoot me, can I first finish my Gin and Tonic?”), the “Heizel” soccer drama (1985) and the “Bijlmer” plane crash (1992). He joined Sanoma in 1998 as editor-in-chief of the Dutch weekly “Panorama” and introduced the first Dutch crime magazine (“Verdacht!”). Since 2005 he lives and works abroad. He started for Sanoma the green field operation in Belgrade, Serbia-Montenegro. Currently he is publisher at Sanoma Magazines Praha in Prague, Czech Republic. Also regular conference speaker and guest-lecturer at FIPP, London, and Charles University, Prague.
