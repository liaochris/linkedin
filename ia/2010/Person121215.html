Larry Kelley’s Summary

Advertising and academia seem like opposite pursuits. Advertising is all about commercialism while academia is about the pursuit of intellectualism. That is one of the reasons that I have spent my life in both camps. Each one feeds off the other.

Where they come together is in "transformation". Advertising can transform a brand from a dull and lifeless lump to a well loved part of someone's life.

Teaching can do the same. Ok, my students aren't lifeless lumps but they are green around the gills when they come to my class. My goal is to help not only transform their thoughts about advertising but to transform their thinking about life. You have to have big audicious hairy goals.

That is one reason why I started on the quest of publishing advertising textbooks. I wanted to help bridge the gap that can exist from the professional side to the academic side. Advertising is the best liberal arts education you can have. It will teach you to think, to clearly write and to understand human nature. Isn't that what life is all about.

With all the rapid changes in the field of communication, it is time for the academics and the professionals to unite. There is so much to learn. New theories of communication are being tested daily.

That is my goal. To prepare students for life and to help bridge the professional and academic world. That should keep me out of trouble for a bit.
