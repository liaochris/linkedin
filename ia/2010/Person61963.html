    (Government Relations industry)

    January 2008 — November 2010 (2 years 11 months)

    The Taxpayer Advocacy Panel (TAP) is an advisory body established by the Secretary of the Treasury pursuant to the Federal Advisory Committee Act, Public Law 92-463 to provide direct citizen input to the Internal Revenue Service (IRS) to help the Service improve its programs and procedures. Term expires by law after 3 years, in November 2010.

    The Taxpayer Advocacy Panel listens to taxpayers, identifies taxpayers’ issues and makes recommendations for improving IRS service and customer satisfaction. The Panel, which is an ethnically-diverse body comprised of 100 civic-minded citizen volunteers from all walks of life representing each state, the District of Columbia and Puerto Rico, provides a national forum for taxpayers to make their voices heard and to help identify “grass roots” tax issues of importance to taxpayers.

    The TAP also works with the Taxpayer Advocate Service and IRS operating divisions to identify issues that will benefit from citizen input and the operating divisions also utilize the TAP to gain pre-decisional input on recommendations regarding key program changes that affect taxpayers.
