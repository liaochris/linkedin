Solomon Kleinsmith’s Summary

Social & political entrepreneurship is most definitely my calling. Over the last several years I've learned the ins and outs of political organizing, through campaigns in both major parties and among independent organizations, as well as gained real world experience founding and directing nonprofits and grassroots groups.

I am currently focusing on building a more effective voice for the vital center of the American electorate. Collaborating with centrist independent and moderate bloggers and activists, we are working on two projects that aim to help organize the independent groundswell that is growing in opposition to the increasingly extreme partisanship coming from the two major parties.

Areas of experience:

- I have managed organizations with budgets ranging from whatever money I could spare to a few hundred thousand dollars.

- I have recruited, hired, fired and managed a paid staff as large as 15 along with hundreds of volunteers, in offices in two cities. Through a focused phone banking, boothing and door knocking campaign we built our list to over sixty two thousand members in a few short months, while registering nearly eleven thousand voters.

- I have designed, coordinated with and pulled the trigger on several small grassroots/guerrilla and online marketing campaigns.

- I have organized, advertised for, and recruited volunteers to staff and/or manage hundreds of meetings and events, for organizations and worthy candidates across the political spectrum.

- I have represented organizations I was a part of in the media dozens of times, ranging from college newspapers, local television stations and even TIME (Obama's Red State Appeal, Nov 2, 2007).

- I've become proficient in website building using the Wordpress and Drupal platforms, and have an eye for design, both online and print.

- I've had great success with online organizing on Facebook, and more recently have learned how to be even more successful using Twitter as a platform to drive traffic.

Solomon Kleinsmith’s Specialties:

I consider myself more of a social entrepreneur rather than an organizer, as I have branched out into learning about more than grassroots and political campaign management.

I have had the most success in volunteer recruiting/management, event organization, online outreach & guerrilla style marketing. I'm a bit of a wonk in some areas, and am capable of launching functional and clean websites quickly, as well as more multifunctional sites with more time.

    Nebraska Matters was a political nonprofit that focused on registering the underserved youth, minority and working family demographics.   • Founder and Chairman

    Omaha for Obama / Yes WE Can Nebraska

    (Political Organization industry)

    February 2007 — July 2008 (1 year 6 months)

    Now dormant local grassroots organization I founded online shortly before Obama jumped into the race in February 2007. I drove out to see him speak at his first campaign stop, in Ames, IA, and came back all "fired up and ready to go". Our first meeting was a few weeks later, leading to a whirlwind of activity over the following year and a half that woke me up to what I wanted to do with my life.

    We gathered thousands of members, were present at as many major events as we could find volunteers for, we made phone calls, knocked on doors, sent hundreds of volunteers to help in Iowa and registered a ton of voters.

    Highest accomplishments were being one of the groups that helped Obama over the top in our congressional district, netting him an historic electoral vote from Nebraska, and setting the foundation for the Obama campaign to take and run with once they set up shop in July of 2008.

    While I have been disappointed in the Obama presidency, I still believe that he was a better choice over the alternatives, and I would not be half the organizer I am without the lessons learned through this experience.
