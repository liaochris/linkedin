Nicola Martinez’s Summary

I teach Privacy, Security, Freedom: Social Concerns for the 21st Century (political philosophy and policy analysis with a strong emphasis on the impact of emerging media and technologies); Digital Storytelling; and independent studies such as The History and Theory of New Media (in Google Wave). I also "steward" a series of courses in digital media, including Digital Art and Design and Information Design; and communications courses such as Interpersonal Communications and Intercultural Communications.

One of the many wonderful aspects of working for SUNY Empire State College is the opportunity to innovate while collaborating across the disciplines. I am the co-author of a science course titled "The Future of Being Human", with colleagues from science and mathematics disciplines. I wrote the sections on emerging technologies and the philosophy of science, media and technology. I also designed the Second Life component, including a machine-driven cyborg avatar (Future Genesis) who provides students with "live" guided tours of the transformation station. I expect to continue to create and collaborate on projects such as these.

Nicola Martinez’s Specialties:

My theoretical research interests include the dynamics of power within the context of taboo and transgression (with a study of ancient Polynesian concepts applied to emergent media such as the virtual world Second Life); virtual citizenry; individual autonomy and human agency within an age of ubiquitous media; and the balance of privacy, security and freedom in the 21st century.

Extensive experience in online curriculum design and development and online teaching and learning.
