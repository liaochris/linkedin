Michael Lackman, PMP’s Summary

Experienced in project managing large, multi-location, multi-company technology projects for high growth companies across a wide variety of industries. Michael has an adaptive project management leadership style that ranges from agile to traditional project management and utilizes best project management practices most appropriate for each project's unique requirements and company culture.

Michael Lackman, PMP’s Specialties:

Significantly improving the business value of technology projects by closely aligning projects with corporate vision and goals; strengthening a company's project management maturity; building high-performance project teams; controlling costs, schedules, scope and quality; identifying and managing risks; and improving communications with the project team, stakeholders, executives, customers & clients.
Certified Project Management Professional (PMP) and member of the Project Management Institute

  • VP Engineering

    Telegroup, Inc

    (Telecommunications industry)

    December 1994 — April 1998 (3 years 5 months)

    Created the Intelligent Networks department to integrate all technology development and support teams for a rapidly growing international telecom carrier into a single, disciplined engineering organization to develop and support a tightly integrated international telecommunications system architecture. Established a Program Management Office (PMO) to foster project management maturity and program managed all technology development projects for the design, development, deployment and operations of all systems, including programmable switching platforms, voice networks, billing, commissions, order entry, provisioning, and customer care. The PMO grew to 5 project managers and improved project management consistency and success within the company. Worked closely with marketing and sales organizations to define new telecom products and services. Built an engineering, software development and operations department from inception to more than 162 engineers, software developers and support staff in five locations worldwide providing service to customers in 170countries. Planned and managed engineering department budgets of $12M without budget overruns.
