  • Coach

    Knebl Communications, Inc.

    (Privately Held; Professional Training & Coaching industry)

    December 1994 — Present (15 years 6 months)

    In my opinion, coaching is not about solving problems, although problems will be solved. It is not primarily about improving performance, attaining goals, or achieving results, although all of that will certainly happen in an effective coaching relationship. I believe coaching is chiefly about discovery, awareness, and choice. It is a way of effectively empowering people to find their own answers, encouraging and supporting them on the path as they continue to make important choices.

    Coaching is a form of conversation with unspoken ground rules regarding certain qualities that must be present: respect, openness, compassion, empathy, and a rigorous commitment to speaking the truth. There are certain assumptions underlying the conversation as well. I assume strength and capability, not weakness, helplessness, or dependence. I assume a deep desire to give the best and achieve potential. My coaching conversations have certain beliefs built into them: that every situation has possibilities and that people really do have the power of choice in their lives.

    I sincerely care that people create what they say they want, and that they follow through when they choose. I assist my clients in clarifying their dreams and goals and hold them accountable and keep them moving forward toward their dreams and goals. From day one, coaching focuses on what my clients want.
