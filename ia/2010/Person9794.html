Anna Marie Kukec’s Summary

Award-winning writer with extensive editing and multimedia experience. Fast, productive and flexible worker, who just loves to dig for news.

Anna Marie Kukec’s Specialties:

As a journalist: Loves to bring a story home to readers. Aims to beat the competition on high-profile stories online or in print. Specialties include business, real estate, law, crime and other news stories. Dives right into searches and quick to source.
As a public relations professional: Organizes complete media package, from press conference, speech and press release, print and electronic coverage, to follow-up questions and materials. Crisis management and strategy.

Anna Marie Kukec’s Experience

  • Business writer

    Daily Herald, Paddock Publications Inc.

    (Privately Held; 501-1000 employees; Newspapers industry)

    2000 — Present (10 years )

    • Lead writer and project coordinator of an award-winning, four-part series about foreclosures in the suburbs, both in print and online with interactive graphics. Appeared on news talk shows on ABC-TV and WGN Radio in Chicago to discuss the series. DuPage County Circuit Court requested to use the graphics as educational tools for defendants.
    • Coverage includes Fortune 500 companies as well as start-ups. Created and continue to write the e-Buzz column about local Web sites and tech companies.
    • Provide updates for Daily Herald Web site; filled in for DuPage bureau city editor and assistant city editors and business editor when needed.

  • Freelance writer

    various publications

    (Newspapers industry)

    1992 — Present (18 years )

    Published in BusinessWeek Chicago, ABA Journal, Chicago Tribune, Chicago Sun-Times, Bar Leader, Chicago Bride magazine and others.

  • Magazine reporter

    American Bar Association

    (Non-Profit; 501-1000 employees; Legal Services industry)

    1995 — 2000 (5 years )

    • Reporter: Wrote about state, local and specialty bar associations, including those for women and minorities. Reported on national legal issues; federal and state court cases; financial practices; association management; non-profit trends and issues. Created beats covering government lawyers and technology's impact on the practice of law.

  • Public information officer, speech writer

    Illinois Attorney General's Office

    (Government Relations industry)

    1989 — 1995 (6 years )

    • Wrote some speeches, talking points and press releases on environmental, consumer, criminal and civil appeal cases. Many cases drew worldwide media attention, including the execution of mass murderer John Wayne Gacy. Helped to develop media strategies, to coordinate press conferences and to arrange press interviews for top level officials.
    • Coordinated a statewide environmental newsletter, Earthwatch.
    • Learned extensively about law, negotiation, government and politics. Translated complex lawsuits, state laws and regulations into understandable language for journalists and the public.

  • Columnist, reporter, section editor

    Daily Southtown

    (Newspapers industry)

    1978 — 1989 (11 years )

    • Section editor: Helped to develop a more Hollywood look to TV Magazine by writing cover stories with interviews of TV celebrities. Duties as TV editor included cover selection, page make-up, headline writing and production of TV Magazine and daily newspaper pages.
    • Columnist: Wrote a consumer action line called Helping Hand, included helping a Chicago man locate his step-sister who worked for the State Department just before the invasion of Afghanistan. Also wrote the Spotlight on Youth column highlighting local teens.
    • Reporter: Wrote news and features on local government, schools and park districts. Major story on a community college professor jailed for espionage in the former Yugoslavia.
