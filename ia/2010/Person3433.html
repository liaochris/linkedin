Adrian Wooster’s Summary

Broadband strategist with over 20 years experience. Special focus on Next Generation Access (NGA) broadband strategy, looking at the frameworks needed to support a fragmenting infrastructure market.

- How can service providers achieve the same reach if there is a multitude of local access networks?

- Can the breadth of choice people enjoy in today's broadband markets be maintained in a next generation broadband world?

Researching wholesale aggregation models needed create a seamless national broadband market which supports innovation and investment.

Adrian Wooster’s Specialties:

next generation broadband network architecture and strategy, technical strategy at a time of change, public and private sector advice, international development through technology, sustainable and environmentally broadband solutions

  • Director

    Kicoshep-K

    (Non-Profit; 11-50 employees; Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    August 2007 — Present (3 years )

    Kicoshep-K is the now famous NGO based in Kibera, Nairobi, that helps people in so many ways cope with life in the world's largest slum. While the heart of their work is in the city slum districts, Kicoshep run a number of self-help programmes all over Kenya, inlcuding HIV/AIDS testing and counselling services.

    The project was the focus of the 2007 Comic Relief Red Nose Day event with the video diaries from Ant & Dec and Billy Connolly coming from the Kicoshep Primary School located in the Kibera slum, which educates and supports over 350 children, most of whom are orphans.

    I was honoured this year to have been asked to join Kicoshep's board.

    For more information about Kicoshep's work see www.kicoshepkenya.org

  • Director

    CBN

    (Telecommunications industry)

    December 2005 — Present (4 years 8 months)

    CBN is a consulting group which grew out of the community-owned broadband schemes when first generation broadband was scarce.

    Today CBN focuses on next generation access projects, working with public and private clients on strategies and project oversight.

    CBN is very actively working with a number of the UK's city authorities and major housing associations on next generation broadband strategies, as well as partnering the UN developing ICT models to support development efforts in Africa.
