Seamus Conlan’s Summary

Created the then 4th largest photo agency in the world, WpN. Named 16th in “100 most important people in photography” in American Photo magazine’s and having taken one of the ‘100 images that changed the world’ by Life Magazine after initiating a photo tracing campaign in Rwanda during the genocide to reunite 21,000 lost children.

The core of what we do is story telling. Whether it is working with a celebrity to promote a TV show, a creative to sell a new lifestyle product or developing a TV show to educate the masses or to just make them smile. It is all story telling, and photography in whatever form it takes allows us to do this. Working as a creative team under Farrell Conlan allows me to produce and develop the imagery to another level.

I have worked in Photography and Media for over 20 years in both creative and management. This understanding of the business I feel is an important role in the development of imagery and it's final use. Understanding the possibilities, the end use and the impact it has allows you to produce stronger more iconic imagery targeted directly at your audience.

Founded World Picture News – WpN in 2001 an international photo agency with over 5,000 media clients with 700 photographers in 72 countries building the agency into a successful brand, the 4th largest in the world, named 16th in the 100 most important people in photography by American Photo before leaving in 2006 to form ‘Farrell Conlan Media’.

Seamus Conlan’s Specialties:

Creating all forms of Imagery for Print, Television, Multi Media and outdoor media.

Seamus Conlan’s Experience

  • COO - Co Founder

    Objective Cinema

    (Online Media industry)

    January 2010 — Present (7 months)

    Objective Cinema is an independent Online distribution network for Objective film makers. Distributing world class quality independent films and documentaries for Film Makers that have an Objective story to tell to the world.

    We are selective about the films and filmmakers we invite into Objective Cinema and we make a commitment to each one. We look for dedicated filmmakers who strive to create films with a voice and a vision about fostering social change and we want filmmakers who strive for excellence in storytelling and technical accomplishment. We are a non-exclusive Online distribution company and we invite filmmakers from all over the world to participate and submit so that our collection is diverse, at times surprising and has an impact on our audience. Our focus is non-exclusive international online distribution whether that is DVD sales, online rentals or streaming download.

    We are building a community network of like minded filmmakers taking a step back from traditional distribution and putting power and money back where it belongs with the filmmakers and the people and issues they work so hard to bring to the forefront.

    Objective Cinema is a Fair Trade Organization, backed by consumers, that are engaged actively in supporting filmmakers, raising awareness and in campaigning for change.

    Consumer website is scheduled to launch July 2010.

  • Co Founder

    International Visual Communication Center

    (Media Production industry)

    January 2009 — Present (1 year 7 months)

    This Not for Profit was launched at the 2009 Oscar Party ‘A Night To Make A Difference’ at Mr.Chows with Leeza Gibbons And Olivia Newton-John.

    With our wealth of experience and knowledge from not only our own professional life but the lives of the extraordinary professionals around us it has compelled us to create a place where aspiring and professional visual communicators can come together to work and exchange ideas, inspire each other and support one another’s work through opportunity, by becoming a community connected by the visual medium. This place is the IVCC.

    The members of IVCC will be dedicated to nurturing new talent and representing some of the world's most accomplished and innovative practitioners, offering expert guidance and inside perspectives.

    We envision a place where young filmmakers and professional photojournalists, commercial DP’s and multimedia whiz kids can come together and showcase their work, learn more about their craft and perhaps try their hand at some new and innovative ways to be creative while encouraging each other to expand their knowledge . Providing support and affordable facilities all housed under one roof so that members can realize their vision. Through educational programs, screenings, exhibitions and large photographic projects the IVCC aims to create a community of visual communicators dedicated to using the visual medium as a tool for change in the world, preservation of history and to come together to serve each other and the community with this universal visual language.

    The IVCC was due to open in Los Angeles in 2010 but due to the present economic climate we have had to delay the process of opening a building until the conditions in our industry start to improve. We would like to thank The Grammy Foundation for their patience and support during this time. The IVCC is currently developing an online platform while continuing to work behind the scenes raising funds and developing the center.

  • Photojournalist

    International photojournalist

    (Media Production industry)

    January 1991 — June 2003 (12 years 6 months)

    Seamus has over 17 years of international photojournalistic and television experience and judges many international photographic awards. He covered during the war in Iraq and set up a news bureau for PEOPLE magazine Time Inc for several months before and during the US led invasion. His coverage as a unilateral in Baghdad during the US bombing campaign and the fall of Baghdad was later used along with his diaries in the book “Witness Iraq’.

    His coverage of news stories and documentaries has been vast where he crossed the front line with the mujahideen for the fall of Kabul; when the US assisted the over throw of the Taliban in Afghanistan. In Gaza his coverage of female suicide bombers led to being driven around bind folded in the trunk of the car in order to get the story and not put anyone at risk. During the NATO invasion of Kosovo however he did end up kidnapped for a short period on his way through Albania for GQ magazine. Every country has a different story and Seamus has covered a lot of them they including coverage in Northern Ireland, Bosnia, Kosovo, Somalia, Serbia, Eritrea, Ethiopia, Burundi, India, Kashmir, Malaysia, Thailand, Turkey, South Africa, Sierra Leone, Nigeria and Zimbabwe for magazines such as LIFE magazine, National Geographic, People Magazine, Newsweek, TIME, Stern and Paris Match. Seamus has also worked for TV news organizations such as CNN, The BBC, APTV, NHK, Canal Plus, ITV, Channel 4 Television to name a few.

  • Consultant

    United Nations

    (Non-Profit; International Affairs industry)

    July 1994 — August 1996 (2 years 2 months)

    UNITED NATIONS / ICRC – Rwanda Project :1994-1996


    July 1, 1994; During the Rwandan war in 1994 Seamus Conlan brought together the resources of the United Nations Children's Fund, the International Committee of the Red Cross, Eastman Kodak and Life Magazine to help reunite more than 21,000 lost children with their parents, using photographic images. The process evolved into one of the largest traveling photo exhibitions in history and is now a standard form of tracing people in developing nations.

    A compilation of these images was chosen by LIFE Magazine as the ‘100 images that changed the world’ after appearing in thousands of publications and television programs around the world. This collection was then later honored by the ‘Directors Club’.

    Exhibition Details:
