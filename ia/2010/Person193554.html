Stefan Groschupf’s Summary

In the age of 19 I worked on a search engine for the university of my home town. They liked my work so much that the volunteer project turned into a commercial project and I founded my first company media style - now 101tec GmbH in late 1996.
Later on I was named one of the 100 Most Important Young Germans", in Stern Neon Magazine 2003.

Within the last decade we worked for companies like BMW, VW, Intel, Siemens, Roche, German Government, EMI Music etc. and some of the most promising start ups in Silicon Valley and I trained engineers from Apple, Sun, HP or Orange on cloud computing technology.

Being an active open source contributor some of my code runs in the largest organisations but also helps in small university or non profit projects getting the job done. Beside contributions to, Hadoop, Nutch, Pig, Gradle, JBoss I founded projects like Katta - Lucene in the cloud, Webdav Servlet or Weta - the first open source map reduce implementation.
