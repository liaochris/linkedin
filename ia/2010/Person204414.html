Timothy Shey’s Summary

My fifteen years as a designer, producer and entrepreneur have included award-winning, industry-leading projects in television, mobile, and the web.

As one of Next New Networks' founders, I've led the company's creative and platform strategy from its beginnings in late 2006. As the original head of development and programming, I assembled the most diverse and successful portfolio of original video entertainment programming on the Internet, including hit networks ThreadBanger, Indy Mogul, Fast Lane Daily, Barely Political, $99 Music Videos, and Ultra Kawaii, racking up over 1 billion video views and ten Webby Awards to date, more than any other independent media company.

In 1996, I co-founded Proteus, a pioneering interactive agency, which was responsible for the first-ever nationwide interactive TV broadcast using mobile phones during FOX’s Super Bowl XXXVI, and produced mobile phone-based interactive TV experiences for NBC's 2004 Olympics and four seasons of NFL, NASCAR, and MLB on FOX Sports. My nine years' work as creative director also included major interactive campaigns for AT&T, Sony, Sprint, Newsweek, ExxonMobil, The Washington Post, Gibson, and Motorola, and network-wide mobile content offerings for HBO, ABC, Discovery, and FOX.

In addition to my work at Proteus and Next New Networks, I've consulted on interactive strategy for organizations such as Brookings, A&E, History Channel, and People for the American Way, and worked as a producer on landmark early web series such as Rocketboom, Amanda Across America, and Afterworld.
