Chris Avellone’s Summary

Co-founder and Creative Director of Obsidian Entertainment, which is a hell of an awesome thing. I didn't realize there were jobs like this, and I'm upset my high school guidance counselor neglected to mention this as a career option.

This position involves a lot of design work where needed, design reviews, management duties, designer hiring, and dreaming up new worlds. Current position: senior designer on Fallout: New Vegas.

I started out as a paper-game designer then a computer game designer at Interplay Entertainment waaaaaaay back in 1995, remained there through the Black Isle Division's rise and fall, contributing to most (if not all) its RPGs over an eight year period. Over the years, I've worked with Interplay, Reflexive, Snowblind, LucasArts, Atari, and SEGA. RPG titles I've worked on include:

Fallout 2 (Level Designer)
Planescape: Torment (Lead Designer)
Icewind Dale 1 (Level Designer)
Icewind Dale: Heart of Winter (Level Designer)
Icewind Dale 2 (Level Designer)
Dark Alliance 1 (Senior Designer)
Baldur's Gate 3 (canceled, Senior Designer)
Fallout 3 (canceled, Co-Lead Designer)
Champions of Norrath (Level Designer and Script Doctor)
Star Wars: Knights of the Old Republic II: The Sith Lords (Lead Designer)
Neverwinter Nights 2 (Senior Designer)
Neverwinter Nights 2: Mask of the Betrayer (Senior Designer)
Aliens (Creative Lead Designer)
Alpha Protocol (Lead Designer)
Fallout: New Vegas (Senior Designer)

I've spent almost a decade and a half in Computer Game Design, and five years in pen-and-paper game design and writing.

I also write and doodle in my spare time - writing credits include game magazine articles (Dragon, Shadis, Adventurer's Club), and scripts for the Star Wars Tales and Clone Wars Adventures comic books for Dark Horse. I also do illustrations for books and comic books as well (the Role-Playing Gamers Bible, Knights of the Dinner Table).

Chris Avellone’s Specialties:

Computer Game Design, Writing Comics, Dreaming. I'd like to write a novel at some point, but you know how time works - I figure I'd start with comics and cartooning and see how that goes.
