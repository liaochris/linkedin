Ray Hayes’s Summary

AIM: rayhayes MSN: rayhayes@rhbe.net ICQ:232-563-553

I got my first break into the field of information technology on the midnight to 8 am shift in 1981 typing on a DEC LA120 connected to some kind of real estate leasing application. Soon it'll be 30 years. I've been writing software in the Group Policy sector for what seems like forever. Before that, I was writing Java in the Oracle Application Server market. I've worked under and learned from some very talented professionals. I've realized lately that I've gotten very narrowly focused and the fun is gone out of technology so I'm working hard now to widen my exposure to new technologies and new techniques.

I worked as an employee of CellularOne on their VAX systems until 1999 using VAX BASIC, COBOL, ACMS, FMS, and RDB; acronyms that seem ancient and crusty now. Before that I worked for myself for about 5 years working on many "for bid" projects in the municipal IT sector especially for the City of Boston. I was at DEC, Reebok, and Aldrich Eastman and Waltch in various IT roles in the 80's.

My goal has always been to create something really cool and have fun.
