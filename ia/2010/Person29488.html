Michael Hamilton’s Summary

I currently work at BBC Radio 5 live - a news, sports and entertainment network in the UK.

My previous experience includes reading news bulletins on more than 25 local, regional and national radio stations across the UK.

Before joining 5 live I worked for Beat106, Juice FM Liverpool, Xfm Manchester, Northsound Radio, Kerrang Radio, BRMB, the BBC in the West Midlands, and Viking FM. I have also worked with Sony Award winning presenters including Christian O'Connell, Robin Banks and Stephen Nolan.

My qualifications include a postgraduate diploma in journalism from the University of Leeds (including law), an arts degree from the University of Liverpool (where I also set up and ran their first ever student radio station - Icon Radio), and an Associated Board Diploma in piano performance.

I currently live in Liverpool and work on the Stephen Nolan and Tony Livesey programmes, which are made in Manchester. My work has ranged from election coverage and breaking news (e.g. the Haiti earthquake) to Children in Need and the 2010 World Cup.

My aim is to continue creating innovative and powerful radio, capable of winning the Rajar battle and awards. I want to continue training/developing the next generation of talent.

Michael Hamilton’s Specialties:

Running a news team, bulletin reading, presentation, programme production, news reporting. Editing audio/packages. Experience on a range of newsroom systems including Burli, ENPS and TeamNews, and playout systems including Enco, Maestro and Dalet.
