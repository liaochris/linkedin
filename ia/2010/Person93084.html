Jeff Davis’s Summary

I run two companies related to food public relations:

Food Fête: After spending two decades as a public relations professional, including running my own 8-person agency, I combined my culinary passions and PR know-how to launch a sponsored media events business that showcases new food, beverage and kitchenware products to the press.

The first Food Fête event (www.foodfete.com) debuted in Jan.' 05, quickly becoming a valuable showcase for presenting new food, beverage and housewares products to food, beverage and lifestyle journalists.

Today, Food Fête has evolved into the "must-attend" press event for new food and beverage products. More than 130 food media attended the Summer '09 Food Fête in New York City. In 2009, we also debuted Food Fete Oregon, a press event held in NYC exclusively for Oregon-made food products.

We are always looking for companies with new products for our events, as well as PR agencies and consumer brands that want to participate as exhibitors or sponsors.

In 2010, we debuted "Food Fête Delivered," an extension of our media events that simplifies the process of getting new product samples into the hands of the food press and bloggers. We send a single shipment of a limited number of food products to approx. 100 media for potential review. The value-add is that it comes from Food Fête and goes to a group of food writers who know and trust Food Fete to deliver trend-setting new products.

My other company is FoodSmart Communications, a niche PR consultancy born out of the relationships built through our Food Fête events. What differentiates our approach is that the media relations strategy is 100% permission-based. Only editors and bloggers who opt-in to receive client information will be pitched. We currently have nearly 250 food writers and bloggers on our opt-in list. Visit www.foodsmartpr.com for more information. We only take on a few clients a year who want our highly focused services.
