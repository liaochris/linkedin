Kathy O'Dell’s Summary

An art historian and critic of modern and contemporary art, Dr. O'Dell has been a professor in the Department of Visual Arts at UMBC since 1992. In fall of 2001, she joined the Dean's Office of the College of Arts, Humanities, and Social Sciences as a full-time administrator, continuing her scholarly research.

Her portfolio in the Dean's Office can be found in the Experience section below.

Her portfolio as an ART HISTORIAN & CRITIC includes the following:

• Author of Book: CONTRACT WITH THE SKIN: MASOCHISM, PERFORMANCE ART, AND THE 1970S, University of Minnesota Press, 1998

• Currently Coauthoring: WORLD ART SINCE 1945, major survey text, forthcoming 2012, Laurence King Publishers, UK. Coauthor Kristine Stiles of Duke University

• Articles & Reviews: ARTFORUM, ART IN AMERICA, LUSITANIA, PERFORMANCE RESEARCH, and TDR: THE DRAMA REVIEW

• Cofounder & Editor: LINK: A CRITICAL JOURNAL ON THE ARTS. Chief-edited issue on “Hysteria,” 2000

• Curator: “Kate Millett, Sculptor: The First 38 Years,” 1997, first retrospective of this well-known feminist author but little-known visual artist (UMBC, then traveled)

• Lectures: Getty Research Institute (L.A.); Guggenheim Museum of Art (N.Y.); Royal Danish Academy of Art (Copenhagen); Danish Design School (Copenhagen), Mt. Allison University (Canada), Manchester University (U.K.), and the John Hansard Gallery (U.K.)
