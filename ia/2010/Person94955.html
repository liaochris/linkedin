Jennifer Gonzalez’s Summary

I am currently working in environmental and urban planning at the Passaic County Department of Planning and Economic Development in Totowa, NJ. My professional interests and career goals include climate change at the local level and exploring what local government can do through effective planning to mitigate the impacts of land use on climate change. To achieve these goals I practice principles of smart growth and strategies for sustainable neighborhood development, urban design, and transportation planning. I am pursuing these interests in my current position by working on and Energy Master Plan, Facilities Plan, and Transportation Element update to the Passaic County Master Plan.

I am a recent graduate with a M.A. in Environmental Policy Design from Lehigh University. Through my graduate program I have gained knowledge, created proposals, and conducted research in various areas of environmental policy, including Sustainability, Land Use Planning, US and International Environmental Regulatory Law, Environmental Justice, Global Environmental Security, and Policy Design. As an undergraduate I completed a double major in International Relations and Spanish, with a minor in Communications. I spent four months studying Spanish culture, literature, and business in Seville, Spain where I lived with a Spanish family.
