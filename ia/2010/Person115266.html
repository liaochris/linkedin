Kevin Beary’s Summary

Sheriff Beary has served in the Law Enforcement and Security fields for the past 34 years. He's been an innovator in the Community Policing, Homeland Security, Emergency Management, and Violent Crime Strategies. In 2003, The National Sheriff’s Association selected Sheriff Beary as the “National Sheriff of the Year”.

Sheriff Beary rose through the ranks as a Police Officer, Detective, Deputy Sheriff, and Crime Scene Technician. He was elected Sheriff of Orange County, Florida and served four terms. He led one of the largest and most professional Law Enforcement Operations in the country.

Sheriff Beary managed the Florida Sheriff’s Association Task Force, which responded to several wildfires and hurricanes within the State of Florida and successfully managed the recovery of the Waveland, Mississippi region after Hurricane Katrina.

The Federal Communications Commission selected him as a member of the “Post Katrina Review Committee”. He co- lead the Region 5 , Domestic Security Task Force after the Sept. 11 attacks on the U.S., and successfully maneuvered several State and Federal Grants to protect the Central FL Region. He was commended for his Private-Public Partnerships in the Intelligence area and was instrumental in the Central FL Fusion Center becoming a reality.

He was a strong advocate of Crime Prevention and Children’s Programs, and took a strong stance on fighting violent crime. He is a graduate of the FBI Academy 143rd Session, the FBI Leadership in Counter-Terrorism Program 2nd Session, the FBI National Executive Institute 18th Session, and the Police Executive Research Forum.

He recently returned from Afghanistan where he mentored the Afghan National Police Counter-Terrorism General Officer and the Counter-Terrorism police, Task Force 24.
