Brooke Warner’s Summary

Brooke Knight Warner is an Internet pioneer and content thought leader who in 1999 was the vision and early muscle behind Mom.com, which took a then cutting-edge, Oprah-like approach to creating parenting media at a time when most were still focused on flaky pie crusts and high gloss kitchen floors. Soon after, this sensibility became parenting zeitgeist. In its day, Mom.com delivered more high-quality content daily with eight producer/editors than iVillage did with 40 editors using an aggregation model, as well as a user- and expert- generated approach. Prior to developing Mom.com, Brooke was the co-producer/co-creator of one of the earliest, most successful cyber soaps, Bhive.com, producing a two full seasons and 44 episodes in 1997/1998. In working with Red Bull, she utilized a radical social networking and enthusiast-oriented approach in developing an online strategy before those were well-known models. While an astute business person with an eye constantly on revenue models, what this tells you about Brooke is that she not only recognizes trends in their nascent stages, but also that she efficiently and effectively captures those trends to drive market opportunity. Over the last 10 years she has created the online strategies and/or run online business operations for a variety companies including Red Bull (redbull.com, givemewings.net), Times Mirror, Primedia (EquiSearch.com), Hispanic Business, ConsumerSearch.com, Bill Cosby’s Picture Pages, Hanley Wood (ebuild.com) and most recently, WBOC/Draper Digital Media. In 2003 she developed a media integration strategy for Hispanic Business that was often emulated by other traditional media companies. Before the dawn of the dot com days she worked in publishing, marketing and film production. When not identifying and capturing trends that drive the economy you can find her at home with her two exuberant dogs, two rambunctious sons and one very patient husband.

Integrating new and traditional media business models, social media, new media products development, emerging cultural trends, cultivating new market opportunities,start-ups, fundraising, strategic planning, leadership, audience engagement, lean operations, media and entertainment, LOHAS, social entrepreneurship and green marketing.
