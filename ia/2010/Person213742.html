Lachlan Hibbert-Wells’s Summary

I have loved the internet ever since my early experiences with text-only web pages and message board communities. I particularly remember finding a full list of Alfred Hitchcock’s cameos (information I had been seeking for years), that I just couldn’t get from anywhere else. From that point, I was hooked.

Years later I graduated from a Cultural Studies degree with Honours, having spent the best part of my studies examining internet culture, online communities and changing modes of spectatorship caused by the proliferation of digital media.

Online spaces are more than the technology and code used to build them: they are living, dynamic social spaces. In addition to my academic interactions with digital technology, I live and breathe the web – to me, understanding interactivity is all about the experience. I spend the majority of my free time browsing online communities - through websites, blogs, forums, RSS feeds and services including Twitter, Facebook and Flickr. Not only am I comfortable online as an observer, I also participate actively through creating my own digital content.

I am passionate about everything the internet has to offer and posses an understanding of online communities which goes far beyond basic user experience.

While I am not a coder or technician, I am an expert in the web at large - from a cultural, historical and technological perspective. I understand how it works, and why, as well as how to make the experience better for all. I know the sorts of things people are after, and where and how to find them - or I can make plans to create them if they don’t yet exist. My talents lie in explaining concepts to those who are only just encountering the possibilities of the internet, as well as those who have been interacting online for years and would like to expand their knowledge. Basically, the internet is part of my life, and I want to make sure it is open for everyone to make it part of theirs.
