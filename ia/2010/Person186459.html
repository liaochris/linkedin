Sergio Consoli’s Summary

Dr. Sergio Consoli is currently employed by Softeco Sismat S.p.A. in Genoa as Software Engineer since March 2009. In December 2008, he was awarded with a Ph.D. in Operational Research at the School of Information Systems, Computing and Mathematics at Brunel University, working on the NET-ACE (Network Theory Applied to the Computing and Engineering) project. The NET-ACE project was sponsored by the European Union Marie Curie Programme as an Early Stage Training Site, and it included a programme of skills trainings, an industrial secondment, and research placements in leading EU universities. From May 2006 until October 2006, Dr. Sergio Consoli attended an university placement at the Universidad de La Laguna (Tenerife) while, from May 2007 until March 2008, he was on an industrial secondment within the Media Interaction Group, Intelligent Algorithms Cluster, at Philips Research Eindhoven.

His general areas of research include, but is not limited, to the development and application of Metaheuristics in Combinatorial and Continue Optimization, with special emphasis to problems in Graph Theory and Network Theory. His research appears in several international journals, books, and prestigious conferences.

In April 2005, he further obtained a Masters Degree in Computer Engineering, with specialization in Industrial Automation and Control Technologies, with the final mark of 110/110 cum laude. During his experimental thesis he worked for 9 months at the Department of Systems, Electrical and Electronic Engineering of Catania University (Italy), with the cooperation of the Automation group of STMicroelectronics. Furthermore, he has a High School Diploma of expert in Electronics and Telecommunications, with final mark 60/60.

  • Internship at Media Interaction Group

    Philips Research

    (Public Company; PHG; Electrical/Electronic Manufacturing industry)

    May 2007 — March 2008 (11 months)

    Dr. Sergio consoli worked on a research project for the comparison of different heuristic algorithms applied to the Quartet Method of hierarchical clustering. Given a set of objects and their pairwise distances, the Quartet Method is aimed to determine a visual representation of the data by computing a hierarchy of clusters of the objects. As this problem is NP-hard, Several metaheuristics were proposed and compared, in order to produce the best approximation of the optimal hierarchy.

  • Research placement

    Universidad de La Laguna

    (Research industry)

    June 2007 — October 2007 (5 months)

    Dr. Sergio Consoli worked on a research project which consisted of the exploitation of metaheuristics for the minimum labelling spanning tree problem. Given an undirected labeled connected graph, the aim is to find a spanning tree whose edges have the smallest number of distinct labels. A Greedy Randomized Adaptive Search Procedure, different versions of Variable Neighborhood Search, and some evolutionary algorithms were proposed, and they were compared with the other algorithms recommended in the literature.
