Brian McDermott’s Summary

I spent 20 years as an "Operations Partner" in a private equity firm, running specialty retailing companies, with a successful track record in rapid growth, turnaround and startup situations.

Perhaps my biggest success is Leslies's Poolmart, which increased, under my leadership, as follows:

Store Count:--------- 66 - 385 stores
Revenues:-------------$57M - $$285M
EBITDA:-------------- $2M - $20M

It became the dominant retailer of swimming pool supplies. Leslies today numbers over 600 stores.

I have twice run a company named BusyBody Home Fitness, a leading retailer of high end home fitness equipment. First we grew BusyBody from 30 stores to 80 and revenues from $57M to $117M, successfully selling it. Later we bought 16 stores from a Chapter 11 bankruptcy and grew it to over 100 stores.

Most recently, I served as President and CEO of Right Start, which was a major turnaround, acquired from Chapter 11. It initially had 34 stores and highly unprofitable operations but ultimately achieved operating profitability. We closed stores, opened others in new markets; re-branded the business; revamped the merchandise assortment; repositioned and updated e-commerce operations; and, re-launched a Catalog dormant for two years. Same store sales, transaction size and margin all grew double digits. E-commerce sales tripled.

In addition to those companies, I was CEO of a "bricks and clicks" internet/retail integration (and raw start up) called PartsAmerica.com. There we succeeded in offering one hour fulfillment of orders nationwide for a full range of auto parts. We connected 3000 retail stores and 6 distribution centers to our e-commerce engine. Marketing programs achieved sales response rates as high as 10%.

I am looking for a value added, impactful executive management position in retail, consumer products or related services I'm also available for Board duty and would consider an equity infusion for the right situation.

Brian McDermott’s Specialties:

General management with P&L responsibility; cash flow optimization, especially under leveraged or private equity-funded conditions; excellent at building and motivating management teams; aggressive sales and marketing focus; rapid growth strategies and implementation; turnarounds and restructurings; deep understanding of retail operations; training, selling skills and sales force management.Oh, and strong finance and investment skills as well. Good experience and excellent instincts.
