Michael Conti’s Summary

Michael Conti is veteran business communications professional with a core competency in Media Arts and Communications. Michael began his career with "King of the Bs" Roger Corman, moved to Paramount Pictures and migrated into the early gaming industry where he then landed marketing and production positions with Mattel and Intel as a Program Manager in interactive smart toy products. Throughout his career Michael has worked as a producer, editor, director and educator in film and video. He holds a Bachelor's Degree in English from The Colorado College and attended film-making programs at the American Film Institute in Los Angeles.

He teaches in multiple colleges and is also the Executive Director of the award winning The Shoot Out 24 Hour Filmmaking Festival in his spare time.

Michael Conti’s Experience

  • Producer

    Cheyenne International Film Festival

    (Motion Pictures and Film industry)

    December 2009 — Present (5 months)

    The Cheyenne International Film Festival (CIFF) is a production of Wyoming Community Media (WCM), a 501(c)(3) organization.
    The mission of WCM is to encourage and celebrate the art of film and digital media through creative forums for artistic and social awareness in the community served.

    WCM offers a variety of programs throughout the year including: (1) the Cheyenne International Film Festival (CIFF); (2) the Wyoming Film Institute training and outreach; (3) The Shoot Out Cheyenne 24 Hour Film Making festival.
    CIFF offers audiences an opportunity to see films from around the world and network with and learn from producers, director, writers and photographers.
    WCM offers year-round community educational outreach programs and screenings including the Shoot Out Top 10 Films, the Wyoming Film Institute and the Film-related tourism conference.
    Through partnerships with Downtown Cheyenne, the Downtown Cheyenne Merchants’ Association, the Wyoming Film Office, the City of Cheyenne, and the Cheyenne Area Convention and Visitors’ Bureau, WCM is creating a strong community-based infrastructure to support film and digital media.

  • Executive Director

    The Shoot Out 24 Hour Filmmaking Festival Boulder

    (Non-Profit; Motion Pictures and Film industry)

    July 2004 — Present (5 years 10 months)

    I brought the first ever 24-hour filmmaking festival of its kind to Boulder, Colorado, in October of 2004. At that first event, nearly 400 people attended, divided into 74 teams, I executive produced 66 short films in just 24 hours. The event included several mini-events including a sponsor party, a breakfast bar, preliminary judging venues and an after party. I enlisted the help of over 50 volunteers, and filled the historic Boulder Theater to capacity (900 seats) for the big event screening of the top ten films of the contest. The event has been voted the Best New Film Festival by Westword in 2004, and Best Film Festival in 2007, Best Local Film Festival 2009 and has received wide regional press coverage.
