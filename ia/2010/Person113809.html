Kelly Coller’s Summary

I've been working in the marketing, fund raising, branding, new business, special events, community outreach, and public relations world since 1994 -- starting off as special events intern for the Northwest Aids Foundation. Pilchuck Glass School was the launch pad of my career, and where I met my husband/designer Tony Secolo.

I moved up the ranks of the fund-raising world (Pilchuck Glass School, Intiman Theatre, Seattle Opera), helping to set the strategy, marketing program, community outreach, special events and PR to raise awareness, attract new audiences, and bring in lots and lots and lots of money to well deserving high profile arts organizations.

During this time (thanks in a large part to Tony) I really fell in the love with the process and end-products of branding and graphic design, and made a switch from selling arts organizations to selling design and business tools, and became the Director of New Business for NBBJ's branding and design studio (1999). While there, we grew the studio from a little in-house studio with gross sales of $200,000 to an internationally award winning studio (with outside clients) and gross sales of $1,000,000 in less than 5 years.

In July of 2004, I was hired to work in ZIBA Design's brand new client services and account management department to work on comprehensive programs for companies like Purina, and made the move from Seattle to Portland.

In 2005, I left ZIBA to help Tony open OFFICE PDX, a brand-new concept in retail and design. Today, we've changed our business model to online, pop up retail, wholesale and graphic design. We've been recommended by The New York Times among other prestigious online and print magazines.

Through OFFICE, I met multi-disciplinary design firm, Twenty Four Seven (www.twentyfour7.com), fell in love with them, and became their Marketing Director in July of 2006.

And yes, I have two jobs and love them both.
