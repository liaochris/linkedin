Laura Wright’s Summary

I am a recent graduate of the Counseling program at the University of North Texas. I received my B.A. in Psychology in May of 2005 and my M.S. in Counseling in August of 2009. I chose to pursue intership with the Children's Advocacy Center for Denton County, where I was able to gain skills and knowledge that will stay with me throughout my professional and personal life. I enjoy working with children and teenagers. Play therapy is fascinating to me, and teenagers are a wonderful challenge. I wish to continue working with victims of childhood abuse and their families as this I have found to be rewarding for both the children and myself.

I am now pursuing temporary licensure as an LPC. I have passed the NCE (yeah!), and now searching for licensed professional that is able to supervise my 3000 hours toward full licensure.

In addition, I am gathering all necessary materials to apply for the Ph.D. program in the Department of Psychology at Baylor University. All that is left on my part is fine tuning my personal statement. Since I was a little girl, I have always dreamed of attending Baylor. Now is my chance, and I am very excited at the possibility of being admitted acceptance to their program.

I have worked in the mental health field since 2005. I started as a Mental Health Technician at Sante Center for Healing, then as an Intake Specialist, Adult Outpatient Coordinator, and Youth Services Discharge Coordinator from 2006-2008. I have also had experience in schools, as I have organized therapy groups for elementary age students and later worked as a substitute teacher for grades k-8. I am currently working with Denton County MHMR as an on call QMHP on the Mobile Crisis Outreach Team, providing crisis services after hours.
