Kate Lister’s Summary

Through research, advocacy, writing, and consulting, my goal is to help companies and communities adopt telecommuting and alternative workplace/workforce strategies that are proven to improve morale and work-life balance, reduce our carbon footprint, increase productivity, save money, reduce stress, improve disaster preparedness, and add employment opportunities for the unemployed, underemployed, retirees, and disabled.

These strategies include a combination of telework, telecommuting, co-working, outsourcing, results-based management, and flexible work.

Kate Lister’s Experience

  • Lead Researcher

    Telework Research Network

    (Privately Held; Professional Training & Coaching industry)

    2010 — Present (less than a year)

    The Telework Research Network provides telecommuting and alternative workplace consulting, research, benchmarking and management cases, advocacy, and training. Our research has been quoted in the Wall Street Journal, Harvard Business Review, and dozens of other publications.

    Our proprietary model has been used by company and community leaders throughout the U.S. and Canada to quantify the benefits of alternative work strategies.

    Less than 2% of U.S. employees work from home the majority of the time (not including the self-employed), but 40% hold jobs that are compatible with telework. If those employees who wanted to did so just half of the time our model shows that:

    The Nation would:

    - Save 289 million barrels of oil—equivalent to 37% of our Persian Gulf imports
    - Reduce greenhouse gases by 53 million tons/year
    - Reduce road travel by 115 billion miles/year saving
    - Save 100,000 people from traffic-related injury or death
    - Raise the standard of living in rural and disadvantaged areas

    Businesses would:

    - Increase productivity by over $235 billion
    - Save $124 billion in real estate and related costs
    - Save $77 billion in absenteeism and turnover
    - Improve continuity of operations
    - Avoid environmental sanctions
    - Improve work life balance and better address the needs of families, parents, and senior caregivers.
    - Reduce the ‘brain drain’ effect of retiring boomers
    - Be able to recruit and retain the best people
    - Better address the needs of disabled workers, rural residents, and military families

    Individuals would:

    - Achieve a better work-life balance
    - Recoup 2-3 weeks of free time per year—time they’d have otherwise spent commuting
    - Save $2,000-$7,000/year
    - Save $15 billion at the pumps
    - Suffer fewer illnesses
