Eric Lancaster’s Summary

Eric Lancaster has been a pilot for over 20 years starting as a flight instructor based in Southern California and working his way up through the ranks by working line service, managing an FBO, flying single engine charter, twin engine charter, Assistant Chief Pilot, Chief Pilot, Lead Pilot and Check Airman.

He has flown a variety of aircraft in a variety of environments from the early morning/late night freight runs to the low-level wildland fire environment to the flight levels transporting patients. He's flown single-engine piston trainers through light twin-engine planes to over 12,500 lbs jump ships to cabin-class turbo props.

Along the way he has managed over 20 pilots and 10 aircraft for FAR Part 135 Air Taxi Operator. Reviewed resumes, evaluated and selected applicants for pilot positions. Oversaw daily operations of Fixed Based Operation at Rialto Municipal Airport. And coordinated with maintenance department for delivery of aircraft for maintenance.

Eric was instrumental in streamlining and reorganizing pilot information folders to comply with FAA regulations. He was often trusted to reconcile company credit and fuel purchase cards utilizing Excel spreadsheets. He also utilized MS Word and Excel to create time-saving documents.

Eric brings with him tried and true experience in all aspects of flight operations from the ground (Line service) up (Chief Pilot).

Eric Lancaster’s Specialties:

Certified Flight Instructor with Instrument and Multi Engine privileges. Former Check Airman for the USDA Forest Service. Former Chief Pilot for an FAR 135 Air Taxi Operator specializing in small feeder freight and charter. Former FAR 135 Company Instructor.
