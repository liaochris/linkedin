Stephen Lee’s Summary

Stephen T. Lee is Senior Technical Executive, Power Delivery and Utilization, in the Electric Power Research Institute, Palo Alto, California, USA.

Dr. Stephen Lee is a critical thinker, innovator and one of the top Thought Leaders of EPRI. His ability to analyze complex system phenomena and derive essential lessons from their failures enables him to identify systemic root causes and propose effective cures to the structural problem, whether it is through innovative policy or engineering solutions.

Examples include:
• California power market crisis in 2000
• Virtual Regional Transmission Organization (VRTO) 2002
• Automatic Transmission Toll Collection 2002
• Northeast Blackout of August 13, 2003
• Third Way of Unity in Diversity 2004
• Holistic Planning 2007
• Vulnerability to Cascading Outages 2008
• Holistic Vision of the Smart Grid 2009

Dr. Lee has over 40 years of electric power industry experience. He received his S.B., S.M., E.E. and Ph.D. degrees from M.I.T. in Electrical Engineering, majoring in Power System Engineering, in 1969, 1970, 1971 and 1972, respectively. He worked for Stone & Webster Engineering Corporation in Boston, Systems Control, Inc. in California, and he was Vice President of Consulting for Energy Management Associates (EMA). Before joining EPRI in 1998, Dr. Lee was an independent Consultant in utility planning and operation.

Stephen Lee’s Specialties:

At EPRI, Stephen Lee is involved with technical research programs for power system analysis, planning and operations, and has been actively developing new concepts and tools for power system operation and probabilistic transmission planning. He is also a co-chairperson of the NERC Reliability Fundamentals Working Group.
