David Rosen’s Summary

Deeply experienced consumer and technology marketing executive, balancing brand-focused creativity with powerful marketing analytics and next-generation technology acumen. Measurable track record of relevant customer engagement for some of the world’s top consumer brands.

A frequent speaker and author on the topic of developing sustainable, profitable consumer relationships across multiple channels, David has deep, hands-on knowledge of strategic and tactical marketing issues challenging retail, consumer products, media and travel.

David Rosen’s Experience

  • SVP, Strategy and Channel Development

    Loyalty Lab, Inc.

    (Privately Held; Computer Software industry)

    October 2006 — Present (4 years 1 month)

    San Francisco-based Loyalty Lab, is the market-leading provider of technologies and services for marketers seeking to drive significant improvement in the value of their customers. Loyalty Lab’s 100 clients span the spectrum of consumer-focused industries: top tier retailers, travel providers, restaurants, financial services and media including Sears, Virgin America, HSBC, Nine West, Guitar Center, AutoNation and Cracker Barrel.

    Loyalty Lab continues to innovate by extending transactional-based recognition to interactional-based engagement – allowing its partner clients to significantly expand their definitions of consumer loyalty and lifetime value through the capture of social media and community.

    As a member of the company’s leadership team, David has responsibility for spearheading Loyalty Lab’s intellectual capital and building and managing our strategic sales channels.

    David is a frequent speaker, author and blogger on the topics of customer retention, social media, loyalty programs and interactive marketing.

  • Senior Vice President, Marketing

    MyPoints (a United Online company)

    (Public Company; UNTD; Internet industry)

    September 2003 — October 2006 (3 years 2 months)

    MyPoints is the internet's largest and most successful loyalty program. Over 8 million MyPoints members earn MyPoints for shopping online, responding to targeted emails and taking surveys from leading consumer marketers.

    While at MyPoints, David had responsibility for managing the company’s multi-million person membership base. Responsibilities included: member acquisition, member retention and activity, marketing insights, user experience, site design and the relationships with MyPoints' 250-plus retail shopping network.

    David was a member of the executive leadership team that successfully sold the company to United Online after a two-year period of nearly 100% annual growth.

  • Vice President, Corporate Development

    chipshot.com

    (Retail industry)

    April 1999 — July 2002 (3 years 4 months)

    Golf-focused Chipshot, was a leading ecommerce, manufacturing and media pioneer (annual runrate in excess of $30 mm, the #1 online golf retailer (Gomez Advisors), 12th largest e-commerce site (PC Data), and Best of the Web 2000 (Forbes) award recipient.) before being acquired in 2001.

    While at chipshot.com, David had responsibility for all product and market expansions. He led strategic and operational extensions into golf apparel and fitness categories, served as GM of chipshot. Asia (based in Tokyo), and led M&A activities by chipshot.com of Golfserv (media) and Impact Golf (equipment) and of chipshot to the buy-out firm Interface Capital and Times Digital.

  • Principal/Partner

    Mercer Management Consulting (now Oliver Wyman)

    (Public Company; MMC; Management Consulting industry)

    1985 — 1999 (14 years )

    Partner of the Retail/Consumer practice. Sold and managed million-dollar client relationships throughout the world in the following industries: retail (children, apparel, toys, mass and specialty); consumer products (sports, juvenile products, packaged goods); technology (wireless telephony, cable TV, media); and financial services (retail banking, credit card, insurance).
