Howard Berkowitz’s Summary

My greatest successes tend to come from filling niches between dissimilar technologies. Some of my current network security work involves melding medical infectious disease epidemiology with virtual worms and viruses. Many computer scientists have worked with virtual pathogens, but I have had the special awareness of working with the biological sort, which can kill more than an operating system.
I was an evangelist for structured programming when systems programmers liked to write in hexadecimal. My long-term involvement in network management began when neither systems software nor telecommunications designers wanted that responsibility, and that has led to a long interest in highly available networks and computing. High availability, in turn, led me into advising on national critical infrastructure and emergency services.
I have strong verbal communications skills and have written several books and RFCs, and presented extensively. My roots, however, are in real engineering, not "training".
Medicine has always been a passion, and I frequently am the person designated to give a clinician confidence I understand the medical sciences. Pharmacology has been a passion since before I was a teenager. "I'm not a doctor but I play one on a computer." This means that I can go to clinicians, speak their language and understand what they want to do, and then explain these in terms of network and computer requirements. My current work deals with the missing pieces to assist nursing staff do even better.

Howard Berkowitz’s Specialties:

On the network side, fault-tolerant large critical networks (carrier & enterprise), router and switch design, Internet/ISP engineering, security and performance, multimedia/triple play, industry & international standards. In medicine, physiological telemetry and intelligent monitoring, expert systems for prescribing, and lab automation. In between, emergency response and WMD preparedness.
