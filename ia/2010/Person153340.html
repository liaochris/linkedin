Lawrence McDonald’s Summary

Lawrence has written a book on the Fall of Lehman Brothers, with Patrick Robinson.

A Colossal Failure of Common Sense, the Inside Story of the Collapse of Lehman Brothers, was published by Random House in July 2009 and became a New York Times Best Seller in its first 5 days on the book shelves, now published in 12 languages globally and remains one of the best selling business books in the world.

Mr. McDonald is on a global lecture tour, recently speaking at Universities, Banks, Investment Firms, Law Firms and Insurance companies. His speaking engagements have been all over the United States, Europe and Asia. He has been a Keynote Speaker at over 30 venues since his book was published.

From July 2004 through September 2008, Mr. McDonald was a former vice president of distressed debt and convertible securities trading at Lehman Brothers. He ran an extremely successful joint venture between the firm's fixed income and equity divisions. In early 2006, he was heralded by many for calling the subprime crisis and making over $46 million for the firm during it. Mr. McDonald was highly respected as one of the most consistently profitable traders at Lehman with over $83 million in trading profits during his time with the firm. More interestingly, he was an integral part of a faction within Lehman that passionately tried in vein to stop Lehman Brothers from heading down the road to destruction.

He was the most profitable trader on the Lehman Brothers convertible securities desk 2006 and 2007.

Mr. McDonald is also Co-Founder of Convertbond.com, the site was named by Forbes Magazine as “Best of the Web” from 2000-2003, noted there as the world wide web’s premier source for convertible securities information, valuation and news. In October 1999, before the dot-com crash, the site was successfully sold to Morgan Stanley.
