Jeff Youngen’s Summary

Accomplished Economic Consultant with over 11 years experience in risk analysis and financial valuation. Demonstrated track record in valuing and modeling a wide variety of financial instruments. Strengths include data collection, processing and analysis, regression and time series modeling, Monte Carlo simulation programming, and ability to present results to diverse clients, arbitration panels and juries.

Jeff Youngen’s Specialties:

Risk management assessment, econometric modeling, interest rate modeling, insurance modeling, variable annuity modeling, hedge fund valuation, litigation damage estimation, FinancialCAD, @Risk, E-Views

Jeff Youngen’s Experience

  • Senior Consultant, Consultant

    NERA Economic Consulting

    (Public Company; MMC; Management Consulting industry)

    2000 — 2009 (9 years )

    Managed confidential projects and performed econometric modeling as part of NERA’s risk management and securities litigation consulting practices. Developed risk analysis and valuation models. Performed econometric time series analysis on historical data and created Monte Carlo simulations to generate forecast trends. Purchased financial and economic data and maintained databases. Trained research staff in use of application software.

    Risk Management:
    • Performed risk analysis of proposed mutual fund strategies for a large mutual fund company.
    • Co-developed NERA’s patent-pending NERA c-far (cash-flow-at-risk) risk management tool.
    • Investigated portfolio risk for transactions in the commodities markets and modeled hedging of the transactions for an investment bank in a litigation case.
    • Examined the quality of the risk management system for a mortgage-backed securities hedge fund, valued the portfolio and estimated damages.

    Financial Modeling:
    • Developed model for home value insurance with sister company Marsh.
    • Simulated variable annuity death benefit risk exposure and potential hedging scenarios, and investigated the impact of modifying the fund portfolio to the reinsurance of the death benefit.
    • Developed model to value the management company of a hedge fund.
    • Validated interest rate model for a large government-sponsored mortgage agency.

    Valuation / Market Analysis:
    • Performed causality analysis between natural gas future and spot prices in a litigation case.
    • Valued financial derivatives (options, FX, swaps, CDOs) and estimated damages in litigation cases.
    • Researched and analyzed bid-ask spreads in natural gas futures market.
    • Performed study of variable annuity and mutual fund fees for a litigation case.

    Patent Pending:
    Jeremy Stein, Stephen E. Usher, Daniel A. LaGattuta and Jeffrey R. Youngen, “System and Method for Estimating Cash Flow at Risk for a Non-Financial Institution,” 2001, 2005.

  • Senior Analyst, Programmer/Analyst

    NERA Economic Consulting

    (Public Company; MMC; Management Consulting industry)

    1998 — 2000 (2 years )

    Supported consulting and litigation projects as part of NERA’s information systems group. Trained research staff in use of SAS.

    • Rewrote and enhanced software for performing multi-trader analysis in 10b-5 shareholder class action litigation, processed brokerage data, and performed multi-trader analyses for several cases.
    • Estimated damaged shares in litigation case involving stock certificates.
    • Estimated financial damages in mass tort litigation involving asbestos and other materials.
    • Measured competitive effects due to corporate mergers and in intellectual property litigation.

  • National Reseach Council Associate

    NASA Marshall Space Flight Center

    (Government Agency; Aviation & Aerospace industry)

    1992 — 1995 (3 years )

    Modeled gas proportional counters and other X-ray detectors. Developed and performed Monte Carlo simulations to understand the response of X-ray detectors to primary and background X-rays.

    • Wrote new software and enhanced existing software to simulate the transport of photons and electrons through common detector materials.
    • Increased run-time speed of simulations by factor of 5.
    • Ported model to several different computer architectures.
    • Wrote image-processing routines to analyze data from an X-ray imaging polarimeter.

  • Research Assistant

    University of Michigan, Department of Physics

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    1983 — 1992 (9 years )

    Assembled and tested detector hardware and electronics during the final construction stages of the Intercryostat Detector for the DØ Experiment at Fermi National Accelerator Laboratory.

    Designed and executed a Ph.D. thesis research program to detect and understand the development of large-scale structure in the universe by measuring the anisotropy in the faint light from subthreshold images of distant galaxies.

    • Developed hardware and software to extract a signal at the level of parts in 10,000 of the background.
    • Participated in the development of an interactive image processing package.
    • Taught elementary physics laboratory and discussion sections.
