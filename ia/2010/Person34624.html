Cees de Groot’s Summary

Apply technology and methodology to help people connect, spaceshift, timeshift, network. If the technology doesn't help, don't apply it. Sometimes, paper and pencil is the most appropriate technology.

The technology is always just the means, never the goal - I'm very disloyal to products and technologies if they don't prove their salt's worth. Among others, I dropped 4GL's, Bad OO (C++, Java), fat&static client/server, stupid distributed systems (Corba, J2EE); I did not drop (so far) Good OO (Smalltalk and to a lesser extent Python/Ruby/Perl), good distribution (Jini).

The methodology should very much not get in the way. Most of the time, that means agile development methods like XP are appropriate. However, at times I've been in projects where a waterfall approach was the method of choice. Pragmatism should rule, software development is no religion.

I dislike the current crop of operating systems. Mac OS X and Linux I dislike the least so far. Maybe someone will be able to push out a decent OS based on OO and capabilities, some day...

Does that sound negative? Well, after 15 years in the industry and being plagued by spam, viruses, worms... Something gotta change and I firmly plan to be part of the something.

My consulting usually centers around problems where various specialists have trouble talking. Being multi-disciplinary (starting from Business Administration to hard core tech) helps me to bring the specialists together and kick things into action.
