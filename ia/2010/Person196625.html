Stuart Nezin’s Summary

Advertising and marketing appealed to me from the start. I enjoy communicating with people and sharing ideas. After college a variety of agencies employed my abilities. Studio work of course, but my drawing skills soon led to page layout, product and packaging and display sketches,

Once hired by Thomson-Leeds, one of the top Point of Sale agencies in NY, things really got interesting.

An artist by inclination and training among Industrial Designers and Engineers, I became the go-to guy for graphics and illustration. This included the necessity and opportunity of learning about materials and manufacturing that was really new to me. I received a hands on education in plastics, metal, wood and more, and the ways of designing with them. This education extended to printing (lithography, silkscreen, hot stamping, pad printing, labeling), supervising photo shoots and doing my own, even occasionally writing copy, and meeting with suppliers.

Client meetings varied from providing a visual framework for existing ideas to working on concepts for new products and promotions.

The variety of clients and products over time has been wonderful. Among those I have worked with (in no special order) are health & beauty (from mass market to salon), automotive, electronic, food, liquor, OTC drug, toy, computer, fine china, cookware, books, appliances, hardware, etc.

This breadth of experience working in point of sale has served me well no matter what else I have done; from graphic concepts to product and package design, to store and fixture design to some web and Flash work. Not only have I learned to face a blank slate and initiate a project on my own when required, but to work as part of a team and to be there for that team. No one can know everything, that’s why there are experts. I value their advice and the ability to ask it. But good advice and ideas are not limited to experts and an open mind and ear has been a personal achievement and a valuable asset to me.
