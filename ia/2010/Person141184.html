Meredith Coen’s Summary

I graduated from the University of Miami Frost School of Music with a Bachelor of Music in Music Business and Entertainment Industries and a minor in Motion Pictures/Film Studies.

As a student I was part of the Presidents 100 organization. This group of 100 student ambassadors give University of Miami tours, participate in various on and off-campus recruitment programs for prospective admitted students (College Counselor Conference, Open Houses etc.), and assist President receptions and programs upon request.

I was an active member of the Delta Delta Delta fraternity for 3 years - holding positions as Licensing Chair, Academic Chair, and New Member Educator. I was actively involved in our philanthropy events, Golf Tournament and Letter Writing, which helped raise money for St. Jude Cancer Research Hospital.

In addition, I was a member of the Dean's List, Alpha Lambda Delta National Honor Society, the National Academy of Recording Arts and Sciences, and The International Special Events Society.

Upon graduation, I began working in the Undergraduate Admission Office in the Frost School of Music and became certified in Microsoft Web Design. I am member of the Junior League of Miami which has been such a learning experience. It has felt rewarding to get involved in the Miami community and help women and children; I have learned a great deal from the league fundraisers to our individual training sessions.

I hope to work in an job where I can interact with individuals and utilize my strong interpersonal, organization, and management skills whether that takes me into the Entertainment Industry or down a completely different path. I am looking to be inspired as I have been by individuals in the League and my work place. I would love the opportunity to at some point to publish the book I am writing on Music Admission and keep up with my international traveling.

  • Undergraduate Administrative Assistant; Frost School of Music

    University of Miami

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    September 2008 — August 2010 (2 years )

    As the Assistant to the Director of Admission and Recruitment, it is my job to help Frost School of Music prospective students through the detailed application process. I typically handle families for about a year to a year and a half from their initial inquiry phone call/visit to the Frost School of Music to their audition in January/February of their senior year of high school. In addition to the customer relationship, I process all of the applications that flow into our office –communicating with applicants constantly from the beginning; I input the applicants information into the University of Miami database, create their file, correspond about missing items, and deal with questions and concerns from the applicant and their parents. I organize the material that travels into our office; prescreening DVD’s, recorded auditions, and composition portfolios. I organize all of the prescreening’s from the moment they arrive in our office, to when they return with decisions – I prepare the decision that is sent to the applicant based upon the prescreening result. I organize the Regional Auditions and assist in preparing for On Campus Auditions; scheduling applicants in time slots for all regional audition locations as well as preparing all the materials the applicant receives when the arrive for on-campus auditions. I oversee the budget and the online system, UMeNET, which the University of Miami uses to place orders and pay invoices, for the Office of Undergraduate Admission in the Frost School of Music. I assist the Director of Admission and Recruitment in every capacity. This job requires a positive attitude, strong communication skills, organizational skills, great customer service skills, and it doesn’t hurt to be a graduate of the Frost School of Music with the vast knowledge of the application process, experience as a Frost School of Music student, and a passion for the University of Miami.

  • Intern - Public Relations and Brand Development

    Entertainment Rights

    (Public Company; Broadcast Media industry)

    June 2007 — July 2007 (2 months)

    This internship took place at Entertainment Rights Plc in London for a six week period. I worked three weeks in their Marketing Department and three weeks in their Public Relations Department. Entertainment Rights Plc now known as Classic Media is a London founded company that owns the rights to children/family television programs; Rupert Bear, Veggie Tales, Postman Pat and more. While in the Marketing Department I spent my time doing brand research; visiting our brands websites as well as our competing brands websites (i.e. Dora the Explorer) acquiring a list of strengths and weaknesses based upon how their merchandize, games, organization, etc compared to our sites. In addition, I assisted with the Rupert Bear/Woodland Trust project which was in place to help preserve the trees in select parks throughout London; Rupert Bear was included in this project to encourage classrooms to teach ‘Rupert Bear lesson plans’ so that children understanding how important it is to preserving tree while learning about how their grow etc. I was involved in organizing the photo shoot used to promote this project and making sure the events of the day ran smoothly. In the PR department, I created a PowerPoint that was presented to Mattel to promote Barbie at the Symphony in London. In addition, I was involved in contacting different companies about publications, scheduling meetings, updating sites, putting together merchandise for different functions – I was utilized anywhere, and flexibility and multitasking was a must in this position.
