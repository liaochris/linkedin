Perry Pound’s Summary

Perry Pound is currently obtaining his M.A.L.D. in International Business and Finance from the Fletcher School of Law and Diplomacy at Tufts University (Class of 2011). Mr. Pound previously led the Southern California property-acquisition efforts for four major development firms, serving as a Director for Alliance Residential, JPI, BRE Properties, and The Olson Company, where he was responsible for the acquisition and development of 2,500 urban infill residential units, for a total investment of more than $1 billion. Mr. Pound also served as a consultant for Recurrent Energy, a solar energy firm, where he acquired 400 acres for 50 megawatts of solar power development in Southern California. In addition, Mr. Pound worked as a Commercial Real Estate Summer Associate for Bank of Texas, where he pursued multifamily, office, retail, and industrial opportunities.

Prior to his real estate career, Mr. Pound served as a Chief of Staff to a Houston City Council Member, as a Project Development Director for a Business Improvement District, and as a Director for a Houston-based non-profit. He also worked on political campaigns and interned for several elected officials, including a Congressman and former U.S. President George H. W. Bush.

He received his B.A., cum laude, from Pepperdine University, where he served as Student Body President. His hobbies include snow skiing, swimming, and hiking. His favorite activity is spending time with his wife, a graduate student at the Harvard Graduate School of Education, and daughter.

Perry Pound’s Specialties:

Leadership, negotiation, production, sourcing transactions, financial underwriting and analysis, business development, networking, governmental and public relations, acquisitions and dispositions, mixed-use development, oral and written communication.
