  • Montana State University-Bozeman

    BA/BS (Eventually from Boston University in '89) , Avalanche Forecasting , 1986 — 1987

    At the time my intended career path was 'Avalanche Forecasting' and being involved with projects like the following:

    Contract with the Service des Pistes de Tignes, "Tignes Ski Patrol", to write, organise and present, "A COMPUTERIZED SYSTEM DESIGNED FOR LOCAL AVALANCHE FORECASTING IN TIGNES, SAVOIE, France." for The 1990 International Snow Science Workshop (ISSW) in Bigfork, Montana, USA. The ISSW theme is, 'A Merging of Theory and Practice'. My job was to write the paper and put together a slide show on the system that academics and practitioners could both appreciate and understand. The final paper and presentation was the result of collaboration between the avalanche forecaster in Tignes, the president of the company (DISAM) who created the forecasting system and me. Funding was obtained through the French Association for the Study of Snow and Avalanches (ANENA) and the town of Tignes, France. The paper was published in the proceedings of the conference.

    Activities and Societies:
    My time at MSU was spent with the Dept. of Geology on an undergraduate thesis entitled, "WEATHER EVENTS AND THE EVOLUTION OF THE SNOWPACK." The objective was to determine how weather events influence the evolution of the snowpack in terms of stability and instability so that accurate avalanche forecasting reports could be broadcast.
    My undergraduate degree finally was a Bachelor of Arts & Science from Boston University. Cum Laude; 1984 - 1988: in French language and literature and Geology.
