Patti Franco’s Summary

The staffing and recruiting industry is not for the faint of heart. Indeed, I recall reading an article years ago which stated that staffing has the second highest turnover as far as industries go, trailing only slightly behind the financial services sector.

I am consistently asked, "Why is there so much turn over in your industry?" To be sure, most companies I encounter turn over sales people and recruiters the way the auto industry has burned though bail out funds. There is no one answer to this question, but the most pressing is this: though we sell an intangible service we are still expected to produce a tangible entity; that entity is a person. And let's face it, there is a lot more to a person than meets the eye. I mean, bluntly speaking, people can kind of suck. They are unpredictable. They make excuses. They make choices, good or bad, that produce consequences. They accept a position, only to decline it a day before their expected start date. They beg you for a job, thank you up on side and down the other when you find them one, and then decide not to show up for their assignment. They show up in your office wearing slacks and a tie, and show up for work with worn jeans and flip flops. Staffing is the only industry where we have virtually no control over our product.

That being said, I must admit, I still love it. I'm not sure if that makes me naive or just a glutton for punishment (or both) but I can't imagine doing anything else!
