Richard Alvin’s Summary

Managing Director of Capital Business Media, a UK based publishing company which also has an office in the USA. Owner of titles like Business Matters, the largest subscription based title for the UK SME business owner, Angels, a new magazine aimed at entrepreneurs and investors and Fund Manager Today, a title specialising in the global fund management market.

The Capital Business Media group also has a dedicated online qualitive & quantitive business research division, Trends Research, which carries out business surveys for some of the UK's leading brands like O2, BT, Business Link and Bibby Financial Services as well as work for leading PR companies like Talk PR, Bray Leino and Nixon McInnes. The company also offers a service exclusive to new start ventures to product test their business model/idea amongst fellow business owners either as part of investment capital readiness or to concept test/tune prior to launch.

Given both of these sides to the business I am regularly called upon to speak and take part in round-table discussions and commentate in the media on UK SME business.

Richard Alvin’s Specialties:

Business magazines, Specialist published literature for corporate clients from Government to leading global financial institutions and SME business specialist. Also qualitative and quantitative online business research.

    Business Matters
    Business Matters is the UK's largest distributed monthly business magazine and is aimed at the owners of Small and medium sized enterprises (SME's).

    Business Matters is read for its news coverage, analysis, interviews with key opinion formers and leading entrepreneurs and opinion on the UK small business sector.

    Established more than 17 years ago, Business Matters reaches influential decision makers in the UK's thriving small and medium sized business sector. Addressing key issues, trends and developments, Business Matters is highly regarded as the leading title for those involved in running their own business.

    Angels
    Targets high-earning professionals in one of the most highly competitive, successful and vibrant business areas on earth. Targeting mainly men (70:30) between 35-64 who work in the professions and active business angels investors, Angels captures the imagination, acclaim and – most importantly – the time of this hard-to-reach demographic.

    In Angels, we profile leaders who are not just big, or trying to make it big in business, but in the angel investment community as well; which given the current economic climate is one of the most vibrant sectors for investment in the UK.
    Critically, we also encourage the people of the angel investment community and beyond to use Angels as their forum – business trends from around the globe, investment ideas as well as a selection of the latest businesses looking for angel funding sit alongside these lifestyle features.
