Christina Rocks’s Summary

Christina Marie Rocks is an Arizona native who is currently a senior at Arizona State University (ASU) where she majors in History. Christina became involved with Student Government as well as the Arizona Students’ Association (ASA) early on in her college career. As an ASA Apprentice she worked on both legislative and grassroots campaigns within Arizona and nationally. During her tenure with ASA she has engaged herself in voter work, three campaigns against statewide tuition hikes, and served as the Steering Committee Chair of the fourth annual Southwest Student Leadership Conference. She now sits on the state-wide Board of Directors for ASA and serves on the Executive Committee as Board Secretary as well as serving as Chair of the Internal Affairs Committee. In her past three years at ASU, Christina has fought for students on the campus level through her involvement with various on-campus organizations including her College Council where she served as Vice President of the Humanities, as well as the Undergraduate Student Senate at the Tempe Campus where she chaired the University Affairs Committee and also established the Student Engagement Committee to ensure that student government was accessible to the entire student body. In 2008, Christina was hired by the United States Student Association as a GROW Trainer which has given her the incredible opportunity to travel to different campuses across the country and teach students about grassroots organizing and advocacy. Christina believes that higher education access is one of the most pressing issues facing her generation as it is interrelated to many other aspects of a socially just society. She works hard toward the goal of ensuring that each person has the means to be successful in her or his higher educational pursuits.
