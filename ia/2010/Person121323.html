Larry Mugler’s Summary

With extensive management experience in Urban Planning and Development, I am positioned to provide direction and leadership for communities seeking sustainable development. My background includes practical, specialized knowledge and experience involving the following important categories:
Urban development - land use management and forecasting for local and metropolitan areas
Project management - manage teams on complex federally and state funded projects
Environmental quality - lead staff member for air quality and water quality plans and programs
Transportation - nationally recognized for integrating land use and transportation plans
Open space - prepared one of the few regional open space plans
Technical skills - adept at GIS, demography and statistical analysis

  • Planning Services Manager

    Denver Regional Council of Governments

    (Government Agency; 51-200 employees; Government Relations industry)

    January 2001 — January 2009 (8 years 1 month)

    DRCOG is the regional planning commission for the Denver Metropolitan Area. I managed the land use and water quality programs. My team successfully implemented a voluntary "urban growth boundary" process to guide the future development of the region. We also created water quality management plans for 11 watersheds in the region that defined the point and nonpoint management strategies for achieving water quality standards. I served on the Colorado Water Quality Forum that developed updated water quality regulations for the state. In order to better coordinate land use and transportation planning efforts, I also acted as the agency point person for the preparation of the Unified Planning Work Program.

  • Director

    Development Services

    (Civil Engineering industry)

    January 1984 — January 2001 (17 years 1 month)

    The Development Services Division provided regional development and environmental planning for the Denver region. I led the effort to create and implement the award-winning Metro Vision Plan and the Clean Water Plan. Under my leadership, the team provided consultation and support to local governments on land use, economic development, water supply, wastewater and air quality issues. I directed the Census statistical areas program for the 1980, 1990 and 200 censuses.

  • Director

    Environmental Services

    (Privately Held; Environmental Services industry)

    January 1980 — January 1984 (4 years 1 month)

    When federal support was adequate to support a full environmental division, I managed a team that developed water quality, air quality and solid waste management plans. Our air quality planning eventually led to the Denver region being the first non-attainment area in the U.S. to reach all federal air quality standards. Our water quality planning included clean lakes studies that led to watershed control regulations to protect 3 important regional reservoirs.

  • Regional Planner

    Denver Regional Council of Governments

    (Government Agency; 51-200 employees; Government Relations industry)

    January 1974 — January 1980 (6 years 1 month)

    My responsibilities included the preparation of the Census Bureau's DIME file for the Denver region, an early GIS system. I also prepared the region's population and employment estimates and forecasts. My team also created the Regional Housing Opportunity Plan, which led to the formation of several suburban housing authorities.
