Jason McBriarty’s Summary

Jason McBriarty currently holds dual positions at Levi Strauss & Co. as the Senior Finance Manager for Worldwide Community & Corporate Citizenship and Controller of the $75 million Levi Strauss Foundation.

Prior to joining Levi Strauss, he was the Director of Technical Assistance for the Northern California Community Loan Fund, a $18 million nonprofit lending institution. In this position he managed all aspects of the organization’s nonprofit financial management consulting program. He has also held various financial and operational positions with Habitat for Humanity and Lead Safe California. Jason holds a MBA degree in finance from Boston University’s Graduate School of Management and a professional certificate in nonprofit management from the same institution. While in the graduate program he was co-chair of the Boston MBA Net Impact chapter, was a finalist in the annual CSR case competition and was an active member in the Public Management club.

Jason’s community involvement work includes serving as an advisory board member for a West Oakland youth organization; volunteering weekly as a mentor for at-risk-youth in San Francisco public schools; serving as treasurer for his daughter’s public elementary school in Oakland; and coaching his son’s Little League baseball team and daughter’s soccer team in Oakland.

Jason is also helping his wife Hiroko to expand her socially responsible blanket making company, hiroko kurihara designs (www.hirokokurihara.com). The company, which donates a blanket to a homeless shelter with each purchase, has been featured in Lucky magazine, Dwell magazine, Sunset magazine (twice) and Paper magazine. Along with retail location in the Bay Area, Seattle, New York and Chicago, the company’s work is featured in the San Francisco Museum of Modern Art and the de Young Museum in San Francisco.

Jason lives in Oakland, CA with his wife and business partner Hiroko Kurihara, their two children, Theo and Denali.
