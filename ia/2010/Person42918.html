Andrew Greenberg’s Summary

After building relationships with corporations around the Greater New York area, Andrew Greenberg realized that this could be of great help to non-profits. Accordingly, he has created Connecting Non-Profit, Inc., based in Scarsdale, New York. Connecting Non-Profit will match well-vetted non-profits with corporate and individual donors.

Greenberg graduated from Scarsdale High School, where he received the Wharton School of Business Award for Excellence in Accounting. He then attended Mercy College in Dobbs Ferry, NY while working. After receiving his B.A. in 1991, he founded the boutique temporary staffing company NYCTEMPS, Inc. As CEO, he built it to a multi-million dollars company over the text ten years.

In 1998, Greenberg helped to found W!SE, Working in Support of Education (www.Wise-NY.org), serving on their board in strategic planning and fundraising. He also joined the Advisory Council of the High School of Economics and Finance, where he worked on fundraising and strategic planning, as well as serving as a volunteer teacher. (http://schools.nyc.gov/SchoolPortals/02/M489/default.htm)
In 2002, Greenberg became a private investor in the retail sector; then, in 2008, he founded Classy Consignments, Inc., a boutique store selling antiques and objects d’art in Scarsdale, New York. He welcomes inquiries about Connecting Non-Profit, Inc.

Andrew Greenberg’s Specialties:

Fundraising, Corporate Partnerships, Cause Marketing, Civic Marketing and working with Nonprofits.

    1991, he founded the boutique administrative temporary staffing company NYCTEMPS, Inc. As CEO, he built it to a multi-million dollar company over the text ten years. The company often coordinated several major events each day, using as many as 250 associates.
