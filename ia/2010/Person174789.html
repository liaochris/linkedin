Robert Briggs’s Summary

A partner at Booth, Mitchel & Strange handling civil litigation with jury trial experience.

More than thirty years of experience in civil litigation and civil trials.

Favorable results in a three-month jury trial defending a building contractor in a San Bernardino County court. Plaintiffs recovered a verdict, but it was less than 1/10th of what they requested.

Another favorable jury trial result in a four-month civil trial defending the surety for an electrical contractor in the Los Angeles Central District court.

Many other trials, arbitrations and mediations of complex civil litigation.

Also a board member of various 503c non-profit organizations.

I have an abiding interest in the history of the American West and some of my articles and book reviews have appeared in various historical and scholarly journals. In connection with that I presented the 2002 Juanita Brooks Lecture and the 2004 Critchlow Lecture. I received the 2006 Dale L. Morgan Award for best scholarly article.

Robert Briggs’s Specialties:

General insurance defense; personal injury and products liability; general construction, construction defect and surety; toxic tort (asbestos, formaldehyde, etc.); landslides and other geology-related litigation; and insurance-related litigation.

Robert Briggs’s Experience

  • Attorney - Partner

    Booth, Mitchel & Strange LLP

    (Law Practice industry)

    1981 — Present (29 years )

    • 1981-1985 – Associate Attorney
    • 1985-1986 – Senior Associate Attorney
    • 1987-present – Equity Partner

    Beginning as a four-year associate, I became involved in the defense of products liability and construction accident cases involving serious or catastrophic injuries. Another arena was toxic torts involving chemical exposures (formaldehyde and other chemicals). Another staple of the practice was the defense of developers whose buildings or homes were damaged by landslides or soil subsidence. This practice area morphed into a major construction defect practice, representing nearly every conceivable trade in the home building industry. As that practice area shifted, I continued in construction and products liability law. In recent years I've been involved in construction surety litigation, products liability defense (asbestos litigation), general civil litigation, and a smattering of plaintiffs' cases for property damage or personal injury.
