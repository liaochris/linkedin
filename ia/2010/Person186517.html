Sergio Vazquez’s Summary

I am seeking an associate position at a law firm, in the tax and estate planning practices.

I graduated from the UCLA School of Law in May 2007. While there, I was enrolled in the Taxation Track of the Business Law and Policy Program, excelling in several courses related to tax and estate planning, including an Estate and Gift Tax course taught by Andy Katzenstein of Proskauer Rose LLP, in which I got an A.

As a research assistant for Professor Kirk Stark, I spent three years researching issues involving Federal income taxation, and drafting sections for several of his articles and books. As the Chief Managing Editor of the UCLA Law Review, I supervised fifteen managing editors and forty-five staff members, developing proficient organizational and managerial skills, as well as a high attention to detail.

I was admitted to practice law in California in December 2007. I am currently a member of the American Bar Association, the Los Angeles County Bar Association, and the Beverly Hills Bar Association. I am very active in these Associations' real property, taxation, and estate planning sections.

I have been working in the tax services department at Ernst & Young LLP since January 2008. As a tax professional, I have gained two years of experience preparing and supervising the preparation of gift tax returns and income tax returns for individuals and all types of entities. I have also done work involving real property taxes, and sales and use taxes.

Most recently, I enrolled in the UCLA Extension Program to pursue a Certificate in Taxation. In addition, I am also pursuing a legal specialization certification from the State Bar of California in the areas of Taxation Law, and Estate Planning, Trust and Probate Law.

I trust that my background and experience will make a positive contribution to any law firm.
