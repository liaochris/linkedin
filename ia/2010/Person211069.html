Veronica Arreola’s Summary

I am a professional feminist, a writer and a mom.

I am the assistant director for the Center for Research on Women and Gender at the University of Illinois at Chicago and direct our Women In Science & Engineering Program. WISE works to recruit, retain and advance women, majority and minority, in science, technology, engineering and math fields. Issues I deal with on a daily basis include: Title IX, Affirmative Action, Educational Equity, and "Non-Traditional" careers for women and girls.

I volunteer and fundraise for feminist organizations both in Chicago and nationwide.

I am a freelance writer. My work has appeared in Bitch magazine, Ms., RH Reality Check, NPR.org, The Guardian's Comment is Free and Alternet. I contribute to a number of blogs including Kenneth Cole's AWEARNESS, Girl w/Pen, WIMNs Voices, Care2.com, Chicagonista and Ms.

I also consult nonprofits and authors on social media projects. Clients have included Planned Parenthood and Women Employed. Workshops occur on request.

I am available for speaking engagements (sliding scale, we can negotiate) on feminist parenting, women's health advocacy/activism, feminism in general, reproductive health/justice/rights, Title IX (sports & education), mentoring and many others.

My goal is to do what I can with my skills to make this world a more equitable place for everyone. I also want to have fun while doing it and include my daughter as much as possible.

  • Director, Women in Science & Engineering Program

    University of Illinois at Chicago

    (Higher Education industry)

    November 2004 — Present (5 years 8 months)

    The goal of the UIC Women in Science and Engineering Program, WISE, is to increase the number of women students pursuing and graduating in science, technology, engineering and math, STEM, disciplines, and to promote the recruitment, retention and advancement of women who have chosen academic careers.

    WISE supports women undergrad/graduate students and faculty in STEM by sponsoring activities that foster a positive educational and professional environment, and enable excellence in scholarship, teaching and service.

    As director, I focus my work on our on-campus programs that impact undergraduate and graduate students. This includes:

    * Peer mentoring program
    * Monthly seminar series
    * Living/Learning community
    * Adviser for student organization
    * Oversee scholarships and travel grants
    * As well as administrative duties such as budgets and hiring

  • Board Co-Chair

    Chicago Abortion Fund

    (Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    January 2006 — October 2009 (3 years 10 months)

    I have been a board member since January 2006. I began as co-chair in July 2007.

    The Chicago Abortion Fund (CAF) is dedicated to guaranteeing low-income women access to safe, legal abortions and related services. CAF provides these women reduced-fee referrals, direct financial assistance and general information about abortion services. CAF also works to increase awareness of the urgent need for government funding of abortions as part of the full spectrum of reproductive health services. CAF officially opened its doors in October 1986. Since that time, it has been committed to carrying out and expounding upon the founders vision.

    The co-Chairs preside at all meetings of the board and the executive committee, and shall oversee the board of directors.
