Alexander R. O'Neil’s Summary

Alexander O’Neil was raised in Springfield, Massachusetts. He spent the first sixteen years of his life in the Massachusetts Foster Care System.
In 1963, Alexander enlisted in the Marines, doing four years of service, including a stint in Vietnam.

In 2005, Alexander self published a Memoir of his childhood in the Massachusetts foster care system.” Is There Any Love Down Back?” The book tells a compelling story of the atrocities Alexander and his three brothers experienced in the system.
Alexander has spoken at various universities and private organizations, regarding the foster care system and the abuses that he suffered.
In 2006 he spoke along with Dr Bill Cosby at Elms College in Springfield, Massachusetts, regarding childhood abuse.

In 2006, Alexander wrote a screenplay, “Momma Where are You?” based on his memoir.
Alexander has written several other screenplays, some of which are optioned with Dujour films and Entertainment Inc. in Tarzana, Cal. “Mr. Lee,” Marked Forever,” Crime Fighters Day and Night, “ The End of the Road,” Barracks 719” and several Docu-dramas.
Recently, Alexander O’Neil was contracted by Parker and Sons publisher Inc., Chicago, IL to write a screenplay, “Momma’s Tears” based on the book, “The Struggle”, a novel self published and written by Penelope Write. This story is about the tragic events that happened in 1984 Chicago, IL. to a young Afro-American female named Cassey Jenkins. This project was also optioned with Dujour Films.

Alexander O’Neil currently resides in Springfield, Massachusetts. He has three children
