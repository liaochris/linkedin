Hamid Ghanadan’s Summary

Hamid's insight and expertise in marketing science stems from his experience as a faculty researcher at the Department of Biochemistry and Biophysics at Oregon State University. Immersed in the culture of the research laboratory and directing primary research on complex carbohydrate functionality, Hamid noted a vast marketing opportunity created by a lack of innovation in scientific communications and slow Internet adoption. With this vision, Hamid launched his company in 1996. For the past 14 years, Hamid has been dedicated to the single goal of understanding the motivational behaviors of scientists as consumers, in order to develop more compelling marketing programs for his clients. The Linus Group, led by Hamid, has successfully launched a myriad of successful marketing campaigns and brands, and has worked diligently with clients to:

—Bring strategic thinking to the front-end of every marketing and communications campaign.
—Design integrated, multi-channel, and multi-faceted campaigns.
—Develop methodologies for measuring the effectiveness of marketing campaigns.

With Hamid's experience and strategic insight into the life sciences, The Linus Group is at the forefront of marketing communications agencies in the life science industry.

Hamid has published a multitude of articles regarding marketing and communications issues within the sciences and frequently delivers keynote speeches at industry conferences and networking events. He is also the editor of Linus Report, the only publication to offer critical briefings on a variety of topics faced by marketers in the life science industry. Linus Report has a loyal subscriber base that considers the publication an essential resource for their jobs.

When he is not at the office, Hamid spends his time immersed in jazz and world music. He also holds a black belt in Taekwondo, and teaches at the University of California, Berkeley. Hamid and his wife live in Berkeley, CA.
