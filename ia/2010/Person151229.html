Nancy Gong’s Summary

My general goals as a future professional are to increase the experience I get as an attorney in various different fields of law. With my Chinese cultural heritage and fluency in Mandarin Chinese, I believe that International Law is a focus that I want to pursue. My undergradate education, with a concentration in Asian Studies, has given me a sociological perspective of the inner workings of Asian Societies which I believe will be extremely useful when necessary to interact with Asian clientele. As a student, though, I believe that opportunities are the biggest advantage that we have and I refuse to be sequestered into one area of law when I really can experience the whole gambit. Therefore, all areas of law really interest me. Another interest I have is the budding industry of fashion law and a career in that field. I believe that with technology, the world has grown increasingly smaller and cultural values are starting to blur. This is something that interests me because fashion law is an industry that has really grown recently and it is becoming more of a focus in topical discussions and social discourse: really, how to deal with such blatant copyright infringements without intruding on people's rights of expression and free speech. It really comes down to a fundamental social value of privacy and rights of expression. The law is a reflection of society and society shapes the law as much as most people believe that law shapes society. Fashion law is an interesting examination into that aspect of global culture(s).

My previous experiences as a retail sales associate, marketing intern, and a swim instructor have all provided me with unique skills that have made me highly adaptable in the workplace to immense amounts of pressure and increasingly higher expectations of performance.
