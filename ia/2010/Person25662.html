Bob Schiff’s Summary

Bob has over twenty years of management experience working with many successful startups. He has extensive leadership experience accelerating growth and profitability in emerging companies. He is known for his high energy and the ability to bring a talented management team to the companies that he is involved with.

Bob was featured in The Wall Street Journal for his innovative approach to hiring and retaining today’s top talented employees. He was also featured in Innovator Magazine, Bank and Technology Magazine, Workforce Magazine, CNN and CNN.com.

Bob Schiff’s Experience

  • CEO

    iBidMotors

    (Computer Software industry)

    2003 — Present (7 years )

    As Founder and CEO, of iBidMotors, Bob has set the strategic vision and direction for the largest dealer to consumer online automotive auction site. Under his leadership, iBidMotors has risen to a prominent position in the automotive retailing industry, differentiated by its commitment to cutting edge innovation. Today, iBidMotors clients include three of the top five automotive dealers in the country (AutoNation, Penske and Asbury Group) in addition to hundreds of car dealerships representing more than 30,000 cars on the site today.

    Over $100M of pre-owned cars have been sold on the iBidMotors platform.

    iBidMotors is headquarted in Basking Ridge New Jersey. Many former NFL players and NFL Hall of Famers including: NY Giants Lawrence Taylor, Harry Carson, Brian Kelley, Howard Cross and former Philadelphia Eagles running back Keith Byars are part of the iBidMotors team.

  • CEO

    ICT Biometrics

    (Computer Software industry)

    1998 — 2000 (2 years )

    ICT Biometrics, a software security infrastructure company utilizing biometric technology. Bob hired and worked very closely with Princeton University Professor Edward Felton (world renowned security expert and one of the key government witnesses in the Microsoft anti-trust trial) to architect and develop this technology. The technology is currently being used in some of the largest brokerage companies in the country.

    The company was sold to a Japanese technology company.

  • Executive Vice President

    iClick-ProAct Technologies

    (Computer Software industry)

    1996 — 1998 (2 years )

    Executive Vice President of iClick, Inc an online employee portal for fortune 1000 companies. Bob worked closely with venture capital firms to raise successful second and third round financings. iClick was named by Computer World as one of the “Top 100 Emerging Companies to Watch in 2000”.

    The Company was acquired by Consumer Financial Network (CFN) in June 2000 for $100M and renamed ProAct Technologies.

  • Co-Founder & President

    inTime Systems

    (Computer Software industry)

    1994 — 1996 (2 years )

    Bob was co-founder and President of InTime Systems, where he negotiated a multi–million dollar contract with Oracle Corporation to include InTime’s software with Oracle’s ERP System (Oracle Time & Labor). In 1996 Bob was instrumental in achieving a successful IPO.

    inTime Systems was sold to Oracle Corporation.

  • Director of National Accounts Division

    ADP

    (Public Company; ADP; Financial Services industry)

    1980 — 1994 (14 years )

    Director of National Accounts Division of Automatic Data Processing (ADP), a $10 billion international computing and services company. As a District Manager, he distinguished himself as a “record breaking” achiever, which led to his appointment to Director of the National Accounts Division.
