EJ Steele’s Summary

EJ Steele is the Chairman/President of the Los Angeles Veterans’ Employment Committee.

In this role, he is responsible for helping veterans and their families transition from military service to civilian life through housing, psychosocial treatment referrals, career counseling, access to vocational education/training, and direct employment placement. As the son of a Vietnam veteran (USAF), EJ has a perspective that lends itself not just to the needs of the veteran, but to the family as a whole as well.

Education, advocacy, economic self-sufficiency and empowerment are at the core of his commitment to those in need. He has been at the forefront of providing guidance for people in transition for years, previously serving as an Administrative Manager at a Temporary Assistance to Needy Families (TANF) Workforce Investment Act (WIA) program.

EJ is active in the community; he is a member of the both the Advisory Committee of the Children’s Collective, Inc., and the California Employment Advisory Committee.

EJ is a JPMorgan Chase Fellow, and holds certifications in Executive Management/Leadership and Nonprofit Program development from Cornell and Fordham University, respectively. He also holds certifications in Employment Law and Compliance and HIV/AIDS Education.

With over twenty years experience in both for and nonprofit organizations, with a focus in financial, operational and managerial disciplines, EJ brings a wealth of personal and professional experience to the table.

To date, EJ has helped many people gain access to the resources and benefits they deserve and re-engage to both family and community.
