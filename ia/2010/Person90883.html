Jason Rekulak’s Experience

  • Associate Publisher & Creative Director

    Quirk Books

    (Privately Held; Publishing industry)

    September 2000 — Present (10 years )

    Of all the places I've worked, Quirk has the best and brightest staff, hands-down. I acquire and edit books on a wide range of subjects: U.S. history, indie rock poster art, Sherlock Holmes, patron saints, the life of Christopher Walken, obscene novelty gag gifts, and much more. Every season brings new formats and new challenges; last year it was a graphic novel, this year it's the bestselling literary mash-up "Pride and Prejudice and Zombies," which now has more than 1,000,000 copies in print. It's a crazy job in a dysfunctional industry that's full of eccentric people...and I can't imagine myself doing anything else.

  • Senior Editor

    Running Press

    (Publishing industry)

    January 1999 — September 2000 (1 year 9 months)

    The best thing I did at RP was write this chunky little book called "The Writer's Block." For reasons I will never understand, the book landed on The Boston Globe bestseller list and spent many weeks on The Harvard Bookstore bestseller list....despite the fact that I have zero friends living in Boston. How did it happen? Beats me. Anyhow, "The Writer's Block" has been in print since 2001 and continues to sell about 10,000 copies a year. Treat yourself to a copy on Amazon so I can keep those royalty statements comin'.

  • Senior Copywriter

    Quality Paperback Book Club

    (Publishing industry)

    March 1997 — January 1999 (1 year 11 months)

    Man, I loved this job. Every three weeks, I'd get some 10 or 12 new books to read -- all different kinds, history, science, literary fiction, thrillers, really good books. And all I had to do was read them and write compelling descriptions for a mail-order catalog. The job paid great and the benefits were superb. Of course, this was all before the Internet came along and crippled the direct-mail industry, but boy, back in 1997, this was the best gig in the world.

  • Assistant Editor

    St. Martin's Press

    (Privately Held; Publishing industry)

    1995 — 1997 (2 years )

    How did I live in New York City on $18,000 a year? I don't remember, but I'm glad this part of my life is over.
