Philippe Masson’s Summary

Philippe Masson has been working on applications of superconductivity since 1999. He received a M.S. in Electrical Engineering and a Ph.D in Electrical Engineering from the Universite Henri Poincare, Nancy, France, respectively in 1998 and 2002. He spent 6 years at the FAMU-FSU College of Engineering and the FSU-Center for Advanced Power Systems as a research faculty and adjunct professor. He is now a Senior Research Scientist at the Advanced Magnet Lab, Palm Bay, FL where he works on superconducting power devices, superconducting and resistive magnet for diverse applications as well as MultiPhysics simulations.
Dr. Masson worked on the NASA/DoD URETI on Aeropropulsion and Power Technology (UAPT) for 5 years on the development of novel configurations of superconducting propulsion motors, generators and actuators for the all/more-electric aircraft. Dr. Masson supervised 4 Ph.D students, 10 graduate students, published over 35 peer-reviewed journal papers and 50 conference presentations. Dr. Masson is co-author of 4 patents related to superconducting motors, magnets and machine excitation.
Dr. Masson is part of the Applied Superconductivity Conference (ASC) program committee since 2002 for which he has been serving as technical editor in 2004, 2006, 2008 and 2010 and as session chair for numerous sessions since 2002, the ASC is the largest conference in the field. Dr. Masson was invited to give lectures on superconductivity and superconducting machines at the ASC 2004, 2006, 2008 and 2010 as well as NASA Glenn Research Center in 2008.
Dr. Masson received the “outstanding young researcher” award from NASA in 2005 and the Cryogenic Society of America Roger W. Boom Award in 2010. He is a member of the Cryogenic Society of America and is a senior member of the IEEE and AIAA. He was appointed on the editorial board of the Transactions on Applied Superconductivity, a leading journal in the field, and has been serving as Editor for Large Scale Applications since 2005.
