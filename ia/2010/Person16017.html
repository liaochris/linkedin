Aren Grimshaw’s Summary

I have a broad experience in sales, marketing, media and public relations roles, spanning more than ten years in a variety of business environments. I now work with organisations of all sizes to leverage the greatest value from their Social Media approach.

Specialising in Social Media, I work with a range of organisations (public and private sector) on a consultancy basis. I am a regular speaker on the subject, presenting at sessions organised on behalf of Business Link throughout the south west region.

As a consultant I am responsible for helping companies benefit from new and emerging technologies, assisting them in developing routes to build stronger relationships with current and prospective customer groups, the wider community and other stakeholders.

How I can help businesses or organisations can be summarised as follows:

~ Explaining how to use Social Media - Training & Mentoring
~ Uncovering the strongest Social Media approaches - Strategy & Consultancy
~ Explaining why an approach is or isn't working - Appraisals & Monitoring
~ Helping organisations get started - Account Set-up, Integration & Management

I am also the co-founder of Cornwall Social Media Cafe, the first active Social Media network in the South West UK, lead organiser of the two 2009 Cornwall Twestivals and UK Regional Coordinator (South West & Wales) of Twestival Global 2010.

  • Marketing Director

    Tonick Media

    (Privately Held; Marketing and Advertising industry)

    May 2009 — August 2010 (1 year 4 months)

    I joined Tonick Business Publishing, now known as Tonick Media, in the spring of 2009.

    On joining the company I carried out detailed analysis of the current market, identifying a need to diversify on the current activities which centred on print advertising sales. With print advertising sales in decline across the industry, a range of titles closing down through loss of revenue and the effects of a recession being felt strongly by our key target markets, it was necessary to move promptly to secure the company's position. Leading on from the research I was responsible for developing a new strategy which dealt with securing company growth and marketing the business into new markets.

    As part of this diversification it was necessary to create a new brand for the company to incorporate the wider services on offer. The new brand offering was created around the key media services, moving subtly away from the idea of 'publishing' being the core business. New services in media consultancy, focused around digital mediums such as social media consultancy, helped create new revenue streams for the business.

    Current activities surrounding the Business Cornwall title and associated website were improved through developing a cohesive community engagement strategy. This strategy relied on building close ties with the local business community, the magazines target audience, through the use of a range of social media tactics not limited to the use of; passive marketing materials; offline and online social networking; harnessing existing online communities and the use of a variety of platforms to improve the customer offering.

    As it stands advertising sales have stabilised through this period enabling the magazine and wider company to maintain a strong market position. New service offerings have been popular with between 20 and 25% of gross revenues now attributed to these income streams. The brand is now in a strong position to continue its growth.
