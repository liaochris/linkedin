Nicholas Candela’s Summary

For five years, I have been settling into my career as a high school art teacher. Every day my job greets me with the exciting possibilities of what my students will do with each lesson, how their minds will interpret the objectives, and what the final product will be for them. In addition to teaching, I am in the midst of my pursuit to earn a Masters Degree in Interdisciplinary Studies with a focus in Studio Art at VCU because I feel it would help me hone my artistic skills. It would offer me the opportunity to focus my creative efforts, increase my repertoire of media, and it will serve as a very important stepping stone as I approach my other lifelong goals of teaching at the college level and becoming a professional artist.
For me, living through the arts is and always has been a way to experience the world more subjectively, which in turn, leaves me vulnerable to new ideas and emotions. These new ideas and emotions are then translated into my artwork, and the entire process becomes cyclic and self-sustaining. In June of 2002 I finished my undergraduate work with a 3.8 GPA in my studio classes, and in the six studio classes I have taken as a non-degree student through VCU, I have maintained a 4.0 GPA.
I believe that my greatest asset in is my desire to excel as a professional artist, as an art instructor, and as a responsible memeber of society. Martin Luther Kind Jr. once said, “Our salvation lies in the hands of the creatively maladjusted.” I believe that for someone who understands the contexts of a studio art curriculum as I do and can execute the processes as thoroughly as I can, there exists an obligation to give back to the world both visually and academically in an effort to offset the malaise brought on by these “creatively maladjusted” people. I embrace the opportunity to fullfill my obligation as an artist and to reveal truth, lies, beauty, ugliness, the vague, and the familiar.
