Ankur Jain’s Summary

Ankur Jain is currently a junior at the Wharton School of the University of Pennsylvania.

In 2007, Jain founded the Kairos Society, a student-run, not-for-profit foundation created to tap the power of undergraduate entrepreneurs to develop business innovations with global impact. Kairos has built a member base consisting of some of the brightest students from top universities around the world. Taking guidance from prominent business leaders and academics, these students are working together to use entrepreneurship and innovation to tackle some of today’s most daunting issues, including alternative energy, education, and health. Kairos members hold a common belief that they will ultimately Do Well by Doing Good.

Each year, Kairos holds a Global Summit where it brings together 500 of its top members alongside a collection of the world’s most significant leaders for a major 2-day event in New York City to discuss the issues emerging opportunities where Kairos students can make a difference using their business skills. The result has been a significant number of successful technology-oriented projects, ranging from renewable energy to new low-cost nutritional products to on-line career planning services that are already reaching markets and generating new jobs around the world.

During his three years at the University of Pennsylvania, Jain has assembled a varied array of experience and accomplishments. Recently he was selected as one of eighty students (out of 1,800) to participate in the prestigious Singularity University Summer Program, an interdisciplinary program based at the NASA Ames campus in Silicon Valley whose mission is to assemble, educate and inspire a cadre of leaders who strive to understand and facilitate the development of exponentially advancing technologies that address humanity’s grand challenges.
