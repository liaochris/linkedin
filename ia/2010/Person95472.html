Jennison Asuncion’s Summary

A pragmatic IT Accessibility professional combining: eight plus years working in the financial services industry; over ten years researching the accessibility of information and communication technology (ICT) in Canadian higher education; with first-hand knowledge born from being an end-user who happens to be blind.

The corporate accessibility journey began by me bringing accessibility subject matter expertise to several strategic e-learning initiatives at the BMO Financial group Institute for Learning, the Bank’s corporate university. Today at the Royal Bank of Canada (RBC), I am a member of a team that, in part, consults on making systems and applications accessible to persons with disabilities.

In my night job, I co-direct the Adaptech Research Network, where we conduct applied, empirical research into the use and accessibility of ICTs by postsecondary students with visible and non-visible disabilities in Canada.

In December '08, I launched the Web 2.0 Accessibility Forum LinkedIn Group, to engage the community of Internet users with disabilities, with those involved and/or interested in making Web 2.0 and the next-generation of ICTs accessible to everyone. I have a concern that a new digital divide may be building for persons with disabilities if we continue to operate in silos. Join over seven-hundred others in these important conversations at http://www.linkedin.com/e/gis/1605077

November '09 saw the birth of the Mobile and Handheld Technology Accessibility Forum LinkedIn group, focusing on the evolving area of mobile and handheld technology, the applications that run on them, and accessibility by and for users with disabilities. Join at http://www.linkedin.com/groups?gid=2484780

In December '09, I set-up the Accessibility for Ontarians with Disabilities Act (AODA) Forum http://www.linkedin.com/groups?gid=2604468 - with a goal of encouraging much-needed conversations on the implementation of the AODA standards.

Jennison Asuncion’s Specialties:

IT accessibility consulting, JAWS usability/accessibility testing, e-learning accessibility, ICT accessibility research, adaptive technology training, and project management.

  • Co-director

    Adaptech Research Network

    (Research industry)

    1999 — Present (11 years )

    A member of the Management Team, I lead project management, HR matters, and knowledge mobilization. This, in addition to participating in research design, data analysis, grant writing and the publication and presentation of findings. I first joined as a Research Assistant in 1997.

    Since 1996, The Adaptech Research Network has been conducting grant-funded, applied research on the use and accessibility of information and communication technologies used in Canadian postsecondary education. Our research scope has expanded to studying barriers and facilitators impacting the academic success of college and university students with disabilities. Finally, we maintain a library of free or inexpensive software that students can use as alternatives to the more expensive adaptive software available. Our work has earned us a listing in the 2009 Educational Media and Technology Yearbook among "Media Related Organizations and Associations in North America."

It holds an annual, week-long event in the USA, where skiers with disabilities are paired with volunteer guides for a week of recreational skiing.
