Jennifer Garcia’s Summary

JENNIFER GARCIA is a Harlem-based multi-disciplinary art director and designer whose work has been featured in design, lifestyle and art publications such as “Death” by artist collective Faile, Soda Magazine, Animal Magazine, Fasthetic, Mass Appeal, 200% Cotton, 300% Cotton, DVD Art and Street T. She has art directed national ad campaigns for corporations and charitable organizations, designed streetwear graphics, art catalogs, music and dvd packaging and has created a line of dolls made from fabric scraps. In 2004, she independently launched the annual limited edition T-shirt and zine project, Star Electric Eighty Eight aka SEEE. Conceptual in nature, SEEE T-shirts have been sold in designer boutiques worldwide including BeamsT in Japan and at the New Museum of Contemporary Art shop in NYC. Exhibitions featuring Garcia’s work include “8.5x11” and “2StepsBack” (55DSL) and “To a Tee” (Museum of Design, Atlanta). She was recently commissioned by Change Design of Venice, Italy to create a mural alongside artists Dave Kinsey, Abel and Marc Cabrera. She is a new mother, currently working from home on her keepsake box project, WE-ARE-FAMILIA, which had its debut at Colette, Paris in December 2007.

Jennifer Garcia’s Experience

  • Co-Founder

    WE-ARE-FAMILIA

    (Arts and Crafts industry)

    December 2006 — Present (3 years 10 months)

    WE-ARE-FAMILIA is an independent creative study on the concept of family, founded by New York creative directors/designers Jennifer Garcia and Christian Calabro. 50 artists, designers and musicians have come together to explore the powerful, complex ties which consciously and unconsciously touch all that we experience as humans. Presented in 25 one-of-a kind keepsake boxes made from recycled furniture and found materials, WE-ARE-FAMILIA showcases original “mementos” contributed by its family of artists.

  • Owner

    SEEE

    (Design industry)

    2004 — Present (6 years )

    Star Electric Eighty Eight (SEEE) was named after founder Jennifer Garcia’s favorite vintage t-shirt; an electrician's baseball league jersey sporting team "Star Electric" across the front and the number "88" on its back.

    SEEE is a home-based design studio from New York City, having launched it’s first collection in 2004. It is an annual limited edition t-shirt, zine and print making project which bases each year's collection on different aspects of pop culture. Previous designs have addressed consumerism, religion, revolution, prescription drug abuse and the concept of self help.

    SEEE t-shirts have been featured in the New York Times, Complex Magazine and on Josh Rubin’s Coolhunting website. SEEE's work has also been included in the “To A Tee” exhibit at Atlanta’s Museum of Design (MODA) and in the debut installation of the We-Are-Familia collective at Colette, Paris. Past collaborations include designs for Brooklyn-based fashion labels H. Fredriksson and Yoko Devereaux, as well as the experimental Swiss publication Soda Magazine.
