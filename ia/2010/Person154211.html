Nick Quintong’s Experience

  • CLP

    GE Healthcare

    (Public Company; GE; Hospital & Health Care industry)

    June 2009 — Present (8 months)

    CLP offers a core curriculum that fosters the development of commercial skills and techniques that are critical to success in all GE businesses. CLP prepares candidates for a successful career in sales or marketing by providing the opportunity to learn about our products, industry, and customers while making valuable contributions to the organization.


    The GE Healthcare Commercial Leadership Program is divided into three phases over 18 months. Phase 1 is a three-month field rotation shadowing many members of the Local Customer Team as well as getting hands-on clinical experience within the hospitals. Phase 2 consists of a Nine-month rotation in Waukesha, sharing our time between Headquarters, Research Park, and the Healthcare Institute. Our time at HQ and RP is focused around marketing projects, executive networking, and supporting our field team remotely, while our time at HCI is strictly focused on modality and sales training to obtain a comprehensive understanding of the GEHC product portfolio. CLPs are put through a rigorous training program that consists of classroom training for four months with frequent pitchouts on key learnings. Phases 1 & 2 prepare us for Phase 3, where we rejoin our Local Customer Team and take on a mini territory of 15-20 accounts. Phase 3 is our opportunity to put into action what we've learned in the classroom. We are fully responsible for these accounts and have a set Operating Plan for equipment and service.
