  • Founder, CTO, VP Engineering

    Coral8, Inc. (acquired by Sybase, Inc.)

    (Privately Held; Computer Software industry)

    January 2003 — March 2009 (6 years 3 months)

    Founded Coral8, the company that created a highly scalable fault-tolerant software platform for analyzing massive volumes of data in real-time, a.k.a. a Complex Event Processing (CEP) or a Data Stream Processing engine.

    - Hired and lead a high-caliber 30+-person team of software, test, support, and professional services engineers
    - Defined the high-level product architecture which allowed the highly scalable fault-tolerant engine to analyze 100,000s of events per second, with 1 millisecond latency.
    - Designed Continuous Computation Language (CCL), an SQL-like language for continuous analysis of streaming data. CCL allows any SQL developer to quickly build highly sophisticated real-time applications.
    - Personally responsible for evangelizing the product to 60+ customers, mostly in financial services (Citibank, Merrill Lynch, Liquidnet, numerous hedge funds). The product is used for algo trading, algo execution, risk management, liquidity management, fraud detection, etc.

    Coral8 merged with Aleri, Inc. and the combined company was acquired by Sybase, Inc.

  • Director, Product Development

    Akamai Technologies

    (Public Company; AKAM; Internet industry)

    May 2000 — May 2002 (2 years 1 month)

    Responsible for the Edge Computing Strategy, shifting Akamai’s strategic focus from content caching and streaming to edge computing (cloud computing). Responsible for product conception, high-level design, architecture, requirements, external and internal evangelizing.

    - Conceived and built the EdgeJava product and program. EdgeJava allows customers to run Java applications and web services on Akamai’s massively scalable distributed platform on the edge of the Internet (20,000 servers, in 65 countries). This brings unparalleled scalability, reliability, performance, and cost advantages to customer applications.
    - Responsible for Building an Ecosystem for Edge Computing. Responsible for partnerships with IBM, Oracle, BEA, Vignette, ATG, and others.
    - Filed for several patents related to Edge Computing
    - Hired and supervised an advanced product development team.

  • Founder & CTO

    CallTheShots, Inc. (acquired by Akamai)

    (Internet industry)

    May 1998 — May 2000 (2 years 1 month)

    Founded a “Web 2.0” company in the late ‘90s. CallTheShots allowed users to create live HTML and XML data feeds out of websites, to automate extracting information from arbitrary Web pages, combine information from multiple pages, monitor information for changes, and publish their own feeds. Today, CallTheShots service would be described in terms of “Ajax”, “RSS”, and “mashups”
