John Wayne S. III’s Summary

BIO
John Wayne began his career in the entertainment industry over a decade ago. He got his start working on an independent film in the Art department as a PA. Ironically the film he was working on was director/producer George Tillman's (Soul Food, Barber Shop & Men of Honor) first film "Scenes for the Soul". After the production wrapped, John Wayne continue to work with George and his partner Robert Teitel on other projects. John Wayne then partnered with a local director and formed a production company. They started out shooting music videos for small labels. During this time he went to work as a publicist for Grammy nominated music producer Steve "Silk" Hurley. Silk has worked with artist such as Michael Jackson, Prince and Madonna. John Wayne started off with little to no experience as a publicist and during his 3 year stint, became Director of International A&R. He setup and oversaw offices/agents in the UK and Japan. He facilitated distribution, production, publishing, artist and record deals around the world. While at Silk, John Wayne gained many contacts and built strong relationships. During this time he started JWS Productions Inc to produce music videos and TV commercials. Over the past decade John Wayne has produced/directed 200+ music video and 70 TV commercials. In 2003 he signed a multi year deal to develop TV program for the Colours TV network. Its combined viewership is 25 million + households. Brothers Ed & Mike Delly of Down Low Records approached John Wayne and sought his advice about the direction of there label. John Wayne joined the Down Low Records team as President & General Manager. In this capacity he was responsible for the labels overall direction and operations. He was instrumental is propelling the career of Bodaiga. The hit single "We Don't Play Dat" produced by Lil Jon, featuring Bun B & Wine-O was an instant radio success. With 1,000 + spins on BDS & Mediabase around the world, Bodaiga was launched onto the world stage.
