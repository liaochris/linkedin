Hugh Mason’s Summary

I mentor and invest in marketing, media and technology businesses and I help to build wealth for nations, regions and communities as a consultant to public sector intervention programmes.

My core talents are generating ideas and making them happen, translating strategy into economic growth, and business facilitation/storytelling for diverse audiences. I add most value around the strategic- and people-issues that often seem to hold back teams from achieving the success they deserve. I have recently started training in Transactional Analysis to support that aspect of my work.

Beginning my career as an electronic engineering apprentice, I went on to read Physics at University. Shortly after graduating I became a science TV producer, joining the BBC's flagship popular Science TV show, Tomorrow's World, broadcasting live to an audience of 8 million across the UK. I went freelance and set up a successful independent TV production business that made multiple award-winning science TV programmes for broadcasters around the world in addition to large-format digital media content for science centres.

Ultimately, running a creative business turned out to be more fun than hands-on creative work. So, in 2001, I co-founded Pembridge Partners to provide finance and advice to marketing, media and technology companies. The sobering experience of investing my own wealth in other peoples’ businesses through Pembridge has given me a vivid picture of the real issues surrounding growth in sub-sectors as diverse as Digital, Design, Music, Television, Film, Animation, Games, Public Relations, Advertising, Direct Marketing and Sales Promotion.

I relocated to Singapore full time with my young family in January 2009. I now work with a wide variety of marketing, media, and technology enterprises to help their owners create, build and realise value.

  • Co-founder / Chairman

    Undergraduate Ambassadors Limited

    (Education Management industry)

    2002 — 2007 (5 years )

    I co-founded UAS with popular science writer and broadcaster Simon Singh OBE to address the chronic shortage of Science, Technology, Engineering and Maths teachers in UK schools. It enables undergraduates studying those subjects at UK Universities to try out teaching as a working experience.

    The Undergraduate Ambassadors Scheme provides the framework for a degree course module awarding academic credit to science, technology, engineering and maths undergraduates working with teachers in local schools.

    137 departments at 48 UK Universities (around 25% of the total population) now provide teaching experience opportunities to their undergraduates are able to try out teaching as a career option and each year 25% of these go on to apply for Initial Teacher Training. As a direct result of UAS, over one hundred new teachers are now entering the profession each year in the UK.
