David Brodie’s Summary

Currently working with Optimum Public Relations. Established in 1996, Optimum is Canada’s leading full-service communications and corporate affairs firm.
As a member of the Cossette Communication Group, Canada’s largest marketing and communications firm, Optimum has access to more than 1,400 employees and main offices in Vancouver, Toronto, Halifax, Los Angeles, New York, Boston, London and Shanghai. Optimum can draw upon the particular multi-disciplinary mix of communication expertise best suited to particular client needs. This convergent, multinational network allows Optimum to provide clients with high-level strategic counsel and communication planning services, as well as cost-effective program implementation.

    David Brodie brings 10 years of communications experience to Optimum Vancouver having worked for elected official at both the federal and provincial level, as well as providing consul to public and private sector clients from around the world.

  • Producer / Host

    Travel in 10 - 10 minute travel podcast

    (Broadcast Media industry)

    January 2004 — Present (6 years 5 months)

    Travel in 10 is a 10 minute travel podcast taking listeners to destinations around the globe to present soundscapes and audiotours from hip hotels, great restaurants, concerts, festivals and events.

    The show was on of the first travel podcasts online and is consistently one of the top rated travel downloads on Itunes attacting on audience of over 15,000 monthly.

    Past episodes have explored topics ranging from tours of boutique hotels in Portugal, snowboard festivals in the Canadian Rockies and interviews with guidebook authors and personalities in the travel industry. It includes both audio and video casts that focus on luxury and adventure travel.

  • Senior Analyst

    Canadian Embassy - Tokyo, Japan

    (Government Agency; 501-1000 employees; International Affairs industry)

    October 2005 — December 2005 (3 months)

    Situated on two major fault lines, the Tokyo Metropolitan area is highly susceptible to earthquake damage. Like all government and private sector organizations in Japan, the Canadian Embassy was concerned with planning for the potential impact of a large scale seismic event on the Japanese capital.

    David Brodie was hired as a consultant to work together with senior embassy staff to design and oversee a realistic simulation that would fully test the embassy’s ability to respond to a large scale crisis. Crisis communications was a key focus of an exercise that was meant to simulate an earthquake measuring 7.9 on the Richter scale.
