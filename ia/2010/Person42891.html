Kathleen Schulweis, PCC, CPCC’s Summary

I am a Certified Professional Co-Active Coach and PCC and I provide coaching and training for business and personal success. I focus on solo-preneurs, professionals and executives and help them overcome their demons and fears that grip them tightly and keep them from fulfillment.

I have worked with key executives on major non-profits to help them with a fear of change, fear of speaking, and fear of conflict. By transforming their fears into inquiries, my clients learn how to be their own coach so that they themselves achieve their goals. My expertise includes helping young professionals transition into key management posts and aiding key executives in developing highly functional teams.

At Confidence Connections™, we provide training and coaching for business and personal success. We offer the following services:

• Confidence Building Coaching and Education Programs
• Life Coaching for Professionals
• Relationship Coaching for Couples
• Adolescent Confidence Building
• Strategic Coaching for Problem Employees
• Problem-solving for the Overworked and Underproducing
• Sales Training for Professionals who HATE the idea of SELLING
• Presentation Training for Professionals
• Goal Setting and Life Planning Strategies
• Creative Time Management
• Career Transition
• Workplace Conflict
• Conflict Resolution

  • Director

    California Institute of Technology

    (Educational Institution; Research industry)

    1993 — 2000 (7 years )

    As Founding Director of the Caltech Women's Center I worked under the provost and focused on developing major policies and programs to integrate women into the sciences while improving communication, teams, and the image of the Institute. While at Caltech I was co-principal investigator on a National Science Foundation grant working with artists to develop images of women in the sciences to further break down the barriers between women and the sciences. Between 1993 and 1998 the number of women undergraduate students at Caltech jumped from 18 to 38% of the student body. In the last years of my tenure at Caltech I focused on business development issues for women who had technological innovations and wished to move outside academia and develop their technologies in the free market. This focus lead me to create a program designed to education, introduce, and fund such enterprises for women scientists. This was yet another first for Caltech.
