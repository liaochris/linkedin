Stuart N. Robinson, Ph.D.’s Summary

Dr. Robinson is a licensed psychologist who specializes in treating college students, middle-aged and older adults. He provides coaching, counseling and therapy and performs symptom screening, neurological and psychological testing. He specializes in diagnosing adults with ADD, Dyslexia and other Learning Differences, and Asperger’s and providing them the counseling they need to not only cope, but to “own” their challenges and to be proud of their differences. Much of his practice includes counseling senior corporate executives and adults who are Talented and Gifted.

In addition to teaching at Southern Methodist University for over six years, he served as Dean of Students, Head of Behavioral Medicine and Ethics at The Medical University of the Americas where he introduced integrating the practice of Psychology and Medicine to medical students. He now practices Integrative Primary Care and collaborates with family physicians throughout Dallas, Texas. Dr. Robinson’s expertise includes Cognitive-Behavioral Therapy and Behavioral Medicine. He treats patients with anxiety issues including post-traumatic stress due to physical and sexual abuse in childhood, obsessive-compulsive, social anxiety and phobias, depression, substance abuse and personality tendencies and disorders.

Stuart N. Robinson, Ph.D.’s Specialties:

I specialize in diagnosing and treating adults with Attention Deficit Disorder or with Learning Differences like Dyslexia. This mostly includes college students, singles, husbands and wives, senior-level, corporate executives and entrepreneurs.

I also specialize in Behavioral Medicine which is an interdisciplinary field that encourages medical doctors and psychologists to work closely together on the behavioral and psychosocial aspects of illness, health and wellness.
