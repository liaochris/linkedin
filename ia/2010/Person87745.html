  • Senior Network Architect

    Dimension Data

    (Public Company; DDT; Information Technology and Services industry)

    December 2000 — June 2003 (2 years 7 months)

    Lead architect of the team managing the US Courts enterprise WAN servicing the entire Judiciary Branch of the US Government. The team administers 700 routers supporting approximately 32,000 Federal users in the domestic US and its territories and access to over 700 public web/e-Commerce servers that generate revenues of several million dollars per year for the Judiciary.

    Assist in managing network security by administering the configurations of nine PIX firewalls and six Internet gateway routers at three National Internet gateways.

    Work closely with the Intrusion Detection System (IDS) Team in order to tighten security and maintain the integrity of the network and server resources available to both the public as well as internal users.

    Major projects included moving the three National Internet gateways from Court buildings to three major service provider data centers and implementing automatic gateway failover via BGP. My responsibilities included building a 20 router lab used for simulation and testing, determining the hardware required, IOS versions, IP addressing. I wrote the Migration Plan, assigned roles and responsibilities to each participant, and assisted in ordering the WAN circuits. I wrote PERL scripts that tested the network before and after each stage of the migration to verify migration success. I also created complete technical documentation that included Visio diagrams illustrating each part of the migration. The migration was a complete success and completed on time.

    Day-to-day responsibilities include proactively participating in network design projects, security reviews, router and switch installations, level-3 troubleshooting, general WAN administration, configuring and supporting Cisco routers/Catalyst switches/Cisco Content Engines.
