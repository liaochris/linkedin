Valerie Fitzgerald’s Summary

For over the last 20 years, I've built a multi-million dollar real estate business in the greater Los Angeles area specializing in Beverly Hills, Bel Air, Hollywood Hills, Santa Monica, Brentwood and Malibu, as well as international destinations.

I've been ranked among Coldwell Banker's Top 10 agents nationwide, and have earned the recognition of the Los Angeles Business Journal for the highest sales volume in L.A. County for residential real estate.

Currently I'm the exclusive sales agent at Latitude 33 - a luxury short-rise condominium project in Marina Del Ray/ Venice Beach area http://www.latitude33la.com.

I was also recently the exclusive sales agent for The Carlyle Residences, a luxury condo high rise on the Wilshire Corridor in Los Angeles.

I've been fortunate to have completed my first book: Heart & Sold: How to Survive and Build a Recession-Proof Business. If you are fresh into real estate or want to learn more about how to get focused, become goal setting and organized, I think you might find Heart and Sold helpful.

My real passion is philanthropy, children and motivating others upwards. I helped to establish a charity foundation, and I speak around the country at business conventions to share my love for helping others.

Some fun highlights in real esate have been when I was a guest correspondent on "Entertainment Tonight" and MTV's "Cribs.”

    Everyone in the life of a foster child is paid to be there and with a revolving door of homes and families, it's not hard to see why these children deserve something more. Children Uniting Nations (C.U.N.) began in 1996 with the goal of making it easier to adopt foster children by improving foster care and adoption laws. C.U.N. has also created a permanent mentoring program in order to help these children bond with a caring person who many be the only constant in their lives. It has been shown that 90% of of at risk children who have mentors stay in school, their overall performance at school improves, their self esteem improves, they are more cooperative in their foster care home and develop better skills when interacting with other children. The results are extraordinary!
    The mentoring program began after the success of International Day of the Child. Day of the Child is an annual event that gives homeless and foster children a day of fun and compassion.
