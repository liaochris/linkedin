Penelope Mudd’s Summary

Early in her Silicon Valley years (88-08) Penny's focus was on l10n+I18n: testing, adapting and marketing software outside the USA. She experienced 1st hand factors that led to the success of several international distributed teams. A classic "leader-servant" w skills forged 'at the coal face' - and informed by her expertise in project management, SQA and relationships (before "social media") - Penny understood what it took to operate globally. She represented her int'l. customers' views and requirements back into the US product team with nuanced insight. She balanced requirements, strategic relationship management and the latest technical advances. She provided a high level of cross-cultural support to the distributed teams who made the products happen. [Her language skills include: native English speaker, conversant in French and German, reading skills in Dutch and Italian and rudimentary Japanese.]

Her most satisfying wins came from initiating/stewarding partnerships to meet US HQ's objectives in EU & Asia. An early hire (1999) of Health Hero Network, Inc. (now Robert Bosch Healthcare), she was instrumental during Health Hero's first international overtures, which evolved into a 5-year push to initiate operations in the highly-regulated European and Japanese markets. Delivery of complex projects to partners in NL and Japan was crucial to establishing HHN's credibility and for its partners striving to create sustainable businesses from pilot projects.

Her accomplishments included 1) delivery of complex programs comprised of SW and HW (ROM) releases, contracts and regulatory documentation, manufacturing process SW and SOPs, training and launches. 2)
leadership/integration of teams in different countries. 3) VAR relationship management. 4) technology transfer.

The Health Hero was acquired by Bosch in 2008, signaling the German giant's entry into health care information technology & services.The firm was relaunched as Robert Bosch Healthcare in 2009,
