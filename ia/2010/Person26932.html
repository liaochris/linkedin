Branden Barber’s Summary

After whipping up Greenpeace's first national website for Australia while I was the Victorian Office Coordinator, and after an exciting time with Marine Division on the Rainbow Warrior and the MV Greenpeace in 1992-93, I moved into the world of software and internet development. I've played roles ranging from Evangelist and Product Manager (www.actioneer.com) to Team Lead and Creative Integrator (Studio Archetype) to Sr. Project Manager and Director of Creative Delivery (www.sapient.com). While engaged with professional services I worked with such clients as Disney and 3Com and managed budgets of up to US$5mm and teams of up to 50 people.

I initiated Different Solutions as a partner with three others (different.com.au) and then moved to rural Victoria taking on small web projects to eCommerce implementations and Branding (ajilon.com.au), as well as overall web and communications strategies, as Twin Lizards Creative (twinlizards.com).

I have taught at the University level for creative media students at RMIT (www.rmit.edu.au) and the TAFE schools in country Victoria.

Working for Rainforest Action Network (www.ran.org) in San Francisco as Development Director and Major Gifts Officer was a great gift - being able to come back to the environmental fold after being away for 10 years. I was directly responsible for the development department and an annual budgeted income of $4.5m. As a member of the Executive Leadership Team I had the opportunity to learn the ropes in non-profit management. The experience was a great boost personally and professionally and I'm grateful for having had the chance to do so.

Now as Development Director at the Hoffman Institute Foundation we save the world in a different way by helping people be better, happier, more loving people. Hoffman has a stellar track record and has taken on a leadership development focus that is making a valuable difference for individuals, families and companies around the world. Check it out!

    The Hoffman Process is an eight-day intensive residential course of personal discovery and development. Held in beautiful retreat settings in both Napa Valley, California and the Berkshire region of Massachusetts, the Process allows you to examine and better understand your life and reveals why you behave the way you do. Published scientific research has also demonstrated the long-term positive effects that the Hoffman Process has had on relieving depression and anxiety.To date, more than 70,000 people around the world have used the tried and tested Hoffman techniques to improve their quality of life and restore their relationships with friends and family.
