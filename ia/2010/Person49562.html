David Hablewitz’s Summary

It's not about the software.

Lance Armstrong said it well with "It's not about the bike." The details of knowledge of specific software are secondary to all the other skills that make someone stand out. It's the skills that cannot be learned from a book that set one apart from the rest and why some people are successful no matter what job they set out to do.

My passion is in bringing people and technology together, finding innovative ways to solve business needs, giving my company a dominating competitive advantage over yours and helping it be agile and adaptive to changing conditions. I have a degree in computer science and 24 years of experience in the field providing leadership, managing projects, providing system administration and infrastructure architecture, and designing and developing software. I am always working with the goal to seek out ways to make people more effective, efficient, and satisfied in their jobs.

Everyone has a passion. If they're lucky, they also work it and if they do, it will show. In my case it shows in my involvement in social media and trade websites.
I am consistently in the Top Innovators list at Ideajam.net
http://ideajam.net/IdeaJam/P/ij.nsf/topinnovators?readform
In fact, my need to innovate doesn't stop at the end of the workday. I have designed or enhanced numerous products to be used in wilderness sports like whitewater kayaking, mountain biking, backpacking and adventure racing. I find innovation addictive.

  • Lifeguard

    Anheuser-Busch

    (Public Company; BUD; Food & Beverages industry)

    May 1981 — August 1983 (2 years 4 months)

    This was not your typical lifeguard position watching a quiet pool or beach. At Adventure Island, lifeguarding was intense work. Our two goals were:

    1. Keep the guests safe.
    The lifeguard profession is similar to other emergency professions like firefighting and EMT and they go through similar special training. Lifeguards must be constantly vigilant and ready to make decisions and take action in life-threatening situations where seconds count. Typical lifeguard positions in other settings have less than one rescue per year. At Adventure Island, guards protect 10,000+ guests per day and may average several rescues per week. On one Friday I made a record-setting eleven rescues!

    2. Make sure the guests enjoy their visit.
    Like any other service-related business, customer relations were key to our company's success. At Adventure Island, the lifeguards are ambassadors of the company. As such we took pride in our job. Working in this environment taught me much about customer relations and professionalism.
