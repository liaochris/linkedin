Matthew Dorson’s Summary

I am a mechanical engineer with an eminently practical view of the world. I can solve open ended problems or work the bugs out of almost released production designs. I am comfortable running every tool in the engineers trade, from the CAD workstation to the welding torch. I have focused on renewable energy throughout my career. I thrive on finding clean, simple solutions to complex problems.

Matthew Dorson’s Specialties:

Matthew Dorson’s Experience

  • Principal Engineer

    Mystic River Engineering, LLC

    (Privately Held; Renewables & Environment industry)

    April 2010 — Present (7 months)

    Mystic River Engineering is a consulting company with a passion for keeping the planet clean by designing and building the prototype hardware required to advance the state of renewable energy technology. We have worked on a range of clean technologies including solar manufacturing equipment, bio-fuels research systems, hybrid electric vehicles, wind turbine transmissions, hydrogen generation equipment, fuel cells, hydraulic and pneumatic energy storage systems, bicycles, sailboats and more. If you have a sound idea in renewable energy, Mystic River can provide the nuts and bolts engineering required to help you succeed.

  • Senior Engineer

    GreenMountain Engineering

    (Privately Held; Renewables & Environment industry)

    July 2008 — April 2010 (1 year 10 months)

    • Developed gas sealing and electrical contact systems for chemical vapor deposition reactors used in solar cell production equipment.
    • Designed, built and tested infrared soldering systems for making multiple, simultaneous front surface connections to solar cells. Used thermal modeling and experimental results to verify temperature uniformity across the system.
    • Developed process, instrumentation and wiring diagrams for a multi-stage ethanol fermentation system. Built, debugged and delivered system.
    • Analyzed novel wind turbine transmission designs.
    • Designed silicon feeding system for production of solar cells in a clean room environment. Reduced equipment cost by a factor of eight and simplified installation and maintenance operations required by the system.
    • Performed engineering due diligence on a range of new technology ventures including: algae bioreactors, microbial fuel cells, waste water treatment, and quantum dot solar panels.

  • Sr. Systems Engineer

    Nuvera Fuel Cells, Inc.

    (Privately Held; Renewables & Environment industry)

    April 2000 — July 2008 (8 years 4 months)

    • Designed, tested and deployed a resistive braking system for an electric lift truck. This design improved the power dissipation by a factor of ten over existing systems while maintaining tight packaging constraints and cutting system cost by a factor of four.
    • Managed the design, documentation and testing of a 50 kilogram per day hydrogen generation system.
    • Designed and directed the commercial launch of a pneumatically actuated, cascade storage and dispensing system intended to refuel hydrogen powered lift trucks.
    • Worked with government agencies (UL and CSA) to obtain approvals and influence emerging codes for hydrogen fueling stations
    • Developed and helped to patent a range of novel fuel processing systems and support equipment United States Patents: 6,641,625 6,861,169 6,986,797 and 7,066,973

  • Engineering Consultant

    Arthur D. Little

    (Privately Held; Management Consulting industry)

    March 1998 — April 2000 (2 years 2 months)

    • Directed the design and development of a compact fuel cell generator appliance. Managed project budget, staffing, customer interface and site preparation. Directed a 5 person international field service team during initial product launch and troubleshooting.

  • Mechanical Engineer

    Transportation Design and Manufacturing

    (Mechanical or Industrial Engineering industry)

    • Designed the air conditioning and accessory drive system for an electric truck. Worked with the motor vendor to develop a unique motor optimized for minimum power consumption and package size.
    • Developed a thermal management system for an electric vehicle traction battery.
    • Provided nationwide field support and troubleshooting for a fleet of electric vehicles.

  • Mechanical Engineer

    US Environmental Protection Agency

    (Government Agency; Government Administration industry)

    May 1994 — August 1995 (1 year 4 months)

    • Developed a compact, high efficiency, variable displacement, hydraulic pump-motor.
    • Tested the exhaust emissions of vehicles entered in the 1994 and 1995 student HEV Challenge.
