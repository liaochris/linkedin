Kasmira Smarzo’s Experience

  • Risk Assessment Consultant - Micro Hydro Energy Generation

    The Green Valleys

    (Renewables & Environment industry)

    June 2009 — September 2009 (4 months)

    The Green Valleys (TGV) installs micro-hydro electric generators for landowners and communities by offering complete services from initial feasibility studies to financing. They had 10 installations, ranging from 10kW to 36kW systems, but were seeking capital financing to increase the pace of installations. As part of a two-month dissertation, I worked with the Chairman of the Board to conduct a qualitative risk analysis of their operations. This work involved research of UK government regulation, academic papers, and industry data. In addition, I expanded buy-in from Board Committee members by successfully communicating the risk management process through an interactive presentation. My report was part of their submission as finalists for the Big Green Challenge, a £1 million contest to encourage community-led responses to climate change.

    My accomplishments included:
    • Clear explanation of complex technical information led to a three-fold increase in interview participation.
    • Concluded that ‘loss of community trust’ and ‘political disinterest’ in micro-scale renewable energy are TGV’s greatest risks.
    • Professionalism of the report demonstrated to the judges that TGV had a thorough appreciation of the task ahead, contributing to being one of three winners and adding £300,000 towards new projects.

    Appraisal by supervising manager:
    “The risk management assessment was a vital step in our transition from a volunteer led ‘good cause’ into a sustainable business with a full understanding of the technological and commercial risks. We still maintain a risk log and this is reviewed by a Board Committee member each month.”

  • Consultant

    E.ON Climate & Renewables

    (Public Company; EONGn.DE; Utilities industry)

    January 2009 — May 2009 (5 months)

    While studying full time for my MBA, I took on a part-time project as an external consultant for the Operational Performance team, within the Asset Construction and Operations group. The group was responsible for a portfolio of 19 onshore and 3 offshore wind farms, leading the European market with 64% in total installed offshore capacity in 2010. I was part of a key task force to identify internal activities to maintain competitive advantage. Through client interviews and activity based costing, I developed a model that ascribed a value to each process and then ranked them based on strategic importance. Core/strategic processes were retained in-house and a focused apprenticeship recruitment program developed to increase technical capacity of the group.

    My accomplishments included:
    • The Task Force’s report enabled the redirection of resources that aligned human capital investment with core capabilities.
    • Built strong client relationships with the head of Asset Construction & Operations and other senior staff, which formed a basis for productive stakeholder discussions that helped identify critical activities that contributed to their competitive advantage.

    Appraisal by supervising manager:
    "The project you were involved in as a challenging one given the size of the subject and the timescales however we were pleased with the outcome considering the challenge. There is an ongoing review of internal and contract employment, the value to roles and skills for the benefit of business efficiency and I can see changes being made over the next two years in line with some of the findings. I was particularly impressed with your engagement in the project and positive attitude, thank you for the work you did for us."

  • Economist - Industry Employment Projections

    Bureau of Labor Statistics

    (Government Agency; Government Administration industry)

    July 2006 — August 2008 (2 years 2 months)

    I was responsible for modelling the government and investment portion of GDP in order to create 10-year forecasts. The 10-year projections forecasted economic growth, employment by industry and occupation, and output by industry. They are widely used in career guidance, in planning education and training programs, and in studying long-range employment trends. I improved the design, operation, and improvement of the division's existing economic models, which produced relevant, auditable estimates, used robust statistical methods, and maintained methodology documentation. In addition, I conducted numerous research projects on methodology and alternative sources for data.

    My accomplishments included:
    • Won an Instant Recognition Award for collaboration with other federal agencies to coordinate, share, and explain data with the Bureau of Economic Analysis when researching GDP revision methodology.
    • Authored economic reports based on solid quantitative analysis of a new review methodology, resulting in upper management’s decision to adopt my recommendations that better corresponded to policy initiatives.
    • Prepared internal research document that explored historical federal defense spending and aggregated results from published studies of war-time defense spending outlook over the 2006-2016 projections horizon, a database was created in EViews and linear regressions were run of defense spending to identify potential correlation with economic trends. The report was well researched, very well written, and contributed fundamentally to projection staff's understanding of the likely impact on future federal spending.

  • Economist - Consumer Price Index

    Bureau of Labor Statistics

    (Government Agency; Government Administration industry)

    November 2001 — July 2006 (4 years 9 months)

    I was responsible for managing the work of 30 economists to guarantee their output was delivered against published release dates. The CPI is the official measure of consumer inflation for the national U.S. economy. The monthly index is used to adjust other economic series into real terms and also adjusts the income of almost 80 million people. I held the only position that worked laterally across the organisation to ensure accuracy by resolving data quality, process, and efficiency issues. This required influencing people to fix errors even though I lacked the authority as their supervisor. I found efficiency gains after independently developing a performance management system. A strategic thinker, my universal understanding of the production of the CPI from field to publication made me the go-to person, resulting in my being on almost every development team, usually the most junior member.

    My accomplishments included:
    • Reduced average program deadline overruns from 3.5 hours to 0.5 hours and eliminated costly data reruns, resulting in a five-fold reduction in budget overruns.
    • Took the lead role in development of a new team performance report, which was commended by members of the steering committee that oversaw the work.
    • Led the development of a detailed production plan, applying business process mapping skills using an adapted version of the Unified Modelling Language (ULM).
    • Led the office-wide effort to implement the Configuration Management (CM) Plan for the tax system.
