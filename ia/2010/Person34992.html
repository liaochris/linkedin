Carolyne Orazi’s Summary

Landscape Ecology is where my heart lies. This branch of Landscape Architecture is where I have concentrated my studies at school and is something I believe is critical to the bettering of our world. I would like to focus my professional development in this area. I have been volunteering with Golden Gate National Recreational Area; working on a BMP document for Horse Keeping practices that will reduce the impact on the environment. I also work with them on early detection of Invasive Plant Species thoughout the park's land. I am a Wildlife volunteer with East Bay Regional Parks, monitoring the activities of various species under environmental protection, especially the Least Tern Project being conducted at the Hayward Shoreline.

While at the University of Washington, I completed a two-year program in Restoration Ecology at the College of Forestry. I am proficient in various editions of AutoCAD. I am also skilled in Adobe Creative Suite 2, and Microsoft Office. I have experience with ArcMap GIS.
