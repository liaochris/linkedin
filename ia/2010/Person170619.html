Tom Parker’s Summary

Tom Parker is a China specialist with a focus on collaboration between Australia and China to broaden our bi-lateral relationship. A fluent Mandarin speaker, Tom’s long term engagement with China has resulted in impressive networks and successful business outcomes. He has held China focused roles in The Asialink Centre, Austrade (Beijing), City of Melbourne and as writer and reporter for the ABC in both China and Australia for Radio National’s Asia Pacific Program.

Tom has managed programs and facilitated business and government partnerships in China for the City of Melbourne including raising the profile of the Melbourne-Tianjin Sister City relationship and recently leading the business delegation for the NSW Premier’s China Mission. In 2007, Tom was keynote speaker at the CPA China Summit, and regularly delivers intercultural training programs for multinational clients in Australia and China including: the Australian Football League, AMP, Intrepid Travel , Lonely Planet, Morgan Stanley, FDI and the Melbourne Football Club.

Tom incorporates a life-long passion for China into his professional life having studied Chinese (Mandarin) since 1987. He has completed a Bachelor of Arts (Asian Studies) with honours from Melbourne University and the Australian National University and a Graduate Diploma of International Business Studies (in Mandarin) at the University of International Business and Economics in Beijing. Tom has also studied in the Advanced Chinese Language Program at the East China Normal University in Shanghai.

Tom is a well connected Australia-China facilitator with extensive experience assisting business and government better understand and access their opportunities in China.
