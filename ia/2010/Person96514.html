Jerry M. Ray’s Summary

Jerry M. Ray is a senior corporate executive with more than 30 years of business communications and public affairs experience. He specializes in doing what has never been done before.

Ray recently returned to Washington, DC after serving as Senior Vice President, External Affairs for The St. Joe Company (NYSE: JOE). Ray came to St. Joe in 1997 and has had a leadership role in its transformation from a 70-year old industrial conglomerate making paper, to Florida’s largest landowner and respected developer, making places.

He can be reached at jerrymray@gmail.com or +1 202.642.1042.

Jerry M. Ray’s Specialties:

Large-scale business-coalition development, strategic alliances, corporate strategy, public affairs, government relations, message development, crisis communications, media relations and public-debate management.

Jerry M. Ray’s Experience

  • Senior Vice President External Affairs

    The St. Joe Company (NYSE: JOE)

    (Public Relations and Communications industry)

    August 2008 — December 2009 (1 year 5 months)

    Ray was most recently senior vice president, external affairs at The St. Joe Company (NYSE: JOE), one of the largest landowners and real estate developers in Florida. At St. Joe, Ray developed and implemented strategy to bring expertise, capital and shareholder value to St. Joe’s extensive land holdings located primarily in Northwest Florida. He led a staff responsible for corporate communications, public debate management, government relations and economic development.

    • Corporate Strategy
    As a member of the St. Joe senior executive and disclosure committees, Ray provided senior leadership for the multi-billion dollar, publicly traded company. He handled relationships and communications strategy in concert with shareholders, government officials and strategic partners. He developed message strategy to optimize investor presentations with major shareholders, financial analysts and investment bankers. He has managed messages for media interviews with journalist for the Wall Street Journal, Barron’s, The New York Times, The Washington Post, The Financial Times, Bloomberg News, CNN and Fox News.

    • Change-Agent Communications
    Ray managed the intensive public debate associated with St. Joe’s role as a large-scale developer and change agent for a broad region of Florida. He strengthened relationships between St. Joe and a wide range of stakeholder audiences, including shareholders, customers, strategic partners, environmental organizations, public officials, community leaders and the media.

    Ray was instrumental in the planning, entitlement, approvals and funding of the new Northwest Florida Beaches International Airport now being completed near Panama City Beach, FL. Ray led a region-wide effort to bring Southwest Airlines service to the new airport. Southwest begins service at the new facility with eight daily flights beginning May 23, 2010.

  • Senior Vice President, Strategic Alliances and Communications

    The St. Joe Company

    (Public Company; JOE; Real Estate industry)

    August 2006 — August 2008 (2 years 1 month)

    As JOE’s business model matured and later, in response to the economic downturn, Ray developed and managed a complex message platform. When JOE shifted its business model from an end-to-end developer to a strategic alliance partner, Ray led the development of successful relationships with a broad range of strategic partners.

  • Senior Vice President, Corporate Communications and Public Affairs

    The St. Joe Company

    (Public Company; JOE; Real Estate industry)

    June 1999 — July 2006 (7 years 2 months)

    Ray managed intense media coverage of the regional impacts of JOE’s unprecedented development plans for Northwest Florida, including over 50 resort, residential, industrial, office and retail projects. In addition, Ray played a leadership role in:

    • Investor Relations
    • Large-Scale Entitlement and Infrastructure Approvals
    • Corporate Rebranding

  • Vice President, Corporate Communications

    The St. Joe Company

    (Public Company; JOE; Real Estate industry)

    November 1997 — May 1999 (1 year 7 months)

    Ray joined a new executive team recruited to transform an aging industrial conglomerate into a dynamic real estate developer. He was the company’s communications architect, creating an broad external-affairs infrastructure for a company seen as a change agent for a large area of Florida.

    • Corporate Repositioning
    Ray was instrumental in all phases of the repositioning of the new JOE from the company that had been making paper to an extraordinary “place maker.” Every aspect of corporate mission, identity and culture were transformed. Ray played a leadership role in the effort to redefine JOE’s vision, corporate name, brand and environmental culture.

    • Intensive Public-Debate Management
    As company spokesperson, Ray managed messages and messengers during intense media coverage.

  • Senior Vice President

    Powell Tate

    (Public Relations and Communications industry)

    1991 — 1997 (6 years )

    Ray was a founder and a senior vice president of Washington’s most prestigious public affairs firm, Powell Tate. Ray grew his practice to be the firm’s largest, with clients that included Delta Air Lines, General Dynamics, McDonnell Douglas, Lockheed Martin, and The Walt Disney Company.

    • Coalition Development
    Ray led the creation of a broad coalition that supported a new bilateral aviation treaty with Japan and opened additional airline service between the U.S. and Japan.

    His team created the Submarine Industrial Base Council, designed to protect the U.S. nuclear submarine industrial base. The coalition became instrumental in the congressional debate on the future of the U.S. Navy’s Seawolf nuclear attack submarine.

    Ray developed messages and organized a broad coalition to support an international antitrust debate on behalf of Travelocity (Sabre Holdings), Netscape and Sun Microsystems. The coalition successfully stimulated a public debate that called into question antitrust regulation of software standards.

    • Crisis Management
    Ray managed crisis communications for Avions de Transport Regional (ATR), the French aircraft manufacturer, when an ATR-72 aircraft crashed in Indiana with 68 fatalities. He also managed crisis communications regarding e coli 0157:H7 hamburger contamination for Jack in the Box, and later, for Hudson Foods.

    • Media and Congressional Testimony Preparation
    Ray led Powell Tate’s media training and congressional testimony preparation practice. He coached corporate CEOs and senior corporate officials in the airline, defense and telecommunications industries before intensive media interviews and congressional testimony. In addition, he provided media training for larger professional groups.

  • Vice President, Washington

    Burson-Marsteller

    (Public Company; WPPGY; Public Relations and Communications industry)

    September 1988 — August 1991 (3 years )

    At Burson-Marsteller, a global public relations and communications company, Ray developed messages and prepared messengers involved in crisis situations, congressional debates, major financial transactions and international initiatives. He organized and led media relations campaigns for high profile issues and clients.

    • Crisis Management Media Training
    • Coalition Development

  • Press Secretary, US Senator Howell Heflin (D-AL)

    The U.S. Senate

    (Government Agency; Legislative Office industry)

    August 1981 — September 1988 (7 years 2 months)

    From 1981 to 1988, Ray was press secretary to U.S. Senator Howell Heflin (D - AL). He developed communications strategy and managed international and national media relationships. He served in communications positions for hearings conducted by the U.S. Senate Committees on the Judiciary and Ethics, as well as the U.S. Senate Iran-Contra Committee. Ray also participated in the proceedings of the Senate Judiciary Subcommittees on antitrust and intellection property rights.

    • Communications Innovation
    Ray implemented the first satellite feed from a U.S. Senate office to a constituent television station, later broadly adopted by other senate offices and party caucuses.

    • Campaign Management
    Ray also managed communications for Senator Heflin’s successful 1984 reelection campaign. He served as communications director for the “Keating Five Hearings” conducted by the U.S. Senate Select Committee on Ethics.

  • Assistant Director of University Relations

    Auburn University

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    January 1976 — 1981 (5 years )

    Ray managed message development for the full range of university stakeholders. He served as the university’s spokesperson, overseeing its news bureau and broadcast services. He worked with members of the faculty to promote new research initiatives. Ray developed messages and produced materials for a statewide campaign in support of higher education funding. He was a member of an administration team that managed a presidential succession at Auburn.

  • Broadcast Editor, University Relations

    Auburn University

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    May 1972 — December 1975 (3 years 8 months)

    As broadcast editor, Ray developed and produced broadcast material for a wide variety of critical audiences, including the state legislature, alumni, athletic constituencies, prospective students and faculty. He produced award winning video presentations and films.
