Bev Van Horn’s Summary

Contact Bev
520-403-5931
www.BevSellsTucson.com

I attended Christian College, now Columbia College in Columbia, MO and Kansas University, majoring in Journalism. I worked on weekly and monthly newspapers, finally founding The Mineral County Miner and South Fork Tines in Creede, CO. in 1975.

After selling that I started Tawa Indian Gallery and Boutique in South Fork, Co. and later Tucson, AZ.

After selling that, I produced 4 audio books of pioneer women's diaries - buying the rights for the books and abridging them to 3 hours of audio on cassettes.

I also wrote two books, Early History of Mineral and Rio Grande Counties: One Hundred Years of the Past Millennia, 1880 to 1980 and Best Friends Are Like Suspenders: They Hold You Up When You Are Falling Down. Books and audio books are available from Amazon.com.

I 've retired 3 times and plan to work in real estate as long as I'm able. I love meeting the wonderful people I've been blessed with to help find their dream homes. My goals are to have 24 transactions a year, so that I can live comfortably and have time to visit my children and grandchildren.
