Thomas J. Noble’s Experience

  • Plant Manager

    Solar Compounds

    (Chemicals industry)

    June 2010 — Present (4 months)

    Plant Manager of chemical plant that produces sealants, adhesives and coating compounds for the worldwide wire and cable industry. Solar Compounds Corporation is also a major supplier of advanced technology epoxy and silicone electrical/electronic potting and encapsulating compounds for a broad range of electrical devices used in automotive and appliance applications. In addition, Solar also produces an extensive product range of high performance adhesives for a wide variety of industrial adhesive applications.

  • Site Manager

    GENERAL MOTORS CORP

    (Facilities Services industry)

    April 2005 — March 2009 (4 years )

    Managed the successful shutdown, decommissioning and sale of the GM Assembly Plant; Lead Plant decommissioning workforce to execute cold shut down of the facility.
    Coordinated asset transfers to other GM facilities for a net cost savings of $5.9M and facilitated an on-line public auction for the remaining assets.
    Conducted on-site reviews with potential developers leading to the ultimate sale of the facility and served as on-site liaison to new owner and demolition contractor.

  • Manufacturing Engineering Manager / Director of Plant Engineering

    General Motors Corp

    (Automotive industry)

    June 1992 — January 2005 (12 years 8 months)

    Managed the Manufacturing Engineering Group, reporting directly to the Plant Manager. Directed facilities engineering and management, maintenance, environmental compliance, industrial engineering, information technology and manufacturing engineering support for a highly automated motor vehicle assembly plant.
    Successfully converted Plant from automobile to light duty truck production on time and under budget.
    Managed numerous new model introductions, line speed changes and productivity improvements.
    Appointed Plant champion for ISO 14001 certification and Team Leader for ISO 9002 certification.
    Appointed Plant champion for the GM Planned Maintenance Program achieving the Phase 1, 2 & 3 awards.
    Negotiated the outsourcing of Powerhouse activities resulting in the construction of an independently owned and operated Cogeneration facility in spite of significant opposition from local public utility companies
    Managed a successful ergonomics program that minimized cumulative trauma disorders and other work related injuries.
