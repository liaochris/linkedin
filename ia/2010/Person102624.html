John Cohen’s Summary

Finance and Operating Executive for Companies from Start-up to mature stages. Project Manager for key corporate initiatives requiring technical and organizational skills.

Especially effective in companies and non-profit organizations needing a common sense, entrepreneurial attitude and a desire to upgrade finance and accounting operations and financial reporting, while reducing internal and external accounting costs.

For public companies or companies preparing for an IPO - design and implement controls and procedures to satisfy Sarbanes Oxley (SOX) requirements at the least possible cost.

Pre- and Post- Transaction organization and integration of "backroom" activities.

Manage the implementation and modification of ERP systems to suit the needs of the organization.

Design and execute rapid operational and profit improvement measures, improve financial reporting, reduce accounting costs, remediate internal control deficiencies.

Career includes serving as Chief Financial Officer; Project Manager for WorldCom during it's Bankruptcy Workout, Fraud Investigation, and Financial Re-Statement initiatives; Sarbanes-Oxley Compliance; and CPA Audit partner for a number of diverse organizations in the manufacturing, distribution, retail chain, high-tech, not-for-profit, and professional services segments.

John Cohen’s Specialties:

Significant experience consulting for companies in Process Improvement, Financial Statement Reporting and Analysis, Pre- Transaction readiness, and Post Transaction Integration, Corporate Governance, Internal Controls, SOX compliance, Reduction of External Audit Costs.
