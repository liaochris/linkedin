    (Architecture & Planning industry)

    Star Island is a 35-acre undeveloped island located near Northern Eleuthera in the Bahamas. Star Island is slated to be the world’s first “green island.” The developer of S.T.A.R. (Sustainable Terrain and Resources) Island has created a new type of green development model that will deliver a carbon neutral footprint when fully operational. A highly qualified team of designers, builders, consultants and engineers have been employed to ensure that every aspect of this development will operate at an optimum. Innovative design, highly efficient construction and responsible operation practices will enable Star Island to set a new level of service and comfort. A mix of solar, wind, hydro and bio-fuels will combine to provide residents and visitors alike guilt-free luxury in a “pure-paradise" with amenities that will feature a marina, world-class spa, fitness center, multiple restaurants, retail shops, tennis courts and a helipad.

    Star Island is receiving extensive media attention. The island has been featured in Newsweek and on ABC News, and Travel and Leisure gave it a full page in the November issue. The New York Times travel section has named the island its eco-destination of the year for 2009. The principals also presented the project at the United Nations by invitation on February 3, 2009.
