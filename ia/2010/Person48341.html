Dan O'Dea’s Summary

Understanding systems and how their parts work, both individually and as part of the system, is my best skill. I can take a system apart into its components (sub-systems and individual parts) and then improve it by understanding the interrelationships. I have a strong history of improving system efficiency and how systems interact with people. All of this makes me a top problem solver.

I am also an innovator. My employee award history shows many instances of designing and developing new ideas. One of my ideas is pending patent approval with IBM; two others were recently published. With a broad background of study (science, history, philosophy, cultures, political science) and business (I've worked in health care, personal and commercial insurance, and industrial businesses) I can understand any situation.

My main IT interests are data governance and security, information privacy (especially personally identifiable information, or PII), social networking, and intellectual property rights. Non-IT interests include the natural sciences, political and social science, history, and philosophy. I have very strong communication skills, both written and verbal, and an excellent understanding of the human factor in communication. I am a very fast learner, able to pick up new responsibilities rapidly.

I would very much enjoy working in one or more of the following fields: human-computer interactions, privacy protections, creation of data security policy, social networking, IT architect, enterprise IT governance, science and/or political writing, teaching (I have a reputation as an outstanding educator), and naturalist. Samples of my work are provided upon request.
