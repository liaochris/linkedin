Wendy Meyeroff’s Summary

Former journalist who took her expertise in what makes stories exciting and turned it into successful marketing communications. Initially provided only writing, but service has evolved into strategies/consulting.

Started in clinical/technical materials for companies like Pirelli Engineering and 20/20, an optical industry magazine. Quickly found I had a talent for explaining complex health/science issues in plain English.

Specific expertise in developing materials in health care, both clinical and (unlike most health writers) "consumer-speak". Special niches: women's health and "boomers and beyond," the baby boomers AND their oft-forgotten parents, the powerful age 70-plus market.

Wendy Meyeroff’s Specialties:

Web content; articles; newsletters; consumer/patient education; clinical audiences (doctors, nurses, sales reps, et al); PowerPoint presentations; CME/CE (continuing medical education); brochures, and advertorials. See my website (My Company) for more examples (including portfolio pieces) plus a list of topics and clients.

  • Health Care Marketing Wizard

    WM Medical Communications

    (Privately Held; Marketing and Advertising industry)

    January 1988 — Present (22 years 7 months)

    I'm a marketing strategist whose expertise is making old material new, and reaching new audiences before they're recognized as viable. The health care industry is my specialty.

    I took a NY Times advertorial section that was about to fail and, by tweaking its focus, saved that section--and its accompanying revenue. It's just one of many successes.

    If you need a medical marketing strategist, an expert in reaching seniors, and/or expert health writer, I'm the person.

  • Owner

    Wendy J. Meyeroff, Clinical Writer

    (Privately Held; Writing and Editing industry)

    1988 — Present (22 years )

    In the 80s, with no clinical training, I fell into a position where I had to write materials first for pharmacists, and then for the optical industry. In the last two decades, I've had to develop a wide range of materials for people in healthcare: doctors of numerous specialties; nurses and their supervisors; allied health pros, hospital administrators; health information technologists, and many others.

    I've had to cover a wide range of clinical topics and also other issues: sales training, business practices, EHRs (Electronic Health Records), political (the Part D drug plan)...the list continues.

    Initially, my work was strictly articles, for magazines or newsletters. (These included conference coverage assignments.) But my skills were so in demand the work eventually expanded to marketing materials of a wide range. Ask about my sales brochure for Coler-Goldwater Memorial Hospital, for example.

    I was also one of the first medical writers to develop material on the 'Net, posting the first physician/consumer website for one of Janssen Pharmaceutica's products.

    With what beyond your regulatory or journal articles does YOUR company need help? If you don't see what you want directly on my website, use the contact info to request my handout on Clinical Publications.
