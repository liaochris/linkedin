Hannah Suarez’s Summary

Early 2008, I started focusing on digital technologies and online content by working as tech evangelist, as an online coordinator and my own startup, BCI. From mid 2008, I was involved in two start-up entrepreneurial ventures. Late 2008, I interned for an industry association and from early 2009 was accepted in the role of event coordination of various online/interactive industry events until 2010. Early 2009, I further developed the BCI project into a website resource to deliver relevant event information/lists, supported/sponsored various initiatives, profiled upcoming and established creative industries professionals and volunteered in a design conference and a monthly mobile industry event. Mid 2009, I started my media blog called 'Media Suarez' and also further developed the concept behind BCI to take into account Sydney and Melbourne out of positive feedback. Throughout the year I also took part in volunteer roles mainly focused around social media and events.

Early 2010, I start my role in a major event for online marketing while resuming (and also concluding) numerous roles.

I am interested in highly relevant, dynamic, entrepreneurial and challenging roles in areas such as online marketing, digital content/strategy and creative industries. I am interested in being involved in projects, large or small-scale, with a defining impact - whether the impact is on specific regions, specific individuals or on specific industry sectors and groups. My professional goal is to continue pushing and challenging myself to further increase, multiply and grow the impact and reach of whatever project it is that I am involved in.

I also attend numerous professional development events due to my past responsibility as Event Coordinator, current involvement with the committee for AIMIA Queensland, via the Brisbane Creative Industries project and also through speaking engagements.
