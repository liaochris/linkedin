Morgan Woodruff’s Summary

Morgan brings a proven track record in developing and scaling organizations to Blueport Commerce, where he focuses on growth and business development. He is widely recognized for creating successful partnerships with customers, teams, and vendors, guiding the development and launch of emerging products to meet business, financial, competitive and customer demands. A multi-faceted executive, Morgan has a clear comprehensive understanding of the culture, operations, and practices of regional, national, and global software, high-tech, and communications industries.
Prior to Blueport Commerce, Morgan served as U.S. General Manager and Sr. Executive Vice President of Sales and Marketing for Owera, Inc., a software maker for the communications industry with a specialty in Voice over Internet Protocol (VoIP) and router device design. At Owera he was innovative in the conceptualization and tactical execution of successful market and business development campaigns, product and service launches, team development strategies, and partner and customer relationship management programs. Prior to Owera, Morgan served as sales and marketing Vice President for Global IP Sound (GIPS), acquired by Google. While at GIPS, Morgan executed new business contracts driving the company’s market cap from $75M to $250M. Creating a company vision, identifying opportunities, building the organization, and delivering strong revenues and profits within an intensely competitive markets place. Morgan’s life before VoIP included the growth and sale of Optical Disc Equipment Group for the Fairchild Corporation, the Boston-based battery development corporation MATSI, Inc. and luxury goods conglomerate Richemont, owner of such brands as Cartier, IWC, Piaget, Dunhill and Mont Blanc.
