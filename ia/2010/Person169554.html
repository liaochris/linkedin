  • Researcher & Part-time Lecturer

    UCD DSP Research Laboratory

    (Research industry)

    September 2002 — January 2004 (1 year 5 months)

    Research, development and deployment of the HW and SW architecture to realise a Brain Computer Interface (BCI). A BCI system harnesses the electrical signals generated by the brain and recorded non-invasively from the surface of the scalp. It performs advanced signal processing to decipher control commands encoded in the neurophysiological signals that are generated by the user. The project aim was to develop an alternative communication system for severely disabled people. The SW framework was coded predominantly in MATLAB but for the online implementations the C# language (MS Visual Studio) and the .NET framework was utilised to facilitate real time processing and graphics engine support.

  • Researcher

    MIT Media Lab

    (Educational Institution; Research industry)

    January 2003 — June 2003 (6 months)

    I collaborated with Gary McDarby within the MindGames Research Group of MIT Media Lab Europe. As part of my Research Masters, I was researching the methods into detecting and interpreting electrical signals from the human brain via EEG & fMRI. I developed algorithms that facilitated the interpretation and decoding of EEG signals across the Motoral Cortex (C3, CZ, C4) and ultimately allowed an individual to communicate via the imagined movement of the left and right arm. The project called MindBalance, demonstrated this via a computer game involving a tight-rope walker. The game player controlled and maintained the character on the rope by imagining using the hands to push the individual back up when he started to fall.

  • Engineering Contractor

    Lufthansa Airmotive Ireland

    (Aviation & Aerospace industry)

    May 2001 — February 2003 (1 year 10 months)

    Provided consultation on a number of Engineering related projects. One such project involved the design and development of a mechanism for the early detection of a No.4 Bearing Failure within a CFM-56 Airplane engine. Ray developed the A/D hardware (Op-Amp, Filters etc) to take signals from the pressure sensors within the engine to detect imminent failure. Fourier spectral analysis over certain frequency bands yielded the necessary distinguishable pattern.
