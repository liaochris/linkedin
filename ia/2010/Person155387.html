  • Senior Copywriter

    Ogilvy & Mather

    (Marketing and Advertising industry)

    1975 — 1979 (4 years )

    Till today, I apply everything I learned at O&M to my work.
    Those were days when there were no computers. We copywriters hammered out award-winning copy on old beaten typewriters and smoked incessantly at our desks; artworks were created manually (with letters saying goodbye to rubber solution while in transit to the block-maker). As everything had to be perfect, we worked harder, we learned perfection. This was a time of true creativity simply because we couldn’t lean on the crutches of technology.

    I was fortunate to have worked on the agency’s first ‘TV commercial’ – for Doordarshan- which was 2 static slides, for Sumeet Mixer, with a VO. I still see many brands using the same housewife pose with both arms held out. A few years later, I was in O&M NY, working with a film team and totally blown away by the attention to detail and preparations before a shoot.

    In Mumbai, I cruised through a mountain of consumer products in every available media: press, print, film, radio, TV, D/M, outdoor - for Cadbury’s, J&J, Corn Products, Godfrey Phillips, TOMCO, and my favourite - Oberoi Hotel. I had the privilege of learning from greats like Mani Ayer and Suresh Mullick. Even after I left O&M, Suresh invited me for a get-together held for David Ogilvy, and introduced me to him. I have preserved the book David signed for me.

    I acquired all the normal aberrations of a copywriter – the scribbling of headlines on tissues in the Sea Lounge; the tantrums; the snapping of teeth at the AEs. This was the best time of my life.

    Because O&M was home, and I grew up here.

  • Copywriter

    Interpub

    (Marketing and Advertising industry)

    1973 — 1974 (1 year )

    I started writing complete ads and campaigns at Interpub.
    A campaign I created for Glaxo baby food got special mention in Busybee's column. I also did work for Arvind Mills.

    But what I learned most was style, and that's because of my gorgeous MD, Nargis Wadia. She would look at a layout, then scribble a headline on it with a pencil held in her left hand. Then she would transfer the pencil to her right hand and draw the visual. Then she would chuck the layout on the floor, look at it from a distance and say, "That's what the ad should be".

    Pure style.
