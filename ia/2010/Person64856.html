Emily Johnson’s Summary

Emily Johnson, senior associate in APCO’s Sacramento office, is a communications specialist on corporate and public policy issues.

Ms. Johnson manages communications and public affairs programs for corporations, trade associations, non-profit organizations and government agencies. She has worked on a wide range of issues, including healthcare, technology, education and civil justice. Her practice focuses on reputational risk, legislative and regulatory matters.

Prior to joining APCO, Ms. Johnson held various positions at the Pharmaceutical Research and Manufacturers of America (PhRMA), most recently as director of Alliances, Affordability & Access. She managed a number of strategic outreach initiatives on chronic disease prevention, the new Medicare prescription drug benefit and prescription drug advertising. Previously, she worked in PhRMA’s policy department, where she analyzed legislation and wrote policy reports on public health care programs, the economics of the pharmaceutical industry and intellectual property rights.

Ms. Johnson graduated with highest honors from Rutgers College in New Brunswick, N.J., receiving a Bachelor of Science in political science and the administration of justice. She went on to Georgetown University’s Public Policy Institute to earn her Master of Public Policy with a concentration in regulatory and health policy.
