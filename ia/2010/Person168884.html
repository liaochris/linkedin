Randy Coleman’s Summary

I've been a practicing attorney and member of The Florida Bar for 31 years. My principal areas of practice include estate planning, including federal estate tax and gift tax planning, asset protection planning, small business law, probate and trust administration. We also handle guardianships and contested probate and trust matters.

I help my clients plan for the protection of their assets from potential unreasonable creditors, and their loved ones from "predators", creditors and themselves, through effective estate planning and asset protection planning. My goal is to become completely familiar with all of the family and personal financial and legal circumstances that impact your own individual planning, so that together we can develop and implement an estate plan and an asset protection plan that will accomplish your goals and objectives, and effectively deal with your concerns and fears.

We have assisted many clients over the years with special needs trust planning, irrevocable life insurance trusts, and charitable planning, including the design, preparation and implementation of charitable remainder trusts, charitable lead trusts, and private foudations.

Our practice includes the representation of personal representatives and trustees, as well as creditors and beneficiaries in probate and trust administration, wills contests, probate litigation, trust litigation, guardianships, including contested guardianships, elder law, and Medicaid Planning.

We also have substantial experience representing our clients who are small business ownerswith proper legal entity selection from which to conduct their business and professional activities, developing and implementing a business succession plan, with appropriate documents, including shareholder buy-sell agreements, operating agreements for limited liability companies, and partnership agreements for limited partnerships, limited liability limited partnerships, and family limited partnerships.
