Sandy Bigelow’s Summary

Sandy Bigelow leads companies to optimize innovation environments to help them create and develop differentiated products for the food, nutrition, personal care and pharmaceutical markets. To learn more, please visit our site, www.vanguardglobalassociates.com.

• Principal, Vanguard Global Associates helps companies develop nutritonal, personal care, and pharmaceutical products to meet their customers' needs.

• Sandy upgraded the R&D effort for Neways Intl., a $1 billion network marketing firm with over 400 nutritional and personal care products. He led the R&D team for discovery, intellectual property, safety evaluation, product development, scientific and clinical affairs, as well as for the pilot plant and the quality analytical laboratory. Served as chief corporate scientist. Accomplishments included: established Neways’ first Science Advisory Board, commercialized Durian Fusion® nutritional drink, Keratonics® line of hair care products, and True Touch® color cosmetics line.

• He led the regulatory affairs and safety effort at McNeil Nutritionals (a J&J Company) for their $500 million product portfolio: Splenda® low calorie sweetener, Viactiv® calcium chews, Lactaid® dairy products and supplements, and Benecol® spreads.

• As a Senior Scientist at Natural Alternatives, Inc., he quickly developed novel products marketed to the network marketing industry.

• As VP, Research & Development at Larex, Inc., he marketed a novel water-soluble fiber/stabilizer to the food, feed, cosmetic, and biomedical industries.

• Sandy had the regulatory responsibility at the Ross Products Division of Abbott Labs for their $800 million portfolio of Ross products, including Ensure®; Vydalin®; Glucerna®; Selsun Blue® and Murine Eye Drops; and Quantum® pumps.

• Sandy had regulatory responsibility at Pfizer Food Science for food ingredients such as Litesse® and Chy-Max® for the United States, Europe, Asia, and Australia.

Sandy Bigelow’s Specialties:

Sandy is an executive with 20 years of experience in leading global product research and development operations in the food, nutrition, personal care, and pharmaceutical markets. Proven track record of designing and implementing strategies to overcome global, regulatory, and marketing challenges. Successful in defining mutual objectives, building consensus, leveraging teamwork, and developing strategies to open regulatory markets, develop product claims, and create new product opportunities.

  • Toxicology Consultant

    U.S. Environmental Protection Agency

    (Government Agency; 10,001 or more employees; Government Administration industry)

    December 1987 — October 1989 (1 year 11 months)

    Dr. Bigelow was a toxicologist for the Office of Pesticide Programs of the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA). He evaluated the safety of biochemical, microbial, genetically modified, and conventional chemical pesticides used in foods, as well as those with non-food uses. As a result of these evaluations, Dr. Bigelow helped determine the safe level of exposure to pesticides, also assisting the agency with setting tolerances for pesticides in food. In addition, Dr. Bigelow helped perform cancer risk assessments to set safe exposure levels for carcinogenic pesticides, and assisted in developing testing guidelines for conducting metabolism studies required for pesticide registration (e.g., for the Registration Standard for Napropamide, see http://www .epa.gov/pesticides/foia/reviews/l03001.htm).

    Dr. Bigelow worked on projects involving litigation support and regulatory toxicology.   • Research Fellow

    Uniformed Services University of the Health Sciences

    (Government Agency; 10,001 or more employees; Research industry)

    February 1985 — February 1986 (1 year 1 month)

    Dr. Bigelow was a research fellow at the Uniformed Services University of the Health Sciences. He performed laboratory studies investigating the role of calcium in the mechanism of toxicity of chlorinated solvents, including carbon tetrachloride, trichloroethylene, and tetachloroethylene. These studies were performed, in part, to develop an antidote to poisoning from chlorinated solvent exposure. He also taught biochemical toxicology to graduate students.

  • Chemist

    National Institutes of Health

    (Government Agency; 10,001 or more employees; Research industry)

    February 1979 — February 1985 (6 years 1 month)

    During his six years at the National Institutes of Health, Dr. Bigelow investigated certain structure-activity relationships of the Ah receptor (a receptor that mediates the toxicity of the dioxins and many related compounds.) He studied the effects of dioxin of lymphocyte subsets in mice. In addition, he performed several studies involving the use of recombinant DNA technology. He also examined the influence of genetic background in mice on the disposition of ethanol and on the toxicity of many therapeutic drugs such as cimetidine.
