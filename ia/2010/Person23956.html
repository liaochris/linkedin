Bill Nobes’s Summary

Bill began his career in 1986 with Digitech Telecommunications, a NY based computer security firm. He designed encryption software for JP Morgan’s first at-home-banking system and received an award of commendation from the National Bureau of Commerce for his work on that Agency’s data encryption verification system. He also authored a personal encryption product that was sold in retail stores nationwide.

In 1989 Bill founded EB-Tide Multimedia, a design and multi-media company. EB-Tide performed work for MetLife, Apple Bank, Kmart and New York Life among others.

In 1992 Bill was engaged by the New York Times Magazine Group to create and lead an in-house ad/promo department that could meet the needs of its national sales force. His innovative technology and project management solutions drastically reduced both lead times and costs. He also provided seminars on design for junior staff members and helped introduce video and multimedia into the organization’s sales mix.

In 1999 Bill joined Miller Freeman/CMP as a Lead Programmer/Technology Specialist where he re-engineered the company’s nationwide asset archiving system. He also designed and executed training programs, which allowed the company to move certain pre-press operations in-house and realize significant cost savings.

In 2001 Bill founded Twin Oak Group, inc -- a small firm which provides design, application development and technology consulting services to a wide range of companies. Bill’s web-based applications have been used by Atlantic Records, Teen People Magazine, Kraft Foods, and Victoria’s Secret.

As a consultant Bill also serves as Director of Technology for Body Health Resources Corporation, the world’s largest compendium of AIDS/HIV information on the web, and as Chief of Technology for The Agema Group, a financial services firm.

Bill also serves on North Hanover’s Land Use Board and owns-and-operates Victory Farm (www.victoryfarm.org).
