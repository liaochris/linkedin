S.D. James MacPherson’s Summary

I graduated from SJSU in May of 2008 with a B.S.E.E. and started working on an MSEE degree in the fall of 2008. The focus of my master's program has been to study conventional power systems and to research ways to make power systems smarter and more capable.

My thesis was developed during a collaborative project with researchers at the Pacific Northwest National Laboratory in which I developed a model for stochastic contingency reserve activation. desire to tackle renewable energy integration as a power engineer. In addition, I have been pursuing collaborative research in the areas of high penetration wind power integration, smart-charging of electric vehicles, and photovoltaic solar array modeling.

My postgraduate career goals are to work as an electrical engineer for Pacific Gas & Electric and to become a licensed Professional Engineer. I look forward to having a long career in the power industry.

In my spare time I enjoy spending time with family, cooking, and engaging in any outdoor activity or sport (particularly hiking, mountain biking, football, soccer, and baseball). Me gusta tocar el cajón tambien.

    UW EE Department

    (Renewables & Environment industry)

    June 2009 — June 2010 (1 year 1 month)

    This is a collaborative project between UW and Pacific Northwest National Laboratory researchers. My efforts include:

    · Developed a model for forecasting stochastic contingency reserve activation in California ISO and Bonneville Power Administration systems based on NERC and WECC disturbance control performance standards
    · Developed a method for sampling contingency reserve activation model in real-time using energy management system data immediately following a generation capacity outage
    · Validated all methodology with a MATLAB program prototype, and demonstrated consistent, expected program output to Pacific Northwest National Laboratory (PNNL) researchers
    · Collaborated with PNNL researchers to statistically combine the contingency reserve activation model with models of capacity outage, wind forecast error, and load forecast error for use in existing Unit Comittment & Economic Dispatch algorithms

  • Electric Vehicle Power Systems Lead
