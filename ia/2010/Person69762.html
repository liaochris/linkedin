  • Computational Biologist

    Harvard Medical School

    (Educational Institution; Research industry)

    January 2001 — April 2007 (6 years 4 months)

    I worked closely with Fritz Roth on a number of projects in his lab. We collaborated extensively with other groups at Harvard and elsewhere on a number of genomic, proteomic, and network-biology projects. Among these were

    ClusterJudge, NOT another expression-clustering algorithm. Rather, it was a tool for comparing clustering results (using any algorithm) based on their ability to group genes with shared annotation (from the Gene Ontology). (Genome Research 2002)

    SILVER, a tool to assess near-miss mass-spectra. Tandem mass-spec operations can generate spectra that don't quite confidently identify the peptides - they're close to various cutoffs commonly used, but not quite there. In the past, these have required hand-curation by experts. Because of the high-throughput nature of tandem MS, if even a small percentage of spectra are near-misses, the amount of work is prohibitive. SILVER is a web-based tool that uses a machine-learning algorithm to confidently identify many such near-miss spectra, significantly reducing the amount of hand-curation. This work was joint with Josh Elias (now at Stanford) and Steve Gygi. (JAMS 2006)

    I also worked on the Human Interactome (Nature Biotech., 2005), in a large collaboration with Marc Vidal at Dana-Farber, performing computational analysis of large set of protein-protein interactions (human proteins expressed in a yeast two-hybrid system).
