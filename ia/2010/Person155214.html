Nikolas Greenblatt’s Experience

  • Publisher | Creative Director

    Living Proof Magazine

    (Design industry)

    August 2005 — Present (5 years 1 month)

    In 2005, Nikolas Greenblatt birthed the idea for a new magazine that would change the publication world. Living Proof Magazine would combine the superior content, feel, and attributes of a newsstand magazine with the do-it-yourself aesthetic of small 'zines. Rather than just a mere trend report, Nikolas cultivated Living Proof into a basic book of facts, knowledge, stories, and pretty pictures designed to easily slide between the Encyclopedia, Ulysses, and the entire Pantone Library on a book shelf instead of winding up in the plastic casket that is a trash can.

    Artists featured have included Ron English, Phil Frost, Martha Cooper, Kevin Lyons, Steven Harrington, Ellen Stagg, Stash, House Industries, Cope2, Alex Prager, Adam Wallacavage, and many others.

    The magazine can be found on the shelves at Supreme, Alife Rivington Club, Reed Space, Dave's Quality Meat, HUF, Ubiq, St. Alfred, Concepts, and Undftd.

    As a relatively young publication, Living Proof Magazine has seen it's fair share of critical success and acclaim. Across the internet, popular websites like Hypebeast, Booooooom, Rad Collector, FlyLyf, GrooveEffect, and Kanye West's personal blog, have written about Living Proof Magazine, helping to grow the publications readership a very large amount in a very short time with readers from all over the world finding interest in the magazine.

    The Living Proof Magazine Artist Alliance is another initiative Nikolas launched under the Living Proof Magazine umbrella. The Living Proof Magazine Artist Alliance brings together artists and goods to produce what they call "creative products." Ranging from hats to skate decks, the Living Proof Artist Alliance will launch in 2010, and feature hats by Steven Harrington and Marco Zamora as well as skate decks from Ellen Stagg and Curtis Kulig. With very limited edition runs, these "creative products" mirror the appeal of the magazine that I've built up over the last few years.
