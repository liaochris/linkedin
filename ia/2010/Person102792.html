Johnny Kalangis’s Summary

Creative lead with a decade of experience in entertainment acting, writing, directing and producing. As well, have thorough understanding of interactive entertainment, multi-platform and creating highly entertaining non-linear narrative experiences.

My goals are to continue using my experiences in long form filmed entertainment and convergence entertainment (broadcast and interactive) and pushing the form through sophisticated fun gaming and interactive entertainment experiences. I believe that at the heart of all good entertainment is great writing through compelling structure and engaging characters.

I have experience in branching narrative structures and internet storytelling, and have been writer/director on three feature films, countless interactive projects, have written for opinion pieces for magazines, internet and have an essay on 'American Psycho' coming out in a UK anthology of horror film writing by international filmmakers. As well, I have worked as a juror for film festivals and national awards, have spoken at conferences and on round tables on convergences, and recently ran a development lab (Vancouver, 08) connecting mentors - and mentoring - the development of conventional entertainment properties into interactive narrative experiences.

Johnny Kalangis’s Specialties:

Writing, directing, pitching, narrative and creative structuring, creative collaboration, working closely with technical and non-technical creative teams and helping them work together seamlessly.

    Johnny Kalangis is an internationally acclaimed filmmaker, writer, director, interactive media producer and national broadcast executive. He received two Bachelor degrees from the University of Western Ontario, one in Film Studies and the other in English Literature. He attended New York University and Ryerson University for film production and graduated from the CFC New Media Lab’s Interactive Art & Entertainment Programme. His resulting prototype, a digital narrative entitled “My Name is Elliot B. (an interactive mind F***K)”, was shown internationally at RESFEST 2000.
