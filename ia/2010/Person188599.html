Shelley Ryan’s Summary

Shelley Ryan recently launched www.KillerWebinars.com to help people who are stumped about online meetings and web conferencing. If you're just getting started, the options out there can be baffling!

Shelley was with MarketingProfs for over five years, playing Den Mother to thousands of paid subscribers. She programmed, produced and promoted weekly online seminars featuring the biggest names in marketing. The result? More frequent broadcasts, increased participation, and higher ratings -- 65% of the seminars last year earned five stars from the audience.

Shelley still dabbles with web development, graphic design and blogging. If you're still here, you have a sample of her writing skills! (She hates writing about herself in the third person, btw.)

During the entrepreneurial phase of her life, Shelley founded BirdNest.com in 2000 while still running InfoLeap, her first company. She left smarter and better-looking people than her in charge of BirdNest in September 2004. It's actually making money now.

The rest of the story:

Shelley has almost forgotten about her many years of experience in designing and deploying technology for Fortune 500 employers and clients. She founded InfoLeap, LLC in 1997, which supported corporate development teams by defining requirements and implementation tools for software and web applications. InfoLeap's employees provided design and usability expertise to clients such as Chevron, Compaq, and Baylor College of Medicine.

Prior to founding InfoLeap, Shelley held positions at Compaq, BFI (yes, the garbage company), and a PC retailer back when there was such a thing. Throughout her career in big fat corporations, she combined her technical agility with her skills in organization development, business process analysis, team building, and training. No marketing whatsoever.

Shelley has been a speaker at local and national conferences on information design, entrepreneurial tendencies, e-government, and web technologies.

Shelley Ryan’s Specialties:

Now: Online seminar production, copywriting, seat-of-your-pants marketing, customer service management, and bechamel sauce.

Then: Web and software design for usability, information architecture, e-government, training and development.
