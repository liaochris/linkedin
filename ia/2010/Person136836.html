Matt Colvin’s Summary

For Bustin Boards, I oversaw and managed all of the production aspects of our custom board operation including the printing of boards, order fulfillment, shipping and customer service. I helped to organize grass roots and guerilla marketing efforts as well as managed our skate team for a 2 year period.

I am an energetic, passionate person that wears my emotions on my sleeve. I speak blunty but know what tact and respect are. Integrity is an ingrained core value of my life stemming from my 6 years in the greatest Air Force the world has ever known. I love a challenge and am willing to take the extra steps to see things through to full execution. The AF also taught me that giving back is something that not enough people do and I have started the nonprofit aspect of Bustin raising more than $10K in our first two events. I hope to one day chair in a nonprofit organization.

I was a flight commander of my boot camp squad managing 60 new Airman. I have overseen more than 75 personnel in my earliest stages of military training in CA. I won several Squadron and Group level awards for leadership and combat related actions, headed every training class that I attended, received the Distinguished Graduate award in Airman Leadership School and was promoted early on 2 occasions. I am the first and only SrA (Senior Airman, E-4) in the history of the AF to have been upgraded to (MCS) Mission Crew Supervisor on my electronic combat platform. MCS duties include but are not limited to overseeing linguists, acquiring targets, target recognition, coordinating air and ground assets, etc. I was granted a TS/SCI clearance and held this Top Secret Clearance for 5 years. I currently still hold a Secret Clearance upgradable to TS. On 12 March, 2009 I was Honorably Discharged from the USAF.

Overall, I am a highly motivated individual that works well in either leadership roles or as a cog in the wheel to reach the desired result.
