Jennifer McClure’s Summary

Who Am I?
An Executive Recruiter, Executive Coach and "Talent Geek". Translation? A Consultant, Advisor and Partner to businesses and business leaders in the areas of attracting, recruiting, developing and retaining Talent in their organizations.

The Founder/Group Manager of LinkedCincinnati - a networking group of over 12,000 professionals who understand the value of networking and building relationships in order to assist each other personally and/or professionally.

A consistently curious consumer of vast quantities of information in the areas of Recruiting, Human Resources, Talent Development, Employment Branding, Employee Engagement, Company Culture & Social Media/Social Networking.

Contributing writer at Fistful of Talent (www.fistfuloftalent.com) - a leading talent management blog and Chief Blogger at Cincy Recruiter's World (www.cincyrecruiter.com) - where I share resources, information and my views on things that fill my world (see areas of consistently curious consumption above).

A Connector interested in connecting my relationships to people they should know, resources they need and information to make themselves or their businesses better.

A former Human Resources Pro with leadership and executive-level experience in privately held and Fortune 500 companies in the manufacturing, distribution and consumer products industries.

Jennifer McClure’s Specialties:

* Attracting and recruiting leadership talent that is a match in terms of Culture/Chemistry/Character/Competency.

* Coaching business leaders to improve or change business behaviors necessary to grow their careers.

* Speaking/Training in the areas of Recruiting, Networking, Employment Branding & Social Media.

* Consulting services in all areas of People Strategy: Talent Attraction/Acquisition, Recruiting Methods/Strategies, Effective Interviewing, Employment Branding & HR Leadership.

  • Vice President - Human Resources

    The United States Playing Card Company

    (Privately Held; Consumer Goods industry)

    April 2002 — November 2005 (3 years 8 months)

    Responsibilities: HR stategery and stuff.

    * Led all aspects of the people strategy and was a key member of the Executive Team brought in for the purpose of a financial and cultural turnaround that resulted in 25% sales and 56% earnings growth and the subsequent sale of the company to The Jarden Corporation (JAH) in June 2004.

    * Developed and implemented a new Performance Management system and trained managers on coaching and developing Key employees as well as poor performers.

    * Implemented an improved employee benefits program and design that reduced expenses by 17.3% after one year of implementation and an additional a 16.1% after two - during a period of double digit increases in health care costs within the industry.

    * Redesigned the Human Resources function and approach to a "Business Partner" model. Results included increased development of training programs, improved coaching and development and improvement in time to hire.

  • Human Resources Manager

    ADVICS Manufacturing Ohio (formerly SEI Brakes, Inc.)

    (Automotive industry)

    July 1995 — March 2002 (6 years 9 months)

    Responsibilities: Hiring, firing, motivating and inspiring at least one person each and every day.

    * Developed and implemented recruiting strategies to staff the start-up of a second facility/new line of business which increased total employment by 136% in 2 years.

    * Reduced turnover 62% through development and implementation of an in-depth New Hire Orientation and Onboarding Program, improvements in working conditions, and regular feedback and follow up meetings with employee groups to address causes of turnover.

    * Developed and implemented changes to the Employee Suggestion Program that resulted in increased employee participation (to 90%) and a 171% increase in the number of suggestions per employee (to 11.95 per person) annually.

    * Increased the number of Continuous Improvement Teams from 21 to 91 annually through implementation of training, recognition and coaching. Facilitated several cross-functional problem-solving teams including five Ohio Manufacturing Association state finalists, and winners of the 1998 Honda America Supplier Team Award and 1999 Ohio Governor's Award for Team Excellence in Manufacturing.
