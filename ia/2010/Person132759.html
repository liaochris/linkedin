Maril Blanchard’s Summary

“We do not see things as they are, we see them as we are.” ~Anais Nin

Maril Blanchard facilitates transformative learning and development. She helps people shift their experience of themselves and the world, so they can more effectively:

• Identify new possibilities for action and achieve meaningful results
• Navigate complexity, appreciate change, and generate breakthrough solutions
• Expand competence, authenticity, and well-being

Her approach—which emphasizes awareness, presence, resilience, and choice—is collaborative, holistic, and pragmatic.

She partners with individuals and groups interested in making deep structural shifts in their personal and professional lives. She also partners with organizations interested in creating learning and development programs that enable people to do great things.

Maril brings a unique perspective to her work—gathered through her diverse professional, educational, and personal experiences. She has more than 25 years of professional experience in high-tech, general business, and non-profit organizations. Her education includes training in Eastern philosophical traditions, ontological coaching, and the system of Reiki; it also includes living and studying on the Rosebud Sioux Reservation for a month. And, her personal experiences, which include moving every year or two when her father was transferred to a new military base, enable her to be comfortable with change, ambiguity, complexity, and multiple perspectives.

  • Learning & Development

    Google

    (Public Company; GOOG; Internet industry)

    April 2005 — July 2008 (3 years 4 months)

    As an internal Learning & Development Consultant within the Engineering organization, I created, implemented, and managed innovative programs that supported rapid organizational growth and change, while remaining true to Engineering’s unique culture, values, and vision. These programs focused on onboarding, performance management, leader/manager development, compensation planning, and career development.

    A few examples of my work include:

    * Google Engineering Coaching Center: A collaborative network of 36 external and 3 internal coaches who provided coaching services to Googlers within Engineering, HR, Sales, and other functional organizations. The unique design of the program, in concert with its successful implementation, led to its adoption as a corporate-wide program.

    * Manager as Coach: A highly interactive, one-day workshop that introduced Engineering managers to the basics of coaching their direct reports. The workshops consistently received ratings of 8.5 on a 10-point scale.

    * Working the Google Way: A series of three videos that helped orient Nooglers to Engineering’s unique culture, values, and vision. The success of the videos led to their integration into the corporate-wide Noogler Orientation program, as well as other L&D programs.

    * Performance Management Guide: An online document that detailed the philosophy and procedures for Engineering’s slotting, promotion, calibration, and performance review processes. The success of the guide led to its adoption (minus Engineering-specific details) in the corporate-wide performance management training.
