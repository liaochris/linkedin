Tim O'Donoghue’s Summary

Tim is a proven software executive with nearly 20 years of software engineering and software product development experience, gained in a broad range of industries and technical environments: consumer internet, banking / financial services, enterprise software, speech recognition / telecommunications solutions, embedded real-time applications and high-tech R&D.

Tim’s strong technical background is coupled with a commercial approach which enables him to effortlessly bridge the technology and business domains. Tim has a rich experience in business leadership roles (operating boards, management teams, etc.) in both small privately-held and large corporate organisations. He has performed product management roles, in addition to product engineering responsibilities, taking full responsibility for the overall product development lifecycle.

In terms of scale, Tim has led small teams where a ‘hands on’ approach is needed and, at the other end of the spectrum, led geographically distributed teams of up to 450 (US$40MM OPEX) where a greater degree of structure, process and orchestration is needed.

Tim was educated at the University of Leeds in the UK where he obtained both a BSc and PhD in Computer Science, the latter focusing on computational linguistics and statistical natural language processing.

Tim O'Donoghue’s Specialties:

Building and leading high performance software product development teams; product and technical strategy; bringing agility to larger, more mature organizations; adding process and structure to smaller, less mature organizations, enabling them to scale; turning around problematic programmes and teams; continuous improvement and transformational change; bridging the business and technology domains; controlling chaos.

Tim O'Donoghue’s Experience

  • CTO, Alternative Investments

    SunGard Financial Systems

    (Privately Held; Financial Services industry)

    May 2010 — Present (2 months)

    Tim is CTO for SunGard's Alternative Investments business which provides specialized solutions designed specifically to address the hedge funds and asset managers. The solutions (APT, FastVal, Monis, InvesTier, Investran, Monis, VPM) range from front office analysis, trading and portfolio management, and specialist support for private equity transactions; through independent valuation, risk management and daily P&L; to accounting, records and investor reporting.

  • VP Custom Solutions, International Markets

    Yahoo!

    (Public Company; YAHOO; Internet industry)

    April 2009 — March 2010 (1 year )

    Tim is responsible for delivering ‘custom solutions’ products – predominately branded experiences and custom targeting – for Yahoo's advertisers in all global markets outside of North America.

    He has product and engineering teams distributed across 12 locations (in the Americas, EMEA and APAC) and works very closely with Yahoo's Ad Solution Sales teams in all international markets to deliver the solutions that advertisers can't simply buy 'off the shelf'.

    In 2009, Tim's teams delivered nearly 400 solutions which accounted for a significant proportion of Yahoo's brand display advertising revenues internationally.

  • VP Engineering, Europe and Canada

    Yahoo

    (Public Company; YHOO; Internet industry)

    October 2007 — March 2009 (1 year 6 months)

    As VP Engineering, Tim led Yahoo's engineering teams in Europe and Canada and was responsible for all Yahoo’s regional properties, including the various portals (xx.yahoo.com) and media channels (News, Finance, Sports, etc) for all of the European and Canadian markets.

    In addition to all of the regional properties in those markets, Tim was also globally responsible for ‘Yahoo Answers’, the 4th largest property in Yahoo’s global network, which was engineered and operated out of Europe.

    In total, Tim's European and Canadian teams delivered and operated the products for 250MM unique Yahoo users who performed 15BN page views each month.

    In addition to running engineering, Tim was also a member of the management team which runs Yahoo’s European business (one of the [then] four regional businesses which constitute the Yahoo business globally).

  • Head of Development

    Misys Banking Systems

    (Public Company; MSY.L; Computer Software industry)

    August 2006 — September 2007 (1 year 2 months)

    Misys plc is a global supplier of IT solutions to the banking industry.

    As Head of Development, Tim was initially responsible for the J2EE re-engineering of Misys’s retail banking solution, leading a team of 170+ software engineers based in the UK and India.

    Latterly Tim was responsible for the development of BankFusion, Misys's next-generation core banking platform which underpinned future product strategy across all of Misys’s banking product lines.

  • Technical Director

    Talgentra Ltd

    (Privately Held; Information Technology and Services industry)

    August 2004 — July 2006 (2 years )

    Talgentra Ltd was a PE-backed (Alchemy Partners) enterprise software and solutions business with two product lines, Gentrack and Tallyman.

    Tim was responsible for Tallyman, a 'credit and collections management' enterprise solution, looking after both product management and engineering.

    Additionally, Tim was responsible for IT (operations and delivery) and was a member of the management team which ran the Tallyman business.

    Talgentra’s Tallyman business has since acquired by Experian Decision Analytics.

  • Head of Development

    SRC

    (Privately Held; Information Technology and Services industry)

    August 2003 — July 2004 (1 year )

    SRC – the Speech Recognition Company – is a VC-backed (Cazenove and 3i) speech recognition solution provider to the ‘document creation’ and IVR / call centre markets, the latter of which was served by SRC's Telecom subsidiary.

    Tim was responsible for the development of SRC Telecom’s speech application hosting platform and delivering new speech recognition telephony services for automating voice transactions.

    In addition to performing a ‘hands on’ role leading a small team of software engineers and dialogue designers, Tim was also a member of the SRC Telecom management team.

    SRC Telecom was acquired by Fluency Voice Technology, who themselves have subsequently been acquired by Syntellect.

  • Technical Director

    Thales e-Transactions

    (Public Company; Information Technology and Services industry)

    September 2001 — March 2003 (1 year 7 months)

    Thales e-Transactions was a 'point of sale' (POS) payment solutions business which has since been acquired by Hypercom from Thales Group.

    As Technical Director, Tim was responsible for all product development in the UK (~60 hardware and software engineers, focusing on EMV IC card handling to support the launch of “Chip and PIN” in the UK market) and was also a member of the management team which ran the e-Transactions business in the UK.

  • R&D Director

    Canon Research Centre Europe Ltd

    (Information Technology and Services industry)

    October 1991 — August 2001 (9 years 11 months)

    Tim had experience at all levels of the organisation, starting as Research/Software Engineer and culminating as Director, with responsibility for the overall R&D organisation in the UK.
