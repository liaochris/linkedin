Michael Wold’s Summary

Green bags are the solution to many of the world’s pollution problems. There are many problems and dangers associated with the use of paper and plastic bags. They not only are harmful to the environment and its wildlife, but the production of them contributes to global warming. Reusable bags, also known as green bags are a solution to this ever-growing problem. A plastic bag takes 450 years to biodegrade. Approx. 380 billion plastic bags are used in the United States every year. Approx. 100 billion of the 380 billion are plastic shopping bags. An estimated 4,000 barrels of oil a day are used for plastic bag manufacturing. The use of green bags and replacing plastic bags with these green bags are a great way to save the planet. The production of paper bags causes 70% more pollution than plastic. Only 1 to 2% of plastic bags in the USA end up getting recycled. Green bags are a great alternative to these plastic bags. Thousand of marine animals and more than 1 million birds die each year as a result of plastic pollution. An average person will use over 350 plastic bags in a single year. In 1999, more than 14 million trees were cut down to produce 10 billion paper bags that were used by Americans. Over 500,000 bags are used globally every minute. The petroleum in 14 plastic bags could drive a car a mile. Disposable bags cost our cities up to 17 cents per bag for disposal. Paper and plastic bags contribute to global warming. The average family accumulates 60 plastic bags in only four trips to the grocery store. These facts are hard to ignore, and they further emphasize the growing need and importance of green bags in society today. Green bags have many uses including when you go to the grocery store, retail store, gym, and much more.

In every study done, green bags are rated best for the environment. We believe that in five years, over 90% of the country will be using green bags.
