Goals:

-Make AAA games that everyone wants to play!
-Create high quality 2D and 3D art for next-gen games

I am a well rounded artist, skilled and trained in both 3D environment modeling and traditional art. I've worked in the game industry as both positions. I've also recently spent time working for Zynga in the social game industry as a Flash artist as well. As a result, I have experience working in very fast paced work environments with constantly changing deadlines. I think what separates me from other artists is that I can take on any art related task/role (be it 3D or 2D) and perform beyond expectations, while taking feedback in a positive manner. I am highly adaptable and can work easily with teams in different production pipelines. On a personal note, I am a fun and friendly guy. I have a determined spirit and always try to add value to my team. I have a strong passion for video games, movies, and all sports. Overall, I'm excited to see where the console/mobile/social game industries are heading in the next 5 years and happy to be a part of the them.

  • Concept/2D Artist - Frontierville

    Zynga East

    (Privately Held; Computer Games industry)

    January 2010 — December 2010 (1 year )

    I joined Zynga East to work on a new social game from Brian Reynolds called Frontierville. While there, I helped create the face of Frontierville. My contributions included collections, decorations, the loading frame art and animation, male/female avatar assets, a few of the buildings, premium decorations, all meal icons, all boost icons, all holiday avatar costumes, the Gold Rush & Oregon Trail preview screen art, and designed the majority of Frontierville characters (the bear, Frontier Jack, the blonde cowboy, the pioneer, and the two frontier girls). I also created and animated Jack's tutorial dialogue pop-up. The art I made for Frontierville has been used in marketing materials for media reviews/interviews, Zynga East team t-shirts, game cards sold in stores nationwide, on Zynga's website, in Zynga recruiting material, and is displayed in several places within the Zynga HQ building.

    * Q2 & Q3 2010 Zynga MVP Awards

  • 3D Artist - Yoville

    Zynga

    (Computer Games industry)

    February 2009 — January 2010 (1 year )

    I worked as a 3D artist for the Yoville social MMO. I was tasked with modeling low/high poly props & environments, creating UV maps, texturing, creating UI icons, and producing flash animation for character avatars/animated props. While on the team, I learned to work with very tight and rapidly changing deadlines in a social game production environment. SVN was used to manage art assets.

  • Environment Artist - Tiger Woods PGA Tour 2010

    EA

    (Public Company; ERTS; Computer Games industry)

    November 2008 — January 2009 (3 months)

    I worked on Tiger Woods 2010 for the Wii. My contract was for 2-3 months to help the team during alpha. During that time my duties included world population, lowering the resolution of next gen models for use in the Wii build, modifying shadow maps, tree and crowd placement, setting gameplay boundaries, crushing and testing courses in Tess, updating Tess data, fixing texturing/UV issues on terrains, creating vert lighting on course prop models, vert reduction on courses, fixing panos, and setting physics on water. We used Perforce to manage our assets.

  • Concept Artist

    Neopong Software

    (Computer Games industry)

    August 2003 — February 2004 (7 months)

    My job was to come up with character and enemy concepts for a Nintendo DS RPG project and a Sony PSP adventure platform game. I designed concepts for Althea, Pias, the Warthog Chief, and various minions. I also did some battle sprites for the DS project.
