    (Higher Education industry)

    2008 — 2009 (1 year )

    AEJMC's mission is to promote the highest possible standards for journalism and mass communication education, to encourage the widest possible range of communication research, to encourage the implementation of a multi-cultural society in the classroom and curriculum, and to defend and maintain freedom of expression in day-to-day living. AEJMC and ASJMC founded the Journalism and Mass Communication Leadership Institute for Diversity (JLID) program in 2000. JLID was created to provide training and mentoring for people of color and women interested in becoming JMC deans, directors or chairs.

  • Director, Academy of Writing Excellence (AWE)

    Scripps Howard School of Journalism and Communications, Hampton University

    (Educational Institution; Education Management industry)

    2005 — 2008 (3 years )

    AWE: A strictly optional professional development program. Students with a GPA of 3.0 or higher apply with a paper application, a resume, references, a three- to five-page autobiography, an essay about how they live the Hampton University Code of Conduct and they take an entrance exam. Weekly writing sessions with emphasis on accuracy, deadline, short-turnaround assignments, current events, Associated Press style, etc.
