Milind Kulkarni (milindshrikulkarni@gmail.com)’s Summary

Vice President of a diverse research group involved in cross functional research across many critical areas of semiconductor and solar wafer manufacturing, from polysilicon production to semiconductor and solar wafer manufacturing.

2009 to Present: Directing global research on polysilicon production and quantitative research on semiconductor and solar wafer manufacturing. The polysilicon research addresses all areas of polysilicon production, from silane and chlorosilane manufacture and purification to the manufacture of polysilicon in both Fluidized Bed Reactors and Siemens Reactors. The responsibility includes directing research for the development of new processes and plants in the US and Europe, and continuous improvement of the existing processes. The quantitative silicon research spans Czochralski crystal growth, Directional solidification, Wafering, and Defect engineering.

2005 to 2009: Guided quantitative research based developments in the production of polysilicon in fluidized bed reactors (FBRs), of silicon crystals by the Czochralski process, of solar silicon polycrystals by the directional solidification process, and of silicon wafers by wire slicing.

2004 to 2005: Provided direct and indirect quantitative guidance in Crystal R&D, Wafering R&D, and Annealing R&D, and, to some extent, in New Materials R&D and Silicon Epitaxy.

1998 to 2002: Provided intellectual and technical direction and leadership for the production of Czochralski Silicon primarily by applied research on defect dynamics.

1996 to 1998: Was responsible for the applied research and development focusing on the wet etching of silicon wafers.

1992 to 1996: Doctoral thesis with Dr. M. P. Dudukovic: "Dynamics of Asymmetric Fixed-Bed Reactors: Coupling of Exothermic and Endothermic Reactions"

1989 to 1992: Master's thesis with Dr. K. L. Levien: "Modeling a Heat Regenerator-Reactor with Temperature Dependent Gas Properties"

1985 to 1989: Graduate of UDCT (UICT), Mumbai
