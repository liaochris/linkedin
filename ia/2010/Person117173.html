Yohan Kim’s Summary

Currently supporting Critical Infrastructure Protection – Cyber Security (CIP-CS) Program at Department of Homeland Security’s (DHS) National Cyber Security Division (NCSD) by providing quantitative and technical subject matter expertise to the IT Sector Risk Assessment (ITSRA). Authored several chapters, particularly those related to DNS in the ITSRA, released December 2009 and contributed to the National Infrastructure Protection Plan 2009.

Links to publications
http://www.it-scc.org/documents/itscc/IT_Sector_Risk_Assessment_Report_Final.pdf
http://www.dhs.gov/xlibrary/assets/NIPP_Plan.pdf

Helped develop risk quantification methodologies for an internal initiative for the Assurance and Resilience team based on elegant, repeatable algorithms to address broad spectrum, low data quality risks. Assisted in quantifying risks with low data availability, reliability, and/or comparability, where there is an increased need for probabilistic methodologies and stakeholder alignment around synthetic extrapolations.

Supported the Department of Homeland Security (DHS) Office of Risk Management and Analysis (RMA) strategic visioning initiative by developing briefing materials. Authored the appendix summarizing the characteristics of existing DHS risk analysis tools, their applications and RMA’s lexicon.

Supported the General Services Administration (GSA) with cost estimation for their transition from IPv4 to IPv6. Developed cost model used to procure and support budgets and helped to gather data by assisting with interviews and providing modeling expertise.

Reviewed and prepared Systems Engineering Readiness Review (SERR) documentation for Program Management Offices (PMO) within the SATCOM Teleport and Services Program Executive Office (PEO-STS). Levied by the Defense Information Systems Agency (DISA) Engineering Programs, the SERR aims to keep accurate records, help in decision making through use of metrics, and streamline PMO operations.

Yohan Kim’s Specialties:

Mr. Kim possesses over five years of risk management and IT security experience, and has been primarily engaged in delivering risk and security assessments for government, and some commercial organizations. Mr. Kim possesses fundamental knowledge in risk management, cost estimation, business case analysis, organizational assessment, strategic planning, statistical analysis and risk analysis methodology development.
