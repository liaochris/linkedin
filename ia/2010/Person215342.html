Martin Willers’s Summary

As an entrepreneur I apply cross disciplinary knowledge from bachelors in both visual communication and industrial design into business reality.

My design thinking in sustainable business development is a new mindset about changing paradigm in management theory, moving from traditional top-down and quantitative approach to a more bottom-up, qualitative approach in innovation and transformational, sustainable processes. It's a new business development discipline that builds on traditional design skills to address people, planet and profit issues.

This highly creative approach to problem-solving leads to practical and innovative solutions for my clients.

Get in touch by email: martin@peoplepeople.com
Follow me on Twitter: http://twitter.com/designmeaning

Martin Willers’s Specialties:

The business case for Sustainability, Exceptional Visual Storytelling, Business Concept Design, Corporate Identity Strategy, Sustainability, Trend analysis and synthesis, Group Facilitation, Idea Generation, Experience Prototyping, Design Ethnography (working closely with users and helping to translate findings into viable design solutions)

  • Board member

    SSCO - Stockholms Studentkårers Centralorganisation

    (Non-Profit; Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    January 2002 — December 2002 (1 year )

    Stockholm Federation of Student Unions, SSCO, is an organisation for cooperation between student unions in the Stockholm region. Through more than 50 members SSCO represents around 80 000 students. Founded in 1896 the organisation’s original focus was providing services to the students in Stockholm. Since then SSCO has moved towards working as a lobby organisation and today the main focus of SSCO is influencing politicians on the local, regional and national levels.

    SSCO main tasks:
    - To protect and develop Stockholm as the leading region of education in Sweden.
    - To offer meeting places for students and student unions in Stockholm.
    - To provide students with common services.
    - SSCO is responsible for Stiftelsen Stockholms Studentbostäder, SSSB, which owns and administrates 8 000 apartments for students in the Stockholm region.
