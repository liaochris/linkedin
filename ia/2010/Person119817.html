Kuae Kelch Mattox’s Summary

Veteran journalist turned media savvy stay at home mother with a proven track record of achievement in the broadcast and print media. Served as former producer for NBC News, education reporter for The Miami Herald, Editor in Chief of Black Family Today and writer for a wide range of media outlets, including Plum Magazine, The Washington Post, The Philadelphia Inquirer and Electronic Media. A recent personal essay appeared in the book, The Mocha Manual to a Fabulous Pregnancy by Kimberly Seals-Allers (HarperCollins, 2005). Currently co-authors the column "Michelle Obama, Modern Mom" on the popular website Mommytracked.com, a website with a mission to inform and entertain time-crunched moms as they tackle the daily tug of war between work and home.

Non-profit experience includes current post on the National Board of Mocha Moms, Inc., a national organization that supports at-home mothers of color, as national director of media and publicity. Credited with increasing the visibility of Mocha Moms in a wide range of national media outlets by highlighting the stories of numerous mothers of color who have made the choice to modify or eliminate their employment to spend more time raising their children. In 2007, became PTA president at Watchung School, a science and technology magnet recognized as a Blue Ribbon School by the U.S. Department of Education. Also selected as one of 12 high profile women from around the world to serve as a consultant on McDonald’s Global Moms Advisory Panel.

Journalism, writing, editing, television production, marketing and promotion, media and publicity, branding, content development
