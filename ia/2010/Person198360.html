Suzie Rodriguez’s Summary

I started my writing career with journalistic training (MA in Communications/Journalism) followed by two years as a magazine editor. Then I struck out on my own. Since then, my writing has taken two forms: Freelance & Corporate.

As a freelancer I've pursued topics that interest me, including my lifelong fascination with 1920s Paris. Two of my books have revolved around this topic, and I'm not done with it yet.

Another passion, cooking, has led me to write about things culinary (I once had a 20-page article in Gourmet Mag based on my book Found Meals of the Lost Generation). Similarly, a love for travel has resulted in many travel pieces for magazines and newspapers since the mid-1990s.

Early on I did a lot of tech writing, which led to writing profiles of computer-industry bigwigs. From there I branched out (most famous interviewee: Gladys Knight). Profiling experience helped enormously when I wrote a bio published by HarperCollins about a prominent 1920s American expat in Paris.

Corporate work offers a different set of satisfactions, not the least of which are easily-defined goals and (usually) workable deadlines. Corporate work demands that I be inventive, find new approaches, and seek fresh perspective. It's fun to always be learning new skills and techniques.

Much of the corporate writing/editing I do involves newsletters, website content, blogging, Help, press releases, social networking updates, Policy & Procedure guides, advertorials, corporate histories, articles, and editing.

When needed, I can access highly-skilled designers and photographers, as well as specialized writers (e.g., marketing, medical, PR).

Suzie Rodriguez’s Specialties:

Wide-ranging, in-depth knowledge of many topics. Technical expertise. Corporate web content and blogging. Extensive archival research experience (Smithsonian Institute, Jacques Doucet Biblio in Paris, Bancroft Library, etc.) is helpful in writing corporate or family histories.
