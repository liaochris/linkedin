Joseba Abaitua’s Summary

Joseba Abaitua is senior lecturer in the Department of Modern Languages at the Faculty of Arts at the University of Deusto. He holds a degree in Linguistics from the University of the Basque Country, Masters and Ph.D. in Computational Linguistics from the Institute of Science and Technology, University of Manchester (UMIST).

Joseba has participated in several research projects funded by public bodies (EC, MCYT, MEC and GV), in collaboration with private companies (CodeSyntax, Sarenet, Stella) and other university research groups (TALP UPC, UB CLIC, IULA/UPF, IXA of UPV-EHU, UNED). He has presented research results at numerous national and international conferences (SEPLN, JOTRI, ACL, COLING, AMTA, ASLIB, CLIP), and has been member of the scientific committees several times.

Joseba has worked in the development of three machine translation systems: One for four years, Fujitsu R & D Barcelona (Japanese-Spanish), later as project manager for AutomaticTrans (Spanish-Basque), in the feasibility study and preparation of bilingual resources; and more recently for Lucy Software in a Spanish-Basque system. He has acted as a consultant for companies on XML editing (Francis Lefebre) and language technology (AutomaticTrans, Sarenet, CodeSyntax, Bitext and Instituto Cervantes). Has been hired by the DGXIII the European Commission as evaluator of projects (thirty) three times for the Fourth Framework Program (Language and Engineering Reseach), for 5FP (Creating a user-friendly information society (IST) and eContent (European digital content on global networks). He also serves as evaluator for the National Evaluation and Prospective (ANEP) of the Interministerial Commission of Science and Technology (CICYT) of the Spanish Government (eight times) and is member of the committee of experts in Information Technology and Communication for the Department of Culture of the Basque Government.

natural language processing, machine translation, translation technology, corpus linguistics, metadata, taxonomies, ontologies, folksonomies, semantic technology, markup languages, knowledge management, semantic wikis, Wikipedia
