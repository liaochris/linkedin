Ari Strauss’s Summary

Government affairs consultant with broad experience in all aspects of federal and state level government relations. Areas of focus include financial services, energy, transportation, manufacturing, high tech, telecommunications and economic development.

Ari Strauss’s Specialties:

Strategic Consulting, State and Federal Lobbying, Legislative Monitoring and Analysis, Issue Advocacy, Tactical Campaigns (development, implementation and tracking), Education and Communication, Grass Roots Mobilization, Research (primary and secondary research, focus groups, surveys, public opinion polls, issue tracking)

Ari Strauss’s Experience

  • Senior Vice President

    Artemis Strategies, LLC & Chrysalis Energy Partners

    (Privately Held; Government Relations industry)

    October 2007 — Present (3 years 3 months)

    Represent a diverse clientele of Fortune 500 companies, trade associations, county and local governments, and public/private partnerships before the White House, Congress, Governors, and various federal/state departments and agencies. Maintain strong relationships with numerous Democrat and Republican Members of Congress, Administration officials, Governors and state officials. Issue areas of focus include high tech, financial services, energy, telecommunications, manufacturing, healthcare, and trade. Also advise clients on the development and management of corporate PACs and grass roots advocacy campaigns. Clients have included SAP, Genworth Financial, H.J. Heinz Co., NICE Systems, PSEG Energy, Teva Pharmaceutical Industries, Cricket Wireless, the Automotive Aftermarket Industries Association and Oak Ridge Associated Universities.

  • Legislative Director

    Northeast-Midwest Senate Coalition

    (Government Agency; Legislative Office industry)

    October 2005 — September 2007 (2 years )

    Developed legislative agenda and strategies for a bipartisan alliance of 29 Senators from New England, Mid-Atlantic and Midwest states. Specific areas of focus included economic development, manufacturing, transportation, energy, water infrastructure, environmental protection, sustainability and smart growth. Primary responsibilities included drafting legislation, testimony, speeches, and letters for coalition members; providing research and analysis of data on federal spending in the region; evaluating key federal programs and legislative proposals to determine effects on the region; and representing the coalition to outside groups through public speaking, participation in panel discussions and attendance at conferences and annual meetings.

  • Legislative Director

    Congressman Tim Holden (PA-17)

    (Government Administration industry)

    September 1995 — October 2005 (10 years 2 months)

    Ascended from intern to Legislative Director over the course of a decade working for my hometown Congressman. As Legislative Director for five years, served the function of deputy chief of staff and senior policy advisor to the Congressman on all public policy and political issues. Primary responsibilities included managing a four person legislative staff in developing and executing the Congressman’s legislative agenda; coordinating the Congressman’s activities on assigned committees, caucuses and taskforces; drafting legislation, speeches, editorials, press statements and correspondence; and representing the Congressman before constituents, interest groups and various state and federal offices.
