Jordan Michaels’s Summary

When you're days are done and people remember what kind of person you were, you don't often hear them talk about the shrewd business deals that person used to make, or how much money that person used to make. At least... those things are generally not remembered in a good way. Instead, when folks remember what kind of person someone else was in a good way, you generally hear them say things like "He cared about people so much", "He was always willing to help", "He was a great father", etc.

Many people that I talk to are immediately confused when I tell them that my business priority is NOT to make money. Instead, in my business relationships, I thrive on the "win-win" scenarios. Everything I do centers around finding those situations and making them work. When you think about it, we only have a finite amount of time here in this world, and I want to do everything I can to help those around me have a better life. That is my only goal.

The following story illustrates this philosophy pretty well in my opinion:

----------------
The rich industrialist from the north was horrified to find the southern fisherman lying lazily beside his boat, with his hands folded behind his head, admiring the lake surroundings. “Why aren’t you out fishing?” said the industrialist. “Because I have caught enough fish for the day,” said the fisherman.

”Why don’t you catch some more?”

”What would I do with them?”

”You could earn more money,” was the industrialist’s reply. “With that you could buy a new motor so your boat could go into deeper waters and catch more fish. Then you could make enough money to buy nylon nets. These would bring you more fish and more money. Soon you would have enough money to own two boats ... maybe even a fleet of boats. Then you would be a rich man like me.”

”What would I do then?” asked the fisherman.

”Then you could really enjoy life.”

”What do you think I am doing right now?”
