  • Business manager/Sales/Estimator

    Stark Mantel & Tile Company

    (Construction industry)

    May 1973 — December 2005 (32 years 8 months)

    The company was founded in 1913. Individuals within the company provided commercial, industrial and residential customers with furnished and installed porcelain floor and wall tile, terrazzo flooring, and acid-proof brick flooring.

    Porcelain tile was used in all applications mentioned above including health clubs, hospitals, assisted and independent living facilities, shopping centers, restaurants, food processing plants, private and public housing and other facilities. Most porcelain tile installations were located in southeastern Wisconsin and northern Illinois.

    Terrazzo flooring was used primarily in public facilities such as hospitals, shopping centers and office buildings. Terrazzo is a poured flooring system with usually polished marble chips as the aggregate and cement as the matrix. However, an epoxy resin can be substituted for cement as a matrix. The product originated in Italy. Most terrazzo installations the company was involved in were located in southeastern Wisconsin. The installation of this product requires highly skilled professionals. Today, it is rare to find terrazzo specified in projects locally. This industry can be found in the largest major metropolitan areas of the country.

    Acid-proof brick flooring systems are usually installed in food processing plants such as cheese and milk facilities, breweries and meat plants. Since the 1980's, much of this industry has moved to the western part of the United States such as California and Colorado. Large installations of thid type took place in the midwestern part of the United States until the about 2002 when the economy brought this growth to a halt in most regions of the country. Stark Mantel & Tile Company had installed this flooring system in California, Colorado, New Mexico, Michigan, Indiana, Illinois, Iowa, Minnesota and Wisconsin. This is a very specialized type of installation and requires highly skilled installers.
