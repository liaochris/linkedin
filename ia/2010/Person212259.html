Vinnie Ferrando’s Experience

  • Chief System Engineer

    Harris Corporation

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; HRS; Defense & Space industry)

    December 1986 — Present (23 years 9 months)

    Vincent “Vinnie” Ferrando has more than 30 years of engineering experience featuring ever increasing responsibilities and growth. He is a highly experienced Chief Systems Engineer (14+ years) with an excellent track record of successful major system developments and challenging software projects. In his role of Advanced Programs Engineer he pursues new Civil Business Unit initiatives. This is a critical position, assigned to only a select few Harris engineers since they must interact frequently and effectively with a diverse set of potential customers to understand their needs. His major technology and customer focus areas include Mission Enterprise and Ground Systems, Digital Archiving, Content Management, eCommerce, commercial railway systems, FAA air traffic control/ voice switch, network and radio data-link communications, system architectures/ service oriented architectures, and real-time systems applications.

  • VP, Technology Development

    USBid

    (Internet industry)

    February 2000 — April 2002 (2 years 3 months)

    As Vice President/Technology Development, joined USBid.com shortly after $10M initial venture
    capital funding in February, 2000. Planned, staffed, and led 30 web, database, and software development personnel in a $1.5M effort, launching a fully functional internet B2B electronics component exchange in 3.5 months. Achieved $7M in sales with 20% margin in FY '00. Equity position in an Internet B2B startup. Developed USBid.com as a 24 x7 global electronics component eCommerce business utilizing four commerce models with full integrated logistics and fulfillment capability. Performed the marketing, sales, and customer service to launch ,maintain, and enhance the USBid.com web features and integration with suppliers / buyers while simultaneously developing the internal engineering systems and structures to execute the business. Full P&L / schedule responsibility for over 30 personnel. Managed the USBid controlled operational web-site hardware/software and all IT servers and desktops.

  • Chief Engineer

    GE Harris

    (Information Technology and Services industry)

    July 1995 — February 2000 (4 years 8 months)

    As Chief Engineer, joined GE Harris as one of the original 45 members of a General Electric and Harris Corporation joint venture founded in July, 1995. Planned, staffed, and led 75 personnel in hardware, software, and engineering over a 3 ½ year period executing the $24M Positive Train Separation program for Burlington Northern Santa Fe and Union Pacific railroads meeting all four ship release dates and achieving a profit of $4.5M. As Chief Engineer, Railway Systems Development - developed and executed Internal Operating Plan for Department of 134 personnel with an $85M orders backlog exceeding sales of $30.4M and Contribution Margin of $5.5M. Patent Committee member organizing engineering review of quarterly patent filings from competitors, assisting in the filing of provisional and final patents. Lead GE Harris-TCS Off-shore Tiger Team in the formation of an Off-shore Development Center in Pune, India. Obtained Six Sigma Black Belt and ISO 9000 Internal Auditor Certification.

  • Senior Principal Engineer

    Harris Corporation

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; HRS; Defense & Space industry)

    January 1994 — July 1995 (1 year 7 months)

    Appointed by the GM and VP/VSCS Programs to Product Line Manager with the responsibility to grow the voice switch business. In this effort I started and delivered on 02/95 a $4.2M IR&D to build the next generation terminal radar approach control and tower voice switch based on VSCS technology in anticipation of a competition to award 465 systems as part of the $100M Enhanced Terminal Voice Switch (ETVS) procurement by the FAA. At completion of assignment, given my track record with Voice Switching and Control System (VSCS) and my previous commercial background, I was recruited by VP/Engineering and GM into a new business area in the commercial transportation market with the charter to develop a new commercial market for Harris Corporation through execution of PTS and follow-on sales to BN and UP railroad customers.

  • Deputy System Manager

    Harris Corporation

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; HRS; Defense & Space industry)

    December 1986 — December 1993 (7 years 1 month)

    As Deputy System Manager at Harris Corporation engaged in a $192M Voice Switching and Control System prototype design competition against the 30 year incumbent AT&T to develop the next generation en-route air traffic control voice switch for the Federal Aviation Administration. As a member of the VSCS design, implementation, and test team I was promoted to increasing levels of responsibility. First, as Software Development and System Engineering department head of 17 with a budget of $1.7M year. Then, as Deputy System Manager, managing 134 personnel in multiple engineering disciplines in the final Prototype VSCS test competition. Harris Corporation won the production competition with a $1.667 billion contract. Simultaneously, as Engineering Manager, managed a System Engineering and System Effectiveness (RMA, EMI/EMC, Human Factors, Simulation) directorate of 52 reporting to VP/Chief Scientist.
