Jennifer Fallon’s Summary

In 2003, Jennifer Fallon discovered an untapped market, gained first-hand knowledge of the Internet's staggering power, and made the leap from sales and marketing to flourishing entrepreneur.

Realizing there was a niche for beautifully packaged wedding favors, Jennifer launched her own site on Yahoo! and watched as sales rocketed to the $1 million dollar level the first year. As sales grew she realized that uninspiring designs, excessive price points and high industry minimums were her roadblocks. She knew she could do better.

Her entrepreneurial spirit again took hold as she decided to manufacture her own line of favors and gifts. She quickly located two partners who gave her insight into the sourcing she lacked and helped her build the IT component this new venture required.

In 2004, Kate Aspen was born and the industry took note. Retailers fell in love with the beautiful gift-ready designs, the no minimums policy, and the drop shipping model Jennifer introduced. Two years later she added bridal party gifts and baby shower favors.

The birth of twins in 2006 motivated Jennifer to expand into the design and manufacture of boutique-style baby gifts under a new Baby Aspen brand. Today The Aspen Brands is a multi-million dollar company encompassing both Kate and Baby Aspen. The company sells to both online and brick and mortar retail trade across the globe.

It was natural for Kate Aspen to direct its charitable focus to women's health issues when it launched the Pink Ribbon Collection in 2008 to initiate the company's In Favor of a Cure campaign. Proceeds from the sale of this collection are donated to Young Survival Coalition.

Recipient of the prestigious 2009 Ernst & Young regional Entrepreneur of the Year award among others, Jennifer and her products have been featured in The Wall Street Journal, Entrepreneur, Modern Bride, and Brides magazines.
