Brian Olson’s Summary

My news career started out in Cheyenne, WY at KGWN-TV with stops after in Texas, Nevada and Arizona as my career progressed.

Major events covered include Superbowl XXX, The 1996 Republican National Convention and the OK Bomb Trial. I served four elected terms to the board of directors of the Radio Television News Directors Association.

Military reporting includes numerous deployments on land, sea and air including embarks aboard the Aircraft Carriers Kennedy, Constellation and Ranger, the Nuclear Attack Submarine USS Gurnard and Guided Missile Cruiser USS Valley Forge. Most memorable of all, a flight with the USAF Thunderbirds. (Thunderbird 8)

But being in the media is like being in the military. You move around far too much. I moved to corporate life to simply stay in one place. Or so I thought!

At Echostar I was on the launch teams of the Echostar V & VI Spacecraft, acted as corporate spokesperson and "voice" of DISH Network, member of the Team Summit Core Team and Echostar-Hughes Merger team. My experience included building the studio video production dept. producing 180 events annually. My team worked with program partners including HBO, Turner Broadcasting and ESPN.

As Vice-President of Public Affairs at Video Professor, Inc. I was responsible for all media and government relations involving traditional and social media.

In May of 2010, I co-founded a new firm. "Conversation Starters, LLC" which is a media and public relations consulting company.

Community Service: Board of Directors, Jefferson County Action Center, volunteer at the Wings over the Rockies Museum and regular blood donor at Bonfils Blood Centers.

Ultimately it's all about results. I'm a strong strategic thinker and planner, (Nothing like the Air War College to help you out with that!) I seek out and exploit opportunity.

I'm "old school" but comfortable working in the new age of communications.

Bottom line: I put integrity over the dollar every time.
