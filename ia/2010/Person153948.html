Nick Firestone’s Summary

Whether you are new to the sport of kart racing or have been doing this forever, FIRESTONEKARTINFO (FKI) is for you. FKI will span the gamut of karting from tips on how to successfully buy your first kart (and not someone else’s problem) to advanced setup, maintenance, testing and driving techniques to help make you faster regardless of your experience. If you are newcomer to karting, the article on buying a kart alone will more than pay for itself by saving you time, money, and aggravation beyond the measly fee. If you’ve been doing this forever, I guarantee you will pick something up that will move you up the grid or at least make your experience more organized and enjoyable. I have been doing this for 16 years and still learn something every year.

Nick Firestone’s Experience

  • Owner

    Firestone Kart Info (Self-employed)

    (Self-Employed; Sports industry)

    July 2010 — Present (5 months)

    I have been racing cars all over the world since 1984 bringing a wealth of experience to FIRESTONEKARTINFO. My background includes Off-Road racing, Opel Lotus Euroseries, British Formula 3000, Indy Lights, and the IRL. I was one of two drivers selected to test Firestone’s new Indy Lights oval tire in 1993 (based on having the fastest race lap in his debut in an Indy Lights car). I was also the first driver to test Reynard’s Formula 3000 sequential gearbox at Silverstone, England in 1992.
    I have had the privilege of racing in two series that supported both F1 (Opel Lotus Euroseries) and IndyCar (Indy Lights). Consequently, I have raced on world famous tracks such as Spa, Long Beach, Nurburgring, Laguna Seca, Silverstone, Barcelona, Mid Ohio, Imola, Hockenheim, Magny Cours, as well as part of the famed Monaco circuit in the 2007 Monaco Kart Cup.

    Perhaps most importantly, I am a current kart racer who does 95% of his own tuning. I continue to apply the testing and development techniques I learned during years of professional racing to karting. The result: 10 wins, 10 Poles, 19 Podiums, and 11 Fastest Laps on 10 different tracks in 25 starts in the ProKart Challenge G1 division, none of which are my hometown track. I won the one and only SKUSA Winternationals G1 Championship in 2008 and have participated in two SuperNats, reaching the podium (5th) at the 2007 SuperNats from a 12th place starting position.

    I kart almost every week to test and improve my kart and provide a constant stream of information and material for this blog/newsletter. If you subscribe to the newsletter, become a faster, better karter in any way, and stay in the sport as long as you can, then I have accomplished my goal. Trust me when I tell you, the pleasure is all mine!
