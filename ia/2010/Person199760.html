Tara Ariano’s Summary

Critically acclaimed writer, editor, and entrepreneur. Extensive experience working online and in print, both taking direction from editors and managing staff. Eager to bring proven talent at creating addictive pop-cultural commentary to new outlets.

Tara Ariano’s Specialties:

Recruiting and developing freelance talent, managing online communities, creating addictive pop-culture commentary

Tara Ariano’s Experience

  • Managing Editor

  • Editor-in-Chief

    Television Without Pity at Bravo

    (Public Company; GE; Entertainment industry)

    March 2007 — March 2008 (1 year 1 month)

    - Continued helming Television Without Pity after its acquisition by Bravo.

    - In a year, wrote over a hundred recaps and weecaps for TWoP, helping to make it one of the internet’s most indispensable destinations for humorous TV analysis.

    - Expanded content offerings on TWoP by developing and creating content for a site blog, which also provided an attractive new platform for site advertisers.

    - Developed the format and created humorous scripts for TWoP video/multimedia content.

    - Produced more than a hundred items for the “TWoPlopedia,” a humorous site featurette that was also the basis for TWoP’s first Facebook widget.

    - Mastered and taught a new content management system.

    - Developed enticing promotional materials for TWoP for use by the ad sales and marketing teams.

    - Managed the challenging transition of TWoP into Bravo without losing a single freelance contractor.

  • Co-Creator/Co-Editor

    Television Without Pity

    (Partnership; 1-10 employees; Internet industry)

    September 1999 — March 2007 (7 years 7 months)

    - One of the top five TV websites on the internet, generating 70 million pageviews per month from 1.5 million unique users.

    - Co-created one of the internet’s busiest – and most widely praised – networks of online message boards, with a six-figure membership.

    - Created top-selling designs and concepts for TWoP- and FT-related merchandise, including apparel, buttons, and paper goods, opening up steady new revenue streams.

    - Edited hundreds of pages of content each week, with exceptional speed and accuracy.

    - Recruited, developed, and managed a staff of both on-site writer/editors, and several dozen remote freelance contributors.

    - Sold in a seven-figure deal to NBC Universal’s Bravo TV in 2007, in Bravo’s first-ever online acquisition.
