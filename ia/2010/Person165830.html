Preeti Sriratana’s Summary

Mr. Sriratana is an Architecture Consultant whose private practice is based in New York City. His professional experiences include tenure at the New York architecture firm Skidmore, Owings & Merrill, LLP, where he was a Senior Designer specialized in transportation and education facilities. While there he oversaw the designs for the NY Metropolitan Transit Authority station at Ground Zero and the New York Jets Training Facility in New Jersey as well as award winning proposals for the United Nations High School and Little Red School House competitions, both located in Manhattan. His own works include residential and commercial projects that have been published nationally and internationally and have been exhibited in galleries such as the I Space in Chicago, the Van Alen Institute in Manhattan, and Gund Hall at Harvard University. Mr. Sriratana also served as a visiting critic in the architecture studios of several universities and is currently a Roving Critic for Columbia University’s Graduate School of Architecture, Planning and Preservation. He started his career working in Paris for Pritzker Prize winning architect, Jean Nouvel.

In his free time, Mr. Sriratana is a youth mentor at APEX, a nonprofit dedicated to promoting the development of Asian American youth living in poverty. Currently he serves on the organization’s Board of Directors as Co-Chairman. He is also a member of the Advisory Board for the Manhattan-based urban arts initiative, The Sound of Art.

Mr. Sriratana earned a Bachelor of Science in Architectural Studies from the University of Illinois, at Urbana-Champaign, and a Master of Architecture from Columbia University in New York.
