Frank Short, M.A.’s Summary

Father Tim Dodd, one of my clients, said this about me: “If writing résumés, LinkedIn profiles and other career communications were Olympic sports, Frank would have won multiple gold medals for his work.”

I work with my clients in the capacity of a personal career branding and promotion agency. For more than 20 years, I have written and designed résumés and career promotion materials for print and electronic media on a full-time basis. My clients range from professionals and managers to directors and chief executives. They are located from coast to coast, with some living as far away as Europe, the Middle East and Hong Kong.

Previously, I was a copywriter and creative director with leading advertising agencies, including Leo Burnett Company and DDB Worldwide. I have written for accounts such as General Motors, Kellogg's and Nestle. That is the reason behind my ability to put so sharp a creative and persuasive edge on personal branding communications. In turn, that is why I am able to make the work and accomplishments of my clients stand out from the crowd.

But the real reason for my success is that I love to help promote individuals. I gain a deep sense of personal satisfaction by helping professionals and executives. I enjoy helping my clients get where they want to go in their careers, especially in these dynamic and complex times.

In addition, I have served as a presenter for organizations such as the Chicago Public Library, the American Society for Training and Development, the Public Relations Society of America, and Women for Hire, as well as a guest on broadcast media. I also taught English at Harold Washington College—one of the City Colleges of Chicago.

I earned a Master of Arts degree in English from DePaul University in Chicago and a bachelor’s degree in communication and English from Northwestern University. Moreover, I completed a 10-course certificate in writing at Northwestern that approximates a second master's degree.
