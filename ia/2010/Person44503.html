John Crenshaw’s Summary

During the past fifteen years, I have worked in three industries, the majority of it spent in a medical informaiton technology environment. First, out of college, I worked in sports marketing as a sales and marketing manager for three different minor league baseball teams. I then switched to publishing, as a sales and marketing associate with Random House, Inc. My territory consisted two specialty markets; religious book stores, suppliers and distributors; and online/internet book selling websites. In 1998 with Random House, I was awarded the Bud Fairbanks Award for outstanding excellence in sales and marketing for my efforts with these unique markets. My interest in technology started at Random House, where I was involved in projects to mesh Random House book content into websites in a variety of ways. This exposure led me to my third and current industry, information technology. I then worked for Johns Hopkins University as a Network Analyst supporting the PACS system for the Radiology department. Hopkins led me to an even greater challenge with Fuji Film Medical Systems as a Customer Solutions Engineer where I work today.
