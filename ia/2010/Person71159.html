C. Frank Igwé, PhD’s Summary

In recent years my research has focused on how, and why, historically underserved groups appropriate information and communication technologies (ICT). More specifically, my research examined how African Americans are socially shaping technology to create virtual “third place” communities as a means to create new social support systems for discussing, preventing, and coping with health crises, such as HIV/AIDS. Included in this is the examination of the performance of racial and ethnic identities in virtual communities, ICT education and workforce participation in the African Diaspora, and the influence of racial, class and gender identities on health information seeking and content creation.

My work regarding health and information technology has been funded in part by the National Science Foundation, and has received national attention, with Op-Ed’s in “The Christian Science Monitor”, “Yahoo! News”, and a piece in the “Chronicle of Higher Education”. Other publications, regarding health and IT, have been featured in the “Journal of Computer Mediated Communication”, and “Encyclopedia of Information Science and Technology”, among others. I have also had several conference presentations, including the “Black Policy Conference” at Harvard University, the “i-Conference” at UCLA, and the “Future Forum”, at Penn State University.

C. Frank Igwé, PhD’s Experience

  • Executive Director

    City ACES

    (Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    January 2009 — Present (1 year 2 months)

    "Professional Athletic Figures, Inspiring the Youth, Transforming Communities, and Shaping the Future through Powerful Motivational Speech and Action"

    City ACES is a non-profit organization committed to working with Middle-School aged youth residing in underperforming school districts, and distressed communities, across the United States. City ACES seeks to instill a sense of purpose and esteem within these children, and aid them in realizing their full potential through education. City ACES approach is to “harvest” local, exemplary, and recognizable professional athletic figures (NBA, NFL, MLB, NCAA, etc.) to engage in a short term commitments at schools within their communities, speaking on the virtues of educational attainment, the role education played in their life journey, and instilling the need to treat education as Plan A, with all subsequent career paths being predicated on a well educated individual.

    Frank Igwe and City ACES have been featured on ABC, NBC, CBS, Fox, USA Today, Black Entertainment Television (BET), The Philadelphia Inquirer, The Harvard Crimson, New York Times, NPR, Yahoo, Foxsports, The Grio, Christian Science Monitor, The Chronicle of Higher Education, and ESPN, among others.
