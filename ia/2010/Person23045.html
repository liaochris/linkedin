Beverly Alexander’s Summary

Bev Alexander has been involved in air quality and clean energy for 30 years. Bev graduated from UC Santa Cruz with highest honors in Environmental Studies and received her JD from UC Berkeley, where she was Editor-in-Chief of the Ecology Law Quarterly. She spent a year on the Ninth Circuit Court of Appeals before joining McCutchen Doyle Brown & Enersen, where she pioneered “first of their kind” environmental cases.

She joined PG&E in 1992, and created California’s first emissions averaging program for power plants, providing the highest air quality protection at the lowest cost to customers. At 38, the National Law Journal recognized her as one of the top 40 attorneys under forty in the US for her ground breaking legal work.

From law she went into business, working in generation, transmission, distribution, and customer services. In 1999, she became PG&E’s Vice President in charge of the nation’s largest utility energy efficiency, demand response, and solar programs. Her organization also included business customer services, customer research, and rates, with an annual budget of $300 million and staff of 700.

During the CA energy crisis, Bev partnered with the Governor’s office, non-profit organizations, and other utilities on what the Natural Resources Defense Council has called the “most successful conservation campaign in history.”

In 2006, Bev led an executive level strategic planning team on how to integrate renewables, demand response, and plug-in vehicles into a "smart" electric grid. Throughout her career Bev also specialized in business leadership development.

In 2006, Bev formed Exergy Consulting and continued her work on clean energy projects. In 2009, she became the Co-Director of the Cleantech to Market Program, Haas School of Business, UC Berkeley. That program brings together graduate students and scientists to commercialize clean energy technologies.
