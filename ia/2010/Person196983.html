Sue Gardner’s Summary

I am Executive Director of the Wikimedia Foundation, the non-profit organization behind Wikipedia - the world's largest and most popular encyclopedia, which is free to use and free of advertising. Wikipedia contains more than 15 million volunteer-authored articles in 250+ languages, and is visited by more than 371 million people every month, making it the number five most-popular website in the world.

Since joining the Wikimedia Foundation in summer of 2007, I have more than tripled revenues, supported an 85% increase in global readership, and launched projects and activities designed to grow readership and attract new editors. Today, the Wikimedia Foundation is recognized by Charity Navigator as the fastest-growing non-profit in the U.S., as measured by year-over-year increase in donations.

Before Wikimedia, I was head of CBC.CA, the website for the Canadian Broadcasting Corporation, one of Canada's most prominent and best-loved cultural institutions. Under my leadership there, CBC.CA won many international awards for excellence, and grew to become Canada's most popular news site.

I started my career as a journalist in 1990 as a producer with CBC's “As It Happens,” an internationally-recognized groundbreaking news and current events radio program. I've worked in radio, television, newspapers, magazines and online.
