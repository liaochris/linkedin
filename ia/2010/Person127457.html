Lorena Colin’s Summary

Served as the National Coordinator for the Mexican-American Coalition for Immigration Reform from 2009 to 2010, organizing different groups of Mexican leaders throughout the United States in order to perform actions that would lead to achieve the reform of the current U.S. immigration system that affects more than 12 million people and their families.
Attorney at Law licensed to practice in all 32 states of Mexico, specialized in Constitutional and Corporate Law and holder of several licenses for the State of Florida such as Real Estate Agent, Mortgage Broker and Notary Public.
Originally from Guadalajara Mexico, moved to the United States on July of 2004 and specialized in Real Estate and financing advisory which allowed me to help minorities (Hispanics) in reaching their home ownership dreams. Due to my involvement, support and work ethics, I proudly became one of the most respected and recognized leaders among the Mexican & Hispanic community in the Tampa Bay Area. This lead to my election as an advisor for the Institute of Mexicans Abroad (IME) 2009-2011 serving as a bridge between the Mexican community and the Mexican Government with the purpose of raising the quality of life for the Mexicans living abroad. Secretary of the Political Matters Commission at IME.
I have the honor of participating as a board Member for the Hillsborough County Sheriff’s Hispanic Advisory Committee (SHAC).
I have co-authored two legal books published in Mexico and published numerous articles in the U.S. related to finances, real estate, immigration and politics.
