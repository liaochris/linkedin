Don Rickert’s Summary

I have over 20 years' experience as thought-leader, consultant and manager of the "people side" of invention, new products (online interactive as well as physical goods), eCommerce (Online Retail, large and niche, Online Banking, etc.), Customer Relationship Management (CRM) and internal operations (often called “back room” systems), particularly in the Customer Support, Financial and Supply Chain/Shipping Logistics domains.

SOME PROFESSIONAL HIGHLIGHTS:

Professional Member of the Industrial Designers Society of America (IDSA) and an occasional Adjunct Faculty Member in the Industrial Design Program at Georgia Tech, where I teach NPD (New Product Development), Research Methods and business-focused Ethnography. I am invited every year to serve as a Referee for the Annual Usability Professions’ Association (UPA) Student Paper Competition, and have also judged design competition submissions for SIGCHI (ACM Special Interest Group for Computer-Human Interaction), the IDSA (Industrial Designers Society of America), the PDMA (Product Development Management Association) and the Art Institutes.

I have an earned Doctorate in Information Systems, with a specialization in usability, user experience and human factors, with post-Doctoral training in ethnographic research.

FIRST-HAND EXPERIENCE WITH ESTABLISHMENT AND OWNERSHIP OF ONLINE RETAIL OPERATIONS:

I have established, own and manage several online stores specializing in several types of niche products: musical instruments (www.AdventurouosMuseStore.com) , fine art (www.JonWhiddon.com), folk art, unique crafts and ceramics by well-known artists (www.AdventureandWhimsy.com). I have developed considerable knowledge and expertise in the art and science of “being found” on the web and the optimal use of social networks to generate store traffic.

Don Rickert’s Specialties:

Strategic “big picture” skills; Inventiveness; Extensive experience in consulting; Working cooperatively with other teams; Qualitative & quantitative research (Usabiility, Marketing Research, Observational Methods, etc.)—wide variety of products and services; Ethnography; Group facilitation; Ability to summarize research findings as they relate to business goals; Consumer psychology; Communication, persuasion, problem solving, leadership, verbal, presentation and organizational skills.
