Ken Hsieh’s Summary

I have a Ph.D. in theoretical physics, with 8 journal publications. With 5 years (2 post-doctoral) of quantitative research, I have a tremendous technical skill set and research experience ranging from the abstract (ex: pure mathematics, quantum field theory) to the concrete (ex: software development, differential equations, statistics).

More than simply being able to perform calculations or develop programs proficiently, I am able to quickly identify the underlying principles and key tasks from the "big picture". I am an effective researcher and problem-solver with a focus on long-term goals and solutions and implementing effective strategies to achieve them.

My academic and research background is complemented by experiences in large-scale software development in an industrial environment. I have participated in all phases of the development cycle: design, implementation, test and debug, and release. I have advanced understanding in C++ and Mathematica, and also a working knowledge in many other programming/scripting tools.

In leadership roles, I have guided graduate students through long-term research projects, lectured in large classes (200+ students), and led small discussion/laboratory sessions. While individually productive (I have a single-author publication and drafted the majority of my published works), I understand that a team, with the proper leadership, is greater than the sum of its parts. I understand my roles in different teams, and strive to make the greatest individual contributions and bring the best of my teammates.

I grew up in *both* US and Taiwan and I am fluent in all aspects of English and Chinese.
