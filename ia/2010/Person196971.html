Sue Dawley’s Summary

My first Honors Attorney rotation placed me in Irvine, CA at the FDIC's West Coast Temporary Satellite Office. For the most part, I have been working with the Resolutions and Receivership section preparing for bank closings, participating in bank closings, and working on post-closing legal issues.

Here's a fact you might not know. The FDIC does not order banks closed. In fact, the order to close a financial institution issued by its chartering authority which could be either the state, the Office of the Comptroller of the Currency (OCC), or the Office of Thrift Supervision (OTS). The FDIC is appointed as Receiver for the failed bank by the chartering authority at the time it is closed. As Receiver, the FDIC winds-up the failed bank's affairs.
