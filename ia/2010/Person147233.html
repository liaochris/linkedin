Michael Iver’s Summary

Michael provides the unique combination of cross-market derivatives strategy expertise and software product development, management and marketing.

He is actively considering new opportunities as either:
1) derivatives strategist, for sell-side or buy-side, or
2) derivatives product manager.

His background in equity, high-yield, convertibles sales, along with derivatives structuring and portfolio management provides a platform to identify relative-value relationships. Having started his career as a CPA, he combines fundamental and quantitative market analysis, needed to identify opportunities.

Over the past few years, he has leveraged his markets background as a product manager for software companies, though he is deeply rooted in the markets. When you meet Michael, you will see he is clearly not an "IT guy", though his analytical creativity has led to many innovations.

At Bloomberg, he led the product development, management, and marketing for equity derivatives globally. These markets covered listed & OTC derivatives, structured products and convertibles. His leadership led to many innovations; a Merton model to identify relative-value between CDS and equities, CDS-calibration for convertible bonds, variance swap term structure, implied borrow-cost from options, option-implied forward volatilities, flat correlations for structured products, volatility surface analysis, term structure of skew, among many more. These innovations are available on 300 thousand terminals globally.

He provides cross-functional leadership needed to reach consensus among quants, research analysts, and salespeople around a shared vision. His client skills earn him credibility needed for productive relationships.

Idea-generating published articles are available upon request.

In the interim, Michael has been developing what will become the first real-time iPhone application for options market-data and analytics.
