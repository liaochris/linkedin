Kim Stone’s Summary

My experience as a one-woman marketing team has always required the ability to design and maintain the company website. Over the years, I’ve come to prefer this part of the marketing role the most. So, I am currently transitioning from a more generalist marketing career to a new, more specialized career in web development / e-marketing.

In total, I’ve redesigned or built around two dozen websites for various organizations or individuals. I have excellent proficiency with graphic design software, Adobe Creative Suite (Photoshop, Illustrator, Acrobat, & InDesign), and web design software, Macromedia Dreamweaver & Fireworks. I have extensive experience working with a variety of customer relationship management databases (i.e. Salesforce, Highrise, ACT) and content management systems (i.e. WordPress, Drupal, Joomla, Squarespace) and know HTML & CSS very well.

In an effort to strengthen my programming skills, I'll be starting evening courses that will culminate in an Associates of Applied Sciences in Computer Information Systems, Web Developer. This more practical training will complement my Bachelor of Arts in Sustainability and Design perfectly for a web developer position at a green company.

My most recent web development project was a redesign of my current employer’s website at http://www.EcofuturesBuilding.com. Working in collaborative environment where I served as project leader, we integrated social media and multimedia features, simplified information flow, improved search engine optimization (SEO), and fully re-branded the company. In just a few months since the new site was launched we’ve seen a dramatic increase in the quantity and quality of leads, not to mention a renewed community and media interest in the Ecofutures’ work building zero energy homes.
