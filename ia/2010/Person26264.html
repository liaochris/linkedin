  • Commercial Director | Sales Marketing | Reading Football Club | Madejski Stadium

    Reading Football Club and Madejski Stadium

    (Sports industry)

    January 1998 — November 2006 (8 years 11 months)

    "Portakabin to Premier League Kid" is how I have been christened by the football industry. (People older than me!)

    I worked with Sir John Madejski in creating business excellence at his leisure venue. Do things the right way and the right things will happen. The journey was a fantastic one. In 2007, after 8 years and a sold-out Premier League venue, I knew things could not get better so I left to help other companies.

    At a football club you have the chance to mix with thousands of business people, hundreds of thousands of customers and hundreds of staff. One of the key highlights was The Business Club, a networking club for regional business owners, run for the benefit of those owners via the club. It was there I decided to focus on small businesses and how I could help them most.

    Whilst running a leisure complex, hotel, stadium, conference centre - you name it, I was involved, some of the key moments were;

    Getting Elton John and Red Hot Chilis to play at Madejski.

    Inventing the double-header Tetley's Bitter Cup semi-final back to back matches.

    Securing World Cup Rugby League in Berkshire.

    Building the fan base from 6000 to 80,000 at Reading FC

    Hosting international sporting events at Madejski

    Creating a family club, that was accessible to fans and put their interests at the core of everything that was done. And creating a team of people that lived that belief.

    The John Madejski story is not finished. And I wait to see what the future has in store for him and his ventures. For me, another adventure is round every corner with the business owners I work with.

    With a bit of luck maybe I'll help to create another John Madejski!

  • Business Manager

    EMAP PLC

    (Public Company; EMA; Publishing industry)

    January 1993 — December 1997 (5 years )

    Doubling revenues and circulations was fun.

    A bit of a highlight was working with editorial and design geniuses before, during and post Euro 96 when a few of us took a kids football magazine to a stratospheric 250,000 a week peak. It seemed every 10 year old in the country was reading our baby.

    Amazing to think we had HP Match beans and burgers and Wall's Match lollies selling in the millions. (Where are the football lollies now? Oh yes, the football lolly now is with the agents.....)

    Just having the conversations about intellectual property rights with Manchester United and Everton were worth the entrance price alone, ( Everton is a part of Liverpool so we're printing the stickers!)

    And I'll never forget the freelancing I did for Wotsits and the £1277 I was paid (which resulted in a 17% uplift - I didn't really understand royalties back then!)

    Happy days and great experience. Hello to anyone that was there and part of it.
