Michael Ossareh’s Summary

I've been working in technology for just shy of 10 years now! Pretty much self taught. I've had the opportunity to work with wildly smart people and my natural want to learn everything I can, assimilating all information available and putting it to use as quickly a possible has resulted in people often thinking I'm a great person to work with.

Starting my own company, with previous co-workers, out in the bay area really changed my perception of "work". For many years I thought I was an entrepreneur - now I know I am! I tend towards being a social bell weather in a company; I bring with me good energy and a can do attitude. I strive to put computing closer to the user of the system, social services which are more than "just database updates" are my bread and butter - I'm extremely enthusiastic about moving large quantities of data around... I just like it!

Technology wise I'm an accomplished developer - the language I use tends to reflect the task at hand. I'm most familiar with Java, though I pick up languages very quickly. I've recently started to transition to a more functional programming world; erlang, javascript and clojure are languages that I'm coding my spare time projects in.

    Good Technology

    (Privately Held; Wireless industry)

    May 2009 — July 2010 (1 year 3 months)

    Good Technology acquired heysan in the spring of 2009.

    Thus far I have merged the heysan technology stack into the overall offering from Good. Their IM offering comprises of a JavaScript based web service engine hosted on Rhino which their Morpheus mobile client communicates with. The web service engine manages the users IM session communicating with the heysan IM stack through an IM proxy-protocol similar to Jabber but slimmed down. I implemented the protocol in Java and the IM session handling in JavaScript. This product is currently being sold into op-co's and OEMs such as Vodafone, T-Mobile, Dell and LG.

I am the lead developer of the client portion.

    Naturally whenever many companies are brought together there are a lot of rough edges when melding the different cultures. I am heavily involved in an internal company process of improving employee productivity through, pioneering better server architecture, better employee hardware, better development processes and talent acquisition.
