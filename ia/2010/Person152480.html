Nancy Byerly Jones’s Summary

Helping clients build success stories that last! As a former practicing attorney and adjunct professor of law, it is my privilege to serve law firms, businesses and individuals. My client work covers many areas and includes helping them define what success means for them whether that be: meeting financial goals; successfully mapping out and transitioning through career changes; resolving disputes fairly and effectively; strengthening employee relations; enhancing leadership skills; simplifying strategic planning; designing a more effective marketing plan; reducing stress; maximizing quality and minimizing risks; and so much more!

I was hired as the initial Vice President of Risk Management for NC Lawyers Professional Liability to establish their RM program in 1990. After a few years getting their program up and running successfully, I was invited in 1993 to establish a similar, but much broader member service for the NC State Bar (served as State Bar's Executive Director of the Lawyers' Management Assistance Program and Management Counsel. In 1997, the requests from law firms across NC for longer periods of consulting assistance were growing (at the State Bar we had an 18 month backlog of attorneys waiting for the service and we could only offer 1/2 day on-site/firm)....so in July 1997 I began Nancy Byerly Jones & Associates, Inc. devoted to assisting law firms on site, via video and phone conferencing and training/seminar presentations. Today, I continue to serve the North American legal community as well as other businesses and individuals.

I write frequently on law practice management and leadership topics including columns since 2002 for Lawyers USA. In 1999, the ABA LPM published my book which is still being sold, "Easy Self-Audits for the Busy Law Office." All clients are provided with updated versions of the self-audits and other "tools" from my '99 book.

Hobbies: Sharing our small farm with horses, miniature donkeys, dogs, and 1 tough cat!
