Eric Fredericks’s Summary

Eric Fredericks is an urban planner who specializes in making communities more enjoyable places to live, work, and play. Mr. Fredericks is an experienced public speaker on livability issues in several cities across the United States. He has also served as a television panelist, workshop facilitator, and a presenter on walkable neighborhoods at conferences. He has worked extensively with federal, state, regional, and local levels of government. Mr. Fredericks is the Founder of Walkable Neighborhoods, a website that promotes pedestrian-oriented neighborhoods through various forms of media. He is also an avid photographer, and has compiled an online gallery of thousands of photo examples of walkable and not-so-walkable places.

A sampling of the dozens of places where Mr. Fredericks's work and talents have been featured include CNBC, The Sacramento Bee, Costco, The Washington City Paper, Treehugger, Brownfield Magazine, The University of California Berkeley and Davis campuses, and Santa Clara University.

smart growth advocacy, bicycle and pedestrian design, high-speed rail, transit-oriented development, public sector and private sector planning expertise
