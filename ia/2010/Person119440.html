Kristof Schreurs’s Experience

  • Intern

    Nike EMEA

    (Public Company; NIKE; Sporting Goods industry)

    August 2009 — May 2010 (10 months)

    Master thesis "Lean management in a logistics environment: Overall Equipment Effectiveness at Nike ELC Footwear"

    Within Nike’s Footwear Additional Capacity & Enhancements project, I was responsible for increasing Footwear’s receiving throughput by focusing on its bottleneck, i.e. the process of automatic palletizing at the Inbound area.

    In order to reach the estimated peak demand by the summer of 2010, the goal of the business case was to increase Footwear’s receiving throughput by 33%. By grasping the current state of automatic palletizing through observations and measurements, I managed to reveal hidden capacity losses. Points of focus were 5S, Total Productive Maintenance, Overall Equipment Effectiveness and linking these results to the theory of Factory Physics.

    After presenting my findings to a cross-functional team of 20 members within Nike Footwear, I then worked full time at Nike doing further analysis. The results of my master thesis subsequently served as input for a Kaizen-week led by a North-American lean consulting firm. During this week, we learned about Kaizen as an approach to continuously make improvements. Finally, I presented further insights, conclusions and recommendations to Nike Footwear’s management.
