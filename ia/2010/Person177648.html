Rosaleen Doherty’s Summary

My path has never felt conventional, but it has certainly been fun. I was educated in the arts and ended up owning my own company in healthcare. The road between one to the other taught me many things. In light of the current situation in the United States, I think it has prepared me for many unforeseen events. After my college education, I took a telecommunications sales job just to make a little money. I found that I was a good salesperson, if not a conventional one. My customers always had a good open relationship with me because I always looked for a win-win environment to do business. This attitude took me from reselling for New England Telephone to working for alternative phone companies just before the Internet. In subsequent years, I was involved with network infrastructure with MFS, WorldCom, Level 3 Communications, HarvardNet and Primus Telecommunications. The Internet bust taught me to look at companies and business with a sharper eye and brought me a conviction that if someone makes up "valuation" based on nothing concrete- it is probably worth nothing. I think we are seeing that in our US economic problems today.

Because of our experience in the Internet, Jay Kenney, my husband and partner and I opened Right at Home in 2002. This has given us and our family the honor of working with other families to provide a service that truly changes people's lives. Bringing supportive services into the homes of elders helps the people who have helped us all. My experience owning a family business has been one of the most valuable in my life and I hope to pass my experiences to my son and daughter as they start to look at the world and business.
