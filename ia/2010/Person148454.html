Monica Hertzman’s Summary

Since 2000 I have worked on RAND's many Web sites and have gained experience in usability, design, and coding (including development of stylesheets, JavaScripts, surveys, SQL databases and ASP and PHP sites).

Prior to RAND, my expertise lay in publishing, editing, and project management, a combination that led to my being hired on to RAND's nascent Web team.

RAND's Web team (now the Web Communications Group) has more than doubled in size since I joined, in conjunction with the recognition of its value and its place within the corporation's Office of External Affairs. Following an organizational realignment in the summer of 2006, I was promoted to the position of Intranet Manager. In that position I managed a small budget that covered the labor for one Web specialist and a Web designer. Most Intranet projects are conducted in conjunction with our IT group; some are short-term but most are long-term capital projects (search improvement, research database development, etc.). Content-related projects also exist; while long-term in nature, they are also incremental.

In April 2009 the WCG -- then 15 people -- was reorganized to include a Web Services team and a Corporate Outreach team. I was promoted to manage Web Services and now oversee five Web Producers who advise and support RAND's research units, centers, and projects, as well as our Intranet.
