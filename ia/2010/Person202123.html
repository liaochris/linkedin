adam j. wilson’s Summary

The first half of my career was spent making TV spots and print ads - movies and posters, as I fondly recall. I thought the internet people were weird and uptight. And loved a good craft services table (still do). But the luster of a career that my Mom could actually understand eventually wore off. I wasn’t feeling inventive enough.

Luckily, I knew a few of those internet-makers. And seven years ago, they thought they could use a guy like me. As my Mom understands it, I now “work with computers to make internet commercials.” Works for me. Because in this second chapter of my career, I’ve had the opportunity to be inventive and speculative, fusing the decades-old art of brand storytelling with the spastic new world of technology, real-time syndication and consumer empowerment. I’ve had a Lewis & Clark-ish experience with the convergence of creative and technology, which has afforded me and the talented people I’ve worked with, the chance to create industry firsts, like:

The first digitally crowd-sourced, published comic book, "The Patriot Factor"; a partnership between Jeep® and MARVEL comics

The first interactive and customizable online film, "Jeep Patriot and the Way Beyond Trail"

After accomplishing these and other firsts, and working the agency “integration” beat along the way, I simply do not subscribe to the digital vs. traditional, um…thing. Sure, it makes for interesting Ad Age articles, digital still shows up as separate line item on most marketing budgets, and tough media planning choices have to be made. But from a creative point of view – creating is creating. I think the more important questions are: How strategic is a creative? How media and technology savvy is a creative? And does that creative still know how to move someone with a good story?

I’m still a hybrid work-in-progress. But will humbly submit a ‘yes’ to all of the above.
