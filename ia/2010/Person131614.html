Marc Normandin’s Summary

Currently a postdoctoral fellow at Yale University School of Medicine using medical imaging techniques to non-invasively study physiological processes in health and disease. General interests and applications include the development of novel data analysis and processing methods, quantitative characterization of new imaging tracers and targets, and pre-clinical assessment of candidate pharmaceutical therapies.

Marc Normandin’s Specialties:

Specialization in mathematical modeling and interpretation of dynamic (i.e., time-varying) medical images to investigate physiological function (e.g., memory, motor control, reward processing) and dysfunction (e.g., depression, diabetes, substance abuse and addiction) and other biological phenomena by creative application of signal processing, experiment design, and parameter estimation techniques.
