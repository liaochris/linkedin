Mark Joyella’s Summary

Mark Joyella is an Emmy-winning reporter and writer, and the editor of LocalNewser, a blog dedicated to tracking the evolution underway in local television.

Mark's covered stories across the country and around the world, and teaches news writing and reporting at mediabistro.com.

Mark began his career at KJCT-TV in Grand Junction, Colorado, and moved on to stations in Savannah, Birmingham, Miami, and New York City, where Mark reported and anchored at FOX owned-and-operated station WNYW. In NYC, Mark also earned bylines in the city’s legendary New York Post.

Along the way, Mark has reported as hurricanes made landfall, American troops deployed to the Persian Gulf, and at the scenes of major events in the United States, the Caribbean, and throughout Central and South America.

Mark's writing and reporting have been honored with five regional Emmy Awards. He’s been honored twice by the Associated Press, and his live reporting during Hurricane Andrew earned him a Spot News award from the Radio-Television News Directors' Association.

Mark lives in New York, where aside from his daily writing duties, he freelances for the New York Post and produces and directs documentary films.
