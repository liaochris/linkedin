Sonya Hamlin’s Summary

ABOUT SONYA HAMLIN

TELEVISION: Sonya Hamlin is a two-time Emmy Award winner for her TV hosting and filmmaking and a member of the Massachusetts Broadcasters Hall of Fame. Her groundbreaking daily talk show in Boston was on for eleven years.

As a communications expert, she appears regularly on network television, analyzing communication issues in business, politics, current trials and media trends. Known for her wit and surprising techniques for audience involvement, she is much in demand as a speaker and television contributor.

BUSINESS: She conducts seminars and consults privately with CEOs and senior executives in such corporations as American Express, Bayer Corp., Bristol- Myers Squibb, Citigroup, CIGNA, DuPont, Ernst & Young, GE, IBM, Monsanto, MTV, Nickelodeon, SONY, the Commonwealth of Massachusetts and the U.S. Govt.

Sonya's best selling books, the latest one being
"How to Talk So People Listen: Connecting in Today's Workplace (HarperCollins) are published worldwide.

TEACHING: Sonya has taught communication skills at the Harvard Law School, Kennedy School of Government; Boston University’s Medical School and School of Communication; the Wharton School, Sloan School of Mgt.at MIT; University of Texas and NYU law schools; Brandeis International Business School as well as at Oxford University and the Judge Business School at Cambridge University.

TRIAL ADVOCACY: Ms. Hamlin is an acclaimed pioneer in the field of courtroom communication, having created that first such course at the Harvard Law School. She lectures worldwide and works on case with law firms, consulting on juror attitudes and prejudices, on advocacy skills, preparing witnesses, developing strategies and creating visual presentations of evidence. She teaches advocacy skills-both oral and visual-across the U.S. and abroad. Her landmark book, "What Makes Juries Listen Today" is now a classic, and Now What Makes Juries Listen" is new in 2008.
