Michelle Laque Johnson’s Summary

Michelle Johnson has more than 20 years of journalism and communications experience, as a reporter and editor for a metropolidan daily and several national publications, as Editor-in-Chief of an 85,000 circulation newspaper, and as Director of Communications for the largest religious media network in the world.
She is an experienced speaker and corporate trainer, has given workshops for journalists at national and regional press conventions on how to use social media to expand a publication's outreach and increase circulation, and has trained journalists on how to become an expert in their niche for a national publisher.
Michelle is also skilled at using social media to promote organizational goals and create communities, and is the founder of the Facebook group, Social Media for Catholics.

Michelle Laque Johnson’s Specialties:

Writing (especially taking very complicated material and making it not only understandable but enjoyable), editing, social media marketing, speaking, training, networking, marketing and promotion.

Michelle Laque Johnson’s Experience

  • Director of Communications

    EWTN Global Catholic Network

    (Non-Profit; Public Relations and Communications industry)

    March 2008 — Present (2 years 6 months)

    Package story ideas, photos and graphics about EWTN, the largest religious media network in the world, and pitch them to national and international media, both secular and Catholic. Lead efforts to reach out to viewers and potential viewers via social media, including Facebook, Twitter, and LinkedIn, and develop campaigns to increase EWTN’s fans and followers. Conceive of and develop content for mini-websites to drive traffic to EWTN’s main website. Develop promotional opportunities and host promotional events to increase press coverage and foster good relationships with the press.

  • Editor-in-Chief

    The Catholic Standard & Times

    (Newspapers industry)

    March 2002 — October 2007 (5 years 8 months)

    Transformed a sleepy diocesan paper into an award-winning newspaper covering social, cultural, lifestyle and religious issues of interest to my audience. Overall responsibility for editorial content, graphic design, advertising, circulation and business. Under my direction, the paper, though religious, received numerous awards and was named “Overall Best Weekly” by the secular Philadelphia Press Association for the first time in its history. Our special sections included a quarterly magazine for teens, which covered, among other things, the paper’s first teen forum for high school juniors on dating and morality and a wildly popular modest design fashion contest. Wrote an award-winning weekly column, was frequently a featured guest on the radio, and often spoke at large community events. Recipient of the 2004 Neumann Award by the prestigious Catholic Philopatrian Literary Institute, which is given “to honor an exemplary Catholic woman who has made an outstanding contribution to family, church, profession or country and whose courage and integrity are unquestionable.”

  • Managing Editor

    LRP Publications

    (Privately Held; Publishing industry)

    June 1992 — February 2002 (9 years 9 months)

    At LRP, the Managing Editor is the top position on a publication. Responsible for innovations and improvements to editorial content, which made my publication number one in an extremely competitive market. Worked closely with marketing, production and

    focus groups. Conducted internal workshops and seminars for journalists at company locations in West Palm Beach, Fla., Washington, D.C., and Horsham, Pa., on topics such as “Top 10 Interviewing Mistakes” and “How To Become an Expert – While Never Missing a Deadline.” These workshops were videotaped and are still being used for training purposes. At the invitation of subscribers, I also spoke around the country on media and business topics such as how to get press coverage and time management.
