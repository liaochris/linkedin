Neil Saunders’s Summary

I’m a passionate insight-driven strategic planner and consultant, with deep experience developing and evolving strategic models for global organizations to improve impact, performance and drive business growth.

I’m dedicated to the creation of customer and shareholder value through the development of meaningful brand relationships.

I’ve successfully held executive management positions at leading creative agencies in London (Grey), Los Angeles (BBDO), San Francisco (Citron Haligman Bedecarre) and Chicago (Leo Burnett); having started out as an ad hoc research manager at Unilever.

My brand experience is extensive and includes leading and managing planning teams on Kellogg’s, Glaxo Smith Kline, P&G Health and Beauty Care, Apple, Sony, Pioneer Electronics, Canon Cameras, Wachovia Bank, Dreyfus Financial Services, Lee Apparel Europe, Virgin, Blue Cross of California, Dreyer’s/Edy’s Premium Ice Cream, Taunton Cider Co., Becks Beer, The Good Guys!, Macy's West, Odwalla among many others. And lots of New Business.

My strategies have created communication and business ideas that have won Effie’s and some of the most prestigious creative awards in the world.

I believe branding is a social not a physical science and that peoples’ behavior is driven by experience rather than understanding. That “there are no truths, only stories” (Carl Jung).

Respected within the advertising community, I’ve served as a Board Member of the AAAA Planning Committee and the APG.US; I have spoken at a number of industry conferences, presented to the graduates of the VCU Adcenter and serve as a long-standing instructor at the Miami Ad School.

Neil Saunders’s Specialties:

Brand renovation and growing core business; New approaches to behavior modification; Growing business through archetypal brand strategies and storytelling; Innovative qualitative and quantitative research methodologies; Creative brand idea and company culture workshops; Portfolio management; Socio-cultural trend analysis; marketing to men; Collaboratively challenging thinking geared to the status-quo
