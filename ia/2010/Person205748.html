Tomas Palermo’s Summary

Tomas Palermo is a writer, editor, DJ, producer, music consultant and coach living in San Francisco, California.

Tomas started his writing career as columnist at LA’s URB Magazine in the early-90s and became Editor at SF’s XLR8R Magazine in 1999 through 2005. He contributes to XLR8R, SF Weekly, Big Up and other publications. He also maintains the ForwardEver blog is former Managing Editor at WiretapMag.org.

Tomas has organized custom playlists for iTunes, written liner notes for Six Degrees and Quango Music and has promoted and performed at hundreds of music events.

He runs the Voltage Music record label, DJs at SF clubs and on KUSF 90.3 FM. Tomas has coached high school cross-country and track runners at Sacred Heart Cathedral Prep for seven years.

Tomas is seeking part time writing, editing, music and teaching positions.

Professional associations: ASCAP, USATF, NCTE.

  • Owner/A&R/Artist

    Voltage Music Label

    (Public Company; NWS; Internet industry)

    January 2000 — Present (11 years )

    Voltage Music label -- the label co-founded in 2001 by DJ Tomas (a.k.a. Double Identity and Dub I.D.) and XLR8R Publisher Andrew Smith (a.k.a. Professor Smith and Jackie’s Army) – recently re-launch as a digital imprint with a new compilation CD and digital singles.

    Co-founder and now sole-owner DJ Tomas says: “We had to retool the label for the digital age, but we’re squared-away now and have some amazing projects on deck.” One of the label’s first tasks was to re-issue on CD a compendium of the label’s many remixed tracks.

    Available now through all major digital music retailers is Dub For The Dancefloor: Voltage Remixed & Unreleased -- 12 songs not available on CD or download until now. Tracks include rare vinyl-only remixes by Domu, Kode 9, Polar, Titonton Duvante, Tino Corp and DJ Abstract plus two unreleased new songs.

    Previously the label had issued ten singles on vinyl only including five 10” singles and five 12” singles. Voltage singles showcased music by West Coast dub, broken beat, electro and dubstep producers Double Identity, Professor Smith, Deep Alpha and Douglas Pagan.

    In addition to the upcoming compilation that’s slated for release in October, Voltage Music has its 11th catalog single in the works featuring tracks by underground Toronto dub producer Earlyw~rm, with remixes by Kush Arora and Babylon System. It’s a dub-meets-dubstep extravaganza that typifies the label’s commitment to meshing traditional dub reggae with cutting-edge club genres.

    “For me, dub is the foundation of what we do,” says DJ Tomas. “But we love the whole spectrum of sound system culture too, so our aim is to present new takes on dub’s dancefloor off-shoots.”
