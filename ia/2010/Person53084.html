Dean Wagner’s Summary

In my years of software development, I most enjoyed spending my time identifying and correcting problems, whether those problems were bugs, performance issues, or problems with code design or maintainability. I always believed that if it was wrong, it should be changed. Much of my learning came from searching for better ways of doing something.

Many of these same desires also drive me as a manager. I don't believe that any one person can come up with all the right answers, and like to listen to ideas and suggestions until the group can come up with the answer that feels right. I believe that the right solution is the best solution, regardless of whether or not it's the one used in the past.

I'm constantly keeping up with blogs and articles to find out what others are doing. If it's not what I'm doing, I want to learn why they're doing it and whether or not it's something I should be doing. I'm currently most interested in developing for an SOA architecture and learning the benefits of the newest of the asp.net technologies. I'm also interested in different aspects of Agile development, what challenges were caused by using agile development methodologies and how those challenges were solved.
