Meghan Paul’s Summary

After graduating from Vanderbilt in 2005, I had brief stints at Duke Law and on Capital Hill, before coming to grips with the fact that adversarial climates weren't for me. Nevertheless, I ended up in New York city, the place I now call home. I quickly joined forces with the esteemed Dr. Leslie Gaines-Ross at global public relations firm Weber Shandwick, where I helped develop the newly formed reputation research department across three main functions: creating a host of new client offerings; building the firm’s thought leadership platform; and optimizing the firm's international corporate communications division’s capabilities. It was my first taste of the extreme fulfillment that comes from building something of value out of nothing. Full of new-found confidence in my skill set, I left Weber Shandwick to travel the world while recording it all in my journal, which I already find so endearingly antiquated.

I knew my step would be something big, and something meaningful. With the economy crumbling around me, I immediately recognized the power of Solvate's model to not only revolutionize the way people delegate, but also provide America's most talented freelancers with meaningful work during an uncertain time. I now serve as the Timesmith liaison, matching specialists with projects that meet their passions, so that Solvate clients can experience the same increase in efficiency and productivity that I have.
