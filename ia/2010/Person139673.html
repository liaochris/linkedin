Meakin Armstrong’s Summary

I'm a former editor and writer of special sections for The New Yorker magazine. I’ve been a group managing editor at Forbes, Inc., an editor of a celebrity-oriented glossy, a research editor, associate editor, and a fact-checker. Esquire specially cited my work at Guernica magazine, where I am the fiction editor. The fiction pieces I’ve published at Guernica have won many awards, including Africa’s top literary prize. I’ve contributed to five books, three of which are forthcoming in 2010. The New York Times praised my work as a contributor to a travel anthology.

I have been writing professionally since 1993, predominately on travel, books, movies, culture, and entertainment. I’ve written numerous features, profiles, blog pieces, and advertorials.

I’ve been awarded various writing fellowships—most notably to the Bread Loaf Writers’ Conference and to Summer Literary Seminars, in Russia. In the late 1990s, I had a script optioned by Columbia Pictures.

I can mark-up in HTML, I'm skilled at SEO, and am familiar with Moveable Type and WordPress. My online work frequently appears on TheAtlantic.com, TheAlanticWire.com, and GuernicaMag.com. My print work has been published in Time Out New York, Maxim, Good Housekeeping, Four Seasons, and elsewhere.

Aside from writing editorial pieces that cover travel, books, movies, culture, and entertainment, I'm available to write, edit, and consult on social media, web content, digitally based ads, and advertorials

Clients include: The Atlantic, Zezza Network, LitSeek.com, the Christopher Ross Collection, Condé Nast Traveler, and The New Yorker.

I'm also a fiction writer--I’ve been published in many journals and nominated for several awards. I work as a manuscript editor for fiction, too.

As an Adjunct Professor, I teach English composition; I enjoy finding new ways to teach students the fundamentals of the English language.

More here: http://www.meakinarmstrong.com
