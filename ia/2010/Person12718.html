Andy Nicholas’s Summary

Technical director for post production in commercials, film, and computer games development. A strong technical background of maths and physics, expert in-depth knowledge of 3D software, and a good eye for visuals. Experienced in undertaking a wide range of tasks, from the technical aspects ( pipeline design, tool & plugin programming ) to production ( 3D generalist, VFX TD, compositing ).

You can view my website at www.andynicholas.com

Andy Nicholas’s Specialties:

3D Generalist and VFX TD (XSI, Houdini, some Maya ), Compositing (Fusion, Shake), Tools Programming, Physics Programming, Pipeline Programming.

Andy Nicholas’s Experience

  • Senior TD

    The Mill

    (Privately Held; Motion Pictures and Film industry)

    November 2007 — Present (2 years 4 months)

    Initially worked as a 3D generalist in the commercials team before becoming a fulltime member of staff specialising in particles, rigid body and fluid simulations using a combination of XSI, Houdini, and Realflow. Other work has included tool writing, pipeline support (for some of the larger jobs) and technical rigging.

    In 2009 I started to expand my role by providing in-house training, leading teams on projects, VFX supervision, and generally becoming more client facing.

  • VFX TD and Compositor

    Axis Animation

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Animation industry)

    October 2007 — November 2007 (2 months)

    A freelance position creating spot effects (sparks, breaking glass, etc) by creating particle and rigid body simulations in Houdini. I also worked with Fusion compositing some of the shots and doing look development for camera lens effects.

  • VFX and SFX Diploma

    National Film & Television School

    (Educational Institution; Motion Pictures and Film industry)

    October 2006 — October 2007 (1 year 1 month)

    This course has let me apply my experience to learning the skills required for producing VFX in film and television. I have also used this time to boost my experience with using software such as Maya and Houdini.

    I acted as VFX supervisor for an ambitious 6 day shoot for a live action film that was later combined with 2D animation. This involved supervision of the shoot, recording camera and lighting data, organising green screen backings, as well as the preparation, building, and execution of various physical gags and elements. I also undertook the CG post production on this film.

    In addition to the CG side of effects work, I also spent a significant time working with techniques in special effects. These have included pyrotechnics, working with guns, atmospherics, and prosthetics.

  • SFX Assistant

    BBC Drama: Waking The Dead (Self-employed)

    (Self-Employed; Broadcast Media industry)

    March 2007 — September 2007 (7 months)

    Worked as a freelance assistant to SFX Supervisor Graham Brown for three episodes of Waking the Dead Series 7. The work totalled 6 full days on set, spread out over the period indicated by this entry.

    I helped to create numerous practical physical effects including:
    - a vehicle explosion using three mortars (two petrol lifters, and one with debris/concrete powder)
    - a head shot gag
    - numerous squib bullet hits on ground
    - multiple blood-bag bullet hits on people
    - propane flames
    - environmental smoke effects with smoke guns, diesel pots, and fire gel.

    More information will appear on my website as soon as the show has been aired (2nd & 3rd quarter 2008) and permission has been granted.
