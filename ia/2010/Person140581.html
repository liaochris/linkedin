Melinda Varley’s Summary

I started my career in the regional press working for The Caboolture News covering news from the South East area of Queensland. Throughout my time at the Caboolture News I also contributed articles to The Sunshine Coast Daily and read the late night news for the local radio station, 4OUR.

In 2004 I moved to the Island and Mainland News on Bribie Island, working with Australia's leading investigative journalist Bob Bottom.

Throughout my career in Australia, I also worked in the media room for Mal Brough (Liberal party) in the lead up to the Australian General Election 2004.

Other stints in Australia included news script writer at Channel Ten News in Townsville and The Townsville Bulletin daily newspaper.

In 2005 I moved to London taking on a freelance career contributing news articles to the Australian press both in London and Australia. These included travel stories, current affairs, general news and politics.

I started at financial compliance firm Complinet in 2006. At this time I was also freelancing for a number of personal finance websites, which continued for three years.

In August 2006 I took on the position of reporter at Centaur's marketing, advertising and design news website Mad.co.uk covering media and marketing. In 2007 I was promoted to the role of senior reporter.

In June 2008 I took on a 6-month contract at Brand Strategy magazine, covering news and features on media, marketing, advertising and brands.

In January 2009 I was appointed acting editor of utalkmarketing.com after freelancing for the site for a period of six months. Since June 2009 I have been working for utalkmarketing as a freelancer.

In September 2009 I started at Business Spectator and I am now covering media and advertising for Commentary.

I am now also writing for Techistan in the US covering social media marketing.

Melinda Varley’s Specialties:

Media, marketing, advertising, brands, social media, popular culture and technology.
