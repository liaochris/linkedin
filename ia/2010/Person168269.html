Rakesh Rathore’s Summary

I specialized in Biochemistry & Molecular biology during my studies at Cancer Hospital & Research Institute and G. B. Pant University. I then joined the Membrane protein Biochemistry group at Gothenburg University/Orebro University, Sweden. After two year, I joined Cardiovascular Sciences Department at Albany Medical Center, NY in a lab focusing on the Calcium Signaling particularly regulation and functions of calcium influx and calcium release in Vascular and Airway smooth muscles. My research work involved advanced Biochemical (Radioactive, Fluorescence & Chemiluminescence based assay development), Molecular Biology, Cell Biology, Microbiological & Microscopy techniques (Confocal Microscopy) and Animal model studies. Over the past two & half years, my research interests were focused on the development and integration of MALDI-MS based enzyme assay development & High Throughput Screening (HTS) technologies, which includes testing HTS technologies for wide ranges of enzymes classes (Kinases, Proteases, Phophatases etc.), screening of chemical libraries, Fragment based screening, Cell-based Enzyme Assays, and Integration of the mass spectrometer system with the liquid handling robotic system.

My current work at University of Cincinnati is focused toward Translational Research on the development of novel form of remote cardioprotection.

Interested in industrial and academic collaboration on Drug Discovery and Development.

• Proteomics and Mass Spectrometry Group owner ( 600 members)
