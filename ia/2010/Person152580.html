Nathaniel Doyno’s Summary

Nathaniel is an experienced cleantech executive, entrepreneur, and community leader focused on developing and deploying innovative alternative & renewable energy systems, advancing triple-bottom line sustainability, and encouraging social enterprise.

A proud Pittsburgh native, Nathaniel has been an active Board member and/or volunteer for multiple industry, environmental, and civic organizations in SW Pennsylvania.

  • Advisory Board Member, Sprout Seed Award

    (Non-Profit; Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    May 2008 — June 2009 (1 year 2 months)

    Founded in 2001, the Sprout Fund (Sprout) is a non-traditional grant maker organized to be responsive to the innovative initiatives of young people. In addition to the size ($500 to $10,000) and timing of its support (bimonthly review of applications), one of the key differentiators between Sprout and a traditional foundation is that Sprout’s funding decisions are made by advisory committee members that are a part of the same demographic it serves—young people making decisions to support projects serving young people. supporting innovative ideas and grassroots community projects that are catalyzing change in Pittsburgh.  Through its programs and activities, Sprout enriches the Pittsburgh region’s vitality by engaging citizens, amplifying voices, supporting creativity and innovation, and cultivating connected communities.

    Nathaniel has a unique relationship with the Sprout Fund, beginning in October 2005 with the receipt of a $10,000 Seed Award to support Steel City Biofuels. In September 2006 both Nathaniel (in his capacity as Pittsburgh Region Clean CIties’ Coordinator) received a $20,000 “Engage Pittsburgh” grant and GTECH received a $15,000 grant (35% of Sprouts awards for that program). From May 2008 - June 2009 Nathaniel served as an active member on the Advisory Board for Sprout’s Seed Award Program. He completed Sprout’s Seed Award Mentorship Program and advised successful Seed Award projects such as Creative Re-Use and Burgh Bees.

  • Co-Founder and former Board VP

    GTECH Strategies

    (Renewables & Environment industry)

    May 2007 — June 2009 (2 years 2 months)

    GTECH Strategies is social enterprise co-founded by Nathaniel Doyno, Matthew Ciccone, Chris Koch, and Andrew Butcher. GTECH, which stands for Growth Through Energy & Community Health, presents a new model for sustainable community development and seeks to establish a fundamental shift in urban revitalization practices.

    Specifically, GTECH utilizes vacant land as a mechanism to extend the opportunities of the Green Economy into the most marginalized communities, linking environmental stewardship, social innovation and economic development through practices such as bioenergy feedstock production, vacant land stabilization, sustainable urban agriculture, energy efficiency & weatherization, and renewable energy instillation.

    GTECH & its team members have been the recipient of numerous awards and recognitions, including the prestigious Echoing Green Social Innovation Fellowship (Co-Founders Andrew Butcher & Chris Koch), a Green Building Alliance Green Building Product Innovation Grant, and the “Pittsburgh Innovates” Audience Choice Award. Additionally, GTECH was a member of the Social Innovation Accelerator’s investment 2009 portfolio.

  • Co-Founder & former Executive Director

    Steel City Biofuels

    (Environmental Services industry)

    July 2005 — June 2009 (4 years )

    Co-Founded by Nathaniel Doyno (Greg Boulos & Peter Lambert) as a non-profit organization in July 2005, Steel City Biofuels (SCB) was established to develop the awareness, technology, policy and infrastructure necessary for the widespread production and use of sustainable biofuels in SW Pennsylvania. SCB pursued this mission through educational programs and workshops, technical consulting & project management, technology demonstrations, collaborative research projects, and advocacy for non-partisan, science based public policies.

    For the past 4 years SCB has intimately involved with the development and deployment of many of the largest biofuel projects in South Western Pennsylvania, from the development of feedstock, production and distribution infrastructure to education and training programs for biofuel industry personnel. As such Nathaniel has unique and real-world experience with all aspects of the Pennsylvania biofuels supply chain.

    From 2006 to 2008 Steel City Biofuels operated as a program of The Pennsylvania Resources Council (PRC), one of Pennsylvania’s oldest citizen action environmental organizations. The has a history of incubating & launching other successful non-profit social venture organizations.

    In July 2008 Steel City Biofuels merged with Penn State University, thereby expanding its geographic service area and ensuring a long-term and stable institutional home for SCB’s mission, programs, and initiatives. As a program of PSU’s Cooperative Extension & Outreach system, SCB was closely affiliated with the PSU Biomass Energy Center and the Bioenergy Bridge initiative. As part of the Penn State Renewable and Alternative Energy Working Group biofuels program team, Nathaniel’s role was to develop new & strengthen strategic partnerships, identify & secure funding streams, and support biofuel related educational, technology transfer, and business development activities across the Commonwealth.

  • Member, Green Government Task Force

    City of Pittsburgh

    (Government Agency; Government Administration industry)

    January 2007 — June 2008 (1 year 6 months)

    The City of Pittsburgh formed a Green Government Task Force (GGTF) late in 2006 to create a plan for reducing the city’s energy consumption and greenhouse gas emissions. The task force was led by co-chairs Pittsburgh Mayor Luke Ravenstahl, Pittsburgh City Councilman Bill Peduto, and Pennsylvania State Senator Jim Ferlo.

    The task force, a coalition whose membership spanned government officials, educators, business people, environmentalists, and concerned citizens, completed an action plan of recommended steps that both city government and individual residents can take to reduce energy consumption and emissions. The task force presented this Pittsburgh Climate Action Plan to Pittsburgh City Council on June 24, 2008.

    The action plan is closely tied to Pittsburgh’s Climate Initiative, part of a larger program, the Cities for Climate Protection, which Pittsburgh has joined. The goal of Cities for Climate Protection is to reduce greenhouse gas emissions and slow the effects of global warming worldwide.

    More information can be found at www.pittsburghclimate.org
