Mark Stanfield’s Summary

Mark is a Business Systems Analyst, Sr. with Capital One in Richmond, VA with 13 years professional experience in the IT, life insurance and financial services industries. He has served 7 years in business analyst capacities gathering requirements, including research, business analysis, user acceptance testing, system design, issue resolution and status reporting related to system changes. He has also interfaced with internal and external resources to ensure successful completion of testing in accordance with the needs of project sponsors and stakeholders.

In layman's terms, a "Business Systems Analyst" (BSA) is often used when you have someone that either wants a brand new software system built or a current system customized. BSA's create the documents that contain all the detailed requirements describing what that software needs to do to solve the need. These documents are either given to a developer to program the software or a technical analyst who turns the functional needs into computer-speak that the programmer can code from.
