Ron Jones’s Summary

My industry experience has given me the capability of developing products from concept to production. Having worked extensively in the OEM automobile industry, I have a bias towards quality in design, performance, appearance, ease of installability, serviceability and manufacturing cost.

Ron Jones’s Specialties:

Developing design concepts to solve problems, detail design of components and systems, product documentation drawings, patent documentation, installation manuals, parts manuals, prototype development and testing, vendor selection and interfacing, manufacturing tooling and assembly design, and production start-ups.

  • Consulting Engineer

    Fulham Company

    (Mechanical or Industrial Engineering industry)

    January 2009 — October 2009 (10 months)

    I designed an inspection machine for the incoming inspection of product for the Quality Assurance department. The machine was composed primarily of machined parts. Following client acceptance, I identified and solicited quotes from vendors to build the component parts. I followed-up the manufacturing of the parts by the recommended vendor, assembled and calibrated the machine, wrote calibration and inspection procedures and trained the client QA personnel how to use the machine. The successful operation of the machine allowed the client to obtain a less restrictive part tolerance, improve the product, and reduce the inspection quantity requirements.

  • Product Designer

    Retrotek Speed

    (Mechanical or Industrial Engineering industry)

    August 2008 — October 2009 (1 year 3 months)

    I designed the housing for a new EFI system electronic control unit and developed design revisions a new fuel control system and distributor. I used Solidworks to prepare the part and drawing documentation for these products as well as other products in the line including an electronic transmission shift system. System components in these products included molded and machined parts. In support of the release of new products, I prepared installation instructions including writing procedures and creating graphics. I also prepared documentation for patent applications.

  • Product Designer

    Kennamore

    (Mechanical or Industrial Engineering industry)

    December 2008 — April 2009 (5 months)

    Working from a concept sketch I designed a exercise device for the inventor. The design involved adjustable air cylinders and was composed of metal and molded plastic parts, all of which were layed out in Solidworks 3D CAD. Following design approval, I indentified and solicited quotes for tooling and building the component parts, starting with SLA models and soft tooling for the prototype and pre-productions units.

  • Designer

    Electric Traction Systems

    (Mechanical or Industrial Engineering industry)

    2006 — 2007 (1 year )

    I prepared mechanical designs and drawings for the electric traction drive system, HVAC drive systems, hydraulic and air brake sub-systems, battery support unit, and fuel cell APU for heavy trucks. In addition to the manufacturing drawings, I also prepared the parts lists, including an illustrated parts manual. I prepared project schedules in MS Project.

  • Vehicle Design & Development Engineer

    Western Golf Car Manufacturing

    (Mechanical or Industrial Engineering industry)

    June 2000 — May 2007 (7 years )

    As the principal engineer for the Lido NEV and the golf car vehicle product line, I was responsible for engineering and development of vehicles from prototype to production.
    Systems and components designed included chassis, hydraulic brakes, steering, electrical system wire harnesses, and body supports and hinges.
    In support of these, I also: tested and evaluated prototypes, prepared drawing documentation, selected and interfaced with vendors, and designed production tooling for manufacture and assembly.

  • Manager, Product Development

    Subaru of America

    (Mechanical or Industrial Engineering industry)

    December 1976 — December 1993 (17 years 1 month)

    As the manager of product development at the Subaru Technical Center I was responsible for managing a group of engineers, technicans and drafters to develop accessory products for the vehicles which accounted for $110 million in annual sales to the Parts Department. Vehicles impacted included the Legacy (3 generations), Brat (2 generations), XT, Justy, SVX and Impreza (1st generation).
    Responsibilities included: developing product specifications, performing evaluation testing, organizing and leading field tests, issuing product approvals, preparing specification drawings, specifying kit packaging and installation instructions, vendor selection and interfacing, interfacing with Parts, Service, Product Planning and QC departments, and negotiating vehicle specifications with vehicle engineers at several factories in Japan.
