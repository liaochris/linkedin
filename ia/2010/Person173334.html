Rick Thompson’s Summary

Greentech Media is the industry-leading integrated B2B media and market research firm, covering green technologies, on the internet today. Since our founding in early 2007, we have amassed the largest online community of green technology professionals focused on next-generation solutions covering green power generation, distribution and consumption. Our primary focus areas are smart grid infrastructure & applications, and solar power generation. Greentech Media is headquartered in Cambridge, Mass., New England’s educational- and technology-innovation center, with operations in New York City and San Francisco. For more information, visit http://www.greentechmedia.com.

Prior to founding Greentech Media, I spent many years working with several of the industry's most successful private and public data networking and telecommunications infrastructure firms focused on LAN and WAN infrastructure technologies. Specialties include Ethernet LANs (wired & wireless), IP data networking, SONET & Optical Networking (DWDM & Switching), wireless networking, IPTV and Internet Video. Functions included Product Management, Product Marketing, Competitive Analysis, Market Research & Analysis, Program Management and Operations.

Rick Thompson’s Experience

  • President, Co-Founder

    Greentech Media

    (Privately Held; Online Media industry)

    February 2007 — Present (3 years 5 months)

    Greentech Media is the industry-leading integrated B2B media & market research firm covering green technology markets. Greentech Media was founded in February, 2007 by Rick Thompson and Scott Clavenna. Greentech Media's primary market coverage areas include solar power generation and smart grid markets.

  • Senior Telecom Analyst

    Light Reading (Heavy Reading)

    (Public Company; Telecommunications industry)

    October 2004 — February 2007 (2 years 5 months)

    Heavy Reading is the telecom market research arm of Light Reading, the industry-leading B2B media firm covering the global telecommunications market. Light Reading was acquired by CMP Media (a subsidiary of UK-based United Business Media) in 2005.

  • VP, Marketing

    PanGo Networks

    (Privately Held; Computer Software industry)

    May 2003 — May 2004 (1 year 1 month)

    PanGo Networks develops location-based services & applications over wireless LANs. PanGo was acquired by InnerWireless in 2007. I was part of a "founding" team of 3 that "re-started" PanGo Networks after several failed market entries since it's original founding. We raised ~$5M in series A funding from IDG Ventures and Columbia Capital. PanGo's original founders and vision were born out of Carnegie Mellon University.

  • Director, Product Management & Marketing

    Sycamore Networks

    (Public Company; SCMR; Telecommunications industry)

    July 1999 — April 2002 (2 years 10 months)

    Sycamore Networks develops intelligent optical networking hardware and software for the world's largest telecom operators. Sycamore Networks went public in October 1999. Sycamore closed with the biggest market value ever achieved by an Internet-related company in its first day of trading, according to CommScan. It also achieved the fourth-largest gain--386 percent--for a U.S. stock on its first day of trading. Primary competitors include Ciena, Cisco, Alcatel-Lucent, Siemens, Ericsson, Nortel Networks.

  • Product Manager

    Packet Engines (Acquired by Alcatel, 1998)

    (Computer Networking industry)

    June 1997 — June 1999 (2 years 1 month)

    Packet Engines was a pioneer in developing the industry's first Gigabit Ethernet LAN switching technology. Packet Engines was acquired in 1998, along with Xylan, to form Alcatel's enterprise networking group. Primary competitors include Extreme Networks, Foundry Networks (acquired by Brocade), Rapid City Communications (acquired by Bay Networks) and Granite Systems (acquired by Cisco).

  • NPI Program Manager

    FORE Systems (Acquired by Marconi in 1999, Ericsson 2005)

    (Computer Networking industry)

    June 1994 — June 1997 (3 years 1 month)

    FORE Systems was a pioneer in developing the industry's first ATM (Asynchronous Transfer Mode) networking technologies. FORE had its IPO in 1994 and was later acquired by Marconi (a General Electric Company) in 1999. Marconi was later acquired by Ericsson in 2005. Primary competitors included Stratacom (acquired by Cisco), Newbridge Networks (acquired by Alcatel), Cascade Communications (acquired by Ascend, then Lucent) and Nortel Networks.
