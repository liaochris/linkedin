David Kinnear’s Summary

David is the original architect and a co-founder of the Global Sourcing Council. He is a frequent contributor, thought-leader on the role and growth of global sourcing methods. He is an acknowledged driver & articulate facilitator of international dialog in regards to the social and fiscal agenda associated with global sourcing, outsourcing & shared services – promoting dialog & ideas for productive outcomes and opportunity creation.

David is passionate & articulate in the need for opportunity creation through socially responsible employment practices multi-nationally and well-versed in the factors required to drive win-win outcomes for both “provider” and “client” economies. He is a co-founder of The Great Hope Initiative, The Philippines BPO Council and an active member of numerous professional associations in the legal, sourcing & socio-economic spheres.

David leads Global BPO & Advisory Solutions for Global Sourcing Advisory Group (GSAG), a subsidiary of FirstCarbon Solutions. Prior to joining GSAG, David was President and CEO of Lisnagol Global Services and brings over two decades of experience and marketplace awareness on both the client and provider side of the outsourcing industry.

An attorney by trade (a qualified UK Barrister), David has worked for leading law firms Masons and Linklaters in London, specializing in major construction & engineering contract management & litigation. In 1995, he relocated Credit Suisse's Banking & Support Operations into its new global headquarters, and then managed significant global outsourcing, shared sourcing and process efficiency initiatives during his subsequent tenure. Afterward, he moved to the provider side, working extensively with the UK, US, Philippines and China during his time with the Black Mountain Group and subsequently.

David has a LLB from Manchester University and is a member of the Bar of England and Wales. He lives with his wife and daughter in New York.
