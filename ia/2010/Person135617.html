Martín Pérez-Cerdá’s Summary

Mr. Martin Pérez Cerdá who was born in H. Matamoros, Tamaulipas (Octuber 23rd, 1966), has twenty years of journalistic and executive management experience in
media companies.
In April or 1982, at the young age of twenty, he began
his career in journalism upon being hired as a reporter
and financial analyst by "El Norte".
During the Fall of 1997, at the age of 30, Mr. Pérez
Cerdá was named Chief Editor of the El Norte, Metro
and Sol newspapers, which at that time had a
combined daily circulation of 200,000 prints.
In February of 2000, he left Grupo Reforma and
accepted the position of Vice President of Internet
Contents for Grupo Televisa, through the newly
formed EsMas.com Company.
Mr. Pérez Cerdá was named CEO of EsMas.Com in
December of 2001, at which time he began a
organizational restructure of all the Internet contents
for Grupo Televisa.
In January of 2002, he simultaneous accepts the CEO
position of Editorial Televisa México and takes charge
of 25 magazines of the most important editorial
publlishing grupo for the Spanish speaking world.
In Octuber of 2002, Mr. Pérez Cerdá came to an inicial
argeement with Steve and Miguel Forbes to published
the Forbes Magazine in Spanish for Latin American
distribution.
In January of 2003, he decides to retire from Grupo
Televisa to form his own company. Grupo Inmediática
S.A., a consulting firm which especializes in media
and public relations for publishing companies.
From March until August of 2003, he became a
full-time adviser for TV Azteca. During his
participation he was named Content Director for
Fuerza Informativa Azteca news group, where he
restructured the newscast of the second largest
television network in Mexico.
At the present time, Mr. Pérez Cerdá is the General
Director of Grupo Inmediática S.A., consulting firm that especialize in the
management and timing of corporative information for
media companies.
