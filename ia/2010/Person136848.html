Matt Cuddy’s Summary

Planner with ten years' experience in urban redevelopment, downtown revitalization, and transportation planning and research. History as a coalition-builder and a catalyst.

Matt Cuddy’s Specialties:

Urban redevelopment land use planning, the Main Street™ approach to business district revitalization, context-sensitive parking standards, parking management programs

Matt Cuddy’s Experience

  • Owner

    Cuddy Downtown Consulting

    (Architecture & Planning industry)

    June 2003 — Present (7 years 3 months)

    Consulting in downtown business development, land use planning, and parking management.

    • Co-founded NewtonVillages.org, a coalition for enhancing the vitality of village centers in Newton, MA.
    • Authored and co-authored master plan revisions, redevelopment plans, and housing production plans.
    • Helped create Downtown Hightstown, a Main Street-style revitalization program in Hightstown, NJ.
    • Conducted parking and retail market studies in Newtonville, MA.
    • Certified by the State of New Jersey direct state-supported Main Street™ programs.

  • Research Coordinator

    Northwestern University Transportation Center

    (Research industry)

    September 2006 — May 2008 (1 year 9 months)

    Business development for transportation researchers in engineering and economics, linking them to industry and public agencies.

    Marketing
    • Designed and implemented a marketing program: a visual identity, website, newsletter and brochures.

    Relationship development
    • Created and managed new funded research relationships with Norfolk Southern Railroad and Philips (Electronics) Logistics Division.
    • Represented the university and the industry at the Chicago Metropolitan Agency for Planning (CMAP), in the development of the freight plan within the Chicago regional integrated plan.

    Grants
    • Created and administered an annual internal research grant program, which made grants averaging $18,000 per year.
    • Managed faculty grant proposals to external funders, with a 40% success rate and an average value of roughly $200,000.

    Internal team management
    • Developed and administered team-building strategies including a weekly internal newsletter and a lunchtime speaker series.
    • Hired and managed marketing staff.

  • Research Associate

    Rutgers' Bloustein School of Planning and Public Policy

    (Research industry)

    May 2004 — May 2006 (2 years 1 month)

    Analysis
    • Developed statistical models of household vehicle ownership data to develop a practical method for setting residential parking standards that depend on location and unit type.
    • Developed a model of residential development cost for multifamily urban infill.
    • Analyzed alternative tax-base sharing strategies for the New Jersey Meadowlands Commission.

    Writing
    • Co-authored guidelines for residential urban infill development for use throughout the State of New Jersey.
    • Co-authored a book on the practical measurement of smart growth, published by the American Planning Association.

    Grants
    • Won a $25,000 grant from the US Department of Housing and Urban Development for the development of an improved, practical method for establishing urban residential parking standards.
    • Worked under a $200,000 grant from the State of New Jersey.

    1993 — 1998 (5 years )

    Analysis
    • Created hybrid vehicle drivetrain simulation software, ADVISOR, in 1995. It was commercially licensed, and remains the basis of an analytical program at the National Renewable Energy Laboratory.
