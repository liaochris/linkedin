Nate Williams’s Summary

With progressive leadership roles in Corporate Finance, Marketing, and Business Development, Nate’s 10+ years of C-level advisory experience unites the most tactical facets of Hollywood with the technological and analytical sophistication of Silicon Valley.

Currently as Chief Marketing Officer for 4HomeMedia, a Dow Jones “Top 50 Company to Watch”, Nate leads the ideation and refinement of 4Home’s strategic roadmap and the requisite go-to-market activities including sales, partnerships, and financial planning. Nate also represents 4HomeMedia in industry relations and as their primary liaison to the Venture Capital community.

Nate joined 4Home as Director of Marketing in October 2006 from the Digital Home Group of Intel Corporation, where he provided strategic finance direction for their Consumer PC business as well as executed investments in Media and partnerships with NBC-Universal, Disney and ClickStar. Earlier, Nate served as an Graduate Associate at The Walt Disney Company and an Associate at JPMorgan & Co.

Nate began his media career as an Agent Trainee at Hollywood tenpercentery William Morris where he facilitated the contract negotiation and artist management processes for A-list talent including John Travolta, George Clooney, and Britney Spears. Over his career, Nate has advised many early-stage companies in areas such as content distribution, advertising infrastructure, and business development and participated in numerous philanthropies including High 5 and the United Negro College Fund (UNCF).

Nate holds a Masters degree in Finance and Entertainment Management from The Anderson School at UCLA and a Bachelors degree in Communications Technology from The University of Connecticut. Furthermore, Nate received Behavioral Research certification from UCLA, completed an Asia-Pacific MBA immersion at The Australian Graduate School of Management (AGSM), and attended The Carlson Leadership Academy.

Digital media & connected home strategy, content distribution services, new media technologies and business models, content industry value chain, cross industry negotiations, venture capital, economic analysis, entrepreneurs interested in startups in the digital media space
