    (Internet industry)

    October 2008 — May 2009 (8 months)

    Hired to bring life back to a stagnant website, I was the first person in the door in over 5 years to work on Let It Ride.com. I worked alone for the first 4 months before a tech director and database admin joined the team. At that time, my role was refined and I was able to dive deeper into the projects and accomplish more in the same amount of time. While working on projects, I received only basic ideas and objectives from management with the expectation that I would scope out the requirements and design (database, business logic, and presentation) for the pages by myself.

In addition to the pages themselves, I developed several libraries that contain common classes and methods. Security and robustness were always the primary concern when developing new features. Many of the pages were designed to take advantage of asynchronous updating. Initially the pages were written using updatepanels. The process of rewriting those pages to use jQuery for updating had been started. Many of the pages have differing appearances and functionality based on the permissions and group membership of the user.

    I also wrote a Windows Service that performs background updating of information in the database. It has an internal scheduling mechanism that spawns individual threads when a task is run. It queries several sites for news articles and race stats. Taking a url from the database, it parses the resulting RSS (or any xml file) feeds, extracting the required information and opening links from other elements grabbing and manipulating the resulting html code and then storing all the data in the database.
