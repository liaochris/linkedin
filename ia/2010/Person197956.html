Susan O'Meara’s Summary

BIO

Promotion, programming, production -- Susan O’Meara has long been considered a wordsmith by clients and colleagues alike. She has written and produced brand-building campaigns for top media, entertainment and financial companies like Sony Pictures Digital, CNBC, 20th Century Fox, Deloitte, E! Entertainment Television, Bloomberg Media, Universal Studios, Sundance Channel, Oxygen Media, Viacom and Discovery Networks.

From sports (Holyfield v. Tyson, Wrestlemania) to concerts (The Rolling Stones), Susan got her start in marketing special pay-per-view events for Showtime Networks. She has since developed advertising programs and launch promotions, as well as strategic sales and corporate initiatives, for national and international brands such as Saatchi & Saatchi, TNT, NBC News, foxkids.com, Galavision, TiVo, Animal Planet, FX, Columbia TriStar International Television, The Walt Disney Company, Lowe Partners International and bigfishmarketing.com.

Susan has also written feature-length documentaries, including The Windsors for Fox’s Family Channel and a 25th anniversary special for the landmark film Giant for Warner Bros. DVD. Her half-hour non-fiction pieces (Riding the Rebel Highway, Roswell: The Real Story, Beyond the Outer Limits and Tales of the City) premiered on Showtime.

Recently profiled in Robin Fisher-Roffer’s latest book, The Fearless Fish Out of Water: How to Succeed When You’re the Only One Like You, Susan is developing By Bread Alone: In the Company of Food Heroes, a documentary film about dynamic food communities around the world. She is also researching and writing a book, Beyond Borat: Adopting from Kazakhstan, on her recent adoption of an 11-month-old Russian boy. Susan contributes monthly to Love Food, the online newsletter of chef/journalist/TV presenter Clodagh McKenna. She lives with her son Taidgh in Dublin, Ireland.
