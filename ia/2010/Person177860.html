Ross Chirnside’s Summary

As Local government in Auckland is being reorganised this creates an opportunity for me to find my next ideal role. While still considering opportunities within the new Auckland council I am particularly interested in investigating senior finance roles (Chief Financial Officer / Financial Controller) in significant external customer and people focused organisations.

I am an experienced senior manager and financial controller who has experience in both the private and public sectors. Due to the transferable nature of my skills I am open to which industry sector I may work in the future.

I am a technically competent accountant but believe my key skills extend beyond those normally associated with finance. These skills include strategic planning (clearly defining finances objectives and aligning them to the business), leadership (leading both large finance teams and providing organisation wide financial guidance), customer focus (providing advocacy to the customers needs), negotiation (from the a values position reflecting fairness and trust) and project management (delivering complex projects on time and within budget.

I believe that finance is a partner of the business and finance professionals must proactively work with business managers to assist them in better managing their business and improving performance and customer outcomes.

Ross Chirnside’s Specialties:

Financial leadership experience in the private and public sectors. Experienced in leading large teams, process improvement (particularly customer interfaces), multi discipline project management, financial management, risk management, business oppportunity analysis.

Ross Chirnside’s Experience

  • Group Manager Financial Control

    Manukau City Council

    (Government Agency; Government Administration industry)

    2006 — Present (4 years )

    The council has annual revenue of $340m, over 1,000 staff and net assets of $5.8bn. Council operations are split over 30 business units including transportation, regulatory, leisure, property and environmental service. The council group consists of 5 subsidiaries, 7 trusts and a joint venture contributing a further $90m of revenue and $1bn of assets.

    A key aspect of a role in an organisation of this scale is leadership where I provide council wide financial leadership and leading, supporting and guiding a large team of 4 direct reports and 48 indirect reports.

    I have responsibility for the organisations annual report and the overall reporting and planning cycles. I manage the financial and non financial reporting on a monthly, quarterly and six monthly basis and the preparation of both the annual budget and 10 year strategic plan which included budgets, service levels and key performance indicators.

    With responsibility for 105,000 customers good process is critical to manage the high volumes of financial transactionals. This includes rates, cashiering, receipting, accounts payable, accounts receivable, reconciliations, fixed asset accounting.

    I also have responsibility for the wider council group in terms of the group consolidation, group accounting policies, group tax planning and compliance (including binding rulings) and liaison with audit.

    Council has a significant borrower and I have a strategic overview on group treasury matters including providing advice on financing strategy, investments (including 10% shareholding in Auckland airport) and debt issues.

  • Regional Financial Controller

    Heritage Hotel Management Limited

    (Privately Held; Hospitality industry)

    2000 — 2006 (6 years )

    Heritage operated 8 hotels trading under the Heritage and City Life brands with my role responsible for the 4 hotels in the North Island (Heritage Auckland, CityLife Auckland, CityLife Wellington and Heritage Hotel du Vin). These hotels had combined annual revenue of $45m and employing over 400 staff.

    This was a new role with the key objectives of increasing financial focus and improving the overall business performance.

    I was responsible for the financial management of the hotel (including purchasing and IT) and leading a team of 12 while also providing wider leadership to the business. As part of my role I also completed feasibility analysis and prepared business cases for potential new hotel properties to add to the group portfolio. This role allowed me to managing multi discipline, organisation wide projects to support growth and improve business outcomes.

  • Commercial Manager Attractions

    Skycity Auckland

    (Public Company; Gambling & Casinos industry)

    1996 — 1999 (3 years )

    The Attractions Division had annual revenue of over $40m through the operation of a hotel, 5 restaurants, 2 bars, Sky Tower, Conference Centre, retail outlets and car parking.

    This was a newly created role to support the General Manager in improving the Attractions business performance.

    My key responsibilities included developing and implementing the strategic and operating plan for the Attractions Division, providing analysis on Attractions business units’ performance and investigating new business ideas and initiatives. I was also responsible for the project management of some significant projects including the preopening testing of sky tower, implementation of new retail operations and 2 restaurant openings.

  • Internal auditor

    Millennium & Copthorne Hotels (was CDL Hotels)

    (Public Company; Hospitality industry)

    1995 — 1996 (1 year )

    At the time CDL had 28 hotels throughout New Zealand. I was employed to establish the internal audit function for the group. This was a continuation of work that I had started while working for KPMG and involved developing the work programme, assessing the risk and then testing the internal control environment at each hotel property.
