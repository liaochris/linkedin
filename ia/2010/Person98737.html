Jim Rankin’s Summary

Manages, coordinates and directs engineering design and maintenance programs in the instrumentation and control discipline in order to maintain and achieve optimal equipment reliability and adherence to regulatory maintenance criteria of the Niagara Project's hydro generation and transmission facilities.

Develop and administer multiple capital installation and maintenance programs for relay protection, communications (including telephone, microwave, RF and FO), metering systems, SCADA, computer systems (both business and operations), instrumentation, plant security equipment, and high voltage apparatus to achieve optimal equipment reliability and adherence to regulatory maintenance criteria (i.e. FERC, NERC, NPCC and ISO). Responsible for the installation and maintenance of the protection and control of 25 generators, and 21 transmission lines representing 25 % of the total NYPA load. Responsible for the installation and maintenance of revenue metering equipment at 33 NYPA municipalities. Provide strategic input to the overall operation and maintenance of the Niagara Project.

Lead and oversee multiple employees, a union maintenance workforce, and contractors to ensure projects and schedules are met. Interview, recommend hiring, train, motivate and evaluate a supervisor, engineers and technicians to develop proficient individuals for the department's work and as a future source of managers. Take disciplinary action as needed; complete and conduct performance reviews and make wage increase recommendations in accordance with company guidelines and standards.
