Jim Sabo’s Summary

Internet pioneer and former digerati-- responsible for such early successes as the Los Angeles Times and Ticketmaster Online, rode the bleeding edge at Digital Entertainment Network, and freelanced for publications such as NetGuide and Internet World.

Most recently with Technicolor Digital Cinema, where I developed their in-theater advertising system which is on ~15% of the nation's movie screens, as well as their acclaimed Theater Management System for digital cinema, as well as web-based tools used by studios to place and track feature film orders.

I bridge the gap between business and engineering, working to find out what the business needs, then turning that into a released product. I particularly enjoy the pace of start-ups.

Jim Sabo’s Specialties:

E-commerce, system architecture, software development management, and large database administration.

Microsoft .NET & SQL, SOA and Agile development.

    Redesinged the database schema to increase performance and provide survivability for flash traffic periods.

    Migrated ticket purchase process to .NET to provide better speed, flexibility, and usability.

    Oversaw software engineering, web engineering, compression, and QA groups.

  • Senior Developer

    Ticketmaster

    (Public Company; TKTM; Entertainment industry)

    March 1996 — February 1999 (3 years )

    Converted ticketmaster.com from a series of static web pages generated from a tab-delimited export from the main ticketing system into a completely dynamic, SQL-driven e-commerce web site. Responsible for all technical aspects of the site, from network architecture to web design.

    Also desgined systems for Technicolor affiliates in England, Mexico, and Australia. The database architecture for Australia was in use for 10 years, and was only replaced to bring the code base in line with the other affiliates; there were no performance issues.

    Working in partnership with Intel, we developed a personalized site that allowed users to get a "view from the seat" from over 20 different theaters around the country.

    In partnership with Palm, developed one of the first two wireless e-commerce applications for the Palm 7, which allowed users to search for and securely purchase tickets using their wireless PDA.

    Ticketmaster was one of the pioneers in distributed web architectures (the serial number on our Cisco LocalDirector had a serial number of 12), and the basic architecture and disaster recovery plan that I desgined remained in use for many years after I left the company.

    Ticketmaster and its various affiliates now do well over 60% of their total business via the web, selling over 142 million tickets and collecting over $8 billion in '07.
