Noah Weiss’s Experience

  • Associate Product Manager

    Google

    (Public Company; GOOG; Internet industry)

    September 2008 — Present (2 years 1 month)

    SEARCH
    As of August 2009, I have been the lead product manager on our structured data related search projects. The team over 20 engineers on it. Below are examples of public projects that have launched:

    Co-created a project to surface answers to fact-seeking queries, like [when did john lennon die] and [texas minimum wage], in web search. Pulls the answers into snippets, and displays a box at the top of the results page if the fact is high confidence Answers millions of queries per day. See blog posts for the top box at http://googleblog.blogspot.com/2010/05/understanding-web-to-find-short-answers.html and for answer highlighting at http://googleblog.blogspot.com/2010/01/understanding-web-to-make-search-more.html.

    One of the first examples launches for the team was Google Squared (http://squared.google.com), which came out in Google Labs in the summer of 2009. It's a way to search for category queries like "yankees players" or "manhattan thai restaurants". Instead of getting a list of links to pages, you get a table full of facts. For "yankees players", you would get a list of Yankees players with relevant attributes about those players like "Salary" and "Position".


    DISPLAY ADS
    From September 2008 to August 2009, I worked on a display advertising platform for publishers. It combined Google's technology with products from our DoubleClick acquisition. I focused primarily on developing an API from the ground up and building out our reporting and analytics functionality. Launched out of beta on 2/22/2010, along with the rest of the rebranded DoubleClick for Publishers by Google. This was the culmination of almost 2 years of work to create a new ad server based on the Google stack to move DoubleClick publishers over to.

    See http://googleblog.blogspot.com/2010/02/next-generation-of-ad-serving-for.html for an overview and http://doubleclickpublishersapi.blogspot.com/2010/02/introducing-googles-doubleclick-for.html for details on the API.
