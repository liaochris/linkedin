Pete Basel - Consulting Electrical Engineer’s Summary

Pete Basel is an electrical engineer with over 25 years of experience in advanced design involving robotics and machine vision, CPU, graphics, and custom processors, analog and digital video design, advanced signal processing, audio and loudspeaker design. I offer engineering design services for both small and large projects with innovative solutions to the most complex engineering challenges. I have provided solutions to the most complex design problems throughout my career and have been awarded 3 patents.

Many have said that I am an Engineer's Engineer. Others have said "they give you the hard problems at this company, don't they?" and this has been true throughout my career.

I have several additional patent ideas and am interested in teaming with others to develop this IP in the most advantageous way.

I have a solution for the classic metastability problem in asynchronous systems that is thought to be unsolvable other than to minimize the statistical error probability. I'd like to market this IP. Background on the problem:
http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metastability_in_electronics

  • Engineering Consultant

    W. W. Gaertner Research Inc.

    (Defense & Space industry)

    December 1986 — December 1988 (2 years 1 month)

    Graphics System Upgrade Analysis - Developed a lower cost parallel processor approach for 3D and area graphics generation. The suggested approach utilized floating point DSP and RISC microprocessor components to quadruple performance, simplify programming, and reduce costs.

    High Speed Digital Design Analysis - Analyzed and corrected major digital design flaws involving transmission line effects, timing, and noise margin problems in real time area graphics and video subsystems, which had caused manufacturing delays potentially leading to contract default. Systems were efficiently delivered by incorporating these design improvements. Redesigned analog video section.

  • Senior Engineer

    CANAAN Computer Corp

    (Computer Hardware industry)

    April 1985 — December 1986 (1 year 9 months)

    Received the CANAAN Pride and Excellence in Performance Award for work on the next generation, 2X speed CANAAN 370 compatible CPU. Designed and debugged the pipeline control logic after it had been looked at by an engineer from MIT, the VP of Engineering (who held several patents), the founder of the company, and several microcoders who all concluded that it could not be done in conventional off-the-shelf technology. The solution worked first pass, with only a few bugs found with complex microcode sequences that were corrected in the programmable logic.

    Championed (as per VP of engineering) raising the bar for the CPU upgrade from 2X to 3X through addition of tight pipelining, instruction prefetch, and a main memory cache.

    Debugged a 370 compatibility problem that was undetected by the CPU validation program and could not be found by any other engineer in the company.

    Tasked to determine why the disk I/O System did not get significantly faster when an expensive caching drive was added. Discovered that the poor software architecture/implementation caused the I/O subsystem to take 17 ms on every disk I/O request before the command even hit the disk controller. This analysis revealed fundamental issues with the design of the I/O Subsystem.

    G471 Super Computer - Tested prototype cards for the custom designed G471 Parallel Processing, Very Long Instruction Word (VLIW), Super Computer. Designed the Unibus Interface, Nucleus Processor Interface, Main Memory, Main Memory Controller, Master Disk Controller and the Data Routing Array Cards. System included a dedicated pipelined scene memory and a microcoded floating point processor, was controlled by an iRMX based MULTIBUS control computer, and was used for flight simulation at Military Air Force bases.
