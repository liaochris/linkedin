Katharine Bierce’s Summary

Katharine (kbierce@uchicago.edu) is currently a fourth-year undergraduate at the University of Chicago majoring in Psychology. In fall 2009, she was a Community Analyst with Campus Catalyst, a course that is also a nonprofit consulting group, to help a Hyde Park community organization improve their performance management with an Excel-based metrics dashboard and qualitative surveys. She has sold her college student-targeted marketing and web development advice to two Chicago restaurants. After college, she hopes to join a consulting, marketing, SRI, CSR, ESG investments, or nonprofit firm with an innovative team culture that rewards creativity, insight and a passion for excellence.

Skills she has: project management, finding the right people for a variety of roles, getting deliverables in on time, event planning, connecting the big-picture vision to the daily details, tailoring the message to fit the audience, people management and teamwork

Skills she wants to develop further:
-How to quantify the impact of her work and demonstrate its value
-Learn frameworks for problem analysis (e.g. in consulting)
-Learn how people/organizations can implement solutions, once problems are solved on paper
-Meet people who share her passion for making a difference in some way that is more than the bottom line.

Passions:
-Connecting people to projects that make an impact
-Solving big problems by breaking them up into small pieces and systematically analyzing the pieces to find workable solutions

For more information or if you'd like to talk, feel free to email me.
