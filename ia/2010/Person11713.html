  • Portfolio Manager

    Minutemen Equity Fund

    (Investment Management industry)

    September 2007 — September 2009 (2 years 1 month)

    Situation:
    I joined the Minutemen Equity Fund two years ago as an equity analyst. After one year I became the portfolio manager in charge of managing $25,000 and growing ($65,000 in May 2009).

    Task:
    The fund was down 20% plus year to date. It was my task to increase the Fund’s performance relative to the S&P 500 and move toward a positive absolute return.

    Action:
    To limit the Fund’s overall risk, I taught the sector analysts more appropriate and conservative equity analysis and valuation techniques, such as discounted cash flow valuation. In addition, I created a portfolio allocation model based on the Kelly criterion and Markowitz model.

    Result:
    The Minutemen Equity Fund is up 10% from one year ago when I became Portfolio Manager and the positive absolute return is quickly becoming a reality.

  • Summer Financial Analyst

    Raytheon

    (Public Company; RTN; Defense & Space industry)

    July 2008 — August 2008 (2 months)

    Situation:
    Raytheon’s Foreign Direct Sales constitute about 7% of their overall revenue. I was brought into a team of executives to develop a plan to increase the amount of revenue generated by foreign direct sales.

    Task:
    Determine which country or geographical region where operations could be expanded to increase direct foreign sales and establish a plan in order to expand into that country or geographical region with the maximum positive effect to direct foreign sales.

    Action:
    I analyzed all of Raytheon Company’s international sales to develop a target region where I believed further expansion would be profitable. I heard input from colleagues in a variety of functions throughout the world and Raytheon to understand the international environment. In addition, I developed a plan, in the form of a flow chart, to follow when Raytheon expands into the region, taking into consideration that region’s specific needs.

    Result:
    Shortly thereafter Raytheon received a $3.3 billion dollar defense contract from a target region.



    Situation:

    Task:
    Determine which cost centers expenses are ultimately paid in and propose a way to eliminate complexity within the current system.
