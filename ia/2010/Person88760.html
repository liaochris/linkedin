Jamie Flinchbaugh’s Summary

I am an entrepreneur, consultant, educator, and executive coach. The Lean Learning Center has become one of the premium providers of lean transformation education and consulting. In 2006 I co-authored the lean best-seller The Hitchhiker's Guide to Lean: Lessons from the Road. Our educational programs have received the highest marks, particularly our flagship program The Lean Experience, as well as the Kaizen Boot Camp and Leading Lean. Our consulting clients have included Harley-Davidson, Intel, Land O Lakes, Simmons, Mars, Detroit Medical Center and many more. We have worked in manufacturing, healthcare, utilities, government and service industries.

Jamie Flinchbaugh’s Specialties:

entrepreneurship, leadership coaching, manufacturing transformation, business process transformation, business strategy, organizational design, board of directors

  • Co-founder

    Rev! Motorcycles

    (Privately Held; Automotive industry)

    September 1998 — November 2003 (5 years 3 months)

    Rev! Motorcycles was a start-up that is best described as the Dell of the off-road motorcycle industry. The intent was to develop a wide-range of options for buyers of off-road motorcycles to customize the bike with. A working prototype was developed that knocked the socks of those who rode it. Both the business model and the product included many innovations. Launching a brick-and-mortar company in the height of the .com boom proved too difficult an investment for people. Rev! eventually acquired the company Cobra Motorcycles (www.cobramotorcycle.com) and was folded into Cobra.

  • Lean Leader

    DTE Energy

    (Public Company; DTE; Utilities industry)

    November 1999 — June 2001 (1 year 8 months)

    DTE Energy is a large integrated gas and electric utility based in Southeast Michigan. DTE Energy began a lean journey by bringing in several people like myself. I was responsible for helping to coach the executives into a state of readiness and building an internal team capable of leading the journey. DTE Energy remains committed to the journey to this day, completing over a decade of progress through many changes in leadership.

  • Operations and Lean Transformation

    Chrysler

    (Privately Held; DCX; Automotive industry)

    1994 — 1999 (5 years )

    I took on a wide range of roles throughout Chrysler from product development in minivans and the Prowler to manufacturing for Jeeps and mid-sized cars. Through most of my roles I was also deeply involved in and committed to the journey of the Chrysler Operating System, which made major contributions to the performance gain at Chrysler from nearly-bankrupt to one of the most profitable companies in the industry.
