Scott Billings’s Experience

  • Senior News Editor

    CNBC.com

    (Writing and Editing industry)

    October 2006 — Present (4 years 1 month)

    Organized and led news staff in relaunching CNBC's web site in December 2006. Current duties include directing all news coverage for the site and a staff of six writers/editors. Since relaunching, CNBC.com has become one of the top five business news sites with nearly one million unique visitors each day and 14 million page views.

  • News Editor

    The Wall Street Journal

    (Public Company; 5001-10,000 employees; DJ; Newspapers industry)

    September 1981 — October 2006 (25 years 2 months)

    2002--2006: Broadcast News Editor for Wall Street Journal. Served as liaison between WSJ and NBC Television outlets, including NBC Nightly News, the Today Show, CNBC and MSNBC.
    1999-2002: News Editor for Wall Street Journal Sunday. Directed editorial coverage for weekly roundup of business and market news from WSJ that was distributed to 60 newspapers across the country.
    1990-1999: News Editor on National Desk of Wall Street Journal. Responsible for coverage of banking, finance, politics and government for the Journal's news pages.
    1986-1990: Wrote Business and Finance summary for page one of Wall Street Journal.
    1983-1986: Editor on foreign desk of Wall Street Journal. Edited news and feature stories from Journal reporters around the world.
    1981-1983: Deputy News Editor for Wall Street Journal's Asian edition. Based in Hong Kong. Edited news and feature stories from Journal reporters throughout Asia.

  • News Editor

    The Wall Street Journal Sunday

    (Writing and Editing industry)

    January 1999 — January 2001 (2 years 1 month)

    Developed editorial mission for the Wall Street Journal Sunday, a four-page weekly roundup of personal finance and investing news that was launched in 1999 and appears in over 60 newspapers around the country. Directed staff of reporters and editors in coverage of markets, investing trends and personal finance issues.

  • News Editor

    WSJ National Desk

    (Writing and Editing industry)

    January 1990 — January 1999 (9 years 1 month)

    One of four editors on National Desk responsible for all news stories appearing in the WSJ. Supervised coverage and editing of stories in such areas as banking and finance, mergers and acquisitions, legal issues, Washington politics and policy, the economy, financial markets and real estate. Did extensive rewriting of stories on deadline.

  • Copy Editor

    WSJ Foreign and Domestic Desks

    (Writing and Editing industry)

    January 1983 — January 1990 (7 years 1 month)

    Edited and rewrote both domestic and foreign news stories for the WSJ. Spent three months in Brussels, Belgium, editing stories for the The Wall Street Journal/Europe.

  • Deputy News Editor

    Asian Wall Street

    (Writing and Editing industry)

    January 1981 — January 1983 (2 years 1 month)

    Supervised reporting and editing staff in Hong Kong and throughout Asia for the Asian Wall Street Journal. Edited and rewrote stories from reporters in the region. Also wrote and announced daily radio broadcast in Hong Kong of top business news stories.
