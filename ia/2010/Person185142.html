Scott Robertson’s Summary

I have engineered a number of successful ventures developing and implementing start-up enterprises, with a particular passion for design and technology.

In the mid 80’s I co-founded American Helix Technology Corporation located in Lancaster, PA. American Helix was a state-of-the-art compact disc replication facility. Together with my partners we created the industry’s most advanced systems for security and production control and implemented an innovative marketing program that quickly made us a leader in the industry.

In 1991 I co-founded a cutting-edge design communications company called Active8, a venture that was accomplished by merging two highly respected visual communications companies, Exhibit Technology, and Graffito. In Active8 the technical expertise and creative skills of these two companies fused to form an innovative new type of visual communications entity that was as unique as it was effective.

It was during that period of time while producing interactive exhibits requiring comprehensive skill sets that I developed the concept for Engine Performance. I always wished there was a recruiting firm available to me that truly understood my design and technology needs. At the time, no such firm existed, so in 1996 I sold my interests in Active8 and started Engine Performance. Since that time we have been recruiting the industry’s top design and advertising talent. Engine Performance utilizes that talent for its productions as well as those produced by its clients, who in some cases are other advertising agencies.
