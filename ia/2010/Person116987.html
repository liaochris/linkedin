Kim Le’s Summary

Kim Le is the CEO and Founder of A2Q2, a professional services firm specializing in accounting projects, business process reengineering, internal audit and Sarbanes-Oxley compliance. A2Q2 helps pre-IPO and publicly traded companies build efficient business processes to prevent and detect accounting errors and fraud.

Kim has over 15 years of accounting, auditing and consulting experience. Kim has led Sarbanes-Oxley Act compliance initiatives, financial statement audits, internal audits, special accounting and process re-engineering projects, initial public offerings, revenue recognition, Securities Exchange Ccommission reporting projects, and mergers and acquisition due diligence and integration. She has experience with technology,software, telecommunications, digital media, life sciences, distribution, not-for-profit and higher education industries. Her clients have included Ariba, Art.com, Autonomy, CNET Networks, Good Technology, Intuit, JDSU, Leapfrog, Motorola, Neoforma, Plumtree, Siebel Systems and Versant.

As the founder, Kim’s personal experiences with persistence, hard work and achievement are the foundation for the firm’s core values –integrity, professionalism and client service. Kim came from humble beginnings; she and her family were among the “boat people” from Vietnam who escaped to Malaysia. They spent a year in a refugee camp surviving on United Nation rations.

Kim supported herself through college where she earned a 4.0 overall GPA. Kim started her public accounting career with Arthur Andersen and stayed until 2002 when the Enron scandal occurred and the firm was shut down. From that experience, Kim decided to start her own consulting company. Kim’s personal struggles frame the way she handles professional challenges today. “I have always had to work hard to catch up and work even smarter to excel, so I thrive on challenges and achievement. When you are not living in a refugee camp, everything else is easy.”
