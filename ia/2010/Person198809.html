Sylvia Montgomery’s Summary

I help organizations gain immediate impact through their marketing. Most professional services organizations either don’t have an actionable marketing strategy or have a lengthy marketplace analysis that leaves them right where they started – not knowing what to do next. Whether your marketing messages need to better communicate your value proposition or whether you need to clearly differentiate your firm against real and perceived competitors, you need to be focusing on the activities that will bring you the desired outcome, and not just a list of to do’s that won’t grow your business.

Working with me, you get an unbiased outsider's perspective on your firm's brand and current marketing strategy.

What else will I bring to our engagement?

• High-level counsel on your brand and marketing programs
• Identification of relevant and measurable business marketing goals – what do you REALLY need and what is just a “bright shinny object” distracting progress
• Execution of marketing goals – I can execute on your firm’s behalf, or I can advise your existing staff -- think of me as your marketing accountability partner

What makes me qualified to trouble-shoot your marketing? I’ve have walked many miles in your shoes -- that’s why I have so many shoes in my closet ;-0

My career spans over 20 years in visual communications, strategy and marketing. I have done everything ranging from sole marketing person in an organization to overseeing marketing in a Fortune 500, and I have accomplished this for various technology to management consulting firms. Nothing gets me more jazzed than pulling it all together for an organization, building a brand and growing the business.

So, let’s grab a cup of coffee, roll up our sleeves and discuss how I can help your firm grow.

Sylvia Montgomery’s Specialties:

My specialties include:
• Identifying firms value proposition and key differentiators
• Developing actionable short term and long-term plans
• Overseeing the implementation of marketing initiatives
• Training technical professionals in their role as marketers
• Providing regular reports on the progress and efficacy of initiatives
• Establishing a marketing department or assisting in identifying appropriate staff positions
• Saving you money while bringing focus to your marketing efforts.
