Francisco Marco-Serrano’s Summary

► Francisco Marco-Serrano has a degree on Economics for the Universitat de València (Spain) and the Diploma d'Estudis Avançats (Advanced Studies Diploma) on Economic Analysis (postgraduate program that includes 210h worth of lessons plus a pre-doctoral dissertation) for the Departament d'Anàl.lisi Econòmica (Economics Department) of the same university.

► He has also completed the postgraduate course on Operations Management for the Universitat Oberta de Catalunya (Spain).

► He has devoted the second half of his academic life to analyse the culture from the point of view of economics, aka "cultural economics". For almost ten years he has been collaborating with the Unitat de Investigació en Economia de la Cultura (Cultural Economics Research Unit) of the Departament d'Economia Aplicada (Applied Economics Department) from the Universitat de València.

► His interests are the analysis of the processes (conventional or non-conventional ones) in order to gain the needed knowledge to improve their productivity. This is the fact that motivated his interests on the logistics-oriented fields from Operations Research.

► Currently he is looking for exciting new opportunities related to economics, business, and technology, including Interim Management or permanent senior positions, socio-economic analysis, and operations research applications development.

Francisco Marco-Serrano’s Education

  • Universitat Oberta de Catalunya

    Postgraduate , Operations Management , 2005 — 2006

    Lessons included subjects on the full supply chain management comprising procurement, production, warehousing, transport and distribution. The dissertation was on the efficiency of the procurement process considering all the cost-related variables ("Procurement Process Efficiency Analysis: Looking for a Managerial Monitoring Tool"), this is, the total cost of ownership and its integration into the Balanced Scorecard.

  • Universitat de València

    MA , Economic Analysis , 1999 — 2001

    Advanced Studies Diploma (Postgraduate program comprising 210h of lessons, plus a pre-doctoral dissertation). It was carried out in the Economics Department of the Universitat de València (Spain).

    Lessons included subjects on Advanced Macro & Microeconomics, Advanced Econometrics, Experimental Economics, and Cultural & Communication Economics. The dissertation was on cultural economics, related to the performing arts sector; "Efficiency Analysis in the Performing Arts Sector: The Case of the CTV (1995-1999)" dealt with the efficiency analysis of a Spanish theatres network, exploring the possibility of monitoring the performance of the cultural managers.

    Activities and Societies:
    During my MA studies I collaborated with the "Cultural Economics Research Unit" analysing several cultural sectors and issues (performing arts, museums, copyright, culture as a developmental tool, the macroeconomics of the culture, experimental economics applied to cultural research...).My collaboration followed further after my leaving from the University.

  • Universitat de València

    Lic. , Economics , 1995 — 1999
