Lillian Lincoln Lambert’s Summary

I speak on the power of persistence, resilience, courage and morality in overcoming hurdles that prevent people from reaching their full potential

As the first black woman to receive an MBA from Harvard Business School, I draw upon my experiences to show people how to use obstacles and barriers as stepping stones to higher levels of achievement and success. I understand the power of storytelling and use my personal story to inspire my audiences to dream big, act bold and pave their own paths.

My memoir, "The Road to Someplace Better: From the Segregated South to Harvard Business School and Beyond" was released by John Wiley & Sons in January 2010. It chronicles my journey to find my place in the world; a journey that had many twists, turns and its share of roadblocks.

Born on a farm in the segregated South, I sensed that a better life awaited me as I journeyed to New York to seek my fortune. I gutted out menial jobs as maid and typist before realizing that my journey was internal. Education was my ticket to a new world. With loans and scholarships, I enrolled in Howard University at the age of 22 and obtained a BA degree. In 1969, an era forever linked with the civil rights and burgeoning women’s right movements, I received an MBA from Harvard Business School.

After Harvard, I was President of Centennial One, Inc., a building maintenance company I founded in my garage on a few thousand dollars. The company grew to $20 million sales and 1,200 employees before being sold.

In 2003, Harvard Business School awarded me the Alumni Achievement Award, its highest award bestowed on alumni which recognizes recipients for "their contributions made to their companies and communities, while upholding the highest standards and values in everything they do."

In March 2010 I was inducted into Enterprise Women Magazine Hall of Fame, the magazine’s highest honor.
