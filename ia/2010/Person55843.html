Darian Rodriguez Heyman’s Summary

Darian Rodriguez Heyman has a history of building businesses and creating community. After five years of service, Darian recently stepped down as Executive Director of Craigslist Foundation, an organization dedicated to educating, empowering, and connecting emerging nonprofit leaders. While there, he helped launch Nonprofit Boot Camp, a one-day conference that quickly grew into the largest nonprofit gathering in San Francisco Bay Area history, and he also helped launch the Environmental Nonprofit Network and the Next Generation Leadership Forum. Heyman also served as a Commissioner for the Environment and Operations Committee Chair for the City and County of San Francisco, where he helped pass the largest solar rebate program in the country. For his work, Independent Sector labeled him a “Vital Voice” in the nonprofit sector in their 2007 annual report. Currently, Heyman is editing Nonprofit Management 101: A Field Guide for Social Sector Professionals, which will include contributions from 37 recognized nonprofit leaders. He recently worked with the Carbon War Room, an environmental nonprofit started by Sir Richard Branson to harness the power of entrepreneurs to solve climate change, and launched CODE Green Agency, which provides strategy, messaging, and fundraising for the green economy. Heyman advises the United Nations’ GAID program and sits on the board of Project Ahimsa, a global effort to empower children through music, and he helps produce the annual Power to the Peaceful festival in San Francisco, a free concert for peace and social justice that draws crowds of over 70,000.

  • Editor

    Nonprofit Management 101: A Field Guide for Social Sector Professionals

    (Publishing industry)

    June 2009 — Present (1 year 2 months)

    The easiest way to describe this project is to describe what it is not. This is not another book on how to start a nonprofit or social enterprise, nor is it focused on only one aspect of nonprofit management, i.e. fundraising, marketing, or technology. It is also not a book written by academics for students, and it is not a career guide for new entrants to the social sector. Instead, Nonprofit Management 101 is a compilation of insights and tips from leading practitioners spanning the complete spectrum of nonprofit management issues and disciplines. It will be written from the “if I knew then what I know now” perspective; our authors will talk about things they would have done differently or the same, along with resources, strategies, and tactics they wish they knew about then they started their careers. There will be an editorial thread woven throughout the book, as well as a common format, both of which will serve to connect these diverse topics related and issues.

    The book will be organized into sections or parts, such as fundraising, law and finance, and board and volunteer management. Each part will consist of 3-7 chapters, all addressing key issues within the various disciplines. 37 leading contributors are confirmed to provide content, and we have devised a standard chapter structure for the book, intended to ensure continuity across all the chapters, thereby enhancing the reader’s ability to use and digest the material. We've also confirmed over 40 promotional partners to spread the word when the book comes out in Q1 2011- this will be a true community effort.

  • Lecturer, UC Berkeley

    Haas School of Business

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    January 2009 — Present (1 year 7 months)

    Based on an award-winning curriculum developed by UC Berkeley undergraduates (Bears Breaking Boundaries Innovation Award 2009), The Economics of Philanthropy is offered for the first time in Spring 2010 by the Center for Nonprofit and Public Leadership at the Haas School of Business. The course is designed to teach participants how to be effective philanthropists. The class is open to business school and other UC Berkeley students. As a group, students will decide how to contribute $10,000 to nonprofit(s) they select as part of the course. The $10,000 contribution is a generous gift of the Sunshine Lady Foundation given for this purpose. To prepare for the contribution, students will engage in interactive exercises and discussions to determine how to best expend the funds for maximum impact, and they will be guided by the instructors and guest lecturers offering a variety of perspectives and frameworks. A second component of the course will be the class’s creation and delivery of a symposium on effective philanthropy for the broader UC Berkeley undergraduate community.

    Our mission is to create new business models that combat climate change.

    To achieve this, we:

    1. Conduct state-of-the-art intelligence in order to identify and inform systemic, gigaton-level solutions;

The challenge calls for a combination of responses that will mobilize capital, innovation, expertise, and international collaboration at an unprecedented speed and scale. A strategic nonprofit that understands innovation, entrepreneurship, and business is needed to accelerate change.
