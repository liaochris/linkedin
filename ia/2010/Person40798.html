  • Producer

    The CJC SPRAWL Project

    (Photography industry)

    2009 — 2010 (1 year )

    A photographic contest (sponsors currently being solicited) that will explore how individual motor transportation has effected the United States suburban and rural landscape development. The pros and cons of how this has affected communities dependent on private motor vehicles and how it has expanded onto the remaining green, open spaces that form our natural eco-system.

    What are novel models for sustainable communities in the new Millennium? How will (or do) we live? Can sprawl be a positive word? To exemplify and explore the concept of sprawl, we propose an interactive photography contest, offered to enthusiast, professional and student photographers, that include working with accredited photographic key educational institutions and urban planning institutions such as Project for Public Spaces (PPS). www.pps.org and photo manufacturers.

    Photographers & digital imaging creatives will be asked to photograph sprawl. Sprawl as based on two definitions posited by urban theorist Michael Lewyn:
    1.) Where we Grow: Sprawl as movement from the core to the fringe of a region
    2.) How we grow: Sprawl as development oriented towards drivers as opposed to non-drivers
