Carla Bolte’s Specialties:

Carla Bolte’s Experience

  • Senior Designer (Viking)

    The Penguin Group, USA

    (Public Company; Publishing industry)

    2000 — Present (10 years )

    Design all interior book elements for approximately 50-60 general adult trade titles yearly, some on very tight deadlines. Design and compose complex 4-color illustrated books. Assign and direct freelance designers, cartographers, and illustrators. Oversee all stages of composition and production. Coordinate design process with acquiring editors, managing editors, copy and production editors, production managers, jacket designers, publicity and subrights, and with outside suppliers. Author contact as appropriate. InDesign CS4, Photoshop CS4, Adobe Acrobat Professional, Microsoft Word.

    Prominent authors: Eric Alterman, T.C. Boyle, Douglas Brinkley, Daniel Dennett, Ceridwen Dovey, Lou Dobbs, Daniel Ellsberg, Vicente Fox, Garrison Keillor, Jack Kerouac (scroll edition of On the Road), John Leland, Andrea Mitchell, Greg Palast, Kevin Phillips, David Plouffe, Ahmed Rashid, Phillipe Sands, Daniel Schorr, Dava Sobel, Rebecca Solnit, Andrew Ross Sorkin, William T. Vollmann, Garry Wills; various commercial bestsellers.

  • Volunteer Advocate (while freelancing as designer)

    CASA: Court Appointed Special Advocates: New York, NY

    (Non-Profit; Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    1994 — 2000 (6 years )

    CASA volunteers are assigned by family court judges to investigate and monitor the circumstances of children in the foster care system. They interview law guardians, caseworkers, foster parents, natural parents, and various support agencies and individuals, either by phone or in person. CASAs then write and present reports and recommendations to family court judges. I worked in the Manhattan Family Court system.

  • Independent Professional

    freelance designer (Self-employed)

    (Self-Employed; Publishing industry)

    1985 — 2000 (15 years )

    Clients included most major NY publishers: Simon & Schuster, Doubleday, FSG, Putnam, Random House, Barnes & Noble, etc; Macmillan, Prentice Hall, Oxford University Press; smaller private publishers, packagers; corporate and individual clients. Textbooks and general adult trade. Some jacket design.

  • Senior Production Editor

    Cobb-Dunlop

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Publishing industry)

    1980 — 1985 (5 years )

    Production service/packager. Oversaw copyediting, design, and production of complex textbooks from manuscript to bound books, often on tight schedules; heavy volume; various publishing houses.

  • Production Editor

    Marcel Dekker

    (Publishing industry)

    1975 — 1980 (5 years )

    Research-level math and science books, international authors, oversaw manuscript to bound books, journal-style monographs and anthologies, either worked with author-supplied repro or designed with very limited typographical resources. Sample titles: series: The Encyclopedia of Electrochemistry of the Elements; monograph: Macrophage-Mediated Antibody-Dependent Cellular Cytotoxicity.

  • Production Editor

    Praeger Special Studies

    (Publishing industry)

    1974 — 1975 (1 year )

    Entry-level job, oversaw production of manuscript to bound book for nonfiction, academic lists.
