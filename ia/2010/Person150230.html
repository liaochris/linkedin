Murdo MacDonald’s Summary

I have considerable experience in the manufacturing and specialist services sector as an executive, start ups and management buy outs as well as managing large £100m businesses. My first board appointment was in a sheet metal fabrication company with three sites and seven hundred and fifty staff. Since then, I have worked in oil and gas, managed some of the UK's largest paper mills and converting operations, in the textile industry including carpet and yarn manufacture and a secure by design transport operation.

I use this experience to review your business and put forward possible solutions to the challenges you face that are causing you difficulties.

I qualified as a Chartered Accountant with the Institute of Chartered Accountants of Scotland and I use these accounting and tax skills to assist firms in setting up and managing finance and supply chain optimisation departments.In particular, I can set up book keeping and management accounting outsourcing where an in house facility does not exist. I also provide personal and corporate tax and general business advice.

Although I am a qualified accountant by profession, I have also been in senior general management for twenty five years
and I provide Financial Leadership and Management courses to key senior personnel for which some funding might be available providing eligible criteria is met. I act as a mentor with the Warrington Job Club providing advice to executives wishing to start up their own business.

I am a board director at one of the UK's largest housing association - they have circa 13,000 homes - and I am a member of their audit, operations as well as Chair of the trading services committees. This gives me a valuable insight into this sector.

I have an interest, through my wife, in health related charity and not for profit issues and in the use of online technologies in this field. Eileen is the recipient of a 2009 Positive Action Award for her voluntary work in the North West.
