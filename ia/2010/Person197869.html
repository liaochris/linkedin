Susan Linck’s Experience

  • Retail Planning and Allocations Manager/Core Inventory Control Analyst

    Crabtree & Evelyn

    (Privately Held; Retail industry)

    2002 — September 2009 (7 years )

    Crabtree and Evelyn is a mid-sized global personal care company established in the 70's.

    Role was to manage the inventory for all US stores for products shipped from the DC. This included setting inventory parameters in the AS400 computer and working with DM and store managers to answer questions, lend support and address individual needs.

    Transformed how the stores were stocked, and established effective working relationships with business partners that respected my knowledge and trusted me to solve problems in a way that benefited the company. Gathered and analyzed historical sales information to forecast sales of new items and promotional offers.

    Performed analysis to answer questions such as: what items can be quickly added as a promotional offer to drive transactions or traffic; team with merchant to develop sales plan and translate into demand plan; based on margin results of a promotion should we do it again? To make reporting efficient developed item hierachy and lead a data warehousing project that resulted in an intranet reporting tool for global markets.

  • Merchandise Allocations Analyst- Core

    Ames Department Stores

    (Retail industry)

    2000 — 2002 (2 years )

    Ames was the nation's largest regional discount Retailer, with close to 500 stores and $5B volume before being liquidated.

    All Allocations Analysts were put through a comprehensive training program to learn how to manage inventory to maximize turn and margin. A wonderful experience!

    Managed basic inventory with 3 replenishment systems (MMS, INFOREM, Ad Planning) using Ladder Plans in collaboration with Buyer(s). This included the in depth bi-annual POG set.

  • Store Manager, Softgoods Buyer

    Mystic Marinelife Aquarium

    (Retail industry)

    1996 — 1999 (3 years )

    At the time this was the #1 tourist attraction in CT and went through a major transformation during my tenure. I participated in the store design meetings with the architect.

    As a buyer I was responsible for a $1M OTB in softgoods and papergoods. This involved developing art for T-shirts and caps, negotiating terms and delivery schedule, and assortment planning. My area experienced significant volume increases and I was the only buyer to make and exceed initial sales goals in the new store.

    As Store Manager I managed the day to day operations of the store, whose mandate was to acheive sales of eduacational souvenirs while providing exceptional customer service for the visitors.

    With 6 registers in the main store and up to 4 registers placed in strategic locations in the outdoor exhibits the transaction volume was significant during the peak summer months. Industrial laundry carts were used to re-stock the store throughout the day. One of the unique skills learned in this environment is (friendly) crowd control as at peak times each register could have a dozen people waiting to make their purchases.

    The number of staff needed during the summer was around 65 and so training was a major task from February through June. Since most of the temporary staff were high school or college students one of my goals was to provide clear expectations with fair treatment and this significantly increased the number of returning summer staff.

  • National Sales Manager, Design Coordinator

    Michelle St. John Design Intl

    (Retail industry)

    1992 — 1996 (4 years )

    Company was a small contemporary women's clothing company at bridge price points.

    As National Sales Manager was responsible for managing the wholesale business which included growing sales and working with independent sales representatives. Attended trade shows, planned and worked trunk shows. Sales increased from $1.5M to $2.1M.

    Promoted to Design Coordinator where my responsibilities were to purchase fabric and trim, negotiate with and supervise cut and sew contractors, and assist designer/owner as needed.
