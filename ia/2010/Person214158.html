Eric Weaver’s Summary

For 19 years, I've had the pleasure of working at the intersection of culture, commerce & media as our society has become connected and digital. My career has been largely spent helping major brands understand this transformation, teaching them how to speak to and engage their audiences using digital tools.

I'm fortunate to have worn many hats: agency CEO, brand strategist, director of marcom, creative director, global account director, & even IT lead—but I'm my best when providing social/digital thinking to major brands. My clients have included Brita, BMW, Clorox, Ford, Kraft Foods, Lincoln, Mattel, the Mayo Clinic, Procter & Gamble, the Clinton Administration, & many others. Over the years, I've had fantastic opportunities: webcasting Ford CEO Jac Nasser, successfully pitching CEOs from Yahoo!, Mattel and Taco Bell, & having work reviewed by Bill Gates, Paul Allen, & Jack Welch.

Company/product launches have included the BMW MINI, Level 3 Communications, Ultra Cascade, the Lincoln LS, SumTotal Systems, the FlipStart PC, the Mercury Mountaineer and Sable, Sebastian, several pharma products and even FemStat3!

Many traditional outbound activities are struggling with reduced efficacy as consumers conserve time/attention, avoid marketing, and turn to search technologies & peer recommendation for company, product & purchase guidance. Given this power shift, our efforts must be relevant, brief, trustworthy and shared, or they become noise. My skill is in helping big brands understand and leverage this shift, establishing and presenting your value through digital and social tools. I've had mentions in AdAge, BusinessWeek, Fortune, Hoover's, the Washington Post, and even the National Review. I present frequently on social marketing, most recently in Mumbai at the 2009 World Brand Congress.

You'll find me quality-focused and easy to work with, along with other clients who have lauded me on my dedication, extra effort and honest counsel.
