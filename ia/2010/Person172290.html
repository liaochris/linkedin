Richard H Holt PE rholt@mrcovington.com’s Summary

After a 25 year career as a Professional Civil Engineer and I think that the greatest job in the world is being a recruiter that gets to work in the area of renewable energy, and EPC and general building construction. I get a kick from using my experience as a design engineer, construction project manager and construction company owner to counsel people and help them find jobs.

Every job that I have ever had has given me an opportunity to better understand both the needs of companies and the abilities of the key people that work for them. I like working with people that will take counsel and work with me in the recruiting process. Nothing is any worse than working with someone who is a know it all.

If you work in the area of renewable energy, and EPC and general building construction and are part of that unique 20% of the work force that usually contributes to 80% of a companies success then I would like the opportunity to speak to you.

    Alfa Laval Celleco

    (Construction industry)

    April 1995 — April 1998 (3 years 1 month)

    Alfa Laval was an OEM equipment manufacturer. They had a product line that had a good image within the pulp and paper industry and wanted to intergrate that equipment into recycle process lines in the industry. Their first venture was providing the process equipment that could remove and recycle apx 1000 TPD of mixed office waste from the landfill. Again, I had an opportunity to help the environment and I wanted to be involved in this cutting edge technology.

    I was the project executive responsibile for managing the detailed process design, equipment installation, start-up and testing of the primary process lines for the first (2) 100% mixed office waste, de-inked recycle pulp plants built by Alfa Laval Celleco.

  • Construction Manager

    Visy Paper, Inc

    (Construction industry)

    October 1993 — April 1995 (1 year 7 months)

    Visy Paper was an Australian company that advertised themselves as an endless urban landfill. They had the capability of removing 1000 TPD of discarded material from the waste stream and turn it into material that could be used to manufacture cardboard boxes. What a novel ideaI, I determined that this was an organization that saw opportunity to improve the environment.

    As an owner/operator they acted as their own E/P/C contractor and I was responsible for the Civil, Structural, Architect and Building Facilities portion of a 750 TPD 100% recycle old corregated container, multigrade linerboard plant. Lapse time from beginning of engineering drawings to paper production was 16 mo.
