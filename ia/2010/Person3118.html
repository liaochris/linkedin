Aditi Madhok-Naarden’s Experience

  • Head of Leadership and Talent

    Prudential Assurance

    (Public Company; Insurance industry)

    February 2010 — Present (4 months)

    Following a recent reorganisation of the HR function in the UK and Europe business, the Resourcing and Talent team was restructured in to the Leadership and Talent team. As the Head of Leadership and Talent for UK & Europe, Aditi reports directly to the HR Director and heads a team of two Leadership and Talent Advisers and two Resourcing Managers. She currently manages an annual budget of £1.18 million and her main accountabilities and accomplishments in the role include:

    Ownership of the identification and development of talent at all levels of the organisation, with personal intervention at the leadership and executive level

    Aditi is responsible for running the Leadership Development Programmes (LDPs) and Management Development Programmes (MDPs) in UK & Europe and also acts as an assessor at both the LDPs and MDPs run by the Prudential Group Head Office and Prudential Corporation Asia.

    She also oversees the management of the ‘Momentum’ Raw Talent programme in the UK business.

    Aditi assesses senior managers for selection and development. Aditi is accredited by BIOSS International to use the Modified Career Path Appreciation (MCPA) and Initial Recruitment Interview Schedule (IRIS) tools for senior and raw talent recruitment.

    She also facilitates the annual Group Leadership Review for the UK & Europe, working closely with the CEO, HR Director and other members of the Executive Committee to maintain and measure effective succession plans at the top two levels of the business

  • Head of Resourcing and Talent

    Prudential Assurance

    (Public Company; Insurance industry)

    July 2009 — February 2010 (8 months)

    This role reported to the Resourcing and Development Director and headed a team of ten managers and professionals in HR. Aditi managed an annual budget of £2 million and her main accountabilities and accomplishments in the role included:

    Merging the Resourcing team with the Talent, Succession & Performance team to provide a more integrated recruitment, talent management and succession planning offering to the business by increasing cross-business brokerage, internal moves, and secondments. The aim of this integration was to better manage talent turnover and mitigate succession risk in the UK business.

    Leading a recently in-sourced Resourcing team to provide a more value-added recruitment service to internal customers

    Overseeing mid and end year People Reviews across all areas of the business. The Performance Management Audit that was conducted in 2009 saw a positive shift in 17 out of the 18 questions asked to 40% of the population about the annual performance management process and practice

    Running the annual Employee Survey to measure the climate of the company. In 2009, the Survey response rate saw an exponential increase to 86% of all employees.

  • HR Business Partner - Corporate Solutions and Retail Life & Pensions

    Prudential Assurance

    (Public Company; Insurance industry)

    June 2007 — July 2009 (2 years 2 months)

    This role reported to the People Director and provided a strategic HR generalist business partner service to the two revenue-generating areas in the UK business. It involved line management of a junior HR business partner. Aditi’s main achievements included:

    Acting as an integral member of the Senior Management Teams in both these areas of the business by working very closely with the Managing Directors of these business areas to increase revenue, profitability and shareholder value. Aditi’s main responsibility was to tailor the corporate HR strategy to help enable the businesses to successfully deliver their operational targets.

    Building a high-performing organisation by ensuring that Prudential people policies and best practice were being applied consistently across the business. Aditi’s key success was creating “buy in” from senior managers in the sales and distribution areas.

    Creating an ‘HR Virtual Team’ consisting of HR specialists from Employee Relations, HR Shared Services, Learning & Development, Resourcing, Reward and Talent, Succession & Performance to increase commercial awareness in the HR Centres of Excellence and, thereby, provide an improved HR service. This model has now been replicated for the Operations and Shared Services functions.

    Managing numerous business reorganisations and restructures of varying sizes and complexity. None of these business change programmes resulted in any appeals or grievances from employees impacted.

  • Manager – Pan-European HR Commercial Engagements & Alliances

    IBM

    (Public Company; IBM; Information Technology and Services industry)

    October 2005 — June 2007 (1 year 9 months)

    This role reported to the HR Leader – Commercial Engagements & Alliances (IBM United Kingdom, Ireland & South Africa). My main accomplishments in this position included:

I led HR engagements for both Business Transformation/Managed Business Process Service Outsourcing as well as Strategic Outsourcing contracts that range up to a total value of USD 53.2 million.

In 2006, I led a complicated divestiture of Mechanical and Electrical engineers from IBM’s Business Continuity & Recovery Services business to a third party strategic partner.
    • Managing Cultural Change Management for employees transferring into IBM from clients. I ran cultural change management workshops in the UK as part of IBM’s USD 865 million acquisition of Micromuse Inc.
    • Contributing to the highest standard of teaming within both the IBM HR CE&A team as well as the overall IBM HR team. I trained the entire IBM UK HR Transition team on HR Engagement and, in January 2007, at the request of IBM’s Senior Vice President - Human Resources, I spent a month in IBM India helping transform the Staffing function there by leading a Measurements and Metrics project.
