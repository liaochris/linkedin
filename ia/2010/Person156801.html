Jacques François Joosten’s Summary

June 2010: Oceanic Art Gallery "François de St. Jacques" has five specialised webshops operational for the five distinct product groups that may be identiefied.
One shop for wooden artifacts from Papua New Guinea. Wooden ancestor sculptures, mythic bird statues, masks and mythic shields.
The second shop for exclusive, authentic, ready to wear, tribal jewellery from the Solomon Islands and wealth ornaments and traditional money from Papua New Guinea.
The third shop is dedicated to exclusive weaponry. Bone daggers from the Papua. Keris from Indonesia, katana (samurai swords) from Japan and original, used boomerang from Aboriginal in Australia.
The fourth shop for Far East Fine Art. Ming and Song porcelain and Neo Lithic (BC 2500-4000) Jade from China.
The fifth is a dedicated life style home decoration shop, selling "Mola", textile tribal art form from the Kuna Indians, San Blas archipelago (Panama) and "Tapa" bark cloth paintings from the Papua. All modestly framed.

The Art Gallery activities may be extended with expositions on exclusive sites a.o. high level business environments, i.e. business premises and top hotels in The Netherlands (major cities), Belgium (Brussels) and Germany (Ruhrgebiet, Köln, Frankfurt am Main).
