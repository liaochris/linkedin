Cathy Brown’s Summary

My name is Cathy Brown. I was born and raised in Wisconsin, the oldest of seven children. That's right, seven! I attended St. Joseph's Catholic School in Rice Lake, Wisconsin. For two years we lived on a farm and I attended a one-room school house. Wisconsin is a beautiful part of the country. In the fall, the colors are gorgeous. I have also lived in San Diego, Phoenix, Arizona and Minneapolis.

I pursued a college degree in education throughout my travels and graduated from the University of Minnesota with Honors in 1982. I have spent most of the time since then teaching and running a private school. I love children and I love animals.

I have four children of my own and am the proud gradparent of two grandchildren. We have also had numerous pets from the time I was a child until just recently. Our last pet was Kipper, a beautiful calico cat who lived with us for 20 years. We had to have her put to sleep when she contracted a debilitating illness and we still miss her.

In October, 2007 one of my sisters, Susie had to euthanize her beloved Sweet Pea to keep her from suffering from advanced kidney disease. She and her husband decided that they wanted her cremated. Her vet gave them a catalog of urns to choose from. Although the choice was small there was a wood urn that they liked. When it came to picking the inscription for this tribute to Sweet Pea's life Susie just did not want to stand there and think about it. She wanted to go home and sob for their loss. Consequently they ordered the urn without an inscription for the name plate. When the urn arrived, they had to take the name plate to a jeweler for the inscription and it cost them over $40.

On one of our "sister weekends", two of my awesome sisters and I found the solution for Susie's dilemma with Ever My Pet. "We Three Sisters" (the name of our company) decided that we should go into business together. We wanted to be our own bosses and own our own business. Voila--Ever My Pet!
