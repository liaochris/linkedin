Pradeep Raman’s Experience

  • Innovation Manager

    Newcastle Science City

    (Privately Held; Think Tanks industry)

    August 2009 — Present (11 months)

    Newcastle Science City is setup by the Newcastle University, Newcastle City Council and One North East to bring new enterprise and economic prosperity to the North East of England. The vision includes an ambitious target of starting 40 new ventures in 3 years. A key project - Newcastle Innovation Machine or NIM employs entrepreneurial individuals as Innovation Managers and supports them with funding, advice, business tools and networks in order to form their own new companies.

    I have investigated business opportunities ranging from renewable energy technologies, human capital management software, digital signage networks and online vertical advertising networks.

    Presently I am building a new venture to place contextual advertisements within images online. See www.adtronik.com

  • CEO

    English-to-go

    (Internet industry)

    July 2007 — July 2008 (1 year 1 month)

    English-To-Go provides English language training software to over 80,000 individuals, schools, colleges and universities around the world. Backed by No8 Ventures, a leading New Zealand venture capital house, English-to-go.com and Selfaccess.com focus on a variety of language training needs. From topical lesson plans and resources to automated software used in self access labs, ETG caters to the British Council, Higher Colleges of Technology UAE, Nagoya University Japan, Griffith University Australia and several other reputed organisations. Progressively the company focused on high value English For Special Purposes programmes starting with a very successful English for Migrants course.

  • Co-founder

    Anonymouse.co.nz

    (Internet industry)

    September 2006 — February 2008 (1 year 6 months)

    Anonymouse, previously called Myskillpool was built to offer passive jobseekers the ability to remain open to great job offers in complete anonymity. Winning the 2006 Spark 40K Challenge at the University of Auckland Business School gave us the opportunity to build this venture from New Zealand's best business incubator, The Icehouse. A strategic partnership with the Employers and Manufacturers Association and Business New Zealand enabled rapid growth to 2000 jobseekers and 1000 employers within 3 months. The company was acquired by QJumpers shortly thereafter.

  • Network Surveillance Engineer

    Woosh Wireless

    (Privately Held; Telecommunications industry)

    September 2004 — September 2006 (2 years 1 month)

    Woosh Wireless provides wireless broadband to residential and corporate users. As Network Surveillance Engineer my role was in network planning, monitoring and maintenance. While in this role I acheived a Cisco Certified Network Professional (CCNP) qualification. I enjoyed watching a young company grow rapidly and understanding the dynamics of the corporate world. I entered Woosh as an intern working on Voice over IP (VoIP) technologies and helped launch their voice communications service.

  • Co-founder

    Virtualforce Online

    (Internet industry)

    January 1999 — May 2004 (5 years 5 months)

    This venture was my very first introduction to Entrepreneurship. At barely 17, a group of 5 guys we launched Virftualforce Online one of New Zealand's first web hosting and web development companies. This was done as a Fletcher Challenge Young Enterprise project at Kelston Boys High School. Two of us - Dharshana Ratnayake and I ended up running the company for nearly 6 years giving it part time effort while we studied Engineering at the University of Auckland. We sold the company in 2004 before starting our first real corporate jobs.
