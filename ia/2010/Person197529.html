Supriyo Chaudhuri’s Summary

The story of my career, spanning 17 years and played out in three different countries, was about experiencing and inducing change in organizations.

I have had the unique privilege of being a close participant and observer of the two recent waves of globalization. First, in 1990s, I was enabling companies go online and watched from close quarters the advent of Internet and the globalization of ideas; then in the new millennium, I worked on the frontiers of global trade in services and witnessed greater global integration of work and skills.

This experience made me what I am: open, to ideas and ways of thinking; multicultural and flexible, learning oriented and positive about change.

I have always loved what I did and sought out things to learn. My work was never mundane - it was always a combination of an adventure, an interesting education opportunity and a bit of fun.

And, I indeed had fun. I took offbeat turns and did many things, but always contributed positively. And, in turn, each job I held helped me a bit to become a better person, a better professional.

Now, I want to get involved in building a great organization, which will change the lives and thoughts of people and make them ready for the post-recession world. We are heading into an age which will scarred by the experiences of the current recession, but will be more global, with a multi-polar economy and based on truely integrated global value chain.

For this emerging reality, we shall need to realign organizational skills and view the opportunities with a fresh pair of eyes. I do feel that my unique, asymmetric experience has suitably prepared me for this oncoming Globalization 3.0.

  • Trustee Board Member

    (Higher Education industry)

    September 2009 — Present (8 months)

    Business & Education London South (BELS) exists to advance the education and training of young people and adults, mainly, but not exclusively, in South London.
