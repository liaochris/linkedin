Chris Norton’s Summary

I have more than eleven years’ experience in the PR industry having worked both in-house and in a number of consultancies. I am PR and social media blogger and I write on more than ten different blogs on a variety of topics from Twitter tools, data management through to IT Support.

In my previous role, as Head of Campaign Strategy at Wolfstar I headed up several account teams and in Jan 2009 we picked up the CIPR's award for Small Outstanding Consultancy of the Year.

My experience spans corporate communications for many industry sectors, including IT, healthcare, education, business-to-business, FMCG, retail, charities and public sector.

I graduated in 2000 and started my career working for Harvard PR, where I gained a wealth of corporate communications experience working for clients such as APACS, One.Tel, Pentax, Lucent and Northcliffe Newspapers

The following year I was head hunted by Harvard Health. Here I worked for a variety of consumer healthcare accounts including Johnson & Johnson, GSK, Pfizer, Merck, Meat & Livestock Commission and Beiersdorf. I delivered a number of high profile campaigns including the ‘Creation of the Cold & Flu Council’ for Beechams which won several industry awards.

I joined Trimedia in October 2003. At Trimedia I was responsible for planning, directing and delivering a variety of public relations programmes for clients including Institute of Directors, Land Rover, Learning & Skills Council, Lloyds TSB autolease, Camelot, the Dickie Bird Foundation, Bradford Council, National Lottery Promotions Unit, Leeds Chamber of Commerce, Department of Culture, Media and Sport, Home Office, Mitchell’s & Butlers and several projects for BT.

I also spent three months seconded to Learndirect business' marketing department to provide strategic advice and help establish an effective in-house communications role.

Currently I work for big brands like Audio Technica, George Foreman, Russell Hobbs, Lucre Social and Hallmark Cards.
