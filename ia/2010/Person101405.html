Johanna Moore’s Summary

Over twenty seven years of experience working as an artist, scene painter, faux finisher, gilder; conservation and restoration of gilded objects; art handler; gallery owner. (I received my first painting commission when I was in the 8th grade :-)) Pinhole Photographer. Fine Art Painter. Broad experience working with contemporary artists designing presentations for exhibits. Collaborate with interior designers and architects to create unique options for corporate and private clients. Continue to research the history of gilding, gilding techniques, history of frame makers in America and in Maine in particular. Independent research in American Art History. Attended many Professional Development Seminars through Sepp Leaf in New York and the Society of Gilders: seminars for gliding, carving, conservation, restoration, and finishing techniques with Carole Halle, Pascal Patris, Giovanni Bucchi, William Gaultier, Deborah Bigelow(Fellow AIC), Stanley Robertson, Howard Serlick, Bill Adair, and Marty Horowitz
I am always expanding the boundaries of my craft.

Johanna Moore’s Specialties:

design, collaboration with interior designers, architects, artists, collectors, ability to draw on knowledge for design sensitivity to historic or contemporary settings, appraisals, collections management. Examination and condition reports for art collections. Can link collectors with conservation professionals and/or designers suitable for the client's needs.
