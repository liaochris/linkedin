  • Senior Vice President, Publisher Credit Ratings and Research

    Standard & Poor's

    (Public Company; MHP; Financial Services industry)

    2001 — 2009 (8 years )

    Led the $200 million digital publishing business that creates credit ratings and research products for financial market participants. With full P&L responsibility, set long term strategy; identified product opportunities; directed the development of new products; provided leadership to the development of new markets; and oversaw product management, editorial, creative services, operations, electronic and print production, information technology, marketing and customer service. Managed a team of 200 across 14 countries.

    • Grew the subscription base from $50 million to $200 million over eight years, a 22% annual growth rate
    • Improved the net operating margins from zero to 44% over the eight year period
    • Created upward momentum – 2009 growth rate was 26%, margin 51%
    • Expanded the global footprint of the client base to 47% from non-Americas sources
    • Consistently delivered major software releases, innovating features and functions with “agile” product development techniques
    • Led the transformation of the flagship web credit research product into a unique web 2.0 styled credit portal, becoming the source of major product differentiation among ratings agencies and the next five years’ growth
    • Expanded the value of the ratings data resource tenfold to $75 million in annual contract value

Artnet is an internet B2B fine art marketplace and e-commerce business traded on the Deutsche Boerse.

    • Initiated financial reporting, institutional investor relations and roadshows for the newly-public company

Additionally, assessed the business plans from potential purchase candidates and ventures seeking investment from Reuters. Provided oversight of the venture-oriented Greenhouse Fund.

    • Identified best merger candidates, assessed strategic fit, evaluated potential contribution, assessed defenses and anti-competition issues, monitored events. Presented findings to the Executive Committee
    • Researched expansion in complementary fields including data, analytics, connectivity solutions, risk management, services to the investment management community, and trading networks.
