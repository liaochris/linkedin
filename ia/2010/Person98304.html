Jim Delaney’s Summary

A veteran team and arena communications executive with a track record of developing successful marketing communications campaigns. Currently working primarily with teams, events, venues and sponsors to build their brands and revenues through integrated creative communications and technology-driven activation programs.

Jim Delaney’s Specialties:

Integrated Creative Communications, Strategic PR, Sports/Event Marketing, Media Relations, Corporate Partnership Development, Interactive Communications, Sponsorship Activation

Jim Delaney’s Experience

  • President

    Activate Sports & Entertainment, LLC

    (Public Relations and Communications industry)

    September 2006 — Present (3 years 6 months)

    :: Lead a team of experienced marketing communications professionals in delivering brand-building and revenue-generating solutions for a client base of primarily teams, events, venues and sponsors. We're a creative activation posse specializing in brand communications, sports/event PR, social media integration, event management and sponsorship activation... and yes, we also occasionally execute some really cool digital/interactive projects too.

    :: We're honored to have worked with a number of tremendous brands over the past several years, developing and executing activation playbooks for companies such as the MA Film Office, MA Sports Partnership, The Life is good Company, Boston Athletic Association (Boston Marathon & U.S. Olympic Marathon Trials), Delaware North Companies, AEG Facilities, Sponsor Direct, T.T. Bud's Popcorn, Newark's Prudential Center, Hartford's XL Center, The Thomas Kinkade Companies, Event & Arena Marketing Conference, Boston Bruins, City of Boston's Office of Special Events and Red Bull North America, among others.

    :: In recent years, we have also tried to help where we can with some great non-profit organizations, including Good Sports (www.goodsports.org), Cradles to Crayons (www.cradlestocrayons.org), and Tedy Bruschi's Train To End Stroke (www.runtoendstroke.org). We admire the amazing work that they do, particularly for children in need. Wish we were more like them.

  • Director of Marketing & PR

    TD Banknorth Garden

    (Public Company; Entertainment industry)

    2000 — 2006 (6 years )

    :: Managed the advertising, promotions, publicity and community efforts of New England’s top sports and entertainment venue. During that time, the venue consistently placed among the Top 5 highest-grossing arenas of its size in the world.

    :: Led the marketing/pr efforts for the arena's extensive schedule of games, concerts, family shows and special functions, as well as some of the region's most high-profile events, including the 2004 Democratic National Convention, U.S. Figure Skating Championships, U.S. Olympic Gymnastics Trials, NCAA Frozen Four, NCAA Women's Final Four, NCAA March Madness 1st and 2nd Rounds, Wrestlemania XIV, etc.

    :: Oversaw the award-winning marketing communications efforts for the re-branding of the FleetCenter to the TD Banknorth Garden.

Fox, Michelle Kwan, Mickey Mouse, Tony Hawk, Dwayne 'The Rock' Johnson, Stone Cold Steve Austin, and even the Dalai Lama among others.

  • Media Relations Consultant

    New England Patriots

    (Sports industry)

    December 1996 — January 1997 (2 months)

    Upon first moving to Boston in '96 and before getting the FleetCenter job, worked with the talented New England Patriots PR group, handling media relations efforts and managing press conferences for then-coach Bill Parcells and top players during the team's march to Super Bowl XXXI.

  • Assistant Director of Marketing & Communications

    Washington Sports & Entertainment

    (Privately Held; Sports industry)

    1992 — 1996 (4 years )

    Stints in both the Marketing & PR departments of the Washington Bullets (now Wizards).

    :: On the marketing side, managed the day-to-day advertising and promotions, including a leader on the team's Promotions Cabinet, developing innovative marketing and customer-focused initiatives. A key player in an organization-wide marketing effort that increased attendance from 9,000 per game to 17,500 per game in five years.

    :: On the PR side, served as a primary liaison between the local/nat'l sports media and the organization/players. Managed the PR both at home and on the road for high-profile players such as Chris Webber, Juwan Howard, Rasheed Wallace and Gheorghe Muresan.
