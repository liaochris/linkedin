  • Budget Manager

    Naval Supply Systems Command

    (Military industry)

    June 1984 — May 2001 (17 years )

    Budget manager for US Navy and USMC worldwide requirements for helicopter parts including the H-1, H-3, H-46, H-53 and H-60 helicopters and the V-22 Tiltrotor Osprey. Also budget manager for all ground support equipment (yellow gear and automatic test equipment). Total value of yearly procurement and repair budgets exceeded a half billion dollars. Used complex math models to forecast future requirements for new aircraft and avionics systems entering the fleet. Participated in budget hearings in support of Naval Aviation Logistics held by DoD and Congressional budget auditors. Worked with mainframe applications and Excel, Word and Powerpoint.

  • Systems Analyst

    Naval Supply Systems Command (NAVICP)

    (Military industry)

    June 1981 — June 1984 (3 years 1 month)

    I was the systems analyst/ statistician in charge preparing all command level performance statistics. Monthly raw data was generated from mainframe applications. I prepared various internal reports showing material availability rates for each aircraft, engine and other aviation systems. I prepared narrative cover letters analyzing the data to be signed by our CO and forwarded to Navy and DoD commands. I prepared an 80 page monthly Powerpoint presentation called the "Key Indicators Brief" that was presented to the entire command executive board including the SES civilians, department directors, the XO and comptroller (Navy Captains) and the CO, a Rear Admiral.

  • Logistics Manager Team Leader

    Naval Supply Systems Command (NAVICP)

    (Military industry)

    June 1978 — June 1981 (3 years 1 month)

    I was the logistics manager for the General Electric F404 and Rolls Royce F402 jet engines used on F-18 Hornets and AV-8B Harrier Jump Jets. I prepared procurement and repair computations for the major systems on these 2 engines and reviewed all the computations prepared by my team members. I attended Requirements Review Boards for all requirements on these 2 engines that exceeded $1M and on many occasions had $5M requirements presented to the CO, a Rear Admiral. I attended budget hearings to defend my computed requirements submitted to DoD and Congressional Budgets. I attended the semi-annual Logistics Support meetings held at GE in Lynn MA, MCAS Cherry Point NC, NAS Lemoore CA and at NAVSUP HQs in Crystal City/Washington and Mechanicsburg PA. I gave powerpoint presentations at these meetings and headed the Spare Parts sub-team meetings.

  • Logistics Manager

    Naval Supply Systems Command (NAVICP)

    (Military industry)

    July 1972 — June 1978 (6 years )

    I was the logistics and inventory manager for 1400 parts used on the A-4 Skyhawk attack aircraft. (This is the aircraft in which John McCain was shot down in Viet Nam and became a POW). The parts I managed included the Bendix brake systems, Constant Speed Drives, Emergency Air Generators and various other airframe and hydraulic parts. The aircraft was flown by the US Navy, USMC, Israel, New Zealand, Argentina and Kuwait. I had daily communications by phone and other telecommunications systems with fleet supply departments. I prepared all requirements computations for these items and presented them to higher level requirements review boards. I also defended my computed requirements at budget stratification reviews. I maintained contact with contractors and repair facilities to coordinate and expedite delivery of material.
