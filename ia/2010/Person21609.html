Ben Hansen’s Summary

Analyst - Designer - Engineer - Inventor ______ User Experience Strategist

PROFILE
Ben makes things work.
Quiet and unassuming at first Ben is a listener and a jack-of-all-trades. He’s eager to help, and if he doesn’t know how to do it, he’s dependable to figure it out or find the people who can. He conceived an integrated set of four programs to care for the needs of the customer using Sun Microsystem’s worldwide web. As Head of Web Usability and Customer Feedback, Ben used the Customer LifeCycle, Usability, Best Practice, and Customer Feedback programs to improve the web site rating for Usability and Site Effectiveness from #10 to #1, according to independent consultant, Summit Strategies.

Prior to Sun, Ben “visioneered” a variety of products and services in telecommunications at U S WEST Advanced Technologies, from interactive TV and mobile technologies to the emerging Internet. Granted several US Patents, he is the named inventor of the "Task Bar" (#5,821,930) and was UI lead developing a method for the Geospatial Presentation of Complex Operational Data, guiding VPs to quickly make informed decisions regarding the strategic use of limited resources.

Ben enriched the Marketing Resources Group managing product development teams related to interactive media information and entertainment products such as tickets for AMC movies and Colorado Rockies Baseball. Managing multiple, simultaneous projects quickly resulted in cost savings of more than $4.5M.

As head chef, Ben lead a team serving dinners to President George H.W. Bush and Carl XVI Gustaf, King of Sweden.

Diverse in education, Ben has a BA in Theology, graduated Summa Cum Laude with a BS in CIS, and holds an ME in Software Engineering. He is a Certified HF/UX Professional. He authored and taught the MS program for Regis University in Multi-Media Development which lead to today’s MS e-Commerce program. Ben has also lead several “small groups” facilitating a variety of topics in the community.

Ben Hansen’s Specialties:

Thinking "outside the box". Program envision, creation and management. Teaching, training and mentoring. Data collection design and analysis (quantitative and qualitative). Interviewing. Independent, team-based, and participatory design. UI, system, information and content architecture design. Writing, educational and technical. Team, vendor and program management.
