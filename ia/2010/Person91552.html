Jay Williams’s Summary

Jay is a Group Manager in the Weber Shandwick healthcare practice focused on Genentech oncology – specifically on Rituxan. He has had a history in a variety of disease categories over the last few years. Prior to Weber Shandwick, he has worked on GSK’s diabetes treatment, Avandia, coordinating data announcements, media briefings, developing press materials and developing strategies for issues management. He has also provided media relations support for Advair (asthma), supported educational programs for Novo Nordisk, and has coordinated strategic planning for Elan’s Alzheimer’s disease initiatives. Jay has most recently coordinated campaigns for GSK pediatric and adult vaccines, successful media tours and national surveys on adolescent immunizations. Jay has also been part of the team for Merck’s first-of-it’s-kind HIV treatment, Isentress – having seen the drug through many data announcements, HIV/AIDS events, conferences, grassroots consumer education programs and FDA approval.

Jay has worked on Merck’s cervical cancer vaccine, Gardasil, GSK’s prostate enlargement treatment, Avodart, and AstraZeneca’s treatment for bipolar disorder, Seroquel.

Jay has had experience in medical and disease areas including oncology, HIV, Alzheimer’s disease, diabetes, prostate enlargement (BPH), medical devices, respiratory, growth hormones, pediatric vaccines and bipolar disorder.

Jay graduated from Cornell University with a Bachelor of Science in Communications and a focus on Campaign Planning and Evaluation. During his undergrad years he was the director of the student run PR agency at Cornell as well as an award winning member of PRSSA, taking home the 2004 PRSA-NY PRSSA Recognition Award For Outstanding Commitment to the PR Profession. Currently, Jay is finishing up his M.S. in Strategic Communication at Columbia University for 2010.
