Michael Alfieri, P.G., P.Hg., CGWP’s Summary

Mr. Alfieri is a professional geologist/hydrogeologist with twelve years of experience throughout the continental United States. Professionally registered and certified in the fields of hydrogeology and geology, he is currently licensed to practice geology in nine states. He is a certified ground water professional through the NGWA and is one of only twelve practicing hydrogeologists in the State of Florida registered as a professional hydrogeologist through the AIH. As required under licensure laws and statues, Mr. Alfieri has conducted and/or overseen the design of hydrogeologic investigations and the collection of field data for water resource investigations, environmental investigations, and remediation projects. He has also provided in-depth evaluations and third-party reviews of hydrogeologic conditions based on published data, groundwater elevation monitoring data, and results from aquifer testing, groundwater modeling, and chemical analyses.

Mr. Alfieri has extensive water supply and development experience. Over the last four years, he has been heavily involved with many supply projects within Florida and the surrounding states. Specifically related to water supply, he has experience in hydrogeologic/geologic evaluations and feasibility studies, permitting, well/wellfield scopes of work, well/wellfield design and construction management, well/wellfield optimization, impact analyses, aquifer performance testing, engineering report preparation, and the preparation and submittals of permit exemptions. He is a contributing author to the NGWA’s Manual of Water Well Standards (in progress) and was also a committee member of the Advisory Committee on Water Information/Water Information Coordination Program Sub-Committee on Ground Water Data Standards and Data Management Work Group. He was the Inaugural Chair of the NGWA’s Deep Groundwater Investigations Interest Group as well as a former Chair of the NGWA’s Groundwater Modeling Interest Group.

Classical hydrogeology, classical geology, applied and quantitative hydrogeology, well construction and construction management, water well efficiency improvements, groundwater resource evaluations/ economics and permitting, groundwater modeling, contaminant fate and transport modeling, site assessment/remediation, branding identity, business development
