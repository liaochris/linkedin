Mark Bottita’s Summary

10+ years teaching communication and marketing at universities and colleges in IN, MI, MA. Currently part-time faculty at Purdue University North Central and Mendoza College of Business at Notre Dame. ND faculty bio: http://business.nd.edu/Faculty_Directory/MarkBottita/

As a result of my experiences with non-traditional-aged students in my undergrad classes, I recently founded Elder Forest Publishing Group (EFPG). Its focus is how communication and social interaction impact wellness in aging. EFPG offers solutions that help lifelong learners (and the firms serving them) sharpen these skill-sets and increase the quality of their professional and social interaction. Books, periodicals, curricula, etc are being planned and tested now. Recently, I refused an offering of funding because it would inhibit a more substantial first-round financing effort.

I've continued to nurture my academic work as the base from which I have researched and developed my business ideas, and I am currently seeking a visiting position in communication (interpersonal, mass media, speech com) or marketing (undergraduate courses, entrepreneurship, web 2.0). I would also welcome a temporary/short-term (2- or 3-year) administrative appointment with a teaching or advising component. I truly enjoy the challenge of teaching, and realize that the greatest learning environment is one which transcends one's abilities in the classroom. Beyond great teaching, students look for camaraderie; the few moments of genuine interaction in a hallway discussion, the true sincerity in the email or phone call in response to a question or deeper problem. They want life mentors. I have been amazed at what students can accomplish when they feel as though faculty truly see them as capable and show true empathy for their situations, for their abilities, and their promise. 10 years' worth of superior teaching evaluations are available.

Mark Bottita’s Specialties:

Interpersonal communication, public (speech) communication, mass media (designed/taught a course on mass media, crime and criminal justice that bridges theory and practice for criminal justice system), marketing/web 2.0 marketing.
