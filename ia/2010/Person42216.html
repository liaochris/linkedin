Joseph Crawford’s Summary

I started out with the internet in 1994 when i bought my first computer with my own money. I started getting into web development in 1997 by viewing the source code for HTML and then picking up ASP. About a year after using ASP I found PHP and decided to make the switch.

I have been using PHP since 1998. I started to really get involved with web development while creating and managing my own websites. I hit the books hard and started reading everything I could find on website development. I decided not to focus on anything design related but to go for the back-end development.

My library has grown over the years and I have learned so much from all of the dedicated authors who have written the books.

In 2005 I started up my own company Codebowl Solutions, Inc. While it was successful I decided to grab a full-time position. Most of my positions have been remote positions where I work from home, however the more recent positions have been in-house. I like working both from home and in the office.

    LicketyShip, Inc.

    (Privately Held; Computer Software industry)

    May 2006 — August 2006 (4 months)

    Developer however so much work came up and they had me hire and supervise 4 other developers under the company Codebowl Solutions. I managed the team to meet the short deadlines they had and when we finished the site was on a solid path to success. This site was 100% custom PHP 5 which made use of external web services and had a lot of calculations.

    LicketyShip is a same day delivery service provider. You could go on their site and place an order for products that you needed. They would have them delivered to your home or office within 2-4 hours and the prices for delivery started around $20.

    All of the products on the site were scoured from a partner company by screen scraping with permission. The partner company wanted to see just how much being a part of this delivery service would increase its revenue before having their developers create an API for consumption. In order to accurately calculate the delivery cost for the products we had to get the actual turn by turn directions and calculate the total street miles vs using a radius based on zip code like most companies do on their sites. Once we had the total street miles we were able to run calculations based on the couriers cost per mile, cost per minute wait time, etc. We used these calculations to choose the best delivery provider and shortest route for the customer so that costs were kept down. In order to alert the couriers we also had to integrate with an online faxing service so that a fax would be sent to the courier notifying them of the scheduled delivery.
