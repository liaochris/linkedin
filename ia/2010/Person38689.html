C. Christian Bizarth’s Summary

Currently, I am a freelance graphic designer and a manager of The Genesis Group d.b.a. Title Loan Express. I am under contract with the City of Birmingham to brand City District 2, the district in which I live, with street banners. Through the efforts of City Councilor Carol Duncan to revitalize her district's business districts and neighborhoods, I was hired to create a visual catalyst to instill pride in four neighborhoods' residents.

East Lake and Roebuck Springs neighborhood banners are installed. My responsibilities included banner design, design review presentation, budgeting, vendor selection, banner site placement and installation management.

The Roebuck and Eastwood Business District banners that I have created will be produced at the discretion of our newly elected city councilor. Ten years of management experience emphasizing attention to detail was a key factor in my sale of the banners that are currently installed. I have sharpened my interpersonal and communication skills through customer service and attending to their needs. My skills of persuasion, both written and verbal, coupled with professional presentation will assit me in completing the sale of the banner project.

I have increased my presence in my community as a volunteer. I was appointed to the Roebuck Springs Historic Preservation Society board in January 2007 as a non- resident to represent the South East Lake community. Commissioned by the Historic Society, I was asked to brand the Roebuck Springs community and the Historic Society to compliment the other. The 2009 Roebuck Springs Historic Home and Garden Tour gave me the opportunity to complete the project with multiple publications reinforcing that brand including a street banner.
