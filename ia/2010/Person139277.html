McHardy Smith’s Summary

Leadership role in drug discovery/development within a pharmaceutical or biotechnology organization including continued bench-side activities.

Senior Research Scientist in Membrane Biochemistry & Biochemical Pharmacology. Skilled leader with over 20 years industry experience at Merck & Co. Committed to focused drug discovery and development through teamwork and critical thinking with a record of success from discovery to preclinical. Experienced in membrane based mechanisms across most therapeutic areas including animal health and crop protection applications. Major driver of Medicinal Chemistry and HTS campaigns.

Relocation is not an issue.

  • Sr Research Fellow

    Merck

    (Public Company; MRK; Pharmaceuticals industry)

    2000 — 2008 (8 years )

    Biochemical experimentation leading towards clinical drug discovery. Continued work on Nodulisporic acid receptor in invertebrates. Continued work on peptide toxin blockers of Kv2.1 (GxTx-I) and Nav 1.7 (ProTx-II).

    Finished up HTS campaign on Nav 1.7 using FLIPR and VIPR using FRET dyes. Started an N-type calcium channel screening campaign including counterscreening using FLIPR 384 and Ca dyes.

    Scientific lead in developing a basic research data analysis tool adopted company-wide. Major driver in writing "Fluorescent plate readers: Merck Best Practices".

  • Research fellow

    Merck

    (Public Company; MRK; Pharmaceuticals industry)

    1994 — 2000 (6 years )

    Identified the mechanism of action of a novel fungal product (nodulisporic acid) with insecticidal activity - it potentiated the opening of the invertebrate glutamate-gated chloride channel. I also found that the Drosophila melanogaster Glutamate-gated chloride channel co-immunoprecipitated with a subunit of the GABA-gated chloride channel. Ran three HTS campaigns on nodulisporic acid and Ivermectin binding sites in invertebrates.

    Setup and ran a high-throughput screening (HTS) assay using the human sodium channels Nav 1.7 and 1.8 on a VIPR using FRET dyes. Purified and determined the primary and secondary structure of spider toxins blocking Nav 1.7 and Kv2.1. They are cystine-knot peptides that block by shifting the voltage-dependence of channel activation to a more positive potential.

    Led program to automate reading of larval death assays. Ended a 6 yr term on Merck Research Laboratories Rahway SIte Safety Committee with last yr as Chair of same.

  • Senior Research Biochemist

    Merck

    (Public Company; MRK; Pharmaceuticals industry)

    September 1988 — 1994 (6 years )

    From spider venom, I identified several activities of interest. I purified to homogeneity an invertebrate-selective sodium channel activator and several calcium channel blockers. Activities were traced by functional assays, electrophysiological assays and by conotoxin binding assays. Proteins were purified by reverse phase and Ion exchange HPLC. Purified peptides and proteins were used as proof-of-concept molecules and as tools for basic science research. Purified proteins were sequenced.

    The HPLC data was acquired using a program built in LabVIEW. The program monitored and recorded absorbance (up to 4 wavelengths), back pressure and %B, and controlled 2 fraction collectors.

    Biosynthesis and Assembly of the subunits of the nicotinic Acetylcholine receptor in the lab of Dr. John Paul Merlie. Also studied phosphorylation of the nicotinic acetylcholine receptor in intact cells in the lab of Dr. John Lawrence.
