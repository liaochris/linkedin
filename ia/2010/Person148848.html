Morgan Monroe’s Summary

In 1973, Morgan Monroe started skateboarding in Anderson, Indiana. He was a member of the Anderson Sidewalk Surfers. In 1978 He moved to Clearwater, Florida, where he became a fixture at Clearwater Skateboard Park until 1982, when it was closed. His mother understood the importance of skateboarding in her kid’s lives and leased Skate Wave Skateboard Park in Tampa, Florida for a year.
In 1986 Morgan built his first back yard half pipe, beginning his long tenure as the builder of many small quarter and half pipes honing his skills in erecting a variety of innovative skateboarding designs.
In 1987 he built his first real half pipe that was 16' wide with 10 ' of flat and 8 foot tall with 8' transition.
In 1989 he put together one of the very first vert to mini spine ramps.

Pro Bones Brigade Skateboard Legend, Mike McGill, asked Morgan to help him with his indoor skateboard park in Oldsmar, Florida.
In 1991 Morgan moved to Cocoa Beach, Florida there he started construction on a new, innovative skateboard park built over a lake on telephone poles. It too, was a spine ramp.
Cocoa Brach is where Morgan got his first real taste of Stunt Modeling, a bug he has not yet shaken. At this point Morgan began taking modeling and acting classes. At the end of 1992 he left Florida for Las Angeles, California to pursue acting and stunt work.

Morgan decided to take a short break and move back to his Florida home, allowing time to mend. In no time, his circle of friends began to call him “HOLLYWOOD” after the place he worked and called home for years. The name caught on fast.
He started networking with old friends from Hollywood by phone and e-mail. Morgan started working on a project called the "HOLLYWOOD Project" with the group called “The HOLLYWOOD Network”; their vision to start producing their own films. Morgan then opened his production company, Monroe Productions International.
HOLLYWOOD was the host of the 2010 OC Maloof Money Cup's Master Demo.
