Gene Park’s Summary

Gene Park is currently a reporter for the Honolulu Star-Advertiser. He is also the incoming president of the Asian American Journalists Association of Hawaii, and vice president of The Hawaii Newspaper Guild.

He has been a professional journalist since 2003. He has triple-market experience, having written for the following publications in California, Hawaii and the Mariana Islands.

- Honolulu Star-Advertiser (Honolulu, Hawaii)
- Honolulu Star-Bulletin (Honolulu, Hawaii)
- Island Scene Magazine (Honolulu, Hawaii)
- Quill Magazine (Society of Professional Journalists)
- Marianas Business Journal (Guam)
- Marianas Variety (Guam)
- Pacific Daily News (Guam)
- The San Gabriel Valley Tribune (West Covina, Calif.)
- The Orange County Register (Santa Ana, Calif.)

His writing expertise includes breaking news coverage, business (tourism, commercial real estate, military spending, personal finance), crime, politics, judicial courts, public education, and the arts (film, music, video games).

He has also written reports for radio broadcasts, for Guam stations KSTO-FM and KIJI FM 104.

He serves as vice president of the Hawaii Newspaper Guild, and sits on the board for the Asian American Journalists Association Hawaii chapter.

His current career goal is to meet and help address the many challenges facing the media industry during this exciting but turbulent period.

Gene Park’s Specialties:

Writing for print and broadcast; Interviewing; Copy editing; Page design; Online content management, production; Legal research; Social media tools, outreach; Media consultation; Newsroom management experience; Photography.

I am adept at the following programs: Quark Xpress, Adobe Photoshop, Publicus, Web Publisher, Documentum, NewsEditPro, SharePoint, ASSIST.net and QNXT.
