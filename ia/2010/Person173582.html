Risa Pappas’s Summary

I love all forms of informative and entertainment media. I would be perfectly happy working at a newspaper, TV station, film studio or radio station.

Radio:

I've recorded, edited and voiced USA News Network audiocasts, which are distributed to 36 radio stations across the US.

Since starting as a trainee at WPSC in 2007 I have ascended the ranks to DJ, Music Director, Program Director and finally Assistant General Manager. I've done several shifts on-air on both the music and talk show formats, hosting a drive-time talk show called "The Morning Blend" for over a year. I have written and voiced several program materials. I have mixed live artist performances over the years, once even mixing 15 bands live on-air over the course of 15 hours. I frequently create promos, sweepers/linkers and public service announcements.

I host the "Beatles Corner" segment of the Britsound Radio Show, doing research into songs and presenting information to listeners. I have interviewed several bands with Rob Quicke, host of the Britsound Radio Show, and have even mixed an in-studio performance for the band Franko.

I am a co-host on the weekly 45 minute program "Newlygreens on the Radio". I write our news segment of the show and frequently write the DIY tips segment of the show as well. I have also booked guests and participate in all of our interviews, of which we record one per week.

Television:

I've recorded sound, operated camera, assisted in lighting, set up backgrounds, acted as grip and edited footage for the Telly Award-winning Newlygreens cable access television show.

Journalism:

I was an award-winning reporter for Cumberland County College's "The Voice" and became editor-in-chief my second year.

Most recently I was a freelancer for the Glen Rock Gazette of Glen Rock, New Jersey.

Film:

I have helped produce three short films as well as writing, directing, shooting and editing my own short film.
