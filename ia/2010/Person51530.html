David Knoble’s Summary

I have 18 years experience as a North Carolina CPA and also hold a South Carolina CPA certificate. My education includes a Masters Degree in Accounting from UNC at Chapel Hill. Add my 14 years in Real Estate designing financial, accounting and operational systems and you have found a full-service CPA firm perfect for a small business owner.

As our web site indicates, we know the goals of a small business owner are vastly different from large companies. We know nothing is more important to you than cash, we know the long hours you work to be successful, we understand the stress.

We also have several CPA's on staff with significant Non-Profit experience. We devote over 40% of our firm resources to serving this important part of our community.

If any of this intrigues you, please contact us so we can talk.

David Knoble’s Specialties:

My specialties include all aspects of the Real Estate industry and Non-Profit sector, compilation, review & audit services, partnership & LLC taxation and operating agreements, bank financing, operational and IT consulting and monthly bookkeeping and payroll services.

  • CFO, Owner

    Evergreen Homebuilders, LLC

    (Real Estate industry)

    December 2002 — December 2008 (6 years 1 month)

    Evergreen was a local Charlotte, NC homebuilder we started in late 2002 that built entry level homes starting at $185,000 through move-up homes starting in the low $400's. At the peak, Evergreen Homebuilders closed over 100 homes annually.

    As CFO and an owner, I was responsible for all areas of finance, accounting, risk management, human resources and IT. We sucessfully raised $2 million in capital prior to the recession and began some land development to feed the homebuilding operations. I created and programmed the estimating system used to build over 300 homes using .NET, Microsoft SQL and a web interface.

  • CFO

    Mulvaney Homes, Inc.

    (Real Estate industry)

    August 1995 — July 2002 (7 years )

    During my tenure at this Charlotte, NC based homebuilder, we grew the company from $25 million in annual revenues to $100 million. We increased the entire accounting and finance department by one position to four people through this growth. This was made possible by the successful installation of a system allowing builders to electronically approve and submit purchase orders for payment.

    Our business financed and developed over 2,000 lots in more than 20 communities. These lots were purchased and built on by the homebuilding operations.

    I also began a cash management program using internally generated cash flows to finance the construction of sold homes generating savings of over $100,000 annually.

    During 2000 we sucessfully started another division outside of the Charlotte, NC region that paid back the starting capital during the first 18 months of operations.

    This company was later sold and operates in 2009 as Mattamy Homes.
