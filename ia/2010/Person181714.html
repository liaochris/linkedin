Sandra Aponte Salazar’s Summary

Sandra is a multi-lingual communicator with a life-long training as an agent of change and a strong work ethic. Sandra’s professional experience began when she was a simultaneous court interpreter while still in high school. She worked as a journalist for ten years, as a public information officer for eight years, and has been a crisis communications professional in the last five.

Sandra’s work ethic is based on three principles: one, her main duty is to protect and preserve her own integrity and that of the agency or workplace she represents; two, she will work for and proudly represent an agency or project she strongly believes in; and three, a fulfilling professional experience must be an opportunity to perform meaningful work.

Sandra is currently a Regional Project Manager for PIER Systems, Inc., a position she has held since 2008. Since then, she has successfully implemented 30 PIER sites for the Houston Urban Area Security Initiative (UASI).

Prior to joining PIER Systems, Inc., Sandra was employed at the Metropolitan Transit Authority of Harris County, Texas, where she began in December 2005 as a Media Specialist and was promoted to Communications Manager, a position she held until September 2008.

From 2000 to 2005, Sandra worked at the City of Houston, mainly as a Public Information Officer (PIO) with the Police Department. During her employment at the City of Houston, Sandra was part of media response teams during such emergencies as the terrorist attacks of September 11, 2001 and Hurricane Rita in 2005.

She holds a B.A. from the University of St. Thomas (Houston, Texas), where she majored in international relations. She is a native Puerto Rico.

Emergency communications, pro-active communications strategy, Web content management, inquiry management, Spanish language editing.
