Jacqui Everett’s Summary

I thoroughly enjoyed my time at university and I am very excited about starting my career in graphic design. I learned so much, during my time at university, from all around me, lecturers and fellow students alike. It was during this time that I discovered my passion for typography, which further grew when I wrote my dissertation, ‘Has the development of technology affected the development of typography?’ I used the knowledge that I gained in this area to create a new brand for the counselling organisation Relate. My final project was working on the Brand for London brief, which was set up by Boris Johnson. I have also worked with clients on other briefs, including creating the identity for a local HR consultant.

I love painting in my spare time asI find it a great way to relax. My other love is photography, whenever I have spare time I am often found with a camera in my hand capturing my environment and the natural world around me. I really enjoy experimenting with my camera and expanding my knowledge in this area. There are so many things I see that inspire me and photography is a brilliant way to record this.

I’m also a very quick learner; over the past three years I have learnt to appreciate the developments in software and this has driven me to keep up to date with new techniques. Subscribing to Grafik and Design Week again helps me keep my finger on the creative pulse. I really enjoy working in a team and like getting to know new people, although I am just as happy working on my own and using my initiative.

I am currently looking to start my career in design, if you have anything to offer me, whether it be advice, work, or placements, please feel free to get in touch.
