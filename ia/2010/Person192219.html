Jim Davidson’s Summary

Jim Davidson is a MOUNTAIN CLIMBER and an INSPIRATIONAL SPEAKER with Speaking of Adventure out of Fort Collins, COLORADO. INSPIRING CORPORATIONS and INDIVIDUALS to persevere and be resilient, by sharing compelling messages from his many climbing adventures. Blending his twenty-eight years of adventure with two decades of successful business experience, Jim distills hard-earned lessons that inspire organizations and professionals to strive for excellence and overcome adversity, no matter how big the challenge.

Jim's compelling survivial story is being featured on "I Shouldn't Be Alive" (airing December 2010 on Animal Planet) and is the focus of his new book ("The Ledge: An Adventure of Friendship and Survival on Mount Rainier", co-author Kevin Vaughan, Ballantine Books, 2011).

As a CLIMBER and EXPEDITION LEADER, Jim has been on eighteen high-altitude peaks in Alaska, Argentina, Bolivia, Ecuador, Mexico, Nepal & Tibet. In 2009, Jim summitted Cho Oyu in Tibet (26,906 feet), the sixth highest peak in the world.

As a KEYNOTE SPEAKER, LEADERSHIP AND DEVELOPMENT TRAINER, and TEAMWORK FACILITATOR, Jim Davidson has inspired corporations, associations and audiences at hundreds of events across the USA, New Zealand and Australia.

His INSPIRATIONAL PRESENTATIONS resonate with the audience and instill compelling lessons about perseverance, resilient leadership and engaged teamwork. Jim Davidson programs always ENTERTAIN, EDUCATE and ENLIGHTEN the audience.

Jim's talks are unique, utilizing climbing props, summit photos, HD video clips, vivid storytelling of real-world challenges. Whether he's recounting harrowing tales of a summit push or relating his leadership challenges to the boardroom, you know you're listening to one-of-a-kind, authentic wisdom. Jim's talks on leadership, adventure, strength, teamwork, and reaching your goals, are tributes to the human spirit.
