Stephen W. Smith’s Summary

Stephen W. Smith, is now the CEO or Rasmussen Reports in Asbury Park NJ. Rasmussen Reports is an electronic media company specializing in the collection, publication, and distribution of public opinion polling information.

As a media company, we make our money by selling title sponsorships, subscriptions, advertising and digital content. One thing we don’t sell is polls. Because we value our independence and credibility, Rasmussen Reports cannot be hired to conduct a poll for anyone. For our reports, we decide the questions to ask based upon the needs and interests of our audience. If it’s in the news, it’s in our polls.

Formerly VP of Digital Products and Services for McGraw-Hill. Currently we are looking for new technologies and services, that can build online revenues, and increase the relevance of online services to McGraw-Hill customers. Grew Digital Revenues from 40% from $27 million in 07 to $45 million in 2008.

Formerly Chief Executive Officer of RichFX (2005-2007) a fast growing online company focused on ASP services to 300 retail customers. RichFX was sold to Channel Advisor Corporation in 2007. Prior to joining RichFX, from 2001 to 2005, Stephen was VP Corporate and Business Development for Register.com (sold to Vector Capital 2005) From 1999 to 2001, Stephen was the Vice President of Corporate Development/Investments for Winstar Communications and a joint venture with CBS. From 1998 to 1999, Stephen was Vice President of Business Development at About.com which was sold to Primedia. From 1986 to 1998, Stephen worked for AT&T as Director of New Business Development for the AT&T WorldNet Internet Service, and previously in several product management and sales positions in the Bell Labs/Lucent/Avaya Premises Networking Groups. Stephen has received several patents for his work
