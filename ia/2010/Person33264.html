Carolyn Lawson’s Summary

As Director, eServices for the State of California, Carolyn is a driving force behind the innovative use of emerging technologies and the re-purposing of legacy technologies throughout the state. Recent projects include the award-winning redesign of the state's portal www.ca.gov; the development of www.data.ca.gov, revealing over 100 million records of state data in one place at no additional cost to the state; along with several inventive implementations of emerging tools throughout state government. She works directly with the Governor's office on critical and emerging issues, applying technologies which match the business and messaging needs, most delivered very short timeframes and under unusual fiscal constraints.

Under Carolyn’s leadership, the eServices office is an active proponent of Web 2.0 technologies bringing about an unprecedented level of communication and engagement with employees and citizens alike. Founded in 2006, the California's Web Master User Group lead by the eServices Office has saved the state taxpayers tens of millions of dollars through its interactive open source, crowd sourced community model developed at a time long before the term “Gov 2.0” was ever coined.

Carolyn is a thought leader and innovator challenging the traditional views of technology within the public sector. She has been described as one of the most highly respected and unconventional IT leaders in government. As a leader in the IT industry with several awards to her credit. Recent acknowledgements include being named in 2009 and 2010 by Information Week among the top ten Innovators in Government and honored by GovFresh in 2010 as one of the 100+ Women in Government Technology.

She sits on several Boards including:
*Board of Directors, Fortune School of Education (established as Project Pipeline in 1989) http://www.projectpipeline.org
*Advisory Board, InformationWeek http://www.informationweek.com
* Advisory Board, Code for America http://codeforamerica.org
