David Drinkwater’s Summary

Multi-lingual Manufacturing Execution Expert with dual American/British citizenship. I am a native speaker of English, fluent in German, and proficient in French. I am open to work in any country that speaks those languages as a first language. The bulk of my experience is in semiconductor/sensor manufacturing. I would like to stay in that field, but being the professional that I am, I have to be open to new possibilities. Materials Science and Engineering, electronic ceramics, and MEMS got me where I was, and I would happy to broaden my career in those fields. Manufacturing execution, however, opens many doors to other fields, and I consider myself lucky to be a quick and willing learner, so that I can apply my skill set in many environments. I look forward to hearing about yours!

David Drinkwater’s Experience

  • Yield Enhancement ("Product") Engineer

    Honeywell

    (Public Company; HON; Aviation & Aerospace industry)

    2006 — 2009 (3 years )

  • Senior Manufacturing Process Engineer

    Honeywell International

    (Public Company; HON; Aviation & Aerospace industry)

    2000 — 2005 (5 years )

    I was responsible for manufacturing engineering support for the Metal Deposition cell. This included manufacturing sustaining on MRC 600 and 900 sputtering systems, Perkin-Elmer 2400 and 4400 sputtering systems, and a Temescal FC-1800 ebeam evaporation system.
    • I performed process rescue and quality assurance exercises on multiple, single-point, technology-differentiating systems. If those efforts had failed and lead to external customer shut-downs, Honeywell would have incurred $10,000 per hour penalties.
    • At the start of my tenure as Metal Deposition process engineer, I realized $70,000 in annual savings by qualifying a second source for sputtering targets.
    • I revised every process specification to meet developing ISO 9001:2008 standards and developed improved training requirements to reduce the time to contribution for manufacturing Operators.
    • Through leadership of multidisciplinary teams (Manufacturing Engineering, Manufacturing Operations, and Repair and Maintenance), I instituted engineering changes that increased overall Metal Line up-time by 40% which allowed the business to delay new capital equipment acquisitions.
    • With the Fab’s transition from 12% 6” to >50% 6” wafers and in my continued service to the Metal Line and to the Richardson Sensor Fab mission, I justified the acquisition of a modern standard $2,000,000 metal deposition system in 2008 that is currently in Production on site in the Fab. I continued to support and mentor in the Metal Deposition Process area throughout my career at the Richardson Sensor Fab.
