Ana M. del Castillo’s Summary

Systems-oriented leader specializing in the operational management of integrated enrollment and student support services. As the current Director of the Division of Student Affairs, leads and manages the IIT Stuart Student Affairs team, which is charged with providing an integrated approach to the non-academic (i.e. co-curricular) aspects of the student experience.

Ana M. del Castillo’s Experience

  • Director, Division of Student Affairs - Stuart School of Business

    Illinois Institute of Technology

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    November 2010 — Present (2 months)

    The Director of the Division of Student Affairs leads and manages the IIT Stuart Student Affairs team, which is charged with providing an integrated approach to the non-academic (i.e. co-curricular) aspects of the student experience. The overall goal of the Student Affairs team is to foster student success through a variety of initiatives and functions, including career management, academic advising, academic service learning, student engagement, co-curricular programming, and special events (including orientation). The responsibilities of the position include strategic planning, integration of services, implementation oversight, assessment and continuous improvement. The Division of Student Affairs at IIT Stuart includes the Office of Student Services, the Office of Academic Advisement, and the Career Management Center.

    The Stuart School of Business's unique approach to education prepares graduates to be strategically competitive by combining the six core constructs of creativity, entrepreneurship, incisive decision-making, innovation, leadership and sustainability.

The College offers high quality, relevant degree programs rooted in current scholarship with a high emphasis on practical application. The College removes the barriers of time, space and economics to provide a major reduction in tuition and deliver an instructional model that brings unprecedented convenience to working educators.
