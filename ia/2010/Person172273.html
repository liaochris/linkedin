Richard Harder’s Summary

At the age of 8, I started working for my father (owner of Baiardi Construction) as a laborer on his job sites. I did so, because my parents had instilled a strong work ethic in me at a very young age and always pushed me to set and achieve new goals. Working for my father enabled me to focus on a job at hand and taught me to work towards a common goal, while using ingenuity and hard work to make a difference. I was always driven to make a difference in any task that I focused on and cherished the valuable life lessons learned along the way. I worked for my father up until the age of 15; that's when I started exploring other employment options. I dabbled as a busboy for a local sports bar (T-Bones) after school, spent a few years detailing cars at the dealership that my mother and uncle both work at (Varsity Lincoln Mercury), and worked various odds and ends as they crossed my path. Then my father built a home for Paul Wagner, then owner of Masters Green, Inc., which opened up a brand new door in my life. I worked 60+ hours per week beginning at the age of 16 for MG during my high-school summers. It allowed me to accumulate enough money to pay for a substantial portion of my college expenses. I also learned that I had grown fond of being outdoors, dealing with customers one-on-one, and working for a company that put such a strong emphasis on quality. I dedicated the next four summers to Masters Green, and learned much along the way. After my junior year of college, I decided it was time to dabble in the field that I was going to school for. This ultimately led to a summer internship at Jabil, Inc. It was a great opportunity for me to experience a new business environment, learn new techniques in dealing with customer and supplier relations, and network with individuals who had similar educational backgrounds as my own. I cherished the experience, but the lawn care industry beckoned. Fit Turf was my next step; in the here and now, I love what I'm doing.
