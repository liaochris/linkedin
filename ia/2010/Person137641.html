  • Director of Rooms & Environmental Programs

    Seaport Hotel

    (Hospitality industry)

    May 2005 — Present (5 years 1 month)

    I currently oversee the Rooms Operation at the Hotel inclusive of Guest Services, Housekeeping, Engineering, Laundry, Wave Health & Fitness, Concierge, Gift shop and Business Center. Serving also as the Director of Environmental Programs - I am focused on developing and implementing environmental initiatives throughout the operation. We have aptly named this program "Seaport Saves". In 2007, Seaport was presented with the Boston Green Business Award for environmental excellence, by the City of Boston. In 2008, Seaport received the "Good Earthkeeping" award for the most outstanding environmental program in the state, by the Massachusetts Lodging Association. Also in 2008, the "Seaport Saves" program received a "Leadership in Innovation" award from the Massachusetts Department of Environmental Protection.

    Assisted in securing a top 50 ranking amongst North American hotels for 2002 as reviewed by Conde Nast Magazine & #1 ranking amongst boutique hotels worldwide as ranked by Robb Report – 2002.

  • Front Office Manager

    Four Seasons Hotel Boston

    (Public Company; 201-500 employees; Hospitality industry)

    May 1994 — October 2001 (7 years 6 months)

    During my 7+ years of tenure with Four Seasons Boston I was fortunate enough to work in every aspect of the Rooms Division, working my way up and through - Security, Reception, Housekeeping, Valet Parking, PBX, Reservations and Front Office operations. This experience was invaluable in providing for me a well rounded education in the industry with the most prestigious Hotel in all of New England.

    My tenure with Four Seasons culminated with the appointment as Front Office Manager. Implemented, developed and improved “glitch recovery” system, guest history enhancements and express registration that were adopted by other Four Seasons properties.

    Assisted in securing top 10 ranking amongst North American hotels as review by Conde Nast Magazine as well as contributed in maintaining New England's only 5-star, 5-diamond property
