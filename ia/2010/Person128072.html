Lourenzo Ferreira’s Summary

I started my career in the late 90s, as a web designer trainee at a Brazilian ISP, EOL.

Later I got into a Digital Interactive Television project for Brazilian Ministry of Education. Mainly, it was a satellite-based IP multi-cast.
The set-top box prototype was a PC loading a fullscreen Mozilla Firebird (which later became firefox).
Most of the user interface was programmed by me using HTML and JavaScript, only except by the CD Recording tool.

I've worked at Presbyterian University Mackenzie when Brazilian Digital Television Standard (SBTVD) was been developed, divided into various universities in Brazil.
Mackenzie took the modulation/demodulation section, and I worked at the Digital Television Laboratory as a Matlab to C/C++ algorithm translator.
I've also developed some testing tools.

In 2006 I've been hired by the first used computer store at Latin America, to build a custom linux distribution which could turn old obsolete computers into something useful. There was success with a Slackware base, XCFE, and custom compiled kernels.

2007 was the Papagallis year, when I came as a programmer/sysadmin. The special thing about this job was the constant growing knowledge.
Papagallis started as a one-man Informal Learning classes company, then started to use various web tools to support the knowledge construction/exchange.
Other great subjects there was World Cafe, Open Space Techniques, and so on.

2008 was the year I've focused in Drupal. Started developing the website for Bio Ritmo, a large chain of gyms from Brazil.

Then I've been hired by Chuva Inc., a Drupal focused development, consulting and training company.

Lourenzo Ferreira’s Specialties:

Web development, mainly with Drupal. PHP development, Python scripting, SysAdmin, Software Debug and Troubleshooting
