Erik Turner, LEED AP’s Summary

I am currently finishing my M.S. in Mechanical Engineering at the University of California, Irvine, where I modeled the dynamic performance of PEM fuel cells, specifically their interaction with the residential built environment.

While attending graduate school I was introduced to the challenges and opportunities present in the area of sustainability of the built environment. Due to this interest I obtained my LEED AP under the NCv2.2 examination, and have upgraded to LEED 2009 with a Building and New Construction designation. Together with my EIT obtained after graduating from Penn State, I believe this gives me the basic tools needed in order to enter the area of sustainable building design.

I am seeking a position in the sustainable building area, either a mechanical design position or a project engineer with a GC. In either event I wish to eventually gain both design and hands on construction experience before moving towards becoming a commissioning agent later in my career.

I am currently a member of ASHRAE (Puget Sound) and ASME (UC Irvine) and an Emerging Green Builder in the Orange County and Cascadia Chapters of the Green Building Council.
