George Johnson, CISSP’s Summary

Since the early 90's, while working at the Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency (DARPA) I have been involved in Internet Security and building Communities of Interest, Extranets, Portals, and other tools that focus on providing a secure platform for secure information sharing. I spent two years at Carnegie Mellon working at the Software Engineering Institute further maturing the processes and methodologies necessary to promote security as a principal requirement to information systems. While security has always been viewed as competing with ease of use, I have made it a career to:

* Move the complex decisions of security into the infrastructure in order to present users with clean, consistent, "hard to make a mistake" interfaces that satisfy the mission and promote good security practices by the users of a system, and

* Provide end-to-end security across a complex and multi-vendor integrated architecture to give the user a seamless product.

The constant balance of aligning mission support and security is a dynamic task that I love to face on a daily basis.

George Johnson, CISSP’s Specialties:

Secure Information Sharing, FISMA Processes, Internet Security, System Architecture, Translating between technical staff and upper management, Identity Management and Single Sign On Processes, Portal Architectures, Mission Support Requirements Analysis, Collaborative System Development, Speaking to raise awareness on computer security and Netcentric warfare issues
