Lucas Zanzi’s Summary

I've gone through a bit of a switch in my professional interests during the past couple of years.

First it was only Mechanical Engineering, until I realized it was too much about the machine, and too little about the user.

And then one day I saw someone pushing a door at the hinged end. A minute later I myself slammed into the same door and realized it didn't matter how conveniently hinged that door was if people kept pushing the wrong end.

Or how a 40mpg fuel efficiency doesn't matter if the driver can't bear sitting in the car.

Or how a PowerPoint presentation can be full of good information, but it's worthless if nobody in the audience can absorb it.

And that's how I've ended up pursuing a field that meets somewhere between design and experimental psychology. I want to be able to understand how we can simplify and make our tools much more intuitive, user-friendly, and accessible to all. Design so the machine can adapt to the user, not the other way around.

Lucas Zanzi’s Specialties:

Design and execution of experiments to better understand and improve HCI.
