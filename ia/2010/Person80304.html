Harvin Moore’s Summary

Harvin's first job was in aerospace - in 1982, after graduating high school, he spent the summer on the launch site of the world's first privately built rocket to reach outer space, the Conestoga 1. He continued to work for Space Services through his college years.

In 1986 he graduated from Northwestern University, then worked for 5 years as a National Bank Examiner with the US Treasury Department in New York, Washington and London. He earned an MBA in Finance from NYU in 1991, and as part of his degree program also studied at London Business School in 1990.

In 1991 Harvin returned to Houston as a merchant banker. He founded Frontera Furniture Company in 1992 and in the early years of the Internet, created the first online gallery of premium brand name garden furniture. For his work in building Frontera, Harvin was runner-up for Ernst & Young's 2000 Entrepreneur of the Year award. In 2002, Harvin founded a company called Decor Americana which sells environmentally friendly garden furniture in the US, Canada & Europe.

Harvin returned to the aerospace industry in 2003 and is currently head of operations at Sentinel Satellite Inc. and Space Services Inc. Sentinel is developing a satellite system which will orbit the sun and serve as a sentinel to warn of dangerous solar storms. Harvin is very pleased to again be working in the industry that first engaged him as a young college student.

Since college Harvin has been involved in public education, beginning with tutoring at the East Harlem Tutorial Program in Manhattan and continuing in several nonprofits in Houston public schools including Teach for America. In 1997 he joined the founding board of KIPP Academy charter school, and over 6 years served as Treasurer and Vice Chairman. In November 2003, the voters of Houston elected Harvin to Houston's school board, and he was re-elected in 2005 and 2009. Harvin has served as President of the Board of Education and currently serves as board legislative liason.
