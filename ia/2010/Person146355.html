Kathleen Miles’s Summary

On June 20th, 2009, I completed my two years as a middle school teacher with Teach for America. While working as a full-time teacher in South Central for the past two years, I simultaneously completed my master’s in Education at Loyola Marymount University through night classes.

I graduated from Berkeley in 2007 with a degree in Political Economy. During my Berkeley years, I had at different times two different full-time political jobs: Field Examiner for the National Labor Relations Board for six months and Political Action Coordinator for the International Iron Workers Union for another six leading up to the 2004 election. At Berkeley, I took courses in Environmental Policy, Urban Planning, Political History & Theory, Economics, and Statistics and interned for the non-profit Urban Ecology. While I was still in high school, I interned for US Congressman Adam Schiff in Pasadena.
