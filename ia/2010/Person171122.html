Renee Cassard’s Summary

Renee has been providing strategic communications, brand/digital planning and market research services to national digital/online firms, PR, branding and advertising agencies for ten years. As a consultant, Renee works across many marketing disciplines, creating strategies for a wide variety of vehicles designed to communicate and interact with consumers.

Renee's ability to discern how consumer insights can be incorporated into brand strategy across multiple communication platforms comes from her unique blend of academic expertise and marketing experience. She holds a Masters in Developmental Psychology from Columbia University, where she examined how people's epistemological understanding or sense of knowing is formed. Influenced by many factors, this sense of knowing plays a major role in consumer interactions in both real world and online contexts.

In addition to her academic expertise, Renee has nearly a decade of designing qualitative research studies including traditional, ethnographic, psychosocial and non-traditional qualitative research. She has moderated hundreds of focus groups and in-depth interviews and worked with quantitative research teams to facilitate meaningful data interpretations for Fortune 500 clients including Merck, Pfizer, Best Buy, Mannington, Toyota, No Nonsense and TheraGenics. Today, she continues to provide consumer insights to clients within a variety of industries including: Consumer Products and Services, Healthcare, OTC Pharmaceuticals, Education, Non-profit, Financial Services, Food/Beverage and PR/Digital Media campaigns.

In her last full time consulting position, Renee served as Brand Planning Director at RAPP in New York, where she led the deployment of online communications strategies for Merck pharmaceuticals. Current areas of interest for Renee include leveraging online and social media platforms to glean consumer insights and develop meaningful brand interactions for consumer-centric businesses.
