Barbara Zarzosa’s Summary

I'm a geek who loves to write.

I fell in love with technology when I bought my first computer - an Atari 520 ST. One look at its glowing green command prompt, and I was hooked. In the ensuing years I've designed and built websites, databases, computer networks and learned CCNA routing. Through 3M Canada's Women in Technology & Trades project, I have prepared and presented workshops for middle and high school students designed to highlight alternate career choices in the technical field.

As a freelance writer, I have written newsletters, flyers, posters, direct-mail campaigns, case studies, grant applications, policy and procedure manuals and how-to guides for various businesses and not-for-profit agencies. This allowed them to successfully market their product/service or solicit funds. A long-time scribbler of poetry, short stories and non-fiction articles, my work has appeared in various periodicals such as Our Times, Mature Perspectives and The Journal.

I help businesses communicate with their customers, prospects, partners and employees using success-focused case studies, engaging web content and engaging newsletters.

My career includes serving as a consultant to non-profit agencies; as an editor and publisher of a nationally circulated membership newsletter; and over 20 years experience in business administration. I attended the University of Western Ontario where I studied science and history and have completed courses in Visual Basic programming, Cisco networking and routing and Technical Communications at St. Clair College in Windsor, Ontario.

When not busy with my clients, I volunteer as the moderator of the Freecycle group for Windsor and Essex County and I am an active member of Toastmasters International.

I currently live on a wine route on the north shore of Lake Erie with my two chihuahuas, Desi and Lucy, and my partner of thirteen years.
