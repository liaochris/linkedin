Lynn Friedman’s Summary

Dr. Lynn Friedman is a clinical psychologist and psychoanalyst in full-time private practice who has subspecialties in: relationships, low self-esteem, narcissistic difficulties and work-life (career coaching/organizational consultation). Her practice includes, evaluation, psychoanalysis, psychotherapy, couple's therapy and executive/organizational (career) consultation.

She is on the faculty of the Washington Psychoanalytic Institute where she teaches clinical technique and psychopathology, emphasizing narcissistic disorders and self-esteem difficulties. Also, she is a guest lecturer at the Baltimore Washington Psychoanalytic Institute.

For over thirty years, Dr. Lynn Friedman has been teaching clinical psychology, psychodynamics, psychopathology and career coaching & organizational consultation to graduate students, pre-professional students and undergraduates at Johns Hopkins University, Carnegie Mellon University, and University of Pittsburgh.

She enjoys writing and presenting on these topics, professionally, and in the popular media. Her writing on psychological, psychoanalytic and workplace concerns can be found at the Washington Post and an array of venues. Her Washington Business Journal Column, Corporations on the Couch, in which she analyzes organizational dynamics, is nationally-syndicated.

She welcomes calls regarding evaluation, psychotherapy, psychoanalysis and about writing and speaking on these topics. She can be reached at: (301) 656-9050. Her work can be found at: <www.drlynnfriedman.typepad.com> and <www.drlynnfriedman.com>

Lynn Friedman’s Specialties:

Evaluation, psychotherapy & psychoanalysis with sub-specialties relationships (difficulties with intimacy and commitment, conflicts in love relationships and work relationships; individual and couple's therapy), low self-esteem, narcissistic difficulties, self-sabotaging behavior, personality difficulties, anxiety, depression and work-life (career and organizational) consultation.
