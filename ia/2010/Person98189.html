Jim Bertsch’s Summary

As Head of Technology for Thomson Reuters Indices I successfully led the evaluation, selection, and consumption of daily equities data from 59 stock exchanges globally. This data was then subjected to a custom ETL process using SSIS packages resulting in both an OLTP business process which was used to source the indices product and a data warehouse which was used for business intelligence and research purposes. The result of this effort was the successful launch of the new Thomson Reuters suite of indices on time and 300k under budget.

I successfully managed the entire enterprise data management process at Lipper. This included the development and maintenance of Lipper’s daily feed consumption system which processed all NASDAQ published, US domiciled mutual fund data points on a daily basis in less than one hour. This allowed for daily mutual fund data to be collected beginning with the close of the stock market, concluding in less than an hour. This data was then subjected to ETL and disseminated via custom Lipper data feeds to media outlets such as AP and Wall Street Journal in time to make their daily print deadlines. Collaborated with leaders from all relevant business units across the organization to ensure that information technology strategy is aligned with corporate business plans and objectives both in the present and ongoing.

Managed the development of the next generation of Lipper calculation engines utilizing a proprietary data caching and compression strategy resulting in an average decreased run time of 600%. As the number of US mutual funds vastly increased the legacy engines could no longer generate results in time for product deadlines. The release of the new engines regained the capacity and performance to source not only all current products but the scalability to handle new products for the foreseeable future.
