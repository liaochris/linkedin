Justin Ketterer’s Summary

My skills are focused in the theoretical disciplines of solid mechanics and fatigue and the process of engineering design. Machine and structural design is my career. I really enjoy the work of 'design-build-test,' especially where it requires the application of my engineering training.

- Worked as a Mechanical Engineer in consumer, medical and dental product design and development. This involved SolidWorks CAD design (part, assembly, and drawing creation and modification), obtaining quotes on rapid prototyping, as well as assembling, troubleshooting and validating concepts through prototypes.
- Master’s thesis: "Fatigue Crack Initiation in Cross-Ply Carbon-Fiber Laminates." Included experimentation and analysis of the lower threshold of fatigue crack initiation in carbon fiber/epoxy laminates in tensile fatigue loading. Funded by Boeing.
- Undergraduate and graduate coursework focused on solid mechanics, fatigue, and mechanics of composite materials.
- Graduate coursework included the study and teaching the use of the engineering design method and process of Pahl and Beitz.
- Over a year's worth of co-op experience with General Electric's Wind Energy division.
- Experience working in heavy assembly, using and teaching the use of machine tools, the process of product design, C.A.D. (UG NX) and C.A.M.
- Numerous design awards from undergraduate design projects.

If you have a need for someone who is passionate about solving problems that require solutions in the form of equipment, contact me.

  • Graduate Research Assistant

    Georgia Institute of Technology

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    August 2007 — December 2009 (2 years 5 months)

    Earned my master's degree at Georgia Tech as a graduate research assistant on a project sponsored by Boeing. My thesis title was "Fatigue Crack Initiation in Cross-Ply Carbon Fiber Laminates." Involved experimental tensile fatigue testing of cross-ply laminates to determine the lower stress and strain threshold at which cracks initiated in the zero-degree plies. Compared these test results to observations made by other authors in the literature, and to the shear-lag model common in composites analysis. Classwork focused on mechanics of materials, composite mechanics, and engineering design theory. Graduated August 2009.
