Nick Wolfe’s Summary

Nicholas Wolfe recently received his Master of Science in the Global Energy Management (GEM) Program at the University of Colorado. GEM is designed to educate future leaders in global energy management and support management succession for the energy industry worldwide. During GEM, Nick spearheaded an international team to Ecuador for an analysis of current renewable energy efforts in the Galapagos Islands. With previous experience working in accounting and software for Cimarex Energy and Teton Energy, combined with a background in economics and international business from the University of Wyoming, Nick has a range of experiences relating to energy issues in the 22 countries he has explored. He is an active member of the Colorado Cleantech Industry Association Smart Grid Working Group and involved with Young Professionals in Energy (YPE).
