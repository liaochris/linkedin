Ask about the GRIPPING AID: (Not just for golfers!)
The Gripping Aid is an inexpensive, light-weight device that gives people with limited use of their hands a strong grip.
Developed in conjunction with physicians specializing in rheumatology, orthopedics, and neurology, Gripping Aids have proven successful in use by patients suffering from arthritis, carpal tunnel syndrome, Parkinson's disease, and missing digits.
People who can't make a fist-like grip may now be able to enjoy activities such as golfing, fishing, tennis and even weight lifting. People who had to stop work because they could no longer hold a hammer, paintbrush, broom or pen can return to their jobs. Medical facilities are using Gripping Aids for occupational and physical therapy, burn units rheumatology, orthopedic units , stroke victims, and after hand surgery.
