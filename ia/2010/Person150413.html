  • Author: Public Radio News Director Guide

    National Public Radio

    (Non-Profit; Broadcast Media industry)

    February 2008 — November 2008 (10 months)

    http://prndg.org/

    The Public Radio News Directors Guide (PRNDG) is the first compendium of its kind providing a comprehensive body of advice, best practices and additional resources.

    The PRNDG is arranged in six chapters:
    1) Your Job -- the roles and responsibilities of news directors
    2) Your Team -- the hiring and performance of the news team
    3) Your News -- the editorial planning and production process
    4) Your Audience -- the needs and preferences of listeners
    5) Your Medium -- the emerging multi-platform environment
    6) Your World -- the greater public broadcasting system

    The guide includes a “Field Kit” section composed of individual case studies, work samples, reusable templates, links to outside resources, and do-it-yourself tutorials. A search function helps users find content quickly and easily.

    Michael Marcotte, the guide's author and the former news director of KPBS, San Diego and KPLU, Seattle-Tacoma, consulted with more than 80 news directors in compiling the guide. New media journalist Andrew Phelps, now editor of wbur.org, produced the PRNDG Web site.

    The guide's launch represents completion of the two-year "start-up phase" in what is envisioned as a multi-stage rollout. The coming phase includes plans to add a front-page blog, more multimedia content and site-based social-networking tools. These will increase the value of the PRNDG as a place for timely discussion of journalism issues while allowing users to add content.

    The project was funded by NPR's LNI and PRNDI.

  • Director: Jacobs Project for Reporting Excellence

    KPBS

    (Non-Profit; Broadcast Media industry)

    September 2006 — November 2007 (1 year 3 months)

    The Jacobs Project for Reporting Excellence is a KPBS Training Program in Local Multi-Media Journalism, Supported by Joan and Irwin Jacobs Fund for Reporting Excellence

    The Jacobs Project for Reporting Excellence offers fellowships (of 9 to 18 months) to professional reporters who want to learn and practice innovative methods of news gathering, news packaging and news delivery.

    The Project was initiated by KPBS and NPR in the fall of 2006. Its goals are to identify and train journalists who wish to serve local public media; and to help KPBS and other public broadcasters to discover and refine tools, technology, and methods of gathering, sharing and distributing digital editorial content.

    The Project is funded on a five-year timeline and would train two participants at a time. The journalists are selected for a full-time, paid fellowship ranging in length from no less than 9 months to no more than 18 months (also with the possibility of granting an extension). The fellows work in concert with the web, television and talk show departments.

    *******************************************************************
    Project Vision: Incubate the Journalistic Practices of Tomorrow.

    Project Planks:
    1. Innovation/Experimentation: Let creativity lead to new products, new concepts, new story forms.

    2. Individual Multi-Media Skills: Advance the abilities of the journalist to work on multiple platforms.

    3. Station Convergence: Provide solutions and guidance to help our organization change.

    4. Community Engagement: Open doors to public participation and deeper local service.

    5. Global Learning Lab: Import and export the best training, from inside and outside the industry.

    6. Evolution: Show progression in benchmarks of success. Chart change in standards and practices. Write “the textbook” on new media.
