George Kopas’s Summary

Entrepreneur, visionary, deal maker - working in multiple geographies, especially San Francisco, NY, Denver, Florida, and converging industries, especially new media, IPTV, broadcasting, e-commerce, e-learning, mobile computing, IT, satellite, wireless, Web 2.0/3.0. International experience in Europe, Asia Pacific, Canada. Work with investors & senior management in business assessments, business launches, pulling together deals & ventures - includes due diligence, funding strategies, strategic planning, market development, division spin-outs, buying & selling businesses. Clients/strategic relationships have included Stanford, E*Trade, LSI Logic, Toshiba, NEC, Intel, Carlson Marketing, Sears, Ingram Micro, Pan Am, NW Airlines, SuperMac, TBC, PacificUS, ADG. Previously, General Manager of Video 7, multi-media division within LSI Logic – helped pioneer multi-media industry, developed top customer rating, channel & brand while forging relationships with Microsoft, HP, TechData, Arrow, Lucas Arts, AutoDesk. Sold Video 7 to SPEA - CAD leader in Germany. At ComputerLand, largest reseller & integrator of PC's in the world - Vendor Relations Manager, Product Marketing Manager, Corporate Sales – responsible for over $1Billion in revenues with management of private label line, IBM, Japanese vendors including Toshiba, NEC, Okidata, Epson, Star. Played key role in establishing mobile computing industry, private TV network, #1 brand in category, marketing calendar & first major private label line in PC industry. Set sales records with Phoenix Metro Store. Organized first airline/laptop promotion in history with Pan Am, Microsoft, NEC. BA, University of California, Berkeley. At Cal, played rugby on national championship team, toured Australia/NZ, sports writer for the Daily Cal, programmed main-frame computers with IBM at Untied Airlines, Southern Pacific. Graduated Top of Class in supply school in US Army. Raised in San Francisco, graduated from Lowell High School.
