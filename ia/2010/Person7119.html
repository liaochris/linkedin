Alfred Glossbrenner’s Summary

I've been a professional writer for over 30 years, and I have helped hundreds of individuals and companies successfully fill a very wide range of communications needs. Whether it's a Web site, a video, an instruction manual, or a book, I'm pretty sure we can help you as well.

I can say that because I've learned a few things over the years. I've learned that giving clients what they want isn't the hard part. The real challenge is getting a clear idea of what they want in the first place. That means asking the right questions and listening very carefully to the answers. This is something we are very, very good at it. We call it the "FireCrystal difference."

Interviewing people to draw out of them what they want to say or what they know gives me the information I need to hit the nail on the head with just about every project. Usually, all it takes is a first draft and one light revision. Again, it's the FireCrystal difference.

The topic doesn't matter. It can be anything from baseball (*The Art of Hitting .300*, written with Charley Lau, the late New York Yankees hitting coach) to hard disk drives to in-ground swimming pools.

When my wife and frequent co-author, Emily Glossbrenner, left IBM to join FireCrystal, she brought not only a strong editorial presence but also the skills of an extremely capable production executive. Emily makes it possible for FireCrystal to take on just about any communications challenge you can imagine.

I'm way over the preferred length of an "elevator pitch," so let me close by saying that you have nothing to lose by contacting FireCrystal about your current communications needs. We can chat on the phone and see where things go. As I said, I'm pretty sure that Emily and I and FireCrystal can help, regardless of your goals.

But if we can't, we will certainly tell you so and do our best to suggest some other firm or solution. For more information, please visit us at www.firecrystal.com.
