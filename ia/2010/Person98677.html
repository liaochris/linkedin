Jimmy Van Alen’s Summary

Jimmy Van Alen is a startup executive specializing in early stage technology companies. He has been on both the venture capital and operational sides of the startup arena and has experience in the funding, management and sale of entrepreneurial ventures.

Most recently Jimmy was President/COO at CourseAdvisor, Inc., where he was responsible for the company's management, operations and strategy as revenues quintupled to over $35 million. He oversaw the sale of the company to the Washington Post (NYSE:WPO) at several times invested capital and in his previous role as CFO he shepherded the company through its first institutional round of financing, a raise of $12 million. He left CourseAdvisor after completing a one-year obligation post-acquisition and overseeing the transition to his successor. He has since then been taking time off before his next venture.

Prior to joining CourseAdvisor, Jimmy was Executive in Residence at the Slater Technology Fund, where he evaluated investment opportunities and advised portfolio companies. Before Slater, Jimmy was VP of Finance and Operations at Incipient, Inc., a venture-backed storage software company, where he raised $19 million in equity and debt financing and was active in strategic planning, corporate marketing and business development.

Previous to Incipient, Jimmy was a VP at the venture capital fund RAF NetVentures. Jimmy also founded and served as President of Greenlight Services Corporation, an internet startup. Out of college he held research and analysis positions with Strategic Advisors International, an investment banking advisory firm.

Jimmy holds a BA in Economics from the University of Pennsylvania and an MBA (summa cum laude) from the F.W. Olin Graduate School of Business at Babson College where he was the Andrew H. Kalnow Venture Capital Fellow, a Ralph Z. and Charlotte R. Sorenson Scholar and winner of the Douglass team-based business plan competition.
