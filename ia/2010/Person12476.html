Andy Blacker’s Summary

ANDREW L. BLACKER
Andrew L. Blacker has been an innovator in the Media space for nearly 15 years. He most recently served as the Vice President of Discovery Communications Commerce division. While in this position, Mr. Blacker launched and managed two new business divisions, and was also a member of the Executive Strategy Group—which reported to the division President.

Prior to his work at Discovery, Mr. Blacker was Director of the AOL Networks Marketing and Strategy group. Adapting the model established by TV/Broadcast, Mr. Blacker created and led a new initiative for AOL, named Network Specials. Mr. Blacker started at AOL as the Director of Creative Development, working on such successes as Look, Listen and Play, Celebrity You've Got Mail, AOL's Boeing Name the Plane and AOL's Holiday Event in 2003.

Mr. Blacker also created America Takes It Off, a partnership between AOL and Good Morning America in January 2006--which was a first ever online and broadcast partnership between the two media companies, utilizing bi-directional programming for the consumer. He is also the creator and Executive Producer of the Chief Everything Officer Network Special

Prior to Mr. Blacker’s work at AOL, he was the Vice President, Sales & Marketing at Ebrick.com in 2001-2002. He also served as the Senior Director, Business Development, Sales and Marketing at Sticky Networks Inc in 1999-2000. Mr. Blacker managed InfoSeek Corporation’s Business Development and various Sales efforts from 1995-1999.

Right out of college, Andrew Blacker worked for Hearst New Media & Technology, where he created the first interactive classifieds section for Hearst New Media and HomeArts.com.

Andrew Blacker graduated in 1994 with a Bachelor of Arts Degree in English and Small Business Development at Cornell University. At Cornell, he received the Small Business & Enterprise Award in 1993.
