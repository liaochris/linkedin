Chad Hassler’s Summary

At the age of 16, on the advice of a friend, I joined my high school tennis team. I had only picked up a racquet one time previously when I played with my Mother while we were on vacation. I was probably 8 when that happened.

I arrived on the court for practice at about 300lbs. On top of that, I couldn't hit a ball to save my life. Everything flew long. But every 15th to 20th ball I would hit a decent shot. That was enough for me to keep coming back and to keep working at tennis.

As Andre Agassi said in his book, 'Open': "Hitting a clean ball, the only moment of peace".

I proceeded to lose over 100lbs in my 3 years of high school tennis. I never was much good at the game at that time, but it had given me so much. I could hit decent balls every now and again. It was enough to keep me coming back.

Then I went to community college and started working a lot more. I gained 60lbs back in 2 years. A girlfriend may have had something to do with that result as well. But after a tough breakup, I hit the courts again to find myself and my game.

I had come back to my high school and to assist both the boy's and girl's tennis team for 2 seasons each. Towards the end of my final season, I was starting to get into shape again. I dropped down to 240 by the time I started college away from home at SUNY Oswego. I wanted to play for the men's team. Even though it was division III, I just wanted to be on the team.

I worked out the entire fall semester. I ran in the frigid Lake Ontario air for miles. I would run to the gym over a mile away, work out, and run back in 10 degree whether.

My hard work paid off. By the start of the men's tennis season I was 220lbs. When I left Oswego at the end of the spring semester, I was 210.

With nothing to do over the course of that summer, I shed my last 10 lbs and finally made it to 200lbs again.

I'm on this journey again. This time from 230lbs down. The long and short of this story is...tennis saved my life.
