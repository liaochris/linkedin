Scott Bundy’s Summary

I am a senior-level RF engineer experienced in the design and development of antennas and phased-array antenna systems (primarily for defense-related applications), including both active (amplifiers and phase shifters) and passive (corporate feed networks) circuitry supporting those antenna arrays. I also have experience in the wireless communication industry, where I have worked for both small after-market equipment manufacturers as well as a wireless service provider. With strong technical & analytical problem-solving skills and attention to detail, I have a well-balanced background in both the theoretical and experimental aspects of electromagnetics, from both an academic and a professional perspective.

Scott Bundy’s Specialties:

Single-Element Antenna Design.
Phased-Array Antenna Design.
Active & Passive Microwave Circuit Design.

Scott Bundy’s Experience

  • Senior RF Engineer

    ATK (Alliant Techsystems)

    (Public Company; ATK; Defense & Space industry)

    November 2002 — March 2010 (7 years 5 months)

    Designed & developed individual antennas and phased-array antennas for applications ranging from satellite antennas, covert concealed antenna arrays, and wideband, wide-scan-volume, low-observable arrays, as well as the associated active and passive circuitry for feeding those arrays.
    • Designed & developed processes for fabrication of individual antennas for guidance of advanced weapons systems, resulting in antennas capable of maintaining the RF link for guidance in a highvelocity environment and withstanding excessive G-forces during launch.
    • Designed & developed processes for fabrication of the passive aperture portion of widebandwidth, wide-scan-volume, low-observable phased-array antennas, resulting in state-of-the-art phased-array antenna technology for defense applications.
    • Designed & developed processes for fabrication of a very-wideband fixed-beam phased-array antenna for covert signal detection, resulting in a single portable low-profile antenna that replaced a full suite of antennas for signal interception over a wide bandwidth. Produced a complete set of production documentation for this product (detailed operations sheets, drawings, acceptance test procedures, and programs for automated processing of test data).
    • Designed two custom high-gain low-profile fixed-beam phased-array antenna systems for covert communication links, resulting in concealed antenna systems deployed in the field.
    • Designed the active portion of two electrically-scanned phased-array antennas (one for the transmit command data link array for the TACSAT-II satellite), including the corporate feed network, power amplifiers, and switched-line phase shifters, with the associated biasing circuitry.

Scott Bundy’s Education

  • University of Colorado at Boulder

    Ph.D. , Electrical Engineering - Electromagnetics , 1992 — 1994

    I have obtained a Ph.D. in Electrical Engineering with a specialization in Electromagnetics. Within my coursework, I focused on both electromagnetic theory and numerical analysis techniques in electromagnetics, as well as graduate-level mathematics classes. My thesis involved a mix of both theoretical and experimental approaches to engineering design. I developed a full-wave moment method for analyzing and designing arrays of quasi-optical oscillators, in which the radiated power is combined in free space rather than in traditional combining feed networks. I also fabricated and tested several active and passive arrays as part of the validation of my thesis work.
