Monica Mallini, PE’s Summary

Monica Mallini, PE is an electrical and mechanical engineer with a master’s degree in engineering, exceptional writing skills, and experience in the electric power, petrochemicals, and aerospace industries. She is proud to be an educator but does not want to be mapped into a stereotyped view of that role as an impediment to other roles. Monica is a superb ideator whose wide angle view and skill in leveraging opportunities and resources benefits all stakeholders. She has functioned in numerous leadership roles, particularly as an IEEE volunteer. Monica expects to eventually complete a PhD in electrical engineering and is an advocate of lifelong learning. She has not mapped out her life's goals in minute detail; instead, she is developing her anti-bucket list, a work in progress to make the next half-century more productive, more energizing, and more profitable than the last. (Please note that the list deliberately omits game show participation and activities that resemble projectile motion but may include manual labor.) Monica is open to interdisciplinary projects, collaboration partners, and unique opportunities, particularly involving development of the Smart Grid. She still holds the youthfully optimistic view that she can make the world a better place, with a well crafted plan and a few good collaboration partners. Let's discuss this over coffee and scones.

    IEEE (the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers) is the world's largest technical professional society. The job of an IEEE Section Chair is to manage the operations of the Section in the best interests of the local members and to provide leadership, guidance and a sense of purpose for other Section volunteers. Northern Virginia Section, founded in 1977, is the largest IEEE Section in Region 2, with about 6,000 members. As Chair, I appoint, direct, guide, and mentor about 20 subordinate committee chairs and fill vacant technical society chapter chair and executive committee positions. I set goals, priorities, and direction in the areas of fiscal management, public outreach, service, and communication, professional development and training, membership recruitment, retention, and outreach, and support of corporate and regional organizational objectives.
