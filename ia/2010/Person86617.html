Jackie Bradbury’s Summary

Marketer passionate about understanding and influencing consumer behavior. I'm constantly contemplating the following questions:

* Why do people choose to buy what they do, when they do?
* What opportunity costs do they incur when they do so? Is there a way to mitigate those opportunity costs, perhaps with innovative loyalty programs to offset the "expense"?
* How does smart targeted marketing influence that behavior (and how is "marketing" completely ineffective)?
* Is it better to consolidate a loyal audience, or to seek incremental growth? Are the two mutually exclusive?
* Are there customers that should be "fired", or at least, not a target for marketing, even if they might be a valuable revenue stream? When do you make that decision, and why?
* If you have a detailed database of customer demographics and buying behavior - how do you use it beyond using simple discounting to grow revenue? What other tools can be utilized to encourage consolidation of spend to achieve aspriational goals?
* How do you pull together very different, but well meaning, ideas for marketing into something that is coherent, consistent, and executable?
* How do we avoid analysis paralysis?
* When is good enough... good enough? Is perfection always the goal?
* Is one-to-one segmentation feasible, and how is it done?
* How does what you can execute from an operational standpoint influence strategic and tactical planning?

Exploring these questions while driving revenue growth is what motivates me!
