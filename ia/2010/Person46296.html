Dana Dean Doering’s Summary

Dana Dean Doering ARNP, Educational Consultant & Clinician

Dana Dean Doering has dedicated a 35 year career to helping children and families improve their lives. She has also distinguished herself as an international child advocate, parenting coordinator, and children's book photo illustrator. She helps children, adolescents, young adults and parents explore creative options for educational planning. Her goal is to uplift a child and student's life and bring new possibilities, strategies and solutions to bear.

She believes in optimizing a child's future, talents and capacities. She and the team build recommendations from a strength based approach centered on child, adolescent, young adult and family development. With over 13 years of college and advanced graduate studies at University of Texas, Johns Hopkins University and University of Washington, Dana continues to be an informed and valued contributor in her field. Dana has served in both academic and research positions, as well as in many decades of private practice. Dana is also involved in volunteer work, community development and community philanthropy.

Other areas of interest include her work in international child advocacy and human rights, reading and photography. She is also an avid garden designer.

Dana Dean Doering’s Experience

  • President

    Dean Doering Educational Consultants

    (Mental Health Care industry)

    September 1996 — Present (14 years 5 months)

    Educational Consultant: National & international specialty in educational assessment, complex therapeutic assessment & placement, 504 & IEP, and adjustment to best fit school environment. Specialists travel over 150 days/year to interact with over 2000 schools & programs to maintain extensive data bases. Networks and contacts are developed with key people such as teachers, administrators & counselors in schools, specialty programs, and universities & research institutions.

    Clinical Practice: Child (age 1-28) psychiatric complex case consultation, Rx evaluation, assessment and testing, diagnostic determinations, and crisis management. Practice provides evaluation & treatment of children and adolescents with a wide range of problems. Special interests include depression/anxiety, ADHD, autism/Asperger’s, suicide prevention/intervention, grief & loss, eating disorders, children in exceptional circumstances, and international child advocacy.

    Dana directs & leads the team on every case in this private practice with pediatric neuropsychologist, psychiatrist, social worker and family counselors and ADHD coaches. We focus on liaison with community pediatric practices, placement recommendations, suicide risk assessment, child neglect and abuse assessment, young adult coaching, parenting interventions, clinical investigations, and consultation to child service agencies & school districts. Teaching and tutorial staff are also under direct supervision. Day treatment and extended assessment capacity with enrollment in Canterbury Academy.
