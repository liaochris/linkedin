Kelcy Erbele’s Summary

When I was in 6th grade, our art teacher gave the class an in-class assignment. We had to make a flip-book animation using a stack of post-it notes. I observed other students animating crude drawings of a ball bouncing or a flat image of a plant sprouting from the ground. All of them barely using the entire post-it note stack that they had been given. Most students finished early so they could chat amongst their friends. My flip-book project required me to ask for a second stack of post-it notes and had taken the entire class for me to finish. The final result was that of a cartoon turtle walking along, soon finding himself stampeded by a group of jogging rabbits. The animation ended with the hares passing along and the turtle spinning slowly to a halt. His eyes blinked several times from inside his shell. My art teacher was astounded; my friends watched my animation with joy. This is what I wanted to do. I wanted to animate.

I am currently a broadcast graphics animator for Office Depot. I love what I do and the team I work with. In my own time I still enjoy and continue to work on freelance projects such as 2D animation and illustration.

If you have any questions or would like to contact me, you can email me at kberbele@yahoo.com
