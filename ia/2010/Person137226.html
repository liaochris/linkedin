Matthew Farber’s Summary

I've worked in the games industry for 8 years now, doing all sorts of things from game design, programming, UI art, even leading a studio early in my career formed from the Frag.Ops mod team. I specialize in engineering, but also enjoy design, both game and technical. I've gained extensive console experience as well since 2006 on both the Xbox 360 and PS3, having since worked only on titles that were specifically designed for these platforms. That said, I'm no stranger to DirectX or OpenGL programming and have no trouble sitting down with a Mac and popping open CodeWarrior when I plan to maintain both PC and Mac versions of a game.

That said, my goals are to eventually return to design, perhaps technical design as a forestep. Spending so many of my earlier years doing both design and engineering, I think I could bring an excellent design perspective as a technical guy, opposed to someone who doesn't understand technical requirements for a game. I'm an engineer -- no doubt about that. But I'm an engineer who wants to mend both design and programming aspects to a game. As someone (Yahtzee) once said, a game isn't worth crap if it's no damn fun. Paraphrased for civility.
