Drew Eckhardt’s Summary

I've spent my fifteen-year career developing complex systems software for business critical applications in a variety of markets including telephony, broadcast television, and storage with an emphasis on distributed systems and high-availability.

One highlight was my position at Allocity, where I started as first technical employee and finished with a clustered, replicated, storage virtualization product, a tight-knit team of a dozen engineers, and a notebook full of ideas which should have kept us busy for another 3+ years.

In those two years I wrote 50,000 lines of code and pursued my research interests in Recovery Oriented Computing (ROC) through disk structures optimized for temporally indexed retrieval (thus minimizing mean time to recovery with operation transfer replication) and zero IO cost snapshots (minimizing data-loss due to human failure in administrative or application software development tasks).
