Charles Roop’s Summary

A recent geography graduate from the University of Florida with a focus in meteorology and climatology, and an outside concentration in journalism. Received a certificate in meteorology and climate from UF. Experience with forecasting for the Independent Florida Alligator as well as creating and distributing forecasts via social networking outlets (Blogger, Twitter, Facebook). Was in the top five in the forecasting contest in Weather and Forecasting at UF. Have also assisted in research at the Geography department: Chasing seabreeze thunderstorms to gather data, and analyzing data for Titusville, FL to determine long-term temperature and rainfall trends for the Cape Canaveral area. Have been very active in local organizations such as the UF AMS chapter and local storm spotter networks. Participated in public outreach such as NOAA Weather Radio programing at public venues.

Also a photographer with five years of experience, at least two and a half in photojournalism. Have photographed sports, breaking news, events, speakers, and extreme weather. Experience in both editorial and public relations photography. Also have experience with videography and video editing. Have produced small, quick video project - which mostly consist of severe weather events.

The goal is to do graduate studies in atmospheric sciences.

Charles Roop’s Specialties:

Adobe Photoshop, Photo Mechanic, Aperture, iWork, Soundslides, Final Cut Express, iMovie, MS Office. Light knowledge of Flash, XHTML & CSS. Familiar with Canon EOS and Nikon camera systems.

Also have experience with Geographical Information Systems using ESRI's ArcGIS. For weather forecasting: Can read and interpret surface and upper-atmospheric maps and data, satellite imagery, Doppler radar and guidance models to create forecasts.
