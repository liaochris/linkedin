Michael Tetreault’s Summary

Michael C. Tetreault serves currently serves as the Executive Director of the Concierge Medicine Research Collective as well as the Editor-In-Chief of Concierge Medicine Today. He brings over 13 years of experience as a marketing, public relations, sales and brand manager. Michael oversees all aspects of advertising, public relations, events, new business R&D and reputation management activities for networks and clients. He also serves as Client Liaison to health care clients, managed care networks and affiliated partners.

Among Michael's accomplishments is the creation of the first online and print publication for the concierge medicine industry, Concierge Medicine Today, as well as Co-Founder of the Concierge Medicine Research Collective, an independent health care research center based in Atlanta, GA. Michael has also created two community and volunteer programs, The Go Fish Project and Healthy Town USA. The Go Fish Project is a benevolence program in which health care providers donate their time, money and services to designated charities each year. Healthy Town USA, is a health care promotions and event management program that provides opportunity for health care professionals to volunteer and inturn connects them with world-class professional athletes as provisional patients. Under Michael's direction, these initiatives have developed health care and marketing partnerships with: AARP; ADP; Dannon Yogurt; Habitat For Humanity; The United Way; Kaiser Permanente Health Plans; USAA Duathlon World and National Championships; ITU Duathlon World and National Championships; USA Triathlon; the Marine Corps Marathon and many others. Michael was the first public relations strategist to incorporate chiropractic health care services into the Marine Corps Marathon in 2003.
