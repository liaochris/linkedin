Sunil Reddy Repaka, P.E.’s Experience

  • Project Engineer

    California Department of Transportation

    (Government Agency; Civil Engineering industry)

    April 2006 — Present (4 years 1 month)

    Currently working on Trade Corridor Improvement Fund (TCIF) projects that include "Port Access" improvements, conducting preliminary design, oversight and project management tasks. The TCIF projects include a new International Port of Entry (East Otay Mesa POE) and a new state highway (State Route 11), plus a number of highway projects that are vital to improving national and international trade in the realm of $1.3 billion.

    Worked on preparation and coordination of Project Initiation Documents, Project Reports, Plans, Specifications and Estimates (PS&E) and Design Exceptions for transportation projects including a major lane-widening project on Interstate 8 (I-8) in San Diego County in the city of El Cajon, a 2.1 miles, $20 million project. Prepared Fact Sheets for Exception to Advisory Design Standards, construction schedule analysis using the Critical Path Method (CPM), and Construction plan for pollutant discharge.

    Worked as an Assistant Resident Engineer during construction of I-5 & 28th St. lane widening project, Rte 75 Coronado Bridge Utilities improvement project, Rte 94 Lane widening project and new on-ramp on Rte 67 near Magnolia Ave. Prior to that held other important positions as Utility Engineer and Pavement Management Engineer.

  • Project Engineer

    Waggoner Engineering, Inc.,

    (Privately Held; Program Development industry)

    January 2004 — March 2006 (2 years 3 months)

    Project lead on various projects:

    Byram Clinton/Norrell Corridor, I-20 & I-55 connecting arterial - responsible for transportation planning, modeling process, community impact assessments, and route location for the proposed roadway using aerial imagery.

    East Metro Corridor, I-20 & HWY 25 connector road - responsible for the route location, preliminary design, including traffic calming design, conducting economical path analysis to increase vehicle capacity, traffic flow. and worked with team on environmental evaluation and right-of-way acquisition.

    Statewide Floodplain Mapping, State of Mississippi - Tasks conducted with Mississippi Emergency Management Agency (MEMA), and the Mississippi Department of Environmental Quality (MDEQ) in response to FEMA Region IV request. Conducted 'Scoping Meetings' to get communities input and prepared scoping reports for floodplain mapping to FEMA Region IV. Performed Hydrology and Hydraulics analysis, Terrain mapping, Floodplain boundaries delineation, DFIRM production. Served as Project Engineer in support tasks for the development of a strategic plan and prototype of the State of Mississippi Digital Earth Model (MDEM) program. This program included digital orthophoto, topography, property ownership, hydrology, transportation, and political boundaries of Mississippi. Pioneer in the practical use and applications of LIDAR, Photogrammetry, Remote Sensing, and GIS. Coordinated and trained CAD/GIS technicians, and implemented quality control procedures.

    City of Richland GIS Program - Designed the Water/Wastewater Utilities Management System in GIS, tasks included setting up Cities GIS system, sending out survey crew to collect GPS location of all utilities and going through 40 years of as-built data sheets and transferring information to GIS database, trained Public Works staff to use this information on a day-to-day basis for repairs and maintenance.

  • Graduate Research Assistant

    National Science Foundation - Engineering Research Center (NSF-ERC)

    (Research industry)

    January 2002 — December 2003 (2 years )

    Worked as a Graduate Research Assistant at the prestigious National Science Foundation - Engineering Research Center (NSF-ERC), Mississippi State University (MSU) as a Graduate Research Assistant on joint venture program with USDOT and NASA to develop transportation projects utilizing Remote Sensing and geospatial technologies for environmental modeling, including environmental impact assessment, data integration and streamlining. Developed analysis tools using Remote Sensed data for planning transportation networks. Work involved retrieving information from satellite imagery. The information retrieved by the analysis helped scientists and environmentalist to analyze detailed environmental and roadway data. Worked on CSX railroad relocation project along Mississippi Gulf Coast and responsible for support tasks at ERC-MSU with AECOM (formerly DMJM+HARRIS). Also involved in research with Remote Sensing and GIS applications for watershed management.
