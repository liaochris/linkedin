Stephen Humes’s Summary

I am an experienced and creative energy and environmental lawyer with a leading practice in the emerging issues of climate change and renewable energy, including solar, thermal storage and other green technologies. I frequently advise clients on state and federal energy regulatory matters and transactions involving public utilities and competitive electric and natural gas supply, cogeneration, distributed generation and energy efficiency. I also advise clients on greenhouse gas emission reduction credit transactions and issues relating to insurance policies and global warming.

Stephen Humes’s Specialties:

Electric and natural gas supplier regulation, licensing, contracts and litigation; solar power purchase agreements and solar leases; renewable energy project finance and development; energy facility siting and permitting; energy contracts; environmental permitting; environmental remediation; Brownfields transactions; environmental liability transfers.

    (Partnership; 501-1000 employees; Law Practice industry)

    Steve's transactional, regulatory compliance and litigation practice focuses on environmental, energy, public utility and climate change law. He co-chairs the firm's Climate Change and Renewable Energy Group. He has more than 15 years of experience on environmental and energy matters, including transactional due diligence and contractual matters and environmental compliance. He also has experience on natural resource damage investigations, assessments and restoration implementation arising in marine oil spills. He is currently advising clients in support of the development of an electric power generation facility and is advising property owner and developer clients on alternative energy facility development, regulatory approvals, interconnection and other siting issues, including solar, wind, biodiesel and cogeneration.
