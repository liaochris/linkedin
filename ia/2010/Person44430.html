Arthur Ceria’s Summary

ARTHUR CERIA

In the last decade, Arthur Ceria has created some of the most impactful work in the digital media world. He is responsible for award-winning online and integrated campaigns for companies including Yahoo!, Cisco, Volvo, MCI, Intel, Evian and Polaroid, and is widely considered a leader in the field. In 2005, Arthur was named Creative All Star by Media Post, an online resource for advertising media professionals. Whether he’s launching pixilated promotions for one of the world’s largest technology giants or concepting a web site that lets fashionistas show off their style, this bona fide media lover knows how to “wow” international audiences with cutting-edge creative.

Until recently, Arthur was Senior Partner and Executive Creative Director of OgilvyOne San Francisco, an office he established and co-managed. In his two years there, Arthur grew the San Francisco team to 70 people, oversaw all of the online marketing for Yahoo! (including the award-winning launch for Yahoo! Music ). He also helped conceive and launch Cisco’s blog-centric branding campaign. Prior to joining OgilvyOne, Arthur was at Euro RSCG Worldwide, New York, where he spent four years as Executive Creative Director for integrated communications on Volvo, MCI, New Balance, Intel and Polaroid. He started his career in advertising as Creative Director at Agency.com, and before that worked in partnership with the architecture firm Gwathmey Siegel as a creative director, where he managed five designers and launched a 3-D interactive experience for the Basketball Hall of Fame. Prior to working in advertising, Arthur headed up his own design firm, Ceria/Cohen Studio, in his native France.

Arthur Ceria’s Specialties:

Arthur holds an MFA from Yale University with a focus on Graphic Design and an MFA from Ecole National Superieure des Arts Decoratifs in Paris. He has received just about every industry honor there is, including the One Show Award, Cannes Lion finalists, the @d:Tech Award for best consumer site, Adweek’s Best Online Campaign of the Year and the MSN Creative Award for Best Rich Media.
