Drew Hevle’s Summary

I have been involved in corrosion control and pipeline integrity activities my whole career, primarily with natural gas transmission pipelines both onshore and offshore, but also with hazardous liquid transmission pipelines and storage tanks, as well as natural gas gathering, production, and storage. I've addressed a wide variety of corrosion threats including external corrosion and cathodic protection, internal corrosion control and chemical inhibition, stress-corrosion cracking dection and remediation, high-temperature corrosion, atmospheric corrosion, and erosion-corrosion. I have developed and managed company standards and training for O&M, design, construction, painting, coating, and integrity assessment procedures. I am chairman of NACE technical committee TG 370 Pipeline Corrosion Management and TG 211 Application of the 100-mV Polarization Criterion, as well as acting as vice-chair for a number of other NACE technical committees and task groups related to cathodic protection, coatings, internal corrosion, and pipeline integrity.

Drew Hevle’s Specialties:

I am involved in the development of the Direct Assessment methods of integrity assessment, with an extensive background in close-interval survey.
