Joe Walter’s Summary

After almost 15 years of experience as Land Development Manager – Golf for Toll Brothers, Inc. and as Design Associate for The PGA TOUR combined, I am confident that my skills in golf course and large-scale community design / construction, with added experience in permitting, budgeting, site administration, and infrastructure would make me a valued contributor.

Joe Walter’s Experience

  • Land Development Manager - Golf

    Toll Brothers, Inc.

    (Architecture & Planning industry)

    June 2006 — November 2008 (2 years 6 months)

    As Land Development Manager-Golf for Toll Brothers, Inc., I had almost three years of experience coordinating, managing, and operating large scale Master Plan Golf Course communities’ nationwide. My responsibilities included overseeing projects from the initial vision through the bid process, engineering, and completion. I would interview and hire essential consultants and understand the importance that all plans, construction, and supervision were environmentally compliant to county, state, and federal standards. Working for the developer I was responsible for budgeting, including balancing the budget throughout the project, while facilitating potential scope changes. I gained experience in commercial Clubhouse construction and Golf Course operations. While working for Toll Brothers, I managed the completion of new construction and renovation projects in North Carolina, South Carolina, Illinois, and Florida.

  • Design Associate

    PGA TOUR, Design Services, Inc

    (Architecture & Planning industry)

    May 1995 — June 2006 (11 years 2 months)

    Before joining Toll Brothers, Inc., I was responsible for various aspects of golf course design and construction while working as a Design Associate with The PGA TOUR for twelve years. My primary role was to develop projects for The First Tee, a non-profit organization that provides life skills and a learning environment while teaching the game of golf to children who would not typically have a chance to experience the sport. In this capacity, I would design the initial routing plan, manage the permitting, and develop construction documents using AutoCad. After all documents were complete, I continued to participate in each project as the liaison between contractors, owners, and engineers. I would also facilitate the bid process including development of the specifications, management of pre-bid meetings, and negotiation of final costs. During construction I was responsible for on-site observation which involved development, communication, and resolution of “punch lists.”
