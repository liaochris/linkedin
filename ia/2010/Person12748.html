Andy Ramirez’s Summary

I've been working on computers since I was 12 years old. It all began with an Apple IIe and learning Basic. By the age of 18 I was the youngest manager at a company called Omnigon, where I ran the I.T. department. At Omnigon I worked on cutting edge technology with computer scientists, engineers and PhD’s. Getting involved in building fiber networks, N.O.C.’s and advanced compression technology allowed me to experience advances in technology very few people get to work with. I knew then that the internet and interactive communication was my future.

I am a natural problem solver. This lead me to focus my career on I.T. and web technology. I now have 10 years of experience working with various operating systems, tools, server systems, client systems, SEO, SEM, HTML and much more.

I strongly believe that no person is ever done learning. I strive to improve myself as a manger, as a marketer and as a technical engineer in order to be able to teach those that come after me

My current position allows me to use my IT skill set to bridge the gap between marketing needs and IT capabilities. We've built a team at Vistage that brings the whole package to help our company grow. Providing value for our members and sharing what that value is with potential members, our execution is akin to what a Ferrari is in the automotive world.

I believe that any organization that wants to have a powerful online presence must understand what it means to be consumer oriented. I understand the difference between packing an application with features and giving the customer what they need. More is less, and less is more. I believe that there is no “magic bullet” to online marketing. The job of an online marketer is to create a clear strategy to answering the fears and needs of the target consumer. It’s not just TGIF (Twitter, Google, Internet Ad’s and Facebook), marketing is about knowing what people want and helping them find the solution at the time that they want it.
