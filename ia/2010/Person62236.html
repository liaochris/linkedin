Edward Altman’s Summary

I spent the first half of my career in the Financial Services Industry, the last half in Media & Entertainment serving as the CIO at MGM and the Global Practice Head - Media and Entertainment, for TCS (Tata Consultancy Services). As CIO, my team rebuilt MGM's IT organization which then developed and implemented a critical corporate-wide system that resulted in a nomination for a Computerworld/Smithsonian Award - a first for any Hollywood Studio.

Since leaving MGM I have been on the Systems Integration side of the business starting both TCS' (TATA Consultancy Services) Global M&E Practice and then GlobalLogic's Digital Media Practice. For many years I was the only Global Practice Head in TCS not based in India. When I started at TCS in 2002, we had (3) M&E clients generating over $12MM in revenue/year. Within (2) years - every major Hollywood Studio was a client. When I left in 2008, TCS had (35) clients generating over $120MM in revenue/year across (7) industry sub-segments/micro-verticals (Hollywood, Music, Publishing, Broadcast Network, Cable Network, Business Information Services, and New Media).

At GlobalLogic, my focus is exclusively on the Digital Media, revenue generating and branding side of the Hollywood Studios, Cable & Broadcast Networks, and the Publishing Industry. I have also developed an industry-wide eco-system of partners and thought leaders to build the incredibly needed infrastructure to make the business of digital media as frictionless and scaleable as possible.

Edward Altman’s Specialties:

Digital Media and Global I/T Executive with deep skills having worked in both the industry side (former Hollywood CIO) and the Services/Consulting/Outsourcinig side (Global M&E Practice/Solutions Head), strong skill set in Business & Technology Transformation, I/T Governance, Sourcing strategies and models, and deep expertise in online media - business models and digital infrastructure, excellent personal network that cuts across industry executives, suppliers, start-ups and thought leaders.
