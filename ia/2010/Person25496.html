Robert Page’s Summary

Award-winning strategist with international experience in high-visibility, high-risk communications projects.

• Five Marketing Awards in 2008-09
• Branding and Product Launch
• Employee / Executive Communications
• International PR Agency Management
• Issues Management
• Global Mergers and Acquisitions
• Speech Writing, Media Training
• Highly Successful Record in China

As communications director for Lenovo, responsibilities included international media relations, trade media, social media, product marketing, and mergers and acquisitions. Directed 20-person team for the Beijing 2008 Olympic Games. Allocated and managed $3.5 million annual budget for PR agencies and supervised agency personnel. Reported to vice presidents in the US and China. Key initiatives included …

Olympic Games

Led Olympic communications team integrating the two corporate cultures after historic 2005 acquisition of the IBM PC Division. Directed communications for Torino 2006 Olympic Winter Games and Paralympic Games, Beijing 2008 Olympic Games. Led contingency and issues management for the controversial 2008 Olympic Torch Relay.

• Led PR strategy placing Lenovo ahead of all Beijing Olympic sponsors in global news coverage, according to two 2008 studies by Global Language Monitor. Dow Jones Insight ranked Lenovo third, behind McDonald’s and Coca-Cola. Generated 1.8 billion impressions, nearly double the stretch goal. Program won five industry awards.

• Promoted and managed social media programs that united athletes as game-time bloggers, selected torch bearers globally, and auctioned athlete memorabilia online – all historic firsts, driving 40% increase in Lenovo.com traffic.

M&A Communications, Executive Counsel

Led global preparation for attempted acquisitions, including Positivo Informatica of Brazil in 2008. Created communications, monitoring and contingency plan. Prepared senior leadership for World Economic Forum events in Switzerland, India, and China.

Robert Page’s Specialties:

Counsel to senior management, international media relations, technology, social media, employee and executive communications, and consumer, B2B and sports marketing.
