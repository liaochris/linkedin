Bill Hood’s Summary

I am a Creative Workaholic. I am a writer of books and articles, business consultant, educator, trainer, mentor, graphic designer, photographer, fine arts painter and more. I own many successful screenprinting related businesses, as well as others in other industries.

I was born into the screenprinting industry as the son of a screenprinter in the mid-1940s.

I begin consulting to other screenprinting shops in 1967 and have enjoyed helping others create new businesses and improve existing businesses.

In 1983, I started Austin Arts, which eventually became Solutions Journal Magazine. Solutions Journal was the first online magazine in the screenprinting industry and today enjoys one of the largest worldwide readerships of any industry trade magazine.

In 1984, I became involved in the printing of textiles, specifically T-shirts and opened Royal Tees Screenprinting in Austin, Texas. The business is still in operation today.

In 2000, I opened the School of Screenprinting where I train and educate individuals in comprehensive solutions for attaining control over the screenprinting process. Classes are held in cities throughout the United States and Mexico.

In 2008, I became a partner in UltraSol Ink. The 15-year old textile ink company produces standard plastisol, non-phthalate, non-pvc and waterbased inks. Having gained a foothold in Latin America, the company is now focusing on the U.S., Canadian and European markets.

I am currently consulting with small businesses, mostly in the screenprinting industry. I have clients throughout the United States, Mexico, London, India and as a result find myself traveling extensively.

Feel free to contact me at bill@solutionsjournal.com

Bill Hood’s Specialties:

Consulting with small and medium-size companies, training, speaking, conducting seminars and mentoring both individuals and teams.
