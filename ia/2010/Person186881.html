Steve Lodge’s Experience

  • Vice President, State Government Affairs

    National Confectioners Association

    (Non-Profit; Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    2004 — 2009 (5 years )

    Held responsibility for state government affairs for association. Lobbied state legislators. Testified before committees and state regulatory officials. Tracked issues in 50 states, editing regular state reports for members. Hired and supervised eight state lobbyists. Organized issue task forces of members and developed allies for association nationally. Attended annual conferences of state food and retail association allies and National Conference of State Legislatures.

    • Defeated proposed tax on candy in West Virginia, working with two in-state association allies and national trade association.
    • Organized panel of opponents testifying against candy tax in Pennsylvania, recruiting large member, small- to mid-size member and supplier member, showing how legislation would not only impact candy manufacturers, but those in state who supply candy industry.
    • Contacted several in-state and national organizations, notifying them of bills and potential impacts on their industry, while running regular conference calls among members on issues.
    • Aided in raising profile on labor bill in California, as two major in-state organizations picked up their degree of opposition to legislation and became face of opposition instead of confection industry.
    • Arranged for candy from association members in Illinois and Texas for use at receptions honoring incoming legislator presidents of the National Conference of State Legislatures (NCSL) from those two states, bringing attention to association and establishing relationship with important legislative leader of top state legislative organization.

  • Vice President of Legislative Affairs

    National Confectioners Association

    (Non-Profit; Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    1999 — 2004 (5 years )

    Lobbied congressional and executive offices. Directed federal and state activity for association, including management of legislative staff, coalition building, grassroots outreach and PAC strategy. Drafted letters and e-mails to Congress and state legislators, wrote issue papers, created reports to Board of Directors and Government Affairs Committee, columns for industry magazines and pieces for association newsletters and Web page. Delivered oral reports to Board of Directors. Presented at legislative conferences. Testified before legislators. Led committee and coalition meetings.

    • Led broad-based peanut reform coalition, winning historic reform of federal peanut program during consideration of Farm Bill by Congress, resulting in realistic prices for product to members.
    • Created grassroots campaign fighting unfair separate candy definition in Streamlined Sales Tax (SST) agreement, having members contact targeted governors, state legislators on NCSL committee overseeing SST and various state officials who were part of SST process, helping convince NCSL committee to delay approval of SST definitions.
    • Organized reception for association, cosponsored with Snack Food Association, for Pennsylvania legislators and delegates to 2000 Republican National Convention, with candy contributed by Pennsylvania members.
    • Solicited members for candy that was used at reception sponsored by National Republican Senatorial Committee at National Convention, bringing attention and visibility to association and industry at high-profile political event.
    • Led food-industry coalition related to school-lunch issue during consideration of issue by Congress, organizing research and lobbying efforts and successfully preventing changes from being made to Child Nutrition Program that would have harmed our industry.

  • Director of Legislative Affairs

    National Confectioners Association

    (Non-Profit; Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    1993 — 1999 (6 years )

    Oversaw sugar reform efforts for association during consideration of Farm Bill by Congress. Prevented changes to sugar program that would have harmed our members further.

    • Helped with coalition fighting Minnesota Governor's attempts to change the rules on food stamps that would have prevented purchase of candy, soft drinks and other snack foods with food stamps.
    • Led efforts fighting state legislative and administrative attempts preventing sale of candy in schools by soliciting assistance from other food-industry organizations and school principals and submitting testimony of opposition or testifying in person.
    • Worked closely with member company's consultant gaining funds in appropriations bills passed by Congress that allocated money for cocoa research that proved extremely valuable for chocolate-producing members.
