  • Senior Manager - Online Marketing and SEO

    Uptake.com

    (Privately Held; Internet industry)

    July 2007 — February 2009 (1 year 8 months)

    During my time as Senior Manager of SEO and Social Media the goal was essentially to take a brand new site that previously did not exist and to grow it into a real player in the most competitive online vertical - Travel.

    This was a major undertaking because starting from scratch in an already crowded space, especially when your major source of traffic is going to be from organic SEO (rather than paid advertising) is a major challenge. Given the magnitude of the challenge the results are dramatic. Traffic to the site, which was launched in 2008 reached one million unique visitors for the first time about 12 months from the time that the very first pages were live to the world. Proving that it is possible to be successful using online marketing techniques, even in a crowded space if you employ the proper strategy.

  • Ecommerce Marketing Manager

    DivX, Inc.

    (Public Company; DIVX; Computer Software industry)

    June 2004 — August 2007 (3 years 3 months)

    This position is difficult to describe, primarily because during my time at DivX I accumulated so many different hats.

    Initially My focus was on maximizing sales revenue and digital downloads of the DivX Player, DivX Pro Codec, (the now open-source) Dr. DivX.

    In order to do this major improvements to our ecommerce capabilities were required, and a transition to the ASKNET ecommerce platform (away from Digital River) both of whom I had worked with previously. ASKNET had much better capabilities internationally at the time, and the customer base of DivX is extremely diverse.

    After steadily growing our ecommerce revenues over the first year of my tenure, our focus shifted to driving the maximum number of downloads of our free software because the primary lever for our business (on the software side) for both strategic and raw revenue reasons. in order to do this we explored cutting edge social strategies in the community as well as traditional shareware and partnerships with video related sites. We also developed cutting edge auto-update and installer technologies to encourage repeat downloads of DivX Technology.

    In 2006 we embarked on the launch of a risky new website called stage6.com. My role was to manage marketing strategy for the new site. After about 9 Months the site began breaking because the load on the system was to great for the infrastructure to handle, and after only a year the site grew into one of the Alexa top 100 websites in the world. Sadly the site became a victim of it's own success and shortly after I left DivX the stage6.com site (which was receiving in the 10's of millions of monthly unique visits/month) was closed down (despite venture firms having pledged funding for a spin-off...) the mystery of how this was rejected by the DivX board is one of the great mysteries of our time :)

  • Software Marketing Manager

    ACD Systems

    (Privately Held; ASA; Computer Software industry)

    May 2003 — June 2004 (1 year 2 months)

    When we formed the "ecommerce team" I assumed the role for managing marketing for our "in-software" sales channel. Essentially It was my job to ensure that as many users would download trial versions of the ACDSee software as possible, and then once they had downloaded, that they made a purchase. This was a great position because it was the single largest income stream for the company.

    As an aside - can you imagine that a 20MM/year online software business did not have an ecommerce team for the first 5 years of it's life!... ahhh when the web was young.

  • Online Marketing Specialist

    ACD Systems

    (Privately Held; ASA; Computer Software industry)

    February 2000 — April 2003 (3 years 3 months)

    At the height of the dot com bubble I began my career in technology in the beautiful community of Victoria BC. This position was a ton of fun, because at the time there were no "online marketing" professionals. The term "SEO" (short for search engine optimization) did not even yet exist. These were exciting times, when success meant topping the list in Altavista, Hotbot and Lycos.

    I simultaneously cut my teeth as the in-house web-analyst providing the "webalizer" tracking, personally managing an install of webtrends enterprise software on a tower that sat in a dusty corner of my cube, and attending board meetings to answer questions about the current trajectory of our traffic (a fun thing for a young guy as it was almost always "up" ;)
