Hendrik Rietman’s Summary

Interested in broad-sense agriculture, with plant breeding as a speciality.

Being a plant breeder, I focus on minor crops, i.e. crops that are not actively bred for.
Some of these crops, like amarant, are outstanding in their nutritional properties but are currently not suited for western climates or mechanical harvesting. Other crops, like Lupinus mutabilis (Tarwi), are candidates to replace soybean import into Europe. Such crops are also under investigation (comparing multiple strains and crossing the promising ones) and will be selected for low alkaloid content and increased producitivity.

As a professional I focus at Phytophthora infestans- Solanum interactions. Phytophthora infestans, a creature with a fungus-like appearance, is the causual agent of late blight which is blamed for the famine that plagued Ireland in the 1840's, although low available crop diversity was responsible for this. Yet, potato is still a main crop and late blight its major pathogen, what asks for solutions.
In the present genomic era, breeding for resistance and characterization of the infection process are expected to halt serious outbreaks. This expectance relies on receptor-effector interactions which causes immune responses abrubtly stopping infections. Unluckily such immune responses are genotype specific and can soon or later be circumvented by Phytophthora infestans, which asks for a clear understanding of the effectors and receptors involved. Thats where my research is all about!
