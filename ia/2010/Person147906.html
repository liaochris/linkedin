Mara Niels’s Experience

  • Technical editor/writer and web developer

    Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory

    (Government Agency; Research industry)

    January 1997 — January 2006 (9 years 1 month)

    From 1997 to 2006 (with family leave from 1999 - 2002 and 2006 to present), I worked as a technical editor/writer and web-site developer at Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory (LLNL), learning and using the emerging online tools and concepts I had been promoting as a manager.

    I have hands-on experience in writing, editing, graphic arts, and web-site development and maintenance. Designed, wrote, and produced scientific/technical reports, business development and public relations materials, brochures, newsletters, reports, covers, and displays. Prepared communication plans. Edited materials for college texts, engineering reports, and public relations publications to reach audiences including students, business executives, and technical personnel. Produced over 75 college texts and many technical reports and business publications, facilitating teamwork among clients, authors, editors, designers, reviewers, illustrators, typesetters, proofreaders, printers, managers, and others. Became a web editor (using HTML, BBEdit, Photoshop, Dreamweaver, Word, Acrobat, Excel, PowerPoint, and InDesign).

  • Production Division Manager; Library Division Manager; Editorial Division Manager; Staff

    Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory

    (Government Agency; Research industry)

    January 1978 — January 1997 (19 years 1 month)

    1978 to 1994: Managed three divisions of the Technical Information Department (TID) at LLNL.

    1994 to 1997: Served as Senior Staff to head of TID. Implemented a multi-year program of annual group-specific metrics; created an Internet training seminar for members of the Editorial Division; and assessed new business plans.

    1990 to 1994: Managed TID’s Editorial Division, building a group that met client needs so well that demand pushed it to grow by 50% in two years (to 86 members and a $6M annual budget). This division responded flexibly and well to client needs, continuously improving its operations and processes. We improved communication among the dispersed all-liaison members of the division; we dedicated ourselves to training, intern programs, and development of selected new services and capabilities; and we remained financially healthy in volatile times.

    1984 to 1990: Managed LLNL’s Library Division. Conceived, proposed, designed, built, and occupied the new 27,000 sq ft library buildings; eliminated large, chronic backlogs; funded and developed in-progress automation (of online catalog and financial-record databases); initiated and completed a major salary/classification upgrade; converted some services to recharge funding; and improved financial accountability. Created two annual strategic plans; established and supported an upper-management Library Advisory Committee; and conducted self-assessments and an independent peer review.

    1978 to 1984: Managed TID’s 100-person Production Division (typesetting, technical illustration, and coordinating services). With the TID management team, brought the division to health by establishing high-quality and timely delivery of services; effective internal leadership; aggressive cooperation with internal and external clients; good morale; and improved salary/advancement for the staff. The department’s first multi-disciplinary teams were developed, and the department began to recharge its services.
