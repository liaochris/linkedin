  • Research Assistant

    Arizona State University

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    August 2005 — May 2007 (1 year 10 months)

Flashover tests and simulations were conducted to understand the effect of atmospheric conditions and VLF (30 kHz) on communication antennas at high voltages.
    • Simulation studies in 2D and 3D using Boundary Element Method to understand the electric field and potential distribution of uniform and non-uniform gaps were also conducted.
    • The research culminated in the final thesis titled "Proposal of a New Humidity Correction Factor for Short Uniform Field Gaps at 60 HZ" for a masters degree
