Charles Stromeyer Jr.’s Summary

Did work in physics, computer science, neuroscience and finance for Harvard University, for MIT or for others.

Helped Benjamin Koo obtain his PhD from MIT's Engineering Systems Division (ESD). For example, you can see my name on the fourth page of his PhD thesis which I correctly edited for any language or mathematical errors:

http://opn.mit.edu/images/Submitted_Thesis_BKOO.pdf

Koo's work became the initial inspiration for the technology underlying the MIT-derived internet advertising technology firm DataXu.

Back in 1996, I was the first person ever to hypothesize that the physics ideas of string theory might one day provide some insights into the enigma of HTc superconductivity.

Worked as an investment consultant to only professional accredited investors or traders. I always started from a global-macro perspective simply because no economy or market exists alone in a vacuum, and I specialized in various derivatives markets.

I was the first advisor to the Mariner Equity Fund which was one of the highest performing long-short equity hedge funds ever.

My long term performance track record of all my investment and trading recommendations since inception in January 1994 to October 2009 was a 31% net average annualized rate of return with a 98% accuracy rate for all predictions (which means that my win/loss ratio is equal to that of Warren Buffett except that Buffett has achieved his win/loss ratio over a much longer time frame of about 46 years).

I have recently, successfully and voluntarily advised various faculty members at Harvard Medical School (HMS) on different research projects within neuropsychiatry.

I have some knowledge of renewable energy and efficiency technologies and projects, and some expertise in the economics and markets of emerging economies.
