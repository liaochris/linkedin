Mark E. Berger’s Summary

I have been in placement and staffing since 1979 and have been involved in the Internet sourcing component of recruiting almost since its inception. I started using the Intenet for recruiting back in the early 1990s by running classified ads for programmers on the text versions of AOL and Compuserve.

In the middle 1990s we actively used the very first online resume boards including Headhunter.net, Online Career Center, and Career Mosaic. These have all been gobbled up by the larger services we have today. In the latter part of the 90s the term "passive candidate" was first coined. It was then that we started using the search engines to find resumes on what I call the "open web".

Back then Google was little more than a funny name and we used primarily Alta VIsta and Northern Lights for our searches. Since then it has been a never-ending process of learning and implement best practices as to the best way to use the Internet to get the information we need for our jobs.

These days...I spend most of my time conducting training classes on using Social Media for recruiting, branding, and marketing, along with how to use the search engines to find the information we need.

I am a published author and industry trainer and speak at conferences and association events regularly. I have obtained both my CPC and AIRS CIR. I also sit on the State Board of Directors for the Mid-America Association of Personnel Services.

Swat Recruiting was started in early 2006 and is developing more products and services every day.
