Eunic Ortiz’s Summary

Eunic Ortiz is the Web/Social Media Manager and a Press Officer to the New York City Council. Within six months of starting at the Council, she brought the City agency to the forefront of Web 3.0 adding vast multimedia applications to http://council.nyc.gov. Soon after, she started their social media profiles including Twitter, Facebook, Flickr, YouTube, Scribd, LinkedIn and FourSquare. As a press officer, Eunic handles LGBT and technology issues for the Council and Speaker Christine C. Quinn. She also handles the Speaker's district press in the Communications division.

Eunic is currently a graduate student at New York University pursuing her Master of Arts in Graphic Communication Management and Technology. The program serves as a crossroads where a worldwide network of graphic communications professionals from a variety of disciplines come together to bolster their management skills and understand the capabilities of emerging technologies. Studying part time, she plans to complete the program in 2012.

Prior to joining the New York City Council, Eunic completed a fellowship with NBC/Universals local City affiliate, WNBC. There she played a role as a field producer and online content producer for morning and evening news broadcasts. She covered breaking news and assisted in local Olympic coverage.

Before moving to New York City, Eunic worked as an producer, reporter and web editor in Gainesville, Florida for ABC affiliate, WCJB. While there, she collaborated with the news team to revamp the then static and non-informative website, WCJB.com.

In August of 2008, Eunic received a bachelor of science degree in telecommunication with concentrations in broadcast news and new media from the University of Florida. During her time at UF, she worked at both radio (NPR) and television (PBS) stations within the University as a news producer and reporter.

Eunic was born and raised just outside Orlando, Florida.

You can find Eunic on Twitter @eunicortiz.
