Ramneek Bhasin’s Summary

Ramneek is a seasoned executive with deep experience in both startups and F100 companies with a global perspective (including a special emphasis on Asia-Pac). Areas of expertise include web/enterprise software (B2B and B2C), broadcast media, messaging, consumer mobile and wireless, and social media communications. Business focus areas include corporate strategy, strategic alliances, business, market and brand development.

Most recently, Ramneek founded Mobio Networks, Inc. and was CEO of this venture backed company focused on the next generation of mobile platforms and consumer wireless services.

Ramneek has served as EIR and remains affiliated with two of Silicon Valley's leading VC firms - Storm Ventures and Mayfield Fund. He has assisted as a strategic and/or board advisor to several Silicon Valley companies such as Good Technology (acquired by Motorola), Kaboodle (acquired by Hearst Corp.), and SnapTell (acquired by Amazon), amongst others.

Previously, Ramneek was CEO and founder of Vialto Corp. Despite an extremely poor macro-economic environment (2001-2003), he successfully led Vialto’s build-out and subsequently led efforts in its acquisition by Cisco Systems Inc. He co-authored Vialto’s concept patent - "system and methodology for voice activated access to multiple data sources and voice repositories in a single session." (US Patent 6,891,932).

In 1994, he cofounded WSB Technologies, a leading SI and Technology Management Consulting firm.

Ramneek has held several executive, management and technical roles at companies such as Schlumberger VLSI Test Division, where he was Director of Software Strategy; Digital Equipment (acquired by Compaq/HP), where he was Group Engineering Manager, and Synercom Technologies.

He has an MS from Louisiana State University and a B.Tech from IIT Kanpur, India.

Ramneek Bhasin’s Specialties:

Corporate strategy, M&A, OEM/channel agreements, business development, strategic planning, product management, product marketing, executive and operations management for early/mid-stage companies.
