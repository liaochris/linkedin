Margie Traylor’s Summary

Margie Traylor is Chief Executive Officer and Co-Founder of Sitewire, an evolutionary marketing agency based in Tempe, Arizona. Traylor founded Sitewire with former business associate, Bret Giles in 1999.

Traylor and her team of innovative marketing experts work with top national brands, such as Sears, Olive Garden, Red Lobster, Avnet and others, in developing an evolving strategy to produce, protect, support and direct their brand’s web presence. The agency took on these large accounts after Traylor became aware of a process referred to as Whale Hunting. Led by President Barbara Weaver Smith, The Whale Hunters’ program focuses on landing big deals and transforming a company’s business development efforts. After Traylor experienced success using the program, she met with Smith and the two formed a partnership. Traylor now serves as The Whale Hunters’ CEO.

Described by both business associates and friends as compassionate, sincere, giving and exceptional, it is Traylor’s dedication to maintaining strong relationships with clients and employees that is among her most outstanding qualities. She imbues both Sitewire and The Whale Hunters with this same strength of character and fortitude.

Traylor began her career as a CPA at Price Waterhouse and later moved to MicroAge, a Fortune 500 Company. It was there she met future Sitewire co-founder, Bret Giles, and in a single year, the two contributed $10 million to MicroAge’s bottom line, marking their department as one of the most profitable in the company.

Before leaving, Traylor became Vice President of Electronic Commerce at MicroAge, and was not only one of the company’s few female Vice Presidents, but also one of its youngest.

Traylor received her degree from the W. P. Carey School of Business at Arizona State University. She is also actively involved with a number of local and national organizations and charities, and remains passionate about contributing her support to the community.
