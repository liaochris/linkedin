Andy M. Perez’s Summary

I believe that I'm here to make a difference. I’m driven by a sense of responsibility to others and a commitment to the community. These beliefs guide my thoughts, my actions and my intent. I never waiver in my determination to make a difference in real ways for real people. In my professional life, I own and work at a technical training academy that measures success by the accomplishments of our students. In my personal life, I volunteer my time as a member of two workforce boards where I focus my expertise in creating programs that have a direct impact on Florida residents.
I started the Academy of South Florida with basic principles in mind–provide affordable technical education and hands-on experience that students need to compete in today’s high-tech job market. Coupled with a personalized “student experience” program our students get the mentoring and support that they need to succeed. Our reputation for making a difference in the lives of others speaks for itself.
I have a long history of volunteering my time and energy to organizations that provide tangible results. I understand that hard work and dedication are needed to turn ideas into a valuable reality. The rewards are in the results. My current work on both the South Florida Workforce Investment Board and the Workforce Florida Board mirror my belief that self-sufficiency brings sustainability and pride.

  • Private Sector Board Member/Economic Development Industry Sector Chairman

    South Florida Workforce Investment Board

    (Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    June 2005 — Present (5 years 2 months)

    The South Florida Workforce Investment Board is responsible for initiating state and federal funded workforce development programs in Miami-Dade and Monroe counties. We assist employers and job seekers with employment services, labor market information, and provide training for economically disadvantaged adults, youth, dislocated workers, individuals transitioning from welfare to work, and refugees.

    While promoting the advancement of underutilized workers, the South Florida Workforce stimulates the labor market by implementing policies such as business incentives, and provides valuable resources to South Florida's diverse community.

    All South Florida Workforce services and resources are available to everyone at no cost through a network of Career Centers located throughout the region.

    (Education Management industry)
