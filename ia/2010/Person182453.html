Sarah Best’s Summary

As a social media strategist, I marry a knowledge of current web and social media trends with 12 years of experience in editorial and communications. I spearheaded a partnership with Foursquare.com--their first with a tourism entity--and I'm creative and strategic when it comes to developing engagement plans around specific campaigns. I'm an expert at cultivating and managing user communities, and at leveraging relationships fostered through these communities to facilitate the creation of specific, on-message, user-generated content.

I've helped numerous resource-constrained organizations extend the reach of their message through creative, low-cost marketing strategies and through strategic partnerships.

Sarah Best’s Experience

  • Web Specialist

    Chicago Office of Tourism

    (Leisure, Travel & Tourism industry)

    February 2006 — Present (4 years 4 months)

    I develop social media strategy for the Chicago Office of Tourism, engaging users around the themes of seasonal tourism initiatives through branded Twitter, Facebook, Foursquare, Flickr and YouTube channels, as well as through games, content strategy, and user experience design.

    I spearheaded a partnership with Foursquare.com, announced in February 2010--their first partnership with a tourism destination. We established a branded presence on their website, and announced three Chicago-specific badges.

    My staff provides Twitter-based concierge service, answering questions and sending alerts about free things to see & do in the city 7 days/week through @explorechicago. In a user's own words: @shawnhorton: "I landed in Chicago, and @explorechicago @ replies to me. This is the future folks. Watch out. I love it."

    Chicago was cited as being #1 in Buzz (organic, user-generated interest & activity in Social Media) in a report "How Social is your City?" by Mojave Interactive, beating out Boston, SanFrancisco, LA, New York City, Seattle and Austin. The Go See Tell Network has frequently ranked @explorechicago in their top 3 out of more than 450 destination marketing organizations (DMOs) on Twitter.

    I also played a lead role in the development of a new $1.5 million dollar website, ExploreChicago.org, the official tourism website for the City of Chicago. The North American Travel Journalists Association (NATJA) named ExploreChicago the #1 Destination Website in 2009. The Huffington Post praised ExploreChicago.org for "tell[ing] the story of both city and citizens perfectly"(http://bit.ly/11diY) in part because of my content strategy, which was based around the idea of positioning ordinary Chicagoans as experts on their city.

    I have a broad knowledge of web based disciplines, from coding and UX design to content strategy and project management, which enables me to communicate effectively with many members of a web team, and to play many roles on a web team.

  • PR and Marketing Director

    Local Infinities Visual Theater

    (Performing Arts industry)

    July 2005 — November 2005 (5 months)

    I oversaw media relations for a new performance about the art and science of anatomy: Corpus Delicti: Just Desserts, and garnered numerous features and reviews, including a half page review in the Chicago Tribune, a photo feature in Time Out Chicago, a Best Bet in the Red Eye, and a Critic's Choice in the Chicago Reader. I also secured strategic sponsorships, for example with the International Museum of Surgical Science, and coordinated a preview for first year medical students, a pre-show discussion in a medical student residence hall, and post-show discussions facilitated by artists and medical professionals.
