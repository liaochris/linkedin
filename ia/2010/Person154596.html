Nicole Forzano’s Summary

“Never buy into the idea that there’s not enough opportunity to go around.”

~Nicole Forzano

As a project manager and producer for POST at ditigal OutPost, I am currently managing the production of law enforcement training videos for the State of California Commission on Peace Officer Standards and Training. My background expertise in broadcast journalism, business management, public relations, marketing and publishing has proven to bring fresh insight to POST training.

My career began in communications as the correspondent and business manager for NY Times bestselling author Dr. Richard Carlson. From 1999-2003 I oversaw all keynote speaking, endorsement, budget and business negotiations, as well as in-house publicity and publishing considerations at the Don’t Sweat the Small Stuff corporate office.

Learning how to manage the small stuff, the big stuff and how to spot the extraordinary in the ordinary from my mentor Richard Carlson, coupled with an education in broadcasting, prepared me for a successful career in the newsroom.

Working with top journalists in stations across the country including the CW11--WPIX in New York, FOX Ch.2--KTVU in San Francisco, and FOX 6--XETV in San Diego gave me a broad perspective of the broadcast industry and sharpened my skill of getting a story on air with accurate and concise detail as an associate producer, investigative field producer, news writer and assignment editor.

As a conference producer and co-facilitator at Pfizer Pharmaceuticals World Headquarters in Manhattan, NY, I learned the ropes of corporate America and fine-tuned my capabilities in leadership and communication by teaching new employees about the corporation’s culture, marketing, products and philanthropic leadership.

I currently live in Orange County with my husband, Jim Forzano and our toddler, James Ryan.
