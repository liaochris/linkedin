Meredith Buck’s Summary

Established in 2006, The Law Offices of Meredith J. Buck, P.C. is a general law practice with a concentration in family law, criminal defense, and medical/legal counseling. We serve clients in Bucks and Montgomery Counties and the surrounding areas. The foremost tenet of our practice is to recognize that “clients retain lawyers, not law firms.” In keeping with this philosophy, Attorney Buck founded the firm based on her commitment to serve others and is genuinely interested in helping clients resolve their legal problems. Clients of the Law Offices of Meredith J. Buck, P.C. are assured that the attorney retained will be responsible for their litigation from inception to resolution, regardless of duration.

Meredith Buck’s Experience

    2006 — Present (4 years )

    Founder of the Law Offices of Meredith J. Buck, P.C., Attorney Buck brings to her legal practice an extensive background in critical care and surgical nursing. She received her R.N. from Chestnut Hill Hospital School of Nursing, where she graduated with honors. She graduated summa cum laude from Philadelphia College of Textiles and Science with a B.S. in Health Care Administration. Elected as a member of Phi Delta Phi, she received her J.D. from Widener University School of Law, where she also became certified in mediation.

    Because of Ms. Buck’s diverse background in nursing and administration, after September 11, 2001, she concentrated much of her time as a volunteer nurse and instructor for the Emergency Services Department o f the American Red Cross, traveling to New York, Colorado, California, and as far as Guam with the disaster relief efforts. She continues to volunteer on both a local and national level.

    As an attorney, Ms. Buck worked as an associate at Plakins, Rieffel & Ray P.C., where she concentrated her practice on medical-malpractice defense. In addition to owning and operating her firm in Chalfont, she now devotes many hours to the pro bono representation of Protection from Abuse clients. Additionally, Ms. Buck continues to work as a clinical nurse on a per diem and volunteer basis.

Meredith Buck’s Groups:

Ms. Buck is a member of the Pennsylvania Bar Association and also the Bucks County Bar Association where she is Secretary of the Board of Directors and sits on the Charitable Foundation of the Bucks County Bar Association’s Board of Trustees. Additionally, Ms. Buck served as Chair of the Members Services Committee of the Bucks County Bar Association in 2008-2009. As an active community advocate, she also sits on the Central Bucks Ambulance and Rescue Squad’s Board of Directors and volunteers with the American Red Cross.

Meredith Buck’s Honors:

Ms. Buck has recently won the 2009 Florence Nightingale Medal, the highest international honor that a nurse can receive. The Florence Nightingale Medal, awarded by the International Committee of the Red Cross (ICRC), “honors exceptional courage and devotion to caring or the victims of armed conflict or other disasters, or exemplary services and a creative and pioneering spirit in the areas of public health or nursing education”.

She received the 2007 Bucks County Bar Association’s Arthur B. Walsh, Jr. Award for her ongoing pro bono representation of clients in Protection from Abuse cases. Ms. Buck has also recently been awarded a 2009 Pro Bono Award from the Pennsylvania Bar Association.
