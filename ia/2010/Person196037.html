Steve Schueth’s Summary

I am an expert in the exciting and quickly growing field of sustainable and responsible investing. My focus is marketing and promotion -- of the SRI industry, and of my company's asset management expertise. I produce and host the annual SRI in the Rockies Conference for the SRI industry in North America.

Steve Schueth’s Specialties:

Marketing, Brand Management

Steve Schueth’s Experience

  • President and Chief Marketing Officer

    First Affirmative Financial Network

    (Privately Held; Financial Services industry)

    1999 — Present (11 years )

    I am President and Chief Marketing Officer of a unique independent Registered Investment Advisor (SEC File #801-56587). We specialize in socially responsible and transformative investment services designed to make money and move our culture and economy toward true sustainability. First Affirmative supports a nationwide network of investment advisers with consulting and asset management services, and other resources designed to enable them to better serve socially conscious clients in their local areas. And we produce the annual SRI in the Rockies Conference, the premier annual gathering of the sustainable and responsible investment industry in North America. The 20th annual SRI in the Rockies Conference will be October 25-28, 2009 at the JW Marriott Starr Pass in Tucson, Arizona.

  • Director

    Alliance for Sustainable Colorado

    (Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    2005 — 2009 (4 years )

    I am proud to serve on the Board of Directors of the Alliance for Sustainable Colorado (http://www.sustainablecolorado.org/) and support the Alliance's mission to encourage and promote sustainability. The Alliance is a non-profit organization dedicated to facilitating collaboration between business, academia, government, and other non-profits to catalyze the shift to a truly sustainable world. I have served on the board of directors since inception, currently as Treasurer responsible for overseeing a $3 million budget.

  • Director

    Social Investment Forum

    (Non-Profit; 1-10 employees; Financial Services industry)

    1991 — 2002 (11 years )

    I served as a director of the U.S. Social Investment Forum for 11 years. During 7 of those 11 years, I chaired the board of this non-profit trade association for the sustainable and responsible investment industry. I headed up the research and production of the first 5 biennial Trends Reports. During my tenure as board chair, I negotiated an arrangement between the Forum and First Affirmative Financial Network which resulted in a partnership which produces the annual SRI in the Rockies Conference for the SRI industry in North America. A couple of years later, I became a principle and owner of First Affirmative.

  • Founder, President

    Socially Aware Investing (Sole Proprietorship)

    (Sole Proprietorship; Myself Only; Financial Services industry)

    1998 — 1999 (1 year )

    Between the time I left Calvert in May 1998 and taking First Affirmative private in July 1999, I worked as a consultant to the social investment industry. Essentially, I provided advice and counsel to mutual funds and asset managers who wanted to become players in the SRI space. They benefited from my deep knowledge of the industry as they worked to grow their businesses while avoiding many of the mistakes made at Calvert during my eight years there.

  • President, Calvert Distributors

    Calvert Group

    (Privately Held; 201-500 employees; Financial Services industry)

    1989 — 1998 (9 years )

    Calvert is a mutual fund company based in Bethesda, Maryland -- the mutual fund company with the largest presence in the sustainable and responsible investment space. I served as President of Calvert Distributors during the last four of the eight years I was employed at Calvert Group, and was responsible for the entire sales organization, including relationships with the 2,000+ broker-dealer firms that Calvert had selling agreements with at that time. During the prior four years, I held the title of Vice President, Socially Responsible Investing and served as liaison between the company and the board of trustees of the Calvert Social Investment Funds. I was also responsible for marketing and public relations for the company and, in large part, put them on the map as a leader in the social investment space.

  • Director of Development, The Wharton School

    University of Pennsylvania

    (Non-Profit; 501-1000 employees; Fund-Raising industry)

    1986 — 1989 (3 years )

    I was responsible for raising money for The Wharton School at the University of Pennsylvania. My day-to-day role was focused on major gifts and acting as liaison between Wharton and the larger University development office. During my three years as Director of Development, along with a dynamic dean and about 30 colleagues, Wharton raised about $80 million.
