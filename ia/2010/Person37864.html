Chris Harrell’s Summary

Chris Harrell is the Brownfield Redevelopment Coordinator for the City of Indianapolis. From April, 2004 until present Harrell has been responsible for identifying, prioritizing and securing financing for the city-controlled brownfield redevelopment projects. Harrell is also responsible for assisting private parties in their efforts to redevelop brownfield sites, creating RFPs for competitive bid solicitation for brownfield projects, drafting grant applications, establishing annual budget priorities from multiple funding sources, drafting and administering contracts for projects awarded, public outreach, and administering the Indy/LISC Neighborhood Brownfield Initiative (NBI) non-profit brownfield grant program. A unique portion of Harrell’s activities includes outreach and technical assistance to multiple not-for-profit Community Development Corporations that drive a significant amount of redevelopment activities in Indianapolis on the neighborhood level.

Prior to joining the City, Harrell’s environmental experience included serving as Project Manager/Environmental Planner for transit projects requiring NEPA review with the Corradino Group (1993-1995), and a Project Administrator for Merit Environmental, Inc. (2002-2004) in Louisville, KY.

Harrell graduated from Wabash College in 1993 with an A.B. in Political Science. He has a Masters of Public Administration from Indiana University's School of Public & Environmental Affairs – Bloomington with a specialized concentration in International Trade, Development & the Environment (2000), and a J.D. from the I.U., (Bloomington) School of Law, with a focus in both international and environmental law (2002).

Sandwiched between his environmental career and education Harrell enjoyed traveling extensively, sailing, and living/ working in both Slovenia and Romania. Harrell still suffers from the constant desire to travel, and hopes to expand his career oversees as now he sees brownfields everywhere he goes.
