Timothy Musgrove’s Summary

I have worked on cognitive semantics and natural language processing for over ten years, resulting in a number of product launches, publications and patents. Tools developed by my teams have been generating content (and/or filtering content) in high volume for several popular websites.

My current goal is to provide the best semantic profiling services to leading websites, and ultimately to launch a ground-breaking semantic-based research tool for the Web.

Timothy Musgrove’s Specialties:

My specialty is knowing how to cross the chasm between technology and business, recognizing how real-world business needs can be satisfied by synthesizing and productizing research results from diverse sub-branches of cognitive science, including not only mainstream artificial intelligence research but also various sub-disciplines within philosophy and psychology.
Meanwhile I have successfully led a startup through growth, acquisition, and integration with a new parent company.

    Smartshop.com

    (Internet industry)

    August 1999 — May 2002 (2 years 10 months)

    As co-founding CTO, led development of a feature-rich comparison shopping engine which was distinguished in its ability to merge catalog data (product specs and features) from different sources, normalizing their divergent syntax and semantics, and presenting one unified catalog. Acted as Smartshop's CEO during its final 6 months of operations, selling the Comany to CNET Networks in 2002. Most of the Smartshop technology is now incorporated into CNET's various related websites.

  • VP, Artificial Intelligence R&D

    Mindmaker, Inc.

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Internet industry)

    July 1996 — August 1999 (3 years 2 months)

    Developed several natural languge processing (NLP) products, the foremost being WordHound, a smart spell-checker/OCR-checker. This was utilized by Yahoo! for over two years as the very first "Did you mean...?" spell correction on a major search engine. It was also used on other sites and in high-end OCR packages such as Prime Recognition.

  • Sr. Speech Research Engineer
