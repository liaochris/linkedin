Kay Bell’s Summary

As owner of Austin, Texas-based SKB Editorial Services, I write articles that make money and taxes understandable so readers can make more money and pay fewer taxes.

My forte: Translating difficult subject matter into lively, readable copy.

My specialty: Everyone's favorite subject, taxes.

My passion: Helping people attain a better understanding and control of their money -- How to make more of it, save some of it and spend the rest wisely!

Although my business was established 2005, I've focused on personal finance, with a keen on taxes, for more than a decade.

"The Truth About Paying Fewer Taxes," my first solo book, was published by Financial Times Press in 2009. Part of FT Press' respected "Truth About" series, my book provides readers an overview of key tax laws and how they can make the tax code work in their favor.

I am Contributing Tax Editor at Bankrate.com, a pre-eminent personal finance Web site. The Bankrate job is a natural; I helped launch the site's tax coverage in October 1999.

I came to tax and financial writing after starting my journalism career at a West Texas daily newspaper. I took a career detour to Washington, D.C., where I spent almost two decades as a Congressional staffer and then moved to the private sector as a government relations specialist for two major corporations.

In addition to consumer-oriented copy for numerous respected media outlets, I also write about finances for corporate clients.

I'm also experienced and comfortable producing TV/radio and video copy. I've provided reports on tax and other personal finance topics for AP Radio and appeared on-air for expert interviews, live and taped, on various broadcast outlets. Most recently, I was a source for the NPR program Marketplace on 401(k) loans.

I also write the popular Don't Mess With Taxes blog, which has been named one of the best tax sites in the blogosphere. I also write the Bankrate.com tax blog, Eye on the IRS.

Kay Bell’s Specialties:

Sorting through legislative and financial language and translating it into English so that readers can understand and act on it in ways that improve their financial lives.
