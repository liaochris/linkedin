    In the last year, we have seen an increase in tax deferred exchange structures for both real and personal property assets held by individual investors and corporations. The 1031 exchange options include delayed, simultaneous, reverse and improvement structures on a full or partial basis. An alternative to section 1031 exchanges is the use of a partnership real estate investment trust (UPREIT), which involves a tiered ownership structure encompassing a realty operating partnership (OP) and a REIT that is a partner in the OP. Property owners wishing to divest their realty can contribute their property to the OP and, pursuant to IR section 721, receive a tax-free partnership interest in the OP. This interest is convertible after a period of time into cash or shares of the REIT.

    Properties typically syndicated for these types of programs fall into the class of institutional quality real estate. Their valuations range from $10- $150 million in acquisition price. Investors working with brokerage firms that provide a broad range of sponsors will typically have the opportunity to invest in office, retail, industrial, multi-family, student housing and hospitality oriented properties.
