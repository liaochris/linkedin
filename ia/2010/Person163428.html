Paul Cook’s Summary

As Director, Clinical Education for Applied Clinical Education (ACE) www.appliedcme.com, Paul is responsible for building collaborative relationships, seeking out new educational partners and recognizing viable opportunities for ACE's core competencies in continuing medical education. Also falling under Paul's role is the development of needs assessments, grant writing, RFP responses, educational analysis, and yes, presentations of all types while simultaneously keeping abreast of current treatment modalities and industry trends in the pharmaceutical and biopharmaceutical spectrum.

Prior to joining ACE Paul held positions at NCME as Vice President, HCI/GEG as a Business Development Executive, The Institute for International Research in their pharmaceutical division and Strategic Forecasting, a geopolitical think tank based out of Austin, Texas and Washington D.C.

An active member of ACME, Paul attended the University of Texas at Austin. He then continued his education at Fordham University where he won the Dean’s Achievement Award and participated in the Honor’s Program while pursuing his B.A. in History.

Paul Cook’s Specialties:

Specialties consist of all aspects of CME including Curriculum Development, Educational Design, Content Development and Distribution, Critical Pathways Programs, and all forms of live, electronic, web-based and print media for the pharmaceutical industry.
