Jim Alekson’s Summary

• More than 25 years of professional experience in commercial/residential mixed-use real estate development and management, a unique history of innovation and a strong record of accomplishment in creating award winning real estate assets.

• Collaborated on the implementation of strategies focused on the planning, execution and management of progressive, award-winning mixed-use developments that have revitalized metropolitan neighborhoods in greater Seattle. These Seattle neighborhoods include Lower Queen Anne (Marketplace at Queen and TriBeCa), Capitol Hill (Harvard Market), South Lake Union (2200), DT Bellevue (Avalon Meydenbauer), DT Kirkland (Lake Street Center), Lynnwood (Scriber Creek) and outside Seattle in Palm Springs California (Indian Canyon Hotel & Conference Center), Las Vegas Nevada (Rivero Ranch Campus) Houston Texas (Aviation Towers), Guatemala Central America (Casino/Hotel/Golf Course Destination Resorts) and conceptualization and development of Luxuriant World Resorts.

• Over the past 10 years, collaborated on and initiated the ground up development and management of real estate assets in excess of $1.5 Billion in the greater Seattle, Washington region and internationally.

• Expertise in (i) leading experienced executive teams in deploying highly successful visionary strategies and (ii) employing field-tested intellectual assets focused on development site selection, project conceptualization and programming, development of equity financing programs, securing of land and pre-development loans, lease negotiations, obtaining land use and building permits for urban developments, as well as, the operational management, marketing and sales of completed projects.

• Recognized opinion leader and a dynamic communicator as an invited speaker at industry forums.

• Extensive professional development in architectural design/programming, construction management, property management, marketing, real estate law and public policy.

Jim Alekson’s Specialties:

Field-Tested Expertise in all phases of the development of complicated mixed-use projects in population dense Meteropolitan locations throughout the United States and Internationally.
