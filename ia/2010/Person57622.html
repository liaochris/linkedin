Denis Ndavi’s Experience

    Ndavi Media Ltd

    (Marketing and Advertising industry)

    September 2005 — Present (4 years 11 months)

    Backed by my multitude of experiences across the continent, I decided to turn it into a brand. Ndavi Media. An expression of my vision for where we need to be as marketing and media professionals.
    A hope for the young upcoming industry stars.
    A lifeline for brand managers and advertising agencies who want something ground-shaking and new.
    We offer 360-degree solutions in the areas of Marketing, Media and Advertising. And we really know digital!

  • Commercial Director

    Homeboyz Radio

    (Privately Held; Media Production industry)

    September 2007 — February 2010 (2 years 6 months)

    Borne from the Homeboyz DJ's, the Homeboyz brand is one of East Africa's most famous names in entertainment.
    The initial challenge was to translate that Homeboyz vibrancy into a cool, streetwise-yet-stylish urban radio brand.
    I manage the creative and commercial aspect of the broadcasting business, creating 360-degree solutions for clients in the realm of radio & web advertising, backed by on-ground experiential marketing.
    I also carry out copywriting, production, voiceovers and event management.
    I have incorporated social marketing into the mix, with the station's Facebook members numbering over 14,000.
    Homeboyz Radio is the fastest growing "movement" in the region.
    We give more than just a radio broadcast. It's a life worth living.

  • Marketing & Creative Services Manager

    98.4 Capital FM

    (Privately Held; Broadcast Media industry)

    1998 — 2002 (4 years )

    It involved growing Kenya's first ever private commercial radio station!
    The brand had to connect with Nairobi's upwardly mobile sector while drawing mass appeal. As such, I had to manage Marketing, Creative, Market Research, Public Relations, Events and Promotions.
    I also doubled as a weekly chart show presenter and as a commercial voiceover artist.
    We were the first Kenyan radio station to have an interactive web site.
    At the time of my departure, Capital FM was earning over 20% of radio advertising spends in the entire country.
