Michael Romero’s Summary

I am a Systems Administrator with a background supporting highly available networked systems. I’ve built, integrated and troubleshot what I believe to be some of the most complex middleware components on the market running the web sites of some of the largest corporations. From Global Load Balancing, to Clustered Web Servers, to Clustered Application Servers, to LDAP authentication systems, to RDBMS and Content Management Systems and more. With the right tools, I can really tune systems performance and squeeze out every last ounce of processing power to make the end user experience quick even with large amounts of simultaneous connections. When problems do arise, the experience I have gained has enabled me to diagnose difficult issues quickly - even while under immense pressure from the business and the service level agreements (SLA’s) associated. I have the ability to discover, break down and solve complex problems even before they affect the availability of my systems. Most of this ability is due to the extensive monitoring I put in place on the systems that I administer. This includes SNMP traps, SNMP probes, URL / Direct Server monitoring and often times writing completely customized scripts that will page me in the event of some predefined system failure and even recover from said failure.
