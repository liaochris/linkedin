Zachary Fowler, PhD’s Summary

Skilled research engineer in a wide variety of metabolic engineering practices suitable for numerous biotechnological applications. Particularly interested in microbial engineering for natural products and biofuels. Always interested in projects focused on environmental renewal and green engineering, as well as those participating in community outreach programs through local lectures and teaching seminars.

Zachary Fowler, PhD’s Specialties:

E. coli and yeast gene deletions; recombinant gene expression; constraint-based modeling; bacterial fermentations; in vitro protein purification, quantification and analysis; HPLC; phylogenetics and bioinformatics; microbiology techniques.

Zachary Fowler, PhD’s Experience

  • R&D Development Specialist - BioPharma

    Praxair

    (Public Company; PX; Chemicals industry)

    May 2010 — Present (8 months)

    Within the BioPharma research and development group at Praxair, we are developing technologies for the BioPharmaceutical industry with a focus on those process steps requiring industrial gas applications. Namely, we are developing high-impact technologies related to the production of therapeutic proteins, cell therapies and the banking of biologicals including cord blood and reproductive cells. I'm currently working on projects related to controlled freezing, protein production, lyophilization and purification.

  • Research Assistant

    University at Buffalo

    (Educational Institution; 5001-10,000 employees; Higher Education industry)

    August 2006 — May 2010 (3 years 10 months)

    My work involves the metabolic engineering of microorganisms to achieve high-levels of recombinant-natural products, in particular flavonoids and stilbenes. My main efforts currently are to identify gene deletion targets through linear optimization methods (flux balance analysis) and to identify and engineering efficient proteins for turnover by homology modeling and site-directed mutagenesis. Techniques employed include cloning, gene deletions, over-expressions, shake-flask and fed-batch fermentations, and other traditional molecular biology techniques.
