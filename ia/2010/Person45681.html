Cynthia Read’s Experience

  • Editor and Writer

    Cynthia Read, Editorial Services

    (Publishing industry)

    1985 — Present (25 years )

    I am an insightful and versatile writer and editor with more than 30 years experience in diverse subject areas. My skills include writing, project management from concept development to final publication, developmental and line editing, copyediting, proofreading, and photo research.

    My particular passion is pet writing, where people know me as Cindy Read. I am editor-in-chief of The Corgi Cryer, a quarterly magazine for which I have received three Maxwell Awards from the Dog Writers Association of America, one for my article "The Integrative Medicine Approach to Lyme Disease," and two for The Corgi Cryer as Best Regional Club Publication. Since I became its editor, The Corgi Cryer has received 35 nominations and 11 DWAA awards. I am also the editor of and contributor to 3 books: Everything Corgi: Wit and Wisdom for Lovers of Cardis and Pems, The Watching: A Corgi Christmas Tale, and The Watching: A Corgi Puppy's Christmas Surprise, all published by CorgiAid, Inc. I am a regular writer for the Pembroke Welsh Corgi Club of America Newsletter and have edited many dog-related articles, book manuscripts, and Web sites.

    I also have extensive experience as a freelance writer and editor for nonprofit organizations, including New York Medical College, the Dana Foundation, the Albert and Mary Lasker Foundation, the Overbrook Foundation, Funders Concerned About AIDS, and the LEAD Program in Business. In addition, I have written Web sites on American classical music composers, Eastern bodywork techniques, and minority student business careers; edited academic papers and fellowship applications; and written newsletters and publicity for modern dance presenting organizations.

    As editor, I am equally comfortable working with novice and academic, scientific, and professional authors, and specialize in helping them translate complicated concepts for general readers and make their works entertaining as well as informative.

  • Editor and Writer

    (Philanthropy industry)
