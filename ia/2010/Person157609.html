Mark Tomizawa’s Summary

"America can do much more with what it's got." –– Air Civic 2009, 6 Degrees of Innovation (2010)

Through Air Civic consultants, America's "elite" and "street" savvy doers connect to create new combinations out of what's already working. It's fast, efficient and local.

Projects:

<> National "cook book" of proven solutions. Meta-searchable.

<> Hands-on music, art and science centers in empty storefronts.

<> Crowd and engagement-building practices from entertainment, media and IT applied to civic challenges, every day.

As a scrappy teen l learned creativity on-the-fly at Second City and media skills at CBS. When a Chicago functionary went after our principal I helped corral vital support from Walter Jacobson (NBC-TV), Mike Royko (Daily News) and Studs Terkel (WFMT).

It changed my life.

Dartmouth connected me with college radio and conventions ('76 presidentials). In Philly I turned pro: news radio, behavioral research, TV News. One hurricane and one Emmy later I left for Fred/Alan and the guys who birthed MTV so brilliantly.

In 1987 I founded Smash and caught the wave just right.

Smash launched big stuff worldwide. Digital cable, the Compact Disc, NBC's Olympics and tons more.

Smash repositioned networks, launched Tavis on PBS and reinvigorated Sesame Street. We pioneered sponsorable cable minutes for stations and made prime time specials for History.

Post 9/11 we comforted New Yorkers with the anthemic campaign "No Place I'd Rather Be".

The "Good egg" projects hooked me.

• To get a young fiancé off the list for murder I coordinated a TV news blitz. 12 hours later, A.G. Janet Reno reversed a staff decision. History proved her right.

• To help an artistic family survive a fire, I built a website where donors could offer just the right belongings to turn their temp apartment into a home.

• To keep a Boston tutoring center open I engaged the Boston Globe. The front page story attracted a vital long-term funder.

Three years ago I came up for Air.

  • Wrangler

    Fred/Alan Inc. (The founders brought MTV to life)

    (Arts and Crafts industry)

    1985 — 1987 (2 years )

    What didn't they make? Fred/Alan did it all. Records, animation, shows, snippets, cereal prizes and off-kilter spots. Clients ate it up and why not?

    Fred Seibert and Alan Goodman had been MTV before MTV. They refused to look at audiences as lifeless piles of stats.

    They kept heads, hearts, guts and funnybones alive. Viewers, creatives and clients responded. It was frisky messy conception, never a slick surgical procedure. Stuff was born with a beat.

    Shortly after F/A opened I came aboard carrying ideas and baggage.

    My position? Somewhere between Fred and Alan, Albie, Tom, Linda, Jessica, Daria and Steve –– close to the ink & paint and kitchenette. The rule: Break the rules. Counterpunch. When "they" shout, whisper. When "they" go flashy, go handmade. And clean your own damn cup.

    We had MTV, Nick, Nick at Nite, Swatch, record labels and a conspiratorial universe of creatives and clients. Stuff stuck out. Strategy was underneath where you couldn't smell it.
