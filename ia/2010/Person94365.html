Jennifer Dalitz’s Summary

Every day for the past four years I’ve spent my time writing about, speaking about, mentoring and consulting on topics related to getting more women into leadership roles. My expertise in this space has been recognised with an Edna Ryan Workplace Award for improving the working conditions of Australian women, and selection as a finalist in the 2010 NSW Telstra Business Awards.

An avid commentator, I’m regularly featured in media publications such as BRW, The Financial Review, The Sydney Morning Herald, The Age, Courier Mail, Adelaide Now, In The Black, The National Accountant, Marie Claire, Madison and Working Women on issues relating to women in leadership.

Prior to founding sphinxx, my career in finance and global consulting firms spanned 16 years, international borders and a host of Fortune 500 companies. An MBA (AGSM) and a CPA, I fully appreciate the impact on the bottom line when female talent is under-utilised; and as an executive, business consultant and working mum, I’ve experienced first hand the challenges women face as they climb the corporate ladder.

I am constantly tracking the latest research from around the world and sharing it in The SheEO Report, a monthly digest of the latest gender diversity practices and primary research data gathered from my own database of 2,500 senior women in business. This primary research data is critical, because what I see locally is not always consistent with the global trends.

Whether you’re interested in a one-off chat or presentation to your leadership team; whether or not you have internal gender diversity experts and you could use a second opinion; or whether you’re looking to outsource the development and monitoring of your entire gender diversity program, I can help you.

I have ten years of experience working in and with the C-suite, and I am absolutely confident you would benefit from the insights and experiences I will bring to you – drop me a line and let's start a conversation!
