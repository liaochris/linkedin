Karimah Westbrook’s Summary

Karimah Westbrook (born October 6,1978) is an American television and film actress. Making her screen debut with a small role in the Paramount box office hit Save the Last Dance (2001), Westbrook earned recognition for her performance as Ginnie in the Sony Classics critically acclaimed biopic Baadasssss (2003), opposite actor/director Mario Van Peebles. Baadasssss was voted “One of the Best Movies of the Year” by Ebert and Roeper.

Upon moving to Los Angeles, the Chicago native went on booking guest starring roles on television shows including ER, Without A Trace, Strong Medicine, The District, In Justice, City of Angels and 10-8 to name a few plus recurring roles on MAD TV, and Girlfriends. In 2009, Westbrook booked a supporting role in the film adaptation of Hunter S. Thompson’s novel The Rum Diary (2010) opposite Johnny Depp and a role in the upcoming weekly Showtime series Look (2010).

Her movie credits now include the highly anticipated Jazz bio-pic Bolden (2010), where she stars as Alice Bolden opposite Anthony Mackie, American Violet (2009) opposite Alfre Woodard, Truth Hall (2008) and Studio (2007). In addition to the aforementioned films, Westbrook also had supporting roles in The Tenants (2005) opposite Dylan McDermott and Knuckle Sandwich (2005) with Morgan Fair child. Always up for a challenge, Westbrook learned to speak fluent Mandarin Chinese when she worked with international movie star Sylvia Chang in the award winning festival hit American Fusion (2007).

In 2006, Westbrook proved that not only was she talented in front of the camera but also a Producer when she co-wrote, produced and starred in her first short film production entitled Best Kept Secret (BKS). BKS has achieved international success and was a finalist in the BET J BEST SHORTS season 3 competition. To date, Westbrook has produced the following short film projects: Pastor Stuart, Wolts & Volts, Almost Famous and A Fire in a Dovecot.
