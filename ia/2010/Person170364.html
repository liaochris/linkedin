Rebecca Marsham’s Summary

My career in marketing began in 1989 in Los Angeles, California within a boutique sports marketing company called Group Dynamics, Inc. I was responsible for creating a ticketing system for their annual jazz festivals and liaising with musicians. I also coordinated perennial nationwide product sampling programs for various clients, including Quaker (Gatorade, among other brands).

After this, I took employment with an international public relations firm called Fleishmann Hillard, where I worked with the Tech. Team working on events such as the 1994 football World Cup. I was an elected member of the Computer Committee and was funded by the company to attend and complete a writing course at the UCLA.

After returning to England nearly 10 years after arriving in America I was employed by McDonald's Development Company, a pan-European division of McDonald's Restaurants, where my main role was to assist and advise our European counterparts on global marketing strategies and legal requirements set forth by the U.S. while keeping the brand in line with the various countries' cultural diversities and obligations. I was also nominated for, and attended, the prestigious McDonald's HMU in Oak Brook, Chicago in 1999.

Since then, I trained with the LTA to teach children 'Mini Tennis' which I privately taught for a number of years.
