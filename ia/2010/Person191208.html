Steve Mills’s Summary

As President of Armstrong Franklin, Steve has both client-facing responsibilities and a firm leadership role. Within Armstrong Franklin, Steve is responsible for providing strategic vision and perspective in guiding Armstrong Franklin as a global leader in premier, retained executive search. Steve’s hands-on approach, strong leadership, and delivery are key attributes that allow him to build and facilitate high performance teams, by attracting skilled talented individuals, building consensus and mutual respect.
Steve Mills has extensive experience in the recruitment industry spanning over 20 years, 13 in franchising within the recruitment services sector. From his start as an Account Executive, Steve has consistently delivered peak results and successfully executed against business plans.
As Chief Executive Officer for MRINetwork Worldwide, Steve had responsibility for the development of the global MRINetwork market where he managed operations in 38 countries, giving Steve unparalleled executive search experience in key global markets. Most notably, MRINetwork Worldwide was recognized as a market leader in the rapidly emerging markets of China, Middle East, Eastern Europe and Latin America
In his current role, Steve runs a specialist franchising and consulting services practice. He consults widely with franchising and consulting services companies, using his extensive global experience, and manages the careers of highly talented Executives.
Steve is married to Amanda, has a 6 year old daughter called Georgia and lives in West Chester, PA. His career has allowed him to do business in many countries and, as a result, he is an avid student of World and Political affairs. In his spare time he is a keen golfer.

Steve Mills’s Specialties:

His knowledge, contacts and experience mean that his opinion is regularly sought on global recruitment matters, for both public speaking engagement and press articles. His visa status with the US government acknowledges him as an “Individual of outstanding ability” in his chosen field of executive search.
In addition, he was a non-executive Director of the British Franchise Association from 1996 until 2002, acting as the Treasurer of the organization from 2000 until 2002.
