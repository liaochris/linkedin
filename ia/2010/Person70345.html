Florin Pop’s Summary

Florin Pop is a PhD student at Computer Science department in University Politehnica of Bucharest. His research interests are oriented to: scheduling in Grid environments (his PhD research), distributed system, parallel computation, communication protocols. He received engineering degree in Computer Science, in 2005, on Decentralized Scheduling Methods for Grid and MSc in 2006. He is a assistant teacher at the Computer Science Department (PUB) teaching Communication Protocols, Programming for Networking Systems, C Programming Language and Data Structure, member of RoGrid consortium and developer in the national project (CNCSIS, GridMOSI, MedioGRID) and international project (EGEE, SEE-GRID, EU-NCIT).

Florin Pop’s Specialties:

Grid Computing, Monitoring, Scheduling, Work-flows, Satellite Images Processing

Florin Pop’s Education

  • Universitatea 'Politehnica' din Bucuresti

    2008 , PhD in Computer Science , 2005 — 2008

    The main objective of this PhD thesis is the optimization of decentralized scheduling strategies in Grid environments. The objective is focused on the determination of near-optimal strategies for application scheduling in decentralized environments. Other important objectives are: to define an evaluation model, based on simulation, for scheduling strategies; to evaluate many various methods of scheduling in Grids; to propose a solution for decentralized scheduling architecture; to offer a solution for the use of monitoring information in scheduling process; to obtain an optimal balancing between execution time and cost using a scheduling solution based on genetic algorithms; to improve the performances of DAG scheduling algorithms; and to use the scheduling model in a real-world system. The thesis objectives aim to realize a decentralized architecture with algorithms for multi-criteria optimization. My entire PhD thesis contributions created DIOGENS system.
