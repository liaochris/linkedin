Bartosz Wojszczyk’s Summary

Dr. Wojszczyk has a wide range of experience including technology application research & development, technology benchmarking & investment justification, technology & business positioning, contract management, strategy & operations, organizational start-up & redesign, and investor relationship development.

He has a number of years of international experience working directly with electric utilities, power/energy vendors and energy investment groups.

Dr. Wojszczyk has authored/co-authored 24 published technical papers. Most recently, on behalf of S. Korean and Italian government representatives, he was the Technical Lead Author of the report “Technology Actions Plan – Smart Grids” developed by the Major Economic Forum on Energy and Climate (MEF) for the G20 Summit in Copenhagen . He co-authored the industry study “Betting on Science – Distributive Technologies in Transport Fuels” and the industry report “Accelerating Smart Grid Investments” (prepared by the World Economic Forum).

He is an active member of the IEEE PES and Standards Association (IEEE 1547). He presently serves as Secretary of the IEEE Distributed Generation & Energy Storage Working Group. He was a member of the System Protection subcommittee and working groups on "Performance of Relaying during Wide-Area Stressed Conditions". He is a member of the IBC Asia, Smart Grid Advisory Board.

Dr. Wojszczyk has been a keynote speaker, invited presenter, and a panelist at a number of IEEE, Smart Grid, DistribuTECH and Renewable Energy conferences and panel discussions presenting topics on sustainable energy (distributed and renewable energy resources), smart grids, protection and automation, power grid management, etc. He is a frequent instructor and has taught courses in many countries in North America, Europe and the Asia-Pacific. He has a BS.E.E., MS.E.E., and Ph.D. from the Wroclaw University of Technology in Poland.

Bartosz Wojszczyk’s Specialties:

Technology and business expert in the areas of T&D engineering & operations, smart grids, renewable & distributed energy technologies, protection & automation, electric vehicles and energy storage.
