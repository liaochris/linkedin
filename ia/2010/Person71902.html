Frank (Francesco) S. Adamo’s Summary

Are you or do you know someone going through difficult times? We must maintain our belief in ourselves. Frank, the Godfather of Believers & Achievers, is ready to guide you through these difficult times and explain how to believe in yourself again.

Frank S. Adamo is a life coach, certified career transition coach, international trainer and instructor, the published author of "31 Tips to Becoming an Effective Presenter" and a contributor to the books "Discover your Inner Strength" with Brian Tracy, Ken Blanchard and Stephen Covey and "Yes You Can!" with Jim Rohn and Dr. Warren Bennis.

Frank is a detailed and results-oriented professional with a strong ability to work with people, both locally and abroad. He is a recognized expert in developing powerful, persuasive, and successful strategies. Frank is also highly proficient in troubleshooting, training, and supporting state-of-the-art technology with the ability to speak to small and large groups of people of all ages. He has also mentored many individuals throughout his career.

Frank grew up on the infamous Route 66 in Springfield, Illinois, and learned his business ethics in the midst of the “Land of Lincoln.” After graduating from high school, he returned to his hometown of Tampa, Florida where he earned an MS degree in analytical chemistry from the University of South Florida. He then moved to Southern California where he began his career as a chemist. After a few years, Frank started a business in computer and business consulting. Now, he is giving keynote speeches, workshops, and coaching individuals and small groups.

  • PowerPoint Designer and Enhancer

    FSAdamo Enterprises

    (Partnership; 1-10 employees; Design industry)

    2000 — Present (10 years )

    Frank designs, but more importantly, he improves PowerPoint presentations.

    Font colors should complement -- not contrast -- with the background. Do not use red fonts on a blue background.

    Add key points -- not text -- to the slides. If the text is not read -- who reads it? The audience and that's where the presenter losses the audience. Don't add a bunch of text to a slide.

    Fancy animation, sounds and whistles are usually a distraction. Also, the presenter, not the slides, should be the center of attention.

    PowerPoint shouldn't drive the presentation. The presenters should be the center of attention.

    These are just a few samples of distractive and ineffective ways in which PowerPoint is used. It takes time, forethought and basic reasoning to design an effective presentation.

    Frank has the experience and knowledge to design and enhance an existing PowerPoint slide show and coach the presenter on effective presentation skills.
