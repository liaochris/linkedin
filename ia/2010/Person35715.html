Chantal Morgan D'Apuzzo’s Summary

Dr. D'Apuzzo's current practice focuses on patent litigation, at both the district court and appellate level.

Dr. D’Apuzzo’s prior experience has been in strategic patent counseling, IP due diligence, and patent prosecution in the fields of pharmaceuticals, biotechnology, medical devices, and cosmetics. Her work has spanned the fields of small molecule pharmaceuticals, antisense technology, gene discovery, pharmaceutical formulations, hair dye formulations, and adhesive polymers. She has represented public and private companies, academic universities and hospitals, as well as venture capital firms.

Dr. D’Apuzzo recently served as a law clerk, with a focus on complex patent cases, to the Hon. Mariana R. Pfaelzer of the U.S. District Court for the Central District of California in Los Angeles. She assisted the judge with biotech, pharmaceutical and software patent cases, including related antitrust, trademark and false advertising claims.

Prior to her current position, Dr. D’Apuzzo practiced in Palo Alto where she assisted both start-up biotech clients and mature medical diagnostic clients with patent strategy, patent validity analyses, freedom-to-operate analyses and managed and prosecuted several patent applications portfolios. Before beginning and while attending law school, Dr. D’Apuzzo worked as a patent agent drafting and prosecuting pharmaceutical, biotech and cosmetic patent applications.

Dr. D’Apuzzo holds a Ph.D. in biochemistry from the California Institute of Technology. She conducted her doctoral work in the field of protein design, including three-dimensional NMR techniques, organic synthesis, protein expression, molecular biology, and computational protein design, as well as optical techniques. Her research culminated in the structural determination of a fully designed alpha-helical protein.

Dr. D’Apuzzo is admitted to practice before the California bar and the U.S. Patent and Trademark Office. She speaks Italian fluently.
