Meaghan Wagner’s Summary

I am an editorial assistant at Putnam and a publishing graduate student at NYU. I've only just started at Putnam but already it's proven to be an invaluable experience to work with authors and learn from an experienced editor.

As for my graduate studies, well my major is totally unique in that I study both publishing business courses and graduate level writing courses- something which not only keeps my editorial eye sharp but also helps me be sensitive to authors. I am currently writing my graduate thesis exploring the mechanics of editing through the concepts of narratology.

I am especially interested in new perspectives within publishing. Examples range from publishing for a young adult male audience, to advance-less publishing or new ideas regarding online/digital interfacing. My interest in this isn't hardlined or specific, I just really like new ideas.

    Perseus Books Group

    (Privately Held; Publishing industry)

    September 2008 — September 2009 (1 year 1 month)

    The short version? I assisted the Publisher, Associate Publisher and Publishing Manager of Vanguard Press, an imprint of the Perseus Group.

    Vanguard was an exciting place to work for a couple of reasons. One: they have a unique business model, wherein they do not pay advances to authors. Additionally, they are a small imprint. These two factors mean that the whole imprint staff is particularly close to the whole process of publishing each book. Because of this I almost never did the same thing two days in a row and I was always learning something new about how we get books out.

    Of course there were regular office duties such as filing, keeping contacts up to date, and making reservations. Even those, however, were done on a rotating basis and more in between my constantly new projects rather than as my high priority work.
