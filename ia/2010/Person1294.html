Abby Fichtner’s Summary

I've been developing software wearing every hat imaginable (albeit with a particular passion for programming) since 1994. Although, technically, I got my start at the age of 8 when my Dad brought home an Atari 800. In the evenings, we'd sit together and type in the machine code from the Atari magazines – because that was the way serious geeks got their computer games!

I've worked for a number of startups, a little company called Oracle, and a boutique firm named Number Six Software that specialized in helping organizations improve how they develop software. I'm extremely active in the development community: I write The Hacker Chick Blog, produce the Boston SPIN newsletter, and am a regular contributor to software groups and developer sites. I also present at many of the national agile conferences: Agile 2009, Agile Development Practices, Agile 2010.

I'm particularly passionate about how we can develop better software and am extremely excited to be working with startups to help them develop the next generation of software.
