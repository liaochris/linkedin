Kevin Wassong’s Summary

Kevin Wassong is an Emmy Award winner and President of Minyanville Media, a next-generation digital network that creates branded business content to entertain, inform, and educate investors.

Kevin joined in 2005 after building one of the first "new media" agencies, "digital@jwt" within J. Walter Thompson.

During his seven years as President/CEO, he grew digital@jwt into a top-10 agency with more than 200 specialists in eight offices boasting a roster of blue-chip clients. He was named to MediaPosts 50 Most Influential People in Online Advertising.

Kevin was a member of JWTs five-person NA Management Team and of the parent WPP.com board. He was a key proponent of media integration resulting in the growth of diversified services for Merrill Lynch, Unilever, Pfizer, HSBC, DeBeers, Nasdaq and Sun Microsystems.

He lead establishment of a group within the AAAAs to change testing methodologies that drive integrated marketing and has chaired the Art Directors Club awards for Interactive and Integrated Marketing and judged the Effie Awards.

With a diverse background in marketing, communications, technology and entertainment, he spearheaded development of various digital firsts including: Sotheby’s first website; Mercedes-Benz’s first kiosk system; and the first-ever user-generated commercial website for Unilever’s Lever 2000.

At Minyanville, he has been instrumental in building the world's first financial "Theme Brand" bringing an animated financial show to TV and the web, “Hoofy & Boo’s News & Views" which won an Emmy Award.

Prior to his career in marketing, he worked as an assistant to the Chairman of CAA, as well as in TV development on The Golden Girls and Empty Nest.

Kevin has been on the cover of the Wall Street Journal, featured on FOX Business, CNN, CNBC, the NY Times, BusinessWeek, Wired, Adweek and AdAge.

He resides in Westchester, New York and graduated with from the S.I. Newhouse School at Syracuse University.
