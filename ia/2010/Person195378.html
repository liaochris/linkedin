Steve Bonneau’s Summary

Steve’s primary focus is in the areas of trusts and estates, estate administration and estate planning. His responsibilities include: designing customized estate plans; counseling clients on lifetime and testamentary transfers and attendant transfer tax issues; assisting with trust funding, maintenance and administration; and directing all aspects of testate and intestate estate administrations. In addition, Steve counsels clients on advanced wealth transfer strategies, asset protection planning, succession planning and charitable planning.

Steve has been an active member of the Chicago Bar Association's Trust Law Committee since 2002, frequently speaking at its general committee meetings and seminars. He has served on the Trust Law Executive Committee since 2006 and will be serving as its Vice-Chair for 2010-2011. He is also involved in pro-bono work for Chicago's CEDLP (Community Economic Development Law Project), specifically presenting seminars on Wills.

Steve is a member of the Adjunct Faculty of The John Marshall Law School, Center for Tax Law & Employee Benefits. He served as a co-instructor for the Spring 2007 Tax Seminar titled "Current Developments in Estate, Gift & Generation Skipping Transfer Tax" and as the sole instructor for the Spring 2008 Tax Seminar titled "Asset Protection Planning."
