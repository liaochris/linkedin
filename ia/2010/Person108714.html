Juliette Powell’s Summary

* A social media expert and commentator, Powell authored her first book: '33 Million People in the Room: How to Create, Influence and Run a Successful Business using Social Networking' (Financial Times Press, 2009)

* In 2007, Powell was commissioned by Canadian Broadcast Corporation (CBC) to create Canada's first cross-platform interactive show designed for TV, Internet, radio and mobile applications, called iQuestion.

* Guest speaker at MIT's Innovation Forum, NYU's Interactive Technology Program (ITP), and the Producer's Guild of America New Media Council.

Based on first-hand videotaped interviews with successful entrepreneurs & industry insiders, '33 Million People' introduces readers to the new seat of influence and presents the keys to personal brand management and successful social networking for business. The book helps readers:
     Understand the underlying dynamics in the relationship economy. (Social capital, cultural capital, financial capital).
     Provide successful business strategies and a lens to measure success and prepare for the future of social networking for business. This practical guide, rooted at the intersection of business, social psychology and technology, weaves together case studies, anecdotes, research and expert interviews to reveal the ubiquitous influence of social networking and details tactics to easily wield social networking power wisely in business.
