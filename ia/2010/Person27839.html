Brett Camper’s Experience

  • Director of Product Management

    Patch

    (Online Media industry)

    May 2008 — Present (1 year 11 months)

    Patch is a network of hyperlocal sites focused on news, business listings, and events.

    - Sole product manager for company's first year and a half of operations. Defined content platform requirements from the ground up through site launch and beyond. Emphasis on: geotagging & mapping, workflow for content submission & review, flexible editor-controlled metadata model, faceted search.

    - Manage the agile development schedule with the head of engineering. Work closely with engineers to keep weekly iterations focused & on-time. Follow a philosophy of lightweight documentation, collaborative requirements, rapid iteration, and modular functionality.

    - Hire & manage the product team as company has grown: design, QA, product managers.

    - Established an open source-based mapping strategy: adopted OpenStreetMap data to empower editors and users, worked with Stamen Design to create custom, brand-appropriate cartographic style.

  • Sr. Product Manager

    eMusic

    (Privately Held; Online Media industry)

    March 2006 — April 2008 (2 years 2 months)

    Defined and built web products that help people discover and download independent music. Consistently expanded the company’s reach by launching new product lines:

    - Audiobooks: the first subscription service to deliver books in DRM-free MP3 format. Defined the company’s first non-music product from the ground up: metadata model, audio encoding and delivery, data licensing, UI / page flow, and subscription business rules.

    - Mobile: the first over-the-air music download service on AT&T. Support for on-demand, real-time encoding into multiple audio formats (AAC, 3GPP); coordinated implementation between AT&T, 3rd party mobile billing platform, several handset manufacturers, and two outsourced software development firms.

    - European expansion: added regionalized site merchandising and multi-currency billing.

    - Developed prototypes of new feature concepts using Flash, JavaScript, and web services.

  • Research Manager

    MIT, Comparative Media Studies Program

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    May 2005 — February 2006 (10 months)

    - Managed the Education Arcade, a research group focused on creating educational video games that adopt, critique, and evolve techniques from the commercial industry.

    - Oversaw development of game concepts from brainstorming to design, focus groups, prototyping, and play-testing.

    - Evangelized the group’s philosophy and research agenda inside and outside the institute.

  • Technical Lead / Graduate Student

    MIT, Comparative Media Studies Program

    (Online Media industry)

    August 2003 — May 2005 (1 year 10 months)

    - Designer and technical lead for Revolution, a multiplayer role-playing video game for teaching American history to middle and high school students. Revolution models the oral & social transfer of knowledge in a small 1770s community through player interactions & autonomous agents. Presented lessons from Revolution's development process at the Game Developers Conference. Revolution was selected for exhibition in the IndieCade showcase of independent video games (indiecade.org).

    - Studied media history & theory, moments of technological change, intellectual property law, games, film, architecture, holography.

    - Co-taught “Game Design and Theory” class for undergraduates.

    - Master’s thesis: in-depth study of the indie game development community surrounding Nintendo’s Game Boy Advance platform, using ethnography, technical platform analysis, and legal studies.

  • Program Manager

    RealNetworks, Inc.

    (Public Company; RNWK; Computer Software industry)

    June 1999 — July 2003 (4 years 2 months)

    - Defined and managed software development projects for the company’s ecommerce and subscription platform, providing all online delivery of premium content and software.

    - Major projects: extending the single-purchase platform to support recurring subscription services; multi-currency payment processing; localization.

    - Designed, developed and maintained an XML and Perl-based scripting language and parser for automation and load testing.

    - Conducted reporting and data analysis via SQL.

    - Coordinated version control, build, and release processes across all application tiers.
