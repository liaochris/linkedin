Simon Ragona III, EnCE, CCE’s Experience

  • Forensic Examiner

    T&M Protection Resources

    (Security and Investigations industry)

    January 2010 — Present (8 months)

    As Case Manager and Forensic Examiner in the Data Forensic division of T&M, Simon is responsible for managing computer forensic projects and information security assessments. He performs forensic preservation of data from a variety of hardware including PCs, laptops, servers, removable media, and handheld devices. Simon also conducts examinations of numerous and various types of electronic media, including locating, recovering, extracting, and analyzing electronic data utilizing standard forensic protocols.

    Simon developed a comprehensive chain of custody form used for forensic acquisitions. He also assisted with the development of standard operating procedures for forensic acquisitions. He designed a framework and standard operating procedures for information security assessments as well.

  • Consultant / Computer Forensic Examiner

    Protiviti

    (Public Company; Management Consulting industry)

    June 2007 — December 2009 (2 years 7 months)

    Simon served as a Consultant in the Security and Privacy Management Solution from the Orlando Protiviti office focusing primarily in IT Security Risk Management during 2009. During 2007-2008, he served as a Consultant in the Litigation, Restructuring, & Investigative Services (LRIS) practice from the New York City office focusing primarily in computer forensics.

    As a Certified Computer Examiner (CCE) and Data Acquisition Specialist (DAS) at Protiviti, Simon conducted numerous examinations of various electronic media including but not limited to personal computers, laptops, Apple computers, servers, hand-held devices, CD/DVD discs, floppy discs and USB flash drives. Using the best practices and protocols in the industry, Simon used various imaging tools including but not limited to EnCase, FTK Imager, LinEn, Helix, and Device Seizure to perform forensic acquisitions and analysis.

    Simon was involved in the research and testing of computer forensic products, including the most modern hardware and software. He held the role of Local Equipment Coordinator (LEC) at the New York Protiviti office from 2007-2008 and in the Orlando Protiviti office during 2009. As LEC, Simon was responsible for preparing equipment when required for an acquisition, as well as testing equipment when it was returned. As Protiviti continued to expand its Computer Forensic practice, Simon had been involved a great deal in the planning of equipment required to expand to other key offices.

    Simon was promoted to Senior Consultant in December 2009 to be effective January 2010.

  • Intern

    DHS-ICE-OPR

    (Government Agency; Government Administration industry)

    January 2007 — May 2007 (5 months)

    Simon served as an intern at the Department of Homeland Security, Immigration and Customs Enforcement, Office of Professional Responsibility (DHS-ICE-OPR) between January 2007 and May 2007. The OPR is responsible for investigating allegations of misconduct involving employees of ICE and Customs and Border Protection (CBP). Simon worked on several high profile cases directly with the Senior Special Agent responsible for computer related investigations.
