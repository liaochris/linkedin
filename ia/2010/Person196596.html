Stuart Hibbert [stuart@icomplete.com]’s Experience

  • CEO and Founder

    icomplete limited

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Internet industry)

    January 2007 — Present (3 years 4 months)

    icomplete.com is the new way to make small business work. We combine telephony, applications (including mobile) and PA services to deliver the complete package.

    We have been in development since May 2007 and will launch our services as both a free to use (advertising supported service) and a monthly subscription service (providing enhanced features and functionality).

  • CEO and Founder

    X200 Limited (aquired by Gandlake)

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Internet industry)

    April 2002 — April 2006 (4 years 1 month)

    The market leader in Local Government eBilling services in the UK.

    X200 was formed at the time when the usual suspects for eBilling took shelter and so we decided to go after Local Government - which for a start-up business isn't the best sales stratergy - however we had the best product in the market place and with lots of determination we won out.

    In July 2004, X200 was acquired by Gandlake.

    Following the acquitition, I integrated the X200 business into Gandlake's exsisting business units - as well as advised on many other technology based areas.

  • Technical Director

    Wishstream Limited

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Internet industry)

    September 2000 — October 2001 (1 year 2 months)

    First employee following the initial round of funding of a new start-up eBilling application service provider.

    Responsible for the technical direction and management.

    Won clients such as Thus, nPower, Virgin and Orange.

  • Technical Director

    TPD (aka Fusion Interactive) / Fluent Communications

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Internet industry)

    January 1996 — July 2000 (4 years 7 months)

    TPD had approached Network Innovations about buying the business - however the other directors in the business didn't want to sell - but I decided to jump and start up TPD's Internet business (initally know as Fusion Interactive - but later rolled back into the main agency).

    Clients included; Microsoft, Compaq, Gateway, Granada Home, BBC, European Commission, Mondex, etc ...

    In April 1999, I moved to over to Fluent Communications to take over the Technical Direction. Our clients included; Microsoft, Isuzu, Oracle, etc ...

  • CEO and Co-Founder

    Network Innovations Limited

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Internet industry)

    September 1994 — January 1996 (1 year 5 months)

    Co-founded Network Innovations with Philip Taysom, whom I had worked with at Activision some 5 years earlier.

    The Internet was just taking off here in the UK and myself and Phil decided to start a 'next generation' Internet company - following on from one consultancy
    project - Phil left and helped set up Planet Online.

    Our first major project was the 'Highland Trail' which was the first multi-retailer shopping enviroment in Europe. We went on to launch Condenast Publications (GQ, Tatler, Vogue) and Microsoft Europe onto the Internet.

    We were also developing some cutting edge technolgy - including a web browser based email application - but was told by a leading vc company at the time (1995) that the internet had no future - I see today that same company has quite a few internet properties !!

  • Producer

    US Gold Limited

    (Public Company; 201-500 employees; Computer Games industry)

    September 1992 — September 1994 (2 years 1 month)

    Responsible for the 1994 Winter Olympics product range - at the time one of the most expensive products to come to market - in excess of $10 million - it was also the product to launch CenterGold onto the London Stock Exchange in a flotation worth more than £50 million.

  • Head of Development

    Elite Systems Limited

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Computer Games industry)

    July 1990 — July 1992 (2 years 1 month)

    When I joined Elite, they had just been awarded one of the first licenses to publish games on the Gameboy and SNES.

    I was responsible for getting the initial products through Nintendo and into the market, including Caveman Ninja

  • Producer

    Mirrorsoft Ltd

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Computer Games industry)

    April 1989 — June 1990 (1 year 3 months)

    Jumped ship to Mirrorsoft - was sold on the position of Head of Development - only to discover that there was already one of those in the company.

    Worked on titles such as Back To The Future 2 and 3, Teenage Mutant Hero Turtles.

  • Producer

    Activision UK Limited

    (Public Company; 201-500 employees; Computer Games industry)

    August 1987 — April 1989 (1 year 9 months)

    Youngest Producer ever be taken on by Activision - just 17 when I started.

    Jon Dean said I could have my hand in many products at once - rather then be a programmer on a single product for many months. Little did I know that he meant as many as 50 SKU's at once !!!

    Worked on many classics, including: After Burner, IK+, Power Drift.

    Was truely a great time and worked with many great people too.

  • Programmer

    Micro-Gen

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Computer Games industry)

    September 1986 — May 1987 (9 months)

    Worked on SAS Strike Force - which didn't exactly set the world on fire (pretty standard platform game - with an SAS tie-in). But I was fortunate to work along side the likes of Dave Perry, Chris Hinsley, Nick Jones, et all.

    The business was bought by a distributor CSD and I was made up to head of development.

  • Programmer

    Dalali Software Limited

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Computer Games industry)

    February 1986 — September 1986 (8 months)

    First games programming position after leaving school. Was taken on as a junior and was responsible for the conversion of Boulder Dash for the Amstrad CPC - this was rated as one of the best Amstrad games to be released by one magazine.
