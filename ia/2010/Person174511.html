Robert Burns, II’s Summary

• Public Relations Specialist
• New Media Strategist
• Freelance Writer
• Non-Profit Director
• Seasoned Event Planner

Professional Communicator, with a degree in newspapers from the UGA's acclaimed Grady College of Journalism, 8 years of experience reporting for local and campus newspapers in Pennsylvania and Georgia, and 3 years of experience developing strategic new media-based PR campaigns for award-winning organizations.

I am the co-founder of The Team Burns Charitable Foundation, Inc., a non-profit org and UGA student group that has raised more than $15,000 for the American Cancer Society in the last two years. As Director of Planning & Public Relations, I coordinate "SpringSing: Voices United for a Cure," one of the East Coast's most prominent annual multi-day a cappella music festivals.

I have also served as former Director of Public Relations for the "#1 HBCU Student Newspaper" (2007), Morehouse College's Maroon Tiger, PR Chair for the UGA Accidentals, and staff writer for countless local, campus and web publications.

My blog, "PR & New Media Today," stresses the importance for professionals to stay up-to-date on social/mobile technology in today's ever-changing world, while still maintaining their credibility and professional reputation. My writing is regularly featured on the homepage of SocialMediaToday.com, one of the leading online communities dedicated to presenting top thinking in the new media world.

My career goal is to lead a team of strategists at one of the world’s leading global PR firms, using my track record in writing and new media to build relationships and truly 'connect' with both consumers and clients. I understand the need for transparency, immediacy and ethics in today’s world, tailoring my own approach to public relations to fit those needs. I aim to make a real difference in the field of communications.


Read my blog, "PR & New Media Today" @ www.RobertBurnsII.com
