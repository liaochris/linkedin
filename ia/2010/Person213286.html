Valerie Talsma’s Summary

I am an educator in both formal and non-formal environments and at all levels from pre-school through post-grad. At NIU, I found many aspects of the faculty position rewarding. I also discovered that I have a much stronger orientation to service than to research. I’m seeking a position that combines my interests in education and service.

Prior to my appointment at NIU, I worked with a number of non-formal programs. I was an environmental educator with Pittsburgh Voyager (now RiverQuest.org) With that organization, I worked directly with students in grades 3-college. I also led environmental and historical interpretive programs for scout groups, seniors, and community groups and I facilitated professional development experiences for both participating teachers and for Voyager staff.

My non-formal educational experiences include other water quality programs such as the Rouge River Education program and Project G.R.E.E.N., the Global Rivers Environmental Education Network. I served as an interim county agent in 4-H and Youth Development in Washington Co., WI in the year after my service as a teacher in the United States Peace Corps (Botwana’84-86).

In working with adults, I have planned and facilitated professional development sessions for educators across the K-12 spectrum and on such topics as using technology in classrooms, supporting student inquiry, project-based education, standards based assessment, student collaboration, and specific content curricula.

I find my experiences and background allow me to connect with a very diverse spectrum of people. One of my strengths is helping to negotiate areas of common ground so conversations can occur and resolutions sought.
