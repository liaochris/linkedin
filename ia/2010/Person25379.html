Bob Duffy’s Summary

Social marketing enthusiast, strategist and futurist. Bob's history of both technical and marketing experience has made him a visionary in digital marketing where he quickly gravitated to new media opportunities including brand & product marketing thru social channels such as YouTube, Facebook, Twitter and corporate online communities.

Bob Duffy’s Specialties:

Social Media, Digital Marketing, Web Design,Graphic design, Blogging, Micro-blogging, Video, Marketing Strategies, Digital content publishing and aggregation strategies, Content syndication, Web publishing systems, IT system & architectures, WAN & LAN architecture & design

Bob Duffy’s Experience

  • Social Media Strategist

    Intel

    (Public Company; INTC; Semiconductors industry)

    July 2005 — Present (4 years 9 months)

    Social Media Strategist for Intel Corporation. Responsible for managing online communication strategies including community & social marketing of Intel's products & technologies Currently managing marketing efforts to build affinity & community for Intel's Atom Developer Program. Bob established Intel's corporate marketing governance and co-authored Intel's social media guidelines. Bob also established Open Port & Intel Communities as Intel's first corporate marketing communities. Bob is a member of and Intel lead for Social Media Business Council, Jive Client Advisory Board, and frequent speaker of industry events such as Web 2.0 Expo

  • President, CEO & Founder

    WiredByDuffy

    (Internet industry)

    September 2002 — December 2005 (3 years 4 months)

    Digital marketing & web development services for small to medium businesses.

  • VP of Technology Services

    Onsite Broadband

    (Telecommunications industry)

    May 2001 — September 2002 (1 year 5 months)

    Lead technical architect & co-founder of Onsite Broadband. Designed low cost, easy to deploy wireless and 2wire network architectures for aggregating broadband for resale to multi-tenant apartments and hotels. Business was dissolved a little over a year in operation as it could not get enough momentum while the market was shy of telecommunications after the dot.com bubble.

  • VP of Technology Services

    PhatPipe

    (Telecommunications industry)

    March 2000 — May 2001 (1 year 3 months)

    Lead technical architect for deploying low cost broadband to multi-tenant office buildings. Company funded by AMB Property Corporation, (former employer) and started based on business plan Bob created on broadband aggregation. Business failed and assets were sold to Prologis REIT in summer 2001

  • IT Manager

    AMB Property Corporation

    (Public Company; AMB; Real Estate industry)

    December 1995 — March 2000 (4 years 4 months)

    Established new LAN & WAN architecture for AMB Property Corporation during the period the company went public. Establish one of the earliest VPNs in 1996 by deploying a PPTP tunnel between routers in SF and Boston offices connected to the Internet. Later managed the Software Development team with a focus on converting client server applications to web based solutions and web client portals, for lower cost, centrally managed property management solution

  • MIS Manager

    Cornish & Carey Residential Real Estate

    (Real Estate industry)

    December 1988 — December 1995 (7 years 1 month)

    MIS Manager for 20 office residential real estate company. Established company wide data network, and local desktop publishing systems in each office to allow agents to create custom marketing materials from our corporate marketing templates. Worked with partners like Apple to deploy new technologies and solutions, and software startups like WildFire to create new MLS software that eventually became the model for the regional MLS system. Left company just before it was sold to Coldwell Banker
