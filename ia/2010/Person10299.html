Amy Rosen’s Summary

Amy Rosen is the President & CEO of The National Foundation for Teaching Entrepreneurship (NFTE), a non-profit organization that provides entrepreneurship education programs to young people from low-income communities. Amy has taken the helm of NFTE during a period where the organization’s programs are stronger then ever, having provided access to more than 45,000 students this year alone, with a rich curriculum designed to provide all young people a pathway to prosperity.

Amy has spent over 25 years in positions of leadership in both the private and public sectors. She is a nationally recognized expert in comprehensive urban school-system-reform strategies where she has worked to close the achievement gap by providing young people with high quality educational opportunities.

Amy has served as an advisor to Mayor Bloomberg's team, designing and implementing the restructuring of NYC's Dept. of Ed. Amy currently acts as education advisor to Cory Booker, the Mayor of Newark, NJ, and is on the faculty at Columbia Business School where she teaches Education Leadership to MBA students.

In addition to her experience in education, Amy has spent 20 years as a leader in the field of transportation, where she helped change the way public agencies conduct business while advocating for national investment in transportation infrastructure. Her work in this field has included managing NJ's transportation department and tenure as Senior VP of Lockheed Martin, where she helped build a subsidiary that provided transportation services to state and local governments. She was a two-term Presidential Appointee to the Amtrak Board of Directors, and has lectured and received much public recognition for her work, including induction into the New Jersey Transit Hall of Fame.

Amy is currently Chairman of the Board of TEAM SCHOOLS, a group of highly successful KIPP charter schools in Newark.
