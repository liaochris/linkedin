Peter Ansel’s Summary

I am an experienced interactive media and technology business development executive, with an understanding of business ecosystems across digital media platforms.

My analytical legal skills lend to being a multi-faceted strategic contributor, capable of wearing many hats. I have negotiated a large variety of business deals specific to interactive media and advertising platforms and driven executive level business and product strategic decisions.

Peter Ansel’s Specialties:

Strategic planning and market creation for interactive media and advertising start ups, including managing business and strategic partners, proving out business models, and contributing to product definitions.

Peter Ansel’s Experience

  • Vice President, Business Development

    Clearleap, Inc.

    (Privately Held; Computer Software industry)

    November 2009 — Present (1 year 2 months)

    While I am leading business development, Clearleap has positioned itself to provide a white label platform for pay TV operators to leverage OTT video viewing habits. Clearleap's on-demand solution is an on-network IP VOD managed service, white labeled so that pay TV operators can bring best in class content to connected IP devices like game consoles, Roku, TiVo, PACE, and Google TV set tops, and broadband TVs. Clearleap is moving pay TV VOD into the IP side of the cable network, with better ad models, reporting, user interfaces, and subscriber satisfaction than legacy VOD.

  • Advisory Board Member

    Hiro Media, Inc.

    (Privately Held; Internet industry)

    July 2009 — Present (1 year 6 months)

    Advisory Board Member at Hiro-Media, a Tel Aviv based video ad decision platform company, providing strategy and business development oversight. Examples include Hiro's recent agreement to white label their video solution to ZEDO display ad server customers.

  • Business Development & Strategy Adviser

    AdPassage Inc.

    (Privately Held; Marketing and Advertising industry)

    June 2009 — November 2009 (6 months)

    Served as contract VP of Business Development for an early stage start up focused on video meta-data classification.

  • Vice President, Advertising Strategy

    Move Networks

    (Privately Held; Internet industry)

    May 2008 — July 2009 (1 year 3 months)

    Developing Move's advertising strategy to support the growth of TV on the Internet and through Move enabled connected devices. Creating business models and ad products that converge traditional TV advertising with online video functionality, offered as part of Move's platform for large broadcasters, online publishers, and multichannel operators. Creating and selling new video platform models to major TV networks and movie studios which included share of ad inventory in order to build a video based ad network.

  • Director Business Development

    AdMission Corp. (acquired by Cobalt Group)

    (Internet industry)

    June 2006 — May 2008 (2 years )

    My strategy work and platform sales helped to reposition the business from a tools vendor for newspapers into a dynamic rich media ad serving platform. Secured our first major direct advertiser clients, convincing management to change product offering to meet advertiser needs and adopt a direct-to-market stance, added new reseller channel partners, and opened up new verticals for sales. This resulted in growing revenue from the business development group and creating new direct and indirect sales channels. The business was acquired by an automotive focused marketing company with a plan to build a vertical ad network using the platform.

  • Director Business Development

    MetaTV (Acquired by Comcast and Cox' TVWorks LLC)

    (Computer Software industry)

    2000 — 2005 (5 years )

    As lead in the business development group facing content providers and ad agencies, I helped to position the company as infrastructure set-top box software provider for cable TV operators. This directly resulted in investments, partnerships and an eventual acquisition by Comcast and Cox while the business was still early stage. The platform now forms the basis for the cable industry's enhanced TV and OCAP technology.

  • Manager Business Development

    AltaVista

    (Privately Held; CMGI; Internet industry)

    September 1999 — June 2000 (10 months)

Results included the rapid development of a content portal that increased page view, unique user and revenue growth, placing AltaVista in competition for content services against other major Internet portals.

  • Corporate Associate
