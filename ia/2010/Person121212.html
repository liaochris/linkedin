Larry De Palma’s Experience

  • Principal/CEO

    TDG-Phenix, Inc.

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Financial Services industry)

    January 2007 — Present (3 years 11 months)

    Headquartered in Brentwood Tennessee, TDG-Phenix provides treasury and payments services and products to financial institutions, third party processors and corporations.  The firm has affiliations with best-in-class providers of health savings account services, remote deposit capture services and ACH processing services. The firm’s website is www.tdgphenix.com.

  • Executive Vice President

    EFC Systems, Inc.

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Information Technology and Services industry)

    January 2006 — January 2007 (1 year 1 month)

  • CEO

    The De Palma Group, Inc.

    (Banking industry)

    October 2004 — January 2006 (1 year 4 months)

    Overview: The De Palma Group is a payments and treasury consultancy focused on providing services to the financial services industry, including:
    • Education and learning through teleseminars and onsite training classes.
    • Provided project-based consulting services as needed

    Notable Projects:
    • Worked with a large bill service provider (CheckFree) to implement and launch PINless debit (RFP, vendor review, contract negotiations,
    technical implementation)
    • Acted as adviser to CheckFree during the acquisition of PhoneCharge, Inc.
    • Worked with third-party ACH processor to craft RFP for ODFI services
    • Worked with large online banking platform provider seeking to be introduced to e-commerce merchants to discuss bill payment opportunities

  • Director, Product Management

    Paymentech

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; Financial Services industry)

    January 2002 — October 2004 (2 years 10 months)

    Managed Paymentech eCheck product, growing ACH transaction volume at an annual rate of 53% to current 120MM item processing platform, processing PPD, WEB, TEL, CCD, POP and ARC standard entry classes

    Developed and launched POP and ARC e-Check processing in 2003

    Re-launched ACH product as eCheckSelect, offering a
    complete suite of ACH services, risk management tools and consumer authentication services through a partnership with eFunds Corporation

    Implemented and launched PINPoint Debit Bill Payment product (PINless Debit)

    Created e-Check University, a one day training class covering the industry, functionality, regulatory and sales/marketing components of electronic check products

    Presented at NACHA eCheck 2003 and 2004, Payments 2004, The Payments Institute East 2003, and opening plenary at The Payments Institutes East/West, 2004

    Interviews published in American Banker, Internet Retailer, Los Angeles Business Journal, The Green Sheet, and Bloomington Times-Herald

  • Vice President

    JP Morgan Chase

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; JPM; Banking industry)

    July 1995 — January 2002 (6 years 7 months)

    Responsible for the management of a $12MM foreign exchange product line for corporate treasury marketplace, focusing on revenue development, market positioning, client issues and collaboration with sales on new client business opportunities. Managed nostro bank relationships. Complete P&L responsibility, including revenue and global technology expense management

    Developed and delivered product-specific training classes, including domestic and international funds transfer, global cash reporting, foreign exchange, investment/debt instruments, disbursement products and ACH products.

    Developed and delivered a wide array of training programs focusing on the Chase InsightSM and Chase WorkspaceSM treasury workstation products, to audiences including sales, customer service, Chase internal technical personnel, and Chase cash management clients

    Traveled nationally and internationally (London, Hong Kong, Singapore, Australia) to deliver training to clients and internal colleagues
