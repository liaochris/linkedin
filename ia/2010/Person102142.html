John Godec’s Summary

John Godec is an International Association of Facilitators (IAF) Certified Professional Facilitator©, Vice-President, course developer, and trainer for the International Association for Public Participation (IAP2), a roster member of the U.S. Institute for Environmental Conflict Resolution, and he helped develop a risk and emergency communication protocol for the National Governors Association in the United States.

He has trained and managed projects in the United States, Canada, Mexico, Europe, South Africa, Australia and Southeast Asia for Fortune 50 corporations, and local, state, federal and tribal governments and NGOs. He’s been involved with nearly 300 complex and oftentimes controversial public communication and environmental projects for mining, manufacturing, energy and public works in his 30 year career. Issues ranged from transportation corridor location, design, and construction, major manufacturing facility development, pipeline siting, hazardous waste remediation, urban and regional planning and a host of others.

Godec has been interviewed by NBC, CBS, ABC, and CNN in the U.S., CNN Europe, Canada’s CBC, the Wall Street Journal, New York and L.A. Times, Washington Post, and dozens of other national, regional and local print and broadcast media. He speaks at events about public participation, environment and sustainability, crisis, conflict, consensus, and issue management, and lectures at Arizona State University and Indiana University’s School of Public and Environmental Affairs, and in Australia at the Queensland University of Technology.

He is the former Director of Issues and Crisis Management for Motorola Corporation, and he was Communications Director of the Arizona Department of Environmental Quality.

John Godec’s Specialties:

Godec, Randall & Associates Inc. is a consulting and training firm that specializes in high stakes communication, outrage management, consensus, public relationships and involvement, and conflict resolution for public, environmental, and social programs.
