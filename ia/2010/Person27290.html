Branndon D. Kelley’s Summary

Branndon brings a Bachelor of Science in Computer Information System, a Master of Business Administration (MBA) and over eleven years of experience managing, directing, governing and implementing technology programs and projects. His professional experience bridges many industries, including financial services, healthcare, retail, consulting, industrial, government, energy, employer services, and education. Branndon is a seasoned professional with exceptional references. He has helped organizations realize business value through the implementation of strategic technology solutions. Those technology solutions range in size, complexity and scope including projects in both the private and public sector(s); up to 100 million in total contract value.

Branndon brings a diverse background in IT operations, project management, executive leadership, strategy, and business development.. His technology skills and business acumen allows him to communicate to individuals from a technical to executive-level.

Today, Branndon is the Chief Information Officer for American Municipal Power (AMP).

AMP is a nonprofit leader in wholesale power supply for municipal electric systems. They help member communities control their destinies in the volatile world of power supply.

Owned and governed by their members, AMP purchases, generates and supplies electrical power for 128 publicly owned utilities serving more than 570,000 customers in six states — Ohio, Pennsylvania, Michigan, Virginia, Kentucky and West Virginia.

Branndon oversees all information technology and SCADA functions, projects and people. He is responsible for setting, facilitating and leading technology strategy and tactical execution.

Branndon is very involved in the local, regional, and national IT community. He participates, shapes and speaks at a number of CIO and IT related events.

He spends free time with his wife Shannon and his son Jackson He is also an adjunct professor at a local college.
