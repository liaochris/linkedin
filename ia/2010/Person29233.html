Bridget Serchak’s Experience

  • Public Affairs Officer

    National Transportation Safety Board

    (Government Agency; Public Safety industry)

    August 2007 — Present (3 years )

    Bridget A. Serchak is a Public Affairs Officer for the NTSB in the Office of Public Affairs. She joined the Safety Board in August of 2007, bringing with her seven years of transportation public relations and marketing communications experience in the areas of passenger rail, transit, highway, and marine. In addition, Ms. Serchak has more than 15 years experience in marketing, legislative affairs, and foreign affairs in the federal government, non-profit, and private sectors.

    As a member of the NTSB public affairs team, Ms. Serchak serves as a press officer on go-teams for the Board and coordinates Board responses to the media and other constituencies at headquarters, accident sites, Board meetings and public hearings. To date, Ms. Serchak has served as an on-scene public affairs officer at the following transportation accidents: Carmichael, MS (propane pipeline explosion), Weaverville, CA (USFS contract helicopter crash), Sherman, TX (chartered motorcoach crash), Capitol Heights, MD (EMS helicopter crash), New York City, NY (Hudson River commercial aircraft ditching) and Washington, D.C. (Metrorail Red Line collision).

    She holds an A.B. in Politics and History with honors from Mount Holyoke College in South Hadley, Mass., and an M.S. in Integrated Marketing Communications in Corporate Public Relations from the Medill School of Journalism at Northwestern University in Evanston, Ill.

    Ms. Serchak belongs to the Federal Communicators Network, the DC Aviation Communicators Group, and the Rail Women’s Network, and the Capital Communicators Group. She was an examiner for the U.S. SPQA in Virginia, the state affiliate of the Malcolm Baldrige National Quality Award, and continues to serve on the SPQA marketing committee. She is a member of the Arlington County (Virginia) American Red Cross Disaster Action Team and is certified in disaster public relations and communications.
