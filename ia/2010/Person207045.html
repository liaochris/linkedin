Tony Rus’s Summary

I grew up working on cars and my first car was a red '69 Camaro with 327 small block engine. Math was easy and I loved to build things so I went to college to become an engineer. My formal degree is in mechanical engineering. After graduating from CASE in Cleveland, I started designing medical implants using Pro/ENGINEER a 3-D solid modeler that then was analyzed by FEA software tools. With my manufacturing experience, I coupled it with my new Pro/ENGINEER skills and I went to work for Parametric Technology Corporation or better known as PTC. Having worked for a software company, my programming and automation skills started to develop. I then started to use these new talents to remove “waste” (i.e. muda) from the design to manufacturing process. To address one of the largest areas of non-valued waste in business it is with exchanging CAD data. I joined a NIST funded project where I worked at Parker and worked with GEAE on a $21M project called Federated Product Intelligent Environment or better know as FIPER. I used my skills to further develop a System Engineering approach in the decoupling of physical interface information from the solid 3-D design models. The goal of this System Engineering initiative is “Zero Defects.” At the GDLS corporation on the FCS MGV program the concept of Modular Product Architecture was developed and deployed within the 600 user Common Intralink Environment (ie. PTC’s Pro/INTRALINK). I now use my Systems Engineering skills to manage the development of customer requirements to develop a product structure for STERIS, a medical device company, which started out with a revolutionary liquid chemical sterilization desktop unit. The System II effort is multimillion dollar project with the goal of defining the next generation of liquid sterilizers.
