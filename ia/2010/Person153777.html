Nichole Allem’s Summary

Nichole Allem is a recent graduate of the Public Diplomacy program at Syracuse University, and received a M.A. in International Relations from the Maxwell School and a M.S. in Public Relations from the S.I. Newhouse School of Public Communications. Through the International Relations program, she was concentrated on two career tracks: Public Diplomacy and Negotiation and Conflict Resolution, with a regional focus on Europe and the Middle East. She is currently studying Arabic, and has experience in Spanish, Danish, and Farsi.

Allem is adept at building relationships with constituents in various sectors and media outlets, a result of her background in strategic communications and media relations. Her management experience stems from her work as a senior account executive for a student-run communications agency, during which she established performance goals, policies, and procedures for team management and operation.

She has consulted on a number of strategic communications and development projects for public, non-profit, and private sector clients. A competent and motivated researcher, Allem has extensive experience writing white papers, memos, case studies, and press releases. Her graduate case study on American public diplomacy broadcasting efforts in the Middle East was recently published by two peer-reviewed journals, and a second case study into leadership and information management in the Abu Ghraib crisis is available in a case bank housed at the Moynihan Institute of Global Affairs. The case bank is used for comparative research, theory-building, and crisis management trainings.

A native of Canton, Ohio, Allem graduated from Elon University (cum laude) with a Bachelor of Arts degree from the School of Communications in corporate communications and a minor in English literature.
