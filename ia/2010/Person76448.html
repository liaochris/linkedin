Rene Godefroy’s Summary

A recognized authority on peak performance and change management, Rene has inspired thousands to stretch, push beyond their limits, be persistent, and accept rejection as part of the process.

His story of perseverance—one that shatters the ceiling of doubt and fear (of change)—touches the soul and rekindles the spirit. From the struggles and seemingly insurmountable challenges of his life, Rene draws 5 fundamental universal success principles that he shares with sales people across America.

He often reminds them that principles don't change based on the economy or the current challenges or the rejections they face daily. They are the compass that govern companies and individuals. They must be the front and center of every transaction when it comes to dealing with prospects, clients and co-workers.

Rene's journey began in an isolated and impoverished village in Haiti. As a child, he was often tormented and tortured by malnutrition, dysentery, cholera and other deadly diseases. Too weak to withstand the strong tropical winds in his village, Rene often spent his childhood days on a dirt floor fanning flies off of his face.

His mother left him behind with a neighbor when he was only nine months old. Young Rene survived primarily on breadfruit and the occasional rare fish he caught with a string and a bent hook. The frail and ailing boy built up strength by climbing coconut and mango trees for survival.

At the age of seven, he went on to the city to join his mother in a little shack infested with rats and roaches.

In 1983, at the age of 21, Rene left Haiti for Montreal, Canada. But Montreal was not the dream—America was. He arrived in this country with five dollars, two shirts and one pair of pants housed in a tattered briefcase. And he didn’t speak any English at all.

Today, Rene energizes, empowers, and inspires audiences worldwide. And above all, he's living the American Dream!
