Laura Lyon’s Summary

Since my departure as a Corporal working for the General of Enlisted Personnel at USPERSOM in July of 1990, I have been in progressive positions in the area of Procurement.

My training and years working at the highest level of the US Army, where all enlisted assignments were made, enabled me to step into the high profile positions managing company suppliers to meet company goals. In the military, mission is key...it's the same in the private sector.

Being able to understand the company's mission and finding the right suppliers to fit the need, at the right cost, while developing new ways of doing business and moving forward, is critical.

Moving from a small company which specialized in procurement for larger companies such as IBM, Timex, Hilton Hotels and others prepared me for direct responsibilities and assignments in purchasing for Pepperidge Farm and Campbell Soup.

Now that I'm in a new position working with a company specializing in the Government Contracting arena, headed by a CEO who is a professional in the field, seasoned, passionate and committed to integrity, deliverables, and quality of work, it's opened up many new possibilities for my background. It's a very exciting time for me as I make some very important changes.
