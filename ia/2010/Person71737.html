Aaron Freed’s Experience

  • Risk Analyst

    FHLB

    (Government Agency; Financial Services industry)

    June 2007 — Present (3 years 3 months)

    FHLB Cincinnati is one of 12 banks in the FHLB system, which is in the Ohio, Kentucky and Tennessee region. FHLB Cincinnati's mission is to supply credit reserves for its member banks through advances and its mortgage purchase program while providing a competitive dividend on member capital stock. For its small members, FHLBs serve as an intermediary to Wall Street. Its $80 Billion fixed-income portfolio includes assets, such as collateralized Advances, Mortgage Backed Securities, collateralized Mortgage Loans, and highly liquid short-term investments; its debt includes deposits, discount notes, and consolidated obligations (variable, swapped, and fixed-rate callable/non-callable).

    As a quantitative risk analyst in the Financial Market Risk Analysis department, my role is to best simulate a range of potential outcomes for the quarterly dividend payable through analysis of the balance sheet. This analysis includes investigating historical profitability trends by analyzing balance growth and decline of Advance, mortgage, and investment asset classes and the types of debt that fund them. Further, the analysis requires understanding the impact of yield spread made as it rises and declines on both short-term and long-term balance sheet items. In performing this task, I utilize financial modeling software, such as BancWare (income simulation), PolyPort (cashflow modeling of prepayment speeds), Excel, VBA, Bloomberg, and other proprietary applications.

    July 2006 — May 2007 (11 months)

    ABR Capital began as a hedge fund trading option spread premium in a decreasing volatility environment. Its primary trading revolved around 10-delta out-of-the-money vertical spreads on indices. During my stint in the option trading operation, my responsibilities included trade execution of all option and spot trades, position risk management, and trade generation discussions with the managing partner. Other areas of responsibility included administrative day-to-day operations of the Fund.

    As the market became increasingly more volatile, the managing partner converted the flagship fund into a fund-of-hedge funds investing in top-shelf, brand-name hedge funds like your Caxtons, SACs, and Highbridges of the world. My new responsibilities were aimed toward fund conversion, fund marketing, raising funds, and finding the best of the elite group of hedge funds.
