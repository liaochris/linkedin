Bjørn Dudok van Heel’s Summary

Founded the Singaporean investment vehicle Veneti Capital in 2008. Focussing on ROIs as well as ethical and social responsible enterprising (ROSI), Veneti Capital invests in under-performing and start-up companies and sets a benchmark on the field of ethical and social responsible enterprising by donating 5% of every (start up) portfolio investment to the Peduli Anak Foundation. Veneti Capital took 5 participations and exited 3 of them. For the remaining 2, Veneti Capital acts solely as an investment holding company.

Worked as trainee/banker at van Lanschot Bankers in 2007.

Developed the Business & Charity Concept and co-founded the Peduli Anak Foundation in 2006 (50 full time employees). Based upon pure business principles, the Peduli Anak Foundation can be characterized as a vertically integrated charity organization with a strong focus on commercial activities in order to become self sustainability over time.

Obtained a master degree in corporate Law (LLM) in spring 2005. Managed an investment project in Indonesia during the summer of 2005 and attended the Rotterdam School of Management’s full time MBA program by the end of 2005. Obtained an MBA degree by spring 2007 and started studying for the CFA Charter in 2010.

Started my career in the Royal Dutch army in 2006. After being dispatched to the Former Yugoslavia and traveled extensively across our planet, I developed the vision that every person and business has to contribute to both the environment as well as the people for whom prosperity is only an abstract term. A combination of business and development work will become the sustainable business trend in the 21st century.
