Kevin Cameron’s Summary

I'm an electronic engineer with a software bias. Experience in analog design (specifically: power electronics), then EDA tools, specializing in parallel processing mixed-signal simulation. I'm used to working with others peoples' large C/C++/Verilog projects.

Contributed to the development of the Verilog-AMS and SystemVerilog standards. Claim to fame: inventor of connect-module methodology in Verilog-AMS.

I'm good at reducing problems to fundamentals, and finding alternative approaches that can be implemented with minimal disruption.

Currently working on an ESL project: extending C++ for parallel processing and hardware description - from "the cloud" to transistors. If you are interested in joining in, link up!

I can provide my C++ and Verilog-AMS parsers that were developed as LGPL open-source under other licenses if that is of interest.

I also have a fair amount of expertise in managing Linux (installing, migrating, upgrading, kernel rebuilds etc.) which I'm happy to do on a contract basis.
