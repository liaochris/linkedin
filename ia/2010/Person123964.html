Leonard Murphy’s Summary

I am a seasoned and respected industry leader with an entrepreneurial drive. I am an innovator and have successfully established several companies in the MR space including Rockhopper Research, a leading full service global research firm and MDM Associates, a MR consulting firm, before founding my current firm, BrandScan 360.I also am the Principal of The LMC Group, a private consulting firm.

My career in research began as the Director of Call Center Operations for The Myers Group (TMG) in Atlanta GA where I built the data collection capabilities of the firm out and was an early adopter of both web and IVR based methods. I led the development of their product development initiatives. After a successful tenure at TMG, I left to assume the role of Vice President of Operations and Client Services at DialTek, a research provider focused on using automated telephony as a methodology for research. There I developed the strategic marketing plan, developed all of the product innovation strategies for the firm, and led the Sales and Operations organizations.

Before catching the research bug, I accrued over 11 years of experience in senior-level roles with Weblink Wireless, Ryder TRS, Jackson Hewitt, and H&R Block. My area of specific expertise is developing innovative strategic marketing research solutions that align with organizational needs to increase market share and profitability. I am a key consultant and adviser to numerous market research agencies, and work across the industry to drive the development of innovative research practices by developing strategic alliances with multiple “best in class” providers.

I serve on the Board of The Market Research Global Alliance, the premier social network for the global MR profession.I am the Founder and Executive Director of the Research Industry Trends Monitoring Group & Publisher of the Research Industry Trends Study.

Leonard Murphy’s Specialties:

Rounding out my busy professional life, I am the Editor in Chief of the GreenBook Blog, one of the most widely read blogs in the MR space.
