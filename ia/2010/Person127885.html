Lou Heldman’s Summary

Excerpt from Wichita State announcement Oct. 31, 2007:
Lou Heldman, former president, CEO and publisher of The Wichita Eagle, will join Wichita State University as Distinguished Senior Fellow in Media Management and Journalism.
Gary L. Miller, provost and vice president for academic affairs and research, made the announcement Tuesday, Oct. 30.
Heldman will teach and conduct research in the W. Frank Barton School of Business and the Elliott School of Communication at WSU. He will also be engaged in a number of special projects in the provost's office.
"We are extremely excited to be joined by someone with Lou Heldman's record of achievement," Miller said. "The unique set of experiences that he brings to the university will be of immeasurable value to our students and our faculty. We are just delighted to welcome him as a Shocker."
"We look forward to Lou's help in improving students' understanding and use of business communications," said W. Bartley Hildreth, interim dean of the Barton School. "It's an excellent example of how the university cooperates to get business leaders into the classroom."
Heldman received a bachelor's degree in journalism from Ohio State University in 1972. He completed a program for management development at Harvard Business School in 1988.
Heldman said he's excited to take on this new role.
"My new assignment is broad enough to cover each of my major interests: journalism, business and projects to advance the university and community," he said.
Susan Huxman, director of the Elliott School, said Heldman's national reputation in media management makes him an ideal fit for the university.
"Our faculty and students will benefit enormously from his special expertise, experience and connections to the community," Huxman said.
