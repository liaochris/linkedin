Gabrielle Yetter’s Summary

Extensive background in media, broadcast and sales with a successful record in creating revenue growth, developing business partnerships, implementing sales and marketing programs and growing businesses.

I started my professional career as a journalist at The Star (the largest daily newspaper in South Africa) then created The Ultimate Dining Guide of San Diego to indulge my love of writing and food.

I subsequently had public relations experience at The Canaan Group in New York, followed by a position as PR Manager at the Batiquitos Festival of the Arts in San Diego.

My most extensive professional experience is with Business Wire -- starting as an account executive in San Francisco then moving to London to open the company's first international office and newsroom. After running the operation, hiring staff members, creating a newsroom and developing a strong client base, I moved to manage the Washington DC bureau for Business Wire.

My next move was to Boston to develop the role of New England Account Manager for Medialink, which produces news-oriented content distributed via TV, radio and the internet.

I founded The Brightside Group in March 2009 with the intention of inspiring people to see on the bright side of dark situations.

My first product, the Screaming Pillow, was created as the result of the death of my sister, Katy, and her baby, Sven, in a car crash many years ago, since my mother was advised to scream into a pillow as an outlet for her emotion.

The name Brightside Group is in memory of my sister who was born in Brighton, England, and whose maiden name was Said (pronounced "side").

While the legacy of the Brightside Group lives on, my life recently took a different direction when my husband and I sold our house and most of our belongings and moved to Cambodia. I am presently working with an NGO which empowers rural people living in the provinces and peeling back the many layers of this fascinating country.
