  • Deputy Assistant Secretary

    U.S. Department of Homeland Security

    (Government Administration industry)

    April 2004 — January 2009 (4 years 10 months)

    As Deputy Assistant Secretary, I provided strategic communication counsel and public affairs advice to the Secretary of Homeland Security and DHS leadership. DHS is the third largest federal department with more than 215,000 employees and an annual budget of more than $50 billion. In this position, I was responsible for the overall management and implementation of communication plans for DHS programs and policies, federal rules/regulations, and complex domestic and international issues requiring extensive stakeholder, surrogate and legislative affairs outreach. Issues included: annual budget request to Congress, immigration reform/border security, critical infrastructure protection, emergency preparedness, bioterrorism, cross-border travel, among others. In this role, I successfully strengthened strategic outreach efforts and proactively sought opportunities to improve awareness of and image of DHS while promoting department programs among key stakeholders including think tank/academic community, industry and associations, national opinion leaders within media and homeland security fields, and members of the former 9/11 Commission. I also helped lead the national incident communications team for past natural disasters and annual hurricane season, as well as preparation and response planning for potential terrorist attacks. Previously served as Director of Strategic Communications and Deputy Press Secretary while at DHS.

  • Deputy Press Secretary, USA Freedom Corps

    The White House

    (Government Administration industry)

    August 2002 — April 2004 (1 year 9 months)

    Served as White House “on-the-record” spokesperson for USA Freedom Corps, a post-9/11 initiative designed to help Americans answer the President's “call to service” by helping their fellow citizens in need. Helped develop and implement media strategies relating to USA Freedom Corps announcements, events, initiatives and programs. Wrote and developed materials including media advisories and press releases, fact sheets, remarks and progress reports about USA Freedom Corps initiatives and accomplishments. Conducted outreach to media, nonprofit, and sports organizations to promote the President’s Volunteer Service Award; identified and booked media opportunities for the President’s Council on Service and Civic Participation to promote initiatives and partnerships as well as the importance of community involvement and volunteer service.

  • Deputy Press Secretary

    U.S. Department of Education

    (Government Agency; Government Administration industry)

    January 2001 — August 2002 (1 year 8 months)

    In this role, I helped develop and implement successful communications plans for the department, including media strategy relating to the No Child Left Behind Act of 2001, the first legislative initiative of the new Administration resulting in historic education reforms to our nation’s schools. I frequently traveled with the Secretary of Education and served as a spokesperson for media attending events. I generated national and local media interest and exposure prior to the event, while also coordinating media opportunities and interviews for the Secretary of Education and other senior department officials. I regularly wrote press releases and media advisories relating to department announcements and events.
