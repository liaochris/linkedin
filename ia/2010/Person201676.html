Mary Elizabeth Yoder’s Summary

Through school, Mary Elizabeth helped support herself by working as a model & continues today as an actress. It was then that she became intrigued with creating images behind as well as in front of the camera. She has had the privilege of studying with Playhouse West Alumni: Jeff Goldblum & Mark Pellegrino. She continues her studies on camera developments in between production projects with Actors Certified Training: Tim Lapros

Currently in production is the documentary, Mary's Story on the power of forgiveness. Mary Elizabeth Yoder was with seven of her family members when a drunk driver struck their car. Yoder's life went upside down and inside out until she finally learned how to create the proper environment to fully grieve the death of her father, Arnold C. Yoder, some 24 years later. Like the Phoenix rising from the ashes, she has emerged as a powerful healer. Mary Elizabeth has taken her personal tragedy and created a miracle to benefit others. In addition, Yoder has developed a model that is innovative and new.

Mary Elizabeth Yoder is a catalyst for others to claim their own light and empowerment. In her presence, you cannot help being captivated by her spirit, her love for life and everyone in it. Mary Elizabeth Yoder will instantly inspire you.

Today, she is based in Beverly Hills/Los Angeles and offers her skills as an entertainer from acting, producing and public speaking engagements to one-on-one healing & empowerment coaching sessions.

Actor, Producer, Empowerment Project Coaching, Intuitive Visionary, Inspiration & Motivation. Healer

  • Founder & CEO

    The Arnold C. Yoder Survivors Foundation

    (Non-Profit; Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    October 2005 — Present (4 years 11 months)

    The Arnold C. Yoder Survivors Foundation (TheACY) is a 501(c)(3) charitable organization dedicated to the support and empowerment of those who have had someone close has died. Our mission is to provide a loving, safe environment in which grieving children, families and survivors on both sides of tragic events discover their own unique Grief - Forgiveness - Healing process through art therapy, support groups & education.

    Mary Elizabeth Yoder is the daughter of Arnold C. Yoder, after whom The Foundation is named. After she lost her father to a drunk driver in 1981, she began an ongoing effort to regain her enthusiasm for life. Yoder's life went dim until she opened her heart to self forgiveness and the forgiveness of others.

    Her passion is self-empowerment and the empowerment of others. One of the ways she achieves this is by mentoring others who have had challenges in their lives and remain stuck in toxic patterns. M.E. also interviews successful people who are open to sharing how they to have transformed loss into a source of encouragement & inspiration. (Please visit http://www.youtube.com/TheACY to watch recent podcasts.)

    To be whole again you must first forgive yourself, forgive others and seek forgiveness." - Mary Elizabeth Yoder

    Ms. Yoder life experiences culminating in the formation of The Foundation as an outreach to other survivors. Her educational background lend her unique expertise and abilities to communicate with people who have had similar experiences.
