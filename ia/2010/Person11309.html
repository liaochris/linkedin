Andre Elijah’s Summary

Andre Fedor is a young film editor from Toronto, Canada. With a long history in both film and music, his enthusiasm and drive have consistently taken him from project to project. Having once been a child actor, Andre has grown up surrounded by the booming Toronto film scene and eventually gave up his life in front of the camera to take a peek at what goes on behind it. That one decision has since landed Andre as camera operator on Atom Egoyan's "Auroras". "Auroras" was the feature piece for Toronto's Luminato Festival and featured seven video images projected across three gallery walls, synchronized so as to enable a collaborative retelling of the story of Aurora Mardiganian, an émigré of the Armenian Genocide who eventually settled in America and became the source of inspiration for a Harvey Gates film.

In hopes of becoming both a better editor and a more valued member of any crew, Andre has now started to work with the RED One Digital Cinema Camera. Andre is now helping crews better realize the potential of this breakthrough camera by establishing and aiding on-set workflows and enabling a better experience in post production by creating a seamless process starting on set all the way through to working in an editing bay. Andre is currently driving the back-end of many RED projects in Canada, and is currently cinematographer, editor, and producer of Banner Isaac’s Mind Fields due out this spring.

Andre also works as a Social Media Consultant. After spending years moving from one social networking service to the next - Andre has found ways to implement them into a strategy in order to further marketing efforts and ensure success in the mobile and social spaces. Andre uses this knowledge to educate companies on how to take an investment of time and turn it into consumer interest and popularity.
