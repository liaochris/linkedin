Daniel J. Sullivan’s Summary

Daniel J. Sullivan’s expertise provides clients with dynamic emergency preparedness, prevention, response, and recovery solutions in both the public and private sector.

Dan specializes in first responder exercises for federal, state, local, and tribal entities. As an exercise designer he has worked extensively with public and private sector groups all over the United States, some of which included international participation, developing both discussion- and operations-based Weapons of Mass Destruction (WMD), All Hazards, and Public Health exercises.

Previously, Dan has worked as a Deputy Project Manager for a major Federal contractor, where he was responsible for exercise development and working on technical assistance programs for the US Department of Homeland Security (US DHS), US Marine Corps, and more than a dozen states. His work as a central figure for hurricane and public health exercises and general master scenario events list development has been recognized by both federal and state clients. Dan also spends considerable time volunteering with various amateur radio communications groups including assuming responsibilities as the Chairman of the South Central Task Force Amateur Radio Working Group (SCTF ARWG) and is a part of the amateur radio planning committee for the US Marine Corps Marathon.

Dan earned a Bachelor of Arts in history with a minor in political science from the University of Massachusetts Lowell and a Master of Arts in International Studies and Diplomacy from the School of Oriental and African Studies, University of London in the United Kingdom. He has been certified under US DHS as a Homeland Security Exercise and Evaluation Program (HSEEP) trainer, has been ICS certified through ICS-300, received Hazmat Operations Level EMS certification and has earned the Professional Development Series Certificate from the Federal Emergency Management Agency (FEMA).
