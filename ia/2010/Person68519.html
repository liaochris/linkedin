Ed Wang (gmail: eymw.linkedin)’s Summary

Technically, I am someone with a strong numerical methods background who is working on quantitative finance problems, predominantly in the options space. I have worked on quantitative projects in the Chicago trading community since 2001.

Projects have ranged from building Black-Scholes-Merton American option pricing models with various underlyings (exchange-traded and OTC products) to pricing volatility skew to options risk management to portfolio re-balancing to quantitative infrastructure for a low latency, options market making desk.

I have a strong interest in the low latency space within trading and would like to focus on all aspects of that business going forward.

Am not interested in speaking with recruiters at this time, but am open to networking and meeting other finance professionals within proprietary trading, investment banking, and alternative investments. Ed Wang (gmail: eymw.linkedin)’s Specialties:

I have implemented 7 finite difference option pricing models for Black-Scholes-Merton American options during a recent 15 month period, of which 3 have gone into production for generating theoretical values, implied volatilites and "Greeks".
