Peter Dannenfelser’s Summary

I started at Johnson & Johnson in 1996 as a coding/design contractor and by the time I left them in 2002, I was Associate Director of eCommunications. I was part of the team that launched the groundbreaking Ortho TriCyclen television campaign by developing and managing the strategic on-line portion of the program. I created the first pharmaceutical eCommerce program by launching the Ortho Personal Pak site/store. I helped develop J&J's initial rules and regualtions around privacy and data collection. I always tell people my passion for using the web for healthcare turned my career from a HTML jockey to "a real job."

I went on to spend five years at Novartis. First paving the road for international product sites in global oncology then moving on to become Director of Interactive Marketing for the US oncology franchise. I called what I did "interactive" before ad agencies pigeonholed it to mean "eMarketing." I was developing "interactive" on- and off-line patient relationships through CRM and adherence programs. After some time there I moved to the General Medicines group at Novartis and headed up "New Channel Development," a position that fostered innovation and piloted new media opportunities for industry leading brands.

I was soon given a great opportunity to become Vice President of Strategic Innovation at Cadient Group. There I was instrumental in successful programs like Novartis Vaccine's Flu Tube campaign and was co-creator of J&J's worldwide Applied Digital Marketing training program.

After a little over a year and change on the agency-side of the world, a phenomenal opportunity presented itself to me a Forest Laboratories where I am now Senior Director of Marketing Communications. Here I oversee eMarketing, Meetings and Conventions and Corporate Communications - all three critical customer touch points that are evolving to meet the communication demands of our audiences.
