  • VP Products

    MOVE Mobile Systems, Inc.

    (Telecommunications industry)

    January 2002 — December 2006 (5 years )

    MOVE is a design research lab focused on finding ways to dramatically improve the speed and ease with which users can interact with full-scale Web services on pocket-size handheld devices. The lab’s early design work (discussed in mobile-device design circles in 2003-2005) influenced several touch-screen handsets now on the market. The lab’s more advanced, patent-pending man-machine-interface & design innovations are being licensed to design services firms. (See www.move-mobile.com for a bit more.)

    Led intellectual property development and prototype development, managing small teams of industrial designers, engineers, and IP attorneys. As a Board member, I continue to help lead technical & design engagement with design services firms and tier-1 notebook computer & consumer-electronic OEMs.

  • Director of Product Management

    Inviso, Inc.

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Consumer Electronics industry)

    January 2001 — December 2001 (1 year )

    Led team of product managers for this 70-person Qualcomm-backed mobile display and device innovator. Represented product-management in discussions with leading handset makers. Company sold its IP to a public company in Texas in 2001.

  • Lead Product Manager, Designer

    MVP Solutions

    (Computer Software industry)

    September 1996 — December 2000 (4 years 4 months)

    Designed & patented the first “group buying” B2C eCommerce platform (patent# 6,269,343), then initiated and led negotiations that resulted in an IP acquisition by MobShop.com, a social-eCommerce startup backed by Mayfield Fund and Marc Andreessen. The acquisition helped MobShop achieve a $150M valuation in its next financing round (during the boom, of course).

    Led design and development of a highly-acclaimed information-retrieval software application named "Retrieve It!", and led strategic-alliance & business-development efforts that resulted in Apple Computer publishing the product, with leading Internet services co-marketing it. Also led PR early on. (Money quote: "You'd have to pry Retrieve It! from my cold dead hands before I'd give it up." - Bob LeVitus, MacUser Magazine & Houston Chronicle columnist.)

    Also created a Web community based on our popular “Talker” speech-synthesis Web-browser plugin, attracting over two million users in the first year (1996).

  • Sr. Product Engineering Manager

    Apple

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; aapl; Consumer Electronics industry)

    January 1990 — July 1996 (6 years 7 months)

    Led cross-functional teams and served as key engineer in the teams that designed and developed Apple's "PlainTalk" suite of speech products, within Apple's Interactive Media & Advanced Technology Groups (run at the time by Kai-Fu Lee). Developed key elements of speech recognition system including the core Mac-optimized recognizer engine, the language model parser (patents #5,689,617 and #5,651,096), much of the MacOS Speech Recognition Manger API, and elements of the Mac OS's speech-related UI. Also led developer evangelism for these MacOS components -- speeches to thousands of developers at Apple's annual World Wide Developers Conference, press briefings, and journal articles.

  • Director of Product Development

    Greenleaf Medical Systems

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Medical Devices industry)

    July 1988 — December 1990 (2 years 6 months)

    Led design and engineering of an advanced medical system used by hand surgeons and physical therapists to measure and track hand injuries. It integrated a set of custom, ergonomically-optimized hand-held instruments into a sophisticated but extremely easy-to-use software application. Drove full product cycle, starting with gathering product requirements by observing surgeons and physical therapists as they used traditional manual methods for measuring hand impairment – then refining requirements using interviews and prototype user-trials. Formalized these into MRDs and PRDs; led development team, serving as software architect and lead programmer; and represented product teams in successful distribution discussions.
