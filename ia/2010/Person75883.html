Gregory Miller’s Summary

From early 2006 through August 2009, Gregory Miller was Managing Director at Google.org leading its philanthropic investments, grants and legal teams. He also was CFO/Treasurer, Secretary and General Counsel of the Google Foundation and chair of its investment committee. Alongside former Executive Director Dr. Larry Brilliant, Greg helped initiate and develop Google.org's programs and funding initiatives addressing global economic development, global public health and climate change. During that 3+ year tenure, Google.org grew to 42 persons, awarded approximately $100 million with 120 grants, led 11 philanthropic investments, and was recognized in widespread news articles, blogs and conferences recognizing its data-driven, outcome-based approaches.

Greg led executive production of "The Final Inch", a Google.org film documenting current polio eradication efforts in India and Pakistan (2009 Academy Award nominee for Best Documentary -- Short Subject, and winner 2009 Pare Lorentz Award from the Int'l Documentary Assn).

Prior to Google, Greg worked with start-up companies, principally with businesses competing in the emerging Internet, network security, communications and software markets, including as a partner with Silicon Valley's Gunderson Dettmer law firm and Of Counsel with Latham & Watkins, and as Sonicwall's (SNWL) first general counsel through its IPO. He was on the launch team of one of the earliest 802.11b wifi deployment companies. He has closed over 100 significant transactions, including public equity offerings (>$3.8 billion), debt financings (>$13.5 billion) and acquisitions (>$2.5 billion).

Greg serves on boards of directors / advisors with several start-ups and non-profits. Currently, he's a director of the American Himalayan Foundation and a few early stage technology and renewable energy start-up boards.

Greg is an Adjunct Professor teaching a class on entrepreneurship for a sustainable furture at Presidio Graduate School.
