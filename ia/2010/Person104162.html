  • Founder

    The Cluster

    (Internet industry)

    2000 — 2003 (3 years )

    The cluster was a loose affiliation of independent new media related businesses. In the deep dark recesses of the first crash I believed that the way forward was through collaboration, not through expansion. This proved to be correct ‘back in the day’. Charter members included ourselves, HI-Res, Preloaded, Neoworks, Lshift, Less Rain and main others.

  • Founder / Owner

    Obsolete

    (Internet industry)

    April 1995 — April 1997 (2 years 1 month)

    In February 1995 I was approached by BBC Radio 1 and given the opportunity to develop their first website Radio1nteract to accompany an evening of ‘digital’ programming. It was this seed capital which enabled me to set up Obsolete. We then went on to do the website for ‘The Net’ on BBC2 with illuminations and then go on to win Levi’s.

    Obsolete grew to about 15 people by the beginning of 1997. My ‘day job’ was Levi’s but at the same time worked did a myriad of projects in the music space in addition to the first websites for Nature magazine and the Fortean Times.

    Key Outputs: www.levi.com, www.warp.net, www.nature.com, I-Candy (pretty much the first rich-media advertising format)

    Highlights: Simply being there and helping define the industry. Meeting Timothy Leary as he was promoting his death blog in LA. Being front page of the Media Guardian.

  • Founder / Editor / Designer

    Convulsion

    (Music industry)

    1991 — 1995 (4 years )

    Started whilst in University, Convulsion was an alternative music fanzine. I edited, designed and wrote a substantial amount of the content. By issue 4 had international distribution and a 10 track CD. Convulsion was in the first wave of entirely digitally produced fanzines and the first to go online in the UK. In addition to the magazine I ran a club night ‘Apocalypse’, which funded most of the magazine.

    Key Outputs: 4 Issues of the magazine, 3 years of club night, 2 weeks of gigs & clubs at the Edinburgh Festival 1993

    Highlights: Interviewing Kurt Cobain a few months prior to his death. Being backstage & onstage at Lollapalooza 1 in Washington D.C. with Jane’s Addition & Nine Inch Nails. Interviewing Underworld @ Tomato.
