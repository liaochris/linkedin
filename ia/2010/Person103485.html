John Zeisler’s Summary

SENIOR TECHNOLOGY EXECUTIVE

....and private investor and advisor with early stage technology companies. I also source investment opportunities with local venture firms, assist portfolio companies, and consult on emerging market opportunities.

Recent investments in Boku(mobile payments), Zetta(cloud storage), Vidyo(HD video conferencing) and Filtrbox (social media monitoring).

A past director of Infoseek(sold to Disney), PlaceWare(sold to MSFT), eVoice (sold to AOL), NetBotz (sold to APC), Airgo Networks (sold to Qualcomm), Lightningcast (sold to AOL) , and Applied Science Fiction (sold to Kodak).

Twenty-five years of Silicon Valley investment and operating experience. A former senior technology marketing executive (Apple, Claris, Netcom) I have experience in venture capital, business and corporate development, and technology and consumer market development, in organizations ranging from development stage through Fortune 500. Served on the board of more than a dozen public and private companies.

John Zeisler’s Experience

  • Private Investor

    Zedventure

    (Financial Services industry)

    2003 — Present (7 years )

    In addition to direct investments, I help source and drive new investment opportunities with local venture firms, assist portfolio companies, and consult on emerging market opportunities.

    Some notable recent investments include Boku (digital goods payment via your mobile phone account), Filtrbox (real time social media monitoring - acquired by Jive Software), Vidyo (H.264 SVC -based HD videoconferencing), Zetta (enterprise cloud storage), Six Apart (blogging software and services); NextG Networks (RF over fiber backhaul for carriers); and Vitalstream (VSTH - content delivery network; acquired by INAP).

    During this time period, I was also affiliated with WaldenVC ($225mm fund in Digital Media, Services, eCommerce) and Gabriel Venture Partners ($260mm fund in Wireless, Software and Services, and Security).

  • General Partner

    Nokia Venture Partners

    (Public Company; NOK; Telecommunications industry)

    2000 — 2002 (2 years )

    I was a General Partner responsible for mobile technology investments for Nokia’s $500 million, global venture capital fund, now known as BlueRun Ventures. I led investments in eVoice (telco software/services company; acquired by AOL), Airgo Networks (80211.n components; acquired by Qualcomm), and Lightningcast (broadband streaming media ad network; acquired by AOL). I also participated in investments in wireless IP networking, 802.1x infrastructure and services, mobile telephony infrastructure, software, components and devices.

  • General Partner

    InterWest Partners

    (Partnership; Venture Capital & Private Equity industry)

    1996 — 1999 (3 years )

    InterWest Partners is a decades-old top quartile Sand Hill Road venture capital firm. I was a partner in the Information Technology group, and was responsible for leading investments in software and Internet services, including web conferencing (PlaceWare; Acquired by Microsoft), digital imaging (Applied Science Fiction; acquired by Kodak), and business-to-business e-commerce (Live Capital). I also participated in investments in enhanced telephony services, carrier-class switching, optical networking, wireless infrastructure, and graphics semiconductors.

  • Senior VP Marketing

    Netcom Online

    (Telecommunications industry)

    1994 — 1996 (2 years )

    We grew Netcom grew from a small regional ISP to become the nation's leading internet service provider, on the heels of the internet boom. We went public with 500,000 subscribers and $120mm in revenue. Netcom was subsequently acquired by Earthlink during an industry consolidation phase.

  • President and CEO

    Pensoft

    (Computer Software industry)

    1992 — 1994 (2 years )

    Pensoft built database applications for the first generation of tablet computers and personal communicators. The company was acquired by AT&T as part of their grand vision of personal communications.

  • Cofounder and VP Marketing

    Claris Corporation

    (Computer Software industry)

    1987 — 1991 (4 years )

    Due to conflicts with its 3rd party developers, we wrote the plan for Apple to spin out its application software business to form Claris. It was a thrill to work with a world class management team led by Bill Campbell, including Donna Dubinsky, Yogen Dalal, Bruce Chizen, and Randy Komisar, and so many other talented individuals.

    I crafted the initial company business strategy and ran Product Marketing, Marcom, Customer Support, Training, and Publications.

    In just 4 years we grew from crazy idea to become 4th largest application software company in the PC industry with $100 million in revenue. Apple re-acquired Claris in 1991.

  • Marketing Director

    Apple Computer

    (Computer Hardware industry)

    1980 — 1987 (7 years )

    Apple invented the Personal Computer industry in the late 1970's. I joined in 1980 to run sales across the northeast. We launched the Apple II that year, created the channels to sell personal computers, went public, and finished the year with $100 mm in revenue.

    In 1981, I moved to California to manage Sales Development, and joined the sales and marketing team that launched the Macintosh in 1984. I later ran the Business Marketing group. I left Apple around the time the company reached $8b in sales to start Claris with Bill Campbell..

  • President and CEO

    Boston Video

    (Consumer Electronics industry)

    1978 — 1980 (2 years )

    Boston Video Center was the first video specialty retail store in New England. We introduced the first generations of large screen television, VCR and laser disc products to consumer, professional and industrial markets.

  • Product Manager

    Advent

    (Public Company; ADVS; Computer Software industry)

    1976 — 1978 (2 years )

    In its day, Advent was the leading manufacturer of home audio products (the industry's top selling loudspeaker), and introduced the video industry's first projection TV's. Advent was founded by Henry Kloss, the brilliant inventor and the K in KLH, and earlier co-founder of Acoustic Research.
