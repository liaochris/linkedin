John Parkinson’s Summary

John Parkinson is a Senior Vice President and the head of the Global Program Office at Axis Capital. He is also the Founder of ParkWood Advisors LLC.

Previously he was a corporate vice president and the Chief Technology Officer for TransUnion LLC.

Prior to TransUnion he was the Chief Technologist for Capgemini’s Americas Region, developing strategy and setting direction for Technology Consulting offers, promoting Technology Innovation, working with early adopter clients and influencing competency and practice models for Capgemini’s 23,000 technology consultants.

Before Capgemini he was a Senior Partner at Ernst & Young LLP. He joined E&Y in the UK in 1985 and moved to the USA in 1991 where he helped lead the turnaround of E&Y’s consulting business and later served as the CIO. John has a BS in Mathematics and an MS in Information Sciences, both from Exeter University, UK. An example of his work as a solution architect is included in the Computerworld-Smithsonian collection.

He has written or edited five books on information systems development and has contributed over 40 papers to conferences and journals. He writes a monthly column for CIO Insight and is a member of their editorial advisory board. He is a frequent speaker at international meetings on innovation, ecommerce; technology strategy; emerging technologies and related topics.

John is an external director for Tap.tv a media services and technology start-up in Chicago. He is also an advisory board member for Morningstar, Inc. and IOCOM in Chicago.

In 2003, John was named as one of the world’s 25 most influential consultants. In January 2005 he was named as one of the Computerworld 100 Leaders in IT.

John Parkinson’s Specialties:

Business and technology strategy; organizational change; organizational design. business and technology architecture; technology operations; information security.
