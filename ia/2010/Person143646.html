Michael Thomas’s Summary

Exceptional creative with high ROI. My passion is creating and executing compelling, relevant and innovative brands, communications, products and experiences for clients of every size.

Working with entrepreneurs lead me to obtain an MBA focused on business planning, leadership and strategy with emphasis on brand and product development and brand planning. My interest in emerging technology, renewable energy, economic and environmental sustainability found full expression in our final project: a business plan for an IP start-up in the algenic biofuel industry.

Working with my clients and contributors, I have successfully:

- re-positioned and branded Alpha Cine as a national resource of choice for independent and digital filmmakers

- worked on re-branding AT&T Wireless marketing communications after their spin-off from corporate. I worked with teams on translating brand objectives into frameworks for visual communication and keeping execution 'on brand'

- played a team leadership and innovation role in developing Microsoft Reader user experience, look and feel, and brand

- re-branded and clarified marketing objectives for Holland America Line. My insight into the marketing challenge presented in the creative brief resulted in a new marketing campaign focused on travel agents

- developed product planning, brand and marketing strategies for TillmanThompson Homes, a small scale homebuilder in South Puget Sound. Sales velocity in Phase I was double that of similar competing offerings

- made key contributions for test market execution of CocoNo cocoa substitute product launch for Coors Food Products

My creative work has been recognized in Graphis Poster, Tellys and Halos

I have been named as co-inventor on 4 U.S. Patents in first commercial utilization of ClearType technology and pioneering graphical user interface design

Please see my portfolio at http://michaelthomascreative.com

Michael Thomas’s Specialties:

Marketing, strategy, advertising, communications and brand development, planning, consumer insight, frameworking and user experience.
