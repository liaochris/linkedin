  • Market Expert in Global Securities Lending - Corporate Actions

    Bank of New York Mellon

    (Public Company; BK; Financial Services industry)

    August 2007 — June 2009 (1 year 11 months)

    I was an EMEA and Asia-Pacific Markets Expert in Corporate Actions, at Bank of New York Mellon.

    Let me explain you what is a Corporate Action... A Corporate Action (CA) is an event initiated by a corporation which impacts its shareholders. Companies wanting to either reward their investors, restructure its capital organization or influence its securities value will do so through a CA.

    Here are some very interesting examples of Corporate Actions that I have processed:
    - GDF Suez Merger on 22 July 2008 (for the creation of a worldwide energy leader)
    - HSBC £12.5bn capital raising through a Rights Issue in March 2009 (in order to cope with the global economic downturn)
    - or General Motors Bankruptcy in June 2009

    My team was specialized in CA Global Securities Lending. We were lending securities from our institutional clients to brokers against an exchange of collateral and we were following up all CA events on the entitled loan trades. The main reason for brokers to borrow securities was to cover a short position.

    Many complications could arise when the borrower (the broker) was defaulting on its obligation to return the loaned securities. This actually happened with our "beloved brothers" Lehman who went through bankruptcy in September 2008. Our business was inherently risky, but it was one of the main generator of revenues for the bank.

    The challenge of globalization in Corporate Actions is real.
    C.A. is plagued by the risk associated with:
    - manual processing
    - non-standardized processing
    - languages
    - market practices
    - a multiplicity of intermediaries
    - inconsistent data quality

    I was also responsible for the programming of automated spreadsheets templates in order to facilitate our daily work and to reduce our manual process. I managed to implement an automated solution.

    The big priority in CA for Custodian banks is to set up a straight through processing system and try to be cost-neutral.
