Editing and writing, teaching students and professionals, developing curriculum, recruiting and hiring, managing a professional staff through coaching and performance evaluation.

Kathy Schenck Price’s Experience

  • adjunct lecturer

    Northwestern University

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    2007 — 2010 (3 years )

    Taught editing to incoming graduate students. Developed the current curriculum to teach editing for print, web and broadcast.

  • Workshop leader

    Publishing industry

    (Newspapers industry)

    2000 — 2010 (10 years )

    American Copy Editors Society
    2000 to 2010: Presenter at annual and regional conferences. Primary seminar on skeptical editing covers news judgment of stories, visuals and Web content, using examples from the Milwaukee Journal Sentinel and other papers. The seminar included different examples each year. Other ACES seminars included polling, newsroom training and work/life balance.

    Also presented Skeptical Editing at seminars by the Associated Press Managing Editors' News Train, American Press Institute, the Inland Press Association and the Freedom Forum. Presented seminars on recruiting and performance management at the Mid-America Press Institute, and seminars on writing display type at the Agricultural Media Summit.

  • Assistant managing editor/copy desk

    Milwaukee Journal Sentinel

    (Public Company; Newspapers industry)

    March 1999 — July 2009 (10 years 5 months)

    • Supervised 30 copy editors and the production of all sections except sports and editorial pages.
    • Helped coordinate and lead changes in deadlines, zoning and editions.
    • Developed staff to take leadership positions on copy desk and throughout newsroom, where the copy desk was seen as a talent pool. Many copy editors were promoted to management as line editors, and some became reporters, columnists and designers.
    • Lead author of Words to the Wise, a blog on language and editing. Other online initiatives included a nightly podcast of tomorrow’s headlines and adding a full-time copy editor to newsroom’s breaking news team.

    Additional responsibilities:

    Sunday editor
    2005 to 2009: News editor of the Sunday paper, selecting stories and directing presentation for A1 and the metro section. Supervised news gathering and production on Saturday nights.

    Trainer
    2004-2005: Lead trainer for conversion to CCI print publishing system. Created curriculum and exercises for reporters, line editors and copy editors. Taught dozens of JS staff members to use the system.

    Recruiter
    1999 to 2008: Traveled each year to Universities of Illinois and Missouri, and Northwestern, to recruit interns. Established pipelines from these schools.

    Culture committee
    2003 to 2009: Leader of group charged with improving newsroom culture. Coordinated quarterly staff awards, including soliciting and collecting nominations, overseeing the judging, and writing and editing the speech the managing editor delivers to the newsroom. Also organized the annual newsroom outing to a Brewers baseball game, which includes a tailgate for 80-100 people.

    Company initiatives
    Summer 2006: Selected as member of first Mid-Level Leadership program. Fourteen managers from around the company received leadership training and chose a project. Our group, dubbed Circular Push, created a reverse-publishing advertising product.

  • Senior editor/copy desk

    Milwaukee Journal Sentinel

    (Public Company; Newspapers industry)

    October 1995 — March 1999 (3 years 6 months)

    Led staff through the aftermath of the Journal Sentinel merger in 1995 and through the transition to pagination in 1997. Supervised production of news sections, editing all local stories on A1 and metro front. Developed staff through training and performance management while maintaining high morale.

  • Copy editor

    The Milwaukee Journal

    (Newspapers industry)

    June 1990 — April 1995 (4 years 11 months)

    Business desk copy editor/slot
