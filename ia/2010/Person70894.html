Francis George’s Summary

With $68.23, no job, and a big ugly duffel bag, Francis George arrived on the West Coast one spring day. When he left later that summer, he had a Clio Finalist, two Adweek "Best of Portfolio" TV spots, and a spot featured on "ABC's Funniest TV Commercials."

Francis' work has been featured in just about every industry publication, including Communication Arts, Archive, Adweek. AdAge, One Show, and Print.

After years of being on the creative end of the marketing and branding process, Francis decided it was time to move to the other: Strategy! Since 2003, he has acted as the Director of Marketing for Albanese + Lutzke, a firm that designs golf course worldwide.

In 2006, he accepted a gig in the marketing department oft a company with arguably the smartest branding in the world - Apple Computer.

Since then, he's been acting as a Brand and Marketing consultant for various clients around New York City.

These days, you can find him wandering the impeccably-designed and Herman Miller equipped offices of The Republik, a company he helped start almost 10 years ago.
