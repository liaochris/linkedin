Santiago Siri’s Experience

  • Founder

    The Whuffie Bank

    (Internet industry)

    March 2009 — Present (11 months)

    The Whuffie Bank is a nonprofit organization dedicated to building a new currency based on reputation that could be redeemed for real and virtual products and services. The higher your reputation, the wealthier you are.

    It’s in the same spirit of Creative Commons, an organization that’s changing the contract between us and our ideas; that we want to build an open organization that aims to measure and enable the exchange of online reputation.

  • Founder & CEO

    Popego Inc.

    (Privately Held; Computer Software industry)

    January 2007 — Present (3 years 1 month)

    We create AI algorithms and innovative interfaces to deliver a more personal and meaningful web.

    Backed by leading venture capital firms Aconcagua Ventures and Monashees Capital, Popego was considered among the 50 best startups of 2008 by TechCrunch, were we launched our public beta in September '08.

    In 2009 we launched Meaningtool (www.meaningtool.com), a Semantic Engine that can learn new concepts and categorize websites and text in multiple languges.

  • Meme Replicator

    Palermo Valley

    (Non-Profit; Internet industry)

    February 2008 — January 2009 (1 year )

    What began as a simple message on Twitter baptizing a neighbourhood of Buenos Aires as "Palermo Valley" due to the high concentration of technology statups ended up becoming a social event for developers, entrepreneurs and investors of the local web scene.

  • Founder & Board Member

    Asociación de Desarrolladores de Videojuegos Argentina

    (Computer Games industry)

    May 2001 — April 2008 (7 years )

    Instrumental in establishing a game development industry in Argentina. Worked organizing the Argentine Videogame Expo (EVA - www.adva.com.ar/eva/) and the Argentine Game Coding Contest (CODEAR - www.adva.com.ar/CODEAR).

    Represented the voice of the game development community to the local press.

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; Computer Games industry)
