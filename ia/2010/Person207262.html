Torsten George’s Summary

Who is Torsten George?

Until recenty, I was vice president of worldwide marketing at ActivIdentity, a provider of strong authentication and credential management solutions. I was initially recruited to apply my expertise in SaaS and assist in building a new business unit from ground up. Later I was appointed to lead the company’s PR, IR, field marketing, corporate marketing, and solutions marketing teams.

Prior to joining ActivIdentity, I served nearly six years as chief marketing officer for Quick Eagle Networks (a.k.a. Digital Link), a leading network communications equipment vendor. At Quick Eagle Networks I managed the corporate marketing, channel marketing, product marketing, product management, business development, and customer services groups. My technology foresight and ability to rally an entire organization to share my vision were essential in turning the company profitable after several years of losses.

Before my tenure at Quick Eagle Networks, I was senior director of marketing for Everdream Corp (acquired by Dell), a desktop management service provider. Previously, I was director of international marketing and products for a renowned software vendor, where I led the re-launch of the company from a core provider of programming tools for developers to CRM software. I started my career as a business consultant at an international consultancy before moving into a product marketing position at an intelligent transportation systems company in Germany.

I have published numerous books and articles in industry publications. I am a member of the advisory board of Cordys, a global provider of software for business process innovation and Enterprise Cloud Orchestration, and served on the advisory board of Solid Information Technologies prior to its acquisition by IBM. I hold a doctorate in Economics, a master of business administration degree in Marketing and Business Strategy, and a bachelor of science degree in Law.

Torsten George’s Specialties:

Marketing Communications (branding and identity, trade show management, advertising, online marketing, PR), Product Marketing (competitive analysis, MRDs, product launches, collateral development), Channel Marketing (sales champion recruitment, lunch & learns, floor days, spiffs with pass-through, instant rebates, promotions, and general channel programs), and Business Development (negotiated strategic alliances with partners such as Hewlett-Packard, Sony, Compaq Corporation, and Siemens AG).
