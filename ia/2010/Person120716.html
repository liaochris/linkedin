Lynette Alcorn’s Summary

I entered the cooperative sector in 2005, when I became a graphic designer for Farm Credit Bank of Texas. We are a wholesale bank, owned by our customers - 19 rural lending cooperatives in a five-state area. Our lending co-ops provide ag and mortgage loans to their owners: ag producers, agribusinesses, country homeowners and rural landowners.

I have a checkered work past: advertising agency, campus ministry, comic book company, freelance and a printing company (15 years). Maybe I'm finally walkin' the line with the Farm Credit System (a nationwide network of financing co-ops, established by Congress in 1916 and regulated by the Farm Credit Admin.). Home, sweet home.

My usual design projects include brochures, ads, annual reports, Landscapes magazine, logos, invitations, newsletters...it's ridiculous to try to list them all.

I'm active in the Cooperative Communicators Association and I'm always looking for ways to connect and network inside and outside this fine group. If you're in communications for a cooperative, I invite you to join this generous and conscientious group (http://www.communicators.coop).

I'm also involved with a camp, Disciple Oaks Retreat Center (DiscipleOaksRetreat.net), and a few churches (UCCAustin.org) and help them with their graphics and promotion.
