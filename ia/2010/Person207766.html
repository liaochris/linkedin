Travis Alber’s Summary

Currently I run BookGlutton: the world's first social reading platform. I cofounded the company, designed the experience and work on strategy and biz dev. We've bootstrapped for three years (it took a while for the industry to catch up - we were thinking about how to integrate communities and reading before the Kindle came out). It's obvious 2010 is the year digital reading hits a tipping point, so we're out looking to raise a round of funding, iterate faster, and change the world.

Previously:
Acted as creative director for rich media pieces, online community projects and digital newsletters. Primary responsibilities included developing high-level concepts, leading discovery, setting goals and requirements, running brainstorming sessions, creating timelines, overseeing storyboards and managing production.

Served as art director on instructional modules for the Creative Learning Studio, an on-line R&D educational division. Primary responsibilities included planning and production supervision for non-traditional interfaces and motion graphics, and functional management of flash creative team.

Concepted, art-directed and scripted interactive Flash and rich media ads.

Introduced role of multimedia artist to consult clients on rich media options. Responsibilities involved client consulting, storyboarding and production of interactive Flash pieces. Management responsibilities focused on coordinating designers, information architects, production artists and engineers on rich media projects. Clients included: Dodge, Macys, Sony, Driving.com, Midway Games.

Worked as designer for a streaming video service, and an early contender in on-line video news personalization.
