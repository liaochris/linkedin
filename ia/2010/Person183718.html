Scott Schablow’s Summary

A Senior Marketing Executive with more than twenty years in advertising, public relations, digital marketing and social media with expertise in a broad range of industry categories, such as consumer goods, telecom, healthcare, financial and tourism/travel.

As a former Capitol Hill Correspondent, is articulate, intelligent and possesses excellent analytical, communication and persuasion skills. A highly adaptive, innovative leader and consummate consensus builder who has consistently produced outstanding results.

Scott Schablow’s Specialties:

Social media marketing, marketing strategy, product brand management, product director, marketing director, social networking, brand planning, general management, user experience, customer insights and online branding

  • President

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Marketing and Advertising industry)

During the mid-90's I shifted the focus of the agency from general marketing to a specialized Internet marketing group. SBMC was one of the first agencies in the region with it's own Web server and Web development team. The agency merged with Luckie & Company in 1998.

  • Capital Hill Correspondent

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Broadcast Media industry)

    September 1986 — May 1988 (1 year 9 months)

    Television news correspondent for network affiliate stations across the U.S. I interviewed House and Senate members in Washington, D.C. and produced TV news stories for local stations in Houston, Phoenix, Chicago, Portland, Birmingham and other markets. I frequently interviewed such notables as Bob Dole, Dick Cheney, Bill Richardson, Phil Gramm, Olympia Snowe and more. I also occasionally attended press briefings in the White House with Ronald Reagan and interviewed George H.W. Bush when he ran for President.
