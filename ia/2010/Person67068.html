Erin Marie Daly’s Summary

I'm a San Francisco-based legal journalist and freelancer with a passion for reporting cultural and human interest stories.

As a senior reporter for a New York City-based legal newswire, I have written hundreds of articles on product liability issues in the health care industry, particularly relating to pharmaceutical companies. My work in this area has included reporting on developments in personal injury, product defect, and wrongful death lawsuits against global drug companies and medical device makers, analysis of state and federal legislative developments involving the health care industry, and features on district and appellate jurisdictional trends. I have also covered nationwide litigation and regulatory issues spanning intellectual property and patent law, environmental law, financial and securities law, and employment law, among other areas.

In 2007, I was nominated for a GLAAD Media Award for Outstanding Digital Journalism for my profile of a homeless transgender teenager battling the streets of New York City. I have spent substantial periods of time freelance reporting in countries such as India, Bosnia and Russia. My cultural analysis of all-girls roller-derby matches, evangelical Christians struggling with homosexuality, pursuing love in Russia’s HIV-positive community, Bosnian war amputees finding new life through volleyball, post-9/11 sentiment in Brooklyn, the debate over an alleged link between routine childhood vaccines and autism, and more has appeared in Law360, the Moscow Times, In The Fray Magazine, Brooklyn Papers, and Art & Understanding, among others.

I hold a master’s of arts degree in cultural reporting and criticism from New York University, and a bachelor’s of arts degree in rhetoric from the University of California at Berkeley.

I currently live with my husband and dog in the Noe Valley neighborhood of San Francisco.

Erin Marie Daly’s Specialties:

I gravitate towards the difficult stories. If it's gut-wrenching, tragic, or hard-to-get, it's the story I want to cover.
