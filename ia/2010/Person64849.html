Emmet Kelly’s Summary

I've been working in digital marketing and research since 1996, and worked with Nua Internet Survey's and Local Ireland from 1997 to 2000. I'm a data specialist and sometime lecturer in statistics at UCD. Monitrack was formed as an online market research specialist in 2000, and carried out the first national internet research project in the world in 2003, with considerable IP attached. This was called Joint National Internet Research, (or JNIR). This project is now largely defunct as Irish people go to more and more international sites. Net Behaviour was formed by myself, Justin Cullen, and Sinead Morris in 2005, and now employes 10 people. NB is a digital media buyer for some of Ireland's largest ad agencies including, Zenith Optimedia, OMD, II and Vizeum among others. Both companies are profitable and are growing in line with the online space. Monitrack provides MIR research for Net Behaviour and other clients. This is an online barometer of internet behaviour and site visitorship complete with full dems and reach and frequency algorythms. We track circa 500 websites used by Irish people in Ireland and throughout the world, and publish tables on industry sectors on the blog (www.thetannoy.com), to Ireland ad agencies and in Marketing magazine. Books and reports are planned. The data will be carried in Media Star. (Btw. I was a key developer of EARS data for radio research in 1996, so am doing the same for the net... kinda... ).

Thats it and that me, when I'm at work. I'm married to Ellen, and have five beautiful sons; Charlie, Daniel, Leo, Myles and Felix. :-)

Emmet Kelly’s Specialties:

Digital research, creative, media buying, tracking, site auding, spend optimization etc. I love writing about the Internet, and exploring how digital communication is changing our society, and psychology.
