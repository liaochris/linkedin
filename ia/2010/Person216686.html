William Volk’s Summary

Mr. Volk began his career helping to launch the computer game division of Avalon Hill. He would create three original game titles there.

Mr. Volk was recruited for a division of Epson … creating easy to use drawing software for their QX10 computer system. He would become the West Coast Technical Manager.

In 1995 Mr. Volk co-founded Aegis Development (Amiga Software). As the VP of Development he oversaw the development the first animation and video titling systems for personal computers.

Mr. Volk accepted a position as Director of Technology at Activision, eventually becoming the VP of Technology. Responsible for the development of the first CD-ROM Entertainment Project (The Manhole, 1989) and the ‘discovery’ of it’s talented authors (Rand and Robyn Miller), who would go on to create Myst. Mr. Volk helped to create the products that secured the company’s success, including the highly acclaimed “Return To Zork.”

At Lightspan, Mr. Volk created a process that enabled title production to go from an 18 month to 4 month cycle. His strategy of moving to the Sony Playstation after the collapse of the Interactive Television Market in 1995, and successful management of the conversion was key to the survival of Lightspan. This lead to a successful IPO in Feb. 2000.

Starting in 1995, Mr. Volk helped to design a secure on-line coupon system. In 1999 Mr. Volk joined Save.com as a full time founding CTO.

Mr. Volk is the founder and chairman of ZipProof, a patent-pending “soft proofing” and collaborative software firm used by Print Shops from Colorado to Chile.

Mr. Volk was the co-founder of Bonus Mobile Entertainment and the co-designer of the social mobile game "The Dozens" insipred by the Wayans Brothers card game.

Currently Mr. Volk is the CEO of MyNuMo (now PlayScreen), a company that publishes mobile games and enables independent artists to publish into the $7B mobile content market.
