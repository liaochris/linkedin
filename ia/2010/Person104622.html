Jon Ward’s Summary

TO MY FELLOW ENTREPRENEURS…

As a business owner there’s only one way to grow: find new clients. In some 30 years of creative consulting, I’ve found that nothing compares to the Internet as a marketing tool — for precision, results and cost-effectiveness.

But you must know how to use it right, or find people who do. That’s why Andy Renk and I created Click for Clients. It’s a place where business owners can drive growth online.

Andy built a $12m online business when most of us didn't know what "online" meant. I'm a strategic consultant to Brandwithin and a Trump University faculty member. So we probably know some stuff that could help you.


IS YOUR WEBSITE A PROFIT CENTER?

Most people's online strategy can be summed up with the infamous phrase: “Build it and they will come.” This is a six-word recipe for failure. I keep seeing the sweetest (and costliest) business websites with no traffic.

A smaller minority have fallen in love with “SEO” and their results are better. But they’re still trying to draw a triangle with two sides. Forget about “having a website” or “doing SEO” . Start creating a total online marketing machine with one focus — increasing revenues. Think sales and work back from that point.

Your marketing machine requires three fundamental elements: Traffic + Leads + Conversion (TLC). In other words, get people to your site, capture their contact information and woo them to become clients.

What could your website do for your bottom line if you applied these fundamentals? If you’re curious, you can find out with a free website cash projection at:

http://www.ClickForClients.com

You may have to be patient: we’re getting tons of requests for these and each one is done by a real human being! Meanwhile, you are welcome to contact me at this email address: jon@clickforclients.com or call me at 480.488.7021.

All success to you and your online marketing!

    (Marketing and Advertising industry)

Within a short time, I was the principal writer serving Pentagram Design and Reuters UK (financial services).
