  • professiomal trainer and coach

    Aquarion Water Company

    (Privately Held; Utilities industry)

    2004 — 2007 (3 years )

    Aquarion Water is a one hundred and fifty year old organization with headquarters in Bridgeport, CT. and MA/NH. Great organization, great people and great change!!! I was brought into the organization to open communication channels and help champion change as the organization undertook a complete transformation caused by layoffs, sale of the company to a British firm, an aquisition by Maquarie Bank of Sydney and a 15 million dollar, company wide, SAP implementation at the same time as going Lean!
    Quite a challenge. What did I learn that is so obvious and yet so misunderstood - communication, communication, communication. 1)Communication doesn't float down through an organization it has to be driven and driven hard - it doesn't happen naturally and what's understood at the top is misunderstood at the bottom! 2) Get people on the bus, get them in the right seat (Good to Great) and then tell them clearly where they're going, why and how - if they don't know where they're going or why they get scared, confused, angry and disheartened.
    There is no magic pill to successful transformation -- there are no shortcuts. Change is looked at by many as something to fear, even dread! The question they ask themselves is "what's it going to cost me, what am I going to lose?" A successful transformation is the result of a well informed workforce asking a different question: "what's the opportunity, what am I going to gain?"
    I was fascinated a few years ago when a Japanese consultant, working on Lean with UTC, shared with me the true meaning of Kaizen. He smiled as he broke the Japanese 'signs' down for me on his napkin and carefully wrote under each HAPPY CHANGE. When I thought of the fear HAPPY CHANGE brings to so many uninfomred employees I couldn't help but realize how far we have to go on the 'change' front before we do it right!
