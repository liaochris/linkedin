Salvatore Scellato’s Summary

I am a Phd student at the Computer Laboratory of the University of Cambridge, where I started my PhD in April 2009. Previously, I have been a visiting student at the same Computer Laboratory from October 2008 to March 2009.

I received a Master Degree in Computer Science from the University of Catania in July, 2008.

In 2007 I was Visiting Student at University College London, working within the Network and Mobile Systems group.

In 2003 I was admitted to Scuola Superiore di Catania, university institution for higher education, where I won a 5-year grant.

I have a broad interest in social networks, human mobility and in any type of mobile and distributed systems, but also on the investigation of complex networks structure and dynamics. In the past I have also been interested in computer vision and image processing.

More specifically, my research interests are in the following areas:

* Social networks and their dynamics
* Human mobility modeling and prediction
* Mobile systems
* Application of complex networks to distributed computer systems
* Wireless sensor networks
* Urban data-mining and network-based city analysis
* Models of urban vehicular traffic and its optimization

Salvatore Scellato’s Specialties:

Problem solving, software architecture design, scientific software developer, leadership attitude.

Strong interest and skills in scientific research.
