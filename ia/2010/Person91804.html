Jonathan Ballon’s Summary

Jonathan Ballon is responsible for the Office of Strategy & Planning at Cisco Systems (NASDAQ: CSCO), a $40B+ provider of Internet & communication solutions. Jonathan's primary focus is the company’s globalisation strategy and $7B+ services business.

Jonathan holds a seat on the company's Strategy Exchange Board, accountable to the CEO. In his role, he contributes to the definition of the firm's strategic priorities, planning initiatives and investment focus. Jonathan's team has accountability for a $250M annual investment portfolio.

Jonathan was previously co-founder and Managing Director of Global Operations for Cisco's growing advisory consulting business. In this capacity Jonathan was responsible for managing the group’s expansion and scale into international markets. Specifically, Jonathan led business development, client delivery and consulting operations across Europe, China, Taiwan, Singapore, and Australia.

Prior to Cisco, Jonathan was a founding member of the executive team at inCode Telecom, a Sequoia Capital-backed wireless professional services firm where he launched and led the Northwest operations and the application integration services group.

Previously, Jonathan was Chief Marketing Officer & EVP for iXL Enterprises - a $500M, 3,200 person e-Business consulting and systems integration firm with 38 offices globally. iXL went public in 1999 and reached a market capitalization of $3.5 billion. Jonathan was also SVP & General Manager for the Digital Media & Broadband solutions practice while at the firm.

Over the span of his career, Jonathan has consulted to many market-leading Fortune 500 organizations.

He has appeared in popular business publications such as the Wall Street Journal, Investors Business Daily, The Economic Times, Newsweek, Business Week, Business 2.0, and Fast Company. He has also been a guest lecturer at Harvard Business School and the Haas School of Business at the University of California, Berkeley.
