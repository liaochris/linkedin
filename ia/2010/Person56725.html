Dina (Piepoli) Udomsak’s Summary

In addition to classroom instruction and student coaching, Dina presents and coordinates developmental seminars & celebratory events, the alumni & women's mentoring programs, and the Sidhu Scholarship program in the Jay S. Sidhu School of Business and Leadership at Wilkes University. Dina is a planning member of the Outstanding Leaders Forum and the Wilkes University Mentoring Task Force.

Previous academic experience includes Pre-Collegiate Program Coordinator at Marywood University where she led the recruitment efforts for the gifted student programs, managed the staff of the GED Community & Prison programs, and secured DOE grant funding for the Pre-Collegiate area.

Prior to Wilkes, Dina was a Technical Communications Consultant contracted by the Pennsylvania Department of Labour and Industry as a Technical Trainer and Instructional Designer.

Dina's background in media and marketing includes experience as a public relations professional, writer (grant, technical, & creative), graphic design artist and desktop publisher. In the television industry, she worked both behind the scenes and in front of the camera lending her skills to regional ABC and PBS affiliates.

Dina's performance background and work with emotional intelligence was recognized regionally by the Scranton Times as the "Northeast Woman" (2009); and as the "Personality of the Week" for her exceptional work in regional, community, and professional theatre and for securing the role of "St. Catherine" in the world-premiere production of "Joan" which was written and directed by renowned New York playwright, Donna Kaz. Kaz's production was later performed off-Broadway and went on to receive acclaimed recognition in Scotland, at the Edinburgh Fringe Festival. Dina continues to work in regional theatre with the Electric Theatre Company (formerly TNT) and works in production with her husband Patrick, an NYU film school graduate and screenwriter.
