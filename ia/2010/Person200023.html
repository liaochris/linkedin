Karl Tatgenhorst’s Summary

Long before I set out as a technologist I was a light infantry soldier with the 82d Airborne in the US Army. While there, I learned perseverance and developed a personal expectation of success. I also developed an affinity for volunteer service while helping to run an animal adoption program out of the Ft. Bragg Animal Shelter. Once out of the Army I found myself working as a full time volunteer at Wolf Park in Battleground Indiana, practicing wildlife photography and dabbling in website development.

Fast forward to now and it seems to make perfect sense that my long winded description of myself would be "A tenacious, creative and civic minded technologist". This self image is what caused me to seek out the position of IT Director at a successful internet marketing firm.

My interests in science and technology, cause me to constantly wonder how things are working. I wonder quantifiably how they are working and this gives me a predilection for analytics, When I hear "things are better now" I wonder "How are they better? How much better?" Imagine an IT Department or operations being run with an analytical mindset. When I start a project I create metrics and begin measuring. Often, I can tell during execution of a project if predicted results are inaccurate. This can lead to much greater efficiencies as tweaks can be constantly applied moving closer and closer to optimum performance.

  • Systems Administrator

    Golden Technologies

    (Privately Held; Information Technology and Services industry)

    April 2008 — September 2008 (6 months)

    At Golden Technologies my primary job description was *nix systems administrator, however I wore several hats while there. First, as part of the admin team I was third tier escalation for tech support. Golden maintained a large group of competent technicians who were the eyes and ears (and hopefully the hands) on site for customer issues. In instances that they needed assistance researching a solution, solving a vexing problem etc... they would escalate to their managers. The managers had worked their way through the ranks and proven their skill and generally it stopped there. However, some problems require specialized knowledge, in these cases since an administrator must by nature be a subject matter expert in his domain the call would be brought to us. This teamwork approach to solving problems was not by nature unique but the effectiveness of Goldens implementation was in a class of it's own.

Maintaining the relationship with this customer was a new and interesting challenge for me and one that I truly enjoyed. I was able to do many interesting projects through that relationship.
