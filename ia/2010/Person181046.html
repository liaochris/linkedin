Sam Diaz’s Summary

For more than 20 years, I have worked as a professional journalist, with the last decade focused on the technology beat. I primarily worked for newspapers but jumped into the online world full-time back in July 2008, following brief stint in the world of PR.

I am a former Financial Officer for the National Association of Hispanic Journalists, a Washington-based non-profit committed to the advancement of Latino journalists and the fair coverage of Latinos in the media. I previously served as San Jose Chapter President for the California Chicano News Media Association. I was also the director of Mosaic, the San Jose Urban Journalism Workshop for Minority High School Student, from 2002-2004.

Sam Diaz’s Specialties:

I specialize in technology - from the products to the trends to the business strategies of the companies in this space.

  • Assistant Technology Editor / Technology Reporter

    The Washington Post

    (Public Company; Newspapers industry)

    August 2005 — November 2007 (2 years 4 months)

    Editing technology news stories of interest to Washington Post readers, with emphasis on consumers and regulatory concerns.

    Lead blogger for Post IT, the Washington Post's Technology Blog. Offering insight into short news of interest items that might or might not have a place in the print edition of the paper, seeking to spark dialogues within the readership along the way.

    Co-Host, Washington Post Technology Podcast: Every Thursday, I co-host a 7- to 10-minute podcast that features an interview with a newsmaker or Post staffer on a story that in the news that week, as well as a regular dialogue with Post tech columnist Rob Pegoraro.

    Regular guest on Washingtonpost radio (107.7 FM in the Washington area or streaming online at washingtonpostradio.com) to talk about a technology story in the news. Airs between 12:45 p.m. and 1 p.m. Eastern Time every Tuesday.

    Oversee a $2.1 million budget for a Washington-based non-profit devoted to the advancement of Latino issues in the news and parity of Latino journalists in U.S. newsrooms.

  • Technology Reporter

    San Jose Mercury News

    (Public Company; Newspapers industry)

    May 1997 — July 2005 (8 years 3 months)

    Worked for five years as a technology reporter, tracking innovations in (mostly) consumer technology, as well as the local Silicon Valley companies behind the innovation. Wrote primarily for the business news pages and the Personal Tech section (while it existed.)

    Prior to that, I covered transportation issues out of the Bay Area's East Bay region.

  • Night City Editor

    The Fresno Bee

    (Public Company; Newspapers industry)

    January 1990 — April 1997 (7 years 4 months)

    This is where it all began for me - I worked as a features reporter as a student writer. I penned a column called Close To Home, which featured "good news" tidbits in a daily column format. In a two-man bureau for two years, I covered Kings County and everything that went with it - one county government, four city governments, numerous school boards, law-enforcement agencies and courts, two state prisons (including one that housed Charles Manson), the Lemoore Naval Air Station and various issues revolving around Interstate 5, the state's primary north-south artery and one of the busiest interstates in the country.

    I spent my last two years at The Bee as the night city editor.
