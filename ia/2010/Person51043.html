David Billings’s Summary

I've always been an odd mix of weirdly creative and highly analytical. Over the years I've learned how to combine these qualities into one big superpower. I take complex concepts and create simple visuals that communicate the right message to the right people.

Early in my career, I helped develop characters and stories for the Nick Jr. show Blue's Clues. If you have kids or have read Malcolm Gladwell's The Tipping Point, you've probably heard of it.

Blue's Clues got kids interacting with TV characters and helped them learn in a super fun way. The producers brought me on board to work with the story team, illustrating the scenes that would make up each episode. We tossed around ideas in that conference room like hot potatoes. On top of creating on-target concepts and illustrations, I got to use my quirky humor to get ideas unstuck.

Working with the smarty-pants producers and directors at Nickelodeon taught me that using a sense of fun and creating a relaxed atmosphere is invaluable in helping people do their best work. Everyone brings unique gifts to a project. The best results come when everyone involved can honor that. It's energizing.

Now that I have my own studio I use all those experiences to help small business people and other designers

Sure, I draw stuff. I’ll give you whatever output you need to get your project completed. That should be expected. What you may not expect is that I care about your whole project. When I'm in, I'm in 100%. I work hard to make sure that the graphics you get are the right graphics for your intended audience.

I’m going to promote the hell out of anything I work on, so I’m driven to make it awesome. Passion is essential.
