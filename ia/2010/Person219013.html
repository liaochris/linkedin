Zheng Shao’s Summary

With a good combination of fast prototyping, system thinking, and research thinking. I am passionate and capable on bridging the gap between researchers and engineers. I will continue to demostrate that, with a world-class programming background and research experiences in data mining, I am able to make better architecture and overall designs during various stages of the projects than by people with only system experiences or only data mining/statistics background.

Zheng Shao is a software engineer in the data infrastructure team at Facebook since Mar 2008. He is a committer of the open-source Apache Hadoop project. His main contributions are in the Hive subproject, plus a few HDFS and Mapreduce fixes.

Before that, Zheng Shao was a senior software engineer at Yahoo Web Search for two and half years, where he worked on large-scale content systems with thousands of nodes (the largest customer of Hadoop in the world).

Zheng Shao is on leave from the Computer Science PhD program at UIUC, with a focus on data mining and data warehousing. He holds a Master's degree in CS from UIUC, a Master's degree in Management Science and Engineering from Stanford University, and a Bachelor's degree from Tsinghua University, Beijing.

Zheng Shao is an active programming competitor, and attended finals/semifinals of Topcoder, Google Code Jam, and ACM/ICPC competitions. He was a gold medalist in International Olympaid of Informatics in 1999 back in high school.

During the two and half years there, I did research in data mining in social network environment based on DBLP data, and data warehouse (especially on efficient algorithms to build data cubes). Published several papers. Following is the abstract of my paper "MM-Cubing: Computing Iceberg Cubes by Factorizing the Lattice Space" at SSDBM’2004

    Iceberg Cubes are data cubes with a support larger than a minimum. I came up with the idea of MM-Cubing in the Data Mining seminar. The algorithm operates in a different way to former iceberg cubing algorithms. It’s based on frequency division while former algorithms are all based on lattice tree. I designed the algorithm and implemented it. After some optimization, MM-Cubing algorithm now outperforms all the former algorithms in almost all data sets. The skeleton of a paper is done and now I am working on some theoretic work about the algorithm.
