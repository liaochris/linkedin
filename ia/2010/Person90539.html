Jason Stone’s Summary

I am a "Corporate entrepreneur"* with a strong background in Internet marketing since 1996. I was one of the early members of HitCom's IntelliPages, having conceived an online real estate sales portal. I then spent six years as the marketing manager for SportingNews.com, where I helped to grow the site to one of the most trafficked sports sites online. I continued to keep my experiences in online marketing fresh by handling the marketing for two construction companies (one historic renovation, one sustainable/green design) that I created after the Internet bubble burst. I am now actively seeking to return to a corporate environment in an online marketing capacity. If you're hiring, call me today.

I have a passion for marketing and advertising, and am always particularly eager to talk strategy and tactics. To "sharpen my saw" in the area, I have been assisting businesses and non-profits with input on their online efforts. In addition, I am faithfully maintaining a blog about my career search experiences and using social networking (LinkedIn, Facebook, Twitter, etc) to enhance my search as well as my skills.

I have a MBA from Washington University and an undergraduate degree in communications with focus on advertising and marketing from the University of Illinois.

* The word entrepreneur is generally accepted to mean someone who starts their own business, taking on the risk of that business's success. Generally, that person is assumed to be a visionary, who cannot work for anyone but himself. While I have created several companies, I have also been part of several startups -- where I wasn't the captain of the ship -- and I still thrived, contributed, and was comfortable with my role. I choose to define myself with the term "corporate entrepreneur" because I have found in my career that I prefer being part of an office culture that encourages innovation, leadership and individual growth as much, if not more, than running the show at my own company.
