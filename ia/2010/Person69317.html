Francesco de Leo’s Summary

Over twelve years of experience in designing, developing, and deploying business applications. Comprehensive knowledge of Information and Communication Technologies. Knowledge Management tools and practices. Experienced in project design, project management and life cycle methodology.

Experienced in e-commerce, messaging, calendaring, web portals, workflow engines, enterprise resource planning, costumer relationship management, help desk case management, data warehousing, contract management, short message service gateways, course management, document management, content management, time reporting and others. Engineered single sign-on to and application interoperability between many of these applications.

He is advisory committee representative to the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C), an international standards body governing the use and development of the Internet to develop interoperable technologies (specifications, guidelines, software, and tools) to lead the Internet and is the Director of Emerging Technologies Program at American University. In 2003, he received the EDUCAUSE award for Excellence in Administrative Information Systems and the Association for Communication Technology Professionals (ACUTA) award for Institutional Excellence in Communication Technologies.

He holds a Bachelor of Science in Mechanical Engineering and a Master of Science in Engineering Management and Systems Engineering from the George Washington University and is currently pursuing a doctorate degree (D.Sc.) at this institution in Engineering Management and Systems Engineering, investigating implications of national culture on Knowledge Management initiatives. Moreover, he serves on the board of directors of the Institute for Knowledge and Innovation.

Francesco de Leo’s Specialties:

designing, developing, integrating, and deploying business applications and knowledge management tools and practices
