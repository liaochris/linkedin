    (Public Company; FDX; Package/Freight Delivery industry)

    October 2004 — August 2005 (11 months)

    Here is the job description provided by FedEx:

    "Service Managers are needed for our front line management team. Roll up your sleeves and get ready for a hands-on supervisory position as you contribute to the smooth operations of one of our facilities. Duties include dispatching, dock management, general operations administration and solving delivery problems."

    Here is how I would describe the job:

    "As a service manager for FedEx Home Delivery, you'll work the most horrific schedule on the planet: Tuesday-Saturday, 3 am - Noon. However, you'll only be paid as a part-time employee.

    You'll be in charge of motivating package handlers who got this job from their parole officer.

    You'll hone your crisis management abilities in situations where one driver may threaten to blow another driver's brains out with the gun he or she apparently keeps in their truck.

    You'll learn how to conduct a proper 911 call when a driver threatens your boss' life and tells him that if he ever sees him outside of work, he'll kill him.

    Finally, you'll sharpen your driving skills by learning how to not only drive a tractor-trailer, but how to back one into a garage without ripping off half of the building."

    All that said, this was a fantastic job.
