Dr. Wade Grindle’s Summary

A graduate of the world-reknowned UC San Diego School of Medicine, Dr. Grindle has extensive experience in all types of anesthesiology, including cardiac, neurosurgical, trauma, major vascular, pediatric, and orthopedic. He was the chairman of his department for four years in the midwest, before returning to California to join the anesthesia group at Eisenhower Medical Center. As a pain management specialist, Dr. Grindle developed a reputation as a compassionate physician who took the time to really listen to his patients. He treats every patient as a whole human being, with unique needs, and customizes the treatment plan accordingly. He was the first physician in the Coachella Valley to become a member of the International Spinal Intervention Society, widely recognized as a leading authority among interventional pain management practitioners.

During his last year on staff at Eisenhower Medical Center, he was the only physician surgically implanting spinal cord stimulators and intraspinal morphine pumps. He left the group at Eisenhower in 1999 to go into solo practice.

In 2001, he also became the first physician in the region to perform intradiscal coblation nucleoplasty. Dr. Grindle has helped thousands of patients conquer their pain, restore their mobility, regain their employment, return to their favorite recreational activities, and repair relationships that have been damaged by chronic pain. He has been overwhelmingly gratified by the response of his patients.
