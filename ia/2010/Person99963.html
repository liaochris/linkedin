Joanna Lord’s Summary

My experience is a combination of both academic and professional adventures. I was fortunate enough to receive my B.A. in Journalism from a liberal arts college focusing its efforts in the online media space, and then finished my M.A. in Communication and Persuasive Media at Pepperdine University. It was during this research-intensive time that I realized my love for conceptual dialog, analytical research, and my passion to apply this to online marketing.

After a year as a marketing assistant I joined the Online Marketing team at OneTime.com, a sister company of the popular travel social network--VirtualTourist.com. I spent 2 years engulfed in PPC, while also contributing to the editorial and product development teams for OneTime.com. My PPC experience fine tuned my eye for monetizing site functionality (cost vs. benefit analysis) and heightened my awareness for what works and what doesn't depending on your online audience. I managed campaigns across the big 3--Google, Yahoo, and MSN, and pushed traffic generation on the social ad platforms--Facebook & Myspace. After realizing that my passion fell at the crossroads of PPC & social media, I started my own company-- YourJobStop.com, a comparison job search site & job resource for those trying to gain an advantage via social media channels.

I am both a Co-founder and the CMO of YourJobStop.com, pursuing all marketing platforms to push traffic and increase brand awareness. I also head product dev & site functionality.

As for professional goals? I have too many to list. I cant think of a better time to be a part of the online marketing industry, and my main goal right now is to immerse myself with as many of it's components as I can, and really challenge what I have used in the past to see where it can take me in the future.
