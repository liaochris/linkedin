Kerry Nay’s Summary

Building personal wealth and financial security is both an art and a science. To be successful, one must use a variety of skills in planning the outcome, implementing the plan and controlling the desired results. Coordinating these three steps is an ongoing and ever-changing process due to the variations in the economic circumstances surrounding one’s life.

Our goal is to provide a more efficient and cost effective approach to building wealth. We use a macro-financial process that focuses on your total financial picture. The only requirement that we make for a candidate who wants to work with our process is for them to be committed to making positive changes to their financial picture.

Kerry Nay’s Specialties:

Using the (LEAP) Lifetime Economic Acceleration ProcessTM, we help our clients to understand that there is no such thing as a separate or segregated financial decision. We do not provide boilerplate solutions. Instead, we incorporate the principles of economics such as supply and demand, opportunity cost, and velocity of money. We use a scientific tool for objectively analyzing your present financial situation and developing a new and more effective approach to money management.

  • Marketing/Sales Support

    RealMed

    (Privately Held; Computer Software industry)

    1996 — 2000 (4 years )

    I was asked by the founders of RealMed to help with the marketing of their new claims paying software system for Medical Savings Accounts. Information Technology was not one of my areas of expertise but I grew to understand much better how software and hardware may be used to create a high level of productivity and cost savings within the healthcare industry. My assistant and I had direct contact with almost every major player at the time within the health insurance industry such as Assurant, Anthem Blue Cross & Blue Shield, Golden Rule, American Community, and Trigon to name a few.

    I also worked with a highly qualified and competent lobbyist to coordinate our executive team with him to schedule meetings with mulitple political and industry leaders to promote a free market within the health insurance marketplace using Medical Savings Accounts, (MSA).

    During this time I was also able to develop several friendships with our company's software developers from which I learned much about the software development process. The men and women at RealMed were among some of the best people that I have ever worked with over the years. They were very considerate in helping me better understand software development and the people who make it work. I have nothing but the highest respect for all the people I worked with at RealMed.
