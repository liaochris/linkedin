Ahmed 'AJ' Bhadelia’s Summary

Having spent my childhood traveling across the globe, I would call myself an international citizen. I have previously lived in India, Saudi Arabia, Sweden, Boston, New York and now in Washington DC. I have interacted with many cultures and traveled to remote places. My travels have seen me work in Peru, Mexico, Europe, and Southeast Asia. I have climbed up to Machu Picchu, hiked across the Andes, traversed the southern highlands of Chiapas, seen the tea farms of Kerala, and much more. My traveling has not come because of a wanderlust, but rather because of a life of curosity and passion.

My life's work, and my life's goal has been towards the less known side of human rights: education and food security. I want to show others that education and food security must be seen within a human rights lens, which in turn should be seen as a national security issue. I'm interested in the frameworks of these issues, and how improving those frameworks can improve the details.

I am a child of the internet generation, and with that I've learned a vast array of technical skills. I strongly believe that my interest in the aforementioned frameworks begins at the intersection of web technology and policy. My current work is a chance to further my previous work at organizations such as NATO-The Atlantic Council of the United States and World Hunger Year, where I combined my diverse technical skills with my deep research interests in policy. I want it to be an ongoing project, and it is something I am pursuing a career in.

Ahmed 'AJ' Bhadelia’s Specialties:

International field research, working in (post)conflict zones, legislative policy-making, non-profit website design, litigation, analyzing local and international policy.
