Lira Luis, AIA, RIBA, NCARB, LEED®AP’s Summary

Lira Luis AIA, RIBA, UAP, NCARB, LEED-AP, is a global American architect specializing in Organic Architecture. She holds multiple architect licenses in multiple countries that include the USA (AZ and IL), Philippines, and a UK Chartered Architect designation from the Royal Institute of British Architects (RIBA). After having gone through rigorous examinations set by each country, she was granted her first license at age twenty-three (23).

Since then she has designed an impressive array of diverse and socially-relevant building typologies in Asian and US Markets from the small-scale award-winning Portable Transient Shelter Pods to large-scale designs for several of the Fortune 100 companies in the USA. She designed, managed, and administered Educational, Healthcare, Retail, Commercial, Office, Multi-Family and High-end Residential, and Institutional Building Projects ranging in budgets from Three-Million Dollars ($3-M) to One-Billion Dollars ($1-B) having gross area ranges of One-Thousand Square Feet (1,000 SF) to One-Million Square Meters. (1-M sqm)

She began earning her diversified experience in 1993, which is further strengthened by a hands-on skill in Building Information Modeling (BIM) Technology. This allows her to delineate integrated design solutions that increase an awareness of environmental responsibility as well as dynamic management of an architectural project.

Award-winning architect, design correspondent, and best-selling author, Lira has contributed articles for some of Manila's major newspapers like the Philippine Daily Inquirer. She has appeared in News Radios in the USA and quoted in leading design and technology magazines in the Philippines, Malaysia, and the USA.
