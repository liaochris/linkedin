Glenn Barrowman’s Experience

  • International Business Development Manager

    Birmingham Science Park Aston

    (Non-Profit; Commercial Real Estate industry)

    August 2000 — Present (9 years 10 months)

    My role within this dynamic multi-functional organisation is suited to my broad previous experience.

    This role operates across key Birmingham BIC functional areas international activities; entrepreneurial activities to stimulate company formation and establishment; technology transfer and university linkages; and specific marketing and promotion.

    Birmingham BIC established in 1982, was one of the first generation Science Park's to be established in the UK. The park in physical property terms, comprising 400,000 sq ft of business space and 100 on site enterprises with approaching 1000 onsite employees. More than 500 companies have been incubated since inception.

    The challenge in my current role is to facilitate the transformation of a highly impressive incubator on a Science Park into an even more successful economic development vehicle possessing extensive international and university networks to achieve tangible company formation results.

    Specific tasks include:

    Delivery of the operating plan for Birmingham BIC and Birmingham Science Park Aston
    Facilitate the management and operation of Birmingham BIC and Birmingham Science Park Aston in a manner designed to maximise the linkages between the University and industrial partners.
    Promote and develop joint initiatives covering key functional areas designed to improve overall performance.
    Deputise for the Managing Director at internal, University and external forums.

  • Technology Park Manager

    Coventry University

    (Educational Institution; 1001-5000 employees; Higher Education industry)

    June 1997 — June 2000 (3 years 1 month)

    The role of the Technology Park Manager was complex. Requiring the project management focus to deliver a multi - use partnership physical works programme, property management experience to establish the estate management systems, promotional and marketing awareness to raise the profile of the Park, and legal knowledge to secure end users as tenants and to broker Joint Venture development agreements.

    Specific management tasks include the responsibility to:

    Prepare, develop and implement an operating plan for the Technology Park
    Secure rolling physical property development programme by direct development and joint ventures
    Manage and operate the Technology Park in a manner designed to maximise the potential linkages between the University and industrial partners.
    Deputise for the Managing Director at internal, University and external forums.

  • Senior Project Manager

    Scottish Enterprise (LEEL)
