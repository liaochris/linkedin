  • Chairman and CEO

    Pernod-Ricard Americas

    (Public Company; PER.P; Wine and Spirits industry)

    June 2008 — January 2009 (8 months)

    Led all of the operations of Pernod-Ricard from Canada to Chile, overseeing more than $ 3 Bn in revenues and 3,500 employees in 15 countries. Achieved record profits for the region despite economic adversity.
    Completed the acquisition and integration of the Absolut brand within affiliates, leading to dramatic sales acceleration in 09.

  • President and CEO

    Pernod-Ricard USA

    (Public Company; PER.P; Wine and Spirits industry)

    September 2005 — June 2008 (2 years 10 months)

    As President and CEO since 2005 of Pernod Ricard USA, the top profit contributor to the company’s 85 worldwide subsidiaries, I led the post-acquisition integration of the former Allied Domecq company, reaching $ 1.5 Bn in US revenues. During that time, Pernod Ricard USA spirits brands such as Chivas, Malibu, Stolichnaya, Wild Turkey and Jameson all saw significant growth, as did wines and champagnes brands such as Jacob’s Creek, Perrier Jouët and Mumm. Innovation was a key factor in this success, with the launch of new flavors, packaging, communication campaigns accross many brands and categories.
    I oversaw major restructuring and efficiency initiatives, consolidating personnel and manufacturing facilities accross North America.
    In January 2006, I became a member of the Group Executive Committee, the company’s senior management team overseeing the strategic and operational direction of all operations worldwide

  • COO/CFO

    Pernod-Ricard USA

    (Wine and Spirits industry)

    January 2002 — August 2005 (3 years 8 months)

    Following the transformative acquisition of Seagram, I provided the operating capabilities for the company's 6 fold revenue growth, making it one of the most successful and visible acquisitions in the industry. During that period of time, and on the heels of the US success, the parent's company stock went up by more than 100%.

  • COO/CFO

    Austin-Nichols/Pernod-Ricard USA

    (Wine and Spirits industry)

    1999 — 2002 (3 years )

    I started my Beverage career in 1999 with Pernod Ricard USA’s predecessor company, Austin-Nichols, then also a significant soft drinks player with iconic brands Yoo-hoo and Orangina. First CFO, then COO in 2000, I negotiated the sale of the non-alcoholic side of the business to Cadbury-Schweppes in 2001. On the heels of this divestiture, I became a key player in Pernod-Ricard’s challenging acquisition of the Seagram’s Company, in partnership with Diageo.
    In the 15 months it took for the FTC to approve the deal, I worked with senior US management of co-buyer and target, to ensure the eventual success with US authorities.

  • Country Manager/SVP

    Dollfus, Mieg and Company

    (Textiles industry)

    January 1995 — April 1999 (4 years 4 months)

    From 1995 to early 1999, I was the CFO, then Country Manager and Senior VP of DMC, Inc. a leading textile company with subsidiaries and divisions serving both retail (Walmart, Michael’s…) and fashion (Donna Karan, Levi’s…) clients. I led the sale and restructuring of several non core assets, and leveraged synergies among affiliates

  • VP Finance

    Bel Brands USA

    (Public Company; Consumer Goods industry)

    December 1987 — December 1994 (7 years 1 month)

    Between 1987 and 1995, I was VP Finance for Bel Brands USA, a global premium cheese company, with household names such as Babybel, The Laughing Cow or Boursin. In that role, I led the successful integration of the Nestlé dairy assets, and set the stage for the later acquisition of the Kaukona franchise.

  • VP Finance

    Allibert, Inc.

    (Management Consulting industry)

    July 1983 — November 1987 (4 years 5 months)

    As VP Finance for Allibert Inc., a supplier of decorative and furniture household items, I created the necessary environment enabling the company to successfully manage explosive growth in the US in the mid 80’s, including through the financing of a large public bond issue and the building of a very innovative manufacturing operations in North Carolina.

  • Senior Consultant

    Accenture

    (Public Company; ACN; Information Technology and Services industry)

    March 1982 — June 1983 (1 year 4 months)

    Provided management consulting services in the Banking and Insurance industry, as a junior member of Arthur Andersen Consulting, the predecessor company to Accenture.
