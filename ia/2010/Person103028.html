John Rosen’s Summary

For well over 15 years I have led the sales efforts in a variety of technology start ups. I have been successful selling into Enterprise Accounts with no brand, little market share, dismal collateral and Fortune 500 competitors. Often times, my success was critical, not only for the growth of the company but to it's continuance.

The ability to penetrate the Enterprise from different levels and the skill set to build rapport both high and low is what I've demonstrated throughout my career. Earning credibility quickly has been essential not only with prospects but from colleagues. Companies who court the Enterprise are looking for leaders who understand teamwork and do not require ego stroking, hand holding or the lime light. Business people who can identify business opportunities are held in high regard. Sales people who cannot maneuver through the labrynth of departments, agendas, personalities and challenges the Enterprise pose are not.

I firmly believe my experience in successfully competing head to head with several of the worlds largest and most respected sales organizations on limited resources makes me uniquely qualified for any challenge. It's easy to be successful working for the industry leader, especially in the Enterprise space where conservative decisions are the norm. It's quite another to achieve success by genuinely working harder and not taking anything for granted.
