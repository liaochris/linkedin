Jody Privette Young’s Summary

Jody Privette Young – Public Relations for Growing Business™

Jody has pioneered professional consumer electronics and technology public relations on the West Coast. Blending leading-edge social media techniques with intregrated communications and traditional media relations, she helps propel companies and products into the national spotlight.

Jody is a multi-skilled public relations professional with a uniquely strong blend of experience that spans from consumer to business and trade media relations. She has more than two decades of experience in strategic communications for companies and new products at the convergence of technology and entertainment.

Jody Privette Young’s Specialties:

Business Press Relations, Consumer Media Relations, Trade and Vertical Press Relations, Broadcast (TV and radio) Press Relations, Public Relations Strategy and Consulting

  • Public and Media Relations Executive

    Professional History

    (Privately Held; Public Relations and Communications industry)

    August 1988 — Present (22 years 5 months)

    Jody served as VP, corporate communications, SoftNet Corp., parent company to ISP Channel (one of the nation’s first cable broadband internet companys), Laptop Lane and Airzone (a past consortium of SoftNet, SoftBank, PCCW, Delta, Nokia and Cisco for bringing the internet n-flight and to airports). She directed an Internet and electronic commerce account group at Alexander Communications (now a division of Ogilvy) where she worked with such clients as Hewlett-Packard, InterTrust Technologies, NetGravity (turned DoubleClick), and Novell.

    Jody also served as director of PR and marketing communications at IMSI, where she managed PR for all six of the corporation’s international consumer and business-to-business software divisions. During her IMSI tenure, the firm expanded its product lines into many countries, selling into retail, OEM and online channels. Her efforts helped the company secure number-one market share in various software categories and grow to multi-million dollar capitalization.

    Her initial work as media relations and publicity manager was for Nintendo and Sega electronic games developers Absolute Entertainment and Mindscape (originally The Software Toolworks – any of you remember that one?). There she managed award-winning consumer software product launches for such educational and entertainment titles as Mavis Beacon Teaches Typing, The Miracle Piano Teaching System, ChessMaster 2000, Space Shuttle Project, Battlefield, Accuweather, and David Crane’s Amazing Tennis. Many of these products became bestsellers resulting from benchmark PR success that included the debut of the products on the Thanksgiving Day Good Morning America show (number one rated TV spot for influencing consumer’s holiday gift purchases), plus product features on key vertical and consumer magazine covers as the products first hit store shelves—in some instances resulting in complete retail sell out of the product within days of launch.
