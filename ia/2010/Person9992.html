Amy Braglia-Tarpey, MS, RD, CSR, CNSC’s Summary

I have worked in clinical and research nutrition since 1998, starting as a dietetic technician before obtaining the Registered Dietitian credential. As a dietetic technician, my roles were diverse and included experience in food service supervision, research, long-term sub-acute rehabilitation, and acute care. Since becoming a registered dietitian, I have worked with Chronic Kidney Disease (Stages 1-5) patients in both chronic ambulatory and acute care settings. Additionally, I consulted for research studies in renal nutrition.

My professional goal at this time is to become more involved in the realm of preventive nutrition care (primary, secondary, and tertiary). I am interested in continuing to work with Chronic Kidney Disease patients at least part-time. Other potential areas of interest include home infusion therapy, intra-dialytic and intra-peritoneal parenteral nutrition, and home enteral nutrition. I completed a leadership seminar offered by my employer in Fall of 2007, and plan to pursue opportunities that emphasize nutrition therapy expertise, leadership skills, independent work, and task variety.

The development of a new TPN Protocol was the task that drew me to take a clinical position at Salinas Valley Memorial Healthcare Systems. I created goals and guidelines for this endeavor based on current research, and successfully implemented the new TPN protocol. I also maintained a ten to twelve patient caseload on average daily, and participated in ICU, step-down unit, and medical/surgical floor multidisciplinary rounds. I attained Certified Nutrition Support Clinician (CNSC) credentialing during my tenure, and more recently achieved specialty certification in renal nutrition (CSR). Additionally, I continued to participate in education projects, including nursing education on renal nutrition, and volunteered as a mentor and educator for the Summer Health Institute, a program for high school students interested in pursuing a career in the medical field.
