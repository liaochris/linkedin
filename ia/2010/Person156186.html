Norma Beasant, PHR’s Summary

I am a Human Resources strategist hired repeatedly to start-up HR departments, grow the HR role of a company to that of a strategic business partner, turn-around under performing functions, break down barriers in diversity efforts, and inspire creative employee relations efforts. I have been in many roles of increasing responsibility but I am at my best when a business unit wants to become much more closely tied to the objectives of the company over the long term, or has a broken HR function/process where someone needs to uncover the root cause of the problem quickly and develop remedial best practices as a concentrated project.

My passion and enthusiasm for the Human Resources profession is evident as I continue my quest for excellence and to operate with a sense or urgency. I have been privileged to work with large reputable corporations with great resources, competent people, and to have had the best mentors throughout my HR career.

I am using social media; it is addictive and rewarding.

  • Lead HR Generalist

    Los Alamos National Laboratory

    (Government Agency; Research industry)

    February 2003 — October 2004 (1 year 9 months)

    I was a deployed Lead Generalist (located with the customer) and my team was responsible for the HR functions of 450 employees in the Business Division (CFO and Supply Chain Management groups). Day-to-day responsibilities included employee relations, performance management, training, compensation, staffing and recruiting and organizational development.

    When I first started this job, I was asked to partner with an external consultant to provide high-level support for a major reorganization that was taking place at the time and which anticipated significant people, processes and system changes. As a team, we evaluated current organization design, collected and analyzed data and provided observations to management to enhance organizational effectiveness. We used scenario planning to create new organizational design. Researched, developed and presented, through three white papers, strategies for culture change, employee retention aligning culture, people and processes with new business strategies and objectives. Also participated in workforce planning activities and metrics development.
