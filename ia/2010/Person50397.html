David Molina’s Summary

As the Founder/CEO of BilingualHire, a leading bilingual [Spanish+ English] talent solution and consulting company, I'm focused on building an integrated online platform that supports organizations diversify their staff and boards as well as provide tools for candidates to advance their careers. Formerly known as RED Consulting Group LLP, we partner with clients to develop their organizations to meet the needs of a changing U.S. demographic.

Previously I staffed the House Veterans and Emergency Services Committee for the 2009 Oregon Legislature regular session. In this role, I provided direct policy support to the Chair, and Committee Members. Prior to the legislature, I served an eighteen month U.S. Army tour at the Joint Personal Effects Depot at Aberdeen Proving Ground as a Summary Court Martial Officer and as the S-2/S-3 Officer in Charge.

Outside of work, I speak at several conferences and functions a year about leadership, college recruitment/retention of Chicano/Latino students, closing the Chicano/Latino high school student achievement gap, Chicano/Latino history and struggles, and community-building. (Contact me to discuss scheduling one for your group).

I am co-founder of the OSU Chicana/o Latina/o Alumni Association, serve at the pleasure of the governor to the Oregon Commission on Hispanic Affairs, and recently was appointed to the OSU President's Board of Visitors.

I have authored for El Hispanic News y Mas and the international publication, Mesoamerica. Currently, my work can be found at http://www.bilingualhireco.com (company) and http://molina.posterous.com/ (personal).

Originally founded in 1971 as the Commission on Chicano Affairs to address the needs and concerns of migrant worker, the Oregon Commission on Hispanic Affairs is authorized by statute to monitor existing programs and legislation, identify and research problem areas, maintain liaison between the Latino community and government entities, and encourage Latino representation on state boards and commissions.
