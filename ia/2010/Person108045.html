Julia Hurst’s Summary

Julia Nienaber Hurst has nearly twenty years state government experience, and more than a decade of successful experience as an executive manager and communications and research professional. “Ending Cervical Cancer in our Lifetime,” a national NLGA campaign under her management, was one of the top six association programs in the nation in 2007, and won an Addy award for design and content, and a Women in Government Presidential Award. Julia was Chief of Staff for four sessions in the Missouri House of Representatives, she lobbied in nearly 20-states and was Director of a Political Action Committee (PAC). She has been in association management for 10-years, including 2 years as Chief Operating Officer of The Council of State Governments (CSG). Julia Hurst has been Executive Director of the National Lieutenant Governors Association (NLGA) since 2002. Julia is experienced in every media. She was a radio news reporter, incl. time in a top-30 market, and was General Manager of a local cable television station. Julia served as a national, regional and local spokesperson for government leaders, for a telecommunications firm and a health insurer. As an author, she published a book "Images of Ft Wright," and also new articles in State News magazine and chapters in The Book of the States. She is a nationally Certified Meeting Professional (CMP) & serves on the Alumni Advisory Board of the Shool of Public Affairs at the Univ of Missouri. She is an alumnus of the American Council of Young Political Leaders (ACYPL) having traveled to the Philippines and co-hosted a delegation from India. In 2008, she completed a diplomatic mission to China. Her volunteer community roles include the Main Street program, a 9-1-1 board, school board, literacy tutor and an emergency preparedness spokesperson. She taught a college Computer Networking class. She served on her local pool board and was a member of the City of Fort Wright, Ky, Parks & Recreation Committee.
