Kris West’s Summary

At present I am owner and director of Widget Works Ltd., a small consulting company holding contracts with UK and US universities, JSTOR/Ithaka and a number of US based startups. Widget Works is currently involved in the development of the Networked Environment for Music Analysis (NEMA), which is an attempt to establish a secure and distributed Virtual Machine based environment for the execution of arbitrary DSP and Machine Learning softwares, provided by the user, against collections and datasets that cannot be shared or moved due to copyright restrictions. NEMA is intended to support an extensible set of machine and software architectures, diverse analysis or search tasks, advanced statistical analysis features for performance analysis and the sharing/dissemination of results.

Other recent projects include JSTOR Auction Catalogs (http://auctioncatalogs.jstor.org/), which involved the development of a community curation platform, data services system and advanced document analysis engine, which is able to interpret OCR documents and apply a document structure model to interpret and segment the catalogues into individual lots, sales and sections for deep indexing/search and browsing activities.

I've participated in the establishment and execution of an annual evaluation of Music Information Retrieval algorithms, known as the Music Information Retrieval Evaluation Exchange (MIREX). In particular I've helped to developed the evaluation of diverse audio analysis tasks including Genre classification, Artist identification, Classical Composer Identification, Onset detection, Drum transcription, Tempo detection, Musical Key detection, Mood classification and Content-based Audio Search and Retrieval.

I enjoy tackling both large-scale application development and research projects, particularly when working with a motivated and skilled team.
