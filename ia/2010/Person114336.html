Kendra Johnson, Ed.D.’s Summary

About Dr. Johnson

Kendra Johnson, Ed.D. is a recognized advocate for promoting college access for students with disabilities. After completing her master’s degree in vocational rehabilitation counseling she worked briefly in state government assisting persons with disabilities find gainful employment.

During her five years as a licensed professional counselor and certfied rehabilitation counselor, and after witnessing hundreds of persons with disabilities come up empty in their employment search, Kendra quickly recognized that she was working the wrong end of the equation. Helping individuals’ access higher education was the key to accessing employment, but students with disabilities were being locked out the door.

Kendra left state government and set out to make a difference in opening doors of access to higher education---working both as a university 504/ADA compliance officer and director of a college disability service office for over twelve years. Wanting to reach out even more, Kendra then co-authored the book 100 Things Every College Student with a Disability Ought to Know and founded her consulting company, Transition 2 College (formerlyTransition Game Educational Consultants) -- helping students with disabilities make a successful transition to college. She is a frequent speaker at regional and national conferences on the successful transition to college for students with disabilities and an invited presenter at high school college fairs for students with disabilities planning to attend – or thinking of attending college. In addition to her private consulting practice, she is the full time director of disability test accommodations for the Graduate Management Admission Council, owner of the GMAT exam.

Kendra Johnson, Ed.D.’s Specialties:

Transition to College Issues for Students with Disabilities; Disability Accommodations in High Stakes Testing (i.e. SAT, ACT. GMAT, LSAT, MCAT, GRE); study skills strategies for college students with disabilities; post-secondary education options for students with intellectual disabilities.
