Joe Russo’s Summary

Joe Russo is a National Grid Lead Economic Development Representative, based in Syracuse, NY. He has worked in economic and community development in New York State since 1978, with experience as a city, county, chamber of commerce and utility economic development manager.

Joe works with businesses and communities to generate investment and jobs that create wealth in upstate New York.

Joe is an active member of the NYS Economic Development Council (NYSEDC) and chairs its Marketing Committee. He is also Course Director and an instructor for the New York State Economic Development Course. Joe serves on the Utility Economic Development Association (UEDA) Board of Directors and Co-Chairs of the Member Services Committee. He also co-chaired the Program Committee for several years. He is a member of the Northeast Economic Development Association (NEDA), the International Economic Development Association (IEDC), CoreNet Global Real Estate Network and a Charter member of the Industrial Asset Management Council, where he serves on the marketing committee. Joe also serves on the boards of several local organizations and is a licensed real estate professional.

  • Director of External Marketing and Vice President of Economic Development

    Greater Syracuse Chamber of Commerce

    (Events Services industry)

    October 1986 — May 1993 (6 years 8 months)

    Aggressively marketed Greater Syracuse to attract wealth generating businesses to the area to create jobs and capital investment. Created regional marketing activities, initiated by the Chamber via COMCO Development Corp. Recruited a Seneca Wire flat wire manufacturing operation to a vacant building on East Molloy Road in DeWitt and a Zayre Corp. back office operation to downtown Syracuse. Subsequently facilitated an expansion of the Zayre operation when they were purchased by Ames, Inc. COMCO efforts produced a Montreal-based manufacturer for Oswego County. Evaluated sites for an in-city industrial/commercial park and selected a 20 acre, former construction and demolition landfill at the top of Brighton Avenue Hill. Conducted environmental and geotechnical studies and obtained financing for the creation of a 14 lot commercial subdivision. Managed construction of infrastructure and marketing of the park and sale of several parcels for development. Worked with the Onondaga County IDA to complete a study which led to the creation of the Clay Business Park, a Semi-NY site which was a finalist site in 2006 for a Bristol Meyers Squibb biologics, bulk pharmaceutical manufacturing plant. Managed the economic development and international trade activities of the Chamber, including the supervision of six economic development professionals, the Greater Syracuse Foreign Trade Zone, Ltd. and Greater Syracuse International Trade Council.. Completed the Brighton Hill Business Park project and sold several parcels, enabling Brighton Industrial Park Corp. to return $160,000 in equity investments to 16 investors in the Samuel Williams Business Center, the first business incubator in CNY. Managed the business expansion and attraction programs of the Chamber including revolving loan funds (regional and local), loan packaging for small business and the Samuel Williams Business Incubator Center. Served on the City of Syracuse Planning Commission, appointed by Mayor Thomas G. Young.
