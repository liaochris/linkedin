Yen Lee’s Summary

I have been helping travel and local businesses and consumers use Internet search to connect since 1996. Prior to starting UpTake, I co-founded CitySearch San Francisco, and most recently was General Manager for Yahoo Travel.

Over my last decade plus in travel, I observed that travelers who didn’t know what to book experienced a time-consuming and often frustrating process to filter unorganized information from multiple sources to decide where to go, what to do and where to stay. I founded UpTake as a travel-specific search engine to solve this problem—to help consumers make better and faster decisions about where to stay and what to do.

The average traveler completes 10-12 web searches and researches 20-24 sites before they decide what to book. We aggregate and analyze content from over 5,000 web sites to enable consumers to make better, faster decisions based on who they are traveling with (e.g. kid friendly hotels versus romantic hotels) and what they want to do (e.g. outdoor activities versus art museums). We return more useful content to facilitate decisions because our proprietary technology performs attribute and sentiment analysis to derive meaning from unstructured content like reviews, articles, descriptions etc.

Our leadership team includes the co-founders of CitySearch San Francisco, the former General Manager of Yahoo Travel, and CTO of Micro Focus. Our investors are Shasta Ventures and Trinity Ventures (e.g. BabyCenter, Blue Nile, Jamba Juice, LoopNet, Mint and P.F. Chang’s.)

I speak at Internet and Travel industry conferences, including Goldman Sachs Technology and Internet Conference, PhoCusWright, EyeforTravel and TIA events etc. and provide analysis and interviews for New York Times, Travel Weekly, Fortune, Bloomberg, and others on search, social media, e-commerce, and travel.
