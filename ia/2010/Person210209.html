Valerie Elston’s Summary

Valerie J. Elston is a Senior Account Executive in Levick’s Digital and Social Media practice. As one of the firm’s go-to counselors in the realms of digital and social media, she has
played a vital role in branding and bulletproofing countless Levick clients’ reputations online.

Her knowledge of the blogosphere and social media tools like Twitter, Facebook, and countless others has been put to use time and again when Levick clients demand greater positive visibility in cyberspace.

Prior to joining Levick, Valerie was Public Affairs Coordinator for the President's Emergency Plan for AIDS Relief (PEPFAR) at the U.S. Department of State where she managed such events as Ambassador Dybul's Swearing-In Ceremony and speaking tour. She also created key PEPFAR collaterals, including the monthly newsletter, fact sheets, and a teaching manual for high school teachers.

Valerie worked in Congress for Senator Lisa Murkowski (R-AK), redesigning the Senator’s website, among other communications responsibilities. It was at Penn State, where she earned her Masters of the Arts in Communication Arts and Science, that Valerie developed expert background on public speaking strategy. She presented her paper, "Ronald Reagan: Creating National Crisis out of Misjudgment," at the 2005 National Communication Association Conference.

In 2004, Valerie received an A.B.J in Public Relations and an A.B. in Speech Communication, Cum Laud with honors, from the University of Georgia. She was also a reporter for the university newspaper, The Red & Black.

Valerie serves as the President for the UGA Alumni Association's Washington, D.C. Chapter, and is a member of the Junior League of Washington. She volunteers for the Washington D.C. Chapter of the Crohn's and Colitis Foundation.
