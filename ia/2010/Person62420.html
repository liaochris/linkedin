Edwin J. Davila’s Summary

My humble beginnings in the wine industry were in the late 1990s as a server in the restaurant industry. It didn't take long for the "wine bug" to infect me with it's venom, and I was no longer satisfied with just serving wine. In the late 1990s, I began working for a winery in Virginia, where I devoured every piece of information and education regarding wine tossed my way. Initially, I operated tours and assisted in the tasting room. I conducted wine tastings and education for individuals and groups in the Williamsburg area. Through my work at the winery, I attended numerous wine festivals, events and related functions throughout the Mid-Atlantic region. In 1999, my insatiable appetite for wine knowledge took me to Bordeaux, where I spent a month meeting and picking the brains of phenomenal winemakers and winery owners at more than 25 chateaux.

Upon returning to the United States, I concluded that the only logical thing to do was to begin formal education in wine.

I have given private education and tastings . I have been a sommelier and wine consultant for restaurants and retail establishments. I am formally and self educated in the world of wine. This is a subject that I am very passionate about and obsessed with.

I have traveled to Croatia and Austria to visit and work with wineries that I chose to represent here in New York as well as provide assistance to wineries that wish to feature their wines in the United States.

I am on the tasting panel for Wine and Spirits Magazine

Edwin J. Davila’s Specialties:

Wine acquisition, wine education (public and private/corporate), wine list compilation, wine service and standards training, taste profiling, and an unequaled passion and obsession for the very essence that comprises this stigma wrapped in an enigma encased in a riddle.

Edwin J. Davila’s Education

  • Wine and Spirit Education Trust - International Wine Center

    Advanced Certificate with Merit , Wines and Spirits , 2006

    The Level 3 Advanced Certificate gives comprehensive coverage of wines and spirits, with an increased focus on tasting technique and on the application of the knowledge gained. Provides a core knowledge of the wide range of wines and spirits around the world to equip those in a supervisory capacity with the authority and confidence to make informed decisions in a wide variety of trade situations. The Advanced Certificate is the appropriate course for those who already have a broad foundation of wine knowledge--including wine labeling laws, appellation systems, major wine-producing regions, and grape types--and are experienced in tasting technique. It covers the major wine regions of the world in depth, and includes more detailed study of viticulture and vinification. This course enables students to describe the characteristics of the principal wines and spirits of the world and to explain the key factors influencing those characteristics.
