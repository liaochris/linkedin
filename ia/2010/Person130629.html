Mandip Samra’s Summary

I've been working in the public sector since 2001, where I got my start as an Adminstrative Intern for the City of Manhattan Beach. My desire to help people led me to study Public Administration at Syracuse University. The frigid temps, lack of a car and isolated living situation really made me ponder what I wanted to do with the rest of my life and alas, I decided 1- I wanted to continue my education, 2- I wanted to work for a City. After returning to California, I enrolled as a Planning student at USC and found a job doing transportation planning for the Port of Long Beach. Soon, I discovered my dislike for transportation planning (plans take too long to implement) and found a rewarding job as a Management Analyst for the City of Pasadena.I continued my education at USC and worked for Pasadena for 2.5 years, doing a little bit of everything for the Marketing/Customer Relations group at Pasadena Water and Power. Probably one of the best jobs I had, I left Pasadena to pursue my dream in urban planning/ real estate. Joining the private sector was a practical step in my career. I learned a great deal in a short time. Though the hours were long, I enjoyed the work. Unfortunately, I still found myself longing to rejoin the public sector. That is how I ended up becoming a Integrated Resources Planner for the City of Anaheim (Public Utilities). So far, I love my job and am thrilled at the challenges I face. The people are nice, the work is challenging and the commute is minimal (compared to living in LA).
