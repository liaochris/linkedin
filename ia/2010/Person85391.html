Tony Hodgson’s Summary

For 21 years, I've promoted business and engineering applications of computer technology. I'm currently Devel. Director for IT Ventures at the Houston Technology Center, and immediately prior, was Pres. of CAP5 – a computer networking and web applications firm. I'm also a Founder of InWorld Alliance – a member association promoting 3D-Web, Virtual World and Augmented Reality technologies for business. Earlier in my career, I was an engineer in the aerospace industry, grew and sold two software services firms and was the senior account manager to NASA for MCI, providing $100M in global data/voice/video connectivity to the space agency.

I have a background in systems engineering and 3D-modeling; helped create one of the first Internet portals for a State of Texas environmental project; orchestrated remote-sensing and pattern recognition projects; and assisted with environmental, technology and space commercialization programs. I've been privileged to serve in board member and advisor positions of several for-profit and 501(c)(3) organizations, including the South Shore Harbour Homeowners Association, the AIESEC Chapter at UofH (world's largest student organization), work to start the Sister Cities of Houston organization, several Boy Scouts of America troops and others.

I thrive anywhere technology and business goals converge - particularly, advanced web applications. I'm also keen on international business and cultural projects, A&M, Rice, UofH, radio and public speaking on information technology for business, and the power of Landmark Education to transform people's lives. Further, I hereby admit to abusing 'personal computers' for 'personal amusement' (irritating my tech guys who keep fixing them). We also have something in common if you are passionate about sustainable development, serious games, commercial space development, or have an opinion on where the coldest beer / warmest friends are on the Riviera Maya.
