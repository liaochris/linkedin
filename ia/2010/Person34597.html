C. Deniz Akyuz’s Education

  • Brown University

    Ph.D. , Physics , 1993 — 1999

    Completed dissertation entitled: "Resonant Tunneling Measurements of Size-Induced Strain Relaxation" under direction of Prof. Alex Zaslavsky. Studied effects of strain relaxation in submicron resonant tunneling diodes etched from strained materials due to expansion of mesa walls. Showed that resonant tunneling measurements provide a novel technique to probe strain in individual structures and that effects of size-induced strain relaxation must be considered in submicron semiconductor device designs based on strained materials. An interesting finding was that inhomogeneous-strain-induced potentials confine holes to quantum-dot or ring states. Confirmed results with finite-element calculations of strain distribution and numerical calculations of subband energy levels using Luttinger-Kohn Hamiltonian.

    BS , Physics , 1990 — 1993

    Completed thesis work on numerical study of two-dimensional dynamics of particles in nonlinear potential field. Member of the Sigma Pi Sigma honor society. Undergraduate research projects include development of Liquid Xe detector to be used in various national research labs, industrial toxic waste plasma-vitrification system, and design of adjustable plasma diverters for fusion chamber.
