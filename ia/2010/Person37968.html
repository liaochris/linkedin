Chris Jarvis’s Summary

Chris Jarvis is the owner and co-founder of Realized Worth, a consulting firm that connects companies with their communities through corporate volunteering and social media. Together, these elements give companies the power and relevance of action and dialogue; involvement and storytelling; the "walk and the talk.” The company blog, "Realizing Your Worth," (http://realizedworth.blogspot.com/) has a broad global following, providing support and insight to practitioners in the field.

Chris and his partner, Angela Parker, built Realized Worth on the power of social media alone. With a blog, a twitter account, and a facebook page, they grew a strong reputation as experts in the field of corporate volunteering. Chris regularly speaks at conferences on volunteering and social media, as well as offering training and consulting through Realized Worth.

Chris’ expertise comes from 20 years of working worked alongside NPO's across North and South America, Europe, Africa and Asia utilizing a model that elevates volunteerism from a task-oriented activity to a transformative experience.

Recently, Chris and Angela acted as advisers in the writing of “Mapping Success in Employee Volunteering: The Drivers of Effectiveness for Employee Volunteering and Giving Programs and Fortune 500 Performance” produced by Boston College and authored by Bea Boccalandro. They also act as expert writers on several websites including Volunteer Canada’s CorporateVolunteering.ca, Businessfightspoverty.com and 3blmedia.com. Chris is currently collaborating on a book that will be a comprehensive guide to Corporate Social Responsibility for HR managers, due out this October.

Chris is the proud father of four children, each of whom are rigorously working to transform him into a better dad.
