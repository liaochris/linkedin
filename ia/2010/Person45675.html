Cynthia Stephens’s Summary

As a communications strategist, I approach my job like a sleuth in a thriller. Is the problem internal or external? Is the solution to be found in marketing, advertising, public relations, or training? Based on sound research, I develop strategic communications plans and media to meet your business goals effectively and economically. Moreover, I am committed to finding solutions that provide timely evaluation and feedback from your clients and employees. For example, a Fortune 500 appliance corporation’s tactical goal was to outsell its competition by compressing sales training in order to launch products ahead of its competitors. Delivering worldwide training in a business TV environment would enable them to meet their goal. Their success hinged on our developing (1) a marketing concept around which to build programming, (2) an instructional design, (3) their personnel for the BTV environment (4) pre-produced media segments, (5) a live TV format, (6) an internal PR campaign to turn negative attitudes around, (7) a method for interfacing multiple communications technologies, (8) a method to deliver training across diverse time zones simultaneously, and (9) a way to evaluate the effectiveness of this kind of training. The sales team greeted the new approach enthusiastically and ultimately generated millions in new business.
