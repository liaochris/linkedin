Scott Killingsworth’s Summary

Scott Killingsworth is a business lawyer focused on high-technology and corporate matters. He has also represented public and private companies in fields as diverse as manufacturing, financial services, logistics and distribution, and healthcare.

Mr. Killingsworth is one of only 113 lawyers nationwide named to the 2007 BTI Client Service All-Star Team; in 2008 he became one of only 17 lawyers nationwide, and the only one in the Southeast, to receive this recognition two years in a row. (The All-Star honorees are attorneys who are singled out for superior client service, on an unprompted basis, by one or more Fortune 1000 clients, in an independent survey by the BTI Consulting Group.)

Mr. Killingsworth counsels on corporate governance and strategy formulation; regulatory compliance and corporate compliance program design and implementation; commercial transactions; mergers and acquisitions; software and intellectual property licensing and protection; strategic alliances both with customers and with owners of complementary technology; government contracting, including both military and civilian procurement; domestic and international distribution systems (including export licensing issues); OEM and VAR relationships; and custom development and consulting contracts.

A frequent speaker and author, he has lectured at Yale, Georgetown, Georgia State and Thomas Cooley law schools and at legal seminars throughout the U.S., and has published in the Emory Law Journal, the Journal of Intellectual Property Law, Computer Lawyer, Journal of Internet Law, E-Commerce Law Report, Technology Transactions, E-Commerce Law and Strategy and International Business Lawyer. His published work has been cited in law reviews from Harvard, Stanford, Berkeley, Vanderbilt, Washington University, Fordham, the University of Georgia, the University of Illinois, by the Supreme Court of Canada and in several books on licensing, internet law, data security and privacy law.
