Jeff Drust (jeff_drust@hotmail.com)’s Summary

I help startups and early stage companies that have new approaches to solving significant problems and occasionally work with young companies not performing to expectations (please see 3rd party recommendations below).

I'm especially interested in helping commercialize new ideas that increase the business efficiency of all types of design and engineering using computers and try to be flexible to suit the stage companies are at - often working for cash or stock or a mixture as an advisor or consultant or interim manager (e.g. until a particular role is large enough to recruit a fulltime person). If necessary I can pull in the right talent from a wide ranging network of solid relationships built by living and working on four continents - Europe, Africa, Asia and N. America.

I can work in, or advise on, many roles based on over 30 years experience in leading public and private companies in many positions including CEO/MD, VP Marketing, VP Sales, VP Channels, VP Business Development, VP eBusiness at companies such as Autodesk, Lattice3D and Datech. For example: helped build Datech from startup to the leading UK CAD distributor and then sold it as CEO; led the team that created, launched and proved the Autodesk Subscription Program which, in recent years, contributed most of Autodesk's growth (in 2009 it was ~40% of total revenue, to the credit of that and succeeding teams).

My formal studies were at Stanford University, University of Natal and the Institute of Directors in London and professionally I'm a Chartered Engineer, Chartered IT Professional, BCS Fellow and ICE Fellow.

Currently involved in multiple advisory, consulting and interim management capacities in startups and existing businesses, performing:
• Strategy and business model innovation and validation,
• Business development of partnerships to implement them,
• Market validation, market research and marketing,
• Milestone planning and change management to ensure detailed execution.
