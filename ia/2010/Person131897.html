Marc Thomas King’s Summary

20 years of experience in the entertainment business, 12 1/2 spent at HBO Films.

Completed my professional baseball career playing in Italy in 1987 and my entertainment career kicked off in San Francisco as a commercial actor in 1988. After joining the Screen Actors Guild, I moved to Los Angeles in 1990.

While completing my degree at USC (Magna Cum Laude, Theater, Play Writing), I worked as a casting intern at Disney/Hollywood Pictures, then to Lucas Films as an apprentice editor. After graduation, I spent 3 years at ABC assisting an award winning executive producer, which led to HBO Films in 1995, where I worked in development, production, on-air, marketing, operations, and international/domestic film/television festival outreach.

Prior to 1988, I played professional baseball for the Montreal Expos farm system and in Europe for the Italian Baseball Federation. In the mid-80's I played division 1 baseball at UCSB (lost to Stanford in 1983 in the regional final for a chance to go to the College World Series). I also played at Cal Poly San Luis Obispo. My baseball also took me to Alaska's elite summer semi-pro league. In 2004 at the age of 40, I was a finalist for the Greek National Baseball team representing Greece in the 2004 Olympics. I'm currently developing multiple film projects and teaching the art of hitting to area baseball players of all ages, focusing on mental preparation for hitters. I'm currently writing a book for Designated Hitters.

During my 12 1/2 years at Home Box Office (from 1995 to 2008), HBO Pictures / HBO Films won "best movie" Emmys an unprecedented 12 out of 13 years, won several Academy Awards, countless other Emmy Awards, Golden Globes, Sundance and Cannes Film Festival top jury prizes.

I was Coordinator of Operations, HBO Films, 2002 to 2008.

On Staff during the development, production and marketing of well over 80 movies, many of which were critically acclaimed and award winning.
