Krish Ramakrishnan’s Summary

Mr. Ramakrishnan is presently the general manager for the Server Virtualization business unit at Cisco Systems. The business unit is responsible for architecting datacenter virtualization and automation solution. The flagship product VFRAME is one of the centerpieces of Cisco’s datacenter 3.0 strategies. In addition to datacenter automation, Mr. Ramakrishnan was responsible for low latency server fabric solutions based on the InfiniBand technology. Mr. Ramakrishnan is leading Cisco’s effort in the desktop virtualization and other allied fields.

Mr. Ramakrishnan was the CEO of Topspin Communications from 2001 to 2005.
Under his stewardship, company re-invented itself as a datacenter virtualization player, capitalizing on the trend of Scale-Out computing. Mr. Ramakrishnan executed several strategic partnerships with the likes of IBM, DELL, HP and SUN.
He developed the executive management team and the Go To Market strategy for the company. Topspin had successive quarters of revenue growth. Cisco Systems acquired the company in 2005.

From 1995 to 2001, Mr. Ramakrishnan was responsible for developing content networking strategies and products for Cisco Systems. He was appointed as vice president and general manager for the Content Business Unit. Under his leadership, Cisco developed many industry-leading products including L4-L7 load balancers, content caches, CDN and micro-webservers.

Mr. Ramakrishnan was the co-founder of Internet Junction where he served as it’s President. Cisco Systems acquired Internet Junction in 1995. Internet Junction developed products to connect Netware networks to the Internet.

Mr. Ramakrishnan has worked for NET, PeerLogic, Bell Labs and Perkin-Elmer.

He has MSCS from Monmouth University and BS (Physics) from Madras University, India.
