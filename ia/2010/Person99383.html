John Morris’s Summary

In February 2009, John left a position with technology leader Hewlett-Packard where he worked for 13 years in the technical training function. At HP, John began by creating end-user training for an SAP project. Promoted to Engineer Training, John led entry-level and mastery-level training for thermal inkjet engineers designing inkjet printers. His last position as Learning Program Manager in HP's Center of Expertise for Technical Development -- gave him training responsibility for global supply chain, business process management, innovation, problem solving and decision making.

Following a four month experiment at bringing change in State Government as a Transformation Coach with the Oregon Department of Human Services John learned his style is not compatible with the state bureaucracy. While working at DHS, John also started teaching two sections of Strategic Management for Oregon State University as an Instructor. With a renewed 9-month contract, John plans to devote more time to classroom activities. He joined the graduate faculty as well to be an adviser for an MBA project.

John's background includes a broad range of experiences in Human Resources, working more than 28 years in high tech, health care, insurance, wholesale, retail and manufacturing. He has worked as an independent consultant and was educated as an MBA. He is active in his community promoting bicycle safety, arts and high school athletics. John is the proud father of two adult children -- Jessie who works in marketing for Microsoft and Scott who is living in the Kansas City area.

John Morris’s Specialties:

Project management, training, organization development, strategic planning, survey design, recruiting, competency development, employee relations, human resource planning, generalist and others. Experience with Supply Chain, Innovation (using TRIZ) and Problem Solving, Collaboration, Knowledge Management, Decision Management and Business Process Management.
