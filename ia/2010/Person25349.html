Robert Cowan’s Experience

  • Research Engineer

    Energy and Environmental Research Center

    (Educational Institution; Research industry)

    January 2010 — Present (7 months)

    Dr. Robert M. Cowan is a Research Engineer at the EERC, where his work focuses on addressing the issue of global climate change through carbon capture and storage (CCS), including developing novel capture technologies, designing equipment and conducting experiments, and assisting with the development of programs to minimize and treat water used or produced during CCS activities. Prior to his position at the EERC, Dr. Cowan served as Laboratory Director and Senior Scientist at Carbozyme, Inc., in Monmouth Junction, New Jersey. He holds a Ph.D. degree in Civil (Environmental) Engineering, an M.S. degree in Chemical (Biochemical) Engineering, and a B.S. degree in Chemical Engineering from the State University of New York at Buffalo.

    Dr. Cowan’s principal areas of interest and expertise include separations science, industrial wastewater treatment, air pollution control, carbon dioxide capture, and bioremediation.
