Sushi Suzuki’s Summary

Instructor, Researcher, Practitioner, and Student of Design. I enjoy working on diverse design challenges both technical and social, making sense of the world from different perspectives, and synthesizing new design ideas of products and services.

Sushi Suzuki’s Specialties:

Design Methodology, Experience Prototyping, Product Design, Needfinding, Technology Monitoring, Team Management, CAD, Mechatronics, Control Systems, Graphic Design, Digital and Analog Photography, Video Editing, Fluent in Japanese and English

Sushi Suzuki’s Experience

  • Guest Lecturer

    ENPC School of International Management

    (Design industry)

    2010 — Present (less than a year)

  • Co-Founder of d.thinking Paris

    ENPC

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    October 2009 — Present (10 months)

    In my most recent adventure, I am starting a new design program in Paris where students from all walks of life come together to experience design and innovation first hand. In our first year, we are coordinating several global projects with Stanford University and Aalto University in Finland, running another one semester course focused on design for the neglected and design for the sustainable future, and hosting several shorter workshops for different schools. Our goal is to become the platform for Design Thinking activity in Paris.

  • Executive Director, ME310: Design Innovation

    Stanford University

    (Educational Institution; Research industry)

    January 2008 — September 2009 (1 year 9 months)

    In my return to ME310 (see below) as the Executive Director, I acquired corporate partners as student project sponsors and raised more than 1.6 million dollars over two years to support the course. I also coordinated the academic partnerships with other universities and regularly interfaced with professors and instructors from Germany, Finland, Switzerland, France, Mexico, and Colombia. As an ambassador of the Stanford-IDEO design methodology, I gave presentations and ran workshops to both academic and corporate audiences in the US and abroad. For more information about the course, visit http://me310.stanford.edu/.

  • Systems Engineer

    DaimlerChrysler Research and Technology North America Inc.

    (Public Company; DCX; Automotive industry)

    September 2006 — July 2007 (11 months)

    As a member of the Connected Infotainment and Consumer Electronics team, I researched and presented new products, services, and technologies and their implications on the automotive market. We were responsible for conceptualizing future automotive architecture and services based on current trends and future forecasts, and I supported the team by prototyping new user experiences in the automobile pertaining to audio video and consumer electronics integration. I was also the main HMI designer for the prototypes in our team.

  • Course Assistant, ME310: Design Innovation

    Stanford University

    (Educational Institution; Research industry)

    June 2005 — October 2006 (1 year 5 months)

    ME310 is a graduate level project course at Stanford University where students undertake product innovation challenges presented by global corporations. As a course assistant, my task was to support the student teams by providing the right resources for design, prototyping, and documentation, coach the student teams through the Stanford-IDEO design process, and organize a community of students, alumni, faculty, and industry coaches.

  • Product Design Intern

    Synaptics

    (Public Company; SYNA; Consumer Electronics industry)

    June 2005 — September 2005 (4 months)

    I was part of the Concept Prototyping Team at Synaptics, responsible for creating new interface experiences using Synaptics technologies. My individual project for the internship was to design an innovative controller for the next generation video game consoles. At the end of the internship, I delivered a form and function prototype of a simplified motion based controller for novice users, several months before the Nintendo Wii controller was announced. The design process included market trend identification and extensive user studies to develop insights on emerging customer needs. I also helped the team develop new concepts for wireless keyboards, portable media players, mobile phones, and automotive stereos.

  • Research Assistant

    Mechatronics and Haptics Interfacing Lab, Mechanical Engineering Dept., Rice University

    (Research industry)

    May 2002 — May 2004 (2 years 1 month)

    As an undergraduate research assistant, I supported the graduate student researchers by designing, fabricating, and maintaining mechatronic and haptic devices to run experiments. During my first summer, my lab partner and I created a fully operable two degrees of freedom haptic device that met the requirements of the professor. In my two years, I worked on both the mechanical system and the software controller. In addition, I developed some laboratory assignments for the Dynamic Systems course.

  • Assistant Photo Editor/Photographer

    The Rice Thresher, student newspaper of Rice University

    (Newspapers industry)

    September 2001 — May 2004 (2 years 9 months)

    As the assistant photo editor, I was responsible for managing the staff photographers and delivering the photographs requested by the different sections (e.g. News, Sports, Entertainment, etc.). During my tenure, I helped manage the newspaper transition from film to digital, fundamentally shifting the way photographs were taken, processed, selected, and delivered.

  • Volunteer English Teacher

    World Teach, Harvard Center for International Development

    (International Trade and Development industry)

    June 2001 — August 2001 (3 months)

    Summer after my freshman year in college, I taught English in a rural town in China to children aged six to fifteen. Through this experience, I wanted to better understand the culture and mentality of the fastest growing nation in the world. I also acted as a conversation partner to the adults in the community.

  • Web Developer and Founding Member

    i-kimono.com

    (Apparel & Fashion industry)

    May 2000 — September 2000 (5 months)

    I was one of the founding members for a new start-up company in Kyoto that sold antique kimono (traditional Japanese garments) and accessories online. My responsibilities included developing the i-kimono.com website and the internal inventory database. I also helped develop the company workflow process from inventory acquisition to auction listing and shipping. As the sole English speaker in the company, I handled all the non-Japanese customer relations.
