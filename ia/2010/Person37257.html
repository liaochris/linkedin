Chong Fang’s Experience

  • Postdoctoral Researcher

    UC Berkeley

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    June 2007 — Present (2 years 10 months)

    • Studied the fluorescence mechanism of green fluorescent proteins.
    • Developed, maintained and utilized Femtosecond Stimulated Raman spectroscopy, a novel structural technique having simultaneously high time and spatial (atomistic) resolution.
    • Discovered the intrinsic chemical reaction mechanism for GFP's efficient fluorescence to be a skeletal wagging motion gating the biologically-significant excited-state proton transfer.
    • Wrote and published the major research finding as a Nature Cover Article with a Coverline "Inside Green Fluorescent Protein: The vibration that powers the classic gene expression marker" in Nov 2009.
    • This work has been reported by numerous news agencies and institutions, including C&EN, Nature Podcast, Science Daily, PhysOrg, Chemistry World, Bioscience Technology, Life Sciences World, Softpedia, Bionity, Biotechniques, Spectroscopy Europe, USTC, University of California, and LBNL, etc.

  • Postdoctoral Fellow

    University of Pennsylvania

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    September 2006 — May 2007 (9 months)

    • Investigated the unusual potency of a novel anti-HIV/AIDS diarylpyrimidine-derivative drug TMC278 (Rilpivirine, NNRTI) with a novel ultrafast structural technique, two-dimensional infrared spectroscopy.
    • For the first time, obtained the dynamic structural snapshots of the drug-protein complex in action
    • Wrote and published the research paper that the molecular flexibility is key to the drug's success in the Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences (PNAS) with a Coverline "Flexible HIV inhibitor" in Feb 2008.
    • Collaborated with CABM-Center for Advanced Biotechnology and Medicine at Rutgers University.
