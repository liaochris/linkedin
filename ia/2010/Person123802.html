Len Bailey’s Summary

A native New Englander, I joined the U.S. Air Force in 1991 and spent the next ten years fixing satellite, wideband, and combat communications systems.

Then, when a family emergency led me to leave the service, I spent a few years in the Quality Control and Environmental, Health & Safety fields.

Life was pretty good, but I eventually decided to shift gears to become a freelance writer. And it's been downhill ever since.

I cut my teeth writing copy and marketing collateral for local businesses, then signed on full-time as a copywriter with Littleton Coin Company. And since March 2008, I've worked directly for America's highest-paid copywriter, Clayton Makepeace.

My copy has appeared in magazines, catalogs, direct mail, and all over the 'Net. I've written everything from text ads and catalog copy to sales pages and advertorials. And I've brought my clients millions of dollars in products and services -- along with hundreds of thousands of qualified leads.
