John Humphrey’s Summary

John Humphrey [john@johnhumphrey.co.nz] : 8,800+ connections

Over the past two decades I have earned a reputation for repeatedly identifying and implementing innovative telecommunications technologies.

I consult to senior executives on projects encompassing software, IT, broadcasting, satellite, fibre, wireless, and undersea cables. My clients have included Tomizone, Optus, IPSTAR, Telecom NZ, Thaicom, Ericsson, Nextlink, Auckland International Airport, SOMA Networks and Freightways.

A mix of consulting, corporate and entrepreneurial experience means that I have developed an unusual blend of skills. My expertise bridges strategy, product development, business development, and operations. I am also fluent in the crucial legal and financial considerations that accompany the implementation of new telecommunications technologies.

A high energy environment is my strong preference, but I work well with all types of people and enjoy bringing together and leading new teams of people for specific projects.

  • Director

    IPSTAR New Zealand Ltd

    (Telecommunications industry)

    2002 — 2006 (4 years )

    Headed the regulatory, marketing and business development activities in New Zealand for the IPSTAR pan-Asian high-speed Internet satellite network for over five years. Formed and was Director of IPSTAR New Zealand Ltd. Project managed the construction of a large satellite gateway in Auckland to provide nationwide two-way broadband satellite services, outsourced the operation of the gateway to a local teleport operator. Initiated the market entry and undertook the service roll-out in New Zealand, hired a team, increased sales. Extensive liaison and reporting to Thailand throughout, ensuring strong support, product development and services were available to New Zealand partners and customers.

  • Founder; CEO; Director Project Development

    Nava Networks, Bermuda

    (Telecommunications industry)

    1999 — 2002 (3 years )

    The Nava project involved building a 9,500 km submarine and terrestrial cable network from Singapore to Sydney. Developed the initial concept and raised seed capital from angel investors, as well as built the initial management team. Negotiated and signed term sheet with a New York venture capital firm for a total investment of USD 650 million. Led the project as CEO until late 2000, then worked primarily on raising finance, legal and planning issues through 2001/2. The venture fell victim to the global collapse of the telecommunications financial markets in 2002.

  • Country Manager, New Zealand

Worked with TVNZ on its DTH plans; assisted World TV to develop its multi-channel ethnic pay-TV service. Achieved four years of Optus Sales Pacesetter status (> 100% revenue target).
