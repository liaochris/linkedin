Garland Harwood’s Summary

Garland Harwood is a group manager with Weber Shandwick’s technology practice in New York with more than seven years of experience working with top consumer and technology brands including, Amazon.com, Samsung, Honeywell, Amdocs and Verizon FiOS.

As Weber Shandwick's lead manager for Amazon.com, Garland oversees strategy and counsel, program development and delivery, team resource allocation, as well as benchmarking and measurement for the account. He leads the aggressive media relations team that secures regular national broadcast segments, multiple monthly long lead placements and inclusion for Amazon executives in high profile consumer buying trend stories.

Garland’s recent work with Samsung has especially helped the company to raise its visibility in national consumer broadcast segments. Garland also manages thought leadership programs for Amdocs that have garnered coverage in top tier media and wireless trades.

Previously, Garland was at Maloney & Fox, where he spent four years working with Microsoft’s Online Services Group (MSN & Windows Live), including promoting the launches of MSN Music and several of Microsoft's major media and nonprofit partnerships, such as Live Earth and Windows Live Messenger's i'm Initiative. Garland also led media efforts for many of the agency’s other tech clients, including, eMusic, Cablevision’s Optimum Online, RCA, PureDepth and Microsoft’s Education Team.

Prior to joining M&F, Garland led grassroots campaign efforts with college students for numerous congressional and gubernatorial races in the Southeast during the 2002 election cycle.

Garland is the founder of Comedy for Cancer in Brooklyn, an annual fundraiser for cancer services in Brooklyn, which has been featured in Newsweek and on Good Day New York. He has consulted for many nonprofit organizations on media relations and fundraising events, including the American Cancer Society of Brooklyn and World Vision of Greater New York.
