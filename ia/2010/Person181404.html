Sam Simon’s Summary

A lawyer and innovator in brining together unlikely allies to form coalitions and find win-win solutions to public policy issues. A member of many boards, inlcluding being Chairman of the National Consumers League; Secretary of the World Institute on Disability.

Sam Simon’s Specialties:

Stakeholder management; issue management, blogging relations, campaign management

Sam Simon’s Experience

  • Fellow

    Intersections International

    (Civic & Social Organization industry)

    April 2008 — Present (1 year 11 months)

    A multi-faith, multi-cultural inititiave seeking to forge common ground for justice reconciliation and peace in the world. An initiative of the Collegiate Church of New York. I am a fellow focusing on the intersections of Power and Values to create a national conversation on the Values of reconciliation, peace and justice in the Economic, Political and Cultural fabrics of our society

  • Chairman

    Amplify Public Affairs LLC

    (Non-Profit; 11-50 employees; Government Relations industry)

    January 2008 — Present (2 years 2 months)

    Amplify Public Affairs, LLC is a public affairs firm formed by merging Issue Dynamics, Inc. resources with those of a a health and healthIT consulting firm.

    Amplify will build on IDI’s accomplishments of securing policy victories to trailblazing in online advocacy. Accomplishments include a successful strategic alliance campaign for a large, multi-national pharmaceutical company which contributed to an expansion of national Medicare coverage. Amplify will continue to innovate in Web 2.0 consulting for clients.

    Most recently, the IDI Internet team was honored with a Webby Award for their work on the International Federation of Animal Welfare (IFAW) Animal Rescue Blog, as well as a Silver Horizon Interactive Award for the Center for Community Change’s Fair Immigration Reform Movement site.

  • Captain

    US Army Judge Advocate Corps

    (Government Agency; Military industry)

    May 1971 — March 1975 (3 years 11 months)

    I served four years in the Army Judge Advocate Corp. For two years I was in Fort Lee in Petersburg, Virginia serving with the Staff Judge Advocate. I spent two years in Northern Virginia serving with the Chief Trial Attorney, working on Government contracts.

    While in the Army I wrote an article in Nation magazine exposing the so-called "drug amnesty program" as a gross trick on many soldiers. The Nixon administration touted this as a way for soldiers using drugs to fess up and get help without being prosecuted. They did not bother to say that the Army would attempt to discharge the soldier administratively under less than honorable conditions.
