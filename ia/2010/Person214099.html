Courtenay Wilson’s Experience

  • Arts and Culture Program Officer

    William Penn Foundation

    (Philanthropy industry)

    November 2001 — Present (8 years 6 months)

    Responsible to the Arts and Culture Program Director for evaluation, analysis, funding recommendations and monitoring of grants to arts and culture programs and organizations in Philadelphia and the five surrounding counties. Responsible for approximately one half of a $12 million annual grants budget. Major projects include: Pennsylvania Performing Arts on Tour (PennPAT); the Pennsylvania Cultural Data Project (PACDP); the Tessitura Consortium at the Theatre Alliance of Greater Philadelphia; and the Arts for Youth Initiative, a ten year plan to make learning in and though the arts available to every child in the Greater Philadelphia Region by 2020.

  • Senior Program Officer

    Southern Arts Federation

    (Arts and Crafts industry)

    August 1999 — May 2001 (1 year 10 months)

    Responsible to the Executive Director for the planning, development, financial, and human resources management of SAF’s programs and services, including: Professional Development and Arts Education; Performing Arts; Literary, Visual and Media Arts; Traditional Arts and ADA; and Information Technology. Accomplishments include: the NEA Regional Partnership Grant that was successfully funded for three years; developed and executed the first substantial SAE Corporate Sponsorship program; coordinated the development with Carnegie Mellon University of eSeminarts; developed business and marketing plans for SAF’s professional development, conference, and traveling exhibit programs; wrote SAF’s technology policy and oversaw development of a technology blueprint.

  • Executive Director, The Grand Opera House

    Mercer University

    (Educational Institution; 1001-5000 employees; Higher Education industry)

    September 1995 — June 1999 (3 years 10 months)

    Responsibilities included: developing new programs and partnerships to support the arts at Mercer; developing long and short term facility plans for The Grand Opera House; expanding the quantity and quality of professional presentations at the Grand; identifying and developing earned and contributed income sources; and developing programs to integrate the arts into the region’s educational agenda. Accomplishments include: established an Arts Administration office within University Advancement; created the Family Series, the Mercer Youth Audience Initiative, and the Georgia International Festival for Young Audiences; doubled the income and attendance of the Broadway Series; developed a summer arts camp for Mercer’s Music, Theatre and Art Departments; initiated over $2 million of capital improvements; elected founding president of the Georgia League of Historic Theatres.

  • Deputy City Representative, Office of Arts and Culture

    City of Philadelphia

    (Government Agency; 10,001 or more employees; Government Administration industry)

    June 1994 — August 1995 (1 year 3 months)

    Responsible to the First Deputy City Representative for developing special projects with City support. Projects included: development of a city-wide information network utilizing a system of kiosks and the Internet, to promote the cultural, historic, and culinary sites and activities of the region; initiated the development of a city-wide arts festival; development of “pocket-parks” and public art projects.

  • Managing Director

    Wilma Theatre

    (Performing Arts industry)

    August 1985 — August 1993 (8 years 1 month)

    Responsible for financial planning, monitoring and reporting; organizational development; and staffing. With the Artistic/Producing Directors designed and implemented the organizational long-range plan. Worked with Board Committees on fundraising, financial management, and board and staff development. Responsible for all aspects of the $7.2 million Capital Campaign. Negotiated all contracts with artists, unions and co-producing companies for original productions, and for co-productions with: The American National Theater, Washington, D.C.; the Joyce Theater, NYC; Roger Stevens and the John F. Kennedy Center for the Performing Arts, Washington, D.C.; Joseph Papp's New York Shakespeare Festival, NYC; and WNET TV 13, NYC. Managed operating budget, overseeing growth of 700% from $200,000 to $1,400,000, and staff growth from 5 to 15 full-time employees.
