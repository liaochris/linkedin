Paul Irvine’s Summary

Experienced and well rounded software development leader, with applications development, data center operations, consulting, enterprise architecture, project management, data modeling, database design and development, and software development background. Some common themes are team building, startups, web commerce and web integration with legacy.

At pCapacity, I am leveraging my own direct need and experience in managing dynamic software engineering teams, both onshore and offshore. pCapacity provides the basic toolset to give engineering leaders a quantitative analysis of their development capacity. This directly facilitates technology and business alignment, and helps set achievable management team expectations. I also provide customers the consulting services necessary to help develop a business framework and model to excel at managing software project portfolios, delivery expectations, and creating an environment where development teams can be successful.

Services offered:-
* Executive level systems architecture and strategy consulting services
* Successful Project Portfolio management
* Process and Communications at management levels between Business and Technology
* Interim CTO or CTO / VP Engineering coaching & Mentoring
* Systems architecture and integration strategies, especially legacy and web
* Custom Software design and development – RIA/Flex, open source solutions
* Excel (VBA) web service integration in dashboard / presentation models
* Database modeling and design - Oracle, MySQL, SQLServer
* Data Center planning, virtualization design, network design and SAS70 management

Other products under development are:-
http://www.bcviewer.com - a multi-project visualization tool for Basecamp to aid engineering managers with a full portfolio and an agile project management style.

http://www.ReachCRM.com - all the functionality you need to manage small/medium business sales force activities, and at a price that is affordable.

Paul Irvine’s Specialties:

Specialty in mid-range engineering resource capacity planning - getting this right is crucial to business success. It can be done by numbers, collaboratively, and measured; it needn't be a guessing game.
