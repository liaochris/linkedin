Vincent Lowe’s Summary

From his experience as a training developer and facilitator, Vincent co-founded the non-profit foundation Transformation of Education. Vincent manages a division called Schoolhouse Earth. Its mission is to facilitate education any place there is a gap. It accomplishes this with delivery systems (like the Rapid Response Learning Center), curriculum (like its Gold Star Workshop Series), and media products (like How Far Jafar).

In his career as an educator, spanning more than 15 years, Vincent taught technology (such as Java, Python, Wireless Networking, Communication and Leadership, Perl, C Programming, and Operating System Internals) at Sun Microsystems, Cisco, Apple, SCO as well as many other high tech corporate clients.

He is the author of "Perl Programmer's Interactive Workbook" from Prentice Hall as well as numerous course guides that are used at various technology companies. He has developed ad-hoc training and advocacy presentations for emerging technology beginning with the onset of the Internet Age.

He is also a Toastmaster in Santa Clara, CA and has served as a Toastmaster Area Governor. He is currently a Toastmaster ACS/CL. (Advanced Communicator Silver, and Competent Leader respectively)

When he's not in the classroom, or attending to other duties for his practice, Vincent is often found in the ballpark at Willie Mays Plaza which he considers an annex to his office.
