Matt Heindl’s Summary

Matt began his career at a media company in Stockholm, Sweden creating online communities for European MBAs, then moved back to the US to head marketing at Kozmo.com where he was thrust into the world of dot-com parties, ad operations and bike messengers. Matt was dubbed by New York Magazine as a “future internet mind to watch.”

In 2000 Matt joined Alltrue.com as the head of online marketing where he made Alltrue.com the first video-centric broadband site to hit more than 2 million unique visitors per month by creating an “affiliate network” of video sites, bulletin boards and “web logs”… as well as tapping a new networking phenom called Friendster.com (boy have things changed!). He built a distribution and content creation network by placing messages where people regularly consumed and socialized online.

From there, Heindl joined Heavy.com in 2003 as the head of marketing where he was key in establishing Heavy’s prominence in the video entertainment sector. He headed all online marketing efforts and the development of raw online “viral” talent and has had a hand in several notable and groundbreaking online social media and UGC campaigns for clients such as Reebok, Comedy Central, Puma, Diesel and Burger King (the masks!)… Becoming an expert in using blog outreach and MySpace as vehicles for engagement, driving traffic and increased ROI.

In 2008 Matt joined Ripe Digital to promote branded content pieces and build social platforms for the likes of Sony, Subaru, Dodge and the National Guard.

Most recently, as Director of Digital Strategy at Virtue Worldwide, he used his social media and marketing chops to promote viral campaigns for Red Bull, Warner Pictures, EA and SyFy on shoestring budgets. He als crafted social media strategies for Harley Davidson, Dell and Hornitos Tequila: Helping clients craft honest, targeted, content-rich messaging for the social web.

Matt Heindl’s Specialties:

Specialize in online marketing: Specifically social media and online viral marketing. Over the past 15 years Matt Heindl has become an expert in results-driven, alternative digital marketing strategies: Tapping into existing online movements to spread messages and create content.
