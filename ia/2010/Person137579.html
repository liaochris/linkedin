Matthew Skala’s Summary

Russian film director V.I. Pudovkin once said, “To show something as everyone sees it, is to have accomplished nothing.”

Cinematographer Matthew Skala strives to show audiences something they’ve never seen. His collaborators and critics agree, and they praise his work and his work ethic.

Skala has worked with 35mm, 16mm, 8mm, RED 4K, HD, and DV formats on features, shorts, documentaries, music videos and commercials.

The cinematographer’s work includes the short film “Universal Theories of the Duke of Zeppelin,” which was selected for an Academy Award, won Best Film at the Southern Exposure Film Forum, and received a Cine Gold Eagle Award. He also shot “A Nice Day for an Earthquake” which was an official selection in the Downtown L.A. Film Festival and the 2008 Vision Fest.

Skala has received much critical acclaim for his cinematography, including praise for his work on “Little Girl Lost.” The Winston-Salem Journal said, “The film benefits from Matthew A. Skala’s evocative cinematography.”

His career started early as a still photographer and evolved into an interest in motion pictures which inspired him to attend the film program at the University North Carolina School Of The Arts where he concentrated in Cinematography and graduated with a BFA.

while attending he was the Still Photographer on the professional film "Two Soldiers" which won the 2003 Academy Award for Best Live Action Short and Best Short over 15 minutes at the Palm Springs International Film Festival. He was also honored with nominations for both an American Society of Cinematographer’s Award and the Budapest Cinematography Masterclass.

Skala admires strong collaboration as much as he admires the power of images. Producers, directors, actors and others speak highly of Skala’s work ethic and his collaborative skills. He’s known to be very prepared and can stick to a plan, yet he’s also proven to be very spontaneous when he needs to be.
