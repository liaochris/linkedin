Max Nelson’s Summary

I have well over a decade of experience performing strategic technology and market research and analysis. I received my undergraduate education at the University of Chicago; my Master's in Science & Technology Policy at the George Washington University; and my Doctoral training in Policy Analysis from the RAND Graduate School at the RAND Corporation.

After College, I began work at the Department of Commerce's Office of Space Commerce and subsequently worked for NASA, Lawrence Livermore and Los Alamos National Labs, and the White House National Space Council where I was instrumental in the redesign of the Earth Observing System and determining the strategic direction of the Space Exploration Initiative. I have also been an entrepreneur and was a Founder of Paragon Space Development Corporation. As the Business Analyst for Donnelly Optics Corporation I was instrumental in the development of their digital camera lens product line which has garnered 85% market share. I was then the Technology & Market Strategist for the Eaton Corporation performing analyses of the logistics market, and recommended a strategic entry into RFID in 1997, long before it became the darling of the supply chain industry. Most recently I was the Director of Trend Research for Faith Popcorn's BrainReserve - providing market trend analysis for Senior Executives of the Fortune 500 such as Nordstrom's, P&G, Motorola and JiffyLube.

I am the new Director of Strategic Planning and Market Intelligence at General Dynamics - Advanced Information Systems where I am broadly responsible for the strategic planning, market research, and competitive intelligence functions. Most recently I was the Vice President for Public Policy and Strategic Business Planning for Lucent Technologies/OFS where I had a wide-range of responsibilities in strategic market research and public policy analysis and implementation for marketing and business development to develop and execute OFS' strategic business plans.
