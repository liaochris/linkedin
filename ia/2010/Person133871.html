Mark Harrington’s Summary

An accomplished marketer with widespread success, Mark's rare experience spans industries from publishing to payments and companies from start-ups to Fortune 500s. He's been instrumental in landmark acquisitions worth over a half billion dollars, essential to catapulting the growth of several organizations and served strategic roles for Citi, eBay, Pearson and Fox.

As Client Delivery Head he sits on Citi Prepaid's Management Team, directing Marketing, Creative, Implementation and Client Services for 1,200+ clients like Target, Verizon and BMW and an award-winning product line. Over his tenure he‘s helped drive revenues 2,400+%. Previously, he built the NetRave quality process, sat on Citi's Global Marketing Council and was GM for Ecount's $200+MM Citi acquisition. He also won the Bertie-Bob Award.

A Half.com co-founder, he built Retention/Direct Marketing, managed 70% of GMS and was the youngest Management Team member. Acquired by eBay for over $350+MM, Half hit profitability largely due to his evolution of the textbook market, earning him eBay's Out of this World Award. He also helped devise "one of the greatest publicity coups in history" (Time Mag), Half.com, OR.

He co-founded Company Sleuth (PC Mag Top 100) and Sports Sleuth (Yahoo Top 50), earning him Infonautics' Innovation Award. He promoted four national top 10 titles at Pearson, earning him the Summit Award. As a hobby he founded FightOnState.com (Fox Sports partner), Penn State's No. 1 fan site, featured on ESPN, USA Today, Sporting News and Big Ten Network.

He’s been featured in Sales & Mktg Mgmt, B2B, TMI and Promo, been an executive panelist for McKinsey and BCG, lectured at Penn State, Vanderbilt, BC and Drexel and advised non-profits like BEST Council, Lift For Life, Penn's Civilians, Big33 and several start-ups.

A Melbourne Wesley Cummings Scholar, he has a Marketing BS, Honors with Distinction, from The Pennsylvania State University and won the Smeal College’s Dorothy M. Kelly Award.
