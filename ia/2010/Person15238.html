Antonio Montes’s Education

  • University of Kansas

    Ph.D. , Chemical & Petroleum Engineering , 1985 — 1992

    Dissertation: "An Investigation of In Situ Gelation of a Polyacrylamide-Chromium(III) System in Porous Media."
    This work involved laboratory experiments to determine the mechanistic features of polymer gels flowing through porous material. After initially determining a suitable polymer gel system that could be studied in sand packs, displacement experiments were conducted. The flow rate was changed in each experiment. As the gelling solution was injected into the pack, the pressure drop across several sections was measured and recorded. The experimental data was simulated using a slightly modified model, based on the filtration hypothesis, originally written by another graduate student. The gel systems generate a three-dimensional network that effectively blocks the porous medium. This blocking is primarily caused by filtration of large gel aggregates as verified by the mathematical model.
