Scott Silverman’s Summary

I am a 11+ year veteran of the e-commerce and multichannel retail industry and have had a front row seat to the dot-com boom and bust in the late 1990s/early 2000s, the re-growth in the mid-2000s and the emergence of social media and mobile in the late 2000s and today.

Since 2001, I have been the Executive Director of Shop.org, the National Retail Federation's digital division and the world's leading membership community for digital retail.

I have grown the Shop.org community to more than 600 companies and thousands of individual participants and I have a deep and broad network of industry leaders.

Other accomplishments include:

> Launched Shop.org’s Annual Summit, the premier event for the digital retail industry and a Fastest 50 event in 2008 and 2009. The Summit has grown to more than 2,500 attendees with an exhibit hall featuring over 150 companies with keynotes from industry luminaries including chief executives of Dell, Macy’s, and eBay.

> Part of the Shop.org team that coined the term "Cyber Monday," which has become the biggest event of the year for online retailers.

> Created Shop.org’s Ray M. Greenly Scholarship Fund to help students pursue careers in e-commerce, along with a fund-raising business model which has led to the fund surpassing $1.5 million since 2006.

I am a frequent speaker and I'm regularly quoted in major news outlets including USA Today, The Wall Street Journal, CNBC, The New York Times, NPR, BusinessWeek, The Washington Post.

Scott Silverman’s Specialties:

E-Commerce and multichannel/crosschannel retail. Association management. E-commerce industry events. E-Commerce research. Association strategy development and execution. Facilitating the sharing of information among Internet and multichannel retail executives.
