Elizabeth Persinger’s Summary

I am a recent graduate from Northeastern University School of Law. I took a quarter off from school due to a death in the family. I just finished my last coop at CPCS in Roxbury/Dorchester. Due to taking a quarter off of school I am not bar eligible until Feb. 2010 and I am looking for a full time job in the criminal defense sector.

I have focused my academic work and internships on developing research, writing and oral advocacy skills; with a specific focus on criminal law. During my legal externships I had the opportunity to sharpen my research and writing skills, gain valuable insight into the development of case strategy, develop client advocacy skills and draft polices and legislation. I was exposed to a variety of substantive legal issues in different areas of the law and given rigorous assignments that challenged my research, writing and client advocacy skills.

In addition to these internships, I completed Northeastern University's Prisoners' Rights clinic where I had the opportunity to represent a state prison inmate at a parole hearing. This experience confirmed my commitment to advocating on behalf of criminal justice reform and my general interest in criminal law. Through my experience at the Prisoners’ Rights Clinic I learned that there is nothing more rewarding or gratifying that providing direct client representation.

My ultimate goal is to become a capital public defender and represent individuals facing the possibility of death. As an attorney I will be an advocate for individuals. I believe that individuals should not merely punished but provided sufficient services while in prison to be rehabilitated and become contributing members of society again. The bottom line is that I want to work with individuals in the criminal justice system in order to defend and enforce the constitutional rights of those individuals in our society who need those protections the most.
