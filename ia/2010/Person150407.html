Mario Vinasco’s Summary

I joined the HR analytics team of Google with the mission to innovate as much on the people side as the company does in the product's by leveraging cutting edge methodologies, tools and techniques in business intelligence and analytics.

Upon assuming the position of senior manager of analytics, I designed programs to boost the compensation team's technical capabilities. Specifically, I managed the creation of a next generation HR analytics database that made possible sophisticated planning cycles, employee segmentation, and predictive analysis; as a result my team was able to manage large allocation process of cash bonuses and stock options ($270 M and 2.2 M shares respectively annually).

We solved problems in the prediction of attrition, identification of top talent, efficient project allocation and statistical studies of pay for performance; we partnered with quantitative marketing and google analytics teams to use and share best practices.
We also partnered closely with HR technology and engineering to provide key reports, metrics and pro-active identification of issues, such as vesting cliffs.

I grew the team by hiring talented business analysts, hosting interns and attracting internal talent.

In my prior experience I provided analytic support to aggressive marketing campaigns in the credit card industry as well as high tech. At Providian, a $100 B in outstanding balances company, I provided critical modeling and monitoring for the first 0% offers that were rolled out in the industry; at Intuit, leader in personal finance software, I managed the TurboTax analytics platform to launch innovative affiliate programs.
