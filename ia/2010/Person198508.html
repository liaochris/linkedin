Swami Manohar’s Summary

Swami Manohar is a founder and Managing Director of LimberLink Technologies. Prior to this, he was a co-founder and CEO of PicoPeta Simputers Pvt.Ltd which was acquired by Geodesic in FY 2006 and was merged with Geodesic in FY 2007. He served as the Chief IPR & Strategy Officer at Geodesic. After obtaining his PhD in Computer Science from Brown University he was a faculty at the University of North Carolina for two years and then was at the Indian Institutet of Science (IISc), Bangalore, between 1990 to 2005. He was on the faculty of the department of Computer Science and the Super Computer Education and research Center at the Institute. While at IISc, he co-invented the Simputer, was
awarded the first Dewang Mehta Award for Innovation in IT, and pioneered the faculty entrepreneurship activity at IISc, which has since catalysed similar activity in IITs and other educational institutes in India. He co-founded Strand Lifesciences (formerly Strand Genomics), and String
Labs, the first academic incubating company in India.

Swami Manohar has contributed to research in Computer graphics, visualisation, virtual reality and CAD for rapid protyping. He has been the thesis advisor for over forty graduate students including three PhDs. His most significant contribution till date is to the design of the hardware and software architecture of the Simputer. As CEO of PicoPeta he was instrumental in converting the prototype of the Simputer in to a real commercial product, the Amida Simputer, India's
first commercial handheld product. His passion is to harness information and communication technologies for a positive impact on soceity.
