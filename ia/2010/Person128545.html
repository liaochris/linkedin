    November 2009 — Present (1 year )

    Develop and lead new PA education initiative that injects 3D video game technology instruction into public school classrooms. I took the initiative to develop this program from concept to reality and daily perform all operations necessary to move the initiative forward including: program conception, program design, secure of internal/external stakeholder support, raising capital, proposal writing, prototype development, presentation development and delivery. I interface directly with top state government officials, education leaders, public school superintendents, and university heads to organize and implement the program such that it is delivered free of charge to schools. The program is designed and modeled after industry practice and developed in close partnership with PA public education leaders, game professionals and is being sponsored by national foundations and industry giants. Communities of students grade 9-12 are taken behind the scenes of game development to learn how games are made. Aligned with national standards, students work as part of a development team to gain practical hands on experience as they assume roles that develop 21st century skills such as team building, collaboration, idea formation, remote communication, problem solving and virtual world design. The program capitalizes on the wide student interest in video games to increase STEM competency providing students with instruction from industry leaders and institutions of higher learning. In addition, the students actually help solve larger problems currently faced by schools.
