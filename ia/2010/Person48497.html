Dan Skwire dskwire@mindspring.com’s Summary

Software problems are my main professional interest. I have had a 40-year career devoted to mission-critical software problem work: I am an expert in both problem resolution and problem prevention, on multiple platforms.

In solving software problems, I have worked in various HELP desks, and mission-critical support organizations debugging server and storage Enterprise-class issues. .

In preventing software problems: I've worked in organizations providing software problem recovery features in zOS, software test tools, best processes in all software development phases, inspections and walkthroughs for design, code, and testing, and availability studies to improve site availability (reduce numbers of occurrences or duration of problems). I've worked on system reconfiguration software features in zOS (testing and improving), I've provided continuous improvement ideas to prevent problem occurrence.

I started an active 52 member linkedin Group, "First Fault Problem Solving". This is a great forum with many new discussions. Join it, please!.

I just completed a book - "First Fault Software Problem Solving: A Guide For Engineers, Managers, and Users".

The book tells the reader how to ensure that problems can be solved rapidly and accurately, as is done in well-engineered mission-critical environments. Examples are chosen from all platforms, and the general techniques and advice can benefit all IT personnel to better solve production problems as soon as possible - of course, the goal is to solve problems when they FIRST occur! Despite everyone's best intentions, problems do occur!

Yes, I enjoy the whole software problem area; I'm good at it, and I welcome more opportunities. Since I've successfully helped to eliminate the source of many critical software problems wherever I've worked, I must move on to more needy areas. How can I help your organization? Let's talk about it!

  • Function/Systems Test/Debug and Software Recovery Designer

    IBM Corp

    (Public Company; IBM; Information Technology and Services industry)

    July 1972 — June 1981 (9 years )

    My first computer industry job and it was with the leader of the computer industry, IBM! What an opportunity! I was thrilled to be hired into the MVS Recovery Design department within the project office for the new MVS (zOS) operating system.

    Quickly the role turned into testing and debugging, where I co-wrote an error-injector test tool and used it to find bugs in software recovery and serviceability. I continued to learn MVS crash-dump debugging, got very good at it, and developed a sixth-sense for finding and fixing software defects.

    I improved my skills, collaborated with developers, and evaluated unsolved problems to find serviceability improvements. I also started the first MVS Diagnostic techniques manual and wrote several chapters.

    My early colleagues, and I created tools that allowed the early, MVS opeating system to be debugged. Serviceability of the highly fault-tolerant zOS (MVS) operating system turned out to be far more difficult than the software designers and developers had anticipated.

    Later, I was proud to see these diagnostic data-gathering and data analyzing tools incorporated into zOS, and they still help make zOS very serviceable and reliable, 35 years later.

    How could a job be any better? I was finding defects in important IBM products, continually improving the products for use world-wide, and knowing that there will always be more problems and further opportunities.

    I continued branching out by studying the use of 'inspections' in software development. I initiated a test-case inspection in my last project and it was so successful that they became standard in MVS development. I planned to teach testing (I did it with new junior team members), but eventually I needed a break from solid testing work, and sought the move into the field (Tampa Systems Engineer) job.

    But the interest in problem-solving and continuous improvement would never leave me, as I was to find out, through all my following jobs.
