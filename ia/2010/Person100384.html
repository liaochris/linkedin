Jody Peters’s Summary

My PhD training has been in aquatic community ecology. Specifically, I have studied how habitat structure, predation, competition, and landscape connectivity with-in lakes and across lake landscapes interact to produce observed patterns in the distribution and impacts of invasive species in in-land lakes and the Great Lakes.
In addition, to my graduate research, I am also currently working with graduate students in the Career Center helping to create professional development programs and workshops and advising students as they explore and apply for careers after grad school.

    Hope College

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    1999 — 2003 (4 years )

    As a biology major at Hope College I conducted a wide range of field and laboratory research on the swimmer's itch parasite. In the lab I conducted ecological experiments to assess how temperature affects the swimmer's itch parasite's ability to infect its snail host. I also examined the parasite at the cellular level by preliminarily charaterized acetabular gland contents of the parasite and examining its effect on cultured cells. In the field at Lakes Cadillac and Mitchell I surveyed swimmer's to determine prevalence of swimmer's itch around the through the summer and determined the main duck and snail hosts of the swimmer's itch parasite on the lakes.
