Emre Sevinc’s Summary

I’m working in the field of CALL, Computer Assisted Language Learning at the Linguapolis Institute that is part of the University of Antwerp. My main responsibility, as a member of the research team, is to do research and development for new language teaching platforms as well as evaluate various technical choices for future projects.

As a former lecturer I helped students develop critical thinking skills in any field they study, be it psychology, sociology or computer science (you name it).

As a former IBM Center of Advanced Studies Lab. coordinator in the computer science department of Istanbul Bilgi University, I was focused on managing scientific projects that involve intensive data processing, parallel / distributed programming, data mining, machine learning, multiagent simulation & modelling, semantic web programming and visualization.

Previously as an application developer, database designer, project manager and scientist I worked for a wide variety of applications including but not limited to e-learning infrastructure and methodologies, content management systems, data mining and machine learning, data visualization and analysis, natural language processing and technical translation.

I'm particularly interested in application of my training, education, IT, management and cognitive science expertise in developing sophisticated systems having a strong R&D side.
