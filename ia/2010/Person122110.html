    Forward Footprint

    (Environmental Services industry)

    August 2007 — December 2008 (1 year 5 months)

    Forward Footprint would like to create a unique way of recycling that will change the way America thinks about the estimated 55 million tons of inert waste that it produces annually. As a material recovery facility, we will divert a community’s inert waste from landfill disposal and transform it into a venue for cultural arts and entertainment. Inert waste will be directed to the site where it will be compacted and formed into a bowl-shaped amphitheatre, then covered in topsoil and planted with grass. This project will be funded by the “dump fees” customarily paid by contractors to dispose of these materials. The completed amphitheatre will generate ticket sales, venue rental, concession, and advertising revenue, which will provide for its own operation. This method of recycling will do more than preserve our environment; it will build and preserve our community. The Eco-Colosseum has the potential to be the solid waste solution for many growing communities.
