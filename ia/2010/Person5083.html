Alan Johnson’s Summary

July 8, 2010

Sooner or Later…

You want your investment assets to work harder, but your financial advisor seems to dodge this issue every time you try to raise it.

Instead, he wants to run computer models showing what will happen to your retirement nest egg if you work longer, save more, or spend less.

But you’re a working professional, and you already know how to do these calculations. You certainly expect a lot more from the 1% of your assets you pay in advisory fees each year.

…Like a written document outlining your advisor’s accountability to you.

…Like a target return for your portfolio and how your advisor intends to achieve it.

…Like a performance dashboard that shows monthly progress towards these goals, and the indicated corrective actions when actual returns stray from target.

…Like a comparison to a suitable benchmark so that you can see how his performance compares to that of other managers (including yourself).

Given today’s “new normal,” sooner or later the major brokerage firms will get around to offering these services.

In the meantime, there’s Johnson Harper LLC.

We manage investments. But we create confidence.

Alan Johnson’s Experience

  • Managing Member

    Johnson Harper LLC

    (Privately Held; Financial Services industry)

    May 2009 — Present (1 year 3 months)

    Johnson Harper LLC is a registered investment adviser with its principal place of business in the State of New Jersey.

    The firm strives to operate according to five key principles:

    1. Transparency. Our investment processes and intentions are designed to be as transparent as possible to our clients. To the greatest extent we can, we try to hold in our own portfolios the same, or in some cases similar, investments that we recommend to our clients.

    2. Education. Our investment process is highly driven by macroeconomic trends and implemented using concepts based on modern portfolio theory. To the extent of our clients’ level of interest, we inform and educate them on the economic indicators we follow and our view of the likely impact on the capital markets.

    3. Risk Management. Because we try to hold the same or similar investments as our clients, we believe that our respective interests are highly aligned. Recognizing that risk encompasses much more than just market price fluctuations, we try to take a more holistic approach that has its basis in industrial risk management practices.

    4. Performance Benchmarking. Perhaps our most significant differentiator, performance benchmarking starts with establishing appropriate and measurable metrics with our clients. These metrics are tracked on a monthly basis and designed to allow clients to quickly determine the extent to which our activities are adding value to their portfolios.

    5. Scalability. At a time when many brokerage firms are moving ‘up market’ to recruit higher net worth clients, Johnson Harper LLC constantly seeks to economically scale its investment management processes to the needs of the broadest possible client base.
