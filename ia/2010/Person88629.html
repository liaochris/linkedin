James Freeman’s Summary

James W. Freeman currently serves as Managing Partner for Cambridge Capital Partners operations in the United States, where he advises clients in areas ranging from transaction structuring, development feasibility and analysis, tax planning, audits, financial consulting, and capital markets. Additionally, Mr. Freeman currently serves as Chief Financial Officer of the Cambridge affiliate Strategic 1031 Exchange Advisors (“SEA 1031”). He obtained his Bachelor of Science from the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill, majoring in Biology with minors in both Chemistry and Business Administration, and later received his Master of Accounting from the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill’s Kenan-Flagler Business School, with a concentration in Tax and Real Estate. Mr. Freeman later earned his Master of Business Administration from Duke University’s Fuqua School of Business, with a concentration in International Economics.

At the beginning of his career, Mr. Freeman joined Arthur Andersen’s Global Tax practice, where he assisted multinational clients with global tax strategy, corporate restructuring, and mergers & acquisitions. He later took an expatriate assignment in the Brussels. He later joined Deloitte & Touche’s Brussels practice where he furthered his expertise by structuring complex tax models for major international clients. More recently, Mr. Freeman worked for Valvoline, first in International Tax and as the National Managing Director of Valvoline’s business in Germany. There, he managed and balanced all aspects of Valvoline’s business in Germany and is credited with doubling the company’s business during his tenure. Mr. Freeman currently lives in the metro Atlanta area with his wife, Jessica, and their dog, Barley. He is an active member of the Georgia Society of CPAs, HBS Real Estate SIG, and Fuqua Atlanta Alumni.
