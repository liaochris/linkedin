Greg Vojnovic’s Summary

Greg Vojnovic is currently Vice President of Development for Popeye’s, responsible for all company and franchise development activity. As the senior development executive at Popeyes, his department encompasses all company and franchise real estate, construction, design, and equipment as well as all franchise recruitment activities.

Greg is a veteran of over 30 years in the restaurant industry with over 20 years of experience in restaurant development. In addition to Popeye’s, he has held leadership roles in a number of restaurant companies including Huddle House as Vice President of Development, MaggieMoo’s as Director Franchise Development and various domestic and international development positions with other major foodservice brands including Denny’s and Arby’s. Greg was also owner of the Atlanta-based casual dining restaurant chain Bridgetown Grill, a Caribbean themed concept that received “Hot Concept of the Year” recognition from publications including Restaurants & Institutions and Chain Leader.

Greg is a regular lecturer on franchising and development for the IFA Development Training series, contributes articles and columns to trade journals, and frequently participates as an industry expert on conference panels. In addition, early in his career Greg was awarded a Gold Medal in the US Chef’s Open.

Greg holds a Bachelor of Science Degree in Hotel Administration from the Statler School of Hotel Administration at Cornell University and resides in the Atlanta area. He is an active member of the International Franchise Association, CFE designated, and a board member of the VetFran program. Greg’s diverse background and experience with independent and chain restaurant operations have produced an accomplished restaurant executive. Prior to attending Cornell’s Hotel school, Greg was immersed in the restaurant business in several hourly and management positions with such iconic brands as TGI Friday’s and Victoria Station.
