Kim Wold’s Summary

Professional background in computer technology with an emphasis in wireless messaging. My career began with AT&T Bell Labs where I was deep into computer programming. My expertise involved everything from MVS/JES3 internals to Unix internals. I voluntarily left AT&T in 1990. I then developed a first-to-market computer software business that sends messages to pagers/cell phones My computerized text messaging experience grew from there to include voice broadcast and the ability to support all spectrums of an organizations' requirements for their messaging operation.

In 2000 I sold Cross Communications to Semotus Solutions.
In 2004 I built National Messaging Solutions focusing on entertainment, business and critical/emergency messaging applications. NMS was sold to TextPower in 2009.

Kim Wold’s Specialties:

Today I am focusing on a few select accounts helping them design, develop and launch successful programs in SMS/TEXT messaging. My experience will guarantee their success.

Kim Wold’s Experience

  • President, Founder

    Text Connections

    (Privately Held; Wireless industry)

    April 2009 — Present (1 year 4 months)

    SMS/TEXT messaging is an exciting technology that is just coming of age in the U.S. Businesses are using mobile marketing to drive revenue. Government at all levels, are using it for general communications. Entertainment and non-profit organizations are using it to connect with their supporting communities.

  • President, Founder

    National Messaging Solutions

    (Wireless industry)

    January 2004 — February 2009 (5 years 2 months)

    SMS/Text Messaging Services for Entertainment, Business and Critical/Emergency messaging applications. NMS developed a powerful SMS Switching system providing gateway/aggregation services to the entertainment, business and emergency messaging clients. Clients like Chicago Public Schools, BSU, NBC, major utility companies, law enforcement, public service and many more. NMS was one of the first to build/offer a single point of service for voice/sms broadcast for its clients through a single interface. That was developed in response to the Columbine crisis.

    As a result of the powerful system NMS designed and developed, we were in a unique position to help a broad range of clients implement their SMS vision with the best cost and performance back-end. This included providing coverage and delivery options for all carriers and price plans specifically designed to suit their needs.

    NMS earned many levels of recognition in the Industry; for instance, NMS was one of only three approved and provisioned for National TV SMS TEXTING programs (like you see on the American Idol, etc.); NMS was one of the first to offer business mission-critical messaging services, used for client banking, law-enforcement, etc.

    NMS was sold in Feb 2009 to TextPower, Inc.

  • President, Founder

    Cross Communications, Inc

    (Wireless industry)

    May 1995 — January 2000 (4 years 9 months)

    Text messaging services for business and emergency/critical messaging applications. Cross was one of the first to develop computer software to send messages to pagers/cell phones around 1991. HipLink software was developed first for Unix in conjunction with Sun Microsystems and Ameritech. Sun Micros began offering HipLink to its corportate clients as a plug-in to SunNet Manager. HipLink earned its way up to be a generally recommended product for both network management products, such as openview, as well as help desk products, such as Remedy.

    Additionally, CCI was one of few providing emergency messaging to the government. Organizations like: O'Hare Airport, the US Army, CIA/Pentagon are examples of clients for this technology. Cellular (SMS) is not mission-critical off the shelf by no means. CCI developed a redundant and high-throughput systems architecture to harden that communications vehicle.

    CCI was sold to Semotus Solutions in 2000.
