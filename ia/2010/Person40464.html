Cynthia Shannon’s Summary

Cynthia completed the International Baccalaureate from the Frankfurt International School in Germany, and then graduated with a Bachelor's Degree in Journalism from New York University. She decided to take a job in the publishing industry, where she started at the small, independent publisher Other Press, LLC as a Publicity Assistant. She then joined John Wiley and Sons as an Associate Publicist, and was promoted to Publicist for the Jossey-Bass imprint in June 2008. In August 2010, Cynthia joined Berrett-Koehler Publishers to focus on creating national publicity campaigns for business and consumer affairs titles.

Cynthia relocated to San Francisco in June 2008 and lives in Russian Hill. She was on the board of the Northern California Book Publicity and Marketing Association and edited their newsletter, taking it from a bi-annual printed report to a monthly, HTML email that reaches over 300 subscribers. In her spare time, Cynthia volunteers with Litquake and 826 Valencia. In New York, Cynthia had been a tutor for Literacy Partners for a year, teaching reading and math to adults. Cynthia also volunteered at the Center for Independent Publishing, where she assisted with coordinating the Writers Conference and Book Fair.

While at Wiley, Cynthia has continued her education by taking classes in the publishing program at New York University, and was selected for a scholarship to attend the Stanford Professional Publishing Course in July 2009. She started taking Arabic classes in April 2009 and continues to practice French any chance she gets.

In her spare time, Cynthia enjoys reading (mostly contemporary fiction), trying new restaurants, going to movies, learning about art, and spending time outdoors. She plans on owning a vineyard in Italy when she retires. She also thinks it's incredibly weird writing about herself in the third person.
