Honggang Zhang (张宏纲)’s Summary

A researcher whose expertise/skills in the academic research & development of wireless, mobile, broadband, and personal communications and networks, and whose experiences of worldwide standardization and regulation activities can contribute to the growth, the development and the benefit of a university, a research institute, or a high-tech identity, as summarized as following:

(1) 20+ years experiences in R&D in wireless, mobile, broadband, and personal communications and networking, especially in the fields of cognitive radio (CR) and cognitive networks (CN), Ultra-Wideband (UWB) systems and technologies, WPAN/WLAN/WMAN, wireless sensor networks, autonomic communications and self-organizing networks, consumer communications & smart home networking, software-defined radio (SDR), wireless fading channel modelling and Wireless 1394 systems.

(2) Standardization advocator, organizer and contributor in IEEE 802.15 WPAN Task Group (TG4a, former TG3a, http://www.ieee802.org/15/pub/TG3a_CFP.html).

(3) Founding member of UWB Forum and principle author of DS-UWB specifications in IEEE 802.15 WPAN standardization, for which he initiated the "Soft-Spectrum Adaptation (SSA)" technique and contributed to its worldwide developments.

(4) International research projects leader, especially within the European IST(Information Society Technologies) Sixth Framework Programme (FP6) and ICT (Information and Communication Technologies) Seventh Framework Programme (FP7) - EUWB, PULSERS 2, etc.

(5) Leadership in the Technical Committee on Cognitive Networks (TCCN) of IEEE Communications Society (ComSoc) as well as a number of international academic conferences (e.g., CrownCom 2006-2009, ChinaCom 2007, and IEEE ISCIT 2006-2009).

Honggang Zhang (张宏纲)’s Specialties:

(1) Cognitive Radio & Cognitive Networks related research & development, technical committee organization and management;
(2) Testbed and prototypes implementation for UWB and Cognitive Radio;
(3) IEEE 802.11 (WLAN), 802.15 (WPAN/WBAN), 802.16 (WiMAX) and 802.22 (WRAN) standardization;
(4) Networking architecture for next-generation broadband mobile communications;
(5) Cognitive wireless mesh networking (CogMesh) and Cognitve Cloud (CogCloud);
(6) Green radio and green communications.
