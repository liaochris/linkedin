Jon Paul’s Summary

Your Direction & Implementation

Insights - new financial and strategic perspectives about your customer/product profitability, production/service/operating costs; powerful analysis/good questions to find hidden value and implement changes to more profits and/or better use of resources.
Strategy/Planning - helping you evaluate alternatives and determine if you are headed down the right path
Processes - finding ways to operate better; developing/automating/implementing the processes to take you there.
Your Resources

People - paving the way for your people to be successful by giving them the resources, processes and training to succeed; helping organize, recruit and develop your finance and administrative staff; interim/consulting help.
Financial Structure - developing the financial structure that fits your company now and where it is heading; giving you room to grow and operate your business plus minimize your capital costs and resources needed.
Technology - determine, add or modify the computer system and office technology you need; getting the most out of what you have or will get.
Excel - getting all areas of your business and people to use this powerful resource in ways you might never imagine; turning this into an additional resource that can add value across many areas of your business.
Your Reporting

Financials - get financials up to top shape on three key measures- accuracy, timeliness and understandability; making the whole financial reporting process run smoother with better underlying support.
Reporting - going beyond financial and current computer system reports to develop/automate key daily/weekly/monthly indicators and reports/charts so you and your people can quickly keep on top of the business.
Compliance - keep your company in compliance with government/bank/investor financial regulations, whether Sarbanes Oxley, external audit, reporting, covenants, tax and other matters; letting you focus more on running your business.
