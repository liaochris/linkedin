Henrik Olsen’s Summary

I'm a seasoned Interaction Designer and Usability Expert. My passion is designing simple, useful and attractive interactive products that everybody can use.

From 2003 to 2006 I was an employee at ACURE, An IBM Company. I worked as project manager and head of the user experience team on the Danish national e-health portal (www.sundhed.dk). In 2005, the site won the special prize of the year in the contest of the best government sites in Denmark. The prize was given for the “…impressive self-service solutions that fulfil their purpose and are easy to use.” The site was ranked 94% user-friendly.

In April 2006, I left my job at ACURE to freelance and find the time to write a book. In january 2007 I joined a partnership with mouseketeers (www.mouseketeers.dk), where I work part-time.

Among colleagues around the world, I'm known as the guy behind GUUUI (www.guuui.com). Here, I write weekly postings and quarterly articles about interaction design and user experience in general.

I'm particularly passionate about prototyping – the creation of quick and dirty models of interactive products that allow us to test their viability before they are put into production. I've written several articles on the benefits of prototyping and how to use prototyping efficiently throughout a development process.
