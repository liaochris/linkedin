Philip Inghelbrecht’s Experience

  • President

    TrueCar

    (Privately Held; Automotive industry)

    October 2008 — January 2010 (1 year 4 months)

    TrueCar is a VC-funded company that brings transparency to automotive retail by publishing actual prices paid for new cars and revealing other industry data (such as the dealer cost structure) to help consumers in their car-buying process.

    I joined as the third employee and have built the company from the ground up. Hired the initial team of 15 people, launched the site within 6 months, put the key data licensing and syndication deals in place, and ramped up traffic between May and October 2009 to more than 2 million price reports served.

  • Head of Sports and Entertainment partnerships

    YouTube (Google)

    (Public Company; GOOG; Internet industry)

    June 2005 — October 2008 (3 years 5 months)

    I joined Google in the Video group and immediately transfered to YouTube following the Fall 2006 acquisition. I initially led the sports and entertainment vertical (e.g. Discovery, NBA, etc.) and subsequently syndication and copyright management.

  • Director EMEA

    Ordinate Corporation

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Information Technology and Services industry)

    July 2004 — June 2005 (1 year )

    Ordinate has a breakthrough speech recognition technology to automatically assess spoken proficiency in a foreign language and reading fluency. I was responsible to initiate, negotiate and close licensing deals (both pure technology and ASP-type) in Europe and the Middle-East. The company was acquired by Harcourt Assessment in October 2004.

  • Founder

    Shazam Entertainment

    (Privately Held; Telecommunications industry)

    January 2000 — June 2004 (4 years 6 months)

    Shazam (http://www.shazam.com) is a VC funded company that specializes in audio recognition. The company’s best-known commercial application is the Shazam music recognition app on the iPhone.

    I co-founded Shazam in December 1999 and was involved in all aspects of starting and building a company: from writing and filing patents, raising capital (US$ 20 million) to hiring the first employee. My initial key responsibilities included the build-out and maintenance of a large database of music (exceeding 2MM tracks). I subsequently worked with the engineering team to shrinkwrap the core technology and license it to businesses worldwide (predominantly in the broadcast monitoring industry). This ultimately lead to a trade-sale of Shazam to BMI, Inc.
