Hamid Arshadi’s Summary

Hands-on experience managing more than 1,200 land development projects in the public and private sectors, ranging from 10 to 8,500 acres in size and requiring local, county, state, and / or federal processes & permits. Expertise in managing projects requiring California Environmental Quality Act (CEQA) and National Environmental Policy Act (NEPA) compliance. In the area of renewable energy industry, responsibilities included involvement during various stages of the permitting and certification processes of two of the world’s largest solar energy projects in the California desert, as well as licensing of 173 miles of high-voltage electric transmission lines and substations to provide electrical facilities necessary to integrate up to 4,500 megawatts of new wind turbine based energy in southern California.

Hands-on experience in identifying and screening sites, and performing due diligence for potential areas to accommodate residential, commercial, and renewable energy projects.

Effective negotiation skills for the execution of various agreements, including cost sharing, development, and intergovernmental agreements, as well as improvement district and engineering contract agreements during various phases of development projects. Also, involved in negotiating options for land purchase / lease and option agreements, managing interconnection requests, and evaluating project viability for utility-scale renewable energy generation.

Hamid Arshadi’s Experience

  • Licensing Project Manager

    Southern California Edison-An Edison International Company

    (Public Company; EIX; Utilities industry)

    August 2009 — Present (1 year 1 month)

    Currently managing the proposed Tehachapi Project's licensing entitlement process, which includes the construction of 173 miles of new and upgraded high-voltage electric transmission lines and substations to deliver electricity from new wind farms in remote areas of eastern Kern County, California, to the Los Angeles Basin. Tehachapi would provide the electrical facilities necessary to integrate up to 4,500 megawatts ("MW") of new wind turbine based electricity generation and other generation resources in the Tehachapi Wind Resource Area (“TWRA”). It will be the first major transmission project in California built for the specific purpose of accessing multiple renewable generation sources in remote renewable-rich resource areas, an essential step for California to meet its renewable energy goals. The estimated total project cost in nominal dollars is $1.837 billion.
