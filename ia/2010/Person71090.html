Frank E. Banks’s Experience

  • Lead Systems Engineer/Product Strategist

    Zynga

    (Privately Held; Computer Games industry)

    November 2008 — April 2010 (1 year 6 months)

    I started with Zynga as a Senior Software Engineer and transitioned to Lead Systems Engineer for the Mafia Wars team under the direction of CTO and co-founder Michael Luxton. I supervised four engineers responsible for the 24/7 operations of the very large scale LAMP platform necessary to serve millions of users per day. We pioneered the use of various Memcache clusters and an asynchronous queue architecture that utilized Memcache and MySQL. We developed leading edge practices and custom technology for code deployment via bash scripts, diagnostics and monitoring using Munin and Nagios, horizontal MySQL partitioning and more.

    In October 2009 I was recruited by Michael Luxton to join the Cafe World team as Lead Systems Engineer to help them with the explosive growth they experienced immediately post launch. In this role I was able to build on our previous hard-won expertise to bring best practices to the massive Cafe World EC2-based infrastructure. Highlights included being only the second studio in the company to add the Moxi memcached proxy to the production stack. I also worked with the Zynga India Ops team to supervise the development and roll-out of MCMux, an enhanced in-house version of Moxi. By the time I left the team in early January 2010 all the infrastructure was in place to support growth to 10 million users per day and beyond.

    In January 2010 I was recruited by co-founder and Senior Vice-President of Product Eric Schiermeyer to work under his supervision in a temporary Product Strategy role. In this position I audited the product management process across all the major studios in the company and produced consolidated reports. I was also charged with managing the development of a proprietary analytics tool that had been outsourced to third-party developers based in Taiwan. Lastly, I was given the opportunity to work with Business Ops as a consultant for their international expansion efforts.

  • Secretary General, Founder, Inventor

    musedot

    (Internet industry)

    May 2007 — October 2008 (1 year 6 months)

    Musedot is a search engine that enables you to discover new music based on the music you already know. It is a cutting edge social data mining application that approaches the problem of how do you expose people to music they don’t know about but would like if they did, in an entirely unique and powerful way. I have been developing the underlying theories for about twelve years even as I went about my professional career.

    In May of 2007 I closed out my contract with InteliMark Enterprises in order to work on musedot fulltime. After building a fairly advanced search engine technology I presented it to my informal business adviser Michael Cerda (now co-founder CEO of Threadbox) in September 2008. Although Michael was positive about the product we were both realistic about the prospects of raising funds in a very challenging start-up environment. I decided it was best to put musedot in cold storage and pursue a more standard career path that would allow me to build on my work involving social data mining and applications.

  • Founder/CEO

    Intraversal Communications Corp.

    (Internet industry)

    November 1994 — December 1996 (2 years 2 months)

    I founded Intraversal with the goal of creating multi-media CD-ROMs for historically important musicians, starting with John Coltrane. Fortunately, I was first exposed to the Internet not long after creating the company and became convinced we should change our focus.

    With only grudging support from my business partner, who also supplied the funding, I managed to land our first client Koeltz Scientific Books. We not only created an e-commerce site for Mr. Koeltz's business but hosted and maintained it as well. It was through this project that I was introduced to ColdFusion, HTML, web server software, etc.

    I was convinced that the Internet, and e-commerce specifically, represented a huge and emerging opportunity, and that we should focus on building an off-the-shelf e-commerce solution for web developers. Unfortunately, my business partner simply did not agree which led me to leave the company and establish MANMACHINE to pursue the opportunity on my own.
