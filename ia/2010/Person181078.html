Sameer Kumar’s Experience

  • Editor and Publisher

    Y Media

    (Privately Held; Internet industry)

    January 2008 — Present (2 years 2 months)

    I'm currently running my own online publishing enterprise - Y Media - which I set up in January 2008. This currently includes two websites - India Automotive (www.indiaautomotive.net) and Faster and Faster (www.fasterandfaster.net).

    Faster and Faster is now one of the biggest, most popular English-language motorcycle websites in the world. And I'm working hard to replicate Faster and Faster's success with India Automotive as well.

  • Executive Editor - CAR India, BIKE India, CV

    Next Gen Publishing Ltd.

    (Privately Held; Media Production industry)

    December 2005 — August 2007 (1 year 9 months)

    Based In Pune, as executive editor for Next Gen Publishing Ltd’s automotive media division, I headed editorial teams for three magazines – CAR India, BIKE India and CV (Commercial Vehicle).

    Was part of the start-up team that launched these three magazines in 2005. The first two are now very successful B2C automotive titles, while the third has carved a strong niche for itself in the automotive B2B space.

    Later, I also served as executive editor for Next Gen’s online media division, where I was responsible for all online content-related deliverables.

  • Executive Editor - Auto Monitor

    Infomedia India Ltd

    (Public Company; Publishing industry)

    September 2004 — November 2005 (1 year 3 months)

    As executive editor at the Mumbai-based Auto Monitor (published by Infomedia India Ltd.), I headed all editorial operations, managing our bureaus in Mumbai, Delhi, Calcutta, Chennai, Bangalore and Pune.

    Worked on setting up the Auto Monitor website (www.automonitor.co.in), which is currently the only dedicated online resource of automotive business/automotive industry news in India.

    While working at Auto Monitor, I was also invited to be a part of the automotive segment for 'Paisa Vasool,' a 30-minute weekly show on DD News, where I got to speak about various automotive-related issues.

  • Road Test Editor

    Business Standard Ltd.

    (Privately Held; Newspapers industry)

    November 2002 — August 2004 (1 year 10 months)

    Business Standard Motoring is one of India's most popular monthly magazines on cars, motorcycles and national / international motorsport.

    During my two-year stint with BSM, I was the Road Test Editor for cars, and was in charge of testing and evaluation of all new cars launched in India.

    See some of the monthly columns I wrote for BSM, here:
    http://bsmotoring.business-standard.com/bsm/wcms/en/home/blogs/sameerk/index.html

  • Editor - TechTree.com

    ITNation India Pvt.Ltd.

    (Privately Held; Media Production industry)

    February 2002 — October 2002 (9 months)

    As founding editor, I helped set up TechTree (www.techtree.com) at the Mumbai-based IT Nation (P) Ltd. This was the country’s very first ‘personal technology’ website, and continues to do very well today.

    During my time with ITN, I also worked as editorial consultant on a joint project with the Industrial Design Centre (IDC is a part of IIT Bombay) and HP Labs India. For this, amongst other things, I had the opportunity to work with Dr. Srinivasan Ramani (Director, HP Labs India), an eminent scientist and a long-time proponent of low-cost end-user computing research and development.

  • Executive Editor - Indian Auto

    Jasubhai Media Pvt. Ltd.

    (Privately Held; Media Production industry)

    August 2000 — November 2001 (1 year 4 months)

    As executive editor at India Auto magazine (published by Mumbai-based Jasubhai Media Limited), I worked towards a comprehensive revamp of the country’s oldest auto magazine, in terms of vehicle testing, content and design.

    Also set up the (now defunct) Indian Auto website.

  • Head Writer - CHIP magazine

    Jasubhai Digital Media Pvt. Ltd.

    (Privately Held; Media Production industry)

    June 1998 — April 2000 (1 year 11 months)

    As head writer, I was a part of the team which got CHIP magazine (published by Mumbai-based Jasubhai Digital Media Limited) started in India in the late-1990s. CHIP went on to become one of the biggest success stories in the Indian mediascape.

    Later, as columnist and editorial consultant, I also contributed to other JDM magazines like Computer Arts, and a series of specials on 3D animation and digital imaging.

  • Director

    Pixels Multimedia

    (Educational Institution; Animation industry)

    October 1995 — June 1998 (2 years 9 months)

    In the mid-1990s, I set up and headed Pixels Multimedia (P) Ltd., one of the very first small-scale outfits in Uttar Pradesh which offered services in the areas of multimedia instruction, digital audio/video post-production and 3D animation.

    At that time, I also served as multimedia technology consultant with the Non-Conventional Energy Development Agency (NEDA, a government undertaking), and the King George's Medical College (KGMC), Lucknow.
