Rafael Osorio’s Summary

Project Manager/Musical Director Rafael Osorio has received numerous awards such as the Iglesia Apostolica Renovacion Leadership and Commitment award (2010), the MassMutual VIP award (2009) and other recognitions highlighting his work in varied projects. He graduated from the prestigious Berklee College of Music in 2005 where he studied Music Performance. He also completed undergraduate work at the University of Massachusetts-Amherst, and graduate work in Information Management at Baypath College. His earlier work with IAR allowed him to work musically around the world, in places such as, Spain, France, Germany, Italy, Amsterdam, Switzerland, and Belgium. Currently, his work is focused on the Caribbean, Central and South American countries of Puerto Rico, Dominican Republic, Peru and Argentina spearheading strategies to strengthen the IAR relationship network.

Born in San Juan, Puerto Rico, he moved with his family to the United States, where he excelled in English and Music. By fifth grade he was accompanying for his school choir performances and at age 13 he began as the keyboardist for his church worship team--a position that has evolved to the current one of Musical Director. He has had the opportunity to work alongside reknown christian artists Claudina Brinn, Eric Perdomo, Eric Lopez, Lilly Goodman, and Nancy Amancio among others. He also works at Aguila Records Studio as a vocal coach and production assistant, while managing the album development process with a consultative approach.

Rafael’s current projects involve the establishment of a not for profit organization (ICMF) with the goal of providing funding for international christian musicians across the world, the expansion of the Renewal Academy of Music, and writing literature for the network churches that will serve as a training tool in the administrative/project management as well as musical fields.
