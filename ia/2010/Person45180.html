Brian Cugelman’s Summary

Over the last decade, I have worked with academic, intergovernmental, non-profit, and for-profit organizations. I have conducted numerous social change campaigns, evaluations, and technical projects for the United Nations and non-profit organizations. My PhD research focused on the design of Internet-based behavioural change interventions. With AlterSpark, I provide research and evaluation services, and I also provide campaign strategy and design consulting.

Brian Cugelman’s Experience

  • Managing Director

    AlterSpark

    (Partnership; Online Media industry)

    September 2006 — Present (4 years 1 month)

    AlterSpark is a campaign research and citizen engagement firm. Its consulting services include customized research, formative research, monitoring, and evaluation of broad social campaigns as well as behavioural change interventions. Recent contracts have included an evaluation of Oxfam GB's Global Climate Change Campaign (with Leitmotiv); evaluation of the United Nations Millennium Campaign (with Leitmotiv & SCRG); reporting as an expert witness in a legal case on digital property theft; and various online projects for research, marketing, outreach, and development.

  • Researcher

    Statistical Cybermetrics Research Group, University of Wolverhampton

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    September 2006 — February 2010 (3 years 6 months)

    My research is focused on the history of social marketing and the role of online media within the context of mass communication campaigns. The empirical part of my research has first focused on the role of website/organizational credibility, audience trust and how these factors can influence online campaign impact. The second part of my research is using meta-analytical techniques to investigate the psychology of behavioural change websites.
