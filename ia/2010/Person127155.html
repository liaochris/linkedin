  • Acting Vice President of Marketing

    Postini, Inc.

    (Privately Held; Information Technology and Services industry)

    2003 — 2006 (3 years )

    I was hired by Postini as Director of Product Marketing to solve the company's problem of me-too positioning in a very crowded anti-spam market (100+ vendors). I immediately repositioned the company and re-wrote all of our messaging to focus on our (novel at the time) SaaS delivery model, starting a three-year evangelism program. Working with our PR/AR team, I placed Postini in the Leader quadrant of Gartner's first Magic Quadrant for our space, fueling awareness, leads and sales. After a year, I was asked to assume responsibility for all of Postini's marketing activities worldwide, including product marketing, demand generation, corporate communications, and channel marketing.. Postini was acquired by Google for $625 million.

  • Vice President of Marketing & Product Management

    Boldfish, Inc.

    (Computer Software industry)

    2000 — 2002 (2 years )

    I was recruited by the CEO of Boldfish to oversee the definition of the company's new, "VPN for email" as Director of Product Management. I was soon promoted to VP and also given responsibility for increasing awareness and leads. Boldfish was sold to Siebel.

  • Director of Product Management and Product Marketing

    Eloquent

    (Computer Software industry)

    1997 — 1999 (2 years )

    At Eloquent, I was recruited to build and lead a product management / product marketing team for the company's streaming media technology and vertical applications (enterprise learning and corporate communications). I created new positioning and messaging, and created a new sales training program to help us beat giants like Real Networks and Microsoft in deals. Eloquent went public in 2000 and was later acquired by Open Text.

  • Senior Product Manager

    Wink Communications

    (Public Company; WINK; Computer Software industry)

    1995 — 1997 (2 years )

    I was one of the first 25 employees at this interactive TV startup, where I was responsible for managing the company's line-up of servers. Working closely with our partners at Time Warner and Toshiba, I gathered requirements, wrote MRDs and PRDs and managed beta tests. After a year, I was promoted and led a team of four product managers responsible for all of the company's product lines, including embedded software for set-top boxes, and interactive application authoring tools, as well as servers. Wink went public in 1999 and was later purchased by Liberty Media.
