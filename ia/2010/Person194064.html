Stephanie Leffler’s Summary

Stephanie Leffler and business partner partner Ryan Noble started an online business selling sun protection products in college. After using a small asp ecommerce company, Website Monster, Leffler and Noble saw great potential in the software as a service model. They turned Website Monster into MonsterCommerce, an ecommerce platform serving over 8,000 small businesses.

MonsterCommerce quickly grew from 10 clients to more than 8,000, with 200 employees supporting annual sales exceeding $20 million. Leffler’s dedication to software innovation and superior customer service helped to fuel the company’s growth.

MonsterCommerce was featured in a variety of e-commerce websites and publications, including Entrepreneur.com, eCommerce-guide.com, Network World Press and Information Week.

The company was voted a SIIA Codie Award finalist for Best E-Commerce Software, recognized as the Fastest Growing Private Company in St. Louis by St. Louis Business Journal and chosen as one of St. Louis’ Top 50 Businesses by St. Louis Commerce Magazine.

Leffler was featured in the St. Louis Business Journal’s “30 Under 30” profile of young entrepreneurs to watch, and was chosen as a finalist for Best Young Entrepreneur in the 2005 Stevie® Awards for Women Entrepreneurs.

Leffler’s latest achievement was successfully spearheading the sale of MonsterCommerce to NetworkSolutions, the nation’s original domain registrar.

Leffler served as President and General Manager of MonsterCommerce, a Network Solutions company for a year and then became Senior Vice President of Partners and New Sales Channels at Network Solutions. In May of 2007, Leffler left Network Solutions.

Today, she and partner Ryan Noble have formed Juggle.com. Juggle went live in February of 2009. The website, which provides facts and information on over 300,000 topics and 1,000,000 products, has grown quickly, accumulating more than 7,000,000 unique visitors since launch.
