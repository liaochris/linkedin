Daniel J Celta’s Summary

Daniel J. Celta Ph.D.

Currently:
General Manager, Central Deal Structuring Group, Shell Trading, Shell Energy North America.

Past:
Project Director - Special Valuations Group, responsible for all Wind Resource Assessment related investment decisions under the Project Valuation team and the CFO Organization of FPL Energy, Juno Beach, Florida. Daniel holds a Ph.D. in Mineral Economics and a MSc. in Operations Research from Colorado School of Mines, and a BS in Chemistry from Universidad Central de Venezuela.

Responsible for the assessment of wind projects net capacity factors for the valuation of investment opportunities. In the past, supported FPLE’s development group in pricing and assessing the value of competitive advantages associated with the optionality around projects. Other responsibilities involve the management of a group of analysts in support of FPLE’s investment as well as asset management decision process.

During November 1998 through April 2000, for PricewaterhouseCoopers as part of the Financial Risk Management team, participating in Mark to Market valuation of storage assets and supply contracts for trading organizations of major energy companies.

Prior to 1994 nine years of experience with PDVSA as a chemical engineer, Project leader for the Development Fuels Department, and head of the Development Fuels Laboratory of the R&D branch of PDVSA. Responsible for a staff of chemical engineers focused on process improvement and downstream refinery optimization projects sponsored by PDVSA.

Daniel J Celta’s Specialties:

Market Structure and Econometric Modeling, Static and Intertemporal-Dynamic Modeling and Analysis for Policy Applications. Linear Programming, Integer, Mixed Interger Programming, and Geometric Programming. Design of Experiments, Analysis of Variance, Regression Analysis, Cointegration and Error Correction Models, Monte Carlo simulation models. Splus/R (Emacs/ESS, NuOpt, bigdata, Finmetrics), C++, Visual Basic, SAS, Excel, Word, PowerPoint, Vi editor, Linux-Fedora, Windows-Vista

Daniel J Celta’s Experience

  • General Manager, Central Deal Structuring

    Shell Trading

    (Public Company; Oil & Energy industry)

    December 2008 — Present (1 year 10 months)

    Deal Structuring, Active Risk Management, Natural Gas, Nat Gas Storage Modeling, Structured Transactions, Financial Instruments, Hedgging/Trading/Investment support.

    “Determination of Revenue Risk Wild Steer Butte Wind Project: As part of the due diligence supporting the project and due to its size (750MW) and contractual structure (off-taker of the project a shell affiliate), an exercise to determine the revenue risk. Consideration about the wind resource and historical market price realization was used to determine the distribution of revenues in support of hedging strategies and transaction structures available.

    Impact of Storm Arrival on The Price of Natural Gas, a Co-integration Model: In an effort to take advantage of early arrival of information and to capture profitable trading opportunities. A forecasting model was design to capture long term as well as short term dynamics while at the same time maintaining consistency and proper treatment of cause and effect.

    Valuation of Storage and Contract Negotiation Support: Supported the management and contract extension of one of the largest storage facility of the US. Due to its size, (96BCF at a $4USD/MMBTU equates about $400Million USD) decisions around the management of the facility involved upper management approval and hence extensive review and due diligence. Financial structures to improve returns on capital included, short term payment deferrals, and collateral lien on the MTM of gas in the ground.

    Modeling of Storage: Built a storage model prototype based on an time spread option approach, this prototype was written in Splus and included a probabilistic adjustment to the optimal injection withdraw path based on the option deltas, ratchets to reflect limitations as the facility gets empty or filled up, forward sales, existing inventory, and other constraints to reflect contractual limitations on the asset.

    FPL Energy

    (Public Company; FPL; Utilities industry)

    Wind Resource Assessment, Renewables, Investment Decision support, Asset Management Decision Support, Capital Markets finance support and due dilligence, Splus, Wind Resource Analytics, Wind Engineering & Site Suitability support.

  • Manager Special Valuations

    FPL Energy

    (Public Company; FPL; Utilities industry)

    September 2002 — December 2006 (4 years 4 months)

    Wind Resource Assessment, Wind resource analytics, Splus, Big data objects, Classical & Operational Wind Resource Assessments, Wind Data Analysis, Site Suitability support.

    “Project and Portfolio Capital Markets Finance of Wind Projects” Development, investment decisions and capital markets finance project support in excess of $3.9 Billion in wind assets distributed across the US. Using a library of models developed in Splus, provided key inputs for the financial valuation, investment decisions, contract negotiations, project development, and capital markets finance of over 3000MW build up during 2003 - 2008.

    “Design and Derivation of the FPLE Wind Index”. Used as an independent measure of the variability of wind resource and to explain variations in earnings from the wind portfolio of FPLE. Based on NOAA available information and used to support monthly internal audit and earnings reconciliation to the wind portfolio as well as support for the quarterly earnings release to the investor community.

    “Degree of Diversification of the FPLE Wind Portfolio”. As to provide upper management with a description of the FPLE wind portfolio, a simulation model was created in order to derive the variability of output and revenues. The model was based on individual variances and a correlation structure derived from long term references near by the actual wind farms and assumed to be representative of the variability of wind resource at the actual sites.

    “Wind Resource Assessment and Derivation of Net capacity factors”. Based on met tower site observation time series, and based on a statistical approach designed and written in Splus, developed library of standard functions, and implemented a methodology for the derivation of capacity factors of Development and Operating projects. These, long term adjusted capacity factors are key inputs for the investment decision pro forma models used in FPLE.

    May 2000 — September 2002 (2 years 5 months)

    “Asset Valuation & Competitive Advantage”: Use of option pricing models based on Monte Carlo simulation techniques to assess the value flexibility around the assets. Assessment of flexibility when the asset has access to multiple power markets and can purchase fuels from different liquid points and/or has dual fuel capability.

    “Option Pricing & Portfolio Benefits”: Assessment of synergies and portfolio benefits of different assets that can be managed together. A combination of rainbow spark spread options used with transportation and Natural gas storage valuation models.

    “Valuation of Peakers, Cost Production Models & Investment Decisions”: Definition of alternative methods for the valuation of Peakers consistent with cost production models and pro formas in support the investment decision process of FPL - Energy group.

    "CNG Transportation": A location spread option model based on a Monte Carlo simulation approach was used to value transportation and natural gas supply contracts. These models were used to audit several contracts MTM including the derivation of the model inputs.

    "Asset Optimization Analysis Nynashamn Refinery": Yield accounting balances and linear programming model sensitivity analyses were performed over the Nynashamn refinery. The results were used to perform an Asset Optimization Analysis over the refinery.
