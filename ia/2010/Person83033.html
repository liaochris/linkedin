Michael J. Hraba’s Summary

Hraba Hospitality Consulting (HHC), a full-service consulting hospitality firm, focuses on eco resorts and green hotels, boutique hotels & NPS lodges. The firm’s goal is to help clients develop an “ethical” brand that will develop their niche within the hotel market. Prior to founding HHC, Michael worked for both large and boutique hotels, gaining extensive operational & management experience in a variety of roles. In addition, Michael has strong knowledge of the rapidly expanding field of social media in relation to hospitality marketing, & has become a leader in this area.

Michael officially began his career in 1998 working for the Hotel Boulderado where he held a variety of departmental managerial positions. In 2000, Michael joined the Wilshire Grand Hotel in Los Angeles, where he played a key role in opening and managing the hotel’s restaurant, nightclub/lounge. Michael then moved into broader management roles from 2002 - 2004 for the Monterey Doubletree (now Portola Hotel & Spa) & the Carmel Mission Inn, working closely with customers and managing several large teams. This management experience enabled him to move into a Project Manager role for Passport Resorts & their newest property, Cavallo Point - The Lodge at the Golden Gate. From 2005-2008, he oversaw the project, acting as Passport's PM for the design & build of the eco campus. As the representative of the management company, Michael facilitated design, construction, interiors, and overall operations for the project. Following this experience, Michael felt well positioned to found Hraba Hospitality Consulting in 2008. HHC consults with its clients to support growth and development of new and existing properties. Among others, clients include The Post Ranch Inn, Big Sur, CA, Cavallo Point, Sausalito, CA, and the new Allison Hotel & Spa in Oregon’s wine country.

Michael received a Bachelor of Arts in Communication from the University of Colorado, Boulder in 1998.

Michael J. Hraba’s Specialties:

To use my excitement and creative energy, my years of guest service, and my robust knowledge of hospitality to help management groups and boutique hotels aggressively develop an ethical brand that will develop their niche within the hotel market.
