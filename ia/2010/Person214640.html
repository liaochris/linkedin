Wendy Jane Carrel’s Summary

Wendy Jane Carrel dedicates her energies to authors and publishers, as well as to green, sustainable projects. She draws on 10 years of business planning, book and script development knowledge (writing and editorial), traditional and new media experience, negotiation skills, and a large network of work relationships. Her branding and identity talents, along with her site content, Internet marketing, and Web 2.0 savvy bring worldwide awareness and opportunities for her esteemed, expert clients.

Wendy Jane began her career in the movie business as an Emmy Award-winning documentary filmmaker in the U.S., and as a production manager in Egypt. She moved up to positions as an acquisitions agent for European film distributors, and then worked six years as VP Special Projects and Research for an international media company which brokered a score of US-European broadcasting and other deals. She was a literary development executive, and became a sought after coach for writers. She has served as a film business lecturer at film schools and universities, and as a seminar host, teacher, juror, and blogger at international film festivals on five continents.

For five years she served low income families and seniors as a creative and enthusiastic public affairs spokesperson and program administrator representing the non-profit ICUC (see pico.org), Riverside County, and the City of Coachella. Since 2003 she has been a health and mental health grant reviewer and site visitor for the Regional Access Project Foundation in California’s Coachella Valley. For two years, she was Assistant VP Corporate Communications for Birtcher, a commercial real estate company with high profile properties.

Wendy Jane participates in educational events with a number of national and regional publisher’s associations, and at book expos and fairs. She is a member of Los Angeles Bioneers (dedicated to re-imagining the future of our planet), Media Mavens, and the Palm Springs Green Scene.

Wendy Jane Carrel’s Specialties:

Book business planning and development, content development, editorial, branding and identity, Internet marketing, international contract negotiations, media relations, Web 2.0 applications, web content writing, representation. All projects related to health, mental health, green living, sustainability (clean energy, food and gardens, housing, self care, water), and, building communities.
