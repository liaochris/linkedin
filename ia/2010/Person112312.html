Kathy Simmons’s Summary

Kathy Simmons joined NETSHARE in 1992, just six months after they opened the doors. She is the guiding force behind the company and the architect of NETSHARE’s business strategy.

Simmons is responsible for the ongoing evolution of NETSHARE as a web-based community for executives, both employed and in transition, who want to evolve and stay current with all facets of executive career management. This includes building a personal brand, broadening networks with other like minded professionals as well as tactical advice and information on growing in one’s chosen profession.

Since assuming the role of CEO in 2004, Simmons has made it her mission to help NETSHARE members harness the Internet to advance their careers, all the while recognizing the need for personal one on one service for their members.

Under her leadership, NETSHARE has evolved into the Web address where executives go to meet all of their career management needs. Prior to joining NETSHARE, Simmons spent 15 years in customer service and technology positions in the telecommunications, construction and healthcare industries. Her eclectic background includes stints as a buyer for an international trading company and founder of an administrative services center in Tehran, Iran.

Simmons’ passion for connecting people has served NETSHARE well. She recalls the time when she took a call from a recruiter who was having a tough time filling an international assignment. The client had very specific multi-lingual and cultural requirements. Earlier that day, the perfect resume came into NETSHARE by a new member. Calls were made and within two weeks the job was filled by the NETSHARE member.

Simmons’ commentary frequently appears in major media outlets. She has been a guest on MSNBC’s Morning Call and has been widely quoted in national publications such as The Wall Street Journal, The New York Times,and Smart Money Magazine discussing issues related to executive employment.
