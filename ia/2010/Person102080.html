John Fossella PhD MBA’s Summary

Inefficiency and disparity in healthcare access is a complex problem that increasingly weighs on society. Management of moral hazard (consumers), demand inducement (providers) and adverse selection (payors) across macroeconomic cycles (investors) has proven difficult.

Science- and social entrepreneurship-based solutions based on personal genomic information may, someday, help resolve disparities and inefficiencies in healthcare when coupled with socially responsible, consumer-driven (health 2.0) business models.

Specifically, my focus is on mental health and wellness.

Please visit my blog - genes2brains2mind2me"dot"com - to find out more about the role of genetic biomarkers and social entrepreneurship in an ever-changing mental healthcare and consumer-directed care environment.
