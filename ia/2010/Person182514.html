Sarah Currier’s Experience

  • Consultant

    Sarah Currier Consultancy

    (E-Learning industry)

    January 2009 — Present (1 year 5 months)

    I'm a freelance consultant specialising in e-learning resource management and sharing, with a particular interest in metadata, controlled vocabularies, repositories, and the use of Web 2.0 tools for resource sharing and community development. Requirements gathering, development, management, implementation, and evaluation are all on offer.

  • Product Manager

    Intrallect Ltd

    (Privately Held; E-Learning industry)

    September 2006 — January 2009 (2 years 5 months)

    I managed the development of Intrallect's learning object repository system, intraLibrary. I also supported Intrallect's customers and carried out consultancy on issues around educational resource management, repository implementation and management, metadata, controlled vocabularies, and interoperability standards. I was (and am) Co-Moderator of the Dublin Core Education Community and participated in other relevant standards activities, e.g. the IEEE LTSC committee working on the IEEE LOM, and IMS's Common Cartridge work.

  • JISC-CETIS Repositories Programme Support Officer

    University of Strathclyde (JISC-CETIS)

    (E-Learning industry)

    November 2005 — August 2006 (10 months)

    I gave research support, guidance and advice to the e-learning projects of the JISC Repositories Programme. This position ran in parallel to my position as CD-LOR Project Manager, which was also part of this Programme. See: http://www.jisc.ac.uk/whatwedo/programmes/programme_digital_repositories.aspx for the Programme and http://www.ukoln.ac.uk/repositories/digirep/index/Repositories_Research for the Support Team which is now known as the JISC Repositories Research Team.

    During this time I also sat on the JISC Repositories and Preservation Advisory Group: http://www.jisc.ac.uk/aboutus/committees/working_groups/repositories_and_preservation.aspx

  • Project Manager, CD-LOR Project

    University of Strathclyde

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    August 2005 — August 2006 (1 year 1 month)

    I secured JISC funding for the Community Dimensions of Learning Object Repositories project, in collaboration with Dr. Charles Duncan at Intrallect and Prof. Allison Littlejohn (then) at Dundee University. This project investigated ways in which the communities that exist or grow around learning object repositories may influence the development of the repositories themselves. The aim was to produce guidelines that would start new repository developments off on the right foot: that of examining the nature and needs of the communities they are being set up to serve. The project ran for another year after I left to take up my subsequent post at Intrallect, under the able hand of Colin Milligan, and proved to be a success. More information may be found on the CD-LOR website at: http://www.academy.gcal.ac.uk/cd-lor/ Intrallect also produced as part of the project an openly available Web tool for querying repositories using SRU.

  • Senior Research Fellow

    JISC-CETIS Pedagogical Vocabularies Project

    (Educational Institution; 1001-5000 employees; Research industry)

    August 2005 — December 2005 (5 months)

    Produced two reports in collaboration with the PedVocabs Working Group: one surveying existing pedagogical vocabularies and one surveying technologies and standards for supporting vocabularies. Also submitted recommendations to JISC based on these reports. Documents available at: http://www.jisc.ac.uk/elp_vocabularies.html

    This work has subsequently fed into the Dublin Core Education Application Profile work currently under way, which I am Co-Moderating with Diane Hillmann at Cornell University: http://dublincore.org/educationwiki/

  • Repository Manager (Librarian), SIESWE Learning Exchange (then known as Stor Curam)

    University of Strathclyde

    (Educational Institution; 1001-5000 employees; Higher Education industry)

    January 2004 — July 2005 (1 year 7 months)

    I managed the repository components for the Stor Curam project, which aimed to serve the nine universities in Scotland delivering social work education with a repository of digital learning materials. Stor Curam was the pilot phase of this service, and was successful in leading to a fully centrally-funded service, which then became known as the SIESWE Learning Exchange. Subsequently, SIESWE changed its name to IRISS. For their current work, see their website: http://www.iriss.ac.uk/learnx

  • CETIS Educational Content SIG Co-ordinator

    University of Strathclyde (CETIS)

    (Educational Institution; 1001-5000 employees; E-Learning industry)

    July 2002 — January 2004 (1 year 7 months)

    I co-ordinated the activities of the CETIS Educational Content Special Interest Group. For current info on the work of this group see: http://wiki.cetis.ac.uk/Educational_Content

    Included producing briefing papers on IMS Content Packaging, Simple Sequencing and Learning Design specifications, assisting with CETIS Code Bash events, advising and supporting those implementing educational specifications and standards, taking part in standards body activities, and carrying out research into metadata quality for e-learning.

  • Research Fellow

    (E-Learning industry)

    - JISC-funded study evaluating JISC 5/99 Learning & Teaching Programme educational content for reusability and interoperability as learning objects.
    - Developing methodology & evaluation criteria; survey of projects; interviews; evaluation of content (including metadata); dissemination; final report & recommendations.

    INSPIRAL Project, CDLR, University of Strathclyde
