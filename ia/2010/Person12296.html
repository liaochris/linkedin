Andrew Trovaioli’s Summary

From the alarm clock that wakes them, surfing the web or even perhaps watching the movie that puts them to sleep at night - like never before, we now have the opportunity to communicate with consumers everywhere and in every way.
Everything is a potential canvas and an improvised pulpit.
We have both the technology and know how to cocoon them with our messages 24/7 literally. But in our industry's race to innovate and clients to rush to convert during this Economic Downturn, we need to safeguard our one true asset.
Consumer Understanding.
Otherwise as a result, we risk bombarding them into rejection.
Certainly the 30 second television commercial and full page press ad are no longer a must have opinion for all clients. However, that doesn't mean the migration to "on-line advertising or hyper-tag messaging" is anymore as effective.
And just because you can advertise in a toilet or on the side of a popcorn bucket, doesn't mean its the right solution.
We need to ensure that as an industry we are not seduced by our own cleverness.
In fact, at what point do we cross that line, that blurred line between ingenious communication and and intrusive marketing?
While every consumer is different, surely they are united by one human truth.
If its not relevant for them, they don't want to know about it. From the most advert hard sell to the cleverest stealth campaign...
Understanding the consumer is much more than knowing how and when to talk to them. Its also about knowing when not to.
Solutions need options, but options are not solutions.
This is why the usual bland, inane, clichéd advertising, founded on half truths and general misconceptions, will be given very short shrift. Integrity, ingenuity and originality will be values that are and will be most sought after and rewarded.
