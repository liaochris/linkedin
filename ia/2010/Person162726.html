Peter Friedman’s Summary

I live in Cleveland and commute to Detroit as a Visiting Assistant Professor at the University of Detroit Mercy Law School, where I teach Contracts. After practicing law as a commercial litigator in New York City from 1984 to 1996, I joined the faculty at Case Western Reserve University School of Law. At Case Western, I primarily taught Legal Analysis and Writing and Contracts; I also taught Civil Procedure and a number of courses in civil litigation practice. I also, since 2007, have had a regular job teaching U.S. Contract Law as a Visiting Professor in the Honors Masters Program in Law at the Universiteit van Amsterdam in the Netherlands in May and June. During the two previous summers in Cleveland I taught students in an Honors Masters Program in Law jointly sponsored by Nyenrode Business Universiteit and the Universiteit van Amsterdam.

In addition to commercial law, my professional interests include copyright law and other intersections between law and creative endeavors.

I write a blog named "Ruling Imagination: Law and Creativity," which explores the ways law affects creative endeavors and the ways creativity informs the practice of law. The blog is located at http://blogs.geniocity.com/friedman/. For my first year contract class at Detroit Mercy Law School, I write 1L Contracts, a blog located at http://firstyearcontracts.blogspot.com/
