Stephanie Dickison’s Summary

My book, The 30 Second Commute: A Non Fiction Comedy About Writing and Working From Home, about my career as pop culture, book, music and restaurant critic is also available in ebook + audiobook formats.

I have published hundreds + hundreds of non-fiction pieces including articles, interviews, essays, columns, profiles, features and reviews in international magazines and newspapers and am ready to take on any assignment!

I have written for The Toronto Star, Metro Newspaper (Restaurant Critic and Columnist), PCWorld.ca, MacWorldCanada.ca, alive, Canadian Running (Celebrity Columnist), BizMe , 2:The Magazine for Couples, BizBash, Lifestyle, Muskoka, The Writer (Contributing Editor, Columnist), eatdrink (Food Columnist), Quill & Quire, WCities, Organic Lifestyle, Positively Green, pan (Editor, Columnist & Feature Writer), Write Magazine (Assistant Editor & Reviews Editor), Film International Journal, Surface & Symbol (Arts Columnist), Pages, Beyond Fitness, TIDINGS, Paste, Washington Asia Press Newspaper, Canadian Homestead, and Impact, to name just a few.

I have over a decade of professional journalistic experience.

I am the restaurant critic for Toronto Metro News. I am also Co-Editor of Pan Magazine. I write about food for a number of websites and publications.I have interviewed many famous chefs during my career as food writer/restaurant critic and continue to be requested for chef interviews and profiles.

I also specialize in celebrity interviews. I have interviewed Jann Arden, Jully Black, Michael Holmes, Colin & Justin, Ivana Santilli, K-OS, etc.

I am an avid and reliable blogger and social media expert.

In addition to the areas above, I write in all areas of technology, travel, health/fitness, beauty/fashion, design/style as well.

Samples of my writing can be found at my website. I always meet my deadlines and take the job of writing seriously, but have fun with the content.

    (Writing and Editing industry)

    February 2010 — Present (6 months)

    The thoughts of Stephanie Dickison, a food-obsessed writer, cook and restaurant reviewer writing from home.

    I started a food site because not only am I out reviewing restaurants and writing about food for a living, but I’m spending much of my “free” time cooking, shopping for ingredients and basically thinking about what I’m going to make next.

    So I hope you’ll follow me and my little adventure here. I will not be posting pictures of everything I make step-by-step, or what I eat at every meal or anything like that. But I will be sharing TMI – what I bought, what I made, what I read.

    I figure if I’m thinking about this stuff, someone out there must be too. I can’t be the only one questioning whether next time I make lime chicken I should finish it with grains of paradise or pepitas.

  • Radio Show Host

    CKLN Radio 88.1 FM

    (Broadcast Media industry)

    2009 — Present (1 year )

    "In Other Words" is a radio show about writing, broadcast every Tuesday at 2 pm, with 5 rotating hosts.

    My show, Portraits on Paper, is every third Tuesday of the month, and itis all about biography, memoir and real life stories. I think that capturing truths and memories onto paper and into spoken word is one of the most powerful forms of writing. Books of this nature continue to sell because we are fascinated by stories that we can learn from, that we can relate to and that we can experience as if they are our own.

    I am often asked about the different between memoir and biography. I’ll let Gore Vidal get this one. He said, “A memoir is how one remembers one’s own life, while an autobiography is history, requiring research, dates, facts double-checked.”

    By focusing on memoirs and biographies, we get to speak about the writing, but also get to delve into the stories of politicians, advocates, environmentalists, singer-songwriters, poets, filmmakers, screenwriters, spoken word artists, visual artists, people from the underground, people otherwise unseen and unheard, changemakers, thrillseekers, protestors, people who have lost or found something and people who have done something worth writing about.

    Each show I will bring you guests from different fields and professions, all in the memoir realm. As well, I will tell you about new and old biographies and memoirs that might be of interest and any literary events that are happening around town.

    Upcoming guests include Kristen den Hartog, Catherine Gildiner and other exciting authors and memoirists.
