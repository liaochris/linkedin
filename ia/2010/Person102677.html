John Lawson’s Experience

  • Executive Vice President

    ION Media Networks

    (Privately Held; ION; Broadcast Media industry)

    March 2008 — Present (2 years 2 months)

    In his role at ION Media Networks, John Lawson is driving policy strategy for restructuring and re-launching the nation's largest broadcast network for the digital environment. He is leading initiatives in distribution of programming, including new digital networks focused on children and wellness, and advocating new regulatory policies that would increase minority ownership of media. Lawson is pursuing business development around digital television, including new content alliances and ION's deployment of a commercial mobile video service in 2009. He is also a major participant in broadcaster efforts for the successful transition to digital television.

    Lawson came to ION from the Association of Public Television Stations (APTS) in Washington, DC, where he was president and CEO. There, he secured federal funding, policy support, and business alliances that ensured the successful digital conversion of public television and the expansion of its services.

  • President and CEO

    Association of Public Television Stations

    (Broadcast Media industry)

    April 2002 — March 2008 (6 years )

    CEO of the Washington-DC advocacy arm of public television in the United State.

  • Founder and CEO

    Convergence Services, Inc.

    (Government Relations industry)

    April 1993 — March 2001 (8 years )

    Owned and operated a successful technology-oriented government relations and consulting firm serving clients in public broadcasting, education, and the telecommunications industry. Managed the entire client engagement cycle, from defining scope of work and negotiating fees, to providing services and producing deliverables. Key clients included PBS, Westinghouse, Cisco Systems, and the JASON Foundation.

    Results
    • Instrumental in assisting PBS, The JASON Project, and other clients in securing nearly $200 million for technology projects from the U.S. Department of Education, NASA, NOAA, and other agencies.
    • Produced and moderated several of the most widely viewed satellite videoconferences (e.g., Funding Educational Technology) ever distributed by PBS.
    • Led DTV business development for public television stations in the top-30 markets.
