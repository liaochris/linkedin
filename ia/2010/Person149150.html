Michael Butler’s Summary

My background includes a 22-year Navy career in heavy industry that 'phase shifted' to software development, embedded controls, and business re-engineering. Upon retirement, I was manager for Rolls Royce Controls and various program management opportunities at multiple companies.

In 2000, I returned to government service at the Defense Manpower Data Center (DMDC) and participated in the introduction and deployment of the Common Access Card. After seven years at DMDC, I was named Program Manager for GSA's Managed Service Office (MSO) providing identity and integration services to 70 different agencies and contributed to starting up a complex cross-agency program.

In 2009 and 2010 I was employed at the NIST (to include a six month OMB detail at the e-Gov Office) with concentration on identity issues, immigration reform, national strategy, and standards.

In August 2010, I returned to the Defense Manpower Data Center to head the Identity Services Directorate.
