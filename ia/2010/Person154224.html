Nick Rodites, AVA’s Summary

Helping successful business owners, working with their advisors, plan for the single most critical financial event of their life - The transition out of their business. With at least a few years of advance planning, and a team of qualified advisors, business owners can significantly increase their business value and odds of completing a successful exit.

  • Certified Exit Planner

    Exigent Plans, Inc

    (Privately Held; Investment Management industry)

    April 2009 — June 2010 (1 year 3 months)

    A Certified Exit Planner (CExP) specializes in assiting business owners with planning their eventual business exit. Whether a business owner plans to transfer their ownership to the next generation, employees, or a third party, a Certified Exit Planner, will help ensure that the owner maximizes their success. This is done by thoroughly assessing an owner's objectives, coordinating a team of advisors (CPA, Attorney, Financial/Insurance Advisor), and creating a written plan that addresses all of the objectives. Too often, without proper time and planning, business owners leave money on the table, pay too much in taxes, and lose control of acheiving their hard earned objectives. According to the Small Business Administration (SBA), only 3 in 10 established businesses ($3 - $10 million in sales) successfully transfer. The number one reason is a lack of planning.
