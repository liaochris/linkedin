Oscar Callejas’s Summary

Oscar’s background in the hospitality industry began at just 16 when he started working at the famous National Hotel on South Beach.What started out as a Summer job to raise money for a stereo system, transformed into a passion for helping and serving people that continues to this day.
Oscar moved quickly through the industry and by the age of 20 had become one of the youngest managers at a luxury hotel while at the historic Mayfair House Hotel in Coconut Grove, FL. Over a period of four years at this property, he was recognized and nominated several times for numerous service awards, including the prestigious Inn Key Award – the highest honor bestowed by the Greater Miami and the Beaches Hotel Association. When the property changed ownership in 2004, he helped facilitate the transition from the Operations side.
Soon after, he returned to South Beach where he helped manage a 355 room resort – raising service scores from 70% to over 90% in the first 4 months. Following this early success, he was promoted to a recently acquired historic Art Deco boutique hotel. At this property, Oscar was responsible for overseeing the transition of ownership and implementing service standards while preparing for a multi-million dollar renovation.Working closely with local interest groups and government, the renovation was approved (quite a difficult task for a historic hotel in a historic district) and plans to convert it to a Kimpton boutique hotel were finalized.
In June of 2007, Oscar joined Driftwood Hospitality and began work on another renovation project where he again helped manage the transition of the newly acquired hotel and oversee operations and revenue management during renovation to hotel Indigo by Intercontinental Hotels Group – a multi-million dollar overhaul that included the creation of new public areas and the gutting of all rooms. In August 2008, this hotel officially opened as the first Hotel Indigo in Miami, FL to a sold-out house.
