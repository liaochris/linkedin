Michael Slater’s Experience

  • LI-WIM Systems & Training Intern

    Air Liquide America

    (Public Company; Chemicals industry)

    June 2008 — Present (1 year 11 months)

    I intern for Air Liquide Large Industries in the Worldwide Industrial Management department.

    My first duties involved taking training programs that were written in Adobe Flash and copying them into a program called Lectora so they could be easily translated and distributed in multiple languages.

    With my strong background in Computers, my talents and expertise was quickly noticed by upper management. After showing off the web browser Firefox to a high executive which ran an internal web application over 7 times faster than in IE, I quickly became the local tech expert in the department to contact.

    My next great break occurred when I started a particular batch of training in flash. I wrote a program in Java that analyzed the XML structure of the Flash text files and automatically prepared them for localization. This completely bypassed the need to put the training in Lectora and after my program was written, a few clicks later we sent out over 35 modules of training to be translated. I defeated the deadline of this project by 9 months.

    Around this time is when the end of my summer came, and supposedly the end of my internship. However, starting with a joke from an upper manager saying that they should just higher me as a regular employee, I was asked to stay on as a part time intern. I was given special permission to work via VPN access from school. Most of my work consisted of maintaining and improving on the various projects I had done in the summer.

    After my first semester of college I was assigned another duty: to completely revamp and maintain the department intranet website. As any web developer knows, these kind of projects never really end, and this is still one of my main functions.

    Among other things I consider myself mostly a Computer and Technology consultant for my department. I still do all the things I was originally hired to do as well as much more.
