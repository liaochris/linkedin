Judith Porter’s Summary

I have over 25 years of experience working with non-profits in the Dallas metroplex area and throughout the state of Texas, particularly in the area of hunger relief. I began in Youth ministry, did a year in pediatric AIDS (volunteer coordinator for Bryan's House) and have helped fund many local non-profits that benefit women and children in the downtown dallas area. My work has included teaching about hunger relief and training volunteers how to safely work with HIV-positive children and mothers. You may have seen my guest articles in the Dallas Morning News Viewpoints section or the Letters to the Editors section, and for the past eleven years my students from Bishop Lynch in the "letters" section: I teach them how to write a good, publishable opinion letter.

Judith Porter’s Specialties:

I am experienced in getting volunteers out in great numbers for special events: picking crops in the fields of East Texas for the hungry in the inner city, providing behind-the-scenes help for large charity auctions in upscale hotels, etc. I used to do grant-writing for a living and now teach my students how to write persuasively and am helping my senior students gain access to the colleges of their choosing and garner large scholarships to these schools through my rec letters.
