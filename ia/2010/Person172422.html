Richard Diamond’s Summary

Richard is currently Principal of RM Diamond Group LLC a retail, product development and sales consultancy based in Boca Raton, Florida. The company represents a number of marquis manufacturers and importers in addition to generating its own proprietary housewares and furniture designs for the office and mass channels. Prior to that Diamond was Vice President of Merchandising for Office Depot Inc. This role leveraged Diamond’s broad experience in hardlines sourcing and merchandising and enabled him to hire and lead a new team of merchants. He successfully created strategies in home and office furniture, lighting, cleaning and breakroom supplies that reinvigorated these businesses in the 1150 stores, catalogs, B2B and websites representing $1B of Office Depots $16B in global revenue. Diamond’s passion for product, strategy and negotiation began in 1980 when he joined the then $1B Burdines, division of what is today Macy’s. He quickly become VP of Furniture, Floor Coverings and Bedding. Diamond led the acclaimed implementation of the free-standing furniture store effort – a strategy that was taken nationwide by Macy’s. Based on his success in growing the $100M Burdines business he was then recruited to run the $800M furniture division at OfficeMax, Inc. After many years in large companies Diamond felt the need to strike out on his own and became a principal in two start-ups. The first – H2O Furnishings – developed and sold novelty furniture. After 2 years as EVP he was aggressively recruited by Harvey Lewis Designs, a second round start-up focused on home storage products and Swarovski crystal/silver plate holiday ornaments. Assuming the COO/Asia role, Diamond moved to Hong Kong for two years to manage the China staff, and the manufacturing, packaging, design and transportation operations. Diamond graduated from the University of Pennsylvania’s Wharton School.
