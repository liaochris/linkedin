Amanda Crater’s Summary

With a background in journalism, branding and marketing communications, Amanda Crater has found her calling connecting movers and shakers in the green industry through her work as founder of CraterCom, a public relations and branding firm for green businesses. She helps her clients - who range from an environmental consulting firm to green business conference organizers to the largest solar installer in Southern California - gain media coverage, utilize social media and articulate their brands through compelling marketing messaging.

Before starting her company in June 2009, Amanda worked with public companies in the alternative energy sector as a media relations specialist for a global investor relations agency. Her background includes writing for The Orange County Register, the San Francisco Bay Guardian and two years at The UCLA Foundation. As an international spokesperson for the "Books not Bomb" student strike in 2003, Amanda appeared on CNN Headline News at the age of 20 and was quoted extensively in national news outlets. More recently she appeared on CNN Local Edition and has been quoted in the Houston Business Journal and Career Doctor. As a public speaker on green entrepreneurship, Amanda is also a contributing writer to several green news sites including Greenopolis, Environmental Leader and Triple Pundit. She holds a BA in American Studies with a focus on ecology from the University of California, Berkeley and loves modern dance, ballet, yoga, pilates and the ocean.
