    For the past three years I worked at the American Chemical Society (ACS) as a Senior Graphic Designer and functioned as the Art Director for ACS Chemical Biology, a monthly print/web publication. In this role I designed artwork for featured research and generated illustrations in both web and print formats. This involved working closely with authors, editors and ACS staff to turn complex scientific concepts into easily understandable illustrations. I also produced promotional materials for the journal. In short, I was involved in every aspect of the design and style of the print and online journal.

    At the ACS I also performed extensive work for the Marketing Department. This role involved designing and coding informational e-communications, development of logos, as well as the design of web and print advertisements.

    Prior to moving to the Washington, DC area I worked at Gradient Corporation, an environmental and risk sciences consulting firm. I began my career at Gradient as a Receptionist, and I was promoted several times, to Graphic Artist/Senior Text Processor. During my tenure at Gradient I used InDesign, Illustrator, Photoshop and PowerPoint regularly to create technical illustrations, marketing materials, posters, and exhibits in support of litigation projects. I also managed the newly formed Graphics Group at the firm.
