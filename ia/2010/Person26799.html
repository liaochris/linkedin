Brad Stephens’s Summary

Brad Stephens is an award-winning actor based in Dallas/Fort Worth. Brad was honored this year with a Column Award for Best Supporting Actor in a Play (non-equity) for his portrayal of George Deever in the ICT MainStage production of Arthur Miller’s ALL MY SONS.

Brad recently completed a critically-acclaimed run as Curly McLain in OKLAHOMA! at Artisan Center Theater. He also channeled the darker side of Paul McCartney in READY TEDDY, a film awarded Best Dark Comedy at the Trail Dance Film Festival in January. Brad been featured in commercials for Pepsi, Southwest Airlines and Joe’s Crab Shack.

Other recent stage credits include Benedick in MUCH ADO ABOUT NOTHING at Stolen Shakepseare Guild; Juror #7 in 12 ANGRY MEN; Nanki-Poo in Gilbert & Sullivan’s The MIKADO and Jeff in Lerner & Loewe’s BRIGADOON, both at Artisan Center Theater; and Robert in DON'T DRESS FOR DINNER at ICT MainStage, a performance which led one critic to declare him “a deft physical comedian reminiscent of the late John Ritter in his prime.¹“

In addition to performing, Brad is also a filmmaker and accomplished editor with more than 20 years of film and television experience. Brad’s clientele have included Cox Communications, Universal Studios, Pie Town Productions, HGTV and TLC. Having assisted numerous feature editors on independent productions, Brad has also written, produced and directed a variety of projects including the festival favorite short film ANIMOSITY. More recently he served as associate producer, assistant director and editor on SHROUD, an independent HD feature based in Texas.

--------------------
¹ Douglas Dodasovich, EDGE Contributor – http://wp.me/pn4d5-gd
