  • Product Director

    Kaplan

    (Public Company; WPO; Education Management industry)

    June 1993 — August 1995 (2 years 3 months)

    Managed product development teams. Rolled out new courses in record time. Chosen by CEO to lead company-wide re-engineering effort called "Project Footprint" that totally reinvented Kaplan's test prep products: In-class hours were reduced from 40 to 24; learning resources were shifted out of the classroom and into homework via my Preview/Classroom/Review system; new test strategies were added; students were allowed to keep their lesson booklets for the first time, etc. All of these and other innovations form the basis of current Kaplan test prep product.

    Invented strategy for "Pattern Identification" question type that forced the Educational Testing Service -- for the only time in the history of American standardized testing -- to remove the entire section from the exam and admit publically that I "broke the code and published it, so we are removing the questions from the test" (ETS to LSU Reveille, 1993).

    Also broke the code on both computer test security protocals and scoring algorithm, forcing massive revisions. ETS pulled all computer testing for months and implemented the changes I and others recommended—adding more questions, rotating pools, etc. ETS sued, though the charges were nearly all dismissed. Court documents show that ETS began calling me "The Antichrist"
