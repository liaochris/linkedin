Henry Moorhead’s Experience

  • Chief Financial Officer

  • Chief Financial Officer

    CNL Commercial Finance

    (Financial Services industry)

    August 2000 — July 2005 (5 years )

    Mr. Moorhead co-founded and was Chief Financial Officer of CNL Commercial Finance, LLC (“CCF”) from inception in 2000 until sell to Lehman Brothers in late 2004. CCF was a securitization-funded small business lender focused on loans collateralized by owner occupied, multi-purpose commercial real estate and was founded through the spin-off of the small business lending division of the Bank of Yorba Linda in southern California. Building on the decades-long success of the BYL team, CCF grew a national platform with a $1.5 billion loan portfolio and offices across the country with wholesale and retail operations originating both conventional and SBA loans. During this period, Mr. Moorhead was instrumental in the evolution of small business loans as a securitization asset class and in doing so, facilitated ownership of business-related real estate by small businesses nationwide. Following four years of disciplined growth, the origination and servicing platforms and the non-securitized loan portfolio of CCF were acquired by Lehman Brothers. Mr. Moorhead was responsible for all financial aspects of the Company including investor and rating agency activities, programmatic loan pricing, GAAP and tax accounting, and development of the Company’s securitization and interest rate hedging programs.

    Subsequent to the sale of CCF to Lehman Brothers, Mr. Moorhead continued with the CNL family of companies into 2006 focusing on integration of residual assets into affiliated companies and resulting accounting and tax matters. Upon completion of integration activities in early 2005, he joined the former President of CCF in seeking investments in financial services companies suitable for rapid value creation through judicious growth and limited equity capital. While several opportunities were pursued through various stages of diligence, activities were terminated in 2006 as suitable opportunities matching the risk/reward/funding requirements of CNL were not available.

  • Treasurer

    CNL Financial Corp

    (Real Estate industry)

    1998 — 2000 (2 years )

    Mr. Moorhead began his career with the CNL family of companies in 1997 as Treasurer of CNL Financial Services, Inc. ("CFS"). In that position he was responsible for all treasury and capital markets activities for this securitization funded real estate lender. He led CNL's inaugural securitization of franchise restaurant loans at the height of the 1998 credit crisis and was subsequently intimately involved in the merger of CFS with CNL's affiliated restaurant REIT.

    Following on the success of CFS, Mr. Moorhead and the former President of CFS pursued investment opportunities in financial services companies suitable for rapid value creation through judicious growth and limited equity capital. These efforts led to the founding of CCF in August, 2000.

  • Manager of Finance

    Seminole Electric Cooperative

    (Utilities industry)

    1983 — 1997 (14 years )

    As Manager of Finance at the nation's second largest electirc cooperative, Mr. Moorhead was responsible for all financing activity for a $1.3B coal-fired power plant and related transmission facilities. His primary focus was on structuring and administeration of complex tax-oriented financing transactions enabling the transfer of tax benefits to entities which could fully utilize such. In this role he worked on over $3B in financings including sale-leasebacks, letters of credit, secured and unsecured lines of credit, government guaranteed debt, tax-benefit transfers, defeased lease-leasebacks, and tax-exempt revenue bonds. He also administered and was responsible for investments in, and regulatory reporting for, the Company's nuclear decommissioning trust fund.
