Jonathan Mallin’s Experience

  • Amateur Blogger (Hobby)

    TechBottle Blog

    (Writing and Editing industry)

    November 2009 — Present (1 year 3 months)

    In his spare time, Jon enjoys reading and writing about technology. Jon has a technology blog, TechBottle, that covers many hot technology topics. His primary interests are Google services, cloud computing, mobile devices, social networking and alternative operating systems. Jon has written many popular blog posts that have drawn thousands of readers from around the world by promoting his blog on Google Buzz, Twitter and Facebook. Working with cellular carriers, Jon reviews upcoming mobile devices on the blog. He also enjoys tinkering with WordPress and tweaks the blog he designed on a regular basis.

  • Attorney (Solo Practitioner)

    QDRO Professionals PLLC

    (Law Practice industry)

    August 2008 — Present (2 years 6 months)

    Jon took over his father's practice and prepares Qualified Domestic Relations Orders (QDROs) for the division of retirement accounts and pensions for divorcing couples and divorce lawyers. This work involves the intersection of law and actuarial mathematics principles. Jon is a member by invitation of the national organization Qualified Domestic Relations Orders Education Society (QDRONES).

  • Associate

    Foley & Lardner LLP

    (Privately Held; Law Practice industry)

    August 2004 — August 2008 (4 years 1 month)

    Jon worked on a wide variety of projects for a diverse group of clients in Foley's Transactional and Securities Department which blends corporate and regulatory legal work. He represented public and private companies in virtually all areas of business law--general corporate matters, mergers, acquisitions/divestitures, joint ventures, corporate governance, securities registrations and SEC compliance. Jon is a talented legal writer capable of distilling complicated legal issues into understandable executive summaries which were helpful to decision makers. He drafted a series of hot topic client newsletters. Jon was also involved in SEC compliance matters which included preparing, reviewing and filing periodic and current reports (10-K, 10-Q and 8-K), proxy statements and other required SEC filings.

    While at Foley, Jon developed particular expertise in Exchange-Traded Funds (ETFs). He was instrumental in the registration of the first ETF to track a foreign currency. He was honored to participate in the opening bell ring at the New York Stock Exchange to celebrate the IPO of the Rydex Euro Currency Trust in December 2005. Jon subsequently registered additional currency ETFs covering most of the major world currencies. Jon also registered the first actively-managed ETF with the SEC, the Bear Stearns Current Yield Fund. Jon's work involved, among other laws, the Securities Act of 1933, the Securities Exchange Act of 1934, the Investment Company Act of 1940 and the Investment Advisors Act of 1940.

    When Bear Stearns collapsed and the financial markets turned in 2008, virtually all ETF projects came to a halt, some permanently. The economy in and around Detroit was hard hit by the decline in the automobile industry. Consequently, Jon's attraction to Foley and vice versa diminished and they parted ways in August 2008.

  • Senior Vice President

    Pension Division by QDRO

    (Law Practice industry)

    May 2003 — August 2004 (1 year 4 months)

    Jon worked along side his father, an experienced actuary, preparing QDROs while he studied for the Michigan bar examination. Although this was only intended to be transitional employment designed to bridge the gap between his experience in New York and his next position in Detroit, Jon really applied himself and learned significant expertise. In addition to drafting legal documents and preparing actuarial valuations, Jon handled all of the computer systems and the corporate intranet without any outside assistance.

  • Associate

    Dewey Ballantine LLP n/k/a Dewey Lebouef LLP

    (Partnership; 1001-5000 employees; Law Practice industry)

    April 2001 — April 2003 (2 years 1 month)

    Jon was a member of Dewey's Mergers & Acquisitions Department, one of the most sophisticated groups of its kind in the world. He primarily represented large public companies in extraordinary transactions, including contested tender offers. Jon advised boards of directors on fiduciary duties and helped mitigate risk. Between deals and to more fully round out his skill set, Jon branched out into other areas of law, including securities registrations with the SEC, employment law, tax law and commercial agreements covering topics ranging from the development of biotechnology to IP licensing. While Jon enjoyed the fast paced environment of a large New York City law firm, he decided to leave Dewey to return to his roots in the Detroit suburbs.

  • Summer Associate (2L)

    Paul, Weiss, Rifkind, Wharton & Garrison LLP

    (Partnership; Law Practice industry)

    May 1999 — August 1999 (4 months)

    Jon worked in the Paul Weiss Corporate Department which covered a wide range of legal disciplines. Jon worked with many esteemed lawyers during his employment at Paul Weiss. He is especially proud of his work with Ted Sorensen. Jon assisted with matters involving multinational corporations, foreign governments, international tribunals and NGOs. He also registered securities with the SEC for a foreign corporation and worked on mergers and a credit agreements during his summer at Paul Weiss.

    Jon was rewarded for his hard work and positive attitude with an offer of permanent employment but ultimately decided to accept a competing offer of employment.

  • Summer Associate (1L)

    Huber, Lawrence & Abell

    (Law Practice industry)

    May 1998 — August 1998 (4 months)

    Huber Lawrence and Abell is a boutique law firm that exclusively practices energy and antitrust law, primarily for regulated utilities. The bulk of Jon's summer was dedicated to a billion dollar divestiture for one of New York's largest utility companies. Jon also prepared drafts of chapters for an ABA publication about U.S. antitrust laws and their application to utility companies.

    Jon was offered the opportunity to return to Huber Lawrence and Abell during his 2L summer and he was offered an opportunity to join the firm upon graduation from law school. He ultimately decided to spend his 2L summer and begin his career at larger law firms which afforded him the opportunity to represent clients in a wider range of industries and to practice in other areas of law.

  • Research and Teaching Assistant -- Professor Raymond Tanter

    University of Michigan

Professor Tanter was among the most popular professors in the Political Science Department and Jon credits him with readying Jon for law school.
