Randy Washburn’s Summary

I have spent thirty-plus years in the media research, planning, and buying side of the advertising business.

While I have worked for major industry research/information companies such as Arbitron and Telmar, most of my career has been spent in media agencies both large and small. This includes my own agency in Los Angeles which I ran for twelve years.

I have found that accounts of a certain size are too small for the larger media agencies and therefore are assigned to the less experienced B or C teams within those agencies.

These same accounts can look to a smaller media agency, but generally will pay higher fees in order to get access to the limited senior level talent that is available within these agencies.

Particularly in the current economic environment, there are very senior level media (traditional, digital, social) and creative professionals available in the freelance market. I have built a network of these folks who I have worked with for many years.

I am able to pull these professionals together on an as needed basis for my clients. Because of the lower overhead inherent in this service model, I am able to manage this very senior level talent on behalf of my clients, at significantly lower fees than those they would pay in a more traditional media agency model.
