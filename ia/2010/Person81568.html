Henri Daher’s Summary

The themes of my career in energy policy analysis have included, evaluation, influencing, positioning and participating in the final rule making process of new electricity markets in the New England region. The focus of my time in this area has included; leading organizations through the development of the Forward Capacity (FCM) and energy markets, Transmission and Carbon policies, and closely monitoring and assessing Regional Greenhouse Gas Initiative (RGGI) auctions. Additionally I have led the integration of the demand response and energy efficiency assets into the regional electricity markets.

The integration of renewable energy resources into the power market is a primer driver in today’s world. I have analyzed the technical, policy and commercial challenges facing this integration and I have briefed stakeholders and working groups regarding the necessary policy changes required to increase the participation of wind and other intermittent resources.

I have been a leading participant in numerous electricity industry stakeholder committees in ISO/RTO New England, New York ISO /RTO and nationally at MISO and UWIG (Utility Wind Integration Group) and I have built strong and lasting relationships with state representatives and energy suppliers across New England. My focus in the external participation has been to identify and evaluate programs and policies that may impact the strategy and positioning of organizations in the competitive energy, capacity and ancillary markets.

My time at Nstar and National Grid has provided me with an exceptional clear understanding of both; the electricity market and transmission policies and the impact that legislation and alternative and renewable resources are having on this critical infrastructure market place.
