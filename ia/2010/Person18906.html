Alexander Kabanov’s Summary

Dr. Alexander Kabanov is a leader in polymer-based drug delivery. In late 80'-ies while working in Soviet Union he pioneered use of polymeric micelles for drug delivery to the brain and was among the first to develop DNA/polycation complexes for gene delivery. His work using amphiphilic block copolymers to overcome multidrug resistance in cancer led to first human clinical trials of a polymeric micelle drug. In early 90'-ies Dr. Kabanov moved to Montreal, Canada, where he co-founded Supratek Pharma Inc. (www.supratek.com) and then joined UNMC in the United States, where he studied nanogels and block ionomer complexes for delivery of small drugs and biomacromolecules to brain and tumor sites. He currently investigates pharmacogenomic effects of nanomaterials ("polymer genomics") to develop safe and efficient modalities for treatment of neurodegenerative diseases and cancer. Dr. Kabanov published 170 scientific papers, edited several books and is an inventor on 25 US patents. His work was cited over 6,500 times. He co-chaired Gordon Research Conference on Drug Carriers in Medicine and Biology (2006), served a program chair of Controlled Release Society Annual Meeting (2007), and co-founded Nanomedicine and Drug Delivery Symposium series (2003-). He chaired Biomaterials and Biointerfaces study section at the National Institutes of Health (NIH) in 2006-2008. Dr. Kabanov is a recipient of Lenin Komsomol Prize (1988), National Science Foundation (NSF) Career Award (1995), NSF Special Creativity Award (2002) and Outstanding Research and Creative Activity Award (2007). His work is supported by NIH, NSF, Department of Defense, and industry. He sits on boards of directors and advisory boards of several companies.
