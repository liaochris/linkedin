Jeffrey Gray, Ph.D.’s Summary

When not traveling on client business, divide time between offices in the Midwest and the New York City area. Member of the Michigan, New York, and District of Columbia Bars. See www.graypllc.net for biographical information, representative speeches, and representative writings. Jeffrey McIntyre Gray PLLC was organized in Michigan, USA in 2004.

Jeffrey Gray, Ph.D.’s Specialties:

Energy marketing and trading; renewable energy development; complex commercial transactions; deal structuring and negotiating; regulatory; electric transmission; international; organized electricity markets; project finance; environmental markets; demand response; smart grid; rate cases; expert witness testimony; compliance; corporate governance; qualitative and quantitative analysis; oral argument; state and federal administrative and civil litigation at the trial and appellate levels.

Jeffrey Gray, Ph.D.’s Experience

  • Energy and Environmental Lawyer

    Gray PLLC

    (Law Practice industry)

    June 2004 — Present (6 years 2 months)

    International practice serving energy and environmental commodities marketers and traders, renewable energy developers, electric and natural gas utility companies, independent power producers, and others on complex commercial transactions, facility certification and permitting activities, and dealings with the Federal Energy Regulatory Commission (FERC), state commissions, regional transmission organizations (RTO), and state and federal courts and agencies.

    Representative matters include (1) advising clients on regulatory issues, renewable portfolio standards (RPS), renewable energy credits (REC), and carbon markets; (2) negotiating and drafting purchased power agreements (PPA), other energy and environmental commodities agreements (e.g., EEI, ISDA, NAESB), transmission interconnection and operating agreements, asset purchase and sale agreements, financing agreements, turbine supply agreements (TSA), balance of plant (BOP) agreements, and engineering, procurement, and construction (EPC) agreements; (3) representing clients in FERC proceedings including rulemakings, RTO tariff dockets, and Federal Power Act (FPA) and Natural Gas Act (NGA) applications and litigation; (4) negotiating, settling, and papering contested FERC rate cases and related tariffs and wholesale contracts; (5) securing FERC approvals for the purchase and sale of jurisdictional facilities; (6) securing FERC market-based rate authority; (7) securing FERC qualifying-facility (QF) certifications; (8) litigating QF avoided-cost proceedings before state commissions; (9) securing FERC relief from mandatory QF purchase obligations; (10) representing clients before state commissions in contested proceedings for the construction of new generation and transmission facilities; (11) securing approvals and permits for wind farms; and (12) arguing cases before administrative and civil courts at the trial and appellate levels.
