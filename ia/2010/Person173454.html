Deborah Robins’s Summary

Currently, Co-Creator/Executive Producer of prime time PBS television series: "THE MUSIC OF AMERICA: History Through Musical Traditions" (working title, and formerly known as "Word of Mouth: The Journey of American Folk Music) a co-production with WETA.

Expert in transactional legalities of the research/procurement/negotiation and contracting of intellectual properties as used in production elements for television, film, advertising, third-party compilation projects, and ancillary merchandise.

With over 15 years of experience working in a Director-level capacity for such companies as HBO, PBS, KQED, The Image Bank, Lonely Planet Images, and currently as Executive Producer at Nut Hill Productions, Inc., I bring a keen knowledge of the delicate dance that takes place between licensees and licensors of intellectual properties in matters such as:

*Recorded and Live Music Performances (and Master Recordings)
*Music Publishing Rights
*Publicity Rights (Name/Voice/Likeness usage)
*Film clips/footage research
*Fine art and stock photography
*Mechanical rights
*Research and permissions from celebrity estates
*Fair Use Doctrine issues
*Public Domain research and verification

Deborah Robins’s Specialties:

Years of successful contract negotiation, drafting, and execution between multiple parties for projects often using hundreds of different production elements such as music, film clips, stills, ephemera, corporate logos and trademarks; including the packaging of multiple production elements under the umbrella of a single copyright holder, for volume rate use in productions or products.
