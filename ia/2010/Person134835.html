Mark Walsh’s Summary

I train people to:

- Get more done without going crazy (stress and time management)
- Coordinate action effectively (team building and communication)
- Lead with presence, build trust and inspire through authenticity (leadership training and coaching)

My specialty is the "embodied" approach to learning and organisational development. I lead Integration Training a community of leading-edge trainers and consultants.

CV

Bsc (Hons) Psychology (Leeds University)

Extensive training experience with large and small organisations in the UK and abroad, e.g. Harrow NHS, UNICEF, Vangent, Domestic and General, The Brighton and Hove Chamber of Commerce and Brazil's national oil company - Petrobas.

Three years organising projects, training and conferences for the international organisation Aiki Extensions Inc. This included leadership training, stress management and conflict resolution work in the Middle East alongside the UN, in the slums of Brazil and with an HIV awareness charity in East Africa.

Residential training in Somatics (the study of the conscious body) and Embodied Leadership at Strozzi Institute, California and with Being In Movement founder Paul Linden in Ohio, USA.

Experience of training in numerous other physical systems such as aikido (black belt level) yoga, tai chi, Feldenkrais, tango, contact improvisation, judo, jujitsu, tae kwon do and Systema.

Four years in the outdoor education industry, training climbing, abseiling, teambuilding, orienteering, fencing, archery, snowboarding and trampolining.

Three years Non-Violent Communication training, including residential training and study with NVC founder Marshall Rosenberg.

Training in the linguistic coaching system of Fernando Flores.

Coach Training - e.g, with the Newfield Network

Numerous training courses on meditation and mindfulness.

First aid training courses, most recently with the British Red Cross

Mark Walsh’s Specialties:

Embodied business, stress management, time management, leadership training. "Conscious" or "human" ways of doing business.
