  • Historical Research

    Joseph Luther, Historian

    (Writing and Editing industry)

    January 2004 — July 2009 (5 years 7 months)

    Chairman, Kerr County Historical Commission Jan 2007-Mar 2009

    In 2008, the Dr Luther developed and presented a Plan of Work based on a Planning, Programming and Budgeting (PPBS) methodology. A Plan of Work was developed, with each Committee identifying its goals, objectives, activities, tasks and resources requirements – all functionally linked together. From this Plan of Work, a Programmatic Budget was developed. This Plan of Work became the basis for the Programmatic Budget and Annual Report.
    Dr Luther researched and wrote supporting documentation for the following historical markers:
    • Apelt Armadillo Farm – approved Feb 2009
    • Old Spanish Trail – approved Feb 2009
    • Great Western Trail – approved Feb 2009
    • Starkey Cemetery (HTC approved last year) – marker approved Feb 2009
    The following markers were approved in 2008.
    • Masonic Building
    • Kerrville Daily Times
    • Mary Ann Morriss
    • Starkey Cemetery (Registered Historic Cemetery)
    In addition, the KCHC applied for and received permission to relocate three historical subject markers:
    • One Mile to Ruins of Camp Verde - to be relocated to Camp Verde, intersection of SR 173 and FM 480 in area of excess TxDOT ROW.
    • Camp Verde C.S.A. Marker #682- to be relocated to Camp Verde, intersection of SR 173 and FM 480 in area of excess TxDOT ROW.
    • Kerr County – Marker #2929 - to be relocated to front of Courthouse.
    The Kerrville Arts and Cultural Center hosted the KCHC annual exhibition in March 2008. This year, the exhibit was entitled INDIGENOUS PEOPLE OF KERR COUNTY, 1730 – 1880 and was curated by Dr Luther and Dr Rector.
    Dr Luther initiated, designed, researched and published the Historical Commission’s first webpage, some 131 pages of local history from the pre-history to the present. These were the consequence of substantial research (both field and literature) which developed new knowledge about the history of Kerr County.
