Michael E Gluck’s Experience

  • Director

    Academy Health

    (Non-Profit; Public Policy industry)

    January 2009 — Present (1 year 7 months)

    Health policy researcher working with the Robert Wood Johnson Foundation's Changes in Health Care Financing and Organization (HCFO) initiative, the Center for Medical Technology Policy and other health policy efforts

  • Consultant

    Independent Health Policy Consulting

    (Public Policy industry)

    January 2006 — December 2008 (3 years )

    Primary and sub-contracting consultant to government agencies, philanthropic foundations and related organizations providing non-partisan analysis related to Medicare, pharmaceutical insurance benefits, health insurance regulation including state-mandated benefits, technology assessment, evidence-based medicine, and other health policy topics.

  • Director

    California Health Benefits Review Program

    (Educational Institution; 1-10 employees; Public Policy industry)

    September 2003 — September 2005 (2 years 1 month)

    First director of program to provide the California State Legislature with analysis of the medical, financial, and public health impacts of proposed health insurance benefit mandates within 60 days of being requested. Responsibilities include primary contact with legislative clients, stakeholders, a national advisory board that reviews all draft work, a task force of faculty from California medical schools and schools of public health who and other external parties, program budget, development and implementation of processes to produce analyses in a timely fashion, coordination of the work of three academic centers centers that produce components of bill analyses, and supervision of a direct staff of four. More information available at www.chbrp.org .

  • Research Associate Professor, Health Policy Institute

    Georgetown University

    (Educational Institution; 1001-5000 employees; Higher Education industry)

    April 2000 — August 2003 (3 years 5 months)

    Conducts policy analysis, program evaluation, and related research on various health topics with funding from private foundations and government agencies. Disseminates such work in written reports, oral briefings for relevant policy makers and interested press, and Congressional testimony. Topics of recent research include: (1) distribution channels and reimbursement methods for oncology drugs, (2) issues in the design of exceptions and appeals processes for a Medicare drug benefit, (3) an evaluation of the Robert Wood Johnson Foundation’s Changes in Health Care Financing and Organization (HCFO) program, (4) management and governance challenges facing the U.S. Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services, (5) the use of and payment for technology in long-term care, (6) federal involvement in the pharmaceutical R&D process, (7) analyses of federal programs to prevent HIV, and (8) a chartbook analysis of the Medicare program.

  • Director of Health Policy and Scholar-in-Residence (2000-2001)

    National Academy of Social Insurance

    (Non-Profit; 11-50 employees; Public Policy industry)

    May 1995 — April 2001 (6 years )

    As Director of Health Policy Studies (1995-2000) and Scholar-in-Residence (2000-2001), directed nonparitisan, foundation-funded policy research on the financing and organization of health-related social insurance programs. Responsibilities include working with volunteer NASI members whose expertise guides the work, supervision of a small multidisciplinary team of health policy analysts, overall guidance of the analytic content of health projects, management of a $400,000 a year budget, preparation of grant proposals to private foundations, developing and carrying out the agenda of national conferences on social insurance issues, and coordination of other dissemination activities to policymakers.

  • Senior Analyst/Project Director/Congressional Fellow

    Office of Technology Assessment

    (Government Agency; 201-500 employees; Public Policy industry)

    August 1987 — April 1995 (7 years 9 months)

    Directed or served as an analyst for the following peer-reviewed OTA studies:

    --How Effective Is AIDS Education? An Update
    --International Comparisons of Administrative Costs in Health Care
    --The Continuing Challenge of Tuberculosis
    --The Federal Response to HIV and AIDS: Congressional Issues.
    --The Costs and Effectiveness of Prostate Cancer Screening as a Medicare Benefit
    --Federal and Private Roles in the Development and Provision of Alglucerase Therapy for Gaucher Disease
    --Pharmaceutical R&D: Costs, Risks & Rewards
    --Preventive Services Under Medicare

    Project director responsibilities include directing OTA staff and contractors in the conduct of economic and policy research, managing the review of draft reports by outside experts and stakeholders, maintaining project budgets, answering press inquiries, communicating with Congressional staff, and the direct conduct of research.

  • Assistant Professor of Health Policy and Management

    Johns Hopkins Bloomberg School of Public Health

    (Educational Institution; 10,001 or more employees; Higher Education industry)

    January 1988 — December 1988 (1 year )

    Taught courses on methods of policy analysis and their application to Federal health policy. Participated in the economic evaluation of medical technologies including recombinant erythropoietin; prepared proposals to funding agencies for health service research grants.
