Rich Rutan’s Summary

While I've never worked in a professional game studio, I have experienced all of the hardship and drama of shipping a title. While at Full Sail, I partook in a right of passage known as "Final Project." As a Masters student, it was my job to lead a team of artists and programmers to design and create a game in five months. Not only did my team decide to create a game, they decided to create a genre. The aggressive weather simulator, known simply as the Brainstorm, was a roaring success in my personal opinion. While working on this game, I focused heavily on time management, interpersonal skills, and constraining creativity. While in production, the team came up with some grandiose ideas for our game, but, with such a short time frame, things needed to be removed in order to ensure we had enough time to complete the core gameplay. There were ups and downs and long nights, but we pulled through and I learned an immense amount from the experience.

Personally, I see myself working in the field of Game Design and fitting in quite well, I just need a few helping hands to pull me into the industry. I hope to one day work side by side with some of the greatest minds of our generation to create entertainment for the masses.
