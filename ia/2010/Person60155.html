Dr. Saket Kumar’s Summary

A proven leader in delivering and selling engagements that align business processes with client’s strategy through analytical process & performance improvements. Over ten years of global consulting experience. Led engagements for Fortune 500 companies across multiple industries, including Oil & Gas, Healthcare, Revenue Collection, and Manufacturing. Extensive experience in Process Improvements, Modeling & Optimization, Performance Measurement, Lean Six Sigma, and Offshore Development.

In addition to above experience, Saket is also a leader within Booz & Company’s Energy & Utilities Operations practice. Whether it is developing macro-level strategic goals or executing capabilities at the micro-level, Saket understands how to align structure, people, and technology to obtain strategic objectives. Dr. Kumar has successfully led performance improvement and change management teams in USA, Canada, and Europe.

Dr. Saket Kumar’s Specialties:

Process optimization, Operations improvement, Enterprise Analytics, Lean Six Sigma, Predictive Modeling, Best practices benchmarking, Offshore Project Management

Dr. Saket Kumar’s Experience

  • Associate, Energy Operations

    Booz & Company

    (Management Consulting industry)

    April 2008 — Present (2 years 6 months)

    For Royal Dutch Shell build a Capacity Planning Model that estimates optimum number of facilities for gas wells. Improved the productivity by reducing the efforts required in facility planning & calculation of production deferment.

    Established a business case for Shell Exploration & Production for allocating appropriate resources in tubing installation. Determined optimum production rate & time for tubing installation.

    For Talisman Energy, a large unconventional energy player in Canada, developed war games that test client’s capital planning process & organizational response to various internal & external stresses. The learning from war game was incorporated in organizational design for client.

    For Caremark, a major pharmacy benefits management (PBM) client, delivered a staffing resource model that supports continuous improvement of benefit operations. Improved estimation of lead time required to meet a particular customer demand as well as associated staffing levels.

  • Associate, Organizational Change

    Booz Allen Hamilton

    (Management Consulting industry)

    October 2005 — March 2008 (2 years 6 months)

    Saket worked with a major client to deliver a turn-key methodology that aligns client resources to more profitable work through systematic use of environmental and internal information. The application of this methodology has generated more than $400 MM revenue in just one year. For second year, the revenue is projected to increase by additional $1.12 billion (equivalent to 66,000% ROI). Saket led the initiative to develop and implement the methodology as Inventory Selection Process which helped the organization identify clients with highest revenue potential.

    Saket also participated in development of the future state vision for client’s two key programs, balancing conflicting objectives of improving service and improving compliance for organization’s 130 million customers. Working with several executives , the team designed concept of operations & supporting capabilities for the five year vision.

  • Product Manager

    American Process Inc

    (Public Company; Paper & Forest Products industry)

    January 2003 — September 2005 (2 years 9 months)

    Managed design and development of a web based benchmarking service for the forest products industry. This service aggregates production data & calculates operational performance in terms of energy usage at many Fortune 500 companies. This way operational performance was benchmarked within a company as well as against other companies in the industry. For individual companies, tool highlighted the plants where efforts to improve energy efficiency should be focused.

  • Senior Consultant

    Applied Value Corp

    (Management Consulting industry)

    June 1999 — December 2002 (3 years 7 months)

    For a major forest products company in Sweden, Saket conceived a framework that stores best operational practices as mathematical algorithms so that these could be used for continuous improvement. The executed solution increased revenue by more than $4 MM on an annualized run rate. Saket’s team designed & implemented the advanced applications that use sophisticated algorithms in real time to auto pilot the paper machine (Machine costs were in excess of $ 100 MM). His role increased production up to 10% for many products while keeping quality within specifications.
    Dr. Kumar led team that utilized decision analytics to convert enterprise knowledge into valuable information and predictions of product quality. This cutting edge solution was unique as it successfully balanced competing objectives of increasing production and maintaining product quality within specifications. In addition, the solution saved quality deviations in an information repository for continual process improvement.

  • Research Assistant

    University of Washington

    (Paper & Forest Products industry)

    January 1995 — August 1999 (4 years 8 months)

    • Developed multivariate statistical and first principle models that relate raw material and process operations to product quality using the operational plant data at two Fortune 500 companies: Weyerhaeuser and Georgia Pacific Corp. Identified potential of up to 45% reduction in process variations if variables correlating with the important factors were brought under control. Models were designed to be incorporated into existing process models to control product variability and understand plant economics.

  • Process Intern

Tool provides reconciled and validated data about processing conditions for a specified product sample. Enabled plant engineers to track and understand process upsets as well interesting production events. Commended by supervisor for technical competence, lateral thinking, and interpersonal skills.
