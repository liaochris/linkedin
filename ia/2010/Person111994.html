KATHLEEN MULHOLLAND’s Summary

I started teaching in 2009 after trying to talk myself out of the new career for many years. People kept telling me that I'd be an exceptional educator due to my outgoing nature and compassionate disposition, but I kept resisting the thought.

However, all that changed one day in May, 2009, when I had a conversation with a teacher who has taught in Philadelphia for 25 years. She told me how secondary English teachers were greatly needed in the district. I started thinking of the children who would benefit from what I had to offer: life experience, a successful artistic life in the Bucks County poetry community and a passion for literature. I had a calling that night - and I don't even believe in such things.

I began making phone calls, searching the Philadelphia School District's website meticulously and calling every number until I got the answers I needed. That summer I took the four exams necessary to obtain an Intern Certificate. I had an interview with an HR representative and then met with an amazing principal in August. I was fortunate to be hired the next day - two days after the results from my final exam were posted.

I hesitated pursuing a career in education because I know how hard teachers work. I now put in more hours a day than I thought possible. I work a second job to finance my part-time graduate studies and I raise two children alone. And while this is an astoundingly difficult feat, I find myself chronically enthused because of the students. My own children are benefiting from this passion as well; they are both putting a lot more energy into their own studies as a result.

KATHLEEN MULHOLLAND’s Specialties:

Writing. I teach juniors; helping them master expository prose and to do well on the PSSA's are my goals. I also want to expose the world of poetry to them from a contemporary perspective, because I feel that the culture surrounding the craft develops intellect in ways others cannot.
