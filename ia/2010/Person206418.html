Tom Robbins’s Summary

Efficient Patent Preparation

I have developed an efficient model for drafting patent applications. Compared to a typical solo practitioner, my model saves more than 50% in preparation costs while devoting at least 40 hours to the preparation of each application. I completely control the drafting of every application. I interview the inventor to gain a complete understanding of the invention before drafting the claims. The technical writer (with the disclosure material, a recording of my inventor interview and the claims) is then cost effectively exploited by drafting the body of the application. My additional value is then more cost effectively exploited in the application body review and enhancement.

This model results in an increased time of preparation per application, which translates into more robust specifications that issue into defensibly stronger and more assertable patents.

Outside In-house IP Services (Balt-Wash)

I provide IP services to companies in the Baltimore-Washington metropolitan area that might not need (or that is unable to budget for) a full time in-house IP attorney, but would like personal access to an IP attorney as needed.

Having worked as a patent examiner, a patent agent and a patent attorney, I have an extensive patent preparation and prosecution background. Having worked in technology transfer, I am familiar with the need to constantly increase a return on investment in IP. Having worked as an in-house counsel, I have transactional experience, focusing on the IP issues arising from CRADAs and from license, joint development, services and procurement agreements. Lastly, having both private practice and in-house experience, I understand the practical need for a quick, accurate and understandable answer to an IP question.
