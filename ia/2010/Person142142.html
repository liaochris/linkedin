Michael Buffaloe’s Summary

I was initially drawn into community management completely by accident. When I was 15 years old, I wanted to contribute to a (rather small) forum I had joined, so I did so in any way that I could. By the time I was 16, I was managing the site on my own and I had transformed this forum from a small group of fans into a huge, devoted community. The moment I realized I was largely responsible for its success, I knew: this is what I have to do for a living.

I approach every obstacle with the same mindset: the only prerequisite for success in anything that I am tasked with is a level of passion and drive. If I can get excited about what I am working on, I'll pour every ounce of my focus into that task until it is completed according to the standard it deserves. Self-teaching any/all necessary information is a given - I started with very little knowledge of this field and now I know more than I ever expected I could, and it is still less than two years after I began, so I am proficient in learning what I need to in order to succeed.

I am looking to obtain an entry-level community management position in a progressive game development studio or publisher, one that will allow me to utilize my skills in writing, community development, and PC/internet technologies in a manner that continuously challenges my existing abilities.
