Peter Vanhee’s Experience

  • Visiting PhD Student

    CRG

    (Research industry)

    October 2009 — Present (1 year 3 months)

    The Fibroblast Growth Factor (FGF) is a growth hormone involved in a series of physiological and pathological activities. In my stay at the CRG, Barcelona, I will work on a peptide design method to inhibit the interaction of the FGF with its receptor (FGFR), based on the concept that protein peptide interactions adopt the same structural motifs as monomeric protein folds, which we recently introduced (Vanhee, Stricher et al. 2009). We discovered striking similarities between intramolecular and intermolecular interactions, and demonstrated the applicability of these resemblances by reconstructing 9 protein-peptide complexes using monomeric protein folds, through docking of the original peptide ligands in 4 cases within 1 Angstrom of the original position. We propose here a de novo design of a helical peptide binding to the FGF Receptor, using the FoldX protein design suite and other tools available in the group of Luis Serrano, CRG. We will integrate our backbone design methods with the sidechain design tools available in FoldX, and will rely on the available expertise in protein-protein and protein-peptide design (Van Der Sloot, Kiel et al. 2009).
