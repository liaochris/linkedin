Caitlin Rosberg’s Summary

Areas of Expertise: Social/Digital/New Media and Marketing, Website Product Design and Management, Product Research and Development, Analytics and ROI Measurement, User Experience/Interface and User Testing, Education, Social Sciences, Anthropology.

Though Product Management and Digital Media Marketing may seem at first quite disparate when considering skill sets, they are in my mind two sides of the same coin. Product management requires a set of skills and the ability to analyse the behavior of users once they reach a site, and use that data to make the site experience as useful and positive as possible, to both retain and attract consumers. Social media, on the other hand, uses the behavior and feedback of users off of your site to analyze what you could do to improve the experience. Never before has it been so easy and inexpensive to connect with customers and solicit their feedback in order to make powerful improvements to a product.

If the web is a tool to help solve people's problems, product management and social media are tools to help learn how to best solve those problems. At the heart of things, both rely on the interpretation of intense amounts of deeply meaningful data, and knowing customers inside and out. I would be lying if I claimed that digging into a few months of analytics to get a good handle on the behavior of a site's users wasn't one of my favorite parts of working as a product manager. Whether striving towards B2B or B2C (or even B2E), for profit or not, every business has a service, message, or product to sell. By using the best tools that the internet has to offer, we can know more about current and potential customers than ever before, and thus learn not only how to market consumers in the most cost- and effort-effective way, but also how to make products or services that are even more compelling and profitable.
