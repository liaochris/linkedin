  • Market Director

    AES Corp

Responsibilities from this position include:

  • Principal Consultant

    TurnKeyMasters

    (Privately Held; Oil & Energy industry)

    2007 — 2009 (2 years )

    Brought on board to help develop the Business Model of TurnKeyMasters. Redefined the value proposition of the surplus Power Plants industry, and shifted the focus to work closer with buyers and projects developers. Addressed operational, marketing, and customer challenges, developing and executing practical strategies to take the company to aggressive growth mode.

  • Project Director

    AES Corp

    (Public Company; Utilities industry)

    2007 — 2009 (2 years )

    Based on previous research, AES saw the potential to apply the technology used by batteries in hybrid electric vehicles to the power industry as a way to create energy storage. AES began working with industry leaders in battery storage to test whether these applications could be modified to meet the needs and specifications of our industry.

    As the Project Director, the challenge was creating a MW scale equipment, capable of being connected to the power grid, and provide Frequency Regulation with such reliability that compete with the fastest power plants. Such equipment would have to meet all kind of technical and safety standards, as well as provide flexible control capabilities and interface.

    • In 7 months, created the world’s first MW-scale, Lithium-ion Energy Storage system safely connected to an Indianapolis Power and Light (IPL) transmission substation. The system was designed and built meeting all standards from IEEE, UL, and NEC for electric equipment interconnected to the grid.

    • Executed successfully certification of the ES unit performance by conducting the rigorous test program designed by consultant firm KEMA (www.kema.com) to validate the use of the new battery storage technology for electric utility system application.

    • In 4 weeks, moved and installed a 1 MW ES unit to PJM’s Headquarters. Once there, submitted the unit to all market integration tests where it was certified and became the first equipment of its kind making revenue in a power regulation market.

  • Manager of Markets Development

    AES Corp

    (Public Company; Utilities industry)

    2006 — 2007 (1 year )

    Promoted to this position to help AES find new markets, develop opportunities and diversify the risk of our portfolio by getting the most from our current assets and creating new sources of profits. The main focus would be exploiting idle capacity from our physical infrastructure and untapping sources of high return value with minimal investment.

    • Developed the AES Business Plan to create a LNG Market for Industrial Applications by working along CB&I (www.cbi.com) to develop a feasible, low cost technical solution and meeting potential customers, users, investors, contractors and partners to identify sources of efficiency and value.

    • Created an internal/external team to evaluate the commercial potential for AES from building different Transmission Lines to interconnect isolated electric systems, mainly touristic zones, operated independently from the wholesale market.

    • Developed a work plan oriented to optimize the use of the three ports owned by AES in DR (LNG, Coal and Fuel Oil) by offering loading and unloading services to interested companies. The work plan included firm Letters of Intention from 5 industrial companies and a preliminary design of the installations/improvements required.

    • Worked along with gas producer Promigas (www.promigas.com) to research commercial applications of NG to replace subsidized propane on vehicles.

    • Issued a Formal Procedure for the evaluation, administration, control and documentation of the Company's projects and delivered technical training in Projects Valuation to AES staff, so they could help identify, document and evaluate new business opportunities.

    • Designed and implemented a Customer Relationship Management system (CRM), to help retaining our Industrial Customer base by delivering a high quality service.

    • Initiated works related to explore opportunities with renewable energy projects, to take advantage from the benefits included in the local normative.

  • Manager of Analysis and Forecasting

    AES Corp

    (Public Company; Utilities industry)

    2004 — 2006 (2 years )

    Promoted to face the challenging responsibility of taking the company to a higher level of certainty by analyzing the factors affecting our commercial performance, and support the Business Vice-president with the decision making activities, at a time when the company faced a highly risky environment, deteriorated relationships with the government (who happened to be a partner in the dying distribution business), skyrocketing gas prices and limitations to meet the Take or Pay with BP, and a huge pressure from AES Corp to report profits.

    • Design and implementation of latching contracts between AES’ generation companies to retain higher margins from energy and power contracts, minimizing the exposition to high fuel prices of our most valuable assets by transferring the risks to less profitable and risk shared units.

    • Creation of two new complimentary tools: the Business Information and the Competitive Intelligence systems to improve the decision making process based on non-conventional sources of information about competitors, partners and authorities.

    • Development of a complete Competitive Analysis of the Electricity Industry in the Dominican Republic, establishing our position in the industry (commercial and operational), pointing out specific strategic movements and contractual arrangements the company had to assume to improve our situation. Most of the recommendations were implemented in the subsequent months, with positive results for the company.

    • Formulation and Evaluation of new power and NG investments for more than 250 MM US$, and the implementation of the Risk Analysis and reports as part of the normal activities to be performed by the business analysts.

  • Senior Business Analyst

    AES Corp

    (Public Company; Utilities industry)

    2003 — 2004 (1 year )

    Recruited to AES to create and implement the company’s commercial models and develop a contract management system, as AES moved from having a single long-term PPA with the government, to be the largest player in the new electricity market with over 600 MM US$ invested in an LNG terminal, more than 700 MW of installed capacity and a major equity in a Distribution Company.

    • Successfully creation and implementation of all commercial models to track, settle, validate and simulate all the transactions and contracts from the Wholesale Electric Market including Energy, Capacity, Transmission Rights and Frequency Regulation.

    • Complete Integration of commercial Risk Analysis Tools with the long-term unit commitment software and the components of the commercial models in a single platform. The result was a high-performing analysis application to forecasting electricity demand, fuel prices and company’s profits, recognized by AES Risk staff as one of the most accurate within the company.

    • Administration of the business and operational Key Performance Indicators (KPIs), to track and asses the performance of our main sources of commercial value (PPAs, Large Customers, Margins, etc.) and the performance of our power plants (Availability, Heat Rate/Efficiency, Use/Capacity Factors, etc.).

  • Commercial Engineer

    Coordinator Body of the Wholesale Electric Market

    (Oil & Energy industry)

    April 2001 — August 2003 (2 years 5 months)

    Hired as Commercial Engineer to bring my college experience with science and engineering software development, to help with the implementation of the Wholesale Electric Market in Dominican Republic. As part of the first group of Commercial Engineers, went though a strict education on Power Markets, and was challenged to develop the tools, procedures and guidelines to implement different sections of the normative.

    • Executed successfully implementation of the compensations for Primary and Secondary Frequency Regulation services through incorporation of formal technical procedures to certify the units and the creation of simple computational tools to track and compensate the periods of service.

    • Development of the PFT, statistical software currently used by the System Operator and all Market participants for the calculation of the Firm Capacity of Thermoelectric Power Plants. This application is critical and central to execute the capacity payments of the units.

    • Implemented the calculation of spot prices in real-time by the system operators. This goal demanded the education of the power system operators on the fundamentals of the market rules and settlements, and required an involvement of myself and the rest of my team around the clock (24/7) for several weeks, so we could understand the nature and “cyclicity” of the market.

    • Administration of the Commercial Metering System for the north side of the country, where more than 100 Distribution and Generation metering points had to be visited along with local authorities to certify the meter settings and to locking them up with the OC security codes.
