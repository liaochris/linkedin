Socorro de Castro’s Summary

Socorro de Castro, pianist, multikeyboardist, songwriter, composer, arranger, producer, is a long time member of the bay area music scene. A graduate of the prestigious BERKLEE COLLEGE OF MUSIC in Boston, Massachusetts, major in Jazz Composition and Arranging with piano as principal instrument, she also went to UCLA to study Film Scoring, skills she later put to use on such projects as scoring Ramona Diaz's award winning documentary "Spirits Rising". Her musical styles draw from a wide range of influences including classical, jazz, nu jazz, pop, R&B, country, latin, christian, alternative, etc.

In addition to her numerous songwriting and commercial credits, Socorro, along with her husband, pianist/keyboardist Norman Landsberg, run Landsberg Music Productions, a full service music production company providing music direction, compositions and arrangements for album productions and live performances and others.

From 2004-2007, Socorro was the musical director of the Filipino Women's Network (FWN) production of award-winning playwright Eve Ensler's The Vagina Monologues in San Francisco and in 2006 in NY upon the invitation of the author herself for the 10th anniversary of the show.

Last year, she was invited to perform at the 3rd Philippine International Jazz Festival in Manila with an array of international jazz artists including Lee Ritenour, Omar Sosa and Incognito. Recently, she performed with the world-famous Kruger Brothers at the Bluegrass Festival, "Carolina in the Fall" along with country, bluegrass, folk music legends of the country. This year, she was one of the jazz artists at the 2nd ANNUAL SF FIL/AM JAZZ FESTIVAL. She is currently working on a new cd of original music. Her new instrumental single entitled "The Audacity of Hope", a tribute to President Barack Obama, is now available worldwide on cdbaby.com, amazon.com, iTunes, etc. This joins her already released original album, "SOCORRO" on lamp88 records.
