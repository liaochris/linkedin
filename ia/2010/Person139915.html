Megan Cunningham’s Summary

Megan Cunningham is the Founder and CEO of Magnet Media. She began her career producing independent documentaries, including films for TVLand, PBS/WNET, PBS/WGBH, HBO and MTV. In 1997, she moved to Virtual Media as Senior Editor, where she worked in creative editorial and post-production on several TV and theatrical productions (including clients like Time Warner, Woody Allen, and MTV). In 2000, she left Virtual Media as the Director of Business Development to start production company and interactive marketing agency, Magnet Media.

The company became a pioneer in interactive production, beginning with DVD and moving to Web-Based TV, with the 2006 launch of its online network Zoom In Online (ZIO). It now produces 6 original award-winning broadband shows, covering arts & entertainment: Film/TV, Music, Photography and Design. ZIO is produced with ad and sponsorship support from companies like Apple, The Sundance Institute, HP, Toyota, Microsoft, Adobe, Lexus, and more.

Ms. Cunningham has led seminars, workshops and panels at industry many events: The Producer's Institute (BAVC, MacArthur Institute), The Wharton School of Business' Entrepreneurs Conference, DV Expo, the Independent Feature Market, and NAB. Her first book, The Art of the Documentary (featuring interviews with Al Maysles, Ken Burns, Errol Morris, DA Pennebaker) was recently published by Pearson Publishing. She served on the Board of Directors of New York Women in Film and Television, and is a graduate of Swarthmore College.
