Andrew Montalenti’s Summary

Software engineer with a broad background in languages, toolkits, and platforms. Special depth of knowledge in Java, Python, Javascript and C. The main enemy of my productivity is multi-tasking; my tools for fighting the enemy are GTD and Agile project management. My allies in productivity are dynamic languages, frameworks, idioms, and UNIX tools. My favorite design pattern is "Interpreter." I believe in thinking before coding. You might call it thought-driven development.

  • IT Analyst

    Morgan Stanley

    (Public Company; MS; Financial Services industry)

    August 2006 — March 2009 (2 years 8 months)

    Worked as developer in Enterprise Application Infrastructure group, aka EAI. Project was called MODeX, or Model-Oriented Data eXchange. It's a framework that allows developers to build model-driven, cross-platform web services on Java, C# and C++. I built a lot of pieces across the framework, including: the graphical modeling tool (built in Java with Eclipse SDK), Python-based "rules engine" (Jython), and the code generator (Java, Freemarker). As a framework, MODeX was pretty close to Google Protocol Buffers or Facebook Thrift (both open source projects now) in that it enabled high-performance, cross-platform messaging.

    By the time I left, I was working in a small development team, acting as a lead developer and "scrum master". I had also presented our work at two open source conferences, EclipseCon 2008 and Eclipse Banking Day NYC 2009.
