Neil Veilleux’s Summary

Neil Veilleux is a project consultant with Meister Consultants Group (MCG), a Boston-based sustainability consultancy. He works with both public and private sector clients on clean energy strategy, market analysis, and program development. In particular, he has recently collaborated with policy-makers at state energy offices, economic development agencies, and municipalities (including City of Boston and New York City) to create market strategies and long-term incentive structures to promote renewable energy and energy efficiency.

Before joining MCG, Mr. Veilleux managed warehouse operations at an industrial supply distributor in Atlanta. He earned a B.A. from The University of the South and a M.A. in Urban and Environmental Policy & Planning from Tufts University. He has served as a Rappaport Public Policy Fellow at Harvard’s Kennedy School of Government and a Fulbright scholar in Germany.

Neil Veilleux’s Specialties:

renewable energy and energy efficiency policy, management development, market research, teaching, writing
