  • Founder, CEO

    Climos

    (Environmental Services industry)

    September 2005 — Present (5 years 2 months)

    Climos is exploring various processes for naturally removing large amounts of CO2 from the atmosphere. The first technology we plan to develop, ocean iron fertilization, has the highest greenhouse gas mitigation potential of all available methods.

    Climos has a scientific advisory board which includes Dr. Rita Colwell, 11th Director of the National Science Foundation, Dr. Bob Gagosian, President of the Joint Oceanographic Institutions, and Dr. Tom Lovejoy, Chief Scientist of the Heinz Center and former Executive Vice President of the World Wildlife Foundation among others.

    This is something new for me. It's a chance to do something that might be meaningful in a way that I haven't experienced before. I dig that.

  • Founder, CTO

    GetThere, Inc.

    (Public Company; 501-1000 employees; Internet industry)

    November 1994 — October 2000 (6 years )

    I co-founded GetThere, initially called Internet Travel Network.

    I wrote the original code, and ran the business until we got funded. Then I ran engineering and later I became the CTO.

    The first travel reservation made over the Internet went through a server in my living room in 1995.

    GetThere was the leading provider of Internet-based travel reservation services. After an IPO in 1999, the company was sold to The Sabre Group (NYSE: TSG) in 2000 for $750 million--the largest cash deal for an Internet company.

    Under Sabre, GetThere continues to serve customers such as United Airlines and American Express, as well as major international corporate customers such as Procter and Gamble, Chevron, Nokia, and Cisco. It is used by 75% of the Fortune 200 and processes more B2B transaction revenue than any other system in the world.

    I dearly hope that they have replaced the original code I wrote.
