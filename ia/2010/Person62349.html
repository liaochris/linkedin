Edward Montoya’s Summary

Edward Montoya is a Florida attorney living in Miami. He has many years experience in personal injury, wrongful death and air disaster litigation matters. He has trial experience in air disaster matters, products liability cases as well as general civil matters. Mr. Montoya has tried over 25 administrative law related cases in Miami during his career. His Florida law firm also handles securities arbitration matters, real estate and commercial litigation cases.

Prior to attending law school, Mr. Montoya worked in banking as an analyst and special projects coordinator for the Federal Reserve Bank of Atlanta - Miami Branch. Thereafter, he was accepted to attend Vermont Law School (VLS).

While at VLS, Mr. Montoya was selected to participate in an internship program at the United States Department of Justice Civil Environmental Division in Washington, D.C., where he gained experience in environmental matters on a national scale. While in law school, he was Staff Editor of the VLS Forum News. Mr. Montoya earned his Juris Doctorate Degree from Vermont Law School in 1992.

He has received several recognitions such as Legal Elite in Civil Litigation by Florida Trend Magazine in 2009; and as "Up and Coming" attorney by the South Florida Business Journal in 2004. He speaks Spanish and limited Portuguese.

Edward Montoya’s Specialties:

Personal injury, automobile law, Jones Act, products liability, commercial litigation, Florida business, and air disaster litigation.
