David Fraga’s Summary

Director of Corporate Development at Shutterstock, leading the company's M&A and corporate partnership initiatives. Shutterstock is the largest subscription-based stock photography agency in the world.

Prior to Shutterstock worked as an Analyst at Insight Venture Partners, a $3 billion private equity and venture capital firm focused on software and Internet investments. Executed three transactions, including investments in Twitter and eVestment Alliance, which together represented over $172 million of transaction value.

Co-founder of LendforPeace.org, an international microfinance non-profit focused on the Middle East and funded by the Clinton Global Initiative.

David Fraga’s Experience

  • Director of Corporate Development

    Shutterstock

    (Privately Held; Photography industry)

    2009 — Present (1 year )

    Shutterstock is the largest subscription-based stock photography agency in the world.

    A clearinghouse for crowdsourced digital content, Shutterstock boasts the largest and highest-quality collection of royalty-free, licensable content on the web. Over 200,000 photographers, videographers and designers from around the world submit images, footage and illustrations to Shutterstock's library. Creative organizations and professionals subscribe to this library for access to the content that they need.

    New content submissions undergo a rigorous selection process before being added to Shutterstock's library of over 9 million images. When an image is downloaded by a subscriber, the creator of that image receives a payment for their work.

    As Director of Corporate Development, my role at Shutterstock is to lead the company's M&A and corporate partnership initiatives. By partnering with the best companies in the industry, Shutterstock is committed to offering its customers with the highest quality content and tools.

    LendforPeace.org is supported by grants from the Clinton Global Initiative, Ashoka Youth Venture, and Davis Projects for Peace.
