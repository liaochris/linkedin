James X. Zhang’s Summary

In this industry, somehow, somewhere--there is always a way. All you need really is just that one shot, even if it's the Hail Mary.

Someone once told me whatever happens in life, happens two times: once in your mind, once in reality.

I see myself having a shot to work after 13 months of job-hunting. From going to other schools' career centers to copy down business cards, busing it 6, 8, 10 hours without complaint, to staying in my seat for days to fill out every form, cover letter, resume revision--I learned to turn my difficulties into assets.

I learned to ask for help, because I cannot do this alone. I learned to never take "no" for an answer, even when turned away from recruiters--I moved on to the next table. Most of all, I learned to dream and inspire others along the way--because without dreaming about a career, I wouldn't last a day against uncertainty.

Some have everything given to them. Others have to pay their dues and play the game as it is. I understand I have to outdo, outsell, and outperform those who aren't hungry, those who don't care.

I can't change my past. I'm proud of where I went to school, because for what I paid cleaning computer keyboards and selling laptops, it was the best I could do. I am someone who gets it.

It's been an incredible journey, but I would like to reach a conclusion.

These next two weeks, for my hardest earned final round interviews, after 1,200+ applications, a disheartening rejection from Teach for America, and hundreds of people and automated responses telling me "no"--I will stand up and deliver, to finally start my life.

That's me, and my willingness to do whatever it takes is far more important than any piece of paper.
