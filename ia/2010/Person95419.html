Jennifer Whistler’s Summary

I began my career as a technical writer in the nuclear industry, first at Clinton Power Station in Illinois, then at Davis-Besse in Ohio. I wrote user documentation for online databases, and employee training manuals. Later, I worked for Fox Software in Ohio, where I again wrote user documentation and training materials. Once Fox was bought by Microsoft, I worked for them for a short time before joining Creative Solutions, Inc., a tax and accounting software company in Michigan where I did more user documentation, training materials, and product promotion materials.

After that contract ended, I began a new career in teaching. I was an adjunct instructor concurrently for both Bowling Green State University and Owens Community College in their English departments, where I taught Freshman composition, technical writing, business communication, and honors composition courses.

After several years, our family moved to Pennsylvania, where I taught similar courses at Reading Area Community College. Another move took me to Miami University where I taught for a short time, and then onto to Michigan again, and University of Phoenix teaching a variety of communication and writing courses.

For a number of years, I was a "professional" volunteer, serving as an elected official on our local school board. For the past five years, my energies have been devoted to improving education not only in our district, but in the state of Michigan overall. I have worked to secure improved funding for academic programming as well as improved technology, new buildings, special education, and arts education.

Currently, I am working on personal writing projects and serving as an advisor on the Michigan chapter of Society of Children's Book Writers and Illustrators, and international organization with 550 members in this state. I served as one of two co-chairs for SCBWI's Fall 2009 statewide conference.
