Martin Kloos’s Summary

Social Media and Webstrategy Consultant. Martin gained relevant experience in web strategy, online community, social media and open innovation projects. Martin is known from his writing (in Dutch) on www.martinkloos.nl, www.frankwatching.com, www.socialmediablog.nl and www.thenextweb.org.

Martin was born on 12 January 1982. He studied bachelor Information Technology & Information management at The Hague and Business Information Systems at the University of Amsterdam. Central themes are ICT in service organizations, information management and knowledge management. Currently he holds a Masters Degree (with honors) in science.

Martin worked as a part time consultant for Exact Software, which is the leading company of financial administration software in the Netherlands.

Martin wrote his Masters thesis on the effects of the application of Web 2.0 concepts on knowledge management, learning and meaning making in groups of people. The theory of knowledge management, learning and meaning is derived from Etienne Wenger, the founder of Communities of Practice and famous for his social learning theory.

After graduation, Martin started working as a consultant for the Technology Advisory practice of Deloitte Consulting.

Since May 2010, Martin further follows his passion and joins Media Embassy. Focus: Internet, Enterprise 2.0 and social media. He has worked on several projects regarding enterprise 2.0, open innovation and online community building.

Martin frequently writes on his weblog: http://www.martinkloos.nl. He also writes for dutch weblogs The Next Web (http://www.thenextweb.org) and Frankwatching (http://www.frankwatching.com). Internally, Martin is involved in a Business Technology Advisory magazine where he writes about social media in general. He also involved in organizing Deloitte's sponsorship of the conference "van Web 2.0 naar Enterprise 2.0" and the development of a new interactive podium for Deloitte.
