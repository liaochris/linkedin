Charlotte Moore’s Summary

Charlotte Moore graduated from Catawba College in 2006 with a B.A. in English and a minor in Spanish. She resides in Raleigh, but she's a Washingtonian at heart.

As a copywriter and proofreader, she concepted, wrote and edited copy for clients like Subway restaurants, UNC Health Care, the N.C. State Fair and the N.C. Aquariums. She's proudest of her TV and radio ads for the Thad and Alice Eure Walk for Hope, and of writing the script that helped win her agency the coveted N.C. Aquariums account.

Charlotte's most recent work was with R.H. Donnelley, helping to develop, write and optimize content for DexKnows, their online business listing.

Creative, assertive and a little bit (okay, fine, a lot) of a pedant, Charlotte loves nothing so much as a good thesaurus and an infinite supply of red pens, not to mention really great, crunchy words like "sesquipedalian" and "spork." Her favorite punctuation mark is the semicolon.

Her dad taught her that a good writer is also a good observer, with a broad range of interests and talents -- and because her dad's a good writer, she took his advice. A proud nerd (and a level 80 druid), Charlotte directs the Anne McCaffrey fan track at Dragon*Con, one of the largest sci-fi conventions in the U.S. She enjoys science fiction, literature, art, astronomy, meteorology, aviation, and blogging. She loves politics so much it makes her teeth hurt. (She also loves mixing metaphors.)
