Julie Taylor’s Summary

Julie Taylor’s consulting practice as a Director with AEGIS Professional Services is dedicated to the comprehensive needs of entrepreneurs and emerging businesses, with a specialty focus in the area of private equity fundraising. She has a developed a niche in business plan development with a focus on strategic business consulting for the purpose of private equity placements, financial modeling, as well as providing general business counsel. In all areas of her work, Julie relies on her background as a writer, and applies her journalism skills to a wide range of client needs – such as the development of operations manuals for franchises, conflict resolution, or writing advertising and website copy. Julie is fortunate to have a diverse client portfolio, as her clients have included a great number of high tech industries, as well as manufacturing, medical, health and fitness, consumer products, construction, and retail.

Prior to her work at AEGIS, Julie was a Senior Manager with QUALCOMM in San Diego, a Fortune 500 wireless telecommunications company. In her role there, Julie was the founder of the QUALCOMM Corporate Giving program, where she was responsible for the direction of the corporate contributions of the company. Julie developed the Corporate Giving department to include a robust Employee Volunteerism program which served as a benchmark for the San Diego community, as well as a Non-Profit Board Leadership training and development program for QUALCOMM executives. Julie was instrumental in the development of the QUALCOMM Social Entrepreneurship Internship Program, in conjunction with San Diego State University’s Entrepreneurial Management Center and Graduate School of Business, which partnered MBA candidates, San Diego non-profit organizations, QUALCOMM executives, and San Diego State professors, on dynamic projects with a focus on entrepreneurship.

Private equity fundraising, business plan development, strategic business consulting, financial modeling, general business counsel, conflict resolution, copy development.
