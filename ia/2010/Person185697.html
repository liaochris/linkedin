Richard Katz’s Summary

About HRMS and Founder and CEO, Richard Katz.

HRMS mission is to support the private sector, government agencies, and non-profit efforts to use President Obama's Stimulus funding effectively to create jobs, increase job growth and reduce unemployment.

About Richard Katz: Following a corporate career as Director of HR for Bullock's Department Stores, and HR Practice Leader for Ernst and Young, Richard established Human Resource Marketing Services (HRMS)

Under the VideoSearch brand, Richard created visionary products including the VideoResume, a pre-screen employment tool. HRMS produced and aired half-hour recruitment television programs called "Meet Your Next Employer." For his pioneering work in recruitment advertising, Mr. Katz received the Employment Management Association's National Pericles Award.

HRMS has successfully secured and managed millions of dollars of government funding from over 50 Workforce Investment Boards and non-profit agencies. In 1993, HRMS established the Government Services Division to operate customized, government-funded job training and placement programs. HRMS designed and operated the Spotlight On Jobs program in partnership with Paramount Pictures. Collaborating with staffing agencies, HRMS was funded to put hundreds of welfare recipients back to work. HRMS received funding to designed and operate Hollywood Digital Careers, a high-end technical training program in partnership with Sony Pictures.

HRMS launched Dot.com Job Search™, a proprietary curriculum designed to teach staff and clients how to use the Internet to find a job.

Through a partnership with Monster Worldwide, HRMS designed a marketing program for a government web site. In partnership with the University of California, and other major colleges, HRMS created and produced a series of Experienced Alumni Career Conferences.

HRMS publishes NetAssets, a semi-monthly electronic newsletter that is read by thousands of workforce development professionals.

Richard Katz’s Specialties:

Government-funded employment and training programs; recruiting technology; retained executive search for non profits, education and government; Internet marketing (B to B) and (B to C); electronic publishing; college and university alumni career conference production; employment television production; Internet products and services related to employment screening, recruiting and selection.
