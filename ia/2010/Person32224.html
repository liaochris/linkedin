Carla Haase’s Summary

I am an independent learning and development consultant with expertise in leadership training and development; designing performance management systems and delivering related training; interviewing, hiring and retention; executive communications and presentation skills; personal awareness and development; and feedback and coaching.

In 2010, I was among the first in the New York metropolitan area to earn the distinction of MBTI "Master Practitioner," a certification which was established to designate only the most experienced MBTI professionals.

I have several large clients for whom I do (or have done) meeting management and facilitation, training design and delivery, and employee attitude surveys and follow-up. In addition to being a long-time member of the Leadership Development Faculty at GE Crotonville, some of my clients include HSBC Securities, Bayer Healthcare, Genworth Financial and BP.

As a freelancer, I am also fortunate to offer the same services through other consulting firms including Gilman Performance Systems (GPS) and accelerated performance development, llc. (apd).

As a Senior Consultant at GPS, I have designed and/or delivered meetings and training at several clients including UBS, Boehringer Ingelheim, Deloitte, PriceWaterhouse Coopers, Varian Medical Systems, Harvard Business School, and Cartus (formerly Cendant).

For apd, llc., I am part of the faculty for curriculum delivery at both Fort Dodge Animal Health (Wyeth Veterinary division) and C.R. Bard Medical Instruments.

From time to time, I will also do freelance work through Wronski & Associates, Treasure Hunt Adventures, and others.

In addition to my consulting business, I own and run a web-based accessories business called 121 Collection -- www.121Collection.com. I sell a wide variety of "accessories for your accessories," many of which I make myself.

  • Program Manager, Leadership Essentials

    GE Crotonville

    (Public Company; 10,001+ employees; GE; Human Resources industry)

    1994 — 1996 (2 years )

    Arguably the best job I ever had! I was responsible for GE's one-week entry-level leadership training program at the Management Development Center at Crotonville. My role was to conduct a needs assessment with top functional leaders at GE businesses, then manage a curriculum to meet those needs. As program manager, I set course objectives, then identified instructors and internal subject matter experts to teach the programs.

    During my tenure, we ran about 15 programs per year with 100-120 participants per program. About half of the courses were held in the U.S. and the remainder were offered in Europe, Mexico, Brazil, Singapore, Japan, China, India, Australia and more. In most locations, we ran programs in English, however, I learned to teach using simultaneous translation in Beijing, Shanghai, and Tokyo.

    During my tenure in this job, I earned certification to deliver the Myers Briggs Type Indicator -- one of my favorite subjects to teach.

  • Project Manager, Technical Entry Programs

    GE

    (Public Company; 10,001+ employees; GE; Management Consulting industry)

    1993 — 1995 (2 years )

    As project manager, I was responsible for tracking and enforcing graduation requirements for GE's Manufacturing Management Program (MMP). I worked closely with the Corporate Program Manager who set strategy, determined competencies, and designed training programs. Then it was my job to follow-up and enforce the program requirements. My role included tracking job assignments for this 6-month rotational program, ensuring that members had exposure to a variety of aspects of manufacturing; and then helping program graduates identify permanent jobs upon program graduation. I also scheduled and tracked completion of the training programs required for MMP graduation.

    During my years in this job, I frequently worked closely with GE's campus recruiting group, sometimes participating in strategy meetings or delivering interviewer training; and other times, conducting invterviews on campuses in U.S. and Puerto Rico.

  • Media Relations/Public Relations Representative

    GE Corporate Public Relations

    (Public Company; 10,001+ employees; GE; Public Relations and Communications industry)

    1989 — 1993 (4 years )

    Here, I was fortunate to work with a small team (about 5, depending on how you count) of brilliant spokespersons. It was our role to create positions and respond to media querries at GE's corporate HQ.

    My specific beats included community service, educational relations and corp. advertising. I did traditional PR activities when GE would roll out a new TV commercial (ex: the anniversary of night football) or sponsor an Audubon documentary on PBS, for example.

    I was also responsible for overseeing photo sessions with GE's top executives including Jack Welch and Vice-Chairman Larry Bossidy.

    While on this job, I worked as a fact-checker on the first book that was ever written about the Welch years at GE: "Control Your Destiny Or Someone Else Will," by management guru Noel Tichy and FORTUNE Senior Editor Start Sherman. I worked closely with Noel, Strat and their researchers to provide information--then fact-check content--for this best-selling book.
