Perig Vennetier’s Summary

I am a motivated and dynamic optics/optronics design and test engineer. I am particularly interrested in developping new state of the art or improving existing products in the high-tech industry.
I am always focused on improving my skills and bringing my knowledge to a dynamic multidisciplinary team.

Perig Vennetier’s Specialties:

Perig Vennetier’s Experience

  • Senior Optical Design and Test Engineer

    Dolby Labs.

    (Public Company; DLB; Consumer Electronics industry)

    August 2008 — Present (2 years 3 months)

    Dolby Laboratories is the global leader in technologies to deliver the best entertainment experiences. Best known for high-quality audio, they recently launched a new set of projects on High Dynamic Range (HDR) HDTV. Dolby Contrast technology dramatically improves the picture quality of LCD televisions. It employs LED backlighting together with proprietary technologies that locally dim backlight area to match the brightness of the image and display true blacks and more-lifelike dynamic contrast while using less power.
    • In charge of the research, study and specification of the LED backlighting, the core of the HDR technology as well as the LCD panel.
    • Design optical/photometric measurement stations for engineering.
    • Actively involved in a multidisciplinary group, with constant interactions with the electronics, mechanics, thermal management, and algorithm team members.
    • Constant communication with LED and LCD manufacturers for a two-way involvement in technology improvements.

  • Optronic System Engineer

    DiCon Fiberoptics

    (Privately Held; 501-1000 employees; Telecommunications industry)

    September 2005 — August 2008 (3 years )

    • Conceive, research, and develop the optical systems of High-Brightness LED-based products, including ray tracing and optical simulations, photometric and colorimetric characterization.
    • Investigate options in optical materials, such as high transparency plastics and glasses.
    • Contacted and worked with injection molding companies for lenses for the Light Cells product line.

    Significant Accomplishments:
    • Designed optical reflector system for new SweetSpot™ LED-driven luminaire introduced at LightFair 2008.
    • Continuous improvement of the optical systems for FiberLamp™ illuminators.
    • Selected to design and deliver courses to incoming junior engineers in colorimetry, photometry, light measurement, optical simulation, and ray tracing.

  • Laser Researcher

    Frequency Standards and Metrology Group

    (Educational Institution; 1-10 employees; Research industry)

    February 2004 — August 2004 (7 months)

    Research manager on a new laser system based on a Cr:LiSAF crystal pumping, part of a worldwide state-of-the-art project. Theoretical simulations with Mathematica and realization of prototypes. Complete management of the project. Reports and presentations in English.

  • Opto-electronical Engineer

    SAGEM

    (Privately Held; 10,001 or more employees; Telecommunications industry)

    March 2003 — August 2003 (6 months)

    In charge of the conception of an optronical performances simulator for military search and track systems (range calculation). Complete integration of manufacturers’ data (power, sensitivity, misalignment, detection probability, etc.) and environmental conditions (weather conditions, visibility).
