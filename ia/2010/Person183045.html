Sara Peters’s Summary

Writer, editor, designer, researcher, content strategist, conference planner, conference presenter and story-teller with over 10 years of varied editorial experience--including in-print, online, on-camera and on-stage. I excel at ripping apart complex topics and putting them back together in easily digestible pieces, with a personable, witty style.

  • Senior Editor

    Computer Security Institute, UBM International Media

    (Computer & Network Security industry)

    January 2005 — May 2010 (5 years 5 months)

    As senior editor, my duty was to develop CSI's content strategy—for publications, live conferences, virtual conferences, and online content—and direct all content-related endeavors. This included blogging, tweeting and writing in-depth analysis of cybersecurity issues for CSI's flagship monthly publication for security professionals. I also edited and designed CSI publications, and managed both full-time employees and freelancers. I worked closely with the CSI conference management team, selecting conference speakers, working with speakers on their presentations, and advising on conference operations and marketing.

    This position also required me to serve as an industry expert and conduct original research. I created the CSI Working Group on Web Security Research Law and authored our inaugural report—the only in-depth research yet conducted on the topic. I also researched, wrote and designed the 2009 edition of the CSI Computer Crime and Security Survey (one of the most widely quoted pieces of cybersecurity research). I have been interviewed by multiple media outlets, including SecurityFocus.com, DarkReading.com, Federal News Radio and National Public Radio's The Sound of Ideas. I taught sessions at CSI conferences and other industry events, organized and moderated panel discussions, and presented a public Webcast to an audience of over 1,800.
