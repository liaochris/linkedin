  • Junior League of Los Angeles - Board Fellows Program

    2009

    The award-winning JLLA Board Fellows program prepares volunteers to become active, productive and effective members of nonprofit Boards of Directors. The Board Fellows Program provides participants with an overview of the role and responsibilities of the board of directors of nonprofit agencies. Participants attend a series of intensive training workshops covering the following topics: Board Basics, Strategic Planning, Financial Management, Fundraising, Marketing, and Evaluation. In addition to course instruction, participants serve the community by completing a six-month internship on the Board of Directors of a nonprofit partner organization. Through this dual-approach of training, participants receive a comprehensive foundation for future nonprofit board positions.

  • American Association of Advertising Agencies - Institute of Advanced Advertising Studies

    Integrated Case Study Training Course 2008

    The IAAS puts young, promising agency professionals into a real-life marketing environment and challenges them to apply their best thinking and efforts to deliver an integrated plan and creative solution for a discerning client.

    IAAS programs are comprehensive evening courses, approximately 13 to 16 weeks long, designed for agency professionals with one to four years of experience. Students attend lectures each week and are required, through teamwork, to develop a total advertising program in their "mini-agencies" for a given product or service. Teams compete by "presenting" to the "client."
