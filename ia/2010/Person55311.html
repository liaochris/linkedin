Desklance Incorporated’s Summary

Desklance is a European, Portuguese multinational content management systems, client relationship management and hosting, domain registration.

2002
Back in early 2002, Desklance surges under the name of PTiGest, a brand new project that raised some major controversy from the Portuguese scene due to its pricing and attitude, which provided some media attention as well for been one of the very few on the market that handle to provide services as low as up to 70% when compared to its competitors.

2003-2004
Following a series of events, Alex Borba (CEO: Chief Executive Officer) and Carla Borba (COO: Chief Operations Officer) ventured toward the online social communication media by releasing Jornal Online, a national non-profit online newspaper that had a daily publishing of up to 30 up-to-date news, articles, opinion writings which soon reached over the 5.000 visitors online per day and was sharing fresh news through an RSS Application on hundreds of blogs online.

2005-2007
Between 2005 and 2007 the Portuguese law changed, and companies with the PT initials on its name were forced to either remove it or re-register under a completely different name. Such was the case for PTiGest which couldn't go on with PT at the beginning of its name. Therefore, and after having some discussion, a new name was found on the form of Desklance (often mentioned as "DESK[l]ANCE" or "DL").

2008
Desklance was re-inventing itself by gaining ground everywhere it was passing, now not only providing the initial products that it had since the very first beginning back in yearly 2002.

2009
Nowadays, Desklance is a name easily recognizable by practically everyone, thanks to the urge to socialize and interact with everyone, keeping as friendly ambience and environment between the working crew and the people that keep on following them either by Twitter, MySpace, FaceBook, Orkut, LinkedIn or even YouTube or by rss subscriptions.
