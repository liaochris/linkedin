David Qu’s Experience

  • Vice President, Solutions Integration

    Allscripts

    (Public Company; MDRX; Computer Software industry)

    July 2010 — Present (2 months)

    David is leading a senior and dedicated team of solutions management at Allscripts Misys with a mission of connecting and integrating all of its enterprise clinical products and solutions across all healthcare vertical market segments, across patient continuum of care and across all healthcare emerging technologies and industry standards.

  • Chief Operating Officer

    CellTrak Technologies

    (Privately Held; Information Technology and Services industry)

    September 2006 — June 2010 (3 years 10 months)

    Chief Operating Officer of CellTrak Technologies. CellTrak Technologies, Inc. is the market leader in providing mobile solutions for home care at the point of care. The company’s flagship product automates and streamlines home care point of care processes using GPS-enabled mobility devices. Key modules include staff scheduling, electronic visit record and patient specific care plans, GPS tracking and travel management, wound care, supply order, clinical messaging, administrative center, staff management dashboard and includes enterprise application integration with all leading back office systems providers for home care. The company's product runs on all major North America carrier network. David provided critical leadership role in helping create the company's initial business plan, go after market strategies and helped the company secure institutional venture capital investment in 2006.

  • Vice President

    Allscripts Healthcare Solutions

    (Public Company; MDRX; Computer Software industry)

    October 2005 — September 2006 (1 year )

    Vice President of Allscripts Healthcare Solutions. Allscripts Healthcare Solutions (MDRX) is the leader of providing Electronic Medical Record (EMR) and Electronic Prescriptions (EP) solutions for the ambulatory healthcare market. After acquiring A4 Healthcare, the combined companies have revenue of $300MM and market cap of $1.0B in 2006. As a senior executive and a member of Executive Management Council (EMG) at Allscripts, my primary focus is developing Electronic Prescription business. Other responsibilities included developing the company’s Patient Health Record (PHR) product in joint venture with Medem, developing the company’s Clinical Trial initiative with Advanced Clinical Solutions and developing new Electronic Prescription standards with the sponsorship from CMS, industry consortium and various business partners.

  • Vice President of Product Management

    Park City Solutions

    (Computer Software industry)

    February 2002 — August 2005 (3 years 7 months)

    Funded by GTCR, Golder Rauner in 1999, Park City Solutions is a leading provider of healthcare information technology interoperability and connectivity solutions, serving the U.S. commercial provider market and the Military Heath Services of the Department of Defense. The award winning emPOWERnetTM product suite provides a unified, Single Sign On platform for interconnecting disparate and distributed clinical applications across the patient’s continuum of care. The product is ranked by KLAS as the top leading solution in the Enterprise Clinical Interoperability and Connectivity market. David provided senior product management and product delivery responsibilities. Park City Solutions was acquired by Medicity, Inc. in 2005.

  • Founder, President and CEO

    Netsteps Technologies

    (Computer Software industry)

    August 1998 — March 2002 (3 years 8 months)

    The company was founded with the vision of connecting, integrating and transforming disparate healthcare legacy systems and providing physician access to patient data in real-time securely over the Internet and mobile platforms. David operated the business with P/L responsibility for 3 years with consecutive growing revenue and positive EBITDA. Customer base expanded from 1 to 25 healthcare inistitutions including the largest health system and IDN in Chicago area. R&D was funded 100% by operating cash flow. The company was acquired by GTCR Golder Rauner in 2002 and merged its operation with GTCR portfolio company Park City Solutions.

  • Management Consulting

    BBN Corporation

    (Information Technology and Services industry)

    February 1996 — July 1998 (2 years 6 months)

    BBN pioneered ARPANET protocol for the US Department of Defense in 1960s. In mid-1990s the company commercialized its technologies and went public on New York Stock Exchange (“BBN”). Being the early adopter of the Internet and World Wide Web technologies, David provided management and technology leadership in deploying multi-million dollar E-commerce and Internetworking solutoins for well known Fortune 50 companies.

  • Product Manager and Software Engineer

    Datastream Systems

    (Public Company; DSTM; Computer Software industry)

    August 1993 — January 1996 (2 years 6 months)

    As one of the top 30 employees of the company, David designed and developed the award winning Windows and handheld based software products for Datastream Systems. The MP2 for Windows 1.0 was the winner of the Product of Year by industry publication. The company went IPO in 1995 with a market capitalization as high as $880MM. The company was the largest public traded company (“NASDAQ: DSTM”) providing software products and services to the Maintenance, Repair and Operating (MRO) industry.
