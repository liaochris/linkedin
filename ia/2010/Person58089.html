Don Cragun’s Summary

Computer Scientist specializing in operating system standards, especially POSIX standards and The Single UNIX Specification. Guided work at Sun Microsystems, Inc. on utilities and libc for simultaneous conformance of SunOS Releases 4.1 through 5.10 to SVID3, X/Open Portability Guide versions 3 and 4, and The Single UNIX Specification issues 1, 2, and 3 (with design work started on issue 4).

Elected to serve as the Organizational Representative from IEEE to The Austin Common Standards Revision Group.

Earlier experience includes porting UNIX System V to the Intel 80286 architecture at Digital Research, Inc.; design, development, and maintenance of MERT, PWB UNIX, and UNIX System V; and working on various directory assistance projects, general payroll, and inventory control programs.

Don Cragun’s Specialties:

Operating Systems Standards: All POSIX standards; all of the SUS (Single UNIX Specification) standards; XPG3 and XPG4 (X/Open Portability Guides), SVID (UNIX System V Interface Definition) versions 1, 2, and 3; UNIX System V ABIs (Generic, x86 processor supplement, and SPARC processor supplement); and the SCD (SPARC Compliance Definition) version 2.x and 3.x ABIs.
