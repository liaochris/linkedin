Steven Frykman, P.E.’s Summary

Registered Professional engineer with a Mechanical Engineering degree that is experienced in Design, Test, and Analysis, with a Six Sigma Green Belt Certification.

Coordinated & scheduled activities with all cross functional teams at my company and outside facilities for product design, and validation testing in a highly regulated industry.

Created test plans, procedures, methods to verify products met the design requirements, conducted / supervised testing, performed product / data analysis and documented the results. Investigated failures that occurred in testing.

Performed design engineering developing tooling, fixtures, and updated parts. Collaborated with all groups on the design worked with manufacturing to produce prototypes, verified the prototype function and FEA analysis. Documented the design concept and result in detailed reports.

Presently have a Secret Government clearance.

Steven Frykman, P.E.’s Experience

  • Sr. Mechanical Engineer

    Ingersoll Rand Thermo King

    (Mechanical or Industrial Engineering industry)

    April 2010 — Present (4 months)

  • Senior Evaluation / Design Engineer

    BAE Systems

    (Public Company; BA.L; Defense & Space industry)

    2006 — 2009 (3 years )

    Scheduled and coordinated verification tests for a major program for the US Navy during the development phase. Reviewed the specified requirements and developed detailed plans to show how the products met the requirements. The testing included RF, load, vibration, shock, environmental, and performance tests. This effort required the following:

    • Designed fixtures and interfaced with the FEA analysis group to ensure designs met the required stress levels.
    • Created test plans / methods / procedures to perform the testing.
    • Researched and ordered the necessary instrumentation.
    • Supervised engineers and technicians performing the testing.
    • Performed data analysis on the test results to find problems with the instrumentation, perform linear regression analysis, and determine trends.

    Designed parts using Pro-E, 3D CAD software / GD&T and interfaced with the materials and FEA analysis group to ensure the designs met the required specifications. Tested the parts to verify the FEA analysis and wrote detailed procedures for the new items for the customer.

  • Senior Engineer / Scientist

    BAE Systems formerly United Defense

    (Mechanical or Industrial Engineering industry)

    2001 — 2006 (5 years )

    Supported a missile launcher development program, which included in house testing and a major test effort at a customer’s facility to qualify a new design which required two tests, an initial test and a retest to resolve major design problems.

    Designed safety fixtures to absorb shock that interfaced with our hardware at a customer’s facility. This required specifying a shock absorbing material and working closely with the FEA analysis group to show the design meet the required stress levels. Documented the design intent and the analysis in detailed reports for the customer.

    Performed systems engineering to determine how requirements were to me met either by test, analysis, or similarity to other systems.

    Researched, specified, and ordered the parts for a high voltage power system for a missile launcher which converted 440AC, 3 phase voltage into 240 AC and 375 DC voltage, which would normally be considered outside my Mechanical Engineering discipline.

    Developed a new laboratory to evaluate a spray cooling system for electronics which required learning about the fluid, performing a material capability study, and researching which equipment to use for this effort. Set up the test for this analysis.

  • Senior Test Engineer

    United Defense

    (Public Company; UDI; Defense & Space industry)

    1995 — 2001 (6 years )

    Performed testing on development hardware. Coordinated & supervised the qualification efforts of a new product at different customers and vendors facilities and was the point of contact to resolve technical issues with the customer on several programs. Wrote procedures for product inspection for the customer.

    • Created a new laboratory to test a spray cooling system in electronics which required learning about the fluid, researching the best equipment for this analysis effort, ordering the necessary equipment, and supervising the evaluation effort.
    • Lead the effort to develop an in-house test fixture saving $200,000 by avoiding going to an outside vendor’s facility. This effort required developing and presenting the concepts to management specifying the major components and supervising engineers performing the detailed design.
