Robert (Bob) Maslyn, PMP, CSWM, ITILv3 Cert.’s Summary

Seeks to transform how government interacts with itself and with citizens by leveraging technologies to enhance digital democracy and enable government to perform superbly for citizens.

More than 30 years career experience in Federal policy development and the application of technology to government programs. Career highlights include (1) created the prototype for FirstGov.gov (now USA.gov); (2) developed portal plans for the Integrated Acquisition Environment; (3) pioneered the use of government multi-application smart cards; (4) created GrantsNet as the basis for creating electronic grants. Significant community organizing experience prior to Federal service.

Robert (Bob) Maslyn, PMP, CSWM, ITILv3 Cert.’s Experience

  • Project Manager in Digital Government

    US General Services Administration

    (Government Agency; Government Administration industry)

    September 1996 — Present (14 years 2 months)

    Currently engaged in eGovernment activities at GSA. Serve in the Office of the Chief Acquisition Officer, Office of Acquisition Systems. Project Manager for the Federal Service Desk (fsd.gov). Managed the Data Quality Review of the Federal Procurement Data System (FPDS) for GSA data. Served on the GSA Enterprise Architecture Program Management Office. Contributes to the development of the Integrated Acquisition Environment (IAE).

    Previously engaged in creating FirstGov.gov as the US Government's first major search portal, developing its prototype and naming it, and in the pioneering efforts of the Office of Smart Card Initiatives to apply multi-application smart cards to government work.

  • Director, Special Grants Initiatives

    U.S. Department of Health and Human Services

    (Government Administration industry)

    July 1978 — September 1996 (18 years 3 months)

    Serving in the Office of the Secretary, I developed special grant initiatives to transform the grants management process. I created GrantsNet, part of Vice President Al Gore’s reinventing government network, which evolved into today's grants.gov. I developed a new classification for Grants Management, to professional that work. I created TAGGS, Tracking Accountability in Government Grants. I participated in the development of numerous grants policies over the years. HHS is the biggest grantor of federal grant money in the US Government, making more grants to more state, local and nonprofit organizations than any other federal agency, and dominates the grant-making influence of Executive Branch agencies.

  • Deputy Executive Director

    Bradford Sullivan, Tioga Counties Drug and Alcohol Abuse Program

    (Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    1974 — 1977 (3 years )

    Under the jurisdiction of the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania, this was a new Drug and Alcohol Program, based in rural Pennsylvania, covering 3,000 square miles and linking three counties. As the Deputy, I built a multi-tiered continuum of care services program, from prevention to inpatient residential treatment to aftercare, which has not existed previously. This required strong community organizing skills working with many communities so as to collaborate on successful delivery of services, in conformance to state standards and policies.

  • Prisons Coordinator

    PROBE, Prisoner Rehabilitation, Inc.

    (Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    1972 — 1974 (2 years )

    PROBE, Prisoner Rehabilitation, Inc. was a non-profit agency funded by United Way and foundations in New York State, based in Binghamton, New York. As a community organizer, I negotiated the release, re-entry and rehabilitation of over 1,000 prisoners to community work, and ran a "rap group" of ex-offenders (even though I have never been incarcerated). This is where I learned about on the streets community organizing – later to be bolstered by my Masters in Social Work education at Syracuse University’s School of Social Work, which had been heavily influenced by the ideas of Saul Alinsky.
