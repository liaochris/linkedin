Fadi Al-Qadi’s Summary

Most of my work experience has been concentrated on human rights, freedom of speech as a human rights issue and journalism in a professional context. Throughout my past work experience I have been acquainted with international mechanisms that provide and/or stipulate for human rights protection; specifically for venerable groups or communities either at times of war or times of peace. In addition; I have carried out specific advocacy activities on issues such as refugees, women rights, child rights and migrant workers rights when I headed the advocacy and communications work for Human Rights Watch in the MENA region.


After being a journalist and editor for quite sometime in early 90’s, I took over a number of assignments with international non profits with a specific focus on assisting emerging independent media and its professional development in the West Bank and later on in Jordan. From 1996 until early 1999 I have managed projects, staff and offices in Jerusalem and Amman on behalf of an international media NGO.

I have also served with MENA local non profits and NGOs such as the Amman based Center for Defending Freedom of Journalists that I co-founded and which provided me with further practical knowledge and expertise regarding training; design of training for specific technical skills such as investigative reporting; media law (known largely in countries of the Middle East as the Press and Publication law) and other aspects of journalism. I also enjoyed serving as a chief editor for an online Internet based radio station; AmmanNet; where I assisted the first generation working in the station in identifying news reporting angles; write and edit pieces for broadcast purposes as well as building up a stable programming pool.

Fadi Al-Qadi’s Specialties:

Communications, Media Relations and Project Management for non-profits in human rights, freedom of expression and social change at large
