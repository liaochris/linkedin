Robert Clay’s Summary

Started business at 19 with no capital. In 3 years achieved 40% penetration of initial market potential compared to 17% nationally for all other brands combined. Achieved much press coverage in Sunday Times, Sunday Telegraph, Guardian, Observer, many regional newspapers & industry publications.

At 23 started franchising. Ran successful marketing campaigns which took business to No.3 in UK within 3 years, with other franchisees worldwide.

Invented the glass sunroof for cars, creating from scratch a market in the UK for more than 250,000 units a year within 3 years, when it became third largest sunroof manufacturer in Europe.

Eventually sold both businesses to one of the largest companies in Europe and was instrumental in taking that business to No.1 in its field in the world within four years.

Set up an unusual decade-long marketing experiment helping hundreds of entrepreneurs to transform their marketing strategies and business fortunes using low-risk/high-return marketing strategies.

Successes include helping a number of business start-ups to achieve market leadership in their field, or in the UK or in Europe or in the world. One was national Ernst & Young “Entrepreneur of the Year” award winner 2002, who a year later appeared in the Sunday Times Rich List and sold out in Dec 07 for £75M. Another was the “Entrepreneur of the Year” runner up the same year. Another award winning client went from start-up to £100M revenues in 7 years.

Since 1996 have developed a unique and priceless 1.8 million+ page knowledgebase detailing the best marketing practices worldwide in every conceivable industry. This is now being systematically developed into a series of modules exclusively available to participants in the Marketing Wizdom Eureka program.

Writes books and columns for magazines, newspapers and online. Highly regarded keynote speaker, meeting or exceeding 99.7% of audience expectations. Runs invitation-only program for aspiring market leaders.

Robert Clay’s Specialties:

Designing low-risk/high-return marketing strategies for entrepreneurs and aspiring market leaders. Providing knowledge, focus, perspectives, strategies, project plans, checklists, documentation, tools and a track to run on in the areas of lead generation, conversion of leads to clients, customer retention, increasing transaction value and increasing transaction frequency. Running seminars, workshops and a program for aspiring market leaders.

  • Founder, Author, speaker and consultant

    DSP Solutions UK Ltd

    (Marketing and Advertising industry)

    June 2000 — December 2008 (8 years 7 months)

    DSP was established as an unusual decade-long marketing experiment. Hundreds of entrepreneurs and business leaders were empowered with world-class knowledge of low-risk/high return marketing strategies.

    Knowledge was imparted via articles, books, manuals, audio CDs, seminars and workshops so clients could implement these strategies for themselves. Ongoing facilitation was provided face-to-face, online, by phone and email to ensure the knowledge was put into practice.

    Many of the participants achieved stunning results and worked with DSP for 2,3,4,5,6,7,8,9 or 10 years. Excellent relationships were also maintained with many former-clients who returned at regular intervals.

    Unusually, also carried out marketing for certain clients with payment based mainly on results.

    The experiment was successful, but I found that working with clients on a one-to-one basis was inefficient, expensive, time consuming and limiting. Due to there only being 24 hours in a day I could only bring my knowledge to bear for a small number of clients. And it also proved near impossible to replicate myself.

    DSP's business model was based on one-to-one consulting which required higher levels of skill and expertise than could be delivered economically, and also was not scalable. I finally figured out a way of working that would allow me to bring that knowledge to a wider range of clients, in an effective and affordable manner ... but for 5 years was too busy to do anything about it.

    At the end of 2008 we were faced with a dilemma. 50% of our clients were in the property, financial services or automotive sectors, all among the worst affected by the recession ... and for no fault of their own. We therefore decided that the time was right to end our decade-long experiment and start pushing ahead with the model we'd had in mind for years, where we could bring the knowledge we'd used to create market leaders to larger numbers of those who aspire to market leadership in their niche.

  • General Manager of Automotive Industry Development

    Tuff-Kote Dinol AB (then part of Axel Johnson Group)

    (Privately Held; 10,001 or more employees; Automotive industry)

    1981 — 1985 (4 years )

    Tuff Kote Dinol, then part of the Multi-Billion Dollar Swedish Axel Johnson Group (second largest company in Scandinavia, owners at the time of Astra Pharmaceutical, Wallenius Shipping Lines, Nynas Oil Company and many others), bought my businesses and I was given the task of convincing car, truck, bus and aircraft manufacturers to adopt our corrosion prevention techniques during production and/or at point of import. Until then they had largely resisted the idea but I developed a very successful strategy and approach to this and in four years secured 100% of contracts we went after. Not one competitor got a single contract in that time. Developed innovative products and application techniques that are still in use today. Literally "wrote the book" on the subject. The company became the largest in its field in the world, absorbing many of its competitors in the process. Brands included: Tuff-Kote Dinol, Dinol, Dinitrol, Tuff-Kote, Bodyshield, Protectol, Endrust and Dual-Gard.

  • Founder and MD

    Skyport Sunroofs UK Ltd (Skyport and Panarama brands)

    (Automotive industry)

    1976 — 1985 (9 years )

    At age 22 invented the glass sunroof as we know it today. Manufactured Skyport and Panarama Sunroofs in the UK and California. The pre-fabricated design of these leak-proof units transformed sunroof fitting from a 2-day skilled operation to a 45 minute semi-skilled operation. Within 3 years of launch, created a market from scratch for 250,000 sunroofs a year in the UK alone, and also sold them worldwide. Supplied the sunroofs to many car manufacturers for production line installation, or fitted at point of import, or sold as branded accessories for dealers to fit. Introduced innovations on factory fitted models like radio antennas screen printed onto the glass panel eliminating vandalism etc.

  • Founder and MD

    Primegraph Ltd (Bodyshield)

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Automotive industry)

    1973 — 1985 (12 years )

    At age 19 developed an innovative method of preventing motor vehicle corrosion. The process rapidly won major acclaim including the AA Seal of Approval, at the time considered unattainable by other companies in the same field. As a consequence, supplied know-how and technology to companies like 3M, Valvoline Oil Company and Burmah Castrol. Entered into a unique joint venture with Burmah Castrol where they developed products to our specification that were sold exclusively by us using the Castrol brand name ... the one and only time in Castrol's 108 year history that a third party has ever been granted exclusivity on a Castrol branded product. Developed new application methods which revolutionised the industry and were subsequently adopted by virtually every car, truck, bus and aircraft manufacturer in the world, and are still in use today. Used an array of self-taught marketing strategies that rapidly turned the industry on its head and took my company to prominence in its field.
