Ahmed Aldaly’s Summary

Ahmed joined ABN AMRO Risk Advisory Services on Nov. 2005 as member of a team mandated for 3 years to restructure Banque Misr.
During early phase of the project Ahmed developed an Excel based Obligor Risk Rating Model based on the methodology of ABN AMRO in developing rating models. Also, he designed Credit Proposal Memorandum and Independent Credit Risk Advice formats for Corporate Banking and Risk Management in addition to the instructions manuals .Also, he developed with RAS team Draft Risk Charter for Banque Misr.
Ahmed provided lectures to Banque Misr and Banque Du Caire staff on rating model implementation and writing skills for credit proposal memorandums. Also, developed and follows up implementation of Credit Administration & Control function. Also, he has developed a Corporate Loan Approval Process . In addition, Ahmed is now contributing to Non Performing Loans in Banque Misr & including; identification of NPLs, design a loan loss provisioning policy and process, and design a management organisation.
Before joining ABN AMRO Bank, Ahmed was Credit Risk Officer in Misr International Bank, where he was responsible of study & review of Credit Proposal Memorandums of corporate clients to assess credit risks as well as obligor risk rating & make the appropriate recommendations.
Before MIBank, Ahmed was a Deputy Manager in Arab African International Bank where his main responsibilities were to prepare and present credit memorandums, including facility structure, financial and risk analysis. Also, develop and maintain corporate relationships of the existing accounts.
Before AAIB, Ahmed was a Senior Investment Officer in Prime Capital,

Before Prime Capital, Ahmed was a Deputy Relationship Manager in Egyptian American Bank where he started his career in the Corporate Banking Division.
