Katrina Scott’s Summary

Although my schooling and work resume thus far has been fashion focused, interior design and textiles has been a huge part of my life since I was young. Growing up in a family of antiquers, we spent many weekends vintage shopping, scouring fabric & wall paper stores and revamping old finds.  Our homes were always pulled together with furniture, decor and ideas that rotated between my mother's, aunt's and grandmother's homes and the vintage stores or spaces they operated.  It was the best foundation of space planning and appreciation for antiques I could have asked for.

More recently, my passion for styling and interior design has driven me to find ways to make the transition into an industry I really care about. In addition to designing a few of my own NYC apartments (for which prospective clients are always welcome to view), I have built a strong portfolio over the past year reflecting the designing and re-working of spaces simply from networking with friends and colleagues. For me, being able to create and execute a vision for a space that encompasses one’s personality and needs is most gratifying.  The joy I get out of these projects, and positive feedback received thus far, has solidified my desire to pursue my true calling in life.
