Nick Ashton-Hart’s Experience

  • Senior Director for Participation and Engagement

    Internet Corporation for Assigned Names and Numbers, Inc. (ICANN)

    (Non-Profit; International Affairs industry)

    December 2009 — Present (4 months)

    ICANN views increasing participation across all stakeholder communities as a strategic priority. This means recruiting new participants, and new communities of interests who are not presently involved, but also to empower existing volunteer communities so that all feel their time spent with ICANN is increasingly valuable and effective. This position is responsible for all ICANN’s participation mechanisms, from public comment processes to meetings to publications.

  • Principal

    Consensus Optimus

    (Privately Held; Information Technology and Services industry)

    January 2005 — Present (5 years 3 months)

    as Principal of Consensus Optimus, Nick provides specialised services to non-governmental organisations, civil society representatives, for-profit and non profit industrial representatives in coalition-building, multilateral treaty development and strategies for success in working with UN agencies and member states. He specialises in helping disparate groups find common ground on difficult issues and then helping take that consensus and effectively represent it to audiences across the spectrum, political, industrial, for-profit and non-profit. He has acted for organisations as diverse as consumer representatives, online civil rights NGOs, intellectual property rights holders, and some of the world's most famous and successful Fortune 50 enterprises, and trade associations representing their interests.

  • Director for At-Large

    Internet Corporation for Assigned Names and Numbers (ICANN)

    (Non-Profit; Internet industry)

    September 2006 — December 2009 (3 years 4 months)

    Responsible for the delivery of supporting services and management of staff associated with the At-Large (individual Internet users) constituency of ICANN, the international not-for-profit organisation which is responsible for the top-level management of the Internet domain name and number system. A complex role involving the employment of diplomatic, organisational development, and consensus-facilitation for stakeholders throughout the world and from all different socio-economic backgrounds, as well as providing strategic advice to the senior staff of the organisation. The position entails responsibility for a seven-figure budget and associated staff. My tenure began as a consultant, with my appointment to Director being made in January 2007.

  • Director of Strategy

    Lansharks (UK) Ltd.

    (Information Technology and Services industry)

    December 1999 — January 2007 (7 years 2 months)

    Responsible for development of the overall strategic direction of the business. Assisted the MD with capacity planning and premier client relationship management for selected longterm client relationships remaining from my term as Managing Director.

  • Executive Director

    International Music Managers Forum (IMMF)

    (Non-Profit; 1-10 employees; International Trade and Development industry)

    January 2002 — January 2005 (3 years 1 month)

    Chief Executive of the organisation, a worldwide umbrella non-governmental organisation representing popular musicians and their managers.
    Responsible for managing finances, administrative affairs, and staff, answering directly to the Secretary General and the Council.
    Developed annual budgets and successfully piloted these budgets through an approval process involving both the executive Council as well as the General Assembly of all members.
    Key role in developing policy objectives and strategies for consideration by Council and General Assembly of the organisation. Coordinated the meetings of those bodies, including draft agendas and preparation of all documents required.
    Main representative for the organisation with the UN and its specialised agencies, in particular WIPO, member state delegations of all levels, the European Commission and Parliament, and other INGOs

  • Managing Director

    Subversive Music Management Ltd

    (Entertainment industry)

    March 2000 — April 2004 (4 years 2 months)

    Responsible for all aspects of the professional careers of some of the music world’s most successful and influential featured artists, from James Brown (“The Godfather of Soul”) to Heaven 17. This included: negotiating with and managing relationships with major recording companies, concert promoters, music publishers, broadcasters, and all other third-parties – relationships which individually could generate more than a million dollars yearly for the artist. Helped ‘reposition’ these artists to be more relevant and ‘cool’ for modern audiences in global PR campaigns. Managing the affairs of a legendary artist like James Brown is the summit of this global profession and involves responsibility for many millions of dollars annually in turnover from a wide variety of sources.

  • Partner / Managing Director

    Lansharks EIS, Inc. / Lansharks (UK) Ltd.

    (Information Technology and Services industry)

    January 1997 — March 2000 (3 years 3 months)

    Successfully managed this IT consultancy business, including relocating the business from the USA to the UK whilst growing revenues and customer relationships. Proven results as a senior consultant, helping companies of all sizes successfully develop and implement IT strategies to maximise strategic business advantage. Periodically served in a consulting capacity to selected clients, primarily when the engagement required acting as an interim IT Director/CTO/CIO. These engagements routinely involved decisions on large capital equipment and services purchasing, as well as management of dozens of IT staff in high-pressure environments undergoing rapid change. Often involved in the selection process of permanent staff to replace me in these interim posts. Proven track record in finding and implementing innovative, cost-effective solutions to a wide range of business issues. During my tenure as MD, the company never lost a client to a competitor or due to client dissatisfaction.
