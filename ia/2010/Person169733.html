Ray Solnik’s Summary

Ray heads Buena Vista, a management services and consulting company that helps enterprise and entrepreneurial technology services companies accelerate the start and growth of new businesses.

Prior experience includes multiple enterprise and midsized business P&L leadership roles a president and COO. Extensive business development experience.

Ray Solnik’s Specialties:

P&L management, business development, product marketing, negotiations, finance, strategy, strategic planning, program management, process improvement, turnaround / business transformation, restarts.

Ray Solnik’s Experience

  • Principal

    Buena Vista Business Services, LLC

    (Information Technology and Services industry)

    August 2006 — Present (3 years 7 months)

    Buena Vista is a management services and consulting company that helps enterprise and entrepreneurial companies accelerate the start and growth of new and existing businesses. We leverage technology and functional expert resources from various industries to best serve our clients’ unique interests and objectives. Companies served include Dolores Labs (aka CrowdFlower) Dreyfuss & Birke (an NYSE:NFP company), Enable Networks, MarketTools (Zoomerang Product), OpSource, SIIA, Softletter, Xerox PARC, and others.

  • President & COO

    OpSource

    (Privately Held; Information Technology and Services industry)

    September 2006 — July 2008 (1 year 11 months)

    OpSource specializes in helping software companies migrate their business onto the web delivering their software as a service (SaaS). The compnay provides the infrastructure services (like managing web servers, application servers, databases, network connectivity, load balancers, firewalls, security), application management, and user management & support for on-demand business. We allow our customers to buy this platform the same way they sell their products, per user, per transaction or per customer.

    OpSource also delivers services for web applications companies, online services companies, OnDemand companies and traditional enterprise software companies making the move in to SaaS, such as Adobe, Agile Software (now Oracle), Business Objects (now SAP), and Taleo.

  • Chief Development Officer

    New Edge Networks

    (Public Company; ELNK; Telecommunications industry)

    May 2004 — December 2005 (1 year 8 months)

    Business development, corporate development, and leader of the company's Retail Broadband Alliance (the RBA). Developed and implemented a creative PCI DSS (payment card industry security standard) solution that involved direct network connections to American Express, ChasePaymentech, and First Data to serve the multi-site retail, restaurant, and convenience store vertical markets.

  • President, AT&T WorldNet Service and Vice President, Strategic Markets

    AT&T

    (Public Company; T; Telecommunications industry)

    2002 — 2004 (2 years )

    Led the transformation of this top 10, multi-hundred million dollar revenue Internet Service Provider (ISP) from significant negative cash flow and earnings to significant positive cash flow and revenue growth. Results including improving cash flow by over $100M from prior year to first year of p&l responsibility. Results driven by new strategy and implementation of a focused set of key initiatives including launching a national broadband solution, restructuring channels, and developing new product offers.

    Led the marketing launch of AT&T Consumer's voice over IP (VoIP) product, AT&T CallVantage.

  • SVP, Business Development and Interim CEO, VersaPoint
