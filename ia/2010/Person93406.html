Jeff Lintz’s Summary

As the Director of Applications Engineering for RainTrapper Environmental Solutions I evaluate our clients' specific needs and physical plants to determine the proper configuration and deployment of our ACTive HVAC and Refrigeration efficiency enhancement systems. Most green initiatives cost money, our saves you around 15%! While our rain capture systems cost effectively capture a valuable resource that is too often ignored and wasted, RainTrapper goes one step further in the realm of environmental sustainability by linking the water store to our unique ACTive system that has solved the problem of condenser scaling that others' adiabatic methods suffer from. RainTrapper's patented water treatment process binds to minerals in the water supply and renders them unable to stick to the condenser fins.

An ACTive adiabatic system will reduce the energy consumption of commercial air cooled HVAC/R by approximately 15%, and often more. Depending on the load to tonnage ratio, especially for systems that are old, undersized, located in marine locales, or attempting to cope with the additional heat from computers, an ACTive system dramatically increases HVAC efficiency. This allows the total system to conserve energy; by increasing the condenser's heat transfer differential and directly lowering compressor current draw, by producing colder air at the evaporator and through longer off cycles. Lower operating loads have the additional benefit of extending equipment's useful lifetime. Taken together these factors yield a return on investment in the two-year range for the typical 40+ ton unit. In refrigeration situations, such as in a supermarket, where the costs of cooling food can run upwards of 60% of the electric bill, a 15% savings translates into an ROI of only one year, or even less. The same is true for data centers and hospital surgeries, and wherever there is a 24 hour demand for cooling.
