Matt Penny’s Experience

  • Database Administrator

    Sony

    (Public Company; SNE; Consumer Electronics industry)

    September 2001 — Present (8 years 6 months)

    I was DBA for Sony Professional. Sony Professional has also been known as ‘Sony Broadcast and Professional Europe’ and ‘Sony Professional Services Europe’.

    My achievements have included

    - Developing an Oracle patching procedure

    - Writing and agreeing a Database Security Policy

    - Consolidating 24 production databases down to 2 main databases

    - Managing the migration of a key database from Sybase to SQLserver

    - Implementing Oracle Text. This made a big difference to search times on the website

    - Creating the Environmental Compliance website in Sharepoint

  • DBA Team Leader

    London Electricity

    (Public Company; Utilities industry)

    January 2000 — June 2001 (1 year 6 months)

    After working entirely on recoverability for more than a year, I came back to more general DBA work. By this time, we were in the process of moving all the systems from London to the data centre in Plymouth. There were 6 other DBAs in the team. Our key achievements during this time were:

    - Successfully transferring all of our databases

    - A ‘strong handover’ for all of our systems to the Plymouth team

    - Maintaining and sometimes improving the existing levels of service.

  • Recovery Specialist

    London Electricity

    (Public Company; Utilities industry)

    June 1998 — December 1999 (1 year 7 months)

    Although my job title didn’t change from ‘Database Administrator’ my role changed to focus entirely on the recoverability of the ‘de-regulated market’ systems. The technical landscape was built around TSM, Oracle’s Enterprise Backup Utility, RMAN, AIX, Oracle Parallel Server (the precursor of RAC – Real Application Clusters) and both standalone RS-6000s and an ‘SP’ frame. The key achievements were:

    - Ensuring the recoverability of 100+ databases. I never failed to get a database back!

    - Proving recoverability at externally audited DR tests.

  • Senior Database Administrator

    London Electricity

    (Public Company; Utilities industry)

    January 1998 — June 1998 (6 months)

    The company is now part of EDF Energy.

    I joined London Electricity at an interesting time. The company had been privatised in the early 1990s, and was in the process of preparing for the de-regulation of the electricity and gas markets. Shortly after that the company was purchased by EDF Energy, who moved much of the IT Operation to the SWEB datacentre in Plymouth.

  • DBA

    T-Mobile

    (Public Company; DT; Telecommunications industry)

    1996 — 1998 (2 years )

    One2one (now T-Mobile) was an interesting place to work. We were the first mass-market mobile network - during the time I was there we went from having no customers to having over a million. I supported many systems, but probably the most challenging was the ‘Front End System’, which supported the call centres. I specialized in the following:

    - Performance tuning – the call centre’s response time was featured on the BBC’s ‘Watchdog’ programme more than once, so the database performance was relatively high profile!

    - Monitoring – I was the DBA within the team responsible for configuring the BMC Patrol interface

    - Documentation – I created documentation templates for all of the systems

  • Junior Programmer to Consultant

    Cap Gemini (Hoskyns)

    (Public Company; CAP; Information Technology and Services industry)

    September 1988 — January 1996 (7 years 5 months)

    I joined Hoskyns, as it was then called, a year after finishing college. The work was project based – much of the time was spent out at clients’ offices. I was, and am, proud of the fact that I only spent 2 weeks not allocated to a project. Projects included:

    Jewsons (1989-1992) – MUMPS programming, system test, functional and technical specification

    Crown Prosecution Service (1992-1993) – Oracle programming and system test

    T-Mobile (1993 on) – Oracle programming, team leading, main client contact
