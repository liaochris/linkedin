Anne-Marie Rábago’s Summary

Anne-Marie is an attorney licensed to practice in California and Texas. Ms. Rábago serves clients in the areas of tax controversy and planning. Currently, she practices law in San Diego and is focused on taxpayer advocacy issues.

Ms. Rábago is a Cum Laude graduate of Ohio University where she was a member of Golden Key International Honour Society and Lambda Pi Eta Communication Honor Society. In addition, she was named an Ohio University Outstanding Senior Leader.

Anne-Marie also graduated from California Western School of Law, where she received her Juris Doctor. While attending California Western, Ms. Rabago was invited to compete as a member of the trial team and the ABA’s National Appellate Advocacy Competition team. She was selected as an Honors Instructor for legal research and writing courses, received the Dean’s Merit Scholarship, and was named a Distinguished Advocate for her First Place finish in the First Year Appellate Competition.

After completing her J.D. degree, Ms. Rábago attended Northwestern University School of Law, where she received her Master of Laws in Taxation. While there, she served as a volunteer Tax Preparer with the Tax Assistance Program. In addition, Anne-Marie won First Place in the LL.M. Division of the ABA’s Law Student Tax Challenge.

Upon graduation, Anne-Marie worked for PricewaterhouseCooper in the area of International Tax Services. She advised clients in a variety of industries on complicated domestic and international tax matters. Prior to her legal career, Ms. Rábago worked in nonprofit fundraising and was a human resources professional.

Ms. Rábago is active in the San Diego legal community as a volunteer with the Legal Aid Society’s Low Income Taxpayer Clinic and a member of the San Diego County Bar Association. In her spare time, Anne-Marie can be found enjoying the sights and sounds of San Diego’s East Village. She is an avid craft beer enthusiast and enjoys traveling abroad with her husband.
