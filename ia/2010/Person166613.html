Basil Miller’s Summary

'The supreme wordsmith' — one testimonial.

Basil Miller is a writer, journalist and expert in corporate communications with over 30 years experience in newspapers and magazines as reporter, feature writer, sub-editor and editor.

He has worked across Europe, with his work appearing in the Irish Times, the Guardian (London), Irish Independent, Sunday Tribune, European Times, Independent (London), the Philadelphia Inquirer and others, as well as on radio and TV.

Since 2001 he has worked as a communications consultant for corporates, social organisations and NGOs. Recently, his main focus has been on developing the new website of the Wellbeing Foundation into one which delivers significant services and assistance direct to existing users and anyone affected by psychosocial distress.

Biotechnology, especially in the area of biofuels and biomass projects, has become a major zone of interest over the past five years, and Miller would like to get involved in start-up projects in this field.

As reporter and foreign correspondent, Miller was chef de bureau for United Press International based in Netherlands, where he covered high-level events such as meetings of the EU Council, NATO, European Space Agency, etc., plus Dutch politics, society and general news. He spent some years as a correspondent for the European Times, and was about to take up the post of environment editor when that magazine closed. As a roving correspondent, he reported on crisis hotspots and many significant events for a range of outlets in Europe, the US, Australia and South Africa.

As a sub-editor and editor in Ireland, he has worked with Irish Times, Irish Press (RIP) and the Sunday Business Post before setting up publicEye media. He has long experience as copy editor and editor with several prestigious academic publishers and, since 2000, in creating focused website content.

Basil Miller’s Specialties:

Media relations, corporate communications, web content, marketing communications, PR, graphic design, editing, and web development, together with project management in all these fields. PublicEye media offers special low-cost packages to Irish charities, NGOs, social and community organisations which wish to outsource for web, media and communications services.
