Alex Ponce de Leon’s Summary

I have assisted in representing both companies and individuals in matters ranging from stock options backdating and trade secret misappropriation to breach of contract and investigations brought by the Securities and Exchange Commission and U.S. Department of Justice.

I have extensive experience representing technology companies -- over 75 percent of my legal practice has been in the defense of traditional and entrepreneurial high-tech companies including Marvell Technology Group, Computer Associates, Selectica, Wireless Facilities, eTreppid, and Ingenio.

In my previous position as a litigator at Pillsbury Winthrop Shaw Pittman, I had the opportunity to independently manage cases, argue motions, take and defend depositions, author numerous pleadings, and second-chair a week long jury trial. I have fine tuned my writing, research, and oral advocacy skills while pursuing a challenging and demanding caseload.

I have experience handling every phase of complex litigation – including real estate fraud, stock options backdating, banking fraud, employment, and securities litigation.

I have led several complex e-discovery efforts and have experience with the technologies available to facilitate such projects. I recently managed an attorney team to a review over six million documents and coordinated productions to opposing counsel.
