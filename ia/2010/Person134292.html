Mark Nichols’s Summary

- Med school faculty, consultant, and expert witness for 20+ yrs in molecular pharmacology, oncology, and endocrinology, with a focus on estrogens, steroids, hormone responsive, lung, and other cancers. Widely published scientist and teacher. Invented patented biotechnology; knowledge of pharmaceutical drug development, safety. Highly skilled in interpreting relevant meaning from complex scientific literature. Can make a strong contribution as a scientific adviser on legal or patent cases, member of a Medical Science Liaison team, or as an analyst of investment opportunities.

- My research lab of 12 yrs studies steroid hormone receptors, the estrogen receptors, and their role in normal and in cancer tissue. Goals using IRB approved preclinical studies include endocrine therapies for treating breast and other estrogen responsive cancers while maintaining positive estrogenic effects against bone loss and menopausal symptoms. Includes study of SERMs (selective estrogen receptor modulators) and aromatase inhibitors.

- 4 publications so far in 2009-10, including
Nichols et al. Breast-cancer derived M543V mutation in helix 12 of ERα inverts response to estrogen and SERMs. Breast Cancer Res & Treat, 120: 761-8, 2010

  • Visiting Researcher- Biomedical Lab Research

    European Molecular Biology Lab (EMBL), Heidelberg, Germany

    (Research industry)

    1992 — March 1998 (6 years )

    Estrogen Receptor structure-function, FLP site-specific recombinases
    Visiting Research Associate, Gene Expression Program. A Genetic Screen for Ligand Binding by the Human Estrogen Receptor. FLP recombinase was fused with the human estrogen receptor ERα to report estrogenic ligand activity in yeast, where genetics could be used to select important amino acids for ER function. Guided lab project to perform extensive in vitro codon-substitution mutagenesis of ERα transactivation domain, followed by a novel yeast genetic color and growth assay. Used optimized 32P radiolabeled RNA probes for use in hybridization blotting for very high sensitivity and quantification. Developed unique yeast plate assay to report estrogen binding as an inherited genetic change.

    Post-Doc work in Gene expression- RNA Polymerase II Transcription Control
    EMBO Fellowship/post-doctorate focused on identification and characterization of proteins that interact at a liver specific enhancer upstream of the tyrosine aminotransferase (TAT) gene. Study aims were on cell-specific and hormone inducible transcription signals that activate these proteins in mammalian cells (cAMP and glucocorticoids). Extensive research problem solving. Made numerous nuclear and whole-cell protein extracts from liver tissue for transcription factor purification. Initiated successful use of FPLC protein purification equipment. Became expert in running 2-D protein gels and studying phosphorylation patterns on transcription factors. Set up and utilized band-shift experiments to study protein-DNA interactions.
