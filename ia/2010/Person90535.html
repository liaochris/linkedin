  • Manager, Accounting and Finance Programs

    NACUBO

    (Non-Profit; Higher Education industry)

    May 1999 — February 2004 (4 years 10 months)

    Located in Washington, D.C., NACUBO serves a membership of more than 2,500 colleges, universities, and higher education service providers across the country. NACUBO represents chief administrative and financial officers through a collaboration of knowledge and professional development, advocacy, and community.

    NACUBO was a foundational opportunity for me to grow into a professional. I was promoted from Accountant, to Senior Accountant, then into a Manager of Accounting and Finance Programs position.

I enjoyed coordinating with external auditors on principle matters and had the unique opportunity to research Sarbanes Oxley (SOX) pronouncements as they related to GASB.

    I lead the development of a revised Basic Instutional Accounting text used by accounting departments in higher education and uncovered $200,000 of lost publication revenue.

    My proudest accomplishment however was earning the respect of a diverse, – age, race, gender and tenure – 5 person department even as its youngest member.

    Near the end of my tenure I had transitioned to a role where the focus was on GASB publication development. At the time it was too early for me to leave nuts and bolts accounting and I soon accepted a postion that was on the Controller track at Adesa.
