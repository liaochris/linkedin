Mike Arcuri’s Summary

Mike is currently the CEO of World Blender, where he's focused on making people's worlds come alive through imaginative play by building the next generation platform for social, location-based games.

Mike is an inventor and product leader at heart. His professional passion is inventing new product ideas that solve real problems, hammering out designs that are simple and enjoyable to use, and working day in and day out with great teams to make these ideas into reality. He gets his biggest thrills by interacting with and improving products in progress, learning from the market as it reacts to products, and seeing things really gel when a great product/market fit is achieved.

Mike has built products and led teams for close to 15 years, most recently as the head of Breakthrough Mobile, and as an executive focused on mobile products and internet services at Photobucket and Ontela. Prior to his start-up work, Mike spent nearly a decade leading teams in developing new products and improving some of the most-used software in the world at Microsoft. Mike has 9 patents to his name and 9 additional patents pending.

Skills:
• Product management: team leadership, product planning, interaction design, usage metrics and goals, conversion goals and measurement, art direction, program management, release management, project management, algorithm and technical design, user research, technical documentation
• Engineering management: aligning output with the business, hiring, agile/scrum, spiral, and waterfall
• Marketing: goal-setting, B2B, B2B2C, branding, positioning, messaging, product naming, promotions, CPM and CPA performance tuning, PR
• IP portfolio development and management

Mike Arcuri’s Specialties:

1. Developing product strategies for early-stage startup companies and executing on those strategies to bring terrific new products to market
2. Making relentless forward progress toward product goals with any kind of team and with any size budget
3. Designing products for massive markets and millions of users (Ontela's automatic photo uploaders, Microsoft Office's user interface)
4. Refining and reinventing category-leading products (e.g. Microsoft Excel, Outlook, Frontpage)

  • President

    Breakthrough Mobile

    (Computer Software industry)

    July 2010 — October 2010 (4 months)

    I founded Breakthrough Mobile based on my belief that product-focused consulting could be done much better if it were simply done with greater commitment. By this, I mean a consultant-client relationship that’s given all the focus and talent of a full-time product leadership position, and a product leader that wants nothing more than to design the right product for the client’s business and market, aid in building and refining this product, release it to the world, and evaluate and improve it based on its performance. After 14 years of designing, building, and leading product teams, this focus on the customer and the product is deeply ingrained in my personality and work ethic and I can’t imagine feeling satisfied without it. Now this is the core principle of how we operate at Breakthrough Mobile.

    The rules we live by that make us different:

    1. At Breakthrough Mobile, a product leader only accepts one significant client relationship at a time. We don’t have issues about which clients to service the best in a given month, or which client’s products are still running through our mind when we go home at night.
    2. We seek out client relationships where we can work as part of the team and stay with the team long enough to build and release the products we’re helping to plan and design.
    3. To ensure we leave all our clients in great shape to continue executing without us, we commit to minimum service durations and achieving major milestones such as product release or hiring permanent product leaders before transitions.
    4. All client engagements start with clear product goals and these goals and the plans to achieve them remain an open book to the client throughout the full term of the relationship.
    5. Every product leader we hire is high enough caliber to be one of the first 10 employees at a well-run tech startup or get an offer from the most demanding blue-chip companies.
