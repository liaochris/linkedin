Colin Bateman’s Summary

I am a writer/entrepreneur hybrid.

For those who fall on this page and are curious as to what I am up to, the answer is that I am working on a unique business model for my new company, Wojolo. I want the foundation to be extremely strong before promoting it, but in a nutshell, it's a 'Free Business Encyclopedia'. The model evolved out of the first company I created, LanguageMeet (which I have set aside temporarily) and the courses I have been taking at the SAJE Montreal Metro (a government-funded school whose curriculum is designed to help ambitious entrepreneurs like myself).

At the moment Wojolo.com is on track to be released by mid-Summer 2010. Yes, I'm excited!

Colin Bateman’s Experience

  • Project Manager

    Wojolo

    (Internet industry)

    January 2010 — Present (7 months)

    Wojolo is a Free Business Encyclopedia that is especially attractive for small businesses. Wojolo offers a Wikipedia-like platform for them to tell their story and link to all the search words they can think of. The idea was spawned when I realized that my LanguageMeet website would take a long time to get to the top of any Google searches and the Yellow Pages didn't meet my needs as a unique new business. Also, despite a Montreal Gazette article and a radio interview on the Team 990, I still didn't qualify for a Wikipedia entry. Thanks to being accepted to the SAJE Montreal program in December 2009 and the experience gained from starting the virtual language school, I have been able to build up a new business model and hope to have Wojolo open by mid-summer 2010.

  • President

    LanguageMeet

    (Education Management industry)

    September 2008 — January 2010 (1 year 5 months)

    LanguageMeet was the culmination of a lifetime goal to create a unique language school. For over a year I organized language classes in French, Italian, Mandarin and Spanish at ethnic restaurants throughout Montreal. Learn Italian while you eat Italian! The business model was based on the purchase of a 15% discount card while students were asked to cover teacher and meal costs. After being accepted to the SAJE Montreal Entrepreneurship program in December 2009, I quickly realized after 2 classes that there was a much bigger opportunity before me, and LanguageMeet evolved into Wojolo, the company I am currently developing.
