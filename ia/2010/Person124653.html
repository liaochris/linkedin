  • Campaign Director

    Oxfam America

    (Non-Profit; Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    January 2006 — March 2008 (2 years 3 months)

    ~Directed the agency’s campaign to reform the U.S. Farm Bill—a five-year, $300 billion piece of legislation that governs America’s food, farm and conservation policy. The campaign won more than a billion dollars in new funding for minority farmers in the United States; billions for domestic hunger prevention; improvements to the international food aid system; and successfully generated pro-reform editorials in nearly every major newspaper in America.

    ~Set strategy and managed key agency resources; including a multimillion-dollar budget and a team of nearly 30 staff responsible for lobby; communications; research; organizing; media outreach; online advocacy and fundraising; policy development and analysis; and popular mobilization.

    ~Implemented new systems, protocols and tools to boost organizational effectiveness—including constituent resource management (CRM).

    ~Pioneered tactics that enhanced campaign impact, such as grassroots house parties, phone banking, print and broadcast advertising in targeted Congressional districts, and a national community leader action program.

  • Director of Sustainable Coffee

    Green Mountain Coffee Roasters

    (Public Company; 501-1000 employees; GMCR; Consumer Goods industry)

    2004 — 2006 (2 years )

    ~Advised senior leadership on strategies and policies that transformed the company into a more sustainable enterprise.

    ~Developed a triple-bottom line—integrating new social and environmental bottom lines aligned with the United Nations’ Millennium Development Goals.

    ~Fair Trade Certified coffee sales grew 51% during my tenure due to the implementation of innovative marketing strategies, introduction of new products and the acquisition of new customers, such as McDonalds.

    ~Collaborated with CEO and VP of Corporate Social Responsibility to develop key metrics, indicators and content for the company’s first annual corporate social responsibility report.

    ~Led the company’s humanitarian response to supply-chain partners devastated by the Asian Tsunami (Ache, Indonesia) and Central America and México’s Hurricane Stan.

  • Coffee Program Manager

    Oxfam America

    (Non-Profit; Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    January 2000 — December 2003 (4 years )

    ~Led the coffee component of Oxfam International’s Make Trade Fair campaign, which sought to end the widespread humanitarian crisis ignited by an unprecedented price collapse.

    ~Highly successful campaign drove the U.S. to rejoin the International Coffee Organization and secured the introduction of Fair Trade Certified coffee by some of America’s largest coffee sellers, including Procter & Gamble (Millstone) and Dunkin’ Donuts.

    ~Represented agency as lead spokesperson on coffee and fair trade with key media (New York Times, CNBC, Fortune Magazine, Wall Street Journal, National Public Radio, USA Today, Washington Post, National Geographic and BBC) and in other appropriate venues (coffee industry conferences, United States Congress and foreign governments).

    ~Traveled to Costa Rica, El Salvador, Ethiopia, Guatemala, México and Nicaragua to introduce corporate executives, journalists and celebrities to the realities of coffee farming families.
