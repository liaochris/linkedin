Robert M. Grom’s Summary

Results-oriented, energetic leader with over 25 years non-profit management experience including more than 19 years in community development, fund raising, grant making and policy development. Established track record forging productive relationships with constituents and with governmental, nonprofit, and community organizations. Ability to focus diverse participants on a common goal and motivate them to reach it. Proven expertise in envisioning and then realizing innovative, effective, cost-efficient programs that reflect the values, diversity, aspirations, and priorities of the communities served.

Robert M. Grom’s Experience

  • Vice Chairman

    Three Rivers Workforce Investment Board

    (Non-Profit; Government Relations industry)

    2008 — 2010 (2 years )

    The Three Rivers Workforce Investment Board informs decision making on local and regional development to ensure that current and future market needs of businesses and job seekers are met. TRWIB fulfills its mission through: 1) Acquiring, managing, interpreting, and sharing relevant workforce data; 2) Identifying and researching critical workforce issues; 3) Consulting and advising on public policy with employers and employment sectors.

    As the leading knowledge broker on regional workforce trends, needs, and characteristics, TRWIB strategically informs economic and community development efforts. Ultimately, TRWIB intelligence helps Southwestern Pennsylvania optimize its greatest asset—the workforce—to attract and retain businesses that result in the region’s economic gain.

    (Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    Chief Executive of this proactive community foundation (hospital conversion) that works as a coalition builder for volunteers and professionals interested in promoting the well-being of citizens and improving the quality of life in the Foundation’s service area. Demonstrated ability to work collaboratively with a broad constituency to secure charitable contributions, and distribute them thoughtfully and appropriately. Proficient at identifying and obtaining support from national, regional, statewide, and local sources, including larger foundations, government, corporations and businesses, service organizations, community groups, and individuals.

  • Vice President of Development

    Braddock Medical Center

    (Hospital & Health Care industry)

    November 1994 — December 1996 (2 years 2 months)

    Served as the first professional development officer for this regional health care provider serving 19 distressed communities in Southwestern Pennsylvania's Monongahela River area. Successfully transitioned organization from an endowment management arm of the hospital to a free-standing, community-based charitable foundation following the merger of hospital with the UPMC Health System in 1996.

    Responsible for complete design, start-up, and management of a full-scale development program for this 250- bed community hospital. Specific responsibilities included board and donor development, annual giving, grant writing, planned giving, donor relations, donor recognition, community relations and marketing.

    Directed conversion of organization into a free-standing community foundation following merger of hospital with UPMC Health System. Led strategic planning process that incorporated community development, fund raising and grant making strategies.

  • Vice President of Development

    The Western Pennsylvania Hospital Foundation

    (Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    March 1992 — November 1994 (2 years 9 months)

    Served as senior development officer for this $42 million organization that raised, managed and distributed funds to support the interests of The Western Pennsylvania Hospital– providing quality health care, community service, education and research. A critical component of this position entailed the mobilization of people and resources to enable the hospital to provide technologically advanced care while supporting various cutting-edge biomedical research projects and management of a state-of-the-art research facility.

  • Development Consultant

    The Western Pennsylvania Hospital Foundation

    (Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    January 1987 — March 1992 (5 years 3 months)

    Developed institutional history program supporting fund raising efforts of The Western Pennsylvania Hospital. Researched, documented, preserved, and promoted the hospital’s history and the medical history of Pittsburgh. Interacted with professional societies, other health-related institutions, and private and governmental agencies in support of the efforts.

    Led development team that created a health care museum and conference facility. Served as project director for facility and exhibit design and construction.

    Researched, wrote, and published monographs, articles, and brochures on various subjects related to institutional and regional medical history. Presented numerous public presentations on related topics.
