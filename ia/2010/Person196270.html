John Stewart’s Summary

John Stewart is Vice President, Corporate Marketing for Progress Software and is responsible for leading Progress’s global public and media relations, industry analyst relations, investor relations, employee and executive communications, web marketing, brand management, advertising, and creative services .

He is also an executive member of Progress’s OPSCOM (operations committee), the cross-functional team responsible for company-wide communications.

John started his career at IBM, where he was a member of the team that launched the PC XT in the UK. Next, he joined GRAND METROPOLITAN, broadening his marketing role with brand management.

He then joined COMPUTERVISION, a CAD/CAM software company (later acquired by PRIME COMPUTER), and was based in Brussels, where he ran European marketing communications and sales training. After that, he was posted to Hong Kong, where he managed marketing for Asia Pacific and ran distributor operations. John was promoted to senior director of worldwide marketing communications and stationed at head-office in Bedford, MA, USA.

He then joined a small telecom start up, named NEXION Inc., built up the marketing department, developed a new brand for the company, created all outward bound marketing (including the creation of a website that won awards), chaired the company's cross-functional product development team and prepared the company for acquisition.

After acquisition by FUJITSU NETWORK COMMUNICATIONS, John led Fujitsu’s North American marketing and PR/AR/IR team, where he started up a new and powerful IR practice for the company as a key marketing activity. In the early days at Fujitsu, John was heavily involved in IPO plans for the North American telecom division. He was directly responsible for the successful launch of several new products and strategic initiatives.

In 1995, John joined PROGRESS SOFTWARE. John holds a BA (Honours) degree in business and marketing.
