Justin Levenson’s Summary

My carreer has always been very diverse and honestly, I can't imagine it being any other way. When I moved to Nashville I began teaching music at several area high schools and began working my way into the scene as a free-lance percussionist. I soon took a part-time job at SESAC working as a bill collector, a position which I kept for the next 6 years. All the while continuing to be more involved as an educator and especially as a percussionst, working in the studio, on tour and around town with numerous artists, bands, songwriters and producers.

During this time I became very involved with the Sociecty Of Leaders In Development (SOLID) and had the pleasure to serve for several years on the board including two terms as President. My tenure in SOLID contributed to my growth in so many ways. SOLID is a "Leadership Workshop" and gives young people the opportunity to practice their leadership skills all while having fun and serving others...check us out at www.solidnashville.com
In January 2007 I accepted a new position at SESAC, Manager of Consultant Support, Writer-Publisher Relations. In this role I was responsible for Managing the development and growth of SESAC's Affiliation Consultancy Program. My deep passion for songwriters combined with my passion for business made this job a logical next step in my carreer path.
In August of 2009 I was presented with another opportunity to grow with my promotion to Manager, Licensing Operations. This job took me back to my roots in the Licensing Department at SESAC working directly under my two mentors Bill Lee and Pat Collins. I affectionately call this role "Sr. Mangment 101"...that seems to be the easiest way to describe it...I love how much I am learning!
My goals for the future are large but broad enough as to not limit my growth in any one area. No matter what my future holds I will approach everything with the same drive, hard work, intellect and passion that I do with everything in my life.
