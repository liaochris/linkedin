Alison Binney’s Experience

  • Founder and Director

    New Science Journalism Project

    (Online Media industry)

    January 2009 — Present (1 year 11 months)

    The New Science Journalism (NSJ) Project is the first known attempt to unite students from all over the world to write for one single independent science news magazine. Using some of the latest internet technologies, the NSJ Project is a platform where students of media and science can network and collaborate on reporting health and environment news.

    The Project is designed to provide students a professional and collaborative space to 'cut their teeth' in the tumultuous world of science journalism.

    The Project was launched on 5th June 2009 to coincide with World Environment Day.

    Alison Binney, Project Director, says that http://www.newsciencejournalism.net/ has the potential to attract thousands of students of science, science journalism and science communication, new media design and more into a new worldwide network for online news about health and the environment.
