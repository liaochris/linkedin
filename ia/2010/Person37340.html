Cristobal Alvarez-Russell’s Summary

I am a Ph.D student in Computer science in the School of Interactive Computing, College of Computing, Georgia Institute of Technology. My research work is done within the context of the Augmented Environments Lab, which is part of the Graphics, Visualization and Usability Center. My research covers topics related to augmented reality (AR) and computer graphics (CG). I am particularly interested on how to make AR experiences seem more visually real and believable. For this purpose, I try to make use of the newest real-time CG techniques and hardware features whenever possible, especially shader programming. My advisors are Prof. Blair MacIntyre and Prof. Greg Turk.

Cristobal Alvarez-Russell’s Specialties:

8 yrs. of industrial, academic and research experience in C/C++; 5 yrs. of academic and research experience in 3D graphics programming; 3 yr. of industrial, academic and research experience in C# and the .NET platform; strong background in computer graphics and augmented/virtual reality, including latest research (SIGGRAPH, ISMAR, etc.); strong background in mathematics (linear algebra, trigonometry, numerical methods); R&D and research experience in user-centered design of CE devices.
