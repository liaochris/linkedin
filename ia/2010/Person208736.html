Benoît Sigoure’s Summary

Software engineer with a strong UNIX/Linux background. My domains of interest include (but are not limited to):
- compiler development
- kernel / operating system design, development and security
- image processing using stochastic processes
- design, build and run reliable, super-large-scale, distributed systems

I love to tackle challenging technical and theoretical issues (i.e, problems that are hard and interesting) and shave hairy yaks. I'm a passionate software engineer ever since I became addicted to the art of programming at the age of 13.

  • Ads Site Reliability Engineer

    Google

    (Public Company; GOOG; Internet industry)

    September 2008 — December 2009 (1 year 4 months)

    From the European HQ of Google (located in Dublin, Ireland) I ran Google's ads serving systems (AdWords, AdSense, ...). These services span on thousands of machines in many datacenters around the world, and have very aggressive availability SLAs as they're critical to Google's business (since they generate over 90% of the revenues of the company). I was part of the primary oncall rotation to make sure our services meet their SLAs (this involves things ranging from the daily care and feeding to troubleshooting large production issues on a very complex distributed infrastructure, to taking deep dives in the code of our servers to understand bugs or performance regressions, and so on and so forth). I also wrote a lot of code to help our teams run the said services and manage their ever increasing complexity.

    I helped the team accomplish significant resource savings by improving the utilization of our infrastructure, which resulted in millions of USD saved each quarter.
    I wrote many tools to help automate ourselves out of job.
    I became tech lead on a project with 4 engineers that involved developing an automation system (which I designed) to automate complex and tricky procedures.
    I gave many tech talks and training sessions to new hires in the SRE organization and received several bonuses from my peers for those.
    I wrote a complete, exhaustive "system map" for our serving systems, detailing almost every interaction between the many components that are in the serving path of a query. This document became a reference training material for new Ads SREs.
    I contributed hundreds of patches to Google's code base and shaved many hairy yaks. Some of the refactoring I've done touched dozens of files scattered across many projects and for those I got a blanket approval to let my changes affect any file in Google's code base.
    I have consistently been rated "exceeds expectations" (or better) during the performance reviews.

  • Google Site Reliability Engineering Intern

    Google

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; GOOG; Internet industry)

    February 2008 — August 2008 (7 months)

    During my internship with the Ads Site Reliability Engineering (SRE) team (in Mountain View, California) I worked mainly on two different project:
    - A tool to profile resource utilization (such as CPU, RAM and HDD space) in our different clusters. The tool provides different metrics and views at different aggregation levels (world-wide, per service, per cluster, ...).

    I also worked on various different things in the huge Google codebase (including in Gmail). I started to implement a library that wraps all the POSIX calls related to filesystem so that standard UNIX tools can use (via LD_PRELOAD) the Google-specific "filesystems" (GFS, BigTable...) without having to be recompiled.

  • Software engineer, Director of operations

    HLTV-Fr

    (Computer Games industry)

    HLTV-Fr was a non-profit association in France providing free services to Counter-Strike gamers. I wrote the entire website, designed and implemented a system that automatically managed up to 100 servers that were broadcasting and recording dozens of games per day.
