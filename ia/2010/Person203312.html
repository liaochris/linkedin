  • Executive Vice President

    TSI Inc.

    (Privately Held; Mechanical or Industrial Engineering industry)

    January 2001 — November 2006 (5 years 11 months)

    This was one of those events in ones career that I am sure everyone has. Meeting the TSI folks in mid 1995 and becoming a Division Manager seemed so natural and "meant to be"
    The ownership changed in 2000 and very soon after I was able to team up with Richard Remiarz to demonstrate a way forward to the new ownership that was devoid of stagnant thinking. I put my career on the line in that period of time, not knowing if I would have a job to go with the vision. But it all turned out for the good when I was tapped to move to Minnesota as corporate marketing VP. Pretty soon the team had taken the "nice" feel at TSI and added a much-needed business edge. We soared pretty much unchallenged to and through minor downturns and set the stage for TSI's future growth. How?
    My contribution was to completely re-develop the Marketing function into a high performing Product Management team. We created a robust product development system that achieved more than 12 major product launches a year yet reduced waste. We identified and developed talent, mostly but not exclusively from within our own ranks. We created a philosophy of growth in international markets by placing a TSI subsidiary in each of the major world economic engines. Simple stuff executed by outstanding people. My wish for TSI is to realize the benefits of this kind of leadership.
