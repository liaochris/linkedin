Brian Bernhardt’s Summary

I am a trial lawyer who represents creditors in all 100 counties in North Carolina in retail, consumer, and commercial collections litigation cases. My firm’s representative matters include auto deficiency files from major automobile companies and auto finance lenders, credit card accounts for major banks and credit card companies, accounts based on services rendered and merchandise sold, promissory notes from various credit unions and banks, insurance premium and former agent salary advance accounts for several insurance companies, and medical accounts, as well as other types of retail, consumer, and commercial debts.

I am also a former trial attorney in the IRS Office of Chief Counsel who has practiced extensively in the area of federal tax controversies and federal tax litigation. In doing so, I represented taxpayers, charitable organizations, private foundations, and other tax-exempt and nonprofit organizations before the IRS and Department of Justice in a variety of administrative matters, including audit examinations, administrative appeals, criminal investigations, and refund claims. I also appeared on their behalf in federal courts, including the U.S. Court of Federal Claims, the U.S. Tax Court, U.S. District Courts, and U.S. Courts of Appeals.

In addition, I have experience advising charities, private foundations, and other tax-exempt and nonprofit organizations on a variety of governance and tax matters. I have advised clients on issues such as entity formation, obtaining and maintaining tax exemptions, corporate governance, reclassifications, private inurement, intermediate sanctions, unrelated business income tax, tax reporting, tax disclosures, self-dealing, income distributions, investment restrictions, expenditure limitations, and related governance matters.

Brian Bernhardt’s Specialties:

North Carolina retail, consumer, and commerical collection litigation; federal tax controversies; federal tax litigation; and tax-exempt organizations.
