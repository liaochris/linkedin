Jeff Lee’s Summary

It took three weeks of talking to secondary school teachers and administrators and a few college professors; a day observing math and physics classes at Scituate HS; some research on the web. I've finally decided to become a math teacher in middle school or high school. I took the MTELs on May 9, the tests for Communications and Literacy and for Mathematics 09 (grades 5-12), and passed. I now have preliminary licenses to teach math in MA.

After visiting my old junior high school in Rego Park, Queens, chatting with the principal, Mr. Suraci, and some of his staff, I've decided to focus on becoming a *middle school* math teacher.

After getting recommendation upon recommendation from teachers regarding the quality and substance of Bridgewater State College's education program, after a lengthy email correspondence with Dr. Tom Brady, coordinator of BSC's Accelerated Post-Baccalaureate (APB) Teaching Certification program, and after chatting with Jason and others at BSC's graduate admissions office... I have decided to enroll in Bridgewater State's APB program this fall, to obtain an initial teaching license in Middle School Mathematics. It's night classes in the fall and then a full semester of student teaching, hopefully somewhere on the South Shore, in the spring. [Classes have started and boy is it a lot of work!]

I'm now applying for substitute teacher positions (PreK-12) and requesting to observe middle school math classes, as part of the 40 hours required before my student teaching (practicum). I'm also volunteering to help out in the library of my younger kids' elementary school and to help out in my kindergartener's classroom. Meanwhile I'm applying for substitute teacher positions, PreK-12.

For some more insight as to why I've chosen this new career path, take a peek at my blog, http://jeffleefromboston.blogspot.com/2009_05_01_archive.html
