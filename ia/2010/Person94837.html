Jennifer Davis’s Summary

I'm an award winning freelance television reporter in Washington, D.C. During my six years here, I have worked for several national affiliate services and now do so at Fox News Edge. I also freelance report at WTTG-TV, the local Fox station.

I have traveled extensively to cover presidential elections, hurricanes, tornadoes, wildfires and all sorts of other breaking news events. I've also done affiliate shots covering more light hearted stories - like the ball drop in Times Square at New Year's Eve and the American Idol finales in Los Angeles. When it comes to local news, I cover everything - crime, weather, politics, consumer and features.

I have been a television reporter for 12 years. Prior to working in Washington, I was a full time general assignment reporter at KTVI-TV, the Fox station in St. Louis, KOB-TV, the NBC station in Albuquerque and KAMR-TV, the NBC station in Amarillo, Texas. While in Albuquerque, I covered the Los Alamos wildfires and while in Texas, I covered the trial between Oprah Winfrey and the Texas Cattlemen. At the end of that trial, I was one of three reporters chosen to be a guest on the Oprah Winfrey Show to talk about the verdict.

I have been nominated for an Emmy Award several times. I have won once - for Live Breaking News for my coverage of a fire in St. Louis. I have my Masters in Broadcast Journalism from Northwestern University's Medill School of Journalism and my B.A. in Political Science and English from the University of Michigan.

As a reporter, my strengths are my ability to do live shots - sometimes more than 60+ in a single shift. I love to ad-lib, I thrive on breaking news, am relentlessly curious and never miss a deadline. I also pride myself on interesting writing and creative approaches to stories. But first and foremost, I am a story teller. I love talking to people, finding out their stories and sharing them with a larger audience.
