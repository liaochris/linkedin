Omar Al-Madhi’s Summary

Omar Al-Madhi hails from Saudi Arabia and is Vice President, Energy & Economic Cities Development at the Saudi Arabian General Investment Authority (SAGIA). His responsibilities include developing the Kingdom’s strategy for inward investments in the energy sector. He also oversees the development of two Economic Cities.

Prior to SAGIA, Omar worked in a multitude of positions within Saudi Arabia’s national oil company, Saudi Aramco. Omar’s tasks included participating in developing the company’s long-term strategy in the technology area as well as various other technical positions.

Omar was also an associate at McKinsey & Company's Middle East office in Dubai, UAE, where he served the lead developer of an economic city in the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia.

Omar obtained his Bachelors of Science degree in chemical engineering from the University of Pennsylvania in 2002, and is the founder and president of the Penn Middle East and North Africa Alumni Association. He also received an MBA degree from the MIT Sloan School of Management. While at Sloan, Omar concentrated on strategy and entrepreneurship. He was the lead organizer of two treks to the Middle East (one to Dubai & Jordan and one to Saudi Arabia). He was elected president of the MIT Arab Students’ Organization, was selected as a Captain of the Sloan Ambassadors Program and was involved in two non-profit mentorship programs for undergraduates. He is a co-author of "The One Thing" business case study.

Omar is an Eisenhower Fellow having been selected as the Fellow from Saudi Arabia for Eisenhower Fellowships’ 2010 Multi Nation Program. He is also the recipient of the 2007 MIT Sloan Martin Trust Fellowship & Achievement Award for Leadership and outstanding potential.

Omar is married with one child, and is fluent in English, Arabic and Greek.

  • Fellow

    Eisenhower Fellowships

    (Non-Profit; International Affairs industry)

    September 2009 — Present (5 months)

    Eisenhower engages emerging leaders from the world to enhance their professional capabilities broaden their contacts deepen their perspectives and unite them in a diverse global community a network where dialogue understanding and collaboration lead to a more prosperous just and peaceful world.

    Multi Nation, Single Region, and Common Interest programs offer a unique opportunity for emerging leaders aged 32-45 with a record of achievement and high potential for further advancements to broaden their horizons through the exchange of perspectives with U.S. leaders.

    Eisenhower Fellowships are awarded for a specific eight-week period and cannot be postponed. The programs typically involve visits to 8-10 cities and 50-70 individual appointments. Fellows from various countries join together for seminars at the beginning and end of each program, laying the foundation for life-long international friendships.

    Candidates are identified by in-country Nominating Committees composed of Alumni Eisenhower Fellows and other eminent citizens. A Philadelphia-based committee makes final selections.
