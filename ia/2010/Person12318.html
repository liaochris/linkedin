Andrew Weiss’s Summary

Andrew Weiss is a skilled educator, writer and lawyer. His emphasis is on encouraging mindfulness in all aspects of daily life, including the work environment. Through mindfulness, his clients learn conflict management and resolution, conscious communication, stress management and creative productivity. He is the founder of Justice Spirit(c), a service offering spiritual and emotional counseling to law clients and their attorneys, and of Living Awareness, a school of spiritual development.

Andrew's book "Beginning Mindfulness," published by New World Library in 2004, was hailed by Publishers' Weekly as "an excellent 10-week course on meditation" which emphasizes "the need to take mindful awareness into everyday life." The review praised the book for its "clarity, kindness and care" and for being "gloriously free of the jargon that can alienate the average person." "Beginning Mindfulness" was a OneSpirit Book Club selection and went on to sell over 20,000 copies. Andrew continues to teach both beginners' and advanced meditation classes and to write articles on practical applications of mindfulness.

For over 20 years, Andrew operated Resolution Law Office, a solo practice in the greater Boston, Massachusetts area emphasizing alternative dispute resolution, family law, and education law. He received mediation training from CDR Associates, Boulder, Colorado. Andrew was a faculty member of the National Institute of Whole Health from 1995 to 2006, where he taught business law and ethics, communication and conflict and client management.

Andrew relocated to the Triangle area of North Carolina in 2006, where he teaches mindfulness practice and provides law practice support, conflict management and consultation services to attorneys and their clients.
