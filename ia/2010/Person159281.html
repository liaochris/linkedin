Parker Thomas’s Summary

I am an entrepreneurial product manager and business strategist with more than 15 years of multi-industry experience. I am currently looking for short(er) term consulting work as well as seeking a position with an early to middle stage start-up interested in building its product management/launch team (e.g., VP/Director of Product Management).

My background spans business launch, day-to-day operations, strategic planning, fundraising and cross-functional management in consumer internet, financial services, business services and cleantech/greentech industries. I excel at identifying unmet customer needs and developing products that fill the gap. I have always been a highly adaptable strategic problem-solver. This has enabled me to cultivate a broad knowledge base across multiple industries. My greatest strength is the ability to bring these skills together to create and bring to market successful, profitable products.

Sampling of professional accomplishments:
• On my watch as VP of Product Management, Plastic Jungle drove a 600% increase in monthly revenue and a 500% increase in Web traffic in 14 months, including launching an online affiliate program with 2,400 affiliates generating 47% of company revenue
• Founded my own business services company with no outside funding, maintaining a monthly revenue growth rate of 21% throughout its entire lifespan, and selling it for 3 times its revenue after 3 years
• Created a software solution for the mutual fund industry that duplicated every single investment possible in a mutual fund and securing funding from the second largest mutual fund company in the country to create an enterprise-level product.

Parker Thomas’s Experience

  • VP, Product Management

    PlasticJungle

    (Privately Held; Internet industry)

    October 2008 — January 2010 (1 year 4 months)

    Plastic Jungle is the leading destination for buying, selling and donating gift cards. This enables consumers to unlock some of the $40 billion in cash currently stored in unused gift cards.

    I was the first product person and the third non-operations employee at PJ. My responsibilities boiled down to helping to define the business objectives and then creating products to fulfill the objectives. As in any startup, the job required dozens of hats and relentless prioritization.

    On my watch here, Plastic Jungle:
    • Drove a 600% increase in monthly revenue and a 500% increase in Web traffic in 14 months, including launching an online affiliate program with 2,400 affiliates generating 47% of company revenue
    • Designed and built a front and back office system using NetSuite and open source software that will support the company's growth to millions of users
    • Conceived of, negotiated, and executed a private-label mini-site for New York–based charity Donors Choose, taking it from concept to delivery in 4.5 months and making them the first nonprofit to accept gift cards as a form of donation
    • Executed a private-label mini-site with Amazon.com as part of an effort to make Amazon gift cards a new currency, guiding the process from concept to delivery in 5 months.
    • Raised $6M in VC funding as part of a 3 member leadership team.
    • Implemented an Agile software development process and changed the culture across the organization to support it.
    • Moved hosting to the Amazon cloud for an 80% reduction in hosting costs and substantial increased website capacity.
    • Created an entire suite of analytics and consumer metrics including lifetime customer value, traffic source conversion, revenue by source and others that enabled us to make business and marketing decisions based on real data.

  • Owner/President

    ShredFirst

    (Facilities Services industry)

    January 2003 — March 2006 (3 years 3 months)

    After tiring of consulting and high-tech, I decided I wanted to start my own business. I made a list of 12 criteria I wanted and the first opportunity to meet all of them was paper shredding. So I founded Shred First as a “green” document and product destruction company. I organized all the marketing and business development activities, maintained client relationships, built and managed a warehouse destruction system handling 100 tons of paper each month, maintained a fleet of 6 trucks, and managed 8 full-time employees.
    • Generated 21% monthly revenue growth from Q1 2004 through Q2 2006, with sales projected at $1.1M for 2006 prior to company sale.
    • Leveraged technology as a differentiator to create Shred First as a product for sale and sold within 3 years from initial concept for 3 times its annual revenue, closing the deal in only 60 days.
    • Became the first Green Certified shredding company in the US as a pioneer in using biodiesel to fuel the fleet of shredding trucks.

  • Owner and Builder

    (Think Tanks industry)

    March 2001 — August 2002 (1 year 6 months)

    I took my first real sabbatical to realize a lifelong goal of building an airplane. With zero experience, I learned how to build with sheet aluminum, rebuild Lycoming engines, design and wire aircraft electrical systems, work with fiberglass and epoxy resin, layout and build instrument panels and repair every part of a small piston engine aircraft. The result was a 2 seat, 200 horsepower, 220 mph, fully aerobatic airplane from a kit. It took over 3,200 hours in 1.5 years to finish.
    - Obtained FAA Repairman’s Certificate
    - Airplane passed FAA inspection November 13th, 2002 and flew for the first time Thanksgiving Day, 2002
    - Flew the airplane from Maine to Florida, all through the Bahamas, from FL to San Francisco in 2 days, all around the SE US, the Sierras, Death Valley and everywhere in between. It was seriously fun.
