JJ Stankevitz’s Summary

I'm an aspiring sports reporter with a specialty in multimedia coverage of college athletics. I've worked for various news outlets in Missouri covering University of Missouri athletics, including KMOV.com and The Maneater. My goal is to cover college sports or baseball using my writing and multimedia skills after graduating from MU in May of 2011.

JJ Stankevitz’s Specialties:

Writing game/feature stories, shooting video, shooting photographs, editing, social media use.

  • Chicago White Sox Examiner

    Examiner.com

    (Privately Held; Newspapers industry)

    June 2008 — Present (2 years 7 months)

    I blog about the White Sox, covering all aspects of the team from the MLB club to its minor league affiliates. My analysis is heavily stats-based using sabermetric/advanced stats, but my goal is to use those stats while still offering accessible content about the White Sox to even the casual fan.

    I was selected by TwinsCentric to write the White Sox 2010 Trade Deadline Primer, which was a 170-plus page e-book that went on sale July 11, 2010. I wrote roughly 15 pages of content for the White Sox-specific version of the book, which included:
    -Mid-season player reviews
    -Analysis of tradeable players and potential trade targets
    -Prospect analysis
    -A ~600-word essay on the Sox leading up to the deadline
    -A ~200-word fantasy focus sidebar

    My work at Examiner also allowed me the opportunity to write content for the Minnesota Twins' official scorecard for series against the White Sox at Target Field. In writing content, I preview the upcoming series with "what to look for" and "stats attack" sections.

    I've also conducted interviews with White Sox minor leaguers such as Aaron Poreda (now with the Padres), Tyler Flowers, and Gordon Beckham for Q&A posts.

    Traffic to my site has grown in each year I've run the site, reaching the 100,000 page view plateau in 2010.
