Daniel Hulme’s Summary

Daniel co-founded Satalia in 2008 and has played a key managerial role in its transition from start-up to a global organisation. Prior to founding Satalia, Daniel was one of the principle architects in designing and implementing the groundbreaking technology at the heart of Satalia’s new business model.

Daniel has been awarded an MSci in Computer Science w/ Cognitive Science and Doctorate in Computational Complexity from UCL. Daniel won an international Kauffman Global Entrepreneur Scholarship and actively promotes entrepreneurship and technology innovation in the UK and US.

Daniel Hulme’s Specialties:

Emerging Technologies, Optimization Methodologies & Machine Learning, Open-innovation and Entrepreneurship.

    The EngD VEIV is a four-year postgraduate award intended for the UK's leading research engineers with the aim to develop inter-disciplinary and translational research. UCLs EngD VEIV programme is a radical alternative to the traditional PhD, aimed at the development of future industrial and intellectual leaders, and providing greater emphasis on the successful engineering of research with a more professionally oriented approach than a conventional PhD.

    The aim of my research is to understand what makes some problems tractable while others intractable, to cross-fertilize old and new ideas in computational complexity and graph theory, spawning interesting and novel methods that extend the principles used to solve restricted NP problems, and to develop and implement new techniques that can be applied to many of the available scientific and industrial problems.
