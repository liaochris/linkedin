Elizabeth Resnick’s Summary

Joined the faculty at Massachusetts College of Art and Design in fall 1977 as a part time instructor in graphic design. At the time I was running my independent design firm, Elizabeth Resnick Design, serving corporate and non-profit clients in the Greater Boston area.

In the fall of 1999 I joined the graphic design faculty as a full-time assistant professor. I received tenure and a promotion to associate professor in 2004.

In 2001, I was elected Chair of the Communication Design Department (Graphic Design, Illustration, Animation), and served two consecutive 3-year terms, ending in July 2007.

In 2009, I was promoted to full professor.

My passion in organizing and curating design exhibitions has manifested in 4 original, large exhibitions:

"Russell Mills: Within/Without" 1991 (with Teresa Flavin)

"Dutch Graphic Design: 1918-1945" 1994 (with Alston Purvis)

"Makoto Saito: The Art of the Poster" 1999 (with Jan Kubasiewicz)

"The Graphic Imperative: International Posters of Peace, Social Justice and The Environment: 1965-2005" 2005 (with Chaz Maviyane-Davies and Frank Baseman)

I am currently working on a new exhibit: a 30 year survey of international AIDS posters with Javier Cortez.

Elizabeth Resnick’s Specialties:

I am one of a small group of MassArt faculty members who offer travel courses. Each of these 3-credit courses is considered a studio elective. A trip is taken during the course time (usually 14-16 days spanning spring break+) to the country we are studying. In Spring 2009, my colleague Robert Linsky and I will be leading: West Meets East: Exploring Communication Design in China. In spring 2010, Leland Burke and I will be offering "Crossing the Pond: Exploring Communication Design in the UK.
