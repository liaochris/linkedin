Nora Nelson’s Summary

My professional experience has emphasized my strong writing and research skills, and I have authored two major theses based off of my own unique, independent research. The first, titled "The Sao Francisco River Basin: Process, Policy, and Problems in Brazil’s Arid Northeast," assesses the ecological, socio-economic, and political factors influencing environmental policy and resource management and the extent to which the policy and management processes have been successful in the basin of Brazil’s longest river, from both a policy analysis and grassroots perspective. The second, titled "Acanthaster planci population dynamics on Nyange Reef, Zanzibar, Tanzania, and its implications for sustainable use and resource management," explores the population dynamics and preferences of the corallivore A. planci, and its implications for management and sustainable use of marine resources as well as economic development in Tanzania’s Zanzibar archipelago. This project involved significant quantitative data collection and analysis in the field. I have had additional field experience at home and abroad.

I am currently participating in a dual degree Masters program in natural resources and sustainable development through American University in Washington, D.C. and the United Nations' sanctioned Universidad para la Paz in Colon, Costa Rica. The program confers Masters degrees in both International Affairs and Natural Resources & Sustainable Development.

I am always open to meeting people and learning about new opportunities, and am especially interested in international opportunities related to the specialties listed below. I am flexible and would be happy working in and with government agencies, NGOs, non-profits, & other areas in the public and private sectors, either individually or in a team setting.
