John South’s Summary

I believe in the power of Internet to help clients drive incremental revenue and lower their cost of operations and customer support. My fascination with the Internet began in the late 1980s, prior to the Web. In 1995, I formed FS3-Interactive to pursue Internet solutions as a fulltime business.

With FS3, we pioneered the use of a blueprinting technique to communicate everything from digital strategy and information architecture to competitive research and solutions design. We were also one of the first companies to adopt formal usability testing and user-centered design principles to make Web sites and Internet applications easier to use.

Through FS3-Interactive, Rare Medium, Andara Digital, and now RAZOR, I've worked with large brands on a wide range of initiatives. Some of the companies that have hired us to help them with their online initiatives include: JCPenney, Pizza Hut, CenturyTel, FedEx Kinko’s, CCIM, CompUSA, Domino's Pizza, Brinker International, Applebee's, The Federal Reserve Bank, Rent-A-Center, AMC, CompuCom, AT&T, Nationwide Insurance, Blockbuster, Citadel Broadcasting, Horizon Digital Finance, and National Semiconductor.

John South’s Specialties:

Business building, Internet strategy, user-centered Web development, usability engineering, search marketing, E-Mail marketing, multi-channel marketing integration, client services, venture incubation, and mergers & acquisitions
