Wellington Kwok, Ph.D., P.Eng.’s Summary

Caterpillar has identified hybrid applications as a critical growth area and a core part of the corporate sustainability efforts. The rapidly emerging product line provides unique challenges in the component areas. One such area with significant need for greater technical leadership is energy storage. As part of the technical career path and key technology governance for 2010, Caterpillar has established a technical expert position for Electrical Hybrid Energy Storage. We are pleased to announce that Wellington Kwok will fulfill this role and will work closely with PD COE, TBU, and EMSD to coordinate our efforts in the energy storage space.

Wellington brings with him a wealth of experience in the field of energy storage. Wellington received his PhD in Chemical Engineering in 1994 and worked at Delphi Corporation for over 10 years in the advanced battery and hybrid electric vehicle areas where he was a named inventor on eight battery and charging patents. Since joining Caterpillar in 2008, Wellington has been instrumental in the development of energy storage concepts for the Hybrid Program. He has also led the investigation of several potential suppliers of energy storage components and component testing services. Wellington is currently working to identify a long-range energy storage strategy to meet the corporation's energy storage needs through the year 2020.
