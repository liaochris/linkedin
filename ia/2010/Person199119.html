Nabil Takla’s Experience

  • CEO/Founder

    Innovative Semiconductors, Inc.

    (Semiconductors industry)

    January 1997 — Present (13 years 9 months)

    Nabil is the Founder and CEO of Innovative Semiconductors, Inc., a self funded IP Company. He was one of the early pioneers of the Silicon IP industry when it started to gain traction in the mid nineties. Innovative Semiconductors was the first company to offer a mixed-signal IP as a product. Today, the mixed-signal IP is the largest growing segment of the Silicon IP market. As Innovative's CEO, Nabil has successfully worked with many leading semiconductor manufacturers worldwide to build tens of millions of chips used in high speed serial interface applications, particularly in the areas of USB, FireWire and digital video Codecs. Licensee of Innovative's technology include Conexant, Creative Technology, Freescale, IBM, Micron Technology, National Semiconductor, Nvdia, PortalPlayer and others. He has the technology vision and insight as well as the business and marketing skills essential for startups success. He was able to compete with companies that have ten times the engineering and marketing staff to win business from world class customers in high volume consumer applications

  • Chairman of the Board

    Phylinks

    (Semiconductors industry)

    January 2008 — September 2009 (1 year 9 months)

    Phylinks is a UK company that was founded to develop mixed-signal PHYs for high speed applications. Products included PCIe, SATA, Firewire, Display port, USB 2.0 and USB 3.0.

  • CEO and Chairman of the Board

    Phylinks

    (Semiconductors industry)

    September 2006 — January 2008 (1 year 5 months)

    Phylinks was founded in 2006 to address the strong demand for integrating analog and mixed-signal functions in SOC designs. In August of 2006, Nabil invested in Phylinks and joined the management team as CEO and Chairman of the Board. Phylinks has offices in Manchester UK, and Silicon Valley, California. Phylinks develped PCIe and SATA unique PHY technology and is emerging as a leader in that space.

  • Vice President, VLSI Products

    Matra Design Semiconductors

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Semiconductors industry)

    1988 — 1992 (4 years )

    Nabil was Vice President of VLSI products at MATRA Design Semiconductor, a US subsidiary of MATRA, the French conglomerate. He was responsible for product development and marketing strategy for Processor, Cache controller chips and software. He worked with other colleagues in the executive team to bring the company's revenue to $10M in 3 years. He was responsible for marketing and product strategy as well the engineering execution of the business plan. The company also pioneered the FPGA to ASIC conversion that was an emerging market in the early nineties. The Company was acquired by Matra the French conglomerate.
