Piotr Drukier’s Summary

The first practicing Polish forensic anthropologist [BSc (1998), and MSc (2000) from Uniwersytet Wroclawski, Poland]

Associate and International Incident Response Team Member of BLAKE Emergency Services consulting on various aspects of incident and emergency response to catastrophic events.

Lectured at Bournemouth University, UK, on undergraduate and postgraduate programmes in the Forensic and Biological Sciences area designing, developing, leading, and delivering course units in Biological Anthropology, Forensic Anthropology, Human Paleopathology, Pathology in the Forensic and Biological Context, and Temporary Mortuary Forensic Simulation.

Worked for ICMP (International Commission on Missing Persons) in Bosnia and Herzegovina on exhumations, examination, and identification of victims of war.

Established and implemented the procedures of anthropological review of remains as part of the identification process.
Developed a system of DNA samples information exchange in mass scale re-association and identification efforts.
Introduced the Vertebral Method for improving age-at-death estimates in the adult human skeletons.
