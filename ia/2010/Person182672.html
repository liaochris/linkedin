Sarah Kaye’s Summary

Originally I trained as a product designer where I made everything from tableware to toilets (the former of which my mum still uses proudly). I also spent a few years managing a nightclub in NYC (mum less proud) and then got into the advertising game.

I had the pure luck and sheer bloody mindedness to land my first job in advertising at Wieden + Kennedy (NYC); further career details about all that below. After earning my stripes in advertising (Print, TV, Outdoor, Online, Product Placement, NPD and Viral) it was time for a change when I fell into headhunting (sorry, “Talent Management”). Not usually a career choice one consciously makes, but I’d already been doing something similar for artists on a voluntary basis. When I was promised The Talent Business was about long term relationships, nurturing and building up candidates, and not hustle & bustle (i.e. I wasn’t going to be on commission) I thought why not give it a whirl.

Turns out I love it, and I feel very luck to be able to say that… I spend my days helping other people getting to a place where they can say that too. It is about the long term thing here, and I’m as likely to help someone find their next job as to help them negotiate their next pay rise or learn how to manage a difficult colleague in meetings. Other perks include good breakfasts, meeting smart people, helping people improve their CV’s, interview practice and exercising the nurturing gene.

Get in touch if you want to talk about you, I work with planners particularly ATL, 360 and digital… (and I’ll try not to talk too much about me.) I do give tough love where needed and expect you to help me help you – but try to make the whole process fun too!

Sarah Kaye’s Specialties:

All facets of communications: advertising, branded content, content leveraging, PR and marketing, emerging technologies. Also covering the strategic end of communication: strategic insight, concept marketing, development, research, and ideation.
