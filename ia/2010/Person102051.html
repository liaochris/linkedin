John Foreman’s Summary

Currently - I am excited to be at SoloPower, commercializing 2nd generation of BIPV Products, using their high efficiency & quality CIGS PV cells made via scalable electroplating processes on steel rolls. I have a strong interest in advancing the commercialization of leading PV technologies with disruptive cost potential and realizing effective integration and penetration into Residential, Commercial, and Utility Scale markets.

Previously, I was a PV Systems Engineer at DC Power Systems. I specialized in Solar PV & Solar Thermal Systems - with some Solar/Wind co-generation systems.

My goals are to utilize, a strong Materials Science & Engineering (EPM) background, extensive technical troubleshooting experience, clean room & laboratory experience, and good networking-communications skills. These skills combined with my Systems Integration expertise make for a unique combination.
--
For a brief period, I was a US Patent Examiner in the semiconductor and laser arts.

Before that, I was a Penn State Materials Scientist and Engineer - focused in electronic/photonic materials research. Some projects included: laser surface welding of extreme carbide ceramics for ballistics and dielectric studies, nonlinear quantum dot optics for targeting cancer cells, and thermal grain boundary segregation of TiO2 electroceramics.

Two things inspired inspired my collegiate transition from Electrical Engineering to Materials Science Engineering was the desire to know at the fundamental level of nature, what drove the circuitry and computers I fell in love with as a child... thanks to all the cool electronics my older brother showed me & a summer Internship at Penn State's NMT (Nanofabrication Manufacturing Technology) program. Forever grateful for opening my eyes to the quantum world of the small (http://www.cneu.psu.edu/ & http://www.mri.psu.edu/)

Going back - I was a Satellite and Microwave Communications Technician in the US Air Force for a few years.
