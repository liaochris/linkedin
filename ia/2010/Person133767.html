Mark Fones’s Summary

:: Award-winning communications professional with 13 years 'through the line' experience across B2C, B2B and Employer marketing sectors
:: Specialist in branding, integrated marketing communications and digital media 
:: Accomplished leader and commercial manager with a great track-record in inspiring teams, growing talent and driving profitability
:: Highly credible communicator with a proven reputation for building trusted client partnerships, developing accounts and winning new business

A first-class BA (Hons) graduate in design, Mark began his career with leading consumer advertising and B2B marketing agencies in both the Midlands and London, building a portfolio of experience embracing brand development, advertising, print, events, sales promotion, video and online for such clients as Apple, Cadbury, Miss Selfridge, EA Games, Lucas Arts, Nortel Networks and Dell. He joined TMP Worldwide in 2000, bringing a refreshing new focus to the agency, it's product and it's clients - both locally and nationally. While continuing to build his portfolio with award-winning projects and integrated marketing strategies for many leading clients – including E.ON, RWE, National Grid, LTSB, Barratt Developments, Vision Express and Jaguar Land Rover – he has become increasingly recognised as a digital specialist, and has been the driving force behind numerous award-winning projects in this arena. While high profile examples include pioneering work for Yell in SecondLife, and GCHQ in online (Xbox) gaming environments – both award-winners at the prestigious RAD and CIPD awards respectively - Mark has directed many innovative campaigns, websites and online projects that have helped secure 7 UK marketing firsts and over 60 industry awards - including wins at the Roses, Cream and Fresh Digital events. In April 2010, Mark joined Wyatt International.

Mark actively leads The Digital Creative Network - the online group for digital creative, marketing and media professionals.

Mark Fones’s Experience

  • Client Strategy and Creative Director

    Wyatt International

    (Marketing and Advertising industry)

    April 2010 — Present (2 months)

  • Executive Creative Director (Regions)

    TMP Worldwide UK

    (Privately Held; Marketing and Advertising industry)

    December 2004 — April 2010 (5 years 5 months)

    My role was focused on inspiring, shaping and leading TMP's creative offering within the UK with real emphasis being on key client service and strategy across the regional business. In early 2005, I restructured the national creative team (+45 staff) in order to deliver quality and efficiency gains for both our business and clients alike. Post MBO in May 2006, I led TMP’s re-brand. Working closely with a large multi-site project team, I successfully created and deployed the 'We're all yours' platform that enabled TMP to share renewed cultural values, deliver enhanced customer experiences and grow a stronger reputation from the inside out. In February 2009, I originated, developed and launched a unique brand based business offering that has been available and successfully promoted to clients/prospects throughout the economic downturn. 'Employer Bonding' takes the principles of research-led branding and Web 2.0 technologies that drive social networking and refocuses them on employee engagement - widely recognised as a major factor in helping the best companies retain key talent, increase productivity, recover performance and increase brand value. Partnered by Google in July 2009, I presented this new product to a selection of Marketing, Comms and HR leaders at an online branding event for the Midlands region. Over the last 4-5 years, the overarching theme of my work was to migrate creative services, agency thinking and key account activity towards the use of integrated solutions, emerging technologies and new channels to market. This led to the delivery of many UK marketing firsts, over 60 industry awards and demonstrable results for my clients. During the same period, I also ensured a pitch win ratio of 2:3 and the addition of significant levels of new business billings to the UK operation.
