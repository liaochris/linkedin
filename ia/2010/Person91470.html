Jay Gilchrist’s Summary

GOALS

To facilitate care for the earth through sustainable, renewable use of its resources. To foster awareness of the beauty of the natural world and the need to live gently and responsibly on our planet. .

To cultivate care for the human community through the pursuit of social, racial, economic and environmental justice and organizing for systemic change by connecting care for the environment with the work of creating justice and peace in the community.

To foster quality care for people who are suffering and for community systems. To engage in theological reflection and spiritual friendship by sharing the journey of life and faith with people of all backgrounds.

EXPERIENCE

Supervise and mentor professional pastoral ministers in rural communities. Utilize a “hands-off” approach allowing for freedom and creative care for people and community systems.

Support collaborative work for racial and economic justice, religious unity, cooperative social and ecumenical ministries and care for the environment in rural counties across the South.

Oversee the design of a passive solar, rural neighborhood-friendly worship and fellowship space and a sustainable land use plan for a church in East Tennessee. Promote earth-friendly practices with coworkers engaged in building and remodeling. Facilitate care for creation as part of various eco-ministry teams. Two-time winner of St. Francis Eco Church award from the Diocese of Knoxville.

Work as a team player to help assess development of community ministries, to creatively plan for effective outreach. Help to implement budgets, projects and innovative strategies that build bridges of compassion and understanding.

Participate in grassroots efforts to end racism, bring justice to workers and immigrants, and stand against the devastation of forests, mountains and waters. Practice a ministry that focuses on the inter-relatedness of these efforts in the web of life.
