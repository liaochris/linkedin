Yann Dietrich’s Summary

Over the last 12 years, I contributed to the definition and the implementation of the Intellectual property strategy of various multinational companies (e.g. France Telecom/Orange, Intel) and organizations (e.g. LiMo Foundation), including leading and managing international teams and projects. Through this work, I developed a strong experience on strategic and business IPR licensing, standardization, lobbying, open source, and within various sectors (e.g. pharma, telecommunications, semiconductors, electricity).

Some of my most notable achievements are:
- The creation of a multi-million dollar licensing program about the Turbo-Codes technology incl. the negotiation with large semi-conductors companies (e.g. Qualcomm, Broadcom …) and my participation to numerous other business (e.g. patent pools) or strategic patent deals or operations (e.g. cross-license, joint-ventures) for France Telecom or Intel.
- Being elected as chair of the EICTA IPR group (Now Digital Business Europe) and representing the ICT industry in Europe for high-level discussions with the EU institutions especially about the European Patent litigation system, Interoperability, IPR and standards …
- Driving the adoption of open source policies for France Telecom/Orange and later for the LiMo Foundation
- Contribution to the rewamp of the IPR strategy of France Telecom as part of the management team of the IP division.
- Creation of several Industry Initiatives (Homegateway, LiPs Forum) and participation to the elaboration and/or revision of multiple standards organizations (ETSI, ITU-T, OMA …)
- Being one of the main architects of the adoption by ETSI of voluntary ex-ante disclosure of licensing terms
- Managing teams of experts and projects in multi-cultural and international environment

Additionally, I teach IP and licensing in two French universities, and speak in many conferences about IPR and related topics

You can also consult my blog: http://ydietrich.wordpress.com

Yann Dietrich’s Specialties:

Patent, technology licensing, R&D, Opensource, lobbying, standardization and more generally intellectual property, international and complex negotiations, management

  • General Counsel

    LiMo Foundation

    (Telecommunications industry)

    January 2008 — October 2009 (1 year 10 months)

    Based in UK, reporting to the Executive Director, I created the legal function of this industrial consortium funded by Motorola, NEC, NTT Docomo, Orange, Panasonic, Samsung and Vodafone and now with a membership of more than 50 companies unified to create the first truly open Linux-based operating system for Mobile devices.   • EMEA Chief IP Counsel (Sr IP attorney)

    Intel Corporation

    (Public Company; INTC; Semiconductors industry)

    October 2005 — January 2008 (2 years 4 months)

    Based in UK (Swindon) I created the IP function in EMEA, responsible for providing support on a wide range of intellectual property matters including patent and technology licensing, R&D agreements and technological partnerships.

    I managed also the representation of Intel in standards organizations such as ETSI or DVB in EMEA and I was a key contributor in the adoption by ETSI of voluntary ex-ante disclosure of licensing terms

    More broadly, I developed the IP policy function in EMEA, being elected as the chairman of the IPR group of the EU ICT Trade association and I represented this industry and Intel in various high-level EU discussions e.g. European patent litigation system in EU, IPR & standards/interoperability

    As part of the global team in charge of Wireless IP (e.g. WiMAX, LTE), I participated to several key initiatives as lead counsel for some of them (e.g. NGMN, negotiation of potential joint-ventures…)

    (Privately Held; Telecommunications industry)

    November 2000 — September 2005 (4 years 11 months)

    After two years as Licensing counsel, I was promoted as the managing counsel of the team of Licensing counsels (5), reporting myself to the Director of the IPR Division (François Jamet) and the Group IP Legal Director.

    In addition to the management of the legal support of all IPR activities of France Telecom Group (200,000 employees incl. 5000 researchers), my main achievements are:

    - The structuring of the licensing counsels team supporting all various IP activities within the licensing division (e.g. support of licensing activities incl. patent pools, lPR litigation as well as other business groups (e.g. Sourcing division, specific product initiative such as Livebox)
    - The representation of France Telecom in numerous Standardization activities and especially through the participation to the Board of OMA co-leading a lobbying campaign in liaison with other operators to defend their specific interests and the evolution of IPR rules accordingly
    - The definition of a comprehensive IPR policy for various type of R&D partnerships incl. the re-negotiation of all agreements with major universities in France and abroad, support for all labs in Japan, China, US and Poland, Joint-venture in China
    - With the strategic partnerships division, the definition of a new way of collaborating with our major partners and negotiation of partnerships agreements with major companies such as Alcatel-Lucent, Nokia, Intel, Motorola …
    - Handling with my team of numerous pre-litigation and litigation both offensive, and defensive, in France, Gerrmany, United States …
