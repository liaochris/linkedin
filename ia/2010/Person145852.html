Michael Perl’s Summary

In February, 2010, I am going to take the California Bar Exam. I have little doubt of my success on the exam, and I expect to be admitted to practice in May, 2010.

I graduated from law school with honors, then moved to Illinois and passed the bar. Unable to secure a position with a law firm, I put out my shingle and did basic preventative legal services.

After moving to California in 2004, I opted against getting my license, hoping to secure a position outside the legal profession, but ended up doing clerical and administrative support. After a brief return to Chicago for most of 2008, I returned to Los Angeles where I have been struggling through the economy to get a position that will assist me in passing the bar or getting a position once I do.

I thrive in situations where no rules seem to be appropriate. It is part of being a lawyer, so I don't think I am anything special, but even as a lawyer I stood out for this talent.

Michael Perl’s Specialties:

My greatest strength is my ability to think. By that I mean I have a superior ability to observe a situation and understand it on many levels, why it is how it is, how it works and how it can be improved. I am able to synthesis seemingly contradictory scenarios and explain how they work on the same principles. This is an essential skill as a lawyer: utilizing many cases to support a legal argument.

  • Novelist

    Michael Perl Writing Venture

    (Entertainment industry)

    January 1999 — September 2004 (5 years 9 months)

    I had been writing stories and snippets for years, and I strung some scenes together, thought of a story to connect them, and wrote my first novel. "The Last Straw" was copyrighted in 1999.
    I turned my creative energies toward science fiction and planned a trilogy. The first was to be from the protagonist's perspective. I wrote the next book as a omniscient narrator. The third book was not written, but was going to be from the perspective of a minor character or characters. Unfortunately, the events of September 11, 2001, took a big toll on me. For one thing, a part of the story involved the invasion of the Earth, and I exaggerated the media coverage. The media's reaction to the terrorist attacks was eerily similar, but even more spectacular.
    After that, I wrote one novel. I had been trying to write it for some time, but I could not find an appropriate setting. The attacks and the immediate aftermath finally provided the venue. "Infamy and the Woman" got its copyright in 2002.
