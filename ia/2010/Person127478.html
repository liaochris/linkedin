Loren Burnett’s Summary

Loren Burnett, Chief Executive Officer and Entrepreneur

In July 2009, Loren founded RLB Technologies LLC. RLB owns and operates companies that license technology from universities and research institutions. On February 4, 2010, RLB launched Madison Assessment LLC. Madison Assessment exclusively licenses computer-based testing instruments created by the Center for Assessment and Research Studies (CARS) at James Madison University. Tests are administered to students at various points during their undergraduate studies for the purpose of assessing the overall performance and quality of the institution's curriculum. RLB is in the process of completing additional licenses of technology from other institutions that will become the foundation for new software products.

Loren founded StackSafe in 2005. Under his leadership as president and CEO, StackSafe received the 2008 ITIL Pink Elephant Conference Innovation of the Year Award; was ranked by Forrester in their top (“On fire!”) category; and was named by Gartner as one of six “Cool Vendors in IT Operations, 2008”.

Loren was the COO of Astral Brands where he oversaw the growth of Astral’s revenue base by 100% over 15 months. Prior to Astral, Loren served as CEO, of Celcorp, a provider of enterprise integration software and services.

Loren served as CFO of Riptech, Inc. where he was instrumental in the sale of the firm to Symantec, Inc. for $145 million cash. Loren was a founder and COO of FutureNext, which grew to $72 million in revenue. He also served as CFO at The Hunter Group, where he led the IPO initiative and sale to Renaissance Worldwide Inc.

Mr. Burnett held management positions with NTS, MAXM, MCI, and Coopers & Lybrand. He serves on the Board of the Northern Va. Technology Council. He also served on the Board of Make-A-Wish of the Mid-Atlantic. He is an instrument rated airplane pilot, scuba diver and is a finisher in the 2002 NY City Marathon and the 2007 Stockholm Marathon.
