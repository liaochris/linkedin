Ted Treanor’s Summary

Consistently performed as a rain maker, and making a difference in my company and in the industry. He is innovative, creative, and visionary, with a good sense of humor. Ted’s collaborative nature works well in forming win-win scenarios. Ted encourages and strengthens others around him, without seeking attention for himself. His personal character applies to corporate values that include; integrity, respect for the individual, stewardship, and excellence.

Passions are; people, leadership, vision, growing companies, creating jobs, community development, clean industry, clean energy, earth stewardship, green, useful technology, real estate, books, reading, ethics, spiritual, family, quality of life.

Some highlights:
• Envisioned the impact of the Web in '93, acquired $5 million initial funding ($14 million total) from the U.S. government for the Electronic Commerce Resource Center.
• Created the vision (with Chris Kahn) and developed NetGalley.com, software as a service, online industry platform, to revolutionize book promotions in the publishing industry.
• Brand name clients (partial list); Microsoft, IBM, Intel, Xerox, AT&T, Sequoia Capital, Boeing, Bank of America, Sony, HarperCollins, Time Warner Books, Random House, Real Networks, Getty Images, American Homes, Hewlett Packard.
• Developed a 1,000 acre high-tech business park located on the freeway along the San Francisco Bay in Fremont, California.
• Contributed to real estate and technology businesses growth that resulted in increased valuation of more than $3 billion.
• Raised more than $100 million in equity and joint venture funding from private and institutional investors.
• Completed the Kitsap County Comprehensive Plan land use in '93 for industrial-commercial property.
• Fortune 100 client- developed a new, highly-profitable business division as a result of a business opportunity consulting project.
• Formed world-class strategic alliances, Board of Directors and Advisory Boards.
