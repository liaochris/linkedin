Jane Rubinsky’s Summary

My specialty is getting at the heart of a place and connecting with everyone there—from the president to the interns—in order to capture the vitality of an institution's community and its mission in a newspaper or magazine that people can't wait to read when it arrives.

As senior editor of The Juilliard Journal, I developed a small in-house newspaper into an award-winning, 28-page monthly publication with a print circulation of 18,000 (and many more readers online). I planned, assigned, and wrote articles that inspired alumni to reconnect with the school, brought wider exposure for faculty projects, got students and alumni excited about each other, and moved donors to contribute to scholarships.

● I will get people enthused about what your organization or company does and make them want to be a part of it.

● I will help those who work there feel more interconnected and appreciate what other departments accomplish.

● I can use Twitter, blogging, and e-newsletters to increase your organization's visibility and extend its community.

● I can guide writers (including non-professionals and non-native speakers of English) through the process of producing lively and cogent articles.

● I reshape tangled writing into a solid piece of work.

● I write in a lively and engaging style, whether in a personal profile or a feature presenting fairly technical material for a general audience.

Working nine years as a copywriter for clients in business, health, publishing, and the arts gave me the ability to walk into any environment or culture and "get" what the client is about.

Contact me to discuss my joining your staff to build upon or create a publication and get your work known by a wider audience.

Jane Rubinsky’s Experience

  • Senior Editor

    The Juilliard School

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    1994 — 2009 (15 years )

    Responsible for all editorial functions at The Juilliard Journal, the Juilliard School's monthly newspaper (24-32 pages; circulation 18,500; also online at www.Juilliard.edu/journal), which was awarded First Place with Special Merit by the American Scholastic Press Association in 2003, 2005, 2006, and 2008, and voted Most Outstanding University Newspaper for 2007-08.

    • Planned content; developed and assigned stories (working with department heads as well as faculty, staff, students, and alumni); edited and rewrote as necessary. Obtained photos and art work; some photography. Also wrote features and profiles; proofread paper and oversaw corrections at printer. Some work on viewbook and catalogs.

    • Expanded paper from 12 to 24 pages over six years (with circulation increase from 14,000 to 18,000); upgraded production and printing process; managed circulation and budget.
