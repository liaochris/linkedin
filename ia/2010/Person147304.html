Mike Jensen’s Summary

Born and raised in Seattle, WA I grew up in West Seattle where I attended Hope Lutheran and Seattle Lutheran High School. These two schools provided me a unique opportunity to be involved in many different activities that gave me a diverse set of experiences that helped shape my future.

I took the values I learned there to my college years at Western Washington University. Initially thinking of pursuing an accounting degree my attention shifted to Management Information Systems. My senior year I took my spring quarter off and accepted an internship with the Weyerhaeuser company doing PC, Network and Server support. After the 3 month internship I stayed on full time, and commuted from Seattle to Bellingham to finish my degree.

Weyerhaeuser offered me great opportunities to learn, grown and become more knowledgeable with systems and business processes. In my final year there I started to engage with the leadership team on a new collaboration tool called Lotus Notes and even did some small application development on that platform. That experience opened my eyes to new ways to collaborate and get work done.

Shortly after Y2K, I decided to take a new opportunity with a small, growing start up called eProject. I joined the team as the IT Manager and within the first 6 months took on management and direction of all the technical infrastructure both internal and in our production customer environment. Over the course of the next 9 years I wore a variety of different hats that resulted in the oversight of all the Technical and Support Operations as VP of Technical Services where I helped scale the delivery of our industry leading SaaS solution.

In my final year with Daptiv, I took on the role of VP of Community Development for Daptiv. In this role I was responsible for the creation of business process, infrastructure and other initiatives that together transformed Daptiv into an enabler of Community.
