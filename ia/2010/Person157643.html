Jonathan Villet’s Summary

I head a unique advertising agency in San Francisco called OneWorld Communications. Our business is connecting to people with the power and magic that causes them to think, imagine, and act. We use a two-way communications strategy that listens to the “voice of the customer”. Our strategic process aligns the offering of our clients -- whether an idea, product or service -- to the interest, imagination, and cultures of their customers. We create experiences that people care about, delight in, and find engaging. That’s our branding and brand messages. Our advertising campaigns are online, on TV, radio, and other media.

But what makes us tick? We’re energized by communication itself, from the power and magic of making that connection.

We are a Member of the American Association of Advertising Agencies (4As) with affiliated offices in 72 countries through ComVort.

  • Chief Technical Adviser (Communications Project Manager) in Southern Africa

    United Nations - Food and Agriculture Organization (FAO)

    (Marketing and Advertising industry)

    October 1994 — August 1998 (3 years 11 months)

    Designed and managed project “Communication for Development in Southern Africa” (US$5 million), seconded to Organization of African Unity (OAU); headed international and regional team to serve 10 countries with training in the use of communications to promote development programs among local populations, and to provide marketing and media consultancy. Established the SADC (Southern African Development Community) Centre of Communication for Development in Harare. Project was financed by the Italian Cooperation for Development through FAO.   • Communications Project Manager and Producer in Italy / Africa / elsewhere

    United Nations - Food and Agriculture Organization (FAO)

    (Marketing and Advertising industry)

    1983 — 1994 (11 years )

    Created public awareness campaigns for a disease eradication program covering 34 African countries: The Pan African Rinderpest Campaign (PARC) financed by the European Union. FAO stationed me in the Organization of African Unity as the Regional Communications Adviser for the international team and participating countries 1989-93. I developed the communications campaign concept, methodology, creative materials, and worked in eight countries with national teams to adapt the campaign, promote participatory communications and social marketing approaches, and produce message materials to be effective in their cultures. (Job Title: Chief Technical Advisor, P-4)

    Also served as the FAO Information Coordinator at the Joint FAO/WHO “International Conference on Nutrition”, Rome;

    I was the emergency communications planner in the UN team sent to Turkey / Kurdistan to fight a disease outbreak, 1997.

    Also as a consultant in Rome, Sudan, Ethiopia, Bangladesh:

    For FAO, I provided communications strategies and original media productions including two dozen audio-visual programs, radio programs, communications guides, and other materials to promote various initiatives and technologies to help people to improve their lives in developing countries. I planned a rural Development Communications infrastructure (Ethiopia) used by policymakers. I co-authored FAO's "Development Communication", an overview of this discipline.

    For the International Institute of Tropical Agriculture in Ibadan, Nigeria, and Agrisystems srl, Rome Italy, I created various communicaitons materials such as scripts and audio-visuals, and provided strategy and advice.
