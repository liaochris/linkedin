Franky Mo, LEED AP’s Summary

Franky Mo is the Senior Manager of Environmental Affairs/Global Responsibility for Gap Inc. (Gap) and has over 12 years experience in Corporate Social Responsibility and Sustainability. Franky leads Gap’s corporate environmental strategy and sustainability efforts and supply-chain environmental responsibility program globally. He also manages teams in Latin America and Asia. He was the architect of Gap’s Clean Water Program that was incorporated into Gap brand’s marketing campaign during the re-launch of its premium jeans collection in 2009. Prior to his current role, Franky re-built and managed the supply-chain environmental responsibility function within the Social Responsibility team and developed compliance officers’ knowledge base world-wide.

Franky started with Gap at their international office in Hong Kong where he managed social and environmental compliance programs in Greater China Region. He also worked in Gap’s Korea office where he developed the first vendor compliance capacity building program in Korea and Russia. Franky has represented Gap on several global industry-partnership initiatives, including the Business for Social Responsibility (BSR) and the Natural Resources Defense Council (NRDC).

Prior to joining Gap, Franky worked in an international environmental consulting firm ERM in Hong Kong. He was also awarded a research fellowship by the Department of Energy’s (DOE) Pacific Northwest Laboratory.

Franky has a Master’s degree in Environmental Management from Yale University and a Bachelor of Science degree in Chemistry from UCLA. With his passion for design and architecture, Franky earned his accreditation in Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design (LEED) in 2009. He is also Registered Environmental Auditor under the UK’s Institute of Environmental Management & Assessment (IEMA).

Franky loves to travel for business and pleasure, and has had the benefit of living and working in Asia and the U.S.
