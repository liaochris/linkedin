Demetrius Washington’s Summary

I am currently responsible for converting data for financial institutions to the FIS Electronic Funds Debit System. My expertise in this area includes project management, relationship management, payment network implementations, and ATM terminal installations.

As a Work Flow Manager, my duties included assigning tasks to the research representatives while maintaining service level agreements with Metavante’s clients. In addition, I analyzed trends in research claims to help balance staffing, reduce inventory, and discover potential issues that could impact the end users.

Some of my major career accomplishments are as follows:
In 2001, I was recognized by management as a key member of the customer service team who drastically increased customer satisfaction and service levels from under 10% to over 85% within two weeks at an office in Nevada.
In 2004, I received an applause award from senior management to recognize my dedication and participation in resolving a major payment issues that occurred in August 2004.
In 2005, I was chosen as the Work Flow Manager for the research department and instrumental in reducing inventory and eliminating the research department’s tradition of mandatory overtime during the winter months of the previous years.
In 2006, I was nominated by senior management to participate in the Leadership Experience Action Program (LEAP). LEAP was established in 2000 as a diversity initiative to provide leadership development opportunities for employees. LEAP participants are recognized as employees who have consistently demonstrated high performance, with the potential to develop as a leader.

My towering strengths are written communication skills, easy adaptability to change, and working extremely well others. With over 12 years experience in financial services, I have an impeccable knack for "wowing" customers.
