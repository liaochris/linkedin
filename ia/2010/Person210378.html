Vanessa Casavant’s Summary

Before my current job as the content strategist / writer for electronic media of AdoptUsKids, I was the online communications specialist / writer for the Evans School of Public Affairs at the University of Washington.

Whew! Those were a lot of big titles dealing with the series of information tubes we love and know as the Internet. I couldn't have earned those fancy titles if it weren't for my background in journalism, which led to a passion for finding the story beneath the clutter.

My journey started as a reporter intern in Albany, NY, for The Legislative Gazette and Northeast Public Radio covering state politics. I then moved to Seattle for a three-month internship at The Seattle Times, became a Chips Quinn Scholar with the Freedom Forum in Washington, D.C., and later paid my dues at a small daily in Port Angeles, Wash.

I decided to make a career move in 2006 after realizing my daily coverage of car accidents, school board meetings, and weather round-ups weren't doing much to inspire change within the media or make significant impacts on the world as we know it. So, I left the newsroom and headed to the Quileute Indian Reservation in LaPush, Wash., where I launched a monthly newsletter called The Talking Raven covering tribal politics and national issues related to Indian Country.

When my hankering for city life got the best of me, I landed my former job at the Evans School before joining AdoptUsKids. And, in all of this, I have somehow found time to be active in grassroots political organizing as the vice chair of communications for the 43rd District Democrats in Seattle, Wash.
