*note* I rarely check LinkedIn, if I do it's to push a bunch of accept buttons so people don't feel like I'm ignoring them. Best to connect w/ me on Twitter or email christine [at] christinelu [dotcom]

Christine Lu is a self professed geek girl, sinophile and foodie who grew up in Southern California, received her BA in International Relations with a concentration on East Asian Studies at Boston University.

After graduating from BU, Christine moved to Shanghai in 1999 at the height of the dotcom bubble. With a stint as a clothing designer and marketing manager for her family’s clothing retail business, she attempted to start an e-commerce site for women in China about 6 years before its time. The company was incubated then rolled into TV Shopping Network (TVSN), a joint venture funded by Duty Free Shoppers founder Robert Miller’s private equity firm Search Group, Prudential Assets Management Asia and IAC’s HSN (Home Shopping Network). Christine served for five years as Marketing Director for TV Shopping Network and oversaw the company’s business development and spearheaded the company’s internet, direct marketing and mail order business units in China.

In March 2007 Christine created The China Business Show and served as the Executive Producer and Host for its launch on Entrepreneur Magazine’s podcast network. The show has since evolved into The China Business Network. Christine remains a founder and shareholder of The China Business Network.

Based on her belief that knowledge should be shared in an open free thinking environment that allows new ideas and connections to thrive, Christine is a strong supporter of Creative Commons, a member of TED and curator of TEDxShanghai.

Her latest role is CEO and Co-Founder of Affinity Ventures China, a new boutique firm focused on structuring cross border deal flow in the China luxury, outbound Chinese tourism and outbound Chinese investment circle.
