Jerry Turbeville’s Summary

I am a strategic innovator within the Supply Chain industry and have held leadership positions with manufacturer/distributor of consumer durable goods and food products for Fortune 500 organizations, and in the supply chain software industry.

I have significant experience in building relationships within organizations as well as with customers and vendors, process change management, development of personnel and systems integration to increase operating efficiencies, safety and customer satisfaction.

I have developed KPI’s, and created statistical standards to enhance employee performance, safety, job satisfaction and service. I have experience in reengineering national distribution networks, global supply chain network analysis, warehouse layout configurations, development of logistics contingency strategies for catastrophic events, union avoidance, contract negotiations for: 3PL’s, carrier rate structures, warehouse leases and leased vehicles and trailers.

I have implemented enterprise purchasing/distribution/financial systems, transportation management systems, wireless data and GPS applications, enhanced warehouse management systems, implemented an MRPII enterprise system, an order entry system, and created an inventory management system to manage deployment and inventory turns for a national network of public warehouses.

Education initiatives that have enhanced my management capabilities include linear programming regression analysis, database design, basic programming, Six Sigma green belt, black belt, lean training and an MBA.

Jerry Turbeville’s Specialties:

Complex supply chain systems integration, enhancing performance and service levels, ensuring safety compliance, development of people and process change management.

  • Director Regional Logistics

    Mohawk Industries

    (Public Company; MHK; Textiles industry)

    2001 — 2009 (8 years )

    I joined Mohawk Industries, the largest flooring company, to implement a cultural transformation separating logistics operations from the direct control of the Sales Organization.

    The objectives were to build a logistics operational infrastructure to increase efficiencies, develop personnel and enhance safety of employees and vehicles for 50 locations, 25,000 customers and 640 drivers.

    My initiatives saved $9 million. Reduced 8% of the workforce and delivery equipment, increased cube utilization 118%, improved load factor 180%, enhanced warehouse loading efficiencies 10%; converted drivers to performance-based pay, increased service 15%. Developed the financial structure to ensure continuous process improvement.

    I designed a real-time visibility program for several thousand customers to receive delivery projections to the hour and minute, which Sales and Marketing used as a competitive advantage, and was noted in corporate annual reports.

    Initiated a contract with IBM/ILOG to develop a strategic global supply chain network analysis and created a three year strategic plan. The focus was on inventory deployment, inbound port validation, regional warehouse consolidations and national cross dock warehouse rationalization across business units. The results identified projected savings of several million dollars.

    Implemented a Quality Department with focus on job functions, job descriptions, process flows and employee development. Created training and job audit processes to increase employee effectiveness. Enhanced applicant selection via a proven international assessment process.

    Initiated a nationally proven training method that addressed a $20 million workmen comp back and shoulder injury history, utilizing Western medical orthopedic and Asian martial art practices and biomechanical sciences, emphasizing a kinesthetic (muscle sensory) approach. Projected results will reduce injury frequency 60%.
