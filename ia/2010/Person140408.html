Melanie Taylor’s Summary

Melanie currently serves as a prosecutor in The Philadelphia District Attorney’s Office, where she splits her time between the appellate and trial divisions. Her case load includes everything from homicides to assaults and economic crimes. Prior to joining DA's Office, Melanie worked as a Legislative Analyst Intern at The U.S. Government Accountability Office in Washington, D.C., tracking pending legislation and researching high profile policy issues for the Comptroller General.

Melanie is an alumna of The University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill, where she earned her Bachelor's degree in Political Science in 2001. She also attended The George Washington University Law School, where she earned her J.D. in 2005. She presently serves on the boards of The Barristers’ Association of Philadelphia and The Young Lawyers Division of the Philadelphia Bar Association. She is also actively involved in the West Philadelphia Child Care Network and the National Black Prosecutor's Association.

Melanie’s practice focuses primarily on criminal appellate prosecution. Her expertise also includes family law, public policy, and policy analysis and research. In her personal time, Melanie enjoys photography and international travel. She has lived overseas in Japan, Italy and the U.K.
