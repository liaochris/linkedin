Rob Perry’s Summary

Rob Perry has been in the software development business for 28 years. From November of 1995 until October 2005 he served as Vice President at Pathlore Software Corporation. Mr. Perry was a member of the $20 million Management Buyout (MBO) of Legent’s Information Technology Division (ITD) which formed the foundation for Pathlore.

As a founder, Director and Vice President at Pathlore Software Corporation, Perry served in several key operational and strategic roles. As Vice President of Professional Services, Perry created and grew Pathlore’s North American and International Professional Services organization. Perry created a successful Federal Sales organization that was a leader in the government e-learning market place. Perry also served as Vice President of sales and operations for two of Pathlore’s product lines, where he grew sales to over $10 million per year. During this time Pathlore opened five offices around the world, acquired three software companies and launched six software products. Pathlore revenue grew from a small spin-off with first-year revenues of $5.6 million to be one of the top eLearning companies with annual revenues of more than $28 million. In October 2005 Pathlore was acquired by SumTotal Systems (see announcement at http://www.pscinvestors.com/051004Announcement.pdf)

From 1993 until 1995 Perry was Director of Federal Consulting at DataFocus Corporation, where he was responsible for winning and performance of Federal contracts for software application development. His success resulted in consulting contracts with gross margins in excess 65%. From 1990 through 1993, Perry worked with Hannon Armstrong & Company, an investment bank in Alexandria, VA, on several international and domestic Merchant Banking projects. From 1979 through 1990 Perry served as a software development manager at Bell Atlantic Corporation, responsible for system design and programming.
