Eleanor Howe’s Summary

I am project lead for a large, open-source bioinformatics desktop application, called MeV, or MultiExperiment Viewer. I coordinate a team of four in the development of the program, support of our users, and promotion of MeV as a platform for bioinformatics software development.

I am also reading for my DPhil (PhD equivalent) in Bioinformatics at the University of Oxford under the direction of Drs. Chris Holmes and John Quackenbush. My project involves using microarrays and statistical tools to investigate the activity of microRNAs in epithelial ovarian cancer.
