  • Progressive Media Data Management System | Technical Lead

    EMC Consulting

    (Information Technology and Services industry)

    December 2009 — March 2010 (4 months)

    EMC Consulting was engaged to provide support and mentoring to an existing development team at Progressive Media. The team was developing a new data management system intended to manage data for around 200 websites owned and managed by the company. These sites were being managed via a number of different applications, and the goal was to reduce the overhead in maintaining all of these systems, as well as develop a common platform on which the front end sites could be rebuilt.

    The EMCC team worked as pairs with the in-house developers to facilitate direct knowledge transfer, as well as running regular brown bag sessions. We also helped to refine the Kanban approach being used to run the project, and to put an automated build and deployment process in place.

    The technical solution was built using a combination of open source and commercial software, including .NET 3.5 SP1, C#, ASP.NET MVC, SQL Server 2008, Endeca, S#arp Architecture, the Spark View Engine, NHibernate, Castle Windsor, xVal, PostSharp, Log4Net, AutoMapper, MSpec, Rhino Mocks and jQuery.

  • www.fancydressoutfitters.co.uk | Technical Lead

    EMC Consulting

    (Information Technology and Services industry)

    July 2009 — October 2009 (4 months)

    Fancy Dress Outfitters is a new entrant into the UK/European fancy dress market, which is estimated to be worth €130 million annually. Their brief was to build a customer facing site that could be market leader within 3 years and to do so in less than 1000 man days. Their tight budget dictated that the solution had to keep fixed costs such as software licensing to a minimum.

    The final solution was delivered in ten 2-week sprints, and went live in time for Halloween – the busiest time in the UK fancy dress market. It was built on the Microsoft stack, with a number of open source projects used to reduce the operational costs. The technologies involved are:
    • .NET 3.5 SP1, C# and ASP.NET MVC
    • Windows Communication Foundation
    • SQL Server 2008 and SQL Server Service Broker
    • Miicrosoft Velocity
    • S#arp Architecture, the Spark View Engine, NHibernate, Castle Windsor and the xVal Validation Framework
    • Solr
    • N2CMS
    • PostSharp, Log4Net and AutoMapper
    • MbUnit, Rhino Mocks and Selenium
    • jQuery

  • River Island website redevelopment | Technical Lead

    EMC Consulting

    (Information Technology and Services industry)

    January 2008 — April 2009 (1 year 4 months)

    EMC Consulting was engaged to completely rebuild River Island's existing website, providing both an innovative new look and feel and a complete new technical platform. In addition to this, we also delivered a set of tools to manage the content and product catalogue.

In addition, heavy use was made of lower level technologies such as Castle Windsor, jQuery, RhinoMocks, Log4Net and PostSharp.

    As well as performing the technical architect and lead role on the project, I carried out extensive performance optimisation and testing work using Site Confidence.

    Conchango

    (Public Company; 201-500 employees; EMC; Information Technology and Services industry)

    January 2007 — October 2007 (10 months)

    The i-Field programme is Chevron Upstream Europe's (CUE) version of the Digital Oilfield programmes now common to most large Oil and Gas Exploration and Production companies.   • Project Seer | Developer, Technical Lead

    Conchango

    (Public Company; 201-500 employees; EMC; Information Technology and Services industry)

    January 2006 — December 2006 (1 year )

    The Seer application provides CUE with a one stop shop for accessing core business data, such as production, health and safety and maintenance and reliability data. It also provides tools to allow users to access and analyse both historic and real time data. The Seer architecture, built on Service Oriented Business Intelligence (SOBI) principles has provided CUE with a foundation for wider CUE initiatives.

    On this project, I lead the team developing an ASP.NET 2.0 based portal for accessing the core set of reports and tools that allow users to access and analyse their data. The team was also responsible for implementing the web services which exposed source system data to the BI components of the solution.

    This project made extensive use of the main components of SQL Server 2005 – Database, Integration Services, Analysis Services and Reporting Services. In addition, we also integrated with OSISoft’s RtWebParts to provide real-time visualisation of data coming from the offshore oil platform SCADA systems.
