Filippo Morelli’s Summary

I am, what the Agile folks call, a Generalized Specialist. An artist at heart, architect by nature and an engineer by trade. I see patterns, abstract frequently and enjoy finding parallel patterns in problem spaces to cull the essence out of challenges and goals. Art is science with too many variables.

My current work interests include building world class software development organizations, startups, turnarounds and most of all shaping groups that are built to last, focusing on the craft of developing high quality product vision and software products. I have a passion for continuous improvement, surrounding myself with people better than I and finding the great things in each individual's personality.

I've developed many software systems, re-written as many as I've built. An old school UNIX guy, I cut my teeth on Sun 3's, NeXT machines and worked with a lot of talented senior geeks both in product shops and in services development orgs. I've programmed everything from Heathkit Robots to suitcases with built-in satellite comm devices. Built systems in areas of intrusion detection, shrinkwrap BI product, and C3I. Coded C/C++/Objective C to Perl and Java, just to name a few.

While a geek at heart, I have a strong extroverted relationship gene, so in the past many years, I've spent time building communities and groups - I run several online communities which are subscription membership based with a host of advertisers. I've spent the first twelve years of my career as a software architect, developer and project manager; the last nine years in technology organizations, designing, build and growing the teams and operational aspects of top notch software development ecosystems.

My interest are varied. Began studying classical guitar at age 9, currently pursuing luthiery (guitar making). I've built homes, apprenticed to a master craftsman, built race cars, auto and motorcycle restorations, and built everything from jigs and tooling to you name it.
