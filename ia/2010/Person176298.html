Rodney Gaylor’s Summary

Rodney Gaylor is a Senior iSeries (AS400) Programmer Analyst with 28 years of successful contracting, consulting and employment in all phases of project development. After graduating in 1981 from United Electronics Institute in Dallas, Texas, Rodney went immediately into contracting and consulting. This early consulting experience gave him broad exposure to a diversity of industries and project development methodologies.

Rodney remained in the contract programming business in Central Texas until September 1998 when he moved from Texas to South Florida for what was supposed to be a three month contract with Bluegreen Corporation in Boca Raton. That contract extended to almost two years, followed by over eight years as a full-time employee of the same company.

The unfortunate downturn in the economy and subsequent tightening of the credit markets in late 2008 forced Bluegreen to make the difficult decision to eliminate a very large portion of its work force. As part of that process, Rodney's position as a Senior Programmer Analyst was eliminated in January 2009.

Being the eternal optimist, Rodney gladly took advantage of the layoff and moved his family halfway across the continent, back to Central Texas to be close to family. He is now looking forward to the next chapter in his professional career with a company that is as happy as he is to be in Central Texas. [UPDATE: As of January 2009, Rodney is happy to be back in the Contract Programming business in the DFW Metroplex.]

PROFESSIONAL STRENGTHS: Ability to step into any environment and quickly get up to speed supporting existing systems and applications, as well as a proven track record of working independently, with little or no supervision. Also equally able to serve as a team player or as a project leader on small, medium and large projects to carry them from original concept to full implementation and user training.
