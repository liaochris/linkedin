  • Software engineer

    CA

    (Public Company; CA; Information Technology and Services industry)

    August 1997 — July 2009 (12 years )

    I joined CA as a developer and initially worked in the Quality Assurance department, testing products being developed in the Unicenter product portfolio (now known as ITCM) so as to better appreciate the quality and usability requirements from a customer's perspective. Over the next 7 years I worked in the Porting Team. I ported the Asset Management, Software Delivery and Data Transport suites to the platforms we supported: AIX, HPUX, Linux, Solaris, Unixware, SCO Unix, OS/2 - and VMS.; I soon became responsible for the CA's internationalisation library known as CAWIN. This library lies at the heart of CA's ITCM product range, allowing it to operate in a locale and platform-independent fashion, handling UNICODE, MBCS and UTF-8 character encodings transparently on all supported platforms, including Windows, and providing multi-language support. After a few years I upgraded and re-launched the library as CAI18N, with vastly improved support for UTF-8 and previously unsupported locales, improved stability and message handling, and without the legacy baggage of its predecessor.

    In recent years I have also been (jointly) responsible for the design, development and maintenance of the Infrastructure Deployment component, dmdeploy, of the ITCM suite on Windows and UNIX. Dmdeploy installs and upgrades the ITCM infrastructure on anything from individual machines to entire networks. It is a multi-threaded, distributed, multi-platform tool that can be run interactively or in an automated fashion, over IPv4 and/or IPv6 networks.

    Furthermore I implemented the Asset Management IPv6 support on UNIX/Linux versions of Asset Management and have also worked on the ITCM installer.

    I have gained a wide range of experience developing on mainstream operating systems: Windows, AIX, HPUX, Linux, Mac OSX, Solaris, Unixware, OS/2, VMS as well as networking, internationalization, C/C++, shell script and debugging tools. Last but not least, I am rapidly becoming proficient in C#.

  • Software Designer

    Nortel Networks

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; NT; Telecommunications industry)

    May 1993 — August 1997 (4 years 4 months)

    I joined BNR Europe to work on the Generic Services Framework (GSF) project, at that time one of the world's largest object-oriented projects. I initially worked on diagnostics for the World Line Card and, owing to my knowledge of the German language and electronics background, I was selected to go to Germany to help negotiate the purchase of test equipment to ensure the compliance of our equipment to German standards.

    On my return I requested and was given funding for the purchase of an Eiffel compiler for object oriented development. I worked on an experimental project with members of my department and presented the results to other departments. I was subsequently invited to train members of other departments in the practical application of OO technology, using Eiffel. The training was very well received by both engineers and management. I was then given the responsibility of developing the connections framework for Global Trunks. On completion I was selected to help integrate and test the trunking software with the ISDN and Lines components,; Afterwards I was rewarded with a short, all-expenses-paid trip to New York by the company.
    I then led a 3-man team to tackle ISUP and CTUP protocol violations and Exception handling, as well as train my members in OO basics and GSF fundamentals.

    My next task was to assist with CAS register signalling provisioning and was given the responsibility of improving the storage, distribution and presentation of information to the craftsperson. My proposals were accepted by my department and implemented. I subsequently volunteered to take on the data download aspect of the work, in addition to my other responsibilities. I also played an active role in the domain analysis and definition of the object-oriented call processing architecture for CAS. I worked on some CAS realtime improvements and began to look into ATM technology. I was responsible for the development of the CAS outpulse functionality.
