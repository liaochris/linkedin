Gail Donegan’s Experience

  • Senior Postdoctoral Research Associate (Reproductive Toxicology)

    Liverpool School of Tropical Medicine

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    August 2009 — Present (1 year 1 month)

    A critical knowledge gap exists in the use of Artemisinin anti-malarial drug combinations in pregnancy malaria. Studies indicate that these compounds are potentially teratogenic and embryoxic in animal models at doses within the human range.

    The goal of this major EU Seventh Framework Programme (FP7) collaborative research partnership is to understand the basis for this teratogenicity.

    Preclinical segment II teratology studies aim to reveal potentially lethal dysmorphogenesis caused by prenatal exposures to compounds after implantation and throughout the period of major organogenesis.

    The development of high throughput whole embryo culture (WEC) screening methodologies, micromass or limb bud culture (MM) and embryonic stem cell testing (EST) complements these Segment II studies.

    These will facilitate greater understanding of the teratogenic potential of peroxide - based antimalarial chemotherapeutic combinations and lead to better recommendations based on more informative risk:benefit analyses.

    The development of these novel ex vivo and in vitro test systems in developmental toxicology have been motivated by the 3Rs. The 3R's refers to the replacement, reduction and refinement of animals in research which use well-designed and well-timed experiments to reduce the numbers of animals used during in vivo toxicity testing of many classes of compounds.
