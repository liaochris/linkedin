Paula Hendrickson’s Summary

I am an experienced writer with over 600 published articles to my credit.

While the bulk of my work has focused on the television industry, I'm always looking for opportunities to expand into new or different markets. In addition to articles, I write a weekly column, blog and provide copy writing services for a select group of small to mid-sized companies.

Whatever I'm working on, I do my best to keep my editors and clients happy by brainstorming, pitching ideas, securing sources, conducting interviews and turning in clean copy on tight deadlines.

Paula Hendrickson’s Specialties:

Topics covered include: entertainment and television, advertising and marketing, pop culture, writing, bungalows, fiber arts (knitting, crocheting, quilting), dogs, education, career planning, food, personality profiles, neighborhood associations...and everything in between.

  • Contributing Writer

    TelevisionWeek

    (Writing and Editing industry)

    May 2007 — October 2007 (6 months)

    When the editor of a bi-monthly magazine I regularly write for thought an article of mine wouldn't be timely for their next issue, she encouraged me to shop it around. The first place I contacted was TelevisionWeek. They bought it the same day. My article was in print within about a week. Shortly thereafter, one of their editors had a lot of assignments to make, but some of their regular freelancers were on vacation. I stepped in and wrote eight articles for sections covering the NABJ Awards, the SEJ Awards, and the RTNDA's Murrow/Unity Awards.

  • Contributing Writer

    Point-of-Purchase Magazine

    (Publishing industry)

    May 1999 — May 2003 (4 years 1 month)

    Writing for Point-Of-Purchase was a crash course in p-o-p and in-store advertising. I loved interviewing the creative teams behind many of the top p-o-p campaigns of that time. A big lesson I learned is that you don't have to be an industry expert to write for most trade publications as long as you have access to the real experts and know how to ask the right questions. I know it sounds odd, but more than five years since the magazine folded I kind of miss discussing end caps, displays, and plan-o-grams.

  • Contributing Writer

    Cablevision Magazine

    (Publishing industry)

    June 1999 — November 2001 (2 years 6 months)

    Writing for Cablevision was a great experience and a lot of fun, so I was very disappointed when the long-running magazine folded. As a weekly magazine it went through tremendous amounts of copy, which gave me the opportunity to write just about everything from 50-word blurbs to a 4,700-word article on the unique collections of some of cable TV's top executives. Some of the articles I wrote back then are still among my favorites.

  • Contributing Editor

    Blue Dolphin

    (Writing and Editing industry)

    1994 — 1998 (4 years )

    How many people can say they got a job because they wrote a complaint letter? I can't even recall what I complained about, but the publisher must have seen something in my letter. I wrote for several Blue Dolphin newsletters: Writing for Money, Editors & Writers, and American Writer's Review.
