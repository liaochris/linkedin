Collin C. MacMillan’s Summary

In 2010, 8- & 12-core processors, 16GB DIMMs, low-cost "enterprise ready" 10GE and SSDs will combine for a massive realignment of infrastructure virtualization. Open storage and desktop virtualization will see big gains, creating greater inroads into SMB/SME markets.

In the datacenter, higher application densities will promote orders of magnitude energy savings that will transcend gimicky "green" marketing and begin to define "engineered efficiency standards." Infrastructure as a service (IaaS) will begin to displace (or transform) traditional managed services creating new agility for the next wave of innovative start-ups.

Our model for orchestrating and delivering targeted elements of these technologies will continue to accelerate returns for our clients. It's going to be a fast-and-furious year!

  • Chief Operations Officer

    UR*ONRAMP, INC

    (Internet industry)

    August 1997 — September 2002 (5 years 2 months)

    Internet startup. Managed operations for the regional Internet Service Provider spanning Tuscaloosa, Fayette, Pickens, Lamar, Perry and Greene counties in Alabama.

    Between 1997 and 1998, Craig White and I designed and deployed the first 100Mbps, Metro-wide Ethernet service in Alabama for the Tuscaloosa City Schools incorporating 8 sites using MMF and SMF and off-the-shelf equipment. Fiber deployment was handled in partnership with the City Schools and Tuscaloosa Department of Transportation saving the system nearly $2M in funding versus competing ATM-based technologies.

    In 2000, we introduced the first wireless broadband service in Central Alabama based. Utilized as a local loop bypass technology, we provided high-speed, broadband Internet and private network access 8-months ahead of the incumbent over a 70 sq-mi footprint.

    While responsible for operations, recurring revenue grew from $86K/yr in 1996 to $580K/yr in 2000.

  • VP Computer Technology

    (Research industry)
