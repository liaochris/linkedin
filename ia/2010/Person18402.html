Audra Sewell-Noblique’s Summary

As director of Noblique Studios I wear many hats. I work diligently to promote, market, and manage the Professional Career of Nic Noblique, sculptor.

I've had the pleasure of owning and operating two art spaces, Noblique's Gallery A-go-go in Abilene, Texas and the Nic Noblique Studio Gallery in Galveston, Texas. Each were highly regarded in their respective art communities and the Nic Noblique Studio Gallery was given the "Best Art Gallery" award by Galveston.com and Company for 2008.

Audra Sewell-Noblique’s Specialties:

Art Consulting, Inventory and Catalog Management, Portfolio Presentation, Competition and Calls for Entry Management, Preparing Proposals for Galleries, Dealers, and Coordinating Special Art Events.

  • Owner, Director

    Nic Noblique Studio Gallery

    (Fine Art industry)

    March 2006 — September 2008 (2 years 7 months)

    The Nic Noblique Studio Gallery, opened in March of 2007, was a 5000 square foot playground for artists and art lovers alike. Located just off the strand in downtown Galveston Island, it was voted the best gallery in Galveston in 2008. Much like our previous endeavor, Noblique’s Gallery A-go-go in Abilene, Texas, we once again created an experience unlike any other art gallery or art studio you are likely to find. The space included Noblique’s working studio with heavy machinery, rollers, welders, canvas, paint, and a chaotic assortment of everything you can imagine an artist might like to experiment with (not to mention a 27-foot half pipe – a sculptural pursuit in and of itself). The idea was to give the public an inside view of what goes into creating Noblique’s monumental sculptures that eventually make their way to galleries, sculpture gardens, private collections, and installations around the globe while presenting other works in a truly interactive environment.

    The Studio Gallery also encompassed a 2000 sq. ft. gallery showroom exhibiting the latest of Noblique’s work as well as works from a range of artists such as Bryan Kuntz, John Palmer, Kay Kemp, Bonnie J. McConnell, Virginia Baker and includes the Metzger Collection of Texas and Regional Contemporary Art. Sculpture, painting, watercolor, collage and assemblage, and photography mingle together in a fairly raw format bringing the creation, presentation, and final exposition of contemporary art to you in an awesome juxtaposition of gallery and studio all under one roof.

    The Studio Gallery was destroyed in the wake of Hurricane Ike in September 2008. Although Nic and I attempted to reopen the space in a new location, this proved to be quite difficult and the decision was made to return to his ranch in West Texas to rebuild his studio and recuperate with his family and develop plans for a new and always exciting chapter in our lives!

  • Owner, Director

    Noblique's Gallery A-Go-Go

    (Fine Art industry)

    March 2006 — March 2007 (1 year 1 month)

    Noblique’s Gallery A-go-go opened in March 2006 (after 4 months of renovation) and closed in March 2007 when we moved to Galveston to expand Nic’s Houston market. We moved into the space (The Cockerell Building on North 2nd Street in Downtown Abilene) and did a complete demo and renovation. We quickly realized the space was exactly what we'd hoped it would be once the drop ceilings went away and the plaster was taken down to reveal a beautiful brick interior with steel beams and high ceilings. The architectural details in the historic building were awesome and it was really exciting transforming the space into a beautiful warehouse gallery.

    The goal was to give abilene a contemporary art gallery that was really stellar and visually could stand up to any great gallery space in a larger city. We were so proud of how we transformed that space and fully realized the vibe and edgy atmosphere we wanted. It was the perfect complement to Nic's sculpture and painting and well suited for the other artist's we hand picked to feature in the gallery. We felt that abilene had a strong cultural arts foundation but it was missing a broader element - it was time to bring the abilene art scene full circle with a real contemporary gallery. Relevant, cutting edge artists from all over the U.S. would truly enrich the otherwise sheltered Abilene art scene. Looking outside of this West Texas box was at the heart of our concept.

    We filled the gallery with beautiful post modern furniture and mid-century furniture for sale - the first retailer I know of anywhere in the area that offered authentic reproductions of such artists as Mies van der Rohe, Isamu Noguchi, and La Corbusier. This was another important element to the gallery and our vision. We were quite proud to share with everyone the hugely relevant contributions these artists made and to be able to offer the highest quality reproductions of their most iconic designs was really thrilling.
