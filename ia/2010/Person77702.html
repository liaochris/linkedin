  • Senior Technical Staff

    IBM

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; IBM; Computer Software industry)

    1998 — 2001 (3 years )

    Fairway Technologies was acquired by IBM after Blockbuster was acquired by Viacom. While I was at IBM (the 2nd time) I led the development team on the "Madison Music Distribution" project. This was a consortium of the 5 major music labels and IBM to create a CD electronic distribution system over the internet. Of course this was several years before iTunes and was limited by the fact the music labels still wanted to distribute CD's rather than individual songs. In this deveopment though we created a highly secure digital rights management system that IBM is still selling today.

  • Senior Engineer

    Fairway Technologies

    (Computer Software industry)

    1993 — 1997 (4 years )

    I left Citrix to join another startup that focused on electronic content distribution. Fairway was a joint venture between IBM and Blockbuster. I lead the team that developed one of the first music distribution systems that would burn cds inside the Blockbuster Music stores. We also developed a Sega video game distribution system that we rolled out in a trial in 30 Blockbuster Video stores in Columbia SC.

  • Senior Software Engineer

    Citrix Systems

    (Public Company; Computer Software industry)

    1989 — 1993 (4 years )

    I joined Citrix as employee number 6, was part of the original 5 operating sytems architects of the system to jump ship from IBM to form Citrix. At Citrix I led the software development for all of our kernel changes to enable the "Multi-User" features of the Citrix products. This work was the core of an asset that Citrix would later sell to Microsoft for $175 Million. To this day I still follow Citrix quite closely.

  • Senior Programmer

    IBM

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; IBM; Computer Software industry)

    1983 — 1989 (6 years )

    I joined IBM right after the release of the IBM PC. It was an exciting to see the birth of the PC industry. During this time I wrote one of the first video games for the PC called LIPTOCOE, sold about 5,000 copies of it throught the IBM Personally Developed Software program. I was one of the first software developers working on the OS/2 1.0 product and lead the Physical Memory Management development team. I worked with a great team out Microsoft to develope the kernel of the Operating System. When I left IBM to join Citrix I was leading the OS/2 Memory Managmenet Architecture for version 2.0.
