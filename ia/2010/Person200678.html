Ted Krekoukis’s Experience

  • Emerging Media Manager

    Conde Nast

    (Privately Held; Publishing industry)

    2005 — Present (5 years )

    • Responsible for operations, distribution, and product management for the Conde Nast online video platform. Consistent publishing to sites and distribution partners of well produced programs, featuring talented personalities, built an audience of more than 6 million video stream per month. My responsibilities in regards to operations, included creating process, workflows, standard policies, automating, and managing the systems used by the process. Our process: ideation, production, archiving & version control, transcoding, CMS entry, & publishing to both internal web sites as well as external partners. Various control points put in place to ensure review, notifications & increased efficiencies. My responsibilities in regards to distribution, included technical Business development support for the VP of Emerging Media. Our external distribution plan had 3 goals: traffic generation, revenue sharing, strategic placement. Success was proven by the fact that partner traffic exceeded traffic from our web sites. As the main partner contact, to companies such as Brightcove, YouTube, Sony, Verizon, and many more – I was able to create revenue opportunities by building strong partnerships and contacts in the industry. These partnerships often lead to the creation of new products. Working with the product development team I was able to launch several new products, creating additional revenue opportunities. For example, the Brightcove platform enabled us to create: 1) Custom “Skin-able” Library experience following proprietary 10 point discoverability plan. 2) Single clip, for staff bloggers 3) Branded Viral Player for social distribution. Other video products outside of Brightcove included: 1) Mobile Video Applications with pre-roll advertising 2) Live Streaming. Traffic analysis, as well as other inputs, such as customer feedback & user testing was used to improve our products. Version 1.2 of our library product yielded 100% increase in streams per user.
