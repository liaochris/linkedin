Fernando Tomasel’s Summary

R&D group leader with strong scientific background in a wide variety of subjects and demonstrated experience in performing research and development in both academic and industrial environments.

Short Bio: I earned a MSc in Physics from the Universidad Nacional de Mar del Plata (Argentina) in 1989. After working in analytical modeling of inertial confinement fusion targets for about two years, I moved to Fort Collins, CO to earn MSc and PhD degrees in Electrical Eng. from Colorado State University. My MSc thesis included the results of the first demonstration of soft x-ray lasing in Ne-like argon capillary plasmas pumped by fast, high power pulsed discharges. During my PhD, I demonstrated for the first time lasing action in Ne-like S ions by capillary discharge excitation of sulfur vapor produced by ablation of a solid target. I also investigated the effects of intense, pulsed magnetic fields in the dynamics of capillary discharge lasers.
After receiving my PhD I returned to Argentina, where I became Associate Professor at the University of Mar del Plata. My research work during the 1998-2001 period included non-destructive optical diagnostics, fiber optic sensors and application of neural networks to the analysis of ecological data and modeling. In 2002, I returned to Colorado to work for Advanced Energy, Inc, where I have held positions as both researcher and manager for R&D and Product Development groups. I am currently Head of the New Technology Introduction Group for Plasma Sources and RF Products at AE. I am also an Adjunct Faculty member volunteering at the ECE Department, Colorado State University. I am author or coauthor of about 30 articles in peer reviewed journals with a total of about 900 citations as of Aug 09. I have also coauthored a number of papers in proceedings and conference presentations, and 9 US patents. Besides research, I have been heavily involved in teaching at different levels, from Kindergarten to College.
