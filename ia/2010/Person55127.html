DERON TRIFF’s Summary

Deron Triff is a Digital Media Business Development & Marketing Executive who combines entrepreneurial start-up experience with a 15-year successful track record in developing new lines of business and driving profitability for world-class independent media brands. A skillful strategist, communicator and consensus builder, Deron has a natural ability to anticipate, conceptualize and execute on growth opportunities. His passion for the intersection between digital media, entertainment and social change led Deron to build two for-profit social ventures -- Changents.com and DAC Media Ventures.

DAC Media Ventures is a boutique media consultancy that specializes in developing new lines of digital business for mission-driven, world-class independent media brands, including Sundance Institute (Sundance Film Festival), SnagFilms, Paley Center for Media, among others. Changents is a unique social media platform and brand that engages audiences in the unfolding stories of "Change Agents" around the world who are taking on the world's most urgent social and environmental issues.

Deron has served as Vice President of Business Development for Scholastic Entertainment where he architected the joint venture with NBC Universal to launch Qubo, a children’s block on NBC and Telemundo. As Vice President of Digital Ventures for PBS, he represented the network in putting together the successful preschool cable network and video-on-demand service, Sprout – a joint venture with Comcast Corporation, HIT Entertainment and Sesame Workshop.

Deron has extensive international experience in setting up cable networks in Latin America and satellite television businesses throughout Europe. He holds an international MBA from McGill University in Montreal, speaks Spanish fluently and is excited by new opportunities in the evolving media landscape.
