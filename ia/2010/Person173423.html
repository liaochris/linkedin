Ridgely Evers’s Summary

Ridgely got his first computer in 1967, and started his first business in the mid 1970s. He has held chief executive positions across the technology, real estate, investment, and consumer products industries.

He currently leads Establishment Capital Partners, where he and his team have developed a breakthrough approach to small business investing – one that stands to attract billions each year in private capital to grow this critical sector of the economy. The firm’s innaugural fund will begin investing in Northern California in 2010.

His passion for small business first surfaced at Intuit, where he led the creation of QuickBooks, the market-dominant small business bookkeeping program. While there, he also played a central role in creating the world’s first online banking system.

Following his time at Intuit he served as CEO of five technology startups, including Exactly Vertical, a pioneer in supply-chain automation for small business; nCircle, one of the leading companies in internet network security; and NetBooks (now WorkingPoint), a company bringing the power of SaaS to small business.

He is also a partner at Tapit Partners, a consulting firm providing assistance to both startups and Global 2000 companies.

In 1988 he imported the first olive trees brought into the US in the 20th century. He and his wife Colleen McGlynn then founded DaVero Farms, today one of the most highly-regarded producers of extra virgin olive oils, wines, and other artisan food products.

He serves on the board of SCORE, a nationwide organization with over 12,000 volunteer executives who provide counseling services to small business owners, and is active in a number of local organizations in Sonoma County in Northern Califoria, where he lives.

He earned a BA in International Relations, with distinction, from Stanford University, and an MBA from Stanford's Graduate School of Business.
