  • Founder

    AllianceQ.com

    (Internet industry)

    October 2008 — September 2009 (1 year )

    Created the AllianceQ concept to solve the "Chicken and Egg" problem faced by every online company - "How do you solve supply without demand and vice-versa?"

    AllianceQ is a very simple solution - and it solved QuietAgent's candidate supply problem very elegantly.

    Corporate America get millions of job candidates apply to them each year, and they simply cannot hire everyone who applies. We aggregate these 'excess candidates' in our database and let the members hire from each other for free.

    Candidates get access to more jobs, Alliance members get more candidates for free, and we get to sell access to the candidate database to other companies.

    AllianceQ gives QuietAgent a $100+ million dollar annual marketing budget for free, while providing 'wins' for everyone involved.

    Initial AllianceQ Members:
    ADP, ARAMARK, AT&T, Baxter, Bayer, Best Buy, Entergy, FPL, Hewitt, Hyatt, National Urban League, Sears Holdings, Sodexo, Starbucks, TJX, Wachovia, 7-Eleven, Allstate, AON, Avery Dennison, Baker Hughes, BASF, Bausch & Lomb, BearingPoint, Broadridge, Ecolab, H&R Block, Intuit, LexisNexis, Mercer, Office Depot, Oracle, OSI Group, Perot Systems, Staples, United Airlines, VMware

  • Founder

    UnitedWeWork.org

    (Human Resources industry)

    August 2008 — July 2009 (1 year )

    Created United We Work - A year long nationwide program designed to stimulate hiring, by connecting job seekers and employers without any recruiting, job posting or advertising fees.

    Any job seeker looking for work can join United We Work and be hired for free. Any employer can easily find, contact and hire job seekers without incurring recruitment costs.

    Brought in nine leading corporations to sponsor the program - 7-Eleven, ADP, Allstate, AT&T, Hewitt Associates, Hyatt Hotels & Resorts, Office Depot, Sears Holdings and Starbucks.

    The goals for United We Work were:
    - Employers can make more hires because there are no fees
    - Job seekers get hired faster
    - We help America get back to work

    The end result will (we hope) be:
    - 3 to 5 million+ candidates
    - 250,000+ employers
    - Stimulated hiring across America

  • Founder & CEO

    QuietAgent, Inc.

    (Human Resources industry)

    October 2005 — June 2009 (3 years 9 months)

    After looking extensively at the (then) online recruiting models and offerings, I came to the conclusion they were not really trying to solve the problem of recruitment - they were trying to solve a problem of revenue - and were using recruitment to do it. Enter QuietAgent.

  • Founder & CEO

    StaffCV

    (Privately Held; Computer Software industry)

    1999 — July 2005 (6 years )

    Founded the company to tackle the growing 'mass applicant' problems being faced by companies, especially airlines, which get inundated with unsolicited resume from potential cabin crew and pilots.

    Built the first common-candidate profile 'matching' based Applicant Tracking System, allowing a candidate to have a single resume profile that could be updated centrally and used to apply to multiple companies, and an ATS system allowing recruiters unlimited definition of field based criteria to match specific requirement from millions of people.

    The software released the notion of 'candidate matching' to the world in early 2001, a first. Today the ATS has powered the recruitment portals for 100+ companies from small business to organizations with over 100,000 employees, for key brands such as Qantas and the Virgin Group.

  • Founder & CEO

    Forte Solutions

    (Airlines/Aviation industry)

    1992 — 2000 (8 years )

    Founded the company and built the world's first PC based airline management suite. Aimed at regional airlines, the product was a low cost alternative to the $10+ million Oracle / Saber solution. We quickly captured around 60% of the regional airlines in the Asia-Pacific region.

    The product covered the entire airline operational sphere from aircraft scheduling, crew pairing generation, crew rostering, change management, aircraft / crew disrupt management, crew tracking, and optimization.

    The company was sold to Accenture in 2005.

  • Co-Founder & CEO

    Forte Communications

    (Telecommunications industry)

    1994 — 1997 (3 years )

    While building Forte Solutions, I was introduced to the mobile phone space, and specifically, the capability of the GSM cellular network to send a short message to a mobile phone.

    We developed and patented the underlying architecture and protocols that took mobile-to-mobile TXT messaging, and made it a ubiquitous two-way platform allowing the sending, receiving and replying of short-messages to and from a cellular phone, to any device connected over the Internet.

    We launched the technology at the world telecom trade fair in Geneva (1995), as a commercial application suite for mobile network operators. Included were tools for Novell, Microsoft Outlook and Microsoft Exchange Server take would take an email received in your inbox and forward it as TXT to your phone, and you could reply from your phone directly back to the sender's email-box - this was 1995.

    The patent was around the two-way messaging protocols used to route messages (and some other secret stuff) - today you simply call it TXT.
