Heidi Bayer’s Summary

While the title of this white paper should seem self-evident - HOW TO MANAGE BRANDS IN THE AGE OF SOCIAL MEDIA - it has no simple answers. At the point where some of our colleagues are embracing social media as a way of sharing innovative and creative content, others find themselves trying to manage their brands in a chaotic environment where, the belief may be that sharing is detrimental to the brand itself. In the chaotic environment, brand managers find that despite their best efforts the brand is shared with a large audience, commented on, good or bad, and put out for all to see. While marketers, advertisers and CMO’s can concurrently be thrilled and frightened by this wide open electronic world, there is no doubt that all of us - brand marketers, content creators, and advertisers - are at a crossroads yet again. Technology, and therefore traditional marketing methods, appears to be subservient to the consumer driven content creators and critics.

An interesting jumping off point, if not a complete segway, is what occurred recently with the Tiger Woods brand. In this case consumers, participants themselves - not marketers, publicists or brand managers - claiming their place in the annals of social media, had a hand in Mr. Woods’ fall from grace. Those who manage public people understand the intense need for privacy, but how exactly to go about managing it can prove difficult when everyone has the ability to make comments, take pictures and video can be uploaded in a second to the internet and tagged for all to see.

So where does that leave us? What will assist brand marketers in controlling the chaos of social media and how do we go about creating a broad based brand management policy? Check your device at the door? No tweeters allowed? The creation of brandbot® crawlers?

As brand marketers the choice is - and should be - ours.
