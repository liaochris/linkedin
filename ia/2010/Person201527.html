Tatjana Greiner’s Summary

Tatjana Greiner is a published writer. Her first novel, Spring, Baby! and its special edition were published by Rowohlt Verlag, Germany. Recently, her short story, Big in Hollywood was published in a Random House Group anthology. Ms. Greiner's play, Wallflowers was one of the winners of the Jerome Lawrence Award and staged at 24th Street Theatre, Los Angeles and FirstStage, Hollywood. Ms. Greiner works as a professional writer in English and German.

As a freelance editor and story consultant, she provides professional, supportive feedback in a personable and constructive environment. Ms. Greiner specializes in expert story analyses for writers, including plays, screenplays, novels and any type of fiction as well as non-fiction, treatments, and book proposals. A former long-term writer's assistant to Emmy award-winner Gail Parent, she is well-versed in story structure and all aspects of the editing process. She has studied with Hubert Selby Jr., Shelley Berman, Paul Zindel, James Ragan, Kenneth Turan, Tristine Rainer, and many others.

As a translator for both language pairs GER/ENGL and ENGL/GER, she has been working for the Goethe-Institut, San Francisco for a number of years, translating, among others, poetry by German spoken word artists Bas Böttcher, Julian Heun, Sulaiman Masomi, and Michel Abdollahi. The translations were available, and some of them read, at City Lights Bookstore as part of Other Words: San Francisco International Poetry Festival and Lit Crawl SF.

Formerly the Vice President of the Board of Directors of FirstStage, Hollywood, Ms. Greiner now serves on their artistic committee.

She is the founder and editor-in-chief of The WestSide Story Contest for short fiction and the founder of Youth of the Bay, a free playwriting contest for young writers between 14 and 21.

In 2009, she was the Youth Programs Director of the SF Theater Festival @ Yerba Buena.

She is certified as a Localization Professional by CA University of Chico.
