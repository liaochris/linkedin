Robert Harvey’s Summary

I have been successful as a CEO and COO of venture-backed companies and in senior executive roles for Pratt & Whitney and GM. I am looking for a CEO/President/COO role for an industrial products company, or a division of a larger company that is ideally international in scope, or a hands-on Operating Partner role for a private equity or turnaround firm that specializes in manufacturing companies.

Experienced Executive with a proven track record of building and restructuring businesses in a variety of roles and industries. Action-oriented change agent that takes a hands-on approach to lead the implementation of a successful business plan. Exceptional ability to quickly and accurately assess products, industries, organizations and key success criteria. Skilled at turning around businesses, driving lean manufacturing, overseeing new product development, corporate finance, M&A, developing and strengthening customer relationships, and assembling and motivating a high-performance leadership team.

  • CEO

    Covega Corporation

    (Privately Held; Telecommunications industry)

    2000 — 2003 (3 years )

    COVEGA Corporation is a successful fiber optics components and modules company created by a merger between CODEON and Quantum Photonics.   • Senior Vice President, Module Centers and Operations

    (Public Company; UTX; Aviation & Aerospace industry)

    1998 — 2000 (2 years )

    Responsible for Production Operations, Current Engineering, Procurement and Quality. Total budget of $2.8 billion with over 12,000 employees. Developed strategy for, and led, progressive integration of Current Engineering and Manufacturing organizations (called Module Centers) to improve organizational effectiveness between engineering and manufacturing in achieving speed to market, product performance and manufacturing cost reduction. Drove restructuring of manufacturing operations, plant closings and significant international sourcing of components and assemblies. Established a comprehensive balanced scorecard business planning and metrics-based reporting process and employed a continuous improvement, lean-manufacturing operating system which yielded a 39% increase in manufacturing productivity, decreased inventory by 28% while improving inventory turnover by 34% and drove on-time spare parts delivery to its highest level at over 95%.

Employed competitive benchmarking process and lean-manufacturing experts that lead Kaizen-based continuous improvement activities. This system drove results that culminated in recognition by the 1997 Harbour Report Europe as the most productive powertrain plants in Europe and North America. More specifically, achieved the two highest rated engine plants (and three of the top four) and the two most productive transmission plants (and four of the top five) in Europe. Responsible for multiple successful new product launches and four new lean production lines that employed team-concept work structures.
