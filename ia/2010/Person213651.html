Wanda Alexis Alexander’s Summary

• In 1995, after purchasing majority interest in Horizon Consulting, Wanda Alexander saw opportunity: pursue contracts in 57 cities where HUD had offices, focusing on its single-family FHA loan business. The strategy paid off. By 2002, Horizon had grown to earn the ranking of #312 on the Inc.500 list.
• Horizon's foundational principles of honesty, integrity, hard work and professionalism are never compromised and are at the core of the company's continuing success. In 2008, Horizon posted its best net revenue year ever and then beat that record in 2009.
• The goal is to continue to grow Horizon Consulting and becoming a Trusted Adviser and supporter to not only FHA, but to the President, his cabinet, and other government agencies as the country recovers from its worst economic depression since 1929.
• Ms. Alexander was recognized as 2005 Outstanding Woman of Loudoun County in Business and Career Development, SBA’s Woman in Business Champion of the year in 2007 and a 2009 Minority Business Leader for the Washington Business Journal. She received the 2009 BRAVA Women Business Achievement Award and was selected to join the 2009 Smart100 for leadership, strategic vision and character. In 2010, recognized as an Enterprising Woman of the Year and also selected at the League of Women Voters Women of the Year in Loudoun County.

Wanda Alexis Alexander’s Specialties:

While supporting FHA MF & SF business throughout career, Alexander has been active over 30 years creating scholarships 4 minority women, teaching investment training 2 youth, providing direction/leadership 2 teens & mentoring young ladies thru & after college. Serves as mentor 2 women business owners; volunteer guest lecturer UMCP on business ethics, serves on Advisory Boards of BB&T bank and BUILD, which transforms at risk high school youth into college students & future business owners.

Wanda Alexis Alexander’s Experience

  • Principal, President and CEO

    Horizon Consulting Incorporated

    (Privately Held; Management Consulting industry)

    September 1995 — Present (15 years 1 month)

    Wanda Alexander purchased a majority stake in Horizon on September 1, 1995, assuming positions of President& CEO. Her insight to pursue contracts in cities where HUD had offices resulted in growth that earned Horizon the #312 spot on the Inc.500 List of Fastest-Growing Private Companies in America. Wanda has over 30 years of experience in asset management, portfolio supervision, and debt re-structuring strategies for financially distressed multifamily real estate.
    Before joining Horizon, Wanda spent 15 years in Asset Management, ensuring compliance with all regulatory requirements of HUD’s insurance programs, addressing owner & management issues including Fair Housing, equity skimming, unauthorized payments, insurance, and real estate tax issues. She has designed & directed training programs on numerous housing-related issues.
    Wanda received her Bachelor’s Degree in General Studies from the University of Maryland, College Park. She also completed graduate studies in Family and Community Development at the University of Maryland and has earned certifications from The Institute of Real Estate Management and The National Center for Housing Management.
    At Horizon, Wanda leads her team in adhering to Horizon’s foundational principles of honesty, integrity, hard work and professionalism, while guiding development of all of Horizon’s processes, procedures and business practices, focusing on quality control and excellence in execution. She motivates and leads the team to ensure the corporate culture is not compromised while designing and executing corporate strategy, ensuring the company remains true to its vision and stays fiscally sound. Through the years, Wanda has received many awards, including recognition from the Washington Business Journal as 2009 Minority Business Leader and 2009 BRAVA Women Business Achievement Award. In 2010, recognized as an Enterprising Woman of the Year and also selected as the League of Women Voters Women of the Year in Loudoun County.
