Martin A. Hoffman, Sr.’s Summary

Mr. Hoffman is a strong proponent of the computerization of educational and administrative systems. At both the Fels and the Andrus Planetaria he was instrumental in the installation and documentation of computer control systems. At Pace University he was a driving force behind the automation of Identification Card record systems, computerized inventories and computer compilation of Departmental records, statistics, online media catalogs and equipment ordering. He was a leader in the introduction of Internet activities in the classroom during the 1990's and championed the use of audio and video conferencing for educational purposes. At Burlington County College he is responsible for training faculty in the use of Internet technologies for content delivery, and for many other aspects of educational technology. Most recently he has taken on responsibility for BCC's Office of Distance Education and is the Director of eLearning at the College.
