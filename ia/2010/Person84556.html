Ilse Marshall’s Summary

I am a widely experienced, intelligent and energetic digital professional, with ten years experience working on both shopping and government websites.

Right now I'm on a short term contract as Web Delivery manager/project manager at the NHS Institute for Innovation and Improvement, based at Warwick University. I'm open to contract roles as senior project manager, Business Analyst in the digital industry, or in leading a project management department or team.

I spent two years as the site traffic analyst and usability expert for Amazon.com, before moving on to be Project Manager for Amazon in the European team. More recently, I have spent four years working for the digital agency Worth, on a wide range of websites and digital promotions.

From October '08 to May '09, I worked full-time project-managing the set-up of a complete new e-commerce website for our client, Lucozade. The new shop aims for £1 million in sales by the end of its first year.

Before starting my career in the internet, I worked developing market research programmes for a company specialising in research for B2C clients. Previous to that, I was for some years an academic lecturer at Stirling University Institute of Retail Studies, researching consumer psychology and teaching marketing, statistics and business.

I love the internet industry, I get invigorated by the speed of change and the constant new demands. There's a big kick from the way you can produce immediate results from tweaking a website to improve conversions, or from putting out a good email. I enjoy using my skills to solve people's problems, and to make all sorts of things work better.

I am studying for the final part of PRINCE2 project management qualification. I'm highly proficient in: Excel, Powerpoint, Microsoft Project, wireframing with Visio and Axure; online survey tools, online project management tools such as Basecamp and Unfuddle.

Ilse Marshall’s Specialties:

Agency experience of high-powered clients; practical and imaginative marketing expertise, problem-solving and project-managing, E-commerce pattern design; email marketing; usability research expertise; designing customer research programmes.
