Zeev Klein’s Summary

Zeev Klein is part of the founding team of Landmark Ventures and responsible for business development, investment banking and venture capital investments working with a portfolio of early-stage technology companies. Zeev gained experience working in the VC & Strategic Investment division of Chase Manhattan Bank (now JPMorgan), where he helped analyze and evaluate emerging technology companies. He was the co-founder of a successful Chicago-based technology services company during the late 1990's, and has since advised dozens of enterprise software, network infrastructure and digital media firms on their strategic development, operational and financial growth strategies. Zeev founded and is actively involved with Landmark's corporate philanthropy program, Doing IT for Good, that provides financial and operational grants to deserving technology-related nonprofit organizations such as Year Up, NPower and Digital Wish. He graduated with a BA in Economics from Northwestern University and received his MBA from NYU Stern School of Business.

Zeev Klein’s Experience

  • Board Member

    Digital Wish

    (Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    March 2010 — Present (3 months)

    Digital Wish is a non-profit with a unique mission to modernize K-12 classrooms and prepare students for tomorrow’s workforce. Just like a gift registry, teachers build a wish list of technology products for their classroom. Donors then browse the wish lists and fulfill requests in whole or in part – thus improving the access and distribution of modern technology directly into the classroom. Digital Wish additionally works with many institutions to set up technology grant programs for local schools, and then matches every donation with an additional 2%-10% in funding.

    In a recent survey, 75% of educators classified themselves as "often in need" or "desperate" for technology resources. Digital Wish focuses on closing the gap, creating equity in our school systems so that each child has equal access to technology.

    In the 2007 Trends in International Mathematics and Science Study, nearly half of eighth graders scored at the advanced level in math in Taiwan, Korea and Singapore, compared with 6 percent of American students. Digital Wish provides a unique solution to remain competitive in global markets, and continue the spirit of productivity and innovation that has long characterized the United States.
