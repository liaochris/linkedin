Joel Manfredo, MBA, MS’s Summary

Mr. Manfredo is a seasoned well-rounded executive offering leadership and vision based on over thirty years of experience working for major corporations in a broad spectrum of corporate functions. He has a proven record of success serving in senior level management roles in the areas of information technology, marketing and business development, operations, sales, development and construction, real estate, and finance. Joel is known for the three Ts: Transformation, Turnaround and Technology.

His most recent endeavors were focused on transformational information technology assignments where he’s been described as a “technology visionary”; he is a sought-after speaker for technical and industry conferences and has been asked to be a source of best practice research by Gartner on such topics as leading enterprise change, risk and strengthening the core of IT.

A testament to his zeal in prosecuting his duties, in 2007, Mr. Manfredo was named as one of the top 100 CIOs in the U.S. by eWeek magazine for green technology and also received the Digital Impact Award for lifetime technology achievement in Real Estate. Mr. Manfredo was the Chairman of the CIO Roundtable sponsored by the University of California at Irvine's CRITO Consortium (Center for Research on Information Technology and Organizations) and The Paul Merage School of Business. He was also a Board member of Southern California’s chapter of the Society of Information Management and was the Chairman of the Advisory Council of Realcomm, an organization focused on the intersection of technology and real estate. On numerous occasions, Mr. Manfredo has been asked to speak in industry events on a broad range of topics including IT Strategic Planning, Enterprise Architecture, IT Governance, Portfolio Management, Web 2.0, IT Strategic Sourcing, "Green IT", Chargeback, Information Technology Infrastructure Library (ITIL) and IT Controls.

Joel Manfredo, MBA, MS’s Experience

  • Chief Technology Officer

    County of Orange

    (Government Agency; Government Administration industry)

    October 2008 — Present (1 year 9 months)

    Mr. Manfredo is serving the County of Orange where he is responsible for Business Information Services (applications, data, quality assurance) and Enterprise Information Services (security, business continuity, networks, telephony and IT operations) for the County of Orange. Includes staff of over 200 serving 18,000 County employees and 30 agencies. Responsible for transformation activites as the County moves from having its core applications on a mainframe to open systems. Results of a reorganization along ITSM functions, include eliminating Service Level breeches, four-fold reduction in incident resolution time, and formalizing monthly capacity and availability planning while resuding staff by over 20%. Also responsible for driving Portfolio Management maturity including detailed resource planning and prioritization.

  • SVP, IT and CIO

    The Irvine Company

    (Privately Held; 1001-5000 employees; Real Estate industry)

    March 2004 — August 2007 (3 years 6 months)

    Mr. Manfredo served as SVP, Information Technology for The Irvine Company. He was recruited to transform the IT function, both in scale and professionalism. He enjoyed extraordinary success in executing an orderly transformation of the IT group into a customer driven, team-oriented culture. He was responsible for all aspects of Information Technology including strategy, architecture, program and project management, business applications, infrastructure, ERP, change management, contracts and financial administration. Sample accomplishments: doubled staff and quadrupled output in the IT department; instituted a world-class Program Management Office (PMO); drove the first cross business unit process focus with SAP; maintained 92% employee satisfaction; achieved technical reliance on system generated financial statements; created publically acclaimed IT Architectural vision documents, created the Company's IT Strategic Plan; and conceived and implemented effective IT Governance

  • Managing Director & CTO

    Carramerica Realty Corp

    (Public Company; 501-1000 employees; Real Estate industry)

    2000 — 2003 (3 years )

    From 2000 through 2003, Mr. Manfredo served as Managing Director, e.Business Solutions and Chief Technology Officer for CarrAmerica Realty Corporation, a major office Real Estate Investment Trust with revenues in excess of $500 million. In this capacity, he was responsible for all IT projects, operations, development and support and was the Chairman of the Technology Investment Committee of the Board of Directors and of the Company’s Technology Steering Committee. He has negotiated major multimillion dollar outsourcing arrangements including the Company’s ERP system and Managed Desktop Services. He has a reputation for being a thought leader in the industry. Mr. Manfredo is credited with transforming all aspects of the Company’s IT department including infrastructure, management and support into a leader in the industry.

  • VP & Director, Information Strategies

    The Rouse Company

    (Real Estate industry)

    1988 — 2000 (12 years )

    From 1995 to 2000, Mr. Manfredo has served as VP and Director of Information Strategies with The Rouse Company. He was chosen for this key position by the Chief Executive Officer of the company after rapidly rising through the ranks in diverse roles. As the IT leader for the Rouse Company during this time, Mr. Manfredo led a $43 million mission critical transformation of the organization’s business processes that drove business expansion. The Rouse Company was considered the most technologically advanced organization in the industry. He acted as the catalyst and change agent for the organization in creating vision, analyzing feasibility, designing process, and executing a fundamental shift in the company’s business practices... Prior to this, he executed a 210% turnaround while running a large business, including green technology initiatives that resulted in annual utility cost savings of $4.0 million and received $3.0 million in utility company rebates.

He was promoted to this position after achieving considerable success as Financial Planner and Strategic Planning Coordinator. He also held pivotal roles in several positions for one of the company’s start-up subsidiaries.
