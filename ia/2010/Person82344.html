Hanna Kutcher’s Summary

Hanna Kutcher is a user experience consultant and systematic strategist with 12 years experience in interface design, web development, usability, information architecture, and accessibility. She has held positions ranging the full spectrum of the IT lifecycle, including designer, developer, architect, analyst, researcher, and manager. Hanna has intimate knowledge of the automotive, publishing, and medical research industries, as well as hands-on experience as an online merchant and real estate investor.

Hanna’s portfolio includes enterprise-level projects for GM, Chrysler, Ford, Elsevier, O’Reilly, Pearson, ProQuest, Deloitte, and other clients. She holds a Masters degree in Information Science (HCI) from the School of Information at the University of Michigan and a Bachelor of Arts in Art with a Business minor from Siena Heights University. She is a member of Mensa and a former board member of the Toledo Real Estate Investors Association.

Skills:

Adobe CS3, assistive technologies, Axure, CSS, DOM, HTML, IA, IxD, JavaScript, Joomla, Office, UX, TechSmith, Visio

User analysis, requirements gathering – observation, contextual interviews, surveys, card sorting, focus groups

Usability/user testing – participant screening and recruiting, session material preparation, recording and broadcasting, quotes and video clips, remote user testing and web conferencing

Research analysis, concept generation – scenarios, personas, detailed reports, presentations, functional requirements

Rapid prototyping, information architecture – wireframes, high-fidelity prototypes, design conceptualization, mockups

Accessibility compliance assessment – Section 508, WCAG 2.0, National Federation of the Blind (NFB), W3C validation, assistive technology (screen readers, magnifiers, and emulators like JAWS, Fangs, MAGic, Window-Eyes)

Hanna Kutcher’s Specialties:
