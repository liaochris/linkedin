  • Senior Program Manager Product Release and Security Services

    Microsoft

    (Public Company; MSFT; Computer Software industry)

    May 2009 — Present (1 year )

    Microsoft offers deeply discounted and free product in all channels through a variety of marketing programs emerging market, educational, volume licensing and other fulfillment. (Examples include MSPP (Microsoft Partner Program), MAPS (Microsoft Action Pack), MSDN (Microsoft Developer Network), Student Media, MSIS (Microsoft Student Innovation Suite), Software Assurance, Open Licensing, samples, launch events, promotional giveaways, and direct marketing campaigns.) Programs can have physical fulfillment, on-line / digital fulfillment, or a combination of the two.
    Pirates have been able to exploit these programs and systems to obtain free or low cost genuine product and then resell it to users at huge profit margins. This deprives Microsoft and its partners of legitimate revenue, and leaves users with improperly licensed and unsupported product. Significantly lower prices also undermine channel integrity and drive additional corruption. This piracy is referred to as “Genuine Infringing” or “leakage.” Unchecked, this can be a multi-billion problem for Microsoft. My role is to drive the program that raises awareness of this risk within the company and provide risk mitigation tools and techniques to stakeholders and constituents
