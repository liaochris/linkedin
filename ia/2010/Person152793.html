Neha Gandhi’s Summary

Hi, I am Neha - an artist, a potter and an ardent lover of crafts. My foray into arts and crafts began from my teens; I used to enjoy dabbling with paint and colour. It led me to join Fine Arts at Sir J.J School of Arts , Mumbai and then there was no turning back. I was mesmerized by the delights of pottery; and my next destination, naturally, was Golden Bridge Pottery, Pondicherry, where I studied Studio Pottery. This course intensified my love for the subject and honed my skills as well. My journey continued, working extensively with artisans in Kutch, Gujarat and Maharashtra. My own contributions and learning's from others helped me gain a world of experience and sharpen multiple skills essential for the crafts sector. Now based in Mumbai, I continue with my passion for expressing my creativity through arts and crafts.... through 'Matsya'.

  • Project Manager

    Behavioural Science Centre , Ahmedabad

    (Non-Profit; 51-200 employees; Design industry)

    April 2001 — July 2004 (3 years 4 months)

    Worked as Project Manager (Embroidery Project) with Behavioural Science Centre (BSC), a reputed NGO based in Ahmedabad working on human rights and development programmes in various districts of Gujarat with the marginalized communities, especially the Scheduled Castes, the Scheduled Tribes and the minorities
    The mandate in this project was particularly challenging and complex considering the fact that embroidery had to be developed as a livelihood option among groups of women who are dependant on agricultural labour and seasonal saltpan work as their primary source of income. Furthermore, these women had never been exposed to commercial production and its logistical compulsions. The project had to be sensitive to the extremely high workload of women and hence had to ensure that it did not enhance the burden of work on them. The activity had to be therefore designed for sustainability even with a part-time / leisure time production cycle.
