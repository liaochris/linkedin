Rachel Caplan’s Summary

I am committed to supporting independent filmmakers and increasing exhibition opportunities for their work, as well as building audiences for documentary film - particularly around environmental and sustainability issues. I have over thirteen years experience in film exhibition and distribution including work for the Edinburgh, London and San Francisco International Film Festivals, L.A. film studios (Paramount, Universal & DreamWorks), and U.K. independent film organizations. From 2007-2009, I was Festival Director for the San Francisco Ocean Film Festival, the first and largest showcase for ocean related films in North America. I recently founded the San Francisco Green Film Festival, to take place March 3-6, 2011 and create inspiring programs that link media arts with environmental action.

I have a Masters degree in Cinema & Television Studies from the British Film Institute (BFI). I have been a member of BAFTA since 2000, the Pleiades Network and TEDxSF since 2010, and have served as Board Treasurer for Bay Area Women in Film & Media since 2008.

Rachel Caplan’s Specialties:

Development and project management for film festivals and non-profit organizations. Includes: funding & sponsorship, marketing & PR, programming, acquisition & distribution, digital technology solutions, long term planning, executive management, and event operations.
