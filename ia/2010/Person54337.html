DEN Architecture’s Summary

Den is an independent architecture firm dedicated to environmentally-conscious design. Our research-based design process recognizes every decision has an impact on the environment. Consequently, we produce buildings that adapt to local climate, culture, and resources. In doing so, we have an extensive background in a variety of projects, ranging from residential remodels to institutional buildings. We are also a carbon neutral office, and as such, are committed to the prudent stewardship of our planet’s resources. Please join us. Let’s improve our environment one building at a time.

Den consists of the husband and wife team of architects Germán Brun and Lizmarie Esparza. They are part of the generation of design professionals instrumental in Miami’s renaissance as a major multi-cultural metropolis; infusing fresh ideas, modern design, and an environmental conscious into the City. Together, they have designed more than 16 million square feet of architecture worth more than 3 billion dollars, and received multiple design awards in the process. Even though the partners’ backgrounds are quite diverse, they share the common belief that good architecture is inherently green.

DEN Architecture’s Education

  • Università IUAV di Venezia

    Fellow , Post Graduate Research , 1999 — 2000

    Guided by Professor Renato Bocchi, my itinerant fellowship with the Fulbright program, took me all around Italy in search of irregular urban spaces. I carefully studied and drew dozens of piazzas all around the country, in hopes of finding the elusive secrets of these irregular spaces' urban success.

  • University of California, Berkeley

    Masters , Architecture , 1995 — 1998

    A month before I graduated from my undergraduate degree, I was accepted to UC Berkeley's College of Environmental Design with a full scholarship. While there, I had the priviledge to work with luminaires in the design community such as Chrisopher Alexander, Allan Jacobs, Stanley Saitowitz, and Richard Keating among others. I quickly developed an interest in sustainability and urbanism.

  • The Catholic University of America

    Bachelors , Architecture , 1993 — 1995

    In the great tradition of the Architect's Beaux Arts education, I received my undergraduate degree in architecture at The Catholic University of America. I learned to appreciate the history of architecture, and developed a fondness for vernacular buildings and the potential lessons they can provide.
