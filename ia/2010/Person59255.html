Madhu Dowlapalli’s Summary

* Senior Scientist / Chemical Engineer at CEHMM. Primary objective is to extract oil from algae and convert it to biodiesel. Process economics and environmental impact would be my primary concerns.

* Materials Scientist Intern at Cabot Fuel Cells. Involved in physical and electrochemical characterization of materials for fuel cell electrocatalyst supports.

* Electrocatalyst Development Intern at Cabot Corporation. Involved in developing new electrocatalyst supports for PEM fuel cells.

Gained industrial experience in R&D of materials for fuel cells. Hands-on experience in preparing MEAs for PEM fuel cells. Enhanced my technical abilities and gave me an opportunity to interact with the industrial workforce.

* Research Assistant for Center for Micro-Engineered Materials, University of New Mexico. I was involved in a project that dealt with Cabot Corporation on studying and characterizing electrochemical oxidation properties of carbon supports for PEM fuel cells.

* Research Assistant Advanced Materials Laboratory, a facility of Sandia National Laboratories in partnership with the University of New Mexico. This job served as a means to do research as a part of fulfilling the partial requirements for my Masters degree in Chemical Engineering. My thesis was titled “Microstructural Analysis of Ultra-high Temperature Ceramics for Next Generation Thermal Systems.”

Through my work at the university I have gained expertise in High Resolution X-Ray Diffractometer (HRXRD), Transmission Electron Microscopy (TEM), Scanning Electron Microscopy (SEM), Atomic Force Microscopy (AFM), Fourier transform infrared spectroscopy (FTIR), Microtrac and Nanotrac for particle size distribution analysis, and XPS for surface analysis. I have also used several sample preparation techniques such as polishing and ion milling of cross-section samples for TEM.

Madhu Dowlapalli’s Specialties:

Solvent Extraction, Transesterification, XRD, TEM, SEM, AFM, FTIR, XPS, Microtrac and Nanotrac for PSD analysis. Sample preparation techniques such as Polishing and Ion Milling of cross-section samples for TEM.

Design of Experiments, Electrochemical Analysis (eg: Cyclic Voltammetry), Fuel Cell Testing, MEA preparation, Electrocatalyst Development and Testing.

QA/QC (Biodiesel testing according to ASTM D6751 standard), Gas Chromatography, etc.
