Zhenmeng Peng’s Summary

My research interests in a broad sense include multifunctional nanomaterials, metal alloys and nanostructures, heterogeneous catalysts, electrocatlaysts, fuel cells, physical chemistry, surface chemistry, colloidal chemistry, solid-state chemistry and electrochemistry. I am interested in studying catalytic properties of metal nanoparticles and their applications in various chemical and electrochemical reactions. I aim at correlating the performance of metal-based catalysts with their electronic and surface structures. I try to explore good catalyst by optimizing these parameters through controlling their compositions and shapes, and to prepare the desired structure using colloidal synthesis.

My PhD study is focused on shape, composition and electrocatalytic properties of platinum alloy nanostructures. It is known that platinum, a precious metal with outstanding catalytic properties, is the key element of catalysts used in proton exchange membrane fuel cells (PEMFCs), the device to covert chemical energy to electricity using hydrogen and other small molecules. Activity and durability of the catalysts, however, have greatly hindered its applications and new catalysts with low cost and high performance are required. Recent studies indicate that both d-band electrons and surface geometric structures can greatly affect on the activity of the catalysts. Another challenge is to realize the potentials of high activities demonstrated for large single crystalline PtM (M=metals) surfaces. To this end, the preparation of Pt alloy nanocrystals with predetermined shapes and compositions is essential. I am interested in understanding the key principles governing the formation of platinum alloy nanocrystals and via predetermined nucleation and growth parameters in organic synthetic media, such as the absorption energy between functional groups of capping ligands and metal surfaces.

Zhenmeng Peng’s Experience

  • Research Assistant

    University of Rochester

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    September 2005 — Present (4 years 8 months)

    I am mainly focused on but not limited to exploration of good proton exchange membrane fuel cell (PEMFC) electrocatalsyt in aspects of activity, stability and cost during my PhD study at University of Rochester. My reserach projects include:

    · Design and exploration of scalable, economical and applicable protocols to prepare metal catalyst and electrocatalyst.

    · Development of low-cost and highly active platinum skin-layer catalyst using electrochemical and chemical dealloying approaches.

    · Screening good alloy catalyst and electrocatalyst by choosing proper metal combinations as well as their compositions with optimized electronic structure and desirable surface geometry.

    · Study of non-platinum alloy electrocatalysts with high activity and low cost for oxygen reduction reaction (ORR) in proton exchange membrane fuel cell (PEMFC).

    · Preparation of platinum-on-metal (metal =palladium, gold, silver, copper, etc) heteronanostructures, metal@platinum core-shell nanoparticles, and hollow platinum nanostructures with dramatic enhancement in activities as electrocatalysts in model reactions, such as oxygen reduction reaction (ORR), formic acid oxidation reaction (FAOR) and methanol oxidation reaction (MOR).

    · Shape control of platinum nanocrystals using selective organic capping agents, which tend to adsorb onto certain surfaces and inhibit growth in these directions.

    · Investigation of the composition-forbidden-in-bulk PtM (M=Ag, Au, etc) alloy nanoparticles as a new group of catalysts in electro-oxidation of small organic molecules.

    · Growth of PtAg alloy worm-like nanowires and mechanism study of their compositional dependency.

    · Phase behavior study of PtM (M=Ag, Au, etc) alloy nanoparticles with compositions in their miscibility gap, in which region alloys in bulk form are forbidden.

    · Synthesis of uniform Ag nanoparticles and study of their assembling into superlattices.
