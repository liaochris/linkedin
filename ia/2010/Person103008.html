John Lawlor’s Summary

As Vice President, Investor Relations, John is the DragonWave executive responsible for assisting the global investment community fully understand the company. John’s business experience prior to joining DragonWave is extensive, including positions in investor relations, research and development, sales, marketing, customer service, and corporate communications.

Prior to DragonWave, John was Vice President, Corporate Relations at Cognos Inc., the world's largest independent business intelligence software company, where he was the lead executive responsible for a number of Cognos’ key stakeholders, including governments, industry associations, financial analysts and shareholders.

Prior to Cognos, John served as Vice President, Corporate Communications for Newbridge Networks Corporation, a world leader in high-capacity data network equipment. Prior to Newbridge, John was Vice President, Corporate Relations for CAE Inc., the global leader in advanced simulation, training and control systems.

Prior to joining CAE, John spent 19 years with Nortel Networks, a leading networking equipment supplier at the time, where he held progressively more senior management positions in R&D, product management, marketing, sales, and customer service in Canada and the United States.

John has received numerous Investor Relations magazine nominations and awards every year since their institution in the 1990s, including Best Overall Investor Relations, Best Investor Relations Officer, Best Investor Relations in the U.S., Best Investor Relations Website, and Best Investor Relations by Senior Management.

John has bachelor and master of engineering degrees in mechanical and materials engineering, respectively, and completed the master of management science and business administration at the University of Ottawa and McGill University.

John Lawlor’s Specialties:

Seasoned business executive with broad experience in a range of disciplines and fields, including capital markets, research and development, sales, marketing, corporate brand and reputation management, customer service, corporate communications, government and investor relations; and a range of industries, including telecommunications, data networking, and information management software.
