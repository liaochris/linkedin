John Watkins - Litigation and Business Lawyer’s Summary

My litigation practice concentrates on disputes involving trade secrets and unfair competition (including confidential information, false advertising and theft of electronic information), insurance coverage (including bad faith), corporations and LLCs (including shareholder and member disputes), commercial contracts and product liability.

I also negotiate and draft sales agreements, NDAs, terms and conditions and other contracts. I use my litigation experience to try to help clients avoid litigation.

I have considerable experience working with international companies, including German, Irish, Swedish, Finnish, Candadian and Swiss companies or their U.S. subsidiaries. I have given seminars sponsored by the IHK in Munich and the German American Chamber of Commerce (GACC) in Atlanta. I am active in the GACC and the British American Business Group and the international business community.

I have worked with clients in many business and industries, but have particular experience with manufacturing, distribution, machinery and equipment, insurance, mining, and natural gas distribution. More specific experience includes engineered wood products, metalforming, formed products, pulp and paper, and motor vehicles.

I was named to the Georgia Super Lawyers list in Business Litigation in 2008 and 2009; AV rated by Martindale-Hubbell Law Directory (its highest rating); rated 10.0 by AVVO.com, its highest rating.

My book, An Insider's Guide on Hiring a Business Attorney (Ocelot Atlanta 2010), explains how business owners and executives can find, evaluate, interview, and work with a business attorney.

I graduated first in my class from the University of Georgia School of Law, was on law review, a member of Order of the Coif, and won other awards.

John Watkins - Litigation and Business Lawyer’s Specialties:

Business litigation: trade secrets and unfair competition (including trade dress, false advertising, misappropriation of computer information), insurance coverage (professional liability, CGL, and excess claims), insurance bad faith, corporate, LLC and shareholder disputes, commercial contracts.

Business law: Negotiating and drafting sales supply contacts, terms and conditions, non-disclosure agreements, and dispute resolution provisions.
