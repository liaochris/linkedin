Josh Tynjala’s Summary

With over five years experience developing products targeting Flash Player, Josh Tynjala offers a wealth of knowledge gained from working with many technologies in Adobe's Flash Platform. His experience includes the development of customer-facing Rich Internet Applications, designing and developing casual games, integration of Flash Platform technologies with native OS applications, the development of two separate charting/graphing frameworks, along with the design and development of many other reusable UI controls and data visualization components for Flash and Flex.

In early 2009, Josh founded Bowler Hat Games to pursue an interest in monetizing casual games for the web and on mobile devices. His game Chroma Circuit was one of the first native iPhone applications created with Adobe Flash to appear in the App Store, and it was featured by Apple's staff under the "What We're Playing" section of the Games category.

Josh may be available for short-term (one to three months) contract work building or prototyping rich applications with Flash and Flex or developing simple casual web games. He is not currently interested in full-time positions.
