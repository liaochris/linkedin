Rohan Irvin’s Summary

Since graduating at the top of my class in Petroleum Engineering from the University of Western Australia I have been working in the Upstream E&P industry for Woodside Energy Ltd in Australia. During this time I have travelled to many interesting countries in Asia, Europe and the Middle East and have a strong desire to work in countries that will challenge me socially as well as technically.

While working on the Enfield offshore oil field for Woodside I wrote and implemented the Reservoir Management Plan ensuring the appropriate data was collected to monitor waterflood performance. This included the interpretation of 4D seismic, tracers and pressure transient analysis among others. I prepared the year end reserves update incorporating the learning's from the first year of production and provided regular production forecasts. While providing input into the second phase of development drilling I worked closely with the Drilling and Completions Engineers as well as Facilities Engineers. In supervising the Enfield EOR screening study I developed a strong understanding of tertiary recovery mechanisms which built on my solid knowledge of secondary recovery.

I was responsible for planning and implementing a work over campaign in the North Rankin field during 2005 which developed my skills in identifying opportunities and optimising production. I was also fortunate to be given responsibility for supervising a “virtual team” of subsurface professionals for a 3 well, offshore, infill campaign for the Perseus field. This taught me valuable planning and communication skills needed to deliver results from a multidiscipline team.
