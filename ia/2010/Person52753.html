Daniel Biesiada’s Summary

My past years shown that I've chosen a work where I have to be technologically excellent at any requested moment. It's a worth lesson that no technology and platform is forever and as I always say - I have to learn all the time or I can go selling hot-dogs on the streets. That's obvious to me and I follow my times, I think quite accurately.

What I've learnt at Microsoft and Evangelism team is direct customer facing with all that knowledge and experiences I described above.

If you asked me what my role means, I can simply answer 1/3 technical consultant and advisor, 1/3 channel manager, 1/3 pre-sales consultant.

What I've really learnt is that evangelism is not only about talking fancy things about technology.

In my rather depth context, It's been mainly about building relationships with the audience, making technological decisions together, managing projects and budgets and connecting right people with right resources to map my partners goals meantime achieving my own ultimate goals.

Technology is just a tool and one of many arguments in conversation.

Evangelism is really interesting for another reason. It teaches better to predict the future technological changes after past and current experiences, trends and impact current platforms and solutions make on the market. That's fantastic feeling when you at some very moment say "Didn't I tell you about that 2 years ago? Told ya that it's most possible option in this area".
