Cliff Clive’s Summary

CEO/GM of consumer businesses for 10 years with Breville ($140M -- kitchen appliances), Nestle/PowerBar ($210M -- food), and Roche ($545M -- OTC pharma), Called a “Profit Lion” for igniting superior profit growth (4X industry averages over last 20 years in 20+ product categories on 4 continents). World-class consumer-goods marketing background: Procter & Gamble (13 years), Kellogg MBA.
 Growth Driver: Spearheads big product innovation. Inspires brilliant consumer marketing. Rigorously ROI-focused.
 Change Agent: Sharpens strategy. Upgrades structure, processes and staff. Uses LEAN concepts to drive cost cuts.
 Deal Maker: Initiates and manages joint ventures, licensing deals, divestitures, and integrations.

Cliff Clive’s Experience

  • CEO

    Breville Group / HWI, North America

    (Consumer Goods industry)

    2007 — January 2009 (2 years )

    Managed the Breville line of luxury kitchen appliances and the smaller, declining housewares division. North American revenues for this Australian company totaled $140M with 165 employees across all functions (manufacturing was sourced in China & India). Hired to: 1) introduce world-class marketing for Breville, 2) turnaround and divest the housewares division, and 3) streamline company procedures then cut staffing. Paul Hill, the Global CEO from 2007-08 said: “Cliff was a ‘Profit Lion’, building our business both through world-class marketing … and bold cost re-structuring.”
     “Rock-Star” Revenue Growth: Led the Breville division to +18% revenue growth at retail in 2008 (and double-digit profit growth) while the industry declined 9%. See the case study with videos and web links at www.CliffClive.com:
    o Launched world-class TV advertising campaign which beat industry effectiveness benchmarks by over 100%
    o Sparked 90% growth in PR-generated publicity (Oprah, Ellen, Today Show, The View, plus opinion-leader blogs)
    o Web 2.0: added celebrity video “webinars” & new video functionality to own website, then embedded this in key retailers’ sites (Amazon.com, Williams-Sonoma, Bed Bath & Beyond).
    o Built record displays & features at all key “brick & mortar” retailers behind Sales’ “Big Push” campaign.
     Housewares Division Turnaround: Achieved sales stability in 2008 (+10% vs. industry, +25% vs. prior-year trend).
    o Signed new business partnerships with growing & profitable premium brands (Scanpan and Laura Ashley).
    o Cut 700 of 1100 SKU’s (60% of past revenues) due to bad margins/outlook, then built the remaining SKUs’ revenues +27% while fattening margins +15 points (+80%) by renegotiating Chinese costs and raising prices.
     Cost Restructuring: Cut office staff -25% (20 people) and outsourced US supply chain (44 people). Sold off inventories of discontinued SKU’s to free 40% of the warehouse, then negotiated sub-letting the space.

  • President of the Americas for Consumer Health Division

    Roche Pharmaceuticals

    (Public Company; rhhby; Pharmaceuticals industry)

    February 2002 — December 2004 (2 years 11 months)

    Supervised Roche’s non-prescription drug business in the Americas ($545M revenues; 220 direct subordinates). This comprised a USA joint venture with Bayer ($315M) and organizations in eight Latin countries ($230M). Was also a key member of the Global Leadership Team, overseeing all major strategic initiatives of Roche’s $1.3B OTC division.
     Latin Growth Records: Became the #3 OTC company in Latin America by building revenues/profits at 2X/4X regional norm (+7%/19% per year vs. 3%/4% industry average). Keys:
    o Built the Flanax brand +40% – Re-applied the USA Aleve marketing model (see video at www.CliffClive.com) plus new strategies for pricing, distribution, and gaining regulatory approval for a higher-dose line extension.
    o Implemented rigorous ROI metrics to the medical detailing of 22K doctors. Made constant profit optimizations.
    o Saved $2M per year and improved customer service by cutting 35% of SKUs (2% of sales).
     Global Strategic Leader: Led major initiatives as a member of the Global Leadership Team, including:
    o Strengthened Global Consumer Segmentation – The successful segmentation method pioneered with Aleve in the USA became the model for Roche’s 4 largest product categories globally (analgesics, vitamins, skin care, gastro-intestinal). Resulted in strengthened TV advertising, doctor detailing, and product innovation globally.
    o Upgraded Global Product Development Organization to increase the net present value (NPV) of the new product pipeline by an estimated $24M (+80%).
    o Led Divestiture of Roche Consumer Health – Attempted to execute a management buy-out. Co-led road shows and due diligence efforts with Private Equity firms (KKR, Blackstone, Carlyle) and industry buyers. Sold to competitor Bayer for a strong €2.4B. As with most of Roche’s Leadership Team, did not join Bayer.
