Herman Lorenz’s Summary

I have a corporate finance and operational management background from NYSE to start-ups. I have a history of working with underperforming operations; in every case I've been able to get them to a profit and grow. Turnaround and growth! Pull priorities from problems.

I run. I have a 31 year streak (July 4, 2009) running the Atlanta Peachtree Road Race 10K, including once 9 weeks after knee surgery, and last year 8 weeks after rotator cuff surgery. I know that makes me either obsessive or compulsive; but I can’t remember which one it is, so I don’t worry about it.

I like to travel and take photos of places I've been. You can see a few at the Facebook, Flickr and Webshots links below. Most are standard "been there; done that" pictures (I like sunsets). I kind of like the group of "you might be a redneck if you go to Australia and take photos like this".


Some experiences that illustrate me and what I do:

•DEAL WITH REALITY; YOU CAN'T JUST OPERATE ON A PLAN! - I am the only business manager in the United States who lost two major customers because of two bombs in two different cities, planted by the same bomber. I grew revenue 10% year-to-year that year. That was my worst performance.

•LEADERSHIP IS NOT ABOUT YOU; IT'S HOW YOU FEEL ABOUT OTHER PEOPLE! - For years I was a volunteer managing the course of the Atlanta Marathon. That involved creating a great opportunity for 300 people to volunteer to stand on a street corner for up to four hours on Thanksgiving morning -- and making it fun for them. And by the way, it might be raining or freezing.

•DO WHAT EXCITES YOU! - When I graduated, I looked for an opportunity in an underperformer where I could do some exciting things. I accepted a position as the first management consultant in the Atlanta office of the Ernst part of Ernst & Young. When I left we had ten people.
