Michael Doukas’s Summary

Attorney Michael Doukas can be retained to help in a wide range of legal matters, including negotiation and litigation. Attorney Michael Doukas is on the highly-regarded lawyer referral panels of the North County Bar Association, the San Diego County Bar Association, and the JAG's Naval Legal Services Office SW.

The Law Office of Michael Doukas is a consumer-oriented law firm that litigates in California and federal court against debt collectors, creditors, or the like, who victimize consumers. Attorney Doukas defends consumers sued for money, and prosecutes those who would harass, abuse, or unfairly treat consumers.

Attorney Michael Doukas enjoys fighting his client's battles against all comers--major banks, attorneys, debt collectors, major corporations. It doesn't matter who is against him.

Mr. Doukas is especially honored to have defended and prosecuted cases for many enlisted servicemembers and officers, including Marines of all enlisted ranks and Navy sailors and their families. He has done pro bono work to support our troops. Too often our troops are easy targets for debt collectors and creditors who wave the flag with one hand, but exploit our servicemembers with the other hand. The Superior Court has appointed Mr. Doukas to represent absent servicemembers under the Servicemembers Civil Relief Act to determine eligibility for a stay of a pending case.

Attorney Mike Doukas is on the highly-regarded lawyer referral panels of the North County Bar Association, the San Diego County Bar Association, and the JAG's Naval Legal Services Office SW. Mr. Doukas has provided on-going support to Navy JAG in training young JAG attorneys in consumer law and consulting on their cases on a pro bono basis.

Representation in California and Federal Courts in San Diego County only.

Michael Doukas’s Specialties:

Legal counsel, negotiation, litigation services, defense when sued by a collection agency or creditor for money, wrongful repossessions, debt negotiation to avoid litigation or bankruptcy, debt collection abuse and harassment relief under the federal Fair Debt Collection Practices Act (FDCPA), credit reporting problems, identity theft counsel, resolving liability issues with creditors and debt collectors, unfair and deceptive acts and practices, and Truth in Lending disclosure problems.
