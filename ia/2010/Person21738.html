    John Bollinger has written a piece on “Combining Technical and Fundamental Analysis;" therein he explains that technical, fundamental, quantitative and behavioral analysis must be combined for solid rational analysis. I believe that I have arrived at that point; I am now ready to seek new opportunities in the portfolio management space. There is a time to be strategic and a time to be tactical.

    Call me to find out how I blend my analysis; your firm’s clients CAN avoid meltdowns like 2001-02 and 2008.

  • Financial Advisor | Investment Manager | Assistant VP

    Merrill Lynch

    (Public Company; BAC; Financial Services industry)

    May 1987 — May 1999 (12 years 1 month)

    It was in 1994 when my passion for technical market analysis began. By then I had built a decent book, but with mostly fixed income; and in 1994, I was on the wrong side of a huge bond bear market. Martin Pring’s book, Technical Analysis Explained, 3ed was recommended to me. It was there that I found a momentum based model to use in buying & selling Treasuries; I reverse engineered the formula (not shown in book), duplicated the exact graphics (wow!), and by 1997 I was ready. A buy signal in the model came in late 1997, and by the end of 1998 a huge bond rally had occurred. I became the “Merrill Lynch bond king” of the local office. Actually, Long Term Capital Management made an arbitrage bet going short US Treasuries and nearly collapsed the financial system. I was on the opposite side of their trade! Awesome! Reading price on a chart is very important.

    • Implemented 3rd party portfolio management via ML Consults

    • Developed MS Excel based model to trade/invest in US Treasury securities. Used this with high success during the 1998 bond market rally (as LTCM collapsed)

    • Trained and taught a team of administrative assistants and interns to leverage delivery of superior client service.
