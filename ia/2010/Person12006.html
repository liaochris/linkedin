Andrew Martineau’s Summary

Andrew began his advertising career at the early age of 17 in Trinidad working at a division of Saatchi & Saatchi until he moved to Miami to attend the Miami International University of Art (formerly IFAC). Upon graduation Andrew began work at Taglairino Advertising Group (TAG) as an Art Director where he worked on two of the agency’s largest clients, which included the complete rebranding of Gulfstream Racetrack and Turnberry Isle Resort and Spa.

Upon leaving TAG, Andrew was hired as the Art Director at Cohen Company Creative where he was responsible for the creation of ad campaigns for the agency’s clients which included; Champion Lyte Energy Drink, GC Homes, and Transeastern Homes. Andrew quickly saw a need to broaden his experiences and was recruited by a division of JWT (J. Walter Thompson) as Senior Art Director. While in this position he designed and developed the agency’s new corporate identity and website, while creating numerous new marketing and public relations initiatives for the agency and its clients. His clients at JWT included; AARP, AOL, Oticon, Bose, MOAA, Galderma Pharmaceuticals, and ACTS Retirement Communities among others.

He has also owned and operated his own an award-winning, full service creative boutique and consultancy developed under a new agency model that utilizes its relationships with various creative entities to deliver progressive creative that benefits the client by ways of notoriety, innovation and profit.

His creative expertise has garnered him numerous feature articles in local and national publications, multiple Addy, Addy Best of Show, Mature Market Media, Florida Direct Marketing, NAHB and Prism awards. In fact, he has won an Addy award at some level every year during the last decade.

He is also the past president of Ad 2 Miami, past president and founder of Ad 2 Fort Lauderdale, past president of the Fort Lauderdale Advertising Federation, past National Ad 2 Fund raising and Public Service chair.
