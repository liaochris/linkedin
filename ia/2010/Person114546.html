Kenn Amdahl’s Summary

I’ve been self publishing books for 18 years via my company Clearwater Publishing Company and have been active in the Colorado book community for most of that time. I've served on the board of a dozen or so book-related nonprofits, and have volunteered with others. Currently on the boards of Book Organizations of Colorado, www.coloradobook.org (which helps connect authors, publishers, librarians and bookstores) and Colorado Independent Publishers Association (CIPA), the largest state-based association of small publishers in the country.

One of my self published titles (There Are No Electrons: Electronics for Earthlings) is approaching 100,000 copies sold. It’s often credited with inspiring all the books for Dummies and Idiots. It may be the only electronics book with blurbs by Dave Barry, Ray Bradbury, George Garrett and Clive Cussler. It’s modeled into all large Barnes and Noble stores and continues to sell well, seventeen years after I released it.

My other books include Algebra Unplugged, which I co-authored with Jim Loats, Ph.D , one of only four algebra books recommended by Encyclopedia Britannica Online. Calculus for Cats, (also with Jim), The Land of Debris and the Home of Alfredo, the first novel ever printed on tree-free paper made from the kenaf plant. and Joy Writing: Discover and Develop Your Creative Voice which has blurbs from literati George Garrett and Reg Saner, as well as Edward Ormondroyd, author of the classic kid’s book David and the Phoenix.

Most of my books are quirky and funny, despite my best efforts. Most have been reviewed dozens of times in national publications.

Obviously, I'm interested in writing, publishing, music, education, science and real estate.
