Deborah Bassett’s Summary

A strong passion for environmental activism with a focus upon ocean and rainforest conservation, Deborah has worked both in the field and behind the scenes with such organizations as The Sea Shepherd Conservation Society, Amazon Watch, Global Green USA, Rainforest2Reef, Surfers for Cetaceans, The Tsunami Relief Fund, Habitat for Humanity, The 11th Hour Action Committee, Orangutan Outreach, US Doctors for Africa, The Heart of Yoga Association, Channel G, and The Oceanic Preservation Society. Combining extensive world travel and international studies with photojournalism and documentary film, Deborah remains dedicated to raising awareness and funding for some of the world’s most crucial social, health, and environmental issues. To date her work has been featured in Surfer Magazine, FreeSurf, NEED, Yoga Journal, Yogi Times, Peace Magazine, Living Nutrition, The Progressive, Transition's Abroad, Patagonia’s Wild and Scenic Film Festival and throughout numerous websites and on-line publications. She has also participated in several global conferences including The Clinton Global Initiative, TED, The Esalen Institute annual yoga retreat, and The 2009 meeting of The International Whaling Commission. A graduate of The Department of Sociology and Anthropology at Concordia University in Montreal, Canada, Deborah is fluent in French and Spanish. Her professional background and life experience lend a deep appreciation for the protection of critically endangered species and habitat as well as the preservation of indigenous cultures and human rights. She currently resides in the Los Angles area where she works as a Non-Profit Consultant as well as Contributing Editor at Sea Voices, a book project dedicated to highlighting the work of leading environmentalists around the globe with a focus upon Ocean preservation.
