Juan Algaba Colera’s Summary

I have worked with Oracle technology since 1994, and I have always enjoyed it. I try to keep up to date with the latest innovations in this field.

I like Business Intelligence projects because you need not just technical skills, but also the ability to listen to the requests from the client and an open mind to understand what they expect from a new BI system. I am a good listener and I am also quite good at explaining BI concepts to end users who are not familiar with IT. Over the years I have realized that this communication and personal skills are essential if you want to keep end users involved in the development process. The payoff of their involvement is the certainty that, in the end, the information that your data mart offers will be relevant to the users, and they are going to make use of it to do their work.

My main areas of expertise are dimensional modeling and Oracle Warehouse Builder ETL development. I am also familiar with the following tools: Oracle Discoverer, Oracle Forms, Oracle Portal, OWB, ODI, Unix, Business Objects, XML Publisher.

While implementing BI systems, I have had to perform increasingly complex DBA tasks, such as tuning ETL processes and storage management of large volumes of data. So, when I felt that I had gained enough confidence and experience in database administration, I took the time to become Oracle 9i and 10g Certified DBA. I would like to have the chance to use those DBA skills, which are especially useful when building large BI systems.
