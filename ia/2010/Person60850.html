Dustin Thostenson’s Summary

I have 10 years of software development experience with focus on VB (6.0 through .Net 3.5). Emphasis over last 6 years on Web development with Classic ASP and ASP.Net using SQLServer databases and IIS web servers.

Strong experience with entire development life cycle. Especially enjoy designing software architecture, frameworks and development/deployment utilities. I believe in developing solutions to complex problems through intuitive building blocks.
I have a lot of experience with producing respectable deliverables under very tight time lines. I understand the life cycle from business details to the software development and implementation details.

I believe in the learning process and find opportunities to teach and learn from most I encounter. I have a passion for promoting team and individual growth.
I've been a part-time instructor at DMACC for the past 8 years teaching introductory and advanced VB (6.0 and .Net 2.0) to students fresh from highschool and other accomplished developers looking to expand their skill sets. It is an enjoyable catalyst to keep up on many facets of technology as needed for people with strongly varied backgrounds. It has given me the opportunity to strengthen communications skills. It is also a great way to have an active role in helping students accomplish their goals.

In addition to formally teaching I spend a lot of time learning myself. I enjoy keeping current on technology through blogs, publications, the Iowa .Net Users Group and regional and national conferences.

I have worked with teams using the methodical waterfall approach, the Git'er Done Chaos approach and the Agile approach. Depending on the needs of the project I understand the strengths and weaknesses of them and how to use them best.

I enjoy the technical and inter person\political challenges that are inherent with providing technical solutions to business problems. I believe in balance and continual growth.

Dustin Thostenson’s Specialties:

My goal is to continue to make a noticeable difference in my job. I want to help build the skills of those around me to share the knowledge and expand the capabilities of the team. I want to remove roadblocks that cause large amounts of unnecessary time deploying or supporting applications. I want to make our processes so simple that a new team-member can make meaningful contributions quickly while advanced developers are not limited by an overly simplistic framework.
