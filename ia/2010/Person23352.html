Bruce Hoag, PhD, CPsychol’s Summary

The employee is dead. There's no job for life! Long live the independent contractor!

I'm looking for clients who want to understand how the world of work has changed and is changing. Employees have to learn how to become employable. Managers need to acquire interpersonal communication skills and eliminate inappropriate behavior such as bullying at work. Leaders need to become aware of the different styles of leadership and their own leadership traits. All of these things are necessary in order to survive and thrive in the new world of work.

I write a challenging and provocative newsletter entitled Two Sides of the Same Coin. The two sides are the perspectives of those who lead and/or manage, and those who don't. Samples of past issues are on my home page.

I'm also the co-author of Managing Value-Based Organizations: It's Not What You Think, a book for serious students who are interested in the changing world of work.

I earned a Ph.D. in Organizational Psychology from the Manchester Business School in the United Kingdom, and became a Chartered Occupational Psychologist (CPsychol) in 2009. I've also been a guest lecturer at Cambridge University, the University of Manchester, the University of Westminster Business School, and the City University Business School.

I'm a member of the Academy of Management, where I've been a regular reviewer of articles and symposium submissions, as well as a participant. I'm also a member of the British Academy of Management and the British Psychological Society.

Bruce Hoag, PhD, CPsychol’s Specialties:

Employers have become contractors, and nearly every former employee in now an independent contractor. This has serious implications for learning how to become more employable, developing new interpersonal communication skills, and eliminating bullying at work which reduces productivity & threatens the retention of talented people. I can help you find your new role and identify how your behavior needs to change as a result.
