Based on significant input from the design and developer communities within and outside the W3C membership, W3C has chartered the group to conduct its work in public and to solicit broad participation from W3C members and non-members alike.

    The HTML Working Group is currently chaired by both Chris Wilson (Microsoft) and Dan Connolly (W3C/MIT). As of this writing, the group has 485 participants, all of which are in good standing. W3C maintains a public list of any patent disclosures made in connection with the deliverables of the group.

The group is scheduled to release the First Working Draft of the HTML 5 spec in the fourth quarter of 2007, a Last Call Working Draft in the second quarter of 2008, a Candidate Recommendation in the third quarter of 2008, a Proposed Recommendation in the second quarter of 2010, and a Recommendation in the third quarter of 2010. The HTML Working Group schedule is an estimate, based on the schedule in the charter, and it is subject to change. For more information on group participation, visit http://www.w3.org/2004/01/pp-impl/40318/status.
