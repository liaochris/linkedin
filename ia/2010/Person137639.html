Matthew Villeneuve’s Summary

I have a background in local and state level campaigning, public policy analysis and research, and corporate-level, high-value project management.

While working with the Repair California Campaign, I had the opportunity to rebuild an under-performing campaign office and turn it into the highest performing office in terms of number of trained staff and volume of petitions signed per week. Though the campaign was short lived, it was an intense effort that left me with the skills needed to build a local campaign office from scratch.

As Data Migration Coordinator for Channel Technologies I had the opportunity to manage the handling of a large volume of electronic data and manage the people working with the data. In this role I learned some of the finer points of project management, including the risk of project-creep and an understanding of how to calculate progress and predict setbacks.

Lastly, by way of an internship with a California State legislator, I had the opportunity to do public policy research and legislative analysis. This summer-long position, paired with my studies at the UC Center for Public Policy at Sacramento, provided me a thorough introduction to legislative research and analysis that has helped me perform well while researching campaign issues and in working with government agencies professionally.

It is on the backbone of these skills that I am building a career in local politics and public policy.
