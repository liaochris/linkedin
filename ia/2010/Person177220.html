Ron Eckstam’s Summary

.Our clients come to us because we help them clarify their life plans and then create financial strategies and structures to accomplish them.

Most people have an idea what they want to accomplish and by when. What they don’t have are the systems in place to actually achieve it on a consistent basis. Most people know the major tools for accomplishing their financial goals. But it gets confusing when it comes to integrating all the pieces from the work life with the family life, not duplicating strategies, and also not leaving gaps in the plan.
This gets even more confusing when the stock broker recommends all stocks, the insurance provider has only insurance solutions, the annuity person says both are wrong, and the news says all of above are bad. What is a person who wants a solid plan but doesn’t want to become a financial expert supposed to do?

Ron was a corporate exec and then a small business owner for 17 years. While he enjoyed analyzing all these issues, it wasn’t how his company grew or made profits for the shareholders. After selling the company, Ron set out to solve these issues for others.
The 1st and most important criterion was to be INDEPENDENT. Many financial services providers require their associates to meet corporate goals on volume and products. This conflicts with 100% customer-focused service. LPL Financial, being the largest independent broker-dealer in the USA, allows this customer focus.
The 2nd main focus had to be the PLAN. Ron’s goal is to help people achieve their life. In order to accomplish that, a key part of any relationship is to have conversations over the family’s life goals and timing. This makes sure that any PLAN that is developed is focused on the family’s goals.
Only after the plan is developed are any TOOLS selected for implementing those plans.

First STRATEGIES, then STRUCTURES, and only then TOOLS results in a PLAN that helps increase the likelihood of achieving your family’s goals.

Ron Eckstam’s Specialties:

Life-planning for families focuses on what is important to achieve and when. Basic discussions around education, retirement, covering risks, etc. are a start, but also discussions around ‘what is important to you’ beyond the basics.
Small Business Owners have more complex planning needs and require business continuation planning for any contingency, retirement savings strategies, etc. The skill is in integrating both the business and the family issues so there aren’t duplications or gaps.

  • President

    Green Eagle, Inc

    (Wholesale industry)

    September 1983 — January 2001 (17 years 5 months)

    Running a small business requires a strong focus on strategies, structures to implement those strategies, and finally selection of the best tools for each strategy.
    Small business owners have unique planning needs. We needed business continuation planning in case either partner died, became disabled, or moved on. We had to have retirement plans and health insurance to attract good employees.
    In contrast to many entrepreneurs, I actually enjoyed analyzing and coordinating these plans. But I had the same problems that all entrepreneurs have – NO time to really focus on the specifics. Business critical path issues needed to be addressed. Too often key financial planning was squeezed into year end deadlines.
    These strategies need to be coordinated with personal financial strategies. Few experts focus on this integration of plans. Do you have a coordinated plan? Do you like creating and integrating it yourself? Would you rather work with someone who has been there, done that?

  • Buyer

    (Retail industry)

    1978 — 1983 (5 years )

    Buying at Target was a wonderful experience. They have excellent disciplines. Long-term strategic planning, great structures, disciplined execution of programs, strong focus on customer service, thought processes on maximizing assortments which interestingly work in financial strategies also, team development and team success tools and more. Many 35 year olds stay under the corporate shield. At 50 or 55, more and more move to either smaller companies or become their own boss. Are you ready? What’s your plan?
