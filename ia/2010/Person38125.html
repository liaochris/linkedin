Chris Lin’s Summary

Chris Lin is currently a fellow in the Leaders for Global Operations (LGO) program at MIT. He is studying for his MBA and a Master's in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science. Academically, he is focusing on studying rigorous decision logic and algorithms to solve questions in business operations. His master's thesis, based on a 6 month research internship at Dell, is on the development and implementation of a decision support system for routing returned computers to the most profitable recovery channel.

Chris was previously a Senior Associate Consultant with Bain & Company, working in the San Francisco Bay Area, Tokyo, and New Delhi. His case experiences includes growth strategy, manufacturing efficiency, and private equity due diligence across technology, financial, energy, and consumer services industries.

Chris graduated from Stanford University with a B.S. in Biomedical Computation, a field that applies principles of computer science to questions in medicine and biology. He was an active leader in the student government at Stanford.

M&A due diligence, corporate strategic planning, reverse logistics, decision support systems
