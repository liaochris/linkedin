Ronald W. Vickery, P.E.’s Summary

Professional Engineer with extensive experience in the geotechnical engineering field, specializing in design of segmental retaining walls, reinforced earth structures & permeable paver systems. Delights in innovative engineering problem-solving and interjecting humor into work with clients & technical presentations. NCMA Train-the-Trainer Certified.

Licensed to provide Professional Engineering services in Connecticut, Iowa, Minnesota, Nebraska, North Dakota, Ohio, South Dakota, Wisconsin

Ronald W. Vickery, P.E.’s Specialties:

Ronald W. Vickery, P.E.’s Experience

  • President/Principal Engineer

    Vickery Engineering & Consulting

    (Civil Engineering industry)

    February 2007 — Present (3 years 4 months)

    Final Design Services (which include certified, stamped construction drawings with full-sized plans & specifications) of Segmental Retaining Walls, Modular Big Block Retaining Walls, Wood Timber Walls, Recycled Material Timber Walls, Boulder Walls, Natural Stone Walls, Gabion Basket Walls, Reinforced Soil Slopes & Permeable Paver Systems.

    Clients include contractors, owners, developers, cities/municipalities, state DOT's, block producers, homeowners, landscapers & others.

    Consulting Services include Consultation on Initial Planning Process, Recommendations of Best Products for Specific Needs, Preliminary Material Quantity Estimates, On-Site Wall Construction Observation, On-Site Forensics Investigation, Recommendations on Distressed or Failing Walls, Preparation of Technical Details & Specifications, Engineering Portions of DOT/HITEC Submittals, Training of Engineers, Architects, Contractors & Block Producers & Block Producers

  • Regional Engineer | Senior Design Engineer | Design Engineer

    Anchor Wall Systems

    (Civil Engineering industry)

    1998 — 2007 (9 years )

    Worked directly with licensees, developers, architects, contractors, and engineers in a 17 state regional territory to help them gain a greater understanding of Anchor Wall products and services through business development, training presentations, DOT Submittals, and problem-solving. Expert in the field of segmental retaining walls and reinforced earth structures. Provided technical and problem-solving support to 45 national and 15 international licensees and their clients. Led over 75 technical training seminars locally and nationally, disseminating information to engineers, architects, and contractors, including providing professional development hours (when applicable). Seminars ranged in length from one-hour 'brown bag lunches' to multi-day seminars. Developed detailed understanding of local market conditions, as well as licensee competition. Designed approximately 23 million square feet of segmental retaining walls in the United States, Europe, and Australia. Designs included global stability analysis, preliminary and final designs, and certified construction drawings. Grew engineered wall market from 1.6 million to over 6 million square feet designed annually. Provided observations during construction and forensic analysis if wall distress was observed. Assisted in production of published technical literature, HITEC Submittals, DOT Submittals, ICBO Submittals, and software development, including Beta testing of four design and estimating software packages. Extensive experience with AutoCAD, AnchorWall design software, Federal Highway design software (MSEW), NCMA design software, global stability analysis software (GSlope, Stabl-6), and familiarity with other retaining wall companies' design software.

  • Project Engineer

    Braun Intertec Corporation

    (Privately Held; Civil Engineering industry)

    1992 — 1998 (6 years )

    Wrote proposals for geotechnical evaluations, consulting services, and construction testing. Scheduled and reported on testing services for clients, including geotechnical evaluation, site observation, and special inspection reports. Performed soil correction and excavation observations; proofroll of pavement subgrades; soil testing services, including compaction tests (sand cone and nuclear gauge), standard and modified proctors, sieve analysis of soils; piling observations; inclinometer readings; caisson observations; concrete testing; masonry testing; and asphalt testing.
    Conducted, supervised, and reported on vibration monitoring programs for pile driving and other construction activities near sensitive structures.

  • Engineering Technician II | Civil Engineer

    Twin City Testing Corporation

    (Civil Engineering industry)

    Performed observations and testing services similar to those at Braun Intertec (above). Conducted concrete reinforcement inspection, concrete testing (slump, air content, test cylinders), masonry testing (mortar, grout, block prisms, brick, block).
    Surveyed worksites using both electronic and optical measurements.
