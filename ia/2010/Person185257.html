Sean McCarthy’s Summary

Sean McCarthy is an experienced outplacement - career consultant working with people displaced by companies including: Disney, The Zenith, Vivendi Games, Cisco, Bank of America, KPMG, Ernst & Young, and many more. Mr. McCarthy has developed this thriving outplacement practice by focusing on the fundamentals of job search using a series of useful tools to help his clients better understand the changing job market.

Job losses from 2008 to 2009 have surpassed the levels of 1982. California’s most recent “official” unemployment numbers have topped 11.5% but the unofficial numbers do not take into account people who are working part time or who have agreed to take salary cuts in exchange for keeping their jobs. When they are added into the calculation the number of unemployed is actually much closer to 18% making this the toughest job market since the Great Depression. Caught in the middle of the crisis are millions of workers, well trained to do their individual jobs but who know little about navigating this uncertain job market.

Mr. McCarthy is experienced in showing jobseekers how to conduct a job search, to effectively interviewing and successfully negotiate their compensation. He can teach his clients how to network and the importance of nurturing their work and social connections for the long haul with the goal of making their next career move that much easier.

In 1994 McCarthy was appointed by LA Mayor Riordan to the Los Angeles County Metropolitan Transportation Authority, Citizens' Advisory Committee and then to the Greek Theater Advisory Committee in 1996. He serves on the board of directors of the Woodland Hills - Tarzana Chamber of Commerce and the United Chambers of Commerce of the San Fernando Valley. McCarthy is an elected member of the Woodland Hills / Warner Center Neighborhood Council, a member of the Pierce College Prop A and AA Citizens Oversight Committee and the Warner Center Specific Plan Citizens Advisory Committee.
