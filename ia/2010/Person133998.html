Mark Bradford’s Summary

I am an interdisciplinary designer lecturing senior studio & theory courses at the Institute of Communication Design at Massey University, Wellington, New Zealand. My current research traverses both applied and theoretical design knowledge, and cross-pollinates his long-term interest in interdisciplinary thinking. In 2008 I enrolled in a PhD Design (part-time. Full registration gained in November 2009) which explores the possibilities for enhancing creativity through the practice and philosophy of the martial art of ‘Aikido’.

Research highlights:

__Bradford, M. (2009). 'Aikido and Design Leadership'. Oral presentation at the New Zealand Postgraduate Conference, Victoria University, Wellington, New Zealand.
__Bradford, M., & Thomassen, A. (2009). 'Theory as Process: “Keiko” and ‘Co-Creative Movement’'. Poster Paper presented at the IASDR 2009 Conference : Design / Rigor & Relevance, COEX, Seoul, Korea.
__Thomassen, A., & Bradford, M. (2009). 'Beyond the Individual: the Complex Interplay of Creativity, Synthesis and Rigor in Design Led Research Processes'. Short Paper included in Proceedings at the IASDR 2009 Conference : Design / Rigor & Relevance, COEX, Seoul, Korea.
__Participated in 3 'Syn-aesthetics' workshops in Auckland in 2009. 
__2008, submission of workshop titled ‘Courageous and Creative Living : You Are What You Practice’ at the ‘Blow 08 Nga hau e wha Creative Arts Festival’ in Wellington. 
__Bradford, M. (2008). 'Takemusu Aiki: Insights into Optimizing Ideational Flow'. Paper presented at the The Fourth Art of Management and Organization Conference, Banff, Canada.
__2008, initiated “Zeme": a monthly research Colloquia at the Institute of Communication Design to assist the dissemination of research experiences. There have been 10 “Zemes” since August 2008. 
__2008, successfully completed Aikido grading for the rank of 4th Kyu.
__2008, submission of ‘CoCA Toshiharu Sawada Shihan (7th Dan) Aikido Seminar’ in Wellington.

Mark Bradford’s Specialties:

My doctorate investigates the possibilities for design leadership through the practice and philosophy of the martial art of Aikido – focussing on ‘how’ we think instead of purely ‘what’ we think. This research will explore co-creative action within the context of design'
