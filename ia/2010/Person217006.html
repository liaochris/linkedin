Xuan Huang’s Experience

  • Ph.D Candidate/ Research Assistant/ Teaching Assistant

    Georgia Institute of Technology

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    August 2004 — Present (6 years 1 month)

    * Performed experiments to analyze the influence of stress amplitude, stress ratio, and contact geometry in the fretting fatigue using a bridgetype fretting fatigue test apparatus.
    * Characterized the dry fretting behavior of Ti-6Al-4V on Ti-6Al-4V in point contact using a new fretting test system. Analyzed the running condition response and material degradation response. Surface cracks were observed when the displacement amplitude is near the transition between partial and gross slip.
    * Designed an inert gas atmosphere fretting fatigue test apparatus with the elevated temperature capability, considerable oxidation and temperature influences on fretting behavior and degradation mechanism were identified.
    * Investigated the surface treatment effect on fretting fatigue, including Low Plasticity Burnishing (LPB) and Diamond-like Carbon Coating(DLC).
    * Performed research in a new calibration procedure between quantitative measures of intragrain misorientations and levels of plastic strain. Electron backscatter diffraction (EBSD) was used to capture the spatial misorientation of lattice that can be directly related to the amount of plastic strain.
    * Researching the fretting behavior of a single crystal Ni-base superalloy in different crystallographic directions. Discovered a crystallographic orientation effect on friction and on plastic deformation, which significantly changed the fretting response and cracking formation.
    * Characterizing the damage and cracking morphology in fretting fatigue, and investigating the crystallographic cracking mechanism by using EDS, SEM and EBSD.
    * Conducting finite element study on indentation and sliding contact of a single crystal Ni-base superalloy using both a conventional J2 plasticity model and a continuum crystal plasticity model that can capture the influence of crystallographic orientation on the deformation response.
