Karl Hardesty’s Experience

  • Managing Partner, Pacific Region

    Tatum, LLC

    (Privately Held; Management Consulting industry)

    February 2008 — Present (2 years 6 months)

    Mr. Hardesty is the Managing Partner for the Pacific Region of Tatum, LLC, which includes the Orange County, San Diego, Los Angeles, Northern California, Seattle, and Portland practices. Tatum, LLC is the nation’s fastest growing executive services firm and currently has over 1,000 professionals practicing in 37 major markets. Tatum provides senior level finance and IT executive leadership and consulting services to a wide range of companies including: private equity sponsored companies, mid-market companies and Fortune 2000 companies.
    Mr. Hardesty founded the Orange County and Los Angeles practices for Tatum in 1999. He has over 25 years of broad business and financial management experience having worked in public accounting, venture capital sponsored companies, rapid growth, healthcare and public companies.

  • Partner, Financial Leadership

    Tatum LLC

    (Privately Held; Management Consulting industry)

    May 1999 — February 2008 (8 years 10 months)

    Mr. Hardesty was the Partner of Financial Leadership for the Orange County Office of Tatum, LLC. Mr. Hardesty possesses over twenty-three years of broad business and financial management experience with special competence in entrepreneurial, start-up, rapid growth, healthcare and public companies.

    Prior to becoming the Partner for the Orange County practice, Mr. Hardesty was a Tatum Engagement Partner. During this period, Mr. Hardesty was the interim CFO for a large physician practice management company. Mr. Hardesty duties primarily focused on strategy, negotiation and integration of a $300 million division of a public healthcare company.

  • Chief Financial Officer & Senior Vice-President

    First Physician Care, Inc.

    (Hospital & Health Care industry)

    January 1994 — May 1998 (4 years 5 months)

    From 1994 through 1998, Mr. Hardesty served as Senior Vice-President and Chief Financial Officer of the First Physician Care, Inc. ("FPC"), a venture capital backed start-up health care management company that grew from a business plan to over $100 million in revenues and 1400 employees over a three-year period. Mr. Hardesty was a founding stockholder in FPC and was instrumental in its rapid growth from 1994 to 1998. He negotiated and closed the acquisition of 12 healthcare companies during his tenure at FPC. Mr. Hardesty worked closely with the company's Board and investment bankers to successful merger with a public company in 1998 in an all stock "pooling" transaction.

  • Chief Financial Officer & Senior Vice-President

    Pacific Physician Services, Inc.

    (Management Consulting industry)

    January 1992 — January 1994 (2 years 1 month)

    Prior to joining FPC, Mr. Hardesty served as Senior Vice President and Chief Financial Officer of Pacific Physician Services, Inc. ("PPS"), a publicly traded healthcare management company with revenues in excess of $300 million. While at PPS, Mr. Hardesty managed two secondary public offerings of stock and convertible debt, totaling in excess of $100 million. In addition, he re-engineered the accounting department, MIS department, central business office, and established the internal audit, development and investor relations departments. He negotiated the acquisition of companies totaling in excess of $150 million while implementing a self-insured workers compensation system that saved the company in excess of $500,000 per year. Mr. Hardesty was a member of the Executive Committee and was responsible for tax planning, finance, accounting, cash management, information systems, risk management and long-term planning.

  • Partner of Audit

    Ernst & Young

    (Partnership; Accounting industry)

    January 1984 — January 1992 (8 years 1 month)

    Prior to joining PPS, Mr. Hardesty was a Partner with Ernst & Young in Southern California. During his career with Ernst & Young, Mr. Hardesty advised clients in auditing and assurance matters, SEC and accounting matters, acquisitions, mergers and divestitures. Advice included business planning, organizational structuring, financing, alternatives, special procedures, general management consulting, selecting accounting and tax methods and joint ventures. He also led the financial and accounting efforts of a healthcare company that was the first of its type to successfully complete an IPO.
