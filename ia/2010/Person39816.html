Christopher Kennedy’s Summary

Christopher M. Kennedy is a partner in the law firm, Kennedy Pierson & Strachan, LLP, with offices in Lake Forest and Chicago. Chris focuses his practice in civil litigation, including personal injury, products liability, business law, contracts, construction, and others. He has won jury verdicts in serious personal injury cases in Illinois and Michigan, and has successfully defended verdicts before appellate courts in Illinois and in the federal court of appeals. Chris also serves as an arbitrator in civil cases in both Cook and Lake Counties, and has handled complex cases involving such diverse topics as traumatic brain injury, recreational watercraft, construction, real estate development, and other fields. Chris is an elected school board member and has also written over a dozen laws in Illinois to assist children and adults with developmental disabilities.

Chris began his career as a prosecutor with the Lake County State’s Attorney’s Office, where he tried dozens of jury trials and successfully obtained convictions in over a thousand cases, including serious felonies and a wide range of other criminal matters. Chris received his Juris Doctor degree from John Marshall Law School in Chicago, Illinois, in 1994, where he was a member of the Law Review, the Law Review Executive Board, Moot Court, a Dean’s Scholarship recipient, and published an award-winning research paper. While in law school, Chris also completed internships for the United States Attorney’s Office in Chicago and a former Chief Judge of the United States District Court of the Northern District of Illinois. Chris received his Bachelor of Arts from the State University of New York at Binghamton in 1990. Chris is admitted to practice before the Supreme Court of Illinois, the United States District Courts of the Northern District of Illinois (trial bar) and the Eastern District of Michigan, and the United States Court of Appeals for the Seventh Circuit.
