Alan Reeve’s Summary

I started working with computers when I was 12 and it's been a passion ever since. I started writing (and selling) software for Atari computers when I was in High School. I went on to pursue both a B.S. and M.S. in Computer Science at Northern Illinois University and then a Ph.D., which I did not complete, at Clemson University. All the while, I continued my side business of writing and selling software first for Atari 8-bit computers, then Atari STs, and finally MS-DOS and Windows 3.x PCs.

After Clemson I got a job at FormMaker Software in Atlanta, GA doing working on their GUI form editing tool for the insurance industry and then later moved over to PrysmTech to work on touch screen movie theater ticketing applications.

I then decided to pursue some software development ideas of my own... mainly bringing low cost Interactive Voice Response applications to the PC. Unfortunately, the TAPI platform which was central to the project, was woefully inadequate at the time. During this span I developed several other utility applications, test marketing PhrontLine via Ingram Micro, and exhibited at PC Expo. It was a great learning experience as too many software developers don't appreciate the marketing side of business.

I then took a job at SPAN2 (started by some of the old FormMaker executives) due to the great opportunity it held. That was actually the most enjoyable job I've ever had, but it was also not sustainable and the company started doing layoffs roughly 9 months later.

I then made the jump back to Radiant Systems (they acquired PrysmTech which I was working there) as a Software Developer III predominantly working on the QSR team. It's pretty cool to have worked there as one of the restaurants we frequent is Culvers (now that I'm back in the Midwest) and I worked on the code for the KPS system they use. After that I worked with a real estate broker to start a company that created among the first content management systems for real estate.

Alan Reeve’s Specialties:

I've worked on projects that allow digging into the compiler's produced assembly code... in a variety of computer languages including VB, C, C++, C#, SQL, and Java... a variety of platforms including Windows, UNIX, and Mac OSX... and a variety of applications including Installable File System/Device Drivers, back end business logic, front end GUI tools, and web development. Current responsibilities have made ASP.NET, C#, AJAX, and Javascript my primary focus.
