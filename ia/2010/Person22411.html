Bernard J. Wilson’s Summary

Administrative/Personnel/Fiscal – Chief of Police with responsibility for over 900 law enforcement officers, security personnel and staff support members at a major “Category X” international airport. Policy/procedure decision maker, responsible for budget of over $80 million. Extensive interaction with other police chiefs, elected officials, community leaders, business community, etc. Part of the management team that earned the only Standard and Poor’s “AA Bond Rating” ever awarded to a U.S. airport.

Crisis Management – Key decision-maker for numerous major crisis incidents, including earthquakes, aircraft crashes, labor disputes, bomb threats, natural disasters, industrial accidents, terrorist incidents, major crime events. Following September 11, 2001, commended by residents of six California cities for "...calmness and confidence..." Commended by the international airline community for "...guiding us through trying times with grace, dignity and in good humour."

Facility and Personnel Security – Airport Security Coordinator (Federally mandated designation) for major international airport, servicing over 100 million persons annually. Extensive training and experience in threat analysis, terrorism vulnerability, VIP/dignitary protection, site security surveys, personnel identification systems, access control systems.

Investigation – Preliminary and follow-up investigations as investigator, supervisor, manager and command officer in criminal and personnel investigations. Witness and investigating officer in California and Federal courts.

Public Speaking and Instructor - Instructor and lecturer in aviation security, terrorism, traveler victimization, police management, crisis management, emergency preparedness, bomb threat management, incident command system, and other topics. Numerous television and print media appearances, including “USA Today,” “Good Morning America,” “CNN” and local media, etc.

Bernard J. Wilson’s Specialties:

Extensive hands on experience in crisis management and dealing with "the unknown." Clear and decisive decision maker during times of local, state, national and international crises.

Extensive experience in managing multi-agency operations incorporating public and private sector cooperative efforts.

Extensive experience in public sector personnel issues, labor negotiations, risk management.

Certified in Homeland Security, Level V (CHS-V) - ABCHS
