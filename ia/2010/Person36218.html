Charlie O'Keefe’s Summary

I am a developer with interests in visualization and online application platforms.

My main area for a few years was data visualization, including but not limited to geospatial data and algorithms. Over the past year I have invested a lot of time into learning how to write clean, scalable, testable web applications. On the 'scalable' front I'm starting to work with message queuing systems. The most interesting to me right now is probably the AMQP protocol and the RabbitMQ server.

I've also become increasingly interested in nonrelational databases, most notably CouchDB. I've been frustrated by the impedance mismatch that object-relational mappers try to bridge, and am increasingly of the opinion that relational databases are a poor fit for many web applications. Along similar lines, I've been reading Werner Vogels' papers on eventual consistency and dynamo with interest, and started to learn Erlang.

I've helped edit and review several books in the Pragmatic Programmers series, mostly dealing with Ruby, Rails, and Facebook.

I like good design. I've been researching how to better design systems for scalability and extensibility, as well as working on learning good visual design. I'm excited about the future possibilities of globally accessible data and services, as computing devices become more mobile and more pervasive.

Goals: To work in a dynamic environment with a lot of creative, intelligent people and make meaningful contributions toward cool stuff. To find good mentors and mentor others. To find a group of people I work well with and can become good friends with outside of work.

Charlie O'Keefe’s Specialties:
