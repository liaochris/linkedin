Jay Greenwald’s Summary

WORLDWIDE TECHNOLOGY EXECUTIVE with
START-UP, STRATEGY, PLANNING & ACQUISITION INTEGRATION EXPERTISE IN 60 COUNTRIES

Jay is an internationally acclaimed expert on enterprise software distribution outside the U.S. with more than 20 years experience within all functions of software companies.

He worked at Sterling Software for 10 years, where he continually advanced in product, marketing and GM roles. Sterling was a $1+ billion NYSE-listed enterprise software vendor that was sold for more than $8 billion. Positions included:

• Products – 7 years in worldwide product management , product launch, int'l business planning

• VP of Int'l Marketing – managed a team of product directors responsible for launch, product planning, strategy and sales support for 65 countries / $170 M revenues

• Division President, Emerging Markets Division – ran $40 million P&L spanning 55 countries in S. / E. Europe, Middle East, Africa, S. Korea / SE Asia, Latin America. Managed direct sales; 60+ independent distributors; total staff of 75 spanning London, Washington DC, Sao Paulo, Singapore.

• M&A – performed 6 post-merger acquisition integrations as part of the select corporate team. His individual responsibility was rationalizing int'l sales forces across 65 countries and developing business and financial plans under severe time pressures that he later executed on as an operating executive.

He started Asian Ops for OpenLink Financial, the leading enterprise trading and risk management software vendor with products targeted at the largest capital market and energy / commodity traders in the world. He chose Singapore as the office location, started the office, set up the subsidiary, hired sales & technical staff and "jump-started" sales personally.

He has worked in 41 countries and understands the sales, business and tech drivers in 65 countries. Has lived in London, Paris and Singapore for 6 years. He was born to American parents in the Alsace region of France.

Jay Greenwald’s Specialties:

(1) Strategy, start-up, go-to-market and worldwide distribution of SaaS and traditional enterprise software products and services (2) Post-merger acquisition integration in technology companies (3) Immersion in culture, sales drivers, technology trends, business protocol in 65 countries.
