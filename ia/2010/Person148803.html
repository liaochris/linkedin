Morey Schapira’s Summary

Successful Executive with CEO and Sales & Business Development background. Have worked at leading top tier public companies such as HP, Raytheon, and Broadcom, and with successful startups, such as Network General, Netcom Systems, BlueSteel Networks and REDmedic. Have been a general manager of an Asia/Pacific division and been successful penetrating the Japanese marketplace.

My sales and business development efforts have helped create 4 liquidity events- one IPO and three Mergers and Acquisitions.

I am an excellent team builder and motivational leader. I was Vice President of Sales at Network General, a publicly traded company, that was ranked #2 in sales growth among 7,700 companies, as measured by Business Week magazine.

Morey Schapira’s Experience

  • CEO

    REDmedic

    (Privately Held; Internet industry)

    2003 — Present (7 years )

    REDmedic was very challenging and filled a great need in the marketplace (online Personal Health Records). Obtaining funding, building out the team, refining our business plan, building a world class advisory board, going live with our system, gaining the first purchase orders, building up strategic partnerships and establishing our firm's position in the marketplace.
    It was great to receive an industry award from our peers which recognized our innovative approach in enabling the timely delivery of cost-effective healthcare.
    We were happy to sell REDmedic to a major player in the US healthcare industry.

  • Corporate Distribution Sales Manager

    BROADCOM

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; BRCM; Semiconductors industry)

    2000 — 2002 (2 years )

    I personally booked the largest cash-in-advance order in Broadcom history for $18,650,000.00, while increasing our customer base by 175 new customers and many more design wins. We dominated our marketplace in Silicon Valley. I grew the business to the #2 business line (behind Xilinx) at the Silicon Valley branch at Insight Electronics.

  • Vice President of Worldwide Sales

    BlueSteel Networks

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Semiconductors industry)

    1999 — 2000 (1 year )

    BlueSteel Networks was a leading supplier of security co-processors which enabled the acceleration of IPSec and SSL at Gigabit speeds. I drove key design wins at key customers like Siemens and Nokia. Due to the excellent performance of our chips and the great team that we had assembled, Broadcom acquired BlueSteel in 2000 for $150,000,000.

  • Western Region Sales Manager

    Netcom Systems

    (Privately Held; 201-500 employees; Computer Networking industry)

    1997 — 1999 (2 years )

    Netcom Systems was the manufacturer of Smartbits(tm), the industry standard for stress testing computer networks, networking equipment and communications chips. We sold to OEM's like Cisco, Nortel, 3Com, Foundry and Extreme, as well as leading carriers/ISPs (PacBell, MCI, QWEST) and corporate users (Microsoft, Nationsbank, U.S. government) with large enterprise networks. We dominated our market segment and were very profitable. We hit the $100 million run rate, filed for an IPO and then were acquired for $465,000,000 by Bowthorpe PLC, a UK firm.

  • Vice President of Worldwide Sales

    Network General

    (Public Company; 201-500 employees; NETG; Computer Networking industry)

    1987 — 1994 (7 years )

    I recruited, trained and directed a third party channel. I then hired an outstanding team of Regional Managers, District Managers and Systems Engineers. I visited over 200 major corporate customers and demonstrated our products. I instituted a Major Accounts, Service Provider, and Telesales program. I drove our sales that enabled us to have an IPO in 1989. I grew our sales past $44 million and managed a staff of 45 people. I grew our marketshare so that it exceeded that of our top two competitors ( HP and Novell) combined.
