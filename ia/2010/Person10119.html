Amy Grunder’s Summary

Amy began solo practice writing asylum appeals on contract for immigration attorneys in early 2008. She is a former associate of Edwards Angell Palmer & Dodge LLP, where she worked in the Public Finance Department and successfully represented an asylum seeker pro bono in affirmative and defensive proceedings. She is a member of the New England Chapter of the American Immigration Lawyers Association (AILA) and the Boston Bar Association.

Amy earned a JD (magna cum laude) in 2001 from American University's Washington College of Law, where she focused on international law, including human rights law. She obtained her MA in International Affairs in 2002 from AU's School of International Service. Prior to graduate school, Amy worked in international human rights advocacy, including writing, editing, publishing and project management. She received a BA in Political Science (summa cum laude) from the University of Massachusetts at Boston in 1991, where she graduated with Honors in Political Science and a Distinction in Political Science, and won the Lillian Lorraine Jones Fiction Prize, the Marcia Keach Memorial Poetry Prize, and the Academy of American Poets Prize the same year.
