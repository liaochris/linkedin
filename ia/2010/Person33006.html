Caroline Campbell’s Experience

  • VP, Corporate Communications

    AOL

    (Public Company; AOL; Internet industry)

    October 2009 — Present (8 months)

    Standing alone as an independent public company (as of Dec. 2009), we are focused on communicating messages to the marketplace regarding our core businesses at AOL, and executing a strategy that will help drive long term value for shareholders.

    I am responsible for all communications around four of our five strategy areas:

    - AOL Media: positioning AOL as one of the largest producers of original, high quality content on the Web. We have more than 80 premium media properties where we are creating interactive original content experiences for consumers. Seed.com, our recently launched content management system, is also a priority for communications as we continue to hire incredibly talented journalism professionals to drive the quality content.

    - AOL Advertising: positioning AOL as the global leader in scale and reach for premium digital advertising; providing differentiating solutions to advertisers & agencies; and performance analytics & insights to help drive their campaigns.

    - Local & Mapping: positioning AOL as a major player in local and mapping including Patch, our hyper-local platform that will be in hundreds of neighborhoods in 2010; Mapquest, a major brand in mapping; and CitiesBest.

    - AOL Ventures: communicating that AOL is in the marketplace investing in early-stage externally-developed opportunities and employee-originated ideas, allowing innovative initiatives to thrive, and providing those ideas the ability to be nurtured in a creative, entrepreneurial environment.

    I am also supporting the C-suite corporate initiatives including earnings, leadership hires, major speaking platforms, AOL's rebranding campaigns, competitive positioning and major marketing initiatives at the corporate and strategy area level.

    Corporate communications has a seat at the executive table and is seen as mission critical in the turnaround of AOL.

  • Vice President

    Porter Novelli

    (Public Company; OMC; Public Relations and Communications industry)

    February 2009 — October 2009 (9 months)

    Was the lead on HP's Imaging & Printing Group's Graphic Solutions Business, responsible for managing a team that supports the Worldwide PR, AR and Exec Comms team. We also integrated our efforts with the regions, across IPG and with HP Corporate.

    Global communications efforts included driving traditional and non-traditional influencer communications programs and activities.

    We developed strategic/creative plans and programs integrating with the worldwide marketing team and the business leaders; executed aggressive media outreach across all mediums (including digital platforms); prepared executives for media interviews and presentations; developed press materials for worldwide launches/ announcements and coordinating those with the regions; executed major worldwide events with press & analysts; managed issues proactively; and drove competitive programs and positioning campaigns.

    I was responsible for managing the worldwide budget for that part of HP's business to ensure effective use of funds across business segments.

    HP is Porter Novelli's flagship client globally.

  • Senior Manager, Global Comm & Public Affairs

    Google

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; GOOG; Internet industry)

    June 2007 — May 2008 (1 year )

    I led the national (and trade) proactive media outreach advertising team in NY charged with garnering coverage for Fortune 500 companies who were using the Google & YouTube platforms in innovative and creative ways to reach their customers through online advertising / marketing campaigns. Placements: WSJ, NYT, CNBC, Ad Age, Businessweek, the wires and a number of industry trades (to name a few).

    Working directly with the president of Advertising & eCommerce for North America, and all of the industry heads, I was also responsible for preparing them for media interviews and various speaking engagements, as well as planning and execution of media events.

    I worked directly with a number of Google's Fortune 500 clients on the strategic messages & direction once a reporter was interested in the story. Most of the stories were larger feature pieces - with the end goal being a more deeper strategic business story on the client's use of the digital platform in its marketing efforts.

  • Director
