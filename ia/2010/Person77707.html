Gregg Swanson’s Summary

Success in life is not a matter of chance; it’s a matter of purposeful execution of technique combined with precise mental training and peak performance coaching.

The most significant difference between elite and great achievers is how they think and their use of mental strength and mental training for peak performance. When a person get’s to the top of their game there is very little (if any) difference in their skills and capabilities and that of their competition.

The difference in is how they have mastered control over their thoughts and emotions before, during and after an important event. I’ll even go so far as to say they have mastered their thoughts and emotions period…regardless of the event.

Mental strength coaching is the study of the mental strategies of human brilliance and then transferring them to the individual to achieve personal excellence. It focuses on the discovery and coding of those models and patterns of human excellence behavior and utilizing them in the pursuit of personal peak performance.

  • Sales Performance Specialist, Sales Coach and Sales Mentor

    Warrior Sales Coach (Sole Proprietorship)

    (Sole Proprietorship; Professional Training & Coaching industry)

    April 2008 — Present (2 years 1 month)

    Warrior Sale Coaching 5 Part Process
    1. Establish Current Mind Set – This is accomplished using a propriety assessment profile based on the latest data derived from evaluating over 15,000 elite sales performers that represent the top 5% in their respective industries. The development report is intended to be a looking glass that reflects the salesperson style of relating with others as they work toward their sales goals. The goal of the report is to reflect to the salesperson how they report themselves on a personality questionnaire and in comparison to other highly successful salespersons.
    2. Review The Results – The sales coach will discuss the results with the individual, receive and provide feedback.
    3. Establish An Action Plan – Along with the sales coach, the salesperson will create an accountability action plan. The accountability for the plan is with both the sales coach and the individual; it will be the responsibility for the salesperson to execute the plan, with the guidance of the sales coach.
    4. Coach The Process - During the entire process the sales coach will be there to assist the salesperson with reflections, observations and feedback.
    5. Review The Results - Once the plan has been executed to the agreement of the sales coach and individual, a progress review takes place. The review will look at, among other items; the sales results, the individuals behavior and if need be, another assessment profile to document the progress.

    In addition to increased sales performance, some of the benefits of sales coaching and having a sales mentor are:
    • Better able to develop long-term relationships for long-term business results. By creating a win-win outcome, they are able to minimize buyer remorse.
    • Improved questioning techniques enables sales people to more accurately define customer needs and stimulate the desire to buy.
    • By understanding their relationship to time and organization, they are able to make more use of prime selling time
