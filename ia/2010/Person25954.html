Bonnie Buckner’s Summary

I have over twelve years executive experience in diverse aspects of business including emerging technologies, business development, marketing, political/market research and entertainment.

I consult with individuals and organizations drawing from my business background and using the Reversing Method (TM) developed at the School of Images in New York. I also head MicroFocus Media, a corporate and political marketing research firm and am a former co-founder and partner of BC, a restoration and urban renewal venture. In these capacities I have worked with U.S. Presidential and other prominent national political campaigns, as well as helped organize community grass-roots neighborhood improvement efforts in conjunction with city boards and non-profit organizations.

I am faculty at the Media Psychology and Social Change graduate program for UCLA/Fielding Graduate University, teaching media and political psychology. I am a published author and have participated in prestigious panels across the United States on topics as diverse as Cognitive Psychology and Producing for the Small Screen to Micro-Targeting for Political Campaigns. In addition, I am Program Director for Imagined Communities, a applied educational initiative that synthesizes emerging technologies and the cognitive sciences for community-building and renewal.
