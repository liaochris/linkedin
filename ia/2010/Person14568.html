Ann Rea’s Summary

Blending old and new world traditions, San Francisco based artist Ann Rea paints colors inspired by sunlight. With the direct mentorship she received from contemporary painter Wayne Thiebaud (an American Art icon), she paints in the timeless tradition of French Impressionists like Monet, plein air (in the open air).

Ann creates field studies in the natural landscape. Winemakers give her a tour of the vineyard where she places her easel to capture the unique beauty of the vines in oils. She returns to her Pacific beach studio to create larger renditions on custom canvases, each original a unique exploration.

Ann’s subject is color inspired by natural light, as it exists in time. The objects of her attention include vineyards, private gardens, and contemporary still life. Her mediums include oil on canvas, pastel, and charcoal on fine Arches French watercolor paper. Ann even uses the same oil pigments as Van Gough from Old Holland Oil Works established in 1664.

Ann’s work is gaining increasingly collectible status. Her work and inspiring story continue to be featured in the national media, most recently on Fine Living and NBC’s In Wine Country, “The Tasting Panel”, “Practical Winery and Vineyard Management”, and “Fortune” magazines. Read about her blue ocean business strategy in “Career Renegade” by Jonathan Fields, published by Random House. Her talent is commended by Wayne Thiebaud (an American Art icon). Her work is acquired by a growing list of collectors and celebrities across the US, Canada, and Europe. Her book “‘Colors of Terroir’ an artist’s tour of Sonoma” will be published soon and available on annrea.com.

marketing strategy, understanding and respect for the design process, custom commissioned paintings, creating win-win agreements, creative problem solving, the language of color and light, creating systems, working with first time art buyers
