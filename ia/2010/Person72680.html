Gareth Howell’s Summary

I improve the resilience of organizations so that they can survive and grow.

All organizations hit the occasional bump in the road. Having a credible Business Continuity Plan is like having shock absorbers on your car: it increases the chance that you will survive with no more than a shake, rather than breaking a wheel; or crash.

Business continuity planning is also a visible sign that your business is planning for the long term. This is valuable when a business is growing as it proves to current and potential clients that you care enough about them to invest in systems that improve your ability to deliver excellent customer service when the road gets bumpy.

I got into business continuity as a natural progression from having spent 10 years as an engineer and 25 years as a consultant designing complex IT systems for organisations of all sizes. Safety, Effectiveness and Efficiency have always been my watchwords and so I always designed systems that were both safe to operate and resilient to failure.

Over time, I got more involved with the business processes that used IT as an enabling tool and again I was focused on making those processes resilient to failure. From that it was a small step to specialise in Business Continuity Management.

Business continuity management is a disciplined way of ensuring that the business processes that are critical to the value creation activities of an organisation are resilient and able to continue delivering customer value even if something has gone wrong. Businesses of all sizes need to take the matter of business continuity seriously if they are planning to stay around for the long term. Whilst the statistical chance of experiencing a serious incident, such as a fire, may be small in any one year; the longer you are in business, the more likely you are to experience some form of business threatening incident.

    What I do

    I work with organisations to improve the resilience of their business processes so that they are more able to continue delivering value when things go wrong and are in a better position to win business from other companies for whom Business Continuity Management is a way of life.

    How I do it

    I work closely with my customers to improve their awareness of business continuity and then help them go through a structured process that results in the production of a workable business continuity plan. I believe that for a plan to be of maximum value, it should be created and maintained by the business themselves, therefore my desired mode of working is to deliver expertise, advice and guidance so that you can do the job yourself. That said, there are instances where I will write your plan for you based on a detailed business impact analysis.

    When you use my service, I always endeavor to give you the advice that is appropriate to your needs: there is no hidden agenda.

    What am I trying to achieve

    I want my customers to have the plans and processes that improve and protect their businesses, position them to win more business and save money through improved effectiveness and efficiency.

    What my customers achieve

    Business that work with me can rest assured that their business is protected. They are also better able to win more valuable contracts from larger companies who demand that their suppliers have in place auditable business continuity plans.
