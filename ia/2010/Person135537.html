Lenore Martin’s Summary

My research has grown out of a synthetic and physical organic approach to solving problems of molecular recognition in the life sciences. I have expanded into using NMR, CD, cryo-TEM and bioinformatics computational tools to further our understanding of how peptides and related natural products interact with larger proteins and biological membranes to specifically target some cell types over others. A significant effort in recent years in my research has been to characterize the physical and chemical interactions that make antimicrobial peptides, isolated from natural sources, more potent and specific than most rationally designed analogues. We have prepared series of small combinatorial "collections" of synthetic peptides that specifically test our hypotheses. A strength of our research group has been the development of Bioanalytical assays and high-throughput screens to evaluate the activity of our compounds. We always try to develop new peptides that might have therapeutic use, as well as being of theoretical interest.
