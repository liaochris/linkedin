Guilherme Ambros’s Summary

I've been working with technology and web since.. well, since it wasn't called "web". It was a time when BBSs were the norm, hackers met face-to-face, unix terminals were cool and gopher was just being launched. I'm lucky enough to say I saw when the web was born =)

I'm the lead of the Digital practice at Wunderman/Y&R Brands Miami, being responsible for digital projects in Latin America and US Hispanic, and more specifically demand generation activites for Dell. My areas of expertise and interest include digital marketing, e-commerce, direct response and social media.

Before moving to the US I spent 7 years in Wunderman São Paulo, building the team and leading Digital & CRM initiatives for Nokia, Microsoft, Ford, Unilever and other global clients. With Nokia specifically I spent 3 years leading the regional account and coordinating teams in 6 markets, thus spending more time on a plane than with family (Skype has been a close friend ever since).

Before that I was one of the founders of Gratis1, a free ISP in partnership with StarMedia/Chase during the dotcom boom. It was a roller coaster but worked with some of the best minds in the industry and enjoyed every moment. Early on on my career I founded Plug-In in 95, the first corporate ISP and pioneer of web hosting in Brazil. The company later became one of the largest independent web hosting providers until being acquired by UOL in 2008.

I essentially grew up with computers, starting with my first TRS at the age of 10 and learning to develop in assembly to hack games, but it was just when I first connected my BBS to FidoNet that realized I'd be hooked to the net forever.

Areas of interest include technology in general, startups, mobile industry, social media, e-commerce, analytics, direct marketing and the science behind consumer behavior. On a personal note I love music, gaming, clubbing and building robots with Arduino on spare time ("spare" meaning anything between midnight and 5am :).
