Cy Caine’s Experience

  • Vice President - Product Management, Marketing & New Product Strategy

    Physicians' Desk Reference - Thomson Reuters, Inc and PDR Network, LLC.

    (Information Services industry)

    June 2008 — February 2010 (1 year 9 months)

    I was recruited to join the executive team for Physicians’ Desk Reference (PDR), a unit of the Healthcare & Science business of Thomson Reuters. A decision had been made to divest the PDR business. As a member of the executive team, I was tasked with establishing a coherent and compelling product and marketing strategy that would appeal to prospective acquirers.

    In this role I managed a $70M P&L and a staff of 27 that included product management, marketing, sales, web operations, business development and customer support personnel. In addition to developing and beginning to execute these plans, I was a key member of the five-person PDR executive team that led the process to sell the PDR business. Our efforts garnered multiple bids. Transaction successfully closed in September 2009. I stayed on with PDR for 6 months following its sale in September 2009 to Lee Equity Partners.

    HIGHLIGHTS INCLUDED:
    ● Crafted new sales and marketing materials based on the cost effectiveness of PDR in helping pharmaceutical companies satisfy their ‘duty to warn.’ This legal doctrine holds that pharmaceutical companies must provide the prescribing physician with a meaningful warning about the risks associated with the use of their products.

    ● Increased electronic messaging revenue by 140%. Improved operational efficiency enabling revenue growth without increased staffing. Dramatically improved performance on key measures (deliverability, open rate and click-thru rate) without slowing revenue growth.

    ● Executed long-term strategic partnership to deploy PDR content on a wide range of mobile device platforms (iPhone, Blackberry, etc.) Developed multi-channel marketing program to promote this new offering. Managed process to customize vendor’s proprietary offering to meet PDR’s needs, including development of a new electronic messaging channel.

  • VP - Strategy and Business Development

    Network Communications, Inc.

    (Privately Held; Publishing industry)

    August 2005 — May 2008 (2 years 10 months)

    Network Communications, Inc. (NCI) is the largest publisher of local printed and online magazines for the real estate market.

    In this role I collaborated with business unit leaders to develop brand-specific online strategies for The Real Estate Book, Apartment Finder, Unique Homes, New Home Finder, and Home Improvement magazines. I spearheaded business development initiatives, led new product development initiatives for NCI's online properties and managed NCI’s search engine marketing (SEM) and search engine optimization (SEO) activities.

    HIGHLIGHTS INCLUDED:
    ● Negotiated and implemented fourteen strategic relationships with industry participants such as Google, The New York Times, Scripps Networks Interactive, Classified Ventures, Zillow.com, RealtyTrac.com, SecondSpace.com and Oodle.com. These relationships were a source of significant competitive advantage for NCI’s real estate properties.

    ● Using technology licensed from Previsite, built and launched InView (see ApartmentsInView.com) to provide virtual tours for all Apartment Finder advertisers, leapfrogging the photo slide shows being offered by our competitors. This program contributed to Apartment Finder’s record growth in 2008.

    ● Developed new web property (FastValues.com) that introduced homeowners to a realtor for the purpose of receiving a free home valuation report, resulting in thousands of leads per month for NCI advertisers.

    ● Identified By Design Publishing as acquisition target for NCI. This was the company’s largest acquisition of 2007.

    ● Directed all SEO activities. Managed $2M annual SEM budget. Employed metrics-driven decision making to achieve dramatic growth in online traffic and lead generation using existing staff and budgets. Reduced cost per lead by 30%.

    ● Led process to select Omniture web analytics platform. Defined reporting requirements. Implementation underway at the time of my departure from NCI.

  • VP - Product Management, Construction Data Business

    Reed Business Information - Reed Construction Data

    (Public Company; RUK; Publishing industry)

    April 2003 — March 2005 (2 years )

    Reed Business Information (RBI) is the business division of Reed Elsevier Group PLC. A division of RBI, Reed Construction Data’s (RCD) portfolio of products and services includes national, regional and local construction data, building product information, construction cost data and advertising channels to construction industry professionals in the US and Canada.

    Having stabilized RCD’s Building Product Information business, I was asked to take on responsibility for RCD’s Construction Data business which represented more than 60% of total revenue. With a critical product release already six months overdue, fixing the product development process was my primary initial focus.

    HIGHLIGHTS INCLUDED:
    ● Grew customer base of Reed Connect, our flagship product, by nearly 40% per year over a two-year period. In line with corporate strategy, grew online revenue from 34% to 45% of total.

    ● Delivered 11 releases of ReedConnect, a Web-based construction project information system. Managed product-related activities from concept to launch, including business and functional requirements, organizational readiness, marketing and pricing.

    ● Led team that conducted in-depth analysis of issues confronting RCD’s legacy print business. Based on findings, implemented changes that stabilized this critical profit contributor following two years of significant declines. Changes included a new pricing approach to reduce channel conflict, product improvements and new sales and marketing tactics. These efforts played a major role in stabilizing this previously declining segment of RCD’s Construction Data business.
