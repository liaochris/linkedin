Karen Pitsley, AIA, CAPS’s Summary

Karen Pitsley, AIA, CAPS is principal architect of Residential Reflections Architects, LLC. Graduating with a Bachelor of Science in Architecture from the School of Architecture, University of Maryland College Park, Ms. Pitsley joined the firm of GMA&D as a Project Designer. Her work during her three year tenure there, included recreational master plans and design, commercial design, tenant fit-out and custom home design. As a designer and then a Project Architect for George C. Harne, Architect, her portfolio included a wide variety of commercial and industrial work, as well as custom residential work. She earned her registration as a professional architect in 2001. In 2003, Ms. Pitsley and Kate McCullough, AIA opened Symmetry First Architects, LLC. Because of her love of residential architecture and desire to work only in this venue, this year she has moved on from Symmetry First, and has started Residential Reflections Architects, LLC. Ms. Pitsley has over ten years experience helping clients design their dream homes, whether it be a new custom home or a renovation and addition to their existing house.

Ms. Pitsley earned her CAPS (Certified Aging-In-Place Specialist) designation in 2005. This specialty enhances her ability to incorporate design elements which allow people to stay in their current home safely, independently, and comfortably or design new homes in which people are able to spend the rest of their years.
