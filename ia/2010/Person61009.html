David Wesley’s Summary

One of the world’s leading business case researchers, with more than 12 years of research, editing, and program management experience.

Provided support and advice to several US and European universities in the development of research and executive education capabilities.

One of four members to be awarded the first National Science Foundation grant in the college’s history.

Founding member of the Institute for Global Innovation Management at Northeastern University, an internationally recognized research center for developing effective approaches and solutions to global innovation management and for the development and dissemination of teaching materials concerning the global challenges facing technology intensive companies.

Works with corporate leaders in diverse industries on a wide range of strategic and managerial issues.

Winner of numerous academic, research, and publication awards.

Contributed to more than 20 management textbooks.

Author of the forthcoming book Innovation and Marketing in the Video Game Industry: Avoiding the Performance Trap

David Wesley’s Experience

  • Research Manager

    Northeastern University

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    October 1999 — Present (10 years 5 months)

    The case research program was created in 1999 with funding from the Darla and Fredrick Brodsky Professor in Global Business. From its inception, my role has been to establish, coordinate and promote Northeastern as a world class case research institution. The success of the program has helped Northeastern foment its status as a world leader in innovation research and promoted ties between the business community, government, and academia.

  • Social Development Officer

    Province of British Columbia

    (Government Agency; 5001-10,000 employees; Government Administration industry)

    July 1991 — February 1996 (4 years 8 months)

    Administrator for all adoption services, both public and private, for the north coast of British Columbia (approx. 80,000 km²).

    Extensive case studies on adoptive families incorporated substantial intergovernmental background investigations and required close coordination with law enforcement agencies and foreign governments (in cases of international adoption).

    Additional duties: Worked with the Minister of Social Services to draft new child welfare policies and legislation and provided emergency child welfare services on an as needed basis.

    Member: Women's Action Committee - Public Services Commission initiative to ensure representative diversity in the public sector.

  • Social Program Manager

    Seton Lake Indian Band

    (Non-Profit; 1-10 employees; Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    March 1990 — April 1991 (1 year 2 months)

    Directed all social programs and counseling services for six aboriginal reserves, while also providing drug and alcohol addiction counseling. Provided logistical support and negotiation services during the Oka land claims crisis, including direct discussions with the Premier of British Columbia and other elected officials. Finally, as appointed media liaison, I represented local tribes on television programs such as BCTV’s “News Hour” and CBC’s “The National.”
