Andy Bartling’s Summary

“Quite simply, marketers spend their entire lives reinventing the wheel.”

I wish I were clever (or bold) enough to have said that first. But the credit goes to one of the most successful marketers in modern history. How is Drayton Bird's insight relevant to you?

1. Do you find yourself constantly trying “something different” with your marketing efforts?
2. Do you wonder if you’re getting all the value you should from your marketing budget?
3. Do you believe that, no matter how strong your sales message is today, it should work harder?

And the most important question of all: How do you know you’re doing the right thing?

I’ve spent years thinking about these questions, especially the last one. What I’ve learned is that no matter how large or small your organization. Your level of industry expertise. Or how much money you have to throw at a problem. None of it matters unless you start with what matters most:

Why you’re in business. Why your customers should care. And how you tell that story.

In a word, your message. Before you write a word, build a plan or spend a dollar. You have to know what to say. But that’s just the beginning:

1. How do you know your message will be as competitive tomorrow as it is today?
2. How do you measure the effectiveness of your message over time?
3. How do you evolve your message, to keep it relevant?

Answering these questions is what Sales Message Marketing is all about. It’s how to say more to every audience. And spend less every time.

In fact, you’ll save 20 – 30% of your time, effort and money on every communications project. You can reallocate those resources elsewhere. Or take the savings to your bottom line.

10+ years in the making, this system works for organizations of every size, from the Fortune 50 to solo business consultants. And for companies in any industry, including financial services, healthcare, life sciences, manufacturing and many more.

Ready to Say More, Spend Less? Visit www.salesmessagemarketing.com

  • Vice President, Creative Services: Marketing Strategist; Direct Response Copywriter

    Aon Corporation

    (Insurance industry)

    September 1991 — December 1998 (7 years 4 months)

    I developed the marketing/communications programs (and wrote the copy) for selling extended warranties at North America's largest big-box retailers, such as Office Depot, Office Max, CompUSA, Computer City, Micro Center and many of the largest regional players (such as The Wiz in New York City and Paul's TV in Southern California). I also wrote the control copy for direct response programs for many of the world's leading technology manufacturers, including Apple, Sony, Hewlett-Packard and others. My best control package: a warranty renewal sales letter for Quebec-based Bombardier (makers of Sea Doos), which hit a conversion rate of nearly 50%. These warranty programs were a critical revenue generator for my clients, often representing the majority of the margin earned on every product sale.

  • Vice President: Marketing Strategist; Advertising & Direct Response Copywriter

    Advertising Associates, Inc.

    (Marketing and Advertising industry)

    1983 — 1991 (8 years )

    I began my career as a 'Creative Account Executive'--better known as a 'copy/contact person.' As both a strategist-in-training and a cub copywriter, I learned from the very beginning how inseparable strategy and tactics truly are--if you're doing it right. I had the good fortune to work on a family of local St. Louis brands (such as Southwest Bank, known globally for being the first to lower the prime rate). Plus, I worked at the national level with Citicorp, writing direct mail and print advertising that introduced many of Citi's innovations in the 1980s, including adjustable rate mortgages and home equity credit lines. Plus, I was there when Citibank became a national brand, helping to open and market new locations in several states. Later, I worked with several divisions of GE Capital--particularly in private mortgage insurance and real estate. My training at Advertising Associates was 'old school' by today's standards--but it's still the best education I've ever received in my nearly 30 years in this business. What I learned in those days I carry with me to this day.
