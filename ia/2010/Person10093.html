Amy Franko’s Summary

I think professional development and learning (along with an entrepreneurial spirit) are in my blood.

My love of learning propelled me to jump into the world of business ownership and launch my company in early 2007.

Prior to that leap, I was in sales for IBM and Lenovo where I managed a $12 million territory. Those skills were so valuable in the early days of my company, and still are today.

From 2007 until just recently you may have known my company as Amy Franko Consulting. While that suited my work for those first few years, in early 2010 I was ready to grow into something more.

It was about that time I did a learning exercise in uncovering my “unique brilliance,” which at first glance doesn’t seem like it applies much to the business of Impact Instruction Group.

But turns out, it applies perfectly. My unique brilliance is “playing with possibilities that matter.”

It’s exactly what I help my customers do - play with the possibilities that matter to their employees, their clients, and their companies – all from the standpoint of training and development. It aligns perfectly with my company’s mission – designing custom learning experiences for business impact.

I bring energy, ideas, business perspective, a sense of humor, and a positive attitude to your T+D projects. My team and I collaborate with you to understand your business and measurements of success.

I understand that training programs must energize the learner, provide knowledge and tools that can be used immediately, and provide a business return.

I’m also passionate about supporting women in business, with programs specific to developing today’s professional woman - based on content from the best-seller Nice Girls Don't Get the Corner Office.

I’m a member of American Society for Training and Development and I run the Learning Executives Network for the Central Ohio chapter. I’m an active member in Women for Economic and Leadership Development (WELD).

Amy Franko’s Specialties:

Designing facilitator/student guides, live workshops, creating courses. Areas of interest: soft skills courses; courses for women in business and women business owners
