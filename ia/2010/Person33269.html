Carolyn M. Scott’s Summary

Award Winning Producer/Director/Filmmaker and Educator with more than 25 years leading teams in education, technical and film endeavors. Carolyn is one of 100 women worldwide chosen by the National Women's History project for the 2009 - Women Taking the Lead to Save the Planet!

Carolyn is Founder and Executive Director of Turtle Island Films, whose mission is to bring “green” films and media to a large audience. Carolyn founded a non-profit REEL COMMUNITY ACTION, which distributes award winning films and activist tool-kits to lead organizers for catalyzing events. The events put in motion long term sustainability strategies (peoples' power co-ops, green investment circles, restoration projects and legislative campaigns).

Carolyn directed and produced the award winning film TEXAS GOLD, which won Best Documentary at the New York City Short Film Festival, and has been an official selection for more than 40 film festivals worldwide. Carolyn was winner of the coveted 2003 Roy W. Dean Writer’s Grant to further develop a film series about women around the world who are working to find solutions to global ecological issues. Texas Gold was acquired by Sundance Channel for Robert Redford’s “THE GREEN” film series premiering 2008. PBS Emmy winning series Natural Heroes has also acquired the film to begin broadcast in the Fall of 2007.

In 2000-2002 Carolyn’s organizational and design instincts led her to found and lead a small creative team who developed a compatibility software system.

Carolyn is a founding member of the Biofuels Research Cooperative in Sonoma County and runs her car on organic, carbon neutral biodiesel.
