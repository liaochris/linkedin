Bill Chamberlin’s Summary

Bill is a Principal Consultant on IBM’s Corporate Marketing & Communications team. Bill leads an internal consulting practice designed to help IBM brand managers launch social media strategies and then extract business insights and value from all the social conversations that take place.

Bill also works with IBM business unit strategists, supporting them with strategic research projects on emerging business opportunities. Most of the work involves research, analysis and reporting on emerging trends and business opportunities that will impact IBM in the future.

Bill joined IBM in 1981 as a Systems Engineer and worked in Customer Sales and Management positions until the early 1990’s at which time he transitioned over to a Marketing/Strategy career.

The last ten years, Bill has devoted much of his time to helping IBMers understand potential futures. Bill created and still leads an internal IBM community called HorizonWatch. The 1900 member community is interested in understanding future customer business issues/trends and how that might translate into growth opportunities for IBM. Bill supports the community with a daily blog, conference calls, and community jam sessions.

Bill also works off normal work hours on building a community called HorizonWatching (with presence on LinkedIn, Twitter, Tumblr, Typepad, and Facebook).

Bill has a Masters of Management (1995) with a concentration in marketing and management strategy from Kellogg Graduate School of Management (Evanston, Illinois. Bill is an avid golfer, enjoys fitness activities, and reading. He lives with his wife, his two daughters, and the family dog in Palatine, Illinois.

Note: Bill's comments/discussions here are his own views and not necessarily the views of of IBM.
