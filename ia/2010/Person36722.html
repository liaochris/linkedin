Christine Hertzog’s Summary

The Smart Grid is more than just a bi-directional electricity and communications network. It is a dynamic convergence of new and disruptive technologies, multiple regulatory and standards entities, and new business entrants.

Christine Hertzog is a consultant, author, and a professional explainer focused on the Smart Grid business sector. As a consultant and advisor, she helps clients understand the Smart Grid, identify opportunities, and build strategies for their solutions in this high-growth sector. She is also the author of the Smart Grid Dictionary, the first and only dictionary focused on the Smart Grid that explains the jargon, acronyms, and terminology used by utilities, regulators, standards organizations, manufacturers, and academic institutions.

Christine is a frequent presenter at Smart Grid industry conferences and events, and participates in a number of Smart Grid industry activities, including NIST’s Cyber Security privacy sub-group standards recommendations, and NAESB’s Priority Action Plans 3, 4, 9, and 10. She also works with organizations to promote innovative solutions and companies in Silicon Valley.

She has a Master of Science degree in Telecommunications from the University of Colorado-Boulder, and two decades of experience working with hardware, software, and services companies that ranged from small start-ups, multi-national corporations, and electric utilities.
