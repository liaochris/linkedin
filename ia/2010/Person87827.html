James Taylor’s Summary

My expertise is market-oriented supply chain design, the oft-neglected 'P' of Marketing: Placement. In short, the "other" marketing.

The decisions I can help you make include:

-- Which customers want which products, and which of those products are you best suited to provide and from where?

-- For those customer and product combinations, do you need more production capacity, supply, or inventory? If so, how much and where?

-- What are the customers' service expectations, and what combination and placement of production, inventory, and distribution do you need to meet them?

-- How do customers compare your offering to competitors', and where can you improve? Will they reward you with more business or higher prices?

While strategic decisions are high level, good supply chain design is a very data-intensive process. As an MIT graduate and former software engineer, I am comfortable with the large modeling tools, databases, and complex spreadsheet analysis that is necessary.
