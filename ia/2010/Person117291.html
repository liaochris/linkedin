  • VP Marketing

    Lost Highway Records / Universal Music Group

    (Music industry)

    November 1999 — May 2002 (2 years 7 months)

    Launch of new record label, a joint venture of Mercury Records and Island/Def-Jam. Roster included primarily singer-songwriters who fit somewhere in between Rock, Alternative, and Country music. Responsibilities included all Marketing functions: overall marketing plans and product marketing management, new media development (website designs, EBRC, intranet), placement and design oversight of advertising and solicitation materials, design and manufacture of merchandising tools, budgeting funds, international marketing liaison, 3rd party promotions and marketing campaigns, overseeing (digital) legal compliance. Created first online street team at a Nashville label; created and directed marketing campaign for O Brother, Where Art Thou?. First release: OBWAT soundtrack in December 2000 (over 8 million sold), label then released 7 albums in 4 months for 5 million units sales, Whiskeytown’s Pnuemonia, Lucinda Williams’ Essence, the Down From the Mountain soundtrack, Robert Earl Keen’s Gravitational Forces, Ryan Adams’ Gold, the Hank Williams tribute Timeless, featuring Dylan, Sheryl Crow, Keith Richards, Johnny Cash, Beck, Emmylou, etc. In its first year, LH garnered 16 Grammy nominations in 12 categories, winning 7 of them including overall Best Album for O Brother, Where Art Thou?
