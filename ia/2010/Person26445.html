Brad Duea’s Summary

Brad Duea is a bright, well-educated, hard-working technology executive with a proven ability to close high-impact deals (i.e., large sales to strategic customers, strategic partnerships, joint ventures, financings, mergers and acquisitions--and related integrations). Brad is action-oriented, thrives on achieving big goals and has a proven ability to deliver strategic growth (i.e., revenues, new markets, new territories). Brad is an enthusiastic leader and communicator who has built strength-based teams around the globe.

Brad currently serves as a member of the Board of Directors of DTS, Inc. (NASDAQ: DTSI), a digital technology company dedicated to delivering the ultimate entertainment experience.

Brad most recently served as president of Napster LLC, the pioneer in digital music and a wholly owned subsidiary of Best Buy, Inc. (NYSE: BBY) since 2008. Brad served as president of Napster from January 2004 to February 2010. Under Brad’s leadership from January 2004 to October 2008 (when Napster was acquired by Best Buy), Napster’s total revenues increased from less than $12 million to well over $120 million, international revenues increased from zero to over $20 million, mobile revenues increased from zero to over $10 million, and subscribers increased over 2,500%.

Prior to Napster, Brad served as Vice President of Worldwide Business Development and Worldwide OEM Sales for Roxio, Inc., the digital media software company, now a division of Sonic Solutions (NASDAQ: SNIC). Prior to joining Roxio, Brad was Vice President of Corporate Development for PeopleSupport, Inc., a leading provider of integrated e-customer relationship management solutions, and a corporate lawyer with O’Melveny & Myers LLP.

Brad is a member of the State Bar of California and an officer and Executive Committee member of the Santa Monica Bay Chapter of the Young Presidents’ Organization.
