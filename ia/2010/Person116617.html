Kimberly Thomas’s Summary

As a recent law school graduate and candidate for the July 2008 bar, my inspiration for attending law school was the hands-on experience I gained from mentoring with Justice Clarence Thomas of the U.S. Supreme Court and working with the facets of constitutional law and the judicial process. In college I developed a passion for real estate transactions and in developing my career goals, I gathered as much information as I could about buying commercial property and mortgage loan acquisitions in working with SN Servicing Corporation. As you can see from my enclosed resume, I worked in real estate development prior to law school and have been working with a mortgage loan acquisition company for the duration of my law school career. This experience has given me insights into the realm of foreclosure, contracts, bankruptcy litigation, real estate development, landlord/tenant law, and asset acquisitions. Although I have a vast background in law, I have never wanted to be limited to its practice and I hope to use this knowledge in the practice of any industry as necessary.

My prior experience in federal grant writing as a Program Assistant for Southern University’s Agricultural and Research Center gave me numerous opportunities to build clear and concise writing techniques which have proven to be valuable practical skills utilized in assisting with litigation. In working with HRI Properties, I maintained as the hub of various corporate ventures which included working in Accounting, Human Resources, and as a Special Projects Coordinator for redevelopment projects. This allowed me to demonstrate my great ability to multi-task to better the company as a whole in its beginning stages of project development.
