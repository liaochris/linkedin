Rhonda Peters’s Summary

Rhonda completed a Ph.D. (2007) in energy engineering, specialized in wind energy with a minor in public administration/political science. She has lived in India, owned a retail store specialized in sustainable/recycled/renewable/fair trade products, taught electrical engineering and mathematics courses, and worked as a design engineer in the aerospace industry. She currently works as a grid interconnection and transmission engineer at Clipper Windpower Development. Co. in the Santa Barbara area and is committed to technologies and personal choices that allow people to live in greater harmony/balance with the environment and with each other.

  • Research Assistant

    Energy & Environmental Research Center

    (Educational Institution; 201-500 employees; Research industry)

    February 2005 — August 2007 (2 years 7 months)

    The Wind-to-Hydrogen project explored the possiblitiy of using hydrogen created by wind energy to displace transportation fuel, and included construction of the first hydrogen fueling station in the state of North Dakota. Rhonda programmed a small scale SCADA system (using Power Measurement's ION Enterprise) to dynamically schedule and monitor a 150 kW electrolyzer to create hydrogen for on-site storage and dispensing using remote wind energy. Hydrogen production can be based on the output of any combination or permutation of 3 wind farm sites in Basin Electric Power Cooperative's control area, using one of several control algorithms. Several Chevy 1/2 ton pickups were converted to run on the created hydrogen (by combustion) as well as E-85, in addition to gasoline for demonstration purposes.
