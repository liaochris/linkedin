Geoffrey Groesbeck’s Summary

I work at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology on international development initiatives. These engagements share a focus on complex initiatives with multiple stakeholders, are activity engaged in resource-constrained regions, and are often driven by entrepreneurial interest in social ventures.

My work encompasses the expansion of existing programs and creation of new ones that provide tangible assistance to the bottom of the pyramid. I establish contacts, help define and communicate local needs, find appropriate solutions, transfer and disseminate technologies (often mobile ones), and work to ensure these initiatives’ sustainability and scalability. I also identify and maintain key relationships with in-country and NGO partners.

I also am the institutional relations officer and occasional class lecturer for NextLab, addressing complex communications and logistics needs through the introduction of innovative mobile-based solutions in Mexico and the Caribbean. I articulate the value of proposed solutions, determine project selections and deployments, and assist in directing long-term engagements.

Previously, I was executive director of international affairs and visiting professor at Mexico’s ITESM at its Zacatecas campus. My work focused on rural poverty reduction, migration alleviation and social development enablers impelled by mobile technology. I was project partner for a groundbreaking project, Proyecto Zaca, which promotes fair trade practices and pricing transparency mechanisms in agricultural communities. I also established a consortium of universities to collaborate on sustainable development projects in Bolivia, Ecuador, Guatemala, and Mexico.

My background includes strategic communications positions with the world’s largest global asset management company (State Street Global Advisors), a Harvard-affiliated international management consulting firm (CMI International Group) and a leading online data analysis firm (NetGenesis Corporation).
