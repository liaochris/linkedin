Rob van der Veer’s Summary

In 1985, just before my Computer Science study at the University of Twente, I started developing software commercially. In 1991 I joined Sentient Machine Research and created the DataDetective software for my thesis project; since then the company's main datamining product. Through the years, I have been involved in many different R&D and application projects in the field of data mining, as engineer, manager and consultant.

In 2002, I made the data mining department independent from SMR and founded Sentient together with Marten den Uyl and Pieter Bison. My new role was that of CEO. We managed to successfully continue and expand the business.

Apart from all this, I have founded and help develop the online gaming community center www.clanbase.com since 1998, together with two partners. In 2005 ClanBase had 1.2 million users in 120 countries and today still is the world's leading online gaming competition organisation. The ClanBase organisation consists of around 400 volunteers from more than 20 countries.
In 2005 we sold ClanBase to the US-based Global Gaming League, where I am now member of the advisory board.
