  • Process Engineer/Project Manager

  • Process Engineer/Project Manager

    Earth Tech/AECOM

    (Privately Held; Environmental Services industry)

    September 1996 — August 1998 (2 years )

    At the start of my employment, Earth Tech was a publically traded international consulting firm and was involved in a diverse range of environmental, engineering and architectural projects. Earth Tech later became a subsidiary of Tyco International and is now part of AECOM, which is headquartered in Los Angeles, California. My employment at Earth Tech was divided into two time periods (as listed separately), both of which included process design, project management and business development activities.

    During my first term, I had the opportunity to work with a unique group of engineers, scientists and other professionals in the process design group providing design services for a large groundwater treatment project in Sacramento, California and evaluation services for several contract operation wastewater treatment facilities in Michigan.

    Although details are understood to remain confidential, the groundwater treatment project was truly a once-in-a-career opportunity to evaluate and design a biological treatment system that could successfully reduce contaminant concentrations in a potable water aquifer, included the following:
    • review of pilot-scale performance data,
    • evaluation of commercially-available treatment equipment,
    • development of process flow and control schematics,
    • layout of mechanical equipment and overall facility,
    • hydraulic design including pump and piping selection,
    • chemical storage and feed system design, and
    • preparation of bidding documents and opinions of probable construction cost.

    Support services for contract operation facilities included process evaluation for both municipal and industrial treatment plants, evaluation of alternate/replacement equipment and design for system improvements.

    During this time I also provided business development support to the marketing group in preparing scope-of-work write-ups, project budgets and schedule outlines for proposals.

  • Staff/Project Engineer

    McNamee, Porter & Seeley/Tetra Tech

    (Public Company; TTEK; Civil Engineering industry)

    May 1985 — May 1995 (10 years 1 month)

    During my employment, MPS was a privately-held consulting engineering firm, primarily involved in municipal and public works projects, and staffed with an incredible group of engineers, surveyors, scientists and professionals. MPS is now part of the Tetra Tech organization with corporate headquarters in Pasadena, California. My experience at MPS spanned a wide range of civil engineering practice including formal EIT mentoring and Professional Engineer development.

    During my first four years, I was trained through a formal EIT mentoring program that included design review, construction observation and limited original design.
    • The design review work included calculation checks, layout and dimensioning verification, shop drawing review and construction document processing.
    • Construction observation included on-site observation of watermain, sanitary sewer, water treatment plant, wastewater treatment plant and road construction projects.
    • Original design was limited to small watermain, sanitary sewer and treatment plant projects

    After professional licensing, my responsibilities expanded to include independent design and project management.
    • During this professional development period, I provided planning and design services for both water and wastewater treatment plants including site layout, process evaluations, equipment selection, hydraulic design for pump and piping systems, chemical feed system design, process instrumentation schematics, specification writing, and construction permit applications.
    • I also provided municipal consulting services including site plan review, utility system mapping and master planning, development of municipal construction standards, permitting assistance for water and wastewater projects, observation of utility repairs/replacement, road resurfacing, bridge deck replacement, and budgeting support for city personnel.
