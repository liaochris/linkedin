Michael Langlois’s Summary

Mike has been working with adults, children and adolescents for nearly 15 years as a psychotherapist. He has had extensive experience working with individuals and groups around the following issues:

* Parenting Conflicts with Adolescents
* School-based behavioural Issues/Learning Disabilities
* Adolescent Depression
* Substance Abuse
* Couples Counselling
* Adoptive/Blended Families
* Sexual Orientation
* Child Bereavement
* Anxiety Disorders
* Trauma

In addition to his clinical work, Mike has been a consultant within and to several school systems, identifying and serving children with Special Needs, including development of Individualized Education Plans and accommodations. He evaluates and helps to develop functional behavior assessments and plans for children and adolescents who need extra support and understanding in order to make effective progress in their public school environment.

Mike serves as a resource for educators and families on Gay/Lesbian/Bisexual/Transgender awareness and safety, as well as other issues of diversity, including race, cultural awareness, and non-traditional families. In 2006 Mike was appointed by the State of Massachusetts to the Massachusetts Commission for Gay, Lesbian, Bisexual and Transgender Youth. He recently serves in an advisory capacity to the National Association of Social Workers on issues including youth suicide prevention.

Mike provides private supervision for psychotherapists as well. This includes psychodynamically-oriented clinical supervision, as well as mentoring individuals who are seeking to start, grow, and market their own private practice.

Mike is currently researching and teaching online technologies and clinical theory and practice. In particular he is focusing on the use of online gaming from a self-psychological and object relations perspective; as well as the impact of social networks on interpersonal relationships.

Michael Langlois’s Specialties:

Clinical Social Work, Special Education, IDEA, use of online gaming and technology in psychotherapy.
