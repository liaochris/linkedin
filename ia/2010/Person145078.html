Mike Billeter’s Summary

Since becoming a part of the marketing and advertising industry, I've used a strong writing skill set to create content that ranges from marketing/advertising/PR-related blog posts to dynamic website copy for clients and internal projects. Along with creative and exciting copywriting, I can also shift my writing style in order to write for more formal efforts such as client proposals, personal letters, and research summaries. I've also written copy for email blasts and, during my time in politics, I gained experience in recording, editing, and uploading video content.

Along with writing and content creation, I've gained valuable research experience from my time spent at Deep Bench. Whether researching and drafting creative client proposals for potential clients or conducting research for new facets of current client projects, I've steadily strengthened my research skill set over the years.

Finally, as a part of Deep Bench, I've been heavily invested in the social media and online marketing facet of marketing/advertising/PR for over two years. From social media account creation and maintenance to self-created blog content and integration into overall marketing plans, my experience in social media has been extremely beneficial.

Overall, my goals include the opportunity to write and create content for exciting efforts and projects in any form or fashion, whether they come from client work, internal work, or my personal life. While I don't aspire to work for any specific agency or even industry, my goal is to be both an employee and a trusted adviser/instructor who does more than write a paragraph for a website. I hope to take a company or client's mission and make it so dynamic that it inspires others to feel as passionate as I feel when I write the copy.

And if I someday make my way into the comic industry, I won't be upset. But that's for a later day.

Mike Billeter’s Specialties:

Mike Billeter’s Experience

  • Content Creator and Curator

    The OTA Sessions

    (Events Services industry)

    September 2009 — April 2010 (8 months)

    In order to bring the OTA Sessions into existence, I worked closely with Hugh Weber (the event's founder) to create and manage content for the various OTA Sessions web presences. From the actual OTA Sessions website to its social networking outlets, as well as content such as press releases to be released to the media, my responsibilities were focused on producing precision work and fast results.   • Director of Content - Copywriter/Online Content Curator

    Deep Bench

    (Privately Held; Public Relations and Communications industry)

    November 2008 — April 2010 (1 year 6 months)

    As the Director of Content, I focused on various duties and responsibilities within Deep Bench. My main responsibilities hinged on my strong skill set in copywriting and web content creation. Coupled with the responsibility of managing Deep Bench's online presence and online content, this led to a constant need to strengthen and develop my marketing knowledge and social media aptitude.

    In the realm of internal organization, I served as the primary correspondence contact for Deep Bench, I planned and participated in the development and presentation of project pitches, and I served as the critical editor of proposals, executive summaries, and other written projects.

    Regarding client interaction and engagement, I outlined requirements and expectations from client requests, I trained clients on social networking tools like Twitter, Facebook, and other emerging online media tools, I created and maintained a majority of the Deep Bench blog content as well as created a content schedule for both Deep Bench and its clients, and I facilitated the execution of strategic creative projects.

    My other duties at Deep Bench included being generally awesome, maintaining a positive work atmosphere with stellar music selections, and quoting great shows like Arrested Development when appropriate.

  • New Media Director

    (Political Organization industry)

    May 2008 — November 2008 (7 months)

    As the New Media Director with Chris Lien for Congress, I kept website media updated, wrote the campaign blog posts, wrote and sent campaign e-mail updates, filmed and uploaded video clips, scoured the blogosphere and social networks for campaign news, and worked to create and maintain a healthy, positive work atmosphere. I also traveled to various events and functions including fundraisers, dinners, parades, and anywhere else Chris could be represented.
