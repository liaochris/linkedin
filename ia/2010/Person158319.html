Brandon N. Owens’s Summary

Brandon Owens is an expert in renewable energy market forecasting and analytics. He has over 15 years of experience assessing global renewable energy markets, technologies, and policies. He is also an accomplished energy modeler, computer programmer, and technical writer. Over the course of his career, Brandon has developed a wide array of energy, economic, and environmental models to assess global energy market dynamics. He has also provided strategic recommendations to both government agencies and global energy companies.

Brandon is currently Manager of Strategic Analysis & Scenario Planning at GE Energy. Prior to joining GE Energy, Mr. Owens was Director of Research at Cambridge Energy Research Associates. Earlier in his career, he served as Senior Analyst for the National Renewable Energy Laboratory. His research has been published in industry leading journals such as Public Utilities Fortnightly, Energy Policy, and Research Evaluation. He has been a keynote speaker, an expert witness, and has appeared in periodicals such as the New York Times, the Wall Street Journal and the USA Today. Mr. Owens holds a Master of Science degree in Mineral Economics from the Colorado School of Mines, and a Bachelor of Arts degree in Mathematics and Economics from the University of Colorado at Boulder.
