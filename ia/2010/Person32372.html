Carl Jacobson’s Summary

My purpose in life is to help people create music and also make a living with their music. At the age of 16, this is what I set out to do with my life and I can't imagine a better job. During my 13 years at Cakewalk, I was able do just that. Through the process I have been blessed to work with some amazing people and companies as a result, including some of my musical idols and the world's leading technology providers.

My work with Cakewalk was spotlighted in Kevin Roberts' book "The Lovemarks Effect" and in 2009 I was invited to speak during Renee James' Keynote at IDF because of the co-marketing relationship that I managed with Intel.

I'm not just a businessman who helps musicians, I am also a musician with formal training in composition from film composer Shawn Clement and a degree in music performance. I've achieved a few notable credits including original music in the Artisan Entertainment film Greenmail and I engineered the PianoWorks solo album by Igor Khoroshev of Yes. In 2007 I contributed to a benefit album for Kiva, a group who provides zero interest micro-loans to individuals in developing countries. Continuing to create music inspires my work in the music industry and helps keep it grounded.

My previous work includes launching the Soundsbig.com Internet broadcasting network, and positions with New World Records and A&M.

I am now considering new opportunities that can benefit from my wide range of talents while allowing me to continue my life's work of helping musicians.
