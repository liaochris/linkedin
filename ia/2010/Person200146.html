Taylor Cisco’s Summary

Rather than rehash my resume, I'll focus on my goals in this summary. I'd like to find a network on-air position, but I'm open to all opportunities so long as it's with a company in which I can establish roots and develop over the next two-three decades.

Where others may see an empty plate, I can find the meat. Whether short VO’s or long term assignments, I’ve proven that if a viable, original story is needed, I’m the one you can count on. At Twin Cities Live, I uncovered hidden gems in lighter fare such as complimentary Auto Brokers, floating B&B’s and handmade gelato. When more hard-hitting content was required, I detailed Dr. Mai Na Lee’s amazing journey from Laos to her comprehensive documentation of Hmong history. I also outlined the Boys & Girls Club’s unique mentorship campaign that targeted North Minneapolis barbershops. No matter what the situation warranted, given a deadline and theme, I hit the mark every time.

I’m naturally empathetic and well traveled. I’ve studied etiquette and theology and I’m quite comfortable in numerous surroundings and personality types. On CrimeWatch interviews varied between Law Enforcement Officers, victims, politicians and community activists however, even across that broad spectrum, I was able to connect with every subject on a human level, and use that connection to get to a deeper level of the story.

I’m old-fashioned and don’t believe that the job is done until it’s done. In the fast paced environment of the newsroom, I have a knack for prioritization and organization. My turnarounds are quick, my research is thorough and my deadlines are never missed. In every position I’ve ever held, I’ve been the first one in the office, and last one out. When it comes to dealing with the inevitable hurdles or longer assignments, I’m not one to crack under pressure-quite the opposite, actually. I never caught a break I didn’t make and I intend to keep working until all objectives are met and my goals are achieved.
