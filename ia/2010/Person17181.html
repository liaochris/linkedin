Amy Sausser’s Summary

The work of nonprofits and the caring people who run them is a critical part of a functioning, healthy society and democracy. All too often their stories go unheard and, worse yet, the needs of the people they serve go unnoticed.
It doesn’t have to be this way. Strong, clear, audience-focused communications can help attract funders, alter perceptions and behaviors, produce sound public policy and change lives.

Do you need help:
* Figuring out who to talk to, and how?
* Developing materials that tell your story?

I have worked with and for nonprofits and foundations since 1991. I bring an understanding of the challenges that face community groups and have hands-on experience crafting effective and economical solutions.

  • Owner

    Amy Sausser Consulting

    October 2008 — Present (1 year 9 months)

    Since 1991, I have worked with a variety of nonprofits, specializing in communications.

    Most recently I served as Director of Communications at Southern California Grantmakers and before that was Deputy Director of Development at the Liberty Hill Foundation.

    I have also served as the Managing Editor of the California Association of Nonprofits’ Alert newsletter and in the early 90s I started the employment newsletter Opportunity NOCs (now known as Nonprofit Directions) and became the Manager of Communications at the Center for Nonprofit Management.
