Danese Cooper’s Summary

In January 2009, I joined my first startup, REvolution Computing, as an advisor on Open Source issues. The company underwent massive restructuring and a change in direction in mid-October and I find myself looking for a new position.

Joined Intel in March 2005 as Sr. Director of Open Source Strategy and traveled extensively to evangelize Open Source, mostly in the Developing World. It was fascinating to see Open Source grow.

Previous to that, spent 6 years at Sun involved in developing and evangelizing the open source movement and methodology, which resulting in release of some very key code (while I was at Sun) under Free and Open licenses (including OpenOffice.org, JXTA.org, NetBeans.org, GridEngine.org) and a growing interest/participation in the Social Software movement, including implementation and launch of java.net and blogs.sun.com.

Also co-editor of the book, Open Sources v2.0, from O'Reilly Media.

I believe that the Open Source Methodology of increased transparency in software development (and possibly also in hardware development) can only have a positive effect on the technology we deliver to customers. The marriage of Open Source to traditional software development has created some interesting hybrid (part Open part Proprietary) models which I believe hold up favorably against entirely traditional methods in both time to market and satisfaction of market demand.

Previously I have held long-term positions as Chief Open Source Evangelist at Sun, Software Engineering Manager & Engineering Program Manager at Apple, Project Program Manager and QA Manager on projects also at Apple and later at Symantec (where I was lucky to work with a great management team on an experiment with extreme programming methods that led naturally to Open Source).
