Kathleen Jordan PhD’s Summary

Kathy is an expert business and personal coach who specializes in helping women over 50 make satisfying personal and career changes. Her research-based approach views life or career change as a fundamentally creative process, and coaching engagements focus on developing and applying 7 core creative skills used by individuals who make successful life transitions. Her book with co-author Fred Mandell "Becoming a Life Change Artist: 7 Creative Skills to Reinvent Your Life at Any Stage" will be published in August.

Kathy is also a seasoned organization development consultant with deep expertise in planning the human resources implementation of major strategic changes. In her work with organizations, she integrates her background as psychologist, senior manager, training designer, and organization development specialist to help managers and professionals reach and exceed their goals. Kathy is known for her inventive and practical approaches to performance enhancement. She is co-developer of two ground-breaking programs "The Leaders' Studio" and "The Innovation Studio" which strengthen the innovative capacity of leaders and teams.

As a business editor and and content developer, Kathy is highly regarded for her ability to translate complex concepts into inspiring, actionable ideas for busy managers.

  • Author

    Discovery CCR

    (Professional Training & Coaching industry)

    2010 — Present (less than a year)

    My co-author Fred Mandell and I wrote "Becoming a Life Change Artist: 7 Creative Skills to Reinvent Your Life at Any Stage" to share what we have learned about how people make successful life changes. It will be released in August 2003.

    Endorsement by Daniel Pink, author of "A Whole New Mind":
    "Fred Mandell and Kathleen Jordan have created a book that manages to be exceedingly smart and enormously hopeful at the same time. With personal stories that will stir your heart and exercises that will strengthen your skills…it can help anyone create a more meaningful future."

    Summary:
    “Leonardo da Vinci, Monet, Picasso, Berthe Morisot—these are some of the most creative thinkers in history. What do these artists have in common with people in the midst of a major life transition? The skills they used to produce their masterpieces are the same skills required to make successful shifts—whether it’s finding a new career or a new purpose or calling in life. In Becoming a Life Change Artist, consultant, artist, and creative catalyst, Fred Mandell, Ph.D. and Kathleen Jordan, Ph.D., a psychologist, coach, and leading authority on organizational change, share the groundbreaking approach made popular in their workshops across the country. There are seven key competencies that the most creative minds of history shared, and that anyone rethinking their future can cultivate to change their life effectively. They are:
    • Preparing the brain to undertake creative work
    • Seeing the world and one’s life from new perspectives
    • Using context to understand the facets of one’s life
    • Embracing uncertainty
    • Taking risks
    • Applying discipline
    • Collaborating
    With inspirational stories and targeted exercises throughout, this is a book for all ages and stages—for those facing an uncertain job market through midlifers and beyond who are about to embark on their next chapter.”
