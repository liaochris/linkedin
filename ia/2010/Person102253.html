John Miller’s Summary

John Miller has worked in Television for over twenty years. While in college Miller created shows for local cable channels which secured his first job as a commercial producer for a CBS affiliate.
5 years later, as Director of Development for Foxlab, he created, developed and produced the reality show, “Trauma Center,” television’s first ever hospital based reality program.

Miller developed, produced and oversaw projects that resulted in some of the highest rated series in MTV’s history, including “The Tom Green Show,” Making the Band”, and “Jackass”. Miller’s programming played an important role in establishing MTV’s successful “Ten Spot” timeslot branding.

He also served as Exec. in Charge of such ongoing series as “The Real World,” and “Road Rules” for over eight years.

As Executive Vice President of Original Series and Animation, MTV, Mr. Miller managed staff, development and current for two major divisions. Miller also helped plan day-to-day strategy and creative direction of the channel as a member of MTV’s senior council.

Recently, Miller held overall deals with Telepictures Productions and Touchstone Television and independently directed and produced 3 television presentations with a few cameras, a powerful Mac and gallons of coffee.

He is currently the EVP of TV for The Weinstein Company.
