Tudor Chiuariu’s Summary

I began my professional career as an attorney at law in 2000. I practiced corporate law, real estate, project finance and tendering, commercial arbitration and dispute resolution. At the same time, I started an academic career as a junior lecturer in several fields of public law: administrative law, constitutional law, EU law and policies. In 2004 I started a PhD in criminal law, having as research topic the procedural safeguards of the accused in a criminal trial.

My legal practice in project finance and tendering and my academic background proved very useful when I began a career in the public service. In January 2005 I was appointed State Secretary in the Prime-Minister’s Chancellery, Head of the Fight against Fraud Department – DLAF. The Department’s pro-active approach and effectiveness have gained the support and appreciation of the European Anti-fraud Office – OLAF. I have overseen the implementation of a National Antifraud Strategy, drafted with the technical assistance of SIGMA – OECD, which led to the establishment of integrated antifraud system, later on appreciated by OLAF as a model for other candidate countries and EU member states.

In April 2007, I became Minister of Justice. My priorities in office aimed at continuing the reform of the judicial system and fulfilling the obligations that Romania had undertaken during the EU accession negotiations, most importantly finalizing the drafting of new civil and criminal codes. Of particular importance was the adoption in Parliament of the law establishing the National Integrity Agency. The law was passed in May 2007 and set-up an independent administrative authority responsible for the control of wealth, conflict of interests, incompatibilities of elected or appointed public officials. Other initiatives related to the amendment of the Citizenship Law, the conclusion of a new Extradition Treaty with the US and implementing the system of liaison magistrates with other EU member states.
