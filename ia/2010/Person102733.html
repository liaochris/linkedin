John N.A. Brown’s Summary

Designed, built and tested a truly ergonomic patent-pending replacement for the computer mouse.

Designed and took to market a system for interactive postural feedback.

Designed a handheld audiovisual device for remote assistance in rehabilitative exercise.

Designed, built and tested a patent-pending replacement for the pen or stylus.

Wrote a full-body kinetic and kinematic model for vicon motion capture.

Designed built and tested a Modified Fitts Law and Steering Test.

Award-winning research from two of Canada's Networks of Centres of Excellence: MITACS (mathematics of information technology and complex systems) and AUTO21 (the automobile in the 21st century).

Presented at more than a dozen academic and professional conferences.

Reviewer for two international conferences.

Wrote and taught senior undergraduate courses in Visual Storytelling and Computer Animation.

Produced the Small Arms Aide Memoire multimedia package for the Armed Forces.

Wrote and drew syndicated comic strips and editorial cartoons.

Directed award-winning animated public service announcement.

I am hoping to license my inventions for production. Foremost among these is a replacement for the computer mouse that has undergone user-based kinematic and performance trials showing it to be both accurate and truly ergonomic. The details were published in the journal Ergonomics (50, 2, 2007).

John N.A. Brown’s Specialties:

Workplace ergonomics and biomechanics; motion capture; problem solving; experimental design; user-interface design; 2D and 3D illustration; public speaking and training; project management.
