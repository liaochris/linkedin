  • Red Cross Nordic United World College

    2004 — 2006

    A particularly interesting project I did in RCNUWC was called Snowshoveler. By chance, the physics classroom, which already had a digital whiteboard, received 30 Windows Tablet PCs. A friend and I built a system in C# that synchronized the digital whiteboard's contents to an application on the tablets. Students then could add their own notes on top of the teacher's.

    Snowshoveler was particularly popular with students who had poor English skills. Since the application recorded the teacher's notes for these students, they could focus on listening to the teacher's lecture. Many of them also wrote down terms and definitions in their native languages next to the teacher's definitions, which helped them a lot with their studying.

    More about Snowshoveler: http://www.rcnuwc.uwc.org/snowshoveler
