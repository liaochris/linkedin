Matthew Lindner’s Summary

I am an experienced communicator who has significant experience handling multiple projects under the tightest of deadlines. As a longtime journalist and current national freelance sports writer, I know how news organizations work and how to craft a pitch that will secure coverage for an organization. I am an accomplished writer who can go from a crisis situation to a lighthearted one at the drop of a hat.

My pitches produce results both on the local and national level. Since starting for ESPN.com back in May, I've become a consistent contributor to the site's Page 2 section, placing stories on everything from indoor football players to peanut butter cheeseburgers. Two years in a row, I've directed coverage of the Midwest League All-Star Game for MLB.com, coming up with a wide range of story ideas designed to promote the league and its players in a very short period of time. Those I've worked with will tell you that I don't have an off button, that I'm always working and always producing results.

I'm also an experienced marketer who knows how to appeal to diverse audiences. In college, I designed and distributed marketing materials that contributed to a 15% increase in fraternity and sorority recruitment numbers the following school year. I've also helped to establish brand identity for a brand new mascot in a market that's crowded with them. My efforts helped to increase exposure for both the mascot and the organization representing it, breaking through the clutter to become a familiar presence on the local sports scene. Hire me and you'll get someone with a wide range of professional experience and the know how to adapt it to grow your organization.
