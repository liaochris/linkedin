Ted Fujimoto’s Summary

Ted Fujimoto, is an experienced entrepreneur and business development executive.

Most recently, Ted co-founded and launched CharterSafe/PrivateSafe, a new insurance carrier/captive that provides insurance to more than 300 private independent schools and charter schools in the U.S. It is backed by the Walton Family Foundation, Arthur J. Gallagher, and Travelers.

Previously, Ted was senior vice president of insurance product development and vice president of services and products for California Charter Schools Association. He is an experienced problem-solver, out-of-the-box thinker, strategist and professional entrepreneur. He founded and operated, for eleven years, a management and technology consulting practice that served a range of customers, including AirTouch Communications, Bank One, Chandon Estates, California Chamber of Commerce, Far Niente Winery, GM, IBM, New York Times, and Remy Martin.

Ted co-founded two for-profit companies that received $15 million in venture capital funding and has helped numerous organizations that collectively raised over $75 million.

As a community business leader, Ted helped design and found the highly regarded Napa New Technology High School and New Technology Foundation, which has built more than forty New Technology High Schools around the country. He oversaw grants from Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation and Carnegie Foundation grants for education reform initiatives in the Sacramento, CA, region.
Ted has served on the California State Board of Education's California Education Technology Advisory Committee, and he received the 2002 Center for Digital Government "In the Arena" award for education leadership in transforming vision to reality. In Converge Magazine's "1999 Year in Review," he was named one of "Education's Dreamers, Leaders and Innovators."
