David Neely, CPP’s Summary

Coordination of the Ontario Public Service Physical Operating Policy, threat risk assessments and Ministry of Finance and Ministry of Revenue security plans. The development and implementation of building access security services, training/awareness and preventative strategies. Development and implementation of emergency management plans and preventative physical security strategies to ensure staff safety, safeguard ministry assets and critical infrastructure and allow the delivery of time-critical business services. Liaison with Police, Security, Emergency Service organizations and Government(s) in support of intelligence and information sharing programs.

David Neely, CPP’s Experience

  • Security Services & Emergency Management

    Government of Ontario

    (Government Agency; Government Administration industry)

    March 2010 — Present (7 months)

    Mr. Neely is board certified as a Security Management Professional by the American Society for Industrial Security, having received the security profession’s highest recognition of practitioners, the Certified Protection Professional (CPP) designation.

    Mr. Neely’s distinguished career includes 26 years of service within the criminal justice sector serving both government and private clients. His duties have included corporate security program management, the introduction and operation of standards based security, investigative and intelligence services, the threat risk assessment of Government and Commercial facilities and the protection of prominent at risk individuals.

    Mr. Neely has attended specialized police training including: United States Secret Service, “Presidential Campaign Candidate/Nominee Protection” and is a graduate of the Canadian Police College, Strategic Intelligence Analysis program. He regularly advises organizations on counter terrorism and holds current government security clearances.

    Mr. Neely is a member of the American Society for Industrial Security, the International Association of Chiefs of Police and serves on the ASIS International Law Enforcement Liaison Council, the Fleming College, Security Risk Management Advisory Board and is a past member of the Toronto Association for Police and Private Security, Executive Board.
