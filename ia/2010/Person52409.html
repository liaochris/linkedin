  • Principal Software Engineer

    Kenetec

    (Consumer Goods industry)

    January 2001 — March 2001 (3 months)

    Placed in charge of leading the software design for a T1 IMA project. The product combined voice and data and used a T1 IMA as the uplink to attach to an ATM router. The Ficon IMA stack and the Inverness ATM stack had to be adapted to meet our internal objectives, such as voice priority. The operating system used was vxWorks. The voice and data use VBR and UBR connections over AAL0/5.

    Implemented an IP forwarding application. To increase data forwarding in our system, IP forwarding decisions were made in real time at the card level so incoming packets could be looked into and were not always run up through the vxWorks network stack. Once a packet was received, it was checked against the internal ARP table, and if a match was made, the necessary IP forwarding requirements per RFC 1812 were performed and the packet was immediately forwarded or dropped.

  • Principal Software Engineer

    Lucent Technologies

    (Public Company; LU; Telecommunications industry)

    September 1999 — November 2000 (1 year 3 months)

    Active member of the media gateway software team assigned to implement call control into our broadband access concentrator. The media gateway would reside at the edge of a packet based voice and data network. The project involved translating a physical Channel Associated Signaling CAS DS1/E1 port to a logical Q.931 ISDN layer, which was used for call control in the network. This translation allowed older legacy circuit switched equipment to connect to the newer packet based network. The task was run within the pSOS operating system in conjunction with Trillium’s ISDN software stack. The media is VOIP over ATM AAL2/5. A considerable amount of time was spent on documentation such as functional and requirement specifications.

  • Senior Software Engineer

    Ascend Communications

    (Public Company; ASND; Telecommunications industry)

    August 1998 — September 1999 (1 year 2 months)

    I was the project lead and software lead on the analog voice compression POTS development, coordinating the software, hardware and mechanical efforts. The card provided four analog FXS ports for the user to connect a standard telephones to, which were mapped onto the ATM network. At the other end of the network, they emerged as timeslots on our DS1/E1 voice compression card that was connected to an OPX PBX, thus extending the extensions of the local PBX over the wide area network. Used a Lucent/Infineon SLIC/CODEC front end and Audiocode’s DSP’s for voice compression.
