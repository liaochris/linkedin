Anne Szeto’s Summary

In 2008, Anne joined Amazon.com (with over $24 billion in revenue) as a Principal in the executive recruiting function. At Amazon, Anne owns a portfolio of global executive searches and while working closely with her clients, she drives research, candidate development and assessment, offer negotiation and onboarding processes.

From 2005 to 2008, Anne was a Consultant/Senior Associate at Chadick Ellig, a boutique executive search firm based in New York. She was responsible for full-cycle executive search execution of a variety of C-level assignments for financial services, consumer products, and business services clients. While managing relationships with her clients, Anne developed recruiting strategies that optimized candidate pipeline based on market conditions, client criteria and compensation/geographical constraints.

She previously spent six years at Highland Partners (formerly, TMP Worldwide) in various capacities, including Associate in the Financial Services sector, Director of Financial Services Research, and Investment Management Research Associate.

During her tenure at Highland Partners, Anne was a specialist in investment management, private wealth management, and alternative investment arenas. Previous experience includes being an Adjunct Professor in the Economics Department of Hunter College and a Research Assistant at the National Bureau of Economic Research (NBER).

Anne received her M.S. in Economics from Hunter College, her M.A. in Slavic Cultures and Languages from Columbia University, and her B.S. in Biochemistry and B.A. in Western History from the State University of New York at Stony Brook.
