Jeremy Polgar’s Summary

I recently graduated from Loyola Marymount University’s four-year Animation program where I worked as a freelance animator/illustrator for many clients and continue to work as a freelance animator to this day. In the course of my education and past work experiences, I have gained valuable knowledge in the pre-production/post-production process of 2D and 3D animation, as well as working with various medium-related computer applications and managing multiple tasks in high-pressure environments. When I interned for Buck LA, I was asked to create character designs for video game characters that would be featured in a commercial on G4TV. I made the designs and produced a test animation of one of the character’s run cycles in hopes that I would be granted the chance to animate that whole sequence. The director watched it, liked it and gave me a week and a half to finish the twelve seconds of completed, quality animation including the clean up and coloring of the characters and background. I managed my time correctly and completed the work before the deadline.

For the past year, I have been using my free time to complete my five-minute, traditionally animated film that I am eager to finish by this winter. For my film, Phaser Boogie Blast-Off! I wanted to tell a fun, simple and entertaining story that sells itself through its cast of unique and likeable characters that make the ordinary extraordinary. From this endeavor, I have developed an exceptional understanding of character motivation and acting. Former Disney animator, Sue Kroyer, has told me that my animation is unique, crisp, graphic, entertaining and appealing. Directing my own film and working with other animators has taught me how to responsibly give and receive direction. I am a very ambitious, self-driven team player who works very well with others.
