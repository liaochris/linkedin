Stephen Lawton’s Summary

Steve Lawton brings 20 years of private-sector business development experience, and eight years of experience in public administration, to the City of Hercules’ Economic Development function.

Previously, as Community Development Director from 2001 to 2008, he managed the Planning, Engineering and Building Inspection Divisions.

Among his current responsibilities is a public/private redevelopment partnership creating a 35-acre, mixed-use, transit-oriented district; the tenant lease-up of a 40,000 square foot street of retail shops; the disposition and development of a complementary retail “Main Street” district; and the attraction of a cluster of biotech firms.

Earlier, in public service and as Chair of the Planning Commission concluding in 2000, Mr. Lawton led the effort to create the Central Hercules Plan, which became the first form-based zoning code to be adopted into the law of a California city.

From 1982 through 2000, Mr. Lawton held management consulting and business development positions with private and public firms providing systems engineering, technology and business innovation services to global corporations. He advised senior managers at client firms including Goldman Sachs, Federal Express, Bank of America, HongkongBank, United States Postal Service and Burlington Northern Railroad.

Mr. Lawton earned a BA degree in Economics from the University of California, Berkeley in 1976.

Steve is a Founder of the Northern California Chapter of the Congress for the New Urbanism.

Steve is the Public Sector Chair of the Northern California Alliance Program of the International Council of Shopping Centers.

Steve is a member of the East Bay Programs Committee of the Urban Land Institute, SF District.

Steve is Chair of the Historic Landmarks Advisory Committee for the Contra Costa County Board of Supervisors, and a recent Director of the Contra Costa County Historical Society.
