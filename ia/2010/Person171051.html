Renata Joy’s Summary

Renata Joy
Biography

Renata Joy is a Daytime Emmy-nominated producer of news, talk and magazine format.

Her broadcasting career began as a disc jockey at WLLE radio station in Raleigh, North Carolina. Little did she know that her stint over the local airwaves would be the start of a multi-faceted career that would lead to her producing veteran journalist Barbara Walters at ABC’s Daytime show, The View.

Prior to signing up with “THE VIEW”, Renata Joy was involved with several successful television programs. Ms. Joy served as Executive Producer for PM Magazine, Charlotte, North Carolina’s version of the highly successful rated national entertainment magazine show. Under her supervision the show traveled extensively around the world, covering everything from the fall of the Berlin wall to Mardi Gras in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil.

Renata Joy joined NBC in 1990 and served as Senior Producer for the NBC Newschannel producing one of President Bill Clinton’s first town hall meetings.

By 1994 Ms Joy had moved over to NBC’s America’s Talking cable network where she developed and produced the “Ask E. Jean” show. NBC’s first live interactive talk show, where she provided vision, supervision of booking and talent, production and the execution of this daily hour long show. A short time later, Ms. Joy moved to the Fox Network where she was one of the producers of “Fox After Breakfast”. The show lasted two years during which she perfected her skill at producing celebrities.

In 1997 Renata Joy brought her more than 15 years of national broadcasting producing experience to ABC’s “THE VIEW” where she studio produced, booked guest and wrote scripts for this highly successful daytime show.

After leaving “THE VIEW” Ms. Joy worked on shows like “The Queen Latifah” show, “Entertainment Tonight” and NBC Weekend Nightly News”.

Ms Joy has also branched out to start her own small fitness business and is owner of Pure Joy Fitness
