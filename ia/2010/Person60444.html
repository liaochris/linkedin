Dario Susanj’s Summary

A former journalist and editor specialised in covering information and communications technology with almost 20 years of experience in the media and several books published; now a PR consultant specialised in managing public relations for technology companies with significant experience in public relations for airlines and the HoReCa industry.

I have helped shape and implement communications strategies for companies such as Microsoft, British Airways, Costa Coffee, and several telecoms in Croatia, including B.net and Tele2.

With my background in technology and extensive understanding of the media landscape in Croatia and in the region, I can help you run a successful public relations campaign in the media, including new media and new communication channels.

Dario Susanj’s Specialties:

Understanding of how information and communication technologies work, what the present trends and emerging technologies in the industry are. Deep understanding of airline and aerospace industries as well.

Excellent writing skills, organisational skills, leadership skills, great at multitasking. Very experienced in explaining complex technologies to casual users through writing.

Used to working in international teams across multiple time zones.

  • Editor-in-chief

    PC Chip Magazine

    (Privately Held; Media Production industry)

    September 2003 — August 2007 (4 years )

    My second term as the editor-in-chief at PC Chip Magazine, Croatia, where I have already been assistant editor-in-chief (Sept 1999 - Mar 2001) and editor-in-chief (Mar 2001 - Jun 2002) previously.

    My responsibilities have included much more than just being editor-in-chief. I wrote numerous articles, reviews, and columns for both the PC Chip magazine and Moj Moby, a telecommunications magazine.

    PC Chip was the second best-selling, but perhaps easily even the most influential Croatian ICT magazine within the industry at the time. Published each month on 200+ pages, it frequently went much further and deeper than any of the competing magazines, covering areas such as the business IT, trends and technologies, application of ICT in various industries, and so on. It has helped shaped the business side of IT and has also made a significant contribution to the development of the open source community in Croatia by having a regular section on Linux.

    Frequent interviews, cover stories and special reports have also made this magazine unique in Croatia at the time

    I have led a team of 50+ people at PC Chip. For my work here I have received the "Bozo Tezak" Award as the "Best ICT Media Editor" in Croatia, awarded by the Croatian Journalists' Association in 2005.

  • ICT journalist

    (self-employed)

    (Media Production industry)

    May 1988 — July 2007 (19 years 3 months)

    I wrote about Information Technology and Communications for various magazines and newspapers for many years. Starting way back when I was just 14, I wrote for magazines such as Svet kompjutera and Moj mikro, reviewing games at the time (1986).

    I was the news editor at the Croatian edition of the BYTE magazine and at Computerworld Croatia when both were launched in Croatia in early and late 1990s.

    I also wrote about ICT and its business applications for daily newspapers such as Večernji list and Vjesnik, business magazines such as Banka, Poslovni dnevnik, Eurotrend, and Business.hr.

    My work in the ICT trade media has included journalist and editorial positions in various magazines such as PC Chip, Moj Moby, Bug, Mreza, and the Croatian edition of T3.

    Some of my work in the more general media has included the regular section on useful websites in the Croatia Airlines' in-flight magazine "Croatia" for several years, and a regular section with computer tips and useful websites in the teen magazine Teen.

    During my career as a journalist, I've had more than 1700 articles published.
