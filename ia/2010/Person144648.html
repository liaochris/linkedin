Mick Flanigan’s Summary

Mick Flanigan currently is employed by California Institute of Technology as the Quality Assurance Officer for Advanced LIGO project. LIGO is an NSF funded project currently being upgraded to improve detection capabilities of gravitational waves. The goal of LIGO is to detect these waves and open exciting physics research. Mick will be ensuring suppliers of cutting edge materials and processes are meeting design specs for Advanced LIGO to achieve its goal of increased sensitivity to possible gravity waves.

Employed by Intel in 1996 as a Systems Engineer, Mick's focus initially was system maintenance, contractor and project management, but shortly became Intel lead Vibration Engineer. He led the effort to standardize the system across all Intel sites and setup corporate monitoring standards. Year over year the average savings for the corporation at the Oregon site alone was $5mil based on factory uptime, machine repair and replacement costs, reduction in lost product, lower headcount, and energy savings.

All Intel sites adopted the Predictive Maintenance and Acceptance Testing program. Mick proved this program helped Intel save money, create better system engineers and raise awareness with OEM's that quality design, materials and testing result in more reliable systems. He has been published and quoted in industry articles numerous times, and sought for his expertise in the area of next generation monitoring.

The inception of Low Power wireless systems provided a great opportunity to implement a low cost predictive machinery monitoring system. Mick subsequently took a position with Intel Research Sensor Networks for the development of the wireless sensing system. This work has led to a groundswell of wireless development activites in the industrial space, and Mick is proud to say he was one of the pioneers in deploying test systems, and looks forward to the day when machine learning, edge network processing, and intelligent controls are a commercial reality.
