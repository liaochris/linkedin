Scott Fossel’s Summary

Scott Fossel has pursued an interdisciplinary career in a variety of fields including property management, real estate investing, green building, urban planning, and the Internet, but has always thrived most at their convergence. He has owned and run his own multi-million dollar residential property management company in the California Bay Area since 1999, Fossel Investments. For three years he was Executive Director of the Green Century Institute, a non-profit think tank specializing in advanced green urban planning, and has spoken widely on the future of green building and green urbanism. In this regard, he most recently served on the Green Business and Clean Technology Advisory Groups for both the Edwards and Obama campaigns. For the last three years he has invested his visionary leadership in the launching of Dwellspring as its Founder and CEO. He attended St. Paul’s School, Haverford College, the Schumacher College of Ecological Studies in England, and holds degrees in Integral Studies and Philosophy from the California Institute of Integral Studies.

St. Paul’s School relies on its traditions to promote friendship, understanding, trust, and respect for those of all faiths and beliefs among its students from 35 states and 23 countries.

    St. Paul's School models and teaches a respect for self and others; for one's spiritual, physical, and emotional well-being; for the natural environment; and for service to a greater good.

    Inspired by its natural surroundings, St. Paul’s School promotes ecological literacy and exemplifies environmental stewardship, making stewardship a practice that remains with community members well beyond their time at this place of extraordinary natural beauty.
