Jonathan Jewell’s Summary

It has been said 'to understand the success of an operation, you must first understand the goal of that operation'. For Goldratt, 'The Goal’ of any business is about making money. But for charities and the Third Sector, this is necessary but not sufficient.

Chargé de mission means 'entrusted with a mission' and for you, that mission, in some form or another will - at its core - be down to two things:

1) Delivery on your charitable/non-profit objects
2) Moving to viability and excellence in business function

So what Chargé de mission does is not just provide a set of services, but to answer the question? What do you need to complete your mission?

We find out... we show you what to do... we will even help you to do it.

With over 14 years experience in charity work, I knowledge and skills of the sector and what works and doesn't.

Cdm Limited is a stable brand that says quality, integrity and evidence-based practice. I have worked at every level of organisation, in diverse fields doing what is necessary to get the job done and help others with common values to get theirs done too.

Chargé de mission is a UK Limited by Shares company that specialises in business recovery and renewal uniquely for the Third Sector.

We take a pragmatic approach, based on evidence and experience to solve your problems, and if we can’t solve them, we will find you someone else.

Working with the third sector, charities, independents, not-for-profits. We only work with the Public and Private Sectors to maximise income, services and talent for the good of the Third Sector.

Jonathan Jewell’s Specialties:

Moving your organisation from a non-viable future, through viability today to excellence tomorrow - viability, dependability and functionality.


Improving the 'wealth' of your beneficiaries, ensuring the resources you steward will be economically, efficiently, effectively, ethically and effortlessly transformed by management of inputs from donors, creditors, your organisation and its partners by simple, smart and evidenced interventions.
