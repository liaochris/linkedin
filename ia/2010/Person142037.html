Michael Barnathan’s Summary

Specializing in Data Mining / Machine Learning, Bioinformatics, Artificial Intelligence, Data Structures, and Discrete Math, I am both a programmer and a theoretical computer scientist. My ultimate objective is to make the world a better place through the acquisition and judicious use of my vision, knowledge, and skills. To that end, I recently completed a Ph. D. in Computer and Information Sciences at the Data Engineering Laboratory of Temple University.

My experience extends beyond what is listed in my profile; specifically, I have published a number of scholarly works that do not appear here. They are present in my resume, CV, and portfolio, however.

I am proficient in C++, Java, Visual Basic, C#, Perl, Prolog, Matlab, sh, SQL, (X)HTML, CSS, Javascript, and XML and have experience with DHTML and AJAX techniques. I also have extensive web development and system administration experience in both Windows and Linux. I'm also quite open (downright enthusiastic, in fact) to learning new skills.

I am quite adaptable and have a very broad range of interests and proficiencies both within and outside of the computer science field, primarily extending into pure mathematics, medicine, biology, music, art, and poetry. I constantly seek to broaden as well as deepen my knowledge and believe, contrary to most who pursue a research-focused education, that the quest for depth need not be a sacrifice of breadth. In fact, I hold such a strong belief that all knowledge is one that I have initiated the process of starting a university that capitalizes on the fusion that can be created from diverse disciplines - I call this Project Polymath.

I also taught the undergraduate and graduate data structures and algorithms courses at Monmouth University during the Fall 2008 and Spring 2009 semesters.
