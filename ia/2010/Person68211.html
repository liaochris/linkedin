Jan Altman ♫’s Summary

Did you ever try to learn a musical instrument and give up? Over 80% of us have. But our ground-breaking technology changes all that.

Piano Wizard is an amazingly simple video game that can teach anyone to play piano and read music instantly. (Imagine a young child playing Beethoven in about 10 minutes, and you get the picture.)

The secret lies in our simple 4-step method that is so easy, it allows 3-103 yr-olds to play a song in seconds. The first step is easy enough for a 3-yr-old, and by end of the fourth step, you're reading real music. Download songs off the web to build your own repertoire. The game is completely scalable for any age and level of musicianship (or lack thereof).

The game's innovation and simplicity have led to an outpouring of response, a number of awards, and some key strategic alliances, including those with:

• Disney: specially-branded Hannah Montana, High School Musical, & Camp Rock versions to be sold nationwide in 2009,
• Hal Leonard Corp: largest music publisher in world will help facilitate distribution, and give us license to their 5-digit library of songs
• Southern Illinois Univ: as of March 2009 they accepted Piano Wizard as part of their official college curriculum.

Also, live meetings with both Billy Joel and Yoko Ono in 2008 resulted in enthusiastic offers of help and support.

Guitar Wizard, coming mid-2009, picks up where Guitar Hero leaves off.
