Chris Geehan & Dan Byrne-McCullough’s Summary

HyperDuck Music Studios is a two man team, consisting of Chris Geehan & Dan Byrne-McCullough. These two are to be 2010 graduates of Newcastle Colleges BMus Honours Degree in Jazz, Commercial & Popular Music. Previous to this Degree they both studied in Belfast Metropolitan College on a Foundation Degree in Music Practice. Both left the course with triple distinctions, which is considered the highest mark attainable on that degree.

With at least 5 years of academic & practical music experience under their belts, they turned to production & recording some of the ideas they had. This led to a few small time projects on freeware games, namely Iji by Dan Remar (http://www.remar.se/daniel/iji.php). This was the initial project that sparked their interest in further music recording and production, namely audio for games.

Shortly after the success in the "Game Maker" program community, who can be found at http://gmc.yoyogames.com with Iji, the team named themselves HyperDuck, then HyperDuck Music, and finally settled on HyperDuck Music Studios.

After Iji, there was another freeware project called Katakijin, which explored a lot of progressive potentials in soundtrack writing for these two, and shortly after this, they took on three commercial projects: 'Zero Gear' by NimbleBit LLC (http://myzerogear.com) Microsoft Dream Build Play 2010 Entry 'A.R.E.S' by Extend Studio (http://ares.x10studio.com) and Microsoft Dream Build Play 2009 Winner 'Dust: An Elysian Tail'.

Zero Gear is released and available on steam, A.R.E.S. is preparing for a release in the next few months, and Dust is heavily under construction by the hand of Dean Dodrill, its creator, and the HyperDuck Music Studio team.

There are 6 or 7 other commercial projects on the plat of HyperDuck Music Studios currently, and they are constantly looking for more project opportunities. So should you find yourself looking for a unique and professional musical sound for your game, give them a shout.
