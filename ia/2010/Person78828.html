Gunnar Skogsholm’s Summary

Software Architect at Skogsholm Consulting. Founder of Couloir Software, software architect of School Assignment, Bachelors of Science in Electrical Engineering (BSEE) from Gannon University.

Special interests include Silverlight web applications, ASP.NET 3.5, Ajax toolkit, rapid C++ development, XML and real-time embedded control systems. I've been writing software since I was 14 years old. As a college lab assistant, I created a graphics software package that was used to create a new course on engineering graphics. I earned my Bachelors of Science in Electrical Engineering (BSEE) from Gannon University in 3 ½ years.

As an aircraft generator engineer at Sundstrand Corporation (part of United Technologies), I designed portions of a new 3 stage synchronous generator, and as simulation lead, I coordinated the simulation effort for the Boeing 777 Electric Power Generating System.

At AEG, I designed algorithms to implement moving block algorithms for trains. At General Electric, I designed a generic control class library, and provided technical leadership to the development team. The class library I created formed the foundation for all the software based control systems on the GE AC6000 locomotive.

At ARINC, I provided technical leadership for a 2,000 requirement custom software project for Trane. I designed a generic application framework for the project, which was completed on time and under budget. I served as the VP of development for a small Maryland company and architected a comprehensive accounting suite for law firms. The project, implemented in 1.5 years, consisted of 55 C++ windows applications, specified by 5,872 requirements.

I also worked on actuarial reinsurance software, a mall lease revenue system, collection agency software, biometric identity management software and pharmaceutical process monitoring software and ad serving software.
