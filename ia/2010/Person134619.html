Mark Soper’s Summary

Having attempted and failed with several startup concepts in 2008, I started working on a consumer web startup called Rally in 2009. Rally is a simple way to make casual plans with friends over Twitter. It finds plans you and your friends make on Twitter and puts them on a shared schedule, so it's easier to find out what friends are up to and get them involved in your plans.

My background is in enterprise software. Serving in engineering and sales engineering roles at SAS Institute and MicroStrategy, I've worked with dozens of companies in Internet, Health Care, Financial Services, and other industries to build data-warehouse and analytical software systems.

I did an M.S in EECS at UC Berkeley, researching network traffic analytics, and a B.S. in EE from Ohio State. I grew up in Cincinnati, OH and lived in San Francisco and Los Angeles prior to moving to Cambridge, MA.
