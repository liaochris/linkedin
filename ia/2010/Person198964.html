Tabitha Marie Helms’s Summary

My extensive background in volunteering with our community, along with my education in psychology and talents in coordinating projects allows me the opportunity to focus on creating positive changes within our community.

Bio for Tabitha Helms–E2 Foundation, Board Chair 2009-2011

Tabitha is a talented grant writer, her previous experience as the Grants and Donation Coordinator with Kaiser Permanente of the Northwest allowed her the opportunity to interact and collaborate with community organizations and leaders on issues related to community benefit participation to elicit information and build mutual positions on local, regional and state initiatives. Prior to that, she spent time volunteering with Medical Teams International, leading a youth group to San Isidro, Honduras to work with Project Global Village to build a school and 35 latrines. This work included education on the prevention of HIV/AIDS, while also training others to be able to do the same. She also spent a summer internship as a Behavioral Therapist, working one on one with autistic children.

Tabitha is a fourth generation Oregonian, with strong ties to our community. She attended Beaverton High School and is completing her Bachelor in Science, focused in Psychology and a minor in Human Development, from Washington State University. Next year, Tabitha plans on attending University of Portland where she will focus her studies on an MBA in Nonprofit Management.

Her dedication to our community has spread over the past twenty years volunteering with local nonprofits, her most recent civic engagement activities include; MIKE Program, Oregon Citizens Review Board (Foster Care), E2 Foundation second term board member, Children's Cancer Association as a Chemo Pal, Community Cycling Center, The Leukemia & Lymphoma Society as a fund raiser, Trinity Episcopal Cathedral, and the Oregon Food Bank as a food deliverer, educator and researcher.
