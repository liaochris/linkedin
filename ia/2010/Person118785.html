Kris Rivel’s Summary

I graduated from Rutgers University in 2000 with a Bachelor's Degree in Fine Art and an emphasis in 3d Computer Animation.

I immediately found a job in New York City working for an animation studio producing visualization media for the pharmaceutical and architectural industries. I worked there from 2000 to 2003 as a lead artist and during this time produced many award winning projects for Pfizer, Bristol Myers Squibb and many others.

Late in 2003, I joined the animation team at Quiet Man, in New York City and soon moved up and became the studios CG Supervisor and Lead 3d Artist. There I expanded the animation department, rebuilt and modernized their 3d pipeline and led the talented team in numerous, award winning projects for clients like Pepsi, Adidas, GE, Frito-Lay, Pizza Hut, Disney and many more. I not only supervised most of the projects during his time there; I was also heavily involved in many of the projects as a lead artist. I always wanted to be involved with the team at all times to be more in tune with the flow of the project and to better interpret the creative needs of the client to the team.

After leaving Quiet Man, I briefly moved around New York City as a freelance artist and soon found that I was in demand by a handful of small agencies, production studios and edit houses that lacked 3d animation services.

So with that demand, I created Apparatus in 2008; a unique, cutting edge, 3d animation and visual effects boutique. In its first year; Apparatus created several high-end corporate animation spots for Nokia and commercial spots for HBO, Estee Lauder, the New York Quit Smoking campaign and many others. Apparatus continues to grow and plans on opening a new facility in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania in 2009.

Please visit Apparatus's website at: www.apparatusfx.com to see our work.
