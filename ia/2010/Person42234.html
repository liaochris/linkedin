Michael Baker’s Summary

After 2 years of college I became restless and joined the Navy to spend 6 years in the US Navy Submarine Service as a nuclear machinist's mate ELT (Engineering Laboratory Technician). Afterwards, I used my GI Bill benefits to work as an apprentice electrician from 1978 until I journeyed out in 1982 with an Oregon JLE license (Journeyman Limited Energy Electrician). I also hold a Washington ME06 license (Master Specialty Electrician) and NICET Level IV certificate in Fire Alarm Systems.

I worked in the field as a service technician until 1997 when I began teaching apprentice electricians in the Oregon Electrical Apprenticeship program. I started writing NICET test preparation handbooks at about the same time, including:

- Fire Alarm Systems
- Sprinkler System Layout
- Special Hazards Suppression Systems
- Inspection and Testing of Water-based Suppression Systems

In 2002 I accepted my current postion as training director for AFAA, which requires seminar presentation and seminar development.

I very much enjoy the opportunity that this job has brought me. I meet designers, electricians, engineers, architects, sales rep's, inspectors, plans examiners, building officials, and fire marshals all over the country to discover how they deal with the same sorts of problems I encountered as a technician in the field.

My goal is to continue teaching until I drop dead.
