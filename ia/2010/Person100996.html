Joe Mangrum’s Summary

Showing early signs of promise as a young finger painter, I have pursued my dreams to be a Fine artist all my life. Attending The School of the Art institute of Chicago in 87-91 and focusing on painting skills with minors in film and photography.
After Chicago I spent many years traveling and experiencing the world at large, unable to paint regularly, I started picking up objects and arranging temporary installations in public places. This path has continued to grow for 15 years, expressing poetic ideas by creating a dialogue among everyday objects.

Living in San Francisco from 1994-2007 and creating art, I took on many diciplines for financial stability including but not limited to designing and building customized furniture, construction and project management and lead carpenter.

For the years 1997-2003 I founded the Facility 3 art space in San Francisco, a germination tank of local arts and expression. Facility 3 was host and facilitator for
performances in music theatre and the visual arts. It served as a space for the emerging artist of the era to express their medium and grow. More information can be found at facility3.com

    Joe Mangrum's installations have been shared with national and international audiences . He attended the Florence Biennale in 2003, where he received the prestigious Lorenzo de Medici award in "New Media", for his striking piece, "Fragile". Joe has also been nominated for the San Francisco Museum of Modern Art "SECA" award. An alumnus of The Art Institute of Chicago, Joe began his training as a painter and moved toward installations of found objects, while traveling abroad. He continues to explore multiple mediums and materials in his work.
