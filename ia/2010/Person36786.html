Cheryl Harris Sharman’s Summary

Cheryl Harris Sharman is an award-winning writer whose writing on topics in science, health, and social issues has appeared in Scientific American, The San Francisco Chronicle, The Miami Herald, The Lancet, Central America’s The Tico Times, and other media. Her first book, NIGHTSHIFT NYC (California), was co-authored with her husband Russell Leigh Sharman and published to critical acclaim in 2008. An active member of the New York City literary community, she moderated a book panel for the 2009 Brooklyn Book Festival and will moderate a book panel for the 2010 American Society of Journalists and Authors (ASJA) conference. She lives in Brooklyn, New York, and rural Costa Rica.

Cheryl Harris Sharman’s Specialties:

Race and ethnicity, health and science, mental health, addiction, drug usage, gender, housing, homelessness, poverty, New York City, global cities, narrative nonfiction, investigative journalism.
