Tony Macaluso’s Summary

Too many of us, when asked to define ourselves, will tell you what they do for a living, whether they’re a Neurosurgeon, or a Fruit-Picker. I have a great deal of pride in being a Real Estate Appraiser. I do it well, but it is not what defines me…and that is the reason I’m good at what I do.

My strongest traits are that I’m creative, compassionate, and loyal to a fault. I’m honest, and respectful. I love my friends, and my family, and I try to spend as much time with them as possible. I love music, and I’m a musician. If it is not obvious why these traits also make me a dedicated professional, then you may need to seek out an over-achieving workaholic.

There’s a cliché we all know that says “work to live…don’t live to work”. That is not a motto for the lazy. It is a reminder that the health and well being of a nation, a big, or a small business, should not be measured solely by accountants. It means that the reward for working hard isn’t just a pay-check. For me, the only way to be happy in what you do professionally is to be happy in what you do privately and personally. They are symbiotic, and you cannot have one without the other.

I’m always excited about learning more about everything. I’m not afraid to grow, and I’m not afraid to start over, if I have to. Happiness is different for all of us…but the “pursuit of happiness” is why I’m passionate about what I do for a living, and what I do for fun.
