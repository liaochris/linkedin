Karen Wolfe, MA’s Summary

I have over 8 years experience therapeutically supporting children with social-emotional or communication challenges. I work holistically, assessing and working with each child’s developmental history, sensory profile, learning and communication style, as well as his/her social, societal, and familial environment. I incorporate a variety of strategies to support each child’s highest potential growth. My approach is practical and efficient, as well as warm and inviting.

I draw from effective psychotherapeutic techniques and evidence-based teaching practices in my work with children and parents. I work to support the child and family's growth and independence, as I believe in the necessity to both strengthen one's inner voice as well as the ability to receive outside guidance. I work from the foundation of a highly interactive and supportive relationship and incorporate a mix of strategies from Greenspan's Floortime, Nondirective Play Therapy, Cognitive-Behavioral Therapy, Michelle Garcia Winner Social Thinking Curriculum, Mindfulness Meditation, and Humanistic and Psychodynamic approaches.

I have worked as a high school teacher, special education teacher, mental health consultant, classroom interventionist, and social coach with children with various social/behavior challenges and learning disabilities including Autism Spectrum Disorder, Asperger’s Disorder, Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder, Anxiety, and Pervasive Developmental Delay.

I have experience with a variety of support strategies such as Occupational Therapy Sensory Integration techniques, Social Skills Training (e.g. Michelle Garcia Winner), Discrete Trial Training/ABA, Floortime/DIR, Motivational Interviewing, Child-Centered Play Therapy, Filial Therapy, and Sandtray.

My main goals in therapy are to guide children and parents toward understanding their feelings and needs so they can be communicated in a healthy way that promotes more harmony in the family.
