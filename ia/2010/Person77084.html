Graeme Hughson’s Summary

PROFESSIONAL SUMMARY
A highly experienced HSE professional with over 20 years experience in occupational health, safety and environmental management. This including extensive experience in chemical, metal and oil and gas industries. Experienced in European, Middle East and Russian Federation Regulatory HSE regimes. Skilled in hazard identification and risk assessment methodology and development and implementation of HSE management systems. An effective communicator at all levels, with management experience and leadership skills.

Graeme Hughson’s Specialties:

Exposure assessment, hazardous substances, risk assessment, hazard identification, H2S, HAZOP, QRA, health risk assessment, incident investigation, project leadership, meeting facilitation, mentoring, microsoft office, presentation skills, process engineering, project design, construction, project management, reports, consultancy, research.

Examples included lifting operations, chemical safety awareness, defensive driving, asbestos management for domestic and commercial premises, ergonomic design of workstations, advised on implementation of a smoke free policy throughout company.

    Provided leadership on incident investgations, including fatal accident, serious injuries and cases of occupational illness.

    Leadership of corporate HSE audits on major projects and other functional groups within KOC operations. Responsible for implementing and coordinating audits with own department.

  • Senior Scientist

    Institute of Occupational Medicine (IOM)

    (Environmental Services industry)

    April 1991 — February 2006 (14 years 11 months)

    Project leadership for operator exposure assessment studies, with responsibility for execution, analysis and reporting on time and within budget. This covered HSE consultancy within oil & gas industry, petrochemicals, primary metal production, coal and quarry industries. Performed high level exposure assessments for UK and EU regulatory bodies and international industry associations. Some examples include:

    Member of RISKOFDERM project team. A multi-centre project involving 15 institutions from 10 EU states investigating the nature and pattern of dermal exposure in European industry. Lead role in developing dermal exposure assessment protocols.

    Diesel fume exposure assessment for transport workers and oil shale miners in Russia and Eastern Europe. Epidemiology feasibility study for International Agency for Cancer Research (IARC), Leon, France.

    Comprehensive exposure assessments for zinc, nickel, lead and antimony compounds, involving surveys of production sites in UK, France, Netherlands, Belgium, Germany and Finland. Produced expert reports for EU chemical risk assessments.

    Monitoring stack emissions from wide range of industrial processes. Dispersion modelling, calculation of chimney stack heights and estimation of ground level concentrations of pollutants.

    Provision of occupational health, safety and hygiene advice relating to ground support of military aircraft. Royal Saudi Air Force, Riyadh.

    Project leader for industry survey of isocyanate users, sponsored by the UK HSE. This included investigations of working practices and controls, together with an exposure measurement programme and recommendations for minimising risk of occupational asthma.
