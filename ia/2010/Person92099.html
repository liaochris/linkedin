John Edwards’s Summary

Seven years at Texaco taught me how to handle the analytical NMR workload of an entire corporation - from oil exploration to refining to product manufacturing and field service. Acting on an opportunity to outsource my function from Texaco along with the support of a contract for process NMR application development I founded my own NMR based company that provides analytical services and consulting to all sectors of industry.

As an application development company we have implemented over 35 online process NMR applications and aided in the development of NMR instrumentation in applications enabling process control and optimization in the petrochemical, petroleum, refining, food, and pharmaceutical industries. In recent years we have developed an analytical NMR business that serves over 300 customers in all fields of industry. We have also diversified the portfolio of technologies that we provide. We are currently developing online FTIR-ATR technology for at-line analysis with Smiths Detection. We consult to a number of petroleum companies in the field of NMR applications.

We fill a training void that has occurred over the past decade in the NMR laboratories of many companies. Expertise was "downsized" over the past decade leaving no expertise in many companies to train the influx of newly hired NMR spectroscopists (who know how to pulse and acquire, but don't know how to interpret and develop detailed understanding in niche areas such as petroleum chemistry). We partnered with several engineering companies and are investigating the integration of complimentary process analytical technologies, such as FTIR-ATR, ESR, NIR, and NMR.

Current philosophy is to develop an NMR spectrometer platform capable of performing analysis at 20, 60 or 300 MHz on the same electronics platform and make NMR spectroscopy an affordable, routine analysis tool...taking a step back from the current ideas that NMR is a multi-million dollar protein analyzer.
