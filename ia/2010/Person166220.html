Professor Peter Hines’s Summary

A Shingo Prize winner for the recent Staying Lean book, I would consider myself to be an expert in the development of Strategy and Operational Improvement using a Lean approach both inside a company and a wider Supply Chain. In addition my recent work has focused on how to sustain this change.

I am an active LinkedIner and am group leader of the following group:
* Lean & Green
* Lean Enterprise Asia Pacific
* Lean Enterprise Benelux

Professor Peter Hines’s Specialties:

I would consider that my expertise cuts across industry boundaries. Over the last 20 years I think I have worked in most manufacturing and service sectors.

Professor Peter Hines’s Experience

  • Adjunct Professor of Lean Enterprise

    University of South Australia

    (Management Consulting industry)

    May 2006 — Present (4 years 1 month)

    As an Adjunct Professor I work with Australian colleagues on various research activity on Lean as well as Lean & Green. I also deliver or co-deliver what are highly rated short courses on Lean Leadership for senior executives involving in running lean enterprises & Lean Principles for those wishing to be lean coaches within lean enterprises.

  • Chairman

    S A Partners

    (Partnership; Management Consulting industry)

    May 1993 — Present (17 years 1 month)

    S A Partners is a consultancy company with its headquarters in the UK focused on assisting clients to implement a full Lean Enterprise.

  • Chairman & Professor of Supply Chain Management

    Lean Enterprise Research Centre, Cardiff University

    (Management Consulting industry)

    March 1992 — Present (18 years 3 months)

    Professor of Supply Chain Managemenmt at Cardiff University's Lean Enterprise Research Centre which is the largest worldwide academic research centre into Lean Thinking. In this role pioneered the use of various techniques such as Value Stream Mapping (1995-), Policy Deployment (1993-), Supplier Associations (1991-).
