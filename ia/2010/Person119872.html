  • Interaction Design Intern

    Hewlett-Packard Labs

    (Public Company; HPQ; Information Technology and Services industry)

    2005 — 2005 (less than a year)

    I designed a unique and an easy-to-use interface and usability exploration of various Indic Script input methods for iPAQ and mobile phones.

    The keyboard is often the most intimidating part of a computer for novice users. To make matters worse, many languages in the emerging markets including Indian languages are not keyboard-friendly.

    The scripts for Indian languages are too complex or have too many symbols to be laid out on a standard keyboard, leading to the use of multiple shift modes. Pen-based interfaces and solutions will facilitate automation and access in the emerging markets. Efforts are presently focused on Indian languages and scripts with the understanding that they have broader application to the languages and scripts in the world’s emerging markets

    The interface has been implemented and working on iPAQ. It is rewarding to see that this project is being filed for a PATENT and will be published in Jan 2008.
