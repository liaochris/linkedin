Mike Haley’s Summary

An energetic technical leader with the ability to operate at all levels of a company my focus is usually on producing well designed software and understanding complex areas of technology. I have worked on a wide range of technologies ranging from 3D graphics and rendering to real-time embedded software systems to highly scalable server and network systems. Typically I am researching new technologies, working hands-on directing engineering teams, understanding business drivers and working with business owners to chart technology and product road maps. As well as operating in a cross-company role to drive new technology adoption I also present at numerous industry events.

Mike Haley’s Experience

  • Director of SaaS Architecture and Engineering

    Autodesk

    (Public Company; ADSK; Computer Software industry)

    February 2009 — Present (1 year 4 months)

    I direct several engineering groups building new online web products and running various aspects of our SaaS infrastructure. I also operate in a software architecture capacity to develop new technologies and to coordinate development and SaaS product activities across Autodesk. Technology areas I am focused on include: distributed data and computing; information retrieval and analysis; and browser/client technology.

  • Senior Architect, Search Engineering

    Autodesk

    (Public Company; ADSK; Computer Software industry)

    February 2007 — January 2009 (2 years )

    I run the engineering group and operate as the senior architect for the development of a new online product, Autodesk Seek for searching and publishing of engineering design content in all of the industries Autodesk services.

    We developed a completely open system that includes:
    - A custom parametric search engine (based on Lucene)
    - Large scale distributed processing of design content (using Hadoop and Amazon Web Services)
    - Machine Learning
    - A vertical ontology system for assisting in specification and finding of design data
    - Driving standards for design content and metadata

    My team developed the first alpha version of this product in 4 months and in April 2008 it was productized and included as part of our design applications.

  • Software Architect

    Autodesk

    (Public Company; 5001-10,000 employees; ADSK; Computer Software industry)

    September 2001 — January 2007 (5 years 5 months)

    Drove the technical direction and led numerous initiatives in the development of the Buzzsaw product for online collaborative project management. In my time on the product it went from a future investment opportunity to a profitable product with over 180,000 users serving some of the largest construction projects in the world.

    In my role as architect I:
    - Researched, prototyped and developed new technology directions (workflow systems, BPEL, ASP.NET adoption, WebDAV, custom templating, monitoring, etc.)
    - Drove the formal architecture and component reuse strategy of the product mostly developed in C# and C++
    - Performed due-diligence on numerous acquisition targets and led technical integration efforts
    - Explored and prototyped new products
    - Liaised with other groups in Autodesk to better integrate products and technology with Buzzsaw

  • Principal Engineer

    Buzzsaw.com

    (Privately Held; 201-500 employees; Computer Software industry)

    June 2000 — August 2001 (1 year 3 months)

    Part of a small team developing and guiding the core infrastructure of the largest on-demand construction management application. This included architecting and developing:
    - Real-time monitoring systems
    - Enterprise server bus technology to tie over 20 services together
    - Data transformation and B2B support services

    Buzzsaw was acquired by Autodesk in August 2001.

  • Senior Scientist

    Fraunhofer CRCG

    (Privately Held; 501-1000 employees; Research industry)

    April 1997 — June 2000 (3 years 3 months)

    Initially joined the Fraunhofer Center for Research in Computer Graphics to work on volumetric medical graphics technology. I developed a completely new platform and product for viewing and interacting with large volumes of medical data (typically from MRI, CT and Ultrasound scanning) as part of a DARPA project for remote battlefield surgery support. This was an extension of my post-graduate work on scalable and incremental volumetric rendering and compression.

    Later I led a team building scalable multimedia collaborative network technologies to support virtual worlds and multi-user collaborative applications. We developed QOS-based network protocols and conferencing services to manage the complexities of virtual worlds and incrementally streaming geometry, audio and video.

  • Private Software Contractor

    Datafusion Systems

    (Telecommunications industry)

    July 1994 — March 1997 (2 years 9 months)

    As a private contractor I developed numerous components of an integrated digital exchange and telecommunications system. In this role I liaised with hardware engineers and a larger software team to build:
    - Real time telecommunications logic
    - Visual applications for managing and responding to large call centers
    - Telephony hardware integration

  • Software Engineer

    Consultancy in Advanced Technology

    (Computer & Network Security industry)

    January 1992 — March 1994 (2 years 3 months)

    As the first software engineer to join CAT I was involved in:
    - Developing a digital video security system (both a graphical client developed in Windows NT as one of the first beta applications ever as well as soft real-time video capture technology). This was deployed in numerous banks in South Africa.
    - Working closely with hardware engineers to develop custom ICs and ISA cards for high speed video capture, recording and communication.
    - Developed simulations of radio technology for testing spread-spectrum communications protocols
    - Developed an entire system for video based access control
