Collin Canright’s Summary

The biggest problem nearly every business marketer faces is content. Most marketers creating a case study, article, website, ebook, or white paper wonder where the words will come from.

That's what I do. My staff and I have written, designed, and produced almost anything that uses words and graphics to communicate how one business solves the problems that another business faces. I specialize in b-to-b marketing for technology (software, in particular); banking (treasury management and payments, specifically); and professional services (accounting and engineering, with a bit of law).

My software manuals have helped IBM communicate complex ecommerce software. My books have explained sales, investments, and Kabbalah (really). My presentations have entertained corporate treasurers about trends in payments--a feat that owes more to the presenter than to my staff and me, I must admit. My white papers have described ebanking systems, executive leadership trends, and electronics connectors. And my websites and brochures have presented the value of benefits consulting, factory automation, electronics technologies, engineering services, and hospital software, among others.

I have worked in media and communications all my life, starting with my family's newspaper, attended two major journalism schools (Indiana University and the University of Missouri), and edited copy at an early online news service. I learned about finance and ecommerce at a Chicago-area business magazine in the mid-1980s and have consulted with business-to-business companies and professional firms ever since.
