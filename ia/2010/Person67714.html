Ethan Kuniyoshi’s Summary

Ethan earned his B.S. degree from University of California at San Diego with a focus in Cognitive Science. He studied the computational aspects of cognition—from artificial intelligence and neural networks to human factors and human computer interaction. In the Fall of 2009, he completed his M.A. in Education focusing on Instructional Design/Technologies from San Francisco State University. His culminating project was conducted at Genentech with the Commercial Training and Development group and involved the analysis, design and development of an ongoing training program for customer contact staff.

Prior to Genentech, Ethan was at Stanford Hospital developing technical training on the use of the EPIC clinical information system. He also worked four years as a software and web developer. Most notably, he developed games for Neopets—which at the time was the most popular youth-orientated website. In addition to work in the corporate world, he also has experience working in the field of K-12 education. He served as a technology coordinator for a public elementary school and as a project manager for Edison Schools where he was responsible for the implementation and support of a standards-based electronic assessment system.

Ethan is a Bay Area native and enjoys spending time outdoors and rooting for the Golden State Warriors. In his spare time, he is an avid table tennis player, plays in a recreational basketball league and loves to run (he has completed the Honolulu Marathons three times). On the second Tuesday evening of the month, you’ll often find him at BayCHI, the San Francisco Bay Area chapter of the ACM Special Interest Group on Computer-Human Interaction (SIGCHI).

Ethan Kuniyoshi’s Specialties:

eLearning, corporate training, social media, instructional design, human factors, distributed cognition, neural networks, Adobe Creative Suite, LAMP-based web development
