Billy Samoa Saleebey’s Summary

Billy Saleebey is a graduate of Loyola Marymount University Film School in Los Angeles. Upon completion of school in 2000, Mr. Saleebey began working in the entertainment industry for companies such as CBS Television, KCET, and Celestial Entertainment. In late 2001, Mr. Saleebey gained global experience while working for London International Television as an Associate Producer where he was responsible for internationally broadcast programming that aired in forty countries to more than eighty million viewers. Upon his return he started Believe Entertainment in 2002 as a event production focused primarily on New Year’s Eve parties.

In the fall of 2003 Mr. Saleebey relocated to New York where he began writing his first feature, Rolling, which was shot in 2005 and completed in the end of 2006. Rolling premiered in 2007 at the in San Francisco Independent Film Festival where it was awarded the Audience Award for Best Feature. It went on to show at numerous film festival around the world, including the UK, Germany, Australia and all over the United States. The film was honored at the 2009 Prism Awards for accurate depictions of drug use in film. Rolling developed a following thanks to its success on the festival circuit and due to an aggressive grassroots marketing campaign spearheaded by Saleebey. The film was released theatrically in select cities and on DVD on May 12th, 2009.

Mr. Saleebey launched the web sites TheTrailerSite.com, a movie trailer site, and ProFilmmakers.com, a filmmaker social network site, in 2009. He is currently in development on his next feature to be shot in 2010.

Mr. Saleebey is also working in the renewable energy industry as a solar consultant for Verengo Solar Plus.
