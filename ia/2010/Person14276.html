Anne Ream’s Summary

Anne K. Ream is a Chicago-based writer and creative director. A past finalist for the Dorothea Lange-Paul Taylor Documentary Prize, Anne's essays and opinion pieces have appeared in the Atlanta Journal-Constitution, the Los Angeles Times, the Chicago Tribune, and other publications.

Anne is a former Senior Vice President and Group Creative Director at Leo Burnett USA and the Co-Founder and Chief Creative "Girl" at Girl360.net, a forthcoming webzine and book series for tween girls. The founder of The Voices and Faces Project, an award-winning documentary initiative that brings together artists, writers and private sector communications professionals, and the co-chair of CounterQuo, a legal and new media project focused on women's issues, Anne's private and public sector interest is encouraging positive media representations of women.

A longstanding fan of rock and roll, Anne is also a contributor to the forthcoming "Cambridge Companion to Bob Dylan," (Cambridge University Press, 2009), a book featuring essays from a host of writers and rock critics, including National Book Award winner Jonathan Lethem, Rolling Stone Editor Anthony DeCurtis, and Nation Magazine Music Critic David Yaffe.

Anne is the executive producer, in partnership with Burn and Shiver Records, of "The Voices and Faces Project, Volume One," a recently released benefit cd that features the New Pornographers, Neko Case, Motion City Soundtrack, and 14 other artists. The record was called a "who's who of today's top indie rock stars" by Virgin Records, and named one of the year's top 25 releases by recording industry trendsetter Amoeba Records.

To hear Anne Ream's recent Public Radio piece on music, social movements and The Voices and Faces Project, Volume One, visit:
http://www.voicesandfaces.org/848_20061212h.mp3

Anne is currently represented by The Frances Goldin Literary Agency and CreativeWell Lecture and Literary Management. Both are based in New York.

Anne Ream’s Experience

  • Co-Founder and CCG (Chief Creative Girl)

    Girl360

    (Design industry)

    2007 — Present (3 years )

    Girl360 is a Chicago and San Francisco cause marketing initiative created to bring the stories of history's and today's most amazing women to the attention of a new generation of tween (ages 9 - 12) girls. Our product offerings include a monthly webzine (girl360.net), an in-progress retail line and a forthcoming book series. The Girl360 brand is grounded in a simple belief: that behind every great woman is a great girl, one who often discovered her own "superpowers" after encountering other super-cool female role models. girl360.net

  • Founder

    The Voices and Faces Project

    (Philanthropy industry)

    2004 — Present (6 years )

    The Voices and Faces Project is an award-winning documentary initiative created to bring the testimony of national and international survivors of sexual violence and trafficking to the attention of the public. Through its lobbying and speakers bureau, website, story archive and creative initiatives (including a forthcoming audio documentary and a book, being authored by Ream) the project seeks to change minds, hearts and public policy. The Voices and Faces Project is a registered 501c3 (non-profit) organization.

  • Founding Co-Chair

    CounterQuo

    (Legal Services industry)

    2008 — 2009 (1 year )

    CounterQuo is a national initiative created to challenge and change media and legal responses to women. Co-founded by Anne Ream in partnership with attorneys Susan Estrich and and Susan Vickers, and author and literary critic Charlotte Pierce-Baker, this new-media project enlists private and public sector allies and communications professionals in our efforts to encourage positive representations of women in advertising, marketing, media and music.

  • Senior Vice President, Group Creative Director

    Leo Burnett USA

    (Public Company; PUB; Marketing and Advertising industry)

    1994 — 2004 (10 years )

    Leo Burnett USA is one of the world's largest advertising and communication companies. As a Senior Vice President and Group Creative Director, Ream worked on dozens of international (and often female-focused) brands and companies, including Starbucks, Hallmark, Allstate, General Motors, and P&G.

  • Associate Creative Director/Copywriter

    Ogilvy and Mather Direct

    (Marketing and Advertising industry)

    1990 — 1993 (3 years )

    In the early nineties, the Washigton DC office of Ogilvy and Mather Direct focused on political communications and grassroots lobbying. Clients included national political candidates (most notably, the Mondale-Ferraro Presidential Campaign), the ACLU, and the World Jewish Congress. O&M Direct also worked with a host of national and international brands that included Coca-Cola, The Professional Golf Association, and Miller Beer.
