Kellie Parker’s Summary

I have been managing online communities, social media strategies, and community-based marketing since 1998. I have extensive experience in message boards, chats, social networking, webcasts, online events, marketing and project management.

I am currently the Community Manager for Sega. In this role, I manage the community & grassroots marketing for Sega game titles in the US. In addition to writing the Sega blogs and maintaining the Sega forums, my team and I write and execute plans to create buzz around upcoming and just-released titles. Through social media and fansite outreach, we release trailer videos, screenshots, interviews, FAQs, and other information to the web.

Prior to Sega, I was the Online Community Manager at PC World and Macworld (part of IDG) in San Francisco. I managed and executed the strategy and implementation of all community and reader-facing initiatives for both brands. This included message boards, wikis, blog comments, user product reviews, and community-contributed content in the magazines. In addition, I was responsible for the implementation of many successful contests, including the Dream PC contest. I worked directly with all groups within the organization, including editorial, business development, engineering, ad operations, sales, and the executive management team.

At LiveWorld, I managed online communities such as Food Network, HBO, and A&E. I also worked with eBay, Slim-Fast, Sony Music, and many more of the web's biggest brands. This was both a consulting and implementation role, as I worked with clients during the sales process to determine their needs, and managed their accounts and projects post-sale to ensure success.

I have presented at numerous workshops and conferences, including OMMA '07, Community 2.0 '08, Web 2.0 '08, and the Online Community Business Forum.

I hold a BA in Criminal Justice from Kent State University, and an MBA in Marketing (with Graduate Honors) from Regis University.
