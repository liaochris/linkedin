Stan Kehl’s Summary

Successfully created and implemented plans for two Equality Days which drew 1,500 to 2,000 people to Olympia. Set up financial systems for a new organization, the Religious Coalition for Equality. Provided pastoral care to patients on the Psychiatric Care Unit at Overlake Hospital and Medical Center. Supervised accounting system transition for Washington Information Network 2-1-1 and solved cash flow problems. Increased municipal aid to the City of Minneapolis from $16M to $50M in one legislative session. Founded and managed KFH Publications for 17 years, producing a 100 page monthly, free, regional computer magazine and generating annual revenues of over $2M. Member of Leadership Tomorrow team that designed and implemented diversity recruitment program for Seattle Girl’s School first class.

Stan Kehl’s Specialties:

Excellent advocate for organizations engaged in promoting social justice and human rights. Enjoys engaging with people in compassionate listening situations and as an advocate. Creative problem solver. Excels at implementing organizational plans and programs for entities in their startup and early growth phases. Possesses strong financial management, general business, and not-for-profit legal skills. Effective group facilitator. Outstanding research, analysis and synthesis skills.
