Mike Fayette’s Summary

Mike Fayette is a CEO-level manager, media and business consultant as well as an award-winning creative and technical leader in a variety of fields related to IPTV, broadcast television, digital media, sales, marketing and training.

His experience embraces all forms of digital communications, including special venue presentations, museums, tradeshows, corporate meetings and even video games.

He is an entrepreneur who founded Chicago's famed Post Effects and generated more than $200M in sales of creative services to clients ranging from Oprah Winfrey, Accenture, A&E Television, MTV, Jewel Foods, Bill Kurtis Productions, Motorola, Travel Channel, Noggin, US Department of Commerce, Bungie/Microsoft (Halo), Chicago Bears (The SuperBowl Shuffle) and hundreds of others.

He is experienced in Mergers and Acquisitions in the Media Industry, having successfully completed acquisition of three media companies and sold one. In addition, he has successfully negotiated more than $30M in leases, technology acquisitions and debt.

He is a media "thought leader" who has written articles, delivered papers at SMPTE and participated at the highest levels of industry trade groups and organizations.

His creative work has won numerous awards and has been exhibited at the Chicago Museum of Contemporary Art.

His technical accomplishments include pioneering implementations of almost every key new media technology from 1" analog videotape to HDTV and IPTV media deployments.

Frequently, Fayette has found himself at the convergence between broadcast television and the internet.

This was highlighted by his successful effort to gain creative “agency” status at the consulting firm Accenture, including key work on their IPTV positioning and marketing materials.

His own recent consulting work has helped traditional print publishers create business models for Web 2.0 video content networks.

He is also a pilot, industry speaker, columnist and science fiction author.

Mike Fayette’s Specialties:

Communications Management, Creative Development, New Technology Deployment, Consulting, Video Systems Design, Interactive On-Line Media, IPTV, Producing, Writing and Directing, Editing and Special Effects, Cyberset Production, Team Building, Sales and Marketing, Financial, Supply Chain and Project Management

Mike Fayette’s Experience

  • Founder

    Fayette Productions, Inc.

    (Media Production industry)

    1981 — Present (29 years )

    Fayette Productions was the "holding" company that did business as "Post Effects" in Chicago for more than 25 years and is currently the umbrella for Fayette's consulting and freelance endeavors.

    After selling the assets of Post Effects to AnswersMedia in December 2006, (and leaving AnswersMedia in May 2007) Mike Fayette has begun to develop multiple business opportunities in the broadcast, corporate, publishing and interactive media industry.

    While this might result in a new "start-up" company, he is currently searching for the "right fit" in order to bring his expertise to others and become a part of another, larger organization.

  • Business Plan Developer and IPTV Consultant

    Chicago Christian Publisher

    (Publishing industry)

    March 2008 — December 2009 (1 year 10 months)

    Mike Fayette was engaged in March of 2008 to assist in the technical planning, video engineering and design for a new state-of-the-art studio facility to be built near Chicago by one of the country's largest Christian publishers.

    This consulting engagement grew to include the development of a Web 2.0 subscription video service with the potential to revolutionize the distribution of Christian media content and - if funded - it has the potential to quickly grow into a $500M a year organization.

    Fayette conceived and wrote the Business Plan, and worked with a small internal team to develop budgets, timelines, Supply Chain planning, creative and technical concepts and staffing.

  • Business Development & Web 2.0 Consultant

    Chicago Media Website

    (Publishing industry)

    January 2008 — October 2008 (10 months)

    In 2008 Fayette had the privilege of authoring multiple articles, analyzing the marketplace and then developing a brand new business plan for one of Chicago's most respected on-line media publications.

    In this partner-level role, he developed a strategy to expand advertising revenue by more than 500% by creating a series of focused "microsites" that would provide exclusive coverage of more than a dozen areas of media interest that are not covered regularly elsewhere - from digital media education, to video games to IPTV initiatives all over the city.

    He designed the layout of this new Web 2.0 site, met with clients, researched, hired and supervised off-shore programmers to execute this exciting new site at a budget that saved almost 90% of the originally estimated costs. Social features, podcasts, streaming video and slideshows were all integrated into the new beta site.

    Due to a confidentially agreement, the publication can not be identified at this time.

  • Chief Creative Officer

    Answers Media LLC

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Internet industry)

    December 2006 — May 2007 (6 months)

    Mike Fayette became Chief Creative Officer for AnswersMedia after he sold Post Effects to them in December of 2006. He stayed with AnswersMedia for a six-month transition period and he ended his non-compete period in December of 2007.

    In this role, Fayette supervised all creative work completed at the company, including work for all outside clients and the conceptual structure of many interactive, web-based channels for AnswersTV.com

    These programs included "Reel Chefs" for FoodAnswersTV and "The Answered Patient" for HealthAnswersTV, among many others.

    He managed a team of more than 30 writers, producers, associate producers and directors. He wrote production "Bibles" for each show, directed the "pilot" episodes and developed procedures to track schedules, control costs and identify deliverables.

    He was also responsible for developing key Branding elements of the websites, including the use of the Cyberset, interactive Flash, HDTV and other technologies.

  • CEO - Founder

    Post Effects

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Broadcast Media industry)

    February 1981 — December 2006 (25 years 11 months)

    Post Effects was one of the most widely recognized television production, post-production and special effects facilities in the world.

    It pioneered many new uses of video technology, particularly in the fields of interactive media, video game development and video on the web.

    The company began in Fayette's basement in 1981 and grew to a beautiful 40,000 sq/ft facility, employed more than 50 people and generated $7M+ in annual sales to broadcasters, corporations, ad agencies, and independent producers.

    Fayette recruited, trained and inspired hundreds of talented professionals who frequently became creative leaders in the industry.

    Together, they won hundreds of creative awards. Fayette himself won Screen Magazine's "Man of the Year: award and Arthur Anderson's "Small Business Award" for entrepreneurs.

    Fayette led the company into many successful deployments of new technology, including 3-D animation, HDTV, Digital Video, motion control, motion capture, and Cyberset.

  • Director of Post Production - Editor

    Optimus

    (Privately Held; Telecommunications industry)

    1978 — 1982 (4 years )

    Mike Fayette's experiences at Optimus in Chicago provided him with many of the final elements needed for his professional growth and the creation of Post Effects.

    Once again, he had joined a high-tech company at a crucial moment in its growth.

    Upon arrival, he immediately assisted their move into a bigger facility, where sales increased by more than 400% during Mike's tenure there as an on-line editor, video department manager and client-oriented project manager .

    Technology introduced there included the city's first 1" editing bays, 1st stereo audio system, 1st ADO special effects systems and many other tools designed for the production of test commercial for ad agencies.

    His creative work included pioneering special effects work, national commercials such as Bud Lite, and complete editorial for Bravo's first series of broadcast symphonic concerts.

    He supervised a team of 12 editors and assistant editors and personally completed more than $1M a year in creative work.

  • Technical Operations Manager

    Amoco

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; Oil & Energy industry)

    1974 — 1978 (4 years )

    Fayette joined Amoco's Television Services Department in time to help manage their move into what became the largest corporate video facility in America.

    In essence, this was one more high-tech "start-up" that he was able to both guide and experience.

    Fayette then worked closely with dozens of in-house clients to help Amoco use video to communicate its position to the public during the height of the first "oil crisis."

    He also learned about the "life-and-death" impact of his work, when he spent more than 3 weeks shooting an oilfield safety video that eventually cut the rate of fatal accidents in that division by more than half.

    He supervised 12 engineers, as well as a state-of-the facility featuring Chicago's first CMX on-line and off-line editing systems, field production teams, 2 studios and audio recording.

    He also spent two years in Amoco's middle manager training program, which acted as "mini-MBA," focusing on team building, organizational and financial management.

  • Assistant Editor

    Editel

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Broadcast Media industry)

    1973 — 1974 (1 year )

    Mike was an early employee at Editel's famed Chicago office, and as a key member of this technology start-up, he helped to develop many creative solutions using a series of breakthrough technological innovations.

    These included:

    • The 1st time-code based, “on-line” CDL computerized editing system in Chicago, which used a PDP 11 minicomputer, 3 Ampex Quad VTR’s and punched tape readers to store metadata such as EDL's

    • The 1st “off-line” process for using film kinescopes with visible timecode.

    • The 1st “scene-by-scene” color corrector for film transfers to video.

    • The 1st HS-200 special effects video disk recorder

    Early clients focused primarily on broadcast commercials, high-end corporate pieces, and "TVN", which was an early precursor to CNN.

    The Editel period for Mike Fayette was a unique opportunity to be part of a start-up and to develop creative techniques and operating procedures that later became the basis of a new video post-production industry.

Mike Fayette’s Education

  • Northwestern University

    BS , Radio, Television and Film , 1969 — 1973

    Fayette began his education at Northwestern in Aerospace Engineering at NU's Tech Institute, with an emphasis on Computer Sciences. He also had significant coursework in physics, astronomy and other technical areas.

    He quickly realized that his interests had expanded from purely engineering to a broader focus that included the more audience-directed, creative uses of the video technology that was emerging at that time.

    He worked as the supervising student video engineer at Northwestern Television Studios, responsible for the maintenance and operation of the entire facility for three years. In 1975, he returned as a paid consultant to Northwestern and designed and installed their first color television studio.

    His creative work during this period including some of the pioneering work in video special effects, feedback, video installations and dance, including the creation of a campus-wide multimedia play called "Insway Onsway".

  • Clarion University of Pennsylvania

    Clarion Science Fiction Writers Workshop 1969 — 1969

    Fayette was instructed and critiqued by world-famous science fiction authors including Harlan Ellison, Frederik Pohl, Kate Wilhelm, Fritz Leiber, Damon Knight and many others who lived with the students for weeks at a time in a unique creative and educational experience.

    As a direct result of this program, Fayette began selling science fiction short stories professionally later that year.
