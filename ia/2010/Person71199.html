Frank LoVerme’s Experience

  • Board Director and Senior VP, Business Development

    Aequitas Innovation

    (Consumer Goods industry)

    May 2008 — Present (2 years 5 months)

    Aequitas prevents retail and transit theft by removing the incentive to steal.

    Theft lowers profit margins by increasing the cost of goods, causing security expenses and creating customer dissatisfaction with anti-theft measures such as "caging" high-value items.

    Aequitas deactivates a product at the factory with a uniquely encrypted RF chip that controls the item's utility (e.g., video cards will not display, products can't power up, etc.). Our secure network authorizes each chip to reactivate products at the POS following a cash transaction.

    Solutions protect optical disc products, apparel, consumer electronics, tools and many other items.

  • Senior VP, Bus Dev

    Kestrel Wireless, Inc.

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Computer Software industry)

    January 2006 — May 2008 (2 years 5 months)

    With significant IP associated with anti-theft of DVDs and CE devices based on RFID and a secure network, Kestrel promised products to be theft-free from the point-of-manufacture to POS by using Radio Frequency Activation (RFA™).

    RFA-protected products can be openly displayed and not be buried in anti-theft packaging. DVDs and inkjet cartridges would be available “on-demand”, whether at-home, in the office or on the road via the Internet or cell phones.

    My role was to jointly develop and present business models, forecasts and retail ROI planning programs to demonstrate potential for numerous industries to eradicate multimillion-dollar losses due to transit theft and shoplifting.

    I served as member of the executive management and strategy team reporting directly to the CEO. The company entered dormancy in May 08. Its IP is being licensed to Larcentech, a company I am currently affiliated with.
