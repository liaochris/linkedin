Don Hauptman’s Summary

I've been a full-time freelance writer since 1974. For 30 years, I worked as a direct-response copywriter, specializing in subscription newsletters and other information products. I have a parallel interest in language and wordplay, and have two published books in that field. I retired from copywriting to focus on writing about language. I also write and speak on advertising and marketing, communicating my three decades of experience in those areas.

NOTE: If you send me an invitation to join your netowrk, please include your e-mail address; otherwise, I may have no way to contact you.

  • Author, humorist

    freelance

    (Writing and Editing industry)

    1991 — Present (19 years )

    I have a lifelong passion for “recreational linguistics" and write books and articles on puns and other forms of wordplay. My celebration of spoonerisms, "Cruel and Unusual Puns" (Dell, 1991) received rave reviews and quickly went into a second printing. My second book was "Acronymania" (Dell, 1993). I'm now working on a third book in this genre. Since 2001, I have contributed an article on wordplay to every issue of "Word Ways," a venerable quarterly journal on language.

  • Independent advertising copywrter and consultant

    Don Hauptman Inc.

    (Marketing and Advertising industry)

    1974 — Present (36 years )

    For more than 30 years, I was a freelance advertising copywriter and consultant. I specialized in the marketing of information. I created successful direct-mail packages, space ads, and renewal campaigns for newsletters, magazines, books, conferences and seminars, electronically delivered news services, and spoken-word audio products. I was perhaps best known for the headline “Speak Spanish [French, German, etc.] Like a Diplomat!” This familiar series of ads sold spectacular numbers of recorded foreign language lessons, generating revenues that totaled in the tens of millions of dollars. In the process, the ad achieved the status of an industry classic.

Don Hauptman’s Honors:

Ten-time winner of the annual "Newsletter on Newsletters" promotion award for writing subscription acquisition packages. (For many years, no other writer could match that record.)

1996: Folio Award (magazine industry) for writing a sucessful consumer magazine promotion.

2007: At an international marketing conference in Courtomer, France, an award for lifetime copywriting achievement.

I have been listed in the Marquis volumes "Who’s Who in the East" and "Who’s Who in Advertising," and in Gale’s "Contemporary Authors."

My work is mentioned in three major college advertising textbooks, and examples of my promotions are cited in the books "Million Dollar Mailings" (1992) and "World's Greatest Direct Mail Sales Letters" (1996).

In a column in the major trade magazine "Advertising Age," my name was included in a short list of direct marketing "superstars."
