Marcus Rietema’s Summary

From athlete to coach, retail management, sales rep, event management, marketing, advertising, network television, and journalism, I have experience in nearly every aspect of the action sports industry. Over 13 years of experience organizing international events, has provided me a great understanding of working with people from a multitude of cultures, ethnicities and backgrounds.

Since 2001 I've spent a large portion of each year traveling to different countries and getting to know the downhill skateboarders of the world on a personal level. I've gained a deep understanding of people from all walks of life. My experience of performing nearly every job in the actions sports industry provides a strong knowledge base for making decisions.

Marcus Rietema’s Specialties:

Action sports, international event management, international marketing, publishing, copy writing and photography.

Marcus Rietema’s Experience

  • IGSA World Cup Editor

    Concrete Wave Magazine

    (Publishing industry)

    November 2007 — Present (3 years )

    Each issue I contribute a 2-4 page article showcasing the IGSA World Cup Series events for this bi-monthly publication. I write each story and select all of the photos within the article. In addition, I contribute some of my own photos that I shoot at events.

    Every year I also publish 10,000 copies of a 32+ page "IGSA World Cup Series Event Guide" showcasing the events and athletes who make up the sport. The guide is included with the June issue of Concrete Wave for all subscribers. It is also distributed at each of the World Cup events and other IGSA related activities around the globe.

    I am responsible for the basic layout, all text, all photo selections, selling all advertising, consulting with the graphic designer, organizing printing and international distribution.

  • Equine Dentist

    CA Equine Dental

    (Sports industry)

    August 1998 — Present (12 years 3 months)

    IAED Certified Equine Dentist specializing in performance and geriatric horses. My specialties include molar arcade balancing, restoration of occlusal surfaces, repair of malocclusions, incisor realignment, wolf tooth extraction and diseased tooth extraction.

    I served a two-year, highly in-depth apprenticeship with Richard Miller DVM where I learned all aspects of advanced equine dentistry from a Certified Master. Upon completion of my apprenticeship, I passed the IAED Dental Certification with the highest score of all candidates.

    Today I perform advanced equine dentistry throughout the San Francisco Bay Area with occasional trips to Southern California.

  • President

    International Gravity Sports Association (IGSA)

    (Sports industry)

    January 1997 — Present (13 years 10 months)

    I founded the international sanctioning body for the sports of Downhill Skateboard and Street Luge racing in 1997. Today we organize the nine race IGSA World Cup Series with events in Australia, Brazil, Czech Republic, England, France, Germany, Italy, South Africa, and the United States.

    During my time as President, the Series has grown from a handful of grass roots events to a professional, international racing series that currently has more than 1000 participants. I have carefully chosen a select group of international representatives who attend to the associations needs on each of the five continents where we stage events. My main job is to steer the association on a clear path of growth and prosperity.

    I attend almost every World Cup event where I perform for a venue inspection, technical inspection, timing and scoring duties, and write daily race reports for the IGSA website. Other job responsibilities include sponsorship development, media relations, event scheduling, new event development, rules & regulations, safety development, website blogging and series marketing.

  • X Games Sport Organizer

    ESPN

    (Public Company; DIS; Broadcast Media industry)

    March 1997 — February 2002 (5 years )

    Organized the Street Luge competitions for the 1997-2001 ESPN X Games. My job responsibilities were to select a qualified field of international competitors, develop & implement competition rules and regulations, assist with venue selection and development.

    In addition I consulted with television producers, directors and executives on all aspects of the competition. I hired and directed a crew of 16 each year to run the competitions for live taping by ESPN. The events that I organized were broadcast both live and a tape delay basis on ABC Wide World of Sports, ESPN and ESPN2.

  • General Manager
