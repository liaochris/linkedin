Lauren A. McAuley’s Summary

I am a third-year law student at the University of Detroit Mercy School of Law. In 2007, I completed my B.A. in Political Science and Communication studies through the Honors Program at the University of Detroit Mercy.

Currently, I am clerking part-time for a respected Bankruptcy and Creditor's Rights firm in metropolitan Detroit that handles clients both locally and nationwide. I have found my clerkship a wonderful learning opportunity.

In addition to practical work experience, I have competed in several appellate and trial advocacy competitions. In Fall 2008, I won top honors at the University of Detroit Mercy's internal trial advocacy competition, the Ernie Goodman Mock Trial. I won Best Overall Advocate and Best Trial Notebook. In Spring 2009, I competed at the Herbert Weschler National Criminal Law Moot Court Competition as a representative of UDM's Moot Court Board of Advocates.

I will graduate in May 2010, and plan to take the July 2010 Michigan bar examination. I would like to pursue a career in trial and appellate advocacy.
