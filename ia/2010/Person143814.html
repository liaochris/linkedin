Mike Yim’s Summary

As a Managing Consultant with Navigant Consulting, I have extensive experience analyzing the economic and energy-driven impacts of Demand Side Management (DSM) programs implemented within a broad range of market sectors. This cross-cutting exposure has allowed me to construct pragmatic, cost-effective evaluation solutions that successfully comply with regulatory requirements. More recently, I've helped develop the Strategic Energy Plan and Evaluation, Measurement & Verification (EM&V) framework for E.ON U.S.

Mike Yim’s Specialties:

My primary areas of expertise include Demand Side Management (DSM) program design and evaluation, simulation/stochastic modeling, and renewable energy technology assessment.

Mike Yim’s Experience

  • Managing Consultant

    Navigant Consulting

    (Public Company; NCI; Management Consulting industry)

    January 2010 — Present (11 months)

    • Project Manager for the strategic energy planning and evaluation of E.ON U.S.’ DSM portfolio. Key responsibilities included regulatory filing support and the development of a comprehensive Evaluation, Measurement & Verification (EM&V) framework that successfully integrated with existing IT infrastructure investments. Annual portfolio budgets exceeded $28MM across 10 DSM programs.

    • Project Manager for the University of California/California State University (UC/CSU) Investor Owned Utilities (IOU) Energy Partnership Program Evaluation. With an annual budget of $32MM, the Partnership promoted custom efficiency retrofits, retro-commissioning (RCx), and education/awareness training. Key responsibilities included the development of a statistically representative evaluation sample, specification of rigorous Measurement & Verification (M&V) protocols, and regulatory reporting compliance.

  • Consultant

    Summit Blue Consulting

    (Privately Held; Renewables & Environment industry)

    October 2005 — December 2009 (4 years 3 months)

    • Project Manager for the incremental cost and stock assessment of 120 residential efficiency measures incented through four Northwest Utility DSM portfolios. Key responsibilities included coordinating retail, contractor, and program manager interviews/surveys; developing regression cost models for discrete measure technologies; incremental cost reporting; and overall project management. Mr. Yim also played a key role in developing and publishing a Best Practice Guide for Assessing Incremental Costs of Residential Energy Conservation Measures (ECMs).

    • Project Manager for the 2006 – 2008 California Database for Energy Efficient Resources (DEER) Cost Update Study. The pricing and market characterization of over 250 separate residential, commercial, and industrial energy conservation applications in the DEER database are nationally recognized as DSM program design inputs. In addition to developing and analyzing hedonic pricing models, Mr. Yim established an interactive web-enabled database that provided a central repository for costs collected through various resources.

    • Project Manager for the design and implementation of Nevada Power and Sierra Pacific Power Company’s 2007 – 2008 Low Income Programs. This project involved research into program demographics, calibrated building energy simulation modeling for interactive weatherization measures, and the development of detailed engineering algorithms for non-interactive measures. A key task for this project was the recommendation of additional cost-effective measures for inclusion in future program cycles.

  • Research Analyst

    The Purdue Energy Modeling Research Group

    (Utilities industry)

    August 2004 — August 2005 (1 year 1 month)

    • Updated and analyzed the Indiana Natural Gas Distribution Optimization Model. This model assessed the economic and physical congestion risk stemming from variations in system wide demand and supply characteristics.

    • Published and presented the 2005 Renewable Energy Resources Study for submission to the Indiana Utility Regulatory Commission. This study tracked market trends and potential for renewable energy technologies deployed in Indiana.
