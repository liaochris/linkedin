Kelly Hoey’s Experience

  • President & New York Chapter Leader (2010)

    85 Broads LLC

    (Privately Held; Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    October 2009 — Present (7 months)

    85 Broads is an exclusive global women’s network whose mission is to generate exceptional professional and social value for its members. Through regional events and online, password-protected community, members engage in a rapid, high-powered exchange of ideas and information which is what makes 85 Broads network so unique.
    The women in 85 Broads are investment bankers, lawyers, educators, venture capitalists, hedge fund managers, philanthropists, athletes, scientists, doctors, engineers, artists, and entrepreneurs. 85 Broads was originally founded in 1997 as an exclusive network for women who worked for Goldman Sachs at 85 Broad Street, the investment bank’s NYC headquarters. In 2000, the network was expanded to include a more diverse group of women from a variety of fields and backgrounds, including the non-profit sector. Today, over 20,000 women who are alumnae and students of the world's leading colleges, universities, graduate business schools, law schools, medical schools, and other graduate programs belong to 85 Broads.

  • Vice-Chair, Board of Directors

    inMotion

    (Privately Held; Legal Services industry)

    June 2005 — Present (4 years 11 months)

    inMotion provides free legal services to low-income women in New York City. inMotion's clients are poor. Without money for life’s basic necessities, hiring a lawyer is not something they can ever contemplate. 43% of the families served live in the Bronx, the City’s poorest borough and the poorest urban county in the United States.

    inMotion's services are designed to address the unique needs of women who suffer from abuse—most clients are survivors of domestic violence. inMotion's focus is on reaching women living in immigrant communities who are isolated by language and cultural barriers. 27% of inMotion's clients live in Queens, the most ethnically diverse county in the entire United States. Nearly half of the people who live in Queens were born in a foreign country—and collectively speak more than 160 languages. 35% of inMotion's clients speak a language other than English as their primary language and 71% of them require interpretors.

    inMotion's goal is to ensure that all women who contact inMotion obtain essential protections for themselves and their children.

    With a modest budget and small staff, inMotion mobilizes support from thousands of volunteers—lawyers, legal assistants, students and other professionals. inMotion connects women in crisis with attorneys who want to help
    These lawyers secure orders of protection that keep families safe. They help battered immigrant women become legal residents, enabling them to obtain good jobs and a full range of benefits for themselves and their children. They obtain child support orders against fathers who refuse to pay, so that mothers with custody can provide for their children. And they represent women seeking divorces in obtaining custody of their children and a fair share of the assets of the marriage. Last year inMotion's legal volunteers donated over 74,000 hours, valued at more than $24 million.
