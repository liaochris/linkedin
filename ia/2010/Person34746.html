Cecilia Domeyko’s Summary

Please scroll to end to see letters of recommendation.

Award-winning documentary writer, director and producer of international broadcast programs on development, education, women´s issues, human rights, health and other social issues.

As owner of Accent Media Productions, a television company specializing in national public service campaigns directed at a general and minority Spanish speaking audience, also am senior writer, director and producer for video and radio programs, PSAs, VNRs, and corporate and training videos.

The documentary "CUBA MIA" about an all-woman orchestra in Havana is airing on PBS in the U.S. and on networks in Australia, France, Iceland, Finland, Hungary, among others.

The documentary "CODE NAME: BUTTERFLIES" about the Mirabal sisters in the Dominican Republic and their fight for freedom and democracy has been on the official list at ten international film festivals, and won the Cine Latino Award at the DC Film Festival, and Silver Awards for Directing, Cinematography and a Bronze for Biography at the Telly Awards. The film had its world premiere at the OAS in Washington, DC on March 30 of 2009, and its Dominican premiere in Santo Domingo on July 1, 2009. To view trailer and "making of" visit: http://www.youtube.com/ceciliadomeyko.

Former on-camera reporter and producer for Spanish-language networks in the U.S. and in Latin America, including Univision (national evening news), Spanish NBC, Channel 13 of Chile and 24 Horas in Colombia. Covered breaking news and feature stories, the White House, Capitol Hill, State Department, and others.

Formerly and still freelance writer and editor for Spanish-language and English language magazines and newspapers in the U.S. and Latin America. Breaking news, feature stories and personal interviews for the magazines, "COSAS" of Chile, LA SEGUNDA newspaper (daily newspaper part of the El Mercurio newspaper chain), and ISTMO of Central America.

Cecilia Domeyko’s Specialties:

Casting and directing dramatic talent from the U.S. and Latin America for programs to reach a minority Spanish-speaking audience, directing in various areas of the world, creative and news writing an short notice, magazine writing, organizing and shooting with large fillm and video crews, drawing up budgets for small and large productions, keeping to schedules and budgets to complete complex television programs and national public service campaigns.

    BA , Communications/journalism , 1965 — 1970

    The degree in Journalism at the Catholic University of Chile also required a thesis, which had to be "defended" during a three hour oral exam before three examiners. The student also had to answer any question posed by the examiners taken from any subject during the four-year study period.

    Activities and Societies:
    The Journalism School at the Catholic University of Chile during the time of my studies, offered a very intensive curricula which was required of all students. Included among the required subjects, were philosophy, ethics, mathematics, and other subjects in addition to the subjects directly related to journalism, like writing, grammar, speech, communication theory, news reporting and news research, etc. Out of 40 initial students, 20 remained by the end of the first year.
