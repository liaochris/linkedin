Deborah D. Roberts, Ph.D.’s Summary

I am a Principal of Flavor Science Consulting LLC, providing scientific project management in the area of flavor chemistry. My goal is to improve the flavor, quality, and consumer acceptance of food and beverages by a better understanding of taste and aroma.

After graduating with my Ph.D. from Cornell University, I worked at the Nestle Research Center for 7 years as a research flavor chemist and project leader. I performed applied research and applied the results to a number of Nestle products including sugar confectionery, petfood, coffee, and dairy products.

From 2003 to the present time, I have consulted in the flavor science area, where my clients have included several Fortune 500 food companies. I have worked on projects that determine the key flavor compounds in their products, and correlated these results to sensory and consumer studies. I have guided research direction in flavor release, performed literature reviews, and given presentations on specialized subjects. I also served as an expert witness in a patent infrigement lawsuit.

I am the first or senior author on 26 peer-reviewed publications in the area of flavor chemistry including 10 articles in the Journal of Agricultural and Food Chemistry and 15 peer-reviewed book chapters. I also co-edited 2 books, “Flavor Release” and “Flavor Perception”, and co-organized two corresponding symposiums.

I am a member of the editorial board for the Flavour and Fragrance Journal and a professional member of the Institute of Food Technologists. As a member of the American Chemical Society (ACS), I served as the Flavor Chemistry subdivision chair.

I received several scientific awards, including a National Science Foundation Graduate Fellowship, an ACS-AGFD graduate research award, Institute of Food Technologists Graduate Scholarships, and the Purdue University Senior Medal for outstanding senior in the School of Agriculture.
