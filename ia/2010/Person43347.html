Corey Olin’s Summary

Corey Olin is a nine-year experienced creative director, graphic designer and illustrator whose design history lies in recognizable branding, ground-up redesigns, clean logo design, team management, marketing and managing print publications, character design for animation, product concept, script writing, website layout, maps and brochures. His visual styles are almost entirely flexible, certainly adaptable, able to target any market.

In January 2008, Olin invented, produced and funded Canino Magazine: a prototype and sleek 100-page design magazine for urbanites with pets in Spain. Olin worked from concept to completion, finishing with 10,000 free hand-distributed prints. His project has received tremendous feedback from subscribers, design bloggers and publicists. Canino was featured in the October 2008 Interactive Annual of Communication Arts Magazine, while being previously spoken of in Spain's most widely-read lifestyle publication, EPS.

Olin currently designs for varying markets, including projects for VANS Shoes, Heineken, Rockefeller Center, entertainers, fashion labels, publishing, educational and online softwares, retailers and business solutions.

Corey's portfolio grows with projects big and small. The concerns of efficiency and low-cost solutions are reflected in all of Corey's work and actions without sacrificing quality or detail. Articulate, friendly, fast and professional, Olin is always on the lookout for interesting new work.
