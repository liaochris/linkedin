Geoffrey Hoffman’s Summary

It's no secret... the web is constantly and rapidly evolving. What's more, the speed at which the web is changing is accelerating.

Even for the largest of companies, keeping up with the latest technology can be a daunting task requiring teams of dedicated IT personnel and full-time creative staff, big budgets and a commitment to staying on top of it all. Yet this is true regardless of a company's size. Staying current takes investment, dedication and ongoing attention. But small and medium-sized companies actually have the advantage of being more nimble and quick to react to trends.

It should come as no surprise that many companies often have a tough time evaluating the best online marketing investments, which opportunities to test, and what platforms to evaluate, particularly as budgets are shrinking in uncertain economic times. Yet one thing remains constant: the demand for a measurable ROI. Growth is woven into the fabric of our culture.

Fifteen years ago we called it Interactive Multimedia. Now it goes by many names... digital communication, Websites, online software, rich internet applications... the truth is, it's all about interactive multimedia. Interactive media has been proven to be the most memorable and effective communication platform again and again, in countless studies over nearly three decades.

The elegant collection of animation, graphics, text, video, sound and music is what moves today's consumer, and will be required for tomorrow's generation. The web made delivering these experiences instantaneous, on-demand, and affordable.

For years, my personal goal has been to help companies navigate the complex digital landscape and to derive the greatest possible return on their technology investments. From the front end to the back end, offsite to onsite, every pixel makes a difference, and every byte matters. Ironically, it's all still just zeros and ones.
