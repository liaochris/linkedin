Sheila "Sarah" Birnbach’s Summary

- Author of "Focusing on the Tip of the Iceberg: Tools for Achieving Success in Your Organization"

- Author of "Using Meetings to Facilitate Change" in The Executive Officer (Spring, 1994) and "Hiring Shouldn't Be a Gamble" in Rental Management (July, 1994) and National Focus (Fall, 2006) and a chapter entitled "Getting the Most Out of Meetings: Employing Quality to Plan and Conduct Them" in Adding Value to the Public Affairs Function: Using Quality to Improve Performance, Public Affairs Council, 1994.

- Personal and Professional Coach offering one-on-one coaching to executives to improve their leadership skills, enable them to achieve work/life balance, and enable them to generate the best for their organizations.

- Completed a year-long management study for the Jefferson County Commission (WV). Interviewed each county employee under the County Commissioners, and developed 78 position descriptions, a new Employee Handbook, a recommended training plan for employes, and a series of recommendations for strengthening the human resources of the county government.

- Cited in several publications including Funworld (May 2008) publication of the International Association of Amusement Parks and Attractions; CEO Update (July 18, 2008); Associations Now (January 2008) published by ASAE & The Center for Association Leadership; Chamber Executive (September 1991) published by the American Chamber of Commerce Executives

- Served on Curriculum Committee, Institute for Organization Management, U S Chamber of Commerce 2006-2007

- Named as one of the "Outstanding Young Women of America" in 1980

Sheila "Sarah" Birnbach’s Specialties:

Training/workshops in customer service, team building, communication, supervisory and management skills, employee retention, managing the generation mix and diversity.

Provide executive and one-on-one life coaching combining 30+ years in the business environment and 6 1/2 years as a clinical therapist.
