Shelley Ross’s Experience

  • Independent Broadcast Media Producer

    Author, Speaker and Writer for Independent News Projects

    (Broadcast Media industry)

    January 2008 — Present (2 years 11 months)

    Shelley Ross is an award-winning producer who changed the landscape of morning, primetime and late night television over the course of her career. She is perhaps best known for her 17 years at ABC News where she won three Emmys, a Peabody award
    and other honors. As executive producer of Good Morning America, Ross brought together the legendary anchor team of Diane Sawyer and Charles Gibson.

    In late 2007, CBS News hired Ross as senior executive producer to relaunch CBS News’ Early Show. During her 23-week tenure, she increased the key demographics of the show by 22%, adding 195,000 new viewers (25-54). This, during a news cycle in which every other daily network news broadcast was down year to year.

    Most recently, Ross produced and directed a unique “passion project,” one-hour PBS Special, “4TROOPS: Live From the Intrepid.” 4TROOPS, the new music group signed to Sony Masterworks, are all combat veterans of the wars in Iraq and Afghanistan who are donating considerable proceeds from CD and DVD sales to veterans causes. Instead of a traditional music concert, Ross created an emotional and uplifting “pop opera,” with a musical and visual story arc designed to acknowledge and honor the sacrifices of all troops and their families.

  • Executive Producer -- Good Morning America

    ABC News

    (Public Company; DIS; Broadcast Media industry)

    February 1990 — September 2007 (17 years 8 months)

    She is perhaps best known for her 17 years at ABC News where she won three Emmys, a Peabody award and other honors while she continually spiked the ratings and grew the audience with
    unparalleled editorial decisions and her legendary marketing and promotion skills. As executive producer of Good Morning America, Ross brought together the legendary anchor team of Diane Sawyer and Charles Gibson. She created “big event” television
    which was often newsmaking, always moving. She was the first ever to broadcast live from a battleship during wartime -- from the decks of U.S.S. Enterprise at an undisclosed location during the early months of the war on terror. She broadcast a 2-hour live special on gun control issues from the White House with Bill and Hillary Clinton five weeks after Columbine. She was the first to originate a live tv show from the Vatican, commemorating the Pope’s 25 anniversary of his leadership; from the Pentagon, one year after 9/11 to reopen the wing destroyed by the hijackers; and from the Tower of London on the 50th anniversary of the Queen’s accession to the throne.

    During her tenure, Ross added nearly a million viewers and boosted GMA income revenue from $19M to nearly $100M annually. Her morning trajectory continued even after she became executive producer of PrimeTime Llve and she was credited with
    driving GMA within 40,000 viewers of Today.

    At ABC, Ross also executive produced David Blaine’s Drowned Alive, a week-long live event in Lincoln Center which ended with the successful live 2-hour televised ABC entertainment special which in the last half hour beat the season finale of “24,” one of
    toughest nights of the season.
