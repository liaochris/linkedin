Ahmad Takatkah’s Summary

A young venture capitalist from Jordan with over six years of experience in business development and capacity building for technology startups focusing on:
- Business planning,
- Angel and venture capital investment advisory,
- Investors-entrepreneurs matchmaking,
- Training on entrepreneurship, capital raising, investment process, and term sheets negotiations.

Currently working as a business development manager at National Net Ventures (N2V), a VC firm focusing on web and mobile web ventures in the MENA region based in Riyadh, KSA.

Successfully conducted matchmaking sessions between 30 Arab startups and 20 regional investors through the 6th Investing in Technology Forum held in Cairo, May 2009.

Helped over 100 Jordanian entrepreneurs from several industries to participate in Empretec program (the United Nations’ entrepreneurship capacity building program under the UNCTAD division).

As a consultant, worked on business development and market research freelance tasks for several companies between Amman and Dubai to help them access the Middle East market and find local partners.

As an entrepreneur, won the MIT Arab Business Plan Competition 2009, and Queen Rania Business Plan Competition 2006. In early twenties, founded and managed an online business company for which he successfully raised angel investment, but the company lasted for only one year before the partners decided to shut it down.

As a trainer, has delivered several training course in business planning for:
- “Queen Rania Center for Entrepreneurship QRCE”,
- “Jordanian Engineers Association JEA”,
- “Arab Science and Technology Foundation ASTF”

As a Mentor, mentored 3 of the winning business plans in the QRCE business plan competition for three consecutive years.
