Steve Flanders’s Experience

  • Principal System Administrator

    EMC

    (Public Company; EMC; Information Technology and Services industry)

    December 2008 — Present (1 year 9 months)

    EMC is the world's leading developer and provider of information infrastructure technology and solutions. Cloud Technical Operations (Tech-Ops) is the production department for EMC's Cloud initiative and we work closely with the Cloud Infrastructure Group (CIG) responsible for designing and maintaining the hardware and software required for the day-to-day. I work as a Principal System Administrator specializing in VMware virtualization as well as scripting and automation. I am responsible for creating formalized infrastructure documentation and operating procedures. Within six month of being hired, I was promoted to a lead position and I am now responsible for overseeing all System Administrators in the infrastructure build team. Shortly after my first year, I was promoted from Senior to Principal System Administrator.

    My work in Tech-Ops includes: spearheading two ATT Synaptic Storage as a Service datacenters, which utilized EMC Cloud Optimized Storage (COS): Atmos; architecting up to $500,000 management racks for customer sites; supporting over 300 Cloud engineers; increasing virtualized environment resouces 20-fold; maintaining five vCenter Server instances for a variety of development, staging, and production environments; automating Cloud software deployments; budgeting expenses for fiscal year; and creating/maintaining the Active Directory forest used by the CIG. I have also written several operational and procedural documents used to standardize data center as well as application deployment and maintenance. Going forward, I will be leading operations approach to maintaining EMC's public Cloud offering: Atmos Online.

  • System and Release Engineer

    Phase Forward

    (Public Company; PFWD; Computer Software industry)

    June 2007 — December 2008 (1 year 7 months)

    Phase Forward is the leading provider of integrated data management solutions in the pharmaceutical fields of clinical trials and drug safety. The Systems and Release Engineering (SRE) Team is part of the engineering department and is responsible for assisting software developers and product testers throughout the product cycle. As a Systems Engineer, I designed, planned, and executed a multi-phase server consolidation project virtualizing over 70% of the research and development laboratory using VMware ESX and Converter technologies. I was also responsible for configuring and maintaining three EMC CLARiiON SANs, over 50 HP ProLiant (BL20P, DL145, DL360, DL580, DL585) servers, and over 500 (ESX, Workstation) virtual machines while providing technical support to over 100 employees.

    I played a central role in working to consolidate and improve the building, installation, and ongoing maintenance of servers and services within the engineering department. I did this through standardization of hardware, virtual machine templates, and automated, unattended application installations for products including Oracle, Visual Studio, and VMware Workstation using primarily Microsoft scripting languages. My experience and knowledge of Perl, PowerShell, and Batch enabled me to provide valuable tools to monitor capacity utilization, enabling better planning and budgeting for future infrastructure requirements.

  • System Administrator

    MIT Lincoln Laboratory

    (Educational Institution; Defense & Space industry)

    June 2005 — June 2007 (2 years 1 month)

    The MIT Lincoln Laboratory is a federally funded research and development center chartered to apply advanced technology to problems of national security. I worked in Group 39, Air Defense Techniques, where I received Secret Security clearance from the Department of Defense. I was responsible for over 100 users and over 100 workstations on a variety of operating systems including CentOS, Microsoft, Red Hat, and SuSE. In addition to assisting users, I played a central role in designing and implementing a RHEL3 Primary Domain Controller (PDC) utilizing NIS for authentication and Samba for file sharing in order to replace an unsupported Windows 2000 PDC. The new PDC was responsible for authenticating all users on all operating systems as well as automounting Linux operating system home directories. To automate account integration, creation, and maintenance, I wrote custom Perl and bash scripts.
