Kathryn Senior’s Summary

I am a well established and highly regarded science and medical journalist, writer and editor. With academic and pharmaceutical industry experience, I am used to working with corporate clients and large organisations, as well as established journals and learned societies.

I have recently completed several large projects for a leading medical communications agency, working on slide decks, training documents, drug development information, guideline updates, core claims, objection handling and doing editing and reference checking as required. My pharmaceutical industry expertise also extends to covering conferences and one-day meetings and I have been appointed as the medical writer on several meetings during the last year for the Biotherapy Development Association.

As an experienced news writer for medical journals such as The Lancet Infectious Diseases and The Lancet Oncology, I regularly produce concise and insightful research reports, usually to very tight deadlines. I liaise with experts from all over the world and am comfortable communicating with academics in all areas of science allied to biology and medicine. I have completed four major feature articles for the Bulletin of the World Health Organisation in the last year and I continue to write regularly for Frontiers, the flagship journal of the Ecological Society of America.

During the last three years I have worked with Freelance Copy on SEO copywriting projects for a range of corporate clients in the UK and internationally. I have specialised in science and medicine but have extended my work into other fields, particularly IT, web copywriting and press releases. I took over as Senior Writer at Freelance Copy in April 2009 and I will be continuing in this role during 2010, managing client projects and a database of more than 300 freelance writers, overseeing and providing quality control on a variety of projects.

science writing, medical writing, pharmaceutical agency work, SEO copywriting, science education, infectious diseases, rheumatoid diseases, neurology and cancer
