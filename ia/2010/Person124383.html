Leticia A. Jones’s Summary

Leticia A. Jones brings more than 11 years of experience as a government consultant, grant project manager, frequent speaker, and national trainer for private, public and not-for-profit sector organizations. As Director of Grants for Grady Health System, one of the largest public health systems in the United States, she is responsible for creating and directing a centralized tracking, monitoring and oversight function within the Finance Department to ensure compliance with government grant requirements organization-wide.

Leticia has long served as a grant advisor to cities and counties across the nation to solve challenging issues through creative and effective solutions. She is a subject-matter expert for various Federal, State and local grant programs providing critical guidance to meet the application, reporting and compliance requirements, including the American Reinvestment and Recovery Act. Leticia previously worked with a grants research and technology firm as a subject-matter expert and consultant to guide product development, as well as design and implementation of a full grant lifecycle training series for grant recipients. In addition, she worked with the Veterans Medical Research Foundation in collaboration with the Veterans Affairs San Diego Healthcare System and University of San Diego to administer millions of dollars of National Institutes of Health (NIH) and other research-related grant funds. Leticia served as a project manager to the City of Jacksonville, Florida, where she acquired over $22.9 million in competitive Federal and state grants and managed more than 100 capital improvement projects totaling more than $68 million.

Leticia graduated from Providence College with a dual B.A. in Poli Sci and Modern Languages and earned her MPA from Troy University while living in Okinawa, Japan. She is an AmeriCorps VISTA alum and member of the American Association of Grants Professionals (AAGP) and the National Grants Management Association (NGMA).
