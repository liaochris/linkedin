Chris Huge’s Summary

Analytical Acumen:
While my education places a high emphasis on decision making, it is critical that such decisions be rooted in an analysis of data and evidence. That is why I made sure to emphasize not only strategy classes but also statistics, corporate financial analysis, and law throughout my MBA degree. This training continues to create the foundation on which I base my project management work today. For example, when I was tasked with managing the roll out of a new performance management system to Babson’s staff, I first did extensive benchmarking of other Colleges’ systems and forms, compiled primary research on the subject, and established a focus group on campus to survey their thoughts and opinions.

Entrepreneurial Mindset:
At a basic level I am excited by novel concepts and new applications of proven ideas. My professional history has instilled a deep appreciation for the power of entrepreneurial thinking and its impact on all types of organizations. When I was the technical director of a theater on Cape Cod I was able to make up for a budget shortfall by utilizing excess capacity in the scenery shop to build sets for other area theaters, thus creating new revenue with existing fixed assets. This and other experiences proved to me that opportunity obsession is not just a game for start-ups but can invigorate companies of all sizes.

These two principles form what I think of as the learning loop. Thought leads to action. Action leads to thought.
