Jennifer (Stauss) Windrum’s Summary

I began my career in 1997 as a general assignment TV news reporter for KMTV-Channel 3, the CBS affiliate in Omaha, Nebraska.

After three years in the news business, I made the transition to the “other side” - public relations - where I became press secretary for Omaha Mayor Hal Daub. In 1999, Mayor Daub’s approval rating among the voters of Omaha was at 32%. Nearly 2 ½ years later, despite his losing bid for re-election, my team helped catapult the Mayor’s approval rating to nearly 70% among Omaha voters.

Following my political stint, I jumped into agency life, where I built a public relations department at Ervin & Smith Public Relations and Advertising in Omaha from zero to four full-time employees in nine months. I was privileged to work on prominent accounts, such as Ameritrade (now TD Ameritrade), Lindsay Manufacturing, HearthStone Homes, among others.

I moved to Swanson Russell, Nebraska's largest advertising, public relations, communications firm in 2004 as Public Relations Counsel. I was promoted to public relations manager at Swanson Russell associates due to existing client and new business growth. I created a public relations department (Omaha office) from zero to five full-time employees in three years.

I provided PR/social media strategy development and execution of comprehensive public relations programs, primarily in consumer and business-to-business categories, for a wide variety of clients, such as Land O Lakes Purina Mills, Schering-Plough Animal Health, Creighton University Medical Center, Sergeant's Pet Care Products, Inc., among others.

I am currently Social Media Strategist at the Bonnie J Addario Lung Cancer Foundation, where I develop and execute PR/social media programs along with the lung cancer campaign I created called "WTF?" (Where's the Funding) for Lung Cancer?

I also founded the True Dirty Laundry blog that holds companies/people/organizations accountable for their actions (or inactions) in a very public way.

Jennifer (Stauss) Windrum’s Specialties:

Social media public/media relations strategy; media relations on international, national, regional, state and local levels; crisis communications; reputation management; media training; media spokesperson; media event planning and coordination; writing public relations materials, such as speeches, media statements, articles, talking points, news releases and various social media networking content.
