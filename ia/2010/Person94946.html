Jennifer Gill Roberts’s Summary

I've spent the past twenty years funding and building IP and wireless infrastructure, applications and service companies.

I've taken a thematic approach to investing, with a focus on the evolutionary capabilities of the network. I believe Mobility software is the Fifth Wave of technology innovation. The mobile device has become the primary vehicle for computing and communications, driven primarily by youth, people in emerging economies, and nomadic workers.

In the fall of 2008, I co-founded Vive, with Doug Keare. Vive turns your cell phone into a virtual health coach. We make it fun to “get it done” through our humorous coaching voice and game-like status and rewards. I founded Vive because I am passionate about health and wellness and believe a mobile application has the power to motivate people to more effectively manage their health.

Prior to Vive, I spent 15 years in venture capital as a founder and Managing Partner at Maven Venture Partners and a General Partner at Sevin Rosen Funds, leading successful investments in emerging markets including broadband equipment, IP platforms and services, and mobile applications.

I co-founded and served as Chair of the Forum for Women Entrepreneurs (FWE) for over a decade. FWE has 1000 + members including venture-backed CEOs, venture and law firm partners and senior corporate executives. (FWE is now the Forum for Women Entrepreneurs and Executives and is celebrating its 16th year at www.fweande.org.)

I served on the Advisory Board of the College of Arts & Science at the University of Texas at Austin, the Stanford School of Engineering Venture Fund and a number of additional non-profit boards. I am a Senior Fellow of the American Leadership Forum.

Prior to my career in venture, I led product and technical efforts at Apple Computer, Hewlett-Packard Company and Sun Microsystems.

My social passions are creating educational opportunities for children and youth sports.

    “Classic” early-stage venture fund which focused on investment opportunities in Mobility. I co-founded the fund with George Richard and Jack Gill.

    Investment Thesis: Mobility
    Mobility is the Fifth Wave. It is the most compelling investment sector in alternative investments. Mobility is defined by the convergence of the enterprise, consumer internet, and communications infrastructure. Maven Venture Partners invested in the platforms, applications, and services that enable Mobility.

    Four previous technology cycles built the foundation for Mobility. During the past decade we have seen the proliferation of mobile devices, the advent of the always-on network, and the explosion of enterprise and consumer digital content. Investment opportunities in mobile include: advertising, messaging, consumer applications, and network intelligence.

Leveraged my technical background and operating experience from Hewlett Packard and Sun Microsystems to build a successful early-stage venture franchise in Communications.
