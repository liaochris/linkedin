Joseph Yeh’s Summary

Joseph has counseled and provided political expertise for government agencies, political campaigns and nonprofit organizations in Washington D.C., Philadelphia, Sacramento, Los Angeles and the San Francisco Bay Area.

In 2004, his efforts were an integral part of the successful No Child Left Behind educational outreach to the Asian American community nationwide, which included then-U.S. Secretary of Education, Rod Paige and reported back to the White House.

As an unwavering leader and literary contributor in over 15 nonprofit organizations, his core interests involve veteran issues, education, and ethnic awareness. As an auxiliary member for the oldest Chinese American veterans post in the nation, American Legion Cathay Post #384, he has executed advance work for United Nation Ambassadors and U.S. Cabinet officials. He belongs to the Churchill Club, City Club of San Francisco, Public Relations Society of America, Commonwealth Club, Lead21, OPEN Silicon Valley, SCEA, and SF Symphony.

He has been involved in fundraising, event planning, newsletter writing, and community relations for the National Association of Asian American Professionals of San Francisco, Asian Business League of San Francisco, Asian Pacific Islander American Public Affairs Association and Asian Americans for Good Government PAC. He helped start the Judge Lillian K. Sing Scholarship and Bok Pon JROTC/ROTC Award Trust Fund.

Critical to his success has been a constant awareness of confirming support from over 140 features in both national and local television, radio and print outlets including ABC’s WBRZ, CBS’s 5 & 11 Evening News, San Jose Mercury News, The Seattle Times, World Journal, and Sing Tao Daily.

He is a small shareholder in Pluto, a luxury home automation company, targeting the Middle East/Asia markets.

His passions include theatre, travel, fine arts, French cuisine and expanding his extensive dvd collection.
