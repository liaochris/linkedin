Katy Lindemann’s Summary

Hello. I find writing about myself in the third person a bit weird, so let's just start things off in a more friendly way. Hello there, and thank you for stopping by.

I'm a senior strategist and creative geek at Naked Communications, where we like to use clever comms thinking to come up with creative solutions to business problems, and to create engaging and genuinely useful interactions between people and brands.

Over the last 5 or so years, I've tried to apply my brainpower to developing strategic solutions to build brands, drive behavioural change, and generate sales & ROI across a wide range of clients and brands.

I prefer to think of myself a strategist for the digital world rather than a digital strategist, as I believe that interactive and social ideas and behaviours can (and should) live in both the on and offline worlds.

In 2009 I teamed up with terrific brains like John Willshire, Mark Earls, Faris Yakob & Amelia Torode as a founding member of the IPA Social collective, developing IPA guidance for social communication and changing the way the IPA approach the social space.

Some very nice people have asked me to speak at conferences like the APG Battle of Big Thinking 2009, Social Media 2009, Playful 2009 and Interesting 09. I've also been asked to deliver guest lectures at Bournemouth University and speak at events like the IPA Stage 2 course and the Omnicom summer school.

The lovely team at Mediaweek also named me as one of their ’30 Under 30’ in 2009.

I did actually gain a history degree from Oxford University but seem to fail miserably in the history rounds of every pub quiz I've ever attended.

My slightly rubbish claim to fame is that I was one of the first ever UK bloggers, having started blogging back in 2000 when all the UK bloggers still knew each other, when it was still called 'weblogging' and everyone thought it was a fad that would never take off.
