Dan Kaplan’s Summary

I had harbored dreams of a byline in "The New Yorker," but decided the model was doomed. I became a tech entrepreneur, started a recommendation engine company and worked on it until it became obvious that the world was doing just fine without what we'd built.

I deemed it necessary to survey the landscape before making another move. As it happened, the opportunity to do exactly this appeared on VentureBeat, Matt Marshall's rising star of a blog.

As a writer for VentureBeat, I wrote about companies arrayed across social media, search, online advertising, mobile and e-commerce, with a bit of AI and other mind-bending future-tech mixed in to keep things surreal.

I moved to the business side of VentureBeat, where I ran product management, planned and marketed conferences, sold advertising and did all the other sorts of things one does on the business side of a world-class blog.

Then I became an analyst for a data company called YouNoodle/Quid, where I wrote reports about opportunities in emerging technology for large technology clients, focusing on enterprise social computing, social games and mobile applications.

Now, I'm a Product Marketer for Salesforce.com. The evolution continues.

Dan Kaplan’s Specialties:

Leveraging data to drive design; creating excellent content, tracking the tech startup landscape.
