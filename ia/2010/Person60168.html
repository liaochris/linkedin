Dr. Scott Coon, DC’s Summary

It's hard for me to believe that over the past 16 years, I've treated more than 6,000 people, taught more than 2500 chiropractic students and delivered in excess of 200,000 chiropractic adjustments. I've met a lot of outstanding people and had many rewarding experiences. I look forward to the next 30 years doing what I love best.

Here's a breakdown of my professional responsibilities:

DOCTOR OF CHIROPRACTIC
I've been a family chiropractor for over 15 years. I specialize in helping people improve the quality of their lives. The number one reason people come to my office is to get out of pain. I am trained to manage "neuromusculoskeletal pain". That's a fancy way of saying, when structure and function compromise your health... a chiropractor is the specialist you should see.

ADJUNCT ASSISTANT PROFESSOR
Since 1997, I have taught part-time at New York Chiropractic College in the Basic Sciences and Clinical Sciences Departments. I enjoy teaching our future Doctors of Chiropractic.

BUSINESS MANAGER / CONSULTANT
The BodyMind Center at Thompson Health in Canandaigua delivers a wide variety of Integrative Complementary Medicine. I manage the providers and the office procedures of the Center.

SO... IF YOU DON'T ALREADY HAVE A CHIROPRACTOR,
WOULD YOU LIKE ONE?
Searching for a healthcare professional you feel comfortable with is a difficult task. I invite new clients to set up a "Complimentary Consultation" at no charge to allow you to meet me, discuss your needs and answer your questions. Then we can move on to the next step of setting up an appointment for treatment.

My treatment philosophy is pretty simple. I believe in using chiropractic care "when you need it". Once your problem is solved, I'll dismiss you from care until the next time you need me. Most patients respond well to treatment after just one visit. Then usually 4-5 additional visits are needed for follow-up and case management of the initial patient diagnosis.
