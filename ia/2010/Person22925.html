Betsy Ko’s Summary

I am thrilled to be entering a new chapter in my professional life as Music Department Head at Chicago High School for the Arts! ChiArts will open in September 2009 with a class of freshman and will add a new class each year until full enrollment is reached in the 2012-2013 school year. For a city of Chicago's size and significance, we are long overdue for a premiere public institution to provide young people with both a high level of artistic training and a rigorous academic curriculum. It is invigorating to roll up my sleeves with curriculum design and I am looking forward to building an outstanding community of music learning!

Teaching the flute remains an essential component of my professional life to which I am passionately committed. Complementing my work with private students, I also have extensive experience working with flute students in groups.

As a performer, I am a freelancer working in a wide variety of settings. Highlights of my performance resume include being a soloist on “Live from Studio One” on WFMT and at the National Flute Association’s annual convention. I can also be heard performing on the compact disc “Dancing on the Edge of a Volcano”, a collection of Jewish cabaret music released in 2002 by Cedille Records.

Betsy Ko’s Specialties:

Private flute instruction, chamber music coaching, flute choir repertoire and direction, blogging, writing, music curriculum development, event planning

Betsy Ko’s Experience

    Chicago High School for the Arts

    (Education Management industry)

    May 2009 — Present (1 year 5 months)

    Chicago High School for the Arts (ChiArts) is scheduled to welcome its first class of freshmen in September 2009. Students will have college preparatory academics in the mornings, followed by pre-professional arts training for three hours every afternoon. Arts majors include music, dance, theatre and visual arts. I am tremendously excited to have the opportunity to build this music program from its inception and am also looking forward to being in the classroom.

  • Flute Faculty

    Merit School of Music

    (Non-Profit; Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    November 1994 — Present (15 years 11 months)

    I am lucky to teach along the entire spectrum of Merit's continuum of programs. My students range from beginners in the public schools picking up a flute for the first time to an advanced high school quartet that is at competition level. I often judge for local competitions and also greatly value opportunities to share ideas with teaching colleagues through my involvement in the Chicago Flute Club. Please keep informed of my teaching activities through my flute studio blog: http://koflutestudio.edublogs.org

  • Director of Bridges: Partners in Music

    Merit School of Music

    (Non-Profit; Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    October 2006 — May 2009 (2 years 8 months)

    As Director of Bridges: Partners in Music, I was responsible for the quality and smooth operations of Merit's offsite instruction programs in the community. Bridges serves approximately 5,000 children at over 80 sites throughout Chicago and Northwest Indiana. Merit's Bridges sites include Chicago Public Schools, private schools, community centers and shelters. With each program presenting a unique blend of students and logistical variables, I worked closely with both my faculty and the site administrators to ensure tailored instructional delivery for each group of children. Each spring I oversaw the execution of MeritFest, an annual two day event in which approximately 1,000 Bridges students come together to perform at Symphony Center. I also worked closely with other Merit staff to facilitate opportunities for interested Bridges students to join Merit's advanced on-site programs. As the numbers above indicate, this position challenged me to juggle a dazzling array of details! It was critical in sharpening my skills in crafting successful partnerships and also gave me an invaluable perspective on the challenges of urban music education.

  • Associate Director of Bridges: Partners in Music

    Merit School of Music

    (Non-Profit; Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    November 2005 — October 2006 (1 year )

    I held this position for the eleven months prior to stepping into the role of Bridges Director. My responsibilities included ordering supplies for the faculty, coordinating instrument inventory and repair, entering data for monthly faculty payroll and overseeing the Tuition Paying Schools program. I also prepared proposals for prospective new sites which gave me experience in creating quotes and guiding potential partners to the best possible fit of programming. Coming from the ranks of Merit's faculty myself, I was fortunate to already know most of the Bridges teachers and was able to concentrate on building relationships with many of the Bridges site administrators. My tenure as Associate Director was an essential period of learning the Bridges program's nuts and bolts and was a huge asset for me as I transitioned to Bridges Director.

  • Research Technologist

    University of Chicago

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    1991 — 2005 (14 years )

    Besides studying math, I also did a solid slate of science coursework in college. My first job was as a technician in one of Brown's labs studying liver regeneration. After a second technician position at Case Western Reserve University, I joined the laboratory of Dr. William N. Green at University of Chicago (Department of Neurobiology, Pharmacology and Physiology). The lab's research objective is to understand the structure and assembly process of the the acetylcholine receptor. My responsibilities included both participating in the lab's research activities and also overseeing supplies and safety in the lab. I was an extremely lucky musician to have a "day job" that was so challenging and enriching!

  • Founder & Director of University Flute Ensemble

    University of Chicago

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    September 1994 — June 1998 (3 years 10 months)

    University Flute Ensemble was a performance ensemble of fifteen flutists performing music from all eras. I conducted weekly rehearsals with the group in preparation for three concerts each year. I conceived the idea for UFE in response to a demand for more performance opportunities for the many flutists within the University of Chicago community. Barbara Schubert, Director of Performance Programs, was immensely supportive of my efforts and also served as a wonderful mentor as I developed the ensemble. My experience of working with University Flute Ensemble was critical in sharpening my skills in the areas of concert programming, event publicity, rehearsal technique and group motivation.
