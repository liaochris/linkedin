James C. Mclane III’s Summary

Licensed Professional Engineer (Mechanical) with multidisciplinary experience in design, materials, structures, fluid dynamics, heat transfer, and technical writing. Active in the oil/gas/petrochemical and aerospace industries for over 35 years. Career includes private and government clients on both domestic and foreign projects.

  • Senior Principal Mechanical Engineer

    Gulf Interstate Engineering

    (Privately Held; Oil & Energy industry)

    November 2006 — September 2009 (2 years 11 months)

    My last assignment: proposals for new business and response to bid solicitations.

    Engineering effort to expand the Russian Caspian Sea oil pipeline system.

    Risk evaluation and integrity management for Marathon Pipeline assets at Cook Inlet Alaska

    2008 I was the Lead Mechanical Engineer on Chevron's Escravos Nigeria (Africa) Terminal upgrade.

    2006-8 provided design and technical support for one of the world’s greatest new gas pipelines. The 48” Reliance East-West project spanning 1000 miles across India uses 30 turbine driven compressors (41,000 HP each) to move 3,000 MM SCFD of high pressure natural gas. I reviewed compressor and turbine plans, coordinated technical support to procure huge pipeline gas coolers, 1.5 mw electric generators for each compressor station, flow scrubbers, filters and metering, blowdown silencers, and many other items of equipment.

  • Design Engineer

    United Space Alliance

    (Privately Held; Defense & Space industry)

    1998 — 2006 (8 years )

    Personally designed unique electromechanical equipment and tools for a NASA lab. Examples include high-pressure oxygen machinery used to recharge Astronaut emergency breathing tanks, equipment to prepare and package Astronaut food, service cameras and other flight crew equipment, and devices to test and maintain complex systems like the Space Shuttle’s Food Galley and Space Suits. A summary of personal designs and inventions is available.

  • Systems Engineer

    Boeing Aerospace Operations

    (Aviation & Aerospace industry)

    1986 — 1998 (12 years )

    As a result of the Space Shuttle Challenger explosion, I was hired to review the safety and reliability of the vehicle's mechanical systems. I wrote standards documents (JSC 17481, JSC 16994), helped write NASA STD 3000 and refined designs for the new Space Station Freedom project. As part of the Space Station Loads and Dynamics Working Group, I worked to establish structural criteria like design safety factors and allowable stresses.

  • Senior Engineer

    Gulf Interstate Engineering

    (Privately Held; Oil & Energy industry)

    1980 — 1985 (5 years )

    I helped design the world’s most ambitious construction/engineering project, a pipeline buried in Alaskan permafrost to bring North Slope natural gas down to the lower 48 states. Cost estimates were 20- 40 billion in 1982 dollars. Hired for my special expertise in thermally insulated pipelines, I coordinated an international program to develop a suitable insulation system. Besides simulation tests in a cold environmental chamber, the effort included field bending insulated pipe in Canada under arctic winter conditions. I wrote the test reports and final recommendations.

    I also worked on other significant projects while at Gulf. Most were located in Saudi Arabia.

  • Project Engineer

    Brown and Root

    (Public Company; KBR; Construction industry)

    1973 — 1980 (7 years )

    Initially joined Brown and Root’s Solids Processing Engineering Department. After designing a conveyor system for a limestone crushing plant I transferred into the Marine Terminals and Pipeline Engineering group to perform complex flow and thermal calculations. I became responsible for project conceptual design when it involved fluid or gas performance prediction, pumps, compressors, flow measurement, thermal management and large valves. I received a company commendation for instructing a six month technical course on fluid dynamics. I took formal one-year company training to learn “Project Engineering” and became a licensed professional (PE) Engineer so I could legally supervise more significant jobs.

    Accepted challenging foreign assignment in Baghdad, Iraq where I examined and critiqued Russian-designed pipelines for the Iraq National Oil Co.

  • Design Engineer

    Designed equipment for a small firm attempting to perfect a cheap seawater desalination machine.

  • Associate Engineer

    Mooney Aircraft

    (Airlines/Aviation industry)

    1969 — 1970 (1 year )

    One of ten engineers in a five hundred employee plant. My focus was engine installations. At that time, Mooney produced one airplane each day and was the world’s third largest builder of private aircraft.
