James Fishman’s Summary

I've been a licensed mental health professional since l981. I began work in Boston, where I worked as a psychotherapist/clinical social worker at Fenway Community Health Center (from 1981 - 85), where I was one of the founders of the AIDS Action Committe of Massachusetts. I moved to San Francisco in '85 to assume the position of Director of Men's Clinical Services at Operation Concern/New Leaf (GLBT outpatient clinic), where I supervised staff, graduate student interns, and volunteer clinicians. I'm very active in both the American Group Psychotherapy Association and the Northern California Group Psychotherapy Society, where I am a frequent workshop leader. My work has been published in professional and lay journals, most notably 2 chapters in Therapists on the Front Lines: Psychotherapy with Gay Men in the Age of AIDS-- chapters were on a sexually compulsive group for gay men which I co-ran, and also one on how the "therapeutic frame" needs to be flexible in dealing with the HIV crisis. I currently run a Gay men's intimacy group and co-lead a co-ed, interpersonal group for adult men and women.

James Fishman’s Specialties:

Individual, couple, and group psychotherapy. Gay Men's issues, sexual compulsivity; teaching and consultation to therapists and interns on group stages and leadership roles in groups.
