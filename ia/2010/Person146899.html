Misha Janette Fleming’s Summary

After graduating from Japan's most esteemed fashion institution Bunka Fashion College, I quickly established myself as a successful freelancer in the Japanese fashion industry.

My scope of work includes: fashion columnist for national newspapers and international news sites, contributor to numerous high-end fashion magazines, fashion industry translation, costume designer, warbdrobe stylist, TV presenter & reporter, PR & marketing, and designer of a line of accessories.

I have been residing in Tokyo for 5 years, and am in fluent in Japanese conversation, business speak, reading as well as writing.

I strive to stay on top of my game, and thanks to my assignments as a fashion journalist I am able to have my pulse on a wide arena of fashion trends and news, and stick to tough deadlines. As a stylist I have learned about teamwork, stamina, working around budgets and concise organization while honing my eye for color, style, and creativity. I have gained invaluable knowledge on the workings of a successful business in a bilingual environment and multinational corporation. Utilizing both my unique viewpoint in fashion as well as my bilingual skills and international upbringing, I am poised to bring fresh views and ideas while being sensitive to cultural nuances and proceedings.

I have conducted a number of exclusive interviews with designers and other top professionals including:

Marc Jacobs
Francisco Costa (Calvin Klein)
Raf Simons
Neil Barrett
Alexander Wang
Josh Schulman (Jimmy Choo)
Rachel Zoe
Marjan Pejoski...

As a stylist, I have designed costumes for music videos and CD jackets for the artist OLIVIA (avex) and styled for actress Rinko Kikuchi (academy-award winning film Babel) and have been featured and contributed to WGSN, NHK, Fuji Television, TBS, Pulp, and Metropolis magazines
Portfolio: www.mishajanette.com
