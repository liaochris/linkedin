Jonathan Herman’s Summary

Starting his career at Columbia Records in 1990, Jonathan’s journey as a consultant began marketing music release for recording artists such as Mariah Carey, LL Cool J, Nancy Wilson, Diana Krall, Peabo Bryson, Regina Bell and Public Enemy; managing sports marketing campaigns for NBA draft picks Brian Grant, Tyrone Hill and Derek Strong; conducting legal services for actress Kim Fields and Death Row Records; coordinating legislative affairs for Congresswoman Sheila Jackson Lee; and assisting multiple start-up businesses through planning and launch.

Establishing a consultancy in 2002 that served the online branding needs of Strickland & Ashe (sports agency) and others, Jonathan began working full-time with Allan Houston two years later to manage his business and charitable activities – resulting in the development of original programs in fatherhood and entrepreneurship that have served 1,350 participants across the country with emerging opportunities to affect public policy through the Obama Administration.

While serving as Executive Director of the Admiral Center at Living Cities, Jonathan developed methodologies for maximizing celebrity impact in both program development and systems change on a variety of issues. Overseeing all aspects of the Center’s growth and development during his tenure, Jonathan took the initiative from concept to reality and brought to membership Eva Longoria Parker, Chris Paul, Russell Simmons and Allan Houston.

Jonathan's entrepreneurial experience includes the founding of Strong Representation, Aspire Holdings (overseas real estate investment firm), and HitMeOff.Com (online entertainment distributor and subsidiary marketing firm). Jonathan has served as a panelist and judge for various symposiums and business plan competitions for the Kellogg Foundation / Council on Foundations, National Foundation for Teaching Entrepreneurship, Greater Harlem Chamber of Commerce, Tulane University and the Massachusetts Institute of Technology.
