Devin Stewart’s Summary

Currently, I direct the Global Policy Innovations program at the Carnegie Council for Ethics in International Affairs, teach a course on Asia at New York University, and am affiliated with the Center for Strategic and International Studies (CSIS).

I am working closely with the Carnegie Council's leadership to raise the financial, intellectual, and technological capital to transform the institution's outreach capacity, developing the Council's brand, studio concept, and worldwide networks for exchange and distribution of content. My work for the Council has also focused on strengthening relationships with major universities, corporations, and NGOs around the world. Our program convenes the quarterly Workshops for Ethics in Business and publishes the online magazine Policy Innovations.

I am also a senior fellow at the Carnegie Council, researching Asian trade, energy, and soft power issues. I am founding editor of Carnegie Ethics Online and oversee the Carnegie New Leaders program.

Previously, I directed a project at CSIS on US-Japan economic integration on which I briefed the Pentagon, State Department, Treasury Department, and Congressional and intelligence offices. I also worked under Patrick Cronin and CSIS trustees Zbigniew Brzezinski and Brent Scowcroft to develop a research strategy for CSIS, a foreign policy think tank with an annual budget of $25 million. For three years, I chaired the monthly Korea-Japan Study Group in Tokyo and Washington, DC.

My articles have appeared in more than ten languages in numerous publications including Newsweek, Ethics & International Affairs, The Globalist, The Wall Street Journal, The International Herald Tribune, The SAIS Review, The National Interest, and The American Interest. Provided consulting to Columbia University, Carnegie Endowment for International Peace, International Risk, Gerson Lehrman Group, and other organizations. Refereed articles for The China Quarterly and Ethics & International Affairs.

Devin Stewart’s Specialties:

Trade policy, economic and political risk, globalization, environmental policy and renewable energies, new media, business ethics
