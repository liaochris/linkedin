Ken Jackson’s Summary

Ken is co-owner and creative director of Centrifuge, a firm created to help B2B brand marketers realize greater market share. With over 20 years experience in B2B advertising, working alongside some of the industry’s sharpest leaders, Ken understands the drivers for effective business communications. Ken optimizes creative execution across all media while ensuring the end result is on strategy and engaging.

Ken offers a highly collaborative approach to branding and marketing communications, helping companies achieve their greater business potential.

Ken Jackson’s Specialties:

Branding, Interactive Digital Media, Social Media, Strategic Brand Marketing, Strategy, Ideas, Research, Transformational Marketing, Marketing Communications for Business Growth, Website Strategies, Design and Development, Video

Ken Jackson’s Experience

  • Principal / Creative Direction

    Centrifuge Brand Marketing, Inc.

    (Privately Held; Marketing and Advertising industry)

    July 2009 — Present (1 year )

    Centrifuge was created on the firm belief that B2B companies need a new agency model, one that puts the creative process in service of strategic business communications, one that will separate your brand from your competitors’ and give your brand more gravitational pull.

    At Centrifuge, you will find people who care about solving your business challenges. My associates John Barnett and Lisa LaCount and I have worked together for many years, creating brand communications that have transformed companies on regional, national and global scales.

    My career has centered on exploring and managing the creative process, from both the client and the agency sides of the table. My first full-time job out of college was as a staff line-artist (yes, pen and ink) at an electrical distributor in Chicago. I was then chosen to establish and lead a graphics department for their private-label business, charged with the creation of sales support materials. After 3 years of mutual growth, I brought my talents for art direction and problem solving to Arends, Inc., a Midwest B2B agency. I dedicated 20 years to perfect my craft, expanding my skills and responsibilities as I grew from art director to creative director to president.

    It was at Arends where I met up with John Barnett and Lisa LaCount, two of the nicest and most talented people you could ever want to work with. As a team, we continue to produce quality solutions that generate results for clients such as Siemens, Powers, Watts, and New Millennium. See our work and the results it delivers at www.centrifuge-now.com. It is work that shows the power of business-transforming communications.

  • President/Creative Director

    Donald L Arends Inc

    (Privately Held; Marketing and Advertising industry)

    April 1989 — July 2009 (20 years 4 months)

    I have the best job in the world! How many people get to say they truly love what they do? Client challenges are the lifeblood to the creative mind. We thrive on digging deep to uncover the truths and then attack them with sound strategies and bring them to life with creative that sells. With a staff of talented individuals working toward a common goal, and supported by clients that know together we can deliver results, each day is both challenging and rewarding (ok, sometimes more of one than the other).
    We have really kept a sharp focus on the changes in our industry and how our clients and their customers are affected. We spend more time and energy online than ever, crafting viral programs, websites that both engage and inform, and everything else that helps make a brand relevant and alive and vibrant in today's world.
    Looking back, the past 20 years here have really flown by! I started here as an art director with most of my skills in design and illustration. I learned from some of the best talents in B2B, and earned my spot as creative director and now as president/creative director. It is a combination creative thinking and business savvy that helps this agency and its clients grow.

  • Art Director

    Newark Electronics

    (Public Company; Electrical/Electronic Manufacturing industry)

    1986 — 1989 (3 years )

    During this part of my career I was on the client side, as a member of a large team charged with creating the print "bible" of the distributor business, not unlike the Grainger "brick". Deadlines, changes, page layouts, keyline and paste-ups, and line art till the cows came home. As the catalog we were crafting in '86 was winding down, I was chosen to create a marcom department for a sister company. It was here that I learned other key parts of the business back then, print buying, quotes and estimates, managing a budget, hiring a staff. A great experience, and key to my understanding of client points of view and how we all need each other to achieve our goals.
