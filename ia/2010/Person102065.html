John Fleischman’s Summary

John Fleischman writes science for the American Society for Cell Biology, books for kids and others, and articles for the "Harvard Health Letter" and "Air & Space Smithsonian." He was a science writer at Harvard Medical School, a science broadcaster at Boston’s WGBH-FM (NPR), and a senior editor for "Yankee" and "Ohio" magazines.
Fleischman’s latest non-fiction book for older children is "Black & White Airmen: Their True History," published in June 2007 by Houghton-Mifflin Children’s Books of Boston. It was named an “Orbis Pictus Honor Book for Outstanding Nonfiction for Children 2007” by the National Council of Teachers of English (NCTE).

Fleischman’s first non-fiction book for older kids, "Phineas Gage: A Gruesome But True Story About Brain Science," was an American Library Association “Notable Children’s Book” and “Best Book for Young Adults” in 2003. It was also named an “Orbis Pictus Honor Book” by the NCTE in 2003. "Phineas Gage" is now (2009) an audio book! It's available from Recorded Books and through iTunes.

In 2006, Fleischman was awarded a John Simon Guggenheim Fellowship to work on his third nonfiction book for older kids about genomes.

Fleischman is also the author of "Mid Century City" about Cincinnati photographer Sarge Marsh (2006), "Free & Public," a history of the Cincinnati public library (2003), and "The Ohio Lands," a natural history book (1995). He has contributed to various magazines including "Discover," "Smithsonian," "Preservation," "Audubon," "The Atlantic Monthly," "Archaeology," "Parents," and "Muse."

John Fleischman’s Specialties:

I translate "science" into English or at least the variety spoken by ordinary people. Science can be tremendously important but it needs help in explaining itself.
