Christopher Cornue’s Summary

Mr. Cornue has been leading operational, quality, clinical and growth strategies for over 15 years in the healthcare industry.

Most recently, he served as chief executive officer of McKee Medical Center in northern Colorado, where he led the organization to strong financial performance, top decile quality outcomes nationally, 99th percentile patient satisfaction results, service line strategy development, conversion from paper to a fully digital hospital (with the implementation of an EMR & CPOE), creation of secondary service area strategies, and the introduction of new technology (including a daVinci surgical robot, low-dose emitting 64 slice CT, electronic ICU, among other advances).  

Previously he served at vice president at Mount Sinai Hospital Medical Center (MSHMC), a 432-bed Level-1 Trauma Center, on Chicago’s near West side from 2004-2008. Included among his areas of responsibility there were the Departments of Medicine, Surgery, Anesthesiology, Pathology, Perioperative Services, Laboratories, Physician Development, Trauma Services and Service Line Development. In addition to these responsibilities, he led efforts to address disparities and improve quality in the Chicago Metropolitan Area as Executive Sponsor of the Robert Wood Johnson Foundation’s Expecting Success: Excellence in Cardiac Care national collaborative project.

Prior to his time at MSHMC, Mr. Cornue worked with sixteen major academic medical centers nationwide in his role with University HealthSystem Consortium (UHC) in the identification of strategic cost containment & revenue enhancement opportunities and the development of operational strategies.

Prior to UHC, he held several leadership roles at the University of Chicago Hospitals and the University of Chicago.

He has been actively involved in international health, completing several collaborative site visits globally and speaking both nationally & internationally.
