Corey Arnez Griffin’s Summary

Corey Griffin, a passionate career professional in the Public Sector, International Development and Non Profit arenas, brings years of experience working with Government, Education and Non Governmental Organization customers around the world in the State, Local, Federal and International sectors. Mr. Griffin recently joined MainStream GS, a veteran owned management consulting firm, specializing in helping to create high performance organizations in the public sector. As Vice President at MainStream GS, Mr. Griffin leads the sales and business development efforts covering civilian federal agencies and the state and local markets. Leveraging deep expertise in Strategy Development, Alignment and Deployment, Continuous Process Improvement (CPI) and Program and Project Management, MainStream GS’s helps the public sector to eliminate waste out of government processes, drive accountability, and manage performance.

Prior to joining MainStream GS, Mr. Griffin spent 14 plus years at Microsoft Corporation working in various leadership positions, including roles in consulting, operations, and sales. As Director of International Development, Mr. Griffin had responsibility for Microsoft’s strategy for international development aid, trade and finance agencies.

Mr. Griffin has a long history of community, association, and non-profit service. In the international development community, he has served on the board of directors for the USGLC and the Society for International Development, Washington DC chapter. He currently serves on the boards of the Greater Washington Urban League, SamariCorp, So Others Might Eat, the Foundation Schools, and is a member of USAID's Advisory Committee on Foreign Voluntary Aid. Mr. Griffin was named by Washington Business Journal as one of the region’s top minority business leaders of the year for 2008. Mr. Griffin holds executive education certificates from George Washington University, Harvard University and UCLA.
