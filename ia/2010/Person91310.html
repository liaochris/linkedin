Jawad Khaki’s Summary

Jawad is exploring opportunties enabled by the next generation of low cost computing devices and cloud computing platforms.

Previously, as corporate vice president at Microsoft®, Jawad managed groups of engineers and business leaders dedicated to delivering integrated networking, communication and device technologies for Microsoft® Windows client and server operating system platforms. Jawad’s joined Microsoft® in 1989 with the focus on networking technologies to enable compelling customer scenarios that require end-to-end ecosystem collaboration. As an engineering leader on the initial Windows NT team and on subsequent Windows releases, Jawad led the design, implementations and deployment of Microsoft’s Internet protocol implementations, related communication technologies and operating system infrastructure that included TCP/IP, HTTP, IPSec, Dial-Up Networking (PPP), Virtual Private Networking (PPTP/L2TP), UPnP, Wireless Networking (802.11, Bluetooth), RADIUS, DHCP, WINS, transport protocols (NetBEUI, AppleTalk, OSI/TP4, IPX), NetBIOS, TDI, NDIS, WinSock. His most recent contributions were on next generation platform advances for networking (such as IPv6, Peer-to-Peer Networking, Network Access Protection) and device technologies (such as simplified kernel and user mode driver frameworks, USB, 1394, Web Services on Devices, iSCSI) which are now a part of Windows 7 and Windows 2008 releases.

Trained and educated as a computer engineer in London, Jawad started his career in 1979 as a hardware design engineer developing hardware and firmware for UK based GEC Computers Ltd. and then in 1985 as a UNIX development consultant for AT&T Bell Laboratories.
