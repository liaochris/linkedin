Jared Stabiner’s Summary

I have been a music fan from the crisp young age of six, really having no choice but to listen to Chicago's own alternative Q101 in my nanny's car. Everything from Soundgarden to Nirvana were rocking the airwaves, and since then I have been striving to turn my passion into my career.

Opportunity came calling when the young and fresh label entitled Rock Ridge Music was seeking young advertising representatives from Chicago and beyond. I took the lead in the Chicagoland area, working with bands such as the Ike Reily Assassination and Stroke 9. I gained some valuable experience and began to learn the ways of the music industry. Through my senior English project, I was given a unique chance to start a non profit record label that went on to sign six bands, raise funds for the Crohn's and Colitis Foundation of America, and create an impact on the Chicago music scene. I gained valuable experience in event planning, promotions, artist development, and management.

My next experience was at Chicago's Victory Records under the direction of Mark Bubb (now VP of Marketing and Music at Atticus), gaining crucial exposure to digital marketing and street teaming. Going to school in Pittsburgh and keeping a Chicago connection was difficult, but I was able to broaden my resume by working for a promotional company the next summer.

Recently, I took on a marketing internship at Universal Music Group’s Universal Republic Records under Frank Arrigo and worked with bands such as Anberlin, Hinder, Erin McCarley, Kevin Rudolph, and more. This past summer I worked as an Associate Project Manager in Artist Development at Jeff McClusky & Associates. I am currently looking for a career position in marketing to build on the skills and experiences I have gained in the past five years.
