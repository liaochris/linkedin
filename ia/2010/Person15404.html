Anupendra Sharma’s Summary

Anupendra Sharma is Partner at Siemens Venture Capital investing in healthcare (devices, diagnostics, software and services) in the United States, China, India and Europe. Anupendra is an investor in BioImagene, China Diagnostics and Cylex, and involved in RadPharm. He is a former investor in Sequenom (NASD: SQNM).

Prior to Siemens Venture Capital, Anupendra helped companies go public and raise capital, and completed $5 billion in M&A transactions at JPMorgan, Salomon Smith Barney and Siemens. Anupendra also worked in strategy consulting for McKinsey London, and Product Development Finance for Ford Europe, where he was a member of the core team that set-up Ford in China and India.

Anupendra is a Founder of Mobile Medics Healthcare LLC, which was named the Most Outstanding Social Venture of the Year at the won the Global Social Venture Competition in 2006.. In 2005, he won's Columbia Business School's Lorne Weil Outrageous Business Plan Competition with his idea to bring inexpensive healthcare to rural India.

Anupendra founded several organizations including The Leadership Program for startup CEOs, Massachusetts Life Sciences Startup Initiative, Center for Entrepreneurial Leadership at BITS Pilani, named one of India's top five entrepreneurship centers and Peking University's Venture Capital Association. He is an advisor to NCI's SBIR Program, NSBRI, CIMIT and MassBio on startup and funding matters.

Anupendra holds an MBA from Cornell University (1998), Masters in Accounting & Finance from Manchester Business School (1993), and MSc (Hons) Economics & BE (Hons) Instrumentation Engineering from Birla Institute of Technology & Science (BITS Pilani) (1992).
