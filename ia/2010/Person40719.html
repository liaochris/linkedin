Francesco Cirillo’s Summary

Francesco obtained a degree in Economics in 1994 and was a Teaching Assistant in Financial Economics and in Securities Market Economics.
Since 1996 he has provided training courses to major multinational clients in software engineering, project management, software architecture, object-oriented design and planning analysis, business processes and java. He delivered tailor-made object-oriented courses for Sun Microsystems to offer to Sun Professional Services and Sun Trainers.
He is a Trainer, Coach and Mentor to teams and companies on the profitable application of object-oriented technology in the industrial, telecommunications and financial engineering sectors. This is where the Pomodoro Technique, a method that Francesco had invented years before as way to improve personal productivity, was first applied in a professional context.
In 1999 Francesco came into contact with Extreme Programming and became an XP pioneer in Europe; in January 2000 he founded the first XP team in Southern Europe. In September 2000 he founded XPLabs, the first company exclusively dedicated to Consulting, Mentoring, Training and Development based on XP and Agile Methods with a focus on research and innovation. He launched the XPLabs Collaborative Engineering Center - the only one of its kind in the world - where teams were hosted in local facilities to enable an intense collaboration and to encourage the steady growth of XPLabs’ Bees Team, made up of university students, which went on to develop a number of successful projects.
In 2007 he launched agilemethods.eu, a division of XPLabs specializing in Agile Methods education and training in Europe, for which he is Director of Education.
In 2007 Francesco launched the Anti-IF Campaign to improve the effectiveness of object-oriented development. The Campaign has been joined by software engineering experts and programmers from around the world.
Francesco is active as a Public Speaker and Author.
