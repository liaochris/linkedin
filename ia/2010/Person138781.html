Maya Luz’s Summary

At just 22 years old, Maya Luz is quickly rising on the vertical plane of the contemporary fashion scene. Born Maya Luz Molina-Araujo in Santa Fe, New Mexico, raised in Southwest Florida, Maya recieved her BFA from Massachusetts College of Art and Design in Boston, graduating with Distinction in May 2009. This remarkable, award winning, young designer is catching the attention of the most discerning eyes in the industry.
In December 2009, Maya Luz was announced a contestant on the hit reality TV show, Project Runway, Season 7, airing on January 14th, 2010 on Lifetime.
After a recent trip to Los Angeles, Maya created quite a buzz in the streets and boutiques of Rodeo Drive with her ‘Ball and Chain’ evening bag, which epitomize her most recent collection: Fashism, inspired by the fashion victim, and the manipulation of beauty.
Maya began making clothes at age fourteen, with her own underground label “Unsweetened,” marketed through open studio/trunk shows and private sales. As a rugged individualist in a small town, she created a quiet storm of enthusiasm for her one-of-a-kind recycled garments and guerilla marketing techniques.
In just a few short years, Maya has shown at Boston Fashion Week '08, and recognized by Surface Magazine, US Weekly, Entertainment Weekly, The Boston Globe, The Boston Herald, and The Standard Times, to name a few.
Durring the Summer of 2008, Maya traveled to Brazil to teach pattern making to a woman's cooperative of traditional lace makers, (Fairloom Inc.) and has also traveled to the many art and fashion cities of Europe. Maya is a published writer, (Color Magazine, Pulse Magazine, N Magazine,) an avid collector of rare vintage music, and an eclectic DJ with her own music podcast, Apocalypstick! available on Garagepunk.com, and the iTunes music store.

Maya currently resides in New York City.
