J.G. Preston’s Summary

Writer/editor/broadcaster with considerable news and sports experience in print, radio and TV. Able to work in communications, public relations, media relations in addition to writing, on-camera work and radio hosting.

J.G. Preston’s Specialties:

Radio and TV on-air host and personality, news and sports reporter, play-by-play broadcasting.

  • Sacramento News Bureau Chief

    Metro Networks

    (Public Company; WONN; Broadcast Media industry)

    I reported and wrote news stories from Northern California for distribution on Metro's news wire to affiliated stations across the country. Gathered and edited audio from a number of sources. Assembled daily Sacramento-area news digests. Anchored news and traffic reports for various affiliated stations in Sacramento and the Central Valley.

  • Morning Show Host, WJON Radio

    Regent Broadcasting

    (Public Company; Broadcast Media industry)

    May 1993 — September 2005 (12 years 5 months)

  • Co-Host, "Almanac"

    Twin Cities Public Television

    (Non-Profit; Broadcast Media industry)

    December 1984 — September 2005 (20 years 10 months)

    For more than 20 years I was one of the co-hosts of a weekly newsmagazine program that is the highest-rated locally-produced public TV news show in the country. Conducted interviews, delivered commentaries. Two of the programs on which I appeared won regional Emmy awards. Also hosted or co-hosted other special news and public affairs programs for TPT.

  • Free-lance writer

    Minnesota Twins and others

    (Sports industry)

    October 1989 — September 2003 (14 years )

    I wrote articles for the Minnesota Twins' program and yearbooks, wrote scripts for season highlight videos, also wrote script for video biography of Kirby Puckett that was narrated by Bob Costas. In addition I have written freelance pieces for college and university alumni magazines, newspaper travel sections, and a health care newsletter. A PDF with some favorite pieces I've written is available on request.

  • Host

  • Host

    WCCO Radio

    (Broadcast Media industry)

    (Public Relations and Communications industry)

    I was editor of the Minnesota Twins' monthly program and magazine as an employee of the Twin Cities' largest public relations firm, which produced the magazine under contract with the team. Determined contents in consultation with team officials, wrote stories, hired free-lance writers and photographers, worked with layout and design staff. During my time at MMM&G I also wrote for other sports publications they produced (Minnesota State High School League state tournament programs, University of Minnesota football media guide) and was involved in public/media relations work for other agency clients.

  • Program host, reporter

    Minnesota Public Radio

    (Non-Profit; Broadcast Media industry)

    December 1982 — August 1989 (6 years 9 months)

  • Sports Director

    Minnesota News Network

    (Broadcast Media industry)

    September 1983 — March 1988 (4 years 7 months)

    As MNN's first sports director I developed and produced sports programming for a network of commercial radio stations that grew to more than 60 affiliates while I was there. Wrote, produced and anchored daily sportscasts, covered regional pro, college and high school sports, produced and hosted interview/call-in programs and other specials. Before I became sports director I was a news reporter and weekend anchor for MNN from its startup in December 1982. After I left my full-time position as sports director I did daily sports commentaries until August 1989.
