  • Morning news anchor "San Diego's First News"

    KOGO Radio AM 600

    (Broadcast Media industry)

    August 1998 — November 2001 (3 years 4 months)

    This ABC Radio affiliate was a joy for me.
    The weekend newscasts where I began, in just a few months changed to weekdays and the title of Co-host/News Anchor on "San Diego's First News" 5-9am.
    The news/talk format was ambitious and garnered #1 ratings- the brainchild of our fearless leader Cliff Albert.
    I was privileged to be part of this "team" and given the chance to prove to MYSELF that in the broadcast media industry I was NOT just a pretty face- could I be as commanding without a camera and have just a microphone? Had I actually moved, in MY mind, from being an "FCC regulation" of the '70's to an accomplished "journalist"? I'll just say this: Fais de ta vieun reve, et dun reve une realite, which in French means: Make your life a dream, and your dream a reality. Well, my dream, in MY eyes, has finally been realized.

  • Anchor, photog, reporter, writer

    KVOA TV Channel 4 Tucson, Arizona

    (Broadcast Media industry)

    May 1974 — February 1976 (1 year 10 months)

    First job as a journalist.
    Age 19.
    I was...you might call an "FCC regulation".
    Mid-70's, the FCC insisted TV stations across the country hire females.
    So, I know...I know...my resume ONLY read "some college".
    And, why was I hired?
    Green eyes, freckles, and a face not too shabby on cam.
    And in the years to come, because of course speeches to students of every age were part of the job, when asked, "So, what did you study in college and what degree did you have to get in order to do what you do?"
    I always told them, "There is no one way to get where you belong. Follow your heart, and the Universe, God...whatever YOUR belief is, will take you there."
