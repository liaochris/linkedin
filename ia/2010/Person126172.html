Lisa Machii’s Summary

Ms. Machii is a seasoned transactional attorney experienced in a wide range of sophisticated commercial real estate transactions, including finance (debt & equity), distressed debt/asset financing, joint ventures, CMBS and securitization, as well as corporate law and entity formation. She has extensive experience in loan restructuring, corporate restructuring, acquisition & disposition, residential real estate, leasing, development, asset management and loan servicing.

Prior to starting her own practice, Ms. Machii worked in the real estate department of the most prestigious global law firms for almost 10 years before joining her then client Bear Stearns as Vice President (in-house counsel) in the L.A. office of the CMBS group, where she structured and closed CMBS loans and assisted in their securitization, among other things.

Ms. Machii has worked overseas in Tokyo on complex cutting-edge acquisitions, financings and securitizations of distressed debt and real property in Japan during the emergence of the industry. She served as outside general counsel for a hedge fund focused on distressed assets in Japan, which allowed her exposure to the laws of the Bahamas, the Netherlands and Cayman Islands.

Prior to practicing law, Ms. Machii worked in Coopers & Lybrand's state & local tax group for 2 years advising organizations and high net worth individuals on tax controversies, audit representations, tax compliance and multi-state tax planning issues.

Ms. Machii's client base includes leading global investment banks, hedge funds, distressed debt funds, major institutional lenders, REITs, insurance companies, real estate advisors, servicers, developers, high net worth individuals, small businesses, family businesses and start-ups.

The first US-born of Japanese immigrants, Ms. Machii maintains strong ties and connections with the Japanese community in the US, Japan and elsewhere abroad. Her entire extended family lives in Japan and she is conversant in Japanese.
