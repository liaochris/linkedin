Alex Alpert’s Summary

Alex Alpert got his start in the moving business working as a driver and foreman in New York City. His reputation for white glove service earned him a client list that included the National Park Service, Verizon, John Mayer, Kara DioGuardi, ATO and Atlantic Records.

Alex started his own international moving company in 2004. He was awarded with multiple government contracts including the 16.3 million dollar renovation of Federal Hall. His knowledge of sales, operations, corporate, COD and international freight forwarding quickly grew the business to over 3 million in annual revenue.

Alpert joined the Wheaton family as the VP of Business Development at Desert Sun Moving Services. His knowledge of Internet lead generation, SEO and controlled filtered telemarketing enabled Desert Sun to become one of top ten agents in COD sales. In 2008 and 2009, Alex helped Desert Sun earn recognition for outstanding performance in National Account sales as part of Wheaton’s Circle of Champions awards. He was instrumental in establishing and maintaining Desert Sun as a main board carrier in the Cartus Domestic Household Goods Supply Chain.

In February 2010, Alex relocated to Wheaton’s flagship location in Orange County, California as the Director of Corporate Relocations. Alpert has championed many incentives during his tenure at Wheaton including the development of an elite corporate relocation model. He also introduced Wheaton to MAGIC, or Making a Great Impression on the Customer that is used to train all corporate employees, in addition to being rolled out to agents and drivers. Alex has identified new revenue streams, developed strategic partnerships and worked to create a culture focused on the customer experience.

Throughout his career, Alpert has consistently bridged the sales to service paradigm. He has launched several customized service delivery programs, which in 2009 earned a Gold Service and Supplier Innovation award from Cartus.
