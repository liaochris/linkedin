C.J. Brexel’s Summary

I began developing an interest in computers while in middle school. My father was always interested in the latest technologies and utilized them throughout the household. As the internet began to disseminate into homes and connections speeds increased, I became fascinated with the process of creating a website. After countless hours spent on tutorials on the internet, I developed what I thought to be a masterpiece, a website about guitars.

I further honed my website development skills learning about usability, graphic design, search engine optimization, content management systems, CSS, etc. I began using my skills to help develop websites for family and friends. My interests shifted from the presentation layers to scripting languages like PHP along with MySQL databases.

As I reached the next major milestone of my life, I held on to my interests. I majored in Computer Science while at the University of Connecticut in order to gain the technical background needed to understand how to solve complex problems using computers. I found throughout my collegiate career, that one of my unique talents was my ability to effective communicate technical problems to non-technical users. Throughout my career, I would like to eventually direct an IT team so that I could analyze which problems could be tackled by IT and then justify these initiatives to business partners.

In my job search I geared toward an area which would allow me to combine my technical skill with my aspirations. I found a great opportunity at CIGNA with their Technology Early Career Development Program which will allow me to gain an understanding of an information technology department from many different levels and views.
