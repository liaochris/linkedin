Rick Kollmeyer’s Experience

  • CTO and Partner

    Direxxis, Inc.

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Information Technology and Services industry)

    February 2003 — Present (7 years 6 months)

    At Direxxis, we create and deliver end-to-end direct marketing solutions. Our dmEDGE™ technology enables marketers to automate the complex, detail-oriented marketing process. Our web based portal integrates asset management, customization, prospect data and lettershop services into a straightforward point and click environment.

    I am a Partner and CTO and my growing teams are responsible for all technical aspects of our products and infrastructure, from product development, creation of marketing databases, hosting of our large data sources, data production, and portal hosting.

  • President and Founder

    Blue Edge Data Solutions

    (Information Technology and Services industry)

    October 2001 — December 2007 (6 years 3 months)

    Blue Edge Data provided project-based custom software development services. We provided services to organizations including American Express, Brown University, Choicepoint, Epsilon Subaru and many other small and large organzations.

    As most small business owners, my roles and responsibilities included sales and marketing (>1.5MM in new sales per year) as well as being chief technologist and systems architect and managing all the teams.

    Blue Edge funded and built the first dmEDGE Marketing Portal that was then morphed into Direxxis.

    (Computer Software industry)

    September 1985 — October 2001 (16 years 2 months)

  • Engineer

  • Director of Software Development

    Professional Trust Administration

    (Computer Software industry)

    February 1983 — August 1984 (1 year 7 months)

    I was chief technologist for a small software company that created the first PC-based insurance proposal system for companies including AIG, AI Life, The Travelers, Aetna and others. Our software ran on all of the existing desktop computing platforms.

    In hindsight, what I probably developed, was one of the first multi-platform database programming environments for the original PC's (Apple ][, IBM PC, TRS80, Commodore, etc.), since I was, and still am, a big believer in building tools that create software.
