PaCE, LLC A Division of Rian Enterprises, Inc’s Summary

PaCE performs forensic audits of a borrowers' loan documents, loan transactions involving servicing and negotiating, and deficiency and/or foreclosure notice documents. We also examine the actual SEC-filed Pooling and Servicing Agreement (PSA) terms of securitized mortgage loans--PSAs are a key part of a foreclosure defense! PaCE views foreclosure as part of the basic contractual right to home ownership. Contracts cannot be changed unless all parties to the contract agree. It is possible to negotiate loan modifications only when all parties in the real property transactions know who the creditor, the secured party, is. Borrowers are as much a party in interest to securitized mortgage loans as investment banks, servicers, aggregators, and trustees because even a securitized mortgage involves a basic loan transaction between the borrower and the investor.

Hence, PaCE asserts that homeowners have a right to know who they are dealing with in a loan servicing and modification process and who is proceeding with a foreclosure process against them. PaCE uncovers the true identity and role of each party to these processes. A forensic audit examination follows the ownership trail of the pertinent loan documents and reveals whether or not a homeowner is being wrongly removed from his/her home. PaCE also examines whether any parties violated state and federal regulations from settlement onward.

PaCE does not provide legal advice, but attends court proceedings as expert witnesses, and provides expertise to law firms, realtors, and mortgage brokers who honor a homeowner’s rights during the foreclosure/loan modification process.

We specialize in the most useful loss mitigation options:
- GSE-Based Loan Modifications
- Forensic Loan Auditing/Securitized Loan Analysis
- Foreclosure Prevention

In 2011, PaCE will be in the TOP FIVE firms in the U.S. for forensic securitized loan auditing, examination, compliance, and research/reporting analysis.
