Mark Borman’s Summary

Strategic, roll-up-the-sleeves CFO and Board Member with a passion for growing companies ranging from startup, private equity, IPO to publicly traded.

Forte is helping companies achieve strategic growth, profitability, cash-flow, capital-raising, and valuation goals.

Dynamic, value–creating entrepreneurial environments fuel me.

Mission is to build an exciting growth company by partnering with a CEO and management team, while applying my comprehensive CFO competencies.

Comprehensive CFO competencies enable me to enhance value for a company’s investors, customers, employees, served communities, and board of directors.

Understand quickly the needs of all stakeholders and have the ability to reach into all levels of organizations.

Assess situations rapidly, find win-win solutions, develop sound guidance, and communicate with multiple teams ensuring strategic, operational and financial objectives are set and met.

Proven leader and developer of talent.

Performance-driven experiences were honed at NASDAQ- and NYSE-listed companies.

Raised $7.5 billion in 12 offerings to finance rapid growth and strategic transformations through a Forstmann Little leveraged buyout to four IPOs and numerous acquisitions.
• $4.0 billion spin off into three new IPO companies;
• $1.5 billion in an IPO and three subsequent secondary public stock offerings;
• $1.3 billion in three convertible notes offerings;
• $500 million refinance of bank term and revolving credit facilities; and
• $200 million bank line of credit.

Wall Street credibility. My solid reputation comes from my responsive, straight talk—clearly communicating strategy, growth drivers, risks, sales, and profitability—building relationships of trust.

Conducted numerous deal and non-deal road shows, analyst meetings, sell-side investor conferences, one-on-ones, broker teach-ins, bank meetings, and credit rating reviews.

MBA with CPA and CFA certifications.

Mark Borman’s Specialties:

Comprehensive CFO competencies to grow a company include accounting, auditing, board interactions, capital raising (IPOs, secondary offerings, convertible notes, and bank debt), financial planning / analysis, financial reporting (SEC, GAAP, FASB), investor relations, LBOs, management partnering, M&A, operating / cash-flow efficiencies, restructurings, share repurchases / splits, spin offs, strategic planning, treasury, talent development, turnarounds, and Wall Street credibility.
