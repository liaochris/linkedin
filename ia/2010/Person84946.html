Jim Robinson’s Experience

  • Founder/Managing Director

    Infinity Search

    (Privately Held; Staffing and Recruiting industry)

    August 2003 — Present (7 years )

    Infinity Search is one of the fastest growing high technology executive, senior management and technology resource providers in the United States specializing in high-impact search for information technology companies. Services include Executive Search, Contingency Search, Custom Staffing Solutions and Contract Programming Solutions. Domain expertise and concentration includes Data Center Infrastructure, Storage Software & Services, Internet Content Distribution, Virtualization, Open Source, Search & Data Mining, Wireless and corporate IT including Applications, Data Architecture, Systems & Network Administration, etc.

    Concurrently serve as Managing Director for Green Edge Search, Infinity's Clean Technology division. Green Edge Search was established with a mandate and passionate dedication to providing the highest quality of Executive Search and Staffing Solutions to promote and encourage the adoption and implementation of Clean Technologies including Clean IT, Solar, Biofuels, Batteries & Storage, Smart Grid, Transportation, Energy Efficiency and Air & Water.

    The convergence of technology, environmental imperatives, energy independence, national security and economic revitalization will necessitate the emergence of new staffing models to fuel the growth of new technology companies and end-users within the Clean Technology space. Green Edge Search is at the forefront of this emerging technology and thought revolution.

  • Vice President/Partner

    Pencom Systems, Inc.

    (Privately Held; 501-1000 employees; Staffing and Recruiting industry)

    January 1986 — July 2003 (17 years 7 months)

    Jim functioned as Partner and Vice President of Pencom Systems Incorporated, a nationally recognized Recruiting/Software Services and Consulting firm. Pencom gained recognition as the leading source of talent within the UNIX Operating System community in the 1980’s and later pioneered emerging technology talent acquisition in Artificial Intelligence, Object Technology, Distributed Systems, and emerging Internet-based technologies.

    In addition to placing hundreds of software management executives and engineers at a range of companies including early stage startups as well as fortune 50 IT organizations, Jim participated in the growth and success of three Pencom spin-offs including the successful IPO of PSW Technologies, Pencom Web Works (acquired by private company) and Collective Technologies (acquired by MTI).
