Olivier Hersent’s Summary

"Getting things to work (better)" is a passion that started early repairing toys and building electronic circuits, and continued through my professional life working on communication technologies, with a focus on IP based networks.
With the startup experience as founder of NetCentrex , I discovered that 'getting people to build something together' was even more rewarding... and more challenging.
Any project which aims at improving the way we live or work, supported by hard working, ambitious, crazy (not too much), fun and caring people will make me want to be part of it. I invest in several start-ups and also my own projects. I also support the Non-Profit organization "Socracy", which tries to bring some of the innovations of social networks to the world of politics (that's perhaps a bit too crazy !).

Olivier Hersent’s Specialties:

All VoIP systems (Softswitches and Telephony Application servers, Voice and Video Media servers and their applications for announcements, voicemail, lawful interception, Session Border Controllers, NAT traversal...) used for transit or residential/IP based Centrex or trunking solutions.
Value Added Services for mobile networks (SMS/MMS, IM, Ringback tones...).
Back-end systems for telecommunication services, activation, provisioning, rating and charging.

  • founder & CTO

    Netcentrex

    (Telecommunications industry)

    1998 — 2006 (8 years )

    I founded NetCentrex with B. Jannes in 1999, then managed its expansion through 3 rounds of financing which allowed NCX to address the US and EMEA markets through local subsidiaries, and acquire MG2 Technologies in 2000. After 2002 I focused mainly on technology management (CTO) and customer facing activities, remaining President while T. Martin Martin, founder of MG2, became CEO.
    In 2006 Comverse acquired NetCentrex. NCX had become a recognized leader in residential VoIP with over 6M end-users (2006) and the largest deployment worldwide. The company was also very strong in corporate services (trunking, IPCentrex(r), VPN) with the largest deployments worldwide (over 30000 sites). NetCentrex also had a strong audio/video media server offering for both VoIP/IMS and TDM/IN networks (over 100,000 ports deployed directly & through OEM agreements).
    In 2006, NetCentrex had over 400 employees and was highly profitable with a revenue exceeding 50M€.
