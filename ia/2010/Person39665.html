Chris Gittins’s Summary

Research scientist and program manager specializing in application of pattern recognition and statistical signal processing methods to problems in chemistry, physics, and image processing. Over twelve years experience developing algorithms for analysis of multispectral and hyperspectral imagery; emphasis on algorithms to enable real-time remote sensing of hazardous chemical vapors from infrared spectral imagery. Related expertise developing and testing infrared imaging spectrometers. Experienced with system requirements definition and systems engineering, sensor field test planning and execution, modeling and simulation, data analysis.

Proposal author, Principal Investigator, and Program Manager on over two dozen R&D projects for the U.S. Government. The vast majority of programs were awarded through competitive proposal processes.

Research interests include application of statistically-robust estimation methods, kernel methods, and development of decision fusion rules for classification and detection problems.

Chris Gittins’s Specialties:

Hyperspectral data processing for automatic target recognition (ATR), statistical decision theory; design, development, systems integration and testing of hyperspectral sensors; optical coating design; radiative transfer modeling; trace chemical detection and identification.

Other skills: Grant and proposal writing. Proficient with MATLAB and IDL.

Academic training in physical and analytical chemistry. Specialization in high resolution laser spectroscopy.
