Vlastimir Milosavljevic’s Summary

Vlastimir Milosavljevic has over twenty-five years’ international work experience, including nineteen years as an executive in foreign trade and six years of senior level management within major U.S. international development organizations. He is experienced in working on USAID-funded development activities, including: agribusiness and SME development, community development, multifaceted grant making, export and investment promotion, association building, institutional and NGO capacity building, strategic planning, program evaluation and project management. He has worked with entrepreneurs, local and international NGOs, multilateral agencies, finance institutions, community organizations and host country officials to connect local government to civil society actors and develop common strategies to solve shared problems. During a life in Austria, Serbia and Poland, Mr. Milosavljevic documented a record of decisive and growth-oriented executive leadership with repeated success in multiple industries and markets. He is experienced in several industry sectors, i.e. textiles, leather, footwear, fertilizers, crude oil, wood, medicines, beet sugar, FMCGs and service industries, with geographical experience comprising Bosnia-Herzegovina, Brazil, Bulgaria, Croatia, Austria, Poland, France, Georgia, Germany, Hungary, Italy, Macedonia, Mongolia, Romania, Russia, Slovenia, Switzerland, Tatarstan, United Arab Emirates, U.K., the U.S., Uzbekistan, and Libya.
