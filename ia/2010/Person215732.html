William Urschel’s Summary

William Urschel is a seasoned entrepreneur and chief executive officer. Over the last 25 years he has started and run five companies, all of which were either sold or merged with larger companies.

William Urschel’s Experience

  • Managing Director

    A3 Alliance, LLC

    (Information Technology and Services industry)

    January 2010 — Present (4 months)

    The A3 Alliance is an investment capital consortium that invests in startup and early stage companies. The directors of the A3 Alliance circulate opportunities to the investors and those who are interested participate as a group through the A3 Alliance structure. Most of the investors in the consortium are experienced entrepreneurs, successful in their fields, who want to pick their investments for themselves. Other investors include companies looking for strategic investments and venture capital funds wanting to nuture companies too small for their portfolio. The funded companies get experienced and connected investors.

  • Partner

    Microsoft Corporation

    (Public Company; MSFT; Computer Software industry)

    August 2007 — June 2009 (1 year 11 months)

    AdECN became a wholly-owned subsidiary of Microsoft upon its acquisition in August 2007. Urschel remained CEO until the company was fully merged in November, 2008. Urschel remained a partner at Microsoft, involved with Microsoft's Display Ad Platform and Insight Tools Group, initiating projects related to business modeling and performance-based employee compensation, until June of 2009.

  • CEO

    AdECN, Inc.

    (Public Company; 11-50 employees; Computer Networking industry)

    October 2003 — August 2007 (3 years 11 months)

    In October 2003, Urschel founded the company that would become AdECN, the first true exchange for online advertising, in which every ad impression is targeted and auctioned individually. Based on the model of a modern stock exchange, AdECN provides liquidity to ad networks and other aggregators of online advertising traffic. The company secured its first angel financing in February, 2004, and began operations, introducing its technology in October, 2005, in an ad network called Experclick (a wholly-owned subsidiary of AdECN). In the spring of 2006 the company raised a Series A round of venture capital with Novus Ventures and others, and closed a second round a year later with Accel Partners, Novus, and others. On March 1, 2007, the company opened its exchange to other ad networks in the USA, UK, and China, and by August it reached 40 members. In August 2007 the company was acquired by Microsoft Corporation.

  • CEO

    The Narrative Press, Inc.

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Publishing industry)

    June 2000 — December 2005 (5 years 7 months)

    The Narrative Press is a publisher of historical adventure and exploration narratives. The books include accounts of pioneers, archaeologists, circumnavigators, big game hunters, Wild West sheriffs, soldiers, and many others. Urschel started the company in June 2000, released its first catalog of 64 books a year later, and grew the list to 106 titles, securing national distribution in the trade and academic markets. All printing was done under a (then) novel print-on-demand model, which reduced the need to tie up capital in inventory. Urschel sold the company to a private investor in December, 2005.

  • CEO

    ExperTelligence

    (Public Company; 1-10 employees; EXGP; Computer Networking industry)

    July 2002 — October 2003 (1 year 4 months)

    ExperTelligence, Inc. (OTC:EXGP.PK), was a moribund but still publicly-traded software incubator when Urschel signed on as CEO in mid-2002. He overhauled the company’s equity and debt structure and then sold off physical and intellectual property assets in a public auction in October 2003. He sold the corporate shell in December, 2003, and set up a trust for creditors and shareholders.

  • CEO

    Alliance Manufacturing Software, Inc.

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Computer Software industry)

    1994 — 1998 (4 years )

    Urschel founded Alliance Manufacturing Software in 1994 and acquired an old manufacturing management software product for MS-DOS. Rewriting the product for Windows and releasing the new version two years later, Alliance soon became the leading publisher of MRP/ERP software to small and mid-sized manufacturers. For a year an a half sales (and overhead) grew more than 30% per month. The company opened subsidiaries in England and Australia in 1997 and built extensive dealer networks in the United States and the United Kingdom. Urschel left the company in March of 1998. The company was sold to Kewill Systems in May, 2001, and then sold again two years later to Exact Software. It is still in business in Santa Barbara, California.

  • CEO

    Arc Tangent, Inc.

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Computer Software industry)

    1986 — 1992 (6 years )

    Arc Tangent, Inc. CEO/Founder 1986 - 1992
    Urschel started Arc Tangent in 1986 to develop and market the first sophisticated mailing list software for the IBM PC and compatibles. The company's flagship product, built on a proprietary database engine, was named Product of the Year by Inbound-Outbound magazine, it won an Editor's Choice Award from PC World Magazine, and PC Magazine called it the best product of its type on the market. Another of the company’s products put every mailing address in the United States on a CD-ROM, and used that data to correct addresses in mailing lists to USPS standards. Arc Tangent also produced software marketed by Pitney Bowes and Group 1 Software. In 1992, Urschel sold the company to Group 1 Software, the leading vendor of high-end mailing list software for mainframe and minicomputers.

  • CEO

    Team Mail, Inc.

    (Information Technology and Services industry)

    June 1989 — June 1991 (2 years 1 month)

    Team Mail was a wholly-owned subsidiary of Arc Tangent, Inc. that sold direct marketing software, computers, and mail processing machines through a nationwide network of value added resellers. The company was sold to a private buyer.

  • Author

    Misc Software, Articles, and Books

    (Computer Software industry)

    1983 — 1986 (3 years )

    In 1986, Urschel produced "Stock Analysis Using Symphony" and "Stock Analysis using Lotus 1-2-3" for Hayden Book Company. These book and software products offered portfolio management, technical analysis, and fundamental analysis, and used live on-line data from CompuServe and other sources. Urschel also wrote two books for Alfred Publishing: "Quick and Easy WordStar" (1984) and "Urschel's Guide to IBM Software" (1985).In 1984, Urschel produced what may have been the first low-priced business accounting program for the IBM-PC called "Ready-to-Run Accounting." In the early 1980s Urschel wrote over 40 magazine articles, principally on computer-related subjects (database engines and spreadsheets in particular) for PC World and Personal Computing. He also reviewed over 300 software products for the 4-5-6 World catalog. During this period he also taught himself BASIC, C, 8086 Assembly Language, and Pascal, the Lotus/Symphony programming scripts, and SQL.

  • Author

    Other Writing (Self-employed)

    (Self-Employed; Publishing industry)

    1977 — 1986 (9 years )

    In the early 1980s, Urschel supported himself as a freelance writer. Most of his writing during this period was about his travels: he hopped freight trains in California, he went to the Ru’b a Kali desert of Saudi Arabia as an oil driller, he paddled a dugout canoe alone down the Ucayali River (a tributary of the Amazon in Peru) and was captured by Conibo Indians, he was in Nicaragua when its second largest city fell to the Sandinistas, and he was in El Salvador the day the civil war there started. He returned to Nicaragua, ever-so-briefly, as an associate producer for CBS’s 60 Minutes. Urschel’s writing appeared in Adventure Travel, Penthouse, Quest Magazine, and The Congressional Record.

William Urschel’s Education

  • Princeton University

    AB , History , 1974 — 1978

    Activities and Societies:
    Urschel graduated from Princeton University in 1978 with a degree in history. For his senior thesis he rode a horse and pulled a pack mule 1,200 miles from New Mexico to California, following the 1846 journals of the Stephen Watts Kearney expedition down the Rio Grande, west along the Gila River, and across the California desert to San Diego. His thesis was reprinted in Quest Magazine and The Reader’s Digest.
