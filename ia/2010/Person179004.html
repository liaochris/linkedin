Russ Mack’s Summary

"Don't ever let a good crisis go to waste," was a phrase uttered recently by the Chief of Staff at the White House.

With companies struggling everywhere, massive layouts, financial catastrophes everywhere in the world, jobs going away, the Industrial Age passing -- a lot of people are asking: "What does it all mean? What do I do now? Where do I turn? Will I have to re-invent myself in this new post-industrial world? Am I right to feel so much fear? Is there any hope?"

So a book publisher recommended I put together a book and a series of workshops around this basic idea:
"WHAT NOW? How to Get Out of Trouble . . . When You Don't Have a Choice . . . And When You Do"

Heaven knows I have had lots of opportunities to help businesses and individuals turn themselves around. Personally I have reinvented myself enough times over a lot of years. Somehow, after graduating with a clinical psychology degree -- working myself into these functions: book editor (in NY), tv writer and producer (LA), speechwriter, strategic planner, trainer, marketing communications specialist, organizational development, consultant.

Finding opportunities in what seems to be impossible situations . . . why not. And why not now!
