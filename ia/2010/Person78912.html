  • Senior Network Engineer

    MicroMenders

    (Privately Held; Information Technology and Services industry)

    September 1988 — January 2004 (15 years 5 months)

    Created LANSET (Local Area Network Systems Engineering Team) the networking division of Micromenders in 1992. LANSET was created to help refine and focus Micromenders ability to handle the quickly changing technologies associated with supporting large business networks. The creation of this division included the development of education and certification programs as well as the personnel infrastructure of the division. The LANSET division became the company’s primary income within its first year of existence and helped the company grow from about twenty employees in 1992 to over 120 by the year 2000. Created what MicroMenders called the Network Deployment Team. As LANSET became more successful and began working more frequently on project level jobs it became difficult to insure quality control and standardization. The network deployment teams were self contained mobile groups that consisted of a senior network engineer and two or more network technicians and in some cases a second network engineer. These teams were used on network projects such as deployments and migrations. Each team was assigned an in-house project manager and sales representative and all of these teams were guided by the LANSET operations manager. Although the teams were often remote and very self sufficient they were deployed and directed by the project managers working on the behalf of the sales reps and their clients. The project managers communicated to the remote teams via the senior network engineer heading up the team in the field. The network deployment teams were not only effective in solving quality control and standardization issues, MicroMenders found that by keeping the teams together through several projects they became more efficient. They were able to deploy large projects consistently under their labor budget.
