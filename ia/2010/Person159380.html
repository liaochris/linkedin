Pascal Caillon’s Experience

  • Advisor

    TransferTo

    (Privately Held; Telecommunications industry)

    April 2009 — Present (1 year 1 month)

    I facilitate the expansion of TransferTo (a mobile airtime remittance network) to the Americas and help raise funding.

  • Director of Innovation and Business Development

    SanDisk Corp.

    (Public Company; SNDK; Semiconductors industry)

    April 2005 — Present (5 years 1 month)

    I manage an international team responsible for identifying new mobile product and service opportunities, developing supporting partnerships and equity investments, and making first market introductions.

    Our areas of focus have been m-commerce (NFC, mobile authentication with One Time Password), mobile content protection and distribution, and mobile advertising.

    We are launching with key mobile operators the Service Delivery Card, a new breed of mobile memory cards that enables m-commerce and other mobile VAS.

  • Advisor

    Gorilla Mobile

    (Telecommunications industry)

    February 2005 — December 2007 (2 years 11 months)

    I contributed to the definition of aryty.com (mobile remittance for the US/Philippines corridor).

    I helped secure deal with Globe Telecom/Jade Telecom for remittance cash out.

  • Advisor

    Plastyc Inc.

    (Financial Services industry)

    October 2004 — January 2007 (2 years 4 months)

    I help define Plastyc’s (a prepaid VISA card issuer targeting the teens) UPside card offering and find partners.

    I participated in the business plan creation and business angel-funding.

  • Director of Strategic Ventures

    VISA

    (Public Company; V; Financial Services industry)

    February 2002 — March 2005 (3 years 2 months)

    I led VISA business development efforts to capture emerging mobile payment opportunities in Asia-Pacific, Europe, and North-America.

    The largest payment volumes were in top-ups of mobile prepaid accounts. I orchestrated the launches of "top-up via Visa" programs with key mobile operators and banks. The goal was to encourage mobile subscribers to register their Visa card details with the mobile operator and then to expand beyond top-up by offering mobile utility bill payment, person-to-person payment, remittances, and micro-payment for digital goods.

    I also facilitated the equity investment in a leading SMS switching company to offer banks SMS-based mobile banking and micropayment.

    In parallel, I built up the VISA Youth Card Program to help banks commercialize co-branded prepaid cards through teen brands and mobile operators. Associating each mobile number with a VISA account number was the ultimate goal.

  • Director, New e-Commerce Channels

    VISA

    (Public Company; V; Financial Services industry)

    February 1999 — January 2002 (3 years )

    I was responsible for adapting VISA payment products and services to new e-commerce channels and piloting them with key partners (mobile operators, handset makers, and cable operators).

    I founded and chaired the OMA m-Commerce Group and negotiated a partnership agreement with the Mobile Payment Forum.

    I also launched one of the first mobile ATM locators.

  • Manager, Visa Open Platform

    VISA

    (Public Company; V; Financial Services industry)

    September 1996 — January 1999 (2 years 5 months)

    I supported the development of the Visa Open Platform (Visa smart card platform that was turned into the Global Platform) and its applications.

    I participated in the evangelization of VOP to the mobile industry (Global Platform is now in most SIM cards).

  • Manager, International Branch Relations

    Societe Generale

    (Public Company; GLE; Banking industry)

    October 1994 — May 1996 (1 year 8 months)
