Barry R Bowen’s Experience

  • Owner

    SoftDraft LLC

    (Computer Software industry)

    April 1992 — Present (18 years 3 months)

    Software developer specializing in AutoCAD applications for the AEC industry. He started writing “The AutoLISP Tutorial” for CADalyst magazine in 1988 and continued writing different AutoLISP programming articles for the next 15 years. He began using AutoCAD software with Release 1.2 in 1983 and has been an AutoCAD/AutoLISP instructor in the continuing education programs at Memphis State University and State Technical Institute in Memphis. He also wrote several articles on AutoLISP programming for Cadence and Technical Design Solutions Magazines.

  • VP of Technology and Partner

    OnLine, Inc.

    (Computer Software industry)

    November 1991 — April 1992 (6 months)

    A company specializing in Facility Management (an outgrowth of ANFW). Responsible for developing FM/GIS applications with interface to AutoCAD. Designed and developed automated parts book information systems for maintenance planners. Links between AutoCAD drawings, scanned parts views, database info, main frames and video images. Developed software to input as-built architectural floor plans for hospitals.

  • CAD Systems Manager

    Askew, Nixon, Ferguson & Wolf, Inc.

    (Architecture & Planning industry)

    January 1986 — November 1991 (5 years 11 months)

    Systems Manager for 25 AutoCAD stations and 15 support systems on Novell network, responsible for research and development, customizing, programming, trouble-shooting, repair, cad standards, hardware/software installations and employee training programs. Analyzed every aspect of CAD operations to implement more efficient and productive techniques. Increased CAD production by 30% to 40% with custom AutoLISP add-in applications. Increased plotting efficiency with overnight spooling techniques. Developed CAD system standards manual for Memphis Shelby County Airport Authority, Federal Express, St. Jude, The MED Hospital, Baptist Hospital and others.

    Researched, designed, and developed interface to MSCAA McDonald Douglas main frame-Land Acquisition Database, for automatic updating of AutoCAD Land Acquisition maps and monthly FAA Grant Maps. Designed and redeveloped an AutoLISP application, “Airport Airspace Analysis Program,” to generate a complete 3D model in AutoCAD, using AME, for Part 77 surfaces for airports of all sizes. Conducted seminars, workshops and training programs on AutoCAD, Auto Architect, Archibus and others.
