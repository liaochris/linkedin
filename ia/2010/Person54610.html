Dennis Briggs’s Summary

Dennis has been with Johnson & Johnson for more than 19 years and is currently the Vice President, Research & Development at J&J subsidiary, Animas where he leads the design team that is focused on developing innovative insulin delivery and diabetes management systems. Prior to joining Animas in the fall of 2006, Dennis was the Director of Product Development at J&J affiliate, Therakos where he was responsible for the development of sophisticated cell therapy instruments. Dennis has been awarded numerous commercially significant patents, has exhibited leadership throughout his career and has focused much of his time directly on the development and mentoring of future leaders. In 2006, Dennis became the first recipient of the peer nominated ACE Award for excellence in innovation, initiative and long term consistent achievement and is also a School for Innovators graduate. He was awarded the 1998 Johnson & Johnson Vice-Presidents Research Award for Technical Achievement and received the 1989 Organon Teknika Research medal.

Prior to joining J&J in 1990, Dennis was employed at Organon Teknika where he worked as a process and design engineer and prior to that, he owned and operated his own business, developing hardware and software for petroleum marketers. Dennis holds a BS in Engineering and Information Systems, as well as an MBA in Executive Management and is an active member of the University of Pennsylvania Wharton Fellows.
