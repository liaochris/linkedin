Jim Mulcahy, CMA, CFM’s Summary

Jim combines strategic insight with practical business knowledge in assessing and determining corporate strategy. He is a Senior level economist / financial analyst with extensive experience, including in-depth knowledge of banking, finance, utility and energy companies. Strong analytical skills in economics, finance, accounting, statistics and math. Proven ability to solve complex problems. Skilled at making operations more efficient.

Jim worked in all phases of the Treasury area to ensure appropriate capitalization, including managing the capital structure, negotiating debt and equity transactions, and executing a $1 billion daily funding program. He provided analytical, financing and foreign exchange support to the International division.

Jim also was responsible for assembling the corporate five-year financial plan which was based on a $1.3 billion annual capital and expense budget. Further, he lead (in fact was the prime mover) the team that obtained a new fleet lease program for CNG. It would save $6-$7 million per year when fully implemented.

In the banking area, Jim developed the portfolio reports that analyzed the dynamics of the bank’s credit related assets, including the Real Estate, LBO, and Energy subgroups. He was instrumental in the development of the bank’s RAROC, economic capital, and PD, LGD, and EAD measures. He prevented losses, including those with a six and seven figure potential, in a number of areas as a credit deputy. He streamlined the approval process for bidding on municipal debt, as well as creating a number of policies that mitigated risks in the Trust and cash management areas.

Jim has taught economics: principles, intermediate macro & micro, money & banking, monetary theory, and industrial organization; and corporate finance at the college level; and micro & macroeconomics, corporate finance,and case studies in finance at the MBA level.
