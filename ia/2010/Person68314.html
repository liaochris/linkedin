Ewan Branda’s Summary

My research and teaching looks at the relationship between built environments and information technologies. It considers various aspects of this relationship in the field of architectural history and theory, including the embedding of information technologies in physical space, the interdisciplinary borrowing of organizational metaphors, and digitally mediated scholarly communication. My minor field of specialization is Information Studies, with concentration in metadata design for cultural heritage information, Information Retrieval, and the Semantic Web. I am currently a finishing my doctoral dissertation at UCLA. I also serve on the board of directors of the Los Angeles Forum for Architecture and Urban Design and the Electronic Literature Organization.

Ewan Branda’s Specialties:

the study and teaching of architectural history and theory, scholarly communication, software design and information modeling, architectural design for cultural institutions
