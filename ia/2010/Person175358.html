Robert Vitello’s Summary

In 2007, we gambled on introducing SOA and Agile practice to restart a $157 million initiative to improve the NYS unemployment insurance system; a project that had been lumbering along for years without much progress. Now at the mid-point of the reorganized, 5 year effort, we're right on schedule and on budget. This, even as we stay ahead of the pack in delivering on federally-mandated unemployment insurance extensions.

We’re also working in the area of legal technology to build a new legal case management system for the Unemployment Insurance Appeal Board. The effort rests on previous experience at the NYS Attorney General’s Office and on Labor’s own legacy systems. It will come to embrace the needs of all legal units in the Department, and could be a candidate for a SaaS in the Cloud.

Considerable focus is also being given to semantic processing for workforce development requirements. One goal is to harvest information from our client’s resumes and their on-line behavior to make them more aware of opportunities for training and in the job market.

Our work on building a virtual One Stop Service Center for youths exploring careers as well as for New Yorkers already in the labor market remain on the drawing board. Both funding and the selection of suitable technology has become a limiting factors.

In areas of governance and inter-agency collaboration, we have made strategic advances that are unique. Leveraging and extending commercial products, we have introduced SOA development metrics and public Service repositories that are unseen elsewhere.

Business planning efforts have shown steady progress toward our three-pronged approach to system improvement: modernization, extension, and consumerization.

Meanwhile, we're tracking progress on conformity with the Federal Information Security Management Act (FISMA), and have improved our adherence to NIST guidelines by over 27% since August 2008.

Governance, development methodologies, innovation, executive decision-making, performance metrics. Special focus on Service Oriented Architecture and Agile development method.
