Tom Player’s Summary

After serving as a trial attorney in two prominent litigation firms, I established Player Entertainment Law in 2000, for the express purpose of providing legal representation, business consultation, and career guidance to musicians, producers, actors, writers, models, and other “players” in the entertainment industry in all aspects of their professional careers.

In 2002, concurrent with my activities in Player Entertainment Law, I joined the Weiss Legal Group, where my practice includes Appeals in all Florida State Courts, First-Party Insurance Litigation, Business, Entertainment, and Intellectual Property Law.

I am active in the Entertainment, Arts, & Sports Law Section of the Florida Bar, and I am a member of the National Academy of Recording Arts and Sciences (the Grammy Awards), The Florida Bar, the Seminole County Bar Association, and the Florida Justice Association.

I have lectured at continuing legal education seminars for the Florida Justice Association, and have been a panelist on the Florida Music Festival’s forum on legal issues in the music business. I also regularly guest-lecture at Full Sail University’s Master of Entertainment Business class “Advanced Entertainment Negotiations”, and have served as a panelist/ judge for the Full Sail University Bachelor of Entertainment Business degree candidates final “capstone” presentations.

I have had the honor of being recognized by my peers as the number one entertainment attorney in Central Florida for the Orlando Business Journal's 2006 & 2007 Annual "Best of the Bar" which recognizes attorneys at the top of their areas of practice. I am also honored to have been chosen by my peers as one of Florida Trend magazine's Florida Legal Elite for 2009.

I also play bass guitar and did so professionally for many years. My experiences as a professional musician have given me considerable insight into the music business and an appreciation of the industry from the musician’s perspective.
