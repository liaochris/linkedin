Charlene L. Fu 傅夏霖’s Experience

  • Co-owner

    Good Words Global, LLC

    (Writing and Editing industry)

    August 2009 — Present (1 year 4 months)

    "Where the right word matters."
    "A word fitly chosen is like apples of gold."

  • Book Editor & Co-Author

    Freelance

    (Writing and Editing industry)

    2003 — 2008 (5 years )

    Edited five books for award-winning journalist and best-selling author David Aikman, including the best-seller "A Man of Faith: The Spiritual Journey of George W. Bush" and an updated edition of "Jesus in Beijing: How Christianity is Transforming China and Changing the Global Balance of Power," as well as a novel set in China. Co-authored a book on Christianity in ancient China called "Faith of Our Fathers: God in Ancient China" (Chinese version released in China in 2005, English in 2006, U.S. edition released in September 2008 under the title "Finding God in Ancient China"). Also edited the Chinese translation and engineered the publication in China of "A Man of Faith," released nationwide in May 2006, and another Aikman title, "Great Souls: Six Who Changed the Century," released in April 2008.

  • Editor-in-Chief, Jiaoliu Magazine

    U.S. Embassy, Beijing

    (Writing and Editing industry)

    July 1999 — December 2005 (6 years 6 months)

    Responsible for all content and entire production process of full-color 64-page Chinese-language quarterly magazine about all aspects of life in America. This U.S. State Department publication is distributed gratis to more than 17,500 of the most influential people in China, including the nation’s top leaders, policy makers and academics (such as the Commerce Minister and the Bank of China Governor), as well as dissidents and human right activists. Each issue’s approximately 15 articles are reprinted in translation from the U.S. press, covering a range of issues including but not limited to politics, social trends, foreign policy and economics.

  • Consultant & Freelance Reporter & Writer

    Freelance

    (Writing and Editing industry)

    June 1996 — July 1998 (2 years 2 months)

    Worked on a project basis researching and writing market analyses for consultancy firms, assisted in the establishment of a business management training center in Beijing, provided customized tours of Beijing for U.S. executives, and freelanced for news organizations including CNN, Newsweek, the Washington Post and The Associated Press.
