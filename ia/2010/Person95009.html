Jennifer Hyland’s Summary

Recently brought on board as artist manager by music industry veteran Ivan Berry (Beatfactory Records founder) under his new company with Daniel Mekinda called tanjola.
tanjola represents the careers of Juno Award winning Keshia Chante and Platinum artists Massari and Shiloh. tanjola also publishes jacksoul, Shiloh, Rupert Gayle, Alex Greggs, Dru, Justin Forsley and Alonzo.

In 2009 Jen founded Cherry Blossom Entertainment. Based between Los Angeles and Toronto, Cherry Blossom offers services in A&R, Exec Production, A&R Admin, Artist Dev/Management, Tour Managing and Producer/Songwriter Management.

Jen worked with ole Music Publishing in Los Angeles 2006 through 2009 to represent ole's song catalog and songwriter roster, with special focus in the area of Pop and Urban music. Jen managed ole's digital label via IODA and was the founder, as well as key organizer, for ole's annual Pop/Urban songwriting camp in LA which was profiled in Billboard Magazine and garnered such US radio singles as "Cause A Scene" by Teairra Mari ft Flo Rida.

Prior to joining ole in late 2006, Jen spent six years at BMG Canada (now SONY Music Canada) in the A&R dept, and became Manager of A&R and International Exploitation in 2003. Jen worked closely with the Canadian Idol projects, executive producing both Melissa O’Neil and Rex Goudie’s debut albums, and has worked with such acts as Keshia Chante, Shawn Desman, jacksoul, Rascalz, In Essence, Kalan Porter, Ryan Malcolm, Sloan, The Philosopher Kings, Our Lady Peace, Chantal Kreviazuk, Treble Charger, Gino Vannelli and Wyclef Jean

Jen has been an active member of the JUNO Award R&B/Soul committee since 2005, JUNO Award Songwriter of the Year committee since 2007 and is a current Juror member for FACTOR. (The Foundation to Assist Canadian Talent on Records)
She was also a teacher at Harris Institute for the Arts, Music Business School, where she founded the “Street Marketing” program in 2001 which she taught for several years.
