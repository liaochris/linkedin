Zack Robbins’s Summary

Zack has worked to support advances at the intersection of technology, university research and industry for nearly 20 years. In January, 2010 he joined the Morgridge Institute for Research in Madison, WI.

In 1989, following graduation from Bates College (Lewiston, ME) with a degree in Political Science, Zack began his career as a legal assistant in the telecommunications group at the law firm Sidley & Austin in Washington, DC, before moving to the University of Wisconsin-Madison (and the Pathology and Medical Ethics programs). He then took on consistently increasing responsibilities in roles at the American Association of State Colleges & Universities (AASCU in Washington, DC), the University of Maryland Materials Research Science and Engineering Center (NSF-MRSEC), and served as Director of External Relations and Faculty Affairs in Physics. After earning his MS in Management in Maryland, Zack moved to North Carolina and served Duke University for 7-and-1/2-years as Associate Director and finally as Director of Corporate Relations. During his time at Duke he also Co-Founded and Co-Chaired the Network of Academic Corporate Relations Officers (NACRO.us) and served on Duke's Research Administration and Policy Group.

The Morgridge Institute is a new, private, not-for-profit biomedical research organization associated with the University of Wisconsin-Madison, one of the best-funded public research universities in the world. It is part of the Wisconsin Institutes for Discovery, a public-private initiative that facilitates interdisciplinary research and breakthrough discoveries to improve human health. Spanning the fields of regenerative biology, computer science and engineering, the $150-million state-of-the-art facility housing the institutes will open in late 2010.
