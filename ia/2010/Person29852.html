Bruce Katcher, Ph.D.’s Summary

Bruce Katcher is an Industrial and Organizational Psychologist and President of The Discovery Consulting Group, Inc., a management consulting firm based in Sharon, MA that specializes in employee engagement and retention.

Prior to founding the firm in 1993, Bruce headed up the Organization Research & Development practice of the Boston office of The Wyatt Company.

He has conducted employee opinion and customer satisfaction surveys for more than 100 organizations including Alcoa, Bay State Gas, Delta Dental Plan, Harvard Vanguard Medical Associates, Johnson & Johnson, the Lahey Clinic, Revlon, Sodexho USA, the Tata Group, Timberland, Tufts University, Worcester Polytechnic Institute, and W.R. Grace.

He publishes a free monthly ezine called “Improving the Workplace” in which he shares the insights he has gained from more than two decades of employee survey work.

His book called, “30 Reasons Employees Hate Their Managers,” published by AMACOM, was named one of the top business books of the year in 2007 by strategy+business magazine and the Library Journal. It also was awarded a bronze Book of the Year Award by ForeWord magazine.

Learn more about his work at: www.DiscoverySurveys.com.

Bruce also helps aspiring independent consultants. He is founder and Executive Vice President of The Center for Independent Consultant and past president of the Society of Professional Consultants. His book, "An Insider's Guide to Building a Successful Consulting Practice" is being published by the American Management Association.
