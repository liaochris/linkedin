Hal Horowitz halh@finapros.com’s Summary

As a child I was the kind of kid who half-listened with the left side of my brain while the right side was out saving maidens from fire-breathing dragons. But I had some good teachers and mentors along the way whose intention was, not to deprive me of the opportunity to learn from my own mistakes, for those were a great learning tool, but to learn more from theirs. So much of who we are is the result of the people we’ve encountered in our lives.

I began my finance career in 1965 as a collector (loan adjuster) for a consumer loan company, became a loan manager, moved into commercial credit and then into equipment leasing where I worked for 30 years in a variety of environments and positions from credit to operations to sales to owner. During that time I had the good fortune to become active in a national trade association eventually serving as its president, and I have also had the honor to have worked with and for other not-for-profit, charitable and community organizations in a variety of capacities.

In 1997 I left leasing for executive recruiting where for the last 12 years I’ve been successful in placing financial professionals, and coaching both candidates and employers on a variety of matters.

Today, with more people joining the talent pool and fewer jobs to be had, my focus has shifted again as I assist individuals with their job searches and career goals and employers with interviewing techniques to encourage candidates to join their firms. This calls upon my experiences as a manager, an executive, and a recruiter, coaching individuals through a variety of processes; and on the war stories associated with making credit assessments, structuring complex deals and working out difficult situations. And it makes me mindful of the teachers and mentors I’ve met along the way, the bosses, the peers, the subordinates, the vendors and the customers from whom I’ve learned. And if I hadn’t learned but one thing, it would have been to pay it forward.

Hal Horowitz halh@finapros.com’s Specialties:

Collaborative coaching and mentoring; strategic partnering; problem solving; leadership development and transition. As a recruiter I collaborate with my clients to further their success by identifying professionals of uncommon ability to whom they might not otherwise have access and who will in turn make a valued contribution to their goals. As a coach I want to help you become confident that you are the best professional you can be.
