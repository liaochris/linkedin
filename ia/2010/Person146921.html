Chris Elam’s Summary

Chris Elam is the Artistic Director and Choregrapher for Misnomer Dance Theater, a modern dance company in New York City.

He is also the founder of the Audience Engagement Platform for the Arts, a web-based platform to enrich interactions between audiences and artists.

Elam has been noted for both the originality of his choreographies and leadership in creating an online model for developing audiences for the performing arts.

In addition to his work with Misnomer and AEP, Elam has been a guest faculty and choreographer at numerous schools and universities.

Chris Elam’s Specialties:

Dance, Choreography, Speaking, Teaching, Web Platform Design

Chris Elam’s Experience

  • Founder

    Audience Engagement Platform (AEPlatform.org)

    (Performing Arts industry)

    2008 — Present (2 years )

    The Audience Engagement Platform is a new site to help artists and audiences worldwide better engage their online audiences. For Audiences, AEP is a place to feel more like an active participant in the arts, to discover and get closer to the arts in your daily life.

    AEP goes into Alpha testing in April, with a public beta release scheduled for summer 2010. To track AEP and to know when it launches, sign up at AEPlatform.org.

    In addition to creating AEP as a web platform to help the arts with audience engagement, Elam has spoken on the subject of audience engagement for the arts at conferences ranging from TEDx and Fortune Brainstorm:Tech, to Dance / USA and the Americans for the Arts' National Arts Marketing conference.

  • Founding Artistic Director

    Misnomer Dance Theater (misnomer.org)

    (Performing Arts industry)

    1998 — Present (12 years )

    In the 12 years since founding the company, Chris Elam has built Misnomer Dance Theater as a critically-acclaimed, international touring dance company.

    Elam's choreography for the NYC-based Misnomer Dance Theater, was recognized by the New York Times as one fo the "top ten" dance performances of 2006. His work with the company has been performed in over 250 theaters across the USA, Brazil, Indonesia, Turkey, Ireland, Ukraine, Cuba, Denmark, Holland, and France.

    In addition to stage choreography, his dance-based collaborations and projects have included working with artists and cultural entities ranging from Bjork to the Sundance Channel.
