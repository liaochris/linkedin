  • Owner

    Us and Others, Nylonate Paints

    (Wholesale industry)

    1970 — 1980 (10 years )

    Sideline business 1970 to 1980.

    This business was built around a unique nylon-based paint which really worked to prevent blistering resulting from moisture infiltration. It was my opportunity to get in on the ground floor of an opportunity. My supplier went bankrupt quickly because they grossly underestimated production costs; the product was way ahead of its time and was difficult for users to accept.

    Moral of this is it may not be the best idea to get in at the very beginning of a new idea.

  • Owner

    Us and Others, Toy Rack Jobber

    (Wholesale industry)

    1970 — 1980 (10 years )

    Sideline business 1970 to 1980

    Business consisted of selling locations to many store locations and periodically restocking the racks. Management activities consisted of ordering and maintaining a new inventory of toys. Business was successful but required more time than I had.

    Lesson learned was there is only so much time available to one person. You must organize and manage a staff to help in any business. If you fail to do this, you will have to quit the business.

  • Vice President & Asst. Trust Officer

    (Banking industry)

    1960 — 1968 (8 years )

    Involved in all aspects of estate management and settlement, estate income tax preparation, estate planning counseling, investment management, and real estate management.

    Received many initially annoying and abusive calls from those left behind, usually a widow. Eventually I realized most of these calls were emotional and the result of being lonely. I began to enjoy them and tried to empathize and understand their problems rather than taking their comments as personal criticism of me.
