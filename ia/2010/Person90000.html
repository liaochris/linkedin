Justin Jarvinen’s Summary

According to WDHB, the world leader in Learning Expeditions™, "Justin is one of the most innovative and dynamic young entrepreneurs in Chicago".

Named one of America's 40 most influential entrepreneurs in America by American Venture Magazine, as well as one of Chicago's "40 Under 40" by Crain's Chicago Business, and a finalist in Entrepreneur Magazine’s Entrepreneur of the Year 2008, Justin is recognized as a pure innovator with a powerful combination of creative and technical skills. His thought leadership has led to the development of one of the country’s first social networking companies, as well as startups in the carbon trading, digital photography, personal finance, and ticketing industries.

Justin regularly guest lectures at schools including Northwestern University’s Kellogg Graduate School of Business, The University of Chicago’s Booth School of Business, Lake Forest Graduate School of Business, Bowling Green State University, Michigan State, and more, and is a sought after speaker at high profile national and international events, including Midem™, the world’s largest music conference held in Cannes, France. In 2007, Justin was introduced by major record label executives as the "creator of an industry that didn't exist 3 years ago, but one that everyone will be talking about 3 years from now". He also regularly presents at events and conferences such as the PMA's, Digital Hollywood, and many more.

According to participants at an invite-only discussion on Innovation & Culture with 30 leading European executives this Summer, Justin, the day's featured speaker, presented ideas that were "eye opening". He is a "true innovator" and "fascinating". He is also co-author of a marketing and advertising textbook that will be utilized by colleges in U.S. beginning in 2011.

Today, Justin guides the growth of VerveLife, a next-generation music company that enables global brands and agencies to fully leverage the power of music.

Justin Jarvinen’s Experience

  • Founder & CEO

    VerveLife

    (Privately Held; Music industry)

    October 2003 — Present (6 years 10 months)

    VerveLife is the leading strategic music marketing company in the US, providing global brands and their agencies with an award-winning suite of strategic and executional services, innovative music-centric products, content delivery and campaign management technologies, and unrivaled access to thousands of world-class artists, their content and their fans.

    VerveLife turns music into money for brands.

  • Co-Founder

    Optionit.com

    (Consumer Services industry)

    August 2007 — 2008 (1 year )

    Optionit is a revolutionary, patent-backed platform that enables consumers to purchase and trade options on items such as sporting and concert tickets, effectively enabling consumers to lock-in face value prices should they decide to purchase at a later date. Optionit.com provides ultimate flexibility and eliminates the need to visit the secondary market to (over) pay for tickets.

    I developed the original go-to market strategy, managed the business set-up, designed and managed the development of the back-end technology, orchestrated and closed the seed capital raise (~$2mm), and recruited their current CEO.

  • Founder

    Photo.com

    (Photography industry)

    2006 — 2007 (1 year )

    A digital photography community and ecommerce site, bought by Calumet Photographic, one of the largest professional photography retailers in the world.

    I established the business, negotiated partnership agreements with key third-party service and technology providers, designed and managed the development of the back-end technology and the website, hired its original business and technology staff including FLEX developers and their CTO, and recruited their CEO.

  • Co-Founder

    CarbInvest Consulting

    (Environmental Services industry)

    2002 — 2004 (2 years )

    The first company in the U.S. established to help U.S.-based multi-national corporations identify, baseline, mitigate, and trade their GHG emission reductions. Today, Carbinvest (the parent entity) is among the world's largest holders of carbon credit applications, with active mitigation projects in India, Brazil, and Egypt.

    Worked as part of a 4-person team to launch the innovative business. Established guidelines, documentation and technologies for greenhouse gas emission baselining and trading, and managed the creation of its go-to-market strategy and marketing plan. Helped recruit key scientists and experts, and developed relationships with Fortune 50 corporations in the U.S. and abroad. Became a subject matter expert on GHG reporting as mandated by the then-recently passed Sarbanes-Oxley act, as well as the Kyoto Protocol, and worked with dozens of corporate CFO's, Treasurers and Counsel to help them understand the risks inherent in non-compliance - not to mention the massive revenues they could generate by implementing a GHG trading strategy.

  • Founder & CEO

    Strateg, LLC

    (Online Media industry)

    1999 — 2003 (4 years )

    A community comprised of senior executives and entrepreneurs, and an early player in the social networking space.

    I created the automation of the "professional networking" concept as a means to locate c-suite mentors and originate new business opportunities for entrepreneurs and startups. Strateg (which means to lead or command; Latin-French) was a first-in-class technology and service that helped startups shorten launch cycles by aligning them with senior executives who could provide mentorship, access to new business opportunities, and capital. Thought leadership included creating a method for establishing and maintaining trust via social networks, compensation, virtual collaboration, introduction or referral tracking, sales life-cycle management, opportunity filtering, and more.

  • Associate Director

    Bear Stearns & Co., Inc.

    (Public Company; BSC; Financial Services industry)

    1993 — 1996 (3 years )

    Reporting to a Senior Managing Director, I worked on the corporate cash management team as its primary new business person. Efforts resulted in dozens of new client signings and the group's expansion into new products such as callable agencies, and new lines of business.

    Left the group to focus on emerging opportunities such as Rule 144-based derivatives and other high-net-worth investment and liquidity strategy implementation.
