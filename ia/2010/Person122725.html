Laurent Cherrier’s Education

  • WSET (Wine and Spirit Education Trust)

    Diploma in Wines and Spirits , 2008 — 2010

    Diploma is a two-year program. It features heavy emphasis on viticulture and vinification; focuses special attention on production and consumption trends, wine marketing and other aspects of the global sale and consumption of wine; and covers the world's wine regions in considerable depth. Various tests throughout the course involve multiple-choice questions, essay exams, blind wine-tasting, and research papers. Diploma is the traditional preparatory course for those who wish to become Master of Wine students.

  • WSET (Wine and Spirit Education Trust)

    Professional Certificate in Spirits , 2008 — 2008

    The Spirits Certificate is designed to provide a thorough knowledge of spirits and liqueurs. The course covers the history, production and style of spirits of the world: Cognac, Armagnac and other brandies; Scotch, Bourbon and whiskeys; white spirits, including vodka, rum, tequila, gin; and liqueurs and other flavored spirits

  • WSET (Wine and Spirit Education Trust)

    Advanced Certificate in Wines and Spirits , 2007 — 2007

    The Advanced Certificate is the appropriate course for those who already have a broad foundation of wine knowledge--including wine labeling laws, appellation systems, major wine-producing regions, and grape types--and are experienced in tasting technique. It covers the major wine regions of the world in more depth than the Intermediate Certificate, and includes more detailed study of viticulture and vinification. This course enables students to describe the characteristics of the principal wines and spirits of the world and to explain the key factors accounting for those characteristics.

  • The Sommelier Society of America

    Certificate Course , Wine, Beer and Spirits , 2006 — 2007

    The Certificate Course program is a 20 week study of the significant countries and wine regions of the world. Professional emphasis is on the varietals, viticulture specifics, techniques of tasting, food and wine pairing and service. More than 120 wines, worth more than $6,000 are tasted. In addition, a class is devoted to distilled spirits and an other one to beer. Cooperage and viniculture are included; business and legal aspects and licensing are also addressed and reviewed in detail. There is a final examination that includes all topics covered in the course. Students who pass this are awarded the Sommelier Society of America, Inc. diploma/certificate.
