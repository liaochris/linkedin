Sara Howland’s Summary

For three years, while completing my college degree, I worked for the GIS department for the City of Concord in New Hampshire. I worked as an intern, and with this position came a great deal of independence and accountability, which I found to be very motivating and rewarding. With some research, I learned the equipment and software, as well as time management and quality control. This position included mostly field and some office work and I found myself very comfortable with both job locations.

After earning my Bachelor’s degree I worked for Hoyle, Tanner & Associates as an Aviation Project Engineer. There I built contract sets, to include drainage, runway reconstruction, obstruction studies, hydraulic barricade systems, fencing plans, wildlife mitigation plans, grading, electrical, cost estimating, technical writing, and importing/interpreting survey data.

By holding positions in both civil engineering and GIS, I have been able to understand how both fields interact and depend on each other. I have also learned the entire process of finishing a project from concept design, data collection, and permitting to the final contract set being delivered to a client. I believe this diverse experience has helped me improve my skills as an engineer immensely.

I hope to continue forward with my engineering career in either Civil Engineer, Architectural Engineering or GIS. Also, I plan on obtaining my professional engineering license at the earliest possible date.

Sara Howland’s Experience

  • Project Engineer

    Hoyle Tanner & Associates, Inc

    (Civil Engineering industry)

    January 2007 — February 2009 (2 years 2 months)

    Projects Involved: Bar Harbor Obstruction Analysis, Millinocket Clearing Plan, Runway Reconstruction Design
    The Bar Harbor Obstruction Analysis was the most important project I did during my time with HTA. I led the entire project from concept to a completed set of bid drawings. It was an analysis of tree toptreetop and structural elevations to determine current and potential obstructions for approach and take-off surfaces. For this project I was able to use my knowledge of GIS to save substantial time on gathering data about the abutting properties, which was then used for obtaining the proper easements.
    The Millinocket Clearing Plan was also substantial because I was given the opportunities to do field investigations and also attend pre-bid meetings. I designed a plan that would provide adequate clearing on the airport property surrounding the runways, while preserving the scattered wetlands also occupying the area. This project gave me an expanded view of my role as an engineer.
