David Nicol’s Summary

I began my career with ambitions to be a surgeon, however a combination of a love of working with people and jobs in sales and catering whilst an undergraduate vet student sparked my interest in business.

Since then, I have focused on building a reputation as an excellent vet with a broad base of skills. My specific areas of clinical interest are diagnostics, cardiology and dentistry.

This passion has been the foundation upon which my success as a leader is based. I can empathise with those who I work with and help them achieve success beyond expectation.

I have worked hard learning how to align the often uncomfortable room mates of clinical work and business needs. And I am delighted to say that those I have mentored and managed not only 'get it' but embrace it, to the ultimate advantage of (in this order of importance ) the pets, the clients and the business. The results are truly fantastic.

My goal is simple. To offer a sustainable vision for practice offering a real opportunity for all to benefit from veterinary life . In short, to deliver a service and job that works for everyone.

David Nicol’s Experience

  • Vet/Manager

    Double Bay Vet Clinic

    (Veterinary industry)

    October 2009 — Present (11 months)

    Double Bay Vet Clinic is a 2 vet clinic situated in Sydney's affluent eastern suburbs. It is small animal practice delivering veterinary services including, surgery, medicine (including radioactive iodine for hyperthyroid cats) and is fantastically well equipped with top of the range ultrasound and digital X-ray. Not bad for a small place!

    In the 10 mths since joining I have:
    *Developed and implemented a business plan to increase turnover by 10% in year one.
    *Improved practice branding with across channel use of the logo and corporate colours.
    *Devised, designed and published the first truly linked in social media site incorporating a blog, youtube channel, twitter and facebook pages.
    *Introduced new HR management practices with inductions, staff objectives, regular one2one feedback meetings and 6 monthly performance reviews.
    *Trained existing managers to improve their management skills.
    *Identified and guided investment in new equipment.
    *Implemented a range of customer care and compliance improving devices to improve the quality of our service.

    The team are on target to comfortably exceed the turnover target and provide a solid platform for continued growth in the coming financial year.

    This adds more depth of experience to my skill set as a consultant and has allowed me to take new management concepts into the field and trial them first hand. The result is that I can truly say I've been there and done that in both larger corporate practice and the more common 1-2 vet practice. Both have different set of issues but common goals and challenges.
