Steve Russell’s Summary

I enjoy the profound gift of having been qualified in the world of suits and business, and intellectual pursuit, together with the quieter side of self-realisation. I dig the buzz of it all.

If it were easy, everybody would be doing it. Why walk down the well-worn path that others have trod before you...where's the fun in that? Why be a paramecium in an ocean when one can be a big fish in a pond of one's own creation? Even a mud puddle will do, if it's your mud puddle.

I've never really been an employee. I did my first LBOs with one cousin's snow cone business at age 8, and another cousin's paper route at 10. Since then I've been: hippie capitalist, traveller, Real Estate Broker, winegrower, student, Real Estate investor, print publisher; and, now, a web developer...watch this space!

Taking my 40 years' experience of street smarts, formal education, property development, print publishing, advertising, sales and marketing — together with a very loose, albeit lifelong, interpretation of Taoism — I am now developing a very large network of branded websites. It is my goal to create a brand that evokes a rich, positive, proactive and informative level of involvement and experience for the end user.

It's very exciting, and daunting, to be dancing at the thin leading edge of discovery. It's full of risk and uncertainty; and, I readily admit I have always bitten off more than I can chew — which is precisely why I enjoy my life and what I do so much!

I'd very much like to connect with like minded folks from all aspects of the Web 2.0 lifestyle and community to share ideas.

Steve Russell’s Specialties:

I would have to say that my specialty — regardless of the genre — is in discovering and developing opportunity that is overlooked by mainstream players. This is not for everyone, but it sure has made my life interesting....

  • Founder/CEO, Publisher

    Villas &... Magazine

    (Publishing industry)

    July 1992 — September 2004 (12 years 3 months)

    In 1993 I launched—from my spare bedroom—a B&W magazine on newsprint for the real estate market of Spain's Costa del Sol. I built Villas &... Magazine from a local rag into one of Europe's top glossies for property, architecture and décor. Villas Magazine covered the coasts of Spain, Portugal and France with a full-time staff of 10 and 40 contributors.

    With the Internet and a glut of lesser publications came pressure to lower advertising rates. Rather than sacrifice quality for which we were renowned, I decided that my goals had been achieved and exceeded, and to "go out on top." We ceased publication with our 137th issue, when "Villas" was considered the benchmark for all publications in our genre. Our quality and editorial standards have never been achieved since—so say our readers, contributors, publishing peers and advertisers—even 4 years after we ceased publication. A compliment, which, as a publisher, I continue to find very gratifying.

  • Cosmic Wanderer

    (Chemicals industry)

    1969 — 1981 (12 years )

I split to, and lived for a year in, Mexico to avoid the Viet Nam draft, then kinda fell into the hippie thing. Ashland and Lake Klamath, Oregon for an adventure.     With a Stanford MBA in my vision, I started university to confirm that my brain was still functionally wired together. Ah, it was. I went on to earn two Honors Degrees in not-for-dummies disciplines knocked out concurrently in 3 years; and earned my MBA from "the Farm", too. So, that was cool.

    I have no regrets and wouldn't trade those years for any of life's other offerings. I was and still am a contented soul.
