Boping Wu’s Summary

Research and development in the field of signal integrity, high-speed link modeling and calibration, electromagnetic analysis and simulation, IC package and 3D interconnect design, RF microwave circuit and antenna, PCB board designs

Current interests include high-speed interconnects and packaging, micro-electronic simulations, CAD of RF, microwave and millimeter-wave circuits, on/off chip package modeling, computation electromagnetics, metamaterials and integrated nano-photonics, layered medium Green’s function, wave propagation and scattering in complex media, electromagnetic interference and compatibility

Mr. Wu is an author or coauthor on 1 US patent and 23 technical papers, in which 9 are IEEE journal papers. He has developed an interconnector simulator, namely Foldy-Lax Tool, which is thousand times faster than the existing commercial software. In 2008-2009, he served as a pre-doctorial lecturer at UWEE teaching computation methods for electromagnetics and optics applications and how to analyze high-speed microwave circuits in both frequency and time domain.

Selected publication:
[1] B. Wu et al., “Fast computation of layered medium Green’s functions of multilayers and lossy media using fast all modes method and numerical modified steepest-descent path method,” IEEE Trans. Microw. Theory Tech., vol. 56, no. 6, pp. 1446-1454, June 2008.

[2] B. Wu et al., “Modeling multiple vias with arbitrary shape of antipads and pads in high speed interconnect circuits,” IEEE Microwave and Wireless Comp. Lett., vol. 19, no. 1, pp. 12-14, Jan. 2009.

[3] B. Wu et al., “Signal integrity analysis of package and printed circuit board with multiple vias in substrate of layered dielectrics,” IEEE Trans. Adv. Packag., in press, 2009.

C. Ye, and B. Wu, “Micro-via structure design for high performance ICs,” US Patent Pub. No. US 2008/0079139 A1, Pub. date: Apr. 3, 2008, Filing date: Sep. 29, 2006.
