Al Duff, SPHR’s Summary

Al Duff, SPHR’s Experience

  • VP, Global Human Resources

    Norgren, Inc.

    (Public Company; IMI; Industrial Automation industry)

    February 2005 — January 2010 (5 years )

    Responsible for all worldwide human resources initiatives of a 6,500 employee global pneumatics manufacturing company. Managed a team of 14 HR leaders from major operations including sites in the US, UK, Germany, Czech Republic, Switzerland, Mexico, China, India and Australia.

    • Created a global human resources function where none existed before. This effort involved creating structure, processes and discipline around salary administration, recruiting, succession planning and performance management. Results included the consolidation of HR consulting, relocation services, recruiting and payroll administration under select vendors saving over $550K.

    • Orchestrated the effort to leverage five independent IMI divisions, CEOs, CFOs, and HR Leaders to see the value of integrating 23 separate US insurance approaches, consisting of over 3200+ employees, into one common medical plan that resulted in a savings of $1M in the first year.

    • Led the effort in conducting HR due diligence for two successful acquisitions in 2005 and 2007, which met financial and integration expectations.

    • Spearheaded a comprehensive reduction-in-force process that outlined key steps necessary to plan, communicate and execute large-scale site closures and reductions. This process was used in five major Norgren reductions/closures across the US, UK and Europe and led to tens of millions in costs savings

    • Orchestrated and led the effort of the Norgren Board members to evaluate and define the culture necessary to meet its 5-year plan of doubling sales revenue. Inclusive in this effort was the identification of company values and leader competencies.

    • Produced and implemented a global succession planning process that led to a 60% improvement over three years of available successors in key positions; developed an international assignment framework across five continents leading to increased governance of over $1.2M in incremental labor cost.

  • VP, Human Resources

    Micro Motion, Inc.

    (Public Company; EMR; Industrial Automation industry)

    November 2000 — February 2005 (4 years 4 months)

    Responsible for the worldwide human resources function of a 1300-employee global manufacturing company. Operations include manufacturing and service centers in Boulder, The Netherlands, Mexico and China.

    • Led the effort to establish an eLearning strategy for Emerson Process Management, a group consisting of 17 divisions and over 15,000 employees. ROI for this platform over a five-year period exceeded 300%.

    • Participated in creating a Leadership Development program for the Flow Group, a group of five Emerson divisions. Launched this initiative for developing future leaders within Micro Motion by enrolling 30 business leaders in the first year.

    • Created an eHR strategy to promote an environment of self-help within our employees primarily using PeopleSoft and our HR website as the foundation.

    • Revamped the division’s mentoring initiative, which involved the creation of program objectives, selection criteria and process, coaching skills for mentors, accountability for regular meetings and an annual survey. After one year under the new structure, 90% of protégés and mentors overwhelmingly indicated their support of the program and the value it brought the organization.

  • Director, Human Resources

    Qwest Communications

    (Public Company; Q; Telecommunications industry)

    May 1998 — September 2000 (2 years 5 months)

    Responsible for the human resources function of the U S WEST Small Business Group, a 2000-employee, $2 billion operation that provides telecommunications products and services to small business owners in a 14-state region.

    • Directed the Small Business Group HR team in hiring 965 management and occupational employees in 1999, reducing pre-employment screening cost from $293 to $38 per hire in our management sales centers.

    • Provided the HR leadership necessary for the Small Business Group to assimilate into the Qwest organization at the time of the US WEST/Qwest acquisition.

    • Created and implemented a comprehensive diversity plan that incorporated recruiting, training, minority and women-owned business spending, mentoring and community involvement into the unit’s business plan. Accountability tool for senior leaders created quantitative and qualitative improvement by over 40%.

    • Directed the human resources efforts to expand sales staffing levels by 400 employees or 25% over a five-month period. Inclusive in this effort were the implementation of new pre-employment sales screens and the closing of four sales centers while simultaneously expanding four others.

  • HR Manager

    Solvay

    (Privately Held; Chemicals industry)

    May 1995 — May 1998 (3 years 1 month)

    Responsible for all company human resource strategies, policies and programs of the North American chemical operations. Provided guidance to the President and his staff on HR issues.

    • Selected by Solvay to attend an exclusive leadership conference in Belgium involving high potential managers (60 employees of 35,000) on a worldwide basis to work through business issues facing Solvay. At week’s end, our 6-person team was one of three selected to present in Brussels to the Chairman and Chief Executive Officer on critical human resources issues and our plan to address them.

    • Developed and implemented a new company-wide performance management process and incentive pay plan that emphasized core competencies and individual and company critical success factors linked to the annual business plan.

    • Developed a career structure and promotion criterion within the company that provides individual growth opportunities within a flattening organizational environment.

  • Employee & Community Relations Manager

    Quaker Oats Company

    (Public Company; OAT; Food & Beverages industry)

    May 1987 — May 1995 (8 years 1 month)

    Throughout eight years with the Quaker Oats Company, held positions of increasing responsibility across multiple divisions and locations.

    • Provided counsel to plant manager and staff on issues relating to hiring, firing, progressive discipline, collective bargaining agreement interpretation and employment law.

    • Coordinated the HR effort for the acquisition and employee integration of Maryland Club Coffee into the Continental Coffee site.

    • Selected as President of the East End Area Chamber of Commerce in the City of Houston and brought business leaders together to solve area problems.

    • Developed comprehensive health & safety program, which reduced injuries and workers' compensation costs by $130,000. Improved departmental safety performance in all areas: OSHA Incident Rate (OIR), Days Away Severity Rate (DASR) and Lost Work Day Case Incident Rate (LWCIR) by aggressively identifying problem areas and correcting them.

    • Championed change from a traditional position bargaining approach to a principled bargaining method that led to a successful 42-month collective bargaining agreement as principle spokesperson representing the company.

    • Represented the Company and interpreted the collective bargaining agreement in all third step grievances with the local union of the United Steelworkers.

    • Prepared plant for an Office of Federal Contracts and Compliance Programs (OFCCP) audit. Received Letter of Compliance from the US Department of Labor.

  • U. S. Army Officer

    US Army

    (Government Agency; Military industry)

    May 1980 — May 1987 (7 years 1 month)

    Held various leadership positions ranging from Infantry Platoon Leader to Special Forces Aviation Detachment Commander. Throughout seven years of active duty military service, responsibilities included: standardization of training, deployment of personnel and equipment overseas, distribution of awards and discipline, coordination and implementation of an aircraft change from UH-1H (Huey) to UH-60 (Blackhawk), and maintenance of equipment, vehicles and aircraft.
