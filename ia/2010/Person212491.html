    Girls In Tech

    (Non-Profit; Information Technology and Services industry)

    January 2010 — Present (7 months)

    Since its inception in February 2007, Girls in Tech’s main focus is empowering, educating and elevating the status of women in the technology field. Local Girls in Tech chapters engage the local community of women in the technology industry, as well as highlight the specific industry concerns in each geographic region.

    The Columbus chapter of Girls in Tech seeks to enrich the experience of women in technology in the Columbus metro area, helping them to make connections, learn valuable career and life skills, share their talents with others and, above all, have FUN with other like-minded women in the thriving Columbus tech community.

    Girls in Tech is a social network enterprise focused on the engagement, education and empowerment of like-minded, professional, intelligent and influential women in technology. As young women with the capacity to inspire, we made it our personal desire and passion to create and sustain an organization that focuses on the collaboration, promotion, growth and success of women in the technology sector.

    Created in February of 2007, ‘Girls in Tech’ (GIT) was founded by Adriana Gascoigne. GIT and was born out of a need to provide a place for women to cultivate ideas around their careers and business concepts involving technology.
    Girls in Tech aims to offer a variety of resources and tools for women to supplement and further enhance their professional careers and aspirations in technology. Some of these resources include, educational workshops and lectures, networking functions, round table discussions, conferences, social engagements, and recruitment events.

    Talk Social Networking, LLC

    (Marketing and Advertising industry)

    January 2009 — Present (1 year 7 months)

    Talk Social Networking, LLC offers live and interactive Social Media workshops aimed at helping small businesses, solo-preneurs, Knowledge Managers and HR professionals leverage social media in their marketing and recruiting campaigns. Called “Social Media Success: Done in a Day.” The workshops will offer structured social media training and education with a focus on getting social media marketing plans up and integrated in one day. The sessions are augmented with facilitated power networking geared to demonstrate the offline power of online networking.

    My role is to find collaborative social media partners in key cities and to facilitate sharing their expertise and moving the social media niche forward.

    Each city will provide business owners with access to business resources in the exhibit hall section of the workshop.

    Offered throughout 2009, these full-day seminars will be taught by local experts and a certified social media specialist. Registration will include two ground level pre-session trainings tailored to ensure core competency around Web 2.0 technology and basic social media strategies which should be addressed prior to social networking activities.

    Following the training, attendees will be able to confidently develop and execute a strategic social media campaign across a variety of Internet mediums. Post training sessions are offered, at no charge, to assist attendees with assessing their ROI (return on influence) and tweak their campaigns at 30, 60 and 90 day intervals.

    The workshop tour will begin in Columbus, OH on June 25, 2009, and be offered in Charlotte, NC, Washington, DC, New York, NY and San Diego, CA and are limited to 100 guests.
