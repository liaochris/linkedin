Danae Ringelmann’s Summary

Danae Ringelmann co-founded IndieGoGo - an online crowdfunding platform for ideas - to democratize fundraising. She brings entertainment industry and finance expertise. Danae serves as an Advisor to The Conversation, and Fast Company Magazine recently named her one of the Top 50 Women Innovators in Technology.

Passionate about helping artists and entrepreneurs embrace the DIWO (Do-It-With-Others) approach, Danae speaks often at conferences. Recent speaking engagements include Producer’s Guild of America, International Documentary Association, Digital Hollywood, Los Angeles Film Festival, Association of Film Commissioners International, Women in Film & TV, TieCon, UGCX, and UC Berkeley.

Prior to IndieGoGo, Danae was a Securities Analyst at Cowen & Co. where she covered entertainment companies including Pixar, Lions Gate, Disney, and Electronic Arts. Danae also focused on cable network, NFL, newspaper and hedge fund clientele while at JPMorgan's Investment Bank and Private Bank. In the wake of 9/11, Danae co-produced a concert reading of Incident at Vichy, an Arthur Miller play addressing the politically charged topic of racial profiling.

Danae is a CFA charterholder and holds an MBA from the Haas School of Business at UC Berkeley. Danae graduated with a B.A. in Humanities from UNC-Chapel Hill, where she was a Morehead Scholar and varsity rower.
