Endre Stølsvik’s Summary

I am a decent Java coder. My current primary knowledge is system architecture and server side technologies. I also have some server (unix and windows) knowledge. During my stay at CoreTrek AS, I developed a full-blown portal server, incl. its portlet API and associated APIs (e.g. for user management, security, entity access control). This server's API became steady a year or so before the standardization effort 'JSR 168 Portlet API' began. Along with the portal server, CoreTrek has a CMS system called CorePublish (in PHP), and a bit of knowledge about websites, content and document handling have thus necessarily rubbed off on me.

Relevant side interests are unix/Linux, high performance computing, supercomputers, knowledge and information organization, -search and -retrieval, computer/3D graphics. Other interests are photography, finance and (stock) markets, molecular biology, neuroscience, sailing. Some might find the following interesting: I had a C-64 at rather early age, and were active on the "demoscene" as coder!

Current professional interests revolves around a rich client side application, coded in Swing, that is set to change the way the people of this planet will organize their pictures!

Endre Stølsvik’s Experience

  • Lead developer, Java, web services and search platform

    Essens AS

    (Information Technology and Services industry)

    September 2006 — May 2007 (9 months)

    Essens (then Marketaudit) have a sophisticated search platform mainly centered around publicly available information (newspapers, and all documents from the government, parliament). In contrast to standard "flat search", the information in Marketaudit's indices and databases is highly organized, in that the system doesn't throw away the structured elements of what is commonly known as "semi-structured" data.

    Marketaudit's services are licensed and consumed by means of a set of web services. In my time at Marketaudit, I rewrote this entire front end services set, making it a bit more maintainable and future proof, and also fixed and implemented a rather large backlog of bugs, enhancements and feature requests that had accumulated due to missing developer resources.

    Check out the search at stortinget.no to get an idea of the structure and drill-down possibilities contained in this system and database. It is my web service that handles the search requests.

  • Lead developer, Java, CorePortal EIP

    CoreTrek AS

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Computer Software industry)

    September 2000 — August 2006 (6 years )

    Early in CoreTrek's life, the development of a "portal base" was initiated based on the requirements of a specific project we had. The specific project was however shut down quite early, but the portal engine had at that time become a standalone platform with a freestanding API which was deemed interesting enough in itself, and thus became its own product: an Enterprise Information Portal server called CorePortal Server (CoreServer). I was the initiator and lead developer of this product in my time at CoreTrek.

    The CorePortal Server is (or was!) used as the core for NSB's, NHO's and Roxar's intranets, Gilde's extranet "slakteweb", Skagenfondene's internal sales application and PointCarbon's extranet/trading platform "Carbon Trader".

    During my time in CoreTrek, I have been the mentor for six students' during their Master's thesis. This includes the devising of the problem/task, and mentoring during the half-year it takes to do such a "diplom".

  • System administrator

    Norwegian University of Science and Technology (NTNU)

    (Educational Institution; 1001-5000 employees; Computer Networking industry)

    September 1995 — January 1999 (3 years 5 months)

    During my education at the Norwegian University of Science and Technology (NTNU), I also worked part-time at the system administrator group for the students' computer network. This included a shared responsibility for all students' (20k users+) email, personal web pages and home-disks on unix systems, as well as several rooms of client computers, in a heavily heterogenous environment including several unices and windows NT and 2k.

    This was in the "start of the (commercial) Internet", resulting in exponential increase in use during these years. Together with a few co-workers, we devised a new scalable backbone system, taking over the responsibility of the monolithic system that had been in place for years before.

    Having the root-password on such a large system, including access to all these user's personal stuff, gives a sense of responsiblity. Having 20k users doing extremely weird things gives insight into how robust a computer system really have to be to cope with the masses.
