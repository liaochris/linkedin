Kim Smith’s Summary

Kim Smith is co-founder of World2Worlds, Inc. (W2W) which specializes in the education, development, production and management of enterprise level virtual world events, applications and programs. She comes to virtual reality as the culmination of a long career in technology training and engineering/public works. She was Sr. Vice President of Office Automation Training, Inc., providing computer and application training and course design for corporations in the Northwest and later spent 19 years in positions of increasing responsibility in operations, logistics, sales and management at several of the nation's largest Architecture and Engineering (A&E) firms, including CH2M Hill, Tetra Tech and Black & Veatch, managing client service, sales and business development for large public works, military and private sector engineering and technology projects in the Northwest and globally.

An early adopter of virtual world technology, Kim founded RM Metaverse Services (now World2Worlds) in 2006, providing metaverse design/build, community, commerce, branding and event consulting services to corporations entering that environment. As a consultant to global publisher United Business Media, she co-founded and produced Life 2.0 Summit, the leading virtual event on metaverse development.

Today many companies are intrigued by virtual worlds, but aren’t sure what they want or how to benefit from a virtual world event, presence, or project. Kim has provided solutions for such entities as Ziff Davis Enterprise, United Business Media, Sun Microsystems, Cisco, Kelley Executive Partners, IBM, Microsoft and Intel, amongst others. Through the W2W best practices process, she and her partner work closely with management teams to develop solutions that achieve corporate goals and objectives tied to ROI insuring project success.
