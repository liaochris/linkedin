CHPA ~ Corporate Housing Providers Association’s Summary

What is corporate housing?

Corporate housing is defined as a furnished apartment, condominium or house made available for rent or let on a temporary basis, primarily for 30 days or more. Corporate Housing typically includes the following features: Furniture, full kitchen, private bath, linens, housewares, electronics and utilities.

Typical corporate housing unit is:

>>Fully furnished and equipped studio, one, two and three
bedroom apartments.
>>Well maintained properties providing residential living.
>>Many convenient locations in and around major
metropolitan areas.
>>Double the square footage of hotel rooms and suite hotels.
>>More cost-effective for companies for long-term housing needs.

What is the corporate housing industry?
>>Corporate housing industry is a segment of the lodging
industry that provides products, services and support of
furnished, temporary housing.

Who is a corporate housing provider?
>>A corporate housing provider is a business that receives
direct compensation for providing corporate housing, as defined.

History
The corporate housing industry has been a significant growth segment of the lodging industry for the past 20 years.

By the Numbers
>>Total industry revenue was $2.36 Billion in 2009.
>>Average one-bedroom monthly rent is $3,450 ($115/night).
>>Approximately 61,280 U.S. corporate housing units on an

>>Average stay in corporate housing units is 92 days.
