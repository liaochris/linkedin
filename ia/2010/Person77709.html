    (Non-Profit; Hospital & Health Care industry)

  • Owner and Master Instructor

    American Family Martial Arts, Inc.

    (Health, Wellness and Fitness industry)

    May 2000 — April 2008 (8 years )

    As a seventh-degree Black Belt and Master Instructor, I owned and operated two martial arts studios in the greater Portland area, where I:
    • Initiated and coordinated more than a dozen satellite programs, serving a diverse population of students in public and private schools, recreation centers, health clubs and apartment houses.
    • Provided one-on-one instruction to adolescent and adult special needs students with multi-axial disabilities, including Asperger Syndrome, oppositional and depressive disorders, increasing their self-awareness, self-esteem and confidence.
    • As the owner of the Sherwood studio with approximately 100 private students, taught as many as forty private lessons a week, supervised six instructors and a front counter person, and interacted with multiple business, Chamber, and School District employees.
    • As the owner of the Hillsboro studio, I developed Washington County’s first transported after-school and martial arts summer camp program that serviced 13 Beaverton and Hillsboro schools and, in the summer of 2003, featured 14 weeks of summer camp for elementary age children, selling the studio in September 2003.

  • Pastor / Head of Staff / Church Administrator

    Churches in Pennsylvania, New Jersey, Nevada, Oregon and Alaska

    (Religious Institutions industry)

    June 1981 — January 2007 (25 years 8 months)

    During the twenty-some years I worked as a pastor in local churches, I :
    • Led the spiritual, programmatic and financial vision of three dynamic churches in the Presbyterian Church USA, supervising as many as 32 full and part-time staff.
    • Formed two independent congregations, focusing on the needs of formerly churched individuals, commuting between fellowships in Oregon and Nevada.
    • Completed a critical interim pastoral assignment in the United Church of Christ and served as an interim church administrator at a Lutheran Church (ELCA) in Fairbanks, Alaska.
    • Initiated significant cross-cultural mission initiatives in Central America, Mexico, Jamaica, El Salvador and Honduras as well as three locations stateside.
    • Raised millions of dollars for operating and capital funds, including the purchase and renovation of a professional office building and counseling center, the construction of a large family life center, the renovation and additions to the oldest church building in Nevada, the purchase and renovation of an abandoned church building in Portland, Oregon, and the forging of an unlikely alliance of a savings and loan, a nearby casino and city government to create a downtown “super lot” for public and private parking needs.
    • Expanded community outreach in each of the churches that I served, enhancing youth, music, and recovery programs including the creation of a successful performing arts academy, a residential-based youth intern program, thirteen AA programs, and congregations where significant percentages of the membership were involved in volunteer work in the church and community or personal growth programs.
