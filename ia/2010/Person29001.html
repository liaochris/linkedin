Brian McConnell’s Summary

An inventor, author and entrepreneur, I have been building telecommunications systems and applications since the late 1980s. I have founded four Silicon Valley companies since moving to California in the mid 1990s: PhoneZone, an online retailer of telecom products (acquired by Hello Direct, NASDAQ: HELO in 1999), Trekmail, a mobile email technology company (acquired by Visto in 2005), Open Communication Systems, a telecom software and conferencing company (firesale) and Worldwide Lexicon, a collaborative translation and localization service and platform for websites and publishers (my current gig).

We are gearing up to release a new version of our collaborative translation platform, and are looking for customers who are looking for an intuitive and economical solution for publishing multilingual web sites and web services.

Outside of work, I have done SETI research, and have published several peer-reviewed articles, and have authored or co-authored two books on the subject. My SETI research focuses on mathematical and algorithmic languages, and builds upon work done by Hans Fruedenthal in the 1960s and Carl L Devito in the 1990s.

Brian McConnell’s Specialties:

Telecommunications system and software design with emphasis on IVR, wireless and multi-modal communications. Translation and localization technology.
