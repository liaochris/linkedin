Lawrence Herman’s Experience

  • Vice President, Advertising Sales

    Miva, Inc. A Division of Adknowledge

    (Public Company; VTRO; Internet industry)

    Miva is a leading independent Performance Marketing Network . The Company distributes ads across its publisher network utilizing technology to match advertiser selected keywords with consumers who are actively searching for products and services to the ad keywords.

  • Chief Executive Officer

    Clickstream Dynamics

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Internet industry)

    March 2004 — October 2006 (2 years 8 months)

CSD's flagship service, www.RevenueEngine.com allows web sites of all sizes to place search boxes, customized directories, ad boxes and contextual links within their web pages. When users search and click on a sponsored link our affiliates profit.

  • Managing Partner

    Common Ground Advisors

    (Privately Held; Venture Capital & Private Equity industry)

    February 2002 — March 2004 (2 years 2 months)

    Private equity and advisory services firm in New York. Developed, structured and executed transactions and developed joint ventures for our clients primarily between US and South Korean companies.

  • Managing Director

    BusinessDevelopment.com

    (Internet industry)

    2000 — 2002 (2 years )

    New York based business development consultancy. BD was started to provide business development services to a large array of mainly technology-based companies that were part of the holdings and investments of BD’s ultra high net worth shareholders.     • Negotiation and creation of consortium of polymerization companies for the purpose of creating an online bid driven procurement system.

  • General Manager

    Saehan Group

    (Public Company; 5001-10,000 employees; Broadcast Media industry)

    1992 — 1999 (7 years )

    Team Leader for the business development team which consisted of 10 managerial level staff.
    Directed feasibility study for the purchase of CATV system operator in the district of Kumi.
    Managed team to obtain terrestrial broadcast license and form a US$100 million consortium to operate terrestrial television station in Inchon.
    Developed contacts with major international entertainment and broadcast companies including Turner, Cox, News Corporation, Rysher Entertainment, JVC and Sony for the supply of foreign television programming to Korea, as well as the co-production of original Korean language material for international and domestic distribution.
    Planned and directed a project to develop a joint venture program production company with STAR TV (News Corp.).
    Developed and obtained sponsorship for the first Korean television program to be produced on contract for a non-Korean broadcaster (Elite Korea Top Ten for STAR TV).

  • Senior Research Assistant

    Federal Reserve Bank of Boston

    (Banking industry)

    1988 — 1992 (4 years )

    Obtained, analyzed, and presented economic data regarding international trade and finance, domestic monetary and banking conditions and social services for use in long term research projects published in the New England Economic Review. Conducted special projects for senior management including data collection and speech writing for the president of the bank. Major projects included:
    Analysis of mutual-to-stock ownership conversions of thrift institutions on profitability and portfolio composition.
    A study of the macroeconomic consequences and policy implications of long term trends in inventories and sales by industrial sector for several industrialized economies.
    A study of Medicaid expenditures.
    A comparison of the early 90’s downturn in the New England banking industry to Texas in the mid-1980’s.
    Analysis of the competitive effects of several large bank mergers.
    A study of the Community Reinvestment Act and home mortgage lending practices in the Boston Metropolitan Area.
