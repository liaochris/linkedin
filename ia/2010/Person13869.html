Anna Hiller’s Summary

I am interested in pursuing a career in higher education, teaching Spanish Language and Literature at the university level. While Peninsular Spanish is my primary focus, I also have theoretical interests that I would like to explore further, in particular the study of the conjunction of literature & science, and translation studies.

I enjoy teaching both language and literature, with special attention paid to the developing role of technology in the classroom. I have developed several websites for courses using Blogger, WordPress and Moodle, and I am always searching for ways to improve the communicative capacity of my classroom both in person and online.

I have finished my dissertation, titled "Science and Literary Culture during Spain's Edad de Plata (1923-1936)", which I hope to edit and publish within the next 5 years.

I graduated with my doctorate in Hispanic Languages and Literatures in March of 2010.

Anna Hiller’s Specialties:

Classroom Web Technologies, Foreign Language Pedagogy, Modern Peninsular Spanish Literature, 20th Century Spanish Poetry, Translation Theory
