  • Customer Relations and Development

    litl

    (Privately Held; Consumer Electronics industry)

    June 2009 — Present (11 months)

    At litl I wear many different hats and appreciate the challenges and excitement of them all. Working at a start up, especially producing and selling a hardware product, has exposed me to so many different sides of the business, from software development to hardware production to customer support and marketing. It's almost too much fun.

    1. Customer support systems and department development.
    Building a customer support system from the ground up requires testing, dedication, feedback, and most of all, a great team. Drawing resources from software, financial, and IT, it is rewarding to be a driving force in developing a sustainable and completely supported customer experience.

    2. Coordination of beta testing and reporting.
    Before our product launched in November 09, we ran beta field tests to develop a greater understanding of the positive aspects of the product (for marketing purposes), and the areas that needed improvement (for software development purposes). Coordinating a 200 person beta test with a product that was still secret at the time was a rush, and being able to provide the results and data to the team was very gratifying.

    3. Quality assurance and testing.
    Talking with customers everyday about what works and what doesn't work makes you want to find bugs even more. Learning how to do thorough software testing and working with the software team to find the BEST solution to a problem does wonders for developing attention to detail and deep thinking about design questions.

    4. Logistics and Operations
    I have assumed a number of logistics and operations tasks working to make sure that the customer has a seamless experience. As part of a team that spans the whole world, it has been fascinating to understand how a technology product makes it into the consumer's hands. Also a nice opportunity to use some Chinese.

    5. Marketing and PR
    What could be better than spreading a litl love around?

  • President

    OEBug

    (Research industry)

    November 2007 — June 2008 (8 months)

    OEBug, the Organismic and Evolutionary Biology Undergraduates Group, seeks to supplement knowledge of and classroom experiences with organisms, evolution, and ecology. We had field trips to local nature sites and museums, sessions on applying to graduate school, lectures and talks by faculty and visiting scientists, as well as social gatherings and internet-based communications on the latest in natural history news. As the organization developed, we also sought to have a conservation role at the University as well.

    As President, I coordinated and executed all of the events for the club, as well as headed up a team of 10 on the planning committee. Working on new projects and leading trips is incredibly rewarding, and the high quality of group members and their fantastic ideas, knowledge of the natural world, and adventurous spirit made this role easy and fulfilling.

  • Co-Founder and Advisor

    OUR HUAM

    (Museums and Institutions industry)

    March 2006 — March 2008 (2 years 1 month)

    The Organization of Undergraduate Representatives of the Harvard University Art Museums is based on Paris' (co-founder) experience working with the Getty on education projects and my experience with science museums and project management to attract students to the museums.

    We began with hosting "Nights at the Fogg Museum," which included after-hours entertainment and short, informative tours.

    From there, the organization has blossomed, with one 2008 event attracting over 700 people, and executive meetings regularly attended by over 20, with much active discussion. We have also worked with the museum administration to develop the OUR HUAM student guide training program, an intensive training program for undergrads who give hour tours of the museum for fellow undergraduates and the public.

    The recent and continuing renovations of Harvard Art Museum have been an lesson in developing an organization that is adaptable and sustainable, and continues to be an exciting challenge.
