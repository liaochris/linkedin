Tim Hayes, AICP’s Experience

  • Managing Partner

    March 2008 — Present (2 years )

    • Lead development specializing in the restoration and sustainable development of properties involving significant natural and cultural resources.

  • Vice President - Development Management

    D9 Development Company, LLC

    (Real Estate industry)

    September 2006 — February 2008 (1 year 6 months)

    • Managed development teams for high-profile projects with difficult planning, design and construction challenges involving significant cultural and natural resources.

    • Projects included the Brownstones at 1st & Church, the Boyd Mill House, and the 131 3rd Avenue Building, all located in the Franklin National Historic District.

  • Senior Project Manager

    TAM Consultants

    (Real Estate industry)

    September 2003 — August 2006 (3 years )

    • Successfully accomplished more than $50 million of health care, commercial, industrial, residential and mixed use projects on behalf of corporate, government, institutional and individual clients.

    • Major clients included Virginia Baptist Homes, Inc., Williamsburg Landing, Inc., Busch Entertainment Corporation, and USAA Insurance Company.

  • Director of Sustainable Development

    Northampton County, Virginia

    (Government Administration industry)

    January 1993 — September 2003 (10 years 9 months)

    • Established and managed what may be the first Department of Sustainable Development for a local government in the US.

    • Led award-winning community development process and authored the Northampton County Sustainable Development Action Strategy, which earned the Presidential Leadership Award from National Association of Counties.

    • Facilitated extensive community involvement and ownership in the process that achieved tangible economic, environmental and social benefits for the County.

    • Built a broad base of support involving more than 50 state and Federal agencies, non-profit organizations and corporations. Wrote $6 million in successful grant proposals for implementation of specific aspects of the local development strategy.

    • Led and supervised professional and administrative staff and an extensive team of consultants and contractors for implementation of local sustainability initiatives.

    • Major projects included Sustainable Technology Park, eco-industrial covenants and restrictions with measurable sustainability standards, restoration of historic Eastville Inn, funding/acquisition of Chesapeake Bay Nature Preserve; planning and approval of prospective 7.8-megawatt Cape Charles Wind Farm, 7.2-mw Seaside Wind Farm.

    • Traveled extensively throughout the US and internationally as a frequently-requested speaker and presenter on successfully putting sustainability into action.

  • Executive Director

    Sustainable Technology Park Authority

    (Real Estate industry)

    September 1996 — September 2001 (5 years 1 month)

    • Developed 200-acre Cape Charles Sustainable Technology Park, a National Showcase Community project co-funded by the US Department of Commerce, US Environmental Protection Agency and others as a prototype of the President’s Council on Sustainable Development.

    • Created the vision and detailed plans, attracted extensive public and private investment, facilitated organization and incorporation of the development authority, administered the first three phases of development from start to finish, including planning and conceptual/schematic design, land assembly and environmental assessments, fund-raising and financing, public approvals, zoning and permitting, design development and contract documents, site improvements and building construction, marketing and sales, tenant improvements, opening and occupancy.

    • Raised $8.5 million of public funding, plus corporate commitments of an additional $8 million; assembled 203 acres of land; built 31,000-sf green industrial-office building meeting US Green Building Council – Leadership in Environmental and Energy Design (LEED) standards featuring 42-kw solar photovoltaic roof.

    • Attracted and signed companies leading innovation in renewable energy, clean water technologies and industrial symbiosis, including a Swiss manufacturer of architecturally-integrated photovoltaic panels, a Norwegian company producing energy recovery components for reverse osmosis desalination, and wind energy development companies from Germany and Ireland.

  • Senior Planner

    City of Newport News, Virginia

    (Government Administration industry)

    October 1988 — January 1993 (4 years 4 months)

    • Managed all aspects of long-range, current and neighborhood planning for a rapidly growing, environmentally-sensitive district on the James River near Jamestown and the Chesapeake Bay.

    • Led award-winning community processes for creation and implementation of a comprehensive plan and special area management plans to establish and accelerate progressive economic and community development in concert with the protection and restoration of diverse natural and cultural assets.

    • Major projects included, District III Area Plan, Deep Creek/Menchville Plan, design and development of Riverview Farm Park, co-authored Framework for the Future comprehensive plan.

  • Planner II

    City of Longmont, Colorado

    (Government Administration industry)

    May 1986 — October 1988 (2 years 6 months)

    • Guided projects through the planning and development process for this growing community at the base of the Rocky Mountains.

    • Worked with developers to achieve high standards, attain public and private goals, and secure approvals from a long list of review agencies.

    • Negotiated agreements with developers, formulated strategies, plans and ordinances to achieve high quality development and conserve the City’s rich natural and cultural resources.

    • Major projects included support for development of Longs Peak Industrial Park, team production of the Longmont Area Comprehensive Plan, administration of City’s Landmark Preservation Commission, developed preservation plan and incentives program.

  • Planner/Designer

    WestPlan Partnership - Boulder, Colorado

    (Real Estate industry)

    • Work recognized for quality, innovative solutions, protection of environmental resources, consensus building and action.

    • Major projects included Rocky Mountain Technological Park and Estes Park Downtown Revitalization Program in Colorado, Rancho La Sierra and Rancho Del Rio mixed-use developments in Southern California, Saint Vrain Valley at Lyons in Colorado.

    • Major projects included Denver Technological Center, Health Development Corp of America, Arvada City Center, Paradigm Conference Center, and Boulder Valley Regional Center.
