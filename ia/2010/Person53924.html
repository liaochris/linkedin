Dee Mason’s Summary

Dee Mason is the founder and administrator of Business Against Substance Abuse (BASA) Coalition, a private not-for-profit organization based in Columbus, Ohio. BASA Coalition serves the Ohio community doing prevention projects funded by the Ohio Department of Alcohol and Drug Addiction Services (ODADAS) and Franklin County Alcohol, Drug Addiction and Mental Health Services Board (ADAMH).

In 1997, Dee established Working Partners Systems, Inc., a private for-profit company, to perform the functions of marketing, product development and statewide/national expansion of substance-free (drug-free) workplace programs and services.

After almost eighteen years, the combination of these two organizations, known as Working Partners Consortium, has become the recognized virtual leader for comprehensive, current information and programming on all issues related to substance abuse in the workplace.

Dee Mason’s Specialties:

Legal, legislative and professional issues impacting drug/substance-free workplace programs (DFWP).

Dee Mason’s Groups:

Dee’s expertise is recognized and often called upon. For nine months in 1997, she served the Ohio Bureau of Workers’ Compensation as the facilitator of a statewide task force to develop the Drug-Free Workplace Program, an incentive program of 10-20% premium discounts for eligible employers who institute a drug-free workplace (to date the deepest discount anywhere in the united States). She was commissioned and wrote the technical assistance manual about the program for the State and trained the first 150 Bureau representatives in the Rule.

Dee is the founder and administrator of Business Against Substance Abuse (BASA) Coalition, a private not-for-profit organization based in Columbus, Ohio. Dee continues to be responsible for BASA Coalition, which serves the Ohio community doing prevention projects funded by the Ohio Department of Alcohol and Drug Addiction Services (ODADAS) and Franklin County Alcohol, Drug Addiction and Mental Health Services Board (ADAMH).
