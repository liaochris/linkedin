  • Product Engineering Manager

    National Semiconductor Corporation

    (Semiconductors industry)

    August 1977 — June 1982 (4 years 11 months)

    Joined National Semiconductor's CMOS Logic product group right after completing the MSEE program at University of Cincinnati. Was involved in all phases of product life cycle engineering from designing, testing, yield improvment to EOL on CMOS Logic components. Contributions included designing new ESD protection circuitry which was adopted in National's 7400 CMOS devices. Led the charge in increasing the yield for 8-bit A/D product by establishing a correlation between width variations of the resistance ladder and linearity error of National's biggest volume and money-maker product during 1978 - 80. This product was considered to be the most successful in that timeframesince it was used in the trip computers in all GM Cadillac cars.
