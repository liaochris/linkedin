Robert Coe’s Summary

Immediately following high school graduation, from Bloomfield Central School, Rob enlisted in the United States Marine Corps. His decision was supported by his family and motivated by his Uncle Chip Coe (USMC) and the death of his Great Uncle: Neil Champoux, a Marine Raider during World War II who had lost his life defending Henderson Field at Guadalcanal.
Rob served as an Airfield Operations Specialist at Cherry Point, NC for a year and a half before being accepted into the Marine Security Guard Program at Quantico, VA. This program is one of a few special duty assignments available to qualified Marines. In the past, less than 1% of all Marines have qualified and completed this duty. As a Sergeant, Rob proudly served his county guarding the American Embassies in Valletta, Malta and Cairo, Egypt. In 2001, Rob received an Honorable Discharge for serving five years active duty in the Marines.
Following the Corps, Rob attended Finger Lakes Community College and graduated with his Associates Degree in Business Administration. He finished his college degree with a Bachelor of Science in Business Administration at the Kogod School of Business at American University in Washington, DC. Subjects such as Corporate Finance, Principles of Operations Management, Sales and Marketing contributed significantly to his education and continue to interest Rob today.
Since College, Rob has developed a professional expertise in Internet Marketing and Alternative Energy product sales. Today, he works for the New York State Department of Labor helping people as a Labor Service Representative.
Rob has recently become engaged to Ms. Gena Alessi from Irondequoit, NY and they intend to marry in the summer of 2011. When he is not working or at the Elks club, he enjoys scuba diving, hunting, fishing and working on his home!
