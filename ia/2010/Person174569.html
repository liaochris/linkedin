Robert C. Raidt’s Summary

Bob Raidt is a marketing leader with a proven track record of effectiveness in bringing forth innovation and bringing out the best in multi-functional, multi-national teams. He is passionate about creativity. He has over 20 years of experience on large accounts, primarily McDonald's and Procter & Gamble, and he has a Master of Management degree from Northwestern University's Kellogg Graduate School of Management ('96).

Over the past 12 years he has worked for Leo Burnett, 6 and 1/2 years in Chicago and 5 and 1/2 years in expatriate assignments (Tokyo and Singapore). He has been a leader in numerous significant new business wins on the McDonald's account including Japan, Australia and most recently six large Latin American countries. Bob's Leo Burnett teams' strategic and creative products have contributed to McDonald's outstanding global business momentum. His teams' McDonald's creative products have achieved award recognition at Cannes, Effie, Clio, and Addy shows, among others.

In 2008, in addition to his McDonald's responsibilities, Bob led the operational implementation of a new agency service model on P&G wherein Leo Burnett has been established as the Swiffer Brand Agency Leader (BAL), which has the responsibility of recommending the appointment and then managing all Swiffer partner agencies globally (ie. Digital and Interactive, Communication Planning, Public Relations, etc), and managing their compensation. This is a highly innovative model that places the responsibility of agency strategic marketing integration squarely with the lead agency. In this model, Bob has successfully integrated agency services from Publicis and Omnicom, as well as independents, into a collaborative team.

The skills he cultivated in winning the McDonald's Japan account in 2003 by designing and implementing an integrated agency alliance between Dentsu and Leo Burnett proved helpful to his successful P&G BAL implementation.

Robert C. Raidt’s Specialties:

Bob specializes in innovative management of large multi-national accounts, as evidenced by his years of leadership experience on McDonald's and Procter & Gamble brands in USA and expatriate assignments.
