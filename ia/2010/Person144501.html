Michelle Sherman’s Summary

I work closely with clients to try and strategize a business dispute so costly litigation can be avoided, and my clients' goals can be achieved in the most cost efficient way. This legal counseling also extends to advising my clients on compliance matters, and includes the drafting of compliance programs for business clients, including social media policies and antitrust compliance programs.

When litigation is unavoidable, I have handled a wide range of cases from alleged unfair competition, price fixing, tortious interference with contract, breach of contract, Cal. Business & Professions Code Section 17200 (unfair trade practices), trademark disputes, misappropriation of trade secrets, fraudulent conveyance, partnership disputes, Clean Water Act, and other business and white collar matters.

I have tried a number of cases, with my client being the prevailing party in most instances, so I am very comfortable with trial work and being in a courtroom handling a daily calendar as an assistant public defender, and as a business litigator. I also serve as a faculty member for the National Institute for Trial Advocacy, and have been teaching programs with them since 1998.

With the exponential growth of social media, and individuals and companies doing business through the internet, I recognized a need for regular legal updates concerning social media, and have started publishing articles in this area on my firm's blog sites, and also third party publications. My interest in social media culminated in a social media blog site that I launched at Sheppard Mullin, www.socialmedialawupdate.com. The blog site covers all aspects of the law and how they intersect with social media and the internet.

Public service has also been a priority for me. Between working as an assistant public defender, presently writing press releases and coaching for Special Olympics of Southern California; and also serving as a hotline counselor and trainer for LACAAW.

Michelle Sherman’s Specialties:

Litigation, trial work, complex business disputes, criminal defense; social media policies; and legal consulting on social media issues.
