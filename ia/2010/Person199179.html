Talisa Chang’s Education

  • New York University

    B.A., Individualized , Writing, English, Sociology of Language , 2005 — 2009

    I graduated magna cum laude from the Gallatin School of Individualized Study with a concentration in writing, english, and the sociology of language. My final colloquium, entitled The Limits and Possibilities of Language, explored the politics of language and identity through texts and coursework in linguistic anthropology, media studies, political science, English, and writing.

    I studied abroad for one year, travelling independently to over a dozen countries. I spent one semester studying through Lexia International at the University of the Western Cape in Cape Town, South Africa, focusing on history, politics, education, urban and women studies, language policy, and linguistic anthropology. My studies culminated with a four credit creative independent research project exploring narratives and images. My second semester, I studied at the American University in Paris, France, taking coursework in urban anthropology, French, Parisian history, and literature.
