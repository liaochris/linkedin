Adolfo Gutierrez’s Summary

I have been a practicing serial entrepreneur/scientist/engineer for over 17 years with degrees in Physics, Microelectronics, Optoelectronics, Nuclear Engineering and Engineering Physics. I have been also a CFA Candidate. I am involved with two global consulting practices:

--> My interest in regards to uBricks Research is that of creating and operating a global engineering and management consulting network of technical consultants and of unique R&D facilities. This network is to be centrally coordinated by uBricks Research thus better supporting the early stages of R&D in new product development efforts based on exploiting advances in miniaturization technologies. We expect this network will drive global R&D efficiencies and become and engine of value creation.

--> My interest in regards to Novatempo is summarized in its mission statement, that is: "Accelerating Technology Ventures and Entrepreneurship Around the World through Workshops, Consulting, Coaching and Investments". Novatempo is becoming a de-facto network, spanning the whole world, that includes universities, incubators, angels and entrepreneurs. We expect that Novatempo will drive global trade of innovative high value added products.

  • CEO / CTO

    Memstar Corp.

    (Telecommunications industry)

    July 1999 — January 2003 (3 years 7 months)

    I was an entrepreneurial co-founder of MEMSTAR Corp and its acting CTO or CEO, depending on how you look at at my activities over those three years. We pioneered "Package-Centric Microengineering" for applications in simplified MEMS optical components packaging and assembly. We developed IP for optical attenuators arrays, add-drop multiplexers and optical cross-connects. We also applied the technology to areas such as adaptive RF-Front Ends and even to cryptographic devices. We developed IP for a novel modular packaging architecture for integration of optical microsystems that we have referred as Microbricks. The work I started on microbricks, an architecture for microsystems integration and complexity buildup is still ongoing as part of the actvities of uBricks Research.
