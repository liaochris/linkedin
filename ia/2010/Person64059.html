Elliott Small’s Summary

Elliott Small first became involved with electric car technology in 1978. Mr. Small has worked as a computer consultant and programmer for banks, accounting firms, associations, retail establishments, political organizations and other types of organizations in the USA and Africa. He has also worked as a research Chemist at General Foods Corporation where he originated a new gelling process utilizing enzymatic activity. As Director, Technology Commercialization Center, Washington, D.C., a U.S. Commerce Department funded program to assist businesses with new technology products, Mr. Small assisted an electric car company in its progression from experimental prototypes to completion of an advanced production prototype. He has a bachelor's degree from the College at Harvard University with a major in Biochemical Sciences. He has also completed a graduate level course at Harvard in computer programming.
