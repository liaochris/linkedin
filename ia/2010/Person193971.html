Stephanie Doty’s Summary

I have worked as a development professional in the non-profit sector since 2002, building on a foundation of diverse, comprehensive experience from the for-profit sector. In the intervening years, I've consistently worked to strengthen and enhance my professional non-profit leadership and management skills and experience. After observing considerable non-profit organizational dysfunction, I returned to graduate school to pursue a master's degree in non-profit leadership. I wanted to understand the genesis of organizational dysfunction in order to achieve long-term economic sustainability and success. From that beginning, I've become increasingly interested in capacity-building funding support to strengthen an organization's infrastructure. Having enhanced and strengthened my awareness and understanding of organizational dynamics, I've sharpened my focus to concentrate on creating solutions that lead to the dynamic impact of social change that women's philanthropy supports by concentrating its funding support on women's issues programs.

I feel passionately committed to overcoming societal issues and challenges that lead to violence against women and children. Creating viable, working, long-term solutions is what fuels my passion and commitment. I sincerely believe women's philanthropy and funding support lays the foundation for empowering women and their families. When women feel safe and distanced from abuse, they experience the freedom that allows them to regain self-esteem, protect their families and rebuild their lives so that all are able to move forward with direction, goals and live long, productive lives..
