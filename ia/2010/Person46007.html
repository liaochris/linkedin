  • Owner

    Dale May Photography

    (Photography industry)

    1993 — Present (17 years )

    Dale May grew up outside Philadelphia in Chadds Ford, PA.

    Dale's family was immersed in the arts. His grandfather was a big band drummer and band leader in the 20’s and 30’s and his Grandmother was a supper club singer. Dale played the drums from age five and was also inspired by his neighbor, the American Contemporary Realist Painter; Andrew Wyeth.

    Dale moved to NY to study painting, color theory, and art history at Parson's School of Design. The foundation in these first few years shaped his current aesthetic style and he graduated in 1993 with a BFA in Photography.

    Dale left Parsons and went on to work with iconic photographer Joyce Tenneson whom influenced him with her ethereal effects. He continued working with fashion and still life photographers including Jose Picayo. Dale chose to apprentice photographers that were both commercial as well as recognized fine artists and was drawn to a painterly style of work. Work with still life photographers provided Dale a technician's training in lighting, equipment and post production.

    Dales early work in the commercial realm was character driven, color intensive photo illustration with a conceptual feel. Dale later became well known within the music industry for his very creative and illustrative work with artists (late nineties to the present). It was with applications in post first used for his work in music and entertainment photography that Dale came full circle with digitally manipulated, stylized work with meticulous detail and unlimited creativity that made him a great advertising hire.

    In 2006 Dale launched his own post production firm aptly named Post Image Group. Working with some of the most talented retouchers in NY and as Creative Director, Dale executes on projects ranging from sleek beauty to anything goes higher concept work.

    Dale works with the inspiration to make the unthinkable real, as well as... believable.
