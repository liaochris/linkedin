Henry Wong’s Summary

Over the years, I have worked in a variety of industries -- including many different areas of the finance industry, and in telecommunications, media, education, manufacturing, and retail. I have also worked with many partners on projects -- in conjunction with -- hardware (OEM), software (ISV), and consulting (PS) firms.

In the roles that I have taken, I started as a developer, and worked up to become an architect and project manager. I have worked in engineering, professional services, sales, education, and marketing. In the end, the role that gave me the most gratification was being a developer. In a sense, I have ended up exactly where I had started.

On the side, I have also co-authored many articles and a couple of books.


This is not a complete summary of my career, but should provide you with an idea of my current thoughts about my career. For more information, you can also take a look at my resume, which I have attached. This resume provides more details, but admittedly, I don't update it frequently.

I also spend time on the Java forums, specifically, the JavaRanch (website linked to this page), where I am the moderator of the Threads forum. In some ways, this is my small contribution back to the industry which has provided me my career.

  • Senior Staff Engineer (Software and Performance Engineering)

    Azul Systems, Incorporated

    (Privately Held; Computer Hardware industry)

    2007 — 2010 (3 years )

    Working for Azul was similar to having two jobs in one. I worked on many customer pilots, optimizing Java programs to run on Azul's hundreds of cores hardware. Azul's profiler (Real Time Performance Monitor) made this job easy.

    At the same time, I also worked in engineering. And I worked on the very same product (RTPM) that made my job in the field easy.

    This mix of work isn't very common, and made my tenure with Azul enjoyable.

  • Senior Consultant

    Enigmatec Corporation

    (Privately Held; Computer Software industry)

    2004 — 2007 (3 years )

    Working for Enigmatec was like going back to basics. We were a pure Java shop, and develop JMX MBeans for managing and monitoring everything from low end machines to high end servers.

    Having a manager that thought everything was possible, and had the skills to back it up, also made the job much more enjoyable. It is unfortunate that the product was somewhat difficult to sell.

  • Project Manager, Architect, & Computer Engineer

    Sun Microsystems, Inc.

    (Public Company; ORCL; Information Technology and Services industry)

    1989 — 2003 (14 years )

    My tenure at Sun Microsystems is basically my career (in the small). All of the domains that I have worked in, and all of the type of jobs that I have worked in, which I have mentioned in my introduction, were with Sun. I can even state that I started with Java in 1994, when the language was first released by Sun (and was still unnamed).

    This is the place where I had my highest highs, and my lowest lows, and I wouldn't ever give any of it up.

  • Senior Software Engineer

    Advanced Graphics Applications, Inc.

    (Information Technology and Services industry)

    1986 — 1989 (3 years )

    Working for AGA was probably the best way to get into the computer and IT business. We worked on drivers for SCSI WORM disks. We worked on our own graphics tools. And even implemented a CCITT compression system. All of this was done using 8086 assembly.

    Having a manager who was as young as we were, and who didn't know what was not possible, only made this more enjoyable (in retrospect). Basically, this job was a trial by fire.
