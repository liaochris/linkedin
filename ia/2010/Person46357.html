Dana Levin’s Summary

After graduating from The Art Institute of Chicago in 1990, Dana learned the Academic Painting techniques of the European masters at the Florence Academy of Art. She completed the rigorous three-year Florence Academy of Art curriculum and upon graduation was asked to join the school's drawing and painting faculty for the next five years. Ms. Levin is part of a Classical Realism lineage that connects her as an artist and teacher to Jean-Leon Gerome, The Boston School Painters, Tarbel, Paxton and Benson, Ives Gammel, Richard Lack, and her own maestro, Daniel Graves.

Dana Levin’s paintings have been exhibited in galleries and museums throughout the United States and abroad; including The Eleanor Ettinger Gallery, NYC, John Pence Gallery, SF, Banks Gallery Portsmouth, NH, Bert Gallery, Providence RI, The Canton Museum of Art, LA, The Spartanburg Art Museum, NC, and Panorama Museum, Bad Frankenhausen Germany. Her work is in the permanent collection of the Everson Museum in Syracuse, NY and the New Britain Museum of Art in New Britain, CT.

Ms. Levin is the recipient of numerous awards including; Who’s Who in American Art, American Artists Professional League- Ridgewood Art Association Award, 24th Annual Artist’s Magazine -First Place Portrait Category,and the Katharine A. Lovell Memorial Award.

Dana Levin’s Specialties:

Painting portraits, figures, still lifes, florals, and interiors. Commissioned portraits. Classical and Contemporary Realism paintings. Representational art Master Instructor for drawing and oil painting.
