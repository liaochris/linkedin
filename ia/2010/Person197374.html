Sunil Paul’s Summary

I'm an investor and entrepreneur in clean energy companies. Through my investment fund, Spring Ventures, I incubate or invest in companies that have the promise to become major, disruptive forces in solving the climate crisis and reducing energy dependence.

I look for ideas that have innovative, protectable technology or business-models that can change the world with teams that are smart, fun to work with and driven. If you have an idea or company that could transform the market and world, I want to talk to you. Take a look at the Gigaton Scale framework at http://www.gigatonthrowdown.org. If your company can "Go Gig" or be a crucial enabler to Gigaton Scale, you will definitely get my attention!

My thesis since 2002: macro forces of environmental regulation, climate change, and energy security are creating new pressues on the energy market. New technology advances from other fields such as semiconductors, biotechnology, surface science, and nanotechnology will create produce profound breakthroughs. Innovations in business and finance models will be necessary to scale up their ideas to their true potential. Engagement with government is a necessary and vital part of a successful scale up of cleantech companies.

In addition to my "for-profit" activities, I'm engaged in a number of non-profit activities: political integrity and accountability, community developmental, and global climate change.

  • Founder

    Gigaton Throwdown

    (Renewables & Environment industry)

    November 2007 — Present (2 years 4 months)

    In 2007 a friend who is now part of the Obama Whitehouse said, "You cleantech guys could all make a bunch of money and make no difference" in climate and energy security. I realized he was right and took up his challenge to figure it out. What does it take to scale cleantech?

    The Gigaton Throwdown is a project to encourage entrepreneurs, investors and policy makers to plan to grow companies to a scale that they change the climate. We studied a portfolio of cleantech pathways that could address climate change, energy security, and economic development. We specifically looked for paths to avoid 1 gigaton of CO2e per year for each pathway by the year 2020.

    The Gigaton Throwdown framework is now being used by parts of DOE and the Carbon War Room as a framework for prioritizing their efforts. It also informs all my work in investment and entrepreneurship. Its all about scalability.

    See http://www.gigatonthrowdown.org for the full report.
