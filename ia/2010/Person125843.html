Lisa Blackstone’s Summary

LISA B. BLACKSTONE graduated from the University of Georgia with a Bachelor of Arts degree in Philosophy. She worked on Name That Tune in Hollywood, Ca. and afterward entered the business world as a stockbroker at the Robinson Humphrey Company in Atlanta, Ga. She owned and operated an Arabian horse training and breeding business. Bringing her expertise in corporate finance to the horse business, she developed limited partnerships and stallion syndications for investors. She competed on a national level in horse show competitions and earned national championship honors as an equestrian.

Lisa served as a Director on the Board of the International Arabian Horse Association in Denver, Colorado for many years. She was elected to serve on the Ethical Practices Review Board, a hearing panel adjudicating allegations of rule infractions by members within the industry from horse abuse to fraud and embezzlement. Lisa is a licensed horse show judge and continues to judge competitions internationally.

Lisa received her J.D. from the University of Georgia School of Law. Before opening her own firm as a sole practitioner, Lisa was an associate with Blackburn, Walther and Sloan and practiced as a general civil litigator focusing on domestic relations, commercial transactions, real estate litigation, real estate broker advocacy, title insurance litigation and real estate closings.

She is a certified mediator and promotes mediating disputes between parties as a strong alternative to litigation.

in 2007, Lisa created a website teaching children about horses using video tutorials and teaching parents about how to avoid financial mistakes as newcomers enter the horse industry. See www.HorseAndRiderClub.com
