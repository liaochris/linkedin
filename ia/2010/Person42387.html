Colette Ellis’s Summary

Colette D. Ellis is Founding Principal & Head Coach of InStep Consulting LLC (http://www.instepconsulting.com). Since 1998, the company has helped people and organizations discover their competent advantage™ through interactive coaching, seminars and strategic retreats.

Colette bases her intuitive approach on nearly 15 years advising leaders from Fortune 500 companies, not-for-profit organizations, and government entities across the United States. Skilled at learning her client’s needs, she has trained executives and managers in communication skills, leadership principles and teambuilding. Her leadership development model, Discover Your Competent Advantage, helps leaders leverage their power, passion and relationships to achieve sustainable results (http://competent-advantage.blogspot.com).

Colette encourages innovative and continuous learning as an Adjunct Professor with NYU Wagner Graduate School of Public Service, teaching courses on Managing Diversity. She also has served as Adjunct faculty with Long Island University C.W. Post (Communication Skills for Managers), the City and State Universities of New York, and a Business Writing Instructor with Gotham Writers’ Workshop. She has been a featured speaker for 85 Broads, 100 Women in Hedge Funds, Brooklyn Chamber of Commerce, Citibank at Work, Downtown Women’s Club, Grace Institute, International Association of Administrative Professionals, National Coalition of 100 Black Women, and the U.S. Small Business Administration.

Colette earned her BA in English (cum laude) from Dartmouth College, and an MPA in Public Finance from New York University, where she was a Dean’s Scholar. In 2006, she completed certification at the Consultation Center of Yale University School of Medicine in Coping with Work & Family Stress™, a work-life balance program. She also is certified by FranklinCovey to teach 7 Habits of Highly Effective People® and FOCUS: Achieving Your Highest Priorities.

  • Adjunct Professor

    (Higher Education industry)

    Managing Diversity: An Evidence-Based Approach
    A critical component in the contemporary organization requires leaders to exhibit exceptional leadership and management skills. A diverse workforce and a diverse client-base contribute to the complexity of every organization. Organizational Leaders are also expected to effectively manage this diversity using the best evidence available.

    This course, Diversity Management – an Evidence-Based Approach, introduces leaders to a variety of organizations, methods, and processes that have demonstrated Best Practices in recruiting and retaining a diverse workforce. These include Diversity Management practices that are effective at meeting the needs of clients, employees, managers, and the organization.

    In addition, the course helps each student to gather a personal understanding of his/her own biases and assumptions that can help or hinder the process of creating an open and inclusive work environment.
