Niall Kennedy’s Summary

Currently rethinking consumer financial services and video publishing for the modern, interconnected Web. Technical leadership and implementation with a global focus.

Current and past clients include American Express, Viacom, General Electric, Sony, Johnson & Johnson, Harvard Medical School, eBay, Twitter, Mozilla, WordPress.com, Fairfax Digital, and the government of Hong Kong among others.

I am best known for my work on the "fragmented web," the idea that a web presence must extend beyond a single website and into the destinations in which consumers already spend their time every day. Companies must build new paths to their user base to spur new activity away from a central hub. iPhone applications, Facebook Open Graph integrations, Twitter update streams, OpenSocial gadgets, and social APIs are just a few of the ways web development and search has changed into an interconnected network of publishing and consumption. How and where will users interact with your content?

I build product development teams focused on exceptional web performance. I focus on front-end development, bringing data-driven websites to the browser render and interaction stage as quickly as possible, integrating local (platform- or country-specific) UI/UE expectations wherever possible. I use HTML5, JavaScript, CSS3, and other advanced techniques present in modern browsers for optimal performance and experience.

My personal products and projects utilize the Python programming language, Django development framework, and YUI or jQuery JavaScript libraries. I build sites using Google App Engine for its heavy data, geo-distributed, and transactional architecture.

I am a contributor to OpenSocial, WordPress, and Google App Engine SDK open source projects. I have contributed to HTML5 video and web storage specifications, OpenSearch core and extension specification, and the OpenSocial REST specification.
