Mark Vega’s Summary

Entertainment/IP attorney responsible for forging innovative deal structures between brand owners and content creators, bringing new models of finance, distribution and marketing to life.

Mark Vega’s Specialties:

Crafting split rights and revenue sharing models; designing compensation structures touching on both IP ownership and revenue sharing; all aspects of motion picture finance, production and distribution; non-fiction storytelling and documentary film creation, finance and alternative distribution systems; and, importantly, banjo picking and cigar appreciation.

Mark Vega’s Experience

  • Of Counsel

    Luce Forward

    (Partnership; Law Practice industry)

    April 2009 — Present (11 months)

    Practice focus on entertainment, marketing & advertising and new media matters. Clients include entertainment content financiers, producers and distributors as well as advertising agencies and commercial production companies. Projects range from creation and maintenance of entertainment production and distribution funds to negotiation of agreements for the acquisition of literary properties to the provision of film production services. Advertising and marketing projects range from establishing standard operating procedures for the creation and exploitation of intellectual property to working with corporate clients who are integrating their brands into entertainment content.

  • Partner/IP Steward

    Omelet

    (Privately Held; Marketing and Advertising industry)

    2004 — March 2009 (5 years )

    Responsible for creating and maintaining new deal structures with and between clients and brand partners. Serve as the company’s primary IP steward. Develop new revenue models merging brand messages, consumer desires and original content when all share strands of the same DNA.

  • Of Counsel

    Greenberg Traurig

    (Partnership; 1001-5000 employees; Entertainment industry)

    2001 — 2004 (3 years )

    Practice focussed on entertainment, technology and new media matters. Clients included writers, directors, producers, distributors and financiers in the filmed entertainment arena. Projects ranged from negotiation of agreements for the acquisition of literary properties to the provision of film production services to agreements related to production finance, distribution and exhibition. Counseled corporate clients with regard to integration of their brands into television programming and motion picture projects as a means of efficiently expending and maximizing returns from advertising and promotional dollars.

  • Senior Associate, Information Technology

    Brown Raysman Millstein Felder & Steiner

    (Partnership; Law Practice industry)

    1999 — 2001 (2 years )

    Practice focus in entertainment, Internet, licensing and new media law, counseling and representing film distributors and producers as well as Internet entrepreneurs who emerged from the independent film arena. Also represented a wide variety of technology and software companies, servicing all legal needs from start-up through IPO.

  • Of Counsel

    Daniels, Baratta & Fine

  • Associate
