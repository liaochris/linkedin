Trish Gant’s Summary

My work has appeared in Sam Stern's Cooking Up a Storm for Walker Books, Dr. Miriam Stoppard's Family Health Guide for Dorling Kindersley, Gardener's World and Property Ladder Magazines among others.

More recently I have been working for schools providing editorial images for web and OFSTED evidence. These include Athelstan House School in Hampton Hill and Turnford School in Cheshunt.

I am experienced in Traditional photographic techniques including Black and White printing and old processes. Currently 90% of my work is digital. I regularly photograph events for Kingston University.

Last year, I diversified into video when I was asked to record Social Work graduates doing role play exercises as part of their degree.

My goals are to travel as much as possible whilst maintaining a successful photographic career. In Summer 2008 I completed a City and Guilds PTTLS course with the aim of teaching photography. I love working in gardens and am currently working on my horticultural portfolio. I am a regular contributor to www.alamy.com.

Trish Gant’s Specialties:

I have developed an informal, lively approach to working with models of all ages, especially children. Sought after for my relaxed style, I can turn my hand to difficult subjects such as family issues and health. I am comfortable shooting in studio or on location, using flash or available light, I have the expertise to get the job done to a high standard.
