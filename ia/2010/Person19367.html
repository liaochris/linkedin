Amitava 'Babi' Mitra’s Summary

GOAL
I know how higher education and corporate e-learning work here in the US and in India. I've successfully led organizations and entrepreneurial ventures at BITS, Pilani, NIIT, Inc., and MIT and I’m the ideal profile as US universities and education/publishing companies seek to expand, explore and enter India. I’m excited by what I see as growth opportunities that bridge the US and India and those that include the shaping of e-learning strategy.

PROFILE
Proven international leader with strong focus on bringing simpler user-propelled coherence in a complex ecosystem where there’s multiplicity. Active in professional organizations and boards promoting the development & dissemination of new/strategic uses of technology in teaching & learning.
> Participated as a founder-Board member, Sakai Project, a higher education community initiated by Michigan, Indiana, Stanford, and MIT to create a pre-integrated collection of modular open source tools such as learning management systems. Helped grow it from a four institution project into more than 100 institutions including delivery of early versions of a Collaborative Learning Environment platform. Set the terms of the debate in the higher education community concerning future infrastructure software choices.
> Transformed a small instructional design project team at NIIT into a profitable $24M Knowledge Solutions Business unit with over 600 professional staff, Fortune 500 clients in the US, Europe, Asia, and Australia, and a strong process-oriented development approach.
> Participated in the formulation of the India’s National Policy on Education and chosen to be first Head of Distance Learning Programs at the BITS, Pilani, India.
> Led or been business/strategy advisor on several projects at MIT including course evaluation and learning management systems. Moving from multiple paper and electronic versions into streamlined, centrally run online systems allowing improved data and analysis for reduced cost.

Amitava 'Babi' Mitra’s Specialties:

Higher education, corporate e-learning, US and India, institutional strategy and user-propelled implementation, distance education, e-learning, hybrid models, educational technology services, course/learning management systems and content development.

  • Associate Director, Office of Educational Innovation and Technology (OEIT)

    MASSACHUSETTS INSTITUTE OF TECHNOLOGY

    (Higher Education industry)

    2007 — Present (3 years )

    Office of the Dean for Undergraduate Education
    An educational innovation incubator that works with faculty, staff, and students at MIT to enable and promote the development and dissemination of innovative uses of technology in teaching and learning. http://oeit.mit.edu/

    Lead OEIT operations and administration, including HR, financial, space, communication, recruitment and budgeting matters, and departmental meetings.

    Strategic projects and initiatives:
    a) Online Subject Evaluation/Who’s Teaching What project (Business Advisor; strategy development, organizational and service alignment, benchmarking with peer institutions, technical vendor selection as core project team member) http://web.mit.edu/se-project
     Results: Online pilot implemented Spring 2008 with 22 subjects in four departments has grown to include 457 subjects in 27 departments during Fall 2009, from 4% of subjects evaluated to 61% over a period of four semesters. Accepted well by faculty, students, and staff. Minimal change in subject and faculty ratings; expected drop in student response rates during pilot being investigated; faculty identified this as an exemplar for developing constructive faculty-staff-student engagements.

    b) Textbook Information Provisioning (TIP) project (Project Owner/Leader beginning 9/09) https://wikis.mit.edu/confluence/display/tip/Home
     Results: In only six months this cross-departmental project has gathered input from and communicated to a spectrum of stakeholders including faculty/student committees, students, staff, senior leadership, individual faculty, and departmental administrators; achieved consensus on the technical solution, worked out collaborative procedures with the bookstore, and initiated development.

  • Academic Computing Coordination Group (ACCORD)

    MIT

    (Educational Institution; Education Management industry)

    2007 — Present (3 years )

    Academic Computing Coordination Group (ACCORD) (Core Member, Leading and stabilizing operations and Coordinator, Use Case Groups) http://web.mit.edu/accord.
    Scope: Ensure that all academic computing service providers (DUE, the Libraries and IS&T) work together in a cohesive and transparent manner to provide faculty and students with seamless and responsive service in the academic computing domain. Initiate and monitor processes designed to foster the continuous improvement.

  • Executive Director, Academic Media Production Services

    MIT

    (Educational Institution; 5001-10,000 employees; Higher Education industry)

    2002 — 2006 (4 years )

    Cost-recovery organization created to provide educational technology services in support of the Institute's strategic goals in learning systems platforms, media-enabled teaching and learning spaces, and production and delivery of rich media content http://web.mit.edu/amps/
    Transformed this 40 person amalgamation of previously existing entities into a professional service organization with strong brand identity.
    Led growth in usage of Stellar, MIT's home-grown course management system, from 17% of MIT's courses to over 67% when it was handed over to IS&T in 2006. Established it as a 24/7 infrastructure service in a scenario where usage by faculty and students was voluntary (http://stellar.mit.edu/). The Sloan School committed to moving all its courses from SloanSpace, its own LMS, to Stellar, as did other departments, allowing MIT to cost-effectively deliver this student educational service across the entire community.
    Led, conceived, and planned with colleagues from Academic Computing a benchmarking study, tracking total cost of ownership of their LMS. The first such cost comparison study in higher education, it created a framework for making these comparisons. Study was conducted by WCET, surveying ten peer universities. (http://wcet.info/services/publications/)
