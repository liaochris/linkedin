Mike Madura’s Summary

Has successfully completed searches for candidates at all levels in the Information Security, ERP, IT Strategy, BI, Outsourcing, M&A, Operations, Change Management, CFO Advisory, Trading, and Sales/Marketing verticals. Major networker with a firm belief in paying-it-forward networking. Previous experience includes recruiting in the Life Sciences space for Pharma (research, regulatory affairs) and Biotech (successfully completed a CEO retained search). Also previously recruited in the Hedge Fund space (software engineers, financial engineers).

Joined KPMG (1/2007) as a Manager of Recruiting for our Midwest Area Advisory Services practice. Assumed Lead Advisory recruiting role for the Midwest in 10/2007. In 10/2009 assumed national recruiting leadership for KPMG's P&T (Performance and Technology) Advisory Services Group.

Particular emphasis is on building relationships with and sourcing individuals for the following Advisory Service Networks and Sub-Services: Financial Management, Supply Chain, Operations Improvement, Business Intelligence (BI), Strategic Sourcing (ITO, BPO and Shared Services), People and Change (P&C), ERP (SAP & Oracle), IT Enablement, IT Strategy/Governance/Performance and Technical Infrastructure Operations (TIO).

The primary reason that I joined KPMG is due to our excellent employee value proposition, not only career-wise, but also with an emphasis on professional growth, work/life balance, corporate responsibility and diversity. I strongly believe that our Advisory Performance and Technology Service Group not only to allows you to provide high impact professional services to our client base but also allows you to enhance your "go to market" business development and selling skills.

Early adopter in the use of social networking sites with respect to usage in sourcing. Senior LinkedIn advisor and educator at KPMG.

Actively involved in KPMG's LGBT community and member of team that established KPMG's Ally program.

Beliefs:

1) the basic elements to a successful business relationship are respect and trust.....
2) listen carefully, since we may answer the question we were expecting to hear and not what the questioner actually expressed.....
3) before we start talking about any potential opportunties you need to clearly and completely express what is important to you.....
4) time kills all deals.....
5) job satisfaction (retention) is driven by 6 key variables - where many companies fall short is by using a cookie cutter approach to retention and not incorporating the fact that the relative importance of these variables is UNIQUE to each employee:
a) type of work being performed..
b) advancement opportunities..
c) availability of professional education..
d) sincerity and effectiveness of company communication (1-way vs. 2-way)..
e) how well the company promotes employee/family interaction both during and after work..
f) compensation/benefits..
