Gigi Rosenberg’s Summary

If you want to make presentations where the audience leans in, engaged and even entertained, I can teach you how.

Whether your audience is one client, a small group, or a packed auditorium, we start with your natural skills and build from there to create presentations delivered with clarity and ease.

With 20 years experience in writing, theater, and corporate communications, I specialize in coaching business people, educators, and artist entrepreneurs.

As a workshop leader, I teach the popular “Make a Great Presentation” and “Grant Writing for Success” nationwide. As a speaker, I offer the keynote “Don’t Go Solo” about how to enlist a team to launch and fund creative projects.

THE ARTIST’S GUIDE TO GRANT WRITING, my book for visual, literary, and performing artists, will be published by Random House in September 2010.

My personal essays and how-to articles have been published by Seal Press, Parenting, The Oregonian, and Writer’s Digest; I’ve performed my dramatic monologs at theaters in Seattle and Portland.

Clients have included Microsoft, Portland General Electric, Mercy Corps NW, Lewis and Clark College, Combined Jewish Philanthropies, Chicago’s Self Employment in the Arts, and individual artist entrepreneurs.

  • Speaker, Workshops & Coaching

    Gigi Rosenberg

    (Professional Training & Coaching industry)

    October 2005 — Present (4 years 7 months)

    Speaker:
    “Don’t Go Solo: How to Enlist a Village to Write and Sell a Book,” is my inspirational speech about how to use the power of teams to launch and promote your projects. In this talk, discover techniques to find the best team members, ask for the help you need, avoid pitfalls, and create a team in which everyone benefits. Learn by my example how teamwork can ignite your brilliant career.

    I also speak on “Strategic Fundraising for Artists” and “Dismantling Roadblocks to Success” based on chapters from my book. These speeches explore creative fundraising techniques test-driven by artists all over the country and strategies to help you ride through the inevitable rejection and jealousy of any creative pursuit.

    Workshops:
    As a workshop leader, I teach the popular “Make a Great Presentation” and “Grant Writing for Success.” These acclaimed hands-on workshops offer organizations the benefits of group learning with in-class demos.

    Coaching:
    In one-on-one working sessions, we rehearse and finesse your next presentation. Our goal? A presentation delivered with a clear call to action and a message that your audience remembers long after they walk out the door.

    You’ll learn to write for the spoken voice, communicate with images, and add appropriate drama. You’ll also discover techniques to foster your own sense of calm. For technical experts presenting to a lay audience, we work to create presentations in understandable language that doesn’t “dumb down” the concepts.

    More clients include Willamette Writers Conference, Seattle’s Richard Hugo House, and Washington’s BizArt Conference.
