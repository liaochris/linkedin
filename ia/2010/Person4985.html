Alana Brody’s Experience

  • VP, Social Media

    CancerConnect.com

    (Hospital & Health Care industry)

    January 2010 — Present (7 months)

    CancerConnect.com will launch in 2010 as a new social network for people living with or caring for someone with cancer. The site will allow people dealing with the disease to connect with others who share similar issues such as making treatment decisions, understanding clinical trials or paying for treatment. CancerConnect.com is member of OMNI Health Media which owns select oncology platforms including Women & Cancer Magazine www.womenandcancermag.com.

  • SVP, Strategic Development

    National Comprehensive Cancer Network

    (Non-Profit; Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    March 2000 — June 2009 (9 years 4 months)

    Led management and strategic implementation of NCCN brand integrity. Identified and created strong relationships with organizations to distribute NCCN content, predominately pharmaceutical and biotechnology companies. Managed 2 websites for clinical professionals and consumers, encompassing eCommerce, advertising, SEO, and content placement. Influential member of senior leadership team, providing CEO with a collaborative report on all aspects of revenue development.
    Supplied leadership and oversight to staff with one director and 5 managers. Directed on-line consumer and business communications, business development, continuing medical education, grants, marketing, creative services, and public relations. Reported to COO.
    • Launched NCCN from a relatively unknown medical association to become the international standard for quality care in oncology. Grew annual revenue from $6M (2000) to $30M+ (2009).
    • Created programming that enabled 21 leading cancer centers to participate collaboratively on a national basis while reducing per-participant cost: national cooperative advertising initiative on WebMD (15 members), monthly communications committee webcasts (21 members), NCCN pavilion at ASCO national conference (14 members), and Communispace on-line consumer market research project (8 members). The original Communispace community was so successful, two more communities were launched.
    • Positioned company for competitive edge by conducting on-line market research (Communispace) with 300+ new cancer patients and utilizing research to customize consumer-focused marketing on WebMD.
    • Broadened relationships with non-traditional media to extend the distribution of medical content. Solicited funding and partnered with Discovery Health CME (division of Discovery Health Channel) to produce made-for-TV programs for breast, colon, lung, and kidney cancers, viewed by 5+ million people and 4,000+ medical professionals for CME credits.

  • Director, Network Development

    National Spine Network

    (Hospital & Health Care industry)

    1995 — 2000 (5 years )

    Spearheaded all marketing activities for 27 Centers of Excellence via national network, including website, public relations, newsletters, brochures, special events, and contracting with payers such as Aetna and CNA.
    • Built coalitions with teams of physicians and administrators at 27 orthopedic practices, including Rothman Institute at Jefferson Hospital (Philadelphia), The Emory Spine Center (Atlanta) and Hospital for Special Surgery (New York City).
    • Presented NSN programs and enhanced relationships for NSN Centers with employers, insurers, and managed care organizations.
    • Launched Spine Surgery Evaluation Program with CNA Insurance’s client, Pepsico, and produced promotional video and referral operations manual. Efforts drew 150 new patients into network hospitals within first 6 months of the pilot program.

    • Contributed to JCAHO survey that resulted in accreditation with commendation.
