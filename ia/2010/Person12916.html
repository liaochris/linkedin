Angela Baumgartner’s Summary

26 community members attended my workshop "Entering the Writer's Market" - including writers with international book sales. I also taught and videotaped "Fine Art Marketing 101" for options of publishing as an e-book. In the last few months have attended an Artist Trust Workshop in Seattle, Writer's Resource Fair in Portland, Working Artists Network Round-table and an Oregon Writer's Colony workshop. Also attended an artist's reception at Clatsop Community College where I did a reading of my juried in essay "Good On A Bad Day" re: experience in Tent City as a documentary co-producer. Will be doing an art show this month in Vancouver, attending a new gallery opening on the Oregon Coast as one of the resident artists...and am dying to paint for a show in August.

  • Co-producer for Documentary

    Manifest Productions

    (Media Production industry)

    June 2003 — October 2003 (5 months)

    This was an amazing opportunity which opened up through a class I was taking at Bellevue Community College. The professor, who traveled the world and had worked with many highly respected corporations teaching about diversity, asked her students what project they thought should be covered by an independant film-maker looking for topics.

    My idea was picked up- addressing homelessness and it's many misconceptions. When the film-maker (Charles Newman, owner of Manifest Productions) picked up the idea and started the project, the professor came back to me requesting additional feed-back for the documentary. Within a few weeks, I was asked to be onboard as a consultant, and eventually as one of the three producers. I worked on written and oral content, timing and film editing, interviews and photography.

    What unfolded during the next several months was eye-opening. I was able to go into Tent City III outside of Seattle and conduct extensive interviews with those residing in the homeless community, along with interviewing police, religious leaders and other social service providers in the area. Some of the residents allowed me to photograph them, I shared meals and talked by the light of the fire barrell in the cool of the evening. Men and women opened up their hearts, shared their lives and struggles and left a lasting impression.

    PBS considered the documentary, but the final product was not able to be finished due to changes in the production company. Since then, I've written two articles about homelessness. The first was an assignment by Hipfish Magazine of 2,200 words for their cover article Dec. 2006. The second, more in an essay form, is currently under consideration by RAIN for it's 42nd annual publication (Feb. 2010)
