Chris Gore’s Summary

CHRIS GORE is a television personality and writer who has built a solid reputation as a hilariously outspoken voice in the film world. He was named one of the 25 Most Influential People in Independent Film by Film Festival Today magazine.

Chris has appeared as a television guest on MSNBC, E!, CNN, Reelz Channel and has also hosted shows on FX, Starz, IFC and is currently the film expert on G4TV's "Attack of the Show."

He has written freelance for The New York Times, The Hollywood Reporter, Variety, Video Business, Playboy.com, Total Movie, Details, and SuicideGirls.com.

Chris founded the brutally honest magazine Film Threat, which began as a fanzine he created as a college student in Detroit. As the 'zine evolved into a respected national magazine, he relocated to Los Angeles. The print magazine was retired in 1997 when it was re-launched as a web site. Chris remains the publisher of FilmThreat.com--the online audience consists of over 500,000 readers and was cited as one of the top five movie sites on the web by the Wall Street Journal.

Chris is an author having written "The 50 Greatest Movies Never Made" (St. Martin's Press) and "The Complete DVD Book" (Michael Wiese Publications). His book "The Ultimate Film Festival Survival Guide" is considered the bible of the industry and required reading for many college film programs. A 4th edition is due in Fall 2009 from Random House.

Chris is also a filmmaker having co-written and produced the feature comedy "My Big Fat Independent Movie" (Anchor Bay DVD) which played 40 film festivals and can be seen on Showtime and the Sundance Channel. Like most cult classics, the film is considered a failure according to unreliable sources online such as Wikipedia.

And while all of the above may sound impressive, the truth is that Chris considers himself a movie geek who was lucky enough to build a career as a "Nerdlebrity." He begrudgingly lives in Los Angeles but his home online can be found at ChrisGore.com.
