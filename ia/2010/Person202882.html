  • Executive Producer Consultant

    Chicago Blackhawks

    (Marketing and Advertising industry)

    September 2008 — June 2010 (1 year 10 months)

    As a consultant to the Chicago Blackhawks, I directed the in-game presentation at the United Center. Game night responsibilities included implementing all game presentation elements including: P.A. reads and announcements, on-ice dance team, national anthems, intermission entertainment, entertainment features during timeouts, music selections and organ cues and overseeing the video production that runs on the center-hung and LED ribbon boards.

In 2010, the Blackhawks won their first Stanley Cup since 1961. In all I directed over 90 regular season and 19 Blackhawks Playoff games helping to restore the Original Six team back to relevance in the sports scene.

  • Senior VP, Creative Director

    National Basketball Association

    (Privately Held; Sports industry)

    June 1990 — January 2003 (12 years 8 months)

    Hired by Commissioner Stern in 1990 as the NBA’s first Creative Director. Held full creative direction for League marketing, advertising and branding during the NBA’s global explosion era. In three years, built a 25-person in-house creative services team from the ground up; spearheaded long-range branding and marketing direction and oversaw execution of all NBA Special Event initiatives. Collaborated with NBA vice presidents and directors to organize and integrate branding efforts across national and regional marketing platforms to broaden fan base and elevate sponsorship value.

    • Creative direction for the 1992 USA Basketball Men’s “Dream Team” including all marketing, sponsorship and brand initiatives for the greatest basketball team ever assembled.

    • Orchestrated creation of 20 of the 30 NBA team re-branding identities, including the development of branded trademark and uniform designs, which ignited sales of licensed product and propelled revenues from $180 million in 1990 to $ 2.5 billion in 2002.

    • Brand architect for the launch of the WNBA, the Women’s National Basketball Association and naming and design of the original eight WNBA teams, the new Spalding ball design and the unique uniform designs for all its' teams.

    • Brand identity creation for the 1995 NBA Expansion Toronto Raptors and Vancouver Grizzlies, the NBA's first expansion teams since 1989.

    • Oversaw the launch of NBA.com in 1995. Originally a presence site, I managed to move the site into a major media component for the League during it’s merger with ESPN.com/ Starwave.com in 1996 and the implementation of all 29 NBA.com teams sites in 1997.

    • Was the creative director in charge of the NBA’s first retail destination, The NBA Store on 5th Avenue overseeing all internal packaging, store fixturing and in-store promotions.

    During my 12-year tenure, I directly influenced the vibrant and pervasive personality of the NBA in today’s popular culture.
