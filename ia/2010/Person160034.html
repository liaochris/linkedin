Patrick Neiler’s Summary

Take a German/Irish, throw him into a kettle with an Italian/Greek, stir it around a few times, pour it out and what you get is me - a fine American mess.

I feel like an American; like a revolutionary. My blood boils when I think about something that is flawed. I have a strong desire to change it; to improve it. I guess it’s my education in design. A design education forces one to criticize everything. There is nothing that I criticize more than the ethics of the prototypical American business man. I look at the business ethics of the American CEO and it becomes obvious to me why our economic system is broken. Purpose is core to ethics, and the majority of our leaders are lacking in it. They place their egos and personal accomplishments ahead of the contributions they make to their businesses and customers.

I am the destruction of the classical idea that a business exists to make money. A business exists first to help your customer and a monetary return is a residual of that accomplishment.

Right now, corporate culture is motivated by fear. Some sort of lingering primal subconscious survival instinct. Aligning yourself to a purpose allows you to release yourself from the fear of failure; fear and the idea of “risk” is core to what holds back progress and innovation. With purpose driven ethics, failure is not possible because there is no end to relate your success to. True purpose can not be completed in a lifetime; only virtuous acts that are aligned to the purpose. It is something that is not measurable because the end is infinite.
Purpose is devoting your life, not to creating a better business, but to “creating.” The concept of creativity transcends one mans accomplishments. The ego becomes futile. Fear only exists in the ego; and to quote the credo of my mentor and creative genius Alex Bogusky:

“Fear is the mortal enemy of creativity.”
