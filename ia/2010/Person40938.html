Choung-kyu RYU’s Summary

The expertise of the applicant who is shortlisted to the final three for the UNV Urban Specialist post with UN Habitat Vietnam can be divided into two main streams.

Firstly, it could be described as a socio-urban study covering aspects from physical urban environment development, including architectural design and residential complex development, to community development considering socially vulnerable classes such as social minorities and women within an urban context.

The other is a human rights perspective that has been obtained from various extracurricular experiences such as volunteering for Amnesty International G48, Meaningful Volunteers and Korean Gay Men's Human Rights Group, Chingusai.

I am seeking a field researcher position that involves grounding theory in the reality of utilizing creativity as a designer, inclusive comprehension on cultural diversity and flexible problem solving abilities coming from diverse working experiences and a multi-disciplinary background. I am especially interested in the African countries that continue conducting exhaustive civil wars under the strong influence of colonial policy of expansionism in 19th centrury, the life of people who are lack of basic living conditions and human rights and finally, sustainable ways to revitalize their life, community and the culture that was once the most flourished on the Earth.
