Sandra Cochrane’s Summary

Sandra Cochrane is a Technology Business Consultant for the MI-SBTDC. In this position she assists start-up and early-stage high-tech companies. Services she provides to companies include business plan development, accessing capital, accessing state programs, and general business consultation. She works with various pre-seed funds, angel groups, and venture capital firms to connect companies to capital. Cochrane’s SBTDC duties also allow her to provide support and advice to the many incubator programs in Michigan. From feasibility studies to financial modeling to program development to facility design, Cochrane helps existing and new incubator programs in Michigan create strong companies. Outside the state of Michigan, Cochrane’s consulting business, Verdandi Consulting LLC, allows her to work with incubation programs across the country.

Cochrane is the former COO and Acting CEO of the Southwest Michigan Innovation Center, a 68,600 square foot life science incubator in Kalamazoo, MI. Cochrane shepherded the Innovation Center through its start-up phase and is credited with the development, construction, and programming of the facility. During her seven year tenure, the Innovation Center incubated 23 companies, graduated three companies, and supported five corporate “skunk works” projects. In addition, Cochrane is viewed as an expert in the incubation industry and is a regular contributor to various publications including the National Business Incubation Association (NBIA) Review. She has been a session presenter at various NBIA conferences, has provided case study material for two NBIA books, and is currently on the board of directors of the NBIA and the Michigan Business Incubator Association. Her work has resulted in two NBIA awards: the 2006 Incubator Innovation Award and the 2008 Outstanding Incubator Graduate (Technology Company).
