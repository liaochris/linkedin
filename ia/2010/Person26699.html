Brad Lyerla’s Summary

Mr. Lyerla has been recognized for excellence in the practice of patent and IP litigation by numerous organizations including: Chambers & Partners, Who's Who, Martindale-Hubbel, Leading Lawyers, Super Lawyers and Chicago Lawyer Magazine, among others.

Brad Lyerla’s Specialties:

Trials and arbitrations of patent, trade secret, copyright and unfair competition lawsuits including antitrust lawsuits.

  • Partner

    (Law Practice industry)

    January 1998 — July 2000 (2 years 7 months)

    I founded this firm with Jim Ryndak when I left Jenner. We saw ourselves as a small elite IP firm dedicated to practicing law at the highest level of excellence. During my short time with Jim, I tried a few cases and argued some appeals. I benefitted greatly in my development as a patent trial lawyer during this period. There is nothing that will focus the mind more than working without a net and a trial lawyer in a four or five man firm has no net. It was a rewarding time professionally and personally. But I learned that I enjoy having the resources of a larger firm at my command.

But I left at a good time for me. Leaving opened some doors that led to some great opportunities. Since leaving, I have had a great run.

Brad Lyerla’s Education

  • University of Illinois College of Law

    JD , Law , 1977 — 1980

    I was lucky to have attended U of I College of Law. At the time that I chose the College of Law, my goals were not very ambitious. I had no specific vision of what my future would look like, except a vague notion that I might try to practice law in rural Illinois where my family is from. Illinois was the only lawschool that I applied to. But I was lucky. I did well. When I graduated, I was courted by the best firms and I escaped to the sophistication of the big city where I have thrived.

    Activities and Societies:
    I was a member and staff editor of the law review which was then known as the University of Illinois Law Forum. I was chosen for the law review based on my class rank. I also received some other honors.

  • University of Illinois

    BA , Philosophy , 1975 — 1976

    When I graduated, I was awarded the Donald W. Doerscher Memorial Prize which is awarded annually to the outstanding senior in the Philosophy Department. It was a nice surprise because I attended Illinois only three semesters. And because it included a cash prize, it was also very welcome.

    Activities and Societies:
    Phi Beta Kappa Honor Society; Phi Kappa Phi Honor Society; Doerscher Memorial Award

Brad Lyerla’s Groups:

From 1990 to 2005, I was editor in chief of INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY LITIGATION published by the ABA. Now, I am editor at large of that publication. In 1996-97, I was chair of the ISBA Intellectual Property Section. In 2007-09, I served on the Board of Managers of the Intellectual Property Law Association of Chicago. I had served as Chair of IPLAC's Litigation Committee from 2005-07 and I am currently chair of IPLAC's Antitrust Committee. I also am a member of the Federal Bar Association, the Seventh Circuit Bar Association, the Supreme Court Historical Society and the Federal Circuit Bar Association.

I was a contributing author to PATENT CLAIM CONSTRUCTION IN THE FEDERAL CIRCUIT, ed. E. Manzo in 2006, 2007 and 2010.
