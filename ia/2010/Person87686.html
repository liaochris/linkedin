Jamal "JJ" Johnson’s Summary

Mr. Johnson’s career in the entertainment industry spans nearly a decade holding the positions of Manager, Artist & Repertoire (A&R), Vice President of a top music production company and President of 11-16 Entertainment before reaching the age of 30. Johnson grew up in the city of Los Angeles, a fast-paced environment that permits the survival of only the fittest and he stayed focused on advancing in the entertainment industry. Mr. Johnson began his professional career in entertainment working in Production on the 1990’s highly successful face-to-face interview show, ESPN “Up Close” hosted by Gary Miller. Mr. Johnson then accepted a position as Director of Management and Production for Spencer/Cowings Entertainment, a multi-media company owned by the current Vice President of Universal Motown that services music, film, television and artist management. Within a year, he was promoted to Vice President and worked hand in hand with the company’s partners in signing and developing new talent, creating opportunities in film, television, publishing, and securing record deals for artists at major labels such as Warner Music Group, MCA, and Universal.Mr. Johnson currently serves as President/Founder of 11-16 Entertainment, an entertainment management and production company dedicated to the development and management of entertainment clientele as well as the production of top quality television and film projects. 11-16 Entertainment past and present clients include actress Elise Neal (Hustle & Flow, All of Us, K-Ville), D. Woods (actress/singer) of the hit MTV show Making The Band 3 and platinum music group Danity Kane, Mikalah Gordon (singer/actress) an American Idol Finalist and star of hit reality show Gone Country and Carmit Bachar, group member of Multi Platinum Pop music group The Pussycat Dolls.
