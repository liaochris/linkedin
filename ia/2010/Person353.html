Eric Tonui’s Summary

• Over ten years of international experience spanning several disciplines and industries.
• Involved in licensing of Universities of California technologies to industry and assisting in formation of entrepreneurial start-ups based on UC research inventions.
• Until recently, worked as a project manager, researcher and lecturer in space sciences at UCLA where I was involved in the management of an analytical science facility dedicated to analysis of primitive extraterrestrial materials.
• Prior to UCLA, worked as a research scientist at both the NASA Johnson Space Center in Houston and Jet Propulsion Laboratory at Pasadena, California. In both these roles, I was involved in design of new state-of-the-art instrument sensitive techniques for analysis of space sample returns.
• Previously worked in Australia as a research scientist for Geoscience Australia where I collaborated with mining exploration companies such as BHP and Rio Tinto Ltd.
• Co-founder and Program Director of e-learning management development initiative, a non-profit program dedicated to management training of HIV/AIDS organizations in Africa.

Eric Tonui’s Specialties:

Business development, technology licensing, mining exploration technologies, space and planetary sciences, environmental science, analytical chemistry and astrobiology
