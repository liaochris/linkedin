James Doulgeris’s Summary

Mr. Doulgeris has twenty-six years of executive management experience, primarily as president, CEO and director of for-profit, non-profit, public and private companies in the hospital, ancillary provider, medical device and health care services sectors. He co-founded Orthopedic Development Corporation (now MinSURG International, Inc.) and MinSurg Corporation, serving as its President, CEO and Director from 2003 until September of 2008. He managed its TruFUSE product from design to an internationally recognized minimally invasive solution for chronic back pain.

Mr. Doulgeris worked in high-level crisis and turnaround management capacities from 1991 through 2003 in a variety of industries with an emphasis on healthcare providers. In 1983, he co-founded and led National Comtel Systems, Inc. as its CEO until 1991, growing it to be the fifteenth largest hospital contract manager in the United States. He is also the co-author of the award winning “The Dyodyne Experiment,” a novel released nationally in June, 2009, the Senior Advisor to GemStone Securities, an investment banking firm and the founder and a director of the non-profit Tampa Bay Opera, Inc.
