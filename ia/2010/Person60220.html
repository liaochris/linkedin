Tony Robinson’s Summary

20 years in Cambridge University, teaching and running a research group, 15 years of founding and running speech recognition companies (SoftSound, Cantab Research and SpinVox Advanced Speech Group).

Tony Robinson’s Specialties:

Expert in all aspects of speech processing, with special interest in speech recognition and search algorithms.

Tony Robinson’s Experience

  • Director of the Advanced Speech Group

    SpinVox

    (Privately Held; 201-500 employees; Telecommunications industry)

    January 2008 — Present (2 years 2 months)

    I am currently (Feb08) setting up a new major speech group in Cambridge. The aim of the group is to increase automation at SpinVox and is primarily concerned with large vocabulary automatic speech recognition. There are a number of open posts - get in touch if interested.

  • Founder and Owner

    Cantab Research Ltd

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Computer Software industry)

    February 2006 — Present (4 years 1 month)

    Cantab Research exists to provide innovative algorithms and implementations to address immediate business needs. We can operate on a consultancy, contract research or joint venture basis as appropriate. With a background in hi-tech startups we can provide the necessary technology to realise a new business vision or enable mature business to enter new markets.

  • Founder and CEO/CTO

    SoftSound Ltd

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Computer Software industry)

    June 1995 — January 2006 (10 years 8 months)

    I founded SoftSound in 1995. The company persued several intesting directions, settling on the management of unstuctured information in video and voice streams. SoftSound received substantial investment from Autonomy in 2000 allowing it to achieve the goal of multimedia data mining in many languages. Under my direction SoftSound built the the fastest large vocabulary speech recognition system which is available in far more languages than any of our competitors. In 2006 SoftSound was merged with Autonomy and I left to run my own company again.
