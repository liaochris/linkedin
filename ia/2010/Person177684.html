Rosario Megna’s Summary

I have a Ph.D. in Physics and have developed a strong focus and research interests lately on Medical Imaging R&D applications. During the last two years my research activities are based on a Computer Aided Detection (CAD) for thoracic Computer Tomography (CT) analysis. By the Root CERN framework, I’m studying the classification and selection of true positives and false positives in CT thoracic scans. For the classification I use feature extraction and pattern recognition developed in C++ software tool. Together with the “RG-MAGIC-CAD” team, I took part to the international competition ANODE09. Our CAD ranked 4th (http://anode09.isi.uu.nl/ Results, Details).

My background is matter-radiation interaction study, by Monte Carlo simulations and data analysis. In fact my graduate thesis was on gamma photons atmosphere interaction and my Ph.D. thesis was on neutrinos matter interaction.

During a fellowship my research activity was on performances study of gas detectors (Resistive Plate Counters), passed through charge particles. Therefore I master the particle physics measurement techniques.

Rosario Megna’s Specialties:

Medical imaging on CT scans. Pattern recognition: features extraction, artificial neural network, support vector machine. Software tools: C++ and Fortran. Frameworks: Root CERN, Matlab, Mathematica. Monte Carlo simulation. Data analysis. Matter-radiation interaction and neutrino-matter interaction. Astroparticle physics. Particle physics measurement techniques.
