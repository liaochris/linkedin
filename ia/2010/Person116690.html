Kimberly Klein’s Summary

As a program coordinator / advisor, Kimberly works with students from admission to graduation. She meets with students one on one, in groups, and through online Desire to Learn sessions, assisting them in academic, career, graduation and transfer planning, as well as helping students navigate the higher education system. Kimberly uses her student development and counseling background to design and implement programming and an AMS curriculum to assist Academy students in developing the skills they need to be successful at Normandale Community College and beyond.

Prior to starting her position at Normandale, Kimberly was an Academic Advising Intern in the TRiO/Student Support Services office at North Hennepin Community College (2007-2008). She also served as an Academic Advisor/Graduate Assistant at St. Cloud State University (2006-2008).

Kimberly is actively involved in the student affairs profession. She a member of several professional organizations, and is currently the Delegate at Large for Community Colleges on the Minnesota College Personnel Association (MCPA) board of directors. Kimberly also serves on the Minnesota's Future Doctors Advisory Committee.

Kimberly has presented at the St. Cloud State Leadership Conference (2008), Careers in Students Affairs Workshop (2009, 2010), MCPA 2009 Fall Conference and was a facilitator/presenter at the November 2009 MnSCU Student Leadership Retreat.

Demonstrating her commitment to community involvement, Kimberly has volunteered at a Minneapolis library and elementary school through the MN Literacy Council (2006) and is a former volunteer with Big Brothers/Big Sisters. While interning at Chrysalis (2005-2006), Kimberly served in many roles including advocate, mentor, and group co-leader. After the internship, she continued volunteering at Chrysalis, teaching community education classes. Kimberly is the recipient of the 2008 St. Cloud State University Excellence in Leadership award.
