Rick Shelton’s Summary

Moved to Las Vegas in 1986 from Utah, where I was involved in a family business for 6 years. During those years, I was a pilot for the search and rescue Civil Air Patrol. I also owned and operated a children’s martial art’s school. While very rewarding and peaceful, I decided in March of 1986 to move to Las Vegas and expand my horizons.

Upon arriving in Las Vegas, I accepted a position as finance director for a large local automotive group. In 1991, I decided to make a career change, attended Real Estate School in the evenings, received my license and was offered a position with Richmond American Homes.

Over the following 4 years, I also had the pleasure of working with Pulte Homes, a large development builder new to Las Vegas. In 1995, I decided to shift to general real estate. With partner, Tom Isabel, we opened “New Home Specialists,” a buyers’ brokerage firm that dealt strictly with new home buyers.

Two years later, in 1998, I merged with Tracy Ruck to open “New Homes of Nevada.” Tracy and I operated the delightful and successful little company, again specializing in new home sales until April 2000. Because of such great success with “New Homes of Nevada,” we decided to establish a higher profile real estate company - Re/Max Associates.
