Raghu Rao’s Summary

Raghu's business mission is to provide innovative solutions that convert data into actionable knowledge.

Raghu has over 20 years experience in Information Technology program strategy, architecture and design, development, outsourcing, marketing and sales. His technology expertise encompasses knowledge management, database and object technology, enterprise portals and ecommerce. Raghu has successfully managed and implemented complex projects on varied platforms for clients in Government, Publishing, Aerospace, Utilities, Chemicals, Legal, Healthcare and Telecommunications industries. Raghu has advised clients on the strategy and rollout of high-profile projects, including the Federal Internet Portal USA.gov (firstgov.gov), National Cancer Institute's cancer.gov and other eGovernment initiatives of the President's Management Agenda.

Raghu has an MS in Computer Science from Virginia Tech, a B.Tech in Electrical Engineering from The Indian Institute of Technology, Madras, and has completed coursework for an MBA in Information Systems at George Washington University. Raghu is on the Board of Advisors of eLawForum.

Raghu founded eSeva in 2008 and co-founded WizNucleus in 2009. Raghu founded InfoZen in 1995. InfoZen has emerged as a leading provider of integrated information management and technology solutions to commercial and government clients. Prior to founding InfoZen, Raghu was a program manager for electronic journals and a member of the R&D group at Reed Elsevier's Technology Information Services, where he helped develop several pioneering databases delivered on CD-ROM, electronic journals and encyclopaedias. Before joining Reed, Raghu was on the technical staff for Online Computer Systems in Germantown, Maryland where he worked on industry leading bibliographic information systems for libraries worldwide including Library of Congress, National libraries of Britain, Canada, Germany, France and Russia.
