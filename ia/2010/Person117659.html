Kevin Hill’s Experience

  • Industrial Designer

    TAM London

    (Design industry)

    July 2008 — Present (1 year 10 months)

    TAM London is an Interior, Graphic and Industrial Design company that currently specializes in interior design and branding of the commercial sector.

    We are a small studio with a Global Client base and regularly work across 3 timezones to complete projects in super quick times while retaining our ability to tell a story about the spaces we create.

    Our current clients are Largely in the Luxury Cosmetic and beauty sector and regular projects range POS and on-shelf/in window displays, to full blown interior branding, including graphics, interior design concept and implementation and bespoke furnishings and fixtures.

    We've helped launch whole new brand strategies with interior and graphic concept and implementation and have re-branded interior concepts for our largest clients, but one thing we have realized more then most is that no matter how small or large the project is, we love to keep our client relationships personal as their projects are as important to us as they are them, and we would never let our clients down.

    In our team you'll most likely meet me:
    - In briefings
    - Leading a brainstorming session
    - Working on and presenting concepts
    - Taking you through the client journey, including discussing feedback and directions for the project
    - Breaking the concepts down into elements, technical drawings for tendering and documents for articulating ideas
    - Comparing costs and value engineering and sourcing manufacturers
    - Project managing budgets and timings to completion
    - Liaising with Architects and Engineers where needed
    - On site (with hard hat of course) making sure of quality control and concept consistency
    - Following up with snagging after completion

    and if you came into our studio you may find me:
    - Briefing China on changes to 3D renders
    - Drawing on the walls of the toilet (don't worry, we painted it in chalk board paint for fun!)
    - Reading magazines and scrolling through blogs
    - scribbling down ideas

  • Event Staff/Supervisor/Junior Manager

    Tate

    (Non-Profit; Museums and Institutions industry)

    January 2003 — November 2005 (2 years 11 months)

    Tate Entertaining was a great view into the world of service with a smile. In the almost 3 years that I worked there I went from casual Bar Staff and tray holder to Event supervisor and finally becoming a Junior Event Manager, full time, before returning to University to complete my degree.

    Tate Entertaining was an incredibly dynamic team that had to pull together on events ranging from tea and coffee for the 4 heads of Tate to a drinks reception for 100 or dinner for 500 sitting for a meal and Televised performances in the Turbine hall of Tate Modern.

    It really was a fantastic opportunity to learn how to deliver a multi-faceted and complicated service, completed on time and to the highest standard possible.
