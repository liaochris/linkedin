Marty Kaszubowski’s Summary

Marty Kaszubowski has over twenty-five years of experience with a variety of public- and private-sector organizations as a technologist, strategist, policy analyst, consultant, manager, and leader. He is currently President of General Ideas, whose customers have included NASA and several technology-based companies in the aerospace and defense industry, specializing in technology assessment, forecasting, licensing, commercialization, and strategic planning.

Marty was the first Director of the Hampton Roads Technology Incubator (HRTI) which he started in October of 1998, and built from start-up to maturity. At HRTI, Marty was instrumental in the establishment and growth of over 25 start-up technology ventures, doing business in a wide variety of industries. He provided the HRTI, and its parent organization, the Hampton Roads Technology Council (HRTC), with visionary leadership and effective execution of strategic planning, project management, marketing, public relations, financial management, event-planning, and fund raising.

In the early 1990s, Marty spent two years in Washington, DC, as a senior staff member of the Aeronautics and Space Engineering Board (ASEB) of the National Research Council, the "working arm" of the National Academy of Sciences. At the NRC, Marty was responsible for the development and execution of several important technology policy initiatives related to the US aeronautics industry, the International Space Station, and Space Shuttle software safety issues.

Marty is a well-known and effective advocate on issues related to technology-based economic development and entrepreneurship at the local, state, and national level. He is currently a member of the Virginia Chamber of Commerce’s recently reconstituted Small Business Committee, where he has worked to promote programs that support technology-based, high-growth ventures.

    November 2008 — Present (2 years )

    Engedi Technologies has a growing suite of technologies aimed at simplifying secure access to remote appliances and devices within a network, and has developed patented technologies that protect data and networks from malicious, careless, or inexperienced insiders by assuring that no potentially compromising action is taken without authorization from two or more cognizant parties.

    Engedi Technologies offers two product lines that help address important, but underappreciated, aspects of the network security problem. The first, Secure Remote Management (SRM), helps network administrators securely manage remotely deployed network devices, providing a fully secure back-up communication path, centralized authorization for more efficient control, and supporting all protocols. Engedi's SRM technology can be delivered in an appliance or licensed from Engedi for embedding into another product.

    The second, Multi-Party Authorization (MPA), adds an additional level of protection to stores of sensitive data and critical network activities by requiring a second (or third, or more, if necessary!) authorized entity to review a desired action before it takes place, and permits that second party authorizer to either allow or deny the requested action.

    Engedi’s customers recognize that threats come from a range of sources, not always external and malicious, and that some actions are so critical no individual should be allowed to execute them without concurrence from one or more independent professionals.

    For more information, see www.Engedi.net
