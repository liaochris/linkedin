Phil Lindeman’s Summary

For the last ten years I've held three different design leadership positions at Motorola, Whirlpool and 3Com. Most recently I’ve founded Return_On_Design, a network-based product development consultancy that smartly scales premium design services to meet the needs of cost-conscious companies. By leveraging twenty years of experience as [1] an award-winning industrial designer, and [2] a design strategist collaborating with a wide variety of ODM/ OEM suppliers and high-profile design consultancies, Return_On_Design is able to deliver a rich mix of design services to their clients at an exceptional value.

Specializing in developing brand-building design strategies and product innovation, R_on_D is just as passionate about the seeing the vision through to fruition – achieving market success.

Equally adept at working with internal engineering, or contract manufacturing teams, Return_On_Design excels at delivering optimized product design [for the user and manufacturer] in an ODM/ OEM environment. In particular, I am well versed at investigating an OEM’s technical and manufacturing capabilities, uncovering design opportunities that align to project goals, and turning this insight into innovative product concepts and design strategies - essentially filling the “creative gap” that typically comes with OEM product development.

Prior to forming R_on_D, I served as Mobile Devices Design Strategist with Motorola, where I directed the design strategy, vision and planning of all Mass Market products. At the project level, I collaborated with a wide variety of internal teams, and external partners to embrace the challenge of delivering compelling product experiences – both physical and digital – within the tight cost structure of the mass market.

  • Principal Industrial Designer

    (Telecommunications industry)

    2001 — 2005 (4 years )

    From July 2001 to August 2005, I was responsible for the industrial design and usability of 7 to 9 “Mega” projects – approximately $100M in capital per year. Worked collaboratively with Whirlpool’s internal global engineering and domestic marketing teams to define and develop new product platforms that balance user needs, business goals and design integrity. In 2003, I drove a 2% market share increase Fabric Care design group - 1.33B revenue part of the business, representing $193 in earnings.

    As a design thought leader in the studio, I had a dual designer/ manager role where I mentored and directed the activity of Whirlpool brand’s fabric care design team, as well as designed my own products as an individual contributor. Collectively we delivered three new platforms in context of WHR’s VBL [design language] strategy that won numerous design awards including IDEA Silver, Good Design, Sparks and more.

  • Lead Industrial Designer

    Motorola

    (Public Company; MOT; Telecommunications industry)

    August 1988 — November 2000 (12 years 4 months)

    I started at Motorola in August of 1988 fresh out of college. Within a year, I was given responsibility for the industrial design of Radius brand of value two-way radios. Participated in the explosive growth of the cellular industry from the beginning and experienced first-hand [on both the winning and losing side] the power of design to capture a market. Over the next ten years steadily gained greater responsibility, eventually leading Motorola’s leadership category and ultimately designing marquee products such as Motorola’s first metal flip phone - the V60.
