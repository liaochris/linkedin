Jack Kennedy’s Summary

Jack was the primary political and legal organizer for the Virginia Spaceflight Liability and Immunity Act passed by the Virginia General Assembly and signed into law by Governor Tim Kaine in the spring of 2007 and patroned by Del. Terry Kilgore. The first of its kind in the nation, the new law is designed to boost the utilization of the FAA/AST licensed commercial Mid-Atlantic Regional Spaceport [MARS] by mitigating insurance costs.

In 2008, Jack successfully organized passage of a favorable Virginia 'ZeroG, ZeroTax' law with State Senator William C. Wampler, Jr. so as to encourage commercial space launch firms to locate in Virginia and utilize launch facilities at the Mid-Atlantic Regional Spaceport.

Jack serves as a member of the Virginia Commercial Space Flight Authority (VCSFA) appointed by the state governor in July 2008 and now serving on the excutive committee of the authority. His first term on the VCSFA expires in mid-2011. One of Jack's unique goals is to foster greater opportunities for commercial space launch development from Virginia's Wallops Island in Accomack County.

On the other hand, Jack works to enhance STEM education opportunities in Virginia's coal-producing counties and to enable a workforce opportunities in new and highly-advanced energy technologies at Wise County's fledgling energy lab and leading the advancement of court information technologies as Clerk of Court.

Jack Kennedy’s Specialties:

Organizational management, space policy and law, state-level legislative affairs, state and local government, judicial administration, and information and energy technology applications.

Jack has traveled to many countries in Asia and Europe including but not limited to: China, Russia, Belarus, Ukraine, Egypt, Jordon, Finland, France, United Kingdom, Italy, Holland, Belgium, Jamica, Grenada, Trinidad, Bermuda, Barbados, Germany, the Czech Republic, Mexico and others for a global outlook.
