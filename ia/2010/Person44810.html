Ted Cruz’s Experience

  • Former Candidate for Attorney General of Texas

    Texans for Ted Cruz

    (Non-Profit; Political Organization industry)

    January 2009 — Present (1 year 11 months)

    Ted was a a candidate for Texas Attorney General from January - December 2009, until Senator Kay Bailey Hutchison changed her mind and decided not to resign from the Senate, which caused the dominoes not to fall and the Attorney General position not become vacant.

    Over the course of the campaign, Ted gained endorsements from virtually every top conservative political leader across Texas and many nationally. He also achieved substantial fundraising success, raising more than $1.3 million from nearly 1,000 donors in over 100 Texas cities and 25 States.

    Please come check out our website: www.tedcruz.org

  • Partner

    Morgan, Lewis & Bockius LLP

    (Partnership; Law Practice industry)

    May 2008 — Present (2 years 7 months)

    Ted leads the firm's U.S. Supreme Court and Appellate Litigation practice. He has authored more than 80 U.S. Supreme Court briefs and presented 37 oral arguments, including eight before the U.S. Supreme Court.

    Prior to joining Morgan Lewis, Ted served as the Solicitor General of Texas from 2003 until May 2008. Ted was the first Hispanic Solicitor General in Texas and, when appointed, was the youngest Solicitor General in the United States.

    Before the U.S. Supreme Court, Ted has won landmark decisions successfully defending the Texas Ten Commandments monument, the Pledge of Allegiance, the Second Amendment right to keep and bear arms, the Texas congressional redistricting plan, and U.S. sovereignty from attempts by the World Court to bind the U.S. justice system.

  • Solicitor General of Texas

    Office of the Attorney General

    (Law Practice industry)

    January 2003 — May 2008 (5 years 5 months)

    Ted served as the chief appellate lawyer for the State of Texas, leading a team of 15 appellate attorneys in the Office of the Solicitor General and supervising every appeal, civil and criminal, in state and federal court, on behalf of the State, its agencies, and its officials.

    Clients included the State of Texas, the Governor, the Lieutenant Governor, the Speaker of the House, the Attorney General, the Comptroller, the Secretary of State, the Health and Human Services Commission, the Department of Criminal Justice, the Department of Public Safety, the Department of Transportation, the University of Texas, Texas A&M, and the University of Houston. In addition, he was the longest-serving senior legal advisor to Texas Attorney General Greg Abbott, and was charged with personally litigating those cases raising the most significant and challenging legal issues for the State.
