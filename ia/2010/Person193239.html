  • Director

    Center of Innovation for Life Science for the State of Georgia

    (Biotechnology industry)

    August 2008 — Present (1 year 7 months)

    The Georgia Centers of Innovation (COI) is an official part of the Georgia Department of Economic Development. It is a program designed to accelerate the growth of technology-based companies. Formed in 2003 by Governor Sonny Perdue, the COI network is comprised of six centers: agriculture, aerospace, energy, life sciences, logistics and manufacturing. Each center provides services to its industry sector which include: access to university-level research and development, product commercialization assistance, industry-specific business counsel, matching research grants for qualified companies, significant networking opportunities and connecting clients to potential investor networks. COI is affiliated with the OneGeorgia Authority and the University System of Georgia, including research universities and technical colleges. The COI – Life Sciences is located at the Medical College of Georgia and is partners with its nationally recognized business incubator.
