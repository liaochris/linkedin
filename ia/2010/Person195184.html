Steve Kirsch’s Summary

Serial entrepreneur and philanthropist. I've started 5 companies: Mouse Systems, Frame Technology, Infoseek, Propel Software, and Abaca Technology.

Abaca is attacking the spam problem. After many years of thinking through the problem and trying different ideas, I stumbled across receiver reputation as the ultimate approach to fighting spam. It has achieved over 99.99% accuracy in real-world use with millions of users because it is based on some pretty fundamental mathematical probabilities that a spammer cannot avoid, namely, the average spam % of people hit by spammers is greater than the average spam % of people who receive mail from non-spammers. Once the system is bootstrapped (with humans correcting errors), the system gets smarter on it's own. Human feedback is used to disambiguate truly gray senders. It's the ultimate, holy grail spam filter.

In my spare time, I helped to rid the world of junk faxers through lawsuits and maintaining www.junkfax.org. Over half of the junk faxes sent in America have been eliminated due to my efforts.

My charitable foundation can be found at:
www.kirschfoundation.org

My favorite book is Jim Hansen's Storms of my Grandchildren. He only asks Congress to do two simple things to stop climate change: build a fast nuclear reactor and use fee-and-dividend to put a price on carbon. They've done neither.
