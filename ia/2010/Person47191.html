Daniel Garrie, Esq.’s Summary

Mr. Garrie is a nationally recognized attorney and expert in the fields of computer forensics, electronic discovery (e-discovery), and information governance. Mr. Garrie regularly consults with companies and law firms on e-discovery, computer forensic and discovery management issues related to litigation, intellectual property, commercial disputes and business claims. He has experience with a wide array of industries, including government, financial services, consumer products, telecommunications, manufacturing, healthcare, and accounting.

Mr. Garrie also serves as a court appointed neutral, discovery referee, and special master hearing disputes that involve the convergence of law and technology throughout the United States, including Colburn v. Dyne, a dispute that involved a dozen plus law firms and a renowned merchant bank that had invested in 50+ technology companies, including Skype. Over the past fifteen years, he has completed a variety of key legal and technology roles, including: founding, managing and selling a legal strategy consulting firm (2007) and Search Engine Company (2000); leading substantial global Internet and software initiatives; and educating legal and technology professionals on legal issues pertaining to various emerging technologies, such as, VoIP, Social Networking platforms, Electronic Case Management systems, E-mail and Archiving solutions, and Enterprise Instant Messaging solutions.

Mr. Garrie is admitted to practice law in New York and New Jersey. Mr. Garrie is the founder and editor-in-chief of the Journal of Legal Technology Risk Management. Mr. Garrie was recognized as a Top Neutral “Rising Star” by the Los Angeles Daily Journal in 2009. He has published more than 60+ articles in scholarly and industry legal journals worldwide, and his writings are widely cited in legal and technology publications.
