David Kasprzak’s Summary

After over ten years of professional experience that frequently left me wondering, "Why are we doing that?" or "How can we do this better?" I decided to return to school and begin working towards my MBA.

Along the way, I have discovered that there is a basic, fundamental connection between the working environment and the success of the business. Through reading, research, and my own observations one thing becomes clear: workplace culture is vital to success.

I have spent several years working with managers to define, develop and analyze the performance of their teams and organizations. Unfortunately, in all this data, there was something missing: how to make things better for the organization, without making things worse. for its people.

I am convinced of the need for change in organizations. Our approaches to management and the managed, to creating sustained advantage, and to understanding the dynamics by which high-perfroming teams can be built, must change. Understanding when change is needed and how it should be conducted requires several things: It requires a vision, a strategy, and resources. Understanding how the strategy, and the resources used to implement it, interact is vital to success.

Strategy development is about change: Changing the way in which you perceive what is transpiring within, and without, your organization. Strategy execution is also about change: Changing the way in which roles are defined and the purpose of the organization is understood.

I am currently exploring opportunities to work alongside others who share these beliefs, so that I may apply what I have learned through experience and observation, and continue to develop my own understanding of the dynamics of change.

If you are looking to grow your consulting practice, would like to connect, or simply have some good advice - feel free to connect with me. We should be talking and finding ways to help each other.
