Daniel O'Neil’s Summary

The day after the earthquake rocked Haiti, I drove across the border and began spearheading PADF's response effort. After the Haiti Country Representative left, I took over his role as well. For the last two months, I have worked non-stop to accelerate all of PADF's programs while managing our relief activities, helping develop new proposals, and starting new projects.

Now I am ready to return to the United States or Canada. I am looking for a new challenge.

I am a strong leader and believe that uniting the organization and its supporters behind a compelling, remarkable vision is critical to changing the world. I was able to dramatically improve staff moral in Haiti following the earthquake by providing this strong leadership and clear vision.

I am an effective communicator whether through presentations to individual donors or large groups. I am good at developing proposals and blogging.

I am a hands-on manager who believes in building the capacity of his team through a combination of effective delegation and executive coaching.

I am a careful steward of my organizations resources. I keep a careful eye on my organizations finances and administration and maintain accurate budget projections.

I am very fond of business management books especially those of Seth Godin, Marshall Goldsmith, Tom Peters, and Peter Drucker and encourage my staff to read them as well.
