Jack Williams’s Summary

Weather writer and editor

When the USA TODAY newspaper began publication on Sept. 15, 1982, Jack Williams was weather editor. He also became weather editor of USATODAY.com when the online service started in 1995. He retired from USA TODAY in 2005.

Williams went to USA TODAY with two decades of experience as a newspaper reporter and copy editor.

While working for the USA TODAY newspaper and Web site Williams become skilled at visualizing how to communicate scientific concepts with images and a few words, and working with artists to realize his vision.

The talents Williams developed as USA TODAY weather editor served him well not only with his USA TODAY readers but also with a wider audience with his six books.

Williams developed his expertise in polar science beginning in 1997 when the National Science Foundation selected him to travel to Greenland to report from a research camp on the Ice Sheet. In 1999 he reported from Antarctica. where he became a pioneer blogger from the South Pole and other parts of Antarctica with his daily “trip diary” reports for USATODAY.com. Between 1999 and 2004 Williams made three more reporting trips to Greenland’s Ice Sheet, and a research icebreaker sailing the Arctic Ocean.

After retiring from USA TODAY in 2005 Williams became director of public outreach for the American Meteorological Society, where is major task was writing "The AMS Weather Book."
