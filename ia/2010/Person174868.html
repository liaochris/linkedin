    (Privately Held; Public Relations and Communications industry)

    2007 — 2009 (2 years )

    As SVP of Taylor’s Digital and Emerging Media Group, I was responsible for the formal introduction of a new discipline to an award-winning mid-sized agency. I assembled and direct a team of specialists while serving as educator, thought-leader, author/defender of policies and best practices and trendcaster for the firm and its clients.

    After auditing the agency’s “non-traditional” capabilities, I was able to identify a set of “Digital Natives” and roll-out an internal learning program that focused on the changing media landscape and our roles as storytellers. By September of 2008, my team had fully integrated and assumed day-to-day account responsibilities. Our product suite had also doubled in size.

    Based on assignments we received from brands like Alltel Wireless, Diageo, MasterCard, Microsoft Xbox 360, BRP International, Staples, Kimberly-Clark and Coca-Cola, the Digital and Emerging Media Group easily exceeded the 2008 revenue goals set forth by management.

As a proponent of leadership by example, I maintained the team’s internal blog, distributed a daily e-newsletter and explored emerging platforms and strategic alliances while offering daily support to the agency’s five practice areas across the globe. It was here, through hands-on experience, that I was able to hone my Social Media skills while conceiving, planning and executing multi-screen programs for clients like General Motors, Pfizer, InterContinental Hotels Group and Milk PEP (also known as got milk?). As the team continued to evolve, I became active external marketing activities and a frequent speaker at industry events.
