Dennis Milligan’s Summary

34 years in the broadcast news business, starting in radio at KCBQ in San Diego as a 17 year old intern. Graduated to television in 1981 at KYEL in Yuma, AZ as a reporter and anchor. Then off to Bakersfield, Fresno and San Francisco in supervisory, off-air roles. We moved east and south in 1999 to take a News Director's job in Charleston, SC (one of the finest places on earth.) I'm in my eighth year as News Director at WBTV, the CBS affilliate in Charlotte, NC (one of the finest large cities in America).

Despite those who'd like to think that television news has seen its day come and go, I think we're entering another "golden age". The key is new media. We have more fun tools at our disposal than ever before. If you're like me, and got into this business because you love to communicate with people, what better place is there in the world than a television station with a committment to news and new media? The challenge is to tap into your own sense of discovery and that of others. There is much we can do that we haven't even thought of yet. Let's go have some fun! (Just like the old days.)
