Roland Gauthier’s Summary

Roland Gauthier provides a wealth of experience both as a supervisor and a solutions-oriented production artist within the commercial and film industries. With over ten years of VFX experience, he has worked on prominent brands including Nike, Target, and Microsoft.

After earning a B.A. from Vassar and a B.A.S. degree in Animation from Expression College for Digital Arts, Gauthier worked as a freelance artist on independent film and video game projects including Batman: Dark Tomorrow. Joining the ranks of Will Vinton Studios, now Laika/house, he began as a Character Technical Director. He quickly advanced to Senior Technical Director, working on commercials for such brands as M&Ms, Honda, Wrigley’s, Cheerios, and Cox Communications.

Demonstrating the ability to grow the department’s artistic and technical capabilities as well as improve overall efficiency, Gauthier was promoted to CG Department Manager of Laika/house where he oversaw a crew of twenty regular and project staff, managed departmental budgeting and resource allocation, and directed the development of new tools, techniques and internal projects. In collaboration with in-house producers and directors, he developed bids and helped determine the scope of commercial projects.

Transitioning to Laika Entertainment, Gauthier additionally served as a Sequence Supervisor and Rigger on the award-winning CG-animated short film, Moongirl, directed by Henry Selick, and modeled the lead character of “Jack” from the CG feature Jack and Ben.

Not only does he offer his talents to commercial and film productions, but Gauthier also strives to share his knowledge with the community as adjunct faculty at the Art Institute of Portland.

A founder of Hinge Digital, Gauthier serves as the company’s Executive Producer, focusing his talents on business development, budget maintenance and creative direction. Additionally, he stays current with tools and techniques through regular involvement in hands-on production.
