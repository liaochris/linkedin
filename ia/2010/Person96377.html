Jerome Offord Jr’s Summary

Jerome Offord, Jr. joined the Online Computer Library Center, Inc. (OCLC) in 2008 as the Manager of Corporate Inclusion. As the Manager of Corporate Inclusion, he is responsible for building and developing external relationships and strategies to attract diverse talent to OCLC, and continuing to drive an internal culture that welcomes diversity as a critical contributor to the organization’s effectiveness. The Manager of Corporate Inclusion also serves as the Chair of the OCLC President’s Inclusion Council. The twenty-two (22) members of the OCLC President’s Inclusion Council are charged with serving as organizational ambassadors, helping with program implementation, managing programs, and aiding in the creation and fostering of a diverse and inclusive workforce. Jerome is also responsible for managing the OCLC Minority
Librarian Fellowship Program.

Prior to OCLC, Jerome was the Director of Diversity Initiatives at the Association of Research Libraries from 2003 to 2008. As the Director of Diversity Initiatives for the ARL, Mr. Offord managed a suite of programs including the Initaitive to
Recruit a Diverse Workforce (a scholarship program); the Leadership and Career Development Program (a training program for mid-career librarians of Color); and the ARL Career Enhancement Program (a internship program for MLS
graduate students of Color).

Jerome is currently enrolled at Simmons College in Boston, MA, where he is working on his PhD in Managerial Leadership in the LIS profession. Jerome has earned a Masters of Science in Library Science from Catholic University; a Masters of Science in Student Affairs in Higher Education from Colorado State University ; and, a Bachelors of Science in Agriculture from Lincoln University of Missouri.

Jerome has extensive nonprofit management and grant
writing experience. He has served in various leadership roles within his church, fraternity, and other community groups.

Jerome Offord Jr’s Specialties:

Jerome is currently serving as the National Archivist for Alpha Phi Alpha Fraternity, Inc. (the “Fraternity”). The Fraternity, founded in 1906 at Cornell University, is the world’s oldest
intercollegiate fraternity founded by the African American men.

Jerome is a Life Member of the Black Caucus of the American Library Association, Alpha Phi Alpha Fraternity, Inc., and the Lincoln University of Missouri National Alumni
Association.
