Brian Model’s Summary

Early Growth Stage Venture Capitalist focused predominantly on high potential technology and business services companies based in the Southeast, Northeast, and Texas, particularly in areas where an abundance of investment opportunities are underserved by a relatively small number of local growth equity investors.

I currently manage a portfolio of eight active companies based in the greater New York area and am active as a Board Member or Board Observer on four of them.

Brian Model’s Experience

  • Board Member

    UVANY - Upstate Venture Association of New York

    (Venture Capital & Private Equity industry)

    2009 — Present (1 year )

    The Upstate Venture Association of New York is a statewide organization formed to advance venture capital and private equity investments throughout upstate New York. Members and participants include seed capital, venture capital and private equity sources, as well as many nonresident-financing sources interested in making upstate investments. Additional members and participants include legal and accounting firms, banks, and economic and industrial development agencies.

  • Board Member

    Lorica Solutions

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Information Technology and Services industry)

    March 2007 — Present (3 years 3 months)

    Lorica Solutions, based in Buffalo, NY, is a managed services technology company serving the hospitality industry. Over the last three years, working with groups like Hotel Technology Next Generation (HTNG), the principal industry tech trade group, and leading hoteliers, Lorica has developed and is deploying the Lorica Room Center (LRC), a true IP based converged managed network for hotels. With a “single pipe” to the room, the LRC provides connectivity and management for all in-room devices – from high-speed Internet access (HSIA) and video on demand (VOD) to in-room controls and electronic door locks. The special requirements peculiar to hotels are engineered into the LRC solution – from security considerations to power and ease of maintenance. Lorica also has a suite of wired and wireless HSIA and VOIP telephony products, as well as services including remote monitoring and management of hotel systems and guest and staff help desk support. Learn more at www.loricasolutions.com

  • Board Observer

    iCardiac Technologies, Inc.

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Biotechnology industry)

    October 2006 — Present (3 years 8 months)

    iCardiac Technologies, Inc., headquartered in Rochester, New York, is a leading provider of advanced cardiac safety analysis technologies. The company evolved from research carried out at the Heart Research Follow-up Program at the University of Rochester. The company’s technology provides more rigorous characterization of the cardiac safety profiles of in-development and on-market drugs. This allows iCardiac’s customers to both accelerate drug development as well as bring compounds forward in clinical trials with more confidence about their cardiac safety. Additionally, the company’s core technology has applications in ECG-based cardiac diagnostics and medical devices. Learn more at www.icardiac.com

  • Investor

    PixFusion

    (Information Technology and Services industry)

    May 2006 — Present (4 years 1 month)

    PixFusion, established in 2001 and located in New York City, creates audio and photo personalized video products and services applying its patented technology to automate the production process. Pixfusion creates and sells children’s high profile branded video/DVD products through its Kideo brand (www.kideo.com).

    PixFusion has acquired licenses from some of the most prominent and respected companies including Marvel, Nickelodeon, Hit Entertainment, American Greeting and WGBH. In addition to its consumer entertainment division, PixFusion has also entered into several licenses of its proprietary intellectual property platform based on the strength of its long standing patent portfolio.

    PixFusion markets its DVD products widely through traditional and new media outlets including distribution channels within the photo-personalization industry. Learn more at www.pixfusion.com

OptiGauge has become the preferred approach to online and QA lab testing for leading medical device and industrial companies throughout the world. Learn more at www.lumetrics.com

    Stonehenge Growth Capital

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Venture Capital & Private Equity industry)

    SGC manages $80MM targeted to Growth Equity opportunities. SGC's growth equity team focuses predominantly on high potential technology and business services companies based in the Southeast (FL/LA/AL/GA/NC), Northeast (NY/NJ/PA/CT), and Texas, particularly in areas where an abundance of investment opportunities are underserved by a relatively small number of local growth equity investors.
