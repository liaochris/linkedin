Jonathan Coffman’s Summary

With a background in journalism and convergent media, I work to ensure that the people behind trusted brands are able to effectively connect and share with their audiences and consumers.

In my role as social media product manager at PBS I manage the roadmap and lifecycle of products and services meant to engage users across the web. PBS' social product portfolio is used on PBS.org, by local member stations across the country, and well as by many independent content producers.

Recent Accomplishments:

2010
- Co-founded "Public Media Chat" a weekly Twitter-based collaboration effort meant to bring together web practitioners from across the public media ecosystem.
- Launched a comprehensive widget platform designed to easily syndicate content to anywhere on the web.

2009
- Co-founded "Public Media Camp", an un-conference movement to spur public/private partnerships and volunteerism for PBS local stations across the country.
- Successfully launched a re-architected online identity platform empowering PBS.org, local member stations, and independent content producers to streamline and share user registration and login information while maintaining the trust of users.
- Implemented a strategy to market PBS national content in social networks and through a series of content efforts and parter development, took the national @pbs Twitter account from 5,000 followers to 500,000 in one year.

Jonathan Coffman’s Specialties:

Product development, social media marketing and site integration, online community building, personal and corporate communications and marketing, partnership development.
