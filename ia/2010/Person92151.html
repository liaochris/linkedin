J. Christopher Frey’s Summary

I established Keystone Preservation Group in 2005. My firm provides comprehensive services in the arena of historic preservation, including architectural conservation and scope definition to historic preservation planning and rehabilitation investment tax credit consulting. We provide Principal-level expertise with every service we offer.

Architectural conservation services include the analysis, interpretation and treatment of historic building materials including paint finishes, masonry and woodwork. We regularly complete interpretive analyses of historic finishes to identify significant decorative schemes and provide insight on building evolution. We do extensive work with historic mortars, plasters and stuccoes, with services including mortar analysis, interpretation and replication.

Having worked with historic buildings for several decades and possessing practical experience with material treatments, we regularly complete conditions surveys and assist our clients with recommendations to define the scope of their projects (including both drawings and specifications).

Our preservation planning documents include cultural resource surveys, surveys for historic cemeteries, National Register Nominations, Historic Districts, Section 106 mitigation and Historic Structures Reports.

Collectively, my firm's staff has submitted Certified Rehabilitation applications for more than 200 buildings in the United States, ranging from modest rowhouses to historic schools to substantial industrial complexes.
