Don Rose’s Experience

  • Director, Carolina KickStart

    UNC

    (Biotechnology industry)

    November 2009 — Present (1 year 1 month)

    Carolina KickStart (formerly known as NC BioStart) is a program to help facilitate and accelerate startup companies spinning out of the University. The program focuses on biomedical and life science technologies (drugs, devices, diagnsotics, tools) and how to bring those technologies to the marketplace through venture formation.

  • VP Marketing and Business Strategy

    Metabolon

    (Privately Held; Biotechnology industry)

    October 2007 — October 2009 (2 years 1 month)

    Metabolon is a metabolomics technology company. I refined the strategy of the service business to move beyond the early adoption phase. By focusing on vertical markets and supporting key applications, broader adoption was realized.

    In addition, I formulated the product strategy for the company's two leading diagnostic tests (pre-diabetes, prostate cancer), focusing on un-met needs, competition, and reimbursement.

  • Adjunct Professor

    Kenan-Flagler Business School

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    August 2007 — August 2009 (2 years 1 month)

    Developed and taught the 2nd year MBA course The Business of Life Science Ventures (MBA 837). This course focused on the elements of successfully launching a life science venture
    • developing the business concept
    • acquiring the IP
    • securing funding
    • product development
    • regulatory hurdles
    • sales and and marketing

  • Partner

    Heleos Advisors

    (Biotechnology industry)

    2005 — October 2007 (2 years )

    Heleos Advisors was a life science tools and technologies consulting group. We specialized in early to mid stage compaies with high-impact technologies. Services ranged from product strategy to tactical marketing to sales force development.

  • President US Operations

    Deerac Fluidics

    (Biotechnology industry)

    2004 — 2005 (1 year )

    Deerac Fluidics, based in Dublin, Ireland, was in the early stages of commercializing a nanoliter liquid dispensing techology.

    I developed their go-to-market strategy for attacking key markets. In addition, I established their North American presence through hiring and managing the NA sales team and developing a strategic marketing plan.

  • President

    DataCentric Automation

    (Pharmaceuticals industry)

    2003 — 2004 (1 year )

    DataCentric Automation (DCA), a division of Titan Corporation, had developed software and instrumentation for automated x-ray crystallography.

    DCA was considered a non-strategic asset and I was hired to increase revenue to make the unit more attractive to an outside buyer. I put together a commercial team and oversaw the purchase of DCA by Kendro, now part of ThermoFisher.

  • Partner

    Catalysta Partners

    (Venture Capital & Private Equity industry)

    2001 — 2002 (1 year )

    Catalysta Partners, now part of Hatteras Venture Partners, was an early stage VC company.

    As partner, I evalutated the life science deal flow and served as senior management of portfolio companies. During this time, I helped to found and run Phase Bioscience.

  • President

    Phase Bioscience

    (Biotechnology industry)

    2001 — 2002 (1 year )

    Phase Bioscience was a start-up company based on novel polymer technology developed at Duke University. The polymers were able to rapidly and reversibly undergo heat-induced phase transitions.

    I helped found the company, identify the key application, raise its seed round, secure SBIR funding, and establish partnerships.

  • VP Product Development

    Cartesian Technologies

    (Pharmaceuticals industry)

    1997 — 2001 (4 years )

    Cartesian Technologies commercialized a nanoliter dispensing and printing technologies.

    As part of the founding team, I worked across many aspects of the company from product development to sales. The company was successfully acquired by Genomic Solutions in Dec 2001

  • Research Investigator

    Glaxo

    (Public Company; GSK; Pharmaceuticals industry)

    1991 — 1997 (6 years )

    Worked on developing novel technologies for internal use in drug discovery. Projects included novel bioanalytical sytems employing capillary electrophoresis, a combinatorial chemisty system for building chemical libraries on plastic sheets, and a high-resolution imaging system for high-throughput screening.

  • Member of Technical Staff

    Hewlett-Packard

    (Public Company; HPQ; Computer Hardware industry)
