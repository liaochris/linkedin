Eli Braha’s Experience

  • Principal, Managing Member

    Berkley Acquisitions LLC

    (Real Estate industry)

    April 1988 — Present (22 years )

    Born in 1956 and a Brooklyn, NY native, Mr. Braha is a managing partner at Berkley Acquisitions LLC and sits on the Board of Advisors of various corporations. He attended Bernard Baruch School of Business at C.U.N.Y., is the father of four children, and is the eldest son of Joseph Braha, patriarch of a third-generation real estate and textile merchant trading family. His global business expertise encompasses commercial trade with international business partners in the USA, Asia, Europe, and Central and South America. Through “BRAHA INVESTMENTS” et al, Mr. Braha has been involved in the real estate industry for over 3 decades. His success in the business derives from his persistence and ability to source, negotiate, develop, promote, structure and market real estate properties. His core capabilities focus on creating exposure and analyzing market need.
    Mr. Braha understands the potential and long-term benefits associated with “value oriented income providing investment properties”. Mr. Braha has been involved in the formation and raise of capital as a developer and lead syndicator of commercial real estate in the roles of principal, property management, facilitating leasing deals, acquiring all types of commercial properties, consulting and co-investing in other real estate entities. He has acquired, owned, developed managed, and operated over 100 buildings in 14 states containing in excess of 13+ million square feet of collective holdings in his past and present portfolio. His partners are comprised of over 100 family members, friends, and close community ties.
    Mr. Braha is of the opinion that there are potential opportunities under the current financial market climate and bases his strategy on the overall impact of the soft economic and real estate environment. With proper valuation, and proper mid and long range projection planning, including accounting for a coming period of inflationary pressure, select assets can be a formula for success.
