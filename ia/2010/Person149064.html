Mary Moudry’s Summary

Over the years, I’ve been fortunate to have had a career that is ever‐evolving. I began at Needham (now DDB) in Chicago, moved to the Bay Area and did a 13 year stint at FCB SF, and then left to open DDB SF in 1996.

Earlier in my career, I worked on many iconic brands marketed by companies such as McDonald’s, Taco Bell, General Mills, Levi Strauss, Disney, Pacific Bell and the “Dancing Raisins.” More recently, I stepped out of my comfort zone of leading accounts and opted to lead an agency instead, assuming the role of President DDB San Francisco in 2003.

Designing client service platforms and determining what is best suited to serve their unique needs is my passion. I discovered this as the lead architect behind The Clorox Company’s global consolidation to DDB San Francisco in 2000, affecting over 40 brands in 20 + countries.

I’m told that clients and co‐workers respect me for my unpretentious style, which inspires great teamwork. Despite the fact I loath the spotlight, I was named in 2002 by Advertising Age as a “Woman to Watch” and for the past five years, the San Francisco Business Times named me “one of the most influential women in Bay Area business." In 2000, I was also featured in Working Mother as an executive who had presumably figured out work‐life balance. You’d have to check with my husband and two sons on that one though as they are my pragmatic life anchors and my largest source of pride.
