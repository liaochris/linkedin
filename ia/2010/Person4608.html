Andrew Mrkva’s Summary

In the past four years, I have researched social change in the Czech Republic following the Velvet Revolution of 1989, assisted in organizing and documenting the Farm Labor Organizing Committee's immigrant labor rights movement in Northwest Ohio, directed and edited an oral history video titled Voices of the Beltline Community, instructed youths in an oral history video project titled Life in the Beltline, coordinated a team of oral historians from the Crossroads to Freedom digital archive to document the Hyde Park community of Memphis, Tennessee, and edited a Native American documentary short film for the C.H. Nash Museum titled Welcome to Chucalissa. My objective is to integrate community organizing with ethnographic research and grassroots media production in order to engage the public, share unsung stories, refute misinformation, and foster participation, support, and action in human rights and social justice movements.
