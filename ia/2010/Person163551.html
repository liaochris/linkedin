Philip Decker’s Summary

Philip J. Decker < Philip [dot] Linkedin (at) Decker {dot} ws >
is an Electrical/Computer Engineer (BSEE + MSCS) with broad experience in embedded systems product design and development:
- Wireless Routers and Bridges
- Asynchronous Transfer Mode (ATM) switches and multi-media concentrators
- Wired Communications Equipment (Ethernet, EIA-232/422/485)
- Fire Safety Systems
- Building Access Control Systems
- Avionics (Flight Recorders)
- Data Acquisition (Magnetic Tape Recorders)
- Professional Audio
- Custom Industrial Instrumentation

Programming Languages used (professionally, academically, or both): FORTRAN, PL/1, BASIC, Batch, Lisp, Prolog, Pascal, Modula-2, C, LabView, Assembler, awk, sed, Shell Scripting.

US Citizen. Have never held a security clearance (nor applied for one).

Philip Decker’s Specialties:

Requirements Analysis, Product definition and specification, Verbal and written communications, System Architecture, Hardware/Software partitioning, System design. Circuit design. Programmable Logic Development (CPLD/FPGA). Embedded processor design (8/16/32-bit). Low-power design. Electro-Magnetic Compatibility (EMC). Power over Ethernet (PoE).

Fully bi-lingual: English/Spanish.

    (Public Company; 51-200 employees; Wireless industry)

    2000 — 2003 (3 years )

    SPEEDCOM, d.b.a. Wave Wireless Networking, produced outdoor wireless routers and bridges for use in the unlicensed ISM bands (900 MHz, 2.4 GHz). Topologies initially supported were Point-to-Point, and Point-to-Multipoint

    I managed and directed the development of the SPEEDLAN 9100 series of outdoor wireless routers, which improved on the legacy products by:
    - Using Direct Sequence Spread Spectrum (DSSS), as in IEEE 802.11b
    - Supporting Wireless Mesh topology
    - Leveraging open source software (Linux and GNU)
