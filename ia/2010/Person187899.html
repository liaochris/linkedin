Sharon Vosmek’s Summary

Founded in 1999 in Silicon Valley, Astia is a not-for-profit organization with a mission to foster the full participation of women in entrepreneurship in high-growth businesses. Astia’s clients are women-led high-growth start-ups.

The Astia model is to connect Astia’s clients to a community of investors, industry leaders, advisors, and service providers through programs and networking to access capital, embolden leadership and accelerate growth. Today, Astia is a community of over 450 experts committed to building women leaders and accelerating the funding and growth of the companies they lead. The Astia Advisor Network includes more than 125 investors and 100 current and former CEOs. Astia offers programs in Silicon Valley, New York, London and India.

Since 2003, companies that have participate in Astia programs have raised over $500M, achieved a greater than 60% fundraising success rate and have realized 12 exits, including two IPOs. Astia is supported through generous sponsorships, member fees, and revenues and warrants from clients it serves.

Sharon Vosmek’s Interests:

Ms. Vosmek is active in the Bay Area nonprofit community, working closely with organizations that promote social, economic, racial, and gender equity and enfranchisement. She serves on the Board of Advisors of Astia (formerly the Women's Technology Cluster) and the Board of Directors of the YMCA of San Francisco. In the past she has served on the Boards of Global Exchange, the Institute for Food & Development Policy, and the Level Playing Field Institute, where she played key roles in strategic planning, project planning, and fundraising.
