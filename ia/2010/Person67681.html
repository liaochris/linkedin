Ethan Alexander Jewett, MA’s Summary

I have spent over 10 years working within the health care arena to promote the best interests of patients through public policy, health services research and child advocacy. My role at the American Academy of Pediatrics has granted me extensive opportunities to partner with other medical organizations to improve medical education, ensure an adequate physician workforce to meet patient needs, and to explore innovations that will improve the health care system.

As I move towards ordination as an Episcopal priest, I am seeking opportunities to integrate my experience in health care policy with my vocation to care for the spiritual needs of people. One of the key roles of a priest is to serve as an advocate for marginalized and disavantaged people by working within the structures of secular society, such as government agencies, not-for-profit organizations, and community-based groups.

I am currently working to develop cross-train.net, a portal for learning spiritual practices through online resources and live events. This initiative is driven by a concern that postmodern people are seeking spiritual growth that traditional religious institutions have been largely unable to provide.
