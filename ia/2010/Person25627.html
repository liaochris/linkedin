Bob Prichard’s Summary

Clients include Kareem Addul-Jabbar; golfers Nick Faldo, Se Ri Pak, David Frost, Miriam Nagl; swimmers Matt Biondi, Amy Van Dyken, Jenny Thompson. On-air analyst for NBC Sports 1992 Olympics Broadcast, monthly column on sports in The New York Times, writer for Golf magazine, Swim, Swimming Technique, The Runner. Subject of articles in Sports Illustrated, Golf Digest, Sport, Los Angeles Times, San Francisco Chronicle.

Author of The Efficient Golfer--first golf book to show readers how to use their home camcorder to videotape, measure, analyze and improve their swing mechanics.

Developed and patented the Somax Power Hip Trainer, first aerobic exercise machine to increase the strength and speed of hip rotation for golf, tennis, baseball, soccer, martial arts, swimming, etc.

Discovered that the power in swimming comes from the rotation of the hips--and not from the muscles of the arms. Developed the Somax Powerbelt--fins that attached to the swimmers hips to increase the strength and speed of hip rotation.

Bob Prichard’s Specialties:

Videotape measurement and analysis of sports mechanics, including running, swimming, golf, tennis, baseball, football, basketball, soccer, javelin, rowing. Improvement of flexibility beyond stretching and reversing age-related stiffness by releasing microfibers (a mild form of scar tissue) in the connective tissue between the muscles. Examples can be seen at www.somaxsports.com
