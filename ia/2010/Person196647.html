Stuart Waite’s Summary

I have worked in the media industry since graduating from university - some 20 years. Firstly as a magazine editor, then Internet project manager, software development manager and CTO, then Founder and CEO of my own company.

I am an evangelist and expert in conceptualising and delivering digital media and content solutions to a multitude of platforms across digital TV, Mobile and the Web.

I work with stake-holders right across the value chain including Advertising and Media Agencies; Broadcasters, Content Producers and Network Operators.

Over the years I've been instrumental in conceptualising and delivering interactive services to the likes of Sky (UK), BBC (UK), Turner Broadcasting (US), Singtel (Singapore), ITV (UK), Deutsche Telekom (Germany), Telefonica (Spain), Directgov (UK), AT&T (US), Five (UK) and La Sexta (Spain).

I have extensive knowledge of delivering interactive applications to all the main digital media platforms, and understand how to develop and implement business models that effectively leverage these technologies for content owners and brands.

Stuart Waite’s Specialties:

Digital interactive media and content evangelist. Helping small companies and startups to generate strong IP and exit strategies.

Strategically strong and a subject matter expert on interactive media. Expert in defining the end-user experience - a true advocate for delivering the best possible consumer experience across multiple devices and platforms. Strategic vision, operations and logistics, people management, financial management, sales presentations, public speaker.

Stuart Waite’s Experience

  • Head of Interactive Media

    Alcatel-Lucent

    (Public Company; ALU; Telecommunications industry)

    September 2007 — February 2010 (2 years 6 months)

    Stuart was the Head of Interactive Media for Alcatel-Lucent who acquired his company, Tamblin, in 2007. He was responsible for the Interactive Media Manager (IMM) product line globally which meant he managed a diverse team of people comprising business development and marketing experts, usability and interface designers, product and project managers as well as software engineers.

    He supported business development of new opportunities throughout Asia Pacific, Europe and North America. Customers included AT&T (USA), Deutsche Telekom (Germany), Singtel (Singapore), Telefonica (Spain), Turner Broadcasting System (USA), La Sexta (Spain), Orange (UK and France), Vodafone (Spain), Portugal Telecom, Telecom Italia and Time Warner Cable (USA).

    He left once he'd completed his earn out after the acquisition of Tamblin.

  • Chief Executive Officer

    Tamblin

    (Privately Held; Broadcast Media industry)

    June 2000 — September 2007 (7 years 4 months)

    In 2000 I set up Tamblin as a sepcialist interactive TV consultancy. I operated at the heart of Tamblin's operations and provided a unique strategic vision for developing simple technical solutions to complex problems. I worked with Tamblin's clients and technical teams to conceptualise future products, and to ensure Tamblin remained at the forefront of Interactive TV technology.

    Tamblin was a software development company based in London, UK. Tamblin's primary product suite, i-ZoneTV®, was a content publishing and template management software solution developed for the TV broadcast industry. It's designed specifically for producers and editors who need to manage and control interactive content from multiple sources published out to multiple platforms. Customers included Sky (UK), BBC (UK), ITV (UK), Channel 4 (UK) and Directgov (UK).
