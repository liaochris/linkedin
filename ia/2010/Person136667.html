  • Director, Product Management

    Convergent Networks

    (Privately Held; 201-500 employees; Telecommunications industry)

    September 1999 — September 2001 (2 years 1 month)

    Convergent was a well-backed high-profile startup poised at redefining the burgeoning voice-over-data field for tandem switch replacement and Class 5 replacement markets. During my tenure, the company grew from $0 to $70 million in revenue, filed an S1 for IPO, and landed more than 70 customers. Ultimately the company was not able to survive the erosion of the CLEC marketplace, however the technology that was developed continues to be deployed at surviving CLECs across the country.

  • Director, Carrier Product Management

    Xylan (acquired by Alcatel)

    (Public Company; Computer Networking industry)

    1997 — 1999 (2 years )

    In 1997, Xylan invested in their first carrier product development initiative by creating a design center in Acton, MA. I was recruited by the founding team to define all the product requirements for this new set of carrier prod-ucts, starting with a high-density multi-channel IP access module. Defined all product requirements for module and subsequent Packet-Over-Sonet OC3/OC12 and BGP route calculation offload processor. Liaised to Xylan corporate product development teams and supported the Carrier sales team with technical collateral, cus-tomer briefings, RFP responses, and competitive analysis. After the acquisition by Alcatel, worked closely with corporate strategic planning to define product integration into Alcatel’s carrier/ISP product line.

  • Sr. Product Manager

    Bay Networks

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; BAY; Computer Networking industry)

    November 1996 — September 1997 (11 months)

    Joined Wellfleet Communications as a Software Engineer to develop fast-packet switch protocols for MCI’s Hy-perStream Network (largest Frame Relay network in the world at the time).
