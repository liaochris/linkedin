Rob Smith MA, FInstSMM, MISPAL’s Experience

  • Commercial Director

    Floral Pavilion Theatre & Conference Centre

    (Leisure, Travel & Tourism industry)

    June 2008 — Present (2 years 4 months)

    The Floral Pavilion Theatre & Conference Centre is in the vanguard of a multi-million pound regeneration programme in New Brighton, Wirral. Building on the tradition of theatre and performing arts in a faded coastal resort, the redeveloped iconic Theatre and Conference Centre is building new audiences, and attracting increasing numbers in the meetings and conferencing sector. Aggressive marketing and promotion is generating a dynamic and exciting profile, attention to design detail is providing a high quality destination, and the venue is supporting job creation and attracting significant inward investment to New Brighton.

  • Head of Arts & Museums

    Wirral Council

    (Government Agency; Leisure, Travel & Tourism industry)

    July 2005 — Present (5 years 3 months)

    I am Head of Service for Wirral Council Venues, Museum and Heritage sites, and events. I am responsible for implementing Wirral Council policy within the context of Arts & Museums policy development and delivery; I have been Wiral Council's Project Manager for the development & delivery of the new Floral Pavilion Theatre & Conference Centre in New Brighton; I undertake Arts & Museums strategic planning; cultural planning; capital and revenue budget management; financial management including strategic & operational budget monitoring and review; project development & management; manage multiple multidisciplinary staff teams, consultancy commissioning & management; marketing and communications; event management. Represent Wirral Council at local, regional and national level via formal and informal networks.

  • Festival Director

    International Guitar Festival of Great Britain

    (Leisure, Travel & Tourism industry)

    1989 — Present (21 years )

    Our International Guitar Festival is the longest running annual Guitar Festival in the UK. The philosophy which underpins this Guitar Festival is 'a celebration of the guitar and its music' in all its guises. We present a programme of the broadest range of styles including jazz, blues, classical, folk, rock, country, pedal steel, flamenco..the list goes on. World class players present a stunning programme of concerts ranging from solo performances, bands, ensembles through to guitar concertos, and we present a number of workshops, all during November each year. The Festival programme includes the breathtaking wizardry of virtuoso players who will dazzle the most demanding critic, in a series of events that deliver the most enjoyable musical experience to those looking for a good time and good company, as well as those technical aficionados. The message is - Miss it and Weep...

  • Arts & Creative Industries Development Manager

    Wirral Council

    (Leisure, Travel & Tourism industry)

    April 1995 — July 2005 (10 years 4 months)

    Responsibility for planning, development and implementation of a comprehensive Cultural Development Programme, within the context of a seven year (1995 - 2002), £82m, culturally-led regeneration programme in Birkenhead, Wirral named the Hamilton Quarter Project. With the aim of creating a Cultural Quarter, the development programme included venue and museum developments, public realm works, community programmes plus Cultural Industries business support and an associated economic development strategy. Due to a coincidental Local Authority departmental restructure, the then existing Arts & Museums Service underwent a review, restructure and staff reduction. I then had to manage these changes and react to refocussed council policy priorities. Represented Wirral Council at local, regional and national levels via formal and informal networks.

  • Arts Officer

    Wirral Council

    (Leisure, Travel & Tourism industry)

    February 1988 — March 1995 (7 years 2 months)

    Wirral's first Arts Development Officer with responsibility for community arts development, regional funding relationships, events management and festivals delivery, whilst developing corporate coordination, multidisciplinary working and resource development. Represented Wirral Council at local, regional and national levels via formal and informal networks. Drama Advisor for the Arts Council of Great Britain.

  • Independent Professional Artist

    Self Employed

    (Arts and Crafts industry)

    July 1975 — January 1988 (12 years 7 months)

    Cultural producer; part-time and visiting fine art lecturer. Producer and exhibitor of fine art drawings and sculpture, published and recorded songwriter, author and record label owner. Graphic designer specialising in mail-order catalogues and marketing. Latin percussionist experienced in live band, musical theatre, and session work. From 1977 to 1988 - voluntary sector cultural events organiser and Chair and Committee Member of Birkenhead Arts Association, Wirral Youth Theatre, Birkenhead Youth Orchestra and Friends of the Williamson Art Gallery & Museum.
