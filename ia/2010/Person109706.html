Jessica Zimberlin’s Summary

My thesis work in the Crosby Research Group includes studying cell/material interactions in early tissue formation by:
• Developing a dynamic surface to determine the applied stresses of growing cell sheets
• Investigating the role of intercellular junctions on stress transmission
• Developing a cavitation-based method to determine the mechanical properties of soft
materials over various length scales at arbitrary locations
• Investigating the role of solvent media and lowering the surface tension on cavitation
• Investigating the importance of mechanical property measurement in vivo by studying the
vitreous in bovine eyes
