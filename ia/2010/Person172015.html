Richard Aplin’s Summary

UK Video games industry 1983-1999,
Got into it early, had my first game published when I was 13. Wrote lots of commercial games on a wide variety of hardware over the years from B&W 8-bits and up. Worked for several companies; Mastertronic, Sega/Virgin, Binary Design, Creative Materials, Codemasters, Fube Industries. Managed teams, wrote code, hired+fired, schmoozed, all that stuff.

Funnest job: Chip design and console hardware reverse-engineering for the Game Genie.

Moved to San Francisco in 2000, was TD of Blam Video Games until a few years later it shrank to small dot and disappeared and I was sick of the games industry. During that time we did a couple of super-early cellphone games, but the phones were frankly junk, and learned a whole bunch about the internet.

Then I took the job of CTO of Mobile Greetings in 2004, a cellphone app company in Walnut Creek, California. I wrote their content vending platform on Brew and J2ME, and spent several years enjoying the very black art of mobile development - a bunch of coding tricks from 1980's came in handy all over again. ;-)
This was a great job, very rewarding, because the stuff they are doing is technically very good, and because the biz people I worked with really love their job and attack opportunities with gusto - it's a great combo.

After years of working in almost every area of the development process, in mid-2007 the opportunity arose to see the business from a completely different perspective;
I hopped over the fence from mobile developer to one of the largest mobile carriers, Verizon Wireless, working on a pleasingly challenging and significant new mobile project. I went from a company with a couple of dozen employees to one with nearly quarter of a million - never a dull moment.

In my free time I build odd electronic gizmos using whatever's currently interesting, and some of them I take to the Burning Man festival to see if they last more than five minutes in the desert conditions.

Richard Aplin’s Specialties:

Cornucopia of geekin' ; from hardware to low and high level software, from 8-bit videogames to most things about cellphones to 64-bit servers. 2d graphics, 3d Graphics, optimisation, reverse-engineering, hardware design, embedded systems, many microcontrollers, FPGA design+test, SQL administration, Linux admin, C, C++, C#, PHP, J2ME, Brew, Beagleboard, J2SE, even nice modern stuff like Android, GWT, Hibernate and on and on and on; I've been doing this stuff a long time. Also I'll make you laugh.
