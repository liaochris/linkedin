Tammie Anderson’s Summary

Since childhood I have had an interest in the thought processes that drive behaviors. This led me to the field of cognitive psychology, studying human perception, reasoning, decision making, problem solving, and learning. As a student at The Ohio State University I discovered comparative cognition, or comparing animal cognition to human cognition, and volunteered for many years at the OSU Chimpanzee Center, learning about primates through non-invasive behavioral research. After graduation, I conducted comparative cognition research with dolphins at Epcot’s Living Seas and the Dolphin Research Center in Florida. My research education provided extensive experience analyzing both subjective observational data and objective empirical data. In 2005, I joined the training firm S4 NetQuest and began applying my cognitive knowledge to content development for training programs. In 2009, Red4 Simulations was born as a joint venture, specializing in creating simulations for the medical device industry. Red4’s simulated medical device prototypes can be used to support usability testing, product demonstrations and training, and are especially effective for addressing FDA Human Factors requirements. I am currently involved in projects for both S4 NetQuest and Red4 Simulations.
