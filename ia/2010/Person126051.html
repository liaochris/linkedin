Lisa Macpherson’s Summary

Experienced consumer products executive with a passion for marketing. An unusual background that combines the immediacy of retail merchandising with the long-range discipline of brand stewardship. Strong track record of driving growth by creating emotional connections with premium global brands. Particular expertise in product development and marketing for women. Have thrived in family-run and family-owned businesses.

Lisa Macpherson’s Specialties:

Brand strategy, brand management, marketing, general management, wholesale product development and retail product merchandising, visual merchandising, consumer packaged goods (CPG), marketing to women, emotional marketing, international, fashion, retail, family-run and family-owned businesses, public speaking

Lisa Macpherson’s Experience

  • SVP, Chief Marketing Officer

    Hallmark Cards Inc

    (Privately Held; Consumer Goods industry)

    April 2000 — Present (10 years 5 months)

    Oversee brand and marketing strategy, advertising, direct marketing, digital marketing, visual merchandising, creative services, consumer research, and marketing operations for one of the world’s leading brands. Scope of the position includes 5 product and service platforms and specialty, mass and digital distribution. Also oversee development and production for the Hallmark Hall of Fame, the only remaining corporate-branded television series on network television.

    Previous Roles at Hallmark: SVP, Chief Merchandising Officer (2004-2009); General Manager, Gifts Business Unit (2002 - 2004); Vice President, Gifts & Celebrations (2000 - 2002).

  • SVP, Global Marketing

    The Timberland Company

    (Apparel & Fashion industry)

    1998 — 2000 (2 years )

    Developed and implemented marketing strategy for US and international divisions with wholesale and both wholly-owned and franchised retail distribution. Directed brand strategy and management; advertising, promotions, and public relations; wholesale and retail visual merchandising; creative services; consumer research; and marketing operations.

  • SVP, Marketing

    Fisher-Price

    (Public Company; MAT; Consumer Goods industry)

    1987 — 1998 (11 years )

    Most recently had global oversight and US P&L responsibility for Toys, the company's core business unit (1997 - 1998).

    Previous roles at Fisher-Price were in marketing and product management: SVP, International Marketing (1995 - 1997); VP, Marketing Communications (1994 – 1995); Director of Marketing (1990 - 1993). Hired as a Marketing Assistant in June, 1987; promoted to Assistant Marketing Manager (1988), Marketing Manager (1989) and Director of Marketing (1990). Fisher-Price was owned by Quaker Oats from 1969 to 1993 and provided a classic consumer packaged goods marketing training program.
