Jurgen Willems’s Experience

  • General Manager

    Crius Group

    (Privately Held; Publishing industry)

    September 1998 — Present (11 years 10 months)

    Originally founded as a prepress company in 1989, today Crius Group offers a broad range of services from typesetting and digitisation to cost effective and innovative data conversion and publishing solutions.
    As a result of our 20 years of expertise in the publishing sector we have the necessary knowledge and competences to ensure the added value our customers are looking for.

    Crius Group stands for an open, honest and down-to-earth approach valuing the importance of excellent customer relationships. We consider our customer to be a partner with whom we constantly look for the optimal win-win situation.
    We offer the services and solutions needed today and take pride in helping to prepare for tomorrow’s challenges.

    New technologies and developments have radically changed the way content is made available. Aside from traditional paper carriers for books, catalogues, product sheets and such, digital media are rapidly becoming more important.
    The technological evolution doesn't only facilitate a faster information transfer but it also becomes more up-to-date and flexible.
    This progress offers great opportunities and challenges for content creators like book and magazine publishers, governmental organisations and academic institutions, but also for manufacturers and distributors with a large collection of products, parts and procedures.

    Crius Group keeps up with technological evolutions and specializes in innovative premedia applications aimed at the future. Apart from merely assisting our customers with the implementation of new developments, we also take the time to consider the optimisation of existing processes. We take a closer look at the entire production workflow, from conception to publication. We don’t limit ourselves to the production of print-ready PDF files but also help in delivering content online.

    "Improving publishing through technology"
