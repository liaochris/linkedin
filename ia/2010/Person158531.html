Pace-O Beats’s Summary

Pace-O Beats, the rising star producer/engineer/songwriter hailing from Southwest Philadelphia started his career in 2002 using a computer program called FL Studio made famous by “Souljah Boy” and “9TH Wonder.” As a child at the age of 7 through 15, Pace-O studied the piano ad music composition, and at the same time developed his love and passion for creating his own music. In the winter of 2002 Pace-o began producing and formed a production team with fellow producer and friend J. Calhoun under the name “The Beat Bangas” (which would later be changed to Major Moves Productions due to copyright disputes). A few months after joining forces “The Beat Bangas” were able to land over 10 credits on records with Peedi Crakk and Men of Respect, Oschino, and Joey Jihad. Pace-O used the recognition he had gained from those credits to build a strong report with a majority of artist in Philadelphia. He has since landed credits with artist such as Hell Rell, Lil Chuckee, Meek Mills, Ar Ab, Reed Dollaz, Kre Forch, Bugsy, Young Hot, M.I., Black Deniro, Philly Swain, Ape Gang, Hollywood, NH, Quilly Millz, and Young Eem. (P.S.: The list goes on) Pace-O has also tried his hand in the mixtape market, hosting and releasing a number of mixtapes including Food 4 Thought, Strap Da F*&k up Volumes 1-3, and It’s a new Day vol. 1, as well as releasing and being featured on numerous DVDs including Hood Celebrities Vol. 1 &2, I’m Not a Rappa 3, and Circle of Money. Pace-O has recently received what he describes as “a wave of exposure” after his work on Hell Rell’s “The Extermination” which received over 3.7 million views on the popular website Worldstarhiphop.com by way of a Jordan Tower directed video. Pace-O has recently been shopping tracks to artists such as Ace Hood, Jay Rock, Mack Maine, Freck Billionaire, Young Chris, while at the same time building his industry relationships through networking both in the streets on the web.

Pace-O Beats’s Specialties:

Pace-O is currently wrapping up his second instrumental mixtape, “Dinner is served” hosted by Rescpectmyhustle.net’s Dj Doe Boy, as well as planning the launch of his website Paceobeats.net.
