Elicia Banks-Gabriel’s Summary

I came to New York City to work for Walrus. Stories of what Mad Dogs & Englishmen stood for inspired me to join the forces that were changing advertising. Unfortunately, Mad Dogs had folded and everyone parted ways, but a few had created Walrus.

I knew I needed a small, privately owned agency. I just wasn't expecting that small, so I continued my search. Anomaly kept haunting my dreams and randomly entering my vocabulary.

"There's no way, no way. I don't even have an internship in advertising," is what I kept telling myself. Until one day, I just threw caution to the wind and wrote the most honest cover letter possible and prayed for the rest of the night.

To make this long story shorter, I was lucky enough to land an interview and a freelancing gig that then turned into a receptionist/researcher role. The next thing I knew, I was working on the best piece of business Anomaly has had in our six years of existence.

Lauren Luke is more than a client to my colleagues and myself. She is our inspiration and our friend. Over this past year, I've seen her YouTube views climb to over 77 million and her makeup brand reach over seas and magazines.

At the same time, I've grown as well. Learning and maturing into an assistant account manager and a social strategist ready to give advice to anyone asking.

The one thing I've learned in these past two years is that it is far more interesting to do something different, not just for others, but also for yourself. To be different is to push yourself to learn something new.
