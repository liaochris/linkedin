Jonathan Anderson’s Summary

My job is usually to be the jack of all trades, holding the line in a number of diverse functions while a company is too small to hire specialized professionals to handle any given area. This has meant that I've had my hands in almost every aspect of growing and managing a small business, including finance, accounting, legal, HR, marketing, IT, facilities, general operations, resource management, corporate governance, project management, and many other domains. I also have significant experience in writing and editing for business and online formats.

My time EffectiveUI gave me the opportunity to participate in work that's on the leading edge of software design and development, which is converging around an increasing attention to high quality user experience, around web-enabled or web-based software, and around technologies that can create unified experiences across multiple platforms, devices, and applications. I've built on this experience and the tremendous knowledge I gained in the field of UX through writing the book to turn my focus to providing resources to industry professionals businesspeople to help them understand the trends, technologies, and business rationales behind improving user experience in enterprise and consumer applications.

  • Managing Editor

    UX Magazine

    (Privately Held; Computer Software industry)

    June 2009 — Present (11 months)

    I recently joined UX Magazine as part of a new partnership. UX Magazine was created to deliver a central place to discuss the critical disciplines that all enhance user experience. Extraordinary user experiences should be the goal of every interaction you deliver to your users at any level. All too often, businesses (large and small) get it horribly wrong. It’s painful to watch and even worse when it happens to you. Every week we combine original articles with the best from blogs and online media to deliver an online magazine that covers the best new thinking on design, strategy, technology and common sense. We believe that the more businesses can bring ideas from these four critical disciplines inside their business the better the user experiences they deliver will be.

    Building on my experience in EffectiveUI, in writing the book, and in managing tech-focused companies, my role will be overseeing the flow of new editorial, writing original content, working to manage and grow the business, and taking UX Magazine on the road to trade shows, events, and industry leaders to develop new content and opportunities, and to begin to produce video and other types of rich content.

    Please visit us at uxmag.com, follow us on Twitter @uxmag or on Facebook at facebook.com/uxmag
