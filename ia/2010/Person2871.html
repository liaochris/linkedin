Adarsh Narasimhamurthy’s Summary

• 4 years research experience in signal processing for wireless communications with expertise in diversity combining for MIMO systems, channel estimation, OFDM, distributed estimation and relay aided multiuser communication.
• 2 published and 2 submitted IEEE journal manuscripts.
• Coauthor on the book “OFDM Systems for Wireless Communication”.
• Instructor for EEE/CSE 120: “Digital Design Fundamentals”.

• Detail oriented strong team player and independent thinker.

  • Graduate Research Associate

    Arizona State University, Tempe

    (Higher Education industry)

    January 2006 — Present (4 years 10 months)

    Advisor: Dr. Cihan Tepedelenlioglu

    1) Proved that antenna selection diversity combining at the receiver in a MIMO-OFDM system leads to reduction in complexity while still being able to achieve full spatial diversity when perfect channel is available at the receiver.

    2) For when the channel knowledge at the receiver is imperfect, it is shown that the system is still capable of achieving full spatial diversity but at a loss in performance due to the imperfect channel.

    3) Analyzed and proved that switch and stay combining further reduces complexity compared to antenna selection.

    4) Illustrated the effects of the switching threshold on the performance and derived an optimal switching threshold for when,
    a) the channel is perfectly known at the receiver,
    b) the channel is not known at the receiver but will be estimated using the pilot symbols transmitted, and
    c) the channel is not known at the receiver and differential transmission techniques are employed.

    5) Contributed to the development of a universal distributed estimator with constant modulus transmissions over a multiple access channel. Both the cases of an AWGN channel and a fading channel between the sensors and the fusion center were analyzed.

    6) Analyzing the benefits of the presence of a fixed relay in multiuser communication schemes with a large number of users, for the amplify & forward, and decode & forward relaying schemes.
