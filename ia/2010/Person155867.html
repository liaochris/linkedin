Nils Stenvig’s Summary

My short term professional goals are to finish my MSEE and MBA (Dec 2010) degrees. I am done with my MSEE classes already but I need the fall '10 semester to finish up a few remaining MBA classes and defend my thesis (which I have just begun writing...). I am currently doing an internship at Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory (in Livermore, CA) in their 'Atmospheric, Earth and Energy Division' - working on some energy related research. Once I have my MSEE & MBA I plan to enter the Power & Energy Industry work force, but I'm also starting to like life at a National Lab. Additionally, I've begun to recognize that I have much more interest in business than I ever thought I would. As I near the end of my MBA, I can see that it will mean much more to me than an extra 3 letters on my resume. I might be a fairly motivated person and I may also have a propensity for seeking responsibility... I pursue opportunities. I have many plans.

I don't like closing doors. I tend to make decisions that give me more opportunities.

I work hard and always do my best. I am also a competitive person. One of the best pieces of advice I've ever received (from a very respected Norwegian woman) was to pursue every opportunity. We have one life, and sometimes an opportunity only comes once. Its easy to say, not so easy to do.

  • Student Scholar / Research Scientist

    Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory

    (Government Agency; Research industry)

    June 2010 — Present (1 month)

  • GRA

    Michigan Technological University

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    August 2008 — Present (1 year 11 months)

    Research in area of transmission line communication – Broadband over Power Lines. Extensive use of network analyzer to characterize transformer admittance and build equivalent high-order RLC circuits for broad range of frequencies. Extensive use of Electromagnetic Transients Program (ATP) to model transmission lines and perform frequency scans to determine current distributions along lines.

    Also somewhat less involved with ongoing research in area of reducing blackout likelihood. Team research topics include transformer voltage distortion, voltage stability, load shedding, communication signals through transformers, and Smart Grids (WAM/WAC). Particular focus with implementing FACTS devices for time-domain analysis of WAC schemes. Extensive involvement with developing WAC capability for SVC tools. Involvement as lead author and co-author to potential conference papers.

Extensive use of ATP (Transient Analysis Software for Power Systems) for stability research, modeling and implementation of Static VAR Compensators, Power System Stabilizers & Active Voltage Regulators (synchronous machines).

Performed experiments to determine effects of harmonic voltage distortion on factory testing of power transformers. Lots of high-voltage experimentation and setup. Also research for control methods for synchronous machine models.
