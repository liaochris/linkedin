Richard Reeves’s Summary

Mr. Reeves works In the Statistics and Methods Group for the Energy Information Administration within the Department of Energy. He is the Division Director for the Survey Methods Group and supervises staff working on electronic data collection processes, cognitive interviewing, and privacy issues (data disclosure). Prior to coming to the Department of Energy, he worked at the National Student Clearinghouse as its inaugural Director of Research. In this role, he established the research department, developed a research agenda, managed the StudentTracker service, presented on topics related to educational research, prepared and executed grants, and designed metrics to measure student progress and completion. Before joining the Clearinghouse, Mr. Reeves was a Senior Research and Planning Associate at Cornell University. There he was responsible for financial aid research and reporting and enrollment management modeling for the university's admissions offices. He also conducted policy analysis for undergraduate financial aid and admissions, reviewed and tested several student data marts, and designed and administered the athletic recruitment database. Prior to Cornell, Mr. Reeves was the Director of Enrollment Research and Technology at Johns Hopkins University where he was responsible for the institution's undergraduate data warehouse, survey research program, policy analysis, and providing research consulting to its administrative offices. He has also taught statistical concepts as an Adjunct Professor at Syracuse University.

Richard Reeves’s Specialties:

Survey research, experimental design, data warehouse design, data mining, data modeling, SAS programming, SPSS, statistical consulting, educational research, enrollment modeling, reporting techniques.
