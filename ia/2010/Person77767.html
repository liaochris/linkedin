Greg Kalkwarf’s Summary

Greg leads the marketing team for the Colorado offices of BKD. He has more than 14 years of sales and marketing experience, including five years in professional services and seven years in professional sports.

His responsibilities include the design and implementation of the practice unit marketing and media relations plan, serving as a sales coach for approximately 40 Account Managers, developing marketing collateral and initiatives, and overseeing company special events.

Among the industry niche teams Greg assists on a day-to-day basis are construction and real estate, financial services, health care, manufacturing and distribution and not-for-profit and governmental.

Greg was named to the 2010 Denver Business Journal Forty Under 40, honored as a 2009 Rising Star by the Colorado Springs Business Journal, and selected as a participant for the 2008 Downtown Denver Leadership Program.

He is the board chair for the eXecs organization of the Colorado Association of Commerce and Industry, the chair for the Denver Zoo corporate committee, and serves on the membership services committee of the Downtown Denver Partnership. Greg has also been involved with fundraising committees for Denver Children’s Hospital, National Jewish Health and YMCA. He is a member of the Association of Accounting Marketers and the Legal Marketing Association.

    Wichita Baseball, Inc.

    (Sports industry)

    1995 — 2001 (6 years )

    In 1995, I started as a commission-only "Assistant To the General Manager" (yes, just like Dwight Schrute on The Office) for the Wichita Wranglers minor league baseball team and the National Baseball Congress, the nation's oldest adult amateur baseball league, handling administrative responsibilities for the GM and generating new revenue primarily through business-to-business cold calls. After blowing the doors off my sales quota, I was moved to a full-time Account Executive role and was responsible for about 10 percent of the organization's revenue. I again surpassed my quota and became Assistant General Manager of our special events division, helping build the start-up operations from scratch.

    In two years, we ran a PGA-sanctioned golf tournament and completed a $6 million fundraising project for Wichita State University, and published a book, "Sales Success in Sports Marketing." I then took over the managerial responsibilities for the sponsorships, advertising and promotions of the baseball team and amateur tournament and in 1999, scored a championship ring as the team captured the Texas League title on the field. The same year, the front office was named the Texas League Organization of the Year.
