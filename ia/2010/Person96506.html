Jerry Mairani’s Summary

I am a thirty year quality professional and Past President and Chairman of the American Society fro Quality, the largest and most prestigious quality organization in the world. I serve on the Board of Directors for the California Council for Excellence and I am a past Senior Examiner for the California Governors Board of Examiners.

I am currently the Dean of the American Institute for Performance Management & Improvement launched January 1, 2009 where I have provided the first Six Sigma training for Safeway Stores Logistics, and the University of California Davis Medical Center.

I recently completed an assignment as a Special Consultant (a legislated position) to the State of California .

I have been serving as a Professor for National Graduate School of Business Quality where I updated their Lean Six Sigma materials and instructed Lean Six Sigma for Boeing, Rocketdyne, U.S. Coast Guard, and Sundstrand Corp.

I served as the Corporate Quality Director for Beutler Corporation where he was their Six Sigma Master Black Belt designing and implementing Beutler’s Quality Management System, and managed training, employee development, and safety programs.

In my training and consulting capacities my client list includes, Delta Dental, H. J. Heinz, Siemens, Coherent, Aids Nation Healthcare Organization, California State Automobile Association, and the Department of Developmental Services – Agnews.

I took an outplacement offer from Pacific Bell in 1996 after twenty seven years. At that time I was Pacific Bell’s Statewide Service and Results Manager reporting to the Executive Vice President for Marketing and Operations.

I hold an undergraduate degree in Organizational Behavior and a Masters Degree in Organizational Development. I hold a certification as a Six Sigma Black Belt and Master Black Belt from Business Systems Architects located in the San Francisco Bay Area.

Jerry Mairani’s Specialties:

Lean Six Sigma Development, Instruction, and Implementation. Operational Management. Organizational Development

  • Special Consultant

    The State of California Department of Toxic Substances Control

    (Government Agency; Government Administration industry)

    July 2007 — May 2009 (1 year 11 months)

    Environmental investigation and cleanup activities (known as a Corrective Action) have been under way in the vicinity of Pacific Gas and Electric Company (PG&E)’s Station since 1997.

    Under DTSC's direction, PG&E has begun the process to assemble and evaluate cleanup options for chromium in groundwater and propose a Final Remedy for the site.

    This project was two years behind schedule and I was contracted to improve the processes that were hindering progress. I identifed the root-cause of the issue as communications that were hindering critical processes from function in a timely manner.

    My involvement in the project resulted in unprecedented new levels of cooperation which moved the project forward. The success of my efforts resulted in a professional documentary intended to capture the success and help others understand how shared understanding and communications might assist them. Please contact me if you have further interest in how this was achieved.

    The above success resulted in a new contract as Special Consultant were I designed the departments first quality training (Lean Six Sigma Yellow Belt) and the departments first improvement projects where I served as their Master Lean Six Sigma Black Belt.
