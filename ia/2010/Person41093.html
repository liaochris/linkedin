Clare Rayner’s Summary

My "day job" and passion is leading and developing Retail Acumen & The Retail Conference.

I also love working alongside my husband, Andrew Rayner, supporting him in his "day job" of leading e-mphasis, an internet marketing agency. Within e-mphasis I have been developing a completely retail focused offering - leveraging the synergies between our businesses and across our expertise.

You'll see from my profile below that I've spent my entire working life in retail. What you can't see is I spent much of my childhood in retail too...

From 1976 until it was sold in 1996 my parents ran what was essentially a multi-channel retailer, pre-dating the internet, providing made-to-measure fine furnishings via mail order, phone order and showroom service! We had own warehousing and manufacturing capability. Family holidays were usually buying trips to various yarn suppliers or dyers and weavers across Europe! My holiday jobs involved working with every part of the business. My greatest pleasure was developing all of the business process documentation to secure BS5750 total quality standards mark - we were the first company of its type to achieve this accolade back in 1989!

Dinner time was a daily board meeting. Thankfully my parents thought it was perfectly appropriate to employ a 4 year old to prepare brochures for posting... So I can thank them for the benefit of all the learning they gave me!

So, I've been immersed in retail since before I could read! As an adult I've worked in most areas - fast food, fashion, supermarkets and general merchandise - with roles across most commercial and operational functions, and latterly in change management, solution selection and implementation, and management consulting.

Launching Retail Acumen in 2006 was the obvious next step - I guess owning and running a business was inevitable...

I look forward to connecting and collaborating with you here on linkedin, for our mutual success!

Thanks for reading my profile!

  • Retail Acumen: Managing Consultant

    Woolworths

    (Public Company; WOW; Retail industry)

    October 2006 — January 2008 (1 year 4 months)

    Retail Acumen were engaged by Woolworths as the leading authority on retail commercial planning, specifically merchandise planning, demand forecasting and replenishment.

    The objectives of the assignment were:

    1. To understand the current state of the commercial supply chain, including causal analysis to identify the most likely contributors to low in store availability.

    2. To evaluate an end-state supply chain that utilised sku-store-day data to create an accurate demand forecast, driving out a bottom-up sales and stock flow plan (WSSI). Assessing the change requirements to achieve that end state. Determining if a viable business case existed for the change.

    What we discovered was that whilst process change could drive benefits, underlying systems constraints would prevent delivery of full benefits unless replaced or improved.

    As a result Retail Acumen supported Woolworths in the delivery of a vendor selection exercise for systems to support the commercial supply chain aims, providing all content for the functional assessment. This included an RFI, an extensive ITT and a series of scored presentations by shortlisted vendors.
