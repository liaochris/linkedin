Bill Kruse’s Summary

Currently involved in creating, testing and adapting a distributed data processing pipeline for large LiDAR data sets with a current focus producing GIS layers and forest metrics.

It is easy to get excited about innovative engineering, software and technology projects and the smart people that work together to make it all happen. I get involved with the newest software development tools and computer systems while solving the problems accompanying innovation. This keeps me up-to-date and able to contribute to, facilitate and motivate the progress of a project team while maintaining the respect of my peers.

I have a passion for advancing digital imaging software and processing pipelines to improve real-time mapping and web access to distributed spatial data and map information. This embraces technology for 3D modeling, visualization and spatial search utilizing digital image resources from the web, photographs and video.

I am a hands-on software/systems/hardware engineer with a broad technical background and a sharp focus on project details. This includes software development and engineering management experience with direct career involvement in remote sensing software, spatial database management, satellite imagery, aerial image acquisition, orthophoto processing, marine bathymetry and the supporting IT infrastructure.

Bill Kruse’s Specialties:

Startup, small technology company and consulting background. Experience and strong interest in software development, raster image processing, geographic data, OGC-WMS/WCS/WFS web services, small engineering team management, information systems architecture and systems administration. A current emphasis on Java for the development of portable, scalable and distributed web applications.
