Liam Goldrick’s Experience

  • Policy Director

    New Teacher Center

    (Non-Profit; Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    June 2006 — Present (4 years )

    The New Teacher Center (NTC) is the premier national organization focused on high-quality new teacher induction. As policy director, I collaborate with NTC staff to connect policy, practice and research within the organization. I have developed a strategic policy plan incorporating consultation, communications, and advocacy in alignment with NTC goals and mission. My responsibilities include: advancing NTC visibility in the policy community; developing talking points, frameworks and tools to help policymakers strengthen supports for beginning educators; communicating research that informs policy in support of induction programs; consulting with federal and state policymakers to write legislation and develop infrastructure that supports robust induction models; authoring policy briefs and white papers; deliver conference presentations; identify states and/or regions that are most conducive to policy reform; and write grant proposals.

  • Education Policy Advisor

    Wisconsin Governor Jim Doyle

    (Government Administration industry)

    October 2004 — June 2006 (1 year 9 months)

    Provided advice & counsel on education policy to Governor Jim Doyle, First Lady Jessica Doyle, senior managers and various office divisions. Served as liaison to the Department of Public Instruction and University of Wisconsin System. Developed policy proposals for consideration in state budget and as legislation. Wrote talking points for Governor’s education-related public appearances and legislative testimony on Governor’s education policy proposals. Interact with education policy leaders—including the State Superintendent, UW System President and education committee chairs in the Legislature—and their senior staff. Spoke publicly at various education policy and constituency group meetings. Participated on a variety of external policy committees and work groups. Interacted with Governor’s Washington, DC office on federal education policy. Wrote/collaborated on successful grant funding proposals. Attend state, regional & national policy meetings on behalf of Governor's Office.

  • Senior Policy Analyst

    National Governors Association, Center for Best Practices

    (Non-Profit; 51-200 employees; Public Policy industry)

    April 2001 — September 2004 (3 years 6 months)

    Employed in Education Division at NGA Center for Best Practices. Managed & worked on grant-funded projects focused on teacher quality, educational leadership, and school health/student achievement. Authored publications on teacher induction and teacher evaluation; wrote teacher quality portion of a web-based clearinghouse on closing the achievement gap. Served as a consultant to state work teams implementing policies and drafting grant proposals on teacher quality & school leadership. Served as a consultant to Governors’ education policy advisors; participated in site visits, facilitated meetings and trainings; and provided written & on-site technical assistance. Planned state policy institutes and roundtable meetings on a host of education policy topics. Contributed to and edited a periodic policy newsletter to Governors’ staff.
