Paul Kuhn’s Experience

  • Singer/Songwriter, Producer, Instrument Designer

    Independent Music

    (Public Company; NWS; Internet industry)

    May 1990 — Present (20 years 7 months)

    I just received a patent and trademark on my new musical instrument, the Cellocaster, which is basically a guitar that you can also play with a bow. It has six strings, is tuned like a guitar, fretted, and has an arched bridge and fingerboard to allow bowing of individual strings. This is the next evolution of the electric guitar! I am hoping to license the design to Fender and Gibson. Limited edition, hand-built Cellocasters are available, custom built to order.

    I recently played the Cellocaster on Dan Bern's title track to the Jonathan Demme movie "Jimmy Carter: Man From Plains." I have produced three recordings, played over 1,000 performances from the Monterey Pop Festival to the HORDE tour, to the Take Root Festival in Amsterdam, to Carnegie Hall. I have opened for Sheryl Crow, Barenaked Ladies, Guster, Whiskey Town, WAR, Warren Zevon, Sonia Dada, Better Than Ezra, the Young Dubliners, Sam Bush and John Cowan, and many others. I have performed with Daryl Stuermer of Genesis, Vertical Horizon singer Matt Scannell, the late Bobby Sheehan of Blues Traveler, Guster, the Samples, and DJ Logic. I am a voting member of the Chicago Chapter of the Grammys. Treehouse's album "Blend," which features the Cellocaster and four songs I wrote, is now available at iTunes and all the other major download services.
