David Chappell’s Summary

25 years of experience in the software industry covering a broad range of roles in leading edge architecture, and spend much of my time of late working with IT leaders to define new strategies for Service Oriented Applications that scale reliably and predictably in corporate cloud and grid environments.

As a public speaker, have delivered 100s of lectures and seminars at over 60 cities worldwide. Well known worldwide for my writings and public lectures on the subjects of Service Oriented Architecture (SOA), Grid computing, the enterprise service bus (ESB), Event Driven Architecture (EDA), Complex Event Processing (CEP), Business Activity Monitoring (BAM), message oriented middleware (MOM), enterprise integration, and have co-authored many advanced Web Services standards.

As author of several books including the O’Reilly Enterprise Service Bus book, Have had tremendous impact on redefining the shape and definition of service infrastructure. Have been a major market influencer on the creation and evolution of the Enterprise Service Bus (ESB) technology category. Posses a strong passion for shaping the future of technology, and enjoy sharing knowledge and experience with others.

Author of several books, including “Enterprise Service Bus” (Oreilly), “Modern SOA Infrastructure” (Prentice-Hall), “SOA Design Patterns” (Prentice-Hall), “Java Message Service” (Oreilly), “Java Web Services” (Oreilly), and “Professional ebXML Foundations” (Wrox).
