Brian Madden’s Summary

Brian Madden is known throughout the world as an opinionated, super technical, fiercely-independent desktop virtualization expert. Seriously, this dude loves desktops. He has like five. (September 2010 update: he now has six.)

He also likes talking and writing about himself and making semi-educated guesses about the future of technology which usually turn out to be widely discredited.

Outside of work, Brian likes to talk. And talk. And talk and talk and talk--filling the silence with everything and nothing at the same time. In early December 2007 his leveraged this "after hours" passion to deliver a rousing 38-minute speech about the economies of user scaling that the the off-shore transcribers later confirmed to be just over 648,000 words long.

When not virtualizing desktops, Brian can be found at the McDonald's in The Mission selling autographed copies of his 2001 bestselling book, "Me and my Meta-Frame, a Life in Screenshots" from the trunk of his '94 Camaro.
