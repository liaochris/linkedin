Clay Cavanaugh’s Summary

I wrote "The FreeLoader Directory" as a way to help individual users and companies weather the recession better by showing them all of the great, totally free software at their disposal. Most makers of free and open source software don't have the budgets to advertise, so they rely on word of mouth. But if users aren't particularly "techie" or don't have the time, they may not frequent the right circles to catch wind of the amazing free stuff that's out there. "The FreeLoader Directory" weeds out all of the useless or dangerous software and gives readers an easy way to find the best freebies for any given need.

Along with spearheading the marketing of "The FreeLoader Directory", I oversee the operations of C-Squared Ltd. publishing and the marketing of all other books and products that C-Squared publishes. I write press releases, administer online marketing campaigns (through Google AdWords, Facebook, MySpace and others), and work on public relations by getting our books into the hands of key readers.

In the course of running the company, I discovered a previously unknown passion for Web design and programming. I've become proficient in CSS and HTML, and, when building a blog, extensively tweak WordPress themes to capture just the right look and feel.

Due to an eclectic education and work history, I am fluent in Japanese (both speaking and reading), and am an expert in both Japanese and Chinese business cultures. I have an MBA from Duke University's Fuqua School of Business, and while pursuing it I participated in a study abroad program in Shanghai, China at the China Europe International Business School (CEIBS). My undergraduate degree is in Computer Engineering, and I still love technology and gadgets more than anything in the world. Like I said, eclectic, right?
