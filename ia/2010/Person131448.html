Marcia Reynolds’s Summary

Marcia Reynolds, PsyD is a recognized expert on how the brain influences our behavior. Her work includes

• Providing one-on-one executive coaching, leadership training and organizational consulting for global corporations, US government agencies, non-profit boards and quasi-government agencies that provide public services.

• Designing customized seminars particularly in the areas of leadership, emotional intelligence, team building, and coaching skills.

• Speaking at conferences. Most recently she is asked to speak at women's conferences on her book, Wander Women, to be released by Berrett-Koehler June 2010 for high-achieving women in today’s workplace.

• Coaching current leaders and top talent employees to be the next senior leaders. She also provides onboarding coaching for new leaders.

Dr. Reynolds a past president of the International Coach Federation and one of the first people in the world to achieve the designation of Master Certified Coach.

Quotes from her book, Outsmart Your Brain, have appeared in Fortune Magazine, Investors Business Daily, Harvard Communications Newsletter, Harvard Management Update, U.S. Business Review, Cosmopolitan, CNN.com, The Los Angeles Times and The New York Times, and she has appeared on ABC World News, National Public Radio, and Japan Nightly News.

Past clients include Medtronic, Nokia, British Telecom, GlaxoSmithKline, American Express, A. P. Moller-Maersk, Ernst & Young, Home National Bank, Microchip Technology and Staples plus many agencies of the U.S. National Institute of Health.

Dr. Reynolds has also spent 16 years working for high tech and health care corporations, providing an experiential foundation to her work. She lives in Phoenix, Arizona.

Marcia Reynolds’s Specialties:

New models of leadership, leading through difficult times, rising-star women in corporations, emotional intelligence, change management, conflict resolution, the brain and behavior, and coaching skills for managers.

  • Author

    Wander Woman

    (Professional Training & Coaching industry)

    2009 — Present (1 year )

    Finally, a book written to meet the needs and provide guidance for today's high-achieving woman.

    There’s a new generation of high-achieving women today—confident, ambitious, accomplished, driven. Yet, as executive coach Marcia Reynolds discovered, many are also stressed, discontent, and frustrated. They’re constantly questioning their purpose, juggling multiple roles, and reevaluating their goals. As a result they’re restless—they move from job to job, from challenge to challenge, almost on impulse. They’re wander women.

    The book (release date June, 2010)
    -- Presents fresh research and powerful stories to give voice to a new generation of women driven by challenge and change
    -- Offers compelling advice on how to make wandering a life strategy, not just a series of unplanned events
    -- Includes probing questions and thought-provoking exercises to help readers find peace in life’s chaos and a strong sense of purpose

    For every woman plagued by frustration and a burning desire for more—“Will what I’ve done ever feel good enough?”—Wander Woman sets the stage to uncover the answers to life’s tough questions about meaning and purpose, significance and value, and the legacy you can leave from a life lived well.

    “Validating and welcome support to high-achieving women and the managers and coaches who seek to help unleash and channel their talent. ‘Wander women’ will find both comfort and challenge here, plus a comprehensive program to address those areas that create frustration in their lives.” --Kathleen Flanagan, founder, Leadership Communication, Inc.
