  • President

    Sibu

    (Health, Wellness and Fitness industry)

    February 2007 — July 2007 (6 months)

    This was an interesting, and abbreviated chapter in my career.

    Sibu, a Network Marketing Company built around a unique drink based on seabuckthorn from Tibet, had consulted with us at their founding. We gave our professional recommendations.

    They went their own way.

    Two years later, the owners came back to us. The company was in trouble, bleeding distributors, and a lot of red ink.

    We jumped into it with a lot of concerted energy. We had to rapidly change nearly everything. Especially important: we conducted a Phase 2 Clinical Trial on the juice. I've been in this industry my entire career, and was exceptionally surprised by the results.

    The seabuckthorn drink had a dramatic effect on blood sugar control. More importantly, it lowered cholesterol counts more than Lipitor, the world's best selling drug. Finally, the subjects in the test lost, on average 7 pounds of body fat, not weight loss, FAT loss, in 30 days.

    With a new plan in hand we outlined the new company to its extraordinary distributors. They loved and embraced it. Unfortunately, the owners could not fund the amounts needed to continue the turnaround, and withdrew the funding.

    To the owners, we wish them the best. To the extraordinary distributors, it was a great ride, and many of you are now lifelong friends.

    A special thanks to the employees of the company, some I inherited, some I brought in--dedicated, hard working, and with the new leadership, exceptionally innovative. (Lindsay, Jamie, Mike, Carol, Ryan, Michelle, Amanda, Bob)

    To my incredible MLM Dream Team: Never has so much happened so fast and so well. You are extraordinary individually and collectively. Laura Gontchar (Internet Development and Marketing), Nick Smith (Nutrition), Dan Bastian (Strategic Vision and Presentations Training), Taylor Jentzsch (Graphic and Web Design), Don Tolman (YES, that Don Tolman--Engrossing Public Presentations), Josh Jentzsch (Video Production), Kris Bowen (Software redesign)

  • COO/Managing Director

    Essanté Corporation

    (Health, Wellness and Fitness industry)

    November 1995 — May 2000 (4 years 7 months)

    This was the beginning of an incredible journey.

    I was approached by a very large, privately held French firm, that had found me via a headhunter company. They were attempting to launch a weight loss company, using MLM as a marketing system. With the CEO in Paris, the CFO in London, and the President in Paradise Valley (near Phoenix), it was a truly challenging management experience.

    Their product line was in disarray. Their marketing system antiquated. Their policies and procedures non-existent. What they had was patience and the capital to do it right.

    We infused a lot of ScaleDown For Life Weight Loss principles into the company. We built a group of innovative whole food herbal supplements, some drinks (the first of their kind in MLM) and some fun food products – all packaged into a lifestyle change program.

    The company had incredible success helping people lose significant weight. It had major impact on blood pressure and on blood sugar control. It was so effective that we had increasing inquiries from physicians wanting to know how we were changing these very serious problems without benefit of drugs. We put together a special binder of materials explaining it all, based upon peer reviewed medical studies.

    My last year with the company kept me on the road 200 days, meeting with larger and larger physician groups.

    The experience left me with a very strong and lasting impression: While supplements were certainly of value, the prime ingredient for success was the direct application of science-based dietary information to weight loss, diabetes, and hypertension.

    After four intense years building and expanding the company, it was time to move on.

    I am grateful for the opportunity and experience. I met some extraordinary individuals that are now lifelong friends. I am indebted to the wonderful people I had the privilege of working with, including Charlene, Kerry, Becky, Tina, Mavis, Margaret, Jeff, Alan, and of course Nick.

    THANK YOU ALL.
