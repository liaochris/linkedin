Jacob Shwirtz’s Summary

Using the 2400 baud modem on my IBM PS/1, I logged onto the Internet for the first time in January, 1993, right after the launch of AOL 1.0 for Windows 3.x. Soon afterward I was interviewed by a journalist who thought I was Jerry Seinfeld because of my screen name.

Eighteen years later I continue to follow my passion for learning, understanding and innovating how the Internet empowers people to share and connect. These days we call it "social networking" but I don't get hung up on catchphrases.

I've gained experience in various areas of the Internet industry: from working at and starting companies that plan, design and build websites for clients to founding and managing destinations themselves. Finally, I've assembled and led teams of writers, moderators and seeders to create, nurture and grow successful communities across the "social" web.

The focus of my career is to craft and then implement the strategies that drive people to interact online - from my early days in New York, through moving to Tel Aviv in 2003, and then back to New York in 2010.

In 2007 I created Definitely Something Ltd., a boutique web agency (http://www.DefinitelySomething.com and http://www.Moderation.Pro). Senior executives at MTV Networks and Tim and Nina Zagat of Zagat Survey rely on the company to concept, build, launch, moderate and grow unique and successful web products, social media channels and digital campaigns.

In late 2009, as an initiative of Definitely Something, we launched TweetBookz (http://www.TweetBookz.com). The service allows Twitter users to customize and print high quality coffee-table books of their tweets. Reception has been amazing, with coverage by NBC's Today Show, the Wall Street Journal, LA Times, Ryan Seacrest and lots more around the world.

In late 2010 I stepped down from the daily work of Definitely Something. I returned to New York and accepted a full-time position at MTV Networks, specializing in the exciting new field of "social viewing."
