Adam Vane’s Summary

Principal of Paragon Global Consulting, a firm dedicated to helping leaders and leadership teams develop their capabilities for extraordinary results. By blending a business perspective with best practices in organization development and behavioral science, he has helped people from over 100 organizations discover sustainable solutions to personal leadership and organizational challenges.

His clients include: Goldman Sachs, United Nations Development Programme, Brooklyn Academy of Music, Citigroup, Catalyst, Montefiore Medical Center, Shearman and Sterling LLP, and Yale University School of Medicine.

As an adjunct faculty member at NYU he teaches a course on conflict management and is one of a select group of faculty at the American Management Association to teach their three-day program, “Responding to Conflict” and “Making the Transition to Management,” an on-boarding program for new leaders. He has also guest lectured at Columbia University and the New School for Social Research on an array of topics, including leadership, negotiation and professional networking.

As an ICF certified executive coach, he conducts strategic one-on-one coaching sessions in areas of leadership, negotiation and relationship management skills. As a team coach and trainer, he has custom designed solutions to address issues in the areas of strategic planning, team effectiveness and professional presence. Clients include senior partners and directors, mid-level associates seeking higher roles and younger professionals seeking to improve their ability to manage up and across the organization.

His other interests include music composition and performance. In his early 20's, Adam toured Europe performing in clubs, concerts, and music festivals, including the International Music Festival in Basel, Switzerland. His pro-bono work has involved facilitating leadership and peer mediation programs for “at-risk” children in schools across the Bronx, Brooklyn, Queens and Yonkers.
