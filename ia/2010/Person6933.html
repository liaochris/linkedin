    Designated by the NJ State Council on the Arts as one of five "Distinguished Arts Organizations" in the state of New Jersey, Playwrights Theatre's Education Department and its programs have become a national model for comprehensive artistic and creative writing curricula. Its main program, the New Jersey Writers Project, reaches 17,000 students a year in all 21 counties of New Jersey. The New Jersey Writers Project is the largest residency program of its kind in the country and since its inception, over 150,000 students have been served through 1,800 residencies at various sites throughout the state.

  • Administrative Director

    Shakespeare At School

    (Performing Arts industry)

    August 2006 — December 2007 (1 year 5 months)

    Shakespeare At School (SAS) is 501(c)(3), non-profit, professional touring educational theatre company of teaching artists dedicated to presenting the works of William shakespeare in an engaging and accessible manner to the young people of New York City.

    By using the works of Shakespeare as an educational tool, the company seeks to illuminate and investigate the connections between timeless literary themes and the world the students live in today.

    All of SAS' services are carefully crafted and executed by trained, professional experts in the field of arts education in accordance with the guidelines provided by the New York City Department of Education's Blueprint for Teaching and Learning in the Arts.

    SAS is a proud member of the American Alliance of Theatre Educators, and the American Educational Research Association.
