Drew Sygit’s Summary

Most consumers shop for a mortgage either by price or convenience.

Some will “Dial for Dollars”, calling numerous lenders in an attempt to get the lowest price. Others will just go with their bank or whoever appears to be the easiest to deal with.

Both these strategies are seriously flawed – they assume a mortgage is a commodity that’s the same no matter where one obtains it, forgetting that the process of getting a mortgage takes a lot of paperwork & time and can be fairly complicated.

You can “sign & drive” for a car within hours, but it still takes weeks to get a mortgage – and many things can go wrong during the process.

In fact, the Housing Crisis has led to a tightening of lending standards and the process of getting a mortgage is getting more difficult. Too many foreclosures are causing underwriters to ask for more and more paperwork and explanations.

For those considering just going to their bank, you may want to Google your bank and check out its reputation for service.

“Too Big to Fail” banks were bailed out by taxpayers, but you’d never know it by how poorly they treat those same taxpayers as customers – constantly raising fees, cutting lines of credit without warning, and don’t even think of trying to ask for a loan modification or short sale if you lose your job later and could use your own bailout help!

So, if you’re looking for a mortgage, you may want to ask yourself if expertise and reputation matter and how they might improve your purchase or refinance experience.
