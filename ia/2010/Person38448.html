Christopher Walsh’s Summary

I started developing websites back in 2000, for a private school that I worked for as a school librarian. A lot of my passion for web development really stems from my interest in database administration, or things that would involve database conceptualization, but not the structures as much as approaching it from a knowledge management perspective. Effective access and storage of information deems well thought out database structures, including object based architecture, data normalization, and straight up common accessibility.
My main goal is to be a guru of sorts in information theory. With my librarian background, I've gained a solid grasp of various information structures, and can hold my own when it comes to analyzing data structures, and applying the concepts I've picked up along the way, and provided solid solutions to all platforms I've encountered throughout my career.
