Marcelo Calbucci’s Summary

Marcelo is the creator of TweepML, an open standard format to share lists of Twitter users and a service for you to create a follow lists with a single click. He's also the publisher of Seattle 2.0, a service dedicated to foster, promote and educate startups on the Seattle area.

Before that, Marcelo as the founder of Sampa, a family web site builder startup, which he founded in 2005 and closed it in mid-2009.

Before Sampa, Marcelo was a development manager at Microsoft where he worked in the MSN division. Marcelo has been developing software professionally and for fun for more than 20 years. He is passionate about using technology to solve people's problems, big or small. Prior to Microsoft, he worked at NEC and before that he was studying Computer Science which he got a degree for. In his spare time Marcelo is an "almost famous" blogger with a personal blog, an entrepreneurship blog, a cooking blog, and a family blog. He also likes to cook, play soccer with friends, and spend time with his wife and two-year-old son.

You can read more on http://blog.calbucci.com

  • Founder & CTO

    Sampa

    (Internet industry)

    February 2005 — August 2009 (4 years 7 months)

    I founded Sampa because I felt disconnect between people’s need for a place on the web where they can safely share what they want and the current solutions available.

    Almost everyone that is “online” has an email address. But how many have a personal or family website? How many create a baby site for their newborn?

    Our vision is to give you all the resources necessary to create an engaging and beautiful website for you, your family, your baby, your wedding and all the big life milestones that you want to share.

    Sampa has a patent-pending integration with email that automatically notifies your visitors of new content on the site. It also gives them a 1-click access to the content, even if the content is private on the website making it suitable for you to invite people on a wide-range of technical skill set, aka, your family!

    And please, send me all the feedback (good and bad) that you can!
