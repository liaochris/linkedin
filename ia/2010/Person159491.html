Patricia Drago’s Summary

Pat founded Drago Consulting LLC to capitalize on over two decades of leadership experience in the insurance industry. Drago Consulting is an advisory & consulting practice focused on arbitration, property/casualty issues, strategy, and leadership.
In 2003, Pat led the divestiture of NATLSCO from Kemper Insurance. As CEO of the new company, Broadspire Services, she led its transition to independent third party administrator and profitable start-up.
Pat joined Kemper June 2000 to lead Client Services, Kemper National Services and NATLSCO, a $400 million claim and safety service platform with 4,000 employees. Under her leadership, external revenue grew 25% and profitability significantly improved, while turnover was halved.
Prior to Kemper, Pat enjoyed a 19-year career with Crum & Forster Insurance, where she assumed progressively greater responsibilities culminating with her appointment to President. From 1993 to 2000, she served on the boards of Crum & Forster Holdings, Inc., North River, and U.S. Fire Insurance Company.
Pat holds a J.D., magna cum laude, from Brooklyn Law School, and a B.A. from SUNY at Stony Brook. Industry board service includes AISG, AIA, and the NICB during its creation through a merger of NATB and ICPI. Pat currently serves as a director of PMA Capital Corp. Prior experience includes chairing the advisory board for Polestar Performance Programs, Inc., serving on the boards of Bellhomme, the Protected Cell Captive Insurance Company, and American Repertory Ballet, and the President’s Advisory Council of the YWCA of Metropolitan Chicago. Pat is a Boardroom Bound Charter Lifetime Friend, and a member of ARIAS-U.S., NACD and the New York State Bar Association. In 2000, she was recognized as one of Business Insurance magazine's "Leading 100 Insurance Women".
