Have you ever wondered what the term "inner child" means, and where your is? Do you feel numb or "too" emotional? Does it feel like you had to grow up to soon, or need help growing up now? Chronological age is not the best way to gauge mental or emotional health. Therapy can help you grow up, or "grow down", as many people who were abused as children or are children of alcoholics or addicts.
Everyone deserves to experience emotional vitality, behavioral sanity, and good-enough self esteem. If emotional regulation isn't learned, emotional health suffers. Being born into stressful (or even traumatic) circumstances is not a child's fault, but out of survival is born the responsibility to acquire healing as well as skills to break any negative personal or family cycles.
Art Psychotherapy can help in emotional thawing, processing, and containment. Therapy at Draw It Out accesses different areas of the brain than talk therapy alone due to the neurobiological processes necessary to create visual art. Art Therapists, who are artists trained in visual arts and mental health therapy (also in chemical dependency counseling in my case), listen with their eyes as well as their ears.

Please read the rest of my profile, and I leave you with a quote:

"Art as a helper in times of trouble, as a means of understanding the conditions of human existence and of facing the frightening aspects of those conditions, as the creation of a meaningful order- these most welcome aids are grasped by people in distress and used by the healers who come to their assistance. But the blessings experienced in therapy can reach further: they can remind artists everywhere what the function of art has been and always will be." Rudolf Arnheim

Karina Ray MA, LMHC, CDP, Art Therapist’s Specialties:

My primary specialties are working with youth, young adults, adults, and families. I am a co-occurring disorders specialist. Additionally I am a WA state LMHC, MHP, CMHS, Art Therapist, and Addiction Counselor who can provide consultation to non-art therapists, youth service agencies, families with a struggling youth, and others. I can provide psycho-education to families, teachers, hospital staff, and others who care for youth and want to gain an understanding of youth developmental needs.
