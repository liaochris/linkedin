Jon Edwards’s Summary

Jon is a creative executive experienced in the entertainment industries and specialized in engineering systems and integrating technologies that have transformed how we entertain & communicate.

Jon Edwards’s Specialties:

Audio Visual system engineering with a complete grasp in control system automation. Design and implementation of GUI for home automation. Corporate boardroom control & automation.

Jon has been a public speaker to the Design, Architectural & Building communities on the impact of new media technologies and broadband on residential design and construction.

Jon Edwards’s Experience

  • CEO

    Sunset Studios Media Solutions, Inc

    (Think Tanks industry)

    June 1985 — Present (24 years 11 months)

    Sunset Studios has evolved over the years to a creative design & technology systems integration firm focused on media & visual presentation technologies.

    Their systems are incorporated into Corporate, Healthcare & Hospitality facilities as well as high-end custom residential automation. Typical projects range from Conference Centers, Boardroom display & teleconference systems to studio quality Digital Cinema.

    Jon's job focus is on the client contacts, design elements and preparing the blueprint documentation while Sunset Studios team members install and program these systems.

    In 2000, Sunset Studios was responsible for all the audiovisual systems integrated in a new 5-star resort built in Santa Barbara. Bacara Resort & Spa was built on 85 acres and consists of 285 rooms and 15 buildings. Amongst the facilities they designed and built, the property has a 300 seat digital cinema and a 90 seat training lab.

  • Technology Innovations

    IdeaHouse Design Marketing Pacific Design Center

    (Design industry)

    June 1995 — September 2000 (5 years 4 months)

    Co-deveolped an outreach marketing program created to introduce architects, designers and elite consumers to the Design Center's showrooms through an innovative marketing venue.

    Fully built home environments contained in the PDC grew to 5 western states Design Centers. Jon's role was to develop national sponsorship in media and technology, integrate the environments and showcase the evolution in lifestyle technology and the broadband revolution through this innovative venue.

    Experienced by over 750 thousand guests during our tenure in Los Angeles, San Francisco, Seattle, Dallas & Houston Design Centers' IdeaHouse installations.

  • Owner

    Beverly Hills Screening Room

    (Entertainment industry)

    1979 — 1991 (12 years )

    In 1979, Jon built a private 35mm screening rental facility on the Sunset Strip for independent producers to view their feature films and production dailies in a plush and private setting.

    Beverly Hills Screening Room was selected in 1982 by EMI as the showcase for their 3-D film system with the assistance of Academy Award Winning Director Murray Lerner. Beverly Hills Screening was the recipient of the Film Advisory Board Award of Excellence in 1982 for showcasing innovative technology and introducing Computer Animation to the Hollywood film community in the late 1970s and early1980s.

    This work resulted in the first use of 3-D computer animation in a feature motion picture with the release of Walt Disney Studios "Tron" in 1981.

    In 1985, Jon founded Sunset Studios as a creative marketing and production company.

  • Director of Sales and Marketing

    AstralVision Communications

    (Entertainment industry)

    July 1979 — February 1984 (4 years 8 months)

    Astralvision Communication was formed as a creative entertainment marketing company presenting innovations in technology to the Film and Television communities. We were selected by Mathmatical Application Group, Inc in 1979 to represent their groundbreaking 3D computer animation technology "Synthavision".

    Many firsts were achieved by the company in these years. Astralvision successfully introduced this technology to Walt Disney Studios who employed for the first time computers in the production of an animated Feature Film. Tron was released in 1981.

    Astralvision was part of the production team responsible for the stereo 3-D computer animation film entitled "From a Seed" directed by Murray Lerner featured at the opening of Walt Disney Epcot Living Imagination Pavillion.

    Astralvision participated in the founding of one of the first computer animation facilities in Hollywood on the lot of Paramount Studios.

    In the early 1980's Jon's expertise as an interactive designer and producer was utilized in the creation of some of the first live-action interactive programming for home entertainment with the VHD Laser Disc format. "You're the Coach" was based on the 1979 Rose Bowl game between Ohio State Buckeyes and USC. The program featured commentary by coaching and player greats Tom Harmon, Mike Garret.

    In 1982 Jon produced the first direct-response 1-800 :30 sec commercial to air on MTV and introduced "Electric Light Voyage", a one-hour VHS of electronics visuals designed to play on TV with your favorite stereo soundtracks.
