  • President & CEO

    Oregon Lodging Association

    (Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    January 2006 — September 2008 (2 years 9 months)

    Oregon Lodging Association is a 501(c)(6) statewide trade association representing more than 1,100 members and a full time staff of four.

    Hired as CEO after having served as a consultant for three years. Led the organization through its most turbulent time in history, taking the board of directors on a year-long exploration of a merger with the Oregon Restaurant Association. Upon the board’s ultimate decision to remain a standalone entity, re-built OLA as a new organization. That rebuilding included an entirely new staff, office relocation, implementation of new and improved membership and financial systems, and enhanced accountability for the overall organization. Over the year the branding and marketing of the association was overhauled, OLA partnered to launch a new travel magazine, and a more effective governmental affairs program was created. Added more than $100,000 to cash reserves in one year.

    Also worked at OLA from 1988-93 as director of member relations and director of governmental affairs. Received President’s Cup for Membership Growth from the American Hotel & Motel Association, 1989 and 1991.

  • Managing Director

    Creative Center, LLC

    (Marketing and Advertising industry)

    2000 — 2005 (5 years )

    Creative Center, owned by the Oregon Restaurant Association, was formed in 2000 to provide communications, graphic design, publishing and marketing for the association, Oregon Restaurant Education Foundation and Oregon Restaurant Member Services Corporation.

    Established new trade magazine and served as publisher (Main Ingredient: 11,000 monthly distribution); directed creative staff on marketing and image enhancement for several non-profit organizations and a select group of business clients; served as the primary marketing partner for Adams & Company, an Oregon political affairs firm; created several hundred pieces of collateral and direct mail during two election cycles; managed business development and year over year revenue growth.

  • Manager, Market Development
