Claudia Girrbach’s Summary

I am now focused on applying the business skills honed in the first half of my career to accelerate the adoption of green best practices. The challenges of deploying green programs are similar to my experiences with emerging technologies. Those skills are directly transferable (skill / green equivalent):

Analyzing problem drivers and then completing diverse research to find solutions that are innovative and practical --- It’s the classic paper compared to plastic and then finding a reusable cloth bag (no matter its origin) is better yet.

Selling new approaches to management --- Make green a viable business model that fits a particular organization’s market position, culture and competencies.

Convincing strategic partners to participate in programs --- Provide a compelling answer to “Green is nice, but what’s in it for me?”

Managing programs to stay focused on scope and benefits to ensure successful delivery --- Deliver green benefits early and continue to iterate

Building of robust operational organization and practices -- Sustain the change

These skills were key to successes achieved as VP & Division CIO for $9B Division / $34 B Corporation and VP Application Delivery for $17B Corporation where I leveraged emerging technology to achieve a competitive advantage:

Worked with Health & Human Resources Government Agency to pioneer Smart Card technology for Electronic Benefits Transfer (EBT) to reduce fraud by 32% and eliminate client stigma.

Developed pioneering use of measurement systems and statistical algorithms to optimize pricing and supplier deliveries with a 37% ROI.

Established debit cards as popular payment option to enhance retailer and bank’s brands. Recognized by Time Magazine as “Best New Technology”.
