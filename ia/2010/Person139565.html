Michael Mayer’s Summary

Over the past 13 years, I have focused my career on eCommerce marketing, business development, people and project management and system development. Currently, I am an eCommerce Manager at Thermo Fisher Scientific, a $10 billion scientific and technical instruments company. I manage 11 global eCommerce sites, 9 content management systems and a team of Internet developers. Under my management, our eCommerce sites have experienced over 40% organic growth (2009 Web sales were $62 million).

Here are some of the projects that I’ve directed at TMO:
o Integrated $20 million acquisition, Davis Instruments, into Web architecture
o Launched four specialty eCommerce sites (i.e. temperatureplanet.com)
o Introduced product reviews, product Q&A and live chat
o Improved site search with autocomplete and long tail search results
o Added tax and shipping rates via UPS, FedEx and back office integrations
o Enhanced customer experience with addition of Web based quoting system
o Configured product feeds to Google, Ariba, Globalspec, SciQuest and suppliers
o Created private label microsites for international dealers (i.e. local language, local pricing, custom branding)
o Built load-balanced, redundant server farm with an offsite, hot swappable disaster recovery environment
o Deployed automated intrusion and performance testing to monitor site availability
o Enhanced site performance with addition of a content delivery network
o Launched integrated Social Media campaign including blog.coleparmer.com, Facebook, Twitter, LinkedIn and YouTube

In my spare time, I created ExpressDrop, a $1.2 million auction drop-off company with 4 physical locations, 16 employees, 3,000 sellers and 30,000 buyers. During our 5 years, we became the 5th largest eBay drop-off franchisor in the world and were featured in Entrepreneur Magazine, FOX News, CBS News and the Chicago Tribune. We also spun off LuxuryDrop, a $100 thousand designer clothing sample sale.
