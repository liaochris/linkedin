Ginny Light’s Summary

Ginny holds two Master’s degrees—a Master of Arts (2001) and a Master of Philosophy (2005) in English—both from Fordham University. She was a Teaching Associate of Composition/Rhetoric and a tutor in Fordham University's Writing Center where she learned how to develop instructional materials, coordinate educational content, and incorporate current technology to assist her students' learning.

Ginny currently tutors privately and attends Pace University, where she plans to obtain her New York State Teaching Certification in Adolescent Education. She is also the chairperson of the Westchester Chapter of the Women@Work Network and an active member of the Armonk Players.

A scholar and lifelong learner, Ginny has authored and presented papers at national literature symposiums, including the: Group for Early Modern Cultural Studies (GEMCS) (1999, 2000), The Midwestern Conference on Film, Language, and Literature (MCFLL) (2001), The American Literature Association (ALA) (2004), The Central New York Conference on Language and Literature (CNYCLL) (2004), and The Antebellum American Women Writers and the City Conference (2005).

She has also been an active participant in several author societies, including: The Louisa May Alcott Society, The Society for the Study of American Women Writers (SSAWW), The Edith Wharton Society, The Catharine Maria Sedgwick Society (former VP, Comm's), and The Stowe Society (former VP, Comm's).
