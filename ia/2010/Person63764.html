Elizabeth Shell’s Summary

I create journalistic, visual documentaries on issues that are interesting and relevant.

The media landscape is changing. Building on this transition, I'm finding new ways to tell stories in compelling ways that are appropriate for the audience and type of story. Together with a colleague I created LayeredMedia, a free, open-source tool multimedia producers can use to layer different types of media assets in a stand-alone video player (think SoundSlides with a video base and an unlimited amount and type of media that can be layered on top). This provides the audience with a deeper, more synergistic experience.

We developed LayeredMedia to tell the story of Hispanic students in the U.S. public education system because we had so much great content in all sorts of different forms. Instead of giving slideshows, audio, datasets, text and videos to the audience in a sequential order, we layered the additional assets on top of the video.

The end product is a 30-minute video and motion graphic documentary. I'd love you to see it and tell me what you think: http://asu.news21.com/building-success/
