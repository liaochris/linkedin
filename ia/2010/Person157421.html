Olegario "Ollie" Cantos VII’s Summary

Olegario "Ollie" D. Cantos VII has dedicated his life toward helping to advance equality of opportunity and access for this nation's more than 50 million Americans with disabilities. An attorney, public speaker, advocate, networker, and leader, his efforts to foster change stem from efforts to bring leaders together via a collaborative process to maximize community impact and promote full participation of children and adults with disabilities in every aspect of societal life.

Mr. Cantos has the unique distinction of being a presidential appointee and career attorney all at the same time. He also dedicates his time to community service and other endeavors. Over his lifetime thus far, he has spoken to audiences totaling more than 50,000. Issues of focus have included criminal justice (victims' side), education, emergency preparedness, employment, transportation, and leadership development. He also has a high Internet presence as Google searches will show. Through the years, he has appeared on radio and television and in newspapers and magazines.

Within an entrepreneurial context, Mr. Cantos now undertakes a series of paid speaking engagements in various parts of the country.

Olegario "Ollie" Cantos VII’s Specialties:

Civil rights law with a focus on individuals with disabilities, network-building and leadership development, and community service promotion.

  • Board Member

    Community Lodgings

    (Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    January 2010 — Present (2 months)

    In 1987, Community Lodgings was founded by eight churches based in Alexandria, VA, with a mission to lift families from homelessness and instability to independence and self-sufficiency. Our Transitional Housing Program currently provides housing and intensive case management services for 14 homeless families empowering them through counseling, skills and stability over a two-year period. To further address the mission of self-sufficiency, Community Lodgings opened a Family Learning Center in 1998 to provide courses in computer literacy and ESL for transitional clients and other disadvantaged adults in the city; we currently serve 50 adults through this program to increase their employability. In 2002, we expanded the Family Learning Center to provide full-family services and added a Youth Education Program (YEP) for our homeless kids and other at-risk children in the community. In Spring 2008 the Family Learning Center space was doubled in size, allowing the program to expand and provide more instructional time for more families and children each day. Now, 78 at-risk youth and 50 adults attend the center daily. With trusted bilingual staff, and growing programs and services, Community Lodgings is Alexandria’s best resource for self-help and life improvement. Through our Transitional and Affordable Housing, and Youth and Adult Education Programs – all within walking distance of the community – we touch over 250 lives per week.
