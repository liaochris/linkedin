ben capozzi’s Summary

Ben Capozzi earned his BFA in Art & Art History from Virginia Tech in 2005 after a serendipitous journey in and out of college, including a 3-year stint in the comic book business (culminating in 1998’s Diamond Award for Best New Publisher of the Year), and a short time doing brand design and development for Wright Massey and Papa-John’s.

After graduation he worked in Learning Technologies at Virginia Tech’s New Media Center, performing digital media triage and project management for faculty, staff, and student clients while supervising a crack-team of undergrad lab assistants. In 2007 he added teaching for VPI’s Faculty Development Institute to his responsibilities and became “the Photoshop guy.”

After attending the HEC’s June 2008 conference on “Building Communities Through the Arts” he was asked by Amy Lammerts and Patricia Ward to head the curriculum development for the Business of Art & Design’s then Graphic Design initiative, which he has since re-visioned as the Digital Art & Design program — an innovative curriculum stressing digital ability, multimedia communication, and collaborative coursework with real-world partners.

When not working as a creative professional serving K-12 and Higher Education, teaching Graphic Arts to Halifax County High schoolers Monday through Friday, or doing grad school coursework in Education Technology, he continues to make pictures including photography around the region and the occasional editorial illustration for 16 Blocks Magazine in his native New River Valley.
