  • Capital Planning

    Merck & Co, Inc

    (Public Company; MRK; Pharmaceuticals industry)

    March 1988 — April 2009 (21 years 2 months)

    Variety of positions (1988 through current) from project management through development and management of annual and long range Capital Plans for the Research Division.
    • Reduced Capital Plan from $1 billion to less than $200 million annually (2000 – 2005) by employing a portfolio of capital effectiveness initiatives with engineering, more aggressive capital management processes, and expanded use of sourcing to improve a critical return on assets metric.

    • Served as the primary capital planning liaison for global leaders at multiple US sites and key research sites in Canada, England, France, Italy, and smaller sites in South America and Japan.

    • Led the development and subsequent evolution of a Capital Review Committee, which reviewed and approved all projects > $1 million with a focus on project-specific drivers as well as alignment with local and global strategies.

    • Led rapid increase in the Capital Plan from $75 million to $1 billion annually during a period of accelerated company growth (1994-1999); processing requirements more than doubled to ~ 4,000 projects per year while only adding one additional headcount.

    • Led a Six Sigma project to design and establish a standardized laboratory technology selection and acquisition process, projected to save millions of dollars (5-10% of the annual lifecycle cost).
