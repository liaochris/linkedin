Linda Carr, Ph.D.’s Summary

Dr. Linda Carr is an accomplished faculty developer, health professions educator, and educational consultant with extensive experience working with physicians and other health science professionals who teach. She received her Ph.D. from The University of Michigan in Ann Arbor. Her research on innovations in learning and teaching in higher education included visiting several medical schools involved in implementing major curriculum reform.

Dr. Carr has coached and mentored new and experienced faculty using a multi-faceted approach that includes one-on-one coaching, seminars, workshops, faculty learning communities, annual teaching symposia, and excellent online academic resources.

Linda Carr has successfully completed over 25 years of teaching and establishing educational programs from start-up to accreditation. She has presented her work at numerous national conferences and has given nearly 100 seminars and workshops to professional health science audiences (physicians, nurses, residents, residency program coordinators, medical students, physician assistants, pharmacists, and veterinarians) on improving the quality of health science education.

Linda Carr's Specialties:
Faculty Development, Learning & Teaching, Curriculum Design, Faculty Learning Communities, ACGME Competencies, Distance Learning, Organization & Management, Career Development, Coaching for Successful Passage of National Board Exams.

Linda Carr's Specialized Training: Program for Educators in Healthcare Professions (Harvard Macy Institute), Faculty Learning Community Developers’ and Facilitators’ Institute (Miami University), University Leadership Institute (University of Arizona), Tutor Training for Problem-Based Learning (University of New Mexico).
