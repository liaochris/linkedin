Chris Owens’s Summary

Chris Owens is the General Manager for Microsoft’s global real estate and facilities function. He is responsible for planning, delivery and operations of Microsoft’s worldwide real estate portfolio, which comprises 25M square feet and over 400 facilities across 103 countries. This responsibility involves nurturing a constant understanding of corporate and business unit goals and drivers, and translating those into key real estate strategies and programs. Chris is responsible for directing the full gamut of real estate life cycle activities, including planning (portfolio, country, site), acquisitions and dispositions, transaction management, design and construction, and facility and services operations. Chris also directs several key supporting functions, such as real estate procurement and communications groups.

During his tenure at Microsoft, Chris has directed several significant strategic initiatives that have elevated the real estate function into a world class corporate real estate operation. He successfully conceived and directed adoption of the Workplace Advantage program, which entailed a fundamental re-engineering of Microsoft’s workplace strategy that focuses on driving increased business productivity. The program focuses on driving productivity through greater design flexibility to align to business work process, increased support for mobile/flexible work styles, and greater attention to branding and environmental sustainability. The success of this initiative rested on deep change management and strategic communications efforts.

Chris also directed the global integration of the worldwide real estate team. Chris designed a globally integrated organization model, evangelized it successfully with global business leaders, and successfully executed the transition process through a strong change management and communications program.
