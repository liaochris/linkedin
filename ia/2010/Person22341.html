Bernadette Bezy’s Summary

I am an Environmental Project Manager and Senior Aquatic/Wetland Biologist with over twelve years of environmental compliance, permitting, and field survey experience in California, the western US, and Latin America. Currently I lead the Environmental Group at ECO:LOGIC.

I successfully assist public and private entities navigate and comply with environmental regulations including the:

• National Environmental Policy Act (NEPA)
• California Environmental Quality Act (CEQA)
• US Army Corps of Engineers Clean Water Act
• California Department of Fish and Game Code Section
1600 et seq (Streambed Alteration Agreements)
• California Regional Water Quality Control Board Clean
Water Act Section 401 Certifications
• “CEQA Plus” State Revolving Fund,
• USDA Federal Cross -Cutting processes,
• Caltrans Natural Environmental Studies,
• Federal Energy Regulatory Commission filings,
• World Bank Environmental Regulations, and
• Country-specific environmental laws.

I manage permitting efforts on a wide array of infrastructure and conservation projects including:

• Water and wastewater infrastructure
• Linear Projects (Fiber-optic cables, transmission lines,
pipelines, transportation projects)
• Alternative Energy
• Restoration and Fish Screen/Fish ladder Projects

In the field I am proficient in special-status species surveys, restoration, field research, stream assessments, and wetland and riparian delineations. I have conducted surveys for, and managed diverse multi-faceted infrastructure projects (including over 10,000 miles of linear projects) in coastal areas, deserts, valleys, mtns, and stream, wetland, and marine environments. I am proficient in the Surface Water Ambient Monitoring Program (SWAMP) protocol for stream studies and SCUBA surveys. I am also an ISA certified arborist.

Bernadette Bezy’s Specialties:

I specialize in California, Western US, and international environmental compliance, aquatic research (marine and freshwater), wildlife surveys, and GIS mapping. I have conducted surveys for: wetlands/vernal pools, amphibians ( Red-legged frog, CTS, etc.), reptiles (WTP, GGS, desert tortoise, CA horned lizard), valley elderberry longhorn beetle, anadromous fish, coral reefs, rare plants, avian species (Swainson's hawk, sandhill crane, etc), oak, riparian tree species, among others.
