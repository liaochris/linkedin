Robb Barnitt’s Summary

Broad based 12-year veteran of clean tech with clear understanding of technical, policy and market constraints/opportunities.

Advanced vehicle technology and fuels researcher at NREL. My work helps develop and refine alternative fuel and advanced propulsion technologies, with an end goal of commercialization and market penetration.

6-year veteran of clean tech entrepreneurial ventures, serving in advisory capacity during bootstrap phase, then on a contract basis (to Amyris Biotechnologies and Propel Fuels) before transitioning to part-time employment at VC-funded, entrepreneurial, revenue generating clean tech venture.

Robb Barnitt’s Experience

  • Senior Project Engineer

    NREL

    (Government Agency; Renewables & Environment industry)

    April 2004 — Present (6 years 2 months)

    Leads staff and research related to Vehicle Systems Analysis, within the Transportation Technologies and Systems research group at the National Renewable Energy Laboratory (NREL). Focus on analysis of performance/cost trade-offs associated with design configuration variations around electric drive components and energy storage (batteries). Analyses are accomplished using a range of vehicle and subsystem simulation tools, modeling hybrid electric (HEV), plug-in hybrid electric (PHEV), and battery electric (EV) vehicles. Responsible for business development and formation of technical collaborations with industry, academia, other government agencies, and regulators. Oversees combined DOE and external annual funding of over $2M.
