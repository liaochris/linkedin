Emre Sokullu’s Summary

Emre Sokullu is the founder and CEO of GROU.PS, the world's largest free do-it-yourself social networking platform.

Under Emre's management, GROU.PS raised $2m in venture capital, built a strong team of 15 people and has become the most popular free do-it-yourself social networking destination with more than 3m registered members, 12m+ monthly unique visitors and 100m+ monthly page views - 25% of its traffic originating from USA.

GROU.PS has brought countless innovations to its space, including ActivityRank (the very first point&reward system), Social Beans (skeleton portability standard across social platforms) the very first subscription based business model.

Prior to founding GROU.PS, Emre managed the West-Coast and blogosphere-wide marketing efforts of the $25M funded semantic search startup hakia, wrote for one of the world's top 20 blogs, ReadWriteWeb, and took a temporary research position at Wordnet Project in Princeton University.

Emre's first project Turkix, which was a Windows-like Linux distribution for the Turkic desktop OS market, became a part of Armador AS and sold bundled with Turkey's leading laptop producer Datron's mainstream models. He also served in thin client transformation of Tupras, one of Europe's biggest energy companies.

Emre is a big open source entusiast. He led the local efforts of Apache Software Foundation in Turkey and participated many international open source projects like KDE, Mandriva, Red Hat, RPM.

Emre can be found blogging at http://www.emresokullu.com
