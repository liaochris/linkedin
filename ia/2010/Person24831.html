Chris Blatnick’s Summary

My name is Chris Blatnick and I have worked as a collaborative technologies architect for over 13 years, mainly utilizing IBM and Lotus software. I am passionate about usability, productivity and cognitive science...why our brains work like they do. You can often find me on my blog (http://interfacematters.com), waxing poetic (or at least trying to) on the virtues of good design within Lotus Notes.

I have architected hundreds of collaborative and knowledge sharing applications for many corporations that combined have generated millions of dollars in savings, both in hard dollars and in people hours.

I'm also an avid mind mapper and technology evangelist. I believe that using technology in the right way, for the right purpose, can greatly enrich our lives. I have a wealth of experience helping companies deploy collaborative technologies with a focus on the organizational culture. I love teaching end users how to be productive with the tools they use everyday.

Throughout my work history, I'm best known for deploying simple and innovative solutions that truly focus on the end user. I inject this drive to improve the user experience into all the work I do, from the very beginning phases of a project all the way through to project delivery. My goal is to always exceed the end users' expectations and turn them into raving fans!

Chris Blatnick’s Specialties:

I am considered an expert in collaborative technologies, having worked as software developer in this area for over a decade. Most of my efforts are focused on application design and development, user interface design, and business process reengineering.
