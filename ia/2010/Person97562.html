Julie Frost’s Summary

I have 14+ years experience in the Music/Arts/Publishing industry with a background in Journalism and Dance. I initially moved to Austin, Texas in 1993 to finish a degree in Journalism that I began at Oklahoma University. My goal was to eventually write about Music and the Performing Arts, but I quickly gravitated to the business and development side of it all. I had the writing skills down, a passion for Music and Performing Arts, and a knack for business, development, management, and production. So, I studied Commercial Music Management and started working my way up. I since have built quite a resume that has included all levels in these areas - too many to mention here, but all have a common denominator: a love for the art of creation.

Highlights of accomplishments: Launched internationally successful indie record label (Dialtone Records) and produced two award-winning CDs; authored feature article in best-selling book distributed worldwide ('09 Songwriter's Market published by F+W Media); assisted in fundraising over $3 million for the arts in Texas and launching premiere Texas Medal of Arts Awards featuring such artists as Willie Nelson and Lyle Lovett; launched the premiere Antone's Blues Festival featuring John Lee Hooker; operated a Ballet academy (300 students) and perfoming company as Assistant Director; represented labels and artists at Midem-France, SxSW, NxNE, International Folk Alliance Fest, and the W.C. Handy Awards; booked tours for artists at legendary venues such as Antone's, Cactus Cafe, and Saxon Pub in Austin, The Living Room and The Apollo Theatre in NYC, The Mint and Genghis Cohen in L.A., Jazzfest in New Orleans, and Chesterfield's in Paris; generated significant press and radio play for artists internationally; and assisted in developing an indie music trade association based in New Orleans (L.I.M.M.A.).

Julie Frost’s Specialties:

Project management, development and A&R, booking, marketing and strategy, public relations, publicity, promotions, production, event planning, contract negotiations, publishing and administration, distribution, consulting, non-profit grant writing, professional writing/editing, and photography.
