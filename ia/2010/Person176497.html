Roger Bentley’s Summary

Bentley Communications helps companies -- from start-ups to global Fortune 500 firms -- communicate convincingly with customers, media, employees and other stakeholders.

Bentley helps clients build awareness, establish brand differentiation, enhance employee engagement, manage change and communicate credibly during times of crisis.

Previously, he was responsible for developing the global communications strategy and plans to support deployment of major business process changes to 75 business units in 66 countries for Cargill, the largest private company in the U.S.
He led a global team of ten communicators to localize and implement all communications relating to the largest changes in the company’s 150-year history.
Bentley developed the intranet strategy for the program, and led internal communications to the thousand-member team.

As Senior Director of Corporate Communications for CSL Behring, a $2 billion global biotherapies company, he led the development and implementation of the company’s internal and external corporate communications strategies including corporate positioning, brand image, issues management and public relations. He also led the company's crisis management team.

Previously, he held leadership roles at Weber Shandwick where he helped numerous clients build awareness and brand recognition.

At Digital Equipment Corporation, he held numerous public relations and corporate communications positions, including managing the advertising and branding for their personal computer division, directing all communications for their service industries division across Europe and leading worldwide communications for the company’s products division.
