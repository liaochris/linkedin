Carrie Peacock’s Summary

Carrie became a registered dietitian in 1994 and worked as a clinical dietitian and food service manager at Oregon State Hospital. The hospital food service produced more than 2,400 meals each day, exposing Carrie to large-scale menu planning and food production. In the clinical aspect of her work she counseled patients on their individual nutritional needs and developed menus and recipes to meet their requirements.

A love of cooking and an emerging talent for combining flavors led Carrie to persue a degree in Culinary Arts from Western Culinary Institute. She graduated 2002 with High Honors and opened her kitchen as Peacock Personal Chef, delivering fresh, healthy, delicious meals to your home or office. She and her staff prepare meals daily from her commercial kitchen in Southwest Portland.

With 13 years of experience creating amazing flavors and her friendly customer service-oriented approach, Carrie and her staff will plan and prepare meals that will thrill your palette regardless of your needs.

Carrie Peacock’s Specialties:

Certified in Adult Weight Management by the American Dietetic Association.

Her master's thesis is considered to be a significant source of data by the Institute of Medicine and, when reviewed with other research, justified the increase of the RDA for folate from 180 micrograms to 400 micrograms per day in 1998.
