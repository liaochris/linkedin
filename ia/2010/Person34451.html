Colin Brumelle’s Summary

Brumelle is the founder and developer of BirdHerd (http://birdherd.com), a twitter application that makes it easy for a group or team of people to manage a single Twitter account. BirdHerd is still in closed beta, but has already received rave reviews from tech media like:
TechCrunch (http://tcrn.ch/birdherd) ReadWriteWeb, (http://bit.ly/62i2qW) and CNET (http://bit.ly/94TXXc)

Brumelle also writes about the intersection of music and technology at his blog, MixedContent (http://mixedcontent.com) and has spots available for select freelance consulting and web development work.

Previously, Brumelle was the founder and CTO of Rilli (http://rilli.com), an exciting web application that aimed to change people's relationship with their city, their friends, and the way they see them both.

Before that, Brumelle was a Partner and Product Manager at Bryght (http://bryght.com), a Vancouver, BC startup focused on offering pushbutton Drupal hosting. The Bryght platform was used to power 1000s of Drupal sites for clients such as GreenPeace, the US Navy and the B&B around the corner. Bryght was acquired in 2007.

Before joining Bryght in 2005, Brumelle led a double life: programmer by day, musician by night. As a professional guitarist for over 10 years, he managed and played guitar with his band Greenroom, a 7-piece jazz/funk ensemble.

A "Jack of all trades" type, Brumelle is equally at home coding genetic algorithms in Ruby, consulting on community strategy, designing a intuitive interface for a web application, or discussing the sociological implications of Jimmy Hendrix's performance of the Star Spangled Banner.

Currently living in Vancouver BC, and San Francisco with his wife and son, Brumelle enjoys rock climbing, biking, and double espressos.
