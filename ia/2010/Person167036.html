Quinn Place, CPA’s Summary

The CPA certification is designed to prove that one can think inside the box; the CMA certification, on the other hand, is about thinking outside the box. When one can understand the Big Picture and go beyond the numbers, that's where value is created.

Quinn is a motivated and goal-orientated person. After she achieved success in her CPA certification, she was not content to sit back and relax. Broad knowledge enables her to "see" more problems and opportunties in accounting/financial information, so she decided to start the CMA certification process. She completed the hardest part of the CMA exam (Management Accounting and Reporting) on February 17. Passing this part of the exam has demonstrated her ability to perform budgeting, analyze the cost structure, solve problems in some very challenging situations in the real world, and to evaluate and improve business processes.

Her goal is to become a valuable CPA & CMA, to work with decision makers and improve their company's processes. Changing the way people do things may not be easy, but can be done. That's why she plans to finish her MBA education after she completes the CMA exam.
