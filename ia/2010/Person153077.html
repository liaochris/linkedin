Nelson Scott Smith’s Summary

As an architect and planner, I'm a practitioner, teacher, and critic. I founded the Artichoke Design Company to research and advance architecture and planning ideas and designs. It is structured to be nimble, accessible, generous, and alert to unconventional connections.

Artichoke Design Company reflects my experience and continued interests: the planning and design of communities, colleges and universities, workplaces, and homes and retreats. Much of my work has been within complex campus or urban contexts and in landscapes of great presence. I have worked with many visionary clients.

I believe architecture and urban design are art forms that profoundly shape our lives and our definition of beauty. Both should advance design to empower people and environments, and to enrich experiences and places with clarity, function, and integrity.

Advocacy and research for sustainable practices are basic to my view of the future. This view is a foundation for my work. It opens the way to a coherent, informed outlook for design and planning. I approach campus planning as a means to realize sustainable futures for institutions.
