Brian Sipes, AIA, LEED AP’s Summary

I joined Zehren and Associates in the spring of 1994 after arriving in Avon from the Midwest and have held a number of positions in the firm since then. I am currently a principal and share the responsibility for managing the firm. My work has included residential, commercial and planning projects in several locations in Colorado and also in Utah, California, South Carolina and Korea.

The things that really get me jazzed up are sustainable design, public processes and community and regional planning. Currently I am more than mid way through serving my second term on the Avon Town Council as the Mayor Pro-Tem.

Last summer my wife and I installed 3.675 kwh of solar panels on the roof of our townhome in Avon. The output of our system is posted online at:
http://enlighten.enphaseenergy.com/public/systems/2c4M1279

During my time on the town council, Avon has seen a renaissance and a resurgence of public interest and support. The town has taken a very long range view of development and committed to renewed planning efforts that include an update to its comprehensive plan, the creation of many sub-area plans, a re-alignment of a major street to give proper frontage to its downtown public park, and has plans for a revitalized downtown core and a new main street.

Within Zehren and Associates, I led the efforts on many significant and complicated, but successful projects involving multiple stakeholders and difficult circumstances including most recently, the Manor Vail Lodge in Vail. I approach every planning or architecture project with a belief that the best results come from collaborative efforts, from building consensus and an understanding that seemingly impossible tasks can always be broken into smaller achievable tasks. My focus in Architecture school was on sustainable design and I have devoted my efforts on improving the lives of people through design that is better in tune with our environment ever since.
