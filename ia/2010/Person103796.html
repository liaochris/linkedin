Jonathan Flowers’s Summary

Strategic leader who has delivered in senior consultancy and top-team management roles in private, public and voluntary sectors. Applying and developing this experience with pre-eminent UK public sector exec resourcing firm, specialising in Local Government sector, and organisational development and top-team facilitation cross-sectors.

Jonathan Flowers’s Specialties:

Organisational development (in the sense which encompasses competitive strategy, culture, performance management as well as structures and staff development)
Senior public-sector search and selection
Partnerships: public-public public-private private-private
Managing innovation

  • Chair, Treasurer, Trustee

    (Arts and Crafts industry)

  • Director of Culture Change

    London Borough of Lambeth

    (Government Agency; Government Administration industry)

    October 2001 — June 2003 (1 year 9 months)

    Line responsibility for HR, Communications & Marketing, and a Quality/Customer Service team. Strategic Management Board member. Chaired Culture Change Board. Led a wide variety of initiatives around management, people management, leadership and culture change.

  • Commercial Development Director

    NatWest Card Services

    (Public Company; Banking industry)

    1998 — 2000 (2 years )

    Led a team varying from 30-40 in size developing highly radical partnership propositions which leveraged NatWest competencies around information processing and card-based infrastructure into very different markets. We used innovative ways of developing exceptionally customer-centric propositions. Unfortunately our work was terminated by the RBS takeover but some of our ideas have subsequently come to pass, implemented by other organisations.

  • (Managing) Director

    NatWest Consultancy

    (Public Company; Banking industry)

    1996 — 1998 (2 years )

    NatWest Consultancy was the 120-strong internal management consultancy for the NatWest Group, and was probably the leading internal management consultancy of its time, certainly in the UK, possibly further afield. It had core competencies in analytics (operational research, statistics and business modelling), management information, strategy, change management and process redesign. Innovative features included a matrix structure which had clear leadership roles across the dimensions of client, business process, and staff mentoring; and a reward system based on personal balanced scorecards including a direct linkage to client feedback on every assignment.
