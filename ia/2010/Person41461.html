Mark Stout’s Summary

Mark Stout is a senior technology professional with over 15 years in high tech and renewable energy positions.

Mr. Stout keeps current on PV solar technology knowledge and maintains industry-wide technology procurement relationships. He applies years of experience with solar project capital cost estimating, from distributed generation to utility-scale. Mr. Stout is an expert on renewable resource data in US, and plays a lead role in defining resource measurement standards for Cleantech America's meteorological monitoring equipment.

During graduate work at the UC Berkeley Energy and Resources Group, Mr. Stout interned at the Union of Concerned Scientists, campaigning for the California Renewable Power Portfolio Standard, which was signed into law in 2002. He later worked as a California Public Utilities Regulatory Analyst with the Office of Ratepayer Advocates.

Mr. Stout is a Professional Member of the American Solar Energy Society, and a long-time promoter of solar power for clean air, energy independence, and job creation. He coordinated the first-ever Fresno Solar Homes Tour, winning NorCal Solar's "Best New Solar Tour 2005" award. Before joining Cleantech America, he handled commercial sales with the oldest and largest locally owned solar power contractor in the Central California.

Mr. Stout began his career in computer hardware, firmware, and software development. As an R&D Engineer at Compaq Computer, he played a leading role in the cost reduction and specification of a single-chip, high-fidelity audio codec, which attracted multiple manufacturers and has been widely deployed. As a Senior Systems Software Design Engineer at Crystal Semiconductor, he then went on to develop the device drivers to bring this audio codec to market.

Mr. Stout graduated Summa Cum Laude in Electrical Engineering from the University of Illinois - Urbana, and earned his Master's degree in Energy and Resources from UC Berkeley.
