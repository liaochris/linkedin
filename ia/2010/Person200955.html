Terence Keane’s Summary

I am an accomplished administrator and experienced leader with particular expertise in communications and marketing, publications and public relations, branding and strategic planning, and crisis management. I have served grassroots, national, and global nonprofits on both coasts with an emphasis on arts, environmental, and health organizations.

I’ve served as editor-in-chief of monthly, quarterly, and annual publications, as well as launching, maintaining, and supervising websites and blogs. I’ve worked successfully with countless media outlets, including more than 600 radio stations, 125 television stations, and innumerable newspapers worldwide. Having been blessed with good mentors at a formative age, I take particular pride in my history as a supervisor, trainer, speaker, and consultant for nonprofit employees across the country.

Terence Keane’s Experience

  • Director of Public Relations / Associate Director of Marketing & Communications

    Berkeley Repertory Theatre

    (Non-Profit; Performing Arts industry)

    October 2004 — Present (6 years 3 months)

    Six years ago, I decided to return to my first love — theater — and became Director of Public Relations at the Tony Award-winning Berkeley Repertory Theatre. In my years with the company, I've increased income, reduced costs, and tripled productivity. More importantly, I've transformed the organization’s national reputation by seizing the airwaves from broadcasters like ABC, CBS, NBC, NPR, PBS, and the BBC and pestering prominent reporters at publications like the New York Times, the LA Times, The London Guardian, the International Herald Tribune, USA Today, Rolling Stone, Entertainment Weekly, Variety, and the Wall Street Journal.

    I have a successful track record with every aspect of the job: writing creative releases, pitching irresistible stories, coordinating important interviews, cultivating reliable relationships, editing eye-catching B-roll, and art directing stunning photo shoots. I have proven expertise in crisis management with precise messaging for protests, catastrophes, etc. I also supervise the production of numerous publications and wrote a thorough history of the theater after establishing extensive online and offline archives to safeguard the organization's legacy.

  • Literary Manager

    SF Playhouse

    (Performing Arts industry)

    January 2005 — September 2008 (3 years 9 months)

    I served as the literary manager for SF Playhouse, an intimate, professional theater company in San Francisco's Union Square. For four seasons, I worked with the artistic director to select scripts and curate an eclectic slate of shows for smart audiences. During my time with the Playhouse, we produced four world premieres and eight West Coast premieres. One of these plays went on to win the Will Glickman Award for Playwriting and was named Outstanding New Play at the New York Fringe Festival.

  • Director of Communications

    National Multiple Sclerosis Society

    (Non-Profit; Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    February 1996 — September 2004 (8 years 8 months)

    Director of Communications (Oct 2000 - Sept 2004)
    Communications Manager (Sept 1997 - Aug 1999)
    Publications Coordinator (Sept 1996 - Aug 1997)
    Programs Assistant (Feb 1996 - Aug 1996)

    Except for a sabbatical as a Fulbright teaching assistant, I dedicated eight years to the local chapter of the National Multiple Sclerosis Society. Serving in four positions that were created specifically for me, I built a strategic, systemic, and successful communications program from scratch. I realized my vision in many ways: by placing stories that reached millions through the media, by producing attractive and intelligent publications, by launching an informative and interactive website, and by managing a million-dollar ad campaign that proclaimed, “MS: It’s not a software company.” Along the way, my team earned 17 national awards and increased public awareness of our cause by a proven 175 percent.

  • Odd jobs

    Back in the day...

    (Writing and Editing industry)

    1989 — 1995 (6 years )

    As a young man, I satisfied my wanderlust through short-term positions with respected organizations such as the American Museum of Natural History, Louisiana State University, the National Audubon Society, the University of California at Davis, and the Big Apple Circus.

    Back in the day, I collected paychecks at a ranch in the Rockies, a circus in New England, a high school in the Alps, an organic farm in Ireland, a village dump in the Hamptons, and a California lab teeming with live rattlesnakes — plus, once upon a time, your tax dollars paid me to sit on the bayou and look at birds. I even spent two months volunteering on a tiny island in the Atlantic which was home to 20,000 terns and 15 researchers. Yet, despite rumors at a recent reunion I didn't attend, I've never been a rodeo clown.
