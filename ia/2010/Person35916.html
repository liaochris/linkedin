Charles D. Jackson’s Summary

A native of Baltimore, MD and graduate of Archbishop Curley High School I attended Emmanuel College in Boston, MA from 2002-2006 where I was Vice President of the Black Student Union (2004), Diversity Ambassador of Emmanuel College to the Kellogg Fellows (2004). I also interned as Director of Multicultural Programming at Wentworth Institute of Technology in Boston, MA. During those years at Emmanuel I found I had an affinity for diversity and inclusion issues especially those dealing with race. After graduating Emmanuel I went onto NTL Institute's Diversity Practitioner Certificate Program, where I trained under the leaders in Organization Development, including Dr. Cathy Royal, Dr.Michael Brazzel and Rick Huntley. I was trained to lead, direct, consult and teach on many issues including: racism, sexism, heterosexism, ableism, ageism, religious oppression and ethnic oppression.

As I was being trained for my vocation in Diversity & Inclusion, I also begin to learn about how to become financially free with Real Estate Investing. After reading many books and networking on real estate investing I chose my second path to run concurrently with diversity & inclusion. I believe in this world there is more than one choice. My goal is to help my family, community, church and all those who I encounter to live their best lives, be it through diversity & inclusion training or finding the best deals for others through wholesaling properties.
