Claudia Heilbrunn’s Summary

Claudia Heilbrunn is the owner of Significant Self Coaching.

Claudia is a certified life coach, author, speaker, and parenting expert who helps first time moms who feel overwhelmed, frazzled and confused become calm and confident mothers who make the right choices for themselves and their kids.

Claudia's background and experience:

Claudia’s passion for helping moms to feel calm and confident was borne out of her own efforts to be a ‘perfect’ mother, and the feelings of deficiency that she felt when she failed to meet perceived expectations. She decided to interview a multitude of moms to discover how other women experienced motherhood. She found that moms everywhere feel overwhelmed, anxious, confused, and exhausted.

In response to what Claudia learned, and in order to fill a critical need, she developed a life-coaching program that enables moms to:

• Take care of – and hold on to – their selves.
• Trust their instinct.
• Balance their commitment to their child and their non-child-related responsibilities with meeting their own needs.

Claudia writes a bi-monthly email newsletter and holds numerous workshops, seminars and groups. Her work focuses on helping moms to become more satisfied and on enabling moms to cope effectively with the challenges they face.

Working with people to help them to achieve their goals has always been Claudia’s passion. Before becoming a coach, Claudia was a successful pre- and post-natal who worked with individuals to help them meet their fitness and personal goals. Her experiences as a trainer gave her insight into the challenges people faced when experiencing even ‘happy’ life-transitions. Claudia received her BA from Columbia University and her MA from the University of Pennsylvania.

She is the author of ‘The Calm and Confident Mom: Solutions to the Top Challenges of New Motherhood’ and the forthcoming ‘Answers to Your Top 20 Questions about Getting Your Baby to Sleep’.

Claudia Heilbrunn’s Specialties:

Self-care for working and stay-at-home moms, and issues, such as: sleep, separation anxiety, limit setting, new mom guilt, going back to work, post-baby marriage, new mom identity and rejuvenation, post-natal fitness, anxiety, etc.
