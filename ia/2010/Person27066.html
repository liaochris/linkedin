Brandon Frame’s Summary

Brandon Frame is a Teaching Fellow at The Fessenden School in West Newton, MA. He duties include but are not limited to Teaching 7th grade English, coaching three sports and serving as a dorm parent.

Brandon prior work experiences includes Aetna, Inc. within their Community Relations department. There he worked on major accounts such as 100 Black Men of America, Black Enterprise and Magic Johnson Enterprises helping to promote health care literacy in Urban Communities across the country. At Kraft Foods Brandon spent time in Retail Sales & as a Customer Analyst. In the retail sales capacity Brandon managed Territories with sales totaling over $500,000. In his customer analyst role Brandon effectively identified ways to improve share and trade efficiencies in major accounts including BJ’s Wholesale & Stop & Shop. While at Liberty Mutual Brandon assisted in establishing new Group Savings Plus Accounts.

Brandon is a true visionary leader. He has established a mentoring program entitled Role Model, Scholar, Gentleman MENtoring in Atlanta at Charles R. Drew Charter School. There are over 50 students in the program and well over 60 students who mentor or are alumni mentors of the program. Brandon also is the founder of the Morehouse Marketing Club. He established the club in Fall of 2008 and served as the first President and the growth of the club into one of the most successful student run clubs on Morehouse’s Campus.

Brandon has received numerous awards & scholarships including but not limited to, 2009 Morehouse Legacy Award, 2009 Morehouse Marketing Star Award, 2008 Inroads New England Region Intern of the Year, 2008 National Sales Network John Watson Scholar, 2008 Earl Graves NAACP Scholar, 2008 BP Student Leadership Scholar.

Brandon is Cum laude graduate of Morehouse College and lives by the phrase “Be Different, Be Great, Never Stop”.
