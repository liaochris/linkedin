Doug Price’s Summary

Some have said that I am a computer geek in an athletes body. I'm flattered, but all kidding aside, I am driven and passionate in everything I do. Perhaps sometimes to my detriment, I am a perfectionist. However, I believe work should be of superior quality and done right the first time. In nearly all cases, this trait has always served my clients and colleagues well.

Professionally, as a senior manager at a large manufacturing corporation and as a business owner myself, I have experienced the whole spectrum. I've enjoyed success, but I have also made mistakes and learned from them. I'm a professionally trained leader, having graduated in the top 1/3 of my class from the prestigious United States Air Force Academy. Subsequent to my time as an officer in the Air Force, I quickly rose through the ranks at Seton Company, the worlds largest manufacturer of automobile leather, to become Manager of Global Enterprise Systems. I architected, staffed and managed several high dollar projects on a global scale. Currently, I own and operate Price Corporation, which runs a successful online retail store, www.PartyFavorMall.com, as well as provides InfusionSoft and Telligent Community Server consulting services.

I have expert knowledge of InfusionSoft (Customer Management System and e-Commerce/Marketing Software) and also Telligent Community (formerly Community Server) software. Furthermore, I have become a recognized expert with RFID technology, being invited as guest speaker at the 2006 Business Information Technology Summit and serving on the board of corporate advisers to the RFID Center of Excellence at Penn State University.

On the social side, I am easy to get along with, loyal, and a team player. I have a wonderful and intelligent wife, who is also my business partner, and two awesome kids. I'm a Steeler fan and enjoy the gym, the outdoors, and electronic music.

Thank you for your time in reading my profile and I look forward to connecting with you.

~Doug

Doug Price’s Experience

  • Founder and CEO

    Price Corporation

    (Privately Held; Computer Software industry)

    March 2005 — Present (5 years 3 months)

    I am the Founder and CEO of Price Corporation. Price Corporation began in 2005 as a consulting firm in the Northeast region of the United States specializing in Radio Frequency Identification (RFID) technologies. We were instrumental in helping many small businesses come into compliance with Department of Defense and retail RFID mandates. I became a recognized expert in the RFID industry being asked to speak at the 2006 Northeast Regional Business Information Technology Summit. Additionally, I was a member of the corporate panel of advisors to the RFID Center of Excellence at Penn State University.

    With the ISO certification of the RFID air interface protocol, the cost (and profit margins) plummeted as the specification became public record. Quickly recognizing this paradigm shift within the RFID industry, I adjusted course and shifted the focus of our company towards e-Commerce with Microsoft .Net technologies. In 2006, we were awarded a contract with StomperNet, LLC, an international leader in Search Engine Optimization (SEO), Internet Marketing and e-Commerce training. We integrated their internal systems, built their customer facing private client portal (on both DotNetNuke and Telligent Community Server platforms), and also built numerous shopping carts for their various product launches. I served in a full-time capacity as their Chief Software Architect for these development projects.

    Building on experience gained through our contract work with StomperNet, in 2007, we launched Party Favor Mall (www.PartyFavorMall.com), an online retailer for party supplies, party favors and wedding favors. This is a highly competitive market and we have shown steady growth from this business.

    I feel that my leadership and focus has helped Price Corporation survive the technology start-up curse. Whereas most technology start-ups fail, we are now in our fourth year of business and posted our first profit last fiscal year.

  • Chief Software Architect

    StomperNet, LLC

    (E-Learning industry)

    January 2009 — October 2009 (10 months)

    Founded by legendary marketers Brad Fallon and Andy Jenkins, StomperNet, LLC is an international leader for Search Engine Optimization (SEO), Internet Marketing and e-Commerce training. What started for me as a contract with them through the consulting firm I own (Price Corporation) turned into a full-time gig as their Chief Software Architect for a period of time to help them migrate their legacy systems to a new integrated enterprise platform built with InfusionSoft and Telligent Community.

    On the public side, I successfully architected, managed and helped develop a customized Learning Management System (LMS) implementation built on the Telligent Community (formerly Community Server) platform. These private client portals utilize a shared membership system so that clients are maintained in a single database but can participate in different courses and levels of membership offered. (www.stompernet.com, my.stompernet.com and f5.stompernet.com)

    Behind the scenes, I have built middleware software that completely integrated their InfusionSoft Customer Resource Management System (CRM or CMS, whichever acronym you prefer) with the public facing Telligent Community membership portal. This has allowed StomperNet to completely eliminate their outsourced customer service and reduce in-house man hours by over 50%.

    I was also the Site Administrator for their elite membership portal. I designed and helped develop numerous module applications including a media library system built for the DotNetNuke open source Content Management System (CMS) that delivered several terabytes worth of content to its members monthly.

  • Manager of Global Enterprise Systems

    Seton Company

    (Privately Held; Automotive industry)

    January 2002 — April 2005 (3 years 4 months)

    I rose through the ranks quickly from Applications Engineer (developer) when I started to Manager of Global Enterprise Systems for the leading global auto leather manufacturing company. I was responsible for coordinating with individual facility management teams worldwide to provide design, implementation and support services that focused on information automation for Seton’s Americas, European and South African operations. I provided technology budgeting, staffing and project decisions/recommendations to top executives. I was also responsible for management of all client/server and AS/400 programming staff. Reported to the Director of Information Technology.

    My most significant achievements include spearheading the negotiation and acquisition of vendors, planning and engineering of an RFID pilot project to study replacement options for an aging production line yield control system, keeping initial project costs under $50K. Additionally, I was an instrumental player in developing a new capital expense report submission/approval routing system, and a new employee evaluation system, as well as single-handedly converting over 100 accounting and payroll reports from legacy systems to Crystal Reports. I also led the team of staff and vendors that was responsible for completing payroll data conversion from Seton's internal PDS Vista system to ADP during outsourcing transition for over 600 U.S. employees.
