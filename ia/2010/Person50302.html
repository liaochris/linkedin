David Byron’s Experience

  • CTO

    Plata Software, Inc.

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Computer Software industry)

    August 2007 — Present (3 years 1 month)

    Plata Software, Inc. spun off of Science South, Inc. after developing software based on discussions with the University of Central Florida. The concept is simple: Platasoft's goal is to distribute to the public the highest quality productivity software available, and to keep it open source but fully supported with the same level of quality as the best commercial products. This will give everyone with access to a computer the opportunity to achieve without the burden of a high software acquisition cost. To this end, "PlataSoft" has created "MultimediaOffice" (tm), the most complete productivity package available. It meets the OpenDocument standard, and it's available for free at www.platasoft.eu or from sourceforge.net.
    Plata has also developed the "GuruStick" (tm), the most advanced collection of professional I.T. tools available. More information is available on our website.

  • CTO

    Science South, Inc.

    (Privately Held; Research industry)

    January 1996 — Present (14 years 8 months)

    Science South, Inc. is a woman-owned company dedicated to providing cutting edge developmental expertise in the fields of materials engineering and software development. We began in the plastic products manufacturing industry, which gave us the manufacturing ethic: "make it right the first time, make it quickly, and make it at a competitive price."
    We have now expanded our services. We perform design engineering to develop products, create a turn-key production lines, or manufacture it. Defense Industry: advanced materials systems for weapon components from polymer and ceramic Our research extends from armor and munitions to secure software and diamond nanodevice fabrication.
    Jewelry Industry: We invented and patented making diamonds at low pressure and low temperature, and to create diamond "plating" on objects.

  • Expert Witness

    Science South

    (Computer Software industry)

    1990 — 2009 (19 years )

    Qualified as Expert Witness in State and Federal Courts in both civil and criminal cases. Experienced with product liability, patent infringement, interior, exterior and terminal ballistics, manufacturing, and weapon design and functioning.   • CTO

    Magnum Technology, Inc.

    (Defense & Space industry)

    March 2000 — October 2007 (7 years 8 months)

    Spinoff of Science South. Company was awarded a U.S. Army contract for Byron’s Composite Reinforced Ceramic Technology (CRCT) machinegun barrels. Materials utilized include advanced engineering ceramics, self-reinforced thermoplastics, thermoset resins, and advanced composite structures.
    Byron was “principal Investigator” on 2 DoD projects: Polymer-cased Ammunition and Lightweight Gun Barrels. 3 patents have issued for the ammo, and there remain pending patents for both projects.
    Byron created technology includes: precision rapid toolmaking for molding polymers or ceramics; alternate materials systems; advanced composite structures; metallizing and coatings, tooling systems. The Byron-designed thermoplastic composite winding machine is considered by the composites industry to be the most technically advanced machine of its type in the world. Also worked on armor systems to protect troops from both kinetic and thermobaric munitions.

  • Director of Publishing Administration

    Cosmi Corporation

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Computer Software industry)

    March 1997 — January 2004 (6 years 11 months)

    Cosmi Corporation is a privately held leading developer and publisher of software for the home and office. With corporate headquarters in Carson, CA, Cosmi is the #1 publisher in productivity software as tracked by NPD Techworld, an independent research company.
    In 1997 I began writing software for Cosmi through Science South. In Nov. 2002 I became Director of Publishing Administration for Cosmi, with the responsibility of continuing to lead software development teams in the U.S., India and the former Soviet Union. I reported to the President, and was also involved in predicting emerging markets, interfacing with venders, choosing titles, government sales, project planning and working with mass-market retail customers.

  • CTO and Chairman

    Vintage Industries, Inc.

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Plastics industry)

    February 1991 — September 2002 (11 years 8 months)

    Created a start-up plastics manufacturing company in 1991 that evolved into a leading regional injection molding, engineering and moldmaking company. Manufacturing technologies included: design engineering, toolmaking, injection molding, thermoset molding, thermoset composite forming, thermoplastic composite fabrication, assembly and packaging. Invented and led the development of novel patent-pending moldmaking technologies. Added a "paperless" engineering and toolmaking protocol that integrated 3DCAD/CAE/CAM with Quality Assurance and Marketing. This helped the company to earn a high vendor rating and excellent "LEAN" percentage from U.S. government prime contractors.
