Jessie Dedecker’s Summary

I am involved in industrial R&D trajectories of software and ICT products/services. My focus is both on innovation and software engineering:

• Innovation engineering is about the generation of ideas, selecting the most promising ones (choosing focus points), evaluating their business potential (w.r.t. customer needs, business models and strategies, go-to market, ...), the engineering aspects of transforming ideas into products. Most importantly, the ideas need to be translated into viable product and business opportunities.

• Software engineering is about all the aspects involved to realize software artifacts. Choosing the right software engineering practices is crucial to realize the optimal ROI for software products and projects. The adopted practices will heavily depend on the customer needs, the product and project types, the internal organization and culture, the areas of expertise of the workforce, team size, etc. Software engineering is all about making the right trade-offs (both on a technological as well as process level) given a company's context.

I enjoy working in small teams and realize that good managerial skills are a key to solving any problem of scale. My background in research enables me to think out-of-the-box and rigorously evaluate alternative solutions to one another.

Jessie Dedecker’s Specialties:
