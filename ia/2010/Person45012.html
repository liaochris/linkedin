Cameron McPherson’s Summary

Having completed my studies in economics in 2002, I gained exposure to the field of health economics, and submissions made towards the PBAC, in a Melbourne-based CRO.

Following this, driven by the desire to develop my scientific technical acumen, I pursued a PhD in Behavioural Neuropharmacology at the University of Melbourne (2005-2008).

Shortly following graduation, in 2010, I travelled abroad for a post-doctoral fellowship in Montreal, Canada, pursuing an independent research project.

I am a professional and well organised communicator, having authored four first-author manuscripts,contributed to significant government grants, and presented scientific abstracts at national and international conferences.

I have demonstrated strong abilities in data analysis and thoroughness in reading detailed research manuscripts and in interpretation of their data.

I harbour a passion for pharmaceutical science, have sound communication skills, problem solving abilities, and am a team player.

I gain pleasure from helping others in a collaborative and supportive environment, and bringing my technical skills, passions and experience to bear in a manner which is net positive for relevant stakeholders, colleagues, and the community.
