Scott Armey’s Summary

Judge Scott Armey is presently a principal partner at The Armey Group, LLP. The Armey Group is dedicated to forging successful partnerships between the public and private sectors to provide quality products and services for the American taxpayer.

Scott Armey was appointed Regional Administrator of the U.S. General Services Administration's Greater Southwest Region by President Bush on June 5, 2002. As the principal executive in the Greater Southwest Region, Judge Armey led a workforce of 1,250 associates and directed operations in Arkansas, Louisiana, New Mexico, Oklahoma, and Texas. The Greater Southwest Region has an annual operating budget of $500 million and a real estate portfolio that includes 385 government-owned buildings and leased space in 757 commercial buildings.

From 2000 to 2002, Judge Armey served as Denton County Judge where he was the presiding officer of the Commissioners Court. Denton County is one of the fastest growing counties in the nation with a population of 500,000 citizens. In this capacity, he drafted and administered a $100 million county budget, oversaw more than 1,300 county employees, and coordinated economic development initiatives.

From 1992 to 2000, he served as Denton County Commissioner, Precinct 3 where his primary objectives were stimulating economic development, attracting new business into Denton County, and expanding the county’s commercial tax base.

Judge Armey served as Chairman of the Dallas/Fort Worth Federal Executive Board in 2005. He has been retained as an expert witness on Texas county government and frequently speaks about government and procurement to local organizations and universities.

Judge Armey graduated from the University of North Texas in 1990 with a degree in business administration. He and his wife, Carisa, reside in Denton with their son, Avery, and twin daughters, Abbi and Alix.
