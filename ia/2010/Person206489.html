Tom Spyrou’s Summary

Software Executive and Technologist. Currently a Distinguished Engineer at Cadence Design Systems working on distributed processing and multi-threaded / multi-core algorithm development. Manager of the world-wide Encounter proto-typing and Hierachical Design R&D team.

Tom Spyrou’s Specialties:

Parallel Processing, Multi Core/Threaded and Distributed Software Development. Cloud Computing. Complex Application Architecture. Engineering Management. Product Marketing. Electrical and Timing Analysis of Digital Circuits. Patents and Intellectual Property.

Tom Spyrou’s Experience

  • Distinguished Engineer

    Cadence Design Systems

    (Public Company; CDNS; Computer Software industry)

    October 2006 — Present (4 years 1 month)

    The technology from Precision Software Development was acquired by Cadence. This technology was related to the parallel processing of key EDA algorithms and an approach for applying parallel processing to legacy code. Within Cadence I have been driving the parallel processing effort utilizing Precison Software’s IP and have created a shared cross-platform framework for multi-threading and TCP/IP based distributed processing. I am also responsible for driving utilization of parallel processing across a 500 person organization by architecting solutions and prioritizing projects based on their value to the overall business and customer base.

    Starting in 2008 I began to also manage the worldwide R&D group developing Hierarchical Design Solutions in the Encounter Digital Implementation System. This responsibility is over a team of 70 engineers based in the U.S., India, and China. The responsibility includes executive sponsorship and relationship management for top accounts.

  • President/Principal Consultant

    Precision Software Development Inc.

    (Public Company; QADI; Logistics and Supply Chain industry)

    March 2004 — October 2006 (2 years 8 months)

    Precision Software is a company I started to provide high value product marketing and software development services to Semiconductor and EDA Companies. I worked with Semconductor Companies and EDA Companies to improve their electrical analysis and design software capabilities. For the Semiconductor companies I actively participated in the architecture of their chip design flow and chip design trade-offs. For the EDA companies I developed code and helped with Product Marketing and Business Development. I used the consulting work to fund my own IP development and developed several patents and code in the area of parallel processing and parallel EDA algorithms. This IP was acquired by Cadence in October of 2006.

  • Vice President of Engineering

    CiraNova Inc

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Computer Software industry)

    November 2002 — March 2004 (1 year 5 months)

    Responsible for Product Development in a 15 person start-up. Managed 8 software developers. Worked closely with CTO to turn product vision into product specification and implementation. Focus on predictable scheduling, repeatable process, and quality improvement. I supported financing by visiting with Venture Capitalists. I supported business requirements by making worldwide customer visits to promote the tool and explain its significance and value. The Software is a next generation analog layout and analysis system.

  • Senior Product Director

    Simplex Solutions

    (Public Company; 201-500 employees; SPLX; Computer Software industry)

    November 2001 — November 2002 (1 year 1 month)

    Acquired by Cadence June 2002.
    Responsibility over Simplex products for Signal Electro-migration and Power Grid Analysis, Delay Calculation and Signal Integrity. Position included direct management of R&D and cross-functional responsibility for the product line with revenue targets. I worked with Marketing on the initial roll-out of Signal-Storm to the market. This included creating presentations, meeting with editors of EDA trade journals, and training the sales force and AE’s on how to engage with the technology. I also made worldwide trips to present the technology to key customers and explain its significance in the design flow. My product responsibility accounted for about 80% of Simplex’s software revenue. I started several key initiatives in the analysis products to tackle new problems in nanometer design and was driving a key technical effort with the CTO on a next generation analysis product.

  • Vice President of Engineering

    InTime Software

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Computer Software industry)

    March 2000 — December 2001 (1 year 10 months)

    Product focused on delivering a next generation RTL design and debug system capable of estimating RTL Area and Performance before Logic Synthesis. The system also had RTL, Mixed, and Gate Level Floor-planning and Placement technology. I was responsible for running an R&D organization of about 25 engineers. My challenge was entering a start-up company after it had executed for 18 months, helping to drive the product definition, refining the software architecture, and driving the execution of the product development. I supported financing by visiting with Venture Capitalists. I supported business requirements by making worldwide customer visits to promote InTime’s products. These visits included product demonstrations followed by in depth technical analysis of the Customers’ design flows and how InTime’s solution might help.

  • Engineering Group Director, Static Timing Analysis & IC Implementation Infrastructure

    Cadence Design Systems

    (Public Company; 5001-10,000 employees; CDN; Computer Software industry)

    December 1997 — March 2000 (2 years 4 months)

    Responsibility for Cadence’s Static Timing technology including Pearl and Ambit timing products. Also had responsibility for the Place and Route Database group which included the team developing the next generation Open Access database. The combined organization had about 65 people.

  • R&D Manager, Core Timing Technology

    Synopsys Inc.

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; snps; Computer Software industry)

    February 1996 — November 1997 (1 year 10 months)

    Responsible for team of 5 people developing Synopsys’ core static timing engine which was used in PrimeTime. This role included responsibility for deployment of a shared static timing engine based on PrimeTime for both the Chip Architect Floor-planner and the Next Generation Synthesis System. I lead the development team and the design of the key algorithms. I was also responsible for setting the direction for a joint timing solution with the IBM EDA development team, driving consensus, and managing the technical relationship. I supported Business requirements by visiting customers and working with ASIC Vendors to help achieve Sign Off status for PrimeTime’s static timing analysis.
