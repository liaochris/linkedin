Alex Yaroslavsky’s Summary

Alex is a dispute resolution professional with over 12 years of coaching, arbitration, mediation and conflict management training experience.

Alex is a member of arbitration and mediation panels with the New York Stock Exchange, the National Association of Securties Dealers, and the New York City's Civilian Complaint Review Board. He regularly trains and coaches new mediators, and is chair of Mediation Advisory Board at Safe Horizon - a nonprofit agency dedicated to violence prevention. Alex also teaches Dispute Resolution at the John Jay College of Criminal Justice.

Prior to founding Yaro Group, Alex worked at Morgan Stanley from 1996 to 2000. His experience included a year-long assignment as a Human Resources generalist in the Tokyo office, as well as project manager in New York working on performance evaluation, compensation, training and technology deployment.

In 1996 Alex worked at the Carter Presidential Center in Atlanta, GA. His role in the Conflict Resolution Group involved tracking political conflicts in Ireland, North Korea and Kashmir. Alex also coordinated an electronic brainstorming session on the Rwanda crisis. After his internship Alex continued to work as an electronic brainstorming facilitator in New York and Texas.

From 1991 to 1993 Alex worked at Accenture (Andersen Consulting). His clients included major firms in the investment banking and telecommunications industries. During his tenure Alex served as instructor at the firm's central training facility in St. Charles, IL.

Alex's holds a Master's degree in Industrial & Labor Relations from Cornell University (1995) and a Bachelor's degree in Marketing and International Business from New York University's Stern School of Business (1991).
