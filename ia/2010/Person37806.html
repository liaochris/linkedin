Christopher Gorka’s Summary

BuyFixandProfit.com is an authority destination for teaching real estate investors how to build a profitable real estate portfolio and business. Our focus is on building a passive income stream while performing activities that also generate immediate income today. The ultimate goal is to live your life on your terms and we firmly believe that investing in real estate is the surest and fastest way of achieving that goal. BuyFixandProfit.com provides REAL, from the streets, Real Estate Investing advice, info, thoughts, and reviews. We focus less on the creative, no money down ideas, and more on the soup to nuts of how to buy distressed properties, fix them up, and the best ways to profit from your efforts.

  • Founder/Owner

    www.buyfixandprofit.com

    (Real Estate industry)

    July 2009 — Present (1 year 1 month)

    I genuinely enjoy real estate investing and the business of rehabbing distressed properties for profit. I can talk about, read about, research, tour, analyze, and strategize real estate deals all day long. I’ve stayed up till 5 in the morning researching neighborhoods and foreclosures, only to leave my house at 6am to start viewing these properties and submitting offers before anyone else beat could beat me to it. I’ve been a licensed real estate agent, I’ve worked for a mortgage company, I’ve been a bird dog, I’ve wholesaled properties, I’ve rehabbed homes, and I’ve been a landlord. I still am today.

    I have spent plenty of time in the field getting dirty and learning first hand as to what it takes to make a deal succeed. Just ask my wife, she’ll tell you.

    Investing in distressed properties for quick flip cash and/or for long term cash flow is definitely exciting but not always as glamorous as TV makes it out to be. Over the past 10 years, I have had an assortment of things happen to me while trying to secure my profits in the field. I’ve been swindled; I’ve been threatened; I’ve thrown up at properties; I’ve cleaned up shit (literally) that intruders left in a broken toilet 5 minutes before prospective buyers showed up; I’ve been steered to misrepresent myself without even knowing it; I’ve called 911 more than once; I’ve been scolded by a judge during an eviction hearing; I’ve had a tenant not pay a penny of rent and milk the eviction process for over 6 months; I’ve entered houses that have just been broken into in the middle of the night; I’ve had contractors try to bill me for work that was never completed; I've had property managers steal money from me, and I’ve strategically defaulted on a property when a bank refused to work me. Did I miss anything?

    I've learned that in order to be successful, take action (just do it), concentrate on the 80/20 principle, and never stop learning.
