Larry Nuñez’s Summary

Drawing on over 25 years of experience in skateboarding and snowboarding, Larry Nuñez is one of the leading editorial voices in action sports. With roots in Southern California and El Paso, Texas, Larry’s two environments shaped his 80’s childhood with a steady diet of punk rock, rap and skateboarding.

Eventually, snowboarding took precedence, and from his early days as a sponsored rider during the 1990s, he moved on to coach at Windells Camp on Mt. Hood, Oregon and became a contributing editor for Transworld Media in 1999. He has since been published in Transworld Snowboarding, Transworld Skateboarding, Transworld Surf, Transworld Business, Snowboarder Magazine, OnBoard Magazine, Rides Magazine and served as the editor of Snowboard Magazine until July 2009. As a founding member of Snowboard Magazine in 2004, Larry helped create the most popular website in snowboarding & quickly rose up the ranks to develop the unique content and current direction of the book. Profiling some of today’s most influential personalities, his innovative ideas & keen sense of style have brought trendsetting subject matter to the forefront of the industry time and time again. He currently serves as a special project manager for the renowned lifestyle and subculture media group Frank151, as well as the social media manager for Vans Snow.

Aside from exploring mountains around the world, he appears in movies and television shows as a stuntman—most notably 2007’s Brooklyn Rules and the HBO series, “Bored to Death”.

Larry Nuñez’s Specialties:

Writing and editing, art direction, event production, product design, video production, marketing initiatives, social media, utility stunt work for film and television.
