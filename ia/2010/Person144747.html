Staci Schipporeit’s Summary

My husband and I started our web hosting and development company a loooong time ago. We set up a Macintosh computer in our basement, connected to the internet via dial-up and every time someone would visit one of the web sites we were hosting, the computer would chime and we'd get all excited about it. Thankfully, that's an amusing memory now.

Our plan from the beginning was to host dynamic data-driven web sites. We believed there was a tremendous amount of power in serving up database content via the web. We founded our company with the name Datahost, Inc. A few years into it and after writing several custom web applications, we decided to build our own eCommerce platform. And that is when MightyMerchant was born.

I am the creator of MightyMerchant which is our flagship product. It's a CMS (Content Management System) and eCommerce platform. In a nutshell, and it is SO hard for me to put this in a nutshell, MightyMerchant allows you to sell stuff online or publish a web site describing your services and allows you to edit and manage your web site using a web browser.

While I still love Apple (and my new iPhone), MightyMerchant is hosted on a linux box, served by Apache, stores data in MySQL, and is written in PHP. AKA LAMP. I still do all my development work on a Mac Laptop and use BBEdit, which is the god of text editors.
