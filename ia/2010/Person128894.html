Luke Froeb’s Summary

Luke Froeb served as Chief Economist of the Federal Trade Commission for two years where he managed seventy-five economists who tore down barriers to competition (often erected by well-meaning bureaucrats), and enforced the antitrust and consumer protection laws of the United States. In July 2005, Professor Froeb returned to Vanderbilt University where he holds the Margaret and William Oehmig Chair of Entrepreneurship and Free Enterprise. He was voted outstanding professor of the 2005, 2006, and 2007 executive MBA programs by his students, and his get-rich-slowly-scheme, Managerial Economics: A Problem-Solving Approach was published in 2007 by Cengage.
