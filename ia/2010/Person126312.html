  • Senior Development Engineer

    Eastman Kodak

    (Plastics industry)

    2002 — 2007 (5 years )

    Selection of alternative materials for extrusion coating and coextrusion, evaluation of highly loaded (>60%) masterbatches.
    We continually screened alternative materials for our formulations, aiming for cost reduction while maintaining or improving the product quality. When the supply of a critical material was interrupted by a Force of Nature (Hurricane Katrina) we had several previously evaluated runners up available to choose from.
    In addition to focusing on polyethylene formulations for extrusion coating, I also had the opportunity to apply and broaden my previous oriented film experience to orientation of a solvent cast film for a LCD application.

  • Development Engineer

    Eastman Kodak

    (Plastics industry)

    1999 — 2002 (3 years )

    Formulation of polyethylene for extrusion coating, oriented film product development, colorant selection, materials selection, performance and accelerated aging testing.
    The most memorable project from this assignment was the initial commercialization of a thermographic medical film. This was a great experience working on a crossfunctional team representing a broad range of disciplines. The project required me to bring my previous knowledge of compounding, oriented film development, and color science together and build on it to add a new product to the company portfolio.

  • Development Engineer

    Eastman Kodak

    (Public Company; EK; Printing industry)

    1989 — 1999 (10 years )

    Assignments in product and process development, including polyethylene formulation for extrusion coating, voided oriented films through use of microbeads (resulting in several patents), color optimization of polyester films, poly(ethylene naphthalate) film process optimization, materials selection.
    During this time, the development group acquired an automatic thickness control system for a pilot scale oriented film line. Understanding the first thickness control system our group acquired took a couple of months of figurative banging head against the wall. Eventually there was an Aha! moment, and that mental breakthrough made it easier to understand the various systems from different suppliers that were on other coating lines. Building on this, I developed a training class for operators and engineers in how to confirm the systems were running properly.
