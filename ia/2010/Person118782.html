Kris Putnam Walkerly’s Summary

Kristen M. Putnam, MSW

Kristen Putnam has worked with and advised foundations and non-profit organizations since 1990. In 1999, Ms. Putnam established the philanthropy consulting firm Putnam Community Investment Consulting. Putnam helps foundations and nonprofits assess need, develop effective programs, align organizational strategies, and evaluate impact.

Ms. Putnam has consulted with numerous foundations to help them plan and implement grantmaking programs. Recent and current clients include the Robert Wood Johnson Foundation, Stuart Foundation, California HealthCare Foundation, Northern California Grantmakers, and Hispanics in Philanthropy. She has advised foundations on grant proposals and programs ranging from $10,000 to $14 million, and served as consulting program officer for The California Endowment as well as for the Charles and Helen Schwab Foundation. She currently serves on the Boards of the Horizons Foundation and the Community Foundation of Lorain County.

In additional to developing grantmaking programs, Ms. Putnam has extensive experience designing, managing, and conducting evaluations. Recent projects include assessing grantseeker perceptions of The James Irvine Foundation’s New Connections Fund, determining the impact of the Blue Shield of California Foundation’s core support initiative, and evaluating the Richard and Rhoda Goldman Fund’s Elder Abuse Prevention Initiative.

Ms. Putnam is co-founder of the Philanthropy Consultants Network, and serves on the Steering Committee of the National Network of Consultants to Grantmakers.

Follow her on twitter @ http://twitter.com/Philanthropy411and check out the Philanthropy411 Blog at http://philanthropy411.wordpress.com/
