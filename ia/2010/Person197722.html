Susan Dean’s Summary

Susan owns Newcomer Service and is dedicated in helping new residents settle into the community seamlessly. Susan became an expert by moving over 15 times and understands the needs a person has when relocating. While it can be exciting moving getting settled in requires a lot of work. After several frustrating moves she discovered welcoming services. They provided information about the community, business referrals, child care recommendations and so much more. In 1984 Susan made a final move back to Central Minnesota, her hometown and was welcomed by Newcomer Service. As things sometimes go full circle Susan bought the Newcomer Service, established by her grandmother in 1948. She is deeply rooted in the community, an avid supporter of locally owned businesses and is committed in bridging them together.

She knows what a newcomer needs. A newcomer is contacted by phone or on line – www.newcomerservice.net and a Business & Community Guide is mailed. The Guide becomes a newcomer’s resource leading them to everything they need to get established. She recognizes the importance of every new resident to the future growth and productivity of a community and establishing this relationship from the moment they move in is vital.

A graduate of Ray Vogue School of Design in Chicago, attended St. Cloud State University and a Val Victorian from the Street School of Hard Knocks. Susan is active in local chambers, arts organizations, women’s groups, St. Cloud Downtown Council and a member of Welcome Service International
”Never lose sight of the fact that just living is fun”, quotes Susan. Outside of her professional life she has two sons (an executive chef and a mortgage lender), and too many pets. She also enjoys just about anything outdoors, traveling, reading, and passionate about gardening and is a mosaic artist.
