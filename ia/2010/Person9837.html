Danny Nathan’s Summary

For years now, I've been the square peg that you can't quite figure out where to place. I am an anomaly, an outlier. I’m at the forefront of a new breed of tech savvy, digitally connected, trend conscious, and socially aware creatives who are working every day to redefine the meaning of "branded communications."

I'm passionate about technology & creativity, and I hate advertising – all of which shows in the work that I create. I believe in designing valuable experiences that ingratiate consumers and encourage them to do the talking. I believe in simple, smart, and social.

Force me to put a name to what I do, and I'd tell you that it's equal parts: conceptual ideation, product and brand development strategy, UX design, social media strategy, and copywriting/art direction.

Want to learn more? Give me a shout on Twitter (@AmongMany) or have a look at my work on my website: www.1.amongmany.com.

  • Professor/Teacher/Lecturer

    Miami Ad School

    (Educational Institution; Marketing and Advertising industry)

    July 2009 — September 2009 (3 months)

    Advertising agencies and brands are running scared. They're being forced to seek out alternative means of engaging consumers because their excessive marketing habits of the past have been rendered obsolete, both by technology and by lack of consumer interest. Face it, no one wants to be advertised to.

    But for many, this movement isn't just some new fad to control costs during troubled times. The best marketing has always come from creating something valuable and useful. People gravitate towards, and like talking about, simple, valuable and entertaining interactions. They're willing to forgive (and even embrace) branded messages that come with them.

    Forget thinking big. It's time to think small.
    It's time to embrace the art of simple, smart and social. It's time to stop creating advertisements and start creating useful things that advertise.

    This class explored these ideas through a series of discussions and hands-on assignments designed to break through the constraints of traditional mediums. It aimed to demonstrate that, by embracing the digital toolkit and focusing on being nimble, effective communication can be executed more efficiently and with greater impact than the clutter that's prevalent today.
