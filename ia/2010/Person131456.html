    Portland Opportunities & Industrialization Counsel

    (Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    August 2007 — Present (3 years 1 month)

    While Portland Opportunities & Industrialization Center (POIC) is the parent company, I am involved with the high school, Rosemary Anderson High School (RAHS). This is a wonderful organaization, not very well known but becoming better known given their exceptional development staff including my dear friend, Deneen King, and their exceptional grant writer, Michael Oreste.

    Rosemary Anderson serves those students that are not able to succeed in our public high schools. The school's graduation rate is above 90%; compare this to PPS's 50% or less. The school also has a GED program for ESL Hispanic students.

    Not only does RAHS provide each and every student with EVERY resource to insure they graduate from high school (from counseling to clothing to physicians to financial support if required). They have a teenage parent program, so that those who choose to give birth and keep their children, have the opportunity to continue their education and become productive and self-supporting adults.

    POIC has several proven program arms beyond RAHS including a mentoring program, a job training program and a transitions program (they now follow and support every graduate through the age of 25). In addition, POIC does not care what color of skin or culture their students are from. As long as they do not have a criminal record or other behavioral issues that would prevent a student from attending, the student is accepted.

    Should you find this to be as worthwhile a non-profit as I do, you can contact POIC or RAHS through joemac@poicrahs.com.
