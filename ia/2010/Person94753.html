Jenn Bollenbacher’s Summary

As a Senior at Tufts University studying Political Science, I have a particular interest in domestic social policy. I have worked with various non-profits, including the Civic Education Project at Northwestern University, WorldBoston, and the Asian Task Force Against Domestic Violence. I have also worked in a governmental capacity, both on the local level with the Medford City Hall Department of Diversity in Medford, MA and on a Federal level as a Constituent Services intern for Congresswoman Melissa Bean (IL-08).

I am currently experiencing the non-profit world from a philanthropy perspective with my internship at the McCormick Foundation in Chicago. This position has provided me an additional dimension to my understanding of how non-profits function in accordance to the needs of society, how they interact with government, and the impact they have on their communities.

As my experience working for non-profit organizations grows, I am becoming more interested in the policy and corporate social responsibility aspect of business. I am particularly interested in further exploring the intersection between for-profit businesses and a socially responsible mission.
