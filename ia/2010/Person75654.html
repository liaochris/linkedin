Gio Hunt’s Experience

  • CEO

    (Privately Held; Information Technology and Services industry)

    August 2009 — Present (1 year 5 months)

  • General Manager

    (Privately Held; Education Management industry)

As General Manager, I lead all aspects of this business transformation initiative.

  • CEO/Co-Founder

    SWARM, LLC

    (Internet industry)

  • SVP & GM, Xdrive / Digital Asset Management Solutions

    AOL, LLC

    (Public Company; AOL; Internet industry)

    August 2005 — February 2007 (1 year 7 months)

    General Manager of AOL’s Xdrive business. Xdrive was an online storage product that AOL transformed into a new type of offering – a digital asset management solution for consumers. Oversaw all Xdrive operations (a combined operation of 90 people in bi-coastal offices). Launched a new version of the Xdrive service in Sept ’06, which received a 5-Star rating from CNET.com).

  • SVP, New Business Ventures

    AOL, LLC

    (Public Company; AOL; Internet industry)

    November 2004 — February 2007 (2 years 4 months)

    Founded a new organization at AOL – focused on incubating significant new online businesses for AOL. Launched several new business efforts: Digital Asset Management, Alternate Broadband, Subscription Music and Location Based Services. Led the acquisition of three companies: Xdrive, Wildseed and MusicNow.

  • SVP, Operational Effectiveness Program

    AOL, LLC

    (Public Company; AOL; Internet industry)

    February 2003 — November 2004 (1 year 10 months)

    Led AOL’s Operational Effectiveness Program (OEP) – a multi-year business operations improvement program. Managed a staff of internal consultants and dozens of external consultants from top consulting firms. Identified and implemented business improvements that produced over $988MM in earnings improvement for AOL.

  • SVP, Google Alliance Executive

    AOL, LLC

    (Public Company; AOL; Internet industry)

    March 2002 — November 2004 (2 years 9 months)

    Architected and negotiated AOL's original Google relationship in Spring 2002, and then served as AOL’s “senior ambassador” to Google for several years. Helped define and develop AOL’s strategy for Search and Search monetization. The AOL/Google relationship generated over $1.6B in operating income for AOL and another $1B+ in equity value for Time Warner.

  • SVP, Technology Business Development

    AOL, LLC
