Nathan Emley’s Summary

Research and development microfabrication engineer with clean room experience at the Cornell Nanofabrication Facility and the Berkeley Microlab.

Doctorate level expertise in nanomagnetism and micro-electrical-mechanical-systems (MEMS) with significant research experience in novel magnetic random access memory (MRAM) devices as well as MEMS sensor and actuator technologies.

Broad hands-on experience in a multitude of clean-room micro- and nanofabrication techniques, including electron beam lithography and deep-Si reactive ion etching (DRIE).

Team leader within various research groups at Cornell and Berkeley, guiding the work of and getting the best out of undergraduate and graduate students.

Nathan Emley’s Specialties:

Lithography: Electron beam lithography, photolithography, and mask design & fabrication.

Thin Film Deposition: Sputtering, ion beam deposition, thermal and electron beam evaporation, LPCVD, PECVD, chemical solution deposition of PZT.

Thin Film Etching: Ion milling, plasma, wet chemical, and chemical mechanical polishing.

Characterization: AFM, SEM, MFM, Profilometry, Thin Film Stress, Interferometry.

Computer: LabView, Mathematica, Matlab, AutoCAD, ANSYS, CoventorWare, and COMSOL.
