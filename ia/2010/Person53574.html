Deborah Chan’s Summary

A patchwork of experiences ranging from training & communications to branding & strategic management and eventually to sustainable tourism and community development across various industries both local and international.

My philosophy in work worth doing lies in the very heart of intent and vision. I run after a personal vision of working towards a goal larger than life, more significant than profit (as the bottom line) and ultimately inspiring and making a difference in the lives of people.

At the end of the day, I'm a story teller - sometimes starting on a chapter, other times from start to finish. I tell stories of brands, organizations, people, places, our planet, encounters, life and its myriad mysteries. I strive to be a better, more profound communicator... one day at a time.

Currently, I manage several programmes at Wild Asia, including; Responsible Tourism Initiative(RTI), Natural Corridor Initiative (NCI) and Environmental Education (EE). With RTI, I work with strategic stakeholders ranging from world class hotel chains to community based travel operators on tools to move forward in sustainable tourism through experiential training courses.

My work takes me to various accommodation operators across Asia to evaluate their sustainable practices in the context of their destination. A lot of my work depends on strong monitoring and auditing systems to identify environmental, economic, and social performance indicators.

The planet and its people is a mammoth tasks to save, but little steps can be made and it involves engagement with a range of audiences and key stakeholder groups in understanding what sustainability actually means. Then it requires strategic communication, consistent funding and social mobilization for the little steps to make big leaps.
