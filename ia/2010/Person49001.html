Darren Ferneyhough’s Summary

Primarily engaged in working with SME business owners and directors to ensure existing business insurance policies are not only competitive in cost but also adequate in terms of cover; a rewarding experience as often neither is the case and it is always satisfying to improve the terms for clients as well as helping them identify and mitigate additional business risks frequently overlooked.

Darren Ferneyhough, together with business partner Michael Cooke, launched The Money Helper in late 2006 as an end-user (both business and consumer) oriented financial services provider offering simple, fuss-free and fast access to financial services products arranged remotely via telephone & email or face to face. Having previously founded, developed and sold B2B and B2C financial services businesses, Darren's experience in IT & operational roles was utilised to develop streamlined systems, processes and infrastructure maximising customer experience & satisfaction as well as business profitability in the provision of non-investment financial services products.

Darren Ferneyhough’s Specialties:

Business Protection: Specialising in business risk review, report and implementation of solutions with regard to uninsured risks and the threats posed by them to businesses and ultimately business owners.

Business process design & development for financial services direct sales models both remote & face to face.

IT Infrastructure design and business process development for scalable distribution of financial services products via the intermediary channel.

Darren Ferneyhough’s Experience

  • Director

    The Money Helper Ltd

    (Financial Services industry)

    September 2006 — Present (3 years 7 months)

    Utilising the extensive experience and knowledge of insurance products gained in the creation, development, management and eventual sale of the intermediary-oriented financial services business Premier together with further experience working with end users of insurance products and further qualifications acheived since 2006, I now spend the majority of my time delivering solutions to small to medium sized businesses and their owners in terms of:

    • Reducing the costs of clients' existing and compulsory insurances;
    • Improving the cover under clients' existing and compulsory insurances;
    • Identifying uninsured risks within clients' businesses and covering them within budget;
    • Ensuring the personal assets of business owners and directors are adequately protected against loss in unforseen circumstances.

    I take a great sense of pride in helping my clients in the ways described above and beyond, and it is with great satisfaction that I read the letters and emails regularly received from clients expressing their thanks.

  • Director

    Tenet Support Services Ltd

    (Financial Services industry)

    September 2004 — April 2006 (1 year 8 months)

    Following the sale of my company Premier Connections Ltd and all it's subsidiaries to the Tenet Group, a condition of the deal was that I was obliged to continue my role as Operations Director for the business to fulfil various roles, primarily the smooth integration of the business into the new parent group.

    I was kept extremely busy carrying out this task whilst continuing to develop the business proposition through a challenging and exciting time of great change where the core products in all 4 main sectors of the business moved under FSA regulation.

    The integration of the businesses was managed efficiently and effectively, including the relocation of the business into group premises, migration of certain functions to head office, and aligning staff and business processes with the group's strategy and philosophy.

    Within just a year and a half these aspects had been concluded and I was released from my contract to pursue my own business interests once again.

  • Director

    The Mortgage Helper Ltd

    (Financial Services industry)

    January 2003 — December 2005 (3 years )

    Whilst building the Premier group of companies, an opportunity arose to recruit some skilled personnel to run a mortgage & protection brokerage, and together with fellow directors Michael Cooke and Neil Burroughs I formed the company The Mortgage Helper Ltd.

    We set the business up in office premises in Reading with an initial full time staff of 3 plus a consultant programmer, and quickly built a consumer facing website, implemented a search engine marketing strategy and created custom operational software to integrate the website with the office network and also the quote servers of numerous insurance providers.

    This enabled us to deliver a seamless workflow from a website visitor's quote request to simultaneously deliver an on-screen quote whilst also notifying the sales staff and initiating an outbound call to discuss the quote on screen and build a relationship with up-sell, cross-sell and close.

    The user experience was ahead of it's time and very exciting, however all 3 directors' commitments to their existing business were becoming increasingly time consuming and eventually the decision was made to focus all of our time elsewhere.

    We sold The Mortgage Helper in 2005 as a going concern to BDS Mortgage Group Ltd and the business continues to trade as a subsidiary of Skipton Building Society.

  • Director

    Premier Connections Ltd

    (Financial Services industry)

    January 2001 — September 2004 (3 years 9 months)

    Together with Michael Cooke, I founded and launched the intermediary-oriented financial services business Premier Protection in 2001 to improve the facilities available to intemediaries in the pure protection sector.

    The business was highly successful, quickly recruiting a number of clients and helping them to write greater volumes of protection business, providing them with greater remuneration for the business written, and simplyfing the process of writing protection business through the use of technology.

    By 2002 the business was attracting attention within it's sector and beyond due to it's rapid success and exposure, and a 3rd shareholder came into the business as Sales Director in the highly enthusiatic, hard working, competent and respected Neil Burroughs.

    With the combination of Mike's leadership, Neil's drive, and my own IT & organisational skills all brought to bear, the business increased it's client base significantly bringing in a number of key clients such as moneysupermarket.com, The Mortgage Lender, Mortgage Next, Mortgage Intelligence, BDS Mortgage Group and others. Business volumes quickly grew to over £1m monthly turnover whilst the business also expanded into the general insurance, healthcare and mortgage sectors, all whilst being courted by a number of more established competitors with an acquisitional eye.

    Eventually the business was sold to the Tenet Group in late 2004, coinciding with a change in the regulatory regime and was transformed into the service provider for directly authorised firms that continues to operate today at http://www.premieruk.co.uk

  • Owner

    Future Technology

    (Privately Held; Information Technology and Services industry)

    March 1998 — December 2000 (2 years 10 months)

    In 1998 I wanted to make a change of direction and explore new challenges, and an opportunity arose to create a business in the IT sector in partnership with a colleague I had known for some time.

    This business became Future Technology and operated a retail computer shop, a web design & hosting operation and an IT consultancy.

    Throughout my period owning and managing the business, with a business partner and a number of staff with differing skill sets appropriate to the 3 sectors of the business I greatly increased my knowledge, experience and understanding of not only the technical aspects that are a part of this particular industry, but of being a small businessman and all the challenges that this brings.

    Whilst I found the experience of owning and running this business both challenging and rewarding, my commitment to developing the next business eventually grew to the stage where I made the decision to exit the business completely, negotiating an MBO with some of the employees in the business, and it is with some pride that I see these individuals continued success as business owners in their own right.
