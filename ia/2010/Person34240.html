Robert Richards’s Summary

Comprehensive, multi-year experience with Catia software and its many applications (including Class A surfacing, sheet metal and composites workbenches).

Advanced ability to develop and present conceptual information to a wide audience.

Skilled at helping others to hone their imaginations toward the purpose of creative yet practical problem solving within an engineering environment.

Over the years I’ve had the rare opportunity to help many engineers and designers work through design obstacles and challenges using Catia as their primary tool. This has proven most enjoyable and rewarding.

Twenty-seven years total of design and engineering experience including the creation of new products by introducing unique concepts. Adept at enhancing quality of product while simultaneously improving capability of production by recognizing potential problems and presenting unified solutions. Able to demonstrate ideas to groups or on a one-on-one basis.

  • Consultant/Senior Catia Instructor

    INCAT

    (Privately Held; Information Technology and Services industry)

    September 2002 — February 2009 (6 years 6 months)

    Catia expert classification is achieved by completing 6 separate certification exams offered only by Dassault. A relative few number have accomplished this in North America.

    Please note that although I have acquired a substantial amount of experience with aerospace engineering, most of it has been while assigned to military suppliers. As such I cannot divulge details that are too specific. Some examples:

    Black Hawk, Little Bird and Chinook- This assignment was fascinating. The location task was to take stock helicopters and convert them for top secret applications (an area in which I shouldn't be specific). I also worked with Sikorsky personnel a number of times in Connecticut and in Alabama to support the Black Hawk specifically. I have also worked with GKN Aerospace to support composite design applications on the Black Hawk.

    In the Fall of 2008 I worked with V Systems and Dassault to convert a rotor housing for the Chinook from a traditional casting design to one made of composite materials. This was a very complicated task that took weeks of very rewarding work. The design was a great success.

    E2C/D Hawkeye, F35, Global Hawk - I've spent time on the order of months training at various Northrup Grumman (NG) locations (St. Augustine, Long Island and other parts of New York, Palmdale, El Segundo) as well as NG suppliers.

    777, 747 - I spent a couple of months last year working with United Airlines maintenance facility in San Francisco helping them to develop a Layout of Passenger Arrangement (LOPA) application with Catia. I showed them how to extract data from the 3D models and how to set up the assemblies so that the weight and balances could be computed automatically as a by-product of the LOPA process.

    Various business class aircraft - I spent months with Nordam in Oklahoma developing composite design standards to support their commercial customers.
