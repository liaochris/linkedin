Philip Tranfa’s Summary

Currently I'm focused on becoming as proficient in all aspects of SharePoint as possible. I started my relationship with SharePoint as a web-part developer which was an easy transition coming from a background in C# .Net development.

After conquering web-part development, I began picking up small administrative tasks: migrating users, creating site collections, etc. With the knowledge I had of the SharePoint API I realized I could automate many of these tasks by creating scripts and small console applications.

This was when I was put on a project where a large amount of its success relied on somebody being able to brand the portal. Having a background in UI development and a thorough knowledge of HTML, CSS and Web Design, I decided to volunteer to learn SharePoint branding. Within a couple of days I had a solid grasp on branding SharePoint: from creating themes and understanding of how they interacted with master pages which in turn interacted with page layouts which again in turn interacted with site columns.

So now it seems that any project I'm involved in, I have my hands in many facets of the life-cycle. I can develop storyboard layouts in Photoshop to get client sign off on design, then transition that design to the branding and creating themes and the associated assets. I assist with architecture of the site and then often develop small applications to help roll the site collection out. I complete any custom development that is required; develop web-parts, create workflows, write web-services, work with third party web-services, assist with permissions and account management and ultimately work in maintenance and support.

Philip Tranfa’s Specialties:

Architecture - based off of requirements gathering and analysis. Branding - creation of simple static storyboards through development of SharePoint themes, Master Pages and Page Layouts. Development - working with third-party web-services, writing custom web-services, creating event handlers, developing custom web-parts, creating console applications to simplify repetitive tasks. SharePoint, any and all things SharePoint.
