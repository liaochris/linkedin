J.R. Wilson’s Summary

As a full-time freelancer since 1992, I have co-authored some 100 books and written hundreds of articles for dozens of magazines, worldwide. I had full editorial responsibility for one US magazine and three UK-published books on defense and security. My writing has covered a wide range of topics - primarily aerospace and defense - but also medicine, computers (personal, business and government), Las Vegas shows/restaurants/hotel-casinos, homeland security, personal finance and more.

During my eight years with United Press International -and three metropolitan daily newspapers before that - I also gained extensive experience reporting on politics, retail and corporate business, crime, natural disasters, trials (civil and criminal), entertainment, riots, energy and much more.

I have covered both college and professional sports, including NASCAR, NHRA, LPGA, football, hockey, baseball, tennis and even international parachute-jumping. Most of those involved print and radio, as well as some still photography and cable TV news.

In 1994, I launched Pollux Communications, producing three of the Web's first professional, commercial e-zines - AeroWeb, CompuWeb and DefenseWeb.

I have traveled extensively as a writer, including international airshows in Singapore, Chile, England and Canada, and coverage of airports, airlines, space launch operations and defense issues in South Korea, Germany, Mexico, French Guiana and Switzerland.

In addition to numerous US publishers, I have written for - and in many cases been on the mastheads of - publications in England, France, Germany, Singapore and Switzerland. I also have served as an editor and writer for a number of websites, including current work on TheYearInDefense.com and OperationFranchise.com.
