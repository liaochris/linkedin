Lydia Loizides’s Summary

Ms. Loizides currently serves as Vice President, Business Planning and Development at Canoe Ventures. She leads a team of business analysts and media researchers that focus on corporate business planning and development efforts, product business requirements development and consumer and market development initiatives across all divisions of the company.

Ms. Loizides serves as Trustee, National Academy of Television Arts & Sciences (NATAS), 1st Vice President of the Board & Chair of the Advanced Media Committee of NATAS, NY, that awards Emmys for excellence in television.
She has been published in industry leading academic journals and trade periodicals and has served as Adjunct Lecturer, Center for Design, Digital Arts and Film at NYU.

Prior to Canoe, she served as VP, Interactive at Paradigm Talent & Literary Agency where she was responsible for digital business development for the agency, working with sister divisions - motion picture, television, music, comedy and personal appearance - to develop commerically viable media products.

Previously, Ms. Loizides was Vice President, Director at TCEP, part of Interpublic Group. Her objectives were to drive greater understanding of consumers’ changing relationships with traditional and emerging media technologies through advanced product development and research initiatives with IPG Clients.

Before joining IPG, Ms. Loizides was Principal of Paphion Inc., a media consultancy where she worked with media companies including Sony, Advanced Newhouse Communications, Scripps Networks, AETN, ASCAP and others, to develop and commercialize digital media products and services.

She spent seven years in business, market and consumer analytics with Ziff Brothers Investments and Jupiter Research - focusing on cable, satellite, and media and entertainment markets. She began her career in high tech, spending six years in software development with VSystems Inc. and Ncompass Labs Inc., now part of Microsoft.
