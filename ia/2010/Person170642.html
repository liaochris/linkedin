Reed Brown’s Experience

  • CEO/President

    Matchbin

    (Privately Held; Internet industry)

    November 2004 — Present (5 years 3 months)

    “Powering Local Online Communities”

    Matchbin is transforming hundreds of community newspapers, radio stations, and TV stations from “traditional media” companies to “new online media” success stories. Local online advertising is growing at over 40% per year and will reach $22B over the next 4 years. Matchbin’s technology platform ensures our media partners can quickly capture a significant market share of this local online advertising.

    Matchbin is a Web 2.0 business creating local, content-driven community websites in a revenue sharing partnership with local media outlets. Matchbin applies state of the art content management and display technologies, aggregates visitors from these sites, and provides local and national advertising specifically targeted to these site visitors. The media partners supply an existing loyal user base and ready access to local advertisers.

  • VP R&D and Manufacturing

    Iomega

    (Public Company; Computer Hardware industry)

    February 1996 — October 2004 (8 years 9 months)

    Led Iomega’s computer data storage product and process development teams in Utah, Colorado, Texas, and Toronto with 150 employees and a $25M annual budget. The engineering team developed innovative data storage solutions which achieved aggressive time-to-market goals using a wide cross-section of engineering disciplines, including electrical, mechanical, manufacturing, firmware, and software engineers.

    Directed the development and implementation of a worldwide manufacturing strategy to support the rapid production ramp required for the very successful Zip and Jaz product lines. The manufacturing teams in Utah, California, Malaysia, and Philippines quickly implemented consistent, high quality, and lean manufacturing systems to enable Iomega to ramp annual revenue from $325M to $1,700M.

  • Director Manufacturing

    Quantum

    (Public Company; QTM; Information Technology and Services industry)

    February 1994 — February 1996 (2 years 1 month)

    Led the manufacturing and process engineering team for Quantum’s high capacity disk drive product line. The operations and product development teams concurrently developed the product and process to ensure the quality, cost, and volume goals of key OEM customers were exceeded

  • Engineering Manager

    Hewlett Packard

    (Public Company; HPQ; Information Technology and Services industry)

    January 1979 — February 1994 (15 years 2 months)

    Led a combined process development and manufacturing team of 30 engineers and 750 production employees, responsible for introducing HP’s industry leading 3.5” hard disk drive product line. Managed the successful start up and transfer of production to Bristol, England for European disk drive sales.

    Fast track management career growth through various lateral and promotional positions at Hewlett Packard which provided the opportunity to develop strong technical and people leadership skills. Leadership positions included Product Engineering Manager, Process Development Manager, Production Section Manager, and Production Engineering Manager.
