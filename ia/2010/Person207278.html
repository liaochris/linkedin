Toshiaki Katayama’s Summary

Bioinformatics researcher working with KEGG project (http://www.kegg.jp/) and developed KEGG API (http://www.genome.jp/kegg/soap/) -- SOAP/WSDL web service, KEGG DAS (http://das.hgc.jp/) -- GMOD based genome browser, and BioRuby (http://bioruby.org/) -- open source bioinformatics library for the Ruby language. Currently developing accurate orthologous gene clusters for all genome-sequenced organisms, coming with new KEGG interface (http://dev.kegg.jp/oc/) to test web2.0 like features. Also working with DBCLS (http://www.dbcls.jp/) to develop integrated database for life sciences in Japan (http://lifesciencedb.jp/) and have organized BioHackathon (http://hackathon.dbcls.jp/) to gather >50 bioinformatics web service developers from all over the world in Feb 2008. As a result, launched two new services, TogoDB (http://togodb.dbcls.jp/) and TogoWS (http://togows.dbcls.jp/) in March 2008. In parallel, continuously developing cDNA databases for malaria parasite and vector mosquitos, and giving a bioinformatics training course in Indonesia. Most recently, started a new genome sequencing project for Tardigrades (http://www.tardigrades.com/), an super animal living in almost everywhere on the earth, to reveal their molecular mechanisms to survive extreme conditions, such as dry, vacuum, cold and hot (-273 to 150 degrees in Celsius), strong X-ray, toxic chemicals etc. by comparative genomics.

* Published serial articles on Linux in several Japanese magazines (1994-95, 96, 98-2000)
* Published books on Linux (ISBN:4756116620; 1996) and Bioinformatics (ISBN:4501622601; 2008)
