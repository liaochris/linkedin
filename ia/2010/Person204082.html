Tim O. Moore II, PhD, P.E.’s Summary

I graduated from Virginia Military Institute in 1997 with a B.S. in Civil/Environmental Engineering, and have been working in the water and wastewater engineering field since that time. While working as a field engineer in Northern Virginia, I obtained my M.S. from the University of Maryland in Civil/Environmental Engineering in 1999 and began designing and constructing various water and wastewater utility components. By 2003, my professional experience led me to Southwest Florida, where I concentrated on utilities engineering and project management of various local, state and federal government projects. The projects included budgeting, design and construction of potable water lift stations, water and wastewater treatment plants, deep well potable water source design and large diameter pipeline design and construction. I also managed projects involving the merging and critical review of Collier County, Florida Specification Documents and Technical Drawings as well as Collier County Master Planning Documents, both of which involved extensive meetings and correspondence with various members of the Collier County Engineering Staff. Additionally, I acted as expert witness in a number of eminent domain cases involving the legal purchase or exchange of land for the purposes of building utilities infrastructure.
