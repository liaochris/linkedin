    Austin Business Journal, Tuesday, August 3, 2004

    Shooting began Tuesday on an independent television pilot based on Austin's Poodle Dog Lounge.

    Produced by Austin-based Big Dog Productions, "Poodle Dog Lounge" will be filmed at various locations around Austin, including the Burnet Road bar in North Austin.

    The script for the pilot, which centers on Poodle Dog characters and their stories, was written by Bonnie Orr of Austin, who has crafted 21 screenplays, two teleplays and another TV pilot.

    Orr says she was inspired to write the script using the Poodle Dog Lounge as a backdrop because of its charm. A Christmas tree graces the corner of the bar all year, duct tape holds together a cracked mirror along one wall, and photos of Marilyn Monroe and Liz Taylor in their prime hang throughout the bar.

    "Just sitting in the Poodle Dog is an inspiration," Orr says. "The lounge opened in the early '60s, and about the only thing that has changed are the people."

    "Poodle Dog Lounge," directed by Darrell Kreitz of Austin, is designed to be a weekly one-hour drama set in Austin.
