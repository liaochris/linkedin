Gary Wise’s Summary

Training does exactly what it has always done, and we continue to "get what we always got" - knowledge & skills. Those outcomes are fine, but no longer good enough. Effective transfer of knowledge & skills in the classroom does not accurately predict sustainable performance in the work context - where our work force actually generates value.

Traditional design models render traditional training solutions, and lack the ability to embody the degree of agility necessary to keep pace with the velocity of business. "Training our way to competency" is dead.

Learning and work are converging - driving new performance and organizational demands. The time has come to break traditional paradigms and create sustainable, dynamic, organizational ecosystems where we support learning continuously.

Work is continuous. Why should we settle for anything less in our ability to render effective, relevant learning opportunities to our workforce?

Our learning deliverables have changed - so have the skills, competencies, methodologies, & technology infrastructure we need to sustain it. To meet the demands of business, we must change our paradigm to provide...

"Seamless, frictionless, and ubiquitous access to the right learning assets by the right learners at their moment of learning need - in the right amount - in a work context friendly format - to/from the right devices."

Easier said than done; especially, if old-school comfort zones and long-held traditions get in the way of integrating innovation and leading the change that comes with it.

This journey faces every training organization if they hope to remain effective in the eyes of the business they support. For some it will be transformational change, while for others, just a bit of fine-tuning. Either way, it will require "change" and better that we lead that change than merely manage it.

If you feel this outlook is worthy of additional conversation, we should chat. Contact me @ (317) 437-2555.
