Robert Cooley’s Summary

An educator and consultant specializing in renewable energy. Training Manager for Heliodyne, the oldest Solar Thermal manufacturer in the United States. Robert has a long history working in energy, technology and education. Robert travels nationally as a renewable energy educator and is named in a Department of Energy grant to develop a hands-on blended classroom and web based training program for use throughout the western United States. Adjunct Professor at Diablo Valley College in Pleasant Hill California actively working with many teachers nationally to collaboratively develop blended web, lab and classroom based Solar Thermal training programs.

Energy experience began in the US Navy nuclear submarine service as a nuclear electrician and continued in the oil industry. Renewable energy has been a passion of for many years. Has purchased and built several electric cars and is a member of the Electric Auto Association and has participated in electric and solar challenge races.

Robert’s love of digital technology started with the invention of the Personal Computer. He was an early adopter using personal computers in manufacturing information systems, factory floor automation and robotics. Robert was selected by the largest printer in the United States, R.R. Donnelley and Sons, to design and manage the digital infrastructure for their first green field startup in Mexico. He has also managed teams of consultants implementing web based Customer Resource Management systems. Robert is currently a partner in a training company that teaches educators to use web based classroom tools. Robert holds a California Teaching Credential and has seven years of classroom experience specializing in adult education and open source web based Learning Management Systems. Robert has a degree in Computer Integrated Manufacturing from Purdue University and a degree in Management Information Systems from the University of San Francisco.
