Brad Furber’s Summary

Brad has more than twenty years’ experience actively engaged in the formation, development, financing, buying and selling of businesses and companies.

Brad is passionate about working with innovative technology and business models that have the potential to make a difference in the world. Brad believes in old fashioned concepts of personal responsibility and integrity, especially those championed by leaders like John Wooden and Theodore Roosevelt.

During his career, Brad has served business ventures in a variety of roles, including executive officer, director, strategic advisor, early stage investor and outside counsel. He is an angel investor in several dozen start-up and emerging growth companies, a majority of which were subsequently backed by large institutional investors and prominent venture capital firms (and over a dozen of which have achieved a liquidity event).

Brad has received numerous recognitions, awards and honors over the years as a top corporate, securities and M&A lawyer and business leader in the Puget Sound area. He was the founding Editor of Preferred Returns, the newsletter of the Venture Capital & Private Equity Committee of the Business Law Section of the American Bar Association, and a former Chair of the Business Law Section of the Washington State Bar Association.

Brad earned a B.A. (with Distinction) from the University of Washington in 1986, and an M.A. (public policy) from Duke University and J.D. from Duke Law School in 1990.

Brad Furber’s Specialties:

Entreprenuer; Executive Officer, Advisor and Director to Early Stage and Emerging Growth Companies; Mergers and Acquisitions (private, public and cross-border); Angel, Venture Capital and Private Equity financings; Public Offerings; General Counsel to Start-Up and Emerging Growth Companies (including corporate governance, general corporate, executive compensation and severance matters, trademark and patent portfolio management, software development, licensing, and strategic alliances).
