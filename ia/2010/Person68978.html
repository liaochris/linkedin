Zhengqin "James" Fan’s Summary

I am currently the leading student researcher for a robot (Dusty) which I co-invented in my lab, and I am actively looking for an investor for this technology.
This robot is mainly designed to help motor-impaired patients and older adults to retrieve dropped objects on the floor. The task of retrieving a dropped object takes a patient at least 30 mins(sometimes forever), now with the robot it can easily be done within 40 seconds.

The robot is extraordinarily effective (success rate > 97%) and very easy to work with. Moreover, it's low-cost.

Now, we are doing user studies with ALS patients in Emory, and the data look fascinating. All the patients are looking forward to buy one on the market, which prints a bright future for the commercialization of Dusty.

If you are interested in our robot, no matter you are a potential user or investor, or a friend of them, please feel free to contact me.

Graduate research:
* Dusty: Household Assistive Robot (Present)
We are developing an assistive robot capable of reliably grasping household objects from the floor for people with motor impairments. I'm currently the leading student researcher of this project. This robot eventually will be helping people in homes and hospitals.
Programming language: Python
OS: Ubuntu/linux

* Human Hand Trajectory Adaptation (07-09)
This research was to examine the critical condition that turns the human arm trajectory from a straightline-like path into a curve path This information helps us better understand how we recognize different mechanical properties of everyday objects and adapt our interactions with them.
Programming Language: C++
OS: Windows


Undergrad research:
* Flapping-wing underwater robot (06-07)
Design and build an underwater 6-DOFs flapping-wing penguin robot, which can dive, swim and dance freely underwater.
Programming Language: Assembly
OS: Windows
