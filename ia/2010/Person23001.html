Betty Huang’s Summary

I have represented individuals from various different backgrounds, ethnicities and nationalities from across the world in assisting them to obtain their legal permanent residency (i.e. greencard), their nonimmigrant visas (including B, E, F, H1B, K, L, O, P, U), waivers of inadmissability (i.e. I-601, extreme hardship waivers), and their U.S. citizenship (i.e. naturalization). I have accompanied my clients to interviews with USCIS officers located in the New York, New Jersey and Pennsylvania. I have represented my clients in deportation/removal hearings in Immigration Courts located in New York and New Jersey and via telephonic hearings in Pennsylvania and Texas. I have represented individuals at the Board of Immigration Appeals and at the U.S. Distrcit Court, Eastern District of New York. I have been successful in working with ICE officers from all jurisdictions across the United States in obtaining bond for clients who are detained. I have been successful in reopening cases in which orders of deporation/removal have already been entered against an individual. I am accomplished in offering zealous and competent legal representation in all forms of immigration and naturalization matters.


Other than immigration and naturalization matters, I am an advocate for women and men who suffer domestic violence. I have assisted these individuals in obtaining orders of protection from Family Court and/or Criminal Court. Furthermore, I have represented individuals in obtaining uncontested divorces in New York.

I am fluent in Chinese (Mandarin and Taiwanese) and proficient in Spanish.

Betty Huang’s Specialties:

Immigration Law, Nonimmigrant Visas, Immigrant Visas (consular processing and adjustment of status), O-1 Artist Visas, K-1 Fiance(e) Visas, Deportation Defense, Removal Defense, Motions to Reopen (with the Immigration Courts and Board of Immigration Appeals), Hardship Waivers (I-601), I-360 Abused Spouse Petitions (VAWA), Family Immigration (including legal permanent residency through marriage), EB1, Naturalization (Citizenship), Uncontested Divorces, Women's Advocate
