Paul To’s Summary

Paul is CEO & Founder of emota.net, a Silicon Valley start-up in stealth mode. emota.net has a team of top notch scientists working on an exciting new category of products, applying latest research in ambient intelligence, awareness systems and emotional communications to Health 2.0.

Prior to emota.net, Paul was in charge of Nortel's Incubator and it's Open Innovation programs. Paul joined Nortel in September 2006 with 18+ years of experience driving innovation and commercialization of new technologies. He ran part of Nortel's Advanced Technology Labs and he brought a Silicon Valley style venture process model into Nortel. Within a short 18 months, the incubator Paul set up generated two "Product of the Year" awards, 40+ patents, and 10 ventues.

Before Nortel, Paul played leadership roles in very large companies like HP, Bank of Montreal, a Silicon Valley 2001 IPO called Riverstone Networks, and ran his own OSS software business. Paul has a proven track record of entrepreneurial innovation, with an ability to recognize & incubate strategic opportunities from the very early stages. Paul's current hobby is in studying and practicing open innovation models, and unleashing hidden power of innovation that is embedded within small and large companies alike.
