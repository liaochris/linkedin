Josh Manheimer’s Summary

Dear Friend,

"Oy, gevalt!" my mother said to me years ago. "You'll starve for sure."

She was talking about my decision to reject medicine and become a copywriter --

-- a copywriter who just learned that his direct mail package for U.S. News walloped their voucher control, won a Gold Circulation Management Award, and caused the entire promotion department to leap from desk to desk like grasshoppers in a May meadow.

Then I discovered my launch for MaryJanesFarm magazine was pulling over 10%, causing the Publisher and list brokers to be caught on security cameras dancing arm-in-arm to the Virginia Reel.

Finally, my new control for the Handyman Club was such a big winner, the Marketing Directors replicated it for all ten of their clubs, and then were heard singing Swedish Christmas carols door-to-door in downtown Minnetonka, Minnesota.

That’s just for starters.

Over the years, I’ve created the most successful direct mail package in the history of the Meredith Corporation, and the most successful package in the history of Rodale’s Book Division.

My winner for More magazine indexed 200. My control for the Ten Minute Tech indexed 340. My package for The Asian Wall Street Journal quadrupled response, and it was written -- in Chinese!

I’ve had a five-year control for Popular Mechanics, an eight-year control for The N.Y. Review of Books, and a ten-year control for Writer’s Digest.

As you might imagine, I’ve won numerous awards ... been featured regularly in the trades ... and published in two books, “Million Dollar Mailings” and “The World’s Greatest Direct Mail Sales Letters.”

In short, I’m pretty good at my day job.

Who knows? Maybe someday I’ll get to write the most successful control in the history of your organization and cause your promotion department to behave outrageously in lederhosen ... on national television.

I certainly welcome the opportunity to try.

Josh Manheimer
Norwich, Vermont
