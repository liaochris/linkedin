Rick Horowitz’s Summary

I'm a nationally recognized writer with strong communications skills in a variety of formats, and across a wide range of media. I'm a nationally respected writing coach, editor and mentor, with a perceptive eye and a practical approach that brings immediate improvements and long-term growth.

I have a background in law, government service and journalism, with a proven record of writing effectively on behalf of clients as well as in my own voice. I bring outstanding analytical, organizational and interpersonal skills, and broad interests in policy and politics.

I'm an energetic self-starter, but equally adept at working with others to come up with effective solutions for specific needs. I'm comfortable in public settings or working behind the scenes, in one-on-one and group settings.

I'm accustomed to meeting deadlines. I'm committed to getting results.

Rick Horowitz’s Specialties:

Opinion writing, column writing, TV commentary, video commentary, writing for multiple platforms, public policy, government and politics, writing coach, newsroom coaching, public speaking, political satire, political commentary, humor, satire, parody

Rick Horowitz’s Experience

  • Adjunct Professor

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

  • Newsroom Coach

    "Getting Your Words' Worth" Writing Workshops

    (Writing and Editing industry)

    1996 — Present (14 years )

    I founded “Getting Your Words’ Worth” Writing Workshops to offer a range of coaching services to journalists nationwide.

    I've conducted successful workshops and critiques for newspapers and newspaper groups, state and regional press associations and other journalism organizations, including the American Press Institute, American Society of Newspaper Editors, National Writers Workshops, National Conference of Editorial Writers, NCEW’s Minority Writers Seminar at Vanderbilt University, and Bloomberg College Editors’ Leadership Workshop at Columbia University.

    I've attended American Press Institute’s “Train the Trainer” and Poynter Institute’s “Best Practices for Newsroom Training” workshops to keep current with latest training techniques.

  • Commentator

    Milwaukee Public Television

    (Telecommunications industry)

    1995 — Present (15 years )

    I've received seven Emmy nominations, and two Emmy Awards, for my weekly commentaries on Milwaukee Public Television's public-affairs program, "InterCHANGE."

    I've presented video commentaries for Milwaukee Public Television and YouTube, radio commentaries for NPR’s “All Things Considered,” and feature pieces for newspapers and magazines

    As chief substitute host/producer for "InterCHANGE," I select topics, provide background information and pertinent links to panel members, write program scripts, and lead on-air discussions.

  • Writer/Lyricist

    Hexagon: Washington's Only Original Political Satirical Musical Comedy Revue

    (Professional Training & Coaching industry)

    1990 — Present (20 years )

    I've written numerous comedy sketches, and the lyrics -- and occasionally, even the tunes -- for dozens of original songs satirizing the news and various public figures who've made their mark on the news. (You know who you are!)

  • Syndicated Columnist

    Rick Horowitz Column

    (Privately Held; Online Media industry)

    1981 — Present (29 years )

    I've written a twice-weekly syndicated column for newspaper opinion pages and websites, and appear regularly in The Huffington Post and on other national blogs.

    I've won two National Headliner Awards for my column, as well as numerous state and local awards for my column, and for TV commentary, feature, magazine and sports writing.

  • Board Member, Foundation Board Member, Committee Chair, Dean of NCEW U.

    National Conference of Editorial Writers

    (Writing and Editing industry)

    1986 — 2008 (22 years )

    I served as a board member of National Conference of Editorial Writers, and as board member and secretary of the NCEW Foundation.

    As an NCEW committee chair, I coordinated NCEW’s participation in, and led multi-member contingent at, first Unity conference for journalists of color. I organized workshop and critique sessions, as well as outreach efforts to current and potential opinion writers.

    As Membership Chair, I helped increase NCEW membership to all-time record levels. I also instituted NCEW's first network of regional captains, talent-spotting members with leadership potential and providing them the chance to test their wings -- and help the organization.

    I led writing-critique groups at many NCEW conventions, and as overall Critique Chair, helped generate the largest critique participation of recent decades.

    As Dean of NCEW U. on three different occasions, I worked with NCEW presidents and convention chairs to choose topics, select knowledgeable and effective member-presenters, schedule, promote and host lineups of skill sessions.
