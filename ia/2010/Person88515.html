James Schmeling’s Summary

Academic administrator offering track record of building successful, publicly-engaged institutes in public and private universities, contributing to revenue generation, creating collaborations, and engaging communities. Extensive experience in improving processes, establishing effective teams, developing and implementing efficient internal and external initiatives, and identifying creative solutions.

Published on entrepreneurship and disability, Supreme Court interpretations of the ADA, applicability of the ADA to Ticket to Work Employment Networks, access to courts, study of corporate culture, and disability as a component of diversity. Experienced as project director for National Council on Disability ADA Employment Study, as senior researcher on National Institute on Disability and Rehabilitation Research projects, and as technical assistance provider for Department of Labor projects.

U.S. Air Force veteran, served 6+ years, honorably discharged at rank of sergeant. Earned law degree, with distinction, from the University of Iowa in 1999, and B.A. in political science with minor in international studies from Iowa State University in 1997.

James Schmeling’s Specialties:

Academic administration, publicly-engaged research and capacity building, veterans and disability, entrepreneurship and disability, corporate culture, diversity, disability law and policy, grant writing and administration, and pro-active management and problem-solving.
