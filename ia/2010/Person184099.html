Scott Jones’s Summary

Summary
25 years experience in sales, marketing, and advertising analytics, web analytics, marketing, CRM, sales support and prospecting, and related areas.

Areas of Expertise and Interest

1) Dashboards, data visualization, graphic analysis, and business intelligence. In today's data-dense, information-glutted environment, it isn't enough just to provide tables of data. I'm a strong believer in dashboards and understandable graphics that effectively "tell the tale." I am a disciple of Edward Tufte, Stephen Few and others who promote this movement of useful and helpful presentation of data and information.

2) SQL and database development. I believe it's important for any S&M analyst to have a strong knowledge of SQL and databases in general. Why? It's the only way to really get at the data and find problems. I've realized in my work that data must be suspect until its integrity has been proven. An analyst who doesn't have knowledge of the underlying technology will probably not see rotten data until it rears its ugly head.

3) Best practices. I'm a strong believer in best practices and standardization because (a) It helps a company to rise above limited experience and institutional politics, and prevents it from becoming ingrown. (b) It allows a company to better compare itself to the competition. (c) It provides more objective measures for improvement and growth.

4) Web analytics. Web analytics provides a great opportunity to find out more about customers, the competition, and potential new business.

5) CRM. Customer relationship management can sometimes be over-hyped, but it really is crucial within the scheme of any company. Software is helpful, but I've found that the related processes are more important. The better the processes and associated business practices, the better the company will be able to manage its customers. Whether it's SaaS or enterprise software depends on the company, its means, and goals.
