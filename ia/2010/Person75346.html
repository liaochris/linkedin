  • US Research , Corporate & Business Development

    Capitalis Technology GmbH

    (Biotechnology industry)

    February 2010 — Present (1 year )

    The optical spectroscopy widely known as surface plasmon resonance (SPR) determines refractive index changes close to a surface in a quick and uncomplicated manner. A guided wave is excited at the interface between a thin metal layer and the aqueous analyte solution using polarized light and a special coupling optic. The energy travelling in this guided wave is missing in the reflected light and leads to a typical minimum of the light reflection - the "surface plasmonen resonance"; its exact position being determined as measured value.

    SPR works without additional molecular labels in contrast to marker-based methods like X-ray or fluorescence. The development of various SPR sensors is a key step for the widespread implementation of this technology. A multitude of SPR applications has been demonstrated previously, whose number will continuously grow with the improvement of the measuring technology and the chip miniaturization.
