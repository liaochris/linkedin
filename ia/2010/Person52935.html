Darrell Commander’s Experience

  • Independent Software Developer

    Self-Employed

    (Computer Software industry)

    January 2009 — Present (1 year 7 months)

    -- Continuing development of VirtualGL, TurboVNC, TigerVNC, and libjpeg-turbo on a contract basis.
    -- Worked with Cendio AB to develop a build procedure for the VirtualBox 3D acceleration libraries on Windows 64-bit guests.
    -- Worked with UCAR to develop authentication extensions for TurboVNC.
    -- Working with SolidBox to develop a SolidWorks benchmark.
    -- Working with Radiant Sage Ventures to add multi-threaded compression and decompression support and an automatic lossless refresh feature to TurboVNC.
    -- Released TigerVNC's high-speed JPEG codec as a stand-alone open source SDK (rebranded as "libjpeg-turbo".) We claim 2-4x speedup over the unmodified libjpeg code.
    -- Worked with Cyan Optics to add X Video support to VirtualGL 2.2, with the goal of restoring optimized support for Sun Ray environments (which was lost with the discontinuation of the Sun Shared Visualization product.)
    -- Worked with Cendio AB to add TurboVNC performance enhancements to the TigerVNC project, a new project formed by former TightVNC developers and based on the RealVNC 4 code base. This involved tuning the open source libjpeg/SIMD codec at the assembly level, resulting in double the performance.
    -- Developed a plugin architecture for VirtualGL 2.2 which allows companies to write their own proprietary image transports.
    -- Working with Aquatic Services to develop and maintain a Windows port of the AquaServ embedded system software. AquaServ provides a low-cost solution for visualizing, storing, and analyzing liquid process data, such as flow, turbidity, temperature, pH, etc.

  • Staff Engineer

    Sun Microsystems

    (Public Company; JAVA; Information Technology and Services industry)

    December 2004 — January 2009 (4 years 2 months)

    Evolved VirtualGL/TurboVNC from research projects into the Sun Shared Visualization enterprise product. This included porting to Solaris and optimizing for that platform, developing a plugin for Sun Ray thin clients, working with ISV's to address weaknesses in VGL uncovered by their apps, extending VGL to work with parallel rendering and virtualization packages, investigating methods of quantifying perceptual loss in compressed images, developing more advanced codecs, performance analysis/optimization (some at the assembly level) to eliminate bottlenecks in certain use cases, working with Sun's Architectural Review Committees to make VGL and TVNC conform to Sun's software specifications, maintaining VirtualGL.org, writing extensive documentation and reports, developing features/fixes to address customer needs, working with other remote display S/W providers to design and develop more optimal next-gen infrastructure for use with VGL, and maintaining a farm of nightly build servers. Also performed extensive research into the feasibility of developing a VirtualGL-like technology for Windows servers.

  • System Architect

    Landmark Graphics

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; HAL; Oil & Energy industry)

    May 1999 — November 2004 (5 years 7 months)

    Designed and developed parallel & distributed computing and visualization infrastructure for Landmark's extensive suite of high-end oil & gas exploration/production applications. This included developing highly-tuned algorithms and code for dynamically adjusting the sampling window of 3D seismic data in real time, exploring the use of GPUs for general purpose computation, designing bricked seismic data formats for direct-from-disk streaming, designing a cluster and developing high-performance middleware for parallel reservoir simulation, and developing novel approaches for the remote display of seismic visualization applications. The latter project ultimately bore fruit in the form of VirtualGL, which was open sourced by Landmark in 2004.

  • System Administrator/Investor

    Bear River Internet

    (Public Company; 1-10 employees; Internet industry)

    1998 — 2000 (2 years )

    Designed, developed, deployed, and administered a Windows NT-based server farm which provided dial-up, E-Mail, news, and web services.

  • Systems Engineer

    Compaq

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; CPQ; Computer Hardware industry)

    January 1996 — May 1999 (3 years 5 months)

    Worked on research projects involving system architecture and performance. Projects included designing and developing benchmarks to quantify the differences between various system architectures (including those of competitors) and various peripherals (particularly 3D graphics adapters), developing a deep understanding of the OpenGL pipeline and its potential bottlenecks, serving as Compaq's representative to the SPEC OpenGL Performance Committee (developers of Viewperf), designing a complete hardware/middleware solution for non-linear D1 video editing with SoftImage|DS, developing a deep understanding of the performance pitfalls of computational fluid dynamics and finite element analysis algorithms on distributed memory systems, editing and review of white papers, and developing a cluster and a high-performance MPI layer to support Landmark Graphics' VIP petroleum reservoir simulator.
