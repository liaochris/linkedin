Ryan Hannink’s Summary

Seven years of consulting and technical experience in energy related projects and have published over a dozen papers and presented internationally on a wide variety of topics in the energy field. Developed cost and performance models for both conventional and alternative power generation technologies and the development of life-cycle cost models in support of energy-efficiency rulemaking efforts. Prepared over a dozen application studies that involved using advanced nuclear technologies for onsite cogeneration, water desalination, and hydrogen production. Prepared a market study for application of advanced nuclear technology for process heat and hydrogen production applications and another market study analyzing the transformation of the decorative light string market to more energy-efficient products. Developed a project risk model that evaluates the construction and operating risks of nuclear and combined cycle projects. Researched the implementation of emerging technologies in advanced nuclear power plants. Developed public outreach plans and materials in support of commercializing advanced nuclear technology in the United States and Canada. Responsible for the review of the decommissioning fund and nuclear safety and licensing issues in support of the formation of a nuclear utility's spin-off of their merchant nuclear business units.

Ryan Hannink’s Specialties:

Power plant economics, energy market analysis, project risk assessment, independent engineering review, business plan development, public outreach program development, nuclear energy
