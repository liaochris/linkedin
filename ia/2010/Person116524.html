Kilong Ung’s Education

  • Bowling Green State University

    M.S. , Applied Statistics and Operations Research , 1987 — 1989

    Coming from my undergraduate study at Reed College, my graduate study at Bowling Green University was relatively much easier. Consequently, I had lots of time to develop my social and leadership skills by being very active in party, student government, humanitarian activities, leisure writing/reading and making more friends.

    Activities and Societies:
    Treasurer of the Graduate Student Senate.

  • Reed College

    B.A. , Mathematics , 1983 — 1987

    My most difficult experience was my surviving the Khmer Rouge Killing Fields. My study at Reed College was my second most difficult experience. By the time when I entered Reed College, I had been learning English for four years -- all in high school. Reed College education had taught me the ultimate academic discipline, excellence, ethic and integrity. Much of who and what I am today has a lot to do with my education from Reed College.

  • Cleveland High School

    1981 — 1984

    With limited language and cultural understanding, I did not have it easy meeting new friends at Cleveland. Fortunately, a few close friends and teachers from WAMO came to Cleveland. Lacking excellence in literature, I made up in science. I leveraged my excellence in math and arts to inspire the teachers and counselors to invest their time helping me improve my language. By my senior year, I made a number of friends, achieved the needed level of English, understood the culture, and gained enough confidence to socialize beyond my comfort zone. Cleveland was the launching pad of my success.

    Activities and Societies:
    TAG, Junior Achievement, National Honor Society

  • Washington Monroe High School

    1980 — 1981

    WAMO has a very special place in my heart. I was a member of the school’s Math Competition Team that brought home the city wide fifth place trophy.

    This is where I met many wonderful teachers who played significant roles in the foundation to my success. I am still connected with a few of these teachers, one of whom has become more of a family member than a friend.

    It was unfortunate that WAMO was closed by the Portland Public School Board. The closure controversy gave birth to my activism. Although, my understanding of the English language and American culture was nascent, I made sign and marched in a student-body-organized protest against the school board’s decision.

    Activities and Societies:
    TAG, Junior Achievement, National Honor Society
