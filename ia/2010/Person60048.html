Dr. Niama Williams’s Summary

Niama Leslie Williams (http://www.blowingupbarriers.com), a June 2006 Leeway Foundation Art and Social Change Grant recipient, and a 2006 (July) participant in a Sable Literary Magazine/Arvon Foundation residential course in Shropshire, UK, possesses a doctorate in African American literature, a bachelor’s in comparative literature, and a master’s in professional writing. Born and raised in Los Angeles, California, she currently resides in Norristown, Pennsylvania.

Dr. Williams has participated in the Squaw Valley Community of Writers (2000), Hurston/Wright Writers Week (1996), and Flight of the Mind (1993). Her work has appeared in Poets & Writers Magazine; Dark Eros: Black Erotic Writings; Spirit & Flame: An Anthology of African American Poetry; Catch the Fire: A Cross-Generational Anthology of Contemporary African-American Poetry; Beyond the Frontier: African American Poetry for the 21st Century; Mischief, Caprice, and Other Poetic Strategies (Red Hen Press); A Deeper Shade of Sex: The Best in Black Erotica, and Check the Rhyme: An Anthology of Female Poets & Emcees. Check the Rhyme was nominated for an NAACP Image Award (2007).

Dr. Williams hosts “Poetry & Prose & Anything Goes with Dr. Ni” the first Saturday of every month from 12:00 - 1:30 p.m. EST on BlogTalkRadio (www.blogtalkradio.com). Her newsletter appears every Friday via aweber.com and can be viewed at http://www.blowingupbarriers.com/drnisnews2.html.

Of her purpose for writing Dr. Williams says: "I frequently do not err on the side of caution in my writing, but I believe in the purpose of it: to speak to the things others do not want to speak of, with the hopes of reaching that one woman, or her lover, or her friend, who refuses to deal with her pain, who hides from it, who doesn't think she'll survive it. That's the audience I hope to reach."

Dr. Niama Williams’s Experience

  • Owner

    Blowing Up Barriers Enterprises

    (Writing and Editing industry)

    November 2006 — Present (3 years 4 months)

    Blowing Up Barriers Enterprises exists to help everyone “blow up the barriers” keeping them from the lives they envision.

    Workshops entitled “Blowing Up Barriers: Attaining the Fully Imagined Life,” are geared toward "the career-discontented or those afraid to pursue their dreams." 29 weeks of drawing, creative writing, meditation and support as they envision then make deliberate moves toward "attaining the fully imagined life."

    A second workshop entitled “It’s Okay to Want: Eroticism and Survival of Sexual Trauma” serves a population close to Dr. Williams’ own personal experience and remains a significant part of her intuitive counseling ministry.

    Dr. Williams has also published nine manuscripts (in hardback and in paperback) via print-on-demand publisher Lulu.com and promotes her work and that of her interviewees on her radio show, “Poetry & Prose & Anything Goes with Dr. Ni,” on which she interviews authors about their new books and writing lives.
