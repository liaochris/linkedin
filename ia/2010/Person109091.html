Justine tenZeldam’s Summary

Justine tenZeldam has over 20 yrs. advertising and marketing experience, along with content, training and presentation skills ... pretty much anything you'd need to run a small to mid-size business. Whether you need exposure on Google & Yahoo, content, help with social marketing or in-house training, she can help.

During her career, Justine founded two community newspapers, still in publication today. Along the way, she wrote hundreds of articles for local non profit organizations, and publications such as the The Stockton Record, Amador Ledger Dispatch, Bay Area Business Women News, and The Streamlined Times.

She has also lent her time to many California Non Profits including The Art Deco Society of California, The Blue Mountain Coalition for Youth, The Alameda Naval Air Museum and others.

A strong believer in free information, she was the first to post local news online in CA in the mid-90's; as Publisher & Editor-in-Chief of the West Point News (www.MyCalaveras.com/UCN) and later with the Calaveras Times (www.GoldCountryTimes.com).

During that time, she also worked as a stringer (correspondent reporter) for The Stockton Record and other publications. Long a believer in cross-promo, she struck a deal with Don & Sylvia Leutz of KKBN and did a weekly local news spot, by remote broadcast. Advertising trade was also in order there.

She’s also a gifted trainer, whether teaching Engineers how to sell or showing "greenies" how to spell check. Whether an organization needs event-management, a newsletter, a BLOG, or someone to negotiate with the media, she can help … or at the very least secure reliable resources and reputable vendors through her extensive Bay Area network of professionals.

In between her professional sales and marketing career, Justine has also done volunteer grant writing, operated a branch of Chamber of Commerce, and is very active in social networking sites such as Myspace, Facebook and Youtube.
