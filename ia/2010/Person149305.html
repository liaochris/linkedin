    Particle Systems

    (Computer Games industry)

    1998 — 2002 (4 years )

    After I left college (actually before!) I started working as an artist at Particle systems. At the time they were working on a game called Dreadnought which subsequently became I-War. My role was as a general CG artist which required me to learn a lot about various techniques. Mostly though I became interested in the amitious CG cinematics that were scripted for the intro and outros of the game. As part of initially only a 3 man art team, we set to work creating the 20 minutes of fully rendered CG that accompanied the game. Looking back it seems almost impossible that that could have been done with such critical acclaim by only a few artists but we did.


    http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kw1m2ZW1u84&feature=related
    http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=L7AsRQXOUSY

    I learned just about all I ever wanted to know about creating exciting movie sequences, modelling, texturing, lighting and animation from that experience, I also discovered an ability to design sound effects and became Particle Systems Sound designer to boot.

    After the experience of the first game, we went on to make the sequel, Edge Of Chaos. I was now at a senior level and my experience helped me to work with and guide other artists. We created more acclaimed CG movies for the game which I also took a role in the visual design of, as well as Sound Design of both the game and CG movies.

    http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hq8P_27BnQA&feature=related

    After the success of Edge Of Chaos, Particle became part of Argonaut.
