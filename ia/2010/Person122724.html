Laura E. Kelly’s Summary

A 20-year veteran of the book and magazine publishing industries, I am now creative partner in author Warren Berger's expanding editorial business, overseeing project strategy and management, web/blog production, marketing & promotion, and editing. Our current big project is the launch of the book GLIMMER: How Design Can Change Your Life and Maybe the World, published by Penguin Press (U.S.) and Random House (Canada) in October 2009.

Read all about this project at: http://www.warrenberger.com (an author website I set up and recently updated) and the magablog http://GlimmerSite.com.

BOOK PUBLISHING

From 2001 to 2008, I served as VP/Global Editor-in-Chief at Reader's Digest books.

There I oversaw the worldwide Select Editions product line, which takes bestselling books from publishers such as Random House, Simon & Schuster, HarperCollins, Penguin and others and repackages them in a shorter anthology format. This gave me the opportunity to work with top authors such as Nicholas Sparks, Ann Rule, Lee Child, Mary Higgins Clark, James Patterson, Walter Isaacson, and many others.

As global editor-in-chief I was direct supervisor of more than 18 editorial, design, subsidiary rights, and production staff and worked closely with marketing & promotion, research, internet, public relations, and finance teams setting creative and business strategies.

My favorite projects at Reader's Digest included starting up a membership blog for the large condensed books readership; redesigning (twice) a 50-year-old, highly profitable product line; and developing and test-launching many new book product lines.

MAGAZINE PUBLISHING

Prior to Reader's Digest books, I worked in magazine publishing for many years. I was managing editor at start-up magazines such as Family Life, a national parenting magazine published by Rolling Stone founder Jann Wenner; Sesame Street Parents monthly magazine; and a variety of Hachette special-interest magazines.

Laura E. Kelly’s Specialties:

Managing startups, directing redesigns and relaunches, training others in publishing technology, both broad/mainstream and niche editorial experience.
