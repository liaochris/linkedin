Joseph Denne’s Summary

Hello. I am a Technologist and HCI specialist.

I co-founded Airlock, a multi-award winning digital indie with clients including the BBC, Channel 4, ITV, MTV, Heineken, Next, BMW, Motorola and Diesel.

Recent:

The nominations for the 2010 Webby Awards have been announced and we’ve picked up three. Battlefront is up for best website in the activism category; Teenage Cancer Trust for best charitable organisation/non-profit website; and Save Our Sounds for best use of social media.

Our major overhaul of Westminster City Council’s digital service delivery platform, MyWestminster, is now live. This marks the culmination of six months work and the end of the first phase of a complete re-imagining of what service delivery means in the borough.

Our work on the TV series ‘One Born Every Minute’ - airing later this month, revealing the ins and outs of birth as it happens - has just gone live. The project is a collaboration with Channel 4 and Dragonfly.

Our re-imagining of Westminster City Council’s website has won best corporate website at the Corpcomms awards. The site - which is powered by Symphony CMS - represents a step change in focus for local Government, providing a platform with user experience firmly at its heart.

Battlefront has won us an Emmy and picked up best youth programme at the international Banff television awards. It was a honoree at this years Webby's, was Bafta nominated and is shortlisted for a Rose d'Or. The project was produced in conjunction with Raw Television for Channel 4.

World On the Move was been nominated for two Webby Awards, including the people choice. It won silver at the Sony Radio Academy Awards. The site was produced for the BBC's Natural History Unit and Radio 4, extending their flagship series for 2008 into a fully participatory experience.

Free Thinking was also nominated for a Webby Award. The site was produced for BBC AM&I and Radio 3, bringing the annual festival of ideas alive online.
