In addition to assisting with the exhibit development, I hired and trained staff to develop and lead interactive programs designed to excite kids about art. Among the programs created were Camp-In (an overnight adventure), the Artist Toolbelt (a hands-on family guide that offered kids and their parents a way to interact with works of art as they toured the museum), The Traveling Art Show (presenter-led art shows that toured local schools and covered the elements and principles in a way that connected to core curriulum objectives), and many more.

  • English Teacher

    The United States Peace Corps

    (Government Agency; 10,001 or more employees; Government Relations industry)

    June 1995 — June 1997 (2 years 1 month)

    I was selected to serve as a Peace Corps Volunteer in Hungary not long after the fall of the Berlin Wall. I was placed in a small town near the Austrian border where I taught English as a Second Language to high school students. I was also elected to serve on the Volunteer Advisory Committee as a delegate representing the needs of volunteers in the field. Although I think it was merely an act of kindness, the high school at which I taught awarded me Teacher of the Year for the 1996-1997 academic year. The most memorable professional experience was an environmental summer camp that I and two Hungarian science teachers organized and implemented. For this, I secured some US grant funds to purchase the necessary equipment and supplies. I'm not sure who had more fun ... the students or the teachers, but that was one of the most fun weeks I have ever spent camping.
