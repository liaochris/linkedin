Rebecca Armstrong’s Summary

After working for fifteen years in the field of Arts and Education, Rebecca completed graduate degrees at the Univ. of Chicago Divinity School and Meadville-Lombard Unitarian Seminary and served two Unitarian-Universalist churches in the Chicago area before taking a position with the Joseph Campbell Foundation as International OutReach & Membership Director. During her 12 years in this position she traveled all over the world giving lectures and facilitating study groups as well as administrating an online membership program and web presence. She organized presentations at the Parliament of World Religions in 1993 (Chicago, U.S.), 1999 (Capetown, South Africa) and 2004 (Barcelona, Spain). In 2004 she received her doctorate from Chicago Theological Seminary and expanded her freelance ministry in interfaith weddings and spiritual direction. She was trained in the marriage counseling program "Prepare-Enrich" in 2004 and received certification in Divorce Mediation from Northwestern University in 2005. In 2006 she began teaching courses in religion, anthropology and ethics at Purdue University and then at DePaul University. She developed a course in business ethics called "Ethics After the Fall of Enron" which will be published as a textbook in 2011. She teaches this course in the MBA program at Purdue as well as at The School for New Learning at DePaul. She continues as adjunct faculty at both institutions, as well as teaching in Purdue's prison program at Westville, IN.
