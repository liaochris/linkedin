  • CEO

    Unified Manufacturing

    (Entertainment industry)

    January 2006 — Present (4 years 9 months)

    In 2006, James Hill established the Unified brand. Unified houses a touring merchandise and media company, as well as an e-commerce fulfillment house. Unified has created webstores and handled fulfillment for Pillar, Thursday, and Willie Nelson, and was also called upon to produce merch for Bob Dylan, The Black Eyed Peas, Willie Nelson, Beck, and Billy Corgan.

    Unified Manufacturing currently handles products for some 145 independent record labels in the United States, and in 2009, produced over 600,000 units for the music industry. Unified has clients in a variety of industries, including music, film, education, sports and publishing.


    About Unified Manufacturing

    Unified Manufacturing is different. We’re like a factory, but one with big machines and a great personality. We are a team of professional music insiders, but without the big egos. We like to have a good time, but we take your project very seriously.

    With years upon years in the music and movie industries, we thoroughly understand the creative process, the chaos, and the time-waits-for-no-(wo)man mentality. We manage graphic design, special printing, custom DVD menus, shipping, insane turn times—anything you need. You don’t have to lift a finger. We’ve also got our own CD/DVD and vinyl manufacturing plant, meaning that we don’t have a middleman shuffling around, costing you extra money.
