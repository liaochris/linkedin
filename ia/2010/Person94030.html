Jeff Six’s Summary

Jeff Six is a professional with a strong engineering, computer science, and analysis background. He was worked in both deeply technical and policy/application positions, throughout government, academic, and business environments. He is an accomplished and published expert in computer, network, and system security and has contributed to industry-wide standards through the Internet Engineering Task Force (IETF) and the Trusted Computing Group (TCG).

Jeff has demonstrated the ability and drive to learn new skills and function in challenging and dynamic environments. He has a proven and tested customer focus and the ability to adapt to new, unique, complex, and special requirements. Jeff believes that every second can be a teaching moment, where he can help someone learn something new and/or learn something new himself.

  • Senior Security Architect

    Legg Mason

    (Public Company; LM; Financial Services industry)

    March 2007 — Present (3 years 9 months)

    As Senior Security Architect, Mr. Six assists the AVP, Security Engineering and Operations, in strategic direction setting for all Information Security-related matters and leads the other engineers and analysts in their duties.     • Respond to all security-related incidents and lead an international team in analysis, containment, response, and follow-on actions.
    • Developed and maintained configuration and policy security standards for all deployed enterprise technologies, including Windows desktops and servers, Solaris and Linux servers, databases, and firewalls.
    • Review all proposed and current IT projects at Legg Mason for security impact and work with involved parties throughout the risk management process, as part of the project management lifecycle.
    • Develop custom programs and scripts to automate maintenance, monitoring, analysis, and alerting tasks using multiple technologies, including Java, JavaScript, Visual Basic Scripting, Windows batch files, Unix shell, C, and C#.
