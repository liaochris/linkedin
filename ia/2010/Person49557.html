  • President

    Personal Funds Recovery, Inc.

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Information Technology and Services industry)

    April 1995 — July 2000 (5 years 4 months)

    Personal Funds Recovery was one of the top 3 "unclaimed money" locators in the US. The company was the first to pioneer consolidated database integration for the over $400 billion in unclaimed money being held by depositories. Thanks to Fundsrecovery, consumers were finally able to search multiple depositories from one website to locate money they were owed.

    In 2000, NAUPA, the National Association of Unclaimed Property Administrators was formed to assist the government help consumers in the recovery of unclaimed funds. The service was free, and depositories we required to report to NAUPA. It became the largest collection of unclaimed records on the net.

    Unable to compete with a free service provided by a government association, Personal Funds Recovery shifted its business model towards online data integration and retrieval technology.
