Tanya (Andersen) Buckingham’s Summary

Most organizations, businesses or non-profit groups could meet their mission statements better, more efficiently, if they found a way to incorporate space...many, just don't know it yet. My work, is to let them know.

Additionally, I dedicate my time to training (and learning) technical geospatial skills with students and colleagues.

In my spare time, I work on business development for small businesses and organizations. I like to help people remember why they love what they do, when they have deviated from their path or they are searching for a new path.

Tanya (Andersen) Buckingham’s Specialties:

Cartography, teaching technical aspects of cartography, layout and design, atlas/reference research and compilation, business development, branding, organizing and planning events and meetings.

  • Assistant Director UW-Madison, Cartography Laboratory

    University of Wisconsin-Madison

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    November 2008 — Present (1 year 10 months)

    You would expect to hear that the thing I love about my job most is teaching cartographic techniques or making maps. I do love both of those aspects of my job, but the truly amazing thing about my job is that I get to work with people who are so deeply passionate about their work, and seeing the excitement they have when I can show them how to think about their work in a whole new way. I work with students learning about cartography, to researchers who have spent their lives studying anything from water, to music, to education. (The other great thing about what I do, is the diversity of topics that I get to be a part of).

  • co-founder

    mapgiving

    (Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    May 2008 — Present (2 years 4 months)

    Giving to you to give to the world.

    A meeting space for non-profit groups and cartographers: cartographers interested in helping non-profit groups by giving a little time to create useful, beautiful maps; organizations that could use these maps to extend the good work they are doing.

    What do we do? Well, we set out to give maps away. What we find ourselves doing is showing people all the incredible ways they could put maps to use to better achieve their mission. It's great to watch people "get it," to watch them discover the power of incorporating space and place into the work they thought had no space or place to be thought of.
