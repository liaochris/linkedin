Yen Cheong’s Summary

As a reporter and subsequently as a News Editor for the Yale Daily News, I learned a lot about writing clearly and concisely. Although I decided not to pursue journalism after college, my experience there -- which boiled down to being able to hook an audience with a story -- serves me well today as I determine how best to pitch our books and authors to the media.

As the Associate Director of Digital Media and Publicity of a 15-person department, I work on an average of 10 books per season and supervise one assistant. I serve as the point person for our publicity databases -- I've always maintained that media lists are underrated! -- which includes answering questions and sometimes holding training sessions.

In the spring of 2008, I started writing The Book Publicity Blog, which has been written up in publishing industry mainstays including Mediabistro's GalleyCat and Publisher's Lunch (as well as numerous other websites and blogs) and it has become popular not just with publicists and others in the publishing industry, but also with authors. As of summer 2009, The Book Publicity Blog was the second listing in a Google search of the terms "book publicity."

I also serve as the Secretary of the Board of the PPA / Publishers Publicity Association, which hosts regular media lunches and workshops.

Yen Cheong’s Specialties:

Media placements (print, broadcast and online). Special interest and expertise in online publicity and digital media / marketing and strategy including use of blogs, websites and social networks (such as Facebook -- profiles / fan pages / targeted advertising -- and Twitter) to promote books. Some experience with SEO and Wikipedia.
