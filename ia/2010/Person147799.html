Milena Mateev’s Summary

I am currently pursuing a graduate degree in Industrial/Organizational Psychology and Development to explore my vested interest in sustainability. I am interested in the relationship between human behavior and the designed environment, and more specifically – addressing the need for interdisciplinary research of organization-level benefits for going green. The elements and principles of design development are instrumental of the way an individual perceives and interacts within a space. Green build environments have positive implications on sustainable workplace behaviors, which in turn facilitate necessary change.

The other faucet I have a particular interest in is aging, and the phenomenon of accommodating the baby boomer, the X-and Y- generations into a coherent creative work force

I believe that the learning environment is the place where new ideas and social change are nurtured, hence my decision to expand and brand my knowledge, with ultimate focus on consulting and teaching.

My current professional experience is one of an Interior and Structural Designer with expertise in design, knowledge of spatial relationships and attention to detail: More specifically, I am involved in
- creating and executing concepts for interior and exterior environments per clients' specifications
- project coordinating and project management of new construction and renovations
- consulting for residential, commercial and hospitality clients
- restoration and rehabilitation of centuries-old residential and hospitality structures
- adaptive reuse projects for the entertainment industry
- set design
- acquisition and management of International Art Exhibitions and presentations
