Richard Baker’s Summary

Richard has been a London-based freelance photographer since leaving the renowned Documentary Photography course at Gwent College of Art, Newport in 1986. Since then he has worked on reportage projects for magazines, corporate image collections and on various high-profile book projects.

In 2004, he photographed and wrote 'Red Arrows', a book of documentary and landscape about the Royal Air Force's aerobatic team during their 40th anniversary year. In 2009, his photography was published in a collaborative book with the writer Alain de Botton called 'The Pleasures and Sorrows of Work' (Hamish Hamilton, 2009) followed by 'A Week at the Airport: A Heathrow Diary' also with de Botton (Profile Books, 2009).

Richard is currently exhibiting pictures from the Pleasures and Sorrows of Work at the Museum of the History of Science, Broad Street, Oxford (until September 2010) plus an ongoing show at The Institute of Mechanical of Engineers in London.

His archive is represented at Corbis and Alamy and on sale directly by searchable e-commerce at Photoshelter where images can be licensed for Rights Managed and Personal use.

Richard does not currently have representation.

Richard Baker’s Specialties:

Strong adaptation of illustrative photography for use in leading magazines, books and corporate literature where the strength of the still photographic image empowers a publisher’s layout or a company's profile. I actually enjoy public speaking too, having given many lectures on my photography projects to ad and design agencies as well as Royal Photographic Society groups nationwide. Richard considers all commissions.
