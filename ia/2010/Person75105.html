Greg Heuer CSI’s Summary

-- 40 years success producing programs of value in public and private education for both children and adults.
-- Trained as a teacher at The University of Michigan, and a trainer of others in the fields of music education, radio and TV production, theatre, photography, videography, and business management.
-- Committed to the success and integrity of fine construction professionals.

Joining AWI in 1987 as a member of the professional staff, Greg provides technical support to the AWI, design professionals, and construction industry. An enthusiastic speaker, Greg presents training and information to varied audiences at international gatherings, national conventions, regional meetings, chapter events, and individual offices.

From 1991 to 2007 he served as editor for the Quality Standards Illustrated, pioneering the migration from cut-and-paste, type-set proofs to fully desktop-published books with electronic versions on CD. He has also been instrumental in the improvement of the AWI Cost of Doing Business Report and the updating of the AWI Cost Book operational estimating system, and Chapter Leadership training. The Design Pro Presentations, prepared for the use of AWI members, have won praise from coast-to-coast.

In 2007, he joined some colleagues in the advanced wood processing industry to found the Woodwork Career Alliance of North America, a 501 (c) (3) corporation for the establishment of woodworking skill standards, evaluations, and credentials. He serves as Secretary of the corporation, and chief content validation editor for the writing and publishing of the Skill Standards.

2010 and beyond witnesses the development of the Architectural Woodwork Institute eLearning site: Always On, utilizing Moodle.

Over the years, Mr. Heuer has delivered programs to thousands of construction industry professionals and educators on architectural woodwork, the Quality Standards and the specification and use of wood, nature’s gift to man.
