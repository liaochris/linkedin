Shawn Duff Intorcio’s Summary

Ms. Intorcio has over 20 years of experience in economic impact studies, energy efficiency, load forecasting, fuel price forecasting and integrated resource planning. She has worked with electric and gas utility clients throughout North American and Europe. Ms. Intorcio specializes in the areas of program design and evaluation, verification and measurement of energy efficiency programs. She has led evaluation studies for National Grid (New York and Massachusetts), Orange and Rockland Utilities, NSTAR, Unitil, Northeast Utilities (Western Massachusetts Electric), NiSource (Bay State Gas), KeySpan, MidAmerica and Consumers Power. In addition, she directed the technical requirements efforts for the design and implementation of energy efficiency tracking data bases for Wisconsin Natural Gas and Consumers Gas (Toronto). Ms. Intorcio has presented and testified before regulatory agencies in South Carolina, Indiana and New York.

    (Privately Held; Oil & Energy industry)

    June 2009 — Present (1 year 6 months)

    Massachusetts Energy Pay & Save Process Evaluation
    Ms. Intorcio was the project lead for a statewide process evaluation of an on-bill financing program for residential and small business electric customers. The study included primary research with program participants and non-participants, a quantitative assessment of program costs, measure costs and incentive levels, and a comparative study of other loan programs offered by the utilities.

    Review of Program Evaluation for the High-efficiency Gas Water Heater Program, Missouri Gas and Electric
    The study entailed a thorough review and interpretation of the results, evaluation of the critical assumptions and conclusions in the reports/analysis and, where appropriate, recommendations for alternative assumptions that deemed superior and defensible than those used in the existing evaluation studies. The project also included a summary analysis of emission levels for alternative gas fired water heaters and an analysis of the energy savings associated with high-efficiency gas water heaters relative to electric water heater alternatives.

    Energy Efficiency and Peak Demand Reduction (PDR) Program Portfolio, FirstEnergy Ohio
    Ms. Intorcio responsible for the development of evaluation, monitoring and verification plans for FirstEnergy Ohio’s 23 energy efficiency and PDR programs. Each program plan included process, market and impact evaluation tasks, framework for data collection activities and reporting metrics.

    Fuel Switching and Carbon Scenario Analysis, British Columbia Hydroelectric Company
    Ms. Intorcio led the study of the greenhouse gas (GHG) emission effects in British Columbia (B.C.) from fuel switching from electricity to natural gas in the residential and small commercial sectors. A series of fuel switching models were developed to determine the level of fuel switching loads that would be required to achieve the targets and the impact on GHG emissions in each scenario.
