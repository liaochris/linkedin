Martin Elsaesser’s Summary

When I started my games industry career in 1997 I did not have a definite idea about where I wanted to go...

I worked as an animator and character artist, became art director, worked for an outsourcing vendor and finally became lead artist at Crytek.

This versatile artistic background enables me to work with artists from various disciplines. My knowledge of the game production process, combined with the joy to organize and optimize the art pipeline, helps me to enable art groups to excel.

I am a big fan of agile development methods used as a flexible toolset to empower teams to organize themselves in a direct, lean and efficient way.

One of my main interests besides managing art teams is the integration of external resources into the production pipeline. To properly integrate specialized freelancers, sourcing teams and outsourcing vendors is a big challenge and an area I continuously strive to learn more about.

At the moment I am leading a small team of artist at Crytek specialized on art sourcing, level art support and tech art/animation research and implementation for Crysis2.
