Thomas O'Grady, PhD’s Summary

Who am I – For the last few years and near future, I am concentrating on building for my retirement. I better, my mother lived to 93. I could live another 40 yrs. Therefore, I identify opportunities appropriate for my wife and I.

I like complex multidimensional problems and issues, particularly marketplace issues for businesses, developing Strategic Advantage and Assessing Competitiveness for opportunities and companies. I did this for the CEOs of major companies or divisions, including Mitsubishi International, some of the original positioning of the Lexus division of Toyota, Fuji, Chrysler, etc.

What made me what I am is what drives me to help others. A few life changing events and 3 people made me realize what I can do. It was just by some remote circumstances that I even met these people, but it affected me enough to change my outlook and direction. However, it still took ten years to overcome my past and develop into someone with confidence and ability and then realize ever growing potentials. I went on to be responsible for 2/3 of Soviet intelligence, then at the top of my class at Hofstra Univ. was nominated for the Rhodes (which I withdrew to marry), Danforth and Rotary (won Rotary but PhD program at UC Berkeley superceded it. After the PhD in Mathematical and Statistical Economics I went to GM Res Labs where I developed the consumer perception of quality extending work that Nobel Prize winner Daniel McFadden did; headed the automotive division at Chase; built Integrated Automotive Resources into an international company, appeared as an expert on all major TV and Radio networks over 300 times and over 30,000 times in the print media.
(That background drives me to help others seek their potential, which will be part of my book) In Japan I received a great education in Japanese culture and languages.

I am working on some investments, speaking and writing a book. I did find one thing very unique? Will it be as big for me as SF Real Estate was? Maybe.
