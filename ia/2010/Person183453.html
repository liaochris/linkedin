    Wells Fargo's Schwab To Resign From Asset-Based Lending Group



    DOW JONES NEWSWIRES

    Wells Fargo Co. (WFC) said Peter Schwab, chairman and chief executive of the company's asset-based lending group, will retire at the end of the year, after working in that industry for 39 years.

    The banking giant said Henry Jordan, currently president and chief operating officer of the group, known as Wells Fargo Capital Finance, will succeed Schwab.

    Schwab, an industry veteran, joined Foothill Capital Corp. in 1982 and became its president and chief operating officer in 1986. In 2000, he was named chairman and CEO of Wells Fargo Foothill, and in 2006, became the head of the company's asset-based lending group.

    Jordan, meanwhile, joined the company in 1984 as controller for Foothill after several years of auditing the company with an outside accounting firm. He served as CFO of the group for seven years in the 1990s, was promoted to president in 1997 and added the COO title three years later.

    Wells Fargo Capital Fiance is the trade name for some asset-based lending, accounts receivable and purchase order finance services of Wells Fargo. It provides financing to specific sectors, including retail, software publishing and high technology.

    Wells Fargo's shares closed down 18 cents to $25.35 and were inactive in after-hours trading.
