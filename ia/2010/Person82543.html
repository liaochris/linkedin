Holly Falk-Krzesinski’s Summary

As Director of Research Team Support, Dr. Holly Falk-Krzesinski focuses on advancing collaborative, team-based, cross-disciplinary translational biomedical research. Current activities include: engaging in research development activities to catalyze new clinical and translational research initiatives; conducting & supporting research on the empirical field of the science of team science; serving as a conduit to translate empirical findings from team science research into evidence-based guidance to scientific teams; developing & implementing resources & tools that promote collaboration; chairing the Annual International Science of Team Science Conference.

Dr. Falk-Krzesinski launched a national network of research development professionals, which evolved into the National Organization of Research Development Professionals. She serves as the first NORDP president & chair of the Annual Research Development Conference.

Dr. Falk-Krzesinski was the founding director of Northwestern’s Office of Research Development. Her group fostered numerous major interdisciplinary team research initiatives spanning structural genomics of infectious diseases, cancer nanotechnology, regenerative medicine, oncofertility, art history, bioenergy, to solar energy, securing over $140M is federal grants and contracts.

Dr. Falk-Krzesinski is a research asst professor in the NUCATS Institute at the medical school & a sr lecturer in the Department of Biochemistry, Molecular Biology, & Cell Biology in the college of arts & sciences at Northwestern. She teaches a graduate-level Team Science course as well as Grantsmanship courses for researchers & nonprofit organizations. She also conducts grantsmanship, finding funding opportunities & CV/cover letter wrkshps nationally. Dr. Falk-Krzesinski has partnered with NU Prof. Katherine Faber to develop and implement the Navigating the Professoriate Program, a professional development program for early career women faculty in STEM disciplines.
