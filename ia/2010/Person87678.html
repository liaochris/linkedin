Jamaal Bell’s Summary

Last year, I developed a media relations and social media plan to effectively advocate the Kirwan Institute as a social justice leader and a race/ethnicity thinktank.

Launched, Race-Talk.org blog online magazine December 1, 2009. Recruited more than writers, educators, social justice leaders and activists from across the US and Canada to volunteer to contribute to Race-Talk.

Negotiated with Arianna Huffington from The Huffington Post to agree to place Race-Talk articles on The Huffington Post web site. I created and I am the executive editor of Kirwan’s Race-Talk blog.

Effectively executed and promoted Kirwan’s Facebook and Twitter page in September. Sought out specific target audiences (educators, social justice leaders, activists and cultural advocates). Within two months we have 265 Facebook fans where 85 percent are our target audience; and 145 twitter followers where 90 percent are our target audience.

In 2008, I’ve successfully conceptualized and designed campaign materials for the Big Walnut Local Schools’ Bond Issue that passed at an unprecedented 56 percent. I was the graphic designer and copywriter for all marketing materials including: yard signs, flyers, postcards, business cards, newsletters, Web site copy and informational brochures. During this campaign, the Superintendent and I worked to ensure that all materials were accurate, brief and clear.

I used Mac-based Adobe Photoshop, InDesign, Acrobat Pro and Illustrator to design the materials. Please read my resume, it further explains numerous successes I’ve had in other organizations.

I have three years of leadership in the Navy. My leadership awarded me the second highest award you can receive without combat, The Navy & Marine Corps Achievement Medal, and the Good Conduct Medal. I managed more than 10 personnel from diverse backgrounds. I've managed people older than me, a different race or cultural background than me, and one time I managed all women.
