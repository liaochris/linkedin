Michael Terpin’s Summary

I have operated a leading high-tech PR and online marketing firm since 1990. During the "first Internet boom" we launched such brands as the America Online Greenhouse, EarthLink, the Motley Fool and Jupiter Communications. Today, we represent a variety of Web 2.0, consumer electronics/videogame, wireless and IT companies, including Augmentum, BitGravity, CyberDefender, Entropia Universe, MyPublicInfo, the Red Herring and Xandros.

In 1999, I spun off our newswire division, Internet Wire, as a standalone company and raised more than $25 million from top-tier investors including Hummer Winblad and Sequoia Capital. Following a strategic investment by NASDAQ in 2003, the company changed its name to Market Wire and was sold to CCN/Matthews in April 2006, which in turn was sold to OMERS Capital Partners in December, 2006, for more than $100 million. Marketwire (www.marketwire.com) is currently the world's third largest public relations/investor relations newswire.

My latest venture is Social Radius (www.socialradius.com), a social media marketing firm specializing in social media outreach, social media content creation and socially-based traffic building, lead generation and search optimization. Our client roster includes Bombay Sapphire, Constant Contact, David Lynch Foundation, Dazzboard, Denihan Hospitality Group, Digital Media Wire, Mogreet, Philips, Power.com, Rallycast, Red Herring, SproutBaby, TuneWiki and VenueGen.

One of our biggest successes was the launch and viral seeding of the "Yes We Can" video by Black-Eyed Peas lead singer Will.i.am in support of the Obama campaign (www.yeswecansong.com). This campaign garnered more than 40 million views of the full video, as well as traditional media coverage of more than one billion impressions, including six articles in the New York Times and prominent interviews on The Today Show, CNN, CNBC and other leading media outlets worldwide. The effort won both an Emmy and a Webby.

  • Founder, CEO, Chairman

    Internet Wire

    (Public Relations and Communications industry)

    October 1994 — April 2003 (8 years 7 months)

    I started Internet Wire, which many years later would be renamed Marketwire, in October, 1994, with one employee and a 14.4 baud modem. The inspiration for creating the world's first Internet-based press release network was the number of complaints I received from journalists who would have to retype press releases faxed to them by the incumbent press release newswires of the day, which were only real-time and marginally digital if you were a member of the Associated Press, which meant that most trade publications got faxes instead.

    We established a solid distribution network, including real-time feeds to Bloomberg terminals and tens of thousands of editorial organizations, by 1998, all on a barebones staff of five. When I made the decision to raise money (first angel money, then top-tier Silicon Valley venture investors), the wild ride was on, and we grew to 135 employees in 18 months, growing revenues by more than 4,000 percent.

    Our angel investors were Osprey Ventures, Montreaux Capital Partners and Mindful Partners. In January, 2000, we closed our Series A round of $18.5 million from Sequoia Capital and Hummer Winblad Venture Partners. Our board members included Ann Winblad, Sameer Gandhi and Chris Kitze.

    We started working on the strategic partnership deal with NASDAQ in early 2001. When it closed, we changed the name of the company to Market Wire.
