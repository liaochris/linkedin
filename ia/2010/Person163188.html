Peter van Vliet’s Summary

Peter JWG van Vliet founded iNSnet, internetwork for sustainability in 2002. The iNSnet Foundation acquired a leading position in providing news and information about sustainable development on the internet, conducting research into sustainable consumer behavior and mainstreaming sustainability in general.

From the iNSnet Foundation several succesful projects were launched, such as www.duurzaamnieuws.nl, www.duurzaamheidmonitor.nl and www.planet-ads.nl.

iNSnet co-initiated the lobby for the transition towards a sustainable economy, which lead to the Declaration of Tilburg ( http://www.economischegroei.net/index.php?topic=TilburgDeclaration&style_id=0 ) and its annual congress.

iNSnet also co-initiated a petition for the introduction of the German EEG regulation (Feed-in tariff for renewable energy) in the Netherlands. This is now followed by the launch of Zonvogel, a cooperative for local solar energy generation. ( http://www.insnet.org/feedin http://www.zonvogel.nl )

Peter van Vliet studied marketing, graphic design and communications at several institutions and followed additional courses in environmental sciences. At present he is studying cultural sciences at the Open University. During and after his college period he took part in numerous environmental groups and was co-founder and first chairman of the regional chapter of the first Dutch green party.

He held consulting and executive positions in several communications agencies and coordinated the Asian network of a major European building equipment firm.

After his return to the Netherlands he established an independent media consultancy that was a pioneer in cross-media concepts, combining both print and electronic media. He was involved in developing future scenarios for governmental institutions and strategic planning for a regional Centre of Expertise for ICT.

Peter van Vliet was and is member of the board of several foundations and social ventures.
