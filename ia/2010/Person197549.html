Jeremy Gislason’s Summary

Jeremy has been around business all of his life and grew up in an entrepreneurial family. His father has had his own business for over twenty-five years and Jeremy assisted with the day to day operations of the family business.

Jeremy traveled overseas a great deal, especially throughout asia in the early 90's and eventually decided to settle down in Japan. He and his wife both quit their 'regular' jobs in the spring of 1997 to fullfill their dreams of running their own business.

Between 2003 - 2004 he was a company advisor at ISORegister Inc where he gained much needed online experience in building membership sites and online marketing.

He then ventured on his own and launched his flagship site SureFireWealth.com in Jan. 2005. He then incorporated SureFireWealth Inc and has been expanding rapidly ever since.

In 2005 Simon Hodgkinson and Jeremy Gislason partnered and went on to create a multitude of successful membership based websites and business ventures. Between those sites they did well over $3 million in sales together.

In 2008 Jeremy and Simon joined forces with internet marketing expert John Delavera and niche marketing expert JP Schoeffel and went on to create the very successful Dynamic4Marketing.com series.

He also partnered with video expert Louis Allport in 2008 to create their very successful private label right DVD collection.

These strategic partnerships over the years have helped to form very powerful mastermind alliances and are a perfect example for the new Mindmap to Riches series of success books.

Jeremy and his wife are currently raising three boys whom they love and cherish. Jeremy enjoys spending time with his family when he's not working. He also is an avid reader and tries to read at least one book a week mainly on business, marketing, and self-development. He also studied Japanese for many years, has over 6 years of training in the Japanese martial art of Aikido and is an avid outdoorsman.

Jeremy Gislason’s Specialties:

Jeremy says, "Doing business in Japan has really opened my eyes to customer service and the way people should be treated. Japanese have the utmost respect for customers, as should all businesses. There is a saying in Japanese - "Kyakusama wa kami sama desu", which literally means "Customers are Gods". If you think about this, it is very true as without customers, we'd have no business. Take care of your customers and they'll take care of you.
