  • Owner

    @ web one

    (Internet industry)

    April 2007 — Present (3 years 4 months)

    I still love doing what I do, and guiding my staff to do the best they can do to server our clients well.

    The motivating factor for me is getting the job done well, not cutting corners to reduce costs, but exceeding our clients expectations, so the work we do for them is a valued investment of both their time and money.

    They expect a return from their website and we go all out to achieve that for them.

    We see that as a major difference between us and our competitors, and even though our prices are very reasonable, we believe that our clients get a good bang for their buck.

    Search engine optimisation research and planning still takes up a great deal of my time, and to this day we are in the top 10% in NZ in creating websites that can be found by search engine users looking for a product or server relative to a location. We can of course get our clients business found under their business name, with a few minutes work, but think of those potential clients who don't know what the business name is and are looking for a service or product. My estimation is that 80% of websites are not search optimised even though those website owners have "paid" their developer for it.

    In a recent case, we were referred by our opposition because their client after 1 year could only be found in position 64 of Google for their major phrase - we had a quick look at it for 10 mins, made changes in the site in 5 minutes, uploaded it, and within a week they were page 1, position 5.

  • Owner

    Cyberlink Web Services

    (Internet industry)

    April 1994 — June 2002 (8 years 3 months)

    When I first saw a browser on the net (prior to that it was all uucp on my trusty Amiga) I thought this was going to change the world.

    My Internet first start company. Started at home, with one employee and the first in Nelson to make websites. In those days, dial up was the only option and websites were pretty primitive.
    In those days there were no dreamweavers, frontpage or much help at all on the web, so it was a case of viewing the source code of how websites were made, and out with Notepad.
    Hosting was pretty foreign (but with the help of the guys at nz.com - different ownership in those days I slowly got to grips with ftp etc).

    I am not the brightest programmer on the block and to this day I don't call myself a programmer, so it was a huge amount of hours learning, and am still learning now.

  • South Island Merchantising Manager

    (Consumer Services industry)

    1989 — 1992 (3 years )

    Responsible after the split from the Post Office for setting up the merchandising retail areas in the "Post Shops" around the South Island.
