Atul Varadhachary’s Summary

Dr. Atul Varadhachary recently stepped down as President & Chief Operating Officer of Agennix, a publicly traded biotechnology company based in Houston, Princeton and Munich. Agennix’s lead molecule, talactoferrin alfa, is a novel anti-cancer agent in Phase III development for the treatment of patients with lung cancer and in Phase II development for other cancers and in patients with severe sepsis.

Before Agennix, Atul was a Senior Engagement Manager at McKinsey & Co., serving in their Houston office since 1994. He co-founded and ran Pratham Health (now the Niramaya Health Foundation), a preventive health program reaching 65,000 poor pre-school children in India, while on leave of absence from McKinsey. Atul, a founding director of Pratham USA, serves on the Niramaya Board and as Pratham USA President.

Atul serves on the Board of the Harris County Hospital District (HCHD). Harris County, the third most populous county in the U.S., includes the city of Houston. The HCHD’s three hospitals, 12 Community Health Centers, a dental center, 13 homeless shelter clinics and four mobile health clinics provides a broad range of health, nutrition, health education and social services to over a million under-insured and uninsured individuals living in Harris County.

Atul served as President of the Indo-American Chamber of Commerce of Greater Houston (IACCGH) from 2007 to 2009, and serves on its board. He also serves on the Life Sciences Advisory Committees of the Houston Technology Center and of the University of Houston. Atul is an Adjunct Professor at the Baylor College of Medicine and at the Jones Graduate School of Management at Rice University where he has taught a course on Entrepreneurship in Biotechnology since 2003.

Atul received his M.D. from the University of Bombay. He earned a Ph.D. in Physiology from the Johns Hopkins School of Medicine in Baltimore, followed by a postdoctoral fellowship in Biological Chemistry, also at Johns Hopkins.
