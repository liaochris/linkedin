    (Non-Profit; Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    September 2006 — October 2009 (3 years 2 months)

    • Relocated to Bolivia to establish and manage an IJM field office in the city of La Paz.
    • Tasked with designing, managing and budgeting the program, conducting risk assessment studies, and studying all challenges and opportunities related to the establishment of a field office in the country, and creating operational and staffing plans for the Bolivia office.

    • Received Knighthood award in 2004 from the Bolivian National Police, the Institution’s highest civilian honor, in appreciation for leading efforts to improve police attitude and civilian treatment.
    • Received “Gold Emblem” award in 2009 from the Bolivian National Police, a recognition given to individuals and organizations that have provided significant assistance to the police in its crime fighting efforts.
    • Regular speaker and trainer to public justice officers in the country in topics related to child sexual abuse, domestic violence and trafficking in persons.
