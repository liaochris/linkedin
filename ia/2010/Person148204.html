Silas Molino’s Summary

2010: Completed a 27 page argument on the survivability of the Al Qaeda network. I looked at four organization which have taken upon them the name of al Qaeda: Al Qaeda in Iraq, al Qaeda in the Islamic Maghreb, al Qaeda of the Arabian Peninsula, and al Qaeda of Somalia.Finished a short paper on ECOWAS efficacy in regional management of security and good governance. Conclusion: Although inefective, the ECOWAS is the only organization capable of addressing West Africa's regional security issues.

2009: Participated in testing a methodology for the Institute for Analysis (IFA) on leadership succession. Our goal was to see if the methodology provided could be used to predict leadership succession in the Russian Mafia organization Vory V Zakone (Thieves-in-Law). I provided research and a data table which concluded in the discovery of loose associations of Russian Mafia leaders, including the Vory V Zakone, which rendered the provided methodology inadequate. We are currently working on an improved methodology which may assist the IC in looking at the Russian Mafia Leadership structure and succession differently.

2008: Participated in the creation of STORM methodology which analyzes the extent to which all countries act as Terrorist Nodes of Operation (TNOP). This project was funded under the National Security Agency (NSA) as a challenge to CSUSB graduate students. My involvement in the creation of the methodology was in participating in a group of four in looking at history, trends, and operations of the terrorist group Al Qaeda of the Islamic Maghreb (AQIM). Our group gained particular notoriety during the presentations to the Intelligence Community (IC) in which the information we provided was used as the example for the efficacy of the methodology.

Spring 2008: Internship with the Hudson Institute in Washington DC under Richard Weitz in the Center for Future Security Strategies (renamed Center for National Security Strategies).
