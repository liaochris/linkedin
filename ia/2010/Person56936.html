Dirk Vandereyken’s Summary

Dirk's first real relevation came to him at age ten, when a classmate told his peers about The Lord of the Rings. Dirk knew he had found his calling and one year later on, he wrote his first novel, Ijs: De verloren planeet. Two years later, a well-known Flemish director and actor read Dirk's script and convinced him to have it published. At age 14, Dirk became the youngest Flemish sci-fi author ever.

Dirk wrote another two novels, was appointed Regional Director for the Role-Playing Game Association Network for Belgium at age 15, founded his own RPG club, started several fanzines and organised many different events and conventions. He took up hypnotherapy in England and continued to take courses in Belgium. Dirk studied psychology at the University of Leuven and became a Master in NLP. By this time, he had already set up his own private practice and organisation, Psycho Diversity Project. He wrote courses for the Belgian Spaar Unie, where he became the top salesman, worked exclusively with troubled children during the day for one entire year and did dozens of lectures. Dirk is still a therapist and teaches communication, hypnosis, presentation, debate and telephone techniques. His lessons in lie detection and the many comical interludes during his courses have long since become legendary.

For 13 months, this centipede worked for a PR bureau. Since Badass PR had already shown an interest in entertainment, convincing him to join the team wasn't all that difficult. Soon, Dirk became the face and driving force of Badass PR.

As a journalist, critic and pr consultant, Dirk is our main multitasker (he himself claims his brain is half feminine). He is intimately familiar with the world of restaurants, lifestyle, games, music(al instruments), films festivals, concerts, travel, fashion (designers), graphics and more.

    Badass PR

    (Public Relations and Communications industry)

    May 2007 — Present (2 years 10 months)

    Badass PR is a young but insanely experienced team of PR consultants and graphic developers who know how to do PR: ballsy and with 'PR' written in capital letters... even though we have to use small letters in Dutch!

    According to the Badass PR guys and gals (even our thirty- and forty-somethings don't think of themselves as 'men and women'), PR consultants have two essential skills.

    Actually, the first skill is important in any occupation. It is a skill that can make sure one can excell in just about any function. A skill one must have if (s)he is to distinguish himself or herself: being creative. Imagination, the ability to think out of the box, finding new and fresh angles, presenting a broad range of opportunities and different ways to handle things... It is what separates a supremely skilled individual from his or her rivals.

    Badass PR was founded because many other PR bureaus try to bind their consultants to their own internal petty rules, an ancient and outdated corporate culture and the smallish world views of their board, manager or director. This way of thinking curbs inventivity, sacrifices the individual and limits all potential.

    Yes, we too understand that rules are necessary, that deadlines need to be met and that discipline is important, but we listen to our clients first, without trying to push our own corporate culture down their throats. What do YOU want? Where do YOU want to go? Questions pertaining to vision. Naturally, we advise and love to debate the possibilities, but first and foremost we want to get to know your world views, as understanding a customer is the building blocks for everything.

    And so we get to the second skill: social aptitude. PR doesn't only consist of sitting in the office and sending e-mails to all known journalists working for large media. PR is also getting to KNOW people, making contacts, meeting others in real life, attending and organising events... PR is communicating!
