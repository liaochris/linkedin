April DuBois’s Summary

April DuBois is Opportunity International's Vice President for International Business Development. In this role, she is responsible for building strategic relationships and identifying new funding sources from corporations, foundations, government agencies, and financial institutions. She has more than twelve years of experience in international business and economic development. Prior to Opportunity International, April served as a strategic advisor to the leadership of Fortune-level companies including Lockheed Martin, HSBC, SRA, EDS, Northrop Grumman, IBM, and Sprint-Nextel. Her career included extensive trade and foreign direct investment experience under the leadership of former Pennsylvania Governor & U.S. Secretary of Homeland Security, Tom Ridge. She has authored and co-authored several books relating to international trade and development and is co-founder and board member of ITS Incorporated, a woman-owned, small business that assists global companies in securing contracts with the U.S. government.

Her development and humanitarian interests first began in the early 1990s when she began teaching English to refugees and immigrant families for Catholic Charities. This experience, coupled with her work as a youth mentor and career counselor, led DuBois to begin focusing on the needs of impoverished women, orphans, and children—both in her local community and around the world. For the past several years, she has served on the advisory board of Orfanato Ninos de Cristo, an orphanage in La Romana, Dominican Republic that provides a permanent home, education and future to more than 145 battered and abandoned children.
