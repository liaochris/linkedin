Mark Basnight’s Summary

Mark is the Founder of Social Media 4 Responders Conferences and President of the The Crisis Communications Network (TCCN). TCCN was created with the idea of establishing an organic network of experienced and talented communicators from Law Enforcement, the Fire Service, Public Utilities, Public Health, Disaster and Crisis Aid, and Emergency Management professions to offer exceptional training experiences. The ‘Social Media 4 Responders’ training conference was designed with the responder in mind.

The concept is to provide high-level hands-on training for the use of social media tools and practices responders can use today! ‘The Crisis Communication Network’ was founded with a commitment “To EQUIP and EMPOWER today’s PIO’s for tomorrow’s crisis.”

Mark is one of two Public Information Officers for the Charlotte Fire Department. In his current capacity as Public Information Officer, Basnight serves as chief editor for the department’s magazine publication, host of CFD Talk Radio, Web content manager, and is responsible for the research, development, and application of social media applications.

Basnight has created a strong social media presence for his organization using a host of web 2.0 tools including Twitter, Yahoo Groups (After-Action Reports), Google Groups (CharlotteFD_ALERT), BlogSpot (CFD News & Information), FriendFeed, LiveJournal, MyBlogLog, Blog Talk Radio, Delicious, and Facebook for communicating critical messaging and public information that encourages people to stay safe and stay alive.

He is a member of the National Information Officer’s Association, the National Association of Government Communicators, the International Association of Business Communicators, and a social media evangelist for public safety agencies. Mark has been a presenter in national forums and continues to be requested to share his experience on the subject matter of social media and its use in crisis communications and public affairs.

Mark Basnight’s Specialties:

Receiving, processing, and disseminating appropriate, accurate, relevant, and timely information to our customers/ audience. Advocate for the use of social media by public safety agencies, public health agencies, and disaster or crisis response agencies.
