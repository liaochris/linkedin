Casey McQuillan’s Summary

Greetings! I have recently graduated from the University of California, Davis with a Bachelor's Degree in Political Science. During my undergraduate education began my study of politics and human behavior in societies. Additionally, I developed a keen interest in the academic debate community. All four of the years I spent getting my degree were also spent on the UC Davis Speech and Debate team. Here I honed several skills in public speaking and also began developing an interest in policy analysis, as this was the subject of the most competitive debates at the national tournaments. My academic career then became about combining the information and knowledge gathered through my undergraduate education with the speaking and analytical skills honed through my debate experience. In essence, academic debate became much like a second major.

Additionally, during the spring quarter of 2007, I took a short internship in Washington, DC with The Council for Court Excellence. This told me a few interesting things: firstly, it confirmed that I enjoyed working in an environment based around quantitative research and policy analysis and secondly, it told me that I was very interested in returning to the District after graduating. If my education and debate experience shaped my interest in the Public Policy field, my experience in the District gave me the best environment to pursue this passion.

Ultimately, I hope to begin a public policy career here in the District, and after gaining some work experience in the field I would like to pursue a Master's of Public Policy at one of the great universities in the area.

  • Debate Team Student Coordinator/Coach

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    September 2006 — June 2008 (1 year 10 months)

    I served as the Student Coordinator for the UC Davis Speech and Debate Team, a student-run National Parliamentary Debate Association (NPDA) team, for two academic debate seasons. This job has three basic objectives:

    2) To oversee the team's travel to and behavior at tournaments.
    3) To coach and mentor the team's novice and intermediate level debaters.

I handled the team's scheduling and travel to tournaments, making arrangements for hotels, cars, and the occassional flights for farther tournaments. I was given the opportunity to manage a team budget and was able to arrange yearly fundraising activities to augment the budget for the upcoming national tournament.

    During my internship at The Council for Court Excellence, I was given a number of projects to work on.   • Instruction Intern

    (Education Management industry)

    November 2005 — June 2006 (8 months)

    My objective at Ralph Waldo Emerson Junior High School was to give a group of interested 7th and 9th grade students a springboard into academic debate principles.
