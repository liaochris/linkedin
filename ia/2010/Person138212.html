Matt Timberlake’s Summary

The growth of our society depends on reliable infrastructure systems. This is achieved through the continual inspection, maintenance, and upgrade of the buried utility structures that provide our communities with water, gas, sewage, communications, and storm water removal. The Ted Berry Company offers internal Robotic CCTV pipeline inspection equipment for pipes ranging in size from 2'-120"+. This information is used to help utility owners make long term planning and capitol improvements.

The Ted Berry Company has helped bring state of the art technologies to the areas of New England once unserved with these services.

We have brought Pipebursting from a technology used extensively in Europe to a reliable and cost effective alternative construction method for utility owners throughout New England. Our experiance runs deep in both Static Pipebursting and Pneumatic Pipebursting for both gravity and pressure pipe applications. Our team has successfully installed hundreds of thousands of feet ranging from 2"-24" in size.

As we continue to find ways to promote these "new" technologies we are aware that there is much resistance to alternative or "new" construction techniques. Extensive efforts are made to educate utility owners, engineers and consultants, and of course the general public. Educational efforts will need to be a continued effort by all members of the trenchless industry going forward.

As the "green" wave continues to be at the forefront of public debate these alternative construction techniques and social values may soon be a consideration when funding public projects. If a traditional excavation project produces
(x)lbs. of carbon emissions and an alternative construction method can do the same work with .5 x (x)lbs. then there may be public interest in requiring new methods. New carbon calculators are frequently being used to compare methods and techniques.
