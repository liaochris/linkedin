Cynthia Maxwell’s Summary

My research is at the intersection of visual and aural exploration for sound synthesis. It contributes new techniques for designing musical objects using geometric models as prototypes. The goal of my research is to develop new ways of generating sounds using physics-based techniques. This research opens up exciting new frontiers in computer music, instrument design and can extend musical performance environments.

I have developed a system that allows composers to interact with 3D models of arbitrary objects to generate sound in real time. The system also allows for geometric modifications, creating changes in the timbre of an object as it changes shape; a truly novel experience.

As computing power increases, complex physical systems will be within the reach of interactive simulations. As this happens, I believe that there will be growing cross-pollination between disciplines such as mathematics, physics, computer graphics and computer music. I am excited to be adding to this emerging interdisciplinary era of computing.
