Tony Russomanno’s Summary

Specialist in environmental, science and technology broacast journalism.

40-year background in radio, television, print and documentary film.

An innovator of new technologies. Named the "godfather" of the KPIX Web site by the station's first Executive Producer for New Media for being the first in the world to integrate television pictures and news content on the Web.

First live underwater broadcast by a reporter in SCUBA gear. The only reporter who gave television viewers around the world any news from Santa Cruz on the first day of the Loma Prieta earthquake. First reporter to land on the islands of Prince William Sound in Alaska following the Exxon Valdez oil spill, and the first eyewitness description of the spill's effects. World's only radio reporter to cover the Peoples Temple massacre in Jonestown, Guyana.

Credits on four documentaries-- "Direct Action," "Hackers," the Peabody and Academy Award-winning "The Times of Harvey Milk" and the upcoming film about Harlan Ellison, "Dreams With Sharp Teeth."

Co-owned and operated a radio news network in Santa Barbara, reported and anchored radio newscasts at NBC News in New York, produced some of the first public access cable TV programs in Manhattan, worked in the shadow of radio greats Bob and Ray and Jean Shepherd as a reporter and film critic at WOR-FM, stringer and freelance newspaper and magazine writer, helped build and later general manager of a community-owned radio station. First Class FCC radiotelephone license. Licensed Amateur Radio operator.

Went online in 1977 with the Plato Users Group (PUG) at Control Data and served as assistant sysop on Compuserve's Journalism Forum (JFORUM) for 16 years.

Resident of Craftsman Farms before it became a National Historical Landmark. It was originally the home of legendary designer and social architect Gustav Stickley, whose philosophy of simple living gained favor in an earlier time of intense technological innovation.

Tony Russomanno’s Specialties:

Fast comprehension. Able to filter and process large amounts of information, then synthesize and accurately report under tight deadline pressure. Will not compromise on ethics. Values principles of humanity and respect for all people.
