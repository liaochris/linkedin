Ryan Parker’s Summary

What is a brand?

A brand is far more than a logo. It’s more than a color scheme or a clever slogan or a great website to showcase your products and services.

Unfortunately, a brand is not something that can be manufactured internally, no matter how creative the minds behind it.

Rather, a brand is a feeling. It is an emotion defined by the reaction your clients and customers have when they experience each part of your company. Of course, this includes obvious things such as your logo, your website and your products. However, this extends to your staff, the way each person interacts with your clients and the manner in which your company is represented on all levels and in each relationship.

Thankfully, we do have some measure of control over such things. We can have great products, we can build engaging online experiences and we can cultivate a strong, knowledgeable workforce to reflect well upon the company and its mission.

The challenge (apart from having great products and hiring great people) is to ensure a clear, unified and consistent message is presented to the world.

After all, messages of great impact encourage us to embrace something unexpected.
