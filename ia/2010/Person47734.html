Daniel Feldman’s Summary

I work with foreign companies, including Asian ones, who do business in China. I work to find practical solutions to problems. I manage cross-cultural teams and projects, bridging culture gaps, and liaising to maximize return on investment. I have transferred, built or developed multi-million dollar electronics production lines, and have provided business development and marketing services to foreign companies in China. I work in factories, primarily in the consumer electronics and industrial automation fields. Working in plants requires comfort with the Chinese language and an understanding of the history and culture of China.

In my former incarnation I was an embedded software systems engineer in the U.S. for about 10 years. I worked in telecommunications in the areas of wireless, VoIP, and digital signal processing, and in flight training/flight simulation in the avionics/navigation area.

Interests: My primary interest is in trying to understand the world in which I find myself. Currently this happens to be China. But since nothing exists unto itself, and all entities are interconnected, I am also interested in how the world influences China and vice versa. I am a student of Chinese culture, primarily martial arts, traditional medicine, painting and philosophy. I am also an enthusiast of literature, and modern art and photography.

Spoken languages: English (mother tongue), Mandarin Chinese (conversational, advanced intermediate), German (conversational), French (intermediate)
