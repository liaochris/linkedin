Theresa de Vera’s Summary

In 1997, I began my own, non-profit organization called, “Faithfilled Decisions.” I minister to families with who have children or loved ones on life support. I have received e-mails from Australia, Colorado, Hawaii, and work with Traumatic Brain Injury Organizations. I believe that I was brought back for a reason, a reason I may never know or understand, but until then- I hope to ensure that no other family has to go through what mine did, and people find comfort in knowing that they are not alone and find hope in my miraculous recovery.
On September 20, 2005- I was appointed by Mayor Antonio Villaraigosa to serve as a Commissioner on the Commission on Disability. It is humbling being the ‘voice of the voiceless’; when disabled constituents feel that all rights and privileges have been stripped of them. I have given numerous talks to organizations, colleges, and churches, about the role of faith in my life and about sticking to your morals and beliefs, and not your party platform.
On February 07, 2007, I was appointed by the mayor to the MTA-Access Board of Directors. This gave me the opportunity to represent the disabled riders who use public or Para transit transportation.
I believe that I was given a second lease on life in order to be a patient and disabled rights advocate. To be recognized for something that you do everyday is humbling. Just knowing that you have helped someone or made an impact on ones life is a reward in itself.
