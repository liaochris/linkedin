Stephanie Nivinskus’s Summary

HISTORY:
Prior to opening Impressions Marketing, I earned a bachelor’s degree in journalism. I then climbed my way up the corporate marketing ladder working at The San Diego Reader, The San Diego Union-Tribune, and Cox Interactive Media. In 2000, I left my corporate management position to start a marketing studio that serviced the needs of non-profit organizations. In 2003 I was asked to consult for The Body Shop at Home, a division of international bath and body retail chain, The Body Shop. For more than 5 years I developed and led a sales and marketing team of more than 500 independent consultants. In just two short years I advanced to a Director position and was amongst the top 1% of performers in the company. In 2008, my story “Confessions of a Former MLM Snob” was published in Chicken Soup For The Network Marketers Soul. Shortly after The Body Shop was acquired by L’Oreal, I introduced Impressions Marketing to small business owners worldwide.

WHAT I DO NOW:
I am the principal at Impressions Marketing, a full-service, boutique marketing studio.Our creative & strategic team awakens brands. Leveraging the power of digital and traditional media, we develop and execute business-building solutions for small business owners with moderate budgets and big dreams. The Impressions Marketing team services the marketing needs of nearly every industry. Our clients include retail store owners, health and wellness professionals, accountants, network marketing leaders, recording artists, attorneys, non-profit organization directors and just about everyone else you can imagine.

HOW WE'RE DIFFERENT:
Our clients tell us we’re different because of the unprecedented amount of creativity, personal attention, and enthusiasm that we pour into every project. Learn more about how we can help you at http://www.ImpressionsOnCall.com
