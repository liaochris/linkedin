  • Executive Director

    (Public Company; Construction industry)

    November 2009 — Present (10 months)

    Our Company Mission includes the statement “Support Our Communities.” This commitment has grown over the years and we now provide a fixed percentage of our annual profit to charitable endeavors. Since 2006, we have committed almost $2.3 million to charity.

    Our charitable work is employee-directed. Our employees select our charitable focus and all employees are welcome to join their local charitable giving committee where giving decisions are made.

    We fulfill our charitable commitment in various ways:

    * We provide in-kind construction services to non-profit organizations
    * We make cash donations to non-profit organizations and to our own corporate foundation—the Deacon Charitable Foundation
    * We encourage employee participation in charitable giving activities and community service projects sponsored by our company
    * We have an Employee Matching Gift Program

    Our Charitable Mission Statement guides our giving:

    Our charitable mission is to improve the quality of life in the communities where we live and work by supporting organizations that address vital community needs and issues in the areas of human services, animal welfare and the environment.

    For more information about the types of organizations we support, please visit the Deacon Charitable Foundation website, www.deaconcharitablefoundation.org.

  • Enthusiastic Member, Leadership Beaverton Alum

    Beaverton Chamber of Commerce

    (Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    2002 — Present (8 years )

    There are more than 900 homeless students attending school in the Beaverton School Distict. These students may be living in cars, coach surfing from fone friend's home to another, be separated from parents and siblings and living with relatives or neighbors. These circumstances make it very hard to do homework, concentrate on learning and to feel good about oneself.

    Our class project created an emergency fund to help the two staff people assigned to help these children and teens have as normal a school experience as possible. The funds allow them to provide essentials like socks and underwear, school supplies, and in some cases food.

    Our Hike 'n' Bike team raised more than $4,100, plus all of the $10 gift cards we won as prizes. As long as the economy is what it is, the need continues to grow. Please let me know if you would like to help. It will make a difference.
