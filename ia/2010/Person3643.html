Manny Nunez’s Summary

November 2010
For the last 5 years we have seen many changes n the way aeromedical services are provided.

Mainly, lack of qualified medical personnel on board and in many cases, inappropriate medical configurations simply place our industry in check. What we have discovered is that many Latin American countries that have medical aviation services, have little or no control or when regulations exist, they do not comply.

The growth in Mexico, for example, of medical aviation services has puilled towards rotor wing services. For that reason, wehave begun a certification program for Helicopter medicla aviation with several modules that begin with knowing what a helicopter is; folloows a progam of certifications such as ACLS, BLS, Prehospital Trauma Life support and others, on modular sections to make economically accessible and graduated to the needs of the operators.

In the Aeromedical spectrum, we are promoting the utilization of high technology aeromedical services for neonates with ECMO and other modalities approved fo in-flight use.
