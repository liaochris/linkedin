Dan Plankenhorn’s Summary

Broad business and technical background that cuts across industries, disciplines, business and product models. This diversity together with a strategic perspective enables me to envision the nexus of emerging technologies with evolving market opportunities.

I am a hands-on designer and design practice leader, experienced in responding to specific business challenges. Skilled in arriving at unique proprietary solutions, often the result of making new meaningful connections between seemingly disparate realms (i.e., bridging across various fields of technology to novel application areas). Technology brokering is a crucial part of what makes me an effective innovator.

Throughout my career I have been continuously engaged in some aspect of research, development and commercialization of new products/process. I have both an academic and professional background in the New Product Development (NPD) methodology and practice.

I am passionate about learning and using new knowledge to innovate. I stay current with advancements in multiple scientific and technological realms. These “solutions looking for problems” have the potential to be leveraged outside of their respective fields to enable the creation of novel proprietary product solutions that satisfy client/customer needs in new valuable ways.

Dan Plankenhorn’s Experience

  • Principal

    Venture Design Technology Consulting

    (Design industry)

    2004 — Present (6 years )

    VDT Consulting is a sole proprietorship created to manage a business enterprise principally involving consulting in applied research; technology search/assessment/acquisition, and all aspects of new product development for breakthrough innovations.

    As Founder and Principal, I am responsible for all activities needed to successfully run a new product development consultancy. Key activities include: marketing of NPD services; account management; proposal development; project planning and scoping; marketing research; technology assessment; new concept ideation; product design, development, engineering, and prototyping; project documenting and presenting results. Clients have included: Alcoa, Reckitt Benckiser, EPRI, Algae Systems, SC Johnson, Kohler Co, Design Integrity, and Thorr Action Sports.

  • Consulting Designer and Key Account Liaison

    Design Integrity, Inc.

    (Privately Held; Mechanical or Industrial Engineering industry)

    2004 — 2008 (4 years )

    DI is a leading new product development consultancy servicing a broad range of manufacturing companies through the application of industrial design, mechanical engineering, electromechanical systems integration, mechatronics, design for manufacture and assembly, and sourcing.

    As Consulting Designer and Key Account Liaison, I am responsible for securing new and maintaining on-going business relationships with client companies who are seeking to arrive at high-value, proprietary products with breakthrough potential. Providing guidance, especially at the front-end of the innovation process.

  • Technology Platform Leader, RD&E

    SC Johnson

    (Privately Held; Consumer Goods industry)

    1999 — 2004 (5 years )

    SCJ is a $7 billion global manufacturer and marketer of household products.

    As Technology Platform Leader, I was responsible for the identification, evaluation, demonstration, acquisition, and integration of novel enabling technologies as the underlying basis of future products. Key activities include: R&D project management; technology brokering; design, engineering, and development of proof-of-principle prototypes; and the introduction of innovative new product models (e.g., unique delivery systems for energy driven processes). Initiated and championed collaborative ventures both internal and external to the corporation. Responsible for establishing a joint venture with a leading US appliance manufacturer that ultimately led to the successful commercialization of a unique new product based on the “durable + consumable” product model.

  • R&D Project Manager

    Electric Power Research Institute (EPRI)

    (Non-Profit; Utilities industry)

    1991 — 1999 (8 years )

    EPRI is a not-for-profit R&D consortium developing electricity-based technologies for electric power industry.

    As Project Manager, I was responsible for all aspects of initiating and managing applied research projects supporting the needs of the electric utility members and their industrial/commercial customers. Primary role was to act as a liaison in the building of collaborative relationships between representatives of the utilities, their customers, and an elite cadre of scientists, technologists, consultants and entrepreneurs. Initiated and built consensus around new research projects; organized forums, technical conferences, and symposia. Managed formal technology transfer activities. Developed in-depth understanding and capabilities associated with contract law (mostly pertaining to crafting technical statements of work and specifying intellectual property aspects of agreements).

  • Senior Design Engineer, Laser Applications

    Westinghouse Electric Corporation

    (Public Company; Oil & Energy industry)

    1981 — 1991 (10 years )

    WEC is (was) a manufacturer of machinery and equipment for U.S. Department of Defense.

    As Senior Design Engineer, I was responsible for providing technical leadership during design, development and launch of an advanced laser-based manufacturing facility. Activities included: machine tool design; robotic, CNC and PLC programming; sensing, instrumentation and data logging; laser process development and qualification (including establishing Mil Specs); metallurgy; FEA and thermodynamic modeling; optics design and micromachining of optical surfaces; equipment design, development and commercialization; patenting and licensing.

  • Applications Engineer, Materials Division

    Illinois Institute of Technology, Research Institute (IITRI)

    (Non-Profit; 501-1000 employees; Research industry)

    1979 — 1981 (2 years )

    IITRI is a non-profit contract research organization providing technology development services.

    As Applications Engineer, I was responsible for developing new laser material processing applications for client companies. Pioneered the development of novel laser-assisted machining processes. Performed machinability testing on titanium and nickel-based superalloys for aerospace applications. Designed experiments. Performed rigorous scientific studies, formally published and presented findings.
