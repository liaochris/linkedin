Rick Abbott’s Summary

• I have worked for 18 years as a structural engineer for consulting engineering companies, A&E firms, and a design build company. I understand the benefits, and limitations of each.
• I have been involved with the construction of many various types of structures including homes, restaurants, retail stores, office building, dorms, apartments, schools, hospitals, theaters, warehouses, museums, churches, and hotels.
• I recognize the intrinsic value of all people and enjoy working with others. I have had very successful relationships with everyone in the building process.
• I understand the importance of budgets, schedules, function, and safety.
• I understand problem solving, and believe if we think, pray, and work diligently with respect and humility, a solution can be found that will satisfy all.
• My most rewarding experiences have come when I have been on teams that have supplied real benefits to those in need.
• I am devoted to my wife and children and understand that family is the prime arena where people are nurtured to love themselves and love others, to gain confidence in who they are and what they stand for, and to give them security to face the world in whatever circumstance.
• I am proud to live in a country where we have freedom to live our lives as people of faith. I have found that actions are the true and only means of conveying one's heart. People are interested what we do. I desire to work with anyone regardless of any religion, race, or social status, as long as they value life and desire to help others. I want to engage more to do the same. It is my desire to unlock the Architects and Engineers of the USA to use their skills as professionals to build a brighter future.

Rick Abbott’s Specialties:

Registration: CA SE, MN, NJ, NCEES, LEED-AP.

As a Christian, I found that virtues such as integrity, humility, and charity have significant and lasting effects.

Rick Abbott’s Experience

  • Founder and President

    Only One Foundation Inc.

    (Non-Profit; Architecture & Planning industry)

    September 2009 — Present (10 months)

    ONLY ONE FOUNDATION Inc. is a 501c(3) non-profit organization. Our mission statement is to show the love of Jesus Christ by providing free Christian professional architectural and/or structural design services to: 1 orphans, 2 widows, 3 single mothers, 4 homeless people, 5 unemployed people, 6 under-employed people, 7 physically poor, 8 spiritually poor people, 9 people or communities in crisis, 10 or other non-profit organizations that have similar Christian mission statements. Only One Foundation brings willing volunteers of Architects, Engineers, and Designers together with non-profit organizatiosn that are helping people in need. Design professionals are allowed to use their skills to serve others.

  • Structural Engineer

    Meyer Borgman Johnson

    (Privately Held; Civil Engineering industry)

    January 2009 — October 2009 (10 months)

    I created an innovative, 3D-structural computer model to design the foundations and the lateral system for a multi-story Hospital. The computer modeling proved to be very efficient in reducing costs in the foundation design and resulted in a significant cost savings to the project.   • Senior Project Engineer, Structural

    Opus Architects & Engineers

    (Privately Held; Architecture & Planning industry)

    September 2005 — January 2009 (3 years 5 months)

    I provided structural engineering services for various projects acting as the Structural Department’s lead Project Engineer. My responsibilities included working on a GSA 9-story mild steel reinforced concrete office building designed for LEED requirements, blast design, and progressive collapse design. I worked on various other projects including office buildings constructed of post tensioned concrete, composite steel with steel braced frames, wood apartment buildings, and large warehouses in California. The most rewarding aspect of this employment was developing good working relationships with people on teams consisting of the owner, the project manager, architects, engineers, and superintends all working for the same goal. While At OPUS I studied on my own initiative to obtain my LEED-AP certification. OPUS experienced a significant slowdown had to lay off the majority of the design staff. While at OPUS I used my vacation time to go to Egypt with EMI to help local ministers design facilities to help the poor.

  • Senior Associate Strucural Engineer

  • Project Engineer

  • Strucural Engineer

    Schultz Design Group

    (Architecture & Planning industry)

    January 1999 — August 1999 (8 months)

    I called on Schultz Design group to grow business for Larson engineering. The past president of Schultz Design was dually registered as an Architect and as an Engineer. After his untimely death, I was asked to take over the structural engineering for the company. I was the only engineer employed by Schultz Design, and I was responsible for the design and development of all structural plans and specifications for a variety of projects. At the time of my employment there were 4 registered architects and 6 drafting personal. Approximately 90% of the work was done for one client, Kroger Stores in the Midwest. I learned AutoCAD and did some of my own drafting. I also completed a 17 million dollar Christian school addition and a 13 million dollar Presbyterian Church. Unfortunately, the owner wanted me to sign drawings unethically. I refused. I soon later left the company based on this ethic problem. We sold our home and moved back to Minnesota to be close to family.

  • Project Engineer

    Larson Engineering

    (Privately Held; Civil Engineering industry)

    January 1995 — December 1998 (4 years )

    At Larson engineering I learned to design every day structures. I designed hotels, restaurants, schools theaters, industrial buildings, churches and various other types of structures. I designed with wood design, bar joists, concrete plank, steel and masonry. Here I learned the lesson of schedules and cost associated with budgets set in contracts. I moved to St. Louis, Missouri to help grow the business and search for new architectural clients. During this time I also provide engineering design for two churches to receive new elevators. The work was done after hours and free of charge.

  • Structural Engineer

    AEC Engineering

    (Privately Held; Oil & Energy industry)

    September 1990 — January 1995 (4 years 5 months)

    The office in Richmond, Virginia provided engineering services for approximately 6 steel plate fabricators. It was here that I learned how to designed tanks and vessels according to API-650, API-620, AWWA, and ASME. We also inspected many existing plate structures so I learned the hazards of existing structures. I become familiar with confined space entry, chemical hazards, heights and harnesses. After transferring to the main office in Minneapolis, I became the main structural engineer to design an industrial laboratory facility for the containment of accidental explosions. We studied the shock waves, the gas pressures, the structural response, and the final design were based on TM-5-1500 “Structures To Resist the Effects of Accidental Explosions”. The analysis was dynamic and very interesting. The final design was efficient and my work and ingenuity resulted in a substantial cost savings to 3M. When my wife became pregnant with our first child, I sought a position that did not require as much travel, nor hazardous work environments. Here I learned the important concept that people, especially family and the husband and wife relationship should come first before business, and that integrity and character in this area bring loyalty and respect.
