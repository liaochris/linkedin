Dan Patrick’s Summary

I started experimenting with web development -- in part -- as far back as 1997 (well before highschool). That was 13 years ago, and back then I did this exclusively for my family using now-dated WYSIWYG tools like Microsoft Frontpage.

After graduating from highschool I became dedicated to boosting the search engine placement of many of these websites. A good website is useless with out eyeballs to soak it in, and this was my first foot in the door at what I would later come to know as "search engine optimization" or SEO for short.

Most of my experience in optimizing for search engine visibility and marketing began with my efforts at promoting my parents' retail website in 2002. This website has continued to receive excellent placement in search engines even many years afterwards because of the time we spent building backlinks towards both the homepage, and subpages relevant to products they were marketing at the time. One of the biggest achievements -- and lessons -- from this website is the sizable newsletter list membership list it has accumulated over the last eight years. Return business is a critical component of my parents' business.

Starting sometime in 2003 I began work with GetSpecific Internet marketing. I developed and refined many skills fundamental to SEO while working with Greg, and still keep in touch with him to this day.

Since then I've come to specialize in using Wordpress as a content management system of choice because of it's ease of use, flexibility, and power. The first step in the creation of any new website, for me, is always the Wordpress install. After the initial install, and after the customization, Wordpress is unique in that it allows for most basic website maintenance to be performed by anyone with the most rudimentary understanding of how to use a word processor.
