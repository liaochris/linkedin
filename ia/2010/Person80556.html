Howard Cleveland’s Summary

Howard never loses sight of the fact that building and sustaining customer relationships are what make companies successful -- same as always.

He helps companies define and translate their business goals through complete brand-driven technology solutions that replicate customer process convictions and natural usage criteria. Howard works closely with his clients to adapt their business objectives to the widening spectrum of new technologies. Using the Internet to enhance and streamline business processes, knowledge management networks and product development are some of the capabilities he offers.

Since starting up his company in his basement with savings and credit cards in 1995, Howard has overseen revenue growth of more than 130 percent per year. His company, DigitalDay, employed more than 130 people and had a client list that included Aetna, Arhaus Furniture, Bell & Howell, BFGoodrich, Ernst & Young, Dreyfus, FedEx, General Electric, Goodyear, HendlerLaw, Lincoln Electric, Medical Mutual of Ohio, National City Corporation, PSC Corporation, Sherwin-Williams, Telxon and the J. M Smucker Company.

Also, Howard founded Cross Town Traffic, a digital marketing firm specializing in full service marketing for dot-com companies and corporate websites, and Hatchbox, Inc., the first ebusiness incubator of its kind in the Midwest.

Howard Cleveland is a former journalist, creative director and marketing executive. While head of the WRC Marketing Group, Mr. Cleveland became immersed in high-tech, computer-related projects for several clients. His faith in technology to enhance business effectiveness and efficiency has been rewarded. In 1998 his 5-D development process won Arthur Andersen’s Best Practices Award and on June 5, 2000, Ernst & Young named Howard Entrepreneur of the Year. He also has been a featured speaker at Accenture’s Presidents’ Council and many other technology events.
