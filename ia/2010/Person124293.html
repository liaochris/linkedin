Leslie M. Levy’s Summary

The last year has really been the launching point for my career as a freelance writer, and I am working at finishing the manuscript of my first book before we leave in June. "Job Hunt Across America: Opportunity Knocked, So We Left" tells the story of how Job Hunt Across America came to be, why I believe it is so needed, and what we hope to accomplish through it.

The current job market is a different animal than perhaps ever encountered in American History. While plenty of blue collar workers have been displaced through factory closings, etc., the white collar world, too, has been rocked like no other time since the tech bubble burst shortly after the turn of the millennium. Displaced workers all over the country are struggling to find adequate work because they are, shockingly, overqualified.

This creates a problem for less skilled workers, too. As they become increasingly desperate for work, overqualified people willing to take a drastic pay cut are more likely to get hired for those positions usually filled by less skilled workers. The service industries continue to grow while the white collar world recovers ever so slowly, and those who made their living in service professions are being pushed out of their slots as well, leaving them with no place to go but the unemployment office--for the long haul, if they're lucky enough to qualify.

My own husband is a white collar worker who is currently unable to find a job in his field, near where we live now, that will pay more than what he receives on unemployment--which is less than half his former salary. The designer and moderator of our Job Hunt Across America website, he and I will both be looking for work opportunities as we report about what Americans REALLY think about our job market and economy.

Our children, too, will have incredible educational opportunities through travel and life experience, not to mention learning about the important role of technology and communication in America today.

Leslie M. Levy’s Specialties:

*Asking relevant questions that reach the heart of an issue, so that problem solvers can adequately prepare effective solutions.

*Getting around common arguments & deflections to refocus discussion on the heart of the problem, uncovering the practical implications of commonly argued positions.

*Listening to average people tell their stories, and translating that into language that captures the hearts of change makers, so that they, in turn, can truly address the average person's needs.

Leslie M. Levy’s Experience

  • Reporter and Columnist

    Ozarks Sentinel Newspaper

    (Privately Held; Newspapers industry)

    May 2009 — Present (1 year 4 months)

    I currently cover human interest stories and local coverage of various non-profits in NW Missouri and SW Arkansas. I also write a weekly column, "Thoughtful Meanderings," which is available for syndication. My personal blog, "Welcome Elephants," complements my column with a page "beyond meanderings," and focuses on discussing topics that tend to be the "elephant in the room" in a productive way, without sensationalizing sensitive issues or being overtly graphic. I also occasionally include some poetry. My current topics tend toward long term unemployment and its consequences .

    Beginning in June 2010, I will be reporting from various locations across the continental U.S. and writing a series of weekly articles entitled, "The View from [location]," which will chronicle my findings on our family's "Job Hunt Across America." The paper is offering a nationally reaching advertising exchange for sponsors providing everything from hotel accommodations to laundry service, and even photocopies for interview questionnaires.

    To see what is happening now in our planning stages or get details about sponsorship, please visit our travel blog, http://jobhuntacrossamerica.com. There, you can post in our forums, apply to become a sponsor, or suggest a business for us to contact. Our family is just one in millions across America who has experienced prolonged joblessness during the Great Recession, and our goal is to facilitate a national discussion of the many issues brought to light for real people in all kinds of communities across the country.

    We will also be actively seeking media outlets along the way, which we will utilize to announce times and locations for interviewing various kinds of people, as well as discussing publicly what we are finding in each community we pass through and as we progress across the nation. You can also visit our travel blog to suggest local radio hosts or TV news people to contact for press releases. Check it out!

  • owner and manager

    Cres-Vu Lodge

    (Hospitality industry)

    March 2003 — Present (7 years 6 months)

    My husband and I currently own a small lake lodge/resort near Table Rock Lake in SW Missouri. Our family has outgrown the residence that came with the lodge and our careers are taking a turn. We are interested in finding someone to either buy or rent the establishment so that when the right career opportunities come our way we can follow our new plans without reckless abandon.

    During the first four years we lived in the Ozarks, we ran a typical tourist-type seasonal pattern, catering to families in the summer time and to weekend fishermen in the spring and fall. Over the last two and a half years or so, as our family grew and my husband continued to work full time in Springfield (an hour away), the summer schedule became too grueling and we ceased most advertising efforts and began accepting short term tenants.

    This property would be ideal for a couple or partnership that has other seasonal income (such as tax preparation or teaching) or a flexible, work-from-home kind of business that provides supplemental income. With just ten rooms, most cleaning can be handled by one person even in the busiest season. Our challenge was in one person handling four children (including a preschooler and infant) at the same time, and the time constraints that come with commuting. The summer schedule was great fun before our family got so big! Your guests truly do feel like friends by the end of the week after spending time lounging and even grilling together in the long evenings.

    This summer season is ripe with possibility for re-launching a summer business in our area. Just two miles down the road from us, an investor has poured $5.6 million into the outdoor amphitheater (formerly known as "Swiss Villa") in Lampe, MO, and there are over a dozen shows already on the schedule. The amphitheater holds up to ten thousand people, and our resort is on the same road "in," but not "out," meaning after-concert traffic will be nil and your comfy A/C kitchenette just minutes away.

  • Home Educator and Lay Leader

    Our Family, both Children and Church

    (Individual & Family Services industry)

    January 1998 — 2009 (11 years )

    As a family, our priority has always been raising our children well. For us that meant living on a single income and being in charge of educational choices. We have both utilized public school and homeschooled our children based on their various needs and the resources available as time progressed. My teaching style is very eclectic, partly because of my personality, but mostly because I have children who learn in drastically different ways. I often incorporate hands on activities and field trips, as well as using various computer and video resources.

    I tend to use delight driven learning methods with one child in particular, while I encourage our oldest daughter to expand her range of talents through more traditional instruction methods, especially in the fine arts. I believe that some talents must be discovered in order to form a platform to build skills on, and that foundation is the key to long-term success and fulfillment, regardless of your chosen interest.

    I have also been extensively involved in volunteer teaching both at church and in homeschool co-ops over the years. I have taught all ages of children and women, including teaching introductory speech and debate to teens, as well as music appreciation and beginning music theory to elementary aged children.

    I am particularly adept at "jumping in the mix" and excel at substitute teaching in children's classes and facilitating meaningful discussion among adults. Pulling relevant questions from learning material and forming easy-to-access allegories is one of my specialties. My illustrations tend to make people laugh, breaking group tension, or to get them engaged in vital conversation, which then leads to practical application of principles that once felt dry or rote.

    It is this skill, perhaps more than any, that permeates my best writing and is rapidly becoming my personal calling card.
