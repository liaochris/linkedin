Jess Wisloski’s Summary

After moving to New York as a student, my experience for many years was firmly in print journalism, aside from that pesky little Film/TV degree.

Following my graduation, I worked full-time as a contracted freelancer and staff reporter at some of the city's leading fast-turnaround publications including the New York Times, the Brooklyn Papers, and the New York Daily News.

In the fall of 2008, I left my staff writer job at the NY Daily News to expand my skills in online journalism and multimedia storytelling. I took a part-time position as a writer and webmaster for a nonprofit housing organization, UHAB, and helped oversee the entire launch of a new website for them, writing all the copy for it. Meanwhile, I continued to work in journalism in financial news as a copy editor – which included layout of stories, trafficking copy, fact-checking, and producing slideshows – at CNNMoney.com.

In late 2009, I was hired by Patch.com to establish a local online news presence in the Westchester village of Scarsdale, N.Y.

As a professional goal, I would like to work primarily in online news production as an editor but also to be involved in design qualities, overall content strategy and enterprise planning.

Jess Wisloski’s Specialties:

Knowledge of various CMSs, Flash, social media work and multimedia story production including video, photo slideshows, and audio production. Fundamental knowledge of HTML language, and keen judgment in fast-paced news environment.
