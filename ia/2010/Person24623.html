Bentley Laaksonen’s Summary

Educated in physics and computer science followed by 13 years of experience as a scientific programmer in the DoD and biomedical industries. Developed physics-based simulations for the multi-billion dollar Airborne Laser program and several other directed energy programs. In the biomedical arena, worked for InLight Solutions to develop scientific software for their non-invasive glucose monitor and later with spinout TruTouch Technologies, commercializing a non-invasive alcohol monitor, which received TIME magazine's best inventions of 2006 award. Expertise in embedded systems design and implementation, large scale simulation design and implementation, algorithm design and implementation, and IT network, intranet, and services design and implantation. Experience with manufacturing, clinical studies, data analysis, systems analysis, application development and user interface design, human factors, and other aspects of product design.

Bentley Laaksonen’s Experience

  • Director of Software Development

    TruTouch Technologies, Inc.

    (Privately Held; Medical Devices industry)

    March 2005 — Present (5 years 1 month)

    Served as the director of software development and senior IT administrator, bringing the first non-invasive alcohol monitor to market in 2007. The device uses near infrared spectroscopy to interrogate tissue and determine alcohol concentration.

    As the director of software development, developed the infrastructure and processes which governed all software development and releases. Responsible for implementing all mathematical algorithms, state logic and data flow, linux hardware drivers, high-level logic & QC, and autonomous health self-assessment. Core improvements to the mathematical algorithms and flow resulted in orders of magnitude efficiency improvements over previous generations, greatly simplifying the hardware and electronics design. Developed in-house tools that resided either on-target or on network servers, automating manufacturing processes, instrument QC, data management and archival, and system maintenance. Developed network applications that communicated with instruments, individually, or in bulk.

    As the senior IT administrator, designed an efficient hybrid Linux / Windows Domain environment and intranet. The intranet integrated a set of sophisticated tools, supporting engineers and managers alike, including software development tracking, bug reporting and tracking, configuration control integration, blogging, manufacturing tools, autonomously updated release notes, VPN portal, search facilities, project tracking, and domain tools.

  • Software Engineer

    VeraLight, Inc.

    (Privately Held; Medical Devices industry)

    October 2008 — February 2009 (5 months)

    Contracted to VeraLight by TruTouch to assist with setting up and configuring an 80 node computing cluster used for calibration model investigation. Developed a web-based front-end using AJAX to manage and monitor the computing cluster.

  • Research Scientist

    InLight Solutions

    (Privately Held; Biotechnology industry)

    November 2000 — March 2005 (4 years 5 months)

    Designed and built a software infrastructure to rapidly transition real-time scientific research into immediate applications on, or interfaced with, prototype hardware. This allowed InLight Solutions to respond to real or perceived market changes, customer and investor needs, technology demonstrations, and internal demands with grace. As with the simulations built for Boeing, the software infrastructure was designed, fundamentally, for reuse, rapid verification, and maximum synergy with all of InLight's programmatic endeavors. This enabled us to deliver ever-changing manifestations of our core technology to investors and industry leaders to build their confidence in our ability to solve technological challenges and to build fully functional instrumentation.

  • Systems Engineer

    Boeing

    (Public Company; BA; Aviation & Aerospace industry)

    May 1996 — November 2000 (4 years 7 months)

    Developed end-to-end physics-based simulations for the world's largest and most complex laser missile defense systems including the Airborne Laser (ABL), Space Based Laser (SBL), and Tactical High Energy Laser (THEL). Assumed the lead role for developing the methods and design principles necessary to build simulations of the complexity mandated by the technologies involved. The success of these simulations led to numerous new contracts and an expanded group, and ultimately, a suite of simulation technologies, rigorously tested and validated over a half decade, and used in all of Boeing's directed energy programs.
