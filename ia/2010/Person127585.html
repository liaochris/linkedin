Loribeth Dalton’s Summary

I am an open networker! loribethdalton@gmail.com

After spending 16 years as a "Head Hunter" and seeing hundreds of smart capable people fail because it wasn't the right fit, including shockingly MYSELF, I have committed the rest of my career to helping individuals find their PASSION, their PURPOSE and the PLACE they can PROSPER! I am THE HEART HUNTER!

I have coached over 450 executives since 2005 in Las Vegas. I work with individuals who are seeking a new position, seeking a whole new career, or perhaps even considering entrepreneurship. I also help individuals gain more visibility in their current company to be targeted for a promotion and prevent being laid off.

I believe that within each of us is the power and ability to be a top performer. The secret is finding your "Success Zone" I use a variety of tools and strategies to help the client create a clear vision of where they fit and a strategy to uncover if the position or corporate culture will make their soul sing or be toxic for them. I am currently writing a book based on my own experiences of being in "Perfect Dream Job/Wrong Culture" and the research I have been conducting over the past 4 years with my 450+ executives who were the victims of layoffs, downsizing and firing.



I have worked in all areas of Talent Acquisition including third party "head hunting" in Accounting and IT, corporate Human Resources, Applicant Tracking System design and training, Retention and Employee Engagement consulting and Executive Coaching.

  • Executive Coach

    Heart Hunter LLC

    (Professional Training & Coaching industry)

    2007 — Present (3 years )

    I have coached over 450 executives in career management and job transition since 2005 in Las Vegas.

    My process is to change my clients paradigm from being a job seeker to being a solutions provider. We walk through a 4 step product marketing campaign.

    Step One: Product Analysis

    WHO are you? WHAT makes you tick and what ticks you off? WHERE will you thrive? WHY should a company hire you? HOW do you bring value to an organization?

    Step Two: Product Packaging

    Your resume is your career obituary. It focuses on what you did for someone else. You must communicate to the market place not as someone looking for a job, but rather someone who solves business problems.What you CAN do...not what you used to do. Remember a resume is only helpful if you are applying for the same job in the same industry.

    Step Three: Product Promotion

    If you are politely sending resumes and cover letters and filling out online applications you are going to stay unemployed. You need to engage in a proactive campaign!

    Step Four: Product Pricing and Positioning

    Do you know what you are worth? Can you quantify the impact you will make on your future employer so that see your value? Are you confident in your ability to negotiate for top dollar in a soft economy?

    There is no more job security. Your security comes from creating a brand and promoting it. Keep your network fresh and always be seen as a solution provider!
