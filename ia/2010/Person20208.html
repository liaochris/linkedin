Barry Eagar’s Summary

I'm an Australian and New Zealand patent and trade mark attorney. I co-founded Eagar & Buck in 2001. In August 2008, we merged with Cullen & Co. In July 2009, I set up my own practice.

I have been practising since 1990, when I started at Adams & Adams, finally becoming a partner in 1997. I emigrated in 1999.

My passion is writing and talking about IP, in particular on procurement and enforcement of registered IP. That has led me to build my blog, Bazpat, which started off as the first Australian patent blog. It now covers trademarks and other aspect of IP. I also speak regularly at the American Bar Association's IP section conference. This experience has led me into an exciting role as a consultant in a number of litigious matters.

I am also a consultant for CCH Australia. CCH is one of Australia's leading business publishers and is a division of Wolters Kluwer. My role currently involves editing and updating their online patent material and commenting on both patent and trade mark cases in Australia.

Personalised service is something that I strive for in my practice. Together with that, goes a commitment to quality. Small is definitely the new big and I'm proud to be part of the growing group of sole practitioners and small firms who have realised that dealing directly with their clients can be very rewarding.

Flowing from that commitment, it is critical for my clients to register IP in the context of their business. An IP portfolio must always be relevant and designed to provide its owner with a competitive edge. I have developed an approach to client counselling that is designed specifically for that purpose.

Barry Eagar’s Specialties:

IP consulting; Litigation support; Patent, trade mark and design validity and infringement opinions; Preparation and prosecution of patents and trade marks all over the world. Building and managing customized international patent and trade mark portfolios.

Strategic advice in connection with obtaining IP protection all over the world, particularly from a commercial perspective.

Patent and Trade Mark validity and infringement advice.
