Lucia Muntean’s Summary

As a scientific analyst for Biogen Idec, I have collaborated with individuals and groups from the research, clinical and business units and have gained a greater appreciation for the drug development process. I have expanded my scientific knowledge base to include the therapeutic areas of rheumatology and neurology and have continued to build upon my current knowledge of oncology, immunology, and virology. I have developed and implemented workflows and produced high quality information regarding competitive intelligence, clinical trials, research techniques, and manufacturing processes. Biogen Idec has provided me with an amazing opportunity to venture beyond bench science and to be involved in the analysis of scientific discoveries, the identification of emerging markets, and the discovery of promising therapeutics.

Prior to joining the Biogen Idec team, I was a graduate student in the laboratory of Dr. Stephen Goff at Columbia University. As a graduate student in Dr. Goff’s laboratory, I conducted research on oncogenic retroviruses and developed strong genetic and molecular biology techniques. My thesis project provided me with extensive knowledge in oncology, immunology, and retrovirology.

Prior to my graduate studies, I worked as a research scientist at Bristol Myers Squibb. At BMS, I was able to gain valuable research experience involving the development and discovery of novel cancer therapeutics.

I graduated magna cum laude from Shippensburg University with dual B.S. in Chemistry and Biology.
