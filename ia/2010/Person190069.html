Simon Rust Lamb, Esq.’s Summary

Simon Rust Lamb is an entertainment industry veteran with almost fifteen years of experience discovering, developing and representing independent artists and start-up companies. Simon's practice focuses on protecting and monetizing intellectual property assets, as well as providing creative, customized strategic business and legal advice to clients across the media spectrum.

Simon has a long-standing love for supporting independent artists and start up companies. He launched his first entertainment business, the limited edition music magazine NOMIS, while still in college. Next, Simon co-founded the annual Dune music festival, which attracted over 6,000 revelers at its peak. Simultaneously, he founded tastemaker independent record label Fragrant Music. Simon's music and motion picture interests first intersected at the Sundance Film Festival, where he booked talent for festival events.

Energized by the film festival marketplace, he enrolled in law school and focused on entertainment law. Simon collected highest honors in many classes before graduating magna cum laude.

Simon's experience spans motion picture, television, music, publishing and new media. At his clerkship at Warner Brothers Pictures, he worked on legal clearances on “OCEAN’S 13,” and “THE BRAVE ONE.” As a clerk at Manatt, Phelps & Phillips, he focused on music business transactions. As an attorney at Lionsgate, he shepherded a bevy of motion pictures through the legal process, including “THE FORBIDDEN KINGDOM,” and “THE SPIRIT."

As a result of his own entrepreneurial activities and his years at entertainment companies large and small, Mr. Lamb has finely tuned his ability to counsel his clients through every phase of production from conception through distribution. Mr. Lamb’s hand-selected clients include motion picture and television producers, writers, musicians, record labels, publishers, artists, fashion brands, circus producers, bloggers and new media companies.
