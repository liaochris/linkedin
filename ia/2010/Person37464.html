Chris Bryson’s Summary

I am a conscientious business professional with over 15 years combined experience in the individual & family services, education and flexible packaging industries. My career highlights include leading quality improvement initiatives (as well as ISO 9000), corrective action response programs, corporate trend analysis of key performance indicators and accreditation/licensure standards development. I am currently an adjunct assistant professor with Thomas More College’s Business Department and an adjunct professor at Strayer University. My curriculum specialties are statistical and quantitative analysis, organizational team-building, and organizational leadership.

I am a results-oriented team leader and am known for my ability to communicate effectively across all levels and delegate responsibilities in order to maximize productivity and increase morale.

I create innovative organizational restructurings AND I develop problem solving-based quality improvement and training programs. I’m the go-to person when a practical approach is needed. My nickname is “slash” as I have performed a number of job roles at the same time.

One of my key accomplishments is reducing MacKay, Inc.’s error rate by 20% as a result of restructuring the organization. I also played a key role in the re-accreditation and full licensure of Talbert House, Spring Grove Center in fiscal years 2008 and 2009.

I completed both my BA in Mathematics/Philosophy and MBA from Thomas More College and have attended a number of professional training and quality development seminars.

My goal is to utilize my creative talents to develop a small-to-medium size organization in the Greater Cincinnati area and help it achieve “World Class” status, be it as an employee or as a full or part-time consultant.
