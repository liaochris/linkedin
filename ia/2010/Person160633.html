Paul Claessen’s Summary

All-round software engineer with a strong focus on embedded systems and firmware, with more than 25 years development and programming experience with both wired and wireless networking as well as with a wide variety of peripherals and bus interfaces. Extremely detail oriented, experienced and skilled at developing and integrating new firmware into embedded systems and system level applications on ARM, PIC and other RISC platforms.

Paul Claessen’s Specialties:

Proficient in most mainstream programming languages. Expertise in TCP/IP and other data communication protocols. Strong test and debug skills with a focus on high quality, architecturally correct solutions. Strong expertise in low level hardware interfacing. Quick in adapting to new technologies, methodologies, tools and platforms.

  • Sr. Staff Firmware Engineer

    High Fidelity Engineering

    (Telecommunications industry)

    February 2008 — June 2009 (1 year 5 months)

    High Fidelity Engineering was active in integrated ubiquitous national two-way, low-speed, data communications networks, utilizing HF frequencies and FM or XM radio as the inbound and outbound communications, respectively.
    I was their firmware engineer, working on anything from simple ultra low power PIC applications, to Zigbee wireless sensor network applications, to complex FM/XM/GPS/HF transceivers doing multirate modulation in firmware. Our main transceiver module is based on an NXP 2388 micro controller, interfacing to various peripherals: radios, synthesizers, relays, amplifiers, antenna tuner and various sensors through a variety of busses: serial, Ethernet, USB, CAN, I2S, I2C, SPI, parallel GPIO as well as ADC's and DAC's.
    I designed and wrote the real time operating system based firmware for this transceiver unit from scratch.
