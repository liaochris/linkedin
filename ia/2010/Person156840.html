Tim O'Day’s Summary

Oversees O’Day & Associates, Inc. Public Relations, a boutique agency initially established that specializes in the entertainment, travel/tourism, experiential retail, consumer products, and service fields.

Team of associates includes six highly experienced PR professionals from a variety of backgrounds.

Was instrumental in securing the National Medal of Arts for the Oscar-winning songwriting duo of the Sherman brothers, presented by President George W. Bush at The White House that garnered int'l media coverage.

Credited with conceiving the most successful ongoing PR stunt in the history of Disney Parks - the annual journey of the National Thanksgiving Turkey to Disneyland or Walt Disney World Resorts following the traditional presidential pardon at The White House).

PR efforts have included work with such notables as Julie Andrews, Shirley Temple Black, Ray Bradbury, Dick Clark, Walter Cronkite, Johnny Depp, Roger Ebert, Frank Gehry, Michael Graves, Elton John, Edward M. Kennedy, John Lasseter, George Lucas, Dolly Parton, Nancy Reagan, Christopher Reeve, etc.

1993 - 2000 O’Day oversaw PR for Disney's fine art/collectibles in addition to their successful auctions at Sotheby’s in NYC. He also spearheaded the launch Disney Auctions, a start-up between eBay, Inc. and Walt Disney Internet Group.

In 2003, upon request from Disney, O’Day returned as PR director and became one of the creative forces behind the 50th campaign - highlights:

Disneyland became the first non-celebrity to receive a star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame.

Inducted into the Smithsonian Institution

One of the first corporations to enter the field of podcasting and live webcasting.

In 2006 O’Day joined the Beverly Hills based firm of Manning, Selvege & Lee as senior vice president of consumer public relations before re-establishing O’Day & Associates, Inc. Public Relations in 2007.
