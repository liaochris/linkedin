John Gaudiosi’s Summary

I've spent the past 17 years covering the $50 billion videogame industry for international print, online and television outlets for millions of readers. I now utilize this knowledge for consulting and custom writing jobs within the videogame industry, while remaining active writing about the videogame industry for dozens of print, online and television outlets.

In addition to full-time Senior Editor positions at CNET GameCenter, Video Business Magazine and MCV USA and incite PC Gaming, I have spent the bulk of my career as a freelance journalist. I covered games and technology for The Hollywood Reporter for seven years and for Variety for three years, as well as Entertainment Weekly.

I have written a regular videogame column for Reuters for the past three years. And I have been a regular videogame contributor to outlets like GamePro Magazine and AOL for the past 10 years. I currently write for NVISION Magazine, Yahoo!, Gamerlive.tv, Playboy, Wired, USA Today Weekend Magazine, Computer Graphics World, MSN, IGN, EGM Now and many other gaming outlets.

I cover the convergence of games with Hollywood (movies, television and music), as well as the convergence of games sports catered to the mainstream gamer.

John Gaudiosi’s Specialties:

I have co-authored two books for Prima Publishing (The 20th Anniversary of Madden and Electronic Arts: 25 Years of Games) and one for Scholastic (How to Get Into Games).

In 2007 I was chosen as one of the Top 50 Game Journalists in the World by influential videogame trade publication Next Gen Magazine.

When I'm not traveling the globe covering videogames, I help my wife run our animal rescue, SoundPet.org, which rescues dogs from local shelters and finds them "forever homes."
