Larry Wayne’s Summary

Energetic, customer-focused, profit-minded financial manager. Proven success driving process and profit improvements in five industries. Known for a strong work ethic, high integrity, and for maintaining company information in the strictest of confidence. Works effectively at all levels – from the plant floor to the executive suite. Bachelor’s degree in accounting; member of the Institute of Management Accountants. Core competencies include:

* Accounting and management reporting. Supervised up to 13 employees (GEO) and oversaw accounting activities for up to 40 employees at 10 sites (G-P). At Georgia-Pacific, for instance, integrated Insulair, a $40M business with two sites, into Dixie Consumer Products. At The GEO Group, improved client service and cash flow; created processes that reduced the invoice approval process by 5 days.

* Financial planning and analysis. Directed financial activities for a $100M plant (Schwan’s) and a $1B business unit (Dixie). At Schwan Foods, used new forecasting process that achieved $1M in savings annually from labor and overhead expenses. At Georgia-Pacific, developed a set of consolidated reporting tools that reduced the month-end reporting and forecasting timeline from four days to three days.

* Process and profit improvement. Proven performance as a cost cutter. At Henkel, led a cost reduction SWAT team that contributed $10M in savings over a two-year period in a $500M business. At Georgia-Pacific, reduced product costs by $20M during a five-year period by changing product formulation, finding alternate sources, eliminating labor, improving processes and controlling overhead.
