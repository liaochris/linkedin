Alejandro Ribo Labastida’s Summary

Alejandro has spent most of his adult lifetime doing research on political science and international affairs. After graduating in Law and Political Science in the Universitat Autònoma de Barcelona (Spain) (1994-2000), including a year in Sciences Po (Paris) (1998-1999), he traveled to Bruges (Belgium) to read an MA in European Political and Administrative Studies in the College of Europe (2000-2001). He liked it so much that he stayed there three years more (2001-2004) as a teaching assistant in the Department of Political and Administrative Studies, where he taught MA students and did research on European affairs, including EU foreign policy and negotiation at the Council of the EU. In the meantime, he went to the Middle East three times to study Arabic and get a deeper knowledge of the region (2002, Egypt; 2003, Egypt; 2004 Jordan-Syria-Lebanon-Palestine). This ended up being more than a hobby, for in 2004-2005 he read an MSc in Middle East Politics at SOAS; and, after one year of freelance consulting and 4 months as a researcher in UNU-CRIS (2005-2006), he started a PhD on the construction of the nation in the Middle East at the Department of Government at the London School of Economics (LSE) (2006-2007). Yet, he was promptly attracted by the links between politics and the Internet . Therefore he started in October 2007 DPhil studies at the University of Oxford (in Oxford Internet Institute & St Antony's College), to write a thesis titled "Seeing Through the Net: An Analysis of Societal Production of Political Transparency on the Internet"; while he continues to offer independent EU affairs consultancy and EU negotiation training services.

Alejandro Ribo Labastida’s Specialties:

Research on political transparency on the Internet, Internet and democracy, training on EU negotiations.
