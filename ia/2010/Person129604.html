Gabriel Rifilato’s Summary

Forty years of experience in visual arts: Gabriel began his career as a journalist in 1973, writing for arts and entertainment magazines.

In the Seventies he specialized in photography in Italy and in Germany and during the Eighties, he supervised radio and television programmes for the Italian Rai, founding 'The Ideal Film Collection' a database on world cinema, published in twelve successive editions by Rai-Eri.

In 1994 he obtained a diploma in film-making realizing screenplays and shorts and in the following years Rai-Eri and Rai-Sat published his 'Dictionary of Film Directors' and 'Dictionary of Film Actors'.

He created the 'Multimedia Film Library' at Cinecittà in Rome and then, in these last few years, he founded a digital imaging production factory 'Secret Ink Studio' whose work is shown on the web site 'Secretink.it'.

Thanks to his digital photo portraits and his digital imaging technique, Gabriel was one of the first Italian users to be certificated by Apple for Aperture, software he teaches (with iLife, iWork and Adobe Photoshop) in courses and seminars held for Apple Resellers and Training Centers.

In 2007 he started the project ‘The Creative Digital Darkroom’: weekly workshops set in natural areas around Italy in collaboration with Pentax. This programme of Weeks of Nature Photography has got the patronage of the Italian Ministero dell’Ambiente, Legambiente and all the main environmental associations.

Recently Gabriel has been certified by Quark as an XPress Training Provider.

Gabriel Rifilato’s Specialties:

Digital photo portraits and nature photography, digital photography courses and seminars, advanced software for digital imaging, multimedia publishing.
