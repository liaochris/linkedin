Thomas Tribone’s Summary

Tom Tribone's work in the power and energy industry began while he was a student, working summers in industrial facilities while earning a degree in Chemical Engineering from Cleveland’s Case Western University. Tribone went on to become the youngest General Manager in the history of Atlantic Richfield, one of the largest oil companies of its time. After moving up the ranks of Richfield, he became an Engineering Manager at ARCO. During his time at ARCO, Tribone formulated the idea to sell electricity from industrial plants and helped lead the push to clean up boiler pollution. He was recognized for developing a computer optimization system that greatly increased energy efficiency. Thomas Tribone built on his success at ARCO when he joined a start-up company, AES Corporation. At AES, Tribone was a prime mover in the expansion of AES from a start-up to the largest power company in the world. Now, Thomas Tribone is the founder and CEO of Guggenheim Global Infrastructure Company. As such, Tribone has initiated and completed transactions valued at over $30 billion, and the company he founded now plays an influential role in helping to meet the world’s need for energy and infrastructure to support economic growth.
