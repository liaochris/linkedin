Judith Glick-Smith’s Summary

I have owned my own consulting business since 1983 and have worked with over 200 Fortune 1000 companies in a variety of industries. I am currently enrolled in the California Institute for Integral Studies Ph.D. program in Transformative Studies. I have a Masters of Science degree in Conflict Management from Kennesaw State University and a BBA in Accounting from Georgia State University.

I am a Fellow and Past President of the Society for Technical Communication (STC), an international organization dedicated to advancing the arts and sciences of technical communication (see http://www.stc.org).

I was an instructor at Richland Community College in Dallas from 1991 through 2004.

I have been published in the Journal of Mind and Behavior, the Journal of Conscious Evolution, and World Futures journal. I have published articles on documentation for high technology systems in Enterprise Systems Journal. I have also published in both of STC’s publications, Intercom and Technical Communication, the Center for Information-Development Management’s newsletter, Best Practices, and The Rockley Group’s The Rockley Report. I have regularly presented at numerous professional organizations and conferences since 1988. A list of presentations is available upon request.

Judith Glick-Smith’s Specialties:

I have worked in a wide variety of industries since 1983. MentorFactor, Inc. helps organizations visualize and implement transformational strategies to ensure sustainability during volatile markets.
