Graham Stewart’s Summary

Graham G. Stewart serves as assistant vice president and executive director of the alumni association at Ohio University - the oldest university in the Northwest Territory, founded in 1804. He directs a staff of 16 in a full range of alumni and student programming, communication and marketing, and operations, serving an alumni population of nearly 200,000.

He previously served as associate vice president for alumni relations at the University at Buffalo, the largest and most comprehensive of the 64-campus State University of New York system, with almost 200,000 alumni.

From 1999-2006 he was director of alumni relations at Ithaca College, a leading private comprehensive college with more than 50,000 alumni.

His involvement as a volunteer for the Council for the Advancement and Support of Education includes a recent appointment to the Commission on Alumni Relations. He has presented at district and international conferences on the topics of marketing, collaboration with student affairs and contract negotiation. A five-time recipient of CASE's "faculty star" award, he served on the faculty of the Newcomer’s Workshop from 2003 - 2007 twice serving as conference chair.

He is a current member of Council of Alumni Association Executives (CAAE), and a former member of the Association of Private College and University Alumni Directors (APCUAD).

Stewart has a bachelor’s degree in vocal performance and a master’s degree in organizational communication, learning and design - both from Ithaca College (NY). He is an active professional jazz singer, trombonist, and competitive whistler.

Graham Stewart’s Specialties:

Program assessment, identifying and implementing best practices in alumni relations, strategic planning, negotiation, board restructuring.
