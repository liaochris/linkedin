Paul Ehreth’s Summary

I am a game designer and environment artist with experience shipping AAA-title video games as well as feature film. I excel at single-player and multiplayer level design and take part in nearly every aspect of creation including: story, mission design, player flow, white box construction, scripted events, building and placing environment art assets, test iteration, feedback organization, and overseeing final art from both internal and outsourced art teams. I actively take responsibility in leading my team and regularly work with each department to ensure the smoothest and most efficient production and the best possible experience for players.

Paul Ehreth’s Specialties:

Level design, mission design, environment art, gameplay pacing, scripted events, 3d modeling, production pipeline analysis and streamlining, test iteration management, feedback organization, research, development, problem-solving, and strong leadership.

Proficient in Alias Maya, Softimage XSI, Unreal Editor 3, Source Engine, Photoshop.

    For the upcoming spy/action-MMO, "The Agency", I was the single-player level and mission designer. On this project my responsibilities included writing mission overviews with interesting gameplay elements, encounters, and pacing; drawing the paper designs considering player flow, building the white box level shell, building proxy art and placing it in the level to help define spaces, directing concept art paintovers, placing all gameplay-related elements such as spawn points, scripted events, and cover; running iterations of the level with the test team for balance and pacing, and overseeing the final art passes from both our internal art team and outsourcing teams.

  • Level Designer & Environment Artist

    Microsoft Game Studios

    (Computer Software industry)

    November 2005 — April 2007 (1 year 6 months)

    On the Xbox360 game 'Shadowrun' I was both a level designer and environment artist. As a designer I invented fun and intelligent playspaces, built them in Maya, and ran them through test iterations. I directed playtest sessions with a private team of over 20 testers, organized feedback and made appropriate changes in gameplay based upon it. After I finished designing levels, I moved over to the environment team to help lead things there. My environment work included modeling and UVing unique environment assets, troubleshooting technical issues, making sure art and design did not interfere with one another, running lighting tests, teaching classes on optimization, and creating a more streamlined creation process for all art assets. Toward the end of the project I was placed in charge of the entire model optimization process which included lowering the poly-count of both render and player geometry and conserving texture and lightmap space.

  • Environment Artist

    Omation

    (Privately Held; 201-500 employees; Animation industry)

    December 2004 — August 2005 (9 months)

    On the 'Barnyard' feature film I was a Senior Environment Modeler and XSI Generalist. I modeled hundreds of environment and prop models (both low and hi-res), taught classes on using XSI, helped streamline the production pipeline, and created modeling standards for LODs as well as normal mapping and instancing techniques. I also spent a great deal of time UVing models for the film and worked with programmers to write custom tools needed to get the job done.
