Boaz Herzog’s Experience

  • Principal

    Herzog Communications (Sole Proprietorship)

    (Sole Proprietorship; Writing and Editing industry)

    2010 — Present (less than a year)

    Freelance editing, writing, research and communication consulting.

  • Copy Editor

    The Oregonian

    (Privately Held; Newspapers industry)

    2008 — 2010 (2 years )

    Provided quality assurance for daily news and sports sections of Oregon's largest newspaper.
    • Enhanced publication's reputation by stopping countless errors from appearing in print and online stories and by preventing possible libel lawsuits.
    • Ensured quick copy turnarounds to meet deadlines; edited some stories in as little as five minutes.
    • Fact-checked, proofed and edited for accuracy, clarity, consistency, fairness, grammar, length, punctuation, spelling, syntax, tense, tone, and Associated Press and in-house styles.
    • Creative thinking generated pithy, succinct headlines that attracted readers.
    • Managed flow of content to website with Movable Type publishing software: applied search engine optimization techniques to headlines and captions, uploaded appropriate text and images, chose stories to feature on home page.
    • Selected and rewrote press releases and wire-service stories for news roundups.
    • Used tact and diplomacy to query writers about possible errors.

  • Sports Writer

    The Oregonian

    (Privately Held; Newspapers industry)

    2006 — 2008 (2 years )

    Researched and wrote game accounts, features and enterprise packages for Oregon's largest newspaper. Beats included basketball, bicycling, extreme sports, hockey, soccer and tennis.
    • Expanded market presence by developing stories that often turned up in oregonlive.com's most-read list and were later distributed by Newhouse News Service and reprinted by newspapers nationwide.
    • Conceived, planned and wrote publication's inaugural Winter X Games coverage. Traveled to Aspen, Colo., to write daily section front features and postcards while gathering audio for multimedia slideshow. Capped project with Page One story.
    • Other projects included covering the U.S. men’s tennis 2007 Davis Cup victory and chronicling how Portland landed an MLS team.
    • Called on by editor to apply expertise reporting on crime to write stories involving athletes in trouble with the law.

  • Nonprofit Executive, Editor

    Asian American Journalists Association

    (Non-Profit; Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    2001 — 2007 (6 years )

    Elected twice to serve as Portland chapter president following roles as treasurer and secretary.
    • Leadership and strategic initiatives helped chapter exceed membership and fundraising goals.
    • Managed budgets, communication, recruitment drives, fundraisers and membership programming.
    • Graduate of weeklong AAJA/Poynter leadership training seminar.
    • Volunteered for multiple AAJA Voices national college student newspaper projects; leadership roles included assignment editor, editor-at-large and copy editor

  • Crime Writer

    The Oregonian

    (Privately Held; Newspapers industry)

    2005 — 2006 (1 year )

    Researched and wrote cops, courts and public safety stories, plus weekly "Street Beat" column for Oregon's largest newspaper.
    • Writing projects helped prompt eBay to shut down illicit sales of ingredients used to make methamphetamine and spurred Oregon jails and prisons to share more information about dangerous inmates.

  • Business Writer

    The Oregonian

    (Privately Held; Newspapers industry)

    January 2000 — December 2004 (5 years )

    Researched and wrote news, features and enterprise packages about health care, Nike/sportswear and small business for Oregon's largest newspaper. Other beats included advertising, food service, hospitality, biotechnology, small business and home entertainment.
    • Expanded market presence by developing stories that were distributed by Newhouse News Service and reprinted by newspapers nationwide.
    • Projects included award-winning Sunday Page One inside scoop on how Nike won an endorsement deal with basketball superstar LeBron James, five-story Sunday Page One package examining the extreme measures some Oregonians were taking to undergo weight-loss surgery and "Lessons in Enterprise," an ongoing series of features about entrepreneurs.

  • Journalism Fellow

    The American Council on Germany

    (Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    2003 — 2003 (less than a year)

    Proposed and planned an itinerary for three weeks of travel, reporting and study in Europe. Wrote a series of stories published in The Oregonian about the expanding global scope of the athletic shoe and sportswear industry.

  • Business Writer

    Detroit Free Press

    (Public Company; Newspapers industry)

    June 1998 — December 1999 (1 year 7 months)

    Researched and wrote news, features and enterprise packages about the health care industry and other businesses for Michigan's largest newspaper.
    • Expanded market presence by producing stories that were distributed by Knight Ridder/Tribune News Service and reprinted by newspapers nationwide.
    • Tracked finances of metro Detroit hospitals and health insurers.
    • Projects included Dow Corning settlement with recipients of silicone breast implants, behind-the-scenes influence of nuns in running health care systems and the emergence of high-tech communities helping aid the rebirth of downtown Detroit.
