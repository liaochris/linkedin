David Shepherd’s Summary

I am currently a first-year MBA student at the University of Wisconsin-Madison and a member of the A.C. Nielsen Center for Marketing Research. The Wisconsin MBA core courses provide breadth in the key functional areas of business and management that establish a foundation for professional success. But the real value of the Wisconsin MBA comes from the depth of education and experience in the Marketing Research field, which can only be provided by the A.C. Nielsen Center… the country’s only full-time Marketing Research MBA program.

My professional background is entrenched in the Automotive Industry, but includes a variety of functions and responsibilities. My first job, at age 15, was in the Automotive Industry washing cars at Holly Ford. I have since held several positions in the retail Automotive Industry culminating with an internship at Lapeer Ford, where I impacted all aspects of the business from Sales to Inventory Management to Human Resources. After graduating from Albion College in May of 2005, I began my professional career in the wholesale and manufacturing realm of the Automotive Industry in Marketing, Sales and Service with Ford Motor Company. As a sales representative for Ford, I functioned as a business consultant and customer service agent. These experiences have afforded me many opportunities to grow, both professionally and personally; travel, extensively throughout the Northeast and Southeast United States; and learn from influential business leaders, within Ford and at Ford’s partner companies.

My goal is to build a career in Marketing Research where I can create innovative solutions to the complex marketing issues found in today’s shifting marketplace. The breadth and depth of the Wisconsin MBA and Marketing Research specialization, combined with a myriad of experiences in the ultra-competitive Automotive Industry, have provided a springboard to begin a successful Marketing Research career and achieve my professional goals.
