Randy Schneider’s Summary

I recently graduated from Purdue University with a Bachelor's in Electrical and Computer Engineering Technology. While earning my undergraduate degree, I developed an advanced understanding of electronic systems' design, testing and integration. My Senior Project featured two embedded microcontrollers, several analog sensors, motor control hardware and an LCD touchscreen. I saw the project through all design phases, including conceptualization, part selection, schematic creation, circuit board layout, firmware development, integration, and testing. The project was successful and I received an award for Outstanding Senior Design Project from the ECET faculty.

In the summer of 2007, I had an internship at Monaco Coach Corp. Inc. where I worked along-side several Electrical Engineers. I was responsible for aiding in the testing of electrical components for performance and feasibility. I also created electrical system schematics and updated diagrams for production and service technicians. Not only did I gain valuable experience from working in a large multi-disciplinary engineering team, but I also become familiar with the exchange of ideas, and the overall design process for an in-house production company.

Now that I have graduated from college, I look forward to beginning my professional career. I strive to be part of an exciting and challenging field where I can use my knowledge and hands-on experience in electronics' design and testing. I believe a career in the alternative energy or consumer electronics field would be most in-line with my interests, and allow me to pursue my passion for developing pragmatic solutions to everyday problems.
