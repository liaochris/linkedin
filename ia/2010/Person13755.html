Andrew Matthews’s Summary

In December 2008, I graduated with honors from Kettering University (formerly the General Motors Institute) with a degree in Mechanical Engineering. During my time at Kettering, I decided to concentrate my degree in Chassis and Powertrain. This allowed me to take classes which provided a background in the mechanics of lift, drag, powertrain, vibration, safety, and many others. Additionally, I spent one term studying abroad at Hochschule Esslingen - University of Applied Sciences in Esslingen, Germany.

Outside the classroom, I worked with the composite spring manufacturer Liteflex LLC as an engineering co-op, alternating school and work terms every three months. My responsibilities there included designing and testing many prototype and production composite springs for automotive, heavy duty, and recreational use. I also completed a thesis paper describing the design and validation of our heavy duty steer axle springs for new National Highway and Traffic Safety Administration regulations.

I am currently working for General Dynamics Land Systems in Lima, OH as a Manufacturing Engineer in the Assembly Department. I have been tasked to create and update operation sheets, train assemblers in new job assignments, design and procure tools and fixtures to aid in production, and generally oversee the assembly process for current and future vehicle builds. I have also been certified to use hydraulic swaging products and maintained a position as the Hours Per Unit Coordinator on our team.

Andrew Matthews’s Specialties:

Chassis and powertrain, composite materials, project management, production support, working on cross-functional teams, design software (Unigraphics, Pro/ENGINEER, SolidWorks, Solid Edge, I-DEAS), HMS-CAPP, MTS test equipment, Simulink, LabVIEW, Microsoft Office.
