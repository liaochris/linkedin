James (Jim) H. Mintz’s Summary

Jim retired from Health Canada where he was Director of Marketing and Corporate Communications. His responsibilities included directing marketing and communications campaigns in a number of health areas. He was also responsible for all advertising, creative services and strategic alliances in the marketing area. He led the development of the Canadian Health Network and a bilingual e-learning social marketing tool.
He is presently Managing Partner of the Centre of Excellence for Public Sector Marketing and Program Director of the “Professional Certificate program in Non-Profit and Public Sector Marketing at Sprott School of Business Carleton University. Has lectured at Sprott in marketing communications and non-profit/public sector marketing. Also lectures at the University of South Florida, College of Public Health (Tampa). Formerly Adjunct Professor of Marketing in the School of Management at the University of Ottawa. Served on the Treasury Board Secretariat Committee for Federal Government Communications Policy.

He has a varied academic educational background in the fields of marketing, advertising and public administration which include diplomas in both advanced marketing management and advertising. In addition to working on health issues, he has been involved in areas such as environment, energy conservation, public safety and, identity theft. Prior to joining the federal government he held marketing positions in the private sector, and crown corporations. He was Advertising Coordinator at Eaton’s (Montreal) and Manager, Advertising and Promotion at Canada Post Corporation (Retail Division). As well, his past experience includes working at a large advertising agency. He is fluent in both English and French
