Erin Monahan’s Experience

  • Convention Services Representative

    Indianapolis Convention & Visitors Association

    (Hospitality industry)

    2009 — Present (1 year )

    This is an exciting time for me to represent the Indianapolis Convention & Visitors Association to guests who attend meetings and events at the Indiana Convention Center and Lucas Oil Stadium. My role as a Convention Services Representative is to anticipate visitors’ needs and provide assistance that exceeds their expectations. I believe great customer service is the foundation of the hospitality industry and I’m happy to help perpetuate the buzz about this world-class city.

  • Gallery Assistant

    Wug Laku's Studio & Garage

    (Fine Art industry)

    2009 — Present (1 year )

    Wug Laku’s Studio & Garage presents innovative and thought provoking contemporary fine art and crafts each month, as part of the Indianapolis Downtown Artists & Dealers Association (IDADA) First Friday Art Tour. It’s a must-see on the gallery circuit and I’m fortunate to be the Gallery Assistant on this night each month. I’m always energized by the big art crowds that come to experience downtown’s creative community. And I relish my role in engaging guests and tending to the details.

  • Marketing & Public Relations Director

    American Cabaret Theatre

    (Performing Arts industry)

    2008 — 2008 (less than a year)

    This professional, not-for-profit theater was located in the heart of Indy's Mass Ave Arts & Theater District and beloved by many for its original production musicals and cozy, cabaret atmosphere. During my tenure at the American Cabaret Theatre, I worked closely with the CEO, as well as the box office and artistic staff to promote and market shows and special events to a diverse audience.

    In order to offset a tight advertising budget, I generated awareness by coordinating media outreach, leveraging key contacts and managing relationships to solicit media coverage through radio, newspapers, magazines, television, Internet, and other means. I also initiated a unique collaboration with fellow Mass Ave merchants that enabled us to reach out to new audiences and significantly increase our respective mailing lists.

  • Marketing & Public Relations Coordinator

    Arts Council of Indianapolis

    (Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    2005 — 2008 (3 years )

    Within 8 months of being hired as a Cultural Concierge for the city’s new visitors center at the Indianapolis Artsgarden, I was promoted to my role as Marketing & Public Relations Coordinator for the Arts Council of Indianapolis.

    During this time, I assisted in the development of a variety of initiatives where my responsibilities included producing electronic marketing promotions, coordinating special events, managing website content, writing grant requests and summary reports, and training participants to use the council's online administration system.

    I also served as a liaison with representatives of local arts, cultural and civic organizations and represented the ACI at citywide events, including the Penrod Arts Fair, Broad Ripple Art Fair and Hometown Tourism Expo.

  • Visitor Services Representative

    Indianapolis Convention & Visitors Association, Simon Property Group & Indianapolis Artsgarden

    (Hospitality industry)

    2003 — 2005 (2 years )

    By helping visitors navigate metropolitan Indianapolis in popular downtown locations, including the Indiana Convention Center, Circle Centre Mall and Indianapolis Artsgarden, I sharpened my knowledge of the city and honed my skills in Hoosier hospitality. These destination shopping, entertainment and meeting sites host millions of guests each year and I catered to many of them in my role as Visitor Services Representative.

    The Indianapolis Artsgarden director subsequently recruited me to help launch a new visitors center, which provides city and cultural information to local, regional, national and international guests. And I was fortunate to be commended frequently for providing exemplary service.

  • Freelance Writer

    Self Employed (Self-employed)

    (Self-Employed; Writing and Editing industry)

    2000 — 2003 (3 years )

    After moving to Indiana from our home in the Chicago suburbs, I continued my relationships with former employers and forged new alliances by freelancing. My projects included writing collateral materials and press releases for clients, including SportsMed Center for Fitness, Mothers & More, Southern Ocean County Hospital, and Waste Management of Indiana.

  • Marketing Manager

    SportsMed Center for Fitness-Wheaton Orthopaedics

    (Health, Wellness and Fitness industry)

    1995 — 1999 (4 years )

    As a fitness enthusiast and overall health nut, this position afforded me the unique opportunity to help other people take good care of themselves. Upon being hired as Membership Director for the medically-based fitness center, I developed the company's first sales lead tracking system, which enabled staff to monitor referrals, define sales objectives and improve allocation of marketing dollars.

    Within two years, I was promoted to Marketing Manager and charged with integrating the marketing and public relations efforts for all three divisions of this multifaceted organization serving orthopedic patients, fitness members and athletes in training.
