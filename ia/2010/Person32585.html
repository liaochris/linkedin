    Hugh O'Brian Youth Leadership (HOBY) was founded in 1958 by veteran actor, Hugh O'Brian (known as Wyatt Earp on TV in the 50's). HOBY is an interactive youth development organization that connects community service projects with practical lessons in leadership. As Director of Funding of the Michigan corporate board, Carlos provides strategic planning and oversight while leading fund-raising and new partnership development. Carlos is a 2001 alumni of HOBY. He is proud to have joined hundreds of volunteers across the country to support their mission of developing tomorrow's leaders. HOBY impacts the lives of 10,000+ ambassadors annually.

  • Search Consultant (Manufacturing - Quality & Continuous Improvement)

    Global Recruiters Network

    (Privately Held; Staffing and Recruiting industry)

    January 2009 — Present (1 year 8 months)

    Carlos Prieskorn joined GRN of Ann Arbor as a Search Consultant in manufacturing industries. Carlos is working with start-up companies to bring their patented intellectual property to production. In addition, Carlos works closely with well-established companies in filling positions related to quality, engineering and manufacturing.
    Prior to joining GRN of Ann Arbor, Carlos spent four years in the U.S. Army as a Military Police Officer. Working at a Level 2 security clearance, he has had the opportunity to work personal security detail for former president, George W. Bush. He has also worked in emergency relief efforts, search and rescue and on the Quick Reactions Force for Homeland Security.
    Carlos has also been an international corporate trainer for a multi-million dollar corporation. He spent time traveling to new locations to train staff in management, team building, company SOPs, and communication.
    While in the military, Carlos attended Ft. Leonardwood Missouri Military College Courses with a focus in chemistry and is continuing his education at Eastern Michigan University.
    In his spare time, Carlos enjoys volunteering with the Hugh O’Brian Youth Leadership Organization as a mentor to high school sophomores with the goal of “Teaching the youth of tomorrow how to think, not what to think.”
