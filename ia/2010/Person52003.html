David Sherbow’s Summary

IO

David A. Sherbow’s perspective and experience in the rock, pop, R&B and hip-hop music business is based on over 30 years of experience. He has seen thousands of live shows and concerts – from Elvis Presley to Otis Redding, from James Brown to the Rolling Stones, from the Doors to the Temptations, from Jim Hendrix to Stevie Wonder and from U2 to Jay-Z. As a writer in the early 70’s, David reviewed and interviewed many of the great artists of those days. He has managed and booked rock, funk, rap and R&B artists and run independent record companies that have released over 25 albums and have sold between 1500 and 50,000 copies. Finally, as an independent record promoter, He has helped break over 100 Number 1 records from 1995 to 2008.

From 1995-2004, David was privileged to serve as Def Jam’s exclusive independent record promoter and was responsible for helping to break such acts as: Jay-Z, LL Cool J, Ludacris, DMX, Ja Rule, Ashanti, Kanye West, Foxy Brown and Dru Hill along with many others. In addition to Def Jam, David has worked projects for most major labels and has impacted the careers of such other artists as Missy Elliott, Janet Jackson, Mariah Carey, Fabolous, Brandy, T.I., Outkast and Pharrell. From 1998 to 2004, David helped to build what became the largest exclusive independent urban radio station record promotion company in America doing $5-10 million dollars a year in business.

For a while in 2004, David occupied the corporate position of VP of Promotion for the Warner Music Group's incubator hip-hop label, Asylum Records, but in 2005 returned to the independent promotion ranks where he started his own company. In addition to his major label work, David has spent the past 4 years working with major hip-hop independent, Koch Entertainment, helping them to become one of the hottest labels in urban music. Koch artists he has helped break nationally are Unk, Jim Jones, DJ Khaled, Yung Berg, Sean Kingston and Ray J.
