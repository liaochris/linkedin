Peter Batt’s Summary

Journalist with 20 years experience of local and national newspapers and magazines, as well as in PR, contract publishing and business development.

Contract publishing: Developing and delivering new publications for clients as part of a communications strategy, within scope, time and budget. Client liaison and management. Print management and tendering.

Business development: Researching, developing and positioning new magazine project, including business case and cashflow projections, strategy, tendering, outsourcing and budgeting.

Writer, including: a) news reporting for several newspapers, including The People, and occasional feature writer for the Times Educational Supplement; b) news and feature writer for client newsletters and magazines, mainly as a contract publisher for NHS clients; c) technical writer covering cutting-edge developments in cancer research; d) copywriter for user guide booklets, such as for people with MS, on behalf of the MS Society.

Production, including: a) production editor, both as a freelancer for the Times Educational Supplement, and for a series of titles as a contract publisher at Whizzywig. Highly experienced in edition/flatplan management, page planning, commissioning, sub-editing, proofing and preparing for press using QuarkXPress and Adobe Creative Suite 4 (InDesign, Photoshop, Illustrator and Acrobat). Eye for detail and a proven track record of producing work to a very high standard to spec and within budget.

Sub-editor, with extended spells at the Times Educational Supplement, the Independent, with additional experience at The Times and Daily Mail.

Public relations: Experienced at handling press and media enquiries in a pressurised environment, writing press releases and arranging access to spokesman, developing PR strategy and organising press events.

Peter Batt’s Specialties:

Writing: green products and services, health and healthcare, IT (especially Apple products), human interest, news and features; copywriting.

IT procurement (Apple systems and compatible peripherals), job spec, purchase, set-up and configuration.

print management: digital output, pre-press.

business development: market research, positioning, suppliers and strategy.

health and healthcare: selling to the NHS, national charities.

  • Press & Publications Officer

    Forest Healthcare NHS Trust

    (Hospital & Health Care industry)

    December 1993 — January 1996 (2 years 2 months)

    Wide-ranging role for high-profile integrated NHS trust, which provided acute, community and mental health services.

    Role included:
    1) Press office: Managing enquiries from local and national press and media at a time when the NHS was under very intense pressure. Negotiated and agreed statements with relevant figures across the organisation; arranged media access to spokesmen within the trust, when considered appropriate; arranged events for the press to highlight particular campaigns or developments - for instance, invited Paul Ross (brother of Jonathan) to open the new Department of Sexual Health at Whipps Cross Hospital after he had been in the news for having an affair; helped develop and implement medium-term press and communications strategy - for instance, I was instrumental in giving Panorama access to Whipps Cross A&E to cover the ongoing 'trolley-wait crisis', in order to change the debate on the trust's funding position.

    2) Major incident planning: I developed and implemented a major incident plan to support the reprovision of mental health services from 'old-style' care at Claybury Hospital into smaller, community-based units. Involved work at the highest level within the trust, as well as with the police, fire and local authorities.

    3) Communications: I redesigned, relaunched and produced the trust's range of newsletters and magazines, specifically the staff and patient magazine, Forest Health, and the GP newsletter, Forest Script, which was designed to increase referrals from local practitioners within the local NHS 'internal market'.
