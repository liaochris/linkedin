Angela Ottomanelli’s Summary

Angela is corporate counsel at TheStreet.com. Prior to her current position, she was an associate in the New York office of Mintz Levin, practicing in the Litigation Section.

Prior to joining Mintz Levin, Angela worked as an Arbitration Assistant at the New York City Small Claims Court. She was also a managing editor at TheStreet.com while completing her J.D. at Fordham Law.

Angela is admitted to practice in New York. She earned her B.A., cum laude, from Iona College in English and Mass Communications (1997), and her J.D. from Fordham University School of Law (2007). She was on the staff of the Fordham Journal of Corporate & Financial Law as well as a member of the Alternative Dispute Resolution Society. As a member of the Brendan Moore Trial Advocates, she participated in the American Trial Lawyers Association Mock Trial Competition and was a semifinalist in the 5th Annual Michigan State University National Trial Advocacy Competition in Fall 2004. Upon graduation from Fordham, she received the Adele L. Monaco Memorial Award, presented to a member of the graduating class in the evening division who has made a positive impact on the lives of evening division students through the demonstration of compassion, courage, determination and a commitment to public service.

Angela Ottomanelli’s Specialties:

Various complex commercial litigation and arbitration experience in breach of contract, tax controversy, secured transactions and antitrust. Pro bono representation includes participation in Group Housing Part action in Brooklyn Housing Court action on behalf of Mitchell Lama tenants and U-VISA application and preparation and interviewing on behalf of at-risk women and children.
