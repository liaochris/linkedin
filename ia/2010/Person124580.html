Laura Frizzo’s Experience

  • Experience Design Director (XDD) / Strategy & Planning (S&P)

    Fullhouse Interactive

    (Privately Held; Marketing and Advertising industry)

    2009 — Present (1 year )

    I am the voice of the user. The target audience.
    What do we want them to feel? Do? See? Hear?

    I am responsible for leading the strategy of the Experience Design practice, consisting of user experience, visual design and storytelling disciplines within the agency. I ensure that the creative outputs (visual design, user experience and content strategies) are in line with objectives and are maintained throughout execution of all program deliverables. I work closely with all discipline leads to make sure the strategy comes to life.

    As the XDD, I am the team lead in new business development efforts for Experience Design including ideation to create memorable and personal experiences that deliver on our customers needs as well as budgeting plans and producing dynamic pitch presentations. I work with the strategic team developing the approach and long-term plans through partnership with our clients. The insights uncovered help us to create meaningful and memorable communication strategies and vehicles (digital and traditional) that produce results. I also continue to fulfill the role of a traditional Creative Director/Art Director as needed on specific programs.

    Fullhouse plans and activates experiences across a range of traditional, digital and emerging channels — as well as via your internal channels. We call ourselves “an agency with a digital heart.” This means our background is as a digital agency, and digital channels are often at the center of the programs we work on. We work with Fortune 500 companies like MillerCoors, GE Healthcare, Johnson Controls, Oshkosh Corporation, Harley-Davidson, Abbott, Motorola and Kimberly-Clark.

  • Creative Director

    Pepper Group

    (Privately Held; Marketing and Advertising industry)

    July 2007 — 2009 (2 years )

    I was brought in as the fourth member of the management team to lead, create and develop a more creative strategic agency, strengthen the talents of the creative team, build internal processes and evolve client relationships to elevate the level of the work. I established workflow processes to improve efficiency, accuracy, accountability and clarify communication. I worked closely with the account and creative teams to mentor, provide direction and review all client work. I ensured that creative and strategic executions were in line with clients goals and objectives and the highest level of creativity was maintained throughout. I was also responsible for new business initiatives and assisted the account/sales team by helping to accurately estimate projects, develop robust proposals, create case studies of successes and evaluate if new opportunities align with the strategic goals of the company.

    I was the direct supervisor of art directors, designers, production artists, copywriters, multimedia directors, interactive designers, web developers and freelance creatives. I performed annual reviews and progress checks with the creative team. I helped establish goals and action items for each of them to grow and progress in their experience and provide training opportunities.

    In addition to managing the creative team and being on the leadership team for the company, I also maintained a large percentage of my time still being a hands-on creative in all areas, branding, collateral, web sites, video, etc., as well as being responsible for self promotion initiatives for the agency.

  • Senior Art Director

    Williams Labadie

    (Public Company; Marketing and Advertising industry)

    October 2005 — July 2007 (1 year 10 months)

    As a Senior Art Director at Williams Labadie, a healthcare advertising agency affiliated with Publicis Healthcare Communications Group, I was responsible for maintaining a leadership role on large accounts and campaigns such as Abraxis Oncology, Factive and Nature Made. I created communications for our clients such as sales support materials for leading pharmaceuticals, national product launches for over-the-counter consumer nutritional supplements, national advertising campaigns for wound care dressings and viral web sites to promote antibiotics. After a short time, I was awarded to be the lead Sr. AD on the agency’s largest client, Astellas Healthcare.

    Communications were developed in print, web or multimedia as needed. I created conceptual direction/ideas and designed the look of the tools while working closely with the account team, copywriters, interactive team, editors and client. I art directed photoshoots and designed/directed layouts for pieces including branding, ad campaigns, web sites, viral or social media, direct mail, sales support, tv commercials and packaging. I was also involved as part of the team developing proposals and winning new work for the company.

  • Art Director

    StudioNorth

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Graphic Design industry)

    September 2000 — September 2005 (5 years 1 month)

    I was responsible for being the lead within a team-based structure and met directly with clients to gather information about their needs, which ranged from branding, lead generation, to product launches. I led internal teams of copywriters, designers, technology developers, project managers, and account managers to brainstorm concepts and strategies. I created effective design solutions and/or art directed and mentored others on executing ideas.

    I produced dynamic and effective communication strategies and tools with traditional print design, Web sites and interactive media such as flash animations, interactive CDs/DVDs, and video. I worked closely with designers, illustrators, Web developers, technology directors, multimedia designers/developers, and video editors to achieve a variety of design solutions. I was an integral part of producing innovative proposals and was a member of the StudioNorth Marketing team to self-promote and market the company.
