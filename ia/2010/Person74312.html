George Grous’s Summary

George Grous’ 25 year career in the field of Information Technology grew from Team Leader, Sr. Software Engineer, and consultant, to a start-up / IPO / entrepreneurial roles. Mr. Grous is an aggressive and strategic thinker with attention to detail and quality.

As Chief Information/Technology Officer at Odimo Incorporated, Mr. Grous was responsible for the company’s intellectual property and all aspects of IT operations. Mr. Grous built the technology group and data centers from the ground up. Mr. Grous reduced operating costs by negotiating better pricing with hardware and software vendors, streamlining IT operations (through continuous improvement). To keep up with changes in technology, Mr. Grous worked closely with vendors like Microsoft and Dell in early adopters technology programs.

As a Sr. Software Engineer at BroadLogic Incorporated and Adaptec, Inc., Mr. Grous worked on architecting solutions and software infrastructures for broadband streaming media applications for satellite and 1394/Firewire technologies. Mr. Grous also worked on off-the-shelf software products, including EZ-CD Creator (now Roxio).

As a member of the technical staff at Princeton Information, Mr. Grous provided consulting services to large corporations.

As an employee of IBM, Mr. Grous worked in manufacturing on the first generation of personal computers. Mr. Grous later moved into software development working on solutions for plant floor automation. These products were used by NASA for the preparation of space shuttle launches and large car manufacturers controlling plant floor equipment. Over his 11 years at IBM, Mr. Grous received numerous performance awards.

Mr. Grous graduated from the University of Miami in 1994 with his Master of Science degree in Management of Technology from the College of Engineering. He also holds a Bachelor of Science degree from Florida Atlantic University in Computer Information Systems.
