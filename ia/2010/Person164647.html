Pita Pit Franchise’s Summary

The Pita Pit was founded on the idea that people want an exciting alternative to fast food restaurants. They serve breakfast, lunch and dinner and provide a casual and fun atmosphere. Their motto is “fresh thinking, healthy eating.” Franchising began in Canada in 1997 and the US in 1999. There are currently 169 Pita Pit locations in 39 states.

Pita Pit Franchise’s Experience

  • Leading Fast Food Alternative Franchise Opportunity

    Pita Pit

    (Restaurants industry)

    January 2009 — Present (1 year 3 months)

    Pita Pit was founded on the idea that people want an exciting alternative to fast food restaurants—a place with great tasting food that’s healthy, fresh, and still served fast. (Because who isn’t burned out on burgers and pizza, and bottomed out on subs?) So Pita Pit ditched all the heavy breads and buns—along with the higher carbs and fat that today’s health-conscious consumers are trying to avoid.

    Instead, we came up with our own special light and tasty Lebanese-style pitas. And we vowed to fill them with only the good stuff. Leaner, savory meats that could be grilled to perfection. Unique flavorful cheeses. A larger choice of crisp, fresh veggies. And exotic toppings, including our own zesty signature sauces. Our pitas were also developed with a special fold, so these tantalizing flavors could be all rolled into one.

    This "fresh thinking and healthy eating" could now be served up quickly for people on the go. And for those who weren’t in a hurry, we designed our restaurants to provide a casual and fun atmosphere—part of which includes friendly employees who are good at what they do and treat every customer as special.

    Once we began serving lunch and dinner (late-night snacks, too), our unique idea took off. People of all ages and professions have quickly come to love Pita Pit. Franchising began in Canada in 1997, and within two years, it came to the US, where today it’s quickly spread to locations nationwide. (We’ve even began serving breakfast.) Pita Pit is a hit.

    October 2008 — Present (1 year 6 months)

    Jack Riggs, MD: Chief Executive Officer

    Jack has served as CEO of Pita Pit USA, Inc., which acquired The Pita Pit system in the U.S. in April 2005, since the company's inception. Prior to that, he served as the Lt. Governor for the State of Idaho and also served five years as a member of the Idaho State Senate. Dr. Riggs is also a licensed physician and has practiced medicine since 1980, including creating and operating a small chain of emergency medical and family care clinics in Idaho since 1986.

    Nelson Lang: President & Chief Operating Officer

    Nelson has the honor of being the first person to ever roll a pita at The Pita Pit in Kingston, Ontario, Canada. As co-founder in 1995 and co-owner of Canadian-based Pita Pit Ltd., Nelson and his partner owned The Pita Pit in the U.S. from 1999 through 2005, until Pita Pit USA, Inc. acquired The Pita Pit concept for the entire U.S. Since 2005, Nelson has brought his considerable expertise to PPUSA.

    Brenda Zosel: Financial Controller

    Brenda relocated from Minnesota to join the Pita Pit USA team in February 2006. As Financial Controller, she oversees the financial functions of PPUSA as well as all of the Pita Pit corporate and franchise locations across the country. Brenda previously worked at a manufacturing company for seven years after graduating from Metropolitan State University with a BS in Accounting.

    Paul Erwin: Vice President of Administration

    Paul has served as Vice President of Administration since April 2005. He previously owned and operated a Pita Pit franchise in Santa Barbara, California. Paul graduated with a BFA. from the University of Idaho in 1996.

    Corey Bowman: Vice President of Franchise Development

    Corey has served as Vice President of Franchise Development since April 2005. Prior to joining the PPUSA corporate office, he owned and operated 3 Pita Pit franchises in Eugene, OR, Bellingham, WA, and Seattle, WA. Corey graduated from the University of Idaho in 2002 with a BBA.

Pita Pit Franchise’s Interests:

Generally, to be considered for a Pita Pit franchise you must have a net worth of at least $250,000, $85,000 - $100,000 of which must be in some form of liquid investment or holding account. We also require all SBA applicants to inject 30% cash into their loan to promote immediate cash flowing and reduce monthly expenses. While this is our typical minimum threshold, we make exceptions to this requirement under special circumstances, or for those individuals coming close to the minimum requirements. Some people interested in opening a Pita Pit franchise may be hesitant to move forward because they have never had restaurant experience. The wonderful thing about the Pita Pit is that prior food-service experience is not necessary. All franchisees are trained to efficiently own and operate a Pita Pit so they will be confident in running a successful store by the time their doors open. We will consider anyone who is dedicated to working hard and following our established operating system.

Pita Pit Franchise’s Honors:

Pita Pit USA offers world-class training to all of our franchisees. Before a store opens for business, we ask our new franchisees to participate in Pita 101, our Franchisee Training Program, at our National Training Center in beautiful Coeur d'Alene, Idaho.

The program is a nine-day course presented by a qualified, trained instructor who covers essential topics for operating and maintaining a Pita Pit restaurant to corporate standards. Franchisees split their time between training in the classroom and in a live restaurant.

The classroom portion of the training provides franchisees with knowledge of how to hire and train employees, work with food and beverage suppliers, implement effective marketing techniques and manage finances such as labor and food costs.
