Camille Paldi’s Summary

Ms. Camille Silla Paldi is a US Citizen and a licensed common law trained Attorney in NSW, Victoria, and ACT, Australia and Auckland, New Zealand and is registered to appear before the Dubai International Financial Center Courts. Camille is also certified in Chinese Legal Systems from the East China University of Politics and Law in Shanghai, China and has received a certificate in Islamic Finance from the Amanie School of Dubai. Camille has received training in FIDIC contracts and dispute resolution from FIDIC and has also been trained in international commercial arbitration by King’s College and the University of Cologne in conjunction with DIAC and in international legal practice from the College of Law England and Wales. Furthermore, Camille has received professional training in corporate law through the Gulf Lawyer’s Training Program of the College of Law of England and Wales and property matters including real estate brokerage and law from the Dubai Real Estate Institute. Camille is an International Bar Association Scholar for 2009 and 2010, receiving a prestigious scholarship award for written submissions regarding labor law to attend the annual IBA conference in 2009 in Madrid and 2010 in Vancouver. Camille received her JD from the University of Melbourne, her Masters of International Law from the University of Sydney, a graduate diploma in World Politics from the London School of Economics, and her BA degree with Honors from Colgate University in Hamilton, New York.

Camille is an associate member of the Dubai International Arbitration Center (DIAC) as well as a member of the International Bar Association, Union Internationale des Avocats, the Law Society of England and Wales, New South Wales, Australia and New Zealand, the Arab Society for Intellectual Property and the Society of Construction Law. Camille is a native English speaker, proficient in Japanese and Spanish, is now studying intermediate French and Arabic and has studied Mandarin.

Camille Paldi’s Specialties:

Real Estate, Construction, Arbitration, Commercial, Employment, Immigration, Company Formation.
