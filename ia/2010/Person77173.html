Graham White’s Summary

Graham White is a Chartered IT Professional having worked in the IT industry since the year 2000 for IBM, before which he spent 5 years studying business and IT related subjects. He is a certified Linux expert, having experience in a wide range of areas associated with operating systems, applications, services and security. Graham has generated repeat business for IBM having been named as a key technical resource on customer projects including high performance clusters, storage and networked solutions, security and security enhanced Linux.

  • IT Specialist, High Performance Linux Clustering

    IBM

    May 2003 — December 2005 (2 years 8 months)

An area which gave him his first exposure to working directly with clients, he gained valuable additional non-technical skills while enhancing and using his technical knowledge. Graham quickly gained the expertise required in this field and was soon to become the technical lead on all the projects he was involved with, becoming a requested resource by many of the customers, generating repeat business for IBM.

    It was a time of rapid change and Graham was able to grow his skill and the team he was working in over a near 3 year period. He was responsible for anything Linux related in support of some 1500 developers with requests ranging from simple desktop installations and queries through to large server configurations and the architecture of Linux based solutions. He developed his interests across all the IBM platforms, eventually becoming more specialist in IBM's distributed platform System X brand (known as Intel/AMD type hardware).
