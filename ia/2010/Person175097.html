Robert Lee’s Summary

My passion for game design started back in the 80s with pen & paper Dungeons & Dragons and early adventure games like Zork. I went to Hobart Institute of Welding Technology and passed QC-7 welding certifications after high school to get a trade under my belt before moving on to the University of Toledo.

I started out in Computer Science and thoroughly enjoyed systems logic and circuits, but the film 'Office Space' made me realize straight coding wasn't for me. I moved on to a double major in Studio Art and Philosophy. During my undergraduate studies, I explored life drawing, sculpture, metal-smithing, 35mm and digital photography, digital drawing, digital video, and interactive web design. My philosophy studies explored moral & ethical philosophy and just about every other branch available for study. My final focus became epistemology, stoicism, and behavioral psychology. Having grown up with parents who run a foster home for developmentally handicapped youths, psychology studies were easy.

For a few years during college after for a few years afterward, I helped my parents run a foster home and eventually ran a home myself with my wife before deciding to continue my studies at Southern Methodist University pursuing a Master's Degree in Digital Game Development. For 2 years I ground away learning game development across 5 different game engines and their variations.

Now, I am working in an industry I love for Obsidian Entertainment as a designer. I couldn't ask for a friendlier group to work with or a better environment than Southern California. I love working in the game industry because my creativity is challenged daily, and I don't mind putting forward the time and dedication to get the job done.

Robert Lee’s Specialties:

Well-rounded knowledge of design, team game production, and asset creation. Speed-mapping, systems logic, environmental design, combat gameplay (especially small squad tactics)... My hands-on experiential background in behavioral psychology, philosophy, as well as Digital Studio Art inspires my design work and allows me to easily communicate across disciplines with my co-workers.

Editor Experience; Unreal 2k4, Unreal 3, Gears of War, Doom 3, Quake 3 & 4, HL 2 Ep 1&2, TESC/GECK, Torque
