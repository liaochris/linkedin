Rohit Kale’s Experience

  • Graduate Student

    University of North Carolina at Charlotte

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    August 2007 — May 2010 (2 years 10 months)

    THESIS - Energy Aware Store and Forward Scheme For Rechargeable Sensor Networks.

    This work involved the design and implementation of an energy aware adaptive algorithm which tries to reduce the current consumption in solar powered wireless sensor nodes. This proposed algorithm is optimized for use in solar powered MICAz nodes performing surface temperature sensing. Laboratory measurements showed significant current savings under low voltage conditions for extended operation (23 hours) compared to periodic non-adaptive algorithm. Current consumption model was developed to estimate the lifetime of the entire sensor network. The proposed algorithm is currently deployed as a part of an experimental wireless sensor network in a power substation for equipment health monitoring.

    PROJECTS
    • Wireless System for Transformer Bushing Monitoring: Designed signal conditioning circuitry for acquiring line voltages from transformers into the data acquisition board and implemented it on a custom PCB. Developed the layout and packaging of the entire system for deployment. Complete testing of the system.
    • Substation-Wide Monitoring Through Applications of Networked Wireless Sensor Devices: Developed software and hardware for monitoring of critical substation equipment like transformers, circuit breakers to perform sensing tasks like surface temperature, vibrations, sound, light intensity and gas density.
    • Measurement of Vehicle Speed using Wireless Sensors: Measured speed of a vehicle using Mica2 motes equipped with photo sensor for detection and transmitting data wirelessly to a base station for display.
    • WLAN Design for an Academic Building in UNC Charlotte: Developed a detailed model in MATLAB for evaluating trade-offs for designing a Wireless Local Area Network for a specific location on campus.
    • Implemented a Light Detection And Ranging (LIDAR) system using commercially available webcam, laser pointer and MATLAB as image processing tool.
