Gill Linton’s Summary

I got a lucky break starting out at M&C Saatchi in 95. I worked on British Airways, Ministry of Sound, Sky Television’s switch to digital and the British Government. In '97 I was promoted to AD.

While I was at M&C, I became increasingly involved in strategy and thinking about marketing ideas other than advertising, so I went to Fallon to be part of their London start-up, to re-brand BBC Radio 1 and Lee Jeans across Europe. At the time Campaign Magazine (UK's Adage), described me as ‘Inventive and open-minded… advertising’s future.’

In '99 I came to New York to be the founding head of planning at dRush, the joint venture between Donny Deutsch and Russell Simmons, which was the first collaboration of its kind to combine authentic pop culture with advertising. I worked with Coca-Cola, HBO, and Courvoisier Cognac - which won the CoolbrandLeaders award from the International Brands Council.

After dRush, I continued to work with Courvoisier to develop Atelier Courvoisier, a luxury fashion business created as part of Allied Domecq's portfolio growth strategy.

In 2004, I teamed up with Rupert Newton to start The Joneses, with a shared frustration that most brand and communication strategies are insipid, and actually limit the potential of a brand. We think great brand ideas are created when a brand strategist and a communication strategist work together and treat strategy with the same degree of creativity as execution.

Our work for Virgin Atlantic was selected as one of the 150 Winning Campaigns for the New Communications Age in 2008, and is featured in the book 'Advertising Next', by Tom Himpe.

I now freelance with agencies and brand consultancies. I've been guest speaker at the She Says forum, BA Reps & Creativity Magazine's 'Beyond the 30 Second Spot' conference, moderated a panel on 'Brand New Rules' and on the 'New Geezers Panel' at The Good Ideas in Advertising conference, both for PSFK.
