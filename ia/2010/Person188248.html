Shawn Steiner’s Summary

Currently, I am consulting for a number of Entertainment based companies who are migrating from traditional film and tape workflows to Digital Production and Post-Production pipelines. I also have a wealth of experience in Stereoscopic Production and Post-Production. I was with Autodesk's Media & Entertainment division from 1997 to 2009. I first started with Discreet Logic, who was acquired by Autodesk in 1999, I managed the Western Sales Region for the Media & Entertainment software division until 2007. I received a promotion to Sales Development Manager where I successfully launched a new compositing VFX/Post-Production tool called Toxik. Walt Disney Company, Dreamworks and ILM are just a few of the early adopters of Toxik. Today the technology is integrated into all of Autodesk's software and systems products.

I started my career back in 1983 as a Software Engineer for a little known company called Truevision, and I received PC Magazine's award for Technical Excellence for our work on the TARGA+ project. In addition, I was one of the founders of AT&T Graphics Software Labs working on software products called RIO and TOPAZ where I developed some of the first image processing drivers for over 100+ color printers around the world. I later moved on to help start several companies in this industry: Electric Image, MetaCreations and Denim Software, which was acquired by Discreet Logic in 1997 and the product is now called “combustion”.

Shawn Steiner’s Specialties:

Colour Accuracy Calibration and Management, Stereoscopic, Pre-visualization, 2d/3d Production/Post-Production Software solutions, project workflows and pipelines for film and broadcast production, internet webisode production, game development, architecture, scientific, legal, medical and visualization markets.
