Catherine Kaputa’s Summary

From Madison Avenue to Wall Street, from the halls of academe to her own brand consultancy, Catherine Kaputa perfected her ability to brand products, companies and places.

Catherine learned strategy from Al Ries and Jack Trout at Trout & Ries Advertising.

She led the award-winning “I Love New York” campaign at Wells, Rich, Greene.

For over ten years she was SVP, Director of Advertising and Community Affairs at Citi Smith Barney.

And she taught branding at New York University, Stern School of Business.

Yet, Catherine discovered that the best application for branding is not for products but for people to define and own their career identity and performance success. Personal branding can help you create more success in your career and more value for your company - particularly important in today’s tough economy.

That's why Catherine founded SelfBrand (www.selfbrand.com), a New York City-based personal branding company and gives talks,seminars and workshops on personal branding as part of talent development, sales force training, client meetings, and women’s and diversity initiatives for a wide range of companies and organizations.

Her book, U R A BRAND! How Smart People Brand Themselves for Business Success (April 2006, www. urabrand.com) won the Ben Franklin Award for Best Career Book 2007, a bronze IPPY award and was a finalist for Career Book of the Year from Foreword Magazine.

Catherine's new book is The Female Brand: Using the Female Mindset to Succeed in Business (July, 2009) is on female leadership.
