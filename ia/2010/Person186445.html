Sergio Araya’s Summary

Sergio Is currently a PhD candidate in Architecture, Design and Computation at MIT and holds a Master of Science in Architecture, in Design and Computation from MIT, a Magister in Architecture from Universidad Catolica de Chile, and is a licensed Architect in Chile.

His research focus is on advanced design technologies, specifically developing digital and physical design tools and methods. His research deals with development of digital tools in conjunction with Computer Numerically Controlled machinery and rapid prototyping techniques to enable innovative design processes. Has collaborated in research projects for US, France and Italy.

His academic practice involves the application of this techniques in the development of innovative research and approaches to design pedagogy. Has tutored workshops, lectured and published in US, UK, Chile, Netherlands, Japan.

Sergio Araya’s Specialties:

Computational Design, Digital Fabrication
