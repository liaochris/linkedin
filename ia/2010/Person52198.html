David Schultz’s Summary

The challenges facing the next Ramsey County Attorney are unprecedented. A troubled economy, increasing street crime, innovative financial predators, and reduced budgets threaten to diminish our quality of life.

I’m running for Ramsey County Attorney because I have the experience to meet these challenges, the leadership to recognize and implement solutions, and a passionate commitment to justice. I am going to protect our seniors from predators who prey on their trust for financial gain. I am going to use innovative probation policies to deter criminals from returning to a life of crime. I am going to work with our police, community leaders, and religious leaders to implement creative measures designed to prevent crime before it happens.

I have been a trial lawyer for nearly 25 years and have been recognized as a leader by my peers. I’ve managed multi-million dollar trial teams and led organizations dedicated to making the idea of justice a reality. I’ve spent a lifetime securing justice for those who most need it. I’m not running because I’m afraid of what’s to come, I’m running because I see the potential for positive change.

I hope you’ll join us in this campaign. I hope you, my neighbors, will let me know what concerns you. Together, we can work to help Ramsey County achieve its potential.

David Schultz’s Experience

  • Partner

    Maslon Edelman Borman & Brand, LLP

    (Partnership; Law Practice industry)

    2006 — Present (4 years )

    Over the course of my career, I have represented clients in a range of intellectual property litigation including trademark and patent infringement, and misappropriation of trade secrets. I also has represented individuals and corporations in complex commercial litigation, mass tort and product liability, as well as matters involving civil and criminal fraud, particularly in the health care arena. I have conducted public and private investigations into issues of complex regulatory compliance, academic fraud, financial fraud and criminal sexual misconduct.

    I am an experienced trial lawyer, having tried numerous cases in state and federal courts throughout the country. I have developed an active appellate practice as well, having argued more than 40 cases before several federal circuits as well as the Minnesota Supreme Court and Court of Appeals.
