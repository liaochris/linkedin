Andrew Matranga’s Summary

Maps have not only helped me find my way while on a trail or on a bike ride, but they have also given me direction in my career. My daily life is consumed with categorizing points of interest and editing basemaps, and I've also been on the user end of the technology while leading trips in the backcountry and logging rides on my local roads.

In my current role, my primary responsibilities fall into two areas: production and technical services. On the production side, I manage tasks for our staff of Web producers and graphic designers and coordinate the production of our map content, which includes database development and maintenance of our online map inventory. I also oversee our print map workflow from the early stages of graphic design to final layout. On the technical services side, I act as the point person for my team, working closely with our sales and client services team to ensure delivery of print and online products for our clients. This service covers anything from database formatting to answering clients’ questions about HTML code implementation.

Prior to NAVTEQ, I honed my cartographic skills and industry insight as the Assistant Map Editor at a start-up GPS project for Backpacker magazine. In this role, I helped establish and coordinate a nationwide, user-generated content project, in which readers contributed GPS data for the magazine and Web site. I served as a liaison for these citizen journalists, often spending hours on the phone with them getting trail descriptions and ghost writing their points of interest. In addition to my work on our Waypoints™ series of interactive hikes, I took the lead on a variety of unique projects—mostly notably a special map series on the effects of climate change in U.S. National Parks and an online guide of the John Muir Trail in California, for which I spent one month in the field collecting data.
