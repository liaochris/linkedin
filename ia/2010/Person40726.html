  • Senior Mechanical Engineer

    NORTHROP GRUMMAN MARINE SYSTEMS

    (Public Company; NOC; Defense & Space industry)

    January 2003 — January 2005 (2 years 1 month)

    My main task was to support the upgrade of the Trident nuclear submarine fleet’s missile launch tubes. I was to develop better maintenance tools and to evaluate new sealing systems at a full scale level. It was exciting to design and full through production a large pressurized fixture which resembled the upper portion of the launch tube. The fixture allowed for the evaluation new underwater sealing materials, maintenance equipment and procedure in a very controlled manner compared to the very limited established field methods.

    Following the vendor’s manufacture of this large fixture was very pleasing as I established a close relationship with them to make certain the valued item was made to specification and within the testing schedule.

    List of accomplishments include:

    •Conceptualized and designed a full-scale pressurized fixture simulating the upper 20 feet of a missile launch tube. This involved using large welding methods, classical stress analysis, ASME pressure evaluation and Pro/E modeling and drafting.

    •Closely monitored fabrication of fixture at vendor to ensure functional, budget and schedule requirements were met in time to retrofit Trident submarine during refit timetable.

    •Devised and conducted tests using this fixture to assess new sealing materials, tools and procedures to extend service life of the submarine fleet for 20 years while saving significant cost and time during refurbishment.
