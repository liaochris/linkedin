  • Co-Founder, President & CEO

    QuitNet

    (Health, Wellness and Fitness industry)

    September 1999 — September 2005 (6 years 1 month)

    Led all aspects of the start-up and successful growth and acquisition of QuitNet.com, Inc. QuitNet, host to the world's largest online community of smokers and ex-smokers helping each other quit, supports smokers through a combination of social and expert support, tailored content and integrated telephone coaching. One of the first efforts to build an online support community -- and one of the only to be in continuous operation for over a decade! Launched as a project of Boston University School of Public Health in 1995, it was spun out of BU as a private company in 2000.

  • Internet Program Director

    Join Together/Boston University School of Public Health

    (Non-Profit; 11-50 employees; Internet industry)

    March 1993 — August 1999 (6 years 6 months)

    Developed online news & activism services designed to assist community based groups working on substance abuse and gun violence prevention nationwide. Early implementations involved collaboration with HandsNet and then a gopher site with links to AOL. Eventually, these transitioned to database-driven websites, www.jointogether.org and www.quitnet.com. Both won top honors at the Global Information Infrastructure Awards.

  • Co-Founder, Associate Director

    Grassroots International

    (Non-Profit; Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    August 1983 — July 1992 (9 years )

    Grassroots was founded in 1983 with a commitment to supporting social movements in conflict areas through a combination of emergency relief, development assistance and advocacy. My focus was on using emerging networking technologies to shape public opinion and support fundraising & advocacy. Created & coordinated news distribution services from crisis spots, including Eritrea, Northern Ethiopia and the Philippines. Organized and led press tour into war and famine areas of Eritrea and northern Ethiopia for NBC Nightly News and The Boston Globe (which won a Pulitzer for the trip), Nov. 1984.
