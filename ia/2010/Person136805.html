Matt Archer’s Summary

Broadly speaking, my overriding ambition is to continue building my skills and experience in the management of business processes, equipment, and people. In order to achieve this, I am actively enhancing my practical operational experience through appropriate professional development opportunities in support of an ideal career path, the identification of strong professional mentors, and through the formal quantification of my industry experience via relevant tertiary education.
Specifically, in conjunction with the project management stream, I am in the process of furthering my business management studies with current enrolment in a postgraduate certificate in business administration via correspondence through the Edinburgh Business School, Heriot-Watt University.
Successful attainment of this qualification will enable me to enter the EBS MBA program, which is my long-term target, and an endeavour that I recognize as being integral to fulfilling my significant development potential.
Overall, my ambition is to continually match my quantified formal learning with valuable practical experience in order to explore the boundaries of my professional growth and potential returns in terms of knowledge capital relevant to my area of expertise.
The scope of my career development over the past seventeen years has spanned the progression from apprentice through to site maintenance superintendent/MARC manager. The scope of my experience means that I have been fortunate enough to be involved in a number of diverse, challenging, and rewarding roles along the way.
This body of work has allowed me to develop a balanced professional perspective through my varied experiences in different areas of the mining industry. It has also allowed me to develop the ability to integrate the primary considerations of safety, cost, and productivity into a strategy of achieving win-win relationships, and high efficiency leading to outstanding overall performance wherever possible.
