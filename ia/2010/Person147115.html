Mitch Galbraith’s Experience

  • COO

    Funny or Die

    (Privately Held; Internet industry)

    April 2007 — Present (3 years 7 months)

    Funny or Die is a burgeoning comedy brand and digital production studio that creates original, celebrity-based comedy for all major media channels. Some of our top initiatives include:

    * Our website, funnyordie.com, which attracts the largest audience of any pure comedy site on the Web - about 8M unique users per month.

    * Our first TV show, Funny or Die Presents, which ran 12 episodes on HBO's main channel on Friday nights during the Spring of 2010. The second season will air in late 2010 or early 2011.

    * Our first feature-length film which will start production in Q3 of 2010.

    Funny or Die's key partners include Gary Sanchez Productions (Will Ferrell, Adam McKay, and Chris Henchy), Judd Apatow, and Creative Artists Agency.

    Our lead investors are Sequoia Capital and HBO.

    The company makes money primarily from brand advertising, production fees, and licensing.

    I was Funny or Die's first employee and its top executive for the first 9 months of operation including 2 rounds of equity financing, assembling the core business team, and building the site/platform.

    Today, I am most deeply involved in viral, grassroots, and social media marketing; BD, syndication, and licensing; product design and development; financial planning and analysis; ad sales, sponsorships, branded entertainment, and general money making; and most other facets of the business not related to the creative process.

  • CEO

    Insider Pages

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Internet industry)

    October 2006 — April 2007 (7 months)

    Insider Pages is a local search site that enables friends and neighbors to share word-of-mouth wisdom about local businesses of all kinds. Millions of visitors come to the site every month to take advantage of one of the Web's largest collections of customer-written business reviews.

    The company was originally funded by Sequoia Capital, SoftBank Capital, and Idealab but was acquired in Febuary of 2007 by IAC.

    As CEO my role was to chart the company's strategy, manage day-to-day operations, oversee business/corporate development, make happy/productive employees, and delight users of our site. I also held a seat on the Insider Pages Board of Directors.

    During my tenure as CEO Insider Pages costs decreased by more than 60%, our traffic grew by more than 4X and our revenue grew by more than 3X.
