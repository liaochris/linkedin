Staffan Kristian Van Dyke’s Experience

  • Geologist

    Nexen Petroleum USA, Inc.

    (Public Company; NXY-US; Oil & Energy industry)

    August 2008 — Present (1 year 9 months)

    • Currently work in the Knotty Head Appraisal Team as a geologist/geophysicist specializing in reservoir characterization. Knotty Head, located in the Green Canyon Protraction Area, Block 512 (GC 512), is one of Nexen’s premier sub-salt discoveries in the Gulf of Mexico. Previously assigned groups include the Miocene Amplitude Team (MAT) and the East Gulf of Mexico Deepwater Exploration Team (EGOM), where my primary function was that of a seismic interpreter and 3D modeler.

    • Basic seismic attributes (e.g., MaxNeg, Absolute Energy, etc.) are used in conjunction with full-stack, full-frequency PSTM and PSDM datasets (WEM, Kirchoff, WAZ, etc.) to help extract as much useful geologic information as possible. In addition to executing these basic attributes, advanced seismic attribute techniques, such as, AVO; spectral decomposition; and seismic inversion, are also applied to most datasets for additional insight into the character of the seismic intervals being studied.

  • Geologist

    Merit Energy Company

    (Privately Held; Oil & Energy industry)

    May 2007 — August 2008 (1 year 4 months)

    • Worked as a seismic interpreter in Merit’s Corporate Exploitation Division (GOM Shelf). Primary duties included generating reserves for the company’s offshore Gulf of Mexico shelf assets; responsible for booking 30+ BCF of natural gas (High Island) and 5+ MMBBLS of oil (South Timbalier). Both of these reserves were found in fields that were deemed super-mature with little to no potential.

    • Seismic interpretation in conjunction with detailed well log analysis and a thorough paleontologic study of the region produced a sequence stratigraphic framework that revealed these behind-pipe reserves that were overlooked by previous operators of said blocks (PDNPs or BHPs); roughly an additional 5+ MMBBLS will ultimately be recovered from these reservoirs due to this approach.

  • Geologist

    Aera Energy LLC

    (Privately Held; Oil & Energy industry)

    May 2006 — January 2007 (9 months)

    • Worked as a 3D reservoir modeler within the company’s Strategic Development and Innovation Asset (SDI). Responsible for booking 10 MMBBLS of reserves in an otherwise abandoned field (located in North Midway Sunset). Applied advanced reservoir characterization techniques to model and document the geometry and distribution of deepwater deposits in an active margin setting.

    • Immersed myself in the multi-disciplinary environment of a production geologist, working intimately with petrophysicists, reservoir engineers, and drilling/operations engineers.

  • Geophysicist

    Vanco Energy Company

    (Privately Held; Oil & Energy industry)

    May 2003 — May 2006 (3 years 1 month)

    • Worked as a deepwater seismic interpreter and 3D modeler for the Geosciences Department. Interpretive studies centered on deepwater stratigraphy/sedimentology and reservoir characterization, particularly those associated with fine-grained channel-levee deposits (e.g., offshore Equatorial Guinea, Ghana, Cote d’Ivoire, Gabon, and Madagascar). Used AVO, seismic inversion, and spectral decomposition to help reduce the inherent uncertainties associated with the geologic interpretation process, lithostratigraphic interpretation, and determination of fluid contacts in frontier basins.

    • Applied sequence stratigraphic principles through the regional correlations of stratal units via 3D PSTM and PSDM seismic datasets alongside any available well log data (ODP and DSDP); these were used to identify and predict seal, source, trap, and reservoir rocks and their locations in both time (geologic and seismic) and space (within a basin). These techniques helped to mitigate the risk of conventional deepwater exploration targets in Vanco's frontier acreage.
