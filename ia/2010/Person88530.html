James Sims’s Summary

James Sims is the CIO for Save Mart Supermarkets and currently oversees all IT functions including: Corporate, Logistics, and Store Systems; PMO, QA, Data Center Operations, Networking, Security, Service Center, Field Support, Desktop Support and Help Desk.

James has worked in IT since the late 70’s and brings with him a world of experience, spanning from structural engineering and heavy construction to multinational web design and marketing/advertising. He has been on the ground floor of small emerging companies and worked with businesses that employ over 600,000 in 28 different countries; he has worked all over the U.S. and abroad including Europe and the Pacific Rim. James’ grocery experience comes from his time with Provigo (Petrini’s, Cost Less, New Deal) in the Bay Area; including time spent with the founder of Trader Joe’s. The strength of his experience rests in his in-depth knowledge of grocery wholesale and retail systems and practices, coupled with experience in growing so many different types of businesses using technology.

James’ current efforts at Save Mart have been focused on the evolution of IT as a true enabler to the business through more capable project initiation and execution, migrating towards a thinner and more dynamic architecture for our 255+ distributed data centers, leveraging virtualization and open source opportunities to increase flexibility, performance and cost efficacy. James represented IT as a key member of the due diligence team for the recent acquisition of the Ablertson’s NorCal Division.

James Sims’s Specialties:

Grocery wholesale and retail, logistics, staffing, heavy construction, property management, software products and professional services, IT consulting, innovative and unconventional thinking, strategy and architecture, Web-based collaboration, EAI, intranets, extranets, internet technologies, virtualization.
