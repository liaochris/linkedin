Wendy Manning’s Summary

Wendy Manning brings expertise in publicity, brand development and product positioning for her clients.

Manning currently holds the position of Marketing Director at Denver Pavilions, a two city-block, three story high, shopping, entertainment and retail complex located on the 16th St. Mall.

During her tenure at VolumePR she worked on projects with NASCAR's Dale Earnhard Jr and the #96 Sprint Cup team with driver Bobby Labonte.

Manning also worked with The Alternative Board securing top tier business media such as; CNN, BusinessWeek, CFO, Entrepreneur, Inc., Fox Business News, The Washington Post, the Associated Press, and The Wall Street Journal. She was also instrumental in securing the agency several prestigious industry awards.

Prior to joining VolumePR, Manning was the Public Relations Manager for retail giant Furniture Row Companies for eight years, where she developed and executed campaigns for the company’s 350 retail store outlets, managing a half-dozen brands. In addition, she managed the company’s corporate social responsibility program, which included The Salvation Army, The National Arbor Day Foundation, and World Vision.

In 2005, Manning became the PR manager for Colorado’s first NASCAR NEXTEL CUP team, Furniture Row Racing. Securing major press hits with popular Cup drivers such as Sterling Marlin, Joe Nemechek, and Kenny Wallace, Manning became an expert in the fast paced world of NASCAR media.

Manning also has a background in independent film production and publicity. Serving as both co-producer and publicist for the independent feature film “11:59,” she was pivotal in securing press as well as foreign and domestic distribution deals for the project in 2006.

Manning was named one of BrandWeek Magazine’s “Guerilla Marketers of the Year” in 2003 for an independent film publicity campaign.

A native of Denver, Colo., Manning earned her bachelor’s degree in Mass Communications from Colorado State University, Pueblo.
