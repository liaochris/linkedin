Clark Phinney, STS, WSO-CST’s Summary

Clark Phinney is the Safety Director for Maine Oxy. Based in Auburn, Maine, Maine Oxy is the premiere independent supplier of welding, industrial and specialty gases in Northern New England via 10 retail locations across 3 states. Maine Oxy is the only Acetylene producer in Northern New England and also runs the non-profit New England School of Metal Work, a state of the art welding and metal work training facility in Auburn, Maine. In addition to his duties as Safety Director, Clark is the Quality Manager for Maine Oxy’s Spec Air division’s ISO 17025:2005 certification.

Additionally, Clark is a managing partner of The ALZASA (pronounced ALL-ZAY- SA) Consulting Group, a health, safety and workers’ compensation issues consulting firm.

In the fall of 2009 Clark was selected as one of 30 individuals nationwide to be named to the Chemical Emergencies Work Group as part of the National Conversation Project (http://www.atsdr.cdc.gov/nationalconversation).

A former HR Manager, Clark is a licensed property and casualty adjuster (Maine) specializing in workers' compensation. In addition to frequent speaking and training appearances across the country, he has been published in multiple safety and HR related publications including; The AIHA Synergist, HR Times, Physicians Practice, World Safety Newsletter, Employment Times and the BLR HR Daily Advisor.

In December 2007, Clark was named as part of Facility Care magazine’s 1st Annual “Who’s Who in Healthcare Facility Management” list. Mr. Phinney currently holds both the STS and CST safety related certifications, he is a Hospital Incident Command System (HICS) trainer, and holds the OSHA 30 Hour General Industry and 10 Hour Construction cards as well as Hazmat Operations level certification.

Mr. Phinney is the manager for the Workers’ Compensation Group a subgroup of the EHS Professionals discussion group on LinkedIn.

You can follow Clark on Twitter at http://twitter.com/alzasagroup
