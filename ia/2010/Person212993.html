  • Product and Services Intern

    Vip mobile d.o.o.

    (Privately Held; Telecommunications industry)

    July 2009 — August 2009 (2 months)

    Vip mobile was my second internship and overall it was a good experience. I worked in Products & Services department with young people that were working on developing new and fresh products. From market research to the development of a real service that was born form the research itself, it was a fun 2 months.

    We were working on a real project, that might have had a chance in the market, if we had stayed in the company a little longer. The best side of this internship was that we were going through some of the phases any project has to go through, but on the bad side I had a feeling that no one really had our back, meaning that since we were interns our ideas weren't taken all that seriously. At the end I turned out to be right, since our project is still on hold. But I found the telecommunication industry very interesting and dynamic one, and definitely see myself working in it in the future.

    Program came with many more benefits, besides this internship, so I would definitely recommend it to everyone, especially since in Serbia there aren't many good programs of this sort.

  • Trade Marketing Intern

    British American Tobacco

    (Public Company; BAT; Tobacco industry)

    July 2008 — October 2008 (4 months)

    This was my first step into the corporate world. Had a good 3 months, although it didn't live up to the expectations. I was working on consolidation of trade data, in excel sheets, hence no fun...On the bright side, I met some interesting people, had a blast with my fellow Interns, and for the first time worked from 9am-5pm...

    The most important thing I learned is that internships are a gamble, with your mentor and the department HR chooses for you being he most critical factors. Basically, if your mentor is a great person ready to devote his/hers time to actually teach you something than you will always feel like you learned something valuable, regardless of he department or the company you are working in. That was not the case in BAT for me.
