Jonathan Winkler’s Summary

Associate professor of history at a mid-tier state university with responsibilities to teach U.S. foreign relations and international security. He is the author of Nexus: Strategic Communications and American Security in World War I (Harvard University Press, 2008), winner of the 2008 Roosevelt Prize in Naval History. He is an editor-at-large for H-Diplo, the online listserv on diplomatic and international history, and serves on a variety of committees and boards relating to diplomatic and military history and international security. His current project is a study of information-communications technology and U.S. foreign relations through the 20th century, including the development of the global infrastructure to serve evolving U.S. national interests and the development of information warfare capabilities related to these interests. In 2010-2011 he will hold a year-long Junior Faculty Research Fellowship in International Security from the Smith Richardson Foundation.

Jonathan Winkler’s Specialties:

Specialization in history of U.S. foreign relations, strategic thought, modern military and naval history, and international history of the 19th and 20th centuries. Experience in management and development of professional organizations in history and international relations. Experience in editorial duties relating to article and book reviews for a prominent scholarly listserv with 5000+ members.
