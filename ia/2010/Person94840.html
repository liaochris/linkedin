    (Non-Profit; 1-10 employees; Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

Advance grassroots and policy advocacy strategies to change and improve U.S. policies (economic and political) in Guatemala. www.nisgua.org

  • Associate Director

    Amazon Watch

    (Non-Profit; 1-10 employees; Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    August 2005 — August 2007 (2 years 1 month)

    Directed the San Francisco-based staff working on multiple campaigns to support indigenous peoples' rights and advance environmental protection of rainforest land in the Amazon basin. Along with the Executive Director, directed programmatic and fundraising efforts, increasing the organizational budget from about $500k to nearly $900k in a two-year period. Provided financial oversight for daily operations and conducted HR/personnel and organizational development and planning. Planned and lead organizational fundraising events. Provided occasional interpreting and accompaniment to indigenous and environmental leaders from the Amazon region.

  • Advisor

    Child Labor Education and Action Project (CLEA)

    (Non-Profit; 1-10 employees; Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    August 2003 — June 2004 (11 months)

    Worked with high school student group at a public high school in Brattleboro, VT to organize against child labor abuses and corporate-led globalization. Organized and chaperoned a delegation of 13 CLEA high school students to Nicaragua to stay with union organizers working for better conditions in Managua's textile factories. Coached the students on sharing their delegation experience and learnings with the Brattleboro commuity upon return.

  • Case Manager

    AYUDA, Inc.

    (Non-Profit; 11-50 employees; Legal Services industry)

    June 2003 — December 2003 (7 months)

    Worked with the Project to Stop Human Trafficking in the Washington, DC area. Provided trafficked individuals with social services and case management. Provided trainings to other DC-area service providers about how to identify trafficked persons and better assist them. This position fulfilled my "practicum" requirement for my M.A. degree.

  • Educator and Policy Analyst, International Team

    Witness for Peace

    (Non-Profit; 11-50 employees; Civic & Social Organization industry)

    August 2000 — August 2003 (3 years 1 month)

    Lived and worked in Nicaragua with partner organizations and communities to challenge and change U.S. economic and military policies in the region. Led delegations of U.S. citizens to Nicaragua to see the impacts of U.S. government and International Financial Institution policies. Collaborated with Nicaraguan coffee farmer organizations, maquila union organizers, peasant farmer communities and other civil society NGOs to work for IMF/World Bank debt relief, and more just U.S.-led trade, labor and environmental policies. Wrote in-depth articles and contributed to published educational documents analyzing trade, debt and the U.S. embargo against Cuba. Also led delegations to Cuba and Mexico.

  • Program Assistant

    Center for Global Education

    (Educational Institution; 11-50 employees; International Affairs industry)

    August 1999 — June 2000 (11 months)

    Accompanied U.S. undergraduate students on a study abroad program to Guatemala, El Salvador and Nicaragua. Worked with CGE staff to coordinate logistics and provide support to students. Served as the primary liaison between CGE, the students and their Central American host families. The study abroad program was entitled: "Sustainable Development and Social Change in Central America: Latin American Perspectives." Also led short-term travel seminars to Nicaragua during the Janaury break.
