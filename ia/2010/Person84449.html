Ilaria Accorsi’s Experience

  • Continuos Improvement Engineer

    Fluidmaster

    (Privately Held; Consumer Goods industry)

    January 2008 — May 2010 (2 years 5 months)

    Fluidmaster is a privately owned company and the leading name in design, manufacturing and distribution of toilet trim components worldwide. As a CI Engineer in a relatively small company, I have had a variety of responsibilities. Some of the accomplishments this far include the participation in value engineering initiatives such as the re-sourcing of packaging materials, product material substitutions and design optimizations. Analyzed and optimized cross-functional processes through the utilization of value maps, resulting in the implementation of new company procedures. Provided guidelines to the product development team for polymeric materials selection, including Polypropylene, Polyethylene, Acetal, ABS, PVC, Silicones, and Rubbers. Developed test procedures to quantify compatibility between polymeric materials, and to predict performance of elastomeric materials in certain aqueous solutions. I am also the principal contact for Wholesalers, Retailers and Original Equipment Manufacturers reporting of quality concerns. I have delivered timely resolution of quality issues by taking care of the customer’s needs, performing root cause analysis, and implementing corrective actions.

  • Engineering Supervisor, Materials Engineering

    DaimlerChrysler

    (Public Company; DCX; Automotive industry)

    April 2004 — October 2007 (3 years 7 months)

    The Materials Engineering Department at Chrysler is part of the Scientific Laboratories, organization in charge of all vehicle, components and materials testing. The Materials Department is a group of specialists in different disciplines which include polymers, metals, ceramics, fluids, and all their processes. With my metallurgical education, I was hired in this group to join the welding team. After 2 years, I was promoted to Supervisor of the group of 5 welding engineers. The welding team shared the responsibility of writing and updating nearly 120 corporate standards and specifications for resistance spot welding, arc welding, drawn arc welding, projection welding, laser welding and more. Performed materials properties characterization and allowables development. Coordinated research and development activities internally, with Mercedes, suppliers, and other research institutions such as the USCAR Joining Group, and the Auto/Steel Partnership (ASP) Welding Team. Negotiated the agreement between automotive and steel manufacturers in the creation of a new American Welding Society (AWS) National Standard for the assessment of resistance spot weld quality in the automotive industry. In charge of the welding laboratory administration.

  • Energy Management Development Engineer

    DaimlerChrysler

    (Public Company; DCX; Automotive industry)

    November 2000 — March 2004 (3 years 5 months)

    The Energy Management Development (EMD) Department at Chrysler, is part of the Scientific Laboratories, the organization in charge of all vehicle, components and materials testing. The EMD Department concentrates specifically in Impact or Crashworthiness Development. In my role in this department, I was responsible to guide the platform engineers through test plans to ensure the development of interior systems that would meet front impact requirements. Tests included air bag testing for Out-of-Position dummies, inclined sled for development of instrument panels, drop tower for knee blockers or doors, HYGE sled for interior system testing and ultimately vehicle testing at the Chelsea Proving Grounds. I processed and analyzed the data, created reports, and made presentations to the vehicle development department for risk vs. benefit analysis. Completed benchmark study on steering columns, and led a team in the writing of guidelines to design a column that would meet upcoming regulations. Collaborated with the SRT Team in the development of the restraint system for the Viper Competition Coupe Racecar.

  • Product Engineer, Toledo Machining Plant

    DaimlerChrysler

    (Public Company; DCX; Automotive industry)

    March 1998 — November 2000 (2 years 9 months)

    The Chrysler Toledo Machining Plant employed 1800 people in 3 shifts to manufacture Torque Converters and Steering Columns. As a Product Engineer, I was hired for the 3D initiative, with the objective of reducing steering columns warranty claims. When after 1 year the objective was met, my responsibility shifted to manage the reconfiguration of the low pivot steering column line to double its throughput, and launch new columns models. Developed and maintained Bill of Materials, Process FMEA, Control Plans, Work Instructions, and Quality Bulletins. Implemented in Just-in-Time and Lean Manufacturing techniques. Worked with suppliers to assure implementation of robust processes.

  • Design Engineer

    Aeroquip Corporation

    (Public Company; ETN; Design industry)

    July 1996 — March 1998 (1 year 9 months)

    The Design Group at the Aeroquip Technical Center was a group of 80 engineers focused in the development of hoses, fittings, adapters, couplings and fluid connectors for all pressures in industrial, aerospace and automotive applications. In this group, I had sustaining engineering responsibilities, provided assistance to customers for the selection and application of products, implemented Supplier Quality Improvement Programs, and performed internal quality audits in the US, Germany and Italy.
