Shadi Rahimi’s Summary

Shadi Rahimi is the Communications Director of the juvenile justice reform nonprofit W. Haywood Burns Institute (BI) and the Community Justice Network for Youth (CJNY). She co-founded the organization Seventh Native American Generation (SNAG).

Shadi has reported for publications including the New York Times, the Santa Rosa Press Democrat, the St. Petersburg Times and the San Francisco Bay Guardian. She lives in California and occasionally writes environmental stories for Indian Country Today. In 2007, Shadi received a Middlebuary Fellowship in Environmental Journalism. She is also an alum of the Chips Quinn Scholars program. Email Shadi to to request a report-back regarding a recent trip to Palestine with SNAG: snagmagazine[at]yahoo.com.

Shadi Rahimi’s Specialties:

Fluent Farsi, conversational Spanish; Fast-paced web reporting, press releases, promotional blogging, journalistic and artistic photography; Broadcast audio/film recording, editing, basic web design; Utilizing social interfaces; Software including Final Cut Pro, Pro Tools, Adobe Photoshop, all Microsoft Office programs; Experience with Panasonic HVX 200 video camera; Grant writing, fundraising and event organizing; Skilled in nurturing relationships with sources, funding and media personnel.
