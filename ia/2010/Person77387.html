Michael Green’s Summary

Michael was responsible for developing strategic global relationships as well as Simulscribe's overall distribution strategy and revenue plan. Simulscribe changes the way people control their voicemail by allowing users to "Read your Voicemail".

Michael, was VP of Alliances at Motorola Good Technology Group where he is built an organization to interact with the strategic hardware partners of the company. Prior to this role, Mr. Green built the carrier sales force as the VP of Strategic, Carrier Sales. Here he directed Good’s sales focus to an indirect model reselling via carriers. He managed teams that built relationships with Cingular, Verizon Wireless, and Sprint. In this position he led Good’s initiative to sell with carriers partners. While VP of Strategic and Carrier Sales, he successfully signed and launched reselling agreements with Cingular and Sprint. As the first sales individual joining Good Technology in 2001, he focused on launching the new company as VP of Sales. Together with a strong and dedicated team of Technical Support, SE, and Sales individuals who have been with him from the early days of launching BlackBerry, together they sold accounts like Wal-Mart, Pfizer, GE and others on the benefits of Good Technology’s vision over RIM.

Michael was Principal at Outercurve Technologies, a provider of wireless applications. He managed sales and business development relationships with vendors and network carriers, he successfully negotiated and consummated multi-million dollar agreements with RIM, Cingular and Motient Corporation.

Michael Green was one of the founders of Wall Street (WS) Communications, where he was EVP of Sales and Marketing; at WS he was instrumental in making the company the largest value added reseller of the BlackBerry solution in the country. He pioneered the adoption and rapid penetration of the new technology at Merrill Lynch, Smith Barney, DLJ, Pfizer, Time Warner and several other Fortune 500 corporations.
