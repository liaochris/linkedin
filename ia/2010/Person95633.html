Jenny Morber’s Summary

Jenny Ruth Morber received her PhD in Material Science and Engineering (MSE) from Georgia Tech with a focus on 1D nanostructures (nanowires) for biological and electronic applications under the tutelage of Dr. ZL Wang and Dr. Robert Snyder. There she led research teams in solving key obstacles to nanotechnology’s application real world, such as efficient synthesis and manipulation, device incorporation, biocompatibility, and energy supply. Dr. Morber has extensive experience in the fields of metal oxide nanomaterial synthesis, nanowire patterning and manipulation, and nanomaterial device design. She pioneered a novel technique for the synthesis of complex metal oxides requiring precise stoichiometries, and is credited through this technique for the synthesis of a material previously believed impossible to achieve in nanowire form. Though a collaboration, Dr. Morber reported the synthesis and growth mechanism of electrically active core/shell nanowires possessing diameters of around 100nm, with lengths up to several centimeters. Most recently, she published work detailing a cheap, simple, and highly scalable method for creating arrays of polymer nanowires, including electroactive polymers and piezoelectric PVDF. She also designed and executed computational simulations to determine ideal nanomaterial characteristics for electronic applications, and refuted the commonly accepted VLS mechanism as a possible nanowire growth mechanism in two industrially important metal oxide systems. In addition to her academic accomplishments, she has mentored undergraduates, presented numerous research topics and contributed to three patentable inventions. Jenny Ruth is also the mother of an adorable baby boy.
