Joshua Wayne’s Summary

Over the past 10 years, I have directed two large-scale federal grant programs for schools, police departments and community mental health agencies. In these programs I managed approximately $7.8 million in federal funds focused on emergency management and pandemic influenza planning; youth and family mental health; and gang, violence and substance abuse prevention.

Running these projects involved extensive training delivery and facilitation; project design, management and evaluation; budget accounting; vendor management and HR activities.

In addition to these projects, for the past eight years I have had a private coaching, training and speaking business focusing primarily on two things:

Personal Development Coaching - The main emphasis has been assisting individuals and groups is to increase productivity and focus, cultivating leadership skills, rapport building and communication, having better work-life balance and decreasing stress.

Helping At-Risk Youth - Primarily I've consulted with parents of struggling teenagers. I strategically teach them what to say (and when and how to say it) so that their teen will start behaving differently.

I also currently serve on the Board of Directors of the New Jersey non-profit organization: The Police Foundation for School Safety.

Joshua Wayne’s Specialties:

Emergency Management Planning; Pandemic Influenza Planning, Grant Writing, Grant Administration, Coaching and Psychotherapy, Organizational Strategic Development, Rapport Building and Communication Strategies, Teen/Parent Relationships.
