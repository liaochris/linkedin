Melissa Conder’s Summary

I have had a long history in financial services. After graduating from Tulane University with an MBA in Accounting and Finance in 1983, I joined Ernst and Whinney (now Ernst and Young; then, one of the “big 8” accounting firms) and obtained my CPA designation as a Certified Public Accountant in 1984 which I actively utilized until the mid 1990’s. Over the years my career path has also included extensive work in the field of technology helping small and medium sized businesses improving their profitability through automation. I now have over 15 years of experience in the small business consulting arena with both accounting firms and private industry and 10+ years in the mortgage industry as an executive and Information technology project manager. My passion has been and continues to be helping small businesses with their financial needs.

After losing nearly half the value in my own 401K due to market conditions in 07, 08, I knew there had to be a better way to manage investment portfolios. Unfortunately like many Americans, I assumed that losses were just a given when the markets declined. I was no longer willing to sit back and hope for the best. All I could think about was learning everything I could about comprehensive wealth management and investment portfolio management. So in early 09 I became an Investment Advisor.

I currently hold the following licenses:
● Series 6: Provides the ability to offer mutual funds and variable annuities
● Series 63: Uniform Securities Agent State Law Exam
● Series 65: Registered Investment Advisor License
● Series 7: General Securities Representative
● Georgia Life and Health Insurance
● Georgia Variable Products

Melissa Conder’s Specialties:

I specialize in wealth management/tax-advantaged strategies for business owners, executives and other high-end net worth individuals and their families. I have found that one of the best ways to grow wealth is to minimize taxes. I also specialize in 401(k) plans that are simple, affordable, and provide protection for the business owner as the plan sponsor. Plan sponsors have a great amount of fiduciary exposure; my goal is to mitigate that exposure when and where ever possible.
