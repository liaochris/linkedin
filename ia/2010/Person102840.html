  • GM Houston Teleport / Transportables

    IDB Communications Group

    (Public Company; 501-1000 employees; IDB; Telecommunications industry)

    April 1992 — May 1994 (2 years 2 months)

    Managed the Houston International Teleport Facility and the Transportable operations unit of IDB Communications Group. Responsible for the day-to-day operations and financial results of the division.

    The Houston International Teleport served customers in the Houston & Dallas Texas area with communications needs in the US, Mexico, Central and South America, the Caribbean, Africa and Europe through its 47 Uplink antennas. Transmitting Video, telephony, and data, HIT was the first non-Comsat license holder to operate on Intelsat Satellites in the US on HOU-1B. A station that I personally commissioned.

    The transportable operation consisted of 10 trucks manufactured by STARS (a division of HIT) and 6 trucks manufactured for IDB Communications Group. This operation offered the largest transportable fleet in the world and was used to regularly uplink TV and radio for sporting events, special events, disasters, breaking weather, and company communications.

  • VP Engineering

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Telecommunications industry)

    January 1986 — April 1992 (6 years 4 months)

    Built the Houston International Teleport (satellite uplink facility) on a shoestring budget, no time off, and long hours. This was the first non-Comsat Intelsat earth station licensed in the US (HOU-1B)! I commissioned it myself.

    Operated one of the first HNS (then M/A Com) shared VSAT hubs.
