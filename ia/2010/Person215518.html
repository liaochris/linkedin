Bill Dixon’s Summary

Having worked in both small organizations as well as large multi-national corporations, I have a wide range of expertise. Over the course of my IT career, I have performed duties in various disciplines, so I have a broad foundation when working with other departments.

With each position I have held, I have been responsible for the protection of the production environment by receiving application software from the developers, testing it in a production-like environment, and promoting the code to production. I have also provided not only primary on-call support for production problems, but also second or third level support for other support persons requiring additional assistance.

I have been Administrator for Tivoli Workload Scheduler (TWS), previously known as Maestro, since 1998. This TWS network has grown to 250 Unix and Windows servers. I have performed upgrades when necessary (so far, from version 5.1 through version 8.4)

I have performed many major conversions and migrations for different companies. In addition to the extensive TWS upgrades I have performed, recently I helped lead a major migration from WebSphere Business Integration Connect (WBIC) to WebSphere Partner Gateway (WPG).

I have continually sought to increase productivity through automation. This began in my first job by rewriting much of the accounting software to shorten the month-end processing from more than three days, to less than a half day. It has continued in each job by writing scripts and utilities to automate tasks. This automation decreases the human workload while maximizing the output; it also increases reproducibility and decreases the inevitable human error.
