    Master of Science Thesis:
    The Influence of Fiber Reinforcement on the Compressive Strength-Splitting Tensile Strength Relationship of High Strength Concretes

    The influences of single and hybrid fiber reinforcement configurations (steel, polypropylene, nylon and PVA) on the 28-days compressive strength-splitting tensile strength relationship of high strenth concretes (12,000 psi) were investigated. Nonlinear regression analysis was utilized to determine the mathematical relationship between splitting tensile versus compressive strength for different fiber reinforcement configurations and fiber volume fractions.
