Ken Mullins’s Summary

Career Background:
20+ years in complex systems engineering, business process management, enterprise architecture, and business transformation, including organizational change management, performance management, enterprise governance, and effective communications.

Federal Government experience includes Department of Defense, Department of State, Department of Justice, Department of Veterans Affairs, Department of Health and Human Services, Department of Commerce, Department of the Interior, GSA, OMB, and the Intelligence Community.

Commercial experience, prior to MITRE, has included Oracle and ITT, as well as consulting stints with BAH, Accenture, and SAIC. Since 2007, Ken has also served as the Editorial Director of the BPM Institute's online Government Bulletin (where, in addition to his editorial duties, he has published several articles and presentations of his own). Since 2008, he has been splitting the bulk of his MITRE work schedule, providing critical support to both the DoD's OCIO (Strategic Planning and Policy and EA) and the CIO Office for the extended U.S. Intelligence Community.

Ken Mullins’s Specialties:

Business Strategy, Enterprise Architecture, Services Oriented Architecture, Organizaltional Change Management, Investment Strategy, Acquisition Planning, Business Process Engineering, Enterprise Governance, Management Consulting
