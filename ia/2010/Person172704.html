Richard Cassidy’s Summary

Richard T. Cassidy has more than 30 years of experience in the practice of law in Vermont. He served as law clerk to the Honorable Robert W. Larrow of the Vermont Supreme Court in 1978-79, and as Chief Law Clerk to Chief Justice Albert W. Barney in 1979-80. He is a 1975 graduate of the University of Vermont and a 1978 graduate of Albany Law School. He has been in private practice in the Burlington area since 1980 and was a founder of this law firm in 1989.

Mr. Cassidy's early practice experience was general in nature. Since 1982, his practice has focused on civil litigation, and in particular on labor and employment, personal injury, and commercial cases.

He often represent professionals, particularly lawyers and law firms.

In addition to his work as a litigator and counselor, he has served as a mediator and arbitrator and is a member of the Panel of Early Neutral Evaluators for the United States District Court for the District of Vermont and on mediation panels for a number of state courts.

Mr. Cassidy is admitted to practice before the bars of the State of Vermont, the State of New York, the United States District Court for the District of Vermont, the United States District Court for the Northern District of New York, the Court of Appeals for the Second Circuit, and the United States Supreme Court.

Mr. Cassidy was a member of the Board of Governors of the American Bar Association from 2005 through 2008, is the Chair of the ABA's Standing Committee on the Delivery of Legal Services, and presently represents the Vermont Bar Association in the ABA House of Delegates.
He represents Vermont as member of the Uniform Law Commission, and was recently elected as the Commission's Secretary and serves on its Executive Committee. He chaired the Drafting Committee on the Uniform Collateral Consequences of Conviction Act.
He is the President of the American Counsel Association.
He is the Chair of the South Burlington School Board.
