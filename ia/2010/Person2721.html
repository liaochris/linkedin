Adam G. Southam’s Summary

Adam G. Southam, founder and chairman of Reshare Commerce, LLC, is a broad based strategic thinker with more than two decades of business start-up, development, branding and marketing experience. As Chief Strategic Officer, Adam Southam is responsible for the development of top-level strategies for the application of his patented collaborative channel commerce methodologies across the multitude of industries where Reshare’s software-as-a-service applies. He teaches and directs deployment of strategies for companies using his methodologies. He also leads the development of new iterations of his methodologies for application in the critically important health care, medical and fitness sectors. As a Member of the Board of Governors, he has an active role in defining direction for the company alongside a seasoned group of highly accomplished senior-level executives. His patent (US# 6,594,641) was conveyed to Reshare in 1999, where he remains the majority shareholder. Southam is one of the leading experts in channel management and speaks regularly at industry conferences. He also sits of the board of SinuGenix, HeartStore, OEAlliance, Syndicast, and Fitness Formulary.

    SinuGenix, LLC

    (Hospital & Health Care industry)

    June 2010 — Present (6 months)

    Adam Southam is the inventor and chief architect of the business model behind SinuGenix which serves the needs of patients of Otolaryngologists, Allergists, and other specialists engaged in the prevention and treatment of upper respiratory tract ailments and diseases in America.   • Member, Board of Directors

    OEAlliance, LLC

    (Privately Held; Automotive industry)

    October 2005 — Present (5 years 2 months)

    OEAlliance is positioned to be the innovative singular market leader in lean supply chain management solutions for Automobile Original Equipment Manufacturers (OEM). OEMs are unable to work with, or sell to, end users of parts (such as collision repair centers, mechanics and consumers) because of statutory regulations and contractual obligations between the OEMs and their Franchise Dealers.

    OEAlliance delivers technology that provides significant benefits to OEMs and their Franchised Dealers with minimal impact on the current workflow throughout the supply chain. Today, Dealers receive thousands of parts orders via fax and phone calls that must be manually entered into their business management order entry systems. Approximately 18% of those parts ordered are in error due to bad part numbers in the collision repair center’s estimating system, manual data entry from faxes & phone calls, or the service department pulling the wrong parts. This results in returns with corresponding costs including shipping, handling, insurance, loss, theft, damage, use of space, loss of time and decreased productivity.

  • Founder/Chairman/CEO

    Reshare Corporation

    (Privately Held; Computer Software industry)

    April 1999 — September 2009 (10 years 6 months)

    With the advent of the Internet and eCommerce in the 1990’s, Southam recognized that brands could not sell online directly to end users without causing significant conflict. At the same time, he knew that to “make a better mouse trap”, brands need to have direct contact with their end users. He also understood that brand messaging erodes as it gets conveyed through multiple parties. The challenge of how a brand could sell online and benefit from a direct relationship with its end users without disintermediating its channel partners was posed to Southam in 97. He immediately envisioned a solution that ultimately resulted in patents being filed for a system and method of doing business on the Internet to achieve these goals.

    US Patent # 6,594,641 was originally filed by Southam on April 16/99. He then founded Reshare on April 19, 1999. Between then and September 09, he acted as Chairman/CEO and Chief Strategist, leading the company through tumultuous times including the market crash resulting from 911, the telecommunications downturn and resulting economic peril, and finally the dot.com crash. Southam credits his Board Members, shareholders, vendors, advisors, friends and mother for their undying support and unfaltering belief during this difficult period.

    Even though a decade had passed and Reshare had not gained the traction it deserved, Southam did not surrender. Instead, he sought out and secured expert management with a proven track record of growth. He then engineered a restructuring to preserve shareholder value and make Reshare more attractive to investors.

    Reshare Commerce was born with a new Board and management. Reshare Corporation assets were conveyed to Reshare Commerce in exchange for over 99.9% of Reshare Commerce stock. Southam remains the sole Director/Officer and majority shareholder of Reshare Corporation and is now Chief Strategic Officer and Board Member at Reshare Commerce.
