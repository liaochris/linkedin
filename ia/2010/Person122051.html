Laura Seargeant Richardson’s Summary

As a seven-year veteran and Principal Designer of frog design and former Director of Design Strategy and Research for M3 Design, Laura specializes in the emotional, social, participatory and future design of products and environments. She has been involved in participatory “game” creation with teens, emotionally meaningful medical identification with eight-year olds, the role of technology in classrooms and the strategy of inflight internet. Laura’s most recent projects involve future projections – from the future of identity to the future of digital signage; from the future of social to the future of electric vehicles as well as the future of play. Laura’s latest thinking appears in frog design’s critically acclaimed design mind magazine on “Seeing The Future Synaesthetic” in which she advocates for a scent alphabet and learning in 3D.

Laura has played critical roles in developing solutions for Best Buy, AT&T, Ford, AMD, Alltel, Motorola, GE, HP, Microsoft, Sun, Thomson Financial, Yahoo! as well as clients in both the toy and medical industries. She is frequently brought in to facilitate client brainstorms.

She is currently working on a business book titled, SHIFT, on training the brain to see innovation and a book for the education/toy space on the 21st Century Super Powers of Play.

Laura Seargeant Richardson’s Specialties:

Laura brings expertise in strategy, design research, product design and transmedia. She was this year’s Sandbox Summit keynote speaker at MIT on the future of play. In 2009 she spoke at ToyCon, CPSI's Creativity Conference & Pecha Kucha. Laura presented at IIT's 2008 Design Research Conference and at the 2007 IDSA World Congress on The Art and Science of Emotional Appeal. Laura has also contributed to Interactions, GOOD, Gizmodo, Forbes, Forrester, Innovation and China’s Global Entrepreneur.
