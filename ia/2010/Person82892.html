Howard Gantman’s Summary

Senior-level public affairs, communications and government relations professional, with more than 25 years of experience as a Senate committee staff director, communications director, speechwriter, strategic communications consultant, legislative director and journalist.

Well versed in building legislative, communications and media strategies around critical public policy priorities. Extensive experience in managing complex issues and logistics and a strong knowledge and background in California government and politics.

Howard Gantman’s Experience

  • Vice President, Corporate Communications

    Motion Picture Association of America

    (Privately Held; Entertainment industry)

    November 2009 — Present (5 months)

    Manages the communications and public affairs program for the MPAA at a critical time in its evolution as the voice and advocate of the American motion picture, home video and television industries around the world.

  • Staff Director

    Joint Congressional Committee on Inaugural Ceremonies

    (Government Administration industry)

    February 2008 — May 2009 (1 year 4 months)

    Managed Congressional leadership committee responsible for the planning and execution of the swearing-in ceremonies and the luncheon for the Inauguration of the President of the United States at the U.S. Capitol. Oversaw relations with Armed Forces Inaugural Committee, Presidential Inaugural Committee, the Secret Service and the Capitol Police in handling Inaugural security.

  • Staff Director

    U.S.Senate Committee on Rules and Administration

    (Government Agency; Government Administration industry)

    January 2007 — January 2009 (2 years 1 month)

    Managed Senate Committee responsible for overseeing federal elections, campaign finance, ethics reform, and Senate rules and regulations. Oversaw operational and financial management of the U.S. Senate, including Secretary of the Senate, Sergeant at Arms and Doorkeeper, and Architect of the Capitol. Served as staff lead on oversight of Smithsonian Institution, Library of Congress and Government Printing Office.

  • Director of Communications

    U.S. Senator Dianne Feinstein

    (Legislative Office industry)

    June 1998 — December 2006 (8 years 7 months)

    Directed Senator Feinstein’s media and communications outreach programs. Served as primary office spokesman. Supervised communications staff. Developed long-term media strategy to articulate the Senator’s position on priority issues. Drafted speeches, floor-statements, op-eds, news releases and video scripts. Coordinated and promoted press events in Washington, D.C., and California. Oversaw development of Senate web page. Worked collaboratively with Chief of Staff and Legislative Director to provide policy advice and analysis on major initiatives, pending legislation and California issues.

  • Director of Government Affairs, West Coast

    The Kamber Group

    (Public Relations and Communications industry)

    September 1997 — June 1998 (10 months)

    Managed Los Angeles government affairs and media relations program for The Kamber Group, a public affairs and campaign consulting firm. Analyzed legislation at the city, county, state and federal level. Wrote news releases, op-eds, campaign mailers and brochures. Performed communications audits. Provided media strategy for the “Dump Diesel Campaign” by the Natural Resources Defense Counsel. Served as pro bono public relations consultant for the Elected Los Angeles Charter Reform Commission. Managed media relations for Richard Katz for State Senate Campaign.

  • Assistant Chief Deputy, Councilman Mike Feuer

    City of Los Angeles

    (Government Agency; 10,001 or more employees; Government Administration industry)

    July 1995 — September 1997 (2 years 3 months)

    Managed Councilmember Feuer’s media and legislative policy teams. Served as primary office spokesperson. Developed and authored new legislative initiatives and political strategies. Developed strategic plans for carrying out the Councilman’s campaign commitments, including earthquake recovery, building police facilities and economic recovery. Developed plan to restructure Council oversight of bond measures and capital improvement projects. Authored legislation to toughen gun control measures and establish new Municipal Gun Violence Working Group.

  • Chief Legislative Deputy, Director of Media Affairs

    City of Los Angeles

    (Government Agency; 10,001 or more employees; Government Administration industry)

    March 1992 — July 1995 (3 years 5 months)

    Directed legislative program, providing policy analysis coordinating the implementation of strategic initiatives for Downtown and South Central Los Angeles. Developed comprehensive communications strategy for the Council office. Formulated strategy for reconvening the Los Angeles Civic Center Authority to develop a Civic Center masterplan for the revitalization of the West Coast’s largest governmental center. Formed coalition with the Santa Monica Mountains Conservancy, People for Parks and other organizations to develop new park and recreation resources in the inner city. Developed plan for implementing Charter Amendment G to provide incentives for businesses to relocate into South Central Los Angeles.
