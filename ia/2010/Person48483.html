Danny C. Shelton’s Summary

“Of all the issues we are concerned with at present, the most basic issue, in my estimation, is that of human-earth relations.”
Thomas Berry
Ecopsychology integrates natural habitat relationships, or ecology, with human psychology and fosters the healing of human attitudes, concerning separation and estrangement, with the more than human natural world of which people are a part.
“It is this inclusion of both ‘ecos’ and the ‘psyche’ which distinguishes ecopsychology from both environmentalism and psychology”
(John Davis, 2006; What Is Ecopsychology)
My graduate dissertation focuses on this healing through a study which re-stimulates our deep need for Belonging and Community with the Earth, of which we are a part and that sustains us.
My Life Work, joy and passion is to lovingly facilitate healing of this human-nature relationship. I am not a licensed therapist, nor do I intend to be. My work is in education through my graduate degree.

    The purpose of the PNC Education Outreach Program is to offer low-cost UNESCO approved coursework to adults seeking higher education for their professional and personal life. Each course is done in the natural areas of your home or ecosystem. The Outreach program is unique to Project NatureConnect and the Institute of Global Education in that it offers a combination of small-group (3-4 students), three in-person Gatherings, and on-line faculty facilitated and consulting services, while enrolled in the Outreach program. From the Directors experience, this approach bringsw about an authentic, trust-building, and lasting sense of community and belonging among all participants.

    Danny C. Shelton: Curriculum Vitae

    After an honorable discharge from the United States Navy, I completed a B.A. degree in Geology and Philosophy using the G.I. Bill.

    Over the next 19 years, I focused on developing my own art and method of nature-connected education called Natureskills, which focused on the human-nature relationship through the senses.

    In 1999 I established an educational non-profit called The Institute for Ecological Sustainability and continued to present nature-connected education programs to help adults explore ecologically minded living and sustainable lifestyles.

    This past July 2008 I completed a doctoral degree in the emerging field of Applied Ecopsychology and Integrated Ecology through affiliations with The Institute of Global Education and Akamai University in Hawaii.
