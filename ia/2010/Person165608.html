Prasad Thammineni’s Summary

Prasad Thammineni is a serial entrepreneur and founder of four ventures. His latest venture, Pixily, launched in August 2007 and went live with their initial offering in July 2008.

Prior to Pixily, Prasad founded and grew jPeople into a well-regarded, high-end consulting firm in Boston that provides technology leadership for clients such as Fidelity Investments and IBM.

Prasad was also the CTO and co-founder of Indolis.com, a B2B trading hub connecting enterprises, portals and intranets. Indolis was ranked in the top 10 business plans in the 2000 Harvard Business Plan Competition.

Prasad’s entrepreneurial ventures are preceded by his leadership roles at Specialized Software. As Chief Technology Officer, he led their technology strategy, developed cutting edge products and built a $6 million consulting practice in 18 months. In addition, Prasad was a frequent presenter at events such as the Java Business Expo and various IBM conferences, and contributor to Java Developer’s Journal and other industry publications.

Prasad is a 2007 MBA with honors graduate from The Wharton School, University of Pennsylvania with a dual major in Operations and Marketing. At Wharton, Prasad chaired the Wharton Entrepreneurship Conference, served as business mentor for student initiatives at the Weiss Tech House, the UPenn incubator, and won the Joseph P. Wharton Award for Leadership and Innovation. Prasad is also a graduate of the Birla Institute and Technology and Science (BITS), Pilani with dual degrees in Mathematics and Computer Science.

Prasad lives in Boston with his wife, Shivani. They enjoy living in one of the most walkable neighborhoods in the city and, in fact, one of the most walkable cities in the country. When he is not ruminating on his ideas, he enjoys dancing, photography, biking and making dinner.
