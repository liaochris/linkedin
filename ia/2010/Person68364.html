Gary J. Hale’s Summary

Policy Arena:

As Chief, DEA HQs Intelligence Publications Unit, authored, edited, proofed, laid-out and published national-level strategic analysis publications (International Narcotics Control Strategy Report; National Narcotics Intelligence Consumers Committee Annual Assessment; Worldwide Cocaine Situation Report; Country Profiles; Laboratory Lexicon - A Guide to Cocaine Laboratory Terminology; more.)

As Chief, DEA HQs Sensitive Operations Unit, served on a multi-agency fact-finding panel of intelligence experts commissioned by Attorney General Janet Reno to assess the drug, crime and security situation in the Caribbean as it related to the overall threat to the U.S. Authored and briefed a theatre-wide assessment to policy makers.

As Chief of Intelligence, DEA Houston Field Division, authored drug policy papers including the Presidential Daily Brief; conceptualized and developed an Alternate Futures - Red Cell Analysis for Washington policy makers; served as a speaker and panel member for bi-lateral drug policy plenary sessions in Cuernavaca, Washington, San Antonio and Houston with U.S. and Mexico Cabinet members, agency heads and policy officials. Briefed Members of the U.S. Congress.

As speaker at the James A. Baker III Institute for Public Policy - Rice University, delivered speeches and served as a panel expert on drug policy issues and Mexico. Member: World Affairs Council - Houston.

    (Security and Investigations industry)

    Gary J. Hale is a professional of 36-years service in Federal law enforcement, the intelligence community and polygraphy.
