Scott Lissner’s Summary

Since January of 2000 L. Scott Lissner has served as the ADA Coordinator for The Ohio State University. Reporting to the Provost he is also an Associate of the John Glenn School of Public Policy and serves as a lecturer for the Moritz College of Law, the Knowlton School of Architecture and Disability Studies. His teaching informs and energizes his work as the university’s compliance officer, his role in creating seamless access to all of the university’s programs and serve as a catalyst for disability related policy and initiatives. Engaged in community and professional service, Lissner serves on the Boards of Directors for ADA-OHIO and the Association on Higher Education And Disability, the Editorial Board for Thompson’s 504 Compliance Manual, the Columbus Advisory Committee on Disability, the Ohio Secretary of State’s ADA Advisory Committee and the Columbus Bar Association’s Profession Ethics and Grievance Committee. Lissner publishes, presents, and consults frequently on disability issues. Recent publications include “Universal Design in the Institutional Setting: Weaving a Philosophy into Campus Planning” in Universal Design: From Accessibility to Zoning (J. Cowley-Evans & J. Nasser (Eds.) and “From Legal Principle to Informed Practice” with J. E. Jarrow.
