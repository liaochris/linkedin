Fran Morrissey’s Summary

TECHNICAL

Operated and maintained two amplified femtosecond laser systems: home-made Ti:Sapphire laser oscillator with 1 mJ 35 fs BMI α1000/US kHz regenerative amplifier, Spectra-Physics amplified Ti:Sapphire Hurricane system

Experience with nonlinear frequency conversion: harmonic generation, optical parametric amplification, hollow fiber and sapphire continuum generation with dispersion compensation

Expertise in time-resolved measurements: built a three arm optical interferometer for multiple pulse excitation; experience with femtosecond optical pump-probe spectroscopy

Implementation and calibration of laboratory-built single-mode fiber optic sensing instrumentation at Oak Ridge National Laboratory: Fabry-Perot interferometric pressure surface strain sensor, diaphragm pressure sensor, and phosphor temperature sensor

Experience with high-vacuum direct and gas-exchange cryostats using liquid N2 and He cryogens

Knowledge of signal processing electronics, computer interfacing, optoelectronic devices; experience in the design/construction of opto-mechanical components

Computer experience: (hardware) installation and configuration of computer peripherals, fixed disk, motherboard/processor, etc.

(modeling) MatLab, Igor, MathCad, Origin Pro, S-Plus, Mathematica; worked with C++, Basic, LabView, TeX, DOS batch routines

RESEARCH

Designed and carried out time-resolved experiments that identified the vibrational and electronic properties of novel quasi-one-dimensional systems using vibrationally impulsive excitation

First identification of coherent acoustic phonon generation associated with carrier localization dynamics

First measurement of the vibrational properties of a metastable self-trapped exciton using excited-state resonant impulsive Raman spectroscopy

Fran Morrissey’s Specialties:

Working knowledge in high power laser systems in nonlinear frequency conversion via periodically poled nonlinear materials in optical parametric oscillator.

Expertise in laser physics, nonlinear frequency conversion, fiber optic sensing systems, optoelectronics, and ultrafast laser science
