John Saint-Denis’s Summary

John has been in consumer goods and B2B and B2C marketing for the past 20 years. He has recently helped launch Andrew Martin Los Angeles Retail and Trade Showroom. He had been with Baker Furniture since 2007 when he opened the first LA Flagship retail store and then managed both the retail store and PDC wholesale showroom for Baker in Los Angeles. He has additional experience with Williams Sonoma Home, L’Oreal, Johnson Favaro Architecture, two national marketing agencies specialising in medical professionals, and he designed his own line of furniture and ran his own retail and wholesale showroom and interior design studio in Santa Monica, CA in the late 90’s. John has served on the board of the Avenues of Art and Design in West Hollywood, CA and currently serves on the board of the La Cienega Design Quarter: www.lcdqla.com.

John’s mother is from the Amalfi coast in Italy, and John has visited that region many times at various points in his life studying the pottery and architecture of the area. John’s father is French, and encouraged John to study communications and linguistic psychology at a French school in Massachusetts. John lived in Paris for a year during high school and in Lyon for a year during college. He speaks English and French fairly well, is studying Italian again, and studied liturgical Latin extensively, having completed 16 years of French catholic school. John’s father was a carpenter and furniture builder near New York City. John holds a BA in communications and was acredited to offer continuing education units to design professionals who completed his History of Luxury training program at Baker.

John most recently completed an intensive month long workshop on art history at the Uffizi in Florence last August.
