Matthew Miller’s Summary

Matthew M. Miller is a native of Mitchell, Indiana. He currently works in Washington, D.C. and is pursuing his personal and professional interests of business and government affairs.

Matthew has worked extensively on taxation, energy, domestic policy, international business development, and budget issues. He has substantial experience in the regulatory rulemaking process, federal funding and in congressional advocacy.

Currently, Matthew works on tax, economic, corporate finance, and government contracts issues as a Senior Director at Financial Executives International and serves on the Board of Directors of the Phoenix Scholarship Fund, Inc. – an Indiana nonprofit organization.

While at the EOP Group, Matthew provided counsel and advocacy support to the utility industry concerning disaster recovery assistance issues and the HUD Community Development Block Grant (CDBG) program in the aftermath of the September 11th terrorist attacks and Hurricanes Katrina, Rita and Wilma. He also served on the Republican Governors Assocation National Finance Committee for two terms.

Matthew received his Bachelor’s degree from Purdue University and his Juris Doctorate from the Seton Hall University School of Law. Mr. Miller also received his LL.M. from Georgetown Law’s graduate taxation program.

Matthew Miller’s Specialties:

Corporate and international taxation, Executive Branch issue resolution, government contracts, congressional advocacy, coalition-building, political management and fundraising, regulatory process and compliance, and appropriations.
