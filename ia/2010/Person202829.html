Thomas Cott’s Summary

I am currently the Director of Marketing for Alvin Ailey American Dance Theater.

Over the last 26 years, I have amassed a diverse range of credits in theater, music, dance, video, media and the humanities. In my multi-faceted career to date, I've worked on about 120 plays and musicals in Broadway and off-Broadway theaters as well as several dozen opera productions at the Metropolitan Opera and New York City Opera. Along the way, I have also produced a variety of memorable one-night-only special events.

As an independent producer and management consultant, my clients have included ArtsConnection, Lincoln Center Theater, The Metropolitan Opera, The Metropolitan Opera Guild, The New 42nd Street, New York City Ballet, New York City Opera, the New York Musical Theater Festival, the New York Public Library, Paper Mill Playhouse, Theater Development Fund and the Richard Tucker Music Foundation.

As an industry expert, I have been a regular moderator, speaker or panelist at a range of events. I have taught or guest lectured on arts-related issues at Yale, Columbia, NYU, Fordham, Marymount, and the Commercial Theater Institute. I have been involved in the evaluation and/or selection process for the ASCAP Musical Theater Workshop, the Jonathan Larson Foundation grant program for emerging musical theater writers, NAMT's Festival of New Musicals and the New York Musical Theater Festival. I am currently in my second year of a 3-year term on the Tony Awards Nominating Committee.

Last but not least, I am -- as one colleague has said of me -- "as well known (almost) for a daily email called 'You’ve Cott Mail,' a digest of articles published online that has virally grown to be an enormous resource for arts professionals in the U.S." If you're interested in signing up, please click on "You've Cott Mail Sign-Up" under the Websites section.

Thomas Cott’s Specialties:

Producing, programming, artistic direction, marketing, non-profit fundraising, strategic planning, copywriting, editing (copy, audio, video), graphic design, event planning and arts education

Thomas Cott’s Experience

  • Director of Marketing

    Alvin Ailey American Dance Theater

    (Non-Profit; Performing Arts industry)

    July 2008 — Present (1 year 9 months)

    As Director of Marketing, I have responsibility for the development and execution of a comprehensive strategy for the wide-reaching Ailey organization. In addition to attracting tens of thousands of ticket buyers to Ailey’s self-produced seasons at New York’s City Center and Brooklyn Academy of Music, Atlanta’s Fox Theater and Miami’s Arsht Center for the Performing Arts, I oversee the marketing for extensive U.S. and international tours by both the main Ailey company as well as the Ailey II company. I also supervise promotional initiatives for The Ailey School, The Ailey Extension, AileyCamps and Ailey’s Arts In Education programs.

  • Director of Marketing & Special Programming

    New York City Opera

    (Non-Profit; Performing Arts industry)

    2007 — 2008 (1 year )

    First as a part-time consultant and then as a full-time Director of Marketing and Special Programming, I rebuilt and expanded the marketing department staff, supervised the 2007-08 season's sales campaign and played an active role in overseeing press and public relations. Simultaneously, I began the extensive process of re-imagining sales and communications for City Opera in anticipation of the incoming General Director, Gerard Mortier. During my tenure, I oversaw the creation of a new visual identity and complete overhaul of the company's website and audience development initiatives to relaunch the company under Mr. Mortier's planned first season in 2009-10 -- although he ultimately withdrew from his position before his first season began. In addition, for the transitional season of 2008-09, I was part of a producing team that devised a year-long series of public events at venues throughout New York's five boroughs -- while City Opera's home at the New York State Theater was being renovated.

  • Artistic Director

    Musical Theater Works

    (Non-Profit; 1-10 employees; Performing Arts industry)

    2002 — 2004 (2 years )

    As Artistic Director of New York’s only not-for-profit theater devoted exclusively to the development of new musicals, I re-activated MTW’s dormant works-in-progress series and established new producing alliances with other theaters. I also contributed to MTW’s marketing, fundraising and education efforts and the refurbishment of its public spaces. A dozen new shows blossomed under my leadership, including five projects I commissioned. Although MTW’s board suspended operations in July 2004 due to a lack of sufficient funding, 11 of the new musicals I developed have continued on, having been picked up by other not-for-profit and commercial producers across America.

  • Director of Marketing & Special Projects

    Lincoln Center Theater

    (Non-Profit; 51-200 employees; Performing Arts industry)

    1985 — 2002 (17 years )

    As a founding staffer, I helped establish this leading not-for-profit theater and worked on dozens of shows over 18 seasons, enjoying a wide range of responsibilities. As Director of Marketing, I created the department and oversaw a strategic plan which generated total revenue of over $300 million. A key triumph was LCT’s Membership program, which enrolled a core audience of about 50,000 people and sustained a 95% renewal rate, making it a model for other arts groups around the world seeking a viable alternative to traditional subscription. Additionally, I worked closely with LCT's Board, senior managers and staff on fundraising, long-range planning, capital improvements and financial management. As Director of Special Projects, I scouted plays, nurtured new musicals and produced a series of readings/workshops. I also produced LCT’s annual Galas, coordinated 14 cast recordings, and created education programs, including a meet-the-artist ‘Platform’ series I moderated.
