Aaron S. Robertson’s Summary

I am a 2007 graduate of Cardinal Stritch University in Milwaukee, where I earned a major in Political Science, minors in Sociology and Philosophy, a certificate in Integrated Leadership, and a non-credit certificate for a course in entrepreneurship. During my time at Stritch, among other accomplishments, I served as president of the student government, Model United Nations Club, and Philosophy Club, which I co-founded.

I am author of the book, Beyond Majors and G.P.A.: A Real Philosophy for College and the World Ahead, which I wrote while still in college; am a correspondent for the Muskego Chronicle since October 2007, reporting primarily on city government news; and contribute articles on entrepreneurship, business, leadership, history, politics/society, and other topics as a freelance writer.

I am co-founder and president of Intrepid Innovations Inc., a firm specializing in Web design, graphic design, Internet marketing, and consulting. We also operate the Intrepid Networking Club for college students, recent graduates, and young professionals. View sample copies of the Intrepid Networking Club's monthly e-newsletter on my Scribd profile at: http://www.scribd.com/intrepidaaron .

Additionally, I have been a member of the Zoning Board of Appeals for the City of Muskego since May 2008 and the Census Committee for the Village of Hales Corners since June 2009.

I'm a generalist (as opposed to a specialist) who refuses to have a one-track career that may pay well, but leaves me with feelings of deep remorse and emptiness because I decided to waste my many diverse talents, interests, and skill sets.
