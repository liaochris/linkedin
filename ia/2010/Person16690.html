Dirk Lester’s Summary

FADE IN:

Thunder, a sporadic bursting gale and the blood-burnt twilight, washed a young man now known as Dirk Lester ashore, at Rio de Janeiro, nearly a decade ago—two days into a particularly debauched Carnival, with two 7.62 millimeter slugs and a curare-tipped dart in his back, ten-thousand francs and a flask of single malt scotch in his hip pocket and of course little or no memory of a past life. His love of alternative media forms began right then and there—when, as fate would have it, he was forced to dispatch one of several would-be assassins by garroting him with an illusive copy of Deadly Hands of Kung Fu # 57, then hide out for several long months in a bedraggled movie palace run by a formally black-listed mad man ad man—who carefully coaxed the young blank slate back to full health while holding him hostage to screenings of nothing but obscure Look Magazine campaigns, radio drama sponsorship adverts, and gang mural inspired banners-n-billboards twenty-four hours a day . . .

TO:

Following his daring escape from that mad, though well-intentioned, ad man’s clutches, Dirk gradually wandered northward, reeducating himself first at Georgetown University’s Georgetown College and Columbia University's School of the Arts, then at an eclectic assortment of occupations ranging form the sublime …Greenpeace Organizer, Child Welfare Advocate, Newspaper Reporter … to the patently ridiculous … Personnel Bodyguard, International “Currier,” Corporate "Spy” inside Ken Starr’s infamous Kirkland and Ellis to the pretty darn enjoyable ... Comic Book Writer, Entertainment Industry Futurist, founding host of the MovieWeb.com column "The Daily Spin," and co-author of the 2006 book Science Fiction America. Today, he walks the Earth sharing the Zen of New Media Marketing and Communications with entrepreneurs from Chicago to Shanghai.
