Max J. Pucher’s Summary

Max j. Pucher has worked for 15 years at IBM in Austria, UK and Saudi Arabia as engineer, consultant and sales. In 1988 he founded what is today the ISIS Papyrus Group, a medium size, but global software business with Fortune 1000 clients. Max Pucher is an expert in machine learning and pattern recognition with related patents and has applied that knowledge in the areas of content and process management. He draws from a rich background in psychology, philosophy and quantum physics.

Max believes that business entities, content and process need to be accessed in context. Old-Style BPM is a straightjacket for any business. He promotes an Adaptive Process concept, meaning technology that exposes structured (business data) and unstructured (content) information to the members of structured (business) and unstructured (social) organizations to securely execute – with knowledge interactively gathered previously – structured (process) and unstructured (case) work in a transparent and auditable manner.

Innovations:
- 'electronic original' for formatted outbound documents (1988)
- PrintPool, a spooling database for 'electronic originals' (1994)
- Papyrus Platform architecture distributed OO TX engine (1997)
- patents for User-Trained Agent, process machine learning (2007)
- Adaptive Process concept for user iterative processes (2008)
