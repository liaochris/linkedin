  • Web Media Producer

    Inman News

    (Newspapers industry)

    April 2006 — February 2009 (2 years 11 months)

    When I joined Inman News in April 2006, I brought along my package of traditional, PC-standard programmer analyst skills. I had been developing Web sites using .ASP, and homegrown applications based on Visual Basic and SQL. Early on, Inman's marketing director decided to harvest some of my graphic skills. There were flash magazines, standalone flash files, and videos to be edited and produced.

    By the time I left, in February 2009, I had developed a full array of skills in the open source sector. As part of the home team, in March 2008 we launched a Drupal web site. It was sitting on LAMPP, developed by an amazing group at Toolhouse.com. While continuing with the daily business of producing an online newspaper, we quickly came up to speed on the new technologies, and the site was turned over to us in May 2008.

    It was indeed a wild ride. Thank You, Inman News, for all of the fun.

  • Media Manager

    Elmwood Real Estate Investment

    (Real Estate industry)

    September 2003 — October 2005 (2 years 2 months)

    My career move from a major lender of residential home loans to a local real estate broker's office could be seen as very logical. Even though there were no major technology initiatives to fall into, the business experience I gained as ERI's Media Manager is invaluable.

    Web site administration came effortlessly. My development of agent farms, mail campaigns, newspaper advertising and online advertising, however, was new and exciting. I particularly loved helping new agents develop profiles. There is a special skill to taking pieces of a person's life and fitting them into a public portrait that creates appeal and trust.

    My design skills were successfully accessed for both print and digital content. And, learning a few new applications was a snap. Getting to the insider options of an MLS, and developing expert level skills in Photoshop and Quark enabled me to create highly professional mailings and collateral for the brokerage. Learning, as I did, the business of real estate while in the trenches is invaluable, regardless of occupation.

  • Programmer Analyst - Webmaster

    World Savings

    (Public Company; GDW; Banking industry)

    October 1994 — January 2003 (8 years 4 months)

    From October 1994 to January 2003 I was employed at World Savings Bank. My duties ranged from Business Analyst, to Systems Analyst to my last role there as a Programmer Analyst. From 1999 to 2003 my duties focused on programming and designing web sites and VB applications for internal business users.

    My career at World Savings came to an end as I chose to remain in the San Francisco Bay Area when my department was relocated to San Antonio, Texas. World Savings Bank was sold in the Spring of 2006, and became Wachovia Bank. And, following the economic crisis in the Fall of 2009, Wachovia Bank became part of Wells Fargo Bank. Thus goes the way of one of this country's most successful billion dollar 'Ma and Pa pop-stands'.

    For the time I spent at World Savings, however, my life was enriched ten-fold. The level of professionalism was superior throughout the organization. Talent was recognized, valued and rewarded. My colleagues, and our managers were top-shelf and dedication came effortlessly. The solid business principles that I learned while there continue to inform my perspective.
