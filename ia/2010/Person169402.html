Ravi Bhatia [PE, PMP, MBA]’s Summary

Ravi has over 20 years of project leadership experience on high-visibility projects for Fortune 500 clients, multinational corporations and large public institutions. He is spearheading the development of boutique residential and commercial high-rise projects in Manhattan. This turn-key effort entails site acquisition, due diligence, financing, design, engineering, construction and disposition. He has significant experience in turning around troubled projects.

Key projects in the past have included the U.S.' first wireless mobile high-speed Internet access network deployment (leading a $100M build-out in the NYC Metro Area); transit signaling and software projects in Toronto ($20M) and Philadelphia ($70M); the retrofit, upgrade and sale of AT&T’s corporate headquarters ($40M); Federal Courthouse at Foley Square ($130M); Giorgio Armani ($14M); Marsh ($40M); the Sheraton New York Hotel restoration ($95M); the Upper East Side Marriott Courtyard hotel ($65M); and luxury condominium buildings and hotels in Manhattan.

Ravi has held leadership positions in real estate development, construction management, general construction, subcontracting, telecommunications deployment and technology/systems integration. He has a B.S. in Mechanical Engineering (Polytechnic University), a MBA (Hofstra University) and a M.S. in Telecommunications and Information Management (Polytechnic University).

Ravi is a registered P.E. (Professional Engineer, N.Y. State) and a certified PMP (Project Management Professional). He completed General Electric’s prestigious sales engineering program (Technical Marketing Program) in 1985 and attended Alstom’s high-potential, corporate Leadership in Action program in 2003 (in Paris, France). He serves as Adjunct Assistant Professor at New York University’s School of Continuing Education (Real Estate Institute). He is a level-two candidate in the CFA program.
