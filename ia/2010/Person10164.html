Amy Johns’s Summary

Amy Johns is a senior business and brand strategist. She is passionate about launching and building high-performing startups as well as helping established businesses to innovate or accelerate growth. In her 15 years leading strategy, she has been responsible for or directly involved with several media startups and has consulted to a range of clients, from startups to Fortune 500 corporations.

Amy is a creative problem-solver and ideas packager with expertise in developing innovative solutions that meet business goals and customer needs. She has deep experience in marketing planning, mission and values development, ideation processes, product launches, branding and communications. She thrives on building and leading top-notch teams.

As principal of Johns Strategy + Consulting, Amy builds strategies for social and environmental innovation. Recently she led a marketing launch of Fulcrum BioEnergy and rebranded green energy software firm eMeter, resulting in sales growth and a major partnership. She also designed mission and values as well as a leadership communications program for CEO Jerry Yang and senior management at Yahoo!

Prior to this, Amy worked as a strategist and writer with Stone Yamashita Partners, a boutique management consulting firm in San Francisco. Her accomplishments include repositioning Marsh as a $5-billion risk consulting leader, and producing HP’s brand-identity system after its merger with Compaq.

Amy came to consulting from a 10-year career in the media and technology industry, where she helped launch and build several high-profile media properties. In 2000, as assistant managing editor of Time Inc.'s Business 2.0, she provided leadership in the launch of the magazine and website, which tripled its growth goals in three years. Prior to Business 2.0, Amy was a senior editor at Wired, an award-winning magazine that grew 1,500 percent in circulation and was acquired by Condé Nast for $80 million.
