Erin Schrode’s Summary

Erin Schrode is a young EcoRenaissance woman. As the “face of the new green generation,” the spokeswoman and founding member of the national Teens Turning Green campaign shares her knowledge of this world on television and the radio, in numerous books, newspapers, magazines, blogs, and websites. Erin was honored with the 2009 Diller Teen Tikkun Olam Award for outstanding leadership in community service and commitment to improving the world, named one of JVibe’s 18 Under 18 extraordinary teenagers, selected as a recipient of the Natural Products Innovator Award, and invited to represent the United States as a delegate at the inaugural One Young World Summit in London this February. This past fall, she began her studies in International Affairs and Cross-Cultural Communications as a Presidential Honors Scholar at New York University.

Goals: A career in international affairs, cross-cultural communications and global public service

    Teens Turning Green

    (Non-Profit; Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    January 2005 — Present (5 years 6 months)

    Teens Turning Green is a national movement of young people transforming the world by working to identify and eliminate toxic exposures that threaten public and environmental health. The movement focuses on exposures in daily life, school campuses, and local communities. By educating peers and communities about globally sustainable choices and advocating for policy change, these young leaders inspire us all to work toward a thriving future.

    PROGRAMS:
    TTG: Lifestyles. Creates awareness around toxic exposures and sustainable practices through education, outreach and advocacy.
    TTG: Schools. Investigates toxic exposures in schools and takes action to eliminate those and implement healthy, greener alternatives.
    TTG: Community. Launches specific initiatives in partnership with community leadership and residents to care for communities.
