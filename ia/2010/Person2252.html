  • Instructor

    University of Florida

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    December 2007 — May 2010 (2 years 6 months)

    From the Spring of 2007 until the Spring of 2010, I was Head Teacher's Assistant for Introduction to Business Finance (FIN 3403) under Professor T. Craig Tapley.

    In this role, I led a team of 42 TAs in creating and grading quizes, exams and assigments for over 900 students per semester as well as instructing groups of 25+ students on concepts such as WACC, bond and stock valuation, and CAPM.

    In the Spring of 2010, I developed the curriculum for and taught Digital Frontiers (IDH 3931), the first class at UF which discussed how the Internet and globalization are affecting the business world. The course was built around a series of guest lectures by speakers from local venture-backed startups and case studies developed by the students in groups.

    As a final project, working in groups of 4, the class developed Internet marketing plans for 7 local businesses - ranging from non-profits to restaurants to educational programs. Two of the groups were featured on the regional news for greatly raising the visibility of their client in the course of the project and three of the students were hired into roles at major firms developing social media strategy based on their experience.

  • Co-Founder

    UF Children's Hope India

    (Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    January 2008 — January 2010 (2 years 1 month)

    UF Children’s Hope India is a social business based in Delhi, India which works to alleviate poverty through a three pronged approach concentrating on impoverished women.

    First, we provide these women with training and education to help them develop a skillset such as bag weaving or toy manufacturing. Then we hire these women to manufacture products for us - which we sell to businesses in both the US and India. Finally, once they have a skill and a steady source of income - we enable these women to take out a microloan - which they can use to start their own businesses or support their family.

    In my role, I worked to manage the US-based team and develop partnerships with major corporations such as Coca-Cola.

    Awards:
    Projects for Peace Grant (2009)
    Clinton Global Initiative (2009, 2010)

  • Founder

    Perfect Wave Records

    (Music industry)

    2005 — 2008 (3 years )

    Perfect Wave Records was a full-service band management and concert production company based in Miami, Florida.

    We launched the business producing small concerts in Fort Lauderdale, Florida and grew the business to be one of the largest independent music production businesses in the state hosting over 150 concert events across 5 cities in Florida with attendance of 1000+.

    In addition, we were one of the first donation-based record labels - maintaining a roster of 5 Florida-based bands - using the increased promotion via free music to generate additional income via merchandise and extended touring.

    In 2007, we spearheaded the effort to release the first annual "Florida Ska Comp" showcasing the best ska and reggae bands across Florida with over 2000 copies sold and increasing visibility for the Florida music scene.
