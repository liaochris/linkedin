Cortney Murphy’s Experience

  • President

    SnagA Inc.

    (Sports industry)

    March 2008 — Present (2 years )

    President of an internet start-up company called SportsLinkster.com. Following almost a year and a half of development, the site launched in December of 2009.

    Site Overview: SportsLinkster is an adventure sports community that fosters friendships and provides a platform to create groups, plan events, find rides, upload videos, post competitions and share information.

    Our site launch campaign is currently underway with a goal of 10,000 users by next ski season. May you never have to ski or ride alone!

  • Golf Professional

    The Country Club at Castle Pines

    (Sports industry)

    January 2001 — September 2007 (6 years 9 months)

    Primary position: Assistant Golf Professional

    Served as the Interim Head Golf Professional from October 2004 through May 2005 during a period of ownership changes and lawsuits until the club was finally purchased by the membership. Responsibilities included oversight of all Golf Shop Operations including hiring, training, scheduling and delegating to a staff of 25+ seasonal and full time employees, including the golf shop staff, outside services, and a team of starters and rangers. Golf Operations budget development and execution of action plans and coordination of over 30 annual member tournaments, 8 charity events and over 60 weekly outings a season. Merchandise responsibilities included OTB development and monitoring receiving procedures and invoice coding.

    November 1998 — October 2000 (2 years )

    Established and managed Government and Education Accounts, providing services such as setting up terms, configuring and submitting bids for governmental purchases, and providing expert product and network consultation.

  • Intern

    The United State Golf Association

    (Sports industry)

    May 1998 — November 1998 (7 months)

Tournament administration preparation for the US Girls Junior, the US Women’s Amateur, the US Women’s Mid-Amateur, the Curtis Cup Team and local Section Qualifiers under the guidance of the Manager of the Western Regional Affairs.
