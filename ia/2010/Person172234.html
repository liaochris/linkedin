Richard Gilliland’s Summary

As a professional network engineer and project manager, I have designed, installed, and maintained small, medium, and large LANs and WANs for over fifteen years. My experience includes both Novell NetWare (3.11-6.x) and Microsoft Windows NT (3.1-Server 2008) in heterogeneous and homogeneous environments. I am currently a Microsoft Certified Systems Engineer (MCSE), a Microsoft Certified Professional + Internet (MCP+I), a candidate for Microsoft Certified Information Technology Professional – Enterprise Administrator (MCITP-EA), a Cisco Certified Network Associate (CCNA),a Citrix Certified Trainer (CCT), a Certified Novell Administrator, a certified expert in WAN technologies, and a certified project manager.

I have extensive experience as a systems integrator, from concept to cutover and am an application integration specialist. I have successfully managed numerous projects and have participated in technical writing projects and the production of winning proposals for both government and commercial contracts totaling in the billions of dollars. My career goal is to advance in the networking and distributed computing field.

The plan included: (1) a recommended network design, including estimated costs, for each site to be added; (2) its impact on the existing network, (3) Operations and Maintenance (O&M) support requirements, and (4) a proposed (prioritized) schedule for providing the new connectivity utilizing the FTS2001 network. The plan also included the list of required equipment, cabling and other interface equipment to complete the rollout of these sites over a timeframe not to exceed six (6) months. The plan provided the necessary actions required to implement the new network connectivity down to the site level. It included specific action items in sufficient detail to define both the sequence of change and the persons or organizations responsible for performing the work.
