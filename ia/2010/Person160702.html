Paul Dix’s Summary

For the last twelve years I have worked in IT filling positions as a software developer, tester, network engineer, and even as a PC technician. Since 2001 I have worked primarily on web applications. Initially this work was in C# backed by MS SQL Server. Since late 2005 my primary focus has been Ruby on Rails and the Linux server stack. In the fall of 2005 I went back to school to finish up a degree in computer science at Columbia University. During my time there I have pursued interests in machine learning, information retrieval, natural language processing and search. While in school I have also continued my professional growth by getting involved in the New York Ruby community and speaking at three professional conferences. I am excited and passionate about solving hard problems and developing applications that many people use.

I'm also the organizer for the NYC Machine Learning meetup: http://www.meetup.com/NYC-Machine-Learning/.

Languages I have been paid to write code in: Ruby, JavaScript, Java, C#, VB.NET, VBScript, and Delphi (you read that last part correctly).

Additional languages that I have worked with either in school or in my free time: C, C++, Python, Flex/Flash, Perl, and Common Lisp.
