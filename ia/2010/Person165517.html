Prahlad Menon’s Summary

I am a strongly self motivated individual with a passion for the engineering sciences. My key proficiencies are Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD), Heat Transfer and Computational BioModeling. I have a tremendous aptitude to understand, simulate and optimize the functioning of engineering systems and have written papers and completed several projects dealing with product design, CFD simulation and engineering Finite Element Analysis (FEA). My research at Carnegie Mellon University has been related to thermal-fluids engineering based surgical planning of CryoSurgery and Cardiovascular Surgery.

The challenges that I have encountered as a graduate student researcher have helped me gain proficiency in understanding solutions to engineering problems from the analytical and computational standpoint. Graduate project work has taught me valuable lessons in conducting meticulous research and writing technical reports, and a Masters in Mechanical Engineering has given me exposure to many techniques in computational research and made me proficient in developing advanced engineering solvers using C/C++ and MATLAB. Although my specialization is in the thermal-fluid sciences and computational fluid dynamics, being a mechanical engineer with course work and internship experience in product design, manufacturing and R&D, I believe I will be able to utilize my analytical abilities and team skills to function proactively in a challenging work environment across domains of engineering.

My research interests have led me to investigate research problems in fluid mechanics of cardiovascular blood flow, aerodynamics and hydrodynamics. I have also worked on Molecular Dynamics and Monte Carlo Simulations for atomistic modeling of solids and fluids.

Given my strong mathematical proclivity and quantitative skills, I believe I could prove to be a valuable asset to any computational / quantitative research group.
