Ruth Laura Edlund’s Summary

I am passionate about helping people solve their problems through legal means--the written and the spoken word. At the same time, I've been on this earth long enough to recognize that the best solutions for the really tough situations my clients find themselves in may well lie outside of a court of law. That can mean mediation. That can also mean never filing a lawsuit in the first place.

My clients can expect a focused and comprehensive approach to their legal predicaments, whatever they may be. Beyond that, it's hard to generalize, because, as Tolstoy noted, every unhappy family is unhappy in its own way.

Ruth Laura Edlund’s Specialties:

Dissolutions, parentage actions, parenting disputes, meretricious relationships, parenting plan modifications, relocation cases.

Note that the Supreme Court of Washington does not recognize certification of "specialties" in the practice of law. See Washington Rules of Professional Conduct Rule 7.4(d)(3)(2006).

    Family Law Executive Committee, WSBA Family Law Section

    (Public Policy industry)

    September 2010 — Present (5 months)

    The Family Law Executive Committee (FLEC) of the Family Law Section of the Washington State Bar Association is a governing committee elected by the 1,100 members of the Family Law Section. Each year the committee actively reviews, analyzes and responds to proposed legislation that may affect family law, the practice of family law attorneys and the general public. FLEC also sponsors a Skills Training Institute annually for attorneys new to the practice of Family Law, and manages the Mid-Year CLE for the Section.
