Scott Tsuchiyama’s Summary

I work in career services, and the highlight of my job is my interaction with students. I enjoy helping students initiate their self-directed job/internship search, and network with professionals in their field of interest. An especially exciting topic for me is teaching students to use social media tools and other technology to explore their career options and connect with professionals in their field.

I'm also a part-time grad student at UM's School of Information. A growing interest in the fields of web usability and interaction design led me to study Human-Computer Interaction, and I've been able to put those skills to use redesigning The Career Center's website, and performing a variety of usability evaluations both internal and external to UM. In my coursework I also try to take away lessons in design, psychology, and learning theory to inform my work with students at The Career Center.
