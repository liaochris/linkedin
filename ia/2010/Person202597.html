Rebecca Schatz’s Summary

Engineers make a difference. The stuff they create can solve problems, can save lives, can make lives easier, warmer, cooler, healthier,safer, more effective, more worldly, more interesting, more fun.

The Works exists to give every child a taste of engineering, a hands-on experience being an engineer for a while. When a child builds a project at The Works, she learns more than the science behind it, she learns that she, too, can make a motor, a polymer, a skyscraper, a solar car. That child learns that he/she can make a difference.

Multiply that by 40,000+ kids every year and you know why The Works matters. It gladdens my heart to know we're making a difference in the lives of all these kids. But an even greater thrill is thinking about the difference that these young people can make in the world.
