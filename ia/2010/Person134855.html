Mark Welch’s Summary

I'm a "web publisher," creating and maintaining a variety of web sites (mostly directories with original commentary) funded by advertising (including affiliate-program earnings).

My current focus is on "affiliate datafeeds" -- trying to identify the best ways to automate the gathering and display of merchants' product information (advertisements) in-context on relevant content pages.

  • Attorney

    (Privately Held; Marketing and Advertising industry)

    April 1993 — December 2008 (15 years 9 months)

    After starting my solo practice as an Estate Planning & Probate Attorney, I created a web site in late 1995 which quickly became the most popular and successful web site about California Estate Planning, Probate & Trust Law; the site attracted many new clients to my practice.

This led me to create a web site (in early 1996) to warn others about online advertising scams. By 1998, this "hobby" web site brought more revenue than my law practice.

    Meanwhile, in 1997 I accepted my first "internet marketing" consulting client, and in 1998 I stopped accepting new legal clients.

  • Editor & Publisher

    Adbility.com

    (Marketing and Advertising industry)

    March 1996 — November 1999 (3 years 9 months)

    I created & maintained the first comprehensive "directory with opinionated commentary" regarding online advertising.

    In early 1996, after creating a web site to promote my estate planning, probate & trust law practice, I learned of a bizarre scheme that promised to pay for advertising on web sites. I quickly recognized that it was a scam, and posted a page on my web site warning others about the company.

    By mid-1996, this had expanded into a separate web site about online advertising companies, with several sections describing different types of advertising (including "CPM-based banner ad networks and brokers," "pay-per-click" advertising offers and networks, and "affiliate programs" promising to pay a percentage of sales).

    By mid-1999, the site had grown to included my "opinionated commentary" about many hundreds of companies, and the site was drawing more than $12,000 per month in advertising income.

    In 1999, several crooks launched aggressive ad campaigns to try to silence my efforts, and in mid-1999 a frivolous lawsuit was filed against me by the "Spam King," Sanford Wallace (in late 2009, news reports mentioned that Mr. Wallace now owes more than $1 billion in legal damages [court judgments] for his ongoing spamming and "phishing" activities). After spending $5,000 to have the frivolous lawsuit thrown out, and after suffering considerable damage to my personal and professional reputation, I was demoralized and I finally decided to sell this web site and focus my efforts on my consulting and law practices.

    In November 1999, I sold the Adbility.com web site through an eBay auction for $175,000.
