Steve Walker’s Summary

Broadcast studio construction and maintenance, as well as RF construction and transmitter maintenance. Also heavy on IT project development and integration of interactive technologies with traditional broadcast Radio. Particularly interested in finding ways in which Radio stations can create non-traditional revenue (NTR) sources through the use of existing and upcoming technologies.

I would like to become more involved in the NTR aspect of broadcasting, both Radio and TV. Broadcast media have enormous advantages over cable and online media, including all things iPod, but few know how to use their advantages. The secret is in the long tail...

Ultimately I want to marry my two favorite things (not counting family, of course): Broadcasting and Publishing. That is why I formed Bottom-Up Media, LLC. I have a plan that will allow Radio and TV stations to actually make money with publishing. Many have tried, most have failed. But I know how to make it generate revenue. I'm always seeking investors to help me move Bottom-Up Media forward.

I also support the Boy Scouts of America. I am a registered adult leader with my son's Troop, and have been involved with Cub Scouts and Boy Scouts for nearly ten years. This is the best organization for boys there is. The program is nearly perfect in design, if not always administered perfectly. We would all do well to live by the Scout Oath and Law.

Steve Walker’s Specialties:

Integrating online technologies with traditional media outlet online presence. Creatively solving personnel, administrative and scheduling problems with IT. Publishing. Writing.

    (Broadcast Media industry)

    January 1992 — July 1994 (2 years 7 months)

    Worked as assitant chief engineer of "the little ticket" helping out with computers (there were only a few), studio and transmitter maintenance and remotes (lots and lots of remotes) and eventually took over as Chief Engineer when "the other half of the Walker Brothers," Tim Walker (no relation) left to go start his own Radio station.

    (Broadcast Media industry)
