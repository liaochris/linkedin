Ginger Korljan, M.Ed., NCRW, CCMC’s Summary

Career coach with more than 15 years' experience helping people choose and land satisfying jobs. Master of Counselor Education, Nationally Certified Résumé Writer, Certified Career Management Coach. Member of National Résumé Writer's Association, Career Directors International, and Résumé Writer's Council of Arizona.

Ginger Korljan, M.Ed., NCRW, CCMC’s Specialties:

Career Coaching, Résumé Writing, Interview Preparation, Life-Work Balance

Ginger Korljan, M.Ed., NCRW, CCMC’s Experience

  • Career Consultant / Résumé Writer

    IMPACT Group

    (Privately Held; Human Resources industry)

    February 2008 — Present (2 years 3 months)

    Coach outplaced individuals in fields ranging from blue collar to middle management in career assessment, résumé development, job search techniques and improving motivation during major life changes. Instruct clients in proper use of company website to effectively search for positions, participate in job search workshops, research companies and utilize job search resources. Write and format résumés tailored to clients’ targeted positions.

  • Résumé Writer/Career Coach

    Take Charge Coaching

    (Human Resources industry)

    January 2008 — Present (2 years 4 months)

    Help job seekers and career changers target specific career goals and write marketing materials to promote their candidacy. Each person has unique interests, skills, and abilities which can be of great value to an employer, but which need to be effectively communicated in order to obtain interviews and land positions. My company is licensed and bonded by the Industrial Commission of Arizona.

  • Résumé Workshop Presenter/Coach

    Covenant Theological Seminary

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    1994 — 2004 (10 years )

    Conducted two-hour résumé writing workshops for 30 to 60 seminary students annually. Critiqued individual résumés, giving feedback until resumes met seminary standards. Participating students had 95% placement rate upon graduation. Demonstrated methods to apply transferable skills obtained from secular professions and unpaid ministry opportunities to pastoral or counseling positions. Assisted students from the United States, South Korea, China, Africa and South America.

  • Career Counselor/Resume Writer

    Career Directions/Resume Writing

    (Professional Training & Coaching industry)

    1997 — 1999 (2 years )

    Worked in conjunction with Sharon Cox to provide career counseling and resume writing services to individuals.

  • Career/Relocation Consultant

    Impact Group

    (Professional Training & Coaching industry)

    1990 — 1993 (3 years )

    Managed caseload of 300 relocated spouses per year. Assisted with family and career issues, relocation concerns, job search skills, company research, résumés, cover letters, and interviewing skills. During a period of rapid company growth, hired and trained ten new consultants. Revised job search manual to focus on unique challenges and techniques in conducting a successful long-distance job search.

  • Career Counseling Intern

    Virginia Commonwealth University

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    May 1988 — August 1988 (4 months)

    Provided individual and group counseling to students and alumni. Administered and interpreted assessment instruments, including Self-Directed Search, Strong-Campbell Interest Inventory, and Myers-Briggs. Role-played and evaluated videotaped mock interviews. Planned and implemented eight-week job search support group.

  • Captain, Intelligence Analyst

    US Army Foreign Science and Technology Center, Charlottesville Virginia

    (Military industry)

    1983 — 1987 (4 years )

    Conducted research on soldier performance issues. Analyzed information and wrote intelligence study entitled Human Factors of Military Systems. Wrote, narrated and produced videotape entitled The Making of a Soviet Soldier, used to educate soldiers in East Berlin. Presented security briefings to new employees, alerting them to potential risks and their important role in preserving security.
