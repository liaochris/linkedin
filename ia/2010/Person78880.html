Guray Tas, Ph.D.’s Summary

tasg@optonline.net

Director of Engineering with fourteen years of experience, nine years in leadership and five years in scientist roles in thin film characterization and development of optical metrology systems

Adjunct Professor of Physics at Brown University in Providence Rhode Island since May 2005.

Received a Ph.D. in Experimental Condensed Matter Physics from Brown University and completed two Post-doctoral Research assignments at University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign and Brown University.

Delivered invited lectures (presentations) at universities, semiconductor manufacturer and R&D organizations around the world as well as international conferences.

Published so far 10 Refereed Journal Publications, 10 Conference Proceedings, 8 Trade journal articles, 6 Conference presentations.

Received one US and WIPO patent, filed three additional applications related to ultra-thin film characterization and optical metrology product development for semiconductor industry.

Inducted into 2005 New Jersey High Tech Hall of Fame (Research Category).

Sample Publications:

Characterization of Copper Line Array Erosion with Picosecond Ultrasonics, Proceedings of the 2007 International Conference on Frontiers of Characterization and Metrology, Volume 931, pp. 357-361 (2007).

Characterizing CMP Processes with Picosecond Ultrasonic Metrology, Proceedings of the ECS International Semiconductor Technology Conference (ISTC), March 16, 2007.

Measuring the Young’s modulus of ultralow-k materials with the nondestructive picosecond ultrasonic method, MICROELECTRONIC ENGINEERING, 83 (11-12): 2346-2350 NOV-DEC 2006.

Applications for Picosecond Ultrasonics for Inline DRAM Process Control, Proceedings of the Advanced Metallization Conference, September, 2005.

Picosecond Ultrasonics Provides CoSi Characterization, Semiconductor International, February 2003.

Opto-electronic Thin Film Characterization, Semiconductor International, November 2001.

Guray Tas, Ph.D.’s Specialties:

Solid state physics and physical chemistry, ultrafast laser spectroscopy, optical metrology and inspection product development, characterization of ultra-thin films and nanostructures.

Guray Tas, Ph.D.’s Experience

  • Director of Engineering

    KLD Labs, Inc.

    (Transportation/Trucking/Railroad industry)

    January 2008 — Present (2 years 10 months)

    KLD Labs, Inc. is the leading manufacturer of laser based high precision inspection and measurement systems for railways, transit and light rails. Core products are the ORIAN (Optical Rail Inspection & ANalysis) system for real-time rail profile inspection and measurements and the WheelScan system for real-time wheel profile inspection and measurements.

    Currently, I lead KLD’s engineering team of 15 engineers and scientists. Responsibilities include directing search and evaluation of new metrology and inspection technology and concepts, new product development, continuous improvements of current products, support of Sales, Marketing, Manufacturing and Service departments.

  • Sr. Manager / Director of Opaque Films Metrology Development

    Rudolph Technologies, Inc.

    (Public Company; 501-1000 employees; RTEC; Semiconductors industry)

    March 2002 — April 2006 (4 years 2 months)

    - Managed Opaque Films Metrology Group consisting of 7 Systems, Applications and Numerical Algorithms Development Scientists working on new product development projects, field escalations and wafer measurements and tool demonstrations.
    - Reviewed, prioritized and managed customer requests, field escalations related to metal film metrology product family on a daily basis.
    - Led proof of concept studies based on ellipsometry, UV-Visible reflectometry and ultrafast pump–probe method (picosecond ultrasonics) to develop new metrology products for ultrathin films used in advanced silicon technology nodes.
    - Supervised development of Low-K dielectric film modulus measurement capability using picosecond ultrasonics, concluded with an internationally approved patent.
    - Finalized Cu CMP metrology product release by completing and deploying various product improvements enabling the system to measure 30x30 mic test sites on Cu/Low-K product wafers.
