Patrick Iwanicki’s Summary

Patrick Iwanicki conceived Connect Work Chicago — originally Out of Work Chicago — when he grew frustrated with his own job search after being laid off in the Winter of 2008. Job fairs were pointless as hundreds of applicants shuffled, zombie-like, though convention centers and gymnasia. He realized that tossing your resume into Lake Michigan and hoping it washed ashore into the hands of the right employer was as likely as securing employment through the online application process. Connect Work Chicago is the product of his dissatisfaction, research, development and hard work.
Formerly a IT project manager at Thomson Reuters, Patrick has an avid interest in technology and how it may be used to achieve business goals. As sole developer of the Connect Work Chicago website, Patrick appreciates the power of the web and social media. (So it should be noted when a tech guy advises career-minded people to get out from behind their computers and make face-to-face connections with other professionals.)
While away from his computer, he enjoys playing golf, tennis and hitting the slopes. Patrick plans to gain admission to a top-tier business school, shoot under 80 on the golf course and amass a collection of great ski stories.

“Your work is to discover your work and then with all your heart to give yourself to it.” – Buddha
