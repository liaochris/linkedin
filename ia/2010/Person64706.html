Emily Stine’s Summary

  • Principal Investigator, Virtual Ethnographer, Senior McNair Scholar

    CU Boulder

    (Educational Institution; Myself Only; Research industry)

    October 2006 — May 2008 (1 year 8 months)

    I have been doing research on Facebook, an online social networking site popular with college age students. This research has been a two part project, including collecting online surveys from fifty CU students this summer as well as more complex in-depth interviews this fall. Preliminary analysis suggests a high importance placed on photographs as currency of authenticating real life identity, and the stress to separate real and virtual lives. Student's seem to be interacting voyeuristically on the site, passively monitoring their "friends" without providing public comments. Students say that the primary reason they use Facebook is "to connect with a wide social network" but my research suggests that there is a narcissistic underlying appeal of Facebook that provides students the ability to see their self-made identity online and edit and augment this identity however they wish.
