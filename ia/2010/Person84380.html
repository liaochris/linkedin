Ian Valentine’s Summary

I have a serial compulsion to start businesses which change the status quo, and add new ways of thinking and behaviour into industries, becoming flagship leaders in their field.

Industries that I have influenced have spanned CRM/Call Centers (Graham Technology), Interactive TV (waptv/BSkyB), General Aviation (FreeFlight Aviation) and now broadband delivered entertainment (Miniweb).

Building complex solutions is never hard, when end result can be visualised as a whole before the first brick is placed in the wall.

Exceptional communicator and public speaker, I have been a regular conference speaker and panelist for the last 14 years.

I thrive on problems that require strategic thinking, risk evaluation, and innovative never-been-done-before solutions.

Technology is not always the solution to industry problems, sometimes an industry needs to change the way it behaves.

Aviation and Technology have always been passions, as well as reaching out and helping individuals with generosity and genuine care.

  • Founder, CEO

    Miniweb Technologies Ltd

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Computer Software industry)

    August 2006 — September 2008 (2 years 2 months)

    Miniweb Interactive operates an aggregated interactive services portal for narrowband and broadband interactive TV devices, and Miniweb Research develops standards and technology to enable broadcasters and content owners to deploy services across multiple TV distribution networks unchanged.

    The Miniweb Group was established in August 2006, as a "spin-off" from BSkyB.

    It addresses the need for CE device manufacturers, traditional cable TV networks and emerging IPTV networks to share interactive TV content.

    By delivering interoperability of the interactive experience across multiple device types, Miniweb enables media rich web sites to deploy internet video to non-PC devices uniformly.

    Miniweb is now actively working with network operators, CE device manufacturers, internet site owners and broadcasters to enable new revenue streams derived from connecting TV audiences to interactive services on non-PC devices.

  • Product Manager

    Graham Technology

    (Internet industry)

    1992 — 2000 (8 years )

    Ian Valentine had the original idea for a "Business Process Server" - a software infrastructure designed to manage realtime business processes and telephone calls in a call center environment.
    Based on a layered architecture it allowed a thin desktop client to prompt a call center agent through a telephone based business process easily and quickly. Hidden were all the "back end" database updates and legacy systems calls, simplifying training and business process change.
    Business processes and even screen designs were all data driven, and manages by a spohisticated set of busienss process management tools.
    Being the first in class it rocketed Graham Technology from a 22 man Scottish Systems integrator, to a global employer of over 200 people, with clients that included some of the largest call center projects in the world (for example 5000 agents across 6 centers at BT)
    Ian championed the product becoming "multi channel" which lead him into the web and interactive TV industries.
