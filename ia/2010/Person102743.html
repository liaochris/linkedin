John Nawn’s Summary

The Perfect Meeting, like most things in life, is more about the journey than the destination.

Meetings, regardless of size, represent opportunities for growth and change – both individually as well as organizationally. The Perfect Meeting is about capitalizing on those opportunities inherent in everyone and every meeting.

While meetings are a business necessity, they are universally loathed and considered by many experts to be one of the greatest barriers to individual and organizational productivity.

“If you had to identify, in one word, the reason why the human race has not achieved, and never will achieve, its full potential, that word would be “meetings,” notes humorist Dave Barry.

The Perfect Meeting is focused on designing meetings that help realize their full potential, as vehicles for efficiency and effectiveness. The Perfect Meeting is focused on designing 'Meetings that Matter'.

I am a learning and performance professional. For most of my career, I have been developing strategies for organizations that focus on growing revenues or increasing market share, using learning for a competitive advantage.

I believe meetings (of all sizes) represent one of the greatest untapped opportunities for performance improvement in organizations today.

Mission:
• Providing the information people need to know
• When they need to know it
• In the manner which is most convenient for them

Quote: “In the Knowledge Economy, only the smart survive.”
