Carl L. Wilkins’s Summary

Carl has over 30 years experience working in the electric utility industry in a variety of managerial, research and consultative roles. Combining experience in engineering, sales, marketing and project management with applied technical skills, he brings a wide array of talents. He is involved in the development of services in areas of renewable portfolio standards, renewable business case development, carbon strategies, renewable engineering and design, system planning and analysis and EPC services. Carl was directly involved in the coordination and development of North Carolina's green power program, NC GreenPower, and renewable energy resources. Currently, Wilkins is developing new strategies for implementing renewable energy, planning and energy efficiency programs for utilities. He has worked with utilities in areas of smart utility grid, plug-in hybrid electric vehicles and policies to address climate change and environmental sustainability.

Carl L. Wilkins’s Specialties:

Renewable energy and energy efficiency policy, facilitaiton of collaborative stakeholder process, business development, and executive strategy development.

Carl L. Wilkins’s Experience

  • Director - Sustainability & Energy Efficiency

    Quanta Technology

    (Public Company; PWR; Utilities industry)

    September 2008 — Present (2 years 4 months)

    Quanta Technology is a premier provider of planning and operations studies, technology evaluation, engineering standards and maintenance support, and expert advice to the electric power industry. Carl is responsible for developing business and providing management, business and technical consultative services to utility renewable energy and energy efficiency sectors. In 2010 Carl was appointed to the Governor's Scientific Advisory Panel on Offshore Energy in North Carolina. In 2009 Carl prepared transmission interconnection application and to provide engineering analysis to a solar PV plant developer for a power purchase agreement. Carl provided management and technical consultation and facilitation between developer and the interconnecting utility. Southern California utility submitted a Renewable Integration and Advancement (RIA) Program to CPUC for approval resulting in several iterations of questions and responses between utility and pulic advocate. Carl helped utility prepare responses to the CPUC. Carl drafted a white paper to address the utility's concerns about integrating up to 33% renewables on the electric system. Carl coordinated four advanced engineering and research projects to investigate the impacts of high penetration levels on the utility grid.

    Responsible for the coordination, development and delivery of consultative engineering, marketing, technical products and services to electric utilities while maintaining business relationships with senior executives. Work closely with mid and senior level utility management in strategic planning and delivery of new products and services. Appeared before NC utilities commission and legislative committees. Working with utilities to develop a smart utility grid, plug-in hybrid electric vehicles and policies to address climate change and environmental sustainability.
