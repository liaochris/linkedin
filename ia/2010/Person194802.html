Steve Bernstein’s Summary

We're building a world class Web Analytics team for MySpace, rockstar by rockstar. We are ALWAYS looking for good people.

We're looking for brilliant research minds with outstanding communication skills.

A brilliant research mind comprises a combination of intense intellectual curiosity, clear-mindedness, and far-above-average creativity. Such a person would possess very strong reasoning skills, usually quantitative, but a trained philosopher might have the magic.

Think about how you think. Do you often find yourself forming and re-forming hypotheses about how things work, what motivates people, whether a business idea is any good, etc., and how you would test these hypotheses? What data would it take? Lacking direct evidence, what would stand in as proxy data to support an approximation? What evidence would be an indicator; what evidence would be necessary, even if insufficient, to gain the truth? Are you resourceful? Can you pursue an idea through to exhaustion without giving up?

Finally, despite the analytical brilliance you can bring to bear, can you step back far enough from the details and weave from threads of disparate data and analysis a cohesive story that explains and predicts consumer behavior? Do you know what it means to start with the general and move to the specific?

Did you get this far? Send me a note...
