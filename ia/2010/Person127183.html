Loek Hopstaken’s Summary

I'm good at significantly improving communication and cooperation skills.

GOALS

My goals: creating more productive, smoother, profitable and cost-efficient organizations, and stimulating working environments.

HOW I MAY HELP YOU

Besides HR, I use disciplines such as organizational design, strategy and communication. My work method:
1. Do a thorough intake with the organization's taste makers.
2. Design a personnel & management development program.
3. Propose the program & if needed, adjustments.
4. Execute this program as trainer, coach and consultant, and/or get assistance from trusted colleagues.
5. Evaluate the progress and the program's results.

WHO I AM

A Dutchman with international experience, who believes in finding all-inclusive solutions for human resource problems.

I live and work both in Vietnam and The Netherlands.

MY NO. 1 REQUIREMENT FOR OPTIMUM COOPERATION

Active two-way communication about everything that matters, to ensure a smooth execution of the program.

FROM PAST TO PRESENT TIME

1977-1983: Personnel & Organization Officer for the NCB Bank (Amsterdam)
1983-1993: Project Manager Privatization Postbank; Project Manager Job Evaluation System Development; Manager Job Evaluation Department; Communication Manager Marketing & PR (NMB Postbank Group); Sr. Quality Manager (ING Bank)
1992-1993 TQM courses at Juran Institute; Lead Assessor (BVQI)
1993-1999: Trainer, Consultant & Coach for Silhouet Management Support
1996-2000: MC & Speaker for Silhouet Business Club
1990-present time: Director Hopstaken Bedrijfsadvies - trainer, coach, consultant
2001: Mediator (Centre for Conflict Resolution)
2003-p.t.: Professor at Dutch business schools (topics: Communication; Organizational Design; HR; PR)
2007-p.t.: Extension of activities to Vietnam (HCMC / Saigon)
2008-p.t.: Lecturing at RMIT, HUI, HUT, Royal Business School, PACE, SPECTRA
2009-p.t.: Director Alliance ITC; Jury Member VTV1 Key To Success tv show

Loek Hopstaken’s Specialties:

Specialist in creating productive harmony.

1. practical management tools for top, senior and junior managers (strategic thinking, delegation, time management, team engineering, organizational design, HR, PR).

2. teaching soft skills (personal communication, presentation skills, meetings, conflict management, negotiations, sales communication, etc.).

3. Personal coaching & training of top managers and entrepreneurs.

4. Mediation on top and senior management levels.

  • Quality Consultant

    ING Bank

    (Public Company; Professional Training & Coaching industry)

    1990 — 1993 (3 years )

    The first assignment was to do a PR job for the Quality Department during the merger of NMB Postbank Group with insurance company 'Nationale Nederlanden'. The best way to handle this was to develop and deliver successful internal quality training programs. My department - which had a direct line to the Board of Directors of NMB Postbank Group - organized seminars, and the yearly (internal) Quality Award event. When the merger was nearly completed, the new Board decided that 'quality management' was no longer an issue. This made me decide to quit, and broaden my horizon.

  • Management Trainee / Interim Manager

    NMB Postbank Group

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; Banking industry)

    1989 — 1990 (1 year )

    From mid 1989 till end 1990 I worked as a management trainee and at times, interim manager, in the Communications Department of the Postbank, when the merging process with NMB Bank was under way. I gained hands-on experiences in activities such as
    - making sponsored magazines (including writing and design);
    - closely working on various projects with Eduard de Vries, the 'guardian' of the Postbank logo (the blue lion; design of a Postbank credit card), and learning about the graphic aspects of corporate design;
    - functioning as the secretary of a project team that developed the NMB Postbank Group corporate logo (and all its uses), participated in the various sponsoring activities (Sail Amsterdam 1990, National Youth Soccer Championship, a Postbank Contest for Young Musicians, etc.)
    - involvement in various corporate PR activities, both internal and external.

  • Head Job Evaluation Dept.

    Postbank

    (Public Company; Professional Training & Coaching industry)

    1986 — 1989 (3 years )

    In my first management job, I had 8 employees: 5 specialists. We adviced on the relative position of a job, which would then result in a position in the salary system. All in all, we were responsible for a professional application of the job evaluation system, which meant that our advice had to be acceptable, workable and affordable. A main activity, which took two years, was the development of a new job evaluation system. When this project was completed and ok'd by all parties concerned, the result was shelved. This happened the day after a negotiation deal on this matter between Postbank and NMB Bank, who were contemplating a merger, was made public. After successfully conducting a research to find out how much this merger would cost in terms of salary for all Postbank and NMB Bank personnel, I decided it was time to move to a different area. When I was offered a management traineeship / interim manager position in the Communications Department, I accepted.

  • Job Evaluation Consultant

    Postgiro RPS

    (Professional Training & Coaching industry)

    1982 — 1985 (3 years )

    During this period, the Postgiro was part of the state-owned PTT. As the 'Postbank Law' was accepted by parliament, the Postgiro would be the first major state-owned enterprise to be privatized, per Jan. 1, 1986. Until that time, I worked in two capacities: 1. as a Job Evaluation Consultant, using the PTT Job Evaluation system, and 2. preparing the new compensation and payment system for the Postbank. We had to make sure that all 10,500 personnel received an acceptable job offer. This project took 6 months, and was finished 'just in time' for the new Postbank.
