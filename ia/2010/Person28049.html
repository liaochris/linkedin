Brian Altounian’s Summary

After spending 12 years in the entertainment industry on the Operations/Admin side of television production, I ventured into the high-tech area with a number of early-stage startup companies. Since 2003, I have been involved with 9 early-stage (pre-revenue) technology ventures that all went public via the OTC Bulletin Board. I sat on the Board of Directors for a few, advised on others and helped bring investors to them all. Early-round investors have had phenomenal returns (over 116 times return on one (XSNX) within 3 years) and I have been able to follow a formula for early-stage, start-up companies to build and grow their businesses by accessing the equity markets without giving up majority control in the process.

Platinum Studios had been the culmination of all of my previous passions. I successfully led a financing round, bringing $5,000,000 in first-round investment and I reorganized the company to expand beyond filmed entertainment, venturing into the broadband delivery of content into online, mobile and other distribution outlets. I successfully architected the going-public process which led to a listing on the OTC Bulletin Board in February, 2008. As President and COO, I had been responsible for leading our execution strategy, guiding our team to success and increasing shareholder value. We completed the production of our own independently-financed and self-produced feature film, DEAD OF NIGHT, due out in theaters in 2010.

In July of this year (2009), I acquired WOWIO from Platinum Studios to give it the opportunity to achieve growth in an independently-financed environment. We have passed the final stage of our patent application and once granted, we will see a significant increase in value, leaving us well-positioned for a possible acquisition or significant merger.

To keep myself grounded, I founded a non-profit organization, Lost and Found in America. I live in LA with my wife and three children.
