  • Research Assistant

    The Micro-Photonics Lab performed cutting-edge research and development in micro-photonics and diffractive optics. My projects and accomplishments included:

    • 3D PHOTONIC CRYSTALS: First ever to fabricate 3D photonic crystals on a standard UV mask aligner. Technique was based on near-field nano-patterning.

Raymond Rumpf’s Education

  • University of Central Florida

    Ph.D. , Optics , 2001 — 2006

    DISSERTATION TITLE:
    “Design and optimization of nano-optical elements by coupling fabrication to optical behavior”

    ACADEMIC ADVISOR:
    Dr. Eric G. Johnson

    ABSTRACT:
    To this day, fabrication and optical behavior remain largely decoupled during the design phase and numerous assumptions are made about “perfect” geometry. As research moves from theory to real devices, predicting device behavior based on realistic geometry becomes critical. In this dissertation, a set of numerical tools was developed to model micro and nano fabrication processes. They were combined with equally capable tools to model optical performance of the simulated structures. The dissertation applies the tools to a number of devices and processes including holographic lithography, space-variant photonic crystals, guided-mode resonance filters, autocloned form-birefringent devices, and metallo-dielectric photonic crystals.
