Rae Lewis-Thornton’s Summary

Emmy Awad Winning AIDS Activist Rae Lewis-Thornton rose to national accliam as the first Black Woman to tell her story of, "Living with AIDS" on the cover of Essence Magazine. In the past fifteen years she has traveled worldwide in an unending crusade challenging stereotypes and myths around HIV/AIDS. Rae is one of the most sought after lecturer on the topic of HIV/AIDS. She is frequently called upon as an expert in the press around this topic. She has been featured on NIght Line, The Oprah Winfrey Show, Dateline, CNN News, Black Entertainment Televsion (BET) and many other naitonal documentaries and news shows. She servered as an contributing Editor for WBBM-TV, A CBS owned station for an on-going, first-person series on Living With AIDS. Rae received an Emmy Awrd for her series.

Her press work around this topic is extensive, she has been featuerd in countless magazines and newspapers, Essence, Emerge, Glamour, "O"-the Oprah Winfrey Magazine, WOW, Jet, Ebony, Lifelines,The Washington Post to name a few.

A minister, licensed and seminary educated, Rev. Rae Lewis-Thornton preaches at churches arcoss the country, as well as, conduct workshops on living healthy- physically, emotionally and spiritually as a Chrisitan woman.

Rae is also a jewerly designer, in the process of launching a line of bracelets. www.rltcollection.com
