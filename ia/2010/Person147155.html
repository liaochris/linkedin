    Personics

    (Music industry)

    1993 — 1994 (1 year )

    Developed strategic marketing and acquired all music product for this start up New Media company. This company was the precursor of i-tunes. Back in the days of audio cassettes this company created kiosks which allowed consumers to create their own mix tapes (we didn't call them mix tapes then) from some of the best music available from the 60s through the 80s. Music was licensed from record labels and, using laser discs as masters in a machine that looked like a jukebox, the consumer could go into their favorite music chain (yes, there were record stores in the early 90s) and, for $0.79-0.99 a song, create their own mix tape to play on their cassette player. What a glorious idea! We were way ahead of our time. Unfortunately the record companies decided they didn't want to sell individual tracks (sound familiar) and the company was purchased by Time Warner. End of experiment until Napster.
