Erkin Cubukcu’s Experience

  • RF communications System Engineer

    Hamilton Sundstrand

    (Public Company; UTX; Aviation & Aerospace industry)

    March 2009 — Present (1 year 6 months)

    Works for NASA on the space communication systems. Modeled forward and return links with BPSK, QPSK, and OQPSK modulation schemes, incorporating Phase Arrayed Antennas, duplexers, RF Power Amplifiers (PA), Low Noise Amplifiers, (LNA), RRC and Integrate and dump (I&D) filters, spreading and de-spreading techniques, power unbalanced, and unequal data rate I & Q channels. Studied the impact of filter mismatch, linear and nonlinear distortions on performance (Bit Error Rate, BER). and generated spectral plots for various modes and configurations to verify the compliance. Used SIMULINK and MATLAB in modeling.

  • System Engineer (Radio Architecture)

    TI

    (Public Company; TXN; Semiconductors industry)

    February 2006 — October 2008 (2 years 9 months)

    Worked on RF and mixed-signal IC systems. Modeled a transceiver in C++ for WCDMA/GGE. The analog front-end of the model incorporates RF circuits (LNA and mixer) and baseband (Variable Gain Amplifiers and ADC). The digital back-end consists of fixed and variable sampling rate change filters, channel selection (RRC), and droop correction filters.

    System developed also incorporates a simplified transmitter, thermal and phase noise, nonlinearity (IIP2 and IIP3) impairment, and a variety of analog and digital (IIR and FIR) filter models.

    Wrote post-processing MATLAB scripts for analysis. Automated simulations for WCDMA were run and SNDR results were matched to the spread sheet for different band, sensitivity, maximum power, modulated/CW blocker combinations.

    RTL coded and synthesized pilot tracking, fractional delay calculation, and SIMO memory manager modules.

  • Senior Systems Engineer

    TensorComm

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Wireless industry)

Used Modelsim for RTL simulations and Altera's Quartus for synthesis and place & route.

  • Staff Engineer

    Prominent Comm

    (Wireless industry)

    February 2001 — May 2002 (1 year 4 months)

    Led an IC design team that developed the digital section of the physical layer of the Bluetooth modem.
    The major accomplishments of the team:
    • GFSK mod and demod, DC offset estimation and cancellation algorithms,
    • Filter design, AGC and synchronization algorithms,
    • Implementation of the data path, BlueRF system controller, and the D bus interface.

    Used Verilog for design and Synopsys' DC for synthesis. Ran C and RTL simulations for functional verifications, performance validations, and timing analysis. Used Artisan's TSMC 0.25 micron process CMOS technology Standard Cell library for this VLSI ASIC chip.
    Implemented AFC and RSSI modules. Coordinated the test and debugging activities in preparation for the next VLSI ASIC design tapeout.

  • Principle Staff Engineer

    Motorola

    (Wireless industry)

    December 1998 — January 2001 (2 years 2 months)

    Led a modem design team. The objective of the team, together with other teams, was to design the physical Layer of CDMA-2000 (1x), multiple-access system.
    The accomplishments of the team:
    • AGC design and FPGA implementation
    • Quick Paging algorithm development
    • Development of the simulation models for the Forward/Reverse Links
    • Comparative study of Direct Spread and Multi-carrier CDMA systems for 3x
    • Investigation of the de-skew buffer length issue
    • Study of the channel estimation filter issue (FIR vs. IIR)
    • Investigation of the required receiver A/D bit precision
    • Development of the Forward Link power control Algorithm
    • AWGN Channel Model design and development targeted for FPGA.
    Implemented the FPGA targeted AWGN Channel Model, used Verilog, Synplicity, and Xilinx for design, synthesis, static timing analysis, and place and route. Performed functional verifications and performance validations using C, MATLAB, and Verilog test-bench simulations.

  • Member of Technical Staff

    Lucent/Philips Consumer Comm.,

    (Wireless industry)

    April 1997 — December 1998 (1 year 9 months)

    Worked on a project to improve the RF front-end gain control algorithm to mitigate inter-modulation impairment and also to determine the optimum AGC reference setting for multi-Walsh Code OCNS.
    Worked on a project to partition the algorithms that make up the IS-95B CDMA physical layer between the ASIC and DSP to optimize the system performance in terms of complexity and speed. Studied also MSM3000 as a reference.
    Investigated the new architecture proposed for the IS-95 reverse channel, which uses a third-order sigma-delta converter and FIRDAC to implement a serial interface between RF and BB ICs.
    Determined the architecture for pilot and traffic channel interference canceller for the Rake receiver. Ran simulations to study the performance and computed the MIPS required to implement it. Investigated the impact of different impairments such as deviation in the channel estimation magnitude, time offset between the interfering and the re constructed cancellation signals.

  • Member of Technical Staff

    NYNEX Science & Technology

    (Wireless industry)

    November 1986 — April 1997 (10 years 6 months)

    Developed a channel characterization technique to qualify a loop for ADSL. This technique is based on obtaining impulse response of the channel. The frequency range of DC to 1.1 MHz is studied. The magnitude, phase, and group delay responses were obtained by using FFT techniques.
    Employed an innovative technique to measure the group delay ripple of a Multi channel Multi point Distribution System, MMDS channel. For this purpose, wrote a MATLAB script and a simulation program in C.
    Developed an MMDS (64 ary QAM) modem model based on the measurements in the potential MMDS market and obtained the BER performance for multi path propagation with AWGN. Studied the C/I threshold degradation due to the Group Delay Ripple. Studied the effects of fixed point arithmetic and limited dynamic range in equalizer design. As part of a team, made lab and field measurements in the 2.5 GHz frequency region.
    Modeled GMSK modem and obtained the BER performance for the AWGN and Rayleigh fading channels.
