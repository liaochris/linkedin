Richard Chambury’s Summary

Richard Chambury has worked as a photographer since 1987 when he began his career at the Herts & Essex Observer in Bishop's Stortford, Herts.

From this important grounding in the industry he made the leap to Fleet Street, London where he worked covering News, Royal Family and Entertainment events which included terrorist bombings, Princess Diana's Funeral and many, many film premieres.

During this period, he was also the set photographer at The Big Breakfast and T.F.I. Friday on Channel 4, where he photographed live events for the images to be used to promote the shows in the media.

In his spare time, Richard continues his obsession with photography, shooting portraits and reportage on a regular basis. His photographic influences are Richard Avedon, David Bailey, Henri Cartier Bresson, Annie Leibowitz, William Eggleston and Martin Parr. Recently he has been using
Lomography cameras as a challenge to himself to counter the day to day formula of the corporate world.

Richard Chambury’s Specialties:

Richfoto specialises in editorial photography, business photography for brochure and website redesigns and social events photography.
