Marcel Boontje’s Summary

Fresh out of University I started working for Infodrome, a think tank for the Dutch government on the role of the government in the information society. Their I developed my technical skills for the web. Web design, database driven websites, Search Engine Optimization (SEO), designing an online forum for opinion leaders, managing and editing a weekly eZine.

After Infodrome I applied the theoretical knowledge of the information age to a practical case. Cultural networker I worked out and executed a plan on ‘how to empower people who are part of the cultural field of a city through a combination of online and offline tools’.

Hooking up with a php-programmer and a graphical designer, I worked on al aspects of making the cultural field visible and interactive through the website knoopoudwest.nl

Also I worked as a freelance web consultant for United Knowledge’s project politiekdigitaal. nl and for Cyburg, the Amsterdam equivalent for Kenniswijk.

As a spin off from the cultural online project as cultural networker I developed the concept amsterdammers.net to empower neighbors (in stead of friends or colleagues as with Facebook or LinkedIn).This being my own project, I got the change to do the whole thing: concept, building, SEO/SEA, content development, project management etc.

In 2008 I was asked by the PvdA Amsterdam to develop a web strategy for their upcoming campaign for the 2010 City Council elections. We did some benchmarking and found our voters liked our politicians but not the partyname. So we ran a ‘people campaign’ that supported the street level campaign through our website, SEO/SEA, Youtube, Twitter, Facebook and LinkedIn. we got people to join up with our top politicians and campaign for us through the web. Google Adwords got us extra traffic to our site.Against the national trent for the PvdA during those elections we won the elections and kept our longterm average of 15 seats out of 45 in Amsterdam
