    September 2000 — September 2002 (2 years 1 month)

As president, I managed projects and activities of the Board of Directors, directed group processes, and conducted meetings and presentations to many professional, legal groups in the Jacksonville community. During my time as President, membership of the organization grew by 22% in one year alone. Perhaps my biggest contribution during my tenure was increasing the educational opportunities to Jacksonville paralegals.

While at Marks Gray, I redesigned, implemented and maintained a database for better project management of documentation and witness information in compliance with discovery regulations. While at Marks Gray, I consistently exceeded productivity goals and was asked to guide and direct other paralegals to reach similar goals.

  • Paralegal

    Waite, Schneider, Bayless & Chesley, Co. L.P.A.

    (Law Practice industry)

    1992 — 1995 (3 years )

    As a paralegal in the complex tort litigation department for this prestigious and nationally acclaimed law firm, I had the privilege of working on many famous cases that were nationally televised. While at this firm, I drafted pleadings for both federal and state courts, managed all phases of the discovery process, assisted attorneys in preparation for trials, and prepared demonstrative evidence for trials. Two of my most successful accomplishments at this firm included uncovering prior fraud and litigation in a case against a state agency and researching and locating an expert witness in England on polar bears and zoo management. I traveled out of town to oversee a mass document production and attended Anti-Tobacco litigation conferences.
