David Kusumoto’s Summary

● I'm a very friendly but sometimes brutally honest PR, PA and communications executive and blogger (nearly 600 active subscribers) with extensive experience handling external and internal mainstream and guerrilla publicity for multi-billion-dollar corporations and other clients. I have worn just three "major" hats in my career. (I do NOT "job hop"):

1) as a news operations manager, director and reporter;
2) as a Fortune 500 corporate communications rep handling public relations and public affairs for Sempra Energy, a global energy services parent company and;
3) as a communications director at a public relations and marketing agency representing companies with annual combined revenues of $30-$50 billion.

● Internal and external public affairs and PR – MUST BE INTEGRATED into your business plans – otherwise they're just "overhead." Savvy pros stay on offense with big-picture themes (not reacting constantly, playing defense), enhancing your image while working with other departments to gain stakeholder "buy-in" of your products, services or political initiatives. This means being fearless to debate your cause, creating a more vigorous profile and framing everything as addressing a global problem/conflict in ways too compelling to ignore.

● RESULTS – Stakeholder "buy-in" of client initiatives; complemented by trade and lay media placements, e.g., the San Diego Union-Tribune, local print and online news organizations and television stations, the Wall Street Journal, New York Times, Barron's, Money, Builder, Big Builder.

● In the end, it's not about me. It's about what a person with my background as a news editor, corporate spokesman, employee communications representative, external/media relations pro and social media blogger – can provide to YOU. My skills are geared toward firms that want improve their outreach with customers, employees and other stakeholders, REGARDLESS of industry.
