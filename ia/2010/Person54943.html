Derek D. Buck’s Summary

My formal training began in high school, when I enrolled in journalism courses during three of my four years at Northern Cambria High School in Northern Cambria, PA. I earned a two-year stint as editor-in-chief of the campus newspaper, The Emanon, beginning in my junior year.

I continued my studies at the Indiana University of Pennsylvania in Indiana, PA, earning my bachelor's degree in journalism and political science.

During my junior year, I wrote a political column for the campus paper, The Penn, for which I earned a Keystone Press Award, an annual prize honoring Pennsylvania journalists who consistently provide relevance, integrity and initiative in serving readers and furthering First Amendment values.

In Sept. 2007, I began writing for Infendo, a popular Web site dedicated to coverage of Nintendo platforms. With more than 85,000 readers visiting the site each month, my writing was being exposed to a larger audience than ever before. My contrbutions for Infendo have been broad, including countless news articles, oft-Digg’d features and games reviews.

In July 2008, I accepted a position with GameZone, one of the Web's leading gaming publications for more than a decade, reviewing games across multiple platforms with sharply written, entertaining content.

From Aug. 2008 to July 2009, I held a position with the Advanced Media Network as a news editor for Kombo, a budding source for industry news, game reviews and commentary. My coverage for Kombo was focused on accuracy, enjoyable writing and consistently following AP guidelines.

I’ve also contributed to Ace Gamez, Face Off Games and more.

Samples of my published work from across the Web, as well as my résumé and contact details, are available at my personal Web site, linked above.
