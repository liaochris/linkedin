David Burns, CEM’s Summary

Recent Projects:

Appeared as a technical expert in "After Armageddon" on the History Channel: http://shop.history.com/detail.php?p=255666

Participated in development of FEMA/EMI "IS-100 Intro ICS course for Higher Education" http://bit.ly/132cgg; and project administrator for an integrated, multi-modal Mass Notification System (MNS), merging independent hardware systems (outdoor sirens, SMS/text, cable TV, radio, etc.) using the Common Alerting Protocol (CAP). UCLA is one of the first campuses nationally to develop such a system.

Current duties focus on exercising emergency organizational staff, campus EOC modernization, public policy development related to the Higher Education Act, crisis communications, use of social media and interagency coordination.

I have 25+ years of diverse experience in Emergency Management in the private and public sectors, including local, county, & state government. Served 2 years as Vice-Chair of the University & Colleges Committee (UCC) for IAEM (2007-2009).

Former first responder (Oakland, Ca), EMT1/Paramedic, directed field operations, and 9-1-1 EMS dispatcher. 9 years in regional EMS/Disaster coordination. Expertise in disaster operations, public policy, and EMS provider contract oversight. Instructor for SEMS, NIMS, ICS, EOC operations & section training. Coordinated construction of EOC's in 2000 & 2010. Terrorism/WMD Instructor. Threat & Vulnerability Team participant (4 years). 5 years of federal grant administration experience through FEMA.

Earned Certified Emergency Manager (CEM®) credential (IAEM) in 2007.

Managed medical operations at the Cypress Freeway collapse (1989 Loma Prieta "World Series" Quake). Experienced in many major disasters over three decades.

These experiences fuel my ongoing passion in this field to never laziness, complacency, sloppiness, cutting corners, and just plain old mediocrity in public policy development and application in our profession.

    Selected as UCLA's first full-time emergency management director for California's largest populated university (69,000+ students, faculty, & staff). UCLA's Emergency Management Office is an independent department, responsible for the administration of the campus emergency/disaster response, mass warning system & activation, planning & coordination, and EOC operations & readiness. I supervise one full-time EM Specialist. The UCLA EM Office operates a comprehensive "all-hazards" emergency management program for the UCLA campus and UCLA-affilated administrative offices in the greater Los Angeles region. I report to the Associate Vice Chancellor -General Services, and support the Emergency Management Policy Group (EMPG), led by the UCLA Chancellor.

  • Vice Chair, University & Colleges Caucus (UCC)

    IAEM

    (Public Safety industry)

    2006 — October 2009 (3 years )

    Chaired the UCC legislative subcommittee and led efforts related to the implementation of the Higher Education Act of 2008.

    The mission of the Universities & Colleges Caucus (UCC) is to represent the emergency management issues surrounding college and university campuses. Although they are a part of the communities in which they reside, higher education institutions take on special and sometimes unique considerations when preparing their students, faculty, staff and visitors for responding to, recovering from and mitigating against emergencies. The purpose of this caucus is to provide emergency managers from higher education institutions a voice on a national and international scale to ensure their needs are also being addressed by government and industry officials.
