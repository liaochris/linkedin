Emily Winn-Deen’s Summary

Seasoned professional with >30 years of experience in the biotech and diagnostics sector, specializing in new product development for emerging markets.

Federal-level advisor on Health and Human Services (HHS) committees for Genetics Health and Society (SACGHS) and Clinical Laboratory Improvement Act Committee (CLIAC), and on the Clinical and Laboratory Standards Institute (CLSI) Area Committee for Molecular Methods.

Emily Winn-Deen’s Specialties:

Specializing in personalized medicine and companion diagnostics. Experience includes evaluating market trends and new technology, corporate partnering, GMP manufacturing, FDA design control compliance, and strategic planning.

Led scientific discussions of Celera’s genetic analysis programs with present and potential pharmaceutical customers, and with academic scientists who wished to carry out collaborative research studies. Wrote project proposals and grant applications, and led execution of successful proposals. Managed a team of 10 lab scientists and 2 bioinformatics professionals, with a departmental budget of $7 million.

  • Director, Diagnostics R&D

Supervised the R&D scientists responsible for synthetic organic chemistry, molecular diagnostics, and FISH, plus the quality control, quality assurance, and business development departments. Provided guidance to bring Oncor into compliance with the new FDA design control regulations. Reviewed outside technology and negotiated licensing of new gene targets and technology platforms. Initiated a vigorous out-licensing program for 3 key Oncor-owned technologies for DNA amplification and homogeneous fluorescence detection. Served as Oncor’s Radiation Safety Officer.

Evaluated technology/instrument systems and diagnostic market opportunities. Established and managed external research collaborations. Hired and supervised scientific staff. Planned and monitored construction of a 7000 sq. ft. diagnostics R&D facility, specified and purchased equipment, and set up the facility in accordance with GLP. Provided regulatory guidance for set-up of GMP manufacturing and external beta-test sites.
