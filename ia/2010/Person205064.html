Torsten Mark Kowal’s Summary

An environmental scientist and natural resources manager, with an international development background in Latin America and Asia, now based in the UK. Interested in UK-based climate change adaptation assignments; and in missions to design and evaluate poverty-reduction and environmental management projects in developing countries.

Specialist in adaptation to climate change and management of the impacts of weather events, also has skills and knowledge in participatory forestry and in watershed management, food security, agroforestry and land-use planning.

Fluent in Spanish, cross-cutting skills include running e training programmes, helping make rural livelihoods more sustainable, applying best-practice, putting research into use, developing policy and capacity building. His approach is based on fullest possible participation of local stakeholders. He is an APM-certified project practitioner (UK Association for Project Management).

In 2008 with the UK Climate Impacts Programme, conducted three climate-change vulnerability assessments to support adaptation planning by UK local government authorities.

From early 2009 onwards, Mark has further developed his international experience in climate risk management. He won direct work for a 5-month study for RELEX and DEV for the highest level within the European Commission (reporting to heads of Directorates) to develop the EC's tools and approaches to fuller include climate risk issues, both for country programming and for project development.

Two recent contracts have deepened his sector-level and geographical experience, first for the Asian Development Bank in Viet Nam to prepare the climate risk management approaches for a large infrastructure investment project in the northern mountains.

Then in 2010, Mark was team leader for a sector assessment in Guatemala of climate impacts and adaption options in the food security, agriculture and water management sectors.
