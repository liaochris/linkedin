Jani Kajala’s Summary

Seasoned software executive who hasn't lost touch to the source code. Extensive project and multi-platform software development experience since -96 in 3D technology/games (PC, consoles, mobile), J2ME and smart phones software and web services (PHP, JavaScript, AJAX). Solid executive management experience from various positions. 3D graphics programming as a passion since -92 demo-scene background. Published author and visiting lecturer in several conferences and universities.

Jani Kajala’s Specialties:

Specialities: Throughout understanding of software design, technology and processes and business from very low level assembly programming to business processes and customer perspective. Special interests include 3D graphics, software architecture design, multi-platform development, user experience.

Jani Kajala’s Experience

  • CTO/Owner

    Kajala Group Ltd.

    (Computer Software industry)

    March 2008 — Present (2 years 2 months)

    Software development and consulting on 3D graphics technology, web services and mobile. Clients include Ocaco Finland, Studio of Modern Architecture, University of Kajaani, Sanoma Entertainment, Standard & Poor's, Lapland Studio, Force Stone (Japan), The Mobile Gamer (Singapore), Secure Action (Finland). Internal projects include multiRender (http://www.multirender.com), a high-end real-time 3D-visualization engine, and Valo Technology (http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gNmCxH9Yud8), the software system powering interactive multi-touch table solutions provide by Ocaco.

  • CTO

    Rovio Mobile Ltd.

    (Privately Held; Computer Games industry)

    April 2006 — March 2008 (2 years )

    After Pixelgene was acquired by Rovio Jani became CTO of Rovio. Rovio is an award-winning European handheld game developer and publisher making games for several platforms, including Nokia N-Gage/NGI, J2ME, Symbian, BREW and NDS. Clients include e.g. Nokia, Vivendi-Universal, Electronic Arts and Namco. Jani was responsible for technical direction of the company, directing and supporting several simultaneous production tracks and solving problems, supporting project sales, coordinating R&D, leading technology development in the company, main focus being multiplatform 3D-technology development. Jani was, for example, Technical Director in critically acclaimed Bounce - Boing Voyage N-Gage title, and Project Lead in relatively big (~100 man-months) in-house Uniform Mobile Tool-chain technology development project.

  • CTO, co-founder

    Pixelgene Ltd.

    (Computer Games industry)

    January 2004 — April 2006 (2 years 4 months)

    3D-technology and game development and consulting on various handheld platforms (J2ME, Symbian, Playstation Portable). Pixelgene's multiplatform 3D-engine (KA3D), developed by Jani, was used e.g. in PSP game 'Puzzlescape', published by O3, and later in next-gen N-Gage Nokia 1st-party title by Rovio Mobile. The 3D-engine has also been used by several universities for game development education. The engine supports Win32, WinCE, Symbian, Nokia N-Gage/NGI, DirectX 9, OpenGL ES, Playstation Portable, Nintendo DS and software rasterization. Jani also helped Bugbear to develop the technology used in Sega Rally Revo (PSP). Pixelgene clients included Nokia, Real Networks, Bugbear and Farmind. Pixelgene employed 15 people. Pixelgene was acquired by Rovio Mobile in April 2006.

  • Lead programmer, CEO, co-founder

    Cat Mother, Ltd.

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Computer Software industry)

    July 2001 — July 2003 (2 years 1 month)

    Cat Mother developed games and technology for PC and console platforms. Jani was the lead programmer in both projects of the company, futuristic team sports game Bladeball and 3rd person action / adventure game Dead Justice. He also developed the 3D game engine and the core technology used in the company. After the company didn't manage to close the second round of funding and closed its doors, the source code of last project and the critically acclaimed 3D game engine was released as open source at http://catmother.sourceforge.net. The open source release has been downloaded over 42000 times.

  • Programmer, co-founder

    Sumea Interactive, Ltd.

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Computer Software industry)

    January 2001 — June 2001 (6 months)

    Sumea Interactive developed Java-based games and software. Jani worked mainly as 3D tool-chain and technology developer, but he also helped to design architecture of an online distributed gaming system for MatchON Sports, which acquired Sumea (MatchON went bacruptcy soon after that in 2001... Sumea was later founded again, and that reincarnation was later acquired by Digital Chocolate in 2004).

  • Programmer

    Hybrid Graphics, Ltd.

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Computer Software industry)

    April 2001 — May 2001 (2 months)

    Renderware dPVS is an advanced multiplatform dynamic visibility system developed by research and development house Hybrid Graphics and distributed by Criterion / Renderware. Jani wrote Playstation 2 assembly version of the library as contract work for Hybrid Graphics. The technology has been licenced for example by Sony Online Entertainment and used for example in Star Wars Galaxies. Later, after Hybrid was acquired by Nvidia, the technology was spinned off in a separate company called Umbra (which was also original product name of the technology).

  • Programmer

    Remedy Entertainment, Ltd.

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Computer Software industry)

    May 1999 — January 2001 (1 year 9 months)

    Max Payne was story driven 3rd person action game developed by Remedy Entertainment and produced by 3DRealms. Jani worked on the MAX-FX engine technology, Max Payne game code and architecture, main responsibilities being game framework programming and design, collision detection and portal systems. Max Payne total sales are 4 million units even without counting the sequels.

  • Lead programmer, co-founder

    Carts Entertainment, Ltd.

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Computer Software industry)

    July 1996 — May 1999 (2 years 11 months)

    In Carts Jani worked in various projects between -96 and -99:

    Thrust Twist and Turn (1998-1999):
    Futuristic 3D racing game developed by Carts Entertainment and published by Take 2 Interactive. Thrust Twist and Turn was the first ever 3D game title developed in Finland which made to international commercial distribution. Jani was the lead programmer in the project.

    Pirate Ships (1997-1998):
    Multiplayer TV-broadcast action game developed for Saraxa Group and Moon TV. Jani was the lead programmer in the project.

    Net Formula I and II (1998):
    Popular Java applet racing games developed for MTV3 (www.mtv3.fi) by Kota Interactive Ltd. Jani developed the race track editor used in both of the games.

    Cubiosis (1996-1997):
    Experimental 3D Tetris clone developed by Carts Entertainment. Jani was the lead programmer in the project.
