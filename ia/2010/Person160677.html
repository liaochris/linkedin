Paul DeGregorio’s Summary

Paul DeGregorio has devoted his professional career focused on the promotion of democracy and elections and is one of the world’s top election experts. He has worked in over 25 countries and served as the chief election official of the United States. DeGregorio has held significant policy-making and top management positions in prominent institutions that have advanced the cause of democracy and freedom throughout the world. DeGregorio’s leadership has been recognized by the United States and the international community. In 2004, he received the prestigious Freedom Award from the National Association of Secretaries of State for his achievements in promoting freedom throughout the world.

Paul DeGregorio’s Experience

  • Chief Operating Officer/Manager of Elections

    Everyone Counts, Inc

    (Computer Software industry)

    June 2007 — Present (3 years 5 months)

    As COO of Everyone Counts, (www.everyonecounts.com) DeGregorio is a strong advocate of the use of the Internet in the election process, particularly secured online voting programs that can especially empower remote and disabled voters in public and private sector elections. In 2007, open-sourced-based Everyone Counts achieved significant online voting success in British and Australian public elections, and in February 2008 provided the technology that allowed US Democrats living abroad the opportunity to vote in a binding online presidential primary that elected delegates to the 2008 Democratic National Convention. Everyone Counts has offices in San Diego, CA, Melbourne, Australia, and London, England.

  • Election Administration Expert & Consultant

    Organization for Security and Cooperation in Europe (OSCE)

    (Non-Profit; 201-500 employees; Government Administration industry)

    June 1996 — Present (14 years 5 months)

    DeGregorio has participated in election observation and assessment missions for the OSCE office of Democratic Initiatives and Human Rights (ODIHR) in Albania (1997), Russia (2000), Netherlands (2006),Estonia (2007), Ireland (2007), Belgium (2007) & Switzerland (2007). He has also been selected to participate in workshops on the development of standards for election observation and election assessments. DeGregorio has represented the United States and several conferences devoted to Human Rights and Democracy.

  • Commissioner, Vice Chair & Chair

    U.S. Election Assistance Commission (EAC)

    (Government Agency; 11-50 employees; Government Administration industry)

    December 2003 — March 2007 (3 years 4 months)

    The EAC is a 4-member bipartisan federal agency established by the Help America Vote Act of 2002 (HAVA) to oversee and improve the process of conducting elections in the United States.

    DeGregorio distinguished himself on the EAC by focusing his efforts on the EAC’s mandates to review state election reform plans and distribute 2.3 billion dollars to the states; establish new voluntary voting system guidelines, develop Best Practices in election administration, adopt the first federal voting system certification program and the first federal accreditation of testing laboratories; provide guidance and advisories to election officials and conduct studies on election reform issues, such as recounts and voter ID. Because the EAC had not existed before, DeGregorio used his considerable talents and worked with his colleagues to recruit and hire key management personnel, formulate budgets and sound accounting practices, and organize a management structure and work plans.

  • Executive Vice President & Chief Operating Officer

    International Foundation for Election Systems (IFES)

    (Non-Profit; 201-500 employees; Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    December 2001 — December 2003 (2 years 1 month)

    IFES (www.ifes.org) is one of the world’s leading institutions involved in the promotion of democracy and governance world-wide. At IFES, he was responsible for the day-to-day operation of this non-profit organization with over 400 employees in 21 countries. DeGregorio reported to the President and Board of Directors, and was successful in building, motivating, supervising, and leading IFES senior management team and staff. During his EVP tenure at IFES, DeGregorio implemented the policy and directives of the Board as well as the organization’s strategic plan and during his tenure helped to increase revenues to $36 million (38% increase).

  • Director of Outreach Development

    University of Missouri

    (Educational Institution; 10,001 or more employees; Higher Education industry)

    December 1993 — December 2001 (8 years 1 month)

    As Director of Outreach Development for the University of Missouri-St. Louis, DeGregorio was on the cutting edge of the effort which made higher education more accessible for citizens of Missouri. Under his leadership, the University developed four satellite education centers and directed the effort that served non-traditional place-bound students at those centers by offering upper level and graduate programs. During his tenure, nearly 4000 students, 77% of whom were women, took UM-St. Louis outreach courses at the four satellite centers and hundreds were able to obtain their college degree and move on to productive employment in a much shorter period of time. The Outreach program, which was described in 1999 by the North Central Accreditation (NCA) committee as a “model for the nation”.

  • Election Administration Expert & Consultant

    International Foundation for Election Systems (IFES)

    (Non-Profit; 201-500 employees; Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    June 1993 — December 2001 (8 years 7 months)

    As a consultant to IFES, DeGregorio assessed the technical aspects of the election process and provided direct assistance to many of the new and established democracies of the world including Russia, Kazakhstan, Moldova, Ukraine, Romania, Albania, Macedonia, Slovakia, Georgia, Sierra Leone, Nigeria, Democratic Republic of Congo, Bosnia-Herzigovina, Kosovo, Cambodia, Thailand, Indonesia, and Phillipines.

  • Director of Elections

    St. Louis County

    (Government Agency; 1001-5000 employees; Government Administration industry)

    April 1985 — December 1993 (8 years 9 months)

    As chief administrator of the election agency of Missouri's largest county, DeGregorio worked with leaders at every level--from the White House to Congress, from the legislature to the councils of county and municipal governments, to the officials who operate schools and fire protection services. During his tenure in St. Louis County, a jurisdiction of one million people, 91 cities, 3 U.S. Congressional districts, 39 state legislative districts, 7 county council districts, 24 fire and school districts and over 1100 precincts, DeGregorio directed a staff of 72 full-time employees plus thousands of part-time workers and instituted major improvements in voter registration, training, disability voting, counting and management procedures. He was successful in prosecuting voter fraud and in drafting legislation to improve the electoral process and was widely-recognized for his achievements.
