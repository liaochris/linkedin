Chad Sweet’s Summary

Previously as the Chief of Staff of the US Department of Homeland Security, I helped Secretary Chertoff manage an organization of approximately 220,000 dedicated homeland security professionals with a budget of over $50 billion annually. An important metric of our performance was this: excluding all deaths in Iraq and Afghanistan, over 20,000 people were killed from terrorist acts over almost eight years since 9/11 - not one of those plots was successful on US soil on our watch.

After leaving DHS, Secretary Chertoff and I co-founded The Chertoff Group to advise providers and consumers of risk management solutions that enhance security for businesses and governments around the world.
