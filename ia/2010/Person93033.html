Jeff Elder’s Summary

As the social media manager at Lowe's Home Improvement, I guide social media strategy for a Fortune 50 company. Connecting fast-growing media with big business is a delicate balance. It requires knowing the latest trends, but not being dazzled by them; it requires being steeped in business traditions while not being constrained by them. Social media is full of noise and irrelevance, but at a Fortune 50 level, it must get real. Connecting social media to real business objectives is my job.
I didn't just hop aboard the social media bandwagon; I took part in this communications revolution, firsthand. In 2008-2009, as a Knight fellow at Stanford University, I spent time at Facebook and Twitter and got to know the companies. I studied social networking with the pioneers of the industry at Stanford, and connected with thought leaders all over Silicon Valley. I met the CEOs of Facebook, Twitter and Craigslist, and built close ties to the industry.
Prior to my Stanford social media research, I broke new ground in combining traditional and new media as a social media columnist at The Charlotte Observer.
I'm a longtime communications professional who's worked for top media companies. I've managed a staff overseas, met President Bill Clinton, talked books with Toni Morrison, music with Elvis Costello, and documentaries with Ken Burns.
I can talk to anyone, and I'm an idea guy. I love to explore.
