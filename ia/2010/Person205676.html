Tokiwa Smith’s Summary

In the fall of 1996, when I enrolled in Florida Agricultural and Mechanical University (FAMU) and declared my major to be chemical engineering; I never envisioned that over 10 years later my career would be devoted to creating educational opportunities for youth and young adults. I have over 6 years experience in educational program development and management for K-12 and undergraduate students with an emphasis in math and science education. I have developed and managed after-school, educational enrichment, summer science program, tutorial and undergraduate research programs in academia and nonprofit such organizations as Ambrose Parks and Recreation District, Atlanta Public Schools, Hands on Atlanta, Georgia State University and Spelman College.

In 2005, I combined my love for math and science and desire to help youth to Science, Engineering and Mathematics Link, Inc. (SEM Link), a not-for-profit tax-exempt corporation, which I currently serve as Executive Director. SEM Link promotes student achievement and career exploration in math and science for K-12 students. SEM Link connects K-12 students with the SEM community to "unveil potential through exposure" through our two programs, Experimental Design Program and Math and Science Career Academy. We provide programs for organizations such like Cool Girls. Our corporate sponsors and volunteers come from organizaitons such as the CDC, Georgia Tech, Georgia Power and the WGBH Foundation.

I've held several leadership positions in the National Organization of Black Chemists and Chemical Engineers, Miss/Ms. Black Georgia USA and am an alumnus of the United Way VIP Program and WEDA AMI Microenterprise Initiative.

My goals as a professional is to create opportunities for youth and young adults to reach their potential through the educational program management, curriculum development and educational consulting. I also want to help others to realize their dreams through business creation and development.
