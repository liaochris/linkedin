Mark Titus’s Summary

Mark Titus is an award-winning screenwriter and the principal owner of August Island Pictures http://www.augustisland.com a motion picture company with its roots in Seattle. In 2001 he completed an intensive directing program at the Vancouver, B.C Film School, then wrote, directed or produced several short films including Fins, which premiered at the Seattle International Film Festival in 2003; 30, a documentary commemorating SIFF’s 30th anniversary; Justin Burris’s Knowing the Game; and Cheryl Slean’s IFP award winning Diggers. In 2006, Paradigm Studio optioned Mark’s script TSONOQUA (The Wild Woman), winner of the 2004 Washington State Screenplay Competition.

Phil Davis (Casanova, Vera Drake) and Marie Matiko (Date Movie, The Civilization of Maxwell Bright) star as Fred and Keiko, the odd-couple cemetarians who drive the story through explosively colorful vignettes.
