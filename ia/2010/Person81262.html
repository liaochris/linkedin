Helen Brice MBACP, FRSA’s Summary

Having grown up as a musician and actress, and after 17 years in the commercial music industry as a performer, music producer & manager, I trained as a psychotherapist specialising in the field about which I know best. Thus, with partner Miranda Jackson, I formed STIMMUNG, Britain's first practice specialising in psychotherapy for musicians & performers.

My approach is predominantly existential-phenomenological, i.e. emphasis on working from a client's own experience and understanding and how he/she makes sense of life in the here and now, notwithstanding previous life experience or trauma.

I am Counsellor and Mentor to the students and staff at Trinity-Laban Conservatoire for Music and Dance.

I am also one of six highly trained Psychotherapists in Dialectical Behaviour Therapy for Forensic Therapies at HMP Holloway working with women with Borderline Personality Disorder who are prone to self-harm. I co-facilitate weekly skills groups and give individual therapy and skills coaching to prisoners.

I am Secretary and Trustee of the Society for Existential Analysis and a Fellow of the Royal Society of Arts.

At Virgin Classics in 1990 I pioneered the notion of composer-led recordings. In 1992 I formed piano duo "Zoo II" and made the world premiere recording of my arrangement of Nyman's "Taking A Line For A Second Walk" which has been played on BBC Radio 3.

In 1994 asKarl Jenkins's Manager and Producer I re-launched the composer's career and, as Executive & Associate Producer, I steered the Adiemus Project to success. Thus Jenkins was propelled from jingles writer to one of the most popular British classical composers, as he indeed remains today. Under my management the success of Adiemus resulted in numerous UK and international platinum, gold and silver disc awards, and Jenkins picked up several honours and accolades including an OBE. The acclaimed "The Armed Man: A Mass For Peace" continues to be performed by choirs worldwide.

Helen Brice MBACP, FRSA’s Specialties:

STIMMUNG. I & Miranda have unique attunement as therapists with musicians & performers, their insecurities, expectations, anxieties - whether successful, popular, young, long-standing, struggling; soloist, session musician, singer, dancer, conductor, actor; of managing stage fright, competition; of substance abuse, living and working with success & high expectations; of coping with failure, low self-worth, discrimination, relationships, sexuality, bereavement, depression, long-term illness, etc.
