Terry Young’s Summary

"BEYOND THE FIRST WORLD: Global News and Best Practices for Managers of Innovation, Technology Transfer and Intellectual Property" is a bi-monthly newsletter to be launched April 27, 2010. The newsletter is targeted to developing and emerging economies, thus is free to subscribers. The corresponding website will feature an original paper written by an international expert every two weeks. In advance of the April 27 launch, there is offered today a new LinkedIn Group (Beyond the First World), a Twitter feed of one news item per day (@BFWNews), and a Facebook Page, where "fans" receive one news item per day.

Terry Young’s Experience

  • Editor

    Beyond the First World

    (Online Media industry)

    March 2010 — Present (2 months)

    Beyond the First World is a not-for-profit organization focused upon the economic development of Developing and Emerging Economies through the dissemination of current news and best practices in innovation management, technology transfer and intellectual property. The project will enhance the networking of practitioners around the world also improving the profession.

  • President

    Terry Young Group LLC

    (Management Consulting industry)

    January 2005 — October 2006 (1 year 10 months)

    The company was devoted to training in technology transfer practices internationally. In my career, I have made more than 60 trips abroad for professional development assignments and consulting with governments in public policy development.

  • Manager of Intellectual Property

    Texas Transportation Institute

    (Research industry)

    January 2004 — December 2005 (2 years )

    As an agency of the State of Texas and a component of the A&M System, TTI conducts research in all aspects of transportation - highway, rail, mass transportation, etc. I managed the protection and licensing of the intellectual property that arose from the Agency's research. Of all 18 components of the A&M System, TTI created more license royalty income than all others.

  • Asst. Vice Chancellor for Technology Transfer and Executive Director, Technology Licensing Office

    Texas A&M University

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    January 1992 — December 2003 (12 years )

    I created the TLO at Texas A&M with only myself, a secretary, and two graduate assistants. By the time I chose to move to a 50% position at the Texas Transportation Institute, there were 25 people on staff. The office budget was "self supported" from 15% of the royalty stream from technology licensing with royalty income to the University of $9 million in 2003.

  • President

    Association of University Technology Managers

    (Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    February 2001 — February 2002 (1 year 1 month)

    Elected President of the 3,500 member professional association. Position involved a 3-year commitment as President-Elect (2000), President (2001) and Immediate Past President (2002).

  • Assistant Agency Director

    Texas Engineering Experiment Station

    (Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    January 1988 — December 1991 (4 years )

    In my responsibilities at TEES, I directed the intellectual property management program of this State Agency, was Director of an Economic Development Administration (EDA) University Center, worked with the International Programs Office of Texas A&M University to create international exchange programs, and initiated the selected NASA Mid-Continent Technology Transfer Center (14 States).

  • Contracts Administrator

    Texas A&M University Research Foundation

    (Management Consulting industry)

    January 1985 — December 1987 (3 years )

    In the Foundation, I managed a division for "post-award" administration. Responsibilities including negotiation of all contracts, including many with Federal Agencies in Washington, DC; assuring contract compliance; assuring that contractor reporting requirements were met, and closing out of contracts. I managed a group of 5 contract specialists, focused upon the College of Engineering in the University.

  • National Marketing Manager

    MicoTech, Inc

    (Marketing and Advertising industry)

    January 1983 — January 1985 (2 years 1 month)

    Micotech was a university start-up company created by a Professor of Engineering at Texas A&M University. I joined the start-up as National Marketing Manager upon receipt of my MBA degree. The product was a digital time recorder for personnel "clock-in" applications. MicoTech eventually was sold to a company in Arizona, and I subsequently began work at the Research Foundation.
