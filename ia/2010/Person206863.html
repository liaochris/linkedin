Tony Dye’s Summary

Professional Profile
Experienced in system strategy, organizing, documenting, and overseeing projects to completion. Knowledgeable in R&D, product development, testing, quality control, and user training. Effective both as an individual contributor and in team environments. Accomplished problem solver and solution finder; especially driven by “impossible” challenges. Always looking from the “how can this be improved” perspective. User experience focus, helping people improve their experience with technology.

Selected Career Highlights
Developed handoff process for new, often non-technical, staff. Results: 100% of new staff productive on first day at work

Created training curriculum based on staff responsibilities, rather than software functions. Results: Hundreds of staff members were able to speed routine tasks by 15-30 minutes per day; effective savings equivalent of several full time staff members each year

Recurring presentations for NACBA and Emory University on Church Technology for Business Administrators. Results: Asked to expand presentation by 300% over 3 years; equipped administrators to handle technology tasks at local churches

One of the charter organizers for the Church IT RoundTable (CITRT). Results: over 500 individuals now have a network of multiple resources for dialogue and researching best practices and solutions in IT. Network has grown to include many technologists with only casual church connections

Managed the Microsoft relationship for Intergraph, keeping both groups informed of needs, decisions, action items. Results: Intergraph became one of the first, and largest, NT development organizations, with more NT programmers than Microsoft itself had. Complete transition from Clipper/Unix to Intel/NT. For several years, more “inside Microsoft” information than the average Microsoft employee

Talk Radio producer/director for Neal Boortz show. Lots of great stories
