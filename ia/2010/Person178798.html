Robert A. Ungaro’s Summary

Robert Ungaro entered private practice following a distinguished career which began at the New York County District Attorney’s Office, followed by the NYC Department of Correction, culminating his public service at the NYC Department of Investigation. He was appointed, under Mayor Giuliani, to head the office of the NYC Fire Department Inspector General. He retired from public service in 1996 to focus his efforts representing disabled workers in complex disability litigation and civil service issues in the state, appellate, and federal courts. Mr. Ungaro, a seasoned advocate, has successfully represented clients in well over 3000 matters, many of which concern NYC police officers and firefighters injured in the performance of duty.

His offices are located at 291 Broadway, Suite 1400, and he can be reached at 212 766-5800. His law Firm is counsel to the New York City Police Captains Endowment Association (NYPD CEA) the union for the police executives of the NYPD (captains and above). His memberships include the New York County Lawyers Assoc., New York Social Security Bar Assoc., National Organization of Social Security Claims Representatives, and the Fraternal Order of Police.

Robert A. Ungaro’s Specialties:

Federal, State and Municipal Disability Law, Civil Service and Employment Law and all General Legal Matters and Negotiations.
