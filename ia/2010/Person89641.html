Jan Putnam’s Summary

26 years of experience in the interactive entertainment industry, on a global basis: sourcing prospective clients and negotiating agency agreements; representing game properties, entertainment content and brands, software development talent, services and technology; negotiating deals and contracts for title publishing, co-publishing, and distribution; establishing partnerships and identifying and implementing strategic alliances and joint ventures; product sourcing and content acquisition. Extensive international experience, primarily in Western Europe and Asia, with specialty in Japan.

Have negotiated publishing deals for hundreds of properties, both original game IPs and branded games. Among the most well-known original game IPs and franchises licensed: “Alone in the Dark”, “Baldur’s Gate”, "Battle Chess", "Boulder Dash", "Dark Castle", "Defender of the Crown", "Deja Vu", "Descent", "Dungeon Master", "Earthworm Jim", "Ecstatica", "Falcon", "Flight Unlimited", "Fool's Errand", "Hardball", "Lemmings" series, "MDK", "Myst", "Out of this World", "Prince of Persia", "Secret of Monkey Island", "Shadowgate", "Sim City", "The Last Express", "Warcraft" and "Zak McKracken". Some of the games licensed based on well-known brands and licenses: "Spy vs. Spy" and "Superman" (comics); "Indiana Jones", "Jurassic Park", "Star Trek" and "Star Wars" (films); "Frankenstein", "Lord of the Rings", "Robin Hood" and "Sherlock Holmes" (literature); "Jack Nicklaus Golf", "Formula 1" and "The British Open" (sports); and "Buzz Aldrin" (American astronaut).
