Steve Plunkett’s Summary

I got my FCC Broadcast permit in 1982 and wanted to be Peter Jennings when I grew up. I started out in Radio/TV /Journalism and Photography in High School.
Changed my mind and decided to become a hacker for the US Gov't, then the internet thing happened. I ended up growing an ISP/web design/hosting company from 3 to 18 people and 500 + websites. then I ran an Ad Agency/Design firm/SEO Agency, then landed where i am now at M/C/C Communications.

I've been doing organic SEO since 1995 when Yahoo! used webcrawler for their indexing agent. I was an early part of the first social search engine (dmoz.org). I monitor statistics and results for multiple clients on a daily basis. I seriously study Google every single day (and night) for a LIVING!

I've had the top blogs on myspace and friendster, which i started sometime around 2003-ish. I've seen a web update move a website from #10 to #3 in 3 hours, and be back at #10 the next morning. I've seen a blog post on my blog go from 0 to #1 in 14 minutes. I've tested almost every blogging system/platform or social network there is.. (email me if you have a new one i can play with and figure out how you rank things.)

Steve Plunkett’s Specialties:

I understand the social networking space like no other, MySpace, Facebook, Friendster, Digg, Twitter, Stumbleupon, etc... The emerging areas of DAO, ORM, Neg-SEO, SMO, etc. All fall within the influences of SEO. The internet's impact on every facet of a full-service ad agency and pr firm is immense. A conscious strategy that focuses on all these aspects for each client can be very, very powerful. Content is king and website metrics are invaluble to every aspect of a business.
