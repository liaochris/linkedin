Frantz Cayo’s Summary

I believe that you will find my background and skill sets particularly suited to assisting your company. At ESPN, I was responsible for developing content acquisition strategies, negotiating licensing deals, and creating programming recommendations for both on-air and online assets. I led cross-functional initiatives to maximize our partners' multi-platform exposure, and drive ESPN ratings and traffic. This often meant that I took risks with content distribution - I was the first person at ESPN to use multiple camera angles as a premium online and iTV offering for the Indianapolis 500, and the Indy 500 was also the first sport to simulcast live video via a mobile platform. I also created a package of Winter X and ESPY Awards content that lived on ESPN360 and ESPN.com to promote the live on-air events.

At ESPN, I also served as the key point of contact for all internal clients - including consumer marketing, ad sales, espn.com, and ESPN the Magazine - and external motor sports partners, including the Indianapolis Racing League and the National Hot Rod Association. I had extensive exposure to many executives at ESPN, and was particularly proud of my ability to establish strong relationships across all departments and with employees of all levels while there.

Prior to working at ESPN, I was a Marketing Manager at Major League Baseball's Advanced Media, where I was the ninth employee hired to build the MLBAM start-up. I was responsible for launching innovative online content and marketing campaigns, including "30 Ballparks in 30 Days", and the "Negro League Experience". I also initiated our e-mail marketing program and worked with our design team to create innovative e-mail messages to promote commerce. MLBAM allowed me to develop and fuel my love for entrepreneurism, and I am confident that this entrepreneurial drive would also serve me well as for your particular project.
