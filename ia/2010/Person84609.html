Patrick King’s Summary

Patrick King began his unconventional design career at the age of nine at a family reunion. Being introduced to the concept of a logo as a way to stay occupied, Patrick designed what is still used as the trademark for his family’s real estate agency. This talent became a profitable commodity for Patrick who, in a family of entrepreneurs, had a long list of projects to his credit before attending high school.

Patrick would later attend college briefly; long enough to learn the relationship between design, writing, and marketing. He worked for many years as a freelancer, working on many projects for local government agencies and small businesses in Southeastern Virginia, before moving to Washington, DC.

In July 2004, Patrick King moved to the capital area to establish himself as a creative force in the metropolitan business scene. Within a few months, Patrick formed ImagineDesign, a full-service graphic design firm specialized in web and print media, serving clients like Jim Beam Brands Worldwide, PriceWaterhouseCoopers, Rubbermaid, plus several local manufacturers, government contractors and non-profits nationwide.
