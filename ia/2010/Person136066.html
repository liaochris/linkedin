Mary E. Nelson’s Summary

My professional experience includes:

Representing developers in housing, commercial, and multi-use development projects utilizing tax abatement, tax credits, and other incentives.

Advising and counseling small businesses in stock and asset acquisitions, joint venture agreements, limited offerings, secured and unsecured financings, and general corporate matters.

Providing guidance to minority and women-owned businesses in DBE, MBE and WBE certification, mentoring, and joint venture matters, corporate formation, and financing.

Representing construction contractors, construction management firms and subcontractors in corporate, contract, finance and collection matters.

Working with producers, recording artists, managers, studios, promoters, songwriters, publishers and independent record labels in organizational matters, copyrights, contract negotiation and drafting.

Advising governmental entities, institutions, nonprofit and for-profit entities in formulating policies to encourage and sustain the growth and development of minority and women-owned businesses, including certification, monitoring and enforcement issues.

  • Commissioner

    Administrative Hearing Commission

    (Government Agency; Government Administration industry)

    August 2010 — Present (5 months)

    Missouri's Administrative Hearing Commission is a neutral, independent, administrative tribunal. Some of its cases are appeals from decisions of other State agencies. Others are cases that an agency or private person starts at the Administrative Hearing Commission. The Commission has jurisdiction in over 100 statutorily specified matters including State tax, professional licensing, Medicaid provider issues, Ethics Commission late filing fee assessments, Highways and Transportation Commission actions relating to railroads and motor carriers and disciplinary actions against State employees
