Robert Sorrentino’s Summary

Dr. Robert Sorrentino is a well-respected, M.I.T.-trained healthcare and biotechnology executive with more than 30 years expertise in the leadership and development of sophisticated managed care and information products organizations, strategic planning, operations management, and bioinformatics. He has a proven ability to deliver revenue growth through advances in new product development and concepts, contract negotiation and management, market development, and business expansion, combined with strong and sustainable cost controls. Dr. Sorrentino also brings broad consulting experience in healthcare, biotechnology, informatics and venture capital due diligence on behalf of a diverse clientele.

“My personal objective is to advance the Patient-centric Medical Home paradigm to improve the quality and lower the cost of healthcare in the US – using three decades of experience in business, biotechnology, informatics and clinical healthcare delivery – and help people enjoy a better life.”

Advances in healthcare technology and quality will center on approaches driven by three market forces:

• Public policy commitments to healthcare for all Americans, with improved treatments and outcomes, offering hope for a longer and better quality of life
• Consumer and societal demand for these advances, but at a price point that the public is able to afford, and willing to pay
• Financial opportunities for healthcare systems, providers, and employers that deliver on these promises at an affordable cost

There is a window of opportunity to transform prevailing "One-Size-Fits-All" medical delivery models into paradigms where patients and disease processes dictate the choices of therapy - with improved outcomes and reduced side effects. Cost-effectiveness will be attained as models based on coordinated, patient-centric healthcare delivery supersede episode-focused care, especially for chronic diseases representing a lion’s share of US healthcare costs.
