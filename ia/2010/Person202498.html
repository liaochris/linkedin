  • Chair

    eSSIP - Scandinavian Standards for Insurance and Pension

    (Insurance industry)

    August 2009 — Present (1 year 4 months)

    Companies, Agencies and all others that interchange data regarding insurance/pension or related, need to adapt a shared standard, based on International core components and processes in order to minimize cost.

    Not only will a standard help run the business – it also will be the tool to re-shape future business-processes and products.

    We are going to use the German model, and create a standardization organization independent from a specific branch – and that are owned by the members.
    In order to avoid branch dominance, and to get support from all shareholders, the “development”, need to be:
    - Democratic
    - Non-profit
    - Be owned by the members
    - Membership open to all interested, companies from all branches, service providers, organizations and governmental shareholders.

    Remember that developing a standard is a process for business-experts, and not for developers and designers – they are to tool !
    Ask yourself: “How can this help me re-shape my business – and not only as earlier, how can it help run my business”

  • Standardization Expert

    eEG7 - The e-Business Expert Group for Insurance

    (Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    April 2009 — Present (1 year 8 months)

    eEG7 is the European forum for the development of e-business standards for electronic communication in the insurance sector. The objective of eEG7 is to facilitate the transfer of information between policyholders, professional intermediaries (agents, brokers), insurers and other involved parties. eEG7 standards support the placing and administration of insurance contracts, claims handling and accounting.

    eEG7 is one of the e-business Board for European Standardization (eBES) groups. As a consequence eEG7 restricts its activities to serve the European insurance sector and to represent it at the global standards level.

    eEG7 is a forum open to

    representatives from insurers associations and intermediaries associations,
    experts from individual insurance companies, and
    members of organizations rendering services to the insurance sector.
    The open structure enables the participation of business orientated analysts and e-business professionals gathering the knowledge and requirements of all countries represented in eEG7.
