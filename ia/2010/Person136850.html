Matt Culbertson’s Summary

I’m a senior studying digital media and public relations at ASU's Walter Cronkite School of Journalism and Mass Communication. My 3+ years of work experience in media and public relations includes six professional internships, including government and agency PR. My reporting experience spans seven major publications as a full-time staff reporter, freelancer and columnist.

I'm currently serving as president of the Arizona State chapter of the Public Relations Student Society of America, and I intern at Allison & Partners (www.allisonpr.com).

On my first day as a college freshman, I had a front-page article about a campus protest in The State Press, the ASU student newspaper. Since then, I’ve authored more than 300 articles for media like the Phoenix Business Journal and Pain Solutions Magazine, a national magazine with a circulation of 200,000.

One story I wrote and photographed—a police-involved traffic collision—had the fifth-most page views for all of 2008 on azcentral.com, the most-trafficked Arizona news website of the (at the time) 10th largest U.S. newspaper. I’ve covered business, public safety, health and government at the local and national level. I've done more than 1,000 interviews of people from across society, including politicians, drug dealers, medical researchers and law enforcement.

I've observed firsthand how the media landscape is evolving — it's fascinated me since running my high school paper as co-Editor.

I transitioned from journalism to public relations in August 2009, when I began interning at the Maricopa Association of Governments. At MAG, I was involved in almost every aspect of the regional planning agency's public relations arm, including drafting and formally presenting a social media policy that was approved by elected officials.

This summer, I studied abroad in three European countries and visited six others. My two-month trip included France, Italy, Northern Ireland, England and Germany.

Media relations, business reporting, government & private sector communication, social media campaigns, B2B and B2C, medical reporting, investigative journalism, breaking news, competitor research, public records, blogging and new media.
