Alfonso del Granado’s Summary

With more than a decade of management experience at healthcare organizations large and small, I've had to continually learn and adapt to an ever-changing landscape. Medicine is evolving at an increasing rate, and the days of Marcus Welby are long gone. Even solo practictioners have to embrace modern strategies as they fight to carve out little corners of the world for themselves.

My expertise with technology has allowed me to keep my employers and clients on the cutting edge as we strive to create and grow new business models. At the same time, my medical background faciltates the translation of these strategies into modes that are understandable and acceptable to medical practitioners.

Technology has also made it easier for me to stay current with a wide range of interests and specialties (see below), which in turn I can bring to bear on my professional life. I refer to people with this combination of breadth and depth of knowledge as "Super Generalists," although Thomas L. Friedman in "The World Is Flat" calls us "Versatilists" – and of course long ago we were termed "Renaissance Men" or "Polymaths."

Whatever the rose's name, the sweet smell is of success. And lest I be accused of false immodesty, let me conclude simply with this: even the most skeptical clients and employers have been surprised to discover that I actually tend toward understatement...
