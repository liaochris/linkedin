Lisa Laurel Weinberg’s Summary

I have been an attorney since 1992. I was a civil legal aid lawyer until 2001 when I began doing political asylum law. I have been representing asylum seekers since 2001. I also represent a handful of Human Trafficking victims. In 2005 was a pro bono legal advisor for six months at the African and Middle East Legal Assistance project (AMERA) in Cairo, Egypt. In 2008 I worked at WARIPNET in Senegal for six weeks and did a country assessment of the situation of asylum seekers in Senegal and developed a training for legal professionals to represent asylum seekers in West Africa for Asylum Access in San Francisco and AMERA -UK. In 2008, I founded the LGBT Human Rights Protection Project at Lutheran Social Services in Worcester, Massachusetts. I am a volunteer legal advisor with Asylum Access and I am Co-Chair of the American Bar Association Refugee Law Committee of the International Section.

Lisa Laurel Weinberg’s Specialties:

LGBT political asylum cases, gender based asylum cases, and representing torture survivors
