Alejandro Badia, MD’s Summary

Alejandro Badia, MD, FACS is a hand and upper extremity surgeon expert who was educated in Physiology at Cornell University and obtained his medical degree at NYU. He trained in NYC, undertook a hand fellowship at Alleghany General Hospital in Pittsburgh, and followed that with an AO trauma fellowship in Freiburg,Germany.

He is the Chief of Hand Surgery at Baptist Hospital in Miami and a member of the ASSH, AAHS, AAOS as well as honorary member of many foreign hand surgery societies.

He leads an active international hand fellowship, serves on the editorial board of two hand journals, and organizes a yearly Miami meeting for surgeons/therapists that is devoted to upper limb arthroscopy and arthroplasty.

In 2008, he completed the Badia Hand to Shoulder Center, a fully integrated clinical facility for the upper limb encompassing digital radiography, MRI extremity imaging, Integra rehabilitation facility, OrthoNow walk in medical center and the Surgery Center at Doral.

He can be easily reached via www.drbadia.com, a patient education portal and website for hand surgeon academic exchange, or via (305) 227-HAND at the Badia Hand to ShoulderCenter.

Created world class center for upper limb surgery including surgery center and therapy unit. Goal is to provide tertiary care to patients from all western hemisphere, particularly countries with less hand surgery accessibility

  • Founder and Course Chairman

    Miami Hand Reconstruction Course

    (Medical Practice industry)

    2005 — Present (5 years )

    The Miami Hand Reconstruction pre-AAOS Course is an annual conference focused solely on techniques for joint reconstruction in the hand and upper extremity. The goal is to present advances and current techniques in the areas of arthroscopy, implant and soft tissue arthroplasty of the upper limb. The meeting is held just before the annual AAOS meeting in order to encourage international participation, both faculty and participant. Miami represents a warm and cosmopolitan destination that allows easy layover access from travel abroad. This environment encourages a dynamic course where participants will interact with the world-class faculty in an equally stimulating social program.

    The Miami Hand Reconstruction Course focuses on anatomy as related to hand therapy practice. The two-day course discusses the anatomic basis for selected examination and intervention techniques. Demonstrations of key points are illustrated through cadaver dissection. This will give the novice and experienced clinician the ability to apply foundations of anatomic relationships to clinical practice.

    The objective of the Miami Hand Reconstruction Course is for participants to be able to:

    1. Review indications for arthroscopy in the hand and upper extremity
    2. Discuss arthroplasty options for all upper limb joints
    3. Gain in-depth understanding of certain specific prostheses from PIP joint to Elbow
    4. Improve technical understanding of how to perform particular joint replacements

    People who benefit from attending the Miami Hand Course are:

    • Hand and Upper Extremity Surgeons
    • Orthopedic Surgeons
    • Hand Therapists
    • Interested OT and PTs
    • Medical Students/ Residents/ Fellows

    For the first time in five years, the Miami Hand Reconstruction Course will allow therapists to have the opportunity to do cadaver training at the Miami Anatomical Research Center (M.A.R.C.) with Dr. Sue Michlovitz.
