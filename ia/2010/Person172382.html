Richard Lewis’s Summary

Currently:
* With the economy and credit markets being as they are, I am utilizing my 30+ years of business and financial leadership and expertise to work with small and mid-size companies. I have been successful, see recommendations, in assisting several to obtain and meet their working capital requirements. While I concentrate primarily on companies in the government sector, B2G, it's not to the exclusion of commercial, B2B, entities.

Experience:
* Wells Fargo Bank, Government Services Group, where assisted fast track companies and government contractors; 8a, woman, service disabled veteran, Native American, and other minority owned or disadvantaged companies that required financial support.

* 1990 to 2003 - CFO/CEO counselor to start up, fast track technology and service businesses needing assistance in financing alternatives; bank relations, strategic planning, IPOs, mergers and acquisitions, accounting, and marketing.

* As CEO/CFO, l985-l990, initiated a "business plan only" Initial Public Offering for Virtusonics Corporation, a software development company, some 10 years ahead of the "dot.com" trend of the l990’s.

* Began career in banking with A.J. Armstrong & Co. (ne: Bank of America Business Credit) in 1974. Moved to The Chase Manhattan Bank as Vice President, Leveraged Acquisition Team, to start its leverage buyout group in 1980, to General Electric Capital Corporation, as a marketing manager, to help start an ABL financing vertical in the Retailer Finance Division.

* B.S. Degree in General Business and a MBA in Finance and International Business from New York University, Stern School of Business, as a scholarship student. Live and work in suburban Washington, D.C. Active in golf, bicycling, minority business support, community activities, and governmental issues.

* A USNR, Vietnam Era veteran.
