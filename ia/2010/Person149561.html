Roger Davis’s Summary

Mobile video and media player Product Line Architect with over 25 years experience in the automotive industry. Currently responsible for future planning and design direction for the in-car high-end entertainment systems used by OEM's. Systems include rear seat entertainment, front seat media players, systems with dual view monitors, touchscreens, over-the-air video reception (ATSC M/H, MediaFLO, ICO, DVB-T/S, etc.) and bringing the internet to the car. Experience with issues related to graphical user interfaces (GUI), video distribution in the car, digital rights management (DRM), content protection, and connectivity to consumer devices.

My responsibilities place me in frequent contact with OEM engineers and buyers as we quote and co-develop in-car entertainment systems. On the other end, I have frequent contact with suppliers as I evaluate alternative competitive solutions.

Since 2001 I have been responsible for developing then staffing at least one new technology installed in an automobile displayed at the International Consumer Electronics Show in Las Vegas.

Experience in many other facets of the automotive electronics industry including Engineering Group Manager for the electrical engineers designing audio systems for global customers including GM, Toyota, and Ford.

BS EET from Purdue in 1984 and MBA from Ball State University in 1995.

Member of Purdue EcET Industrial Advisory Board for over 14 years. Have taught Purdue electronics courses as adjunct faculty. Currently operate my own photography business.

Electrical design, engineering supervisor, adv. engineering, supplier selection, customer quoting, systems design, technical problem resolution, project management.
