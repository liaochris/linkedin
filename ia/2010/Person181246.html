Sam Katz’s Summary

My two passions in life are sports and law. I attended UC Irvine, and was personally recommended by the Dean of the Law School, Erwin Chemerinsky.

I created Scouting The Sports to help me introduce my new baseball scouting approach to the sports world. My book became a major hit online as the hits multiplied exponentially and today we are one of the premier websites for professional Athlete Blogs.

The success of Scouting The Sports translated into an internship opportunity with Athletes First LLC, one of the most prestigious sports agencies in the NFL. I learned from the best sports agents in the industry. Whether I was researching NFL contracts, creating new marketing opportunities, organizing an athlete clients’ busy schedule, or taking notes in my weekly collective bargaining agreement class taught by the agents, I always had something challenging in front of me.

When the college football season ended, Athletes First had their 30 rookie clients live in Irvine for the four months prior to the NFL draft. The Athletes all lived together while training at our workout facility. I became close with this group of players after moving in with Brooks Foster (Currently with the St. Louis Rams).

My relationships with the players I lived with blossomed into friendships I hold dear. It made me privy to a world of information about professional athletes to which most people lack access.

With all of the legal troubles that athletes have to deal with in today’s celebrity obsessed society, I want to focus my career on reducing the number of problems that arise more and more frequently for these professionals. My reverence for the legal system stems from my admiration for my law professors and from my personal experiences with professionals currently in the field of sports law. I would be a great fit in this field because it requires quick thinking, diligence, contacts within the industry, and a passion for sports.
