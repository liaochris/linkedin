Joe Borgstrom’s Summary

Joe Borgstrom is the Director of the Specialized Technical Assistance and Revitalization Strategy (STARS) Division for the Michigan State Housing Development Authority (MSHDA).

Mr. Borgstrom was an original member of the Michigan Economic Development Corporation’s Community Assistance Team when it was created in 2001. While there, he worked with over 60 communities on a range of downtown and Brownfield projects. Also during that time he played a critical role in the creation of the Michigan Main Street program, now housed at MSHDA. Borgstrom left the CATeam in 2003 to become the first Director of Economic Development at the Shiawassee Regional Chamber of Commerce. Two years later, he became President/CEO of the Chamber. While President/CEO, the Chamber introduced several new programs and was the first Chamber in Michigan to receive the prestigious “5-Star Accreditation” from the United States Chamber of Commerce. He holds a Bachelor of Business Administration from the University of Michigan-Flint and is a certified Economic Development Finance Professional.

The STARS Division includes the Michigan Main Street Center and Pure Michigan Living units. The Michigan Main Street Center @ MSHDA provides technical assistance to communities who seek to revitalize their downtowns using the National Trust for Historic Preservation’s Main Street Four-Point™ Approach. The program currently assists 36 communities. The Pure Michigan Living unit exists to promote Michigan as a quality place for people to live, work and play. It provides a centralized website as well as technical assistance to communities who want to use social media as a way to promote their communities.
