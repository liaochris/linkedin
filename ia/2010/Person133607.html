Mark Minie’s Summary

Consultant, Researcher, Advisor, Educator and Communicator. Doctoral level scientist and bioinformatics expert with 15+ years industrial and academic experience. Research experience in gene structure and expression at UC Berkeley as a graduate student and as a post doctoral fellow at the NIH. Industrial experience at two biotech firms, along with work in IT at Microsoft and Corbis. Experienced educator and consultant in the fields of molecular and cellular biology and bioinformatics at the University of Washington (UW) and the National Center for Biotechnology Information (NCBI). Specializes in online and distance learning. Seasoned communicator who has worked with individuals at all levels. Public presentations to scientific and lay audiences and work with prominent science fiction writer on hard science fiction “bio-thriller” novels (Greg Bear-Darwin’s Radio and Darwin’s Children). Excels at explaining complicated scientific facts and concepts to non-scientists, especially bioscience subject matter areas such as genomics, gene annotation, synthetic biology and medical genetics. Available for consulting or perm hire opportunities, with a fully equipped home office for telecommuting and can travel. Recently become a 23andMe subscriber and user of that site’s data and tools.

Mark Minie’s Specialties:

Online learning, educational video production, training and education in introductory to advanced biosciences, bioinformatics, genomics, imaging, animation, molecular and cellular biology, biological engineering, synthetic biology, web programming, SQL database design, software design, website design and development, (Subject Matter Expert) SME for developers, finance, business development
