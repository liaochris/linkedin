    (Investment Management industry)

Covestor's trade replication platform has so far executed over $100M worth of stock trades. I love this company and miss all the great people who work here.

  • Investment Associate

    EARNEST Partners

    (Privately Held; Investment Management industry)

    May 2006 — August 2006 (4 months)

    For my Harvard Business School summer internship, I decided to learn about a new industry and joined Atlanta-based investment firm EARNEST Partners. EARNEST Partners is a bottom-up, long-only firm (40 people) which manages $25B primarily for institutional investors. I made investment recommendations on public technology companies and influenced $30M in invested capital. I thank my colleagues here for giving me an opportunity to explore a new industry while working alongside some great people. My experience here formed the basis of my work at Covestor.

  • Senior Consultant

I worked with some great, hardworking folks at Manhattan Associates and continued to leverage my RFID expertise in a senior role. We pushed forward a vision, developed new ideas, and learned about the world together. My time here was cut short because of my acceptance to Harvard Business School.

  • Business Analyst, Technology Architect

    Accenture

    (Public Company; ACN; Management Consulting industry)

    August 2003 — October 2004 (1 year 3 months)

    After MIT, I chose to work with Accenture because it was a major sponsor of my research lab. Here, I was exposed to numerous large manufacturers and senior executives to develop an RFID strategy, perform cost-benefit analysis, and identify RFID opportunities beyond retailer and Department of Defense mandate compliance. I was also the lead inventor on a concept that Accenture and our client at the time Wrigley jointly patented.

  • Research Associate

    MIT AUTO-ID Center

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    August 2001 — June 2003 (1 year 11 months)

    For my master's thesis, I collaborated with senior MIT researchers to define the standards governing usage of the next generation barcode. I personally witnessed my thesis advisor, Sanjay Sarma, take this lab from nothing to having over $20M in funding with over 100 sponsors, including corporate titans like WalMart and Best Buy. This phase marked the beginning of a wonderful personal growth period that helped me realize I can achieve the seemingly impossible.

  • Research Intern

    Sun Microsystems Research Labs

    (Public Company; ORCL; Information Technology and Services industry)

    May 2002 — January 2003 (9 months)

    As part of my master's thesis, this was my first opportunity to work in a corporate sponsored research lab. I was fortunate to be alongside industry veterans. We conceived and prototyped Sun's first RFID product, a business appliance solution that had tremendous long-term revenue generation potential in e-business Supply Chain Management applications. I also obtained my first patent here.

  • Founder, Software Architect

    Isovia, Acquired by J.P. Mobile

    (Wireless industry)

    January 2000 — June 2001 (1 year 6 months)

    While a full time Junior at MIT, 6 friends and I dove headfirst into the world of entrepreneurship and started Isovia. My role was that of a developer, and I created mobile application prototypes to help secure $2.5 million in funding by ITU Ventures and angel investors. Isovia was eventually acquired by JP Mobile (MOT) and I returned to MIT to start my Master's work in the RFID space.
