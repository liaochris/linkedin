Neil Tinson’s Summary

At Guild of Sage & Smith we believe design can help to overcome any challenge; be it commercial, social or environmental. By working collectively and uniting discovery with craftsmanship, we help astute clients to frame problems and devise solutions.

We approach every project with an open mind, so wherever possible the end result emerges naturally from a rigorous design process. We have worked for clients including Conflicts Forum, Cornwall Council, Dyson, Faber and Faber, Hidden Art Cornwall, Hoipolloi, Labour Middle East Council, Oasis College, Plymouth Hospitals NHS Trust, Royal Albert Memorial Museum, RIBA Enterprises and Theatre Royal Plymouth.

Every project follows a similar process to make sure we not only solve a client’s problem, but fully understand it in the first place. We balance discovery (uncovering issues and exploring new areas of knowledge by observing, liaising and facilitating) with craftsmanship (creating ‘things’ – virtual or physical), to produce both meaningful and beautiful work with people at its centre.

Guild of Sage & Smith is built entirely around a collaborative model, working with a diverse team of like-minded, motivated and committed associates plus an outstanding network of delivery partners. Project teams are structured so clients get to talk directly with the people involved (no ‘middlemen’ account handlers here). Clients also enjoy the reassurance of working with a single practice that takes full responsibility for delivering projects on time, and on budget.

Our growing roster of associates currently includes consultants working in digital, innovation, advertising, marketing and business psychology; as well as designers in the fields of communication, 3D and architecture.

Neil Tinson’s Specialties:

We don’t label ourselves specifically as ‘graphic’ designers, or ‘web’ designers, or ‘interior’ designers… simply because different problems get solved in different ways.

Our solutions have included brands, printed material, interactive experiences, products and physical spaces.

  • Chair

    Cornwall Design Forum

    (Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    2008 — 2010 (2 years )

    As voluntary Chair of the Cornwall Design Forum, my role was to strengthen and develop this non-profit organisation, founded in 2004, into a sustainable model that could fully serve, promote and encourage Cornwall's growing design industry.

    During my tenure the Forum's vision and purpose was redefined; our committee grew and represented a wider diversity of design disciplines; all administrative processes were redesigned to be more effective and more efficient; a new members' benefits package was devised; a programme of regular events started; and links with local, regional and national bodies were enhanced.

    Over two years we hosted a range of great inspirational speakers, CPD courses and social events; and our national reputation grew among the UK's design industry and press. I represented Cornwall's design community within the public sector on many occasions - both regionally and nationally - and the CDF now benefits from ongoing investment and support from both Cornwall Council and Creative Skills.
