Mark Hamill’s Summary

Partner in the Litigation and Antitrust & Competition groups at Kirkland & Ellis LLP focusing on litigation and investigations arising out of alleged financial wrongdoing, accounting improprieties, audit failures, and antitrust violations.

Mark Hamill’s Specialties:

Accounting malpractice, GAAP, PCAOB auditing standards (formerly, GAAS); SEC, DOJ, and FTC investigations and inquiries; securities fraud; and antitrust.

Mark Hamill’s Experience

  • Partner

    Kirkland & Ellis LLP

    (Partnership; Law Practice industry)

    November 2004 — Present (5 years 5 months)

    Accounting firm defense in U.S. District and Bankruptcy Courts, State Courts, and State CPA Societies in disputes challenging the propriety of financial statements and alleging failure to comply with auditing standards.

    Post-closing purchase price adjustment arbitrations involving accounting for inventory, receivables, and accrued expenses.

    Internal investigations addressing Foreign Corrupt Practices Act compliance by the European subsidiaries of a U.S. company in the medical devices industry, stock-options backdating, pension accounting, and inventory accounting.

    Represented surety in action against reinsurers to recover amounts paid on guarantees of energy delivery contracts for Enron Corp. Drafted arbitration briefs.

  • Associate

    Represented hospital in FTC retrospective analysis of hospital mergers. Responded to document and information requests. FTC ultimately did not challenge the client’s transaction and instead elected to challenge a different merger of two nearby hospitals.

    Represented printing equipment manufacturer in merger clearance. Attended FTC deposition of client management and prepared schedules for Hart-Scott-Rodino filings.
