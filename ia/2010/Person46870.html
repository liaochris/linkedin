Daniel Hohler’s Summary

Since May 2009, I have been a full time graduate student and Business Law & Ethics Fellow at Suffolk University - Sawyer School of Management. I completed my MBA in August 2010.

My professional legal expereience is broad as a civil litigator in Construction, Business, Insurance Defense and Securities litigation. Until April 2009, I was a Senior Litigation Associate at Kenney & Sams, P.C. where I handled business, construction and probate litigation matters. Prior to joining K&S, I ran my own general law practice from (September 2004 - January 2007). Before starting my own practice, I was an associate at the Boston boutique firm Gelb & Gelb LLP (September 2000 - August 2004) where I concentrated my practice in business litigation and securities arbitration. Before that, I was an associate at Gadsby Hannah LLP's Boston office (now combined with McCarter & English) in its construction and litigation departments (April 1997 through August 2000). I am admitted to practice in the Commonwealth of Massachusetts, the State of New York, the First Circuit Court of Appeals, the Eleventh Circuit Court of Appeals, and the United States District Court for the District of Massachusetts. I have lectured in the area of construction law, co-published in MCLE, and co-authored and authored articles on various topics in Massachusetts Lawyers Weekly, the Boston Law Tribune and Securities Arbitration Commentator.

I received my J.D. from Suffolk University Law School, cum laude, in 1996, where I received an American Jurisprudence Award in Property. While in law school, I was a member of the Moot Court Board, and an author and inaugural editor-in-chief of the Suffolk Journal of Trial And Appellate Advocacy. I received my B.A. from the University of Massachusetts at Amherst, cum laude, in 1993 with a double major of Legal Studies and Psychology. I was inducted into Phi Beta Kappa in 1993.
