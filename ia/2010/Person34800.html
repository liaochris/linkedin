Cédric Laurant’s Summary

Cédric Laurant is an independent privacy consultant based in Brussels and a Senior Research Fellow with the Central European University (Budapest). He is working on projects in the areas of data protection, privacy, information security, Internet law and policy, and telecommunications.

His work concentrates on international privacy issues and comparative policy and legal aspects of European, US and Latin American privacy and data protection regimes, the impact of new technologies on users' privacy, consumer protection, cybercrime and transborder data flows in the world.

He is an experienced multilingual public speaker, who has talked at more than 85 conferences on these topics in Europe, the United States, Latin America, Canada, Africa and Oceania.

Until 2008, he was a Visiting Law Professor at the University of los Andes in Bogota (Colombia), teaching privacy law, comparative law and the fundamentals of American law, investigating in the field of privacy, data protection and Internet law, and presenting conferences in this field throughout Colombia. He is currently writing a book about the new challenges for privacy and data protection in Latin America.

He was the Research Director and Principal Editor of EPIC's annual report "Privacy and Human Rights" in 2003, 2004 and 2005.

He is an attorney, member of the District of Columbia Bar (US) and a graduate of the University of Louvain (Belgium), with a Master of Laws from Columbia Law School (New York).

Expert in information and Internet public policy, with experience in government affairs and public advocacy on online privacy and consumer protection matters, he has worked and interacted with non-governmental organizations and human rights groups, government agencies, international organizations, academic institutions, multinational corporations, consumer groups and the media.

Trilingual in French, English and Spanish, with a working knowledge of Portuguese and proficient in Dutch.
