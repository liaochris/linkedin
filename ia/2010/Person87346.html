Jaime Jurado’s Summary

One can't complain about life when you have the team of colleagues that I have, who manage the breweries and QA departments at our regional breweries, and the corporate-level colleagues I have as our staff Physical Chemist and staff Brewery Engineer. At the plants themselves, we have thoughtful, committed employees with science and brewing formal qualifications. Add in my peers in sales, marketing, legal and accounting, as well as order fulfilment/scheduling...and it's a good orchestra.

I respond to the commercial needs of our organization, take direction from our owner and company President, and balance in objectives which those of us who are technical professionals bring to the table. Resources being ever finite, we work as a good team to prioritize Engineering Purchase Requisitions, safety initaitives, productivity improvements, plant expansions, as well as work through analyses created by accounting professionals or develop some for all of us.

Jaime Jurado’s Specialties:

Plant and sytem optimization, using the engineering, brewing and math-based toolboxes.

Jaime Jurado’s Experience

  • Director, Brewing Operations

    The Gambrinus Company

    (Privately Held; Food & Beverages industry)

    April 1997 — Present (13 years 10 months)

    Senior managers at each brewery report to me, as do two technical staff based in the company HQ. Tess, our Controller, is a great assist in fiscal data provision and analyses of production costs. We formalize production protocols and procedures in our office and develop new production-related systems collaboratively with the plants to support new beers, manage some engineering projects as well as control system/plc architecture (this is my colleague Peter's arena), product development (a role shared with the breweries and sometimes driven by them at the brewery-level but supported by our HQ team). Contract negotiation for some materials/procurement, technical planning involving plant expansions and upgrades, new facilities infrastructure and plant staff resource utilization. I work in conjunction with the sales and marketing departments, too. Research and Development represents a small component of what we do; we have a 30 bbl development brewery in town.

    I report to the President, but also must answer to our Treasurer, CFO, and Corporate Counsel. Much of my work is financial-technical analyses to a level where the M.B.A.'s pick it up and put it to the additional tests and sensitivity analyses.

  • Manager, Brewing Development

    Stroh Brewery Co.

    (Privately Held; 1001-5000 employees; Food & Beverages industry)

    1994 — 1997 (3 years )

    A technical Cog in the machine! There are reasons why a large organization needs a top-down approach, which is why my peers and I had specific rules of engagements in the plants. I stayed for 3 years and had to move on...yet I learned an extraordinary amount in that short time. Being in a place that is top-down in management allows you to learn proven systems of how to progress things.

  • Special Projects Manager

    Courage Brewing Ltd.

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; Food & Beverages industry)

    1991 — 1993 (2 years )

    What a terrific job! I focused on CIP improvement and upgrading across what was then Europe's single largest brewery. Objectives were to increase quality of cleans, quantified by Simon Loseby's work in the micro lab, to reduce overall cycle time, reduce water consumption, and most importnatly...reduce consumption of cleaning chemicals. I worked for a fair boss, Nick Bott. I reported to Australians assigned to manage the place from then-owner Foster's Brewing Group. And I enjoyed working with three fine Australian leaders who worked in the brewery...as well as a good group of Britsh brewing mangers.

  • Master Brewer / Brewmaster

    The Lion, Inc. Brewery

    (Food & Beverages industry)

    1985 — 1991 (6 years )

    Union brewery, too few managers, old hardware and insufficent cap ex and mainenance, but some bits of innovation to support malt-based coolers, soft drinks, etc. Solid beers.
