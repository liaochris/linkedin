Jon Reed’s Summary

Jon Reed is an independent SAP analyst and SAP Mentor who writes on SAP consulting trends. He is the President of JonERP.com, an interactive Web site that features Jon’s SAP Career Blog and his podcasts for SAP professionals. Jon has been publishing SAP career and market analysis for more than a decade, and he serves as the career expert for SearchSAP’s “Ask the Expert” panel. Jon is the author of the SAP Consultant Handbook. Recently, Jon was named as a Top Contributor for the SAP Community Network 2008-2009 in the Business Process Expert Category.Jon provides consultations on talent management and marketing strategy to third party SAP services and software firms.

From 2003 to 2006, Jon was Vice President and Founding Editor of SAPtips. Jon is also the author of Resumes from Hell, a humorous “how-not-to” of the worst resumes ever created. Jon is planning on publishing additional SAP book titles through his company, including a companion to his original SAP Consultant Handbook.

Jon’s latest venture, JonERP.com, is a resource-rich web site that monitors SAP consulting trends and, as per its tagline, provides “Career Answers for SAP Professionals.” JonERP.com provides a forum for SAP professionals to learn how SAP’s technical evolution will impact their SAP career choices. JonERP.com also provides guidance for SAP project managers on how to approach the “human side of SAP implementations.”

Through the JonERP.com “SAP Career Blog,” SAP professionals can pose career questions to Jon across functional and technical skills categories. JonERP.com also features podcasts with industry insiders, where Jon and his guests examine the “human side of SAP implementations” and identify the skills necessary to implement the latest SAP technology.

Jon was recently named an SAP Mentor, a highly selective program which recognizes those individuals who are making an outstanding contribution to the SAP community.

I billed more than $1,000,000 in recruiting revenues during my years with SoftwareJobs.com, and the writing I did for the SoftwareJobs.com web site became the basis for the SAP Consultant Handbook.
