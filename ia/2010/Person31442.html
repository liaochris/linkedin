Calla Bedda (Papademas)’s Summary

I am a Board of Behavioral Sciences Registered Marriage and Family Therapy Intern planning to test for professional licensure in the Fall of 2009.

In my first Internship I led Certified Batterers Intervention groups of men and women with convictions for Domestic Violence; I coordinated the Child Socialization Program; I ran High Conflict Parenting Groups and Co-Parenting sessions between divorced or separated couples in custody disputes. Also, I saw individual clients.

My next placement was as a school counselor in socio-economically disadvantaged East Palo Alto. My work there was largely with substance-abusing and gang-involved youth.

Next I began working with foster children and families in Oakland and became skilled in working with pregnant and runaway youth. That agency later offered me a position running a program which works with children disabled by conditions such as Autism, Cerebral Palsy and/ or Mental Retardation. I hired and trained staff to assist the families of these children in managing their behaviors so that the children would not have to be placed in residential settings.

In my next placement I became a local expert in conducting Trauma Focused Cognitive Behavioral Therapy with children who were abused or otherwise traumatized. At the same time I worked with their care givers and other collateral sources to affect change in the domains of education, vocation, medical health and housing.

Most recently I worked for the City and County of San Francisco as a Protective Services Worker. At this position my duties were to serve as the Legal Guardian for up to twenty children while providing case management and coordination to their parents as these adults worked their Family Maintenance or Family Reunification plans. Also, I investigated and responded to new allegations of Child Abuse and/ or Neglect and frequently wrote court reports and testified in Dependency Court as to my Department's recommendations on various case matters.

Calla Bedda (Papademas)’s Specialties:

Populations served: convicted batterers (group and individual work) ; children (groups, dyads and individuals); parenting groups and couples; gang-involved youth; pregnant youth; runaway youth; foster care; developmentally disabled; Autistic Spectrum Disorders; traumatized children and adults; suicidal individuals, homicidal individuals and others requiring 5150 (involuntary, crisis hospitalizations).
