Robert Niles’s Summary

How can you, or your news staff, make the connections with your community that will help grow your audience and increase your customer base, whether you are an established news site or a start-up?

Fortunately, there is an answer - and veteran reporter, entrepreneur and journalism instructor Robert Niles can show you the way to find it.

Robert applies the well-established principles of community organizing to show aspiring journalist entrepreneurs, as well as newsroom staff and managers, how to make those all-important connections with the communities that they cover.

These principles can help a news publication expand its audience, improve its editorial coverage, recruit and retain advertisers, and secure and expand outside funding.

Robert has conducted training for the Knight Digital Media Center, WAN-IFRA, the American Press Institute, the Orlando Sentinel and other news organizations. He also provides training on the use of math in news reporting, and has spoken on that topic to the American Copy Editors Society and the Association of Capitol Reporters and Editors.

Robert's ThemeParkInsider.com has been named the top theme park site on the Internet by Forbes and Travel + Leisure magazines and has been a finalist for the Webby Award for best overall Guide/Ratings/Reviews site. In 2001, Theme Park Insider became the first website to win a major journalism award for a "citizen journalism" report when the Columbia Graduate School of Journalism and Online News Association honored Theme Park Insider's Accident Watch with the Online Journalism Award for Service Journalism.

Columnist Dan Gillmor called Robert's ThemeParkInsider.com "pathbreaking." New York University's Jay Rosen called Robert "a shrewd observer of online journalism" and Peter Preston of The Guardian newspaper in the United Kingdom called Robert "one of America's top media academics" for his work at the University of Southern California's Online Journalism Review.
