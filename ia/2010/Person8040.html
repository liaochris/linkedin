Allen Wyke’s Summary

Turnaround or critical launch technologist available for onsite work in New York City area, Philadelphia, Atlanta, Washington DC, Boston and Charlotte.

Since 1995 I have worked for Internet technology companies. Unlike many technology executives making the leap from Enterprise to online, my entire career has been in the interactive Web space making it easy for me to assess situations, plan and execute. Generally brought in for quick movement: turn around, rebuild, transition, or startup. But not just a technologist - applying technology to drive business and revenue is what excites me.

Worked for software and media companies to build and run development, QA, documentation, technical support, professional services, project and product management, and information systems teams. Worked with or for Microsoft, Lycos, c|net, Ask, McAfee, Sony Online, Modiv Media, Zumobi, Ringleader Digital, Global Grind, Hearst, RadarOnline, and approximately 100 others.

Experience includes mergers and acquisitions, mobile advertising, online/interactive media, content management systems, open source and commercial software, Web/Internet standards, online communities, SEO, and nimble ways to execute with immediate impact - most times well under budget. Very hands on in defining and driving product and technology strategies and execution.

Standards participation include co-founding and initially chairing the Emergency Management Technical Committee at OASIS, participating on P3P Working Group at W3C and CPExchange at IDEAlliance, and working on early online advertising standards effort (adXML). Written over a dozen books, numerous articles, and a training video on standards, Internet technologies, and programming languages such as XML, PHP, JavaScript, .NET, and Perl. Most recent authoring is on open source CMS and using Mac OS X for small/home office.

Often brought in by a CEO or COO and available as a consultant or a full time position if the circumstances are right.

Allen Wyke’s Specialties:

Technology strategies, product strategies, contract negotiations, staffing strategies, building engineering teams, mergers and acquisitions, mobile advertising, online/interactive media, content management systems, open source, commercial software, Web/Internet standards.
