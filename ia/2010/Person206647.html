  • Vice President

    Ohio GATE Advisory Council

    (Utilities industry)

    April 2006 — January 2010 (3 years 10 months)

    The Ohio GATE Advisory Council exists to work with Ohio's regulated utilities to ensure the inclusion of minority, women-owned, and service disabled veterans in the procurement process. Ohio GATE was founded in June of 2005, as an initiative by Commissioner Emeritus Clarence D. Rogers, Jr. at the Public Utilities Commission of Ohio. My role included development of the concept for the Council, researching the supplier diversity programs at all regulated utility companies, studying the "best practices" of other like advisory councils across the country, and ultimately working to establish the Council as a 501(c)3 organization made up of supplier diversity managers, government affairs managers, and advocacy partners which represent the interest of the diverse suppliers. I also served as the liaison between the PUCO and Ohio GATE. Upon retirement from state government, I was able to take on the role of Vice President for the organization.
