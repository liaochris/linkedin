Craig Keefner’s Summary

Working in self-service, wireless and kiosk technology since 1988. Started first industry website for kiosk industry (kiosks.org) and founded the self-service association (now SSKA). Original moderator of usenet group comp.infosystems.kiosks.

Self-service started with Gift Certificate Center in Mn. Then as contractor for IBM evaluating GIS geospatial systems (ESRI) and first line support for IBM PC Radio (laptop using Ardis). Worked for Art Lazere and Northgate. Irwin Jacobs & internet auction site. Northwest Airlines in web development.

Currently working for KIOSK in multiple management role including IT, Internet and Sales/Pre-Sales. Lead management.

Most often characterized as a wide technologist - internet, hardware technology, sales process automation, self-service automation software and hardware, internet search engines, developing new hardware and software technology for use in our industry, and working with clients and sales people for best practice. Quote systems for customers and for reps.

Market, information, intelligence gathering and statistics research (current and archived) skills including what can be called "black ops".

Large client prospecting and identifying emerging markets and opportunities is another skill.

Serve as the PCI compliance expert in my current company.

Large client engineering & technical support during proof of concepts and trials assisting with configuration, development tools/assistance. I work directly with technical counterparts on client side.

Manage and develop software application partners as well as evaluate and recommend new hardware and devices for use in self-service terminals. Close to 50 application partners. Hardware expertise in embedded systems and mini systems (down to pico/POE).

Assets include one of oldest/largest personal and electronic networks in self-service, kiosks and now thin clients. I maintain and encourage relationships with complementary and competing companies.
