Steven Ellis’s Summary

Over 20 years in the game industry with over 10 years in the interactive game industry with companies like GT Interactive, Atari, Destineer and WizardWorks. I have over 30 titles to my credit on multiple platforms and genres.

My responsibilities were as a Project Coordinator, Producer and Executive/Senior Producer. Worked almost exclusively on the publishing side of the business with some development studio experience. I have worked on everything from casual games to AAA titles to classified military tactical training software. I even dabbled in licensing acquisition.

I also worked in the tabletop game industry at Things for Thinkers (Tucson, AZ), Attactix (Aurora, CO) and Wargames West Distribution (Albuquerque, NM). I cut my gaming teeth and design skills on board war games, miniatures games and RPGs. As a 12 year-old I designed my first game- a Vietnam War RPG (which I still have laying around waiting for a complete revision).

Currently I am full-time faculty and academics advisor in the Game Design Bachelor's program at Brown College. I also do consulting on historical or military-themed projects within the game industry. The years spent playing both interactive and tabletop games has made me a better designer and I make both mediums part of my design classes.

Steven Ellis’s Specialties:

I have managed large and small budget development projects, licensing acquistions, game design and conceptual work along with acting as a subject matter expert and consultant for historical/military interactive and tabletop projects.

Steven Ellis’s Experience

  • Game Design Faculty

    Brown College

    (Educational Institution; CECO; Higher Education industry)

    January 2006 — Present (4 years 2 months)

    I primarily lead and teach two specific classes- the Introduction to Game Design class and what is essentially physical prototyping where students develop a working tabletop product from concept to finished product in 10-11 weeks.

    For the introduction class I use Tracy Fullerton's "Game Design Workshop (2nd Edition)" as the primary textbook. The ciriculum for this class was developed by Steven Charbonneau and myself. This class goes over the basics of game design, marketing/PR, the history of games, genres, development proceedures, business models, etc..

    The overall goal is to give students a good dose of reality of what goes into making games- which is a lot of hard work. Most students coming into the class confuse "playing games" with "making games" so the class gives them some cold, hard truths on what they can expect IF they becoming employed in the game industry.

    The physical prototyping (i.e. 'tabletop game design") class deals with developing a working game based on a specific topic or subject. I rotate the topics to avoid student's reusing past student work- the expectation is the students create 100% of the mechanics, components and documentation. The ciriculum for this class was developed by myself. The one thing I really enjoy about this class is exposing interactive gamers to the "old school" board and card format!

    I also serve as the Academic Advisor for the Game Design Department and assist in program ciriculum development.
