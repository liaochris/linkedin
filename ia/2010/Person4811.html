Fabio Akita’s Summary

As an evangelist, I have three years of proven track record working with open source and Agile communities. I have spoken at dozens of events in major cities, organized conferences, blogged and written articles for magazines. In the Ruby on Rails world, I am well known in both the Brazilian community and the international community. Having worked as an SAP consultant, I am comfortable dealing with enterprises, executives, negotiating contracts and managing corporate projects. As a programmer I constantly strive to learn and experiment with cutting edge technologies. Be it Java, Python and now Ruby, design patterns, software architecture, I have more than 10 years of development experience in startups, enterprise, and open source projects.

As a professional, I am solution-oriented or, a “problem-solver”, doing whatever it takes to deliver optimal results within the given constraints. I consider myself a pragmatic programmer, and as a manager I am people-oriented. Instead of trying to directly motivate people, I start by first eliminating their frustrations, and that’s usually by improving channels of interpersonal communication. Instead of focusing in any single particular technical detail in a project, I am comfortable thinking and acting based on the big picture. As an individual, I am very inquisitive and always exercise critical thinking, meaning that I hardly follow anything blindly. This also means that I am not dogmatic about a single way of thinking and I can keep myself open to new ideas and solutions. I like to read and research a lot not only about technology but anything that helps me better understand people’s relationships and behaviors, with a special focus on the science of management.

Please, read more about my achievements at my About page: http://www.akitaonrails.com/pages/about
