Stewart Marshall’s Summary

Director of Special Initiatives in the Office of the Principal of The University of the West Indies Open Campus and UNESCO Chair in Educational Technologies. Held senior management positions in higher education in England, Papua New Guinea, Australia, Southern Africa and the Caribbean. Research and consultancy interests in the role of communication and information technologies in distance education, especially in developing countries. Managing Editor of the International Journal of Education and Development using Information and Communication Technology (IJEDICT) – http://ijedict.dec.uwi.edu/. Published over 90 publications, including book chapters, refereed articles and conference papers, plus several books, the latest ones being in the series "Educational Design and Technology in the Knowledge Society": http://www.infoagepub.com/series/Educational-Design-and-Technology-in-the-Knowledge-Society

Stewart Marshall’s Specialties:

- linking ICT enhanced economic, social and cultural development efforts with emerging opportunities in such areas as e-learning, e-commerce, community and civic networks, telecentres, e-democracy, e-governance, on-line participation, self-help and virtual health communities, advocacy, cultural enhancement and others;
- Web development using HTML, JavaScript, CSS, Drupal, Joomla, Dreamweaver, Flash.
