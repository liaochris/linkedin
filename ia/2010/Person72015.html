Furhana Afrid’s Summary

I am a multilingual video journalist with an international background from Africa and South Asia.

With a burning desire to realize my American dream to pursue international journalism, I left my home of 30 years in Nairobi, Kenya and re-located to the U.S.

As a reporter and anchor for a CBS affiliate in Montana, I wore many hats including shooting, editing on linear and non-linear systems and producing newscasts.

In my journalistic career, I've had my finger on the pulse of a rural community in Butte, Montana. I covered everything from local politics to business, education, crime and famous festivals. I also did a 5-part series on avalanches and covered dare devil Evel Knievel's funeral.

I Most recently reported on the then presidential candidate Senator Obama's visit to Butte and had the opportunity to conduct a brief interview with the president-elect.

When the 2008 post election violence in Kenya led to death and destruction, I brought that story to a rural community by conducting interviews with my family in Nairobi and other Kenyans living in the U.S. The story triggered enormous reaction including letters and phone calls of support as well as generous offers to help the victims of violence.

As a Kenyan of Pakistani descent who speaks five languages native to East Africa and South Asia, I am in a unique position to pursue stories for your American viewers through engaging and compelling news reporting.

In addition to my broadcast journalism skills, I also have academic and professional experience in business. As a general assignment reporter, I’ve also had the opportunity to report on a variety of business stories such as burgeoning businesses, visits by international dignitaries and the economic summit featuring top political and business leaders including Bill Gates.

I am a mature journalist who brings an international perspective and many value added skills critical in today's shrinking newsrooms.
