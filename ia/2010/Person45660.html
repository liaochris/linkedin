Cynthia McCloud’s Summary

For 11 years I was a journalist at a daily newspaper. I did everything: took photos, designed and constructed pages, reported spot news and longer in-depth features, brainstormed stories that I wrote or assigned to others, wrote a general interest lifestyle column and managed contributors. During that time I had a photo I took of an accused kidnapper appear on the front page of an edition of The Washington Post. I reported on the Sago Mine tragedy from the scene for The New York Times. I have ideas for nonfiction books and plots for a couple of culinary mysteries.
