Matthias Gries’s Summary

Matthias Gries works at system engineering (Access Communication Solutions) at Infineon Technologies in Munich, Germany, on MP-SoC architectures and programming views for network applications. He was a post-doctoral researcher in Prof. K. Keutzer's Mescal group at UC Berkeley / Gigascale Systems Research Center (GSRC) from 2002 to 2004. He received his Ph.D. from ETH Zurich, Switzerland, in 2001 for his work on QoS network processors. He received the Dipl.-Ing. degree in electrical engineering from the Technical University Hamburg-Harburg, Germany, in 1996, working on reconfigurable architectures for video processing. His interests include methods for developing ASIPs, system-level design, and analysis of real-time embedded systems.

Matthias Gries’s Specialties:

Network processors, embedded systems' architecture and software, design space exploration, system-level design flows, programming views, MP-SoC, VoIP, QoS, modeling and simulation, models of computation, scientific publications and reviews, university relations.
