Gary Guseinov’s Summary

Gary Guseinov spent his college years learning about technology, business and psychology. Immediately after graduating with a BA in Psychology and Business - Gary began his entrepreneurial career.

Since graduating from college in 1994 Gary has been actively involved in developing new internet technology. In 1994 Gary started a web development firm, Digital Media, which quickly gained Fortune 500 clientele including AT&T, British Petroleum, Disney, Sony and Bank of America. In 1998 Digital Media merged into Synergy, a web marketing firm. While serving as President of Synergy, Gary patented the first online media buying system. While developing new technology and business processes at Synergy, Gary was able to generate over $2 Billion in revenue for Synergy's clients.

In 2003 Gary founded CyberDefender Corporation. CyberDefender develops internet security applications which protect consumers and businesses against spam, spyware, viruses and phishing attacks.

In 2003 CyberDefender developed the first (patent pending) Collaborative Internet Security Network based on a Peer-to-Peer network infrastructure. The CyberDefender Collaborative Internet Security Network is the first threat agnostic network which detects and updates all users within hours of new Virus, Spyware, Phishing and Spam attacks.

Currently residing with his wife in Los Angeles, California, Gary spends his free time with friends and family, traveling around the world, snowboarding, and always developing new technology. Gary participates in various professional organizations as well as numerous non-profit organizations including the Clinton Global Initiative (www.clintonglobalinitiative.org)

Gary Guseinov’s Specialties:

CyberDefender develops internet security software that protects PCs against Spyware, Spam, Phishing and Viruses. CyberDefender is the first company to develop a patent pending Collaborative Internet Security Network - which uses client to client peering infrastructure to update PCs against the latest threats.
