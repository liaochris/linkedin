Al Tepper’s Education

The University of Stirling

MPhil, Publishing Studies, 1997 – 1998

The course covered editorial, design, sales, production, legal, marketing and online publishing.

My dissertation involved looking at the works of Neil Postman, Joseph Campbell, Marshall McLuhan and examined the role of publishers in the online world

The dissertation was titled: 'Go Forth & Inform: The role of the publisher in the post-information age'.

I argued that publishers would find it harder to succeed in the internet age due to a shifting of ground underneath them. Publishers were traditionally used to functioning in a controlled and limited information space where information was restricted due to limited access to the media and production tools used.

In contrast now value is defined by cross-platform accessibility and delivery and that is how publishers must add value. They must faciliate time-shifted access and cross-platform delivery to a variety of content in a variety of formats.

Those that do, win. Those that don't will be forgotten.
