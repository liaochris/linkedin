Darren Weber’s Experience

Postdoctoral Scholar

UCSF

(Biotechnology industry)

June 2003 – Present (3 years 7 months)

I am currently a post-doctoral scholar at the UCSF Dynamic Neuroimaging Laboratory (see the DNL website, http://dnl.ucsf.edu/). The work is supervised by Dr. Gregory Simpson and it forms part of a larger Network for the Study of Brain Systems and Dynamics (http://dnl.ucsf.edu/NBSD/). The position involves applications of source estimation tools to EEG/MEG and fMRI data recorded in attention and working memory tasks.

Research Assistant

The Flinders University of South Australia

(Higher Education industry)

December 1991 – June 2003 (11 years 7 months)

After completing a BSc(Hons), I worked as a research assistant in the Cognitive Neuroscience Laboratory at the Flinders University of South Australia. This was initially to complete work on publications arising from the honours thesis. The employment continued from 1992-2003, while I worked on a new research project, began a BA degree and later started a postgraduate thesis project. During these 10 years, especially after 1995, the laboratory made a transition from 16 channels of EEG only into a sophisticated brain imaging laboratory, incorporating 128 channel EEG (Neuroscan SYNAMP) and anatomical and functional MRI (Siemens Magnatom 1.5T). Furthermore, these acquisition methods comprised the basis for investigation of multimodal integration and brain source estimation.
