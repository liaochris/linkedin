Coordinator aka "market droid"
Cyberian RC5-56 challenge (Non-Profit; 1-10 employees)
Computer Software Industry
May 1997 – August 1998 (1 year 4 months)

The Cyberian RC5-56 challenge was a hobby effort run by me and the other then-to-be founders of Teraflops Ltd. We participated in a competition organized by the RSA Security Inc: decrypt a message encrypted with the RC5-56 algorithm.

As a result, we built a distributed computing effort which allowed volunteers around the world to give the excess computing power of their computers to the meaningful purpose of brute force cracking the code. At peek hours, there was around 10 000 computers crunching the keys.

For more details, see http://www.cyberian.org/, the site for our project at that time. Or browse to www.distributed.net - who was our major competition and actually found the key - they are still in the business of distributed computing efforts.
