Mark Roth’s Summary:

My current focus is expanding business operations for Centerstage Chicago (CenterstageChicago.com) and find an organization to acquire us and buy out all of us or at the least, my original partners.

One of my extended goals is to build a media production business around Centerstage and find new opportunities to expand our media offerings beyond the internet and newspaper that we have today. (think TV and Radio)

On the business development side of things, this is an example of what I would like to create more of for Centerstage:

In the summer of 2003, I negotiated for Centerstage to license our web site content to the Chicago Sun-Times for print use in their "Red Streak", a new youth-oriented newspaper. This positioned us as a direct competitor to Metromix.com (owned by the Chicago Tribune and mixed in with their own youth tabloid, the "Red Eye").

We would like to develop more of these kinds of syndication opportunities for people that want micropublishing solutions about Chicago or to use our technology content management system to do it in your own locations. (Our tools really are ready to go!)

In consulting, my own company Sinergi Media,Inc. recently began working on a project where I worked as a blended CTO & Dir. of Communications role. www.ChannelG.tv is a media startup that is piloting a viewer interactive television show showcasing the work of non-profit organizations and raising donations for those profiled. Channel G is now invovled in fundraising and network broadcast negotiations. (got any intros for us?)

My diverse experiences with both the "science" and the "art" of communications, provides my clients with the understanding of considerably different scales and scopes that can be implemented when producing a vision that involves the internet and media, for profit or not.
