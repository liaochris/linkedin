Malik Singleton’s Summary

The work I do is a combination of web programming, web design, multimedia production, content development, editing, promotional communications and more -- the working title is web production, however, this kind of work, in an industry is that is still experimenting and growing, is constantly redefining itself so I'm not too tied to titles.

Web production duties vary from environment to environment but basically it's the glue between all of the aforementioned specialties. I have worked in newsrooms, design studios, and social networking sites and in each case I've found that web producers are depended on to be idea people as much as we are expected to be techies or writer types or project managers. We have to know what trands and innovations are emerging and will affect this business in order to be effective. We have to know strategy, planning, and audience behavior much more than we need to know how to update pages. Of course every job description has its mundane parts but web producers are often mistaken as producers of only the few mundane things when, in fact, this work takes far more specialization and skill than it gets credit for.

If you don't really know (or value) what web producers do then think of us as being comparable to music producers. A music producer is the glue between the musicians, the engineers, the singers, the songwriters, etc. He has to do some of each of those things in order to get an album done. So likewise, we web producers wear a lot of hats. There's always something new to learn from someone or to teach someone which is the main reason I enjoy it (I even hear that I'm pretty good at it).

And in order to produce for a news organization I have to demonsrate that I have journalism credentials under my belt. I have reporting and editing work, commentary, photojournalism, reviews and interviews to my credit and it all comes in to play as I produce in a news-specific context, so this new gig is a good fit.
