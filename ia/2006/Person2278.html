Keith Schacht’s Summary

My latest passion -- JobCoin.com. That and the evolution taking place online. The incredible ability for internet companies to get leverage on their time and resources, to setup simple frameworks that facilitate interaction between millions of people—it's an amazing world we live in!

I spent the last 4 years with the company Inventables that I co-founded. Inventables pioneered a new method of inspiring consumer product companies to come up with new product ideas. Inventables has assembled a client list that includes the world's biggest consumer product companies: Nike, Proctor & Gamble, Motorola, BMW, Kraft, and many others.

Recent Accolades:

-- BusinessWeek a “Top 20 Entrepreneurs Under 25” (Dec 2005)
-- TED conference speaker (Feb 2006 & Feb 2005)
-- Forbes write-up, “Gadgets to Go” (July 2006)
-- Crain's Chicago Business profile (July 2006)
-- LA Times product concept feature (May 2006)
-- Business 2.0 company profile (Feb 2004)
-- Fortune write-up, "Inside-The-Box Thinking" (Oct 2004)

Prior to all this, I studied computer science and psychology at the University of Illinois. There I co-founded a boutique software development firm that was sold to Leo Media in 2001.

I've been involved in software development and the internet since the BBS I started in '94. It's been a fascinating ~10 years, can't wait to see what's in store for the next 10!
