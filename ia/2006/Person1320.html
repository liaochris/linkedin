Ernest Svenson’s Summary

Went to high shool in Panama, and then moved back to New Orleans in 1977 to go to college and then law school. I spent two years clerking for a federal judge in New Orleans, where I learned the importance of procedure (both written and unwritten) in litigation.

I spent over 18 years working for a mid-sized New Orleans firm, handling a range of business disputes. In 2006 I decided to start my own solo law practice. I enjoyed my old firm and miss the camradarie there. But, I wanted to practice law in a different way.

Today, lawyers can use technology in novel ways to lower overhead and increase efficiency. We need to be more adept at handling digital information to fully represent our clients. And, of course, we need to think creatively too.

Ernest Svenson’s Specialties:

Copyright, franchise, Internet, software, intellectual property, and trademark litigation. Fluent in Spanish.
