Dean Eckles’s Summary

I am a user experience designer and researcher focused on mobile interactions, persuasive technology, and social software. My most recent work includes context-aware mobile social media (see ZoneTag at Yahoo! Research Berkeley) and convincing mobile users to disclose or withhold information about their location.

My approach to a problem in this space is an iterative process that usually involves rapid prototyping, applying social and cognitive psychology to design for target behavior and attitudes, and evaluating success through exploratory and/or controlled studies. I've taught workshops on mobile prototyping at Stanford and Yahoo.

Dean Eckles’s Experience

Research Specialist at Stanford Persuasive Technology Lab

(Educational Institution; 11-50 employees; Research industry)

June 2004 – Present (2 years 5 months)

The Stanford Persuasive Technology Lab investigates how technology is and can be used to change behaviors and attitudes.

My work has focused on the persuasive power of mobile phones. We are currently investigating strategies for changing mobile location-disclosure behavior -- that is, getting people to reveal or withhold their location to e.g. a service when they want to find a nearby coffee shop or anyone online when they post an annotated video.

I also did design, prototyping, development of a networked mobile phone application for consuming news, blogs, and other mainly text based content that we've used as a research platform in studying mobile advertising, the power of praise and flattery, and more. This work resulted in a patented system for mobile content consumption for which I am a co-inventor.

Mobile Designer and Researcher (Intern) at Yahoo! Research Berkeley

(Public Company; 5001-10,000 employees; YHOO; Internet industry)

September 2005 – September 2006 (1 year 1 month)

The goal of Yahoo! Research Berkeley is "to explore and invent social media and mobile media technology and applications that will enable people to create, describe, find, share, and remix media on the web."

I worked with a team of researchers, designers, and engineers on ZoneTag--a public research prototype that leverages location information and community knowledge to help users upload, share, and organize their cameraphone photos. This has included web and on-device design, prototyping, and development. I also contributed to creating and executing protocols for mobile research.

Mobile Prototyping Intern at Yahoo!

(Public Company; 5001-10,000 employees; YHOO; Internet industry)

June 2005 – September 2005 (4 months)

I used rapid prototyping as part of a user-centered design process to collaboratively design, develop, and refine exciting new mobile interactions leveraging the depth and breadth of the Yahoo! network.
