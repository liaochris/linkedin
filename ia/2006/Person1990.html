Jim Benson’s Summary:

I have been involved in the provision of in-field and management technologies to the transportation industry for 20 years. I started working in transit, went on to design large scale public rail systems, and then ran a national transportation technology practice for an ITE 50 firm. I left that firm in 2000 to start Gray Hill Solutions.

At GHS, we create GIS based systems for government and business. We have created systems for the Lake County Illinois; US Forest Service; the Departments of Transportation in Washington, Oregon and Arizona; the Metropolitan Transportation Commission in the Bay Area; several municipalities in Washington, Oregon and Calfornia; and others.

I am also an AICP urban planner. I have lived in Nebraska, Colorado, Oregon, Washington, Vancouver, Tokyo and Hong Kong (well, I've been to Hong Kong enough to know my way around).

I am also very interested in the evolution of the Web and in collaborative technologies. My involvement with the cooperation commons and my blog are parts of those explorations. I have been working over the last few years to introduce the concepts of on-line collaboration and Web 2.0 to help agencies work more effectively.

I am here looking for new business opportunities, business partners, new employees (esp. rainmakers) and whatever may come along.

We create GIS enhanced management software for governments and private clients. We create asset management and real time status applications.

Our products include:

BusCarte - a transit web interface for riders (see www.intercitytransit.com)

We have completed dozens of customized projects for clients over the years as well. Current projects include customized embedded GIS application, XML data transfer mechanisms and an advanced traffic management system.

Our clients include four state DOTs, dozens of municipalities, several federal agencies, and private companies.
