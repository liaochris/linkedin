Frank Scaramuzza’s Summary

I started working with computers back in 1978 with a TRS-80 and immediately found myself drawn to the field. Since then, I have worked as a computer operator, operational manager, database administrator, programmer, analyst, consultant, designer, and have finally arrived at Chief Information Officer. The industries that I have worked with include Manufacturing, Distribution, Healthcare, Consulting, Educational and Homebuilding. I have filled the roles of being an accountant, order taker, mail room clerk and supervisor, bank teller, vault teller, sales person and product manager. When you own your own business, it is amazing what you get exposed to. While my associates degree is in Computer Science, my bachelor degreee is in Management. I have really found management to be interesting despite the challenges that you face as a manager day in and day out. My ultimate role will probably involve getting more into theoretical and less practical application of technology, but I have to admit that I have often been complimented as being a very practical technologist. It must be my very conservative nature at work.

CIO

Kimball Hill Homes

(Privately Held; 1001-5000 employees; Construction industry)

May 2001 – August 2006 (5 years 4 months)

I started at KHH as their Director of IT, but was quickly named their CIO. Homebuilding is not a strong technology sector, but by replacing many consultants with expert in house associates, we improved the internal departmental ranking from worst to best. The turnover rate for the department remained well below industry and national averages. During my tenure our team implemented a national Frame-Relay network and replaced that with a VOIP capable MPLS network. We also deployed a Sales Automation tool across the entire enterprise and helped the company double in size in 5 years. Other highlights include implementing a hand held construction scheduling solution across most of the company, moving to a dot-net architecture, and bringing in a revenue stream through a partnership with a major telecommunications company. My duties included representing KHH at an industry consortium, and with many outside vendors, and negotiating deals as well as managing the company technology budget.
