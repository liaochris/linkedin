Barbara Dobrinsky Holtzman’s Summary

I own and operate two distinct but oddly related businesses. I’ve developed a new practice area that I call “Personal Change Management.” It’s like change management in corporations, in that it involves gathering information and data, analyzing it, and developing a solution, but with added aspects of helping the individual with personal issues, in handling mental and emotional stress, and then building the motivation and skills to accomplish the developed solution.

My target market for personal consulting is exclusively male, he’s having trouble with his primary relationship, or he’s getting divorced, he’s had a heart attack or been diagnosed with a chronic condition like diabetes or high cholesterol that requires a life change, he’s got a small business but it’s not going the way he wants it to, or he’s an executive in some corporation suffering from burnout or stress he can’t seem to get under control. I gather information, collect data, analyze it, come up with a plan the client can understand, help him deal with the emotional issues, like a counselor, and help him get motivated, focused, and stay on-track.

My other business is Health & Wealth Direct, which offers people who want to start their own businesses a way to tap into the vast resources of the huge MLM firms without going broke in the process. How are they “oddly related?” Often, one of my personal consulting clients will need one of the products in my several direct lines, or one of my “downline” will be looking for a job or something, and I’ll network them into the other set of clients.

There's still no way I can tell you much of anything of substance in this little space. I've tried. So if you have questions, subscribe to my Be a Success! Newsletter, and you’ll get ebooks and reports that will help you be a success in all that you do, and my complete contact information so you can call or write and ask.
