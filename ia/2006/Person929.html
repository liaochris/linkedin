Dan York’s Summary:

As the worlds of voice, data and video all come crashing together, Dan York, CISSP, enjoys working on the bleeding edge of technology in the realm of VoIP and Internet security. With over 20 years of Internet expertise and as a veteran of multiple startups, he enjoys tackling new challenges and bringing people together to work toward a common goal.

Dan has authored several books on Linux and networking, written numerous articles for technical magazines and is a frequent speaker at technical and training conferences. He co-founded the nonprofit Linux Professional Institute, served on the board of directors for Linux International and currently reports to the CTO of Mitel focused on evaluating new communication technology and leading Mitel's public communication on VoIP security issues.

As part of his long-time interest and participation in online communications, Dan is a strong advocate and promoter of the appropriate use of so-called "social media" communication tools such as weblogs, podcasts and wikis. His blog is available at http://blog.danyork.com/ and his weekly podcast on VoIP security is available at http://www.blueboxpodcast.com/
