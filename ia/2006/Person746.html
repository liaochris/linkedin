Christian Aubry’s Summary

After his university studies in History of Art, Christian Aubry worked in the fields of Marketing Research and Travel & Tourism before becoming a professional journalist in France in the mid-80s. In 1989, he moved to Montreal and pursued his career in the Tourism business, designing, planning and marketing tours in Canada.

Fascinated by the Internet he "dived" into this new medium in 1997. He was first a Managing Editor at the IT daily news website Multimedium and, in 1999, he launched an online travel agency. The startup eventually merged with the Vancouver-based inbound tour operator SNV International, where he became Internet Content Manager. Weakened by the stock market crisis, SNV went bankrupt after the 9/11 attack had ruined the high season.

Aubry turned to consultancy: he managed tour & travel operator SOHO's computer systems, wrote Web content for Via Rail, and advised the Web team at CRIM (Montreal Computer Research Centre). In August 2002, he produced the new website of the RISQ (Quebec Scientific Information Network) and managed it as a consultant until his hiring as Webmaster in September 2003. His responsibilities soon grew to include corporate communications, project management related to Web and Open Source computing, RISQ's videoconferencing service management, and the organization of RISQ's annual conference (2004 and 2005).

After 42 months of good and loyal service, Christian turned to consultancy again in February 2006, with RISQ as his first customer.

Christian Aubry’s Specialties:

Executive and public communication of technologic issues. French writing and editing. Webmastering and paper products edition. Seminar and other event organization.
