Bruno Giussani’s Summary

Bruno Giussani is a Swiss writer and entrepreneur, idea generator, and a connector. He's the European Director of the TED conferences (http://www.ted.com) and the producer of the first TEDGLOBAL (Oxford, 2005) and of several other events, and the Special Projects Editor at newsmagazine L'Hebdo. He lives in Switzerland. He is member of the Boards of Tinext (http://www.tinext.com), a software company he co-founded, and of Namics (http://www.namics.com), and through Giussani Group LLC, a firm he co-founded, consults with private and public organizations. He writes a monthly column for the "Innovation & Design" section of BusinessWeek Online and is a regular contributor to several publications in Europe and the U.S. including the Herald Tribune, the Wall Street Journal Europe, and others (his multilingual articles' archive is at http://www.giussani.com). He is an award-winning blogger (http://www.LunchOverIP.com) and a recognized specialist of the social and business impacts of technological innovation. In his past lives he was an executive at the World Economic Forum, an Internet columnist for the New York Times, co-founded two other companies and authored several books on the social impacts of new technologies. He was a 2004 Knight Fellow at the Department of Communication of Stanford University and is and an Affiliated Fellow at the Freeman Spogli Institute for International Studies at Stanford.
