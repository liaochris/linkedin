J. Bruce Daley’s Summary

J. Bruce Daley has founded or co-founded five successful enterprises with very different business models - a publication (The Siebel Observer), a radio business (eCommerce Update), an event (The Enterprise Software Summit), and a consulting business (Great Divide Research). Currently he is one of the founding partners at Rabbit Ears Capital Advisors (RECA).

Mr. Daley is a recognized authority in software, and has been quoted in The Wall Street Journal, The New York Times, The Financial Times, The International Herald Tribune, The San Jose Mercury News, The San Francisco Chronicle, CBS/MarketWatch, The Street.com, The Denver Post, The Rocky Mountain News, Computerland Sweden, The Deal and CNet.

He has conducted over 250 consultations with investment firms such as Agnos Group, AIM Management Group, Bear Stearns, Brummer & Partners, Cambium Capital, Camelot Management Corporation, CQ Partners, Credit Suisse, D.E. Shaw Company, Deutsche Asset Management, Fidelity, Fortress Investment Group, JANA Partners, Janus, JPMorgan, Lehman Brothers, Maverick Capital, Morgan Stanley, Neuberger Berman, Pioneer Investments, Polygon Investments, Porter Orlin, Silver Lake Technology Management, Stark Investments and T. Rowe Price among others.

He has been a key note speaker, track host or a speaker at a number of different industry events including the Accenture Leadership Council, DCI, and Johnson & Wales School of Business.

Before becoming an entrepreneur, Mr. Daley had broad experience playing multiple roles within the software industry, with direct experience as programmer, project manager, sales and marketing executive, consultant, industry advisor, and research analyst.
