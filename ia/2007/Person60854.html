  • Graduate Research Assistant/Engineer

    University of California - Davis

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    June 2004 — March 2005 (10 months)

    [GeoStream Research Project]
    Project:
    The focus of this project was the development of a generic stream format, called GeoStreams, for remotely sensed, geospatial image data, based on the widely used data formats such as HDF5 to process continuous
    queries in an adaptive fashion and to deliver tailored geospatial image data to consumers. In particular the GRASS tool was used to enable an in depth analysis. GRASS GIS is a general purpose raster/vector GIS combined with integrated image processing and data visualization subsystems. It includes a large number of modules for management, processing, analysis and visualization of georeferenced data, further more it provides complete access to its internal structure and algorithms. eCognition was used to segment a multispectral image into homogeneous objects, or regions, based on neighboring pixels’ spectral and spatial properties, which helped during the analysis and modification of received streams.

  • Research Assistant

    University of California - Berkeley

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    May 2003 — August 2003 (4 months)

    [Center for Hybrid and Embedded Software System (CHESS)]
    This research project focused on the creation of an Intelligent Road Navigation System linked to a dynamic data warehouse, with the ultimate goal to revolutionize commuting. The dynamic route planner differs from current inventions, by placing a greater emphasis on real-time data, such as traffic conditions, road constructions, road inclination, and weather conditions. This project was a component of the Ptolemy project which studies modeling, simulation, and design of concurrent, real-time, embedded systems. The focus was on assembly of concurrent components. The key underlying principle in the project is the use of well-defined models of computation that govern the interactions between components. A major problem area being addressed is the use of heterogeneous mixtures of models of computation.
