Portman Wills’s Summary

Over the last 10 years, Portman Wills has led small engineering teams to deliver over 250 web sites and desktop applications. He has served as Chief Architect, CTO, and VP Engineering at several technology companies.

Career highlights include: deploying communications systems at Voxiva that are used by Ministries of Health across Africa to track disease outbreaks; architecting the software that allowed Digital Divide Data to expand into Laos and Vietnam; building the popular commencement speech archive Fly Little Bird; designing the communications protocols between Liberty Wireless (an early MVNO in the US) and Sprint; and founding the Cavalier Daily Online Edition.

Portman studied mathematics, economics, and computer science at the University of Virginia.

Portman Wills’s Specialties:

Enterprise architecture, software development, information and communications technologies, consumer web properties, business-to-business web services
