Jeremy Shull’s Summary

Federal Tort Claims: Represented claimants in federal tort claims against the Department of Veterans’ Affairs. Filed claims, requested medical information, responded to requests for information including itemization of damages, and negotiated settlements with the VA.
Collection: Supervised collection of delinquent accounts, judgments, and all related collection activities for over 900 active cases. Filed petitions, tried cases before court or jury, initiated post-judgment proceedings such as garnishments, execution of leans, and other creditor remedies.
Criminal Defense: Vigorously represented individuals in all stages of the criminal justice system, including arraignment, jury selection, trial, and sentencing. Experienced in defending against Driving While Intoxicated, Sexual Battery, Theft, Manslaughter, and Juvenile matters.
Appellate Law: Researched and wrote brief for Amicus Curiae in Wilson v. Mike Steven Motors, Inc., No. 92,468, 2005 WL 1277948, 111 P.3d 1076 (Kan. Ct. App. May 27, 2005).
Bankruptcy: Prepared and filed Chapter 13 and Chapter 7 Petitions. Performed budget and debt analysis to determine bankruptcy eligibility and repayment schedules in Chapter 13 cases. Experienced in handling Adversarial Proceedings. Certified to electronically file pleadings using ECF.
General Civil Litigation: Well versed in dispensing with all levels of civil litigation, including construction disputes, personal injury, collections, and family law. Responsible for drafting Petitions, initiating and responding to discovery, trial preparation, jury selection, depositions, and presenting the final case to judge or jury.
