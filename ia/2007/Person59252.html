Miguel O. Roman III’s Summary

My research is centrally concerned with the development and application of remote sensing, LIDAR, radiative transfer, and geospatial techniques to measure and monitor global biophysical variables, e.g. surface albedo, land cover, leaf area index, and forest phenology.

My goal is to improve our understanding of how different types of models (e.g. coupled-climate, general circulation, and carbon-water-energy processes) react to the intrinsic properties and anisotropic behavior of the land surface with varied spatial resolution, temporal aggregation, and inherent accuracy.

Miguel O. Roman III’s Specialties:

Remote Sensing, Geographic Information Systems, Ecosystem Modeling, Radiative Transfer, Satellite Applications, Digital Image Processing, Optical Systems, Statistical Modeling, Pattern Analysis, Under-Canopy LIDAR, Systems Engineering, and Education Outreach.
