Gerald Buckley’s Summary

I'm an intensely curious person. I went to college at OU to study to be an aeronautical engineer. Calculus kicked my behind. Thoroughly and completely. I reevaluated and found what I really wanted to do was technical illustration... Transferred to Oklahoma State Univ (OSU) and was educated as an art director which suited me just fine.

Then came the Mac. Everything changed. I became interested in the power of the machines, networks, applications and what they could do combined. I became a consultant to ad agencies and design firms in Tulsa.

Won several retainer contracts and drew the attn of Apple Corp + the local MicroAge franchise. They bought me out and I became an Apple Systems Engineer consulting to lg business in the OK, AR, TX and KS region.

Apple was awesome. Learned a great deal very quickly. Worked as an application developer on the Newton Messagepad. Wrote the first cellular modem connection strings for the Newton and built an application for Kamar Aulakh, a Director at Williams Companies (later became CEO of Quark).

From Apple I went on to work for the Williams Companies' Digital Frontiers. There I was the Media Architect for their commercial web design shop. We built Thrifty.com, the national UnitedWay.org. We created the first online baby gallery for new parents at St. Francis hospital. I won the first web site Addy in the nation (and several more after that). Worked lots of really cool projects.

As sometimes happens with extremely good things... Williams was going through changes and our little outfit was fitting less and less with the parent company's corporate objectives. So, I eventually went to work for three of our largest clients: Pennwell, Thrifty and AAPG.

The AAPG has shored up my operations experience. I feel exremely well prepared to take on my first internet startup as CEO and am embarking on my dream as an entrepreneur. Here's to the crazy ones. The dreamers.

    GROCIO is a comparison shopping engine for groceries.

    Let that sink in just a bit... Unlike comparison shopping for a camcorder or a package of software, groceries are usually bought in bulk and there are normally dozens of possible places to shop locally. The technical aspects to pulling this off are immense and part of the reason it's not been done before.

    GROCIO will make it easy to find the least expensive grocery checkout WHILE also rewarding your visits with highly relevant coupons to your shopping trip. Shopping for a large can of Lipton Instant Ice Tea? Great, we'll add that coupon to your shopping list (along with several others relevant to what you told us you're shopping for). So, we'll save you money at the counter and time you no longer have to spend clipping coupons.

    Contact me if any of this sound interesting from an investors point of view or as a programmer. Again, the technical challenge... while big, won't be intimidating for the right people.
