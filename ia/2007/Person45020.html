Judy Rees’s Summary

I love learning about new ideas, understanding them, writing about them, and using the best of them to make change happen. I'm passionate about the magic of metaphor, and about Clean Language, a superb way of exploring the metaphors at the heart of a person's thinking.

I had been a news journalist and media executive all my working life, and when I came across Clean in 2005, I could see its potential immediately.

Its roots are in the world of therapy, but it could change many more lives for the better by being adopted in business and other organisations.

It works on the principle that metaphor is the stuff of thought, and that the truth about what a person really believes can be found in the metaphors they use in language and elsewhere.

Working with a person's metaphors can be the most effective way to make change happen for them, because the metaphors we use have a profound effect on our everyday lives. Clean is the most effective way yet devised to explore metaphoric worlds and to use them to effect transformational change, if that's wanted.

With fellow ecademist Wendy Sullivan I own Clean Change Company, which is training a new generation of 'magicians of metaphor' through the world's most comprehensive training in Clean approaches. I also use Clean in coaching, facilitation and consulting.

History

I worked as a reporter, sub and news editor on papers in the Midlands (Lincolnshire Standard, Coventry Telegraph, Birmingham Evening Mail and Nottingham Post) before heading to London to seek my fortune. I joined Teletext, then one of the most profitable companies in the British media, and rose through the ranks to become Executive Editor, running a department of more than 100 journalists.

I hold an MA in Media Technology Adminstration, a project management qualification and am a Master Practitioner of NLP.
