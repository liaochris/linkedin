Ali Sajid’s Summary

I started working in IUCN Pakistan Country Office in Karachi in 1998, and in 1999 moved to the Asia Regional Office in Bangkok, where after working initially as a Personal Assistant to the HR Director, I was promoted to a Human Resources Officer. This was followed by another vertical move to the position of Country Group HR Manager for Cambodia, Lao PDR and Vietnam in 2006.

I have a post graduate degree in Law from the University of Karachi in 1997 which was followed by a post graduate diploma in Human Resources Management from the SASIN Graduate Institute of Business Administration in Thailand (2004). I then pursued and acquired a specialized Master of Science degree in Human Resources Management from the same institute in 2006. The topic of my Masters research was “Comparative Study of Work Environment as Perceived by Female and Male Professional Employees in the NGO Sector in Thailand and Pakistan.”

I have attended several HR related trainings within and outside IUCN. These include the HRM in a Briefcase (Karachi, 2002), Excellence in Leadership (Celigny, 2005), Communications Intelligence with NLP™ for Managers (Bangkok, 2005) and other HR workshops. I have also attended training in other functions such as Project Information Management Systems PIMS (Bangkok, 2002) and APTECH Worldwide training in Microsoft Excel (Bangkok, 2003).
