Starre Vartan’s Summary

Starre was born in Sydney, Australia, and spent long sojourns,
summers and holidays Down Under. Contrasting with the sandstone
cliffs, ocean culture and dry climate of the Sydney environs, Starre
spent the balance of her younger years attending school in the tiny
town of Garrison, New York. Situated in the Hudson Highlands, the lush
woods, deep green lakes, and precipitous mountains of Garrison (not to
mention the majestic Hudson River) still inspire her.

The environments of her youth and world travels led her to achieve a
B.S. in geology, a B.A. in English and a minor in Biology at Syracuse
University. She is currently pursuing a Master of Fine Arts in
nonfiction writing at Columbia University. Starre thinks that the health of the environment is the most pressing issue of our time.

Now residing in coastal Connecticut, Starre is a freelance writer who
specializes in environmental health, green fashion, eco-beauty, wildlife issues, and food. She is the founder and editor of Eco Chick, a blog for hip, environmentally oriented women. Eco Chick has been featured in the Wall Street Journal, the Christian Science Monitor, USA Today, Grist, Treehugger, and a host of green
blogs. Starre has been an eco-expert on morning news shows for ABC-7
New York and the WB New York.

Starre is a contributing writer for E/The Environmental Magazine, a dining
columnist for The Fairfield County Weekly, and the 'Green Guru' columnist for Audubon Magazine. Starre has also been published online at Hearst's The Daily Green, where she writes a column called Starre Power:Fashion and Beauty. She also writes for the Huffington Post, Alternet, Worldchanging, and Jewschool.

A book based on her blog, "The Eco Chick Guide to Life: How to Be Fabulously Green," will be published by St. Martin's in the Spring.

She is excited to have been an associate producer for the Discovery
Channel's upcoming mini-series, Final Hour.
