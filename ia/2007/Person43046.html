  • Software Design Leader

    Matrox - Imaging Products Group

    (Privately Held; 1001-5000 employees; Computer Hardware industry)

    May 1995 — September 2000 (5 years 5 months)

    Matrox Imaging solutions combine easy-to-use, device-independent software with state-of-the-art hardware, giving OEMs and integrators the tools they need to reduce time-to-market and decrease development costs.

    In my time at Matrox, I designed and productized the ActiveX/COM version of the flagship Matrox Imaging Library software. I lead a team of software developers in the implementation, and worked with product management and marketing towards a successful launch in 2000.

    Prior to this work, I re-designed Intellicam, an advanced software tool for interfacing proprietary cameras to our digitizers. This project involved working closely with the lab engineers, understanding their needs, and ultimately implementing a visual user interface and an advanced scripting facility fast enough to allow real-time adjustments of hardware parameters.

    See http://matrox.com/imaging/products/software.cfm

  • Shareware Author

    John McCalla LLC (Sole Proprietorship)

    (Sole Proprietorship; Myself Only; Computer Software industry)

    1992 — 1996 (4 years)

    While a student at McGill University, I wrote and commercialized a shareware application called MDesk for OS/2. It is a shell replacement that provides a streamlined user interface for OS/2, and is focused on low-memory PCs. It allowed OS/2 to run smoothly on laptops, the bulk of which have 4MB of RAM.

    This was my first introduction to producing software for a commercial audience, and to providing support over the then nascent web. It was a success in that it was deployed on all continents, and payed for most of my university fees.

    MDesk is now discontinued. It is still available on the web from serial cracking sites. Some press is still online at http://www.monitor.ca/monitor/issues/vol4iss1/os2stuff.html and http://www.os2ezine.com/v1n2/rave.html
