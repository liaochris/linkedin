Greg Searle’s Summary

Atf BioRegional North America, Greg is responsible for delivering One Planet Living in Canada and America – a non-profit joint initiative with the World Wildlife Fund (WWF) which helps local authorities and developers build neighbourhoods that reduce the ecological footprint of residents by 80% to a truly sustainable, “one planet” level. He is currently working on Zero Carbon/Zero Waste developments in New Orleans, Washington DC, and Montreal. Greg was previously based in London, UK, where he lived at the BedZED eco-village. He sits on the Advisory Committee for the Living Building Challenge.

In 1999, Greg co-founded Tomoye Corporation, a KM software company, and served as CTO until 2005. Greg is the inventor of Tomoye’s Community of Practice software, which is used by hundreds of thousands of knowledge workers globally. At Tomoye, Greg worked directly with customers such as the World Bank, the US Army, Smithsonian Institution, the IRS, and Fortune 500 firms such as John Deere. Greg continues to serve as Chief Visionary Officer, and as an owner and Director.

Greg has been a regular speaker at Sustainability, KM, and International Development conferences for over a decade. Highlights include being invited in 2004 to speak to the US Senate sub-committee on Knowledge Management, providing the Keynote address at the 2002 Internet Global Conference in Barcelona, and addressing IUCN’s Quadrennial Congress in Amman in 2001. More recently, Greg has spoken about sustainability and ecological footprinting at conferences across North America. He was invited to speak on a panel on Eco-Density at the 3rd World Urban Forum, and in November spoke at GreenBuild in Denver, Colorado. Greg has been a guest lecturer on sustainability at Ryerson and Texas A&M Universities, and on KM at Queen’s University.

Greg has also served as a consultant to the United Nations (FAO - Rome), the World Conservation Union (IUCN - Brussels), Wetlands International, IDRC, and Industry Canada.
