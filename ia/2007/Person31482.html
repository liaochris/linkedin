Geordie Keitt’s Summary

I have been testing software since 1994. I've tested UNIX APIs, MQ Series apps, Windows apps, multimedia training courses, Web marketplaces, a webmart builder IDE, the websites that run bandwidth auctions for the FCC, and now critical chain project scheduling software.

In the early years of my career I did an adequate job, but did not feel as though I had "traction" in my profession before 2000, when I took my first RST course from James Bach.

I aspire to be a respected and respectful practitioner and teacher of the craft of rapid, exploratory software testing.

Geordie Keitt’s Specialties:

rapid software testing, test management of exploratory testing teams, session-based test management, web application testing, perl scripting

Geordie Keitt’s Experience

  • QA and Testing Manager

    ProChain Solutions Inc.

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Management Consulting industry)

    December 2004 — Present (3 years 1 month)

    We are a small consulting firm specializing in implementing "critical chain" project management processes in Fortune 500 companies, with a software arm that supports our methodology. The products include a Windows application, a Microsoft Project plugin, and an enterprise-scale web server. I am in charge of testing all of it, with the help of a fulltime tester and support ad hoc from the development team.

    We practice Rapid Software Testing and Session-Based Test Management, as taught by James and Jon Bach and Michael Bolton. I have used these methodologies very successfully at ProChain and at other companies since 2000. We take a lot of pride in how quickly we find important problems and how clearly we communicate the status of the product.

    I also act as a toolsmith, building small perl scripts to do such chores as parse log files, compare data in a database before and after running a test, or to make visual models of interacting components.

  • Test Manager, FCC Auctions System (ISAS)

    Computech Inc

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Computer Software industry)

    January 2002 — December 2004 (3 years)

    Managed a team of up to 12 testers to test the FCC bandwidth auction system. I was brought in to practice exploratory testing and session-based test management, unusual in a government contract. ISAS has extremely high-order math involved in the core processing, a user base that is highly motivated to hack / cheat / game the system, stringent load handling and uptime requirements, and business rules that change radically between phases of an auction.

    The team had been working to pre-written sets of tests, and major bugs were finding their way to production. I substituted risk-based exploratory testing and got the team working in pairs, in chartered sessions, with a single public dashboard showing our progress and status. We engendered a great deal of trust and credibility with the developers and analysts as we caught design and execution problems early and convincingly. The results were very apparent in the successful auctions that have been held using the system.

  • Tester

    TechTrader, Inc

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Computer Software industry)

    1999 — 2001 (2 years)

    This was a very difficult and rewarding position. We wanted to build a platform that would allow us to create B2B web portals for any industry. Our talented, prolific developers created custom tools and languages on the way to creating actual websites for production. In this environment the Rapid Software Testing concepts found fertile soil: focus testing on the riskiest areas, do only as much documentation as absolutely necessary, keep the team informed of the quality of the product and also the quality of our knowledge of the product, and demonstrate how their actions and decisions change the quality of knowledge and the quality of the product. I was able to demonstrate the need for much better visibility on to the software and enlist the team in improving testability and the quality of the product at handoff.
