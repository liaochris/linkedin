Andras Nyiro’s Experience

  • President

    Login Initiative

    (Non-Profit; 1-10 employees; Telecommunications industry)

    March 2007 — Present (9 months)

    Login Initiative provides home internet access to Romany people across Europe.
    - I managed to set up a pilot settlement in Northern Hungary (Tomor, Borsod county), and we are expanding the pilot to 4 other settlements in Northern and Southern Hungary.
    - Based on the experiences we will launch in 2008 a project which will cover 200 settlements in Hungary and the neighboring countries.
    - Website: http://LoginInitiative.eu

  • Director of E-Media Services

    Ringier

    (Privately Held; 1001-5000 employees; Media Production industry)

    January 2005 — August 2007 (2 years 8 months)

    Ringier AG is a Swiss family owned publishing company.
    - I was responsible for delivering mobile content and internet services in the Czech Republic, Slovakia, Hungary, Romania and Serbia.
    - I set up a mobile ASP in-house, and contracted with 12 mobile operators in the region.
    - I worked out the new media strategy of the five countries in 2006.
    - I participated in the development of the new media training material, see more here: http://21stCenturyJournalism.com

  • Director of Content Development

    T-Mobile

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; TMO; Telecommunications industry)

    January 2000 — 2004 (4 years)

    - Former Westel is the largest Hungarian GSM operator, with more than 4 million customers.
    - I built up and maintained content services in SMS, MMS, wap, and web. I focused on synergies between different media types, and to create a reliable, simple and useful service. I set up a content quality control system.
    - I created and managed a successful marketing strategy based on a dozen special market and usability analysis.
    - Our wap service became one of the most visited mobile multimedia applications in Europe. In 2003 Westel had the highest non-voice non-SMS ARPU among the T-Mobile companies.
    - I created an experimental 3G portal. I have a lot of experience in mobile imaging, video and gaming.
    - I was country manager of the T-Mobile content service (t-zones). I worked closely with T-Mobile International Marketing (based in London).
    - I worked with 34 people: editors, designers, musicians, developers and project managers.
    - I was member of the Management Board.

  • Editor-in-Chief

    Index

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Media Production industry)

    May 1999 — November 2000 (1 year 7 months)

    Index is the successor of my previous internet project, Internetto.
    - Successfully introduced a new startup brand on the Hungarian internet market.
    - Index.hu became the most visited portal in 3 months.

  • Editor-in-Chief

    IDG

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Media Production industry)

    1995 — May 1999 (4 years)

    IDG is a US based market leading publishing house focused on IT business, with periodicals like PC World and Computerworld.
    - I was editor-in-chief of Internetto, Hungary’s first internet portal, from 1995.
    - I was the founder and editor-in-chief of ABCD, the first Hungarian interactive
    CD-ROM magazine, until 1995.
    - I published regularly on the web. As founder of the first Hungarian internet magazine appeared regularly on TV and printed media.
    - Made the first online interview with the Hungarian Prime Minister
    in 1997.
    - I did an intensive lobby activity to defend the interests of the internet users.
