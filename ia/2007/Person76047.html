Sam Sethi’s Summary

I have worked in the IT industry for 15+ years, starting my career with Microsoft as a systems engineer/consultant before joining Netscape soon after the launch of the first web browser as Marketing Manager EMEA. Before the AOL acquisition of Netscape, I joined Gateway Computers to manage their ecommerce business across EMEA before migrating to a venture capitalist called CMGi.

I then decided to combine my technical, internet, ecommerce and VC knowledge/experience to become a consultant helping start-ups get up and running. This naturally led me to start my own company Abrocour which won "best new (UK) business of the year 2004". I sold this last year to Watford Electronics.

I am now running a “think tank” consultancy called Vecosys which helps companies better understand how Web 2.0 technologies (Blogs, RSS, Podcasting, Ajax, etc.) are already affecting dramatic changes to their virtual ecosystem. – partners, suppliers, competitors and customers.

My most recent consultancy engagements have been with MSN UK helping them with their "Live" UK strategy and with BT helping them develop their new web services.

Sam Sethi’s Specialties:

An ability to decipher seemingly complex new technologies and translate them into a simple set of business benefits for non-technical executives to understand. I am currently working on "Attention" based technologies and services. i.e Federated Trust, Attention MetaData, MicroFormats and XHTML.
