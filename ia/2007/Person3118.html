Alexandra Watkins’s Summary

I am an in-demand brand name consultant and tagline writer who owns Eat My Words, a boutique naming firm in San Francisco. Unlike other firms who enjoy mangling perfectly good words into gobbledygook, the names we create are real words that are evocative, emotional, hip and absolutely unforgettable. And most of our names make people smile. For instance, we just named a home cleaning robot "Neato" and a frozen yogurt chain "Spoon Me." We make the naming process fun. Sure, naming is serious business, but it doesn’t mean we have to meet in stuffy conference rooms and act like we’re in church.

While I started out specializing in food & beverage accounts, thus the name Eat My Words, my clients quickly discovered that I have a talent for naming things you don't put in your mouth - like consumer electronics, beauty products, and jet engines. And I became busy enough to morph from a freelancer into a "firm" and take on a sous chef/creative partner, Sherry Petersik.

Our clients include Frito-Lay, Stanford University, THX, The Sydney Opera House, Dunkin Donuts, Jelly Belly, Del Monte, Clorox, Mani Pedi, a lot of start-ups, and many leading branding firms (including Igor and Landor) who put their good name on our great names.

I have generated thousands of names for snacks, software, sunscreen, social networking sites, sportswear, shoes, sugar scrubs, serums, and seafood. (And that’s just the S’s!) I’ve also named lots of things that make you fat and drunk including a nationally recognized bacon cheeseburger and no less than 4 vodkas.

Prior to becoming a full-time namer, I was an advertising copywriter for 20+ years. In the mid-nineties I jumped on the dot com gravy train, and rode it until it crashed in my backyard. I broke into the naming business when Gap hired me to create names and taglines for their first line of body care products and I weaseled my way into branding powerhouse Landor, who started sending business my way and helped me build Eat My Words.
