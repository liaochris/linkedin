Larry Windes’s Summary

Larry Windes, president of the firm, is a native of Arizona and has a Bachelor of Architecture Degree from Arizona State University (1968). He studied landscape architecture under the tutelage of the Hon. Rene Perchere – 1965. (Landscape architect to the Crown of Belgium). Mr. Windes is the author of the book “High-Rise Cluster Matrix Housing”.


Early in his career he pioneered The Mixed-use Urban Village concept as a solution to Sub-urban sprawl, (Hayden Neighborhood, Tempe, Arizona, 1974), and was an early creator of the Festival Market concept for urban shopping malls, (Bridgemarket, Manhattan, New York, 1973). In 1986 he moved to Saudi Arabia for two years where he was project manager with the eminent firm of Zuhair Fayez in Jeddah. Next he remained 6 years in Cairo, Egypt where he established the Egyptian Branch of Larry Windes and Associates and executed the original residential master plan of Dr. Ahmed Bahgat’s “Dreamland” – 1994. His most significant work in Egypt was the children’s Television Amphitheater for the Ministry of Communications.
