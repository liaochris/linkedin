Robert Haddock’s Summary

Technology and Business Innovator with proven experience in imagining, planning, financing and starting new ventures, and in refocusing existing ventures.

Integrative thinker, able to synthesize new solutions and bring them to profitable reality. Holder of four major patents, author/ developer of print publications that generated sales of more than 10 million copies.

Creator of the first smart phone, upon which more than 1.5 billion mobile phones are based, the first phone with web browser and the first phone with Voice Over IP. First conceived of integrating a telephone with chip card, now implemented in all GSM phones worldwide. Creator of the first browser to access multiple content sources worldwide, the first commercial concept-based search engine and the first news reader.

Business Strategy, Strategic Marketing, Business Development, Technology Planning and Implementation

VP, Global Report

Citicorp

(Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; Banking industry)

July 1984 – August 1988 (4 years 2 months)

Conceived and built Global Report (GR), an online service that transformed how people access content from diverse sources. In 3 years built from concept to customers in 20 countries. GR integrated and provided simple access to 4 million pages of content from 15 sources worldwide, indexed it by 18,000 concepts.

Created the first online browser - 7 years before Mosaic. Created the first concept-based search engine - long before Google; and the first commercial dynamic news reader - long before RSS. GR provided simplified access to content from Dow Jones, Knight-Ridder, Business International, S&P, etc.

Mr. Haddock grew GR at a 30% compound rate for 8 quarters by using ground-breaking marketing concepts he developed, including his "Free Disk + Free Hours" marketing strategy later used by AOL to grow its subscriber base 100-fold. Mr. Haddock twice received the prestigious ECHO Award, from the Direct Marketing Association, for most innovative direct marketing worldwide.
