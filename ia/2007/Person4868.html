Harold Taylor’s Summary

Responsible for collecting, storing, transforming and analyzing the security-related data for the FedEx family of companies. My professional experience gives me a strong background in distributed system design, case management system design, statistical analysis, military operations, physical security, information security, data analysis, law enforcement, loss prevention, intelligence gathering and analysis, and most recently, online collaborative system design. I've been involved with analytical loss prevention and security since 1986, and hope to continue to further this field.

I am particularly interested in transforming corporate security into corporate "resilience", online collaboration between investigators, management and law enforcement agencies...particularly in large international companies with strong connections with government and law enforcement.

Very interested in link analysis, evidence extraction and link discovery, knowledge discovery in databases (KDD), XML, WXML, PHP, AJAX, SOA and SOAP applications, mashup applications, textual data mining, data visualization, and most recently, risk visualization and management. Being the perpetual geek, I also enjoy studying mathematics, exploratory data analysis and social networking.

I spent a year living in Iraq, as the Personal Security Detail (PSD) Team Leader for a Brigade Commander, General Augustus L. Collins, and a Force Protection Platoon Leader in one of the most active areas of insurgency. As a PSD Team Leader, I protected the General on several hundred missions throughout the Iraq/Kuwait area, living just south of Baghdad, and North of al Hilla (Babylon). I toured the ancient city of Babylon several times, and am fascinated with the history of Mesopotamia. I made friends in Iraq, and currently maintain a close, but long-distance relationship with my former interpreter, who is currently a physics professor at al Hilla University, Iraq.
