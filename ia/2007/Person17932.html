Christopher Locke’s Summary

Christopher Locke is an experienced, creative professional with a strong understanding of the commercial realities surrounding the development of cutting edge interactive entertainment.

Chris started his career as a programmer for the Nintendo Game Boy before co-founding Cave Barn Studios LLC, a million-dollar developer of video games with offices in Chicago, Toronto, and Warsaw. The company developed innovative electronic entertainment for clients such as Vivendi/Universal, Telegames, Cartoon Network, Warner Brothers, NewKidCo, and Electronic Arts. While at Cave Barn, he oversaw design and development efforts based on licensed properties including Tom and Jerry, Battlebots, Curious George, ET: the Extra Terrestrial, He-Man: Masters of the Universe, Powerpuff Girls, M&Ms, Thrasher Magazine, and Animorphs.

He later joined Groove Media, a start-up publisher of video game software. At Groove, Chris established the production pipeline and its processes, and managed all aspects of their highly profitable value line of Xbox and PC titles. He then served as the Executive Producer at ZAPiT Games, a company dedicated to bringing casual gamers a family-friendly alternative to hard-core gamer consoles. While at ZAPiT, he oversaw custom hardware and software development for the Game Wave Family Entertainment System.

Chris is currently the Sr. Director of Product Development at Capcom Interactive.
