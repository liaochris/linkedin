Andrew Hoppin’s Summary

I'm a serial social entrepreneur currently working with NASA to help the Agency participate more strategically in the open-source software movement, and also consulting in the CivicSpace/Drupal community on open-source software tools, community building methodologies and strategy for progressive political organizations.

I'm actively organizing communities in the virtual world Second Life for NASA (http://www.SpaceCoLab.org), progressive politics (http://www.rootscampsl.org), and the YearlyKos Convention (http://www.yearlykosconvention.org).

I have co-founded and lead business development for http://www.GoodStorm.com (progressive e-commerce), http://www.Trellon.com (Drupal web development for political campaigns and non-profits), http://www.CivicSpaceLabs.com (online community software from the Dean campaign), http://www.MoonFront.com (aerospace consulting), and http://www.NSpaceLabs.com (online geographic information and data visualization systems).

In 2003, I founded the largest local organization in the "Draft Clark" movement (New York for Clark) and helped to create a national network of Draft Clark web sites. I then joined the Clark for President technology staff to help manage the campaign's voter data and the nation's first open-source campaign software volunteer program, "Clark TechCorps."

I serve on the Advisory Boards of the Space Generation Foundation, the Yearly Kos Convention, Yenza, and the New Organizing Institute.

He blogs at www.GlobeHoppin.com and CivicMinded.Corante.com.
