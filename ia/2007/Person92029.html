Yoni Greenbaum’s Summary

Yoni got his introduction to journalism working as an editorial assistant at New York Newsday during its heyday in the ‘80s. He was fortunate to learn from the likes of Jimmy Breslin, Murray Kempton and Sidney Schamberg. Hired as an Editor at the Bergen Newspaper Group in 1997, Yoni was in charge of the production of three weekly newspapers serving 25 communities in Bergen County and was involved in the launch and production of a weekly entertainment magazine serving Bergen County.

In 1998 Yoni joined the Herald News, a 40,000-circulation daily newspaper in Passaic County. Prior to being appointed City Editor, Yoni was Business Editor and Research Editor. Under his editorship, for three straight years, the Herald News received the New Jersey Press Association’s General Excellence Award and his reporters received recognition by the Deadline Club and the Society of Professional Journalists.

As an editor, Yoni has traveled to Israel and Palestine to report on the start of the second Intifada, and also to Poland, to report on the country’s westernization and movement towards acceptance into the European Union.

In October 2005 he joined the Courier-Post in Cherry Hill, NJ as Metro Editor and was also instrumental in the expansion of the newspaper’s online presence.

Yoni founded Jewish New Jersey in July 2006. He served as the newspaper’s Executive Editor and is President of its parent company JNJ Media Group, Inc. In addition, Yoni conceived and built JNJ's online presence.

In April 2007, Yoni joined Ottaway Newspapers as the only Internet Content Producer based out of their corporate headquarters in Campbell Hall, NY. In this position, Yoni works with Ottaway's newsrooms helping them develop online content and grow their online audience. He also works with the corporate web development team to ensure that editorial content has a home in new products. In addition he leads newsroom training on topics including social networking and audience development.
