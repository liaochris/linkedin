All the things we KNOW that turn out to be untrue or misguided are the real burden we carry. Releasing ourselves from these cultural and self imposed burdens is a lifetime's work. My web site, Adapt to Experience is devoted to that concept. http://www.ate.co.nz/

The root of this problem lies in our culture reinforced by Education. Real learning and real knowledge is developed by engagement with other people and in trying to achieve things in a real world. Our best ideas begin as vague notions that struggle for the right to be named and validated and turned into strong ideas with clear outcomes. Innovation is possible but social, political and economic barriers make it a difficult achievement. Everybody "wants" innovation, so long as nothing changes.

Sadly it looks like our inability to solve political, economic and environmental problems condemns us to a future that is CLOSED. In my own view it is possible for ME to change, and if I can change MY future is open. That's what I can do: that is what I'm doing. YOUR future can be open too. Some of the things we "know" are false, and are barriers to our understanding and our progress. In the process of conversation with other people, we can identify what we need to learn, and what we need to change.
