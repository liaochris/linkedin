Vanessa Braunstein’s Summary

I studied molecular and cell biology at UC Berkeley. While I was a student there, I worked 3 jobs. I was assistant to the University Chancellor, I was a research assistant under Marc Hellerstein MD, PhD in UC Berkeley's Public Health Department on the discovery on a UDP hepatic cycle, and I was a research assistant on a lactation study. After graduation, I worked for UCSF's Department of Medicine on a clinical research project on the effects of infertility medications on women's health. I worked at UCSF, Alta Bates, USC and UCLA Medical Centers. After my research at UCSF, I worked as a chemistry consultant for Phenomenex Biotechnology where I assisted New York government labs, the EPA, pharmaceutical companies, universities and other R&D departments with HPLC & GC techniques and products. While working at Phenomenex, I also was an intern in the Publicity Department at the Sunset Strip House of Blues. There, I photographed performers and learned about the music industry. Some of my photographs were published in various music magazines. After Phenomenex, I worked as a territory consultant for Abbott Labs in their cardiovascular division. After Abbott Labs, I worked for Sanofi Aventis (formerly Aventis) as a territory consultant in their Osteoporosis division, then as a specialty territory consultant in their metabolism division, and now as a manager in the metabolism business unit. I have been employed with sanofi aventis for almost 5 years. I have been employed since high school. Other jobs that I have had include assistant to a CPA at Merrill Lynch, grammer school and junior high private academic tutor, floral designer and undergraduate teacher at UC Berkeley in the Bioethics department. I played volleyball in junior high, high school and college and softball in high school. I play tennis, swim and hike recreationally.
