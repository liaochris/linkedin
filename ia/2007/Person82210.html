Steven Crane’s Summary

Steve Crane has a powerful body of experience and a unique perspective, having served both as CEO and investment banker to various public and privately held companies. Crane began his career in the late 1970’s as VP-Sales for an oilfield equipment supply company with operations throughout North America, Southeast Asia and the Middle East. Later as President of a manufacturer of proprietary composites, Crane engineered the company’s growth from two man engineering office to $100 million internationally renowned company over a six-year period. After being named CEO, he spearheaded the Company’s emergence into the public capital markets. Mr. Crane left management in the early 1990’s to enter corporate finance as a Senior Partner with a west coast-based investment-banking boutique, where he managed the firm’s micro-cap operations. Crane later returned to management as co-founder of CorpHQ, then the Internet’s first business-to-business destination for the small office-home office market. Under Mr. Crane’s leadership, the company achieved profitability; major media acclaim; average annual revenue growth of 100%; 12 major corporate partnerships; a public offering of securities; a corporate acquisition; and consolidation into a larger publicly traded Internet company in late 1999. In 2001, Mr. Crane returned to corporate finance as the Chairman/CEO of a publicly-traded venture incubator, where his team generated five consecutive years of growth and earnings and assisted in the development of seven companies. Steve Crane is also a knowledgeable and passionate advocate for small publicly traded companies, and has testified before White House staff and Congressional Members. He served on the National Association of Securities Dealers’ OTC-Bulletin Board Best Practices Committee during 1999, and co-founded The CEO Council, a Washington DC-based advocacy organization for small publicly traded comapnies. Crane is a Los Angeles native and is married with one child.
