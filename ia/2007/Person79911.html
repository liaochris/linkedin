  • Sales Associate

    Amsterdam Weekly BV

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Publishing industry)

    June 2005 — March 2007 (1 year 10 months)

    This was an advertising sales position, working on a US backed newspaper in Amsterdam, The Netherlands. At my time of joining the publication it was still at a fairly early stage in its development. I was required to present the paper to as many suitable prospective business partners as possible and to encourage them to promote themselves in our pages. In the process I was fortunate to make many good contacts in the Amsterdam business community and gained an invaluable insight into the workings of the Dutch world of commerce . I introduced many of the cities major employers to the benefits of advertising their vacancies to The Weekly's international audience whilst also bringing many other important clients to the paper such as University of Amsterdam.
    Advertising revenues rose dramatically during the period I was working on the paper. Hopefully the present team will continue to build on the hard work that has been put into that project so far. See www.amsterdamweekly.nl

  • Account Director

    Admark Advertising Ltd

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Marketing and Advertising industry)

    March 2001 — September 2002 (1 year 7 months)

    I was approached by Admark having competed against them, for a number of years in the highly volatile UK motor advertising market. I had always had a grudging respect for the guys who ran the place. Their advertisements had always been just that bit more powerful than anything we seemed able to produce. I wanted access to their studio to see how they could interpret my ideas and was confident that many of my existing clients would migrate with me, as they did . During the relatively short period I was with the agency I also brought in a respectable amount of new business and was soon travelling throughout the UK dealing with large main dealers of some of the Worlds largest motor manufacturers including Ford Motor Company, Peugeot and Vauxhall / Opel.

  • Account Manager

    Michael K Howard Ltd

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Marketing and Advertising industry)

    July 1994 — December 2000 (6 years 6 months)

    I joined this full service advertising agency having dealt with them for a number of years in my role as a Sales Manager at Westminster Press. During a 6 year period I was never out of their top 5 employees for the highest monthly client billings despite competition from 30 others also prospecting for business within the group. During my 6 years with MKH we rose from being a small provincial agency to a group with 5 UK offices. Billings increased over 20-fold during my period and the group ended up expanding and eventually taking over another competitive London agency. Eventually i left to travel Australia leaving behind a thriving business which went on to be bought out itself by one of the UKs leading ad agency networks.
