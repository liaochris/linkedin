Laura Wessner’s Summary

Laura Wessner is a communications and media professional with exceptional skills in the development and execution of strategic and tactical objectives in the public relations internal/external placement of information and ideas.

Ms. Wessner was a Communications Manager at IBM, where she generated messaging, strategy and media placement for software solutions including speech technology, telematics, sensor solutions and RFID. For six years at IBM Corporate Headquarters, as Manager, Corporate Communications Broadcast Media Relations, Ms. Wessner was in charge of electronic coverage for all IBM divisions managing product, earnings and corporate announcements. She led corporate environmental communications, created and executed print and electronic campaigns for corporate and divisional news, was proactive in crisis communications and worked with media to promote IBM strategy, foundation interests, social policy, patents, academic initiatives and technology leadership, including troubleshooting and damage control. She served the Company from 1996 to 2007.

Prior to joining IBM, Ms. Wessner was a press representative at CBS-TV Network News and Senior Press Representative at ABC-TV Network News for eleven years working for Ted Koppel at ABC News "Nightline." While at ABC-TV, she served as spokesperson for "PrimeTime Live," "20/20," "World News Tonight," and "Good Morning America."

Ms. Wessner was Media Relations Supervisor at Burson-Marsteller, working on major pharmaceutical, financial and consumer accounts, where she was the recipient of two American Women in Radio and Television Pinnacle Awards for outstanding women in the media.

Ms. Wessner received a Masters Degree from San Francisco State University graduating magna cum laude; a Bachelor's Degree from Franconia College, New Hampshire and completed a Columbia University Business School Executive Education Course.
