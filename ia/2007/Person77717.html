Sean Ellis’s Experience

  • Vice President, Marketing

    LogMeIn, Inc.

    (Privately Held; 201-500 employees; Computer Software industry)

    March 2003 — Present (4 years 8 months)

    First marketing hire at LogMeIn, which provides "connectivity as a service" to more than 25 million users of our SaaS solutions. Apps include remote PC control, systems management and mobile smart phone support. Recruited by CEO (former Founder/CEO Uproar) to replicate our marketing model that led to Uproar IPO. Raised $10 million in Series A VC financing largely on go-to-market strategy, execution and reporting. Responsible for marketing strategy, product marketing, branding, advertising, trial/lead generation, PR and trade shows. Results confidential. LogMeIn's VCs often request that I consult with other investment ventures regarding their marketing strategy. Launched LogMeIn brand in 2004 and gained market traction through innovative "free-to-premium" business model and efficient direct response online advertising programs.

  • Director Worldwide Marketing

    Graphisoft

    (Public Company; 201-500 employees; GPHG; Computer Software industry)

    September 2002 — April 2003 (8 months)

    Graphisoft publishes architectural CAD software and was the largest publicly traded software company in Hungary. As the senior marketing executive, led all marketing activities including product packaging, branding, advertising, PR, website design, and coordinating co-marketing activities with subsidiaries and resellers in 80 countries. Tripled daily assigned trial leads in six months by introducing effective online lead generation programs. With improved marketing efficiency, Graphisoft returned to profitability in H1 2003 (following several unprofitable quarters). Experience illuminated my preference for clean slate startups rather than turnaround situations. Recruited to join LogMeIn. Graphisoft was acquired in 2007.

  • President European Operations

    Uproar LTD

    (Public Company; 201-500 employees; UPRO; Internet industry)

    August 2000 — March 2001 (8 months)

    Uproar was the market-leading web property for advertising supported online games and publicly traded on NASDAQ. Promoted to replace founder as the senior international executive responsible for overall European operations. Uproar Europe had 50 employees with offices in London and Hamburg and a development center in Budapest, Hungary. My charter was to streamline costs while maintaining the European sales contribution. Reduced costs by 40% through deep staff cuts and maintained sales growth. Retained all key team members through challenging staff reductions. Uproar was acquired by Vivendi Universal in March 2001.

  • Vice President, Marketing

    Uproar LTD

    (Public Company; 51-200 employees; UPRO; Internet industry)

    July 1997 — August 2000 (3 years 2 months)

    Led Uproar's marketing from inception to online game category leadership, surpassing Yahoo, Microsoft, Sony Station and several VC-backed startups in traffic. Responsible for marketing strategy, customer acquisition programs, external advertising via agency, PR, as well as refining key brand touch points and managing customer support team of 30. Built Uproar's traffic to the 8th biggest web property overall in terms of total usage time, a key metric for an advertising supported property. Achieved the lowest registered user acquisition cost of any publicly traded company for a free online service, efficiently attracting over 8 million registered users. Key programs for achieving success were creating cutting edge ROI measurement systems (optimized all marketing dollars on direct impact to Uproar's banner inventory) and introduced an innovative trivia widget in 1997 that eventually appeared on over 30,000 websites including CNN.com.

  • Product Marketing Manager

    Uproar LTD

    (Public Company; 11-50 employees; UPRO; Internet industry)

    July 1996 — June 1997 (1 year)

    Led 2-person marketing team responsible for customer acquisition and creating compelling brand experience. First US-based employee, initially hired in sales role. After recommending aggressive marketing strategy to CEO, he suggested that I temporarily lead marketing (role soon became permanent). Within six weeks, established enough market traction to attract Microsoft as first paying advertiser. Cost efficiently drove traffic with tighltly tracked online marketing and through a co-branded CBS SportsLine trivia game, a biz dev deal that I initiated via a cold call.

  • Advertising Executive (tech accounts)

    New World Publishing

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Newspapers industry)

    October 1994 — July 1996 (1 year 10 months)

    NWP published business journals in Budapest, Warsaw and Prague. Became number one Budapest-based sales executive by cold-calling PC manufactures and launching innovative program to automate faxes to local resellers suggesting they piggyback on the PC brand advertisements. Negotiated several sales speaking only Hungarian. Managed profitable technology-focused advertising supplement with Hungary's largest technology exhibition.
