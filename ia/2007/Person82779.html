Robert Stoudt’s Summary

Mr Stoudt is a Senior IT Specialist for IBM's Global Security Services Delivery (GSSD) . He currently provides web Vulnerability Assessments for a global financial services company

Robert has over 12 years experience in Information Technology (IT). During his career he has held Senior UNIX, Windows, and Network Administrator positions but found once mastered, these positions could not challenge his desire to learn for long. He has spent his last 6 years dedicated to Information Security which has enabled him to utilize the breadth of his knowledge to secure and defend corporations in a ever changing landscape.

He has been the lead IBM Information Security Advisor ( ISA ) for Dow Chemical, NiSource and IBM's Universal Server Farm ( USF ). In this customer facing position he grew his soft skills while developing and managing the implementation of the customers corporate security policies, procedure development, security architecture planning/review, Security Incident Management and management of contracted IBM MSSD services.

Robert moved to the Vulnerability Assessment team (Ethical Hack) July `06 to allow him to further grow his technical skills. Some of his accomplishments while on his new team includes: publishing a remote AIX DOS, giving a security presentation at the 2007 S+P Symposium and a windows local assessment utility.

His passions includes forensic analysis, incident response, vulnerability analysis and security R&D.
