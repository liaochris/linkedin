Jeff Salisbury’s Experience

  • English & Journalism Teacher/Publications Adviser

    Wayland Union Schools

    (Educational Institution; 201-500 employees; Primary/Secondary Education industry)

    August 1985 — Present (22 years 6 months)

    My primary classroom assignment is to teach two journalism courses: Jrn. I - Intro to News Writing and Jrn. II - Desktop Publising. In Jrn.II students design and produce print and online versions of the school newspaper, yearbook and literary/arts magazine. Additionally, I often teach at least one section of such courses as Current Issues, Technical Writing, Creative Writing, Drama as Literature and College Writing.
    My students maintain "personal production portfolios" containing various assignments and projects. This portfolio is evaluated periodicallyand serves as a component in determining the final trimester grade.

    My classroom is designed to be a 21st century classroom in the sense that we make use of as many new technologies as possible. This includes the use of email and the Internet for communicating with parents and students on a weekly basis. Assignments are electronically distributed and progress reports are available via email each week.
