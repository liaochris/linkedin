Jeremy Stone’s Summary

Jeremy has 27 years leadership & management experience in the military, law enforcement, financial institution & risk management arenas. He was Chief of Security Operations at the International Monetary Fund's headquarters in Washington DC, providing strategic & operational oversight of the physical, executive protection, travel & fire life safety security programs related to risk, crisis management, contingency planning & the effective delivery of contract security services. He worked with international institutions, local, federal, & international law enforcement agencies to coordinate security efforts for visiting Heads of State, special events, the IMF/World Bank conferences & when responding to terrorist threats.

Jeremy developed his leadership & management skills as a British Army Officer, commanding field artillery & air defence troops in the United Kingdom & Germany & with active service in the Falklands War & Northern Ireland conflict. Later, as a Detective Chief Inspector in the Hong Kong Police, he spent several years as a crime manager, investigating & bringing to justice suspects involved in a wide range of criminal activity. During his 18-year career, he enjoyed tours of duty with the Organized Crime & Triad Bureau, Regional & District Crime Units, the Force's Information Systems Wing as an IT project manager & was trained as a Bomb Disposal Officer. In his final year, Jeremy was appointed Assistant District Commander in a busy police district in the bustling Kowloon peninsula.

Jeremy was educated at Hurstpierpoint College & is a graduate of the Royal Military Academy Sandhurst (International Affairs & Strategic Studies). He holds an MBA Certificate in Accounting from Heriot-Watt University, a Graduate Diploma in Personnel Management & Public Administration from The University of Hong Kong & is studying for a Master in Security Management from Edith Cowan University. He is fluent in Cantonese & conversant in French, German & Spanish.
