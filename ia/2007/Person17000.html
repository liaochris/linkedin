Christine Comaford-Lynch’s Summary

Five-time CEO/entrepreneur defining new markets, developing products to serve them, executing sales, marketing and product strategies. Assisted over 700 of the Fortune 1000 in implementing new technology. Was a founder of First Professional Bank, Kuvera Associates, Corporate Computing, planetU and Artemis Ventures (all acquired or taken public).

Over 20 years in operational high tech positions at Microsoft, Lotus, Adobe and Apple; strategy advisor to Oracle and Symantec.

Former Board of Director/Observer roles: ViewCentral, Motiva (NASDAQ: SEBL), Taviz, Toolwire, DataMirror (NASDAQ: DMCX), PlanetU (NYSE: NWS), Corporate Computing (NYSE: CA), Kuvera Associates.

Current Board roles: Member of Advisory and Investment Committees for Novus Ventures, Advisor to Volunteer Match, Committee for Litquake.

• Led her startup Corporate Computing (acquired by LBMS, then acquired by Computer Associates, NYSE: CA) in building a customer base of 700 of the Fortune 1000 in three years

• Led her startup planet U (her shares acquired by News Corp, NYSE: NWS), to an increase in value seven-fold in the first 16 months

• Participated in two IPOs: DataMirror (NASDAQ: DMCX, as a Board Director for 4 years) and LBMS (formerly NASDAQ: LBMS, acquired by Computer Associates)as an SVP

• Received numerous additional entrepreneurial awards and recognition from press and business schools including Fortune, Forbes, Wall Street Journal, Stanford, Harvard, Haas and Northwestern Universities.

• Published over 300 articles to date on entrepreneurship, software development, I.T. management for publications including PC Week, PC Magazine, Forbes, Association for Computing Machinery (ACM), Harvard Business Review

• Delivered over 400 speeches to date. Keynote speaker for Intel, Microsoft, Palm, Department of Defense, and many more. Has spoken in 14 countries on four continents.
