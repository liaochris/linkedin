Robyn Weisman’s Summary

I have a knack for taking seemingly abstruse and sometimes tedious subject matter and making it accessible and compelling for my readers. Although my liberal arts background may seem to put me at a disadvantage when writing about such technology-based trends as server virtualization and mobile device management, my desire to grasp as much as possible about a given topic leads me to write about it in a clear, straightforward manner-if only to better understand it.

When I interview technology professionals, I take for granted that they love their work, whether they are analyzing enterprise migration toward on-demand CRM services or the way in which they developed their spot-cooling appliance. I find their enthusiasm to be contagious, and I see reporting as an excuse to get these experts to teach me about subjects I would otherwise not know about.

Perhaps my greatest strength as an editor is my ability to assess a reporter's background and aptitude and help him or her leverage these skills so that they can write a great piece. Moreover, I maintain productive and enjoyable relationships with my reporters because they know I come from a writing background and can identify with their aspirations and concerns.

My objective as an editor has always been to put forth the highest quality material possible within given constraints, and I believe that can only happen when writers know how essential they are to the fulfillment of such a goal.

Robyn Weisman’s Specialties:

I have reported on myriad subjects within the business and high-tech spaces, from publishing workflows to Microsoft antitrust. You name it, and chances are I covered it at one time or another.

I have specialized in covering CRM and enterprise applications and services; topics germane to enterprise CIOs and IT managers, such as EDRM and database management; and products, services, and trends emanating from Apple—from its Xserve line of enterprise servers to the iPod.
