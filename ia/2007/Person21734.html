David M. Johnson, CFA’s Summary

My career path continues to focus on distressed portfolio investments in a hands-on, get-shit-done manner: preserving value, creating value, and growing value by being aggressive and creative in designing, negotiating, and executing strategies to improve financial performance.

From January 2006 to July 2007 I worked with Health Care Property Investors, Inc., a publicly-traded REIT, to provide solutions for under-performing assets. To date I have managed the acquisition, disposition, or workout of commercial real estate worth over $500 million.

In the fall of 2005 I phased out of my position as a Managing Director of a boutique investment bank in New York representing privately-held businesses for sale. I had also provided due diligence and back-office support to the firm's start-up activist hedge fund, which targeted special-situation investments.

Prior to that I spent four years with Alvarez & Marsal, Inc., a turnaround consulting firm with such clients as HealthSouth, Spiegel and Warnaco. My work involved the financial restructuring and hands-on operation of multiple troubled companies, including negotiations with vendors, creditors, and government prosecutors, and much crisis management.

Most significantly, my tenure at A&M included a 24 month period as Vice President of a $150 million (sales) client company during its financial and operational restructuring. I worked closely with the Chief Restructuring Officer, attorneys, and company creditors in restructuring the company's balance sheet to avoid a bankruptcy filing. During that process, we also restructured and ran the company, not only keeping it afloat but expanding the business.

I am a CFA Charterholder; my proficiency with financial modeling, budgets, projections, and tax ramifications is superb. I am skilled in scenario and sensitivity analysis, LBO models, derivative pricing and valuation, budget creation, and financial statement analysis.
