Sylvain Simoneau’s Summary

« Community, Content and Commerce ». The formula is nearly ten years old and, yet, it still defines the business model and the key to success of the majority of the leading websites.

My activity :
It consists in boosting the efficiency of my clients’ websites, mainly by identifying the best ways to increase the “average revenue per user” and by applying the appropriate changes. Depending on the cases, the work can focus on increasing the traffic, the number of “leads” or their degree of qualification, to initiate a mutation of the business model, to redefine the market segment aimed by the website, to adapt the communication to the new expectations of internet users (blogs, video…), or to improve the overall ergonomics and various shopping incentives.

My methodology :
If the considered solutions vary from one case to another, the initial analysis systematically relies on the recipe that made the success of Amazon, CNET, AOL, MSN and many others : Community, Content and Commerce. When appropriate, the process consists in combining the three levels to make them as complementary and natural as possible. A community-enabled site must allow the visitors to share useful advice and interact with the content; a content-enabled site must provide recommendations and guidance about its products or themes; a commerce-enabled site must provide a simple, efficient and convenient way to obtain the featured (or derivative) services and products.

My experience :
I achieved high-end projects for several leading companies, among them :
• CNET Networks France
• Microsoft (US, EMEA)
• INRIA (french National Institute for Research in Computing and Automation)
• Groupe Pages Jaunes (France Telecom’s Yellow Pages)

My formation :
Internet user and website creator since 93, I have a Master of Multimedia Information Systems, summa cum laude. Also a high-tech journalist over the past 15 years, I regularly cover the technical and behavioral mutations of the IT sector.
