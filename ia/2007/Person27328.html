Eric Blum’s Experience

  • Franchise Owner

    1-800-GOT-JUNK? (Self-employed)

    (Self-Employed; 11-50 employees; Consumer Services industry)

    August 2004 — Present (3 years 4 months)

    I own the Bucks County (Philadelphia North) franchise of 1-800-GOT-JUNK? We are the world's largest and most professional junk removal service. Our professional teams will clean out your basement, attic, garage, backyard or storage unit/warehouse. We do all of the loading and cleanup, so all you do is point! Old appliances, construction debris, electronics and furniture are the most common items we haul away.

    I started my franchise in August of 2004. I worked in the IT field for over 8 years and wanted a change. So, I bought the franchise, started with one truck, one helper and a lot of energy. Today I have 5 trucks, 10+ employees, an Operations Manager and the goal of making it a $1M+/year business (we are on our way!)

    Owning my own business has been the hardest and most rewarding experience in my life.

  • Programmer/Systems Analyst

    NCS Technologies

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Computer Software industry)

    2002 — 2004 (2 years)

    I went back to work for NCS (thanks for having be back!) as a Programmer/Systems Analyst. My assignment was for AT&T Consumer running their ORS Reporting system containing 8TB (that's a lot for you non-geeks) of data. Developed custom Oracle Reporting and OLAP solutions for various user communities. It was a very interesting project to say the least. Got tired of sitting in a cubical and decided to started my own business. I even got my AT&T manager to leave AT&T and buy his own 1-800-GOT-JUNK? franchise!! I love watching his success and watching him grow his business to a $1M+ business like mine.

  • Programmer/Consultant

    Hesta/MI Services

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Information Technology and Services industry)

    2000 — 2001 (1 year)

    A friend in the IT industry recommended working for Hesta. I was very interested because they specialized in Oracle Financials. Can you believe I went to work for a company that was run out of the owners home! That was a leap of faith and when I started my own business out of my home, it gave me hope for my own future. I did some ColdFusion dev for a while. Finally I was able to get onto an Oracle Financials project that was interesting to say the least. Good expereince, learned some hard lessons, but made me a better person in the end. The company was bought out during the summer of 2001 and lost my job just after 9/11. I think that was one of the best things that ever happened to me as my generation never expereinced a market downturn like that. It made me realize that no job is 100% secure and you need to control your own destiny. That is what led me to start my own business several years later.

  • Programmer

    NCS Technologies

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Information Technology and Services industry)

    1998 — 2000 (2 years)

    NCS is a small consulting firm in Piscataway, NJ. I left CSC to go to a smaller firm where there was more opportunity to be noticed and wear more hats. First assignment was at AT&T Business developing a platform for customer service agents to adjust customers bills. We wrote it in JAVA with a Tuxedo interface to the legacy backend. This platform never got past the prototype phase (as far as I know). After that project I went in-house to work on some various projects and that is where I picked up Oracle Reports experience. From this point on I developed more Oracle Reports then I would like to admit. After a while I got restless and looked to work for SAP, Oracle or a firm that specialized in those products.

  • Consultant

    Computer Sciences Corporation (CSC)

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; CSC; Information Technology and Services industry)

    1997 — 1998 (1 year)

    CSC was the first consulting firm I worked for in the IT industry. It was a great experience and a great place to get started in the industry. Their training program taught us to be professionals on our clients work sites and other useful skills. We were trained in COBOL, Powerbuilder and C. That was a well rounded set of languages to get us started and gave exposure to various types of programming.

    Most of my assignments were either COBOL or Internet related projects. RPR Pharma and First USA were heavy COBOL assignments. I remember having to fix a 10K line program that had no comments or indents....that was fun. After that I got into website design and programming.

  • Assistant Project Supervisor

    Ryan Homes

    (Public Company; Construction industry)

    1996 — 1997 (1 year)

    Ryan Homes was my first job out of college. I worked as an electricians helper since I was 15 (don't tell IBEW Local 98 as I was supposed to be 18) so I was comfortable in the construction field. Not really sure what career I wanted to pursue (and the Secret Service and other federal law enforcement agencies do not hire people right out of college usually), I jumped at the opportunity to learn the home building industry. My PM and I developed a community in South Jersey. While there I supervised the building of over 30 single family homes. I think I decided to make a change after my PM got punched in the mouth by a plumber and the plumber threatened to kill me. IT sounded like a safer place for me :) My friend Josh worked for CSC and got me an interview there.
