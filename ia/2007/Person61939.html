Matthijs Kouw’s Summary

Technology and new media savvy 28-year old, who has acquired master’s degrees in Philosophy and Science and Technology Studies (STS); is well-read in contemporary debates surrounding technological innovation; has extensive working experience in ICT; and who hopes to extend and deepen his knowledge about the various ways in which technology and society are intertwined.

Main areas of interest and specialization:

- The socio-technical dimensions of various forms of ubiquitous computing, i.e. RFID (Radio Frequency Identification) and the EPCglobal network, GPS, and other locative media: now that information technologies are becoming ubiquitous, how does this affect notions of ‘subject’, ‘innovation’, ‘technology’, ‘information’, ‘meaning’, ‘place’, etc.?

- The so-called physical Internet, which consists of RFID hard- and software, and a global information infrastructure that distributes information pertaining to objects that have been tagged with RFID transponders: now that the material and the informational are becoming more and more intertwined, what are the implications for institutions, companies, and people’s lives in general?

- Knowledge-based economies, innovation, and social computing: to what extent and how do networked information technologies and social computing change the dynamics of innovation?

- Data visualization, primarily in relation to social cartography and ubiquitous computing: how can the vast amounts of data generated by ubiquitous computing be understood?

- Philosophies of Technology in the work of Simondon, Deleuze, and Latour that aim to understand that the world is not only all that is the actual, but also everything that may become actual.

- Contemporary Philosophy and Sociology in relation to contemporary science.

- New media studies: what is ‘new’ about new media? How can they be understood critically? Do we need 'new' media to create 'new media'?

- Trends in ICT and coding communities / Coding Cultures

Matthijs Kouw’s Specialties:

Technology assessment; technology and ICT consulting; philosophy of technology; new media theory; ubiquitous computing.
