Jason Holderness’s Summary

I've worked with computers since I was a kid, which for me means Apple IIc's and Commodore 64's. I started doing web development in 1996, when HTML was still a new term in the American dialog. I've since then learned many aspects to web design and development. I started with ASP and moved to ASP.Net. Then I moved to PHP and more recently Django. Now I'm back with PHP 5, using Symfony.

Some sites that I've worked on in the past: www.diningsantabarbara.com, www.sts.siemens.com (and their internal intranet site, many different websites for customers while working at Cuker Design, and now www.clickforlessons.com and www.enspired.com.

During my computer career, I also took time to study recording arts at Chico State. After graduation I toured for two summers on the Vans Warped Tour, where I was front of house engineer for the Ernie Ball stage. My audio career is just starting out, but I will hopefully be able to do more of it as time goes on.
