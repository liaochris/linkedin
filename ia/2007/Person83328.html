Surabhi Gurukar’s Summary

Apostrophe Design, a Bangalore, India, a graphic design firm that specializes in corporate communications projects. Clients of Apostrophe Design include: Wipro, Manipal AcuNova, Indian Institute of Science, MIT (USA), Success Across Borders (USA), 4 ways Healthcare (UK), National Centre for Biological Sciences and Musicraft.

Prior to founding Apostrophe Design in 2005, Surabhi was an Associate Designer at Ray + Keshavan, Bangalore, where she worked primarily on the Daily’s supermarket Branding, Mindtree Consulting Interiors and Himalaya Drug Company accounts. Previously, Surabhi worked for Pearlfischer (London), Baseline Magazine (UK), Singh Khosla Associates (India).

Surabhi’s marketing communications experience covers a range from consumer expendables, with particularly strong expertise in Pharma and Science related fields. She won two prizes in Wisconsin State Regional Visual Art Classic Competition. Additionly, she was the State Award Winner for the ‘Youth Art Month’ Logo Design Competition held by the Wisconsin Art Education Association, USA. Her design was chosen for the cover of Baseline, International Typographic Magazine, issue 44, UK. Her thesis project, a website on Chaos Theory was exhibited at the D&AD New Blood, Britain's Best New Creative Talent 2005 Show in London.

Surabhi is a firm believer in giving back to the community. She volunteered with runaway and abused kids in a rehabilitation centre in Wisconsin. She has taught art to the Mentally Challenged & Hearing Impaired at Mumbai’s SAVE The Children. She has taught an 8 weeks art program with the homeless children at Christel House, Bangalore.

Surabhi graduated from Srishti School of Art, Design & Technology (2001), Bangalore, where she has taught Typography I. She studied Breaking Points of Corporate Identity at Rhode Island School of Design (RISD), USA and Graphic Design at Kent Institute of Art & Design, UK.

Surabhi Gurukar’s Specialties:

Projects include Brand identity development, company brochures, web-site design, poster design, exhibition design and more recently package design.
