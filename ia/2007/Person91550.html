Bill Snyder’s Summary

About ten years back, I started to slip from the world of journalism into the "great online" — all thanks to amazon.com's decision to launch a music store. (Remember when they were just selling books?) It didn't take long until the medium won me over.

Since those early days, I've learned to combine architecture, design, technology, and usability to deliver a powerful message. First, I became an interactive copywriter who knew the medium like the back of his hand, then an effective, forward-thinking product and content manager who guided sites from vision to launch and through their cycles of growth, reorganization, and redesign.

Technology is a blast, but it should always deliver a message, never bury it. After all, it's still about getting the message to the intended recipient — on brand, in a clear, concise, compelling manner.

Nuts and Bolts:
• Led development of dynamic sites, managing strategic planning, hiring of vendors, design and information architecture, production of content, quality assurance, and the launch and maintenance of the sites.
• Managed, wrote, and edited content, assuring it was accurate, strategically focused on organizational goals, and compliant with brand standards.
• Improved usability of major corporate sites by providing clear, concise instructional copy to guide users through complex processes.

As a footnote: I'm currently looking to move back to NYC (though I will also consider opportunities in Boston and the Twin Cities).
