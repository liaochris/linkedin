Benjamin Sayers’s Summary

Over the past fifteen years I have been building my business experience by creating multiple successful start-ups, tremendous trial and error, extensive reading and research, learning from those around me and maintaining a positive forward thinking mindset.

My first formal company (IVR, Inc.) was founded in 1995 and specialized in the creation and hosting of Interactive Voice Response applications. Sold in 2001 to a foreign competitor, IVR grew to more than 15 people and several million in annual revenues.

In 2002, B2 Technologies, LLC was created while I waited out a three year non-compete in the IVR marketplace. During the first year, B2 concentrated on writing a day-training software platform that traded automatically using back-tested technical indicators and strategies. Successful and fun, this proved to be a distraction to future plans.

During 2003 and 2004, B2 focused on the resale and brokerage of used networking equipment and was the initial catalyst for our growth. With the decay of this marketplace and the upswing in VoIP equipment sales, our focus shifted to strictly Voice over IP hardware resale, leveraging the internet for marketing and global sales. This shift occurred in August of 2004 and has remained the sole focus of VoIP Supply, LLC.

In January of 2007, the company was segmented to allow VoIP Supply to focus on its core business and for our marketing and software development teams to form Sayers Media Group, LLC. Sayers Media provides a variety of services, primarily online channel marketing expertise and service to our own companies as well as other commercial customers. Sayers Media Group also consults with other businesses and organizations sharing expertise across a wide range of topics. Sayers Media Group also creates and markets its own applications and services such as PBXPrompts.com a leader in IP PBX and IVR voice prompt services and manages dozens of web properties and ecommerce web sites.

Benjamin Sayers’s Specialties:

Interactive Voice Response (IVR), Voice over IP (VoIP), online retail, small business start-up, service bureau creation, online marketing, business management, idea analysis, business plan creation and maintenance, telecommunications, customer service, problem resolution and dealing with being the CEO of a start-up as it matures.
