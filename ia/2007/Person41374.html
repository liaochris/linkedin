History
My formative programming experience was late 70's . I somehow received a TI-99/4a on a parent's employee discount (sometime around 1980-81 ?) .... assembly or BASIC with a tape recorder 'hard drive' and 32k mem. 1st time online was 1984, I remember having access to a fidonet node..cant remember if it was mine or perhaps I was 'borrowing' a well known ivy league's college's ;).

I moved to the UK in the 90's and after a string of high profile technical positions in London; post bubble, I decided that I needed to get back to programming. Since 1999 I have focused on my development and management skills and acquiring 'real world' commercial experience.

I have performed the following roles;developer,senior developer,technical architect,tester,senior tester,software configuration manager,technical reviewer,technical author,technical manager (small, mixed dev teams),technical director (SME),software estimator,finance director

I have had direct signoff on budget and line management responsibility for up to 30 individuals.

Some days, I wished I pursued my university degree in geophysics and spend my days tramping through the woods and looking at rocks. Instead I followed my interest in computers, which really is only a 'side effect' of my curiosity and desire to solve problems.
