David Perry’s Summary

As a founder of Perry-Martel in 1988, my partner Anita Martel and I launched the information technology search firm which today dominates the Canadian technology space.
A veteran of more than 910 projects, I am a student of leadership and its effect on organizations ranging from private equity ventures to global technology corporations.

I have built an extensive personal network of leading CEOs as well as up-and-coming future CEOs, permitting Perry-Martel to quickly identify the best qualified executives to grow a company and increase shareholder value.

In the past 20 years I have negotiated more than $150 million in salaries. Building executive teams that beat expectations is my passion.

Board and volunteer work includes: HR policy adviser for the Canadian Advanced Technology Alliance [CATA] 1992 - present, Vice-chair of the Canadian Technology Human Resources Board 2001-2004, Board member of the Software Human Resources Council 1996-2001, fundraising director for the Western Quebec Education Foundation 1996- present, and a Cub Scout leader 1996 -present.

My first best selling book is: "Guerrilla Marketing for Job Hunters" [John Wiley & Sons 2005] My first book was "Career Guide for the High-Tech Professional: Where the Jobs are NOW and How to Land them" [Career Press May 2004]
