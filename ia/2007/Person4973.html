Alberto Nardelli’s Summary

Alberto is currently living in London, where he works as a Development Manager at UnLtd a foundation that manages a £100 Million endowment that enables to resource social entrepreneurs through grants, project development, networking and consultancy support. Alberto focuses mainly on supporting young social entrepreneurs and Internet-driven ideas, and on strategies to develop social networks amongst social entrepreneurs.

Previously, based in Toronto, Alberto acted as Special Projects Coordinator at TakingITGlobal, developing & coordinating partnerships and collaborative projects with UN agencies, international organizations, grassroots NGOs, the public and private sectors, especially focusing on youth engagement through the Internet and creative expression. He also facilitated communications for the UN Youth Caucus for the World Summit on the Information Society. At TIG he also coordinated the Global Gallery, a programme on creative expression gathering other 10.000 images.

In Rome, Alberto worked at the Digital Youth Consortium as a Programme Manager and for the Metro Global City - Glocal Forum, as an Event Manager during the launch of Quincy Jones' We Are The Future.

Alberto has an MA in Media and Communication Studies, from Goldsmiths College, University of London, with research focusing on new media, political economy of the mass media and the creative industries. He also holds a degree in Political Sciences from LUISS University, where he also piloted the Italian version of cafebabel.com.

Alberto has written extensively, and spoken at a dozen conferences about youth engagement, the Internet and social networks, including the World Summit on the Digital Divide, the Second World Urban Forum and the International Chamber of Congress (ICC) World Congress.

He occasionally advises companies, start ups and non-for-profits on Internet strategy and web and media 2.0. Alberto runs an Italian Blog on Network Economies, Internet Markets & Innovation.

Alberto Nardelli’s Specialties:

Project management, network economies, Internet Strategy, social networks, media, branding and web 2.0, development and management of partnerships.
