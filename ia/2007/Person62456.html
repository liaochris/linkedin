Natalia Quintana’s Summary

I started my career at the age of 15 as the lighting designer for my high school's plays. At 16, I started producing a Teen Talk Show called the Green Room for the city of Pacifica's community Channel 8 in the Bay Area.
I graduated from San Diego State University with a Bachelor of Science degree in Television, Film and New Media in 2002. I worked as a camera operator on two award winning, PBS education documentaries produced by Instructional Technology Services at San Diego State University and I was also interning as a camera assistant on shows currently filming in the San Diego Area.
I joined the International Cinematographers Guild in 2002 and began working as a freelance camera assistant immediately after graduation. I also held a regular "day" job as an entertainment segment producer and weather field producer for San Diego's CBS affiliate KFMB-TV.
I focused my energy on my camera assistant career and became a full time 2nd AC on shows like the CW's "Veronica Mars" and 20th Century Fox TV's "Point Pleasant."
I moved to New York in 2005 to explore new options like acting and TV production. I graduated from the Neighborhood Playhouse's acting summer intensive in 2005 and I continue my Meisner studies to this day. I also continued to freelance as a 2nd AC on shows like HBO's "The Wire" and Spike Lee's "When the Levees Broke: A Requiem in Four Acts."
I began a new career in TV as an Associate Producer for BBC's "Honey We're Killing the Kids" and quickly moved up to Produce/Director for the same series.
I really have a passion for telling stories and I am looking forward to future Producing and Directing opportunities. I love to learn new ways to tell a story and I am currently looking to find opportunities in advertising and commercial production.
I am also focusing a lot of my energy into the craft of acting for future acting and directing opportunities.
