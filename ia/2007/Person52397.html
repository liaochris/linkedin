Luca Cutolo’s Summary

The Technical Account Manager (TAM) is the onsite owner for webMethods implementations. He is the person who understands customer environment in detail. He works with customer managers and teams to consistently provide valuable direction for critical decisions by leveraging the extensive webMethods implementation experience and product knowledge. The TAM’s major responsibilities include:
Program Management
To ensure success, the TAM is involved at every level of the project, including scheduling resources and defining end user requirements. The TAM also participates in the testing, rollout and development of training strategies and tools.
Architectural and Technical Guidance
As the webMethods technical lead on your project, the TAM owns customer webMethods technical architecture and is responsible for resolving any technical issues. The goal is to drive a technically successful engagement and ensure your environment is scalable and manageable.
Customer Advocate
The TAM should be considered an extension of customer internal team. He is the onsite customer advocate for resolving issues, escalating services needs, and product enhancement requests. He is there to make sure customers are successful, especially during critical times like final testing and production deployment.
