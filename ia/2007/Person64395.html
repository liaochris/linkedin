Nestor J Portillo’s Summary

I am a 10-year Microsoft veteran. In 1997, I joined Microsoft and I was responsible to develop the Breadth customer segment, then I moved onto winning the Financial Services where I got the highest penetration of Windows Srv licenses as well as a the highest penetration of enterprise support and consulting services; then I moved on to the partners group unit managing key partners; later I was responsible for the Public Sector as a sales manager dealing with a Open Source bill for government agencies.

I moved into US to develop the Developer Support Business for Latin America based in Dallas.

Actually, I am responsible for the Microsoft technical Communities and MVP Program for the Americas and Japan. I lead team of program and relationship managers based in Tokyo, Toronto, Sao Paulo, Mexico City and Seattle managing 2,000+ program members called Most Valuable Professionals. I am currently based at the Sammamish Campus in WA State.

Prior to Microsoft, I worked for Siemens Nixdorf as a senior consultant and then as an account manager for the high-end UNIX systems product line as a product manager for Latam. Later I moved onto The Venezuelan Internal Revenue Service as a Development lead team manager responsible for modernizing the Organization’s customs collection capability based on Microsoft Platforms. After Seniat I moved to IBM Corp responsible for RS/6000 business for Oil industry, banking and Telcos.

Academically, I hold a B.S. in Computer Science (1989), a Marketing postgraduate degree (1991), an innovation management postgraduate degree (1992), a Master in Business Administration – MBA (1996) and Advanced Management Program and leadership @ Universidad de La Sabana, Colombia (2000). I also participated in the Latam Leadership Development Program (2000), Microsoft Bench (2005) and Microsoft Expo (2006) as well as several other Exec Education Programs at Microsoft.

I speak Spanish, English and Brazilian Portuguese. Currently studying Japanese.
