Alexander Saint Croix’s Summary

I grew up on the Internet frontier. I designed my first web site when I was 14. At 17 I was working with image morphography and interactive multimedia design.

In college I studied electronic art, human computer interface, cognitive linguistics, sentential logic and first order logical language design before I ever knew what a for-loop was.

At 19 I was designing flash games for Golden Books in Manhattan. Since then I've worked for Fortune 50 clients, mom & pop shops, small nonprofits, giant international corporations, service companies, industrial companies, world-class research institutions, and for the simple love of it. I loved that the most.

I got into the Open Source Java scene late in 2002 and got interested in rule engineering. I gave the salutatory address for the University of Minnesota's College of Liberal Arts class of 2003 later that year.

After finishing school, I spent 18 months studying the classics and writing Open Source software. I became active in the Drools rule engine project and implemented a JSR-94 stack before privation sucked me back into the market. After a very successful 90-day, 90 hours a week stint developing e-commerce sites in Java and XML, I returned to the University of Minnesota's Office of Information Technology.

I helped the U of MN craft new security policies to encourage responsible and safe computing among students. We used to have hundreds of student computers infected at any given time. Now we have perhaps 10 on a bad day.

In 2005 I served as an Innovation Fellow at the University of Minnesota, identifying and testing strategically important technologies. My findings have all been implemented, and I was asked to serve a second term in 2006.

Now I research modal logic and aporetics, and work on my rule engine "Arete".
