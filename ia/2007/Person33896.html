Hassan Aït-Kaci’s Summary

Formal design and efficient implementation of very high-level and multi-paradigm programming environments, with an immediate, but not exclusive, emphasis on rule-based and constraint-based programming, object-orientation, logic and functional programming. I am also interested in combining (symbolic) Artificial Intelligence (AI) with (numerical) optimization as done in Operations Research (OR) and Probablistic Models. Areas of application are: specification, certification, and optimization of business and government organizational logistics, knowledge acquisition (data mining), bioinformatics, computational linguistics, machine learning, ``semantic web'' (XML/RDF), automated reasoning, etc., ...

Hassan Aït-Kaci’s Specialties:

Exploiting cutting-edge research results applied to the correct specification and efficient implementation of intra- and inter-enterprise logistic systems.

  • Professor of Computing Science

    January 1995 — September 2001 (6 years 9 months)

    Full professorship appointment with tenure; Director, Intelligent Software Group. Until December 1996, NSERC Industrial Chair in Intelligent Software Systems. Research: Objects and Constraints for Intelligent Internet Resource Processing. Interests in programming paradigms based on logic, constraints, and type theory, and the formal interplay of computation and approximation. Teaching - CMPT117: Introduction to Internet Programming in Java and its Friends, CMPT-212: MS Windows Application Programing with C++, CMPT-383: Principles of Programming Languages, CMPT-379: Compiler Design, CMPT-384: AI/Symbolic Computing, CMPT-883: Graduate seminar on advanced Internet Application Design using Java, CORBA, and XML.

  • Senior Member of Research Staff

    Digital Equipment Corporation

    (Privately Held; 5001-10,000 employees; Computer Hardware industry)

    September 1988 — December 1994 (6 years 4 months)

    Research Project Director (the Paradise Project) Paris Research Laboratory, Rueil-Malmaison, France. I set up and led a research project on executable constraint specifications. This effort extends my previous work on LIFE to encompass theory and practice of constraint-based programming, including the design and implementation of a multi-paradigm environment for advanced applications with enhanced programming productivity.
