Kasper Kanstrup’s Summary

Policy development and project administration have been my primary focus for my academic and professional experienced – especially in a development context. I have been involved in both the strategic and pragmatically aspects of project management as well as in establish partnerships between relevant organisations.

Working with development aid, capacity building and resource management programmes have provided me with a thorough insight in preparing, implementing and monitoring programmes. Qualifications, which have been refined in my job as a political consultant, where policy and project development are among my main responsibilities, while my job as monitoring development programmes funded by Danida in the Ministry of Foreign Affairs have good understanding of important parameters to optimise the quality of development programmes.

Through my work I have been involved in developing methodological exercises for organisations when they operationalise health programmes, which resulted in that I am a credited co-author on the publication ‘Confronting HIV/AIDS Through Youth Involvement – A Catalogue of Ideas for NGOs’, to optimise the effectiveness and sustainability of health programmes.

Furthermore, I have developed both good political and cultural sensitivity working for organisations as the Ministry of Foreign Affairs and the Danish Parliament as well as in foreign environments in Uganda and South Africa. This have improved my advocacy skills and taught me the importance of working in a process orientated manner, as it is the quality of all the different components, which ensure that the goals are achieved in the best possible way.
