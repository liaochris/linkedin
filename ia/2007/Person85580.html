Thor List’s Summary

Thor List is an expert software designer and developer with more than a decade of commercial experience from major European and US companies. In the last 8 years he was the CTO of CMLabs Inc. in New York, Head of Technology for Orcawise in Dublin and Chief Scientist for Pointstar in Denmark. He is a specialist in designing and developing large-scale, cross-platform software solutions bridging multiple programming languages (such as C++, C#, Java, Smalltalk and advanced web development). He has developed solutions for Windows, Linux, UNIX, Macintosh, hand-held computers, set-top boxes, mobile phones and proprietary hardware. He is very hands-on and always expects the highest standard work from himself and his teams. With years of practical experience coding everything from kernel drivers, hardware interfaces, multithreaded client/server solutions, financial software and most technologies in use on the Internet today he effectively runs large projects where cross-skills are required.

In his roles as founder and head of technology he has more than 7 years experience with project management and budget planning. For CMLabs, Orcawise and Pointstar he headed both initial customer contact, technology sales presentations and on-site project management. He has filed two technology patents in the US and Europe and participated in numerous investor meetings, academic projects and government and EU project applications.

Thor List expects to receive his PhD in Artificial Intelligence from the University of Edinburgh in 2007. He holds a Master's degree in Astrophysics and a Bachelor’s degree in Physics and Computer Science from the University of Copenhagen. He also held the position of Research Fellow at the Institute for Perception, Action & Behaviour at Edinburgh University.

Thor List’s Specialties:

Intelligent software architectures, multi-platform distributed software, computer vision, human interfaces, network and server installations, software project management, technology design and development.
