Geof Lambert’s Summary

I am affiliated with: California IPv6 Task Force IPv6 (IPv6 is Internet Protocol version6...the new and improved version of the Internet), the North American IPv6 Task Force, the global IPv6 Forum, Rotary International, Digital Divide Fellowship, TechCoire, Rotary Peace Center at Cal Berkeley. I am the founder of the The Tuesday Group which is a culinary social group that gets together at various restaurants to have fun. At various stages in my life I have been a professional chef, and enjoy spending time in kitchens, restaurants and wineries. In the Rotary world I have been a Rotary Youth Exchange student, and a Cultural Scholar sponsored by Rotary District 2090 in Perugia, Italy. As a Rotarian I was a member of the Rotary Club of Fair Oaks for over 15 years. I was elected to be the club president in Rotary year 2005-2006. I am Past Chair of the District Foundation Scholarship Committee. I am one of the original founders of the Interact Club at Bella Vista High School. I am also a past co-counselor for the Rotary Center for International Studies in Peace and Conflict Resolution at the University of California at Berkeley, and I am Past District 5180 Peace Scholar Chair. The International Fellowship of Digital Technology Rotarians is an organization that I founded to help put affordable Internet access in the hands of everybody. I am a member of the Rotary Foundation Bequest Society. I am the founder of Digaria, and I am on the board of the San Juan Education Foundation.

Professionally I am a Chief Internet Evangelist/Vice President with Coversant.

Also, I am a founding partner of senior level executive search and advisory board consulting firm known as the Maxson Group, and a consultant with Vector Consulting Network.

  • Member

    XMPP Standards Foundation

    (Non-Profit; 11-50 employees; Internet industry)

    August 2007 — Present (5 months)

    MISSION OF XMPP STANDARDS FOUNDATION: To build an open, standardized, secure, feature-rich, widely-deployed, decentralized infrastructure for real-time communication and collaboration over the Internet.

    We seek to achieve that goal by developing the world's best open protocols for instant messaging, presence, and other forms of near-real-time communication, based on the IETF's Extensible Messaging and Presence Protocol (XMPP). By "best" we mean simplest, most extensible, most powerful, most secure. We continually judge our work by these criteria, and invite others to do the same.

    The XSF's product is protocols; the XSF's market is developers. We do not write code; instead, we make it possible for others to write code. We listen to developers, service providers, and end users. regarding the kinds of problems they want to solve, and we work with them to create protocols that solve those problems.
