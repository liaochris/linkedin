Andres Armeda’s Summary

Andres Armeda has over 10 years of IT experience in the United States Navy. Mr. Armeda managed a team of 20 network agents as a technical lead to over 300,000 users on the United States Navy Marine Corps Intranet (NMCI). When stationed in Naples, Italy, Mr. Armeda worked as the Operations Manager at Naval Satellite Communications Station, Lago Patria Italy, the US Navy's priemere Standard Tactical Entry Point (STEP). he served as the site manager, coordinator, and liasian for the Defense Information Systems Agency (DISA), where his management of over 36 operators resulted in the achievement of the European STEP Site of the Year award from DISA in 2002.

Mr. Armeda designed, implemented, and managed over 340 tactical ground mobile forces communication missions and more than 160 Fleet SHF missions, including several Presidential support missions. Mr. Armeda was recognized by the Defense Information Systems Agency for flawless planning, coordinating, and executing the termination of 21 high priority missions in support of crucial wartime operations.

Mr. Armeda's training includes Journeyman-Networking Core from the Fleet Training Center in San Diego, Ft. Gordon Satellite Communications (SATCOM) Operator training, Information Systems Technician School, EDS training on Security + Certification, C-Band and Ku-band Operator Course from ArrowHead, Multiplexer Integrated DSCS Automated System from ISEC/SAIC, Teleport TMCS Monitoring and Control System Course from ITT, Packet Star Asynchronous Exchange (PSAX) from DISA, Heavy Terminal/Medium Terminal Modernization Program Course from Harris Corporation, and Primary Leadership Development Program Course from the USN in Naples Italy.
