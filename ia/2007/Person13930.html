Abbas Zaidi’s Summary

Honed in a Database Consulting background in niche industries for a decade, Abbas gained his Business Transformation edge through years of tactical experience in Information Management and Business Process Engineering. Information Management Consulting on Document Management Systems and Workflow Management Systems like SpyderWeb (by BTG at the GOMO, Pentagon,) and NovaManage (worldwide) through the mid 90's enabled Abbas to gain an advanced perspective of the field years before Documentum and Hummingbird defined an entire genre. Strategic vision is his specialty, as well as his passion. Abbas likes to examine an industry from a variety of perspectives, and using proven communications skills, builds bridges where none exist while gaining precious insight few can claim. This has proven to be the case time and again, and is the reason why Abbas finds great friendships with previous colleagues, even where he may not have maintained contact for years.

Abbas Zaidi’s Specialties:

Strategic Business Transformation and Tactical Business Process Engineering, using available as well as newly developed applications of proven technologies.

  • Systems Analyst / Consultant

    SEMA Inc., Alexandria, VA

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Computer Software industry)

    June 1993 — May 1995 (2 years)

    Architected the User Interface on WMATA's ground-breaking implementation of ParaTransit, with BDM as prime. Utilized XDB Pro, the powerful XDB 4-GL, to construct a foolproof character-based user-interface which managed memory within 640K. This eventually increased to EMM-aided 1MB, which allocated and de-allocated resources and menu permissions based on a user's modifiable role-designation. Our character-based system was fast, responsive and conflict independent despite parallel transaction processing.
    My PM, Kelly O’Neil, empowered us to take risks, which paid off when a re-architecture of the UI resulted in additional capabilities we hadn't even considered before. I received a glowing recommendation after the 13 month project was over, and thanks to great recommendations from my SEMA managers (Kay Mahan & Tony Starks), I was awarded the "Sustained Superior Performance Award for 1994". This was my first professional project, and my career couldn't have started on a higher note.
