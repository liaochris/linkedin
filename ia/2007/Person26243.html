Eric Hines’s Summary

Eric Hines, a GIAC Certified Intrusion Analyst (GCIA) and Certified Information Systems Security Professional (CISSP) has worked in the Information Security Industry for over 13 years. Before founding Applied Watch, Mr. Hines worked as a Defense contractor to the Department of Defense in information warfare. Eric was subcontracted to perform penetration testing on the Internal Revenue Service and worked closely with several large defense contractors later presenting directly to the Defense Information Systems Agency (DISA) Technology Insertion Panel. Mr. Hines previously held positions as the Manager of Penetration Testing for SBC Datacom/Pacific Telesis Company, NUASIS Corporation, and is the former CEO of NetStream, a company he successfully sold to a public stock exchange company in British Columbia. In previous open disclosure research, Mr. Hines published the first advisory on circumventing Virtual Private Network appliances and spoke on the subject at Blackhat Briefings 2001 in Las Vegas, NV. During his research in open disclosure, Eric and other researchers in his organization published over a dozen advisories over a period of two years.

Mr. Hines' other security-related whitepapers have been published by SANS Institute, OpenBSD.org, LinuxSecurity.com, and SecurityFocus.com and was nominated by MIT University as the Top 100 most influencial technologists in 2002. Eric is responsible for the company's overall direction and management of operations. Additionally, Eric handles advertising and marketing communications.
