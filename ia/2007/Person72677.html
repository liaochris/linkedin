Rick Van Ness’s Summary

Rick Van Ness is a marketing specialist with over 25 years of product marketing, strategic planning, and entrepreneur experience for technology products.

Rick is a facilitator for major new business initiatives to help companies grow revenues. He helps organizations with strategic focus, message creation, and then translation of overall strategy into individual plans and initiatives. Rick is trained in business planning with the technical strength to recognize and initiate breakthrough contributions. He is excellent at understanding and communicating complex ideas to new audiences.

As Consultant at GrowthConnection, LLC, Rick specializes in three areas: business plans for entrepreneurs to obtain new venture financing, strategic or operational plans for established companies to enhance revenue and profit, and radically simple personal plans to propel teams and managers to success.

As Co-founder and CEO for Compography, Inc., Rick started a company that made internet software using on-the-fly adaptive layout technology to increase advertising revenue for online publishers.

As VP Marketing for Zetec, Inc. (owned by Roper Industries), Rick led the strategic planning that refocused the company’s initiatives onto its most lucrative market and executed many initiatives to reach new customers and sell more effectively.

As Business Development Specialist for Hewlett Packard Inc., Rick directed business planning, marketing, sales, and public relations for a team focused on bringing the “world’s smallest web server” to market. These efforts resulted in two “Best 100 Products of 1999” awards and a “Best of Show” award.

Rick was ranked in the top 10% of Hewlett Packard’s Product Marketing professionals and combined his technical background with his business strengths to introduce innovative electronic test and measurement products and enter new markets.

Rick Van Ness’s Specialties:

Product Marketing: Product Strategy, Pricing, Distribution Channels, Promotions

Strategic Planning: Helping leadership teams focus on major initiatives and develop the supporting plans down to the individual level to ensure successful execution.

Scenario Planning: an exercise to make plans more robust

Business plans to raise capital for new ventures.
