Matthew Edmond’s Summary

Originally working with a local company in Japan to establish Japan's first SWsoft reselling business. This provided a stepping-stone for SWsoft into the "particular" Japanese market, while at the same time providing a valuable localisation headstart and knowledge about working with Japanese businesses.

SWsoft decided to launch fully into the Japanese market in 2004 and I was brought on to establish the Japanese Branch and pipeline. Setting up shop in Japan is more about relationships than anything, but money does play a rather key part in the success of the venture. SWsoft is now working with a few very good companies to build strong partnerships to see us move forward smoothly.

In early 2005 SWsoft Japan transformed from a Branch entity to a fully established corporation installing Nagaoka-san as CEO, a former salesman from a major hosting company, working closely with a major ISP in a JV. SWsoft started pushing harder into the hosting market and with the launch of new Virtuozzo versions and Japanese Windows scheduled for early 2006 release, with focus towards the Enterprise market.

Coming into 2006, it was clear that SWsoft needed to further grow from a JV to a fully-owned and managed corporation, to install new local staff. Year 2006 is the year to set up strong support and sales as 100% SWsoft.

There are a fantastic number of opportunities to work with SWsoft as either Hardware, Software or Service Partners. Working with SWsoft, Software Partners can make Modules, Plugins and ASPs that can be launched to millions of exisiting users.

Now, after establishing SWsoft corporation and bringing on a number of employees, I have decided to move onto my own business, Saloob, Inc. adding value to various businesses in Japan. If you want to reach Japan - then please do contact me.
