Rebecca Lawin McCarley’s Summary

Rebecca Lawin McCarley, an architectural historian, is the principal of SPARK Consulting. This historic preservation firm has served eastern Iowa and northern Illinois as well as the broader Midwest region since 2003. She holds a Master's degree in Historic Preservation from Ball State University and a Bachelor's degree from Washington University in St. Louis in Architecture and History. She meets the federal professional qualifications for an Architectural Historian as well as a Historian, and her qualifications have been certified by the State Historic Preservation Offices in Iowa and Illinois.

Services offered include architectural surveys to meet local preservation planning needs or Section 106 requirements, preservation planning, National Register of Historic Places nominations, historic rehabilitation tax credit applications, grant applications for historic preservation projects, heritage tourism strategies, historic building rehabilitation recommendations, historic research for individual buildings or historic context development, historic documentation of buildings through measured drawings, and public education and interpretative programs about historic buildings, architectural history, and historic preservation.

Rebecca Lawin McCarley’s Specialties:

National Register of Historic Places nominations, Architectural/Historical Surveys, Section 106 surveys, Historic Rehabilitation Tax Credit applications, research on historic buildings and historic contexts
