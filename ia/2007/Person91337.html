William L. Mason’s Summary

William Mason has more than thirteen years experience in financial services including Private Wealth Management, Corporate Consulting, Institutional Bond Trading, and Investment Banking focused on raising debt and equity capital for both publicly and privately held companies.
William is the Founder and Managing Director of Boston First Capital, LLC, a private investment banking company and general business consulting group.
Prior to Boston First Capital, William served as a Vice President, Senior Financial Advisor for Merrill Lynch in their Private Wealth Management Division where he managed more than $600 million dollars for both high net-worth individuals and small to mid-size corporations. A representative list of clients include: Exodus Communications, Read-Rite Corp., Cabot Industrial Trust, Mindography.com, and Global Life-line, Inc.

William’s career includes trading government bonds on Wall Street in the form of overnights or repurchasing agreements “repos”. The volume of bonds he traded were between $25mm-$4bln daily and included industry leading clients, i.e. Goldman Sachs, Merrill Lynch, UBS Warburg, and Wachovia Securities. While Vice President for Dutchess Advisors, William worked closely with CEO’s of publicly traded companies, raising capital in the form of both debt and equity; strategic planning; and aligning/negotiating buyers and sellers of various companies. As a young entrepreneur, William raised the capital and founded North East Brewing Company, an award-winning brewery in downtown Boston.

William served four years in the U.S. Marine Corp, primarily serving as an air traffic controller in El Toro, CA. William was awarded the Unit Citation Award for outstanding performance as well as a Good Conduct medal.

Having attended the University of Southern California, William majored in international relations with a focus on Japan. He studied abroad in Japan at Tokyo International University, and is fluent in the Japanese language.

William L. Mason’s Specialties:

I have actively participated as a “big brother” in the Big Brother program; volunteer at the Veteran's Shelter of Boston; and board member of Antoine Walker’s 8 Foundation.
