Francine McKenna’s Summary

Ms. McKenna has managed business development, client relationships and project delivery in a range of industries, such as financial services, automotive and manufacturing.

She is working on independent projects and a writing project. Her book, tentatively entitled, "Keeping Watch - Can the Big 4 Still Protect Shareholders?", is planned for 2008. Her blog, www.ReTheAuditors.blogspot.com, gives a sample of the book's topics.

She recently spoke at the Illinois Information Technology Association's June 14 Compliance Roundtable on, "The Globalization of Sarbanes-Oxley" and at the IIA's Chicago Chapter Conference in April on, "Stock Options Backdating and Executive Compensation Issues - An Update for Auditors."

Her firm is interested in project opportunities leading a PMO related to finance/accounting transformation, IT governance or SOX remediation on the finance or IT side, especially as they relate to operations in Mexico and South America.

She most recently served PwC clients in Internal Audit Services and was a member of PwC's internal service team, auditing PwC operations globally.

McKenna was Midwest RVP for Jefferson Wells, Inc., a subsidiary of Manpower, responsible for managing sales, operations, client relationships and overall P&L for ten offices, including Toronto.

She was the frst female MD in Latin America for BearingPoint, leading the Industrial, Automotive and Transportation practice in the region and serving clients such as Cemex and the auto makers.

As Vice President at JP Morgan in Latin America, she led their Y2K PMO and is fluent in Spanish.

McKenna has completed the Harvard course, "Leading Professional Services Firms" and "The Women's Leadership Program" at the Center For Creative Leadership.

A CPA, she is a member of the IIA and ISACA (Chicago Chapter Secretary) and speaks frequently for both. A Graduate Student At-Large at the University of Chicago, she earned her B.S. in Accountancy at Purdue University.

Francine McKenna’s Specialties:

Experienced in creating, improving and expanding international service delivery to clients of professional services firms and in leading the Project Management Office for systems upgrade/integration, finance transformation, IT governance and SOX remediation in finance or IT.

She has established new offices and new practices globally, identified and sourced personnel globally, and developed/managed of models for successful international engagements.
