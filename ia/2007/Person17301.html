Chris Webb’s Summary

I am an Executive Editor in the Professional/Trade division of John Wiley & Sons, Inc. where I develop new titles and new series for our technology imprints. Primarily I work on programming titles for the Wrox imprint as well as books in the PC Magazine and ExtremeTech series although I am always keeping an eye out for new and innovative books and other publishing opportunities - so feel free to pitch me new ideas.

Like many, I learned computer programming in the 1980s on TRS-80s, Commodore 64’s and my beloved Atari 400. I began my technology career in 1994 as a programmer and consultant for clients that included Universities, hospitals, banks and other financial institutions, and spent most of my time implementing Microsoft solutions.

In 1998 I entered the world of technology publishing and have worked for Macmillan USA (now Pearson,) and managed to work for IDG Books, Hungry Minds, and John Wiley & Sons without even changing offices.
