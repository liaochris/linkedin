Davion Hill’s Summary

I am an applied physicist with a materials science and electrochemistry background. My most recent work involves research and consulting for materials-related problems and design issues. Related industry areas include oil and gas, automotive, sensor testing and real time monitoring, acoustics, historical preservation and conservation, maritime, aerospace and aviation, utilities, nanotechnology, process qualification and standardization, and energy.

My skillset includes a strong emphasis on electron microscopy, metallurgy, and most recently, electrochemistry. I have strong technical knowledge concerning welding, fabrication, and design. Because of my physics background, I also have experience with electronics and communications design and manufacturing.

Publications:

"Corrosion Condition Assessment, Mitigation, And Preservation of USS Monitor Artifacts" C.S. Brossia, M. Yunovich, D. Hill, et al. NACE Corrosion 2007

"Nanoscale TiB Precipitates in Laser-Deposited Ti-Matrix Composites" Scripta Materialia Vol. 53 Issue 12 2005. Banerjee, R.; Genc, A.; Hill, D.; Collins, P.C.; Fraser, H.L.

"Formation of Equiaxed Alpha in TiB-Reinforced Ti Alloy Composites" Scripta Materialia Vol. 52, Issue 5, 2005. Hill, D.; Banerjee, R.; Huber, D.; Tiley, J.; Fraser, H.L.

"Structure of TiB Precipitates in Laser Deposited In Situ Ti-6Al-4V+TiB Composites". Materials Letters, Vol. 60 Issue 7 April 2006. Genc, A.; Banerjee, R.; Hill, D.; Fraser, H.L.

Davion Hill’s Specialties:
