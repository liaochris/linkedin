Jed Cawthorne’s Summary

ECM specialist. I am responsible as the 'face of ECM' for internal consultancy and providing advice to business unit managers. My current areas of interest include intranets (portal software in particular) and enterprise search, 'enterprise 2.0' technologies, collaboration and social networking.

    As IT & Systems Manager for the Science Faculty, I was the first incumbent of this post and formed a central IT support team from the seperate IT support staff previously managed within each of the departments.   • IT Systems Ground Engineer / Science Data Archiving Manager

    Beagle 2 Mars Lander team, Open University

    (Educational Institution; 51-200 employees; Research industry)

    June 2002 — February 2003 (9 months)

    Initially I acted as the 'Ground Systems IT Engineer' with responsibility for building an IT infrastructure for the Asceptic Assembly Facility (AAF)

    I was then officially seconded to the Beagle 2 project as the 'Science Data Archiving Manager' becoming 50% grant funded by PPARC. I recruited a Systems Administrator and we set about building an IT infrastructure for the Lander Operations Planning Centre (LOPC) at the OU in Milton Keynes.

    Far more interesting was leading the design and build of a science data archiving system, conforming to NASA's Planetary Data System (PDS) standards. The end system was able to take telemetry data packets from NASA or ESA networks, unpack the science data, put the raw data into the system making it available to the science teams, and allow them to put processed data back into the central archive, all in a completely secure way.
