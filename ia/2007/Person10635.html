Bio:
Pablo Bertorello enjoys founding, nurturing, and scaling global businesses, challenging products, and high performance teams—always with a major technology scope. Pablo started Bertorello, Inc. in 2002 and built peer-to-peer software products distributed by Skype to millions of consumers across the globe, ranking as the fourth most popular application out of 70 partners. He bootstrapped this company via an enterprise and telco consulting operation that created call center software for TelphonyAtWork, acquired by Oracle, where Pablo previously acted as director and general manager. Previously at Intel, Pablo co-founded the Early Access Program, which has become a marketing franchise to promote the Pentium, Itanium, and Dialogic product lines.

Pablo’s insight has made him the trusted advisor of social media, business collaboration and viral ecommerce startups. He holds a dozen patents, and is co-inventor of the Itanium microprocessor communication system and Oracle’s Contact Center Anywhere software. Pablo graduated with a degree in Electrical Engineering from the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign. Pablo enjoys sailing competitively, and is a US National Champion in the Olsen class. He speaks Spanish fluently. Some of Pablo’s ramblings can be found at http://bertorello.blogspot.com

Pablo Bertorello’s Specialties:

- Innovation in software, particularly communications (12 patents): consumer internet plays with eBay Skype, enterprise and telco execution via Oracle Telephony@Work
- Social netorking, viral effect, peer-to-peer, IP television, p2p video and photo distribution, jxta, CDN, DHT, grid, VoIP, call center, workspaces, CRM, document management, dekstop sharing, remote access
- Building startup teams of up to 50 people. General management spanning the Americas and Europe
