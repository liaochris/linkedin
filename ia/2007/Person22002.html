David Lang’s Summary

Mr. Lang has over 25 years experience in technical program & risk management, training, counterintelligence, counterespionage, antiterrorism, security, and law enforcement. He has managed major technical programs for the Department of Defense and has experience with several branches of the U.S. Intelligence Community.

Mr. Lang served over 20 years in the United States Air Force retiring in 1998 as a Special Agent in Charge. He is a combat veteran and has served in numerous overseas locations in both military and civilian capacities. Mr. Lang has supported the U.S. Drug Enforcement Administration, the U.S. DoD Computer Forensics Laboratory, the National Reconnaissance Office, and the DoD Counterintelligence Field Activity. He also served as a United Nations WMD inspector in Iraq in 2003 and currently serves as an adjunct professor in the Master of Science in Information Assurance Program at Norwich University.

David Lang’s Specialties:

Risk management, physical & technical security, investigations, and forensics; IT governance & information assurance; cybercrime, and counterintelligence; technical program & project management.

Certifications: CPP, PMP, CISA, CISM, CISSP-ISSMP
Senior Member, IEEE
