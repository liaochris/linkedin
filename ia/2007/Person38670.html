Jason McIntosh’s Summary

I am an independent software consultant, specializing in creating robust and maintainable web-based applications. I am always happy to hear from prospective clients. Visit http://jmac.org/consulting to see my portfolio and résumé.

I am also a technical writer whose major published works include "Mac OS X in a Nutshell" and "Perl & XML", both published by O'Reilly Media, as well as a number of articles, and quite a lot of programmer and user documentation.

The Volity Network (http://volity.net) is my entry into the casual games market. It provides game players with a community based around interesting digital games, and gives thoses games' designers a platform for rapidly developing and launching them.

I'll add that it'd be nice if I could somehow convert my hobby of television and video podcast production into a paying job, but I really have my doubts about that.

    (Partnership; 1-10 employees; Computer Games industry)

    Volity Games manages the Volity Network, an open platform for multiplayer casual games, and my own invention. Through its website and other services, the Volity Network strives to allow independent game developers rapidly author and publish games to our platform, and then profit through a revenue-sharing system of our own design.

    As president, my principal roles involve managing the Volity project's technological development, as well as overseeing the operation of the company and its employees. My technical tasks include maintaining the Perl-based code that runs all of the Volity Network's public services, including all the various applications found on the volity.net website, which variously use AJAX and simple JavaScript. I am also responsible for that site's design, text, and general operation.

  • Software Tools Specialist

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Publishing industry)

    November 2000 — October 2001 (1 year)

    I maintained and created software that helped the company publish its books, both in print and in its more recent strides to publish online, with XML as the vehicle of choice in all cases. My work environment was primarily Perl on Unix.

In fact, just about all my projects involved folding, spindling and mutilating DocBook documents in one way or another - much of my other interesting work involved working on the software that prepares O'Reilly book files for Safari, a service that makes book content available on the Web.
