Robb Montgomery’s Summary

Robb Montgomery is a media consultant with extensive experience developing advanced news products for print, video and interactive online multimedia.

His clients include The Examiner newspapers for the San Francisco, Baltimore and Washington D.C. markets, Chicago Sun-Times News Group, The World Editors Forum, IFRA/Asia, The World Association of Newspapers, the Atlanta Journal-Constitution, Media Giraffe and American Press Institute.

He works with newspaper groups to develop state-of-the-art editorial print and online projects in newsrooms around the world. e.g. Projects like video newspapers, advanced multimedia blogs and interactive community forums.

Robb Montgomery partners with editors and publishers to redesign their newspapers and produce interactive online projects. For SND, IFRA/ASIA API, and WAN he authors and teaches journalism workshops worldwide on tabloid design, video reporting, visual editing, photo editing and interactive media.


A fully-trained journalist, educator and entrepreneur.

As a visual editor and internet media developer he has produced work of distinction with small community weeklies, metropolitan dailies, alternative weeklies and built Visual Editors in 2004 before the terms 'social network,' 'crowdsourcing' and 'user-generated content' were popularized. Since 1999 Robb has been developing and directing multimedia, interactive, social network and online video projects.


------ ] Who is Robb Montgomery?

Robb Montgomery has worked in large and small newsrooms as an editor, designer, and senior manager. He was a principal editor on the Chicago Sun-Times' design upgrade. He also designed the historic Red Streak tabloid paper in Chicago.

In 2004 Montgomery founded Visual Editors.com, an education non-profit that serves a global audience of journalism scholars. Members share interactive strategies, editing tactics, media trends, and design knowledge.

Robb Montgomery’s Specialties:

Advanced news products for print, video and interactive online multimedia.
Redesign and brand strategy, reporter-produced multimedia projects, video podcasts, digital and mobile strategies, advanced multimedia blogs and interactive community forums.

News Design Editor

Chicago Sun-Times

(Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; HLR; Newspapers industry)

September 2001 – November 2005 (4 years 3 months)

As a senior editor in the newsroom Robb was a principal designer in the 2003 redesign of the Chicago Sun-Times and designed many of the paper's award-wining news investigations. Including 'Clout On Wheels' - the Hired Trucks scandal that uncovered vast corruption in Mayor Daley's City Hall.

Robb had daily responsibilties for Page One of the paper and he designed the famous Red Streak - a commuter youth tabloid edition that launched in October 2002.

He won a Peter Lisagor Award for Exemplary Journalism in 2004 and was nominated again in 2005 - both awards were for high-profile series about local people and their issues.

Design Editor

The staff of Sun Publications won numerous awards from the Pictures of the Year competition, the Illinois Press Association and the Society of News Design for outstanding documentary photojournalism and presentation.
