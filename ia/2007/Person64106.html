Norman Kincl’s Summary

ISM for Next-Generation Networks Solution Manager in Hewlett-Packard’s Communications, Media and Entertainment (CME) Solutions organization.
• responsible for providing the technical and business direction for Hewlett-Packard's Integrated Service Management for NGN
• work with customers, product divisions, partners and HP architects to define and bring to market the HP offering for managing service provider networks.

Prior roles at HP:
• Chief ISM Architect, responsible for the overall ISM architecture
• Solution Portfolio Manager responsible for the business and technical leadership necessary to successfully address the HP service assurance consulting business
• Solution architect, focusing on understanding customer problems and applying technology to solve the problems; successfully helped numerous customers improve their business through solving operational support system issues.
• Senior Technical Contributor in a group defining HP distributed system architectures and strategy; initial focus was on understanding how to manage distributed systems and networks. Over time, broadened work to define the architecture framework for HP Open Distributed Computing
• Researcher and project manager at HP Labs focusing on networking and systems management; managed the project that started the HP Internet and oversaw its growth to an international network interconnected with the Internet before transitioning it to the Corporate Networks Department.

Before HP
• Regional Software Specialist for Digital Equipment Corporation supporting Tops-10 and TOPS-20.
• Systems Programmer at Columbia University working on IBM 360 and TOPS-20

Bachelor of Arts and Master of Science in Computer Science from Columbia University.

Presented and published papers in both industry and academic forums; contributed to multiple industry-wide organizations including the IETF, OSF, the TeleManagement Forum, and the International Engineering Consortium.
