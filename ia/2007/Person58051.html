Martin Garthwaite’s Summary

I practice patent law (prosecution) in the areas of software, communications, and mechanical devices.

I have a personal interest in mathematics, information theory, and tinkering.

I have been the general counsel of eNom, a large domain name registrar, and, before that, of Conversational Computing Corporation, a small-footprint speaker independent speech recognition company. Out of law school I acquired considerable experience with wireless while finding and building wireless (cellular and PCS) sites for The Walter Group.

I qualified to take the patent bar by taking and passing the Fundamentals of Engineering Exam (the "FE"), which is an unusual route to satisfying the science requirement. I was able to pass the FE based on my own studies of math and physics.
