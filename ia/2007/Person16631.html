Chris Christian’s Summary

My experience starts w/ apps/web design in early 90. I quickly went into infrastructure & loved working w/ the HW. I was writing custom policy templates for NT 3.51/4.0, trying to secure/lock-down standard corporate desktops in the mid 90s. I also implemented centralized servers to store drivers/SW to ease deployment; scripted & simplified imaging the pc's (common place now, cutting edge then). I spent time w/ Crosscom routers & Cabletron switches until Cisco. I architected/deployed our early Citrix farms (Winframe now Metaframe). Moving to Web Services on Sun Solaris I worked w/ WebLogic Clusters. I managed all our IBM AIX & Sun Solaris HW in our data centers (spec'ing, standards, logistics, racking, inventorying, vendor relations). We basically have every flavor of RS-6000,Regatta,P-Series,SP2, etc.

I became Lead SAN Architect & worked w/ McData, Hitachi, EMC, IBM. Fabric experience is Intrepid 6140,6064,i10k, Sphereon 3232,4700, Eclipse 2640 & mgt on EFCM w/ Perf/Event/SANav. Storage experience is Lightning 9980,9970, TagmaStor NSC-55, old Symmetrix, new DMX-3, Clariion CX-500,CX-700,CX3-80,Centera, Tape Library 3584 w/LTO-3/2 drives. Storage Mgt w/ HiCommand Device/Tuning Manager, HiTrack,Storage Nav, Replication over Ciena DWDM using True Copy,MirrorView & SRDF w/ McData Eclipse & Sphereon. I ensured all our environments replicated w/ scripts (CCI/NaviCLI). I was a key resource in the moving/buildout of our new prod data center, including our new backbone fabric design & state of the art cable mgt/dist. I spoke on a Gartner Group panel called "Building the high performance data center of the future".

My move to Lead Enterprise Architect was my experience coming full circle from Infrastructure back to Applications. Currently I am involved in (lots of learning) Identity Management,Mobility,SOA,ESB & UML training (we built the course & are teaching it in-house to IT). My cross-road now is whether to go down the management path or stay on the technical path.
