Brian Fenn’s Summary

I quickly got my deep in the world of technology joining Onvia (originally Onvia.com) as a software tester working in testing e-commerce systems using Oracle, ColdFusion. Working in such a close-knit environment also gave me the time to reach out and touch many parts of the organisation including liasing with external partners (Visa), setting up releases with Operations and getting a better idea of where I wanted to take my career.

At Microsoft I started as a software tester in the Home Imaging and Publishing Team (HIP) that became the Digital Imaging Group (DIG) shipping photo editing software (Digital Imaging Suite) and is now a fundamental component of Vista's built in photo editing and organisation. Then it was a short jaunt over to the MSN Photos product as a tester and as test release lead for live site. We launched - Photo E-mail for the MSN Client, PhotoSwap for MSN Messenger, purchasing prints online through FujiFilm, photo album management for Windows Live Spaces and of course our online storage solution.

As our team was rolling deeply into helping Vista I took the opportunity to join the MSN Channels team working as the Producer for MSN City Guides. Working closely with CitySearch to power the MSN offering for local information across the United States. As things go in Microsoft and MSN I was then shifted onto MSN Travel helping prepare it for launch in 2006 and then personally working in partnership with the MSN Video and ShermansTravel to augment the editorial offering for MSN Travel.

In the fall of 2006 a fantastic opportunity lay before me to join the MSN Media Network International team in London as a Program Manager. I manage the International Entertainment and Movies products. I work with markets around the world and everything in between. I'm organising monthly development cycles, prioritising and laying out product roadmaps for a fiscal year, innovating in side projects involving vendor resources and so much more.

Brian Fenn’s Specialties:

Organising focus groups, developing product roadmaps, prioritisation across multiple markets, negotiation and making appropriate trade-offs.
