Chuck Eesley’s Summary

Chuck Eesley is a 2nd year Ph.D. student at the MIT Sloan School of Management. He is an aspiring high tech entrepreneur and has co-authored a number of papers and book chapters on strategy and high-tech entrepreneurship.

While a student at MIT, Chuck served as Acting CEO for Learning Friends, a Woodside, CA based software start-up and finalist in the HKUST (Hong Kong University of Science and Technology) International Business Plan Competition. He also served as mentor to materials science startup Hydrophen, an MIT Enterprise Forum Ignite Clean Energy Competition finalist in 2006.

Prior to returning to school, Chuck worked as a research assistant at the Duke University Medical Center, focusing on cognition in schizophrenia and fMRI neuro-imaging studies and at the Fuqua School of Business on research related to business strategy and innovation. He also worked for a start-up, NeuroCog Trials, Inc. consulting with pharmaceutical companies such as Pfizer, Johnson & Johnson, and GlaxoSmithKline on clinical trials for schizophrenia.

Chuck holds a BS degree in Biology with a focus in biological bases of behavior from Duke University. While an undergraduate, in 2002 he led a team to win first prize ($50,000) in the Duke Startup Challenge with his first startup company Sun Dance Genetics, listed as one of Forbes Small Business top 14 startups in 2003.

http://www.mit.edu/~eesley

Passion, curiosity, excellence, honesty, and commitment are the values I live and work by everyday and have been key to my successes. The main research interests I am pursuing at MIT are determinants of entrepreneurial activity and success including university-industry linkages, strategies for the commercialization of scientific research in emerging technologies and new science-based industries.
