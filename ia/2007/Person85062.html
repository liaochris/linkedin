Kathy Cinquemani Shields’s Summary

I am a native Dallasite. I have studied gardening and gardened in the Dallas area for 35 years. Before I had my own piece of property on which to garden, I was a prolific houseplant gardener with grow lights indoors and pot plants on the stoop of my apartments. Upon moving to my own home, I ordered seeds from mail order catalogues trying unique and unusual things. I raised my own seedlings with heating cables and grow lights in my kitchen in the dead of winter. My gardens expanded to include vegetable gardens, growing 100 pound pumpkins 2 years in a row. My one acre yard afforded me shade and sun gardening, water gardens and "cooking" my own compost. I have overhauled and created landscapes for dozens of family and friends. I have hosted formal events in my gardens. In 1995, I was certified in Denton County as a Texas Master Gardener.

Kathy Cinquemani Shields’s Specialties:

My expertise is in north Texas, zone 8. The Master Gardener program teaches all aspects of horticulture: diseases, pests, landscape design, cellular biology of plants, soil structure and ph levels. My own personal studies have been in plant genus' and varieties, learning of their unique growing requirements.
