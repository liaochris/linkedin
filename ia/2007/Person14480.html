Carl Howe’s Summary

I'm a generalist interested in how people think, act, and interact in society and business. I focus my business work on how corporations can communicate more clearly in all ways -- interpersonally, electronically, and in print -- to be effective in today's markets. I also spend much of my time analyzing how technology disrupts or reinforces our ability to communicate with one another.

Prior to founding Blackfriars, I was a principal analyst and research director at Forrester Research. While there, I was named one of the 20 most influential analysts in the U.S. in 2002. I've commented on technology trends in media ranging from CNN and the Today Show to the Times of London. I've also spoken at events throughout the U.S., Europe, and Asia.

Carl Howe’s Specialties:

Analysis of communications clarity and understanding, technology marketing strategist, public speaker on marketing, communications, and technology trends

  • Principal Analyst and Research Director

    Forrester Research, Inc.

    (Public Company; 501-1000 employees; FORR; Market Research industry)

    September 1996 — September 2002 (6 years 1 month)

    While at Forrester, Carl directed Forrester's research in networking, computing, and infrastructure and was also a principal analyst. Carl's research predicted the next wave of Internet innovation in his 2001 report "The X Internet," forecast the impact of open source software in his 2000 report, "Open Source Cracks The Code", and predicted the decline of the PC industry in 2000 in his 1998 report "PC Industry Roller Coaster." While in this position, Carl commented on technology for TV, radio, and print media worldwide.

In addition, Carl helped firms like Mastercard, Wang, and National Westminster Bank deploy early Internets.
