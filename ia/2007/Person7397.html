Henry N. Huta’s Summary

Exceptional contributions have been achieved for Fortune 500 and mid size clients through effective and timely execution in highly challenging environments.

In each case, focus was on team building, change management, cost reductions, introduction of new products and the expansion of markets and market share. Extensive training and dedication to “Lean Manufacturing and Lean Enterprise” provided these companies with operational excellence and continuous improvements that met and exceeded customer expectations.

The high level of success is primarily due to a keen understanding that no matter what technology, product or markets a company is in, “it’s all about people.”

Henry N. Huta’s Specialties:

Lean Manufacturing, Six Sigma, Turnaround Management, Financial Restructuring, Mergers & Aquisitions, Certified Public Accountant, Peter F. Drucker Graduate School of Leadership.

Henry N. Huta’s Experience

  • CEO

    Archfield Consulting Group, Inc.

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Management Consulting industry)

    September 1989 — Present (18 years 4 months)

    The company provides business owners, private equity firms and corporate clients with an alternative leadership platform through the engagement of an interim CEO.
    Many companies have an immediate need to improve their market and financial positions. This is the ideal time to acquire the resources of an experienced leader who can parachute into a company and hit the ground running; a leader who has a past record of delivering substantial results in a quantified period of time. Such a leader is typically needed on an interim basis until a new strategy is implemented and a permanent leader can be recruited to maintain the new momentum and growth on a long term basis.
    Clients are from diverse manufacturing and service industries and have included start ups and major organizations with over $300 million in annual sales. Clients were rewarded with substantial increases in shareholder wealth ranging from $5 million to $180 million during engagements which lasted an average of 18 months.

  • Interim CEO

    The Fitzpatrick Company

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Machinery industry)

    February 2005 — September 2006 (1 year 8 months)

    Current assignment for Archfield Consulting Group, Inc. and Hamilton Robinson LLC, a private equity group (www.hrco.com)

  • Interim CEO

    TradeSource, Inc.

    (Privately Held; 1001-5000 employees; Staffing and Recruiting industry)

    October 2001 — August 2003 (1 year 11 months)

    Assignment for Archfield Consulting Group, Inc. and the private equity firm of Saugatuck Capital LLC.
    A temporary staffing company providing skilled labor to the construction industry. The company’s sales were $82 million with a $13 million loss in 2001 and a $13 million loss in 2000.
    Closed non profitable offices and reduced annual operating costs by over $10 million without jeopardizing the core business.
    Transformed organization from a bureaucracy to a lean enterprise driven by entrepreneurial branch managers.
    Implemented a pay-for-performance incentive system that improved morale, employee retention and profitability.
    After six months the company achieved profitability.
    Reduced annual workers’ comp premiums by over $3 million and reduced injury claims by over 50%.
    Employee sales productivity increased from $350,000 to over $1 million.

  • Interim CEO

    BIW (Draka Holding) Company

    (Public Company; 5001-10,000 employees; DRAK; Electrical/Electronic Manufacturing industry)

    January 1999 — June 2001 (2 years 6 months)

    A wire and cable manufacturer and a subsidiary of the $2 billion Draka Holding Company headquartered in Amsterdam, Netherlands. BIW consisted of three operating companies with annual sales of $108 million with over 300 employees.
    Reduced annual operating expenses by $2.5 million.
    Implemented “lean manufacturing” using Toyota Production Method tools: 5S, visual management, pull systems, standard work, mistake proofing and other continuous improvement techniques.
    New field sales commission program was implemented
    Over 50 distributors and worldwide sales representative organizations were rationalized.
    Within the first 12 months expanded into two new key markets with product modifications: Power Generation Gas Turbines and Telecommunications.
    Sales volumes increased 42%,
    Productivity increased by 38%,
    Quality improved 86%
    On time delivery improved 53% reaching a company record of 98.6% on time.

  • Interim CEO

    Thermal Group (Invensys plc)

    (Public Company; 5001-10,000 employees; Electrical/Electronic Manufacturing industry)

    October 1997 — January 1999 (1 year 4 months)

    BTR plc’s sales were $10 billion in 1998. BTR was merged with Siebe plc in 1999. With combined annual sales of $16 billion the name Invensys plc was adopted in 2000. As a result of a two year restructuring and improvement of EBITDA of the Thermal Sensors Group by 74%, Invensys was able to significantly improve shareholder value by selling the Controls Group to Honeywell for approximately $400 million. Thermal Sensors’ sales were $155 million with over 1,500 employees located in the U.S., Europe, Caribbean and Southeast Asia. Companies in the Thermal Sensors Group were: Elmwood Sensors, Inc., Pawtucket, RI; Elmwood UK plc, New Castle, England; Elmwood China, Shenzhen, China;Fenwal Electronics, Milford, MA: Fenwal Caribbean, St. Lucia.
