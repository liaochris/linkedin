Ash Huzenlaub’s Experience

  • Managing Director

    Ashco Group, LLC

    (Privately Held; Management Consulting industry)

    January 1999 — Present (8 years 11 months)

    Ashco Group, LLC is the business vehicle of Ash R. Huzenlaub. Ashco Group teams with companies, investment partnerships, BOD’s, and/or restructuring groups that seek to add additional experience in operating private, or public-to-private companies (as well as turnaround initiatives); B.O.D. representation; interim management; diligence; business plan and market analysis; business development / sales leadership; and extensive marketing / branding experience that require a "roll up your sleeves" work ethic.

    Ashco Group has been a shareholder of emergency department information systems company, Emergisoft, where Huzenlaub served as turnaround management (2002 - 2006); and a startup airline initiative (Mexus). Since Q1 of 2006, Ashco has been contracted to assist industry leaders in Service Awards (marketing and promotion); University Entrepreneurship Programs (extracurricular development); and currently, food and beverage (growth & expansion at Sweet Leaf Tea Co.).

  • Chairman, President & CEO

    Emergisoft Corporation

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Hospital & Health Care industry)

    May 2002 — November 2005 (3 years 7 months)

    Implemented as turnaround CEO of this public company by primary shareholder, Berlwood V, Ltd in May 2002. We took the company private, rebuilt the company, and successfully launched two new Emergency Department Information System platforms in hospitals across the nation.

    Turnaround Challenges Achieved:
    • Public to Private
    • Assessed financial structure and eliminated more than $3mm in annual expenses
    • Worked with investors to completely eliminate debt
    • Rebuilt company's talent pool of employees to pursue the turnaround
    • Built a mgt. team capable of recruiting key implementation, clinical, sales, and technical talent from the healthcare IT industry
    • Assessed strategy / eliminated acquisition efforts outside of company's core competencies
    • Negotiated relationships to eliminate litigation
    • Developed brand credibility for which the industry could trust
    • Developed / negotiated service contracts with multiple IDN's respective of state and federal regulations

  • President

    Texas Run To The Cross, Inc.

    (Non-Profit; 1-10 employees; Events Services industry)

    January 2000 — April 2003 (3 years 4 months)

    As volunteer President, managed this competitive annual running event and charitable concert in downtown Fort Worth for three years. Between 2000 and 2003, grew event to 5,000 runners and gained national sponsors for product promotion such as Propel Fitness Water, Dallas Mavericks, Mizuno, as well as regional Texas business and media sponsors. This was a non-profit 501 C3 company benefiting youth church organizations that send kids to summer camps. Responsibilities included growing the event year over year, working with phenomenal volunteers, working with city departments, insurance, liability, P&L, and a great deal of "rolling up sleeves." Additionally, the event doubled as the Southwest USATF 5,000 meter road race Championships.

  • Analyst

    Berlwood V, Ltd PE Investment Partnership

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Investment Management industry)

    February 2002 — June 2002 (5 months)

    Worked under investment partnership's principals to provide due diligence on their existing three year investment in a emergency department information systems company. Within five months, I was transitioned by the partnership to become the turnaround CEO for this company which was re-branded Emergisoft.

  • Analyst

    Woodcrest Capital

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Investment Management industry)
