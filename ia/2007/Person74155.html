Rommil Santiago’s Summary

It took a few years for me to find what I was looking for. I started out as a mechanical engineer, graduating from McGill, working at a leading flight simulation company, CAE. But something didn't feel right. I started getting into photography, which led me to graphic design, which led to desktop publishing, which finally ended up in web design.

Over the following years, I've honed my web design skills though freelancing, my own media company - Tokoro Media, Steam, and finally with Concordia University. I've learned there is more to web design than bells and whistles, and layout. The most important factors missed are usability and accessibility. Most think that these things only apply to disabled people, but with more and more devices being web-ready, like cell phones, etc - sites have to be more flexible to service a wider audience. I strive to keep up to date with the latest best practices, browser updates, and what the industry leaders are cranking out.

Over the next few years, I, along with the newly formed web team at Concordia, aim to lead higher education institutions by showing them how to effectively merge, information architecture, layout, usability and accessibility on large websites. I welcome every opportunity to educate people about what can be done to improve sites - and as Concordia is a large institution, we have a lot of work ahead of us in order to reach our goals - but it will be worth it. With over 1000 sites at the university, we're going to revamp a great majority of them - starting with the home page.

Career-wise I feel that I am ready for and will soon seek employment as a Senior Web Designer. With my almost a decade in the web and multimedia as a whole, along with my previous project management experience - the time will come to make a move onto something bigger and better - and I'm itching for a new challenge. But first thing's first...

Keep an eye out on http://www.concordia.ca over the next year. It's going to get overhauled.
