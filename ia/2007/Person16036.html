Cheryl Antier’s Summary

Ghostwriter: Can't write? Got these symptoms? Dry mouth, sweaty palms, upset stomach, uncontrollable urge to clean out your junk drawer? You've got writer's block! Instant relief in just 30 seconds - email me now - no obligation.

Specialties: E-books/books, special reports, white papers, case studies, articles, interviews, scripts, press releases, proposals.

Copywriter: Need clear, concise copy to outsell your competition - even if they're bigger than you are, have more money than you do, and are more experienced with a bigger share of the market?

Specialties:
Taking a complex idea and boiling it down to a simple statement, vision or idea. Creating a specific emotion, spotlighting a condition or problem, and painting a picture in the mind of a reader.

Reaching the reader on their level, using words and phrases they identify with. Leading reader through the minefield of objections, skepticism and concerns until he decides the only "logical" action to take is your solution.

Journalist and Travel Writer covers France and Europe: Will consider contract assignments for events, business, boating, travel features and tourism.

Specialties:
Travel Agents, Rental companies, Travel and Tourism promotors: Hire a local writer - cut down on costs. Up-to-date intel on concerts, special events, attractions. Will review hotels, restaurants, spas, etc. Reasonable rates, fast turn-around time. Can include photos.

Public Relations/Media Consultant:
If the circus is coming to town and you paint a sign saying
'Circus Coming to the Fairground Saturday' -- that's advertising.

If you put the sign on the back of an elephant and walk him into
town -- that's promotion.

If the elephant walks through the mayor's flowerbed -- that's publicity.

If you can get the mayor to laugh about it -- that's public relations.

And if you planned the elephant's walk -- that's marketing."

Specialties: Let me help you "plan the elephant's walk" for your business.

  • Owner

    Automatic Writings - Ghostwriting, Copywriting, Marketing/Publicity Consultants (Sole Proprietorship)

    (Sole Proprietorship; 1-10 employees; Writing and Editing industry)

    July 1996 — Present (11 years 5 months)

    I work with small business owners, consultants and coaches who are uncomfortable with selling because they think that means that they have to turn into a high-pressure, pushy phony and become someone they're not. They often equate the word selling with lying or coercion or manipulation.

    So first of all, let's get this straight - effective sales copy is honest and compelling and comes from the gut. It's a reflection of who you really are and what you have to share with the world.

    It uses the power of persuasion - not lies, not hype, not high pressure. It's about your honesty, integrity and expertise, and the reason that you're in business doing what you do.

    Effective sales copy informs and educates the reader. It stimulates their interest and makes them want to know more. And it explains "why you" and not someone else.

  • Development Director

    J.E.D.I. Women

    (Non-Profit; 1-10 employees; Fund-Raising industry)

    November 1995 — August 1996 (10 months)

    How do you describe being a part of Camelot? Of watching low-income women step outside their comfort zones and find and wield their true power? Fight for what mattered, what was right and what was seldom easy? Like the street law project, so battered and abused women could find justice and protection under the law? The employment resource center, so low-income parents could take classes, look for work and improve their skills? The free onsite child care space, so those same parents knew their children were safe and secure while they made a better life for their kids? Or "My Sister's Closet" so that with the support of professional businesswomen, women had business clothing for interviews and new jobs? We challenged unfair legislation, worked for new laws, and helped to educate, inform and engage a community. "Don't let it be forgot...that once there was a spot...For one brief, shining moment that was known as Camelot."
