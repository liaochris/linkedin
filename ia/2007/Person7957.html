Aruni Gunasegaram’s Summary

After undergrad I worked as an outside auditor at KPMG and at Dresser Industries (now Halliburton) as an internal auditor which gave me the inside and outside financial picture of several great companies. I left Dresser to get my MBA in 1996.

In 1997, I co-founded Isochron (www.isochron.com). As the founding CEO, I coordinated the raising of $15 million in equity financing, hiring of 30+ people, closing the company's first $1 million in revenue, hiring my replacement, as well as managing Investors and attempting to manage our Board of Directors. :-) What a great education!

After leaving Isochron in 2001, I consulted, taught entrepreneurship at The University of Texas at Austin Red McCombs School of Business, had two kids, co-wrote articles on the topic of success (www.babblesoft.com/articles.php) and launched a new company called Babble Soft, LLC (www.babblesoft.com).

We are currently bootstrapping Babble Soft, and I'm in the process of building strategic partnerships with key players in the baby and parenting industry.
