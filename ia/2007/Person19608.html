Dale Beyer, MBA, PMP’s Summary

With over 20 years of experience, Dale possesses a unique blend of coaching, organizational and real-world experience in project management. From project team members to executive staff, her attentive approach to learning and development prepares project managers to learn, execute, build teams, lead, and ultimately facilitate change within their careers and their organizations. With expertise in the areas of program/project management, workplace learning and performance, organizational behavior and leadership development, Dale is committed to designing and facilitating evolutionary development to create and maintain “Healthy Organizations.”

Dale is a certified Project Management Professional (PMP) and a trained Co-Active Coach. She has also served as adjunct faculty at Roosevelt University's Walter E. Heller College of Business Administration, teaching in the area of Managerial Project Management and Behavioral Science, as well as Organizational Theory. In addition, she is a contributing member of both the Project Management Institute (PMI) and the Association for Training and Development (ASTD).
