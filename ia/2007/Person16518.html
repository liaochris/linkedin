Chris Heuer’s Summary

I have often been ahead of the curve, developing memes and business models for soon to be explosive emerging industries - but without proper financial connections and the talented teams required to execute. Earned a BA in Communications, Legal Institutions, Economics and Government in 1991 then worked as a cabana boy, waiter, warehouse manager, ad sales person for New Times and then helped to run all aspects of a business opportunity company where at 23 I was teaching 40-50 year old people how to run their own business. I was marketing director for a very small software company for a couple of months before starting my first Internet companies in 1994 - Guru Communications and Virtual Community Network. I resigned in 1997, started teaching and doing independent consulting. In 1998 I took a position with the United States Mint to head up their ecommerce efforts, launching their online catalog and taking them from zero to $1.8 million per week in online sales. I left the Mint to pursue the development of InfoApps, a destination site for Information Appliances (ie pervasive computing) only to hear the sound of the bubble pop in April 2000, just a month after launching. I turned the platform ideas for how I was going to differentiate the service into an idea for conversational intelligence software. Luckily for me, some people I considered friends and a local VC firm took those ideas and changed their business model to mine. In trying to sell a more specific software solution to Palm as Conversal, I built a relationship that has lasted over 4 years where we provided web development, software and consulting services to many departments throughout their organization. Today, what keeps me up at night is my pursuit of the holistic solution for the knowledge economy that amalgamates the best emerging ideas for the Internet 2.0 – I am sure you will be reading more on this soon…
