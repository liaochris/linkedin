Hari Floura’s Summary

Hari Floura is the President of Floura LLC, established in 2005. Hari has worked exclusively in the field of Containment Technology within the pharmaceutical industry. During this time Mr. Floura has coupled his formal training in industrial design, the science of ergonomics and materials handling with his practical knowledge of the requirements of pharmaceutical process to deliver real world, workable solutions to his clients.

Prior to launching Floura LLC, Mr. Floura was an executive/shareholder and was responsible for establishing ACE, a UK based company in the USA market. Mr. Floura over a period of 5 years secured a number of key clients for ACE and achieved a gross turnover in excess of $2M. In addition Hari also helped set into place a number of strategic alliances with vendor companies both in the USA and India by licensing technologies or supplying OEM equipment to them.

Mr. Floura has also held positions with Parsons Inc, Kvaerner Process, Total Process Containment Ltd, Process Containment Technology Ltd and Extract Technology Ltd. He has been responsible for consultancy, business development, R&D, conceptual/detail design, project management, assembly, installation and validation on a number of successful containment projects.

In addition to these activities Hari has provided consultancy to many major Pharmaceutical Companies with respect to their containment guidelines, facility and containment system designs. He is also a member and regular presenter for International Society of Pharmaceutical Engineers and sat on the SMEPAC (Standardized Measurement of Equipment Particulate Airborne Concentration) Committee. The SMEPAC committee contributed to the ISPE Good Practice Guide – Assessing the Particulate Containment Performance of Pharmaceutical Equipment.

Hari Floura’s Specialties:

Design & specification of containment equipment to handle potent products, development of potent material handling philosophies, Design & specification of potent material process equipment, material handling, front end studies.
