Ian Hunter’s Experience

  • Director of Software Development

    ePublishing.com

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Computer Software industry)

    June 2004 — May 2007 (3 years)

    As Director of Software Development I was responsible for managing a number of developers, consultants and designers through the continuous development of the company's flagship product: the ePublishing architecture (ePub). ePub is a custom built MVC framework designed specifically for CMS clients with an 80/20 rule in mind; to service 80% of clients out of the box and implement the remaining 20% with custom development. During my tenure I directed the release of three versions of ePub and numerous client releases on the technology and administered 10+ machines to which they were deployed.

  • CTO / Director of Technology

    Transasctis Inc.

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Computer Software industry)

    September 2001 — March 2003 (1 year 7 months)

    Working with Jeff Greenberg, Co-Founder of eStara, we raised funding via angel investors in the range of $200,000 and built the TransactMail API and client extranet. Clients can build the pieces needed for dynamic forms and get the resulting creative wire frame to be used for data mining and form processing. Transactis has increased revenue for clients like USA Today (by 50%), FTD.com (100%), John Kerry For President (255%), the National Republican Senatorial Committee (75%), Cox Communications (200%), John McCain (27%) and many others using the TransactMail and BillMail products.

  • Lead Developer

    eStara Inc.

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Telecommunications industry)

    September 2000 — July 2001 (11 months)

    As one of the original employees of eStara, I saw the company go from a startup to the VoIP powerhouse that it is today. I was in charge of the web delivery technology for the eStara phone application (Trojan horse design) as well as lead on a number of emerging technology projects. Some of the emerging technologies include call center to user key based push system, JavaScript/web based text chat system, ActiveX collaborative browsing system and others. In September 2006 eStara sold to ATG Dynamo for $48.3 million.

  • Lead Developer

    SingleShop Inc.

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Internet industry)

    January 2000 — September 2000 (9 months)

    SingleShop was one of the original leaders into the online comparative shopping consumer market. Bringing many unique ideas to the net such as a cross-website shared shopping cart, SingleShop.com was a leader for some time before being overcome by competitors' JavaScript drop-in API's as opposed to SingleShop.com's ASP based service model, to which I fought hard against. I was the lead on a number of R&D projects such as a spidering capability for search results (as it was all results were pinged in real-time) and a WML based delivery system for searching and adding results to a users' cart.
