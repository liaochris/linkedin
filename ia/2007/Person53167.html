John Mennell’s Summary

I founded MagazineLiteracy.org, a 501(c)(3) charity, as the first-ever, only national, magazine industry-wide family literacy program.

My professional career spans Capitol Hill, Wall Street, Main Street, and the Information Superhighway. I am a social entrepreneur recognized for innovation, leadership, and public service, and have extensive experience as an information technology consultant in the financial services, telecommunications, and human service industries.

During a summer between semesters at Cornell University, I walked/hitched 20 miles each week to help teens start their own community newspaper. After graduation and working as a congressional campaigner, and a press secretary and legislative aide on Capitol Hill, I took a spirited run for the Maryland legislature. During the campaign I discovered empty food pantry shelves that led to organizing community and national hungery relief efforts.

A few years ago, to raise awareness about hunger in America, I walked from Washington, D.C. to Boston - over 1 million steps - twenty miles a day, for 30 days. I left with 63 cents in my pocket and the clothes on my back. There were many memorable moments along the way. Somewhere in New England, I left a 63 cent tip on a free cup of coffee.

I've spent many hours contemplating the road ahead. In my life, I have encountered many forks in the road. Regardless of what path I choose and what attractions I encounter, my destination remains the same. My travels in this life are about joining with others to share and leverage our talents and resources, to improve the opportunities for children and families, and to create social and community wealth.

Some say, "when you give a person a fish, you feed them for a day." The essence of my journey is to "give a person a fish so they can have the strength to learn to fish...to teach a person hungry to read so they may learn all about the fish, and the ocean below, the world around, and the limitless sky above."
