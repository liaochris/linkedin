Ronald Damhof’s Summary

Senior Architect in information management, focus on (Enterprise) Data Warehousing and it's place in an Enterprise Architecture. To be honest-I am a bit of a freak when it comes to this field of expertise. I love the dynamic thats going on at the moment, many interesting developments, discussions, trends. It just proves that there is a huge need for an Intelligent Enterprise that is capable of (re)-using its greatest treasure in an efficient and effective manner..information.

I am not a 'white paper' architect..put your money where your mouth is, is my motto. I like to see architectures 'live' in enterprises, not just write about it (powerpoint; the only platform everything works good on..). In most organisations my role extends architecture often. I need to be a missionary (selling the value of an information architecture), a project manager (getting it done) and a specialist (educating hardware peeps, data architects, data logistics peeps etc.). But thats the beauty of the trade...

A Data Warehouse can not be implemented without a clear vision and view on the Enterprise architecture:
- What's the vision regarding Service Oriented Architectures
- Application integration, data integration or both?
- Propagation or federation, EII realistic?, now or in a decade?
- EAI, ESB - and it's incredible value towards an information architecture
- Realtime information...farce, foe or friend?
- Master Data Management - is it that new?
- Hybrid modelling standards, dimensional modelling, Data Vault, anything exciting? YES!
- Dynamic data warehousing, interesting...
- Data Warehouse appliances..worth it?
- platforms EDW: Mainframe, Midrange (Unix, Linux), Windows?
- etc..

oh...yeah...almost forgot..duh...Dont forget the business - they face the challenges, get connected to those problems and create value!

Over 10 years of experience in the field. At the moment lead architect of one of the largest Enterprise Data Warehouse projects in Europe (Government).
