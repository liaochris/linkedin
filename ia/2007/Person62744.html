Nazy Massoud’s Summary

Nazy Massoud is a corporate consultant, trainer and executive coach, specializing in the financial arena. Like the immigrant that she is, she bridges radically different worlds, translating one lingo to another, and teaching others the powerful communication and success tools she has developed over the course of 20 years.

Nazy burst through not one, but two glass ceilings on her way to the top – the patriarchal society of her homeland in which few women become engineers, and the male-dominated, ultra-competitive world of Wall Street. She went on to work for more than 20 years in corporate America, including 15 years on Wall Street with such financial giants as Morgan Stanley, Bankers Trust, UBS, Chase and JP Morgan. She rapidly advanced through the financial landscape -- becoming a Business Analyst, Strategist, Risk Manager and Project Manager for many of the biggest clients and portfolios in the world.

While on Wall Street she was in the heart of the action on the trading floor, fielding a daily barrage of crises and conflicts. Nazy has a unique gift for making the complicated simple. Her analytical background trained her to survey the issues, detect the pattern, define the simple blocks and come up with solutions that pave the way to success.

Having been in the trenches first hand, Nazy knows what it’s like to be so stressed out that you can no longer function. She knows first hand the challenges IT people have in translating the fuzzy demands of traders into the systems talk they understand. She understands the frustration of managers who see their employees jump ship after they have been trained. She knows that communication among traders, legal, IT and vendors is no less difficult than that between Russian and Chinese. She knows the cost of red tape, the difficulties in asking for help, the burnout from taking on too much, and the paralyzing, costly fear of making mistakes.

And she knows what to do about it.
