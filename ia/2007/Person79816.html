Simon Deane-Johns’s Summary

A co-founder and co-inventor of Zopa, the world's first online marketplace where people meet to lend and borrow money, launched March 2005. Advising on UK and US product design, web site and systems; regulatory compliance; public affairs; and contracts with franchisees, suppliers and distributors. Leading the project to enable UK lenders to lend tax free at Zopa through self-invested personal pensions.

Also a consultant lawyer with Lawyers Direct, advising start-ups and innovative businesses on e-commerce, IT and financial services, including M:Metrics, the mobile market measurement provider; Everybodybetter.com, the complementary healthcare community; The School of Everything, the new bottom-up education system; Mondo Visione, publisher of the Exchanges Handbook; ProCheckUp, the IT security services firm; an online retail financial service provider; a leisure industry service provider; the creator of a new mobile telephony feature; a commercial leasing company; and others.

Speaks regularly on Internet developments and regulation, including "We, the Lunatics, Control the Asylum" at GikII, London, Sept 07; "What is Web 2.0?" at the SCL's "Law 2.0" conference, London, Sept '07; at the SCL's Annual Conference – "Web 2.0 – Internet Law in the 21st Century", Oxford, June 07; "Facilitation, not Regulation" at the SCL Policy Forum on "Decentring Internet Regulation", London, Oct. '06. Articles: "Signing Hundreds of Contracts Online Made Easy" (July '06); "Counter-regulation" (Mar '06); "Why Can't I... is the New Can Do" (Nov '04) - www.scl.org.

Previous board experience with responsibility for legal, compliance and company secretarial at GE Consumer Finance; and Earthport plc, the listed P2P e-payments provider. Initiated and co-ordinated e-commerce strategy for DLA (now DLA Piper), with early commercial exposure as a lawyer with Reuters in London and New York.

Simon Deane-Johns’s Specialties:

Managing legal, compliance and company secretarial functions; giving strategic legal input to board level decisions; advice on innovative online product launches; obtaining regulatory approvals; managing public affairs programmes; evaluating legal risk; drafting and negotiating major distribution, franchise and supplier agreements; business planning; legal advice on e-commerce, IT and consumer financial services; legal advice to start-ups
