Robert Niles’s Summary

Robert Niles got sucked into Internet when computers and journalism collided in the early 1990's. A long-time math and computer geek, Robert turned to journalism after graduating from Northwestern University and deciding he didn't want to spend the rest of his life writing reports as a management consultant. But the lure of marathon coding sessions proved too strong. Robert soon quit his job writing editorials for a red-state newspaper, and he began making websites instead.

Robert started with an online tutorial showing other journalists how to use math and data, then branched out to niche sites on theme parks and classical music. He's also edited major newspaper websites and taught online journalism skills and techniques to both college students and mid-career professionals.

Robert Niles’s Specialties:

News reporting and editing, website development, online community creation and management.
