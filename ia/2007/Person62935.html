Neil Hinrichsen’s Summary

I spent eighteen years in the computer industry, as a director at Realtime, and subsequently as managing director for Netscape's operations in Southern Africa. I hold a Bcom Honours degree in Information Systems from UCT, and I am a member of the Computer Society of South Africa.

I have extensive experience in working with rapid growth companies in the high technology industry. My first company was acquired by Datatec in 1997.

I have spoken at many conferences across Southern Africa, including the ICL User Conference, the CTOS User Conference (keynote speaker two years running), South African Direct Marketing Association, Hewlett-Packard User Conference, Internet 95 and 96, and Didata's "Networkers" conference for their top 100 clients (concluding speaker).

I am a member of Mensa, and appear in "Who's Who in the World". My pastimes include water sports, reading and youth ministry. I live in a scenic little coastal town called Knysna some five hours drive from Cape Town.

I founded Worldshift in 1997 to teach people how to change their worlds. See www.worldshift.com for more information.

I am also a cofounder of Fundamo, a leading international mobile payments company, where I served as their head of strategy.

  • Director

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Information Technology and Services industry)

    April 1979 — February 1997 (17 years 11 months)

    Realtime started as one of the first online bureaus in South Africa. I worked for the company part-time from its inception till December 1980, when I took over the technical management, which included development of all the applications software for our clients, and managing our network. In the 1980's we also developed application software for large corporates, did facilities management for large sites, and marketed the first portable microcomputer system in South Africa.

    In the late 1980's we shifted our emphasis to representing leading software companies in SA. I was responsible for securing the rights for Southern Africa for Progress Software, Object Design, and Netscape. We also represented Patricia Seybold in SA. I developed the marketing strategy for these products, and was also intimately involved with sales.

    In 1997 my partners and I split the company between us, and I sold my division to the Datatec group.
