Randy Abramson’s Summary

My experience in interactive media dates back to 1994 where I began my career at a bulletin board/CD ROM hybrid company called Visual Radio. I then became the Interactive and Entertainment Producer at New Jersey Online where I oversaw innovative web cam projects, concert play-by-play simulcasts, the production of the daily Entertainment section and the creation of full-featured fan sites for Frank Sinatra and Bruce Springsteen. The latter was voted one of the ten best music sites on the Web by Rolling Stone magazine.

Later I joined the Newsweek.MSNBC.com team as their Interactive and Multimedia Producer where I produced and oversaw the weekly online version of Newsweek On Air, interactive projects original video shoots.

My next position was Executive Producer for TLC.com at Discovery. I now hold the title of Executive Producer at Discovery Interactive. I specialize in finding new technologies and platforms for our network producers to use on their sites.

Randy Abramson’s Specialties:

Web 2.0, social media tools and video streaming platforms. I also like to think that I can weed out lasting technologies from the flavor of the week. Also, once upon a time, I was a music critic for MSNBC.com's Independent Study column.
