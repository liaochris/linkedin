Laurence Loumes’s Summary

Laurence Loumes is a young PhD from the California Institute of Technology, currently on a merit fellowship of Quebec to conduct research in the area of micro-fluidic mixing.

At Caltech under a Rotary fellowship, Laurence developed and patented an innovative way of pumping for medical applications, that is based on elastic wave interactions.

In addition to a MS and PhD in Aeronautics at Caltech, she holds a degree in multidiciplinary engineering from the highly selective Grande Ecole Centrale Lyon, France, and a 3-years degree in Mechanics from Universite Aix-Marseille I, France, for which she received the highest honors.

Laurence posses an expertise in computational fluid and solid mechanics with an emphasis on bio-medical applications.

She has an extensive writing experience including scientific articles and reports, fellowship winning applications and grant writing to the national institutions. In addition, she was invited to speak about her work several times in Europe and in the US. She initiated collaborations with diverse labs that brought her to work twice in Germany at the TU Berlin and Humboldt Universitaet Berlin.

Laurence received the Charles Babcock award in 2003 for her exceptional contribution as a Teaching Assistant at Caltech. She speaks fluently english, french and german, and has basic knowledge of spanish and italian.

In her spare time, Laurence is a devoted ballet dancer. She likes to read, travel, paint, photograph and spend time with friends and family.
