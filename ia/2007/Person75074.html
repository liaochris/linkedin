Russell Fincher’s Summary

Primarily focused on managing art development at Sickhead Games, a Dallas-based independent game development studio; running a solid, full-time, purely self-funded indie startup for over 4 years. I formed the company with programmer Tom Spilman after leaving a larger game studio in late 2002. We are currently concentrated on development tools, simulations, various contract work, and prototyping games for the Torque game engines.

In 2006 we released the Adventure Kit for Torque Game Builder, a 2D RPG creation tool. In 2007 we released Torsion, the premier Torquescript development environment, and by summer 2007 will complete and release the Torque Forest Kit, which offers advanced foliage replication and SpeedTree implementation for Torque Game Engine Advance.

In 2004 and 2005 I temporarily moved over to Klear Games, subcontracting as lead artist and lead game designer on a number of high-profile mobile titles, including Batman Begins, Harry Potter: Wizard Duel, and several Looney Tunes casual titles.

I'm an Associate Developer with Garage Games, a community-oriented game developer/publisher, and in my spare time teach digital arts at Dallas-area colleges and universities. Prior to game development I spent 7 years as a professional freelance digital imaging specialist, serving major corporate clients such as Dell Computer, Blockbuster Video, and American Airlines.

I'm a creator. If stranded on a deserted island I would first negotiate shelter, and second devise a method of feeding myself. Third I would probably construct something completely unecessary. I build because I'm constantly compelled to, sometimes against my better judgement. I draw, hammer, weld, digitize, vectorize, rasterize, drill, manipulate, write, compose, construct, wire, install, fold, align, configure, perfect, invent, tinker, and otherwise experiment.

Russell Fincher’s Specialties:

Multi-disciplinary lead artist for an indie videogame development studio. I do it all. Concepting, modeling, rigging, skinning, texturing, animating, terrain building, user interface. Not to mention managing artists, working with clients, scheduling. etc. Indies wear a lot of hats.
