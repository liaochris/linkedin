Melissa Shields’s Summary

I have four years of experience teaching high school English at a fairly large suburban high school (2600 students) in South Carolina. I have taught both honors and CP level English, and I have taught students in all grades from Freshmen to Seniors. I am currently teaching Film Criticism and Broadcast Journalism. I have also taught Drama and helped to organize the school's International Festival. I have a Master's Degree, and I am Highly Qualified.

Before I became a teacher, I worked for a finance company as a collector and wrote for a small weekly newspaper. I also wrote for several churches and my college paper, The Daily Reveille. I am still interested in writing as a free lancer, and I am very open to opportunities in curriculum development or publishing.

I have very definite ideas about the nature and purpose of American education today, and I am very interested in learning more and working in any way I can to improve our faulty system.

For the last few years I have focused on improving reading skills for struggling readers. Students do not read the way we did in school. Sometimes we focus on writing instruction to the exclusion of explicit instruction in reading because writing is external and therefore easier for the teacher to teach. Reading is internal and requires the teacher to think deeply about what good readers do, but we must do this thinking if reading skills are to improve.

My master's thesis was on media literacy, which I believe to be a critically under-addressed area of education in America today. Reading isn't just for printed text. Reading media texts is part of everyday life. So much so that many teachers, parents, and students take it for granted that students are critical and able readers of these texts. Meanwhile advertisers take it for granted that they're not.
