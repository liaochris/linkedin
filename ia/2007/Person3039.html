Alex Bernstein’s Summary

Since I stopped living the life of an NFL superstar *, and started to use my head for more than a battering ram, I've spent the last 9 years working as a senior executive, board member, entrepreneur, and investor at the intersection of new technologies and digital media surrounded by genius technologists from MIT, a billionaire entrepreneur, and a rock star team of branding and marketing gurus.

I'm currently part of an amazing team at North where we work with smart CEO's, investors, and entrepreneurs to provide the necessary leverage (think: capital, people, strategies) to quickly grow ideas into operating companies.

* Truthfully, I was a NFL league minimum earning, tiny division III college undrafted free agent, a perennial 6' 3" 340 pound long shot academic All-American, who was traded twice, released 5 times by four teams over 4 seasons, and who was once affectionately referred to by a Pro-Bowl teammate as an "all-effort no-talent white boy", which may be why my John Madden NFL '99 video game rating was only a 63. In my defense, he (Madden) rides in a giant bus everywhere because of an irrational fear of flying, so I don't think anyone should take anything he says too seriously.

Alex Bernstein’s Specialties:

The North sweet spot is helping to incubate early stage ventures (ideas, plans, joint ventures, etc) through enabling highly efficient sourcing of capital and human resources needed to scale a business quickly.

If you are sitting on a remarkable product or business idea and you are not sure how to make it happen, then you should talk to North.

    (Educational Institution; 1-10 employees; Philanthropy industry)

    January 2007 — Present (6 months)

    Working with a number of other retired NFL players I set up the Relentless Foundation in early 2007. Our mission is to build a bridge between retired NFL players and amateur football players to strengthen the game of football through the sharing of knowledge. We operate a number of platforms that seek to accomplish this mission. 1) We run free leadership and football skills camps for elite high school football players that are coached by retired NFL players. The first camp is in Oakland, CA in July 07, with more camps expanding nationally in 2008. 2) We publish a free email newsletter that is written by former NFL players. The Relentless email gives young players insight into training tips, life lessons, football history, and more all from the perspective of football's greatest players. 3) We operate a toll free hotline for retired players in dire need financial situations (1-888-NFL-VETS) which provides players in need with free grant application underwriting services.
