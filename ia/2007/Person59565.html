Michael Koenigs’s Summary

Michael Koenigs has spent more than 20 years pioneering multimedia technologies for a variety of clients including Sony Entertainment, 20th Century Fox, 3M, General Mills, Dominos Pizza, Ralston, Mazda Motors and New York Times bestselling authors including Tony Robbins, Deepak Chopra and Debbie Ford.

His first company, Digital Café, produced the world's first branded CD-ROM game, "Chex Quest" that shipped in more than six-million boxes of General Mills cereal and produced many of the world's first movie web sites, promotional screen savers and games for Sony, Columbia-Tristar and 20th Century Fox as early as 1994. He sold the company during the dotcom boom to a billion-dollar advertising agency in 1999.

His newest company, continues that tradition of firsts with the "Internet Infomercial Toolkit", a complete how-to course that teaches anyone how to make their own web marketing videos to sell virtually any product or service.

One thing very few people know about Michael is he plays the didgeridoo and has performed on five albums including the soundtrack to the Imax Film, “Extreme”.
