Greg Peterson, CFP’s Summary

An MBA with training and recognitions from various Entrepreneurial Societies, the CFA Institute, as well as Governor's Task Force on Venture Capital.
On May 31, 2006, The Wall Street Journal reported, "Most advisers have had little formal financial education. For instance, maybe 5% of brokers, financial planners and insurance agents have bothered to become a certified financial planner, or CFP, which has become the basic credential for any half-decent adviser. To ensure your adviser is knowledgeable, stick with CFPs..."
Of the 5% who are CFPs, only a small fraction have an MBA in Finance and have significant experience creating and/or managing business enterprises.
Wholistic Financial Planning means more than picking investments. Like any doctor examining your physical health, a CFP is uniquely qualified to optimize the distinct positions in each area of your financial health--especially for those with sizeable invested assets.

Greg Peterson, CFP’s Specialties:
