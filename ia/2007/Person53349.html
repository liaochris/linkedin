Maitri Venkat-Ramani’s Summary

My scientific interests lie where geoscience and computers meet, specifically in:

1) three-dimensional (3D) visualization and volume modeling techniques as tools for geoscientific data exploration, interpretation and collaboration
2) scientific software development, and
3) knowledge sharing and education.

With a background in structural geology, borehole geophysics, programming and application development, I am most interested in the development of 3D and 4D (animated) interpretation environments for data from the shallow subsurface and upper crust of the earth. This type of interdisciplinary approach has immediate benefits for the petroleum and gas, environmental, and nuclear waste and mining industries as well as in education and research.

I have worked as a geoscientist with Shell Exploration and Production Company since 2003. Current work includes quantitative and qualitative interpretation of high-resolution seismic data from deepwater oil and and gas fields in the Gulf of Mexico and application to software development for 3D subsurface data visualization and interpretation environments.

I obtained a B.S. degree in Geology from the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign (1998) and M.S. degrees in Geology and Geophysics (2000) and Computational Sciences (2002) from the University of Wisconsin-Madison. While at UW-Madison, I worked in structural geology with Dr. Basil Tikoff conducting physical experiments of transtensional folding, and with Dr. David Alumbaugh in the application of high-performance computing and 3D visualization techniques to the creation of software for electromagnetic geophysics.

My concern for literacy, information access, and the public domain has led to my involvement with Project Gutenberg (PG) for the past 12 years in the capacity of volunteer representative and Indian Languages team lead.
