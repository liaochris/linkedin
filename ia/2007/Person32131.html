Rosanna Tarsiero’s Summary

My facilitation skills span from face-to-face to online settings, where I devote particular care and attention toward the use of computer-mediated communication in conversation and how they are impacted by culture. My approach entails a blend of socratic method and dialogic facilitation (as opposed to active listening). My area of expertise is the nucleation, facilitation and repair of communities of practice (CoPs).

As a coach, my approach is a very pragmatic one, in that I assist people in problem-solving and troubleshooting their everyday life, with particular expertise in coaching people with bipolar disorder and/or ADHD. I abhor the victim mentality and therefore help my clients in taking responsibilities for what they can change in themselves and letting go of what they can't change in others. In particular, when it comes to people with mental illness, I stress the need for inner work and self-determination, as opposed to sole reliance on professionals of any kind (conventional as well as alternative).

My nonprofit management skills involve the management of on-site & online volunteers as well as the implementation of knowledge management initiative in nonprofits. I priviledge a people-first approach mixed with a loose hierarchy kind of management.

Among my management skills that can be useful in for-profit settings too, I can assist you in implementing reasonable accomodations for persons with mental illness, as well as setting up online communities for self-help and/or support of such population.

My research fields encompass: volunteering, mental illness, ICT, motivation, knowledge and empowerment, and their various intertwinings. I tend to follow action research methodology and blend qualitative and quantitiative methods. In particular, I'm methodologically very fond of social network analysis and pattern recognition methodology.
