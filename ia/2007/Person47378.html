Ken Stampe’s Summary

In 1997 I accepted a position as a mortgage loan officer with Centex Home Equity Corporation. Over the next 18 months, I was to win the honors as top loan officer for 1998 and received a trip to Las Vegas as a reward.

In 1999 I was recruited to join Countrywide in their sub-prime lending division called Full Spectrum Lending. I was a member of the team that set the record for funded loans in one month at 50 units. We later went on to set the record for funded loan volume of 15 million in one month.

During the spring of 2000 a manager asked if I would be interested in interviewing for a position with Countrywide Correspondent Lending. Despite knowing very little about what a correspondent lender does, I interviewed and was hired as an account executive. I spent 3 years - through 2003 - with this division of Countrywide Home Loans. My last position was as a Product Development Manager responsible for creating new business plans and affinity relationships.

In 2003 I took a position with Beal Bank in their subsidiary, LoanStream. The concept was a call-center and local-market mortgage lending operation. I was in charge of training and development.

From late 2003 through the end of 2005 I worked for IndyMac Bank as a wholesale lending regional manager. My territory covered 9 states and ultimately 15 wholesale account executives covering over 500 mortgage brokers.

Launching in 2006 with a business partner and a business plan, we formed AMC-TradeMark Financial in Richardson, TX. Over the next 19 months we grew in size, shape and focus. It was alternately the most rewarding and the most frustrating thing I have ever done. In October of 2006. the financial realities were such that it was best to close the company and move on.

I now am very excited and envigorated to be working for a great company in Bank of America. I'm back to doing what I do best, talk to homebuyers about providing them a mortgage loan.
