Mark Anthony’s Summary

After a career in information technology, communications, and management, I returned to college and earned my M.A. in Professional Writing with a concentration in Rhetoric and Composition. In addition to teaching part-time at North Georgia College & State University, I am currently working on my Ph.D. in Rhetoric, Composition, and Professional and Technical Writing at Georgia State University. I spend a great deal of time studying technology in relation to culture, communication, and rhetoric.

My research interests include:

o Digital Rhetoric and Online Discourse
o New Media
o Identity Issues
o Issues of embodiment/disembodiment (i.e. how to conduct online ethnography)
o Web 2.0, Web 3.0, the “Social Web,” and the “Semantic Internet”
o Intellectual Property
o Multimedia Writing (Writing Across Media)
o Computers and Composition / Technology in Pedagogy
o Genre Theory
o Civil Discourse: Especially as related to discourse of politics, religion, and race
o Postmodernism
o Visual Rhetoric (special interest in digital and online visual rhetoric)
o Popular Culture and American Studies (special interest in digital culture)
o Gaming Rhetoric
