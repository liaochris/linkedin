Brian Wang’s Summary

Brian's leadership experience began at UC Irvine, as a resident advisor and a freshman orientation counselor. After noting the lack of acting opportunities for minority students, Brian assembled the school's first multi-ethnic performance troupe. He was also a columnist and photographer for the school newspaper.

During college, Brian worked part-time as a tutor for autistic children, as a counselor for formerly incarcerated people afflicted with HIV, and as a life skills coach for adults with mental and developmental disabilities.

Upon graduation, Brian served as Head Counselor for the Chinese Youth Leadership Camp in Dallas. He then went to Shanghai to establish a summer program for an English-language boarding school, where he recruited and trained the instructors and counselors. Brian also developed the ESL curriculum for an after-school program in Arcadia, CA. Brian has taught several classes ranging in size from 15 to 100 students.

At UC Hastings, Brian resumed his extracurricular activities as President of APALSA and the Associated Student Body (ASUCH). He also served as editor of the Hastings Independent newsmagazine, and as a volunteer for the Asian Law Caucus and Hastings Tenderloin Tutoring Program.

An avid actor, Brian has performed onstage with artists from Asian American Theater Company (AATC), Bindlestiff, and Lodestone. His most recent production was "The Love Edition 2: Turned On!" with Bindlestiff in February 2007.

Currently, Brian serves on the Board of Directors for the Hastings Alumni Board of Governors and AATC. He co-founded Citizens for the World, an organization that promotes awareness of poverty issues in Asia. Brian also co-founded an interdisciplinary leadership organization named APA | FIVE, which assembled INSPIRE '07, an APA Leadership Conference in San Francisco.

Brian is presently employed as a contract attorney at Minami Tamaki LLP, where he worked as a summer associate in 2005.
