Jean Fahmy’s Summary

In his 10 years of experience, Jean Fahmy has built, managed and sold 2 successful IT businesses and in IT outsourcing services (12 employees), in mobile middleware software (43 employees & 4 international offices). He has also restructured a 10-year-old ISP, doubled ebitda and revenues in 2 years. In April 2004, he has started a venture capital fund for breakthrough humanitarian technologies where he acted as Chief Strategy Officer for the fund and its ventures. In 2006, he started and is in the process of now selling a Web 2.0 experience marketplace website.

Jean Fahmy lectures the Technology Entrepreneurship course at the bachelors and master's level at L’École Polytechnique de Montréal and at Concordia University.

He received no less than a dozen entrepreneurship and technology awards and is a speaker at tech and entrepreneurship conventions. Jean Fahmy was a selected participant in the Governor General Leadership Convention 2004 with Mr Paul Desmarais Jr. as Chair and is a vice-chair for the 2008 Quebec tour. He is a very involved member of the exclusive YEO (Entrepreneurs Organisation) on the local and international board, where he is responsible for the Intl Benefits Committee and Technology Advisory Committee. In YEO, he took care of a 25 000+ internet peer to peer network. As well, he is an award-winning member of the Toastmaster’s Organization on his way to becoming a Professional Speaker.

Along with his professional work, Jean Fahmy volunteers his time to weekly hosting of a group of thirty young people aged between 15 - 25 years as well as mentoring and teaching many future entrepreneurs. His wife is a criminologist who works with Child Protective Services. They have 2 boys under the age of four.

An experienced and results-oriented leader, a relationship-builder, always exceeding goals and objectives, able to make the tough decisions, solve problems and drive consensus.

Jean Fahmy’s Specialties:

entrepreneurship / intrapreneurship, internet/web strategy and strategic planning, partnerships, Web 2.0, new media, commercialization, leadership and management, technology, breakthrough technology, teaching, mentoring, humanitarian investments
