Bob Killough’s Summary

Bob Killough has held IS positions in public and private companies spanning Financial Services, e-Commerce, Government, Retail and Chain Store Systems.

In 2007 Killough joined IBM as a Retail Solutions Consultant. In 2005, Killough directed Applications and Data Services for Multnomah County Oregon, where he advocated for integration of Open Source software.

From 2002 to 2004, Killough directed Store Systems at Hollywood Video, delivering the retail industry's first .Net-based POS system based on the A.R.T.S. data model.

Killough's IS career began at HEB Grocery, an innovator in the Supermarket industry. As Director of Retail Technology, he directed development of the industries first PC-based POS. As Director of Advanced Technology, Killough implemented digital shelf displays, wireless inventory management, customer self-scanning, electronic payments and an online shopping site.

In 1999, Killough joined eMercatus Consulting as CTO/VP, E-Commerce Systems where he managed the development of the world’s first Spanish/English web auction site. During this time Killough served on the Board of Virtual Select, a technology startup in San Antonio.

In 2002 he was VP/IS for Billserv.com, a publicly held Electronic Bill Presentment and Payment company recognized as an innovator in the use of Internet ASP services for Fortune 500 clients.

In 2003, Killough was elected to the A.R.T.S. Board, an industry standards body he has been involved with for over 15 years. He is a past Editorial Advisor to Retail Systems Alert, and has spoken at the Retail Systems, Marketechnics and A.R.T.S. conferences. His work has been recognized in several publications, including Progressive Grocer, Chain Store Age Executive, Retail Systems Alert and Supermarket News.

Killough holds a B.S. in Computer Science and is active in the Society for Information Management and the Project Management Institute. He holds PMP and ITIL Foundation certifications.
