Bayard Kohlhepp’s Summary

I started playing guitar more than thirty years ago and developed a real passion for acoustic instruments. When I started recording music 10 years ago, I became fascinated with the challenge of accurately capturing acoustic music, the sound of vibrating wood and steel. Tone, whether acoustic or electric, is the heart of all music, and the most difficult attribute to preserve when making a recording. My goal is to capture the essence, immediacy, and passion of the live performance so the listener can re-experience that moment with the performer, rather than listening to technology tricks and the recording effect du jour.

My audio production work led to a client request to produce a corporate video, and I now produce TV commercials, training videos, and infomercials. Again, my goal is to present the message simply and without adornment. Video production is an intensely technological process, but that technology should be invisible to the viewer. The goal is to communicate the client's message, not to showcase a current production fad.

These technology-based creative endeavors are built on a foundation of more than twenty years' computer experience, on platforms ranging from mainframes to handhelds. As a computer integrator and software designer, I am an innovative leader, successful at leveraging technology to attract customers, increase revenue, improve efficiencies, facilitate growth and reduce costs.
