Nate Hansen’s Summary

Narrative journalist, feature writer and columnist turned freelance writer and ambitious ficiton writer. Broke stories ranging from international rock star's new venture in wine-making [Tool and Puscifer's Maynard James Keenan] to investigating an alleged Arizona cult to covering the notorious Sedona Gun Club, a fun-loving group of undiscovered artisans; poets, painters, photographers and the like. After graduating with a B.S. in Pre-Law and Sociology, serving in the United States Army then teaching Arabic, English, and Physical Education at an international boarding school, entered the field of journalism and was self-taught. Within a year, was offered a column and quickly established a voice, an audience and award recognition. Before resigning to work as a contractor and freelance writer, was nominated for a Pulitzer Prize in both criticism and commentary categories.

Nate Hansen’s Specialties:

Interview skills, structure and form, investigative skills, editing, finding a voice, branding and identity, Arabic translation, public relations

    natespeak

    (Privately Held; Myself Only; Writing and Editing industry)

    November 2007 — Present (3 months)

    “Natespeak” is a base language of mixed sarcasm, cynicism and wit meant to provoke thought and action by means of creating controversey and, in turn, discussions which lead to brainstorming, decisions and solutions. “Natespeak” follows the theory that, as the information age evolves into new media and becomes more accessible to the masses, vernacular must transform itself to accommodate and facilitate the people.

    Thomas Wolfe’s New Journalism stepped beyond the bounds of typical news writing, combining both narrative and first and third person accounts, shortly followed by a generation later calling themselves the New New Journalism, which has done much the same. But today, New New New Journalism, or as writer Nate Hansen coined “New Cubed,” is the trend.
