Thomas P. (Tom) Logan’s Experience

  • Investment Manager (US/Europe/Asia)

    TNP On The Road Venture Capital

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Venture Capital & Private Equity industry)

    November 2006 — Present (1 year)

    Mid-sized and growing "hands-on" Japanese venture capital firm in Yokohama, Japan focusing on promising early stage companies, founded with the assistance of Kanagawa Prefecture government, private industry, non-profit associations and academia. Parent Tsunami Network Partners (TNP) first round fund size at 14.3 Billion Japanese Yen, and subsequent TNP On the Road (TOR) fund raised at 7.18 Billion Japanese Yen, as of mid-2007 in an additional round of fundraising worldwide and in Japan.

  • Manager, Asian Business Development

    Fairfax County Economic Development Authority

    (Government Agency; 11-50 employees; Government Administration industry)

    August 2001 — November 2006 (5 years 4 months)

    Leadership in managing outreach activities for County of Fairfax, Virginia, USA to companies and institutions located from Asia (Japan, Korea, Taiwan, Singapore, India, Malaysia, Philippines) in their efforts to establish business operations in the USA and create job growth through employment of local US citizens, managing two overseas offices (Seoul and Bangalore) and contract staff therein. Professional assistance to US firms developing Asian markets also offered.

  • Vice President, Japan

    American Electronics Association (AeA) Japan Branch

    (Non-Profit; 51-200 employees; Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    April 1996 — December 2000 (4 years 9 months)

    Major results: US-Japan Bilateral NTT Agreement to lower interconnectivity costs, and a highly successful US-Japan Onsen Communications Series program in tandem with the Foreign Commercial Service of the US Embassy, Tokyo, which resulted in hundreds of thousands of dollars in sales for AeA member companies in Japan in information technology products and services, introduced through this vehicle to major electric power companies and new common telecommunications carriers (NCCs) throughout Japan.

  • Vice President, Asia; Japan Branch President

    The Austin Company

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Construction industry)

    December 1988 — April 1994 (5 years 5 months)

    The Austin Company. Now owned by Kajima Corporation, it was the third most successful US engineering company in Japan in terms of competitive bidding and other projects in the late 1980s and early 1990s--following Bechtel and Schal-Bovis. It secured design and construction projects throughout Japan based on aggressive marketing efforts in-country and backed up by various US-Japan Bilateral Government Agreements on Japanese construction market opening measures--with billings totalling more than $55.5 million dollars over a six year period. These results are recognized in a banner article in the December 29, 1990 issue of "Business Week" magazine and in a host of other publications.
