Maximiliano Marzetti’s Summary

A native of Argentina, Maximiliano Marzetti's own research interests are in the area of antitrust law, intellectual property and comparative law and economics.
He obtained his law degree from the Pontificia Universidad Católica Argentina Santa María de los Buenos Aires, where he also taught commercial law.
He earned two master of law degrees, one from Università degli Studi di Torino in intellectual property, another from the the Universität Hamburg in law and economics, for which he was awarded the Erasmus Mundus grant by the European Commission.
He is currently working toward a joint doctorate in law and economics at Università di Bologna and Erasmus Universiteit Rotterdam.
He served as a stipendiat at the Max Planck Institute for Intellectual Property, Competition and Tax Law in Munich, Germany and also provided legal counsel to the XX Winter Olympic Games Organizing Committee in Turin, Italy.
