  • Senior Process Safety Engineer

    Chilworth Technology, Inc.

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Chemicals industry)

    August 1993 — June 2004 (10 years 11 months)

    Investigated fires and explosions at chemical process industry (CPI) and U.S. Department of Defense (DOD) facilities and conducted preventive process hazard assessments using accepted methods, principles, and practices, including National Fire Protection Association (NFPA) and Center for Chemical Process Safety (CCPS) guidelines and standards.

    Collected, developed, and analyzed incident evidence and assessment data, reviewed operating procedures, interviewed witnesses and plant personnel, reconstructed incidents, and determined incident root causes and contributing factors.

    Prepared comprehensive reports on investigations and assessments, recommended corrective actions to improve process safety and prevent incident recurrence, and presented findings and recommendations to client management, engineers, and operators. Conducted training classes in areas of expertise at client facilities and through Center for Professional Advancement (CPA).

    (Non-Profit; 201-500 employees; Chemicals industry)

    July 1987 — December 1990 (3 years 6 months)

    Formerly known as the "Chemical Manufacturers Association."

    Analyzed and critiqued emerging and promulgated OSHA and EPA regulations, including Toxic Release Inventory Program, Accidental Release Inventory Program, and other regulations dealing with chemical release reduction, pollution prevention, waste classification, and hazardous waste treatment and management. Communicated results of analysis and research to ACC member companies, government agencies, other trade associations, and the media.

    Developed industry safety and environmental codes of practice incorporated into Responsible Care – the chemical industry's environmental, heath, and safety performance measurement and improvement initiative. Provided regulatory compliance assistance to ACC member companies. Developed a working knowledge of OSHA and EPA regulations, including RCRA, CERCLA (Superfund), and SARA (Title III) issues and requirements.

  • Chemical Engineer

    Jacobs Engineering Group

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; JEC; Environmental Services industry)

    June 1986 — June 1987 (1 year 1 month)

    Supervised the remediation of inactive hazardous waste sites under EPA’s Superfund Program. Supported EPA in its development of regulations and compliance assistance materials under various regulatory programs, including the Land Disposal Restrictions, Underground Storage Tank, and Hazardous Waste Relisting programs.

    Inspected CPI facilities to document waste generation sources and waste management practices. Researched CPI waste management and chemical storage practices. Developed waste treatment and chemical storage standards. Conducted a national survey of waste treatment and management capacity.

    Assigned additional responsibilities as Occupational Health and Safety Officer. Ensured that personnel were prepared for hazardous materials investigations and protected against exposure to hazardous substances. Maintained inventory of personnel protection and environmental monitoring equipment. Maintained records for personnel health surveillance and safety training.
