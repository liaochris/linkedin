Dean Whitbread’s Summary

I'm creative. I make content for internet, produce video and audio, and I'm writing a book in longhand. I'm also creative in business. My background in interactive media often equips me for jobs nobody has yet described.

So far, I've made the news doing four things: ecological art (1983), the world's first internet band (1995), writing and performing music (1987 - 2007), and co-founding and heading up the UK Podcasters Association (2006-7).

In 1994 I started Netmare, the first professional web design agency in Britain, and so began putting cool stuff on online. 2 years later started Netcast Ltd (still running) which pioneered live audio streaming, August 1996 netcasting the Radio 1 Rap stage from the Notting Hill Carnival. Live streamed Vince Clarke and his videos from a Shed December 31st 1999. On it goes...

Working with DJs in Switzerland and UK set up an early social media website www.djpool.ch 1998 - 2003.

Began blogging and podcasting 2004. In 2006 I started Talking Voices Ltd, the UK's first social media company.

I invented the words "Retox" "Cockabindi" and "Sputnoef".

I regularly work for non-profit and charitable causes, which helps me deal with the fact that human greed is rapidly destroying our beautiful earth. I initiated and took part in many "interruptive" performance art events in public places; I wallpapered trees in a piece entitled "Camouflaging Trees from Further Attack", I dressed people as postage parcels and invaded Kings Cross station, and I distributed "free money" (pro-ecology propaganda) at many performances and festivals.

More at http://funk.co.uk/dean.html - less at http://deanwhitbread.com/blog/
