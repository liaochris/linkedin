Mike Clark’s Summary

A talented, confident, creative, deadline-oriented, technically skilled and commercially aware editor, chief sub, designer, content developer and new media expert that can bring flair, experience and originality to every project.

Mike Clark’s Experience

  • Communications adviser

    Informing Healthcare

    (Government Agency; 51-200 employees; Hospital & Health Care industry)

    January 2007 — Present (1 year 1 month)

    Informing Healthcare is NHS Wales's technical hothouse, where new ways of using technology to improve healthcare in Wales are dreamed up and built. I'm in on a contract to bring their web sites and electronic comms up to speed. The job involves using marketing, design, copywriting and technical skills to present the sort of polished and cutting-edge image one would expect from a well-resourced organisation that provides technology leadership on a national level.

  • Partner

    Fryers Wharf Research

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Online Media industry)

    April 2005 — Present (2 years 10 months)

    Currently exploring several web-based projects with partner Sarah, including New Model Media and newsnetix.com

  • Deputy editor, Bristol Observer

    Northcliffe Newspaper Group

    (Public Company; Newspapers industry)

    December 2003 — April 2005 (1 year 5 months)

    Joined Northcliffe's largest free weekly (180,000+ circulation) as chief sub-editor. Directly responsible for training and running a team of four subs and for all design and production work. As deputy editor I was also actively involved in the management of the news and picture desks.

  • Editor and publisher

    The Riviera Gazette

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Newspapers industry)

    September 2001 — October 2003 (2 years 2 months)

    Started and ran the French Riviera’s first English-language weekly newspaper. The paper, pitched as a mass-market tabloid and designed to attract a diverse readership from itinerant yacht workers in Antibes to millionaire merchant bankers and international celebrities in Monaco, quickly built up a readership of over 20,000 a week, reaching over 85% of local English speakers.

  • Partner

    SJB Research

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Information Services industry)

    1992 — 1999 (7 years)

    Managed the production, design, sales and marketing of the company’s ever-growing portfolio of newsletters, research reports, conferences, internet and email services as the business expanded in the international smart card, biometrics and customer loyalty sectors. Negotiated the sale of SJB Research's customer loyalty products to Centaur Communications Ltd in 1998 and its smart card and biometrics portfolio to Reed Elsevier in 1999.

  • PR & marketing manager

    Codemasters

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Computer Games industry)

    1986 — 1992 (6 years)

    Joined CodeMasters in its start-up phase as a games author, where I initially authored three published games. Worked in many roles during the company's exciting early years, handling game development, packaging and production, marketing and public relations.
