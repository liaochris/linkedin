Michael Tyson’s Summary

I am a Computer Science PhD student, living in Melbourne, Australia.

In 2005 I completed a Bachelor of Computer Science with First Class Honours at Monash University; my PhD (started March 2006) continues with the research group I worked with in 2005, covering mobility issues in ad hoc networking. I have experience in application software development, with C, C++, Objective-C and Java, using Cocoa, wxWidgets and Win32/MFC; web development with PHP/MySQL, some graphic design, network programming and networking, and UNIX/Linux administration. My research interests include intelligent adaptive ad-hoc routing, radio propagation environment modeling, mobile station movement tracking and prediction, differential GPS over IP, power consumption optimisation and fairness in mobile ad-hoc networks, and ad-hoc network security.

I write software, primarily for Mac OS X, and have worked as a software developer at Internet Vision Technologies and Smartpath Pty. Ltd.

I have interests in Open Source Software, music (trombone since 1993, and guitar since 2004) and music composition, photography, art, and saving the world.
