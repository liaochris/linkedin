Robert Gebhardt’s Summary

My experience in UBS was in the field of commodity trade finance. While working there I was financing international transactions as well as executing stock, option and foreign exchange trades for our clients.

Having lived and gone to school in Europe, South America, and the United States, my work with Synovate provided me with exposure to Asia. My job consisted of supervising and/or conducting all market research necessities: from questionnaire development, to fieldwork and data processing, to analysis and recommendations. I was dealing with two of Synovate’s most important clients in the Asia Pacific region – one a leading global tobacco products company, and the other a leading global fast foods chain – and helped consolidate the Company’s relationships with both the clients. These two clients combined accounted for revenues of approximately US$ 5 million for our Korean office in fiscal 2003.

As a graduate student at Cornell, I undertook a thesis project focused on determining how the efficiency of markets can be utilized to forecast levels of inward foreign direct investment for specific countries, thereby serving as an indicator for country development. To support my research, I enrolled in a set of studies that included a variety of business and economics courses, such as managerial finance, game theory, business law, international trade policy, global competitive strategies, and behavioral finance.

I am now in Atlanta, where I am a co-founder and vice president of two companies, Tripsar and Magister, which deal with Real Estate development and Natural Stone imports respectively. My role as VP is to set up the marketing and finance functions, which I have now accomplished. I have set up a new rent roll, invoicing and financial statement system for our real estate division, as well as a pricing and shipment model for our import division.
