Tracy Nolan’s Summary

In 2005, Tracy joined Cameron International as Chief Information Officer. Prior to his role at Cameron; Tracy was the CIO for Merial Ltd., a $1.8Billion animal heath company headquartered in Duluth, GA.

Tracy joined General Electric in 1983 as a senior consultant and account executive with GE’s Consulting Business. He spent over 20 years with GE in various businesses including GE Plastics, GE Silicones, GE Power Systems, and GE Corporate. He held a variety of positions with diverse responsibilities; New Product Introduction Systems Leader, Manufacturing Systems Leader, Operations and Technology Leader, Applications Development Leader, Global Employee Services Leader, and Leader of Cost Reduction Initiatives.

Tracy was involved with the conception and introduction of many of GE’s world famous management techniques, such as, Workout, Business Process Re-engineering, Cost Bullet Trains, Six Sigma, and Digitization. He is a Master Black Belt in Six Sigma.

Tracy began his career in banking as a programmer/analyst and account executive with Norstar Bancorp and State Street Bank of Boston.

Tracy is on the board of advisors for SAP’s Industrial Machinery and Components Council and Microsoft’s Oil and Gas Advisory Board. He is also a member of British Telecom’s Customer Partnership Council. He is a graduate of Syracuse University with degrees in Finance and Psychology and attended GE's Management Development Institute.

Tracy and his wife Nancy live in Katy, TX and have six children – Michelle (1980), Christopher (1981), Colin (1983), Stephanie (1986), Katy (1987), and Allison (1991).
