Steven Robertshaw’s Summary

Steve Robertshaw is a Principal Researcher and Consultant specialising in studying the relationships between Distributed Artificial Intelligence (DAI) and Knowledge Representation (KR) within the context of High Performance Computing.

Currently, Steve is working with Dstl, a UK Government Agency. For Dstl, he works in the areas of Semantic Web technologies and Grid computing; within these areas, his remit is to consult widely across UK Government in general and within the MoD particularly.

Currently, Steve is also working with units in the European Commission (EC) on matters related to Grid technology, Semantic Web technology and in the Exploitation of High Tech research products. He has also been active at various levels in several EC funded research projects as researcher (DAI), evaluator (Grid) and reviewer (Grid, KR, and Semantic Web). These activities have been spread across frameworks 4, 5 and 6.

Steve has previously taught at the University of London (QMUL and UCL) designing, delivering and assessing postgraduate (MSc and MEng) courses in eCommerce engineering.

In the past Steve has worked for other parts of the UK Government, Nortel Networks Plc and emorphia Ltd. In the latter two organisations he conducted research into complex problem solving techniques, DAI and ubiquitous computing. He also contributed to World's first Open Source MAS Agent Platform: FIPA-OS.

Steve's main research interest lies in Ontology, particularly in relation to knowledge acquisition and information exploitation. He is especially concerned about the increasing potential for long term harm that is being brought about by the current divergence of real Ontology and computational ontology. The two disciplines must be united.

Steve is also involved in long term "Horizon Scanning" projects.

Steven Robertshaw’s Specialties:

Distributed Artificial Intelligence, Multi-Agent Systems, Grid Computing, Knowledge Engineering, Ontology, Taxonomy, Metadata, eCommerce Engineering, Semantic Web, Exploitation of research, Communication.
