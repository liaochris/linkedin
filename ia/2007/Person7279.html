Scott Miller’s Summary

I have been very successful over the last few years in Information Technology team leadership and project management.

Unlike most Information Technology workers, I know what IT means to the business. My MBA provided the business tools to be able to work with organizations and teams of business sponsors to refine business processes so that those project stakeholders could truly benefit from the project or organizational initiative being implemented. After all, customer satisfaction is a true test of a successful project, whether in Information Technology or in business itself.

Software craftsmanship. I personally have a passion for the design and development of software. I'm not talking about the mundane activity of software coding, but rather the architecture: the design and layout of the business processes and business rules, UML and class diagrams, the interaction between systems. Does the system and software satisfy the customer? Does it match what it was originally intended for, and does it have a fitness of use?

Project management fits perfectly into this mode as well by providing the tools to execute the software development process in a repeatable way while still maintaining the creative spark.

In May 2007 I graduated from the Southwestern College MBA program. In June I successfully passed the Project Management Professional (PMP) certification exam.
