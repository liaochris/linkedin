Phil King’s Summary

Managing design teams and defining product strategies responsible for eBay's diverse casual and professional sellers. History of creating and designing engaging consumer oriented applications, especially focussed on building online communities around photos and other digital media.

Phil King’s Specialties:

Specialize in user experience and user interface design for desktop software and web applications. Focus on balancing business needs/requirements with user experience needs.

Phil King’s Experience

  • Manager, User Interface Design

    eBay

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; ebay; Internet industry)

    July 2006 — Present (1 year 7 months)

    I manage a team of seven user interface designers responsible for maintaining and improving eBay's Seller Experience, Trust & Safety, and Customer Support teams. I provide leadership on product strategy for these areas, working closely with product management and business partners to propose new features for our products. My goal as a manager is to ensure that my team has the tools, knowledge, and support to excel in their work while encouraging a collaborative and fun working environment.

    As a User Experience manager I also work as the primary liaison between the UED organization and the business groups that I support. I keep the UED management team informed regarding the strategies in my areas, and help shape the overall direction of Design at eBay.

  • Senior User Interface Designer

    eBay, Inc

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; ebay; Internet industry)

    September 2005 — July 2006 (11 months)

    Lead creation of product strategy for eBay's advanced selling tools based on in-depth user research, business needs, and opportunities to create a rich and satisfying user experience. Following the product strategy I lead the redesign of eBay's successful TurboLister application, making significant improvements in user interface to support increased efficiency, flexibility, and satisfaction. User experience goals and design were vetted with the executive staff on a regular basis, and success has been measured closely in the months following release. After working closely with product development to ensure the launch of a high quality product, our success criteria have been exceeded for adoption, efficiency, and satisfaction. eBay's advanced seller tools are used by millions of people around the globe in 18+ languages, accounting for 25% of global listing activity on the site; well over 1 billion listings / year.

  • Lead UI Designer / Producer

    Homestead, Inc

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Internet industry)

    August 2000 — June 2004 (3 years 11 months)

    Homestead, Inc builds products that help people build personal and small business websites.

    In 2003-04 I helped create the PhotoSite product line, which includes desktop and web functionality to help people manage and share their digital photos. This product successfully launched in early 2004, and was acquired by United Online (Netzero) in late 2004.

    From 2000 to late 2002 I lead the successful redesign of Homestead's marketing website as well as the online functionality that subscribers use to manage their websites.

  • Vice President, Creative Development

    OnShare, LLC

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Internet industry)

    June 1999 — May 2000 (1 year)

    OnShare.com created a powerful Peer-to-Peer file sharing system allowing users to securely share files from their personal computer with anyone on the Internet. I designed the software and the web site, which worked in tandem to build a large file sharing community. OnShare provided various community based features for sharing files, but also allowed individuals to securely share files between home and office computers.

    I was the first employee and helped create the vision for OnShare. As Vice-President of Creative Design, I designed multiple versions of the OnShare web site and software.

  • Interface and Graphic Designer

In addition to UI design, I spent a lot of time writing cross-browser compatible, efficient HTML and JavaScript.

    Jump! was acquired by Microsoft, Inc. in the Spring of 1999 and integrated into MSN as MSN Calendar, a service of Hotmail.
