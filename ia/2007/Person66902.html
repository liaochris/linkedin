Pavel Antonov’s Summary

Pavel P Antonov is the Editor in Chief of Green Horizon (greenhorizon.rec.org), the magazine of the Regional Environmental Center for Central and Eastern Europe (REC), based in Budapest, Hungary. Author of articles, stories and reports on the state of environment in Central and Eastern Europe (CEE). Researcher of environmental journalism in CEE. A frequent trainer to environmental journalists, civil society and state officials in CEE and the New Independent States. In 2001 – 2003 managed the launching of the Regional Environmental Press Centre - a media support network for the Balkans. Communications and media consultant for government and civil society projects, including the EU’s Water Framework Directive Support Project, Tacis Cities Award Scheme for Environmental Protection, the UNDP/GEF’s Danube River Project and Green House Gas Inventories Support Project; Natura 2000 promotion. Co-founder and Member of the Board of BlueLink – the Bulgarian civil society electronic information network (www.bluelink.net). Co-ordinator of the Information Technology for Environmental Sustainability initiative of the Association for Progressive Communications (www.apc.org). In 1994 – 2001 Antonov has been producing television programmes and documentaries on nature protection, civil society, EU accession, politics and cultural diversity in Bulgaria. Former Editor in Chief of Information Programmes Department at Nova - the first independent TV channel in Sofia. Prior to that as a news reporter he covered the work of Bulgaria’s first democratically elected president Dr. Zhelyu Zhelev. Antonov graduated with honours from the Journalism Faculty of the Sofia University, and specialized environmental sciences and policy at the Central European University. Nominated finalist in the Young Environmental Journalist contest in Paris, 2000.
