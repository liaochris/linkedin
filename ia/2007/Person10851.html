Brad Glorioso’s Summary

I began my research experience as an undergraduate at Southeastern Louisiana University. There I assisted a graduate student in his Master's project examining amphibian recruitment along transects in a near-pristine marsh versus an open marsh. I also assisted in updating the amphibian and reptile inventory of the Manchac Wildlife Management Area on which the transects were placed. After my B.S. I enrolled in a M.S. program at Middle Tennessee State University. There I gained invaluable experience in many areas of wildlife biology. First, I was part of a lab which examined the movements of neonate timber rattlesnakes based on radio telemetry. Also, early on, I was part of a group who studied intraspecific interactions of banded sculpin in a first-order stream. I assisted another graduate student who was examining chronology of migration and patterns associated with migration of the five ambystomatid salamander species inhabiting the area. I worked on an amphibian and reptile inventory at a national battlefield in middle Tennessee. I was a field technician for a colleague who was examining the phylogenetics of the Tennessee Cave Salamander complex, a subterranean neotenic species. I conducted my Master's thesis research at Reelfoot Lake, and examined the population demography and feeding activity of stinkpot turtles at a roadside slough. In addition, I led the team that trapped turtles in the lake to get an idea of the assemblage and numbers of turtles in the lake. We captured nearly 2,500 turtles of nine species in one year. I also worked on an inventory of fishes at the same battlefield that I did the amphibian and reptiles inventory. After graduating with my M.S. I took a job as a seventh grade science teacher in southeast Missouri. This was not my calling as I belong in the field with the animals. I am slated to lead a turtle inventory of a state park in southeast Missouri.

Brad Glorioso’s Specialties:

I have written grant proposals and authored or co-authored many technical papers and notes on my research. I am knowledgable on trapping or inventory techniques for reptiles and amphibians as well as fish. I am confident in my ability to identify reptiles and amphibians in the eastern half of the United States.
