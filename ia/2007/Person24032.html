D'Angelo Merritt’s Summary

At the Institute of Marine Affairs, I am responsible for all aspects of accounting from transaction recording, through reporting on several bases to financial planning. I also help our scientists and other professionals with issues of Budget management, organisation systems and procedures, government liason and advocacy.

I have been in the accounting field since 1986 starting at the local firm of what is now KPMG. At KPMG I conducted audits of companies of various sizes and in several industries, from small mico-enterprise funds to large petroleum corporations. I learned to appreciate the full story of what financials say, and the issues within various industries. I also helped with devising and promoting financial plans for expanding companies and addressing and advising on taxation issues.

After nine years of at KPMG I moved to a small hotel as the Manager, Finance and Administration. At the hotel I learned managing on tight budgets and customer awareness. I helped put together a business plan that pointed the way to improved commercial success. Even though I was certain the plan would have worked, we failed to convince the financial partners and banker.

I moved from the hotel to InterServ Ltd. which is a small ISP that pioneered the way Internet services are provided. At InterServ I learned the struggles of a small start-up company as it matches ambitious ideas with a competitor that was its main service provider (the local telecom). After being wooed by several interested companies, InterServ was sold to an Information Technology based group of companies and I was let go.

I returned to Public Accounting after InterServ to re-examine my goals and decided that I should try independent advisory services for small entities to help them in their financial activities and prospects. Here I came face-to-face with the struggles of smaller companies as they claim space in competitive markets.
