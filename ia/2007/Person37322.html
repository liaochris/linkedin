Yi-Wen Liu’s Summary

Currently a postdoc with a research hospital, my background in engineering and mathematics helps me to collaborate with scientists, thus extending my skills across disciplinary boundaries. My professional interests include audio signal processing and computational modeling of the human auditory system. To pull an analogy from an electronic engineering perspective -- ionic channels are like PN junctions, sensory cells are like transistors, nerves resemble transmission lines, and the cochlea is literally a spectrum analyzer. Then, how the human brain processes auditory signals is still something hard for a computer to mimic. Broadly speaking, computational modeling of physiological systems is a hot emerging research area, like the way semiconductor device modeling was 20 years ago. Topics are numerous both at the "device" (cellular) and "system" (physiological) levels. If things work out with my postdoc training, I would like to start teaching at a research-oriented university in a few years.
