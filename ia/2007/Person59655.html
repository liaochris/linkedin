  • President and General Manager

    WORT FM (Back Porch Radio Broadcasting, Inc)

    (Non-Profit; 51-200 employees; Broadcast Media industry)

    1972 — 1977 (5 years)

    My first gig -- started this community radio station in Madison, WI. Got help finding the frequency from Jeremy Lansman. Tom Thomas and Terry Clifford helped write the license application. Jeff Lange, Pat Ryan, Don Allan, Vinnie Curren, John Ohliger, Mark Stoelting, Julie Priese, Joannie Rubel and many more helped raise the money and build the station. That was one hum-dinger gang. I'm happy to report that the station lives on, some 35 years later. Darn nifty.

  • Co-Founder

    National Federation of Community Broadcasters (NFCB)

    (Non-Profit; 1-10 employees; Broadcast Media industry)

    1974 — 1977 (3 years)

    First there was NARK (National Alternative Radio Konvention) that Bill Thomas organized, Lorenzo Milam inspired and I hosted. Then there was the League of Stations. And then we formed the National Federation of Community Broadcasters (NFCB). I was the first President, for 10 minutes until we realized it would be politically incorrect to have a white-guy in that role (the story of my life). So I drove Tom Thomas crazy as Treasurer.

    Now days NFCB represents stations, producers and others committed to community radio.
