Alf (Arnold) Kaech’s Summary

My professional career began as an Airbus A320 copilot for Swissair based out of Zurich flying European short haul routes (less than 4 hours). Following Swissair bankruptcy after 9/11, I went to Canada and completed a 2 year MBA at the prestigious Richard Ivey School of Business in London Ontario. During the program, I completed a 1 year consulting project with Bombardier aerospace together with three classmates to determine the viability of Bombardiers entrance into the 100 seat aircraft market. Following graduation, was recruited to join Bombardier Aerospace as a Customer Support specialist on the fledgling CSeries future airliner program. Following 2 years of development, the program was delayed out over the horizon and I decided to venture into the entrepreneurial realm. Following a year long trip through Southeast Asia investigating potential business opportunities, I have settled in San Francisco and Canmore, Alberta in my exciting current role as president of Big Richard Capital.

My career goals are lofty and exciting. My experience from the cockpit to the boardroom in the aerospace industry combined with my global network have led to some very interesting opportunities. My intention is to stay the current course and develop my entrepreneurial and financial skills within my new role with a global focus.
