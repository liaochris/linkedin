Peter Dickinson’s Summary

An experienced director, manager and consultant and has worked in and with small and medium sized companies for the last sixteen years. Over the last 6 years has worked with over 200 companies as a consultant and business advisor helping companies develop and implement strategy.Used strategic analysis and facilitative leadership skills to develop networked organisations in the Digital Industries and Advanced Manufacturing in Lancashire.

As a one of the founding directors of E4A, Peter is currently working with schools, businesses and organisations to help build effective teams, group problem solving and providing enterprise challange days to whole year groups at key stage 3 & 4. See www.e4a.org.uk and www.e4a-business.co.uk for more information.

Peter will help you crystallise your vision, rationalise your strategy and help you develop a consistent sales approach, improve customer service, streamline your processes and identify key performance indicators to help you drive your business to meet your goals.

Peter Dickinson’s Specialties:

Practical strategy, one page business plans, key performance measures, creativity, mind mapping, ecommerce strategy, process maps, marketing, sales, customer service, information technology. Key sectors include education, digital, manufacturing, business to business products & services, environmental and specialist civil engineering contractors.

  • Director

    KUB Ltd

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Management Consulting industry)

    June 2001 — Present (6 years)

    KUB helps individuals and companies develop key performance indicators and measures to help owner managers drive sales, improve customer service, streamline processes and promote learning and innovation.

    Using simple documents, KUB works with individuals and companies to help them focus on what they want to achieve and what needs to be done to achieve those goals.

    The result is a one page business plan that contains the key drivers and measures needed to increase the value of a business covering all its aspects.

    All this can be achieved by spending half a day a month over a period of time with the senior management of a company.

    There are no reports. Change is driven at a pace decided by the company and a key part is that knowledge is transferred into the company so that over time key skills are gained where they are needed.
