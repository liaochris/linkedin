Atul Chitnis’s Summary

I have been a technology consultant since the mid 1980s, focussing on data communication and networking, Free and Open Source Software (FOSS) promotion, deployment and migration, wireless networking, mobile and handheld computing, virtual and online communities, technology market enablement, etc.

I enjoy working with disruptive technologies such as Free and Open Source Software, technology-oriented communities and all aspects of mobile computing.

More can be found out about me at http://atulchitnis.com or you could just google for "Atul Chitnis".

Atul Chitnis’s Specialties:

Data communication and networking, Open Source deployment and migration, wireless networking, mobile and handheld computing, virtual and online communities, technology market enablement.

Extensive experience with dealing with conditions on the ground in India, interacting with international companies, extensive experience in interconnecting heterogenous networks and platforms, integration of Open Source solutions in the Enterprise.

  • Founder & Organizer

    Linux Bangalore

    (Non-Profit; Information Technology and Services industry)

    1999 — 2004 (5 years)

    Linux Bangalore (http://linux-bangalore.org) was India's first and largest annual Free & Open Source Software event.

    I conceived the idea of a event for the FOSS community in 1999, when I caused the community to participate in various technology events as an entity. In 2001, I, along with several like minded friends, helped the community put together its own event, called Linux Bangalore/2001. This event grew from a small regional event to a massive, national and internationally recognised event, and garnered support from industry, community and governments.

    It was eventually replaced with the current FOSS.IN conference.

  • Contributing Editor

    PCQuest @ Cyber Media India Ltd.

    (Privately Held; 201-500 employees; Information Technology and Services industry)

    1995 — 2002 (7 years)

    PC Quest is one of India's foremost technology magazines. I became associated with the magazine in January 1993, when I began writing a column named COMversations for the publication. In 1995, I was invited to be a contributing editor for the magazine, a position I held till 2002.

    While with PCQuest, I kicked off the PCQuest Linux Initiative, and led the team that created the annual Linux cover CD for the publication, which exposed hundreds of thousands of budding engineers to Linux and Open Source. My team conceived, created and mastered the annual CD, wrote most supporting articles for these special issues and provided write-in support for thousands of readers.

  • CTO

    ARB Constellation

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Information Technology and Services industry)

    1989 — 1994 (5 years)
