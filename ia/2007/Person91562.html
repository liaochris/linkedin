Stephan Wuermlin’s Summary

Dr. Stephan Würmlin is co-founder and CEO of LiberoVision Inc., a company specialized in visual content enhancement of sports media. During his PhD thesis at the Computer Graphics Laboratory at ETH Zurich, Stephan Würmlin pioneered a novel media technology called three-dimensional video which is the core of all LiberoVision products. Since the beginning in early 2000, he envisioned a real-world and productive application and commercialization of this technology. Being a computer graphics specialist and enthusiast as well as a big sports fan, a combination of both interests has always been a great dream. His PhD thesis was carried out in the context of a large research project at ETH Zurich, the blue-c (http://blue-c.ethz.ch). Upon receiving his PhD degree in 2004, Stephan Würmlin became a post-doctoral researcher at ETH Zurich and managing director of the blue-c-II (http://blue-c-II.ethz.ch), the successor project of blue-c, leading 12 PhD students and post-docs over three years. Stephan authored more than 15 publications and holds three international patents on the developed 3D video technology. Part of this has been adopted by MPEG as an extension of the MPEG-4 standard. He is 31 years old, native in German, fluent in English, and knowledgeable in French and Spanish. Stephan was awarded the "Venture Leaders" young entrepreneur's prize by the Gebert Rüf Foundation in 2006.
