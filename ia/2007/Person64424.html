Nance Schick’s Summary

I paid my way through undergraduate and law school by working multiple concurrent jobs. I experienced everything from event, sport facility and operations management to coaching, fitness instruction and sports agency…from babysitting and home health care to basic home improvement and automobile repair...and from restaurant service to customer service. There are few jobs that I haven’t done!

UPS gave me management, employee relations and workers’ compensation expertise. There, I implemented safety procedures and performed follow-up contacts with injured employees. I learned the interests of employer and employee. I only needed to better understanding the law and insurance. That came with my first attorney position.

Hired on Thursday as a workers’ compensation associate at a litigation firm in New York City, I was managing the department by the following Tuesday. I worked night and day learning the law and procedure while managing the existing caseload. I didn’t want to show how new I was, so I did all I could to reach the level of competence of my competitors. My efforts apparently paid off.

I was reluctant to risk sole proprietorship early in my career, but my clients, mentors and co-workers encouraged it. On June 23, 2003, I opened my firm with one case. By the end of the year, there were close to 100 cases, and I appeared in civil court on negligence cases for other attorneys who had scheduling conflicts. Currently, I manage up to 200 cases for a major insurance carrier, a national third-party administrator and six corporations. I also provide business and conflict resolution counseling, as the needs arise.

I really enjoy what I do. I am more than a lawyer. I am a part of the resolution team, and I recognize that litigation decisions and actions affect more than the instant case. I want to be a part of the solution because I want to see my clients succeed. If they succeed, then I succeed.
