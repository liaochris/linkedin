Sean Chapple’s Summary

* A extreme adventurer/explorer who has organised and led several ground-breaking expeditions to the Polar Regions. For each venture Sean has selected a team of ordinary individuals and developed them into peak performing teams. Sean is a Fellow of the Royal Geographical Society.

* A professional public speaker, Sean delivers inspirational keynotes on adventure, leadership and team dynamics by interweaving a lifetime of extreme adventure with a coherent message to businesses. Sean is an Associate of Professional Speakers Association.

* An experienced trainer and performance consultant Sean provides executive leadership and teamworking solutions to meet business needs. Accredited TMSDI practitioner in the Team Management Profile, Linking Skills Profile and Team Performance Profile.

* GOALS : To realise full potential in self and others and to continue to pursue polar challenges.

  • North & South Pole Expedition Leader

    Polar Quest

    (Government Agency; 11-50 employees; Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    July 2006 — February 2007 (8 months)

    In 2006/07 Sean planned and led the first UK Armed Forces expedition to the South Pole, Sean had just returned from leading another team to the North Pole earlier in the year. After hauling to the pole his team then retraced their steps, and in doing so completed the longest military overland journey in Antarctic history and, for Sean the first Royal Marine to walk unaided to the pole. His team faced near starvation, suffered from frostbite and during the return leg Sean fell and dislocated his shoulder. Committed to success Sean continued to motivate and lead his team, and through determination and exceptional teamwork they completed the grueling 1,400-mile journey in seventy-one days. Live updates broadcast direct from the ice engaged a global audience attracting international media interest, and generating 2 million pounds in advertising for the commercial backers of the expedition.
