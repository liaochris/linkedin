Scott Miller’s Summary

In 1987, I founded Apogee Software, which pioneered episodic gaming, game demos, and online digital distribution, all three still in use today. Apogee helped birth real-time first-person 3D action gaming, the most prevalent form of gaming today, with the release of Wolfenstein 3D in 1992.

I re-branded Apogee as 3D Realms Entertainment in 1994, and co-founded the successful publishing company, Gathering of Developers, in 1998. I co-created and co-designed three major original game IPs, with sales totalling over 11 million units: Duke Nukem, Max Payne and Prey. (In 2002, I negotiated the sale of the Max Payne IP to a publisher in a deal totalling $48 million--the first deal of its kind in the industry.) I was also involved in the creation of the mega-franchises, Descent and Wolfenstein 3D. Throughout my career I've established strong business relationships with third-party game studios, going back to Id Software in 1990, helping these studios to create and develop new, successful game IP. In total, I've been significantly involved in the creation of multiple IPs with sales totaling over $1.1 billion at retail--and not one Hollywood license among them.

I've spoken at E3, SIGGRAPH, ECTS and several other events.

Scott Miller’s Specialties:

Creating successful original IP, storyverse design, collaborative game design, game hooks, branding & positioning, paradigm shifting.

    (Entertainment industry)

    April 1987 — Present (20 years 6 months)

    For many years in the 80's and 90's, Apogee revolutionized and dominated the shareware game industry, pioneered demo distribution for games (no publisher did this before Apogee), episodic releases, and digital distribution. Apogee lead the way in the rise of the financially independent PC studio, and was the first studio to partner with peer studios to create original games. Apogee was at ground zero with the creation of the FPS genre, as the exclusive distributor of this game in 1992, and contributing design and all marketing direction to this title.

    In 1994, Apogee rebranded itself as 3D Realms, and continued its success with Duke Nukem 3D, many Duke console releases, Max Payne, and Prey.

    3D Realms has always been fiercely independent, loyal to other development studios and the developer community, and a strong, vocal proponent of studio IP ownership. As part of this, 3DR runs the largest, most influential underground developer-only discussion group in the industry, since 2001.
