Tim Stephens’s Experience

  • Founder & CEO

    ToolPax Corporation

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Computer Software industry)

    June 2002 — Present (5 years 8 months)

    We accelerate commerce for build-to-order companies so they get the right parts right now.

    Am responsible for overall operations with focus on business development and co-architecting our patent pending web-based engine.

    This engine solves three problems in the industry:

    Problem: Online CAD files are not shop ready because they are partially complete and partially correct.
    Solution: We create CAD files on demand so they are shop ready to the specifications of the user.

    Problem: Inaccurate BOMs from part number crossover errors result in ordering the wrong parts.
    Solution: We build BOMs with an engine that automates crossover so the results are accurate.

    Problem: The customer must waste time by waiting for quotations before making a purchase decision.
    Solution: The user can buy components on demand with real-time price and delivery results.

    Tim has been bootstrapping ToolPax since 2002 and will reach critical mass by Q1 2008.

  • President

    DieWorks Corp.

    (Privately Held; Myself Only; Management Consulting industry)

    September 1999 — May 2002 (2 years 9 months)

    DieWorks was a management consulting endeavor focused on bolstering the competitive strengths of North American tooling manufacturers.

    Also served as an expert witness in several litigation cases. The client had a favorable outcome with Tim as an expert witness.

  • President

    Stamping Technologies, Inc.

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Mechanical or Industrial Engineering industry)

    July 1986 — September 1999 (13 years 3 months)

    Stamping Technologies was a design firm focused on engineering stamping dies. Markets served include automotive, aerospace, and consumer products. Tool types processed and designed were line dies, transfer dies, progressive dies, jig and fixtures.

    Die operations included blank, draw, trim, form, flange, aerial cam, and die mount cam mechanics.
