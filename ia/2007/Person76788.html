Sasha Strauss’s Summary

Adjunct Professor of Brand Strategy at USC. When not teaching, I'm the Executive Director of Brand Strategy at Innovation Protocol, a brand development firm serving innovators in enterprise systems, biomedical engineering and interactive consumer technologies.

Regularly guest lecture at USC Annenberg & Marshall, UCLA, UC Irvine and professional organizations on a range of brand related subjects, from global brand strategy to personal branding.

Past:

Served as Director, Brand Development at Brand Sense Partners, a strategic brand extension and business development firm. Led brand strategy and development programs for all firm clientele, which range from multiple Clorox brands to Electronic Arts.

Previously, was a Senior Brand Strategy Consultant at Siegel & Gale, developing and managing international strategic branding programs. Client partner on all global Yahoo! brand programs while also leading initiatives for TiVo, BMC Software, Microsoft and Adobe Systems.

Before joining Siegel & Gale, worked at RiechesBaird Advertising as a Brand Planner; authoring strategic marketing and communications business plans for a varied clientele like Edwards Life Sciences and Toyota Industrial.

Earlier in career, co-founded and managed a promotional marketing firm. Over five years and with 80 contracted employees, the firm organized and staffed brand building events for clients such as SoBe Beverages, Saran and Philip Morris. Worked for Rogers & Cowan and TBWA\Chiat\Day, prior to owning the business.

Lead strategy work for clients world wide; managing brand initiatives in Korea, Japan, China, Singapore, Germany, France and the UK.

Sasha Strauss’s Specialties:

Rebel with a cause. Change agent. Strategic brand development by passion and by trade. 12 years leading the charge at agencies and consultancies. Active philanthropist and proprietor of ProBonoBrands.org.

    Brand Extension and Business Development Strategy

    As opposed to traditional brand consulting, Brand Sense's business model is focused on growth through strategic brand extension. Informed by the upfront brand strategy program, we then build a brand extension plan - identifying ways to grow & expand the brand's relevance into neighboring markets by leveraging the consumer's affinity for attributes of the master brand. We then use a proprietary category evaluation model to evaluate potential extensions on both brand relevance & market appeal. Surviving categories are built into a brand architecture model to ensure the master brand isn't diluted by the new initiatives. Subsequently we find licensees to manufacture & distribute the new product lines, with Brand Sense remaining a partner through retail.
