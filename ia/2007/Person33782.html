Gwendolyn Regina Tan’s Summary

Gwen loves the colours orange and green but detests the triteness of blue (”it’s everywhere”) and usually does out-of-point stuff like writing about something relatively insignificant such as this sentence. (When all you do is to write essays to get a degree, that is what you end up doing.) A European Studies major in the National University of Singapore (NUS), she spends most of her time dabbling in organizations such as the NUS Entrepreneurship Society and the NUS Entrepreneurs’ Association (based in Silicon Valley). During her one-year stint as a teaching assistant for an MBA course, Gwen also co-led the Start-Up@Singapore national business plan competition while playing field hockey for NUS. She is also presently sitting on the steering committee of the competition. Her involvement in sports range from the mild, like skydiving, to the extreme like speed-walking from class to class at the pace of 1 metre per second. She has been lucky enough to have had the chance to intern full-time at a startup company in Silicon Valley while taking part-time courses at Stanford University in sunny, semi-surfable, super snowboardable, California where she could combine entrepreneurship with extreme sports. A love for adventure also leads her to globe trot to places like France and Tahiti (to improve her French), and to various parts of South America (to improve her Spanish). In a yet another lucky situation, Gwen also got to represent NUS and Singapore in the Sino-Singapore Undergraduate Exchange Programme where she visited China for two weeks and found her Mandarin to have gotten more conversational. She also represented Singapore in the first ever Francophone summer university for young leaders of Europe in Romania. An ex-ballerina and cheerleader, Gwen still misses dance but her goal is to be a successful strategist, entrepreneur, consultant and citizen of the world.
