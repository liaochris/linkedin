Gayley Knight’s Summary

Co-founder, in 1995, of Connecting Links Web Consulting and Development, I delight in learning. My company shows small businesses, municipalities and nonprofits how to use technology economically and profitably -- and how to use the Web for easy and efficient marketing. I early on recognized the value of the ADA accessibility issue for local governments and County agencies and promoted development of web sites that were both accessible and usable. Notable among these are the Work-Life Toolkit Web site developed by the Montgomery County Commission for Women.

As co-producer of a public access TV show (WEBurbs, begun in 1999), we educate viewers to the thrills and pitfalls of using technology in business, My latest foray into technology and media is radio. My show, AM Coffee Break, went live on May 15, 2006, quickly morphing into Business Her Way -- and shortly going live on Internet radio. Join me daily as I talk business with women and men in the Washington, DC metro area.

Before starting my technology business, I owned and operated a successful contemporary American craft gallery and shop in Bethesda. I organized the first art walks in downtown Bethesda, a precursor to the current local art scene -- and was instrumental in introducing the Metropolitan Home Magazine Craft Awards to the Washington, DC area.

I am a graduate of Mills College, Oakland, California, a 7th-generation native of Rochester, New York, and a resident of the Washington, DC area since 1975.

Gayley Knight’s Specialties:

Anything Web, making connections (I am a connector), supporting and assisting women in business and business owners, community activist.

Gayley Knight’s Experience

  • Founder, On Air Host

    Business Her Way Radio & Resource

    (Partnership; 1-10 employees; Broadcast Media industry)

    March 2006 — Present (1 year 4 months)

    Talking business with women is the format for this unique radio show & web site. We share experiences and bounce ideas off leading corporate minds in the Washington, DC area. Amid today’s media deluge and formulaic programming, Business Her Way knows its target audience (women in business or who want to be) are hungry for the connections and success stories offered here. Drawing on decades in the local business and radio community, this program and its web site explore all facets of finance and life important to women now in the workplace. We keep it local (even in this era of Internet and global reach, most business decisions are made within 10 miles of the office) concentrating on Washington-area companies and people, without neglecting those voices on the national and international scene. You'll learn about events taking place in your backyard and about companies and resources that are nearby as well as the global markets available to women entrepreneurs in this 21st century.
