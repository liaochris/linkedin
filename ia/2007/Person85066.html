Thede Loder’s Summary

I am an Internet business and technology geek, currently focused on enabling the "right kind" of consumer-marketer interaction over email.

Not too long ago I was a fellow in Michigan's Socio-Technical Infrastructure for Electronic Transactions (STIET) cross-disciplinary PhD program. While there I managed to get my masters (second try, whew!), research the economics of communication (related to spam), produce a few peer-reviewed papers ("An Economic Answer to Unsolicited Communication"), and study incentive structure aka "mechanism design" in multi-agent systems.

In the 90s I was involved in the early consumer Internet, starting my stint in Silicon Valley as the 12th employee at match.com. At match we built out of necessity what later became known as an application server (in Perl, if you can imagine). A few of us left to start our own company, Leverage, to commercialize app servers. At Leverage as CTO I designed and built a Java-based app server, and for a few years we licensed it and provided professional services to our clients. As CEO, I helped find us a new home as part of IT-Stategy firm Diamond Technology Consultants (NASDAQ: DTPI). At Diamond I was responsible for technical architecture, project team management, and recruiting.

In my spare time I am like to read, travel, spend time with friends, hike, think about economically-minded public policy and how to help our educational system, keep up to date in information-economics, and mountain bike.

You can reach me via email -- use thede@boxbe.com

Cheers,
Thede
