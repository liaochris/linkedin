Lisa Dale Miller, MFT’s Experience

  • Marriage and Family Therapist

    Self-employed

    (Self-Employed; Myself Only; Mental Health Care industry)

    November 2004 — Present (3 years 2 months)

    Mindfulness-based psychotherapy increases well-being and decreases mental, emotional, and interpersonal distress by cultivating a calm mind and an open, loving heart. This approach is psychodynamic and cognitive behavioral, and uses mindfulness practices to awaken profound insight into oneself and one's life. These complementary modalities promote change by reducing stress and increasing self-awareness, compassion, and peace of mind. I work with individuals, couples, and adolescents. I also work with cancer survivors in all stages of recovery. In addition to private practice I teach Mindfulness-Based Stress Reduction (MBSR), Mindfulness-Based Cognitive Therapy for Depression Relapse Prevention (MBCT) and have created a DBT program which integrates mindfulness meditation practices with DBT’s cognitive-behavioral emotion regulation skills. I also offer 2-hour mindfulness experiences to cancer support groups at local area hospitals.

  • BHE Instructor Mind/Body Wellness

    Kaiser Permanente

    (Privately Held; 10,001 or more employees; Mental Health Care industry)

    April 2004 — Present (3 years 9 months)

    In addition to private practice I am also on staff in the Mind/Body Wellness Department in Psychiatry at Kaiser Permanente Santa Clara, CA. At Kaiser I teach these Behavioral Health Education classes: 8-week Depression, Depression Overview, 8-week Stress Management, 6 week Couples Communication, and 8-week Mindfulness-Based Stress Reduction (MBSR).

  • Visual Artist

    Self-employed

    (Self-Employed; Fine Art industry)

    1985 — Present (22 years)

    Lisa Dale Miller produces artwork on social, political, and spiritual issues. Primarily a painter, Lisa also combines painting with installation, performance art, and video art. Lisa moved to Los Gatos, CA from Tokyo, Japan. While in Japan, she completed two solo shows, including her 1993 Family Values paintings and lecture tour, sponsored by USIA at the American Centers in Tokyo, Osaka, and Fukuoka. Lisa participated in four group shows, and was the first “foreign” artist to exhibit in the Fukuoka Biennial at the Fukuoka Museum of Contemporary Art. Lisa has also exhibited in New Mexico, San Francisco, Sacramento, Mountain View, CA, the San Jose Institute of Contemporary Art, the Santa Cruz Museum of Art & History and Triton Museum of Art. In 2000, Ms. Miller completed a two-month arts project in Kosovo for CRS.
