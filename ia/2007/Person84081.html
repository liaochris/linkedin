Tamara R Pearlman, CPC’s Summary

I have been working in both tangible and in-tangibles sales all of my life. I have been an Executive Recruiter for the past 14+ years, with my forte for most of those years in building practices for the former Big 6, IBM and other Tier 2 and 3 firms. We have been primarily technology focused, with a strong emphasis in placement of Executives and Sales personnel.

Technology & Executive Resourcing expertise in building practices for firms such as Pricewaterhouse Coopers, Accenture, Arthur Andersen, E&Y, KPMG, IBM and Medtronics Aided many pre-IPO go public. Executive Placement on all levels, BDMs and Sales personnel in a variety of industries. Further background in Off Shore, BPO, CRM, ERP, EC, BI, and more.

I provided a number of corporations with training in Process Refinement in the recruitment process. In-house recruiters and hiring managers are so bogged down with their work load that they are unable to respond in a timely manner to candidates in the process. The best way to hire a candidate is to streamline the process so that your firm is assured of hiring the best and the brightest in the market place. We at Endeavor Group can aid you in doing this!

Technology & Executive Resourcing

Endeavor Group, is a Texas based recruiting firm, which was founded in 1999, and is still growing strong. Endeavor Group MD is our newest division, which focuses on Medical Recruiting, and we are very excited about this latest addition to our vast array of services. We also provide contract services in both the IT Recruiting, SCM Financials and Medical Recruiting divisions. I look at every facet of business on your behalf.

I welcome opportunities to network with Linked-in members from firms I have represented, and peers in my field. I love meeting new people, making new friends and connecting both professionally and personally. I am a firm believer that our network can never be strong enough.

  • Founder and Director of IT Recruiting Division

    Noll Human Resource Services

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Staffing and Recruiting industry)

    July 1995 — June 1999 (4 years)

    Recruited by the founder of Noll Human Resource Services to found their Dallas office. I came on board to build their IT Division from the bottom up. I was charged with building the database, new business development, recruiting and training recruiters for the IT Division, while working a desk managing both candidates and clients.
    This was a great opportunity for me and I will be forever grateful for the ability to expand my horizons via this firm in the recruitment arena. I worked with both the internal recruiters, as well as networked with key external recruiters, to keep multiples revenue streams flowing into the Dallas office. I learned to work in logistics and medical recruiting arena, while there which has been invaluable to me.
    After almost five years of commuting 50 miles round trip in Dallas traffic, I decided it was time to work at home, while raising a young son by myself. I bid Noll Human Resource Services farewell and began the next chapter in my life as a recruiter.

While CTG was not the firm for me, I did find a new love, and that was recruiting. And that is how I stumbled into my 4th career!
