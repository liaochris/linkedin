Igor Clark’s Summary

Although it doesn't really feel like it, I've been a professional computer person for more than 10 years. Saw the Internet and a Mac in 1994 and just thought "I'm going where that stuff's going".

I like to know how things hang together, so I spent a while with my hands covered in Internet and computers coming out of my ears until I was able to start making my own versions of what I saw out there, and before long I moved on to stuff I made from scratch.

Some of the systems I've worked on include e-commerce and financial applications, content and document management, web-based email, reporting and data reconciliation tools, and a streaming client/server application. That last one was certainly one of the most unusual: a web front-end to an art installation featuring hungry AI creatures following real-time gas trails to find the high-volume stock trades they liked to eat. Nice.

In February 2005 I took on a permanent, full-time role as Technical Development Director at POKE London, which is my current position, and one which means I get to work with some really sound people on good-looking projects sprouting from creatively interesting ideas. I like that.

Igor Clark’s Specialties:

Internet/Web applications architecture, development & management. Particular interest in ORM, Unix, web applications and increasingly systems administration/configuration management.

Specifics? Well, started out in C with Faircom binary DB; moved on through Perl, PHP, Java, even Cold Fusion (hmm); MySQL, Oracle, etc. Probably had most fun with WebObjects; raw PHP/SQL after that made me feel queasy enough to write my own lightweight PHP/ORM layer. Oh, you know, Unix, shell. Apache. nginx. Etc.
