Thomas Chevrier’s Summary

There are over 400 Executive MBA students linkedin to my network (Xp 72-77, Axp 2-6, and Exp 8-12). They are the absolute cream of the crop.

Harry Markowitz (Journal of Finance, 1952) resolved what he calls the "second stage" of the portfolio selection problem, and earned the 1990 Memorial Nobel Prize in Economics. I am working on the resolution of the first stage, using Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) simulation methods. You can find an abstract of my paper on my web page, http://home.uchicago.edu/%7Echevrier/research.htm. I posted an early draft of my paper, which I will be updating soon. In a nutshell, I show how using economically motivated prior beliefs, you can outperform other traditional methods.

My committee accepted my dissertation proposal:
George Constantinides, Chaired Professor at ChicagoGSB
Ravi Jagannathan, Distinguished Chaired Professor at Kellogg
Rob McCulloch, Chaired Professor at ChicagoGSB, Committee Chairman
Lubos Pastor, Professor at ChicagoGSB

Any research ideas are always welcome. Drop me an email.

Feel free to visit my web page for more information and updates, including lots of nice jokes. http://home.uchicago.edu/~chevrier
