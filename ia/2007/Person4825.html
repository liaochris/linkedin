Amy Webb (amy@webbmediagroup.com)’s Summary

I have more than a decade of experience working with electronic content products and have developed software, websites, podcasts and more for use in journalism and in higher education. I've worked as consultant for newspaper, magazine and broadcast companies, museums, foundations and more. I founded two successful publications, a digital design group and consulting practice. I have also been a featured speaker at digital media and journalism conferences and events throughout North America.

My Specialties:

• Multiple-channel digital communications and channel diversification (web, Podcasting, blogs, syndication, e-newsletters)
• Leading strategic planning to launch publications or relaunch websites
• Monetizing web traffic to increase revenue and ROI
• Search engine optimization regardless of site content
• Leading development and design teams to implement new web applications
• Providing strategic analysis of a site’s effectiveness for newspaper, magazine, broadcast and other non-media organizations.
• Increasing traffic in highly competitive segments
• Identifying content management systems or working with a development team to create an in-house system.
• Increasing visitor stickiness and increasing unique visitor traffic

Awards for my work at Dragonfire:

• Finalist for Best Online Magazine, Webby Awards
• Finalist for Best Rich Media Site Editor & Publisher EPpy Awards
• Finalist for Best Food Journalism on the Internet IACP Bert Greene Awards
• Silver tropies for Best Internet Magazine and for Best Internet News, W³ Awards.

I split my time between Philadelphia and Baltimore and maintain a personal site: http://www.mydigimedia.com. Email: amy@webbmediagroup.com. Complete Resume: http://mydigimedia.com/images/AmyWebbResume.pdf.

    (Non-Profit; 11-50 employees; Online Media industry)

    January 2005 — January 2007 (2 years 1 month)

    I am the founder and current publisher of Dragonfire (http://www.dfire.org), an online magazine of news and culture. Under my direction, we went from a one-person office to a staff of more than 25. Our traffic spiked from 4,000 unique weekly visitors in our first few issues to more than 80,000 uniques every day from around the world.

    I established Dragonfire as a nonprofit and have spent the past two years creating relationships with external stakeholders to ensure a properly-funded budget for my organization. Everyone on the Dragonfire staff works collaboratively: Editors develop stories alongside programmers, freelance journalists are cross-trained on audio production, staff reporters have all learned how to code in Flash. I believe very strongly that the development of a converged media organization depends on cross-training as well as cooperation.
