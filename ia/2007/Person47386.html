Kent Cooper’s Summary

Kent Cooper is a marketing, sales and communications executive with almost 25 years of global leadership on three continents. He has been branding, positioning and selling products and services for leaders like AT&T, IBM, Procter & Gamble, Coca-Cola, British Airways, Siebel Systems and other global leaders his entire career.

He was one of the original leaders who helped establish IBM’s Business Consulting Services, the organization formed with the acquisition of PriceWaterhouseCoopers Consulting. As a partner and the unit's lead sales executive in EMEA, he was a key contributor to integration of this acquisition. Mr. Cooper led this multi-billion-dollar P&L, with a field of 30K employees, to record-breaking quarters.

Prior to joining IBM, Mr. Cooper was a top-performing partner for Siebel Systems in Europe, where he sold and implemented consulting services into Microsoft, Dell and IBM. His IBM team at Siebel consistently produced the highest level of customer satisfaction of any Siebel customer worldwide, as well as the highest profitability.

Mr. Cooper started his communications career in Washington, D.C. on Capitol Hill and later for a PBS television station. He then joined Burson-Marsteller and began cutting his B2C and B2B marcomm teeth on such diverse client campaigns as AT&T's 1984 Olympic Torch Relay (the first), the global recall of the Dalkon Shield, the introductions of New Coke/Classic Coke, and dozens of other high-stakes fights for market and mind share.

He eventually managed several of the firm's most profitable accounts (from Washington and from Los Angeles) before assuming senior leadership responsibilities in the Asia-Pacific region (from Bangkok).

He is a graduate of Memphis State University and has completed coursework in the Executive MBA program at Copenhagen Business School (Denmark).
