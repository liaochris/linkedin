Hilde Roothart’s Summary

Hilde Roothart (1966) studied fashion design at the Academy of Arts in Utrecht. She gained experience as a stylist at the studios of Dominique Peclers and Lidewij Edelkoort in Paris. Back in Holland she worked at the styling and design studios of Günther Frank and Milou Ket. In 1996 Hilde Roothart established Trendslator, a consultancy office that translates trends into strategies and concepts. She writes about trends for several magazines and is a regular speaker for seminars and meetings on trends and innovation.

Trendslator offers a way of thinking for the development of new and updated strategies and concepts. Social changes and trends in consumers’ behaviour are translated into marketing focused proposals, combining analytic and creative methods. The office creates and uses trend instruments and translation methods. Trendslator develops trend reports and concept proposals and tunes the identity of organisations to the mentality of consumers. Clients include companies in financial services, housing and project development, telecommunication and mobile networks, tourism, interior decoration and accessories.
