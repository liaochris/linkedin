Nadejda Loumbeva’s Summary

More than 9 years of experience living/studying/working in international environments.

Experience:

intra-organisational networks, change management, knowledge management and communities of practice, knowledge management using communication technologies, discussion forum design and development, usability testing/human-computer interaction, online community facilitation, bank information systems, corporate social responsibility experience acquired through volunteer work, social entrepreneurship/entrepreneurship experience (Net Impact Geneva), social enterprise business planning (plan entered in Global Social Venture Competition)

Interests:

I am interested in how knowledge management and communities of practice can improve all organisations, partnerships and other types of ventures. Knowledge is at the heart of all effective work processes and so it should be optimised for organisations to best possibly deliver on their goals. This is especially so in the context of organisations, partnerships and ventures that are humanitarian and aim to bring social and environmental sustainability -> sustainable development.

I am also interested in how to design, create and align organisations for corporate social responsibility and cross-sector partnerships. This would be at the levels of strategy, investment decisions, structure, operations and last but not least corporate culture. What makes partnerships 'tick' and how they deliver on their promises and social value 'on the ground'. What changes do all organisations (corporations, small businesses, IOs and NGOs) have to go through in order to bring and maintain sustainability and sustainable development. What is the role of social entrepreneurs and entrepreneurship in this picture. How to make socially responsible investment an integral part of what organisations do.
