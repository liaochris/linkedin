Luis E. (Lucho) Osorio-Cortes’s Summary

I have the privilege to be the International Coordinator of Practical Action’s Markets and Livelihoods Program. The program is currently working in Bangladesh, Nepal, Peru, Sri Lanka, Sudan and Zimbabwe. I put most of my energies to improve the quality, efficiency and effectiveness of the teams’ work in the field. My roles involve supporting the teams in project design and monitoring, and learning facilitation. I recently coordinated a cross-country learning project about the applications of Participatory Market Chain Analysis and am currently managing the project ‘Becoming an Effective Learning Organization’, funded by USAID and administered by Pact Inc. I am currently the focal point of the Markets and Livelihoods Group of the Livelihoods Network. I moved from Chemical Engineering into appropriate technologies in 1995 and then worked for seven years in the Colombian NGO Fundacion Social in the field of International Cooperation. During that period I acquired experience about participatory local development processes and project design, monitoring and assessment. I have been a fellow of the W.K. Kellogg Foundation in two different programs; have a degree in International Cooperation and Development Projects and an M.Phil. in Development Studies from the Institute of Development Studies (Uni of Sussex) where I focused mainly on value chains, innovation systems and social networks.
