TJ Downes’s Summary

I have a passion for the Internet. It is constantly changing and evolving, and the possibilities are only limited by your imagination.

I feel in order to be successful in this industry it is necessary to be as flexible as the very technologies we work with. If you have 20 years or 1 year it doesn't matter, as long as you apply a few basic principles:

1. Learn from the mistakes of others around you
2. Listen to those who have experience in the industry
3. Listen to those who may have less experience. A fresh perspective could be invaluable.
4. Trust your instinct
5. Have fun with your work
6. Work hard, but make time for yourself.

I do not feel that resumes are an adequate way to communicate just how experienced a person is. It is all too common to have a long list of acronyms profiling those technologies one "has experience with". The real proof comes with time.

My focus has and always will be making the client happy. First and foremost is customer service. No matter how much you excel at what you do it does not matter if you cannot communicate with the client and make them feel like they are getting exactly what they want.

Secondly, focus on what benefit you bring to the client's business. Does what you are developing really bring value? How can you truly enhance the overall solution? Bells and whistles are nice, but will it really benefit the client's bottom line? To establish a long-term and trusted relationship you must produce solutions that have a visible and long-term affect on the clients work flow and, hopefully, profit margin.

Lastly, but not least important, focus on the things which enhance the long--term viability of the solution: usability, reliability, security and maintainability. By using industry-standard practices and watching industry trends you can develop solutions which will grow with the client's business.
