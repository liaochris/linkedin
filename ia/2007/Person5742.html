Andrew Soderberg’s Summary

Over 27 years experience in marketing and product development across several business markets; including the Internet, consumer electronics, personal computers and peripherals, and broadcast television. Successful product development experience managing Windows, Macintosh, and web based products and services.

At Atari, Inc, I was involved in the development of the first computer and laserdisc-based interactive information kiosks used in retail. I was also the Production Manager for Atari's television commercials which won several Clio Awards. At ViMart Corp., I further developed and marketed interactive kiosks to sell computer software at Sears and K-Marts stores nationwide. As Director of Marketing, at Aapps Corp., I was the first to market digital video products for the Macintosh, before Apple’s QuickTime was developed.

I joined Apple Computer in 1990, as their Evangelist responsible for the adoption of QuickTime and Firewire technologies by third party developers. During my tenure at Apple, I was instrumental in the creation of many strategic partnerships as well as enlisting over 100 companies worldwide to develop new products using QuickTime. As Apple’s Advanced Technology CPU Evangelist, I was one of the five-member team that managed the development of the 660 & 840 AV Macintosh. I am co-author of the book Desktop Video Studio published by Random House Electronic Publishing in June of 1995.

As a pioneer of multimedia – from my roots as a director in broadcast television, to my role on Apple's QuickTime development team, and as President of Merlin Media – I have built a career and now a business based upon blending my experience in computer, video, and Internet technologies, providing new-media solutions for corporate and institutional clients.

I recently relocated to the La Crosse, Wisconsin area to work closer with our strategic partner, Interact Communications, providing web services and solutions to the national community college market.
