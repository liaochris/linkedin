Kristina Pike’s Summary

I accept industry-relevant invitations to convextech@windstream.net; please make sure your request is detailed and specific as to your intentions.

My specialty is total-concept digitization and publishing, including document translation from multiple formats, conversion to and from SGML/XML, technical editing, and composition. I have experience with most software and scripting languages related to this field.

PROJECT SUMMARY:

*Round-trip conversion of Army manuals from Microsoft Word to XML and back. Converted unstyled Word documents based on ARTEPXML00CT and SMXML00STP DTDs.

*Conversion of a 4,000-page U.S. Air Force IPB from unstructured FrameMaker to SGML IAW 38807C-AV6aD1P0 under extremely tight deadline. Edited XSLT and XML configuration files, developed automated process, solved book-wide consistency issues.

*SGML validation, Quality Assurance and Program Management support for conversion of U.S. Marine Corps technical manuals in PDF format through SGML to XML/IETM. Assist in design of automated translation techniques, analysis of NAVSEAC2 DTD and modification of USMCV1 DTD to new USMCLAV-V1 DTD.

*Digitization of legacy U.S. Air Force technical manuals in PDF format, including editorial markup and OCR processing, through structured FrameMaker and IPDF conforming to MIL-STD-38784 and MIL-PRF-38807, from start to finish.

*Validation of Calvert Lesson Manuals in XML; split lessons into entities via scripting; perform composition on client’s remote 3B2 server; analyze output and report inconsistencies.

*Conversion of U.S. Patent Office documentation through validated XML and client-proprietary dot-matrix printer code output. Design and maintenance of automated TIF-to-XML conversion techniques.

*Cross-platform translation from Adobe PageMaker for Macintosh to Adobe FrameMaker for Windows. Repair and restore corrupted files, verify technical data crosses platforms intact and perform page layout services where necessary.
