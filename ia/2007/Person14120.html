Caleb Stegall’s Summary

Caleb Stegall is a lifelong resident of Douglas and Jefferson counties in northeast Kansas. He is a country lawyer, writer, conservationist, public interest advocate, and part-time farmer. He lives with his wife and their five sons on their farm in Perry, Kansas.

Caleb Stegall’s Specialties:

Kansas law, civil and appellate litigation, small business law, public policy, public interest advocacy, conservation, American Protestantism, American regionalism, conservatism, agrarianism, localism

  • Co-Founder & Editor-in-Chief

    (Non-Profit; 1-10 employees; Publishing industry)

    Editor-in-Chief of nationally renown online journal, The New Pantagruel. From Wikipedia: "tNP investigated various social and political topics such as localism, agrarianism, and anarchism, e.g., Christian anarchism and anarcho-capitalism. ... tNP promoted a catholic religious sensibility that implied a set of values and habits favoring commitment to land, family, local communities and their economies. A recurrent theme in tNP ... was to contrast the depth and wealth of Classical and Catholic culture with the superficiality and poverty of the contemporary West, particularly within the varieties of Anglo-American (and primarily conservative) Protestantism. Western Christianity in general tended to be seen as hollowed out and, if not liberalized on a theological level, neutered by tacit acceptance of the mundane habits, assumptions, and reflexes of life in wealthy, technocratic, advanced capitalist societies."
