Dan Ferguson’s Summary

Dan is one of the founders of Blockdot, a leading producer and provider of casual games and company sponsored Advergames across multiple platforms, including the Internet, print, handheld and mobile devices. Dan is considered by many to be the forefathers of Advergaming having co-created the indie game Elf Bowling, the first Advergame to reach over 7.5 million installs in one month. Since then Blockdot has created over 700 games for a broad-range of leading brand name companies, including American Airlines, AT&T, Baskin-Robbins, Kimberly-Clark, Kraft, Motorola, M&Ms Brand, Nokia, Pepsi, Post Cereal, T.G.I. Friday’s, Universal Pictures and Verizon. To date, Blockdot games have been played over half a billion times and are responsible for nearly 100 million hours of lost productivity. He apologizes.

In 2005, Dan and his business partners sold Blockdot to Media General, Inc., a publicly owned communications company situated primarily in the Southeast with interests in newspapers, television stations and interactive media. Media General is based in Richmond Virginia.

Dan continues to work for Blockdot where he runs the game group and helps drive the ideas that keep Blockdot on the leading edge of online marketing.

Millions of people play games built by Blockdot on their gaming portals www.kewlbox.com and www.boxerjam.com.
