Lars Toomre’s Experience

Managing Director

Toomre Capital Markets LLC

(Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Financial Services industry)

January 2002 – Present (5 years 1 month)

Enterprise Risk Management (“ERM”) and financial engineering consultant advising firms such as AMD, S&P, Streambase Systems, Harrison Group and Kidd & Company on risk management, capital markets issues, CDOs/structured finance, technology implementation, reinsurance and Sarbanes-Oxley matters.

Managing Director

American Re Capital Markets

(Public Company; 5001-10,000 employees; Capital Markets industry)

September 1997 – 2001 (4 years)

Enterprise risk coordinator focused on the integration of traditional property/casualty (P&C) insurance risks with capital market exposures (including fixed-income, equity, foreign-exchange, commodity, energy and weather exposures) using actuarial and capital markets pricing methodologies. Also started and led weather derivatives trading business and created ‘generation risk facility’ for electric utilities/energy concerns to cover losses due to combination of generation outage, weather and electricity price spikes

Director - Captial Markets

Coopers & Lybrand L.L.P.

(Partnership; 5001-10,000 employees; Capital Markets industry)

June 1996 – August 1997 (1 year 3 months)

Firm subject-matter expert on asset-backed securities, securitization, asset origination, investment management and capital markets issues. Advised wide variety and range of clients on asset-backed securities and the process of securitization; including asset origination, structuring, servicing, accounting, valuation and retained interests for ABS, MBS, CMOs, CBOs, CLOs and CDOs. Provided independent valuation of a variety of investments, tranches, loans and leases.

Managing Director

Smith Barney

(Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; Financial Services industry)

May 1989 – 1991 (2 years)

Head of 48-person mortgage and asset-backed securities trading department. Responsible for all trading activities with institutional, middle-market and retail customers, mortgage-backed research group, mortgage information systems team and trade support personnel

Senior Vice President

Lehman Brothers

(Financial Services industry)

1982 – 1989 (7 years)

Manager and head trader of CMO/ABS business. 1988 net revenues exceeded $38 million. Staff of 12. Started trading short average-life CMO bond classes and U.S. Agency securities in 1984. Earlier, first Associate in Mortgage Research area; started as Analyst supporting computer modeling needs of investment banking, capital markets and asset management divisions.
• Led Lehman Brothers to be recognized as the leader in CMO trading and bond structure innovation.
• Created structuring and tax analysis software for first arbitrage CMO: $500mm Investors GNMA Series 1983-1.
• Introduced first floating-rate CMO bonds and residuals. Also created first CMO backed by project loans.
• Marketed and lead-managed first Federal ABS: a $2+ billion transaction backed by non-investment grade loans, including first-ever public, subordinated debt offering from an ABS deal, and sold with no government guarante
