marcus collins’s Summary

Work is art.

My "curriculum vanitas" has grown pretty long in the past decade, with intense involvement in information systems design, development, and administration. My experience has both broadened and deepened, and my interests likewise have widened and intensified, as i've developed my art in diverse industries in information technology, media, health, education, research, and other service industries. My primary technical contributions to these fields have been in web application development, database design and implementation, and systems administration. Currently, my focus lies in the architecture and administration of scalable systems -- software systems and the growing community of people involved in those systems.

Art is hard work.

Along the way, i've augmented my professional work with further academic studies in computer science, psychology, education, and linguistics, and i've sought to apply and extend myself and my art in mentoring, writing, and learning, not letting any experience go unexamined as it has cast its shadow or ray of light on my canvas.

Work -- art -- has meaning and gives meaning

i've been privileged to work, and to work with great people on great projects. Much of the art of work lies in understanding and contributing to the mosaic of sundry talents, personalities, projects, ideas, opportunities, and so forth with which i come into contact, and contributing in such a way that the mosaic is the better for the contribution.
