Robert Lipman’s Summary

Currently doing research related to product data models and interoperability for the steel construction industry. Previous experience involves visualization and finite element analysis for a wide variety of problems.

Robert Lipman’s Specialties:

Product data standards and modeling: CIS/2, IFC, BIM, STEP
Knowledge of steel construction industry
Computer graphics and animation: VRML, X3D
Computer programming: Tcl, Java

Robert Lipman’s Experience

  • Senior Research Engineer

    NIST (Gaithersburg, MD)

    (Government Agency; 1001-5000 employees; Civil Engineering industry)

    February 1994 — Present (13 years 9 months)

    Research related to product data standards for the steel construction and building industry to make software systems more interoperable. Expertise in CIS/2 (CIMsteel Integration Standards), IFC (Industry Foundation Classes), BIM (Building Information Modeling), VRML (Virtual Reality Modeling Language), and Tcl programming. Participation in the NBIMS Development Team.

  • Structural Engineer

    Naval Surface Warfare Center, Carderock Division (Bethesda, MD)

    (Government Agency; 1001-5000 employees; Shipbuilding industry)

    July 1981 — February 1994 (12 years 8 months)

    Formerly known as the David Taylor Model Basin (DTMB), the David Taylor Research Center (DTRC), and the Naval Ship Research and Development Center (NSRDC).

    Performed finite element analysis for underwater acoustics, vibrations, underwater explosions, and magnetostatics. Developed facility for scientific visualization of finite element analysis results.

    Group leader of the Computational Signatures and Structures Group from 1992-1994.
