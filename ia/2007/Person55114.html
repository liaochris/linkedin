Mark Koffsky’s Summary

Chief corporate intellectual property attorney with 12 years of experience in all aspects of intellectual property law including procurement, licensing, litigation and counseling. Background includes in-house, law firm and government positions.

Mark Koffsky’s Specialties:

Patents, trademarks, copyrights

Mark Koffsky’s Experience

  • Deputy General Counsel for Intellectual Property

    SMSC

    (Public Company; 501-1000 employees; SMSC; Semiconductors industry)

    March 2006 — Present (1 year 10 months)

    As SMSC’s chief IP lawyer, manage worldwide patent and trademark portfolio for semiconductor business units in personal computers, USB and embedded networking devices, and automotive infotainment systems. Draft and negotiate IP licenses to support business objectives. Manage offensive and defensive IP investigations and litigations. Institute company-wide IP training and promote IP awareness for engineering and marketing personnel.

  • Associate

    Goodwin Procter

    (Partnership; 501-1000 employees; Law Practice industry)

    July 2003 — March 2006 (2 years 9 months)

    Advised clients on prosecution, litigation, and licensing-related intellectual property issues in a variety of arts, including radio frequency identification (RFID), pharmaceuticals, and camera control systems. Responsible for all phases of patent litigation from complaint through appeal.

  • Corporate Counsel

    Symbol Technologies

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; SBL; Computer Hardware industry)

    March 2000 — July 2003 (3 years 5 months)

    Managed successful Symbol v. Lemelson bar code litigation. Implemented patent prosecution, litigation, and licensing strategies for wireless business unit. Prosecuted a docket of over 50 patent applications relating to wireless local area network technology.

  • Associate

    Kenyon & Kenyon

    (Partnership; 201-500 employees; Law Practice industry)

    November 1997 — March 2000 (2 years 5 months)

    Participated in all phases of patent trial litigation for prevailing party Faulding in Purdue v. Faulding once-a-day cancer pain drug litigation. Prosecuted patent applications in the fields of computer software, telecommunications, and computer architecture. Advised clients on the licensing of intellectual property rights in the fields of electrophotography and dentistry.

  • Attorney Advisor, Office of the Chairman, Policy Planning

    Federal Trade Commission

    (Government Agency; 501-1000 employees; Government Administration industry)

    August 1996 — August 1997 (1 year 1 month)

    Developed intellectual property policy initiatives for the Commission. Participated in an inter-agency U.S. Government task force on Internet issues.

  • Law Clerk, Hon. Raymond C. Clevenger, III

    U.S. Court of Appeals for the Federal Circuit

    (Government Agency; 51-200 employees; Judiciary industry)

    August 1995 — August 1996 (1 year 1 month)
