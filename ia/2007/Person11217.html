Bill Nathan’s Summary

Since 1985 Bill has delivered a 75% award rate in government and private sector grantwriting, with a specialty in complex, high-value, highly competitive federal and state grants. After 21 years as a fundraising executive, he is currently an active member of the Leadership Council at Craigslist Foundation, Board member of North Valley Community Foundation, and was Executive Director at the Fund for Wild Nature, a national grantmaking foundation. His experience on "both sides of the fence" in the world of grants affords him a unique perspective that clients value.

Harvest Grant Consulting addresses the full spectrum of subject matter (health, education, environment, urban housing, transportation, community service, etc.), but is first and foremost a team of highly experienced grantwriting professionals. Our most current focus has been in the public safety/Homeland Security/military sector, where we hold an 85% win rate. Bill serves as a client-based consulting member of California's Bay Area Homeland Security Urban Area Security Initiative (UASI) Area, and is a veteran of the U.S Navy's Trident Submarine Force. He provides program design and funding consultation to clients in the areas of counterterrorism, intelligence, international private security concerns and military special operations. Harvest recently produced a Grants Guidebook for Philips Medical Systems (www.medical.philips.com) that addresses the medical equipment funding needs of law enforcement, fire, EMS and other first responder units. In 2005 Harvest sponsored a well-received California-wide Department of Homeland Security grants conference (www.homelanddefensejournal.com/conf_grantsCA.asp).

Harvest serves a wide variety of public and private sector clients across the U.S. and in an ever-increasing number of foreign countries. Our Principals are based in Northern and Southern California. For more information please visit www.harvestgrant.com or contact Bill at bill@harvestgrant.com.

Bill Nathan’s Specialties:

Government, foundation and corporate grantwriting, contract writing, exhaustive research, program/project design (NDA provided if necessary), grantsmanship training, speaking engagements, multi-level strategic analyses, comprehensive needs assessments, causal models/evaluations, development planning, board development, agency profile-building, other types of technical and subject-matter assistance.
