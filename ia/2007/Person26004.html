Ed Trainor’s Summary

SVP/Chief Information Officer (CIO) and Sr. Adviser, Paramount Pictures (January, 1994 to December, 2006), a $5 billion global entertainment entity with an Information Technology (IT) budget of approx. $100 million and 250 people

• Led large systems integration teams through a variety of mergers and divestures, re-engineering international business processes and integrating data/systems in acquired and/or restructured companies.

• Implemented high-volume supply chain systems with SAP, EAI, EDI and VMI to support $2 billion/year global Home Entertainment business, part of a total team effort that earned Wal*Mart’s coveted “2005 Supplier of the Year” for North America and Target’s “2005 Vendor of the Year” awards.

• Developed Web-based solutions for business intelligence, data warehouse architecture with ad hoc end-user reporting environment, and other best-of-breed ERP solutions (e.g., PeopleSoft, SOPHOI, HwS).

• Planned strategic data/systems architecture for Television to exploit product rights and availability and support double-digit earnings increases.

• Employed selective on-shore and off-shore outsourcing to reduce development and infrastructure costs and improve software delivery.

• Initiated internal staff development programs to identify, invest in, and develop high-potential people for future leadership positions.

• Created Paramount’s first Internet monitoring and anti-piracy interdiction program to protect intellectual property on P2P networks and Web sites.

Ed Trainor’s Specialties:

Expertise includes Web-enabled electronic commerce, high-volume supply chains, enterprise resource planning (ERP) systems, business intelligence (BI), data warehouses (DW), global systems, domestic and international outsourcing, mergers and acquisitions, and cost containment.
