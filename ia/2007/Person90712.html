Wendy Belcher’s Summary

I manage a small ethnic studies press, have edited academic books and journals for over twenty years, teach academic writing workshops for graduate students and faculty members in the United States and internationally, and write about African politics, arts, and literature. I write a free electronic monthly newsletter titled Flourish to encourage scholarly writers and am finalizing a writing workbook for academics.

Wendy Belcher’s Specialties:

academic writing workshops, academic writing, academic editing, academic journals, academic publishing, foreign academics

    UCLA

    (Educational Institution; 5001-10,000 employees; Higher Education industry)

    1996 — Present (11 years)

    I revitalized and manage a historic small press, the UCLA Chicano Studies Research Center Press, where we produce three book series, monographs, a peer-reviewed journal, research reports, newsletters, and policy briefs, including Aztlan: A Journal of Chicano Studies, the journal of record in the field, and the ground-breaking A Ver Latino art book series. I increased earnings ten-fold. I provide advice and consulting to editors of other academic journals.

  • Writer

I have written articles, reports, radio scripts, and multi-image scripts for numerous nonprofit organizations, newspapers, and magazines, including the National Museum of African Art, United States Agency of International Development, Ghana Institute of Linguistics, Jubilee Ministries, Potter’s House Press, Washington Project for the Arts, United States Information Service, Seattle Central Community College Institute of English, the Los Angeles Human Relations Commission, the Center for the Strategic Initiatives of Women, Pacifica Network News, BBC Africa News, The Seattle Times, The Ethiopian Tribune, The Ethiopian Review, LA Weekly, San Francisco Bay Guardian, and others.

  • Lecturer

    UCLA

    (Educational Institution; 1001-5000 employees; Higher Education industry)

    1995 — 2006 (11 years)

    I designed and teach ten-week workshops, one for humanities graduate students and a second one for social science graduate students, to aid them in writing, revising, and publishing academic articles. Course participants have excellent records of publication. Wrote a workbook for the course that is in demand. I also designed and teach workshops on other writing and editing topics, including a six-week course on revising for nonfiction writers, one-day workshop for graduate students on managing an academic journal, a one-day workshop on managing a small press, and three-hour workshops on copyediting nonfiction texts and performing electronic editing. Workshops held at UCLA (various departments and classes).
