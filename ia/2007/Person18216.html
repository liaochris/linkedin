Colin Masson’s Summary

20 years of experience evaluating, implementing and selling manufacturing, enterprise and supply chain software.

5 years researching and advising the global Fortune 500 on manufacturing and supply chain best practices, and on designing software architectures that support demand-driven supply networks and demand-driven manufacturing.

Strategic advisor to major ERP companies such as Oracle and SAP, and major automation vendors such as Invensys and Rockwell Automation on their manufacturing software architectures, product development roadmaps, product marketing, partnering and acquisitions.

Evangelist of Manufacturing 2.0 that..."capitalizes on service-based and collaboration-based architectures to let manufacturers dynamically reconfigure sensor and mobile-worker-enabled supply networks to make products right first time and on demand".

Colin Masson’s Specialties:

Enterprise SOA, Manufacturing SOA, Business Process Management, Enterprise Manufacturing Intelligence, ERP, PLM, SCM, MES, Process Manufacturing

Colin Masson’s Experience

  • Research Director, Manufacturing Operations and Chemical and Process Industries

    AMR Research

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Market Research industry)

    April 2003 — November 2007 (4 years 8 months)

    Colin Masson brings more than 20 years of experience with a strong foundation in real-time manufacturing technologies and applications to his role as research director within AMR Research’s Value Chain Strategies Service. He specializes in operations excellence, demand-driven manufacturing, innovation and supply chain best practices, and supporting architectures and applications. Colin is a trusted advisor to global Fortune 500 companies.

    Colin has leveraged his background aligning manufacturing and corporate IT with his experience architecting real-time enterprise systems to evangelize and define Manufacturing 2.0 as a critical market need that overcomes traditional MES/ERP limitations and capitalizes on emerging service-based and collaboration-based architectures that manufacturers must leverage to make products right first time and on demand.

  • Chief Technology Officer

    Invensys Process Systems

    (Public Company; 51-200 employees; Computer Software industry)

    October 2002 — March 2003 (6 months)

    Responsible for architectural evolution of IPS Production Management products and related integrations. Primary activities include development of Protean/PRISM Roadmaps to support Services Oriented Development and the associated development of new Web Services ‘Smart’ Clients. Also responsible for Business Intelligence, Application to Application (A2A) and Business to Business (B2B) integration architecture. Developed technical architecture for a Software Assembly Environment to support the delivery of Real Time Enterprise solutions by Invensys, and included the assessment of current 'Enterprise Enabling Technologies' of the broader Invensys software portfolio (including Baan, Marcam and Wonderware). Related activities include selection of OEM products (e.g. BPM, Workflow, Integration Broker, BRE/BAM, Portal, IDEs, etc), 3rd party Alliances, development of Marketing Collateral, Press and Analyst Briefings.

  • Technology Marketing Manager

    Marcam

    (Public Company; 201-500 employees; Computer Software industry)

    April 1999 — October 2002 (3 years 7 months)

    Responsible for developing technical requirements for Sales and Marketing of Marcam's Protean ERP Solution, targeted specifically at Pharamaceutical, Chemical, Food and Beverage industries. Reported to VP Marketing, and CTO. Primary activities include selection of OEM products, 3rd party Alliances, development of Marketing Collateral, Press and Analyst Briefings. Responsible for defining scope and architecture of new Products and blending Marcam and Wonderware technologies.

  • Sales Account Manager

    Aspen Technology Inc

    (Public Company; 501-1000 employees; AZPN; Computer Software industry)

    January 1998 — March 1999 (1 year 3 months)

    Responsible for developing Sales and Marketing of AspenTech's range of products and services to the Process Industries in South Africa. Apart from direct Sales, my experience with ERP and Control and Instrumentation vendors was utilized to improve AspenTech's Channels, particularly for their Information Management Products for which there had been no direct Business Support staff in South Africa.

  • IT Director

    TML (Times Media Limited)

    (Public Company; 201-500 employees; Newspapers industry)

    May 1997 — December 1997 (8 months)

    Responsible for IT Strategy and Implementation for the Times Newspapers Division of TML. Initially recruited to transition the Times Newspaper Division from a Sun Solaris environment to a Windows NT Client/Server environment. I Successfully transitioned their premier daily publication, the Business Day, and subsequently the countries largest Sunday newspaper - the Sunday Times - to this new environment. Under Colin's guidance TML successfully moved their pilot foray into Internet Publishing onto a commercial, fully workflow enabled platform (Lotus Domino), which became the busiest independent Internet Site in South Africa (http://www.netassets.co.za).

  • CIO

    National Chemical Products (NCP)

    (Public Company; 501-1000 employees; Chemicals industry)

    September 1993 — April 1997 (3 years 8 months)

    Initially reporting to the Business Development Manager, Colin's primary responsibility was to develop the use of information as an integral component of NCP's turnaround business strategy. NCP's existing IS function was primarily financially focussed, and was considered a hindrance to the Company's performance.
    Colin elevated IT to one of NCP's (and in fact Sentrachem's) strategic issues, and developed and implemented various projects aligned around the concept of Enterprise Engineering, a holistic view of World Class techniques, including elements of Business Process Reengineering, Change Management, and strategic outsourcing. Colin eventually took over as CIO.

  • Manager, Modern Integrated Manufacturing

    Karbochem

    (Public Company; 201-500 employees; Chemicals industry)

    March 1989 — August 1993 (4 years 6 months)

    With his background in Information Systems (IS) and Advanced Process Control (APC), Colin was appointed to Sentrachem's SRMP (Sentrachem's 'Strategic Raw Materials Project' was a multi-million Dollar Feasibility Study of a multi-billion Dollar chemical complex) to develop the scope of work for "Computer Integrated Manufacturing (CIM)". Colin's primary responsibility was to articulate, justify and execute a strategy to ensure that SRMP obtained maximum benefit from the appropriate utilization of computers, information, technology, and new organizational practices and that SRMP would be competitive internationally.

  • Assistant Manager, Process Development Group

    Karbochem

    (Public Company; 201-500 employees; Chemicals industry)

    May 1987 — February 1989 (1 year 10 months)

    The group's primary function was to prepare the economic justification of projects, and submit appropriate documentation for approval by the Sentrachem Board. In addition to working on the development of specific projects, the group was responsible for ensuring that the company kept abreast of technology, and utilized appropriate technology effectively. Colin was responsible for the specification and implementation of the Group's computer network system, the acquisition of technical software and the establishing of access to offsite databases, and process simulation software. Colin's specific areas of technical expertise within the group were the fields of 'advanced process control' and 'process information technology'.

  • Technical Officer, Process Investigations

    AECI

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; Chemicals industry)

    December 1984 — April 1987 (2 years 5 months)

    Colin was recruited out to South Africa as a Permanent Resident, specifically to work on AECI's PVC Computer Replacement Project. This was essentially a re-instrumentation project. The PVC plant is a highly complex batching plant, which cannot be operated without the plant computer. Over 30 man-years of software development had been performed by AECI/ICI, but the hardware was becoming obsolete. AECI selected a replacement system, and rewriting of software was required. Colin made modifications to the software, which enabled AECI to increase the plant capacity 8%, with no hardware investment.
