Guy Eckstine’s Summary

GUY ECKSTINE is a distinguished Music Industry A&R & New Media Executive, Record Producer, Musician and Composer, who has Produced and Executive Produced over 40 records. Four albums that Guy has Produced/Executive Produced have won GRAMMY AWARDS, including, "The New Standard", by HERBIE HANCOCK.

Guy has held A&R positions at Virgin Music, Columbia Records(SonyMusic), and Verve Records (PolyGram), and was also a New Media pioneer, as SVP/Creative Development, at MP3.com.

Guy is currently the Co- Founder/CEO & Owner of MIAA.TV - Music Industry Television- ( a Music Industry Association of America Company). MIAA.TV is the Music News, Entertainment and Information Web Network, scheduled to go online in 2007.

Guy Eckstine’s Specialties:

The Digital Music realm inlcuding, Online Media, A&R, Music Publishing, Artist/Brand Development/Integration, and Web based Televsion Production. I have an extensive Rolodex from A-Z, covering the Film/TV/Music/New Media and Advertising worlds. As a longtime music industry A&R executive, and former protege of Quincy Jones, I also have Produced 4 Grammy winning albums.

  • SVP/Creative Development

    MP3.com

    (Public Company; 201-500 employees; Online Media industry)

    January 1999 — March 2000 (1 year 3 months)

    At the dawn of the new millennium as SVP/Creative Development at the original MP3.com, I was an early advocate of the .com revolution, adding New Media expertise to my executive profile. At that time MP3.com was the leading online music service provider, and I was the Film, Television and Music Business liaison, based in Los Angeles.

    My responsibilities included creating and implementing synergistic, netcentric, marketing plans for MP3.com and its' artists. These marketing plans included the A&R and second stage development for the MP3.com's , "Music and Technology Tour", with Alanis Morrisette, Tori Amos, TLC and Christina Aguilera.

    I was originally hired as Genre Manager for Jazz, World and Classical Music, covering all facets of "genre buildout" and branding for MP3.com artists, including Ezine Publications, Mesageboard Moderation, Online Music Editor, and the complete lexicon of critical music invective.

  • VP A&R

    Verve Music Group (PolyGram)

    (Public Company; 11-50 employees; Music industry)

    July 1991 — August 1997 (6 years 2 months)

    Other accolades include Billboard Magazine and The Gavin Report Label of the year during entire tenure at Verve, (1991-1997). A&R/Creative Direction for PBS Special- Carnegie Hall Salutes the Jazz Masters, (Verve 50th Anniversary TV Special), Featuring, Vanessa Williams, Herbie Hancock, Pat Metheney, John McLaughlin, Antonio Carlos Jobim, etc.
