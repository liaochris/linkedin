Erwin Oliva’s Summary

Since 1998, I have been writing stories about the Philippine information technology and recently the gaming scene. Two years ago, I discovered blogging and been hooked ever since. I also dabbled in medical journalism for 3 years but got tired and moved to writing for a computer trade publication. My typical day starts off with making calls to sources of news, checking news websites, blogs, and e-mail. Then I update several blogs I maintain (mostly non-profit). Since I work for an online medium, my day is quite frantic. I often break stories online, as they happen or when information becomes public.

You can view my daily output at: http://http://services.inquirer.net/express/07/01/17/html_output/index_infotech.htm

Erwin Oliva’s Experience

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Online Media industry)

    October 2000 — Present (6 years 5 months)

    INQUIRER.net is the top Philippine news website that delivers breaking stories as they happen. We have over a million unique users. I write mainly for the tech and gaming section of this online news website, with focus on Philippine issues.

  • reporter

    Metropolitan Computer Times

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Online Media industry)

    April 1998 — October 2000 (2 years 7 months)

    This is the first and oldest but defunct computer trade publication in the Philippines. This is where my break in tech journalism happened. I wrote for its weekly publication, PC Week Philippines, and also defunct Newsbytes.com, a tech wire service run by Washington Post back in the 1990s.

  • medical journalist

    Medical Observer (Mosman Communications Inc.)

    (Privately Held; 201-500 employees; Publishing industry)

    1995 — 1998 (3 years)

    Medical Observer is a magazine written for doctors. It is given for free, which means advertisers are mainly supporting it. The magazine has popularized the soft-side of medical journalism in the Philippines. It also brought patients and doctors closer, as the magazine tried to focus on health issues. It is still being circulated in the Philippines, and has decided to set up a website.
