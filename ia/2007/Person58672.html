Michael Hill’s Summary

Michael P. Hill has a unique and extensive range of experience in newspaper and online journalism, graphic design and Web design and development.

For more information visit www.michaelphill.com.
For contact information, visit www.michaelphill.com/contactinformation

Michael Hill’s Specialties:

Web design, Web development, newspaper journalism, online journalism, graphic design, copy editing, public relations, marketing, Adobe Photoshop, Adobe InDesign

Michael Hill’s Experience

  • Marketing and Web manager

    FX Group

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Broadcast Media industry)

    June 2006 — Present (1 year 8 months)

    Michael joined FX Group in 2006 as a marketing assistant/Web specialist. FX is a broadcast set design, fabrication, installation and lighting design firm in Orlando, Fla. In 2007, he was promoted to marketing and Web manager.

    At FX, Michael works on the company's marketing strategies and materials, which includes developing and designing advertising and marketing campaigns, writing press releases and copywriting. In addition, Michael manages the company's corporate identity and branding.

    Michael also serves as the company's Web manager, responsible for designing and maintaining the company's Web presence. This includes front-end and client-only Web sites, e-mail and Web server administration and e-mail and Web marketing initiatives.

  • President/creative director

    Clearhill

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Graphic Design industry)

    April 2003 — Present (4 years 10 months)

    In 2003, while in college, Michael co-founded Clearhill Creative, a communications company.

    Clearhill specializes in graphic and Web design as well as marketing and advertising work. Michael serves as the creative director and designer, charged with developing and executing all client design work and advertising campaigns, as well as working with printers and other production companies to ensure projects are completed in a quality and timely manner.

    Michael received the Lackawanna County Young Entrepreneur Award for his work with Clearhill.

  • Page designer/graphic designer

    The Ithaca Journal

    (Public Company; 11-50 employees; GCI; Newspapers industry)

    January 2006 — July 2006 (7 months)

    As a member of the newsroom's copy desk, Michael was responsible for editing local and wire copy and page design. He also worked on enterprise story package design and news infographics.

    In addition, Michael researched, wrote, edited and designed "Zero Magazine," a public service publication on underage drinking that was distributed to local schools.

  • Copy editor

    Pocono Record

    (Public Company; 11-50 employees; DJ; Newspapers industry)

    March 2004 — August 2005 (1 year 6 months)

    Michael was selected as an intern with the 2004 Dow Jones Newspaper Fund, one of the nation's most prestigious newspaper journalism internships.

    Initially, Michael spent 10 days at the Pennsylvania State University for an intensive copy editing residency. While there, Michael attended numerous journalism seminars on topics such as Associated Press style, word usage, clarity and ethics.

    After this residency, Michael spent the rest of the summer in the Pocono Record newsroom in Stroudsburg, Pa. A member of the Ottaway Community Newspaper group, the Pocono Record is an established leader in journalism in the Pocono Mountain region.

    During the summer of 2005, Michael returned to the Pocono Record to work on the copy desk.

  • Camera operator

    WNEP-TV

    (Public Company; 51-200 employees; NYT; Broadcast Media industry)

    November 2004 — August 2005 (10 months)

    Michael worked as a camera operator for WNEP-TV, the top-rated ABC affiliate for the Wilkes-Barre/Scranton market.

    As a camera operator, Michael was responsible for running the station's robotic and manual studio cameras during live broadcasts. Michael was also responsible for preparing the studio and outdoor weather studio for live news broadcasts as well as maintaining white balances and video quality.

  • Managing editor, Internet operations

    UofSNow.com

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Online Media industry)

    October 2004 — June 2005 (9 months)

    In 2004, Michael co-founded an independent online newspaper serving the students, staff and faculty of the University of Scranton.

    Michael's initial responsibilities included developing the online newspaper's design and selecting and deploying a content management system which allowed multiple reporters and staff members to submit, edit and publish stories and photographs online without complex coding knowledge.

    Michael continued to maintain the site on a day-to-day basis and was also responsible for developing new features as well as provided training and technical support for both staff members and end-users.

    In addition, Michael also reported and edited copy for the site.

  • Editorial assistant/reporter

    Press Enterprise

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Newspapers industry)

    September 2000 — August 2002 (2 years)

    While still in high school, Michael joined Press Enterprise Inc. as an editorial assistant and reporter in 2000 after spending the summer as an intern in the daily newspaper division.

    During this time, Michael managed the newspaper's 30-member teen writing program, which included planning meetings for the staff and processing incoming articles and writer invoicing and payments. He also developed a Web-based article submission system for the writers.

    Michael worked on the newspaper's electronic archive system, designed pages and served as a general assignment reporter. He covered a wide range of stories, including multiple front page articles. In addition, as interim special editions editor, Michael coordinated the production, writing and design of a "Best of the Area" publication and Fall Home Improvement supplement.

  • Webmaster

    Central Columbia School District

    (Government Agency; 51-200 employees; Education Management industry)

    September 2000 — August 2001 (1 year)

    While still a high school student, Michael was hired as the webmaster for Central Columbia School District.

    His initial duty was a complete redesign of the district's Web site, which included developing an intuitive and easy-to-use navigation scheme. During the school's summer break, Michael developed hundreds of pages of content and deployed a uniform graphical look on all the pages.

    Individual applications including a school lunch menu system and integrated help desk were also developed, with a focus on user-friendliness.

    In addition to the Web site, Michael also designed a viewbook for the district, which included taking many photographs around the district and gathering and editing all the text for the booklet.
