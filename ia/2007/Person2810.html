Albert Wang’s Summary

• As a student at the University of Cincinnati, he majored in Electrical Engineering with a computer engineering option, co-oped at Children's Hospital Medical Center, worked on several interesting biomedical technical projects, and graduated in 1986 with a BSEE.
• After graduation, he quickly landed a civilian position as an electronics/data acquisition engineer, working for the US Air Force at Wright Patterson AFB in a jet engine compressor test facility.
• After his civil service duty, he entered the private sector as an IT support professional for a brief stint with Proctor and Gamble in Cincinnati.
• His strong desire to do technical work led him to Matrix Systems in Centerville, Ohio in 1991, where he worked his way into becoming their technical expert on automated photo-image badge fabrication, Access Control software support, and Access Control software training.
• He began the LexisNexis part of his career in Miamisburg, Ohio in 1996 in Data Fabrication as a software engineer, moving to product development in 1999 as a capacity and performance management engineer, where he applied and developed his technical, analytical and leadership skills into a lead role as a performance/load tester and SAS programmer.
• Following a downsizing event in 2002, Albert has worked steadily as a load testing expert in consulting and direct hire roles for Cincinnati Financial Corporation, Medplus and General Electric in Cincinnati.

Albert has a strong interest in analyzing complex systems, and the information that helps them function efficiently. Albert’s career objective is to find a challenging role in data-based IT business intelligence with an emphasis on software performance quality.
