  • Emerging Technology Entrepreneur

    Self-financed (Sole Proprietorship)

    (Sole Proprietorship; 1-10 employees; Telecommunications industry)

    January 1998 — April 2000 (2 years 4 months)

    Artificial Intelligence: Bayesian signals interception and interpretation engines. Winner of Anvar National Innovation contest (2001). US & European Patents pending, March 17 2005 #US2005/0058261 A1.
    The project started as a prototype that would simulate "implicit knowledge" using a neural network. The initial concept was that a neural network could weigh and remember meaningful links between individuals, the content they exchange and their communication context. Contrary to existing technologies at that time, the idea that human communication could be encapsulated in universal and fixed rules (e.g. "I trash therefore I dislike the sender") was discarded. Instead, the T.A.N. bayesian engine emulates an individual cognitive framework, and results in a "cloned" cognitive framework that hence can predict an individual's interest in an incoming communication, or vice-versa, can predict which individual would be interested in what the individual is typing in real time.
