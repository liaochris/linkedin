Joshua Stein’s Summary

Experience in business, consulting, startups, company management, project management, and more.

With a friend, I started and built from the ground up my first company in eighth grade, and my second in high school. I am the CEO of HyTek and have played an integral part in obtaining and maintaining thousands of worldwide clients. At HyTek, I was directly responsible for generating accumulated revenue in the hundreds of thousands of dollars and estimated recurring six figure revenue. In addition, I have acted as a project manager for two previous summers at General Electric having obtained my first job there as a freshman in college. I then went on to work at PricewaterhouseCoopers under their Advisory practice this past summer.

In my business endeavors, I have been exposed to many functions including but not limited to sales, customer relations, marketing, accounting, technical support, security, risk and regulatory, disputes/analysis/investigations, and governance/risk/compliance. My first summer at GE I worked on the information security team. I started with two assignments and ended up taking on and successfully managing nearly ten (which included leading a security audit and campaign of GE’s global headquarters). My second summer at GE I worked on the treasury systems team. I was an integral team player and had tremendous responsibility documenting existing applications and driving gap analysis which was critical of a company-wide simplification initiative. At PwC, I was a member of the Advisory Technology network. I worked on various projects including a risk and regulatory assessment for a major world bank which required identifying politically exposed persons, an investigations engagement for another big four firm, and more. In every instance, I was critical to each project’s success either as an individual or a team player.
