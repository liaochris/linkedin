Tracy Crossley’s Summary

Cut to the 1970s:

As a mini-mogul I baked cookies, popcorn balls, and anything else I could find; selling them at my corner stand for a profit.

The early 80s:
I was a dot etcher (a deceased trade) at Graphic Arts Systems, moved into production and then sales by the age of 21. My first major client I landed was Jack in The Box. I worked until 1987 and took off a few years, had children and focused on volunteer work in sponsorships and event production.

I have a multitude of experience in sales and marketing with years of outstanding performance in promotions, management, opening new accounts and developing business relationships. I have done it all!!! From grass roots with no budget to budgets in the millions!

I am a runner, the more miles the better! So, it transcends to my professional demeanor--I thrive and excel at motivation, development and inspiring others toward their professional and company goals. It is very rewarding to me to help others reach their potential.

At Cox Auto Trader where I was General Manager, I was responsible for revenue growth in two territories, strategic planning, and overseeing managers and sales personnel.

I was Director of Marketing/Sales at Dimensional Impressions, and owned my own business for several years where I initiated relationships with new clients and trained account executives.

Through my own business, I worked with 20th Centruy Fox and Lawman Jeans for several years.

I created promotional events, such as successful trade show involvement, non-profit fundraisers, several retail consumer events, ie.: modeling contest designed to promote denim line and client involvement in a fashion show at event attended by over 300,000 consumers, public relations for all 5 McCormick and Schmicks in So. Cal and implemention of the premiere party for Rocket Power (cartoon series).

I can keep going, and if you read this far...you deserve a medal for perseverance. Have a good one!
