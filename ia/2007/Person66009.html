Paul Artz’s Education

  • Nova Scotia College of Art and Design

    BFA, Fine Arts and Electronic Media, Social Action, Art History & Education, Philosophy, 1987 — 1989

    I value the time and so much that I learned, did & discovered here as highly as anything in my history. That may not be saying much. On the surface, academically this was a remarkably unremarkable stint greatly frustrated by the storied events of that era & its related distractions which consumed so much of what was, for many of us, our real education. On the whole, while this was surely and in large-part regrettable, much of it has also been an almost invaluable series of lessons.

    Activities and Societies:
    Vice-president for Student Affairs & a multi-partisan, multi-party negotiations Intermediary and student media representative during the great imbroglio, Co-editor & founder of NSCADPUB and ReWire (extra-special credited to, though by one means restricted to Eugene Pope & Philip Bensaid), Stocked shelves at The Red Herring Bookstore, Attended additional classes through the off-campus program at Dalhousie & Mount Saint Vincent Universities

  • Minneapolis College of Art and Design

    Drop-out, Intermedia (new & traditional forms & processes), Philosophy, Cognition studies, Art history, 1978 — 1981

    An extremely uneven period in my extremely uneven life. Entered as a painter with an eye on a day-job in the graphic-arts and/or academia. I left with an radically atomized approach to what constitutes creative endeavor and what is substantial & valuable in it. I should also add that my placement on the Dean's List was immediately proceeded by a semester on academic probation which was itself proceeded by one of considerable accomplishment and productivity. Go figure.

    Activities and Societies:
    Student body president for the fine arts division, Student representative to the administration's academic console, Editor-in-chief for student publications, Dean's list, Attended additional classes through the off-campus program at the University of Minnesota & Macalester
