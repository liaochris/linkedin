Todd Asevedo, SPHR’s Summary

I have devoted my professional life to the HRMS industry specializing in consulting and applied technology. I have been involved through all phases of HRMS from initial automation of manual processes to more strategic projects dealing with Performance Management and Compensation. I have studied the various vendors in the space and understand their strengths and weakness relative to functionality, delivery, sales channels, vision, marketplace, price and total cost of ownership.

After deciding in 1995 to make my job at ADP a career in HRMS I went on to become accredited in as both a Certified Payroll Professional and a Senior Professional in Human Resources. I now split my time between writing within the industry and professional presentations on behalf of various vendors and professional organizations.

My favorite consistent piece of feedback I get from my HR technology presentations is that my passion and entusiasm for HRMS comes through loud and clear. There is no doubt in my mind that a Human Resource professional needs to embrace technology to allow him/her to deliver value to the organization. The value first comes from the time saved through the automation of manual tasks and record keepping. Then, the value flows from the data as it is turned into information (decision support) and a seat at the executive table is opened for the representative from HR!

Todd Asevedo, SPHR’s Experience

  • Managing Partner

    HR Technology Partners

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Computer Software industry)

    July 2006 — Present (1 year 7 months)

    www.hrtechpartners.com

    We are a unique company in that we are essentially a Human Resources Software brokerage. We can sell a customer the software they need because we carry all of the top packages. We are able to do this because of our long term commitment to the HRMS field and our integrity in the relationships we have with each HR Software company. So a customer can go to each of the companies we represent and get into 3 or 4 sales cycles and hear the "pitches" of each of the sales people from the individual vendors or they can come to us, be in one sales cycle and get an unbiased presentation of the software THEY need. The customer doesn't pay us for this. The software company pays us a fee because they don't have to pay a sales person.

    This model works nicely and our customers feel that they have an advocate in a very crowded and confusing marketplace. They also like the fact that it is "free" to them.
