Charles Bill’s Summary

As a consultant and trainer to a corporate and small business clientele, I have provided support for a variety of training programmes. In this capacity, I have on certain occasions managed the complete training project, including needs analysis, training strategy development, training design (both classroom and self directed training approaches as well as the use of e-learning), and the development of training and other support materials. In other cases I have been called on to lead or facilitate events. This experience has also involved developing and coaching client trainers and designing training programmes which they could implement.

Certain projects were born out of the necessity to deal with change created by transformations in the sales, finance or administrative functions, the establishment of shared services centres or the development and introduction of new IT systems and processes.

Clients have included established companies, small and medium enterprises (SMEs) and start-ups, as well as public sector organisations, in Switzerland and in France. A number of projects have required interaction with top management or at board level and working in multi-cultural teams.

Prior to concentrating on client-related training consultancy, I was responsible for setting up and managing three different language training centres in French speaking Switzerland and in France, which involved hiring and training staff, locating facilities and suppliers, launching, marketing and selling the services and putting turnkey solutions in place to facilitate handing the organisation over to a permanent management team.

I am currently responsible for Learning & Development within the Eurofirms Advisory practice and continue to contribute to Knowledge Management and methodology development initiatives.

Charles Bill’s Specialties:

Management development; Facilitation of training events, strategic brainstorming retreats; Delivering soft-skills training; Designing and developing training programmes; conducting needs analyses and stakeholder reviews.
