  • Ph.D.

    The Danish University of Pharmaceutical Sciences

    (Educational Institution; 501-1000 employees; Research industry)

    October 1996 — September 2001 (5 years)

    Antimicrobials are applied in fish farming to treat and prevent infections among the farmed fish.

    Investigations have shown that antimicrobials can be found in the environment as a possible result of antimicrobial treatment in fish farms.

    When antimicrobials are discharged to the evnrionment various processes take place. Biotic and abiotic degradability is often low. Despite their low hydrophobicity some antimicrobials interact with Aldrich humic acids, thus lowering their free concentrations and enhancing their persistence.

    Laboratory experiments have shown that antimicrobials in low concentrations inhibit the growth of algae; especially cyanobacteria.

    Based upon specified exposure scenarios most antimicrobials would upon request require more detailed investigations.

    The study resulted in the Ph.D. thesis: Environmental Risk Assessment of Antimicrobials (ISBN 87-988069-0-4).
