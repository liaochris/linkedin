  • External Innovation Management Team

    LFPI

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Management Consulting industry)

    2006 — April 2006 (less than a year)

    LFPI uses a project management innovation technique called "the kubus concept" developed from practice.

    The concept unfolds in the pre-project phase called "the preject phase" and targets optimazation of project goals and themes through a substantial increase in the companies needed knowledge base - thereby setting the "right" (profitable, cost saving, etc.) projects to be carried out by the company.

    The kubus concept targets what companies and organizations do not know that they do not know. This non-knowledge in companies and organization contributes to sub-optimal solutions and projects on everyday basis. However, the kubus concept identifies the relevant non-knowledge, makes it into knowledge and relevance for the companies - thereby innovating and setting the right projects and project goals given the companies ressources and time frames.
