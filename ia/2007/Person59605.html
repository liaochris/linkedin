Mike Madura’s Summary

Has successfully completed searches for candidates at all levels in the Technical, Information Security, Risk Management, Strategy, Trading, and Sales/Marketing verticals. Major networker with a firm belief in paying-it-forward networking. From a LinkedIn perspective my network is #62 worldwide and #3 in Chicago with respect to the number of level 1 connections (as of 7/2007).

Joined KPMG (1/2007) as a Manager of Recruiting for our Midwest Area Advisory Services practice. Our Midwest area consists of: Chicago, Cincinnati, Cleveland, Columbus, Des Moines, Detroit, Indianapolis, Kansas City, Milwaukee, Minneapolis, Omaha and St. Louis.

Particular emphasis is on building relationships with and sourcing individuals for our IT Advisory Practice.

The primary reason that I joined KPMG is that we have an excellent value proposition for our employees, not only career-wise, but also with an emphasis on professional growth, work/life balance, corporate responsibility and diversity. We have projected 75% growth for our Midwest area by 2010 which is creating many excellent opportunities at all levels.

Recently FORTUNE magazine announced that KPMG was named to the 10th annual “100 Best Companies to Work For” 2007 list. Other KPMG recognitions include being named for the tenth year as one of the best 100 companies for working mothers by Working Mother magazine and Business Week recently published their ranking of the top 50 businesses for college graduates to launch their careers, and KPMG was among those recognized. KPMG has also been recognized by several organizations for its diversity efforts, including the “100 Best Places for Latinos to Work” by Hispanic magazine, DiversityInc magazine's “Top 10 companies for People with Disabilities,” and the Human Rights Campaign Foundation’s annual list of the “Best Places to Work for GLBT Equality.”

Beliefs:

1) the basic elements to a successful business relationship are respect and trust.....
2) listen carefully, since we may answer the question we were expecting to hear and not what the questioner actually expressed.....
3) before we start talking about any potential opportunties you need to clearly and completely express what is important to you.....
4) time kills all deals.....
5) job satisfaction (retention) is driven by 6 key variables - where many companies fall short is by using a cookie cutter approach to retention and not incorporating the fact that the relative importance of these variables is UNIQUE to each employee:
a) type of work being performed..
b) advancement opportunities..
c) availability of professional education..
d) sincerity and effectiveness of company communication (1-way vs. 2-way)..
e) how well the company promotes employee/family interaction both during and after work..
f) compensation/benefits..
