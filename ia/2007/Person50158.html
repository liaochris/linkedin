Laura Lee Dooley’s Summary

Laura Lee Dooley created WRI's first web site in 1996, and continues to raise WRI's bar of excellence by providing oversight, advice and measurement of WRI's online space.

Laura Lee spends much of her time online -- developing online tools and applications, researching and implementing best practices in online communications and measurement, and creating online networks of people and resources. Recognizing that improvements in online communications enhance WRI's work, Dooley seeks opportunities to meet the needs of the growing community of users that seek to turn ideas into action.

Ms. Dooley has been with WRI since 1986, holding positions of growing responsibility. Ms. Dooley has worked wth multimedia communications technologies since the early 1980s. Prior to joining WRI, she worked in the telecommunications law firm of Fly, Shuebruk, Gaguine, Boros, Schulkind and Braun (Washington, DC; 1983-86), at WKPS-FM and cable-TV station WWNW, New Wilmington, PA (1979-1983), and as assistant to Pennsylvania State Legislator Al Rasco (Pittsburgh, PA; 1978).

Laura Lee has been an active participant in:

* NTEN - Nonprofit Technology Network
* Web Analytics Association
* Web Manager's Roundtable
* HTML Writers Guild
* World Organization of Webmasters
* Toastmasters International

In her spare time she serves as a Youth Counselor with the Fairlington United Methodist Youth Group in Alexandria, Virginia.

Laura Lee graduated from Westminster College with a degree in telecommunications and minors in art and religion. Originally from Penn Hills (outside of Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania), she now lives in Arlington, Virginia with her husband, two sons, and two poodles.
