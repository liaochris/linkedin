Scott Clark’s Summary

My crusade seems to come back to helping internet marketing clients focus their energy on what truly works online. Web business development projects and web marketing is full of waste that is based on shaky intuition of inexperienced psuedo-professionals. My advocacy of online testing and consumer-centric design allows companies to get *footing* from which they can build a unique brand identity.

My experience in web marketing goes back to 1983 when I took an independent graduate course that was designed by my instructor as "Computer Art for Advertising" - it was then I discovered Edward Tufte's work, and before I knew it I was working on GUI systems for everything you can imagine. My favorite non-consulting job of all time was at Software Publishing Corporation where I helped develop Harvard Graphics. When I finally met Mr Tufte at Stanford, I was hooked and have been involved in one form of Information Presentation job or another.

The best moment in my work is when I find that special client who gets it. One who really knows that there is no silver bullet - no guru on the mount. My Internet Marketing Firm has dirty fingernails and sweat on its brow. The results are clearly visible - hundreds of percent improvements on critical business indicators.

I'm an avid reader of Seth Godin's books and blog, and consider John Peterman both a friend and one of my strongest influences at present. I've found the passion to think outside the box and to stand out, even when the tide is moving the other way.

I'm a writer for Business Lexington - the main business journal in Central Kentucky, and maintain a regular blog (see link.) I enjoy writing and speaking about topics such as search, internet culture, and consumer patterns. I'm always interested in lively debate over web designs and occasionlly pick up a camera and do some half-decent photography.
