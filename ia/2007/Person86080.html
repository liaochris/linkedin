Tim Waddel’s Summary

After 5 years in education I left teaching to pursue a more varied role in business.

Worked as freelance graphic and multimedia designer, a magazine editor, webdesigner and database programmer before joing Alshamel group full time as Special Projects Manager with responsibilities for IT, Marketing, Branding and Infrastructure development. (The perfect mix of all my skills!)

Over the next 24 months:
• Developed and deployed Alshamel's first network
• Planned and deployed Alshamel Call Centre
• Managed and created the process for major business growth including software design and programming to allow for consolidated reporting and QOS measurement.
• Designed the company's annual Marketing strategy including concept, graphics, trade show stands and brochures
• Became very proficient in writing RFP responses and designing and creating accompanying presentation materials.

Left Alshamel to start up SWITCH - Travel Technology Company - and launch Sabre Travel Network Kuwait.

GOAL: To be constantly challenged - (and get rich!)
OBJECTIVE: To stimulate all facets of my typical Gemini nature and utilize my logical, analytical and artistic skills to improve and enhance the company I work in.

Tim Waddel’s Specialties:

  • Managing Editor

    MBS Publishing - Kuwait This Month

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Publishing industry)

    August 2002 — July 2003 (1 year)

    I attended an interview for Marketing Manager for a magazine that was in trouble and told the Group General Manager that the magazine was unmarketable and needed a redesign and relaunch if it was ever going to become successful.

    I turned down the Marketing Manager role.

    Two weeks later they called and offered me the role of Managing Editor.

    I accepted; with no prior publishing experience other than my freelance graphic design work and producing company brochures and theatre programs.

    Two months later we relaunched a new look Kuwait This Month Magazine and within 12 months it was the most read English Language magazine in the country.

    I left the position after 12 months as the management at MBS Group would not invest in the future potential of the publication and this limited the growth of the project and challenge of my role.
