Erica Janine Henson’s Summary

I started off as a VB3 coder while in college. At the time, the latest cool OCX control was TrueGrid Pro, which I quickly mastered. I figured out I really liked programming and design, so I dove into learning VB6, XML-based service programming, and learning only enough in C++ to be a bit dangerous. Then I started really programming in C# and ASP.NET. So, to a few folks I have only Microsoft-oriented experience. But that's okay, since my love for programming isn't limited by the Giant's stamp.

After a while, I got tired of being a code monkey and longed for a new challenge, to manage and implement true architecture solutions and processes to make existing areas more stable and flexible. I moved to a Project/Process Management position and happened to land a liaison position with an industry-leading group affilliated with my company. So I get the best of both worlds, and occasionally get to go rub shoulders with the smartest people in the world.

My main goal is to be excellent in my profession and to learn as much as I can about new (rehashed old, rather) methodologies and processes. I also strive to make things work better, period. I love the challenge of development, streamlining, integration, and creation. My near-term goal is to become well-versed in Agile principles and work to rearchitect applications currently programmed in near-legacy languages.
