Jason Pearson’s Summary

Jason got his start in New York City as a Creative Director in the music industry creating album and marketing campaigns for David Bowie, Nine Inch Nails, Aphex Twin & a host of people you've never heard of.

In the early days of New York's Silicon Alley, Jason co-founded Blender Magazine with David Cherry. Blender was aquired by Felix Dennis and Dennis Publishing UK. It was the first digital music publication and has been honored in the permanent collection of the Smithsonian Museum and the Victoria & Albert Museum. Blender was named Ad Age's Launch of the Year and produced some of the very first interactive advertising for clients including: Nike, Calvin Klein, Toyota, and Apple. Today, Blender is the market leader in reaching men 18-34.

In the late ninties Jason formed Dennis Interactive, a NYC interactive advertising agency. Clients included: Mercedes Benz, Levi Strauss, Coca-Cola, & Disney. Jason has received design awards including the One Club Award. The website developed for Star Wars was honored with the CA "Entertainment Website of
the Year."

In 2003, Jason formerd Pearpod, a strategic marketing and design. Pearpod serves a unique mixture of clients in the entertainment, technology & nonprofit markets.

In 2006, Jason co-founded FreeChurchPrinting.com to provide low cost, high quality communication tools for churches utilizing an exclusive online design application.

Jason has appeared in numerous periodicals and was named one of New York Magazine's "Cyber Sixty Visionaries." The Samsung Corporation selected Jason as a national spokesperson for the "Samsung Brain Trust" campaign.

Currently Jason lives in a small beach community with his wife, Melinda and their 4 children.
