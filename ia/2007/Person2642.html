Alan Paris’s Summary

Alan L. Paris is a Director in the PricewaterhouseCoopers LLP, Financial Services Advisory Division. Alan has more than twenty years of innovative business, management, and technology experience. Formerly Managing Director and Global Head of Trading Floor Support at Deutsche Bank, Alan has led 200+ person teams in trading floor technology and risk systems implementation, application development, infrastructure implementation, strategic planning, technical and business process design, project management, and vendor management in Fixed Income, Equities, FX, Commodities Energy and Derivatives environments for Global firms and hedge funds. Alan has published articles in Risk/Waters Magazine, Securities Industry News, Wall Street & Technology, and the Journal of Corporate Accounting. He has been interviewed and quoted by The Wall Street Journal, CNN, ComputerWorld, and Smart Money Magazine. He is married with one child and lives in Westchester County, NY.

Mr. Paris’ accomplishments include:
Managed the requirements definition, evaluation and selection process for an Order Management and Trade Execution system for a Major Hedge Fund.
Managed the requirements definition, evaluation and selection process for a trade capture and margining system for Hess.
Led a Global Market Data cost reduction and strategic sourcing effort for Lehman Brothers.
Led a Spreadsheet Re-Engineering effort for BP to improve their critical Sales & Revenue, Forecasting, and Amortization and Depreciation workbooks.
Led a Data Management Quality improvement project for Reuters.
Led an Energy Trading system application re-engineering and transformation program review; technology, governance, data and controls audit for BP.
Performed a Murex derivatives trading system review for FAS 133 functionality and compliance. Created testing strategy, test cases, and test plan for Genworth.
