Paul Allen’s Summary

I am a serial social entrepreneur, trying to build businesses that improve the world and connect and strengthen families.

I am best known for co-founding what is now called The Generations Network (formerly MyFamily.com). We launched the Ancestry.com website in 1996 and the fast-growing MyFamily.com web site in 1998. We started attracting millions of users. We brought in an executive management team that raised $90.5 million in venture capital. I moved from Utah to Silicon Valley during the bubble, and we attempted to take the company public.

We survived the dot com bubble burst but after we became profitable I left in 2002 to start new companies. The company had decided to focus soley on genealogy while I thought the best strategy was to also provide free web sites for every family in the world.

I left the company in February 2002, but have watched the company continue to grow. The company generated approximately $150 million in revenue in 2006.

Like many founders, I did okay with this business, but not as well as I had hoped. As Ray Noorda of Novell fame once quipped, "finders keepers, founders weepers."

I enjoy starting companies that provide valuable content and communication tools that can improve the quality of life. I have been involved in electronic publishing since 1990, mainly in religious, educational and genealogical content.

In 2006 I ran an internet incubator called Provo Labs that developed several startup companies and invested in others.

In 2007 I have chosen to focus all my attention on the most promising company in our portfolio, World Vital Records. We have launched a new social network for family history called FamilyLink.com.

My vision is to help families worldwide to use technology to connect to ancestors and living family members. I am trying to spend a week a month travelling in different countries working with archives, libraries, genealogy companies and publishers, who have significant family history content.
