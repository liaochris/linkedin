Dario de Judicibus’s Summary

From 1980 to 1984 he participated to several High Energy Particles experiments as a member of the Italian Institute of Nuclear Physics. He worked at CERN (Geneva, Switzerland), SLAC (Stanford, California, USA), DESY (Hamburg, Germany), and CNUCE (Pisa, Italy). In 1984 he graduated with full marks in Physics.

He joined IBM in 1986.

In 1991 he began to collaborate with one of the major Italian PC magazines, MC Microcomputer, with articles concerning C programming for AmigaOS.

In 1996 he began to collaborate with one of the major Italian Internet magazines, Internet News, with articles dealing with TCP/IP Protocol Suite. From 1996 to 2006 he published about 200 articles on various subjects.

He also participated as speaker and chairman to several conferences on KM both in Italy and other countries, and wrote several articles on Italian business magazines. In 1999 he published a book about TCP/IP Protocol Suite, edited by Tecniche Nuove (2nd edition, 2002): “TCP/IP in pillole”, ISBN 88-481-1441-5.

In 2000 he began to collaborate with another magazine, e Business News, with a series of articles on Knowledge Management, and published another book dealing with the XML architecture: “Imparare XML in 6 ore”, ISBN 88-481-1098-3.

In 2001 he joined the Strategy & Change Consulting group, and led several KM Projects in various industries (financial, distribution, fashion).

In January 2003 he published a book about pedagogy: “Le 10 Regole dei Buoni Genitori”, ISBN 88-344-1505-1.

At the end of 2004 he began a collaboration with other magazines: Digital and Internet Pro dealing with IT, and Quaderni Radicali dealing with politics and social issues.

On July 2005 he published another psychology book, “Le 10 Regole per Vivere Sereni”, ISBN 88-344-1803-4, and on June 2006 his first fantasy novel, "La Lama Nera", ISBN 88-344-1882-4. Second volume was finished in 2007.

On March 2007 he became the owner of IBM Italia island in Second Life.

Dario de Judicibus’s Specialties:

Pioneer in Knowledge Management in Italy. Senior Consultant and Certified Management Consultant. Know-how: Problem Solving & Decision Taking methodologies, Knowledge Strategy methods, Organizational Change & Business Transformation, Social Networking, Web 2.0 and 3D Internet.

He is also an IBM inventor, since between 1999 and 2001 he patented five inventions: US05874962, US05859640, US6163317, US20010013036A1, US06651054.
