Hannes Seifert’s Summary

My career started in 1987 with my first Commodore 64 game. 1993 I co-founded neo Software, which 2003 became Rockstar Vienna, where I served as managing director for production and as executive producer for 13 years.

My most recent titles include my work on the “Max Payne” and “GTA” series for which I was the responsible producer and executive producer to bring “Max Payne 2” to Xbox and PS2 and “GTA III”, “GTA Vice City”, and “Max Payne” to Xbox.

I worked on over 25 titles on 11 platforms ranging from C64 to Xbox 360 and PS3. These titles include among others “1869”, “Whale’s Voyage”, “The Clue!”, “Prototype”, “Oscar”, “Rent-A-Hero”, and “Amazons and Aliens”.

My academic and public lectures include talks about studio management and growth, about production methods, and about careers in the games industry. I held talks in the United Kingdon, Germany, Austria, and the USA.

Hannes Seifert’s Specialties:

Creative and organizational producing, studio management, intercultural and international communication. Quality, time, and budget estimation, planning, and controlling.

    January 1993 — December 2006 (14 years)

    Until the sudden closure of the Rockstar Vienna studio on May 18th, 2006 I was responsible for production and development. I worked directly for the president of Rockstar Games. I managed 85 of the 102 employees in the Vienna Studio.

    My main role was Executive Producer for all projects developed in Vienna and partly in close cooperation with other Rockstar Studios. Most recently we developed one project for PS2 and Xbox and one project for Xbox 360 and PS3.

    Another task was to evaluate, suggest, and develop synergies between Rockstar Studios. One result of this was my contribution to the game engine that’s now publicly known as “RAGE” and was first used for “Rockstar Games Presents Table Tennis”.

    After the closing of the studio I managed the proper shut down of all operations for several months.
