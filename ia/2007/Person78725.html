Scott Hample’s Summary

Currently a Marketing Research Manager at McCormick & Company, the spice company. Responsibilities include marketing analysis and competitive intelligence. Responsibilities include modeling primary and syndicated (IRI and Nielsen) data for the CPG firm. Some of the projects I have worked on include inventory and point-of-purchase display optimization, new product introductions, and supply chain analysis.

Previous experience in e-Commerce, Search Engine Marketing and Search Engine Optimization; Customer Satisfaction and Customer Loyalty; CRM; market segmentation; Win/Loss Analysis; Advertising testing; as well as new product and package testing.

Created and managed competitive intelligence systems for use in strategic market planning. Designer of marketing information system (MKIS) to retrieve and analyze internal sales and marketing data. Developer of analytical models for use in sales territory realignment, deriving quotas, and forecasting in shaping pricing and sales strategies.

Data mining expert. Models used to predict amount of consumer consumption; sales trends and forecasting; and segment customers based upon profitability and future revenue streams. Model market share data to spot higher profitable customers and product/service lines. Data used to identify possible new geographic and customer markets. Skilled in SPSS, SAS, Business Objects, Crystal Reports, and Cognos.

Former Internet Specialist with IBM. Developed of e-Commerce strategies for small- and medium-sized businesses.Conference and seminar speaker for the international computer company.

Adjunct Marketing professor for the University of Phoenix; both graduate and undergraduate levels. Teach Principles of Marketing, Consumer Behavior, Marketing Research and Statistics online and on campus.

Formerly a professional journalist and photographer for weekly and daily newspapers. Expertise in graphic communications and commercial printing.
