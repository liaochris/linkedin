nancy mazzei’s Summary

Nancy Mazzei is the Vice President of VH1 Off-Air Creative – a position she
has held since 2006. She joined VH1 as the Off-Air Design Director in 2004.

As Vice President, Mazzei currently oversees the management and
implementation of all Off-Air Creative for VH1, VH1 Classic, VH1 Soul,
Classic Records and The Save The Music Foundation, and has designed some of the company's most successful and award-winng projects – including the LOGO and VH1 logos.

Before coming to VH1, Mazzei was the Creative Director of Marketing at Time
Warner Cable in New York City, where she directed commercials and concepted
advertising and marketing campaigns. Prior to her time at Time Warner,
Mazzei worked for the branding company PittardSullivan, where she created
print campaigns for iControl – Time Warner Cable/AOL's video-on-demand
product. Before joining PittardSullivan, Mazzei worked for Showtime Networks
as a Senior Art Director. In this role, she contributed to the "No Limits"
print branding campaign among others. Her work won numerous awards
during her tenure.

Mazzei earned her BFA at The School of Visual Arts in 1991. She later
returned to the school to teach typography for two years. Following
graduation, Mazzei became a partner in her own studio, Smokebomb. She
freelanced in this capacity for six years, creating original fonts for
Emigre and David Carson/Raygun magazine. The Gladys font she created for
Raygun magazine was displayed in the Cooper-Hewitt Design Museum's "Mixing
Messages" show. Mazzei's fonts are now distributed worldwide.
