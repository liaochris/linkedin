fran jurga’s Summary

My primary experience is as a niche-carver. Armed with tools I didn't know I had--curiosity and creativity--I have created two careers for myself that are unique and shared by almost no one else.

The first is as an editor, writer, and public relations person in the high-flying world of horse sports...but with the finely-focused goal of enlighteningi the world on the biomechanical abilities of equine athletes...and the tragedy and suffering of their injuries and diseases.

The second is an exhibit design consultant whose job is to be the storyteller and create a clear, easy-to-follow narration through museum, zoo, and aquarium exhibits. Using photography, multimedia, interactive computer kiosks, audience participation and good old text on the walls, I've told stories that translate complex scientific concepts into simple, strong images and clear, resonant sentences.

fran jurga’s Specialties:

Writing for print, web, blogs, and screen, editing, story development, photo and art research, artifact acquisition, meeting planning and development, event conception, book packaging and proposals, marketing and advertising, all forms of public relations.

fran jurga’s Experience

  • Blog writer and editor

    The Jurga Report on Horse Health Headlines, sponsored by Pfizer, for Primedia Equine Group

    (Public Company; Internet industry)

    December 2006 — Present (1 year)

    My newest project is writing and editing a blog on horse health and equine science for Primedia's portal site, http://www.equisearch.com. I am creating a new go-to resource on the web where I will serve as a personal guide through the tangle of science, research, and technology to explain how the field of horse health is changing. Among my goals are to stimulate more interest in new careers in this field and to support equine science students current matriculating at universities around the world.

  • Editor, Writer, and Creative Consultant

    Fran Jurga Publishing and Hoofcare & Lameness: Journal of Equine Foot Science (Self-employed)

    (Self-Employed; 1-10 employees; Publishing industry)

    January 1985 — Present (22 years 11 months)

    I provide freelance and contract services in the areas of nature, pet care, and veterinary science for publishing, media, public relations and marketing. My speciality is horse health, and I have a particularly strong background in equine lameness. My primary "contract" is with myself; I publish a quarterly journal, manage a web site, and blog about new developments and continuing education on the subject of the horse's hoof and related equine sport sciences. I also consult for other companies in the horse industry and write for publications and media. My other area of expertise is in creative development (research, writing, and image/artifact procurement) for natural sciences exhibits and publications for zoos, aquariums, and natural history museums. If you have ever been to a zoo or aquarium in the United States, you have probably read my words or seen images/media that I have developed.

  • Exhibit Design Consultant, Writer and Researcher

    Subcontractor architectural and design firms

    (Educational Institution; 51-200 employees; Museums and Institutions industry)

    1985 — Present (22 years)

    Contract assignments providing many different levels of expertise in conveying a cohesive story to museum, zoo, and/or aquarium visitors through text and visual imagery, enhanced by media, sound, and light installations. Most contracts were for new institutions, while others were for a special exhibit, publication, or event associated with an existing institution. Some other projects involved proposal development on behalf of architects and designers. About half of my work has been in Europe and Asia.
