Harry McCracken’s Summary

I'm an extremely lucky guy who gets to use the world's most exciting medium (the Web) and a wonderful tried-and-true one (print) to help millions of people get more out of the technology in their lives.

I've been editor in chief of PC World (the site and the magazine) since 2004, and have worked at PCW since--this always startles me when I think about it--October, 1994.

And yes, I'm the guy who resigned from PCW and then, after an unlikely and remarkable series of events, happily accepted my old job back.

PCW is the highest-circulation computer magazine in the world, and we're international, with about 60 editions. We have a large and growing site, and our content also appears on Yahoo, MSN, ABCNews.com, and WashingtonPost.com, among other places.

I've been fortunate enough to have worked on many award-winning projects here, including stories that won four Neal Awards for "Best Subject-Related Series," and a 2000 Grand Neal Award from American Business Media. Our magazine won Folio's Eddie award as best consumer computing publication in 2002, 2003, and 2004; PCWorld.com won the Neal award as best site in 2004 and 2005. In 2006, my blog won MIN's Best of the Web award.

Over the years, I've guested on many TV and radio programs (ABC, CBS, CNBC, CNN, MSNBC, NPR, the BBC, the History Channel, etc.), and I collaborated with Dateline NBC on a multi-state undercover investigation into PC repair in 2000.

I spearheaded our site redesign in 2006, and am currently working on a cool magazine redesign which will further strengthen the ties between PCW's print and online incarnations.

Outside of PCW, I've written for Slate, USA Today, Family Circle, and other publications, and have worked at InfoWorld; years ago, I edited a magazine on animation, Animato, which lots of folks seem to remember.

I graduated from Boston University and have lived in Boston, Portland (Oregon), London, and--since 2002--San Francisco.
