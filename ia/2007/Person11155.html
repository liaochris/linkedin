Bill Fishkin’s Summary

Professional plate spinner, serial entrepreneur, dot connector.

I founded The Synthesis Weekly at the age of 22 in a spare bedroom with a Visa card in 1994. The Synthesis Weekly (which is a regional music and entertainment free weekly newspaper) has achieved unprecedented access through its ties with the music industry that rival that of other publications in much larger markets.

In 1995 we launched a web site for The Synthesis Weekly (currently located at www.synthesis.net). We were one of the first free weekly publications in the country to maintain a web presence.

In 1995 I was the founding partner and served as President of Shocking Internet Services until it was sold in 1998. During my tenure as President I oversaw the development of hundreds of web sites and directed numerous projects.

In 2000 I successfully negotiated for the rights of the BAM Magazine content archive. BAM was the largest circulated free music/entertainment publication in the country for over 20 years.

In 2002 I co-founded the advertising firm Juggernaut Marketing with partner Stephen Pankhurst. In 2004 I co-founded Division72 Media Inc and subsequently launched InSideOut Magazine, a regional focusing on home improvement and lifestyle in Northern California. In November 2004 we launched The Upstate Business Journal which a regional monthly business journal currently being direct mailed to business owners in Northern California.

In June 2005 we launched Synthesis Magazine. The Synthesis Magazine is a a national subscription / newsstand edition of our www.synthesis.net & www.synthesisradio.net website. The magazine focuses on music, culture, fashion, film, photography, art and life. Issue 8 is out now and available all over the US and Canada - check it out at http://store.synthesis.net.
