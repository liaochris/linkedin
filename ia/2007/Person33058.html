Gregory Daines’s Summary

I am an economist specializing in the dynamics and management of innovation. I am interested in all aspects of managing science, R&D, emerging technology, intellectual property management, and technology licensing and commercialization. In particular, I have extensive expertise in the strategic management of innovation and technology licensing at innovative organizations.

I have worked on many aspects of this area including designing a leading enterprise management system supporting all aspects of technology licensing currently in use at many of the most important licensing organizations worldwide. I also provide consulting support to organizations interested in improving innovation management by designing and implementing new models and processes, and providing the necessary infrastructure to support them.

I am also interested in the development of novel new approaches to some of the major challenges and obstacles to the emergence of fluid global markets for technology and intellectual property. Some examples of projects currently in the works include:
- Building advanced licensing market information techniques and software tools,
- Developing internet-based tools for streamlining the transfer of scientific materials (MTAs),
- Creating improved methods for business development in technology transfer,
- Designing new scientific research documentation/authentication techniques and software tools,
- Building technologies for closing the gap between science research and commercialization,
and more.

If you are looking for software and management solutions for technology transfer, Knowligent provides the leading products and support services in the field (www.knowligent.com). The Ideanomics Group (www.ideanomics.com) is a new company offering advanced research, data and information, and consulting services supporting highly innovative scientific organizations, both public and private.
