Michael Frick’s Summary

As Executive Producer/Founder at leading bi-coastal music production company Mophonics, Michael has developed new business with nationwide agency clients such as Wieden & Kennedy, BBH, TBWA/Chiat/Day, BBDO, McCann Worldgroup, Goodby and others. He has researched and discovered branding opportunities involving music for brands Apple, Visa, Verizon, T Mobile, Scion, Nissan, Pepsi, Electronic Arts, Axe, VW & more.

An original music production company and digital label, Mophonics combines emerging technologies with innovative licensing deals for artists and/or labels in partnerships with commercial brand clients to strengthen brand identity.

Mophonics created the first fully commissioned original song to be featured in an Apple iPod campaign. Mophonics artist Zeroleen's single "All Good," featured in Verizon and 24hr Fitness campaigns, has seen extensive airplay on specialty radio, retail outlets via DMX, and American Airlines in-flight radio. Mophonics' Nissan "Sounds" spot, featuring artists Ming & FS, was nominated for best music in a TV commercial at the 2003 DMA Awards in Miami. Artists slated for 2007/08 releases include Joey Ryan and Daniela Bove.

Before making the leap to music and film Frick spent 8 years at Award Winning Digital Effects house Digital Domain where he held positions in Production, Operations, Scheduling, and as a Digital Artist earning credit on Academy Award winning films "What Dreams May Come" and "Titanic" as well as "True Lies," "Apollo 13," "The 5th Element" and others.

As a freelance music journalist Frick has written for numerous music publications including Urb, Raygun, Bikini, Revolution, MTV.com, and VH1/Sonic Net.
