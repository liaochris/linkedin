Vinayak Agrawal’s Summary

I started my career at ST Microelectronics Noida, India as an Associate Design Engineer in the Central Research and Development group. I did an exhaustive comparative study of SRAM Sense Amplifiers (Later presented many talks on sense amplifiers designed a novel circuit that worked the first time), and later a major debug program on 0.18um SRAM compilers.

Next I joined Epson Cambridge Laboratories in Cambridge UK as a researcher. I helped in testing of ferroelectric RAM that was designed earlier and designed sense amplifiers (in fact the whole sensing scheme, it is much different in FeRAMs than in any other memory), linear regulators, decoders etc. and also helped other colleagues with their queries on circuit design.

I joined ST Microelectronics again in late 2002. as the Project Lead on a very prestigious SRAM design project and was assigned a team of inexperiencd but extremel talented engineers. I designed a new SRAM architecure from scratch that exceeded all specifications on the first test. It has been ported in almost a dozen technology platforms. Moreover everyone on the team has been rated top amongst peers ever since.

Later I shifted to IO development group within ST Microelectronics. I worked on Oscillators, Slew Rate correctors, USB HS transceiver, and high speed IOs. I proposed and proved in simulations the feasibility of a multi-standard high speed PHY, including the IO, and a novel Gbps speed digital CDR with extremely high jitter tolerance.

After this I joined Sarnoff Research Labs' Bangalore office. I hired and mentoredthe rest of the team which rached a strength of more than a dozen people. My team worked on ESD protection, IO cells, High speed ADC/DAC, CCD and CMOS Image Sensors, High Voltage ICs and very low noise BiCMOS ICs for Medical Applications.

Presently I am with NXP semiconductor where I came through their acquisition of Silicon Labs' Wireless Division. I'm working on the audio section of a cellphone SoC.

Vinayak Agrawal’s Specialties:

Organising, training, mentoring and managing teams; Conceptualising, proposing and managing projects; Analog IC design in which I believe I have both depth and breadth; Mathematical Modelling and Analysis of Engineering problems.
