Jon Hudson’s Summary

Jon Hudson received a BSc honours degree in Physical Electronics from the University of Kent at Canterbury in 1977 and joined Texas Instruments in the UK as a design engineer. During the 1980's he built up TI's European ASIC design capability in Bedford, England. From 1988 to 1994 he was responsible for TI's European Linear telecoms business. In 1995 he was appointed Technical Marketing manager for Europe where he directed TI's field applications resources with an increasing focus on DSP and High Performance Analogue circuit solutions for the Telecommunications market place. In 2000, reporting to the president of TI Europe, he headed up TI's Optical Networking business unit for Europe and his final TI appointment was manager of emerging markets for TI Europe where he was driving TI's strategy to provide innovative solutions for emerging end equipments being designed in Europe. His two final legacies to TI in 2003 were initiating a re-entry into the TV set-top box market and orchestrating TI's first efforts to address the now obvious converged VoIP/cellular phone opportunity.

In early 20004, Jon Hudson left TI to form the Jon Hudson Consultancy where he assisted with the trade sale of a small independent ISP, helped TV production companies embrace picture messaging technology and drove a number of business development projects for companies such as Starcut Oy/Nokia Multimedia, Interactive Rights Management Ltd., Atlantic Bridge Ventures, Optical Metrology Innovations, AdvanceSis, ARM Ltd., Bitwave Semiconductor and CSR. Working with the latter resulted in Jon Hudson joining CSR full time in March 2006 where he is now Senior VP and General Manager of the PC, Automotive and Consumer Strategic Business Units. www.csr.com
