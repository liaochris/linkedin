Kate Andrews’s Summary

Kate Andrews is a Communications Designer and Researcher. In the past three years Kate has developed a conceptual appreciation for graphic design and visual communication, heavily influenced by the growing recognition for Socially Conscious Design.

With an combined knowledge and enthusiasm for human creativity and design innovation, Kate finds her inspiration from the diversity of cultures, educational reform, social change and scientific discoveries of human intelligence. Integrating predictions of our future society, with her own surrealist photography, her communication design responds to innovation, in a direct and appealing way: with a critical yet ever optimistic outlook.

Kate holds a First Class Honours in Graphic Design from The Arts Institute at Bournemouth and Merit Award from The International Society of Typographic Designers. Since her BA Kate has worked for The Sunday Times Magazine in London and Synectics Innovation Consultancy. She is the founder of design blog Anamorphosis and the UK Editor for sustainability weblog Inhabitat.com. As a designer, Kate is curious about the ways that design affects daily life, reflects human needs and inevitably impacts the human evolution.
