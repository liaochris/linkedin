Todd Siegel’s Summary

I am now focused on the question: How exactly can creators and performers of music leverage emerging tools and platforms to create new revenue patterns in order to thrive as a "middle class act"?

From head to toe: I have the perspective of the non-geek user, the heart of a designer, with my feet grounded in the business objectives.

I'm always excited to discover fresh opportunities to get involved as a consultant or advisor for web spread applications, and their mobile experiences – in these areas:

• Music and video discovery and sharing
• Location based communication services
• Focused social networking applications
• And broadly: web services

I strongly prefer to work within a startup or R&D environment that is focused on conceiving, designing, and developing user-centered products and services.

I excel at:

A) Synthesizing and analyzing information from these perspectives: social psychology, cognitive science, memetics, ethnography, business, usability, and design (interaction/interface/visual/ and information architecture)

B) Strategizing for: business development, partnering, market positioning, branding, and site/application/service design (as a user experience director.)

C) Ideating, conceiving consumer and creator directed experiences (for mass and niche markets)

and D) Naming (products, taglines, and title/label/button copy)

    Mozes

    (Internet industry)

    2005 — 2005 (less than a year)

    Conceived the Mozes SMS based conference panel text to projector screen service. Anybody can easily post a comment or question by texting it to the Mozes short code (66937). It appears as a live updating list that is projected for everybody in the room to see. The service has now been used in over 10 conferences with extremely useful effect. In effect, it's a form of IRC backchannel "for the rest of us" using text messages (on your phone), instead of an IRC client (on a laptop) - but importantly, everybody (including the ones on stage) can see it. Given its relative anonymity (only a phone # is revealed), it serves as a public expression tool for the shy.

    During its use for all the main panels at the recent Digital Music Forum in Hollywood, I noticed that it served as a way for the audience's comments, questions, and quips to influence the panel without stopping it. No running over with a microphone.
