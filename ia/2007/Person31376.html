Gene Dolgoff’s Summary

One of the world's first holographers (1964), Mr. Dolgoff invented holographic transfer printing in 1971 (used to print holograms on credit cards). 1970 - 1972, taught optics, lasers, and holography at City University of New York, authored text. 1972, founded DHI, engaged in holography and laser R&D, consulting, and custom work for industry and military until 1981, working with top researchers, scientists, and Nobel laureate (and inventor of holography), Dr. Dennis Gabor. 1981 to 1983, worked with Dr. Raymond Damadian, Fonar Corporation founder and inventor of MRI, to develop holographic MRI imaging, subsequent to developing 3D X-ray, CAT-scan, and ultrasound imaging. 1984, made world's first single-panel LCD video projector. Invented and received broad world-wide patents on LCD video protection technologies, including optical depixelization, optical brightness enhancement, and thin-profile video projection systems, licensed to companies including Matsushita (Panasonic) and Samsung.1985-1987 formed and ran real estate investment and NY State licensed mortgage brokerage firm with 200 employees. 1989 won $1 million contract from Pentagon's DARPA agency to study and demonstrate application of his LCD projection technologies to HDTV, and was first to recommend US HDTV format should be digital. Founder and original CEO, president, and chief scientist of Projectavision, Inc., from 1988-1995, which he took public on NASDAQ in 1990. Published papers in 3-D imaging, optics, holography, and LCD video projection, and has over 60 granted patents worldwide with more pending. Selected to be on American Electronics Association (AEA) High-Definition TV Task Force (where he lobbyed politicians in Washington for US support of HDTV development funding) and National Association of Photographic Manufacturers (NAPM) Standards Subcommittee, IT7-3, where he was instrumental in defining current world ANSI standards for fixed resolution electronic projectors.
