Kaido Kikkas’s Summary

* Mostly an academic person, but can also manage in other spheres (government/NGO, also business) if needed.
* Lectured a wide range of courses from B.A. to doctorate levels. Examples (all of them are self-authored) include:
- Social, professional and ethical aspects of IT (Estonian IT College, since 2000)
- Network applications I (Tallinn University of Technology, since 2002)
- People with special needs in a changing society (Tartu Academy of Theology, since 2000)
- Educational technology for people with special needs (Tallinn University, since 2005)
- Intellectual property in the age of new media (Tallinn University, since 2006)
- Open source management (Tallinn University, since 2007)
* Participated in a number of international research projects, including EU FP5 and FP6 as well as others, have received 2 research grants and led some national-level projects.
* Consulted a number of facilities and projects in accessibility and assistive technology
* Board member of Estonian IT Society and Estonian Union of People with Mobility Impairments
* A supporter of free software and open-source movements, uses solely Linux in his computers since 2000.

lecturing, course authoring, research, E-learning, disability issues (assistive technology, education, employment), free and open-source software, programming, web development
