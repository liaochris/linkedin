Konstantinos Kostopoulos’s Summary

A registered architect in Greece since 1990, teaching building technology & design since 2003, balances his time between academic research and professional work. His practice includes custom residential work, multi-unit housing, and mixed-use development. Besides his studio appointment at DUTH, he has taught at other architectural institutions. He participated in the preparation of the 2004 Summer Olympic Games as a consultant for the Athens Organizing Committee (ATHOC), and in the construction of the New Athens International Airport with Consortium Leader HOCHTIEF. He has been involved in sustainable building design, earning a commendation at the international competition Zephyr.

Konstantinos Kostopoulos’s Specialties:

• Residential design & project management, private residences, villas, multi-story/ multi unit housing, mixed-use development
• Significant experience in architectural & construction building design & coordination, airport & sports facilities & other building types
• Involved in sustainable building design, earned a commendation at the international competition Zephyr. Attended one semester of advanced academic training in building physics & sustainable technologies at the U. of Athens
