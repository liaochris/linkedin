Jason Turgeon’s Summary

Guy Kawasaki's tips for maximizing LinkedIn: http://blog.guykawasaki.com/2007/01/ten_ways_to_use.html

I'm a licensed boat captain, a student, an environmental scientist, and the founder of textbookrevolution.org.

I'm a student at Northeastern U, where I study environmental geology and marine studies. Through NU's co-op program, I've spent much of the last couple of years working both full-time and part-time at the EPA. There, I've:

*become an expert on roadway winter maintenance (putting down salt and sand)
*learned about green building and sustainable design
*developed supplemental environmental project ideas
*worked to clean up the Island End River, Chelsea Creek, Mystic River, and Merrimack River
*managed an in-depth sampling program on the Lower Merrimack River trying to find sources of bacterial contamination.

A couple of years ago, I got fed up with the outrageous cost of college textbooks and started looking online for an alternative. I found that there were lots of free books available, but they were very poorly organized. I started a website, http://www.textbookrevolution.org, to catalog them. The press likes my site, and it's been mentioned in BusinessWeek.com, USA Today, NPR's Marketplace, US News and World Reports, and others. This fall, I sold the site to Freeload Press, a startup that's giving away ad-supported textbooks. I'm still on board part-time as a consultant.

I'm active in the Big Brothers program, which linked me up with Kamiah, a rambunctious 9-year-old. Since 2004, we've enjoyed movies, Boston sports teams, and shooting hoops. Recently, I took him for his first bike ride.

I'm also involved with the UV Tube, an open source project to develop a reliable, affordable, easy to use ultraviolet drinking water purification device for the third world. In the summer of 2006 I went to Sri Lanka for two months to work on the device.
