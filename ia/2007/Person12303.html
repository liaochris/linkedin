Bradley Dewey’s Summary

An experienced CEO and venture capitalist with over 30 years in the information technology industry, Brad Dewey was the Founder in 2000 of Christian & Timbers’ European Executive Search Operations. Following the 2004 merger of this office of Christian & Timbers with London-based Renoir Partners, Mr. Dewey established his own senior executive search practice serving helping clients to recuit senior leadership talent in the Information Technology Industry.

Prior to joining Christian & Timbers, Mr. Dewey was a Partner with the European search firm Hightech Partners in Geneva and Brussels, where he developed a significant European search practice in information technology, telecommunications and e-commerce. Before becoming an executive search consultant, he was a venture capital Partner with Baring Venture Partners in London and Geneva, where he specialized in early-stage information technology and telecommunications investments. Mr. Dewey was also a founding Partner of Advent International’s central European private equity fund.

Earlier in his career, Mr. Dewey served as Director of International Sales and Marketing for Apollo Computers Europe. Before joining Apollo, he founded a 21-store European microcomputer retail distribution company and went on to establish the European operations for a market-leading American MRP software company.

During his career, Mr. Dewey has held seats on 14 Boards of Directors and has lived in the US, Denmark, Holland and Switzerland, where he has resided for the last 22 years. He is fluent in English and French and speaks conversational Dutch. He holds an MBA from the University of Chicago and a BA from Dartmouth College.

Bradley Dewey’s Specialties:

Brad Dewey is a senior executive search consultant based in London and Geneva. His clients include the leading technology firms such as Cisco, MicroFocus, NetJets, Symantec, Macromedia, eBay, Lastminute.com, Cambridge Silicon Radio and Adobe, as well as many leading venture capital-backed firms in France, Germany and the United Kingdom.
