Neal @ Rauhauser dot net’s Summary

How do you come to own a network engineering firm?

I'm the seventh or eight generation of my family to grow up in rural Iowa. Mine was the first generation not born at home, the first to have indoor plumbing, and the first to finish high school, let alone attend college. My railroad engineer father was disabled when I was in junior high but I was already a farm hand for hire at that age; when you're on the farm if you must have a Remington 870 shotgun, a Honda Express moped, your own pickup truck, or date money you'd best get cracking, because mom and dad don't pay for that sort of thing.

I attended Iowa State and majored in Computer Science. The first five years out of school I did software development but I lingered over network configuration starting with my first ethernet installation in 1988 and I made the final jump in 1994. The only programming I do now is integrating various free tools for network monitoring. I've made my living with internetworking for the last nine years and I'm excited about 2007, as I can now claim I've done that longer than I was a pitchfork technician and soil fertility engineer.

While its good to get out and get a job I started on the entrepreneurial track early.My lawn care business began with me driving that moped with one hand and towing a push lawnmower behind me in the other. Automation in the form of a pickup truck and a riding mower came two years later when I got my driver's license.

These days I'm taking on the hardest networking/telecom design, implementation, and troubleshooting projects I can find. I'm working on an online community building effort at http://going2extreme.com, and I've set aside Cisco after achieving the CCNP and CCDP so I can broaden my horizons with Extreme and Juniper. As always there are other shadowy, half formed things swirling around, and I'm doing what I can to make them more solid in 2007.
