Bart Deconinck’s Summary

Before joining the Private Banking and Trust industry, Bart Deconinck worked for three years in financial audit with Ernst & Young Belgium. After this first step, he joined MeesPierson in Belgium as director of Finance and Operations and a year later in 1994, he was appointed Managing Director of the Belgium office.

After three years at this position and another two years in Curaçao, as Managing Director of the banking, asset management and trust divisions of MeesPierson, Bart Deconinck was appointed Managing Director of MeesPierson Private Bank and Trust worldwide and soon after became a Member of the Overall Executive Board of MeesPierson. In this position he combined the responsibilities of Chief Financial Officer of MeesPierson with other operational responsibilities, including the worldwide trust business and the private banking activities in Asia. After the merger with Intertrust in 2002, where Bart was highly instrumental, he expressed his desire to focus more on the trust business, after which he was appointed Chief Executive Officer of MeesPierson (Fortis) Intertrust. Under his leadership MeesPierson Intertrust became recognized as the world’s leading provider of trust and corporate services.

He decided to leave MeesPierson Intertrust in February 2006 to build with other leading figures in the industry, a brand new international Trust & Corporate services provider, named Vistra. Today Vistra is active in 8 jurisdictions and employs some 180 staff.

Bart Deconinck holds a master degree in “applied economics” from the University of Antwerp (Belgium) and a master degree in “accountancy” from the University of Ghent (Belgium). Bart is a member of the board of the International Trust Companies Association and is a member of the International Fiscal Association.
