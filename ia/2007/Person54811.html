Mark McAlister’s Summary

My Great Aunt, Caroline Stookey Lutz, was an English teacher at the University of Richmond. Her students say that her classroom had no walls, and I take this to mean they went on many journeys together. People today also have classrooms without walls, but the landscape and weather of their journeys are very different.

I'm not sure how my Aunt would deal with the term "paradigm shift". Perhaps she would say it's an (awkward) synonym for "metaphor". But she would no doubt agree that our ability to grasp new concepts and embrace startling worldviews is important.

Which brings me to business. My consulting assignments always start with the INSPIRATION - why is your project so important and why should anybody care? Next, we study your "stakeholders" (I believe my Aunt would prefer the term "audiences") and figure out ways to attract their INTEREST. Finally, we earn their INVOLVEMENT. No more dipping in and out - they'll want to read the whole book.

Tell me about your business goals, and perhaps we can plan a journey together...
