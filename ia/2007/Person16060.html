Cheryl Thompson-Draper’s Summary

Cheryl Thompson-Draper retired in 2006 as a Commissioner of the Port of Houston Authority -only the third woman to serve as a Commissioner since 1909. She was Chairman of the Small Business Development Program, Small Business Task Force, Cruise Task Force and Pilot Task Force. She has served on the Port’s Employee Pension Committee, the PHA International Corporation- Board and as a Commissioner of the Pilot’s Board.

Cheryl is the retired CEO of Warren Electric, a $315 million woman owned enterprise that distributed products through locations in 4 states and 4 foreign countries. Warren was the largest woman owned business in Houston and the third largest in Texas. The company was sold in December 2002.

Cheryl currently owns several businesses: Cypress Pony Land Company -real estate investment management company and the T bar D Ranch,a bovine, equine, pecan and hunting operation. She is a charter investor of the Texas Women’s Venture Fund and of Post Oak Bank.

Cheryl is the Charter President of Faith Builders Toastmasters Club. She is the past president of the Miss Rodeo Texas and past chairman of Theatre Under The Stars. She is a Stephen Minister and on many committees at First Methodist Church Houston, a Paul Harris fellow of the Rotary Club of Houston.She was appointed by Texas Governor Rick Perry’s to The Economic Development Task Force and Governor’s Business Council.

She is currently or has served in board positions for the Boys and Girls’ Country, Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo, Greater Houston Partnership, Super Bowl XXXVIII, Hugh O’Brian’s Youth Leadership, Junior Achievement, United Way, Miss Rodeo America National Advisory Council and other organizations. She also served on the University of Houston Chancellor’s National Advisory Council.

With all this life experiance and expertise she is called on to be keynote speaker at many organizations- most recently the graduation keynoter at University of Houston.

Cheryl Thompson-Draper’s Honors:

Cheryl has been named “Texan of the Year” by All Those Texans, honored as “Women on The Move” by the Texas Executive Women and recognized by the Mayor of Houston, Harris County Judge, and US Chamber of Commerce as the ”International Executive of the Year.” The Texas Women’s Chamber of Commerce named her a “Woman of the Century”.
