Martin Kloos’s Summary

Martin was born on 12 january 1982. He studied bachelor Information Technology & Information management at The Hague. Currently He studies at the University of Amsterdam, where he follows the Master Business Information Systems. Central themes are ICT in service organizations, information management and knowledge management.

Martin did two internships during his bachelor study. The first internship was focused on the implementation of e-Synergy at an Exact Software dealer. He was rewarded an 8 at a scale of 10. The second internship was focused on restructuring the ICT department of NTI Netherlands. He had to examin shortcomings in the light of ISO 9001 and write recommendations for improvement. Martin was rewarded a 9 on a scale of 10.

Martin worked as a part time consultant for Exact Software, which is the leading company of financial administration software in the Netherlands. He focuses on e-Synergy, the web-based ERP / e-business suite, who recently won the innovation price from Microsoft.

martin recently finished is his Masters Thesis. He examined the effects of the application of Web 2.0 concepts on knowledge management, learning and meaning making in groups of people. The theory of knowledge management, learning and meaning is derived from Etienne Wenger, the founder of Communities of Practice and famous for his social learning theory. His central hypothese is: What are the effects of the application of Web 2.0 techniques on meaning making of groups of people. Martin was rewarded an 8 on a scale of 10 and finished his masters "cum laude".

Martin is currently working as a business analyst for the Custom Development and Integration department of Deloitte Consulting.
