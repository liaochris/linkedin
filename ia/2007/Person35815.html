Ian Blenke - ian@blenke.com’s Summary

Professional experience:

TCP/IP v4 and v6 internetworking. Starting in the pre-"Internet Boom" times, when reading RFCs over FTP was the big thing to do. Then Gopher came, and led to the World Wide Web, and here we are.

Networking equipment experience: most major networking gear. I prefer OpenSource solutions to embedded appliances whenever possible.

Telephony: years of Telco experience. From TDM and ATM signalling debugging to building datacenters. My home phone is VoIP, my cellphone rings when it does. Network convergence is here. I've moved on to more interesting problems.

Wireless: all of the modern "wifi" things you might expect a geek to know. My home network is meshed pre-N MIMO. I know the difference between HSPDA and EVDO-RevA. WiMax is the last-mile killer app; we're getting closer to wireless utopia every year.

System administration: There are few things in this arena I haven't fiddled with. Today I manage my current employer's debian variant, acting as a package maintainer for a private distribution of Linux. The large Linux based network of systems is maintained under one configuration management harness homegrown to meet the needs of my employer.

Development background: everything from 6502/80x86/370 assembler to C/Java and Perl/Ruby/Python. Programmers like to solve problems: the language is just a tool to that end. My background is primarily in OpenSource development, though I've dabbled in Microsoft's APIs now and again as needed.

Goals: Total mastery of OpenSource tools, integration and migration of legacy systems to OpenSource platforms, and to eventually automate myself out of a job.

If its digital, I strive to understand how it works. From the physical components through the firmware to application software and intra-system interactions, my goal is always to have a thorough understanding of the technology.
