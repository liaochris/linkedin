Linda Jenkinson’s Summary

I love how words sound as they skip "trippingly off the tongue" and how the pictures they create engulf the senses. Words let you see, touch, hear, and feel like no other tool known to the world.

In 1974, I learned the true power of compelling sales content in my first copywriting position as Copy Director of KDHL Radio in Faribault, MN.

In the early 80's, as editor of the "Cathedral Bells" I was introduced to design and layout concepts and learned how to create a union of imagery and words to catch a reader's attention and bring his/her focus to my message.

In 1997, I purchased my first computer and fell in love with digital design. Shyflower's started in 1997 under the name of Paquette Custom Gift and Printing. In 1999, using a nickname suggested by a friend, "PCGP" became Shyflower's and the business expanded into the area of publication.

In 1999, I purchased my first domain and opened Shyflower's Garden Library, a literary ezine that introduced both my readers and me to the talents of some wonderful authors and artists. Shyflower's Garden expanded into publishing e-books with the Shyflower Press and, although neither is currently in operation, I remain proud that both the ezine and publishing house were cited by Writer's Market (2000-2001) as one of the best 25 places to get published on the Internet.

Since 2002 I have put words together for many Web Developers and Webmasters — Web content that merges with web design to make Extraordinary Web Pages.

Aside from the jobs listed above, I have worked in many other industries including Real Estate, Medical, Pharmaceutical, Clothing, Large Appliance, Grocery Produce, and Customer Service. You might say I"m a "Jenny" (?) of all trades, but although I don't hold a Master's Degree in any field, this 'life experience' enhances my expertise in copywriting... the words that make the Web go 'round' and help Internet businesses turn Web visitors into paying customers and clients.
