Ola Onuch’s Summary

Work Experience and Professional Skills:
I have extensive experience working with different multilateral organizations, research institutes and in academia. I worked as Personal Assistant to, the Director of The George Soros Centre for Contemporary Arts (Kyiv, Ukraine) and Professor Villia Jefremovas (Queen’s University, Canada). Also interned as a Research Assistant to Mr. Gregory T. Jedrzejczak, at the IBRD/World Bank in Kyiv, Ukraine, and to Mr. Marc-André Franche, at the UNDP in New York. While holding the above posts, I was privileged to work with people from different backgrounds, cultures and positions, and thus feel that I am a highly professional and motivated team player, able to work cooperatively as well as independently.

I feel I have excellent communication and research skills, which have been honed while at my most recent posts; Consultant with the Institute for Strategic Studies, Research Assistant and Conference Director. While holding each post I was obligated to compose documents and analytical briefs in a professional and timely manner. One such example is the background paper I produced for the Democratic Dialogue Program at RBLAC.

Furthermore, I am fluent in four languages including: English, French, Polish and Ukrainian. I am comfortable with Russian and have a basic understanding of Spanish. Thus, I am confidant that I have developed very effective and professional communication skills.

Goals:
I look forward to building a career in Academia, Foreign Diplomacy and Canadian National Politics .

Ola Onuch’s Specialties:

Comparative politico-economic theory and practice of democratic transition and development in Eastern Europe and Latin America. DPhil thesis focus is the dynamics of social movements, protest and the development of civil society in countries undergoing transition to democracy. Development of particular social movements in Poland, Ukraine, Argentina and Brazil. North American and European Politics. MSc dissertation on the political dynamics of the Informal Economy in Ukraine and Brazil.
