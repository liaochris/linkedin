Aditi Nadkarni’s Summary

My professional degree in Pharmaceutical Sciences from the University of Bombay, India prepared me for a career in the pharmaceutical industry. For the later half of my undergraduate degree I trained at Merck as an industrial apprentice. This experience gave me an early overview of the Pharmaceutical/ Biotechnology industrial set-up. I was admitted into the PhD program at the Medical University of Ohio (University of Toledo-Health Sciences Campus) and awarded a predoctoral fellowship to pursue research in the field of Molecular & Cell Biology. In recent years I have successfully applied my pharmaceutical background to the advancing field of cancer genomics. I have designed cell based models to study the effect of genetic status on response to anticancer drugs. I am also investigating the effect of cancer associated genetic variants on response to anticancer therapy, as part of my thesis. My research project involves development of a logical rationale and analysis of data using statistical models. Additionally, during my academic pursuits I have also written research proposals and received a grant to assist my project. I have trained undergraduate research fellows and graduate students in scientific technique and assays. As a scientist, the conceptual aspect of research is what interests me most and hence I believe my significant strengths lie in 1) being able to independently develop a hypothesis and 2) in designing innovative strategies to investigate the hypothesis. I enjoy teaching and working within a team. My experience in technical writing is an added asset.

Aditi Nadkarni’s Specialties:

Experimental design, Analytical reasoning for development of a focused hypothesis, Innovative assay techniques, Animal models of disease, Drug-response assays, Development of cellular models to study mechanism of drug action or genetic effects of cancer associated variants, Protein interaction analysis, Data analysis (statistics), Teaching, Technical writing.
