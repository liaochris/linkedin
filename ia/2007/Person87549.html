Mark McClellan’s Summary

Mark McClellan served as a staff member for Congressman David McIntosh specializing in Technology, Military, Veterans and Labor affairs.

Mr. McClellan is currently a Vice President for Tridge Alliance, Inc. in Indianapolis. Please visit www.tridgealliance.com.

Mark has served in a variety of key positions for such organizations as IBM, Paychex, Boy Scouts, Indiana Chamber of Commerce, and several Veterans Organizations. Mark has worked with organizations such as SAP, Oracle, PeopleSoft, J.D. Edwards, Microsoft, Cisco, Citrix, Daimler/Chrysler, Eli Lilly, Roche Diagnostics, American Legion, National Inter-Fraternity Council and Federal & State Government Agencies.

As Foster parents, Mark and his wife, Michelle, have provided care for over 50 children in the last 8 years.

Mark is active with several non-profits and organizations such as: TechPoint, The Childrens Museum, Indiana State Museum, Lilly Foundation, The Eiteljorg Museum, St. Vincents Hospital, Riley Childrens Hospital, Promise Keepers, Boys and Girls Clubs, Indianapolis Chamber of Commerce, Rainmakers, and several Midwest companies. Recently, Mark was asked to join Congressman Dan Burton's Congressional Advisory Comittee.

Despite being a full time parent, business owner and political/community activist, Mark is always looking to help make this world a better place.

"My mission is to learn from the past, focus on the present, dream about the future and live life to its fullest potential. There are three great essentials in achieving excellence; innovation, persistence and common sense. Past experiences give me the ability to understand multiple processes. I am an outgoing Type A Personality who works as an aggressive and open communicator. I have the ability to communicate clearly and demonstrate value of services and products. I've presented at multiple trade shows and work well within Team Environments."
