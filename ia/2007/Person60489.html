Mark LaRosa’s Summary

I work for Google's User Experience group as a User Experience Researcher and System Architect. I thrive in ambiguity, work very well with diverse groups of people, and am more than accustomed to high-stress, last-minute deadlines and adversities. I’m fluent in Windows, Mac, and Linux development and operating environments (including server platforms), and I work with all of them extensively on a daily basis. My biggest contributions at Google involve designing, building, and integrating unique new enterprise storage and media distribution services into the existing corporate network. I am also researching and creating several large enterprise-grade internal management applications written in Ruby on Rails with a MySQL back-end. I feel that my many years as CEO of Shift Systems has taught me a lot about how to be successful: by integrating high-quality deliverables with the most personal business relationship possible, our company has grown exclusively from countless referrals from our diverse range of clients. I am always able to find a solution for a client, no matter how small or restrictive the budget.

Goals: I enjoy a career that allows me to thrive in unique and challenging environments - particularly those that are not very well-defined. I enjoy braving new territory and I want to be continuously challenged to improve. I also like to remain on the cutting-edge of new technologies and development paradigms to ensure that I am providing the best possible services and experiences for my clients. Most importantly, I enjoy having a lot of responsibility within a company - the more I have, the better I perform! I'm particularly well-suited at leveraging all available assets to accomplish a goal -- especially when it may not be possible "by the book".
