Nils Lahr’s Summary

Nils is a recognized technology leader with more than 10 years of unparalleled experience co-founding 8 technological related broadcasting & communication type startup companies as an executive level technology officer (CTO) and consulted within many fortune 50 companies as well as lead architect for some of the most advanced Internet and Communication projects in the world. Nils co-founded and helped fund three separate & successful high-tech ventures and worked as an EIR in 2003/4 at Matrix Partners, one of the top Venture Capital firms in the Silicon Valley.

Nils was the CTO and co-founder of iBEAM Broadcasting, the inventor of the first ever distributed 'Edge' Internet delivery service concept which became the largest global streaming media network & provider in the world. iBEAM raised over 400 million in funding and became responsible for nearly 80% of all the streaming media. It went public in 2001 and reached a market cap of just over 2 billion dollars. The company was finally purchased by Williams Communications.

Nils was one of the first key Microsoft architects & developers for the original Windows Media & Streaming Media division and consulted for their largest clients to deploy video streaming applications. He was a key developer & strategist in the startup company VXtreme, which was purchased by Microsoft for over 50 million in cash and stock options.

Nils worked under contract for CNN America as the senior architect/developer & programmer over a three year period and designed most of the technologies behind the CNNfn.com website as well as helping design & invent their initial digital broadcast media infrastructure. In addition to CNN and Microsoft, Nils has worked and consulted for numerous top companies in the world, including Bell Atlantic/Verizon Communications, Boeing, PCCW, The US Air Force, Fox News Broadcasting, CNET, Bloomberg Network, Warner Brothers, US Web, and Lucent Technologies just to name a very few.
