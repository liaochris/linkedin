Josh Kaufman’s Summary

I started with P&G when I was a sophomore at the University of Cincinnati in interactive marketing. I moved over to traditional brand marketing (Dawn, Cascade, Swiffer, and Mr. Clean) for a few years, learned a lot, and now I'm doing a mix of both, helping brands around the world use online media in a way that makes sense. I'm basically working to completely change the way the world's biggest advertiser markets brands, which I think is pretty cool.

One of the many things that I've learned in my time at P&G is that big-company marketing and the political path to "upper management" is not for me. Someone else will be CEO of P&G one day, and I'm fine with that. I'd rather kick butt as an individual contributor on a smaller team than manage a lot of people while climbing the corporate ladder. I don't need a corner office to be happy with my life and work, just a worthwhile goal and a lot of personal freedom.

I'm also the creator of the Personal MBA, an entrepreneurial approach to business education that was featured in BusinessWeek in 2006. The basic idea is that it's possible to get a really great education by reading good business books and discussing them with other people... no $100,000 traditional MBA program required. You can find the blog / forum at personalmba.com.

I have a bit of an entrepreneurial streak, so there are always a few new projects in the mix. If you're curious about what they are, just ask!
