John Tersigni’s Summary

Twenty years experience as chief executive and senior marketing executive. Entrepreneur in emerging technology markets: software, enterprise computing, interactive multimedia, educational technology, direct marketing, targeted media, and training systems. Direct P&L responsibility, new product development, and sales channel development. Founded Mercantile Software in 1987 – acquired by Harte-Hanks NYSE:HHS in 1998.

Serve as advisor to Bear Stearns Primary Insight; a consulting organization that links institutional investors with subject matter experts in emerging markets.

Founder, inventor: DigitallyU, Bloomsbury, NJ - interactive self-marketing software –2005 to present. DigitallyU portfolio builder built using Flash is used by colleges, high schools, and individuals for college placement, job search, students, and families.

Co-Founder & CEO of Mercantile Software Systems Inc, Piscataway, NJ – one of the first complete CRM targeted media marketing services solutions in US. Mercantile Software Systems formed in 1987. Sold Mercantile to Harte-Hanks Communications Inc. NYSE:HHS in 1998. Grew net revenue by at least 35% annually for 10 straight years.

Named to Harte-Hanks Winners Circle for outstanding performance: 1 of 14 employees recognized from 4,500 employees. Chairman of Harte-Hanks product and services council.

Senior Vice President of Marketing and Sales – Harte-Hanks, Piscatway, NJ and San Antonio, Texas. SVP for Direct Marketing and Targeted Media Emerging Markets 1997-2000. Delivered $196,000 of net revenue per employee. Ran Mercantile Software Systems Inc as a Harte-Hanks business unit. Increased gross profit margins by 79% in 1998. Increased gross profit margins by 243% from 1997 to 1998.

Adjunct appointment to Wharton Business School as a Lecturer Taught Mktg 781 to full time MBA students, 2005-2006.

Member of Hewlett Packard Computer Systems Advisory Board 1992-2000.
