Donna Milgram’s Summary

I am the founder and Executive Director of the Institute for Women in Trades, Technology & Science (IWITTS), founded in 1994.

Currently, I am the Principal Investigator for the CalWomenTech Project, funded by a $2 million National Science Foundation Award. Through this five-year grant, 8 to 10 California community colleges will receive expert support and technical assistance to help recruit and retain women into technology programs where they are under-represented.

In 2007, the Project sites are the City College of San Francisco Computer Networking and Information Technology Program, with a focus on the new Digital Home Integration Technology certification, San Diego Mesa College’s Geographic Information Systems program, Cañada College’s new 3-D Animation and Video Game Art Program and El Camino College’s Air Conditioning Refrigeration program.

Donna Milgram’s Experience

  • Executive Director

    Institute for Women in Trades, Technology & Science

    (Non-Profit; Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    June 1994 — Present (13 years 7 months)

    IWITTS provides the tools to successfully integrate women into male-dominated careers such as technology and law enforcement. Our national audience includes educational institutions, employers, and women and girls themselves. Careers range from automotive technician to pilot, computer networking technician, telecommunications engineer, electrician and police officer, to name just a few.

    To accomplish its mission, National IWITTS uses the following strategies to assist its clients and partners:

    (1) National research projects
    (2) Trainings and technical assistance (both in person and online)
    (3) Multimedia resource publications for educators and employers
    (4) Web site development, online community building, online training and other technical solutions
    (5) The WomenTech e-Store, a central clearinghouse for products related to integrating women into technology, trade, and law enforcement occupations
