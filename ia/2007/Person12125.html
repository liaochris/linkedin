Andrew Borene’s Summary

Andrew Borene is an attorney, a former banker and a veteran U.S. Marine Intelligence Officer with academic and professional experience in business, law, and government.

Borene worked for two years as a Public Finance Investment Banker at Wells Fargo & Co. During that time, he completed a program in International Development Finance at Harvard University. He also served for more than three years on active duty as a U.S. Marine Intelligence Officer, including combat service in Iraq.

While attending the University of Minnesota Law School, Borene served as a Judicial Extern at the United States District Court for the Honorable Judge Tunheim and worked as a law clerk at Bassford-Remele PA, a civil litigation firm. In 2007, Borene practiced law as county criminal prosecutor under the Minnesota Supreme Court's student-practice rule, where he appeared on behalf of the state in felony criminal matters and at civil commitment proceedings.

Borene also has experience as a commentator on politics and policy issues, with highlights including published Op-Ed pieces for The New York Times, The Baltimore Sun, The Star-Tribune and other publications. Additionally, Borene has appeared live on CNN 360 with Anderson Cooper, ABC News, Fox News, CBS, National Public Radio and BBC/PRI's "The World" to provide analysis.

Borene has two young sons, Magnus and Dane.
