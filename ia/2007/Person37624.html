James Ketchell’s Summary

Starting in 1997 as the co-founder of Web Host Directory I was in the privileged position to see the final throws of the "first" internet bubble as it went out with a bang. In reality our world was actually pretty much unaffected by the rapid decline in headlines and IPO's. At the time we really didn't appreciate just how fortunate we had been in choosing the web hosting market as the home for our first internet venture.

What the journalists and observers at the time didn't fully appreciate was the effect the internet would come to have on the world over the coming years. Underpinning the rapid growth in web sites and technology was the unsung hero of the internet services sector. The good old web host. Hosting never did really get into the limelight, largely because it was perceived as an unglamorous if necessary utility. In reality behind the scenes the demand for web hosting services was insatiable. Thousands of providers entered the market over the next few years in an attempt to satisfy the demands of the consumer. The result was the creation of one of online worlds most highly competitive marketplaces, valued at over $7 Billion in 2006.

Our business has emerged as the recognised leader in matching buyers and sellers of hosted services. Almost 10 years on, we now successfully operate sites in the USA, UK, Canada and more recently Australia, providing buyer information on over 11,000 individual providers around the globe. Our continued goal is to develop the worlds largest marketplace for hosted services, through a combination of innovative marketing products, and a dedication to the highest standards in data aggregation.

The rise in specialised hosted services is finally bringing a small level of recognition to the hosting industry. Data backup, Video storage, photo storage, online gaming, internet television and VOIP, all owe the continued success to the pioneering efforts of the worlds web hosting providers.
