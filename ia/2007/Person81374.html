  • VP, Internet Solutions

    TIBCO Software, Inc.

    (Internet industry)

    1991 — 2000 (9 years)

    Transformed the company from an enterprise software company into an Internet leader by embedding core TIB platform into major Internet portals like Yahoo, Netscape, Altavista, CNN, etc. Ran TIBCO.net hosting center.

    Mid-way through the company's life we sold it to Reuters. A notable quote from that period was one of the Reuter's men harrumphing "The Information Bus? Sounds like a large, lumbering vehicle holding up traffic on the Information Highway!" We bought the company back in a couple of years and were recognized soon on the Internet as the fastest and most scalable messaging technology to power the largest sites.

    What attracted me to TIBCO in its early stages was the heady blend of trading floors and distributed computing. I managed the trading systems product line and sold into major brokerages and stock exchanges.

    Learning to be a Dream Merchant was a transformational experience in my professional life.

  • Consultant

    AT&T Bell Labs

    (Telecommunications industry)

    1985 — 1987 (2 years)

    Integrated world's largest distributed database machine cluster for monitoring nationwide telecommunications network of switches. This equipment was located in a massively fortified bunker somewhere deep in the earth in New Jersey. How they let a foreign engineer manage the guts of the nationwide phone system during Mother's day, Thanksgiving and Christmas is something unimaginable post 9/11.
