Daniel Appelquist’s Summary

I've been working with Internet content since 1989, before most people had heard the word "Internet." I've worked for companies large and small, mostly in on-line content industries. In 1999, I hopped the pond to the UK and I now work at Vodafone Group where I live in the culture clash between new media and telecommunications. In 2001, I published a book with Addison Wesley on XML and content management ("XML and SQL: Developing Web Applications") and I have since written articles for InformIt on various topics. I also have spoken at many conferences on the topics of XML, Content Management, Metadata and Mobile Data Services. I am currently chairing the W3C Mobile Web Intitiative "Mobile Web Best Practices" working group (www.w3.org/2005/MWI/BPWG/). I am also the founder and chief instigator of Mobile Monday London (www.mobilemonday.org.uk). I also ran an event, mobile2.0 (www.mobile2event.com) on the November 6th, 2006 in San Francisco and I plan to run a follow-up event next year around the same time.
