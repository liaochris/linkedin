Elizabeth Stubbins’s Summary

Currently working with a Harvard-based research program on international humanitarian law (the law of armed conflict), I was a Legal Adviser at Amnesty International's International Secretariat in 2005 and 2006, applying international human rights law to Amnesty International reports on Europe and Central Asia, Middle East and North Africa, and Africa. Since 2006, I have also been a consultant lawyer with the Coalition to Stop the Use of Child Soldiers, and will be responsible for legal accuracy in their Global Report 2008.

I held a Helton Fellowship from the American Society of International Law in 2006, and conducted a fieldwork mission to Bulgaria to investigate conditions in institutions for children and young adults with mental disabilites. Mission diaries from my research are posted online at: http://warehousesofneglect.civiblog.org/blog/

I also have knowledge and experience in the UN human rights system, and can offer advice to non-governmental organizations (NGOs) on the United Nations and Council of Europe, in addition to international law.
