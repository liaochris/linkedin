Elmien Wolvaardt Ellison’s Summary

I am a science graduate with extensive experience of print and broadcast journalism and specialist expertise in science communication.

In January this year, I accepted a position as editor of the Community Eye Health Journal (www.jceh.co.uk), which is based within the International Centre for Eye Health at the London School of Hygiene and Tropical Medicine. The journal is an educational publication that is circulated to 15,000 readers, mostly in low- and middle-income countries. Readers include ophthalmologists, optometrists, doctors, nurses and managers.

My position at the journal allows me to continue my freelance work in the fields of editing, writing and science communication. I am currently commissioning editor of the E-Guide to Science Communication for the Science and Development Network (www.SciDev.net). Recent freelance work has also included editing and proofreading a brochure for the Sahgrenska Academy in Gothenburg, Sweden (http://www.sahlgrenska.gu.se/english/).

At present, I am also involved in a British Council-funded project in South Africa developing training for health workers in evidence-informed decision making for HIV prevention, treatment, and care.

Prior to joining the Community Eye Health Journal, I consulted for the Science Media Centre (www.sciencemediacentre.org) in London about the expansion of their services to the UK's regional media.

Before I moved from South Africa to the UK, I worked for the South African Medical Research Council (MRC), where I was editor of MRC News and supported MRC researchers to communicate their research to policy makers, the public, and the media.

I have also written a mathematics textbook for Grade 4, and have edited several educational textbooks for Pearson Education and Maskew Miller Longman.
