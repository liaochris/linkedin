Alex Singleton’s Summary

Alex Singleton is President of the Globalisation Institute, a Brussels-based think tank that develops practical policies to help Europe better harness globalisation.

He is an experienced speaker, giving talks from from Berlin to Las Vegas, and is noted for his ability to communicate complicated subjects in a simple way - and grip an audience.

A frequent commentator on TV and radio, his TV appearances have included the BBC’s Newsnight and Working Lunch, ITV News, CNBC, CNN, Al Jazeera, Bloomberg, Channel Four News, and Sky News. Radio appearances have included all the major BBC radio stations including Radio 4’s flagship Today programme and the World Service.

Alex Singleton is Editor of The Concise Encyclopedia of Globalisation (forthcoming, autumn 2007); author of Trade Justice or Free Trade?; and co-author of Positive Environmentalism and of Microfinance: harnessing enterprise to fight poverty, which was launched by David Cameron.
