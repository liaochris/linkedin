Toke Ward Petersen’s Summary

Head of Portfolio Analysis in New Product Planning in Novo Nordisk (world leader within diabetes) with 6 years biopharmaceutical experience within valuation, business strategy, portfolio analysis, generation of strategic development options, assessment of market potential and disease areas, forecasting using patient based models, forecasting using statistical models and market analysis. Before that 5 years of economic modelling experience while doing my PhD with in the area of computational economics. Experienced with strategic planning, decision analysis modelling/analysis of business problems, forecasting and leading commercial assessment of business development opportunities. Experience with departmental management, including HR, budgets, mangement and leadership.

* New Product Planning
* Valuation of pharmaceutical and biotech projects (development projects as well as pre-clinical); risk-adjusted NPV, decision/event trees.
* Project and Portfolio Planning and Analysis. Implementation of the Enrich valuation system (EPS).
* Commercial assessment of in-licensing and business development opportunities: including TPP generation, market research, market assessment and forecasting, scenario generation.
* Strong knowledge of diabetes and haemostasis therapy areas; significant experience with the value of devices; knowledge of many other disease areas from BD&L activities.
* Improve basis for strategic decision making and challenge business plans; advocate for fact based decision making.
* Strategic Marketing: market modelling of pipeline products using patient based models, as well as modelling and forecasting of marketed products using statistical techniques.

Toke Ward Petersen’s Specialties:

portfolio planning and analysis, cross functional project management and leadership, portfolio management, pipeline planning, valuation of assets that are preclinical or in development, ENPV, NPV, ROI, decision analysis, assessment of in-licensing and business development opportunities, forecasting, scenario modelling, diabetes, haemostasis, devices, incidence driven patient based (Markov stock-flow) models, statistical models, demand forecasting (supply chain management), pharmacoepidemiology
