Andrei Cabanban’s Summary

I am the sole proprietor for Raging Sea Design, which is a graphic design company and independent record label that was founded in Los Angeles in 2004. I am currently residing in Chicago doing graphic design work for Izip bicycles and other various clients. I hope to start new musical projects based in Chicago for release on my record label.

Andrei Cabanban’s Specialties:

Print Design, Web Design, Sound Design, Illustration and Video.

Andrei Cabanban’s Experience

  • Director of Design

    Currie Technologies

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Consumer Goods industry)

    May 2003 — November 2007 (4 years 7 months)

    As the Director of Design for Currie Tech, I was responsible for the conceptualization and execution of the following: bike and scooter decal graphics, sales collateral, packaging, website design and installation design. Every year my design work was showcased at Interbike in Las Vegas, and in TV shows like Living With Ed, ESPN2's Cold Pizza and ABC's The View.

    Because Izip and Currie has a partnership with HGTV’s Living With Ed, I have art directed various lifestyle photo shoots with Ed Begley Jr. riding Izip bicycles for content to be used in print work and web marketing campaigns.

  • Art Director

    Magnani Continuum Marketing

    (Marketing and Advertising industry)

    2002 — 2003 (1 year)

    As an Art Director at Magnani, I worked with copywriters and back end programmers to develop front end interfaces for MacGregor Golf's website. I also worked in conjunction with copywriters and account executives to develop print materials for Geyser Peak Winery, Duck Cruise Lines, YAYA! Bike, Gunnar Bicycles, Schwinn, Mongoose and GT Bicycles.

  • Art Director

    Boom Design

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Design industry)

    1999 — 2001 (2 years)

    At Boom Design, I was part of a design team that developed the Nike Diversity ad campaign which was an ad series dedicated to promoting diversity in a corporate environment. For corporate ID, I conceptualized and developed the corporate ID system for Leapnet and Quantum Leap, which were Interactive Marketing Strategy companies. For more conservative print work, I art directed the Lasalle Street Project's Economic Summit 2001 folder and brochure system. With clients like the About Face Theater, Tangueray and Malibu Rum, I developed flyers for theater performances and/or liquor sponsored events. Finally, I art directed a One Eleven One Marketing ad which was featured in Select, a visual aural design magazine printed in Chicago for release in anticipation of Summer Solstice which was an art and music fest held at The Chicago Museum of Contemporary Art.

  • Art Director

    Brainforest

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Design industry)

    1998 — 1999 (1 year)

    At Brainforest, I developed corporate ID work for Motorola's Impact 21 radio, art directed print work for Hyundai Construction's Scoop Newsletter and developed print work for the Crain's Chicago Business Auto Guide, Golf Guide and Beyond 2000 ad campaign. I also conceptualized and art directed the Brainforest website, which won an award in PRINT Magazine in 1999. For flyers and brochure work, I did various pieces for Jam Theatricals and The Lookingglass Theater Company's Metamorphoses.
