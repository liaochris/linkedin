Stacy Rosenberg’s Summary

Stacy Rosenberg is Assistant Director for External Relations for the New York University Department of Media, Culture, and Communication (MCC). She also teaches the courses Media Criticism as well as Media and Identity at NYU, and freelances as a Story Analyst (evaluating the potential and feasibility of script properties to be developed into films) for RHI Entertainment. She has been a guest panelist for Woman in Communications and a guest lecturer for Consumer Behavior in Sports at NYU.

Prior to joining MCC, Stacy served as Associate Director at the NYU Preston Robert Tisch Center for Hospitality, Tourism, and Sports Management. In this role, Stacy directed the NYU Hospitality Industry Investment Conference, chaired by Jonathan M. Tisch. Previously, she worked as a strategic partners producer for the arts and entertainment guide Citysearch.com and in television production for The Walt Disney Company and Universal Studios, Orlando. Projects included media relations for the Walt Disney World Animal Kingdom and Downtown Disney grand opening press events and Walt Disney World’s 25th Anniversary press event, and production work on Oprah, Roseanne, Walt Disney World’s Christmas and Easter specials, Miss America, Children’s Miracle Network Telethon, N’ Sync's "I Want You Back" music video, and more.

Stacy, an NYU alumnus (Media Ecology, MA '05), presented on "recreational surveillance" at the department's graduate program conference, and published an article on "The Elderly and Television" in the 2004 edition of the Encyclopedia of Television. In fall of 2004, she completed her thesis on Environmental Journalism. This work compliments her bachelors in Telecommunication, with an outside concentration in Business, from the University of Florida where she was a Florida Academic Scholar, graduated summa cum laude, and was awarded the Dean's Cup for Professional Promise.
