Joey Caisse’s Summary

Chief Technology Officer (Media) at News Digital Media providing technical oversight and guidance for News.com.au, FoxSports.com.au, over half of News Corp's metropolitin newspaper websites, including TheDailyTelegraph.com.au, TheAustralian.com.au, CourierMail.com.au, and HeraldSun.com.au.

Owner, Founder, CEO, President: Web-X Inc., Mail Mediary LLC, Digital Operations Management LLC, Caisse Digital Management, llc (http://www.caissedm.com), and XLarge.com (http://www.xlarge.com)

Nominated Most Valuable Entrepreneur 2001 by The Venture Forum

Member of Washington State Congressman Adam Smith's Technology Advisory Committee, consulting senior government officials about trends in the technology industry and providing guidance regarding future emerging technology's benefits and pitfalls.

Technical Editor of Ziff Davis's eCommerce Alert publication.

Steering Committee member for the Tacoma Technology Consortium, a monthly group of 150+ members dedicated to promoting the technology growth and stability in the South Puget Sound region.

Steering Committee member for the Washington Software Alliance (WSA), South Sound chapter, the oldest and largest state-wide technology trade association emphasizing networking, education, technology advocacy, and funding.

Member of Rotary #8, the 8th oldest Rotary club in the world. I was the youngest member in the 450+ membership from 1999 until 2001.

Speaker at Thunder Lizard Production's Web Broadcasting Seminar series in San Jose demonstrating the implementation of seven of the leading Push technologies.

Panelist at Pacific Lutheran University's eBusiness Day 2001 conference, Digital Marketing panel

Member of the Corporate Board of Directors for the Pierce County Boys and Girls Clubs.

Panelist on KBTC's TV show, The Digital Demand: How to Get a Dot Job

Joey Caisse’s Specialties:

Business management, web planning from strategy to implementation (project planning, contract negotiation, resource planning, information architecture, user interface design, database and server architecture). Other areas of expertise include: cat herding, lawn jarts, and overstated opinions.
