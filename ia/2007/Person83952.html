Tadej Brdnik’s Summary

Tadej Brdnik began his professional dance career in Slovenia. Since moving to New York in 1993, he has performed with Coyote Dancers, Avila/Weeks Dance, Baryshnikov’s White Oak Dance Project, theater director Robert Wilson and MoveOpolis as well as in works of Maurice Béjart, Lucinda Childs, Yvonne Rainer, Susan Stroman, Steve Paxton, Deborah Hay and others. He is a principal dancer with the Martha Graham Dance Company (since 1996) and is working with Pick Up Performance Company and Battery Dance Company.

His choreography was performed in many US cities, Germany and Slovenia. It includes works: God is playing golf – winner, Benetton Dance Award, taped for Slovenian National TV, In every one of you, One of me – for Milwaukee Dance Theater, Where memories run free and K-Mart Shopper. He also choreographed Will You Harbor Me for students at George Washington High School and Untitled for Washington Irving High School students, both under the auspices of Battery Dance Company’s Dance in Schools program. He is currently an Assisting Choreographer for “Ardent Song (Redux)”, for the Martha Graham Dance Company.

Tadej has taught extensively in the United States, Slovenia, UK, Germany and Scandinavia. He is a member of the faculty at the Martha Graham School of Contemporary Dance. He established and directed Teens @ Graham (2003-06), a year round after school dance program at the Martha Graham School and has been a teaching artist in the New York City public schools. In 2007 he was appointed as an Education Director of the Downtown Dance Festival as well as a Workshops Coordinator for the Martha Graham Dance Company, all in New York.

He is a recipient of the 1993 Benetton Dance Award and the 2003 Eugene Loring Award. As a student, Tadej was awarded a full scholarship at the Martha Graham School of Contemporary Dance as well as by actor Gregory Peck. He was a recipient of a three-year grant, given by the Ministry of Culture from Republic of Slovenia.
