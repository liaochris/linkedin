Kumar Rajamani’s Education

  • Universität Bern

    PhD, Biomedical Engineering, 2002 — 2006

    Dissertation titled "Statistical Deformable Models in Computer Assisted Surgery for Estimation of 3D Anatomy from Sparse Data "
    A majority of planning and navigational guidance during computer assisted orthopedic surgery uses 3D models of anatomy. A common approach for this is to use imaging such as CT. The disadvantage with imaging is that they are expensive and/or induce radiation to the patient. Surgeries such as total hip arthoplasty (THA) and total knee arthoplasty (TKA) may not warrant a pre or intra-operative scan. The mental recognition of 3D structures from 2D views could be very demanding for the surgeon.
    The main goal of my thesis is the formulation and evaluation of techniques to construct a patient-specific three-dimensional model that provides an appropriate intra-operative visualization without the need for a pre or intra-operative imaging.
