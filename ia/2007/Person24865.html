Dominique R. Antonini’s Summary

Dominique R. Antonini is Chief, Aviation Security (AVSEC) and Facilitation (FAL) Specifications and Guidance Material (SGM) Section of the Air Transport Bureau (ATB) at the International Civil Aviation Organization (ICAO). In this function he is in charge of all ICAO core activities for aviation security (Annex 17, Security Manual, deliberative bodies, AVSEC Panel and other technical Groups), Facilitation (Annex 9, Facilitation Manual, deliberative bodies and FAL Panel) and Machine Readable Travel Documents / MRTDs (technical specifications and advisory groups).

He joined ICAO in 1992 as an AVSEC Officer and undertook technical evaluations, training and assistance missions. In 1997 he was appointed as an AVSEC Training Officer responsible for the development of the ICAO AVSEC training programme, Aviation Security Training Packages (ASTPs) and the Aviation Security Training Centres (ASTCs). In 1999 he was appointed as Chief, Aviation Security Specifications and Guidance Material Section and in 2000 as Chief, Aviation Security Section in charge of all aviation security related programmes ranging from ICAO core activities such as Assembly, Council, Panels, maintenance of Annex 17 and the Security Manual, new and emerging threats (such as MANPADS), development of the ICAO Training programme (ASTPs and new e-learning programme) to the provision of direct assistance to States (technical evaluations, training and assistance) via the AVSEC Mechanism and from 2002 the ICAO AVSEC Plan of Action.

Prior joining ICAO, Mr. Antonini was working in the AVSEC field in Research and Development programmes, national inspections, equipment and airport design issues in the French Civil Aviation Authority.

Mr. Antonini holds a Degree in Civil Aviation Engineering, Air Transport, from the Ecole Nationale de l’Aviation Civile (ENAC) in Toulouse, France (promotion-1987) after high level mathematical studies.
