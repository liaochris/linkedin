Tom Stone’s Summary

I've been an artist for much of my life. During my youth, I studied the cello and was a musician-singer-visual artist. In 1991, I received a BFA degree from Hartford Art School, W. Hartford, CT with a major in Sculpture. In 1995, I completed an advanced study of life sized and monumental sculpting through an Apprenticeship/Internship at a Fine Art Foundry called The Johnson Atelier. In 1996, I took classes at Union County College, Cranford, NJ to study Computer graphics, video editing and 3D modeling. In 1997, I joined a printing company as a color correction specialist. By 2005, I received an MFA degree in Computer Art with an emphasis on 3D Animation. From my experiences at the printing company, I used my flatbed scanning skills to create my MFA Thesis animation and combined this with After Effects to produce an abstract experimental animation.

Since June of 2005, I've been teaching various disciplines of computer art at a 2 year vocational school in Raleigh, NC. While there, I continue to learn and centralize my professional interests in animating with After Effects and 3D modeling using Maya. Since June of 2007, I've been developing instructional video's for the President of the School of Communication Arts.
