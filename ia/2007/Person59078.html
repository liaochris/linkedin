Michelle Ulrich’s Summary

As a Virtual Professional for five years, I have been extremely active in getting the word out about Virtual Assistance. My involvement in the community college arena led me to public speaking where I was invited to speak in Vienna, Austria to give a presentation on Virtual Assistance. The Business Education Statewide Advisory Committee (BESAC) works collaboratively on curriculum standards throughout the state of California. Last year we developed community college standards for various degrees and certificates, including the Virtual Assistant certificate program.

Through my involvement with the BESAC, speaking, and working as a Virtual Professional, I was recently offered the opportunity to teach a VA certificate program online. Out of this experience, I have decided to start a new company that will launch in Spring 2008. It will bridge the gap between business leaders who want to get into teaching and the educational standards for teaching virtually (online).

Working with coaching clients has been the highlight of my career. I feel very connected to my clients and helping them become more successful through our work together is extremely gratifying. We specialize in helping authors, coaches and speakers who struggle to keep up with e-commerce and new technologies, so they can fulfill their passion!
