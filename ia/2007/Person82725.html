  • Assistant Project Manager

    GAM3 Arabic Street Basketball Project

    (Non-Profit; 1-10 employees; Program Development industry)

    February 2007 — Present (6 months)

    The GAM3 Arabian Street Basketball Project (ASBP) in Cairo and Beirut is an urban community based street basketball project. A central aspect of the project is the ‘sport-for-all’ approach that GAM3 takes towards street basketball. The focus in this street level project will be on the cultural meeting that rises and is settled in and around the common language of sports. The project endeavours to reduce the mental distance between the participants through sub-cultural street basketball activities. The activities will bring together the participants around the common values and norms that thrive in sports across borders and local expressions. The project strives to involve participants from all stratums of society, especially low-income classes.

    As a project assistant I assist the Middle East Coordinator in:
    - Overseeing the activities in Cairo
    - Marketing and PR
    - Production and updating of website (www.GAM3.dk/ASBP)
    - Project development
    - Budgeting
    - Fundraising

  • Volunteer

    GAM3

    (Sports industry)

    February 2006 — January 2007 (1 year)

    GAM3 is an integration project funded by the Danish Ministry of Refugee, Immigration and Integration Affairs. It is a non-profit organisation that uses a fusion of street basketball and hip-hop music as a tool to reach children in ghetto areas with mainly non-Danish ethnical background. For four months in the summer, young children and adolescents gather once a week at designated housing development playgrounds, to learn and practice the basics of street basketball. The purpose is to create a better dialogue and understanding between youngsters with different background.

    As a volunteer my main tasks were organising and communicating the project in the local area of Vesterbro, Copenhagen. I participated in practices, the monthly tounament and in the Finals, and I was the primary contact person on the ground. Furthermore, I was in charge of promoting the activities in the local area.
