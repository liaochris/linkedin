Jeri Cartwright’s Summary

Thanks for your interest!

I started my media relations business in 1996, working for a number of well-known national and local clients. In 2005, I had the honor of being selected as one of "30 Women to Watch" by Utah Business magazine. My company, Cartwright Communications, is the recipient of a 2002 "Giant Step" Small Business Award from the Salt Lake Chamber.

I've always strived to create and sustain positive, credible relationships with reporters. I'm a former journalist, worked as the evening anchor and reporter at KOLD TV (CBS affiliate) in Tucson, Arizona and also at KUTV (CBS affiliate) in Salt Lake City as an anchor, reporter and producer. My prior experience as vice president of communications for the Salt Lake Convention & Visitors Bureau and communications director for the Salt Lake County Commission (1990) laid the groundwork for some deep community insight.

I helped host the NBA All-Star Game and the U.S. Olympic Committee's Nagano Olympics media briefing event. Just a day prior to the 2002 Winter Olympics, Cartwright Communications helped Reebok host its annual Human Rights Awards in Salt Lake City. The winner of two Golden Spike awards for excellence in public relations, I also received a regional first-place award for Best Reporting from United Press International (UPI).

I love being active in Utah's executive associations. I am past president of the Utah Advertising Federation (UAF), past president of the Utah Chapter of the International Association of Business Communicators (IABC), current board member of the Greater Salt Lake Chapter of the Public Relations Society of America (PRSA), and also served on the boards of the Utah Better Business Bureau and Salt Lake City's Tracy Aviary.
