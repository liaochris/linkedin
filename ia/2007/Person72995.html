Rebecca MacKinnon’s Summary

I'm currently an Assistant Professor teaching "new media" - and everything related to the intersection between the Internet and journalism at Hong Kong University's Journalism and Media Studies Center.

From mid- 2004 to December 2006 I was a research fellow at Harvard Law School's Berkman Center for Internet and Society. (see http://cyber.law.harvard.edu/home), where I founded "Global Voices Online" (http://www.globalvoicesonline.org), a global citizens' media project. I also spent a lot of time writing, speaking about, and researching the nature of public dicourse on the Chinese Internet - as well as the issue of Chinese internet censorship and corporate responsibility.

Until January 2004 I worked for CNN in Japan and China, covering major events in Northeast Asia for more than a decade. My job positions included Tokyo Bureau Chief & Correspondent and Beijing Bureau Chief & Correspondent. In January 2004 I left CNN to be a media fellow at the Shorenstein Center for Press & Public Policy at Harvard's Kennedy School of Government, where I conducted a research project on new forms of internet-based, participatory journalism, and how the redefinition of journalism will change my job.

In addition to the Global Voices weblog I have a personal weblog at www.Rconversation.com.

My project examined the future of journalism as it is being redefined by weblogs and other forms of internet-based, participatory journalism.
