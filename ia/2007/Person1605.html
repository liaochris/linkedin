Adrian Chan’s Summary

Adrian Chan brings over a decade of experience in web development and user interface design to communication technologies and applications. He is currently engaged in an effort to define and advance social interaction design—a combination of user interface and interaction design with an understanding of social interactions and communication. The field is in the sweet spot of his professional efforts and his theoretical and philosophical interests.

The social interaction designer must go beyond the computer or device interface to the social interface. How does a technology transform communication? How well does it capture or convey attributes of interaction? Is it designed for interpersonal talk, or for community use?

Adrian's current projects include consulting to social software companies, blogging, and writing. He is working on a Framework for Social Interaction Design that will suggest design methodologies and, separately, a study of Social Software.

Adrian has been a web developer since 1995. Prior to that he was a content producer for CDRom projects that included game and educational titles. He has written a number of curriculum units for use in social studies classrooms, and was the content designer for the laser disc teacher's edition of Ken Burns' Civil War. Adrian is a regular participant in the Philosophical Reading Group at Stanford (hosted by Sepp Gumbrecht and Robert Harrison). He lives in San Francisco.

As Gravity7, Adrian has won several awards for his work online and with interactive media:

• 2003 Maggie Finalist Best Online Publication Home Page Design, Best Overall Online Design, Best Online Publication for Yoga Journal.
• 2002 Maggie Winner Best Online Publication for Yoga Journal
• Best Educational Technology Product The Civil War, by Ken Burns. Interactive Laser Disc.
