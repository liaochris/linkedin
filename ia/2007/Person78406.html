Steven E. Savage’s Summary

"I see no reason why my profession, my hobbies and interests, and my community service efforts cannot overlap."

I counsel clients in a wide variety of litigation practice areas, including intellectual property, constitutional law, mass tort, products liability, securities, antitrust, and white-collar investigations.

I have represented clients from entertainment, publishing, media, telecommunications, technology, automotive, hospital, pharmaceutical, chemical, insurance, hospitality, finance, and banking, and am experienced at resolving clients’ IP issues while addressing their business interests, specifically as they are affected by the development of Internet-related technologies.

My pro bono efforts have touched each jurisdiction to which I am admitted. I have worked with indigent artists, handled family custody issues, defended the rights of accused criminals, and restored the rights of recently released ex-offenders. As counsel to the NJ Office of the Child Advocate, I investigated the state’s Division of Youth and Family Services (DYFS) and its failure to discover four severely malnourished boys living with a foster family; this matter was covered by the national press and led to congressional hearings and court-ordered reform of DYFS and its constituent field offices. I am also a proud inaugural member of the Newark (NJ) Re-Entry Legal Services (ReLeSe) Network, a non-profit campaign designed to render free legal assistance to recently released ex-convicts to address their residual legal issues to facilitate their smooth transition into life as law-abiding residents of Newark. I became involved in this initiative at the invitation of Newark Mayor Cory Booker. I have been annually recognized for my pro bono efforts, most recently as a Patton Boggs LLP 2006 Pro Bono Award Honorable Mention for my work with immigrants, a famine relief organization in Africa, the Newark ReLese Network, and a DC-based housing organization for people living with AIDS.

Steven E. Savage’s Specialties:

Trademark and copyright litigation and dispute resolution; entertainment and media law and exploitation; mass and toxic tort litigation and dispute resolution.
