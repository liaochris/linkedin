Christopher Van Buren’s Summary

I am currently serving as a freelance blog editor and online content producer for a number of companies. I also establish syndication, promotion, and technical direction for their blog spaces.

I started my career in publishing by writing documentation for Epson America, Inc. I drafted the user’s manual for the Epson FX-80 dot-matrix printer in 1982 and then took a job as a writer and editor with CompuSoft Publishing, one of the first computer book publishing companies in the states.

In 1983, I was hired by Megahaus Corportation, as their publications manager.
I also co-wrote my first book in 1983 for Prentice-Hall, called Using 123 and the IBM PC. In the following years, I completed over 15 computer books, including Using Excel for the Macintosh (Que), The MacWorld Excel 5 Companion (IDG), You Can Do It With Windows (IDG), and The HTML Quick Reference (iUniverse).

In 1986 I started my own company publishing several for-pay newsletters, including The AppleWorks Journal and the Microsoft Works in Education Newsletter, co-published with Microsoft Corporation.

In 1994, I was invited to serve as literary agent for Waterside Productions, Inc., the largest computer book literary agency at the time. My projects included many best selling computer books, cookbooks and spiritual books. I served as literary agent for eight years.

In 1999, I co-wrote the book Poor Richard’s Creating E-Books, published by Top Floor Publishing in 2000. In 2004, I was contracted by Avalon Travel Publishing to write Moon Handbooks Brazil.
