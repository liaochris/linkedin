Ricardo Schiesser’s Summary

Product Lifecycle Management for Parcel Handling Equipment.

Development of Materials Handling products: conveyors, sorters, diverters, singulators and other equipment for transportation, accumulation, induction (singulation, gapping) and sortation in unit handling systems for distribution centers, airline baggage handling, parcel, work-in-process and other industries (16 years)

Development of military equipment (wheeled and tracked armoured vehicles, gun turrets, fire control systems) and off-road equipment (14 years)

Ricardo Schiesser’s Specialties:

Development from concept thru Development Engineering (incl. verification and validation testing) to release to Production and Sustaining Engineering of equipment with mechanical and electronic (HW & control SW) content. Experience in the areas of Defense (14 years) and Materials Handling (16 years).

  • Product Manager

  • Director of Advanced Technology, later promoted to Director of Product Center Sortation Systems

    Siemens L&A

    (Logistics and Supply Chain industry)

    2001 — 2006 (5 years)

    Advanced Technology was dedicated to feasibility studies and accelerated development of innovative products. Coordinating resources from within Siemens (Siemens TTB/Berkeley, Corp. Research/Princeton) with outsourced prototype manufacturing, the AT team successfully completed projects in record time. One such example is a Singulator using image processing to control servo-actuators in quasi real-time for distributed manipulation of parcels. This technology was later transferred to Europe, where some of these machines are installed and run daily the whole throughput of a large parcel hub in Germany.

    Product Center Sorters was the consolidated in-house department dedicated to Development and Sustaining Engineering for sortation, mech & controls HW+SW, from concept, design (incl.analysis like FMEA and prototyping for verification & validation tests) and other products (AGVs, Pick-to-Light). This particular position was responsible for a budget in excess of $3M (2006) and a staff of 36.

  • Manager, Product Development

    Rapistan

    (Privately Held; 1001-5000 employees; Machinery industry)

    1990 — 2001 (11 years)

    Rapistan was the largest global manufacturer of Materials Handling Equipment for distribution centers, airport baggage handling and other markets. In the 90's, it was acquired by Mannesmann Demag. Later, Siemens acquired Mannesmann and, after operating for a few years, Siemens split the Materials Handling company, keeping selected operations (Postal Automation, Parcel Systems) and selling others (this was the birth of Dematic, a spin-off from Siemens Dematic). Ricardo joined Rapistan in 1990, and has been in Product Development functions until 2007.

  • Manager, Product Development

    Engesa

    (Machinery industry)

    1976 — 1990 (14 years)

    Well known by military forces around the world at the time (early 1970's thru 1990), Engesa was among the largest manufacturers of wheeled armoured vehicles, having supplied thousands of EE-9 Cascavel reconnaissance vehicles and EE-11 Urutu amphibious armoured personnel carriers to armies of about 35 different countries. Ricardo has played a major role in Product Development during the expansion of Engesa's product line, including the development of gun turrets and tracked vehicles (battle tank EE-T1 Osorio and light tracked vehicle EE-T4). He has also participated in the development of civilian products (forestry and agricultural tractors, all-wheel-drive systems for trucks and the civilian version of Engesa's EE-4 military jeep).
