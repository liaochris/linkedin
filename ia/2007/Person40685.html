Prof. Jerald L. Feinstein’s Summary

Professor in the School of Business at the George Washington University, former Visiting Fellow at Harvard, and Managing Partner at Buzz Analytics providing services to public corporations and the government by data mining the Internet employing computerized techniques similar to those used by the World's Intelligence Agencies to scan, monitor, and harvest On-line Media for general Buzz, Opinion, Popularity, and Trends, on brands, organizations, products, and your competition in over 30 Languages, Regions, and Nations - http://www.buzzanalytics.com

Worked with the Naisbitt Group in futurist studies (John Naisbitt, Megatrends) to forecast the impact of rapidly changing technology on corporate profitability as well as material for the National Science Foundation's Leadership group.

Consultant to Fortune 100 Companies in the areas of telecommunications, emerging technology, and advanced information systems; and was formerly associated with Booz, Allen & Hamilton, The Stanford Research Institute of Stanford University, and MITRE (earlier, a part of MIT Lincoln Labs). Responsible for helping to deploy billion dollar telecommunications, information systems, and IT infrastructure upgrade initiatives.

He serves on the advisory boards of start-ups, teaches and develops courses in telecommunications and advanced networking technologies, Internet Commerce, and other technology courses within the MBA, Master of Science, and Executive Programs, and is recognized as an international authority in areas of Computer Security, Open-Source Information Mining, telecommunications, emrgging technologies, technology change management, and artificial intelligence, and served in senior management positions with profit center responsibility in several large corporations.

Guest lecturer at Harvard, Princeton, University of Pennsylvania, Oxford University, University of Virginia, and American University, and North American Editor of The Expert Systems Journal.
