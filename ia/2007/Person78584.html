Shabbir Safdar’s Summary

For most of my life I have used technology to organize people. In high school I used bulletin boards to create offline communities, because that was the technology available at the time. In college I used e-mail lists to organize against homophobia because all we had was e-mail lists. In the early nineties I used e-mail and gopher to support online free speech, and in the late 90's I used the web. I'm now working with mobile.

Although the technology changes, the answer is the same: people want to belong to meaningful movements they can feel passionate about and change something larger than themselves.

I suspect if civilization fell tomorrow I'd be doing this with carrier pigeons.

Shabbir Safdar’s Education

  • Purdue University

    B.S., Computer Science, 1985 — 1992

    I took the long way around to get a bachelor's degree from Purdue in West Lafayette, IN, stopping in the middle for a year and a half when I ran out of money, working full time from then on, living cheaply, and supplementing my income with blood plasma donations.

    In the early 1990's a West Lafayette city councilman, discovering his son was gay, introduced an ordinance protecting gays and lesbians from discrimination in housing and employment. The city erupted into a hate-filled debate, and I organized a silent protest that garnered support for the measure from the centrists, and made the opponents look like the extremists they were. Although voted on in the dead of night, the measure passed with several council members abstaining.

    I have to say that although I did a lot of great technical work at Purdue, that organizing experience is one of the things I am most proud of in my life from that time period. For a short time, I helped make the world a better, more tolerant place.
