Professor John Walker’s Summary

In the month of April 2007 appointed as a Visiting Professor at an academic institution in the discipline areas of Infomatics, Computing, Science and Technology.

Currently working as an evangelist in the areas of Zero Day Threats, and Rootkits, and their interlinking to and with Cyber Crime and Computer Forensics. This work is based on long term research encompassing a period from 2000 to date, utilizing materials and contacts from the worlds of academic research, Black Hat, DefCon, and specialist groups like 2600.

Recognizing that in Corporate, and the SME community, e-Discovery, and the understanding of what Forensic Security 'really' is has some significant gaps, recent work and research in Computer Law, Computer Forensics, and Investigations, covering all areas of technology, from PC, Phone, Hand Held device, and more challenging technological solutions like U3 and am developing low cost solutions to meet the expecations of Computer Forensic Investigations, and what i term Distance Forensics (Internet based).

Held Management Security Positions, and involved with Technical hands on IT Security posts for 20 years working in USA, Germany, South America, Turkey, Belgium, Netherlands, Ireland, Italy, consulting, and deploying security solutions.

In the last 3 years I have been engaged with researching, investigating, and developing solutions and practices to counter eCrime, eFraud, and eTerror, and have, and am, working with both Commercial Organisations, and Government Agencies alike. In this area, I have worked on the areas of bleeding edge eCrime/Fraud looking forward to the aspect of emerging threats, a situation, which I am currently providing consultations on.

I have written and presented 30 papers on IT Security, and have presented International to technical and academic delegates.

Support community projects, and funding/supporting Local Community Web Site in the village of Breaston.
