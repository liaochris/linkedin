Steve Waterhouse’s Summary

Steve Waterhouse is a technology entrepreneur with over 14 years of startup experience. He is currently consulting for a number of startup companies in the San Francisco Bay Area and Europe.

During 2005 and 2006 Steve worked as Entrepreneur in Residence at Foundation Capital in Menlo Park, California. His responsibilities included finding and introducing early stage companies to Foundation Capital and vetting new financing deals.

From 2001 to 2005, Steve was a Director at Sun Microsystems where he invented a new storage technology, code named Honeycomb. He founded a new product group at Sun and hired and managed the Honeycomb team from inception to product in 2005. Before starting Honeycomb, Steve ran the engineering team for Sun’s open source peer-to-peer research project – JXTA. He was also VP of Engineering and founding member of Infrasearch, a highly innovative peer to peer and web services startup which was acquired by Sun in 2001. From 1997 to 2000 Steve ran an elite data analytics consulting company whose clients included eBay, NASA and Cygnus Biosciences.

Steve holds a PhD in Engineering from the University of Cambridge in England where he specialized in the development of speech recognition systems using advanced pattern recognition technologies. He also holds a MPhil and MA from Cambridge in Engineering and Computer Science.
