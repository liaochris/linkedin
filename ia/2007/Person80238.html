Steve Kosakura’s Summary

Dedicated professional with thirty years experience in motion picture visual effects, imaging technology, theme park control systems, project management and administration.

Steve Kosakura’s Specialties:

Electronics, Motion Control, Opto-Electronics, 3D Digitizing, Real / Virtual Spatial Integration and Technology R & D

California State Fire Marshal - Licensed Special Effects Pyrotechnician
US Dept of Treasury - ATF High Explosives (Transport) License
California State Contractor's Board - C-7 and C-10 Contractor's License

Steve Kosakura’s Experience

  • Senior Engineer

    Walt Disney Imagineering - Special Effects / Creative Blue Sky

    (Entertainment industry)

    September 1998 — Present (9 years 3 months)

    Currently employed designing and producing electronics for the WDI Special Effects Shop and project design / development support for the Creative Department - Blue Sky Technology Group. Activities include a diverse combination of technology development projects including projection, media serving, show control systems, and interactive systems.

  • VFX Camera Dept. Engineer

    Walt Disney Feature Animation

    (Motion Pictures and Film industry)

    April 1996 — August 1998 (2 years 5 months)

    Responsible for design and construction of motion control and location cinematography grip equipment. Co-designed and co-fabricated Dino-Cam - a motion controlled cable suspended "aerial" camera rig that could span 1000 feet and travel at 35 MPH that was designed and built with Dwayne McClintock.

  • Electronics / Motion Control Engineer

    Sony Pictures Imageworks

    (Motion Pictures and Film industry)

    October 1995 — April 1996 (7 months)

    Electronics and Stage support for numerous motion picture projects.

  • Electronics Engineer / Stage Support

    Digital Domain

    (Motion Pictures and Film industry)

    1993 — 1995 (2 years)

    Electronic design and construction for the Stages from DD's inception. Worked to put the stages together with John Fante, Mark Moore, Scott Salsa. Involvement with True Lies, Interview with the Vampire, Color of Night and Strange Days, models, miniatures and motion control.

  • Electronics Engineer / Stage Support

    mass.illusion

    (Motion Pictures and Film industry)

    1994 — 1995 (1 year)

    Returned to the Berkshires to work on Judge Dredd visual effects with mass.illusion and for VFX plate cinematography and control of a large 6DOF McFadden motion base in England.

  • Electronics / Motion Control Engineer

    Warner Brothers

    (Motion Pictures and Film industry)

    1995 — 1995 (less than a year)

    Assembled and supported stop motion stages built for Mars Attacks under Dan Radford.

  • Electronics / Motion Control Engineer

    OutTakes, Inc

    (Entertainment industry)

    1992 — 1993 (1 year)

    Designed and built motion control camera system for automated photo compositing system in an entertainment / retail venue (Universal CityWalk).

  • Electronics / Motion Control Engineer / Cameraman / Pyrotechnician

    Boss Film Studios

    (Motion Pictures and Film industry)

    1989 — 1992 (3 years)

    Design and construction of camera systems and devices for motion and camera tracking. Designed and built robotic camera systems for motion control cinematography and extensively supported the model and miniature stages. Boss Films was one of the premier visual effects houses in the industry.

  • Electronics / Motion Control Engineer

    Berkshire Motion Pictures

    (Motion Pictures and Film industry)

    1989 — 1989 (less than a year)

    Provided motion control and motion base electronics support for the Back to the Future Ridefilm. This project was lead by Doug Trumbull with Chris Reyna as Technical Director and David K. Stewart - DP. The entire project was shot using very special 65MM camera systems and extensive motion control and mechanical gags throughout.

  • Electronic Technician

    Tondreau Electronics

    (Privately Held; Motion Pictures and Film industry)

    1985 — 1989 (4 years)

    Designed and built motion control electronics for numerous clients under Bill Tondreau, based out of Robert Abel and Associates & Image G. Clients included RA&A, ILM, Boss, Rhythm & Hues, Praxis Films, Title House, Graphic Films, Hollywood / Tokyo, etc., etc., etc.

  • Animation & Live Action Cameraman / Equipment Designer

    Calico Creations

    (Privately Held; Motion Pictures and Film industry)

    1982 — 1985 (3 years)

    Shot motion control multi-pass in-camera composite animation, multi-plane, optical printer and live action projects for this special effects / animation house in Northridge. Also designed and built several pieces of equipment used in daily production at the facility.

  • Show Design Technician

    The Showpros, JMB Associates

    (Privately Held; Media Production industry)

    1979 — 1982 (3 years)

    Programmed Multi-Media productions that involved multi image, film, video and live cues. AVL based control systems and graphics design with Marron Carrel compositing camera.
