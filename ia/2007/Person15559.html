Chad Eggelton’s Summary

Chad possesses strong business, data modelling and data warehousing skills initially developed in the mining and primary industries sector. He has enhanced his skill base with 3 years in the highly competitive and data intensive environment of one of the world’s leading airlines. He then broadened his experience while in the position of Senior Business Intelligence Consultant at Australia’s largest financial institution, The Commonwealth Bank of Australia (CBA) and most recently team leading and designing the migration and development of reporting systems for Barclays Bank in London.
In 2004 he completed two short term contracts in Australia. The most recent of which involved the delivery of a GL Data mart & Reporting Solution from Oracle Financials for Boral Construction materials. Chad took a leading role on this project, managing external consultants and co-ordinating internal IT groups to deliver the reporting requirements of the financial management team. The reporting system delivered substantial cost savings to the organisation by allowing them to phase out 100’s of Oracle reports in the upgrade from Oracle 9i to 11i.
At CBA, Chad was initially responsible for standardising and implementing a robust Cognos Business Intelligence environment to meet the Banks expanding Business Intelligence requirements. More recently he developed business critical data-marts for Investment & Insurance deploying this information to approximately 250 users using OLAP technology.
He expanded his Business Intelligence experience at Qantas in Customer Marketing, Airports Management and Service Centre projects. He was part of a team that developed a Bookings Reporting System for the Qantas Commercial Analysis function, which enhanced Qantas’ competitive advantage in the market place.
In 1994 Chad was awarded the Mathematics & Buss. Systems Cadetship with BHP. This involved studying part time whilst working full time in various IT & business related roles.
