Justin Tauber’s Summary

I have a twelve years of teaching experience, from primary to tertiary, in formal and informal settings. My academic home has been Philosophy, but in 2007, I will be teaching the Online Media course in Media & Communications at USYD.

Working specifically in e-learning over the last four years, I have developed my understanding of using LMSs and standards-based web design to achieve educational objectives.

I have long played a de facto KM role within my team, developing internal resources including knowledge management strategies, CRM, internet and intranet development.

In the coming months, I'll be cultivating a wiki community dedicated to educational design through a secondment to the META Centre in the Faculty of Arts.

Justin Tauber’s Experience

  • Sessional Lecturer in Online Media

    University of Sydney

    (Educational Institution; 11-50 employees; Higher Education industry)

    July 2007 — Present (6 months)

    The Online Media course is a third-year unit, aimed at giving Media students an introduction to new media studies and the practical web-production skills required to participate in a contemporary media organisation. The course covers everything from blogging as a form of journalism to open source production values, as well as basic Dreamweaver, CSS and online production management skills.

  • Project Manager

    META Centre, University of Sydney

    (Educational Institution; 1-10 employees; E-Learning industry)

    July 2007 — Present (6 months)

    I am responsible for co-developing and team teaching the Arts & the Social Web program, introducing academics in the Arts to Web 2 technologies.

    I have also initiated a project aimed at cultivating a wiki-based elearning community (called the Test Tube) for lecturers and educational designers across Sydney University to share educational know-how. The hope is to create a grassroots pattern language for tertiary education.
