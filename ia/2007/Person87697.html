Troy Rutman’s Summary

I have worked in communications for 12 years, with public sector experience that extends from education and economic policy to federal regulation. I am an excellent writer, researcher and project manager. In addition, my Harvard Law education has taught me to investigate exhaustively, to build a convincing argument, and to communicate logically, passionately and economically.

I spent years at Golin Harris Communications in New York, where I was selected to join McKinsey & Co. to explore the role of communications in raising the international investment profile of the nation of Suriname. My work also included included reputation and crisis management, investor relations, brand planning, publicity, and events. I’ve crafted plans to protect the value of some of the world's great brands: MasterCard, McDonald's, Coca-Cola, Disney, Lipton, AOL Time Warner, Keebler, MGM and more.

I consider art to be a link between public and private good. To that end, I've promoted Broadway, music, and films from the art-house to the multiplex. My corporate work for McDonald's centered on its Gospelfest inner city fundraiser, and I worked with Coca-Cola to promote the arts among urban children.

I now run the communications department of a marketing agency, where I’ve tripled margins, created a new procedural infrastructure, improved ROI for the existing client roster, and ported the agency to new sectors like government and health care.

My professional goals are to focus on substantive policy issues, to learn while I teach, always to write, and to create wealth, in its many definitions, with a sharp and well-rounded collection of peers
