Jamie Scarborough’s Summary

My first job was selling carpet and rugs on my uncle’s market stall in East London. I was 14 and it was always cold and wet out. I loved it.

Six years passed with me selling everything from steak-knives in London pubs (bad idea – don’t try it!) to beer and wine in bars all over the world (great idea – try it!). I found myself in Canada at 20 looking to make my fortune. Instead, I got a job with an advertising agency as a Copywriter and by 25 was a rising star Creative Director for the agency (great title, terrible wage).

Something had to change. It did.

The new VP of Sales asked me to join her team. I spent the next 2 years selling into the Vancouver market. I had some big successes, some miserable failures, and I was well thought of by the company (I won Sales Person of the Year). But I knew there was a lot more to learn, so I joined Monster.com in Toronto.

For the first time I was mixing with strong sales people. The next 5 years were a Doctorate in Sales. I learnt about cold-calling, presenting and negotiations. I learnt about process, discovery and consultation. I learnt why sales is more about empathy than about fast-talking. I was Sales Person of the Year – twice! More than that, I became a professional sales person.

By the time my 5 years with Monster were up, I was managing the entire Canadian field sales team and Monster had emerged as the Canadian market leader.

I was recruited back to Vancouver as the VP of Sales for Hcareers.com. They let me completely overhaul their sales operations and – 1 year in – I was ready for my next challenge.

Today I am one of the owners of Sales Talent Agency. A Canadian company that finds quality sales people excellent sales roles. I love being back in the sales seat.

At 20, I hoped to retire at 25. Now, in my mid-30’s, I hope I never have to retire. For the love of sales.
