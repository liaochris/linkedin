Stephen Ferber’s Experience

  • President

    Golden Gate BPO Solutions, LLC

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Outsourcing/Offshoring industry)

    July 2005 — Present (2 years 5 months)

    United States’ subsidiary company of a call center and BPO company listed on the Philippine Stock Exchange and majority owned by of one of Southeast Asia’s largest conglomerates. Golden Gate BPO Solutions’ mission is to create a brand in the United States and to successfully grow its call center and BPO businesses with operations currently located in the Philippines and Malaysia. Key components of this successful effort include marketing, business development, corporate development, account management and other activities.

  • Managing Director

    CrossKeys Capital, LLC (Formerly Apex Partners)

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Investment Banking industry)

    July 2004 — January 2007 (2 years 7 months)

    Investment Banking, primarily sell and buy side engagements for companies in many industries, including call center, BPO, technology, healthcare and internet. Operational and business development consulting for several US companies and international companies in the call center, BPO, healthcare and other service-related industries.

  • Executive Vice President and General Counsel

    Precision Response Corporation ("PRC")

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; IACI; Outsourcing/Offshoring industry)

    January 1996 — September 2003 (7 years 9 months)

    Established and managed a legal department as well as created and executed the strategic direction of PRC as a provider of Business Processing Outsourcing, Fulfillment, and Warehousing and Technology services. Co-managed the company with the President and CEO and took full responsibility for the legal affairs of the company as General Counsel. Fully responsible for strategic planning and implementation (including mergers and acquisitions), international business development and operations, affiliate business development and operations, information technology and services and overall sales and business development. Restructured the company in many aspects and drove and supported the growth of the company during a period of severe industry decline. Managed seven direct reports and 24 production and service facilities throughout the United States and Asia.

  • President

    Florida Manufacturing and Distributing Company

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Furniture industry)

    August 1994 — December 1995 (1 year 5 months)

    Initiated and managed all aspects of a successful business turnaround of a family-owned furniture manufacturing, importing and distribution company with rapidly declining revenues, extreme losses and on the verge of bankruptcy.

  • Senior Associate - Financial Advisory Services Group

    PriceWaterhouseCoopers (Formerly Coopers & Lybrand)

    (Privately Held; 10,001 or more employees; Management Consulting industry)

    June 1991 — August 1994 (3 years 3 months)

    Conducted comprehensive financial and operational analysis of various business enterprises in Chapter 11 or needing business turnaround assistance and/or direction and implemented plans along with company management. Assisted companies with corporate reorganizations, mergers and acquisitions, capital sourcing and business valuations. Prepared and reviewed all types of tax returns as well as researched and advised the firm’s partners, managers and clients with respect to the legal and tax implications of various business transactions, including business combinations, and corporate structures.
