    (Educational Institution; Myself Only; Research industry)

    February 2007 — August 2007 (7 months)

    My Master Thesis asks the research question; What is the innovation potential of Virtual Worlds; Exemplified by IBM's 3D Internet & V-Business initiative.

    My choice of research question was due to my prior first hand virtual world experience going back to 1997, my awareness of Second Life that began in early 2006 during my 6 month Silicon Valley adventure at Institute for the Future, and the fact that Innovation Lab and IBM Denmark was doing business in this area.

    To answer the research question, I took a deep dive with key IBM Second Life avatars, conducted data collection with the Metaverse Evangelists at IBM Hursley outside London, and numerous key personal of IBM's newly formed 3D Internet & Virtual Business Opportunity Group present at the Virtual World 2007 conference in New York.

    IBM is really a great organization and I can't thank them enough for giving me this opportunity, of acquiring, what I believe is currently the best research data in the world.

  • Other foresighted individuals who contributed ideas

    Metaverse Roadmap - Pathway to the 3D Web

    (Non-Profit; 11-50 employees; Research industry)

    May 2007 — May 2007 (1 month)

    Over the past year the Acceleration Studies Foundation (ASF) and its supporting foresight partners have explored the virtual and 3D future of the World Wide Web in a first-ofits-kind cross-industry public foresight project, the Metaverse Roadmap (MVR).

    The MVR has “near-term” anticipation horizon of ten years (to 2017), a “longer-term” speculation horizon of twenty years (to 2025), and a charter to discover early indicators of significant developments ahead. Seeking diverse points of view, our process included an invitational Metaverse Roadmap Summit, public and expert surveys, a few workshops and
    roundtables at major U.S. conferences, social meetups, and a public wiki. Many helpful people from the IT, virtual worlds, professional, academic, futurist, and lay communities contributed ideas to the MVR.
