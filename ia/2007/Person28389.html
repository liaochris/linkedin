Ev Nucci’s Summary

At heart I'm an entrepreneur. I was an Executive with two Fortune 500 companies, involved with three start-ups during my tenure, and ran 15 operating companies.

A visionary and pioneer, I struck out on my own. Over the next four years we shifted the paradigm and revolutionized an industry until tragedy struck when my youngest son was born with heart and lung disease. Suddenly you get what life is all about. I sold my company a year later.

My brother suggested the retained search business. I hated recruiters, I hired them for 15 years. They were worse than used car salesmen.

But, if I could provide clients with what I needed. Strategy. Vision. Objectivity. Integrity. Trust. A True Partner. Industry Intelligence. A Unique Perspective. Built On Experience. Emotional Distance. Long Term Solutions. No Short Term Short Cuts. Positive Influences. Management Objectivity. Calculated Risk. Passion. Wisdom. Mistakes Made On My Dime. Failure.

A friend was Chairman of a Wall Street firm as he asked me to help him out. I started in the institutional asset management business and learned everything about the products, companies, business models, operations, culture, and the industry.

How much intelligence does it take to hire 1000 people when the market is up, then fire 500 people when the market is down versus build an organization to sustain multiple economic cycles?

Which is harder? Recruit people by paying them a whole lot of money or build a culture than retains talent?

And what motivates? Passion or Money? We have to be passionate about what we do, and the vision. And the culture. That is the thread that weaves an organization together. Money is nothing more than the by-product of passion.

I build high performaning organizations. Only those I think have the capacity to be great. Like BlackRock. I've worked with them for the last 10 years. I'm looking for the next one. I also help people's dreams come true.
