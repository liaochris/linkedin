Tambra R. Stevenson’s Summary

With a rich background in communications, research and policy, Ms. Stevenson develops recommendations and initiatives on women's quality of life within the District.

Prior to the Mayor's office, she handled public affairs the U.S. Department of Commerce covering key issues: Rebuilding the Gulf, Trade and the President's American Competitiveness Initiative. Instrumental in developing media opportunities for the National Director on minority business, she planned press conferences and developed the Agency's materials. Featured as a 'rising star' in 'Federal Computer Week' Magazine, she help to build the Young Government Leaders brand.

As an Emerging Leader in the HHS Secretary's office, she managed projects on mental health, pandemic flu, diabetes and small business. Also her volunteerism and leadership with Combined Federal Campaign was recognized with the Secretary’s Leadership Award for achieving 200% of the donor goals. Prior to HHS, she worked in academic, private and nonprofit sectors on media/marketing projects at CBS, WGBH-TV, Harvard Magazine, Hill Holiday, Kelley Chunn PR, Tufts School of Medicine, and Boston Black Women’s Health Institute.

She received a master's in health communication from Tufts Medical School and completed MBA coursework at Boston University. She obtained her B.S. in nutrition/premed and Spanish from Oklahoma State University. She studied and practiced community health in Dominican Republic on the David L. Boren National Security Education Scholarship. She received a certificate at New York University in partnership with the World Health Organization.

She serves on the Washington Health Empowerment Coalition and chairs public affairs for Alpha Kappa Alpha Sorority's Xi Omega Chapter. She is a member of American Public Health Association, Public Relations Society of America, and federal advisory panel on HIV/AIDS prevention and women and is a proud mentor.
