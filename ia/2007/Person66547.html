Paul Gage’s Experience

  • Strategy Director

    Carat

    (Public Company; 201-500 employees; Marketing and Advertising industry)

    January 2000 — Present (7 years 5 months)

    At Carat, I helped set up and ultimately run the Data Planning department with James Northway, which now has over 25 employees and contributes over 20% of Carat's income.

    From 2005, I now work across clients and teams as a Strategy Director. I am responsible for integrating data and research techniques into communications planning for our clients. This means having a consumer / customer centric view and a channel inclusive approach.

    I help our clients consider how they should communicate with known customers through a variety of channels and help them evaluate how multiple touchpoints combine to change behaviour.

    Since 2000, primary accounts have been Royal Mail, Renault, Asda, Abbey National, BAA, Vodafone, Churchill, Dell, ntl and Philips.

    At Carat, I invented and developed Microcosm - a way of fusing Carat's attitudinal and media consumption survey with customer databases.

  • Analyst

    dunnhumby

    (Marketing and Advertising industry)

    1997 — 2000 (3 years)

    I joined dunnhumby as a graduate analyst. My role was to analyse customer data to inform marketing initiatives such as CRM, pricing and promotions. I worked on key accounts such as Tesco, Tesco Personal Finance, Sky, B&Q, C&A, East Midland Energy, Cable & Wireless. Analysis was completed using SAS.

    Tesco was my lead account, and it was an exciting time for both dunnhumby and Tesco as we were breaking new ground in what customer data could mean for businesses - as described in the book 'Scoring Points' by Clive Humby and Terry Hunt.

    Specifically, I designed the 'Shopping Habits' segmentation for Tesco, and built the original product targeting models that led to over 250,000 variations of the Clubcard statement.

    In my last year, I worked with Chris Lawer and Mark Hinds to write the business plan for selling the Clubcard data reports and analysis to FMCG manufacturers - a very profitable part of Tesco / dunnhumby, now being replicated by many other retailers in the world.
