Barry Eagar’s Summary

I'm an Australian and New Zealand patent and trade mark attorney. My partner, Michael Buck and I started Eagar & Buck about 6 years ago.

I've been involved with patents and trade marks for 17 years.

My "in" to patents was a degree in Mechanical Engineering followed by a year and a half in industry, during which I started studying law.

At the moment, I am passionate about teaching entrepreneurs how important it is to protect their software inventions. The narrow-mindedness of the "open source" community is particularly frustrating. There is no reason why a software product should not be regarded as a form of engineering product. After all, that's why they call it "software engineering".

On the other hand, I enjoy seeing the rapid growth in patent applications covering software products. Also, many patent jurisdictions are beginning to accept that software patents are here to stay. The second phase is now to look at ways to speed up prosecution. Nowhere is this more needed than in the US, where software patent application filers are being held back from enforcing their rights due to the slow pace of prosecution.

I have lectured from time to time at Australian universities, mostly to post-graduate students. I have also delivered presentations to the Australian Computer Society and to the Intellectual Property Society of Australia and New Zealand (IPSANZ) on software and business method patents.

A high point for me was delivering a talk at the American Bar Association's IP summer conference in Boston in June 2006. The talk covered patent prosecution and patentable subject matter in Australia. Material related to the talk can be found at my bazpat blog.

My bazpat blog keeps clients, associates and other subscribers up to date on Australian patent developments. I am also using it as part of my ongoing campaign to illustrate the importance of software patents.
