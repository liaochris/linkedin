Jenni Seppä’s Summary

My strength as a designer and my professional enthusiasm are to be seen strongest in inventing ideas, when I have an opportunity for questioning the functionality, meanings and problems of existing solutions, and according to that, create new concepts, possibilities and values for products.

Another exciting phase is form giving: investigating differences while changing small details, searching for right proportions, finding the characteristics of each material and finishing the end result.

These two factors, inspiring concept and deep delving into form, together create the main thread to maintain interest during the whole project.

My effort as a designer is to develop a deeper understanding of my liability, possibilities and responsibility as a designer. In addition to develop in areas of giving form, material knowledge, project management, team working, convincing presenting and 3D-modeling, to name a few, I am also looking forward to get deeper knowledge of sustainable design.

Many products are ethically corrects without grand sacrifices concerning form giving or material choices. Ecological products do not need to be unesthetics. It is possible to make a difference with small acts – just only being aware of one’s choices.

My goal is not to become an eco-designer in particular but to internalise environmentally friendly principals in a way that in my future works ecological aspects will be considered in habit, sometimes even without being aware of it.

I comprehend an ethically correct product as an article that carries in it more than
just ecological values. Behind material and producing choices, human rights questions, product recycling possibilities and life cycle concerns there is an opportunity to carry – or resist to carry - the responsibility and authority that our profession include. As a designer I am privileged because I have a possibility to make an effect, not just on my own consumption habits, but also on other peoples’ habits.
