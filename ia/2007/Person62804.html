  • Layer1 DSP Software Engineer

    Texas Instruments

    (Privately Held; 10,001 or more employees; Telecommunications industry)

    January 2006 — July 2007 (1 year 7 months)

    Audio Software Framework (ASF) Team

    Barcelona Project

    This family of processors targets mobile phone applications (2G/3G). This product is structured around ARM11 and TI C55 DSP. ASF is a software component made for the management of the audio streams, both primary (voice) and secondary (voice memo, tones, key beeps) of a mobile phone.
    My work was first centered around upgrading ASF to 16 kHz sampling frequency (keeping 8 kHz available for « narrow-band » vocoders) thus greatly improving the perceived audio quality. Meanwhile, I have also integrated AMR-WB vocoder inside ASF (both primary and secondary streams).
    Then I had to develop a new software abstraction layer in order to support all of our hardware interfaces such as MCSI, DMA or AVC.
    Finally I have worked on a synchronization issue between our Acoustic Echo Canceller and ASF. This issue involved all of the different packages inside ASF requiring a strong theoretical study plus a pretty tricky development step.

  • Senior DSP Software Engineer

    ATMEL

    (Privately Held; 5001-10,000 employees; Semiconductors industry)

    2000 — January 2006 (6 years)

    Internet Appliance Processors Application Group

    SIAP Project (Smart Internet Appliance Processor)

    This processor family targets applications such as VoIP terminals or gateways. It is structured around ARM7/ARM9 cores and OAK/TEAK DSP core (ParthusCeva).
    I was in charge of developing and supporting the embedded DSP real-time software offer. My work was focused on the integration of speech and audio processing algorithms (such as ITU-T G.711, G.723.1, G.729, Acoustic Echo Cancellation) and their associated device driver for µC-linux operating system.
    I was also involved in the development/integration of VoIP software library (SIP for signaling layer and RTP for transport layer).

    During the year 2004, my personal involvement in the customization of our standard DSP offer, allowed ATMEL to win a 2M$ contract with a Taiwanese company.

  • DSP Software Engineer

    ATMEL

    (Telecommunications industry)

    1999 — 2000 (1 year)

    ASSP Application Group

    DTAD Project (Digital Telephone Answering Device)

    This product integrates all the functions of an up-market phone. I was in charge of the telephony and speech processing algorithms implementation (DTMF, Caller ID, echo cancellation, speech coding) and in charge of the test against telecom standards.
    This advanced work was performed on a proprietary embedded fixed-point architecture DSP. The vocoder has been designed in cooperation with Berkeley University and the result was a 2 kbps stream with outstanding quality (personal achievement: a gain of 25% on the bit rate).
