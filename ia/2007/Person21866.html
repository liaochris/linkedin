David Giebink’s Summary

Created Inscite Inc. in 1999 to develop new business opportunities with the science and technology communities in the countries of the former Soviet Union.

Shortly after founding Inscite Inc., took position as the Director of Industry Programs at the U.S. Civilian Research and Development Foundation (CRDF).

Returned to Inscite Inc. in 2007 to focus solely on the tremendous opportunities available to business through global innovation and entrepreneurship, in particular from emerging economies.

Extensive experience in the global development of science and technology driven enterprises.

One of two senior U.S. scientists assigned by the U.S. Department of Energy and U.S. Department of State to assist in the creation and early years of operation of the International Science and Technology Center (ISTC) in Moscow, Russia

Advised major firms on issues related to nonproliferation, arms control, and international business development.

Freelance software-development engineer and business-development consultant in Munich, Germany.

M.S. in electrical engineering from Stanford University. Ph.D. in physics from the University of Texas. Doctoral research in theoretical physics as an Associated Western Universities Fellow at the Los Alamos National Laboratory.

Research and teaching positions at the Center for Theoretical Physics at the University of Maryland, the Physics Department at Texas A&M University, and the Institut de Physique Nucléaire, Orsay Cedex of the Centre National de la Recherche Scientifique in France.

Scientist in the neutron measurements group at the Los Alamos National Laboratory. Member of team conducting neutron-imaging experiments at the Nevada Test Site. Member of tteam that developed GEONET, an initiative for a multinational, multi­disciplinary program to understand and address environmental issues using the expertise of weapons scientists and engineers.

Languages: German and Russian, in addition to native English.
