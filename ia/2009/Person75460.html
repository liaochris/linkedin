  • Primary Researcher

    Study of Visual Word Recognition in Bilinguals

    (Research industry)

    May 2005 — May 2007 (2 years 1 month)

    Spanish-English and Italian-English bilinguals were given word recognition tasks in this ongoing study of word processing in the bilingual brain. The role of lexical neighborhood size and word frequency on response time was measured for words in English and Italian. For Spanish-English bilinguals, response times were collected for interlingual cognates and homographs. A diffusion model approach was proposed for modeling data from the lexical decision tasks.

  • Primary Researcher

    Meaning & Demeaning: Life Accounts on the Worth of Work

    (Research industry)

    May 2004 — August 2004 (4 months)

    Research was conducted on menial labor and job satisfaction. Specific attention was paid to the occurrence of meaning in working-class jobs. Attributes of job satisfaction and dissatisfaction were defined and their relevance to productivity and workplace environment were studied. Community members were interviewed and their work experiences were compiled with literary analyses and critiques of work environments and job satisfaction.
    Funded by a fellowship from the Lilly Foundation Values & Vocations Project.
