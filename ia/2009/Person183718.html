Paul Sholar’s Summary

I'm a very experienced senior information developer (technical writer) and editor, mostly addressing software developer audiences but also including solid experience in scientific editing.

I blogged ( LINK http://www.neuraliq.com/news/ ) and authored print and and web content about an intrusion detection product for virtualized servers (NeuralIQ). I produced marketing pieces and usage/reference guides about an embedded communications applications toolkit (Motorola/Freescale). Also, I documented C-language multithreaded programming tools (Pthreads) at Compaq/DEC and documented an object-oriented expert programming environment (Gensym's G2). I was an award winner in regional New England competitions of the Society for Technical Communication.

I performed full-cycle editorial work on complex hydrology- and geology-related reports targeted to Federal (DOE, NRC, BLM) and Nevada (groundwater resources) regulators.

I improved the productivity of publications teams and sw development groups by creating specifications templates, document project plans, task checklists, processes for structured reviews of drafts, style sheets, and standard bibliographies for authors' use.

I also delivered robust FrameMaker templates for three organizations, including for the DOE Yucca Mountain Project's License Application (1,000s of pages).

Early in my career I made a living as system- and application-level programmer (C, PL/I, Fortran, assembler).

Experience shows these are the key steps in successfully producing technical documents:

-- Create project plans that address all relevant requirements.

-- Understanding the audience's profile

-- Identify the information structure of the content being presented.

-- Select content formats that fit both the presentation medium and the content's information structure.

-- Constrain the content's terminology and phraseology.

-- Follow a structured review process for continuous improvement from draft to draft.

Paul Sholar’s Specialties:

Structured document reviews, document review process, requirements-driven document plans, editorial resources for authors (style sheets, checklists, knowledge bases, content reuse), robust FrameMaker template design/rollout/update (three different organizations), Acrobat PDF tweaks, robust Word-to-FM conversion macro, information-dense table designs, XML-based authoring, configuration management tools, systems-level software development experience
