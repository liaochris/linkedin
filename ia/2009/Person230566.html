Teresa Ward’s Summary

With the flu season already upon us having your home cleaned is not just a luxury, but a necessity!
Even if you take every precaution to prevent spreading the flu from washing your hands to covering your sneeze with your elbow, flu germs can still be transferred from person to person on “touch points” in your home for up to 72 hours! This is a crucial time period that your home needs to be disinfected, but you and your family are too ill to even THINK about cleaning those germ infested areas! We at Teresa’s Family Cleaning can do this for you with our trained staff that knows exactly where to disinfect your home to keep others in your household or who visit your home from catching those nasty flu germs that you unintentionally brought home with you a few days earlier. Give us a call! Below are a few things you can do in addition to our cleaning that can also help prevent the spread of those nasty flu germs:
~Throw away tissues and other disposable items used by the sick person in the trash.~Wash your hands after touching used tissues and similar waste. ~Linens, eating utensils, and dishes belonging to those who are sick do not need to be cleaned separately, but these items should not be shared without washing thoroughly first.~Wash linens (such as bed sheets and towels) by using household laundry soap and tumble dry on a hot setting. Avoid “hugging” laundry prior to washing it to prevent contaminating yourself. Clean your hands with soap and water or alcohol-based hand rub right after handling dirty laundry. ~Eating utensils should be washed either in a dishwasher or by hand with water and soap. (Source: The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention).

Teresa's Family Cleaning Inc. strives to create a feeling of welcome, comfort, and beauty in the homes of it's valued customers, while fostering the spirit of generosity in the lives of it's employees and the surrounding community.
