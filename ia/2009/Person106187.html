  • Sr. Engineering Program Manager and Architect

    BEA Systems Inc.

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; BEAS; Computer Software industry)

    2003 — 2006 (3 years)

    I was the product architect and engineering program manager for AquaLogic Service Bus.

    Program Managers are outward facing architects helping with presales and postsales activities, and also working on integrations with partners and other organization's products. Program Managers also help guide the design and testing and refine and help define requirements for the product based on real world use cases.

    As an architect I worked closely with the development design leads to help design the core components of the product, primarily in the areas of runtime engine, transports, security, repository, web services foundation, and monitoring.

    I was awarded the honor of being named the employee of the year for AquaLogic Service Bus for 2005.

  • Chief infrastructure architect

    Commerce One

    (Public Company; Computer Software industry)

    2000 — 2003 (3 years)

    I was the chief infrastructure architect of Commerce One Conductor, Commerce One’s composite application platform. I lead the architecture, design and technical coordination of Conductor.

    Conductor was a fusion of ESB and Orchestration technology with interoperation driven by service and routing configurations in a centralized registry and repository. Interoperation included bridging transports, bridging formats with Contivo and XSLT transformations, selection of the security scheme based on receivers configuration, and selection of a common conversational choreography supported by sender and receiver. The orchestration was driven by visually defining the flow in a graphical tool using a powerful though nonstandard language.

    Before Conductor, worked on the Marketsite Infrastructure platform.

  • Architect

As a OLTP consultant helped facilitate successful completion of many large and complex deals.
