James Kinloch’s Summary

“I have a huge passion for the internet and how sites can merge communities and industries. I have achieved much in the sector with my work with HybridLab.org, Moveme, Norwich Union and Sky (to name a few). This site is about helping Women Helped to Advance in Media (WHAM) and supports well known London-based artists and musicians. During my time here, a creative, in-house team was set up and I helped give the charity a more productive, modern approach to new media. I gave talks on new media and how it can help SME’s move forward and expand their service offering for the parent company Innovative Enterprise Action (IEA). I learnt from this that a strong cultural understand, behind that of work by Hofstede, is vital for a site to work. I passionately believe that you have to understand the mission statement of a non-profit organisation and be able to match that and that of the potential user’s in a harmonious way.

“I worked with organisations such as ESF, LSC, WHAM, CSC, B2C companies such as moveme.com, Sky and Norwich Union and liaised with big name brands such as ITV and Microsoft. All these roles involved huge amounts of what I call 'Creative SEO' which is all about matching the needs of the users and clients (and search engines) to make a great site that ranks well and makes the users keep coming back to the site. I am a huge fan of work by Mark Trammell, Ben Hunt and Tammy Sachs and their views on how a site can make a user happy. Usability and site structure is an area I have a huge interest in. I have great experience in freelance work with magazines, security companies, online radio and Artists.

“My knowledge of the internet and SEO is very elevated as I have helped launch (and made popular and successful against the objectives and strategy) many websites and managed the marketing behind them. I have worked with an NMA Award winning and Yahoo People's Choice winner in moveme.com. This has been done in both a freelance capacity and a salary capacity.
