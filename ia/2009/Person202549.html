Roger Kemp’s Summary

Managed all aspects of full-service cities in ethnically, racially, politically, and economically diverse communities in three states during his city management career.

Roger has written and edited numerous volumes dealing with various issues relating to America's cities. These books focus on the best practices in these areas, and help make citizens aware of solutions to important issues that may exist in their community. Roger Kemp’s Experience

  • President

    Kemp Consulting, LLC

    (Management Consulting industry)

    February 2009 — Present (9 months)

    This company performs consulting services to local governments, primarily citys and towns. Roger is a Credentialed City Manager, and has been a CEO in cities in Connecticut, New Jersey, and California during his career.

    Roger has been designated as a "Credentialed City Manager" (ICMA-CM) by the International City/County Management Association, Washington, DC, since 2002.

    He has also been an author, editor, and contributing author of nearly 50 books dealing with all aspects of municipal governments, including such subjects as the condition of their infrastructure, how to manage government in hard times, to the likely future condition of our cities in the US.

  • Adjunct Professor

  • City Manager

    Communities located in Connecticut, New Jersey, and California

    (Government Administration industry)

    1980 — 2008 (28 years)

    Roger has been a career city manager in three states -- Connecticut, New Jersey, and California --during his city management career. He also teaches part-time at several universities in CA, MN, and CT (both classroom and online courses), and just formed an LLC to do municipal government consulting.

    The cities that Roger has managed range in size from about 30,000 to over 120,000 in population.
