Adam Masri’s Summary

Need a Mac? Need to learn how to use a Mac? Need to get your Mac fixed? Need to use Mac in business? I've worked with Apple computers since the Apple ][ in 1978, and Macintosh since 1985. I've followed Apple technology since they literally founded the personal computer industry in 1976. I can help you at your home, home-based business, or small business throughout the San Francisco, California Bay Area.

How can I help you?

- Adam

Adam Masri’s Experience

  • President

    Nolex (Sole Proprietorship)

    (Sole Proprietorship; Myself Only; Computer Hardware industry)

    August 1992 — Present (16 years 7 months)

    I founded Nolex in 1992 to help people utilize Macintosh technology in their home, home-based business, and business. My original goal with Nolex was to be a Macintosh Systems Integrator - a one stop source for all your Mac hardware & software needs. No finger pointing, no, "it's that guy's fault" waste of time. You have one number to call, one person to speak to. I help you choose what to buy, help you buy it, help you set it up, help train you, and fix things when they break.

  • Manager of Information Systems

    Label Art of California

    (Public Company; 11-50 employees; Printing industry)

    1989 — August 1992 (3 years)

    Responsible for development of a custom database management application for the flexographic label industry. Application had ability to store data on customers, customer orders, and specialized tooling (dies) used to cut paper in the label manufacturing process.

    Recommended accounting package that had open source code; made modifications to the accounting package for the specialized needs of label manufacturing.

    I continue to work for Label Art as a consultant today. I helped with integration of the latest digital printing systems into their environment. I am sometimes brought in for complex database-driven print jobs, allowing every label to be entirely unique (different text, images, etc). I have experience with database-driven barcodes, including complex formats with checksums, and even 2D barcodes.

  • Programmer

    Julian Systems

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Computer Hardware industry)

    1988 — 1989 (1 year)

    Julian Systems was a Macintosh VAR that built RAM upgrades for Macintosh computers, a video editing system, and a variety of other hardware devices for Macintosh computers. Julian had one of the first Lino imagesetters in the San Francisco Bay Area, and had created a custom application allowing customers to send files electronically for imagesetting. The application would gather information on a job including positive or negative, film or paper, mirror image (emulsion up or down), would compress the customer's file, then transmit via modem long before the Internet was open for public consumption.

    I worked under Aaron Disario as a 4th Dimension programmer, a 4GL programming environment with an integrated database & Pascal-like programming language. We were building a custom POS (Point Of Sale) system that had several paid installations.

  • Manager of Information Systems

    Label Art of California

    (Public Company; 11-50 employees; Printing industry)

    1984 — 1988 (4 years)

    Managing Macintosh computers on a network in a graphics-intensive business environment. Computers were used for a variety of office productivity tasks including word processing, spreadsheets, accounting, etc.

    Wrote a timecard program to keep track of employee hours electronically. Timecard application had ability to read & write mag-stripe cards; employees would swipe their card to clock in or out. Reports used to calculate payroll.

    Macs were used for graphic design tasks with programs such as Photoshop, Illustrator, Freehand, Quark XPress, Suitcase. Helped develop methods of utilizing digital technology to create typesetting & artwork to replace older camera & film based methods. Helped develop method of directly modifying Postscript data to achieve specialized graphics requirements such as distortion when no applications were available. Responsible for selection/recommendation of high end scanners ($50K), imagesetter ($150K), RIP (Raster Image Processors).
