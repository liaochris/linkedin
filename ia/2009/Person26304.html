Benjamin Sinclair’s Summary

Since graduating with a business degree at the Richard Ivey School of Business, I have been actively pursuing a career in the financial services sector. I immediately completed the Canadian Securities Course, and I passed the CFA Level 1 exam at the beginning of December. I am currently an Investment Specialist at BMO, which has given me the opportunity to deal directly with mutual fund clients and give them advice.

My main interest has been in investing, which means I would like to eventually be an equity analyst and then portfolio manager. I have also developed a keen interest in both politics and economics, which I think fits very well with my career goals. When investing in any company, it is obviously very important to be able to understand the political & economic environment it operates in.

In 30 years I can see myself working as a portfolio manager, as a university professor teaching finance, or working for the government.

Benjamin Sinclair’s Specialties:

Equity research, mutual fund research, and economic research.
