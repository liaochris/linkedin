David Lusby’s Summary

David Lusby

David Lusby started in the hospitality business in 1992 as a bar back in an Orange County, California jazz club. While attending San Diego State University he worked both in restaurants and clubs, holding almost every front-of-house position. After college, he went to work for a number of wine and spirit companies in Los Angeles, San Diego, and Portland, Oregon, selling to both to retail stores and restaurants.

In 2005, he was hired to run the bar program at Summit, the newest restaurant at the Broadmoor Hotel. As a member of the opening team Lusby helped put together an innovative wine, spirit, and cocktail program respectful of the classic cocktails, as well as maintaining a level of innovation to meet the needs of an international clientele. In 2006 Summit was named one of the best new restaurants in the United States by both Esquire and Robb Report.

In August 2006, Lusby began an internship with Bobby Stuckey, a Master Sommelier, at Frasca Food and Wine in Boulder, CO. The wine team at Frasca helped provide David with the knowledge and ability to became a Certified Sommelier in September of 2006, as well as begin preparations for the Advanced Sommelier exam which he passed in April 2009.

In October 2006, Lusby passed the intermediate B.A.R. exam focused on fine spirits and cocktails and led by some of the most respected names in the beverage world, including F. Paul Pacult of the Spirit Journal, Dale DeGroff, Doug Frost M.S., M.W., David Wondrich Ph.D and Steve Olson.

In June 2007, Lusby took the position of Beverage Director for Tom Colicchio’s newest restaurant, Craft Los Angeles. Here, he oversaw every aspect of the beverage program, focusing on small, high quality producers for both wine and spirits. It’s his greatest pleasure to steer people towards wines that they would otherwise never explore and at Craft he felt he had that opportunity.
