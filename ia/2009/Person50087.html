Craig Hohnberger’s Summary

Strong visionary leader with a passion for growing socially-conscious businesses, charities and the leaders that lead them while helping success-driven people find their true calling.

A life defining moment for me was in Sep. '97 flying home from a 2-week business trip. On the 2nd day of that trip my daughter learned how to walk and I couldn't see her for another 11 days. I was incredibly blessed to sit next to Rose, a mid-70's woman, who was on her way back from a cruise. As Rose heard about my daughter, she gave me some life changing advice I will never forget. She told me the worst part of her life, looking back, were the regrets, and that if I did 3 things well, I'd get to her age with no regrets someday.

...Rose said, "Craig do these three things: (1) Take the time to form strong relationships with other people and make a difference in their lives, starting with those closest to you - your family. Always remember, your little girl doesn't spell 'LOVE' like we do as adults, she spells it 'TIME' and just wants you there, in her life, so be there. (2) Pursue YOUR dreams, whatever they are. Get clarity on them and go for it, because it appears you're chasing your company's deams. (3) Never let fear stop you, in either of the above. Fear is crippling and is at the root of all real regrets, and remember, regrets are the worst part of life when looking back."

It took me a few years to fully appreciate the impact that plane ride would have on my life and I am eternally grateful to Rose.

Life is far too short, so take advantage of the little time you have left and do something you are truly passionate about. Don't look back with regrets, you will look back and realize they were the worst part of your life.
