William Ulicny’s Summary

I have been involved as a Federal government consultant or contractor since 1990. During that time, I have been involved in and/or led successful proposal teams for procurements for Department of Energy (DOE), Nuclear Regulatory Commission (NRC), Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), and commercial clients. I have also led proposal development teams for Department of Defense (DoD) clients, including the Army Corps of Engineers, the Defense Logistics Agency, the Air Force Institute for Operational Health (AFIOH), and the Defense Advanced Research projects Agency (DARPA).

Relating to Federal Government Systems, I was the lead contractor in charge of preparing a Low-Level Radioactive Waste Disposal National Capacity report, which required my management of the integration of two separate, large-scale DOE databases. For DARPA, I am overseeing the development of a software modeling tool by a contractor company that will predict the deposition of radioactive material on urban canyon building surfaces following the activation of a radiological dispersion device (RDD) as a terrorist act. As a support contractor with DARPA, I have worked with DARPA technical and procurement organizations, as well as AFIOH to identify, select, and bring under contract several research and development organizations (national labs, universities, and private companies) to meet DARPA program goals. While not for a Federal government agency, I was responsible for the selection, procurement, and installation of LAN and computing systems within the Parks Township, Pennsylvania, site of the Babcock & Wilcox Nuclear Environmental Services organization. During this time, I was also responsible for the procurement and installation of a major health physics data management software system that performed the legally required dosimetry and survey records for the company.

My current DoD security clearance is SECRET, and I am currently INTERIM TOP SECRET while my investigation is performed.
