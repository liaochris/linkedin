Ty Chandler’s Summary

I am an aggressive journalist with nearly a decade in the business. My work has been honored with more than a half a dozen awards including an Emmy and Edward R. Murrow Award. I knew I wanted to be journalist since my fourth grade teacher assigned a current events project. I attended New York University, where I majored in Broadcast Journalism with a double minor in History and Politics. I landed my first job at CNBC as an overnight tape producer. I then got my big break as a reporter at KTXS in Abilene, Texas.

After two years in West Texas, I returned to the Empire State, this time in Rochester. I anchored the weekend Ten O’clock News and reported for the FOX and CBS affiliates. In Rochester one of my enterprise stories on teen pregnancy would grow into something bigger than I ever imagined. “Eye on Rochester: The Pregnancy Codes,” aired as a commercial free one hour special. The special received four Emmy nominations, winning the News Special category. It also won an Edward R. Murrow Award and two New York State Broadcasters Association Awards.

Next would be Kansas City at KCTV 5. At KCTV, I turned multiple stories a day, performing ten to twenty live shots a week. I also stepped into the world of radio. I hosted “KC Currents,” a weekly magazine show focusing on diversity issues on KCUR-FM. If two jobs wasn't enough, I added graduate school to my plate. I started an International Studies program at the University of Kansas.

I returned home to New York City in August 2008, continuing my studies at Rutgers University. In May 2009 I earned her M.S. Degree in Global Affairs with a focus on Terrorism and Global Security. I never stopped working while I was in school, balancing a full-time course load and at one point freelance work from three different stations. I graduated with a 3.75 overall G.P.A. I am currently a freelance reporter for NY 1 and host of "Diverse Long Island" on News 12 Long Island.
