Brian Piurkowski’s Experience

  • Contract Engineer

    COMFORCE Technical Services

    (Aviation & Aerospace industry)

    April 2008 — Present (1 year 7 months)

    Responsible for the overall analytical support for main and tail rotor composite/metallic blade design. Specific responsibilities include support for all production problems related to rotor blade design, disposition of Material Review Board discrepancies, analytical static and fatigue substantiation of S-76D rotor blade drawing releases, along with responsibility for particular aspects of rotor blade bond control and process specifications. Other project involvement includes static and fatigue stress analysis support for the Carson Services Inc. designed all-composite main rotor blade for the VH-3D Presidential helicopter.

  • Engineer/Scientist 4

    Spirit AeroSystems

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; SPR; Aviation & Aerospace industry)

    January 2005 — April 2008 (3 years 4 months)

    Responsible for assuring the structural integrity of the composite cabin skins for the Sikorsky CH-53K Heavy Lift Replacement Helicopter. Responsible as the stress analysis lead for the sustaining activities relative to the Boeing 777 Thrust Reverser structure. Prepared the strength check note packages for the cargo door surround structure that was an integral part of the Boeing 787 Section 41 composite skin & stringer structural assembly.

    Responsible for developing the structural analysis methodologies and templates for the Boeing 787 hybrid composite Wing Fixed Leading Edge upper panels. Oversaw the design and test verification of the composite integral splice of the wing fixed leading edge upper panels to the wing box. Coordinated an effort to optimize the weight of the wing fixed leading edge upper panels resulting in 83 lbs. of weight savings per airplane.

  • Senior Technical Specialist (Scientific)

    Northrop Grumman Shipbuilding

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; NOC; Defense & Space industry)

    June 2003 — January 2005 (1 year 8 months)

    Composites Research & Development at Northrop Grumman Shipbuilding, Pascagoula, MS

    Responsible for overseeing the development of the DD(X) Composite Deckhouse coupon and sub-component test specimens. Responsible for maintaining the composite material allowables database and for directing the development of critical software to perform composite analysis. Composed, organized, and conducted Composites Structural Analysis methodology classes for Engineers, as well as providing ongoing training to junior engineers in composites design and analysis.

  • Engineering Specialist, Stress Analysis

    The NORDAM Group Nacelle/Thrust Reverser Division

    (Privately Held; 1001-5000 employees; Aviation & Aerospace industry)

    July 2001 — May 2003 (1 year 11 months)

    Composites Stress Analysis for Nacelle/Thrust Reverser Systems Manufacturing Division.

    Responsible for assuring structural integrity of all composite primary and secondary structure on the Gulfstream GIV-X, Falcon 2000, Hawker Horizon, Learjet 60, and Gulfstream G200 nacelle and thrust reverser assemblies. Specific responsibilities included specification of composite analysis procedures, composite material selection, composite material acceptance criteria, and definition of the basic laminate ply layups for each component. Oversaw qualification of graphite epoxy and graphite bismaleimide prepreg material systems. Prepared the Structural Certification Plan for Gulfstream GIV-X Thrust Reverser Unit.

  • Staff Engineer

    Hawker Beechcraft

    (Privately Held; 5001-10,000 employees; Airlines/Aviation industry)

    1997 — 2000 (3 years)

    Responsible for the overall structural integrity of the forward, center, and aft composite fuselage shells, along with the various composite fuselage detail components, for the Hawker 4000 aircraft. Specific responsibilities included specification of composite analysis procedures, composite material selection, composite material acceptance criteria, definition of the baseline laminate ply layups for each component, definition of the laminate ply layups and critical dimensions for all required reinforcements, and shell laminate qualification. Coordinated in a Design Build Team environment along with design engineering, systems engineering, manufacturing, and test engineering groups. Responsible for recommending DER approval of dispositions and for defining structural repairs relative to nonconformities on all of the composite fuselage structure for the Hawker 4000 RC-1 through RC-4, full-scale static test, and full-scale fatigue test aircraft.

  • Lead Engineer, Stress Methods Group & Fatigue Test Support Group

    Gulfstream Aerospace

    (Public Company; 5001-10,000 employees; GD; Aviation & Aerospace industry)

    May 1995 — July 1997 (2 years 3 months)

    Responsible for coordinating the implementation and documentation of analysis methods for structural integrity evaluation of metallic and composite structures. Released several Macintosh FORTRAN analysis codes enabling interactive execution of the SQ5 point stress laminate analysis, the BJSFM composite bolted joint analysis, and the COMPBUCK laminated composite plate buckling analysis developed previously. Provided analytical support for the Fatigue & Damage Tolerance Group to develop the test load spectrum for the full-scale fatigue test of the Gulfstream V fuselage. Developed and correlated the NASTRAN finite element model of the test fixture support structure for the full-scale test of the GV fuselage. Specified the associated fuselage to fixture interface loads and displacements for the derived fatigue test load spectrum.
