Graham Robinson’s Summary

Since Graham started his own online show "DJVIBE" (www.djvibe.com) in 2000 he hasn't looked back. It was an dedicated to the emerging underground music scene that had little to no coverage at the time. Graham documented shows and concerts using digital video and published them on the Internet to be streamed to his audience. A concept that was very new at that time. DJVIBE.com quickly became popular and was soon one of the biggest of its kind on the west coast.

Through DJVIBE Graham began to make important contacts in the entertainment industry, and was soon able to turn his passion into a profitable business. Graham decided to start his own company ‘Studio X Networks’ (www.studiox.tv) to meet the growing demands for his video services. Since starting his company Graham has produced videos for Molson Canada, Labatts, Honda, Interwest, Lancome, Bacardi, United Nations and more. As the projects grew in size Graham learned quickly how to deal with large production teams. Back in the early days of online video, DJVIBE.com videos were hosted on Grahams own server that was constantly being overwhelmed with traffic and always going over its monthly bandwidth. So now with broadcast partners like Youtube.com and ARC2 TV (www.arc2.tv) locally in Vancouver things are alot easier to manage.

Graham’s work has been showcased on a documentary by Sonya Buyting for Discovery Channel (http://www.exn.ca/Stories/2002/02/04/54.asp), and a fashion show on MTV (www.mtvcanada.com). More recently Graham is heading up the Shanghai office for StudioX and currently producing a music video for Bacardi featuring Jin the Emcee.

Graham's currently pushing to get some quality Canadian content out there and to give people something cool to watch besides back to back episodes of reality dating shows..
