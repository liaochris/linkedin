Derron Fairbanks’s Summary

Over the last 15 years I have run an Internet Marketing consultancy (Wasatch Web Internet Marketing), taken the web development lead at a significant regional interactive agency (Candesa Interactive) and played a key role in turning around the Ancestry.com brand as a senior member of the creative team and leader of their first dedicated front-end development team.

Today I own web design and development in corporate marketing for Omniture, soon to be part Adobe. Life is good!

Derron Fairbanks’s Specialties:

I am a team player and a team builder focused on online creative execution. I'm a firm believer in interactive marketing programs that achieve great results and build great brands. I understand the role creative plays in this process and how to reliably deliver product that looks and works the way it should.

Derron Fairbanks’s Experience

  • Sr. Web Manager

    Omniture

    (Public Company; OMTR; Computer Software industry)

    January 2009 — Present (10 months)

    In January 2009 I joined Omniture's in-house creative team as Sr. Web Manager, responsible for design and execution of Omniture's web properties.

  • Principal Front-End Web and Email Specialist

    Ancestry.com

    (Privately Held; Internet industry)

    April 2004 — December 2008 (4 years 9 months)

    In 2004 I stepped into a new role on the creative team at Ancestry.com managing creative design and execution in a very large and growing email marketing program (ultimately 13+ Million monthly sends across multiple targeted campaigns). I established and enforced on-brand email design and production standards based on accepted best practices for each class of communication.

    Under my leadership the Ancestry.com email design program became renown for elegant and effective email creative.

    I created and staffed Ancestry.com's dedicated front-end web team, originally under the marketing organization, responsible for producing one-off email and landing page creative as well as home-page mark-up and other key web marketing assets.

    I oversaw the successful transition of my front-end web team from the marketing organization to the software development organization before moving on to my next opportunity.

  • Senior Web Developer

    Candesa

    (Internet industry)

    May 1998 — April 2004 (6 years)

    In 1998 I accepted a position on the development team at Candesa Interactive, soon to become one of the top regional Interactive Agencies of its day. While at Candesa I took the lead web development role on many high profile projects for the likes of Disney, Hitachi Data Systems, Intermountain Health Care, Iomega, Lucent Technologies, Park City Chamber of Commerce, Park City Mountain Resort, Vignette Corp, William Morris Agency and many others. While at Candesa I was privileged to work with and learn from some of the best creative and web development professionals in the intermountain region.

  • Founder and Principal

    Wasatch Web Internet Marketing

    (Internet industry)

    October 1995 — October 1998 (3 years 1 month)

    I incorporated Wasatch Web from a one bedroom apartment as an alternative to community college, where no one at the time had heard of the Internet. I started with zero experience and one client, my father's real estate business. I began teaching myself HTML, JavaScript and how to use Photoshop

    I was a one man shop, handling everything from Strategy, Design and Production to Sales and Bookkeeping. I assembled and managed multi-disciplinary teams of contractors where necessary.

    Over the next three years I strategized, designed and built Web sites and programs for some of Utah's most recognized brands of the day, including Blaine Jensen RV, Crystal Inn Hotels, Templine Employment Services, The Sandy Area Chamber of Commerce and VCBO Architects, among others. The site I designed and built for my father received national recognition within the CENTURY 21 system as one of the top franchisee web sites in 1996.

    I attracted the attention of regional agencies and ultimately elected to repay my investors and formally close Wasatch Web after accepting a full-time position where I could focus on what I was best at. However, I continued to accept many freelance engagements in the following years.
