Ilyas Bhatti’s Summary

Ilyas Bhatti has been the President of the BHATTI GROUP, a Boston based consulting engineering firm engaged in local and international projects since 1998.

He also served in many senior executive level positions in Massachusetts. He
was appointed by Governor Weld as the Associate Project Director for the Central Artery/Tunnel Project in Boston in 1995 and served until 1998. He oversaw the completion of Ted Williams Tunnel connecting I-90 with Logan International Airport.
Bhatti was appointed as Commissioner of the Metropolitan District Commission (MDC) by Governor Michael Dukakis in 1989 to head one of the largest regional government agencies in the U.S. As Commissioner, he was the chief executive officer of an agency responsible for managing parks, police, recreational facilities,parkways, bridges, flood control and water supply.
Bhatti holds BS and MS degrees in Civil Engineering; and received management training at Harvard. In 1994, he was awarded an honorary degree of Doctor of Engineering Technology from the Wentworth Institute of Technology, Boston.
Bhatti joined the faculty of Roger Williams University in Bristol, Rhode Island as Assistant Professor of Construction Management in 2006.
He has been an active member of several professional and civic organizations. He was President of the Massachusetts Society of Professional Engineers, and General Chairman of National Engineers Week in Boston. He served on the Industrial Advisory Board of Northeastern University’s School of Engineering from 1999 to 2007.
Rrecently, he served on the Massachusetts Water Policy Task Force to review state’s water policies and to develop a recommended plan to deal with water supply management issues. Bhatti is currently the President of the Commissioners Council, Inc. in Massachusetts.
Bhatti has received numerous professional and civic awards. He was the recipient of Governor’s New American Appreciation Award in 1998.
