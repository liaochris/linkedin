Taleish Daniels’s Summary

Writing is in me... always has been. I remember being in grade school writing poems for my friends because they didn't have the words to express to their crush what they felt. Had I knew what I was doing back then, I would have charged a little bit...lol. Truthfully it wasn't about that. For me, writing has always been about a feeling, a freedom of expression, giving a voice to those who may not have one, or may not know how to use theirs.

My love of film, and screenwriting came to me by surprise. I'd always thought I'd major in journalism, and start my own magazine. I never expected film. During my freshman year at Clark Atlanta University, my friends and I decided to audition for a student film called "Watch Ya Self," written and directed by J. Swain, who was a senior film student. At the time I was a journalism major and had began working on what was to be my first novel. During filming I had a vision of the characters from my novel coming to life on the big screen. It was a life changing moment. Taking the script I received from my part in the movie, following the format with no previous knowledge of screenwriting I created my first screenplay. The very next semester I changed my concentration to film and the rest is history.

Currently I'm working on a number of projects:

Dutch: The Movie
A film adaptation of the Essence Best Selling Novel written by Kwame Teague

Death Around The Corner
Also a film adaptation of the novel written by Corey Miller aka C-Murder

The third project is untitled as I have been in contact with the author and am awaiting their decision on which novel they would like adapted.

I'm also working on my own project though I haven't decided which direction I'd like to take it. Stay tuned for more on that.
