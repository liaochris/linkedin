Mike Trekas’s Summary

Most people outside of the investment field don't know how bad it has truly become. Computer modeling, stock screeners, and fast, backtested information have all essentially removed thought from the investment process. Many advisors and financial planners — focused on bringing in new accounts and assets — tend to overdiversify in an attempt to reduce swings in the portfolios they oversee, ultimately trading your goals for their peace of mind.
We believe in the old philosophy of only putting money into our best ideas while trying to avoid mediocre and bad opportunities.

How We Are Paid

We do not earn commissions. We do not accept kickbacks from mutual funds. Our only revenue source for managing portfolios is our investment advisory fee: 0.25% each quarter based on the size of the portfolio at that time. That's it.

Does our fee model make sense? If you believe that we can help you earn at least 1.00% more and lose at least 1.00% less on average each year (over the long-term), our fee model makes sense.

How We Strive to Manage Your Money

Our investment philosophy is very "old school" in that we try to invest the way it was done fifty years ago. Sure, we use technology to leverage our research; still, here's a sampling of how we manage money:
stocks are pieces of businesses to be valued, bought when they are cheap, and sold when their businesses are no longer attractive relative to the price.
people should have their money invested in their best ideas. There are two types of investments: great and "pass". If an investment opportunity doesn't look great, we'll pass. You are far better off holding wonderful investments and sitting on them than actively trading to play catch-up. We're not growth or value investors. At The Meridian Business Group, we're business investors — investors that strive to invest for growth and safety by making smart business decisions with our clients' portfolios.
