Heinrich Schwella’s Summary

So, tell me about yourself they say…

OK, here goes:

What does one know about the law when one starts thinking of studying it? What one sees on Alley McBeal? Luckily I wasn’t as naive as that and since my psychometric tests all came back with a “you can do what you want”, I decided to kick off with a B.Comm (Law) degree at Stellenbosch. I soon realised that I really enjoy the law and that led to my LLB. At the end of my studies I spent some time in Belgium as an exchange student - an event that changed my views on my career dramatically.

Armed with my LLB, I tackled my articles. Been there, done that, I practised law for a while, post articles, and did some very interesting ICT cases (domain name disputes) and some ICT legal consulting work (legal risk profiling from an ICT perspective, etc).

Although I was loving my job, I have a continuous need for excitement and an extremely interesting opportunity came past to join a private equity firm. My love for the law and the great ICT work I was doing, was waging war with my inner voice, still calling on me to put my commercial skills to the test.

In the end my curiosity won and I joined the private equity firm, where I worked on the operational / investment side.

However, as the wise men say, it never rains, but pours and shortly after joining the private equity firm, I was presented with an opportunity to join Accenture as a consultant (well, to be honest, consultant in training, but who’s counting!). I’ve been interested in Accenture since my second year at varsity and even though I love Cape Town (my birth town!), that is the reason why I have relocated to Johannesburg…

To satisfy my hunger for some legal exposure I’m keeping a (legal) blog running (see http://blog.brightshark.co.za) and plan on doing some volunteer work at the local legal aid clinic.

In a nutshell, that’s the (professional) me.
