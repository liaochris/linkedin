Neil Capstick’s Summary

Neil is a practical and pragmatic consultant, very interested in contemporary management theory but as interested in translating that theory into tangible techniques that managers, directors and business owners can use in the workplace.

Following completion of his MBA and being awarded Chartered Manager for the first time in 2005, Neil took a short break before studying Business Research Methods (Quantitative and Qualitative) with Teesside University. Then in 2008, he was awarded Chartered Manager for a second time.
Both awards were given for significant change management projects and are further proof of Neil’s practical leadership and management skills.

In 2009 Neil decided to set up Executive Compass Business Consultants, as precursor to this he attended the Advanced Management Skills courses on Value Management and is currently studying for the Diploma in Management Consultancy. Further details of these courses can be found at http://www.amskills.com/

Neil has a broad range of interests in business and management theory but is particularly interested in knowledge management, especially organisational memory (or the lack of it) and knowledge transfer.
His practical interests and applications focus on discovering and researching new markets for existing products and how to penetrate those markets. He has a broad client base which includes micro businesses, SME and corporate accounts.

Neil is an active member of the Chartered Management Institute and various other professional bodies and is always happy to discuss management issues across a broad range of functional disciplines.

Further details of his company can be found at http://www.executivecompass.co.uk/business/home
