Natasha Deganello Giraudie’s Summary

Natasha is a social entrepreneur with a commitment to leverage business practices for meaningful and measurable social impact.

She is CEO and founder of Micro-Documentaries, a young company of Stanford filmmakers that produce 1-minute micro-documentaries that are authentic, affordable and actionable for organizations that are making a positive contribution to the world. Clients like Stanford University, green technology companies like EnergyHub, socially responsible businesses like Rickshaw Bagworks, new generation venture funds like Physic Ventures and nonprofits like the Second Harvest Food Bank are using the micro-documentaries to connect in a more engaging way with their audience, to support their sales efforts and to raise more money. Their videos are distributed strategically through a number of channels like email campaigns, websites, social networks, events, and so on.

Before Micro-Documentaries, Natasha was CEO and founder of Papilia (now A.R.S. Media), which offered Internet software that helped nonprofits like United Nations World Food Programme, KQED and ODC Dance raise millions of dollars by helping donors understand the difference their gifts make.

Natasha started her philanthropic work with kids in Venezuela, where she grew up, and has been working and volunteering ever since for children’s organizations in five continents. She currently serves on the board of Musicians for World Harmony which brings the healing power of music to AIDS orphans and refugee children in Africa. She earned her M.A. from Stanford and her B.S. from the University of Texas at Austin. Natasha has also studied Tibetan Buddhism regularly with the Dalai Lama since 1994. Today, she lives in Muir Beach, close to San Francisco, with her husband and their daughter. In her free time, she enjoys making messy art and tending to beets, potatoes and parsley on a beautiful organic farm on the Pacific Coast.
