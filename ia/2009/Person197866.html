Richard Wallace’s Summary

Software and system architecture for high performance computing systems.

Extensive software and hardware engineering covering the entire spectrum from FPGA/ASIC to systems of systems.

Original Development Manager for VHDL and current VHDL advocate for ASIC and FPGA development.

Formal methods (formal mathematical proof) advocate and practitioner using Z, Alloy, and some ACL2.

Richard Wallace’s Experience

  • Manager, Real-Time Systems

    UT-Battelle

    (Government Agency; Research industry)

    June 2009 — Present (3 months)

    Research, design, and development of high-performance computers and computation algorithms for data acquisition, signal processing, analysis and systems for measurement & control.

  • Technical Director

    Northrop Grumman

    (Public Company; NOC; Defense & Space industry)

    October 2002 — April 2009 (6 years 7 months)

    Technical Director for technical computing using FPGA & Stream Architecture Use for Sensor Technologies (FAuST) research group. Advanced studies in carbon nanotube structures for HPC/HEC/CCS, embedded processing for ONIR, EO/IR, RF sensors processing. IR&D on application of FPGA and stream co-processors (GPU, Cell, etc) for rapid reconfigurable computing of flight and mobile electro-optic/infrared processors.

  • Principal Consultant/Owner

    Quantum Solutions

    (Telecommunications industry)

    March 1995 — June 2002 (7 years 4 months)

    Consulting and software development for telecommunicaitons companies (MCI, Qwest, and BT). Major products: enterprise middleware (full custom, MQ Series, TIBCO, MessageQ) and inter/intra-company data transfer between diverse distributed, federated database systems.

  • Sr. Software Engineer

    Digital Equipment Corporation

    (Research industry)

    March 1988 — 1995 (7 years)

    My time at Digital was spent working on two major programs. My initial work was as the System Architect for the Granite Sentry Phase II program; designing and building the real-time, launch event, telemetry (land/satellite), distributed, client-server C3I system on VAX/VMS clusters and SUN/Solaris client systems for USAF NORAD command. After Digital left that business to classical DoD contractors, I was part of the RdbStar effort to validate the single service and multi-service execution plans for this federated, distributed, relational database system. I used some formal methods (Z, Verdi) to do proofs correctness for security of what would be very long and hard system to validate.

  • Computer Scientist

    USAF

    (Defense & Space industry)

    June 1982 — March 1988 (5 years 10 months)

    Air Force Wright Aeronautical Laboratory (now part of AFRL). While at AFWAL, I was the Chief Engineer and manager for the development of the VHDL suite – the IEEE-1076 standard. Iam still involved in VHDL formalizing the VHDL property specification language, IEEE-1850.

    I was also involved in the implementation of the Ada language validating Ada compilers, and designer of components of the RTOS for the Integrated Communications, Navigation, IFF "Identification, Friend or Foe", and Avionics (ICNIA) program.
