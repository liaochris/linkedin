Corey Frank’s Summary

“Running a start-up is like being punched in the face repeatedly. But working for a large corporation is like being waterboarded.” - Paul Graham

Since I’m not a fan of water-boarding I guess that means I’m - at heart - an idea guy, as well as a sales guy, who can take a punch in the face...repeatedly. I love launching relevant technology, sales-driven organizations and growing teams to spark serious revenue. It’s been rumored that I’m addicted to Diet Mountain Dew, whiteboards and love a cool story. I'm often found hanging with maniacally-driven sales and creative folks. Unfortunately, I suffer from incurable Sales ADD and also am known to break out in hives upon any launch of dry PowerPoint.

I’ve gained a some street-cred as an amped-up sales leader and innovative entrepreneur in my roles at various technology companies. For the last 17 years, I’ve served as an original and/or founding member of leadership teams which have played a big part of shaping, respectively, several different industries. As a father of six, I make decisions based on how I'd like my kids to deal with their "fellow man." I expect integrity and personal ethics to be the standard, and I labor to put my actions where my mouth is. I'd like to think this approach, so far, has enabled me to make many long-lasting relationships with some of the technology industry’s most talented, successful, and generous people (you know who you are!). To drill into the weeds a bit, some of my most valuable experience lies in the soup-to-nuts processes of creating a company: venture & angel fundraising, building wildly successful B2B & B2C direct sales teams, optimizing lead generation activities, kick-starting marketing campaigns, organizing killer customer service teams, structuring KPI-driven sales operations and emphasizing the proper financial management and burn rate of business units during the wild fun of pre - EBITDA.
