Marc Schroder’s Experience

  • Chief Executive Officer

    MHL Support Ltd.

    (Management Consulting industry)

    December 2007 — Present (1 year 8 months)

    Holding full P&L responsibility for one of the fastest growing UK Compliance/ Risk Management Consulting Businesses. Recruited to re-organise/ streamline business activity with the aim to jump-start stagnant sales and business development. The initial brief included a new business strategy, product diversification and process re-engineering to deliver very ambitious Operating Plan. Led restructuring initiative by developing several new product streams, adopted new organisational structure able to sell new product offering, streamlined back office support, introduced IT front end services which resulted in the reversal of past negative sales trends.

  • European Business Unit Managing Director

    A-Novo UK Ltd. (UK/POL/IRL)

    (Telecommunications industry)

    September 2004 — December 2007 (3 years 4 months)

    Spearheaded the recovery of an under-delivering Telecoms Business Unit (1000 employees; £47 million turnover) through a well executed business turnaround and organizational change program, with the aim to improve revenues and a reduction in the overall cost base without effecting service delivery. Business development initiatives resulted in a year on year revenue growth in excess of 30%. Restructuring Initiative, achieved £2.1 million bottom-line improvement within the first year through turning £800k loss into £1.3 million profitability, led rapid turnaround of Telecoms Business Unit by signing three major contracts with two of the largest mobile operators and a retailers. Within a period of three years revenues increased from £20 million to £48 million in after sales services and technology logistics services, improving EBIT by 10% in a highly competitive/ declining market.

    January 2003 — September 2004 (1 year 9 months)

Divested from loss making activities in Jackson Mississippi and strategically re-positioned company in Dallas Fort Worth. Fostered enterprise wide vision to instil a new sense of direction, revised overall cost structure and heightened profits, energised growth in domestic and international markets, which led to confidence in the financial community.
