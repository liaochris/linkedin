Bryan Rank’s Summary

I am a business and technical expert with an intimate understanding of the order fulfillment and billing life cycles of broadband network service providers. Specialties include; inside & outside plant engineering, automated network resource allocation, and subscriber driven service order management and network element activation.

Bryan Rank’s Specialties:

On the business side: Proven track record in sales, business development and product evangelism, RFP/RFI authoring and responding. Proof of concepts and customer management.

Bryan Rank’s Experience

  • OSS Expert

    Volubill

    (Privately Held; Telecommunications industry)

    November 2008 — Present (11 months)

    VoluBill offers unique "on the network" charging and control technology to provide the basis for a range of high value, real-time applications such as revenue management, intelligent usage policy management and subscriber usage monitoring.

    The combination of VoluBill DPI (Deep Packet Inspection) technology and The DCP (Dynamic Charging Platform) provide customers with the most advanced pre/post paid revenue generation platform in the industry. The system is capable of:

    • Managing bandwidth based on subscriber and service centric usage policies & quotas in order to maximize revenues & service experience and eliminate churn

    • Eliminates revenue leakage and fraud via the enforcement of real-time spending limits and usage quotas across all services and payment methods

    • Realizes rapid time to revenue for new product offerings through ease of implementation and “drop in” network integration, irrespective of the underlying network technology.

  • CTO (VP Operations)

    Cadtel Systems

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Telecommunications industry)

    January 2005 — October 2008 (3 years 10 months)

    CADTEL Systems is a software company dedicated to providing Service Fulfillment Solutions to Broadband Service Providers.

    Specifically we provide proven technology for GIS enabled inside & outside plant engineering. The team is expert at developing network aware business process management (BPM) tools. We have a proven track record of utilizing those tools to automate traditionally time consuming labor intensive processes such as network service activation and network resource allocation. Our goal is to streamline the process of ordering services from Broadband Network Operators. We have also been successful training our customer's IT staff to use our plant and BPM tools themselves to solve their own problems as an alternative to purchasing the services from us.

    I am directly responsible for our technical direction and software development resources. I am also intimately involved with our customer support, project implementation and sales cycles.

  • Systems Support Specialist II

    Georgia Institute of Technology, College of Computing

    (Educational Institution; 501-1000 employees; Computer & Network Security industry)

    January 1994 — June 1997 (3 years 6 months)

    I had the supreme good fortune of be allowed to work at the College of Computing's Network Services Group (CNS) while attending graduate school at Georgia Tech. As a help desk monkey I learned about customer service in one of the challenging academic environments in the country. I spent the better part of a year with a small team pulling old thicknet 10base5/AUI and coax cables out of the walls and ceilings of the College, replacing them with 10/100baseT.

    I enlisted into the Army as a way to pay for college.
