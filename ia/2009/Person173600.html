Tse Mei Ng’s Experience

  • Editor

    The Oswegonian

    (Newspapers industry)

    October 2007 — May 2009 (1 year 8 months)

    The Oswegonian is the multi award-winning college newspaper of State University of New York (SUNY) of Oswego. I was successively the Copy Editor, Asst. News Editor and Laker Life (Entertainment & Lifestyle) Editor.

    In those capacities, I managed and revamped the entertainment section, launched and managed the World and U.S. News section, developed and taught layout and copy editing crash courses. My experience in those tasks includes the menial works of assigning and writing stories, section layout, arranging and conducting interviews, participating in press calls and press conferences, fact checking, and proofreading articles for grammatical and AP style errors.

    In the 2008-2009 press awards season, The Oswegonian received the First Place Award from the American Scholastic Press Association, as well as the Third Place Award for the highly competitive Society of Professional Journalists (SPJ) Region 1 Best All-Around Non-Daily Student Newspaper.

  • Teacher & Examiner

    Tun Huang Music School

    (Music industry)

    June 2005 — June 2007 (2 years 1 month)

    Tun Huang Music School is one of the premiere Chinese Cultural Arts center in Malaysia, specializing in Music (Qin), Chess (Qi), Caligraphy (Shu) and Painting (Hua), with emphasis on Chinese classical music education. Tun Huang is headquartered in SS2 PJ, with over 30 branches scattered around Peninsular Malaysia.

    I gave one-to-one music lessons on the Chinese classical music instrument Gu-Zheng, and coached students for examination and performance. I travelled around the country as an acredited gu-zheng music examiner. I also transcript and arrange songs into gu-zheng pieces to help expand the musical syllabus at Tun Huang.
