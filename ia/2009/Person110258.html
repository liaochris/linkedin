Jen McCabe’s Summary

Jen McCabe is CEO/Founder of Contagion Health, an early stage consumer-centric health software firm born in 2009 to advance participatory medicine.

Contagion Health designs+builds mHealth (mobile health) and eHealth applications to incentivize healthier daily behaviors ("microchoices") created when consumers use technology for wellness, including Observations of Daily Living (#ODLs) and personal biometrics ("me-trics").

Contagion's first app, Get Up and Move! (http://getupandmove.me), coded by Andrey Petrov, allows users to challenge each other via Twitter by 'bartering' microfitness (dance for 2 songs and I'll run for 5 minutes). Planned releases will include team challenges, geolocation, and enable integration of employer incentives. A mobile version is also in design.

Contagion Health will also build a mobile application for users to participate in "olympic" fitness challenges, built in the spirit surrounding the 2010 Olympic Games.

A hospital-based web+mobile game with QR code integration and a personal health narrative platform are longer term Contagion Health goals.

In August 2009, McCabe designed concepts for 2 award-winning mHealth applications (for iPhone and Android) coded and demoed by volunteer teams at Silicon Valley hackathons (iPhoneDevCamp 3 and Google's GTUG Campout).

A Kisaut Fellow at the Health Strategy Innovation Cell, Massey College, University of Toronto, McCabe's projects include work on low-tech, high-impact service-oriented design from the patient perspective, including the #getupandmove campaign. Her academic research focuses on the role (theoretical and applied) of narrative self-identification and microrelevance in individual health/wellness decision-making.


@jensmccabe is a prolific blogger, "tweet," keynote speaker (Mayo Clinic, Health 2.0, Medicine 2.0, Eli Lilly), and advocate in health and technology communities, online and offline, particularly in the mobile, tech and HIT startup spaces.
