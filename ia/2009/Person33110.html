    Working for Kaiser Group was a very multi-faceted job that greatly challenged my ability to wear several hats. My only regret was that the contract we were working under was awarded to another organization 16 months after I was hired.

    My job involved working for a program that unemployed or underemployed delinquent child support cases were court-ordered to participate in. As a Case Manager, I would set up a job seeking plan with individuals, based on their ability, skills, and their job seeking knowledge. I met with everyone weekly and gave them a weekly plan, which they were responsible for completing. It involved a certain number of employer contacts and applications, as well as requiring them to attend workshops for resume writing, interview skills and the like, depending on where their challenged areas might be. When they reported back the next week I went over the plan with them to determine whether they were in compliance, or not. Prior to their next hearing, I was trusted to submit a status report to the court. This report was crucial in determining what, if any, legal action should be taken on an individual.

    This job was a total diversion on my career path, but it was quite a revelation and life-changing experience for a guy who has always been in commissioned-driven positions where results and success are based on numbers.
