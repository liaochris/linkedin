Joseph Tomaino’s Summary

Healthcare consultant with senior management experience in hospitals and continuing care health systems, and governance responsibility for a major academic tertiary medical center and a community based home care agency. Has taught graduate level clinical and management courses in several colleges, and held a full time assistant dean appointment at an Ivy League university.

Consulting engagements have included operation reviews and performance improvement initiatives, revenue cycle analysis, compliance and due diligence studies, and HIPAA privacy and security gap analysis and program development. Also has served as a patient care ombudsman for healthcare bankruptcies for the Office of the United States Trustee.

My goal is to provide my clients with support and assistance to help them to be successful in this challenging healthcare environment. Because I have managed healthcare organizations, I bring practical and tested solutions to each of my engagements.

Joseph Tomaino’s Specialties:

Clinical operations, quality improvement, budgeting clinical services, revenue enhancement, strategic planning, board governance, management coaching, program development, bankruptcy and turnaround management.

    (Public Company; 5001-10,000 employees; HRB; Accounting industry)

Costs of labor and supplies continue to rise steadily. And to add insult to injury, the reimbursement for the care provided continues to be scaled back by government and commercial payers. In my current role, I use my years of experience as both a provider and as a consultant to help my clients analyze their challenges and to identify and implement innovative and effective solutions. Because I have managed facilities, my practice is reality based and grounded. Because of my academic background, my solutions are creative and outcome driven. I am an effective change agent to help organizations plan to meet the evolving needs of their consumers, get the reimbursement they deserve for care provided, and contain costs to ensure ongoing viability.
