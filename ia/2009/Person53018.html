Dan Garlick’s Summary

I have a background in radio, bullion and commodity futures sales and hospitality services prior to starting my company in 1999.
I have a Diploma in Multimedia.

I left school at the end of 1978 and moved into a magazine publishing partnership with a couple of friends. At the end of 1979 I started working in a store that bought and sold gold and silver bullion and coins. This was an exciting period as I moved to Sydney with the company returning to Brisbane 18 months later. Following that I transferred to Adelaide for 6 months to trade in commodity futures. Upon returning to Brisbane I started working in the hospitality industry for the next 7 years with a one year stint in the commodity futures industry in Sydney in the middle.
I studied a radio announcing course in Brisbane in 1988 coming top of the class and secured my first radio job in Lithgow, NSW at the beginning of 1988.
I stayed there for 2 years and moved back to Brisbane in 1990 to a job in radio there.
In 1991 I moved to Tasmania "for a look" and have now settled here. I started working in radio and then moved into TV voiceovers while running a community radio station south of Hobart.
I started Ozvox Media in 1999 and have not looked back since. I grew the business to a stage where I had 5 staff but upon taking over the state directorship of BNI in Tasmania in 2005, I found I did not have enough time to do two jobs and consequently downsized the firm to where it is now specifically concentrating on audio and video production. I now act as a media consultant and only take on specific clients. I also continue to grow the BNI brand in the state region of Tasmania
