Allan White’s Summary

Prior to joining Guidon, Allan was associated with JPMorgan Chase. This tenure included roles as site manager for a government lockbox operation as well as an internal consultant with the Consultant Services Group. During his time with CSG, Allan successfully merged excellent facilitation skills with a background of lean and Six Sigma in a number of Kaizens in the bank’s operations departments.

Allan also has a background in the healthcare industry with ten years at General Electric Medical Systems (now GE Healthcare) and with New South Healthcare-Hospice at Charlotte. At GE, he spent 7 years in Field Service Operations and his last 3 years as a Green Belt and then as a Black Belt in Field Service. During his Six Sigma tenure, he coached and mentored both team and individual projects across the Southeastern United States. Allan was recognized for his efforts in making “Six Sigma real” for clients with a Management Award for “Making Six Sigma the Way We Work.” At New South Health Care, he worked with the Medical Director in establishing relationships between diagnosis, referring physicians and medical specialty and their impact on patient length of stay at Hospice. The results of this effort provided the Medical Director better information with which to engage physician to physician “marketing” of the organizations services.

Allan began his career on active, world wide duty with the United States Air Force and has continued his global exposure with corporate work in Mexico, Brazil, Argentina and Canada. His global cultural experience has been enhance via personal travel to Africa, Europe and South / Latin America including Argentina, Ecuador, Chile and Paraquay.

Allan has a both a graduate and undergraduate degree in business from the University of North Carolina at Charlotte. He and his wife currently reside near his hometown of Davidson, North Carolina.
