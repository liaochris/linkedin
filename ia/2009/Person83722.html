  • Operations Director

    Royalblue

    (Public Company; 201-500 employees; RYB; Information Technology and Services industry)

    December 1999 — November 2000 (1 year)

    Recruited to integrate 4 divisions:
    Operations, Education, Customer and Professional Services and to harmonise (yet again) systems, processes and people.

    Brought in a new leadership team and established a new culture based solely on merit and performance.

    Reduced attrition from 42% to 24%, grew team by 18%. Increased revenues by 32% and profit by 14%. Overall, another exciting and demanding adventure.

    Here I learnt some powerful lessons:

    1. Give people what they want, not what they need.
    2. Do the job the way the people who hired you want it done.
    3. When people hire you they want to be reassured about two things:

    a. Can he do the job? (With my eyes closed - I loved it!)
    b. Is he one of us? (Sadly I wasn't and was fired when I didn't fit the established senior leadership approach).

    Looking back on it now, I can see how we weren't a good fit.
    At the time it was much harder to accept: I'd done a great job and still got fired!

Gary Haslam’s Education

  • The Open University

    BSc. (Hons), Psychology, 1993 — 1997

    After 14 years of studying and working as an Electronics Engineer - I'd had enough. And so in 1993, I formally began a lifelong journey to understand what really makes people tick.

    Studying with the Open University is tough - Its all self-study.
    You've already got a busy, demanding job; family commitments and an active social life and you decide that that's not enough. You want some more education as well.

    At the time, I was stationed in Germany and didn't have a lot of choices. In the end it was a simple reality: Open University or Open University.

    Every year for 6 years: 6 x 2000 word essays - 1 every 6 weeks for 9 months at a demanding level of writing and comprehension, with no teachers or peers to learn from.
    If you can succeed educationally here, you can succeed anywhere.

    (At the time of writing the Open University is ranked 6th highest in the UK of all universities - this includes all distance learning AND full time courses)

  • INLPTA Trainer Training

    NLP 1995 — 1995

    On 5 August 1995 I attended INLPTA's second European Trainer training. 3 weeks with some of the brightest, most capable trainers and people on the planet.

    Wyatt Woodsmall - Mr. 'Personal Development'. An outstanding author, speaker and systems thinker.

    Marvin Oka - 10 years on the stage as a Magician in Vegas. His presence and personal impact has to be experienced to be believed - impressive, truly impressive.

    Richard Diehl - Feldenkrais Trainer, Trained Rolfer and Guru in all things Bodywork - just watching this man walk was a lesson and experience all by itself!

    Wyatt put together an outstanding team of trainers, support staff and students and created an absolute riot of learning and development.

    Imagine a mix of 70 Europeans with some Australians and Americans thrown in; together for 21 days and nights, laughing and learning together. A rich, boisterous cacophony of cultures, languages, ideas, opinions and fun!

    For me a life-changing experience - Thanks Wyatt!
