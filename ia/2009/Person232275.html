Thomas Møller Jensen’s Summary

I usually say i did my first programming when i assembled a TV tennis game back in the mid 70's in my Dad's TV repair shop. Since that i have done most it, Fortran on punch cards, Pascal, a lot of proprietary sh**, until i jumped on the MS train when DOS 3.1 was released, and went for web programming back when a 9600 bps modem was state of the art. In 2000 i relocated to Seattle to subcontract at Microsoft, first working for Rainier Technologies, later Compaq and HP. Program/Project Manager and Architect on many demo applications for product launches, .NET, BizTalk, Commerce Server, CMS, ..., and consulting on joint MS/HP projects. Working with MS product teams inspired my interest in development methods/process and technologies. My other track of interest is architecture, with a preference for SOA, Design Patterns, Contract First, eXtreme Programming and cross platform integration.
Currently i work for IBM Global Services as Enterprise Architect
