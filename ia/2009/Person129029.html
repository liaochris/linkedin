Keith MacKay’s Summary

At Village, I have been responsible for over 1000 custom software projects (80+ projects for Harvard Business School; over a dozen projects for Microsoft; typically 100 projects a year). I have served as President of the MIT Club of Boston, a 1500-member volunteer organization for Boston-area MIT alums. I speak about current and future technology trends, use of technology in non-profits, and managing/motivating volunteers. I currently teach in Northeastern University's online MBA program. I've recently been doing lots of Data Warehousing (large insurance company, oil refining company, higher-ed institution), closely following the Ralph Kimball methodology. I've done a lot of writing (Microsoft course materials, co-author for a Que Publishing book on Microsoft Office, etc.)

Keith MacKay’s Specialties:

Project management. Data warehousing. SQL Server, SSIS, SSRS, SSAS. .NET development for desktop and Web. Nonprofit organization management. CRM. Microsoft Office automation/integration. Automation and reporting systems. Technology consulting, explaining tech in understandable language. Speaking, writing.
