Bob Eidson’s Summary

The current dislocation in the real estate capital markets has revealed extraordinary value for private capital placement. Even without the Home Affordable Modification Program, loan acquisitions and compassionate modifications are economically attractive opportunities. Due to unresolved tax complications in securitized trusts (RMBS), mass modifications will continue to be unrealistic; at least until Congress/FASB/IRS address FAS 140 & the REMIC statutes.

Most recently, my model for private capital modifications was featured in Housing Wire magazine (April 2009). In addition to my experience with two west coast hedge funds, I have leveraged my experience to create an attractive fund structure to our investors with the opportunity for outsized returns.

In reality, home affordability in CA, OR, & WA will be a pure function of job growth. Robust job growth is not likely to return until late 2010. This is the reason why compassionate and realistic loan modifications make economic sense. Before the financial crisis is "over" it is entirely possible that we could see an implosion of the commercial debt world and a collapse, or nationalization, of one, or more, of the top nine financial institutions. So, the best case scenario is that we have stabilized and can expect growth. The worst case scenario is that we are in the eye of the storm, and the back end could look as ugly as the front end.
