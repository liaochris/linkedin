Rina Goodman’s Summary

I am the owner of a mediation practice serving individuals and families who are experiencing conflict in marriage, divorce, relationship dissolution, sibling relationships, probate, estate planning, elder care planning, and family business succession.

Before becoming a mediator, I briefly consulted on commercial law matters for a law firm and served as an Assistant Circuit Executive for the Second Circuit Court of Appeals where I worked on exhibitions on legal history. In Washington DC, I had the honor of working with several highly regarded attorneys in the specialized fields of aviation and maritime accidents. I enjoyed handling complex legal and financial issues pertaining to damages and facilitating multi-million dollar settlements for some of my clients. Later on, I worked in the areas of employment discrimination, civil rights, and products liability.

My Core Values

I have a deep understanding of the human costs of litigation (e.g., the loss of self-determination, privacy and opportunities for healing). I was drawn to mediation because it offers more hope for parties to achieve win/win solutions even when their conflict appears to be intractable. I believe that, with compassionate and skilled guidance, even very entrenched parties can learn to work collaboratively on finding ways to resolve their conflict. When necessary, I coach my clients on communication skills and empathic listening and encourage them to create new frameworks for measuring ‘fairness.’ For family members who will have a continuing relationship, mediation offers opportunities for new perspectives, improved communication skills, and understanding.

Professional Recognition

Throughout my professional career as a lawyer and mediator, I has been commended by employers, clients, associates, and judges for my professional knowledge, integrity, and commitment. In 2007 I was recognized by the Dispute Resolution Center of King County as Mediator of the Year.

Rina Goodman’s Specialties:

Mediation & facilitation of family conflict issues related to: marriage, divorce, LGBT relationships, cohabitation, elder care, estate planning, probate, will contests, family business succession planning, open adoption -- and any other family matter.
