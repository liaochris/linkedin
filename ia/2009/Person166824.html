Mitchell London’s Summary

I am a software executive and financier active in a number of technology businesses. My entrepreneurial or investment focus tends to be related to educational development & eLearning.

I am a 1/3rd partner in the iCanMentor.com business venture. This website, which is currently in pre-beta usage is a connecting point for tutors, coaches, trainers and their potential clients.

My team at Headroom Learning created a comprehensive math tutoring program that is available throughout most major retailers in North America. It is called Math Success Deluxe 2007, published by TOPICS Education.

Together with partners, I also operate a safe (spam-free) email service for kids (see: www.kidmail.net) and a service for registering for scholastic chess events (see: www.signup4chess.com).

Most of my software development is implemented "offshore". I travel frequently to India. The core of my team is located in Chandigarh. I also engage freelance software developers and artists in the Middle East, Europe, and Asia as well as the US and Canada.

Currently I am a member of the Board of Directors or Board of Advisors for the following companies:

- Astrobotic Technology, Inc. (Pittsburgh, Tucson, Redmond) One of the most promising contenders to win the Google Lunar X Prize.

- SIMPOL Ltd (Cambridge, UK) www.simpol.com
Developers of the next generation of the classic SuperBase development platform

A leading manufacturer of USB 2.0 based servo motion controllers

Mitchell London’s Experience

  • Partner, Co-founder & CTO

    iCanMentor Payment Services

    (E-Learning industry)

    May 2007 — Present (2 years 2 months)

    iCanMentor Payment Services provides a web-based system used by professional and part-time tutors, coaches and trainers.

    Parents (or adult students) use iCanMentor to search for and compare services from various skilled teachers, and easily register for courses or recurring time slots. Search is primarily local, but it can also be national or global, especially for eLearning.

    The system enables tutors, coaches, and trainers to actively promote their services, schedule sessions, track cash payments, and process online credit card transactions. iCanMentor also facilitates social networking among like-minded professionals. For example: to share lesson plans and strategies.

    Last but not least, iCanMentor.com also provides an integrated state-of-the-art system for one-to-one and multi-student "distance e-Learning" sessions using audio, video, collaborative whiteboard, and many more online classroom features.

  • President/CEO

    ConnectSoft, Inc.

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Computer Software industry)

    January 1988 — May 1996 (8 years 5 months)

    I founded ConnectSoft in 1988, shortly after leaving Microsoft. Working out of my home/basement I recruited 5 undergraduate interns from CMU to spend the summer working with me. Gradually we grew this into a $12 million/year software developer/publisher, with multi-million relationships with UPS, MCI, and SpaceLabs Medical.

    I sold ConnectSoft to a publicly traded holding company (NASD:AUGI) in 1996 for $22 million.

    ConnectSoft consulting division sold to United Parcel Service, and later spun out as iShip.com (NASD:STMP). ConnectSoft "NTerprise" division was spun out and sold shortly thereafter to GraphOn Corporation (NASD:GOJO).

    For a little over a year, I also remained on the Board of Directors of "ConnectSoft Ruksun Software" (Pune, India). This Indian company was later renamed "Ruksun Software Technologies Pvt. Ltd." I helped attract VC investment from ICICI Ventures in India.

    Note: ConnectSoft was originally formed as "Adonis Corporation".
