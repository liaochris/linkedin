Superna Mehta’s Summary

ASIC/System-on-Chip/IC Physical Design & EDA Applications + Project Management

Technical lead responsible for implementing and managing several ASICs contributing to multi-million-dollar design successes to both startups and large corporations. Over 12 years of hands-on experience in RTL to GDS/Netlist to GDS completion and flow development using Synopsys, Cadence, Magma & Mentor Graphics tools. Synthesis, floorplanning, place & route, clock tree synthesis through chip finishing to silicon. Tape-out Closures for Timing, Signal Integrity (SI), Power & Yield (DFM/DFY) & DRC/LVS down to 45nm.

Recent achievements/Special interests:

• Low power designs: dynamic & static power reduction, including multi-vt, multi-voltage supply (MSV)/voltage islands, clock gating, and power gating/shutdown techniques
• Design Optimizations for DFM/Yield
• SI Crosstalk/Noise prevention & fix. On-chip variation (OCV) & Multi-corner multi-mode (MCMM) optimizations
• IR drop analysis and resolution
• High-speed (GHz), hierarchical designs and flip-chips

Applications:
Fiber Channel over Ethernet (FCoE), Network Switching Processor, High Definition CMOS Image Sensors for Digital Camcorders/Medical & Surveillance images & Video, Network Security, Server Management Chipsets for PC Servers & Workstations, Digital Cameras, Wireless Modem, Storage

Superna Mehta’s Specialties:

• Synopsys: Design Compiler (DC/DC-topo), IC Compiler (ICC), Astro, Primetime, Primetime SI (PT-SI), Formality, StarRC-XT, Hercules, Jupiter XT
• Magma: Blast Create/Blast RTL, Blast Fusion, Blast Noise, Blast Rail, Blast Power, Blast DFT, Blast Yield
• Cadence: SOC Encounter/First Encounter (FE), Nanoroute, RTL Compiler, Celtic NDC, Voltage Storm
• Mentor Graphics: Calibre, Calibre RVE
• tcl & perl programming
