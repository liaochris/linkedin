Jim Paymar’s Summary

Jim is the President and founder of Paymar Communications Group, a leading edge Media Training, Presentation Training and Consulting firm based in New York.

Clients enlist PCG's services to advance their executives communication skills, get messages straight and elevate their public profile and that of their company in the media.

Jim is best known for his work as a business correspondent for CNBC, national correspondent for Fox, Senior Financial Anchor at BusinessWeek, General Manager of KCNS-TV in San Francisco and Editorial Director at KNTV in San Jose.

Combined with his experience as SVP of Corporate Communications at PepsiCo in New York, EVP and Director of Communications at ICIC in Boston, and his senior broadcast experience at some of the largest network TV affiliates on the West Coast, Paymar is uniquely qualified when it comes to knowledge of how media works from the inside and the outside.

Paymar Communications Group specializes in Reputation Management Services, raising the bar for a company, its executives, as well as government officials and spokespersons so they become competent, composed communicators, telling a more compelling story, developing powerful headlines, giving memorable interviews, speeches and presentations and getting the positive story out about their orgranization.

PCG’s Reputation Management practice uses a four step process, assessment, strategic planning, media training and placement to ensure that a company avoids the pitfalls of negative stories and also develops positives stories that insure a solid reputation on Main Street as well as Wall Street.

Jim Paymar’s Specialties:

Media and presentation training clients include financial giants, Bank of America, JP Morgan Chase, Wells Fargo Bank, Barclays Global Investors and ABN AMRO.
He has also trained executives at PepsiCo, Allstate, Pfizer, Time Inc., Citigroup Smith Barney, Novartis, BNP Paribas, ING Investment Managers and the Globe.com
