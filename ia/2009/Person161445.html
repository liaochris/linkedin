Michael Houghton | Ohio Photographer’s Summary

Michael Houghton has been working in Photography of all genre, for over 25 years. Located in Downtown Columbus, Ohio since 1983, he he focuses on architectural, interior design and fashion photography, as well as photo journalism and environmental portraits.

Michael Houghton's specialty 'developed' over the years as Architectural and Interior Design Photography. However his professional and environmental portraiture and journalism are in use State wide and Nationally and in the last couple of years the portfolio has grown to include hair and makeup fashion images for Paul Mitchell and The Michael Farrell Salon. This work is providing the space for some ground breaking techniques for the studio that involves time based imaging as well as still. As well Michael's personal work of the American West is ongoing with a book in the offing.

Michael Houghton began shooting for the 'New York Times' as a stringer 6 years ago and now, also works for the 'Ohio Farm Bureau' and 'Ohio Magazine' as well and is also shooting assignments for 'House Trends Magazine'.

Michael began teaching for Columbus College of Art and Design in 1998 and continues teaching in an Adjunct capacity. He teaches Architectural Photography and courses in Basic Introduction to Photography/film based, as well as, Digital Imaging basics and Still life imaging.

Michael plans on continuing to shoot and image for the commercial world as well as what he sees as interesting and vital and lovely as well as the real world ugly or not.
