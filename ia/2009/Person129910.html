Kenji Onozawa’s Summary

I’ve been very actively involved in the Seattle social media community in many aspects; most recently becoming a board member of Social Media Club Seattle (SMC Seattle) where my responsibilities revolve around retrieving straight-to-air video content for use on our blog and as well as managing SMC Seattle’s YouTube page. In addition this, I’ve started my own blog called Seattle Social Media Profiles where I interview and profile key members of social media community within Seattle. Despite the blog being relatively young, the response to it has been very positive; I have been able to interview many successful social media professionals, including Microsoft’s Blog Queen, Betsy Aoki, Paolo Tosolini (Microsoft’s Podcast Guru) and Ethan Yarbrough, the President of Allyis.

While I’m not involved with these SMC Seattle or Seattle Social Media Profiles, I’ve been attending a variety of social media related events around the Sound, which have included the Seattle Podcasting Network Meetup and the Social Networking for Businesses Conference in Bellevue. Thanks to my involvement in these things, combined with my passion for social media, my understanding of these areas is constantly growing.

Other social media based events I’m involved in are Comcasts’ Volun-tweetup, as well as Twitter’s biggest fundraiser, Twestival. Furthermore to this, I’ve also been covered the Puget Sound Blood Center’s first blood drive tweetup, as well as Alaska Airlines first Aviation Geek Night which was primarily a twitter based promotion.

In addition to my experience, I also have a B.A. in Communication from WWU, which I received in 2005. During my time at WWU, I also took many courses in marketing and public relations as well. It’s because of my degree that I’ve feel like my strongest asset as a professional has been my ability to facilitate progress through communication (and a little laughter as well). If you speak with any of my references, I believe they would agree.

    (Online Media industry)

    April 2009 — Present (5 months)

    I created this website in April of 2009 as a way to nurture the social media community within Seattle. Seattle truly is a hub for social media - from non-profits to corporate giants to the news media, it seems there isn't an area within our city social media hasn't touched. With that said, I wanted a way to connect all these brilliant people and ideas and what better way to do that than using social media to talk about the social media community we have here within our city?

    The response to the website has been phenomenal and I've since added three more members to this project; an editor, and two content contributors. I've also expanded content in addition the profiles, which includes a weekly "Caught in the #SeaTweetStream" feature.

    We also have other projects in the works, which includes hopefully a monthly tweetup as well as more social media/technology event coverage.
