Eric Hillerns’s Summary

I work on issues related to brand. Some like to add an '-ing' to that term, but it just doesn't feel right to me. Sure, it's a process and we can provide a really good name. We can assign a visual identity. We can shape. We can make it beautiful. But really, it comes down to the client and their product or service. Brand is in the mind of the customer. We can't expect to change that mind but we'll make sure that our client's customer is at least thinking about it.

Eric Hillerns’s Specialties:

Identity development is how I make my money. Call it what you'd like; branding, graphic design, marketing. It's all about identity.

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Design industry)

    September 1997 — Present (11 years 5 months)

    Pinch is a strategic design company based in Portland, Oregon. We help our clients figure out what to say, to whom to say it, and how. I am one of two principals who chart this course.

    Some might describe a design practice with terms like brand development, brand stewardship, and so on. But in truth there is no codified process. Every problem is different; every solution is unique. We are able to relate and react to our clients' business problems because we are in business, and we've been at it for twenty years now. In practice, it means listening carefully, thinking, talking, and then doing. And then doing it all over again. It involves common sense, good taste and a little luck. It also involves real relationships between humans and all that entails: trust, confidence, honesty and open communication.

    If you're really interested in knowing more about Pinch, we encourage you to ring us up and come over for a drink.
