Craig Mungons’s Summary

Turning great ideas into effective communication is something I strive for with every project - Making Ideas Happen.

I have a well-rounded understanding of media-related marketing through my extensive experience at large advertising agencies helping creative directors, marketing executives and clients implement their strategies to effectively impact audiences with artistically designed messages. As a film and video production executive, I effectively manage relationships with advertising agencies, cost control consultants, film production, post-production and music production companies, and all other aspects of film and video production.

My organizational strength in the management of time, people, and resources allows me to enhance an idea without compromising the creative concept or marketing strategy. The breadth and depth of my film production experience across a variety of styles and executions helps ensure that the creative output of all my projects is of the highest quality, faithful to both the concept and project expectations. My exposure to a diverse roster of clients has afforded me a background that is very suitable for either a staff position or freelance work.

I’m interested in applying my talent and expertise as a film and video production executive in the related fields of marketing, media, entertainment and film production. I'd like to discuss with you where our professional interests might overlap and the unique perspective I can provide for your company.

    Below are two extensive projects I completed in 2008 as a freelance producer that can be viewed at www.craigmungons.com.

    In early 2008 I produced a four-minute webisode entitled “Invasion of the Bulls” directed by Tony Kaye for Ford Trucks. We shot a “live” event on the streets of NYC the first week of January with professional bull riders bucking 1800-pound bulls in a specially built outdoor arena adjacent to Madison Square Garden. We also shot storytelling scenes with super-duty trucks pulling livestock trailers and stopping to shoot real-people reactions along the way. It was a wild ride!

    In late 2008 I produced a seven-spot campaign for the launch of the reengineered and redesigned ‘09 Ford F-150 pick-up truck. The graphically driven concept is based on a “rant” using Denis Leary as the VO that visually shows the spoken word as “living” type integrated with graphic backgrounds and live-action vignettes of people interacting with the truck. They're crazy smart!
