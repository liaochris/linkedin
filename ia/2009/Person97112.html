Ian Houston was born in 1970 and is a born and bred liver-pudlian he currently holds the position as CEO of Origo Industries, previously he worked for the MOD as a Gunner Mechanical Engineer for the British Armoured Cavalry Regiment and spend 12 years serving his country.

He is a self taught Bio-Chemist and a family man with 2 children and one on the way. His purpose is to persuade current governments and corporations that there current strategy on delivering a global cut in emissions is wildly over quest-mated and therefore needs to deliver an alternative prior to Billions of pounds being spent unnecessarily, he also recognises a gratitude to his wife’s patience for helping him realise his life’s dream.

Ian is the sole inventor of the Ecobox; “through pioneering mechanics” he has invented a way of recycling C02, by capturing emissions and regenerating using algae - a feat that has long been recognised by the greatest scientists in the world as impossible. 25 years away, “it is said” ………………………….. ‘The impossible is now possible’.

Ian is a down to earth, dedicated individual who does not suffer fools gladly and would like to linkin with the likeminded individuals within the scientific research R&D, Bio Chemistry and engineering communities who are helping fight to turn the tide of climate change catastrophes including global warming.

    Origo was started at the end of 2007 on the belief that there is a better way to control, reduce and recycle CO2 emissions and in a way that can be easily deployed and commercialised. The company is owned by Ian Houston and supported by a board whose members are taken from the fields of commerce, engineering and bioscience.
    Origo’s immediate goals focus on the capture, storage and recycling of CO2 to produce bio fuels and other revenue generating materials, such as foodstuffs and building materials. These goals are being achieved through two current projects; one focused on the automotive industry and the other focused on the power generation industry.
