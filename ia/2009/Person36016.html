Buzz Busby’s Summary

Currently project managing and supporting the roll-out program of several start-up companies technology plans where I have developed or adapted a strategy to deliver the benefits to support the business objectives. These companies included a Customer Loyalty Card programme, a mobile phone platform utilising VoIP and an ‘out of the box’ Wireless Hotspot solution. This involves negotiation with suppliers and 3rd party contractors to fulfil the requirements. Between this and leaving British Airways I planned and commissioned a mesh wireless network for a company in Fuengirola. All of these projects have involved the managing of contract developers and external suppliers of different nationalities and business practices.
For 19 years I was at British Airways where I was involved in many aspects of developing the strategic IT capabilities of British Airways within the ITIL framework. I was an influential knowledge base and consultant to all facets of the core business, utilising my wealth of business and IT experience. Much of my time was spent explaining technology in layman’s terms and supporting the needs of the business departments whilst managing expectations. Many years were spent supporting Desktops, servers, networks and client software and applications. I spent much of the latter years in leading the drive for standardisation and reduced TCO (Total Cost of Ownership) in British Airways through the use of appropriate technology, project management and good communication. Large parts of my work include project and people managing service partners, (such as Honywell, Microsoft, Cisco, IBM and programmers) to assist their understanding of our business and its needs, in order for the partners to provide the service that matches those requirements. I received many awards for excellence for my commitment and dedication to the successful running of the business.
