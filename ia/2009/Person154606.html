Mary Parke’s Summary

I am the new Senior Instructional Designer at City College of San Francisco (CCSF) - an instrumental member of their award-winning Education Technology team. CCSF recently selected a new LMS, Moodle, to replace their existing WebCT installations (4.1, CE6). I advised on the Moodle implementation, integration with Banner via external database, and configured their installation as the systems administrator. I collaborated on the redesign of their current trainings to reflect Moodle. My current focus is on migration support & training for legacy LMS users (faculty) to the new LMS - including design, training, & support. I continue to participate in the Distance Teaching & Learning Moodle LMS Consortium hosted through San Francisco State.

Prior to this position, I was the Instructional Designer for Distance Learning at De Anza College. In that position I co-lead on the implementation, design, training, & support of a new Moodle installation. I analyzed existing resources (equipment/personnel), policies, processes, and procedures necessary to successfully implement a learning management system. I also: co-wrote policies and procedures; migrated courses from legacy LMS systems to the new one; created documentation for the software & administrative processes; created & conducted training for faculty; provided ongoing technical, instructional, and software/hardware troubleshooting support; and was integral in usability, accessibility, performance, reliability, and scalability tests of the software, enrollment, and maintenance systems affiliated with the LMS project.

I founded my own consulting business - MaryParke.com and provide consulting for various Moodle-using institutions.

My interests are in the current adaptation and implementation of LMS systems as well as the future of LMS systems, and the standardization of SCORM for modularized, flexible, timely delivery of elearning.
