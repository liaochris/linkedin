Scott Edward Anderson’s Summary

Scott Edward Anderson has over 16 years of experience in non-profit management, most recently as vice president for global development at Ashoka, a social venture capital organization investing in leading social entrepreneurs.

Prior to joining Ashoka's leadership group, Scott was a director at the Nature Conservancy (TNC), where he was responsible for securing major and principal gift contributions from high net worth prospects in support of global priorities, led a creative team developing philanthropic marketing concepts, products, and materials for the organization, and conceived TNC’s $5B capital campaign.

He also served as the lead fundraiser and director of development in three state chapters of the Conservancy: Pennsylvania, Alaska, and New York.

Scott serves on the advisory board of GreenMicrofinance, LLC, which develops and markets green energy products (including biofuel, solar, wind, and hydropower) and certifies microfinance institutions through its branded certification product.

As a media entrepreneur, Scott has founded print and online magazines, as well as the popular blog, The Green Skeptic, which covers climate change, social entrepreneurs, microfinance, and clean tech innovations and investments.

While at TNC, Scott was elected a John Sawhill Conservation Leadership Fellow and was part of its Marine and Climate Change strategy teams. He is currently a Senior Fellow with the Environmental Leadership Program (ELP) and co-chaired the organizing committee for ELP’s “Emerging Leaders, Emerging Solutions” climate change conference at the New Jersey Institute of Technology in 2007.

He has taught fundraising strategies at the University of Pennsylvania, as well as at conferences and workshops around the country.

Scott is also an accomplished and award-winning poet with many publications credits and public readings nationwide.
