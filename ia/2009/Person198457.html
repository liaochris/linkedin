Rick Bunch’s Summary

I am Managing Director of The Erb Institute for Global Sustainable Enterprise at the University of Michigan, which fosters global sustainable enterprise through interdisciplinary research and education initiatives, including our acclaimed MBA/MS Program. Utilizing a collaborative approach, we help business, government and civil society organizations to achieve meaningful progress toward sustainability. Created in 1996, the Erb Institute is a partnership between the School of Natural Resources and Environment and the Stephen M. Ross School of Business at the University of Michigan. The Institute fosters professional education, public outreach and scientific scholarship supportive of the transition to sustainability – that is, meeting the fundamental needs of a growing human population in an equitable manner within the means of nature.

I have 12 years experience with several non-profits and business education institutions helping them integrate sustainability into their programs.

In 2007-2008, as a Fellow of The Aspen Institute Business and Society Program, I developed an initiative to help Chinese business schools teach and research more about how business can contribute to social progress and environmental quality.


Previously I was executive director of Bainbridge Graduate Institute, one of the first schools to offer an MBA in Sustainable Business.

From 1996-2003 I was business education director at World Resources Institute, where I worked with business schools around the world to infuse sustainable business topics into their curricula and research.
