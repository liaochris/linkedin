Marcel Sorger’s Summary

There're a lot of topics I'm interested in ranging from hardcore coding to psychology and management. I’ve studied these topics extensively in university, on the job and in other aspects of my life. This makes me an excellent analyst and developer.
My extensive knowledge of computer systems combined with my focus on long term technical as well as business effects make me ideally suited as an architect.
Effective communication skills and political insight help me keep my projects moving in the right direction. Seeing all sides of a problem is critical for creating the optimum solution.
Possessing creativity and down to earth logic enables me to come up with unconventional but practical solutions.

Professionally I am always trying to sharpen my skills and to learn new technologies.

To create a quality solution I always try to balance the requirements of management, users, engineers and developers. Most requirements in the SPECTRUM dimensions witch stands for Secure, Performant, Elegant, Configurabe, Transparant, Robust, Usable and Maintainable. Normaly not all the stakeholders get what they want, but they all get what they need.

Architecture is key to any successful solution. Whether it’s an OO design with Patterns or a SOA, I’m always trying to achieve “Lose coupling, high cohesion”.

Talent 4 MS development
I currently specialize in .NET development and I believe the new .NET features will revolutionize the development process. At least 1 of the following technologies must be involved or I’m NOT interested in a project:
Linq - Elegant ORM for agile projects
Entity Framework - Full ORM, but easier than nhibernate
WCF - Change the communication protocol without changing code. Less architecture, more configuration.
WF - Bistalk for everybody. Executable diagrams for analyst.
WPF - Best of Windows and Web development together (and it only took 5 years to develop ;)
Silverlight - 1.0 is WPF as a HTML extension 2.0 is .NET everywhere.
