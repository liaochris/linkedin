Kim Fenolio’s Summary

I've been engaged in Social Media and the internet since before it received its title. In the early 90s I began by teaching myself how to code HTML using Notepad and created my own graphics using MS Paint. I created websites that defined who I was and allowed people the means to contact me.

Shortly after that I started working in an online gaming community that was in its infancy. This was a time where internet communities hadn't really even figured out how to identify themselves. By 1997 I attended my first community meet-up where people from all over the USA met in person for the first time. This past year I personally organized and ran a meet-up for a podcast (GirlsNightInPodcast.com) that I created in May of 2007. I have now met hundreds of people face-to-face via social media and have made more connections than would have ever been possible in previous years.

I have formal training in graphic design and multimedia via an associate’s degree. That training now allows me to display myself in an attractive way but also my communications. This is far beyond what many designers and/or social media artists are capable of. It's this vast array of capabilities that make what I can do so very marketable.

I know what it is to communicate and create meaningful relationships that last. I know how to market myself, a business or a brand with a communication strategy that involves the written word, spoken word or graphic appeal. Relationship building, brand building, marketing: it's all the same. We're just learning new and innovative ways to make it more efficient.

This is what I'm capable of.
