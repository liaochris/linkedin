Michael Horning’s Summary

Entrepreneurial sales / business development executive with more than 24 years of success in creating and developing innovative new markets across diverse industries, including radio advertising, health care products and services, sports marketing, retail energy, financial risk managment and digital out-of-home media networks. I excel at reviewing and understanding company financial statements and current market trends, to maximize long-term, large revenue relationships with my clients. Professional networking and strategic planning skills aid in my ability to identify, qualify, prioritize, design and implement business solutions that drive profitability for my clients and revenue for firms I represent. Experienced at communicating, presenting and selling to the C-Level of organizations.

Michael Horning’s Specialties:

Marketing and media/advertising sales in the global Digital Out Of Home Video Network market.

Michael Horning’s Experience

  • VP, Advertising Sales

    Premier Retail Networks

    (Public Company; 201-500 employees; Broadcast Media industry)

    December 2001 — August 2008 (6 years 9 months)

    Responsible for generating and growing annual sales revenue, by building relationships with both endemic and non-endemic advertisers for PRN's national in-store TV Networks. Engaged client sales, brand, marketing and corporate media teams as well as their respective ad agencies, to utilize PRN's national in-store TV network to create awareness, drive brand value and purchase intent.

  • Founder and Director

    Enron Media Services

    (Financial Services industry)

    April 2000 — August 2001 (1 year 5 months)

    Created an OTC futures market in advertising. Provided media price transparency and price certainty for both buyers and sellers of advertising inventory. This secondary market utilized financial risk management tools / derivatives; swaps, floors, caps and collars, to help buyers and sellers of advertising time and space, protect company profitability against advertising price volatility. Presented financial risk management options to the C-Level of organizations, where the contract was managed by the CFO or the Tresurer.

    Prior to creating EMS, Michael structured multi-year, fixed-price contracts in Enron's Pulp & Paper Trading group, as well as Enron's Energy Services group where he developed a 5-year fixed-price contract for natural gas with PepsiCo, the single largest user of natural gas at retail in the U.S.
