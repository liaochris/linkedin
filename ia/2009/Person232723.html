Tom Patton’s Summary

• Tom Patton is a finance oriented executive who has spent much of his career working with high growth and technology companies. He has taken on increasing management responsibilities and has served as Controller, Treasurer, VP-Finance, COO and CEO for a number of companies. He has had P&L responsibility and understands how all the business functions must work together for success. Tom is a strategic thinker, yet is not afraid to roll up his sleeves and perform a variety of functions spanning finance, operations, strategic marketing, human resources and general management.

• Tom’s latest management and ownership activity has been directed in developing a California based company with patented technology in micro-dosing fertilizers onto landscapes. This “Green “ technology saves water, reduces fertilizer needed and consequently eliminates fertilizer runoff. Under his management the Company developed prominent relationships with organic and natural fertilizer suppliers as well as marquis accounts like The Rose Bowl, Bellagio Hotel, and the cities of Santa Clarita and Pasadena, CA and Tempe, AZ.

• Tom previously served as the CFO of an educational software company that was spun out of Carnegie Mellon University. He was involved in raising over $20 million in venture financing and was hired as the #5 employee before it grew to more than 140 people. Carnegie Learning’s cognitive tutors are recognized as one of the top high school mathematics programs in the country. Tom was instrumental in developing site licensing and the revenue recognition guidelines.

• Tom has worked in a variety of businesses from software, wholesale and retail, manufacturing and service segments and understands various business models and the business drivers that can make each one of these types of companies successful. His formal training in finance came from 17 years at Ernst & Young where he eventually rose as a CPA to Partner in the Entrepreneurial Services Group.

Tom Patton’s Specialties:

Finance and control; management reporting, business process re-engineering using NetSuite, strategic planning, M&A due diligence and operations analysis. He is also proficient in driver-based financial and operations planning using Alight driver based Planning and Analytics software.

Tom teams with strong sales and marketing teams since he understands their focus and works with them to develop the systems and sales management infrastructure that allow them to scale quickly and profitably.

    (Environmental Services industry)

    January 2003 — Present (6 years 11 months)

    EZ-FLO manufactures fertilizer dispensing systems that connect directly to any in-ground irrigation system. Using patented technology, EZ-FLO proportions minute amounts of nutrients into the irrigation water. Feeding plants consistently in small doses improves the overall health of the plant material and reduces the amount of fertilizer required.

    EZ-FLO systems are in use at prestigious properties (i.e.Bellagio Hotel and The Rose Bowl) as well as tens of thousands of residences throughout the US, Canada, South Africa and Australia.

    Tom was instrumental in developing strategic marketing programs (alliance partnerships, authorized installer training and web based marketing programs) to create end-customer awareness of EZ-FLO's value proposition. That along with the introduction of value pricing and attention to product costing, sales at EZ-FLO increased by 70% over a five year period with increased gross margin percentages going from 24% to over 52% during the same time.
