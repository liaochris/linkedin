Eric Miller’s Experience

  • Senior Memory Validation Engineer

    Numonyx

    (Public Company; INTC; Semiconductors industry)

    April 2008 — Present (1 year 2 months)

    Component Design Engineer and Product Validation Engineer, Numonyx BV, Folsom, CA (April 2008 – Current)
    • Programmed microcontroller firmware to enable the device to read the entire memory array in response to an IO command and record failing IOs and addresses to scratch RAM. Defined circuits needed by the microcode and the required interactions between the circuits, the microcode, and the test equipment. Resulted in 10% decrease in wafer level test times. Implementation was novel as this level of self-test had not been done previously.
    • Programmed microcontroller firmware to gracefully handle complicated user interrupts to Flash Cell program and erase operations. Firmware detects if an address that was being programmed was corrupted during an interrupt of the program operation. If corruption occurred, the firmware programs special bits to maintain coherency. Involved complex interactions among erase, program, and suspend handler portions of the firmware.
    • Implemented bit-field constructs in firmware to more elegantly manage the use of many status variables. Resulted in more manageable code and removed microcontroller memory limitations.
    • Validated and debugged microcode by constructing varying test cases and analyzing the results using Modelsim/Logic+ simulations
    • Discovered and debugged a logic circuit issue related to above tasks by reading schematics and examining VHDL code, resulting in circuit changes
    • Managed silicon Product Validation for 65nm flash memory product requiring coordination of efforts among varied cross-functional teams and cultures (China, Philippines, US)
    • Championed and employed Theory of Constraints in Project Management resulting in shipment of samples to customers one month ahead of schedule
    • Drove timely resolution of over 200 circuit related issues by methodically tracking and working them through the functional teams

  • Senior Product Development Engineer

    Intel

    (Public Company; INTC; Semiconductors industry)

    1993 — 2008 (15 years)

    • Wrote and debugged test programs on ATE to detect defects in array and periphery circuits of Flash Devices (3 product families on progressively narrower litho technologies)
    • Performed the Sort (wafer-level) Test start-up for manufacturing of 0.8u Flash Memory in Fab 7 by analyzing yields, gross-fail areas, and test times, understanding root causes, creating roadmaps for improvement, and implementing solutions
    • Drove root-cause fix of depressed yield due to a process fabrication and layout interaction by correlating wafer and unit level failures to specific wafer locations, experimenting to determine failing circuit and layout structures, and collaborating with Process Integration to construct a solution. Resulted in 100% yield increase.
    • Performed failure analysis on qualification rejects, bucketed the failures into like root causes, and developed screens to detect the failures earlier in the manufacturing flow, resulting in lower DPM
    • Improved quality of manufacturing (wafer and unit) test tape releases by chairing a Test Change Control Board and implementing systems to insure changes were validated through well-thought-out experiments with proper documentation and analysis of results. Reviewed and dispositioned over four Whitepapers (each with 10’s of changes) per week.
    • Managed silicon Product Validation of four Flash memory products spanning three process generations (130nm to 65nm). Defined, prioritized and coordinated the activities of cross-functional, cross-geographic teams of Design, Characterization, Quality and Reliability, Wafer Test, Unit Test, and System Validation engineers. Each of those products was sampled and qualified on schedule to key cellular phone customers.
    • Demonstrated excellent customer service by working directly with cell phone customers to understand their needs and problems using the devices and by providing options for and implementing solutions.
