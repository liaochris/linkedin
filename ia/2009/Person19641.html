Arjan de Raaf’s Summary

In the early 90's Arjan de Raaf exploited his cable information services on television channels from the cable companies freed up for this purpose. In 1996 he sold his company to the largest publishing company in the Netherlands, VNU (now Nielsen), and went looking for a new challenge.

Together with Gert-Jan Strik, Arjan decided to set up Media Highway International in 1997. The years after the launch of the first product RealTracker, Arjan also set up different other Internet companies like PublisherBucks and a local US news website (Yolone). August 2008 Arjan did move to the United States to set up a sales office for his Dutch products and expand with new businesses.

  • CEO

    Media Highway International

    (Internet industry)

    1996 — Present (13 years)

    Media Highway International was founded in the summer of 1996 and developed a service that enabled visitors to give their opinion on the quality and contents of a website. In the Netherlands, Compass Interactive had a monopoly in the area of website statistics. Their page counter offered quantitative information about websites. Media Highway International came up with the idea of offering qualitative information in the same way Compass Interactive provided their clients with quantitative information. Suddenly, the picture was complete: Media Highway International should offer a package including both qualitative and quantitative information. The total idea for this product (named RealTracker) was born and was successfully introduced on the market in 1997. At present, RealTracker has grown into an internationally operating enterprise with more than a million companies and individuals that use the RealTracker products.
