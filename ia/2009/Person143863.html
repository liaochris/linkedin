Lou Flores’s Summary

When Lear Corporation promoted Lou Flores from Product Engineer to Project Manager—he suddenly found himself racing to factories across the USA and Mexico, fixing production problems and making quick decisions in extremely high-pressure situations. “I learned that only the strong survive,” he says. Another critical asset was his fluency in Spanish and English.

Flores graduated from Florida State University in 1997, a newly minted chemical engineer. He began his career at Security Plastics, where he became expert in the injection-molding process and quickly demonstrated a natural knack for troubleshooting.

In 2000 Lear Corporation - - the world’s biggest manufacturer of automobile interiors - - hired Lou as a Product Engineer. He again showed a natural talent for solving difficult engineering and production problems, for example, inventing a plastic component that solved a critical quality problem. (Two years later, Lou’s invention won U.S. patent.)

In 2003 he was promoted to Project Manager. Lear Corporation presented Lou with an award and commended him for “acting in a concise and effective manner during critical situations.”

During the next five years (2003-2008) he won steady promotions and increasing responsibility through engineering and project-management ranks. Most recently, he managed $100 million automotive manufacturing programs for International Automotive Components.

His expertise includes launching complex manufacturing plants, Overseas Outsourcing, cost cutting, capital budgeting, six-sigma quality and P&L improvement.

Lou is a natural leader, consistently commended for excellence by clients, peers and management. He complements on-the-job experience with intense pursuit of professional-development coursework.
