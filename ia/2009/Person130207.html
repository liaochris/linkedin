Ken Porter’s Summary

Professional profile

An enthusiastic, innovative and accomplished project manager – a techno-literate communications generalist with more than thirty years’ experience working in private and public sectors. Strong strategic, creative, technical, commercial and interpersonal competencies – across a wide range of design, marketing and public relations disciplines.

Key skills

Communications: Marketing communications and PR strategy and implementation; writing and editing, and presentation, persuasion and negotiation.

Organisational: Methodical and meticulous workload prioritisation, highly aware of the needs and aspirations of an organisation’s staff, key stakeholders and target audiences.

Commercial: Briefing and commissioning of professional media services; project management; financial and budget management; marketing and selling; research, audit and data analysis.

Creative and problem-solving: Applied linear and lateral thinking; ensuring a solution’s form follows its function, and valuing other people’s input to those processes.

Information management: Data and metadata structuring; data useability and accessibility, and intuitive record-keeping and retrieval.

Technical: Art, graphic design and photographics; systems and general design; IT-literate across Apple Mac and PC platforms, video and digital media, internet and associated technologies.

Attended and contributed to World Wide Web Consortium (W3C – effectively the internet’s governing body) seminar on web accessibility for disabled people, June 2004, Paris.

Until recently, an active member of an informal grouping of Scottish public sector webmasters, including Scottish Enterprise, Scottish Development International, Careers Scotland and Talent Scotland. The group’s aim has been to share best editorial and technical practice, and improve web-related cross-organisational co-operation. 2003-2008.

Ken Porter’s Specialties:

Internet strategy and editorial training.
