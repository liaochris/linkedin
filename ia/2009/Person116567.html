John Hartman’s Summary

I was very fortunate to have had family that owned a computer store in 1981 which seeded my technology journey. From desktop to server and all the topology in-between I have worked the full spectrum of Information Technology. I have been working with connected networks since before the web existed.

Inspired by Rheingold and Lanier I had a stint as a Virtual Reality consultant back in the mid 90's. The world was not ready for VR on the consumer end and I moved into my other passion at the time this little thing called the World Wide Web.

I worked with Intel early on beta testing some of the very first "web" servers and developed what I have to guess were some of the very first Intranets. I ran a consulting firm prior to the dot bomb and then took an offer I could not refuse from one of my clients that I was doing Y2K consulting for.

Lately I was working on a project that is trying to change the world by creating the Digital Universe. "The Digital Universe aims to be the definitive repository of human knowledge online." I ended up as the Technology Evangelist for the Digital Universe Foundation (DUF) where I worked with major scientific organizations and universities to create next generation vetted internet content and a customized Media Wiki implementation called the Encyclopedia of Earth.

This was an amazing project to work with but the itch of entrepreneurialism in me needed to be scratched and thus I stepped back from the DUF and started my own company around social media and this new web 2.0 space. I also wanted to jump into the metaverse space and extend my experience from projects like creating a prototype for 3D data visualization of information about the Indian Ocean Tsunami Disaster in 2004 and present that data overlaid onto a globe in web browser. Looking at platforms like Second Life, Google Earth and others to start developing what some are referring to as web 3.0.
