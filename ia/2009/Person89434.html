Gregg Dietrich’s Summary

Over the past 25 years Gregg Dietrich has studied, catalogued, valued, and sold thousands of pieces of maritime art, including: paintings, ship models, prints, vintage and period photographs, instruments and other nautical collectibles. As a maritime art and antiques specialist, Gregg has managed two galleries, formed a partnership and founded his own maritime gallery, and went from a consulting position at Christie's to a Vice President and Senior Specialist in the Maritime department, specializing in Maritime, Ocean Liner, Naval and Engineering Works of Art. As a collector, art and naval historian, and a world-class sailing competitor, Gregg has developed a reputation as one of the foremost specialists in the field of Maritime Art working with veteran collectors as well as those wishing to begin collecting.

Gregg Dietrich’s Specialties:

Currently specializing in collection management, appraisals, installation and display, and moving of both private, corporate and museum collections.

  • Managing Director

    North Star Galleries, Inc.

    (Fine Art industry)

    January 1991 — November 2004 (13 years 11 months)

    Specializing in the purchase and sale of maritime art and collectibles, North Star Galleries grew into a worldwide business and market leader, growing quickly over a 12-year period to annual sales exceeding U.S. $1 million a year. As director and day-to-day manager, I was involved with every aspect of the business, including: inventory (purchase and management), cataloging, sales, personnel, finance, operations, management, web site development and maintenance, logistics, exhibits, publications, and more. In addition I reported to investors with P&L statements, cash flow reports, A/R & A/P reports, and together with the investors set goals and budgets for the gallery. North Star Galleries also had an active consulting and appraisal business in which we set up databases of collections integrated with digital images for large corporate and private clients. These databases are then published as catalogues and worksheets for their insurance companies.
