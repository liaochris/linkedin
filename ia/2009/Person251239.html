Zach Ferres’s Summary

I am a dedicated full-time student at Ohio Northern University. I plan on graduating in May of 2010 with a dual bachelors degree in computer engineering and mathematics with a minor in entrepreneurship. I am also the owner of Bouncehost IT Solutions (soon to be BounceIT), located in Bellevue, Ohio. Finally, I am a member of the Ohio Northern University cross country team, president of the Collegiate Entrepreneurs' Organization chapter at ONU, technology advancement chair for ONU Student Senate, and co-founder of the ONU MultiSport Club. I am an active triathlete and an enthusiastic entrepreneur.

Bouncehost (www.bouncehost.com) provides a complete array of information technology solutions to small- to medium- sized businesses. Some of the services we provide are IT Consulting, Point-of-Sale, Website Development, Software Development, and 24/7 Technical Support packages. Today we have over 40 active clients spanning seven counties in Ohio and customers as far as California. We have never lost a client since the day we opened.

The operation started out in 2007 as just a second job. The business was bootstrapped and has continued organic (internal) growth, no loans or investors needed. Today, we have grown to two full-time and five part-time employees including an ONU intern. I look to grow the business to around 100 clients before graduation in 2010 and then I will commit full-time to the business. Then we look to expand nationally while still maintaining a constant client-to-professional ratio.

Bouncehost is also dedicated to giving back to the communities in which we operate. We are in the strategic planning process of starting BounceGreen, a computer recycling program in which we pick up used and broken equipment, refurbish it, and donate it to community businesses. We are also working with newspaper publishers and radio stations to facilitate the launch of BounceBlogs, a place for people to go and ask their IT questions to the experts.
