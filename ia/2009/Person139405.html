Leif Madsen’s Summary

* More than five years full time experience with Asterisk and Voice over Internet Protocol (VoIP) technologies (first started using Asterisk late 2001)
* Heavily involved with the organization and production of AstriCon, a conference dedicated to Asterisk and related technologies.
* Instructor of courses dealing with Asterisk and it's related technologies
* Proctor of Digium Certified Asterisk Professional certification
* Development and deployment of SOAP enabled proxy server and GUI portal for E911 solution for an ITSP
* Development of ITSP backend based on Asterisk and her related technologies focusing on large scale deployment by developing a clustered system with heavy use of replicated databases to create a peer-to-peer, decentralized environment with self-healing network technologies
* Co-Author of O'Reilly published, Asterisk: The Future of Telephony (1st and 2nd Editions)
* Founder of the Asterisk Documentation Project

Leif Madsen’s Specialties:

Specializing in large scale deployments of Asterisk by utilizing various tools within the software to create a decentralized, clustered solution to scale simply with the addition of more nodes to process calls.

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Telecommunications industry)

    January 2005 — Present (5 years )

    In this position I am a co-author of Asterisk: The Future of Telephony, the first widely distributed book on the Asterisk PBX system. This book is an introduction to the software, and includes a brief history of the software, obtaining, compiling, and configuring the software for the SIP and IAX2 protocols, & Zaptel hardware. It then delves into how analog and digital communications differ, a look into the authors predictions to where the software and industry as a whole is headed, as well as an extensive reference manual.

    A second edition is due for release in August of 2007. I am also participating in the creation of the Asterisk Cookbook, a public effort being written at http://www.asteriskcookbook.com.

    Information about Asterisk: The Future of Telephony is available at http://www.oreilly.com/catalog/asterisk/
