Yun William Yu’s Experience

  • Undergraduate Researcher

    Indiana University Bloomington

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    December 2005 — August 2009 (3 years 9 months)

    Worked under Professor J. Michael Walker in a molecular neuroscience laboratory studying mechanisms of pain transmission (December 2005 - March 2008).
    + Used combination of HPLC and ESI mass spectrometry to identify and characterize novel putative endogenous murine nociceptive neurotransmitters.
    + Wrote in-house software to act as a first pass filter for the ~10,000 spectra generated from each mass spectrometer run. Afterwards examined remainder of spectra for the presence of fatty-acyl amides. Found ~60 such compounds.
    + Transfected NF-kappa-B luciferase expression plasmid into RAW 264.7, a murine macrophage cell line, to determine bioactivity of some discovered fatty-acyl amides.

    Worked with Professor Michael J. Larsen on classifying representations of Fuchsian groups using experimental mathematics (September 2008 - August 2009).
    + Wrote C++ and Maple programs to find dense homomorphisms from particular Fuchsian Groups to SO(n).
