Chin-Ning Chu’s Summary

Chu runs several businesses in Antioch, California. She is the president of the Strategic Learning Institute, president of Asian Marketing Consultants, Inc. and chairperson of NeuroScience Industries, Inc. She advises top-level executives and multinational corporations around the world and is considered one of the foremost authority on understanding the Asian business psyche.

Synthesizing the Asian "warrior" philosophy into practical strategies for decision-making, Chu presents the warrior philosophy of Asia as the premier vehicle for mastering strategic thinking, both in the corporate world as well as in daily life. Her life experiences have been her main, personal tutor.

Chu also conducts workshops and lectures in the areas of personal development, peak performance strategy, Asian negotiation tactics, leadership, cross-cultural training and spirituality. As an author and business consultant, Chu has readers and clients in over sixty countries, counting among them presidents and prime ministers, chairmen and CEOs of Fortune 500 corporations. Throughout Asia and Australia, her books have outsold those of Stephen Covey, Tony Robbins and Tom Peters.

Her work has been highly praised by the international media, including CNN, USA Today, the London Financial Times, Washington Post, Asia Wall Street Journal, the People's Daily in China and many others. In 1996, she was honored the "Woman of the Year" by the international organization, Women of the World.

Chu was recently named among the all-time Success Writers by Nicholas Brealey Publishing, a prominent British-US publisher.

    Reputedly the most popular American author in Asia, Chin-Ning (Thick Face, Black Heart; Asian Mind Game) brings together her background as a Chinese-American, the writings of Carl Jung and current trends in time management and quantum theory in this unique self-help treatise.     Thick Face Black Heart

    (Privately Held; Myself Only; Publishing industry)

    January 1997 — Present (12 years 5 months)

    This book is great! The author has many examples to illustrate her points. The book explains what it is to have a thick face (similar to a "thick skin" but more encompassing) and a black heart (being ruthless in the pursuit of your goals). Though this sounds bad, it really isn't. An example of black heart in practice, given by the author in the book, is of a doctor in the Old West that is treating an injured person. The person is screaming because of his injury (and because there is no anesthesia), but the doctor is emotionless as he performs his duties. If he were sensitive to the screams of the person he is operating on, he would be unable to carry out his duties to the best of his abilities. He knows inside that it is better to cause pain now in order to save the man's life.
