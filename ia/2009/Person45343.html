Christopher Mims’s Summary

I'm the co-founder of Small Mammal, an online video production company / consultancy. Currently, we:

* Produce a weekly news show called <a href="http://scientificamerican.blip.tv">The Monitor</a> for my previous employer, Scientific American

* Consult for a growing roster of clients on every aspect of online video content production and business strategy

* Are in development on new shows with a handful of clients

Before co-founding Small Mammal, I was the special projects editor at Scientific American, where I wrote, blogged, lobbied for best practices, and managed the launch of new websites and improvements to existing sites. I also acted as executive producer on two new online-only shows, Instant Egghead and The Monitor.

I also served in the same role at Seed magazine, where I oversaw the launch of ScienceBlogs.com, including conceiving its editorial and business structure. I also acted as its first community manager. Before that I was the editor of Seedmagazine.com, which is the sort of job you're immediately promoted to when it becomes apparent that you're the only editorial assistant on staff who happens to have any interest in the web... fortune favors the prepared?

In another life I was a crayfish neuroscientist who worked on the development of novel functional MRI imaging techniques.
