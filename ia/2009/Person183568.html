Paul Patruno’s Summary

Worked in the semiconductor industry for more than 35 years, after joining THOMSON Semiconductors as a process engineer, back in 1973.

Held various positions in photolithography, etch, diffusion and metal deposition areas. In 1988, became part of the initial project team for the SGS-THOMSON Crolles fab and was responsible for equipment specification and selection for diffusion, cleaning, metrology, silicon, ion implant and wet processing during the next three years. In 1991, the Central R&D Crolles 200mm fab started operations and was responsible for the Wet Processing operations. In 1994, named Equipment Strategy Manager with SGS-THOMSON Central R&D and began work on developing and promoting modeling techniques and new metrics concepts for 300mm fab operations and equipment selection.

Joined the International 300 Initiative subsidiary of SEMATECH in Austin in 1996 as Front-End of Line Director of Technology. Involved in numerous 300mm tool demonstrations done by I300I during the 4 years of this industry initiative and specifically developed a joint set of Equipment Performance Metrics and joint evaluation with Selete.
Moved in 2000 to a senior assignee and account management, director position for STMicroelectronics at International SEMATECH for the next 3 years.

Joined Soitec USA to help accelerate the development of new-generation transistors through a partnership in Austin, TX working on 45-nm technology and beyond. Help develop advanced substrates based on SOI SmartCut technology to support a semiconductor partnership Multiple Gate FET (MuGFET) development during the last 4 years.
