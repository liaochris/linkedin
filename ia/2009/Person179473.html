Oryal Tanir’s Summary

P.H.D. in Engineering.
Teaching Executive- McGill University & Adjunct Professor at École de Technologie Supérieure.
Advocate of Enterprise Architecture principles, SOA, IT performance, data analysis.
Active International researcher / consultant in Discrete-event simulation. Some milestones are:
- Major models made of Call Centers, process engineering, supply chain management, and computer systems for Bell Canada.
- Established IEEE P1170 working group on simulation standards which eventually set the foundation for simulation portability standards.
- Wrote, edited, co-authored 3 text books and dozens of articles on simulation and S/W engineering.
- Managed extensive research programs with numerous canadian universities.
- Applied Quality Engineering principles to different organizations.
- Regarded as performance engineering guru in Bell Canada for understanding performance/reliability related issues in systems.
- Served on the NSERC research grant comittee for S/W engineering.

Oryal Tanir’s Specialties:

Oryal Tanir’s Experience

  • Enterprise Architect

    Bell Canada

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; BCE; Telecommunications industry)

    January 2006 — September 2008 (2 years 9 months)

    Responsible for architecture decisions and solutions for the SMB market, Master Data Management and Internet process simulation. I was part of technology think tanks to position SOA, BPM, and other practices in the organization. In simulation I modelled an end-to-end process simulation of major provisioning systems for Sympatico, ExpressVu and Bell Wireline. These were used extensively to optimize processes and examine alternative designs. Some major accomplishments are:
    - Architect on Master Data Management project and Small & Medium Business IT designs.
    - Simulation projects: Sympatico provisioning process (accurate within 7%), IPTV order fulfillment process, broadband test coverage analysis, workforce management (comparison of various workforce schemes), wireline workforce forecasting.
    - Established and implemented an EA best practice for simulation, integrated with lean six-sygma.
    - Collaborated on an EA reference model, combining principles such as eTom, and SOA.

  • IS/IT Delivery Consultant

    Bell Canada

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; BCE; Telecommunications industry)

    January 2003 — January 2006 (3 years 1 month)

    I worked on the delivery of front-end systems and also the simulation of their processes. Some key accomplishments are:
    - PM and SME of a critical Oracle/Weblogic based OM sub-system. Resolved technical issues on Weblogic, JDBC and Client-server web architectures.
    - SME for Order Management system code releases (SOA, Oracle, Selectica and Weblogic based design). Technical liason with IBM and CGI.
    - Created National ExpressVu distribution center model to identify optimal geographical placement.
    - Designed unified Call Center Metrics and mechanized all front-end routing data for project into a common database.
    - Created a novel simulation of ”disruptive technology” based on game theory and systems dynamics used by the strategic marketing team to identify price points for future technologies.
    - Created end-to-end simulation of a “lean re-design” of the Enterprise Services provisioning process. This was used in conjunction with other process improvement initiatives.

  • Director - Business Intelligence & Simulation

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; BCE; Telecommunications industry)

    - Identified $Millions/year worth of productivity improvements and benefits.
    - Developed a key corporate online project management/tracking system.
    - Developed standard Call Center metrics across all business units to ensure comparative benchmarking and productivity verification.
    - Created the first fully functional end-to-end simulation model of the entire Bell Canada customer experience.
    - Created over 50 different simulation models ranging in scope from Broadband traffic congestion to Call Center productivity.
    - Initiated and participated in research portfolios with the University of Montreal (MITACS) in the area of data mining, and the National Research Council in the area of Software engineering (Software architecture, structural synthesis and reuse).
