Vyacheslav Palchevskiy, Ph.D’s Experience

  • Postdoctoral Scholar

    UCLA

    (Educational Institution; Research industry)

    July 2007 — Present (2 years 4 months)

    Lung transplantation is a therapeutic alternative for patients with end-stage lung disease. However, long-term survival in these patients is impeded by chronic lung allograft rejection, known as bronchiolitis obliterans syndrome (BOS), the clinical correlate of obliterative bronchiolitis (OB). Chronic lung allograft rejection is a process that ultimately results in allograft airway fibro-obliteration/obliterative bronchiolitis. The mediators that orchestrate the fibro-obliterative process of BOS are not well understood.
    Thus, we are applying translational studies using a murine model of post-transplant obliterative bronchiolitis (heterotopic s.c. airway/trachea transplantation with a MHC class -I & –II disparate combination) to determine the effects of various receptor/ligand biology during the pathogenesis of mOB.
