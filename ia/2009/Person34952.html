Bruno Lopez’s Experience

  • VP/GM UNIVISION ONLINE

    Univision Communications

    (Public Company; 5001-10,000 employees; Internet industry)

    June 1999 — Present (9 years 9 months)

    Part of the small core development group of the site that has become the largest Spanish-language portal in the United States.
    Set up and supervise Univision Online's bureaus. Assisted in/or supervised many facets of the site: information architecture, content development, editorial structure and workflows. Managed some of first Online/TV integration efforts. Before heading the programming dept. of Univision Online, Lopez had a 20-year journalism career; serving as Univision’s Mexico City Bureau chief; roving West Coast correspondent for CNN Español; bureau chief for the Telemundo newscast Mexico City; The Arizona Republic's Mexico correspondent; worked for ABC News and United Press International.
    Knight Fellow at Stanford in 1998-99, concentrating on business and media studies also a former fellow of the American Studies Program at the World Press Institute in Macallester College.

  • Regional Spanish Language Editor

    United Press International

    (Privately Held; 1001-5000 employees; Newspapers industry)

    April 1980 — May 1983 (3 years 2 months)

    Operating of Mexico City, I was one of the editors in charge of helping coordinate coverage of all of México as well as Central America for what then was one of the largest news wire services in the world. Edited copy of all reporters in the region. Covered the early stages of the contra war in Nicaragua, broke the story on the beginning of the largest exodus in the history of Guatemala, one that uprooted dozens of towns, sparked by the brutal repression of the military government of Efrain Rios Mont. Also uncovered the biggest scandal of the presidency of President Miguel de la Madrid, that of his corrupt private secretary who embezzled millions of dollars from the Rural Credit Bank, responsible for helping the poorest peasants in México.
