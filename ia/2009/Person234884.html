Tom Moritz’s Summary

As Director of Public Programs at the Internet Archive, Tom Moritz advocates for national and international programs to digitize knowledge resources -- providing jobs while supporting economic growth, innovation, education and government transparency.

He has been a leading participant in efforts to provide for open access and responsible use of knowledge resources in biodiversity conservation and in the biological sciences. Moving to The Getty Research Institute in 2006, he shifted focus to the arts and humanities and continues to closely consider the integration of knowledge in the arts, humanities and sciences.

Working in both the public sector and the non-profit private sector as a librarian and information manager since 1975 and has participated in grants from the Mellon Foundation, the Sloan Foundation and the US National Science Foundation. He has won support from private donors and for-profit corporations. In the Fall of 2005, he served as Visiting Assoc. Prof. at the Pratt Institute Graduate School of Library and Info. Science.

He has worked as an advisor on knowledge management in Africa, Asia, Europe, the Pacific and Latin America, was a lead organizer of the Biodiversity Heritage Library Project and has served as a member of the Interim Steering Committee of the -- now UNEP-based -- Conservation Commons (http://www.conservationcommons.org/). A leader in the development and release of the first World Database on Protected Areas. He is a member of the Science Advisory Board for the US National Biological Information Infrastructure, served on the National Visiting Committee for the US National Sci. Digital Library (NSF), and was an adviser to the Science Commons. Co-editor of the Sierra Club Wetlands Reader, he compiled IUCN’s Conservation Thesaurus (2006) He has served/serves on numerous other advisory bodies, visiting committees and peer review panels and is author of many publications and presentations.

Tom Moritz’s Specialties:

Knowledge Management, Design for Knowledge Equity, Strategic Program Planning and Development
