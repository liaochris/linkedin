Amit Gadhia’s Summary

As Shakespeare says..."All the world's a stage & all the men & women merely players. They have their exits and their entrances; & one man in his time plays many parts." While circling life on this stage; below is a summary of my parts, my exits & entrances:

Stage 1) MERRILL LYNCH + CCG ADVISORS
Started my career at Merrill Lynch selling term loans & lines of credit to farmers, home builders & food processing plants in rural Georgia. Soon, I jump start my consulting career with CCG Advisors, an investment banking M&A boutique helping SME's craft exit strategies.

Stage 2) IP&R & STAPLES
Summer 2003, moved to California & picked up a project with Inventors Publishing & Research (IP&R), a company supposed to be helping inventors market their product; IP&R turns out to be a scam house. I soon exit from IP&R & my next job was with Staples Inc. I was hired to sell pens, pencils & paper clips + help with their west coast B2B sales launch pad.

Stage 3) LAUNCH OF FIRST VENTURE
Late 2004, I launch my first company. World of Ayurveda, distributor of a plant based pharma company with manufacturing in North India. After 2 short years, my partner takes over operations.

Stage 4) TECH CONSULTING + LAUNCH OF SECOND COMPANY
Shortly, I was offered to work on a consulting gig with a web2.0 application development consulting company; goal was to help pregnant entrepreneurs launch their ideas.

Thus, I find my brains storming around web2.0, open source and mobile application development. It was love at first sight & Artyllect Consulting was soon born in San Francisco at the cusp of web1.0 (static content) v/s web2.0 (dynamic content) platform upgrade.

The dna of Artyllect speaks for itself i.e. an eye of the artistic & a mind of the intellect. We're a bunch of geeky & passionate software engineers, focusing in 3 areas of tech dev: web2.0, open source & mobile/wireless app dev. Other areas of expertise include iphone apps, facebook apps, android + ruby on rails.
