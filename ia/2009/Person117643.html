John Jones’s Summary

I am a digital media consultant with 14 years experience of hybrid project management/business analysis work. I have a track record of working on technology-led websites and other IT-related solutions that succeed in putting the users and their requirements at the centre of the project approach.

John Jones’s Experience

  • Head of Business Analysis and Testing

    Syzygy UK

    (Public Company; SYZ; Internet industry)

    August 2008 — Present (1 year)

    I provide business analysis on a wide range of projects providing web-based technical solutions to the company’s clients. I act in the Product Owner role on the Company’s projects run under the Scrum Agile development methodology. And, I manage a team of business analysts and testers.

  • Project Director and Client Partner

    Syzygy UK

    (Public Company; 51-200 employees; Internet industry)

    June 2007 — July 2008 (1 year 2 months)

    Syzygy is the UK’s 6th largest digital media agency. I guided clients through the entire project lifecycle, from first consultancy, through analysis, and then project/programme managed the solution’s development.

    My main achievement was to project manage and provide business analysis to a project to design, develop and launch 10 websites across EMEA supporting the prescribers and users of Wyeth’s drug, Enbrel. All 10 sites, built on the EPiServer CMS, were released to their owners for content entry on-time and on-budget, www.enbrel.nl being the first to go live.

    I was also a member of the company’s management team.

  • Business Analyst and Project Manager

    Jones Olson

    (Online Media industry)

    June 2004 — May 2007 (3 years)

    Provided project management, business analysis and consultancy in all aspects of digital media.

    My main achievement in this role was to provide for Rolls-Royce PLC project and programme management, business analysis and stakeholder management, on a project to develop a knowledge management and collaboration Intranet, the final development of which was credited with a 56% user effort reduction as a result of improved Intranet effectiveness.

    Other clients included Ofsted, Syzygy UK, the UK Debt Management Office, and the Central Office of Information (on projects for the Security Industry Authority, the Department of Work and Pensions, the Learning and Skills Council, and the Qualifications and Curriculum Authority).

  • Head of e-Communications Specialists

    Central Office of Information

    (Marketing and Advertising industry)

    September 2000 — May 2004 (3 years 9 months)

    COI is the UK Government's centre of excellence for marketing and communications.

    Initially as an e-Communications Specialist, then from 2003 as Head of the team (six members), I managed a team that provided consultancy to central government departments and agencies on all aspects of digital media, including digital strategy, requirements capture (from users and stakeholders), accessibility, usability, and technical issues such as infrastructure and applications. I additionally project managed the team’s projects.

    Clients included the Army, Asset Recovery Agency, Cabinet Office, Dept. for Education and Skills, Dept. of Health, Dept. Trade and Industry, General Social Care Council, Home Office, Learning and Skills Council, Lord Chancellor's Department, Office of National Statistics, Ofsted, Qualifications and Curriculum Authority, RAF, and Security Industry Authority.

  • Senior Consultant

    First Consulting Group

    (Public Company; FCGI; Information Technology and Services industry)

    2000 — September 2000 (less than a year)

    Provided consultancy to global pharmaceutical companies on the development of web-enabled versions of their existing Documentum-based document management systems. FCG is now part of CSC Health.
