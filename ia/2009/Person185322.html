    Calvin Capital Corporation

    (Financial Services industry)

    November 2001 — June 2005 (3 years 8 months)

    Calvin Capital was the result of my visions as a young, successful and idealistic loan officer. Having invested years learning the mortgage business, I recognized gross inefficiency and disorganization - the lack of effective systems.

    Following Gerber's eMyth model carefully, I documented my entire mortgage origination process from origination to processing to closing, and systematized it - with methodologies, technology, and manuals to teach it - but with a much higher sales volume. Now there was a team of people, each person systematically performing a segment of what just I had been doing previously.

    This was a prototypical "young founders" startup -- a partner and I self-funded it with less than $250K, created the systems, increased our customer base, and ran the company for a total of about 24 months before grinding to a halt due to a combination of circumstances, lack of funds, and inexperience. It was probably one of the most valuable experiences I'll ever have in my life!
