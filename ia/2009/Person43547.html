  • Chairman

    Wine.com

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Food & Beverages industry)

    January 2000 — August 2005 (5 years 8 months)

    A difficult turnaround situation for the e-commerce category leader was handled through debt financing, hiring a new management team and relocating the company from Portland, OR to San Francisco. We grew sales from $11M to $32M from 2002 to 2005, when we had an offer to sell the Wine.com to an NYSE listed company for $67M that was subsequently denied by the VC involved.

  • CEO

    Yaga, Inc.

    (Internet industry)

    2000 — 2003 (3 years)

    Yaga was a startup that built a secure and legal peer to peer filesharing network to share rich media like music, pictures and videos. Features such as SHA-1 download confirmation and robust payment engine were 5 years ahead of their time. Company was sold in 2004.

  • CEO

    NBC Internet (NBCi)

    (Public Company; Internet industry)

    1999 — 2000 (1 year)

    NBC Internet resulted from the business combination of Xoom.com, Snap.com and the NBC internet assets, plus $400M of NBC on-air promotion. Working with the banking team at Bear Stearns, I put together this complicated deal that resulted in a $6B marketcap company with 600 employees and was the #7 trafficked internet site. Along with several members of my team, I did an extensive roadshow for a $600M offering led by Goldman Sachs after the deal closed.

  • Chairman

    Xoom.com

    (Public Company; 201-500 employees; XMCM; Internet industry)

    October 1996 — May 1999 (2 years 8 months)

    Founded in my spare bedroom in a condominium at Lake Tahoe, this company was the category leader in direct marketing to a community of web users. Featuring a variety of services such as home pages, the largest internet chat network, pagecounters and free email, Xoom.com boasted over 10M users and was a top 10 internet site. With less than $10M of paid in capital, it went public two years from its founding, raising $55M in the IPO. A $250M secondary shortly after IPO resulted in returns to investors of 30x. Merged with NBC and CNET assets to form NBC Internet.

  • VP Marketing

    Lycos, Inc.

    (Public Company; 51-200 employees; LCOS; Internet industry)

    October 1995 — July 1996 (10 months)

    I led the Lycos marketing team through one of the first internet IPOs. As part of the management team, I helped develop innovative business models to monetize the web traffic from this early internet leader.

  • CEO

    Point Communications

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Online Media industry)

    January 1995 — September 1995 (9 months)

    Point Communications was a leading internet directory company in the early days of the internet and became famous for the "Point Top 5%" badge - an award given to the best web sites. Started in my bedroom in Brookline, MA with a Sparc 20 running Unix on a T-1 line, the company moved to New York several months after inception to take advantage of the rapidly growing internet advertising market. Point grew from nothing to a top 20 web site and was sold to Lycos ten months after being started.

  • Publisher

    July 1994 — June 1995 (1 year)

    I joined Softkey as a publisher when I sold them Aris Multimedia. I was responsible for integrating the Aris products into Softkey's sales force, as well as development of new multimedia and internet titles.

  • Publisher

  • CEO

    Aris Multimedia

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Computer Software industry)

    October 1991 — July 1994 (2 years 10 months)

    Aris Multimedia developed multimedia CD-ROM products including a line of multimedia clip art called MediaClips, a computer based training product called WinTutor and MPC Wizard, a test and tune utility for multimedia PCs.
