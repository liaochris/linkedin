    2000 — 2005 (5 years)

    * Oversight of a product called the Common Technology Platform, designed to link GE Power's various products into a cohesive whole.

    * OpenGIS representative for GE Power

  • Internet Development Group

    GE Smallworld

    (Public Company; Computer Software industry)

    January 2000 — February 2003 (3 years 2 months)

    Led skunk works development project of Smallworld’s Internet product suite (server and client). Hand picked a team of three developers and designed, built and launched the first version of the product in 7 months. The product went on to become one of Smallworld's most successful. Over the next three years I helped build the team to 15 people and oversaw the release of three additional, on-time major releases. Specific responsibilities included:

    * Managed day-to-day activities of Internet Development Group, which consisted of fourteen developers, testers, interaction designers and documenters.

    * Architect for Smallworld Internet Application Server and HTML Client Framework (HTML).

    * Product evangelist - presented at user conferences, frequently visited customers in North America and Europe.

    * OpenGIS representative

  • Technical Lead

    Smallworld Systems, Inc.

    (Computer Software industry)

    Technical lead for the Public System division, which provided GIS (geographic information systems) software to water utilities and city and local governments. Developed a number of products, including an integrated development environment (IDE) for Smallworld called Magician that was later released as part of the Magik Studio product suite. Also developed a spatially enabled customer relationship management (CRM) application called iCityServices.
