Buckley Brinkman’s Summary

Change Catalyst and Entrepreneurial Professional with diverse industry experience in both Service and Manufacturing environments. I bring out the best in organizations by galvanizing teams and creating a laser focus on results. My leadership focuses on bringing out the best in people from the board room to the front lines and aligning those abilities with the challenges in the marketplace. I am a true generalist with hands-on experience in Sales and Marketing, Operations, Customer Service, Accounting, and Executive Leadership.

When I enter an organization, sales grow, costs fall, profits increase, market position improves, and the tactical and strategic direction of the company improves.

If these outcomes interest you, let's talk about how we can help each other!

Buckley Brinkman’s Specialties:

General Management, Strategic and Tactical planning, Service Solutions, Union and Non-union workforces, Process Management (including ISO and Lean Six Sigma approaches), Turnarounds, Organizational Design, Acquisition Analysis and Integration, Change Management Consulting and Management, Contract Negotiations

Buckley Brinkman’s Experience

  • Change Catalyst & Partner

    Launchpad Partners

    (Executive Office industry)

    2008 — Present (1 year)

    Business advisory firm specializing to mid-sized organizations under stress -- either because of exceptional growth or disruptions to the business. We make it fun for owners to run their businesses again by focusing our improvement efforts on building strong teams, eliminating wasted effort, and aligning the organization with the marketplace. Our expertise positions companies for profitable growth. Our Principals have created almost $1 billion in market value for the companies we helped.

  • President & COO

    US Music Corp

    (Privately Held; 201-500 employees; Music industry)

    2007 — 2008 (1 year)

    Led this 124 year-old manufacturer and importer of quality musical instruments. Redesigned the organization with a "matrix, plus" structure, clearly aligning product management around specific offerings and the sales force on specific geographies. The sales force reorganization saved over $500,000 per year while improving sales effectiveness. Negotiated multi-year licensing contract with the Walt Disney Company for musical instruments. Instituted changes in management and IT systems in order to improve control and responsiveness. Changes resulted in a 31% increase in sales.

  • COO

    Manchester Companies

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Management Consulting industry)

    2004 — 2007 (3 years)

    Led this nationally recognized Corporate Renewal/Investment Banking/Consulting firm. Participated in the creation of a new strategy to leverage the company’s platform and expand to new markets. Opened and supported target markets.

    Recruited into the practice to lead multiple, mission-critical engagements, including the engagement that won the “2006 Turnaround of the Year” award for creating and installing the management processes that enabled a medical device company to eliminate backorders, substantially reduce lead time, and recover from two product recalls. The changes enabled the company to successfully exit a divisive receivership and be sold for over $300 million. Other successful assignments included projects in the electronics manufacturing, banking, telephony, and service markets. Authored the white papers, “Confessions of a Change Catalyst: Three Skills Colleges Don’t Teach” and “The Soft Side of Strategy and the Hard Edge of Change”.

  • Vice President -- Service

    De La Rue Cash Systems

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; Business Supplies and Equipment industry)

    2001 — 2004 (3 years)

    Recruited to refocus the U.S. Service operations for a billion-dollar secure transaction company that creates and implements cash handling, teller automation, and electronic voting solutions for Customers in Banking, Retailing, and Government Agencies. Grew sales and profits for three straight years, improving revenue by 26% and profits by 31%. Decentralized responsibility and accountability for Customer satisfaction and profitability to Regional General Managers at multiple sites across the country. Strengthened core support operations by reengineering the Call Center, Technical Support, IT, Logistics, and Finance.

    Provided flawless election support for several key Florida counties in the first election following the “hanging chad” race. Performance provided a coup for our voting systems group as our major competitor struggled across the state in this highly visible election.

  • President and General Manager

    Carta Mundi

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Consumer Goods industry)

    1997 — 2000 (3 years)

    Carta Mundi is the largest card company in the world, manufacturing over 160 million decks of cards, generating $60 million in sales. Their state-of-the-art North American plant failed to make money or support the local market. I was brought in to resuscitate the operation. We implemented a team management system, revamped Sales and Marketing, recruited key employees, and designed the structures that:
    • Improved margins by over 34 percentage points and reduced the break-even point by 44%.
    • Solidified relationships with major Customers, including Mattel.
    • Profitably grew sales by 41% through Direct and Indirect channels.
    • Reduced manufacturing cycle time for our major Customer from eight weeks to less than four days using JIT and Lean Six Sigma principles.

  • Vice President & General Manager

    Rand McNally

    (Privately Held; 501-1000 employees; Publishing industry)

    1994 — 1997 (3 years)

    Rand McNally purchased the world’s largest trade bindery and a renowned manufacturer of deluxe leather-bound books, looseleaf binders, and multimedia packaging for a national base of Customers. The operation was an enigma, producing high-quality, custom designed products in a company focused on long runs of standard books. I was recruited to create and execute a new strategy for these plants. Our management team:
    • Created and established a new strategy and re-engineered functionally organized operation into independent business units which effectively responded to Customer requirements.
    • Used Customer project teams to establish links between key Customers and the production floor, implementing change based on Lean Manufacturing.
    • Returned to profitability for the first time in eight years, grew sales from $24 million to $41 million, and created a flexible workforce of 400 employees.
    • Successfully marketed the company and sold it to an independent buyer.

  • General Manager; Controller; Corporate Planning Manager

    Smurfit Stone

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; Packaging and Containers industry)

    1986 — 1994 (8 years)

    Joined this $4 billion paper company after graduate school. As Corporate Planning Manager, flew more than 250,000 miles in one year to support multiple acquisition and integration efforts. Company grew from $300 million to $3 billion during this period.

    Took line responsibility as Controller, Operations Manager, and then General Manager of a 90 year-old recycled paperboard mill with $14 million in sales and 88 employees. Our team:
    • Catalyzed profit turnaround, from losses of over $700,000/year to profits of over $1 million/year.
    • Developed new product that increased sales by 40%.
    • Restructured the mill, including the establishment of TQM, based on ISO-9000 guidelines, engaging both salaried and unionized employees in a team management system.
    • I also served five months of Oregon strike duty, working 12-hour shifts, seven days a week, while performing regular duties in Indiana.

  • Buyer

    Kohl's Department Stores

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; KSS; Retail industry)

    1982 — 1984 (2 years)

    Bought Paint, Hardware, Automotive, and Summer Seasonal. Set in place the plans to eliminate the Paint, Hardware, and Automotive departments to better utilize ad dollars and floor space for the company.
