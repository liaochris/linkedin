Hirak Sen’s Summary

A structural engineer, entrepreneur and lateral thinker, I continually learn from informed clients and talented pools of architects and engineers. Together, we have conceptualized, designed and delivered numerous architectural/ engineering/ construction projects spread across nine states of India.

Townships, multi-storied buildings, recreational/ commercial/ institutional/ industrial complexes, road bridges, urban developments, power plants, airport terminals, and environmental impact assessment studies are projects that we have delivered. Many of these are nationally acclaimed projects. My firm strives to deliver excellence at all times.

I am serving as a Council member at the Institution of Structural Engineers, London, and as a Committee member at the Bharat Chamber of Commerce, Kolkata. Earlier, it has been my privilege to chair the Board of Governor's Committee on Campus Development at Welham Girls' High School, Dehradun for six years steering its campus modernization.

I have authored a design manual that was sponsored and distributed by the Construction Industry Research and Information Association of UK. I have also published a number of technical papers in international refereed journals and conferences.

Currently, I am working at kick-starting a Knowledge Process Outsourcing venture in the A/E/C space. I am looking for professionals with complementary skills in marketing, HR, finance and IT to join the venture. I am also looking out for angel funding.

Most of all, I am proud to be husband to my wife Kalpana, and father to our son Kaustuv and daughter Kasturi.

Hirak Sen’s Experience

  • Founder & CEO

    H. K. Sen and Associates

    (Civil Engineering industry)

    October 1973 — Present (35 years 8 months)

    Consulting service provider in A/E/C space. HKSA has designed and delivered many nationally acclaimed projects across India . These include:
    --122,000 capacity Asia’s largest soccer stadium at Salt Lake, Kolkata
    --Three complete townships at: Vindhyachal, Dadri, and Rangit
    --India’s largest horse-racing grandstand in Hyderabad
    --Main turbo-generator building (6x210MW) at Vindhyachal
    --India’s most skewed road bridge on NH-1 at Karnal, and
    --Domestic airport terminal building in Kolkata (adjudged best terminal building in India two successive years).

  • Senior Design Engineer

    Atkins Das Pvt. Ltd.

    (Civil Engineering industry)

    May 1972 — October 1973 (1 year 6 months)

    Led a team of engineers to finalize the geometric design of approach viaducts (14km long) and main bridge of the Second Hooghly Bridge project. This was a pioneering work in India at the time.

  • Research Fellow

    Imperial College London

    (Educational Institution; Research industry)

    April 1969 — March 1972 (3 years)

    --Researched industry sponsored problems on composite construction and shear walls.
    –Analyzed ultimate load capacity of composite columns at Almondsbury Interchange (4 level interchange between M5 and M4 near Bristol).
    --Investigated lateral strength of in-filled large panel walls after Ronan Point collapse (progressive collapse of one wing of a newly built tower block triggered by gas explosion) at Newham, East London.
    --Served as a co-opted member on British Code on Composite Construction (CP117) drafting committee.
    --Mentored two graduate students helping them with their research.
