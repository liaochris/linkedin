Darren Garside’s Summary

Analysing play, pshe and P4C enquiries has led to my completing a Masters of Teaching in Critical and Creative Thinking in 2007. I am now taking this further and am a doctoral candidate at the University of Bath (supervisors Professor A W G Stables and Dr Michael Bonnett) exploring 'Children and Philosophy.' (See 'Current scholarship' below)

Current Scholarship:
Primary English Research:
Writing paper on The Possibility of using Social Network Analysis for Conversational Analysis for Social Networks.

Involved in BSU PedR project on e-portfolios for professional development. Responsibility for CAQDAS strand of evaluation.

Education Studies:
Writing paper on Praxeomorphic understanding and professional identity formation.

MPhil/PhD:
1st year of research. General area of Children and Philosophy
A variety of positions are being evaluated on their own merits including analytical and phenomenological frameworks, The analytical framework comprises a tripartite conception of philosophical programme design which is to be used to examine illustrations taken from core texts in the field. The phenomenological framework provides a radically different conception of personal ontology and provides grounds for re-conceptualising the pedagogical space.

It is intended that exploring the tensions between these approaches, initially from a Wittgensteinian perspective, will provide ground for a subsequent narrowing of scope for the development of the final thesis.
