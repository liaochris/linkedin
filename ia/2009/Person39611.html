Colin Brace’s Summary

As a native speaker of English, I have carved out a comfortable niche here in the Netherlands doing various kinds of writing for hire, including translation, copywriting and journalism. Much of my work has been in the IT industry, but I have also written articles for non-technical audiences.

Over the years, my activities have encompassed not only crunching text but also technical tasks, such as localizing software and building web sites, and organizational responsibilities (administration and project management).

Companies and organizations I have worked with or for include Ahold, Canon Europe, Dow Chemical, Draka, the European Commission, the European Association for Machine Translation, KLM (Holland Herald), Koninklijke PTT Nederland (KPN), Nederlands Omroep Stichting (NOS), Shell Exploration and Development, the University of Pisa, among others.

Although I work in English, I am a fluent speaker of Dutch and an advanced Spanish speaker. In 2001-2002, I spent some eighteen months on work/study in South America, mostly in the Andean region, where I gathered material for a writing project.

As a Linux and BSD user, I have a great interest in all things related to open-source software. I contribute to the community by editing documentation wikis, sharing the odd shell script, and filing bug reports. For several years, I contributed heavily to the open-source encyclopedia Wikipedia, creating and editing articles on technology, journalism, music, and Latin America, among other topics.

Samples of my writing can be found on my website.

Colin Brace’s Specialties:

Linguistic software, telecoms, environmental technology, current affairs, Dutch history and culture, Latin America; also basic competency in website design (HTML, CSS), shell scripting, and project management.
