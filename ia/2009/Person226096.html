Charles Sue-Wah-Sing’s Summary

I've been designing since 1995 on various traditional mediums - in print and digital. In 1998, I joined Cyberplex (Toronto) as a interface designer creating sites for ING Direct, Sprint Canada and Ford.ca. In 2000, we started an Information Architecture and Usability practice across at Cyberplex. I was involved in establishing process for requirements gathering, and developing IA documentation and best practices. I had lead on the online experience strategies for clients such as RBC Royal Bank, Thomson Learning, National Bank and Computer Science Corporation (CSC).

I then joined Infinet Communications (now called Elsevier) in 2002 as practice lead Information Architect, later becoming Creative Director in 2004. I was responsible for strategy, usability, design and content development for one of our primary clients - Organon Global - an international pharmaceutical company with presence in Europe, the States and Canada.

Over 3 years ago, I started NexKlix Inc with business partner Ravi Dindayal PMP, MBA. We provide clients and agencies with support in online customer experience strategy, marketing, information architecture and usability, design, development and project management. Our clients have included, Black Co., Globe and Mail, RBC Royal Bank, and ICICI Bank of Canada.
