Tom Eames’s Summary

My experience began with circuit and firmware design in early digital PABX systems. This evolved into architectural, system design and implementation of the Harris 20/20 digital PABX. My efforts at Harris/DTS led to my being asked by Don Green to evaluate the opportunity presented to him that ultimately led to the formation of Optilink. I then joined the founding team of Optilink and in collaboration with the founding team, I led the system design and implementation of the Litespan 2000. My architectural work led me to become the primary technical sales spokesman for Optilink/DSC which sold into all of the RBOCs plus GTE. Over 50 million homes are served by the Litespan 2000.

With this experience as a co-founder, architecture, system design, management, design, leadership and sales spokesman, I founded my own startup. Myself and Pete Keeler formed Next Level Communications in 1994. At NLC, I gained experience at a higher level in the overall business including raising venture funding, business formation, leadership, lawsuits, sales involvement with customers, helping set up a Vietnam design group, negotiating an acquisition and eventually participation in NLC's IPO. After the IPO of NLC with Motorola as the majority shareholder, I left NLC in 2000. While at NLC, we had set up a Vietnam design group around Lac Trinh, a veteran of Optilink. Due to the market setback of 2000, Motorola offered the Vietnam assets to myself, Pete Keeler and Lac Trinh as an alternative to closing it down. We took over the Vietnam team and assets and started Arrive Technologies.

I drove Arrive Technologies direction toward the development of chips utilized by Telecom systems. Once funding was raised, Arrive Technologies began development in Vietnam under the leadership of Lac Trinh and now has begun to commercialize its second chip and continues as a privately funded operation.

Tom Eames’s Experience

  • Founder, Chief Strategic Officer, Board Member

    Arrive Technologies

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Semiconductors industry)

    2002 — 2007 (5 years)

    Arrive Technologies was founded from the former Motorola/NLC Vietnam design team. Arrive's opportunity was based on combination of the extremely high cost of Telecom chip development, the slowdown by existing Telecom Chip companies after the 2000 crash and the transition from TDM to packet switched networks. Arrive leverages the significantly lower development costs plus availability of extraordinary design talent in Vietnam to develop ASSP, ASIC and FPGA solutions enabling the transition from legacy protocols over PDH/SONET networks to unified Packet Switched Networks.

    1994 — 2000 (6 years)

It provided a fiber fed GR-008/303 DLC along with fiber fed VDSL/DSL DSLAM and Fiber to the Curb or Premises (FTTC/P). I held a variety of positions at NLC which was bought by GI, then Motorola and spun of to a very successful 1999 IPO.
