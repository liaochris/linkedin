Tanya Seaman’s Experience

  • Co-Founder & Executive Director

    PhillyCarShare

    (Non-Profit; Transportation/Trucking/Railroad industry)

    September 2002 — August 2008 (6 years )

    Created successful business plan for award-winning, nationally acclaimed nonprofit car-sharing organization signing up over 50,000 residents and business, enabling 25,000 members to trade in their personal vehicles for a car-independent lifestyle.

    • Starting with $25k became solvent in two years and grew revenues to $12M in six years.

    • Directing significant grant money in third and fourth years toward highly visible brand awareness and marketing efforts, quadrupled membership in the fourth year while improving staff productivity sixfold.

    • Achieved highest market saturation rate of any car-sharing organization in the world: one in five residents of Center City became a member of PhillyCarShare.

    • Developed and implemented extensive technological and operational efficiencies that ensured very high standards of customer service, meeting membership growth with very moderate increases in staff.

    • Designed and implemented world-first municipal car-sharing program with the City of Philadelphia, replacing 350 vehicles with five shared vehicles for a savings of $2M annually.

  • Transportation Planner

    Center City District

    (Non-Profit; Civic & Social Organization industry)

    2000 — 2002 (2 years )

    Using online tools and specially designed promotional materials, marketed the use of public transportation and commuter benefits to office workers in Philadelphia. Performed benchmarking surveys of office workers about their commuting habits; residents about their experience with neighborhood parking; and bicyclists about their travel behavior and experience. Reports published in quarterly Center City Digest and in annual report, State of Center City.
