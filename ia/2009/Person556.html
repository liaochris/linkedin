Andrea Rippon’s Summary

I was born and brought up in India and after 12 years, moved with my parents to Sierra Leone in Africa. From the age of 11, I was educated in the UK in Southwold, Suffolk.

I'm certain that this element of travel, with all the difference and diversity it entailed, has contributed to my main and major interest in life: people.

After working in London to get enough money to travel some more (2 years backpacking and working around the world) I settled in Norwich, Norfolk to start a family.

It was when I had my first baby that I discovered a course running at my local University: a certificate in Counselling Skills. Being listened to well has always felt like a real luxury to me and this, combined with a real desire to engage with other adults, led me to becoming a student.

I haven't looked back.

Now here's the official bit:

Andrea Rippon is a Person Centred Practitioner (Carl Rogers, 1957) She is a Course Director of Counselling Skills at UEA’s Centre for Continuing Education and her interest lies in the application of the Person Centred Approach in a variety of non-therapeutic settings.

Her clients include the BBC, HM Prison Service, the NHS, the Association of Reflexologists, Norfolk County Council (Vauxhall Centre) and schools in the local area. She has designed workshops on Self Awareness (including Reflective Practice), Active Listening and Responding skills, Group Work Skills, Creative Writing (Life Story/Memoir), Personal Development Groups and Working with Emotional Intelligence.

She has a First Class Honours Degree in Professional Development and continues to add to this with training that meets her interests. Andrea has two young children; she is a Parent Governor at her son’s school and a Volunteer Helper at her daughter’s school.

Andrea is also a Member of the Board of Directors for Community Music East (CME) Ltd.

Andrea Rippon’s Specialties:

- Works with people at all levels: from professionals to individuals in rehabilitation.

- Develops creative ways to encourage learning from experience, within a context set by the client.

- Values the individual and discovers ways to get the most from them. Increases their self esteem and self confidence.

- Facilitation a strength – able to work ‘in the moment’

- Track record of meeting Learning Aims and Objectives and ensuring quality and validation.
