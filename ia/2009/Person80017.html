Floyd DePalma’s Summary

Floyd DePalma is the President of the IASA (International Association of Software Architects) Nashville chapter. The IASA is a non-profit business association created by architects. Its current membership is nearing 6000 with approximately 50 chapters around the world. The IASA is focused on the professional first and the profession second, and is for all architects: Software, Infrastructure, Business, Information and Enterprise.

With over 17 years of corporate IT experience, Floyd started his career in 1985 as an applications programmer for the U.S. Army’s 1st Infantry Division. After completion of his military obligation he attended the University of Michigan – Dearborn and graduated with a B.S. in computer information systems. While in Michigan he was employed and or provided software consulting services to companies in the automotive industry such as: Ford Motor Company, General Motors Corporation, AppNet, Commerce One, and Covisint. He moved to Nashville in 2004 and started his career in the healthcare industry working first for digiChart Inc. and then Hospital Corporation of America (HCA).

Floyd DePalma’s Specialties:

Specialties would include: understanding of maintainability concerns and business stakeholder management, understanding of real ROI value from architectural advancement, powerful communication skills due to dealing with a broad range of stakeholders, deep understanding of managerial and human dynamics due to internal politics and managerial responsibility overlap, balance of technology depth and breadth.
