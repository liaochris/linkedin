Bill Wales’s Summary

Ph.D. in Business Strategy and Entrepreneurship. My research and consulting interests span the development and maintenance of firm competitive advantage through the identification, evaluation, and pursuit of entrepreneurial opportunities. My interests also include analyzing the corporate innovation process, focusing upon the ability of existing firms to better recognize, evaluate, and effectively exploit opportunities for new products/services, markets, ways of organizing, or other means through which they may leverage their strengths.

Bill Wales’s Specialties:

Identifiying and developing sustainable business models, market profiling and analysis, branding and identity, information systems engineering

Bill Wales’s Experience

  • Visiting Professor of Business Strategy

    Skidmore College

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    August 2008 — Present (10 months)

    During my visiting assistant professorship I taught courses in Entrepreneurship and Small Business Strategy, Business Organization and Management, as well as the senior-level capstone course covering Corporate Strategic Management. I managed the development and launch of several small business enterprises within my course on entrepreneurship including KD energy bars. I was also responsible for the creation of an institutional wide speaker series on Innovation and Entrepreneurship.

  • Post-Doc, Researching Business Strategy & Entrepreneurship

    RPI

    (Educational Institution; 1001-5000 employees; Research industry)

    August 2007 — May 2008 (10 months)

    In addition to continuing my research exploring the entrepreneurial behavioral orientations of individuals and organizations, I also worked to investigate the social, governmental, and industrial antecedents and effects of India’s growing telecommunication revolution.

  • Contracted Researcher

    US Air Force Research Laboratories

    (Research industry)

    September 2002 — August 2004 (2 years)

    I was part of a four person multidisciplinary team which explored the implications of various testing and evaluation methodologies for newly developed information technology based decision support systems. The goal of the research was to determine what steps are necessary to conclude that a given decision aid significantly improves a commanders decision-making effectiveness while avoiding the adverse unintended consequence of limiting his or her understanding of the situation (e.g., strategic blindspots, overconfidence in a limited set of potential courses of action, lack of adequate consideration of adversarial responses or long term effects, etc.).

  • Information Systems Developer

    GE Power Systems

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; GE; Financial Services industry)

    May 2002 — August 2002 (4 months)

    During my internship with GE Power Systems, I completed a six sigma project considered sufficient to award the title of six sigma green belt. The project was an international information systems integration initiative to communicate forecasted gas and steam turbine spare parts production requirements. I designed a prototype database to consolidate, track, and distribute the production order requirements among the multinational supply chain partners.

  • Systems Engineer

    Plug Power

    (Public Company; PLUG; Oil & Energy industry)

    May 2001 — August 2001 (4 months)

    During my internship with Plug Power I developed recommendations for the creation of a wireless real-time inventory management process. I was also able to accomplish the implementation of a bar coding standard for product shipping and storage.

Bill Wales’s Education

  • Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute

    Ph.D., Management, 2004 — 2007

    Concentration: Business Strategy & Entrepreneurship

    My doctoral studies examined a variety of managerial topics focusing on business strategy & entrepreneurship including formulating sustainable business models, developing and sustaining organizational competitive advantages, fostering organizational innovation, crafting viable go to market strategies, and developing customer captivity through an understanding of the consumer mindset.

    My research interests include firm strategic orientations, top management teams, and new venture development. My research exploring the concept of entrepreneurial orientation, or entrepreneurial behavioral dispositions thought to characterize new as well as established firms, has been presented internationally, published within prominent book series, and is currently under review for publication at several well respected journals.

  • Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute

    M.S., Industrial & Management Engineering, 2003 — 2004

    Principally studied models and tactics for understanding and improving the efficiency of organizational processes, with particular emphasis upon the important role of human-computer task partitioning in information technology based process control solutions.
