Shibley Rahman’s Summary

My first degree was in medicine. As part of my Ph.D., I produced one of the world's seminal patients on the diagnosis of frontal variant frontotemporal dementia. This paper has stood the test of time, and has been a major influence on subsequent research.

I have produced 2 textbooks in clinical medicine (one to be published in 2010) and 20 first-author international papers on dementia and Parkinson's disease. Finally, I have given about 10-15 presentations on clinical medicine/cognitive neurology, including the Royal Society.

I passed all my examinations for the Royal College of Physicians (UK) in March 2005. This is a very complex examination of medical knowledge and skills.

In 2004, I passed at my first attempt the Advanced Life Support examination in running a cardiac arrest. This course was run by the Resuscitation Council. Shortly after passing the exam, I ran three cardiac arrest teams, and my patients survived in all of them and were taken to ITU (they all were in cardiac asystole.)

Recently, I have studied for and passed successfully the examinations in PRINCE2 complex project management
(October 2009).

I was on life support in a coma in Summer 2007 for an uninterrupted 6 weeks. Notwithstanding this, I completed my Graduate Diploma in Law in September 2008. Finally, I became one of the first handful of graduates from BPP Law School with a Bachelor of Laws, with my dissertation in the law and ethics of human organ donation.

At the moment, my efforts are very much directed to my company. I also act as a freelance consultant in scientific evidence for the diagnosis, treatment and care of neurological conditions/diseases. I am hoping in 2010 to set up an international collaboration to examine apathy as an early non-motor symptom of Parkinson's disease.

My goal is simply to stay healthy and happy. I have given up alcohol and smoking. These were the hugest challenges in my life. I am now totally devoted to my parents, whom I adore.
