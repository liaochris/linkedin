Piers Hogarth-Scott’s Summary

In life before the Internet, I used to organise acid-house dance parties. In many ways it was a good foundation to what I do now, because it taught me how to leverage digital media and social networks.

My early professional career was spent working for London based multinational advertising agencies in the days when everyone had secretaries and the long lunch was legendary.

By the mid 90’s I was hooked on the web and spent far too much time in a virtual environment that enabled multi-user, realtime music making across the Internet.

As an early adopter of the web I’ve participated in the Internet’s extraordinary growth, the indulgence of the dot com boom, the crash of the tech wreck, and now the social web’s meteoric rise to the mainstream market.

I have spent the last 15 years running digital agencies in the UK and Australia and I find the web just as exciting now as it was at the outset. I’ve been lucky enough to work with some of the most talented people in the industry and led businesses that have won awards including a Silver Cyber Lion, the Grand Prix Milia d’Or and the Grand Prize Revolution Award.

I've been invited to speak on the topic of digital and WOM marketing at many of the key industry events, sat on the advisory board of ad:tech, the judging panels of AIMIA, and been recognised by Business Age as one of the Top 50 Entrepreneurs to have made the most significant contribution to the Internet, innovation and wealth creation.

Contact me on piershs@gmail.com

Piers Hogarth-Scott’s Specialties:

Digital marketing, WOM marketing, social media, bottom-up connect and collaborate marketing strategies, the hunt for new business

  • Executive Management

    HotHouse Interactive

    (Privately Held; Internet industry)

    2007 — September 2009 (2 years)

    HotHouse is one of Australia’s most experienced interactive marketing agencies in the business of making brands work for people online. I was brought on to help transform the business for growth. In July 2009 the Forrester Wave named HotHouse as a Leader in Australian Interactive Marketing Agencies.

  • Co-Founder

    Yooster

    (Marketing and Advertising industry)

    2006 — September 2009 (3 years)

    Yooster is a leading word of mouth marketing and social media agency in Australia. It provides a new way for brands to engage and connect with influential consumers, and deliver enhanced product and marketing experiences that generate word-of-mouth advocacy to aid business growth. Clients include Blackmores, Beiersdorf, Unilever, Simplot, Nestle, the Cancer Council, Starlight Children's Foundation.

  • CEO

    Patts Digital

    (Marketing and Advertising industry)

    2004 — 2006 (2 years)

    The digital arm of Australia's oldest and greatest advertising agency. I was brought in by The Communications Group (TCG) to restructure the business and maximise it's profitability in preparation for a trade sale or IPO. The business was successfully sold to WPP. Key clients included Telstra, News Limited, CARSguide.com.au, the AFL, Fosters Group, Cricket Australia, Arnotts.

  • Managing Director

    Digital Media Communications

    (Marketing and Advertising industry)

    2001 — 2004 (3 years)

    Recognising the increasing ineffectiveness of mainstream advertising, media fragmentation and clutter, I established the Australian arm of DMC as a new generation of digital agency which shunned the old ‘command and control’ mentality of mass-marketing, and instead provided revolutionary connect and collaborate marketing solutions to major brands, where marketing is done with people rather than at them. Clients included Mazda, Virgin, Ford, Microsoft, Sony Ericsson, Diesel, Eidos, MTV, Pacific Brands, Levi’s.

  • CEO

    Blueberry

    (Marketing and Advertising industry)

    1996 — 2001 (5 years)

    Blueberry was one of the first digital agencies in London. Over the course of 5 years, we grew the business from revenues of $0.2 million to $13.0 million; personnel from 8 to 100+; deployed 300+ online campaigns; won more than 30 industry awards; and propelled the agency from unranked outsider to the number one or two independent digital agency in the UK. Key clients included Ted Baker, FatFace, CNN, Laura Ashley, Bluesq.com, Motley Fool, Cable & Wireless and more.

  • Account Director

    Bainsfair Sharkey Trott (BST)

    (Marketing and Advertising industry)

Around 1997 BST-BDDP was taken over by GGT and then merged with TBWA. Key clients included TAG Heuer, Hertz, Classic FM, Monsanto, Credit Suisse and others.
