Richard Whelchel, MSB, PMP, Six Sigma BB’s Summary

How does your Enterprise Project Portfolio align with corporate strategy? How do your Line of Business Portfolios align? How many dollars are at risk in your Project Portfolio? Does your Project Portfolio's risk equal your risk tolerance? How many projects are in a Green, Yellow, or Red status? Why? What is the Earned Value position of your overall Portfolio? Subsidiary Portfolios? Subsidiary Programs? Subsidiary Projects? What happens to your Resources if the next project is approved? Does your Project Approval Process account for real constraints like your workforce, including the needed skillsets?

I am a seasoned PMO/Portfolio/Program/Project Manager with experience in building Project Office and Portfolio Management disciplines and processes. These activities have included applying and tracking realistic metrics, implementing and managing integrated Portfolio Management solutions (Clarity's predecessors: ABT and Niku), and Project Methods Authoring - all with an eye toward providing solid benefits that exceed related costs.

My experience includes decades of Program/Project Management and 10+ years of Portfolio Management.

I am also very experienced, and very interested, in Process Improvement. I do not construe this to be a sideline, but as an additional toolset to help create or improve efficiencies in the current environment to the extent justifiable by the benefits to be realized.

It is not as hard as you might think to answer the questions above, but the benefits that accrue from tracking that information, properly implemented, will far exceed their costs.
