  • Deputy County Executive

    Nassau County

    (Government Agency; Government Administration industry)

    December 2007 — January 2010 (2 years 2 months)

    The Deputy County Executive has powers and responsibilities similar to the County Executive in operational, legal, legislative and contractual matters. The primary role of the position is to oversee County departments and implement the political and governmental agenda of the County Executive both strategically and operationally. My primary duties are:

    • Oversight of Parks, Recreation, Museums, Public Works and partnerships.
    • Create, manage and coordinate all public relations matters for the County on all issues pertaining to my departments and area of oversight.
    • Act as primary media spokesperson for the County on a variety of policy and governmental matters.
    • Strategically plan a comprehensive roll out and out reach on new polices, services and projects both internally and externally to County department, elected officials, stake holders, constituents and the media.
    • Responsible for annual operating budgets totaling over $300 million, an annual Capital Improvement Plan of over $150 Million and over 1,900 full-time, part-time and seasonal employees.
    • Strategic planning and operations for 77 public parks, golf courses, museums, historic properties, preserves, beaches and swimming pools as well as 200 lane miles of roadway including 52 fixed bridges, 1600 traffic signals, 600 recharge basins, 200 miles of streams, 53 sewage pump stations and 3 sewage treatment plants.
    • Implementation of Nassau County’s first comprehensive plan to historically preserve and renovation of a vast portfolio of historic properties by leveraging tax dollars, capital funds, state and federal grants and establishing unprecedented private support through public private partnerships.
    • Expansion of park revenue through adding new programming, partnerships and facilities.
    • Consolidation of various duplicative County functions in the Department of Public Works creating efficiencies, cutting waste and saving millions in tax dollars.
