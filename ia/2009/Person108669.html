Jeffrey Weiss’s Summary

* Platform-agnostic media content creator, as comfortable in the traditional format of dead-tree print as blogging or talking into a camera.
* Functional staff leader – expected by peers and supervisors to ask hard questions and find answers.
* Liaison between management and senior staff.
* Plays well with others – from mentoring down to working up with a dozen different bosses.
* Critical thinker/problem solver – newsroom activist for product development and quality assessment.

Jeffrey Weiss’s Specialties:

* Taking complex topics -- from religion to health care to personal finance -- and translating them for a general audience.
* Fairness, accuracy and speed – no matter how complicated or controversial the topic.
* Creativity on deadline – finding the new angle and making it work.

Family motto: "Run and find out."

Jeffrey Weiss’s Experience

  • Senior Writer

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; Newspapers industry)

  • Religion Reporter

    The Dallas Morning News

    (Public Company; Newspapers industry)

    1996 — 2007 (11 years)

    * Assigned to the succession of popes from Rome, discussed faith with Joe Lieberman during his run for the vice presidency, covered the most contentious religious disputes in the nation, including those among Baptists, Methodists, and Episcopalians.
    * For more than six years, made regular appearances on TxCN, a Belo-owned statewide TV news network.

  • General Assignment Reporter

    The Dallas Morning News

    (Public Company; Newspapers industry)

    1991 — 1996 (5 years)

    * Investigations of Hooked on Phonics, Cancer Treatment Centers of America and “cold fusion.”
    * Dispatched to cover floods in the Midwest, brush fires in East Texas, and Mardi Gras during the Gulf War.
    * Covered abortion as a mini-beat and spent a week in Wichita in 1991, covering the “Summer of Mercy” protests.

  • Social Services Reporter

    The Dallas Morning News

    (Public Company; Newspapers industry)

    1988 — 1991 (3 years)

    The first local reporter to every hold this assignment for the Dallas Morning News. My analysis of state spending on social services was used by staffers in the Texas legislature.

  • Reporter

    Miami Herald

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; KRI; Newspapers industry)

    1980 — 1988 (8 years)

    * Covered police, small and medium-sized city government, county government, schools, lifestyles and general assignment in Miami-Dade and Palm Beach counties.
    * Assignments ranged from a statistical assessment of murders during Miami’s most violent year to exploring plans by the Palm Beach County Solid Waste Authority for a garbage-fueled power plant.
