Richard Lindsey’s Experience

  • CEO

    Callcott Group

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Financial Services industry)

    January 2007 — Present (2 years 3 months)

    Responsible for providing state-of-the-art qualitative and quantitative advice to pensions, endowments, foundations, family offices and others regarding trading strategies, investment strategies, portfolio construction, and risk management.

  • Senior Managing Director

    Bear, Stearns and Co

    (Public Company; 5001-10,000 employees; BSC; Financial Services industry)

    March 1999 — December 2006 (7 years 10 months)

    President of Bear Stearns Securities Corporation. Responsible for broker-dealer with over $2 billion in capital, $290 billion in assets and 1,700 employees. Doubled assets and pre-tax income in five years; decreased arbitrations and litigation by 80 percent. Annual revenues of over $1 billion and pre-tax income of over $350 million. Provided prime brokerage, custody, clearing, and execution services to more than 3,500 hedge fund, broker-dealer, and investment advisor clients in 73 countries.

    Member of holding company Bear Stearns Companies Inc.’s eight-person Management Committee, and of the Operations, Principal Activities, Internal Audit Advisory, Compliance, Retirement Planning, and Wealth Management Services Advisory Committees; Chairman of the Client Review and Best Execution Committees.

  • Director of Market Regulation

    US Securities and Exchange Commission

    (Financial Services industry)

    November 1995 — February 1999 (3 years 4 months)

    Responsible for SEC oversight of securities markets and professionals, stock and options exchanges, bond and OTC derivatives, clearance and settlement systems, and trading practices, and for implementing the SEC’s national market system, risk management and control, and anti-money laundering programs. As primary US representative to the international securities and banking regulatory community, worked actively with regulators and industry leaders worldwide to promote consistent international regulation, control systemic risk, make available international investment opportunities to US investors, and open US markets to foreign trading.

  • Assistant Professor of Finance

  • Consultant
