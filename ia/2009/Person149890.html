Marguerite Barrett’s Summary

Marguerite began her career in academia working towards a Ph.D. in English Renaissance Literature. The dearth of available university-level jobs in her field led her to switch careers in the late 90s at which time she joined Lamalie Amrop International, one of the world's largest retained executive search firm. She parlayed her teaching experience into a training position in 2000 when she stepped into the role of Training Manager responsible for the design and delivery of a global roll-out of a new database and business process for TMP Worldwide Executive Search. From 2001-2003, she successfully delivered the new program to employees in 11 countries and led the corresponding change management initiatives.

In 2003, TMP Worldwide spun off their mid-level and executive recruiting divisions into Hudson Highland Group, and Marguerite remained with the executive search division, renamed Highland Partners. She served as a member of the senior management team working closely with the CEO, CFO, CIO and VP of HR on a process improvements, executive recruiting best practices, and employee development initiatives.

In January 2007, Marguerite joined The Hartford as Director, Learning & Development - Staffing, and in August 2008 was asked to lead the centralized executive recruiting function for The Hartford. In January 2009, Marguerite transitioned into a new role overseeing recruiting technologies for the Talent Acquisition organization.
