Sharad Mittal’s Summary

Sharad founded Crimson Interactive in 2005 providing English language solutions in the Japanese market. The innovative business model - working with experts from over 20 countries to provide services from India, enabled Crimson to be amongst the top 3 editing providers in Japan. With Sharad's focus on innovation and determination to re-define the industry, Crimson has successfully changed the entire editing landscape of Japan in the past 2 years. Crimson is currently more than 50 people strong and works with more than 200 experts around the globe, with more than 6000 clients. It also has an office in Japan with over 250 corporate clients.

Sharad also initiated and guided the project to publish a book about 12 top researchers in Japan (including Nobel prize candidates Shuji Nakamura- inventor of the blue diode), who have overcome the language barriers to publish in journals. The book is called 英語のバカヤロー! ~「英語の壁」に挑んだ12人の日本人~— literally translated as "English idiot! I hate you –- 12 Japanese people who tackled the English language block." It is available on Amazon.

Crimson also successfully partnered with Japan’s largest Academic Publisher, Maruzen, to be the exclusive service provider for their editorial services. Crimson has also tied up with Dai Nippon Publishing (DNP), the second largest printer in the world for their editorial needs. This partnership opens up a new frontier for Crimson to becoming one of the largest editing players worldwide.
