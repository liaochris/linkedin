Forrest Holleman’s Summary

During the last 18 years, I have spent a great deal of time and effort setting up and running various businesses in the international arena, predominantly in the IT sector. Using this experience, I have focused most of my energies during the past 10 years on coaching and mentoring entrepreneurs in the areas of international expansion, globalization, strategic partnership development, investment capital raising, group dynamics, and corporate culture.

I have a number of characteristics that greatly assist me in achieving my business objectives, including:

* Natural ability in lateral thinking

* Attention to detail

* Making relatively complex subjects seem simple to lay-people

* Persistence and perseverance

* Fine balance between logic and intuition

* Positive mental attitude

* High degree of motivation

* Very strong "people" skills

* High degree of patience (with others, less so with myself!)

* A keen understanding of my own weaknesses and a natural ability to surround myself with others who are strong in the areas I where am not

* Ability to effectively communicate - in speech and in writing

  • VP, WW Strategic Relationships and Business Development

    Clickatell, Inc.

    (Privately Held; Telecommunications industry)

    My current role is to build strategic relationships with multinational clients, technology partners and telcos to extend Clickatell's lead in the Global Enterprise Messaging space.

  • Founder and General Partner

    Global Eyes, Inc

    (Management Consulting industry)

    2002 — Present (7 years )

    Global Eyes, Inc. focuses primarily on assisting companies that have proven themselves in their respective local markets to expand internationally.

    Candidate clients must have a product or service that has been accepted in their local market and that is truly exportable to other geographies. Typical companies with whom we work have proven business models and now need guidance in creating and implementing their globalization strategies. Such strategies include which markets to address first and how to address them (set up organically, partner with foreign firms, acquire foreign companies, etc.).

    Global Eyes is also very active in raising the capital required for its clients' globalization efforts and works very closely with a number of venture capital and private equity firms in this regard.

  • Founder and General Partner

    Add Venture Capital (Pty) Ltd

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Venture Capital & Private Equity industry)

    2000 — 2002 (2 years )

    Add Venture Capital (AVC) was created to help bridge the large gap between entrepreneurs and sources of investment capital (venture capital, private equity, seed capital, angels, etc.).

    The company specialized in lowering the risks for potential investors by qualifying and quantifying non-tangible assets within start-up ventures, such as intellectual capital.

    Another key differentiator between AVC and other "intermediaries" was its strong commitment to ensuring that entrepreneurs were connected with the right investment partners for their businesses.

  • Founder and Chief Executive Officer

    African Middleware Corporation (Pty) Ltd

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Information Technology and Services industry)

    1998 — 2000 (2 years )

    During my time with BEA Systems, I identified the tremendous need for a truly vendor-independent services company, focused on the needs of middleware and enterprise application integration in the South African market and dedicated to providing an environment that would be conducive to the creation of skills in the local market.

    In 1998, I left BEA Systems and created African Middleware Corporation (AMC) with two other founding partners. The new company was created to provide professional, unbiased consulting, support and training services in the field of middleware to Southern Africa’s corporate and governmental market. During this time, AMC provided these services to many large blue chip South African organisations including Nedcor, Standard Bank, Absa Bank, Eskom, Sanlam, Telkom, MTN, South African Revenue Service, Transnet, Spoornet and others.

  • Vice President, African Operations

    BEA Systems, Inc.

    (Public Company; 201-500 employees; BEAS; Computer Software industry)

    1996 — 1998 (2 years )

    In 1995, BEA Systems, Inc., was formed in Sunnyvale, California, to address the needs of the rapidly growing, worldwide middleware market. In January 1996, BEA Systems acquired Novell’s entire TUXEDO division, including all development, technical and marketing staff. In September of the same year, BEA Systems completed the acquisition of Innov-X SA as part of its aggressive international expansion strategy. I served as Vice President, African Operations for the two years following the acquisition, responsible for all aspects of BEA Systems’ business in the region.

    Innov-X and BEA South Africa were engaged in several major projects in South Africa, including enterprise application integration projects in retail (Shoprite-Checkers), utilities (Eskom), agriculture (Unifruco), telecommunications (MTN), government (Department of Home Affairs) and banking (Absa Bank).

  • Founder and Chief Executive Officer

    Innov-X SA (Pty) Ltd

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Information Technology and Services industry)

    1993 — 1996 (3 years )

    In 1992, Novell resumed discussions with AT&T regarding the acquisition of USL (Unix Systems Laboratories), a wholly owned subsidiary of AT&T. USL was the owner of two key technologies in the area of distributed computing, namely the UNIX operating system and TUXEDO, the world’s leading open systems transaction processing monitor (middleware).

    In early 1993, I opened discussions with renowned UNIX and distributed computing guru, Professor Ken MacGregor, of the University of Cape Town, regarding the market for such products in South Africa. As a result of these discussions, I left Novell in late 1993 to create Innov-X SA (Pty) Ltd, a South African company dedicated to providing the software and skills required for the design, development and deployment of distributed computing solutions.

    After Novell completed the acquisition of USL, Innov-X SA, founded by myself and Professor MacGregor, became Novell’s sole agent for UnixWare and TUXEDO for Africa.

  • Manager, Mediterranean, Middle East and African Territories

    Novell, Inc

    1986 — 1993 (7 years )

    I held several positions during the seven years I spent working for Novell (and Excelan, a 1989 Novell acquisition). Prior to that, I held positions such as Manager, Distribution for the UK, Ireland and Nordic Region, Strategic Accounts Manager, Europe (liaison for Novell with vendors such as IBM, HP, Digital, Prime, etc.) and Manager, Technical Services for Europe (Excelan).
