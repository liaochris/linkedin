Nick Bomford’s Summary

Nick Bomford is a seasoned and influential executive with over 25 years in the music business, working on live performances and recordings with some of the best classical musicians around. He is currently a partner at NB Management, a musical solutions provider that focuses on painstakingly matching musicians to each client's particular event. The company's reliable contact list includes some of the world's most talented performers.

Nick Bomford’s Specialties:

Selecting soloists, orchestra contracting and managing musicians to cultivate each client’s unique ethos. Latest bizarre request was 5 ukelele players for a TV location shoot at 48 hours notice - no problem.

  • General Manager, Director and Company Secretary

    London Soloists Chamber Orchestra

    (Music industry)

    1991 — 2007 (16 years)

    For 16 years this orchestra was my major client. My aim was to present talented musicians at the beginning of their career, most players being in their mid-20s, with more experienced people in key positions. I provided encouragement, calm and thoughtful mentoring, and dealt with any attendant issues in a non-confrontational manner. Loyalty and morale were always high, and the orchestra provided an exceptional and sparkling launch-pad, with many alumni now performing in world-class orchestras.

  • General Manager

    MKCO (Milton Keynes Chamber Orchestra)

    (Music industry)

    1988 — 1991 (3 years)

    This is where I started in management. I systematically drew together the orchestra’s first business plan and effectively boosted the number of concerts by 150%. Sponsorship increased 165%, ticket income 55%, advertising revenue showed a ten-fold increase and earned income six-fold. Re-vitalizing public perception led to an overall attendance figure of 96%, and we achieved three government awards for sponsorship. I was also a bit lucky, and generated finance for five commercial recordings that received critical acclaim and were broadcast worldwide, thereby raising the stature of both Milton Keynes and the orchestra.
