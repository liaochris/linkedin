  • Director of Marketing and Sales

    (Semiconductors industry)

    1983 — 1983 (less than a year)

    Successfully changed marketing direction to take advantage of a long shortage of bipolar logic ICs. Defined a CMOS chip with mask options to drop into over 25% of the huge bipolar logic market, octal interface circuits. Captured initial 100K unit order - contingent on socket-compatibility. First to announce plans for CMOS drop-ins for bipolar mask-programmed and programmable "PALs". Tripled NASDAQ stock price in 3 quarters.

  • Sales and Applications Manager

    Hughes Aircraft Company

    (Semiconductors industry)

    1981 — 1983 (2 years)

    Failed to get my best invention produced. Hughes' unique CMOS EEPROM technology suffered from low yields, typically due to single-bit errors. Innovation was to produce smaller Self-Healing EEPROMs, providing guarantees of a couple of orders of magnitude more write cycles. We'd have captured millions of sockets for odometers, keys, etc. Managed Western US sales of CMOS LCD drivers (into gas pumps and consumer products), EEPROMs, and processors.

  • New Product Definition Engineer

    Harris Semiconductor

    (Semiconductors industry)

    1979 — 1981 (2 years)

    Process, Design, and Test Engineers do the heavy lifting in the semiconductor industry. Product definition facilitates communications and forges options into firm specifications... Wrote the original specifications for CMOS 8086/8048 families, adding 2 Op Codes (Sleep and Standby) to the instruction set, and including mask-option 'tacks' for pure CMOS or NMOS/bipolar-compatible systems. Intel and Harris cross-licensed 8086/8048 with it's CMOS implementation a couple of years later. Wrote some of the source documents resulting in the Matra-Harris joint venture. Studied digital telecom.

  • New Product Definition Engineer

    Texas Instruments - Houston

    (Semiconductors industry)

    1976 — 1979 (3 years)

    Architect of the TMS9909 Floppy Disk Controller - precursor to modern DSP's, and TMS9911 DMA Controller.
