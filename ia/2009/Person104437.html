Jared Hoffman’s Summary

Jared Hoffman is a seasoned and successful business builder and entertainment industry strategist.

At present, Jared is developing digital and mobile content strategy for AEG Network Live, structuring content distribution and sponsorship partnerships for America's biggest music festivals including All Points West, Lollapalooza, Virgin Mobile Free Festival and more. Jared is also developing entertainment industry mobile commerce strategy and offerings for ShopText, a leading mobile commerce and marketing platform.

As CEO of Knitting Factory Entertainment, Jared transformed the Knitting Factory into the nation's most prolific presenter of new and emerging talent. His secondary market expansion strategy has brought Knitting Factory Concert Houses to Boise, ID, Spokane WA, Reno, NV, and into concert operations across 18 states. Hoffman's strategic digital initiatives, anchored by the company's new HD content aggregation systems, are giving rise to an exciting array of content and distribution partnerships, artist services revenue streams, and new ways of harnessing the community that forms nightly at Knitting Factory concerts.

Jared Hoffman’s Specialties:

Live venue, concert promotion, record label, music publishing operations, finance, strategic planning, legal. Digital strategy, content development, branded entertainment, sponsorship, strategic partnerships, and acquisitions.

  • Chief Executive Officer

    Knitting Factory Entertainment

    (Entertainment industry)

    June 2002 — December 2008 (6 years 7 months)

    Jared Hoffman led the transformation of Knitting Factory Entertainment (KFE) into a leading national concert and tour promotion company with nationwide operations. KFE owns and operates the legendary Knitting Factory music clubs in New York and Hollywood, and Knitting Factory Concert House venues in Boise, ID, Spokane, WA, and Reno, NV. KFE produces and promotes venue, amphitheater, and arena concerts across 18 states.

    In 2007, The Knitting Factory venues became the first in America to offer ticketing by TXT, and Live From The Knitting Factory became a staple of XM Satellite Radio. Anchored by the company's new HD content aggregation systems, these, and other recent digital initiatives are transforming the depth of the KFE's connection to it's audiences and giving rise to an exciting array of strategic content partnerships and artist services offerings. KFE's direct content interests include the Instinct Records Label Group and Urbana Arts music publishing.

  • President and Founder

    Instinct Records Corporation

    (Entertainment industry)

    September 1989 — May 2002 (12 years 9 months)

    Instinct Records Label Group: Instinct Records, Shadow Records, Evolver Entertainment, Urbana Arts

    In 1989 Jared signed a then unknown artist by the name of Moby. It was a precocious move which led to the release of a string of worldwide hit singles propelling this start-up record label onto the international stage. With an initial focus on electronic and dance music, Instinct Records also brought the Acid Jazz genre to the US where it flourished and earned Instinct a leadership role in transforming smooth jazz radio. More recently, Instinct has focused on AAA radio and iconic artists such as Siouxsie Sioux, Kirsty MacColl, and Jeff Buckley. Shadow Records was created to focus on underground and alternative music, introducing the US to DJ Krush, the NinjaTune family, and DJ Cam. Evolver was formed to bring world music to the roster with releases by Tony Allen, Bebel Gilberto, and Luka Bloom to name a few. Instinct Records was acquired by Knitting Factory Entertainment in 2002.
