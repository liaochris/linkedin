Francisco Ruiz’s Summary

Francisco Ruiz joined the National Alliance of State and Territorial AIDS Directors (NASTAD) as a Program Manager in the Racial and Ethnic Health Disparities Program in 2007. He provides guidance on how to address the disproportionate impact of HIV/AIDS and viral hepatitis among Latino communities. As Manager, he leads NASTAD’s Latino Advisory Committee that consists of health department staff members from the U.S. and its territories. The core objective of the committee is to strengthen state and local health departments’ capacity to implement culturally competent, relevant, and effective programming.

Francisco’s experience in the HIV/AIDS field extends to work in the US, Latin America, and the Caribbean. He served two years in Ecuador with the Peace Corps. After his Peace Corps tour, Francisco spent two years in Washington, DC working on HIV/AIDS prevention at two community-based organizations: the Latin American Youth Center (LAYC) and the Sexual Minority Youth Assistance League (SMYAL). In 2006, Francisco spent time in the Dominican Republic with the Consejo Presidencial del SIDA (COPRESIDA- Presidential Commission on AIDS). At COPRESIDA, he assisted with the formation of “Alianza GTH,” a network of community-based organizations and groups throughout the Dominican Republic that specifically work with gay men, transgender women and other men who sleep with men. Francisco facilitated workshops that focused on building skill sets to effectively address stigma surrounding HIV/AIDS in the Caribbean country.

Francisco received a BA in Political Science and International Studies from Loyola University Chicago and a MS in Urban Policy Analysis and Management from Milano The New School for Management and Urban Policy in New York City.
