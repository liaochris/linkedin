Joseph Dwyer’s Summary

Venture capitalist and Internet entrepreneur / senior executive with over 15 years experience starting, building, and selling consumer Internet and enterprise software companies – Acquired over $20 million in venture and angel funding – Built technology consulting business serving companies such as Bain & Company, Sempra Energy, and Albertson’s – technical expert in distributed web software systems design and implementation, online lead generation, Software-as-a-Service, and social web solutions

Joseph Dwyer’s Specialties:

Venture capital, technology strategy, market strategy, software design, social web / web 2.0, Software-as-a-Service, online lead generation, web development, entrepreneurship, international business

Joseph Dwyer’s Experience

  • CEO

    Brill Street + Company

    (Privately Held; Staffing and Recruiting industry)

    March 2009 — Present (9 months)

    Brill Street was originally a conventional temporary staffing and contingent recruiting firm. Joe completely revamped the team, strategy, and product in less than six months. The company is now a rapidly growing technology leader in the online job matching space.

  • Senior Associate

    OCA Ventures

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Venture Capital & Private Equity industry)

    October 2008 — Present (1 year 2 months)

  • CEO

    ArtistData

    (Internet industry)

    May 2008 — October 2008 (6 months)

    ArtistData is an online social web platform and tool enabling music artists to manage their online presence. Joe began as an adviser and board member, and then came on to shepherd the company through a period of rapid change through new product development, business development, online lead generation, and viral growth. During this period the company forged relationships with nearly every major music label, and many prominent music artists.

  • CEO and co-founder

    PlayNames Publishing

    (Media Production industry)

    February 2002 — September 2005 (3 years 8 months)

    PlayNames produces personalized music products for children. The company leverages its own proprietary on-demand technology to dramatically improve productivity and reduce costs of delivery. The company's first music album was placed on the ballot for a Grammy Nomination in 2003, and the company continues to receive acclaim for both product quality and business efficiency.

  • President and co-founder

    TouchPoint Solutions (now Esprida)

    (Computer Software industry)

    February 2001 — October 2002 (1 year 9 months)

    Conceived, designed, and led development of groundbreaking web application with enterprise back-end designed and integrated local client for management of distributed managed devices. Clients included Ford Motor Company, Lockheed Martin, and British Petroleum. The software was licensed by Kodak for remote management of their network of 70,000 photo kiosks.

  • CEO and founder

    Digital Design Studios

    (Privately Held; Computer Software industry)

    September 1994 — March 2002 (7 years 7 months)

    Joe founded Digital Design Studios in 1994 with $1,000 cash and a credit card. He quickly grew the company to over 60 employees with offices in St. Louis, Branson, Los Angeles, and Toronto. The company served as a springboard for multiple other companies, with clients such as Bain & Company, Exodus Computer, and Albertson's.

  • CEO and founder

    Blir

    (Computer Software industry)

    May 1998 — October 2001 (3 years 6 months)

    Blir was a high technology strategy, consulting, and custom development firm. The company specialized in cutting-edge web applications, online marketing / lead generation, and technology strategy for major national companies such as Bain & Company, Exodus, and Sempra Energy.

  • CTO

    LoanSurfer.com

    (Financial Services industry)

    January 1998 — November 1999 (1 year 11 months)

    LoanSurfer.com was one of the top five online mortgage banks in the industry. By leveraging exceptional (at the time) technology, the company acquired substantial market share. Examples include one of the first integrations with Fannie Mae, and a powerful online mortgage support system with audio and video (in 1998!).

  • CEO and founder

    VirtualMarket.com

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Internet industry)

    January 1995 — August 1996 (1 year 8 months)

    One of the first dynamically generated web sites ever created. Originally built upon proprietary code, the company was also one of the earliest B2B2C marketplaces ever created. Won numerous national awards for technology and design.
