  • Director Advanced Market Planning

    Zitel Corporation

    (Public Company; 201-500 employees; Electrical/Electronic Manufacturing industry)

    February 1991 — May 1996 (5 years 4 months)

    Working with my engineering team, this part of my career was marked by the innovation of two virtualization products and a business technology software tool. The first virtualization product was a mainframe co-processor that greatly reduced application-processing costs. The second virtualization product was conceived in 1994, and earned my team and I the Best New Product of the Year award by the DEC Professional magazine. My third innovation was a software tool called the Value Analysis Method, which allowed customers to deploy optimum system architectures by evaluating vendor neutral ROI analyses on various alternatives.

  • Director, OEM Marketing

    Zitel Corporation

    (Public Company; 201-500 employees; ZITL; Computer Hardware industry)

    February 1988 — February 1991 (3 years 1 month)

    In this technical sales/marketing position, my product engineering teams and I were responsible for many design-wins including a $10M+ design-win with GE Medical and a design-win with IBM that created $20M+ in high gross margin revenue. The GE design-win was in part attributable to my creative negotiation with a major Japanese DRAM vendor during a DRAM shortage, of a “futures contract’ that enabled my company to procure DRAM chips at a time when customers could not. My projects also included various electronic and signal intelligence projects for the P-3 Orion, the F-15E fighter, the Miles Tornado, the B-52 bomber and highly classified projects such as Ashtoreth, Project 1365, Vestage "B" and the EC-135.
