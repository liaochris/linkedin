Rob Durst’s Summary

Experience with all aspects of business strategy / business development and product commercialization; from concept creation and development in R&D, through post launch and business operations.

Extensive experience with the following:

• Building marketing, business development, R&D and engineering organizations, operational line management, public and private company representation, and the process of raising capital. Also working with and coordinating internal and outside counsel on contracts, licensing agreements, intellectual property development and litigation related issues.

• Developing comprehensive business and product strategy and marketing plans with effective presentation to executive management, boards of directors, partners, press and investors.

• Executive management in a startup through a successful IPO, and as an independent entrepreneur / consultant advising new and existing companies.

• Creating and managing portfolios of intellectual property to create sustainable competitive advantage.

• International marketing, business development and operations experience in the EU (Austria, Germany, UK, France), Canada, Mexico and Japan.

Rob Durst’s Specialties:

- Intellectual property portfolio development and management including IP asset development protection and licensing.

- Company assessment and valuation, particularly technology and IP assets.

- Monetization and commercialization strategy.

- R&D and engineering and technical marketing management.

  • Managing Partner

    The Durst Group

    (Privately Held; Printing industry)

    May 2005 — Present (4 years 1 month)

    (Public Company; Computer Software industry)

  • Founding Partner

  • Executive Vice President, Chief Technology Officer

    Neomedia Technologies, Inc.

    (Public Company; NEOM; Information Technology and Services industry)

    April 1996 — July 2001 (5 years 4 months)

    NeoMedia Technologies develops and markets proprietary software and technologies that link commercial products, print, and other physical objects to online information and services.

    • Recruited by founders to help launch the company. Defined R&D requirements, recruited key management and technical personnel, and built a development organization from the ground up that successfully architected, implemented and patented multiple generations of scalable and extensible software products to support a 24/7 worldwide commercial service.

    • Defined and implemented an intellectual property strategy in the print / web media convergence space. The resulting portfolio continues to serve as a competitive barrier-to-entry and also produced multi-million dollars of licensing revenue in 2000. Negotiated and documented this agreement with the legal and business and legal teams of a hostile licensee. Core patents in this portfolio where recently largely upheld in an EFF re-examination challenge and are now considered the core patent portfolio in the rapidly emerging mobile code space.

  • Vice President, 2D Systems

    Symbol Technologies

    (Public Company; SBL; Computer Hardware industry)

    March 1992 — March 1996 (4 years 1 month)

    Symbol Technologies, Inc., now part of Motorola, provides barcode data capture products, radio frequency identification technology, mobile computing platforms, and wireless infrastructure products and service to customers worldwide. It is a multi-billion dollar international business.

    • Recruited by executive management to commercialize PDF417, a high-density 2D barcode symbology capable of storing kilobytes of error corrected machine-readable information on print media. Formulated business development strategy and recruited and managed a multi-disciplinary business development and technical support team of 13 engineers, software developers and business professionals supplemented by technical, marketing and sales liaison in the US, Europe and Asia, with a multi-million dollar annual operating budget.

    • Succeeded in establishing PDF417 as the standard 2D symbology for key federal and state government agencies (e.g. DOD, DOE and state DMVs), industry standards bodies (e.g. ISO MH10.8, AIAG) and a variety of domestic and international applications. This created a multi-million dollar international market for related equipment, software and services. Applications include drivers’ licenses, ID cards, logistics, cell phone identification, transactional documents (e.g. boarding passes and tickets) and the USPS “electronic stamp”.
