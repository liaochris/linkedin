Chris Adamson’s Summary

Chris is a recognized data warehousing expert who specializes in dimensional design, also known as “star schema.” He provides consulting services through his company Oakton Software LLC, teaches at The Data Warehousing Institute, and writes books about data warehousing.

As a consultant, Chris works with customers in all industries to develop data warehousing strategies, identify and prioritize projects, design solutions, and manage implementations. His customers have included Fortune 500 companies, government entities and software tool vendors.

Chris is a faculty member at The Data Warehousing Institute (TDWI). He teaches classes in advanced star schema design at TDWI Conferences and through TDWI On-Site Education.

Chris's newest book is The Star Schema Handbook: A Complete Reference to Dimensional Data Warehouse Design (Wiley, 2009). It provides in-depth coverage of dimensional design principles that can be put to use in any architecture, including those advocated by W.H. Inmon and Ralph Kimball.

Chris also wrote Mastering Data Warehouse Aggregates (Wiley, 2006) and co-authored Data Warehouse Design Solutions (John Wiley and Sons, 1998). It provides a series of case studies linking dimensional designs to key business objectives, and remains a top seller in its category.

Chris answers reader questions and blogs about data warehousing at StarSchemaCentral.com.

Chris Adamson’s Specialties:

Defining data warehouse strategy, data warehouse schema design, dimensional modeling / star schema design, project definition and planning, schema enhancement and redesign, design reviews.
