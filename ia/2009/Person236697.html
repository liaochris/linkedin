Tony Breslin’s Summary

Tony Breslin has been CEO at the Citizenship Foundation, the independent education and participation charity, since September 2001.

Tony sits on a range of national policy bodies and advisory groups and works extensively with various departments and agencies including the Ministry of Justice, DCSF, CLG, the Cabinet Office, the Home Office and QCA on issues as varied as community cohesion, youth participation, political engagement, qualifications reform and citizenship education.

Previously, Tony was an adviser on 14-19 Education in the London Borough of Enfield and has taught and held senior and middle management positions at schools in Haringey and Hertfordshire. A past Chair and current Vice-president of the Association for the Teaching of Social Sciences, he is also a Trustee of the Association for Citizenship Teaching, the Chair of SpeakersBank and a non-executive director at ARC Theatre for Change. He has served as a Chief Examiner at GCSE and a Principal Examiner at A level and is an experienced teacher trainer and qualified OFSTED inspector.

Granted the Freedom of the City of London in 2004, Tony is widely published and frequently gives keynote addresses at conferences and seminars, in the UK and overseas, on matters of public policy and has given presentations at venues as diverse as City Hall, Tate Britain and 11 Downing Street for a range of organisations including the British Council, the BBC, EMAP Media and the Runnymede Trust. Beyond office hours, he is currently working on two projects, the publication of his recently completed doctoral thesis, Teachers, Schools and Change, and an edited collection on the future of secondary education, provisionally titled Re-schooling Society.

Although committed to an intolerance of underachievement, Tony was a season ticket holder at Tottenham Hotspur Football Club for seventeen years and remains an avid supporter.
