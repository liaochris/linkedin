Danica Brinton’s Summary

For the past 12 years, I have managed international product management and localization for consumer applications, operating systems, user experience, web search, vertical search, natural language products, MMORPG, mobile phones and mp3 players.

For Linden Lab, I am currently heading international business and product strategy; I am leading Second Life localization and spearheading one of the most extensive community localization projects in the open source community.

I also serve as a consultant to multiple departments at Apple, Inc. on iPhone localization and international marketing.

My localization agency, LocLabs, has been focused on localization and localization QA of Mac OS (standard and iPhone), localization and DTP of Apple manuals, and globalization of the Apple website.

Previously, I led internationalization and localization of Ask.com's search engine, launching the brand in 7 markets and then building the business and launching dozens of Ask's core and vertical search products internationally.

Prior to Ask, I managed localization and international launches of the first 4 generations of iPod, iTunes and iLife application suite for 26 markets. During my tenure with Apple, I also managed iPhoto, iChat, iDVD, and DVD Studio Pro localization projects.

I started in the industry as a technical translator with clients like Brown & Root, Orenstein and Koppel, Bakker Electronics, Bechtel, RASCO, HP, and Motorola. Additionally, I free-lanced as a foreign-language voice-over talent and cultural advisor on a number of video games (most prominent being the Prince of Persia series).
