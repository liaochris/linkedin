John South’s Summary

I believe in the power of Internet solutions to help clients drive incremental revenue and lower their cost of operations and customer support. My fascination with the Internet began with Usenet and WAIS in the late 1980s, prior to the evolution of the Web. However it wasn’t until the commercial use of the Internet became an acceptable practice that the light bulb really went off for me. In 1995, I formed FS3-Interactive to pursue Internet solutions as a fulltime business.

With FS3, we pioneered the use of a blueprinting technique to communicate everything from digital strategy and information architecture to competitive research and solutions design. We were also one of the first companies to adopt formal usability testing and user-centered design principles to help companies develop and launch intuitive Web sites and Internet applications that customers found easy-to-use and enjoyable. I’m also a strong proponent of providing solutions that are tightly integrated with a company’s broader marketing platform.

Through FS3-Interactive, Rare Medium, Andara Digital, and now RAZOR, I've worked with large brands on a wide range of initiatives. Some of the companies that have hired us to help them with their online initiatives include: JCPenney, Pizza Hut, CenturyTel, FedEx Kinko’s, CompUSA, Domino's Pizza, Brinker International, The Federal Reserve Bank, Rent-A-Center, AMC, CompuCom, AT&T, Horizon Digital Finance, and National Semiconductor.

John South’s Specialties:

Business building, Internet strategy, user-centered Web development, usability engineering, search marketing, E-Mail marketing, multi-channel marketing integration, client services, venture incubation, and mergers & acquisitions
