Erica Motley’s Summary

Erica Motley was formerly the Vice President of International Acquisitions at United International Pictures (the international joint venture between Paramount, DreamWorks, & Universal Studios). While there, she was an integral part of the UIP worldwide distribution team. In this role, Erica evaluated scripts, acquired films, and provided financial projections for each country’s manager. She attended local and international film markets as well as managed relationships with sales agents, producers, writers and directors. Acquisitions included: ELITE SQUAD, TORRENTE 3, STEP UP, VINICIUS, and OLD BOY.

After two years on Wall Street and a Harvard MBA, Erica began her film career as an international acquisition executive at HBO. She later joined Fox where she launched channels and negotiated deals with film studios, international sales agents and independent producers. As VP of Programming at Bertelsmann, Erica structured studio output deals, managed original productions and developed programming strategies for German Pay-TV. She later acquired content from emerging broadband companies at IFILM. Erica was the head of the film group for Shine Entertainment. While at Shine, Erica wrote the business plan and raised funding from the 3I venture capital partners.

Erica is currently a film consultant in New York where she packages projects for international producers and advises clients on digital distribution strategies.

Her clients include:
The American Film Company (THE CONSPIRATOR (Robert Redford), Web Stone)
Cine Mosaic (THE RELUDCTANT FUNDAMENTALIST (Mira Nair), Lydia Pilcher)
Six Sales (COLD SKIN, Denise O'dell)
Wildness Films (THE INITIATION,Cornelia Ravenal)
Alquimia Films (1954, Roberto Buso-Garcia)
Pure Grass / Endemol (Twist, Ben Grass)
SnagFilms (Digital Documentary Distribution)
Tribeca Film Festival (Digital Distribution)
Media Business School (Guest Lecturer - Spain)

Erica Motley’s Specialties:

Negotiating content deals; managing relationships with rights owners; creative and financial evaluation of films; international co-productions, film financing, content acquisitions.
