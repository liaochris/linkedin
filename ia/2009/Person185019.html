TKP Nair’s Summary

Have experience of about 32 years in various facets of Computer & Information Technologies in DRDO (Defence Research & Development Organisation) in so many capacities viz., programmer, system analyst, SW & system manager, hardware designer, HW maintenance engineer, Data Centre manager, Training-in-charge, Chairman of major IT-related facility-buildup committees and Dy. Director/Director of Computer, Communication & Info Technologies & Services, in different labs of DRDO of Govt. of India. The experience includes approx. 11 years at working level, 3 years in junior-level management, 11 years in middle level management and 7 years in senior level management positions.

Also functioned as DOE's (Dept. of Electronics, Govt. of India) accreditation expert for IT training, member Board of Studies for Computer Science curriculum at Osmania University, project guide for MCA (Master of Computer Applns.)/MTech (Master of Technology) for Central University, Osmania University, JNTU Hyderabad, Devi Ahalya Viswavidyalaya, Indore, member CSI Hyderabad Chapter management committee. Also a Fellow of IE (Institution of Engineers,India) & IETE(Institution of Electronic & Telecommunications, India), Member IEEE, Senior Member of CSI (Computer Society of India).

Had 4 years of experience as Professor & Dean of Information Technology at Engineering Staff College of India, the prestigeous staff training institution of the Institution of Engineers (India). Responsibility included education management and teaching computer engineering/applications for under-graduates.

  • Chairman, Computer Networking Committee

    Research Centre Imarat,DRDO, Ministry of Defence,Hyderabad

    (Government Agency; 1001-5000 employees; Computer Networking industry)

    March 1995 — August 1998 (3 years 6 months)

    Planning & design of a computer network which catered to the expectations of a demanding scientist/engineer community of users in the R&D environment of a laboratory engaged in weapons design. The users were geographically dispersed in a vast campus area. Each user premise had substantial computing resources. One requirement was to share them optimally. In addition there were realtime hardware-in-loop modelling hardware & software positioned at disparate locations which had to work together. Also the network should support collaborative engneering design. Another requirement was to facilitate videoconferencing. The design prepared was on ATM-cum-ethernet technologies over fibre optic media. It was subsequently implemented and is currently operational for the past few years. My role was as the leader of the team and my major personal contributions were in the design of the network and in negotiations with international vendors to arrive at a cost-effective impementation plan.
