  • Group Business Development Director

    October 2007 — Present (1 year 10 months)

    Responsible for the company’s M&A activities worldwide. Coordinate New Media business development, and developing new market entry strategies. Geographic focus is on Switzerland, Germany, Central and Eastern Europe, as well as Asia (with special emphasis on Vietnam and China). Report directly to the Executive Board.

  • Managing Director Hungary

    Ringier

    (Privately Held; 5001-10,000 employees; Publishing industry)

    January 2003 — October 2007 (4 years 10 months)

    Headed Hungary’s leading print media group: top-selling tabloid daily Blikk, sports daily Nemzeti Sport (including its Sunday edition), quality daily Népszabadság - over half a million combined sold copies daily with close to 2 million readers.
    Group also published the weekly women magazines Tina and Blikk Nők, the gossip weekly hot!, as well as sports and youth magazines.
    In 2005 greenfield investment in a cold set printing plant, and in 2006 we established a nation-wide subscriptions distribution company. Four major internet sites, book publishing and events marketing businesses. Ringier Group’s total sales in Hungary exceeded Euro 95 million in 2007. During my tenure revenues increased by 60% and profits trebled.

  • Business Press Division Head

    Sanoma

    (Public Company; Publishing industry)

    February 1999 — February 2003 (4 years 1 month)

    Established the Business Press Division representing end-2002 over USD 4 million in newsstand, subscription, advertising and internet revenues, making it one of Hungary’s leading B2B and B2C information providers.
    Oversaw relaunch of the Figyelö business weekly (Observer), leading to a doubling of circulation and a 300% increase in ad sales within 18 months, making it the country’s foremost business title. Part of the process was to establish a licensing agreement with Business Week. Launched related business portal site in November 2000. Re-positioned conference activities, generating a 30% ROS in the first full year of activities. Headed the acquisition team behind the purchase of the internet portal www.startlap.com; subsequently turning it into Hungary’s most frequented site.
    As a member of the Executive Board I also supervised the newsstand distribution and subscriptions departments

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; ABN; Banking industry)

    April 1996 — February 1999 (2 years 11 months)

Also developed and coordinated a regional MBA financing loan programme with Europe’s leading business schools INSEAD, IESE, LBS, Erasmus School of Rotterdam and the US-based University of Michigan
