Brad Nielsen’s Summary

Represented a developer in the acquisition of raw land in San Bernardino County, California and ultimate development and disposition of 7 separate "build-to-suit for sale" industrial buildings totaling approximately 4,000,000 square feet and over $225,000,000 in sales proceeds.

Represented (and currently representing) a national pension fund landlord in the leasing of over 5,000,000 square feet of industrial space in Southern California.

Represented (and currently representing) a landlord in the leasing of approximately 1,000,000 square feet of retail space located in Orange County, California, including the negotiation of "big-box" leases with major tenants.

Represented a seller in 6 separate transactions involving the disposition of over 2,000 residential lots to national home builders.

Represented national home builder in the preparation and negotiation of infrastructure improvement and cost sharing agreements.

Represented 2 separate national landlords in the leasing of entire multi-floor office buildings (comprising in excess of 300,000 square feet of office space) to national tenants.

Represented (and currently representing) numerous landlords in the leasing of approximately 10,000,000 square feet of office space located in California and Arizona.

Co-author of the article entitled "Miscellaneous Lease Provisions, What You Don't Know Can Hurt You", published in the Winter 2003 Edition of the California Real Property Journal, and author of the article entitled "Making Sure Your BTS Fits", published in the April, 2007 Edition of Real Estate Southern California.

Actively involved in the Urban Land Institute's Orange County Chapter and the National Association of Industrial and Office Properties Souther California Chapter. Also a member of the American Bar Association, the United States District Court (Central District of California), the State Bar of California and the Orange County Bar Association.

Brad Nielsen’s Specialties:

Real Estate Attorney, all areas of Real Estate Transactional Work (e.g., leasing, acquisitions, dispositions and all ancillary documentation)
