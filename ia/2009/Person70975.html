Eladio Castrodad’s Summary

Eladio Castrodad, PE is a registered engineer with over 12 years of experience in the design and management of major civil, transportation and structural engineering construction projects. His experience includes commercial and residential buildings; energy; medical and transportation facilities; convention centers; stadiums; as well as municipal projects in Florida and Puerto Rico. His transportation background includes provision of QA/QC management and structural engineering services for the $2.25 billion Tren Urbano Heavy Rail Mass Transit Project in San Juan, PR.

Mr. Castrodad's computer skills include; Adobe products, Visual-Analysis, MathCAD, FL-Pier, ADOSS, PCA-Col, PCA-Mats, PCA-Beam, PCA-Wall, ETABS, SAP, SAFE, RAM Steel, RISA-3D, RISA-Footing, RISA-Baseplate, EnerCalc, ADAPT-PT, PT-DATA, STAAD-PRO, Presto, AutoCAD, Primavera, MS Project, WDOT-Q-Com Bridge, DESCON, LEAP software products and other structural, civil engineering applications.

Eladio Castrodad’s Experience

  • Sr. Project Manager

    Hill International

    (Public Company; Hil; Construction industry)

    December 2008 — Present (10 months)

    Mr. Castrodad oversees all aspects of the day-to-day management of construction projects including monitoring and coordinating work performed by architectural, engineering and construction firms, institutional, state and local entities. His duties include review and interpretation of proposed designs, architectural drawings, and building specifications for appropriateness to required function and/or institutional standard; initiating revisions where appropriate; and advising consultants and clients on how to best achieve required results. Mr. Castrodad participates in the planning and formulation of design alternatives and solutions; reviews conceptual cost estimates, schedules, and feasibility studies; and coordinates the production of basic design plans and construction documents. He is responsible for establishing performance and delivery criteria, ensuring that client and institutional requirements are met; overseeing preparation of project specifications; and coordinating procurement. His duties include establishment of project objectives, policies, procedures, and performance standards; initiating and maintaining liaison with client and contractors to facilitate construction activities; monitoring construction through administrative direction of the on-site CMs to ensure projects are built on schedule and within budget; and managing financial aspects of contracts to protect the company's and client's interest.

  • President

    CP Engineering Consultants

    (Civil Engineering industry)

    January 2006 — November 2008 (2 years 11 months)

    As Project Manager, Chief Structural Engineer, and Principal in Charge, Mr. Castrodad oversaw design, consulting, inspection, construction management, and construction administration services for various clients in Florida, Puerto Rico and the United States. Scope of services included the design and design review of several high-rise and moderate-sized commercial and residential threshold type, reinforced concrete and steel buildings. His responsibilities included:

    -Design services; analysis and design of several high-rise type buildings under contract. Scope of services included design of; deep and shallow foundations, basement retaining walls, shear walls, wind load analysis (ASCE-7), P-Delta analysis, reinforced concrete slabs, post-tensioned concrete slabs, beams, columns, cantilevered retaining walls, moment frames, tilt-up, connections and other structural elements.

    -Construction Management services; review of cost estimates during all phases of design development through final construction documents, including provision of change order estimates during construction and development/analysis of CPM schedules.

    -Inspection services; direct supervision of all field special inspection personnel and assurance of proper implementation of construction threshold plan.

    -QAQC Consulting Services: QA review services as directed by FTA Region 4 Task Orders. His projects included the Charlotte Area Transit System (CATS) South Corridor Light Rail ($485 million) which included construction of a conventional electrified 9.6-mile light rail line extending from Uptown Charlotte Central Business District south to Interstate 485. In addition, provision of QA/QC consulting services to Lane Transit District in Eugene, Oregon, assisting in the development of LTD's BRT--Pioneer Parkway EmX Project QA/QC Plan, in compliance with the requirements of the Federal Transit Administration's (FTA) QA/QC Guidelines (FTA-IT-90-5001-002.1).
