Conrad Mohr’s Summary

Since starting IT Industries in 1999 myself and the team have led the way in delivering solutions to the IT and CE Channel.

With an annual T/O of £12M/15meur/$24M and offices in Europe, Asia and the USA we are able to deliver our services on a global basis.

Focusing solely on helping OEM's and the IT Channel reduce losses for their surplus products and mitigate channel conflict we have established many channels for all types of new and refurbished computer and consumer electronics.

We continue to strive for excellence and to exceed expectations and nothing gives us greater pleasure than proving ourselves time and time again, sometimes in the face of almost impossible odds and circumstances.

    Seltech (Holdings) Ltd

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Computer Hardware industry)

    1991 — 1999 (8 years)

    In charge of purchasing, new business generation, vendor management, sales of products into new markets.

    Business focus was in the purchase of large QTY of new and refurbished surplus computer and consumer electronics from Tier 1 vendors and OEMS in Europe, Asia and USA.

    Largest deal done was the purchase of the assets of Escom a German computer retailer that went bankrupt in the UK in the mid 90's. In total we purchased over 300 Containers of Stock for around £4 Million (inc 24 containers of just software) and this was re-sold within 90 days of receiving the stock.

    Other deals were the purchase of the entive inventories of Tandon a US PC Mfr for £2 Million and the purchase of the assets of the "Leading Edge" PC Brand that was launched by Daewoo in the US that also failed.

    Left in 1999 to start my own company IT Industries, www.it-industries.com that offers the same services to OEMS and Mfrs and the team has grown the business to £12 Million T/O with 10 people.
