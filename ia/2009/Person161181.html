Michael Gray’s Summary

2003-Current
Publisher - Rolling Hills Publishing; Ozark, Missouri
• Published and authored Auto Upkeep: Basic Car Care (Copyright © 2003) and co-authored Auto Upkeep: Basic Car Care, Maintenance, and Repair (Copyright © 2007) – over 50,000 copies in print.
• Created a homeschool curriculum kit for basic car care.
• Developed a wiki to help automotive teachers collaborate between the 300 high schools, community colleges, universities, and technology centers in the United States and Canada where Auto Upkeep has been adopted.

2004-2008
Assistant Supervisor of Career and Technology Education – Carroll County Public Schools; Westminster, Maryland
• Supervised, observed, and evaluated 60 middle school, high school, and technology center teachers in Career and Technology Education (CTE) classes in 18 buildings over a geographical area of over 450 square miles.
• Worked cooperatively with principals and assistant principals to procure equipment, evaluate teachers, and interview candidates for teacher openings.

2001-2003
Technology Curriculum Specialist - Illinois State University; Normal, Illinois
• Developed integrated math, science, and technology curriculum on a National Science Foundation funded project, co-authoring and revising ten integrated middle school textbooks on a math, science, technology team.

ACHIEVEMENTS
• Received the Technology Education Leadership Award (2006) by the Technology Education Association of Maryland.
• Successfully defended thesis (2002) entitled Identifying Effective Recruitment Techniques and Factors that Influence Students to Enroll in Midwest Undergraduate Technology Education Programs.
