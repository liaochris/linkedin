Clovice Lewis’s Summary

Currently CEO of my 3rd startup, Advanced Housing Technologies, LLC. I was previously the founder and CEO of the Tax Sales Center, LLC and Technology Media Enterprises.

I have had an extremely varied career in the fields of electronics, computer programming, education, technical documentation, training, and music composition. My experience is quite eclectic, but all focused around a finely honed creative impulse that has allowed me to explore a wide variety of fields, making innovative contributions along the way.

From 1979-1984 I served as an Associate Professor of Computer Music and Theory at the College of Creative Studies, UC Santa Barbara. In 1984 I started Technology Media Enterprises (TME). TME is an Apple Developer-Certified firm offering a wide variety of multimedia services to companies in the Silicon Valley. I led TME through a $10 million contract with Lockheed Integrated Solutions Company to provide the Veterans Administration with custom software for an office automation program known as NOAVA in the early 1990's. During that period I also invented the world's first computer-based interactive training program for private pilots called "Wilbur's Flight School", that was marketed by Azure Technology.

In the early 2000's I was the Vice President and database designer of an internet company called Internet Certification Technologies (ICT), which offered internet-based customer recommendations of company services nationwide.

In 2003, I founded the Tax Sales Center, LLC (TSC), which provided internet-based databases of all tax sales properties in the United States. Activities in ICT and TSC ceased when I formed Advanced Housing Technologies after Hurricane Katrina inspired me to create a total solution for disaster relief in late 2005. The system incorporates innovative small GREEN, transportable, energy self-sufficient disaster relief housing with transportable utilities that allows communities to operate off-the-grid for extended periods.
