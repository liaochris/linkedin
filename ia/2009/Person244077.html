  • Production Technician & Programming

    (Public Company; Broadcast Media industry)

    January 1985 — June 1994 (9 years 6 months)

    Television/News Production

    As a production technician, we had many various duties. My responsibilities included, but not limited to; everything from teleprompter/floor directing, running studio cameras to technical directing, computer graphics (old Texta computer graphic system), and audio for live newscasts, worked commercial shoots and other recorded and live shows we had at the time. I had to keep track of times (to the second), content and keeping everyone informed of script/cam changes. I am great at organizing and have a great eye for details. I also have a knack for knowing what looks good and is attractive to the public.
    I helped create the on-air "character generated" on-air look and helped write scripts from time to time (helping the producers during time crunches). Stepped in when we lost our program editor and made sure all programs were in house and out (in order) ready for air.

  • Program/Film Director (temp)

    WTVZ-33

    (Marketing and Advertising industry)

    September 1985 — November 1985 (3 months)

    I was called upon by their operations director after they officially went on-air to help them get new station off the ground by editing all programming, making them ready to air 4 weeks ahead of scheduled air dates, while training new staff on how to take over. Teaching proper editing and use of the equipment needed to do so until they were ready to go on their own. Unfortunately, the person they had lined up for the position had to leave suddenly, leaving them little options.

  • Film/Programming Director

    WYAH-TV

    (Marketing and Advertising industry)

    January 1977 — January 1985 (8 years 1 month)

    As film director/programming director (secular programming), I was in charge of several editors in my department. My responsibilities included, but were not limited to; suggesting new programming our viewers would enjoy, training quality editors on editing 16mm film. We prided ourselves on never interrupting the flow of content within the programs. I cut shows not only for time but also content (yes, back when some shows were still being censored). I dealt with various production companies and syndication houses in LA and NYC, scheduling shows and keeping track on contractual runs while making sure nothing in the contract was breached (credits weren't altered etc), made sure all programming was out and ready, in order, for master control to run. Oversaw the shipping department while making sure all programming was in as well as sent out as scheduled. Kept meticulous records of all secular programming, detailing times ran, times remaining, length etc and reported all scheduling to the appropriate syndicators.

  • Film Editor

    WAVY-TV

    (Public Company; Broadcast Media industry)

    January 1976 — January 1977 (1 year 1 month)

    I was responsible for, but not limited to the editing and positioning of commercial breaks in all 16 mm film syndicated programming making sure all cuts were clean and all edits unnoticeable. I was responsible for making sure all programming was out and in order of air for master control. Watched daily network news feeds while keeping detailed notes of exact times each story came up on tape, subject matter and length for the news dept. During my spare time I would hang around the newsroom absorbing as much as I could on how things operated. Modeled part time and had the privilege of being around a crew of THE most creative people I ever met when I appeared in one of the local "spoof shows" that aired weekly.
