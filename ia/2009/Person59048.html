David Van Assche’s Experience

  • Oasis PC

    (Computer & Network Security industry)

    August 2008 — Present (7 months)

    A small business created to expand and spread the wonders of thin client technology and sub notebook computer access across schools and educational institutions in Europe. By reusing old hardware, and focusing on a centralised mainframe type system with dedicated learning software and interfaces, the institutions save both time and money. We currently work with 2 schools in Southern Spain but hope to expand the ideas throughout Europe soon enough.

  • OLE Nepal

    (E-Learning industry)

    July 2008 — August 2008 (2 months)

    This was an exciting opportunity to find out how one of the poorest nations on the planet copes with growing technology and Education demands. Clearly the disparity between the rich and poor are quite prominent in developing nations, and from what I experienced, helping all children join the information technology highway is great way to gap the divide. I worked on networking, server side, and collaboration technologies, including the XS server, ejabberd, squid, load balancing, and dansguardian.

  • Mayfair Academy

    (Education Management industry)

    September 2006 — June 2008 (1 year 10 months)

    My main objectives were to build a system that was completely Microsoft free so that licensing issues and costs would be kept to the bare minimum while students and teachers could enjoy the growing power and popularity of open source technology. By building a thin client based system which allows for centralized control of all applications, students, security and files, it made the school IT system both manageable and extremely efficient. The added bonus is that because students are now using open source tools, they will have a far better advantage to their peers who won't be learning about anything but Microsoft until they hit University.

    I taught at this institution from November through June, trying to tie in as much of the open source world as possible. The new teacher has done a great job of continuing this tradition and keeping the pupils of this school on a level far above that of any other in the area, especially in the field of IT.

  • Q Logic

    (Computer Software industry)

    January 2000 — March 2005 (5 years 3 months)

    During the web applications and web development in general boom, I managed this company and was primarily responsible for the sales side, in which I consulted with a team of technicians and programmers and came up with solutions that best fitted the needs of our customer base. The majority of our projects were web design or web programming based applications, but in most cases we were doing web 2.0 type apps before that was a known acronym for linked and interactive websites. We focused quite extensively in the real estate and project management/content management area, building our projects with as much versatility as possible. By using open source tools like mysql, postgres, php and apache, we were able to keep licensing costs down to nothing while focusing on quality products that continue to be used today.

    I also focused quite heavily on the search engine positioning and e-marketing of respective clients as this was one area that was mostly ignored during that time period.
