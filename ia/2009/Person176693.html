Davin Hutchins’s Summary

Davin Hutchins is a filmmaker, producer and entrepreneur specializing in news, documentaries and new media with a emphasis on international social issues and online delivery platforms. He also runs the NomadsLand Network, a worldwide network of social issue filmmakers with more than 750 members. (www.nomadsland.com)

Hutchins has fourteen years experience in television journalism and has worked with CNN International, BBC, Headline News, TechTV and Huffington Post. His producing and journalism assignments have taken him to Egypt, Sudan, United Arab Emirates, Israel, Palestine, Lebanon, Turkey, Kuwait, Jordan, Oman, Kenya, Tanzania, South Africa, Iceland, France, Britain, Denmark, the Netherlands, Italy and the United States.

He is Managing Producer at American News Project (www.americannewsproject.com) in Washington, D.C. He produced investigative, muckraking video reports for outlets like the Huffington Post, Al Jazeera English, Talking Points Memo, The Real News and other independent media. His ANP reports have tallied more than two million views. The project is funded by the Schumann Center of Media and Democracy (Bill Moyers, President) and the Tides Center.

Among career highlights, Hutchins directed The Art Of Flight, his debut feature documentary which featured at AFI Fest Hollywood, International Documentary Festival Amsterdam and Bangkok International Film Festival. He also trained Palestinian journalists in the West Bank in news production and new media during 2007-2008. In 1994, Hutchins began his career at CNN in Atlanta, Georgia, U.S.A, where he helped produce newscasts, “Generation X” news pilots and a retrospective on human rights with Peter Gabriel and Jimmy Carter.

Hutchins holds a Masters of Arts Degree in Middle East Studies from the American University in Cairo and two Bachelors of Arts Degrees in Journalism and History from California State University: Fresno.
