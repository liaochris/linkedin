Alita Holly’s Summary

Entertainment industry digital media executive, with practical foundation in creative marketing & production, for traditional and interactive media, with proven success building creative brand value & dramatically increasing revenues.

I am currently consulting with several companies and non-profits. Before that, I was the VP of Production & Creative Development at Alacre, a company providing content development, management, post-production & platform solutions to leading entertainment brands, including Consumer Reports, LimeLife, VOCE, TV Guide, Disney Mobile & iPlay. Before Alacre, I was the Executive Producer at Blink Digital, an Ascent Media Company, where I oversaw the creative production & all new business development of their DVD menu and special features production business. I began working in DVD in 1998, as the Senior DVD Consultant for Columbia TriStar Home Entertainment, where I was a major force in the creative production of special edition DVDs.

In 2000, under the umbrella of Organa West, I continued to produce major special editions for Columbia, New Line, MGM, LionsGate and Miramax, including Columbia TriStar's first world-wide release of a DVD title, Men in Black. Other titles included New Line's first infinifilm™ release, Thirteen Days, as well as Swingers, Rush Hour 2 and Goldmember, establishing Organa West as one of the most innovative independent DVD production companies. I have directed and produced various featurettes and documentaries. My credits are available at: http://www.imdb.com/name/nm1109494/

I began my career by putting myself through Columbia University, selling the Criterion Collection, as their first sales representative. Following my graduation with a degree in English & Comparative Literature, I became the Dir. of International Sales and Marketing for Voyager, Criterion's holding company. There I headed all aspects of international marketing, as well as online content and localized CD-ROM production.
