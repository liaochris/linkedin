Beth Robinson’s Summary

As a formulator of moisture-cure adhesives, I collaborate with manufacturing and marketing to commercialize and support a range of products. This position followed directly from the degrees in Materials Science and Engineering I earned eight years ago. I am looking to move out of the lab and into product management as I complete my MBA through Arizona State University's online program.

Outside of the office I blog about thinking towards the whole at Inventing Elephants and I create mixed-media art.

Full name: Elizabeth Jean Robinson nee Oborn

Beth Robinson’s Experience

  • Chemist

    Bostik

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; Chemicals industry)

    February 2001 — Present (8 years 2 months)

    Formulator - I develop the chemical compositions for new products and alter existing products. I make goop in the lab, test it, and see it through to the manufacturing stage. My technical expertise is in moisture-cure polyurethanes and silylated polymers.

    Process Analyst - I spend time in our three manufacturing plants, as needed, to scale-up products from the lab, figure out how to improve production from a chemical point of view, and investigate quality concerns.

    Technical Service - I work with our technical service representatives to resolve quality concerns and occasionally visit customers.

    Project Leader - I've helped guide one product through to a successful commercialization in a "gate" system begun in 2004 and was also the project leader for two more.

    Safety Committee Member - For five years I helped keep the processes and inspections up to date.

    Received General Manager's Award for Technical Excellence in 2003, 2004, and 2006.
