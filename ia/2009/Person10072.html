Alex Mavro’s Summary

During my 30-year business career in the Service Sector, I covered a wide range of activities -- Project management; training; enterprise strategy and risk assessment; operations; marketing.

For the past ten years I have been working in the sustainable development field, and for the last three years, have been focusing more and more on CSR as a process that leads to sustainability, a goal that the world increasingly demands of the corporate sector...

I publish regularly on different aspects of my journey, and often lead seminars when the theme relates to CSR, sustainability, or the Sufficiency Economy Philosophy.

Alex Mavro’s Specialties:

Helping companies re-connect to their CSR souls... employee volunteer programs; Socal marketing; socially responsible business practices; cause marketing; cause-related marketing; philanthropy; CSR benchmarking; and measurement tools. Above all -- to make these activities successful -- brainstorming and change management.

  • Marketing Director & Partner

    DHL (Thailand) Co., Ltd

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Transportation/Trucking/Railroad industry)

    1980 — 1990 (10 years)

    Stan Griffin set up the DHL office in Thailand in 1972. It was only the fourth location for DHL, which today has thousands. He invited me to join his venture in the mid-70s but I was committed elsewhere and couldn't join until 1980. Stan, his wife, Wei, and Dave Daly became steadfast business partners and lifelong friends as a result of this venture.

    Today, there's no need to explain what DHL does. But when we started, it was a hard sell: nobody wanted to believe that a private company could outdo the post office. And since we dealt only in documents in those days, it was the post office we were competing with.

  • Operations Manager

    MPA Ltd. (Sole Proprietorship)

    (Sole Proprietorship; 201-500 employees; Security and Investigations industry)

    1976 — 1980 (4 years)

    MPA Ltd was Thailand's very first professional security protection service, opening as a branch of the original Korean-based operation in the early 1960s. I worked sales, marketing, training, administration, and bottle-washing, all under the rubric of "Operations Manager." We had nearly 400 guards covering some 60 locations. I departed when the owner, Carl Menge, sold the company.

  • Captain

    United States Air Force

    (Government Agency; 10,001 or more employees; Military industry)

    1969 — 1976 (7 years)

    Commissioned in 1969, I went off to training before being assigned (and returned) to Thailand in Dec 1971. I worked in various mufti liaison roles in Bangkok, Udorn, Nakhorn Phanom, and Ubol before separating in place (i.e., leaving the service without returning to the United States) in 1976.

    Even though (or because?) the AF paid for my schooling, my eyes went bad over the four years and I could not qualify for the flight training I was committed to. So in the end, I did learn how to fly while in the AF, but at my own expense.

    I also became a certified SCUBA instructor in YMCA/NAUI/PADI/CMAS.

  • Assistant Commissary Manager

    American Community Support Association

    (Government Agency; Government Administration industry)

    1964 — 1965 (1 year)

    In Vientiane, Laos, fresh out of High School. I reported to the ACSA manager -- ACSA was responsible for all the support and TLC needed by the official American community in Laos, which was sizable. I oversaw stock control, shelving, maintenance, HR, and accounting, learning as I went.
