Larry Dunville’s Summary

I'm the second generation owner of an Overhead Bridge Cranes manufacturing company. While in high school and college (BS Finance, Univ. of Notre Dame) I spent summers working in our shop learning general steel fabrication and with our field installation crews, working as an Ironworker apprentice.

I joined Dearborn full-time after graduation in 1975. My initial duties were sales, marketing and finance. During the middle 80's I attended the Harvard Graduate School of Business.

In the late 80’s, I was increasingly responsible for the management and direction of the company. I became convinced that we had to change the direction of the company from job shop fabricator to a specialist. The question was specializing in what?

After evaluating our available resources; our experience, employee skill set, supplier capabilities and customer base, we determined that overhead cranes were our strongest suit. Although we had been building Overhead Cranes since 1947, we were now going to do nothing but Overhead Cranes. We spent the next three years restructuring the company, our people, our vendor network and customer base, to become a world class crane builder.

During the 90’s and early 2000’s, we wrote our own proposal estimating program that reduced the time to produce a proposal from 24 man hours to less than 30 minutes. We also made investments in shop equipment that made us one of the lowest cost producers of box girder cranes in the world.

By 2006, we had firmly established ourselves as one of the largest crane builders in the Great Lakes region. Due to the de-industrialization of the “Rust Belt”, we were faced with the challenge of how to economically compete outside of this region. In November of 2006, we made the transition from conventional field sales reps to a 100% web sales business model.

We now have five websites and are ranked number one on Google for our primary product lines and experienced 40% growth nationwide in 2008.
