Steven Ericsson-Zenith’s Summary

In 2002, after many years in industry, I returned to research and development, to science. I continue to apply the results of that effort to problems I know well in high technology.

I am asked exactly what I mean when my LinkedIn status reads "is changing everything, for everyone." Obviously, you should treat any such claim with skepticism. In my case it is a simple recognition that if we achieve our stated goals at IASE everything will indeed change, for everyone. No one will be untouched by it.

This is a remarkable claim. How can it possibly be the case? A constructive explanation of complexity and of experience in nature will have an impact upon us as great as the Copernican revelation. Long held beliefs about how we come to be here, in science and philosophy, will be unsustainable. It will change how all of us see ourselves in the world.

Further, our work promises a new foundation to logic that will enable both an explanation of what is happening in biology and the means to engineer motile machines that experience.

We challenge a number of current expectations in technology development. For example, according to our models the fundamental problems of recognition cannot be solved using software and silicon for reasons that are fundamental to the world. The Singularity is not near.

IASE is a 501(c)3 non-profit science organization with California charity status. I am fundraising and recruiting for this defining commitment.

The scope of our work is broad including physical engineering, systems operation and high order systems-of-systems behavior (as seen in our work on reasoning about behavior in social networks).

The IASE institutional goal is to establish a world-class international institute of scholarship, science and engineering focused on our mission.

IASE undertakes a few select external projects that benefit from our expertise and promise to provide some new empirical insight in our problem space.
