Marc Rubin’s Experience

  • Mentor Volunteer

    Connecticut Career Choices (CCC) program

    (Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    January 2009 — Present (6 months)

    CCC is an exciting technology-focused education and workforce initiative that provides a context and a resource for advancing Science, Technology, Engineering and Mathematic (STEM) skills to enable students to pursue post-secondary education seamlessly in technology fields; while emerging with the skills necessary to work effectively in the 21st century. The Governor's High School Innovation Challenge is its signature event.

    In my role as a volunteer mentor, I act as an advisor during working sessions and online with high school teachers and their students to provide specific experiential learning opportunities particularly in support of the Challenge for the E-Commerce and Entrepreneurship and IT R&D programs.

    The goals of each program are:

    IT R&D Innovation Challenge – to develop a computer-based learning game that teaches the concept of sustainable technologies and environmental energy concerns as they develop mock companies and assume positions in each company to complete the Challenge; and

    E-Business Challenge – to develop an innovative for-profit or not-for-profit e-business that is related to sustainable energy and/or environmental responsibility and is capable of receiving Internet-based electronic payment for its product or service.
