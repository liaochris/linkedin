Betsey Dalbeck’s Summary

As a management consultant and executive coach, I guide CEOs, business owners, and executives to innovate, achieve measurable business results, and create positive change for themselves, their teams and their organizations.

I started Fresh Tracks, Inc. in 1999 after rising into the senior executives ranks in advertising. Over the last 10 years, my business expanded beyond my direct work with Fresh Tracks clients, and now includes strategic partnerships with other consulting firms needing to expand their capabilities.

By combining 20+ years of top-tier branding and change agent expertise with world class wilderness leadership training, I bring intense curiosity, fresh ideas and clear understanding of human dynamics to clients across a range of industries. I help these business leaders become masters at swiftly navigating change management, strategy, organization and performance improvement initiatives.

My industry experience currently spans consumer products, defense, education, entertainment, financial services, food service, government, health care, high tech, manufacturing, marketing communications, recruitment, special events and nonprofit organizations.

Over my career, I've gained extensive real world leadership experience in strategic planning, marketing, operations, business development, recruiting, organizational development and change management. Consequently, I have the ability to dig deep and ask provocative questions that help clients draw connections between their past, present and envisioned future to set a clear course and immediately begin to move toward their vision of success. In fact, I'm delighted to not only share the insights I've picked up along the way, but also to receive praise for my work from a growing list of clients and colleagues.

Outside of work, I believe in linking my success to the communities in which I work and live through knowledge sharing, speaking engagements, board work and direct community service.

Betsey Dalbeck’s Experience

  • Owner/President

    Fresh Tracks, Inc., Sr. Consultant at Mage, LLC

    (Management Consulting industry)

    January 1999 — Present (11 years )

    Founded Fresh Tracks, Inc., a privately-owned Boston based management consulting and executive coaching firm, back in 1999 to offer strategic planning, change management, organizational planning and leadership development. Attracted and serviced national clients ranging from industry giants like Fidelity Investments; to entrepreneurial think-tanks like Babson College; to numerous start ups, family businesses and nonprofits. Coached a wide range of leaders from CEOs; to Army colonels; to business unit directors; to functional departments heads. Credited for quickly grasping the strategic big picture of companies and leaders in transition, lasering in on pain points, and offering common sense advice and strategies to steer everyone involved toward new horizons. Add value by guiding leaders to 1) define and clearly articulate an inspiring vision their team can embrace and understand, and 2) take the right action steps to swiftly achieve desired change that delivers better, sometimes "never thought possible" results -- all while flowing with an organization's work and a leader's workstyle. Achieved repeat or referral business from majority of clients. Achieved ten-year milestone marker for small businesses. Published author and speaker.

  • Senior Vice President, Director of Creative Resources

    Hill Holliday

    (Public Company; IPG; Marketing and Advertising industry)

    October 1995 — December 1998 (3 years 3 months)

    Fresh from NOLS/Alaska, I brought my wilderness expeditionary leadership skills and understanding of human dynamics and change management to one of the world's most respected brand communications firm and quickly perfected operations and streamlined firm's multi-disciplinary creative organization. Identified as mission critical in doubling new business including winning Dunkin Donuts, Budget Rent-A-Car, AutoNation USA and Wall Street Journal. Generated and implemented business development best practices. Working with one of the best creative leadership teams in the country, we pulled together to resurrect firm's creative reputation. Pushed mission into all areas of firm by bringing together mission-critical internal constituencies and energizing firm to commit to shared vision of producing a better product through cooperation and collaboration. Given the rapid results in my area, I was asked to act as an internal consultant to train other business unit executives how to lead change more effectively. At the end of 1998, I chose to take my emerging success as a consultant and coach, and share my knowledge and expertise with a wider audience by leaving the agency to found Fresh Tracks, Inc.

  • Senior Vice President, Director of Creative Administration

    J. Walter Thompson

    (Public Company; WPP; Marketing and Advertising industry)

    December 1988 — June 1995 (6 years 7 months)

    Rose to senior executive position at second largest regional office of one of the world's largest advertising and brand communications firms. Joined firm when they were seeking a strategic business mind with an eye for good copy to add to their creative management team. Office needed strong team to rebuild the office after experiencing major clients losses following British WPP Group's hostile takeover. I saw the opportunity as an intriguing challenge to learn from a true industry legend and master, while adding a higher level of customer focus and strategic insight to the role. It was here that I honed my natural talent for gently yet effectively influencing diverse, highly creative personalities and learned the art and science of leading change. First, I shepherded creative unit through profound upheaval following dismissal of my first boss. Then, I guided transition of new general manager and new executive creative director into organization. And finally, I created a smooth transition between departing executive creative director and internal successor. Each time, I assumed an instrumental role in migrating the firm toward implementing each new leader's mission. In fact, I was sought out as a trusted advisor by every wave of top leadership -- something exceptionally rare in the ad business -- and acknowledged as one of seven mission-critical people who helped agency make its numbers from 1992 - 1995. I was also credited with building a digital studio profit center from scratch and overhauling back end operations to increase revenue objectives. Given my contributions, I was one of the youngest SVPs ever elected at J. Walter Thompson. After years of figuratively trailblazing corporate terrain, I left the firm to head to Alaska to attend the National Outdoor Leadership School.

  • Account Supervisor

    HDM Dawson Johns & Black

Left when offered the opportunity to switch into a creative management role.

  • Account Executive

    DDB Needham

    (Marketing and Advertising industry)

    1986 — 1987 (1 year )

    Managed Chivas Regal blended scotch whiskey, the showcase brand at this legendary New York City creative ad agency. Saw this as a great opportunity to work side by side with the BEST industry talent at the time -- executive creative director John Noble, Jack Mariucci, Robert Mackall and Helmut Krone. It was at DDB that I experienced my first merger and problematic change management situation when the stylish, witty, urbane DDB merged with the midwestern, homespun, jiggle famous Needham Harper Worldwide to form DDB Needham Worldwide. Chose to return to Chicago after the different styles didn't mesh, DDB leaders left, and morale plummeted.

  • Assistant Account Executive

    Leo Burnett

    (Public Company; PUB; Marketing and Advertising industry)

    1983 — 1986 (3 years )

    Selected to join Agency's highly prestigious and competitive management training program. Participated in this training program which was like earning an MBA in marketing on the job. Started in marketing research studying the customers of McDonald's, Proctor & Gamble, Oldsmobile, Salada Tea and Samsonite. Moved into client service managing advertising projects on 9 Lives cat food. Identified early on as a strong judge of copy -- unlike many of my left-brained analytical peers. Left to experience a better balance of strategy and innovative creative.
