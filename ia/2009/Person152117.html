  • VP & CIO

    RepeatSeat Inc.

    (Public Company; 11-50 employees; RPS; Events Services industry)

    March 2002 — November 2002 (9 months)

    I was recruited to assist RepeatSeat in the revamping of their event ticketing application. The system prior to my joining the company was oursourced and the excessive costs of hosting and development was unsustainable.

    I assessed the existing software, customer engagements, and vision of the executive and proposed a plan to keep the company viable from a product perspective.

    Along with a couple of key hires, we were able to rapidly redevelop the product and move the company away from the outsource partner. This ensured the ongoing viability for the company.

  • Founder and Executive VP

    Suite Systems Inc.

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Telecommunications industry)

    April 1997 — March 2001 (4 years)

    Suite Systems Inc. was a wholly owned subsidary of Boardwalk Equities Inc. Our mandate was to provide converged Telephone, Cable TV, and Internet services to multi-dwelling units (MDU) buildings.

    We were early adopters of technologies and successfully developed systems to deliver our services. We were licensed by the CRTC (Canadian FCC equivelant) as a CLEC as well as a Class 1 BDU (Cable TV company).

    We pioneered IP multicast video distribution and had over 150 channels of licensed channels for our customers. My team also assembled the technologies that enabled us to be one of the first companies to provide telephone services trunking PSTN access over IP.

    Our parent company chose to shut down the company when the .com bust happened in 2001.
