Steve Curtis’s Summary

Steve is a successful entrepreneur with a passion for business and science. A talented manager and innovator, Steve started his first company at the age of 19 with a start up budget of $500 and with no external funding grew the firm to just under 5 Million of annual revenue in less than 5 years. Steve’s company started as a marketing firm, today exists as SMI, a diversified consumer products company with offices in Canada, and the US. The company’s primary focus is developing scientific innovations into mainstream consumer products through a number of subsidiary companies.

Choosing an alternative path, Steve gave up on structured education at the age of 14, but did not give up on learning. Steve completed the Canadian Securities Course and after a number of successful sales and marketing positions in his early career and prior to starting his own business, Steve chose to return to school, attending the Harvard Business School Negotiation Course. Steve, out of a group of 47 senior global executives, achieved top of the class. Later Steve attended the MIT/Sloan Entrepreneurship program and the prestigious 3 year Harvard Business School Owner President Manager Program.

Steve is a fan of lifelong learning and commits each year to reading between 25 and 50 books on a diverse range of topics.

In 2006, Steve became the youngest winner of of the Business in Vancouver, Top 40 Under 40 Award.
