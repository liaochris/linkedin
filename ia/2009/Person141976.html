Lisa Emrich’s Summary

Currently a contributor for The Health Central Network's Multiple Sclerosis Central, I write weekly articles which focus on aspects of living with an incurable, chronic disease from the personal viewpoint of a person living with multiple sclerosis. As a patient expert, I also answer questions posed by community members and respond to Shareposts throughout the community.

Blogging at Brass and Ivory, I discuss life with autoimmune disease, current events, health policy, insurance coverage, the pharmaceutical industry, and health reform. I also actively welcome new members to the MS Blogging Community, provide support to newly-diagnosed patients, and learn from those patients who have gone before me.

Developed as an adjunct feature of the Brass and Ivory blog, I founded the Carnival of MS Bloggers, "a bi-weekly compendium of thoughts and experiences shared by those living with multiple sclerosis." The best blog posts throughout the community are featured in the Carnival, often surrounding a single topic. Our community continues to grow and provide support for MSers worldwide.

I am open to freelance writing opportunities.

With over 20 years of performing and teaching experience, I cater to developing talents of young musicians. Specialties include classical piano, french horn, and natural horn (both baroque period and classical period). You can find me in the Washington, D.C., area performing with the orchestra of the National Gallery of Art and various contract orchestras in the region.
