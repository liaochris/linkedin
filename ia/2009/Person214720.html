Shailesh Kumar’s Summary

Over a decade of experience in applying and innovating machine learning, statistical pattern recognition, and data mining algorithms to hard prediction problems in a wide variety of domains including: (i) remote sensing, (ii) text mining, (iii) bio-informatics, (iv) computer vision and image understanding, (v) transaction data, (vi) retail data, (vii) neurological data, (viii) risk analytics in financial domain,and (ix) web analytics.

Shailesh Kumar’s Experience

  • Sr. Scientist, Y! Labs, Bangalore

    Yahoo Inc.

    (Public Company; Research industry)

    July 2008 — Present (1 year 4 months)

    Lead the Multi-Media Sciences and the Information Retrieval Sciences groups at Yahoo! Labs in Bangalore. We are engaged in pushing the frontiers of text understanding, image understanding, and information retrieval for enabling the best match between user intent with web content and building better search engines - web, image, and video.

  • Analytic Science-Principal Scientist

    Fair Isaac Corporation

    (Public Company; FIC; Computer Software industry)

    September 2007 — June 2008 (10 months)

    Computer Vision: Build a biologically inspired, hierarchical, bottom-up, unsupervised framework for extracting semantically rich features from images and using these features for improved image retrieval, image clustering, and image classification systems.

    Text Analytics: Developed language independent, statistical methods for mining text data both syntactically (scalable phrase discovery, automatic grammar discovery) and semantically (discovering semantic families of words, disambiguating polysemous words in context)

    Retail Data Mining: Developed a novel framework for mining large volumes of retail transaction data to discover actionable insights both through a new paradigm for market basket and purchase sequence analysis as well as building recommendation engines.

    Mining Neural Spikes Data: Developed novel ways of finding "neural cliques" representing memory n-grams from neurological data.

  • Analytic Science Director

    Fair Isaac Corporation (Formerly HNC Software)

    (Research industry)

    September 2005 — August 2007 (2 years)

    Visualization: Developed scalable methods for visualizing "financial portfolios" spanning across a number of "scores" to capture both global trends and local anomalies.

    Text Analytics: Developed SVM based classifiers for large scale text classification problems involving call center notes data and e-mails.

    Retail Analytics: Developed a "Product Space Browser" and a "Purchase Sequence Browser" - platforms for understanding and exploring insights discovered by our novel retail data mining algorithms for large volumes of retail data.

    Retail Analytics: Successfully delivered retail data mining solutions including market basket analysis, purchase sequence analysis, recommendation engines, layout optimization, and ad-campaign optimization solutions to more than a dozen large retailers world wide including electronic retailers, grocery retailers, and consumer packaged goods companies.

  • Analytic Science Senior Scientist

    HNC Software (acquired by Fair Isaac Corporation)

    (Research industry)

    September 2000 — August 2006 (6 years)

    BioInformatics: Implemented a number of algorithms for finding "gene probes" for various pathogens using Hidden Markov Models and Phylogenetic Trees.

    Expert Systems: Built a system that combines expert knowledge coded as rule-trees with noisy data in a robust/Bayesian framework to reduce data noise from expert rules and reduce expert bias from data.

    Modeling Model Uncertainty: Developed algorithms to identify "low confidence" areas in the feature space for a given training data and a model. The confidence estimate combines uncertainty due to missing variables, lack of training data, or inability of the model to find the right decision boundary in various parts of the input space.
