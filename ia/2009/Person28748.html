Bill Simpson’s Summary

A brewing technologist with a background spanning Industry, R&D and Consultancy. Experience of leading multi-disciplinary teams and providing vision within commercial FMCG organizations. Worked in more than 200 medium to large breweries in 70 countries. Author or co-author of more than 100 scientific papers, book chapters and patent specifications. Effective communicator with well-developed training and education skills.

Bill Simpson’s Experience

  • Director

    Cara Technology ApS

    (Food & Beverages industry)

    April 2009 — Present (5 months)

    Cara Technology ApS incorporates the longest established pure yeast supply business in the world - the Alfred Jørgensen Laboratory. Located in Copenhagen, Denmark, the ISO9001:2000-accredited operation houses a collection of more than 700 brewery yeasts and supplies breweries throughout the world with production yeast cultures.

  • Founder and Non-Executive Chairman

    FlavorActiV Limited

    (Privately Held; Food & Beverages industry)

    December 1995 — Present (13 years 9 months)

    FlavorActiV Limited is a UK-based firm that provides specialists products and services to help manage the performance of professional beer tasters. The company has customers in more than 170 countries. In 2005 and 2007 FlavorActiV won the UK's most prestigious business award, the Queen’s Award for Export Achievement, in recognition of its success. Since the company was founded I have been responsible for the design and implementation of its market and business strategies and contributed to the design of many new products and services.

  • Executive Director

    Cara Technology Limited

    (Privately Held; Food & Beverages industry)

    August 1995 — Present (14 years 1 month)

    Cara Technology Limited is a UK-based specialist provider of problem management products and services to the brewing industry. The company services customers in more than 70 countries, providing yeast management services, beer quality management services, problem solving tools, and training services. Strategic management of the company. Provision of technical consultancy to the brewing industry and related fields. This includes strategic and tactical advice, technical briefs, process improvement and troubleshooting, and specialist training. Management of strategic partnerships. Clients include major beverage producers in most of the economically important regions of the world.

  • Visiting Lecturer in Yeast Technology

    Siebel Institute of Technology

    (Food & Beverages industry)

    October 1995 — March 1999 (3 years 6 months)

    Teaching on the Institute's prestigeous Diploma in Brewing course, and Advanced Brewing Microbiology course. I spent a month a year each year as a member of the visiting faculty. Topics included ester control, control of volatile sulphur compounds, fermentation management, yeast flocculation, detection of beer microbiological contaminants, and rapid microbiological detection techniques. At the time, the Siebel Institute was the largest seat of learning in brewing technolgy, training more than 400 brewers each year,

  • Visiting Lecturer in Biotechnology

    University of Strathclyde

    (Educational Institution; 1001-5000 employees; Higher Education industry)

    September 1994 — January 1999 (4 years 5 months)

    Responsible for teaching classes related to beer and beer production in the MSc in Industrial Biotechnology course. Also lectured on Rapid Microbiological Detection and Identification techniques.

  • Head of Fermentation Research and Principal Scientist

    Brewing Research Foundation

    (Food & Beverages industry)

    January 1995 — July 1995 (7 months)

    Responsible for a multi-disciplinary team of 15 graduate and post-doctoral staff, researching various aspects of fermentation microbiology relating to production of beer. Staff development, team building, and promotion of communication with clients were key tasks in this role. Management of consultancy projects of a confidential nature. Provision of specialist consultancy and advice. Liaison with clients to find cost-effective solutions to their technical problems.

  • Head of Product Quality Research

    Brewing Research Foundation

    (Food & Beverages industry)

    June 1991 — January 1995 (3 years 8 months)

    Responsible for ten graduate and post-doctoral staff and for negotiation of applications for public funding. Provision of consultancy services to members. Research areas included methodologies for assessment of beer flavour, control of beer flavour stability (both with respect to oxidation and light) and beer foam quality. Under my direction, the Product Quality Research Team achieved a four-fold increase in the number of papers annually published in peer-reviewed journals. Revenue generated from private research increased six-fold.

  • Research Scientist, Fermentation Research Team

    Brewing Research Foundation

    (Food & Beverages industry)

    July 1986 — June 1991 (5 years)

    Research in a variety of areas including yeast physiology, physiology of contaminant bacteria, traditional and rapid detection of contaminant microorganisms, antibacterial action of natural plant constituents. Undertook a variety of confidential consultancy projects, mostly of a problem-solving nature. Developed and patented reagent systems for rapid detection of microorganisms. Involved in bringing these reagents to the marketplace. Obtained a PhD studying part-time.

  • Quality Assurance Microbiologist

    Tennent Caledonian Breweries

    (Public Company; INB; Food & Beverages industry)

    July 1977 — July 1986 (9 years 1 month)

    Responsible for routine microbiological tests, maintenance of culture collections, troubleshooting and special projects in one of the largest, and most successful, breweries in the UK. Qualified as a microbiologist while studying on a part-time basis in this position.
