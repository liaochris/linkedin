Tish Shute’s Summary

My career in new media and technology began with innovation in motion control, robotics, and special effects for film, television, theme parks and aerospace. I continue my interest in innovation and paradigm shifts as an entrepreneur and writer interested in sustainable living, the real time internet, social networking, ubiquitous computing, augmented reality, and virtual realities.
I have an M.Phil., "Culture and Media," from NYU, Dept. of Anthropology, where I pursued my interest in the uptake of new technology from a more academic POV.

connecting people, project design and development, and new media

  • President

    Chocolate Money Productions, Inc,

    (Privately Held; Myself Only; Media Production industry)

    April 1998 — December 2006 (8 years 9 months)

    Chocolate Money Productions, Inc, New York City, New York. – a company dedicated to producing and promoting experimental ethnographic film making.

    I co-produced and edited, "From the Dreaming: Australian Aboriginal Art Arrives in New York " (58 mins) 1999, and, “Pulo du Gato,” a film on the life and work of the great Brazilian Capoeira Master, Mestre Joao Grande.

    I also directed a short vignette of capoeira with Mestre Joao Grande’s ensemble for a street scene in The Interpreter, 2005, directed by Sidney Pollack.
    My work as an independent producer has been shown on PBS, Canadian TV, and at the Australian, Berlin and Athens International Film and Video Festivals (“News World Order,” and “The Perfect Victim”).
