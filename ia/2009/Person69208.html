Walter McLeod’s Summary

For nearly 20 years I have successfully negotiated complex energy and environmental policy decisions involving stakeholders from the public and private sectors. I have a broad understanding and knowledge of several key industry groups, including energy, agriculture and offshore resource management.

During my tenure running a successful nonprofit organization, I gained valuable insight and understanding of how companies can create and leverage "environmental stewardship" to achieve public policy goals.

Walter McLeod’s Specialties:

clean technology, renewable energy, climate change, government affairs, coaching, strategic analysis, coalition building, ecolabelling, public policy

Walter McLeod’s Experience

  • Principal

    eCocapitol, LLC

    (Environmental Services industry)

    - Monitoring and strategic analysis of U.S. energy and climate change policy (state, federal and congressional)

    - Technical analysis of climate change markets, including GHG credits, RECs, offsets, and banking.

    - Green coaching, training and mentoring (corporations, HNW individuals, and sovereign states).

  • Founder

    Clean Beaches Council

    (Public Policy industry)

    January 2000 — May 2008 (8 years 5 months)

    - Testified before the United States Congress and successfully negotiated passage of House and Senate Resolutions (H.Res 186 and S.Res 243) by unanimous consent under the 110th U.S. Congress.

    - Lobbied White House officials, governors, mayors and county commissioners to issue over 150 proclamations and resolutions supporting beaches legislation.

    - Negotiated memorandums of understanding between CBC and UNEP RONA regarding World Environment Day and UN Decade for Education for Sustainable Development activities.

    - Served as national media spokesperson, including interviews and appearances on major news outlets (e.g., CNN, Today Show, New York Times, USA Today, National Public Radio).

  • Director, Business Development Environmental Management Strategies

    Science Applications International Corporation

    (Environmental Services industry)

    January 1999 — December 1999 (1 year)

    - Led business development and marketing efforts to secure emissions reporting contracts with major petroleum and chemical manufacturing companies.

    - Developed and led emissions inventory training seminars and workshops for Fortune 500 clients including Chevron, BP, ConocoPhillips, GE, FMC, DuPont, Tosco, MarathonAshland, and Sunoco.

    - Provided ongoing environmental compliance support and strategic analysis to assist client interactions with federal, state and local regulators.

  • Senior Regulatory Analyst

    American Petroleum Institute

    (Oil & Energy industry)

    January 1994 — July 1999 (5 years 7 months)

    - Led petroleum industry participation in the High Production Volume (HPV) Chemical Testing Program. Negotiated White House recognition of the petroleum industry, including participation in the launch press event with U.S. Vice-President Al Gore, Environmental Defense President Fred Krupp and EPA Administrator Carol Browner.

    - Leveraged petroleum industry efforts to negotiation emissions reporting regulations for the exploration and production (E&P) and marketing terminal industry segments.

    - Managed petroleum refining industry sector participation in the Common Sense Initiative. Worked with EPA Administrator Carol Browner and staff to craft Refinery Air Information Reporting System (AIRS) identifying emissions reporting requirements that are duplicative and/or obsolete.

    - Co-chaired United States Chamber of Commerce Right-to-Know industry coalition.

  • Manager, Waste and Pollution Prevention Programs

    American Chemistry Council

    (Chemicals industry)

    January 1992 — December 1993 (2 years)

    - Championed successful chemical industry efforts to preserve the availability of underground injection, a viable, long-term environmental management technology now advocated for geologic sequestration of greenhouse gases.

    - Developed and cultivated effective working relationships with federal, state and tribal officials responsible for implementing the U.S. EPA Underground Injection Control (UIC) program and the Ground Water Protection Council (GWPC).

    - Managed the Toxic Release Inventory (TRI) compliance support and emissions databases for the chemical industry. Produced the annual environmental performance report for the chemical industry.

    - Managed the Pollution Prevention Responsible Care Code for the chemical industry.

  • Agriculture/Residue Chemist

    Jellinek, Schwartz, & Connolly, Inc.

    (Environmental Services industry)

    January 1989 — December 1992 (4 years)

    - Responsible for FIFRA registration of agrochemical products for multinational companies including Monsanto, Dupont, Sumitomo and Zeneca.

    - Managed contract laboratories conducting plant and animal metabolism (xenobiotic) studies and field crop studies of pesticide residues.

    - Published and presented research papers at American Chemical Society meetings and national conferences.

    - Organized and led recruiting efforts at Colleges, Universities and Institutes.
