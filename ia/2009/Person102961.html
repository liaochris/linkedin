James Smith’s Summary

Specializing in System Software, Low-level Software, Diagnostic Development and Engineering Infrastructure, a hands-on Engineering Management professional with 25 years experience in directing the delivery of Platform and Test Products and Engineering Support within the Computer and Networking design industries. Skilled in multi-site team building, project management, strategic planning and designing complex products. A results-oriented leader with the unique ability to develop and motivate teams, deliver consistent, timely customer focused products and service, achieving financial objectives in a competitive environment.

James Smith’s Specialties:

Software / Diagnostic Design Manager and Project Management for hardware-software development.

James Smith’s Experience

  • Senior Manager / Director, Software Engineering & Infrastructure

    Nortel Networks

    (Public Company; NT; Telecommunications industry)

    October 2000 — November 2008 (8 years 2 months)

    Manage Diagnostic Engineering for new Enterprise Modular Ethernet Switches across multiple teams and sites for three generations of products. Local Diag team developed an entirely new environment including a multi-threaded Diag RTOS to replace VXWorks and a diagnostic manager for Next Gen product. Created successful Diagnostic and Emulation teams that meet all goals and ship products on time. Each team works closely with customers including HW/SW and Operations. Support all phases of engineering including hardware bring-up, manufacturing test and enabling diagnostics for customer support. Define test strategies and coordinate / integrate with Operations and outsourced manufacturing contactors. Supported DVT (Design Verification Test), EMI testing and DMT (Design Manufacturability Test).

All strategies, planning and development was directly coordinated with Fujitsu Global Server organization in Kawasaki, Japan.
    Products included high and low level diagnostics and testing tools to be used in support of Engineering, Simulation, Bring-up, Manufacturing and Customer Service. Also included development of emulators, monitors and other special purpose software. Development environments are VM/Assembler, LINUX/UNIX/C and Assembler and Windows NT/C++ (Obj. Orien. Prog.) . Hardware platforms are OS/390 Global Server (Fujitsu custom), Sun and Intel-based
