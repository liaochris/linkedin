Christopher Korody’s Summary

I am passionate about great design. I am especially interested in working with companies that build innovative, technology-driven products - including early stage and funded companies, as well as established companies.

My skills include business development, research, concept development, proposals, presentations and the creative that ties it all together. I work by the day, the night or the project.

I have spent my career writing, directing and producing product launches and introductions for corporate clients. I have founded three award winning companies, been an executive in a publicly traded agency, and am now an independent consultant. I am experienced presenting to and working with senior leadership.

My vertical expertise is in aerospace, automotive, consumer electronics and information technology. I am an experienced strategist for professional services organizations.

For a detailed description of my services and examples of specific projects, please visit my website.

Christopher Korody’s Specialties:

Writer, creative director, marketing and competitive research, website development, proposal development, presentation development, video and film scripts, speeches, collateral copy, business development, sales training

Christopher Korody’s Experience

  • Freelance strategist and creative

    Campos Creative Works

    (Writing and Editing industry)

    2002 — 2007 (5 years)

    Worked closely with senior managers on account strategy and proposal development. Worked on the Intel creative team doing research, writing backgrounders, developing treatments and questionnaires for use by location teams and editing video.

  • VP, Senior Creative Director

    Jack Morton Worldwide

    (Public Company; IPG; Marketing and Advertising industry)

    October 1997 — September 2001 (4 years)

    I was VP, Senior Creative Director for the Western Region. Originally this encompassed offices in Los Angeles and San Francisco. We later added Seattle after acquiring Carabiner International.

    I worked with our creative directors and account teams to ensure that we responded completely to the client's RFP and presented on-target, on-brand ideas.

    I was tasked with developing a national account manager training program. I worked with an independent consulting firm to develop this program which included a 500 page binder and the agenda and curriculum for two days of training. It also included a decision matrix that engaged office managers and account executives in a consistent methodology for deciding to pursue new business. Later, working with our in-house Notes developers, we developed a national tracking system for use by all senior managers. This program reduced time to productivity by 50%.

    I was also in charge of developing relationships with our IPG sister agencies in the Western Region. This included meetings to present our capabilities, to understand their areas of focus and to review clients of potential mutual interest. This resulted in a half dozen opportunities to do joint new business presentations in a two year period.

  • Founder

    Silver Strand Films

    (Writing and Editing industry)

    1986 — 1998 (12 years)

    I founded Silver Strand Films to produce video and live events.

    The live events we designed, directed and produced were primarily new product introductions. They targeted press, analysts and other influencers as well as partners and potential customers. The events typically combined a highly designed set with live product demonstrations, corporate speakers and pre-recorded video elements. Award winning examples included the launch of Momenta, the first pen based computer; and "The Big 10/4" which was a launch for the Power and Personal Iris from Silicon Graphics.

    In addition to the video work was done for live events, there was also a new opportunity in producing videos specifically for use in direct marketing programs. Our clients in this arena included Adobe Systems, Apple Computer and Porsche Cars North America.

    In addition we did work for other production companies, generally sub-contracting to develop and produce specific video or live theater elements.

    Later, Silver Strand served as a loan-out company when I freelanced for other companies.

    I closed Silver Strand after joining Jack Morton Worldwide.

  • Senior Creative Director

    Dick Clark Productions

    (Privately Held; Entertainment industry)

    1994 — 1997 (3 years)

    Responsible for proposal development and new business presentations for the corporate production group. (This was well before the company went public)

    Oversaw production and delivery of all creative deliverables. This included hiring and managing freelance talent, and overseeing shoots, editorial, recording sessions and audio and video post production. Worked with the producer to establish budgets and then managed my budgets with an agreed upon reporting structure.

    We won the Grand Prize at the New York Film Festival for our work on the Boeing 777 Introduction. Our BMW Z3 campaign was honored with a Super Reggie Award presented to MGM and BMW for the best integrated film promotion of the year.

  • Founder and President

    Image Stream Inc

    (Events Services industry)

    1978 — 1986 (8 years)

    Image Stream was my first company. We produced multi-image shows which were computer controlled slide shows used for corporate events, World Fairs and in some entertainment venues.

    We used 35mm slides, sometimes with as many as 30 slide projectors, all synchronized to a soundtrack by punch tape or tones. Later we used computers with a early version of something like SMPTE time code.

    Being in Los Angeles we started out doing presentations for record companies who wanted to showcase new product to their retailers. Our first big client was RSO Records. Eventually we worked for every label but Warner Brothers.

    This was flashy content free work. Corporate clients started hearing about it, and decided we were just the thing to spice up a sales meeting or a tradeshow presentation.

    The business grew quickly to 35 staff in a 10,000sq ft facility, and we were named to the Inc 500 three years in a row. Our largest client was Apple Computer but we also did a lot of work in aerospace with a number of divisions of Rockwell and the USAF. We worked with four different divisions of Yamaha for many years doing dealer shows. And we worked with Alpine Luxman doing dealer shows.

    At the end the company died because the technology moved from slide to video presentations which were easier to present live and more importantly to distribute.

    But it was a great time and we did a lot of great work.Many of us are still friends and work together when we can. It was one of those once in a lifetime deals.
