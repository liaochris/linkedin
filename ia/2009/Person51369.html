Curtis Campbell’s Summary

At MPRO Technologies, we have built a team of professionals who maintain the highest levels of training and certification in the areas of IT, Education, and Engineering.
Our team members average more than 20 years in IT, Recruiting, Customer Support, and Engineering.

I believe that when you are recruiting or staffing candidates for clients, you had better be very knowledgeable about the hiring process AND THE POSITION. No one can be up to speed on all fields and their vast positions, so we chose to represent the areas of IT, Engineering, and Administrative Support because I have spent my career in many aspects of each field. Being able to lead, project manage, and implement voice and data networks, web development, interactive media, coding and defining across campus wide nodes, and training users in all company cultures has made MPRO the strong organization for client partnerships in staffing and placement.

Our relationships with our clients and candidates are our lifeblood. We find the right fit to exceed your business expectations because we truly bellieve that people are key to sustaining a business' growth, success, and profitability.

Our business model is based upon integrity and character, honesty and trust, respect and excellence. Previous work in character education for a nationally funded interactive website provided the foundation that is appropriate for all businesses as well as schools. It was important to me to adopt the philosophies of those traits into the workplace for our staff as well as our clients.

  • Director of CharacterEd.net

    Thinking Media

    (E-Learning industry)

    August 2002 — August 2004 (2 years 1 month)

    I was appointed Director of this US Congress Appropriated Initiative for a K-12 Interactive Web Site for Character Education under the Safe and Drug Free Schools Program and No Child Left Behind. This website is for K-12th grade students, parents, teachers who believe in learning the difference between right choices and wrong choices based upon 9 character traits. Congress funded a generous grant to develop this interactive site to be used in the public schools, primarily. I trained school districts across the United States to implement this program in computer labs. Guidance counselors, teachers, parents, and students welcomed this site as a fun and exciting way to learn moral principles. Although the grant closed funding in 2004, the site is still in operation today. Check it out. www.charactered.net! It is awesome.
