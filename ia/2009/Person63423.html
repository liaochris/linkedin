Dick Gourley’s Experience

  • Managing Director

    GSMR Consulting

    (Management Consulting industry)

    August 1999 — Present (9 years 10 months)

    GSMR Consulting is a global management consulting firm committed to building enduring great organizations.

    The company was originally established in 1999 with the purpose of “creating world-class organizations”. A world-class organization is one that consistently delights its customers, has enthusiastic and committed employees, executes with excellence, and delivers profitable business results.

    From 1999 through 2008, the firm worked with many different types of clients including start-ups, turnarounds, nonprofits, emerging private enterprises, and global public companies.

    Today the firm is focused on providing professional services that enable existing enterprises to become enduring great organizations. Core competencies include customer assessment, strategy, business planning, business development, and leadership development.

  • Director

    The Conference Board

    (Non-Profit; 201-500 employees; Research industry)

    2007 — 2008 (1 year)

    The Conference Board is the world's preeminent business membership and research organization. Best known for the Consumer Confidence Index and the Leading Economic Indicators, The Conference Board has, for over 90 years, equipped the world's leading corporations with practical knowledge through issues-oriented research and senior executive peer-to-peer meetings.

    In an uncertain world of intense competition, increasing public accountability, and global risks, The Conference Board provides its members — top executives and industry leaders from the most respected corporations in the United States and around the world with vital business intelligence and forward-looking best practices.

    The Conference Board is a not-for-profit organization and holds 501 (c) (3) tax-exempt status in the United States.

  • Chairman

    Pacific Tweed

    (Retail industry)

    October 2001 — December 2006 (5 years 3 months)

    Pacific Tweed is an innovative, high-end, traditional, men's and women's specialty retailer located in Carmel, California. The company provides value by developing one-to-one customer relationships, resulting in excellent buying experiences and extremely high customer satisfaction. Pacific Tweed actively leverages information technology in support of its relationship marketing strategy, resulting in effective execution and predictable and sustainable business results.

    GSMR LLC provided the seed funding, infrastructure, and startup expertise that enabled Pacific Tweed to become a reality. Dick Gourley is a co-founder and served as chairman from 2001 through 2006.

    The Monterey Peninsula Chamber of Commerce recognized Pacific Tweed for business excellence and community service from 2003 through 2007.

    2003 / 2004 - "Rookie of the Year" Award
    2004 / 2005 - "Retailer of the Year" Award
    2005 / 2006 - "Retailer of the Year" Award
    2006 / 2007 - "Business of the Year" Award

  • SVP - Sales & Marketing

    Talk City

    (Internet industry)

    February 2000 — December 2000 (11 months)

    Senior Vice President of Sales & Marketing responsible for helping Talk City transition from a consumer-focused, advertising-based business model, to a Fortune 1000-focused, fee-based provider of online relationship marketing services.

    Talk City was established in 1996 by former members of Apple Computer’s Internet/Online Services business unit, and provided a destination web site for consumers. The company completed a successful IPO in July 1999.

    During 2000, developed the companies new positioning, brand look and feel, and advertising, built around strengthening customer relationships using online collaboration and research. Refocused the sales effort to enterprise account business development. Key new accounts included ABC, EBAY, GE, Kodak, Kraft, Mattel, NBC, and Proctor & Gamble. Revenues increased from $7.7 M to $14.8 M during the 2000 business year, while sales and marketing expenses decreased by $4.3 million. The company is known today as Live World.

    Established a marketing organization and developed a new market plan with a focus on six vertical industries. Re-positioned the company with a focus on operational excellence instead of high volume/low cost. Helped build-out the global sales team and strengthened customer relationships. Key global customers included Xerox, Lucent, Alcatel, and Johnson & Johnson. The company was acquired by Jabil Circuit in September 1999 in lieu of an IPO.
