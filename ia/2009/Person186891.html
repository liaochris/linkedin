Phil Marsh’s Summary

• Highly skilled with LSS and CAD software suites. With these programmes I have been able to produce high quality plans, as built surveys and volumetric calculations.
• Experienced with modern survey equipment including TPS, GPS & HDS.
• Especially skilled with the latest one man Leica robotic total stations.
• Vast experience with topographical and internal measured building surveys.
• Proficient with 3D laser scanning including the complexity of 3D modelling.
• Knowledge with all types of engineering setting out.

Phil Marsh’s Specialties:

Laser Scanning, Land Surveying & Measured Building Surveying.

  • Senior Surveyor

    Met Surveys Ltd

    (Privately Held; Architecture & Planning industry)

    March 1998 — Present (11 years 8 months)

    Met Surveys Ltd, Southgate House, Pontefract Road, Stourton, Leeds, LS10 1SW.

    Position:
    Senior Land & Building Surveyor

    Duties include:
    • Providing topographical survey and measured building survey quotation to clients.
    • Advising clients on large scale surveying issues and providing solutions to any issues with projects.
    • Many large topographical surveys which are processed using LSS and AutoCad software suites.
    • Implementing primary control stations using GPS & TPS.
    • Use of the 3D laser scanner for building conservation work.
    • Mentoring junior members of the survey team and helping with in house training programs.
    • Producing plans, plots, sections, 3D models from surveys.
    • Volumetric surveys and calculations (using LSS software).
    • Setting out works on site.
    • On site maintenance and calibration of all survey equipment.

    Projects Overview:
    I have worked on large multi million pound construction projects over the years and recently I have been involved with measuring a 200 000sq ft central London hotel that is to be refurbished into luxury apartments. It is a revival scheme to create 100 prime apartments with underground parking overlooking Hyde Park to be completed by 2010.

Phil Marsh’s Education

  • University of East London

    BSc (Hons) , Surveying and Mapping Sciences , 2004 — 2007

    This programme is concerned with mapping of the earth's surface and the management of information gathered. Combining the skills of physics, mathematics, environmental science and IT, you learn how to map, measure and analyse spatial measurements for a variety of uses. With a real practical emphasis, it offered me the opportunity to study specialisms such as engineering and geodetic surveying, hydrographic surveying, measurement systems, software engineering and remote sensing. Scientific principles are supported by practical applications relevant to the surveying profession today and in the future.

  • Barnsley College

    BTEC (HNC) , Building Studies , 1996 — 1998

    This course was part of the built environment programme and is a construction, building and project management course.

    The study topics gave me the academic, technical and industrial skills needed to progress in my career.

    I was already working in the geomatics and construction industry when I undertook this course which was sponsored by my employer.

    You study alongside other built environment students and take some modules with degree students.

    This course introduced me the construction industry and the various built environment professions, focusing on
    • building science
    • building techniques and practices
    • building technology
    • construction mathematics and computing
    • building law and contracts
    • design
    • site surveying

  • Barnsley College

    GNVQ (Adv) , Construction & the Built Environment , 1994 — 1996

    This Diploma provided an exciting, motivating and relevant, broad-based programme of applied learning, which introduced me to the fabric of construction and the built environment, demonstrating the impact they have on the lives of individuals and communities. The Advanced Level Diploma provided opportunities to analyse, evaluate and explore prinicples and practices relating to the social, economic and cultural contribution of the built environment and the wider factors influencing design, creation, maintenance and management of the built environment.
