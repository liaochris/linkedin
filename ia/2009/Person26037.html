Ben Godfrey’s Summary

I cut my teeth as a developer at Soup Ltd where I worked on web projects for Fujifilm, Norwich Union, Start-rite Shoes and Severn Trent. Went freelance in 2002 to focus on application development, recent projects include Business Link Norfolk and Transport for London.

I started Cohack with two collegues in 2004, we developed an analytics and dashboard reporting solution aimed at online marketers working in the SME sector.

In August 2006 I joined MyMart as their CTO and Lead Developer. MyMart is a mobile auction site and mobile commerce platform.

I joined moblog:tech in 2006 to take technology underpinning moblog:UK - http://moblog.co.uk/ - to a wider market. It's fair to say I'm a director without portfolio at the moment.

I'm currently a Wandering Hacker, working on projects with a variety of platforms and tools including Python, Django, PHP, Google App Engine, Amazon EC2 and S3, REST API mashups, OAuth, HTML/CSS, JavaScript, Ajax with jQuery, social media tools including WordPress and Twitter and much more.

Ben Godfrey’s Specialties:

Programming, Python, Django, HTML, CSS, Javascript, Ajax, Java, SCJP, PHP, Google App Engine, Amazon EC2 and S3, APIs, OAuth, WordPress, Twitter, Ajax, jQuery, Apache, MySQL, Linux/Unix, Shell, Perl. Mobile web apps, payments, advertising and search marketing. Design, usability, web analytics, online marketing, community development. Startup founder. Cycling.
