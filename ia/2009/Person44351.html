Christian Jorg’s Summary

General Manager and Entrepreneur consistently on the cutting edge of technology's impact on the entertainment business. Has developed and implemented successful growth strategies in several companies and founded a start-up. Significant deal and money raising experience. Interested in seeing the promise of technology coming to full fruition by developing new revenue streams for media and technology companies, specifically in the mobile and online space.

Christian Jorg’s Specialties:

Operating experience/P&L responsibility in public and private companies, new business development and international business expansion, M&A transactions, turnaround skills and hands-on turnaround experience, start-up experience, fund raising.

Christian Jorg’s Experience

  • Senior Vice President, New Media and Commerce

    Island Def Jam Music Group - Universal Music Group

    (Public Company; 51-200 employees; Music industry)

    July 2007 — Present (1 year 7 months)

    Overseeing all business activities of the Island Def Jam Music Group in the digital, mobile and online space. Also responsible for expanding the company’s business beyond its traditional focus by finding new creative outlets for its artists and building new revenue streams. Island Def Jam artists include Mariah Carey, Kanye West, Bon Jovi, Rihanna, Jay-Z, Lionel Richie, The Killers, Melissa Etheridge and many others.

  • Executive Vice President

    Flycell, Inc.

    (Public Company; 201-500 employees; ACO.MI; Wireless industry)

    October 2005 — July 2007 (1 year 10 months)

    Executive Vice President at Flycell, Inc., one of the world’s leading direct to consumer mobile entertainment companies, based in New York.

    Responsible for Flycell's worldwide content strategy. This includes original content production in music, video and games as well as licensing deals with third party content providers such as the major music companies and movie studios. Also integrating alternative programming sources and building Flycell’s dual online and mobile strategy. In addition involved in developing new mobile entertainment applications in areas such as social networking and blogging.

  • Co-Founder and Co-CEO

    E-Magine Entertainment

    (Entertainment industry)

    May 1999 — December 2004 (5 years 8 months)

    Co-Founded E-Magine Entertainment, a New York based record label with an internet marketing, on-line community and e-commerce focus.
    Raised three rounds of equity financing from private and institutional investors for a total of $ 4.5 million and developed the business, responsible for operations, finance, administration, business affairs and development. Negotiated company’s artist, distribution and marketing agreements.

  • Senior Vice President

    Imax Corporation

    (Public Company; 201-500 employees; IMAX; Motion Pictures and Film industry)

    August 1995 — April 1999 (3 years 9 months)

    Hired to turn around Imax subsidiary Ridefilm Corp. and build Imax’s Business Affairs and Development departments. Served on Imax’s R&D Committee and on the Board of Directors of Imax subsidiary Sonics Corp.
    Built Imax Business Affairs department from scratch and developed Imax’s theme park business. Engineered turnaround of Ridefilm division from a substantial loss in 1995 to profitability in 1997.
    Negotiated film, theatre and product development deals for Imax with Hitachi, Sony, ITT, Sega, Universal, Mainframe Entertainment and many others.

  • Vice President, New Technologies

    BMG Entertainment

    (Privately Held; 10,001 or more employees; Entertainment industry)

    August 1990 — August 1995 (5 years 1 month)

    Spearheaded BMG’s participation in new technologies, starting up a New Technologies Division. Responsible for developing new businesses related to new technologies including interactive entertainment, video games, on-line services, and internet related activities.
    Targeted and completed BMG’s equity investment of $12 million in technology start-up companies. This included the initial funding for ION, now part of Gracenote, one of the most promising players in the digital copyright and database space. Served on Board of Directors and as strategic advisor to top management. Key role in building BMG’s multi-media distribution and international infrastructures. Initiated BMG’s strategy for Internet presence leading to the development of Web pages for both the record club and labels. Served as advisor on new technologies to Chairman of BMG and CEO of Bertelsmann AG. Served as BMG’s representative on the RIAA’s (Recording Association of America) Technology committee.
