Ben Palmer’s Summary

I now have roots that are buried deep within entertainment software development. I have a passion for the communities behind them, and the great things that can be achieved in software development with patience, skill, and drive. Now, as Operations Director for InterWave Studios, I hope to push these ideas into my team so that we can really come out on top with our products. I want to show community developers that I have worked with in the past that success is not necessarily about skill, it is about the effort and commitment - and ultimately what drives you to the top is the experience you get when you pour your soul into a project with your colleagues close by you.

Ben Palmer’s Specialties:

Operational management, Project management, Level Design, 3D Art , 2D Art, Animation, Sound Editing and Video Editing. Public relations, Customer relations, Executive relations and staff supervision are among the strongest expertise from my personal experiences.

Ben Palmer’s Experience

  • Operations Director

    InterWave Studios

    (Privately Held; Computer Games industry)

    April 2008 — Present (1 year 6 months)

    InterWave Studios is an entertainment software and technology company founded in 2008 by members of the SteamFriends community and Vertical Game Studios.

    As Operations Director for InterWave Studios I am required to muster and concentrate all of my organisational skills on a day-in, day-out basis.   • Business Machines Technical Associate

    Staples

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; SPLS; Retail industry)

    August 2006 — Present (3 years 2 months)

    As a technical associate on business machines for Staples UK Ltd, I found myself dealing with many of the common business and consumer issues that people encounter everyday. I worked with consumers to solve their computing issues, and got them back on track when needed. As part of my work I advised similar consumers on products that would enhance the application of their computers, and allow them to complete the desired tasks as efficiently and as simply as possible. This position has allowed to to expand from my personal train of thought and think outside the box when it comes to problems that I wouldn't necessarily encounter in my day to day life. The experience of this is key and builds as a strong tool for managing customer relations and keeping consumers happy in the knowlege that they can trust their retailer.

  • Lead Level Artist

    Stargate The Last Stand

    (Computer Games industry)

    October 2004 — January 2009 (4 years 4 months)

    As lead level artist for Stargate The Last Stand my patience was tested, my stress levels thrown through the roof and my sleep deprivation extended beyond human ability. With a deadline pushing us constantly later and later with our community, as a technical demonstration I really had to stick by our team and help keep everyone else motivated, in the same way they motivated me. As always with my more pleasant occupations I have had, I enjoy the people I work with, and this is the driving force or my productivity and the source of quality within my work.

  • Head Level Designer & Project Manager

    Vertical Game Studios

    (Computer Games industry)

    October 2004 — April 2008 (3 years 7 months)

    As a head of level design and environment design at Vertical Game Studios I pooled together design planning and strategy in an attempt to get as much work done in as little time possible. As well as developing actual levels for in-game and for testing use, I was also responsible for other departments feeding assets into my own, by liaising with other department heads and keeping communications between artists flowing. The position tested me, working to a perfect plan in an industry that is imperfect by nature is sometimes hard to get a grip of, but suffice to say, by the end of this company I was a much more hardened individual.

  • Level Designer

    Stargate Total Conversion

    (Computer Games industry)

    March 2004 — August 2005 (1 year 6 months)

    StargateTC was my teething ground within level design. Away from the safe haven under the wings of my mentors at Sven Coop, I was free to branch out and show what I could do. Unfortunately, my work mostly revolved around texture work for other artists, and, believe it or not, character art. Despite this, I felt that I managed to mature as a developer through this experience. I learned to stand up for my opinions and justify them in large group discussions, and reason with lead artists on their own preconceived ideas. This quickly lead me into a management position for the final months of my stay on the game, and I believe pushed me to come to where I am today.
