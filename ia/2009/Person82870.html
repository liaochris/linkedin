Gabriel Salas’s Summary

I think I could be classified as an experienced flexible programmer and as an enthusiastic and easy going person. I'm usually helpful in positions where proven adaptability to languages and technology is necessary, where a good sense of law and order will be nice.

I have experience on several areas and that's because I have been on this business long enough to experience change in paradigms and technologies. Although greatly curious about the specifics of breakthrough technology, with time I have learned to focus less on aspects that change with time, and focus more on developing the patterns, practices, methodologies and character required to build good software, regardless of the language or technology.

Hence, I'm not a hard-core defender of any language, technology or vendor: I like keeping up to date but in order to use what works best for a particular scenario or project while giving my two cents for a good and respectful working atmosphere. I have never seen a project fail because of lack of technological expertise (there are tons of technology experts nowadays), but rather because of lack of communication, respect and space for development, rest and quality of life.

I tend to think a lot of the future of a project while being practical, so I strive to keep things real and effective without forgetting to keep them also nice and tidy and with capacity to evolve with ease over time. Everything that allows the long term quality and evolution of software interests me: such as the use of proven methodologies and practices like OOP, UML, Design Patterns, Test Driven Development, documentation and 'good old' face to face communication. I also enjoy the ideas, strategies, meetings, relationships, lessons and conversations related to a worthy, healthy and evolving project.
