Adesina Iluyemi’s Summary

Adesina Iluyemi is a dentist (DDS equivalent) by training with almost three years postgraduate working experiences in a teaching hospital. Adesina has experience in implementing and evaluating small scale mobile ICT projects in England. Presently, he is undergoing a PhD research degree programme exploring policy and change management issues in the implementation and use of mobile/wireless information and communication technologies (ICTs) in the health systems reform and development in Africa. He is a member of New Economic Partnership for Africa's Development (NEPAD) Council.
I have expertise and consult in developing products and services in eHealth/Telemdicine and sustainable technologies in developing countries. Areas of interests are in the diffusion & adoption of innovations like low-cost technologies (laptops & mobile devices), mobile/wireless ICTs-enabled services development and implemnetation (Telemedicine & Telehealth), mobile/nomadic working, sustainable BOP business models, technology-enabled economic growth and micro-industrailization, SMEs, rewenable energies, Global Health, Open hardware and software. He has attended and presented in various national and international conferences on this areas.
