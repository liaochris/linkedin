Pam Ozaroff’s Summary

I have been an educational editor and writer for twenty-five years, specializing in literature and language arts at the high school, middle school, and college level. Both on staff and as a freelance editor and writer, I’ve worked for a variety of publishers, including Prentice Hall/Pearson; Holt, Rinehart and Winston; Bedford/St. Martins; Houghton Mifflin; and Scholastic. I have also worked as an editor and writer for several development houses, including Chrysalis, Brown, Aptara, and Mazer, on projects for Prentice Hall/Pearson, McDougal Littell, Hampton-Brown, and Glencoe.

My editorial work at the secondary school level has focused on creating engaging instructional material that makes literature, language, and the writing process come alive for students. I have worked as an editor and writer on the top four 6-12 literature programs and as a writer on several writing and grammar programs. Although my specialty is language arts, I have worked in several other disciplines, including social studies, economics, sociology, political science, and science. I have also copyedited magazine and scholarly works on medical and health topics.

Pam Ozaroff’s Specialties:

Language arts (literature, writing, reading skills and strategies, grammar, vocabulary), student editions, teacher's editions, ancillaries, assessment practice, differentiated instruction, lower-level adaptations of on-level material. I am particularly comfortable with upper-level literature (including poetry). I love to do lit searches, especially for contemporary and multicultural literature. I have written extensively about literary terms. Believe it or not, I actually love grammar.
