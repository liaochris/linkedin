Jack DeNeut’s Summary

30 years of computer programming experience, and over ten years experience managing large-scale web technology projects with distributed team members. Strong programming skills, including Java, SQL, Perl, Python, CSS, and web services.

Comprehensive background in investment banking and finance, including start-up financing. Won 1997 Wall Street Journal European Analyst Contest.

Widely quoted in The Economist, Financial Times, Reuters, Bloomberg, The Wall Street Journal, etc. while working as an equity analyst covering banks and insurance companies (press clippings available upon request).

Fluent in Czech, reading knowledge of French, native English speaker.

Jack DeNeut’s Experience

  • Entrepreneur

    Nelso

    (Internet industry)

    March 2007 — Present (2 years 2 months)

    Founder and only employee of Nelso, a local search site targeting Europe. The site is available in 10 languages, with the English-language site at http://www.nelso.com/

    Did all coding related to the site, primarily using Python, Django, and PostgreSQL.

    With zero marketing budget, built Nelso into one of the leading local search sites in the Czech Republic and Denmark, reaching over 100,000 unique users per month only a few months after launch. On-line marketing was conducted through the Nelso blog, Twitter, Facebook, and viral promotions such as the now-famous Prague beer price map ( http://www.nelso.com/beer-price-map-of-prague/ ).

    Google has contracted with Nelso to provide local business data for Google Maps and Google Search. Nelso also provides local business listings for Volny.cz, one of the top portals in the Czech Republic.

    Now targeting additional markets in Europe, including Germany, Sweden, and Norway.

  • President

    Dekadu Inc

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Information Technology and Services industry)

    June 2004 — June 2006 (2 years 1 month)

    Founded this one-person company to further develop some of the ideas I had while running AlbumCity.com (see below). Built a complex e-commerce platform for France Telecom’s Polish subsidiary, and then launched this platform in eight other languages. All translations were handled by a web-based translation system that I wrote specifically for this project.

    Site was written primarily in Java (using Tomcat, Hibernate and Struts), and interfaced with a PostgreSQL Unicode database. In addition, made extensive use of RDBMS stored procedures, Ajax, CSS, and XML-based web services (including RSS and Atom).

  • CEO

    AlbumCity.com

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Computer Software industry)

    August 1999 — October 2003 (4 years 3 months)

    I was a co-founder and CEO of this B2C e-commerce retailer modeled on Amazon.com. Managed a staff of 24 in three offices in Central Europe (Prague, Bratislava, and Warsaw). I wrote most of the software for a cross-border, cross-language, e-commerce platform. Site was written in Perl and Java, with Oracle as the RDBMS. PL/SQL was used extensively to speed up database operations. U.S. patent was filed for inventions resulting from this development.

    I personally raised $2.2m from private investors to fund company’s operations. After the “bubble” burst in 2000, funding for e-commerce companies dried up, and AlbumCity.com had trouble raising funds. Company closed in June 2003.
