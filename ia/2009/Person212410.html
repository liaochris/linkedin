Sean C. Casey’s Experience

  • Senior Scientist, Lead Instrument Scientist

    Universities Space Research Association (NASA Ames Research Center)

    (Nanotechnology industry)

    1997 — Present (12 years )

    • Chairman of the Sensors and Instrumentation theme for the U.S. National Nanotechnology Initiative (NNI) Grand Challenges Workshop on Nanotechnology for Space Exploration.

    • Lead author for the Congressional report on Sensors and Instrumentation via the NNI and the Office of Science and Technology Policy.

    • Technical oversight of a $24M instrument program for NASA’s Stratospheric Observatory for Infrared Astronomy (SOFIA).

    • Lead USRA/SOFIA participation in NASA’s vision for a National Virtual Observatory.

    • Participated in peer review of research proposals for NASA’s Cross Enterprise Program, Research Opportunities for Space Sciences, and Medium Explorers Program.

    • Committee of visitors for the NSF’s Office of Polar Programs.

  • Principal Scientist

    NASA Goddard Space Flight Center

    (Government Agency; 10,001 or more employees; Defense & Space industry)

    1990 — 1997 (7 years )

    • Provided the technical background and science requirements for teaming agreements with NASA Ames, Hughes Danbury, and the University of Arizona.

    • Developed partnership between NASA Goddard Space Flight Center and the NASA Langley Research Center using newly adopted solid modeling engineering tools.

    • Developed instruments for astronomical observations aboard the NASA’s Learjet and later at the NSF’s South Pole station.

    • Received the 1995 NASA group achievement award from the Goddard Center Director, for the SPIRAC program as an example of NASA’s goal for “more efficient and cost effective methodologies to [instrument] design and construction...”.
