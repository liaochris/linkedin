Jim Janis’s Summary

Jim Janis is a strategic management consultant and advisor to private, public, and non-profit organizations and companies, primarily in the energy and environmental fields. You can reach Jim at JimmyJanis@gmail.com, and find out more about him at www.JanisGroup.com. The objective of his practice is to help his clients achieve sustained competitive advantage in their chosen markets. He has focused most recently on helping his clients understand and benefit from the stimulus program for green energy projects, developing positive branding strategies, finding new executives, and winning major new contracts for his clients in the United States and in the United Kingdom.Follow Jim on Twitter: @JimmyJ96, and on Facebook: @Jim Janis.

Jim Janis’s Specialties:

Jim works with firms offering scientific and technical services, engineering, construction, and anti-terrorist security systems.His clients include non-profit organizations, many small businesses as well as large, international defense, engineering, construction, and energy firms in the US and in the UK. Jim has a special practice helping small, minority, service-disabled, and woman-owned firms. If you need help with projects eligible for stimulus funds, visit our website at www.JanisGroup.com.

Jim Janis’s Experience

  • Principal

    Janis-Bradburne Executive Recruiting LLC

    (Environmental Services industry)

    2006 — Present (3 years)

    Janis-Bradburne Executive Recruiting LLC was founded by Jim Janis and John Bradburne, former President of Fluor Fernald, to assist clients in recruiting senior managers and business development executives in the energy and environmental business. We have special expertise in the nuclear, health and safety, and environmental remediation businesses, and work extensively in the United States and United Kingdom.

  • President

    The Janis Group, Inc.

    (Environmental Services industry)

    May 1996 — Present (13 years 1 month)

    The Janis Group, Inc. works with firms that offer engineering, construction, technology, scientific and technical services, protective force services, and anti-terrorist security systems. Mr. Janis began his consulting practice in June 1996, with Margaret Tifft Janis, and serves as President of their firm, The Janis Group, Inc. Their clients include several non-profit organizations and about twenty companies, covering a wide range from small businesses to large, international engineering, construction, and energy firms. Most of the Janis Group’s clients work in the energy, defense, homeland security, and environmental marketplace in the US and in the United Kingdom. Most of Jim’s clients are CEO’s, Presidents, Boards of Directors, or Business Development managers.

  • Executive Vice President

    BNFL

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; Environmental Services industry)

    October 1992 — May 1996 (3 years 8 months)

    As Executive Vice President of BNFL Inc. (now part of Energy Solutions, Inc.), the American subsidiary of British Nuclear Fuels, plc., Jim was responsible for the operations of BNFL in the United States in environmental restoration, waste management, decontamination and decommissioning, facility management, and government site operations.
    • Jim provided strategic guidance to overcome barriers of foreign government ownership, geographic location, cultural differences, and resistance to new technologies. He led the company into new markets including the first operating contract at a U.S. Department of Energy (DOE) site to be managed by a company owned by a foreign government – the Rocky Flats site near Denver, CO.
    • BNFL Inc.’s market share grew from an annual rate of $2 million to more than $200 million (unconsolidated) during Jim's three years as Executive Vice President.

  • Chairman and Chief Executive Officer

    ICF Incorporated

    (Environmental Services industry)

    February 1980 — October 1992 (12 years 9 months)

    Before joining BNFL in 1992, Mr. Janis held a variety of senior management positions at ICF Kaiser International, beginning in 1980. Among other assignments, he was Chairman and Director of Kaiser Engineers Hanford Company, Chief Operating Officer of ICF International, and Chairman and Chief Executive Officer of ICF Incorporated (today it is known as ICF International, Inc.), the founding company of the ICF family of companies.

    Along with his colleagues, he devised and carried out the strategy that helped ICF grow from a small policy and economics consulting firm of less than 100 people in 1980 when he joined ICF, to a large, international engineering and construction company of more than 4,000 when he left in 1992 to help establish BNFL Inc. in the United States.

  • Deputy Assistant Secretary

    US Department of Energy

    (Government Agency; 10,001 or more employees; Government Administration industry)

    November 1977 — January 1980 (2 years 3 months)

    Before joining ICF, Mr. Janis was a senior manager in the Federal Government in Washington, DC. He was an employee of the U.S. Department of Energy (DOE) when it was created and an employee of the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) when it was created. Mr. Janis was also a Commissioned Officer of the United States Public Health Service in Washington, DC.

    He served DOE as Deputy Assistant Secretary for Planning and Evaluation, responsible for strategic planning for energy crises. Mr. Janis was also Executive Director of the Energy Policy Coordinating Committee, a Cabinet-level group created by the President to coordinate energy policy across thirty-six Federal agencies and Departments.

  • Director, Program Evaluation Division

    US Environmental Protection Agency

    (Government Agency; 10,001 or more employees; Government Administration industry)

    December 1970 — August 1977 (6 years 9 months)

    Mr. Janis held a variety of senior management positions at the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency, and helped organize the agency's Office of Radiation Programs when EPA was created. As Director of EPA's Program Evaluation Division, he was responsible for evaluating the effectiveness of all of the agency's programs. He managed EPA’s Regulations Steering Committee, responsible for reviewing the economic and environmental impacts of all regulations issued by the Agency for air pollution, water pollution, hazardous waste, noise, solid waste, toxic substances, radiation, and drinking water.
