Arnaud Miege’s Education

  • University of Cambridge

    PhD , Mechanical Engineering , 1999 — 2003

    Theoretical design and practical implementation of a computer controlled hydraulic system to significantly reduce roll-over likelihood on an experimental tanker/semi-trailer. Aspects of the project included: vehicle modelling & validation, hydraulics commissioning, electronic circuit design, vehicle instrumentation, design, implementation and testing of control strategies, and microprocessor programming. Work on the experimental vehicle involved constant teamwork with my fellow research students and external collaborators. Every 6 months, the progress to date was reviewed by the industrial sponsors of the Cambridge Vehicle Dynamics Consortium. The first 16 months of my PhD counted towards my National Service working for the scientific service of the French Embassy in London, as a 'Researcher in a Foreign Country'. Authored several journal papers, conference papers, and a Ph.D. thesis entitled "Active Roll Control of an Experimental Articulated Vehicle".
