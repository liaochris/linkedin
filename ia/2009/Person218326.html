Carl Brown’s Summary

It all started with an Apple II when I was about 12. I learned enough to become a software engineer and I moved from startup to startup. I guess I always did startups because no one else would hire me? No degree. No formal training. And, well, I like getting things done which typically unbalances ... well - never mind.

I moved from programmer, to leader, to director, to VP of Engineering. Each startup had more money to start with than the last -- and each found a way to spend it all. At its peak (the Internet bubble), we spent $100 Million in two years. We created a $3 Million dollar company -- that spent $10 Million a year.

In 2002, I started SimplyRFiD. SimplyRFiD has grown to a successful startup with low millions in revenue. We continue to focus on great engineering and support -- and getting things done.
