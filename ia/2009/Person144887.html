Ludovic Blain’s Summary

Ludovic is the Program Manager for the Closing the Racial Wealth Gap Initiative, where he is leveraging the more than 100 financial and economic experts of color to build public will and policymaker commitment to enact policies that close the racial wealth gap.

Previously, Ludovic was Director of the StopDogWhistleRacism.com, a non-partisan project to confront the use of political symbolic racism, and lessen it's influence on elections and policy debates.

Ludovic Blain was the National Campaign Coordinator for the Margeurite Casey Foundation's Equal Voice Campaign. He managed the $5 million, 18 staff and consultant national campaign, culminating in 64 town halls over 5 months across 12 states with over 13,000 attendees.

Previously Mr. Blain was Organization Services Director of the New Progressive Coalition. where he supported synergy among 200 members. He also was Associate Director of the Democracy Program at Demos, leading national campaigns for Election Day Registration and felon voting rights. He was also Associate Director of We Interrupt This Message, an anti-racist strategic communications group, and led environmental justice, voting rights, and other campaigns for over a decade at NYPIRG.

Ludovic’s international work includes engaging Denmark's ruling party to oppose the right-wing's strategy of promoting racism and xenophobia to erode public support for progressive policies. He led discussions about sectarianism and other forms of racism in Northern Ireland and the Republic of Ireland. He has led capacity-building work in Haiti, Canada and The Gambia, where he received honorary citizenship.

Ludovic currently serves on several boards, including the Rockwood Leadership Institute, NYPIRG Fund, and the Communications Leadership Institute.

Mr. Blain holds a BA from the City College of New York, where he received awards for his activism. He’s a graduate of the Industrial Areas Foundation and the Rockwood Leadership Institute.

Ludovic Blain’s Specialties:

Issue advocacy, community organizing, strategic communications, coalition and collaboration development, organizational development, leadership development, anti-racist analysis.
