Patrick J. Nugent’s Summary

Beginning as a college professor, I was bitten by the bug of fundraising when I wrote a $2 million grant which established the Institute for Quaker Studies at Earlham College. As the founding director of that institute, I was then bitten by the bug of administration. From there I became Principal (chief executive) of a small professional college in Kenya, inspiring my interest in international development and higher education, as well as major gift fundraising. Returning to Cincinnati to care for elderly family, I was privileged to join my family's favorite regional museum as a development officer. Here, I have come to love the world of major and planned giving, and am fortunate to have substantial administrative responsibilities as Director of Individual Giving and Advancement Services, leading a team of six professionals.
