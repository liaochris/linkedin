Nicolas Holzapfel’s Summary

I'm a well-rounded designer currently finishing off an MA in Interactive Media at the University of the Arts London.

As a freelance designer and as a student I have worked on many projects in diverse media, from book illustration to content management systems. Nearly all of these projects involved intense group work, for which I was project manager several times. This experience has led me to discover that there's little more demoralising than working with people don't do their bit, and little more rewarding than working with able people who care about what they do. As such one of my main goals is to work in a team who find value in their work as more than just a source of income.

Although I have experience in graphic design and development, I want to focus on user-centred interaction design and information architecture, especially in the area of social media. I'm particularly enthusiastic about pushing forward the internet's capability for improving collaboration and knowledge-sharing.

Nicolas Holzapfel’s Education

  • University of the Arts London

    MA Interactive Media, 2007 — 2008

    Graduated with Pass with Distinction

    This course has mainly consisted of a series of group projects where each team is given a brief and a few weeks to conceive, prototype, test and develop a working solution. This culminates in an industry-style pitch in which the product is presented and justified to the rest of the class. All the briefs fall within the amorphous field of 'interactive media' and the focus is on developing team-working, time-management, prototyping, brainstorming and presentation skills. The course includes a very strong emphasis on user-centred design at every stage of the design cycle. In all this I found the course was very successful and has certainly shaped my own career goals.
