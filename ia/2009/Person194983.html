Bob Quigley’s Summary

Insurance industry consultants – forensic accounting, auditing, litigation support, management advisory, and tax practice. Engagements include actuarial / commutation support services, advisory services to insolvent insurers, business valuations, expert witness services, MGA audits, reinsurance inspections and dispute resolution, Sarbanes-Oxley documentation and controls testing, and tax return preparation.

Bob Quigley’s Specialties:

Team Leader/member, NAIC financial standards accreditations of state insurance departments from 1992 to present.

Expert witness on insurance insolvencies, reinsurance arbitrations and litigation, including audit failures.

Consultant, Integrity Insurance Co. in Liquidation, Paramus, NJ 1988 to present. All aspects of estate admin, including forensic accounting, reinsurance commutations, and marshaling other assets.

  • Vice President & Treasurer

    The Mutual Fire, Marine and Inland Insurance Company

    (Insurance industry)

    September 1985 — December 1986 (1 year 4 months)

    Initial efforts to rebuild function overshadowed by a financial guaranty program experiencing massive defaults in both the oil and gas and real estate sectors. Assumed control of accounting functions in early 1986, and published 1985 Annual Statement with discounted loss reserves and extensive footnote disclosures. Declared a payment moratorium to financial guaranty bondholders, and fashioned a rescue plan with the support of the Pennsylvania Insurance Department while progressing through Orders of Supervision and Suspension to Rehabilitation. Confident that the state regulators, creditors, and judiciary were in the best positions to affirm and implement the plan, left to establish insurance industry practice. Rehabilitation successfully completed in 1996.

Developed underwriting cash flow models, assumed responsibility for GAAP accruals from Actuarial Department and recommended strategies to maintain favorable IRIS ratios.
