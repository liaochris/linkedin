Brent Oswald’s Summary

Brent Oswald has over 25 years of creative development and project management experience in film, video, computer graphics, special events, and new media. He has worked in the areas of marketing and corporate communications, commercials, continuing education, brand identity campaigns, and eCommerce.

For the past seven years, Brent has focused on interactive/online marketing with a specialty in the hospitality industry.

Brent has planned and managed Web site development projects for a variety of clients, including the Kennedy Space Center, Yosemite National Park, the California Culinary Academy, Paramount’s Great America, and Kimpton Hotel and Restaurant Group. He also held the position of Vice President Executive Producer of TOTALnet, a Web design and development firm located in San Carlos, California.

Prior to his involvement with the Internet Brent was a producer of sponsored documentaries and corporate image films. He co-founded Doros Animation, an award winning commercial graphics and animation studio in New York City. He planned and supervised the production of computer animation for commercials and broadcast identity packages while at SFPG, one of the leading computer animation facilities in the West at the time. He also worked as an advertising agency account executive in New York.

Brent graduated from Ithaca College with a B.A. in Philosophy and Cinema Studies, and later apprenticed at The Gray Film Atelier in New York. He studied Web site design with David Siegel, noted author of the international bestseller, “Designing Killer Web Sites”, and studied information design with Edward Tufte of Yale University.
