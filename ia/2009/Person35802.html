Noel Burkman’s Summary

Noel Burkman has 16 years of experience in the business of strategic planning and technology. He has managed small and large teams through every phase of the software development life cycle process. Noel has led strategic planning initiatives and brainstorming sessions resulting in product and branding road maps, all detailing efforts, resource allocation, milestones and success measurements.

Most recently as the Director of Digital Transformation at the American Press Institute(API), Noel worked directly with top news media executives, assisting them in evaluating and mitigating the decline in revenue. He developed and presented solutions to help regain lost market share. Noel identified social media, user generated content, and the semantic web as areas with the most potential for audience development and revenue growth.

Prior to the API Noel worked for Follett Higher Education Group (FHEG), the largest wholesaler and retailer of college textbooks and apparel in the world, where he helped the 120-year-old company cope with a rapidly changing market landscape. During his tenure there he spent much of his time exploring the impact of new technologies, the emergence of social networking and user-generated content. He utilized the application of these technologies in order to retain market share and develop new revenue streams . He was initially brought to FHEG to build and lead the technical team and develop infrastructure. The efforts of his labor went on to support FHEG’s rapid e-Commerce growth moving them into the top 70 revenue generating websites in the world. Under his leadership, e-Commerce revenue grew from $3 mill to $250 mill in under five years.

Early on Noel was an entrepreneur. His ventures included a company that consolidated and auctioned advertising, and a business-to-business portal helping specialty sporting good retailers optimize their supply chains. He holds a bachelor's degree in computer science from DePaul University.
