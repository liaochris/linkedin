Alef Arendsen’s Summary

In 2002, Joost van de Wijgerd and me set out on a journey to become leaders in the Enterprise Open Source Java world. Together we started JTeam, a company building great software, taking utmost care of its customers and telling the world about it.

I was part of the first groups of engineers developing Spring, now the de-facto standard Enterprise Java development platform. Spring is used by many Fortune1000 companies such as HSBC, JP Morgan Chase, Toyota, KLM and LinkedIn to implement mission-critical systems.

The company backing Spring is called SpringSource. I co-founded SpringSource in 2004 and since then, we closed several rounds of financing (totalling more than $25M) with mainly Benchmark and Accel. In 2007 and 2008 we made two strategic acquisitions: Covalent (the company sustaining the development of many Apache projects) and G2One (the company building the Groovy and Grails technologies).

Together with Steven Schuurman, I set up the continental-European branch of Springsource which involved hiring some our key engineering and sales staff, rolling out our professional services program and overseeing day-to-day operations.

Taking a break from management, between 2006 and late 2008 I traveled the world to tell the community at large about the software we build, the values we share and the idea of community innovation in general. I spoke at numerous conferences and trained key engineering personnel how to successfully use our technologies.

In 2009 I left SpringSource to move back to JTeam as a strategic advisor. With SpringSource well over 100 people and offices around the globe, I needed a challenge again. JTeam employs a good number of very talented people that haven't lost the values we started out with: building great software, taking utmost care of our customers and telling the world about it.

In my free time, I travel a lot, spend my time in the mountains in Italy or out at sea on my kiteboard.
