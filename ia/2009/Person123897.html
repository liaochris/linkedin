Julie Bates’s Summary

Julie Bates is a Twin Cities writer and spoken word poet with a passion for community arts administration. She currently works as Literary Programs Manager for Intermedia Arts, managing the highly successful literary and spoken word programs that have become a part of the organization through its 2005 merger with SASE: The Write Place. In 2008, Ms. Bates was awarded a Joyce Grant by Americans for the Arts’ Professional Development Fund for Emerging Leaders of Color.

Julie Bates’s Specialties:

Community arts administrator with more than 8 years experience developing, running and managing community arts programs. Freelance writer/spoken word poet/teaching artist. Experience creating agency source documents such as brochures, web content, success stories, program descriptions. Skills include: project planning/management; grant writing; program evaluation and documentation; deadline management.

  • Spoken Word Artist/Performer

    Local and Regional Venues

    (Performing Arts industry)

    2000 — 2005 (5 years )

    Julie Bates (a.k.a. sOliloquy) has been on the twin cities’ spoken word scene since 2000. She has been featured at the MN Fringe Festival, Amazon Fridays, Urban Griots Saturdays, Art-A-Whirl, the University of Wisconsin’s Throw Parties Not Bombs peace festival, the People’s Open Mic, and the Raising Our Voices protest of the Iraq war, an event she co-curated and hosted with local artist Rush Merchant III. Her first chapbook, State and Being, was published in the spring of 2003.

    Bates has studied theatre and music for fifteen years, and has performed in more than a dozen staged productions, including Oscar Wilde’s A Woman of No Importance (Hester) and the musical revue Sondheim and Company (soloist). She has worked with young people throughout the twin cities, teaching classes in music, French, and spoken word; she has also worked as a private vocal coach.
