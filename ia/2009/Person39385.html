Cathy Malchiodi, PhD, LPAT, LPCC’s Summary

Scholar, research psychologist, educator, visionary, and organizational entrepreneur and founder of International Art Therapy Organization/Art Therapy Without Borders. Author in field of art therapy, arts and health, and mental health, including numerous books translated into 12 languages: Art Therapy Sourcebook, Handbook of Art Therapy, Expressive Therapies, Creative Interventions with Traumatized Children, and Understanding Children's Drawings. Given over 300 invited keynotes, workshops, and courses throughout the United States, Canada, Asia, and Europe. Visiting professor at numerous universities throughout the US and the world.

Served on the boards of American Counseling Association (ACA), serving as the first Representative from the Association for Creativity in Counseling (ACC); Counseling Association for Humanistic Education and Development (C-AHEAD; President 2009-2010); American Art Therapy Association (AATA); and on numerous national and international boards in mental health, counseling, arts, and public service.

Adviser and non-profit consultant and staff to a wide variety of community, national, and international agencies, including the Issues Deliberation America/Australia, International Criminal Court, MAD to Live, and Save the Children Foundation. Regularly interviewed by the news media in publications such as CNN, Prevention, Better Homes & Garden, Family Circle, Cosmopolitan, Psychology Today, Congressional Quarterly, Alternative & Complementary Therapies, Boston Globe, New York Times, US News & World Report, and in other print and internet sources.

Please visit website at http://www.cathymalchiodi.com for more information; subscribe to popular blogspot, The Healing Arts, on Psychology Today at http://blogs.psychologytoday.com/blog/the-healing-arts; and get your daily dose of what's happening in the field of art therapy @ www.twitter.com/arttherapynews.

Cathy Malchiodi, PhD, LPAT, LPCC’s Specialties:

Board Certified and Licensed Professional Art Therapist, Licensed Professional Clinical Counselor, and Certified Trauma Specialist, Level III; expert art therapy program development for children, adolescents, adults, and families, survivors of trauma, abuse, domestic violence, medical illness and returning military. Consultant to courts and legal counsel, including expert opinion of children's and adults' drawings, child abuse, child custody, posttraumatic stress, and trauma reactions.
