Russ Lane’s Summary

After slugging it out in newspapers since college, I opted to go freelance and pursue two different avenues in my writing. My most notable newspaper exploit includes working as a food writer and losing more than 200 pounds at the same time.

So I have two major focuses in my writing. As my web magazine Second Helping shows, I pick apart a question that many in weight loss don't tackle: what now? What happens after you hit goal, and how do people make the transition from "dieter" to "former dieter?" How do you view food, fitness, others and especially yourself differently?

It's an aspect to weight loss that I feel isn't addressed enough: getting what you want and being prepared for it aren't the same thing.

Beyond the issues examined in the column, I also write on food, music and pop culture for various Web sites and magazines. I've written event previews, profiles and enterprise stories on culture for years. I tend to give a literary nonfiction treatment to profiles and panel discussions -- I feel readers should have a strong sense of my subject's voice and mannerisms, the little quirks that demonstrate their humanity and allow a reader to connect with them.

I also prefer enterprise stories that take an element of pop culture and reveal its larger significance to society and what it means for our lives.

Russ Lane’s Specialties:

Versatility, lively copy, unassuming interview style, methodical planning, finding patterns and interesting connections in my beats; willingness to travel, establishing details with editors on the frontend to avoid hiccups on the backend.

    You lost weight. Great! So now what?

    That's the central question guiding Second Helping, an online magazine examining life after weight loss. It's written by people who have seen both sides of obesity under unique circumstances. I myself dropped from 350 to 175 even while taking a job as a food writer.

    I write a news column and utility pieces and co-write a cooking column, Rabbit Food for Lions, with pastry-chef-turned-gym owner Kevin DeMarco. We also feature a handful of columnists who write about how they answer "now what?" Updates post Monday-Friday daily.

    Beyond Second Helping, I write for various magazines, newspapers and Web sites. Weight loss, food, fitness and cultural beats are my reporting and commentary focus. I specialize in trend stories, and finding creative story angles and quirky patterns and connections.
