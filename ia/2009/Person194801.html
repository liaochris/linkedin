Rodney Bliss’s Summary

Purple Crayon Consulting draws its inspiration from "Harold and the Purple Crayon." Just as Harold's crayon brought his imagination to life, I strive to help people more easily use the tools and programs they have. I've been a lifelong trainer, whether it was training support professionals at Microsoft, or training IT departments on how to maintain the latest email system we just installed.

I've been fortunate to be able to move easily between the boardroom and the server room. I'm as comfortable presenting the merits of new programs and processes to the C-level executives as I am rolling up my sleeves and helping to reboot a cranky server.

I came up through the ranks as a support professional, first at WordPerfect and later at Microsoft. Every position I've held has been a new opportunity for me to focus on making computers and software easier to use. I spent years creating training curricula for Microsoft Exchange and writing books and articles on the topic.

I've spent 25 years in and around software; writing my first program in 1981 using BASIC on a TI99; storing it on a cassette tape and displaying it on my parents' television. While I still love programming, most of my passion centers around helping people use software to make their lives easier.
