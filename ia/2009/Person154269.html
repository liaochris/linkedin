Marven Krug’s Experience

  • Vice President, Product

    Slingshot Labs

    (Internet industry)

    April 2008 — March 2009 (1 year)

    Brought on board to fill the high-profile role of realizing a product idea brought to the company by Wendi Murdoch. Turned a niche offline product into a broadly appealing website.

    * Successfully launched Linfo.com (a local recommendation site like Yelp or Citysearch but with a strong social platform component and a low-cost model) from scratch in just four months with a small, lean team
    * Leveraged user testing results to develop a coherent user experience driving excellent engagement metrics
    * Quickly grew user-generated content at very little cost
    * Note: As a small start-up in a tough economy, the site will live on but without a full-time VP in charge of it

  • Director, Consumer Product

    Citysearch

    (Public Company; Internet industry)

    December 2006 — April 2008 (1 year 5 months)

    Brought on board to lend my Amazon.com expertise to the improvement of the consumer-facing user experience. Helped develop a metrics-driven culture within the product management team. Grew new markets at low cost. Introduced personalization and behavioral targeting features to the content management system.

    * Championed and then successfully implemented a state-of-the-art web analytics solution using Visual Sciences (Omniture)
    * Opened five new regional markets at low cost
    * Introduced automated content management to the Citysearch website platform
    * Deeply involved in the complete site re-design that launched in early 2009 (working with consultants Adaptive Path)

  • Senior Director, Product Management

    Abebooks.com

    (Privately Held; Internet industry)

    July 2004 — April 2006 (1 year 10 months)

    Brought on board to help raise the bar, implement best practices and potentially prepare for an acquisition. Streamlined the product planning and prioritization process. Optimized the interaction of the business and technical teams. Completed major overhauls of the website's core systems.

    * Sat on the senior management team, which developed the company strategy
    * Recruited, managed and mentored a staff of product and project managers, designers and web developers
    * Led the product prioritization process for the entire company
    * Modernized company systems in areas like accounting, catalog, site merchandising, check-out funnel, search engine optimization, inventory management and more
    * Overhauled the look-and-feel of the Abebooks websites, resulting in significantly higher conversion
    * Thanks in part to these efforts Abebooks was sold to Amazon.com in 2008

  • General Manager

    Amazon.ca

    (Public Company; AMZN; Internet industry)

    July 2000 — June 2004 (4 years)

    Entrusted to lead the entire Canadian strategy for Amazon.com. Transitioned from growing export sales to launching a Canadian store. As GM, held overall accountability for the entire Canadian business.

    * As business owner for Amazon.ca, held P&L responsibility for this $100 million+ business
    * Led a team of 25 in all areas of demand generation: marketing, PR, pricing, selection, and website design
    * Negotiated with the government of Canada to find a legal framework for the launch of Amazon.ca within existing laws
    * Helped negotiate a massive third-party logistics deal with Canada Post
    * Dealt directly with such suppliers as publishers, music labels and movie studios, as well as PR and ad agencies
    * Exercised final control over marketing
    * Acted as official spokesperson to the media
    * Provided business updates to Jeff Bezos (founder, CEO) and the senior management team weekly
