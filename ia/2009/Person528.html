John Olsen, CFP®’s Summary

PERSONAL CFO • STRATEGIC PLANNER • INCOME ARCHITECT

John is a results-driven, indispensable business executive with over 18 years of experience helping Fortune 500, wealth management, and startup companies exceed their goals.

Currently John helps executives and retirees maximize investment performance by using investments not tied to the stock market or real estate, in most cases double digit returns.

Q: What is your background and qualifications?
A: Prior to owning his own firm, John worked in high level corporate positions with companies such as Pacific Life, Lincoln Financial Group, Conseco, Bankers Trust, and Western Southern Life where he trained and educated financial advisors on how to better serve their clients. He has held the CFP® designation in good standing for over a decade and is both securities and insurance licensed. With over 18 years of technical experience, John’s passion to help executives, business owners, and individual families help secure their financial future became so great that he opened up his own practice in 2005.

Q: What is your philosophy or approach?
A: Our philosophy is to offer precise advance planning techniques and outside the box solutions but on a conservative basis. We never want to have to apologize to our clients and what you may have learned from others doesn’t mean it will work going forward. We enjoy working as a cohesive quarterback leading your team of professionals which may also include other financial advisors. If you are missing a key professional to your team we can provide one from our extensive network of legal, tax, sports, and business professionals.

John Olsen, CFP®’s Specialties:

Q: What do you specialize in?
A: We specialize in three main areas. The first is principal and family protection which means keeping what you’ve earned. The second is designing an income stream for life…at some point most of our clients want to make work optional. The third area is estate planning which gives our clients the peace of mind on how to minimize tax liability while maximizing wealth potential and generational transfer.
