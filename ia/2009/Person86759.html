Gina DeLapa’s Summary

Real-World Etiquette provides onsite training for our clients' young associates who may not know how to survive and thrive within a corporate environment.

Professional service firms (banks, CPA firms, and law offices in particular) express frustration that their new hires, despite being bright and well-educated, don't always know how to conduct themselves like professionals. More than simply exasperating managers, this lack of professionalism can stall careers, sabotage client relationships, and diminish employee engagement/retention. Both managers and new hires benefit immediately when all new employees receive the same information and are held to the same standard.

As these new associates are groomed for greater responsibilities, founder Gina DeLapa will be well-positioned to provide them with the coaching they need to grow and develop within their respective firms.

DeLapa is a former university career counselor who received certification as a Corporate Etiquette Consultant from The Protocol School of Washington, in Washington, DC. Her presentation credentials span from the International Career Development Conference in Sacramento, California to Deutsche Bank in New York City. She has a proven passion for helping young professionals succeed.

Gina DeLapa’s Specialties:

Highly interactive, results-oriented training/coaching on workplace adjustment issues. Building rapport with millennial-generation employees; helping them transition successfully from college to career.
