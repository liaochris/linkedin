Cory Wagner’s Summary

Cory Wagner is Director of Product Strategy and Design for Patient Care Technology Systems. This Charlotte, NC-based company develops, markets, and supports software for hospitals at enterprise and departmental levels. During his ten years leading the company’s design and development efforts, Cory and his team have built multiple versions of six major applications, including PCTS flagship products Amelior EDTracker® and Amelior ORTracker®.

Debuting in 1998, EDTracker was the first application in healthcare—and perhaps any industry—to integrate realtime indoor positioning technology into a tool that provided more than just locating. It was a pioneering concept for tracking patients, staff, and assets: combine location data, business process intelligence, a dynamic rules engine, and a browser-based UI to disseminate vital tracking and workflow information to caregivers in realtime. Most importantly, EDTracker did all of this automatically based on interpretation of location, movement, and interactions, thereby freeing caregivers from burdensome data-entry chores that distract from patient care.

Today, Cory's work helps streamline patient flow and improve coordination in more than 30 emergency and perioperative departments across the United States. Noted facilities like Albert Einstein Medical Center (Philadelphia, PA), Christiana Care Health Network (Newark and Wilmington, DE), and Providence St. Vincent Medical Center (Portland, OR) rely on products he designed and helped develop to manage more than 900,000 hospital visits each year.

Cory is also a noted speaker on the topics of realtime locating technologies, automatic tracking, and automated workflow intelligence. Since 2005, he has presented at several industry conferences (including HIMSS 2007) and private, invitation-only technology briefings. The Tracking Hierarchy of Needs he developed in 2005 is well known in healthcare informatics circles and is referenced by vendors throughout the RTLS industry.

Cory Wagner’s Experience

  • Director of Product Strategy and Design

    Patient Care Technology Systems (PCTS), LLC

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Computer Software industry)

    November 2004 — Present (4 years 7 months)

    Cory leads the Charlotte, NC-based analysis and design team for Patient Care Technology Systems. His primary responsibilities include

    * helping define the company's strategic product direction;

    * identifying, defining, and analyzing business processes, workflow, and business requirements throughout the healthcare enterprise;

    * designing new software solutions to meet the tracking, communication, and workflow needs of users in multiple hospital departments; and

    * establishing PCTS as a thought leader in the area of RTLS-enabled automatic tracking.

    Cory and his team define the requirements, design the user experience (including UI and functionality), and prepare specifications for new PCTS products and major upgrades.

    Cory also works with other PCTS organizations such as sales, marketing, and client services to evangelize the product line and gather feedback to inform ongoing efforts to enhance existing products and identify new products and features.

  • Director of Product Development

    Healthcare Information Technology, Inc.

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Computer Software industry)

    August 1997 — November 2004 (7 years 4 months)

    Cory was the visionary and chief solutions designer behind Healthcare IT's logging, reporting, and web-based automatic tracking solutions for emergency and perioperative departments. These products strategically gathered, interpreted, analyzed, and disseminated patient, staff, and resource information to improve communication, enhance coordination, and streamline the overall workflow of hospitals nationwide.

    To implement this solution's information-gathering component, Cory designed and oversaw development of the HIT passive tracking rules engine, a powerful tool for gathering patient, staff, and equipment location data from diverse indoor positioning technologies and applying complex logic to that data in realtime. This engine went beyond simple locating, actually making and recording decisions about bed occupancy, staff assignments, care progression, and patient/staff/equipment interactions based on location data received from third-party technologies.

  • Performance Support Specialist

    The Oxford Associates, Inc

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Writing and Editing industry)

    May 1993 — August 1997 (4 years 4 months)

    Cory began work at The Oxford Associates (TOA) as a technical writer developing computer user and systems manuals. Over the course of two years, he documented and developed classroom training for a half dozen different accounting and cost-estimating systems for major clients like Cincinnati-based consumer products giant Procter and Gamble.

    Cory then transitioned in to a role of developing computer-based training for NCR Corporation of Dayton, OH. That eventually morphed into development of full-blown web-based electronic performance support systems (something unheard of in 1996). NCR recognized Cory's efforts with two consecutive innovation awards in 1996 and 1997.

    During his time at TOA, Cory also developed and published the FACTIRS model of performance support system design. The model describes and explains how to address core user needs in both computerized and print-based performance support systems.
