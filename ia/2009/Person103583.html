Dr. Kenneth Jandes’s Summary

My leadership has contributed to the Oak Lawn School District’s commitment to develop a strong Professional Learning Community where all students, including special education and English Language Learners, are taught using 1) on-grade level materials, 2) a common curriculum map and, 3) a pacing chart at each grade level for each core learning area. Because of this, almost all of our students throughout the district are experiencing nearly 100% of a curriculum that is written and endorsed by our teachers and which is based on the Illinois Learning Standards. As testimony to our accomplishments in the area of instruction, beginning this fall, all seventh grade students will be enrolled in pre-algebra. Systematically, all eighth grade students will receive high school level algebra beginning in 2008-09. Our ability to hire exemplary staff and to effectively evaluate and nurture them over several years is another tribute to our supervisory skills and strengths. Our Oak Lawn school district has maintained successful AYP status for all schools, subgroups and the District because of our involvement in this process.

I teach graduate courses at Concordia University and Governors State University, am Chair of the Department of Educational Leadership and Director of Graduate Internship Experiences at The American College of Education located on the 17th floor of the Civic Opera House in downtown Chicago.

Additionally, I am a professional jazz and classical saxophonist having chaired the Department of Applied Saxophone for ten years at the American Conservatory of Music on Michigan Avenue in Chicago. For the past 28 years I have performed and recorded with the Chicago Jazz Mass Ensemble (www.churchjazz.com). My band, The Ken Jandes Orchestra, performs for ballroom dancers and "black tie" events about 100 times a year.

Finally, I am a member of the Naperville Classical Saxophone Quartet which is available for hire at social events, church services and concerts.

Dr. Kenneth Jandes’s Specialties:

I have been a Superintendent of Schools in Cook County for 12 years. As President of South Cook Illinois Association of school Administrators (IASA) for six years, I have lead 66 school districts though difficult times. As one of only four school superintendents from across the nation to be appointed I have served for five years on the Federal Legislative Insurance Commission (FLIC) in Washington, DC creating legislative language on many national educational issues brought before congress.
