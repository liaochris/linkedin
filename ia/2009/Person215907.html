Shawn Lindstrom’s Summary

I have been involved in both administering education loans at the school level as well as marketing education loans to consumers for over 10 years. I received my B.S. in Political Science/Criminal Justice from SUNY College at Brockport in 1995. Concurrent to earning my degree, I worked in Brockport’s financial aid office advising students and parents on the financial aid process. Upon graduation, I worked at University of Rochester before moving on to Ithaca College as Assistant Director of Financial Aid. While at Ithaca, I managed a $30 million student loan program and implemented electronic processing of all loan applications via ELM. I left Ithaca in 1999 to join in the founding of the Internet’s first student loan marketplace, eStudentLoan.com. In 2001, I took over as President in connection with Alloy/360 Youth’s (NASDAQ: ALOY) acquisition of the company from Student Advantage, Inc. and have continued to innovate and assist lenders in achieving their marketing objectives..

In 2005, I facilitated the fourth direct acquisition of eStudentLoan by Goal Financial, LLC. At Goal, I furthered my mission of making finding and selecting education financing easy for students and their parents by reinventing the student loan marketplace.

Along the way, I have conceived and executed a number of innovative and profitable ecommerce projects not directly related to eStudentLoan. Over the years, I have gained deep knowledge of the relationship between great technology solutions and the users that access them. My extensive ecommerce background as well as my strong general management experience has made me a well-rounded operator and entrepreneur.

In starting Efundia, I hope to mitigate the need for many traditional financial services products by more closely connecting consumers with sources of funding. I'm still writing that chapter so check back soon.
