Reuel Barksdale’s Summary

Reuel Barksdale B.S., M.S.A., A.B.D.

An established organizational development specialist with over twenty years of corporate experience. As an Assistant Vice President of a Fortune 500 corporation, he had direct responsibility for the design, development, implementation, and evaluation of organizational development strategies and activities. Mr. Barksdale had functional and operational responsibility at regional and corporate levels for managing Human Resources, organizational development, and training in areas of sales and marketing, management and diversity.

Reuel Barksdale is a full-time faculty member in the Business Management Technologies Department Columbus State Community College where he teaches Organizational Behavior, Human Resources Management, Business Management and Interpersonal Skills Management. He is also an adjunct professor at Ohio Dominican University where he teaches the Management Capstone course.

As a motivational speaker and organizational development consultant, Mr. Barksdale has addressed audiences across the country on the topics of change management, diversity, management, strategic planning, conflict resolution, leadership, and employee development.

Reuel Barksdale received his Bachelors of Science degree from Franklin University and a Masters of Science in Administration from Central Michigan University. Reuel has finished the coursework necessary for his Doctorate in Higher Education from Ohio University and is currently working on his dissertation.
