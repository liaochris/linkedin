Rob Colling’s Summary

Subtitler and subtitling manager for the past decade. Seven years ago I founded a Newcastle office for Visiontext, a leading international subtitling company. I managed the office, team, technical equipment, assets and workflow, and created an efficient, low-overhead operation that gave Visiontext a quantifiable competitive advantage.

It's now time to strike out on my own, and I've started a subtitling and captioning company dealing specifically with web video. We make website video clips accessible to new audiences. If you'd like to learn more, please come and visit us at www.internetsubtitling.com.

Rob Colling’s Specialties:

Subtitling, captioning, training & presentations, editing, proofing, writing, team leadership, office management, digital asset management, IT support, technical management, strategic planning, facilities management, event management and promotion, sound engineering, music production and programming, playing and composing music, cooking a mean chilli and fixing old cars.

    • Some training of team members e.g. for new software or technical procedures.
    • Discrete project responsibility for certain titles e.g. boxed DVD sets, plus creative problem-solving for unusual projects.

    Technical
    • Lead role in developing new technology for the company (for example working with a software house to pioneer voice recognition in DVD subtitling).
    • Providing the office’s front-line IT support – everything from problem-solving or dealing with the London IT team through to building PCs, installing servers and configuring network equipment.
    • Providing all the office’s digital asset transfers, record-keeping and storage management, including problem-solving with video formats, conversions, aspect ratios etc.
    • Making sure everyone had the resources they need, on time and in a form they could use.
