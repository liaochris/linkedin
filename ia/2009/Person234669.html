TJ Sullivan’s Summary

I'm a Los Angeles-based writer and award-winning, independent journalist whose work has appeared in many publications (magazines and newspapers) from coast to coast, including the Los Angeles Times, Chicago Tribune and Dallas Morning News, as well as online posts for such Web sites as LA Observed and NBC.

The Sigma Delta Chi Award, the second-oldest national journalism award after the Pulitzer, tops the list of state and national honors that recognized my work during more than 15 years spent writing full-time for newspapers.

Other honors include first-place awards from Best of the West, the American Association of Sunday and Feature Editors, the Associated Press News Exec Coun. and the LA Press Club.  

In a career highlighted by turns as an investigative reporter, state government bureau chief, feature and sports writer, I’ve covered a broad range of issues and topics, from the tempestuous beginnings of tribal casino gambling in the high deserts of New Mexico to the Academy Awards in Los Angeles, CA.

Although primarily a writer for print, I have considerable experience in radio, and television. While a statehouse reporter in New Mexico, I appeared as a regular guest on a televised public affairs roundtable, and often participated as a panelist for televised candidate debates.

I’ve given lectures and organized presentations featured at conferences conducted by both The Poynter Institute and the national Society of Professional Journalists.

I’ve also served as an adviser to The Working Press, a college internship program sponsored by SPJ.  

I’ve taught basic journalism courses at UCLA's student newspaper, The Daily Bruin, as well as a credit-level course at CSU, Northridge.

I am a University of Kentucky alumnus, as well as a past journalism fellow at the Western Knight Center for Specialized Journalism at USC. I've studied writing at the American Press Institute and participated in many workshops, including the New York Writers Workshop.

TJ Sullivan’s Specialties:

Writing, editing, photography and media relations. Journalistic specialties include: investigative reporting; public records; real estate/growth-and-development issues; feature writing (covered Academy Awards, profiled many celebrities in entertainment and politics); sports reporting (incl. pro horse racing and rodeo); education reporting (elem, sec.. and higher ed); local, state and national government reporting; tribal casino gambling and issues of tribal sovereignty as related to the IGRA.
