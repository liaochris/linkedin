    (Self-Employed; Myself Only; Writing and Editing industry)

    January 1994 — Present (15 years 7 months)

    Over 200,000 children in the U.S. are autistic and this number has been rising each year. Yet, few novels try to understand the mystery of this disorder and dramatize the ways it has changed so many. What also singles out this novel from others on autism is that the story is also told from the perspective of the autistic man.
    The book is written in a simple prose style so it can be read by all ages. However, it is not a children’s book. Certain passages that deal with depression are intense and a few scenes may be too violent for children. Nevertheless, its tragic elements, moral tone and magic realism make it a book for those who enjoyed such recent best-sellers as The Five People You Meet in Heaven and The Lovely Bones, as well as old favorites like Flowers for Algernon and The Sound and the Fury.
