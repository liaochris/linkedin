Steven Davis’s Summary

My personal mantra is "give your best, be the best". My career in sales, management, and marketing is a testament to the success of this outlook and attitude.

I had to adopt this ideal early on in my life. Growing up in Asheboro, NC was a wonderful experience. Almost my entire family were within a 15 minute drive. But at that time not a single person in my family had ever graduated from college. It just so happened that along the way I found something that I had a knack for, Baseball. It was a great life instructor. I learned the harder I worked the more success I had, and I applied that same lesson to all areas of my life, including school and work (had my 1st job at age 12). So I developed a dream to play Major League Baseball.

The great thing about dreams & goals are they propel us, motivate us to the next step in life. That's my story. I went on to be named the best Baseball Player in my area, and was given a partial scholarship to play Baseball at Lenoir-Rhyne College in Hickory, NC. I was never the most talented player, not even 6 feet tall, but I decided that "no one" would ever work harder than me (give your best). The work ethic paid off. I was named the Team's "Most Outstanding Pitcher" my Sophomore year, and Team MVP my Junior year (be the best). After my Junior year I was blessed to be drafted by the Chicago Cubs and went on to play Minor League Baseball in the Cubs organization.

Today, I draw upon these life experiences/accomplishments to achieve success everyday.

"What the mind of man can conceive and believe, It can achieve."
- Napolean Hill
