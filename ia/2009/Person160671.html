Michael Cote’s Summary

Michael Cotē is an independent researcher and returning graduate student at Vermont Law School and the University of Massachusetts-Amherst. A former writer, he left the field of journalism to "do" environmental policy work rather than write about it. He is interested in practical, reasonable city planning policies that focus on sustainability and climate change issues that impact small cities in the Northeast.

Active in the field of planning, Michael is Outreach Coordinator for the American Planning Association's International Division. Most recently he, along with 5 other scholars, was competitively selected to represent Vermont Law at COP15, the United Nation's Climate Change Conference in Copenhagen, Denmark December 2009. In addition, he is working on several articles for publication covering the intersection of land-use law and climate policy. Michael is also working with scholars from Sun Yat-sen University in Guangdong, China regarding a climate adaptation policy framework for Guangzhou Province.

He has presented practical solution for city planners to create climate change adaptation policy at several city planning conferences in New England and nationwide.
