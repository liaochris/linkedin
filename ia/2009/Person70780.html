Andrew B. Einhorn’s Summary

Andrew B. Einhorn is a systems engineer and management consultant specializing in information architecture, digital media, public health, and strategic communications. With over 9 years of experience working for global public health and management consulting firms such as ICF and John Snow Inc, Mr. Einhorn has provided technical assistance, project management, and systems design for a wide variety of public and private sector clients. His client lists includes the Department of the Navy, Department of Defense (DoD), Federal Aviation Administration Office of Commercial Space Transportation, National Aeronautics and Space Administration (NASA), Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Nuclear Regulatory Commission (NRC), American Wind Energy Association, Santee Cooper power company, American Chemistry Council, MA Department of Public Health and various non-profit organizations.

Mr. Einhorn is a member of the Adjunct Faculty in Georgetown University’s Health Systems Administration Department. He is a published author, both in technical and non-technical fields, has spoken about his varying experiences on television, radio, and at academic conferences, and is the cofounder and President of a new media and technology company aimed at improving innovation and the exchange of information in the public sector. He holds an M.S. in Engineering Management from the George Washington University and a B.A. in Clinical Psychology and Biological Anthropology from Emory University.
