Grier C. Raclin, Esq.’s Summary

Accomplished Chief Legal and Administrative Officer, with in-depth experience as both Managing Partner of a major corporate law firm and General Counsel of Fortune 500 and other public companies. Known as an effective and decisive leader able to build and motivate high performance teams and implement corporate visions. Recognized for positioning dynamic companies to succeed in challenging environments.

Grier C. Raclin, Esq.’s Specialties:

Skilled in successfully managing complex legal and financial issues associated with international operations and transactions, strategic restructurings, financings and other "transformational" events. Brings a diverse skill set and a hands-on approach to directing major corporate functions, including Business Development, Human Resources, Facilities Management, Procurement, Supply Chain and Government Relations.

Grier C. Raclin, Esq.’s Experience

  • EVP and Chief Administrative Officer, 2009-Present; EVP, General Counsel, Corp. Secretary, 2005-2009

    Charter Communications, Inc.

    (Telecommunications industry)

    2005 — Present (4 years)

    Charter Communications, Inc. is a publicly-held, Fortune 500 ($6.5 Billion, 16,000+ employees) company and the country’s fourth largest provider of broadband cable (video entertainment, high speed internet and telephone) services.

    Recruited through Vulcan Capital (Paul Allen's investment vehicle) to improve operational performance and ultimately undertake a transformational balance sheet restructuring. Member of the senior management team, reporting to the CEO, with responsibility for all legal, regulatory, governmental relations, intellectual property, business development, procurement, facilities and supply chain functions.

    • Restructured department organization and external counsel relationships, reducing external legal fee spend by >40% in two years while dramatically improving client service and employee morale.

    • Through strong negotiation skills, able to recover more than two years total legal expense through a single plaintiff’s action. Successfully resolved numerous multi-million dollar patent infringement suits and consumer class actions.

    • Ensured compliance with all corporate and Board of Directors’ governance requirements during period of heightened scrutiny, including governmental (e.g., SEC; SOX) and listing agency (NASDAQ) requirements

  • EVP; Chief Legal Officer and Corporate Secretary

    SAVVIS, INC. (NASDAQ: “SVVS”)

    (Public Company; SVVS; Information Technology and Services industry)

    2003 — 2005 (2 years)

    Savvis is a leading, Welsh Carson-backed international provider of IT outsourcing, virtual private networks, managed hosting and internet services to commercial enterprises worldwide.

    As member of Senior Management, reporting to the CEO, directed all legal and regulatory affairs, facilities management, procurement and protection of intellectual property. Managed all Board affairs and ensured compliance with corporate governance capitalization and regulatory requirements in the U.S. and 30 foreign countries.

    • Instrumental in almost tripiling company revenues (from $236M to $667M) in four years, organically and through transformational acquisitions.

    o Helped devise and managed implementation of program which achieved >$100M in cost reduction synergies in the first year, greatly exceeding corporate goals and expectations.

    • Identified and implemented best option to protect multi-million dollar, corporate intellectual property.

    • Devised and implemented program which assured compliance with corporate capitalization requirements in over 30 countries while minimizing capital investment

  • EVP, Chief Administrative Officer and General Counsel

    Global TeleSystems, Inc.

    (Telecommunications industry)

    1997 — 2002 (5 years)

    GTS was a leading, George Soros-backed provider of international voice, mobile and data services to businesses throughout Eastern and Western Europe (including Russia) and Asia, which grew from $100M to $1B in revenues, with 4,500 employees in 20 countries.

    As a member of senior management, reporting to CEO, directed all legal, regulatory, human resources, facilities and administrative functions, including all litigation, corporate development and transactional matters, and interactions with regulatory bodies in 20 European and Asian countries.

    • In anticipation of business requirements, re-sized and reorganized multi-national corporate departments and budgets, saving millions of dollars annually

    • Structured and negotiated $3.5B in public and private, foreign and domestic debt and equity financings; and the acquisition, reorganization and divestiture of $3B in European and Asian companies and assets for Global expansions.

    • Managed the resolution of major legal and regulatory issues related to constructing and operating the first trans-European fiber optic network, and operating the largest non-government telecommunications service provider in Russia and Central Europe ultimately creating a company worth $750M.

    • Led the resolution of all legal issues in connection with the recapitalization of the company's balance sheet, reorganizing the company into four stand-alone businesses, and divesting those businesses for $1.5B, in part through pre-packaged bankruptcies in the U.S. and the Netherlands.
