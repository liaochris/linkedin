Marshall Eubanks’s Summary

1980-1987 : Member of the Technical Staff, JPL. Developed a Kalman filter for Earth Orientation and spacecraft navigation support for the NASA Jet Propulsion Laboratory. Was technical head of the JPL Timing and Earth Orientation Program for operational spacecraft navigation support.

1987-1999 : Technical head of the Very Long Baseline Interferometry progam of the U.S. Naval Observatory, providing navigation calibrations for the entire Department of Defense and the GPS satellite constellation.

1999-2003 : CEO of Mullticast Technologies, a start-up devoted to bringing media broadcasting to the Internet using IP Multicasting.

2003-2004 : CTO of TeleSuite Corporation, a start-up devoted to providing truly immersive videoconferencing.

2004-present : CEO of AmericaFree.TV, a start-up devoted to providing of broadcast resolution video entertainment over the Internet.

2005-2007 : CTO of Destiny Communications, a start-up devoted to telepresence.

Active in Multicast protocol development for the Internet Engineering Task Force. Co-chair of the "Multicast Backbone Deployment" and "Forward Error Correction Framework" Working Groups. Secretary of the IETF Administrative Oversight Committee. Active in popularizing IP Multicast; provide seminars and workshops explaining Multicast to technical audiences.

Marshall Eubanks’s Specialties:

Development of IP networks for streaming media distribution. Design, implementation and debugging of multicast enabled networks. IP Transport for high data rate loss-tolerent applications such as Very Long Baseline Interferometry. Kalman and other optimal linear and non-linear filters. Error and erasure protection for psycho-acoustic and psycho-visual compression technologies. Telepresence development and deployment.
