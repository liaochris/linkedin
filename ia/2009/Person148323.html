Marc Kowalski’s Summary

Dr. Kowalski has professional experience as an associate at McKinsey & Company, one of the world's largest management consultancies, and as a technial advisor at Pennie & Edmonds LLP, one of the world's premier intellectual property law firms. Dr. Kowalski’s technical experience includes tenure as a Senior Research Engineer at the Stanford Linear Accelerator Center (SLAC), where he was involved in the development of fast methods for large-scale finite element simulation of electromagnetic fields in the time-domain. He has served as a technical reviewer for the IEEE Transactions on Biomedical Engineering, IEEE Transactions on Antennas and Propagation, Magnetic Resonance Engineering, and Magnetic Resonance Materials in Physics, Biology, and Medicine (MAGMA). He is currently a member of the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers (IEEE), an associate member of Sigma Xi, and a full elected member of Commission B of the United States National Committee of the International Union of Radio Science (USNC/URSI). His current research interests lie in the analysis and design of irregular and aperiodic phased-arrays for remote sensing applications, particularly for opportunistic bistatic surveillance and reconnaissance.

Marc Kowalski’s Specialties:

Computational physics, antenna array design, array processing, wave propagation

  • Sr. Applications Engineer

    Optimal Corporation

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Computer Software industry)

    March 2006 — October 2007 (1 year 8 months)

    Serves as product marketing manager for Optimal’s full-wave power & signal integrity CAD tools, defining functional specifications, developing roadmap presentations, and generating white papers. Works closely with key customer accounts and internal research and development department to ensure that product development meets market needs in a timely manner. Work closely with American and Asian sales in both pre-sales engagements, coordinating post-sales support, and conducting marketing campaigns. In particular, focuses on educating the high-speed digital microelectronics package and integrated circuit communities on the need for and benefits of accurate electromagnetic extraction. Integrates Optimal’s tools into customers’ upstream and downstream CAD tool flows, with emphasis on Apache Design Systems' RedHawk and Cadence Allegro.

  • Adjunct Professor

    Santa Clara University

    (Educational Institution; 1001-5000 employees; Higher Education industry)

    September 2004 — May 2005 (9 months)

    Taught a three-semester course on antenna theory and applications. Significant emphasis placed on modern CAD practice, surveying planar circuit simulation tools, full-wave field solvers, and the various theories of diffraction. Additionally, an end-to-end Doppler RADAR circuit was designed, constructed and tested in class to illustrate principles of radar cross section (RCS).

  • Senior Research Engineer

    Stanford Linear Accelerator Center

    (Privately Held; 1001-5000 employees; Defense & Space industry)

    June 2003 — October 2004 (1 year 5 months)

    Lead the development of T3P, a massively parallel time-domain finite-element modeling suite. Implemented a parallel multilevel preconditioner to reliably analyze electrically large structures involving as many as 100 million degrees of freedom. Calculated short- and long-range wakefields for the Next Linear Collider (NLC) structure. Assisted in the development of multipole-accelerated (reduced computational complexity) algorithms for beam dynamics and coherent synchrotron radiation for the Linear Coherent Light Source (LCLS). Interacted closely with sponsors (DoE HEP and DoE SBIR/STTR) of the Advanced Computations Department, writing proposals and attending PI meetings. Interacted with accelerator physicists in the analysis of high-frequency effects in NLC, PEP-II, RIA, LHC, and Tevatron.
