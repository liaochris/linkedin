Kathryn Senior’s Summary

As an experienced news writer for medical journals such as The Lancet Oncology, I regularly produce concise and insightful research reports, usually to very tight deadlines. I liaise with experts from all over the world and am comfortable communicating with academics in all areas of science allied to biology and medicine.

I have many years experience in educational publishing; I have edited and managed the production of several biology and human biology books for Harper Collins Publishers and am co-author of their current A level texts in both these subjects. In addition to writing, much of my work has involved managing other freelance suppliers, including editors, picture researchers, illustrators and designers. I currently also write for Hodder Science in the UK.

During my early freelance career, I worked for two years for the Physiological Society, based in Oxford and, more recently, for Macmillan Cancer Relief. In 2000-2002, I researched and wrote two large internal reports, one on cancer prevention and screening and the other on cancer care in the private sector. I was also an Information Officer for Science Line, a public information service run jointly by the BBC and Channel 4, between 1994 and 1996.

I enjoy making my writing fun to read, whatever the level, and have written a dozen highly illustrated children’s science books on the history of science, volcanoes, dinosaurs and the human body for the David Salariya Book Company in Brighton. One of these titles, Fast Forward: Rainforests was runner up for the Times Educational Supplement non-fiction award in 2000.
