Hamby Hutcheson’s Summary

Imagine the possibilities of an on-line community of more than 120 million communications-enabled vehicles and users on the roadway at the same time, all connected to the internet in a rolling MESH network. Such a network would create unprecedented opportunities for convenience, safety, entertainment, information, advertising, service delivery, emergency communications, and research.

Telematics, the remote communication between systems and devices, has evolved to refer to an emerging market for communications, entertainment and information services delivered to moving motor vehicles via wireless technology.

The development of an integrated solution offering will enable an Advanced Traveler Information System to provision a suite of new InfoTainment services to vehicles/consumers anywhere on the roadway moving at mainline speeds vs. a stationary vehicle on the roadside as in the current motor club or independent service center model. This defines and changes the whole paradigm and ushers in a new ‘upscale service center’ that has the capability to be in “constant contact” and one that leverages a “connected vehicle” and a connected community of vehicles, service centers and drivers anytime … anywhere ... the "Smart-Traveler Vision".

I am an active Engineering Advisor specializing in Intelligent Transportation Systems (ITS), telecommunications, infrastructure engineering and Telematics. As a former designated U. S. Expert in Transportation, I served U.S. General Fleet Management interests on the Organization for International Standardization (ISO) Technical Committee (TC) 204, which is chartered with harmonization of transportation (TELEMATICS) issues at the international level.

Also served ISO/TC204 as a Voting Member of the Technical Advisory Group (TAG) from 1995 to 2000 and I am the Former Chairman of the Working Advisory Group (WAG) to ISO/TC204 WG6, "General Fleet Management". I also served WG6 as Rapporteur (General Secretary) from 1994 to 1997.

Hamby Hutcheson’s Specialties:

Telematics, Intelligent Transportation Systems (ITS), Advanced Traveler Information Systems (ATIS), Advanced Vehicle Information Systems (AVIS), Vehicle to Vehicle and Vehicle to Roadside Communications, On-Board Computers, Roadside Assistance, Metered Billing Services, Secondary Tow Services and Emergency Messaging Systems.
