Andrew J. Fry’s Summary

I joined the (UK) Department of Business, Innovation and Skills (BIS) Economic Development Division (EDD) on 1 October 2009.

Economic Development strengthens the UK's regional economies, reduces growth gaps and encourages business success through the utilisation of European and English regional funding.

I lead the EDD - Partnerships and Delivery Team responsible for the BIS partnership with the Government Office Network and the Northern Way, a grouping of the Regional Development Agencies responsible fo the North East, North West and Yorkshire and Humber region of England. I am the policy lead on Skills, Innovation, Enterprise, Employment, Environment and Rural Issues.

Over the last 4 years I led the British National Space Centre (BNSC) in the development of the UK Government policy and in the implementation & delivery of UK strategy on satellite telecommunications & navigation. I later oversaw the transfer of this work to the Technology Strategy Board.

I managed BNSC's satellite telecommunications and navigation programmes, with a particular focus on telecommunications projects including HYLAS and ALPHASAT - for which I led negotiations with the London Development Agency, South East of England Development Agency and East of England Development Agency to develop and secure a novel package of support worth €50m for the UK space industry to build the world's most advanced civil telecommunications satellite valued at over €500m. The Alphasat project secured over 500 highly skilled, high-valued added jobs across the UK.

I was responsibile for the telecoms & navigation preparatory action plan, that shaped policy for the UK's participation within the European Space Agency (ESA) at the Ministerial conference held in November 2008 and secured funding for the UK Satellite Telecommunications sector worth £100m over 5 years.
