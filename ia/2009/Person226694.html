Supriyo Chaudhuri’s Summary

The story of my career, spanning 15 years and played out in three different countries, was about experiencing and inducing change in organizations.

I have had the unique privilege of being a close participant and observer of the two recent waves of globalization. First, in 1990s, I was enabling companies go online and watched from close quarters the advent of Internet and the globalization of ideas; then in the new millennium, I worked on the frontiers of global trade in services and witnessed greater global integration of work and skills.

This experience made me what I am: open, to ideas and ways of thinking; multicultural and flexible, learning oriented and positive about change.

I have always loved what I did and sought out things to learn. My work was never mundane - it was always a combination of an adventure, an interesting education opportunity and a bit of fun.

And, I indeed had fun. I took offbeat turns and did many things, but always contributed positively. And, in turn, each job I held helped me a bit to become a better person, a better professional.

Now, I want to get involved in building a great organization, which will change the lives and thoughts of people and make them ready for the post-recession world. We are heading into an age which will scarred by the experiences of the current recession, but will be more global, with a multi-polar economy and based on truely integrated global value chain.

For this emerging reality, we shall need to realign organizational skills and view the opportunities with a fresh pair of eyes. I do feel that my unique, asymmetric experience has suitably prepared me for this oncoming Globalization 3.0.

Supriyo Chaudhuri’s Experience

  • Director - International Business

    Rutledge Joblink

    (Education Management industry)

    April 2007 — Present (2 years 2 months)

    I joined Rutledge because it presented me with the exciting possibility of working internationally, building a network of vocational training and recruitment outlets in different parts of the world. Since then, we have started or restructured our operations in India, Dubai, Philippines and Poland, and connected up with partners in many other countries. It allows me to do what I do best - seeing possibilities, innovating and building product and service portfolios to match the opportunity - within the training and recruitment space.

    I love the job, though this means endless hours of travelling and living out of a suitcase. I love meeting people in different countries, and engaging with them with respect and an open mind, leading to enormous amount of learning and an opportunity to see globalization from close quarters.

  • Sales Manager

    Course Source Ltd

    (Privately Held; Computer Software industry)

    August 2005 — June 2007 (1 year 11 months)

    I enjoyed my work at Course-Source, a small e-learning company based in London with loads of exciting, high visibility projects, like none before. I could use all my learning about selling e-learning, and worked extensively on database marketing and emails. I finally achieved consistent success in sales, which led to an expansion of my role, and soon I was doing what I am really good at - product innovations and building strategic partnerships.

    It was exciting time for me - trying out new marketing ideas, building a all new website, working on products and possibilities - all made even more worthwhile by the consistent sales success.

  • Consultant

    Learning Resources International

    (E-Learning industry)

    December 2004 — July 2005 (8 months)

    I came to England as a Highly Skilled Migrant, which meant, without a pre-arranged job. After a few months of freelancing, this was my first proper job - a hardcore sales job in a small, entrepreneurial, e-learning company.

    In the few short months I was there, my contribution in terms of actual sales was insignificant. But I opened doors on long term opportunities, bringing new, profitable long term accounts to the company. I worked on new products and ideas, and LRI was always full of those, and explored new sales methods in a highly competitive market.

    It was a difficult job, but I contributed, built relationships and learnt new things, not least how to pronounce 'Reading' and 'Southwark' in England.

  • Regional Manager

    NIIT Limited, International Education Business

    (Public Company; NIIT; Information Technology and Services industry)

    December 2000 — July 2004 (3 years 8 months)

    I went back to NIIT just before the party ended on Dotcom, exiting profitably and with my reputation intact. I experienced the contraction while in NIIT, managing its territorial expansion in Bangladesh. Amid the contraction, and an unstable political climate, this was never going to be easy. But I made friends and engaged with them with respect and an open mind, understood their issues and innovated on our standard offer - and eventually succeeded. Soon, I turned around the operation, achieving high growth defying the gravitional pull of the downturn. I introduced new products and built new partnerships, promoted the brand through new channels and cobranding opportunities, and soon established clear market leadership. This was a time when all my learning was adequately tested, and I learnt to steer a business through bad times.

  • CEO

    Netpro Training

    (Education Management industry)

    December 1998 — December 2000 (2 years 1 month)

    While I did well at NIIT, I found it hard to resist the temptation to participate in the internet era boom. The big companies like NIIT and APTECH were changing slowly, and there was an opportunity in the internet space, both in terms of training and new projects. I saw the opportunity, researched, prepared business plans and sought private equity, and left the job.

    I created Netpro, the first pure-play Internet training company in India, the first one to offer Certified Internet Webmaster [CIW] certification. We became one of Macromedia's first training partners in the country, when Flash was still a new concept. We engaged with large organizations to build their web presence. This was deja vu once again - I was again at the cutting edge of change, advocating a new way of doing things.

  • Business Executive

    NIIT Limited

    (Education Management industry)

    June 1997 — December 1998 (1 year 7 months)

    I thought I knew the trade as I joined NIIT from APTECH. However, I learnt the analytics side of business while in NIIT, looking at operations in detail and identifying and working on the key drivers of the business. I learnt fast and well, and was soon handling some of the most complex Partner accounts. I was appointing and working with franchisees, energizing a franchise chain with new ideas, activities and initiatives.

    So, I was the ideas person in the team, and my team became company's top performing team consistently during the period, at a time when the business was growing expoentially. I helped expand the partner network, innovating along the way who and how we engage with. I made a significant contribution to the company's success and it was well acknowledged.

  • Senior Sales Executive

    Aptech

    (Public Company; Information Technology and Services industry)

    April 1995 — May 1997 (2 years 2 months)

    I joined APTECH at a time of great change too, when the IT industry in India experienced its first wave of growth and consequently, the IT training industry grew - both geographically and in terms of the variety and depth of offering. Working for one of APTECH's regional offices, I had the chance to experience the change, with all its excitement. I participated in the launch of www.onlinevarsity.com, one of the first such attempts in India; Zed Points, first chain of neighbourhood learning centres; Arena, a specialist multimedia training brand; Hardcore, a training brand for networking.

    My stint at Aptech was exciting - in scope, ideas and reach - where I was coordinating the brand building efforts in the region, launching new products, handling major partner accounts and working in an expanding industry. I learnt to innovate, and handle complex situations and projects at Aptech - this was indeed my key takeaway.

  • Area Manager, Customer Service

    ICNET Limited

    (Telecommunications industry)

    January 1993 — April 1995 (2 years 4 months)

    Managed customer relationship, training and support for the first commercial e-mail service provider in India. This meant selling a new concept - email - at that time, and training the IT departments in various large organizations on installation and usage of email systems. Our clients included large Multinational organizations like British American Tobacco, Union Carbide, P&G, Honeywell, Castrol and Misui. This was my first job, which came with abundant exposure of implementing technology to induce change in the way the organizations work.
