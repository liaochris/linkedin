Nicola James’s Summary

Established Thomas Brooke International in 1993, a search firm serving senior decision makers at companies operating with lean organizational structures.

Things I try to do consistently:
*Ask good questions.
*Improve what I do every day, make my work better, smarter, more effective.
*Give more than I take.

I like meeting tough challenges and solving complex problems. Probably just as well.

Bring many manufacturing concepts to the executive search process, emphasizing impact of strong process and creative thinking on end results. Especially in the new economy, risk & cash management are critical issues and a key hire is a big investment. It had better be a good one!

Deming said that if you put enough into the first 15% of any project, you will guarantee at least 85% of your desired outcome. That holds equally true in hiring for a key role. And, root cause analysis and prioritization of issues are essential in complex hiring situations, where a role may be mission critical and have a large matrix of requirements. It’s essential to ask questions early, and understand the factors that influence the particular need, to inform decision points that come up later.
