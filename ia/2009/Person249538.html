Yann Le Tensorer’s Summary

I've started making video games in the early 80s, I was 11 years old. Two of them were later published on the Apple IIGS (Gate & SpaceFox). Then, I studied maths and computer science, and was first hired by Ubisoft in 1994 as lead programmer on Rayman. Also lead programmer on POD, Rayman 2, I co-developped at the same time some of the key engines and technologies for Ubisoft. I founded in 2000 my own studio, Tiwak, together with 6 co-founders, most of them from the Rayman 2 Team. Worked then as Executive producer on Tork, Prehistorik Punk, a platform/action game for xbox, with Microsoft Games Studios as client (game was published later by Ubisoft) while co-creating the Yeti technology, later used by multiple titles within Ubisoft (including GRAW, GRAW2, Beowulf)

By the beginning of 2004, the co-founders and myself sold Tiwak to Ubisoft.
Still at the head of the studio, I worked as producer on the title "Ghost Recon Advanced Warfighter" (the game won the UK Bafta "Game of the Year" and "Best Technology" awards); then, still as producer, on Beowulf, a game for Xbox360, PS3 and PC, based on the animation movie Beowulf produced by Paramount, directed by Robert Zemeckis.

I left Ubisoft in 2008 to start my own company again. My goal is to provide high quality consulting for video games studios woldwide, in the fields of technology, production, gameplay, human resources and management. My motto ? "Let's make better games !"

Yann Le Tensorer’s Specialties:

Building up high quality video game studios, managing and organizing big productions on multiple sites (up to 180 people), Starting new projects and brands (pre-production), Hiring top quality experts that match together, creating a professionnal and yet relaxed company culture, directing the creation of innovative technologies for games, negociating deals with publishers and technology providers, programming controls and cameras, game design, gameplay.
I speak fluent English, German and French
