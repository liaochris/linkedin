Andrew Gadomski’s Summary

Hello and thanks for reading my LinkedIn profile - my name is Andrew Gadomski (I go by Andrew).

I am the Northeast Advisor and Founder of Aspen. A truly unique consultancy firm, Aspen is the first staffing organization to focus purely on staffing strategies management, enabling its clients and partners to focus on execution of open positions.

After a diverse and successful career in staffing and branding, I founded the company in June 2006 so corporations that are socially and globally conscious can become great at recruiting talent and advance their value propositions exponentially. Aspen was founded on the principle that companies that do great things deserve to find great minds easily.

In 2008, I joined the faculty at NYU as an adjunct professor. In forwarding my desires to have great companies find great talent in the wake of this economy, our team has created curriculum that enables people to use online tools to advance their careers.

Andrew Gadomski’s Specialties:

The majority of my career has been spent evaluating and managing complex problems in staffing, branding, and technology. In talent acquisition, I managed projects across matrix hierarchies, transformed recruiting organizations from reactive to proactive, aligned recruiters from low value to high value work, and created measureable processes that are scalable and replicable. With lots of help of course.

  • Founder

    Aspen Search Advisors

    (Human Resources industry)

    June 2006 — Present (2 years 10 months)

    Aspen markets to the healthcare, energy, manufacturing, hospitality, and banking verticals, and drive recruiting efficiency companies with a commitment to social and global responsibility.

    Over the past few years, I have worked on great projects, and helped companies doing great things acquire great minds.

    In the northeast / west coast, we helped the largest producer of vaccines acquire more customer centric people who could educate doctors on their products. We installed talent pipelines, centers of excellence, and administered new sourcing and screening training.

    In Philadelphia, we helped a CNC machining company doing crafted work improve their workforce and keep it on shore. Installation of new positions, an ATS, and processes were critical.

    In Chicago, we helped recruit students to programs that teach how to start up and build your own business. We had to teach a team how to recruit, onboard, and use tools to find the right type of entrepreneur.

  • Manager of Staffing

    Honeywell Specialty Materials

    (Public Company; HON; Aviation & Aerospace industry)

    February 2005 — May 2006 (1 year 4 months)

    Honeywell was a step to opening Aspen. At Banister, I knew there was a more efficient way to recruit. My supply chain mind was in the way.

    Corporate recruiting was ready for efficiency, but with little F50 experience, it was going to be difficult to start up. With Banister’s blessing, I landed the top staffing job at the former Allied Signal chemicals division.

    With a great HR team, 70 people within 2 steps of the CEO were changed and our division piloted one of the largest RPOs ever. We dropped a few million to the budget by changing sourcing strategies and avoiding fees. We cut time to fill by 35%. We started with 2, and now 15 people staff chemicals. With the RPO in, the door was opened to start up Aspen.

    In 30 days, Aspen acquired its first client, and in 60 our largest. The relationships from Honeywell are strong, and the staffing team we started with had great people – all of which have gone on to fantastic careers in HR and staffing.

  • Director of Research

    Banister International

    (Privately Held; Staffing and Recruiting industry)

    September 2001 — January 2005 (3 years 5 months)

    Banister International is a global executive search firm located in Philadelphia, PA. While at the firm, I was an executive recruiter in the Supply Chain and Operations practice, and during the last 18 months, served as the head of research for the North American business.

    I arrived there after a few years in branding and marketing in Philadelphia. I went in for a candidate interview as an executive looking for a new job, as I thought they could place me. Instead, they asked me to join the firm.

    During my time there I worked closely with Patrick Sylvester, Jim Parker, and Ed Mamrak, who mentored me into a solid producer in executive search. After placing several dozen end to end retained positions, we started to look at the centralization of research and recruiting across the practices, and Patrick and Ed asked me to develop an internal research team to support all the practices. It was here that I started thinking about how to drive efficiency in recruiting.
