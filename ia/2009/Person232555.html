Thomas Langan’s Summary

Accomplished visionary Executive with experience in operations, P&L management, multi-discipline and single source project management, strategic business development, and technical design experience in small, medium, and large engineering companies. Results-oriented, decisive leader with proven success in new market identification, corporate cultural change, strategic thinking, and problem solving. Proven track record of increasing sales, growing new markets, and profitability. Tendency to thrive in dynamic and fluid environments while remaining pragmatic and focused.

Over 25 years of experience in the design of structural systems for new construction and the preservation of historic fabric on a variety of significant architectural projects, including government buildings, hospitals, college and university facilities, museums, theaters, churches, and residences. Have received many awards and have lectured on historic preservation and structural engineering. Possesses extensive experience in developing and refining structural designs for individual projects that benefits clients with the most appropriate, cost-effective structural system.

My work in preservation specializes in the assessment, repair, alteration, and expansion of older structural systems and building materials. Some notable projects include the restoration of the New Jersey Statehouse Complex, preservation of the Essex County Courthouse, the construction of the new Observatory Hill Dinning Facility at the University of Virginia, the new Beck Center for the School of Hotel and Restaurant Management at Cornell University, Lincoln Financial Stadium in Philadelphia, and the restoration of the Rosenbach Museum in Philadelphia, and the Count Basie Theatre in Red Bank, NJ.
