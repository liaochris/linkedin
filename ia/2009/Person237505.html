Tracy Cote’s Summary

I’ve seen tremendous shifts in how we find and nurture talent over the years. The digital space is the catalyst in this evolution, and I’m delighted to be in an industry involved in leading the charge of change.

After I graduated with a cum laude English degree, I found a position as HR Manager at American Stores. I dealt intensively with EEO compliance and diversity issues, which ingrained in me a deep respect for the importance of diversity in the workplace. From there I segued into the retail sector. As regional HR manager at Mervyn’s I created corporate policies, advised field HR and management on HR issues, and oversaw all of their employee relations for Northern California. Then in 1999 I got a call about a position at Organic.

When I first visited the Organic offices, there were people playing foosball and video games, working on desktops made from doors. It was a complete departure from what I was used to. I loved it. I helped develop and manage the Company’s first corporate human resources program. I was involved in Organic’s expansion, setting up domestic and international offices. After three years, I moved to Charles Schwab, and spent two years as a lead HR generalist for their Technology division.

In 2004, I was offered an amazing opportunity to return to Organic as the head of Human Resources. In my role as Executive Director of Talent at Organic, I direct all programs related to talent acquisition, learning and career development. I’m also responsible for local HR operations, performance management, HR information systems, and worldwide benefits and compensation programs.

While my HR career is currently focused in the digital sphere, my personal passion is, surprisingly, non-digital photography. I love the entire process, the play of light and shadow. I prefer portraiture, which means I focus on capturing the essence of a person. Which is quite a useful skill for a person who’s career is people-focused!
