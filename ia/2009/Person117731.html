  • Recruiter and Teacher (dual role)

    International School of Choueifat, Lebanon and UAE

    (Education Management industry)

    July 2007 — December 2007 (6 months)

    Worked for the largest school network in Arabia and started in the UAE HR Department as a recruiter of anglophone teachers (mainly Irish, British and American). Then in September they sent me to teach Ancient History and English in the Mother School in Choueifat, Beirut, which was located in a Druze village high up on a steep mountain overlooking the Shia suburbs and Beirut Airport. This area had seen a lot of fighting during the Lebanese civil war due to its strategic location and the school had been the wartime headquarters of the Druze militia leader Walid Jumblatt. It was quiet at night.

    The Maronite Catholic neighbourhood of Hadath, which was up another hill a mile or so away, was a good spot to relax, because there were no restrictions on leisure pursuits involving beer, wine and liquor. Many of the regulars in the bars there were fellows from the Hizbollah controlled Shia suburbs just below, who would come up to Hadath to drink beers, as alcohol was unavailable down there. So this kept the bars of Hadath humming and it was an encouraging sign that a cool Al Mazah (finest beer in the Middle East) can facilitate social intercourse between the different communities in Lebanon. If you wanted to go a bit further in search of other recreation, you could head to Christian East Beirut and try your luck in the chic nightclubs and casinos or knock back the Guiness in an Irish pub.

    It was a link to a bygone time because I had lived in Beirut from 1976-78 in the early years of the war, and my family went on holidays in the Holiday Beach in Nahr el Kalb (River of the Dog), Jounieh, as far back as the sixties. This stint in the UAE and Lebanon was valuable as I got to refresh my Arabic. However I cut short my stay in Beirut after the situation became tense due to a number of car bombings against anti-Syrian politicians which killed dozens, and at that time based on an old friend's recommendation I decided it would be a good move to join Huxley Associates.
