Brian MacDonald’s Summary

Experienced executive with over 20 years of international experience in sales, marketing and general management.

Brian MacDonald’s Specialties:

Carrier and OEM sales, distribution partnerships, mobile advertising sales, mobile content partnerships. Sector expertise in wireless carriers, handset OEMs, digital media and mobile advertising. Particular experience in the North American and European markets.

Brian MacDonald’s Experience

  • VP, Worldwide Sales and Distribution

    mywaves inc.

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Computer Software industry)

    August 2006 — Present (2 years 7 months)

    mywaves is a Menlo Ventures-funded startup staffed by senior executives from Yahoo, Napster, PayPal and Danger. Our company's mission to to bring web video entertainment to mobile phones worldwide.

    We launched a beta version of our service in December of 2006, and as of Q4 2008 have signed up more than 30 million users in 200 countries.

    I manage the team responsible for sales and distribution of the mywaves service. My role is to drive company revenues, primarily through mobile advertising and premium content sales. I am also responsible for establishing distribution relationships with major handset OEMs and carrier partners. Distribution partners include Vodafone, Verizon/Alltel, Nokia and Sony-Ericsson.

    For more information, go to http://www.mywaves.com.

  • VP, Carrier Sales

    (Privately Held; 201-500 employees; Computer Software industry)

    Ran the worldwide carrier sales team. Successes included T-Mobile (US), One (Austria), KPN (Netherlands) and Fido/Rogers (Canada).

    In February 2008, Microsoft bought Danger for approximately $500M.

  • VP, Marketing and Business Development

    iPass, Inc.

    (Public Company; 501-1000 employees; Information Technology and Services industry)

    Established bundling and co-marketing relationships with Lucent and Cisco.

    Successfully closed major buy/sell access agreements with Equant/France Telecom, AT&T, Deutsche Telekom, Swisscom and other carriers around the world. Negotiated and launched bundling and distribution agreements with Lucent and Cisco.

  • Managing Director, International Development

    Netcom Online Communication Services, Inc.

    (Public Company; 501-1000 employees; NETC; Internet industry)

    1995 — 1996 (1 year)

    Netcom was one of the first large Internet service providers (ISPs) in the world. I was responsible for rolling out our US-based business model to additional countries. Successfully launched new ISPs in the UK and Brazil during my time there.

    Netcom is now a part of Earthlink.

  • Managing Director, Consulting Group
