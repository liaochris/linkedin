Randy Harrison’s Summary

I am a field-tested, results-oriented Integrated Marketing strategist with track record of driving growth, competitive advantage and most especially sales and ROI for innovative products and services in highly competitive markets. Programs that engage F500/G2000 senior business executives are a specialty.

Over the years I have developed the requisite skills and agility to cross discipline, industry & role boundaries in order to apply the appropriate tools old and new to meet large-scale business objectives and challenges with predictable, consistent and measurable results. I also have strong negotiation skills and am known for delivering maximum value to clients at lowest cost.

As a professor of Marketing at Emerson College and in my new role as VP of Collegiate for the American Marketing Association’s Boston Chapter, I am now able to expand my personal mission to inspire, motivate and provide students with strategic tools and support they can use to add maximum value to their clients and employers in the years ahead, while also advancing marketing's role as a mission-critical business function.

I have almost completed writing a book called Godai: The Five Forces of Modern Marketing. Drawn upon work in the field and research in the classroom, I have extracted a set of five elemental principles that marketers and executives can use to mange risk and successfully meet core business challenges.
