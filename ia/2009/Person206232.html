Ryan Magnussen’s Summary

From NATPE Conference 08 -"A visionary entrepreneur in digital entertainment and advertising, Ryan Magnussen is founder and CEO of Ripe Digital, a media company that develops short-form, on-demand entertainment for broadband Internet, cable TV and mobile outlets. Since the company’s launch in September 2004, Magnussen has rolled out three distinct lifestyle brands – RipeTV (original programming for young men), OctaneTV (auto) and FlowTV (urban) – with the ability to satisfy young consumers and advertisers alike.

With over a decade of experience, Magnussen remains in the creative vanguard of an entertainment revolution that continues to shift control from the major networks toward the more consumer-focused Internet era. Magnusson pioneered RD’s “connective advertising” model that allows users to simultaneously watch programs with ads as commercial messages embedded in each show.

Prior to RD, Magnussen founded Zentropy, Inc., one of Hollywood’s first interactive ad agencies. Over a four-year period he built it into one of the largest interactive agencies in the U.S with annual revenues of over $80 million. Zentropy developed online platforms and strategies for such entertainment companies as Fox, MGM, Paramount, Warner Bros., Polygram, Universal Music and Virgin Records, while leading e-commerce and interactive advertising for other major corporate clients such as Microsoft, GM, Coca-Cola, Reebok and Procter & Gamble. By the time Magnussen sold Zentropy to Interpublic Group in 1999, the company had ten offices operating in six countries.

Magnussen developed Zentropy at USC’s Marshall School of Business, where he received top honors, including the Most Outstanding Student Award and Best Business Plan of the Year, as a 1995 graduate. Today, as an authority in digital entertainment and advertising, he has appeared on covers of numerous publications and been a guest speaker for trade organizations including OnHollywood, Digital Hollywood, Red Herring and NATPE.”

    1994 — 2001 (7 years)

    Ryan Magnussen founded Zentropy, Inc. in 1994, one of Hollywood’s first interactive ad agencies.   • Founder and CEO

    Vertical Addictions Inc.

    (Recreational Facilities and Services industry)

    April 1992 — November 1994 (2 years 8 months)

    Vertical Addictions helped pioneer the sport of Bungee Jumping in the United States. Founded by Ryan Magnussen during the spring of 1992 in Bolder Colorado, Vertical Addictions grew from one location to seven in the span of one year. These locations included three Hot Air Balloons, two cranes and two bridge jump spots with over 100,000 safe and successful jumps performed. Ryan and his brother Bo Magnussen once held the world record for the longest bungee jump off a bridge which was performed during the summer of 1993 at the Rio Grande Gorge Bridge in New Mexico with a 600 foot bungee jump.
