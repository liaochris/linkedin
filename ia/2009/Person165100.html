Mike Yuen’s Summary

Mike is responsible for new growth opportunities for games and services in the corporate services strategy and market development group at Qualcomm.

He previously led the BREW gaming group and developer relations and helped create a global ecosystem that has paid out more than $1 billion to content providers such as EA, Disney, and Sony as of March 2007.

Mike is a well-known pioneer, evangelist, and visionary in the mobile gaming industry. He is a champion for concepts such as cross platform design, wireless digital distribution, and emerging market consumer electronics platforms.

He has been quoted in publications such as the New York Times, Forbes, MarketWatch, Associated Press, Hollywood Reporter, GameSpot, Gamasutra, EDGE and MCV, as well as is a requested speaker at conferences such as GDC, E3, Leipzig, CES, CTIA, and the Milken Institute Global Conference.

Mike sits on the board of Zeebo and the advisory board of the LA Games Conference. He was previously a board member of the San Diego Software Industry Council, GDC Mobile, and MEF Americas. He has also been a judge for the IMGA and IGF awards.

Mike is an experienced veteran of gaming across multiple platforms and previously worked at Accolade (Atari), GTE Entertainment, and Hammerhead Entertainment (co-founder). Commercial titles he's worked on include Timelapse, Titanic (#6 best selling PC game in 1998), Star Control 4, Deadlock 2, Dust, and Redjack.

He has also worked at AMD as a product marketing engineer and NCR as a software programmer.

Mike holds an MBA from UC Davis and a bachelors in computer science from UC San Diego.
