Paul McManus’s Summary

Executive-in-Residence & Lecturer at Boston University's School of Management. In addition, I hold an appointment as the Director, International Programs for the Institute for Technology Entrepreneurship and Commercialization (ITEC).

Courses currently taught:

Competition, Innovation & Strategy (Req'd Undergrad & Grad courses)
Government, Society and the Global Economy (Grad elective)
Global Sustainability and the Integrated Ecosystem (Grad elective)
Inventing a Sustainable Future (Grad elective)
Entrepreneurship (Grad elective)
Entrepreneurial Management (Grad elective)
Norwegian Entrepreneurship Program

Over thirty years of experience in building, leading, advising and investing in high-growth international ventures within the high technology and venture capital industries.

Previously, a Venture Partner with Boston Millennia Partners, a $750M venture capital fund with international investments in early stage technology companies in the life sciences, healthcare, medical device, information technology and telecommunications industries for six years. Previously, he spent eight years as an international management consultant and fifteen years corporate venture development executive, product management and engineering roles in the high tech industry.

Paul McManus’s Specialties:

Strategy, innovation, technology commercialization and new venture creation in the energy/clean tech, ICT, healthcare, medical device, biotech, and pharmaceutical industries with specific focus on opportunity recognition & assessment, global startup strategies, entrepreneurial leadership & early team development and the venture capital process.
