Le Quoc Trung’s Summary

I took the national university entrance examination in 1997. I entered Hanoi University of Technology (HUT) - a multifield technological university having the largest scale in Vietnam. I spent 5 years studying computer science at the Faculty of Information Technology - HUT. Here, during my study-time, I made strong efforts in studying and conducting research projects to gain professional knowledge. Besides, I also had the opportunity to work as an on-the-job trainee and then as a collaborator at the Laboratory of Internetworking belonging to Department of data communications and computer networks. It was the great opportunity that provided me with many realistic experiences, especially in the field of computer networks and software engineering, under the supervision of experienced professionals and researchers who both guided and instructed me.

Immediately after university graduation, I wished to pursuit my postgraduate studies for having a teaching job and research; thus I decided to take the required examination to the master programme on computer science in Hanoi University of Technology in 2002. I carried out a master thesis in the subfield of software evolution with the subject of “Techniques, methods, and models in software evolution”. If I chose this subject, it is because software evolution is a new direction of research in sofware engineering not only in Vietnam, but also world-wide.

I have become very interested in further pursuing my study in the following fields:
• Software Engineering with such aspects as: techniques and models in software evolution, software cost estimation, risk analysis and management, software quality assurance, software configuration management.
• Advanced processes of software manufacture and management
• Advanced techniques and models in designing and analysing information systems, software systems…

  • Đại học Bách Khoa Hà Nội

    Bachelor of Science, Computer Science, 1997 — 2002

    My graduation thesis on “Z39.50 protocol in searching and retrieving information via inter-electronic libraries”.

    At that time, in Vietnam, the Z39.50 protocol was a very new direction and almost no large-scale library could offer a complete system of e-library based on Z39.50 standard. My mission was to study the model of the protocol for retrieving information from multimedia data warehouses. I had to simulate the process of data exchange among Z39.50 servers and Z39.50 clients by means of networked environment at the simplest level. Actually, connecting to Z39.50 illustrative servers provided by e-libraries through the world was necessary and practical in order to test whether client-side modules would run well or not. However, the difficulty was that I would have to pay for connecting to those servers. So, I had to build a Z39.50 server by myself. I succeeded in setting up Z39.50 servers written in C programming language, and interfaces of Z39.50 clients by using JBuilder.
