  • Senior Marketing Manager, Word of Mouth Marketing

    Intuit

    (Public Company; 5001-10,000 employees; INTU; Computer Software industry)

    February 2006 — December 2006 (11 months)

    As with most products and services, the purchase of Intuit's products are largely driven by word of mouth. As one of the first "official" word of mouth marketers at Intuit (in the Small Business Division), I developed a testing strategy to help us determine how to use word of mouth marketing as a lever. The testing strategy included projects that helped us engage with influential bloggers, harness offline word of mouth and learn how to scale an influencer network. In addition to the testing strategy, I built internal capacity around word of mouth through a series of Word of Mouth Marketing 101 presentations, the creation of the WoMWiki (a collaborative tool for sharing ideas internally) and the sponsorship of Intuit WoMMer meetings, which connected word of mouth marketers across the company.

  • Marketing Manager, Customer Acquisition

    Intuit

    (Public Company; 5001-10,000 employees; INTU; Computer Software industry)

    August 2004 — January 2006 (1 year 6 months)

    Intuit has a long and important relationship with accounting professionals, particularly in the QuickBooks division. As the marketing manager for accountant acquisition, I doubled the number of accountants using QuickBooks products year-over-year. With deep customer insights from switchers, new accounting practices and laggard accountants, I built a series of programs that drove a rapid increase in our accountant base. As more accountants use QuickBooks, they are more likely to recommend it to their clients, so these efforts also drove a significant increase in recommendations.
