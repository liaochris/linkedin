Ted Anthony’s Summary

Veteran journalist, news manager and multimedia content manager who has reported from more than 20 countries and extensively covered post-9/11 conflicts in Pakistan, Afghanistan and Iraq. As founding editor, launched The Associated Press' "asap" experimental alternative-storytelling multimedia service in 2005, worked with product specialists to promote and sell it, and helped spread innovative storytelling skills and principles around the AP news system. Leader and participant in AP strategic projects designed to leverage company's more market-focused approaches to newsgathering.

Specialist in long-form storytelling about changing American culture and author of cultural history book, "Chasing the Rising Sun: The Journey of an American Song" (Simon & Schuster, 2007). Also specializes in stories about food and culture, particularly Chinese. Nominated for Pulitzer Prize twice, in 1998 and 2001.

Frequent teacher and guest lecturer about journalism principles and techniques, including alternative storytelling, storytelling on deadline and using sense of place to make stories come alive. Has presented workshops at Poynter Institute's National Writers Workshop and dozens of state press association and Associated Press Managing Editors meetings across the country.

Ted Anthony’s Specialties:

Fluent since childhood in spoken Mandarin Chinese and written Chinese.

  • Author, "Chasing the Rising Sun: The Journey of an American Song"

    Simon & Schuster

    (Public Company; Publishing industry)

    2007 — 2007 (less than a year)

    Wrote narrative nonfiction book tracing the cultural history of the old American ballad "House of the Rising Sun," examining through interviews, primary-source research and memoir how a piece of culture moved through the 20th century propelled by technology, globalization and recorded sound. The New York Times praised the book for its "plenty of memorable adventures... (Anthony) momentarily transforms an ominous piece of Americana into a genuinely touching work of art." The Los Angeles Times called the book "a strange and stirring thing"; the San Francisco Chronicle said that "Anthony uses music as a tool for boring into history's rock, extracting valuable ore from heretofore untapped sources"; and Edmonton's Vue Weekly called it "one of the best reads this year." Ed Cray, author of "Ramblin' Man: The Life and Times of Woody Guthrie," said of the book: "Ted Anthony offers a journey into the soul of America."
