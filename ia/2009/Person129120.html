Keith Sharpe’s Summary

beyond the CV....

>> I was originally inspired and energised by what could be achieved on Macintosh computers at that time 1990 (Adobe illustrator a88, and Photoshop 1.0 & Quark 1.0 yuk)

>> Spent serious time learning all Graphic Design related software early 90's after hours, and weekends at WYSIWYG Design (http://www.wysiwyg.com.au) to the point where I was successful in creating illustrator files that would crash their best high end RIPs! These guys love their toys and this became a bit of a testing
ground for trying out new software and problem solve printing issues (before the public would send files using the new software)

>> I left WYSIWYG armed with a vast understanding of computers/high end scanning and albeit limited design experience (depite being called a senior designer, at my first job, go figure)

>> Raw Design was where I started a real design career. At my tenure here the company changed forms from Raw Design, to Choice Communications Group.I was fortunate to spend some very long hours getting an education in design from good designers. This company was ultimately taken over and re-created by Ron Dyer as a new version of Lunn Dyer.

>> An astounding bonus, working under some of the best designers in the country (Tony Lunn - responsible for The Qantas logo, the angular BHP logo, etc). To be exposed to this talent was an honour, and an experience I will never forget.

>> The experience at Lunn Dyer afforded me a fantastic grounding in a much higher level of design studio environment. Working with other older designers on projects for Westpac Banking Corporation, SOCOG (Lunn Dyer handled an enormous amount of work for the Sydney 2000 Olympics) I was fortunate enough to learn vast amounts about how a studio should function from Ron Dyer and Lucy Unger.

>> Left here to pursue my own goals of running my own studio based on all that I had learned from what I consider to be some of the countries best....
