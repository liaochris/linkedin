Travis Jordan’s Summary

I've always been interested in the lifestyle of being successful ever since I was 14, reading books on marketing, sales, psychology and various subjects. I always took notes on what I studied but never implemented on my new knowledge, until 2008.

My "real" (not working for the man) professional career started in 1999 working on web sites for indie bands/artist and moved onto professional celebrities around 2000 working as an intern for a well known artist at the time. From that moment I decided to focus on high profiled professionals and businesses as the exposure to the company brand was higher.

In 2007 I decided to stop designing web sites and took a break from the world and locked myself in study mode, focusing on marketing and sales. Studying marketing and sales material from Dan Kennedy, Dale Carnegie, Jim Rohn, Brian Tracy, and Napoleon Hill to name a few.

I got a little into real estate starting with Trump's material, then ventured into learning about foreclosures, mobiles and apartment properties by various authors.

I then realized the importance of psychology and reading through people, as being young I was tired of being vulnerable to older adults. I've studied everything from body language to NLP. I've studied Dr Paul Ekman, Albert Einstien, Richard Bandler, and Kenrick Cleveland.

With all this new knowledge, a lot of things were a lot more clear for me on how success is obtained and how to keep it. To this day I implement time management, goal setting, writing in a journal, taking action, outsourcing, and staying current with the latest knowledge and trends.

I have now transformed my company from a web design company to a full marketing and interactive agency.


My goals by October 2009:
*Publish a book
*Be a multi-millionaire
*Start STICKAD Foundation
