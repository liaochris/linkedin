Mark Stadhouders’s Summary

I have a keen interest in delivering the convergence of telecommunications, services, and mobile devices. As technology advances so do opportunities for creating a rich location aware experience in a variety of products. As a result, over the last 10 years I have initiated, developed, launched and marketed the latest content and services on mobile phones (SMS, MMS, and GPRS), Internet portals and recently on Personal Navigation Devices.

It is my ambition to take my leadership, commercial drive, and entrepreneurial creativity to the next level and lead new ventures, market or product introductions.

Mark Stadhouders’s Specialties:

Allround senior management experience in leading product development, project teams, marketing of content & value added services, and international business development.

Mark Stadhouders’s Experience

  • Managing Director

    GPS-Buddy

    (Privately Held; Program Development industry)

    June 2008 — Present (10 months)

    GPS-Buddy is an international provider of vehicle tracking and mobile data solutions for vehicle fleets. In close partnership with Garmin, the worldwide leader in navigation equipment, GPS-Buddy is at the forefront of easy-to-use business solutions for tracking, managing, monitoring, messaging, navigation and door-to-door routing. In 2007, the GPS-Buddy CONNECT won the SEMA award in Las Vegas.

  • Director Strategy & Corporate Development

    Navigon

    (Privately Held; Consumer Electronics industry)

    February 2007 — May 2008 (1 year 4 months)

    NAVIGON is an acknowledged player in the navigation industry. Its superb software and user interface powers brands like Becker. Their product line has been awarded with the Red Dot design award(s).

    I was responsible for initiating key Strategic Focus Points that supported the long term growth of the company, as well as delivered initiatives that gave an immediate edge over competitors. During this time I developed a sales strategy for the OEM software business unit, prepared a strategy for entry into the Tracking & Tracing market, took the lead in developing the connected navigation roadmap, was team member for www.skobbler.com, and launched an international sales expansion tool.

    Finally, I managed the online shop, special channels and B2B sales creating new ways to sell navigation devices outside normal retail. During this time I opened the eBay channel and prepared entry into tax-free shops at airports, online shop at Xing premium market place, and catalogue sales at Lufthansa.

    Extra course: German speaking and writing

  • General Manager Content & Services

    TomTom

    (Public Company; Consumer Electronics industry)

    November 2004 — November 2006 (2 years 1 month)

    TomTom is the fastest growing navigation company in Europe and the US. It was my role to expand the products with downloadable and real-time Content & Services.

    I was responsible for the business unit Content & Services, delivering location based services like realtime Traffic information, Weather forecasts, downloadable navigation voices, speed cameras, and maps. Managed a team of 16 including software engineers, content and product managers. Portfolio was launched under name TomTom PLUS in May 2005. As the first mass-market location based service platform, it paved the way for new innovative services like TomTom HOME and HD Traffic.

    Finally, I developed an innovative single SIM-card global data (GPRS) model that included negotiating with international wireless carriers and selecting a customer care & billing system. Results of that study are now being integrated into connected services.

    Extra in-company courses: Personal Efficiency Program (Time management) by Weesie Consult

  • Marketing Manager Services

    Orange Netherlands

    (Telecommunications industry)

    May 2001 — November 2004 (3 years 7 months)

    I started under the Dutchtone brand as marketing manager to expand the limited portfolio of consumer value added services. I launched and managed SMS, MMS, and GPRS (Orange World) services like ringtones, games, infotainment and online tools. I was also part of the re-branding team to change Dutchtone service portfolio into Orange, later acquired by T-Mobile.

    Extra in-company courses: Brand Management by Erasmus, Leadership by Krauthammer, Effective Negotiations by NCOI, Effective writing (see book The pyramid principle, Barbara Mint)

  • Business Development Manager

    Infospace

My role was to retain and increase revenues from existing operators like KPN, Vodafone, Ben and Dutchtone. I closed two renewal contracts with KPN and Dutchtone before taking a backpacking trip through Asia.

  • Product Marketing Manager Services

    T-mobile

    (Public Company; DT; Telecommunications industry)

I launched data services like SMS infotainment, business data services and targeted (youth) chatting and gaming services (based on the Danish Realtime platform). Being the first SMS entertainment platform in the Netherlands at the time, it generated over 40.000 messages per day.

The business unit was terminated by Sony Europe soon after its launch, having acquired more than 50.000 customers.

  • Financial Economic Advisor
