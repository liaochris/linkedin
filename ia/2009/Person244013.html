Virginia A. Williams’s Summary

[ Multi-Platform Project Management - Creative Content - Technical Expertise ]

Virginia leads creative and technical teams to create media campaigns that despite running on the best of 2.0 technology’s steam, feel people-centered, not cold or corporate.

Virginia strives to come up with new and unique ways to expand existing brand-awareness strategies beyond SEO and straight analytics. She helps clients overcome issues such as social media and cause fatigue, “opt-outable” brand messaging, and quantity vs. quality in the social network space.

As owner of the media communications firm New View Films, Virginia has had the opportunity to take on many roles related to account and project management: agency director, new media editor/writer and development director, among others.

Her clients include PR firms and ad agencies, as well as NGOs and government agencies such as: CDC, FDA, AED, Big Brothers Big Sisters, the Whitman Walker Clinic, and USAID.

Virginia is an Emmy™ award-winning producer and director of documentaries and series for broadcast. She's produced for Discovery Channel, TLC, Travel Channel, Animal Planet, NGT, HGTV, and PBS. She is producer and director of the independent feature documentary FRONTRUNNER, about the first woman to run for President of Afghanistan (2008).

She developed WomenRULE, a leadership toolkit utilizing films about women leaders as its inspirational core. As a fellow with the BAVC premiere Producer’s Institute for New Media Technology in 2007, she created Be a Frontrunner! -- a social network prototype for girls ages 9-14, that allows girls to learn media and leadership skills while contributing to their favorite social cause. More information can be found at www.womenrule.tv.
