Linda Danaher’s Summary

If you are tired of attracting the wrong customers, you may be sending the wrong message. The right message can make a big difference for your image... and your bottom line.
Your image influences customers, staff, retailers, distributors and suppliers. Image also affects a prospect's decision to use your product or services.
Whether or not we like to believe it, our decisions are based on emotions. Our emotions effect our buying habits and our business and personal decisions. At Danaher Design, we are experienced at tapping into the emotions that affect decision-makers in specific target markets to help our clients achieve their goals.

Linda Danaher’s Specialties:

Branding and corporate identity. Communicating difficult messages to a target market. Custom illustrations – traditional and digital for a unique approach.

Linda Danaher’s Experience

  • Danaher Design, LLC

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Marketing and Advertising industry)

    December 2003 — Present (5 years 3 months)

    Founded by Linda Danaher, Danaher Design relocated from New York to Northern Nevada in 2003. Our award-winning design solutions get results. We provide advertising, promotional design, corporate identity, custom illustration, website design, package design, and editorial services for a complete program.
    Our experience working with leading companies including, American Parkinson Disease Association, Avis, Bristol-Myers Squibb, Jones Lang Wootton, The NY Chinese Scholar’s Garden, Wyeth, Snug Harbor Cultural Center and Yellow Book USA gives us an edge in the use of design to achieve specific goals and build company image.
    Visit danaherdesignusa.com and view our portfolio.

  • Island Communications (Sole Proprietorship)

    March 1997 — September 2003 (6 years 7 months)

Client list includes: Wyeth, Bristol-Myers Squibb Company, Avis Rent A Car System, Inc., Jones Lang Wootton, USA, Yellow Book USA, The New York Chinese Scholar’s Garden, Staten Island Botanical Garden, Inc., Snug Harbor, Staten Island Historical Society and NY NJ Minority Purchasing Council.
