Nick Vasiliadis’s Summary

Financial Planning is much like sailing a boat. You can't really plan a trip on something so trechurous as an ocean without a proper plan and charting of a course. By definition a financial planner must collect data and analyze financial information from his clients, establish financial direction, make certain external market assumptions, communicate with clients and analyze their specific situations, teach the time value of money, prepare for college savings for the kids, manage risk for catastrophic loss through insurance, manage investments, minimize taxes, prepare for retirement, and minimize estate taxes through proper legal planning.
