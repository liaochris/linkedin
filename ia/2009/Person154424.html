Mary Dees Griffith’s Summary

Mary Dees Griffith began her career at CBS in NY. She entered the financial services industry in 1978 and since then has worked for leading financial and payment processing technology companies such as Merrill Lynch, Barclays Bank, Citicorp, First Bank System, First USA and Paymentech, in leadership and executive management roles.

During her tenure with these companies, Mary has brought numerous leading edge products to market both in merchant acquiring and card issuing. Her background includes extensive experience in executive management, as well as strategic vertical market development.

From 2000 to 2007, through the company she formed called creditranz, Mary facilitated many new payment technology products being brought to market. One of these is the innovative technology system called A-Claim.

In 2007, Mary joined BCBS of SC as President & COO of Preferred Health Technology to deliver A-Claim to the healthcare market. A-Claim is the patent pending, state-of-the-art, value-add payment & transaction processing system integrating real-time health benefits information, real-time claims adjudication & automated collection of patient responsibility payments designed to accelerate healthcare provider's accounts receivable collection.

Mary served on the ETA Board of Directors for 8 years and served in all officer capacities. She was President of the ETA from 2003-2004.

Mary has also served on the VISA US Commercial Product Advisory Committee, the MC US and Global Commercial Products Advisory Committees and the MC Acquirer's Committee.

Mary currently serves on the Board of Trustees of Women Networking in Electronic Transactions as Secretary. She also serves on the Board of Directors of Sterling Payment Technologies.

She has a BA in Broadcasting and Mass Communications from the State University of New York College at Oswego.
