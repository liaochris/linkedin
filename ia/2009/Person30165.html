Robert Fishkin’s Summary

Bobby came up with the idea for Reframe It while working on an academic project looking at marginalia in Shakespeare's work. Bobby was also a Special Advisor to Tomorrow's Europe, a project of the European Commission, & Special Advisor to the Center For Deliberative Democracy at Stanford University. He worked as a social entrepreneur and Fellow of the Richard Florida Creativity Group and Fellow of the Office of the Mayor of Baltimore, USA. He conceived and developed ACCESS Baltimore Arts Creating Community Energy and Social Solutions, a program which creates opportunities for at-risk youth and the mentors who work with them to attend cultural events together. He also conceived and developed the Books for Teachers program, which has created hundreds of classroom libraries in the state of Connecticut. A magna cum laude graduate of Yale College with a B.A. in Philosophy, Bobby is also a playwright who has been produced in London and New York. A documentary about the making of one of his plays is available at: Richard III (http://www.fishkin.net/bobby/richardiii/).

Robert Fishkin’s Experience

  • CEO & President

    Reframeit Inc

    (Internet industry)

    June 2006 — Present (3 years 3 months)

    reframeit.com

    Reframe It lets people comment in the margin of any webpage without the permission of the site. We let people discuss any content that matters to them specifically with whom they choose.

    This process creates virtual real estate that we monetize through advertising in the margin and by licensing use of our technology for private use. We incentivise the distribution of our technology by sharing revenue from the advertising with communities who recruit people to download the technology and to use it. We get distributed through mass-member communities, ad-networks and through channel sales.

    We make money through private licenses of our technology when there is information that needs to be communicated specifically and confidentially and when a company or organization can use our technology to let employees or focus groups convey this information more effectively than with other tools.
