Jonathan Stutz’s Summary

I have more than two decades of experience in corporate communications, investor relations and marketing of financial services, primarily for Financial Security Assurance, which Assured Guaranty acquired in July 2009. I helped establish the brand of what was then a young company building a financial guaranty franchise in the global asset-backed and municipal bond markets. I developed communications for all of FSA's important marketing and business initiatives, from its acquisition by US West and emergence as one of the leading Triple-A-rated guarantors through its IPO on the New York Stock Exchange and subsequent acquisition by the European banking group Dexia.

Along the way, I planned, wrote and launched the company's first website (and two subsequent redesigns) and wrote and produced all manner of strategic marketing materials, speeches, PowerPoint presentations, executive communications, press releases, by-lined articles and annual reports. I have managed media inquiries, written the narrative portions of SEC filings and executed solutions to difficult investor communications problems.

One of my particular strengths is writing clearly and effectively about complex financial concepts without "dumbing them down." I believe strongly that successful execution of any marketing or investor relations strategy depends on intellectually honest and appropriately transparent communications.

I came to FSA in 1988 with an MBA in Finance from NYU. I had previously run the corporate communications department of the Depository Trust Company (now DTCC), the central depository for marketable securities in the United States. My introduction to Wall Street was in the editorial department at Institutional Investor magazine.
