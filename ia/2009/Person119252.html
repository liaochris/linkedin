Jon Coifman’s Summary

With over 15 years experience in energy, environmental policy and public affairs, Jon is one of today’s most knowledgeable practitioners in clean-tech communications. He has worked with corporate, nonprofit and public sector partners to create solutions-oriented initiatives on energy efficiency and renewables, organization branding, carbon policy, and sustainable business.

He is an accomplished speechwriter, media strategist and spokesman who has appeared frequently on network and cable television, National Public Radio, and in print outlets ranging from the New York Times to People magazine. He speaks regularly about trends in environmental marketing and communications.

Before joining Waggener Edstrom in spring 2008, he was the media director at the Natural Resources Defense Council (NRDC), one of the country’s most respected environmental organizations, where his portfolio ran from Supreme Court cases and legislative advocacy corporate partnerships including Microsoft, Google, Bank of America, Wal-Mart and Toyota. He also handled top-tier celebrity media and worked closely with NRDC’s Environmental Entrepreneurs (E2) partner group.

Prior to NRDC, Jon was with the bipartisan Global Environment & Technology Foundation in Washington, D.C., working with companies including Johnson & Johnson and IBM.

Before that, he developed successful communications campaigns at Environmental Media Services. During that time he coordinated events with the White House, and headed the nongovernmental organization’s media operations at climate treaty negotiations in Kyoto, Japan; Buenos Aires, Argentina; and Bonn, Germany.

He has also worked at the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency, and served with two senior members of the U.S. House of Representatives.

Originally from Minneapolis, Jon has a master’s in public policy from the University of Texas and a bachelor’s in political science from the University of Wisconsin. He lives in New York City with his wife and son.
