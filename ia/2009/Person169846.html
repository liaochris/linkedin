Murali Devarakonda’s Summary

I have an insatiable desire to leverage technology to solve end-user problems. I am a die-hard entrepreneur & an activist at heart, a software engineer & a reformer in my soul, and a technology consumer & an informed citizen in mind. As someone who cares as much about the journey as the destination, I founded Agile Entrepreneurs, a self-help organization for under-privileged entrepreneurs working on hi-tech product startups. I am an active member of entrepreneur-oriented organizations like TiE, SDForum, SVASE, and SVPMA, and a recurring volunteer of TiECon (www.TiECon.org), the world's largest conference on entrepreneurship hosted by TiE. Since July 2007, I've been a member of the 'Entrepreneur Committee' on the Board of Advisors of SVASE.

I've taken the plunge as a startup founder twice. I worked on RideStation.com from 1999 till the 2001 collapse, and revived it in 2005 as a product of Crystal Ball Corp. amidst a widespread revival of consumer internet, consumer & business social networking, direct-response advertising on the web, software-as-a-service (SAAS), and green & clean-tech startups. I've learned several invaluable lessons in bootstrapping and the challenges of being a founder. Managing teams across multiple timezones on two continents and 5 cities, including engineering teams in India, over half-day away in time, 10,000 miles away in distance, and light-years away in *startup-maturity*.

Previously, I was also jointly responsible for one of the major successes of the internet in 2000/2001 - Immigrants Support Network, a national not-for-profit volunteer-only organization dedicated to helping legal immigrants fix a system full of "bugs". ISN had over 20 thousand registered members and over a quarter million supporters. Our efforts resulted in several legislative and regulatory reforms starting with the passage of the American Competitiveness in the 21st Century Act (AC21) in October 2000.
