Eric Jackson’s Summary

Ironfire Capital LLC is an activist, long-biased hedge fund. It utilizes proprietary selection models for identifying investments and social networking tools to help catalyze positive change at public companies which are target investments.

Jackson Leadership was founded in 1989. The consulting practice advises executive leaders and boards of directors on leadership, strategy, and governance.

In January 2007, Eric started the world’s first Internet-based shareholder revolt against Yahoo! He began his “Plan B” campaign on his blog and through YouTube videos to band together support from fellow individual investors disappointed by 3 years of under-performance. He built a coalition of 100 shareholders with a combined $60 million stake in the company (2.1 million shares). He was also able to build up support among the institutional shareholders and pension funds, so that 33% voted against the current slate of directors at Yahoo!'s June 12th annual meeting. On June 18th, Terry Semel resigned and Jerry Yang took over as CEO. The “Plan B” campaign has been covered in: the Associated Press, Bloomberg TV, Business News Network, BusinessWeek, The CBS Evening News, CNBC, Conde Nast Portfolio Magazine, The Financial Times, Forbes, The Globe & Mail, The Guardian, LA Times, The New York Times, Red Herring Magazine, Reuters, San Francisco Chronicle, TheStreet.com, TechCrunch, Time Magazine, USA Today, and the Wall Street Journal.

In July 2007, Eric began a 2nd "Plan B" campaign, this time targeting Motorola. CEO Ed Zander resigned in November 2007.

In February 2008, Eric launched Ironfire Capital LLC as a "hedge fund 2.0" platform, using social networking tools to gather information on and better conduct activist campaigns, while utilizing proprietary regression models for selecting which targets to pursue.
