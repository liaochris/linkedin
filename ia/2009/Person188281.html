Tim Pixley’s Summary

My first exposure to computing technology was in 1984 when I purchased my first computer while attending the University of Michigan in Ann Arbor.

While owning and running a small restaurant in the late 1980's, I became interested in writing programs to streamline operations. I got tired of slinging pizzas and decided that programming and systems analysis was more lucrative. So far, it appears as if I called this one right!

My skillsets have migrated over the years, from database programming and writing reports on an HP3000 to creating desktop and web-based multimedia applications that incorporate rich media such as sound and video.

The key element towards staying employed these days tends to be having a balance of business, communication, and technological skills. In essence, knowing the technology and hyping it is not enough...you must also have the ability to implement cost-effective solutions that will achieve a reasonable return on investment (ROI).
