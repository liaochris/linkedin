Mahesh Bhagwat’s Summary

Goal
To successfully transform the role of Police from that of primarily a law enforcement agency to an agency that also contributes “rescue, rehabilitation and re-integration of trafficking victims into society” with the help of other stake holders.

Contribution to prevention of Human trafficking
•Project aasara implemented in Nalgonda District of A.P. state in India helped in prevention of 90% trafficking.
•Was in-charge AHTU officer for the Hyderabad unit of UNODC project working in synergy with anti-human trafficking NGOs and Women and Child Development Department Coordinated anti-human trafficking activities for the entire state of Andhra Pradesh. Main responsibilities included mapping of traffickers, identifying vulnerable areas, coordinating with civil society organizations, training & capacity buildings of 22 District Police units & 4 Commissonerates, Prosecutors and Judiciary
oAs traditional law enforcement techniques were proving blunt to break the cycle of sex trade, my team set up a comprehensive intelligence/informant network across the state of Andhra Pradesh. This resulted in registration of 600 cases, rescue of 1000 victims including 125 minors, arrest of 1217 traffickers and 400 customers in a period of 8 months between January 1, 2007 to September 19, 2007.
•Successfully worked towards shifting the role of Police from that of primarily a law enforcement agency to an agency that also contributes “rescue, rehabilitation and re-integration into society”.
•Contributed significantly towards inculcating victim centered approach amongst Law Enforcement Officers as a part of UNODC training• I
