Richard Young’s Summary

I'm a freelance writer and editor with 13 years experience as a business journalist. As deputy editor of Financial Director, then editor and publishing director of Real Finance, I spent eight years as a leading commentator on, and observer of, board-level management – particularly financial executives. I've written features on subjects such as corporate IT, private equity, strategy, people management, marketing, branding, governance, banking, investor relations – pretty much everything related to business.
I now divide my time between feature writing for a range of business publications, editing books and magazines for corporate and public-sector clients and running training on Better Business Writing. I'm also an experienced conference chair and have hosted debates and round-table discussions for a number of business organisations including the Market Research Society and the FDs' Forum. Contact me via www.writerandeditor.co.uk

Richard Young’s Specialties:

Private equity, business technology, management, financial management, board-level decision-making, market research, the 2012 Olympic Games and Paralympic Games, intellectual property rights, flexible working, the future of work, corporate responsibility - but plenty of other areas, too.

  • Editor and publishing director

    Caspian Media Holdings

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Publishing industry)

    October 2000 — July 2006 (5 years 10 months)

    Launch editor of Real Finance magazine, the UK's largest circulation magazine aimed specifically at finance directors and other senior financial executives. In 2003, I became publishing director, taking a seat on the Caspian Publishing Ltd board and assuming partial responsibility for Real Finance's commercial activities.

  • Deputy editor

    Financial Director (VNU Business Publications)

    (Public Company; 201-500 employees; Publishing industry)

    February 1998 — October 2000 (2 years 9 months)

    After Business Computer World closed, I re-joined former BusinessAge associate Andy Sawers as his deputy editor on Financial Director, the longest-running magazine for UK finance directors. I worked on a variety of features, honed my interview style and got even more intimately involved in magazine design, feature commissioning and editing. As a result, when Sawers was seconded to a web-only operation for VNU in early 2000, I took over sole editorship of the magazine for six editions.

  • Business editor

    Business Computer World (VNU Business Publications)

    (Publishing industry)

    August 1996 — February 1998 (1 year 7 months)

    Business Computer World was aimed at IT decision makers in medium to large sized organisations, with an emphasis on connecting their technology decisions to business fundamentals. My role was to ensure copy had a distinct business flavour, but I also acted as news editor for the title.

  • Staff writer

    BusinessAge (VNU Business Publications)

    (Media Production industry)

    August 1995 — July 1996 (1 year)

    VNU bought BusinessAge from maverick publisher Tom Rubython and under the stewardship of Peter Kirwan assembled an excellent team of writers and freelancers to turn it from a writ-ridden scandal sheet into a UK version of Forbes. The plan didn't work, sadly. But as the most junior member of the team, I learned a huge amount about solid journalism, good writing and tight subbing before the plug was pulled. (Any connection between VNU's re-engineering of the ad sales department - including the closure of the training function - and the failure of BAge is entirely co-incidental. We just spent too much money on "content".) I wrote my first articles about the then-emerging "world-wide web", including an analysis of the potential for internet commerce, for BAge.

  • Senior sales executive, Personal Computer World

    VNU

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; Publishing industry)

    September 1992 — July 1995 (2 years 11 months)

    VNU Business Publishing London still had a world-class sales training facility in-house in 1992, allowing me to develop excellent sales, negotiation and presentation skills. After a period in display ad sales, I managed the MicroMart small ads section of the magazine (including an Internet section I started in 1993), then returned to display with agency and large business accounts. In less than three years, I closed over $3m-worth of ad sales.
