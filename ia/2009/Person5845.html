Fabio Akita, (a.k.a. AkitaOnRails)’s Summary

Even though I realize that number of years don't account for anything, I've been working in the field for more than a decade and I've fortunately accumulated a dependable skill set.

I am specialized in 'diversity'. Which means that I have free pass through a wide range of technologies. I am a skillful Java, .NET programmer. And I have experience in several other platforms such as ABAP/4, PHP and even some considered commercially "exotic" like Python and Ruby. I believe in well architected solutions, whichever platform the customer is willing to choose.

In 2006 I published my first book, called "Repensando a Web com Rails", the first Ruby on Rails book published in Brazil, exclusively to the brazilian audience. I bet everything on a technology that I considered that had great potential since late 2005 and I think it paid off. I am one of the most prolific Rails evangelizers in Brazil right now.

I am most interested in researching, realizing new and interesting solutions, coaching programmers, architecting solutions and coordinating development teams. I'm also a certified PMP project manager and I was responsible for a few projects that fortunately delivered very well.

As a project leader I am also very interested in everything related to Agile Processes. I understandthe queerness of processes such as CMM and RUP and I 'd rather work toward efficiency, not paperwork. This doesn't mean that I simply ignore the 'status quo', otherwise I wouldn't be able to deliver as efficiently as my customers always liked. Either Scrum or XP (or both) are much better toward real productivity and team work.

That said, I know that HR people expects some kind of ad hoc 'list' of key words, so here goes: Java, J2EE, ASP.NET, Ruby on Rails, PHP, Perl, Python, SQL Server, Oracle, MySQL, Eclipse, Core J2EE Design Patterns, UML, XP, CMMi, PMI, TCP/IP, HTML, XML, CSS, SAP, ABAP, Webmethods, Business Connector, Jco, Apache, Jakarta, Tomcat, Struts ... and so on and so forth.
