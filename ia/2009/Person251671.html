Zoe Marmara’s Summary

Every CV tells a story. Granted. I prefer to tell you this story myself.

“We are the people we meet”, a French actor told me once during a magazine interview. It took me time to realize that he was right. In an average lifespan you are expected to meet more people than your grandparents met in their lifetime. Each one can, and will, profoundly affect you. I was lucky to meet many inspiring people, most of whom you will find among my Connections. Had I not met them, I know I would have been an entirely different person. I'm extremely lucky. In 1999, right after I earned my diploma in Multimedia, I was spotted by Andreas Tsourinakis, leading video games journalist, who recommended my work to Yiannis Patrikos, director of the newly founded department of On-line Services at Compupress. Patrikos was in many ways the force behind “PC MASTER”, Greece's premier pc games magazine. Thus, it didn't take much to find myself compelled by the editors' room, a room that in my eyes hosted people of supreme significance. Nearly everything seemed a little out of proportion at Compupress. In a similar manner my mockups for the company's web sites transcended the same sense of scale. I realized then that as a web designer I ought to see a world of shapes. Instead, I was looking at a world of words. Immediately I sought my concerns in the world I saw, while still committed to the dynamics of the visual ground. I wrote many articles for consumer technology, computer and cinema magazines and created even more web sites and CD/DVD applications. Seeking to retain a better understanding of both, I attended drama and art school. Superficially, at the least, I left Compupress and founded my own web design agency in 2008. Honesty and inspiration are my personal values. I believe life has to be like colours: Intense and so vivid that “it achieves a palpable existence in its own right”.

And I believe CV s have to be like portraits: They have to tell tales of the presence of a personality.
