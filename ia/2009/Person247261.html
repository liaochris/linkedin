William Bramhill’s Summary

My background is in daily newspaper journalism, PR and marketing. I am a volunteer transport campaigner and helped to lead the team which won £4.2m cycling town status for Colchester.

I began my career as a copy boy before becoming a reporter then subeditor. I rose to become chief subeditor of the daily Colchester Gazette, a post I held for nine years. I am currently a casual subeditor on The Sunday Times and the Guardian and have launched Birch Leaf Books to publish people's family histories.

All my roles since 1995 have included using content management system to update websites; I was launch editor of the This Is Essex web site (joint venture between Newsquest and Northcliffe). I am especially interested in the development of communication via Facebook, Twitter and You Tube.

My time out of journalism was spent at a London PR agency where I organised events for:
• Polaroid (launch of camera in Scotland by senior MP Malcolm Rifkind)
• Prowting (meet and greet for south-of-England homebuilding company aiming for a public listing, as well as local press conferences)
• West and Welsh (annual sales conference for Cardiff double glazing company, presented by Raymond Baxter of Tomorrow’s World), and
• Hussmann Margaux (TV news event showing latest developments in supermarket freezer technology).

My PR and marketing skills have been put to good use with Colchester Cycling Campaign, where I have helped to organise and run conferences and consultations with Colchester and Essex councils; these included a cycling towns officers’ conference, where I spoke on improving relationships with cycle campaigners. I have also given TV and radio interviews.

Throughout my career I have developed a wide general interest, including science and technology, foreign affairs and environmental issues.

I am educated to A-level standard and hold the NCTJ journalism proficiency qualification. Wide vocational training including management and administration topics.
