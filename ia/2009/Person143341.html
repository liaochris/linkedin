Lonnie Helgerson, CFE’s Summary

Lonnie has over twenty three years in the franchise industry working with companies such as Computerland, MicroAge, Super 8 Motels and many more. Recognized as the pioneer in the mobile computer service franchise sector, Lonnie was the founder of Computer Doctor the first franchise of its kind and two other technology franchise chains; Expetec Technology Services & InstantFX Web Services.

Currently serves on:

* Elected to the International Franchise Association Board of Directors starting in February 2010.

* Chairman of the VetFran committee of the International Franchise Association.

* The Institute of Certified Franchise Executives Board of Governors.

* International Franchise Association Franchisor Forum.

* Board of Advisors of The International Institute of Franchise Education, H. Wayne Huizenga School of Business and Entrepreneurship, Nova Southeastern University.

* Board of Advisors of the Rosenberg International Center of Franchising, Whittemore School of Business & Economics, University of New Hampshire.

* Board of Directors of the Franchisor Association of Florida.

* Board of Advisors and Strategist to Ident-A-Kid Services of America.

He is also a frequent speaker and facilitator for the International Franchise Association, Franchisor Association of Florida and many other various franchise and business seminars. He has also served as a member of the Board of Advisors for the Institute of Franchise Management graduate school, University of St. Thomas, Minneapolis, MN and is a three time graduate of their MBA program for Franchise Management. In 2002, he became a designated CFE (Certified Franchise Executive). Throughout his career, Lonnie has been featured or written about in many books, magazines, newspapers, TV and radio including USA Today, Wall Street Journal, Entrepreneur Magazine, CNBC Squawk Box, Your World with Neil Cavuto and Paul Harvey.

Lonnie Helgerson, CFE’s Experience

  • CEO

    eMed-ID Franchising, LLC.

    (Privately Held; Health, Wellness and Fitness industry)

    September 2008 — Present (1 year 3 months)

    eMed-ID™ franchisees provide a wearable wristband USB device to consumers that contain their medical history. The USB device may be given to a doctor in lieu of filling out all of those pesky medical forms, given to a pharmacist for a list of meds, or for first responders in a medical emergency. In addition the consumer receives eMed-ID™ Caregiver Cards that can be given to law enforcement should a loved one go missing. The eMed-ID™ Caregiver Cards were developed specifically for usage with the new Silver Alert system. Much like an Amber Alert, the Silver Alert is issued for anyone eighteen years or older. The entire eMed-ID™ package is priced at $70 and includes the eMed-ID™ USB Wristband, (4) eMed-ID™ Caregiver Cards, (2) eMed-ID™ Refrigerator Cards and a bound report. Franchisees provide the eMed-ID™ program at retirement communities, health fairs and a variety of other venues.

  • Chief Brand Strategist & Board of Directors

    Ident-A-Kid Franchising Corp.

    (Executive Office industry)

    February 2007 — Present (2 years 10 months)

    Founded in 1986, Ident-A-Kid Services of America, Inc., is the pioneer in child identification. Through nearly 300 franchise locations, Ident-A-Kid annually provides over three million Immediate Response Cards to parents at schools and other locations throughout the United States. The Ident-A-Kid Immediate Response Card is a driver's license-like card that contains a child’s photograph, fingerprint and description. Parents keep the card with them at all times and, in an emergency, can provide law enforcement with it for immediate response.
