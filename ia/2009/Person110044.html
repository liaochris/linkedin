Jenny G. Bowen’s Summary

My undergraduate concentration was incorporating multi-media & digital arts into the theatrical experience with the broader query of examining how technology and media interacts with & thus alters the theatrical experience of life. I also studied documentary film and photography extensively in my liberal arts & humanities background.

While in college I was engaged in extensive policy roles by interning in the US Congress, being elected the Student Body Vice Pres. & President of my University, & serving on multiple boards and committees.

Post graduation I enrolled in the AmeriCorps VISTA program and was placed in Reading, PA at a private college tutoring elementary & middle school students while creating an after school arts community. My time in the program was cut short due to sharing beer with a twenty-year-old friend & student at the time. My position at the college was terminated & I was prosecuted to the full extent of the state law. In losing my position I also lost my housing, but was confined to the city on bail. I consider my time being technically homeless & completely impoverished in one of the most dangerous crime-ridden cities in America to be one of the most beneficial experiences in my life. Going through the absurd court system with public defenders and forming life-long friendships with struggling people in a lawless land taught me a great deal about humanity, myself, & the divided forces of America.

I returned to my hometown of Asheville in 2005 where I have since dedicated myself to the causes of sustainable community, creative culture, & bridging the arts with sciences for the benefit of human endurance.

Currently I am focused on a community enrichment project and portrait documentary: <a href="http://www.facesofasheville.com">Faces of Asheville,</a> which is in itself an inspiring story.
