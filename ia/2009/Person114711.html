Joan Blaas’s Summary

Currently I am heading up the Mobile Division at Goal.com. I am part of the Management Team and report directly to the CEO. Previously I have worked for Ericsson as Program Manager managing the global SAP implementation.

Four years ago I decided to change my career and I joined a start-up in the Mobile Arena, MobzMedia. At MobzMedia I have been working mainly with Football. Initially launching a football application for the FIFA World Cup 2006. Later on launching mobile internet services in corporation with Goal.com. At the end of 2007, the CEO of Goal.com asked me to join the organisation.

Goal.com is the largest independent football website in the world (Alexa #350) with over 13million unique users online and over 2million users on Mobile. Goal.com has 22 different editions an editorial team of ~450 writers. Goal.com has more traffic than Sky Sports or UEFA & FIFA together.

In 2006 I initiated the Goal.com Mobile internet site which went live in the summer of 2007. Goal.com Mobile is the largest mobile football website in the world and has grown in 2 years to over 60million page views per month (without any external marketing budget).

This growth was established through distribution partnerships with Mobile Partners and Operators. I closed content distribution/licensing and/or barter deals with: Yahoo, Verizon, AT&T, Vodacom, Vodafone, ZAIN, Bouygues Telecom, Orange, Turkcell, Arvato, BlackBerry, Nokia, Handmark, BBC, Mobage Town (Japan) and many more.

The initial business model of Goal.com mobile was based on Mobile Advertising. Beside or own direct sales I set-up Mobile Advertising agreements with Admob, Millennial Media, Quattro Wireless, Screentonic, 4th Screen Media, Ring Ring Media, Smaato, Nokia, Buzzcity etc.

As part of the UEFA Euro 2008, Goal.com launched premium mobile download services. In 2009 we decided to continue this pilot and launch SMS Football Alerts, Football Quizzes, Premium Wallpapers, and Ringtones etc.
