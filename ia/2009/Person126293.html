KAREN KANE’s Summary

Karen Kane has 20 years of experience advising Fortune 500 CEOs and senior executive teams in all aspects of corporate affairs. Her skill and judgment have helped clients create effective communications in the face of some of the most difficult business and national crises– including environmental emergencies, the high-tech bubble and 9/11.

As former Federal Reserve board secretary, Karen is experienced in directing and executing the board nomination process, planning agendas and refining background materials, and managing meetings to ensure maximum board effectiveness. She has successfully helped clients identify vulnerabilities, develop pragmatic plans, and craft credible responses in order to create relevant shareholder communications.

Recruited to serve as the highest-ranking and only female senior vice president for the Federal Reserve Bank of Chicago, she served as a change-agent, re-engineering the department, reducing staff and increasing impact—as well as recruiting and on-boarding her successor.

From managing a department of 25 reporters, copyeditors and designers as the Lifestyle editor of the Houston Chronicle, to re-engineering the deployment of 85 people scattered within a dozen departments at the Federal Reserve Bank of Chicago, she has demonstrated success time and again at managing budgets for maximum impact.

Identifying a need among boards of directors to develop strategic, thoughtful governance communications programs, Karen founded Karen Kane Consulting. She has published articles and editorials in The Corporate Board Magazine, Directorship and the Houston Chronicle, and also led a broad-based study of board communications among the Fortune 50. Her most recent thought leadership focus is in helping boards to use governance communications as a risk-management tool.
