Kathy Schneider’s Summary

I have a solid 20+ years of administrative/operational experience in the construction and restoration industries that I am beginning to put to work in a different way. Through the last 5 years of working with large loss catastrophe's, I have learned a lot about working remotely. And multi-tasking (to say the least). I am now offering administrative and logistical support, on-site or off.

Today's economy (or lack thereof) is teaching us new ways of doing business, of finding ways to cut costs and use our resources. Working with a virtual professional allows you to have a high level of support, without the normal costs associated with an employee.

I recently came across a quote from a Virtual Assistant that stated, "You can't use yesterday's methods on today's jobs, and be in business tomorrow". (Props to Tiffanie Tillman) Take a moment to think about that, and know that there are alternative ways to position yourself and your company that will put you ahead of the curve in today's new market of companies trying to recover from the economic downturn we have been in.

Working with a virtual business professional is not a new industry, but it is quickly becoming an accepted and alternative method of doing business. Entrepreneurs, small businesses, writers, coaches and sales people have been utilizing this resource for years. VA's are more active than ever before, they are quickly becoming a vital asset in the global workplace that we live and work in today. Our availability and the speed at which we can now communicate allows us to basically perform almost any project or task, just as if we were in the office down the hall. The difference is, with a virtual assistant, you have removed all overhead costs of maintaining an employee. You are paying for the work you need completed. Period.

So, think about it and if you would like to talk with me further to formulate an action plan that will work for you, I am available for a consultation.
