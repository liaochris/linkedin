Jag Gill’s Summary

Currently working as a Technical Design Authority at Capita IT Services delivering TDA support to New Business activity, whether at Pre-ITT stage, Due Diligence, Pre-BAFO and post service win to deliver transformation and integration services.

Was previously working as an Lead Infrastructure Architect across a range of technologies and platforms including Wintel, Linux, and Unix. Having worked on large cross technology architectures including all of the above, SANs, NAS', Networking - LANs WANs MANs, Backups Databases, Oracle RAC, CRM, and many others.

I've worked my way up (the traditional hard way!!) through the ranks since 1997 from a second line helpdesk analyst for Coca-Cola which gave me a good appreciation of very large company infrastructure and how it all worked together - has proved an invaluable experience on many occasions since.

Recent years have seen me specialise in the Criminal Justice and Central Government arena having delivered major infrastructure projects for The Law Society, Home Office, Police Information Technology Organisation (PITO), 6 Police Forces, information exchanges for Criminal Justice IT (CJIT) and Department for Constitutional Affairs as well as being involved in defining, designing and delivering the infrastructure for the Department of Constitutional Affairs.

Jag Gill’s Specialties:

Enterprise Level Architecture, Disaster Recovery and Business Continuity, Wintel Servers, Unix Servers, Linux implementations, Clusters, High Availability Infrastructure, Resilient systems, split site and failover and many many more.
