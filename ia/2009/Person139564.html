Lena Blonsky’s Summary

I was born in London and moved to Athens with my family and attended St Catherine's British Embassy School in Lykovrisi. However, I moved to the UK for secondary and high school where I attended St Stephens College boarding school.

I consider myself fortunate to have grown up both in Greece and in the UK and especially for having grown up amongst a multi-cultural environment.

So far my life has been a path of self-discovery through a variety of professional experiences, sometimes out of necessity, sometimes out of desire and oftentimes circumstantial.

A false start in my early years lead me to discover my 'true calling' at the wonderful age of 20 when I first started studying interior design in 1988 with the Sheffield School of Interior Design, attaining the Professional Interior Design Certificate in 1990. And this while working on board cruise ships with my husband George Apistolas, (see: http://www.linkedin.com/pub/9/651/7a7) a cruise ship captain.

I ‘retired’ from cruise ships when we started our family and began building our first home in Athens, Greece. The experience taught me enough, but also that I wanted to learn more about the architecture/construction of interior spaces. So I enrolled with Rhodec International in the UK and am approaching the end of the Higher National Diploma in Interior Design.

While studying I have completed several interior design projects as a freelance designer. However, I am also open to collaborating with local and international designers and architects.

Lena Blonsky’s Specialties:

Creating fabulous colour schemes that match the interior setting, style and function and enhance the positive and, if necessary, camouflage the negative elements bringing harmony to the whole and adjoining spaces.
