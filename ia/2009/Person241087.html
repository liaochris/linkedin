Valerie Fitzgerald (valerie@valeriefitzgerald.com)’s Summary

Over the last nineteen years, Valerie Fitzgerald has built a multi-million dollar real estate business in the greater Los Angeles area.

Recently she's completed her first book, Heart & Sold: How to Survive and Build a Recession-Proof Business.

The title will be released May '09. The book can be ordered through the link below or at Amazon.com keyword search: Heart and Sold.

Valerie is ranked among Coldwell Banker's Top 10 agents nationwide, and has earned the recognition of the Los Angeles Business Journal for the highest sales volume in L.A. County for residential real estate.

She was also recently nominated for the publication's 13th annual "Women Making a Difference Award”. In 2001 she was awarded the Women of Achievement Award by the Big Sister’s Organization of Los Angeles. From 1996-2006 she has consistently been the #1 Top Producing agent in Beverly Hills.

Valerie has also established her own charity foundation, she speaks around the country at numerous business conventions, and has appeared on the television show "Entertainment Tonight" as their real estate correspondent and MTV's "Cribs” among others.

  • Children Uniting Nations

    (Non-Profit; 201-500 employees; Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    January 1996 — Present (13 years 3 months)

    Everyone in the life of a foster child is paid to be there and with a revolving door of homes and families, it's not hard to see why these children deserve something more. Children Uniting Nations (C.U.N.) began in 1996 with the goal of making it easier to adopt foster children by improving foster care and adoption laws. C.U.N. has also created a permanent mentoring program in order to help these children bond with a caring person who many be the only constant in their lives. It has been shown that 90% of of at risk children who have mentors stay in school, their overall performance at school improves, their self esteem improves, they are more cooperative in their foster care home and develop better skills when interacting with other children. The results are extraordinary!
    The mentoring program began after the success of International Day of the Child. Day of the Child is an annual event that gives homeless and foster children a day of fun and compassion.
