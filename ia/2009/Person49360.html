  • CEO and Founder

    Camara Education

    (Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    August 2005 — Present (3 years 7 months)

    Camara (www.camara.ie) is a West African word that means "one who teaches with experience". Camara is also the name of a registered Irish Charity that was set up in 2005 driven by two core beliefs:
    1. Education is the key for Africans to lift themselves out of severe poverty;
    2. ICT can be harnessed to effectively enhance the African education system.

    Camara collects and refurbishes used computers that have been donated by Irish organisations and individuals and uses this equipment to establish computer labs in schools and colleges across Africa. Camara also develops open source educational software and delivers ICT training to the schools receiving computers.

    Camara is head quartered in the Digital Hub in Dublin's inner-city Liberties area and has regional centres in Belfast and Tralee. In Africa it is head quartered in Nairobi, Kenya and has centres in Ethiopia, Lesotho, Uganda and Kenya. It also supplies computers and ICT training to Zambia, Tanzania and Rwanda.
