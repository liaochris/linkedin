Gus Venditto’s Summary

I made the transition from magazines to online publishing in 1998, gaining experience with all types of interactive publishing models.

Gus Venditto’s Specialties:

Broad experience with interactive techniques and targeted marketing. Specifically, building online businesses, including educational offerings, lead-gen marketing, display advertising, and subscription services. Experienced in project management, technical development, ad sales targeting and budgeting.

Gus Venditto’s Experience

  • General Manager, Internet.com

    WebMediaBrands Inc.

    (Internet industry)

    February 2008 — August 2009 (1 year 7 months)

    Internet.com is a network of more than 80 websites and communities serving software developers and IT managers. It was visited by more than 12 million unique visitors/month. I left in August 2009 when the division was sold to Quin Street.

  • Editor-in-Chief

    Jupitermedia Corp.

    (Online Media industry)

    January 1998 — January 2008 (10 years 1 month)

    Worked on all phases of building Internet.com into a network of more than 80 websites, including assembling freelance writers and fulltime editors who have created a library of more than 2 million original article; website development and planning, including company-wide initiatives to improve our technology platforms. We cover a broad range of topics and reach more than 15 million unique visitors per month with over 100 individual newsletters and Websites, ranging from Internet.com to DevX to Graphics.com and many more.

  • Editor-in-Chief, Internet World magazine

    Internet World magazine

    (Public Company; 51-200 employees; MECK; Publishing industry)

    January 1997 — January 1998 (1 year 1 month)

    Responsible for the content published in Internet World, a monthly newsstand magazine with a circulation of 400,000.

  • Adjunct Professor

    NYU School of Continuing Education

    (Educational Institution; 5001-10,000 employees; Primary/Secondary Education industry)

    November 1996 — May 1997 (7 months)

    Created and taught a course on Interactive Publishing within the professional publishing program at New York University School of Continuing Education.

  • Freelance writer

    Self-employed

    (Self-Employed; Myself Only; Writing and Editing industry)

    April 1995 — December 1996 (1 year 9 months)

    I wrote for a number of magazines, helped launched "CD-ROM Advisor" and finished 6 books, on the use personal computers and the Internet (eg., "The AOL Money Guide," and "Best Uses for Your Computer").

  • Various editorial management jobs

    Ziff-Davis Publishing CO.

    (Privately Held; 501-1000 employees; Publishing industry)

    September 1984 — April 1995 (10 years 8 months)

    Started at PC Magazine when it was going through a rapid expansion. Over the next 6 years, I was involved in most every aspect of PC Magazine's editorial growth, including the launch of its first online service (PC MagNet, a bank of modems offering free downloads). Eventually, I was Executive Editor responsible for one-half of the magazine's content operation. When I left PC Magazine in 1992, I was asked by Ziff-Davis to create the editorial plan for a new magazine. We launched Windows Magazine in 1993. The magazine went from concept to first issue in less than six months. That first issue was 420 pages.

  • Managing Editor

    Associated Business Publications

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Publishing industry)

    August 1979 — August 1984 (5 years 1 month)

    Did a little of everything at this small trade magazine publisher. "Everything" included proofreading page layouts to writing cover lines to writing features. It was a great introduction to all phases of the magazine business. Our publications were in several markets in the food-service industry including health foods and retail chains.

  • Director Foreign Rights

    Scott Meredith Literary Agency

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Publishing industry)

    September 1977 — August 1979 (2 years)

    Represented the works of agency clients to foreign publishers, including Norman Mailer, Arthur C. Clarke, Henry Miller, and Carl Sagan..
