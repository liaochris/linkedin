  • Device Modeling Consultant

    Boeing

    (Public Company; BA; Aviation & Aerospace industry)

    May 2009 — August 2009 (4 months)

    Device Modeling Consultant: Boeing Corporation, Seattle, WA.

    - Cryogenic modeling of IBM's 7HP SiGe process down to -180 oC for analog circuits in aerospace applications.
    - Development of test structures.
    - Involved in infrastructure set-up such as installing the IBM 7HP kit and interfacing it with Spectre.
    - Extracted parameters for CMOS devices of various sizes using the BSIM3v3 model.

  • Device Modeling Intern

    National Semiconductor

    (Public Company; NSM; Semiconductors industry)

    May 2006 — September 2006 (5 months)

    Spice Modeling Group, National Semiconductor Corporation – Santa Clara, CA.
    Mentor – Dr. Pascale Francis.

    -Worked on implementing the industry standard PSP MOS model for one of National Semiconductor’s advanced CMOS technologies.
    -Involved in setting up the infrastructure required for using the model such as setting up IC-CAP and Spectre for Linux environment, installing SimKit etc.
    -Characterization of devices using probe station, parameter analyzer etc.
    -Evaluation and parameter extraction performed for DC, CV and over temperature (-50 oC to 150 oC) for the entire geometry using IC-CAP.
    -Comparison and benchmarking of PSP model performance with that of BSIM4 model was done to analyze if National should shift to the new industry standard.
    -Documented the procedure and created custom extraction routines for in-house usage.
    -Presented recommendations on deploying the model for circuit design to the SPICE Modeling Group.

  • Research Associate

    Oak Ridge National Laboratory

    (Government Agency; Research industry)

    May 2005 — August 2005 (4 months)

    Wide bandgap research group, Oak Ridge National Laboratory (ORNL) – National Transportation Research Center (NTRC), Knoxville, TN. Mentor – Dr. Burak Ozpineci.

    -Involved in the characterization and compact modeling of Silicon Carbide JFETs and Schottky barrier diodes used in voltage source inverters for hybrid electric vehicles.
    -Deployed the compact JFET model that I developed in my thesis in real-life automotive applications.
    -This was part of the Department of Energy’s (DoE) FreedomCAR project. The work also involved building test benches for device characterization and system level modeling and validation of inverters.
    -Conclusively proved the increase in efficiency in inverters with the use of SiC devices.
