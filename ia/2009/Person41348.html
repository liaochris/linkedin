Charles J. Sanders’s Summary

One of the nation's leading commentators and practitioners in the area of music copyright law. Counsel to The Songwriters Guild of America. Licensed to practice in New York, California, Washington, DC and The Supreme Court of the United States. Chairman of the music industry's premier social outreach charity, World Hunger Year / Artists Against Hunger and Poverty; Governor, National Academy of Recording Arts and Sciences (NARAS/Grammy Awards); Co-founder and Board Member of The James Madison Foundation (for Freedom of Information); Award winning author and historian of "The Boys of Winter: Life and Death in the U.S. Ski Troops During the Second World War," winner of the I.S.H.A. Ullr Award for journalistic excellence (2005) and the F.W.S.A. Bill Berry Sports Journalism Award (2006); Professor at New York University. Former professional musician with multiple album credits and platinum RIAA certification for production.

Charles J. Sanders’s Specialties:

Copyright Law; Music Industry Legal, Legislative and Business Issues and History; Freedom of Information Law; U.S. Military History (10th Mountain Division, U.S. Ski Troops); Former studio musician (saxophone) and producer with numerous album credits and platinum sales certifications.

    (Non-Profit; 11-50 employees; Philanthropy industry)

    WHY/AAHP is the music industry's premier social outreach program, helping to support the teaching of self-sufficiency skills and the provision of emergency survival services for tens of thousands of people in the United States and throughout the world who are afflicted with problems related to poverty and malnutrition.

  • Counsel; Senior VP of Legal and International Affairs

    National Music Publishers' Association

    (Non-Profit; 51-200 employees; Entertainment industry)

    June 1986 — December 2004 (18 years 7 months)

    Duties included serving for two decades as legal, business and policy advisor to the U.S. music publishing industry’s principal trade group (owner of the then-world’s largest music rights licensing organization). I was a negotiator and drafter of the Digital Millennium Copyright Act of 1998, the Digital Performance Rights in Sound Recordings Act of 1995, and the Audio Home Recording Act of 1992. Litigation participation included the landmark Grokster, Napster, MP3.com, Two Live Crew, and DAT cases. During my tenure, HFA successfully collected and distributed over $4 billion in royalties.
