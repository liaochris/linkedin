EXPERIENCE OVERVIEW

Wilder is a seasoned professional with over 10 years of experience in information technology and 3 years in the testing area. Working within the education sector in the later 90ts, he has moved to the health insurance sector, specially finance and auditing department, in the beginning of the 2002. In his current job, since 2004, he has entered in the financial and telecommunications sectors. Wilder has worked with projects and technical proposal for Sweden, Brazil and Bolivia. Due to his experience, he has managed development teams up to 6 people in projects using Java technology and Oracle database.

As a self-study learner, Wilder has great discipline regarding his time administration. As a result of this effort, Wilder is about to conclude the Java certification path. To keep an evolving process of maturity in his skills, Wilder has joined the course of Bachelor Degree in Computer Science in the University of Fortaleza – UNIFOR –, in the early 2006.

Wilder has written 2 scientific articles to local magazines in the second semester of 2007. The first one, accepted in the Science of Computer Journal (from a local University), was about the migration of large applications, commonly used in desktops, to mobile devices environment; the second one, presents a solution for micro payment applications with the Java ME platform and the new Payment API from Sun Microsystems.

The devices’ data, represented in XML files, follows a well defined life-cycle: as soon as new device are distributed, XMLs files are sent from distributors to TomTom and then stored in TomTom’s database. From this time on, as soon as the device has any problem and its owner opens a service request, the device can be traced between TomTom and the repair centre via the message broker system.

The system has a simple user interface, used only by logistics employees, and a message broker service used to process the files coming in via Microsoft BizTalk and also send devices’ status to some other internal systems using ActiveMQ.
