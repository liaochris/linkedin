Mel Futrell’s Summary

I was admitted to my first Bachelor's program in pursuit of a Languages & Linguistics career. Halfway through, I found myself deeply immersed in the business end of the modern music industry resulting in a degree in Music & English rather than Russian, Chinese, or Syntax Analysis. I also logged 3.6 hours of Single-Engine Airplane time.

A year following graduation, I pulled stakes and relocated to the sunny left-coast of California and finished my Master's in Communication Management. Though I'd tenured 12 yrs. in all aspects of the music industry, along the way I completed my pilot and ground instructor certificates and I became more immersed in aviation than entertainment. In 2002, I landed back in school seeking a PhD and combining my passions of Aviation & Culture/Language.

I am now wrapping up the thesis for a 2nd Master's in Human Factors Psychology while I pursue a doctoral education in the same. I focus on cultural influences on flight crew (pilot, ATC, co-pilot, etc.) performance, safety and interface with technology (airplane, navigation systems, communications, etc.), including comparative studies with deaf pilots. I was fortunate enough to spend a year on temporary assignment with the FAA doing multicultural Human Factors research. I continue to collaborate on some ongoing projects.

In industry, I regularly give seminars at local airports and regional and national air-shows, doing my best to introduce the deaf pilot population to their hearing counterpart. As a hearing person, I am by no means an expert, but seek to encourage further integration in any way possible. Regular attendees of Oshkosh' Airventure may recognize me on their aviation station, EAA Radio's 1210AM, as "Magneto Mel", (also broadcast world-wide on the web).

In a perfect world, I intend spend my life researching and designing assistive and adaptive education, training, technology and systems in general aviation for a multicultural population, which includes Deaf culture.
