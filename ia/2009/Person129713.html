Ken Becker’s Summary

Goal: a leadership role with accountability for the user experience. I bring insight to design and am enthusiastic and passionate about improving the user experience. I believe in taking a user-centered approach to interaction design in order to create a positive difference for the product and the company. I bring 10 years of experience in user experience as a manager, consultant, information architect, user researcher, interaction designer, usability engineer and graduate course instructor. Being a quick study with strong technical skills, I come up to speed fast and I am a great liaison between users and developers. A technologist who cares about people, I am highly motivated and self-managed, have great leadership, communication and presentation skills, and take initiative to make improvements where I see opportunities

I have managed a user experience organization, developed and worked to a long-term master plan to improve the user experience, independently managed many usability projects in cross-functional teams involving user research, usability testing and user-centered interaction design.

I employ interaction design principles in my work, applying the most appropriate approach to the situation, such as formative or summative usability evaluation, contextual inquiry, personas, design patterns, user satisfaction surveys, interactive prototyping, data analysis, usability goals, task analysis, conceptual models, performance metrics and web usage analytics.

My most significant experience is in managing and leading projects involving intranets, portals, handheld computers, printers, web applications, online communities and security.

Ken Becker’s Specialties:

Applying design principles, formative and summative usability evaluation, contextual inquiry, personas, design patterns, user satisfaction surveys, interactive prototyping, data analysis, usability goals, task analysis, conceptual models, performance metrics and web usage analytics to intranets, portals, handheld computers, printers, web applications, online communities and security.
