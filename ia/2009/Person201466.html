Robin Preisler’s Experience

  • Director of Educational Marketing

    UMDNJ Center for Continuing and Outreach Education

    (Educational Institution; Hospital & Health Care industry)

    October 2005 — July 2009 (3 years 10 months)

    Responsible for building and marketing a robust portfolio of online and live activities for the continuing education division of the largest free-standing health sciences university in the country.

    Buillt and nurtured an educational partnership with Medscape, the CME arm of WebMD and the largest provider of online CME programming in the country. This partnership has obtained close to $1 million in grant funding from pharmaceutical companies to produce a variety of CME activities, including a series of programs on cultural competency and healthcare disparities.

    Managed the grant submission process for CME funding, overseeing the development of over 125 grant applications annually, and securing $2 million in funding in 2008. Responsible for writing/budgeting of all grant applications related to online educational initiatives.

    Planned and implemented a complete overhaul of CCOE’s website. Continued to manage all content and quality improvement processes.

    Established CCOE’s first email marketing initiative, including subscriber development and targeted messaging.

    Personally conducted extensive qualitative market research to support planning and implementation of a new UMDNJ biopharma educational initiative offering online academic certificates. Included in-depth interviews with potential sponsoring executives, students, and competitors, as well as launching shorter online surveys.

    Reduced CCOE’s dependence on external vendors and built a new revenue stream for CCOE by introducing and overseeing new online education platforms (Camtasia, Adobe Connect Pro, etc). These platforms allow CCOE to produce and disseminate its own online content.

    Planned and executed a digital advertising campaign for the Biopharma Educational Initiative.

    Held Principal Investigator role in two CDC grant-funded educational activities, fulfilling all responsibilities of the PI, including budget creation and oversight.

  • Executive Director, Marketing and Media Relations

    UMDNJ University Hospital

    (Hospital & Health Care industry)

    March 1984 — October 2005 (21 years 8 months)

    Responsible for oversight and execution of all communication functions on behalf of a 518-bed urban academic medical center and its faculy/medical staff. Throughout tenure, department size varied from two professionals to eight at its peak (including an advanced practice nurse) with annual marketing budgets ranging from less than $100,000 to $2 million annually.

    Recipient of 34 state, national and international awards for marketing, crisis communications, advertising, and website development, including five national highest-achievement awards in 2005 for University Hospital’s website.

    Specific responsibilities included crisis and planned media relations; publications – print and online; website development and content management; employee communications; physician/faculty relations; community marketing and outreach activities; government relations; support to the CEO, including speech writing and communications to employees; special events; advertising (print, radio, TV, including broadcast and cable); special employee recruitment projects; and market research.
