Robert Imrie’s Summary

• Fluent in Japanese; 16 years experience working in Japan.
• Experience in digital and traditional marketing.
• Free-lance writer with two books and 200+ articles published.
• Corporate trainer with 10 years experience in customer service training and teaching English as a second language to business groups and individuals.

My next novel, Drop Dead, due out in late 2009, is a suspense novel that takes place in the Twin Cities.

Robert Imrie’s Specialties:

Writing, editing, journalism, tourism marketing, database marketing, data compiling, web and e-mail user tracking, public speaking, Japanese language (spoken only), corporate training

    April 1995 — July 1997 (2 years 4 months)

  • Columnist

    The Asahi Evening News

    (Newspapers industry)

    October 1989 — October 1989 (1 month)

    They used to be called The Asahi Daily News, but were a distant fourth among the English newspapers (behind The Japan Times, Daily Yomiuri and Mainichi Daily News).
    They were in the early stages of testing whether publishing in late afternoon might create a niche they could survive do better in. The publisher asked me to write a piece on anything I wanted for their first "test" edition, which came out on October 15, 1989. (I wrote about Columbia Pictures recent sales to Sony by Coca Cola. Who remembers that?)
    They eventually did switch to become The Asahi Evening News, and became popular with foreigners heading home from work.
