Bruce Leigh Myers, Ph.D.’s Summary

Sales, training and management professional with over twenty years in the printing industry. Holds a Ph.D. in Graphic Communications from New York University. Held various sales, technical, training and management positions at Agfa Corporation and X-Rite, Incorporated. Work with consumer packaging companies to develop and implement corporate brand color control programs. Served as Global Sales Training Manager with extensive travel to Asia to train Far Eastern sales force in color measurement and management. Long time adjunct professor at New York University, and former resident professor at Kean University, specialties in teaching color reproduction, quality control, and management.

Bruce Leigh Myers, Ph.D.’s Education

  • New York University

    Ph.D., Graphic Communications, 1987 — 2004

    Dissertation Title: Moving Beyond Computer-Based Information Technology: A Comparison of the Technology Acceptance Model with the Theory of Reasoned Action and the Theory of Planned Behavior in An Industry Specifications Context.

    Using a sample consisting of intended adopters of a particular printing industry specification, specifically the Flexographic Image Reproduction Specifications and Tolerances (FIRST), this study evaluated tthree theories from the realm of social psychology: the theory of reasoned action, the theory of planned behavior and the technoloogy acceptance moodel, through data obtained from a cross-sectional survey. Using behavioral intention to adopt FIRST as a criterion variable, this research examined the appropriateness of the respective models in a particular context by determining how much variance in the criterion variable each explained.
