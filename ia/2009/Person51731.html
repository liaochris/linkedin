Cynthia Lilly’s Experience

  • Managing Attorney - Internet Safety Team

    Microsoft

    (Public Company; MSFT; Computer Software industry)

    February 2009 — Present (8 months)

    Leads a dedicated team of legal professionals, including investigators, paralegals, and outside counsel, responsible for Microsoft’s global online safety, child protection and fraud prevention, disruption and enforcement programs. Serves as the subject matter expert for online fraud, spam, phishing, domain defense, search engine and platform online safety issues, child pornography matters, child protection issues, and online advertising fraud. Advises Microsoft business clients and executives; Legal and Corporate Affairs Division including assistant general counsels, product attorneys and paralegals; and policy directors on legal strategies and policies regarding defending against online criminal activity relating to computers and the Internet. Legal services include managing litigation, counseling on online safety regulations and legislative activity, negotiating contracts, incident handling, and advising and establishing safety and security best practices. Participate on boards and working groups with a similar mission to protect people, children, and data from online threats. Works closely with industry, governmental authorities, and corporate partners to protect consumers from cybercrime. Advises on Internet domain name strategies across the company and manages a portfolio of corporate domain names. Manage outside counsel, staff and budget.

  • Lead Product Attorney

    Microsoft

    (Public Company; MSFT; Computer Software industry)

    June 2006 — February 2009 (2 years 9 months)

    Served as lead product attorney for Windows Live, including Hotmail, Windows Live Mail, Windows Live Calendar, authentication services such as Windows Live ID, family safety applications such as Family Safety Settings, and anti-spam, malware and phishing issues and policy. Provided advice on legal, technical and policy issues in the provision of end to end global online services, including communications, intellectual property law, privacy, Telecommunications, commercial law, criminal compliance and incident response, and geolocation of data centers. Worked closely with the business team to strategize on combating malware, phishing, spyware, and spam, including handling account compromise, hijacking, and other malicious activity. Worked on a number of initiatives with the Internet Safety Team regarding cybercrime matters. Provided first chair advice to Windows Live businesses to launch the first customer support social media site for Windows Live. During the first 12 months, served as the lead product attorney for Windows Live VOIP services providing advice on legal, technical and policy issues on VOIP services, including communications, intellectual property law, privacy, Telecommunications, and commercial law. Work involved the launch of VOIP services in over twenty markets and this was a first endeavor for Microsoft. Regulatory telecom research and analysis was conducted in over 30 markets.
