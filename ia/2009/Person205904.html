Ryan Alford’s Summary

My passion focuses on writing…and maybe some editing too. But writing is what keeps me motivated. And it’s not necessarily what I write, but it’s more centered on the craft. I tend not to follow what’s average or what’s been done already.

And then there’s Snowshoe Magazine. What a long, strange trip it’s been. I’ve been working on this gig for more than five years now. I started this hobby as a highly sophisticated blog focused on the sport of snowshoeing – my favorite thing to do in the winter. Later, after many requests, it became an official Internet magazine. I printed one issue totaling 48 pages, a variety of content and not enough advertising. It’s a labor of love for sure. Check it out: http://www.snowshoemag.com. Enjoy.

My early years were spent convincing professors at Abilene Christian University that I AM A WRITER and not just another student. I made the Dean’s List (GPA of 3.65) during the spring semester of my senior year. Not a bad performance. I even met my wife at ACU – good times.

I quickly launched my education into hyper-drive when I was accepted by the University of Denver’s Publishing Institute. At the time, they only accepted 90 applicants per year. Or at least, that’s what they told me. But, it was a privilege and an honor to be a part of it. Throughout the month and a half attending PI, I learned a “horn of plenty” on publishing – spanning editing, marketing and the business aspects. Ahhhh…higher education.
