Kelly is a software entrepreneur and the the founder of Curious Office, a Seattle seed-stage software incubator. He was the founder of Imagekind, the largest market for buying and selling art, which was acquired by CafePress in 2007, as well as a co-founder of thePlatform which was acquired by Comcast in 2006 and is most recently the founder of Inkd, a marketplace for buying and selling graphic design. Before founding Inkd, Kelly served as VP of Product Marketing for the IP Television group within Nokia Siemens, and worked at RealNetworks from 1994 to 1999 in various marketing and business development roles.

Along with Ignition Venture Partners, Curious Office has invested in SEOmoz.com - the largest SEO tools vendor and has made other investments or holds shares in CafePress, Imagekind, Wishpot, CoolSpotters, Shelfari (acquired by Amazon) and FeedDigest (acquired). Kelly is also a board member or board advisor for NetConcepts, PatentExpress, SEOmoz and Rouxbe.com

Personal Summary:
Versatile, analytical, entrepreneurial technology executive with 15 years product marketing experience in both domestic and international posts. Primary domain expertise includes online marketplaces, interface driven experiences, digital media, broadband video applications, IP Television (IPTV), Video on Demand (“VOD”) and Web Services to include software, content licensing & distribution. Strong blend of product design, development, product marketing and business development experience with exceptional knowledge across all other disciplines of the media/technology intersection. Personal interest in social software, social networking, online collaboration and next generation web development frameworks.
