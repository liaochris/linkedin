Martin Selmayr’s Summary

Dr. Martin Selmayr (born 1970) is an expert in European law and communications with a focus on competition, the EU's single market, telecoms, media, copyright, central banking and finance.

He studied law at the Universities of Geneva and Passau, at King's College London and the University of California (Berkeley/Davis). He holds a doctorate in law, following his thesis on "The Law of Economic and Monetary Union".

From 1998 to 2000, Selmayr worked first as Legal Counsel, then as external Legal Advisor for the European Central Bank. In 2000, he was sent as Visiting Official to the Legal Department of the International Monetary Fund in Washington D.C. where he worked on the arrangements for the international representation of the euro area.

In 2001, Selmayr changed to the private sector where he started as Legal Advisor of the international media company Bertelsmann, with special focus on competition, trade and media law. In 2003, Selmayr became Head of the Brussels Office and Vice President Government & Legal Relations of Bertelsmann.

Having succeeded in a competition for lawyers, Selmayr joined the European Commission's Spokesperson's Group in November 2004 as Spokesman for Information Society and Media (Commissioner Viviane Reding). Since then, he has been speaking on behalf of the European Commission and advised senior EU officials on strategic issues.

In his spare time, he is lecturing EU Law at the Universities of Passau, Saarbrücken and Schloss Hofen. He is Director of the Centre for European Law at the University of Passau, with responsibility for Law of Economic and Monetary Union, Anti-Trust Law, and the Law of the Lisbon Treaty. He has published three books and numerous articles on EU Law issues. (see
http://www.cep.uni-passau.de/mitgliederCep/selmayr.htm).

He is fluent in English, French and German (mother tongue), has a good knowledge of Spanish and also knows some Italian, Russian and Polish.

Martin Selmayr’s Specialties:

European Union Law (in particular Anti-Trust Law, State Aid Law, Public Procurement Law, Law of Economic and Monetary Union, Law of the European Central Bank, EU Financial Market Law, EU Media Law, EU Telecoms Law; EU Institutional Law and New Lisbon Reform Treaty); Public International Law, especially International Economic Law and Trade Law; German Constitutional Law; US Anti-Trust Law; Comparative Constitutional Law.
