  • President/CEO

    AirTEL Wireless LLC

    (Telecommunications industry)

    January 2008 — February 2009 (1 year 2 months)

    AirTEL Wireless is a cellular carrier throughout the State of Montana using iDEN (Nextel) technology. This is a turnaround opportunity where we are offering unlimited usage with all features on a prepaid flat rate basis to lower income families and attempting to expand into the business community.

  • President/CEO/Founder

    Alliance Solutions, Inc.

    (Privately Held; Computer Software industry)

    October 2002 — December 2007 (5 years 3 months)

    Start-up, nationwide, wholesale VoIP and Internet provider with distribution through private label/branded resellers. Emphasis placed on the branding & identity of resellers. Gained significant knowledge and skill in Internet marketing & web sales, as well as search engine optimization. Merger in 4Q08. Participated on the Board up to and including merger.

  • President/CEO/Founder

    Quantum Communications Group, Inc.

    (Telecommunications industry)

    1988 — 2002 (14 years)

    Start-up cellular/PCS management and consulting company. Built and operated (incubated) dozens of start-up rural cellular and PCS systems nationwide through management agreements and assisted in eventual sale to larger wireless telcos, making a substantial profit for the licensee and Quantum. Created the AmeriLink brand and licensed it to smaller PCS operators. Held experimental PCS licenses in Minneapolis & Denver. During this time held minority interests in dozens of cellular and PCS systems nationwide. The birth of Internet access and World Wide Web led to in-depth study and application of Internet Marketing & Sales.

  • Regional Director

    Western Wireless

    (Public Company; WWCA; Telecommunications industry)

    1995 — 1998 (3 years)

    As a start-up company, responsibilities in this role included general management for the properties in Minnesota, North Dakota, South Dakota, Iowa and Nebraska, Accountable for personnel, distribution management & programs, sales & marketing, sales training. Even though this region has the lowest population and population density, as the company grew to become a large carrier with cellular properties across America and the world, this region was often #1 in both sales and operations.

  • Advisory Board Member

    Cellular One Group

    (Telecommunications industry)

    1993 — 1995 (2 years)

    Cellular One Group was a start-up formed for the purpose of building a nationwide brand identity and recognition for the Cellular One brand used by many large and small regional nonewireline cellular system operators throughout America. In addition, licensed members combined their purchasing power to secure the most favorable pricing on handsets and other purchases. The Group created many marketing programs used by licensed members. Large carrier members included McCaw Cellular, Vanguard Cellular and Southwestern Bell while smaller carrier members included companies like Quantum Communications Group.

  • Regional General Manager

    McCaw Cellular

    (Public Company; Telecommunications industry)

    1986 — 1988 (2 years)

    The acquisition of MCI Air Signal, including the Minneapolis/St. Paul license, in 1986 was the beginning of McCaw Cellular’s (d.b.a. Cellular One) dramatic growth to the largest cellular carrier in America prior to being acquired by AT&T in 1993. Promoted from General Manager of the Minneapolis market to Regional General Manager and responsible for the start-up and day-to-day operations of dozens of markets in Minnesota, Wisconsin, Illinois and Indiana. Built strong direct and indirect distribution channels. During this time the region and various markets received numerous awards, multiple times, for “Best Subscriber Penetration”, “Lowest Subscriber Churn”, “Best Overall Performance”, “Best Marketing & Promotion”, and “Circle of Excellence”.

  • General Manager

    MCI Telecommunications

    (Telecommunications industry)

    1985 — 1986 (1 year)

    As a result of the birth of cellular telephone service was recruited by MCI to head a start-up joint venture with a few local investors called MCI Cellcom with the purpose of building and operating the non-wireline cellular system in the Minneapolis/St. Paul MSA. In spite of an advertising budget at 1/10th of the competitor’s (the local telephone company) advertising budget, achieved 55% market share. Licensed the Cellular One brand for this market. Built both direct and indirect distribution channels. When the FCC ruled against MCI for the Los Angeles cellular license, they sold their entire MCI Air Signal division (including MCI Cellcom) to McCaw Cellular.
