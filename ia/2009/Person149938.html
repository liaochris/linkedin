Maria Bostenaru’s Summary

Dipl.-Ing. M Bostenaru Dan obtained an engineering degree in architecture, with specialisation in urban planning, in 1999 from the Universität Karlsruhe (TH), Germany, after studying also at the Architecture Institute "Ion Mincu" in her native city, Bucharest, the capital of Romania. She was involved, within the Collaborative Research Centre SFB 315 "Preservation of historically relevant constructions", in a building survey in Poland, and was employed as part time student assistant in the SFB 461 "Strong earthquakes: from geosciences to civil engineering" both at the Universität Karlsruhe. With a scholarship in frame of the Research Training Group “Natural Disasters” in Karlsruhe and a Marie Curie Early Stage Research Fellowship in Pavia, Italy, research was done on "Applicability and economic efficiency of seismic retrofit measures on existing buildings". Between 2005 and 2007 she is conducted research on the two years project "Preservation of historic reinforced concrete housing buildings across Europe" (CA’REDIVIVUS) a Marie Curie Intra-European Fellowship in Pavia. After successfully finishing that project she returned to her home country, Romania, after winning a Marie Curie Reintegration Grant. In 2008 she was also employed at the "Ion Mincu" University of Architecture and Urban Planning. She now returned to doing research in her initial area of architecture but keeps in contact with earthquake engineering through one of the many projects she is currently involved in.
She has been active member of a number of professional associations, responsibilities including being member of the advisory board, editing journal special issues, organising conference sessions.

Maria Bostenaru’s Specialties:

20th century architecture, seismic retrofit of historic buildings, housing buildings, multicriteria decision, citizen participation, survey techniques, multimedia, project management, fluency in more European languages
