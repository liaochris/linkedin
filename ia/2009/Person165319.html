Miles Keller’s Summary

There is no question in my mind that design is a mix of equal parts art, science and engineering. I believe that a well designed product can and should create an emotional reaction in the user. Unquantifiable attributes like beauty and spirituality are as real a need for humans as food or water.

I believe that it is possible to solve real problems, contribute to substantial improvements in people’s lives and still produce products are cool, sexy and inspiring. What we as designers are supposed to be doing first and foremost is improving people’s quality of life. We must also give serious consideration to the role of the designer in the health of our environment and ultimately the future of the planet.

Two well known award winning projects projects I've worked on are are the Fluid ergonomic task chair for Allseating and the Zeeweed municipal water filtration system for Zenon Environmental. Both required the ability to oversee complex design projects from start to finish, including initial concept development, concept modeling, design development, detailed CAD modeling, tooling and production implementation.

This is balanced with over seven years as the sole retained design service for Umbra from 1989 to 1996, always staying at the forefront of consumer, fashion and design trends. This helped sustain a ten fold growth in sales volume and made Umbra an industry leader and a household name. Fifteen years later the “Stream” bath accessory line is still a sales leader.

One–off furniture and lighting designs, created primarily during the years 1986- 1996, won multiple awards and were published widely domestically and internationally. The most well known examples are the “Sail” lamp of 1985 and the “Dolphin” chair of 1989. Both demonstrate a careful use of materials, making the most of their physical properties, and a sensual, progressive aesthetic.

Miles Keller’s Specialties:

advanced concept development, aesthetics, market forecasting and trend analysis, human factors, production methodology, ergonomics, material properties, 3D CAD modeling, project management and marketing strategy.
