Carla Willis’s Summary

PhD economist passionate about helping policy makers, individuals, and organizations make informed decisions related to health insurance coverage. Areas of expertise include benefit design, market regulation (premium rating, terms of issue, and benefit mandates), risk pooling, targeting mechanisms, tax treatment of health insurance, and expanding coverage to the uninsured.

Carla Willis’s Specialties:

Distilling complex issues, framing them in relevant context(s), and translating for a variety of audiences. Synthesis of academic and trade literature, data. Creation and analysis of customized data sets, e.g., from NHE, MEPS, Treasury, eHealthInsurance. Formal modeling of alternative scenarios. Visual presentation of information in tables, graphs, slides. Legislative analysis of reform proposals. Coaching individuals, small businesses on coverage options.

Carla Willis’s Experience

  • Principal Economist

    American Medical Association

    (Non-Profit; Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    1997 — May 2009 (12 years)

    Advised physician leadership and senior management of AMA on wide range of issues related to health system reform.

    From 2005 to 2007, played key role in development of historic consensus reform proposal by Health Coverage Coalition for the Uninsured (HCCU). Extracted essential insights from highly technical analyses and described in plain English. Positioned AMA as "honest broker" in the national health policy community.

    Prepared ~25 reports for the AMA Council on Medical Service on topics ranging from individual mandates to domestic partner benefits. Research and analysis helped shape AMA policy positions on covering the uninsured, rising health care costs, insurance coverage of high-risk patients, health savings accounts (HSAs), and comparative effectiveness research (CER). Numerous white papers, legislative analyses, etc. include "HSAs at a Glance" used as prototype for U.S. Treasury Department brochure (www.ama-assn.org/go/hsaglance) and series of “wonk light” briefs describing the AMA reform proposal to a general audience (http://www.voicefortheuninsured.org/resources.html).

    Frequent invited speaker at medical and law schools, graduate classes, and professional meetings.

  • Senior Policy Analyst

    Abt Associates

    (Privately Held; Research industry)

    1992 — 1995 (3 years)

    Researched alternative approaches to financing health care services in developing countries, with major focus on targeting services to the very poorest. Duties included data analysis; survey design and implementation; and presentations to government officials of sub-Saharan African countries, funding agencies, advisory panels, Think Tanks, and academic researchers. Worked in both the Cambridge, MA and Bethesda, MD offices with brief tours of duty to the Congo (Brazaville) and Senegal.

  • Resident Health Economist, Niger Health Sector Support Program

    Abt Associates

    (Public Policy industry)

    1990 — 1992 (2 years)

    Served as a resident economic advisor to the Ministry of Health in Niger, West Africa through program funded by the US Agency for International Development. Helped to design and plan a three-District household survey to compare the impacts of different approaches to financing health care services.

  • Policy Analyst

    The World Bank

    (Public Policy industry)

    May 1989 — August 1989 (4 months)

    Worked in the Population, Health and Nutrition Division as part of a multidisciplinary team led by Richard Feachem to write a book on adult health in developing countries. Constructed a data set to analyze the prevelance of chronic conditions among adults in selected countries, developed graphs of findings, and co-authored the corresponding chapter.

  • Research Laboratory Technician, Department of Pulmonary Medicine

    University of Pittsburgh School of Medicine

    (Educational Institution; Research industry)

    1985 — 1986 (1 year)

    Co-authored two abstracts in the American Review of Respiratory Disease.
