Chris Hansen’s Summary

Mr. Hansen is a serial entrepreneur with a passion for developing solutions to small business problems. He and his wife Sharon are the owners of CIE International and co-founders of geeXmedia. His interest in business and entrepreneurialism was sparked as a child, while watching his father, a used car dealer, make deals on a daily basis.
Mr. Hansen graduated from Old Dominion University with a Bachelor of Science degree in Business Administration. Immediately after college, Mr. Hansen was introduced to eBay and CIE was born. With savings of a little over $1,000 and a credit card, he has taken CIE from a small one person eBay seller to a corporation with a staff of 11 and sales well over $1.5 million annually. CIE currently lists and sells more than 1000 unique items per month. Since its inception, he has successfully worked with clients such as UPS, NASA, NIH, NCIC, SAIC, and many others.

During his time with CIE, Mr. Hansen contracted with a software development company to develop software to more efficiently manage a large warehouse and sales on eBay. This software has blossomed into Listapp – a full featured, web based, asset management, and sales platform. After beta development, Listapp was re-written and improved upon by Jeremy Tharp. With the success of Listapp, Mr. Tharp and Mr. Hansen co-founded geeXmedia in January of 2008. In addition to ListApp, the flagship application, geeXmedia has since produced the SignApp digital signage advertisement platform, as well as eZ barcodeZ, a web application built for easily designing and printing labels with barcodes. In addition, it has produced eZ labelZ for eBay which ports the eZ barcodeZ engine into eBay’s Selling Manager Applications platform.

    (Internet industry)

    March 1998 — Present (11 years 8 months)

    Chris currently oversees business development and purchasing.

    CIE Surplus is a full service asset liquidation and conversion company located in Berryville, northern Virginia. We have developed a highly optimized system of redistributing excess goods across multiple electronic channels, most notably eBay, in an attempt to make sure excess, surplus, but still usable goods get reused.

    We specialize in the resale of business/industrial, medical, lab, pharmaceutical, and electronic equipment, and have tailored our work process to maximize the asset's conversion value, as well as the efficiency of redistribution. We currently have about a dozen employees sorting, cleaning, repairing, inventorying, and shipping well over 1000 unique items, big and small, every month, and our capacity to do more continues to grow.

    Much of the equipment we receive can't be reused or resold, so we strip that equipment into its recyclable components and do our part to make sure those components get reused elsewhere. We recycle thousands of pounds of scrap metal every week.

    We often see perfectly working excess equipment sit stagnant, unable to get redirected to someone who could use it. We also know how many people are out there looking for a specialized piece of equipment who don't have the resources to buy it new. If that is you, be sure to keep a constant check on our inventory. We have implemented RSS feeds to help you do just that -- where you want to, when you want to. The deals are always there!

    We also know how hard it is to find someone to just take your unused equipment and convert it to cash. At CIE Surplus, we can handle the entire process with absolutely no intervention on your part. From acquisition, shipment, valuation, sales, and distribution, you won't lift a finger, and the check will be in the mail.
