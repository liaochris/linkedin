Peter Gladkin’s Summary

I did it My Way

If you settle for "good enough"you'll never know what level of excellence you're actually capable of.

Most often, those who know HOW, end up working for those who know WHY. Once you master the how,focus on learning the why.

Too many Corporate Managers are like sailors, they always know which way the wind is blowing, but they don't know where the ship is going.

The street smart Corporate Manager never denigrates the process Du Jour. However it is permissible for a Corporate Manager to criticize the process of "Yester Jour"

A "Vision" fervently believed in only by its beholder is most appropriately defined as a "Hallucination". Beware the Hallucinator posing as a Visionary. There is neither water nor sustenance in a mirage. There is no "there" there.

Avoid turning "current clients" into "prospective customers" thru intemperate short sighted policies and failure to listen to what they are saying. Most companies do not LOSE clients, instead they drive clients away thru institutionalized stupidity

If you and your subordinate(s) agree on everything, then one of you is unnecessary.

On occasion it is wiser to seek forgiveness than it is to ask for permission.

Money is always a terrible master, but it can be a good servant.

May you realize the Karma you so richly deserve and have worked so hard to achieve.
