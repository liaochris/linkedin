Karen Doering’s Summary

I am currently working on several pro bono projects and pursuing job possibilities at government civil rights enforcement agencies. I am also open to the possibility of an in-house or general counsel position with a college or university.

Accomplished sports law and civil rights impact litigation attorney:
•13+ years investigating discrimination claims and managing complex litigation
•Proficient in Title VII, Title IX, FLSA and Constitutional law
•Lead Attorney for National Center for Lesbian Rights’ Sports Project
•Focus on education/prevention strategies
•All aspects of legislative lawyering including policy creation and implementation
•Public Policy Advocacy
•Highly effective speaker and negotiator
•Masterful at making complex legal issues understandable to non-lawyers
•Extensive expertise in conflict resolution and media relations

During my 13+ years investigating and managing plaintiff-side impact litigation I garnered the knowledge and expertise I now use to advise businesses, Athletic Administrators and school officials to prevent litigation and avoid legal liability through implementation of smarter, more effective education/prevention programs and clear, unambiguous and consistent investigation procedures.

From 2002-2008, I ran the National Center for Lesbian Rights' Southern Regional Office based in St. Petersburg, Florida and served as Lead Attorney for NCLR’s Sports Project. Prior to that I founded and ran Equality Florida's Legal Advocacy Project and worked for the boutique civil rights firm Burr & Smith, LLP.

I am a frequent guest lecturer and speaker at colleges and universities, national conferences, and continuing legal education programs. I have appeared on numerous television programs to discuss some of the civil rights cases I managed, including Court TV, Good Morning America, Larry King Live, ESPN’s Outside the Lines, the Dr. Phil Show, and others.

Karen Doering’s Specialties:

Training/education/prevention strategies, public policy advocacy, sports law, LGBT legal issues, conflict resolution & media relations strategies, legislative lawyering, managing complex litigation, employment discrimination and wage and hour issues

Karen Doering’s Experience

  • Labor, Sports and Civil Right Attorney

    Law Office of Karen M. Doering

    (Law Practice industry)

    April 2008 — Present (1 year)

    Karen's law practice focuses on Sports Law and Policy, working primarily with collegiate athletic administrators and General Counsel to help prevent discrimination lawsuits from occurring, and to effectively investigate and address allegations of discrimination before they require litigation.

    The past several years have brought an upsurge in the number of lawsuits filed against athletic administrations alleging gender and sexual orientation discrimination, retaliation, and failure to fully comply with Title IX. The current generation of student athletes have grown up in the post-Title IX era where they and their coaches expect, and increasingly demand, enforcement of the equality promises of state and federal civil rights laws.

    Proper prevention strategies and effective conflict resolution are the key to successful programs where the goals of the educational institution, athletic administrators, coaches and student athletes can all be met.

  • Senior Counsel/Sports Law

    National Center for Lesbian Rights

    (Legal Services industry)

    March 2002 — April 2008 (6 years 2 months)

    As Senior Counsel, Karen ran NCLR's Southern Regional Office and served as NCLR's Sports Project attorney. NCLR is a national legal resource center committed to advancing the rights and safety of lesbian, gay, bisexual, and transgender people and their families through litigation, public policy advocacy, and public education. NCLR’s Sports Project aims to level the playing field for LGBT players and coaches by working to eliminate homophobia and gender identity discrimination in sports.
