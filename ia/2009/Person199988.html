Rob Celin’s Summary

Currently, I am a rising junior at Boston College matriculated in the Wallace E. Carroll School of Management (CSOM) with majors in Finance and Information Systems and a minor in Environmental Studies with an emphasis in Environmental Policy. My coursework includes a broad liberal arts education coupled with a variety of management courses. My goal at Boston College is to study and gain an understanding of the vast intersections of economics and the environment and their ramifications in a complex business environment. In Spring 2009, I participated in a selective field study focusing on business strategy and the evolution of business models from start-up to blue chip in the technology industry called Undergraduate Tech Trek West. The course included rigorous preparation before departing on an intensive field study in Silicon Valley where we visited 20 firms and engaged in over 50 executives.

This summer I am interning as a Summer Finance Associate in the Hospital and Community Services Division of the University of Pittsburgh Medical Center gaining analytical skills in finance as well as refining my communication and leadership abilities. Finally, I have been accepted to Università Commerciale Luigi Bocconi (Bocconi University) in Milan, Italy to study abroad during the fall of 2009. Bocconi University is widely recognized as one of Europe's and the World's premier educational institutions in business and economics.
