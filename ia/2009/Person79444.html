  • Training : Study and Design of a 480MHz Ring Oscillator based on a 65nm CMOS Differential Delay Cell

    ST Microelectronics

    (Public Company; Semiconductors industry)

    April 2007 — July 2007 (4 months)

    Four months of training in the STMicrolectronics company in Rabat, Morocco, for the design of a 480MHz Ring Voltage Controlled Oscillator (VCO) based on a differential delay cell for the 65nm CMOS technology.
    The aim of this project was essentially to propose a design methodology, for the studied Ring VCO, based on an optimization algorithm, and to modelling the frequency sensitivity (Kvco).

    • Study and modeling the oscillation frequency, the frequency sensitivity (Kvco), and the phase noise.
    • Sizing of the cell using an optimization algorithm programmed using Maple software.
    • Characterization by simulating the entire VCO with output buffer.
    • Analogue Design in technologie : 65nm CMOS
    • Knowledge on following STMicroelectronics’ process and flow: CMOS65
    • Design environment is Cadence
    • Simulators: Eldo
    • Characterisation (oscillation frequency, timing jitter, phase noise, frequency sensitivity: Kvco and Kvcc, consummation)

  • Training : Study and Design of a 432MHz Ring Oscillator based on a 65nm CMOS Differential Delay Cell
