Richard Clark’s Summary

Starting in the legal industry in early '01 in Orlando, FL after graduating at Liberty University with a B.A. in Communications, I became quickly acclimated with the legal process as it related to discovery. I was an account manager with a litigation support company, and since the company was ahead of the curve with the changing technology, I was able to assist his clients with better productivity and cost cutting discovery. After a few years, I left the company and opened the Tampa office of a computer forensics and litigation support company, that was based out of Orlando. With immediate success, I was able to establish monthly technology and software training seminars in Orlando and Tampa, as well as increase the client base.

After working with partners of the litigation support company I was hired on by one of the partner companies to head their court reporting and records retrieval vertical. In just 4 to 5 months I quickly grew its records retrieval business to well over double the orders on a monthly basis for all of Central and North Florida.

After my sales were no longer needed, I went to work with Wave Software as the VP of Business Development, and have established a young company to rapid growth in a changing marketplace. Our core focus is the large data culling and native production to answer better workflow and best practices for electronic discovery. I now, through Wave Software, consult on best practices and incorporate our technology to the necessary workflows.

In starting with Wave Software, I was able to assist in the development and marketing of The Masters Conference in WDC. My core focus was to work with the sponsors and generate interest and attendance. Once The Masters Conference was a success it was able to be managed by its own core group.

Richard Clark’s Specialties:

Electronic Discovery and Technology Consulting; Best Practices in Electronic Discovery for large and small cases in light of the new changes in the Federal Rules and data preservation for review and production. Case Management from paper to electronic data and review.
