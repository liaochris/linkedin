Mark Tomizawa’s Summary

1.12.09

Like firefighters, AIR's semi-virtual network is led by fast-moving professionals. They're ready and resourceful. AIR draws from 300+ youth, elders and leaders to create lively teams with talent, life skills, smarts, tools and synergies.

AIR's first-responders pull together zero-dollar synergies to unleash well-timed solutions and productive media. Quickly. Without meetings, oversight, money, contracts and frustration.

AIR heads hail from news, PR, advertising, social media, entertainment, art, science, education, human + social capital, academia, and foundations. Every sector.

As a teen I learned collaboration on-the-fly at The Second City and big-time media at CBS. My PR campaign kept Metro High open by activating NBC-TV (Walter Jacobson), newspapers (Mike Royko) and radio (Studs Terkel).

Teen activism changed the arc of my life.

At Dartmouth I jumped into radio and the '76 political conventions. In Philly I dived into news radio, behavior research and the adrenaline world of news. One hurricane and one Emmy later I departed for Fred/Alan, MTV's legendary ad agency.

Two years later I founded Smash Advertising.

My boutique launched global tv networks, digital cable, the Olympics and the Compact Disc.

Smash reversed Sesame Street's slide and reinvigorated Ringling Bros. We made prime time History and A&E specials. We comforted post-9/11 New Yorkers with "No Place I'd Rather Be" for CBS.

Along the way, non-commercial civic projects hooked me...

• To get a young man off the federal suspect list for murder I coordinated a 12-hour TV news blitz that persuaded A.G. Janet Reno to reverse a staff decision.

• To help an artistic family turn their temporary apartment into a real home after a fire, I built a site where donors could offer to donate just the right belongings easily.

• To keep a tutoring center open despite D.C. foot-dragging I got the Boston Globe involved. The front page story pulled in a critical funder.

  • Wrangler

    Fred/Alan Inc. (The founders brought MTV to life)

    (Arts and Crafts industry)

    1985 — 1987 (2 years)

    What didn't they make? Fred/Alan did it all. Records, animation, shows, snippets, cereal prizes and off-kilter spots. Clients ate it up and why not?

    Fred Seibert and Alan Goodman had been MTV before MTV. They refused to look at audiences as lifeless piles of stats.

    They kept heads, hearts, guts and funnybones alive. Viewers, creatives and clients responded. It was frisky messy conception, never a slick surgical procedure. Stuff was born with a beat.

    Shortly after F/A opened I came aboard carrying ideas and baggage.

    My position? Somewhere between Fred and Alan, Albie, Tom, Linda, Jessica, Daria and Steve –– close to the ink & paint and kitchenette. The rule: Break the rules. Counterpunch. When "they" shout, whisper. When "they" go flashy, go handmade. And clean your own damn cup.

    We had MTV, Nick, Nick at Nite, Swatch, record labels and a conspiratorial universe of creatives and clients. Stuff stuck out. Strategy was underneath where you couldn't smell it.

  • Director of Audience & News Research
