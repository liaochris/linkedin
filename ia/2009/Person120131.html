Jorge López Hernández-Ardieta’s Education

  • Universidad Carlos III de Madrid

    Ph. D. , Computer Security , 2006 — 2009

    Doctoral Thesis about enhancing electronic signatures reliability.
    Although electronic signatures based on Public Key Cryptography and Public Key Infrastructure provide non-repudiation respecting the signed data and are considered, under current legislation, as a legally binding evidence, there are many situations where it cannot be assured that a secure signing process was carried out, specially at client side: private key compromise, document integrity attacks, semantic attacks, uncertainty on the digital certificate revocation status, cryptanalysis, side-channel attacks, etc.
    In this thesis I propose a set of security protocols oriented to B2C and contract signing scenarios where the reliability of the signatures produced by the user is highly enhanced.
