Arnie Lund’s Experience

  • Principal Director, User Experience

    Microsoft

    (Public Company; MSFT; Computer Software industry)

    September 2002 — Present (7 years 3 months)

    Building a team responsible for customer self-service, partner, and sales support experiences; and driving relationship through customer interaction (e.g., solution search and support). Leading efforts to transform the IT culture and create a user-centered development process. Before moving to IT, managed the user experience team in the mobile platforms division consisting of software and industrial designers, usability and ethnographic researchers, technical communications professionals, and developer support. Delivered the VISTA mobile computing experience, and led research on mobility in the consumer environment and shaping the definition of a new category of personal, lifestyle solutions and scalable user interfaces using natural interaction technologies. Work included technology for collaboration, education, entertainment, and productivity; and the globalization of technology. Projects also include a focus on personal expression and the experience of intimacy with technology products. Collaborating with MSR in research on what drives technology adoption and how to predict product success, and using the metrics to drive product design. Previously managed the user experience team for Windows Server System. In addition, serve on Microsoft’s user experience leadership team and chair the user experience managers across the company (driving innovations in methods and tools for user research and design, and evolving Microsoft’s user experience career development program).

  • Lecturer

    University of Washington

    (Computer Software industry)

    2007 — 2009 (2 years )

    Taught User Centered Design for the Human Centered Design and Engineering Department and Systems Analysis and Management for the iSchool.

  • User Experience (Information Architecture and Research), Director

    Sapient

    (Public Company; SAPE; Information Technology and Services industry)

    2000 — 2002 (2 years )

    Responsibilities included developing new research methods, managing Sapient’s global R&D initiatives (introducing consulting solutions based on emerging technologies), developing user experience training, and providing design direction and career oversight for information architects on a variety of projects. Conducted research to project long term technology trends in order to drive the research program and led the development of a speech interface offering. Served on the leadership team for Sapient’s information architecture practice, and also served as a Metro-area Practice Lead, and as a researcher on the Advanced Research Team.

  • Distinguished Member of Technical Staff and Director of User Experience

    US WEST Advanced Technologies

    (Computer Software industry)

    1997 — 2000 (3 years )

    Managed a team of designers and researchers responsible for converging technology products, as well as personally providing design and conducting ethnographic and lab research for U S WEST’s early electronic commerce efforts. Products included a next generation media center, cell phone, and computer applications driving the experience of ubiquitous information, entertainment, and computing; and developing new forms of electronic communities, shopping metaphors, multi-modal experiences, and other innovations. Also served as interim senior director of software development for Advanced Technologies, and U S WEST’s representative to federal and state regulators on accessibility issues.

  • Senior Director, User Experience

    Ameritech

    (Public Company; AIT; Telecommunications industry)

    1989 — 1997 (8 years )

    Helped found the Science and Technology organization; and managed design, user research, and software engineers in order to uncover and deliver new product opportunities. Personal research included developing a tool for assessing the dimensions of user experience and to predict technology adoption; and the tool has been used widely in the industry and in various universities. Product areas included CSCW and teleconferencing, speech, and intelligent tutoring systems. Helped Ameritech enter the distance education and healthcare areas, move into the emerging internet area, and launch its interactive television product line. Managed early research on the use of distance education within high schools, collaborated on groupware for high school science education, and provided design direction for a games-based trainer for telephone installers. Funded and managed Roger Schank’s work to develop an intelligent computer-based training system for customer service representatives (at Northwestern University) and Judy and Gary Olson’s research on remote collaboration (at the University of Michigan). Provided Ameritech’s oversight for the Institute of Learning Science’s research program, MIT’s Media Labs, and Bellcore’s research. Helped Ameritech develop its advertising campaign around the user experience team’s work, and represented Ameritech to a wide variety of external press, analysts, and other forums. Served as interim general manager for product development.

  • HCI Researcher and Manager

    Bell Labs

    (Computer Software industry)

    1980 — 1989 (9 years )

    Managed human factors and systems engineering teams for one of AT&T’s largest product lines (the 800 services family), helped launch what became the ANSI-200 HCI standards effort, and led an effort to develop new design tools (including a prototype of an AI-based design tool). Conducted research on decision tree-based navigation. Served as a leader on the steering committee for the AT&T behavioral sciences community, and served on AT&T’s Market Research Council. Defined and launched a program of research on the cognitive processing of technical content during problem solving. Led an early voice-data system design project, and managed the design of a major user research lab. Led the requirements effort for an early design by example system. Designed and developed portions of the code for a screen handler, and guidelines for electronic forms applications.
