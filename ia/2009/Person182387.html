Pat V. Sonti’s Summary

Pat V. Sonti is an Advisor / Business & Life Coach to companies, trade / policy groups, non-profit & non-governmental organizations worldwide. Mr. Sonti’s diverse career spans nearly 29 years in various sectors and industries covering project management & development, process management and consulting/contracting services. He has worked in multiple areas through the years including energy (conventional / alternate / renewable energy, carbon capture & sequestration (CCS), greenhouse gases (GHG), sustainability, smart grid and energy efficiency), infrastructure, defense, life sciences, and healthcare covering specific areas; technical, commercial, trade, policy, capital formation and structuring investment. He has hands-on knowledge and experience in design & engineering, cost & scheduling, economics, finance, contracts, transactional matters, corporate business operations, policy advocacy, regulatory framework and government relations. He has worked in nearly 25 countries worldwide.

Pat V. Sonti’s Specialties:

Mr. Sonti specializes in; Commercial Market Assessment, Comprehensive Risk Management & Mitigation Analysis, Project Management, Project Development, Project Engineering, Economic Analysis, Project Finance, Legal & Regulatory Framework and Advocacy, and Business Development.

Pat V. Sonti’s Experience

  • President

    Strategic Capital Investments LLC

    (Management Consulting industry)

    January 2006 — July 2009 (3 years 7 months)

    Mr. Sonti, as President, was at Strategic Capital Investments LLC which is an operating company in Short Hills New Jersey USA. He oversaw corporate operations, business development via strategic partners, project management & development and process management activities. Mr. Sonti was involved in structuring of investment, capital formation of debt and equity, structuring of carbon offsets/credits, and regulatory approvals. In addition, at the sister company Resources Assistance Development LLC, Mr. Sonti supported process management and websites/portals services as well as federal government bidding. Specific projects included:

    Enhanced hydrocarbon recovery technology for carbon capture and sequestration (CCS) while producing combined heat and power/polygeneration.

    Waste energy recovery technology using organic rankine cycle technology which captures energy from waste heat and gases and producing combined heat and power/polygeneration while reducing greenhouse gases (GHG).

    Contract generic drug manufacturing; a new facility producing both over-the-counter (OTC) and controlled substances under a U.S. government contract.

    Coal fly ash technology using patended pilot-stage process for handling residue fly ash from coal fired power plants while reducing solid & liquid hazardous wastes.

    Electronic health records technology; Cutting-edge technologies targeting large, medium and small healthcare centers as well as individual physicians.

    Manappad LNG Project, 2.5 MMTPA of imported LNG with 2000 MW power plant and natural gas pipeline in Tamil Nadu, India. Reported directly to India Gas Ltd., Chennai, India.

  • Principal Engineer

    The Ralph M. Parsons Company

    (Management Consulting industry)

    October 1988 — March 1992 (3 years 6 months)

    Mr. Sonti was with The Ralph M.     R-P Conversion; Turnkey up-rating of nearly a dozen GE Frame 5 single-shaft gas turbines from Model R to P driving high pressure tandem compressors from Dresser-Rand, USA.

    Produced Water Injection (PWI): Two, two-shaft, Solar Centaur gas turbines driving high pressure pumps from United Pump-USA.

  • Piping Engineer

    Bechtel Power Corporation

    (Management Consulting industry)

    August 1980 — March 1986 (5 years 8 months)

    Mr. Sonti started his professional career with Bechtel Power Corporation, an international engineering-procurement-construction firm in Norwalk California USA, in fossil and nuclear power projects worldwide. As part of the plant design department, he has worked in various critical aspects commercial nuclear power plant design, ASME Code Sections III & Section XI and NUREG-0588 safety-related aspects including post-Three Mile Island equipment and environmental qualification issues with focus on Loss of Coolant Accidents (LOCA) and Main Steam Line Break (MSLB) incidents. He has extensive experience in pressurized water reactors (PWR) and boiling water reactors (BWR) and related aspects of feasibility reports, permits & clearances, Nuclear Regulatory Commission (NRC) hearings as well as resident field engineering, construction, startup and commissioning. He has also done critical safety systems analysis of Nuclear Steam Supply Systems (NSSS) and ASME Section III Code Break Analysis for major commercial nuclear projects. He has worked on nearly 10 major power projects worldwide. Specific projects included:

    Springerville Power Generating Station, 2 X 500 MW, coal fired power plant, Tucson Electric Power Company.

    Arizona Nuclear Power Project (ANPP), 3 X 1200 MW, Combustion Engineering pressurized water nuclear power plant with GE steam turbine, consortium of Arizona Power Supply and Southern California Edison.

    Korea Nuclear Power Units 5 & 6 (KORI 5 & 6), 2 X 900 MW, Westinghouse pressurized water nuclear power plant and steam turbine, Korea Electric Power Company.

    Korea Nuclear Power Units 7 & 8 (KORI 7 & 8), 2 X 900 MW, Westinghouse pressurized water nuclear power plant and steam turbine, Korea Electric Power Company.

    San Onofre Nuclear Power Generating Station (SONGS), Unit 1, Westinghouse pressurized water nuclear power plant and steam turbine, Southern California Edison.
