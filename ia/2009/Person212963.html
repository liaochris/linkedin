Sebastian Kunz’s Summary

I am a news reporter. It's that simple.

My abilities include live reporting from the field or newsroom, and re-editing radio news stories for online viewing / listening. I'm a skilled live shot reporter, investigative journalist, interviewer, news copy writer, and traffic reporter. In addition, online skills include in-field digital photography/videography, and digital photo/video post-production (Photoshop, Premiere, Avid, Moviemaker).

For a current aircheck example: http://iLike.com/s/8Plzr

My experience is comprised of 15 years of being a news reporter on the radio in small, medium, and large markets around the western U.S. With that experience has come enormous education and the ability to do news for the radio as well as for online video audiences and online readers.

My goal is to be a better news reporter. While it seems most people think that radio people wish they were on television, that isn't necessarily my goal.

  • News Anchor / Reporter

    Clarke Broadcasting (1450 KVML-AM Sonora,CA / 92.7 KZSQ-FM Sonora,CA / 93.5 KKBN-FM, TwainHarte,CA)

    (Broadcast Media industry)

    December 2003 — March 2005 (1 year 4 months)

    Sonora, California is a small town about a half-hour past the turnoff to Yosemite National Park. After Kansas, I graduated to small-town California, where prospecting was a founding industry (and actually still practiced by some locals, apparently).

    While here, I covered Governor Arnold Schwarzenegger's first two State of the State addresses from Sacramento, tons of breaking wildfire coverage live on the scene, as well as the school board, city council and county supervisors meetings. Breaking weather and traffic conditions too... believe me, in mountain country like Sonora, they go together.

    Areas I covered which you may have heard of: Calaveras County (as in Mark Twain's Jumping Frogs story), Angels Camp, Murphys, Twain Harte, Columbia, Copperopolis, Dodge Ridge.

    KVML Radio won the News Station of the Year Award for 2004 by the Associated Press.

  • News Director

    Ingstad Broadcasting (1420 KULY-AM Ulysses, KS / 106.7 KFXX-FM, Hugoton, KS)

    (Broadcast Media industry)

    January 2003 — December 2003 (1 year)

    My first full-time news job after graduation. I _was_ the news department for a station that covers the southwest corner of Kansas. I went to the city council, county commission, and school board meetings. I covered the governor's trip to the Home Products Dinner, the weekly Chamber of Commerce Coffees, trekked to eastern Kansas for the all-state fair, and reported from the all-night walk for breast cancer (which had to be moved from the high school track to the Civic Center because of Breaking News: Severe Weather!) Eddie Ochoa was my supervisor and partner, Eric Richards and Jennifer Diaz were the board ops, and James Janda was the boss that loved us from afar, way up in Garden City KS!

  • News Anchor, show producer

    Arizona State Radio (KASR 1260-AM Tempe, AZ)

    (Broadcast Media industry)

    January 1997 — January 1999 (2 years 1 month)

    I'm not sure they knew what to make of me at Arizona State. Fortunately, it was an alternative station so most people were open minded. I took some ribbing from the sports talk show guys, but it turns out that's just what sports talk guys do.

    They respected my ability, though. Since I'd done the radio news thing for a few years by this time, I'd found my voice and knew how to rock the mic. Most of them were still learning, but hey, college radio is nothing, if not a blast.

    And I produced the Ender show, as well, and picked up another lifelong friend at the same time.

  • News Anchor / Reporter

    Prism Radio Partners (790 KNST-AM, Tucson AZ / 93.7 KRQQ-FM Tucson, AZ / 92.9 KWFM-FM Tucson, AZ)

    (Broadcast Media industry)

    January 1993 — January 1996 (3 years 1 month)

    I brag about being the last American male to learn to edit using a razor blade, grease pencil, and sticky tape.

    My first newsroom actually had no computers at all. Electric typewriters (with erasing-backspace capability), a dot-matrix printer that was the AP wire, reel-to-reels and cart machines were my life those first three years.

    I interned in high school, and they hired me as weekend overnight board operator at semester's end. Eventually I began doing midafternoon reporter work as well, and I started going to city council meetings, the courtroom trials, or just live out in the field somewhere.

    I also started producing the night show down the hall, at top-40 station KRQ, with the DJ who became my good buddy, Ryno, and would substitute for Betsy Bruce on the Mojo & Betsy Show, as heard at:
