Kris Iyer(Sudhir)’s Summary

I started off working as a unix system admin - thought open source/linux were cool and that true power comes from being able to hack systems! While I've come a long way since installing linux from floppies back in 1991, I found myself leaving root windows open and setting suid bits too often. After I almost managed to crash a production system, I decided that I'd be better of as a developer since no one is upset when developers break systems! :-)

Development was fun - liked the creative part of it, but soon realized that there are other people who just seem to code better and faster. So there I was having proven as a second class sysadmin and second class developer and wondering if there is any chance of survival in the industry! :-)

After the bubble burst, a company approached me and said - you seem to be good at nothing, but you have done a little bit of everything, why don't you come start off our test group!? Thanks to the number of offers I had then (zero), I said why not - the worst that can happen is that I become a second class tester! :-)

Automated testing was fun thanks to my development background, and working as a sysadmin helped to understand core infrastructure and architecture better. I liked being able to look at all components of a system and have the freedom to choose any tool that gets the job done best!

At times when I had no idea how to proceed next, it helped to be able to get the work done by sweet talking or bribing people to do it! :-) When people noticed, they went - this guy is delegating (i.e. slacking off! ) a lot; it might make more sense that his title be architect or manager, as in both roles you get to define stuff and let other people actually worry if it makes sense or not! :-)

Jokes apart, I do have the most fun designing and developing solutions to make sure systems work as they are supposed to, and finding ways to make them work better, faster and in more innovative ways that than they did yesterday!
