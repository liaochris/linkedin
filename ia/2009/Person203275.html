Ron Ambrosio’s Summary

Research scientist with a focus on technology, business and policy issues related to smart grids in the electricity industry. Over 25 years of software engineering experience in distributed, real-time systems and their interoperability with enterprise computing environments, and 10 years of experience specifically in the smart grid space.

Oversees IBM’s Energy & Utilities Industry activities in its eight Research Laboratories around the world, and helped establish IBM’s activities in both Intelligent Utility Networks (i.e., smart grids) and Sensors & Actuators.

Began working with the U.S. Department of Energy in 2000 on the planning, collaboration and workshops that led to the creation of the DoE GridWise initiative in late 2002, and later on the planning and launch of the GridWise Alliance industry consortium in 2003. Selected in 2004 to sit on the 13-member DoE GridWise Architecture Council (closely aligned with, but not part of the GridWise Alliance), and was elected Chairman of the Council in 2009.

Active member of ISO/IEC JTC 1/SC 25 Working Group 1, which deals with residential and building automation technology (editor and working party lead for an application interoperability standard -- ISO/IEC 18012), and of IEC TC 8, which addresses System Aspects for Electrical Energy Supply. Also a member of Southern California Edison’s six person external Technical Advisory Board, which advises and provides feedback on SCE’s smart grid program activities, and an external advisor to the U.S. Dept. of Energy’s Pacific Northwest National Laboratory electric vehicle Smart Charger Project.

Ron Ambrosio’s Specialties:

Smart grids, distributed energy resources, demand response, plug-in electric vehicles, interoperability frameworks, event programming models, real time systems, control systems, sensor networks, smart cities, standards, smart grid business models, smart grid policy
