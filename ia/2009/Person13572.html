Andreas Umland’s Summary

I study the post-Soviet extreme right at the Eichstaett Institute for Central and East European Studies (ZIMOS), a small, yet active research center, in Upper Bavaria, focusing on current Russian political and intellectual history.

I would be glad to hear from people with similar research interests, and from those wanting to publish their dissertations, monographs, edited volumes or document collections in the English- and German-language book series "Soviet and Post-Soviet Politics and Society" where 85 volumes were published in 2004-2008 (www.ibidem-verlag.de/spps.html ). Among the particular (but not only) foci of this series are Russian nationalism, post-communist politics, and higher education in CEE or the NIS.

ZIMOS's web journal "Forum noveishei vostochnoevropeiskoi istorii i kul'tury" (www1.ku-eichstaett.de/ZIMOS/forumruss.html ) invites Russian-language scholarly papers in the fields of contemporary East European history, post-Soviet politics, and Eurasian cultural studies.

If interested, please, do not hesitate to join the groups "Russian Nationalism" (links lists, papers, listserv & newsletter) at http://groups.yahoo.com/group/russian_nationalism/ , "Researchers of Russian Nationalism" (networking, updates & discussion) at http://www.linkedin.com/groups?about=&gid=157897 , and "Researchers of Historical and Neo-Fascism" (networking, updates & discussion) at http://www.linkedin.com/e/vgh/1640147/ as well as at http://www.facebook.com/group.php?gid=52910686741.

If you use AmazonConnect, I would be glad to add you to the "Interesting People" or "Friends" sections at http://www.amazon.com/gp/pdp/profile/ABDE6L94T5H8R/.

If you have worked in Eastern Europe or the NIS, I would be interested in comments, additions and corrections concerning the guide "Teach at a university in the former Soviet bloc" at http://www.amazon.com/gp/richpub/syltguides/fullview/REKTGK0DL1N8F/ .

Andreas Umland’s Specialties:

TEACHING EXPERIENCE:
Late Tsarist, Soviet and post-Soviet history; post-communist transitions; Russian politics; ultra-nationalism in today Russia; rise and fall of fascism; Nazism in comparative perspective; inter-war German ultra-conservatism; contemporary European right-wing radicalism; history and problems of European integration; EU institutions; introduction to the methodology of social science; macrohistorical comparison; political concepts and terminology.
