Luke Wallace’s Summary

I’ve worked in the media industry for 9 years, and throughout my career, I’ve gained invaluable experience in the fields of publishing, digital communications, marketing, media sales, public relations, sales promotions and journalism. From this background, I’m an advocate on basing communications around content, which in my methodology, is the most engaging platform for communication, and the most exciting form of currency being traded online by consumers, powered by publishers and fueled by brands. I have a solid insight into the digital media landscape, new and emerging media such as social networking, blogs, podcasts, web 2.0, RSS, widgets, viral campaigns, content sharing, IPTV, interactive gaming, mobile web and applications. I’m a part time blogger, and social media activist, in which I combine my 2 favourite interests, digital media and surfing in my blog; digitalsurfmedia.com

I’ve lived and worked overseas for 6 years from London to California, traveling extensively and immersing myself in multiple cultures and influences which have enabled me to paint a fresh, colourful outlook on how I approach my goals and objectives.

The label drew it’s name from the Nina Simon classic, Funkier Than A Mosquito’s Tweeter.

    Marketing & PR Strategy: Conceived the brand identity, marketing direction and launch model of the label ‘Mosquito’s Tweeter’, implementing the strategic release of records by generating word of mouth hype and street presence around the label and its bands via the integration of guerilla marketing and teaser campaigns. Employed a PR agency to conduct press campaigns, obtain radio play, TV coverage and promote national tours. Planned, negotiated and booked advertising campaigns with various publishers across print, digital and street. Leveraged the hype surrounding emerging social media platforms such as Myspace and blogs at the time to promote the label & releases. Commissioned video directors to create music video clips for promotional use online & TV.

    Contract Negotiation / Licensing: Negotiated contractual obligations of music copyright and ownership with artist management to further artist development. Licensed music to including overseas record labels, marketing companies for promotional use and 3rd parties for sync licensing for TV shows and DVD release. Commissioned 3rd party remixes from internationally known producers, further generating PR coverage and awareness to a wider and new demographic.

    Sales Promotion / Point Of Sale: Developed P.O.S promotional items and price promotion incentives and partnerships with selected retail outlets and chains.
