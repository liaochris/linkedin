  • Co-Founder

    RightSide Advisors, Inc.

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Internet industry)

    2001 — 2006 (5 years)

    My first venture as an owner...We produced a line of investing newsletters and related research products. We started with virtually nothing except our laptops and brain power...That first year we netted six hundred thousand; for the next five years increased revenues (and profits), acquired two companies in our segment and launched around fifteen products. We sold to a private equity group who is building out an internet media portfolio. We became experts at customer acquisition and the ever-changing online advertising market. This was also the time in my life when my two real babies arrived...so I started the balancing act of mothering & running a company.

  • VP Community

    StockJungle

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Internet industry)

    1999 — 2001 (2 years)

    Like GeoCities, StockJungle.com also was a community but it brought together stock market addicts who managed virtual portfolios. I ran the community and oversaw development of the website. We raised venture capital funding and opened the first naked mutual fund - a fund fully disclosed to its investors and powered by the stock picks the community generated.

  • Sales Executive

    GeoCities

    (Public Company; 201-500 employees; Internet industry)

    1998 — 1999 (1 year)

    This is what I call my real-time MBA. I started on a small but growing sales team at GeoCities, saw the staff triple to over 300, went through a quiet period and very exiting IPO and then the 4.7B acquisition by Yahoo. It was an amazing experience; dot com fever at its highest. I worked on the ad sales team but being in the home office, I also got to see the community evolve (this was the site that had "neighborhoods" connecting people with common interests), the battle for the top spot in Media Metrix among the big sites, the struggles of a start up as it grew & became more bureaucratic, the huge ad buys by clients trying to buy clicks to boost their traffic (a la GoTo.com which became Overture and then Yahoo).

  • Sales Executive

    Discovery Channel Online

    (Privately Held; 501-1000 employees; Media Production industry)

    1997 — 1998 (1 year)

    As an Sales Assistant this was my induction into the world of online advertising. Under an excellent manager I learned the ropes of selling, client presentations, the automotive industry and managing ad traffic. This was a fun time; I was working for a big brand during the initial stages of internet growth. Major companies were just starting to allocate some of their ad dollars online and they felt very loyal to Discovery. Discovery Networks itself is also a fascinating company, with excellent product & quite a large staff.
