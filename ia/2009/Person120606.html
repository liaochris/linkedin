Joseph Alpern’s Summary

My thesis at Hampshire was on "Entrepreneurship Within the Context of Sustainability". I am currently enrolled in the Environmental Governance masters program at the University of Freiburg, Germany.
Up until now I have spent much of my time and creative energy focused on analyzing the current trends in the eating patterns of the Western world and their connection to human health and environmental sustainability. I seek to influence as many people as possible to become informed on the connection between human health, eating patterns and sustainability in order to improve the condition of peoples lives and the environment. I feel the best way to do this is to create and invest in companies which accomplish this task.
Recently I have become completely absorbed in and inspired by the emerging phenomenon of "Location-Based Social Networking". I believe that the future potential of this phenomenon is incredible and that the opportunity to play an active part in the evolution of relevant technologies should not be neglected.

    MSc , Environmental Governance , 2008 — 2009

    The program has been developed with the clear ambition to be ranked among the top 10 international study programs in the field of sustainability, society and environment worldwide.

    Unique worldwide, its focus is on analysing the ways of reconciling the manifold stakeholder interpretations of sustainable use of environmental resources and of facilitating the institutionalisation of context-effective environmental governance arrangements that combine market, state and civil society regulation modes across functional, sectoral and institutional boundaries for sustainable development.

    A maximum of 30 students are selected each year based on criteria of excellence and geographical representation (industrialized countries, countries in transition, developing countries as well as geographic regions).
