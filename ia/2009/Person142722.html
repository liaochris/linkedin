Liz Moench’s Summary

When it comes to healthcare I'm a firm believer that information empowers patients to be a part of the decision making process. Over 20 years ago, I pioneered the first DTC advertising campaign in pharmaceutical history for prescription ibuprofen. Several years later I applied the DTC marketing model to clinical trials for Taxotere, a drug now used to treat women with breast and ovarian cancer. This was the impetus for establishing MediciGroup (now MediciGlobal) in 1991; a specialty patient recruitment-retention firm dedicated to marketing clinical trials directly to patients and their families across the globe. Before starting this niche industry to raise awareness about clinical trials, few people knew that they could consider study participation as an option. Now patients know they have choices, and as a result biopharmaceutical and medical device companies across the globe have benefitted from access to patients.

I work with and advise biopharmaceutical companies of all sizes; publicly held and venture-funded. We work to accelerate their studies, minimize their burn rate, and enhance the value of their portfolios by expeditiously moving their products through the different phases of research. As an industry expert I speak on all aspects of accelerating clinical trials and increasing recruitment-retention rates. I’ve held workshops for sponsors, physicians and study coordinators in nearly 20 different countries, giving them the necessary tools to be successful in recruiting and retaining study subjects. I started the Drug Information Association conferences and tutorials on patient recruitment to specifically help clinical sponsors plan and execute patient recruitment-retention programs.

I'm privileged to be doing what I really enjoy. I continue to make a difference, set trends and see the results. It is so gratifying to know that my team has helped so many new treatments and line extensions get through the clinical trial process and reach the market.

Liz Moench’s Experience

  • President & CEO

    MediciGlobal

    (Pharmaceuticals industry)

    1991 — Present (18 years)

    MediciGlobal is the global leader in marketing clinical trials to patients, caregivers and health care professionals. We’ve achieved this by implementing culturally sensitive patient recruitment and retention programs to meet ethics guidelines in countries around the world. We are working to bring information about clinical trials to patients and their families across a range of therapeutic areas. I lead a dynamic team of experts who excel in their respective disciplines; some of the best minds in the industry. The results they achieve and the national and international awards they’ve earned attest to this. I have clients with whom we have worked for years tell me our team is “the best!” I agree with them. I ensure our clients can depend on MediciGlobal for results - bringing their new drugs to market faster, saving them time and money; factors critical in today's environment. Watch us as our technology innovations continue to expedite clinical trial recruitment and retention!

  • Corporate Vice President, Public Affairs & Corporate Communications

    Rhone Poulenc Rorer (sanofi-aventis)

    (Pharmaceuticals industry)

    1989 — 1991 (2 years)

    Rhone Poulenc Rorer (now sanofi-aventis), is still one of the top 10 global pharmaceutical companies, and it was in this position that I headed Public Affairs & Corporate Communications on a worldwide basis, leading a cross cultural team in France and the U.S. I spearheaded industry’s first clinical trial recruitment program for Taxotere®, and I led product PR campaigns for OTC and prescription brands including Maalox®, Lovenox®, Nasacort® and Granocyte®. I personally led meetings between clinical teams and AIDS activists and established relationships with key patient advocacy groups. In this position I also oversaw all media inquiries and represented the company publicly. I developed public policy positions, directed the content and design of the corporation's annual and quarterly investor reports, and managed public relations agency relationships.

  • Executive Director, Public Affairs

    Ciba-Geigy (Novartis)

    (Public Company; Pharmaceuticals industry)

    1984 — 1989 (5 years)

    At Ciba-Geigy I led all patient directed marketing efforts leveraging the use of PR and advocacy. I directed and developed high-profile PR/media relations campaigns working with and involving famous celebrities to launch several major prescriptions brands including Ritalin®, Voltaren®, Estraderm®, Transderm Scop®, and Tegretol® which resulted in national media coverage reaching millions. Voltaren became the #1 most widely prescribed NSAID at the time following our 3 months of publicity alone. I also directed and oversaw issues management and the development of position papers and strategies to handle product withdrawals, congressional inquiries, FDA hearings, drug risk issues, animal welfare and more. When animal activists were boycotting products and picketing pharma, I wrote a book about the use of animals in research that featured celebrities and nationally recognized medical specialists, including the Inspector General C.Everett Koop and others. Over 200,000 copies were disseminated.

  • Director of Marketing Communications

    Boots Pharmaceuticals

    (Pharmaceuticals industry)

    1980 — 1984 (4 years)

    Boots Pharmaceuticals (now BASF Pharma) marketed prescription drugs for hypothyroidism, pain and arthritis. I started initially as a Product Manager and was later promoted to Director. It was here that I pioneered the first DTC advertising campaign for a prescription medicine (ibuprofen). I strongly believe that the patient is the ultimate consumer of prescription medicines; after all they are the end user. As such, I wanted to get consumers involved in their treatment decisions, (I follow this belief today through patient recruitment for clinical trials). It was through the launch of the DTC campaign that I was catapulted into high level FDA discussions, public policy and speaking, and developed resilience to the initial backlash from physicians who were threatened by a more informed patient. Wow, have times ever changed! In being a trendsetter and an industry pioneer, I learned skills that have transcended my career. I thank so many people for believing in me and allowing me to succeed.
