Brian Costello’s Summary

Media executive with many years experience at the crossroads of technology and creativity. Interested in interactive and mobile marketing as it relates to enhancing the consumer experience. I enjoy working to create more usable, interesting, and fun applications and online experiences that are better designed, more focused, and offer greater depth to brands and consumers. Creativity is what drives me most -- in building teams, businesses, and experiences for consumers.

Brian Costello’s Experience

  • Valassis Inc.

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; VCI; Marketing and Advertising industry)

    September 2006 — Present (2 years 8 months)

    RedPlum.com is an award-winning consumer portal for deals, values, coupons, tips and articles for the busy woman. I'm working with a great team to create The RedPlum Network -- a series of destination sites for women to find great deals and great editorial. I'm working closely with all of our brands and partners to explore new ways to offer them a better way to access and interact with consumers via online, mobile, new media, live events and printed media.

    As always, I'll be working to develop ideas -- fun, creative, interesting ways to create interactive experiences that work for a brand and make people smile and buy stuff. Internet, mobile, integrated new media -- we use it all and come up with great ways to engage and acquire customers.

  • MetroMystics Technology Corp.

  • Cambridge Resources Group

    (Marketing and Advertising industry)

    July 1996 — October 1999 (3 years 4 months)

    Built a professional education and marketing services company that catered to high level executives and professionals in large and medium sized corporations. Included development of media campaigns for product launches, internal executive education, and consulting. Marketing services clients includes Pfizer, Johnson & Johnson, GlaxoSmithKline and Merck.

  • Advertising, Lobster, Jewelry and More!

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Consumer Goods industry)

    May 1983 — July 1996 (13 years 3 months)

    I worked in advertising and marketing services for a number of years -- had a few entrepreneurial gigs, early on I worked as a jewelry maker, a plumbing sales guy at Sears, an abuse investigator and even a lobster fisherman.

    All in all I would say that these years formed the entrepreneur that is me today -- from learning how hard it is to be a fisherman on Massachusetts Bay -- and having the drive to stay with it (and having scars to prove it!), to seeing how a craftsman makes a living selling handmade jewelry, to building businesses with no money but a lot of hope and real sweat (being the CEO, Customer Service Rep and everything in-between), to learning how to sell to consumers, -- even learning how to work within major bureaucracies -- it all taught me things that no business school ever could -- basically that life experience does matter more than you could know!
