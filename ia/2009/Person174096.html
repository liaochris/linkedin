Nick Gagalis’s Summary

I strive to be the best storyteller I can be. I aim for accuracy and compelling writing and pictures to bring the reader/viewer in. I consider myself an educator and entertainer, because the best journalists fill both those roles.

I have a strong background in Print and Multimedia Journalism to go with my Broadcast Journalism major. Being able to report from many different angles gives me a better knowledge of the subject, and it shows in my stories.

Nick Gagalis’s Specialties:

broadcasting, play by play, color commentary, hosting, reporting, anchoring, writing, still photography, video photography, audio producing, video producing, public relations, media relations

Nick Gagalis’s Experience

  • Play-by-Play Broadcaster

    Brewster Whitecaps, Cape Cod Baseball League

    (Broadcast Media industry)

    May 2007 — Present (2 years 1 month)

    For the past two seasons, I have called Whitecaps games that streamed over the internet. I have written and produced the pre- and post-game shows, including interviews with players, managers, and other guests.

    I have also hosted the two episodes of Cape League Now. The first was a preview of the All-Star Game in Chatham, and the second was a playoff preview we recorded in Harwich. I have encouraged broadcasters from other teams to break down the action, and we included player and manager interviews as well.

  • Host, Panelist, and Producer

    BUTV 10

    (Broadcast Media industry)

    February 2007 — May 2009 (2 years 4 months)

    I have roles both on- and off-camera for two different shows on BUTV. I am a producer for Offsides, a sports magazine show, and I also regularly contribute to the debates on-screen. Additionally, I have interviewed our experts on different leagues, including the NBA and the NHL.

    I have been an anchor, editor, and assistant producer for Terrier Nation, which is the BU athletics equivalent of Sportscenter.

  • Reporter

    Neighborhood Network News

    (Broadcast Media industry)

    September 2008 — May 2009 (9 months)

    I have reported on various issues in Boston, ranging from the ban of trans fats in its restaurants to the closing of a local record store. My most recent project is on a program that helps keep kids out of trouble in Dorchester.

  • Broadcaster

    WTBU

    (Broadcast Media industry)

    January 2007 — May 2009 (2 years 5 months)

    I have filled many roles at Boston University's sports radio leader. I'm the Color Commentator for men's ice hockey, and I've been the Play-by-Play Broadcaster for women's softball and women's soccer. I have done extensive work in-studio for all of the sports, covering the pre-game, intermission, and post-game shows.

    I've also been a host of the Friday Night Sports Block, as well as an updater for the station and Public Address Announcer too.

  • Writer

    New England Hockey Journal

    (Writing and Editing industry)

    May 2008 — August 2008 (4 months)

    I reported on and wrote stories for publication in both the print and online editions of the journal. I learned a great lesson in putting away bias when I interviewed the NCAA Champion Boston College Eagles (BU's rival) for my first story.

  • Morning Show Production Intern

    WEEI Sports Talk Radio

    (Public Company; ETM; Broadcast Media industry)

    January 2008 — May 2008 (5 months)

    Rising early was no problem for me after my first internship, so the biggest potential hurdle was out of the way. I edited clips of the show to put on the website, gathered clips from other stations to use, and searched the internet for relevant and/or wacky news stories.

  • Production Intern

    New England Sports Network

    (Privately Held; Broadcast Media industry)

    January 2008 — May 2008 (5 months)

    I learned more about television in my internship at NESN than anywhere else. I had to do a laundry list of different tasks to make the content run smoothly. Whether it was managing the stage at an on-site pre-game show, helping the talent prepare through research, or writing reports on various subjects to educate the producers, I did it all.

  • Station Intern

    ESPN Radio

    (Public Company; Broadcast Media industry)

    August 2007 — December 2007 (5 months)

    I had a more important role at 890 ESPN than at any of my other radio or TV station internships. 890 relied on me for production and public relations help, and it gave me a great understanding of how mediums like it function. The relationship between sales and content was one I had not truly grasped until I worked there.

  • Morning Show Intern

    WZLX Radio

    (Broadcast Media industry)

    May 2007 — August 2007 (4 months)

    Working for Karlson & McKenzie was my first internship. It was a great segue from being a kid to being someone aware of the entertainment world's function and motives. I researched topics the talent wanted to talk about, wrote some material for the show, appeared on it a few times, and edited highlights for the website. I also catered to the guests the station had when they visited. Meeting Peter Gammons again was one of the best parts of working there.

  • Staff Writer

    Daily Free Press

    (Writing and Editing industry)

    September 2006 — May 2007 (9 months)

    I attended BU sporting events and wrote game stories and sidebars for the paper. The majority of my work was on the track, tennis, and wrestling teams, which challenged me to write well about topics not as interesting to the average viewer as the most popular sports.

  • Youth Council Member

    (Religious Institutions industry)

    I planned fundraisers and other events for the parish, did community service at various churches and non-profit organizations, and was an interim co-director while the director was on sabbatical.
