Marco Mancuso / Digicult’s Summary

Marco Mancuso (IT/HON) studied Letters/Contemporary Art and Journalism in Milan. He is a freelance journalist, critic, art curator and consultant about new media, digital culture and electronic arts in Italy.

He lives and works in Milan and he has been working and writing for many technology, comunication, art and design Italian magazines in the last 10 years and he's writings were pubblished in some festival catalogues (like Share festival, Mixed Media festival, Dissonanze and Screen Music) and exhibition catalogues (like 0006_Limiteazero in 2006 and op7_Otolab in 2007).

He is the founder and director of the digital art & electrionic culture project DigiCult (www.digicult.it), of the monthly magazine DigiMag (www.digicult.it/digimag), the podcast DigiPod (www.digicult.it/podcast) and the newsletter service DigiNews. For DigiCult he worked as guest curator for some festivals like Piemonte Share Festival, Diesel-U-Music Award, Dissonanze Festival, Mixed Media Festival, Screen Music Festival and for international events like Cimatics and Strp festival. He worked through DigiCult also as media partner for some electronic culture festivals and events like Audiovisiva, Netmage, Peam, Dissonanze, Struttura, ClubToClub, Freeshout, Lpm, Cimatics, Optronica, Elektra, Todaysart, Art Tech Media, Sonar, Strp among others.

Finally, he's working with the new born art-agency DigiMade as curator/promoter for some Italian artists, pushing their work inside the main international festivals, events, platforms and cultural centers in Europe working with digital and electronics.

Marco Mancuso teaches "Digital Creative Media" at NABA-New Academy of Beautifual Arts in Milan and "New Media Art & Communication" at IED-European Institute of Design in Milan, and he's invited as guest lecturer in many Italian Universities

    Digicult

    January 2005 — Present (4 years 2 months)

    "Digicult" is a cultural project involved in digital culture and electronic arts.

    The DigiCult project is based on the active participation of 40 professional people about, who represent the first wide Italian network of journalists, curators, artists and critics working in the field of electronic culture and digital art.

    Translated in English, DigiCult is today a web portal updated daily with news and informations, but it's also the editor of the monthly magazine DigiMag. DigiCult produce the electronic music and audiovisual podcast DigiPod and the newsletter international service DigiNews.

    DigiCult is finally involved in side activities like media partnerships for festivals and exhibitions, curatorial projects and special productions, and is now working for Italian artists international promotion through the new born art agency DigiMade.
