Matt Pallatt’s Summary

I'm a confident, passionate and multi-disciplined Technical Director who has won two international innovation pitching awards in recent years. I have a love of all things digital, especially data and how we can use it to connect in truly technically innovative ways with people.

I've 7 years client facing experience and over 3 years experience managing the cross-skilled development departments of two top 20 UK digital marketing agencies at the same time - growing them over 1500% in that time.

Matt Pallatt’s Experience

  • twentysix Leeds

    (Marketing and Advertising industry)

    November 2005 — Present (3 years 4 months)

    In November 2005 CMW North became part of the twentysix group, and I became Technical Director of the northern agency, taking the role of Technical Director for CMW Interactive. I ceased to be TD of CMWi in September 2007.

    While in my dual roles I was responsible for the standard of all technical solutions that the companies produces, being involved in the conception of all applications that are developed. My roles also involve hands on development across multiple platforms, as well as sole responsibility in architecturing & documenting the solutions that we provide and also management of the development teams.

    I continue to work very closely with client relationship teams and drive technical solutions with our current clientbase, as well as to work with potential clients to get to the bottom of their requirements and come up with answers to deliver against them.

    In the time since taking on this role the technical department has increased in size to over 15 people.

  • Brand New Media

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Online Media industry)

    June 2000 — December 2002 (2 years 7 months)

    From June 2000 I worked at BNM as a web developer, alongside up to 8 other developers. Unfortunately as the dotcom bust happened the agency went down to just me and the head of technology and so my skill set had had to be all encompassing.

    This meant that while I originally joined the agency as a client side developer, albeit with extensive server side experience, my proficiencies quickly had to grow to encompass the whole gamut of developer skills - from front side JavaScript, through to back end ASP and PHP, as well as a thorough knowledge of SQL databases.

    I worked as primary developer for many clients including Powergen, Heinz and Hasbro, and was primary technical contact for clients including Durex.

  • Entranet

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Online Media industry)

    February 1999 — June 2000 (1 year 5 months)

    My role at Entranet was one of facilitating the relationship between back end developers who had no design considerations, and a creative team who had no desire to water down their designs to make it easier for the programmers to deliver a final product.

    When not acting as the liaison between these two teams I spent much of my time focussing on developing my client side programming skills, at the same time as developing my ASP development, for clients such as Williams F1 and Goldfish Bank.

    Towards the end of my time at Entranet I moved into a client facing role where I would work with key members of the Entranet management team to make technical recommendations to clients including Royal Bank of Scotland.

  • IXL

    (Public Company; 51-200 employees; Online Media industry)

    March 1998 — February 1999 (1 year)

    After university (which I left early in order to pursue my career) I joined iXL, which was at the time the world's largest internet development agency. Spent a year working on front end interface development (specialising in both Javascript and VBScript) for clients such as GE Capital Bank and Forte Hotels.

    The highlight of my time at iXL was working on the first incarnation of the NHS Direct website, and also being the lead client side developer for General Electric - at the time the largest e-development contract in the world.
