Ivonne Valeria Muñoz Torres’s Summary

Since august of 2002 she has been a facilitator in the Information Security Extension Course sponsored by ITESM teaching the Legal and Ethic Aspects of the Information Security. In August 2004 she participated as instructor of the module: Legal Aspects in Telecommunications, for the Degree of Networks and Telecommunications in Campus San Luis Potosi of the ITESM.

In her academic preparation is important to include: Post degree in E-Government sponsored by Mundial Bank and ITESM; “Cyber Crimes and Computer Forensics” sponsored by the United States Secret Service , the Federación Panamericana de Seguridad Privada, Chapter México, A.C., & Prisma Consulting Services (2003); “Ethics across the curricula” at Loyola University Chicago, Center for Ethics and Social Justice. (2002); Specialization course MERCOSUR NAFTA-ALCA, “Formation and training of international consultants and arbitrates in E-Arbitration”, organized by the Conciliation & Arbitration International Court of MERCOSUR. Certificate as International Arbitrate received (2002); Post degree in Information Systems Law. Buenos Aires University (Argentina) – Law Faculty (2001). By the University of Post graduate studies in Law, achieved the: Course in Societary Law (2000), Course in Registrar Law (2000) and the Course in Notarial Law (1999).

She has been interviewed by diverse communication media as TV Azteca, Reforma Journal, La Jornada Journal, El Financiero Journal, Radio Monitor News, El Universal Journal, Notimex News and Terra Networks News to give her expert opinion related to: privacy, theft identity, phshing, computer forensics, e-commerce, electronical signatures, electronical fraud and other issues related to Informatics law.

In the last two years, external consultant of IFAI (Instituto Federal de Acceso a la Información) and Legal Advisor in cases involved with computer forensics.

Recently, she´s involved in computer forensics issues as an expert witness on mexican trials.
