Rajanish Dass’s Summary

Prof. Dass is currently a faculty in the Computer & Information Systems Group at the Indian Institute of Management, Ahmedabad. His ares of interest is primarily primarily focussing on how technology can for better decision making, create competitive opportunities for organizations. His primary research and teaching interests are in the areas of Strategic Information Systems, IT Policies of organizations, Technology impact on society, Competitive Intelligence and Data Mining. He also works on current issues of e-Governance in India and has a keen interest in the evolution of the Indian IT industry. He has received numberous scholarships and has published a number of research papers in forums like national and international research conferences, journals and as book chapters.

Some of his consulting clients include organizations like the SEWA, Cisco, Sun MicroSystems, TCS, IBM, RedHat Linux, Call One, Ceon Solutions, John Deere, University Grants Commission of India and Government of India. He has done executive training programs for top level management executives of ACC Cement, Cisco, Hemas, Astro, Maxis, Apollo Tyres, Indian Bank, CallOne, Lupin etc. Prof. Dass is also serving as a member in the Board of Directors in e-GovServices, Ceon Solutions and the Banknet Group. He was the External Consultant to the Ministry of Finance of India for all the IT initiatives for Direct and Indirect Taxes from May 2006 to March 2008 and has played key role in identifying information bottlenecks, advising on the overall IT portfolio, helping departments identifying roadblocks proactively for successful implementation of technology projects etc.

Rajanish Dass’s Specialties:

Technology Strategy Planning for support, operational efficiency and competitive advantage, Technology adoption, How business problems can be addressed using technology as an enabler, Competitive advanrage using advanced analytics techniques, Technology planning for rural businesses
