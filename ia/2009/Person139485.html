Lei (Leon) SHU’s Summary

Lei SHU is a research scientist in the Digital Enterprise Research Institute (DERI), at the National University of Ireland, Galway (NUIG), with DERI Vice-Director Dr. Prof. Manfred Hauswirth. He received his B.S. degree in Computer Science from South Central University for Nationalities, China, 2002, and M.S. degree in Computer Engineering from Kyung Hee University, Korea, 2005, and currently is writing up his PhD thesis in Digital Enterprise Research Institute, NUIG. He has published over 50 papers in related international conferences and journals. He has served as editor of 10 International Journals. He has served as reviewers of many conferences and journals, such as EUC06, ICC06, SOSA07, CIMK07, BROADNETS08, ICION09, WCNC09, AINA09, IEEE Network Magazine, Springer Wireless Personal Communication(WPC), Wiley Wireless Communication and Mobile Computing(WCMC), and Mobile Networks and Applications(MONET). He has served as TPC members of BROADNETS08, CONET08, ACHI08-09, WAMSN08(FGCN08), PerDev09(Percom09), IWCMC09, and BROADNETS09. He has taken part in several projects: “Integrating Heterogeneous Sensor Networks into One ”, “CAMUS: Context-Aware Middleware for Ubiquitous Computing Systems”, “Sensor OS”, “GSN: Global Sensor Network Middleware for Interconnecting Heterogeneous Sensing Systems”, and "Semantic Reality". His research interests include semantic sensor networks, wireless multimedia sensor networks, wireless sensor network, context aware middleware, and sensor network middleware. He has implemented a new wireless sensor networks simulator & visualizer: NetTopo. He is a member of the Libelium WSN group. He is a member of ACM and IEEE.
