Mary McGarry’s Summary

My last 12 years of work have been the most challenging and rewarding. As a community nurse serving the mentally ill population I have come to appreciate the many obstacles that they face every day. I also see that the public is so poorly educated and therefore the stigma remains an ongoing issue.
It is a social issue that is poorly funded, yet has proven to be the most draining on our system.
I believe there isn't a family in our society that has not been touched by mental illness.
Goal: to make a difference in those lives I touch.
Dream: to have psychiatrists working along side family doctor/or to have Mental Health Clinics set up in the city of London.

Mary McGarry’s Specialties:

Helping to provide quality of life to the severely mentally ill of our community.

In 1987 I made the leap over to Mental Health and never looked back! I have returned to LHSC as a Casual RN on their Mental Health units to keep up my skills in acute care settings.
