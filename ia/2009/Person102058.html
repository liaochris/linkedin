Jakub Suder’s Summary

Last year I finished my M.Sc. studies in Computer Science at AGH University of Science and Technology in Krakow. Since February 2008 I'm working at Lunar Logic Polska as a Ruby on Rails developer.

I've recently became interested in dynamic languages such as Python and Ruby and learned a bit about both of them. I agree with Paul Graham that “Wasting programmer time is the true inefficiency, not wasting machine time”, that's why I would like to use languages like Python and Ruby in my current and future jobs - they let programmers make less boring work, even if it means more boring work for computers (that's what they are for anyway).

In the world of IT, everything changes at a terrible speed - what was a novelty yesterday, will be obsolete tomorrow. I'm aware that to stay in touch I need to constantly learn new technologies, languages, frameworks, etc. So I prefer such jobs that let me learn new things, and not only use the things that I already know.

I've heard a lot about agile development methods (Extreme Programming, Scrum, Test-Driven Development, etc.), and although I didn't have much opportunity to try them in practice yet, I believe they are a better approach to creating software than older, more strict methodologies, like waterfall. I hope I will be able to gain some experience in those agile techniques soon.
