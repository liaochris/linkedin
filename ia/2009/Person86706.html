Gil Parsley’s Experience

  • Regional Manager

    Countrywide Home Loans

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; CFC; Financial Services industry)

    January 2004 — January 2007 (3 years 1 month)

    Responsibilities included the generation of residential loan portfolios purchased from Mortgage Bankers in the region.
    This was an extremely competitive and crowded field, but the region grew from about $20,000,000 monthly to over $50,000,000 monthly. This was achieved by developing my own sales team to cover this large geographic area.
    My region included the counties of Los Angeles and San Diego, and the states of Arizona, Utah, and Nevada.
    Compensation was performance based and I was ranked in the top two income spots for Regional Managers nationally.
    Training materials for customers and sales people were non-existent when I took the position and the materials that I created on the job were later adopted by the entire national sales team.

  • Client Sales Manager

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; HBC; Financial Services industry)

    January 2003 — December 2003 (1 year)

    This position was my entree into the mortgage industry.

  • Regional Manager - Florida

  • National Leasing Director

    Basic Capital Management

    (Real Estate industry)

    January 1993 — January 1996 (3 years 1 month)

    Served as the traveling trouble shooter for this 12 million square foot national portfolio of office, industrial, and retail properties.
    Worked my way out of my position in a three year period by solving the major vacancy problems in the portfolio.
    Leasing agents under my supervision leased 1.2 million square feet, 2.2 million square feet, and 2.9 million square feet in successive years. Increased the occupancy of the entire portfolio to 90%.
    Took a 73% vacant office building in downtown San Francisco to less than 5% vacant in eighteen months.
    Improved the vacancy in a major office building in St. Petersburg, Florida from 45% vacant to less than 5% vacant in two years.
    Both of these successes required some really creative thinking and aggressive decision making to achieve these successes.

  • Regional Leasing Director

    Southmark Commercial Property Management

    (Real Estate industry)

    February 1989 — August 1990 (1 year 7 months)

    Supervised a leasing team of twelve in-house leasing agents and orchestrated the leasing efforts of nine commercial real estate brokers scattered over ten western states.
    The leasing team completed 420 leases totaling 1.4 million square feet in eighteen months.
    The 5 million square foot portfolio consisted of office and industrial parks. Southmark was one of many companies that declared bankruptcy due to the change in capital gains tax laws that greatly affected the value of the portfolio.
