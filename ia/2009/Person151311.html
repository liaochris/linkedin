Mark Barbash’s Summary

I have served in government policy and management positions at the local, state and federal levels.

At the local level, I served as Director of Development for the City of Columbus for seven years, managing a 400 person department, from 2000 through 2007.

At the state level, I currently serve as Assistant Director/Chief Economic Development Officer for the Ohio Department of Development, a department of 400 people. From 1985 through 1989, I served as Deputy Director of the Division of Business Development/Development Finance, a division of ODOD, which had a staff of about 50. In the early '70's, I was assistant to Governor Patrick J. Lucey of Wisconsin.

At the federal level, I served as Executive Assistant to the Federal Cochairman of the Upper Great Lakes Regional Commission, an agency of the U.S. Department of Commerce, promoting economic development in Northern Wisconsin, Minnesota and Michigan.

Also at the federal level, I served on the staffs of two federal level elected officials, U.S. Senator Gaylord Nelson and U.S. Congressman Les Aspin, both from Wisconsin.

On a consulting basis, I was Vice President of the National Development Council, providing training, technical assistance and real estate development services NDC is a a New York based consulting firm that worked with local, federal, and state government, as well as non-profit and private economic development organizations.

Mark Barbash’s Specialties:

Strategic planning and policy development, development finance, real estate development, international trade and development, budget and finance, management and motivation of professional staff.
