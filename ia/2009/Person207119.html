Robert E. Novak’s Summary

As Director of Enterprise Servers for Supermicro, Robert has created the Supermicro Superstructure(tm) line of rack products to deploy high density servers. Robert Novak has served on the board of directors for GarrettCom, Inc. from 1992-2006. An experienced computing professional, Robert has had a hand in pioneering some of the major technology advances in the computer revolution. Initially affiliated with the telephony industry at GTE Automatic Electric, he moved to Silicon Valley to work with both Pyramid Technology and MIPS Computer Systems prior to their IPOs. He was a founder of Axil Computers, a division of Hyundai Electronics. At MIPS, Robert chaired the SPEC Steering Committee and shepherded the first release of the SPEC performance benchmarks for CPUs. At Sun Microsystems, he was in charge of Nomadic strategy for SunSoft and introduced the SolarNet PC-X product, an X-Windows environment for MS-Windows PCs. Robert later returned to Sun as Director of E-Commerce marketing as part of the Sun Netscape Alliance.

He has served as Vice-President of Sales and Marketing for CommTouch Software, Inc. Robert was Vice-President of Technology for Digeo, a company focusing on Interactive Television. Robert also served as the General Manager of GarrettComs contract manufacturing facility, the New Product Nursery where he wrote, implemented and had an ISO 9001:2000 program certified. Robert also served as Vice-President of Product Marketing for Quantum3D, Inc. Robert holds sixteen patents - fourteen (14) utility patents and two (2) design patents. Robert holds a bachelors degree from the University of Illinois at Champaign -Urbana and is the author of several papers on computing as well as a seasoned speaker in demand at conferences, universities and professional computing organization meetings
