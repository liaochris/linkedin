Reem K. Yared’s Summary

Fifteen years of experience working with product launch teams to develop new products and services. Consulting services include developing a product strategy, commercialization strategy, and competitive and market analysis.
Industry experience in municipal wi-fi, cleantech, online marketplaces, information services and publishing.

Reem K. Yared’s Specialties:

Developing a product strategy typically includes the product/service's content, features, delivery, pricing, competitive positioning, and value proposition.
The product strategy is based on an analysis of competitive, technological and market trends.

  • Chief Business Officer

    Waltham Technologies, Inc

    (Renewables & Environment industry)

    September 2009 — Present (4 months)

    Start-up focused on wastewater cleaning for the beverage industry. By using cyanobacteria, Waltham Technologies delivers re-usable water with saves significant energy. Step 2 is producing biofuels out of the sludge.

  • Founder

    GrowthWise

    (Management Consulting industry)

    October 1995 — September 2009 (14 years )

    I have served as a business advisor to a number of Fortune 1000 companies as well as successful startups, including several in the online service provider space. Advised clients on market entry strategy, product definition and creation, and developed business plans and financial projections around new products and services. When developing a product strategy, I focus relentlessly on user-value. What set of features, pricing, and delivery mechanism constitutes compelling –irresistible- value to the user? To answer, I integrate a thorough analysis of user needs, market dynamics, competitive and technological trends.
    GrowthWise clients include Dow Jones, Aquent, HotDispatch, H3.com, the World Bank, a DRM start-up, and a municipal Wi-Fi start-up (both still in stealth mode).

Worked closely with the inventors and the DOE's market research consultants to build a go-to-market strategy for each start-up. All seven companies were recipients of the Department of Energy (DOE)'s $250,000 Inventions & Innovations grants. Technologies spanned bio-oils, wind, AC pumps, diesel engine control systems, LP gas valves, and glass and paper manufacturing.
