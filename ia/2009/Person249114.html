Debra Xiangjun 杜湘君 Hayes’s Summary

Dedicated to health and healing for all, collective knowledge and wisdom, especially integrating traditional and indigenous medicine with western medicine.

Conducting fieldwork at an international Red Cross CM hospital and others within China

Classical Chinese Medicine and its efficacy in addressing cultural disparities, chronic and difficult conditions.

Languages- Mandarin, Hunan and Jiangxi hua, French, Spanish, Italian, Portuguese, Read Brazilain Portuguese and German and am learning Arabic.

Goals:

To integrate healers and healing modalities more fully within the World System to achieve the goals of the WH0 2008 Beijing Declaration and the 1978 WHO Alma Ata Declaration "Health for One". After all, health and healing are universal human rights. One life is SO important.

To promote understanding and worldview "translation" of traditional medicine, medicinal values and methodology across cultures, engage and apply them appropriately within medical testing through a two-step process and incorporate these practices into the mainstream Global Health Network via integrated healthcare teams.

To build and/or facilitate integrated medical mission, rescue and relief teams.

To help countries develop and implement health policies informed by cross-disciplinary studies and research.

The marriage of medicines, the gathering of traditional healing sources within native culturally moderated means for collective knowledge while empowering the Peoples from whom it came.

The translation of other medicines, their worldviews and diagnostics and how they correlate to the ICD

To build clinics to facilitate these medicinal practices in locations and countries that lack them so that diseases might be healed and chronic conditions could be addressed.

Currently building a Chinese medicine clinic in Algeria

I previously worked with SEDA in Lao PDR - collaborating on their project, including their traditional medicine. http://seda-laos.org

Debra Xiangjun 杜湘君 Hayes’s Specialties:

Comparative medicine-Chinese, Mayan, Unani, Cherokee, Sioux & Energy anthropology, ethnography, writing and editing

Treating various conditions/disorders and diseases across culture&race via culturally moderated means, reaching the depths of molecular medicine through sustainable practices including but not limited to herbology.

Quality of life and "true costs" of illness-including social, emotional, spiritual, lifestyle and allopathic consequences
