Karen Peltz Strauss’s Experience

  • Co-founder and Co-Chair

    Coalition of Organizations for Accessible Technology

    (Telecommunications industry)

    March 2007 — Present (2 years 11 months)

    COAT, consisting of over 220 local and national organizations, is dedicated to ensuring that in the 21st Century, products and services used to access communications and video programming are fully accessible to people with disabilities. In particular, COAT seeks to ensure that as our nation migrates to more advanced digital and Internet technologies, people with disabilities are not left behind.

  • Principal

    KPS Consulting

    (Law Practice industry)

    November 2001 — Present (8 years 3 months)

    Legal Consultant on telecommunications access policy issues for non-profit disability organizations, telecommunications relay sevice providers, research institutes, and government agencies. Principal clients are Communication Service for the Deaf, Ultratec, Inc., and the Rehabilitation Engineering Research Center on Telecommunications Access.

  • Deputy Bureau Chief, Consumer Information Bureau (now CGB)

    Federal Communications Commission

    (Government Agency; 1001-5000 employees; Telecommunications industry)

    November 1999 — October 2001 (2 years )

    Oversaw release of numerous regulations expanding telecommunications access by people with disabilities, including rules on relay services and captioning; assisted in initial staffing and development of first Disability Rights Office; oversaw consumer education and strategic planning divisions of the bureau.
