Tyler Weaver’s Summary

Tyler Weaver is a Cleveland-based independent filmmaker and new media producer with a particular interest in how digital filmmaking and internet distribution can transform the competition for eyes and ears.

Working with the Mary Ferrell Foundation, his credits include "The Fourteen Minute Gap," "Withheld in Full: Morley V. CIA," and "Unredacted - The Video Interviews" in addition to a number of promotional films. For these projects, Tyler took on on numerous roles, including writer, director, producer, editor, composer, cinematographer, and voice over artist, and he conducted more than 50 hours of interviews with noted authors and historians.

Independent of the Foundation, Tyler has produced music videos for NYC-based composer Francesca DiGiovanna, and in April, 2009, released his first feature-length film, the music documentary, "Gather ‘Round the Mic," filmed in Cleveland and featuring an international cast of musicians.

These days, Tyler is focused on writing his next feature film and producing a new music video. He is a regular contributor to Pulptone.com, and blogs about all things filmic and fascinating (to him) at Multi-Hyphenate (http://www.multi-hyphenate.com).

With a unique background in non-profit management and independent film/new media production, Tyler is available to create engaging video content for a multitude of clients, including corporations, small businesses, artists, and non-profits seeking high-quality, cost-effective promotional films and marketing materials.
