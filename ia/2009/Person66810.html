DAVID POMERANZ’s Summary

I am working as a consultant on health reform and as a grant proposal writer.

In the area of health reform, I conduct workshops on health insurance options and national health reform for clients of the state’s Career Centers. On October 27, 2009, I will be speaking on whether the 2006 Massachusetts Health Reform Law can be a model for national reform. See http://hcpcinc.com/webinar to register.

I have been working on health reform since 1988. The Massachusetts Medical Society hired me to analyze the state’s Universal Health Care Act. The Medical Society then hired me as its Senior Policy Analyst. Relative to the 1993 Clinton Health Security Act, for example, I led a panel that included the Editor-in-Chief of The New England Journal of Medicine; the CEO of the Tufts Health Plan; and the President of the Massachusetts Association of Older Americans. In 2006, the Blue Cross Blue Shield of Massachusetts Foundation was a major player in the enactment of the state’s Health Reform Law. I was working for the Neponset Health Center and watched the Law’s implementation from “ground zero.” During a site visit by a Foundation Senior Officer, I told him that the Law would significantly hurt the state’s community health centers (CHCs). Unfortunately, I was correct.

As a consulting proposal writer for my previous employer -- Harbor Health Services, Inc. -- I won a $49,500 MassHealth outreach grant. Another client is the Hillel Council of New England (HCNE) which provides a "Jewish community" experience for students attending smaller colleges in the greater Boston area. For HCNE, I won a $10,000 grant, compiled educational programming, and recruited Ruth Nemzoff (see ruthnemzoff.com) to speak at a fundraiser.
