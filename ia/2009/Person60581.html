Debra Polson’s Summary

Currently I have an ongoing academic position at the Queensland University of Technology where I lecture in Interface and Interaction Design (Games and Socila Networks).

At the same time, I run a number of projects that began as research experiments that grew into larger undertakings with multiple funding sources. Some of the projects have now been released for public interaction... namely SCOOT and Cipher Cities. The project MiLK, was developed for the Australasian CRC for Interaction design. I was seconded to ACID to design and develop MiLK which was acquired by Secret Sauce IP Ventures to commercialise on Dec 19 2008.

For the last 15 years I have conceived and implemented a heap of innovative games for community, education and commercial organisations. Im most happy when I am exploiting and combining past and contemporary aspects of computer games, mobile devices and digital social networks to create unique interactions. The ultimate outcome of any project is new creative agency and new friendships - for the team, the stakeholders, the players...

  • Creator and Lead Designer

    Cipher Cities

    (Design industry)

    2005 — Present (4 years)

    While with ACID, I designed "CIPHER CITIES", an authoring tool that allows anybody, anywhere to create, share and play location-based games. At its core, Cipher Cities is a mobile game builder, with social networking and content distribution scaffolded around it to encourage content creation. The game builder provides users with a simple storyboard process for building, editing and distributing games. Cipher games can be authored to suit a wide range of game types, from simple orientation games and question and answer quizzes to narrative rich investigative and code breaking challenges. Every game is instantly accessible on mobile phones to be played exclusively by a group of friends, or openly available for anyone anywhere to access, depending on the game designers wishes.

    Cipher Cities is now an independent project that I continue to develop with a small team of programmers and designers.

    http://ciphercities.com

  • Creator and Lead Designer

    SCOOT Agency

    (Design industry)

    2004 — Present (5 years)

    SCOOT is a mixed reality game that employs web, mobile devices and public displays as tools of play to guide groups through unique public places. Simply, players must follow prompts on their mobile phones to seek out interactive console games that are hidden in a public place. Along the way they participate in activities and solve clues by seeking out facts about the sites.
    .
    I have worked with a core team of developers to complete and host 8 major SCOOT events for festivals, parks, museums and universities. Each time we improve and extend our inventory of game resources by custom designing the game play and game path/s to suit the site and the clients desired outcomes. Each event invites local undergrad students to join the team to create various new SCOOT components.

    SCOOT was originally part of my PhD submission and now attracts many interested clients. Due to time/life constraints I am limited to designing and hosting only 2 SCOOT events a year.

    http://scootgame.com
