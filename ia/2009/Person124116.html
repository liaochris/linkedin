Julie Roth’s Summary

Julie started her career in the legacy media of books and magazines, then made the transition to new media in 1997. As Executive Producer of NetGuide, one of the Web's early general-audience sites, Julie helped create many of what are now considered "best practices" in website content, usability, and marketing—making the site one of the Web's first profitable properties.

At nationally acclaimed APBnews.com, Julie produced content that was a finalist for the Scripps Howard Foundation's National Journalism Award for Web reporting, then tripled the site's unique visitors as the Director of Marketing and Online Strategies.

As an independent consultant in digital strategies and management, Julie helped JusticeTalking.org double its traffic and develop the Webby award-winning spin-off site JusticeLearning.org in partnership with The New York Times Learning Network. Her Web strategy enabled the UN Millennium Campaign to increase its site traffic 400% and its average visit duration 500%. Her other digital clients have included Evian Natural Spring Water, Remington Products, U.S. PIRG, In My Name, and Witness.

Julie also conducts qualitative research for MTV Network brands including AddictingGames.com Shockwave.com, and ParentsConnect.com; Nickelodeon, VH1 and CMT television; and Nick Magazine. (This as an independent contractor with research firm Dan Joseph & Associates.)

She has a B.A. in English from Allegheny College, with minors in graphic design and in history, and an M.A. in English from NYU. She is co-founder of Tech4Good, a networking organization for tech professionals seeking to contribute to the common good. In her spare time, she teaches English and history to her high school nephews.

Julie Roth’s Specialties:

Web strategy; content strategy; managing of cross-functional teams; "crunch" management; interactive project management; content development and marketing; website usability and design; e-newsletters; and copywriting for any medium
