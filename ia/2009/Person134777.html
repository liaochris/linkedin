Kristina Owens’s Summary

I have a diverse professional experience and would like to continue to develop a career which encompasses many of my interests and skills. Currently, I work on developing new and fast molecular tools for the early detection of Plant pathogens which impact economically important agriculture crops such as Sweet potatoes, Potatoes, Sugarcane, plums, peaches, and citrus. Working in a small lab, I not only do the technical work but I am involved in the project management(budgets, procurement,inventory, reports, SOPs).
My most significant experience was the development and implementation of my Masters thesis and Peace Corps project "Genetic Diversity of Annona cherimola. mill in south central Bolivia. This project was comprehensive study of the fruit tree which encompassed all my interests; International economic and agriculture development, Latin America, the sciences: Horticulture, soil conservation, Biodiversity, Genetic diversity and Natural resource management.

My current interest is taking complex scientific knowledge and tools applying it for the benefit various stakeholders(scientists, farmers, agronomists and policy makers); and for the protection of the world's agriculture and environment. I believe understanding the level of diversity, the horticulture needs, the economic value and the environmental impact of a crop will give one the tools necessary for continual and effective development.
Finally developing my career where I can continue to develop my skills and have a greater impact on international agriculture/environmental development is my most significant goal.
