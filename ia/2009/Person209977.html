Sarah Zisa’s Education

  • Brown University

    MA , Environmental Studies: Self-Designed Concentration in Corporate Strategy , 2005 — 2007

    Thesis asks, “What are the necessary ingredients for a successful corporate sustainability initiative?” Answered by examining the conceptualization and implementation of three Walmart sustainability projects. By comparing disparate, but successful, initiatives, thesis identified the process by which Walmart seized previously untapped eco-efficiency opportunities and the common elements across the cases that appear requisite for success. Data collection includes document review and over 30 interviews with Walmart executives; suppliers including GE, Unilever, Cummins, and others; external consultants. Analytic methodology is Most Different System Design. Research resulted in the development of a two-tiered template to guide future sustainability efforts.

    Readers on committee included Janelle Kearsley, Walmart’s former Director of Sustainability, and Andrew Winston, Director of Yale University's Corporate Environmental Strategy Project and leading author on corporate sustainability.

  • Connecticut College

    BA , Environmental Science , 1999 — 2003

    Visioned and executed a campaign that resulted in Connecticut College becoming the first school to purchase a significant percentage of electricity from renewable sources. Campaign required establishing and facilitating key stakeholder relationships, inspiring student peers, hosting informational events, developing strategic communications, and participating in negotiations with renewable electricity and renewable energy credit providers. Campaign gained national recognition and has since been a framework for campus renewable energy programs across the country.
