Lynne DeVenny’s Summary

Lynne is a N.C. State Bar Certified Paralegal who specializes in workers’ compensation, serious injury and wrongful death cases. She has also provided litigation support on medical malpractice and employment cases, including class action wage and hour claims and a class action sexual harassment case. In 2008, she co-authored with attorney J. Griffin Morgan a national workers' compensation book, Workers' Compensation Practice for Paralegals. She continues to promote paralegal education, career development and community on her blog, Practical Paralegalism. She is a former adjunct paralegal instructor at Forsyth Technical Community College in Winston-Salem, NC. She has also lectured at N.C. Advocates for Justice (NCAJ) and National Business Institute (NBI) paralegal and attorney continuing legal education seminars; authored seminar manuscripts and articles for paralegal publications; served several terms as Publications Chair and Membership Chair for NCAJ's Legal Assistant Division; and volunteered as a Site Coordinator for NCAJ’s Wade Edwards High School Mock Trial Regional competition in Winston-Salem, NC for many years. Lynne provides experienced and substantive litigation support and paralegal consulting services on workers' compensation and civil injury cases.

Lynne DeVenny’s Specialties:

Reviewing, investigating and evaluating complex injury and workers' compensation cases; reviewing and summarizing medical records including identifying evidentiary and causation issues; drafting legal documents, articles and manuscripts; and preparing cases for settlement, mediation and hearings, including calculating damages, valuing claims and preparing demand packages.
