Patrick Baty’s Summary

Patrick, a former Army officer, is a specialist in the use of paint and colour in historic buildings with many years experience.

Following a research degree in the subject he has consulted on countless restoration projects. Buildings range from Royal palaces; country houses and cathedrals to museums; a wartime RAF station and London housing estates. In the USA he has assisted in a number of notable projects, some examples being for the Metropolitan Museum of Art, Historic Charleston, Monticello, Colonial Williamsburg and the Tryon Palace. Many jobs have also been carried out in Scotland and Ireland. Recent projects have included Hampton Court Palace, Stowe, Tower Bridge and the Royal Festival Hall. A list of some others can be seen on his website.

He has been a Trustee of the Georgian Group for 20 years and a Fellow of both the Society of Antiquaries of Scotland and the Royal Society for the encouragement of Arts, Manufactures & Commerce. He is a founding member of both the Architectural Paint Group and the Traditional Paint Forum. In 2007 his company was granted a Royal Warrant of Appointment to Her Majesty The Queen for his work on colour.

Patrick lectures frequently on the subject of paint and colour in the eighteenth and nineteenth centuries. He has spoken at several international symposia and assisted with a number of courses in the United States. He has written many magazine articles and chapters in books and has contributed to and edited several works on the general subject of paint and colour. He has also appeared on television and national radio.

Patrick has pioneered the use of spectrophotometry in historic buildings and is frequently employed to make colour surveys.

Patrick Baty’s Experience

  • Colourist & Managing Director

    Papers and Paints Ltd

    (Design industry)

    1981 — Present (28 years)

    For nearly thirty years Patrick has been dealing with paint and colour issues on a daily basis. These range from the minor domestic question of “what colour should I paint my front door” to high profile projects concerning iconic structures such as Tower Bridge and the Royal Albert Hall. Many major London landmarks can be seen on his list of projects.

    He also carries out the analysis of the painted surfaces in historic buildings in order to ascertain how they appeared at an earlier time. Advice is given on the recreation of an earlier scheme or on one that takes into account the client’s wishes and own taste.

    In the 1980s Patrick Baty produced a range of fully researched paint colours based on those in use in the late 18th and early 19th centuries. This sparked off a trend for historically-themed paint ranges which are still seen on the market today. In recent years, on two separate occasions, he has been employed by ICI (Dulux) and another paint company to develop ranges of traditional paint colours for English Heritage. Colour ranges have also been produced for the French and German markets and further work for the European market is currently being undertaken.

    The colours traditionally employed on the exteriors of buildings in Bath, Edinburgh, Penzance and in Gloucester have been investigated for the local planning authorities and guidelines produced for them. He has recently finished work on a range of exterior colours based on regional use in France.

    Patrick has been employed to carry out colour surveys of a number of Crown and Government buildings and several of the St James’s clubs so that existing colours can be identified and reproduced. A maintenance schedule is produced and small quantities of paint can be made up at short notice.
