Martin Crockatt’s Summary

Typographer & Studio Manager with over 20 years experience working in the advertising industry.

Studied Typography at the London College of Printing, followed by a year’s apprenticeship with a traditional lettering artist. Then worked at Conway’s (typesetting house), creating fonts and distorting type by hand (in those Jurassic pre-mac days), before being hired by Young & Rubicam (London advertising agency).

Have spent the last fourteen years as Typographer & Studio Manager at advertising agency that was Banks Hoggins O’Shea and is now, following several mergers, Draftfcb London. Was nominated for “Best Typographer” in IPA’s “Best of the Best” Awards 2002 and past D&AD credits as typographer include campaigns for Virgin Interactive and Waitrose. Have also been involved in campaigns winning awards at Campaign Press, Campaign Poster, Cannes Lions & Creative Circle.

Full Member of D&AD (British Design & Art Direction) since 1998.
D&AD Juror (Advertising Crafts: Typography) 2000 and 2006.
Member of Type Directors Club since 2002.

Martin Crockatt’s Specialties:

Typographic & advertising design. Setting up & running profitable, efficient and cost effective studios. Keeping abreast of industry design trends, and developments in relevant hardware & software. People management. Quality control.

Martin Crockatt’s Experience

  • Typographer & Head of Studio

    Draftfcb

    (Public Company; 51-200 employees; Marketing and Advertising industry)

    April 1995 — Present (14 years 5 months)

    Set up internal studio, handling all agency’s typesetting, artwork and hires retouching requirements. I run every aspect of the studio myself, monitoring costs, resources and accurate project management, thus ensuring an efficient and cost effective service for the agency.

    On Hewlett Packard, we produced master print and poster artwork for upwards of 20 markets across Europe, requiring outstanding organization and attention to detail. On Heal’s, the premium furniture and homewares store, we provide the fast turnaround and pragmatic value-led approach demanded by all high-volume retail businesses. As part of a global network we have produced multilingual work for a number of clients over the years.

    Instrumental in migrating to InDesign (July 2003) when I felt it was the way forward - 1st major London agency to make the leap (from Quark). Proved correct as InDesign now established as the superior industry standard. Also had personal involvement with Adobe’s own InDesign developers.

  • Lettering Artist/Manager of Typesetting Dept

    Young & Rubicam

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; WPP; Marketing and Advertising industry)

    April 1989 — April 1995 (6 years 1 month)

    After starting at Mechs Creative Services (studio of Y&R) as a finished artist, I was promoted to run the typesetting dept which was equipped with an extensive range of Apple Mac/Linotype hardware.

    Majority of work produced was national press and magazine advertisements for the blue chip clients of the advertising agencies (in addition to Y&R) who used Mechs. As well as ensuring the smooth running of the dept my responsibilities included organizing upgrades and purchases of both software & hardware, troubleshooting problems, in addition to overseeing and organising the total dismantling and then installation of the whole department on the two occasions the company moved premises.

    Spent a year running a satellite of Mechs called Pukka Studio, which exclusively serviced Banks Hoggins O’Shea.

  • Type Designer/Lettering Artist

    Conway Group Graphics

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Marketing and Advertising industry)

    June 1985 — April 1989 (3 years 11 months)

    Within a large typesetting company, I was responsible for designing, distorting and cutting logotypes, typefaces and other graphic elements, all by hand (in rubylith film). I was trained in this technique (year's apprenticeship) by Geoff Clements and Shelly Winter, both highly experienced type designers and rubylith cutters.

    I was involved in researching the prototype version of Linotype’s ‘Logostation’ which operated on an IBM XT (linked to Linotype 202's), and subsequently operated a Linotype ‘Logostation 2’ on an IBM AT for over two years. I also designed, cut and made typositor and VIP fonts. In addition, I had the opportunity to work on early Apple Macs using rather primitive software.

    Conway’s clients included: Abbot Mead Vickers, Dorlands, FCB, Lewis Moberley, Marks & Spencer (direct), Marshall Cavendish, McCormicks, Octopus Books, Saatchi & Saatchi.
