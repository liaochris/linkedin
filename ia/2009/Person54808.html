Danny Ricci’s Summary

Most of my professional career has been based on customer interaction. When interacting with customers, one of my strengths is to adjust to their level and establish rapport to ease tough situations, whether the issue be caused by the company or by the customer. I am a strong believer in making decisions that benefit customers, and work best in an environment where I have the ability to go above and beyond expectations to appease customers.

My most recent customer service interaction was via e-mail ticketing and incoming phone calls. In the past, I have worked the sales floor for selling and product demonstration.

I also gained a lot of knowledge during my time at Apple as an administrator for the service department. My job was to manage the work flow, and prioritize various tasks. I also ventured into Inventory Control as the leader of a small team. This was one of my biggest professional challenges, which taught me a lot about leadership and the importance of clear, open communication.

Outside of my professional experience, I have launched several small web projects in the past. The first was a site called PCtoMac.com. The focus of the site was to help people influenced by Apple's "Switch" advertising campaign make the transition from Windows to Macintosh. The site had an active community forum, and I wrote most of the tutorials and product reviews. This site has been shut down for several years and I no longer control the domain name.

I am the owner of the domain BrowserWars.com. I love technology, and it's amazing to see how much the web has changed over the last decade. While I've had lots of ideas for this domain (blog, news site, reviews, community forum), I have not been able to develop this into a working site.

I have learned a lot from my personal projects, and from the two great companies I am honored to have worked for. I have made many friends along the way.

I am ready to move on to a new challenge, whatever the field may be.
