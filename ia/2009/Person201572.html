Rob Kenner’s Experience

  • Editor In Chief

    SoulSummer.com

    (Publishing industry)

    April 2009 — Present (4 months)

    Editor of an interactive multimedia website devoted to soul music. Soul Summer is a division of JBF Entertainment, an independent concert production company and record label.

  • Editorial Director

    RJK Media LLC

    (Publishing industry)

    2000 — Present (9 years)

    Independent book packager and editor with a special interest in the intersection of entertainment, politics, and socio-cultural criticism. In the past I have conceived, written proposals for, edited, and sometimes co-written a wide variety of books for VIBE Books. The first was VIBE Hip Hop Divas 2001 (Three Rivers Press). Then in 2003 came UNBELIEVABLE: The Life Death & Afterlife of The Notorious B.I.G. by Cheo Hodari Coker (Three Rivers Press). This book was recently optioned for the Biggie movie coming from Fox Searchlight Films. Also in 2003, I was the co-author (with George Pitts) of VX: 10 Years of VIBE Photography (Abrams). Then in 2006 I launched the VIBE Street Lit imprint with Kensington Publishing Co. So far we have published four titles: Death Around The Corner by C-Murder, Snow by Kenji Jasper, Mercedes Ladies by Sheri Sher, and The VIBE Q: Raw and Uncut, 15 Years of Classic VIBE Interviews. I was also responsible for strategic marketing and promotion, organizing promotional events, as well as negotiating all contracts, licensing deals, and reprint requests. I am interested in working with highly motivated people who can make things happen rather than just talking the talk.

  • Freelance Writer

    Self-employed

    (Self-Employed; Myself Only; Publishing industry)

    1999 — Present (10 years)

    My freelance writing has appeared in Poetry, The New York Times, Wired,
    ego trip, Riddim, Blaze, In Style, Travel + Leisure, and TV Guide.

  • Founder

    RaggaMedia Worldwide

    (Internet industry)

    January 1997 — Present (12 years 7 months)

    Drawing on 20 years of experience as a DJ, producer, editor, and journalist covering reggae, dancehall, and hip hop music and culture, I established the think tank, idea incubator, and multimedia production company RaggaMedia Worldwide, a division of RJK Media LLC. Some of RaggaMedia's projects include the popular blog boomshots.com, as well as a number of forthcoming films, magazines, books, and events documenting, highlighting, and leveraging the pervasive yet under-appreciated influence of Caribbean music and culture on all aspects of global pop culture.

  • Editor At Large

    VIBE Media Group

    (Publishing industry)

    1993 — Present (16 years)

    I was a founding editor of VIBE, and have edited pretty much every part of the magazine over the past 15 years. My first story for the magazine was a profile of Super Cat that appeared in VIBE's 1992 test issue (the one with Treach on the cover). Ever since then I've written a reggae column called Boomshots that remains the only consistent reggae coverage in any major music mag. I've also written cover stories on Nas and Sean Paul, as well as too many articles to count ranging from celebrity profiles to investigative journalism and cultural criticism. Some of my best music stories were written while Sacha Jenkins of Ego Trip fame was VIBE's Music Editor, especially a piece on Eminem called "13 Ways of Looking at a White Boy" and "That Old Black Magic" which looked at the behind-the-scenes influence of black producers, songwriters, managers, and choreographers on white pop acts like Britney Spears and N'Sync. I may be an even better editor than I am a writer, though I love both.

  • Editor In Chief

    Art + Antiques Magazine

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Publishing industry)

    1987 — 1993 (6 years)

    This was my first job out of college. I was hired as an Editorial Assistant, and became Editor In Chief within six years. At A+A I commissioned and edited articles by writers like John Updike and Pete Hamill, interviewed Ralph Lauren and Horst P. Horst. I also wrote two books about art. Meanwhile I was working weekends at a reggae record shop called Irie Ites and DJing at various clubs around NYC. I eventually quit to join the start-up team at VIBE.
