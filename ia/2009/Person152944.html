Mark Walls’s Summary

Insurance professional with over 19 years of insurance experience, focusing on workers' compensation claim handling and self-insurance.

My early career was handling workers' compensation claims for large self-insured, self-administered employer.

I currently work for a leading excess carrier. My responsibilities include supervising excess workers' compensation claims in a region of the country.

I am also involved with our Loss Portfolio Transfer (LPT) product. A Loss Portfolio Transfer allows a self-insured to transfer their claims "tail" to a carrier. This cleans up their balance sheet, reduces the joint-and-several liability for self-insured groups, and could result in a reduction of the collateral they are required to post with the state. LPTs are very useful in mergers/acquisitions, or when a self-insured shuts down operations in a state.

My team completes all LPT prospect audits,and we are responsible for the claims handling on any completed LPTs.

My experiences have given me a great perspective into the world of claims handling. I have participated in hundreds of TPA audits around the country, so I have seen first-hand what the industry "best practices" are.

As an excess carrier, our entire caseload is made up of complex or catastrophic claims. The average adjuster handles an occasional complex claim, and may never handle a catastrophic claim. This unique perspective helps make us experts in the handling of such claims. Our goal is to use our experience to assist our policyholders and third party administrators in the management of such claims.

I have been a speaker on workers' compensation topics at meetings of the Illinois Self-Insurance Association (2006), Illinois Municipal League RMA (2007), Self-Insurance Institute of America (SIIA) 2008 National Educational Conference, and Safety National Workers Compensation Education Conference (2009).

Mark Walls’s Specialties:

workers' compensation laws in multiple jurisdictions, self-insurance, excess workers' compensation, medicare set-aside rules, structured settlements, handling of catastrophic and complex workers' comp claims, and loss portfolio transfers
