Tom Gillispie’s Summary

I was a career newspaper writer and copy editor, and now I'm a full-time freelancer. I'm a terrific editor and a good writer, desktop publisher and photographer, and I do all of those things on a freelance basis.

If you're a writer or editor of a publication, I can be your best friend. My strengths include tightening, focusing and improving copy. Plus fact-checking. Everything I edit gets a little shorter, a little tighter, and, usually, a lot better.

I've edited for newspapers, magazines, web sites, individuals and businessmen, and I've edited fiction and non-fiction books.

As a writer, I'm concise and precise. I can say a lot in few words, and my stories make you feel that you were there. You'll feel the heat off the car's engine or see the driver's bright-red scarf.

Most of my freelance writing has been about sports, particularly auto racing. My freelance credits include the Miami Herald, the Baltimore Sun, the Washington Post, The Sporting News' auto-racing preview editions, and Sports Illustrated (a short basketball story). My third book, Angel in Black: Remembering Dale Earnhardt, Sr., was published March 1, 2008 by Cumberland House Publishing of Nashville, Tenn. I've even written comic-book scripts.

Tom Gillispie’s Experience

  • Sports editor

    Surry Messenger

    (Newspapers industry)

    September 2008 — January 2009 (5 months)

    I started out as a jack-of-all trades, writing, editing, laying out pages and taking photos as needed. I might write news stories on one day or sports stories on another. The last two months or so, I was the sports editor. I wrote stories, laid out pages (InDesign), edited copy and took pictures (Canon EOS Rebel) for the sports section. And I still helped news side when they needed it.

  • Editor

    The Racing Journal

    (Media Production industry)

    February 2008 — May 2008 (4 months)

    The Racing Journal covered short-track racing in the Piedmont of North Carolina and southern Virginia. I produced the story budgets, wrote the stories and took the pictures. Then I decided what went on each page. The first edition was in mid-March; the Winston-Salem Journal cancelled the newspaper in late May.

  • copy editor/writer

    Winston-Salem Journal

    (Public Company; Newspapers industry)

    February 2000 — August 2005 (5 years 7 months)

    I worked as a copy editor in the sports department two or more days a week. I edited copy and wrote headlines and cutlines. I also wrote freelance stories for the sports, metro (news), zones, features and niche publications departments. I still write for the Journal on a freelance basis.

  • sports copy editor/writer

    High Point Enterprise

    (Writing and Editing industry)

    July 1999 — February 2000 (8 months)

    I was hired as a sports copy editor and writer. I covered auto racing and high-school sports, and I helped put out the newspaper. I wrote two auto-racing columns most weeks and occasionally wrote a motorsports feature.

  • Publisher

    Think Fast

    (Writing and Editing industry)

    February 1998 — July 1998 (6 months)

    I was the publisher, editor, writer, photographer and page designer for Think Fast, a monthly auto-racing newspaper published in the Charleston, S.C., area. At the same time, my wife Holly and I produced a weekly newspaper page that we sold to small newspapers in the Carolinas. It was also called Think Fast. The monthly paper lasted only six months, but the newspaper page lasted two years.

  • copy editor/writer

    Charleston Post and Courier

    (Writing and Editing industry)

    October 1987 — January 1998 (10 years 4 months)

    I started out as a full-time copy editor, but I became the auto-racing writer in 1990. At that point, I was also acting as sports slot man two or three days a week. On those days, I put together the story budget, decided what went on each page, went to the budget meeting, assigned pages to other copy editors, laid out the sports front and at least one other inside page, back-read all of the copy. I remained a copy editor and the racing writer, but I later also became the Charleston Southern football and basketball writer, and I covered prep and college sports, as well as minor-league baseball, hockey and soccer when needed.

  • writer/copy editor

    Wilmington Star-News

    (Writing and Editing industry)

    January 1985 — October 1987 (2 years 10 months)

    I started as a sports writer for the Star-News in 1985 and slowly began to add copy editing to my duties. By the time I left in late 1987, I was more copy editor than writer. I was the auto-racing writer for two years and covered high-school and college events as needed. I wrote a column once or twice a week. I also laid out the sports section on nights when the regular slot man was off. I once had to lay it out nine days in a row while he was on vacation.

  • writer/copy editor

    Hendersonville Times-News

    (Writing and Editing industry)

    October 1983 — January 1985 (1 year 4 months)

    I helped the sports editor put out the sports section six days a week. I covered high-school sports and wrote features as needed. I laid out part of the sports section most days; when the sports editor was off, I wrote stories and laid out the sports section myself. I took pictures regularly for the sports section and occasionally for news side.
