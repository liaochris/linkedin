David Wopschall’s Experience

  • Executive Vice President and CFO

    Universal Space Network, Incorporated

    (Defense & Space industry)

    October 2001 — June 2009 (7 years 9 months)

    Universal Space Network was started in 1999 as a private startup venture primarily backed by Warburg Pincus and private investors. The Company provides low cost satellite telemetry and engineering support services with a worldwide network of ground stations to capture the global satellite market including commercial, DoD, NASA and the European Space Agencies.In 2008 the company acquired a division of Honeywell and most recently in 2009 the management team finalized and executed the sale of the Company to the Swedish Space Corporation.

  • Vice President and Chief Financial Officer

    Stellex Electronics

    (Wireless industry)

    1999 — 2001 (2 years)

    Stellex Electronics a broadband wireless company with revenues over $100 million and over 600 employees. The company manufactures and sells radio frequency microwave products for telecommunications, fiber optic, and broadband wireless markets.

    Improved the financial cost structure of this $100 million high tech manufacturing business and doubled EBITDA in 16 months. Restructured the LBO and sold this high tech wireless public company to Tyco International.

  • Division CFO and Business Support

    UNOCAL

    (Chemicals industry)

    1997 — 1999 (2 years)

    A worldwide oil and gas company having annual revenues of $6 billion. The chemical products division’s annual revenues are $600 million with 1,000 employees; three manufacturing plants operating 24/7, including Alaska Oil and Gas operation, and global transportation and wholesale distribution centers throughout the world.

  • Finance Manager

    LORAL Corporation

    (Aviation & Aerospace industry)

    1990 — 1997 (7 years)

    High tech company with annual revenues over $9 billion – electronics, telecommunications, and space; division with $500 million annual revenue performing systems integration, manufacturing with five manufacturing plants and 3,600 employees. Responsible for accounting, financial planning, budgeting, forecasting, capital investment analysis, control, government compliance, contract administration and reporting for both multi-site manufacturing and product line profit centers.Turned-around underperforming Division, implementing cost controls, reducing cycle time, yield analysis, combining support functions, reducing supplier base, improved annual profits over 30%.

  • Finance Manager

    Ford Motor Company--Ford Aerospace

    (Aviation & Aerospace industry)

    1978 — 1990 (12 years)
