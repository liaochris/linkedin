Richard Shea’s Summary

I love solving problems and defining and building things from the ground up.

Over the years I've moved "up the stack" from early work in embedded systems (C, various assembly languages, C++) then on to Java, J2EE and Web Services, and most recently Android phone development, Firefox plugins, Javascript, PHP, and Python.

I still enjoy the odd occassion when C++ bugs need to be debugged down at the assembly level. Helps keep the rust off.

I've defined architecture, written core infrastructure, implemented features at all levels of the stack, and led and managed technical teams and groups.

I've been on the founding team in key design/architecture roles of three startups. At New Oak in 1996 we built the first enterprise VPN appliance, acquired by Bay Networks (later Nortel). At WaveSmith Networks in 2000 we built a carrier ATM and Frame Relay switch (later expanded to MPLS and Ethernet), acquired by Ciena. The core architecture of that product is used for newer Ciena offerings as well. At BlueNote Networks in 2005 we built an enterprise VoIP software product with web services for business process pluggability.

One of my most recent independent projects was an Android application for managing scheduled calls, including a Firefox plugin to schedule calls from the web. I exposed this as a Facebook application also, and expanded it to any cell phone by including scheduled call reminders via SMS. Web part was all in PHP on this one.

My most recent independent project is a web application for bookmarking and sharing online advertisements. On the client side this includes a Firefox plugin to allow for recognizing and allowing saving of online ads. Web side is implemented in Python using Django on Google App Engine.

Publications:
1996 (paper) in American Journal of Physics: "Degeneracies of the Spherical Well, Harmonic Oscillator, and Hydrogen Atom in Arbitrary Dimensions"

1999 (book) "L2TP: Implementation and Operation"
