jonathan siao’s Summary

When I graduated from high school in 2000, I was dumbfounded and could not decide what to do. Fortunately, my interests in computers was already high, having been actively interested in overclocking my old AMD/INTEL based machines.

I would spend nights with my friend customizing, and tweaking my computers to get the best performance out of it. But, one thing always bugged me. How did the software run? How did it all fit together.

That was computer science crying out loud. After I graduated in 2004, I joined MFUSE(www.mfuse.com) in 2005 and got involved in developing J2ME applications using all the cutting edge technologies.

Having satisfied my hunger, I decided to take a few months off work and try to get involved in the opensource community and decided to write an open source J2ME library(www.openbizu.org). I gained a lot of managing experience and discipline since working from home is not the easiest. I decided to call it a day when I found out that SUN released an opensource J2ME lib called LWUIT which was exactly what my lib was trying to emulate. It was pointless re-inventing the wheel.

I went back on the job market and landed a job at NOKIA. After 1 year and having learnt as much as I could there, I decided to take 1 year off freelancing for POSTERITA and writing firetomobile( a web site that sends images free to the phone using SMS instead of MMS) . Having done that, I decided to go back to mobile development and developed BIBEEP, a social app for the mobile phone. I received more than 3000 downloads in the first week. To date, BIBEEP received more than 9000 downloads on www.getjar.com
