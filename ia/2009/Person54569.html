Dan Lippe’s Summary

Dan Lippe’s Specialties:

Content strategy and management. Project management in communications. Editing, rewrite, writing, proofreading. Recruiting, coaching, managing other editors and writers.

Dan Lippe’s Experience

  • Research health scientist/Project manager

    Cooperative Studies Program, U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs

    (Research industry)

    April 2009 — Present (8 months)

    Project manager at Cooperative Studies Program Coordinating Center, Edward Hines Jr. VA Hospital, Hines, Illinois. Administrative management of clinical studies focusing on improving the healthcare of U.S. veterans. Responsibilities include working closely with researchers and other VA healthcare professionals, as well as tracking and managing budgets. Current projects include three of CSP's new Cooperative Clinical Trial Award projects. Past editor skills in writing, editing and multitasking have transferred to project manager role.

  • Managing editor-Special Reports, Advertising Age

    Crain Communications

    (Privately Held; Publishing industry)

    January 2001 — January 2009 (8 years 1 month)

    Position created for me when most of Ad Age's editor jobs were moved from Chicago to New York, and I declined to make the move. Involved in editing about 40 special sections annually. In project manager role, directly planned and oversaw editorial content for about half these sections, with themes such as magazines, TV, digital media, sports marketing, marketing to kids, prominent women in marketing and prominent young adults in marketing; many of these sections were linked to sponsored events. Took these sections through entire process from planning to final product. Assigned stories, editing, rewrite. Also wrote stories for these sections. In addition to running in the flagship publication, many of the sections were featured prominently on AdAge.com.

  • Daily news editor, Advertising Age

    Crain Communications

    (Privately Held; Publishing industry)

    May 1994 — December 2000 (6 years 8 months)

    During this early era of the Internet, I was editing a daily page of news that was faxed to subscribers; suggested that person editing fax report should also edit news for Ad Age Web site, leading to creation of daily news editor title. Edited and posted news items on Ad Age site. Added these duties to previous responsibilities as news editor. Wrote "Adages" column for more than two years during this period.

  • News editor, Advertising Age

    Crain Communications

    (Privately Held; Publishing industry)

    September 1990 — April 1994 (3 years 8 months)

    One of top editors producing weekly edition of Ad Age. Worked closely with other editors and reporters in planning magazine. Editing, rewrite, copy editing, headline and caption writing, proofreading. Wrote some features.

  • Copy desk chief, Advertising Age

    Crain Communications

    (Privately Held; Publishing industry)

    February 1988 — August 1990 (2 years 7 months)

    Supervised copy desk and totally restaffed operation after all other copy editors either left or transferred to other jobs in a period of a few weeks. Worked closely with Ad Age's top editors in planning each week's edition. Copy editting, headline and caption writing, proofreading.

  • Copy editor, Advertising Age

    Crain Communications

    (Privately Held; Publishing industry)

    June 1984 — January 1988 (3 years 8 months)

    Joined Ad Age, flagship publication of Crain Communications, at time of expansion from weekly to twice-weekly frequency. Copy editing, headline and caption writing, proofreading. Assignments included compiling and editing late-breaking news briefs, at that time the most-read feature in Ad Age.

  • Copy editor, Modern Healthcare

    Crain Communications

    (Privately Held; Publishing industry)

    November 1981 — May 1984 (2 years 7 months)

    Joined this publication, which covers the hospital business, at a crucial growth period for the magazine, soon after it was acquired by Crain. First, and at that time only, copy editor for the magazine. Copy editing, headline and caption writing, proofreading. Wrote some stories.

  • Makeup editor, Daily Herald

    Paddock Publications

    (Writing and Editing industry)

    September 1980 — October 1981 (1 year 2 months)

    Promoted to newly created post from copy editor at this suburban newspaper that competes with Chicago's major metro dailies. Laying out most news pages, and other business and feature pages of the newspaper; overseeing pages until they become plates for the printing press. Worked closely with top editors, copy desk and composing shop. Supervised police reporters.

  • Copy editor

    American Bar Association

    (Non-Profit; Legal Services industry)

    September 1980 — October 1981 (1 year 2 months)

    While working nights full time at the Daily Herald, also worked part time days at the Chicago national headquarters of the ABA. Copy editing for the LawScope section of the ABA Journal (which goes to all ABA members) and for Bar Leader (who goes to bar association officers).

  • Copy editor, Daily Herald

    Paddock Publications

    (Writing and Editing industry)

    June 1978 — August 1980 (2 years 3 months)

    Joined newspaper during key growth period, when it was increasing frequency to seven days a week. Copy editing, headline and caption writing. Also substituted for copy desk chief, wire editor, night news editor. Wrote some features.
