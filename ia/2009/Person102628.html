James Peet’s Summary

From technology user to orchestrator...

Starting firstly as a draftsman for an architectural firm, I found quickly that I was a natural at using computer aided design systems, later moving to managing them, and then onto training and supporting users of these systems for a CAD vender.

It was from that role that one of our big clients, and now my current employer (BVN Architecture), hired me to firstly manage their CAD systems nationally, and then expanded the position to managing all of their ICT systems. Today I lead a team of seven ICT professionals serving BVN Architecture.

The specifics of my current role include ICT Management in general. Specifically I concentrate on aligning ICT systems with business vision, building staff competencies, implementing good knowledge management, processes, redundancies, support and ensuring good uptime to support the 24x7 business (architects never go home!).

The other important facets of my role include software and hardware asset management, budgeting, purchasing, reporting & decision support, HR, network architecture and development, vender relationships and contract negotiations.

Today I see my role (and future) concentrating on the alignment of business and technology, ensuring ICT systems enable the business rather than imposing a burden.

James Peet’s Specialties:

In addition to general ICT, I specialise in Computer aided design systems. Specifically setting usage standards, training etc for Bentley products.
