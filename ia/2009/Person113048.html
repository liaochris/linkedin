Visionary:
- Created Wildcat! BBS System
- Created Mustang Message Center (ERM)
- Created EventMingle (Event-Based Social Networking)

In 1986 I founded Mustang Software to develop a line of modem communication software. In 1994 we took that company public with a $25m valuation (NASDAQ:MSTG), only to be crushed by the World Wide Web in 1996. Left for dead, we reinvent the company. In 1997 we released an email response management system called Mustang Message Center. This product handled email inbound email to generic mailboxes (sales@, support@) for companies like Nordstrom, Dow Jones, Carnival Cruise Lines, and others. In 2000 the company was acquired for approx. $290 million.

In 2001 I assisted a young Internet Professional Services company, Web Associates, perform a turnaround. They had completely exhausted their credit line and faced bankrupcy. As their CEO I streamlined operations, consolidated locations and improved sales processes, billing and time management. Today, Web Associates is profitable and debt free.

In 2002 I lead a turnaround for Starbase Corp. (NASDAQ:SBAS). Starbase was a 300 person, $40m+ sales, with offices around the globe. They needed a leader with fiscal discipline, process driven with the ability to lead and make tough decisions. Starbase was cleaned up and sold to Borland in early 2003.

Since that time I have been working in the social networking, social media space.
