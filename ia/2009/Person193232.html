Ramesh Loganathan’s Summary

Presently, Managing Director (and Vice President- Products), Progress Software India, responsible for leading product development at the India Development center, organized in a Centers of Excellence model with each center owning a suite of products in that competency. Ramesh is responsible for providing strategic guidance to all the product teams, scale the product operations, with specific focus on cross-product considerations for the SOA and Data integration products.

Prior to taking the above position, Ramesh was Vice President of Middleware Technologies at Pramati. Since 2000 Ramesh was heading Product Engineering at Pramati, then helped setup the team (for Progress Software) that built the Sonic Workbench on Eclipse, eventually transitioned to Progress Software- India. Returned to Pramati in 2006 to start the Technology consulting division offering high value consulting around middleware infrastructure, applications design and architecture, Web2.0 collaboration in the enterprises, SOA and emerging architectures such as Web Desktops’ based solutions.

Hold a Masters degree in Electrical Engineering (Control Systems) from Anna university, Guindy, India. Has over 17 years Systems engineering and R&D leadership experience in product development organizations. Including Sonic Software (Technical Director- India Dev Center), Pramati Technologies (VP, Engineering) and Informix (Principal Engineer).

An accomplished Technologist and evangelist regularly speaking at workshops and seminars in India. Recently co-authored a book on ‘SOA approach to Integration’, published by Packt Publishers- he wrote the chapters on SOA, POA and ESB. He is also Adjunct Faculty at IIIT-Hyderabad, teaching courses on middleware technologies and research/exploratory work in the area of distributed systems. Strong interests in student-entrepreneurship and e-governance.

Ramesh Loganathan’s Specialties:

Built from scratch, a complete business around high value middleware technology consulting at Pramati. Member of several Standards Expert groups including J2EE 1.3. Organizes BarCamps and Startup events. Run a well read blog (http://jroller.com/rameshl).
