Megan Jarvis’s Summary

My post-undergrad career began in a project management position at the Orlando Opera, where I was responsible for business trips, work crews, press releases, contracts, and many other aspects of theatrical production, all the way down to heavy lifting, painting, and construction.

My switch to information technology came about because it was an area I had never worked in before, and it seemed interesting. After reading through a 2,000 page computer hardware manual and taking operating system and software lessons from expert acquaintances, I became employed as a Dell technician in August 2000.

In December 2001 I became a helpdesk technician at Lockheed Martin, where I worked in technical and customer service areas, troubleshooting everything from VPN to hardware difficulties, and I graduated from Rollins College in May of 2005 with an M.A. in corporate communication and technology.

I left Lockheed Martin in July of 2006 for a position with a company which manufactures video conversion equipment, and there I learned quite a lot about how smaller businesses operate and more about how to relate to external customers, not just internal ones.

I rejoined LM in October 2007 for a position with the Corporate Information Security Office and enjoyed the opportunity to extend my responsibilities to include more forms of communication and design, creating training projects and documentation, and presenting to groups small and large.
I now work in the LM Corporate Business Continuity Planning Office, mostly in training and communication.

I am able to deal quickly and efficiently with many topics, from computer trouble to writer's block.

I learn and think quickly, value innovation and integrity, and enjoy working with people and sharing knowledge. I am very interested in continuing in the fields of information security, education, training, and communication.
