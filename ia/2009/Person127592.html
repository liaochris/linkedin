Kathleen Flinn’s Summary

Award-winning journalist whose work has appeared in more than three dozen publications, including the Chicago Sun-Times, Smithsonian, Men's Fitness, Adweek, USA Weekend, Playboy, the Globe and Mail, among others.

Author of "The Sharper Your Knife, the Less You Cry," a memoir with recipes about her experiences at the famed Le Cordon Bleu cooking school in Paris (Viking, October 2007).

Flinn worked as a staffer at the Sarasota (Fla.) Herald-Tribune and Adweek and was founding editor of Internet Underground, one of the first print magazines about the internet. From there, she was hired as the first restaurant editor for Sidewalk.com, Microsoft's series of city guides that later merged with Citysearch, and served as the head of editorial for MSN.com in the United Kingdom based in London.

She presently serves as chair of the Food Writers, Editors and Publishers section of the International Association of Culinary Professionals (IACP).

Kathleen Flinn’s Specialties:

Writing features and hard news in a wide variety of mediums, focusing mostly on food and technology, knowledge of French cuisine, writing, editing, editorial coaching, recipe writing, web site content development and traffic analysis.
