The team typify the freeform generation that have discovered new ways to work, and the consultancy itself adopts an innovative operational model enabled by digital technologies. High energy, hands-on and fast turnarounds...

  • Co-founder, then CEO, President of the European and UK IABs, Chair of Standards & Mkt along the way

    Interactive Advertising Bureau

    (Non-Profit; Marketing and Advertising industry)

    December 1996 — August 2007 (10 years 9 months)

    From the early 90s it was clear that the web would open up the most vast range of possibilities in entertainment and media, but that someone was going to have to pay. The models for putting newspapers and magazines online in the early 90s, mail and search services in the late 90s, and TV in the 00s would all hinge on ad revenues; a strong online advertising economy would make for a strong online entertainment sector.
    But back in the mid nineties the online ad sector was neither strong nor structured; a chaotic mess of jargon, flaky business models and unsustainable content. Consumer brands were scared, agencies lacked the tools, research houses hadn’t yet delivered, and media owners were facing an Everest-like challenge. In short, the online ad economy simply didn’t have the tools to work.
    So that’s why I helped pull together a group of media owners and agencies to figure out how we could change this. If the standards weren’t there, someone would have to make them. If the research wasn’t there then someone would have to commission it. If the marketing messages were chaotic, then someone would have to figure it out. And the fate of the industry was pretty much in our hands.
    In 1997 when we created the in IAB in the UK my focus was on the standardisation of the advertising formats, and the market positioning of the sector. A year later when the European IAB launched, it helped trigger interest across the continent. I was pretty much the backroom guy, but honoured to be elected President, and even more so to be invited back four times to run both the European and UK operations.
    The UK and European IABs brought us in to accelerate them in the way we were helping other young digital brands. Along the way they invited me to step in as CEO and give them an extra boost, but this was always a real team effort; a real industry effort. Looking back on those first ten years of the IABs, the industry should be proud of what it achieved. The results speak for themselves.

  • Publisher of Telegraph.co.uk and the website network, Director of Hollinger Telegraph New Media

    Telegraph Media Group

    (Privately Held; Newspapers industry)

    November 1995 — January 2000 (4 years 3 months)

    These were the pioneering days of online media; a privilege to be able to help develop The Telegraph’s network of online newspapers, magazines, shops, web apps… and so much more.
    Initially we were a tiny band, just a few of us figuring out the model for digital publishing: how revenue models for entertainment sites would work, how to build engagement through content, how to encourage social participation through the web, how to create journalism through hyperlinks, and how to weave stories together between the content we owned and the exploding resources on the web.

    Helping the UK’s first online newspaper, first as its brand manager, then publisher, and then the director of it’s parent company at Hollinger meant helping a media group build out the most successful network of online services of any in its sector. The group of sites the corporation had created or bought became standard setters for web media in Europe.

    Not surprisingly the exceptional people who cut their teeth here on the web helped us win dozens of awards, including online newspaper of the year, every year. We went from being the first daily newspaper on the web to the first online newspaper classified advertising service, to the first rolling news operation, the first newspaper on web TV, the first online football games, the first dedicated travel sites and many more… We pioneered social media and blogs before there was the language to describe them, led the industry in creating best practice and defining how the commercial models would work in publishing, and tackling some of the structural challenges new industries face.
