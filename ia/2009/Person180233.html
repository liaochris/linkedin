William Paddock’s Education

  • Bates College

    B.A., Psychology, 2004 — 2008

    Senior Thesis:
    The CSI Effect: Relationships between Evidence Type and Amount Presented in Criminal Trials

    Excerpt from Abstract:

    "...The current study expands on the pretrial publicity literature to include the effects of exposure to fictional television crime dramas. 161 participants from Bates College were recruited to help examine an effect the popular media has termed the CSI Effect. The goal of this study was to show that watching the crime drama Crime Scene Investigation affects the judgments jurors make in real world criminal trials. Anecdotal evidence exists to support this claim and thus the pretense for this study. The results indicate that while no cognizable pattern exists, watching CSI does in fact influence juror’s decisions of guilt, the strength of the defense’s case, the sufficiency of the evidence provided by the defense and the thoroughness of police investigations."
