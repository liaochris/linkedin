Jill Sasso Curtis’s Summary

When I was fifteen years old, I lived abroad for the first time. Franco had just passed away and Spain had begun to open up. My grandmother, who was active in AFS and the Experiment in International Living, made it possible for me to live with a family in Zaragosa, Spain. Later, as a junior in college, I spent a year studying in Spain and traveled unaccompanied throughout Europe. These were formative experiences, which contributed to my global perspective and fueled my passion for international affairs. As a young adult, I fed this passion for things international by focusing my undergraduate studies in International Business and Spanish Literature and earning an MBA in International Management from the world-renowned Thunderbird School of Global Management. I spent the next ten years working in international marketing roles for Colgate-Palmolive, Polaroid and The First Years, developing valuable marketing and management skills and doing business all over the world. My international expertise led me to an international role as a senior administrator at Tufts’ Fletcher School of Law and Diplomacy and I spent ten years happily working in higher education. I had the privilege of serving as a faculty member in Tufts’ Entrepreneurial Leadership program where I taught Entrepreneurial Marketing to undergraduate students. Teaching was one of the most rewarding experiences of my career - if you want to truly master something, teach it. My own entrepreneurial venture was born of my passions for international work, education, marketing and entrepreneurship. At New Millennium Marketing, we serve educational institutions and entrepreneurial ventures in their efforts to develop new businesses, products, services and programs and our unique value proposition is our ability to help them navigate the global marketplace.
