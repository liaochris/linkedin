Nathaniel Stevens’s Summary

Nate is an economics major who will graduate from SUNY Genseo in May 2009. Nate has an interest in the role of government in a capitalist society as well as an interest in the economies of the developing world.

Nate has experience with analysing capital markets. This experience has been gained through countless hours of study on topics such as "Open Economy Macroeconomics", as well as through extra curricular activities such as Finance Club, Econ Society, and Fed Challenge.

Nate has experience in organizational leadership and event planning. For 3 semesters Nate served as the Activities Commission Special Events Coordinator. Activities Commission is a part of student government and is responsible for programming events that serve the interest of the student body. As Special Events Coordinator, Nate managed a budget in excess of $25,000 per year. Nate used that money to plan campus wide events such as Springfest, Siblings Weekend, Homecoming, Winterball, and the Monster Mash Bash. These events drew crowds that numbered in the hundreds. Nate routinely had to collaborate with many different groups to ensure the success of these events. Additionally, Nate was responsible for developing and implementing the inaugaural Winterball and the first ever Monster Mash Bash. In 2008 Activities Commission won the award for "Organization of the Year" at SUNY Geneseo.

Nate's future goals include learning as much as he can throughout his life, pursuing interests in the fields of finance and economics, helping others realize the benefits that proper money management can bring, developing the human capital that our society has to offer, working with talented individuals to develop world changing ideas, and positively impacting the world he lives in. Finally, Nate will be attending the Maxwell School of Citizenship at Syracuse University as a Master of Public Administration candidate.

  • Team Member

    SUNY Geneseo Fed Challenge Team

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    January 2008 — April 2009 (1 year 4 months)

    Fed Challenge is a nationwide competition that tests students knowledge of the Federal Reserve and the US Economy. Fed Challenge is to Economics majors what Mock Trial is to Pre Law majors.
    The competitions involve a 20 minute presentation followed by a 15 minute question and answer session per team. The competition revolves around each team analysing and recommending the appropriate federal funds rate. Each team is scored and the team that scores the best wins.
    For Geneseo, there are up to three rounds of competition. The first round is the Buffalo round. If Geneseo wins they move on to the New York City round. If Geneseo wins that they move on to the national round in Washington DC. If Geneseo wins that they are national champions. In 2007 the Geneseo Fed Challenge team finished second in the Washington DC round to the Harvard team.

  • Money Manager

    SUNY GENESEO Finance Club

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    February 2007 — April 2009 (2 years 3 months)

    The SUNY Geneseo Finance Club works towards the goal of increasing the financial literacy of students at SUNY Genseo. This is achieved by giving students the oppurtunity to manage money by investing in the bonds and equities markets. The Club is student run and all investment decisions are made by the club as a whole. The club is broken down into educational teams composed of groups of 5-10 students. The team leaders work to educate the teams and then the teams, as a whole, are free to propose investments to the overall club. The club then listens to the proposal, debates the proposal, and acts on the proposal.
