Kendall Davis’s Education

  • Ohio State University

    BFA, Expanded Arts, September 1990 — May 1992

    I received my Bachelor of Fine Arts degree from The Ohio State University with an “Award for Excellence in the Arts” in June of 1992. My major area of study was in the Expanded Arts program – an interdisciplinary program that allowed me to study non-traditional media in the arts. I worked in combination of video, live performance, computer applications, music composition and recording, as well as the inclusion of traditional media under the supervision of Professor Susan Dallas-Swann.

    I was also the first undergraduate art student at OSU to work with the Department of Physics to study holography and laser light applications as an art form under the supervision of Professor Harris Kagan,. Professors Kagan, Dallas-Swann, and I coordinated the first exhibit of Artist’s Holography at OSU, and the three of us worked to secure a grant which resulted in the establishment of a holography facility within the Department of Art.
