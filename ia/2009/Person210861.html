Sheri Bell-Rehwoldt’s Summary

I am an award-winning freelance writer, editor, and author who pens features and profiles of people, food, and places for businesses, websites and publications including American Profile, Family Circle, Go, Ladies' Home Journal, and The Washington Post.

I have published nine children's books (fiction and nonfiction), and helped to co-write three non-fiction books for adults.

I also write/edit brochures, ads, proposals, articles, books, etc. for business clients.

The part you really need to pay attention to: I'm Linkedin because I'm seeking editing/proofreading work, school visit/conference bookings, and article/book gigs -- especially of cool people, companies, or places.

I'm pursuing a gig as a personal assistant, or fabulous jobs in communication/marketing in tourism, hospitality, media or healthcare.

Sheri Bell-Rehwoldt’s Experience

  • Writer/Author/Editor

    (Self employed)

    (Writing and Editing industry)

    May 2000 — Present (9 years)

    Award winning author and freelance writer and editor of features and profiles for publications and websites including American Profile, Family Circle, Ladies' Home Journal, The Washingon Post, and numerous inflight, regional and alumni magazines.

    Collateral writer/editor for business clients, helping to develop brochures, newsletters, articles, training materials, and book drafts.

    Author/co-author of 10 books, eight for juvenile market. My latest children's book, a humorous picture book titled You Think It's Easy Being the Tooth Fairy?, has sold 15,000+ copies since its August 2007 launch, and is in its third printing. It will soon be printed in French.

    Special skills: quality writing, interviewing, researching, copyediting, and critiquing. Desktop publishing, graphic design, web site development, marketing, public relations, tactful management of client/editor/source expectations.

  • Baking Student

    Erie County Community College, Culinary Department

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    September 2008 — December 2008 (4 months)

    I took a semester of baking classes offered by ECC's culinary department. I attended class six days a week, three days in the classroom studying hospitality math and sanitation, and three days in the kitchen (225+ hours of kitchen experience).

    Experience garnered:
    * basic understanding of hospitality math (from recipe conversion to budgets)
    * knowledge in creating and maintaining a sanitary environment for food prep and service
    * comfort and confidence participating in the operation of a commercial kitchen
    * mastering the basics of various baked goods, including cakes, cookies, pies, pastries, and breads.

    I garnered five A's, one in each class. I also received an A on the sanitation certification test, meaning that I am certified to teach sanitation within a restaurant establishment.

  • 2008 Conference Co-Chair (Volunteer Position)

    American Society of Journalists and Authors (ASJA)

    (Writing and Editing industry)

    April 2007 — April 2008 (1 year 1 month)

    My wonderful co-chair Salley Shannon (a fabulous writer) and I planned the overall look and feel of the 2008 conference, held April 11-13, in NYC.

    It was a challenge, given the diversity and strong opinions of the ASJA members. But I enjoyed further honing my flexibility, creativity, and interpersonal skills, and watching our all-volunteer conference committee gel into a cohesive group. Without them, the conference would not have offered up its first-rate educational sessions, writer/editor one-on-ones, and numerous networking opportunities.

    Per the ASJA website: "Founded in 1948, the American Society of Journalists and Authors is the nation's leading organization of independent nonfiction writers. Our membership consists of more than 1,100 outstanding freelance writers of magazine articles, trade books, and many other forms of nonfiction writing, each of whom has met ASJA's exacting standards of professional achievement."

    http://www.asja.org
