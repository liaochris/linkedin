Tom Vasko’s Summary

Tom Vasko is one of i3Logic’s longest tenured team members having joined the company as a Graphic Designer in 1984 (when it was called VuCom Graphic Systems).

His unique ability to understand the nuances of graphic design coupled with his project and personnel management skills quickly placed Tom on a management track. He served several production management roles before becoming General Manager of VuCom’s Cleveland office in 1989.

In 1991, Tom moved to corporate headquarters in Detroit to become Director of Creative Services, and later, VP of Solutions Management. He was ultimately promoted to VP of Innovative Technologies in 2004, after the acquisition of VuCom and the founding of i3Logic.

In his capacity as VP of Innovative Technologies, Tom coordinates i3Logic’s staff of eLearning developers (ELD’s) and oversees the evauation process for new graphic and multi-media software technologies and its implementation. Under his direction, the i3Logic ELD team develops highly interactive and sophisticated simulations, gaming, and scenario-based courseware solutions.

Recently, Tom took over the management of i3Logic’s product development team, providing day-to-day oversight of the staff developing i3Logic’s comprehensive learning and community building ASP software system, Via>Grapevine.

A 1984 graduate of LaRoche College, Tom has a BS in Graphic Design. He also earned his Project Management Certification (PMC) from Villanova University in 2004.

Tom and his wife, Lisette, are the proud parents of daughters Aviana and Renell.
