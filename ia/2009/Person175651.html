Niko Roorda’s Summary

Niko Roorda studied physics and philosophy of science at Utrecht University. He worked as a teacher in secondary and higher education.

From 1991, together with others he developed the new university study programme ‘Sustainable Technology’, of which he was the manager for five years.

He designed and managed Project Cirrus, which worked on the integration of sustainable development in the study programmes of the Faculty of Science and Technology of Avans University. For this project, in 2001 he received the “Columbus' Egg”, the Dutch National Award for Innovation and Sustainable Development.

At present he works for DHO, the Dutch Foundation for the Advancement of Sustainable Development in Higher Education (www.dho.nl). For DHO he developed the AISHE system, a method to assess the rate of integration of sustainable development in higher education, which has been applied in universities in several countries in Europe. His primary task for DHO consists of consultancy and coaching of sustainability releated educational development projects in universities , and the training and certification of AISHE assessors.

He wrote two course books about sustainable development, which are used in many universities (see: www.duurzaam.wolters.nl).
He developed, coordinated and partially wrote a study course on sustainable entrepreneurship and CSR for staff members of commercial companies.

His latest book was published in November 2008. In this book he explains the whole concept of sustainable development for the main public.
