Peter Astley-Sparke’s Summary

IT/Operations executive with an outstanding track record of product and service delivery. International work experience in the USA and Europe. Inspirational manager who has built organisations from scratch, and led geographically dispersed teams of 200+. Polished presenter and persuasive negotiator, able to deal with stakeholders at board level. A track record of successful outsourcing, change management, process improvement and customer service projects.

Interested in both established and startup companies, particularly in the financial services sector.

Peter Astley-Sparke’s Experience

  • Director

    Software Magic

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Animation industry)

    January 2002 — April 2008 (6 years 4 months)

    • Director with overall P&L responsibility.
    • Led the design and development of Cortege®, a relationship management system employing a sophisticated network model and graphical interface.
    • Oversaw the 2005 launch of Cortege®, including successful advertising campaigns on MySpace, Friends Reunited and LinkedIn.
    • Cortege® won first prize in its category at the ISTC, and has customers in fourteen countries worldwide.
    • Created a turnkey online sales process, with purchase incentives, partner sites, promotions and a customer referral programme built into the website and software.
    • Prior to Cortege®, served as interim Vice President of Operations for SATLYNX, Europe’s largest provider of satellite broadband services.
    • Responsibilities covered program management, SATLYNX’s installer and reseller networks, procurement and customer service.
    • Major clients included British Telecom, France Telecom and ExxonMobil; who run the largest private VSAT network in the world (5000+ sites).

  • Operations Director

    Protagona Worldwide plc

    (Public Company; 201-500 employees; Computer Software industry)

    August 2000 — November 2001 (1 year 4 months)

    • Operations Director on the Group’s main board reporting to the CEO.
    • Responsible for product planning, development, QA, training, customer service and process improvement. Managed a staff of 80 and a budget of £3.5 million.
    • Led a multi-disciplinary team charged with delivering a new product strategy for Protagona. This strategy was adopted by the board of directors, and formed an integral part of the company’s fund-raising prospectus.
    • Closely involved in Protagona’s 2001 fund-raising that secured £15 million of additional cash via a placing of 258 million new ordinary shares.
    • Initiated a major re-structuring of Operations, reducing expenses by 30% while preserving investment in core services and product development.
    • Significantly improved the efficiency of customer services via the introduction of a new Help desk, Service Level Agreements and revised working practices.

  • CTO

    (Public Company; 51-200 employees; Information Technology and Services industry)

    December 1998 — August 2000 (1 year 9 months)

    • Chief Technology Officer reporting to the CEO.

    • Developed Kokua’s product strategy, providing an end-to-end service from sales to installation and customer care.
    • Oversaw development of the company’s satellite terminal, incorporating broadband Internet access and a point-of-presence for value-added services.

  • Vice President

    Dow Jones Telerate

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; DJ; Financial Services industry)

    February 1991 — November 1998 (7 years 10 months)
