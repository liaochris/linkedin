Gary Thompson’s Summary

Gary brings over twenty years of experience in business development and strategic leadership in both the public and private sectors with emerging growth and leading global technology companies.

Gary sees trends, and both SilverLining and C.L.O.U.D. are being formed to tackle the most important and enduring trend of all, people.

In addition to his role as Managing Director at Tolarus Partners, Inc., Gary was most recently a member of the founding team and Vice President of Sales & Marketing for Kimbia Software, Inc., a leader in online fundraising technology. Previously, he served as Region Manager for Apple's education business in Texas from 2004 to 2006.

Gary's varied interests have also included the launch of the Texas eCommerce Association in the late 1990s to bring together executives in Texas around the rapidly evolving internet and eCommerce business models. It was during his time running Texas eComm that he was appointed by Governor Bush to the eGovernment Task Force, work which continued when he was reappointed by Governor Perry to the Texas Online Authority. Gary is engaged politically through his affiliation with MavPac and the Leadership 2020 team of Senator Kay Bailey Hutchison, as well as serving as her Travis County chair in her run for Governor.

Gary's passion for education is reflected in his own pursuit of knowledge and learning. Gary received his BA at Northwestern University in 1987, continuing with an MBA at the Kellogg Graduate School of Management in 1992 and a Juris Doctor from the University of Texas School of Law in 2000. During his legal studies, Gary also served as the Editor-in-Chief for the nationally recognized Texas Review of Law and Politics.

Gary's core passion and mission is to "to unleash the power of people. To allow all to dream; to allow all to harness their dreams; and to collect the power of those dreams to transform lives and society."

Gary Thompson’s Experience

  • Co-Founder and President

    Consortium for Local Ownership and Use of Data (C.L.O.U.D.), Inc.

    (Information Technology and Services industry)

    April 2009 — Present (3 months)

    The Consortium for Local Ownership and Use of Data (C.L.O.U.D.), Inc. is being organized to facilitate the advocacy and research necessary for a fundamental reorganization of the Internet. That reorganization is premised on the idea that individuals should be in control of their information and not the organizations that happen to be storing the information.

    CLOUD, Inc. has a membership structure for both individuals and corporations, so as to engage all those who wish to see public and international awareness raised on the new role of profile-based computing and privacy.

    Through white papers, member-centric web communities, web blogs and other publicity mechanisms, the organization will solicit, involve and advocate for this transformation of the Internet and a new path to more robust privacy that benefits its members and the public through the Internet.

    In the longer-term, the organization, through its governing body, and votes of its membership will house critical open standards and other necessary enabling resources within the C.L.O.U.D., so as to make those tools fully available in the public domain, in a similar fashion to XBRL International and XBRL US.
