Judith Christensen’s Experience

  • Costume Maker/Designer

    Olympus Flag and Banner

    (Wholesale industry)

    July 2004 — Present (4 years 11 months)

    I design and create custom walk-around mascot costumes for sports teams, community organizations, and corporations.

    Working from a creative design sketch, I drape and pattern a costume that reflects the customer's vision and is comfortable and easy for the performer to wear.

    Walk-around mascot costumes are made of fabric, fur, or spandex; they are often built over a foam frame or "body pod", which I also design and build.

    I make accessories for the character costume as well - fabric-covered foam feet or shoes, hands or gloves, and hats.

  • Technical Product Manager

    Heyman Corporation

    (Apparel & Fashion industry)

    March 2001 — July 2004 (3 years 5 months)

    As Technical Product Manager, I was responsible for the fit and quality of children's apparel imported from Asian factories. I visited the factories often to work directly with their designers and managers, and conducted fittings in Seoul, Bangkok, Hong Kong, and New Delhi.

    I conducted fittings of samples in the corporate office as well, ensuring quality garments and on-time deliveries.

    I was responsible for the safety of children's apparel, monitoring the testing of zippers, snaps and other small parts; flammability; and overall quality testing.

    I over saw a staff of 1 - 5 Technical Designers.

  • Technical Designer

    Kohl's Department Stores

    (Public Company; KSS; Retail industry)

    August 1997 — March 2001 (3 years 8 months)

    As Technical Designer, I was responsible for the fit and quality of Men's Sportswear and Dress Shirts.

    I wrote detailed specifications for use by overseas factories in the manufacture of private-label merchandise.

    I conducted fittings and recommended changes and corrections to produce a garment to meet Kohl's standards, and within shipping deadlines.

    I oversaw the procurement of a color-matching system to ensure color consistency throughout the product line.

  • Cost Estimator

    Florence Eiseman, LLC

    (Apparel & Fashion industry)

    April 1989 — March 1994 (5 years)

    As Cost Estimator, I calculated the cost of labor, raw materials, and overhead to estimate the cost of production of children's clothing.

    I was also responsible for writing the "routing" directions, or the sequence of operations to efficiently produce garments.

    I analyzed data seasonally to see where we were significantly off-estimate, and recommended changes in design or technique to simplify production and improve profitability.

    For a short period of time, I was also responsible for writing purchase orders for fabric and supplies and tracking inventory.
