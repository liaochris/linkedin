Floyd Teter’s Summary

Floyd started his career in the early 80s at Northrop Electronics as a Manufacturing Engineer, developing an expertise in estimating and cost analysis. While at Northrop, Floyd narrowed his specialization to negotiating major subcontracts. He then moved on to establish and lead a group of negotiation specialists at Parker Hannifin and Fairchild Controls. From there, Floyd expanded his role into a wider array of financial management duties at Infotec Development, Pilkington Aerospace and Sony. While at Sony, Floyd participated in his first Oracle E-Business project. As a result, Floyd's career shifted in a new direction and he joined Oracle Corporation. With a strong background in business processes, Floyd quickly became a Managing Principal Consultant and excelled at leading Oracle E-Business implementation teams. Floyd is now a Project Manager for institutional business systems at the California Institute of Technology - Jet Propulsion Laboratory.

Floyd has a Bachelor of Science degree in Business Administration and a Juris Doctor, as well as project management-related certifications from the California Institute of Technology and the University of Southern California. He has been published in many magazines and technical journals, has presented at numerous professional conferences, and was formerly an instructor in database design with California Polytechnic University. Floyd is involved in the Oracle Applications Users Group (OAUG), where he serves as Chair of the Fusion Council as well as a memer of the Customer Support Council and the Education Committee. Floyd is also a member of the Product Development Committee in the International Oracle Users Council (IOUC), working in with Oracle Corporation's product development teams in the development of Oracle products. Floyd was recently named as the first Oracle ACE from the applications user community, and was also honored as the first recipient of the OAUG President's Award.

Floyd Teter’s Specialties:

I have developed three skills that help enterprises maximize the value of their investment in the Oracle E-Business Suite:

1) gathering user needs and mapping those needs to functionality in the Oracle E-Business applications;

2) leading cross-functional project teams in achieving project objectives;

3) translating between functional and technical people, so that technical people understand functional requirements and functional people understand technical solutions.
