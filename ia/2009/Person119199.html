Jon Billett’s Summary

There was a time when I called myself a graphic designer, however, that title has become all too broad of a term these days. Interactive designer? Sure, but wait – I can still design a mean page layout and set it up for five-color printing. Hmm... artist? That term is naturally associated with an easel, a wooden stool and an oil paint palette. Most of my days are spent in front of a computer with a wireless mouse or a Wacom tablet by my side. Web designer? That's too techy. How do I sum up everything I do into one title? I don’t. Simply because it would be unfair to do that. But I did come up with a quick sentence to give you an idea of what I’m all about. I help people digitally paint their future, through means of marketing, advertising, corporate identity, user interface, movement and interactivity, using all means of multimedia. Take a deep breath if you need to.

I’m also proud to say I’m now a part of that ever-so-popular "five-years experience" club. No confetti? A little disappointing. But reaching that nice round number – there’s definitely a sense of accomplishment there. And I’m ready to kick things up a notch for the next chapter of my career. Every year has been a giant stepping stone for the next. I earned a degree in Graphic Design while being trained by some of the best professionals the industry has to offer at the Rochester Institute of Technology (R.I.T.). Since then, I have tried to sample as many flavors of the industry as possible, including page layout for a free local newspaper. I spent eight months at a design firm, handling small- to medium-sized clients. I introduced interactive design to an in-house ad agency where I was able to bring new life to the homepages of the Philadelphia Flyers and 76ers. And all the while, I've been running the show with my freelance business. I’ve also been contracted to work on-site at some of the largest ad agencies in the area. It’s safe to say I’m excited about what the future holds for me.
