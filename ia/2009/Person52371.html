Damian Radcliffe’s Summary

Whilst studying Modern History at Oxford University Damian was Head of Programming for launch Oxygen FM, the UK’s first city wide FM radio station where all output was produced by volunteers. He presented a range of music and speech programmes before going onto do development work for the stations commercial partner, The Local Radio Company.

In 1999 he moved to the BBC, working firstly on the DAB Digital Radio team, before moving to BBC TV Presentation as a Network Director. In 2001 he joined the fledgling interactive TV team where he was the producer for the launch of the BBC’s first 24/7 interactive TV service.

Later that year Damian became Business Manager for the BBC’s New Media & Technology Division, reporting directly to the Divisional Director, Ashley Highfield.

In 2003 Damian joined the UK charity CSV, where he undertook a number of roles across media training and broadcast production activities.

Between 2005 and 2008 as Head of Broadcasting & Media Services he managed CSV’s strategic partnership with the BBC across the UK, including the SONY award winning CSV Action Network and media training projects supporting BBC Scotland and Northern Ireland's desire to broaden the diversity of their workforce.

In Summer 2008 he joined the Ofcom as Manager for the English Regions. Ofcom is the regulator for the UK communications industries, with responsibilities across television, radio, telecommunications and wireless communications services.

Damian writes a regular column for Third Sector magazine and is a Fellow of the Royal Society of Arts. From 2005-2008 was a member of the Radio Skills and Development Forum run jointly by Skillset and the Radio Academy.

In his spare time he like to sleep, eat cake, take photographs, watch movies and travel. Not necessarily in that order.
