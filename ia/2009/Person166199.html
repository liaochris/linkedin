Mitchell C. Shapiro’s Experience

  • Board Member

    Universal EMR Solutions LLC

    (Computer Software industry)

    2008 — Present (1 year)

    I am actively involved in the day to day operations of Universal EMR Solutions, a company that provides Electronic Medical Record systems for physicians, practice groups, nursing homes and hospitals (under the trade name Physican's Solution). Universal EMR also provides a Patient Direct medical vault product where an individual can update his/her own medical records, advanced directives and emergency contact information and provide access to EMT, emergency room or other medical personnel through any computer or smart phone via the web, a two-dimensional bar code on an alert card carried by the individual or a flash drive key fob carried by the individual. Universal EMR's CCHIT-certified Physican's Solution software was designed by doctors for doctors, and has been operating successfully in active physician practices for five years. The EMR system is available to be installed in a practice group as designed for that specialty type or customized for the individual practice. Inquiries should be forwarded to me at mcs@universalemrsolutions.com.

  • Managing Partner

    Law Offices of Mitchell C. Shapiro

    (Law Practice industry)

    2004 — Present (5 years)

    After leaving C&P immediately following the approval of the mult-billion dollar settlement of the Visa Check/Mastermoney Antitrust Litigation, I founded my own firm as the vehicle to continue to counsel and represent my clients while pursuing other interests and challenges. I have gotten involved in a number of start up companies, serving on the advisory boards of a number of them, and remain involved in the day to day operations of Universal EMR Solutions LLC, a company that provides electronic medical record systems for physicians, practice groups, nursing homes and hospitals (www.universalemrsolutions.com). In addition to my own business ventures, I have continued to counsel clients on antitrust and litigation matters and to represent them in complex commercial litigations, including Arnold Chevrolet et al v. Newsday (a case brought on behalf of 60 auto dealers against Newsday alleging fraud over the circulation figures used as the basis for advertising fees and an attempt/conspiracy to monopolize the market for auto advertising on Long Island, which settled at the end of discovery) and numerous matters involving partnerships and limited liability companies in both traditional and high-tech fields. I have spoken at numerous conferences regarding Class Actions, the Bankcard Industry and Strategies to Respond to a Data Security Breach. The successful practice of law is a passion of mine that I believe will never be extinguished. Contact me via email at mcs@mcshapirolaw.com.

  • Founding Partner

    Constantine & Partners

    (Partnership; Law Practice industry)

    April 1994 — January 2004 (9 years 10 months)

    Previously associated w/Stroock (worked closely with USDJ Al Hellerstein), and McDermott (1992-94, beginning the affiliation with Constantine that led to founding C&P, which was named a top plaintiffs’ litigation firms in the country (Nat'l Law Journal) in July 2003. In Wal-Mart et al. v. Visa and MasterCard (a/k/a In Re VisaCheck/ MasterMoney Antitrust Litigation), I coordinated the pre-trial discovery, countless motions (including the successful class certification) and massive class notice (to 5 million merchants) on behalf of the plaintiffs in one of the largest cases in history, involving production of more than 5 million documents and approximately 400 depositions in the retailers' challenge to the bankcard associations’ alleged attempt/conspiracy to monopolize the debit card market. While logging more hours over the decade-long course of the case than anyone else - I was responsible for hiring, supervising and coordinating the countless vendors and co-counsel necessary to support this monumental effort. In addition to coordinating, negotiating and litigating over the pre-trial discovery, and handling key depositions, I was one of the 4 members of the trial team that simultaneously mediated and negotiated the largest antitrust settlement in history on the morning of trial -- $3 billion fund & unprecedented injunctive relief (valued at $100 billion). In a press release, C&P heralded my major part in the firm’s role as lead counsel for the retailers in achieving the historic settlement, approved by the federal court in December 2003, and called a “Home Run for the Retailers”.

    I also played a major role in numerous other significant cases, including Beyer Farms, Inc. v. Elmhurst Dairy, Inc. (a case featured in BNA’s Antitrust Reporter), and Goodstein-Milstein v. City of New York. I left the firm and the full time practice of law immediately following the historic multi-billion dollar settlement of the Visa/Mastercard case.
