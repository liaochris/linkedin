Kevin Mote’s Summary

I have been writing quality code in numerous languages since 2001. I enjoy working on teams and have experience as lead engineer on complex group projects. I'm a strong developer who knows how to work well with clients, colleagues, and management to produce high-quality, easily maintainable code.

In recent years I have been one of the principal code developers on numerous projects in the fields of graph and image analysis, as well as information visualization. I have made significant contributions to several analytical tool-suites, where I have participated both in theroretical research and algorithmic implementations as well as user interfaces design.

My enthusiasm for cutting-edge research and software development makes me a diligent worker and a quick learner.

Kevin Mote’s Specialties:

My expertise is primarily in the areas of information visualization, image processing, graph theory, map-label disambiguation, and graphical user interfaces. I also have recent experience in social network theory and social media analysis. (Note blog link, below.)

My language specialties include VisualC++ (MFC), Qt, and Open Inventor.
I am also familiar with Bison/Flex, HTML/XML, Python, and graphic design (including GUIs).
