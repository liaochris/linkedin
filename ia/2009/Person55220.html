Darrin Brown’s Experience

  • Strategic Advisor

    AARP

    (Non-Profit; Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    August 2008 — Present (1 year 5 months)

    In this position, I conduct research and analysis on a broad range of market, industry and competitive issues, largely in the financial services and economic policy arena. My responsibility is to identify strategic market implications and competitive trends, and advise senior leadership through well-argued monthly/quarterly/annual and ad hoc reports and presentations.

  • Senior Legislative Representative

    AARP

    (Non-Profit; Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    May 2007 — August 2008 (1 year 4 months)

    As Senior Legislative Representative, I was AARP's chief representative to the United States Congress and the Executive Brance on financial services-related issues, which special emphasis on insurance, banking and investments. I was expected to negotiate on behalf of the association with policymakers and their staffs, exercising substantive judgement/discretion to achieve a result most consistent with the association's stated policy. The financial services industry is highly complex and closely regulated, and having intimate knowledge of how policies affect the operations of the industry I was able to provide a well-informed and unique consumer perspective.

  • Associate State Director for Advocacy

    AARP Maryland

    (Non-Profit; Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    June 2005 — May 2007 (2 years )

    The Associate State Director for Advocacy is AARP's main (and often the sole) representative to the Maryland General Assembly and Executive Branch. I was responsible for advancing all of the association's state policies, ranging from expanding health care to the uninsured, promoting home and community based services for long-term care, and ensuring consumers have reliable and affordable electric utilities. This was an exciting opportunity, during which I expanded my knowledge base considerably and in an environment that has a sense of urgency. My writing skills were critical in that the testimony, letters, and technical documents I wrote were highly visible and referred to by many stakeholders in the Maryland policy community.

  • Assistant Director of Government Relations

    AEGON USA, Inc.

    (Public Company; AEG; Insurance industry)

    June 2004 — June 2005 (1 year 1 month)

    Working for AEGON allowed me to accomplish two specific goals: to gain experience working for an international firm (and exposure to the unique issues that operating in a global market poses), and to make a significant contribution to extending a firm's reach and effectiveness. To accomplish the latter, I opened AEGON's first Washington, DC office and served a primary contact to the U.S. policymaking community and the international business community. To increase my knowledge of the insurance industry was a great opportunity; to witness first hand the nature of global markets and the international flow of money was an extraordinary education that I will draw upon for the rest of my career.

  • Federal Affairs Liaison

    State Farm Insurance Companies

    (Privately Held; Insurance industry)

    December 2000 — June 2005 (4 years 7 months)

    This cross-cutting role required me to work closely with senior company leadership (including the CEO, COO and CFO) to advance our national public policy and regulatory agenda, and it also provided the chance to work with regional and divisional leadership on developing strategies for company legislative priorities. I was responsible for monitoring business trends from a policy/regulatory perspective and to counsel company leadership as needed. My work regularly appeared in executive briefings, white papers, speeches, and public statements.

  • Public Affairs Coordinator

    State Farm Insurance Companies

    (Privately Held; Insurance industry)

    December 1995 — December 2000 (5 years 1 month)

    Working in a field and regional offices, and later corporate headquarters, I was responsible for coordinating all public affairs activities, especially legislative and media related for one of the largest insurance companies in the world. Company leadership relied on my counsel on the external environment to make business decisions. I communicated daily with state government officials, members of the media, and industry contacts to advance the company's business goals. This was especially true during our successful efforts to achieve regulatory reform that not only helped increase our market share, but also helped lower insurance premiums for consumers. Also during my time in this role I was asked to transition this job, which had traditionally been a regional office function, into a field setting. The resulting operation became a model for the rest of the company's public affairs divisions.

  • Senior Underwriter

    State Farm Insurance Companies

    (Privately Held; Insurance industry)

    August 1993 — December 1995 (2 years 5 months)

    Insurance is a complex, highly regulated industry where business and public policy are inseperable. My education on the business side of insurance began as an underwriter in an auto insurance division. I was responsible for selecting risks that met our risk guidelines, assuring they were properly priced and endorsed, and terminating risks that no longer met our underwriting standards. For my assigned book of business I worked with agents and their staffs to build a book that was consistently profitable.
