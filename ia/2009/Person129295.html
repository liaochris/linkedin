Kenneth Ellis’s Summary

I have a broad understanding of the technology behind the digital age, from the subatomic up to global social networks. I have conducted research, written papers, and filed patents in several industries: materials used in microelectronic devices; the devices themselves; software built upon the devices. From the small to the large, I have:

1) Grown single-crystal materials, and silicon crystals from sand

2) Calulated from first principles the properties of silicon, using the local-density approximation to density functional theory

3) Performed research on MOSFETs (transistors), the basic element in integrated logic circuitry, specifically ultra-thin gate dielectrics. I have fabricated and tested, in a clean room with my own tweezers, MOS devices. I wrote an Elsevier encyclopedia entry on the subject.

4) Designed circuits, fried op-amps, am a licensed Ham, and can build a transmitter. Although my understanding of ULSI circuit design and wafer-scale integration is sketchy. I do not have significant experience with firmware or machine code.

5) Programmed kernel modules for Linux

6) Built deep-level packet inspection systems, and understand networking and the TCP stack

7) Architected and built various types of software applications, am proficient in a variety of languages from C to python, PHP, and even Flash. I have served as DBA, architect, developer, project manager, salesperson, unix administrator. I can cover any job in a modern internet company, including plumbing and espresso machine repair.

8) Served as chief scientist for a company that collects and scrapes news from around the world, and helped make sense of it all.

I am also an expert in the operation and management of small companies and startups, and have at various occasions performed project management, product management, sales, business development, strategic planning, IP strategy, research, and development.
