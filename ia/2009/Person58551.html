David P. Otey’s Summary

Nationally recognized broadcast technology expert, reinvented as a motivational speaker! Out to prove that an introverted engineer-type can be an engaging public speaker with a compelling story to tell. Check out my web site for more details, and continue to watch this space.

David P. Otey’s Experience

  • Owner

    David Otey Seminars

    (Professional Training & Coaching industry)

    June 2009 — Present (7 months)

    Award-winning speaker known for engaging audiences at a gut level with an inspiring message of recovering from depression

  • Consultant/Owner

    David P. Otey Consulting

    (Broadcast Media industry)

    November 2007 — Present (2 years 2 months)

    Self-employed consultant specializing in technical, regulatory, and implementation issues centered on emerging broadcast and broadcast-like technologies. Retained by AZCAR Training Systems as Subject-Matter Expert on Digital ENG systems. Areas of expertise include spectrum management; DTV measurement (both 8vsb and DVB-T); technician re-training for emerging digital technologies; and group facilitation, for both problem-solving and strategic planning.

  • General Manager

    AZCAR Training Systems

    (Public Company; 201-500 employees; Broadcast Media industry)

    January 2007 — November 2007 (11 months)

    Led team of instructors and media professionals creating a satellite-TV-based, distance-learning, technology training program intended to reach news-operations technicians at 900 domestic TV stations in a two-year period. Integrated equipment from various vendors into a single demonstration studio, and trained three instructors (with no previous broadcasting experience) in its use. Created the instructional curriculum and co-authored the course manual. Continue to provide subject-matter expertise and quality control (since Nov. '07 in the role of contract consultant).

  • Engineering Manager

    SignaSys

    (Broadcast Media industry)

    August 2005 — November 2006 (1 year 4 months)

    Provided subject-matter expertise and team leadership for a unique, industry-wide training effort around Digital Electronic News Gathering (DENG) to facilitate relocation of 2GHz Broadcast Auxiliary Services to new channel plan. Responsible for development of curriculum and primary design specifications for Hands-On Training System (demonstration equipment). Re-trained cadre of broadcast engineers to be instructor/demonstrators.

  • Frequency Coordination Director

    Society of Broadcast Engineers

    (Broadcast Media industry)

    April 2002 — July 2005 (3 years 4 months)

    Provided staff support to all of the Society’s program areas related to Broadcast Auxiliary Services (BAS) frequency coordination and related Spectrum Management issues. During 2004-2005, filled critical liaison role between Nextel and broadcast engineers nationwide on BAS Relocation Plan. Other responsibilities included managing SBE/NFL Game Day Frequency Coordinator program; representing SBE before the FCC (Media Bureau, Wireless Bureau, International Bureau, and Office of Engineering & Technology) in rulemaking proceedings of interest; tracking regulatory changes and making recommendations to SBE board and staff; and informing, equipping and managing 200+ volunteers nationwide.
