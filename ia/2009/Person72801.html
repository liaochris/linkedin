  • PR-advisor

    Edgy

    (Public Relations and Communications industry)

    February 2009 — Present (1 month)

    Edgy concentrates on small companies with great ambitions. Especially in times like these, they could do with a bit of free publicity. With my journalistic experience, I know exactly what journalists want (and more importantly, what they don't want). So if you need a biting pen with which to write press releases, newsletters, website texts and leaflets, get Edgy!

    Feel free to contact me on emily@edgywords.nl for more information, also about translations and other word-related activities. Edgy's website is in the making.

    "Edgy. Bijzonder betrokken, scherper geschreven."

  • Novelist (well...)

    Me, myself and I

    (Writing and Editing industry)

    November 2008 — Present (4 months)

    Mid-November, I decided to leave Quote temporarily to work on my first novel. The time was right, the urge was there, so why not jump? (I never ever heard of financial crises urging people to stay put.) Quote's editor-in-chief stimulated me to try it out for three months and to come back in March 2009.

    Then I tasted the sweet flavour called 'entrepreneurship', and I liked it. I decided not to go back to Quote but to found my own little company. In February 2009, Edgy was born. I'm convinced it's viable, and it will finance my rather decadent life as a novelist.

    For more information on my novel, do check out my blog! http://boekopdeplank.blogspot.com/

  • Journalist

    Quote

    (Privately Held; Internet industry)

    October 2007 — November 2008 (1 year 2 months)

    Why would a young journalist want to change jobs if she's working for a major magazine with a great reputation? Because opportunities may arise that are too attractive to decline. With the departure of its editor-in-chief Jort Kelder, Quote magazine has to rebuild its future. Quote's potential is great, something which not everybody is willing to admit. It's a fantastic feeling to be a part of a vibrant team, determined to turn Quote - a tongue-in-cheek Forbes-type of glossy - into something that is taken as seriously as it should be. My expertise: the art market. I'm also getting rather proficient at convincing people to co-operate - a useful quality for a journalist.

  • Journalist

    Elsevier

    (Public Company; Writing and Editing industry)

    March 2005 — September 2007 (2 years 7 months)

    I had my first experience at Elsevier, the biggest opinion magazine of the Netherlands, as an intern (March 2005, economics department). This internship was part of my MA degree at the University of Amsterdam. As planned, I finished the internship in May 2005, but I continued to write for Elsevier as a freelance journalist, combining this work with my studies. In September 2005, I was offered a full-time position - an opportunity I gratefully seized, even though that meant I'd have to work during the day and study in my free time. (I am glad to say I graduated in December 2006, like everybody else.)

    Expertise: trends, luxury, tourism, marketing/advertising, retail, hotels/restaurants/catering industry. Temporary co-ordinator of the weekly features on digital stuff (May-September 2007). Frequent contributor to Elsevier Thema (glossy magazines on fashion, career paths, interior design, etc) and to www.elsevier.nl. Occasional anchor woman of Elsevier's webbased television.

  • Journalist

    Trouw

    (Newspapers industry)

    March 2004 — May 2004 (3 months)

    I never really dreamt of becoming a journalist. Somehow, I ended up at the editorial office of Trouw - one of the country's quality newspapers - which set the ball rolling. As an intern, I covered all sorts of national news. It was my first taste of journalism, after which I decided to go for a Master's degree in journalism. That, in turn, led me to Elsevier.
