Randy Wilson’s Summary

I'm a Life Performance Coach working with individuals, families and in groups – in person or on the telephone helping them to become more active creators of their lives. I work with all kinds of people including those who want to change their relationship to how they experience pain – emotional or physical. I also lead workshops on this approach.

At the Center, we offer personal, professional and executive coaching which deals with a broad range of development issues with a particular focus on relationship building. We draw upon the latest discoveries in the field of human development, most particularly that making long term qualitative changes in our lives involves learning that we have the capacity to perform.

I received my BA in human development from the SUNY, Empire State College. I completed a 3 year postgraduate certificate program in social therapy at the East Side Institute for Group and Short Term Psychotherapy in NYC, and studied clinical psychology for 5 years at the California School of Professional Psychology with a focus on serving underprivileged and disenfranchised populations. A gay man, born in Hawai'i to Chinese and African-American parents, I have been living creatively with HIV for over 27 years.

Believing strongly in community building, I volunteer with the All Stars Project (www.allstars.org), a non-profit organization which sponsors cutting edge after school performance based development programs to address and reverse the ways that poverty stunts the development of young people and limits their life choices.

I paint (abstract land/skyscapes on acrylic) in the mornings, rockclimb and skydive whenever I can (250 jumps & counting).

Contact me at 415-835-1010, ext. 3.
