Pascal van Eck’s Summary

Researcher and lecturer in Information Systems. Research interests include alignment of business and IT architectures at the enterprise level and IT security architecture. Focus is on case studies in the Dutch financial services industry and large-scale transaction processing, as well as cross-organizational integration of enterprise applications.

Pascal van Eck’s Specialties:

Computer Science, Information Systems, IT Architecture, Security Engineering

  • Co-ordinator Western Europe Group

    SIW International Volunteer Projects

    (Civic & Social Organization industry)

    September 1995 — September 2001 (6 years 1 month)

    SIW International Volunteer Projects is a Dutch voluntary service organization based in Utrecht, The Netherlands. SIW sends several hundreds of Dutch young (mostly 18-25 years old) voluntary workers to mostly short-term projects in countries in several continents. In addition to this, SIW hosts around 200 incoming volunteers from other countries in around 20 projects that SIW organizes together with partner organizations. The Western Europe Group, of which I was first a member and later co-ordinator, is responsible for training of Dutch volunteers who are interested in joining a project in Western European countries. Apart from this role, I had several other roles, including project leader (three times) in projects in The Netherlands and Greece and IT administrator. All of these were voluntary (unpaid) part-time positions that I (probably like all SIW's volunteers) consider a hobby.
