John Zimmerman’s Summary

- Five years as assistant professor at Washington University in St. Louis in the Computer Science and Radiological Sciences departments, specializing in digital imaging as applied to medicine.

- Fourteen years at Sun Microsystems in the Graphics, Imaging and Visualization group.

- Conceived and supervised the design of the Java Advanced Imaging (JAI) software API; head of JSR expert group 34.

- Currently conducting research in the nuclear medicine group at UC Davis.

John Zimmerman’s Specialties:

Medical Imaging. Digital image processing. Software design and implementation. Java. Marketing support, evangelism, people management, prognostication of industry trends.

  • Assistant Professor

    Washington University in Saint Louis

    (Educational Institution; 10,001 or more employees; Higher Education industry)

    September 1985 — June 1990 (4 years 10 months)

    Assistant Professor of Computer Science and Computer Applications in Radiology. Joint appointment in Computer Science and Radiology departments.

    Teaching, research, and public service; twenty-three reviewed papers in learned journals, some containing actual science. Research centering around the effective display of digital images, algorithms to improve image display, and using the visual psychophysics of the human visual system to assess display effectiveness. Sideline research including development of effective use of pseduocolor to aid image interpretation, volume visualization using successive refinement algorithms on the Pixar Image Computer, early research in wavelet transform as an alternative to sine/cosine transforms (ala JPEG). Strongly involved in the development of adaptive contrast enhancement algorithms beyond unsharp masking, principally Adaptive Histogram Equalization (AHE) and Contrast-Limited Adaptive Histogram Equalization (CLAHE).
