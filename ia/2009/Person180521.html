Pamela Jolly’s Summary

Pamela Jolly is a graduate of Hampton University and the Wharton School of Business, currently she is pursuing her Masters in Theology at Boston University. Prior to Wharton she worked at NationsBank in roles ranging from credit analyst to Commercial Lender to VP of Treasury Management for the Mid-Atlantic Region. While at Wharton she was active with positions on the AAMBAA Board the WMY Board, Wharton Women in Business, Wharton Career Center, and the Wharton Admissions Committee.

Post Wharton she has worked at the following:
Accenture, as a Manager in their New York Financial Services Strategy Practice working with Institutional and Large Commercial Banking Clients, Dubois Partners, a small private equity firm, which she helped to start with a mentor, Braun Consulting, where she helped start up a financial services strategy practice, and Rosetta Marketing Strategies Group, where she created marketing and selling strategies for some of the nations largest firms.

In 2004, Pamela launched Torch Enterprises, a strategic invesment firm that focuses on women and minority owned businesses. Torch stands for Passing the Torch, to build wealth on OUR communities. Her firm which has a fundraising target of $3 Billion will be the alternative for minority companies to remain competitors and not subsidiaries.

Since October 2005 Pamela has been working in the Southern Gulf Coast with the rebuilding effort post Katrina. As a result of the needs in the area she has launched a non-profit subcidiary, OneTorch which serves as a data intermediary for the displaced and is assisting 50,000 displaced residents in returning home.

Pamela is Chairman and Founder of the AAMBAA Alumni Network, a non-profit focused on uniting African American Alumni of the Wharton School of Business to work together to be a powerful resource for each other, the university, current and prospective students, and our communities at large.

Together with God we can do anything.
