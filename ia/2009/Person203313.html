Ron Berthiaume’s Summary

10 years of my 20 year career has been in in Private Equity. I started in the US doing middle market leveraged acquisitions and turnarounds of non-core divested divisions of public companies.  Working in a private fund, our strategy was to expose value by restructuring and active management of the acquired companies.  This often involved turnarounds of underperforming divisions and division divestitures.  We would bring new management with domain expertise into businesses in diverse industries.  I would occasionally take a management role as either the Chairman, CEO, or CFO.  Exits included sales to third party, MBO, or division close – down.  Sometimes the whole was a sum of parts, each of which requiring differing strategies.

I have been involved in acquisitions or start-ups in a variety of industries including billing, collections, call centers, and software.  I started a boutique LBO firm doing distressed acquisitions throughout the US headquartered in Colorado.  We sold our last company and ceased acquisitions in 2005.  I am enrolled in the executive MBA program at the University of California at Berkeley and Columbia Business School, and will complete classes in December 2009.
