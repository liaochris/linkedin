Christine Lu’s Summary

**best way to reach me is via a quick note on Twitter**

I am not a fan of structure. I make my best contributions to my life and the lives of others when I'm not stuck trying to conform to the title of a business card. I know this sounds like some kind of flowery internet hippie language, but it's not. Seriously. We're entering -- or already are in -- an era of communication where the line between who you are personally and who you are professionally has blurred.

Bio below helps to give you an idea of where I've been and how I ended up here:

Christine Lu is a self professed geek girl, sinophile and foodie who grew up in Southern California, received her BA in International Relations with a concentration on East Asian Studies at Boston University, then moved to Shanghai in 1998. After an internship at the American Chamber of Commerce in Shanghai, a stint as a clothing designer and marketing manager for her family's clothing retail business, she attempted to start an e-commerce site for women in China about 6 years before its time because hey, it was the dotcom bubble and all the cool kids were getting funded. The company was incubated then rolled into TV Shopping Network (TVSN), a joint venture funded by Duty Free Shoppers founder Robert Miller's private equity firm Search Group, Prudential Assets Management Asia and IAC's HSN (Home Shopping Network). Following the 2000 dot-com crash in China, Christine served for five years as Marketing Director for TV Shopping Network and oversaw the company's business development and spearheaded the company's internet, direct marketing and mail order business units in China.

In March 2007 Christine created and served as the Executive Producer and Host of Entrepreneur Magazine's The China Business Show which has since evolved into The China Business Network - a membership organization re-launching soon to fulfill a need for connecting individuals involved in cross border trade & investment with China.

Christine Lu’s Specialties:

Christine is a shareholder, advisor and contributor of The China Business Network. She is also a shareholder and advisor of M1NT and advises on digital strategy for non-profits Go Green Initiative and Project Rebirth.

  • Founder

    The China Business Network

    (Online Media industry)

    May 2007 — Present (1 year 11 months)

    If the gap in business culture and landscape between China and the West were a physical bridge, it might be immeasurably long, spanning two vast and vastly different terrains. It takes more than any one person to bridge the gap, or act as a guide on both shores. It takes a Network.

    The literally millions of people who have started building their own bridges to or from China face the continuing need to update information, improve skills, expand networks. Yet the sheer number of conferences, organizations, consultants and books makes it obvious: the space is so huge in scale, geography and subject matter that it's almost impossible to navigate.

    The China Business Network's mission is to provide the open platform where everyone with something to offer can profile themselves, index their skills, locate partners and customers, contribute to the conversation, learn, and reach the entire, targeted community of international business professionals active in China.
