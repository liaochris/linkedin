Deborah Brannon’s Summary

I have taught English both in Japan and America (Montgomery, Alabama) for the past four years, finally deciding to return to university for my PhD in Literature (primary concentration in Mythology/Folklore, with a possible secondary concentration in Victorian literature). I graduated from Huntingdon College in 2003 with a B.A. in Biology and Mythology (and a minor in English).

I am also a writer and have been practicing my craft for fifteen years: publication credits include The Prelude (Huntingdon College's literary magazine) and Scheherezade's Bequest (http://www.cabinet-des-fees.com). I have many pieces of fiction and poetry out for consideration. I also review books for Green Man Review (http://www.greenmanreview.com).

Finally, I also dabble in photography and jewelry-making.

Deborah Brannon’s Specialties:

curriculum development, lecture delivery, fairy tales & folklore, genre writing (fantasy/science fiction/creative nonfiction), poetry writing (structured/freestyle), reviewing literature (novels/anthologies/poetry)
