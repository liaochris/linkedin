Andy Levy-Stevenson’s Summary

Fundamentally I'm a marketing guy, with an extensive background in online marketing. Before I moved to Israel in 2004 I spent four years as the Manager of Interactive Marketing for Time Warner Cable in Minneapolis.

In the last couple of years I've begun to focus more on the practical business functions of web sites, and have worked in search engine marketing and search engine optimization as both a freelancer and an employee. I've managed pay per click campaigns for a variety of businesses, both B2B and B2C, and have an expertise in Google AdWords and other online ad management.

I've been successful in helping companies increase their visibility online through both natural and paid search, and as a result their revenues have grown.

If I had to name one thing I want from my work, it's to be proud of what I do. For instance, I've had fantasies for some years about running a coffee shop (no, I have no idea why, it just looks fun). But I'd want it to be a FANTASTIC coffee shop, the best one in town, where everyone felt welcome, the coffee was superb, and the atmosphere was great. Not a greasy spoon, but a really good place.

Now, I don't have to run a five-star, Michelin-rated, gourmet, white tablecloth restaurant; I'd be happy to run a little coffee shop. But it's got to be a GREAT little coffee shop.

I could extrapolate that out to all sorts of other things ... I'd sooner work for Ben & Jerry's than Baskin Robbins. I'd prefer to work for a small publisher producing the very best books in a particular field, rather than a huge publisher churning out junk.

Do you see what I mean? It's hard to express succinctly, but it's become a big issue for me. I want to be able to look at what I do/produce/make/sell and be really proud of it. Size or prestige is much less of an issue for me than quality.
