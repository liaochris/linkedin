Andrew Thorp’s Summary

Born Leeds, UK 1962.
Graduate of Manchester University (History, Politics, Sociology) 1983-86
Worked as a Tournament Director for Professional Golfers Association of UK 1987-91. Ran tournaments worldwide and refereed in Ryder Cup.

Managed/marketed commercial golf & leisure clubs throughout UK 1991-99, primarily business turn-around with an emphasis on driving membership & conference sales.

Set own business up 1999, specialising in the introduction of new people to golf (qualified as a teaching professional 2002).

Numerous consultancy and event management projects between 1999 and 2007, including a two-year spell as editor of a national golf publication.

During this period I used golf as a tool for developing business relationships and this network has provided the springboard for the work I currently do as a business speaker and soft skills trainer.

As a business speaker, I use sport (and the performing arts) as a source of inspiration and education in performance-related areas like sales, leadership and customer service.

Current Goals
To develop a reputation as a renowned inspirational speaker, thought-leader and people skills coach.

Andrew Thorp’s Experience

  • Owner

    Andrew Thorp (Business Speaker & Skills Trainer)

    (Professional Training & Coaching industry)

    January 2008 — Present (1 year 11 months)

    As a soft skills trainer, I coach essential communication skills with particular emphasis on leadership, building networks and public speaking/presenting.

    Coming from a mixed business/pro sport background, I draw analogies and inspiration from the world of elite sport and released a book in July 2009 - The 7 Pillars of Sporting Success: and how to apply them in business.

    As a business speaker I use material from the 7 Pillars book to emphasise the importance of leadership, partnerships and skill development in business, and highlight how attitude and emotional intelligence has a crucial role in success in the workplace.

    In January 2009 I co-founded the Business Skills Forum in Manchester, a personal development club for business owners and performance-related workers. It brings together peers from a wide range of industries to discuss core, generic skills like effective networking, sales techniques, presentation skills, motivation and emotional intelligence. The BSF combines networking with facilitated discussions, usually featuring a guest speaker.

    I also run a Discussion Group in association with Waterstone's book store in Macclesfield, Cheshire - again with a personal development theme.

    In October 2009, I agreed (along with associate Phil Harris) to launch the global social phenomenon Pecha Kucha in Manchester, a series of social evenings where people with passion for their subject present ideas to an invited audience – in an innovative format.

    Other activities:
    Sit on the advisory panel for the Sports Management Degree Course at Manchester Metropolitan University Business School.
    Advisor to the AIESEC student recruitment organisation.
    Provider of mentoring services through the NWDA business mentoring programme.
    Provider of pro-bono support for the iNspire Programme, a collection of experts in various fields who help charities survive and thrive during recession.
