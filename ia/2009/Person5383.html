Stephen E. Smith’s Summary

After 10 years in retail as a customer service manager, responsible for both customer service and numerous operation functions, I followed my dream to work in Japan. What was supposed to be a short three year stint as an English instructor turned into a major career change. I fell in love with teaching and have taught English as a Foreign Language (EFL) at a variety of institutions, ranging from kindergarten to university. The bulk of my teaching experience has been at the secondary level and in higher education.

My secondary experience started when I spent the first three years of my teaching career in an Intensive English program at a Japanese government high school located in Fukuoka Prefecture (1995-1998). I followed that up several years later by helping to establish an Intensive English Program at one of the most prestigious girls' high schools in Bangkok, Thailand (2002-2004).

My higher education experience began in my fourth year in Japan where I worked for four years at a foreign language vocational school (1998-2002). For the last four years (2004-present) I have been teaching EFL at one of the premier universities in Thailand. During this time, I have also taught Introduction to Management.

I was appointed the Head English Teacher in my department a year ago. Having managerial responsibilities again has fulfilled a latent desire to get back into the business realm. As such, I have taken on more responsibilities in my current role.

I believe that the next step in my career will be in education management. I would like to remain in the classroom, but would also like to take on more managerial and decision making responsibilities.

I also have a wide variety of experience teaching corporate English classes and language school conversation classes.
