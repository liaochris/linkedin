Ronald Wimer’s Experience

  • International Media Consultant

    Self-employed

    (Self-Employed; Myself Only; International Affairs industry)

    May 2001 — Present (8 years 2 months)

    Advise media in developing countries, particularly in North and West Africa. Assist English- and French-language newspaper editors refine and improve their publications. Also teach continuing education seminars for broadcast and print journalists.

    Since 2003, have helped Guinean television implement and manage a network of regional bureaus. Frequently visit West Africa for the U.S. Department of State to present lectures and workshops on political and economic reporting.

  • Managing Director

    The Press Box Limited

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Media Production industry)

    February 1998 — June 2000 (2 years 5 months)

    Created The Press Box Limited, a partnership of nine Anglo-American media professionals, which had representatives in London, Paris, Geneva, New York and Nairobi. Also supervised Press Box New Media, the company's web design service, which was mentioned in Inc magazine in 1999.

    The Press Box Limited originally helped foreign broadcasters in France during World Cup '98. The Press Box later expanded to offer news and public relations services, including reporting, interpreting, crewing and videotape editing. The Press Box produced features, documentaries and live-event programs for a variety of clients, including Reuters, The Associated Press, Sky News, Sky Sports, Arte and the WPP Group.

  • Senior Producer, Paris

    Worldwide Television News

    (Privately Held; 501-1000 employees; Media Production industry)

    1996 — 1998 (2 years)

    Was Paris bureau chief of WTN, which was an ABC News subsidiary before it merged with the Associated Press.

    Supervised a full-time staff of seven and 20 freelancers. Decided news coverage in France, and managed a regional budget of more than $3 million. Also supervised WTN journalists in French-speaking Europe and Africa. As a field producer, was the first television journalist on the scene of Princess Diana's mortal car crash and was the first reporter to cross the border from Rwanda into Zaire as rebels advanced north under the command of Laurent Kabila.

  • Senior Producer, London

    Worldwide Television News

    (Privately Held; 501-1000 employees; Media Production industry)

    1994 — 1996 (2 years)

    Alternated between Editor of the Day and Eurovision Editor, the number one and two editorial positions in WTN’s newsroom.

    Assigned and supervised WTN bureau chiefs and field producers as they worked on news packages for the agency. Also traveled extensively as a senior foreign producer. Returned to Paris during the first half of 1995 to coordinate WTN’s coverage of the French presidential election and the 50th anniversary of VE Day.

  • Assistant Managing Editor

    France 2

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; Broadcast Media industry)

    September 1992 — June 1993 (10 months)

    Was only American journalist working at Antenne 2 – France's largest public TV network (now called France 2). Was the Assistant Managing Editor of Direct de Christine Ockrent, a 90-minute weekly news show.

  • Associate Producer, 60 Minutes

    CBS News

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; CBS; Broadcast Media industry)

    1991 — 1992 (1 year)

    Paris-based producer: Traveled Europe and the Middle East, reporting foreign stories and writing draft scripts for 60 Minutes, including Palestinians in Kuwait and MEA, the story of Lebanon's national carrier Middle East Airlines, which survived the country's 15-year civil war and came to symbolize the hope and resilience of the Lebanese.

  • Freelance Arts Reporter & Editor

    Self-employed

    (Self-Employed; Myself Only; Newspapers industry)

    September 1987 — July 1991 (3 years 11 months)

    Was managing editor of Ricochet, a multilingual arts magazine, published in New York, was the contributing foreign editor of Thespis, a bilingual theater review in Tokyo, and covered New York theater as a freelance reporter for U.S. and European publications.

  • Instructor of Journalism

    Columbia University

    (Educational Institution; 10,001 or more employees; Higher Education industry)

    1986 — 1987 (1 year)

    Was a Broadcast Teaching Fellow. Taught reporting and writing for television at the Journalism School.
