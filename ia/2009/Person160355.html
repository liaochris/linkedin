  • Corporate Lean Manager

    Palm Harbor Homes, Inc.

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; PHHM; Construction industry)

    April 2007 — Present (1 year 10 months)

    Promote Lean thinking at Corporate level and across 12 manufacturing locations nationally. Develop training, perform audits, support improvement projects.

    Results: $5 million in savings in the first year due to inventory reductions, productivity gains, and safety improvements.

    What I learned: It's really hard to bring about change in a large organization. As a change agent, it's critical to create a link between improvement activities and business results. Otherwise, most high-level managers will not pay attention to the initiative and the whole team will follow suit and ignore it as well.

  • Continuous Improvement Manager

    Palm Harbor Homes, Inc.

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; PHHM; Construction industry)

    March 2006 — April 2007 (1 year 2 months)

    Coordinated improvement activities at two homebuilding factories. Performed training, led process improvement events, instituted an idea development system to harness employee creativity.

    Results: $100,000 in savings generated in the first year due to efficiency gains. Our successful pilot program led to company-wide adoption of the Lean Manufacturing methodology.

    What I learned: Lean Manufacturing is more about the thought process behind it than the various tools associated with it. Constantly identifying and eliminating waste through team-based problem solving is at the core of lean thinking, and leads to organizational learning.

  • Personal Construction Manager

    Palm Harbor Homes, Inc.

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; PHHM; Construction industry)

    November 2004 — March 2006 (1 year 5 months)

    Managed all aspects of field construction: initial meetings with customers, design, engineering, permitting, installations, inspections, close-out, and customer service.

    Results: facilitated the completion of over 100 homes in two years & became a Certified Building Contractor for Florida (CBC 1255085).

    What I learned: Communication is critical to customer satisfaction. The homebuilding process is extremely stressful for the customer, and "over-communicating" always helps to assuage their stress. And if the customer is upset, it doesn't matter how efficiently the project was completed.

  • Manager-in-Training

    Palm Harbor Homes, Inc.

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; PHHM; Construction industry)

    January 2003 — November 2003 (11 months)

    Trained in every aspect of the manufacturing/homebuilding process. Had responsibility for hiring, training, and directly supervising a team of 16 manufacturing associates.

    Results: "Rookie of the Year" award by the Division President.

    What I learned: In a chaotic work environment, leadership MUST focus on safety & quality all day every day. Even a slight loss of focus can result in hugely wasteful mistakes, or worse, injuries to the workforce. Management has to provide leadership in these areas.
