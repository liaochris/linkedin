Cheryl Culbertson, Founder’s Summary

Elder Options of Texas initially began as an 8x11 spiral bound publication back in January 1999. Featuring a variety of senior services and elder care resources throughout the Austin and surrounding areas, it was the first of its kind available.

When the first publication went to print I had just 19 advertiser's, many of whom I'm proud to say, are still advertising. I sold the publications donating a percentage of the proceeds to area not-for-profit organizations.

The Austin Public Library system, Texas Department of Human Services, the Texas Department of Aging and Disability Services, and many other organizations and agencies purchased copies. Many copies were also sold to individuals and family members.

To reach a broader based audience I discontinued printing the Elder Options of Texas publication and went 100% online January of 2000.

Austin In 2001, I founded Texas Elder Options (TEO), a networking group of individuals who work within the aging and health care community. In 2003, I passed it on to two professionals who continue to work in the Austin senior community. TEO continues to meet monthly.

I designed Elder Options of Texas to feature products and services by Texas regions. By doing so, individuals can quickly gain the information they then by simply accessing their specific region.

Many senior sites today are capturing the entire USA. But Elder Options of Texas specializes ONLY in the state of Texas. We cater to a very 'niche' market...Texans!

However, Google analytics show that our visitors not only live in Texas, but throughout the USA and world-wide including Canada, Mexico, the UK and Spain due to the global society we live in today. Our primary market is the Internet savvy baby boomer, followed closely by senior adults, one of the fastest growing demographics logging on today.

Today, 10 years later, I'm proud to say that Elder Options of Texas continues to remain strong!!
