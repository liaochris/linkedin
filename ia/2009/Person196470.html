Johan Andersson’s Summary

Johan is a senior software architect in the Frostbite Engine team at DICE. For the past 9 years he has been working with rendering, performance, and core engine systems for games in the Battlefield and Rallisport series. He leads the rendering and performance side of the Frostbite engine for future game titles on multiple platforms (PS3, Xbox 360, DX11).

Johan is a member of multiple industry graphics advisory boards in and has previously presented at GDC, Siggraph and Graphics Hardware on topics such as procedural texturing, graph-based shaders, terrain rendering and future GPU / game engine interaction.


Worked/working on the following games (in chronological order)

- RalliSport Challenge 1 (Xbox)
- Battlefield 1942 (PC)
- RalliSport Challenge 2 (Xbox)
- Battlefield 2 (PC)
- Battlefield: Bad Company (Xbox 360 & PlayStation 3)
- Battlefield 3

Public talks:

- SIGRAD 2006 keynote. "Frostbite Rendering Architecture"
- GDC 2007 talk. "Frostbite Rendering Architecture and Real-time Procedural Shading & Texturing Techniques"
- SIGGRAPH 2007 course. "Terrain Rendering in Frostbite using Procedural Shader Splatting"
- Graphics Hardware 2008 keynote. "The Intersection of Game Engines & GPUs: Current & Future"
- GDC 2009 talk. "Shadows & Decals - D3D10 Techniques in Frostbite"
- GDC 2009 talk. "Your Game Needs Direct3D 11, So Get Started Now!"
- SIGGRAPH 2009 course. "Parallel Graphics in Frostbite - Current & Future". Beyond Programmable Shading course.
