Mike Hanbery, M.B.A.’s Summary

The dynamic nature of Web 2.0 presents risks and opportunities for businesses and organizations. The double-edged sword of social media and social networks, especially blogs, LinkedIn, Facebook and Twitter, must be wielded strategically.

A few things you'll hear Mike say:

♦ If you are a business with customers, you don't choose whether or not to have a social media presence. There is a conversation about you happening right now on social networks. You choose how and at what point to engage in that conversation.

♦ Social media does not fundamentally change the way we do business. It is a new tool in which wise businesses apply the acumen we already have.

♦ In marketing: First, identify your customer. Build out your plan from there. In this way we maximize ROE (Return On Effort).

♦ Social media marketing is neither an end nor solution in itself. Like all business initatives, it yields the greatest return when integrated with larger, overarching strategies and assiged clear, attainable and measureable goals.

When choosing a social media partner, please weigh approach, acumen, background and perspective heavily. Social media strategy can be learned and/or outsourced to the exact degree optimal for your business.

Mike is a 20 year veteran of marketing, media and business launches from entrepreneur startup to Fortune 500 subsidiary. He is an alumnus of one of the top Executive MBA programs in the world. Achievements include envisioning and spearheading the first successful combination of HDTV, digital projection and satellite TV for a live audience. Mike has been a live TV director and event producer. He thrives in and on the moment.

Mike is a devoted father and husband. He grew up in Louisiana but now lives in, and loves, Denver. When Mike was a kid, he wanted to play 3rd base for the Yankees. If they call, he's still willing to talk.

    The questions we continually ask are:
    • Who is your customer?
    • What are her needs?
    • How is she currently meeting those needs?
    • Why is she using those products or services?
    • When will she be at her most ready to make a purchase decision?
    • Where will she make her purchase?

    This formula places your company and message in front of the warmest of prospects when they are at their most receptive.
