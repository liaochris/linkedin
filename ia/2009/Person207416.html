Sally Stephens’s Summary

A biotech business development and strategy executive who identifies targets, obtains senior leadership commitment and executes agreements, which help organizations develop and implement growth strategies. Creatively resolves obstacles in negotiations through exploring alternatives with a direct and personable style. Led and managed three functional teams – business development, strategy and project management.

Sally Stephens’s Specialties:

Sally Stephens’s Experience

  • VP Business Development & Strategy

    Novartis Diagnostics

    (Public Company; NVS; Pharmaceuticals industry)

    2006 — 2009 (3 years)

    Led business development activities for diagnostics business unit (annual revenue of more than $800M), including licensing, collaboration and acquisition negotiations. Oversaw activities of Project Management Office. Total of 12 staff and budget of ~$5 million.
    • Led several cross-functional strategy teams to identify technologies, markets and potential acquisition targets for entry into the molecular diagnostics market, specific emphasis on infectious disease and oncology. Quickly assessed market potential by utilizing internal and external experts, leveraging network to secure introductions to target companies. Presented strategy and due diligence findings to senior executives to gain commitment and approve investments
    • Led negotiations team and executed agreement for a worldwide exclusive license of a cancer biomarker, which represented the first major milestone in the execution of the molecular diagnostics strategy. Agreement allowed minimum risk to Novartis, thus gaining necessary approval from senior leadership
    • Chief liaison and negotiator with a large partner, Ortho Clinical Diagnostics

  • VP, Business Development & Strategy

    Chiron Corporation

    (Biotechnology industry)

    2000 — 2006 (6 years)

    Played a leading role in supporting the worldwide blood testing business, leading business development and strategy activities. Conducted due diligences on business opportunities, merger and acquisition candidates, partnering candidates.
    • Led several cross-function strategy teams to identify technologies, markets and potential acquisition targets to expand Chiron’s blood banking business
    • Led negotiations team and executed agreement for a collaboration with a small biotech company specializing in creating universal blood that expanded Chiron’s business beyond testing. Quickly assessed potential competitive advantage and company needs to create and execute an optimal agreement. Key liaison with this strategic partner, holding an observation seat on Board
    • Led negotiations and executed acquisition of a small biotech company allowing Chiron to solidify its position in the prion field (key strategic imperative and large potential market)
    • Led strategic marketing activities, including a world-wide market expansion strategy to prioritize target markets, obtaining buy-in of process and results from senior leaders; and a cost effectiveness analysis for the lead product to assist in gaining reimbursement

  • Director of Performance Improvement

    Kaiser Permanente

    (Non-Profit; Hospital & Health Care industry)

    1994 — 2000 (6 years)

    Director of Performance Improvement (The Permanente Federation)
    National consultant for key performance improvement goals of the Permanente Medical Groups (PMGs), with total of 10,000 physicians.
    • Assessed PMG performance and developed action plans for needed improvements, with particular focus on HEDIS scores and member/patient satisfaction
    • Acted as liaison with the Pharmacy groups throughout the country, including development of a health economics-based protocol for the use of COX-2 inhibitors

    Director of Medical Group Affairs (The Permanente Federation)
    Developed, managed and implemented national policies impacting The Permanente Federation and its partnership with Kaiser Foundation Health Plan.
    • Assisted in development of Kaiser Permanente national strategy and principles guiding the financial relationships between the PMGs and the local Health Plans
    • As the second employee of the Federation, played the role of CFO, head of Human Resources and Medical Group liaison for the first 6-9 months. Used communication, planning and management skills to build functioning organization in three months

    Assistant to the Associate Executive Director (The Permanente Medical Group)
    • Assisted in development and creation of The Permanente Federation, focusing on legal, financial and operational issues
    • Served as staff to the National Partnership Agreement that created the first formal agreement between the PMGs and the Health Plan at the national level, focusing on a national decision-making process

    Senior Consultant, Kaiser Foundation Health Plan, Northern California Region
