Anthony Rotolo’s Summary

I view design as not merely a creative endeavor but a business differentiator, the factor that allows companies to stake out and hold unique market positions longer.

Experienced design and e-learning manager, media and content producer, who has crafted solutions for the publishing and e-learning industries.

I have made a career of designing customer-centric solutions by uncovering the unique needs of clients. As an organizational leader, I foster a collaborative ethic that brings out the best in teams, aligning them toward a unified product vision.

Highlights:
Built in-house creative department for elearning startup, and developed portfolio of high-quality interactive learning products that generated continuous, large follow-on contracts. The success of any organization lies in its people--the true "capital investment." As supervising department head, I attracted, developed, and retained the best talent, fostering unusually high organizational stability and knowledge retention.

Produced the print and audio release of "Who Goes There?" (the story that formed the basis of THE THING films) for RocketRideBooks.com, including related podcast, Masters of Scifi and Horror. The new edition features an Introduction and Screen Treatment by William F. Nolan (Logan's Run author).

Produced and directed the documentary, Army Nurses of World War I, for the U.S. Army's Heritage Center, currently airing on PBS stations, and available as DVD and companion book at http://www.armynursesmovie.com/trailer.html.

Host of the popular podcast, Design Guy, available in iTunes as free subscription. Recent editorial selection by Apple, in their "Design Culture" featured picks at the iTunes Store. The show has garnered many votes and reviews from its user community, maintains growing subscriber-base, and recently enjoyed its 100,000th download.

Anthony Rotolo’s Specialties:

Leadership, management, design (experience, usability, interaction), book design and packaging, writing, new media, flash animation, branding and identity, marketing, sales.
