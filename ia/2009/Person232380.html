Thomas Clifford’s Summary

Uh-oh!

You've just been commissioned to produce a short film for your organization. A "who-we-are" video. Or a "diversity" initiative film.

No doubt, you probably have butterflies about hiring someone.

I totally understand.

I’m Thomas Clifford.

I’m the filmmaker organizations trust to capture and communicate the essence of what they do.

Take a second to read my recommendations. You'll see how much others have enjoyed working with me to achieve the results they were looking for.

I believe remarkable organizations deserve remarkable video stories. Who is telling yours?

Sometimes the message is the medium. And, in the right hands, nothing makes a statement better than film.

I'll breath life into your company's story in an authentic, reliable, and memorable way.

For 24 years, Fortune 500’s to national nonprofits commission my compelling, engaging short films to capture their messages in an emotional and dynamic way.

I love assignments where the result is not already pre-determined but rather the organization is looking for help solving a problem.

Remember, customers don’t buy products. They buy stories about who you are and what you stand for.

That's why I'm passionate about capturing your message through your best storytellers--your employees.

►Do you want to bring your "brand to life?"
►Need to tell your story in a clear, authentic and remarkable way?
►Wish you could bring your organization’s brand story to life and make it jump off the screen and into your customers' heart?

That's what I do.

Fortune 100's to non-profits depend on my films to:

► Recruit volunteers
► Generate sales leads
► Inspire & empower employees
► Teach new ideas
► Share corporate culture & values
► Orientate new-hires
► Dispel myths
► Create virtual company tours

Contact me---if you have questions or know someone who would prefer something "remarkable" over the same old, boring song and dance.

    "The Men Who Brought the Dawn" was a one-hour, world-wide documentary featuring the surviving airmen who dropped the two atomic bombs on Japan.

    "Enola Gay" is a shorter version of "The Men Who Brought the Dawn" and was created specifically for the Smithsonian Institution's 50th Anniversay commemorating the end of WWII.

    Both films are now a part of the Smithsonian Institution's permanent archive library collection.
