Christine Tran’s Summary

After graduating from UC Berkeley at the height of the tech boom, I did what any new graduate would do: Moved overseas to teach English in Vietnam! The year I spent teaching at a local university in the mountainous regions of Vietnam turned out to be one of the most transformative periods of my life. After returning to the US, I continued working with underserved communities as an organizer for a Bay Area-wide youth program and researcher/writer for a community publication on the state of Asian Americans.

Vietnam kept calling, and I soon returned to the organization that first enabled me to volunteer in Vietnam. For four years, I was responsible for facilitating exchange between the US and Vietnam through my role at Volunteers in Asia. There, I recruited, hired and trained volunteers, cultivated an alumni/donor base, and frequently visited Vietnam to build relationships with Vietnamese partners.

Finally, I returned to Vietnam full-time again in 2007 to pursue a US accredited MBA program through the University of Hawaii, and transition to the private sector. While managing a young team of content developers and a client relationship with one of the fastest growing internet retailers in the US, I became increasingly excited about shifting attitudes and behaviors online in the US and Asia. I wrote my final thesis on online customer engagement in Vietnam, researching US models and interviewing Vietnam's early adopters, entrepreneurs, VCs, and brand managers.

I recently returned to the Bay Area and love the fact that I now live in Oakland (rather than SF) and get paid to research customer strategy and emerging technology with Jeremiah Owyang at Altimeter Group.

In my free time, I blog, work on a couple websites, volunteer at tech conferences, bike, and actively seek out the best bowl of pho in Oakland.

Read my BusinessWeek article to learn more me: http://bit.ly/yHhtO.
