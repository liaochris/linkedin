Erica Donnis’s Summary

Erica Donnis is an independent historian and museum consultant with expertise in curatorial, collections management, and visitor service projects, including: research and interpretive writing for exhibitions and publications; development of interpretive programs and furnishings plans for historic sites; project management for interior restoration projects; collections policy development; assessments of collections management needs; management of visitor services systems; visitor surveys; and grant writing projects.

Selected Publications:
The History of Shelburne Farms: A Changing Landscape, An Evolving Vision (Shelburne Farms and Vermont Historical Society, forthcoming, 2010)

"Authenticity? It Means Everything," History News 63 (Autumn 2008): 18-23 (co-author, with Susan Wilkening).

"Puzzling Together the Wallpaper Pieces," Old House Journal 34 (January/February 2006): 60-65.

"Pan of Rohallion," Antiques and Fine Art 3 (Autumn 2002): 196-197.

  • Curator of Collections

    The Mount

    (Museums and Institutions industry)

    June 2002 — May 2006 (4 years)

    Erica worked as full-time Curator of Collections from 2002 to 2004, and then full-time Consulting Curator of Collections from 2004 to 2006. Her accomplishments include:
    • As first professional curator on staff, establishing museum practices for collections management, exhibitions, and interpretation
    • Managing collections of 3,000 objects and 250 linear feet of archival material
    • Writing the museum’s first collections policy and housekeeping manual
    • Writing and managing grants for restoration projects totaling $225,500
    • Managing interior restoration projects, including paint analysis, wallpaper reproductions, and the cquisition and fabrication of furnishings, draperies, and floor coverings.
    • Researching and writing detailed furnishings plans for three significant rooms
    • Overseeing registrarial activities related to acquisition of Edith Wharton’s 2,700-volume library from a book dealer in England
    • Managing interpretive programs and supervising up to 20 seasonal staff and interns
    • Managing a major loan exhibition from the Museum of the City of New York

    A National Historic Landmark, The Mount was designed by American author Edith Wharton as a country residence. It is located in Lenox, Massachusetts.

  • Curator of Collections

    Shelburne Farms

    (Museums and Institutions industry)

    August 1998 — May 2002 (3 years 10 months)

    Erica's accomplishments at Shelburne Farms include:

    • As first professional curator on staff, establishing museum practices for collections management and interpretation
    • Managing collections of 15,000 objects and 500 linear feet of archival material
    • Overseeing several major conservation projects benefitting the collections
    • Writing and managing grant projects totaling $122,000
    • Writing a conservation plan encompassing three-dimensional and archival collections, 27 historic buildings, and 1,400 acres of historic landscape
    • Developing a photographic services policy and managing rights & reproductions requests
    • Managing internal and external research inquiries and assisting on-site researchers
    • Co-managing interpretation programs and training a large volunteer corps
    • Overseeing an internship program focusing on collections management projects.

    Shelburne Farms is a 1,400-acre National Historic Landmark and environmental education center in Shelburne, Vermont.
