Bob Lenthart’s Summary

Twitter: http://twitter.com/blenthart
email: blenthart@derekhartent.com

Dragons. What goes through your mind when you hear the word?

Sir Thaddeus Osbert is a 1,500-year-old dragon who resides in Cornwall, UK. He's not your typical dragon in any way, for he has been meddling in human affairs for many years and has a sweet-tooth beyond compare. Yet now it's 1940 and the Germans threaten England with invasion and Thaddeus is charged with looking after one very special teenage boy. Gavin Kane has no idea why he's so bloody important, only that a certain dragon has chosen him to make a difference in the outcome of WWII.

This is the story behind the Dragon's Secrets books series, which includes 6 novels. We are currently designing and developing a full line of products to compliment the historical fantasy novels. This includes, but is not limited to, screenplays, action figures, video games, graphic novels, comic books, audio books, plush toys and a myriad of other related products.

Yet Derek Hart Entertainment isn't limited to just dragons and fantasy. We also have 15 adult-themed action & adventure novels going through the same process, with high concept screenplays written and PC games scripted, awaiting coding and testing.

As a start-up entertainment company, we decided to go a different route, by using our initial capital injection to create all the material in advance. Now poised with an impressive lineup, we are seeking capital investment to spring us loose. It's not your typical price tag either, because most of the groundwork is done. On top of that, we've created a very unique 24-hour system of design, so our games can hit the QA process faster than traditional houses.

Needless to say, we're looking for the right partner, with equity included and earnings achievable in a surprisingly short window. The network is in place. Anyone interested in adopting a grumpy, sugar-fixated old dragon?

Bob Lenthart’s Education

  • Roosevelt University

    1978 — 1980

    When I finally settled down to get an education, Roosevelt in Chicago was an excellent choice. In the heart of the city, it was fun just going to class. The teachers were superb and I learned more there, than any other formal education up to that point. My fellow students were as diverse as the city itself and as tolerant of differences as humanly possible. I strongly recommend this school for anyone who wants both a quality education and classmates who are real people.

  • University of Utah

    English - Creative Writing 1976 — 1978

    The University of Utah was a fantastic experience, but more from the people I met, than the formal education I was supposed to get. After all, it was Salt Lake City and so close to great skiing, incredible backpacking, and awesome pizza. I saw David Bowie live, met one of my best friends - Dan Hubbs, and wrote my first novel -Tracks of the Predator. It was my first taste of environmental activism, a Cheyenne warrior with AIM and other wildly unsocial behavior. It was grand!

  • Oakton Community College

    1973 — 1974

    Oakton was a great place to go to college, while I was trying to decide if I wanted to go to college. Rinda West, my Freshman English professor, was a fantastic teacher and she deserves the lion's share of credit for setting me on the journey of an author. I went to Oakton for one year, did very well, but then went off in search of other adventures. It would be a few years before I went back to college, but my memories of Oakton will always be very positive!

    1970 — 1973
