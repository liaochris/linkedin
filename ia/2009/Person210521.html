Saul Shapiro’s Summary

Senior level business builder and strategic planner with unique blend of skills creating, promoting and implementing technology and policy. Sophisticated acumen in business, engineering and public policy. Deep experience in private and public sector, including start-up, multi-national, city and federal organizations. Strengths are communicating across technical and business issues to keep executives, engineers and entrepreneurs engaged; to develop relationships and alliances based on cooperation and “getting to yes.” A highly creative problem solver adept at managing others while keeping a close eye on customer’s needs.

Saul Shapiro’s Experience

  • President

    Metropolitan Television Alliance (MTVA)

    (Broadcast Media industry)

    2008 — Present (1 year )

    The Metropolitan Television Alliance (MTVA) is a coalition of eleven broadcast television stations serving the New York Metropolitan Area.

    The President coordinates efforts of MTVA member stations to shift from analog to digital signal transmission, as required by federal law, and to oversee development of new transmission facilities to replace the shared transmission tower which was destroyed at the World Trade Center.

  • Vice President, Business Development, Media, Technology and Sustainability Sectors

    NYC Economic Development Corp.

    (Government Administration industry)

    2003 — 2007 (4 years )

    As Vice President, Business Development, Media & Technology at EDC I encouraged the attraction, investment and expansion of major companies within my sectors in New York City. I was responsible for managing relationships and serving as an advocate between the city and companies in Media and Technology. The sector includes broadcast and cable television, radio, online services, newspapers, magazines and telecommunications. In 2006, I added Sustainable ("green") Technologies to my portfolio. This entailled developing a marketing outreach to increase global awareness of the City's green profile.
