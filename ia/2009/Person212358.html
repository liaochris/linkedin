    (Privately Held; 10,001 or more employees; Writing and Editing industry)

    June 2002 — June 2005 (3 years 1 month)

    • Macworld Plus is a monthly newsstand computer magazine.

    • My brief was to produce a top-quality publication as popular with advertisers as with readers, and within a closely monitored budget.

    • In the space of 15 months I reduced the per-issue spend from over £5,000 to under £1,000, while maintaining readership in an ever-more competitive sector. This I achieved through negotiating free content from publishers of quality IT books; authoring as much material myself as possible; negotiating contra-deals with contributors (involving free online and print advertising); and by assuming control of our cover-mounted CDs from an out-of-house agency.
