Gabriel London’s Summary

For the better part of a decade, I have been producing and directing documentary films. Whether an independent project or a client seeded film, my documentaries often deal with political issues, and topics have ranged from a film on climate change to one on prison conditions in the United States. Over the years, these films have played in varied venues, from film festivals to MTV to the US Congress.

In 2003, I started a true story and documentary film production company, Found Object Films, to expand into client seeded films that could range in subject matter from political to promotional. Projects include a Rock the Vote documentary on youth voting with Drew Barrymore's company, Flower Films, a film with Live Earth for the 07.07.07 concert series and an autobiographical film with Snoop Dogg on the streets-to-prison cycle that aired on Spike TV.

In the future, I aim to collaborate with like minded individuals interested in using documentary media to affect audiences personally and politically. As with my first film on prison conditions, I would like to see documentary become an integral part of major progressive policy pushes, and in particular would like to continue working in the 'green' sphere.
