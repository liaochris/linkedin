Lupita Figueiredo’s Summary

GOALS
To bring my many years of experience in media and community relations to companies and organizations ready to act, move, and achieve.

To create an environment of openness to diversity and multiculturalism not only in terms of race & language but also in terms of age, gender and other groups.

To work either as consultant or employee.

To work internationally and experience other cultures. Special interest in Europe.

PROFESSIONAL EXPERIENCE
Public relations bilingual professional with ample experience and knowledge about media and community affairs. Excellent at creating, developing and maintaining relationships with community groups, business, governmental entities, educational organizations, and media corporations.

Seasoned journalist who has worked in, and knows how to best use television, radio and print, social media and other new media strategies as well as ethnic media to spread a message, promote a cause and create a buzz.

Very creative, proactive, problem solver, risk taker, persistent, disciplined, organized, great communicator and excellent people skills.

Lupita Figueiredo’s Experience

  • Principal

    Capacity Media & PR

    (Public Relations and Communications industry)

    June 2007 — Present (2 years 7 months)

    Capacity PR specializes in media and community affairs for U.S. and international markets, with an emphasis on the Spanish-language market, beyond language.
    Partial list of clients: Marin County Registrar of Voters' Office, Lindsay Wildlife Museum, Health Access, Alameda County Office of Education, Brian Rogers for School Board.

  • Legislative Aide

    San Francisco Board of Supervisors

    (Public Relations and Communications industry)

    2006 — 2007 (1 year )

    Managed press coverage with local, national, mainstream and ethnic media outlets, including bilingual press conferences and press releases. Interacted with diverse community groups, wrote legislative bills. Worked directly for one of the members of the San Francisco Board of Supervisors.

  • Media Trainer

    Capacity Consulting

    (Public Relations and Communications industry)

    March 2005 — May 2006 (1 year 3 months)

    Developed media and community strategies for local businesses and non-profits, including newsletters, events and media workshops. At a critical time, helped revamped a local weekly newspaper.

  • Assistant

    Equal Access Office

    (Government Relations industry)

    August 2004 — March 2005 (8 months)

    Managed a diverse group of college students who volunteered as interpreters for an Equal Access to Services project, from monitoring daily tasks to working one on one and tracking their experiences. I translated from English to Spanish when needed and helped to strengthen relationships with reporters and other members of the ethnic and mainstream media.

  • News Reporter

    Telemundo

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; GE; Broadcast Media industry)

    1998 — 2004 (6 years )

    Oakland News Bureau Chief and Bilingual General Assignment Reporter. Generated story ideas involving research, interviewing, field producing, writing and editing. Live presentation of news stories. Live Breaking News when needed. Developed a strong network of sources of information from grassroots activists to key leaders from every sector. Made public appearances on behalf of the Station, created strong alliances for community events. Engaged with key governmental players in the State Capitol by covering news from Sacramento once a week.

  • News Reporter

    KDTV14 Univision

    (Public Relations and Communications industry)

    1996 — 1997 (1 year )

    Provided news coverage in the San Francisco Bay Area and surrounding counties such as Napa, Solano, Pajaro and other counties, successfully enterprising community, education and immigration stories. Worked on special series and segments about employment and small businesses.

  • Staff Writer

    La Opinion

    (Privately Held; 201-500 employees; Newspapers industry)

    1992 — 1994 (2 years )

    Held several positions starting with being a free lance writer for the entertainment section and moving to advertising supplements editor. As editor, I was in charge of the Employment, Travel, Automobile and Real Estate special supplements and helped produced the Fiestas Patrias and other Hispanic Traditions sections. Later I became Staff Writer for the Entertainment Section and as such I interviewed Latino stars and reviewed concerts, plays and other spectacles.
