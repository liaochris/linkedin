Ludo Welffens’s Summary

After 29 years with UNICEF, I took early retirement on September 1st 2003. It had become important for me to look from a distance at how we sculpt our mandate in a rapidly changing world; at how abstract “log-frames” interface with priorities defined and changed in towns and villages, in good times and bad. I wanted to reflect on the validity of our programming-by-objectives approaches and paradigms, to ask the hard questions on issues of methodology as it were – and from all angles.

I decided to take time to internalize my unique multi-faceted exposure to how people live and survive, how they struggle with women and child rights, with systems-constraints that slow the achievement of MDGs, with how governance’s aims can turn fragile fast. Since 2003, I have learned to see my career accomplishments as a privilege that comes with new responsibilities. I am committed to be available for new assignments in Africa and Asia, along the following two lines:

• to share experience with junior professionals, at national and local levels, especially with public health actors in both urban and rural areas, with local government officers and non-governmental and private sector organizations, and help them initiate sociologically valid situation analyses – with a priority-focus on the survival genius of women in very difficult circumstances, and on sustainable basic services for children,

• and to encourage longitudinal participant observation in research on grass roots’ social change dynamics, with a particular interest in further studying – interactively with a new generation of women and men - the causes of societal violence and failed development, and how these aggress values and norms, and impact on families and communities.

Ludo Welffens’s Specialties:

(1) Team-building in complex socio-political environments

(2) Comprehensive understanding of how development concepts, - including for example behavior change communication programs - evolve and shape policy, and how these can be evaluated throughout their implementation in a participatory manner

(3) Contributing to build the new typologies of situations that result from the fact that the UNO is growing increasingly weaker.

(4) Contribute to social change theory.
