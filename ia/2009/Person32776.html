Bret Bernhoft’s Summary

Just a few years ago, Bret Bernhoft, a native of Vancouver, Washington, was just another energetic and in-tune communications student working towards completing his MBA. However, in a very short time, his life was completely changed in a radical way. As he embarked on his career, Bret had difficulty bypassing the stereotypes about his generation that were prevalent in the workplace which became barriers to employment. He found that there were rampant assumptions that anyone who is a Gen Y is lazy and demanding, when in fact, statistics and reality showed that Gen Y members are tireless workers who don’t see a separation between work life and social life. This, in turn, has placed the ‘old school’ of distinct lines of office authority and structure into a tailspin.



As Bret looked around and examined this issue even more closely, it was clear that workplace synergy has shifted to a new dynamic, and companies are struggling with the generational divide in a way that had never been experienced before. He also found that there was a complete vacuum of resources and services addressing the widely divergent communication patterns and work dynamics between Generation Y and other age levels within office structures. Bret realized at this moment that his generational perspective could be an asset, not an obstacle. And InsYght Consulting was born!



Today, Bret is busy providing consulting work to corporations on inter-office generational issues and develops social media marketing plans to large businesses and non-profits through InsYght Consulting on these high-demand topics. He is also currently engaged in the Portland Gen Y project, which is an effort to provide a snapshot into Gen Y life in Portland, Oregon, by providing insights and candid interviews into attitudes and perspective on life/work balances of Gen Y members.
