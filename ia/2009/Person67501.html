Michael Mantell, Ph.D.’s Summary

Author, "Don't Sweat The Small Stuff: P.S. It's All Small Stuff" in 1988 ("Best Audio" Publisher's Weekly)
Author, "Ticking Bombs: Defusing Violence in the Workplace"
"Top 25 Business Speakers" Successful Meetings Magazine
Spokesperson, Gov't of Bermuda Dept. of Tourism advertising campaign

The comprehensive services clinical and corporate psychologist Dr. Michael Mantell has brought to the corporate world for more than three decades have been charted to provide management with the skills necessary to most effectively understand, organize and direct others.
He has concentrated his efforts on efficient methods to assist companies and employees arrive, and remain, at the forefront of unrivaled healthy management-employee relations.
Since completing his doctorate at the University of Pennsylvania, he served as Chief Psychologist for Children’s Hospital and Health Center of San Diego and created the nationally recognized Psychological Services program of the San Diego Police Department. He has been a member of the faculty of UCSD Medical School, Department of Psychiatry, and has taught at the School of Public Administration at USC.
Dr. Mantell has authored thousands of articles on stress and trauma, employee-relations, and mental health issues for professional and lay publications and has appeared before mass audiences nationwide.
His widely regarded public relations and communications skills led to his serving as national spokesperson for the Government of Bermuda’s Department of Tourism in an ad campaign.
His insights have provided the impetus for segments on “Oprah,” “Nightline,” “Larry King,” "Today" and "Good Morning America."
He is regarded by national media as a definitive spokesperson on the issues of healthy employee/management relations and the prevention of workplace violence and organizational administration, leadership and successful corporate and organizational management.
