Benjamin Kinney’s Summary

1----->As a Quality/Process Engineer I have successfully used quality tools, and solved a wide variety of manufacturing issues in the medical device industry. Along with that I used statistical manufacturing control methods such as SPC, problem solving tools such as DMAIC, TRIZ, CAPA and product enhancement/development methods like DOE's and associated techniques (like VCA, hypothesis testing, and descriptive stats).

2----->I am also an experienced Analytical Chemist with a broad base of experience with Chromatography (LC, GC, GPC, SEC, TLC), Optical spectroscopy (UV-Vis, FTIR, Focal planer FTIR, AA), ICP, thermal analysis (TGA, DMA, DSC, TMA), bulk mechanical analysis (i.e. Instron methods), and Mass spectrometry (ESI, APCI, on MS, MS-MS, TOF, Q-TOF and EI using GC at the interface). I have also used techniques like ELSD and CLND in line with LC-MS.

For each of the above systems I have developed methods, developed equipment design modifications for special applications, and worked with their liquid handling systems along with Gilson and LEAP liquid handlers.

I have used equipment spanning, HP, Agilent, Waters, Shimadzu, PE-Sciex, PE, Applied Biosystems, TA Instruments, Micromass, Mettler and these manufacturer's associated system software.

I also have an MBA that assists me in gaining a wider view point on the impact of my work, has assisted me with formal methods in making teams more effecitive and focused, and its relation to company strategy, finances and internal/external performance measures.
