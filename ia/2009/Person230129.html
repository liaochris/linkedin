Ted Donnelly’s Summary

Sales and marketing executive and “master locksmith” in the high technology industry. Responsible for dialing-in the combination lock at IBM which broke Intel’s server monopoly and netted AMD approximately $1 billion in cumulative revenue over the last decade.

In addition to enabling and delivering industry-leading server technology to the marketplace, I was successful at picking the lock on “high value solutions”. As the Director of Linux Alliances at AMD, I was able to rally a 21-member cross functional team who successfully delivered differentiated hardware/software solution bundles to targeted industries and vertical segments, including the creation and installation of one of the world’s most powerful supercomputers at Los Alamos National Labs.
