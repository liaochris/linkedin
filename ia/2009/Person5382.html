Aja Raden’s Summary

Aja Raden is currently finishing her fourth year as a senior designer for Los Angeles based fine jewelry firm Tacori. At Tacori, she is charged with designing the fine jewelry line. She was responsible for the first run of original designs for Tacori’s first ever (and famously successful) QVC line. She also was at one poin charged with redesigning the bridal line, and is a continued source of designs for engagement rings and wedding bands. For the last four years she has helped redefine the entire scope of Tacori’s market with the introduction of a fashion jewelry line. The signature Champaign Sunset pendant has been seen on hollywood notables, in numerous magazines, and is the center piece of the companies new advertising campaign.

Her experiences in the rarified air of Estate Jewelry took place at the legendary House of Kahn in Chicago. There, her responsibilities were exceptionally broad...appraisals, authentications, documentation and pricing of pieces, the creation of large scale, elaborate auctions, as well as the grading and identification of gems and precious metals. These duties were all facilitated by her extensive knowledge of various eras, styles, and design techniques appropriate to their time period. The time spent in Chicago led to a liaison with renowned Beverly Hills estate jewelers, Kazanjian Brothers. After a private meeting with Michael Kazanjian, they requested a signature piece for their popular Jewels for Charities project.

Raden’s next opportunity was working exclusively with important stones under the tutelage of the internationally influential Leonard Himelsein. Raden familiarized herself with the world of global diamond trade, while assisting Mr. Himelsein in grading and appraising his vast jewelry inventory. Various private commissions for celebrities and other exclusive clients resulted in her own private lines, 100 CTS, Stars, and the inimitable Peace Line.
