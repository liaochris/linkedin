Gunther Sonnenfeld’s Summary

I specialize in social media marketing and interactive development. This means that I help build online communities, help create and distribute viral content, as well as develop tools that enhance consumer experiences across multiple platforms. My philosophy is simple: there is a distinct intersection where commerce and common interest meet. The primary goal, therefore, is to help brands communicate creatively and authentically with consumers so that messaging ultimately carries a higher purpose, and products can provide greater utility.

I’ve worked with a number of Fortune 500 brands including Kraft, Motorola, Safeway, Sony, Toyota, the NBA, The PGA TOUR and WB Music. My non-profit work includes initiatives for groups such as the NRDC, The Annenberg Foundation and the American Cancer Society.

I have helped architect software applications, search platforms, eCommerce platforms, ad targeting tools and social media platforms. My business skills include: creative development, client & agency relations, consultative sales, project & issue management, resource allocation and strategic partnerships.

I frequently consult investment banks, private equity firms and venture capital groups on new media and technology trends.

I am a contributor to leading industry publications such as MediaPost and iMedia Connection and a published author. My most recent work can be seen in The Project 100, a collaborative book featuring insights from various thought leaders in social media, led by Jeff Caswell, VP of Interactive Marketing for Danone of North America. Proceeds are being dedicated to the Susan G. Komen Foundation for cancer research.

Gunther Sonnenfeld’s Specialties:

Branding and identity, digital production, media negotiation, strategic alliances, web & software development, social media marketing.
