Kirsten Broadfoot’s Summary

I live an ethnographic life and am dedicated to the pursuit of new forms of working and workplaces as the world evolves through global flows of technology and immigration. I am particularly interested in diverse understandings of what constitutes 'good work' in everyday life. My current projects revolve around communication, health literacy and indigenous health; the uses of social media in community building and healthcare contexts, and postcolonial and diverse forms of organizing practices for humane workplaces.

My current professional goals are as follows:
- To construct, sustain and implement creative, care-full and culturally sensitive workplaces and work practices.
- To integrate my interests in post colonial forms of organizing and communicative practice, good work and communication in clinical contexts to focus on issues of concern in global health.

Kirsten Broadfoot’s Specialties:

Training and Development, Facilitation, Qualitative Research, Writing and Copyediting, Organizing Forms and Practices, Team Construction, Uses of Social/New Media on Organizing and Work Practices
