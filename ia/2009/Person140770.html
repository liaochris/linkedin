Rand Brenner’s Summary

Extensive business career in the entertainment industry; held executive positions at Saban Entertainment and Warner Bros Consumer Products. Negotiated numerous merchandise and promotional licensing agreements, many with Fortune 1000 companies including Hewlett Packard, Kellogg's, Bandai America, Hasbro, and others. Entertainment properties managed included Mighty Morphin Power Rangers (a $3 billion worldwide retail sales licensing phenomenon), the Batman movie (Tim Burton/Warner Bros production generating over $500 million in merchandise revenues), and the classic Looney Tunes(i.e. Bugs Bunny).

Advisory board member of several private companies. Business development advisor to WallStreet Research, an investment research firm with offices in New York and Los Angeles, specializing in covering emerging growth and middle market public companies on the OTCBB, NASDAQ and AMEX exchanges.

Public speaking and teaching experience at the university level. Guest lecturer at the CEO Space entrepreneurial program, a week long intensive business training for small to midsize business owners, entrepreneurs and individuals. Guest speaker at the Hong Kong FilmArt Cross Media and Cross Business Symposium. Former adjunct faculty member at the University of Redlands - taught undergraduate classes in marketing.

  • President & CEO

    Licensing Consulting Group

    (Privately Held; Myself Only; Consumer Goods industry)

    2006 — Present (3 years )

    Licensing Consulting Group is an intellectual property marketing firm that specializes in assisting consumer products and entertainment companies in leveraging their intellectual property assets. LCG works with property owners seeking to license out their intellectual assets, and with new and established companies seeking to expand their revenue opportunities through licensing. LCG assist its clients in developing and implementing a full spectrum of strategic marketing and licensing options:

    - A new company seeking to build its distribution channels or license its proprietary product to a third party manufacturer

    - An established entertainment property seeking to capitalize on its market recognition by developing revenue sources through the licensing of consumer products and services.
