Brennen Lukas’s Summary

If you took an avant-garde poet with a masters degree and combined him with a professional copywriter, a social media guru, a blogger, a former journalist, an interactive producer and an SEO aficionado, then crammed all that into a rail-thin managing editor’s body—well you would have one crazy dude. A crazy dude who knows how to get it done. That’s me.

I'm a strong believer in the incredible power of online social media as a marketing tool, and I'm looking for opportunities to apply my skills to cutting-edge integrated campaigns. If you need your corporate blog to communicate better and reach more customers, I can probably help you out. Ready to dip your toe into MySpace, Facebook and Twitter? Don't know what Twitter is? I can help you out there too.

I've been doing this stuff for almost ten years. Let's take it to the next level.

Brennen Lukas’s Experience

  • Vice President of Marketing and Advertising

    Whipped Bakeshop

    (Food & Beverages industry)

    January 2009 — Present (2 months)

    Whipped Bakeshop is a start-up custom bakery in Philadelphia. I have employed a broad array of social media marketing tactics to make the new business a success even before it officially opens its doors.

    Thanks to innovative products and targeted online marketing, Whipped Bakeshop has appeared in dozens of blogs, two regional magazines, Fox television, the Philadelphia Inquirer and the cover of the Philadelphia City Paper.

  • Managing Editor

    Greater Philadelphia Tourism Marketing Corporation

    (Non-Profit; Leisure, Travel & Tourism industry)

    October 2006 — December 2008 (2 years 3 months)

    I manage uwishunu.com, the centerpiece blog of a $5M campaign to market the city of Philadelphia from an insider's point of view. In addition to writing and editing for the site, I direct outside agencies in web design and content creation. I also provide analytics, social media and SEO expertise.

  • Quality Assurance Editor

    Cadient Group

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Marketing and Advertising industry)

    February 2006 — September 2006 (8 months)

    During my time at Cadient, I edited numerous consumer online projects for leading pharmaceutical brands, including Seroquel, Doxil, Listerine, Lyrica, Nexium, Diovan and many more. I also worked with ZLB Behring to help them edit their worldwide corporate eStyle guide.

  • Web Copywriter

    Free Library of Philadelphia

    (Government Agency; 201-500 employees; Libraries industry)

    July 2005 — September 2006 (1 year 3 months)

    The Free Library redesigned their heavily visited website in September of 2005, adding features like a unified search tool and downloadable audiobooks along with a completely new database-driven user interface. I was contracted to write customer-friendly copy that would make the new features attractive and intuitive to users accustomed to the old site. In the process, I either wrote or rewrote a great deal of the content and navigational copy, making it bright and clear.

  • Associate Producer: Rich Media & Design

    USA TODAY

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; GCI; Newspapers industry)

    July 1999 — August 2003 (4 years 2 months)

    Developed content for USA TODAY’s premier online graphics. This involved managing interactive media projects, including audio, video, animation and photography. In an earlier position, I managed usatoday.com's high-traffic homepage, writing headlines and directing story selection with an emphasis on breaking news.
