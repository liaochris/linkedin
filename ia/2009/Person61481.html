Denise (Schmidt) Olson’s Summary

My love for photography started out in high school while a member and Editor of the yearbook staff. While obtaining my Masters Degree in Bacterial Molecular Genetics, I became involved in film photography and darkroom techniques while doing electron microscopic imaging. My career proceeded into Cytogenetics where I analyzed prenatal and other genetic cases for abnomalies. There, digital imaging was utilized to capture cellular images of chromosomes for detailed analysis and medical records. A few years later, I joined a start-up company, called Cellomics, where I was trained and became an expert in the field of digital fluorescent imaging and proprietary digital imaging software programs. My last corporate job, before plunging into the role of Manager, Home Operations and Child Development, a.k.a. motherhood, I worked for Applied Biosystems as an Applications Scientist for the Product Development department supporting sales & marketing, service, R&D and customers on the genetic analyzers (you know, those instruments used in the Human Genome Project). During all my adventures, I was taking pictures preserving my own memories.

Since 2005, I have become a stay home mom, and began taking my digital photography hobby seriously. I am a member of the Professional Photographers of America, Inc. (www.ppa.com), and the National Association of Photoshop Professionals (www.photoshopuser.com) where I can obtain further training and education on current photography and digital imaging techniques.

My photography style is a casual and journalistic composition with an artistic flair. I like to add creativity to ordinary photos; at the same time, capture moments that tells a story to document your memories for a lifetime.
