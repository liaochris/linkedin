Ross Arntson’s Summary

As an entrepreneur all my life, I have devoted myself to personal success and that of those around me. I’ve owned several of my own businesses taking them to the top of their field. I have amassed an incredible repertoire of achievement, including working around the world and with many charities, all of which are experiences I would like to share.

Self taught in the art of entrepreneurship, I have shared the stage with some of the most accomplished speakers in the world. But even so, I still consider myself a student of business and persevere to continue to be the best in my field and never give in to, what I call, “the disease of complacency”.

I have a number of projects that I am involved with and will share details of each throughout my blog. Currently, I am very excited about finishing my book “Rebuilt with Purpose” which will be the first in the “Rebuilt” series. Its website www.rebuiltwithpurpose.com is coming soon.

I’m also focusing my energies at the protection of the world ecological systems, I have teamed up with a group of incredibly talented inventors, scientists and businessmen to offer a phenomenal product to the cities of this nation and others and totally revamp their water treatment systems and completely eliminate the expulsion of toxins into the environment and create a renewable source for bio-fuels and more (www.biofueldynamics.com).

As an active philanthropist and member of Olive Crest, Habit for Humanity, The Red Cross, Greenpeace and many other similar organizations, I have set my targets to give back and help those around me. My greatest personal achievement is the directing of “The Mercer Foundation” which is dedicated to the betterment of the planet through ecological conservation and education. This came about through my activities in marketing properties in Costa Rica and Mexico (www.paradisesbestproperty.com) something I still continue because it is an area everyone should experience at least once.

    Bio Fuel Dynamics

    (Environmental Services industry)

    January 2009 — Present (8 months)

    In short; the beauty of the process we provide is that we capture everything that goes into a city's sewage treatment plant, including all the toxic gases, and convert them into many forms of reusable bio fuels and resources. For example; solid mass is converted to both bio-diesel and fertilizer, methane gases are captured and used to power a generator for electricity and remaining mass is converted to reusable lubricants. After our system has completed all the conversions, the only thing that is released from the facility is clean water.

    What do we do with all these products? We sell them on the open market, sharing the profit with the city, thus creating a revenue stream while at the same time absorbing their operating costs for the treatment plant. Oh and by the way, the facility is built for free using federal bonds.

    So to put it mildly, their profits really are in the toilet!
