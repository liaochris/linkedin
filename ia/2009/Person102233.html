Jim Gray’s Summary

Gray most recently served as Director of Marketing for the engineering firm, Oliver-Rhoads & Associates. He was responsible for Federal and State marketing, public relations, and government relations for the firm.

While pursuing his undergraduate degree, Gray worked for the Tennessee Democratic Party under the direction of current U.S. Rep. Bart Gordon of Tennessee.

Following graduation, Gray began his career in Washington, DC, as a Legislative Assistant for U.S. Rep. Jim Cooper of Tennessee.

He later was named Director of Nuclear Employee Communications for TVA and spent five of his seven years with TVA as a member of the Washington Government Relations staff. He served on two Vice-Presidential Task Forces and was selected by both Republican and Democratic Administrations to ensure the successful Senate confirmation of Presidential Appointees to the TVA Board.

He returned to his hometown of Fayetteville, TN, to head economic development efforts where from 1994-98, 14 new and existing industries invested more than $250 million under his leadership from. Gray was also a charter member of the Fayetteville – Lincoln County Regional Airport Authority. For these economic development efforts, Gray was recognized by the Tennessee General Assembly. He also served as Director of Marketing for Huntsville, AL's successful 1995 BRAC round.

Gray later served in a similar economic development capacity in Enid, OK, where his marketing efforts gained him recognition for landing one of the top 100 economic development projects in the southeastern United States, by “Southern Business and Development Magazine." While in Enid, Gray was appointed a Trustee of the Vance AFB Trust Authority.

Gray's management experience also includes serving as Vice President of Marketing Operations for Synectics, a brand consulting firm, and in sales and planning for the world's largest retailer, the Wal-Mart Corporation.
