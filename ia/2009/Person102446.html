James Fazio’s Summary

I specialize in intellectual property and business litigation.

I represent public, private and emerging-growth companies alike in resolving intellectual property, patent and business disputes and have handled a wide variety of cases, including breach of contract, unfair competition, patent and trademark infringement, false advertising and other business tort cases. I have experience in a wide variety of technologies, including telecommunications, computer hardware/software, database management, web conferencing, wireless Internet access, injection molding, semiconductors, Internet business methods, biotechnology and pharmaceuticals.

As a former federal prosecutor with the United States Attorneys' Office in San Diego, I also have well over a dozen federal jury trials and appeals to my credit.

I am active in civic and pro bono matters. I am on the Board of Trustees of several non-profit organizations in San Diego and am also a member of the State Bar of California Executive Committee on Federal Courts. I have also been appointed by Judge Louisa Porter of the Southern District of California to represent indigent criminal defendants in pro bono appeals of federal convictions and sentences to the 9th Circuit Court of Appeals.

I am also an active publisher and speaker in my field and have published or presented over a dozen articles on various legal articles in numerous periodicals, including IP Today, IP New Matter (published by the State Bar of California), IP Wire, The Federal Lawyer, California Lawyer, The FBA Newsletter, The National Law Journal, and the San Diego Daily Transcript.
