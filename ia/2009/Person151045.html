Marissa Lopez Donatt’s Summary

1987: Found photography to be my second profession creating wedding, corporate and family portraits, dance performance photography, commercial and fashion editorial.

1994: Graduated from Santa Monica College in Commercial Photography. Became a Pioneer Hispanic woman who created and taught photography classes in Spanish at Santa Monica College. Interviewed by Channel 54 & the media in photography education. The SMC catalog promoted the Spanish Photography classes being in the catalog cover page three consecutive years having more than 25 students per class.

1995: Through the county of Los Angeles created a program to teach photography to low income children receiving a certificate award from Miss. Gloria Molina of the first district for a successful program.

1996: Became a certified instructor at Santa Monica College given a special project to teach commercial photography to Hispanic teens from the Mexican American Opportunity Foundation. The program gave vocational certificates in photography to teens after completing the program.

1998: Created La Foto, Inc. A photography gallery, Center of photographic education, a Darkroom and Studio rental and a professional photography studio creating commercial and corporate portraits, dance and stage photography, Fashion Editorial Photographic Art with Landscapes and Artistic Wedding Imagery.

2008: La Foto, Inc celebrated it's 10th anniversary continuing to provide a home to those who love photography by encouraging, exhibiting photographers and students leaving an influence in photography. Many Hispanic students are professionals now, many are the pride and joy in visual arts communication. I thank each and everyone for supporting La Foto through the years. The love to teach photography in Spanish and English through out the community, has brought great satisfaction to my profession.
