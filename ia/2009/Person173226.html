Stuart Haining’s Summary

Despite wanting to work in Marketing, out of necessity I started my career in Banking, with a focus on commercial lending.

To get my career back on track I jumped to Barclaycard to concentrate on Market Research and Marketing. Here I worked on B2C, B2B and partner channels. My expertise (with a budget of circa �20m) becaming building brands using below-the-line (particularly Direct Marketing and Magazines) and CRM.

I have always been wary of technolohy so to "fight the fear" I jumped into the Internet whole heartedly in 1995 and worked on BarclaySquare, the worlds first online shopping mall. A move in 1998 to Barclays renowned Emerging Markets Lab - enabled me to set up the new Commercial Team working on leading edge projects such as encrypting MP3 music/DRM (long before iPods) and online/micro-payments (before PayPal).

The constraints of working in a conservative industry led to jumping ship in 2000 when I secured £1.8m Venture Capital investment to start Something4 - a unique incubator of new technology ideas. Our USP being a technology business run by Marketing/Brand people. The business grew through acquisition of ClickandBuild (acquired from Nat West Bank) in 2003, Cyrane Systems in 2005 and netcallidus SEO in 2007.

My personal goal was to retire at 45 and spend my twilight years investing in others businesses.....the technology bubble put me a little behind track but I was able to exit Something4 and sell my shares to my co-directors in 2008, enabling me to focus my time on learning new skills in areas like Search Engine Marketing.

What little spare time I have is spent with family and friends, plus a few Internet based hobby businesses!

Stuart Haining’s Specialties:

Using new channels to create leading-edge business models and new brands. My current focus is on leveraging the power of the Internet to deliver massive growth.
