    Jimmy Turrell

    (Design industry)

    July 2000 — Present (9 years 7 months)

    Jimmy Turrell is a graphic artist based in London. He studied at Liverpool School of Art and then Central St Martins. He combines a love of handmade collage, drawing, screen printing and painting alongside digital techniques.

    His clients to date include Nike, Intro, Channel 4, Saatchi and Saatchi, MTV, The Guardian, The New York Times, Universal Records, Capitol Records, XL Recordings, Lexus, Tokion Magazine, Dazed & Confused, The Prodigy, and Colette Paris.

    Recent commissions include all branding and art direction for The Guardian at Glastonbury 2009, a book cover with Pentagram that won Best in Book in Creative Review's Annual and a collaboration with Intro for Nike.

    Presently his work is inspired by Kulchur magazine, Gang Gang Dance, and Get Physical Records. A solo exhibition will follow later this year.

    Recent Commission:

    Glastonbury 2009 Commission

    Jimmy Turrell was commissioned by The Guardian to illustrate all their promotional material for Glastonbury 2009.

    The brief was entitled "Flyposter My Life" and was based around the concept of
    a person flypostering all of the music memories they'd ever had ( their favourite gigs, their first ever record, the record they first fell in love to etc).

    He also designed the interior and exterior of The Guardian Lounge. This included an exhibition of original framed artworks, the flypostering of all the stage panels and the creation of large 3d letters above the stage. My design assistant on this project was Marcos Villalba.

    Here's all the imagery with pics of the bands playing ( including Little Boots, White Lies, Ebony Bones, Golden Silvers, The Maccabees and Peter Bjorn and John)
