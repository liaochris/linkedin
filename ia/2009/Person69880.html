Edward ("Ed") Gibson’s Experience

  • Chief (cyber) Security Advisor

    Microsoft Ltd, United Kingdom

    (Public Company; MSFT; Computer Software industry)

    July 2005 — Present (4 years 2 months)

    Chief spokesperson for Microsoft Ltd UK on all cyber, hi-tech, internet security/ risk matters. Serves as the senior adviser to Microsoft’s customers, partners, government elites, and the public on how to best respond to the current security environment - from internal leakage of intellectual property to best practices for online cyber security - and how to improve security through Microsoft's solutions and services. One of the key skills Mr. Gibson brings to Microsoft is his ability to forge and maintain strategic alliances across public and private sector organisations in an international environment. He is the link between Microsoft and industry specialists, government and academia, law enforcement and commerce, facilitating the sharing of security knowledge between these disparate groups.

    Prior to joining Microsoft, Mr Gibson completed a 20-year career as a Special Agent of the Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI). He specialized in investigating economic espionage, complex money laundering, cyber fraud schemes, and intellectual property theft. 2000-2005: Assigned to the U.S. Embassy in London as the FBI's Assistant Legal Attaché in the UK and Ireland responsible for all FBI Hi-Tech, Internet Extortion and Blackmail, Cyber Terrorism, Infrastructure Protection, Intellectual Property, and Crimes against Children investigations.

    Before his appointment to the FBI, Mr Gibson served for 5-years as a lawyer in the General Counsel’s Office for a multi-national corporation based in the U.S. While assigned to the American Embassy he qualified as a Solicitor in England and Wales and completed a 2-year ‘computing’ program at Oxford University. He is a Certified Information Systems Security Professional (CISSP), Fellow of the British Computer Society, and serves on several technology association Advisory Boards and Steering Committees. He is a sought after speaker given his ability to bring current security problems to life and make IT personal.
