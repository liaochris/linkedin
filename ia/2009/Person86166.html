Ghazal Badiozamani’s Summary

Ghazal is a Policy Analyst at Hara Software, working to ensure that product offerings are in line with evolving regulatory and voluntary GHG reduction obligations. She is also an MBA Candidate at The Wharton School majoring in Finance with an interest in investments in clean tech and renewable energy. She has over eight years of work experience in sustainable development project implementation and climate change policy.

Ghazal was a program officer with the United Nations Division for Sustainable Development and the UN Forum on Forests. She was active in the planning and implementation of a number of initiatives to promote energy efficiency and renewable energy development. She also helped create a global partnership of transportation industry associations, governments and civil society organizations to find ways of reducing local and greenhouse house gas emissions in urban environments and spearheaded the UN Car Free Day Series in which over eight million people in 12 cities around the world participated. Most recently, Ghazal was selected to represent the Department in negotiations of the UN Framework Convention on Climate Change and its Kyoto Protocol and has been active in the process of creating financial incentives to reduce deforestation.

In addition, Ghazal maintains a keen interest in entrepreneurship. She serves on the advisory board of Arzu, an organization which promotes female entrepreneurship in Afghanistan and EcoUmbrella, devoted to supporting budding environmentally minded entrepreneurs in Los Angeles. She is the former Executive Director of DAWN, a women's professional development network. She is also a Member of the Board of the Lower East Side Harm Reduction Center in New York.

Ghazal graduated from Stanford University and completed an MSc from the London School of Economics with Honors. She has been been awarded the Robert Toigo Fellowship as well as the Edmund T. / Jeannette C. Pratt Jr. and Joseph Wharton Fellowships.
