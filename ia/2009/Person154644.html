Mary Shields’s Summary

Expert marketing communications seamed to the visual: adverts, headlines and taglines, product copy, press releases, and corporate communications, including new social media endeavors such as Twitter and FaceBook. Product and color naming whizz.

Specific highlights: I've run my own cottage-industry business and managed a staff of 10 employees. I've waxed poetic about $15,000 sets of luxury linen sheets and explained the complexities of thread count to neophyte editors; I've written all sorts of merchandising copy for the mass market at Martha Stewart Omnimedia and come up with hundreds of color names for her Robin's Egg Blue. I Tweet for an alternative healing practitioner, and am advising a nonprofit humanitarian .org on their troika of branding, outreach, and social media.

My personal and professional background is steeped in design and writing. I spent 15-years as the co-owner of a small arthouse jewelry and giftware firm and functioned under many hats: brand and marketing manager, all-around business engine/driver, 3-dimensional designer and creative director. In my early college years, I studied creative writing, poetry, and copy writing, but took a break from my studies to be a partner in Eve Kaplin Design, Inc (the aforementioned arthouse jewelry line). I finally returned to school to complete my bachelor's degree here in New York City at FIT in 2000. I graduated with a degree in Home Products Marketing in 2002 magna cum laude, and am well versed in the intricasies of the home furnishings industry.

I'm good. I'm versatile. And I like a good challenge.

Mary Shields’s Specialties:

Brand identity: creation, maintenance and overhaul; concepting/project management of advertising campaigns and media buys; marketing; website management; photo-shoot art direction; social-engagement media communications.
