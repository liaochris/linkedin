Carla Drago’s Summary

After several years working in film and television as a writer, director, and producer, I moved into digital media when it became clear that new technologies were changing the face of communications. As someone with a passion for digital technologies it was exciting to see convergence between old and new media evolve. Audiences were now part of a dialogue, rather than passive recipients of a story or message.

After working as a producer and project manager on iTV ‘red button’ applications for AUSTAR, I began working for Massive Interactive, one of Australia’s most innovative interactive companies, on web and touch screen applications. These included large, technically complex websites like the Emmy Award nominated BigPond Movie Download site, and the online game Reach Out Central, winner of the international Why Games Matter competition, as well as smaller online advertising campaigns like the 3 Mobile Ashes flash games and photo uploads.

As producer I was required to interface with clients and digital account managers, scope projects and manage timelines, budgets, and documentation, and work with teams of creatives and developers.

During this time I also completed a completed a Masters of Interactive Multimedia at the University of Technology, Sydney, focusing on user experience, interaction design, and digital storytelling. I co-authored a chapter of Digital Experience Design, and participated in cross-platform innovation labs, including Crossover Australia (run by Frank Boyd, of the BBC Innovation Labs).

I’m very passionate about the world of interactive digital technology and love the challenge of managing the process that brings digital projects to fruition. I believe we are now able to present information and tell stories in new and engaging ways and I love being part of this innovation.
