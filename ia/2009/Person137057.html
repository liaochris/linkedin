Larry Mendte’s Summary

Over 30 years as a news anchor and reporter in New York, Los Angeles, Chicago, Philadelphia and San Diego.

The First Host of Access Hollywood.

Wrote and Directed "Alex Scott: A Stand for Hope," a documentary that won the national Sigma Delta Chi Award, an Edward R. Murrow Award, 9 Emmys and is on permanent exhibit at the Paley Center/

Responsible for changing two state laws in Illinois after a series of reports on school bus safety.

Larry Mendte’s Specialties:

Writing, directing, producing, anchoring, reporting, speeches and new media.

Larry Mendte’s Experience

  • Main Anchor

    KYW-TV

    (Public Company; cbs; Broadcast Media industry)

    June 2003 — June 2008 (5 years 1 month)

    (Philadelphia) - Main anchor at 6 and 11 O'clock.

    Led KYW, which had been in third or fourth place for more than a decade, to within a point of beating WPVI and becoming #1 in the market.

    Was awarded 30 Regional Emmy Awards for anchoring, writing, hosting, producing and directing. Won a national Sigma Delta Chi award for writing and directing the Documentary "Alex Scott: A Stand for Hope." Won 3 Edward R. Murrow Awards, two dozen AP awards and dozens of Philadelphia Press Club and Society of Professional Journalist awards.

    Was voted favorite anchor five times in reader's polls conducted by area magazines and newspapers.

    Solo Anchored Live from The Reagan Funeral, The Fifth Anniversary of 9/11 and Live 8. Founded the Alex's Lemonade Stand Telethon that has raised three million dollars to fight childhood cancer,

    A series of reports forcedthe city to relight the etrnal flame to honor the me and women who died fighting for America.

  • Anchor

    WCAU

    (Motion Pictures and Film industry)

    1997 — 2003 (6 years)

    (Philadelphia) - Anchored the 4, 6 and 11 O'clock newscasts.

    Led WCAU to topple WPVI in the ratings and become #1 for the first time in 30 years.

    Helped to create the first 4PM newscast in the market and created the market's first live Sunday Morning news interview show, "Live@Issue."

    Won over 20 Regional Emmys, including four in the best anchor category. Anchored live from the Clinton Impeachment, the NBA Finals and the Y2K New Year's Eve celebration.

  • Host of Access Hollywood

    NBC

    (Public Company; GE; Broadcast Media industry)

    May 1996 — September 1997 (1 year 5 months)

    (Los Angeles) - The first host of Access Hollywood.

    Co-hosted weekday shows with Giselle Fernandez and the weekend show with Nancy O'Dell.

    Covered The Academy Awards, Emmys and Grammies.

    Interviewed George Clooney, Tom Hanks, Jerry Seinfeld, Meryl Streep, Martin Scorcese, Robert DeNiro and every major Hollywood star.

  • Investigative Reporter, Anchor

    WBBM-TV

    (Public Company; Broadcast Media industry)

    July 1990 — May 1996 (5 years 11 months)

    (Chicago) - Anchored the Noon Newscast with Joan Lovett.

    Produced Long Form special reports as part of the "News Extra" unit.

    Set a record for Regional Emmy awards winning 6 in one year and a total of 22.

    A series of Investigative reports on School Bus Safety led to two new state laws. Governor Jim Edgar signed one into law on the set of the 10 O'Clock newscast..

    Covered the trial of Mike Tyson, the Winter Olympics in Lillehammer and the Great Chicago Flood.

  • Weather Anchor

    KFMB

    (Privately Held; Broadcast Media industry)

    August 1987 — July 1990 (3 years)

    Main Weather Anchor in San Diego.

    Creator of very popular "How Come?" reports, "The Movie Jury" and "The Gloat Phone."

    The "How Come?" reports also ran on the nationall syndicated show "Hard Copy" and on "The Movie Channel."

    Won 12 Regional Emmys for Feature Reporting, writing and performance. Also performed stand-up comedy at clubs in Souther California and Arizona.

  • Weekend Anchor

    WABC-TV

    (Broadcast Media industry)

    August 1984 — August 1987 (3 years 1 month)

    (New York) Anchored the Weekend News at WABC-TV.

    Main fill-in anchor for Ernie Anastos, Bill Beutel and Roger Grimsby.

    Covered the Baby M trial, the Super Bowl, the World Series and the Vietnam Veteran's ticker tape parade.
