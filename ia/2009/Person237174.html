Tori Orr’s Summary

I am a librarian and artist who has managed projects and teams exploring the convergence of old and new public information spaces and services. Interested in culture-based information seeking behavior and how it intersects with storytelling and personal histories.

Tori Orr’s Specialties:

library science, information architecture, information graphics, usability analysis, grant writing, copywriting, experience and interaction design, knowledge management, content strategy, branding and representation, internet information resource design, information retrieval systems, wayfinding systems.

    MIT Lincoln Laboratory

    (Research industry)

    2008 — Present (1 year)

    MITLL is a Federally Funded Research & Development Center managed by MIT and funded by the DoD. My responsibility is ensuring scientists, engineers and researchers can effectively communicate highly technical and sensitive information to external sponsors and the lab community using a variety of video, digital media and traditional publishing tools.

  • Manager, Library Information Services

    Montana State Library

    (Libraries industry)

    July 2004 — June 2006 (2 years)

    Managed the Information Services Division of the Montana State Digital Library (MSdL) with a patron base of 10,000 state employees, 30 state agencies and the citizens of Montana. Administrative responsibility for library services with a budget of $300K and a materials replacement cost of two million. Our mission was to provide public access to government publications, GIS data, and biological information on Montana’s wildlife through the first digital institutional repository for state-produced information.
