    Youth Forum for RTI was created on 14th October, 2007. Its main objective is to create awareness about the Right to Information (RTI) Act among the citizens of India. The reason behind creating the forum is the ignorance of Indian citizens about the Act. Although it came into force on October 12, 2005, still many people aren’t aware of its usefulness. The Act empowers the Indian citizens to actively participate in the governance of the society and give a befitting reply to the corruption. The power of the Act lies in its simplicity which makes it quite robust and unchallenged.

    I head the forum and devise the strategies via which this Act can be popularized among the masses. Also, I keep a close watch on the latest happenings related to the RTI Act and make sure that they reach to the online audience via platforms which are in my control.

    I also keep on exploring the options of entering into partnership with other non-profit agencies to further our causes. To do this, I often attend seminars and discussions organized by International agencies such as United Nations Office on Drugs and Crime (UNODC) and Amnesty International.
