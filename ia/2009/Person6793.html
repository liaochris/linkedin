Al Cattabiani’s Summary

Al has always focused on distributing high-end niche programming. He is now active -- either as a principal or consultant -- in a variety of startups, ranging from original content for mobile phones to electronic distribution of wellness information to new models for releasing music and films.

He was President/CEO of Wellspring Media, a company he co-founded in 1993 and sold in 2004. Wellspring, now controlled by The Weinstein Company, was a leading independent distributor -- worldwide, in all media -- of arthouse cinema and programming promoting holistic living. Its library of over 700 titles included many Oscar, Emmy and Grammy winners.

Before starting Wellspring, Al served as President and Chief Operating Officer of Los Angeles-based Pacific Arts Corporation, where he helped create the PBS Home Video label.

He serves on the boards of the Global Film Initiative, a non-profit foundation to assist filmmakers in developing countries; and of Acorn Media Group, a distribution and direct-marketing company based in DC, London and Sydney.

Al is an active musician; and relishes all the creative, technological and marketing changes in his various fields.

Al Cattabiani’s Experience

  • Owner

    1409 Entertainment and Garagista Music

    (Entertainment industry)

    March 2004 — Present (5 years 5 months)

    1409 and Garagista are each incubators for niche media properties -- in visual media and music, respectively -- with an emphasis on emerging technologies and distribution channels. They are currently involved in startups related to mobile video content, online film distribution, holistic health/wellness information and new models for music distribution.

    1409 has produced several TV/DVD projects for Public Television and other networks. It is also provides consulting services to several clients in film, TV, DVD and digital distribution.

  • Co-founder/CEO

    Wellspring Media

    (Entertainment industry)

    December 1993 — February 2004 (10 years 3 months)

    Wellspring, which is now controlled by The Weinstein Company, was a leading independent distributor of arthouse and foreign-language cinema to theatres, on DVD, through its own direct-response catalog company, and to worldwide broadcasters. It also focused on programming promoting holistic living, featuring Joseph Campbell, Andrew Weil, Deepak Chopra and many other leaders in the field. Al and his partners founded the company in 1993, built it through internal growth and acquisition (including saving two acquired companies from bankruptcy); and sold it in 2004.

  • President/COO

    Pacific Arts Corporation

    (Entertainment industry)

    August 1988 — October 1992 (4 years 3 months)

    Pacific Arts was best known for music and comedy programming produced by its founder, Michael Nesmith, and for creating the PBS Home Video label around such acclaimed titles as "The Civil War," "I, Claudius," "The Creation of the Universe," "Amazing Grace" and "Eyes on the Prize." It was one of the first companies to sell, rather than rent, video programming -- which helped create the special-interest "sell through" market.
