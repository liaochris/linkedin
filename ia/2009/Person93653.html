Heidi Hertler’s Experience

  • Distinguished Research Professor

    Inter American University

    (Environmental Services industry)

    October 2007 — Present (1 year 7 months)

    I am currently a Distinguished Research Professor at Inter American University of Puerto Rico’s Center for Environmental Education, Conservation and Research (CECIA) focusing on island land use changes in tropical and sub-tropical environments and the impacts on the surrounding marine system. My responsibilities also include administrative aspects of proposal development for CECIA, manuscript preparation and review, program development, community interactions on local environmental issues and student supervision.

  • Senior Scientist

    Eyak Environmental Science

    (Environmental Services industry)

    October 2003 — October 2005 (2 years 1 month)

    I was a member of a team that started an 8(a) Native Alaskan environmental consulting firm (Eyak Environmental Science). As a subsidiary of the Eyak Development Corporation, I was responsible for work plan development, budget management, scheduling, contract proposal development and client interaction. Because of our size, my responsibilities extended to contract coordinating, assisting with office and field staffing, data collection and analysis, and report preparation.

  • Staff Scientist

    The Academy of Natural Sciences of Philadelphia

    (Non-Profit; 51-200 employees; Museums and Institutions industry)

    September 1995 — October 2003 (8 years 2 months)

    The eight previous years, I was employed by The Academy of Natural Science, Patrick Center for Environmental Research, and involved in both contractual and applied research projects. As project manager, I was responsible for implementing a program for the Susquehanna Steam Electric Plant (Berwick, PA). My experience includes contract preparation and client interaction, field and laboratory activities for the monitoring of radionuclides in aquatic and terrestrial systems, data analysis, report preparation and development of special projects funded on a year-to-year basis. I was also involved in pre-construction marine benthic surveys in the Bahamas and the development of a post construction mitigation plan, including monitoring and funding for ecological research.
