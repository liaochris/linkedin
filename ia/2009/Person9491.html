Alissa Knight’s Summary

Mrs. Knight works in the Office of Counter Intelligence and Special Operations (SpecOps) division at the C2I (Counter Terrorism Electronic Warfare and Intelligence Centre); an international think tank to the US intelligence community that consists of all-source intelligence analysts, field operations officers, and operational support personnel.

Mrs. Knight has authored numerous intelligence reports providing key intelligence on terrorists and terrorist activities to the US intelligence community since December 2007 in electronic warfare.

In October of 2007, Mrs. Knight executed her second successful M&A exit of her company Applied Watch Technologies, a security software company focused in the open source security space.

Prior to selling Applied Watch, Ms. Knight sold her previous company Netstream to AlphaTrade, a publicly traded company in British Columbia.

Mrs. Knight has over thirteen years of experience in the information security space working several years as a defense contractor for the Department of Defense. Mrs. Knight also presented directly to the Defense Information Systems Agency (DISA) Technology Insertion Panel, prior to that performing risk assessments on our nation's critical infrastructure.

Mrs. Knight has published numerous writings, advisories, and has worked closely with both the NIPC in investigating cyber crime.

Mrs. Knight has been interviewed live on Fox News regarding the Al-Qaeda cyber threats, published numerous papers in network security at the Sans Institute, SecurityFocus.com, Linux Magazine, and numerous other industry respected sites and news publications.
