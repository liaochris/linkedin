Rishi Jain’s Summary

An enthusiastic, goal-oriented electrical engineering graduate student at the University of Arkansas with experience in diverse areas including Digital VLSI Design and Wireless Communication Systems. During the fall '07 and spring '08 semesters I served as a Silicon Validation Engineer intern in the Embedded and Communications Group at Intel in Portland, Oregon. My undergraduate degree is a B.S. in Electronics and Telecommunications Engineering from Shri G.S. Institue of Technology and Science, Indore, India.

During my internship at Intel, I was part of the post silicon process, working on a low power IA core embedded System on Chip (SoC) targeted for network servers. Integrating the processor, memory and I/O in the SoC involved a complex architecture and a lot of stress testing before the chip was launched onto the market.

Prior to coming to the University of Arkansas, I studied at Texas A & M for 3 semesters. Courses I took while at this institution were a combination of Digital VLSI and Wireless Communications. My projects involved extensive working with tools like Matlab, Cadence, Synopsis and Verilog.

These projects included: "Implementation of a Low Density Parity Checker using FPGA"; " MIPS Processor in Verilog"; " Baseline Simulation of OFDM in 802.11 Architecture"; "Design of a 64 bit Dynamic RAM in Cadence"; "Secured wireless communication with PIC18F452 Microcontrollers", "Design of a Duplexer for a communication device"; "TCP Vegas Simulation using NS-2 ";and "Lab View Express (LVE) to publish embedded systems data on Internet".
