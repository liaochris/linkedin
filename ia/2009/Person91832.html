Hamish MacDonald’s Summary

I am a full-on entrepreneur, particularly startups. I guess my best talent is throwing myself passionately into what I do. I love the daily challenge of getting ideas to market and overcoming all barriers in between.

Through 16 years in Japan, I learned the basics by jumping into the deep end of the marketing and selling pool. Over time I learned to "Move Concepts to IP to Prototype to Market to Strategic Partnerships".

As every aspect of human life rapidly integrates to the web I try to marry it to real world needs. Geeks think I am a Suit, Suits think I am a Geek! But I am neither, really. I like to think I am a creative type with a clear understanding of business processes. I am committed to being an effective bridge between the finely-tuned needs of programmers and the more earthy needs of the rest of us! If you can get that balance right, magic can happen.

I have led venture capital negotiations with firms in Silicon Valley and Wall Street and practice the principles of Lean Development. I know the pressures of startups and how to bootstrap effectively. What you originally imagine your product to be never survives first contact with the marketplace - so create enough runway and summon nerve to adapt, adapt, adapt. Paul Graham sums it up nicely, "Build something people want, and be relentlessly resourceful".

My deep motivation to keep going from morning through late night is changing the world for the better for more people. I hold 6 beliefs for a just and prosperous global society:
1) Individual Rights, 2) Property Rights, 3) Equitable Enforcement of those Rights.
A) Freedom to Work for a business, B) Freedom to Start a business, C) Freedom to Invest in a business.
A society built on these foundations allows the indomitable human spirit to soar as high as it seeks, with freedom to follow its inspiration.

My ultimate dream is to create global web applications and business models built upon these factors, person by person, area by area.

    2003 — Present (6 years)

Functionality includes e-consultations (including mobile phone interface), linkable to clinic e-records via secure web interface. Partners include several of Japan's largest IT vendors and makers. Underlying technology has received its first patent in New Zealand (15 other countries plus EU pending).
