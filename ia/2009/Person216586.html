Sheryl Torr-Brown PhD’s Summary

I have spent almost 20 years in the pharmaceutical industry in a variety of positions including Head of Thrombosis Research for Searle, Director of Knowledge Management and Strategy for Monsanto Corporate Division, and Head of Science and Technology for Pfizer Drug Safety Evaluation.

I am now writing a book on the relevance of science to the public, and I am conducting research into the patient's experience of disease before they present to their doctor, and in the period of recovery after treatment or stabilization. Patient experience is key to the development of better approaches to therapy, and more effective recovery after illness. My research sits between the consumer and the scientist, often with tangible benefit to both. Translating science to the public, and then transmitting an understanding of public perceptions to the scientist, is my passion. Recent clients include a major retail pharmacy, a large biotechnology organization, a health intelligence company and a small innovation company.

My goals are to complete this first book and get it published before I get too old too market it (!) and to bring awareness to the scientific and public communities of the temporal nature of the disease experience, and the advantages of a more holistic approach to diagnosis and treatment.

My particular areas of focus for research are primary care, eye diseases, disease of the elderly, psychiatric diseases, personalized medicine, and cancer. I am a member of the Scientific Advisory Boards for the Personalized Medicine journal and a UK- based IT company.
I am also currently serving on the board of a local Food Center and working with the center to end hunger through building community resilience in the county of New London.
