Samir Palnitkar’s Summary

A serial entrepreneur, Samir Palnitkar brings 18 years of experience in marketing, sales, management, technology and operations. Samir has founded three successful startups and he is currently at AirTight Networks, his fourth startup.

At AirTight Networks, Samir Palnitkar is the co-founder and General Manager of Products and Services. He responsible for engineering, product delivery, technical support and operations. He is also performing the acting VP of Marketing role by providing leadership to market demand generation and marketing.

Samir was an angel investor and the Chairman of the Board at Ingot Systems, Inc., CA a multi-million semiconductor IP company that was acquired by Virage Logic of Fremont, CA in 2007.

Samir was the co-founder and Chief Operating Officer of Obongo, Inc. a high profile online identity management company that was acquired by America Online (AOL) in July 2001.

Prior to Obongo, Samir co-founded and served as the President of I2P, Inc., a multi-million dollar semiconductor design company that was acquired by Lattice Semiconductor, Inc.

Before I2P, Inc, Samir held key management and engineering positions at Sun Microsystems. He is the holder of three U.S. patents. Samir has authored two authoritative books on chip design and published several technical papers.

Samir holds a B.Tech in Electrical Engineering from the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT), Kanpur, India, an M.S. in Electrical Engineering from the University of Washington, Seattle and an M.B.A. from San Jose State University.

Samir Palnitkar’s Experience

  • Founder and General Manager

    AirTight Networks

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Computer & Network Security industry)

    2004 — Present (5 years)

    AirTight Networks is the world's first company to deliver wireless network security as a service, online, on-demand. AirTight is a leader in the Wireless Intrusion Prevention (WIPS) and has more than 25 pioneering patents for this technology. AirTight has raised $36.5 million to-date. AirTight WIPS competes directly with Cisco's WIPS offering that was announced in June 2008.

    At AirTight, I am responsible for marketing, engineering, inside sales, customer support, software-as-a-service (SaaS) operations and a variety of other functions at AirTight. As a founder, I was instrumental in growing the company from a 3 person team to over 100 people.

  • Investor and Chairman of Board

    Ingot Systems

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Semiconductors industry)

    2003 — 2007 (4 years)

    Ingot was a multi-million dollar semiconductor IP company specializing in high speed memory controllers. Ingot was acquired by Virage Logic based in Fremont, CA in 2007.

    At Ingot, I was an advisor and board member. I advised Ingot on sales, marketing, organization building and customer acquisition. I was also involved in instrumenting the eventual acquisition of Ingot.

  • President

    Jambo Systems, Inc.

    (Semiconductors industry)

    2001 — 2003 (2 years)

    During the period after the acquisition of Obongo, Inc. in 2001, I decided to take some time off and did a lot of traveling around the world. Then I set up a consulting company called Jambo Systems, where I worked with customers as Sun Microsystems, Cisco and Ikanos providing expertise in areas of engineering, marketing, and sales.

  • Founder and Chief Operating Officer

    Obongo, Inc.

    (Internet industry)

    1999 — 2001 (2 years)

    Obongo was a high profile internet company providing single sign-on technology that allowed users to log in once and access any site on the internet. Obongo raised at total of $33 million and had marquee customers such as Citibank and Merrill Lynch. Obongo was later acquired by America Online (AOL) in 2001.

    At Obongo, I grew the company from two people to over 70 people. I was responsible for strategic alliances, operations, internet based marketing strategies, user acquisition and support.

  • Founder and President

    I2P, Inc.

    (Semiconductors industry)

    1995 — 1998 (3 years)

    I2P was a multi-million dollar semiconductor IP company that specialized in PCI, 1394, USB and other bus controller architectures. I2P was self-funded and was later acquired by Lattice Semiconductor, Inc. based in Oregon, USA.

    At I2P, I was responsible for growing the company from 2 people to over 30 people. I was in charge of marketing, sales and engineering functions. In that role, I was responsible for acquisition of more than 70 key customer accounts including names such as Philips, Sun Microsystems, Motorola, Texas Instruments, Hitachi, Nvidia, Synopsys and Cadence.

  • Engineering and Management

    Sun Microsystems

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; SUNW; Computer Hardware industry)

    1992 — 1995 (3 years)

    At Sun Microsystems, I was involved in the design and development of multiple ASICs and two microprocessors including the UltraSparc. I also got an opportunity to contribute to the system architecture of UltraSparc based systems.

    During my days at Sun, I also wrote a book "Verilog HDL: A Guide to Digital Design and Synthesis". This book later became a reference book and text book of choice for anyone wanting to study chip design using Verilog.
