Thomas Willis’s Summary

My aviation career began in 1971 with the United States Air Force, Air National Guard in Reno, Nevada where I served as a turbine engine technician during the Viet Nam era. During my ANG service I earned an FAA private pilot certificate.

In 1977 I relocated to Tulsa, Oklahoma where I earned an FAA airframe and powerplant certificate at the Spartan School of Aeronautics. During A&P school I maintained light aircraft and served as a customer service agent with Continental Airlines. In 1979 I joined American Airlines as an A&P certificated aircraft cleaner.

Shortly thereafter I qualified as a Flight Engineer in the USAF reserve C-141 program. Once qualified I returned to Tulsa and taught aviation subjects in the Tulsa Vo-tech until recall from furlough with American Airlines where I served as a structures mechanic, powerplant mechanic, pre-qualified crew chief, various acting management roles, quality auditor, quality supervisor and quality manager until early retirement in late 1992.

From January 1993 I lead teams around the world conducting Aviation Quality Management Consulting, Training and Auditing until accepting the Chief of Quality Management role with Honda R&D Americas - later Honda Aircraft Company.

Since becoming an FAA Designated Airworthiness Representative in 1995, I have certificated nearly 100 experimental and or light sport aircraft including the HondaJet. From 2003-2004 I served as builder and test pilot of a RANS S-18 II factory award winning Amateur-built airplane.

Today I lead a team of quality professionals at Quest Aircraft Company in Sandpoint, Idaho building the 750 shaft horsepower Kodiak. The aircraft was designed and built to support humanitarian and mission aviation needs around the world. See www.questaircraft.com
