George Arriola’s Summary

Leveraging over a decade of experience and expertise in brand and human-centered product design, George works within research and development on emerging graphics technologies and interactive experiences, enabling holistic solutions for consumer electronic devices, software, and operating system platforms.

Now with Sony + Sony Ericsson (Sony U(X)nited), he leads a global multi-disciplinary creative team that advances UX disciplines jointly alongside engineering and product planning management teams.

While at Apple, George jointly led a grassroots, cross-organizational project sponsored by executive management to assess the current and future Apple technology portfolio, and combine key architectures to deliver a next-generation Web standards-based WebKit engine. This engine, comprised of WebKit, Core Animation, HD Media Frameworks, and the new HTML5 and CSS3 syntax, enables developers and designers to create hardware accelerated 3D user experiences on Apple devices running OS X previously not possible.

Prior to Apple, George spent over 7 years with Adobe, where he was one of the lead Adobe Creative Suite architects within product management enabling mobile and Web customer workflow's across Adobe's diverse product portfolio.

George has also worked at WIRED Magazine (Japan), and Macromedia. While with Macromedia, George was one of the first Japanese Product Managers and an original founding member of Macromedia K.K. (Japan).

Living in SoMa/Potrero Hill, George keeps involved in the local and global design community through active participation in AIGA events and through conference and seminar speaking engagements around human-centered design and ethnography. When not working or traveling, George is an active Papa, Sous Chef, Dishwasher, and Epiphany Engineer - inspirational catalyst for his daughters Emi and Maia.
