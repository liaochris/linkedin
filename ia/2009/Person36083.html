Bill Willis’s Summary

I have over 15 years of experience in the software industry successfully directing, developing and delivering mission critical systems, products and services for financial, telecommunication, media, healthcare and software product companies.

I specialize in helping companies of all sizes choose the right technologies and then successfully implement them with unmatched efficiency and quality.

I have a proven track record of recruiting, mentoring, leading and retaining world class technical talent. When consulting, I often serve as a trusted adviser to business and IT executives alike, and I provide decisive guidance in highly stressful, even chaotic, environments.

I am most at home with ground-breaking research and products. Not ruled by conventional wisdom, I think outside the box and thrive on great ideas and their ability to positively transform organizations and entire markets.

Co-author of Mastering JavaServer Faces, Wiley 2004

Bill Willis’s Specialties:

enterprise and application architecture, product development, mentoring, team building, technologist

    OSG

    March 2004 — June 2009 (5 years 4 months)

  • Enterprise Architect

    FINRA

    (Financial Services industry)

    February 2007 — May 2009 (2 years 4 months)

    Led a program level architecture effort to streamline and further automate Member Regulation’s new member application process. Developed logical, component and deployment views of the overall architecture and documented architecturally significant scenarios through each. Managed key integration points between systems. Meticulously tracked, surfaced and mitigated technical risks.

    Then moved on to architect the Member Regulation Desktop (MRDT), a key NextGen web application used by FINRA and NYSE analysts to review applicant and member filings. Recommended and helped implement a more agile approach to development (e.g., proper unit testing, continuous integration environment, scrum-like process, etc.). Led web user interface development for two successful releases.

    Introduced FINRA to Seam, JSF, Facelets and Richfaces. Successfully implemented these new technologies in two releases of MRDT.

  • Chief Architect

    Fannie Mae, Finance & Portfolio Technologies

    (Financial Services industry)

    March 2004 — December 2006 (2 years 10 months)

    Led a team of architects, analysts and business executives in creating a FAPT enterprise architecture. Developed a detailed technology roadmap for moving from multiple application silos and a store-and-forward, data-centric, highly redundant architecture to a near real-time service oriented architecture. Developed a reusable application architecture technology stack based on best of breed open source technologies and commercial products.

    Once defined, moved to lead product development and application architecture within Treasury Services - one of Fannie Mae’s most critical business units. Developed a multi-year, multi-phase technology roadmap that is transforming a critical set of systems responsible for trillions of dollars in funds and debt securities transfers each year. Successfully completed the first two of five phases despite multiple business and IT reorgs. Phase one completed the first version of a web interface (TCM - Treasury Cash Management) while phase two introduced a shared business service for accepting wire requests from business units around the company and reporting on their progress in near real-time via a standard set of interfaces.

    Introduced the FAPT division of Fannie Mae to Spring and JSF. Successfully implemented these technologies in two releases of TCM.

  • Enterprise Architect

    Veteran's Hospital Administration

    (Hospital & Health Care industry)

    November 2003 — March 2004 (5 months)

    The VHA provides healthcare to United States veterans at well over 100 facilities nationwide. The software that supports these medical facilities (clinical patient records, pharmacy, imaging, financial, etc.) was being migrated from Mumps to J2EE. I served as one of two J2EE experts in a small applied architecture group responsible for defining the enterprise architecture and overseeing its proper implementation across the organization. Also served as the VHA's expert on limiting vendor and technology lock-in.

  • Director of Engineering and Product Development (CTO)

    ObjectVenture

    (Computer Software industry)

    January 2001 — September 2003 (2 years 9 months)

    Software startup specializing in pattern-based software development tools and services. Responsible for all product research, development and consulting services (a description of each is provided below). Routinely presented ObjectVenture’s offerings and research efforts to some of the world’s leading software product companies (e.g., Sun, Rational, HP, Flashline). Spoke at user group meetings, industry trade shows, and gatherings of technology executives. Helped secure over one million dollars in venture funding. Technical lead in acquisition talks with Sun and other companies. Along with the CEO, led the company through one of the worst economic downturns in the software industry.

    Voxiva MediaServer

    Initiated and developed a relationship with Voxiva that led to a large product licensing agreement and extensive consulting services.

    ObjectAssembler

    ObjectAssembler is a pattern-driven, component-based, visual J2EE development environment used standalone or integrated with popular Java IDEs.

    Pattern and Component Markup Language (PCML)

    PCML is an XML-based language for representing pattern languages, individual patterns, implementation strategies and their relationship with software components independent of any particular technology platform or vendor.

    PatternsCentral.com

    PatternsCentral is a community web portal devoted exclusively to software patterns with a monthly newsletter that catered to thousands of subscribers. It focused on educating, communicating, and innovating best practices and the state-of-the-art in software patterns.

    UML Bridge

    The UML Bridge provides roundtrip engineering of J2EE components and patterns between ObjectAssembler and popular UML tools via XMI.

    Former Member of Expert Group for JSR 198, the Standard Extension API for IDEs - This JSR was an attempt to provide a standard extension (or plugin) API for IDEs.

  • Software Architect and Lead Engineer

    Inline Software

    (Computer Software industry)

    InLine Standard (or BeanStation) was the first EJB component-based development tool on the market. It provided a number of innovations that would later appear as staples of modern Java IDEs: component views of EJBs, creation of EJBs from database tables, UML to code round tripping, refactoring and a generic plugin architecture for IDE integration.

    Member of product development and strategy team. Developed a component-centric view of EJBs – as opposed to the typical collection of files found in most IDEs. Member of R&D team that designed the first model-driven architecture (MDA) tooling.

  • Product Manager, Software Architect

    Geneva Software

    (Privately Held; Computer Software industry)

    August 1997 — April 2000 (2 years 9 months)

    ICASS and RABIT

    Both applications were used worldwide at most U.S. embassies and missions for political planning, budgeting and the distribution of administrative costs. RABIT was also used to prepare annual reports for Congress.

    Product manager and architect for both client-server applications and a system for collecting and reporting on data world wide. Provided technology guidance to the DoS and led software training sessions for embassy financial analysts at regional conferences around the world. Managed and mentored a talented team of developers and support staff. Both systems reduced the collection of information by weeks and provided significant cost savings.

    DTSPO Software Integration Effort

    DTSPO is the DoS office responsible for telecommunications support for embassies worldwide. Several departments within DTSPO used separate software systems that did not integrate well. The integration effort was charged with developing a new central system that was heavily workflow based. Guided the architectural specification of the system, which consisted of a multi-layered, service-oriented approach. Promoted and successfully used object oriented analysis, design and implementation techniques. Obtained a Secret clearance for work in an open Secret building.

    Development Tools

    Developed a robust querying and reporting tool. This tool served as a generic and highly configurable module that was successfully reused for several high profile financial software systems. It included an editor and file explorer for building, storing and organizing complex queries and reports. Also developed a fully featured requirements and defect tracking tool that dramatically increased the efficiency and effectiveness of my engineering teams. This software also provided up-to-date information for my clients and helped improve communication between my engineering team and clients.

  • Lead Software Engineer

    Dewberry & Davis

    (Privately Held; Civil Engineering industry)

    October 1993 — August 1997 (3 years 11 months)

    FEMA Disaster Recovery

    Served as a software engineer on a number of large, on-site hurricane disaster recovery efforts in the eastern US. Developed several client server applications that tracked, analyzed, and reported on damage survey reports with possible flood hazard mitigation alternatives.

    Water Quality System

    The South Brunswick Water & Sewer Authority in North Carolina developed a system of weather stations and manual water quality reporting sites throughout the county. They needed a software system for collecting, tracking, and reporting on the results. Developed a client server application that collected, tracked, and reported on the data. Developed visualization tools that used GIS and graphing software to analyze the data.

    C&O Canal Disaster Recovery

    Worked as part of an interdisciplinary team and the National Park Service to survey, catalog, estimate costs, and track engineering mitigation efforts for thousands of historic sites along the canal in response to flooding. Developed a client server application that stored, processed, and reported on the surveying, engineering, and mitigation efforts for every site, including thousands of high resolution photographs.

    Engineering Tool Integration

    Produced a number of tools that linked various engineering applications together to dramatically improve efficiency.
