Sarah Willis’s Summary

The sculpture that I create is large-scale, industrial, experiential, poetic...

Theorists and writers such as Marjorie Garber, Alain Badiou, Susan Stewart, Jacques Derrida, Julia Kristeva, Gertrude Stein (and many others), have all directly or indirectly sparked an exploration of sculpture as line and space as material. I plan to excavate this further as I research, participate in, and examine museums and institutions in the Bay Area.

It is my goal to continue the development of my work through sculptural investigation and composite drawings while working at zeum. I am considering participating in an upcoming show at Root Division entitled, "Raw", November 2009.

Sarah Willis’s Specialties:

It is important for me to be capable with my hands. Woodworking, welding, electrical wiring, mold making, sewing, general construction, and drawing are consistent in each artwork I create.
My hands can't make the work alone so I recently picked up new tools to articulate the future of my work through: digital photography, Photoshop, Illustrator, Vectorworks, and web design.

    September 2008 — Present (9 months)

    I experience many obstacles and successes day to day at this fast growing, fairly new, not for profit in the Bay Area. Much like the Exploratorium, this "museum" for kids-teens and beyond provides tools for exploration.   • assistant to John Allred

    private contractor

    (Architecture & Planning industry)

    June 2008 — September 2008 (4 months)

    I came into this job in the framing and drywall stage. For the past 2-3 months we have built stairs leading to the three decks that surround the 3,000 square foot home, planked the decks with recycled boards, sheetrocked and finished the walls, and prepared the railing that follows the perimeter of the 3 decks. I do pickup work for the other three employees that are tiling, painting, and plumbing. My partner and I drywalled an elaborate entry way with three arches. The greatest thing about this job is that I learn it all...The contractor builds only one home a decade and is willing to teach you on the job. He does however insist on speed and attention to detail.

  • Cashier, Ordering, and Stock Maintainence

    Center Hardware

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Building Materials industry)

    August 2006 — February 2008 (1 year 7 months)

    clerk, responsible for manual ordering and replenishing of hardware goods for store + customer service and checkout.
