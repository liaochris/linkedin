Joanna Maxwell’s Summary

I am a corporate trainer, professional coach and writer, through my business, WorkInColour.

Until I found the tools to create my ideal mix of working in colour, my explorings led me to jobs as a lawyer, lifeskills teacher, therapist, childcare worker, academic, freelance journalist, creative consultant, filmwriter, barmaid, market researcher, general dogsbody and (if you trawl back far enough) shop assistant and babysitter….not to mention my most satisfying (and challenging) job to date, as a parent.

I have also walked on fire, climbed Mount Kilimanjaro and travelled solo - and not - through the Middle East, India, Africa, Asia and Tibet. Along the way, I learned a lot about overcoming fear, stopping to look at the scenery from time to time…and that combination of patience and persistence that only comes from long dealings with the Indian train system.

Now I work with organisations that are ready to think about things in a different way, to find creative solutions for problems, and to give their staff the tools to do their work efficiently and with flair. I am fascinated by the power of creativity and its ability to revolutionise lives and businesses. Using a range of different techniques, I teach creative thinking workshops that allow participants to tap into their creativity and channel it in ways that are both productive at a professional level and fulfilling personally.

And I work with individuals who are sick of hanging up half their personality with their jackets when they arrive at work each day. I run career change programs that equip participants with the tools to create a genuinely compelling and satisfying career.

(And I do what I can to help out in my community, too, cos belonging is a big thing with me.)
