Phil Kasiecki’s Summary

I have two careers, both of which reinforce one another.

By day, I am a software engineer with diverse experience. I am experienced in software development tools, embedded systems, device drivers and diagnostics, and possess a good hardware background. I am a versatile team player and quick learner of new technologies who works well with others. A pro-active engineer, I improve existing software through refactoring, conversions and fixing problems found elsewhere in a code base. I have also thrived in non-technical aspects such as documentation or presenting a new development to a group of people. A strong communicator, when called upon I interact directly with customers and other stakeholders to understand and resolve technical and non-technical issues of concern.

By night, I am a basketball writer whose coverage is centered on the college game and has been for over nine years. I do some coverage of high school and the NBA Draft added in, but Division I college basketball is the center of it all. My coverage consists of features and analysis, giving fans insight into what is happening with coaches, players and teams. I look beyond the box score and go beyond the simple details of what happened to tell fans what a box score and game recap can't tell them. In covering recruiting, I analyze the potential of many top high school players at the college level for the benefit of fans and college coaches. I routinely cover about 100 college basketball games per season, including conference tournaments at all levels and postseason tournament play. Additionally, as Managing Editor I manage a staff of writers who assist in bringing excellent coverage of everything centered on college basketball.
