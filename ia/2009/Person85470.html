George Wilmer’s Summary

RF/wireless communication systems engineer with 27+ years experience in board level through system level design, integration, test, project and program management. Experienced in detailed planning and implementation of wireless communications projects (3 MHz – 42 GHz) from system architecture to component level.

Expertise in EME, HIRF and lightning mitigation and testing. Development of EME test plans and procedures. Hands-on testing to MIL-461/462 as well as DO-160D/E standards.

Experienced with project management methodologies. Knowledgeable of business and cost issues, risk assessment and mitigation. Excellent written and oral communication skills. Multilingual (English, French, German).

George Wilmer’s Specialties:

• Wireless: SATCOM, 802.11x, 802.16, Trunked & conventional LMR, Bluetooth, ZigBee, RFID
• EMI//HIRF/Lightning/Environmental: per MIL-461, MIL-462, MIL-810, RTCA-DO-160
• Antenna Design: UHF/VHF/ FM - slot array, cavity back resonators, panel antennas
• Network & Telecom: Ethernet, Token Ring, TCP/IP, xDSL, X.25, FDDI, SONET, XML
• Applications: Department of Defense, Aerospace, Homeland Security, Public Safety & Law Enforcement and Commercial arenas

George Wilmer’s Experience

  • Principle Consultant

    Wilmer Engineering (Self-employed)

    June 1993 — Present (15 years 9 months)

    Provides technical consultation in the areas of RF communications, Electromagnetic Effects (EME) and systems engineering to include system, subsystem and board level design. Responsibilities include providing technical consulting to clients in the Department of Defense, Aerospace, Homeland Security, Public Safety and Law Enforcement and Commercial arenas.

  • Staff Systems Engineer

    DRS C3 Systems

    (Public Company; 5001-10,000 employees; Defense & Space industry)

    November 2005 — January 2007 (1 year 3 months)

    Responsible for the system architecture, design and analysis of C3 and Homeland Security projects involving communication and surveillance systems. Includes integration of IR/EO radar, microwave, 802.11x, IP-based trunked radio systems, mesh networks, broadband wireless, VHF, UHF, HF and SATCOM technologies.

    Responsible to lead project teams, develop requirements traceability matrix, risk assessment, work as part of senior management team on bid captures. Developed schedules, manpower requirements and project costs. System level work included site surveys for developing system level documentation, propagation studies, communication room and rack layouts. Developed interconnection documentation and diagrams.

    As part of the Thales Radar technology transfer program, responsible for translating system engineering documents from French to English, developing system specifications and evaluating domestic vendors’ products for applicability to this program.

  • Project Manager

    CSC / DynCorp

    (Public Company; 5001-10,000 employees; Telecommunications industry)

    January 1999 — September 1999 (9 months)

    Managed the Mobile Office Environment (MOE) design and development, part of the PA. State Police’s (PSP) major statewide Incident Information Management System (IIMS) project.

    Designed and developed system architectures integrating the latest RF technologies including CDMA, CDPD, 802.11B wireless LANs, LMDS, 800 MHz trunked and conventional RF systems, handheld PDAs and pagers, satellite systems and advanced technologies as they related to wireless applications in public safety and intelligent transportation systems.

    Developed the proposed architecture for a nationwide APCO 25 compliant 800 MHz, IP-based, trunked land mobile radio network for the Treasury Department (for utilization by Customs, the Secret Service and other Treasury agencies).

  • RF Spectrum Manager

    Sverdrup Technology

    (Privately Held; 201-500 employees; Defense & Space industry)

    August 1991 — August 1992 (1 year 1 month)

    RF Spectrum Manager responsible for technical aspects of all RF communication systems at NASA - Lewis Research Center (HF, VHF, UHF, SHF, microwave, satellite, FDDI, RF to WAN/LAN interface, etc.). Required extensive RF and data communications knowledge in compliance with NTIA requirements.

    Analyzed NASA requirements and redesigned a new trunked mobile radio system. This involved presenting to NASA management an analysis of the present system (conventional LMR) and presenting the concept of a 400 MHz trunked LMR system and the overwhelming advantages of this technology.

    Evaluated new technologies for application at NASA-Lewis (wireless LAN, mobile data terminals, etc.). Represented NASA-Lewis at the annual NASA Spectrum Manager's conference, providing written and oral presentations.

  • Electrical Engineer

    (Public Company; 501-1000 employees; Defense & Space industry)

    Served as a research & development design engineer, field engineer (domestic and international), project engineer, qualification engineer, systems engineer and program management staff member for various complex military communication projects (HF, UHF, SHF & EHF, 3 MHz to 42 GHz).

    Performed board level design of RF, microwave, digital and analog circuitry. Extensive knowledge gained in transmitter, receiver & synthesizer design, frequency hopping and direct sequence spread spectrum techniques and power distribution.
