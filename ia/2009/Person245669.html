Warren Farrell’s Summary

I began my research on gender issues in the ‘60s. My first book, The Liberated Man, was published in 1974. By the ‘80s, I began noticing that men were feeling misrepresented, and my award-winning national best-seller, Why Men Are The Way They Are, was written to answer women’s questions about men in a way that rings true for men. The New York Post calls it "the most important book ever written about love, sex, and intimacy."

By the ‘90s, I felt the misunderstandings about men had deepened and become dangerous to the survival of families and love. I confronted the misunderstandings head-on with the award-winning The Myth of Male Power.

By the turn of the century I wanted to provide the sexes with the tools to communicate-- in particular to hear personal criticism from a loved one, especially when given badly. That was the take-off point for Women Can’t Hear What Men Don’t Say, a selection of the Book-of-the-Month Club. By 2001 I completed research I had been working on for 13 years on the conditions under which children of divorce are most likely to be raised successfully. That book, Father and Child Reunion, has renewed the commitment of many dads to be with their children, and its research has helped judges understand the importance of dads.

Warren Farrell’s Specialties:

I have appeared on over 1000 TV and radio shows, and been interviewed frequently by Oprah and Barbara, and by Larry King and Peter Jennings. I have been featured repeatedly on 20/20 and in The New York Times, in People and on Real People, in men's journals and The Wall Street Journal, and on the Today Show, the Tomorrow Show, and even To Tell The Truth.
