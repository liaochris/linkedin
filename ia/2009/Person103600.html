Andrew Jeffery’s Summary

Andrew worked in the mortgage business from 2002-2007, first for an independent warehouse bank then running the warehouse lending group at a small investment first specializing in securitizing subprime mortgages. The warehouse lending business is smack in the middle of the origination/securitization process, and from this perch he was uniquely positioned to watch the boom and bust of the residential mortgage market.

Last year, he left New York last year to strike out on his own and co-founded Cirios Real Estate with Ryan Taylor and Austin Nelson. Cirios is a boutique real estate brokerage offering property valuations and other real estate analytics to distressed mortgage investors around the country, as well as value added brokerage services to first time home buyers in the San Francisco Bay Area.

Andrew has always loved writing, and in the beginning of 2008 started producing original content for the financial education website, Minyanville.com. In the past year, since he founded Cirios, he has written over 250 articles for Minyanville on a freelance basis. He writes a weekly real estate column, “Keepin’ It Real Estate,” which centers around real estate and mortgage-related subjects, in addition to covering general financial market and economic news. He is particularly interested in the social and demographic trends developing alongside our current economic situation.

Andrew recently completed his first freelance commission for Housing Wire's monthly magazine. His work has been cited in the Wall Street Journal, the Huffington Post and syndicated to Bloomberg, Yahoo Finance, MSN, AOL and Dow Jones.
