Rosa Cortes’s Summary

I am actively seeking a new professional challenge, and I bring the following qualities and skills:

Publications record: In the past three years, I’ve written and published two papers (one as first author, based on my graduate work) and one book chapter. I made substantial writing contributions to two other manuscripts.

Effective time-management skills: I took on the role of project manager for each publication and prepared each submission from figure design to final submission, without compromising my experiment and data analysis schedule as a Senior Research Associate. Only 10-20% of my time was allotted to medical writing; I made substantial writing contributions to my company in light of such a limited schedule.

Leadership, initiative, and teamwork: I volunteered to write manuscripts and experimental protocols and also to provide editing and software technical assistance for co-workers preparing posters. No task is too small if it needs to get done: I volunteered to update the company’s chemical inventory database, helping to achieve compliance with local regulations. In graduate school, I led a training session on how to use a new imaging acquisition and analysis software. I am able to work independently but also to take direction.

Solid background in biomedical research: I trained in biochemical research in my undergraduate studies and expanded my knowledge base with coursework in immunology and cancer biology. I specialized in neuroscience in graduate school with technical expertise in biochemistry, molecular biology, and electrophysiology.

Personality traits: I am meticulous, focused, detail-oriented, and I have the flexibility and ability to multitask that is necessary to accommodate shifts in company priorities. I have a great capacity for learning and growth. I am a dedicated and conscientious worker and my goal is to excel in whatever I do.
