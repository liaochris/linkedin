Ted Kartzman’s Summary

Ted spent the past decade immersed in the independent music industry. He has been at the forefront of applying technology to help level the playing field for independent artists and labels, helping them connect with fans worldwide.

Ted’s roots in the music business began in 1998 when he co-founded JamBase.com, a web site dedicated to providing musicians and their fans with a grassroots community for finding artists on tour. Ted built the business, growing key contacts through band/venue/promoter/label relationships as President of JamBase.

Ted then learned all aspects of artist management while managing avant-rock trio The Slip. The most valuable lesson was to learn one aspect of the music business and do it well. Ted set his sights on knowing all aspects of digital music distribution.

In 2004 Ted gained a deeper understanding of the digital music licensing landscape while at Rhapsody, a joint venture of RealNetworks, Inc and MTV. As Director of Independent Label Business at Rhapsody, Ted was responsible for managing more than 400 independent label and artist partnerships. Ted was also responsible for overseeing the ingestion, promotion, and legal contract processes for the five million songs from thousands of independent labels available through Rhapsody.

At Rhapsody, Ted developed a series of corporate sponsored live performance events (6 "Rhapsody Rocks" shows), recording and capturing exclusive live audio/video content in a private party setting, then selling the audio through Rhapsody.

Ted first became a fan of IODA in his role as Chairman of the Board of Directors at reapandsow, a marketing and distribution company for independent artists, which has a partnership with IODA for digital distribution.

In April of 2008, Ted joined the IODA team as Vice President of Client Services, bringing both a unique client perspective and an intimate understanding of the benefits IODA delivers.
