Glen Gatin EdD’s Summary

Formerly employed in the Canadian community college system, teaching and developing vocational and technical programs.

Taught college instructors as part of a Certificate program in Adult and Vocational Education, which introduced college teachers to web-based tools and computer-mediated instruction.

Consultant for Romania education capacity-building initiative sponsored by World Bank.

As the interim director of a distributed education initiative offered by a consortium of traditional, publicly funded universities and colleges, I acquired a unique insight into the workings of higher education. Institutions face great challenges in the transition from traditional modes of delivery.

Developed and delivered a course for a graduate M.Ed. program. This ongoing course promotes ICT literacy for K-12 teachers and administrators using web-based tools for teaching and learning and action research into the effectiveness of the same.

Goals:
Seeking employment and/or contracts in higher education.

My doctoral studies specializations are in the Grounded Theory Method (GTM) and Media Studies. GTM accommodates the use of quantitative and qualitative data and discovers mid-range theories respecting patterns of social behavior. Such theories are incorporated into applied programs to direct social action.

In addition to academic research, I am interested in the use of the GTM in non-academic, applied settings, concentrating on human behavior patterns in virtual professional and business networks.

Action research using GTM can be used to identify and address social and behavioral impediments to the productive adoption of technology in web-based information and new media communication networks.
