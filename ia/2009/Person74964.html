  • Senior Project Manager

    Turner Construction

    (Privately Held; Construction industry)

    September 1994 — February 2002 (7 years 6 months)

    For over 7 years I was integrally involved in the K-12 market for Turner Construction's Chicago business unit. In 1994, it was in it's infancy. Over the next 5 years the K-12 market grew to about one third of Turner-Chicago's managed construction volume.

    Growth resulted from my involvement in creating a systematic delivery system for the publicly bid projects that we managed. Customer satisfaction, great client relationships and testimonials regarding the quality of service lead us to securing most of the K-12 in the "North Shore Communities."

    The relationship ran deep into the trade contractor community as well. I picked up a "following" of those trade contractors that wanted to work with us because of our fair management and project delivery systems on the projects they were involved with.

    I was personally involved with 12 school districts, representing 42 projects prior to being assigned to K-12 business development in 1998.

  • Project Manager

    C.R. Meyer and Sons Construction

    (Construction industry)

    September 1991 — September 1994 (3 years 1 month)

    At an exciting transition in my career, I was fortunate to work for a "real" design build contractor. The designers were at the other end of the hallway. I was involved with 200,000 square foot manufacturing facility that was designed "in house."

    The facility, a pre-engineered building, went from breaking ground to a roof overhead in 67 days. The Owner needed to move in, as their old lease was ending soon.

    The ability to work closely with the designers made it both fun and challenging. We put the entire $4M facility in place and had the owner operational in 90 days.

    The design build delivery method and this experience provided me with the opportunity to learn, and to find new ways to accomplish the goals required by our difficult project schedule.

    In the course of this project I also helped the company institute a "standard subcontract" for use throughout the company.

  • Project Coordinator

    The Lathrop Company Construction

    (Construction industry)

    September 1986 — September 1991 (5 years 1 month)

    The Lathrop Company provided me the opportunity to transition from Engineering Design into the world of Commercial General Contracting.

    During my time at The Lathrop Company I gained a great understanding of the project control systems and tools that are utilized to keep a construction project on schedule and running smoothly.

  • Structural Design Engineer

    Consulting Engineering - Toledo

    (Design industry)

    September 1982 — September 1986 (4 years 1 month)

    With a B.S. In Civil Engineering, focused in Structural Design, the first years of my career were spent designing concrete and steel structures.

    A good portion of my experience involved structural modifications and building additions for the Jeep Corporation. All of that work was at the historic Willy's Jeep Overland facility in Toledo, Ohio. The facility is no longer there, but the design experience was invaluable.

    Other design opportunities involved temporary shoring and support structures for commercial general contractors. Project specific shoring or piling systems were designed, drawn, and inspected in accordance with OSHA safety standards.

    Finally, design of miscellaneous structural supports and analysis of structural modifications in manufacturing facilities rounded out my design experience.
