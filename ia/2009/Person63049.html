Sridhar Dhulipala’s Summary

80% of work experience has been in the software application development services industry with a focus on requirements management. Prior work includes brand related work in retail and some luggage design. Interested in conceptual approaches in software (IT) design and development.

Sridhar Dhulipala’s Specialties:

user requirements modeling, design of collaborative user interfaces, software application architecture conceptualization, integrating and managing design activities

Sridhar Dhulipala’s Experience

  • Principal UI Designer & Senior Technical Architect

    Infosys Technologies Ltd

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; INFY; Information Technology and Services industry)

    October 1995 — September 2006 (11 years)

    Started off to 'jazz up' the UI, which I didnt know how to do then, but I promised to give it a shot, and worked at it for nine years. Thereafter, got slightly disenchanted with the UI bit and got into conceptualizing around technology, leapfrogging boldly the programming fundamentals stage. Dug into this for next 3 years, mostly in software engineering requirements management and human collaboration issues. I think Animators and IT Architects are two similar creative professions; you write/sketch and press play to experience your creation, at least conceptually. Perhaps, cooking food is the next most creative thing to do.

  • Deputy Manager

    BPL Limited

    (Consumer Electronics industry)

    October 1993 — September 1995 (2 years)

    Mostly involved in brand promotion activities at retail outlets and exhibitions. My interest in furniture got me there, initially. I assumed a marketing department that spends a few million on in store furniture is like a great opportunity to create furniture with graphics like it is some pop stuff. As a designer with attitude, quickly, learned to tuck my shirt, wear a tie and be corporate. Also, realized that design thinking works well with 'multidisciplinarity'.

  • Design trainee

    VIP Limited

    (Plastics industry)

    June 1991 — March 1992 (10 months)

    Industrial design of molded and soft luggage. Thought I would change the typical Indian train traveler's luggage dressing. Instead learned what it is like to do a job (my first) and how putting in energy and hard work can lead to results.
