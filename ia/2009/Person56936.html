David Brickley’s Education

  • Art Center College of Design

    BFA (Honors), Communication Design, 1985 — 1987

    Art Center is where I had my first "hands-on" experience with computer graphics (animation of any kind came later). I was among the first to sign up for a new set of computer graphics classes - way before it became an actual department. By today's standards, the computers were VERY, primitive. The first "graphic" I created was a 320x240 pixel image that had all the 4 possible colors the system was capable of displaying - with black and white being 2 of them. So I was there from the start of creating graphic anything on a desktop PC - a very humble beginning.

    With newer software, the classes got more interesting, and doing anything with computer graphics was facinating enough to keep me in the classroom, sitting on one of those hard stools, through the night, working on assignments and my own stuff, oblivious of the time. I still have all those files, but they're "techo-orphans," incompatible file formats, nothing will show them. At least I shot pictures of them off the monitor.
