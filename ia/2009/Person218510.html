Siren Hakimi’s Experience

  • Consultant

    Sustainable Development & Planning

    (Renewables & Environment industry)

    July 2007 — Present (1 year 7 months)

    Facilitation of community feedback through various public meetings, and updating the City documents in 38 City Neighborhoods with a focus on sustainability and climate change for the City of Seattle. The first plans consistent with Light Rail Station openings being updated are; Beacon Hill, Mount Baker, Columbia City, Othello and Rainier Beach Stations. Member of USGBC LEED for Neighborhood Development Committee in providing strategic advice for pilot projects around the country. 703 Cedar Dr SE, Auburn, anticipation LEED Platinum by the United States Green Building Council (USGBC). The existing house is being modified to be demonstrated as a sustainability model within the community. 100% of rainwater is being collected and used to irrigate the garden. Minimal energy usage methods will be applied. Salvaged organic materials and resources will be utilized. Composting and recycling are being practiced via permaculture.

  • Associate Planner

    City of Sammamish

    (Government Administration industry)

    June 2006 — June 2007 (1 year 1 month)

    Reviewed and processed permits for environmental, residential and land use development proposals, reviewing parcel maps, site plans and building plans for compliance with appropriate city regulations. Worked with property owners and development community in providing appropriate guidance to meet City development regulations, assisted general public on zoning and land use related regulations. Compiled information on special studies, analysis and preparation of written land use decisions, coordinated project activities with other staff, city departments, county and state agencies, architects, engineers, developers, contractors. Provided assistance in Code amendment and ordinance development for low impact development. Educated the applicants and public regarding technical issues. Assisted in the City Town Center Development process as a facilitator in Charrettes at an ongoing basis. Upgraded City View documents to comply with Plat conditions.

  • Development Manager

    TDF Foundation

    (Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    1993 — 1998 (5 years)

    Assisted in the creation of public-private partnerships between landowners and municipalities through REIT mechanism for large scale long term urban development projects. Represented Department of Land and Building Ministry in behalf of TDF at NAREIT Annual Convention in Chicago in1997. Designed the conceptual site plan for Natureland in Antalia; accommodating 1500 people in a self sustained eco-village.
    Developed preliminary site plans for Hallhouse Project, England. Prepared development proposal on Hallhouse for Overseas Trade & Finance Ltd. Designed Hallhouse Project to accommodate a recreational center, an education center, residential and commercial units, situated on 150 acres of undeveloped land at Hawkhurst, 40 minutes south of London, with numerous public benefits, creating substantial investment returns for the local community.
