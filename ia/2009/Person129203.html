Keli Richmond’s Summary

Keli is a practicing speech-language pathologist for the Indiana School Systems. She specializes in early literacy development. Keli has a teaching license as well as degrees in speech-language pathology and audiology with a minor degree in special education.

With over 14 years experience in the speech and literacy disciplines, Keli began her career in the medical field with an emphasis on neurological disorders. Currently, she provides therapy and promotes literacy skills for preschool through middle school students.

Keli presents in-services for school based personnel and continuing education seminars nationwide for Northern Speech Services (NSS). She presents nationally at Speech-Language-Hearing Conventions and Educator Conferences.

Keli is a founding member of the United Way Women’s Initiative; the Women’s Initiative is an organization dedicated to closing educational gaps through early literacy development. She is associated with the Project Reads Program; the Project Reads Program provides intervention for students reading below grade-level expectations.

A highlight of Keli’s career is receiving the Indiana Speech-Language-Hearing Association Professional Achievement Award in Recognition of Advancement of Knowledge in Clinical Practice. The award is based on outstanding professional development and community involvement.

Keli is the author of Literacy Speaks!® Kit 1 (Early Developing Sounds) and Literacy Speaks!® Kit 2 (Later Developing Sounds). She is the co-author of Kit 3 (Vowels). The publication of Literacy Speaks!® Kit 4 (j, qu, Vocalic r, Prevocalic r, x, y) is forecast for 2010. Literacy Speaks!® is a comprehensive program designed to improve speech intelligibility and language skills while establishing a strong literacy foundation.
