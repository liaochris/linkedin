Jonathan Leess’s Summary

Jonathan is a digital media veteran known for his strategic and operational expertise across all aspects of the media business. A unique executive successful at 'bridging the gaps' between traditional and new media businesses for over 15 years.

As President & General Manager of the CBS Television Stations Digital Media Division at CBS Inc, Jonathan was charged with the strategic oversight and general management of all digital media business for 29 CBS owned and operated television stations. He created and led a digital media division overseeing the content, distribution, ad sales, technology, research, product development, business development and digital strategies for CBS's broadband, Internet and mobile platforms.

Jonathan co-founded and managed the Enhanced TV division for The Walt Disney Company and the ABC Television Network’s venture into the new media convergence business as Senior Vice President and General Manager, launching some of the first-ever live, prime time television interactive programming applications for the BCS Championship Game in 1999, Who Wants to Be A Millionaire, Super Bowl XXXIV and the presidential election coverage on ABC in 2000.

Leess spearheaded ABC Sports’ expansion into online and interactive programming, creating and managing the original division responsible for the development and distribution of all internet based programming at ABC Sports. Leess pioneered convergent programming with live television, prompting TV viewers to interact while watching TV.His division was also responsible for creating and managing ABC Sports, NFL, Nascar, Monday Night Football, and College Football content areas for America Online.

Leess is currently founder and president of DoubleVision Media LLC, a digital media consulting firm providing strategic, operational, audience and revenue growth plans for traditional, digital, wireless, social networking and content production media companies.
