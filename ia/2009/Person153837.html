Martin J.D. Otis’s Summary

Martin J.D. Otis has previously worked with the Laval University Aerospace Team (GAUL) for the design and the development of a high power metallic airframe experimental rocket with hybrid engines (polymer+NOx). He has worked as the manager of this multidisciplinary team for two years. However, its principal interest concerns biomedical applications where robotic systems can help physicians or practicians in the rehabilitation field. Its research and development projects have covered control and mechanical design of an hexapod BioMicroRobotic (BMR) including Central Pattern Generator (CPG) implemented on FPGA, with the GRAMS (Groupe de Recherche en Appareillage Médical de Sherbrooke) team at University of Sherbrooke for its implantation inside human body. He is currently a Ph.D. student at the Computer Vision and Systems Laboratory (CVSL) in the Dept. of Electrical and Computer Engineering and Robotic Laboratory in the Dept. of Mechanical Engineering, Laval University. The current works are about the intergation of a locomotion interface as a system engineer position for walking rehabilitation and haptic in a virtual world for gait analysis, video gaming, or real-time visioconferencing.
