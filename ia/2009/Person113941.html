Joan Krajewski’s Summary

Joan Krajewski is a business leader and manager of global product environmental and safety compliance, sustainability, and supply chain social and environmental accountability programs. Her former career as an attorney spans private practice as a partner representing energy, transportation and real estate clients and work in-house at Microsoft Corporation, American Airlines, Inc. and Pacific Gas & Electric Company.

Joan participates in and leads industry activities that seek to expand the sustainability potential of electronic and software products. Her work is featured in the book "Saving the World at Work" by Tim Sanders. Most recently, she chaired the Steering Committee that sponsored the SMART 2020 - U.S. Addendum Report (available at www.GeSI.org), a groundbreaking analysis of information and communication technology's ability to enable the U.S. transformation to a low carbon economy. Joan also serves on the International Program Committee for CARE Innovations 2010 (Vienna, Austria), the world's largest symposium devoted to the creation of green electronics. In 2005, she was elected to the Fellows of the American Bar Association for service to the legal community.

Joan Krajewski’s Specialties:

Global environmental and product safety regulatory and administrative expertise; environmental and safety systems; related transactions; sustainability programs and development; supply chain social and environmental accountability.

Joan Krajewski’s Experience

  • Sr. Director of Compliance and Safety

    Microsoft Corporation

    (Public Company; MSFT; Computer Software industry)

    October 2005 — Present (3 years 11 months)

    Joan Krajewski is responsible for establishing and managing multi-national policies and programs across numerous electronic product and packaging lines in Entertainment and Devices for environmental, electromagnetic compatibility, radio frequency and product safety compliance; championing the Environmental Management System; and counseling business groups on domestic and international environmental and product safety regulatory requirements, operating procedures, administrative actions, and transactions. She manages international teams of compliance engineering specialists and auditors, responsible for supporting these programs in EMEA, Asia, and the Americas.

    She also directs global product sustainability through Supply Chain Social and Environmental Accountability and Design for Green programs and serves on the Board of the Global e-Sustainability Initiative (GeSI), a global partnership of ICT companies that promotes technologies for sustainable development.
