Kim Burgess’s Summary

An eclectic fusion of software developer, sound designer, audio engineer, user interface and user experience designer, sound recordist, AV geek and code monkey Kim Burgess is multimedia professional who will also occasionally talk about himself in third person.

Growing up thoroughly immersed in the entertainment and media industries he's been involved in events and productions such as the Woodford Folk Festival, Rove Live, Parklife, Marilyn Manson, Australia's Funniest Home Video's, Brisbane International Film Festival, Children of the Swarm and various other events and festivals. He's spent time as an event coordinator, audio and lighting engineer, AV technician and also did his time as a rodie.

After realizing it was possible to combine a passion for software development with his love of audio he went on to study fine arts at the Queenland University of Technology majoring in sound design and submajoring in computational arts. During this time he became involved in the world of interactive audio and worked creating immersive aural environments for computer games in addition to creating interactive audio art installations.

He now combines his knowledge and experience from these disciplines and mixes it with a little bit of computer geekiness to create interactive media environments and clean, efficient, invititing and intuitive AV control systems.

Kim Burgess’s Specialties:

AMX programming, user interface and user experience design, DSP programming and system tuning, sound design, interactive and immersive audio, music production, game development, web design, XHTML, CSS, PHP and JS programming, live audio, lighting design and operation.
