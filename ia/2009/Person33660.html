  • Director, Interactive Technology

    (Privately Held; Information Technology and Services industry)

  • Public Affairs Chief

    Combined Joint Task Force - Horn of Africa

    (Military industry)

    March 2005 — March 2006 (1 year 1 month)

    As a U.S. Marine, I served as senior enlisted advisor to the Director of Public Affairs on best practices in campaign marketing, media and public relations. My responsibilities involved arranging day-to-day operations across an 11-nation area around Somalia; to include coordination with foreign-national governments, U.S. Department of State, and other agencies. I was counted on to facilitate the development of communications strategy – expanding media efforts to garner domestic and international support. As a combat correspondent, I placed photos and print collateral into hundreds of publications worldwide while tracking media absorption and reaction to that collateral. I was also tasked with the design and development of a W3C and 508 conformant, multi-lingual (Arabic, Amharic, Somali and French) website content management system (developing in ASP.NET, MSSQL, HTML, CSS, JavaScript) to drive both public web properties as well as automate news releases to local (African and Middle Eastern) as well as U.S. media outlets. Finally, during this tour I received the Joint Service Commendation Medal for “Exceptionally Meritorious Service” for actions taken during a dual helicopter crash recovery mission in February 2006. At the end of this tour I was honorably discharged – with no further military commitment.

  • Convoy Security Augmentee

    Provisional Combat Augmentee, USMC

    (Military industry)

    February 2003 — August 2003 (7 months)

    As squad leader, I served as assistant platoon sergeant and was responsible for training and administrative oversight of 38 U.S. Marines during Operation Iraqi Freedom. The unit was assigned daily duties throughout Kuwait and Iraq alongside third party military units to relieve operational strain caused by combat casualties. As an augmentee to those units, I assisted in providing combat security for Humanitarian Aid missions across Southern Iraq as well as providing personal security for diplomats and contractors assessing war torn areas in Iraq for possible relief missions.

  • Community Relations Chief

    United States Marine Corps

    (Government Agency; USMC; Military industry)

    May 1997 — June 2003 (6 years 2 months)

    As the Community Relations Chief, I assisted the Public Affairs Director in improving community relations in Fairfax and Prince William counties through the coordination of outreach events and community improvement projects – resulting in a significant year-over-year decrease in noise complaints as well as an increase in local military recruiting. Duties included working to resolve issues where possible through town hall meetings and community involvement. As Chief Webmaster, I co-authored the first comprehensive official military instruction for the upkeep of public facing websites in response to the creation of the Section 508 Amendment to the Rehabilitation Act of 1973 – and worked as part of a group tasked with reorganizing the Marine’s online presence to better align with the recruiting marketing efforts of J.W. Thompson. During this time I developed a dynamic content management system (ASP, Access, DHTML) to maintain three 508 compliant public websites for the distribution of news, photo and video to the community and national media. Additionally, I received the Navy and Marine Corps Achievement Medal for design and development of the Marine Corps Operational Test and Evaluation intranet site, which streamlined the sharing of test results among contractors and military advisors bringing new technology to the fleet.
