Amy C. Verel, Associate ASLA’s Summary

Hard-working, team-oriented professional with diverse design, analytical, communication and people skills developed in a variety of work environments in the public and private sectors.

My career as a landscape architectural designer and urban planner began with an interest in the human response to different physical environments, and is focused on improving the built environment from the intersection of planning and design.

Design experience includes work on all phases of waterfront, streetscape, recreational, tree preservation, courtyard and planting design in residential, public and institutional settings.

Planning experience includes transportation-oriented development, downtown branding, community, comprehensive, historic preservation, corridor, environmental assessment/impact, and energy conservation/climate action planning.

Experienced and adept at participating in charrette-style public meetings and guiding group discussions/guiding small table interaction in design and planning processes.

Creative and passionate about utilizing technology to streamline and enhance all stages of the creative and production processes of design and planning. Dedicated to environmental sustainability in my professional and home life.

Actively involved with the Illinois Chapter of The American Society of Landscape Architects and Chicago Women in Planning + Development.

Current professional goals are:
- Completing and defending MLA Master's Project in September 2009
- RLA licensure
- AICP certification
- LEED accreditation
- Expanding leadership and project management skills

Amy C. Verel, Associate ASLA’s Specialties:

Hand drafting and rendering, research and technical writing, AutoCAD 2007, Adobe Creative Suite CS2 (Photoshop, Illustrator, InDesign, Acrobat & GoLive), ArcGIS, Google SketchUp Pro, Macromedia Dreamweaver, and Microsoft Office.
