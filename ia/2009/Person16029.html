Angie Pascale’s Summary

My current position keeps me extremely busy with various tasks: writing and editing internal and client-side documents (websites, articles, press releases, sales proposals, case studies, white papers, ad copy, etc.); managing marketing and PR efforts (social media, award entries, speaker proposals, philanthropy, print advertisements, conference exhibition organization, search engine marketing, email marketing, media relations, company newsletter, marketing collateral, etc.); organizing multivariate landing page experiments through Google Website Optimizer; helping to organize client website design and creative projects; and much, much more. I’ve written case studies for campaigns that won numerous awards: 2007 & 2008 ECHO Award, 2008 SES Award, 2007 & 2008 AMA Peak Awards and 2007 BMA Gold Key Award. I am very active with a number of organizations: AMA (currently on event planning committee), SEMPO (helped to found local chapter and current secretary), PRSA and New Denver Ad Club. I helped the company become WOACs and presented a case study at Google’s headquarters last November.

Prior to joining Location3, I was an account manager at Marich Communications, a small public and media relations firm in Los Angeles. My time at this company, although short, afforded me many opportunities to gain experience. Because we were small, I handled every aspect of client campaigns—pitching newspapers, magazines, TV shows and radio programs; organizing schedules for on-air spots and articles; writing press releases; creating press kits; writing articles for and about clients; and planning promotional events.

I have also dabbled in random writing, editing and proofreading positions over the years. At one of the less glamorous points in my career, I even proofread rubber stamps! It was somewhat dull, but allowed me to exercise my eagle eye for grammar and spelling mistakes. And I got a pretty cool “Angie” stamp out of it!
