  • Owner/Director

    Pico Computing Services, Lusaka, Zambia

    (Information Technology and Services industry)

    1994 — 1997 (3 years)

    Self-employed as local computing consultant. Hired to provide in-house training and software installation/support services to organisations and private individuals. Organisations that made regular use of the Pico teaching/installation services in Lusaka included: Lutheran Church of Central Africa and Catholic Secretariat – Yatsani Studio, Friedrich Ebert Stiftung, Embassy of Finland and Asif Electronics.
    One main project included an assignment for the Zambian National Institute of Public Administration (N.I.P.A.): contracted to design, set-up and program a system for the Zambian Agricultural Research and Evaluation Project (ZAREP), which was affiliated to the Zambian Ministry of Agriculture.
    The aim of the “Training & Visit (T&V) Database” was to gather and process relevant agricultural data with regards to the types and numbers of different crops which were produced in the various Zambian agricultural districts. The T&V Database also organised statistics on the types and numbers of agricultural officers that had received training and/or visits from senior ZAREP Head Quarter officers. Relevant statistics could be printed in a presentable format in various standard reports. The system programs were written in dBaseIV code.
    Another main project included the development of a tailor-made Herdbook database application in Access 2.0 for Grasmere Farm.
    This database had been designed to calculate calf performance indexes per farmer and per breed. It has been put into use to improve the administration of the Herdbook Society of Zambia.
    Later on the Pico services extended to developing and maintaining web-pages for both the Embassy of Finland and Taiwo Car Hire in Lusaka (Z).
