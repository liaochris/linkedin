Diane Levin’s Summary

As a mediator, I work with litigants and private clients to resolve disputes involving business, tort, real estate, and family/family business issues. In tough economic times, talking things out can save not just money but also strategic relationships.

An experienced trainer and coach for over 12 years, I have taught thousands of people to resolve conflict, negotiate better, or become mediators -- from lawyers and judges from Eastern Europe through the US Agency for International Development to Fortune 500 executives.

My profession of origin is the law: as an attorney I practiced tort, municipal, labor/employment, and probate law for both sides of the legal aisle. It gave me an appreciation for seeing things from all sides. I'm also an avid experimenter when it comes to digital technology. I have been blogging for four years and advise dispute resolution professionals on social media, web site design and web strategy.

Writing is an important component of my professional life, allowing me to creatively explore and share ideas relating to the resolution of disputes or negotiating business and interpersonal issues. Together with three colleagues, I write an ADR column in The Complete Lawyer, and publish the award-winning blog MediationChannel.com.

Public service matters, and I am committed to finding ways to support the administration of justice or raise public awareness of alternatives to litigation for the amicable resolution of disputes. I have served on several non-profit boards of directors of organizations serving the needs of communities large and small, including four years as an officer and director of the Association for Conflict Resolution's New England Chapter. I am a past member of the Massachusetts Trial Court Standing Committee on Dispute Resolution, which I continue to advise on digital technology and the web, and was recently appointed a fellow of the Massachusetts Bar Foundation, where I serve on the IOLTA Grant Advisory Committee.
