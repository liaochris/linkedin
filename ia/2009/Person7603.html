Alexander WEIR’s Summary

I was born in the UK and graduated with a Politics BA from Hull University. I then joined the Metropolitan Police and enjoyed ten years serving in London, working primarily in central London, first as a Home Beat Officer and then running a Divisional Intelligence and Information Unit (DIIU) where I was responsible for Criminal Intelligence Analysis using the Anacapa system. I then moved to Vienna where I worked for ORF (Blue Danube Radio) and ITN (Channel Four Daily) as a News Editor/Broadcaster/Stringer covering first the collapse of the Berlin Wall and then the conflicts in Slovenia, Croatia and BiH, resulting from the breakup of the former Yugoslavia. I was then offered the chance to work in the Gulf and moved to Bahrain, where I worked for the Ministry of Information and Dow Jones Telerate to establish the annual Gulf Economic Forum Conference and Seminars. In 1998 I returned to Europe to work for the European Commission DG Enterprise to develop a pilot project for the support of Intellectual and Industrial Property Rights targeted at the EU Research Community & SME's. This project was the IPR-Helpdesk and since 2004 I have managed a number of projects broadly related to Technology Transfer & Intellectual and Industrial Property Rights issues for the EC, WIPO, USPTO, SIPO and others. I have worked as a Consultant dealing with concept develoment and proposal writing, Knowledge Management issues, ISO 9001 Quality Assurance issues, IIP Asset Management support, IIP Training & Enforcement issues. Recent projects have been concluded in China, South Africa, Argentina, Russia and the CIS, the EU and the Phillipines.

Alexander WEIR’s Specialties:

Concept development and proposal writing, Knowledge Management, Project Management, ISO 9001 Quality Assurance, IIP Asset Management, IIP Training & Enforcement issues, Analysis using the Anacapa system.
