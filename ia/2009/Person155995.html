Matthew Mishory’s Summary

I am a filmmaker, writer, and media professional based in Los Angeles. My chief pursuits currently are feature films and new media ad campaigns benefiting progressive causes and candidates.

I studied film theory and screenwriting at the University of California, Santa Barbara, where I was a Regent Scholar and Golden Reel Award recipient. My writings on film criticism and international cinema history have been published in the Focus Media Journal, housed at MOMA, and in Glitterati Magazine.

My short film Nick and Kate screened in competition at Filminute 2008, one of twenty-five selections culled from a field of two thousand by a jury that included Oscar winner Paul Haggis and German film critic Andrea Dittgen. I recently collaborated with television star Erin Daniels on the film Sunday Afternoons and directed the commissioned marriage equality piece The Marionettes, shot entirely with miniatures.

My current film, the short format Delphinium: A Childhood Portrait of Derek Jarman, now in post-production, explores the work and life of legendary iconoclast Derek Jarman. The film was shot in Los Angeles, London, and at Jarman's Prospect Cottage estate in Dungeness, Kent.

My next project is the feature film Portland, starring Jonathan Caouette (Tarnation) and Erin Daniels (The L Word). It was set to be the late screen legend David Carradine's next film; our producing team is currently recasting his pivotal role.

I often collaborate with and have been photographed by London-based cinematographer/photo-artist Lili Wilde. Bringing a stylized sensibility to narrative filmmaking, I work primarily in mixed media, combining saturated super8, 16mm, and high-definition video.

I also hold a Juris Doctor from Loyola Law School and am active in progressive political causes. I contributed Public Service Announcements to the No On Prop 8 effort and have written articles about such diverse subjects as aid-in-dying and the effect of obscenity laws on transgressive art.
