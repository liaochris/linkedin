Carolyn Anderson’s Summary

I am a bipolar disorder survivor and have many friends who have it too, as well as some with schizophrenia and alcohol depression. I have seen at first hand the dreadful treatment they have received not only in psychiatric hospitals but after they have been discharged. The benefits system in the UK is so fragmented that it takes a genius to negotiate through the system. The image of mental health in this country has improved a little mostly due to coverage in the media but it has a long way to go. I am launching a campaign to try to highlight the problems which psychiatric patients encounter in the UK.

I worked as Education Officer for a professional body for book-keepers. I discovered that the Russians had to come onto the International Standards and that they didn't even have the first clue about basic book-keeping. I liaised with a Russian working in this country who had a wealth of information and wonderful contacts. I travelled to Moscow and St Petersburg and set up all that was necessary to kickstart this project. I then approached Sir Peter Kemp who agreed very kindly to give me funding to bring professors over to London and I organised courses for them to learn basic book-keeping (as well as enjoying nights out on the town and shopping expeditions!) so that they could then go back to their cities and set up these courses for their accountants. All in all, it was a marvellous experience which took me to the Kremlin in Moscow with my Chairman who had been invited to give a speech (I had to write it for him!) . I was then invited to Ekateringburg to give my very own speech (my first!) in their Kremlin. I also enjoyed the wonderful hospitality the Russians showed me in Moscow, St Petersburg and Ekateringburg and the wonderful churches, palaces, art galleries, ballets etc they took me to. Altogether an amazing experience and one that I am not likely to forget.

Carolyn Anderson’s Specialties:

PR Skills to bring about a huge shift in the image of how mental illness is perceived, particularly bipolar disorder. I also have a great many other interests e.g. the persecution of christians worldwide .. I am currently putting the final touches to a campaign to highlight the very serious problems in the psychiatric services and to try to educate the general public that mental illness is just that - an illness like cancer of heart disease and requires professional treatment.
