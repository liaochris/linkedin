Alan Silberman’s Summary

Alan Silberman has a wide experience on both sides of the technology counter: technology vendors and technology users. He has worked for both local and international companies, with regional responsibilities. He speaks English, Spanish and German and has an excellent academic background, with degrees in Information Technology and Economics, as well as published papers and one book.

He delivered results at companies in different industries (manufacturing, financial services, health, education, consulting, software) in positions in both information technology and software development areas, leading teams successfully and covering business requirements with information technology solutions.

Other skills that describe him are:

Managerial skills: people leadership, development and motivation, teamwork, decision-making, business problem-solving, organization, delegation, opportunities and threats detection, risk-taking.

Interpersonal skills: effective communication, persuasion and influence, conflict management, negotiation, empathy, global and multi-cultural conscience.

Personal effectiveness skills: integrity, coherence, self-control, self-confidence, self-determination, self-development, self-motivation, resiliency, flexibility, change adaptation, multi-tasking, pragmatism, cooperation, participative.

Cognitive skills: analysis and synthesis capability, seniority, concept building, creativity, innovation, research, academic background.

Strategy skills: strategic vision, business acumen, change management, business partnership, industry and market knowledge, organizational alignment and awareness, strategic business development, business relationship building.

Achievement skills: results orientation, customer orientation, quality orientation, initiative, proactivity, commitment, working to deadlines, information search.
