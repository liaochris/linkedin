Phill Provis’s Experience

  • Enterprise Architect & SOA Governance Leader

    GE Capital

    (Public Company; GE; Financial Services industry)

    October 2006 — Present (3 years 1 month)

    Appointed to deliver an enterprise architectural roadmap to integrate and simplify the global systems landscape. Results: The loose coupling & interoperability of the SOA concept simplified access from the 50+ businesses and reduced the number of interfaces dramatically

    Delivered messaging and framework standards to unite and replace a myriad of local variations. Results: Within 2 years over 45 global services were defined based on the Interactive Financial eXchange (IFX) banking domain standards, extended to cover the Financial Services aspects previously lacking. As a Working Group chairman for Card Products this helped GE drive its own requirements into the standards.

    The governance of the service life cycle was a major component in being able, not just to deliver the global services, but also localised variants. This meant that individual business units and regional headquarters can easily extend and build using the global standards and technology implementations. Results: Service reuse is a reality, business units have implemented local and regional Enterprise Service Bus’ (ESB’s) and integrated into the global ESB

    Developed a governance toolset solution that encompassed code repositories, (UDDI) repository and code quality tools.

    Developed and launched enterprise wide certification standards and processes that allow localisation without compromising the shared service environment or overall standards.

    Established and ran a global Architectural Council to share ideas, design issues and knowledge. Result: Items in the technology stack are refreshed regularly, standards extended and a push to Open Source commenced. The Open Source GlassFish ESB will be implemented in production October 2009
