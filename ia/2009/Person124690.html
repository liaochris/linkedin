Justin Gray’s Summary

I am an associate with Foley & Lardner LLP, where I focus on patent litigation.

Prior to joining the firm, I participated in Foley's summer associate program and was an extern for the Honorable Mark Filip, United States District Court for the Northern District of Illinois. I have also worked as an assistant webmaster for the University of Wisconsin Athletic Department, helping to integrate new technologies into the department's Website.

I obtained my bachelor's degree in computer science and mathematics from the University of Wisconsin - Madison (B.S., with distinction, 2003). I focused my studies on areas such as cryptography, combinatorics, database management systems, and artificial intelligence. My law degree was conferred by Northwestern University School of Law (J.D., cum laude, 2006), where I was a staff member and symposium editor for the Northwestern Journal of Technology and Intellectual Property. While in law school I was a member of the Northwestern National Trial Team.

I am also a member of the Richard Linn American Inn of Court and an adjunct professor at the Northwestern Unversity School of Law, where I teach Patent Law.

Publications:
