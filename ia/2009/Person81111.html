Frank E. Banks’s Experience

  • Zynga

    (Privately Held; Computer Games industry)

    November 2008 — Present (5 months)

  • musedot

    (Internet industry)

    May 2007 — Present (1 year 11 months)

    Musedot is a search engine that enables you to discover new music based on the music you already know. It is a cutting edge social data mining application that approaches the problem of how do you expose people to music they don’t know about but would like if they did, in an entirely unique and powerful way. I have been developing the underlying theories for about twelve years even as I went about my professional career.

    In May of 2007 I closed out my contract with InteliMark Enterprises in order to work on musedot fulltime.

  • Lab Nine Inc.

    (Internet industry)

    July 2002 — Present (6 years 9 months)

    Lab Nine Inc. is the organization under which I contracted out all independent Web developer services since 2002, and which is now the corporate entity behind musedot.com

  • InteliMark Enterprises LLC.

    (Internet industry)

    June 2004 — May 2007 (3 years)

    I joined a two man development team at InteliMark as a Senior-level ColdFusion developer to continue work on their highly successful main property, greataupair.com, the world’s largest online au pair/nannie matching service. In this position I was largely responsible for the implementation of all new features, the technical design and implementation of a new set of partner sites, and routine code maintenance. In addition to the usual technologies I was given the opportunity to work with both Flash and Flex ActionScript.

  • Sevin Rosen Funds

    (Partnership; 11-50 employees; Venture Capital & Private Equity industry)

    October 2001 — May 2007 (5 years 8 months)

    Sevin Rosen funds contracted with Lab Nine to provide CommonSpot content server installation, implementation and maintenance services. I have implemented custom ColdFusion add-on modules for data importation and user management, as well as basic technical support and training services.

  • PaperThin, Inc.

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Computer Software industry)

    May 2000 — December 2003 (3 years 8 months)

    In my long relationship with PaperThin I was called upon to perform a variety of tasks, but my main role was developing new features for the company’s flagship CommonSpot content server product, code maintenance, and customer support. I began working with the product following the release of version 1.5 and continued through the release cycle for version 4.0, with many service packs and point releases in between.

    In addition to my regular duties, for a brief period of approximately 6 months I provided PaperThin clients with on-site installation and training services. I traveled to customer sites in Ottawa, Canada, San Francisco, Washington D.C., Seattle, Virginia, etc.

  • vFive Digital Services

    (Internet industry)

    February 2000 — April 2000 (3 months)

    I designed and implemented the entire application infrastructure, including most of the database design for www.senditon.com, which was launched on 4/7/00. This innovative B2B marketing services application rewards Internet users for 'sending on' offers from B2C retailers to their friends and family. The application enables users to create hierarchies of affiliates, and users are rewarded if anyone in their hierarchy clicks on, or takes advantage of an offer.

  • SPSS

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; SPSS; Computer Software industry)

    August 1999 — January 2000 (6 months)

    I developed custom modifications to a retail e-commerce application built using AbleCommerce Builder 2.61, working directly with the source code. All modifications were implemented as custom tags and include support for EU VAT (Valued Added Tax), localization (multiple language and currency support), integration with a custom SPSS user registration system, and support for SPSS direct mail "special offers".

  • CDBlowout.com

    (Internet industry)

    November 1998 — December 1999 (1 year 2 months)

    In my role as CTO I customized third-party Cold Fusion auction software, including integration with custom back-end order tracking and fulfillment modules of my own design. All back-end code was also implemented in Cold Fusion. I was responsible for the administration and maintenance of the entire application infrastructure, including co-located servers running WinNT 4.0 with IIS 4.0 and SQL Server 6.5.

    I designed and implemented a completely custom business-to-consumer catalog, including extensive back-end order fulfillment/management, inventory management, and user management features. Some features of note included CyberCash integration for credit card processing, and extensive use of stored procedures for maximum performance. Administered and maintained entire application infrastructure including co-located servers running WinNT 4.0 with IIS 4.0 and SQL Server 6.5. The catalog was superseded by the auction and retired from operation.

  • ACEWeb Interactive, LLC.

    (Internet industry)

    July 1999 — September 1999 (3 months)

    I designed and implemented code for the Fasturn.com (beta) trading system. Implemented notification system that creates custom list of products for each customer based on user-provided preferences. I implemented back-end administration functions for managing company/customer accounts, a truly hierarchical product class system, and data export/download of account information.

  • Koeltz Scientific Books

    (Publishing industry)

    June 1996 — November 1998 (2 years 6 months)

    I supervised the original design and coding of this online catalog of scientific books targeted at professional academics. Implemented a great deal of back-end management features, including a mailing list system that provided users with personalized lists of new books based on past purchases and stated preferences. I was also responsible for maintaining and administering the site on both third-party hardware and my own dedicated server for 2.5 years.

  • MANMACHINE, Inc.

    (Internet industry)

    February 1997 — October 1998 (1 year 9 months)

    I conceived the overall design and architecture, and implemented 75% of the coding for the IGNITION Pro 1.0 commerce platform for Cold Fusion and 100% of development for versions through 1.2.x. IGNITION Pro is a complete Web e-commerce application tool built using Cold Fusion, SQL, HTML, and JavaScript. When originally released, it was one of only two commercially available e-commerce products for Cold Fusion. The product had about twenty licensees when it was acquired outright by StreamWorks.Net, LLC in 1998.

  • Intraversal Communications Corp.

    (Internet industry)

    November 1994 — December 1996 (2 years 2 months)

    I founded Intraversal with the goal of creating multi-media CD-ROMs for historically important musicians, starting with John Coltrane. Fortunately, I was first exposed to the Internet not long after creating the company and became convinced we should change our focus.

    With only grudging support from my business partner, who also supplied the funding, I managed to land our first client Koeltz Scientific Books. We not only created an e-commerce site for Mr. Koeltz's business but hosted and maintained it as well. It was through this project that I was introduced to ColdFusion, HTML, web server software, etc.

    I was convinced that the Internet, and e-commerce specifically, represented a huge and emerging opportunity, and that we should focus on building an off-the-shelf e-commerce solution for web developers. Unfortunately, my business partner simply did not agree which led me to leave the company and establish MANMACHINE to pursue the opportunity on my own.
