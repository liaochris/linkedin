Marlis Humphrey’s Summary

Marlis Humphrey is director of Strategic Marketing IPTV and Mobile TV for Harris Corporation’s Broadcast Communications Division. She is responsible for definition of the company’s offerings and go-to-market strategy.

Marlis joined Harris in 1999 as Corporate Director of Technology and Standards Planning and recently held the position of Director, Growth Programs and Commercial Segments Growth on the Corporate Technology & Development team.

Prior to joining Harris, Marlis was president of Vitreous Communications, Inc., a consulting firm focused on broadband access. At AT&T Paradyne, she was director of Business Development for Broadband Access Products and responsible for strategic planning for ATM Products. In 1996, Marlis worked at AT&T Bell Laboratories in planning for Broadband Multimedia Products and Services.

Marlis began her career in 1978 as a developer of retail and point of sale communications software with Carson Pirie Scott & Company. She joined AT&T–Paradyne Corporation in 1981 as a software engineer and held positions of increasing responsibility in engineering and product line management for wide area TDM, fast-packet and ATM products.

Ms. Humphrey is an active leader in national and international standards organizations. For eight years, she led The ATM Forum Board of Directors as chairman. She served as chairman of the North America Market Awareness Committee for the forum from 1994–1997. In addition, Ms. Humphrey serves as vice chairman of TIA TR-47 which is developing mobile TV standards. She has participated in the ATIS IPTV Exploratory Group, the ITU-T IPTV Focus Group, Broadband Content Delivery Forum, ANSI T1E1, the DSL Forum, the UAWG, the MMCF, ETSI TM6, ITU-T SG15, and the Frame Relay Forum. Within T1E1, she served as chair of the CAP/QAM RADSL Ad Hoc and editor of “TR59—September 1999—Single-Carrier Rate Adap¬tive Digital Subscriber Line (RADSL).”

She is an internationally noted speaker and author.

Marlis Humphrey’s Specialties:

New business and market development in the communications, media, and broadcast industries. Growth strategy development.
