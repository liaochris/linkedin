Kevin O'Hara’s Experience

  • Senior Vice President and General Counsel

    HealthStream, Inc.

    (Public Company; 201-500 employees; HSTM; E-Learning industry)

    September 2001 — Present (8 years)

  • Vice President, Operations and Development

    smallbusiness.com

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Internet industry)

    March 2000 — July 2001 (1 year 5 months)

    Many will say they were ahead of their time, but we really were at smallbusiness.com. If we were to pitch it now, we could say we were LinkedIn meets MySpace meets Facebook for small business people. Of course, at the time, there was no LinkedIn, MySpace or Facebook. We were business/social networking before it was fully defined. We knew what we were doing, and we did it in the fashion of the time - go big/go fast. We leased big iron and prepared for the masses. Ultimately the burn was too fast and the cash wasn't flowing from investors. Our all too short a run was a great classroom for many of us, and the alumni have spread out and are doing well. Smallbusiness.com even lives on in a form suited to the technology freely available today, managed ably by Rex Hammock, our visionary leader.

  • Attorney

    Bass Berry & Sims PLC

    (Partnership; 201-500 employees; Law Practice industry)

    September 1996 — March 2000 (3 years 7 months)

    As my mentor here said - "a great platform" for a legal career. Bass Berry & Sims is a firm that does very sophisticated work in an environment that is challenging but not deadly. Working with people whose talent and judgment you respect is a necessity for your first "real" job and I certainly found that here. I had the good fortune to spend my short time here in the midst of the Internet boom and led the formation of the E-Commerce practice. Before I found that comfortable spot and got to work on two IPOs, I spent a good bit of time as the intellectual property guy in the corporate group and the corporate guy in the intellectual property and entertainment group.

  • Research Assistant

    Public Works and Transportation Committee, U.S. House of Representatives

    (Government Agency; 1001-5000 employees; Government Administration industry)

    January 1992 — May 1993 (1 year 5 months)

    My 18 months in the sausage factory. After growing up the son of a lobbyist, I decided to see the process close up. I went to the Water Resources and Environment Subcommittee because they had jurisdiction over the Clean Water Act and they were due to reauthorize the Act the year I was there. Instead, I got to see how pork is made, watching the Water Resources Development Act from hearings to signing, bloating up from $500MM to $4BB and back to a manageable $1.5BB for signing.
