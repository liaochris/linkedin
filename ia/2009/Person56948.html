David Brodwin’s Summary

David Brodwin advises non-profits and sustainable businesses on strategy and framing issues. He Integrates the political language and messaging techniques developed by George Lakoff (renowned for linguistics-based insights into political communications) with proven practical marketing and messaging techniques from the corporate sector. His functional experience, in addition to his work in framing and messaging, includes product and go-to-market strategy, business development, IT strategy, and operations effectiveness. His industry experience, in addition to non-profits, spans computing, software, online services, internet, computer components and peripherals, computer graphics, digital video, consumer electronics, television, radio, and publishing.

In addition to his consulting practice, David is co-founder of American Sustainable Business Council, an association of business networks committed to building a more sustainable economy. ASBC mobilizes the resources, skills and dedication of the business community to address critical public issues in support of the triple bottom line.

Career Highlights:
• President, New Voice of Business (2008-09)
• Independent Consultant (2005-2008)
• Managing Director, Rockridge Institute (2004-2005)
• Partner, Accenture (1996-2003)
• Vice President - Marketing, Radius (1993-1995)
• Director, Arthur D. Little, Inc. (1981- 1993)

Mr. Brodwin holds an MBA from Stanford Graduate School of Business and a BA from Harvard College.

    (Political Organization industry)

    The American Sustainable Business Council is committed to creating the foundation and framework for a transition to a new, 21st century American economy grounded in principles of sustainability and equity. We are united in the conviction that the current economic, social and environmental crises are rooted in inequitable and unsustainable practices and structures that must be transformed if there is to be a renewal of hope and prosperity.

    The mission of the council is to provide a forum for business organizations to engage in the policy process and support policies leading to more equitable and sustainable economy in the U.S.
