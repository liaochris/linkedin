Ann Tracy Mueller’s Education

  • Heartland Community College

    History 2008 — 2008

    I took a course called "The Life and Times of Abraham Lincoln." As our text, we used the Lincoln biography by Pulitzer Prize winning author David Herbert Donald, whom I had the pleasure of meeting at the Abraham Lincoln Presidential Libary and Museum's opening symposium in 2005. Lincoln is my passion and the class was the opportunity of a lifetime.

    www.hcc.cc.il.us

  • Western Illinois University

    BOG BA, Board of Governors program, 1991 — 1994

    Strong concentration in non-fiction writing.

    I finished my degree twenty-four years, two kids and one grandchild after first going off to college. Waiting that long for something makes the accomplishment ever more special. I could not have done it without the flexibility of WIU's Board of Governors (now Board of Trustees) BA program. The independent study and extension classes, along with a few at my local junior college, made going to school while working full-time a manageable feat.

    In addition, thanks to some really great professors and courses at Western, I resurrected my passion for all things related to the Land of Lincoln. On a number of occasions, I was able to draw upon that passion for assignments. Finishing my degree wasn't work - it was a pleasure!

    www.wiu.edu/ses/bot

    Phi Kappa Phi honor society

Ann Tracy Mueller’s Interests:

- Non-fiction writing, regional history and literature, especially related to Lincoln and Illinois. Many of my "must have" books are from the University of Illinois Press, www.press.uillinois.edu. - Special interest in the life and work of Carl Sandburg, the rich history of Galesburg, Ill. and Knox College and the Lincoln connection to both. The Lincoln Studies Center at Knox, www.knox.edu/lincolnstudies.xml, does exciting work on Lincoln. His spirit seems ever present where before the Oct. 7, 1858 debate with Stephen Douglas, Lincoln supposedly stepped through a window at Old Main and quipped, "At last I have gone through college" - a moving but disputed story. - Healthy living: Eating right (Weight Watchers is my secret, keeping a food tracker is my key to success), exercising vigorously (walking, swimming, paddleboating, biking), making the first two a habit and a priority (which I've done in the past year). I hope to do my first 5K walk soon and a 5K run next year.
