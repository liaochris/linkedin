Tess Taylor’s Summary

Tess Taylor, a music and record industry career strategist, is President of the National Association of Record industry Professionals (est. 1998) and of the Los Angeles Music Network (est. 1988), and author of many articles and interviews with top record executives. Tess has consulted leading businesses such as MySpace.com, InsideSessions (a joint venture between the Universal Music Group and Penguin Putnam, Inc.), The Walt Disney Company, BMG Entertainment (now Sony BMG), Concord Records and others. A frequent featured expert on national television and radio, her analyses have appeared in national and international business press. She speaks to colleges, professional associations and organizations around the world guiding individuals to personal and professional fulfillment. Through her work in the U.S. and abroad Tess has helped connect countless people to jobs and opportunities, and facilitated in launching hundreds of projects and several companies. She is editor and publisher of the NARIP News, now distributed to 84,000+ record industry professionals, including CEOs, tastemakers and top execs, around the world. In its Dec. 2008 edition, Music Connection magazine included Tess in its list of "50 Innovators, Iconoclasts, Groundbreakers & Guiding Lights of 2008" and calls NARIP, "top-notch and brilliantly executed." The list includes Robert Plant, Trent Reznor, Irving Azoff, Tena Clark, Brian Eno, AC/DC and others. A classically trained pianist, she studied music at the University of Vienna, and is a Phi Beta Kappa, Magna Cum Laude graduate of the University of Redlands' Johnston College where she received her BA in music, German and literature. Tess loves nibbling dark chocolates and drinks lots of tea. She also loves traveling the world, fountain pens, gripping crime novels and classics, and anything ever written or recorded by Glenn Gould or Pete Townshend. For more information, please visit www.narip.com.
