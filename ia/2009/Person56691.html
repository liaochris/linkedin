David Allcock’s Summary

David has been involved in the EDM market since its inception back in the late 80’s starting with the first document image processing systems, electronic document management, and now enterprise content management. In recent years he has been directly involved in the defining the strategic requirements for ECM and their procurement. Many of these deployments required complex integration with existing systems for several thousand users.

His main activities more recently have been in helping medium and large public sector organisations implement better information management, through the adoption of document, records and content management solutions. Anyone who has experience in this area will appreciate that the technology is less than 20% of the challenge. The remaining 80% is building a convincing argument for change in a business case, gathering together the business requirements, procuring the best solution, then implementing it and getting real financial benefits.

He therefore has developed skills over the last 20 years to provide these services:
• Business Strategy
• Feasibility Studies
• Requirements Analysis
• System evaluation and selection
• Project management of implementation
• Back-file conversion of existing documents
• Legal admissibility audits
• Training

In addition to providing consultancy in this area David has lead a number of education programmes for AIIM, delivering all aspects of implementing EDRM systems.

The projects listed below show the extensive experience David has obtained in the public sector, dealing with all levels from chief executive to administrative staff. David prides himself on building an excellent working relationship with his clients and aims to transfer his skills during any assignment. David can call upon a large pool of resources from the experience he has obtained.

David Allcock’s Specialties:

An experienced Project Manager and technical specialist with key skills in:

- Design and implementation of EDRM & ECM Systems;
- Information management audits, feasibility studies, business case development;
- Training, covering all aspects of implementing information and records management systems;
- Writing information management strategy, process analysis, and functional requirements definition.
