Edmund Scanlan’s Summary

Edmund Scanlan, an entrepreneurial CEO, spent the first five years of his career in sales and development with some of the largest and most successful web development companies in Chicago, counting among his clients United Way, World Book Encyclopedia, and Red Lobster. Recognized early as a pioneer in the electronic commerce industry, Scanlan was invited to present at the World Electronic Commerce Conference and had his work highlighted in the Electronic Commerce Report at 21.

In 2002 Scanlan took the skills and knowledge he’d gleaned from those positions and formed Infra-Strategy.

Infra-Strategy began as a web/IT firm, and Scanlan started out alone. He quickly harnessed the power of Google and was one of the first advertisers utilizing Google Adwords.

Seven years later his company has re-branded as Total Attorneys, he has a partner and more than 170 employees working on legal process outsourcing, Internet marketing, and web software development. Scanlan’s company, Total Attorneys, currently provide services to more than 1000 small law firms nationwide.

Total Attorneys' has grown substantially, but Scanlan’s office door is always open, and he’s as likely to be found brainstorming in another department as he is in his office, anyway. He views his ability to “bring together incredibly talented people and unite them in a common vision” as the key to his success, and that idea is borne out by the fact that the first employees he recruited in 2002 each lead successful teams within his companies today.

Scanlan’s businesses have expanded from a one-room office with a laptop to over 23,000 square feet at 25 East Washington in Chicago's Loop.
