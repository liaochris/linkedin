Sarah Wolf’s Summary

In May 2005, I received my MFA in Creative Writing from Emerson College, which only solidified what I learned as an undergraduate at Kent State University, where I earned my a BA in English Literature in 2002. I also did extensive work with the study and application of journalism, history, and film. I have a well-respected editorial eye among my peers and have participated in a number of informal writing groups. Additionally, I have a publishing history that spans back to 1992 and includes entertainment reviews, informational articles and essays, poetry, and fiction.

To date, my work-life has focused on sales and promotions, employee management, and maintaining a positive, efficient relationship with the public. I was rapidly promoted to management, first at the Pets Pajamas and then at Holiday’s Hallmark, and I was hired as a manager at Bel Tempo, where I increased business 30% within the first three months. In December of 2006, Bel Tempo was sold to industry competitor Papyrus and I was instrumental in transitioning my store to meet the new owners’ guidelines. I thrive in people-oriented environments, especially when I am passionate about the business and/or product. For instance, I developed a successful inventory and ordering system for Bel Tempo’s #1 product line, Vera Bradley. I also was the only manager in a district of fourteen Papyrus stores to make my 2008 first quarter sales goal. Additionally, I have experience in special event planning.

With the combination of my writing, analytical, research, editorial, and other communication skills I mastered as a student and my remarkable record as both a customer service professional and staff leader, my goal is to gain employment in a new environment where my prior skills can be better utilized. Although I do not have a car, I am also willing to travel as often as any such job description may require.
