Mike Ren’s Summary

Mike Ren is an investment professional focused on cross-border China opportunities. Mike specializes in private equity and merchant banking investments between China and Latin America, in strategic sectors including energy, natural resources, commodities, food, agriculture, infrastructure, and real estate. He has worked at Merrill Lynch Global Private Equity upon completion of the investment banking analyst training program, and at Bank of America in its Mergers and Acquisitions Group. Mike began his investment career at Apax Partners (formerly Patricof Ventures), as assistant to chairman Alan Patricof investing in new media startups. He received a Bachelor of Arts in Economics from Columbia University as a Z.Y. Fu Scholar, and a Certificate in General Management from Stanford Graduate School of Business. Mike has been licensed by U.S. National Association of Securities Dealers (NASD / FINRA) in Series 7 and Series 63 certifications.

Mike is a member of Young Venture Capitalist Club, China Venture Capital Association, and local chapters of Columbia and Stanford alumni clubs. As a freelance contributor, he has been published in The New York Times in its DealBook section, and in The Columbia Daily Spectator as an editorial columnist, as well as appearance on BBC Radio. Mike co-founded a New York-based nonprofit focused on promoting pre-professional ethics among college students. He received the King’s Crown Leadership Award from Columbia University, and the Senator Daniel Inouye Scholarship from the Asian Pacific American Heritage Council. Mike enjoys golf, yoga, sailing, and the beach. He is a native of Shanghai, where he is based.
