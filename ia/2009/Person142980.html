Lester McCarroll, Jr.’s Summary

Lester McCarroll, Jr. is President and CEO of The McCarroll Group.

The McCarroll Group creates and seeks ideas that enhance urban lifestyles through technology, and helps individuals and companies create new revenue streams from these ideas.

He has been recently engaged by the Illinois Institute of Technology as a business consultant to help create and implement a program to foster entrepreneurship and economic development in Chicago; implementing business development strategies for technology-based entrepreneurs and companies.

Lester is under contract with IMI Innovations, Inc. as Director of Corporate Development; guiding strategy, product portfolio, and business development efforts to create new products and revenue streams from IMI's multiple-user and bimanual technologies.

Formerly, he was a Managing Partner with The Technology Consortium Group (TCG), an information technology and services concern based in Chicago.

During a 10-year tenure at Motorola, and while rising to the level of Vice President, Lester impacted the company in a number of fundamental areas of marketing and product planning, international strategies and operations, country management coordination, wireless infrastructure and feature sets for telecommunication carriers, software development processes, diversity market and employee development, and mentoring.

Lester started his career at AT&T Bell Laboratories at the age of 15 as a summer intern. After receiving degrees in Electrical Engineering from the Illinois Institute of Technology in 1983 and Stanford University in 1984, he rejoined Bell Labs, leading development and project management of various digital services systems, hardware, software releases, and features for the switching business prior to joining Motorola in 1994.

Lester McCarroll, Jr.’s Specialties:

Strategy and business development, strategic marketing, business process reengineering, project management, systems development, product planning and development, urban marketing, community relations.
