Irshad Ahmed’s Summary

Mr. Ahmed is the President and CEO of Pure Energy Corporation, a New Jersey based Renewable Energy and Biotechnology company. At Pure Energy, Mr. Ahmed is leading the development and commercialization of technologies and products for chemicals and transportation fuel markets with a focus on cellulosic ethanol, E-Diesel (Puranol fuel blending additive) and Alternative Fuels/Performance Fuel Additives. He is in-charge of all corporate, operational and management of the Company, including all R&D, project implementation and construction of biofuels manufacturing facilities.

At Pure Energy, Mr. Ahmed has managed over $30 million in product and technology commercialization efforts within biodiesel, biomass-to-ethanol and fuel additives business. He is also the primary intellectual property developer for all products and technologies at Pure Energy with 31 issued US and International patents to his name. He has managed several investment banking relationships, including with DLJ/CSFB, US Bancorp Piper Jaffray and several venture funds. He has established strategic alliances with multinational corporations and governments to implement Pure Energy’s business plan. Mr. Ahmed has successfully executed joint ventures and strategic alliance partnerships with several Fortune 500 companies, including Shell Chemicals, BASF Corporation, Abengoa, Hindustan Petroleum (India), ADM, United Biorefineries Corp., and others.

Irshad Ahmed’s Specialties:

Mr. Ahmed is an internationally recognized expert with in the renewable energy and biotechnology fields with an in-depth knowledge and understanding of business and corporate management, public policy, international business, technology and project development. Over the past two decades, his work has integrated advances in agricultural, biological, chemical and environmental engineering with public policy, corporate and business development strategy, management and projects implementation.

Irshad Ahmed’s Experience

  • President & CEO

    Pure Energy Corporation

    (Oil & Energy industry)

    June 1997 — Present (12 years 7 months)

    Irshad Ahmed is one of the founding members of Pure Energy Corporation. At PEC, he is currently leading the implementation of multiple Integrated Biorefinery Manufacturing Complex Projects in the United States, Costa Rica, India, Canada and other parts of the world. He has successfully developed a Technology Licensing Model for PEC's Cellulosic Ethanol process, entered into multiple Joint Ventures and positioned Pure Energy as one of the leading Advanced Biofuels companies in the United States.

    Pure Energy projects have received $221 million in Infrastructure Development Bonds from the the States of Idaho and Missouri, as well as significant support from the Federal, State and local levels in the form of incentives.

    At Pure Energy, Mr. Ahmed serves both as its President & CEO as well as a Director on the Board of the Company. He manages all corporate development, operations, intellectual property development, corporate finance and project implementation activities worldwide. His efforts have positioned Pure Energy at the fore front of a major liquidity event for its shareholders.

  • Associate & Director of Renewable Energy & Biotechnology

    Booz Allen & Hamilton

    (Privately Held; Management Consulting industry)

    June 1993 — June 1997 (4 years 1 month)

    Mr. Ahmed served for as an Associate and the Director of Renewable Energy and Biotechnology Worldwide Technology Business practice at Booz Allen & Hamilton Inc. in McLean, Virginia.

    While at Booz Allen & Hamilton, he supported the Office of Administration at the US Environmental Protection Agency, US Departments of Agriculture and Energy, Federal Aviation Administration and Fortune 500 companies, including Monsanto. Mr. Ahmed was part of the lead Booz•Allen team that restructured Monsanto from a Chemical to an Agricultural Biotechnology company.

    In 1995, while working for the US Environmental Protection Agency, he developed the United States’ first pollution prevention strategy and implementation plan for The White House under the Clean Air Act Amendments and President Clinton’s Executive Order 12856. Recently, as part of Booz Allen Team, he supported the strategic plan and roadmap development efforts for British Petroleum and Bunge to facilitate their entry into biofuels industry.
