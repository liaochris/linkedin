Raymond Clarke’s Summary

As a solo concert pianist, Raymond's official professional debut after completing his postgraduate performance studies was in March 1988 at London's South Bank Centre. He has given hundreds of solo recitals in twelve European countries, as well as appearing as soloist with orchestras in over forty concerti and in national radio broadcasts, including programmes for BBC Radio 3.

Whilst continuing to welcome invitations from concert promoters to give professional solo recitals, flexibility over the choice of dates offered is essential, as Raymond's priority now is to avoid disruption to his extensive teaching commitments at two of the UK's finest university music departments, where he has around forty undergraduate and postgraduate pupils working with him on a one-to-one basis towards formal performance exams. He does not undertake professional chamber music or accompanying engagements.

Raymond Clarke’s Specialties:

Magazines, journals and newspapers have often misleadingly classified Raymond as a specialist in 20th-century music, although this area of the repertoire accounts for only a tiny proportion of the content of his concert programmes.

He welcome invitations from enterprising promoters to present 'classic' 20th-century scores by Pierre Boulez, Michael Tippett or Elliott Carter, but his advocacy of such music is in addition to his work within the 'standard' repertoire, not a substitute for it.
