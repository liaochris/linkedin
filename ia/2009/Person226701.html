Surabhi Gupta’s Summary

In a decade long career, Surabhi started with being a pure personal investments advisor (as a wealth manager at ICICI Bank) and very quickly rose to becoming the Head of Treasury for the Indian operations of Dawnay Day International (DDI) - a UK-based group with interests across financial services, real estate and private equity. The DDI experience imparted hands-on knowledge of cross border money flow, corporate structuring, regulatory environment relating to FDI investments, taxation, company law, capital markets and gave her the opportunity to directly help build different businesses for the group viz Financial Services, Hospitality, Real Estate, Research and Art.

  • Executive Director

    (Financial Services industry)

    April 2008 — December 2008 (9 months)

    She was the youngest Executive Director because of the pivotal role that she played in building sizable businesses for the group across both financial services and hotels. She was directly overseeing key functions of Finance, Human Resources, Legal and Compliance as well as serving as the group’s contact point with regulators like SEBI, stock exchanges, bankers and professional consultants. Post a liquidity crunch in late 2008, faced by the UK partners Dawnay Day, she was instrumental in identifying and successfully selling off the different businesses to different investors. Financial Services was sold to New Silk Route, a US based USD 1.5 bn Private Equity fund, while the 4 star Hotels business with 3 live properties in Ahmedabad, Jaipur and Pune was sold to the Duet Group of UK.

She reported directly to Guy Naggar and Peter Klimt in UK with respect to this group function. Her job required incorporating investment vehicles in Mauritius, obtaining FDI and other regulatory approvals, on-going capitalization, inter-company treasury management, commercial debt – raising and the group's property acquisitions including the 3 land parcels for the 4 star hotel chain of Dawnay Day in India.
