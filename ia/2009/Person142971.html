Luis Masyebra’s Summary

Two things have led my professional life since late 90's: the Internet and Customer Management. I love the way online initiatives are maturing, changing the way we communicate and live our life in today's world.

I am proud of having been able to directly witness (and be part of) the Rise and Fall of late 90's dot-com Fever from management positions at Ecuality and Ya.com, focusing more and more on the business side of things from a relational marketing and customer management perspective. Along that trip, ICEMD has granted me the great chance to stay fit in one of my all-times passions: Academics. Since 1999, I have been part of numerous programs, courses and roles, teaching different aspects of today's Marketing techniques that are customer-driven.

At home, I have three other passions: my family, serious-amateur photography and computers.

Thanks for stopping by!

  • eCommerce Development Manager and Business Process Manager

    Ya.com

    (Privately Held; 201-500 employees; Telecommunications industry)

    January 2001 — January 2005 (4 years 1 month)

    Ya.com taught me how to take eCommerce very seriously… and make business while at it.

    In the four years I spent there, I held mainly two positions:

    As eCommerce Development Manager, I managed all B2C emerging initiatives of Ya.com Network including Viajar.com, TiendaPC.com and Ofertadvd.com, among others. Once the site, the offer, the marketing, the systems and the processes were in place, I passed the leadership to focused teams to further expand each business case.

    Once it was clear to us that Telco was the way to go, I became CRM and Business Process Manager for all ISP activities at Ya.com. This horizontal role gave me an excellent scenario to learn how to pin down operational costs, deploy new operational and collaborative systems, improve business processes and –at the end- produce happier customers overall. Here, risk management and team leadership was the key to success.

  • Integrated eCommerce Manager (Channel Director)

    Ecuality

    (Privately Held; 201-500 employees; Consumer Goods industry)

    2000 — 2001 (1 year)

    Ecuality was probably the biggest promise and the biggest crash in Spain's early dot-com age. Meant to become the Amazon of any Spanish-speaking market, it expanded to up to 8 countries and launched the success-driven Alcoste.com and the Amazon-alike Diversia.com within 1 year.

    In the midst of that never-to-be-forgotten NASDAQ's dot-com fever, I was in charge of all third-party driven sales: from partnering with top-level sites like Terra to deploying Affiliate programs aimed to smaller personal websites. Too much money, too few orders and many incoherent approaches were involved in that game. I learned what NOT to do on the Internet and how to manage a team and a business while the whole company was rapidly crumbling down from trees to ashes.

    A nice ride with a rough landing: Ecuality stopped its activity in Q4 2000, around two years after it was born.
