Ronen Avidar’s Summary

“The only real voyage of discovery consists not in seeking new landscapes but in having new eyes.” (Marcel Proust)

Simerax was born to bring a new perspective of the people who are at the center of action to the management landscape: Managers.
The technology era gave rise to new terms that naturally found their way into many areas of our lives.
I have chosen to relate to two of them to illustrate the concept and sense that Simerax tries to give the managers who undergo processes under its guidance.
The term usability comes to us from the Internet domain and refers to the perception of hardware and software as a single cohesive unit with defined objectives. We see usability as part of the experiential process we undergo with the managers; no company can work without a good and trustworthy management, and managers themselves need the right company to serve as a “business incubator” for them, where they can grow, develop and achieve self-realization.
The second term, flow, comes from psychology. It means that the experience is the payoff and that there is no need for external payoff. Entering the process full force means that you are not dependent on external factors; you have a sense of security and inner strength – and it connects you 100% to yourself and to your desires.
Our purpose is to convey this sense even within the company, which the manager will see as an inherent part of his life (even if for a limited time). The manager at the helm of the company has to view his job and the experience he so desired as the payoff.
I believe that a good match between management and company can lead to incredible and far-reaching results.

The journey with Simerax is filled with experiences, insights and the joy of action. I cordially invite you to join us.

Wishing you happiness and success,
Ronen Avidar
VP, Business Development
