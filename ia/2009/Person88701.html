Greg Quilop’s Summary

Having worked in Web Development and Software Engineering, I understand the limitations and resources available to technically explain and document usability flows and specifications. My experience and expertise in design, coding, and documentation is needed for a web developer position. I have designed and developed numerous web interfaces and applications in my current and previous employers including client web sites, admin portals, content management systems, Internet Banking, Cash Management, and other internal support applications. My experience with a Software Development Life Cycle has enabled me understand the process and documentation needed to hit milestones and the project’s estimated end date.

One project for the Internet Banking product called for a configuration file that would have been too complex and difficult to maintain for our client facing departments. I created a prototype admin application that used a database backend to make the internal configuration simpler and presented this to the business analyst, customer service and technical support departments for review. The application was quickly approved without any modifications in the design or user interface. I then created the business specification, technical documentation, and training material for this application.

Starting in a customer service role, I developed an ability to communicate high-level ideas and across all levels of expertise. I can work out details and have the ability to see the big picture. This allows projects to be designed correctly the first time reducing the time backtrack to redesign or quickly patch errors that should have been caught earlier.
