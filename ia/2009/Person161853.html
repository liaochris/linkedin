  • Owner/CEO

    Hamond Industries Ltd

    (Mechanical or Industrial Engineering industry)

    May 1992 — March 2009 (16 years 11 months)

    Hamond Industries was a small (15 people) slide forming company. Slide forming is a specialized type of metal stamping and forming. Hamond had it's own toolroom and design team. I worked in most parts of the business, operating machines, shipping, receiving, designing and building tooling, purchasing, sales, estimating. I started as an employee, became a partner, finally owner/CEO. In 2008 the company was sold to Bohne Spring Industries Ltd, where I worked for a period on transition issues as part of the sale.

    When I became a partner, there were 3 of us. I was the youngest. As the other 2 retired, I took over their responsibilities, so that, for the last 5 years, I was the management team.

    Project management: each set of customer tooling was treated as a separate project, with elements of quoting, sales, design, tool build, material purchasing and scheduling, scheduling an available machine for testing, feedback from initial testing, etc. At times, there were 3 or more sets of tooling on the go at the same time.

    Business management: as in any such small business, the boss participated in customer relations, supplier relations, accounting, etc.

    Computing: I trained my partners in the use of computers. We used MS office for all correspondence, sent faxes from our computers, did all our design for the last 15 years on AutoCAD. Quotes were done in Excel, posted to ACT (a contact manager) so the sales manager could communicate to the customer using a script I wrote. The central database of the company was the orders and product database, which contained all part manufacturing data and ensured that we made parts the same way every time.
