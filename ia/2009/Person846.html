A. Adam Glenn’s Summary

I am an award-winning journalist, Internet veteran and independent online media consultant, who has held posts with news media in New York and Washington, most recently as senior producer at ABCNews.com.

My client roster includes NBC Universal Chairman Bob Wright's charitable foundation Autism Speaks, Columbia Univ. Graduate School of Journalism, The J-Lab at the Univ. of Maryland, The Shelley and Donald Rubin Foundation, Rodale Inc. and others.

I'm a startup expert who has guided many major news and online projects from conception to implementation, while building editorial teams and collaborating within and outside organizations. I'm also a well-rounded generalist with a full range of writing, editing and web skills, and an array of specializations including health, science, environment, technology, business and media.

Citizen journalism is a major interest for me, and in 2005 I formed "I, Reporter," a citJ training business and blog with Poynter's E-Media Tidbits Editor Amy Gahran. Most recently, we were awarded a Knight News Challenge grant to launch a citJ web site on global warming in Boulder, Colo. I, Reporter has also launched a searchable online citmedia directory for J-Lab, and a local newspaper web site in suburban New York.

I am an active member of the Society of Environmental Journalists, where I serve on the editorial advisory board, and of the Online News Association. I was awarded a 2002 Ford Environmental Journalism Fellowship to teach in India, and a 2005 Environmental Media Fellowship at the Vermont Law School. Also in 2005, I trained at the University of Colorado-Boulder, the Centers for Disease Control in Atlanta and the National Institutes of Health in Washington, DC. I previously earned a mid-career Masters of International Affairs (environmental policy) at Fletcher School of Law & Diplomacy in Boston. I currently live in the New York area with my wife, who is an educator, and our preschool-age daughter.
