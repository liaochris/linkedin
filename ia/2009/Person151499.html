Mark Crafts’s Summary

Mark is a senior sales executive in the digital media industry with career experience stretching from Madison Avenue to Silicon Valley to our Nation's Capital. 

Mark has a proven track record of building award-winning sales teams and regions for some of the country's most respected and successful publishers - The Washington Post Company, Ziff-Davis, Inc., International Data Group and CMP Media.  Working a combined 20+ years for these publishers (the Grahams, the Ziffs, the McGoverns and the Leeds), Mark has constantly been on the front line of digital publishing and competitive national media sales.

In Mark's 8 years of building and managing the west coast for Washington Post Digital and The Slate Group, annual advertising revenue for his franchise increased tenfold.

Notably in 2009, the Jack Meyers Media Survey recognized Mark's team for best sales rep responsiveness and delivering on promises.  And working in the field, Mark led the Washington Post Company's senior executive presentation team for the Microsoft Bing pitch, resulting in one of the largest media deals in Slate's history.

Mark is a successful implementor and relentless champion of sales technology that can help his sales teams be more productive and his companies be more profitable.   Most recently he led Washington Post's internal Salesforce committee and was invited by Salesforce to present his innovations in document sharing and team collaboration, using Salesforce Content, at the 2009 Salesforce Dreamforce conference.

Mark currently serves as an advisor to start-ups and media companies on the west coast. He specializes in sales team building, sales pitch development and Salesforce customization - specifically report building and KPI tracking that can be used from the field to the boardroom.
