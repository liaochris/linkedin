  • SVP, Marketing

    Sigaba was acquired by Proofpoint in August 2008 in fulfillment of it's exit strategy.

  • VP Marketing

    Infodata Systems, Inc

    (Public Company; 51-200 employees; INFD; Computer Software industry)

    February 2003 — June 2006 (3 years 5 months)

    Provided oversight, direction and leadership to the Market Department, which encompassed corporate marketing, product marketing, product management, quality assurance, and customer support. Key member of the executive team that helped to drive strategic direction for the company. Instrumental in analyzing vertical market needs for Federal, Commercial, and Life Sciences and translating those needs into proprietary solution offerings. Lead the integration of Infodata’s three stand-alone products into a fully integrated modular architecture and then re-branding the applications into a platform offering. Member of the Infodata Merger and Acquisition task force and responsible for analyzing new technologies and how they strategically and operationally fit into the company’s Content Management Services Layer technology. The company was purchased in a public-to-private acquisition by McDonald Bradley.

  • VP Professional Services

    Ikimbo, Inc.

    (Privately Held; Computer Software industry)

    January 2001 — January 2003 (2 years 1 month)

    Provided leadership and management for the Professional Services group, which encompassed consulting services, pre-sales support, deployment implementations, partner support, and customer support. Led multi-disciplined teams that developed detailed solution prints for each engagement, including process mappings, business process improvement, as well as detailed return on investment financial impact templates. Headed up software installations at companies including Lockheed Martin, Garban Intercapital and Deloitte & Touche.

  • EVP and COO

    KHA, Inc.

    (Privately Held; International Trade and Development industry)

    January 1996 — December 2000 (5 years)

Specific corporate consulting included the development of strategic plans for various companies and organizations outlining Vision, Trend Analysis, Constraints, Strategies and Tactical Actions plans. Designed and developed a training program for private sector Senior and Mid-level managers associated with the stand up of the Management Development Institute in the Middle East. COO management functions included instituting financial accounting and control systems, human resource, corporate policies and procedures, recruitment of technical field teams and internationally based short-term consultants, and development of new business and marketing strategies.
