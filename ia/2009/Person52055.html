Dain Schult’s Summary

Involved in radio broadcasting for over 40 years:

· Chairman/CEO of AMERICAN RADIO EMPIRE, INC.
· Chairman/CEO of AMERICAN COMMUNICATIONS ENTERPRISES, INC.
· President/CEO of TEXROCK RADIO, INC.
· Founder/President of RADIOACTIVITY, INC.
· EVP/COO of SUNBELT RADIO GROUP, INC.
· Corporate Consultant/Program Manager of WAVE-FM
· General Manager of WFOM-AM
· General Manager of WGRI-AM
· Air Personality at WQXI-FM
· Training Director & Announcer at WRAS-FM

· Formed a successful consultancy. Personally consulted over 150 clients in markets as diverse as London; Houston; Minneapolis, Dallas; Pittsburgh; Charlotte; Atlanta; Tampa, FL; Laredo, TX; New Bern, NC; Vail, CO; Bangor, ME; McAllen/Brownsville, TX; Roanoke, VA; Fort Wayne, IN; Knoxville, TN; Vienna and Bainbridge, GA

· Successfully acquired/operated stations as COO of Sunbelt Radio Group. Increased revenue 25% over previous owners' performance in first year. Doubled the ratings of one of the group’s FM stations in one rating period. Increased sales in all markets.

· Formed a TX-only group, that included up to 71 stations increasing the sales by 14% in the first full year of operations despite a GM strike and the worst drought in TX history.

· Served as CEO of American Communications Enterprises and helped to take the company public from scratch on OTCBB.

· A graduate of Georgia State University, Atlanta, GA, where he taught non-credit broadcast classes while still a student.

Chosen to Who's Who in Entertainment, Advertising, Industry and Finance and America.

Published in trade publications including BILLBOARD, THE PULSE OF BROADCASTING, THE RADIO MANAGER, and RADIO WORLD - published author of "The Radio Guide," "Roll Away The Stone," "Nashville, Chattanooga & St. Louis - The Dixie Line" and "Grandpa's Road."

Formed a non-profit group (NC&StL Preservation Society) for preserving any and all historical artifacts dealing with the Nashville, Chattanooga & St. Louis Railway.

Dain Schult’s Specialties:

Hands on experience in every facet of the radio industry with excellent contacts with wireless and Internet professionals as well.

Hands-on experience in M&A, building and running both private and public multimedia companies.

A published author of a variety of different books. columns and articles including industry related materials, railroad history, self-help motivation and historical novels - good communications skills.

  • President/Chief Operating Officer

    Sunbelt Radio Group, Inc.

    (Broadcast Media industry)

    September 1989 — December 1992 (3 years 4 months)

    Created a Texas -based station group in Texas.

    Located and negotiated the first acquisition of KRRG FM, Laredo, TX. Helped staffed the station by bringing in a new GM. Revenue grew over 15% in first year of ownership.

    Provided complete operations support. KRRG FM was operated profitably during its time with Sunbelt. This was followed by the addition of KRGE AM/KRGY FM, Brownsville, TX.

    Successfully brokered KRGE AM to a local religious broadcast group, first as an LMA and then as a purchase.

    Restaffed the FM programming staff and reformatted the station from Album Rock (AOR) to Top-40 (CHR). KRGY went from 11th place to 5th place in the very first rating period measuring the format change. Every adult rating on the station was doubled or tripled.

    Brought in an aggressive turnaround-experienced General Manager to help lower overhead and maximize revenues. Revenues grew 20%.

    The stations were sold in late 1992 for $550,000 more than was paid for them.

  • Program Manager/Corporate Programming Consultant

    Cosmos Broadcasting - WSRZ (later WAVE) FM

    (Broadcast Media industry)

    November 1980 — December 1982 (2 years 2 months)

    Assumed the Program Manager position after having first served as station consultant for a ratings turnaround.

    Supervised the Programming, Promotions, and News/Public Affairs Departments. This included hiring personnel, budget management and development of short/long range operations plans, with weekly presentations at manager meetings.

    Achieved immediate ratings increases with the use of my MAINSTREAM format, which converted WAVE from progressive Album Rock to Adult Rock-Adult Contemporary. Before this change, in the Sarasota/Bradenton ratings, WAVE had a 1.8 share 12+ and jumped to a 4.5 12+ share in the very next ratings sweep, in a market with 42 radio signals. The format helped WAVE reach market dominance in the crucial 25-34/25-54 adult demographics.

    Before station changed hands in ownership, it reached a 7.2 12+ share, giving it a commanding lead over all stations in Sarasota/Bradenton. Assumed the role of Corporate Programming Consultant. Provided music consultation to WAVE's sister station, WIS AM in Columbia, SC.

    Introduced new specialty programming features to aid WAVE's image and create new sales marketing opportunities. Some of these programs were: THE NIGHTCAP, SATURDAY NIGHT SPECIAL, ALL THAT'S JAZZ, TIDAL WAVES, THE HONORARY MUSIC DIRECTOR SHOW, and SUNCOAST SHOWCASE.

    Constructed a local news department at no cost by utilizing specially trained high school students via a pilot intern program with the Cobb County School System.

    Contributed to the development of an oldies show, THE ROOTS OF ROCK, for weekend programming that was eventually syndicated through Radioactivity, Inc.
