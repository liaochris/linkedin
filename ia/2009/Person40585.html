Chad Alexander’s Experience

  • Executive Director

    Chalex International

    (Executive Office industry)

    February 2008 — Present (1 year 2 months)

    This is a newly created company within the business consulting arena. Current clients include Monster.com, Making it Count, GoDaddy.com, Phisher & James Investments, Crown Distributing and Plan B Enterprises.

  • CEO/President

    Prickly Pickle Landscape

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Construction industry)

    2003 — 2007 (4 years)

    This was a start-up company specializing in high dollar residential landscape construction with projects ranging from $45,000 - $300,000. I was contracted to establish and develop this company for a private investment group, with an intent to sell the company within five years for acceptable profit. I created a three year business and strategy plan, conducted P&L management, introduced and implemented business development procedures, conducted evaluation of marketing initiatives and acted as the Senior Project Manager for multiple projects. As with any endeavor such as this, there were many challenges that were met and defeated. I consider my inaugural reign in a Chief position a success as we achieved our goal. Above all, it was the greatest learning experience of my life to date. My command came to an end with the successful negotiation and completed profitable sale of the company for all share holders.

  • Regional VP

    Aroma Coffee Company

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Retail industry)

    2000 — 2003 (3 years)

    At my introduction this was a $21 million company with one region failing to meet both sales and operating objectives. I was originally brought on as a District Manager in charge of Arizona. Within one year I was chosen to transform this struggling region for profitability and sustainable growth as the next Regional VP. Key activities included oversight and growth of sales and profits, P&L management, proper execution of strategy and promotional calendar and regional account negotiation. While occupying this position I managed a budget of $3.45 million and allocated funds amongst marketing efforts and operations in 4 states.

  • Director of New Store Operations

    Homeplace Inc.

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; Retail industry)

    1997 — 2000 (3 years)

    I began my employ with this company as the New Store Logistics Director. I arrived at this position through an acquisition of my prior employer. Advancing quickly over an eighteen-month period, I found myself in charge of new retail store front establishment. My key activities included coordination of construction completions, traveling road crews, loss prevention, logistics department. and information technology department while managing budget and travel constraints. In early 2000 this company permanently ceased all general business operations.
