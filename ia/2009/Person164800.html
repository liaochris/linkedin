Michael Riffle’s Summary

Over 12 years professional experience designing, developing and being accountable for progressively larger and more complex data-driven software systems—both for the private sector and publicly-funded scientific research. My current focus is making complex data highly available to users in a manner that facilitates understanding and discovery. My passion is science, and my present work is particularly focused on the dissemination of rich and complex sets of experimental data to users across the whole spectrum of technical expertise. I find this work intellectually fulfilling—combining my degree in molecular and cellular biology and extensive experience with database programming and software engineering. My success in this field is largely driven by imagination, creative problem solving and having a big picture view.

Active entrepreneur and steer my business interests towards projects solving challenging problems or developing novel and interesting data-driven software (mostly web-based). Have run this business for approximately ten years, and during that time I’ve had sole ownership and responsibility for projects of many sizes, shapes, visibility and business domains. I’m always on the hunt for new projects and challenges.

Michael Riffle’s Experience

  • Informatics Research Consultant

    University of Washington

    (Educational Institution; 10,001 or more employees; Higher Education industry)

    February 2002 — Present (7 years 8 months)

    Lead the informatics group for the Yeast Resource Center and the data management initiative for the UW Proteomics Resource.

    The YRC is a NCRR resource center consisting of leading laboratories utilizing different technologies to characterize proteins, including mass spectrometry, protein structure prediction, fluorescence microscopy, and yeast two-hybrid.

    Primary role is management and dissemination of data generated by the YRC, but also includes developing software tools, datamining and bioinformatics, and novel research for predicting protein complexes, protein-protein interactions and protein function.

    Data integration is one of the biggest challenges of this position. Developed a relational database model that eases incorporation of new data types, and a common interface for searching, viewing and analyzing different types of data. This data platform is utilized by YRC researchers, collaborators and the public at large through our public data repository.

Example industries include biotechnology (scientific database design/implementation and bioinformatics), online learning (developing curriculum dissemination platforms, online testing, online communities, etc) and market research (online surveys/polls and statistical analysis). In this position, I have worked with many more industries than those listed here, which has necessitated gaining expertise in a vast array of technologies and methodologies.
