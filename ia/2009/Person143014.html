  • Senior Vice President

    Lehman Brothers

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; LEH; Investment Banking industry)

    January 2008 — September 2008 (9 months)

    Structured and marketed alternative financing products for emerging markets, including agglutination of CDS, CLN, exotic options on futures, etc. to reduce cost of financing for selected corporations, and hedge exposure to specific risks taken by several financial institutions.   • Managing Director

    SAGA Capital, LLC

    (Privately Held; 501-1000 employees; Information Technology and Services industry)

    February 2005 — December 2007 (2 years 11 months)

    SAGA Capital, LLC is an advisory group specialized in structuring non-traditional funding solutions for hedge funds, private equity capital firms, and early-stage growth companies in energy, mining, technology, media, and financial services. SAGA seeks to capitalize existing but under funded operations by developing advanced analytical tools and Expert Systems to evaluate new markets, custom made risk transfer mechanisms, government subsidies, and the impact of various capital structure decisions on enterprise value. SAGA matches projects with existing network of New York & London based hedge funds, investment banks, and private equity capital groups.

  • Vice President

    Deutsche Bank

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; DB; Banking industry)

    June 2000 — January 2005 (4 years 8 months)

    Asset Backed Securities Group/Debt Capital Markets - New York & London: Performed several tasks such as client presentations, interaction with rating agencies, preparation of sales points for traders, road show support, reverse engineering selected deals, and pricing, sizing and securing investors for structures exposed to several asset classes including insurance linked securities, music and film royalties, mining & oil drilling equipment leases, construction loans, and project finance loans. Developed quantitative models to enhance returns of equity pieces of balance sheet and arbitrage CDOs, and structured one off deals for several European and Latin American governments.

  • Vice President

    NetRisk

    (Management Consulting industry)

    August 1999 — June 2000 (11 months)

    An independent advisory and technology firm now affiliated with Fitch Ratings, created by the developers of the Risk Adjusted Return on Capital (RAROC) methodologies at Bankers Trust. Developed new Monte Carlo simulation methodologies to enhance the calculation of Operational Capital at Risk (CaR, an offshoot of RAROC) for their RiskOps software, jointly developed with a major European insurance company. Advised clients on risk management issues, and interacted on a daily basis with clients, as well as with members of both the advisory and technology groups of NetRisk.

  • Senior Associate

    AIG

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; AIG; Insurance industry)

    May 1998 — August 1999 (1 year 4 months)

    Provided internal consulting in interest rate modeling, hedging strategy, finite risk solutions, default risk evaluation, KMV credit analysis, mathematical/statistic methodologies, and Monte Carlo simulation. Interacted frequently with officers of corporations that were evaluating credit enhancement policies for CMBS and CLO deals, residual value insurance policies, completion guarantees for film production, as well as combined risks and political risk insurance policies to smooth earnings volatility for their corporations. Supported the international division of AIG in their dealings with foreign corporations and governments seeking risk finance solutions.

  • Director of Quantitative Analysis

    Parallax Partners, L.P.

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Investment Management industry)

    April 1993 — April 1998 (5 years 1 month)

    Structured quantitative strategies and managed a portfolio to trade commodity futures, and plain and exotic options on the Oil & Gas index (XOI) and the Gold & Silver index (XAU), for a New York based hedge fund. Calculated the Net Asset Value (NAV) of the fund on a weekly basis, and reported figures to clients. Advised CFOs and CEOs of foreign corporations on mergers and acquisitions, and advised the boards of directors of associated insurance companies on special investment opportunities
