Natalie Halpern’s Summary

My passion is telling stories that capture the immeasurable capacity of the human spirit.

Much of my 17-year broadcast journalism, media production, and public relations career has focused on producing long and short-form programming/content for television, radio, online, and live audiences and using innovative approaches to increase an organization or company's visibility and brand, engage the public, and market its services.

That includes TV news reporting and anchoring; producing and directing documentaries, videos, and digital media content for nonprofit, government, and corporate clients; creating spots for cause-related campaigns; editing and supervising edit and voice over recording sessions; writing scripts; and developing strategic messaging and managing media outreach activities for a large nonprofit.

This new era where Web video is vital tool in engaging a broader audience and communicating directly with the public is a fascinating time.

I'd like to capitalize on this explosive growth in digital media by continuing to create video stories and documentaries that inspire, educate, entertain, and promote positive social change.

"Africa's Daughters," the second film I produced and directed, captures the inspiring journey of two Ugandan girls with one big dream: to lift themselves and their families out of poverty. To do that they must challenge cultural traditions and graduate high school, something nearly impossible for most African girls.

The film recently won the Grand Jury Prize for Best Documentary Short at the Atlanta Film Festival.

It has also been shown at numerous film festivals including the Pan African Film Festival in Los Angeles and the Tallahassee Film Festival.

"Tumaini Letu" (Our Hope), which tells the story of three women raising AIDS orphans in Kenya, received the Best Short Documentary Award at the 2006 New York AIDS Film Festival and aired nationally on The Africa Channel.
