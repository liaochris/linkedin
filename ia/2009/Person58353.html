David M. Soltis’s Summary

David Soltis is a dedicated professional educator and manager who has experience in both the healthcare and education sectors. On the educational front, David has served as Vice Principal for Heritage Academy of Learning Excellence and has provided input into projects as a consultant for the Library of Parliament in Ottawa. He has also designed and evaluated training modules for post secondary students, working professionals seeking accreditation and clinical trials projects.

With his cross over into the Telemedicine sector, David has become a key resource assisting in the integration of three legacy networks to form the Ontario Telemedicine Network (OTN). The Ontario Telemedicine Network uses advanced information communication technologies to deliver clinical, educational and administrative services to more than 250 members across 420 health care sites province-wide.

As Manager of Training, David is a champion of e-learning and has worked with various stakeholders to establish training programs for doctors, nurses, allied healthcare professionals and patients. Leveraging a number of e-learning tools including webcasting, webconferencing, and videoconferencing, David ensures that training is fun, interactive and meets the needs of adult learners.

David holds degrees from a number of academic institutions including the University of Western Ontario, University of Ottawa and the University of Toronto. He is also a Certified Training and Development Professional (CTDP), Project Management Professional (PMP), and member of the Ontario College of Teachers. David is currently completing his Executive MBA at the University of Ottawa.
