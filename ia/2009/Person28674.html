Bill Orner’s Experience

  • VP Engineering

    OptoElectronix

    (Semiconductors industry)

    October 2008 — December 2009 (1 year 3 months)

    OptoElectronix is a company in the LED technology industry. Headquartered in San Jose CA, with manufacturing and operations in Penang Malaysia. Managing a design team of optics, thermal, electrical, and mechanical engineers (also IT) located in both San Jose and Penang. Lead the development of 5 highly innovative new product with UL & FCC approval and all put into mass production. One of the product designs yielded a level of capability and performance that no other company in the world was able to achieve. Put into place engineering infrastructure, PLM, CAD/CAM and other internal development and prototyping tools. Sadly, OptoElectronix closed in December 2009 after failing to secure round B funding.

  • Director, Silicon Valley Design Center

    Pure Digital Technologies

    (Privately Held; Consumer Electronics industry)

    November 2007 — October 2008 (1 year )

    Manage group responsible for firware engineering, electrical engineering, optical engineering, and mechanical engineering for company that produces digital video camcorders under the "Flip Video" brand name. Tripled the size of the engineering group, brought in Cruise Control, Omnify PLM, Jira, Pro-E, Cadence engineering tools, setup development processes. Lead the design of new HD video cameras based on Zoran processors. Lead design of new development platforms, high density cameras and Lithium-Ion based power management systems. Moved the engineering group from a tiny remote office into a state of the art design center (literally located the office space, drew the floorplan, whipped the IT contractors to get the network completed on time and installed some of the equipment myself in order to meet schedule). The good times there were good but the exit strategy and global economy motivated me to explore opportunities in less precarious industries.

  • Sr Director Platform Engineering at Transmeta

    Transmeta Corp

    (Public Company; 11-50 employees; TMTA; Semiconductors industry)

    March 2003 — April 2007 (4 years 2 months)

    Managing group responsible for product design, reference design, validation board design, power supply design, test automation, documentation and signal integrity for low power x86 compatible computing platforms. Lead the product development team for the Microsoft FlexGo secure computing platform development project. Traveled to Seattle and China extensively for Microsoft design activities. Ghost wrote under contract to Microsoft their white paper on hardware security for PC platforms. Lead development of a custom ultra-secure flash memory device using new and novel package technologies. Lead the engineering development of the Microsoft Media Center PC program. Produced the lowest power 1.7GHz media center PC in the world. Lead the development of a new hardware platform supporting PCIe, 1GHz HT, SATA and AC-3. Transmeta has now closed down the entire VLSI, SW and Platform Engineering groups. I have shut down my engineering group and now looking to place many former employees.

  • Secretary

  • Director of Systems Engineering

    Portalplayer

    (Public Company; 51-200 employees; PLAY; Semiconductors industry)

    2002 — 2003 (1 year )

    Managed engineering group responsible for system architecture, reference designs and silicon validation for System on Chip semiconductor manufacturer. Portalplayer produced ASIC's for the MP3 audio market that feature dual Arm 7 processors with integrated IDE, USB, AC97, I2S, SDRAM interface and flash memory controllers. Created reference designs that lead to several successful MP3 player products. Brought in new tools and methodologies that greatly improved the audio performance of Portalplayer's products. Created roadmap for new features, functions and platforms for advanced MP3 players. Walked into a highly disorganized situation and turned it into a highly productive and highly focused group. Managed group through transition of engineering staff to India which lead to the eventual end of my position with Portalplayer.

  • Hardware Engineering Manager

    Philips Electronics

    (Public Company; Semiconductors industry)

    1999 — 2002 (3 years )

    Managed engineering group that created Internet terminals and set top box products that have sold more than 1,000,000 units. Developed Philips brand WebTV consumer internet terminal products Developed Philips brand Tivo digital video recorder products. Developed Philips brand DirectTV satellite products Created development plans, architecture plans, department budgets and engineering processes. Managed agency compliance and homologation activities for worldwide sales. Implemented new design tools and development systems including the use of Mentor Design tools and Agile PDM. Participated in architecture development for custom ASICs that performed multiple MPEG 2 stream decode and MIPS R5000 core for OS support. Held group together through six management re-organizations, recruited key personnel, kept moral high and department focused throughout company changes. Philips eventually moved all their product development back to Eindhoven.

  • Director of Engineering

    Orchid Technology

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Computer Hardware industry)

Represented company at all trade shows. Company eventually purchased by Harman International (parent to JBL).
