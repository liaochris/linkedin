David Henderson’s Summary

I have extensive training and experience in genetics, genomics, statistics, and statistical computing. I know several computing languages (C++, Octave/Matlab, S/R, SQL, PERL, shell script, SAS) and am learning some new ones (C# and Python). I have served as an Assistant Professor for a major university and have worked in research departments in two international corporations.

I am interested in furthering my career by directing a research group in biotechnology involved in some aspect of statistical computing and/or statistical genetics. I am also interested in opportunities that will apply and strengthen my data analysis and experimental design skills.

David Henderson’s Specialties:

Statistical Genetics, Statistical Genomics, Biotechnology, Bioinformatics, Statistical Computing

  • Research Associate

    Iowa State University

    (Educational Institution; 10,001 or more employees; Biotechnology industry)

    April 2002 — December 2002 (9 months)

    Developed a C++ program for analyzing gene expression microarray data. Along with my postdoctoral advisor, Dan Nettleton, and another faculty member in the Statistics Department, Tapabrata Maiti, we developed an innovative shrinkage estimator for individual gene variance estimates used in testing for differential gene expression. I then took the written mathematical description of this estimator and programmed it in C++. This program was used to analyze data from muscle biopsies in Rats performed by Dr. Jim Reecy's group also at Iowa State University.

    I also taught a course in introductory statistics for non-statistics majors. My section included approximately 30 students. My duties included creating course material, creating and grading tests, and developing laboratory material and exercises.

    I also received training from a grant writing consultant on how to write NSF and NIH grants. During this exercise, I submitted a NSF grant on statistical genomics.

  • Graduate Research Assistant

    Virginia Polytechnic Institute and State University

    (Educational Institution; 10,001 or more employees; Biotechnology industry)

    August 1998 — December 2001 (3 years 5 months)

    Had two research projects while studying statistics and genetics. First project involved developing a multiple trait quantitative trait loci mapping tool. This is essentially a large mixed model with two random effects that follow inheritance traced through a pedigree. Information from the inheritance of marked genomic regions is also used. The second project involved identifying genetic members of metabolic pathways using factor analysis. I developed a Bayesian exploratory factor analysis method and implemented it in C++. I also used simulation to create data from metabolic pathways using the software Gepasi. My coursework was almost entirely from the Statistics Department as I already had a background in genetics.

  • Graduate Research Assistant

    South Dakota State University

    (Educational Institution; 1001-5000 employees; Biotechnology industry)

    August 1993 — October 1995 (2 years 3 months)

    Studying molecular genetics in beef cattle. Research project involved investigations of Kappa casein and Beta lactoglobulin as maternal markers of pre-weaning calf performance. Concept was genetic variants in both milk solid genes may encode nutritional differences that the calf could exploit during nursing. Extracted DNA from 600 cows and calves, used RFLP-PCR and gel electrophoresis to genotype subjects. Used proc mixed in SAS to analyze data.
