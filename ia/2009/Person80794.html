Francis Friedman’s Summary

Francis J. Friedman is president of Time & Place Strategies, Inc., a New York City-based full-service consulting firm which serves the domestic and international tradeshow, exhibition, event and association communities.

Under Friedman’s leadership, Time & Place Strategies provides consulting services to clients in strategic planning, business development, branding, sales, marketing, as well as mergers and acquisitions.

Francis draws upon his extensive industry experience to help owners of organizations and organizers of tradeshows and events boost scope, client base, and profits.

He earned a B.S. Degree From San Jose State University in Industrial Relations and an MBA Degree in Marketing and Manufacturing Management form the Kellogg Graduate School of Management, Northwestern University.

Francis Friedman’s Experience

  • President

    Time & Place Strategies, Inc.

    (Management Consulting industry)

    1991 — Present (18 years )

    Friedman is well known as an industry futurist whose articles and speeches have pointed the way for many of the changes which have taken place -- and ARE still taking place -- in the industry. His by-line has appeared in such influential tradeshow and association industry publications as Expo, Tradeshow & Exhibit Manager, Exhibitor, Business Marketing, TSNN.com’s ExpoFiles, ASAE’s Association Management, and Cintermex to name a few.. Friedman has also addressed major industry groups, including IAEE, PCMA, ASAE ACME, MPI, CITAC and TSEA, and spoken at leading events including TS-2 and the Exhibitor Show. Internationally, he has spoken in Budapest, Florence, Helsinki and Prague, as well as Mexico, Singapore, Korea and Australia. He is an author of "Secrets of Exhibiting Success", popular among tradeshow exhibitors, producers and association meeting planners, and Friedman contributed key chapters to each edition of the IAEE-sponsored college textbook, "The Art of the Show".

  • Vice President, Corporate Development

    The Interface Group

    (Management Consulting industry)

    1987 — 1991 (4 years )

    Francis has been a tradeshow industry leader and executive since 1987 when he joined The Interface Group, producer of then-giant COMDEX®, as vice president, corporate development. At Interface, he created and implemented a domestic and international merger and acquisition program, headed new tradeshow startup and joint venture development programs, and created the global branding strategy for COMDEX.
