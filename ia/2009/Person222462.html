Steffan Ileman’s Summary

When I first started my travel career and proposed a modest sales incentive plan, the manager thought I was a naive kid. Fortunately upper management thought it was worth a try. Sales jumped and quadrupled in six months. Soon I was appointed manager.

Do you have accountants or clock watchers in your sales department that are cramping your growth? Is your call center staffed with order takers or sales people? Is your service rated less than excellent?

Incentive plans don't work unless you have the right type of people at the right position. Our competitors and suppliers were always amazed at our productivity that was out of proportion to the number of employees. If you have a service business and sales are sluggish despite advertising, or you think you should be doing much better, you may benefit from a sales efficiency analysis and reorganisation.

If your company spends a substantial amount of money on travel, you may want to know if you're getting the "best possible" deals from the airlines, hotels or your corporate travel agency. You may benefit from a cost-supplier analysis and an informed renegotiation with suppliers to maximize your travel buying power.

If your international business deal is important, and you wish to avoid potential pitfalls and problems, it's important that you fully understand the business culture you are dealing with, or employ negotiators that do. Don't rely on local interpreters to do your business.

I'm an entrepreneur and management/business consultant with extensive experience in all aspects of retail and wholesale travel industry, merchandise marketing and international trade. I've had exposure to most world cultures and done extensive business with the Japanese, Chinese and peoples of the Middle East.

I've also clocked over 100 hours of courtroom experience as a lay litigant and my company's legal representative, most of it successfully.
