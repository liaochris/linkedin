David Martin’s Experience

  • Chairman

    The Martin Group

    (Real Estate industry)

    February 1984 — Present (25 years 4 months)

    The Martin Group is a privately owned real estate development company which focuses on urban in-fill mixed-use developments in the major markets within California. With a total portfolio exceeding $4B, sample projects include;
    1. Emery Bay; 80 acre redevelopment of the Emeryville, CA waterfront with 552,000 sf office, 375,000 sf retail, 684 residential units and 300 hotel rooms.
    2. Hamilton Field; 300 acre redevelopment of a historic Army Airbase in Novato, CA, with 900 homes, 400,000 sf commerical, 150,000 sf retail and 150 hotel rooms.
    3. Downtown Pleasant Hill; development of a new downtown for the City of Pleasant Hill, CA, with 335,000 sf retail, 75 homes, City Hall and 150 hotel rooms.
    4. 1100 Wilshire Blvd.; adapative reuse of a 37 story office tower in Los Angeles, CA, with 228 homes and 10,000 sf retail.
    For more information: www.TheMartinGroup.com

  • Chairman, President, CEO & Director

    YPO

    (Non-Profit; 51-200 employees; Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    2001 — 2008 (7 years)

    Founded in 1950, YPO-WPO is the world's largest network of CEOs with over 18,000 members in 100 countries. During my tenue, the following is some of the accomplishments;
    1. Membership more than doubled,
    2. Events more than trippled,
    3. Business, Personal and Social Enterprise Networks were launched,
    4. Financial Reserves grew from a negative number to over 6 months of operating costs
    5. Significant funding was returned to the Member's Regions
    6. Quality rating reached an all time high
    7. Membership Satisfaction grew to an all time high.
    For more information; www.YPO.org

  • CEO and Director

    Burnham Pacific

    (Real Estate industry)

    1995 — 2000 (5 years)

    Burnham Pacific Properties (BPP) was a real estate investment trust (REIT) which was traded on the New York Stock Exchange. During my term as CEO, BPP became the exclusive partner with the California Public Employees Retirement System (CalPERS) for all non-mall retail property investments nationwide. As a result, it quickly became one of the largest and fastest growing REITs in the US. The company was liquidated as a result of a hostile take over attempt.

  • Partner

    CPS Real Estate

    (Privately Held; 201-500 employees; Real Estate industry)

    1981 — 1984 (3 years)

    I joined CPS as a Partner in 1981 to lead the development of Hacienda Park, an 860 acre, 13 million square feet, master planned mixed-use development in joint venture with Prudential Insurance Company of America. At the time, Hacienda was the largest such development in California. I left in 1984 to start The Martin Group.

  • Regional Manager & Vice President

    Union Bank of California

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; UB; Banking industry)

    1979 — 1981 (2 years)

    I joined Union Bank in San Francisco in 1979 as a real estate lender and established the Silicon Valley Real Estate Center for the Bank in 1980 which I lead as the Regional Manager. The Region quickly grew into one of the largest in the Bank. I was also one of the youngest Vice Presidents' in the Bank's history. I left the bank to enter the real estate development business as a Partner with one of my customers, CPS.

  • Vice President-Real Estate Lending

    Central Bank

    (Privately Held; Banking industry)

    1977 — 1979 (2 years)

    I joined Central Bank after college and completed their real estate lending traning program and became an active real estate construction lender in the southeast US. I left to join Union Bank in California.
