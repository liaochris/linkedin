K. Kris Hirst’s Summary

Currently, I am a temp technical writer, working with Pearson publishing company translating some of their business processes into organized English. It is the latest version of how I've been able to put my peculiar set of abilities as a translator of technical writing to work. After decades of experience writing and editing, I find that I can translate even the most tricksy, detailed technical writing into plain, non-insulting English, and the Pearson gig is a perfect example of that.

I am also a freelance science writer and ex-archaeologist, bent on making the sweet hidden bits of archaeological science available to the public and described in plain English. The best place to do that, I've found, is on About.com, where I get to run the Archaeology page and write whatever I please in whatever detail I choose. Most of my recent experience has been online but I have also occasionally contributed to Science and Archaeology magazines.

For about nine months I worked as a news aggregator at Mahalo.com, a site aimed to keep users on top of the latest news trends. I wrote summary articles for the hottest keywords on science, politics, and celebrity gossip; and it was a huge adrenaline rush. I quit when I got a temporary rush gig as an audio transcriber, which ended in September 2009.

I began working with the Internet in the mid-1990s, developing a webpage for the Iowa Office of the State Archaeologist. I was webmaster and document manager/archaeologist at the Louis Berger Group's Cultural Resources Group, and am part of the Wasteflake Project, a wiki begun in 2001.

In addition to having been a working archaeologist, I've been associated with the editing and writing business since the late 1970s, managing and editing traditional academic journal publications and technical reports in paper, pdf and html formats.
