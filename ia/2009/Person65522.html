Donald French’s Experience

  • President

    F8 Technologies

    May 2008 — Present (1 year)

    F8 Technologies specializes in delivering software for Real Estate document management.

  • SVP Research & Development

    Optio Software

    (Public Company; 51-200 employees; OPTO; Computer Software industry)

    August 2004 — April 2008 (3 years 9 months)

  • Founder and President

    VertiSoft Corp

    (Privately Held; Hospital & Health Care industry)

    September 1988 — August 2004 (16 years)

    Created and patented a Automated Medial Record Delivery System and licensed it to twenty-five hospital chains including Scripps and Sharp in San Diego, University of Colorado Medical School, and Banner Healthcare Colorado Division. Entered the international market with the licensing of the product by the Cromwell Hospital and NHS in London. I sold VertiSoft to Optio Software in August 2004 and joined them as SVP Healthcare Products.

  • Founder and President

    Epic Development Corporation

    (Hospital & Health Care industry)

    1983 — 1988 (5 years)

    I founded Epic Development after selling FMG indirectly to Lanier to contract development and maintenance work on the medical management system now called PractiCare I created. I provided development, maintenance, marketing and other services to Lanier to successfully release the product nationally.

    After Lanier decided to exit the healthcare software business I re-acquired the PractiCare product and formed Datix Corporation to own the product and merged Epic into Datix. Due to poorly written contract, Lanier was able to take the product back. I left and created VertiSoft to create a new medical system for small hospital.

  • Founder and President

    FMG Corporation

    (Hospital & Health Care industry)

    July 1978 — August 1983 (5 years 2 months)

    Founded FMG Corporation after leaving Radio Shack to create software and hardware for the TRS-80. Ported CP/M to the platform working with Gary Kildahl at Digital Research. Ported MicroSoft language product to the TRS-80 platform working with Bill Gates and Paul Allen.

    Purchased Graham-Dorrian Software and re-released the physician billing system as Medical Management System originally on CP/M and MP/M then ported to the IBM-PC.

    Sold the company to CGA Corp out of Holmdepl NJ which then sold it to Lanier Business Products in Atlanta GA. I then created Epic Development to contract maintenance and development for that product to Lanier.

  • Buyer/Product Manager

    Radio Shack Headquarters

    (Hospital & Health Care industry)

    August 1973 — June 1978 (4 years 11 months)

    At Radio Shack I was responsible for the company entering in to the personal computer market. I was also co-creator of the original TRS-80 computer and established Tandy Computer Centers for the company.

  • Store Manager

    Radio Shack

    (Public Company; RSH; Retail industry)

    September 1966 — August 1973 (7 years)

    Joined Radio Shack when they had 35 stores. I was the youngest store manager in their history and worked my way through college working full time and school full time. Was promoted to the corporate office in August 1973 to become Product Manager/Buyer for 22/27/50 series. There I created the original versions of the TRS-80 in my personal time. After my creation, the TRS-80, the most successful product in Radio Shack history, was released, I opened the first TANDY Computers in Tandy Center.

  • Weekend DJ
