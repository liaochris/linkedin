Valarie Cascadden, PhD, LMFT’s Summary

I am a humanistic, empathic therapist and coach who treats individuals, families, couples and groups, and specializes in mid-life issues. I support people through "decisions and transitions" at this turning point in their lives, and empower them to re-define their goals and create the lives they have always dreamed of!

Our clients may have done a great job "being there" for loved ones, but now they feel that it's "their turn," and don't want to waste any more time waiting to make the most of it!

I'm a "go-to" boomer who has experienced these issues myself. Many of us don't want to retire! We want to keep working and playing, doing what we love, finding our passions, and making our "next act" our "best act."

About me -- I began my own "next act" after age 40. After 22 years in the insurance industry, I obtained my Master's in Counseling Psychology from National University, and my doctorate in Clinical Psychology from Pacifica Graduate Institute. As a student of the psyche, especially from a female point of view, I did my dissertation on the lived experiences of 15 women who transitioned from Judeo-Christianity to Paganism as part of their individuation process. If you want to know more about how they found resonances in goddess archetypes, please feel free to ask me.

Valarie Cascadden, PhD, LMFT’s Specialties:

Cognitive therapy (finding alternative ways of thinking about problems); looking at a family unit as a dynamic system (and therefore open to positive changes); experience working with children in the foster care system; experience working with the frail elderly and their families; experience with working with patients in hospital inpatient and outpatient mental health units.

My specialty is midlife issues, including caregiver burnout, fear of one's own eventual aging, challenges with launching reluctant adult children, midlife career/academic transitions (such as going for that long-deferred, but much-desired degree).

    (Privately Held; 1001-5000 employees; Mental Health Care industry)

    I'm here four days a week in the Behavioral Health Unit. I divide my time between the Partial Hospitalization Program (outpatient) and the inpatient unit. I run therapy groups that have ranged from as few as 3 people to as many as 20. My approach in this setting is largely cognitive-behavioral-educational, and I also add my own humanistic touch when needed (with some Carl Whitaker-que humor!).
