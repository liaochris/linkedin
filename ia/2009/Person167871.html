monique lai’s Summary

YARDEDGE INTERVIEWS MONIQUE LAI

http://www.yardedge.net/art/jamaican-photographer-monique-lai-talks-about-when-fashion-meets-construction


IB WORLD by Regina Tosca

“I was pretty much jumping for joy,” Monique Lai (BBA ’96) said, when describing her response to finding out that her photography exhibit, “Private Property,” would be displayed at the famed ArcLight Hollywood.

“It’s [ArcLight] an amazing venue with huge exposure,” Lai said, in a gush of excitement that conveys the passion she brings to both her work and her life.

Lai’s star seems be rising in the world of art. In addition her show at ArcLight, she hit the road in August as director of Jamaica Farewell, a one-woman play written and performed by fellow expat Debra Ehrhardt. What, one may ask, ever possessed this multi-talented creative type to pursue a degree in business? Lai, chuckling, says she gets asked that from time to time.

“In many ways, creative talent is either something you have or you don’t,” she said. “But there is a business side to art as well, and if you are going to be successful you have to know who your audience is, who you’re creating art for.”

In addition, says Lai, her time in the College of Business Administration built her confidence by making her feel she could “do anything and rely on my own talents.” She noted that as a “different” sort of business student, she felt a lot of support from both peers and professors to express her authentic self and explore a wide range of interests and capabilities.

My intention in going into the arts is to inspire people to follow their passion and expose themselves to new experiences,” Lai said. “In many ways, that’s what going to business school [at the college] did for me.”
