Rico Chow’s Summary

As a person, I am self-motivated with exceptional leadership skills encompassing the values of integrity, trust and teamwork. Away from work I am a big fan of Scuba Diving, as well the UK I regularly enjoy diving adventures in Malta.

Graduated from The University of Manchester, I am reading an MSc in Human-Computer Interaction with Ergonomics. I study at the University College London Interaction Centre (UCLIC), a department that specialises in HCI research.

In collaboration with Paperstone, my MSc project involves understanding users of their website in order to create suggestions for its structure and design. In addition, my project aims to provide knowledge to the HCI community by generating theory about power and social relationships relating to the process of stocking office supplies within the context of an organisation.

I am planning to use a range of qualitative methods for data gathering including Diary Studies, Interviews and Fieldwork. Grounded Theory will be used to analyse the data.

My research supervisors are Jennifer Rode (UCLIC) and Max Trotter-Landry (Paperstone).

Rico Chow’s Experience

  • Researcher

    Paperstone Ltd

    (Business Supplies and Equipment industry)

    March 2009 — Present (2 months)

    Through the Knowledge Transfer Partnership (KTP) with UCL, it was decided that Paperstone would benefit from the restructuring of their website. In order to redesign the website, understanding its users is vital. More importantly, understanding the office supply restocking process within these organisations would provide useful information on how users are currently making use of Paperstone and how this interaction could be improved.

    The focus of this project will be to explore the regular clients of Paperstone in order to understand how small businesses handle the process of stocking office supplies. This will include understanding how office workers handle their stationary and where the restocking process fit within the larger organisational workflow.
