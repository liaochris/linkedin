Peter Karsten’s Experience

  • Executive Chairman

    Cecure Gaming Ltd

    (Computer Software industry)

    April 2001 — Present (7 years 11 months)

    Executive Chairman for the world's leading provider of poker and casino services through cellular network operators such as Vodafone, T-Mobile, 3, O2 and Orange.
    Backed by Bessemer Venture Partners since 2006.
    Peter formed and built Cecure Gaming, and then reshaped Cecure Gaming to become a Mobile Poker Network Operator by heading partner relationships and forming alliances with key industry players.

  • Sonera Senior Vice President

    Sonera SmartTrust

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; Telecommunications industry)

    October 1998 — October 2000 (2 years 1 month)

    Sonera SmartTrust was a global leader in secure wireless commerce. Delivered items such as the world’s first WAP phone with digital signatures, the world’s first Bluetooth vending machine, and the world’s first 3DES and PKI secured prepaid top-up services – nominated as most innovative product at the 2001 GSM World Congress in Cannes.

  • VP Mobile Banking

    Citibank

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; Banking industry)

    January 1998 — October 1998 (10 months)

    Responsible for Citigroup’s wireless strategy. The link between Sonera SmartTrust and Citigroup was created at this time, culminating in the joint venture company 724 Solutions (Sonera SmartTrust, Citibank, Bank of America and Bank of Montreal).

  • Wireless Services Business Development Manager

    Nokia Mobile Phones

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; NOK; Telecommunications industry)

    June 1991 — January 1998 (6 years 8 months)

    Wireless Services Business Development Manager at Nokia for 7 years, and wrote several World First business plans, including downloadable ringtones, business cards, over-the-air configurable menu items (pre-wap), and for the first Communicator. Prior to Nokia, Peter Karsten held international sales roles for a semiconductor companies for five years.

  • Sales and Application

    Waferscale Integration

    (Semiconductors industry)

    June 1989 — June 1991 (2 years 1 month)

    Sales to Europe, Middle East (e.g. Tadiran), Japan and South Korea.
    High speed communications semiconductors to major companies in consumer and military communications and graphics. Applied in base stations, radar, sonar, graphics and arbitrary high speed interfaces.

  • Business Development Manager

    Nordisk Elektronik

    (Semiconductors industry)

    June 1984 — June 1989 (5 years 1 month)

    Nordisk Elektronik AB, which owned companies such as Traco AB, dominated semiconductor sales in the Nordic region during the 1980's. Peter Karsten was responsible for finding and introducing new suppliers to the Nordic region. He found and signed distribution agreements with VLSI Technology, Waferscale Integration, Three Five, Sprague, Logic Devices and Hyundai. He then successfully sold VLSI Technology PC chip sets to companies such as Electrolux, ASEA, SAAB, Volvo and Ericsson, and high speed Waferscale memory products to Ericsson, Nokia, SAAB and Bofors.
