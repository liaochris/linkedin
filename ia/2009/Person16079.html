Angus Davis’s Summary

I helped start a small New England ISP in 1995. I moved to California to join Netscape in 1996. Co-founded Tellme with Mike McCue in 1999; acquired by Microsoft in 2007.

Outside of work I am active in education reform, serving on the state board of education in Rhode Island and on the Board of the Center for Education Reform in Washington, DC.

Angus Davis’s Specialties:

Product/technology strategy, sales, early stage technology investing, product marketing, etc. The stuff you learn over a decade of start-up life.

Angus Davis’s Experience

  • Co-Founder

    Tellme Networks

    (Privately Held; 201-500 employees; Telecommunications industry)

    January 1999 — Present (10 years 5 months)

    Founded Tellme in 1999 with Mike McCue. Went through many ups and downs, learning experiences and different roles over our journey. Grew business to profitability with over $100 mm in annual revenue. Shaped software-as-a-service that redefined the industry and changed the way people use their telephone with pioneering speech interfaces. Latest milestone was Microsoft's acquisition of Tellme in 2007.

  • Product Management

    Netscape Communiations

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; NSCP; Computer Software industry)

    October 1996 — January 1999 (2 years 4 months)

    Led prototyping efforts as part of a small technology strategy group led by then-CTO Marc Andreessen. One interesting "special project" was serving as the technical / product resource to the legal team pursuing the DOJ case against Microsoft (ironic since I would later work for Microsoft). I was product manager for the technology today known as Gecko, the core technology inside the Mozilla Firefox Web Browser. Worked on the AOL merger and the launch of Mozilla.org before leaving to start Tellme.

  • Product Management / Development

    IDS Network

    (Internet industry)

    July 1995 — October 1996 (1 year 4 months)

    Led Web site development for medium-sized businesses like radio stations, TV networks and car dealerships (common customer quote: "We need to get on the information super-highway!") that were establishing their initial presence online at the dawn of the Web. Raised the financing for the company from angel investors. Notably, we had a Class B IP address allocation, which was sort of cool looking back nowadays (155.212).
