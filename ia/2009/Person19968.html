Arne Hessenbruch’s Summary

Arne Hessenbruch believes in listening attentively to his clients and in working with them to come up with the best financial solutions to fit their needs and concerns. Dreams and anxieties usually have a money component. Arne has a PhD in history of science from Cambridge University in England, and has conducted research at Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT), at Harvard University, and at several universities in Europe. He still teaches one course a year at MIT on how to bring new technology to the market.

Arne has three children, born in 1989, 1993, and 2006. He resides in Arlington, Massachusetts, where he is a Director of the Historical Society and produces videos for the Town’s cable channel. He is also actively involved in the local soccer community, both as a player and as a referee. At his son’s school, Boston College High School, he organizes Career Night under the auspices of the Fathers’ Group.

Arne grew up in Denmark. He is the New England representative of Danes Worldwide, the Boston City Manager of Danes Abroad Business Group Online, and an active member of the Danish Society of Massachusetts. He has lived in half a dozen different countries and speaks many languages. He is also an avid reader, plays as much soccer as he can get away with, especially in the aptly named Over The Hill Soccer League, and at times he loses himself in the intricacies of chess.

Arne’s overarching interest is the human condition and he delights in really trying to understand his clients.
