Charles Stromeyer’s Summary

Once worked in Harvard University's Division of Engineering and Applied Sciences (DEAS) in the field of neurobiology.

Helped Benjamin Koo obtain his PhD from MIT's Engineering Systems Division (ESD). For example, you can see my name on the fourth page of his PhD thesis which I correctly edited for any language or mathematical errors:

http://opn.mit.edu/images/Submitted_Thesis_BKOO.pdf

Worked as an investment consultant to only professional accredited investors or traders.

I always started from a global-macro perspective simply because no economy or market exists alone in a vacuum, and I specialized in various derivatives markets.

I was the first advisor to the Mariner Equity Fund which was one of the highest performing long-short equity hedge funds ever.

To the best of my own knowledge, my long term performance track record of all my investment and trading recommendations since inception in January 1994 to January 2009 was second best in the world with a 29% net average annualized rate of return, second only to that of James Simons of Renaissance Technologies who has clearly beaten me with a roughly 35% net average annualized rate of return for his Medallion Fund since inception in 1989.

During these 15 years, my recommended trades had 96% winners and thus 4% losers which means that my win/loss ratio was roughly equal to that of Warren Buffett except that Buffett has achieved his win/loss ratio over a much longer time frame of about 44 years.

I have documented evidence too that I have recently and successfully advised various faculty members at Harvard University in my former field of neurobiology.

I have some knowledge of small scale renewable energy and efficiency technologies and projects, and some expertise in the economics and markets of emerging economies.
