Dan Powers’s Summary

I am responsible for advocating for local and state policies and regulations of benefit to nearly 1700 Chamber member businesses. Frequent involvement in the planning stages of new policies is a key aspect of my role. I am responsible for communication strategies regarding Chamber advocacy efforts for Chamber newsletters and the media; manage the activities of the Chamber's Community Affairs Council; present and speak to Boulder City Council and other organizations on behalf of the Chamber Board of Directors and develop membership surveys and polls to help determine Chamber priorities.

2009 key activities: Organizing the developing industry cluster of the Boulder area's clean/green technology businesses and attendant organizations via the CleanTech Boulder effort (see website above). The goal is to synergize numerous organizations' supportive efforts to ensure Boulder remains a leader in promoting the growth and vitality of such companies. Advocating for reasonable and equitable changes to a range of policy issues from building regulations to the city's Carbon Tax (first in the nation) to development fees and growth control measures. The 2009 Boulder City Council election and the Climate Action Plan implementation are key issues as well.

I am also responsible for organizing and/or representing the Chamber at a wide range of workshops, forums and various working groups on topics ranging from building regulations to taxes, federal code compliance to utility fees, health care reform to immigration laws. Events and forum series I am responsible for include Climate Leaders, 501 Club-Boulder, Issues Over Easy and CleanTech Boulder. I also assist the Boulder Economic Council with projects.

Additionally I am championing the effort to move the Chamber's internal activities to zero-waste and other sustainable and energy efficient goals.

See more information on the Advocacy efforts I oversee at this link: http://www.boulderchamber.com/advocacy.aspx.
