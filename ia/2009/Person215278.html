Toby Shapshak’s Summary

I’ve done a mixed bag of jobs in journalism, starting out in hard news, working for wire services, investigative reporting, covering politics, running two online newspapers, editing a national newspaper’s sport and technology sections at the same time and editing a men’s magazine.
I am the editor of Stuff, the South African edition of the world’s largest-selling gadget magazine. I write a column for The Times newspaper and am a contributing editor for Business Day’s Wanted magazine.
Until the recession struck, I was the digital publisher of Avusa's magazine division, taking 50-something print brands online.
I was the Telkom ICT Journalist of the Year for 2002, and was a runner-up in the mainstream newspaper category in 2003 and 2004. I also won the fixed telecommunications category in the 2003 Profile Awards.
I have written for the Mail & Guardian, Sunday Times, Sunday Independent, Financial Mail, This Day, The Observer in London, and various local magazines, including GQ, GQ Cars, Maverick, Empire, Best Life, Elle Decoration, Visi, Marie Claire, Fair Lady, Cosmopolitan and SA Sports Illustrated.
I was a contributing editor for GQ for 10 years before launching Stuff in November 2007 for Avusa.
I was the M&G’s technology editor on three occasions, having been both the paper’s sports editor and technology editor during my first stint. I edited the sports section through three World Cups. I ran the then Daily Mail & Guardian, now known as the M&G Online, when it was the country's largest online news site.
I covered the Truth and Reconciliation Commission for the national news agency Sapa, amongst other beats, including shadowing Nelson Mandela’s Gauteng activities when he was president.
For the six years prior to joining Avusa I was running Maven Media, where we published numerous magazines and managed major website projects for blue chip clients.

Toby Shapshak’s Specialties:

Writing, editing, magazine production, investigative reporting, dealing with stringers and advertisers, reviewing new technology, writing lists like this.
