James Florey’s Summary

Mediphonics is a start-up service provider for scheduled compensated medical communications between physicians and their patients. The company provides a patented web-enabled automated system for managing telephone appointments, documenting the transactions and collecting payment – resulting in alignment of incentives for the entire value chain to efficiently and effectively improve clinical outcomes and to increase patient, physician, and payer satisfaction. I rewrote the business plan, redefining the customers and target markets and re-devising the revenue model and pricing for each of them. The prospect of the company changed from a small direct-to-consumer model to an annually recurring $10 billion healthcare payer-based customer market. The CTO and I led the redesign of the product and user interface and a working prototype was built. I built a management team and eminent Board of Advisors. We wrote a cap table and investor and customer presentations, which I gave. We secured angel capital and presented to VC. We secured commitment from seed round investors and first customers in three market segments three times, each of which abruptly fell through due to external circumstances: an external threat to a customer, Katrina, and the outpatient VA scandal and 2006 national election. Having taken the company to first customer and investor capital three times exhausted our angel capital. Currently the patent is being offered for licensing and royalties.
