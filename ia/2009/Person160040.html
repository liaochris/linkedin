Meryl Ginsberg’s Experience

  • Senior Public Relations Manager

    Varian Medical Systems

    (Public Company; VAR; Medical Devices industry)

    November 2000 — Present (9 years 1 month)

    Varian is a publicly traded company that primarily provides hospitals and clinics with technology for treating cancer. Manage media relations and projects in corporate, marketing, customer, and employee communications. Assist in establishing annual PR goals and objectives, and strategies for achieving them. Establish strong internal relationships with product managers, researchers, engineers, and marketing personnel to keep corporate communications activities aligned with business priorities. Manage a “hospital partners PR program” to help hospitals promote new technology. Help to develop the company's annual report; generate content for the internal and external Websites, including patient interviews and case studies. Manage external vendors, including freelance writers, web page designers and programmers, media monitoring and print collateral design firms. Plan and execute corporate and employee-oriented events, including an annual United Way campaign.

  • Director of Communications

    Alliant International University

    (Non-Profit; Higher Education industry)

    1994 — 2000 (6 years )

    Directed the corporate communications functions for a graduate school of clinical psychology (the California School of Clinical Psychology, or CSPP, with campuses in four California cities. Just before I left, CSPP merged with another institution and became Alliant International University). In this position, I executed projects in student recruitment, institutional and faculty advancement, public affairs, and media and alumni relations. Supervised the production of newsletters, magazines, viewbooks, marketing materials, and website content; authored articles, position papers, speeches, press releases, and promotional materials. Managed a public affairs program to influence developments in the field of psychology; produced editorials, position papers, speeches, and Congressional testimony.

  • Marketing and Account Manager

    Concept Design Productions

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Events Services industry)

    February 1993 — November 1995 (2 years 10 months)

    At this corporate and special event production company, worked on corporate events from inception and budgeting through contract negotiation and fulfillment. Developed event concepts, solutions, budgets, and proposals in accordance with clients’ needs; made client presentations; managed event logistics; selected and supervised subcontractors; purchased goods and services. Handled all aspects of client relations before, during, and after events. Generated new business through direct mail marketing, advertising, and cold calling; presented the company’s capabilities to prospective clients. Managed our participation in industry trade shows; successfully placed numerous stories about the company in targeted trade publications.

  • Program Manager

    UCLA Extension

    (Higher Education industry)

    1987 — 1993 (6 years )

    Managed the Writers’ Program, a unit that planned, implemented, and promoted 350-400 adult education courses and conferences annually, with a gross income of approximately $1,250,000. Prepared budgets and monitored expenses. Devised and implemented marketing and promotion strategies. Supervised, trained, and evaluated a staff of four program representatives and their assistants. Oriented new instructors, many of whom were published writers but lacked teaching experience; helped them structure their courses and anticipate students’ needs. Organized and managed special events: departmental Open Houses; publication parties; readings; multi-day intensive workshops; instructor development seminars. Also served on a number of special cross-institutional task forces during an institutional reorganization effort; chairing the task force on market research.
