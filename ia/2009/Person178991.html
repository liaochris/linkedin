  • Osaka Daigaku

    MS, Computer Science, Artificial Intelligence, 1987 — 1990

    I believe this was ground breaking research because I discovered that to build expert systems you really need to interview more than just the so-called subject-matter experts. You need to go beyond that. That's when I came up with the idea of generic vocabulary and generic tasks, which would help me interact with various people, including subject matter experts. These generic vocabulary and generic tasks later became known as ontologies and are now the basis for the so-called Semantic Web.
