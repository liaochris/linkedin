Bob Mehta’s Experience

  • President

    Supremus Group LLC

    (Information Technology and Services industry)

    January 2006 — Present (3 years 6 months)

Entered into partnership with Thomson Inc (www.Thomson.com) to update their HIPAA manuals. Created different templates for Contingency Planning, which includes Business Impact Analysis, Risk Assessment, Disaster Recovery Plans and Business Continuity Plan.

Site has compliance white papers, related news articles, resources on the web, training, webinars, conferences, rules & regulation overview, job and solutions & products for compliance.

  • Sales Manager

    Shah Traders

    (Building Materials industry)

    December 1999 — November 2000 (1 year)

    Joined the organization as Sales Manger. The company is a wholesaler of industrial paints having dealership with major paint companies. A team of eight sales representatives was under my supervision. My main duties were to negotiate the best commission & terms with paint company, building good relations with the customers, helping the customer to find the best & cost effective paint system to fit their requirements, collecting payments, inventory management including projecting seasonal sales, increasing returns on sales expenses, identifying & targeting new market segments previously not served, reorganizing sales strategy, setting targets for sales team & helping them to achieve it. Due to persistent hard work and marketing strategy we increased sales for the year 2000 by 230% to Rs. 18 Million and were ranked as the best dealer for western region for Asian Paints Ltd.

  • Sales cum Administrative Manager.

    De Ster Ltd – Hongkong. (Delhi Representative Office)

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; Consumer Goods industry)

    April 1998 — November 1999 (1 year 8 months)

    De Ster ltd, Hong Kong launched its Indian operations in New Delhi in June 98. I joined as Sales cum Administrative Manager. The immediate task on hand after joining was to get the office operational as quickly as possible. After working long hours I was successful in opening the office before the scheduled date for opening. Since the products of the company were very new to India it was a challenge to create marketing strategy. My main duties were looking over day-to-day activities of the office, developing & managing departmental budget, coordinated marketing, importing, warehousing, & recovering payments, designed, created & conducted presentations for industrial products, identifying and educating prospective clients.

  • Sales & Marketing Director

    Topworth Investments Ltd – Macau (Mumbai Liaison Office)

    (Privately Held; 501-1000 employees; Financial Services industry)

    November 1995 — June 1998 (2 years 8 months)

    Started working as an Account Executive with Topworth Investments, a broker in the international currency market trading for clients in spot market. The concept of currency trading was brand new to India. I contributed to 20% of the turnover for the year. Within a year of joining I was promoted as a Marketing Manager supervising a team of 18 account executives. My team contributed to 40% of the turnover. In January 1998 was promoted as Marketing Director and was responsible for Mumbai liaison Office. My main duties were to identify & target new market segments previously not served, developed a sophisticated incentive/bonus plan, conceived & executed marketing plans, utilized sales data to focus efforts on greatest potentials, provided a daily feedback of day’s trading to head office, advising clients, taking reports from the marketing manager & helping them in sales. Revenue from Indian operations was highest among other offices in Asia & Africa.
