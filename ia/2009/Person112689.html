Jillian McRae’s Summary

I believe in the advent of Information Technology as a tool in the tool belt of the individual, the organization, and society – not the absolute solution to all issues. This tool must remain in the context of humanity in order to benefit society. It is from our nature over history that the elements and infrastructure of IT has been derived and these same elements and infrastructure will continue to imprint themselves on our lives through our future. We must remain responsible to this. Social and individual rights, consideration, and respect must remain paramount in the solutions that we derive through this tool.
A prime attribute to technology is automation. This attribute encourages us to responsibly automate those patterns in our information and nature that will benefit the individual, organization, and society. Thus, it is important for the technologist to not impede the responsible implementation of automation that might replace their current role in an organization or the industry. Otherwise, a technologist should strive to work themselves out of their current role through responsible automation.
My goal in my life and profession is to practice within these guidelines and to encourage and mentor others in this practice.
