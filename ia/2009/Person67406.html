June Shepherd, Ph.D.’s Summary

I have been leading workshops and providing psychotherapy for individuals, couples and families for the last 16 years through my private practice here in Austin. I am best known for my work in cooperative co-parenting and divorce.

I specialize in providing short term, solution focused coaching,
aimed at impacting your life so that you make the best decisions, take appropriate actions and produce your intended results……right now.

What I do best is train, educate and coach people on how to behave or perform on a daily basis in ways that are consistent with their values, goals, and integrity.

People who come to me for psychotherapy and performance coaching are often interested in
......taking a current relationship to a new level
......creating a good will agreement with their former spouse
regarding the co-parenting of their children
......re-inventing their marriage rather than getting a divorce
......confronting that it is time to leave a current job
......confronting the creation of a whole new career
......overcoming the fear of loving again after divorce
......getting connected & networking with others
......asserting yourself powerfully and asking for what you want in a personal or professional relationship
......bringing adult family members together harmoniously
......stopping old patterns of sabotaging yourself financially
......taking on health concerns powerfully
......getting over betrayal, disappointment or anger
......getting along with a parent, former spouse, sibling, etc
......forgiving yourself or someone for something "unforgivable"

Consistently causing a shift in the ways you are being, thinking and behaving is what makes the difference……and will make a difference in the lives of those around you.

I am soft spoken, focused and compassionate.I am a straight communicator and my intention is to support you in having the results you want for your life....not some day but now.

  • Boys Town National Research Hospital

    (Non-Profit; 201-500 employees; Mental Health Care industry)

    Boys Town National Research Hospital is a Communication Disorders Hospital that specializes in working with children who are deaf, hearing impaired, speech impaired, or mute. I provided psychological testing which included intellectual, academic, neurological, developmental, and personality assessments. I was a member of the Abused Handicapped Children Team and Craniofacial Team. An important part of my job was communicating in a kind and compassionate manner very difficult information for parents to receive for the first time regarding their child being diagnosed as deaf and/or mentally handicapped.

Psychiatric disorders we treated included schizophrenia, Tourettes, organic brain disorder, sexual disorders, and psychotropic medication withdrawal.
