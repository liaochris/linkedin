Jay Best’s Summary

I thrive on solving complex operational and business issues with innovative technology, new business models and old fashion hard work & tenacity. Some of the issues I’ve dealt with include:

- How do you enable credit card transaction processing, GPS tracking and on-board WiFi connectivity for a fleet of 50 buses with no net cost to the transit company and net revenue back to the transit company?

- How do you get over 30 vendors with products on line with hundreds of products is a web commerce portal that did not exist in under 6 months using 1999 technology?

- How do you correct the disastrous deployment of a consumer financial services application to mobile devices across North America for one of the largest financial services companies?

- Creation of a business model that allows for universities to build on campus cellular coverage for free, generate income, improve communication and establish themselves as innovators

Over the years I’ve worked to bring innovation to a number of different industries from environmental cleanup, financial services, transportation, and higher education to name a few. Most recently this has focused on the use of mobile and wireless technologies. In doing so I have been everything from software developer to purchasing agent. This gives me a broad set of experiences to tackle almost any issue.

Jay Best’s Specialties:

My expertise is in translating the needs of the business and customer into attainable objectives. Then meeting those objectives with existing or nearly existing technology and creative development of revenue opportunities. Recently, my focus has been on developing wireless and mobile technology.

Jay Best’s Experience

  • VP of Client Management

    Rave Wireless

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Wireless industry)

    April 2004 — August 2008 (4 years 5 months)

    Rave was founded to capitalize on the opportunity created by the mass adoption of mobile technology by college students. From 2002 to 2005, cell phones on college campuses went from an extravagance to a must have for college students. The impact to universities was profound. They found themselves unable to communicate with students and losing vast revenue streams associated with the operation of wireline telephone networks.

    Joining at its inception, I worked with universities first to identify and understand their issues. We then defined a solution that partnered Rave with the institution and the carrier. After defining the solution, my job was to implement a new business model that allowed the university to provide discounts to its students, while providing significant revenue to the university to offset the costs of the program. Essentially, we created a model that was self funding and beneficial to both the university and the student.

  • Co-Chairman of the CTI Convergence SIG

    LISTnet (Long Island Software & Technology Network

    (Telecommunications industry)

    January 2004 — January 2005 (1 year 1 month)

    I joined the CTI (Computer & Telephony Integration) SIG in early 2004 to add wireless subjects to several of their meetings each year. This group meets monthly and is always looking for interesting wireless and telecommunications presentations.

  • President & Founder

    Best Mobile Computing & Wi-RAN

    (Wireless industry)

    2002 — 2004 (2 years)

    How does a transportation company implement real-time credit processing from a moving vehicle? Add in their desire to provide Wi-Fi service and GPS vehicle tracking. All of these were difficult enough independently, be we wanted to do them simultaneously, and at no cost.

    By developing a single-board solid-state Linux computer with WiFi connectivity, GPS capability and a 3G data backhaul, we were able to accomplish all of these. In addition, we developed a low-cost handheld device application integrated with both a credit card swipe and a printer. To save development costs we used off-shore resources.

  • Director of Fincial Services

    Vettro

    (Wireless industry)

    July 2000 — March 2002 (1 year 9 months)

    I managed Vettro's financial services group as we created new products for the mobile financial workforce and their equally mobile customers.

    Was able to right the ship when Citi Group’s Smith Barney unit was struggling with a potentially disastrous deployment of a consumer hand-held trading application. Smith Barney was implementing a 3rd-party application and the deployment was suffering numerous delays and quality issues. I was able to work with a multi-organizational team to get the project back on schedule and resolve the quality issues.

  • Co-Founder and Chief Operating Officer

    Dad's World Enterprises

    (Internet industry)

    1999 — 2000 (1 year)

    Launched a new media company with a magazine and website for new fathers. I was involved with all stages of the business (concept, raising money, development, content creation, purchasing, e-commerce, etc) as we caught the dot-com wave and then crashed on its shore. On a very limited budget, we created a content management system, ecommerce engine, and set up products from over 30 vendors.

    My responsibilities included the design, oversight of the development, and implantation of all of the technology. In addition, I managed our partner relationships with 30 different product vendors who provided products and drop-ship services.
