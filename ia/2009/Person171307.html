Napoleon Biggs’s Summary

What is all this 'palava' about digital media?

My journey in Asian media started with reading 'dazibao' newspaper boards in Beijing in the late 80s, on graduation I got a job in Hong Kong sports sponsorship (do you remember the Michael Chang playing at the Salem Open in Hong Kong?) and satellite news services for the Associated Press where I clinched the first news deal with CCTV in China!

Then the internet came along...I leapt in with glee in 1998 to launch an online bandwidth exchange (ACE - Asia Capacity Exchange), sold it and formed Web Guru Asia, an interactive agency to serve all those gold diggers.

After six years advising all kinds of clients how to engage and entertain people over the web, I sold my shares and took time out to enjoy humanity, a new child.

But the internet syrens didn't stop calling and I returned to build a massively multi-player online game for adidas with Outblaze. Once this was launched, I ventured back out into the wilderness on my own (under Palava Digital) to advise media owners and large advertisers on how best to nurture the internet and it's ever emerging channels, from blogging, to search, to games, to twitter, to social media...and onwards.

Still, I never forget the timeless value of a face to face meeting or friendly phone call (even if it is on skype ot jaxtr!)
