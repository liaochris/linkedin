Don Foley’s Summary

I've been creating informational illustrations for over 25 years, and my company's client list includes some of the top publications in the country. In particular, I've been working with National Geographic for more than 13 years and my work can be regularly found in the publication.

As we face a major transition point in publishing for magazines, books and newspapers I'm working towards bridging the gap from traditional to digital media. In addition to the internet, new digital products will soon open up wide horizons.

Over the year's I've been guided by great minds such as Gary Cosimini Howard Finberg, George Rorick, Chris Sloan, Michael Mrak and Bill Baker. To them, I owe much.

Additional info:

Wrote and illustrated two books for Peachpit Press:
Animation and 3D Modeling on the Mac
Animation Tips and Tricks for Mac and Windows

College courses taught:
3D Modeling
Animation

Don Foley’s Specialties:

My specialty is detailed informational illustrations and diagrams. If you need to explain something visually...that's what I do. Throw your complex data at me, and I'll give you back an illustration that speaks to your audience.

It's about the reader. I have no illusion that I'm nothing less than a 'content provider.' and so my goal is to provide the most accurate and inviting infographic I can produce to help the reader understand the subject.

Don Foley’s Experience

  • Owner

    Newsinfographics and DonFoley.com

    (Media Production industry)

    1996 — Present (13 years)

    My company creates information based illustrations, animations and interactive experiences. As visual journalists, we've worked for publications such as National Geographic, WIRED, Scientific American, Popular Science, TIME Magazine, Sports Illustrated, Field & Stream, Outdoor, Outside Life, Backpacker, Readers Digest, The National Geographic Channel, The National Park Service, NASA, Newsweek, Men's Journal, Entertainment Weekly, Discovery Channel, Runner's World, Mountain Biking, Chicago Magazine, Toronto Life, Harcourt School Publishers and many, many more.

    A portfolio of our work can be found at: http://www.newsinfographics.com

  • Art Director

    Knight Ridder Tribune, News In Motion

    (Publishing industry)

    1992 — 1996 (4 years)

    Helped establish News In Motion (other founders: Bill Baker, Marty Westman and George Rorick), was responsible for creating daily animations on breaking news topics and later art directing a team of five animators (Kudos to George Rorick for having the vision to create "KRT Video Graphics").

    Designed many of the processes for being able to produce daily animations as well as designed a fiber optic to satellite uplink system (this was before in internet was available for product delivery).

  • Visual Journalist

    The Arizona Republic

    (Public Company; GCI; Newspapers industry)

    1988 — 1992 (4 years)

    Covered breaking news stories and feature stories as a visual journalist (kudos to Howard Finberg for creating this as a position at a time when no other newspaper had such a title) . I was also was in charge of innovation of technology in the art department, included writing scripts for automated daily updates (the weather page!) and porting graphics from art department computers to our pagination output via Adobe Postscript). I also spearheaded a team of artists in creating a new, cohesive infographics desgin stylebook.

  • Staff Artist

    Gainesville Sun

    (Public Company; Newspapers industry)

    1982 — 1988 (6 years)

    Began in News Graphics and then Ad Graphics departments while in college, then moved to Staff Artist position.

    Created artwork for breaking news stories and feature projects in the pre-computer days (Florocolor, rapidiographs and zipatone!).

    Then the Macs came out and changed the playing field.

    Was a founding member of the New York Times Graphics Network (kudos to Gary Cosimini who spearheaded the project) and an early adopter of computers in newsroom art departments. Beta tested Adobe Illustrator. Trained Florida NYT newspaper groups in incorporating computers into their art departments.

    I also played a key roll on the newspaper's redesgin team.
