Matt Cutler’s Summary

Intellectual property attorney with a practice that includes all varieties of intellectual property contested matters, as well as otherwise counseling emerging businesses up to multi-national companies with regard to their intellectual property portfolios.

Matt Cutler’s Specialties:

• Patent, trademark, copyright and trade secret litigation
• Freedom to operate opinions (non-infringement and/or patent invalidity)
• Trademark oppositions
• Due diligence relating to intellectual property acquisitions or divestitures
• Counseling emerging companies and established businesses regarding intellectual property matters throughout the world

Matt Cutler’s Experience

  • Principal

    Harness, Dickey & Pierce, PLC

    (Law Practice industry)

    October 2001 — Present (7 years 5 months)

    • Primary role in litigating both large and small intellectual property cases on behalf of plaintiffs and defendants, including through trial.
    • Experience developing and implementing intellectual property strategies on behalf of foreign and domestic clients.
    • Preparation of freedom to operate opinions (non-infringement and patent invalidity) and due diligence matters relating to IP acquistions or divestitures.
    • Provide seminars and training regarding intellectual property issues to emerging companies and established businesses.
    • Participation in licensing matters, trademark prosecution, trademark oppositions, and copyright issues.
    • Responsibility for recruiting attorneys to HD&P’s St. Louis office.

  • Associate Intellectual Property Attorney

    Senniger Powers

    (Partnership; 51-200 employees; Law Practice industry)

    May 1996 — October 2001 (5 years 6 months)

    • Primarily worked on intellectual property litigation matters, including the taking of depositions, preparation for and participation in Markman hearings, participation in trademark preliminary injunction hearing, primary client and opposing counsel contact.
    • Prepared numerous patent applications for a client in the semiconductor chip manufacturing business. Continued prosecution of those applications in the United States and abroad.
    • Participation in licensing matters, trademark prosecution, interference practice, and trademark oppositions.
