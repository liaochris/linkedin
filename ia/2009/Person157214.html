Ben Palmer’s Summary

My passion is to help people and businesses improve their current situation. I have run several businesses and consulted many. My focuses are in the areas of cost-reduction and running a lean, mean profit machine; as well as the creation, implementation, and successful achievement of a strategic growth plan that makes sense for the business; and how to effectively empower the human resources to take their organizations to the next level.

My current project (The Cash Coupon) is very exciting to me as I am creating the systems for and teaching people how to improve their financial future. Many need additional income and I believe that this is the greatest vehicle for them to achieve it. Getting paid to save money and help others save is a concept that will reach many. The Cash Coupon is just getting started and already many have been able to make it a successful full-time position. Those who are helping local small business owners with The Cash Coupon's low-cost, effective, and flexible advertising solution are earning an average wage over $20 per hour.

On a personal level, I want to help people achieve their maximum potential in order to live a life of great significance. Signifacance is measured by the impact you make in the lives of others. I believe that having a foundational faith is the key to this. Without a guiding hand, people quickly get lost. Life is a journey; never stop equipping and improving yourself. Set your goals, develop a plan, and give it your all. Nothing goes perfectly according to our plans, but if your heart is right and you give it your best effort, the cards will fall into place and you will be a top performer in the game of life.

I have the heart of a teacher and want to help people find joy in life (no matter what their situation).

Ben Palmer’s Specialties:

Communication; Project and Business Management; Team Motivation, Encouragement, Accountability, and Development; Training and Systems Creation; Time Management and Organization; and the Achievement of the Task at Hand.

    The College Years

    (Consumer Services industry)

    June 1999 — February 2003 (3 years 9 months)

    I always kept a full schedule and worked very hard. I served tables, tended bar, and even worked in a recycling factory as the owner recruited me (nearly all my jobs found me). One of my favorite jobs was with The Executive Committee, "Chief Executives Working Together". I worked as an assistant to Michele Bernstein, their Director of Marketing and a great woman. Michele bought me my first official business cards (Benefit Consulting). My experience at TEC gave me the desire to use my passion to help people by moving into teaching, professional speaking, coaching, and consulting (of course in addition to my investments into business ventures).

    I founded Benefit Consulting to help people find a vehicle to achieve their financial goals. It was "Making Dreams Reality" and "Some people dream of success, while others work hard at it". At 18, I was drawn to the industry of network marketing, direct sales, and home businesses. I was the kid that was going to make millions and traveled the country meeting the best of the best and learning from them. I had tremendous success, unfortunately my team was not seeing the same results. So with a heart to help people and not just make money, I stepped back to study the industry as a whole. What made some successful when others failed? I learned it was not the companies fault, but rather people not aligning with the right company or getting started with an improper understanding of what they needed to do. Sure people need to find a solid company, powerful compensation plan, and have the right training and marketing tools. But, more importantly, they need to find a product that they believe in and then do the work (input = output). There is no get rich quick. To be successful in business one must successfully move a product or provide a service. For 10 years, I have remained close, but outside the industry as I wanted to earn the respect of being successful in traditional business thus that is where I focused.

  • Misc. Experiences

    My Early Days

    (Consumer Services industry)

    April 1993 — May 1999 (6 years 2 months)

    I have always been entrepreneurial in my thinking. From ages 7-11, I would go to school with my pockets stuffed with "inventory" (candy and school supplies) and sell them for incredible profits. I would also mow lawns, baby and pet sit, shovel snow, rake leaves, clean houses, wash and wax vehicles, paper routes, etc. I was always finding ways to make a buck. I also learned the power of leverage early. I decided if I mow one lawn myself, I can earn $10. But, if I get 4 projects and hire my buddies to do the work; then I just take my cut for selling and managing the project. I am a great worker, but I learned that I enjoyed and was good at being the boss.

    At 12, I started working at the local country club as a caddy. I worked their in different compacities (caddy, bagroom attendant, server, bartender, bar and grill supervisor) until I was 19. The experience of surrounding myself with the most elite businessmen taught me that being a successful person in business was a powerful way to be able to make a significant impact.

    During high school, I was involved in several sports (Baseball, Football, Rugby, Wrestling). I even let my wife convince me to cheerlead with her for a year. She'll tell you that I did it so the other guys wouldn't try to make their move. Well, you protect what is good and to be honest it was a great time. Even with a very busy schedule, I found time to EARN a paycheck. I worked as a lot attendant at a local car dealership, a waitor at two different restaurants, and established a caddy program at another golf club.

    I was also very involved in just about every business program and by the time I graduated, I had over 100 business plans in my someday folder. I view an entrepreneur as someone who sees ways things can improve and is willing to take the risks to make it happen.
