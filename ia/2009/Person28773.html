Bill Taggart’s Summary

Senior Executive with 24 years of experience in corporate America. Worked for Wachovia Corporate for 13 years and IBM Corporation for 11 years. Life Member of the National Black MBA Association. Member of the Urban Financial Services Coalition, NAACP, and Urban League. BBA Howard University and MBA Harvard Business School.

Bill Taggart’s Specialties:

Business Efficiency, Organizational Effectiveness and Strategy Execution Consulting Services.

Bill Taggart’s Experience

  • COO

    Federal Student Aid, Department of Education

    (Government Agency; Financial Services industry)

    June 2009 — Present (3 months)

    The 1998 Reauthorization of the Higher Education Act (HEA) established a performance-based organization to administer the Federal Student Aid programs at the U.S. Department of Education. These programs include Pell grants, Stafford loans, PLUS loans, and the "campus-based" programs: Federal Work Study, Perkins loans, and Federal Supplemental Educational Opportunity grants. These programs provide almost $96 billion of financial aid a year to over 11 million college students attending 6200 post secondary education institutions - resulting in 30 million borrowers with a loan portfolio of nearly $600B.
