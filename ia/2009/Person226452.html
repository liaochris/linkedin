Sung Ko’s Summary

Sung Ko is a motivated young professional graduating with a B.S. in Finance, in the W.P. Carey School of Business at Arizona State University. Along with a Certificate in Small Business and Entrepreneurship, he has managed to gain experience through multiple internship and graduate with a 3.8 GPA.

Started his first internship at Intel as a sophomore in high school, he has gained 5 years of work experience in multiple finance, marketing, product development, and strategy roles.

He spent the summer of 2007 in Nike’s highly competitive Adrenaline internship program conducting research in developing their information management strategy. In the summer of 2008, he helped Institutional Investments group in multiple Private Equity and Hedge Fund, fund of funds research projects. Working directly with the Director of Research and Strategy, he designed, implemented, and trained all the institutional consultants on using Microsoft SharePoint to maximize collaboration.

Sung has been involved in many extracurricular activities including, Investment Banking Industry Scholars. This is a highly competitive curriculum that selects 20 of the top students and develops professional skills to place candidates into the most competitive careers after graduation. He is also a freshman business success course facilitator as well as My Life Venture entrepreneurship teacher assistant. He has guided and helped over 60 business students in strategies and techniques to be successful in college. Sung is also involved in the Spirit of the Enterprise Center as a Spirit Student Ambassador helping local start ups and companies with all aspects of business and entrepreneurship.
