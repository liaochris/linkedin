Jim Soderberg’s Summary

HOPKINS APARTMENT COMPLEX UNDERGOES EXTREME MAKEOVER
‘The apartments went from filthy, rundown and leaky to modern, stylish and beautiful!
It’s a phenomenal change!’
December 23, 2008 (Hopkins, Minn.) – Hopkins Arches had always been one of Hopkins’ most affordable rental communities, but after 45 years, it had fallen into disrepair with leaking pipes, and a host of structural and security issues. That’s until it underwent nothing less than an extreme makeover under the direction of Jim Soderberg, President of Soderberg Apartment Specialists, who renamed the property Second Street Station for the future light rail station that will be located directly across the street.

In merely four months, the complex was completely gutted and remodeled. Broken, stained, aging walls, floors, cupboards, fixtures, plumbing, carpeting, bathrooms and kitchens were replaced with amenities such as modern maple woodwork, granite countertops, maple-style floors, and stainless steel appliances – oftentimes in the course of a few hours.

“We take special pride in doing very high quality work fast, and we’ve been doing it for years. We work extremely effectively and efficiently, so we can keep costs low. That’s why our rents are so reasonable – incredibly reasonable when you consider that this is a condo-quality conversion; there are plenty of million-dollar homes that don’t have stainless steel appliances, granite countertops and maple cabinets,” notes Soderberg.

“Jim has exceptional vision,” notes Tom Cooper, Owner of Minnesota Brokers Group which sells apartment complexes. “If he’s visiting a distressed property, he can see its potential. Jim always makes a building much, much better than it was before. When I work with Jim, I feel like I’m doing more than selling a building, I’m making a contribution to our local communities.”
