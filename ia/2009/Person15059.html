Andy Blumenthal, MBA, CEA, PMP, A+’s Summary

A visionary IT and business thought leader and the founder of User-centric Enterprise Architecture, The CIO Support Services Framework and The Information Management Framework.

Currently, Chief Technology Officer (CTO) at the Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, Firearms and Explosives (ATF).

Previously, Chief Enterprise Architect for the U.S. Coast Guard (USCG) and the United States Secret Service (USSS).

More than two decades developing leading-edge technology solutions for premier organizations in both the private & public sectors, including the U.S. Departments of Justice, Homeland Security, and Treasury, the Dreyfus Corporation and IBM.

Master of Business Administration (MBA) in organizational behavior and a Bachelor of Business Administration (BBA) in Accounting, summa cum laude; a Chief Information Officer (CIO), Certified Enterprise Architect (CEA), Project Management Professional (PMP), E-Government Leader (EGOV), IT Infrastructure Library (ITIL) Foundations, Information Technology and Information Management (ITIM) and CompTIA A+ in Hardware and Operating Systems (A+).

Received numerous professional honors and awards, most recently the Department of Homeland Security (DHS) Team Excellence Award for Enterprise Architecture.

Regular public speaker, published extensively, featured in Federal Computer Week cover story and interviewed by Federal News Radio.

Lecturer at Carnegie Mellon University and National Defense University, and associate editor of the Journal of Enterprise Architecture.

Council Member American Council for Technology Industry Advisory Council, CIO Perspectives Regional Leadership Council, the IT Management Group Advisory Board, and the Government Technology Research Alliance.

Dual blogs at: The Total CIO (http://totalcio.blogspot.com) and User-centric Enterprise Architecture (http://usercentricea.blogspot.com).
