Steve Woodward’s Summary

In the 1980s, I helped American City Business Journals change the way newspaper business journalism was practiced, introducing the concepts of business expertise, sourcing outside the usual suspects, feet-on-the-street energy and snappy writing. Twenty-five years later, I have another chance to help change journalism, bringing databases, aggregation and social media to bear on local reporting, with distribution accomplished through microblogging.

Steve Woodward’s Experience

    Nozzl Media Inc.

    (Publishing industry)

    March 2009 — Present (7 months)

    Nozzl Media is creating a next-generation news service that looks and acts like a "newspaper" on Twitter or Facebook. But unlike a social-networking site, Nozzl Media offers a wide variety of original local content that can be filtered by consumers and delivered in real time straight to their smart phones. End users will never have to worry again about missing information that's important to them.

  • Reporter and editor

    (Privately Held; Newspapers industry)

    August 1988 — November 2008 (20 years 4 months)

    I took a buyout offer after 20 years at the paper. During those years, I reported on general news, business, health care, technology and Y2K. In addition to writing features, I also served as an assistant city editor and business editor.

  • Editor

    San Francisco Business Times

    (Newspapers industry)

    August 1987 — July 1988 (1 year)

    I inherited editorship of the paper, then only one year old, from its founder, Don Keough. Keough also was the founding editor of the American City Business Journals chain, which later acquired the San Francisco Business Times.

  • Executive business editor

    The Hartford Courant

    (Public Company; TRB; Newspapers industry)

    October 1986 — July 1987 (10 months)

    I was in charge of the daily and Sunday business sections of Connecticut's largest daily newspaper. I left to return with my family to the West Coast, where we felt more at home.

  • Editor, general manager

    Portland Business Journal

    (Newspapers industry)

    January 1984 — October 1986 (2 years 10 months)

    I was on the start-up team for Portland, which was the fourth paper in the American City Business Journals chain. I was later promoted to general manager, in charge of all departments of the paper; during my GM tenure, I overhauled the advertising rate structure and turned the paper cash-flow-positive.

  • Editor, associate editor

    Kansas City Business Journal

    (Newspapers industry)

    August 1982 — December 1983 (1 year 5 months)

    I was on the start-up team for the KC Business Journal, which became the first in the nationwide chain of business publications now known as American City Business Journals.

  • Reporter

    The Kansas City Star

    (Public Company; Newspapers industry)

    1979 — 1982 (3 years)

    I spent one year covering Kansas City's northern suburbs, one year covering City Hall, and one year covering science and medicine. I participated in coverage of the Hyatt Regency Hotel skywalks collapse in 1982, which earned the Star's staff a Pulitzer Prize for local news coverage.

  • Coordinator of displays, exhibits and advertising

    Wright State University

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    1975 — 1977 (2 years)

    I worked with the university's graphics department on newspapers ads, wrote articles for the in-house employee publication, oversaw traveling exhibits and assisted in writing press releases.
