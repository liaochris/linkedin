Christine Crandell’s Summary

For 20 years, Christine has been advising B2B enterprise technology CEOs, around the world, on business strategy and marketing. Her reputation is rooted in 3 personal mottos: ‘Take business personally’, ‘deliver impactful results’ each and every day, and ‘go outside the box’. Christine is recognized in Silicon Valley and European technology centers for her expertise in defining successful business strategies, driving sales and alliances, exploiting emerging trends that build thought leadership mindshare, and building marketing organizations that deliver qualified leads.
Her entrepreneurial and leadership experience was built on extensive international business dealings; highly relevant yet cost efficient, accountable marketing; and on building teams that deliver exceptional results. Her ‘sweet spot’ is working with companies to realize their next level of growth – whether it is fueled through M&A or international expansion, corporate turnaround or restructuring, or breaking out of performance plateaus.

She held executive leadership positions in global marketing, business development and strategy for publicly traded and privately held companies. She also spent eight years building New Business Strategies into a widely known and respected strategy consultancy serving enterprise technology vendors in North America and Europe. There she helped over 30 clients including Clarify (acquired by Nortel Networks), Oracle, SatMetrix, Interwise (acquired by AT&T), Indus, Rapt (acquired by Microsoft), Maconomy, and Workshare develop and successfully execute innovate business strategies that accelerated their growth.

Christine is a business advisor and frequent public speaker, writer, and guest lecturer on issues of technology, strategy and marketing. She holds a Masters degree in Management from Florida Atlantic University as well as completed post-doctoral studies at Golden Gate University. Christine calls the Santa Cruz Mountains in CA home.

Christine Crandell’s Specialties:

Business Strategy, marketing strategy and planning, product strategy and direction, new market entry, revenue growth planning, channel and sales management and international marketing and management.
