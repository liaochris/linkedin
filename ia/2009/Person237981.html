Trevor Bollers’s Summary

I provide a spark to any project that I get engaged in, immediately changing the focus from one of we can't get that done to one of what can we get done. I have turned around stagnant projects on multiple occasions saving a 40 million dollar contract from default, turning the product a frustrating tangle of pages to a streamlined focused web application. I thrive on challenges and see problem solving as the fun part of tackling a days work.

I provide sound leadership in positions that require me to take the lead, consistently fostering a team environment based on the fundamentals of shared responsibilities. I am a team player and have had multiple opportunities to be a part of teams that operate at very high levels. Those experiences allow me to rapidly adjust and do what is best for the team. I currently lead the usability/accessibility aspects of several multimillion-dollar Federal projects.

I am a researcher that can provide a human view of a technical world. With my advanced education and experience focused on the interactions that go in between humans and computers I can easily communicate with professionals and stakeholders on both sides of the fence. I have conducted research for the military on how to decrease the cognitive workload required of the user when participating in distance learning.

I am a volunteer and a community steward working with youth as young as 3 to teens as well as adults. As a student athlete I served on the Student Athlete Advisory Board. I now serve as a member of the National Iowa Varsity Club and the Minority Focus Group.
