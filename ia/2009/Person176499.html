Noah Melnick’s Summary

Noah is a U.S. Associate (Member of the New York Bar) in the New York office of Linklaters LLP, and is part of Linklaters’ U.S. Structured Finance and Derivatives practices. Noah has experience representing leading financial institutions, hedge funds, investment managers, insurers and other large corporates in connection with credit, equity, FX, IR, commodity and other plain vanilla and exotic derivatives and related products and contracts (cash and physically settled); repos and securities lending arrangements; prime brokerage arrangements and related documentation and amendments; CLNs; structured products; securitization (e.g. whole business and receivables), repackaging and related matters. Noah has additionally provided advice in respect of a variety of asset types including, commodities (base metals, bullion, etc.); emissions allowances and credits (CERs, VERs, RGGI, etc.); fund-linked products; power and energy bonds; airplanes; municipal bonds; credit cards; trade receivables; CDOs (all varieties), CLOs, RMBS and CMBS, and repackaging of same; as well as various other structured finance and derivatives matters. Noah has acted as deal counsel and review counsel on numerous cash, hybrid and synthetic CDOs (bespoke single tranche, multi-tranche and complex hypothetical capital structures) CDO2s and CLOs. Recently, Noah has advised a wide array of sophisticated multinational clients in respect of risk mitigation, prime broker/counterparty insolvency risk and various other matters relating to troubled assets and distress/opportunistic situations.

Noah Melnick’s Specialties:

Derivatives; commodities; securitization and structured finance; risk assessment and mitigation; prime brokerage and related documentation and amendments; and various other ancillary areas complimentary to the foregoing (see above).
