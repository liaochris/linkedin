Bart Hofstede’s Summary

Cultural attaché at the royal Dutch embassy in Berlin. Formerly policy advisor at the Dutch Ministry of Education, Culture and Science, sociologist of culture and cinema, professor of media and entertainment at Inholland University of Applied Sciences' School of Communication, Media and Music.

Bart Hofstede’s Experience

  • Counsellor and Head of the Press and Cultural department

    Royal Dutch Embassy in Berlin

    (International Affairs industry)

    April 2009 — Present (5 months)

    The Counsellor for Press and Culture of the Dutch embassy in Germany is commonly known as Cultural Attaché. I am responsible for public diplomacy and cultural relations, as defined by the Dutch governments' international cultural policy. The section's work is carried out by Dutch consulates in München, Düsseldorf and the embassy in Berlin, together with many organisations in Germany and the Netherlands.

  • Advisor

    Ministerie van Onderwijs, Cultuur en Wetenschap

    (Government Agency; Government Administration industry)

    2002 — 2009 (7 years)

    Policy advisor in the field of cultural policy and media policy. Responsible for Dutch creative industries policy (jointly with the Ministry of Economic Affairs) and themes related to the cultural programming of Dutch public broadcasters.

  • Professor of media and entertainment

    Inholland University of Applied Sciences

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    2008 — 2009 (1 year)

    A professor at a polytechnic university - 'lector' in Dutch - acts as a head of research, in my case in questions of creative industries. The professorship is connected to the media and entertainment programme at Inholland University of Applied Sciences (Hogeschool Inholland).

  • Director

    Nederlands Instituut voor Filmeducatie

    (Motion Pictures and Film industry)

    September 2001 — August 2002 (1 year)

    Responsible for the merger of four small subsidized organisations in order to set up the Institute; for policy, external relations and finances.

  • Director

    HOVO Rotterdam

    (Education Management industry)

    2000 — 2001 (1 year)

    Responsible for programme, personnel and finance: seven people at the bureau, 70 activities per year, 3000 students. HOVO organizes courses, lectures and travels at academic level for the elderly (elder than fifty years).

  • Researcher and teacher

    Erasmus Universiteit Rotterdam

    (Higher Education industry)

    1992 — 2000 (8 years)

    Published about film, photography and other subjects in the sociology of culture. Got my Ph.D. in 2000.

  • Board member

    Metropolis festival

    2000 — 2000 (less than a year)

  • Employee

Bart Hofstede’s Education

  • Erasmus Universiteit Rotterdam

    Ph.D. , Sociology , 1985 — 2000

    Dissertation: "In het wereldfilmstelsel. Identiteit en organisatie van de Nederlandse film sedert 1945" ("In the world film system; identity and organisation of Dutch cinema since 1945"), about changes in the international position of the Dutch film world. The concise edition ("Nederlandse cinema wereldwijd") has been reprinted.

    Activities and Societies:
    Masters degree in the sociology of culture with prof.dr. A.C. Zijderveld. Thesis about the professionalisation of photographers. Awarded with a national prize from the Boekman Foundation for best thesis in the study of arts and culture.
