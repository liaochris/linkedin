BJ Birtwell’s Summary

(Excerpt from Vanguard Magazine)
BJ Birtwell has become one of the premier trend-spotters and marketers in youth and urban culture. Birtwell's talent quickly caught the attention of a large, iconic American company, Chrysler, which asked him to become its National Youth and Urban Marketing Manager.

At the time Chrysler felt it had lost its connection with young drivers. The company was about to launch the newly designed 300 automobile, and its target demographic was a a traditional 50-year-old white male target demo. Birtwell had a different idea.“We saw it connecting with the 18 to 35 urban mindset consumer,” he says. While at Chrysler, Birtwell helped turn the re-introduced 300 into an automotive phenomenon by connecting it with young buyers and celebrities.

Working alongside marketing agencies and media outlets like MTV, Birtwell executed a strategic plan to organically feature the car with opinion leaders and celebrities, as well as build buzz utilizing print media and events. The response was overwhelming. The 300 quickly became a salesleader not just in the urban market, but in every demographic. The reason, Birtwell says, is because youth and urban culture drives broader cultural trends. “Most trends start and end with young people. The influence they have on mainstream culture is powerful,”

"We helped the company see the positive impact that youth and urban marketing could make on the bottom line. Upper management saw a trickle-down effect on main-stream buyer behavior which translated to sales.”

After four successful years helping Chrysler connect with new consumers, Birtwell accepted a vice president of marketing position at DUB Publishing, one of the leading marketing and promotions companies in the U.S.

Now in his new post as President at The Armory Marketing Group, Birtwell is continuing his efforts in helping Brands connect with the elusive Gen Y consumer.
