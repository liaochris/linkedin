  • eMarketing Intern

    Charles Schwab

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; SCHW; Financial Services industry)

    June 2007 — August 2007 (3 months)

    At Charles Schwab, I worked on a variety of marketing projects for Schwab.com. Using data collected from Web Analytics, I rewrote and redesigned the error page for Schwab.com to make it more user-friendly and to help redirect people to the ten most popular pages on the site.

    Several of my projects aimed at collecting user experience data. I collaborated with a survey vendor, Opinion Lab, to design a web visitor survey for Schwab's public website, enabling the collection of customer feedback about the site's usability. Working with Susan Reale at REALeResearch, I also organized and supervised a usability study for Schwab.com. The live person feedback gave the eMarketing team more insight about what could be improved. I also had the experience of targeting existing Schwab customers by proposing Gen-X targeted messaging banners for Schwab’s client website. I worked closely with Creative Services to invent new ways to attract a specific customer segment to Schwab’s financial products.

  • Administrative Support Intern

    Aerojet

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; GY; Defense & Space industry)

    June 2006 — August 2006 (3 months)

    At Aerojet, I worked with engineers and knowledge resources personnel to achieve a vast 'dispositioning' of engineering file cabinets, boxes, and hardware to clear a valuable space in the office. The file cabinets contained a mixture of valuable or historical documents and outdated, useless materials. I organized the engineers and assisted them with the sorting of all the materials in the space, and I then oversaw that the materials (sometimes classified) were either subsequently retained for the Engineering Knowledge Resources Library or properly disposed of.

    I also helped coordinate and schedule employee volunteers for the 2006 Joint Propulsion Conference in Sacramento, CA, of which Aerojet was a sponsor. I attended the conference for three days to work the Logistics Room and to ensure that all areas of the Sacramento Convention Center were manned with helpful Aerojet employees ready to assist any guests of the conference in finding their appropriate lectures.
