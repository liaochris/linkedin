Clive Zickel’s Summary

Clive is a strong, hands on entrepreneurial and results oriented finance and operations wallah with US and European experience in Financial Services, Legal & Professional Services, Medical Devices, Specialty Foods and Open Ocean Aquaculture. During his career, he has built numerous cutting-edge financial management, executive reporting, business intelligence, customer profitability and key performance measurement systems; started and managed new businesses; and turned around ailing business segments.

He specializes in advising early stage companies that have unique and scalable solutions to environmental and social problems and focuses only on companies that can make a difference while at the same time generating superior returns. In other words, doing well by doing good.

Clive began his professional career in public accounting, training with Hill Vellacott, the oldest firm of chartered accountants in London. After being admitted to the Institute of Chartered Accountants, he was engaged in audit and consulting engagements with BDO Stoy Hayward in London and KPMG Peat Marwick in Italy. He has held senior financial executive positions at JP Morgan Chase and Dechert LLP, has founded, grown and sold a business, and is actively involved as an advisor to early stage business ventures. Clive is also an adjunct professor at Stony Brook University Graduate School of Business.

He is a fellow of the Institute of Chartered Accountants in England & Wales, a graduate of the Harvard Business School Program for Management Development and is a graduate of the Executive MBA Program at Stony Brook University Graduate School of Business.

Clive does not count beans nor does he wear green eye shades.

Clive Zickel’s Specialties:

Early stage business planning and funding, financial and general management advice, business modeling, financial management systems, performance measurement,business intelligence, balanced scorecard, business process improvement.

    Chantrey Vellacott DFK

    (Partnership; Accounting industry)

    1972 — 1977 (5 years)

    Hill Vellacott

    It is believed that Hill Vellacott was the only accountancy firm that could trace its ancestry back 200 years. Based on this history, Chantrey Vellacott DFK is regarded by the Institute of Chartered Accountants in England and Wales as one of the five oldest firms in the country.

    In 1788 Edward Thomas Jones set up in business and his grandson later went into partnership with Arthur J Hill and William Edward Vellacott. Hill was taken into the partnership in 1867 and Vellacott in 1878. It was not until 1967 that the firm became known as Hill Vellacott.

    Arthur Hill’s son, Lancelot, became very involved with the Council of the Institute of Chartered Accountants and served as president twice. During the 1930’s Sir Henry Peat who later went on to Peat Marwick and David McClintock of KMG Thomson McClintock, both served their articles with Hill Vellacott.

    By 1967 Hill Vellacott had offices in London, Birmingham, Canterbury, Croydon and a presence in Northampton and Leicester. At this time their London offices were at Hanging Sword House, Whitefriars.

    Hill Vellacott merged with Chantrey Wood King in 1988 and both firms moved into Russell Square House as the firm of Chantrey Vellacott.
