Rhyen Coombs’s Summary

I'm a freelance multimedia reporter and web producer in the San Francisco Bay Area, where I received my master's degree in journalism from UC Berkeley in 2009. Most recently, I completed a News21 fellowship with the Carnegie-Knight Initiative on the Future of Journalism Education, where I led a team of five to develop BARThood, an experimental look at community and mass transit. I've produced multimedia for the Center for Investigative Reporting and The Chauncey Bailey Project, and taught social media workshops for the Knight Digital Media Center. My master's thesis “Foreclosed,” documenting items left behind in California foreclosures, received the 2009 Dorothea Lange Fellowship for photography. Prior to journalism school, I was an online editor at World Pulse, where I helped develop a social networking site for the magazine's international female readers. My work has also appeared in several Bay Area publications, as well as AlterNet, China Digital Times and NBC. If you need help with a story or website, or would like to collaborate, please get in touch.
