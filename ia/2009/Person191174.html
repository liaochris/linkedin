Pierre-Yves Bolus’s Summary

A graduate in public and international affairs, Pierre-Yves Bolus also has an education in fiscal sciences and town planning. Born in 1969, he started his city centre apprenticeship 29 years later, at La Louvière. There he put in place one of the first city centre management cells. He then participated, as a European expert, in the Asia Urbs project, where he took part in several missions to rejuvenate the economy of the Old Quarter in Hanoi. Consequently, he became director of the Shopping Area Contracts project in Brussels.

Pierre-Yves Bolus’s Experience

    (Public Policy industry)

    October 2000 — Present (9 years 2 months)

    Pioneer of city centre management in Belgium, Pierre-Yves Bolus has been director of the Brussels project, ‘Contrats de quartiers commerçants’ (Shopping Area Contracts), since 2000. In 2005, he became executive director of the new agency Atrium, created by the Brussels-Capital Region to train the management in its shopping areas. Today, Atrium manages 18 retail districts and covers forty areas, principally in the hypercentre and inner suburbs of Brussels.
