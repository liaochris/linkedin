Erik de Bruijn’s Summary

MSc student in Information Management at Tilburg University.

My passion and professional experience:
Combining a wide range of technologies in order to create whole new business opportunities and unmatched product quality and delivery. Experience in server infrastructure and virtualization, managing unix/linux and other open source system architectures. I have a special interest in newly arising technologies that will have a major impact on future business ecosystems and society, such as 3D printing (currently a Rapid Prototyping technology). In my spare time, of which there is only so little, I like to hack hardware, and create my own machines, software, etc.

With my companies I set up server clusters and a virtualization architecture by mastering Citrix' Xen. I did some rackspace management, a lot of ISP/network activities and co-developed several hardware appliances that are now in use in our datacenter locations.

Erik de Bruijn’s Specialties:

server architecture and virtualization, Linux, hosting services, web-development, AJAX, 3D printing, 3D, Fused Deposition Modeling, distributed R&D and fabrication, open source, open business strategies, replicating technology.

Erik de Bruijn’s Experience

  • The RepRap Project

    (Electrical/Electronic Manufacturing industry)

    February 2008 — Present (1 year 3 months)

    The RepRap project aims to create a 3D printer that can create objects of arbitrary shapes through a layered fabrication process. This particular printer is capable of replicating most of its own parts and is developed worldwide through the open source process. The replication and open source aspects serve as enablers for a unique form of distributed innovation that was previously only seen in software and (virtual) open content. RepRap has the potential to give the power to manifest ideas into tangible objects to anyone with a creative mind. Convergence of the virtual and physical world, that's the revolutionary change that RepRap is part of.
    My activities are: giving lectures, presentations and hands-on workshops around personal fabrication. Furthermore I develop and optimizing software for the RepRap (mostly firmware and driving software, some electronics as well).

Developing their stationery and website. Under this position I've had the opportunity to attend several high profile conferences on EPD (electronic patient record) and health care and IT in general.
