Beatrice Benne, Ph.D.’s Education

  • University of California, Berkeley

    Ph.D. , Architecture, Social Systems, Living Systems , 1998 — 2005

    Ph.D Dissertation: "Managing AEC Project Organizations at the Edge of Chaos: An Analysis of AEC Projects' Adaptive Capacity from a Living Systems Perspective."

    Through the Ph.D. journey I have investigated interdisciplinary fields such as social science; organizational theories; behavioral theory; economics theory; cognitive science; communication science; complexity theory, living systems theory; and evolutionary biology. Certainly, my research does not make me an expert in any one of these different disciplines, but I can apply these findings and knowledge to the solution of complex business problems.

  • College of Environmental Design, Berkeley, CA, USA

    Master of Science , Architecture, Design Theories and Methods , August 1996 — May 1998

    The MS program focused on the study of Collaborative Design in the context of multi-disciplinary, geographical dispersed project teams in the AEC industry. Specifically, I analyzed how professionals (e.g., architects, engineers, clients, contractors, and others AEC consultants) involved in the design of a facility work with one another, exchange information, coordinate their work, collaborate, and negotiate to resolve inter-disciplinary conflicts. I also investigated the impact of information technology on the transformation of designers’ work processes and analyzed the requirements for developing conceptual web-based environments to support distributed work.
    For my MS thesis I developed a Web-based database of architectural components that could be used in the context of a 3D Computer-Aided design model to assess a building’s energy efficiency.

    Architect Diploma , Architecture , September 1989 — July 1995

    Completed six-year professional program in Architecture and Urban Planning from the University of Geneva, Switzerland.
