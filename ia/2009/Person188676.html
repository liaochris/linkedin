+Aziz Ali, MS’s Summary

As an insights driver, I am a balance specialist, finding a desirable point between two seemingly opposing forces. I learned to do this from an early age, wherein balancing Eastern and Western values was essential in my adapting to my constantly changing environment.

Balancing thinking and doing, safety and risk, theory and practice, quantitative and qualitative research methods, traditional and emerging media, art and science, the list goes on.

I enjoy balancing opinions, filtering the abundance of information, and demonstrating to my supervisors that I clearly know where our business is headed.

This explains a lot of my professional development:

• 2006: I did post-graduate work in advertising because I wanted to integrate my two undergraduate majors, philosophy and advertising, which seemed to be at odds at first.

• 2007: Then I interned in London because I wanted to get a taste of how advertising is done in a fast-paced, cut-throat market, as opposed to a laid back college town.

• 2008: I would later move to Kuwait because in emerging markets, agencies are smaller; thus as a “planner” I was expected to manage client relationships, author briefs, guide agency wide workshops, and be at the forefront of new business pitches.

Having obtained that holistic perspective, I moved to New York to find a golden opportunity. Give me the opportunity and you will find that I grow and learn faster than the average candidate, thanks to the way I have balanced my ambitions in life and work.
