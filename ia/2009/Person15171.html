Andy Glew’s Education

  • University of Wisconsin-Madison

    Computer Science 1996 — 2000

    Pursued a Ph.D. in CS, Computer Architecture, under Prof. Guri Sohi, as an Intel Scholar, paid by Intel. Returned to Intel (a) when my daughter was born, (b) when promised a neat new project, which never materialized.

    Never got my Ph.D., although I did make several significant advances in computer architecture, including:

    (1) hardware performing compiler like optimizations on the fly, such as flattening / dynamic path length reduction / hardware parallel prefix, and dynamic common sb-expression elimination.

    (2) developed the MLP, Memory Level Parallelism, concept, including hardware skip pointers for linked lists using space freed up in memory lines by compression.

    (3) refined my ideas about speculative multithreading microarchitectures (IMT/DMT/SpMT/SkMT), including multicluster multithreaded microarchitecture, multilevel schedulers, trace log execution.

    (4) multilevel branch predictors

    Activities and Societies:
    Hoofers Outing Club

  • University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign

    MSEE , Computer Architecture , 1987 — 1990

    Invented HaRRM, the Hardware Register Renaming Mechanism, which became the P6 RAT (and which is now the renaming technique used by most modern microprocessors - it was supposed to be one of the "big innovations" in the Alpha EV8/21464). {I wish that I had patented it.}

    Refined ideas for out-of-order CPU microarchitecture that evolved into the Intel P6.

    MS thesis was on "A Feature Taxonomy of Synchronization Primitives, including the Bus Abandonment Lock". I.e. multiprocessor synchronization, locking, and cache protocols.

    Activities and Societies:
    Worked full-time at Gould and Motorola while taking classes; part-time while writing MS thesis.

  • McGill University

    BEng , Electrical Engineeering , September 1980 — June 1985

    SEISM, Small Efficient Instruction Set Machine
