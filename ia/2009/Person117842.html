John Patterson’s Summary

In the late 80s, John birthed and led a citywide leadership council that declared Austin, TX “The Arts Capitol of the South”. From 1995-2007, he worked for a global leader in the field of training and development managing the Midwest office. Having facilitated leadership and community development programs for over 10,000 people, he joined Pixel Brothers (television production) in 2007 to break new ground for the media industries.

With 23 years of training in a variety of leadership and performance disciplines, his focus is on existing and new distinctions which help people exceed their primary personal, performance and financial ambitions.

He is founder and president of The Chicago Convergence, a high profile think-tank for initiatives that boast the cities prominence in digital technology, entertainment and design. Welcomed by Mayor Daley, the annual conference and cohesive large-scale network of entrepreneurs and talent plays an integral role as the cities "new media glue".

In May of 2009, He made Los Angeles his primary residence with homes in both cities. He is as an entrepreneur involved in a variety of media related businesses.
