Riikka Salonen’s Summary

Riikka Salonen, a native of Finland, is the manager of cultural competency, inclusion, and service excellence for the Oregon Health & Science University (OHSU) hospital. She is currently leading the diversity and inclusion strategy and intercultural training initiatives for the Food and Nutrition Services Department, teaching cultural competency in the OHSU dietetic internship program, and is an intercultural specialist on the hospital’s room service project management team.

Ms. Salonen is an engaging trainer and educator with 14 years of experience in intercultural competence development in corporate, governmental, academic, and non-profit contexts globally. Before her current position at OHSU, Ms. Salonen served as a professor of intercultural communication and as an intercultural specialist on the transnational ocean-going university, "The Scholar Ship." The eight-month long global assignment took her to 17 different countries and involved intercultural teaching, training, coaching, and program development in a community of over 50 nationalities.

Prior to this, Ms. Salonen worked for over seven years at the Intercultural Communication Institute in Portland, Oregon, specializing in assessment and program development in the area of intercultural competence and inclusion. She managed 1,200 clients who used the Intercultural Development Inventory (IDI) in over 35 countries.

Ms. Salonen received her master's and bachelor's degrees from the University of Jyväskylä, Finland, focusing her research in intercultural customer service. She also has completed the Training of Facilitators of Personal Leadership and the Professional Intercultural Certificate from the Intercultural Communication Institute.

Riikka Salonen’s Specialties:

• Training and development of intercultural programs
• Intercultural competence in dietetics and hospitality
• Intercultural assessments, focusing on the Intercultural Development Inventory and Intercultural Conflict Style Inventory
• Intercultural customer service training and development

    According to Ramsey, Schaetti, and Watanabe (2007) "Personal Leadership is a framework, or a methodology, consisting of two principles and six practices. Its purpose is to help us access higher levels of learning and insight, mutual collaboration, and creativity in situations of cultural difference. In short, Personal Leadership is designed to help us do better, and even ‘our best,’ when we are interacting with people, places, or projects that are new and unfamiliar to us. The methodology includes a powerful process technology called ‘The Critical Moment Dialogues (CMD)’. The CMD further helps us translate our commitment for effectiveness across cultural difference into strategic action." To learn more, visit http://www.plseminars.com/.
