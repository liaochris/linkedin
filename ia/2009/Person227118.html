Susanna Speier’s Summary

Susanna Speier is a scriptwriter whose work has shown at The Tenri, The World Financial Center, HERE Arts Center, The Cocteau Theater, Collective Unconscious, 45 Bleecker Theater in New York City and at AIA in Burbank, CA. Internationally, her work has been performed at The Green Room and Dartington College of Arts in the UK and Boom Chicago in The Netherlands.

Her work has been covered by The New York Times, which stated, “Ms. Speier’s ear here is excellent,” the Villager, Newsday, offoffoff.com, The Drama Review, Le Petit Zine, Digital Culture Magazine and as a primetime story on the Dutch Public Television Station, VPRO. She has lectured at The Rietveld Academie in The Netherlands, Parsons in New York City and was a panelist for Sherwood Oaks Experimental College.

She has received grants from The PEN American Center Writers Fund and the Andrew W. Mellon Foundation Arts Recovery Fund, the Dramatists Guild and done residencies with Artward Bound at Wild Meadows, Dorset Writer’s Colony in Vermont and The Writer’s Room in NYC.

She earned her BA at Hampshire College in playwriting under the mentorship of Constance Congdon and was a guest student at the Object Theatreschool in The Netherlands. She obtained her MFA degree from Mac Wellman's playwriting program at Brooklyn College, C.U.N.Y. and was a guest student in Boston University’s MFA Screenwriting program. After graduating, she relocated to Los Angeles and worked as a Writing Department Intern on CSI and CSI: NY.

She is currently scripting her first full-length feature in collaboration with Barney Cohen of Pterodactyl Productions and being mentored by Rod Lurie through the Women in Film Mentorship Program.

Susanna Speier’s Specialties:

Entertainment: Scripts for stage, screen and television. Coverage, long form and short form episode synopses.

Publishing: Websites, blogs, magazines, trade journals, direct mail, catalog and ad copy. Using in-house style guides, AP and Chicago Manual of Style to proofread and copy edit textbooks, novels, catalogs, newspaper ads, indexes, websites table of contents and magazine articles.
