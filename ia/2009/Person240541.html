Miguel Angel Uriondo’s Summary

I have been working at the Spanish media industry for a while, and I hope to be around for a long, long, time.

I started my career ten years ago at Europa Press, where I had the opportunity of learning a load of useful things. I got a PhD in swallowing night shifts, a degree in political manhunt and a MBA in translating foreign reports and pursuing European commissioners. Best school in Spanish journalism.

Summer of 2005. After only three months writing on the telecom industry, I received a call from La Gaceta de los Negocios, the third/second spanish financial newspaper by then. I said yes, of course, and I was very lucky.

After three years, and because of some difficult times for the masthead, I accepted an offer by Actualidad Economica, a weekly magazine with a long history in Spanish journalism.

I love all the telecom and technology industries, but I have a I special interest on videogames, national telecom regulation and all the convergence phenomena.

Talking about personal issues, I am quite geeky. I love reading, basket, movies, american TV series, Sci-fi and, over all, comic books. I like drawing, too, and I use almost all my scarce spare time trying to prepare a semi-proffesional portfolio.

I am married with a brilliant writer and proffesional engineer, and we both love playing with out little dog, Buffy. And yes, she is named after the Vampire Slayer.

I told you, I am quite a geek.
