Kalpesh Upadhye’s Summary

Looking for a position that requires multidisciplinary skills including atomic force microscopy, MEMS design and fabrication, electrophysiology and molecular biology.

Currently pursuing PhD in Bioengineering from University of Pittsburgh. The thesis project involves development of instrumentation for multiparametric studies of mechano-sensitive ion channels using electrophysiology in concurrence with atomic force microscopy.

  • PhD. Researcher

    University of Pittsburgh

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    August 2004 — Present (5 years 5 months)

    Development of Instrumentation for Multiparametric Study of Mechano-sensitive Channels:
    • Designed a silicon (dioxide) based chip to replace a conventional micro-pipette based whole cell patch setup by a planar geometry
    • Fabricated a planar patch clamp chip using silicon microfabrication techniques
    • Designed a microfludic perfusion chamber for integrating electrophysiology using the planar patch clamp chip to integrate it with an inverted optical microscope and an atomic microscope (AFM)
    • Performed proof-of-concept planar whole cell patch clamp experiments with PC12 and Chinese Hamster Ovarian (CHO) cells
    • Ongoing studies on mechanism of activation in mechanosensitive channels using HaCaT and HEK293 cells

  • Senior Research Engineer

    Indian Institute of Technology, Bombay

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    September 2002 — July 2004 (1 year 11 months)

    Technology development for fabrication of piezoresistive SU8 affinity cantilever for detecting cardiac markers:
    • Setup a reduction camera based facility for mask-making
    • Optimized electron beam direct writing process for SU8
    • Developed the fabrication process for piezoresistive SU8 microcantilevers with a capability of antibody immobilization on top; the process is being be used by the group for all its related work
    • Demonstrated a novel technique for mechanical characterization of microcantilevers using an AFM which was accepted as the method of choice by the department for future work

  • Summer Intern

    Tata Institute of Fundamental Research, Mumbai, India

    (Research industry)

    April 2001 — August 2001 (5 months)

    Investigation of spin-injection in high temperature superconductor (YBCO):
    • Deposited thin film bi-layers of YBCO and LCMO (a CMR material) using PLD technique and characterized the thin films using X-ray diffraction and AFM
    • Designed, fabricated and I-V characterized (at 77K) spin-injection devices
    • Published a paper titled: “Direct injection of spin-polarized carriers across YBa2Cu3O7-δ - La0.3Ca0.7MnO3 interface at 77 K.” Pramana – J. Phys. [58, 1199 (2002)]
    • Awarded the Best Poster Award at the International Symposium on Advances in Superconductivity and Magnetism: Materials, Mechanisms and Devices (ASMM2D-2001)

  • Summer Research Scholar

    • Analytically calculated (for a simplified structure) and detailed analysis of designs (using SUPERFISH and PARMELA) for the RF fields and basic particle dynamics
