Louise Tonkinson’s Summary

highly experienced communications professional, with a 12 year career in senior public sector roles. Recognised as one of the ‘Top 40 to watch under 40’ by the Municipal Journal, my campaigns, programmes and initiatives have not only received accolades such as CIPR Gold and Silver awards, but have also significantly contributed to the positive transformation and growth of the organisations she has worked for.

Louise Tonkinson’s Specialties:

strategic communications planning, cultural change, internal communications, employee engagement, press and media relations, brand and reputation management, partnership communications, local government and public sector

Louise Tonkinson’s Experience

  • Head of Corporate Communications

    Calderdale MBC

    (Government Agency; Education Management industry)

    July 2007 — Present (2 years 4 months)

    Strategically led, influenced and advised the Cabinet, elected members, the Chief Executive and the senior management team; predicted the impact of their decisions on key stakeholder groups and then implemented planned programmes of activity to support them. Managed a significant budget and accountable for all communications professionals and competencies within the council. Lead responsibility for customer services and complaints. Responsible for making all major decisions related to communications policy, lobbying, strategy and corporate activity. Communications lead for all emergency planning and defence activity.

    Provided strategic communications advice on all programme and project activity relating to the council’s vision, ambition and eight priorities; this included being Chair of the LSP (Local Strategic Partnership) Communications Board, the development of place brand for Calderdale, a major internal cultural change initiative, a senior management review, Access to Services Audit Inspection and improvement strategy, the Halifax First Programme, the future of waste and Building Schools for the Future.

    Career highlights
    • Significantly raised the profile of communications with elected members and senior management.
    • Realigned communications priorities to meet the needs and aspirations of the business.
    • Established effective partnership working for communications in Calderdale (PCT, Pennine Housing, West Yorkshire Police, Business Link, Calderdale College and more).
    • Led the development of ‘Grasp the Nettle’ internal cultural change programme.
    • Developed a framework for and advised on all strategic work programmes identified in the council’s Vision to Reality.

  • Head of Corporate Communications

    Leeds City Council

    (Government Agency; Government Administration industry)

    June 1999 — July 2007 (8 years 2 months)

    Led all Corporate Communications policy, strategy and activity for the second largest metropolitan local authority in England. Accountable for an annual budget of £500,000 and two teams (Press and Media, Communications, Marketing and Consultation) comprising 17 staff. Responsible for advising and influencing the senior management team and 99 elected members, leading internal communications strategy for 35,000 employees and the communications for more than 500 different local authority services to over 750,000 residents.

    Career highlights
    • Redesigned and restructured the communications function to better meet the authority’s needs.
    • Established communications in the Council and Corporate Plan that for the first time included key performance indicators for future development.
    • Developed the council’s first three-year strategic communications strategy and annual service improvement plans for media, marketing and consultation.
