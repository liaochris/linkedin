M. Omar Faruque Sarker’s Summary

I have become a member of Robotics Intelligence Lab, Univ. of Wales, Newport (UK) since Nov 2007. For me it's a thrilling experience to shift my research paradigm from intelligent robots to swarm robots that run by a few kilobytes of 'emergent' code.

From late 1999, playing with Linux was my hobby and passion. I instrumented Linux on network servers, small robots and my everyday PCs. I wrote on dailies and monthly journals on Linux. A publisher was happy to publish my Book on Linux in Bengali. I ran a Linux powered small ISP. That time was full of fun working with local Linux users group to advocate for Linux.

Since my graduation, I have been working as a programmer of robots, devices and web pages; using low level C to object-oriented C++ or Java, basic HTML to shell scripts, from simple procedural to imperative codes in PROLOG.

I started my career in a ISP as Linux systems engineer and shortly after that I got a position in a project in my university, Bangladesh Univ. of Engineering and Tech. (BUET). I also conducted Linux and networking courses for students. That was a great time to learn and love, earn and enjoy through Linux!

Before coming to the UK, I was enrolled as a Masters student for two years and worked as a visiting research scientist at Korea Institute of Science and Technology. There, I researched a lot on real-time control using Linux/Xenomai, RT-Firewire etc. When I defended my Masters thesis on intelligent robots, my hands were fully dirty on coding for Player/Stage robot simulator and Java-Player API. Some of my technical papers had been published in referred journals like Lecture Notes in Computer Science. It was nice to travel to Beijing to present a paper in IROS 2006 conference about using Xenomai/Linux for robots.

Now what? Swarms of mini e-puck robots are moving around me and I'm again thrilling to put some ‘emergent’ codes on them so that they don't ask me to help them in moving every little step!
