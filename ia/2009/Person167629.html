Moneesh Arora’s Summary

Executive with comprehensive experience combined with a passion for building and growing information and professional services businesses. Results-oriented general manager with proven P&L management skills and distinctive leadership capabilities.

Moneesh Arora’s Specialties:

Moneesh Arora’s Experience

  • Vice President & General Manager

    Thomson Reuters Corp.

    (Public Company; TRI; Information Services industry)

    May 2006 — Present (3 years 4 months)

    Moneesh Arora is Vice President and General Manager of Thomson Reuters' Legal Education and Professional Development business unit.

    In his role, Moneesh is responsible for setting the strategy and overseeing day-to-day operations for a portfolio of on-line, print, interactive and mobile media businesses that provide information, software and services to the legal vertical. The portfolio of assets include West, Foundation Press, West LegalEdCenter, Attorney Jobs, West Legalworks and Reqwired.

    In addition, Moneesh is responsible for driving key Thomson Reuters corporate wide products and growth initiatives for mobile and electronic media.

  • Vice President - General Manager

    D&B (Dun & Bradstreet)

    (Public Company; 5001-10,000 employees; DNB; Information Services industry)

    June 2002 — March 2005 (2 years 10 months)

    Moneesh led the global Supply Management Solutions (SMS) business unit with full ownership of $30M U.S. P&L and $10M European P&L. Directed product management, marketing, sales and professional services organizations; consisting of 80 U.S. staff and 20 European staff. Successfully led organization through business and cultural transformation.

  • Vice President - Marketplace & Services

    Pantellos Group, L.P.

    (Information Technology and Services industry)

    July 2000 — June 2002 (2 years)

    Created and launched B2B business model to serve the business needs of a 21 company North American Energy consortium. Served as company co-founder and company officer, responsible for design, development and delivery of product portfolio consisting of web-based applications, enterprise software and professional services for Fortune 500 and mid-tier energy companies. Oversaw business segment P&L, including all related and supporting initiatives (e.g. operations, finance, human resources, legal, product marketing, sales and service). Managed all solutions and activities, including development, integration and operation of marketplace platform, e-commerce website, enterprise resource planning systems (ERP), customer relationship management system (CRM), sales force automation (SFA), data warehouse (DW) and infrastructure. Built and led a high performing team of 40 business, technology and professional services staff, in addition to outsourced staff.

  • Principal Consultant

    PricewaterhouseCoopers L.L.P

    (Partnership; 10,001 or more employees; Information Services industry)

    August 1997 — July 2000 (3 years)

    Practice Leader within Management Consulting Services (MCS) practice with responsibility for leading business development, service delivery and overall practice management (including P&L). Played hands-on role in all areas of account management, lead generation, proposal development, service delivery and client relationship management for large-scale Business-to-Business (B2B), Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP), Supply Chain Management (SCM), systems integration and strategic consulting initiatives in the U.S and abroad. Played key role in support of sales and delivery of Contact Center, Customer Information Systems (CIS) and Human Resource (HR) platforms.

  • Director – Finance and Information Systems

    Ogden Energy Group, Inc

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; Oil & Energy industry)

    June 1993 — August 1997 (4 years 3 months)

    Managed and led major corporate business re-engineering projects and change management initiatives, in addition to having responsibility for integration and maintenance of corporate Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) systems and processes; including all application development, implementation, operations and support initiatives of the company’s global supply chain.

  • Industrial Engineer

    The Port Authority of NY & NJ

    (Government Agency; 10,001 or more employees; Airlines/Aviation industry)

    May 1992 — May 1993 (1 year 1 month)

    Responsibilities included program management of $700M capital improvement plan and budget for JFK, LaGuardia and Newark Airports, which encompassed over 200 concurrent capital projects and 3,000 contractors. Led the implementation of a department wide project planning and contract management system which provided automation of capital planning and project management for the 2,000 person organization.
