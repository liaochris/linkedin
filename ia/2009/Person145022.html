Luis Cabrera’s Summary

Luis brings exceptional competencies to any company, most notably a deep expertise in marketing and a wide-ranging vision of digital technologies coupled with a strong business sense.

Today, Luis heads up the marketing efforts of Leapfactor and leads the Latin3 offices in Mexico City. Latin3 provides Digital Marketing services in Latin America and the U.S. Hispanic markets and is one of the top U.S. Hispanic Advertising Agencies, according to the 2007 Annual Report by Advertising Age.

Previously, he worked at New Media Strategies (NMS) in Washington DC as the architect and leader of the Hispanic practice. NMS is one of the first online media firms of the Web 2.0 era, a pioneer in online intelligence, word of mouth marketing and digital PR; NMS was recently acquired by Meredith Corporation (NYSE: MDP).

Earlier Luis worked as Managing Director of Bravo Uno-a-Uno, the experiential business unit at The Bravo Group, WPP’s US Hispanic advertising agency (NYSE:WPP). At Bravo, he led the creative and strategic operations of his unit and worked closely with other non-Hispanic agencies to ensure brand consistency and maximize efficiencies at all time.

Luis’s background consists in a formal education in media and communications, and by a solid professional development that includes being a strategist for Neoris, a global consultancy where he designed programs to leverage digital platforms to deliver operational efficiencies and elevate marketing program results.

Having lead international award winning projects, his exposure to a wide range of industries and having worked in different Latin American countries, gives Luis a unique perspective to approach business challenges and great insights and multicultural management skills.

Luis Cabrera’s Specialties:

Digital Solutions, Interactive Marketing, US Hispanic Market, Hispanic Advertising, LATAM, Web Development, Communication, Knowledge Management, Product Management, Business Consulting, Fortune 500 experience, startup, new business development.
