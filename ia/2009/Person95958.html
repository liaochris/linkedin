Howard Lewis’s Summary

During my career I have worked in operational management in telecoms, in both start up and complex, multi-national organisations. An engineer by training, I’m a business builder able to develop enduring value propositions, build and excite teams and create sustained competitive advantage. I have generally focused in developing a bottom line, objectives based management program with a strong emphasis on the competitive performance of the product range and a passionate approach to serving the customer. I particularly enjoy achieving ‘more than we thought possible’ through close teamwork and strong leadership.

Having lived in Europe, Asia and the US I’ve developed a keen understanding of management in different cultures – from the factory floor in Shenzhen to board rooms in London.

Howard Lewis’s Specialties:

Product lifecycle management, business & product plan formulation, ODM suppliers, business financing, non-exec advisory, mediation, telecoms due diligence.

Howard Lewis’s Experience

  • CEO

    Pinquet Ltd

    (Telecommunications industry)

    October 2008 — Present (7 months)

    Pinquet is a telecoms consultancy working in mobile products lifecycle management - from definition, development and sourcing through to EoL. I have built a practical knowledge of how to define winning mobile devices across the entire product range. At Entry, sourcing and developing the cheapest GSM phones through to EDGE based devices. I am intimately familiar with all chipset providers, particularly IFX and TI, and have a strong software sourcing knowledge. I've worked with mid-range HSPA devices using proprietary and open operating systems with Java apps frameworks, also Microsoft and Symbian/S60. I'm working with companies on LiMo and Android.

    I advise companies on device strategies - how to create organisation structures, consistently winning products, complete lifecycle management - and work with these companies to execute these programs with their own development teams and ODM's.

    I'm open for more opportunities to work with growing, energetic companies in this space.

  • COO

    Sendo

    (Privately Held; 201-500 employees; Telecommunications industry)

    August 1999 — July 2005 (6 years)

    Until the sale of its development activities to Motorola, Sendo was the UK’s only indigenous mobile phone company, directly employing 320 people in 4 continents. Headquartered in Birmingham, England the company designed, developed, manufactured, sold and serviced a broad range of customised mobile phones under its own and Network Operators brands.

    As one of the founders I was instrumental in the development of the business from scratch to an annualised turnover of US$450m. Until I left, after Motorola acquired the business assets, I was a main board Director and the Chief Operating Officer, with operational responsibilities for the complete product range, technology roadmaps, 2.5G and 3G development, business development, supply chain and customer service.
