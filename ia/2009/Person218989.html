Skye Pillsbury’s Summary

Skye began her career at Niehaus Ryan Wong:
- helped build the agency’s first “Internet” and “People and Technology” divisions;
- directed the strategic public relations of over 10 clients, including Yahoo! (where Skye was the central client and media contact before, during and after the company’s high-profile IPO), Pixar, Rocketmail (now Yahoo! Mail), Mapquest, J.Crew Online, Compare.net (now part of Microsoft), Virtual Vineyards (now wine.com), etc.

After leaving Niehaus Ryan Wong, Skye joined LinkExchange, a Sequoia-funded Internet start-up, to be its director of marketing:
- helped grow the company’s customer base from 100,000 to one million;
- generated enough “buzz” to attract the attention of Microsoft, who acquired the company one year later.
At Microsoft, Skye managed a yearly budget of 20+ million and drove all strategic marketing activities for her department including public relations, advertising, promotions, brand creation and collateral development.

Eventually Skye came full circle and returned to the PR agency world:
- VP at Eastwick Communications
- directed the strategic PR efforts of IT giant Mercury Interactive and voice technology leader Nuance.
- VP at OutCast Communications
- helped Wyse Technologies, a leader in thin computing in the 1980’s, reposition itself and set the stage for an industry comeback.

During her career as a publicist, Skye has garnered press coverage in numerous leading publications and media outlets including the Wall Street Journal, BusinessWeek, CNN, New York Times, NPR, CNBC the San Jose Mercury News, Fortune, GQ and Rolling Stone Magazine. She has been on tour with clients on both coasts and around the country and has managed hundreds (if not thousands) of media interviews. Skye is as comfortable working with small, scrappy start-ups as she is with billion dollar corporations; she can be as passionate about consumer ventures as companies rooted in deep technology.
