Philip B. Storm’s Summary

SYNOPSIS OF BOOK:

Seven young men from vastly different backgrounds meet and form friendships during army basic training in the Vietnam era. JJ Sharkey, an orphan from New York City, and Mike Stott, an Ohio boxer, advance through training to each earn a Green Beret, becoming two of the youngest special operations soldiers in the US Army. Assigned to the elite Fifth Special Forces Group in 1964, SEVEN SOLDIERS tells the story of their remarkable bond, and how Mike and JJ became extremely wealthy while serving their country with honor and distinction.

From their rigorous stateside training and R & R’s in Bangkok, all the way through secret missions in the heroin production center of the world, the Golden Triangle in Southeast Asia, SEVEN SOLDIERS is the Vietnam tale that’s never been divulged. Until now….

Philip B. Storm’s Specialties:

I am a retired automotive executive who served in the Army and Air Force Reserve. I graduated from the University of Cincinnati in 1969 with a BA in marketing and worked at Ford, Federal Mogul Corp., and The Equion Corporation prior to consulting.

I wrote SEVEN SOLDIERS while being a full-time caregiver to my wife, who died in May, 2007.
SEVEN SOLDIERS is the first of three novels describing the adventurous life of First Sergeant John “JJ” Sharkey of the Army Special Forces.
