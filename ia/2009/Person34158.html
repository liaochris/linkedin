Bridget Kavanagh-Patrick’s Summary

The The Michigan Department of Agriculture Animal Industry Division conducts surveillance for and controls/eradicates infectious animal diseases in Michigan. A number of these diseases are zoonotic in nature, including Bovine tuberculosis (TB), BSE, Avian Influenza, Rabies, Lyme disease, West Nile Virus and Eastern Equine Encephalitis.

This position liaises between the executive office and the Animal Industry Division and communicates the risks associated with disease, how the eradication efforts unfold and what MDA is doing to assist livestock producers in recovery activities associated with disease outbreaks. The position requires interagency communications with the Michigan Departments of Community Health and Natural Resources and the U.S. Department of Agriculture Veterinary and Wildlife Services, as well as stakeholder communications where multiple organizations are involved in disease response, control and recovery efforts.
