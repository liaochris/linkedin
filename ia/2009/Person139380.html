Bill Lehane’s Summary

  • Interactive Journalist

    RTÉ

    (Public Company; Media Production industry)

    August 2003 — February 2009 (5 years 7 months)

    For five years I held a full-time position on the online newsdesk in RTE Ireland's national public broadcasting station. My primary task as an interactive journalist was to generate content for the online news services, which run 16 hours a day. This involved developing both hard news and lighter stories using material from the newsroom input file, broadcast output files and news wires.

    I also worked with the News Editor to source and publish special content and news highlights. This role involved boosting traffic on the news site by creatively promoting RTE output and securing web-exclusive content.

    In April-May 2007, I dedicated two months to research and content management for the phenomenally successful subsite RTE.ie/election on Ireland's last general election. This included an interactive guide I wrote on voter registration and a First Time Voter's Guide I compiled in association with Rock The Vote. I also performed a similar role on the microsite for the Northern Ireland Assembly Elections that year.

    I was also involved in monitoring the content of news and other services such as weather, travel and public information pages.

    In my time working on the service, RTE.ie became Ireland's second most popular website and the news site won the Golden Spider internet Media award three years in a row.

    I also wrote 48 music, film, theatre and book reviews for the arts subsite, RTE.ie/arts. In addition, I worked as a web reporter at the national Festival of World Cultures, completing two festival blogs in 2007 and 2006.

Bill Lehane’s Education

  • Dublin City University

    MA , Intercultural Studies , 2005 — 2007

    Completed MA in Intercultural Studies at DCU with second class honours, including a first class honours for a dissertation on interculturalism in Irish public service broadcasting, which drew together the views of senior RTÉ staff and prominent migrants. Over the course of the programme, I also conducted interview-based research on the Festival of World Cultures and wrote essays on media theory and Africa, depictions of childhood in European cinema and the riots against disadvantage in France in late 2005.

  • Dublin City University

    BA , Journalism , 1999 — 2003

    Graduated with 2:1 in BA in Journalism at DCU. My dissertation was a feature series on tobacco in Irish society coinciding with the announcement of the workplace smoking ban. Part of the project was published in In Dublin.
