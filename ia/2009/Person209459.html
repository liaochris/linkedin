Sara Davison’s Experience

  • Hardware/Trim Developer

    Spyder Active Sports

    (Privately Held; Apparel & Fashion industry)

    August 2007 — June 2009 (1 year 11 months)

    Trim developer, R&D, pricing and sourcing specialist. My main focus is non-fabric components such as zippers/buttons/snaps/heat transfers/labels/buckles etc. I also develop all packaging/hang tags as well as promotional items and any other non apparel related items. I work to track and maintain a list of over 600+ trim components within our system per season, as well as monitoring all of sales sample, and bulk booking and production. Addressing issues/problems, and finding solutions on a daily basis. Establishing and complying to all development/production time lines, ensuring proper delivery dates of materials. I work directly with our overseas vendors and have established strong working ties with dozens of international and domestic companies that offer Spyder the best product/service available on the market. In addition to above responsibilities, I am now in charge of trim development for Cloudveil Mountain Works, since Spyder acquired CMW in 2008.

  • Product Development Intern

    Spyder Active Sports

    (Privately Held; Apparel & Fashion industry)

    June 2005 — December 2005 (7 months)

    This was a great experience as I was able to work with colleagues from Product Development, Trims, Merchandising, Color and Fabrics. Given multiple assignments daily in different departments (with the focus on PD), I was able to bounce around and really get a well-rounded and full experience of working in a fast-paced, corporate setting and a good idea of what each team member and department role contributes to the product development process.

    Worked to assist the senior product developers to measure, fit and record all changes for Fall 2007 product protos. Working in Gallery Systems to help assist in bill of material (BOM) building for garments. Worked on several small lines, to send proto comments to our factories overseas and make modifications for sales sample garments.

    One of my on going tasks was to organize all proto garments for the Spyder Sample Sale. This is an annual money raising event for the company. I worked with PD to transport, and set up the sale at our locally based showroom in Louisville, CO. It was very successful.

    Overall, this was a great learning experience. I was thankful to have been given this opportunity at such a prestigious and well respected company within the sporting industry.
