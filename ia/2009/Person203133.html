Romulo Romero’s Summary

Discovery Research Experience. Separations specialist who consistently delivered on-time results while providing separations services to a medicinal chemistry department consisting of approximately 100 chemists.

• Performed chiral and non-chiral normal phase and reverse phase method development and preparative purifications of intermediates and final compounds meeting tight timelines.
• Implemented at column dilution (ACD) protocol to help purify poorly soluble samples.
• Collaborated with high speed synthesis (HSS) group in establishing an efficient workflow.
• Innovator whose teamwork has been recognized with four Science and Technology Awards.
• Provided equipment support and training on Unipoint and purifications to end-users.
• Effectively managed sample workflow and duties of separations lab.
• Responsible for a multiplatform laboratory: Gilson, Shimadzu, Agilent.
• Proven expertise in Gilson Unipoint software and Gilson Nebula series equipment.
• Excellent troubleshooter capable of some repairs.

Pharmaceutical Sciences Experience. Analytical development activities supporting projects in pre-clinical stages through early phase II.

• Consistently demonstrated expertise in sample analysis with a variety of instruments (HPLC, DSC, TGA, Dissolution, XRPD, VTi, GC).
• Acted as lead analytical chemist in number of dealings with CROs.
• Co-authored CMC submissions.
• Developed and collaborated in subsequent validation of an HPLC method capable of quantifying two genotoxic impurities at the 0.01 % level in API.
• Developed HPLC methods for in-process control and release of API in GMP manufacturing runs.
• Collaborated with CROs in method validation, Drug Substance and Drug Product release activities and stability testing

Solid background in synthetic organic chemistry

About 50 % of my work load was Chiral separations via HPLC. This position involved prioritization of the workload to serve the needs of several projects in a timely fashion. I also interacted closely with the high speed synthesis group to help them meet their challenges by tweaking HPLC methods and working with them to design hardware to make their process more efficient.
