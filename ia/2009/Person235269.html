Todd Parker, Sr.’s Summary

GOALS: To acquire a Corporate Security Manager position involving developing, coordinating, and staffing overall security, awareness, and the best-of-class teams of security professionals for a diverse and challenging organizational environment.

EXPERIENCE: Developed, coordinated, and staffed the United States Army Antiterrorism & Force Protection Training Requirements, Policies, and Procedures. Authored the Antiterrorism & Force Protection policies and procedures that established the standards from which training for all soldiers, Department of the Army Civilians, and family members undergo annually, as well as individuals within 6 months of traveling outside the United States, its territories, or possessions. Results: The policies and procedures were immediately instituted worldwide, and subsequently adopted by the Department of Defense, and remains unchanged today.

Researched and found security gaps and vulnerabilities, coordinated and received bids from several companies, and subsequently drafted proposals to the headquarters for funding. Coordinated with other CEOs to place emphasis on security. Results: Totally upgraded the building’s security – from obtaining barriers, a magnetic card key access system, and updating the surveillance monitoring system. This significantly improved overall safety, security, and morale of the entire building’s 300+ tenants.

Led meticulously thorough and comprehensive investigations on staff with allegations of improprieties, fraud, waste, and resource abuse. Interviewed and observed extensively in fact finding missions. Results: Found the facts and evidence that led to the successful prosecution of over a dozen cases within a 1 year time period. Trained leaders on telltale signs of problems or illicit activities, ultimately all leading to improved morale, a “clean” work environment, and reductions in improprieties, fraud, waste, and resource abuse cases by 84%.
