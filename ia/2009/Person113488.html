Jim Van Buskirk’s Summary

Pioneer in real-time digital signal processing device virtualization. Co-inventor of the host-CPU-based virtual instrument (music/audio).

Inventor/creator/publisher of the GigaStudio™, GigaSampler™, GigaViolin™, GigaPulse™ fast convolution engines, GigaPiano, and GigaPiano II commercial products.

Authored numerous Patents and articles for Texas Instruments, Rockwell Semiconductor (Conexant), and TEAC Corporation of America.

Specialist in low-level, real-time, pipelined code implementation for efficient CPU or DSP implementation.

Educator/driver of the Giga (.gig) file format standard to third party developers, authored training seminars, manuals and technical documentation, key contributor to the acceptance and proliferation of the GIGA (.gig) file format.

Former co-founder and CEO of an Austin-based audio technology corporation, from start-up funding through to successful acquisition. Negotiated funding and business dynamics with clients, vendors, and corporate counsel, corporate spin-off, exit strategy, due diligence, Patent and Trademark registration, international technology product distribution, litigation settlement in Federal Court.

Expertise in product development, branding, trade show and marketing experience.

Ability to communicate effectively with and coordinate projects between highly diverse participants (business, technical, and creative) entities.
