Alanda Kariza’s Summary

Alanda Kariza was born in Jakarta, February 23, 1991, and still studying at SMA Negeri 82 Jakarta as a senior. Her passion in writing leaded her to publish her debut book, Mint Chocolate Chips, when she was 14. She established a non-profit organization, The Cure For Tomorrow, which is working on empowering youth through certain issues, especially environment and education in 2006. She became the youngest finalist of CosmoGIRL! of The Year 2006, dismissed other 700 applicants.

In 2009, Alanda has been selected as Indonesia's single delegate in Global Changemakers events, provided by British Council. She attended Global Changemakers Guildford Forum 2009 in January and Global Changemakers at the London Summit 2009 in March-April 2009 where she got the chance to address young people's aspirations to some of the world leaders, including PM Gordon Brown, President Felipe Calderon Hinojosa and President Susilo Bambang Yudhoyono.

Alanda is a freelance writer for several magazines, including Hai, Gogirl!, CosmoGIRL!, Provoke! Bukune, 21-Degrees and kaWanku. She also works as the media relations staff of JakartaConcerts.com. Related to her passion in films, she takes part in the making of the movie, Queen Bee, under the mentoring of Bernhard Subiakto (Octovate Group) and Salman Aristo (scriptwriter), which hopefully is going to be released on June 2009.

Alanda is currently planning her upcoming project, a Community Action Project for the Global Changemakers network: Indonesian Youth Conference 2010.
