Peter Gustafsson’s Experience

  • Founder, CEO

    Inserve Technology

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Information Technology and Services industry)

    February 1999 — Present (10 years 7 months)

    Inserve is a system integrator focused on identity management (IDM) and mission critical server and storage infrastructure. We're the best partner to really measure, and realize, the effects of identity management!

    We have the knowledge, delivery capacity, and the references to prove it. I'll be happy to share those success stories!

    Personally i drive sales and marketing besides being CEO. I'm also heavily involved in our major identity projects to develop methods and models to measure ROI and the (actual and measurable) business effects of IDM.

    With leading expertise, broad partner network and a strong commitment from idea to full production, Inserve has rapidly become one of Scandinavia's leading providers of robust and scalable identity and server/storage solutions.

  • Founder

    May 2000 — June 2006 (6 years 2 months)

  • Sales Executive

    Sun Microsystems

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; SUNW; Information Technology and Services industry)

    May 1994 — February 1999 (4 years 10 months)

    In Sun's Gothenburg office I was responsible for the "education and research"-market and account manager for Saab Automobile and other large customers. My responsibilities include high-level strategic selling, extensive account planning and long-term cooperation with partners and ISVs.

    In this position I increased revenue on all named account with 40% annually and made the Sunrise club (over 100% of revenue goal) among the top 5 sales representatives in Sweden four years in a row (averaging 145% of goal). I got top ratings from management ("Always Exceeds Requirements") and twice earned the "International Special Recognition Award" (1997 and 1998)

    I made Chalmers the most important education-account in the Nordic region. I also initiated one of Sun’s most important Collaborative Research Projects between Chalmers and Sun Engineering in California, USA. I also made Saab Automobile Sun’s third largest Manufacturing account in Sweden.

  • Author

    Konsultförlaget (Self-employed)

    (Self-Employed; 1-10 employees; Internet industry)

    May 1994 — December 1994 (8 months)

    During the summer/autumn 1994 I wrote a book together with Per Florén and Jörgen Jedbratt; "Internet - en vägvisare till morgondagens affärer" ("Internet - a way to tomorrows business").

    It was the first book in Swedish that explained Internet from a business point of view, a practical book aimed at executives, market development people and other non-technical people involved in business development.

    The book described Internet and how it could help a company to be more competitive and contribute to visions and goals. It also gave examples and ideas for possible uses of the Internet.

    The book sold over 4.000 copies in Sweden and Norway.

  • Sales Manager

    Gothenburg Universities Computer Center

    (Government Agency; 51-200 employees; Information Technology and Services industry)

    October 1989 — May 1994 (4 years 8 months)

    GD marketed IT services: mainframe and UNIX hosting, network-management, consulting- education and sales of PC, Mac and UNIX-workstations. GD had 85 employees and ~100 MSEK in revenues.

    I managed GD’s sales and product business with a staff of 20. We focused on networked computers and integration of Mac, PC & UNIX, to increased market share and profits. I also increased marketing resources and advertisement, increasing customer awareness of our key competencies.

    In the spring 1990, as a joint venture between Chalmers University of Technology and GD, I established a "on-campus-shop", and created an organization for marketing and sales of UNIX-workstations from major vendors: Sun, DEC, IBM and HP. With high technical competence in UNIX, networks and workstations, we made it easier for departments and faculty staff to buy technical workstations

Peter Gustafsson’s Education

  • Göteborgs universitet

    MBA , Management , 1998 — 2000

    2 year part time, Executive MBA, focusing on management and general perspectives.

    The curriculum was divided into ten areas: management, leadership, change management, organization, marketing, economic analysis and valuation, economic history, international business, business development and strategy. Each part included a real business case at companies like Volvo, Schenker and Asko.

    As a master thesis we developed a full business-plan for a mobile business-to-business company: This later became OnPosition.com.

  • Chalmers tekniska högskola

    M.Sc. , Computer Science and Engineering , 1985 — 1991

    Major subjects: mathematics, operating systems, data communication and information technology. Complementary courses in for example economics, business-law, leadership and information-security.

    The final exam was delayed due to full-time work and involvement in student activities: two years as editor and art-director for a student paper, one year as secretary in "Teknologföreningen C.S." and other engagements at the university.

    I completed my master thesis at Gothenburg Universities Computer Center (GD) and the department of Administrative Data Processing. It was named ”Personal Computers at Universities” and its purpose was to describe the use of personal computers at universities: experiences, benefits, problems - and what GD should do to better support the users. In the analysis I used the American PDS-method (Problem Detection Study), later used in several large customer-projects at GD.
