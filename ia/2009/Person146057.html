Maarten Baas’s Summary

Compassion and the ability to truly understand what drives someone, combined with my excellent technical skills and analytic look on life, make me excel in almost any task I embark on. I always keep a complete view of the whole situation, on all levels and thus I have a lot of experience and success with implementation of various complete system solutions. All in all, I am a professional with a broad experience in the world of Java and Open Source, with a specific focus on SOA and Web2.0.

I am always looking for that a new challenge that is unique, that you don't want to give to just anyone. I am best at that one challenge where quality really matters.

  • Implementation Architect

    ICTU

    (Information Technology and Services industry)

    January 2008 — January 2009 (1 year 1 month)

    As Implementation Architect at ICTU I have been responsible for the implementation of HP SOA Systinet as UDDI registry in the Overheids Service Bus.

    The real challange was to fit the Systinet product into the very specific requirements the dutch government made. The HP SOA Systinet product is a wonderfull product dveloped with great SOA philosophies such as openness, clearity and completeness, ment to break through the silos that hold so many companies hostage. Making the product fit the government silo structure and need for some level of secrecy was a challange, but a happy customer waited at the other end.

  • Serviceleader and Software Architect

    January 2007 — September 2007 (9 months)

    I was one hand responsible as a service leader for applications in the HR department responsible for leading a service team to meet not only their service levels, but most importantly also the non-written quality expectations of the client.

    On the other hand I had my feet in the mud being resposible for the architecture and implementation of all Java applications maintained by Atos Origin throughout the whole Albert heijn Service organisation.

    The challenge here was to balance the demands of my two roles and the expectations of the client and the service organization, all of which were in frequent conflict with each other. A lovely time!
