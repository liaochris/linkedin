Christopher Slay’s Summary

I would like to meet:
Employers with vacancies that could potentially be filled by skilled Polish labour

Employers who are currently employing Polish labour on long term contracts, but whose contracts are coming to an end (therefore releasing Polish workers back into the market)
·
Background information:

How do you find employers and workers? Networking , existing clients’ recommendations, email newsletters, website enquiries and chance!

Geography? We can cover any geography, with a few caveats…

How much cheaper is Polish Labour? Employers use the same criteria for choosing employees, as I use for choosing services for my business. Price isn’t everything! They appreciate hard working, generally conscientious, prepared to do any job and wanting to work 45 hours + a week, and are prepared to pay for those benefits.

Language? It is harder and more expensive to obtain fluent English speakers, but it is possible if essential for the role. However those workers who are less fluent English speakers often more than compensate for their language deficit by ‘going the extra mile’. We provide advice on team structuring and rewards.

How do we anticipate who needs your services? Anyone with potential or need to recruit for a vacancy is a potential client. Their recruitment challenges will be the generic ones affecting all recruiters, and where they are seeking a solution for

· Filling vacancies

· Retention and attrition

· Poor performance

Employers might well be willing to ring the changes, and fish in a new pool rather than try traditional paths.

All I need is the briefest of details but the perfect lead would comprise:

Person to contact

Company name

Email address

Website address

Mobile Number

Plus a one liner: .."Pete's a fellow cricketer, they've just got a new contract and they reckon they need an extra 5 people, give him a bell you can mention my name" or similar.
