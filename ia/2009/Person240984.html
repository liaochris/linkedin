Valentina Giannella’s Summary

A passion for digital media, and what’s around it. It started very soon, when I was a teenager, and led me to focus my business management studies on the digital convergency of media. Later on, after some years spent in the Information Systems development, I entered the main door of the media environment, thanks to Mediaset: the largest italian commercial Bradcaster.

This phase of my life offered to me the opportunity to work across a wide variety of digital media initiatives, all based on the interception between traditional linear TV and other media: from teletext to the web until mobile. And, what's more, I have evolved from a technological project management role to a content architect function then to become a business developer and a strategist.

Including my latest experience in De Agostini (Publishing), that's make almost nine years of practical experience in developing and managing large digital projects from concept to successful commercial products.

There's a lot more to build on top of this personal and professional path, especially now, when the ability to understand and master the social evolution of the digital environment is a 'must-do' both for media companies, retailers and whatever institutions need to reach, retain and ultimately talk to their customer base.

In this challenging scenario I can count upon a hybrid and quite large profile. What I lost in terms of deep specialisation, I gained in term of ability to cope with many management, technical, commercial and editorial issues as well awareness of the connections among those areas.

Goals: opportunities coherent with this profile and the inherent passion, and a strong willingness to work on a paneuropean perspective. Available to relocation, with preference to german-speaking countries.

Valentina Giannella’s Experience

  • Consultant

    Emporion Ges.m.b.H

    (Management Consulting industry)

    March 2009 — Present (6 months)

    Currently I am working on short term consultancy assignment, both in Vienna and in Milano, developing the online marketing and editorial strategy of players both in the financial and in the publishing industry (NDA covered).

  • Head of Digital Media Development - Responsabile Sviluppo Media Digitali

    De Agostini Editore

    (Privately Held; Publishing industry)

    January 2007 — January 2009 (2 years 1 month)

    Within the Corporate Business Development Unit, I was responsible for developing and implementing a digital media strategy encompassing TV, Web and mobile in order to reaffirm the De Agostini Editore position in the digital space and to create new revenue streams.

    Since there were very few existing traditional assets able to be digitally exploitated and repurposed, we came up to a radical diversification strategy, based mainly on brand equity valorisation and implying the creation of products and content from scratch.

    The result has been the set up of a self-standing business unit (Digital Dea), focused on two lines of business: new TV (satellite linear channels as well online video projects) and strategic partnership and acquisitions.

    Apart from the strategic business development role, I took direct charge of the following:

    (1) Thematic TV channels – Deakids satellite children channel (on air on Sky Italia channel 601).

    I defined the initial channel proposition: positioning, programming and commissioning strategy. I worked then to the business plan and to the staffing needs definition of the channel.

    (2) Online channels – Deagostini 'How To' Videoguide see http://www.deabyday.tv/

    I have lead the web video project from the product conception until the setup of operations. I have developed the Editorial Plan; I have defined and setup the video production, postproduction and video delivery architecture; staffed and organised the content factory.

  • Research & Development Manager

    Mediaset

    (Privately Held; Broadcast Media industry)

    April 2004 — December 2006 (2 years 9 months)

    I reported directly to the General Manager of RTI Interactive Media division, providing him with an insight into the diversification strategies of the main European Broadcasters and analysing the emerging media consumption patterns, as well relevant product and case histories in order to orient and extend our New Media portfolio.

    The most important outcome of this work has been the evolution of www.mediaset.it (the Broadcaster’s news and entertainment portal) from textualcentric to video centric and the development of the catch-up videosite: www.rivideo.mediaset.it.

    My job implied to maintain a strong awareness of the global digital landscape by speaking at and attending industry forums and seminars.

    In this function, I have also acted as internal consultant of other Mediaset Departments, namely the Information System Department (unified user database) and International Relations Office (helping to develop Position Papers for the discussion within EU Commission).

  • Project Manager New Media

    Mediaset

    (Privately Held; 1001-5000 employees; Broadcast Media industry)

    October 2000 — March 2004 (3 years 6 months)

    As Project Manager I was responsible for managing the execution of a wide variety of projects from product conception to delivery. That function included both the development of consumer products and internal IT projects (e.g. content management, reporting...).

    I have developed, inter alia, www.tgcom.it that is leading in the Italian news online and represents the first European case of multiplatform and multidelivery news feed, ranging from FTA television to radio, mobile, teletext and DTT.

    I have developed and launched several interactive applications on Teletext and in particulare a Mediavideo sms chat that in 2004 was the most popular text-to-screen application in the Italian Television.
