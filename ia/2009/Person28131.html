Dennis Bidwell’s Summary

I have spent over 30 years working in non-profit management, fundraising and real estate. In 2001 I combined this experience with a range of interests to form Bidwell Advisors, a consulting firm that provides a variety of real estate services to non-profits, private property owners and professional advisors.

Bidwell Advisors specializes in real estate philanthropy, concentrating on helping non-profits of all shapes and sizes to attract, structure, close and dispose of real estate gifts of all types. The firm also helps non-profits analyze and dispose of surplus real estate, and assists organizations in their search for property for building or lease. In addition, Bidwell Advisors works with private property owners, and their professional advisors, to craft creative and tax-efficient property disposition solutions, often addressing charitable and/or conservation objectives.

I speak frequently on matters pertaining to real estate gifts and land conservation. He contributes articles regularly to planned giving professional journals and other publications.

From 1990 to 2001 I served as National Director of Land Protection for American Farmland Trust. I was in charge of AFT's conservation real estate transactions, and created the Farm Legacy Program, which combined planned giving techniques with conservation real estate transactions.

I serve on the board of the Greater Northampton Chamber of Commerce, where I chair its economic development committee. My daughter and I are founders of Northampton High Crew/Northampton Community Rowing. I serve on the board of National Priorities Project, and am a member of Northampton's Zoning Revisions Committee.

I received my BA from Dartmouth College, my M. Ed. from Harvard University, and my M.B.A. from Boston University.

Dennis Bidwell’s Experience

  • Owner

    Bidwell Advisors

    (Real Estate industry)

    2001 — Present (8 years)

    I formed Bidwell Advisors in 2001 to provide a range of real estate services to non-profit organizations, private property owners, government agencies and other clients. The firm specializes in real estate gift planning, conservation-related real estate transactions, development consulting, and surplus property disposition consulting. Among the firm’s clients are Middlebury College, Northwestern University, Vassar College, Amherst College, Berkshire Taconic Community Foundation, Tufts University, The Boston Foundation, Trust for Public Land, Dana Farber Cancer Institute, Elderhostel.

  • National Director of Land Protection

    American Farmland Trust

    (Non-Profit; Public Policy industry)

    1990 — 2001 (11 years)

    I was responsible for AFT's conservation real estate program: conservation transactions and revolving fund investments (land purchases, conservation easement purchases and donations, limited development projects); Farm Legacy program, combining planned giving techniques (charitable remainder trusts, charitable gift annuities, etc.) with conservation real estate transactions; partnerships and technical assistance for private land trusts and public land protection programs; and stewardship (monitoring and enforcement) of AFT's 70 conservation easements.

    Speaker and author on wide range of land conservation issues; manager of the Land Protection Division's $1.0 million budget and staff of seven; member of AFT's senior management team during much of the organization's growth from a staff of 25 to a staff of 110 in 15 offices around the country.

  • Vice President

    Leggat McCall Advisors

    (Real Estate industry)

    1983 — 1990 (7 years)

    I conducted real estate appraisals, market and financial feasibility studies, disposition consulting and development consulting. Clients included non-profit organizations, government agencies, private developers and financial institutions. Leggat McCall Advisors was the successor firm to Minot, DeBlois & Maddison, where I served as Vice President and Treasurer. In addition to providing real estate consulting and appraisals for non-profit, private and public clients, I. handled financial management, administrative and marketing responsibilities for the company.

    (Non-Profit; Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    I developed new regional preservation programs. I was responsible for fundraising and communications with corporate, foundation and individual donors. I also handled real estate consulting, media relations and promotion for the National Trust in the Northeast.
