Cathy Brooks’s Summary

I guess you could say it was fate. With nicknames ranging from Chatty Cathy to Babbling Brooks, and my family tendencies, you could say that I'm a genetically engineered communicator. The business connector part came later. While I've spent some time in and around PR/Marketing firms, my core experience lies on the media side of the communications fence. In my current iteration, I am focused on producing content for various platforms - largely conferences and events, but also including podcasts and other interviews.

Most recently I was engaged by European entrepreneur, Loic Le Meur, to develop and produce the program for his fourth annual LeWeb conference in Paris.

Another current client is Guidewire Group, a market intelligence firm focused wholly on emerging markets, technologies and companies - anyone and everyone who's involved in the innovation ecosystem. My role is developing and producing two events for this organizations (Innovate!Europe and Leadership Forum), as well as an ongoing podcast - I of Innovation - an in-depth interview series that profiles the entrepreneurs who build companies and the people who invest in them.

Immediately prior to this role I had the perfect job for polishing my psychological analysis skills - heading Business Development in the Bay Area for one of the largest PR firms in the US. I've spent some other hands-on time in PR and marketing, but mostly my career is comprised by time in newsrooms in Philadelphia, Chicago, Miami and San Francisco. Primarily a broadcaster, I have a degree from the Medill School of Journalism at Northwestern University.

My technology-focused work lives on-line at http://www.guidewiregroup.com

Everything "other than that" lives on-line at http://www.otherthanthat.com.

I'm interested in talking with people of all ilk - especially if they have an interesting story to tell.

Cathy Brooks’s Specialties:

Writing, personality profiling, behavioral trend spotting in business, making people laugh, starting conversations, unraveling meaningless marketing blather, dog behavior & training (skills that I often wish transferred to the business world).

  • LeWeb3

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Internet industry)

    July 2007 — Present (1 year 8 months)

    I am working with Loic Le Meur to develop, produce and co-host one of Europe's most well-attended technology gatherings. My role is identifying themes and topics for the conference, selecting the speakers to address those topics, book the speakers and then help Loic emcee the event.   • Seesmic

    (Privately Held; Internet industry)

    December 2007 — December 2008 (1 year 1 month)

    Web 1.0 was about content. Web 2.0 was about people and community. The next iteration of the Web is about blending the two. That’s where Seesmic comes in. Seesmic’s rich platform enables deep connections among its users using video as the conduit. The highly interactive and engaging interface is a platform to power conversations on any subject among users who are spread all around the world.

  • Guidewire Group

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Information Technology and Services industry)

    February 2006 — December 2007 (1 year 11 months)

    Guidewire is a global market intelligence firm focused wholly on emerging markets, companies, technologies and products. Led by Chris Shipley - technology industry pundit, journalist and executive producer of the DEMO conference - Guidewire serves as an ally to entrepreneurs and guide to those who invest in them. We kicked off 2007 by launching the Guidewire Report - a bi-weekly subscription newsletter providing in-depth analysis of key trends we are seeing, and including profiles of companies with which we've met that fit into that trend. TGR includes a map outlining the taxonomy of the segment and shows where each company fits in relation to the market and to each other.

    I produced the content for Guidewire's conferences and also launched their first podcast series, I of Innovation. That show ended in July 2007 and evolved into a series currently run by Network World on the DEMO site called "Six Minutes With".

  • TechTV

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Broadcast Media industry)

    February 1998 — November 2001 (3 years 10 months)

    At TechTV I was responsible for any person who appeared in front of the camera - whether interviewer or interviewee.
