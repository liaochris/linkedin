Luke Brindle’s Summary

Originally trained in Fixed Income options trading on the LIFFE floor in London, Luke has spent his career trading Equity & Index Options in Frankfurt, London and Chicago

Graduating from Bristol University (England) with a BSc (Hons) degree in Mathematics, Luke moved to London in 1995 to work for Man Group, the world's largest listed hedge fund manager, where he helped to develop and run the 24-hour automated Technical Analysis trading system for Man’s flagship AHL Funds

In 1996 Luke was hired by the London office of Cooper Neff & Assoc. Luke worked for BNP Cooper Neff in London for a year before he was transferred to BNP Frankfurt to work in the bank’s European Equity Derivatives division, the largest liquidity provider of German & Swiss Equity Options.

Luke moved back to London in 2000 and took up an opportunity with Equitec Group. After two years trading German & UK listed Equity Options Luke moved to the USA to help with the US group’s migration to electronic option trading

At Equitec, in Chicago, Luke ran a US vs European derivatives trading book with strategies involving cross border individual equity & sector arbitrage, dispersion trading and ADR arbitrage. Luke was also responsible for market-making functions as DPM/Specialist in options on Sector HOLDRs, IShares & SPDRs

Luke introduced self-developed volatility surface algorithms to Equitec and was responsible for developing automated trading tools and models for use within Equitec’s in-house trading system

Luke left Equitec Group early in 2004 to trade with 2 partners, backed by a brokerage firm. The group used self-developed research & analysis tools to run a Relative Value Volatility trading book

Due to the group's success Luke was introduced to TradeLink LLC, a large Trading & Asset Management Group. Luke joined TradeLink as Head Equity Options Trader responsible for all Equity Options Strategy & Methodology development & direction

In December 2008, Luke joined Bengal Capital
