Rachel McDonald’s Experience

  • Teen Librarian

    King County Library System

    (Government Agency; Libraries industry)

    May 2007 — Present (2 years 5 months)

    Provide reference and reader’s advisory services to patrons of the Auburn and Muckleshoot libraries in King County Library System. Run successful videogaming, book club, and teen advisory programs for teens ages 12 and up, creating publicity, scheduling sessions and supervising volunteers. Responsible for soliciting and budgeting monies from friends of the library group. Serve as a liaison to nine secondary schools, sending out regular e-newsletters to promote library services and programs. Present booktalks and database demonstrations to middle and high school students. Responsible for maintenance of teen collection across three libraries, including weeding, replacing materials, and identifying collection gaps.

  • Children's Librarian

    King County Library System

    (Non-Profit; 501-1000 employees; Libraries industry)

    November 2006 — May 2007 (7 months)

    Presented weekly story time, toddler time, and baby time programs to patrons at the Auburn and Algona-Pacific libraries. Presented booktalks and promotional programs to students in area elementary schools. Created unique low-cost programming for children ages 6 and up, often reinforcing literacy or scientific concepts. Responsible for providing reference and reader’s advisory services to patrons of all ages, with a focus on children and young adults. Maintained juvenile fiction and non-fiction collections.

  • Youth Paraprofessional

    Kent District Library

    (Libraries industry)

    October 2003 — October 2006 (3 years 1 month)

    Presented programming and performed reference services at the largest and one of the smallest branches within a suburban library system. Assisted patrons in locating materials and using library resources, including the online catalog, databases, Microsoft Office software and Adobe Acrobat. Responsible for reading professional literature and ordering, replacing, and weeding materials in the Juvenile and Teen Fiction and Nonfiction collections. Conducted teen programs, as well as story time and baby time programs for young children. Supervised, scheduled, and assigned tasks to teen volunteers. Created special system-wide programs, including one based on the popular Spiderwick Chronicles series. Helped develop a training session on using the library catalog and databases for new elementary school teachers. Organized and maintained a collection of reader’s advisory materials for teens and children. Responsible for creating thematic displays for youth area.
