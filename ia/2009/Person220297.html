Sony Kapoor’s Summary

BioSketch

Sony is the Executive Director of Re-Define (rethinking development, finance & environment, a new international Think Tank.

He is also an International Finance, Development & Environment Consultant with a background in leveraged finance, investment banking and derivatives trading.

Sony has been working extensively with the World Bank, the United Nations, several international NGOs and both developing & developed country governments, advising them on key policy and strategic issues related to international finance, development, the environment and governance.

He is a former Director of Policy & Advocacy with Stamp Out Poverty (UK), Strategy Advisor to Oxfam Novib (Netherlands), Senior Advisor to Christian Aid (UK) & the Jubilee (USA) Network. He is also a former Member of the Board of Directors of Eurodad (European Network on Debt and Development) where he tackled key policy and advocacy issues.

Sony played a leading role in the international advocacy & policy effort around the multilateral debt cancellation deal reached in 2005, has been a prominent voice in shaping the discussion on innovative sources of financing, financial stability and has been co-ordinating aspects of the policy, advocacy and organizational effort on tackling capital flight and tax evasion.

Sony has worked for firms such as the Industrial Credit and Investment Corporation of India (ICICI), Lehman Brothers and Aquila Energy (Europe) Limited in several roles and has also led a consulting team of independent derivatives experts in London.

He holds a Chemical Engineering degree from the prestigious Indian Institute of Technology, an MBA from the Faculty of Management Studies (University of Delhi) and an MSC in International Finance from the London School of Economics.
