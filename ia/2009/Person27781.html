William F Gollan’s Summary

Bill served 3 years as CEO of Cybiz, a Supplier Enablement SW company with funding from Middle East, delivering integrated procurement solutions, clients included Renault Fr, Dimension Data, Econocom Fr and ePlus. Mr. Gollan served 2 years as President of Bidders Edge an auction aggregation & media-advertising site offering on-line auction buyers the ability to search on-line auctions across 100 auction sites without having to search each host site.

As of March '00, the site contained information on more that 5 million items. Starting in '97 Bill held the position of SVP, the equivalent of COO at Earthweb (NASDAQ; EWBX) and a member of the Corporate Governance team. Bill had global responsibility for DICE.com, e-Commerce, sales, marketing as well as R&D. Bill played a key role in the company’s November '98 IPO which raised $34 million, the May '99 follow-on raising $26 million, and finally the $70 million convertible, led by Merrill Lynch. During Bill’s tenure, revenues grew from $1 million to over $50 million, with staffing increasing from 37 professionals to well over 350. Bill leveraged his extensive IT & media industry knowledge and business experience in executing EWBX's strategic direction.

Mr. Gollan was a Senior Vice President of LitleNet beginning in February '96 focusing on electronic software distribution (ESD). From March '94 to April '96, Mr. Gollan was a Vice President, Sales and Marketing for Kurzweil AI. From December '87 to March '94, Mr. Gollan was a Managing Director of Weatherview Management Consultants. In '90, Mr. Gollan co-founded Computer Buying World Magazine, an IDG monthly trade magazine focused on the computer distribution channel.

Between '80-'87 Mr Gollan, was founder/CEO of very ealry stage mail-order catalog company focusing on Radio Shack TRS-80, Apple and IBM PCs. Prior to '80, Mr Gollan was a professional ice skating instructor (hockey), and IBM programmer.

  • President & COO

    Bidders Edge, Inc

    (Computer Software industry)

    May 2001 — September 2002 (1 year 5 months)

    Venture backed, Burlington MA company with 22 employees. BE is an auction aggregation site designed to offer on-line auction buyers the ability to search for items across numerous on-line auctions without having to search each host site individually. As of March 2000, the BE web site contained information on more that 5 million items being auctioned on more than one hundred auction sites. The information available on the BE site is contained in a database of information that BE compiles through access to various auction sites such as eBay. When a user enters a search for a particular item at BE, BE searches its database and generates a list of every item in the database responsive to the search, organized by auction closing date and time. Rather than going to each host auction site one at a time, a user who goes to BE may conduct a single search to obtain information about that item on every auction site tracked by BE.
