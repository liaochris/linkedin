Clive Ansell’s Summary

From July 1st 2007 until mid 2008, Clive Ansell was President for Strategy, Marketing & Propositions for BT Global Services, leading global strategy, marketing, M&A, product development & management, professional practices, and new business incubation across 170 countries. This included leadership roles in major developments in India and China.

He was formerly the Group Strategy Director for BT Group plc, and BT’s Regional Director for London (which included a role in the core London 2012 Olympic Bid team) and as the company champion for Equality & Diversity (where BT has won a wide range of awards).

As Group Strategy Director, Clive also led BT’s Regulatory activities at the Group level and was the architect and chief negotiator with Ofcom in putting together the Regulatory Settlement in the UK in the Telecoms Strategic Review in 2005/06 and launching BT’s Openreach business. He has also been the lead for some major public/3rd sector programmes outside of BT, such as the UK study on ICT & Productivity.

He was a long term BT employee, but returned to the UK in 2002 after three years as an executive Board Director of Japan Telecom (a TSE-quoted company), as the Senior Managing Director responsible for the Business Division (2,000 employees, 7 subsidiaries and ~$2bn turnover), and several years working extensively in the US on very major M&A/partnership deals before that.

He has worked in many different line and staff functions in BT, and in his early career, also worked for IBM in production control (and exporting to South America), in UK health service administration, and started a small engineering business.

Clive is a graduate of Oxford University (Master's in Psychology), and a Sloan Fellow of the London Business School. He is currently a Patron of Crimestoppers, having been the Chairman of London Crimestoppers, an independent charity focused on the anonymous reporting of crime, and a Board member of a number of business representative groups.

Clive Ansell’s Specialties:

Board-level strategy and organisational development; major win-win negotiations, either regulatory/governmental, M&A or commercial; deep intellectual and creative skills; independence of thought combined with a very safe pair of hands at any level of project or programme, informed by extensive line management experience.
