David Lackner’s Summary

David Lackner is a hands-on technology commercializaiton expert with experience as an engineer, innovator, and entrepreneur. He specializes in spin-out venturing for large firms developing "dangerously" advanced technologies.

He has worked with large multi-national companies like Motorola, Lockheed-Martin, Xerox, Dow Corning, Sherwin Williams, Boeing, Agilent, HP, Sprint, DEKA, and Northrop Grumman, as well as various start-ups like Nanosys, Natural Nano, TEBio, Nanomix, and Cambrios. He also helped spin-off and found companies, including Crescendo Power, Nanoconduction, NXAR, and Integrated Nanosystems, Inc.

In positions as Program Manager, Senior Analyst, and Director, Lackner spent eight years in engineering and commercialization management with Stanford Research Institute, NASA, Lux Research, and the University of California’s Bio Info Nano R&D Institute. Having the dual expertise developing advanced systems as well as leading productization, Lackner has formed a unique niche offering a strong business development network and very specific strategies in deploying cutting edge innovation.

Today, Lackner is applying his skills in nanotechnology, cleantech, and aerospace as an advocate, advisor, active engineer, strategic consultant, and business development resource. He is currently serving as VP for a solar start-up.

Lackner holds a B.S. in Aerospace Engineering from UCLA and a M.S. in Technology Management & Policy from MIT. Lackner is a highly sought after speaker, addressing nanotechnology issues by invitation at Oxford University, Dow Jones, and Deloitte & Touche, among others. He is also widely quoted in publications such as Wired, L.A. Times, San Francisco Chronicle and San Jose Mercury News.
