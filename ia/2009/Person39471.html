Chris Caudill’s Summary

Some of the notable projects that I have heavily contributed to are as follows:

ANN AND ROBERT H. LURIE CHILDREN’S HOSPITAL – CHICAGO. 1.25 million sq. ft. composite steel structure with concrete and steel lateral systems. Features include intense heavy diaphragm bracing, hanging floor systems, transfer truss systems, built-up beams and columns, sloped columns, and long-span (curved) non-homogeneous composite sections.

CLEVELAND CLINIC – ABU DHABI. 2.5 million sq. ft. fast-track composite steel structure, featuring steel and concrete lateral systems, stringent vibration criteria, eccentric floor plans, mobile cladding access system, tuned mass dampers, and performance-based lateral systems.

UW (UNIVERSITY OF WASHINGTON) HOSPITAL-SEATTLE. Concrete building featuring concrete lateral system, flat slabs, PT transfers, hanging concrete columns, and steel-framed expansion tower.

PROVIDENCE PARK HOSPITAL SYSTEMS-NOVI. Composite steel fast-track buildings featuring steel moment frames, eccentric plans, and stringent vibration criteria.

  • Research Lead

    Pennsylvania Historical and Museum Commission

    (Civil Engineering industry)

    2006 — 2008 (2 years)

    The Cornwall Iron Furnace, originally built in the early 1700's, went through a series of renovations before it was decommissioned in the mid 1800's. It was the objective of the PHMC to make structural improvements to the building under the stipulation that a) historically accurate materials must be used and b) any changes to the building must be noninvasive.

    Among the tasks that I completed, I focused my research efforts on developing a noninvasive means of preserving historical iron in an unstable environment. Additionally, I developed historically accurate mortars for repointing the building. My recommendations are currently being implemented.
