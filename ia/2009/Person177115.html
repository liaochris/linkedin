Neil Saunders’s Summary

Neil is an experienced brand strategist dedicated to the creation of consumer value through the development of meaningful brands. His planning strategies have helped build world-class brands for 20 years. He began his career in the U.K. at Lever Brothers (Unilever) where he focused on brand-leaders in the laundry detergent and household cleaner categories including Persil, Domestos, Frish and Jif. Known for his business-building approach to brand development, Neil joined Grey London in 1986 and was elected to the firm’s Board in 1991. A year later his report, “About Men”, received widespread national and international media attention.

Recruited by BBDO West in Los Angeles in 1993, Neil went on to become the Director of Planning at Citron Haligman Bedecarre in San Francisco. As a member of the firm’s core team, Neil’s client strategies helped make Citron the fastest growing independent agency on the West Coast and Adweek’s Agency of the Year in 1997. Prior to joining Leo Burnett in 2002, Neil served as the Director of Planning for Mullen LHC.

His brand experience is extensive and includes leading planning teams for Apple, Mars Confectionery, P&G Health and Beauty Care, Cover Girl Cosmetics, Sony ITA, Kellogg’s, Virgin, Dreyfus, Lee Jeans, Taunton Cider Co., Becks Beer, Wachovia Bank, Pioneer Electronics, Canon Cameras, PowerBar, Blue Cross of California, the U.S. Army and Glaxo Smith Kline Beecham among many others.

Respected within the advertising community, Neil has served as a Board Member of the AAAA Planning Committee as well as the APG.US. He has spoken at a number of industry conferences, presented to the graduates of the VCU Adcenter and serves as a long-standing instructor at the Miami Ad School. Neil lives in Chicago with his wife and two daughters.

Neil Saunders’s Specialties:

Consumer insights; Brand strategy and positioning; creating consumer value and growing business through archetypal brand strategies and storytelling; qualitative and quantitative research methodologies; brainstorming techniques; portfolio managenment; social and cultural trend analysis and marketing to men.
