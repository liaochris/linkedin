Alexander Klimov’s Summary

Software Engineer, Technologist, Web2.0 Enthusiast and Open-Networker.

I started professional career in 1998 in the Game Development by joining a group of software developers and enthusiasts united by the common goals and interests. We founded our own startup-company and released a number of successful software products on a Windows platform.

After that, I was one of the early employees of American company which focused on the producing the CAD/CAM Systems. I was involved throughout the all phases of Application Lifecycle Management (ALM) and provided software development, support and testing. I worked remotely in the globally distributed team with different time-zones.

Since April 2006, I'm working for the IBM at Russian Systems and Technology Laboratory (RSTL) in the area of software support/maintenance for IBM z/OS (mainframe's operating system). I'm a team-lead of a group of developers providing a software support for the z/OS File-System components. I'm active member of the different internal IBM communities of practice, participating and contributing to the IBM studies in the area of software development. Besides my primary tasks and responsibilities, I have a number of activities in the company towards consolidating technical leaders and establishing groups and communities to foster collaboration, share experience and knowledge assets, interconnect business lines and technical experts to support IBM's business in Russia.

I'm interested in the Application Lifecycle Management (ALM) disciplines, Agile/Scrum methodologies for co-located and distributed teams, Virtual Worlds and particularly in Second Life, Social Software and Web 2.0, Cloud Computing, IBM Jazz Platform and IBM Rational products based on that technology.

I have special interests in the area of modernization of software development tools for the developers on a IBM Mainframe Platform and IBM Jazz Platform.

It helped the company to improve the quality of software, minimize costs and to reduce the Total Cost of Ownership (TCO).
