Ana Ottman’s Summary

Ana Ottman thrives on empowering women to lead authentic and creative lives. She provides life coaching to individuals around the country.

Raised in Tucson, Arizona with a stint in Reading, England, Ana built her expertise in mentorship, nonprofit advocacy, grassroots organizing, fundraising, policy research and analysis, and strategic planning in Washington, D.C. Ana has worked for a boutique government relations firm, a national anti-violence against women nonprofit, and a women's political action committee, among other organizations.

Having obtained her graduate degree in women's studies, she volunteers with the Tranquil Space Foundation, which offers yoga, creativity and leadership workshops for girls, as well as leads creative writing classes for Rachael's Women's Center. Ana also leads a women's creativity group, Women of Mass Creation, which provides support for participant's creative dreams and goals, and inspires creative living in D.C.

Ana says, “I believe every woman has the power to create a fulfilling life for herself at any age. My clients are respected and talented women who are seeking change, and I partner with them to unearth their passions, reach their goals, and guide them as they seek more creativity and meaning in their lives.”

When she is not working, she can be found practicing vinyasa yoga, "mentoring" her four younger sisters, browsing local bookstores, and dreaming up ideas for expanding her business. Ana resides in Washington, D.C. with her husband, John.
