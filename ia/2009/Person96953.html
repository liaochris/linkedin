  • Co-Author - Complete Web Monitoring

    O'Reilly Media

    (Privately Held; Publishing industry)

    September 2008 — Present (1 year 3 months)

    I co-wrote a book with Alistair Croll for O’Reilly called “Complete Web Monitoring”. The problem statement: If you’re running a website and aren’t using analytics, you’re flying blind. It helps to know what your users are doing, whether they could get there, if they had a good time, if they’d come back, and what they’re saying to their friends about you.

    The book covers every possible way that one can measure a website, including traditional web analytics, web interaction analytics (ie – heatmaps & overlays), voice of the customer/surveying, web performance monitoring including synthetic monitoring and real user monitoring and finally, social media & its listening platforms. Since social computing is a nascent field, we took the time to differentiate between the major archetypes before we dove into the metrics and analytics considerations for each.

    Some book facts (they make me weep): 187,049 words, 670 pages, 428 Figures and 365 days from start (proposal submission) to finish. We dedicated three months to research and administrative tasks, nine months to writing & researching.
