  • Nadar Saraswathi High Scool, Usilampatti

    9th & 10th Std, 1966 — 1968

    He did his 9th and 10th Standards here.Usilampatti was, may be still is, a very backward area, in terms of literacy, standard of living, etc. Sugu still remembers with adoration Miss Suganthi, the 10th standard teacher, who was his first heroine in life. (Dont u know or guess Sugu's age, 16 years!!). She used to depute Sugu to correct (!!) annual exam papers of smaller classes, and he remembers that as the first recognition of merit in life. May be the headmaster was not aware of such practices!.
    He remembers his classmates Ramabadran, Thiagarajan, etc.

  • St.Joseph's Higher Secondary School, Cuddalore

    7th and 8th Std, School, 1964 — 1968

    The turning point in Sugu's education.
    Father Peter was the Principal. What a man!! Strict to the core, Father Peter was an awe-inspiring icon. Morning study hour precedes even the Prayer. The whole school sits in the verandah and reads silently.And then, it happens. Yes Father comes on his "Rounds", a white flowing figure with the Bible in hand, walks past them.The shoe sound 'tuck,tuck,tuck' still is heard in Sugu's ears. U r not supposed to raise ur head to look up. Some did, and there were stories going round about what happened to those guys. Father Pater watched Sugu's rise from the 26th rank to the first. Boys who score 3rd and 2nd rank receive a handshake from the father. The first rank scorer gets a hug too! The starched stiff white gown of the grim-looking Father Peter rubbing against Sugu's cheek !! Father does not speak or congratulate. But in Sugu's case, Father told Sugu in the ear. "Increase the gap between u and the second rank." And he left the class!!
