Derrick Cruz’s Summary

I am a multidisciplinary artist and designer with a knack for combining contemporary and endangered technologies to bring uncommon ideas to life.

My current focus as an artist is influenced by the Diaspora effect to create works that explore the role of memory and their relationship to objects. As a vehicle for this endeavor I created the popular cult brand Black Sheep & Prodigal Sons -- a sustainable art and design studio producing work in the realm of fine arts, fashion, craft and communication arts.

Established in 2006, Black Sheep & Prodigal Sons may be seen in high profile outlets such as Barneys, Earnest Sewn and Bblessing in New York, The Santa Monica Museum of Art and American Rag in the West Coast, and internationally in Australia and Japan. Critically, the work has been noted in dozens of major publications the likes of GQ, W, New York Magazine, Elle, Men’s Health, Tokion, and The New York Times, which in November of 2008 commissioned me to interpret their iconic “T” for the Sunday Style Magazine. In 2007 Black Sheep & Prodigal Sons won the GenArt International Styles Award for accessories.
