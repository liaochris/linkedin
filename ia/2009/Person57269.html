David Drissel’s Summary

I am professor of social sciences at Iowa Central Community College with over seventeen years of teaching experience. I served as department chair of social sciences, education and psychology, and physical education at Iowa Central from 1995 to 2001 and have been a faculty member since that time.

I have recruited and advised students, designed new courses and modules (including online courses), written and received financial grants for academic programs, advised student government, lobbied state legislators on behalf of community colleges, organized special activities for students and staff, and chaired political forums and voter registration campaigns on campus.

I have served as a member of the campus diversity team, international focus team, final exam week team, faculty senate, curriculum and instruction council, outcomes assessments team, and at-risk students team. I chaired the business department external review committee, institutional values team, and service learning team.

I have studied abroad in China (as Fulbright Scholar), Czech/Slovak Republics (as Fulbright Scholar), United Kingdom (as Oxford Roundtable participant), Costa Rica, Germany, France, Ireland, Northern Ireland, Spain, Netherlands, Austria, Hong Kong, and several other countries.

I have presented papers at numerous academic conferences and published articles in academic journals and periodicals such as the Cambridge Review of International Affairs, Internet and Society, Asian Journal of Criminology, International Journal of Interdisciplinary Social Sciences, International Journal of Diversity, Midwest Connections for International/Intercultural Education, and Policy and Politics in a Globalised World.

In addition to teaching full-time time at Iowa Central, I am working on a doctorate degree part-time at Iowa State University. My goal is to complete my doctorate in sociology within the next few years.

David Drissel’s Specialties:

Academic specializations include comparative politics, international relations, criminology and deviance, social change and development, social stratification, Asian history and culture, East European history and culture, Middle Eastern history and culture, urban space, symbolic interaction, computer-mediated communication, Internet governance, transnational social movements, post-communist/post-conflict countries and cities, youth subcultures, social networks, and popular culture.
