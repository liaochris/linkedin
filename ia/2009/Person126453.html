Karen Vega’s Summary

I am managing a portfolio of 70 projects around the world at the Development Marketplace a program of the Governance and Innovation Department at the World Bank. A competitive grant program that identifies and funds innovative, early-stage projects with high potential for development impact. I hold a Master degree in Public Policy focused in political and economic development from Harvard University. I spent a summer at the UN Headquarters in NY and Tanzania in Africa developing performance management for a Grass-roots organization. Prior to my studies in the US, I worked for 5 years with women,mayors and rural leaders in several Government Offices in Peru such as the Congress, the Vice ministry of Water and Sanitation and the Ministry of Health. My work included high official management roles and design & implementation of social development plans. During my time as master student I acquired several skills as a teaching assistant of the Leadership Courses taught by Ronald Heifetz and Professor Dean Williams, recognized professors of the Center for Public Leadership at Harvard. I am interested in continuing the application of this framework in my current and future professional positions. Earlier in Peru, I taught Research Methods at the University of Lima. Native Spanish speaker ,fluent in English and French.
