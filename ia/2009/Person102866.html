James A. Pearson’s Summary

James began his career at Harpo Studios Inc, in 1989 where he worked for Oprah Winfrey on the ABC episodic "The Women of Brewster Place" and eventually for John Hughes' Hughes Entertainment in Lake Forest Illinois. From 1989 to 1996 he worked on over thirty feature films and television shows, which led him to develop an interest in public relations and crisis management.

For the last ten years, James has worked for a number of public and private companies throughout the world developing corporate media relations and communication campaigns. Before his current position at Audible.com, he was Senior Publicist at Manhattanville College in Purchase New York, James founded Sons Of Thunder Inc. a full service Public Relations Agency. Prior to that he was a Vice President at Rubenstein Communications, a newly created division of one of the nation's leading generalist firms, servicing blue chip clients including, but not limited to: Phat Farm, Joseph Abboud Inc., BMW, Ian Schrager, and PR Newswire.

In June 2007, James was appointed to the Newark Workforce Investment Board by Newark Mayor Cory Booker.
