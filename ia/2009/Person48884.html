Conor Rafferty’s Summary

Started working as a software developer in 1994 and got very good at it, including spells as a programmer in the financial sector near Canary Wharf in London, and then freelance consulting work in DEC/Compaq (accquired by HP), and Nokia. However hankered after a more interactive work-experience so switched to IT Recruitment in 1998.

Learned how to sell, and what it takes to develop a market in a tough competitive environment (IT Recruitment in London between Y2K slowdown and the dot com bubble bursting). After three years, wanted a new adventure and set up my own IT recruitment business. Made a living in London for a year, before relocating back to my native region of Northern Ireland (NI) where the business really took off.

Particularly skilled in understanding trends in IT and recruitment and leveraging this knowledge to clients' advantage.
Penetrating new markets - business development.
Also recruiting, training and leading teams of recruitment consultants.

Passionate about applying principles of good communication, empowerment and coaching in a business setting.

Goals include creating a level-playing field in the UK recruitment industry by promoting standards, such as membership of the REC and enforcement of the Conduct Regulations.

Still make time to do a little bit of technical IT work, and am interested in applying technology to the recruitment space. My aim is for the consultants at Altmore to have the very best tools available to them to do their job.

Someday I will sit down and create the world's leading recruitment software product.

  • Senior IT Recruitment Consultant (Contracts), London

    Hays IT

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; Information Technology and Services industry)

    December 1998 — December 2001 (3 years 1 month)

    My area was IT contract jobs, London region, I was targeted on performance: Gross margin billed and Number of contractors running.

    At my peak I was bringing in over £300k p.a. Gross Margin for the company (on approx. £2m turnover p.a.), and running 32 contractors.

    Some jobs came from existing clients (PSD - Preferred Supplier Deal or volume discount deal in place). However the best jobs are “New Business” jobs because we can charge higher fees (not having signed a previous PSD), and there may be fewer other agencies working on them. A consultant needs to be proactive to find these - maintaining an ever growing list of clients that one is ever getting closer to. I spent a lot of time developing new clients along the sales cycle.

    I became particularly expert in devising ways of searching and contacting candidates on our own database - I created Skill Codes to summarize and help me quickly find the right person. I used mass emails to quickly contact appropriate candidates.
