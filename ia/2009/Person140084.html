Les Breeding’s Summary

Les is a college instructor and legislative research consultant who seeks positive empowerment.

Les Breeding’s Specialties:

Legislative and Congressional Research; Instruction of PC and Mac Applications; Citizen Empowerment Training

Les Breeding’s Experience

  • Instructor, Business Department

    Virginia College

    (Educational Institution; 501-1000 employees; Higher Education industry)

    May 2006 — Present (3 years 1 month)

    Les is an instructor at Virginia College, Austin's largest career college, where he teaches Windows XP, Keyboarding, and Microsoft Office applications (Word, Access, Excel, and Publisher). (However, his true expertise is on the Macintosh platform). Les has found he has a knack for effectively sharing all of that computer knowledge he has learned over the past 20 years -- and some of the students even laugh at his jokes.

  • Owner & Research Consultant

    Capitol Research

    (Legal Services industry)

    April 1997 — Present (12 years 2 months)

    Les Breeding is the owner of Capitol Research Services and is its research consultant. Les has had several roles in the Legislature including five years as Legislative Director for a Member of the Texas House of Representatives. He learned the tricks of the trade necessary to get legislation passed and killed, and now puts that knowledge to use helping his clients understand the evolution and intent of legislation.
    With 14 years of legislative research experience, Les has researched hundreds of bills. In 1997, he founded Capitol Research Services, a full-service legislative intent research firm. He can ensure that you get the full story of how your bill was passed, the impetus behind it, and the specific meanings attached to its wording.

  • Legislative Director

    Office of State Rep. Lon Burnam

    (Government Administration industry)

    November 1998 — June 2003 (4 years 8 months)

    For three sessions of the Texas Legislature, Les was in charge of the daily operations of the Capitol Office of State Rep. Lon Burnam. Les managed 3-4 full-time and as many as 10 part-time staff and was responsible for the development of Rep. Burnam's legislative agenda.

  • Legislative Assistant

    Office of State Rep. John Hirschi

    (Government Administration industry)

    September 1991 — January 1994 (2 years 5 months)

    Les was responsible for the development of Rep. Hirschi's health and ethics legislation. The primary focus of their work was the Children's Tobacco Control Act and the Tuberculosis Control Act.
