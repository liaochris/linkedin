Petros Chamakiotis’s Summary

Petros is a multilingual (English, Spanish, Greek & German) young researcher currently studying for his PhD at the University of Bath, UK. His research, as further explained below, is multidisciplinary and, though sponsored by the Department of Mechanical Engineering (and specifically IdMRC), is co-supervised by the School of Management. He has gained professional experience with two leading international organizations based in Madrid, Spain and has therefore developed expertise in the areas of Online Marketing and Human Resources. His research interests lie in Creativity and Innovation, Design, Global Virtual Teams and Virtual Networks, e-Leadership, Motivation and e-Marketing, and his work, principally empirical, has thus far appeared in MCIS, EGOS, IGI Global, and IFIP WG 9.5, whilst more publications are forthcoming.

Petros Chamakiotis’s Experience

  • PhD Researcher

    University of Bath

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    October 2009 — Present (1 month)

    It has been acknowledged in the literature that the environment plays an important role when it comes to the effectiveness of collaborative and creative business processes, such as design. Traditionally, the design environment has been looked at through cognitive, sensory and physical elements. Yet, with the advent of virtuality, the environment can no longer be seen as just physical. As a result, today’s design environment is made up of both the virtual and the physical environment and thus my PhD research, very broadly described, seeks to identify, conceptualize and discuss the factors that influence (positively and/or negatively) creativity in virtual design teams and collocated design teams.

  • International Marketing Assistant

    IE Business School

    (Educational Institution; Education Management industry)

    May 2009 — September 2009 (5 months)

    My duties consisted in supporting the development, management and evaluation of IE’s online advertising campaigns. In consideration of the budget and the current needs, we developed an online advertising strategy per academic year. I in continuation dealt with national and international media and I also liaised among the partner agencies which accomplished our ideas, and the online media wherein IE advertised. Though the job itself was focused on online advertising, my duties also embraced offline advertising, social networks, viral marketing and website marketing.

  • Human Resources Officer

    AFA Press

    (Marketing and Advertising industry)

    August 2008 — April 2009 (9 months)

    The job tackled the areas of recruitment and selection, training and performance management, jobs advertising, and lastly employee relations. Main tasks included CV analysis, interviewing, coordinating recruitment and informative events (Europe-wide), shadowing the training of entry-level employees and organizing their induction sessions, negotiating with recruitment advertising providers (account managers) to get the best deal, while aiming to develop a long-term relationship with them, measuring the outcome of each advertising campaign against the initial goals, liaising with candidates and trainees, and meeting departmental managers to identify recruitment needs.
