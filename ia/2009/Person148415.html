Marcello Barnaba’s Experience

  • IT engineer

    Antifork Research Inc.

    (Non-Profit; Computer Software industry)

    February 2006 — Present (3 years 2 months)

    I am the current maintainer of Antifork.ORG services. Antifork is a non-profit association of hackers, focusing mainly on security related topics and that produced lots of interesting code from 2001 to 2006. All of it is available on the public TRAC located at http://antifork.org/trac/.

    Back in 2003, I developed a PoC protection against the ARP spoofing MITM technique implemented by ettercap (http://ettercap.sf.net) and released on the Antifork repository as "anticap" (http://antifork.org/trac/browser/trunk/anticap) for Linux, FreeBSD and NetBSD.

    I currently maintain the italian OpenSource.ORG mirror on behalf of Antifork (http://opensource.antifork.org/), implemented with the following script: http://gist.github.com/61474

  • CTO

    Adelao Group

    (Information Technology and Services industry)

    While working at the Adelao start-up, I leveraged my skills of Web2.0 application development, marketing and strategic use of social networks to harness the collective intelligence that's behind and inside the web. I learnt and used new technologies and online services, and managed the team that brought myousica.com online.

  • Software engineer

    Marley and Cooper

    (Online Media industry)

    December 2008 — February 2009 (3 months)

    At Marley&Cooper, I developed the successfull and innovative Web2.0 tourism platform for the municipality of Città Sant'Angelo, Abruzzi, Italy. It employs the krpano flash panorama viewer and mashes it up with Google Maps and the site own data to let an user to dive into the city's points of interest looking at them with a 360' vision: http://www.visitacsa.it/.

  • Developer, IT engineer

    myousica.com

    (Internet industry)

    February 2008 — January 2009 (1 year)

    I developed a consistent part of the myousica.com codebase, and was responsible of the whole IT process that brought it online.

    Myousica was an innovative web2.0 application that aimed to give musicians (and not) a new way of making up music online, building up on the concept of sharing songs and the tracks they're composed of, and making possible to assemble them online.

    It was composed of two Rails applications, one implemented the HTML and XML frontends, and another handled the long-running background tasks that encoded and mixed down song's and track's MP3 files. It used Flash Media Server to implement live recording via a Flex multitrack editor developed by Vàclav Vančura.

  • Consultant and developer

    Spada S.p.A.

    (Privately Held; Public Relations and Communications industry)

    March 2007 — December 2007 (10 months)

    While working for Spada S.p.A., I designed and set up intranet and messaging services using Windows 2003, Exchange server 2003, Debian Linux and Postfix / SpamAssassin, whilst also managing the security assessment of the whole Intranet.

    I developed a custom solution to handle fail-over of multiple Internet Service Provider uplinks and released it as open source on http://github.com/vjt/ispfailover/tree

    I also designed and implemented a business-critical web2.0 application for initial evaluation of residential and industrial buildings construction costs, using the Ruby on Rails framework.

  • Software engineer, Network administrator

    Softmedia s.c.r.l.

    (Privately Held; Internet industry)

    December 2000 — March 2007 (6 years 4 months)

    While working at Softmedia, I leveraged technical skills learnt on my own and at the university, entered into the vision of team work and learnt how to establish and maintain customer relations

    Public projects:
    - http://voicebook.com/ - learn foreign languages while listening to them
    - http://morfimare.it/ - online ferry booking company
    - http://premiobiol.it/ - international award for extra-virgin organic oil
    - tweaked, improved and maintained ~50 sites

    Private/corporate side:
    - OscarWEB, a damage assessment managerial and information software
    - M.A.P, an enterprise general-purpose monitoring application of geographically distributed devices, deployed for Telestet/TIM Greece
    - Ecole, an ecological sanitation managerial software that allows design, mapping and cost/bill of materials provisioning of sanitation services

    I also set up, designed and maintained the company network infrastructure (200+ client hosts and 15+ server hosts)

  • Services engineer

    Azzurra.ORG

    (Computer Networking industry)

    January 2002 — November 2005 (3 years 11 months)

    I wrote the most of the Azzurra IRC server customization patches, adding support for IPv6, SSL, cloaked client hostnames and a bunch of other features on top of the bahamut software, originally developed by dal.net. The code is available on sourceforge: http://bahamut-inet6.sf.net/.

    During this process I also wrote a patch for the irssi IRC client that gave it SSL support and was integrated in the main source tree. I also started an ambitious project that aimed to realize multithreaded, sql-backed IRC services, that grew until the interest in IRC fell down. The code still compiles and works, though, and is available on http://suxserv.sf.net/.

  • Associate Network Administrator

    Centro Reti Puglia - Università degli Studi di Bari

    (Computer Networking industry)

    June 2002 — September 2003 (1 year 4 months)

    During this period I maintained and improved a cluster system composed of four Debian GNU/Linux machines and used as a Web, Real Media, LDAP and SMB server. It used LVS for network addressing, DRBD for filesystem replication and Heartbeat for master/slave failover.

    I also developed a patch for the burneye binary encryption software wrote by the TESO team. It adds support for the Pentium-III processor serial number as a key to check whether execution was allowed on a particular machine.

    Finally, I held three lessons at the end of the 2002 Operating Systems course in Computer science, focusing on the Unicode character encoding system, Windows error handling and Windows NT Kernel Objects. All the artifacts are available on http://www.barnaba.openssl.it/
