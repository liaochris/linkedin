Ralph Cochrane’s Summary

Ralph is an award winning entrepreneur and innovator. He co-founded MOFILM in 2007 after delivering the pioneering Sundance Global Short Film Festival collaboration with the GSM Association, which saw world-famous Hollywood film makers produce made-for-mobile films.

A chartered engineer by background, Ralph has a long history in innovation and marketing, having built leading innovation marketing agency Clarity International and more recently Tactable, an MIT spin out focused on multi-touch surface computing.

Ralph began his career at the world famous BT Research Centre in Martlehsam UK working on the web, search and personalisation in the mid to late 1990s, before helping to create BT's Brightstar corporate incubator by raising $10M to spin out BT's content distribution technology. More recently, Ralph also founded the 21st Century Global Summit to foster better relations between media, technology and major brands in understanding business innovation.

Other projects have included international music licensing mainly for mobile operators in SE Asia and Africa; BT Global Services Mobility, which the team turned around from a loss making unit to generating over £100M + in revenue through enterprise mobility solutions and content aggregation for Mobile at iO.
