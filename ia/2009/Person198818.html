Ricky Steele’s Summary

May 1st, 2009 I started a new chapter in my career by joining Hunter Technical Resources as their Chief Development Officer. Founded in 1998 by Georgia Tech engineers, HUNTER Technical Resources (www.htrjobs.com) is the preferred technical staffing firm for companies seeking outstanding IT, telecom and engineering professionals. HUNTER’s knowledgeable executives leverage deep industry connections and long-term relationships to deliver ideal candidates for contract and permanent positions. HUNTER serves a national client base from its headquarters in Atlanta, GA.After 2 wonderful years at Definition 6,

My new role combines 30 years of being an entrepreneur with a focus on new business development, client retention, public relations, and community development.

My background includes creating the largest destination management company in the southeast during the 80's. I created Georgia Travel Publications in 1986 and published several titles including the official travel guide of the state of Georgia, "Georgia On My Mind". In 1992, I became the President of Innovative Media which produced videos for newcomers in 6 cities. Our production won an Atlanta Telly award and within two years we sold the company to Cox Broadcasting for a triple digit return on our shareholders investment.

Director of Emerging Growth Technology Company's for PricewaterhouseCoopers in early 2000 moving in 2002 to become a Client Partner with Korn/Ferry International. I left K/F in September of 2002 to write The Heart of Networking which has received positive reviews across the country and is available on my website at www.rickysteele.net. My most recent engagement was two great years as the Chief Development Officer for Definition 6, www.definition6.com.

Ricky Steele’s Specialties:

My entire career has been focused on building relationships that drive growth through relationships.
