Susan Cohan, APLD’s Summary

Award winning landscape designer, who specializes in the creation of beautifully crafted outdoor spaces for gracious living. Each design is carefully conceived to complement its surroundings and enhance the lives of its owners. In addition to residential landscapes for private clients she has won the highest design honors for her concept gardens for the New Jersey Flower and Garden Show and the Mansion in May and Stately Homes by the Sea designer showhouses.

From woodland walks to patios and outdoor kitchens to formal garden rooms, Susan draws on a wide range of influences to develop her designs. Attention to detail, sustainable practices and fine craftsmanship are hallmarks of her work. Artisanal stonework, handmade details, and plants chosen specifically for each site combine to address each project’s unique characteristics. No two finished landscapes are ever the same.

Susan guides her clients through the design/build process from concept to completion. Each step is carefully communicated to the client enabling them to visualize how all of the elements will be combined into a unified and very personal garden setting.

After achieving a successful career in the fashion industry, Susan switched gears and fused her love for design with a lifelong passion for gardening. In addition earning an honors certificate in garden design from the English Gardening School at the Chelsea Physic Gardens in London, Susan has earned certificates in horticulture and landscape design from Rutger’s University.

Susan Cohan's work has been featured in traditional and digital print as well as other media outlets. Her blog, ‘Miss Rumphius’ Rules’ features work in progress, musings on the creative process and insights on design. A founding member of the NJ chapter of the Association of Professional Landscape Designers (APLD), she also serves as a member of the international board of directors as its Membership Chair.

Susan Cohan, APLD’s Specialties:

Client driven, architecturally sensitive landscape design, garden design, outdoor living spaces for gracious living, sustainable landscape design, garden features and garden architecture
