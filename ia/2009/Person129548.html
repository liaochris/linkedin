Kelly Rutschilling’s Summary

I am currently a senior at BGSU aspiring for a career in graphic design, and especially in the packaging design field. I have had a passion for design since a young child. From my very first design project in high school (creating a cereal box), I knew what I wanted to do for the rest of my life. As a child, my parents would find me constantly climbing, exploring, playing with legos, building things, and asking why and how things were made. This has continued throughout my life (yes, I still occasionally play with Legos).


My ultimate dream / goal in life is to design a product, see it on the shelf in a large mass merchandiser, and watch someone purchase the product.



I have had many types of experience along with my employment that is listed below. In college I have been in many organizations implementing and building my talent and skills. I have been a member of the following organizations:

Fine Arts Society - Public Relations Officer
Alpha Omicron Pi - Vice President of Communications
VCTO - Visual Communication Technology Organization
Technology Ambassadors

Each of these organizations have pertained to the graphic design industry in some form. In the organizations I held offices in, I did the advertising and promotional work. I made flyers, banners, newspaper ads, websites, etc.

I have also done a large amount of freelance work.

  • ITS continued...

    ITS Lab Consultant

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    January 2007 — Present (2 years 7 months)

    One example out of the many interactions I have encountered is:

    "A client entered the lab asking how to scan, import, and insert pictures into Powerpoint. The type of project she was trying to complete was a presentation showing hundreds of music sheets and the variations between them. She wanted to insert all of the pictures in one single powerpoint document. I first showed the woman how to scan using Adobe Photoshop. and explained about resolution, and the sizing of images. This helped her realize how to effectively scan her images. I then explained that using a powerpoint slide show for all images may not work the best because the size would be huge, slow to work with, and take a long time. I suggested using the automate tool in Photoshop to make a pdf presentation. This file would be much smaller in size and easier to make - just one click! The woman was extremely impressed, especially because I had other recommendations and an easier way for her to get her project completed."

  • Design Co-op

    (Privately Held; Packaging and Containers industry)

    January 2008 — May 2008 (5 months)

    I learned about the corrugated paperboard industry, making box samples for various projects and products. I worked closely with a CAD/CAM system in order to make the samples.     - Learned about the Flexographic printing / die cutting industry

    I aspire to become a graphic packaging designer, and this position greatly boosted my experience.

  • Pre-Press & Bindery Co-op

    Globus Printing & Packaging

    (Printing industry)

    May 2007 — August 2007 (4 months)

    As my first co-op experience for my Visual Communication Technology major, I learned a large amount of information about the printing industry. Globus produces mostly college text books, and they have many printing presses and a huge finishing department. On my job, I worked in the bindery and pre-press departments, but experienced the surroundings of every department. In bindery, I worked in re-binds (custom updates for text books), perfect binding, shrink wrapping, and the three-hole-drill departments. I also packaged the products, loaded the pallets, and attached the mailing labels. In pre-press, I designed covers for the books and prepped the files for output. I also shadowed the other employees for various tasks such as preparing the plates, setting up impositions, and many more. This job allowed me to learn the basics of the printing industry and see them happen. It also allowed me to learn the important aspects of printing that need to be considered when designing any piece.
