Imran Bashir’s Summary

An innovative, creative, and experienced engineer with extensive practice in radio architecture design, analysis, and debug. Extensive R&D background combined with hindsight, teamwork, focus, and goal oriented attitude.

Imran Bashir’s Specialties:

• 7 years experience in RF measurements such as modulation accuracy/SNR, spectrum, phase noise, impedance matching, source/load pull, amplifier distortion, spurious emissions, noise figure, sensitivity, IP2, IP3
• 6 years experience in transceiver architecture design, system analysis, and effect of analog impairments on system performance of wireless SoCs for 2G/2.5G/3G applications
• 6 years experience in non-signaling / signaling / protocol layer tests on GSM/EDGE/GPRS mobile station

Imran Bashir’s Experience

  • Member of Group Technical Staff

    Texas Instruments - WTBU

    (Public Company; TXN; Semiconductors industry)

    May 2003 — January 2009 (5 years 9 months)

    Design debug and evaluation of Digital Radio Processor (DRP) based cellular derivatives for GSM/EDGE/GPRS applications. Collaborate with customers and other TI worldwide groups including system, design and product engineering to help define, schedule & execute post-silicon design verification activities. Identify design / protocol layer issues and drive debug activities till all performance limiting issues are resolved. Promote Digital Radio Processor (DRP) technology, instill leadership, and train peers.

  • Electrical Design Engineer

    Texas Instruments - Sensors and Controls – RFID

    (Electrical/Electronic Manufacturing industry)

    April 2002 — April 2003 (1 year 1 month)

    Support 13.56MHz / 134kHz Wireless Commerce / Access Control Technology Products from concept stage to (RTP) release to production in circuit design and simulation, schematic / build of material development, layout, characterization & testing, product qualification & failure analysis. Developed prototype units within 4 months of concept stage and shipped several hundred units in final plastic before customer deadline.

  • RF Applications Engineer (Co-op)

    Texas Instruments - HPA

    (Public Company; TXN; Semiconductors industry)

    June 2001 — December 2001 (7 months)

    Assisted in customer designs involving ISM Band Wireless Communication transceiver ICs for remote control, keyless entry, modem, medical and voice applications.
