Spiros SPIROU’s Summary

I am a Project Manager and Software Engineer at the R&D Division of Intracom Telecom, Greece. I have been a Research Associate at NCSR “Demokritos”, Greece, and a Teaching Assistant at AIT (Carnegie Mellon University partner), Greece.

Previously, my interests included high-energy physics data acquisition (CERN LHC/CMS), neural networks, speech interfaces, and grid computing (FP7 EGEE, FP7 EGEE-II). I am now working on IPTV, peer-to-peer networking, network management (FP7 SmoothIT), optical networks (FP7 SARDANA), and embedded systems.

I have a Ptychion (BSc) in computer systems engineering, a Postgraduate Diploma in mathematics and an MSc in information processing and neural networks, all with distinctions. I am currently studying part-time for the PhD in electronic & electrical engineering.

I have presented my work in numerous academic and industrial conferences around the world, and I have received merit awards and scholarships from Greek and British institutions. Also, I was the recipient of a Best Student Paper award at the 10th IEEE International Symposium on Consumer Electronics. I am a member of the IEEE and the ACM.

I am interested in high-quality, high-risk, and high-ROI engineering projects with like-minded people.

Spiros SPIROU’s Specialties:

Research proposal authoring, project management, software engineering, peer-to-peer streaming, networking, IPTV, grid computing, embedded systems, speech user interfaces, neural networks, pattern recognition, communication, presentation.
