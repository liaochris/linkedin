Erin Kennedy, CPRW, CERW’s Summary

Erin's Writing Philosophy:

I am a Certified Professional Resume Writer, Certified Expert Resume Writer and President of Professional Resume Services http://www.proreswriters.com
I write for resumes and cover letters for all clients. Mid-to-senior level professionals, executives, and c-level executives leading billion dollar companies. I've written for just about every industry and samples of my work can be seen in 8 different best selling career books. Every year I've been nominated for the "T.O.R.I" awards (Toast Of the Resume Industry), chosen out of hundreds of resumes and certified writers.

I've never met a client "situation" I didn't like. From too many jobs, to career changes, to a disgruntled former employer, you name it and I'm up to the challenge. Typically though, what a client thinks is a "challenge" really isn't as bad as they think.

My writing is somewhat aggressive-- direct and to-the-point-- but it really depends on the industry and the client's experience. I like to have fun with the project, be creative, play with wording until I get the perfect fit. I look at each project as a new "creation". Each project is unique. I don't use templates or prepared formats. I take pride in my work. It makes my day when my clients say, "WOW!! Is this really me? I'd hire me!"

I try to establish an easy and collaborative relationship with clients. I poke, prod and ask, ask, ask to uncover more tidbits of information. I remain very conscious of what the client wants to get across. To me, that is the key element in a client's satisfaction. Satisfaction is what keeps my clients coming back and what encourages them to tell their friends and family about me.

I strive to stay very current on what is new and what the latest trends are. Fresh and current are extremely important to your resume's success. I like to create a "story" for readers, so it piques their interest and makes them want to read on.

Erin Kennedy, CPRW, CERW’s Specialties:

Taking a so-so executive resume and turning it into a power-packed personal campaign- defining your brand and showcasing you as the obvious choice.

Aesthetically, resumes need to look great. Space, font, and format are essential to a well-written resume. Too much content, too little? Everything matters when it comes to format. It has to look perfect.
