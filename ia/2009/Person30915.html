Bonnie Petrie’s Summary

Bonnie Petrie’s Specialties:

News Anchoring (specializing in breaking news and long form coverage), News Writing, Column Writing, Essay Writing, Reporting, Audio Production, Voice Over Talent (commercial and industrial), Pregnancy and Babies

Bonnie Petrie’s Experience

  • Host/Producer

    Pea in the Podcast

    (Internet industry)

    January 2008 — Present (1 year 3 months)

    This podcast series is targeted at pregnant women, including everything they need to know about their bodies, their babies, and the big changes ahead in their lives in their journey to becoming a mommy. It includes 40 week-by-week podcasts with information on what’s going on with your body and your baby during each week of pregnancy, and 35 longer form podcasts (15 to 40 minutes in length). Producing the podcasts has involved researching topics, soliciting experts, doing interviews, writing, and hosting the final product.

  • Afternoon Drive Anchor

    NewsRadio 740 KTRH

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; Broadcast Media industry)

    September 2001 — January 2009 (7 years 5 months)

    2005-2009 Responsible for anchoring regularly scheduled newscasts, breaking news, and long-form news shows with in-studio guests and callers. KTRH anchors also enterprise and write stories on a wide assortment of subjects, and write and produce multi-part series.

    2002-2004 Co-anchor of all-news wheels, afternoon drive.

    2001-2002 Afternoon News Editor -- Responsible for writing the station's all news wheels and directing newsroom activity.

  • Afternoon Drive Anchor/Reporter

    News/Talk Radio 810 WGY

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; Broadcast Media industry)

    November 1999 — September 2001 (1 year 11 months)

    Responsible for writing and anchoring all afternoon newscasts, interacting with the talk show host, managing afternoon newsroom activities, and some street reporting.

    WGY is a 50,000 watt heritage station with a clear channel on its frequency. It is located in New York State's Capital District.

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Broadcast Media industry)

    November 1998 — November 1999 (1 year 1 month)
