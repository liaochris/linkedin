Tom Dawkins’s Summary

Media and arts project junkie. Founded youth organisations at high school and university before establishing Vibewire Youth Media and Arts Inc. to create expression opportunities for young Australians through media and arts in 2000, whilst still at university. During this time I've also worked for a variety of organisations and projects, including Next Media, InTheMix.com.au, The Community Broadcasting Association of Australia, Noise Festival and local government. A regular speaker at conferences, seminars and events I've also been published in Internet.au, Lip, Interface and Ministry magazines, and Next Music, CBOnline, InTheMix.com.au, ResidentAdvisor and Vibewire websites. I've assisted several organisations with program design and youth communication strategies, including NRMA Motoring and Services and Dendy Cinemas. For five years I organised electronic music events in Sydney under the name 'circuitry'.

In 2003 I was awarded the NSW Home-Based Microbusiness of the Year and Non-Profit Microbusiness of the Year Awards. In 2005 Vibewire was awarded the World Summit Youth Award for Community Engagement by the United Nations as part of the World Summit on the Informaiton Society and I was featured as one of the "Hot 30 Under 30" by the Sydney Morning Herald. In 2007 I was given the Future Summit Leadership Award at the Future Summit, organised by Australian Davos Connections and a Global YouthActionNet Fellowship from the International Youth Foundation. I am on the board of the International Institute for Communications Australia and was previously on the National Steering Committee of the Australian Youth Climate Coalition.

In April 2008 I left Vibewire and spent time traveling before being appointed Digital Marketing Strategist of Ashoka - Innovators for the Public.

Tom Dawkins’s Specialties:

Online media, youth engagement and communication strategies, e-Democracy, project management, event production/promotion, partnership development and management.
