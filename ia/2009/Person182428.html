Paula Arturo’s Summary

My professional experience is quite rich. I began as a language trainer at several multinational companies. As time progressed, language training made me connect with my profound love for foreign languages and inspired me to seek a career in translation. After becoming a translator, I discovered two areas of specialization that I found particularly appealing: Law and Medicine.

While working as a translator, I once again discovered a new passion: Law. This passion was the reason why I signed up for Law School. Upon graduation I will seek a Master’s Degree in International Law and Human Rights. I believe my years of experience as a translator will be of great value while pursuing a career in Law in the future.

Paula Arturo’s Experience

  • Freelance Translator

    PaulaArturo.com

    (Translation and Localization industry)

    January 2001 — Present (8 years 8 months)

    I’m a legal and medical translator with training in both areas of specialization.

    Law: I’m currently a third year law student and feel my legal training makes me a very strong candidate for legal texts. I have the necessary tools to understand complex legal issues and deep knowledge of legal vocabulary in different areas of the law.
    Medicine: I’ve been translating medical texts since 2001 for both multinational translation companies as well as direct clients. Some of my clients in this field include industry leaders like Genzyme Corporation and Bayer, as well as several local Hospitals and international organizations such as IPPFA and PPFA (its US affiliate). Some of my translation work has been published in major journals and can be referenced online.

    As someone who grew up in the U.S. in a half-English and half-Spanish speaking household, I’m very comfortable with both languages and can render accurate, idiomatic, natural-sounding work.

  • ACE Trainer

    Teletech Holdings

    (Public Company; TTEC; Telecommunications industry)

    May 2004 — March 2005 (11 months)

    Thanks to my background in Language Training and my years living in the U.S., I was given the opportunity to provide English Accent Neutralization Training and Coaching to Teletech employees. I am a certified ACE (Accent Neutralization) trainer and trained over 100 people for several different Teletech accounts including Kodak and Best Buy.

    My duties consisted of providing full-day accent neutralization training to Teletech employees. This included gathering and designing training materials, providing one-on-one and group training sessions, providing feedback to learners, evaluating learners, and providing progress reports to my superiors.

    Accent Neutralization is taught mainly through imitation and communicational approaches, therefore, in order to train others, one must have excellent language skills. Because I was raised in the U.S., I have a perfect American accent and was able to help Spanish speakers with strong Hispanic accents sound as neutral as possible. Training and Coaching also requires very good people skills and listening skills.

    I’m a certified Business English Trainer and have provided language training services for major multinationals, most of which now use my translation services, including but not limited to: Gillette, Motorola, Unilever, and Genzyme Corp.
