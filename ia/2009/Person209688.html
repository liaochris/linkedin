Sarah Happel’s Summary

In 1998, on the recommendation of her theater teacher, Sarah Happel sent photos to the Ford Modeling Agency in Scottsdale, Arizona. She began her modeling career with agent Robert Black. Within two years, she was working internationally while balancing business school at Arizona State University.

After graduating with an honors degree, Happel moved to Los Angeles to begin intensive theatrical scene study and improvisation classes. Happel studied screenwriting at UCLA Extension. She had success booking soap operas, and national and foreign commercial spots. These bookings afforded Happel the production of her first short film, Fairy Tale Follies.

Fairy Tale Follies was written, produced, and directed by Happel. Becoming a festival success, Fairy Tale Follies played at the famous Hollywood hotspot Mann’s Chinese Theater. Among numerous festival wins, Happel won “Best Director” at the New York Downtown Short Film Festival and “Best Short Film” at the Real to Reel Film Festival. (See www.FairyTaleFollies.com)

Happel went on to create a two-act farcical play entitled Modelogues. Based on her life as a model, Modelogues pokes fun at society and the fascination with idol worship while questioning the importance placed on beauty. The play ran for six weeks and received rave reviews from various LA critics including The Ticket Stub, LA Weekly, and KCLA Radio. Players Press is publishing Modelogues and it is due out in Spring 2009. (See www.Modelogues.com)

Happel went on to work at World Race Productions on The Amazing Race. Following the completion of Season 14, she moved with an Emmy Award winning Executive Producer into television development. She worked on various genres including competition reality, scripted, music specials, docu-reality and other programming.

Due to the success of Modelogues, Happel serves as SoCal Magazine's theater reviewer and currently works at Tijuana Entertainment in story.
