Brandy W. Kreisler’s Summary

As a teacher, my life has been greatly enriched by the ability to reach non-traditional students. The ability to teach asynchronously has allowed instructors like me to reach out to students who are striving to balance school, work, and often-complex personal lives. It is our duty and our calling to inspire as well as educate these students.

The students who attend our classes have been abandoned by traditional educational institutions. They begin our classes feeling insecure, under-prepared, and often pressured by outside forces. As instructor, we have to allay their fears, boost their confidence, and often teach them soft skills such as time management and punctuality.

This type of nurturing not only fosters student retention—it also gives non-traditional students the confidence to succeed in all aspects of their lives. I have witnessed time and again the blossoming of a non-traditional student who has developed a pattern of success in one of my classes.

At the same time that we are supporting the development of these new skills in our students, we have to create and maintain exacting standards in our classroom. We must do this in order to foster the expectations of excellence that begin in our individual classes and ripples outwards through the entire university. We must continuously juggle the need to be supportive and nurturing with the requirements of rigorous and objective academic standards.

In this vein, we must also be on alert to make sure that our students leave our classes having been taught the fundamentals of academic honesty and due diligence. The Internet has allowed the vices to plagiarism and sloppy scholarship to flourish, and we have to be aware and responsive to these issues.
