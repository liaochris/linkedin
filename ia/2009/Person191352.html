Queen Quet Marquetta Goodwine’s Summary

Queen Quet is a published author, computer scientist, lecturer, mathematician, historian, columnist, preservationist, and "The Art-ivist."   She was one of the first inductees to the Gullah/Geechee Nation Hall of Fame. She has not only provided histo-musical presentations throughout the world, but was also the first Gullah/Geechee person to speak on behalf of her people before the UN in Genevé, Switzerland.  She was selected, elected, and enstooled by her people to be the first Queen Mother of and official spokesperson for the Gullah/Geechee Nation. As a result, she is respectfully referred to as "Queen Quet, Chieftess of the Gullah/Geechee Nation."  She is the head-of-state for the Gullah/Geechee Nation.

Queen Quet is the Permanent Representative to the UN for IHRAAM which is an NGO with consultative status with the UN. She is on the ICHR board. She is one of the voices archived at the UN telling the human rights story of the Gullah/Geechee Nation at UNESCO headquarters. She was one of the first guests of the US Department of State under President Obama's administration. She was invited to present on the "dynamism and diversity of the United States" in Guadalajara. Mexico.

Queen Quet’s work is what led to the passing of the Gullah/Geechee Cultural Heritage Act by the US Congress..  She was officially vetted by the White House of the United States to serve as an expert commissioner for and to be the Chair of the Gullah/Geechee Cultural Heritage Corridor GMP.

Queen Quet has studied ethnography and historic preservation and is a leader in cultural preservation. The Gullah/Geechee Nation International Music & Movement Festival™ was the vision of Queen Quet. She is one of the founders of and the Chancellor of the International University of the Gullah/Geechee Nation.  

Queen Quet's writings include a thirty volume series on Gullah/Geechee culture, a Gullah anthology, and encyclopedias. She continues to consult for and appear in numerous films.
