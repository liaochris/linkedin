Brandon T. Miller’s Summary

"Data is the new black" ~~Chris Wexler, Interactive AMD, Crispin Porter + Bogusky

My time at Carmichael Lynch has been a tremendous growth period for me professionally. Starting out in May of 2007 as an assistant planner, I had some great experience in brand management and campaign strategy through my experience with the National Student Advertising Competition and various internships, but was certainly lacking in the executional know-how; particularly in the online arena, as I had really only sought an online position because I had a "this-is-the-future" mindset about it, without really understanding why or how true that was.

Fourteen months (and a promotion to digital planner) later, and I've been fortunate enough to have had the opportunity to learn the ropes in online media while working on some fantastic brands. I've begun to understand some of the ways that data can make online perhaps the most powerful marketing tool available, and certainly the most measurable. I've learned that engaging your market in conversation is invaluable, as well as tactics to go about generating that conversation. Online marketing is a very exciting place to be, and in whatever roles I occupy, I want to continue to develop my expertise in the space, and help my brands to continue to evolve in that conversation (based, of course, on some of that cold, hard data).

  • AE and Media Planning Intern

    Risdall Marketing Group

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Marketing and Advertising industry)

    2006 — 2007 (1 year)

    My internship at Risdall afforded me the opportunity to wear two hats within the advertising industry--an experience that proved very beneficial.

    The first role was as an account intern. In this capacity, I was given the opportunity to work directly with Risdall's clients to develop advertising strategies and recommendations; then to sell them through with formal client presentations.

    In the second role, I served as a media planning intern. I was charged with researching and uncovering emerging media, and providing cost-effective recommendations of both new and traditional media.

  • President

    National Student Advertising Competion

    (Educational Institution; 11-50 employees; Marketing and Advertising industry)

    August 2006 — June 2007 (11 months)

    I served as Co-President, Media Lead and a Lead Presenter of the 2007 National Champion N.S.A.C. team for the challenge issued by the Coca-Cola company. We at the University of Minnesota bested over 160 teams en route to our school's first national title.

    In N.S.A.C., universities across the country are provided an objective by the event's corporate sponsor and are then charged with creating an integrated marketing communications plan to meet that objective.
