Ron Schott’s Summary

For some, the world would be much simpler if it were all played out in predicable fashion; each and every action carefully typed-out in crisp, clean Arial 10 font. That’s not the world for me and definitely not the kind of work for me either.

While I may have studied PR and began my career in a media relations-focused track, I try not to categorize myself as simply a PR professional. My experience in design and advertising make me the kind of communication-minded specialist I am today; a sort of out-of-the-box marketer mixed with a tech junkie.

For the past three years I’ve worked for a number of clients ranging from a local not-for-profit to the world’s largest silicon chip manufacturer, bringing creativity and a fresh look to each project. I used my education and experiences to create new and lasting messages for my clients that helped cement their places in their markets.

I’ve used my copywriting skills to produce web copy for numerous sites, garnering eyeballs for clients and accolades for the sites. Whether it’s static content, interactive copy or banner content, I treat every project as a new chance to tell a story.

I’m in my element in front of whiteboard working on brainstorming or sitting on a couch taking endless whacks at that perfect bit of a copy for a Website or ad.
