Jon Lund’s Summary

Jon Lund is director of Foreningen af Danske Interaktive Medier, FDIM (the IAB Denmark). As such Jon Lund is in charge of the national internet adspend and audience measurement studies, of arranging conferences and network opportunities, keeping the industry informed of trends in the Danish newmedia-landscape, and lobbying the political establishment through a close cooperation with both national and European industry bodies.

Jon Lund is member of the board of IAB Europe, member of the Danish Ministry of Cultures advertising forum, member of The forum of Danish Media, member of ICC Denmarks marketing committee.

Prior to this Jon has worked with both media corporations, Internet- and advertising agencies and in the software industry. This background has given Jon a very fine-grained perspective to all sides of new media development.

From July 1st 2009 Jon Lund starts up his own consultancy, analyzing, speaking and advising on how companies and organizations can take full advantage of the latest trends in new technology and new media.

Jon Lund’s Education

  • Aarhus Universitet

    Master , Political science , 1987 — 1995

    Average grade 3,9 (10,7 Danish grading system) at graduate level-courses (gradate level: 3 years studies at "overbygningsuddannelsen").

    Master thesis “With a view to the civil war: representative democracy and creation of conciliatoriness in high modernity” (168 pages) traces the development of representative democracy and the psychological makeup of the modern mindset from medieval Europe and addresses the question on how societies of today can cope with problems of un-conciliatoriness and disharmony.
    The thesis proposes the introduction of a parliamentary bi-chambre structure feautring an elected Folketing (first chamber), with the rights to initiate legislation and a virtual internetbased second chamber, consisting of 10.000 randomly chosen citizens, who in cycles of half a year are dedicated to the role of discussing - and deciding on – the proposals laid forth by the first chamber.
