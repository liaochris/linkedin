Norman Jentner’s Summary

“Systems psychological” scientist-practitioner engaged in research and consultation. Focused on clarifying and explicating human systems structures and processes associated with improved human “communication-(conflict)-creativity-(control)”ⓒ -- leading to improved organizational effectiveness, defined terms of the bottom-line, employee engagement, product quality, and organizational adaptability. Provide personal consultation to C-level organization leaders interested to increase organizational effectiveness via organization development efforts by specifically developing the human (individual and systems) side of their enterprise.

Norman Jentner’s Specialties:

Human resources development, management consultation, strategic planning, leadership coaching, employee involvement, team building, participation, conflict resolution, problem-solving, creativity, workplace culture, excellence, ESOPs, co-ops, workplace democracy, control, confidential consultation, change management, succession planning, communication.

Norman Jentner’s Experience

  • Consulting Systems Psychologist

    Satisfying SolutionsⓇ

    (Management Consulting industry)

    1990 — Present (19 years)

    A focus on “the people side” of the enterprise -- on “communication-(conflict)-creativity-(control)”ⓒ -- allows for identification of both strengths and gaps in human engagement (broadly defined) and control of essential processes within the enterprise. Individual and organization development efforts are designed particular to that setting to improve both the structures and processes of human engagement on behalf of that enterprise. Conscious as well as “unconscious” (or hidden) dynamics of “communication-(conflict)-creativity-(control)”ⓒ -- emotional and behavioral as well as intellectual -- are uncovered in a safe, affirming, and effective manner to effect individual and collective changes for improved adaptive functioning of the organization over time. This includes enhancement of employees’ engagement and in their satisfaction within the organization, in product quality and consistency, and in customers’ satisfaction and loyalty, and in the bottom-line.

  • Public Health Psychologist

    City of Akron

    (Government Administration industry)

    October 2001 — September 2009 (8 years)

    Advanced professional position engaged in planning, administration, consultation and supervision concerning human interactions to support individual and program performance within and across 30+ Divisions of the city municipality employing more than 3200 full-time employees at one point. Worked closely with Labor Relations, Law, Benefits, Training, Police, Health, Fire, and Service Departments -- and several unions -- to provide confidential consultation to department managers, supervisors, program managers, and non-management employees concerning issues of "communication, conflict, creativity and control" in myriad forms. Coordinator, trainer, consultant, and counselor for the City's in-house Employee Assistance Program. Clinical Director of the Counseling Division, Akron Health Department.

  • Consulting Psychologist for Program Development

    Family and Community Services of Portage County

    (Mental Health Care industry)

    1992 — 2001 (9 years)

    Human relations and psychological consultation provided to invigorate programming in a fledgling outpatient behavioral healthcare center. Program development efforts focused on exploiting a challenging community need (domestic violence). Successfully integrating conflicting community perspectives, providing in-house professional development, and empowering workplace excellence, facilitated cooperative engagement among multiple parties -- in-house and in the community -- turning a community problem into a business opportunity. Behavioral healthcare programming expanded six-fold from a $200,000 annual budget to a $1.2 million annual budget at this center, in large part as a function of the consultation provided by yours truly. The program has continued to expand (doubled) in the years since.

  • Organizational Psychologist Trainee

    Virginia Harvey & Associates

    (Professional Training & Coaching industry)

    June 1987 — August 1990 (3 years 3 months)

    Organizational psychology pre-doctoral traineeship focused on human systems problem identification, personnel training, and facilitation of communication, conflict resolution, team-building, and group problem-solving performance development.

  • Clinical Psychologist Trainee

    Coleman Professional Services

    (Non-Profit; Mental Health Care industry)

    1981 — 1989 (8 years)

    As an outpatient Clinical Psychologist trainee, in addition to outpatient behavioral health consultation for the full variety of concerns brought to the center, developed The Alternatives in Conflict Training (TACT) Group, designed to empower men and women to develop their understanding and skills for effective conflict resolution with others. The group, starting in 1985 and lasting beyond my exit from that organization, was a great success!
