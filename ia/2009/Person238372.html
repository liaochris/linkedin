Tristan Harris’s Summary

Internet and digital media entrepreneur with a genuine, sincere, and authentic passion for embedding empathy into communication.

I've thought up, designed, built and shipped products and new ways for people to communicate-- both at big companies and starting one of my own. What makes me get up every morning is getting to work with brilliant, passionate, self-aware and zealous people with that enthusiastic spark and impatient attitude for making the world better. Overall mission is to enhance the way people fundamentally understand each other and the rich depth to the world around them.

Tristan Harris’s Specialties:

Tristan Harris’s Experience

  • Co-Founder and CEO

    Apture

    (Internet industry)

    November 2006 — Present (2 years 3 months)

    Leading a team of brilliant people to enhance how people experience the world through the web. Together we're developing a richer, more intimate, and accessible way for people to connect to an entire universe of media and information.

    Most people understand the word 'multimedia' more closely connected to 1995-esque CD-ROMs and Encarta rich encyclopedia. Apture is like that, but leveraging all multimedia on the Internet, hyper-connected and one click away while viewing any web page. You get exactly what you want - or didn't know you wanted - surprising and delightful, presented in a simple yet elegant interface.

    So far we've signed up Reuters, Washington Post, BBC, Wall Street Journal, SFGate, and thousands of blogs. Things are really taking off. If you're interested, email me at tristanh AT apture DOT com and we'll talk.

  • Software Engineer

    Apple Computer

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; AAPL; Computer Hardware industry)

    June 2003 — October 2005 (2 years 5 months)

    • Summer 2003 Inkwell team: Developed innovative applications taking advantage of Apple's Inkwell handwriting & gesture recognition technology left over from the Newton PDA. I have one patent pending from my work this summer.

    • Summer 2004, Mac OS X Dashboard team. Co-developed four of the shipping widgets in Mac OS X Tiger: Flight Tracker, Unit Converter, Yellow Pages, and Translation widgets.

    • Summer 2005 I worked for Apple in Hamburg, Germany in their Emagic offices, Built prototype of "Spotlight for Help," an idea I conceived now shipping in Mac OS X Leopard. This major feature is showcased in Leopard's "Help" menu, which contains a search field to let users search an application's user interface for commands (e.g. "where is the 'Columns' menu item in Word?"). Users navigate the list of results and as they hilite each one, the the system expands menu hierarchy leading to the item. I have another patent pending on this work.
