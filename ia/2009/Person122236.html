J. Ray Rice’s Summary

J. Ray Rice received his ABS-71’ & MSW-77’ from George Williams College. Presently he is a doctoral candidate at Walden University in Human Services. His books can be purchased on Amazon.com. Thank You for Loving Me! The Psychology of Abandonment, Healing and Loving & What I Must Give Myself...First! He is available to speak, lecture, and train international.

He has been a member of the Academy of Certified Social Workers (ACSW) since 1979. He has worked, trained, & studied human interactions in 19-countries.

He was a YMCA Youth Worker in Chicago and worked in a maximum-security state correction facility for youth. He has served as a ER Social Worker.

He was listed in Who’s Who in the World, Who's Who of the Midwest, & Who's Who of Emerging Leaders.

He has been a therapist & director of residential treatment centers where he designed community-base programs for youths transiting from in-patient to home & school.

He was a delegate from IL to the White-House Conference on Children & the White-House Conference on Aging.

He served as the first youth coordinator at The Threshold in Chicago.

He was listed in Who’s Who in the World in 1987, Who's Who of the Midwest, & Who's Who of Emerging Leaders.

He has trained transatlantic on cruise-ships.
He has trained in high-schools, colleges, universities, law enforcement agencies, military bases & transatlantic cruise-ships crossing.

He was director for corporate training at Miami-Dade College & written grant for job development programs.

He has been an exchange social worker to Sweden studying their delivery of health care with the International Council of Fellowship.

He was an invited presenter to the 5th International Conference on Conflict Resolve in St. Petersburg, Russia.

Presently he is setting-up a think-tank to train-the-trainers.

J. Ray Rice’s Specialties:

Train-the-trainer, group-work/team-building, conflict resolution, treating the difficult to treat client, and grant writing.
