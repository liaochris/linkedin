Mark Rossman’s Summary

I concentrate my law practice in complex commercial and business litigation, construction litigation, business transactions, distressed loan work-outs and related litigation, and class actions. I have also represented several clients in high asset divorce and custody disputes. In the past 6 years, I have obtained over $9.5 million in settlements as lead attorney and chief negotiator, and over $650,000 in trial and arbitration victories.

In November 2007, I was featured in Lawyers Weekly as one of Michigan’s “Up and Coming Lawyers.” In May 2007, I co-authored an article that was published in the Michigan Bar Journal entitled Reinsurance Contracts and the Role of Fiduciary Duty. I co-authored another article on the role of fiduciary duty in the context of partnerships, which is due to be published in the Michigan Bar Journal in December 2008. I am a neutral case evaluator in Oakland County on the commercial panel. I have handled several high profile cases, which have appeared in the local media, both in publications and on television.

I graduated cum laude from Wayne State University Law School in 2001. While in law school, I earned the Silver Key Certificate for outstanding legal scholarship, winning the school trial competition, and being awarded the Donald E. Barris Award for excellence in trial advocacy. I previously graduated with distinction from the University of Michigan in 1998, earning a Bachelor of Arts degree in English.

Mark Rossman’s Specialties:

Commercial and business litigation; shareholder, breach of fiduciary duty, and fraud cases; business transactions, including distressed loan workouts; class action lawsuits; divorce and custody matters.
