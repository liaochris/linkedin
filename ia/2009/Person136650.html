William Morse’s Summary

I began working in private business in the early 70's, eventually owning my own small manufacturing company in Southern California. We sold the company in 1998 and I began a management consulting business concentrating in sales, marketing, and operational procedures.

In 2003 I met a young Cambodian man, an ex-child soldier, who lost his family in the infamous Killing Fields of the late 70's. He was clearing landmines by hand and had adopted over a dozen maimed and orphaned children, that he and his wife raised in his Landmine Museum in Siem Reap Cambodia. I returned home and began the Landmine Relief Fund to support his work.

In 2007 he was required to cease all his de-mining work and apply for a license from the government. I closed my business and spent 6 months in Cambodia helping him establish a new, all Cambodian, demining company. We have promised to help fund the work that his NGO, Cambodian Self Help Demining, undertakes.

The Landmine Relief Fund is dedicated to supporting the contiuing efforts of Cambodian Self Help Demining to clear landmines and UXOs from low priority villages throughout Cambodia.
