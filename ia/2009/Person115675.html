Joel David Palmer’s Summary

My professional goal is to produce clear, concise communications for a variety of media and subject matter by editing and writing for the web, for the printed word, and for the several-sized screens. Wherever I have worked the past 25 years, I've been the "go to" guy for proofing and revising correspondence and reports, and in recent years this has become a larger part of my professional interest and livelihood. I wrote the novel Fugue State: Saddam Dreams (still unsold - do you know a publisher?), created the website Ag & Livestock Waste Intelligence, and am now primarily a freelance editor and writer. I am the author of ten peer-reviewed technical journal articles and numerous other technical and newspaper articles.

My background is agricultural, hydraulic and environmental engineering. I have seven years' experience conducting research for the federal government in soil erosion and in irrigation water delivery. I have eight years' experience enforcing and permitting state environmental regulations for flood plain development and for livestock waste water pollution. I have five years' experience as an independent consulting engineer and water right examiner, and another five years' as a family and business mediator in private practice.

Joel David Palmer’s Specialties:

I have an excellent feel for language and words, and a genuine interest in helping writers connect with their readers. I am well-read and familiar with the vernacular of many technical and descriptive fields. I have particular expertise in animal waste management, water pollution control, negotiation of environmental compliance and remedial actions, technical assistance and outreach, policy research and analysis, conflict management and resolution, writing, editing and proofreading.
