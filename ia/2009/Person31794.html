Brad Sorensen’s Summary

Brad Sorensen has 10 years of experience in the entertainment and new media industries, having worked at various entertainment law firms as well as at media and new media companies in the capacities of business affairs, content licensing and business development. Sorensen is proficient in all aspects of content-oriented licensing and business development, for all types of content including music, television programming, feature films and videos, for any type of media or new media venture.

Brad Sorensen’s Specialties:

Content Strategy, Content Licensing, Form Contract Development, Acquisition Road Map (targeting and prioritizing), Rights Analysis, Deal Structure, Contract Negotiation / Drafting / Administration, Operations

  • Principal

    Digital Business Affairs (DBA)

    (Legal Services industry)

    July 2007 — Present (1 year 8 months)

    DBA prepares and/or negotiates, revises and finalizes licensing agreements between content providers and any new media platform (Internet or mobile).

    DBA fills a niche in the new media space. New media deals rarely have enough at stake to justify paying a full time senior attorney or, alternatively, spending $250-$400 per hour on outside counsel. However, leaving a business development associate who doesn't have the requisite contracting drafting and IP licensing experience alone to bring the deal from FORM TEMPLATE to EXECUTION COPY is a risky proposition.

    DBA bridges this gap between investing excessively on legal resources and putting too much responsibility on your business development associate.
