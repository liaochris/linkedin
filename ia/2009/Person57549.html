David Gewirtz’s Summary

"...One of America's foremost email experts" -- Independent Computer Consultants Association

"...Leading Presidential scholar specializing in White House email." -- CNN

Best known for non-partisan national security analysis of the Bush White House email controversy. Author of the book "Where Have All The Emails Gone? How Something as Seemingly Benign as White House Email Can Have Freaky National Security Consequences," which explores the controversy from a technical perspective and, according to The Intelligence Daily, is "the definitive account about the circumstances that led to the loss of administration emails." Makes six very doable policy recommendations that can quickly and easily bring safety and security back to White House email.

Cyberterrorism Advisor for the International Association for Counterterrorism & Security Professionals and a columnist for The Journal of Counterterrorism and Homeland Security. A member of FBI InfraGard, the security partnership between the FBI and industry. Also a member of the U.S. Naval Institute and the National Defense Industrial Association, the leading defense industry association promoting national security.

Regularly writes commentary and analysis for CNN's Anderson Cooper 360. Guest commentator for the Nieman Watchdog of the Nieman Foundation for Journalism at Harvard University, has been awarded the prestigious Sigma Xi Research Award in Engineering, and was a candidate for the 2008 Pulitzer Prize in Letters.

Author of four traditional books and nine digital books, and has written more than 700 articles about technology, competitiveness, and national security policy. Also the author of The Flexible Enterprise.
