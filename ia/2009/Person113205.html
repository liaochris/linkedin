Jim McKinstry’s Summary

Over the past 19 years, my career has progressed down different roads. Directly out of college, I started working at IBM as a Micro-code developer; a position I held for four years. From there I moved into an UNIX systems administration role where I quickly excelled and eventually ended up as a consultant and an “industry expert” for Sys Admin Magazine authoring their monthly Q&A column. I have also had many articles/white papers published in national/inter-national technical magazines. During my time as an UNIX administrator, I was exposed to many varying Datacenter (i.e. 24x7) environments and was responsible for all aspects of mission critical systems (project management, implementation, etc.). In 2000 I made the jump from a hands-on technologist to a Senior Sales Engineer role in the enterprise storage industry. My time at LSI was spent being the liaison between LSI and our major OEM+ partner. The past 1.5 years I have been a Senior Technical Marketing Manager in charge of Global Competitive Intelligence at Pillar Data Systems.
