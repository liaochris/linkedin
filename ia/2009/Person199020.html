Alan Rimm-Kaufman’s Summary

Hello!

In 2003, I founded a marketing services firm, The Rimm-Kaufman Group, focusing on paid search for online retailers. Thanks to our great clients, RKG has enjoyed robust growth, reaching the Inc500 in 2008. Our website is at www.rimmkaufman.com.

My professional interests are a mashup of direct marketing, PR, databases, internet, technology, algorithms, and analytics.

I speak at the marketing trade shows and write for the marketing trade mags.

I'm a Visiting Professor at UVA's Darden School of Business, where I teach marketing at the MBA and Exec Ed levels.

I blog at www.rkgblog.com.

Away from work, I spend my time with my wife and kids and enjoy doing projects.

Alan Rimm-Kaufman’s Experience

  • Founder and CEO

    Rimm-Kaufman Group

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Marketing and Advertising industry)

    2003 — Present (6 years)

    Online marketing services, including paid search & web usability.

    RKG ranked #315 on the 2008 Inc500 list of fastest growing privately held companies.

  • VP Marketing

    Crutchfield

    (Privately Held; 501-1000 employees; Consumer Electronics industry)

    1998 — 2003 (5 years)

    At Crutchfield, I was responsible for all direct marketing efforts, both the national catalog mailings and the national web advertising. My print team and I handled catalog circ, media buying, paper buying, retention, and name acquisition. My web team and I handled paid search, seo, portals, data feeds, and email marketing.

    During my five years at Crutchfield, company revenues grew 48%, reaching $192 million in 2002. Internet revenues grew over 450%, reaching $80 million in ‘02. During this period, Crutchfield earned “best of the web” commendations from Time Magazine, Internet Retailer, BizRate, Forbes, and US News & World Report.

  • AVP Marketing

    Signet Financial Services

    (Public Company; 5001-10,000 employees; Financial Services industry)

    1996 — 1998 (2 years)

    I joined Signet just after CapOne split off. It was a wild ride: I came in as a freshly minted PhD with little background in direct marketing or financial services, and so I learned a great deal in time there.

    We cleaned up a broker dealer unit from significant annual loss to modest profitability in a year. We trimmed costs and introduced new products, notably very successful high-yield FDIC checking account. I had responsibility for a Richmond-based team of marketing analysts and a Vermont-based creative team.

    When First Union bought Signet, I didn't want to join a larger bank, and so I headed on.
