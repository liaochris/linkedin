Igor Kokarev’s Summary

- Scholar and teacher: Soviet and American Film History, Film as Political tool - current course in the State University High School of Economy, Moscow. During the 1980's, he served as vice president of the Soviet-American Film Initiative. At the begining of 2000 he was an editor in chief of the film weekly trade paper "SK-News".
- Leading Russian specialist in community development. Range of knowledge, experience and scholarship covers community organizing practice and theory both in general and in post-Soviet Russia; the conditions that preceded and resulted in the current state of civil society in the Russian "fake" democracy; perceptions through numerous US travels of American civil society.
- Formerly headed the Citizens Foundation, a US AID, Ford Foundation and Mott Foundation-funded effort that trained scores of citizen participation organizers and activists to populate Russia's early 90's budding democracy.
- Speaks English fluently and is reasonably well-acquainted with Southern California and the United States from past travels here. Experienced scholar, published author - 8 books, liberal thinking internationalist, democracy advocate and an expert in Soviet film as a propaganda wihicle.
- He want to develop lecture opportunities and scholarly relationships to universities and colleges in the region. Needs help to widen his network of contacts, with whom he might speak. His expertise may be beneficial to the students and scholars at USC in a variety of arenas, including at the Annenberg School, but also across other disciplines.
- He”ll be with Community Partners through this August, return to Russia to teach until February at the University, and return to America after that. His plans on spending much of the remainder of his career in the US are connected with permanent residence status granted him and his family by the US government as a "person of exceptional merit".
