Kevin Slavin’s Summary

Co-founded area/code in 2005, after a decade+ in design, advertising, and branding; mostly technology brands, products, and services. Operating systems, cable services, telcos, wireless, defense, etc. Art director, creative director, and a few years as strategic planner.

area/code is focused on cross-media entertainment: games and other forms of entertainment that extend from the internet cloud to the real world and back. These are commissioned and licensed by various clients to generate revenue, promote brands, educate and entertain.

Clients include: Nokia, Disney Imagineering, A&E, Nike, the Discovery Channel, CBS, MTV, the History Channel, Qwest Wireless, and most every ad agency in town, working with their clients to develop award-winning "third channel" initiatives.

Our projects have received widespread attention, with features about them in BusinessWeek, the New York Times, the Wall Street Journal, AdWeek, Ad Age, Creativity, and many of the most influential blogs. Awards include the Clios, the One Show, the Webbies, and Creativity, including being included in this years "Creativity 50."

Together with Adam Greenfield, I've been teaching Urban Computing at NYU's Interactive Telecommunications Program since 2007, and speak frequently at prominent venues including: MoMA, O'Reilly, DLD, the Guardian, NBC, Cooper Union, and the Storefront for Art and Architecture.
