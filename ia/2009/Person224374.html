Steven King’s Summary

Current division director in the U.S. Department of Homeland Security’s Office of Infrastructure Protection with extensive security, emergency response, and program management experience.

20 years of experience as a Firefighter, Fire Officer, Fire Marshal, and EMT-Paramedic in both career and volunteer departments. Former sworn local law enforcement officer and investigator. Currently volunteer as a Fire Officer and EMT-Paramedic in a busy northern Virginia fire department.

Published author and frequent presenter at national and international conferences on issues related to infrastructure protection, terrorism, firefighting and emergency medical services (EMS), and emergency response planning and operations. Published in numerous fire/rescue and security related magazines.

Currently a doctoral (PhD) student with an MBA and an Master of Science in Biodefense. Board Certified in Security Management as a Certified Protection Professional (CPP), certified Project Management Professional (PMP) and numerous fire, technical rescue, EMS, and law enforcement related certifications.

Steven King’s Specialties:

Homeland Security, project management, fire/rescue, emergency medical service, explosives, training, education, disaster preparedness, anti-terrorism, critical infrastructure protection, intelligence, risk management, emergency preparedness, public speaking, program management, emergency management

Steven King’s Experience

  • Division Director, acting

    DHS Headquarters

    (Government Agency; Government Administration industry)

    July 2009 — Present (7 months)

    Currently the Acting Division Director for the Contingency Planning and Incident Management Division (CPIMD) in the Office of Infrastructure Protection at DHS headquarters. The division ensures that the Department's infrastructure protection mission is integrated into the nation's unified approach to domestic incident management. In response to potential threats or actual events affecting critical infrastructure, CPIMD's National Infrastructure Coordinating Center (NICC) shares timely, relevant, and accurate information with critical infrastructure private sector owners and operators, DHS senior leadership, other government agencies, and nongovernmental organizations.

  • Deputy Division Director

    US Department of Homeland Security

    (Public Safety industry)

    August 2008 — July 2009 (1 year )

    Deputy Director of the Infrastructure Information Collection Division (IICD), which leads the Department’s efforts to protect and provide standardized, relevant and customer-focused critical infrastructure data to homeland security partners at the Federal, State and local government and the private sector. Several IICD projects, programs, systems and mission areas implement the vision of providing DHS enterprise solutions for the collection, protection and sharing of infrastructure data.

  • Deputy Director

    Department of Homeland Security

    (Government Administration industry)

    November 2007 — August 2008 (10 months)

    Deputy Director of the U.S. Department of Homeland Security’s Sector-Specific Agency Executive Management Office (SSA EMO). This office is responsible for coordinating national security efforts for six of the 18 critical infrastructure sectors under Presidential directive HSPD-7, including the chemical, commercial facilities, critical manufacturing, dams, emergency services, and nuclear sectors. I was the principal author of the congressionally mandated National Strategy for Securing the Chemical Sector, the DHS Chemical Sector-Specific Plan for the National Infrastructure Protection Plan (NIPP) and the DHS Office of Infrastructure Protection’s Best Practices for Infrastructure Protection guidebook. Also served as COTR for multiple contracts.
