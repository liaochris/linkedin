John Boyden’s Experience

  • Co-founder & Chief Creative

    Themepark

    (Internet industry)

    January 2000 — Present (9 years 6 months)

    As a creative director, project manager, and experience designer, lead clients from inception to completion on a wide range of internet projects including ecommerce websites, iPhone apps, social networking sites, and team-collaboration software. Helped clients to clarify their objectives, then flowcharted and built clickable wireframes to test shared assumptions; designed all UI elements and worked with engineers to implement, test, launch, and iterate based on analytics.

    Past clients include Tesla Motors, Pandora Internet Radio, Scholastic's Klutz brand, Jerry Zucker's National Banana, Nozzle Rage, Sirius Satellite Radio....

  • Creative Director & Director of Design

    Pets.com

    (Internet industry)

    1998 — 2000 (2 years)

    Directed the creation of the user experience as a founding member this top-10 internet retailing startup with a $40 million run rate. Assembled and managed the designers, photographers, production artists, and HTML coders that made up the Pets.com Design Department. Identified opportunities for improvement, then generated flowcharts, wireframes, and sketches to communicate them. Worked cross-functionally with other departments to prioritize, define, create, and maintain site enhancements and promotions. Championed the goal of achieving a simple, anxiety-free, and intuitive experience through consumer testing and feedback. Reported directly to the company president.

  • Director of Design

    Reel.com

    (Internet industry)

    1998 — 1999 (1 year)

    Directed the complete front-end overhaul of this top-20 internet retailer's website that was subsequently sold to Hollywood Video for $100 million. Managed the company’s Design Department, including a team of designers, production artists, and coders. Established efficient processes and protocols for massive weekly site updates. Reported directly to the company president.

  • Creative Director, Game Designer & Content Producer

    Jellyvision

    (Internet industry)

    1994 — 1998 (4 years)

    As one of the creators of the You Don't Know Jack online and CD-ROM game series, managed the Jellyvision end of the creative process for the game franchise which sold over four million units worldwide. Assembled and trained our first team of 23 writers. Oversaw 13 separate product releases including the $1.5 million development project of the “Jack Netshow” — the free online gaming site that won two back-to-back Webby Awards for best internet gaming site. Created, flowcharted, and oversaw the implementation of new features. Provided writers, engineers, artists, voice talent, and musicians with direction. Responsible for legal, scheduling, resource allocation, and budgeting. Worked closely with marketing and ad-sales departments to define and execute traffic-generating online events with NBC, ESPN, and others.

  • Creative Director & Creative Producer, You Don't Know Jack Series

    Berkeley Systems

    (Internet industry)

    1994 — 1998 (4 years)

    Was an employee of Jellyvision, but worked closely with (and sometimes out of the offices of) Berkeley Systems, who coded, QA'd, animated, and marketed You Don't Know Jack.
