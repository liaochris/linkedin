Matthew Neco’s Summary

20+ year seasoned lawyer in Intellectual Property, New Media, Technology, Licensing, Entertainment, with Reorganization and Workout (Bankruptcy) Experience and the acquisition of assets from BK estates.

Involved in MGM, et al. v. Grokster, et al case that went to the United States Supreme Court in 2005. Seminal copyright, secondary liability for infringement, and technology case. I didn't argue the case, but managed the team and worked on the brief. (See, http://w2.eff.org/IP/P2P/MGM_v_Grokster/)

Executive Committee member for the Intellectual Property Section of the State Bar of California; Copyright Committee Vice-Chair; In-House Counsel Committee Chair. See www.IPsection.org

Professional Mediator, Los Angeles, CA (1995 to Present)
Certified, Pepperdine University School of Law's Strauss Institute for Dispute Resolution.
Court appointed member, mediation panel of United States Bankruptcy Court, Central District of California.

Attended Fiorello H. LaGuardia High School of Music & Art, New York, NY in visual arts

Seasoned professional, including in the areas of corporate and business law, intellectual property, licensing, cyberlaw, entertainment, music, film, TV, new media / new technology, digital distribution, and reorganization / workout / acquisition of distressed business entities, assets, and individuals (creditor & debtor); strategic, analytical, effective problem solver; personable, diplomatic, skilled communicator, negotiator, mediator, closer and documenter; projects confidence without being overbearing; big-picture as well as detail oriented; multi-tasker with prioritization skills; leader, team player and self directed; able to “partner” with business executives and teams; entrepreneurial; adaptable; technology oriented; respected by peers.
