Bryce Benjamin’s Summary

Mr. Benjamin has been a successful angel entrepreneur in Los Angeles since the early 1980’s when he learned the craft from David Goodman, third on the all-time genealogy chart of West Coast Venture Capitalists. Over his career, Bryce has alternated between stints as investor and CFO, COO or CEO in nearly a score of companies. From 1991 to 1997, he served as COO of Brandon International, a component manufacturer, where he was instrumental in expanding the firm’s annual revenues over 600% through a combination of organic growth and acquisition. He was an early investor in and board member for IntelliChoice, Inc. until it was sold to Primedia in 1997. Bryce served as Chairman of MyCarPage.com, an ASP of CRM tools for the automotive industry, from 1999 through early 2005 when the company was successfully recapitalized in a management buyout. Bryce was the Founding CEO and Chairman of Language Weaver, Inc., the world’s leading provider of statistical-based language translation software, and served in those capacities from January 2002 through April 2007. Bryce currently serves on the boards of two non-profit entities; Project:Possibility, an organization with the mission to develop and distribute open source software to people with disabilities; and VitalLink, whose goal is to provide realistic exposure to career options and career guidance to teenagers. He is also a member of the Executive Committee of the Los Angeles Chapter of the TechCoastAngels. Bryce is a graduate of the University of Wisconsin, Madison and received a Master in Business Administration with Distinction from the Harvard Business School.

Bryce Benjamin’s Experience

  • Principal

    Alta Ventures

    (Privately Held; Venture Capital & Private Equity industry)

    March 1997 — Present (12 years 6 months)

    Alta Ventures is a family private equity fund aimed at making investments in, typically, very early stage ventures located in California. Bryce is a very proactive investor in early stage companies and often provides executive management and leadership help, as well as board participation.

  • CEO

    Language Weaver

    (Privately Held; Information Technology and Services industry)

    January 2002 — April 2007 (5 years 4 months)

    Language Weaver is a spin-out of the University of Southern California's Information Sciences Institute and a developer of state-of-the-art human language translation software. Bryce provided the initial funding for Language Weaver, became the Founding CEO and led the company through dramatic early growth and other award winning achievements. Under his leadership, the Company was named a "Top 25 Rising Star" for North America in Deloitte's Fast 500 competition and a "Top 100 Innovator" by Red Herring magazine. He successfully closed two rounds of angel and venture capital investment and positioned the company so that it has become the recognized world leader in its field. In 2006, Bryce was named a finalist in the Ernst and Young Entrepreneur of the Year competition for Greater Los Angeles.

  • Chairman

    Interval, Inc. dba MyCarPage.com

    (Privately Held; Internet industry)

    May 1999 — March 2005 (5 years 11 months)

    MyCarPage.com provides on-line direct marketing technology and services to the retail automotive industry. Bryce helped the company raise capital and was instrumental in steering the company away from its early cash-burn mode of operation and toward a sustainable and profitable customer centric business model. He was also actively involved in the development of key customer relationships and partner negotiations. Bryce stepped down as Chairman when management successfully recapitalized the company and provided liquidity to the company's outside investors.

  • Founder and CEO

    Riva Alta International, LLC

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Sporting Goods industry)

    September 1997 — February 2002 (4 years 6 months)

    Riva Alta was created to take advantage of favorable tariff regulations under NAFTA and operated a contract sewing and assembly operation (maquiladora) in Mexicali, Mexico, serving principally the outdoor products industry.

  • Board Member

    Strategic Data Corporation

    (Privately Held; Online Media industry)

    2000 — 2001 (1 year)

    Started by a UCLA Professor, Strategic Data Corp was a developer and supplier of on-line ad targeting technology solutions. Bryce served on the Company's board during its early formative stages. SDC was acquired by Fox Interactive Media in 2007.

  • Board Member

    Vyou.com

    (Privately Held; Online Media industry)

    1999 — 2001 (2 years)

    Vyou.com developed technology solutions to protect on-line content from unauthorized copying. The company was unsuccessful in raising a needed round of funding in the dot-com bust year 2001 and its assets were sold to CCC.

  • GM and COO

    Brandon International

    (Privately Held; Business Supplies and Equipment industry)

    1991 — 1997 (6 years)

    Brandon International was a diversified manufacturer of precision die cut parts, specialty materials, clean room supplies and pressure sensitive labels in three facilities in Southern California and provided turn-key and contract manufacturing (maquiladora) services in three manufacturing plants in Mexico. During his tenure with the company, Bryce ran three of its divisions at different times, spearheaded one divestiture and two acquisitions and ultimately became the Chief Operating Officer with all operations reporting to him. Brandon International was acquired by Brady Corporation in 2003.

  • Board Member

    Intellichoice, Inc.

    (Public Company; Online Media industry)

    1988 — 1997 (9 years)

    IntelliChoice develops and publishes automobile cost of ownership information through a variety of mediums. It has been frequently cited by car manufacturers as an authority in advertisements when one of their vehicles gets a "Best Value" designation. Bryce helped raise early investment capital for the company and was an engaged board member throughout its independent life before being acquired by Primedia in 1997.
