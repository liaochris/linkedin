Visiting Los Angeles, Nelson toured the Pathe studios with relatives and met Gloria Swanson. His enthusiasm for the new International style modern design persuaded her that he should be the art director of her next film, What a Widow! (1930). He spent nine months designing sets for that film before returning to architecture. During his fifty-year career, Nelson designed several successful modern hospitals. For added chic, publicity for What a Widow! labeled the designer "Paul Nelson, Paris" returned to France - Marseilles after completing the film. He designed the Suspended House and hospitals.
