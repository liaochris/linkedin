Scott Hunter’s Experience

  • Sr. Manager, 1st Party Relations and OEM

    Logitech Inc.

    (Public Company; LOGI; Computer Hardware industry)

    October 2001 — January 2009 (7 years 4 months)

    As the Sr. Manager of 1st Party Relations in Gaming, I was instrumental in growing the company’s brand profile beyond PC gaming and into consoles. Working with Sony, Microsoft, Nintendo, NVIDIA, Intel, Dell, AMD and other manufacturers of game hardware – I negotiated branding and software licenses which helped grow company revenues far beyond stated goals.

    I also worked closely with game developers and publishers to gain support for the company’s gaming products and executed promotional opportunities to raise awareness of the company’s products and brand alongside partner products at trade shows, public relations events and in consumer media.

    As a member of the strategic development team, I investigated potential products and technologies that broadened the company’s product portfolio – while also managing product marketing activities for consumer OEM products from customer concept through product development, manufacturing and sales, with revenue of more than $100 million.

  • Member Representative

    PCGA

    (Computer Games industry)

    March 2008 — January 2009 (11 months)

    Represented Logitech's interests within the Personal Computer Gaming Alliance.

  • Director of Licensing

    VM Labs

    (Computer Games industry)

    May 1998 — September 2001 (3 years 5 months)

    As the Senior Account Manager for Third Party Development, I created the Third Party developer / publisher relations program while evangelizing the advanced technology and mass-market potential of interactive-DVD gaming.

    As the Director of Licensing, I identified, initiated and negotiated independent IP licenses with software developers and publishers to bring content to the NUON platform.

  • Account Manager

    Sony Computer Entertainment America

    (Public Company; SNE; Computer Games industry)

    May 1995 — May 1998 (3 years 1 month)

    At SCEA, I worked to create and maintain positive relationships with Third Party game developers and publishers, while managing their software through the company’s process -- including concept approval, technical support, quality evaluation, packaging and manufacturing.

    I was also instrumental in preparing the Third Party quality assurance department for the PlayStation launch in September 1995. While there I evaluated for publication the concept, graphics, sound and playability of Third Party software.

  • QA Department Manager

    Atari

    (Public Company; ATAR; Computer Games industry)

    January 1994 — April 1995 (1 year 4 months)

    Managed daily activities of an eight-person test department, while performing alpha, beta and game play testing on titles for console and hand-held game hardware.

  • Staff Writer

    The Sacramento Union

    (Computer Games industry)

    1989 — 1993 (4 years)

    Authored weekly columns covering video games and television/radio and wrote entertainment articles ranging from film, music and book reviews to celebrity interviews.
