Eric Robichaud’s Summary

Seasoned leader with broad experience in general business management, as well as technical software development.

Career began as a highly technical software engineer. Designed and coded the centralized credit card, health care, and check-processing ("Online Transaction Processing") system for CVS Pharmacy. Has programmed in 9 languages, including systems programming in assembler and C.

Founded RI Soft Systems (later renamed Mediaweave) and guided it to becoming the most-recognized name in screensaver development, producing several of the all-time most popular screensavers for Microsoft Windows.

Launched Screensaver.com and built it to become the most popular, highest-traffic screensaver web site on the internet. Sold in 2003 for approx. $1 million total contract.

Has managed all aspects of building a business from 0 to 25 people and maintaining it over the long term (18 years), including marketing programs, sales initiatives, employee management, HR issues, finances, acquisitions, contract negotiations and more.

Strongest competency is still in leading development teams, managing software projects, and troubleshooting technology issues.

18 years of experience of running a business from the inside along with providing consulting services to external clients has built a clear, crisp understanding of business requirements and operating principles. This gives the ability to design solutions that are intelligent, with considerable forethought to business issues (ie "seeing the forest for the trees.")

Eric Robichaud’s Experience

  • Mediaweave / RI Soft Systems

    (Computer Software industry)

    July 1988 — December 2005 (17 years 6 months)

    President and CEO. Took company from start-up with zero employees and built it to multi-million dollar operation with 25 employees, over the course of 18 years. Executive-level guidance of technology, sales and marketing teams. Negotiated all contracts and licensing deals. Negotiated various acquisitions of smaller businesses (6-figure deals). Negotiated numerous 6- and 7-figure sales transactions.

    The company was initially formed as RI Soft Systems in 1988 and was a leader in try-before-you-buy software. Robichaud spent 6 years from 1992 - 1997 as Chairman of the Board of industry trade groups (ASP and SIC), and was inducted into the Shareware Hall of Fame.

    In 1997 Robichaud founded and built Screensaver.com until it was sold to Freeze.com in 2003 for approx. $1 million.

    The company was renamed to Mediaweave, Inc. circa 2003 as the company focused on corporate projects, providing a blend of marketing, design and technology solutions.

  • CVS / Pharmacy

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; CVS; Computer Software industry)

    May 1988 — June 1993 (5 years 2 months)

    Began as a college intern, and worked my way up very quickly, with one or two promotions each year. Became full-time in 1990.

    Designed and programmed CVS' centralized transaction processing system for credit cards, healthcare adjudication and check processing.

    Initially management assumed they needed 3 separate systems, but I envisioned a single, generic "online transaction processing system" (OLTP) that could intelligently route & manages all three types of transactions through one single system. This involved concocting a new script language, and building recognition, routing/switching and database manipulation engines which all worked together to create a three-tiered network application.

    Over 15 years later, this same system is still the heartbeat of the centralized processing system at CVS, but processing 15x the initial volume. (The system was built for scalability!)

    Left in 1993 on good terms, because my own business needed full-time attention by that time.
