Michael Pakenham’s Summary

My name is Michael Pakenham. I am currently an EFL Lecturer, French as a foreign language Lecturer, and Head of Modern Languages in Caen University Banking and Finance School, France. I am also a qualified teacher of French and Spanish, and hold registration with the General Teaching Council of Northern Ireland.


I was born in Belfast, but have been living in France since 1987. I graduated from QUB in French and Spanish in 1985, and completed my PGCE in 1986. I lived in Spain for two years and speak the language fluently.

I hold a degree in English from Caen University, France, and obtained the CAPES in English (French PGCE)

I currently work at Caen University's Banking and Finance School, where I am an English Lecturer and French as a foreign language lecturer, Head of our Languages Department-English, Spanish, German, and French as a foreign language-and International Coordinator for Erasmus programmes with Universities in the UK-Paisley Business School, Manchester Metropolitan University-Germany -Frankfurt Bankakademie-and Spain-Universidad San Pablo CEU, Madrid. I set up these exchanges and the language department in 1994, recruiting full time lecturers for our English, Spanish, and German departments. We have a permanent staff of five lecturers. One of my main tasks is to ensure that our language teaching is in adequation with the syllabus and the language level required for study in our partner universities. I also decided that our students' language skills needed external accreditation and introduced compulsory preparation for TOEIC. I also prepare my students for Cambridge ESOL.

My main specialities are


Teaching Cambridge ESOL

EAP

English in the Workplace

Financial and Banking English

Translation Theory

Curriculum development

Preparation of foreign students for French as a foreign language exams DELF and DALF

Head of Language Unit EFL, French as a foreign language, Spanish, German
