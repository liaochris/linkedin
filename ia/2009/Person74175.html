Eric Earling’s Summary

An innovative communicator with a penchant for turning complex issues into clear, persuadable language. Background includes a diverse array of work in government, communications, and advocacy. Skilled in working with broad range of professionals, ranging from government officials, interest groups, the media, and business executives. Combines a passion for success with a zeal for having fun along the way.

Eric Earling’s Specialties:

Public affairs, policy analysis, media management, government relations, and internet communications.

Eric Earling’s Experience

  • Senior Communications Manager - Media Relations

    Premera Blue Cross

    (Non-Profit; Insurance industry)

    March 2009 — Present (1 month)

  • Contributing Editor

    SoundPolitics.com

    (Online Media industry)

    July 2006 — March 2009 (2 years 9 months)

    Leading contributor during the 2008 Election cycle to the most-read conservative blog in Washington state. Coverage included a broad range of topics, from Presidential politics to local issues. Ongoing unique content ranges from traditional blogging to op-ed column/essay length analysis.

  • Deputy Secretary's Regional Representative

    U.S. Department of Education

    (Government Agency; Government Administration industry)

    January 2003 — January 2009 (6 years 1 month)

    Served as a proactive and innovative spokesperson for the Administration’s education policies. Was at the vanguard of Regional Representatives working directly with the media through reporters and op-ed pages to advance the cause of No Child Left Behind at a time of significant White House interest in such activity. Additionally managed contacts with all levels of state and local government in Idaho, as well as related constituent groups, to expand knowledge of federal education policy in an otherwise challenging environment.

  • Vice Chairman

    Snohomish County Charter Review Commission

    (Government Administration industry)

    January 2006 — December 2006 (1 year)

    Elected as one of three commissioners per council district for a one-year term; was later selected as Co-Vice Chairman. Took an active role in guiding and at times chairing Commission meetings. Successfully advanced six proposed charter amendments to the ballot, five of which were approved by voters.

  • Director

    Dave Earling for Snohomish County Executive

    (Political Organization industry)

    July 2002 — November 2003 (1 year 5 months)

    Served in a key leadership role helping to guide this campaign, for a jurisdiction approximately the size of a Congressional district. Counsel included all levels of campaign activity, ranging from strategy to on the ground tactics.

  • Director of Public & Government Affairs

    Washington Biotechnology & Biomedical Association

    (Biotechnology industry)

    January 2001 — December 2002 (2 years)

    Managed government relations at the federal, state, and local levels. Organized grassroots interaction of WBBA executive members with government officials and other opinion leaders. Represented WBBA at various public and industry forums as well as additional public policy related speaking engagements.

  • Field Director & Outreach Coordinator

    U.S. Senator Slade Gorton

    (Legislative Office industry)

    July 1997 — December 2000 (3 years 6 months)

    Field Director for Senator's re-election campaign during much of 2000. Served in various positions of increasing responsibility on official Senate staff prior to campaign position, ending with Outreach Coordinator.
