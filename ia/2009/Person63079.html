Bob Diamond R.Ph’s Summary

I was the President of the Western Michigan Pharmacists Association, the Political Action Chair of that organization, and the District Chairman of the Political Action Committee of the Michigan Pharmacists Association.

I have been the spokesman for pharmacists in contract dealings with large area businesses, insurance companies, local government; and, have negotiated successfully with an HMO to provide prescription services from pharmacies in our area.

During that period I was elected President of Pharmco, Inc., a buying group that represented about fifty drug stores—independent pharmacies and small chains—located in the Western Michigan area. Total annual sales that went through Pharmco’s books were over 50 Million Dollars.

For seventeen years prior to coming to North Carolina I owned and managed South Shore Pharmacy in Holland, Michigan. The two former owners were unsuccessful at that location. I was able to make the business profitable during the first year. It remained profitable until I sold it to Walgreens before moving here.

I have been to Haiti on medical missions several times, am active on the Finance Committee at church; also, closely associated with with the Spanish outreach program. I work with people who need help budgeting.

I am a member of American MENSA, an organization for gifted people.

Bob Diamond R.Ph’s Specialties:

I am a consultant pharmacist for Medication Therapy Management (MTM) for the State of North Carolina through its Pharmacy Outcomes Program.

I have been a community pharmacist for over thirty years.

I recently completed six months of Clinical Pharmacy training at Carolinas Healthcare System at Mercy Hospital in Charlotte.
