Mark Lupo, CBCP’s Summary

I served in the U.S. Army in the 1980's as a medic on a Special Forces 'A' Detachment, with work in the Middle East and Europe, language specialty of Turkish. In 1989 I accepted a commission in the U.S. Army, subsequently serving in the Middle East during Operation Desert Shield/ Desert Storm. Following graduation from Auburn University, I worked in the hospital setting, from the late 1980's until the early 1990's, mostly within the surgical arena. Following my return from the Middle East, I moved into the home health setting. For the next 15 years, I served in clinical and administrative roles within home health care, assisting patients and directing operations with care needed within the home setting.

Since the mid-90's I have been working on developing small businesses, from a direct marketing company, to two art galleries (1999 through 2004), a real estate investment company (ongoing) and an internet domain company (ongoing).

I currently serve as a consultant with the University of Georgia Small Business Development Center, in the area of business contingency planning and working with local law enforcement and The Department of Homeland Security. My primary goal is to assist in the development of emergency and contingency planning processes for individuals, businesses, and communities through one-on-one consulting and through seminar development and presentations.

I also have a private consulting business that focuses on interests outside of Georgia.
