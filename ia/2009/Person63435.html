Dick Raman’s Summary

Dick Raman is currently acting as the Chief Software Architect for TIE. He will be concentrating on the further development of TIE Kinetix. Until early 2008, he CEO of TIE Commerce, Inc., a Burlington, Mass.-based eCommerce software firm where he was responsible for driving company vision, growth and profitability. In 1987 he founded TIE Holding NV. In 2000 he took TIE public as the first Internet Company in the Netherlands. He moved to the Boston area in 2005 to expand TIE into the US market place.

Mr. Raman has gained worldwide recognition as a leading authority in the field of eCommerce. He is also an author, having published a number of successful books. He is a recognized visionary and frequent keynote speaker at electronic commerce events worldwide.

After a brief teaching career, Mr. Raman got his start at IBM. In 1987, he left IBM to start his own electronic trading software company, laying down the blueprint for what would later become TIE Holding NV.

In addition to managing TIE Commerce, Mr. Raman has played a key role in standardization of global eCommerce through various private and United Nations-sponsored eCommerce standards organization. For many years, he has served as Head of Delegation of EEMA for UN/CEFACT. Mr. Raman also served as Chair of the UN/CEFACT Forum Management Group, providing leadership for global B2B integration standardization from 2004-2006. In 2002, Mr. Raman was elected Chairman of eBIF, the European Interoperability Forum, set up under the auspices of CEN/ISSS and the European Commission. In 1999, was appointed Team Leader of the Quality Review Team for the Steering Group of ebXML. Later that year, Mr. Raman was appointed Chairman of the CEN/ISSS XML/EDI Workshop, which merged with EBES to form the European B2B eCommerce Standardization body eBES.

Drs. Dick Raman, is a graduate of Free University of Amsterdam, having received a degree in Business Economics.
