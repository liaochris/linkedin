Jorge Newbery’s Summary

AHP PROCESS:
1. Seller agents will list homes of families interested in participating in AHP’s Program. Next, agent will assist in writing offers from AHP to purchase homes at sub-REO value, as well as gather all short sale documentation required by existing lender.
2. AHP will submit offers and negotiate with lenders. To get the steepest discounts, this may be a slow process, sometimes taking 3 to 6 months. Once approved by lenders, AHP will offer the short sale investment opportunities to investors.
3. Buyer agents represent investors seeking high returns by purchasing homes at steep discounts. AHP will provide investor with short sales approved by lenders, along with home inspection reports and background information on the family. Investors must agree to five-year leases and recorded options to keep the families in their homes.
4. Agents offer properties to investors, and write offer if they are licensed in the state where the property is located.

EXPERIENCE:
Former principal in mortgage, property management and real estate brokerage firms.

2009, transitioned AHP Inc. to for-profit American Homeowner Preservation, LLC.

2007, started American Homeowner Preservation Inc. nonprofit organization.

2004, Woodland Meadows destroyed by ice storm which tested resolve in a high-profile battle with insurers, city of Columbus, creditors and contractors.

2002, led $12,500,000 acquisition of 1,100-unit Woodland Meadows Apartments. Columbus, OH. http://solvewoodlandmeadows.com/

2000, brokered over 600 REO properties.

1998, led the turnaround of 298-unit Ford Hotel on Los Angeles’ Skid Row, resulting in release of property from Los Angeles Slum Housing Task Force: three prior owners had all been jailed due to inability to cure the property’s problems.

    AHP initially anticipated funding from tax exempt bonds issued by the Summit County Port Authority. In September 2008, SCPA voted unanimously to induce up to 12.5 million in bonds to fund AHP.
