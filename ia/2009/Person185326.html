  • Online Marketing Manager

    Choicepoint

    (Public Company; CPS; Internet industry)

    January 2003 — October 2004 (1 year 10 months)

    I was originally hired at ChoicePoint to manage the implementation and delivery of advertising accounts on partners like Switchboard.com, WhitePages.com, and AnyWho.com. At the time, our two business units - KnowX.com and ChoiceTrust.com - were failing to properly leverage Paid Search as a marketing vehicle, so I proactively wrote a business case to encourage the allocation of more advertising dollars into this arena. After I received approval, I single handedly designed, researched, and implemented all keyword accounts, actively managed them on a daily basis, and performed daily tracking analysis to determine a positive ROI across all partners (which were Google Adwords, Overture, and Findwhat, Kanoodle, Enhance, and Search123 at the time). When I began, PPC revenue represented roughly .03% of total revenue, and grew to represent nearly 40% of all revenue under my management. As such, I’m pretty darn proud of what I was able to accomplish here : )

  • Online Traffic Analyst

    AutoTrader.com

    (Internet industry)

    March 2002 — January 2003 (11 months)

    After the dot com bomb, AutoTrader.com was kind enough to hire me as an Advertising Analyst within their new Advertising Traffic department. My duties revolved around compiling data mining inventory reports, ensuring the proper delivery of existing internal advertising campaigns, and generating ad hoc reports for senior leadership. I was also responsible for conducting pre- and post-campaign analysis which contributed to future marketing activity. This position enabled me to develop a deeper appreciation for hard-nosed mathematical analysis, and how business goals can be met by strictly managing trends and ROI.

  • Advertising Product Manager

    eTour.com (acquired by Ask Jeeves)

    (Internet industry)

    August 1999 — February 2001 (1 year 7 months)

    My first job on the web, and it proved to be a watershed business experience for me. eTour was essentially a search engine which presented results in a slide-show manner (similar to todays StumbleUpon). I was initially hired as an Advertising Traffic Coordinator to oversee all aspects of our internal advertising campaign operations. As I grew comfortable with my position, I ended up assuming all Product Manager responsibilities for our department, and developed an in-house application to dynamically insert new advertisers into our ad rotation without having to hand code SQL. From this position, I was promoted into the role of Advertising Product Manager, responsible for authoring all ad product launch plans and functional specifications. eTour soon ran out of funding, though. I was fortunate to make it through 2 rounds of layoffs, but the third proved to be my, the businesses, undoing. eTour wound up being sold to and absorbed by Ask Jeeves.
