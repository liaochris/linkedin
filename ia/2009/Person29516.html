Blair Kidwell’s Summary

Goals:
Conducting research at a major research university.
Top tier publications in my field and related fields such as management and psychology.

Experience:
I suppose my professional experience, besides working at Micron Technology and managing a polygraph lab, was spending four years active duty in the USMC prior to college. My experiences involved leading a squad of 13 Marines in every aspect of their lives from personal issues to training them how to survive in combat. There is more to know than you could imagine.

I also gained experience during Gulf War I guarding "special" weapons aboard an Aircraft Carrier (USS Midway) in the Persian Gulf.

I gained further experience (and confidence) by doing static line jumps during Airborne training at Fort Benning GA. I earned my "wings" by completing 5 jumps including 2 combat jumps and 2 night jumps.

My last year in the Corps I was a marksmanship instructor which involved training young Marines how to pinpoint a round 500 meters downrange with an M16A2 rifle. Not an easy task.

I have always believed that these experiences have made up for any deficiencies in business experience.

Blair Kidwell’s Specialties:

Consumer decision making, particularly focusing on emotion and emotional intelligence; Perceptions of control on consumer judgment and choice; The role of emotion in the service encounter; Emotional influence strategies in personal selling; Cognitive and emotional knowledge calibration.


Teaching:

Internet Marketing, Consumer Behavior, Advertising, and IMC
