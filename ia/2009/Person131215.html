Kevin Norberg’s Experience

  • founder and creative director

    The Tevye Project

    (Non-Profit; Entertainment industry)

    2009 — Present (less than a year)

    The Tevye Project is an arts and entertainment company, bringing to the stage Kevin Norberg’s FIDDLER IN AMERICA, the musical sequel to Fiddler On The Roof.

    Inspired by the stories of Sholem Aleichem, Norberg is writing the book, music and lyrics, and will debut the work as a one-man show: TEVYE! (2009), a novel and audiobook: TEVYE & THE STREETS OF GOLD, and a full stage musical: FIDDLER IN AMERICA.

    Over a century ago the Jewish writer Sholem Aleichem introduced Tevye to the world. The Broadway musical FIDDLER ON THE ROOF immortalized him. And now, told with the same wit and charm that has endeared the humble dairyman to the hearts of millions, an all-new sequel by Kevin Norberg assures us that the Tradition continues... this time in America!

    This is the story of Tevye, a poor dairy pushcart peddler, and his family who struggle as immigrants on "the Streets of Gold" in New York’s Lower East Side. Set against the backdrop of Ellis Island in 1911, Tevye’s daughters want only to escape the harsh conditions of the tenements and sweatshops, while Tevye makes a small fortune, and then blows it. Tevye is funny, warm, learned, earthy, and hopeful – and his story is every immigrant’s dream of finding acceptance in a strange land and making a better life for his children.

  • Performer/Producer-Director Theatrical Productions

    North Heights Lutheran Church

    (Non-Profit; 201-500 employees; Religious Institutions industry)

    1988 — 2007 (19 years)

    Kevin Norberg has written, produced, directed and played leading roles in some of the nation’s top stage productions. Attendance reached over 75,000 annually, with more than $1 million in yearly ticket sales. During his stage and concert career, audiences of more than one million have attended Kevin's live performances.

    In the North Heights PASSION PLAY, which began production in 1988 and closed in 2007, Norberg originated the leading role of the Apostle Peter, which he continued for 19 years. He also produced and directed the FREEDOM CELEBRATION, SCROOGE!, and the PASSION PLAY in their final season of 2006-2007.

    During Norberg's tenure as performer, annual ticket sales and attendance increased from 4,000 in 1988 to more than 35,000 in 2007, including more than 250 charter buses and tour groups. Ticket revenues increased from $10,000 to more than $400,000.00/year.

    As Producer/Director, Norberg produced THE FREEDOM CELEBRATION, THE GOSPEL ACCORDING TO SCROOGE, PASSION PLAY.
