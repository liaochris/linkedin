Marilyn Walker’s Summary

My current focus is on helping parents of gifted, talented, and divergent-thinking kids find toys that will stimulate and develop their unusual interests. Before I had PlayFair Toys, I was a biology professor and researcher focusing on arctic and alpine ecosystems and environmental change. I maintain a keen interest in ecosystem science, botany, and the natural world, and keep up with what is happening in those fields. I am also very interested in local economies and the slow money movement, chaos and self-replicating systems, the 2nd law of thermodynamics, and the linkages between economics and ecology.

Marilyn Walker’s Experience

  • CEO

    PlayFair Toys

    (Retail industry)

    January 2004 — Present (5 years 6 months)

    PlayFair Toys is home of the Magical Educational Fun system of play, where we partner with parents and grandparents to find playthings that help their children thrive in our rapidly-evolving world. The four pillars of Magical Educational Fun - Creativity, Consciousness, Invention, and Exploration - are the basis of everything that happens at PlayFair and everything that we offer to our parent partners as we help them find toys, games, books, and more for their exceptional children.

  • Team Leader - Boreal Ecology Coop Research Unit

    USFS

    (Government Agency; Government Administration industry)

    June 1999 — June 2004 (5 years 1 month)

    I was the formative leader of this cooperative research unit between the USFS Pacific NW Research Station and the University of Alaska Fairbanks. My primary duty in this position was to co-direct the Bonanza Creek Long-Term Ecological Research program funded through the National Science Foundation and the USDA FS. As one of the two lead scientists on the project, I was responsible for integrating and presenting our findings and proposals to the funding agencies as well as the scientific community.
