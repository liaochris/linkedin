Greg Fisher’s Summary

I remember building my first website in 1996. The simple website (dedicated to my favorite bands at the time), was completely powered by HTML code, a little bit of Javascript (or ECMA-262), and one PHP mySQL web database. There was no shortage of animated ".gifs of fire" and other flashing graphics. I built the entire site with just a few Lycos searches and a simple Windows notepad. Marketing the website was as simple as passing a few links to friends. If the hit counter on my page turned every once in awhile, I was a happy guy.

Things were simple back then... Wow, how things have changed!

Today when we can't find something, we 'Google' it. Most of us don't use America Online any more. Dial-up has just about kicked the bucket with just under 9% of US users still keeping it alive (Source: Neilsen-Online). We've traded kbps for mbps and get a little 'torqued' if a page takes more than two seconds to load. Online marketers can do so much more than target by site now. Behavioral targeting allows advertisers to follow us from website to website. Social media sites like Facebook and YouTube, are amongst the most visited sites across the globe.

Since my internet marketing career began, I've seen CPM rates increase exponentially. Paid $3.50 a click on the same keyword I purchased three years ago for $.67 a click. Sites I've optimized for Google hit the top spot on Monday, and fall to the fifth spot on Wednesday- only to find them back at position one the following week. I've sent over 20 million emails and don't remember the last time I bought stamps. Web analytics is so much more than 'hits' to me now, and involves conversion rates, path analyses, bounce rates and much more. Tomorrow when I get a cup of coffee at Dunkin Donuts, I can check on my email marketing campaign in mid sip. I've never used mobile marketing, but it's been the next big thing according to OMMA magazine for past four years.
