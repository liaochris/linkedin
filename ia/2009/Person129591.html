Kelly Winkler’s Summary

Overview

Corporate publishing business development manager with experience in developing and growing magazine ancillary business profit centers including editorial conferences and events, client-sponsored custom events, licensing and foreign editions, syndication and content re-purposing, reprints, and custom publishing.

Corporate publishing executive with proven track record for growing Inc. magazine’s conference business by 1,500% from the 25th Annual event (Jan 07) to the 26th Annual event (Sep 07). Executive Producer of the annual Inc. 500/5000 event from Nov 02 to Oct 08 responsible for curriculum, logistics, sponsorship sales, client relations, and marketing.

Experienced Managing Editor: Inc. magazine (2002-2006); HomeStyle magazine (2001-2002). Managed all editorial processes and systems from idea conception to printed magazine including lineups, freelancer contracts, scheduling, manufacturing coordination, and M&A (manuscript and art) budget.

Experience negotiator. Contracted the first two foreign licensing deals for Inc. magazine and directed training sessions for the foreign publisher partners on editorial, sales, digital, consumer marketing, events, and ancillary business best-practices in preparation for the foreign edition launches. Contracted first book licensing deal for Inc. under new ownership.

Manager with proven track record for process improvement and project management. Developed vendor savings program for G+J USA yielding annual savings of nearly $700k.

Guest lecturer for the NYU Masters of Science in Publishing program focusing on editorial business and process, managing editor function, and editorial budgeting and forecasting.
