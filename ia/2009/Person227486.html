Suzanne Khan’s Experience

  • Program Manager, Internet Business Solutions Group

    Cisco through Artizen

    (Public Company; CSCO; Computer Networking industry)

    October 2007 — January 2009 (1 year 4 months)

    As an Innovations Program Manager, I assembled multidisciplinary teams and led projects to help Cisco’s top customers demonstrate thought leadership via advanced technology demos, prototypes and pilots.

    Successes include the development of a first-ever mobile video conferencing prototype with multiple commercial applications (patent pending), and an Internet-and-touch-screen-enabled hybrid bus to convert auto drivers into bus riders thereby reducing greenhouse gas emissions. The "Connected Bus" was created as part of the Clinton Global Initiative and a public-private partnership between Cisco and the City and County of San Francisco. It created interest worldwide from public and private sector customers, and was covered by the Discovery Channel and Fox Business News among others.

    In parallel with advanced technology development work, my team authored and published work templates and a scalable SDLC-based project management process for developing demos, prototypes and pilots.

  • Field Enablement Program Manager

    Cisco through Artizen

    (Public Company; CSCO; Computer Networking industry)

    March 2006 — October 2007 (1 year 8 months)

    I joined the Services Sales Enablement team with an initial charter of covering Service Sales in the United States, Canada and Latin America. Shortly into the engagement, my scope increased to globalize program material and roll it out to those organizations and to Sales in Europe, and Emerging Markets and Channel Partners worldwide.

    My team's goal was to improve understanding of how to position and sell Cisco Technical Services. To do this, we created an engaging online learning experience, and developed communications strategies and plans in partnership with executives in theater. We measured uptake, value, and adoption, and reported participation and passage rates while executives cascaded messaging to reinforce the importance of understanding key concepts. Field interviews, online evaluations, and instant feedback mechanisms were created to ensure the ongoing relevance and quality of material.

  • Communications Program Manager

    Cisco through Artizen

    (Public Company; CSCO; Computer Networking industry)

    August 2003 — February 2006 (2 years 7 months)

    As a Communications lead of a $350 million initiative, I was responsible for program and executive communications to build and sustain support for migrating the business to Oracle 11i through releases impacting more than 30,000 internal and external stakeholders. Primary audiences included the executive steering committee (CxO level participants), and 3000+ program team members around the world.

    From developing communications strategies, plans and strategic messaging to producing presentations, case studies, articles, and a winning nomination for the Teamwork Across Cisco award, serving as a member of this program management office drew on my abilities to work well with, and influence members of a matrixed team.

    Following several highly successful Oracle 11i releases, I collaborated with my PMO peers and leadership to develop an enterprise center of excellence and brand it as the Release Management Organization.

  • Senior Program Manager, Cadence Interactive

    Cadence Design Systems

    (Public Company; CDNS; Computer Software industry)

    April 2000 — February 2002 (1 year 11 months)

    As a charter member of a management team leading a $15 million initiative to improve the customer experience on the company website, I was responsible for client moves and migrations from a static to a dynamic corporate website. Building on relationships I had established while working on earlier releases of Cadence.com, I developed a Global Ambassador Network to understand requirements for, and drive organization adoption of new web-based applications. In addition, I co-authored a corporate web roadmap to clearly communicate plans for functional improvements, process optimization and system consolidation.

    In addition, while on a rotational assignment for the Chief Technology Office, I managed the successful evaluation and enterprise implementation of real-time web collaboration technology for highly secure collaboration on chip designs.

  • Senior Marketing Program Manager

    Cadence Design Systems

    (Public Company; CDNS; Computer Software industry)

    July 1998 — April 2000 (1 year 10 months)

    In my first job at Cadence, I managed Product Marketing programs to drive growth in a $300 million system-level design and verification business. I produced campaign strategies, promotions plans, and schedules, fleshed out value cases, facilitated EDA community leadership, and ensured value proposition integration across corporate marketing, theater and sales team communications.

    As a means of encouraging candid feedback from Cadence’s top tier customers, I sponsored adoption of a wireless audience response system that allowed Technical Advisory Board members to confidentially express themselves amongst their competition.

    In a move that led to significant improvements in creative workflow, visual asset management, and intellectual property retention, I influenced company wide adoption of a content management system, and retained its first user community to engage and adopt it in production workflows.

  • Marketing Manager

    Data Physics Corporation

    (Privately Held; Electrical/Electronic Manufacturing industry)

    April 1997 — May 1998 (1 year 2 months)

To those ends, I also designed and managed a successful rebranding campaign that improved brand recognition, and planned and executed marketing campaigns with web, print collateral, advertising, PR, direct mail, exhibit and training components.

  • Consulting Marketing Manager

    InSite Marketing

    (Program Development industry)

    October 1993 — April 1997 (3 years 7 months)

    Building on my relationships in, and understanding of the industrial health and safety monitoring market, I consulted for several manufacturing clients to define and implement strategic marketing programs that promoted their solutions.

    For a Florida-based monitor manufacturer, I researched target market and user segment priorities, guided product development, improved distribution channels, and created goodwill and positive recognition from leading industry associations and media.

    To support the launch of American operations for a Japan-based client, I fleshed out well-defined value propositions, and designed and placed targeted print advertising.

    For a start-up providing an advanced monitoring system, I defined and implemented early adopter programs, placed automotive, petroleum, pulp & paper and defense experts to serve as beta and user acceptance testers, and recruited, negotiated agreements with, and trained manufacturer’s reps, distributors, and leasing agents.
