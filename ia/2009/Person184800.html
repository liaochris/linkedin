Pema Dhondup’s Summary

Pema is a second generation Tibetan born and raised in India by refugee parents. He holds three Master’s degrees in Accounting, Business Administration and Fine Arts.

Pema began his professional career working for CBS-SONY INDIA in the music division as a management trainee and soon became Regional marketing manager overlooking all the aspects of business covering Northern India. On March 17th 1993 Pema and his wife initiated the first and the only News video of the Tibetan people titled SARGYUR (The News). Leading the exile media revolution again, Pema initiated the formation of Press Club of Tibet and was also its first president.

After six years of video productions under TIBET COMMUNICATIONS, a Non-Profit organization that Pema and Yangchen floated making documentaries, promos, institutional films and TV series, Pema wanted to be trained as a filmmaker so that he could expand his activities. In 1999 he came to Los Angeles and joined USC film School to learn the craft of filmmaking. He had received the prestigious Fulbright scholarship to study in America.

While acquiring technical skills Pema concentrated on directing and writing at USC. He was chosen from the entire graduate program to direct one of the four USC produced narrative short films.

After graduation in 2002 Pema went back to India where he wrote, produced and directed the first Tibetan digital feature film titled ‘WE'RE NO MONKS’. Pema shot on location in Dharamsala where the headquarters of The Dalai Lama has been established and literally transformed this little Tibet into a huge set. While making his first feature in India Pema also began script doctoring and creative consulting Bollywood films. He also finished three scripts for development through his production company CLEAR MIRROR PICTURES.

Pema's goal is to make Clear Mirror Pictures a speciality production company based in Hollywood in making entertaining films set in eastern cultures specially Tibet and India.
