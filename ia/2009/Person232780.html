Thomas Stagliano’s Summary

Tom is experienced in the design and assessment of air and ground vehicles that are exposed to severe dynamic environments, including:
Crash and Structural Impact
HE Blast exposure and Fragments
Nuclear Overpressure, Gust and Thermal Radiation
Laser thermal radiation

Tom and his staff wrote and maintain over ten detailed computer codes used to assess these environments on vehicles.

Additionally, Tom and his staff can design, conduct and evaluate tests on small to full scale components/vehicles for these environments or that simulate these environments.

Tom designed a wind tunnel that is available at the National Solar Thermal Test Facility for use in nuclear thermal simulations of in-flight aircraft, and Tom assisted in the design and construction of the Target Arrestor System at the DTRA/Army Large Blast/Thermal Simulator (LBTS).

Tom has assisted in the evaluation (and the design of some):
B-52H, B-1B, B2A bombers
A-12, F-16C and F-15E tactical bombers
AH-64D, AH-1, UH-1, UH-60, OH-58, and RAH-66 helicopters
VH-3D, VH-60N and proto-type VV-22 transports
ALCM and GLCM cruise missiles
HMMWV, MRAP, and several LAV vehicles
Several C4I aircraft

Tom currently is assisting in the evaluation of lasers as weapons on MH-130 aircraft, the shock mitigation in deep earth penetrating fuzes, the design of specialized blast and fragment protection systems, and the assessment of new Army vehicles to the effects of landmine and IED exposure.

Additionally, Tom and associated ITT engineering staff provide advice and assistance to the FAA's Office of Commercial Space Transportation related to the certification of new maneuvering reentry vehicles.

Tom was a co-Author of:
DNA EM-1 Damage to Aircraft
Effects of Nuclear Weapons on Composite Aircraft
Effects of Nuclear Weapons on Army Enclosures

and a Reviewer of
Fundamentals of Aircraft Combat Survivability

Thomas Stagliano’s Specialties:

Nonlinear dynamic finite element modeling, and structural design and assessment
