Mark Schleifstein’s Summary

Co-author with John McQuaid of “Path of Destruction: The Devastation of New Orleans and the Coming Age of Superstorms,” Little, Brown & Co., 2006. Co-author of 2007 series “Last Chance: The Fight to Save a Disappearing Coast,” which won 2008 National Academies Communications Award and 2007 John H. Oakes Prize for Env. Reporting. His reporting on Hurricane Katrina was among his paper's work honored with 2006 Pulitzer Prizes for Public Service and Breaking News Reporting and the George Polk Award for Metropolitan Reporting. Coastal science issues before Katrina won 2006 award from American Geophysical Union. Co-author 2002 series, "Washing Away: How south Louisiana is growing more vulnerable to a catastrophic hurricane," won American Society of Civil Engineers' 2003 Excellence in Media award , 2003 National Hurricane Conference media award and finalist, 2003 Edward J. Meeman Award for Environmental Reporting. Co-author 1996 series, "Oceans of Trouble: Are the World's Fisheries Doomed?”, which won 1997 Pulitzer Prize for Public Service, Sigma Delta Chi Award for Public Service from Society of Professional Journalists. 2001 series, "Unequal Opportunity: How local programs to help disadvantaged businesses are enriching wealthy entrepreneurs," won 2002 Gerald Loeb Award for Distinguished Business and Financial Journalism. 1998 series, "Home Wreckers: How the Formosan termite is devastating New Orleans," was finalist, 1999 Pulitzer Prize for national reporting, won awards from American Association for the Advancement of Science and American Institute of Biological Sciences. 1994 series, "Stacking the Deck: The Birth of Louisiana Gambling," won 1995 APME award for public service journalism, Selden Ring Award for investigative reporting, and Gerald Loeb Award. 1991 series, "Louisiana in Peril," was finalist for 1992 Pulitzer Prize for explanatory journalism.
Board member, Society of Environmental Journalists.
