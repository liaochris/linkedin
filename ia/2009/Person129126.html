Keith Simmons’s Summary

Keith provides Chief Financial Officer and Trusted Business Advisor services on a scheduled basis to owners of emerging growth and mid-market companies. Business owners benefit by having a seasoned business advisor while avoiding the cost of a full time Controller or CFO who will typically have less experience.

With over 30 years of finance and operations experience, Keith works with business owners to create focus, establish goals and implement actions to improve current results and prepare for sustained growth. A hands-on approach consistently leads to increased cash and profitability, improved systems and staff performance, and effective business practices and controls. Among his areas of expertise are accounting, operations, business analysis, strategic planning, budgeting, cash management, cost accounting, problem solving and effective management reporting.

Keith’s experience as CFO and business consultant brings leadership and insight that generate confidence, team building and a pro-active methodology to all situations. Additional competencies include implementation of software solutions, policies and procedures, and design of motivational sales compensation programs. Keith enjoys challenging situations where his clear understanding of business issues allow him to evaluate complex situations and guide executives and employees to improved decision making.

Keith’s background includes rapid growth, mid-market and corporate improvement situations, where accurate evaluation and decisive action are critical elements. His experience in manufacturing, electronics, software, printing/converting, engineering and construction, distribution and service industries, in combination with financial, technology, human resources and administrative skills, present a well-rounded professional who brings competence to all situations. Working with owners and staff, areas of concern are soon transitioned into centers of corporate strength.

Keith Simmons’s Specialties:

Profit improvement, business analysis and alignment, executive coaching, operations improvement, budgeting, financial models, cash improvement, banking relationships, meaningful management reporting
