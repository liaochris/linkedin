Sergei Klimov’s Summary

Since 1996 I run Snowball Studios, a Russian games publisher that me and two partners created 13 years ago. We have an excellent creative team that I'm very proud of, and an ambition to make good business by publishing good games.

At Snowball, we back up not only well-known properties but also original independent titles that are a pleasure to work with (the latest are Jakub Dvorsky's Samorost and PomPom's Astro Tripper). As for major releases, we' delivered some of the best and most challenging localizations in the industry (the latest are fully localized Mass Effect, and fully localized cross-platform WALL-E), and we keep working on bigger stuff.

Snowball's strengths are in localization (both creatively, text and voices, and technically, working from raw assets and achieving same-day release) and in marketing (positioning the game towards proper audience and promoting the release with all the available tools). Feel free to contact me or my colleagues if you have a project to publish in Russia.

Since 2006 I also manage Snowberry Connection, a fund we set up for international financing and production of games from independent teams. We work on bringing original projects from concept to international release.

I would describe our budgets as moderate to medium. For Snowberry, the major resource is the time and effort of our production team spent on review of the concepts and work with local publishers to secure release of the game on key markets.

Because of the scope of our involvement in each project, Snowberry has the capacity to work with only 3-5 projects each year. Once we back up a project, it's full-speed ahead until the game is successfully published. I spend half a year in Moscow and the rest at the offices of our international partners, working to make sure that the on-going production proceeds as scheduled and will be released to meet the expectations of the markets and our forecasts.

Sergei Klimov’s Experience

  • Managing Partner

    Snowberry Connection

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Computer Games industry)

    September 2006 — Present (3 years)

    Snowberry Connection is a fund focused on international co-financing and co-production of games developed by independent teams.

    (1) we seek original concepts, primarily for PC platform and from teams who have at least one game already finished; (2) we work with the teams to refine the concepts during pre-production, to prove project's commercial viability for target territories in order to build the budget; (3) once the budget/revenue model of the project is proven, we finance and produce the game, then release it worldwide through our publishing partners.

    In 2008 we opened 5 projects: an adventure series by Frictional Games (SE) (both parts successfully released in 2008); a quest by Pollene (FR) (scheduled for release Q2 2009), a game for kids by Bajoum (SE) (scheduled for release Q2 2009) and a wargame by Apeiron (RU) (scheduled release Q3 2009).

    In 2009 we plan to open 3 to 5 projects, and we are currently open to any pitches. Please send a message with your address.

  • Associate Producer

    Snowbird Games

    (Design industry)

    September 2005 — Present (4 years)

    Snowbird Games is a micro-studio focused on experimental game design. The team creates gameplay prototypes that are used to evaluate the viability of opening further full-scale external productions. The company presently works only for Snowberry Connection and its development partners.

  • Managing Director

    Snowball Studios

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Computer Games industry)

    May 1996 — Present (13 years 4 months)

    Snowball Studios is a games publisher in Russia (and the Republics).

    The company runs two publishing labels: core games (strategies, RPGs, adventures) and games for kids (education and entertainment, ages 3-12).

    By 2009 we released over 250 titles on PC, PS2, PSP, PS3, X360, Wii & DS platforms (however, most business comes from PC).

    In 2008 we published a total of 47 titles. In our schedule for 2009, we have 52 releases. As of January 2009, 33 of the games we released have sold over 100,000 units in Russia.

    Snowball publishes both local partners (independent Russian studios) and international partners (European and American studios and publishers).

    We are open to reviewing new products for release in Russia, especially wargames, adventures, RPGs and games for kids. Please contact us with the details of your project
