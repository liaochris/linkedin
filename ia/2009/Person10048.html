Alma Delic-Ibukic’s Summary

Research interests include real-time calibration techniques for high-speed, wide-bandwidth analog to digital converters and mixed signal design.

Alma Delic-Ibukic’s Specialties:

Computer Skills:
• Operating systems: familiar with working in UNIX (Linux, Solaris) environment.
• Programming languages: MATLAB, C/C++, LabView.
• CAD skills: Cadence circuit schematics, layout and simulation tools, VHDL simulation and synthesizer tools.

  • Visiting Assistant Professor

    Bucknell University

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

  • Research Assistant

    University of Maine, Orono, ME

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    September 2004 — August 2008 (4 years)

    Developed a continuous gain calibration technique for parallel architecture delta-sigma analog-to-digital (A/D) converters. Working on implementing the technique in hardware using custom designed delta-sigma A/D converters and FPGA.

    Publications:
    - A. Delic-Ibukic and D. M. Hummels, ”Continuous digital calibration of pipeline A/D converters,“ IEEE Transactions on Instrumentation and Measurement, vol. 55, no. 4, pp.1175-1185, Aug. 2006.

    - D. Kotecki, T. Monk, V. Tkachuk, Z. Zhu, A. Delic-Ibukic and S. E. Turner, ”Custom analog and mixed-signal integrated circuit design at the undergraduate level - a university/industry collaboration“, in Proceeding of 6th International Workshop on Microelectronics Education, (Stockholm, Sweden), pp. 47- 50, June 2006, (ISBN: 91-7178-402-0).

  • Research Assistant

    University Of Maine, Orono, ME

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    September 2002 — May 2004 (1 year 9 months)

    Developed a continuous digital calibration technique for pipelined A/D converters. The new scheme utilizes an existing digital calibration algorithm and extends it to work in real-time. The calibration scheme was realized using a hardware description language (Verilog HDL) and two extra stages located at the end of the pipeline.

  • Research Assistant

    University of Maine, Orono, ME

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    September 2002 — May 2003 (9 months)

    Using Cadence designed, analyzed and laid out a 10-bit, 50MHz pipelined A/D converter in 0.6 microns AMI’s C5N CMOS process technology. Two-layer PCB test board was designed and fabricated for ADC characterization. Project report is available at www.eece.maine.edu/~adelic-i.

  • Data Converters Test Engineer, Student Intern

    Texas Instruments, Dallas, TX

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; TXN; Semiconductors industry)

    June 2002 — August 2002 (3 months)

    Was responsible for test and characterization of ADCs. Duties included data collection, analysis, software and test development, test hardware debugging, design and product support.

  • Data Converters Test Engineer

    Texas Instruments, Dallas, TX

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; TXN; Semiconductors industry)

    January 2001 — July 2001 (7 months)

    Helped with testing and characterization of the ADCs. Also, did automation of the Cascade Probe Station using LabVIEW software and DDE server, so the preliminary characterization of the ADCs could be done directly on the wafer.
