Stephen Rauch’s Summary

Accomplished, hands-on product development leader, and passionate technologist who has applied emerging trends and new technologies to initiate, develop, and bring a number of “disruptive, game-changing” innovative software products, technologies, and solutions to market.

Able to develop and organize ideas, develop strategies, and bridge technology, business strategy and customer experience. Comfortable in entrepreneurial and high-growth environments, leading new and turn-around software development efforts and a skilled leader in developing and running innovation initiatives and technology transfer within large organizations and in partnership with other companies.

Twenty plus years experience at a number of organizations filling leadership roles in diverse areas which include program management, product design, software development, architecture, trend development and innovation programs, technology and business strategy, human factors and design, technical marketing and developer evangelism, technical partner management and industry standards development.

Interested in start-up and turnaround environments that embrace emerging technologies and innovative computing scenarios involving complementary software and hardware design that maximizes value for the customer.

  • PerformancePoint Server Product Team - Principal Group Program Manager

    Microsoft Corporation

    (Public Company; MSFT; Computer Software industry)

    June 2006 — August 2008 (2 years 3 months)

    Established a new software product called Microsoft PerformancePoint Server. Product is an integrated server-based Performance Management platform that is designed to allow customers to monitor, analyze, and plan their business as well as drive alignment, accountability, and actionable insight across the entire organization. PerformancePoint Server 2007 is Microsoft’s first appearance in Gartner’s “Corporate Performance Management Magic Quadrant”.

    Defined the multi-release strategy for the Dashboard, Monitoring (Scorecards and KPIs) and Analytics functionality and the server-based platform of the product, led the product development team through the full product development life cycle and participated in product launch activities for version one.

    Completed the integration of Microsoft’s acquisition of ProClarity Analytics Corporation based in Boise, Idaho. Delivered Microsoft ProClarity 6.3 made available on Microsoft Volume Licensing in March 2007 and ProClarity product functionality in a number of Microsoft products.

  • Microsoft Dynamics Axapta Product Team – Principal Group Program Manager

    (Public Company; MSFT; Computer Software industry)

    Served as key member of management team in establishing a new product development organization for the Microsoft Dynamics Axapta product spanning Copenhagen, Denmark and Redmond, Washington. Hired and built eight new feature teams. Managed a team of program managers that was responsible for the development of the Axapta run-time platform service, developer platform and reporting and business intelligence functionality and server platform.

  • SQL Server Reporting Services Product Team – Lead Program Manager

    Microsoft Corporation

    (Public Company; MSFT; Computer Software industry)

    December 2000 — January 2004 (3 years 2 months)

    Established a new product called Microsoft SQL Server Reporting Services. Microsoft SQL Server Reporting Services is a comprehensive, server-based platform that is designed to support a wide variety of reporting needs, including managed enterprise reporting, ad-hoc reporting, embedded reporting, and Web-based reporting, that support the needs of business intelligence applications and enable organizations to deliver relevant information where needed across the entire enterprise. Product generates approximately $300M in revenue yearly and contributed to positioning Microsoft in Gartner Leaders Quadrant for Magic Quadrant for Business Intelligence Platforms.

    Completed market and competitor research and developed product strategy resulting in funding. Responsible for the scenario and feature set definition, architecture, programming interfaces and the products extensibility framework and customer feedback systems through the product development life cycle. Established and managed strategic alliances and participated in global launch activities that included early adopter and beta programs.

  • Exchange Product Team – Lead Program Manager

    (Public Company; MSFT; Computer Software industry)

    January 1998 — December 2000 (3 years )

    Defined the feature set and program managed the development of the Exchange Local Store through the full development life cycle. The Exchange Local Store was established as the first client-side object database and cache developed by Microsoft.

  • Developer Relations Group – Director, Technical Evangelist

    Microsoft Corporation

    (Public Company; MSFT; Computer Software industry)

    September 1996 — January 1998 (1 year 5 months)

    Managed and led a worldwide developer evangelism team that was responsible for encouraging and facilitating Independent Software Vendors and Fortune 100 company adoption of all object oriented COM-based technologies on both the server and the desktop. The specific COM-based technologies include standard interfaces for enterprise-class data storage and access, transaction processing, and message queuing. Developed evangelism plans and established technology design conferences for the purpose of gathering early feedback into the product development of the Windows platform.

    Participated in the development and established the adoption in the software industry of Microsoft’s OLE DB and OLE DB for OLAP (multi-dimensional) data access interface technologies. These interfaces have become the standard by which data source vendors and application vendors exchange data on the Windows platform.

    Established a COM-based standard in the Insurance Industry called OLE for Life Insurance. Identified need and mindshare with Independent Software Vendors and Fortune 100 companies in the industry, developed a draft of the specification and negotiated changes in standards organization meetings. COM standard was implemented by over 30 ISVs and Fortune 100 customers.

  • Product Development Manager, Development Technical Specialist, Architect

    Reuters Information Services

    (Public Company; TRI; Information Technology and Services industry)

    September 1990 — September 1996 (6 years 1 month)

    Senior Product Development Manager responsible for the architecture and software development during the full product development life cycle and global launch of the GLOBEX, the first 24-hour-a-day mission critical computerized automated matching system and service for real-time global trading of futures and options on the Chicago Board of Trade, Chicago Mercantile Exchange and Marché à Terme International de France Exchanges. Over two billion transactions have been processed since launch and approximately 70 percent of total volume at the CME occurs electronically on this system.

    Led an innovation team in the application of new and emerging technologies to create prototypes of new market opportunities in the distributed trading and transaction market space. A new trading business in South America that is based on a distributed trading system that enables clients to submit bond trade requests to Market Makers using mail facilities over a global private network was established as a result.

    Implemented a worldwide company development strategy that exposed functionality that was resident in several Reuter’s products using Component Object Model methodologies and technologies. Result of efforts saved the company approximately $10M in development costs.

  • Human Factors Engineer

    Grumman Aerospace

    (Public Company; Defense & Space industry)

    July 1984 — September 1990 (6 years 3 months)

    Created the user experience and symbology of all primary display systems (heads-up, multi-function and data entry unit displays) and hardware used to manage all systems in the F-14D fighter jet aircraft. Developed and executed human factors studies to determine Human Machine Interface parameters and designs in flight simulation, physical jet and aircraft carrier environments.

    Primary liaison between Grumman Aerospace and the US Navy Aircrew Systems Advisory Panel during the development of display systems and hardware in the F-14D fighter jet aircraft.
