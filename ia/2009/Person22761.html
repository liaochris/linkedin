Aviad (Avi) Eyal’s Summary

From 1992-1997, Avi started and ran a software development and infrastructure/outsourcing business. He began with $1,000 in scholarship money and 4 years later sold Insight Technologies to Datatec plc. He stayed on the board as a director for a year before leaving to pursue venture capital interests. Avi launched Real Technology, an early stage venture capital investment company exiting this to a Merchant Bank. In total Avi has been involved in over 20 technology businesses over the past 15 years. Avi also co-founded Zenprop, a commercial real estate business.

From 2001-2003 Avi did an LBO of a logistics software company, served as its COO and exited the investment in late 2003. Avi co-founded Cura in 2001 and led an LBO of the business in 2004.

Cura’s flagship product is Cura Enterprise – an innovative governance, enterprise-wide risk management, compliance, and performance management software solution. Cura is uniquely applicable to multiple verticals with no customization required, and is used in more than 15 countries by over 200 companies (across LSE, ASE, NYSE, JSE) such as BHP Billiton, Rio Tinto, Dimension Data, GlaxoSmithKline, Johnson & Johnson, Kellogg's, Standard Bank, Vodacom, a number of Governments, KPMG, PwC, Total, Old Mutual plc, and many more. Cura is rated as a leader in its field by Gartner Research and Forrester Research.

Avi has led Cura for the past four years achieving a compound annual growth rate of ~85% over a 4 year period, recording consistent profit and cash flow growth in each year while expanding from 1 offices to 5 international offices, and a staff headcount exceeding 100 people. Cura was sold to SoftPro Limited (India) in June 2009.

Avi has a unique blend of entrepreneurship, seasoned management expertise, technology competence and strategy understanding.
