Sam Wornom’s Summary

Seasoned, hands on financial executive with broad industry experience as consultant and CFO for small to middle market firms. Experienced in the full range of CFO functions. Record of sound judgment, team focus while working with all levels of staff and outside service providers. Comfortable working in start-up, growing, and mature companies to prepare detailed plans, implement, and then measure performance.

Sam Wornom’s Experience

  • Partner

    The Kingson Group, Ltd

    (Financial Services industry)

    January 2002 — January 2004 (2 years 1 month)

    Co-developed a strategic risk management (SRM) consulting firm focusing on delivering processes, frameworks, and techniques to improve dramatically the profitability and efficiency of businesses. Clients included Boeing, Wachovia Bank, and Praxair.
    • The Kingson Group approach to SRM: identify, measure, and respond to the full range of risks and opportunities, which impact the firm’s ability to achieve their stated objectives.
    • Prepared plans, budgets, and marketing plans for this start-up business.
    • Had direct responsibility for developing and maintaining strategic alliances critical to building and growing the business, and delivering quality advisory services to clients.

  • Chief Financial Officer

    REM Organization

    (Food & Beverages industry)

    February 1997 — May 2001 (4 years 4 months)

    Assumed CFO position of a newly formed $30+ million general partnership consisting of three regional food brokers and marketing agents for major national consumer products selling to retail grocery chains.
    • Refinanced partner debt with new lender to consolidate funding and reduce interest expense.
    • Consolidated accounts payable, payroll, insurance, employee benefits, and cash control.
    • In environment of decreasing margins, managed cash to satisfy vendors and meet payroll.
    • Converted to Navision computer software systems for GL, HR, PR, and fixed assets.
    • Established safety programs to reduce insurance losses and premiums.
    • Directly managed the accounting department and the company’s non-degreed controller, without the assistance of a full time CPA on staff.
    • Analyzed and assisted in corporate mergers.
