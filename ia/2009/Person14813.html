Andrew Der’s Summary

As a regionally recognized environmental practitioner and leader, for the last 26 years, served as a government regulatory official and environmental consultant providing regulatory, technical and scientific guidance in the environmental disciplines for public and private sector clients. Critical deliverables include assessment, feasibility studies, and regulatory compliance.

In private practice as of 2000 as an Associate and Director of Environmental Services at Loiederman Soltesz Associates, Inc. (LSA) and consultant and project developer. In 2000, completed 18 years of service at the state Maryland Department of the Environment (MDE) as a manager successfully leading major initiatives to manage, regulate and restore water resources. Also an accomplished independent consultant for donor program as well as committee member on several industry Commissions, presenter at industry and continuing education seminars and expert witness and media interviews.

As an additional endeavor and diversion, expanded technical writing aspects and skills successfully into freelance travel journalism.

Andrew Der’s Specialties:

Water resources management and regulation, environmental law, wetlands, mitigation, water quality, nonpoint source pollution, stormwater management, erosion and sediment control, forest conservation, stream monitoring and restoration, NEPA, NPDES, training, expert testimony, lobbying, technical writing and freelance travel writing.
