Alan Spencer’s Summary

Alan Spencer (alan@alanspencer.com) is a Chartered Engineer that has worked in the telecoms and IT industry for over 30 years and has experience of many aspects of the subject including TDM and IP network and system design, infrastructure design, network planning and roll-out, technical programme/project management (using Prince 2 methodologies), telecoms regulation and standardisation, IP and telecoms signalling and control protocol design and implementation, and training provided on many IT and telecoms subjects. Also has worked on the design and implementation of telecoms, microwave, radio and IT systems for the oil and gas industry. In additions, has acted as an Expert Witness for a number of high-profile cases involving the IT and telecoms arena.

Member of many UK/European industry groups and standards bodies on QoS, protocols and architectural models.

Has written over 20 reports, 30 papers and presented at over 20 conferences and provided over 100 training courses within the IT and telecoms subject area.

Has worked as a consultant to the European Union (projects in Cyprus, the Czech Republic and on a Europe-wide study). Has worked as a consultant to the UNHCR on the evaluation and recommendation of a worldwide network infrastructure. Has worked for ETSI on developing and writing European standards for a range of telecoms topics. Has also carried out investigations on behalf of the ITU-T in the development of worldwide telecoms recommendations. CCNA course. Has SC security clearance.

Main areas of expertise include; voice/network engineering, next-generation network (VoIP) design and implementation, transmission/lower layer technologies (SDH, MPLS, etc.) design and implementation, QoS network design and implementation, RFP/RFQ/SLA processes and evaluation, analysis and report writing, and training within the IT and telecoms subject area.

Will not consider permanent employment - only contract/consultancy positions.
