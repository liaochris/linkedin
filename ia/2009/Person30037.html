Boayan Island’s Summary

Boayan is the largest island in the municipality of San Vicente, Palawan which is part of barangay Poblacion having an aggregate area of 1,327.31 hectares. It can be reached through a 45-minute to 1 hour motorboat ride from Poblacion proper.

During the 2008 CBMS survey, Boayan island registered a total population of 905 sparsely distributed in three (3) sitios, composed of sitio Casuyan 336, sitio Daplac 301 and sitio Pulang Bato 268. It has a total household of 185.

Next to sitio Panindigan, Boayan is the second largest fishing village in Pobacion with fishing as its main economic activity due to abundant marine resources. There are about 220 individuals or more than 78% of its total labor force is engaged in fishing.

Boayan Island’s Experience

    (Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    January 1900 — Present (109 years 5 months)

    Boayan’s strategic location and natural beauty both marine and terrestrial is perfect to develop the island as tourist hub of the municipality. As per Environs study in 1991, the first ever Comprehensive Development Plan of the municipality of San Vicente, Boayan was proposed as tourist landmark and as a regulated fishermen’s zone. After 37 years since San Vicente functioned as distinct municipality, the LGU is taking risks to deviate from conventional pattern of development and to level up the LGU’s enhancing capabilities through utilizing sensibly its resources, combining agri-ecotourism as alternative source of income. In adherence to environmental advocacy, the LGU declared Daplac-Pulang Bato as Marine Protected Area in collaboration with barangay Poblacion and the fisher folks of the island to effectively protect and preserve its marine environment.

    As stipulated in its recent proposed Municipal Comprehensive Land Use Plan (2009-2020), the LGU’s objective as far as tourism is concerned is to develop and sustain viable tourism program that will significantly develop strategies basically focusing on eco-tourism, environmental conservation and community development within its physical carrying capacities. Along this line, tourism development shall be regulated in the manner of strict implementation of the Municipal Tourism Code and the Comprehensive Zoning Ordinance where both land and water uses are clearly defined.
