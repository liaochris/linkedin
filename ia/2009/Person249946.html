Yinglan Tan’s Summary

A recipient of the Public Service Commission (PSC) Overseas Merit Scholarship, Yinglan graduated with University and College Honours from Carnegie Mellon University, USA, earning dual degrees: a B.Sc. in Electrical and Computer Engineering and a B.Sc. in Economics. At graduation, Yinglan was awarded the William L. Everitt Student Excellence Award for being a distinguished graduate.

After graduating from Carnegie Mellon University, Yinglan was awarded the Milton and Rose Friedman Fellowship. In his stint in Washington D.C. under the Milton and Rose Friedman Fellowship, Yinglan worked closely with the Chief Economic Consultant to The World Bank and was heavily involved in facilitating a trade infrastructure diagnostic study of Ethiopia and exploring the options to develop Ethiopia's entrepreneurial ecology. Subsequently, Yinglan obtained a Masters degree in Management Science and Engineering from Stanford University, USA in 2004, where he concurrently obtained certification as a Certified Risk Analyst by Stanford Center for Professional Development. At Stanford, he was a consultant to a startup in Silicon Valley, leading product innovation, developing market expansion frameworks and spearheaded process re-engineering.

Yinglan returned to Singapore in 2004 to join the Singapore Public Service and was a recipient of the National Innovation and Quality Circle Award in 2005. Yinglan was also an adjunct faculty at Singapore Management University and taught analytic business modeling. He has guided teams on successful projects on social entrepreneurship. He has led teams to develop models for the Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS) which will used in planning and operations of the international IMF/World Bank conference to be held in Singapore in September 2006.

Yinglan Tan’s Specialties:

Yinglan was the first Director for China, 3i VentureLab, INSEAD in China. As the representative for INSEAD in China, Yinglan has been implementing expansion plans to enhance 3i Venturelab's and INSEAD’s market share in the China market. He is actively involved in venture opportunities, identifying high potential companies in China, working closely with CEOs in China and USA to develop and manage growth strategies for these high potential companies.
