Nina Carstens’s Summary

I am looking forward to a long term commitment.

In 2004 I commited myself to supporting my husband in his quest to attain his Computational/ Biotechnology PhD. It was a difficult situation for me to progress career wise, but I was rewarded with a beautiful baby girl, and the continuation of my studies into Clinical Psychology.

My past education and work experience was of the highest calibre, with strong mentoring, and has always focused on the dynamics within the internal functioning of organisations, and how effective the communication was coming in and going out. I always enjoyed logistics and industrial psychology in my degree, and appreciated the rules Mercantile Law brought into the whole equation. However, I couldn’t have prepared myself for the real world with the theoretical alone. Once in business, I noticed how every industry maintained the similar characteristics, and how each puzzle piece needed a tight team, whether this was a communication channel or a team in the traditional sense of the word.
In the end if the client is not happy, the organisation needs to look within itself to find solutions.

I am ready for a refocus in my career, and I am open to the right opportunity.
