Frank Radice’s Summary

Frank J. Radice is the President and Chief Marketing Officer of the National Academy of Television Arts & Sciences, and managing partner in the marketing and media firm, VIDA F.R. Company, in New York and London.

Frank serves on the board of of the New York Festivals. He received the TV Week Magazine/PROMAX Campaign of Distinction award in 2007, was inducted into the PROMAX Hall of Fame in 2004 and named Brand Builder of the Year by Multichannel News and Broadcasting & Cable in 2003. Frank is also named in the 60th Diamond Anniversary Edition of Who’s Who in America.

In addition, Frank oversaw the deal between NBC Universal and Proctor & Gamble that launched "Petside.com"

In 2005 he headed the promotion of the transition between NBC News anchors, Tom Brokaw and Brian Williams, and in 2006, the addition of Today show host Meredith Vieira. In 2007, he oversaw the promotional launch of the 4th hour of "Today."

An Emmy award winning songwriter and composer, Frank co-created the “America’s First Family” theme, and was Executive Producer of the Emmy nominated “Live For Today” theme, both for NBC’s Today show.

In 2005, he was senior producer/music director of NBC’s “Concert for Hurricane Relief” which yielded 40 million dollars in aid for the victims of Katrina.

In addition to extensive experience in advertising, promotion, and music, Frank has a lengthy background in news production and journalism. During his 11 years with ABC News, he was honored with two DuPont Awards and an Emmy. He was a producer for “Nightline” from 1980 to 1984, the “ABC World News Tonight” and “The Last Word with Phil Donahue and Greg Jackson.”

In 2006, Frank and his wife, Vida, co-wrote "Sam Katz on the Loose." A pop-up children’s book illustrated by Charles Fazzino and published by Random House. This year the book was released as a set of children’s puzzles available exclusively at Barnes and Noble, nationwide.

Frank lives with his wife, Vida, in New York and London.
