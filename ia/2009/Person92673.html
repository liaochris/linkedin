Harry Knapp’s Summary

Knapp has been a producer on some of the most critically received and financially successful independent films in the past ten years.

Last year Knapp’s film, Werner Herzog’s RESCUE DAWN, starring Christian Bale was the darling of the indie world. It was the top film at the Toronto Film Festival and went on to gross more than $60 million worldwide. Knapp also produced the much publicized $30 million dollar action picture REDLINE starring Eddie Griffin.

Independent films have always been Knapp’s’ passion. Throughout the years he produced other festival favorites like “JEFFREY” and “IT’S MY PARTY” . Both films were considered courageous at a time when AIDS was still a very sensitive issue. Early in his career he brought French actress Irene Jacob to the US for the first time for his film TRUSTING BEATRICE.

During his three years at the helm of the ultra-hip World Of Wonder Productions Knapp produced over 50 hours of television and documentary films including “THE EYES OF TAMMY FAYE” and “PARTY MONSTER”. After joining WOW Knapp helped transform the company from a boutique house doing $3mil in sales to a major indie player by bringing his industry contacts and expanding the business footprint into a full service production entity grossing $35 mil per year. Part of that business model included music production and management where WOW represented a handful of artists including RuPaul and Kristine W which integrated music and television production deals.

Prior joining World of Wonder Knapp was a Production Manager/1st AD on films like the mini-series “OLDEST LIVING CONFEDERATE WIDOW TELLS ALL” starring Diane Lane and the film “Me & The Kid” starring Danny Aiello.

For two years Knapp was a Production Supervisor at Republic Pictures and served as an Executive Production Manager on several Movies of the Week in the US, Canada and abroad.

Knapp produced his first film at 21, the coming of age film THE RUNNIN’KIND starring Juliette Lewis.
