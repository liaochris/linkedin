Apostolos Topalidis’s Summary

Part of Boartes Profile for Apostolos Topalidis:
Apostolos is an energetic, multi-disciplined, Technology Project Engineering Professional, who brings a broad set of technical and project management skills to any project team.
Apostolos has particularly relevant experience in TETRA Training/Transfer of knowledge delivery plans, clarification of customer, operational TETRA Fleetmap requirements and the development of purpose-built databases to capture customer TETRA Fleetmap data.
Apostolos’s multi-disciplined Technology skills have been built upon a foundation of expertise established through his hands-on involvement in a variety of high technology projects such as:
• Mid-scale Web Database software development projects
• Research and Development (R&D) for the Pharmaceuticals/Engineering Department of Brighton University in the UK (MSc Course)
• Athens Olympic Games C4I Security Project
• Qatar – C4I, PSS & TETRA Projects
In relation to the first project above, Apostolos successfully managed the implementation and development of region-wide, web-based, Knowledge Management and Human Resource Management systems
Apostolos’s relevant knowledge and expertise has been further developed through his integral involvement in C4I, TETRA and Security Operational Planning projects successfully completed in support of the recent Athens 2004 Olympic Games and the C4I and TETRA projects for QATAR. During this time, Apostolos supported:
• The establishment of an overall Program Management System for:
• The Command Centre Systems (CCS) Work Group
• The Physical Security Systems (PSS )Work Group, and
• The TETRA Work Group (Primary Focus)
As a member of the QSTRS and Athens 2004 Olympic Games TETRA Work Group, Apostolos helped at Qatar in the Training and Transfer of knowledge implementation and supervised the installation of DWS at eight sites and in Athens supported the Work Group in completing the TETRA Fleetmap used by the Public Safety and Security Agencies.
