Sameer Vittal’s Summary

Engineering Technical Leader at GE Energy's Advanced Technology Operations, responsible for driving advanced analytics solutions into both Wind Turbine and Gas Turbine products and services.

10+ yrs expertise in the Energy Industry (Wind Turbines, Gas Turbine Combined Cycle Power Plants)
5+ years experience in the automotive & aviation industry

Current responsibilities include

1) Developing core technologies for power plant monitoring & life management. Includes advanced algorithms for sensor integration, signal processing, anomaly detection, diagnostics, risk and remaining life prediction.

2) Driving advanced data-driven solutions for asset optimization (Data-Mining, Predictive Modeling, Stochastic Optimization, Reliability Engineering, Condition-Based Maintenence, Decision Support)

3) Developing actuarial / financial risk-management solutions and business processs used in modeling extended warranties and long-term service agreements (LTSA's)

4) Technology Planning and Program Management: Developing strategic analytics technologies across multiple GE businesses (Global Research, Energy, Aviation) and external agencies / universities. Managing teams of scientists, engineers and statisticians located in Europe, Asia & the US.

  • Senior Technologist - Advanced Technology Operations

    (Public Company; GE; Oil & Energy industry)

  • Lead Engineer & Program Manager - Gas Turbine Services Engineering

    GE Energy

    (Public Company; GE; Oil & Energy industry)

    September 2005 — February 2008 (2 years 6 months)

    In this role, I was tasked with developing the core mathematical damage accumulation models, risk-based inspection processes, workflows and analysis tools for our gas turbine Condition-Based Maintenence (CBM) programs. This is a critical component of Energy's contractual services and asset management capabilities.

    Based on this program's success, I was tasked with expanding the technologies developed in CBM to include sensors, controls, physics and advanced data analysis algorithms that were originally developed for Aviation applications.

  • Reliability Engineer

    GE Energy

    (Public Company; GE; Oil & Energy industry)

    June 2000 — August 2005 (5 years 3 months)

    In my 5+ years in reliability engineering, I worked on all aspects of reliability analysis and probabilistic design ..ranging from life data analysis (E.g. Weibull's), fracture mechanics, warranty models, spare parts & logistics optimization, accelerated life tests & inspection plans, discrete-event simulations (system reliability), Markov models, etc.

    These techniques were use to improve the reliability of the full spectrum of GE Energy equipment - ranging from combined cycle plants, gas & steam turbines, wind turbines, pipelines, large sensor and data collection networks, etc. In addition, I was a technical consultant to many GE businesses like Aviation, Oil & Gas, NBC, GE Capital, amongst others.

  • Research Assistant

    Clients included Pitney Bowes, Bausch & Lomb, NYSERDA (NY State Energy Research & Development Agency)
