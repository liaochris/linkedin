Clinton Smith’s Summary

Demonstrated Leadership and commitment to excellence throughout my career. As a Marine helicopter Crew Chief, I was in charge of managing all technical aspects of the $22Million dollar CH53-E Helicopter and all personnel and equipment. I always performed in the top of my class in all Marine Corps performance metrics. Expert rifleman, expert pistol, 96+% in all physical fitness tests, and led by example with over 950 flight hours performing missions over land, and sea in a fast-paced environment with no losses.

I have also demonstrated leadership qualities as an R&D technician fresh out of the Marine Corps on a Microturbine generator product development program for Allied Signal. I was promoted two times in three years after exhibiting a strong desire and technical aptitude to learn and develop complex technical equipment that consisted of rotating machinery, three-phase power, and complex software and controls systems. I was approached by Senior Management to provide product support in the field on the new product launch.

I have participated in two Fortune 500 "green technology" product launches and supported the product solutions in the field.

While commissioning the first 30-40 machines and supporting the product remotely in the field, I continued to pursue my academic career and graduated with honors to include: AA in general studies 3.8 GPA, Bachelor of Science in Management 3.8 GPA Magna Cum Laude, and a Masters Degree in Business Administration with a 3.75 GPA Beta Gamma Sigma. I completed this while managing a growing family, and excelling in a Management role as West Coast Service Manager for Ingersoll Rand. I have demonstrated a commitment to excellence, lead by example, and have a strong work ethic that embraces the team work approach to achieving high level goals.
