Kate Nickerson’s Summary

I have held a strong interest for graphic design since I was in middle school, when I taught myself HTML coding, made my own websites, and founded Custom Layouts, a self-operated business that provided unique blog designs for free upon request. I also joined Yearbook Staff when page layouts were created manually with carbon-paper grids, rulers, and chalk crop marks. I became a member of Yearbook Staff again in high school, and the technology was much more advanced this time around. I used PageMaker to create several layouts for various clubs and organizations, and this enjoyable responsibility was one of the primary factors that led to my choice of study at Cal Poly.

I am now pursuing a B.S. degree in Graphic Communication and excelling in general education and major courses. This program at Cal Poly has a strong emphasis on printing processes and teaches them in laboratories on donated presses. There are also many creative-based classes about subjects such as photography, typography, web design, book design, magazine and newspaper design, and much more. These classes have tremendously improved my skills as a designer.

I believe that my careful eye, aesthetic ability, and diligent attitude have helped me thrive as a student and as a designer. Upon graduating, I plan on working full-time in the graphic design field, and I am especially interested in layout design and web design positions, although my education provides for a broad spectrum of creative capabilities.
