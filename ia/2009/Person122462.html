Jessica Scheibach’s Summary

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; AMZN; Internet industry)

    2005 — 2006 (1 year)

    I was the Director of two teams with similar names but fairly different roles - Customer Experience and Shopping Experience. (Odd but typical Amazon)

    The Customer Experience team was made up of a group of usability engineers responsible for testing, measuring and improving Amazon's websites and internal tools. We operated Amazon's small, homegrown usability lab.

We designed, built, tested and launched a bunch of website improvements. Well, mostly improvements. We launched some bad stuff too but we learned from it.

  • Director, CS Integrated Applications

    2004 — 2005 (1 year)

    I was the Director of four teams in WW Operations group: Customer Service Applications Engineering, Contact Reduction and Automation, Knowledge Management, and Training/Quality Improvement. I had responsibility for the web-based CRM tool (developed by Amazon), knowledge management and training systems customer service associates used to handle customer contacts for Amazon.com, Amazon’s international sites, Target.com and Bombay.com. This was probably the most metrics-driven, operationally focused group I was ever in at Amazon. It was awesome.

My responsibilities included strategic and project planning for group, tracking and reporting on ordering and self-service metrics (including usability, order conversion, and customer contact data), performance management, and recruiting efforts.

    During this time, Amazon transitioned from a matrix organization to the "2 Pizza Team" model and managing that transition for my group was a big part of this role, and a big learning experience for me.

  • Group Program Manager, Consumer Ordering Applications

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; AMZN; Internet industry)

    We did a lot of improvements to ordering that are still in place today on Amazon, such as "single page checkout." (Not easy while you also have to keep ordering up & running and seamless 24/7). We also did the first major platform work to build out Amazon's technology and user experience on other websites such as Target.com, which launched during this period.

  • Senior Program Manager, Buying & On-site Support Systems

    Amazon.com

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; AMZN; Internet industry)

    2000 — 2002 (2 years)

    I was responsible for program management of projects that focused on customer self-service tools such as Your Account and Online Help.

    For about the last year of this period, I had the opportunity to lead a significant strategic effort called "C4' (the 4 C's all related to customers). It was a company-wide initiative with a goal to reduce operational and customer service costs by dramatically increasing customer satisfaction and providing tons of customer self-service options on the site. We divided into 3 main areas of focus: root cause reduction (figuring out the root cause of customer pain and fixing it), customer service efficiency tools, and customer self-service. We accomplished a TON of work in a year and saved millions of dollars. This was a huge proving ground for me at Amazon and paved the way for my career there, and it was also one of the experiences I am most proud of.
