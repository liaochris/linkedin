Bonnie Friedman’s Experience

  • President

    Bonnie Friedman Strategic Communications, LLC

    (Public Relations and Communications industry)

    October 2005 — Present (4 years 1 month)

    Creativity. Imagination. Vision. These are the hallmarks of Bonnie Friedman Strategic Communications, LLC. The boutique consultancy serves the communications and marketing needs of non-profit, government and small business clients.

    Bonnie Friedman brings insight, energy and purpose to all her work. She is committed to providing her clients with professional and cost-effective service while ensuring an imaginative and creative response to their needs. Individual attention and care go into every project. With more than 35 years of organizational experience, she sees things from the client's perspective and guarantees customized solutions designed to achieve results. Bonnie Friedman's background, expertise and insight make her an ideal partner for communications, marketing and branding projects, large and small.

    Specialties also include outreach and education campaigns, crisis communications, press relations, media and speech coaching, speech writing, website management and audiovisual production.

  • Public Affairs/Communications

    U.S. Government

    (Public Relations and Communications industry)

    January 1971 — September 2005 (34 years 9 months)

    Before starting her own business, Bonnie served as the Director of Marketing and Customer Service at the U.S. Small Business Administration where she refined the SBA brand and built a new marketing program.

    Prior to that she was the Director of Communications of the Occupational Safety and Health Administration. In that role she provided immediate, on-the-ground response and long-term follow up at Ground Zero in New York City after the World Trade Center attack. She also rebranded the agency and managed a wide range of communications initiatives, including ergonomics, workplace violence and other complex, controversial issues.

    Her background also includes public affairs management in employment and training, women's issues, veterans programs, wage and hour laws, equal employment opportunity, employee communications and more. She began her career at the National Institutes of Health while a student at American University in Washington, DC, where she earned a B.A. in Communications.
