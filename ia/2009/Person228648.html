  • Director/ Owner

    Vorb Limited

    (Online Media industry)

    November 2000 — August 2009 (8 years 10 months)

    Vorb is New Zealand’s most popular cycling/ outdoors website (Nielsen//Netratings) and one of the most active New Zealand based online communities. I started it as a hobby in 2000 and went full-time in 2005. I have managed every aspect of the site including developing a community/ content management system in a Linux, Apache, MySQL, PHP environment, content generation, developing mobile and video web applications, advertising sales, administration and more. Vorb has won a number of awards including “Best Sports And Recreation Site” at the Netguide People’s Choice Awards 2007, 2008 and 2009

  • Online Community Editor

    ID Digital Media

    (Online Media industry)

    October 2007 — June 2009 (1 year 9 months)

    ID Digital Media (www.id.co.nz) manages a number of successful New Zealand websites combining development with in-house online publishing. My main role in ID was to help clients develop online communities/ communication strategies and act in a project management position for the development of their websites. This included assessing client needs, client relationship management, talking clients through the realities of online community interaction, working with the developer/ design team to address client needs, creation of training material and training clients in the use of Enterprise Manager – ID’s in house content management system.

  • Editor - Spoke Magazine

    First Floor Publishing

    (Publishing industry)

    July 2005 — October 2007 (2 years 4 months)

    Spoke Magazine is New Zealand’s longest running mountain bike magazine, it has received praise from senior members of the international cycling community and featured in the “The New Cool” exhibition at the Dowse Art Gallery. As the editor of Spoke Magazine I was responsible for co-ordinating articles and features from dozens of contributors. I also wrote a regular editorial, news and generally contribute one major feature an article. I have been contributing to Spoke Magazine since 2001.

  • Co Producer - Drop In New Zealand

    Fall Line Productions

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Media Production industry)

    December 2004 — May 2005 (6 months)

    Drop In New Zealand is a 13 episode mountain bike themed television programme, during it’s first showing it was the third highest rating programme on C4, behind “The Family Guy” and “Futurama”. Drop In was filmed by a Canadian production company and as co-producer I acted as the primary contact in New Zealand, planned the itinerary, organised logistics, collaborated with securing sponsorship, and guided the crew around New Zealand.

  • Library Systems Administrator

    Victoria University of Wellington

    (Educational Institution; 1001-5000 employees; Libraries industry)

    May 2003 — December 2004 (1 year 8 months)

    In December 2003 the VUW Library migrated from its old library management system “Dynix” to the Oracle based “Voyager” system shared by the CONZULSys consortium (now LCoNZ: www.lconz.ac.nz) and hosted by Datacom. As the Library Systems Administrator I was the main technical role for the Library and worked with Adrienne Ridley-Houlker (my manager and head of Digital Services) and members of the CONZULSys consortium towards a successful migration. After the migration I supported the system for Library staff members and students. I collaborated with other CONZYULSys members, travelling to share my skills with colleagues. I developed a large number of reports for staff members using Crystal Reports 10.

  • Technical Analyst

    Victoria University of Wellington

    (Educational Institution; 1001-5000 employees; Libraries industry)

    January 2001 — May 2003 (2 years 5 months)

    My role encompassed working on and managing hardware and software related projects with Library staff, broad support for library specific technology both for staff, students and desktop support staff. I also administered the Library Intranet, including a two server Windows 2000 domain, Active Directory Service, an IIS hosted website, file sharing and user/computer level security. Projects I took an active role in included the delivery of secure catalogue and web access to library users, and the earthquake strengthening and refurbishment of the central Library building (Rankine Brown).

  • Library Computer Support

    Victoria University of Wellington

    (Educational Institution; 1001-5000 employees; Libraries industry)

    December 1999 — January 2001 (1 year 2 months)

    I was employed as library specific desktop support for the VUW Library. My role also included planning hardware upgrades and other library specific applications inside and out of the normal desktop environment. My major project was transforming the mixed platform environment (Windows 95, Windows 98, Macintosh and Windows NT) to a server based Windows 2000 domain with a standard (Windows NT then Windows 2000) desktop image.
