Mario Martin’s Summary

Mario R. Martin’s life is entertainment. His experience, both personally and professionally, has cultivated a background based on successes and networks. While still in college, Martin co-hosted a radio show and wrote for his university's newspaper. Those experiences evolved into producing commercial radio at WTMJ in Milwaukee and writing for various publications. Next stop: Coca-Cola. Integral in sales within the largest region in the territory, Martin lent his passion for entertainment as an essential component in the “If You Don’t Know, You Don’t Go©” campaign that showcased then little known groups Blink-182 and Black Eyed Peas. Later he went on to build visibility for industrial manufacturer, Meltric Corporation, securing the largest marketing budget in the company’s history.

Later yet, Martin fought hard for a spot at Virgin/EMI-owned Narada Records in Milwaukee, WI. Jockeying for over 6 months, Martin was brought on board in the publicity department. Standing the test of time and restructuring, he eventually oversaw the entire department by himself and celebrated various awards with his artists. Restructuring finally caught up as EMI Worldwide closed the Milwaukee offices and operations were absorbed by the New York headquarters.

Counteracting such events, Martin moved to Los Angeles to increase the client base for boutique agency Gorgeous PR. His countless contacts, passion for music and charisma proved to be a true asset to the business. Sought out by GoTV Networks, Martin brought his knowledge and experience to a technological medium as his publicity and entertainment background served the company well and raised awareness and visibility.

The next chapter is Martin's own publicity brainchild, aptly named Soulfunk Publicity. Stay tuned...
