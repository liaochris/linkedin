Joanna Scavo’s Summary

I am currently Director of Multi-language Subtitling & Translation for Aberdeen Captioning. A U.S. national living in Spain and managing subtitling contractors and clients around the world.

The idea of Aberdeen Captioning, Inc began with two sisters, Amy & Becky, and their big brother, Matt, as they were sitting around a campfire in the summer of 2001. They had the perfect combination of passion and talent. Amy had a passion for closed captioning and had been working as a real-time captioner for years. Becky had fervor and training for broadcast television. Matt had always been the entrepreneur in the family and loved having his two sisters in on this great opportunity. So there you have it: Captioning knowledge, broadcast training, and entrepreneurship—the perfect brother and sister union for what now has become Aberdeen Captioning, Inc.

Being from a small farming town in Michigan, they were brought up with a strong work ethic, integrity, and Christian faith. These values are what Aberdeen is built upon. Whether it is clients or employees, Aberdeen stands behind these values, and it shows—clients who work with Aberdeen, stay with Aberdeen.

From three siblings working out of an apartment to over 30 team members strong, Aberdeen has continued to grow year after year, never losing sight of their mission—to provide access for everyone. They believe all viewers—the hard of hearing, the deaf, English language learners, and more—should have equal access to all programming.

Today, Aberdeen Captioning is a place people come to find solutions to their closed captioning, subtitling, and post-production needs and where employees love coming to work.
