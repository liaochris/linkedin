Salar Salahshoor’s Summary

My personal mantra is "ideate, create, execute".

At the core I'm passionate about building organizations through entrepreneurial management, and I have a deep desire to empower young people to realize their full potential in life.

I believe that any vision is achievable given the right mixture of hard work, passion and tenacity.

My leadership experiences range from strategic planning and managing sales teams to developing partnerships and negotiating contracts as a co-founder at Novel Conception.

I have a broad range of experience in finance, marketing and IT through building and implementing information systems and financial models for start-up companies, designing and developing marketing collateral in various mediums (web, print, presentation), producing feasibility and risk analyses for new projects and working with cross-functional teams to produce operating budgets, econometric forecast models, procurement analyses and accounting controls for a Fortune 500 and BusinessWeek #1 IT company.

    Seattle University

    (Educational Institution; 501-1000 employees; Higher Education industry)

    March 2008 — June 2008 (4 months)

    Albers School of Business and Economic’s MGMT 489 is the capstone course all business undergraduates must take prior to graduation. It is the pinnacle one reaches to be challenged and tested prior to receiving a BABA from Seattle University. The course offers students an opportunity to integrate all the knowledge they have gained in the past four years by building individual business plans from the ground up. Alongside writing full business plans, the students engage in daily discussions around leading-edge management articles they are assigned to read and write reflective papers on. It was my role in the class to act as a mentor to the students, offer them support with their business planning and lead daily open-discussions on a broad range of business subjects from value statements to entrepreneurial finance, but mostly ranging within the art and science of management practice.
