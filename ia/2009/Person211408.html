Scott Dax’s Summary

Scott L. Dax is Senior Director and Head of Chemistry at Galleon Pharmaceuticals. Dr. Dax brings 20 years of experience as a pharmaceutical researcher.

Scott received his B.A. from Shippensburg State College. Under the mentorship of Prof. Joseph P. Marino, Scott earned his Masters and Doctorate degrees from The University of Michigan. Scott developed new methodology for the generation of ortho-quinone methides and the synthesis of cannabinoids. Scott conducted a National Institutes of Health Postdoctoral Fellowship working with Prof. Edwin Vedejs at the University of Wisconsin. There he developed chemistry to generate and trap azomethide ylides via cycloaddition reactions. Scott then joined Hoffmann-La Roche where his lab was the first to synthesize carbapenem-quinolone dual-action antibacterials. His devotion to this research led to Scott writing his first textbook. Scott moved to the DuPont-Merck Pharmaceutical Company working on HIV protease inhibitors. Scott joined J&J and rose to Research Fellow & Analgesic Team Leader at Johnson & Johnson Pharmaceutical Research & Development. There he led his labs in the discovery of modulators of ion channels and G-protein coupled receptors that play a role in various pain pathways. He has published in the area of neuropeptide Y and bradykinin inhibitors, delta and mu opioid agonists and ion channel blockers such as vanilloid antagonists. Scott pioneered new methodologies including solid-phase Mannich chemistry and the utilization of beta-substituted alpha-aminotetralins as versatile surrogates for the ubiquitous phenethyl pharmacophore. In 2008, Scott moved to Galleon Pharmaceuticals.

Scott has over forty publications, several book chapters and numerous US patents. He has served on NIH study sections, as an editor for an international medicinal chemistry journal as well as guest editor for two book series. Scott has been teaching chemistry in an adjunct role at Neumann College since 2006.
