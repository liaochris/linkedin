Jasper Silvis’s Summary

From 1994 to 1999, was an aerospace structures engineer working on the Bell/Boeing V-22 Osprey, as part of the analysis team for the aft fuselage section. I supported design of the aft skin/stringers and the aft escape hatch, supported static and flight testing, and aircraft battle damage repair analysis. From 1999 - 2001, while still at Boeing, helped form a automated tools development team charged with standardizing the software used to to analyze structures. Also worked as a Unix systems administrator, managing servers and applications used by engineers. From 2001 to 2004, took my knowledge of the IBM Tivoli framework and rules development to Cendant Mortgage (now PHH Mortgage) and developed many monitors and event processing environments for servers and computing systems. Returned to Boeing in 2005 and joined the analytical tools team in Phantom Works where I continue to work to this day, developing Java applications and functions to help engineers perform their tasks easier, quicker, and to Boeing standards. Since 2007, have been setting up businesses designed to help people expand their lifestyle and wellness in many areas such as health and fitness, home and mortgage, and wealth management and accumulation. Exposing people to methods for eliminating debt, growing wealth, protecting assets, reducing taxes, reducing risk of foreclosure, selling unwanted or troubled real estate, improving fitness, and developing self confidence has completely changed my life as well. I am a much more happy and driven person.
