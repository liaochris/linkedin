Larry Eason’s Summary

As President & Founder of digital strategy and communications firm DotOrgPower Larry helps .orgs harness the web and new media to achieve their goals. He works with cause leaders to expand their sense of what is possible online and to use that transformed thinking to innovate and succeed.

Larry is an evangelist for the power of online story-telling to move the public, donors and influentials. In 1999 he pioneered the use of new media for the Public Interest Research Groups (PIRGs) starting a first-of-its-kind in-house media center that has used digital media captured by the center and center-trained videographers around the nation to inspire a decade of donors, staff, and volunteers. In 2008 Larry helped International Medical Corps use web video to win $100,000 in the 2008 AMEX Members Project

Larry's work on the successful campaign to pass Prop 63 earned him the national Golden Dot Award for Best Statewide Internet Campaign in 2005. Larry led the online campaign to defeat CA Prop 85 in 2006 the campaigns to defeat Props 1D and 1E in the 2009 CA Special Election.

Clients include the CA Department of Mental Health, RAND Corp, Planned Parenthood Federation, International Medical Corps, and Parent Revolution.

Larry was a founding partner at The e Organization, which specialized in online strategies for nonprofits and campaigns. The e Organization was acquired by Virilion where Larry worked with cause and corporate clients such as eBay and Georgetown University.

Before The e Organization, Larry spent 17 years working with nonprofits and political campaigns.

Larry is a frequent speaker on strategic planning, web 2.0, and online storytelling. He serves as Chair of the Board of Directors for Healthy Child Healthy World.

Larry is a surfer and artist, and teaches ceramics at a local studio. He lives with his wife, daughter, and five rescue dogs in Los Angeles.
