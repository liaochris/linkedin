Jason Chesebrough’s Summary

A creative thinker beginning a life in design that traversed from gallery artist to muralist, Chesebrough aims to live & breathe ‘the idea’. His career as a professional muralist had his daily clientele including Molson, Jack Daniels, Sport Chek, The Pickering Town Centre, Alexander Keith’s & a variety of local businesses – until a six month 7,000 square foot stint on a scaffold in late ‘99 caused Chesebrough to rethink his course… he transitioned the fundamentals of his painting techniques into the digital realm.

By 2000 he had started studio:cmd, an interactive shop that garnered industry acclaim, & became a white label studio working on interactive projects with companies from GM to Microsoft, RBC to TVO in addition to holding a client base that included UOIT, John Steinberg & Kingston’s Headstones.

In 2003 he focused on the co-creation of another successful boutique design studio, Nemesis Group, whose clients included Mercedes Benz, ATI, West Coast Choppers, HP & Paul Soles, voice of the original cartoon Spider-Man series. Nemesis was nominated for Canadian Agency of the Year in 05.

In 2005 he took on a position with Dashboard, as ACD – tasked to grow the creative team & bring a powerhouse design shop to light. Through his knowledge of HTML & Flash from a design perspective, plus experience operating his own boutique shops, Chesebrough has been able to form an alliance between creative & developer, equal to the collaborative model found in other shops of art director & copywriter.

Dashboard gained much of its recognition with the help of Chesebrough. He has been instrumental over the past four years, aiding in the growth of a shop of five to thirty people, bringing award winning ideas to life for a client list that spanned from Toyota to Wayne Gretzky.

His commercial & professional work has received numerous awards while Chesebrough himself was recognized as one of ‘Canada’s most influential innovators in the diverse field of media innovation’
