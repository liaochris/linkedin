Ian Waring’s Summary

I guess this is where most people bang their chest and say how great they are. I'm a failed ex-programmer who can still cut code to this day, but find that you don't get invited to bask in the glory of a project if you do the impossible and keep on delivering on time and under budget. No carrot and all stick.

So I moved into various Business Management and Marketing roles - and was in the right places when the business boomed into a multi-million profitable spectacular or where teams I ran had the lowest new customer acquisition costs in their industry. I ensured folks measured everything important, shared my mistakes but kept learning from them well ahead of me being fired. Sometimes business grew with a nuclear bang, and at other times they were a source of good learning material.

I'm blessed with good directors that I work for, good managers and their various teams that work for me, and a long list of people i've worked with in the past that I have the utmost respect for. A handful of them even made it to the Times Rich List. If you're honest, straight forward and enjoy learning new things, then i'm very easy to deal with.

Above all, I try to be synonymous with the word "help". That's probably another mistake - but I guess you wouldn't be here if you were afraid of asking...
