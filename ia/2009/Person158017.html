Mark Chavez’s Summary

Mark’s work in animation spans many years with his first professional work executed in animated laser light in 1980 and projected on a landmark building on Sunset Strip in Los Angeles, California. Other early work in laser animation includes the largest animated projection of laser light, projected onto the huge carvings at Stone Mountain State Park, Georgia, animated while a MFA student in 1983 among other professional-research into computer generated animation and a research project (his MFA thesis project) consisting of the projection of an animated short based on Meso-American mythology onto the Westwood, California Federal Building during the 1984 Summer Olympics. His professional experience includes work in Japan at Tokyo Broadcasting System, at Dreamworks Feature Animation and visual effects work at Rhythm and Hues Studios in Los Angeles. He is proficient at creating stylized cartoon animation and photorealistic visual effects and has worked on many Academy Award winning and nominated projects.

An animator who's interests are in emergent computer animation including sythetic sculpture with computer toolsets and related forms in popular culture. Research interests are in characterization and storytelling with real-time and prerendered imagery exploring visual and behavioral representation in the animated form; including the creation of intelligent animated forms with a richness in personality and emotive evocative states that are flexible enough to respond to the viewer within a predetermined simulated performance.

Mark Chavez’s Specialties:

Visual design, sculpting with computer software, animation, narrative design, real-time system design
