Maria Aretoulaki (PhD)’s Summary

I have been working on speech applications since 1996. I have specialised in adaptive Voice User Interface or VUI design for spontaneous speech and mixed-initiative dialogues, persona design and prompt crafting, Usability testing and application tuning for several industries and languages, but have also acquired extensive experience in Project and People Management: as Head of Speech Design at Vicorp (UK); Head of Dialogue Development at SemanticEdge (Germany); Project Manager at FORWISS and one of the founders of Sympalog (Germany); as owner of DialogCONNECTION (Germany and UK) and through the Gerson Lehrman Group’s Council of Technology Advisors (USA); as EU Post-Doctoral Fellow at the University of Erlangen-Nuremberg (Germany); and as VUI Designer at Clarity AG and 8hertz (Germany) and at Intervoice (UK).

Currently I work as a VUI Design consultant, Speech technology trainer and Copywriter through my own company DialogCONNECTION. I am based in Manchester (UK) but my clients are all over Europe and even the US and Asia.I focus on customer training and consulting on the targeted introduction of speech automation in their business; on the conception, specification, design, localisation, testing and tuning of user-centred and user-friendly but also robust and efficient VUIs.

I hold a BA in Linguistics & English, a MSc in Machine Translation, a PhD in Language Engineering and a Post-Doc in Spoken Dialogue Management, and have authored several publications, including the Chapter on Spoken Dialogue Systems in the Oxford University Press "Handbook of Computational Linguistics". I have been serving in Advisory Committees for EU-funded Speech projects and in the Review Panels of International Conferences and Journals. Originally from Greece, I have spent 9 years in Germany and 8 years in the UK, and I speak English, German, Greek and French fluently.

  • EU HCM Post-Doc Research Fellow

    University of Erlangen-Nuremberg

    (Educational Institution; 1001-5000 employees; Higher Education industry)

    April 1996 — October 1997 (1 year 7 months)

    At the Dept. for Pattern Recognition, I implemented adaptive dialogue strategies in the presence of domain-relevant Out-of-Vocabulary words (OOVs) for the EVAR speech system (SUNDIAL, MSDoS projects). OOVs are statistically determined by the recognizer and assigned a semantic category (city name vs. garbage). City names are then interpreted by the dialogue manager as source or goal locations, accordingly. I programmed new dialogue strategies in PROLOG and formulated appropriate responses: request for repetition to avoid false alarms, then request for spelling, finally, warning and restart. Moreover, I carried out system evaluations and participated actively in international scientific conferences with publications.
