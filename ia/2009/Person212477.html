  • PhD Candidate

    University of Victoria

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    September 2005 — March 2009 (3 years 7 months)

    In my PhD work, I investigated human factors for mapping heterogeneous schemas, specifically ontologies. I conducted a number of user studies where I looked at how people work with mapping tools, what process they follow, what tools they use, and how people work in team environments. Based on these studies, I discovered a number of user information needs and designed a model that describes the opportunities tool developers have for aiding users during the mapping process. I used this model to develop my own mapping tool interface called CogZ.

    I have completed my thesis defense and will officially graduate in May 2009.
