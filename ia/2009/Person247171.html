Ruth Wikler-Luker’s Summary

Nonprofit performing arts professional with experience and expertise as a manager, scholar and artist. I bring dynamism and initiative, creativity and cultural knowledge, and fundraising skills and experience to the work of performing arts management, production and programming, with a particular interest in working with performing arts presenters and community cultural organizations. My scholarly work has an international focus, investigating the relationship between the performing arts and society through areas like cultural policy and community-based arts. My artistic work has focused on using circus and theater to create innovative expressions of cultural and social content.

Ruth Wikler-Luker’s Specialties:

Fundraising and development (including government); project management and development; performing arts producing and programming; research and writing; theater directing and workshop facilitation. Multilingual.

Ruth Wikler-Luker’s Experience

  • Board Member

    Bond Street Theatre

    (Performing Arts industry)

    2008 — Present (1 year)

    Bond Street Theatre, based in New York City, is "a physical theatre company with a social consciousness, a global view, and a sense of humor." Check out their web site to find out about their important humanitarian theatre work in post-conflict areas around the world.

  • Principal

    Project Management, Development & Grant Writing Services for the Performing Arts

    (Performing Arts industry)

    2001 — Present (8 years)

    I work on a freelance and consultant basis with nonprofit performing arts organizations in the United States and abroad to conceive, produce and raise funds for innovative projects and programs. Areas of expertise include performing arts presenting, community-based and neighborhood arts, cross-disciplinary collaborations, the creative campus, community engagement, international arts and cultural exchange, theatre, circus, music and dance.

  • Manager of Government Relations

    Brooklyn Philharmonic

    (Performing Arts industry)

    2005 — August 2008 (3 years)

    I managed grants and contracts in connection with federal, state and local arts agencies; liaised with the public sector at all levels, building awareness of the Philharmonic's education and community programs around the borough of Brooklyn; and led the government component of the Brooklyn Philharmonic's current capital campaign. I worked closely with the Education and Community Engagement department to build new programs and ensure programmatic compliance with legislative priorities. I previously served as the Brooklyn Philharmonic's Development Associate.

  • Artistic Director

    Cirque Boom Circus Theater

    (Performing Arts industry)

    January 2002 — May 2008 (6 years 5 months)

    I founded Cirque Boom in 2002 as a circus theater troupe dedicated to making "content-driven" circus theater: circus that matters and theater that amazes. I conceived, produced and directed original full-length performance works such as Madness and Joy! (inspired by Leaves of Grass); The Hoffmann Circus (a circus-opera version of The Tales of Hoffmann); and The Circus of Vices and Virtues (inspired by Pieter Breugel's Vices and Virtues prints series). Cirque Boom is currently on hiatus, but keep checking our web site for updates!

  • Program/Development Associate

    Arts International

    (Performing Arts industry)

    1999 — 2004 (5 years)

    At Arts International, I provided fund raising and administrative support to projects such as the MexicoNow Festival (now Celebrate Mexico Now), the International Hispanic Theatre Festival (New York edition), the International Presenters Forum, the Arts and Minds Conference on Cultural Diplomacy, Artists in Context; and the Global Partnerships Initiative, among many others.
