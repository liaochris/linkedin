Bob Petrie’s Summary

I am currently pursuing opportunities as an executive in an early stage technology company. To date I have completed two full startup cycles (launch, growth, exit) and have provided leadership in all phases of the early-stage lifecycle including business planning, capitalization, launch, go-to-market, growth, restructuring and exit.

My career focus is on business operations management and all that it encompasses. I have demonstrated the ability to develop a consistent company vision and then design, integrate and coordinate the organization required to attain that vision. I view Excel as a killer app and I love the challenge of modeling a business in a way that makes the mechanics of that particular business evident. I have served as a Chief Operating Officer (COO) of a public company and have owned the responsibility for a wide variety organizational functions including P&L management, sales, marketing, engineering, professional services and customer support.

In 1998 I received a Masters Degree in Technology Management from the University of Pennsylvania's EMTM program: an MSE program from Penn Engineering, co-sponsored by the Wharton School of Business.

I have always been passionate about technology. At one time or another I have been a self-taught programmer, a computer science undergraduate, a technology consultant, a CASE expert and an application developer. I've spent the past 10 years focused on Internet technology and services, in a variety of forms: managed services, licensed software, software-as-a-service (SaaS). I am intrigued by the promise of a number of emerging technologies, including but not limited to cloud computing, wireless, rich media, data visualization, RFID and security.

Although I have little desire to work for a VC firm, I think I share many perspectives of the typical VC: rapid value creation, risk/reward appetite, emerging market exploitation and aversion to lifestyle businesses.
