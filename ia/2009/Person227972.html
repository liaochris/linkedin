  • Principal Research Scientist

    Roche Bioscience

    (Pharmaceuticals industry)

    July 1995 — February 2006 (10 years 8 months)

    Supervision of one staff level scientist and six senior level chemists in the planning, writing and execution of in vivo and in vitro studies in the support of drug discovery IND enabling and clinical studies. Supervision of the writing and scheduling of animal and in vitro study protocols, GXP method development, data analysis and report submission. Discovery project support involves screening of compounds for bioavailability, metabolism and pharmacokinetic properties, both in vivo and in vitro. Data is routinely presented at senior level management meetings in support of project updates. Instrumental in expanding the departments in vitro techniques by implementing the use of dynamic organ culture using tissue slices. Studies using culture of liver, and kidney slices has lead to the use of this new technique in the study of drug-drug interactions and in the isolation and identification of both phase I and phase II metabolites.
    Programs included efforts to design a bioavailable prodrug of ganciclovir resulting in co-inventor patents and clinical development of Valcyte for treatment of CMV infection. Research in the metabolism of matrix metalloproteinase inhibitors led the metabolism/PK/bioanalytical programs of Roche Bioscience resulting in the discovery of novel compounds for the treatment of osteoarthritis and emphysema.
    Director of the MBC mass spectrometry facility supervising the training and maintenance of the departments current instruments. The facility includes Finnigan LCQ, TSQ 7000, Micromass LCZ, ZMD, Quattro Ultima and Sciex API4000 instruments. Developed high throughput methods utilizing robotic sample preparation coupled to LC/MS/MS for GXP studies.

Scott Womble, D.Sc.’s Education

  • Vanderbilt University School of Medicine

    Medicinal Chemistry, Clinical Pharmacology, Mass Spectrometry 1989 — 1992

    Research Fellow in Clinical Pharmacology, Vanderbilt University, Mass Spectrometry Resource, Department of Pharmacology. Research was supervised by Dr. Ian A. Blair, Resource Director.
    Extensive in vitro studies were undertaken in collaboration with Bristol-Myers Squibb on the metabolism of several antipsychotic drugs using rat and human liver incubations. Kinetic in vitro studies were undertaken to determine the human P450 responsible for the metabolism of the antipsychotic agent Buspirone. Kinetic studies utilizing both chemical and antibody inhibition of specific P450 isozymes were employed.
    Additional areas of study included the synthesis of a series of Amiloride analogs containing a photoactivatable group which was used in the radiolabeling the specific amiloride binding site of the a2-adrenergic receptor.
    Supervision of the Mass Spectrometry Resource’s instrumentation personnel.

    Activities and Societies:
    1989-1992 National Institutes of Health Training Grant in Molecular Toxicology.

  • U. of Houston/M.D. Anderson

    Medicinal Chemistry 1988 — 1989

    Postdoctoral fellowship at the University of Houston under the direction of Dr. Edward M. Acton of the National Cancer Institute and Dr. Steven C. Welch of the University of Houston. This research involved the synthesis of anthracycline C-glycosyl isosteres using traditional and regioselective Diels-Alder reactions.

  • The University of Texas at Arlington

    D.Sc. , Organic Chemistry , 1982 — 1987

    Research under the direction of Dr. Andrew L. Ternay, Jr. (UTA) involving the synthesis of unsymmetric disulfides for biological evaluation. This research was funded by the Department of Defense (DOD) and the compounds synthesized were to be tested for antiradiation activity. This work involved the synthesis of thiol containing steroids, amino acids, peptides and polymers. These thiol compounds were then coupled, via disulfide bonds, to known radioprotective compounds for biological evaluation.
    Additional work was done on the synthesis and conformational analysis of substituted thianthrenium ylides.

  • Texas Christian University

    BS , Organic Chemistry , 1977 — 1982

    Undergraduate Research under the direction of Dr. William B. Smith involving the study of 4,4'-bis disubstituted geminal dihalides and their spontaneous oxidation to ketones in an O2 atmosphere.
