Jeff Forristal’s Summary

I am a professional technology architect and strategist with over a decade of experience in the security industry. I dually function in both a technical contributor role and a strategic business role. Throughout my professional career I have been responsible for conceiving new security service offerings, developing industry-first and market-leading product features, and driving research into new security industry areas. I am an accomplished writer having written multiple features and cover-story articles for Network Computing and Secure Enterprise magazines; I am also a contributing author to multiple books. Under the pseudonym “Rain Forest Puppy,” I have been recognized as an industry expert in web application security and was responsible for the first publicized responsible security disclosure policy and the first intelligent web application scanner. I have presented my research in many forums, from established security events like BlackHat and CanSecWest to smaller regional conferences around the world.

A detailed account of my various published works is available on my personal site, www.forristal.com.

Jeff Forristal’s Specialties:

Long-term security technology strategy; innovation leading to new markets and market-leading opportunities within the security industry

Jeff Forristal’s Experience

  • Security Validation Engineer

    Intel

    (Public Company; INTC; Semiconductors industry)

    September 2009 — Present (5 months)

  • Co-Founder and Advising Director of Security Research

    Wiretrip Research

    (Non-Profit; Computer & Network Security industry)

    1998 — Present (12 years )

    Wiretrip is a non-profit collective of security and technology professionals engaged in forward-thinking applied security research activities and the pursuit of disruptive and revolutionary technology for multiple industries. As the Advising Director of Security Research, I oversee and review all research initiatives and act as primary liaison between Wiretrip and all third-parties. I was personally responsible for research and activities which included the first responsible security disclosure policy (RFPolicy), the first intelligent web application security scanner (Whisker), and the earliest documented discussion concerning SQL injection (Phrack magazine, 1998). My early leading research in the emerging web application security space incurred invites to security industry events; I have publicly presented at BlackHat, CanSecWest, and smaller regional security conferences held in the US, Ireland, France, Singapore, Hong Kong, and Australia. I have also engaged in privately-requested presentations, research advisement, and technology analyses on behalf of multiple organizations and universities. I was a contributing author to the inaugural book “Hack-Proofing Your Network” in Syngress’s now-popular “Hack-Proofing” series.

  • Senior Security Research Engineer

    Zscaler

    (Privately Held; Computer & Network Security industry)

    March 2008 — August 2009 (1 year 6 months)

    Zscaler is a SaaS provider offering end user security protection and business policy enforcement. My role as a Senior Security Research Engineer was to conceptualize, investigate, and implement industry-first security features aligned with the vision and strategy of the Zscaler service offering. The course of my work led me to be listed as sole inventor or co-inventor on at least six pending US patents. I also provided technically-focused marketing collateral and pursued demonstrations of thought leadership through online publishing and speaking engagements.

  • Senior R&D Engineer

    HP (via SPI Dynamics acquisition)

    (Computer & Network Security industry)

    August 2007 — April 2008 (9 months)

    I became employed at HP through the acquisition of SPI Dynamics. My position, role, and responsibilities at HP were a continuation of my previous position at SPI Dynamics.

  • Senior Security Research Engineer

    SPI Dynamics

    (Privately Held; Computer & Network Security industry)

    January 2007 — August 2007 (8 months)

    SPI Dynamics is a security software company most known for the WebInspect web application security scanner. The very first versions of WebInspect incorporated data and concepts (with permission) from my earlier research and development of the Whisker web scanner. I occupied the position of Senior Security Research Engineer in SPI Labs, where my initial role was to research and develop new industry-first features aligned with SPI Dynamic products and to contribute to various marketing efforts and demonstrations of thought leadership. Eventually I grew into a product expert role as I became intimate with product internals; I leveraged that expert product knowledge in product support situations and customer education campaigns including a session at the SPI Dynamics customer conference, SPICon. I also inherited the responsibility of maintaining and creating appropriate application security assessment signatures (“checks”) for the SPI Dynamics product line. Through expert re-evaluation and re-organization of the product signature collections, I was able to increase product efficiency and accuracy while also reducing the size of the signature collection. Later I successfully developed and implemented methodologies, processes, and training to convert on-going signature maintenance responsibilities from a limited individual-contributor task to a scalable team-wide process supported by multiple contributors. I lead and managed the team-wide signature effort up to the time of my departure.

  • Lead Security Consultant

    Neohapsis

    (Privately Held; Computer & Network Security industry)

    1999 — December 2006 (7 years )

    Neohapsis (prior to merging with KSR in 2006) was a boutique security services and product testing firm. Even though I was officially employed a bit after initial company formation, I was informally involved and contributing to Neohapsis since inception. My official responsibilities at Neohapsis included senior and lead consulting roles to deliver best-of-breed security services to Fortune and global companies as well as smaller organizations. Throughout the growth of Neohapsis, I was responsible for defining and implementing technical methodologies and processes necessary to deliver marketed services; this included training and mentoring other consultants in proper methodology, and educating the marketing and project management teams regarding what could conceptually be offered and how it could be positioned as a differentiator. In some cases, I was responsible for originating entire new service offerings. As with many small startups where individuals “wear multiple hats,” I unofficially played and contributed to other roles included marketing, business strategy, and project management. Notable projects I lead or participated in while at Neohapsis included an assessment of a large-scale ERP solution landscape, an extremely large-scale network assessment (encompassing over one million IP addresses), a federal DITSCAP accreditation of a medical system, and multiple venture capital firm pre-investment technology evaluations. I also performed hardware security evaluations; highlighted projects include multiple encrypted storage devices and an enterprise-class distributed wireless networking solution.
