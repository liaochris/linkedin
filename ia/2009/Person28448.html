  • Consultant to NASD (STAR)

    Waterford Associates, LLC

    (Information Technology and Services industry)

    May 2004 — October 2007 (3 years 6 months)

    Client: National Association of Securities Dealers (NASD)
    System for Tracking Activity for RPO (STAR)

    The STAR project was the latest incarnation of several unsuccessful attempts to consolidate case management systems across the Regulatory Policy Organization. The STAR project was off to a shaky start and I was asked to assume leadership of the project. After re-basing the project, we successfully developed, tested and deployed the initial release on time and within budget, followed by three major additional releases over two years. The success of the STAR project rationalized case management business processes; provided consistent reporting of case status; improved accuracy and responsiveness to the SEC, Congress, and member firms; and retired eleven stove-piped legacy case management systems.

Directed application development, testing, infrastructure build-out, staffing, and contract management. Coordinated teams in Sweden (OMX - development); Rockville, MD (functional, integration, and acceptance test); and Manhattan (interface). Directed negotiations with NYSE, NASDAQ, SIAC, and other securities industry organizations for successful deployment and systems interoperability. Sought and received SEC approval for technology deployment. System delivered in just seven months, receiving a Computerworld award for innovative technology. Subsequently directed successful negotiations for outsourcing production infrastructure and operations services to OMX.
