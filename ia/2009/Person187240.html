Phillip J. Hoying’s Summary

I recently graduated from Hamilton College with an undergraduate degree in government and music. In addition to maintaining high-level academic work, I was extremely involved in extracurricular activities. From singing and playing trombone, to playing ultimate frisbee and volunteering, I tried to be as active in my community as possible.

Volunteering has been a passion, as well as an opportunity for leadership and creativity. On the executive board of Hamilton's largest volunteering group, HAVOC, I have served as the staff coordinator and the publicity director, where I redesigned web layout and lead a complete re-branding of the organization's image, slogan, and presence on campus. During HAVOC service days, I supervised other volunteers while we painted, cleaned, organized -- whatever needed to be done. I also continue to volunteer bi-monthly to edit articles for StreetSense, a street paper based in Washington, DC.

During the school year, I worked between 6-10 hours per week for the Alumni Review. During the summers, I worked full-time writing and editing for all areas of the media department. (Several articles are available below in the Alumni Review folder.)

In Spring 2008, I studied at Hamilton's Washington, DC, program. There I became active in homeless issues and worked for a women's homeless shelter. I eventually wrote my thesis on a related topic. (My senior thesis is available below - phoyinggovthesis.pdf)

When I returned to Hamilton in Fall 2008, I participated in the inaugural HANSA (Hamilton Association for Nonprofit Strategic Advancement) program, which allowed me to work at a national non-profit in an executive-level strategic planning position.

As a creative outlet at Hamilton, I founded and directed the X-Viper Hour, a student organization that performs live audio theater over the radio and in front of live audiences. (A few varied styles available below in the X-Viper Hour folder.)
