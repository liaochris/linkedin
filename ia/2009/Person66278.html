Doug Price’s Summary

Some have said that I am a computer nerd in an athletes body. I'm flattered, but all kidding aside, I am a driven individual and passionate in everything I do. Perhaps sometimes to my detriment, I am a perfectionist. However, I believe that work should be of superior quality and done right the first time. In nearly all cases, this trait has always served my clients and colleagues extremely well. I am also blessed (or cursed) with the gift of photographic memory (selective according to my wife...smile).

Professionally, as a senior manager at a large corporation and as a business owner myself, I have seen and done it all. Well, most of it, anyway...wink. I am a professionally trained leader, having graduated from the United States Air Force Academy in the top 1/3 of my class. Subsequent to my time as an officer in the Air Force, I quickly rose through the ranks at Seton Company, the worlds largest manufacturer of automobile leather, to become the Manager of Global Enterprise Systems. I architected, staffed and managed several high dollar projects on a global scale. Currently, I own and operate Price Corporation, which runs a successful online retail store, www.PartyFavorMall.com, as well as serve as full-time Chief Software Architect for StomperNet, a leading SEO and e-Commerce training company.

I am an expert with Telligent Community (formerly Community Server) software and also InfusionSoft (Customer Management System and e-Commerce/Marketing Software). Additionally, I have become a recognized expert with RFID technology, being invited as the guest speaker at the 2006 Business Information Technology Summit and serving on the board of corporate advisors to the RFID Center of Excellence at Penn State University.

And yes, believe it or not, I get along well with others and have a personality (sometimes not always the case with computer geeks...smile).

Thank you for your time in reading my profile and I look forward to connecting with you.

~Doug

Doug Price’s Experience

  • Chief Software Architect

    StomperNet, LLC

    (E-Learning industry)

    January 2009 — Present (9 months)

    Founded by legendary marketers Brad Fallon and Andy Jenkins, StomperNet, LLC is an international leader for Search Engine Optimization (SEO), Internet Marketing and e-Commerce training. What started for me as a contract with them through the consulting firm I own (Price Corporation) has since turned into a full-time gig as their Chief Software Architect (or their "secret weapon" as they like to refer to me...smile).

    On the public side, I successfully architected, managed and helped develop a customized Learning Management System (LMS) implementation built on the Telligent Community (formerly Community Server) platform. These private client portals utilize a shared membership system so that clients are maintained in a single database but can participate in different courses offered. (my.stompernet.com and f5.stompernet.com)

    Behind the scenes, I have built middleware software that has completely integrated their InfusionSoft Customer Management System (CMS) with the public facing Telligent Community membership portal. This has allowed StomperNet to completely eliminate their outsourced customer service and reduce in-house man hours by over 50%.

    I am also the Site Administrator for their elite membership portal, which I am currently working on migrating to the Telligent Community platform. The old portal was a DotNetNuke installation upon which I designed and helped develop numerous module applications including the media library system that delivers several terabytes worth of content to our members monthly.

  • Founder and CEO

    Price Corporation

    (Computer Software industry)

    March 2005 — Present (4 years 7 months)

    I am the Founder and CEO of Price Corporation. Price Corporation began in 2005 as a consulting firm in the Northeast region of the United States specializing in Radio Frequency Identification (RFID) technologies. We were instrumental in helping many small businesses come into compliance with Department of Defense and retail RFID mandates. I became a recognized expert in the RFID industry being asked to speak at the 2006 Northeast Regional Business Information Technology Summit. Additionally, I was a member of the corporate panel of advisors to the RFID Center of Excellence at Penn State University.

    With the ISO certification of the RFID air interface protocol, the cost (and profit margins) plummeted as the specification became public record. Quickly recognizing this paradigm shift within the RFID industry, I adjusted course and shifted the focus of our company towards e-Commerce with Microsoft .Net technologies. In 2006, we landed a contract with StomperNet, LLC, an international leader in Search Engine Optimization (SEO), Internet Marketing and e-Commerce training. We integrated their internal systems, built their customer facing private client portal (on both DotNetNuke and Telligent Community Server platforms), and also built numerous shopping carts for their various product launches. I also currently serve in a full-time capacity as their Chief Software Architect for ongoing projects.

    Building on experience gained through our contract work with StomperNet, in 2007, we launched Party Favor Mall (www.PartyFavorMall.com), an online retailer for party supplies, party favors and wedding favors. This is a highly competitive market and we have shown steady growth from this business.

    I feel that my leadership and focus has helped Price Corporation survive the technology startup curse. Whereas most technology startups fail, we are now in our fourth year of business and posted our first profit last fiscal year.

  • Manager of Global Enterprise Systems

    Seton Company

    (Privately Held; Automotive industry)

    January 2002 — April 2005 (3 years 4 months)

    I rose through the ranks quickly from Senior Applications Engineer when I started to Senior technology manager for the leading global auto leather manufacturing company. I was responsible for coordinating with individual facility management teams worldwide to provide design, implementation and support services that focus on information automation for Seton’s Americas, European and South African operations. I provided technology budgeting, staffing and project decisions/recommendations to top executives. I was also responsible for management of all client/server and AS/400 programming staff. Reported to the Director of Information Technology.

    My most significant achievements include spearheading the negotiation and acquisition of vendors, planning and engineering of a production line yield control system RFID pilot project keeping initial project costs under $50K. Additionally, I was an instrumental player in developing a new capital expense submission and approval system, an employee evaluation system, and completing payroll data conversion from internal PDS Vista system to ADP during outsourcing transition for over 600 U.S. employees.
