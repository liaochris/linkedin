Lisa Johnson’s Summary

Currently, I research a variety of topics as a freelance writer for the legal document website LegalZoom and I'm doing some social media consulting for Cross Cultural Collaborative, an educational non-profit. I’m a licensed Massachusetts attorney and an intuitive SEO writer with a knack for finding original stories.

In addition, I blog about my life, food, current events, and share my photography at Anali’s First Amendment, the blog that I founded in 2006, while working full-time as a Contract Attorney. I’ve added a review arm to my blog called Anali Says, where I also review products and services.

My interest in food and writing has led me to participate in the blogging community through involvement in blog events, writing guest posts, and contributing recipes to two cookbooks that will be published. I wrote a food blog called "And Razzleberry Dressing" for the EPpy award winning website BoomerGirl. I also wrote a guest post for The ConverStation, the blog for WBUR, Boston’s local NPR station.

I’ve written book reviews for the Masschusetts Bar Association’s Lawyers Journal and contributed restaurant reviews for the book Hungry?Boston.

Previous work subject matter includes: state and local sales and use tax, fire codes, life insurance, professional discipline, white collar crime, and discrimination impacting housing, public accomodations, and credit.

My forte: I’m very, very, curious.

I’m seeking opportunities for collaboration. I’d like to use my legal experience, affinity for research and writing, along with my love of food, blogging, photography, and social media. Is this possible? I sure hope so!
