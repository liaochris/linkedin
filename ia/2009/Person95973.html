Howie Chang’s Summary

A designer, entrepreneur, technologist, lecturer and husband living in Singapore. Howie is the Founder and Principal of Tera Eikon, a development and design agency building web applications for people like you.

Howie is also Academic Associate at the School of Technology for the Arts, Republic Polytechnic. His facilitation modules include Anthropological Studies, Interaction Design and New Media Enterprise.

Before venturing out, he was with Creative Technology Ltd as a Visual and Interaction Designer for the Graphical User Interface group and have been influential in laying the early foundations for Creative’s entrance into the next generation of web services. Prior to Creative, he was an Application Engineer with Giesecke & Devrient, a leading supplier of banknote paper, banknote printing, currency automation systems, as well as smart cards and complex system solutions.

He is known among his peers as someone who is passionate in all things digital and has deep appreciation for what he calls “thoughtful” interactions. He enjoys engaging in social slipstreaming and finds fulfillment in spending time with his loved ones. Besides work, Howie spends the rest of his time being actively involved in his church and holds the leadership role for The Digital Movement; a thriving, not-for-profit organization striving to build and connect thought leaders in the digital space.

Born in Penang, Malaysia in 1983, Howie received his formal education at Nanyang Polytechnic (Singapore), where he graduated with a Diploma in Multimedia Infocommunication Technology and subsequently received his Bachelor of Science in Multimedia Technology & Design at SIM University (Singapore). He is also a recipient of the King Scout Award, the highest rank in the Boy Scouts of Malaysia.

He recently got married to Joy and they now live in the westside of Singapore.
