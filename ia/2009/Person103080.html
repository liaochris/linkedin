James T. Parsons’s Summary

For those that have read all the above, and end up reading all that is below, I might provide a more poetic vision of the need for people to connect: "... though much is taken, much abides, and though we are not now that strength which in old days moved - earth and heaven, - that which we are we are! One equal temper of heroic hearts made weak by time and fate, but strong in will to strive, to seek, to find - and not to yield." (William Wordsworth). If we as a community continue to prosper, we must not allow modern life to lead to isolation, for none of us is an island. We must find common ground to make life better and more connected. To that goal I in all things serve.

As an attorney and connector, I have many skills that will benefit the companies for which I work, and the non-profits and causes I serve. The growing size and diversity of this database of connections I think speak to that skill.
