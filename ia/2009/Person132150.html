Kimberly Krisch (kimberly.krisch@gmail.com)’s Summary

My consultative selling & communications skills equipped me as a consistent sales performer to earn corporate recognition, bonuses & increased responsibility. Willingness to learn, be coachable, refine my skills and share best practices & proven tactics were critical too.

A "Sales Development Manager" role was created twice for me. Detailed objectives re: where/how I spent my time & resources were key to providing quantifiable value to field professionals.

I measure success by value added contributions & justified ROI. Meeting goals, developing teams or reporting results, to be of service is a given. I know my greatest competition is “me” and this challenges me to grow my expectations, benchmarks and performance.

Communicating detailed tasks premiere front line sales pros perform to differentiate themselves & consistently exceed goals has huge value. Experience proved that leveraging my writing skills to effectively capture, synthesize, distill and document steps reps took to ensure success resulted in stories with purpose.

The written word, stories about people we know is powerful. It opens doors to acknowledge individuals for contributions made. Sharing innovative approaches to doing business differently also inspires reps to take risks, try new ideas.

Sales & Marketing groups used my writing to create business strategies, increase team efficiencies, develop clear communications and recognize real people. Tools, resources, collateral materials, executive presentations and course curriculum are other outcomes.

Nothing justifies “time out of the field” for a manager more than their team working w/ "real world practical training exercises & business applications." 10/10 course ratings, increased rep productivity and sales were proof.

Learning, growing, sharing: best practices, information, tools & resources translates to time well spent, positive energy, contagious attitudes, skill building and cultures that ensure “everyone wins!”
