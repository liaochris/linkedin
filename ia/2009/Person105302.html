Jason Ojalvo’s Summary

Mr. Ojalvo has 15 years of business development, marketing, entrepreneurial, corporate strategy, and writing experience, with a strong focus in the music and new media industries. He has guest-lectured at universities, spoken on international music business panels, and appeared on national network news several times to discuss the future of the music business.

Ojalvo began his career by helping lead the marketing and strategic initiatives for Disc Makers, America's leading designer and manufacturer of CDs for indie labels and artists. While there he founded and ran numerous artist-friendly projects including the world's largest unsigned band contest and co-authored the book "Finding Fans and Selling CDs." He also founded "Fast Forward," the world’s most widely-circulated music industry newsletter (circulation 200,000) offering career advice for professional musicians, and served as editor from its inception in 1993 until 1996.

From 1997 to 1999, Ojalvo worked with consulting firm PricewaterhouseCoopers on traditional and new media projects in corporate strategy, including work for record labels, sports leagues, publishers, and cable networks.

After stints in business development and marketing at the early social networking / user-generated content site Bolt, as well as the fitness company Fluidity, Ojalvo launched the London office of digital music distributor, The Orchard, in 2003. After returning to the US, Ojalvo ran their marketing and business development efforts, growing distribution and retail partnerships to help record label clients get premier placement on digital music services like iTunes, eMusic, and Napster.

He is currently SVP at Audible, where he oversees Audible's exclusive partnership with Apple/iTunes and the company's short-form and non-book content development and acquisition.

Ojalvo holds a bachelors degree from Lafayette College in Pennsylvania and an MBA from Columbia University in New York.
