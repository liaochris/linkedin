Rick Riehle’s Summary

Company founder interested in promoting organics, Fair Trade, and other triple bottom line business practices. Particularly interested in promoting and developing local, self sustaining cooperative businesses.

Cooperatives directly address the economic and social problems that concern many progressive thinkers. For example, cooperatives mitigate income disparity because revenues are equitably distributed among members. This lessens the bifurcation of society brought about about by extreme income disparity as we have seen in the United States over the last few decades. Cooperatives promote equity by putting ownership of the means of production within reach of regular people willing to pool resources. Cooperatives promote democracy and accountability on the basis of one vote to each member rather than one vote to each share. Cooperatives are vastly more robust and prevalent than most people realize—if taken as a group they would represent the ninth largest economy in the world—yet they are still overlooked and misunderstood. I seek to change that.
