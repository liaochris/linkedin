Brent Pope’s Summary

So this is where people usually write about themselves in the third person. What? Wasn't there a Seinfeld episode about that? "Jimmy likes Elaine." Whatever.

Can't we all agree that we don't have to do something a certain way just because that's the way everybody else is doing it? Okay, sure, you could say what you need to say just like everyone else, and yes, you'd be covering your, uh, bases, by doing it that way. But then, wouldn't you just be part of the clutter? And more importantly, would you really and truly be communicating?

I don't think so. And since that's not the approach I take when helping clients solve their marketing communications problems, I'm not going to do it here. Yeah, I know all my job titles scream "career in advertising." But really, what I do is help clients "say" the right thing to the right people in situations where the message has a shot at being heard and acted upon. That's how you build brands. And that's what I do.
