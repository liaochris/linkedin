Holly Hester-Reilly’s Summary

Eager professional seeking an early career environmental engineering position that involves team-based project work. Areas of focus may include air quality, hazardous waste, drinking water and wastewater treatment, separation processes, process design, and feasibility studies.

Enthusiastic about transitioning to an environmental focus; motivated by knowledge of positive societal impact. Completed senior design team project, Remediation of arsenic from drinking water in rural Bangladesh using locally available resources, which won departmental award. Coursework included alternative energy resources and advanced surface and colloid science.

Key strengths: Strong research skills and passion for learning new things. Acutely analytical; innovative and creative problem solver. High empathy and emotional intelligence; experienced community leader and team player. Skilled communicator, writer, and presenter. Well organized and detail-oriented. Practiced in learning new computer programs and technologies.

~~~~~~~

Holly is a young alumni of Columbia University, having earned her BS in Chemical Engineering in 2006 and an MS in that field in 2008. In her six years at Columbia, she worked on the board of numerous clubs in the undergraduate and engineering communities, holding various roles involving mentorship, outreach, event planning and coordination, team management, and program development. She gained four years of experience in scientific research, during which she was first author on a paper published in a peer-reviewed journal.
