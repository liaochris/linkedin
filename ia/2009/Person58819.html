Dave Shimada’s Summary

David Shimada is the Local Marketing Director, Team Lead for KQED Public Broadcasting. As a Local Marketing Director, he engages corporations and organizations to sponsor – all of KQED’s media properties and community outreach and oversees all interactive product development. David helps lead a team as the top sales producer.

David has spoken/presented at several of PBS and NPR national annual conferences, the National Public Radio Development (NPRDC) and Integrated Media Association (Integratedmedia.org) Conferences. He has also written several articles for the DEI on sponsorship public radio’s fund raising and marketing service organization serving over 245 national public radio stations. He is also part of DEI’s Online Sponsorship Toolkit Advisory Team responsible for building a national toolkit meant to guide the online sponsorship neophyte step-by-step through creating an online sponsorship program. In addition David is past president and current board member of the San Francisco American Marketing Association (SFAMA.org), an Advisory Board member for UC Berkeley Extension and has served on the board for the Direct Marketing Association DMANC.org.

David is an executive with over 10 years experience in revenue generation, strengthening relationships, market share and profitability in businesses.

David has a MBA from the Ageno School of Business - Golden Gate University and a BA in Marketing from San Diego State University.
