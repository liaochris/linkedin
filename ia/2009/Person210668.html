Sav Remzi’s Summary

Sav is responsible for many critically acclaimed projects since the early 90's, ranging from establishing his own venues/clubs & record labels, to artist management, A&R, festival, radio and TV productions.

In 1992, Remzi founded the prolific label Nuphonic, which released over 170 records through the 90's and grew to become a highly revered label that defined the British 'Nu House' movement.

Between 1991 & 1994, he created and developed the Blue Note Club in Hoxton Square Shoreditch, a 500 capacity club which was the first venue in the area and widely hailed as the catalyst that triggered the gentrification of Shoreditch.

Between 2000 - 2002 Remzi produced the London Xpress Radio Shows on Xfm, which successfully launched the then 'Indie' station towards new dance audiences.

Between 2004 - 2006, he fronted Groove Armada's Lovebox Festival, facilitating the events' move to its current home in East London's Victoria Park.

He set up his Disco label, Tirk Recordings in 2003, where he discovered various hit bands in Mercury nominee’s New Young Pony Club and Fujiya & Miyagi. Sav continues to release cutting edge music on his label which is now hailed as one of the UK's premier Disco labels.

Remzi recently merged with ITCH Agency to create a new music division called INTONE aimed at servicing brands with their music strategy mandates. Through an understanding of brands needs and established relationships within the music industry, Intone successfully source, negotiated and unite brands and music at all levels.

Intone clients include Intel, Second Life, Virgin Media (V Shorts & Road To V), Pernod Ricard (Jamesons & Havana Club), NFL, GSMA, Cubo, The Guardian...

Clearly, Savs' many years in the music business has provided him with an extensive network, allowing him access to some of the finest producers and talent in the world.

    TIRK RECORDINGS

    (Music industry)

    2003 — Present (6 years)

    Remzi founded his own record label Tirk Records in 2003. The label's first signing, New Young Pony Club caused an immediate sensation with their singles 'Ice Cream' and 'The Get Go' and were nominated for the Murcury Prize 2007. His second signing subsequently unearthed another hit band in Fujiya & Miyagi with their album Transparent Things, which further established the label as a 'hot-house' of dynamic British talent. The Tirk roster also includes dance acts such as Maurice Fulton (Syclops), Tom Findley (Groove Armada), Idjut Boys, Greg Wilson, Chaz Jankel, Martin Rushent, Drrtyhaze, Love Supreme, Arcade Lover, Richard Norris, Morten Sorensen, Sugardaddy, Richard Sen, Todd Terje and many others. http://www.tirk.co.uk. In 2007, having been noted for his A&R success with the label, Sav merged Tirk Records with independent new-players MRC.

  • DIRECTOR

    NUPHONIC RECORDS

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Music industry)

    1993 — 2002 (9 years)

    Gripped by the business of music, Sav went on to found the record label, Nuphonic Records in 1993. Nuphonic's first release, Faze Action's In The Trees, bacame the first of many club classics released by the label. In the following years, the label went on to release over 140 releases and defined the British 'Nu House' movement. The label earnt itself a reputation as a primary source of quality ground breaking music.
    Nuphonic ceased operations in 2002 due to the then uncontrollable rise in free downloads and the resulting drop of physical sales of its catalogue.
