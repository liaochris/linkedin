Benjamin Stone’s Experience

  • Senior Vice President & General Counsel

    Indego Africa Project

    (Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    September 2008 — Present (1 year 3 months)

    Oversee Indego Africa's PR, Legal, Fundraising, Sales & Marketing, Regional & Advisory Boards, Volunteers, Graphic Design, Events, Partnerships, and Rwandan Training Program initiatives. Guest speaker at various academic institutions and conferences about leadership and the intersection between law and social enterprise. Chief Photographer for Indego Africa with exhibits in NYC, Chicago and DC.

    Indego Africa - currently the subject of a Harvard Business School case study - is an innovative social enterprise with a simple yet powerful business model ~

    (a) partner with cooperatives of world-class artisans in Rwanda;

    (b) sell their masterful handicrafts – from stylish yoga bags to vibrant wine coasters to exquisitely hand-crafted baskets – at high-end retailers across the U.S. and on Indego Africa’s webstore;

    (c) pay the women a fair wage, including 50% up front;

    (d) return 100% of the profits for training in culturally-tailored training programs in financial management, accounting, computer skills, global commerce, and literacy;

    (e) harness local talent to conduct the training programs through a ground-breaking internship program with Orphans of Rwanda International (ORI), a nonprofit that provides university scholarships to high-achieving orphans.
