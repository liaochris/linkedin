Alex Levitch’s Summary

I was trained as a prosecuting attorney to isolate the critical issues, measure truth and marshal a persuasive case -- all valuable skills for a transactor, as well an entrepreneur.

My first venture was created, as so many are, out of whole cloth and credit card lines. It became: a Harvard Business School case re a gamut of issues (triple digit growth, global distribution, unionization, patent suit, financing, multiple distribution channels...); a "Street MBA" for me; and a solid double for the VC's.

If my first venture proved I had a gift for bringing people and capital together around a single vision, it would take a day job, next, as an executive recruiter with a role in the firm’s adjunct investment arm, to teach me how to tell an average manager from an excellent one, as well a good investment from a bad one.

If you’re lucky, the formative years stick with you. And so they have with me. I’ve gone on to co-found additional ventures (e.g., the first supermarket based music distribution company, forever-green thousand acre ownership resorts, Internet based teacher professional development). I’ve played a significant leadership role in the acquisition of a major US airline; and I have co-founded one of the first of new Russia’s integrated financial houses.

There, in Russia, we worked around the clock in nine time zones, engaged in projects that ran the gamut: rebuilding Russia's largest far eastern container port with P&O, taking a controlling interest in Moscow's regional cement market with Lafarge, negotiating Siberian fly-over rights for American carriers….

Some things have lasting value, and those are the most dear. So, it has been for my Russian experience as some significant number of the young Russians under my watch have gone on to become leaders in Russia’s financial community – perhaps, Russia’s best hope, presently, for constructive change.

New conclusion – they’re all, every blessed one of our years, formative.
