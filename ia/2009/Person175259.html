PROFESSIONAL GOALS = involvement in:

FIDUCIARY GOVERNANCE: The system, by which fiduciary activities are directed, managed and controlled. It specifies the distribution of rights and responsibilities among all participants in the fiduciary process, and spells out the rules and procedures for decision making. Thus it provides the structure through which objectives and related means are set and performance is monitored.

FIDUCIARY RELATIONS has as elements: (1) vulnerability and dependency of the beneficiary, (2) great disparity of position and influence between parties, (3) discretionary power or (de facto) control or supervision of the fiduciary, and (4) trust that he will exercise that power or control faithfully and in the beneficiary’s best interest. The fiduciary’s duties are based on that trust, statutory law, regulations, court decisions, company codes of conduct, industry best practices, fiduciary standards and on the wording of a possible written agreement.

FIDUCIARY RATING: A quantitative judgement of fiduciary governance quality, based on an audit on the fiduciary risk or probability that (co)fiduciaries are unable to fulfill their duties towards beneficiaries, due to their non-compliance with the (un)written rules and procedures of fiduciary governance, and that they consequently are held liable for damages, caused by inadequate direction, management and control of the fiduciary process.

FIDUCIARY ASSET MANAGEMENT: Aims to fulfill the duties of (principal) fiduciaries towards beneficiaries. Key elements are: (1) information management to enhance transparency and beneficiary satisfaction, (2) hiring-and-firing of third parties, including (secondary) fiduciary managers, (3) rigorous management of conflicts of interest, (4) integrated management of fiduciary compliance and risks, including enterprise & operational risks, and (5) achievement of the desired fiduciary governance level substantiated with quality assurance, external audit and rating.
