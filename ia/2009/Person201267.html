Robert Hahn’s Summary

It's hard to write an overview when you've had the wide range of experiences that I have had. My background is pretty much as diverse as it comes, and I bring a unique perspective drawing on many experiences from the smallest of startups to Fortune 500 companies. The ability to cross-fertilize, taking a proven idea or concept from one field to apply it to another, or to synthesize by blending them, is a major advantage.

I won't bother trying to summarize, except by reference to an interview I had with the President of a major multinational company. We talked for an hour and a half, at the end of which he said, "If you get a bit more seasoning, get exposed to a couple of areas, you will be uniquely qualified to run a company by the time you're 40." To which I say, from your mouth to God's ears.

I loved my time at Realogy. Some ups, some downs, but on the whole, I've made some great friends, had some great experiences, and in retrospect, really grew as a professional, as an executive, and as a manager. Now that I'm in a smaller and nimbler environment, it's wonderful to take on challenges and responsibilities having both the confidence (and I hope the judgment) to handle them. Onboard Informatics is a great place at which to work. Small, but industry leaders, with the talent, the will, and the clients to make great things happen. Great times.

Goals? Do great things with great people and have a great time doing them. Life is too short, and work takes up far too many hours in a day not to enjoy it.
