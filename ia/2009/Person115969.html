Joe O'Bannon’s Summary

I have almost 30 years experience in public and private sectors in the air quality field in such areas as emissions inventory, air quality planning, project management and coordination, government and community relations, public education, regulatory compliance issues, legislative review, committee participation, urban emissions modeling, and consensus building. I have public and private sector experience in CEQA/NEPA with an emphasis in air quality issues, including general conformity, climate change, environmental analysis, mitigation strategies, Native American issues, and expert testimony.

Joe O'Bannon’s Experience

  • Instructor

    Northwest Environmental Training Center

    (Education Management industry)

    March 2009 — Present (3 months)

    Teach classes in air quality in CEQA/NEPA documents and climate change/greenhouse gases in CEQA/NEPA.

    Climate change is a challenging new field for planners. Since emissions of greenhouse gases (GHGs) are a contributing factor, typically climate change is relegated as included in air quality sections of a CEQA/NEPA document. However, with the Office of Planning and Research offering a separate section in Appendix G – Initial Study Checklist for greenhouse gases and the overall complexity of the project’s effects to and from climate change, a separate section/chapter for climate change may be more appropriate.

  • Adjunct Professor - Extensions

    UC Davis

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    April 2007 — Present (2 years 2 months)

    Teach class on Air Quality & CEQA.

    Air quality is an increasingly important factor in CEQA/NEPA analyses. With EPA's adjustments to the particulate standard, ARB's inclusion of and 8-hour ozone standard, global warming, indoor air quality, and the connection of air quality to environmental justice and children's health issues, environmental analyses of air quality issues have to be more exacting and comprehensive to fulfill the basic purpose of CEQA, to "inform governmental decision-makers and the public about the potential, significant environmental effects of proposed activities."
