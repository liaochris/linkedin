Carsten Roensdorf’s Summary

Geographic Information (GI) professional with 13 years experience in Indonesia, Germany and the UK. Focus on the lifecycle of geographic data from capture, modelling and databases, map production, data management, maintenance and update, data integration, spatial data infrastructures and web services to the use GI in government, utilities, private sector and consumers.

With a passion for geography, maps and geographic data I am working towards advancing the use of geographic information to improve the world we live in.

Carsten Roensdorf’s Interests:

I am involved in developing an international standard for 3D geospatial data with a rich semantic context as part of the SIG3D and Open Geospatial Consortium (OGC). At the OGC I currently chair the CityGML Specification Working group that aims at issuing a the CityGML standard v1.0 by summer 2008. In 2004 and 2005 I have organised two international workshops to raise awareness and develop best practise for Positional Accuracy and Interoperability of Spatial Data triggered by re-surveying or changing coordinate systems. I have also publicised a number of papers and articles about this topic. Most recent publication: Positional Accuracy Improvement: in Encyclopedia of GIS, Shekhar, Xiong (Eds), Springer, New York, 2008
