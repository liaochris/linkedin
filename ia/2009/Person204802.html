Robert Shady’s Experience

  • C.E.O.

    Central Calling

    (Telecommunications industry)

    February 2008 — Present (1 year 2 months)

    Central Calling primarily provides call direction services to companies with multiple locations, as well as VoIP telephone service to business and residential customers.

    A caller dials a single number, and then based on predefined criteria is transferred to one of your locations.

    A prime example is a pizza store. Your customer dials a single number to reach your pizza chain and is automatically connected to the store closest to them or closest to the location they are heading.

    Other examples include: Real Estate Agents, Insurance Agents, Independent Sales Agents (Ie: Jafra, Mary Kay, Tupperware, etc), Drug Stores, Tobacco Stores, or really any "chain" that wants their customers to be able to quickly and easily find the locations nearest them.

    During the implementation of this application, we became intimately familiar with VoIP, Speech Recognition, and Interactive Voice Response (IVR) programming.
