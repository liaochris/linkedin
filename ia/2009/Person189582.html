    Indian Institute of Technology, Kharagpur

    (Educational Institution; 10,001 or more employees; Higher Education industry)

    July 2004 — April 2006 (1 year 10 months)

    Designed and implemented prototypes for portable ECG signal acquisition, processing and monitoring system using micro-controller and FPGA based systems. It can be used to acquire ECG signal from the patient and display it on a graphical LCD. The signal is analyzed to delineate PQ, QRS and ST complexes and derive parameters like heart beat rate and PR, PQ and QT intervals. The motivation for the project was to develop a low-cost, easy-to-use system that can be used with little training by healthcare personnel at places where adequate medical/ treatment facilities are not available.
