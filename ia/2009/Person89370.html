Greg Day’s Summary

Greg Day has worked in the virus and malicious code industry since 1991, initially with Dr Solomon’s, which today trades as McAfee.
He wrote his own TSR virus interceptor in assembler language as part of his University degree dissertation on Computer Viruses. Following his degree he built and led a consultancy team at Dr Solomon’s dealing with clients’ anti-virus issues both at strategic and technical level.
In McAfee Greg helped found the Best Practices team. There aim to promotes awareness of virus-related issues and “best real world methodologies” for designing and implementing protection strategies based around the McAfee product suites.
Greg has been one of the key instructors on McAfee’s Live Virus Workshop.
Today Greg works in a Security Analyst role, he has had numerous papers published and speaks regular at security events worldwide, promoting awareness of today’s broader security and malicious code problems; and the initiatives to manage these issues both today and in the future.
