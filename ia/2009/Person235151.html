Todd Foster’s Summary

Todd Foster serves as the chief financial officer for the Texas High School Project at Communities Foundation of Texas. A $346 million public-private alliance, the Texas High School Project (THSP)focuses its efforts on high-need schools and districts statewide so that all Texas high school students will have the opportunity to achieve their highest educational potential and promote state competitiveness in the 21st century. Public and private organizations in the THSP include the Texas Education Agency, the Governor's Office, the Texas Legislature, the Texas Higher Education Coordinating Board, the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation, the Michael & Susan Dell Foundation, the Wallace Foundation, Communities Foundation of Texas, Greater Texas Foundation, the Meadows Foundation and National Instruments.

Foster has primary fiscal responsibility for the project’s $30 million annual operating budget and coordination of its 24 full-time staff members. His responsibilities include oversight for the financial and accounting functions, budgeting, forecasting, financial reporting, compliance, cash flow management, and accounts payable. In addition to his financial role, Foster is responsible for the project’s overall organizational administration, including information technology, grants management, human resources, and policy and general operations.
Foster began his corporate career at Citicorp where he was Senior Account Officer in the Global Corporate Bank in Atlanta, New York and Dubai. He has also worked for Sabre Holdings Corporation as Director of Business Development for Travelocity.com and as Vice President of E-commerce and Product Management for Frontline Capital Group’s HQ Global Workplaces.

Foster received a BA from Vanderbilt University and a MBA in finance from Emory University, and is an alumnus of the Executive Program for Philanthropy Leaders at Stanford University.
