  • Project Coordinator and Plant Manager

    PSA Peugeot Citroën

    (Privately Held; Automotive industry)

    September 2005 — March 2009 (3 years 7 months)

    Project 1:
    • Identification of the structural causes leading to excessive commercial diversity
    • Definition of processes making it possible to reduce the number of vehicle product lines by 30%, while better meeting the needs of customers and distribution networks

    Project 2:
    Creation of operational solutions intended to simplify development processes for vehicle product lines, as well as optimize the Product/Commerce interfaces

    Project 3:
    Revitalization of a medium-sized business (sales of €30 M, 50 employees, 1,100 new cars, 500 used cars, 10 agents) being reestablished on a single site

    Result:
    • Commercial diversity reduced by 30%
    • Lean process implemented at Peugeot Belfort with significant increases in customer satisfaction (+10 points in customer service) and gross profit margin

  • VP Marketing, Style & Communication

    Peugeot Motocycles

    (Public Company; Automotive industry)

    April 2001 — August 2005 (4 years 5 months)

    Objective:
    To renew the product line of Peugeot Motocycles in order to become one of the European leaders in the motor scooter market

    Actions previously implemented:
    Redefinition of the positioning of Peugeot Motocycles by taking into account three essential factors: Safety, Economy and Design

    • Safety: Introduction of world’s first 125cc motor scooter with ABS and motor scooter with supercharger
    • Low cost: Introduction of Ludix, first European motor scooter under €1,000 (50,000 sold in the first year), which made it possible to offset the decline in the moped market
    • Design: Creation of new style standards (Vivacity – Satelis – Geopolis – Jet Force), featuring simple but satisfying forms

    Result:
    In spite of the invasion of Chinese-made motor scooters, Peugeot Motocycles is today ranked second in Europe in the 50cc market and is one of the European leaders in the 125cc market. For example, Satelis is ranked third in 125cc in France, all categories combined (motorcycles and motor scooters)

  • VP Strategic Marketing

    SEB Group

    (Consumer Goods industry)

    June 1995 — March 2001 (5 years 10 months)

    Objective:
    Complete restructuring of the product line in order to respond to Moulinex

    Proposal:
    Lines of food processors to simplify food preparation and service

    Result:
    • Sales doubled in 5 years and operating margin grew by 10 points
    • At the industrial level, the new product lines enabled the Lourdes plant to secure its future (it still has 400 employees today), although it had initially been slated to close
    • The new product lines have helped the Group’s brands (Arno, Calor, Rowenta, SEB, Tefal, etc.) establish a lasting presence in the field of food and drink preparation

  • Associate Director

    Arion Consulting

    (Automotive industry)

    May 1993 — April 1995 (2 years)

    Result:
    • Editorial reorganization of Dunod Éditeur based on clear and easily-understandable segmentation of the product line
    • Editorial reorganization of Éditions Retz
    • Design management training for Hachette Livre teams

  • President

    Design Service

    (Design industry)

    October 1984 — April 1993 (8 years 7 months)

    Initial situation:
    Industrial design market experiencing strong growth, given that most small and medium-sized industries and businesses had been lagging in this area

    Result:
    • Creation of the Design Service agency, structured to offer manufacturing businesses a simple, accessible package of products and services
    • Design Service was one of the fastest-growing businesses in France between 1984 and 1989 (15 people + 1 US subsidiary)
