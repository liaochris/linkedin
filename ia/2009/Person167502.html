Molly Buckley’s Summary

Born and raised outside of the nation’s capital, Washington D.C., Molly was exposed to a diverse culture full of bustle and excitement. Her array of experiences led her to the state capital of Richmond, VA where she taught high school English and photojournalism for two years. In the summer of 2009, Molly finally decided to migrate south and moved to Carrboro, North Carolina.

Molly has been ghostwriting, blogging, and consulting for a little over three years. She started small, blogging for lobbying groups and professors in college and expanded her freelance business to working with non-profits, startup companies, and small businesses. She now works as a full-time freelancer coaching, consulting, and facilitating workshops on a variety of social media and new media topics. She is enthusiastic and passionate about social media and is always learning something new.

++++

In her spare time, Molly is a comedian. Molly has been improvising her whole life — living life by the seat of her pants. Formally, Molly has been improvising for three years. Now, she is in a long-term relationship with improvisation. Molly has studied and workshopped the art of sketch and improv comedy with: Ari Voukydis, Betsy Stover, Michael Rock, Kevin Scott (Burn Manhattan & Centralia), James Bailey, Chris Gethard, Asaf Ronen (YesAND.com), Christine Walters, Barry Hite, Jill Bernard (Drum Machine), SNL writer Bryan Tucker, and Zach Ward (DSI Comedy).

Prior to catching the improv bug, Molly was involved in theatre and performed sketch comedy for over 10 years. Her best years of sketch were with a fantastic sketch group in college known as CNU TONiGHT. There, she was able to coin phrases such as, “horse-shoe it up" and “never fear Chuck Bauer.”

In addition to all of this, Molly loves Chipotle burritos and Audrey Hepburn, both the actress and her dog which shares that name with the actress. You know what I mean.
