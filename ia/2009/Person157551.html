May Wuthrich’s Summary

Veteran media executive across multiple platforms including film, book, audio, e-book and d-audio. Extensive relationships throughout creative and business community. Association with premier projects and talent.

Recently produced book events with best-selling author Larry Beinhard (WAG THE DOG, THE LIBRARIAN, SALVATION BOULEVARD) and Children's Poet Laureate Mary Ann Hoberman.

Currently, editing first novel by Thomas Nelson, a self-proclaimed political junkie whose book, CATCH THE WIND, is drawn from his experience as a speech writer and chief of staff to two majority leaders in North Dakota’s House of Representatives. The novel is reminiscent of such Frank Capra classics as Mr. Smith Goes to Washignton and It’s a Wonderful Life.

Partnered with Todd Harris (independent producer most recently Yonkers Joe and Bottle Shock) to adapt and finance an independent film based on Alexandra Gersten Vassileros stage play,THE ARGUMENT.

Working on a freelance basis as an audiobook director. Recently for Hachette Audio: Jane Alexander in Anne Rivers Siddons OFF SEASON, Cassandra Morris in Billie Letts MADE IN THE U.S.A. {starred PW review) and the GOSSIP GIRL PREQUEL. Jill Apple in James Patterson book 4 and 5 of the MAXIMUM RIDE tween series, and Ellen Archer in Joyce Meyer & Deborah Bedford 's ANY MINUTE. For HarperAudio, produced and directed 3 Philip Margolin stories in the d-audio format only.

Other clients include Just Books II, UJA Federation Greenwich, Ledig House International Writers Residency

Currently, on the business side: Launching company to help provide author tools to market books through a variety of channels, including the creation of long and short-form audio content, preparing authors for media and lecture tours, and producing book events.

May Wuthrich’s Specialties:

Content development and production, Book editing, Book Proposal Writing, Audio Production, Event Producing, Digital Righs consulting, Writing Coach
