Trista Chen’s Summary

My goal is make media active. We have been captured and stored image and video, retrieved them passively (or lost them ;-)). How can we let media serve us actively?

Active media requires computer vision technology so cameras have intelligence. Active media also requires machine learning and signal processing technologies to learn, classify, and serve.

I am with Gracenote, an emerging media company, and have been with Intel/Nvidia, two giants in processors. At Gracenote, I am leading the video technology effort to expand the horizon of the company, from music to true media. Prior to Gracenote, I helped bring up the first video processor of Nvidia as an architect and have been a researcher at Intel seeing the birth of multi-core computing paradigm. I got my training at Carnegie Mellon University as a Ph.D.

Trista Chen’s Specialties:

Computer vision (detection, tracking, analysis of objects from single-/multi-view camera), digital watermark/digital right management/media fingerprint, video streaming (rate shaping, rate control, error-resilient video streaming, QoS), video coding: H.264, H.263+, MPEG-4, MPEG-2), workload-driven computer architecture
