Karen Strum’s Summary

Do you need someone who can manage, develop, and implement creative multimedia marketing strategies? I’ve been a member of Web, graphic design, copywriting and marketing teams for Fortune 500 companies over the past 12 years; helping them build strong brands and increase customer loyalty. I’m proud of being on the winning teams of several prestigious awards—I’d be pleased to help you implement this kind of philosophy!

Karen Strum’s Experience

  • Owner

    Karen Strum, LLC.

    (Marketing and Advertising industry)

    June 2004 — Present (5 years)

    After the US Department of Creative closed its business in August 2007, I decided to concentrate full time on my freelance work (which I also did for a year after leaving Finelight). I meet with clients and sell Web design and marketing services. Then, if necessary, I screen, interview and outsource the work to experienced professionals based on the project. Projects include developing business plans, CRM management, copywriting, e-commerce, print & Web site design for companies that ranged from agriculture to high-end hand bag and luggage retailers. My client list included Monsanto, Arm & Hammer, Bloomington Chamber of Commerce, South Central Indiana Workforce Investment Board, Penn Life Insurance, Meridian Kessler Home Tour, Mountainfilm in Telluride, Indianapolis Sailing Club, Indiana Sailing Foundation, Citizens Gas, That’s Our Bag, Mayne View Bed and Breakfast and Topher Morrison, Inc.

  • Creative Director

    US Department of Creative

    (Marketing and Advertising industry)

    August 2005 — August 2007 (2 years 1 month)

    My responsibilities entailed overseeing the design of branding and advertising for our clients and ensuring that the new branding and advertising fits in with the client’s requirements and the image they wish to promote for their company or product. The main aspect of my role is to interpret a client's communications strategy and then develop proposed creative approaches and treatments that align with that strategy. While managing a campaign, I was also responsible for directing the graphic designers, copywriters, web designers and programmers needed for the project. Our client list included US Oncology, CTI Group, Hill-rom, DQE, Roller Skating Association of America, Meridian Kessler Home Tour, Mountainfilm in Telluride, Indianapolis Sailing Club and the Indiana Sailing Foundation.

  • Senior Web Designer

    Finelight Strategic Marketing Communications

    (Privately Held; Marketing and Advertising industry)

    August 2002 — June 2004 (1 year 11 months)

    Finelight Strategic Marketing Communications provides advertising services to clients nationwide, including WebMD from Medscape, Humana, Baxter Biopharma Solutions, Raytheon Aircraft Company and Transamerica Occidental Life Insurance. I was responsible for identifying Web/Interactive new business opportunities, generating proposals, project management and creative/technical direction on Web/Interactive projects. Many e-commerce projects had strict traffic goals, click-thru rates and ROI parameters. Other challenges included Flash animation and 360 degree QT projects.

  • Owner

    Imagination Street

    (Marketing and Advertising industry)

    September 1999 — August 2002 (3 years)

    I started ImaginationStreet.com as an interactive agency, differentiating it by offering a mix of Web design/development, search engine marketing, Internet advertising/marketing, and E-business/E-commerce consulting. Some of the accounts I won included ATA Airlines, Liberty Mutual Insurance, Motorola, St. Francis Hospital and Cornerstone Information Systems (travel agency software). I primarily defined the company as providing specialized advertising and marketing services for the digital space. The 'digital space' translates to: Internet, kiosks, CD-ROMs, DVDs, and lifestyle devices (iPod, PSP, and Mobile). We delivered services such as strategy, creative, design, video, development, programming (Flash and otherwise), deployment, management, and fulfillment reporting. Our office was located on the courthouse square of Bloomington, Indiana with a staff of 3-8 employees.

  • Web Designer

    Monster.com

    (Public Company; MWW; Internet industry)

    September 1998 — July 1999 (11 months)

    Monster is the Internet’s largest job search and recruitment Web site. With more than a million job postings, it ranks among the top 25 most visited Web sites on the Internet. Part of my job consisted of designing and developing Pipeline Web sites for clients; using Monster’s content with the client’s brand identity. I also shared the responsibility with a content producer of populating the HR Zone with content. We contracted HR writers, edited their articles, illustrated them if necessary and posted them online. We constantly developed new surveys, quizzes and games that built repeat visitors and loyalty. Because I worked in the marketing department, I was lucky enough to participate in creating their Super Bowl commercial. It was the first online job board to ever advertise during the Super Bowl, let alone having 4 commercial spots.

  • Web Designer and Technical Illustrator

    Thomson Multimedia

    (Marketing and Advertising industry)

    September 1997 — July 1998 (11 months)

    Thomson provides technology, systems and services to help its clients realize their goals and optimize their performance in business and residential environments. Thomson’s Intranet was the 7th largest in the world due to the international structure of the company. I worked with product managers and writers to design instruction book covers and illustrate diagrams needed for the manuals. When I wasn’t working on manuals, I developed an Intranet site utilizing database integration. The site allowed users to search for any of Thomson’s instruction manuals based on part number, product line or language and then download it as a pdf file.

  • Account Executive and Content Manager

    Digital Media, Inc.

    (Marketing and Advertising industry)

    July 1996 — September 1997 (1 year 3 months)

    Digital Media, Inc. was the nation’s largest provider of prepress training and employee skill certification. They produced a series of CD-Roms and maintained a small Web development team. I was an intern turned employee during my senior year of college. I maintained account relations and Web site content for WTTS, WGCL and Aerchem/Aerbio. I was also frequently included in projects relating to the CD-Roms and attended digital prepress and printing tradeshows as part of the Digital Media team.
