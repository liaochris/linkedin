Rob Ross’s Summary

I've led efforts that resulted in 2 IPO’s and 5 acquisitions after raising $112M in multiple financing rounds.

Each of my companies (following my first job at Apple) have been acquired or IPO'd: CRI, Zero G, Adero, ValiCert, Individual. They've ranged from consumer content to enterprise software to web services to security & infrastructure.

I've built strategic revenue relationships with consumer web publishers, media/technology companies and mobile operators including Microsoft, Yahoo, AT&T, Verizon, Intel, News Corp/Fox, Sony, Disney, AOL and several others.

Rob Ross’s Specialties:

My interests include contextual social networking - web/mobile based experiences that are dynamically aware of context, profile, location, time, etc. Logistics are 80% of life, so I like building & using smart tools to help me so I can focus on the other 20%, which is the really tough stuff that trips people up - like discipline, character, values, fun, integrity and the like.

Rob Ross’s Experience

    CenterJet, Inc.

    (Internet industry)

    2006 — Present (3 years)

    Provide product, strategy & business development consulting for digital media companies focusing on web, mobile, social networking and ecommerce.

  • VP Business Development

    CRI (acq by Macrovision)

    (Internet industry)

    2006 — 2007 (1 year)

    CRI built a content technology solution called "BD+" which was adopted by every member of the Blu-ray Association (including Sony, Fox, Disney, Warner-Bros, Apple, Adobe, Netflix & many others) as the exclusive solution to protect hi-definition movies in the Blu-ray format. BD+ is now integrated directly during production into the hi-def movies of major Hollywood studios & the BD+ engine is built into every Blu-ray player on the market. Macrovision acquired our business from CRI for $60M in 2007.

  • VP Sales & Business Development

    Zero G Software (acq by Macrovision)

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Computer Software industry)

    2003 — 2006 (3 years)

    Zero G provided software distribution solutions. We were acquired by Macrovision, a $1B market cap company providing digital media and ecommerce solutions. Macrovision also owns TV Guide and their media technologies are embedded in 9 billion consumer electronics devices and 500 million PCs.

  • VP Business Development

    Adero (acq by COLT Telecom)

    (Internet industry)

    1999 — 2002 (3 years)

    We built a content distribution network service, with servers in 40 countries caching & delivering content worldwide. We got venture funding from Microsoft, AOL, Intel, Reuters, Mitsubishi, Inktomi, Alteon, Bowman Capital, Fidelity Ventures, Spectrum Equity & MC Ventures. Adero was acquired by Inktomi & COLT Telecom. Inktomi was later acquired by Yahoo.

  • VP Business Development

    ValiCert (IPO, acq by Tumbleweed)

    (Internet industry)

    1997 — 1999 (2 years)

    ValiCert developed secure e-commerce services. We got venture funding from August Capital, Bessemer Ventures, Draper-Fisher-Jurvetson, USVP, Intel, France Telecom, Lucent, Gemplus, Thomson, Mitsui & Korea Technology Banking. After our IPO, ValiCert was acquired by Tumbleweed Corp.

  • Dir, Business Development

    Individual, Inc. (acq by Thomson Corp.)

    (Internet industry)

    1991 — 1997 (6 years)

We were funded by Kleiner/Perkins, Knight/Ridder, Microsoft & several others. After our IPO, Individual merged with our largest competitor to become NewsEdge, Inc. which was acquired by Thomson (now Thomson Reuters).
