Magdalene Chan’s Summary

Professional Fine Artist & Art Teacher- Photorealistic still life, portrait, and landscape paintings

I've loved art since I was a child, but never had a chance to pursue it. I worked in banking, advertising, and child support prior to a work injury that forced me to quit my job. The injury left me with severe chronic chest pains. Doctors tried all kinds of painkillers and treatments. Nothing worked. The only thing that helped me was painting. I ended up going to UCSF Pain Management Center and studying Illustration at Academy of Art at the same time.

I am a professional artist. My paintings are in art galleries, Wikipedia, and my website has over 65K hits from over 40 countries. I paint photorealistic still lifes, portraits, and landscapes in watercolor like they’re oils. People say my paintings look so real they think they’re photos. I also teach art. What’s unique is I teach students one-on-one in art school basics so they learn to draw and paint in 3-D.

Goal
The goal of Art School 101 is to teach students one-on-one with a passion for art the skills they need to draw and paint, while making the classes fun and tailoring them to their individual style, talent, and interests.

Overview
Students will begin in charcoal and pastel to learn how to draw in 3-D.

Then students will learn about the color wheel and mixing colors. Using what they learn about value and color, they will do still life, portrait, and landscape drawings and paintings.

Having art classes tailored to their individual talents and interests, students will discover their own personal voice and artistic style and learn skills to draw or paint whatever they want.
