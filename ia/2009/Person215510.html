Sharon Minsuk’s Summary

Software developer and research biologist, with recent college teaching experience. I am now leaving the classroom behind and seeking a position in software development, ideally utilizing my science research experience. Diverse interests, experience and skills in biological computer simulations, bio-inspired computation, developmental and evolutionary biology, and other areas of scientific and educational software. I aim to combine my formal training and research accomplishments in biology with my other life-long love, computer programming.

In the lab: Embryo microsurgery; Microscopy (confocal, fluorescent, DIC, scanning electron microscopy). Have achieved the academic equivalents of products released (peer-reviewed research publications) and contribution to the bottom line (grant awards).

Sharon Minsuk’s Education

  • University of California, Berkeley

    Ph.D. , Developmental Biology , 1989 — 1995

    Investigated frog embryonic development mechanisms both by writing computer simulations and by microsurgical transplant experiments.

    In the former, I contributed critical insight and momentum to a flagging computer simulation project investigating mechanisms of biomechanical cell interaction during frog embryo development. Coded in C, Unix, on a Sun Workstation. My contribution made the project results publishable, resulting in two co-authored peer-reviewed publications and a presentation at an international research conference.

    In the latter, I performed surgery on embryos less than a millimeter in diameter, and analyzed the results using a variety of cell labeling and microscopy techniques. Presented the work at international research conferences and published two first-author peer-reviewed publications plus one secondary co-authorship. Scanning electron microscope image featured on the cover of the journal Developmental Biology.
