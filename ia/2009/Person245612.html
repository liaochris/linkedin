Laura Phillips Ward’s Summary

• Award-winning, exceptionally well-written, scrupulously detail-oriented public relations professional.
• Broad industry experience encompassing such categories as marketing, health care, banking, food and beverage, home furnishings, and non-profit with national and regional brands, including McDonald’s, Ronald McDonald House Charities, Garelick Farms, Friendship Dairies, AGA Ranges, Agbar, Bertucci’s, and Northeast Utilities, as well as State of Connecticut agencies such as the Department of Revenue Services and Department of Public Health.
• Founded WardComm, a public relations consulting firm, in 2007. Works with Hill & Knowlton New York on projects for Deloitte; the Connecticut Women’s Hall of Fame; Farmington, Connecticut-based Crimson Communications; and West Hartford, Connecticut-based Wiggin Communications.
• Formerly employed by Glastonbury, Connecticut-based Cronin and Company, Inc. Spearheaded numerous public relations programs, including: campaign promoting the 2002 Connecticut Tax Amnesty Program, which surpassed its statutory collection goal by 300 percent; Garelick Farms Milk Masterpiece Carton Design Contest, which resulted in nearly 7,000 entries from students in kindergarten through sixth grade across seven states; Ronald McDonald House Charities identity campaign which, within just seven months, generated over 3.2 million media impressions worth almost double the client’s PR budget and nearly doubled the average number of quarterly grant requests received by the charity; Middlesex Hospital Leadership Campaign, which generated national media recognition for the Hospital through various editorial meetings, and included development and placement of byline articles; and Cronin’s own public relations program, which secured national publicity for the agency.
• Bachelor of arts degree from Hamilton College, guest lecturer in public relations writing at Quinnipiac University.
