Carol Kaplan’s Summary

As a spokesperson for the American Bankers Association, the Nation’s largest banking trade association representing $13.6 trillion in assets, Ms. Kaplan manages a portfolio of issues including retail banking, consumer credit, small business banking, securities, and trust and wealth management. She is highly experienced at building and maintaining solid working relationships with members of the financial press, both print and broadcast, and is frequently quoted.

Previously, Ms. Kaplan was a television news anchorwoman and reporter with 20 years of experience at network-affiliated television stations across the U.S. Her reports have appeared on major cable network news outlets including CNN (Wolf Blitzer), CNBC (Geraldo Rivera), and MSNBC. Ms. Kaplan’s work has earned an Emmy, numerous Emmy nominations, and several awards from the Associated Press, the Public Relations Society of America, American Women in Radio and Television, and the Federal Executive Board. She reported extensively on the FBI investigation that led to the arrests of the “Lackawanna Six” – the Nation’s first known Al-Qaida terror cell operating in the U.S. – beginning with the raids on their homes to their convictions in federal court.

In 2006, Ms. Kaplan returned to Washington, D.C. to become spokesperson for an international trade association and then moved into strategic communications consulting. As a Senior Consultant at Booz Allen Hamilton, Ms. Kaplan managed media relations for a major U.S. Department of Homeland Security National cyber security initiative involving 5 Nations, multiple Federal agencies, and numerous U.S. states and Fortune 500 companies.

Ms. Kaplan earned a master’s degree in Communication: Journalism and Public Affairs from American University in 1983 where she authored her thesis, “U.S. Vulnerability to Domestic Terrorism,” warning of the Nation’s lack of preparedness against domestic terrorist attacks – a full 18 years before September 11th.
