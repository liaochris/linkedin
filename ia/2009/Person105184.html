Jason Leong’s Summary

I'm a founder of PocketSmith, a startup based in New Zealand specialising in innovative personal finance software.

PocketSmith makes complex financial tasks simple through a calendar interface and visualisations, allowing users to see their budgets in a relevant and understandable format.

I'm also a founder of The Distiller, a cluster of technology entrepreneurs based in Dunedin, New Zealand. I was inspired by the relatively hands-on approach taken towards pre-seed funding by US-based Y-Combinator, and I sought an opportunity to create a similar, but non-profit environment in my own city. This is an effort to prove that startups can achieve great things through collaboration, adherence to structure, and support.

I am responsible for the ongoing design and refinement of the Startup Sprints, which is The Distiller's framework for enabling startups. Like the other co-founders of The Distiller, I take a hands-on approach towards providing local entrepreneurs with advice and support.

I continue to seek opportunities for The Distiller by forging relationships with entrepreneur support organisations and private and public entities both local and abroad, as well as with faculties at the University of Otago.

From 2000 to 2008 I worked at eMedia, a listed web development house comprised of an eclectic and friendly mix of creative individuals from all walks of life. It was here I gave a chunk of my life, and worked my way up from the mail room to the top in six years ;-)

As part of a hardworking group that believed in a collaborative, interdisciplinary approach to problem-solving, we developed aesthetically-pleasing solutions, but focused especially on complex and dynamic web applications.

From 1997-2000 during my student years, I ran a consultancy that developed customised web solutions for clients including Herman Miller Inc. and the University of Otago.

Jason Leong’s Specialties:

The biggest challenge in my recent years has been as eMedia's Chief Executive, where I took a hands-on approach towards strategic business development as well as the steerage of a remarkable team in a high-pressure, successful turnaround situation.

As such, I bear the skills of an experienced manager well-versed in all aspects of running a company, as well as the heart and mind of a technology entrepreneur who loves to innovate and is a highly-capable designer and developer.

Jason Leong’s Experience

  • Co-founder

    The Distiller

    (Information Technology and Services industry)

    2008 — Present (1 year)

    The Distiller is a network of technology entrepreneurs based in Dunedin that was founded in October 2008 by Jason Leong, Pierre-Emmanuel de La Bussiere, Tim Calder, James Wigglesworth and Francois Bondiguel.

    It is an independent non-profit project that focuses on enabling technology-related pre-seed entrepreneurial ventures. The Distiller aims to help entrepreneurs bring their ideas to fruition by providing a hands-on environment that encourages progress and creativity through knowledge sharing, networking, and cross-inspiration.

    While there exists government support for entrepreneurial ventures, The Distiller assists those at the ground level, lowering barriers to entry for those who do not know where to begin. By building a community of entrepreneurs we also better understand their requirements through active and candid interaction.

    (Self-Employed; Information Technology and Services industry)

    2008 — Present (1 year)

    PocketSmith makes complex financial tasks simple through a calendar interface and visualisations, allowing users to see their budgets in a relevant and understandable format.

    It guides users to think about the future - not the past - and clearly illustrates the impact of their financial decisions. Users model future outcomes by setting goals and adjusting income/expenses and track progress by matching their actual cash balances to their forecast.
