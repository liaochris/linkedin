Mariam Memarsadeghi’s Summary

Mariam Memarsadeghi has a passion for open societies. Her mission is to help courageous individuals fighting for justice and freedom in their countries. She is an advisor on democracy, human rights, and conflict prevention programs, helping support civic engagement.

Leveraging the latest technologies in even the most closed political environments, Mariam works with civic activists and citizen groups to advance public voice and promote transparent, accountable government. The range of her human rights expertise and on-the-ground experience across the Middle East and in the Balkan region mean highly tailored, results driven programming strategies.

Much of Mariam’s passion for open societies is rooted in her experiences as a child during the Iranian Revolution. As a witness to the violence of radical Islam and the ongoing repression of an ideological state, she instinctually and philosophically believes in the universality of human rights. Mariam is committed to promoting both the value tenets and the institutions of liberal democracy as a means to upholding human dignity in any culture.

In collaboration with global networks of activists, democracy practitioners and civil society experts, Mariam helps craft practical solutions to political repression. She helps grassroots groups, international organizations, donor institutions, think tanks and governments to promote open societies where all people can achieve their full human potential.

Her current projects include an e-learning activism portal http://www.activisminstitute.org
for women civic leaders in the Middle East (with the Center for Liberty in the Middle East, George Washington University and regional partners), research and development on internet freedom and cyber-activism for activists in repressive regime contexts, and community based conflict mitigation initiatives in Iraq.

    Mariam Memarsadeghi advises human rights and democracy organizations internationally and is an outspoken advocate for women’s rights and civil liberties in Islamic contexts.

Ms. Memarsadeghi studied political science and political theory at Dickinson College (BA) and the University of Massachusetts, Amherst (MA).

    She was born in Tehran, Iran and emigrated to the US during the Iranian Revolution. She has traveled back to Iran several times since, and has also lived and worked in Italy, Germany, Kosovo and Macedonia. She is fluent in English and her native Persian, and conversant in French, German and Italian.
