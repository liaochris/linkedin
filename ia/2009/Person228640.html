Vishal Talwar’s Summary

Lecturer and Marketing Manager with experience in developing and implementing marketing and communication strategies.

My principal research interests aim to understand how managers manage organisational markets as well as business customers/clients where relationships are complex and there is a prevalence of multiple networks, interdependencies and complexities, in the context of both developed and emerging markets. This highlights the differences in external environment, its effects on overall strategy, intensity of cooperation/competition, standardisation/flexibility and thus variations in how markets and customers are managed. The underlying focus is not only on the structure but also the process and exchange mechanisms including operational interfaces.

To further my research agenda, I have aligned with various research collaborators from different parts of the world viz. the US, UK and India. I am currently working on a retail spatial/temporal study, the research outputs of which are being presented at the INFORMS Marketing Sciences Conference to be held in June 2009 in Michigan, US.

I am also working on a research project titled ‘Power of Absence in (B2B Relationship) Management’ which presents empirical evidence of how ‘mechanisms of absence’ are used in social ordering in organizations, which cannot be addressed purely by what is ‘present’.

In another project, the viability of the portfolio approach to managing customer relationships is being investigated.

Over the next three years, I aim to focus on and compare complex network dependent organisational marketing practices in both maturity/decline as well as growth markets.

Apart from my research programme, I lead the Marketing Strategy courses conducted at the London School of Economics for both the undergraduates as well as postgraduates. I am also a Visiting Lecturer at the Manchester Metropolitan University Business School where I conduct the Strategic Marketing Management module.

Vishal Talwar’s Specialties:

Go-to-market strategy, Strategic Level CRM, Customer Portfolio Management, Developed/Emerging Markets
