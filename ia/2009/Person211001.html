Brent Schimke’s Summary

Brent Schimke has spent a couple decade playing strategic roles in business ventures, projects and client relationships. His career includes project management, sales & development and corporate strategy roles. Schimke has worked both domestically and internationally in telecommunications and networking.

Currently, Schimke works at the City of New York with the Department of IT & Telecommunications (DoITT) as a project director of technology & systems for 911. Schimke was recruited to join a mayoral initiative: transforming how the NYPD, FD & EMD call centers communicate and operate in NYC.

Prior to joining the public sector, Schimke sold for and developed distribution for information and technology companies such as AT&T and MicroAge as well as such start-ups and small businesses as Sonexis, Geotek and Multi-Media Communications.

Early in his career, Schimke was an entrepreneur and traveler. He volunteered for the MBA Enterprise Corps. (a "Peace Corps" for businesses) where he was a resident in Budapest & Warsaw with a computer networking remarketer. But it was much earlier, in the 1980's, that Schimke was first introduced to technology while at Alex Brown; the investment bank that launched public offerings for Microsoft and Sun Microsystems.

Schimke holds an MBA from Carnegie Mellon’s Tepper School of Business. He received his BA from Whitman, a prestigious liberal arts college in Washington.

Schimke, 43, currently resides in New York City. He volunteers in his son's local school district. He is married to Joan Stein.
