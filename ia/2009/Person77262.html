Emanuele Ziglioli’s Summary

After spending a year in Madrid (Spain) on an academic interchange programme, Emanuele graduated from Milan's Polytechnic (Italy) with a ME in telecommunications in 2003. He has focused his primary interest in software system design through attending advanced courses of Digital Signal Processing (DSP), and completing his Masters with one year work experience in the semiconductor multinational ST Microelectronics.

In New Zealand he began working for Exicom Technologies, a digital radio manufacturer, as a DSP engineer, gaining exposure to modern Texas Instruments processors and Code Composer Studio 2.

At Paragon he has taken a position of software engineer focusing on embedded applications. His real-time skills include low level C, Matlab and Simulink.
Emanuele has also an interest in object oriented design and design patterns, and has worked with C++, Java and network programming.

He has been employed for a one term contract at the Wellington Polytechnic (Weltec), teaching a course of Computer Architecture & Operating Systems.
