Deanna Dial’s Summary

The closest recognizable title for my position would probably be something like 'account manager' or maybe 'relationship manager.' That still doesn't encompass the bulk of responsibilities. Let's try 'content developer' or 'brand manager' or maybe just 'catch-all content, brand and relationship development manager.' Now that that is clear as mud, lets just focus on what I like to do.

I started off with a technical concentration: biomechanical engineering. That was simple - I really like math and science. Then, I decided I didn't want to sit at a bench all day since I really enjoyed the other facets of my life: creativity, people, relationship, events, networking, etc. I also love art, film and music and desperately wish I had more time to enjoy these. Finding ways to implement all of these passions has been interesting and rewarding.

In a corporate job, I vent my creative fumes in, well, creative ways. I'll spearhead a new initiative which lets me take the reigns on developing best practices and training scenarios. I volunteer to coordinate a team learning experience where I can construct an un-formatted presentation. I bring in a speaker to give a different spin on our understanding of healthcare, our chosen field. Regardless of method, I believe adding this little bit extra turns up the dial on both my career and my happiness thermometer.

I have also had the opportunity to tweak my relationship building and networking gears to many different business perspectives. At one point, I may be speaking to the world's top orthopaedic surgeon vetting his knowledge on a given topic, then I may be speaking to a small consultancy to help establish their consulting practice. The next hour I may be working with my colleagues in London to coordinate an event. The different types of relationships and different types of conversations have given me a quite a few more notches on my communication belt.
