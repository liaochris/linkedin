    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Telecommunications industry)

  • Founder, Chairman, CEO and Majority Partner

    Diga-me.net

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Telecommunications industry)

    July 1999 — May 2001 (1 year 11 months)

    Diga-me.net, Marc’s first “dot bomb,” was a pioneer in developing vendor independent speech recognition projects for fixed and mobile network operators in the Portuguese and Spanish-speaking markets. With initial funding of over 300,000 euros, Marc assembled a world-class team of devoted engineers, designers and marketers within three months. DIGA-ME.net was “put into a coma” in June of 2000 by its principle investor after that small dip in the US capital markets.

  • Founder, CEO and Majority Partner

    Interphone LLC

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Telecommunications industry)

    September 1995 — November 1997 (2 years 3 months)

    Interphone, a US-based company, was Portugal’s largest "call-back" alternative telecommunications operator with over 1,000 clients and 150,000 USD per month in revenues. Forced to cease operations in October of 1997 by the telecom regulator under political pressure from Portugal Telecom, Interphone was considered a pirate for having captured, in less than two years, nearly 4 million euro in annual billing revenues from the former monopoly operator.
