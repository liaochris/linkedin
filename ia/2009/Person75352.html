Erik Wennerstrom’s Summary

I have been working in the web space for ten years and I am just as passionate today as I was when I first realized the potential it had to transform the way the world operates. I started my web career in IT but moved toward the business/marketing side of it when I realized I enjoyed developing strategies more than writing code. This breadth of experience allows me to work with IT and roll up my sleeves when necessary which results in a better end result for both sides.

I thrive when it comes to explaining the business benefits of the web to non-technical executives. One of the things I love about my work is when executives have that "a-ha" moment and realize the web is more than a place to find the best travel deals and make bids on eBay. The most recent example of this is when we launched a digital marketing component in conjunction with a public relations campaign that involved a dedicated domain name and a YouTube channel.

I sit at the intersection of the technical and business sides of the web and my experience in both enables me to translate business requirements into successful Internet and Intranet initiatives. These have included rolling out new intranet functionality that allows employees to access data more easily, web analytic reporting capability that provides insight into website visitors and entire website overhauls. More recently, I have been doing more with digital media as video and audio content have become easier to incorporate into online initiatives.

At the beginning of my career, I worked as an assistant to a high-ranking government official, dealing with many people on a daily basis. This taught me how to work beyond organizational lines and geographic boundaries to build effective internal and external networks.

I am also a Design For Six Sigma (DFSS) Certified Green Belt. I devised a process for website keyword selection that utilized Six Sigma methodologies and had immediate results in achieving top search engine rankings.
