Monica Henry’s Summary

Highly motivated graduate of the United Nation’s mandated University for Peace with a Masters degree in “Gender and Peacebuilding” (2006) and current Program Manager and Victim Advocate for the Quileute Tribe’s domestic and sexual violence victim assistance program (2006 to present). Served at-risk Nepalese youth for 27 months as a Peace Corps Nepal Volunteer through the facilitation of programs providing training and increasing educational opportunities (2002-2004). An excellent communicator with the ability to research and analyze critical issues, create high impact written proposals and deliver presentations to diverse audiences. Practical experience in grant writing, event planning, fundraising, administrative duties and clerical functions requiring solid computer skills. Has dual citizenship with Canada and the United States of America. Seeks challenging position within an organization with a mission to improve human rights with specific areas of interest in gender based violence, refugees, and internally displaced people.
