Robin Roth’s Summary

Robin Roth is an organizational and educational consultant. Robin’s work as an educator, activist, and arts advocate is profiled in a plethora of publications and journals; she makes radio and television appearances on behalf of non-human animals everywhere. As a public school teacher, Robin continues to make an original contribution to 21st century pedagogy focusing on humane education, literacy, leadership and social justice. Currently, Robin is a third year doctoral student in Educational Leadership at Pepperdine University; dissertation plans include qualitative feminist research on mentoring, global literacy and service learning. She holds a M.A. in Literacy and Language Arts from Loyola Marymount University and studied humanities at Reed College, where she considers herself fortunate to have been apart of Portland, Oregon’s literary and art infused scene; her work with local musicians, artists, and writers led to the creation of PDX Pulse, her weekly arts and culture column in The Oregonian, in addition to regular bylines in newspapers and magazines from Los Angeles to Greece. She is the managing editor of Ark Online, the enormously popular website dedicated to animal rights and the environment. Robin is currently at work on her first novel, set in Southern California, where she currently resides with her family, including countless animal companions.
