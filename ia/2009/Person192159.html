    (Architecture & Planning industry)

    2006 — 2008 (2 years)

    Building Information Modeling (BIM) is a computer software technology that uses 3-dimensional modeling to create blueprints, or construction documents, required for construction. The field of Architecture and its use of traditional 2-D drafting methods is being transformed by this relatively new technology. Designers are able to create a virtual model of a building, and incorporate all major systems (electrical, mechanical, structural) into the computer model. Clients now have the ability to see photo-realistic renderings of the building's appearance upon completion. They are also able to do a "walk-thru" of the virtual building model during each phase of the design process. BIM technology is also being used to calculate structural load-bearing capacities in building foundations, and in bridge design. In the future, BIM technology will be used by the architect and building engineer to identify and diagnose issues regarding the mechanical, electrical, and structural systems.
