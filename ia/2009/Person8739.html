  • Volunteer

    IEEE Professional Activities Committee (PACE)

    (Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    2004 — 2005 (1 year)

    After returning to the bay area, I got involved with IEEE PACE - not for an engineering job but to get plugged back into the local tech community. I was outspoken making proposals on many issues, but one of the primary issues I worked on was the job site. I had met an engineer with a recent MSEE degree working as baggage handler at the airport and at the same time I noticed that the IEEE was charging high prices for job postings and resume searches.

    I contacted the president of this 360,000 member organization, IEEE-USA board members, the VP publications, and job site management. I was granted access to internal data, generated extensive analysis and uncovered many prior misrepresentations. I made a proposal for a board resolution that the IEEE published (http://tinyurl.com/aw8vof). A much less ambitious proposal passed and the board did not hold management accountable. Although it did not result in much change, I am proud of the work and got to know many IEEE leaders.

  • Manager, Senior Product Development Engineer

    Intel - Santa Clara Microprocessor Division

    (Public Company; INTC; Semiconductors industry)

    1988 — 1994 (6 years)

    As part of silicon debug team, helped resolve design and manufacturing issues to transform prototypes of the original Intel486™ and Pentium® processors into multibillion-dollar products. Developed one of the debug platforms for the original Pentium processor. First person ever to operate the original Pentium processor (first silicon on the debug platform). Managed design and manufacturability verification process for Pentium microprocessor, evaluating functionality and performance to specification across manufacturing process skews, voltage and temperature. Teamed with IBM representative to satisfy Pentium processor qualification at IBM. Managed a small design project to create Intel486 SX microprocessor in a PQFP package. Managed key aspects of product development and global production testing for Intel386™, Intel486, Intel486SX and Pentium processors, working in collaboration with factories worldwide. This included the development and release of wafer sort test programs for all of these products and packaged unit test programs for the 486SX. Performed failure analysis, generated productivity, quality and yield improvements and helped enable annual production of millions of processors.
