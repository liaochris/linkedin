Wesley Dunton (AKA. Wesbo)’s Summary

I've always been told by my peers that i seem to really 'understand' the male focused entertainment genre of the internet industry and how to plug the viral advertising market into it. I'm guessing that this has derived from my past experience, always working on the cutting edge of advertising in the youth market sector and it has obviously evolved (as i have) with the coming age of online digital media.

It's something that i have never had to try very hard at getting my head around, as i am as close to being addicted to this medium as is healthy for my constitution.

My main fascination with this area is the belief that if you have a good enough idea and the experience to back it up, it's probably going to work. It excites me that i have been involved in helping push the frontiers of the digital viral revolution.

Moving forward, I'd still like to try things off-line, the editor of something like Wired magazine maybe, or the head of programming at a major TV station? However, I feel like the internet is way more plugged in ATM than any off-line offering, so maybe the editor of YouTube, I'd love to get my hands on that website and make it 'right.'

As for the future, I'm waiting for the day when you can get a frontal cortex USB plug-in so that you can work while you sleep. I do my best thinking in bed, dreaming.

Meantime, i'd like to get my hands dirty doing more global viral campaigns and get more involved in initial viral strategy, pre-production.

Wesley Dunton (AKA. Wesbo)’s Experience

  • Creative Director & Website Editor - Kontraband

    Dennis Publishing LTD

    (Online Media industry)

    July 2009 — November 2009 (5 months)

    After the sale of Kontraband from the Diffusion Media Group to Dennis Publishing it was important that the hand-over and bedding-in transition went as smoothly as possible. The key idea was to enhance the profiles of their already successful Maxim and Monkey online publications.

    "Due to its extensive experience with high-profile brands in the men's lifestyle and entertainment sectors, Dennis Publishing understands our market well. This acquisition is a logical step for Kontraband.com and will ensure continued growth. We have built a brilliant entertainment portal in Kontraband.com and I am sure that our loyal readers will be as delighted as I am with Dennis Publishing's future plans for the site."

    I agreed to go with the product in order to make sure everything operated as efficiently as possible during this time and the editorial staff retained in the acquisition were comfortable in taking the website forward.

    James Tye, CEO, Dennis Publishing Ltd said: "Kontraband.com is a huge site with a growing community that will sit perfectly alongside our other digital brands, especially Monkey. This acquisition is the third for Dennis Publishing in 12 months and extends our reach to over 10 million unique users a month. The move also underlines our position as a leading digital publisher in the UK."

    The advertising team at Dennis & relevant Lifestyle section management also needed to get to grips with their new acquisition and i was on hand to make sure that all relevant information about the structure and template design of the site were conveyed to their understanding and advantage.

    The acquisition of Kontraband.com has boosted the total traffic to Dennis Publishing's online portfolio to over 10 million unique users.
