Debbie Westwood’s Summary

I started my legal career in the US in the mid-90's as a paralegal where I gained experience with large litigation matters for an AMLaw 100 firm. My love of all things technical led me to computer programming classes and development of software to simplify creation of legal documents using existing client, case and court data in databases.

On moving to Canada I moved into the litigation support field as National Litigation Team Leader for a national law firm where I was involved in the development of their in-house litigation support services and complex eDiscovery matters across the country.

I hold a number of certifications in both the IT and litigation support fields, including Summation iBlaze, Summation WebBlaze, CaseMap and Timemap certifications, and the CompTIA A+ certification.

Through my business, InTechGration, I offer legal-specific training, litigation support workflow design and department set-up, drafting of RFP’s and litigation readiness plans, as well as project management for ediscovery cases. Later this year, Intechgration plans to start offering data collection services.

I am the Assistant Director of the Toronto Chapter of Women in eDiscovery, a founding member of the Association of Litigation Support Professionals, Golden Horseshoe chapter, and am actively involved in the wider litigation support community in both the U.S. and Canada. I've spoken at seminars and participated in panel discussions in a variety of forums, and am committed to the intelligent integration of technology into the practice of law.
