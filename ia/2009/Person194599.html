Raymond Morneau’s Summary

I came up through the tree care industry, starting with dragging brush after a storm and progressing through most aspects of caring for trees, though my emphasis has been more preservation and maintenance (as compared to utility line clearance or fire ecology).

I have competed in and judged at the International Tree Climbing Championships. I have served my industry in varied volunteer roles -- including terms as president of the Western Chapter ISA and of the CAA (California Arborists Association) -- yet always staying active in selected nonprofit roles [currently treasurer on the Board of Mountain View Trees, applying for our 501(c)(3) status!].

I never have enough time to twiddle my thumbs - - my goals include expanding the basic concepts and nomenclature to become household words - arborist, arboriculture, cambium, xylem, phloem, root crown, root flare, wound response tissue, lion tail, ANSI A300 Standards, ISA BMPs, ASCA, ISA, WCISA, PNW-ISA, CAA, TCIA, quality nursery stock, stem girdling roots, stub cuts, topping, over-pruning, ....

SAFETY, also! The tree care industry needs to enhance our focus on safety as an underlying attitude that reaches out to the weekend warrior, homeowner, and pickup-truck-n-chain-saw operators who have yet to realize the profound importance of and complexity of SAFETY!
