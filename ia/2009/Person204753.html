Robert Salesas’s Summary

Robert Salesas has over 18 years of experience in software engineering, project management, and change management. Robert has held roles as Director at Service Works Global, Senior Director of Engineering at Macrovision Corporation, Senior Vice-President of Product Development at Aspelle Limited, Chief Architect at ResponseLab Corporation, Lead Technical Architect at Microsoft Corporation, as well as founder and President of Eschalon Development, a company Microsoft acquired in 1997.

Robert has successfully delivered over a dozen major product lines at six different companies, from start-ups to the Fortune 50, implementing change initiatives in existing departments, establishing project management offices and green field sites. He is an expert in all facets of the product cycle, from research and analysis, to development and quality assurance. With a long history of lucrative business endeavours, and established expertise in the media, security, and development process, Robert has a reputation of delivering difficult projects on tight deadlines.

Robert Salesas’s Experience

  • Director, Engineering and Global Support Services

    Service Works Global

    (Privately Held; Computer Software industry)

    2007 — Present (2 years)

    Robert heads Engineering and Global Support Services for a line-of-business ERP suite at Service Works Global, where he is responsible for current and new products, through concept and development, to infrastructure, deployment, and support. With offices in the UK, Australia, Abu Dhabi, and Canada, the role presents significant challenges in product customization, localization, and 24/7 product support. In addition Robert is responsible for transforming the way products are offered, by establishing a Saas (Software-as-a-Service) capability with Rackspace, for leading clients, including Brookfield, T-Mobile, and LloydsTSB.

  • Principle Consultant

    Sambalegre Consulting Limited

    (Computer Software industry)

    2007 — 2008 (1 year)

    Sambalegre Consulting is a Process and Change Management consultancy that specialises in advising small and medium corporations on how to optimise and better organise their IT and development departments. Through a unique blend of applied research, specialist knowledge, and engineering expertise, we pinpoint how and where your department can best make progress.

  • Sr. Director of Engineering

    Macrovision Corporation

    (Public Company; 501-1000 employees; MVSN; Computer Software industry)

    2004 — 2007 (3 years)

    Robert joined Macrovision Corporation to lead the engineering department of the Entertainment Technologies Group in the United Kingdom. He successfully turned around an engineering group that perpetually missed deadlines, to deliver over a dozen releases on schedule, with an exceptionally high degree of quality. During the 2005 re-organization, he was subsequently promoted to head up the merged groups in the UK, including both Entertainment and Software divisions.

  • Sr. VP of Product Development

    Aspelle Limited

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Computer Software industry)

    2001 — 2003 (2 years)

    Robert joined the Aspelle project pre-launch while at Dresdner Kleinwort Wasserstein, to commercialize an internal infrastructure project and create an enterprise solution delivering bank-grade security for client-less remote access to network resources. He led the company through several successful product launches, resulting in significant industry recognition.

  • Chief Technical Officer

    ResponseLab Corporation

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Computer Software industry)

    1999 — 2001 (2 years)

    ResponseLab Corporation supplied web-based solutions for small- and medium-businesses to leverage the Internet, in order to provide faster and more accurate customer service. Robert was instrumental in developing the strategic direction of the company and participating in fund-raising activities, where he was responsible for providing technical and feasibility reports for the business plan, as well as conducting technical presentations to potential investors.

  • Technical Architect

    Microsoft Corporation

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; MSFT; Computer Software industry)

    1997 — 1999 (2 years)

    Robert joined the language development group as part of the acquisition of Eschalon Development in 1997. He was responsible for the design and architecture of several application deployment products, including Visual Studio Installer, which shipped as part of Visual Studio Enterprise Edition and the Windows 2000 Readiness Kit; as well as Visual Studio Deployment, which shipped as part of Visual Studio .NET.

  • President and Founder

    Eschalon Development Inc.

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Computer Software industry)

    1990 — 1997 (7 years)

    Robert founded Eschalon Development in 1990 to produce video and graphics software for the Commodore Amiga.
