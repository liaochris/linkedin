Benjamin Nicolle’s Summary

After a PhD in Mentor Graphics with LEAT laboratory, Systems'ViP will entrust me with an office deployment on Nice Sophia-Antipolis. Systems' Virtual Prototyping develops, produces and sells to companies, through behavioral virtual prototyping, systems design and optimization solutions (software, models and services) integrating multiple technologies (mixed and RF electronics, mechanics, hydraulics, magnetics, thermics...). You can take into account environmental effects (thermal, stress, magnetic field ...) and interactions between physical domains within the product. Based on the classical V diagram, you can reinforce your implementation (top-down) and validation (bottom-up) cycles on each step and level with modeling and simulation. Our technology for modeling system is VHDLAMS IEEE 1076.1 standard language. The latest modeling and simulation tools available and we can integrated in your flow.

  • PhD student - RF engineer

    Mentor Graphics

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; MENT; Computer Software industry)

    October 2005 — September 2008 (3 years)

    Nowadays, electronic systems are more and more complex, not only because of the integration density but also because of the use of “more than Moore” components. Design methodologies of such mixed signal and RF systems need to gain in flexibility in order to reduce verification and design time, spin numbers and so, the time-to-market. Our solution, based on a unified EDA platform, consists in finding the best trade-off, for each design step, between model accuracy and simulation time. This enables us to see the impact of some critical low-level blocks on complete system performance. In order to illustrate the different levels of the V diagram design flow, we applied our method to the design of a Bluetooth transceiver with a stress on the architectural exploration, functional prototyping and behavioral modeling steps. Finally, the validation of a satellite receiver shows the reuse capabilities of our models and demonstrates the interest of our approach.
