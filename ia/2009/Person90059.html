Gretchen Goldsmith’s Experience

  • President/CEO

    Rose Publishing, Inc.

    (Publishing industry)

    February 1991 — Present (18 years)

    President and co-founder, 1991. Responsible for all areas of the company. Our staff is solid, motivated, and experienced. We have some of the best sales people in the industry. We produce Bible study and Christian education material that is carried by more than 2,500 bookstores in 60 countries, and have more than 50,000 customers. We love to come to work every day.

  • National Sales Manager

    Branches Communications

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Broadcast Media industry)

    January 1990 — January 1992 (2 years 1 month)

    Selling commercial air time for nationally syndicated radio talk program, "TableTalk with Rich Buhler." Reported directly to Rich Buhler, one of the top-ranked Christian talk radio hosts in the nation. Worked for Rich at KBRT radio (Los Angeles, Crawford Broadcasting) 1986-1988 and was consistently ranked each month as #1 or #2 in sales for the new crop of salespeople.

  • Trade Sales Field Representative

    David C. Cook Publishing

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Publishing industry)

    January 1984 — June 1986 (2 years 6 months)

    Called on bookstores and increased the sales in the Western region (8 Western states) by 30%. Consistently #1 or #2 in trade sales for the company. But my biggest achievement was to talk my boss's boss into letting me—a person with no sales experience—have this job. He never regretted it.

  • Assistant Editor

    David C. Cook Publishing

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Publishing industry)

    June 1981 — January 1984 (2 years 8 months)

    Part of a four-person book publishing team in a large educational curriculum company in the dark ages before Macintosh computers. We were an entrepreneurial division in the company and were under the gun to be both creative and profitable, which we achieved—with a lot of work and a bit of flair. Our division was a financial success; and we won ECPA Gold Medallion awards for "How to Be Your Own Selfish Pig" (Macauley), "Christian Kids' Almanac" (Flood), and "Tales of the Kingdom" (Mains) during that time. (The Gold Medallion Award is now called the ECPA Christian Book Award.)
