Christopher Clark’s Summary

With over 20 years of experience, Christopher Clark is an internationally recognized expert in game design and the application of game methodology and digital technology toward the development of instructional systems, simulations, and virtual worlds for education and training. The creator of the Principles of Game Based Learning and Dynamic Adaptability in Instructional Simulations models; Mr. Clark has been called “one of the foremost minds in game based learning today.”

Perhaps the single most prolific designer in this discipline, Christopher has designed and developed serious games and simulations for, Naval Education and Training Command, Center for Homeland Security and Defense, Special Operations Command, Naval Postgraduate School, the MOVES Institute, American Telephone and Telegraph, Northrop Grumman Mission Systems, American Systems, Hopelab, BreakAway Games, and the Consumer Electronics Association of America. His credits include the original concept designs for Re-Mission™, NetStrike™, Twenty-four Blue©, and Counteract©.

Mr. Clark has developed 16 digital entertainment titles and received "Best of E3 Awards from IGN and Gamespot”, and a European Multimedia Association Award for his entertainment works. He has acted as a liaison to Paramount/Viacom, founded his own Educational and Advergame development studio, and is a University of Baltimore Adjunct Professor. In 2005, the University of Baltimore and the Community College of Baltimore County awarded him the Simulation Innovation and Design Award for his achievements in game and simulation design, education and his contributions to the Maryland Community as an entrepreneur, teacher, and technical adviser. Christopher is currently serving the Department of the Navy as an appointee to the Naval Postgraduate School’s MOVES Institute.
