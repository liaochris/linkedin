Megan Green’s Summary

I graduated from the University of Texas in May 2009 with a Bachelor's Degree in magazine journalism. I am hoping to use the skills I acquired in college and various internships in a full-time position with a magazine, design firm, or corporation as a graphic designer.

Photography is a hobby of mine right now, but I would like to hone my skills so that I could one day integrate it into my career. With both of my parents as artists, I feel like I was destined to do something creative. I have a passion for design and feel best when I'm utilizing my creative and organizational skills.

I have had two internships with design firms and two with magazines over the past two years. The design firm internships showed me much about what it's like designing for a client and how to listen to their needs. Once you hear what the client wants, it's much easier to execute an effective design to give them a clear message.

Working with a magazine was very different from a design firm. Instead of working for a client, you're working for an audience. Each department of the magazine must work together cohesively in order to distribute a quality publication on time. I saw all of the behind-the-scenes work that went into producing a magazine. I really enjoy the creative freedom that magazine design gives you and the lack of design restrictions you sometimes face with clients.

My classes at UT taught me how to work with a team of designers through various projects. In college, it's easy to learn how do things as an individual. But the classes at UT really focused on developing skills as a team because that's what it's like in the workplace.
