Jeannine Vegh’s Summary

Children’s Protective Services
•Currently serving the Group Homes unit. Work with adolescents who have serious mental health issues. Empowering them to expand their minds and take responsibility for their lives.
•Collaborate with Probate and Juvenile Courts, Juvenile Probation and various county agencies including Behavioral Health Care, Catholic Charities – Special Immigration Juvenile Status, Casey Family Programs, and the Regional Center of the East Bay.
•Field Supervisor for a social worker intern from the University of California at Berkeley.

Production and Management
•Published a book in 2003 on Domestic Violence and Child Abuse entitled Absent Hearts Missing Pieces, the Story of a Survivor.
•Coached a group of adolescent girls to create social change in the city of Fremont for three years.
•Created and managed a website for survivors of domestic violence from 1998-2006. Included an international message board and had available research papers, and books.
•Supervised five case managers 1997-2000; coordinating recruitment activities, family goal planning, social service referrals, and training for eight pre-school centers.
•Organized and implemented the Family and Community Partnerships Advisory Board for the Berkeley Head Start program.
•Designed a training manual for the family advocates at Head Start.
•Collaborated with CARD and developed an emergency plan for Head Start staff and children.

Training and Thesis
•Thesis entitled “Revisiting Domestic Violence: Empathy for the Batterer can lead to Developing a Holistic Treatment Program.”
•Trained and have facilitated the Fatherhood Development Curriculum.
•40 hour training certificate for Battered Women’s programs.
