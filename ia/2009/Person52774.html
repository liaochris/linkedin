Dan Bowdey’s Summary

I am an Operations Executive who has guided two steel fabrication & field services firms through cultural change and capacity expansion required of 33% to 40% per year growth. My skill is in anticipating needs and filling them; new team members for missing skills, new software/equipment to improve productivity/efficiency, or new/revamped facilities to expand capacity. My passion is leadership of these operational functions:
• Organizational Development – I create longstanding relationships with customers & vendors, fellow industry society members, and providers of federal grants, banking, accounting, and legal services. I recruit and mentor executives, managers, and functional professionals. Result: increased sales due to the perceived excellence of our product and service.
• Process Improvement – I continuously study current context and future needs, enlisting input throughout the organization from shop floor to chairman. I lead the executive team through revised strategies and documenting new policies and procedures for all programs in HR, product design, contract negotiations, customer service, production, quality, and inventory control. Result: clarity of purpose, to be the industry’s best, and empowering all team members to have a voice in the process.
• Systems Implementation – I lead the research, purchase, and implementation of software used ultimately for measurement of key performance indicators (KPIs). Result: accurate data and daily accessibility to targets for sharpened awareness of performance.
• Bottom Line – By my applying the above three functions, teaching managers to pay close attention to KPIs and their operating budgets, and carefully choosing capital expenditures including facility site development, building design/layout/construction to increase capacity, I have directed efforts that resulted in tripling output at two organizations and improving net income percentage at both, quadrupling at one.

Dan Bowdey’s Specialties:

Dan Bowdey’s Experience

  • LEED FABRICATION SERVICES, INC., Brighton and Greeley, Colorado

    (Oil & Energy industry)

    2005 — 2008 (3 years)

    $27-million operating budget, six Direct Reports (Managers and Senior Professionals), 21 Indirect Reports (Supervisors, Administrative, Inspectors and Technicians). Directed capacity growth of two manufacturing plants, all field services crews (welding, construction, technical) and support staff for QA, code compliance, production scheduling/coordination, raw-to-finished goods inventory, and process engineering. Initially had R&D oversight and edited written communications to customers of our products’ unique technical capabilities. Communicated closely with Directors of Sales & Marketing, Engineering, and R&D regarding products and service. Coordinated programs and data from Accounting, Procurement, HR and Safety.
    • Increased sales from $24.7 million (2007) to $34.1 million (2008), improved productivity 3.8% and increased output capacity from 21 to 30 units per week.
    • Grew sales from $11.6 million (2004) to $24.7 million (2007), net income from 7.9 % to 9.4%, productivity 1.6% per year, workstation quantity 43%, and increased output from 10 to 21 units per week.
    • Installed new labor reporting system from which labor cost KPIs could be tracked. Result: more sophisticated analysis when making Power Point presentations to Chairman of the Board.
    • Laid groundwork for implementation of Leed’s first MRP system with necessary data entered into the Made-To-Manage (M2M) software. When implemented, M2M provides further cost control and feedback for KPIs.
    • Encouraged participation of all management team members in the budgeting process, previously done by the Controller. Coached field services and plant managers to use labor standards in their manpower projections for capacity needed to match sales projections. Result; department buy-in/ownership of financial goals.
    • Created alliances with two community colleges to provide welding training for our employees in the evenings.

  • BOULDER STEEL, INC., Broomfield, Colorado

    (Construction industry)

    1989 — 2004 (15 years)

    $21-million operating budget, seven Direct Reports (Project Managers, Chief Detailer and Planner), nine Indirect Reports (Detailers). Directed customer service to clients such as Wyatt, Golden Triangle, & G.H. Phipps, contract management (fabrication and field installation of steel for office, warehouse, retail, industrial, school, residential, healthcare and municipal buildings), detailing, production scheduling, inventory control, QA, and subcontractor oversight at jobsites. Obtained approvals from Architects, Engineers, and General Contractors. Assisted Sales & Marketing by estimating smaller jobs. Coordinated support from Controller, VP of Production, Procurement Supervisor and HR & Benefits Generalist.
    • Increased sales 32% per year and net income from 1.6% to 6.6%. Grew staff only 15% per year during the same time period by utilizing timely outsourcing to subcontracted manufacturing partners.
    • Purchased farm land, obtained site development approvals from city council after working with city planner and rezoned property to light industrial. Designed and constructed a new facility; relocated in 1996.
    • Mentored three new Project Managers hired with MBA or MS degrees. Taught them the business and groomed one to become Chief Project Manager.
    • Researched, specified and purchased 3-dimensional SDS steel detailing and materials control software. Nearly tripled output per detailer and eliminated errors in transfer of bills-of-materials to purchasing.
    • Directed improved A/R turnover through revised procedures to insure timely invoicing for product and service delivery, change orders and back charges. Recovered “uncollectible A/R accounts” totaling $400,000 over a three-year period. Filed extensions on company lien rights, investigated facts and retained Primavera scheduling expert to assist in investigating sources of delays to jobs blamed on the company. Negotiated company case and won payments.
