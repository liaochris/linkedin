BB Robinson’s Summary

I was born in the middle of the South, to middle-class parents, in the middle of last century.

My first job was selling Grit newspapers: I learned persistence, negotiation, and the art of meeting new people. I also earned about 1-2 cents per copy.

A self-confessed word-nerd, as a boy I was more happy reading encyclopedias than watching television. In school I won spelling bees and speech contests. I loved reading, hearing, and writing stories.

It is no surprise that I believe in the power of words.

And as the son of a master salesman I was early exposed to personal growth and excellence through the books of Dale Carnegie and the albums of Earl Nightingale.

I have studied widely in the spheres of history, psychology, philosophy, law, sociology, economics, politics, theology, science, fiction, biography, sales, marketing, advertising, pr, management, and organizational behavior.

I, consequently, bring a multidisciplinary approach to my coaching, integrating numerous branches of learning.

I have benefited from many mentors during my half-century of living. I, moreover, have served as an informal coach to others, individually and corporately.

People have longed urged me that coaching is a natural fit for my interests, skill-dimensions, life experience, and that I should pursue it.

I am.

I have already begun a formal career in coaching and seek 50 clients.

BB Robinson’s Experience

  • Personal/Executive Coach

    VirtuCoaching

    (Professional Training & Coaching industry)

    January 2009 — Present (8 months)

    In the world of coaching, Tony Robbins, Stephen Covey, David Allen, Marcus Buckingham, and Marshall Goldsmith are household names.

    But who needs a coach?

    Many people, for a host of different reasons. Even Tiger Woods has a coach who helps him with his swing.

    And Google CEO Eric Schmidt said that getting a coach was the best advice he ever received.

    Exactly how can a coach help you?

    A coach is a trusted person on the exterior who helps individuals go from where they are to where they need to be. Coaches are unbiased thinking-partners focusing not on the past, but on the present and future. Coaches support and guide individuals to achieve their own outcomes and goals. Coaches help folks see the blind-spots they are missing, facilitating growth and change.
