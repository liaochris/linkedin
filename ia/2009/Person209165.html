Sankarson Banerjee’s Experience

  • CEO

    Futurebazaar India Ltd

    (Public Company; 51-200 employees; Retail industry)

    July 2006 — Present (2 years 10 months)

    FutureBazaar is part of the Future Group - India's largest retail chain operator. FutureBazaar wants to become India's largest virtual retail company, and is well set to become that in the first full year of operation.

    I have led this company through its conceptualization, fund-raising, hiring a team and launch and into its current leadership position.

  • Sr. Architect

    Mphasis

    (Public Company; 5001-10,000 employees; Information Technology and Services industry)

    April 2003 — June 2006 (3 years 3 months)

    Consulted with clients on technology trends and issues in the areas of Internet, SOA, and Microsoft Technologies. Authored the Mphasis Outsourcing Strategy Evaluation Methodology (MOSEM) and contributed to founding the Mphasis Application Portfolio Services (MAPS) practice.

  • Venture Partner

    TDA Capital Partners

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Financial Services industry)

    April 2001 — March 2003 (2 years)

    Helped evaluate investments for TDA, especially in the IT space.

    Headed marketing for an investee company - Deccanet. Turned around the lossmaking company and increased sales by 400%. It was later sold to Flextronics at an attractive valuation for TDA.

  • SVP Marketing

    Deccent Designs Ltd

    (Information Technology and Services industry)

    May 2001 — March 2003 (1 year 11 months)

    As part of my responsibilities in TDA Capital Partners, I became the head of marketing for this telecom software company. The company was going through troubled times, was facing high cost pressures from their dominant customer and was struggling to survive.

    This company was focued on the telecom sector which went through a major downturn in 2001-2. In spite of that, in the two years under me the company managed to gain customers in every geography, achieved a sales increase of 400% and profitability. The company was acquired at a premium by Flextronics

  • COO and Executive Director

    Indbazaar.com

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Internet industry)

    January 2000 — March 2001 (1 year 3 months)

    Co-founded this horizontal portal in 1999, and raised a large portion of its capital. Took it to 100 people and India's 3rd most popular portal in a year (Alexa rankings), beating many bigger players with much larger budgets. Built a profitable web solutions business that still survives.

  • Technical Manager

    IBM

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; IBM; Information Technology and Services industry)

    May 1995 — December 1999 (4 years 8 months)

    Pre-Sales Technical consulting especially in the client-server and the (then) web space. One of the first evangelists of web technologies for IBM in India and South East Asia. Helped launch Rediff shopping, spoke and taught on extensively on IBM's e-Commerce solutions in India and ASEAN. Co-authored a redbook on IBM's e-commerce solution on the AS/400.
