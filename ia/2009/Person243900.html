Christian Howes | Violinist | Producer’s Summary

The Producer:
I provide live strings through mutltiple overdubs. Hear audio samples at this address: http://christianhowes.com/samples.htm

Clients pay nothing up front. I submit mixes as mp3 to clients for their review. Once approved, I ship a cd or upload the tracks to FTP.

My team of string players can contribute when needed. We handle everything, enabling my clients to consolidate or eliminate costs associated with studios, multiple players, conductors, transcribers, arrangers, etc.. We eliminate risk to our clients by offering a satisfaction guarantee.

Christian Howes String Productions is one of the premier providers of live strings to a global clientele of production companies, producers, and self-produced artists.

The Educator:
I founded the Creative Strings Workshop and Festival, which will be held for the 5th year in 2009 (see www.christianhowes.com). I teach at the Berklee College of Music and have presented at education conferences for years.

The Performer:
Howes has performed and/or recorded with prominent artists including Greg Osby, Randy Brecker, James Carter, Jack DeJohnette, Akua Dixon's Quartette Indigo, Billy Hart, D.D. Jackson, David Murray, Steve Turre's Sextet with Strings, Bill Evans, Bela FLeck, Victor Wooten, Frank Vignola and Lenny White, to name a few. For more information about Howes, visit www.christianhowes.com.

My overall goal is to continue to be involved in creative music, whether through performance, production, education, composition, or presenting. Variety keeps me on my toes.

Christian Howes | Violinist | Producer’s Specialties:

I specialize as a producer, educator, composer, and jazz violinist performer. My genres include: jazz violin, classical violin, rock violin, bluegrass violin, and other violin styles.

    Christian Howes' Creative Strings Workshop is an intimate and realistic learning experience for any aspiring string player. This year's camp will be held June 30 - July 6 in Columbus, Ohio.

    Through clinics, community outreach performances, one-on-one coaching, gigs at popular clubs, and public and private jam sessions, participants immerse themselves in an intense week of experimentation and experience with nearly every genre of improvisation-based music.

    The workshop's high level of energy and musicianship is fueled by its world class faculty as well as its passionate, accomplished, and supportive participants.

    In addition to Christian Howes, instructors will include: cellist Dana Leong, violinist Rob Thomas, arranger/pianist Bert Ligon, pianist/composer Daniel Kelly, bassist Chris Berg, and drummer Cedrick Easton -- plus Billy Contreras, Alex Hargreaves, and more! Also joining is a seasoned battery of rhythm section players and bandleaders for professional accompaniment.
