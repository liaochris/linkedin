Ferry Leeflang’s Experience

  • Strategic Recruitment Officer

    Centre for Strategic Labor market Expertise and Communication

    (Government Agency; Government Administration industry)

    January 2006 — Present (3 years 6 months)

    At the Centre for Strategic Labor market Expertise and Communication I am responsible for developing recruitment strategies and creative concepts. Furthermore I give strategic advice to numerous departments and government related agency’s on recruitment and labor market issues. The strategies and concepts are developed in close cooperation with the departments and are aimed at target groups from which recruitment is a bottleneck for the Dutch government.
    At this time I am targeting financial specialists and well educated generalists. I already successfully finished this process for the legal target groups. I also developed the government’s strategic plan for diversity (aimed at bi-cultural and gender diversity).
    My duties cover the range from doing/ facilitating research to leading projects and implementing the strategies and creative concepts. I advice departments on recruitment and labor market issues. And I am closely involved in the acquisition of new internal government assignments for the agency. At various recruitment events and face to face activities I represent the Dutch government and interact with the target groups and other interested people. I give presentations, take job interviews and give general and specific information about the various career opportunities at the Dutch government

  • Senior Advisor Job market Communication

    Department of Health, Welfare and Sports

    (Government Agency; Government Administration industry)

    September 2001 — January 2006 (4 years 5 months)

    As Senior Advisor at the Department of Health, Welfare and Sports I was responsible for all labor market activities and internal recruitment procedures of the department and its divisions. I launched and guided a European tender to a new recruitment campaign. To professionalize the recruitment process, I developed and released new recruitment- and selection procedures and transferred the department’s traditional recruitment policy to a modern policy where an effective mix of traditional and digital recruitment instruments was used.
    I managed a recruitment budget over one million euro’s and monitored the placement of ads. Further I advised managers on labor market- and recruitment issues and guided them through the process of selection.
    Finally I was involved in the selection, development and participation of various recruitment events.

    During this period I have also been the coordinator of the traineeship at Department of Health, Welfare and Sports. I guided about 16 young professionals a year.

  • Campus Recruiter

    Cap Gemini Ernst & Young

    (Public Company; CAP; Management Consulting industry)

    August 1998 — September 2001 (3 years 2 months)

    At Cap Gemini I developed myself from System Development Trainee, through the role of Resource Allocator to Campus Recruiter. The role of recruiter at Cap Gemini means that you are responsible for the recruiment of students from specific universities. The tasks cover the development of new recruitment projects (f.e. internship policies, workshops and excursions), giving presentations at faculties and universities, guide selection days and conducting preliminary- and job interviews.
