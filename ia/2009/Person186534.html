Philip Kearney’s Summary

Engineering Executive who has spent the last 25 years developing and creating telecommunications products that lead their respective industries. From back in the early 1980s where I wrote one of the first Bulletin Board System (BBS) software packages for the Apple ][, through my time writing industry leading, ahead of their time TCP/IP software packages for the Mac & PC up to now where I have developed the industry's most compelling Wi-Fi products at Apple, my goal has always been to create the best products of their class which provide real revenue and value propositions for the company.

I love building world-class products and I love motivating the teams which create those products. I am looking now for my next challenge where I can be at the leading edge of technology and build a team that creates the next cool new thing.

Philip Kearney’s Specialties:

Visionary with expertise in wireless communications, software development, systems architecture and strategic planning. Building world class teams to work on the best projects. Leading and guiding a large group of hardware, software and QA engineers.

Philip Kearney’s Experience

  • President & Co-Founder

    Trailing Zee Productions, LLC.

    (Privately Held; Computer Software industry)

    November 2008 — Present (1 year)

    Development of mobile application solutions for the Apple iPhone, iPod touch and other smartphone platforms. Creating the best, most innovative mobile platform applications and games to meet client needs. Leading a team that develops back-end foundational technology and software that makes a client company's blog or online portal accessible to their users, as well as daily downloads of their content. With these solutions, a client can deepen their marketing impact while reaching out to new customers in ways not previously available without creating and dedicating an internal application development team to such an effort.

  • Director, Communications Technologies

    Apple, Inc.

    (Computer Hardware industry)

    January 2002 — November 2008 (6 years 11 months)

    Leader and manager of all of the software, hardware, firmware and QA teams responsible for developing the telecommunications technologies deployed in all of Apple’s Macintosh computers.

    These included the Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, Ethernet, dial-up modems and 3G WWAN technologies where I oversaw all aspects of internal cross-functional development and design. My duties required, but were not limited to, management of all internal teams as well as driving all relations with silicon vendors in these technology areas.

    As also the visionary of Apple’s innovation and standards development in these technologies, I managed a Standards & Architecture group which sent representatives to organizations like the IEEE, Wi-Fi Alliance, Bluetooth SIG and PCI SIG. Apple’s agenda and objectives were pushed into these groups to form new standards and drive innovation of these technologies to where Apple needed them to go. Co-authored a number of patents for Apple in these areas also.

    In my capacity as Apple’s board member on the Board of Directors of the Wi-Fi Alliance where all Wi-Fi policy and standards coordination and interoperability compliance is driven, I was required to interact with many executives from many of the member companies so I could try to help represent their interests at the board level of this organization.

    In addition, I also oversaw and planned all aspects of the development of Apple’s entire line of 802.11 access point products including four different AirPort Extreme models, two different AirPort Express models and the Time Capsule product where I grew the business over tenfold in revenues. Also introduced many new interesting technologies like AirTunes, Wide Area Bonjour and IPv6 link local networking in the home.

  • Sr. Technical Manager, Wireless Communications & Networking Division

    Agere Systems

    (Public Company; LSI; Semiconductors industry)

    January 2001 — January 2002 (1 year 1 month)

    Built the team responsible for the specification, design, implementation, integration, and testing of a variety of Wi-Fi (802.11) products including wireless client software for Linux, Macintosh, and Windows as well as embedded Wi-Fi access point products covering Enterprise, Secure Enterprise, and Small Office Home Office (SOHO) market spaces.

    Delivered multiple products under the labels of Apple, Avaya, Compaq, Dell, HP, IBM, Lucent, Toshiba & others.

    [The Wireless Communications and Networking Division of Lucent, among many others, were spun out of Lucent Technologies and became Agere Systems.]

  • Technical Manager, Wireless Communications & Networking Division

    Lucent Technologies

    (Public Company; LU; Telecommunications industry)

    June 1999 — January 2001 (1 year 8 months)

    One of the people responsible for Apple’s market-defining first generation AirPort base station product after leading the development of the world’s first modern wireless home router, the Lucent BG-2000. Lead a team of software engineers who designed and implemented wireless client software solutions for the WaveLAN and Orinoco line of 802.11 products. This included driver and configuration interface development for the Windows, MacOS and Unix platforms. The team also developed all client software for the Lucent secure 802.11 product line called the AS-1000.

    After leading my team in integrating all of the embedded VxWorks code used in Lucent’s different WaveLAN 802.11 access point products for the Enterprise, Secure Enterprise, SOHO and Outdoor markets, I was promoted by the Director of the division to Sr. Manager and tasked with the duties described above.

    About six months after this promotion, Lucent spun it’s semiconductor division out into a new company called Agere Systems and the entire Lucent Wireless Division was moved into that new company.

    [Ascend Communications was bought by Lucent Technologies.]

  • Senior Software Engineer

    Ascend Communications

    (Public Company; ASND; Telecommunications industry)

    March 1997 — June 1999 (2 years 4 months)

    Worked on the design and implementation of embedded data networking software solutions at Ascend and eventually was promoted to manager of my team. Team wrote low level embedded networking software technologies for products like the Ascend Max 6000 dial-up concentrator as well as the TNT and PPPoE concentrator devices and Pipeline broadband modems. Also wrote and debugged TCP/IP client software products like TCP/Connect4 and NFS/Share.

    [InterCon, a subsidiary of PSINet, was bought by Ascend Communications]

  • Senior Software Engineer

    InterCon Systems

    (Privately Held; Computer Software industry)

    October 1992 — March 1997 (4 years 6 months)

    Responsible for specification, design, implementation, and testing of IETF standards-based TCP/IP client software for Macintosh and Windows platforms. Shipped over a dozen commercially successful networking software products including AOL’s first web browser for the Macintosh, TCP/Connect, NFS/Share, Networker, InterPrint, InterPPP and InterSLIP among others.

    InterCon Systems was purchased by PSINet in late 1995 who held the company as a wholly owned subsidiary until early 1997 when they sold the engineering assets of InterCon to Ascend Communications.

  • Network Engineer/Software Engineer

    Falcon Microsystems

    (Computer Hardware industry)

As a network design engineer I helped customers design the local, metropolitan and wide area networks for their facilities. As a software engineer I worked on internal software systems for the company to run on their Macintosh computers to aid in sales management and supply chain management.
