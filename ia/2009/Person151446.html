Mark Catron’s Summary

I am a versatile, patent-holding designer with multiple product launches under my belt. Products I’ve designed span a wide spectrum of industries, including fashion, consumer products, tools, and commercial equipment. During the course of my 7½ year career, I’ve repeatedly proven my ability to study the needs of the end-user and translate them into innovative, manufacturable, and successful products.

Mark Catron’s Specialties:

• Conducting on-site end-user research.
• Working with multidisciplinary teams to create the design brief.
• Conducting trend research and assembling boards.
• Concept generation through both sketching and renderings.
• Concept evaluation using ergonomic prototypes and end-user surveys.
• Concept refinement through interaction with engineering and manufacturing teams.
• Concept execution through completion of on-product artwork, instruction manual content, and creation of marketing images.

Mark Catron’s Experience

  • Senior Industrial Designer

    Rubbermaid Commercial Products

    (Public Company; NWL; Consumer Goods industry)

    April 2006 — January 2009 (2 years 10 months)

    Responsible for multiple launches including:

    • Four-SKU line of high-aesthetic Infinity™ Smoking Management receptacles, which launched within eight months of project kickoff and displaced competitors from catalogers. US utility patent for funnel-feed top design.

    • Twelve-SKU commercial vacuum program, with responsibility for product design, all on-product and consumables artwork, as well as managing instruction manual content and development. Intense communication, both via email and factory visits, with Chinese engineering team to ensure correct execution. Worked with Marketing Communications department as the Engineering department’s liaison to provide content for vacuum troubleshooting guide on RCP website.

    • Four-SKU cleaning cart platform specifically designed for the Asia-Pacific market. Conducted end-user interviews and observational research at multiple sites in Hong Kong & China. US and Asian utility patents pending for double-swing trash compartment door.

  • Industrial Designer

    BernzOmatic

    (Public Company; NWL; Consumer Goods industry)

    August 2004 — March 2006 (1 year 8 months)

    For 16 months, was the lone designer for the entire BernzOmatic division. Chosen as one of two recipients of BernzOmatic's "Top Performer" Award for 2004 - after only 4 months on the job.

    Responsible for seeing multiple programs from inception to launch, including:

    • Creation of Lenox “Tanktote” and “Gaugeguard” Pro Hose products, which were granted a US utility patent and were budgeted for $6-8 million in sales for 2006.

    • BernzOmatic outdoor patio heater product line.

    • BernzOmatic flexible utility lighter product line.

    Accomplishments include:
