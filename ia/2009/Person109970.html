Jennison Asuncion’s Summary

I am an IT Accessibility professional who combines: seven plus years experience working in the financial services industry; over ten years researching the accessibility of ICTs in Canadian higher education; with first-hand knowledge born from being an end-user of technology who happens to be blind.

Professionally, I began by participating in several e-learning accessibility initiatives while at the BMO Financial group Institute for Learning, the Bank’s corporate university. Today at the Royal Bank of Canada (RBC), I contribute to their goal of making systems and applications accessible to our employees and clients with disabilities.

Research-wise, in my night job, I co-direct the Adaptech Research Network, where we have been conducting applied, empirical research into the use and accessibility of ICTs by college and university students with disabilities in Canada. Our work has resulted in numerous presentations and publications.

In December 2008, I created a group on LinkedIn called Web 2.0 Accessibility Forum, because I saw an opportunity using LinkedIn to engage the community of Internet users with disabilities, with those directly involved and/or interested in making Web 2.0 and the next-generation of ICTs accessible to everyone. Much is evolving in areas such as Web 2.0, social networking, Government 2.0,etc., and I have a concern that a new digital divide may be building for persons with disabilities in this area if we continue to operate in silos. Join over five-hundred others in these important conversations by subscribing at http://www.linkedin.com/e/gis/1605077

Information on how to invite me to join your network can be found under Contact Settings. I invite you to get to know me by following me on Twitter www.twitter.com/Jennison
