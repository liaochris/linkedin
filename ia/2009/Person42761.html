Rashid Chotani, MD, MPH, DTM&H’s Summary

Providing technical and programmatic support to the Chemical/Biological Directorate (CBD), Joint Science & Technology Office (JSTO), DTRA as part of the A&AS Contract with the Agency with emphasis on the Medical Science & Technology Division of the Directorate. Within the Information Systems Capability Area managing Medical Surveillance Systems & Medical Effects Modeling Thrust Areas. The objectives for this area are: Combine integrated early warning detection and real-time epidemiology, medical & syndromic surveillance, and modeling & simulation to develop technologies and models that can identify anomalies related to infectious diseases in warfighters (and civilian populations if data is available) in-theater using bio-surveillance and early detection models. Conduct casualty estimation and prediction of human performance in hazard environments.

Rashid Chotani, MD, MPH, DTM&H’s Experience

  • Assistant Professot

    Johns Hokins University

    (Privately Held; 10,001 or more employees; Hospital & Health Care industry)

    November 2001 — July 2006 (4 years 9 months)

    Mentoring and advising graduate medical students and assisting in epidemiological, infectious diseases and disaster related issues. Worked with the department on evaluating the urban chemical fire caused by a train derailment with hazardous materials spill in Baltimore.

  • Director Global Infectious Disease & Alert System

    Johns Hopkins Bloomberg School of Public Health

    (Educational Institution; 10,001 or more employees; Higher Education industry)

    November 2001 — July 2006 (4 years 9 months)

    Evaluated the endemicity of circulating pathogens in developing and data-poor reporting countries. Assisted and enhanced disease surveillance in developing countries by developing indigenous capacity. Countries of interest were Pakistan, Iran, Uzbekistan & Kazakhstan. Identified critical high risk districts for infectious diseases such as Crimean-Congo Hemorrhagic Fever (CCHF), Dengue, Leishmaniasis, Malaria, Acute Respiratory Infections (ARI) and Diarrheal diseases. Conducted studies on: healthcare professional educational interventional for decreasing nosocomial transmission of CCHF, seroprevalence of CCHF in Kali Matu, Baluchistan, sheep-brain vs. vero-cell line Rabies vaccine efficacy, geo-spatial distribution of cutaneous vs. visceral leishmaniasis in Pakistan & Afghanistan. Most recently assisted the MOH to develop Pakistan’s Influenza Preparedness Plan. In Uzbekistan, was a collaborator on the geo-spatiotemporal distribution of anthrax, tularemia and plague.

  • Assistant Professor

    Johns Hopkins Bloomberg School of Public Health

    (Educational Institution; 10,001 or more employees; Higher Education industry)

    November 2001 — July 2006 (4 years 9 months)

    Mentoring and advising graduate and post-graduate, conducting research, presenting findings and publishing. Instructing in Tropical Medicine Summer Institute, Co-directing the Nosocomial Infection course, as well as sitting on various committees.

  • Senior Scientist

    Johns Hopkins University Applied Physics Laboratory

    (Non-Profit; 1001-5000 employees; Defense & Space industry)

    November 2000 — October 2001 (1 year)

    Developing the proof-of-concept for real time epidemiology for detection of a large bio-terror event, using non-traditional epidemiological methodologies. Co-PI and knowledge domain expert on infectious diseases (in particular influenza and Category I, II & III agents), epidemiology and surveillance issues related to ESSENCE II. The project required daily observation, compilation and in depth analysis of medical claims (ICD-codes & syndromic), over-the-counter pharmacy (OTC), emergency room (ER), school absenteeism, State laboratory and Washington Zoo, zoonotic disease data. As a result two patents were filed on the methodologies developed: Information Technology in Support of Early Detection of Bioterrorist Event & Hierarchical-Adaptive Algorithms for Detecting the Onset and Propagation of Health Events in Populations. Also served as the PI for the DARPA funded Presidential Inauguration (2001) bio-surveillance task.

  • Medical Epidemiologist

    Walter Reed Army Institute of Research

    (Government Agency; 10,001 or more employees; Research industry)

    2000 — 2001 (1 year)

    Assisting in syndromic surveillance and ESSENCE II related issues and being the liaison between the Johns Hopkins Applied Physics Laboratory and the GEIS (ESSENCE program). Assisting GEIS in the first syndromic conference and writing-up the critical findings.

  • Epidemiologist

    DEPARTMENT OF HEALTH & MENTAL HYGIENE, Maryland

    (Government Agency; 10,001 or more employees; Government Administration industry)

    June 1996 — November 1997 (1 year 6 months)

    Working with the State Epidemiologist to develop the LOINC/SNOWMED tables for setting the HL-7 standards of electronic laboratory reporting http://www.cdc.gov/nedss/ELR/ELR_LabData_1997.pdf Appendix A, B & C. Additional duties included atomizing the Maryland State Health, Department of Epidemiology & Disease Control for conducting electronic laboratory surveillance. In addition, was responsible for conduction surveillance and addressing immunization related issues on influenza, hepatitis (B & C) and meningococcal meningitis.
