Fran Battaglia’s Summary

Visual effects artist with broad experience in many aspects of production and post-production, including creative and technical direction, project leadership and management.

Focus is on
- innovation and quality results in terms of project delivery, client expectations and ROI
- integrated media solutions & cross-platform repurposing in terms of technology
- sustainable business development in terms of business philosophy.

Currently full-time staff at Solid LA (solidla.com).

Diverse professional experience.

Detours from Flame vfx work since 2000 include:
- directing Webby Award-winning digital production
- co-creating modern green lifestyle web programs
- documenting sustainable businesses in Africa in HD
- documenting the first 60ft surf session in the middle of the ocean

Prior to Flame vfx, professional experience includes:
- delivering over 100 programs as a broadcast linear editor (Grass Valley, CMX, Sony)
- Avid, Media 100, Final Cut Pro and Discreet Edit editing
- Quantel Hal paintbox and compositing
- award-winning cinematography for Quiksilver in Grajagan, Indonesia
- international broadcast production in Australia, Hawaii, Europe, Alaska & the Caribbean
- producing and directing live events (1st & 2nd Annual XXL Big Wave Awards)
- creating an educational big wave surfing documentary (Making The Call: Big Waves of the North Pacific)
- Director of Photography for Premiere Magazine / Women in Hollywood Awards (private celebrity interviews with Steven Spielberg, Tom Hanks, Steve Martin, Ron Howard, Brian Blazer, Jeffrey Katzenberg, Cameron Crowe, Wes Anderson, Jennifer Jason-Leigh, Martin Short, Jennifer Biels)
- Video Editor for Duran Duran in Santa Monica Airport's Barker Hangar (creating live synchronized video projections for their world tour)
- Avid Editor for Sublime's documentary

Fran Battaglia’s Specialties:

visual effects, compositing, editorial, 3D camera tracking, 2D / 3D object tracking, advanced keying, color correction (Color Warper, Color Finesse), post-production workflow, project management, client relations, writing and producing, camera operation, directing multi-camera production (P2 HD)

  • Director | Big Wave Filmmaker

    Surfline.com

    (Privately Held; Sports industry)

    January 2000 — December 2003 (4 years )

    Surfline.com merged with and then split from Swell.com around 2002. Located in Huntington Beach, Surfline.com is the most popular wave forecasting company on the planet, serving wave riders, coast guards, boaters and lifeguards on coastlines around the world. Historic surfing moment, January 19, 2001: after ten years of waiting, Surfline predicted 'perfect' conditions 100 miles at sea at underwater seamount Cortes Bank. Fran was sent to document the journey onboard the boat Pacific Quest with big wave surfing's hellmen along with Photographer Aaron Chang and Step Into Liquid's Dana Brown (on his own separate yacht). Mike Parsons caught a 66-foot wave and the story and photo made the front page of the LA Times. Fran's footage was utilized to initiate and market the first XXL Big Wave Awards (and earn sponsorships from Billabong, Nissan and others). Fran also negotiated and closed licensing agreements with NBC Dateline, Discovery and Activision. Fran also produced and directed live event programs for the first and second annual XXL Big Wave Awards, and created two documentaries, including "Making The Call: Big Waves of the North Pacific". (http://www.surfline.com/mtc/04/).

  • Video Director

    Swell.com

    2000 — 2002 (2 years )

    This Webby Award-winning action sports internet start-up company was co-founded by Nicholas Nathanson and Jeff Berg, who signed up Steve Hawk to bring on the best in the surf, skate and snow board industries. What started as 25 or so in SF bloomed to about 110 employees in OC and SF, and spawned the XXL Big Wave Awards among other fun projects, like AMC Adrenaline Theater, The XXL Documentary and commercials for Monsterskate.com with Tony Hawk.   • Avid Editor

    Surfer Magazine Video Awards Show

    (Broadcast Media industry)

    1996 — 1999 (3 years )

    Former Surfer Magazine Editor Steve Hawk and Writer Ben Marcus sat down with Fran for a weekend to edit the first big screen program in 1996. The first event was such a hit that Hawk hired Fran the following three years (and later as his Video Director in San Francisco at the Webby Award - winning startup Swell). After the third annual event, Hawk sent Fran to Fiji as a bonus. Now dubbed "The Biggest Night in Surfing", the Annual Surfer Poll and Video Awards show is still an industry-favorite event in 2009.
