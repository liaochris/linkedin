Steve Howard CPA’s Summary

Developed restructuring plan to turnaround a global semiconductor manufacturing company. Planned and implemented significant cost reductions and created a restructure plan to maintain solvency during a global market downturn. Result: Yielded $14m in annual savings.

Negotiated dispute resolution. Contract disputes threatened the viability of a $1 billion contract. Developed unit pricing metrics; negotiated annual review process and resolution of all contract disputes. Result: Expanded sales by $100m and generated $40m in profit.

Collected large past due accounts receivable. More than 60% of A/R’s were past due. Implemented aggressive collections efforts, weekly meetings and tracking of key A/R metrics. Result: Over three month period, past due A/R reduced by 57%, DSO reduced by 7 days and cash on hand increased by $2.5m.

Managed IPO. The company wanted to increase market visibility, investment capital and liquidity for employee shareholders via an IPO. Created financial infrastructure, implemented SEC reporting, managed stock appraisals, led IPO process including underwriter due diligence, communication, drafting of key SEC filings and road show materials. Result: Completed IPO raising more than $100m in capital.

Upgraded accounting organization and controls. Skill gaps and internal control weaknesses existed preventing the company from going public. Augmented existing staff with new top-tier recruits. Trained and mentored existing staff; established high performance culture; gained trust and respect of organization. Addressed control issues implementing SOX quality assurance and compliance. Result: Within six months, all gaps and weaknesses were addressed and the company began filing 10K’s and 10Q’s with the SEC.
