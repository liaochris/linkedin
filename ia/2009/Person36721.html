Cameron Siewert’s Summary

My love for writing first led me to an editorial internship at Texas Monthly in Austin, where I fact-checked, researched, and wrote for both the magazine and the website.

In April 2004, I moved to New York and began my career as an editor for an online travel community startup, IgoUgo.com. Over the next four years, I worked at IgoUgo through its corporate acquisition by Sabre Holdings, who developed the site into Travelocity's media brand. Through this transition, I advanced first to Managing Editor and then originated Travelocity's Content & Community team, which I led for the remainder of my time there. Growing with a company through such a transformative period both solidified my editorial skills and made me an adaptable, agile team member capable of contributing to everything from email marketing to public relations to product management. As Travelocity’s Content & Community Manager, I founded the IgoUgo Community Blog, drove editorial product efforts that improved the user experience and bolstered IgoUgo's content library, and led a full rebranding study.

In the spirit of putting my skills to use in a completely different environment, I then moved on to one of my favorite places in the world: The American Museum of Natural History. As Senior Editorial Manager, I directed the production cycles of all regular publications (the quarterly Museum Calendar, monthly contributions to Natural History magazine, and print and online newsletters), edited all press releases, and worked closely with an outside design firm to publish the Museum’s annual report online for the first time, integrating Web 2.0 design, contextual links, fresh visuals, and social networking highlights from around the Web.

In May 2009, I returned to Austin and am currently searching for writing-centric opportunities in print or online media, both freelance and full-time.
