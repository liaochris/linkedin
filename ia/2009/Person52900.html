Daniel Del Rio’s Summary

Dan Del Rio is an associate in the law firm of Dreyer, Babich, Buccola & Callaham, LLP. Mr. Del Rio has dedicated himself to the courtroom since his first year of law school.

After graduating from San Jose State University, Mr. Del Rio pursued his law degree locally at the University of the Pacific McGeorge School of Law. There he excelled from the beginning becoming a Top Trial Advocate in his first year and receiving the Witkin Award for highest marks. Mr. Del Rio left McGeorge with similar success, graduating with greatest distinction and receiving awards such as Order of the Coif, Who’s Who, Top Trial Advocate, and Top Appellate Advocate.

While obtaining his law degree, Mr. Del Rio endeavored to become a part of the Sacramento legal community. He began his extra-curricular legal education by researching for Professors Franklin Gevurtz and Fred Galvez on their respective law review articles. Mr. Del Rio then went on to intern with Sacramento Superior Court, Law and Motions. Next, Mr. Del Rio continued his legal education with Justice Nicholson of the California Court of Appeal, 3rd Appellate District. In his third year of law school, Mr. Del Rio clerked for Joseph Babich of Dreyer, Babich, Buccola & Callaham. Mr. Del Rio joined Joseph Babich’s team in 2005 where he has exclusively represented injured victims.

Mr. Del Rio has been active in the Association of Trial Lawyers of America since his first year of law school. After founding the Student Chapter at McGeorge school of Law, Mr. Del Rio became the president for an additional year seeking to bring together local trial lawyers and students.

Daniel Del Rio’s Specialties:

Mr. Del Rio’s emphasis of practice is in personal injury trial practice, specializing in the areas of catastrophic injuries, construction site accidents, industrial accidents, public entity liability, wrongful death, general negligence, products liability, vehicle accidents, and insurance bad faith.

I do honor State Bar Rule 2-200 for client referrals.
