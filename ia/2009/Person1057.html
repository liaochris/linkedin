Aaron Brown’s Summary

I combine all of my life experiences and apply them to the work I do. My greatest skills comes from my deeply innate desire to serve, customer service is my passion.

Over the past year I have worked extensively with a business coach. I apply the skills learned to all aspects of my business - working directly with clients, time management, negotiation and communication.

You can often find me with a personal development book in my hand, or listening to one on my Iphone.

I have also spent every day of the last four years, with a great teacher - my wife Yoko.
Yoko is from Japan. She is an immigrant and we met in college while we were both studying Hospitality Management. The Japanese culture is quite different than ours - in many ways, they are opposite.

Communication: Direct vs. Indirect.
Focus: Individual vs. Group.
Denial of Blame vs. Blaming one's self as a default

My time with my wife has made me a better person. It has taught me to truly slow down and think before I speak and to contemplate how my words will affect those around me. She has also taught me greater negotiation skill - by truly thinking from the other persons point of view and crafting my actions in anticipation of their reaction. I still have many areas where growth is possible, and I strive for it every moment.

My dive into the Japanese culture has been difficult at times however I have grown greatly because of it. I am gracious to my wife for believing in me and pushing me beyond even my own expectations.
