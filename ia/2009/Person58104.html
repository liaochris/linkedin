David Levitsky’s Summary

I am an executive in the interactive field with a wide variety of business and product development experience. I specialize in e-commerce, mobile, direct email and database-driven web applications.

My current goal is to obtain a position in which I can leverage my strengths and work as part of a product or business development team in the interactive area,

Most recently I headed the business development and sales and technical teams that developed and marketed Maxomo’s "Send to Mobile" services. These services allowed websites to let their visitors to “send" any multimedia object on a web page to their mobile device. Prior to developing this service, Maxomo had been a developer of "video ringtone" content, and had the content out to over 20 mobile aggregators and storefronts.

Prior to Maxomo, I ran a boutique consulting business, working on specialized IT projects for clients such as Edgar-Online. Before consulting, I supervised the development of Advaya, a personalized email/wireless messaging delivery system, (spun-off from Bigstar Entertainment) that was sold to Bigfoot Interactive (later purchased by Epsilon Interactive) in 2000.

I gained experience in building an e-commerce company at BigStar Entertainment, where I headed strategic planning, operations and technology. BigStar had more than $16M in revenue by its second year, resulting in a $30 million IPO with Prudential Securities. Prior to BigStar, I was Director of Internet Marketing at Columbia House where I developed websites for the video, CD, audiobook, videogame and other clubs. These sites had more than $25M in sales in year one and columbiahouse.com was ranked #1 by Media Matrix in the category.

Prior to moving into the interactive arena, I was Director of Marketing for Columbia House Video, while earning an MBA in Information Systems from NYU in the evenings. During my tenure as Marketing Director, sales increased from $100M to $500M; and membership rose to over 3 million people.
