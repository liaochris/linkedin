Matt Heindl’s Summary

Matt Heindl, a second generation glass blower with over 16 years of experience, is the owner and creative director of Maatwerk Studio. He received his BFA in glass/ceramics with a minor in metals from University of Wisconsin - Riverfalls. Since then he's built two commercial glass blowing studios and consulted on the construction of several others.

He studied and worked under Master Glass Artist Colleen Ott, who was a student of Harvey Littleton (whom is considered to be the father of the studio glass movement and started the first university glass program at the UW - Madison). His work has been exhibited in several galleries across the country. In addition to teaching numerous classes at Maatwerk Studio, he is also an instuctor at UW - Platteville.

At Maatwerk Studio you can find one-of-a-kind hand blown glass bowls, vases, sculptures and lights. You can also commission us to make a custom piece of glass art for your home or business.

Matt Heindl’s Experience

    Maatwerk Studio

    (Fine Art industry)

    2003 — Present (6 years)

    Maatwerk Studio is a full service production and teaching Glass Studio. I founded the Studio in 2001 to provide not only a facility for glass and ceramics artists to produce and refine their work but also a place in the community where people could learn to create glass art.

    I've added an architectural line of glass lights, sinks and tiles that are designed to incorporate the beauty of glass into the structure of your home. These complement my existing line of beautiful yet functional hand blown vases and bowls. Please visit my website at www.maatwerkstudio.com to view our online gallery or if you are interested in commissioning a custom piece.

  • Apprentice/Production Assistant to

    Colleen Ott

    (Fine Art industry)

    July 2001 — April 2009 (7 years 10 months)

    Served as an apprentice to Master Glassblower Colleen Ott, who studied under and worked with Harvey Littleton (considered to be the father of the studio glass movement and organized the first hot glass program at an American University - UW Madison).

    During this time, I assisted her with the production of pieces and was also responsible for fabricating pieces that she had designed. I became adept at creating pieces using the different methods of glass production: blowing, fusing, lampworking, and coldworking with diamond and carbide tools.
