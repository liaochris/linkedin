Brendan O'Rourke’s Summary

Brendan has 22 years experience in the telecommunications and information technology industries focusing on Operations Management, Technology Implementation and Business Transformation.

Brendan’s in-depth knowledge of information and communications technology, excellent communication skills, commercial acumen and extensive executive management experience makes him uniquely positioned to provide business leadership within the modern technology-based organisations.

Brendan has held senior roles in “blue chip” as well as “start-up” organisations. He has shown he is a strong and dynamic leader who is at his best in environments where change is a fact of life; successful delivery is critical to success and an ability to quickly make the right decisions is essential.

Brendan O'Rourke’s Experience

  • Chief Operating Officer

    FREEDOM4

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Telecommunications industry)

    January 2007 — September 2008 (1 year 9 months)

    As Chief Operating Officer Brendan was responsible for all areas of operations including sales, marketing and product development; network build, engineering and operations; customer service and all commercial & legal activities.     Key achievements include the development of the FREEDOM4 brand; development and launch of the WiMAX product portfolio; negotiation of critical supply contracts for network equipment, network build and operation and sites and growth of the business from 10 to over 30 employees covering all business areas. Brendan worked closely with the board in seeking investment including the development of investment cases and presentations. Brendan presented on strategy and operational progress to financial and industry analysts.

  • Managing Director, Systems Integration Practice

    Previous roles in BT included Chief Information Officer for BT Openworld / General Manager for the Retail Broadband Services with responsibility for the delivery of all BT Openworld’s /Retail’s broadband access and value added services with an annual budget of approximately £70m.
