Tom Markham’s Summary

Tom is an ambitious, analytical, numerate MBA with a prominent academic record and passionate interest in football who is seeking to secure a financial position within the football industry. A self motivated, decisive and goal orientated individual, he is able to work independently or in a team. Combines strong interpersonal skills with a proactive, can-do approach. Tom will bring proven professional financial experience coupled with extensive football knowledge to a role in the football sector.

Having already forged a successful career within financial services, a football related financial career has always been Tom's number one objective. He recently completed the University of Liverpool’s prestigious MBA in Football Industries (http://www.liv.ac.uk/footballindustry) taught modules with distinction. Covering all course fees and living expenses for the twelve month programme himself illustrates Tom's commitment and desire to work in this sector.

Tom is currently conducting research for his MBA dissertation - ‘The Valuation and Solvency of Top Tier British Clubs since the Inception of the Premier League. Is the Current Model Sustainable?’ whilst simultaneously writing a weekly column on football finance for Apnafootball.com. He is also actively seeking a financial position within the football sphere to commence on completion of his dissertation in September 2009.

  • Foreign Exchange Dealer

    Allied Irish Bank (AIB) Capital Markets

    (Public Company; AIB; Banking industry)

    August 2006 — September 2008 (2 years 2 months)

    Allied Irish Bank (AIB) is Ireland’s largest and most respected financial institution. Tom became the youngest FX Dealer in AIB Capital Market’s Wholesale Treasury arm in 2006. He was responsible for short term trading and liquidity funding in all major currencies. The role entailed ensuring the bank had desired levels of each currency in reserve whilst complementing the needs of internal propriety traders along with those of both corporate and branch clients. Liquidity was maintained through dealing in deposits and derivatives which are conducted through Reuters, Bloomberg or currency brokers. Managing relationships with brokers and clients was crucial enabling the development of Tom’s relationship building skills. Such a demanding and important role has thought him the importance of adherence to strict guidelines. Dealing in the equivalent of billions of dollars on a daily basis has also fine tuned Tom’s ability to pay attention to detail whilst in a pressure situation.

  • Management Accountant

    Allied Irish Bank (AIB) Capital Markets

    (Public Company; AIB; Banking industry)

    February 2005 — August 2006 (1 year 7 months)

    Tom was appointed as an Accountant in the Global Treasury Finance team within AIB Capital Markets on gaining a much coveted place within the AIB Graduate Scheme. Foremost responsibilities included the preparation of reports and accounts for AIB’s Irish, UK, US and Polish operations to ultimately meet the bank’s annual reporting requirement. He gained valuable financial and management accounting experience whilst also attaining experience in the use of International Accounting Standards. Working in such a structured environment in a highly competitive financial institution developed Tom’s communication skills and his ability to meet deadlines while also increasing his knowledge of the modern banking industry. It also provided Tom with excellent training in the use accounting software and ultimately acted as a stepping stone to a front office role.
