Michael Gamache’s Summary

At 15 years old I began my career in manufacturing at a steel company (Hall Steel) making full scale pencil line templates which were used to burn steel shapes; an electronic eye would follow the pencil lines and cutting torches would then cut those shapes from steel plates. This was just before CNC.
By 17 years old I was able to read complex drawings, breakout all details for machine bases, fixtures and machine components, and develop BOMs for our fab shop.
I became a sales engineer at 19 (we had computers and fax machines by then), graduated from UW-Milw in 1993 at 25, and in 1999 became a sales manager/sales engineer for a large fab and machine shop (Trade Tech). In 2-1/2 years we grew from $400K/yr to $2.1 million/yr in sales.
In 2002 I was recruited as sales manager and applications engineer for Cornerstone Design. By 2004 I became the COO and enjoyed being a Lean Consultant. The owner closed Cornerstone in 2007 due to financial and health related issues.
At that time Greg Chapman (operations and engineering manager at Cornerstone) and I started CDLMS, Inc. We hired 4 of the most talented employees from Cornerstone and in 2008 completed $1.5 million worth or work. Including 2 robotics systems for Ford Motor, 2 robotic systems for Aprilaire, a customized 55 ton press for Hamilton Sundstrand, weld fixtures for Waterloo Industries, PLC, Robotic, and vision system troubleshooting/programming for Modine...

Our goal in 2009 is $2 million
Our goal for 2010 is $3 million
