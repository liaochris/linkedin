Leif Skoogfors’s Summary

Over 30 years experience photographing for major magazines and corporations in the US and overseas.

Freelance radio reporting for Reuters, Mutual Broadcasting System, KYW (TV) and Radio Zambia.

Past experience includes 18 years teaching (Full Professor, created a BFA program at Moore College of Art and Design; two years as Visiting Professor at The University of Arts, lecturer at Temple University's Tyler School of Art).

Exhibition design and concepts both at universities, FEMA, and special exhibitions for the United Nations shown in Zurich and Venice.

Received grants from The National Endowment for the Humanities ("Academic Humanist") for museum design and The Pennsylvania Council for the Arts for a documentary photography.

Past member Swedish Photographers' Association, SFF.

Other photography on environmental and corporate responsibility projects for Dupont, Arco Chemical, the Ford Foundation, and the Knight Foundation among others. Involved close collaboration with graphic designers, art directors, and sometimes nervous clients.

Covered The White House and Presidential Campaigns as well as refugees in Kosovo, Bosnia, and other areas. Comfortable working with executives or very diverse economic and ethnic groups. My goal is to work calmly, leaving with photographs without disrupting my subjects or their work.

Also worked as a union negotiator while teaching; community activist, and organizer for non-profit groups. All of which brought out skills as a facilitator and communicator.

Leif Skoogfors’s Specialties:

Documentary photography and photojournalism. Exhibition design and photo research. Developing visual aspects of communications strategy. Media relations, teaching, union negotiating. Research on copyright issues, legal strategy, and resolution of copyright issues through non-confrontational negotiations.

Leif Skoogfors’s Experience

  • Corbis

    (Privately Held; 501-1000 employees; Photography industry)

    2000 — Present (9 years)

    Corbis distributes my photographs through its world wide offices. The work includes assignments I did for Time, Newsweek, The New York Times, Paris, Match, and publications in over 40 countries.

    Much of my work involved covering US and foreign military. The research I spent on military affairs led to my creating my own projects which were then underwritten and assigned by major magazines. I was often responsible for working independently in difficult environments, a challenge I enjoy.

    One of 12 official photographers hired by the Clinton Inaugural Committee to document both inaugurations, Washington, DC.

  • Department of Homeland Security/FEMA

    (Government Agency; 1001-5000 employees; Government Administration industry)

    2000 — Present (9 years)

    Disaster Assistance Employee (DAE-Reservist) photographer, on call for federally declared disasters, promoted to also work with all media as an External Affairs Officer. Attended FEMA's External Affairs School and received continuing training in External Affairs as well as National Emergency policy and procedures. Works with state and local Emergency management officials and volunteer organizations. Field Manager, External Affairs. Work with TV, Radio and Print Media.

  • A Visual Resource (Sole Proprietorship)

    (Sole Proprietorship; Myself Only; Photography industry)

    January 1970 — Present (39 years 3 months)

    Visual communications, involving photography, design, and exhibitions are an effective, but often overlooked part of public outreach.
    Photography is a critical component of communications, but it is an element often given short shrift. My experience combines developing visual communications to further the goals an communications objective.

    I have designed projects that provided effective tools which use elements to influence opinion. Examples include exhibits for the UN in Zurich and Milan on open air burning waste to diminish the presence of Persistent Organic Pollutants (POPS) in the atmosphere.

    Other projects were undertaken for FEMA and the AFL-CIO. The latter was funded by the National Endowment for the Humanities and involved photo research of public and private archives, my own photography and assigning photographers in areas susch as Japan, the Phillipines, and Kosovo.

Leif Skoogfors’s Interests:
