Matt Hemmendinger’s Summary

The campaign trail was great excitement and fun. It provided a great transition from college into the working world. Now I want to hone these skills and utilize them to move progressive policy forward that will help the community in which I reside. I would love to advocate for universal broadband access, a wider-reaching alternative transportation system as well as help Colorado earn more money off of the minerals that are extracted from its land.
In addition to political advocacy, helping kids enjoy the game of baseball is a favorite hobby of mine. I hated the umpires that called my games as I kid, but now I'm doing my best to get those calls right so the players can enjoy a well-called game and focus on the more important half of it - not winning, but growing to be a better person through the positive experiences in sports and athletics.

  • Field Director

    Geno Zamora for Attorney General

    (Political Organization industry)

    January 2006 — May 2006 (5 months)

    I organized all field operations for Geno's primary bid for New Mexico's Attorney General. Our initial focus was on submitting the requisite nominating signatures to place Geno on the ballot. The office required approximately 3000 valid signatures and we easily met this goal collecting over 8000 total signatures.
    The second half of placing Geno on the ballot was earning the requisite 20% of support at the Democratic Pre-Primary nominating convention. Through dilegent outreach to convention delegates we surprised all onlookers and won the nominating convention vote and placed Geno's name at the top of the ballot.
    Going forward, the field program consisted of tradition voter outreach such as knocking on doors and making phone calls as well as non-traditional contact by utilizing special events, internet forums and interest groups. Geno would go on to lose to the son of popular former Governor Bruce King, but he has now solidified his name as a rising star in New Mexican politics.
