  • Member

    Workforce Planning Community of Practice

    (Human Resources industry)

    2008 — 2009 (1 year)

    The primary purpose of this group is to ensure leading-edge workforce planning practices, including the implementation of workforce plans.
    The greatest benefit to member organizations is the ability to collaborate, rather than compete, to ensure the workforce needs of the future are available through collective initiatives which have greater influence on social change, government policy, and general workforce awareness and planning.
    Participants are senior HR professionals from operating organizations who have accountability for workforce planning.
    Diversity of membership is valued and promoted; including municipal, co-operative, agriculture, oil and gas, retail, and other energy sector representative company members.
    The Community of Practice is a working group, where active participation by all members is essential and has contributed much to it's ongoing success.
    Current work being done includes a Capability and Maturity Model for Workforce Planning, a defining Matrix and assessment tool, and a variety of data sources and analytics including networking connections that are being complied to support the supply and demand research of these companies.
