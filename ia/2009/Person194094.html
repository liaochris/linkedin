Rasmus Klarskov Storm’s Summary

Rasmus K. Storm holds a position as senior academic researcher at the Danish Institute for Sports Studies (Idan), moreover he is PhD Scholar at the University of Southern Denmark and Idan. Storm is engaged in elite sport and sports economy.

He has previously managed several research projects on Danish elite sport, and has recently contributed to an international journal on Scandinavian Soccer (forthcoming title on Routledge, Soccer and Society) together with editing a book on Danish team handball and one on sports in the Danish experience economy.

Rasmus is regularly interviewed in the Danish media; moreover he gives a large number of public lectures and writes regularly in Danish newspapers.

Rasmus Klarskov Storm’s Specialties:

Sports Economics, Sports Sociology, Public administration.

Rasmus Klarskov Storm’s Experience

  • Senior Academic Researcher & Ph.d. Scholar

    Danish Institute for Sports Studies

    (Research industry)

    October 2008 — Present (6 months)

    My work at the Danish Institute for Sports Studies is centred on three main topics:

    1) research in the field of sports economics
    2) research in the field of sports sociology
    3) communication of research results to the public

    I have published (edited and co-edited) three books: One on Danish professional soccer (2005), one on Danish sport in the experience economy (2008) and one on Danish Team Handball (forthcoming in 2008). Recently I completed a research based evaluation on the Danish elite sport organization, and equivalent of UK Sport, Team Danmark. At the moment, besides working on my thesis, I work on an international anthology on modern systems theory and sports (Working title: 'Observing Sports') with Ulrik Wagner, University of Copenhagen and John Hoberman (University of Texas).

    Please feel free to look at the complete list of publications and other activities following the ‘my portfolio link’ below.
