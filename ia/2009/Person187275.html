Phillip Tomé’s Summary

04/2009 - Present: Senior Scientific Collaborator at the Institute of Microengineering, Swiss Federal Institute of Technology of Lausanne
Engaged on the research activities of the Electronics and Signal Processing Laboratory (ESPLAB) related to indoor Local Positioning Systems (LPS) based on Ultra-Wide-Band (UWB) technology.

Navigation expert and technical leader responsible for the company's second most important program aiming the integration of GNSS and dead reckoning sensors (inertial MEMS sensors, pressure and magnetic sensors) for consumer market equipment (PNDs, mobile phones), targeting both car and pedestrian navigation.

Responsible for the general management of the center’s headquarters and the ongoing R&D projects. Responsible for project acquisition and contracting.

Phillip Tomé’s Experience

  • Senior Scientific Collaborator

    École Polytechnique Fédérale de Lausanne

    (Research industry)

    April 2009 — Present (1 month)

    Study, conception and development of a large-scale demonstrator of an indoor Local Positioning System (LPS) for locating a very large number of ASIC-based tag-transceivers using Ultra-Wide-Band (UWB) communications, which includes the following specific activities:
    1 - development of multi-layers high frequency PCBs;
    2 - development of additional ASIC blocks;
    3 - development of methods and algorithms to:
    (a) synchronize a large number of fixed UWB receivers;
    (b) detect the tags' transmissions and estimate their time-of arrivals at each receiver;
    (c) collect the data from the large number of fixed UWB receivers to a central computer;
    (d) calculate the positions of the tags.
