Ryoma (Ray) Collia-Suzuki’s Summary

Specialist in dynamic optimisation, planning and scheduling.

Magenta customers typically experience an 8-10% rise in efficiencies and a 5-7% reduction in fuel costs (where there is a fuel based element) following deployment. The incremental business improvements made possible by Magenta tend to focus on 3 areas;

- Real-time events - Many businesses are negatively impacted by real-time events or cannot exploit the opportunities created by real-time events. Examples of these events include late order requests, delayed supply of goods, broken down assets, high operator absenteeism, last second changes in schedules, unforeseen circumstances leading to ripple effects in the service or delivery process, etc.

- Uncertainty - Businesses plan to create certainty and reliability in execution of business processes. However, limitations with the current generation of technology usually leads to a design approach which builds inefficiencies in to the business model. A classic example is the building of fixed routes in transportation networks leading to lots of empty vehicles being run. A dynamic approach to network routing is now feasible and offers large efficiency gains in some types of business models.

- Dependencies - Supply chain, asset management, planning and scheduling decisions often involve complex decision dependencies, e.g. certain loads cannot be mixed, production A cannot follow production B without a clearing process, availability of suitably skilled staff or suitably fitted vehicles may be limited, etc. A new order might impact previous decisions on order splitting, routing and previous planning decisions.

Our solution has been described as one that transforms businesses.
