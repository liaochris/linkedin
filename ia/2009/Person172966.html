Neil Robinson’s Summary

"a good consultant should be what the client wants." but just how many do you know who are?

Well, I believe I am. You see, LANZen wasn't really my idea. I was working for a big global consultancy and my client (a major City bank) suggested they would like it better if I was able to work without the constraints and limitations of some company with an agenda they couldn't really relate to compromising the services I was providing.

So LANZen, the strategy consultancy was born. I had a clear brief. To deliver what works best, no if's, but's or maybe's. Technology resourced from wherever, doing what it should. And you know what, it works. For my clients, for me and it will work for you as well.

There aren't many consultancies whose first client is their biggest and stays with them from the very beginning through three changes of corporate partner. Or who gets to start off Jamie Oliver's TV career in Jamie's Kitchen, featured on Channel 4.

But its not all about big city clients and famous names. Its about setting a clear direction for your business. Its about getting it right, first time. About not wasting money, no false starts. About finding a great way of working that releases your true business potential. In other words, its about you.

After all, that's how it should be, isn't it?

Neil Robinson’s Experience

  • LANZen Limited

    (Information Technology and Services industry)

    February 2000 — Present (9 years)

    I started out as an IT engineer - fixing things that stopped people working.

    That gave me a unique approach to IT strategy and design. How can I fix what doesn't work and make what does work, work better?

    That worked. I found I was designing corporate infrastructures in City banks, national service centres and even helping prestige clients like Jamie Oliver.

    But after years of designing enterprise level infrastructures around Microsoft architectures, I've realised the Internet is changing everything.

    What used to cost a great deal of money to build in a data centre has been built much better in "The Cloud" - the Internet.

    So, that's what LANZen is about today. Tomorrow, who knows what we'll be able to do. I just know that I'll be keeping LANZen looking forward.

    I work on HOW to do it and let my clients concentrate on WHAT they want to do.
