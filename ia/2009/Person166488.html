Michael Primeaux’s Summary

Michael Primeaux is the Founder and Chief Architect of iDynamics Corporation with over 19 years of experience specializing in the design and development of large-scale parallel and distributed systems.

Current theoretical and applied research topics include the design of distributed algorithms in the areas of High-Performance Computing (HPC), Event Stream Processing (ESP), autonomic computing, the semantic web, Peer-to-Peer (P2P), HTTP-based service orientation, real-time trading for the Forex market, and adaptive autonomous agents for parallel and distributed systems.

Michael has spent the last sixteen years providing architectural guidance, software design engineering, and technology trending analysis to Fortune 500 companies.

Before founding iDynamics, Michael spent six years at Microsoft Corporation first as a member of their consulting practice and then as a principal member in several product groups, including the Distributed Systems group working on Microsoft Windows 2000, where he designed and developed core algorithms in the Microsoft Active Directory Service, and Database and Transaction Processing group, advancing the design of highly scalable and fault tolerant systems.

Classical areas of interest include cryptology, synchronization algorithms, geometry and topology analysis, and distributed scheduling.

Michael Primeaux’s Specialties:

Parallel and distributed system design. Specific research interests in autonomic computing, adaptive autonomous agents and multi-agent systems, distributed scheduling, grid computing, recovery-oriented systems, security, the semantic web, automatic system topology generation, normalization of multi-parented tree structures, and abstract data modeling.
