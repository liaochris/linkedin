Sam Wayne’s Summary

Sharp, professional and collegial attorney with focused academic experience in business law and contract negotiation and professional experience in litigation.

On August 1 I will open my law office, Madison Business Law, LLC, and look forward to representing members of the Madison, WI business community.

Sam Wayne’s Specialties:

Contract Negotiation, Business Startups and Organization, Dispute Mediation

Sam Wayne’s Experience

  • Judicial Intern

    Wisconsin Supreme Court

    (Government Administration industry)

    August 2008 — January 2009 (6 months)

    Wrote memos and advised Justice Patrick Crooks and his clerk on:
    • Appropriateness of direct claim for breach of duty of loyalty against majority shareholder in closely-held corporation.
    • Subrogation and spoliation of evidence in multi-party fire insurance case.
    • Unilateral changes to public employee benefits programs.
    • Balance of relevance and undue prejudice to a criminal defendant of introduction of prior criminal conviction to prove a status element.

    Evaluated oral arguments on cases I briefed.

  • Summer Associate

    Cadwalader, Wickersham & Taft LLP

    (Partnership; 1001-5000 employees; Legal Services industry)

    May 2008 — August 2008 (4 months)

    Assisted corporate counsel in preparation of loan closing documents, model leaseback agreements, letters to opposing counsel and a review of terms of recent private equity purchases.

    Advanced promising theory of ineffective counsel for inclusion in habeas corpus petition for federal death row prisoner.

    Evaluated chances of striking evidence procured from client's employee by fraud.

    Researched for an article on changes to Medicare and private insurance coverage for the New York Law Journal.

  • Law Clerk

    Wisconsin Department of Justice

    (Government Administration industry)

    May 2007 — January 2008 (9 months)

    Prepared consumer protection cases for review by Assistant Attorneys General. Advised AAG's on litigation strategy, performed legal research, drafted memos, motions and complaints, interviewed victims and witnesses, and assisted in successful settlement negotiations.

  • Policy/Legislative Intern

    Office of Governor Jim Doyle

    (Government Administration industry)

    January 2006 — June 2006 (6 months)

    Authored paper on supplemental health plans and universal coverage programs in other states which Governor's team used to craft legislation expanding state health coverage with BadgerCare Plus.

    Wrote over 75 bill briefs for presentation to Governor Doyle. These documents highlight the bill's legislative history, fiscal and policy impacts, and interested organizations' opinions.

  • Analyst

    Wisconsin Department of Health Services

    (Government Administration industry)

    August 2005 — January 2006 (6 months)

    Advised Division of Public Health on how best to apply new data to their local department funding structure.

    Authored study creating a methodology to evaluate the performance of state and local public health departments.
