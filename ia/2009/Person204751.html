Robin Salant’s Summary

Artist, Photographer, Curator with deep interest in social service and ideological intervention. Will travel for work, durable and adept traveler, culturally sensitive and pragmatic. Currently shooting an intrinsic character portrait project and developing a talent incubator and collaboration hub for photographers, film-makers and other artists. Always on the lookout for an adventurous opportunity to travel, participate, and document.

Also interested in gathering teammates to purchase and develop a sustainable permaculture mixed residential property in North Carolina, which would provide multiple examples of sustainable and alternative building for educational purposes.

Robin Salant’s Specialties:

conceptual curating, participation, leadership, event planning, crisis intervention and suicide prevention, social entrepreneurship, sustainable building and alternative materials, live performance photography, creative problem-solving, international travel experience.

  • Director, Curator

    Space 2B

    (Fine Art industry)

    2000 — 2002 (2 years )

    Directed, managed, curated, collaborated. Following an initial installation of Antarctica (35 interactive slide projectors, surround sound, a field of eggshells and a waterfall) I used this underground artspace to house multiple interactive art installations and happenings. Space 2B was open to the public, bridging communities and fostering teamwork and collaborative productions between artists of different genres and schools. I also taught a pinhole camera workshop at Space 2B which was followed by an exhibition of the cameras made by students, which included a cigar box, and old television, and a suitcase, and the silver prints students made using them. The opening of a cooperative community darkroom at Space 2B accompanied the pinhole exhibition.
