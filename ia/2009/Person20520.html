Lori Milani’s Summary

I graduated with two AAS in Communication Design (print) and Interactive Design from Northern Virginia Community College, Alexandria Campus, in December 2007.

While taking classes, I have done freelance jobs for several student groups on campus and The Africa Society of the National Summit on Africa, a non-profit organization in DC. I also have designed a Flash website for The Altered Book Project that is hosted on the Northern Virginia Community College web.

I do both print design and interactive design for the web. I love doing my own illustration, photography and fine arts, which I sometimes use for my designs. I'm fluent in English, Spanish, Italian and French. I can also speak basic German and Portuguese, read Cyrillic and understand/speak a little (very little!) Russian.

My goal is to find (or invent!) an exciting job where I can combine my creative thinking and art/design skills with my writing skills, knowledge of different languages, and passion for humanitarian/environmental causes. I'm particularly interested in social marketing, advertising, visual journalism, children's books, and greeting cards (2-D, 3-D, interactive).

Right now, I'm interested in freelance/contract work, paid graphic design internships (above $12 per hour, 20-25 hours a week) or part-time positions. I need some time to keep taking classes and eventually get a BFA in Graphic Design!

Lori Milani’s Specialties:

Adobe Creative Suite for print and web (Photoshop, Illustrator, InDesign, Dreamweaver, Flash); digital and traditional illustration; typographic layout; photography (SLR & DSLR); B&W darkroom; video/sound editing; copywriting; storytelling; project managing; basic XHTML, basic CSS, and basic ActionScript2.
