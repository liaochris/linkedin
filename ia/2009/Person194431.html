    Affiniti

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; KCOM; Information Technology and Services industry)

    January 2000 — Present (9 years 7 months)

    Post sales consultancy on a number of major projects requiring security infrastructure expertise. The role is primarily delivering design and engineering documentation with about thirty percent of my time involved with proof of concept labs or supporting internal
    Project Highlight #1:
    A global investment bank based in Boston USA asked Affiniti to handle multiple data centre re-locations in England and Scotland.
    I was assigned the role of lead consultant for the security elements of the project in addition to the secure Cisco Centralised VoWLAN design. There were several security zones under consideration:
    Internet Tier (Juniper Firewalls, Blue Coat proxy -WCCP)
    Intranet and Extranet Tier (Juniper Firewalls)
    Web VPN tier Juniper (VPN appliance)
    User and Voice Distribution (Cisco FWSM)
    VoWLAN (Cisco WLC,ACS and WCS with EAP-TLS Authentication)

    In addition to design, I was asked to implement the more complex elements of the secure WLAN because the customer did not have experience of this technology.
    Project Highlight #2:
    A Large regional Police force asked us to upgrade several discrete Checkpoint clusters to a centralised and resilient Cisco FWSM topology. I was asked to design the infrastructure layout and convert the rule base. Despite the advantage of the Cisco security conversion tool, the rule base conversion required some degree of close inspection and modification.
    Project Highlight #3:
    A national Building Society asked Affiniti to help them deploy a secure branch network. I designed a secure DMVPN network that incorporated Cisco's NAC appliance with single sign on for all spoke network users as well as incorporating a VPN server on the resilient head end devices for remote access workers who would also be inspected by the NAC component.

    The customer was highly delighted with the design and the implementation and has since been a referee for potential customers investigating the benefits of Cisco's NAC solution.
