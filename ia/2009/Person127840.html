Kathy Lewis’s Summary

I am currently seeking other employment opportunities to ultimately enhance my career as a chemist.

Productive and motivated synthetic organic/medicinal chemist with a proven track record in multistep synthesis with modern methods and techniques as well as solving synthetic challenges. Many years of industrial experience in biotech/pharmaceutical including experience in contract research and parallel synthesis. Able to propose new target molecules based upon SAR or structure-based design where applicable. A team player with the ability to independently conduct important experiments to further projects as well as analyze results and implement new experimental protocols where necessary to further individual projects as well as team goals. Consistently generates reliable experimental results and effectively communicates results at team meetings as well as synthetic challenges and solutions.

Kathy Lewis’s Specialties:

Synthetic organic chemistry, multistep synthesis, contract research, route development for multistep synthesis and parallel synthesis, organization, analytical and problem solving skills

  • Agouron Pharmaceuticals

    (Biotechnology industry)

    September 1987 — 1999 (12 years)

    Synthetic chemistry-more development, synthesis and purification of small molecule targets for projects such as

    1) HIV Integrase ---Prepared many compounds difficult to synthesize and purify. Frequently employed reverse phase flash column chromatography for purification of final compounds

    2) HCMV Protease---Successfully completed targets requiring a fair number of steps and whose properties made them difficult to work with.

    3) Matrix Metalloprotease ---Employed frequent use of crystallization as many targets were difficult to purify.

    4) HIV Protease ---Successfully completed several difficult syntheses as well as several scaleups to provide valuable intermediates for other members in the project team. Rapidly developed a multistep synthesis of a particular subunit and delivered the final target in less than two months from the birth of the idea at a consultant meeting

    6)HCV Polymerase--- Extensive use of Biotage to purify intermediates and final targets. Volunteered to take over the scale-up synthesis of a crucial lead compound from a fellow teammate who had experienced difficulty with its preparation. The lead was successfully completed within the tight deadline in sufficient quantities for PK experiments thus allowing the project team to reach a critical go/no go decision for the series.

    7) Thymidilate Synthase-Accomplished multi-step synthesis of a series of quinazoline targets for the anti-tumor project.
