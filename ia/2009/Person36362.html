Charlotte Cain Lajoie, LMHC’s Summary

Charlotte Cain Lajoie grew up in Wilmette, Illinois and received an undergraduate degree in Business Administration from Saint Mary's College, Notre Dame, Indiana. She learned first hand the benefits of team work as a member of the Notre Dame Women's Varsity Rowing Team.

Her career began in Chicago, in financial accounting, working for Chicago Title and Trust, and continued with her sister firm, Chicago Title Insurance Company, in the Southeastern United States. She extended her business education in a graduate MBA program.

She was intensely trained in change management and success psychology by the Pacific Institute in Seattle, Washington and in turn became a psychological educator. She joined a national team that shaped the architecture of the company as it restructured after a buyout; working with senior and middle management, and national customers. Since then, human potential and development became a passion.

An accomplished manager, she received the coveted, Distinguished Achievement Award, for increased profits while managing an underwriting office in Florida.

She was a founding board member of a local charity that took a concept and made it a reality; The Samaritan Center, a long term transitional shelter for Families with dependent children, a program of Catholic Charities of the Diocese of Palm Beach. She currently sits on this board.

With the advances in neuroscience dominating the field of business development, she obtained clinical experience to balance her business accomplishments and background. She is an accredited consultant of the Hay Group, Boston, Mass.

She incorporated Cainlajoie Consulting in 2009 and works with corporations and individuals to get results from properly structured goal setting and an honest assessment.
