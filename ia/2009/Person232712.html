Thomas O'Neill, Ph.D.’s Summary

I have worked in the certification/licensure testing industry since 1990. I have experience with fixed-form written tests, fixed-form computer-based test, computerized adaptive tests, and performance assessments that control for rater severity. I have worked with small scale examinations that have tested only a few people per year and large scale programs that have tested over 250,000 people per year. I have worked with certification & licensure tests, entrance tests, and test that provide feedback to medical residency programs.

My interests are primarily in measurement issues and the detection of cheating. I enjoy research, presenting research, and making testing programs run smoothly.

My professional mission is to provide high quality measurement in arenas that matter.

Thomas O'Neill, Ph.D.’s Specialties:

Rasch Measurement, Equating, Standard Setting, Job/Practice Analysis, building Test Plan Specifications, and Item Analysis.
