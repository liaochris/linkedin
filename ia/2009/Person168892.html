MRITUNJAY PANDEY’s Summary

Founder and Managing Director

A history honours scholar, he gravitated to the media just when television was opening up and new technologies were bringing personal entertainment home to consumers. His first stint was with Pahlaj Bajaj & Co, then sole concessionaire for Doordarshan sports. During this period, the state broadcaster served up a series of big-ticket sporting events to a sports-hungry nation, and Mritunjay earned precious experience in marketing Wimbledon, World Cup Football, Sharjah Cricket, Australasia Cup, etc.
Launched a Bollywood video magazine under the brand name Lehren, way back in 1987, which became an industry standard. He steered his company’s association towards many popular and successful TV programming and marketing, such as, Chitrahaar, Mrityunjaya, Mungeri Ke Bhai Naurangilal, Imtihan, Naya Nukkad, Khiladi, Lehren. He also brought the Miss World contest to India in 1997, to herald a profusion of such pageants. Commercializing feature films on TV all throughout the 1990s was one of Mritunjay’s biggest strengths. In 1995, Pandey’s company formed a joint venture with Amitabh Bachchan Corporation Limited for marketing and production of television content, and later in 1999 with Nimbus for the World Cup Cricket in England. Similar eventful associations happened with Sony Entertainment Television and Channel Nine Gold (DD Metro).
Mritunjay Pandey launched Lehren Entertainment Private Limited (aka Lehren Technologies Pvt Ltd) in 1999 just when the media began to acquire the Internet dimension. Foreseeing the potential of the medium, he digitized the entire Lehren library – some 10,000 hours – and began to make plans for a multi-media foray ranging from satellite television to IPTV, personal media, and digital products, all centered around Bollywood, which he knows like the back of his hand.
Lehren, India’s First 24 hours Bollywood News & Entertainment channel is the outcome of his cohesive vision.
