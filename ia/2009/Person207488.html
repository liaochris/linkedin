  • Defense Red Switch Network Engineer

    ITT Systems

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; ITT; Information Technology and Services industry)

    May 2007 — September 2008 (1 year 5 months)

    Completed 1.5 years as a defense red switch network (DRSN) engineer for ITT Systems Division in Kuwait. As lead technician I spearheaded operational and maintenance activities for millions of dollars of Department of Defense communications infrastructure. I was responsible for secure (encrypted) command and control capabilities for U.S. military forces in Kuwait in support of the global DRSN network. I worked seamlessly with counterparts from the Defense Information Systems Agency, Raytheon, and the U.S. Army while executing my duties.

    Accomplishments include but are not limited to:

    • Planned/executed SCIF accreditation project authorizing TS/SCI security access level communications.

    • Planned/engineered/oversaw 200k protective distribution system (PDS) installation project for SCIF accreditation.

    • Integrated/programmed Cisco VoSIP call manager with DRSN equipment -- enabled VoSIP/DRSN interoperability with secure voice customers world wide.

    • Supervised/managed/lead five technicians of mixed age, skill, and background.

    • Supported various contingency operations requiring DRSN service in remote locations.

    • Received outstanding performance appraisal for my efforts.

    The Defense Red Switch Network is the Department of Defense (DoD) global senior level secure voice telephone and conferencing system. Its mission is to provide rapid, high quality secure and non-secure communications services to special command and control (C2) and other users. For more information visit: www.disa.mil/news/pressresources/factsheets/drsn.html.
