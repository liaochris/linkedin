Madhur Malik’s Summary

Consumer credit risk analyst, specialized in credit scoring, portfolio credit risk management, Basel II implementation and stress testing. Developed models for stress testing of portfolio of consumer loans by incorporating macroeconomic variables in the framework of Survival analysis and Markov Chains. Implemented scalar methodology to get through the cycle long run average probability of default and managed models for Impairment Provisions and RWA forecasting for mortgage portfolios. Interested in working on different areas of financial risk associated with consumer and corporate loans.

Madhur Malik’s Specialties:

Credit Scoring, Portfolio Credit Risk, Stress Testing, Impairment Provisions, Basel II

Madhur Malik’s Experience

  • Senior Analyst Portfolio Modelling

    Lloyds Banking Group

    (Public Company; Banking industry)

    November 2008 — Present (11 months)

    In my current role I work on testing, implementation and management of the risk models for forecasting regulatory capital and impairment provisions for mortgage portfolios. I tested and implemented scalar methodology for calculating long-run-average TTC PD’s. I run scenario based forecasting of bad debts for economic stress testing. Besides, I analyse parameter change impact on capital and impairment provision model, prepare documentation for senior management and liaise with key stakeholders.

  • Research Analyst

    University of Southampton

    (Educational Institution; Research industry)

    September 2005 — October 2008 (3 years 2 months)

    I developed hazard rate/survival analysis based credit risk models on SAS for credit card portfolios by incorporating economic time series variables to estimate default correlation between customers. I also developed Markov Chain models on SAS to forecast portfolio credit risk over longer horizons where the transition probabilities are made functions of the state of the economy, months on books and the behavioural scores. These modelling approaches could be expected to have an impact on the way banks segment and stress test their portfolios under the current Basel II regulations. My analysis is based on large real data sets of customers provided by major UK and Hong Kong banks.

  • Credit Risk Analyst

    HSBC Bangalore

    (Public Company; HBC; Banking industry)

    March 2005 — August 2005 (6 months)

    My role was to construct application scorecards for US credit card customers to assess their default risk. I processed raw data sets and built application scorecards using SAS, Model Builder (Fair Isaac Product) and Excel.
