Kate Duttro’s Summary

As an independent career consultant/coach, I delight in working with extraordinary individuals with advanced degrees who want to modify their career journey.

I write the Career Change for Academics Blog for current and recovering academics, and I write for Job-Hunt.org, as the Careers for Academics expert.

Before I retired (2009), I provided career services for more than 10 years at the University of Washington, where I advised individuals, taught classes and workshops, and dug out information for thousands of undergrads, grad students, post docs and alumni in all phases of career development.

Issues I've helped students and alumni tackle include decisions on career choice, choosing grad schools and finding money, leaving grad school or finishing, exploring careers, searching for (and finding) jobs, and changing or reinvigorating careers.

My own earlier career has included academic positions, non-academic positions in academic institutions, freelance gigs, and jobs in businesses, with the full range of hourly/salary full-time/part-time experience. When it comes to working inside, outside and around academics, I've been there!
