Ray Keefe’s Summary

Starting out from my interest in music, I studied electrical and electronic engineering and initially designed and commisioned customised professional recording equipment including commissioning a recording studio and upgrading another overseas.

That led to instrumentation design, software development, project management, International product development, International patents, line management and now owning my own electronics product development company, Successful Endeavours.

I hope to add significantly to the wealth of Australians through creating the opportunity for better and more successful products for Australian manufacturers. This will boost employment, exports, future opportunities and generally improve everyone's position.

I want this to be a great country for my grandchildren to thrive in.

Ray Keefe’s Experience

  • Owner

    Successful Endeavours Pty Ltd

    (Electrical/Electronic Manufacturing industry)

    1998 — Present (11 years)

    Successful Endeavours is a privately owned electronics and software development consultancy particularly focussing on small to medium sized Australian manufacturers who want to improve their existing products, margins or market share through development of their next generation of market offerings.

    As a software developer with an honors degree in Electrical and Electronic Engineering and additional studies in physics and chemistry, I can see the potential for huge improvements in product function, reliability and margin through applying the right mix of electronics hardware and software to both new and existing market opportunities.

    Having an advantage over your competition requires more than just working hard these days. You also need to leverage all the angles available to you.

    This is where we come in.

  • Major Product Development Manager

    Invetech

    (Public Company; DHR; Mechanical or Industrial Engineering industry)

    1988 — 1997 (9 years)

    During this time I worked on a number of large projects aimed at launching new international products in the medical and fire protection industries.

    These included diagnostic support instruments focussed on automating histology sample processing for hospitals and independent laboratories, and the VESDA LASER based smoke and particle detection system.

    Toward the end of this period I ran the group responsible for all these projects. During the time I also ran several of the projects and looked after recruiting and managing of electronics and embedded software development.

  • Electronics Development Engineer

    Varian Techtron Pty Ltd

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; VAR; Medical Devices industry)

    1986 — 1988 (2 years)

    I was part of a small team in Melbourne developing a new generation of UV and Visible Light spectrophotometers capable of a 6 decade measurement range withotu rear beam attenuators. This was an industry first.

    My focus was the power supplies including several switch mode converters, digital electronics, customised dual slope ADC and ROM sequencer and interconnections.

    This was a fantastic introduction to instrumentation design for international markets. There were some very talented people I learnt from during this time.

  • Broadcast Engineer

    Australian Broadcasting Corporation

    (Government Agency; Broadcast Media industry)

    1984 — 1986 (2 years)

    Television Broadcast Engineering. This covers many things. I oversaw equipoment upgrades, wrote and reviewed tenders for equipment supply and was involved in outside broadcast support.

    Specific things I enjoyed participating was the outfitting of the new post production suite including the Harrision Rainbow MR3 mixing desk, purchase of upgraded monitors for the television studios and outside broadcast, and upgrading to a common PABX across all 14 Melbourne locations covering Television and Radio.

  • Freelance Audio Engineer

    Freelance Audio

    (Music industry)

    1983 — 1985 (2 years)

    As I studied I realised that there were a lot of things I could do that were really useful. Such as customise special purpose audio electronics equipment, and even design new electronics equipment from scratch. And that is what I did for a couple of years.

    At this stage I knew nothing about business but I did have a lot of fun. This including designing and building all the equipment for a recording studio from scratch including the sound desk, amplifiers, monitors and effects equipment. The only thing I didn't try was to build my own tape deck.

    I also did some volunteer work with FEBA in Pakistan, again developing and upgrading a recording studio in Faisalabad. I learnt a lot about inequity in the world during this time and saw the opportunity I had due to my birth in a western country and the responsibility this has with it to try and make things better for everyone.
