Ken Chang’s Summary

From my summer internship at Omnivision, contractor job at Synaptics, to my current job at Intersil, I've done various types of testing. At Omnivision and at Intersil, I was fortunate to work along side many analog designers and managers and work with them on design evaluations and validation. I naturally grew admiration and interest toward analog circuit design as my future goal is to become one.

I was exposed to interesting products. Those product include Omnivision's CMOS imaging sensor chips, Synaptic's touch sticks and various capacitive sensing touch pad, as well as Intersil's LDO chips, switching regulator chips , and automotive battery cell monitoring chips. Those are all different products and having experience working with various products broadens my view and increases my creativity.

From the first paragraph, I've stated that my goal is to become an analog circuit designer; however, my real long term goal is to be an entrepreneur. I wanted to build a company that sells interesting products. However, the lack of good ideas and financial opportunities might be holding me back on building my own business entity.

My current goal is to finish up my MSEE degree at Santa Clara and learn as much as I can during work while being able to contribute to my company as much possible.
