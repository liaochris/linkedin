Jørgen Søberg Petersen’s Summary

Twelve years of pharmaceutical and biotech industry experience with executive management experience in both Drug Discovery and Drug Development. Before entering industry in 1997, I spent 10 years in academic and clinical medicine in Denmark and in the US.

As Senior Vice President at Merck Serono I am responsible for the global pipeline in Autoimmune and Inflammatory Diseases and for research organizations in Geneva, Switzerland and Darmstadt, Germany. In addition, I am responsible for the diabetes research organization based in Chily-Marzarin near Paris, France.

Co-founder, Chief Scientific Officer and Executive Vice President at Zealand Pharma. Under my leadership we advanced eight (8) molecules into development in eight years of which two were ‘first-in-class compounds’.

Key person in major pharmaceutical deals and alliances. Member of executive management team and closely involved in fund raising from major Danish and European venture capital funds (€ 102 million). Key person in IPO preparations in 2005.

Demonstrated ability to build and lead a world-class, high performance team that successfully has moved innovative projects forward towards commercialization.

Demonstrated ability to serve as a project champion and to establish strong international networks in both academic and industrial settings.

Strong background in academic and clinical medicine.

Theoretical and practical experience in management of technical, organizational, financial, regulatory, and market risks in Drug Discovery and Drug Development.

Effective leadership profile in both strategic R&D management and at the team level.
