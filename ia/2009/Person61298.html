Demetrius ("Jim") Maraganore’s Summary

I lead a large, multidisciplinary research team at the Mayo Clinic in Rochester, Minnesota, USA. The mission of our research is to develop methods to predict, prevent, and halt Parkinson's disease.

We and our collaborators have shown that common variations in the alpha-synuclein gene are associated with an increased risk of Parkinson's disease in populations worldwide, via a mechanism of over-expression (too much protein). We are developing a method of treating Parkinson's disease. It involves the use of small interfering RNA molecules to reduce alpha-synuclein expression.

I also lead the Genetic Epidemiology of Parkinson's Disease (GEO-PD) Consortium. Our consortium includes investigators from 37 global sites, 22 countries, and six continents. We are sharing DNA specimens and data for more than 24,916 Parkinson's disease cases and 23,001 control subjects, and conducting collaborative studies of genetic risk factors for Parkinson's disease. We hope that these studies will also lead to additional methods to predict, prevent, and halt Parkinson's disease.

Finally, the approaches that have yielded success for Parkinson's disease can also be applied to other brain disorders. Our Mayo research team is developing DNA fingerprints within biochemical pathways that predict not only Parkinson's disease, but also ALS, Alzheimer's, stroke, ADHD, schizophrenia, bipolar disorder, and major depression.
