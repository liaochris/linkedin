Brett Holt’s Summary

I'm currently a young professional looking to make a career within the aviation industry. Corporate and charter aviation has interested me from the beginning. I enjoy the ability to offer one-on-one service with my customers. This probably stems from growing up with parents who both owned their own successful businesses. I was taught from day one the importance of ethical hard work, punctuality, and completing work I can be proud of. I thrive on being challenged and immersed in the aviation industry.

My work at CAE SimuFlite has offered me a unique way to transition into the crew environment of flying jet aircraft. I've been through pilot in command initial and recurrent classes for both the Beechjet and Falcon 900. As a result I'm PIC type rated on the BE-40 and Part 135 SIC qualified on the DA-50. When our clients do not have a training partner, I fill in as either captain or co-pilot in full-motion, level-d simulator training. This equates to over 500 hours of extensive training with nearly 100 clients from all around the world.

I'm also an active general aviation flight instructor. I teach private through flight instructor applicants in both FAA parts 61 and 141. This instruction takes place primarily in newer model Cessna aircraft.

In the past I've served as an intern for American Eagle Flight Operations. There I was responsible for gathering pilot information and generating their FAA training documents (8410's and Temporary Airman Certificates). I also managed, organized, and distributed company training manuals.

I'm currently seeking any opportunities both flying and non-flying in order to advance my career within the aviation industry. I am willing to relocate.
