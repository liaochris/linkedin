Nicole C. Wong’s Summary

I'm a multimedia journalist at work and a cultural anthropologist at heart. I love communicating compelling stories -- with words, photos, infographics, audio and video -- about intriguing people, communities and companies, especially as they find creative ways to tackle challenges arising from technology, globalization or other larger-than-life forces. My resourcefulness, creativity and versatility have enabled me to quickly learn the inner-workings and cultures of businesses, education systems, government bodies and foreign cities and to distill important ideas about them on tight deadlines.

I have recorded and edited audio interviews and video footage using GarageBand, iMovie, and Final Cut Pro for multimedia reporting packages that were posted on major metropolitan newspapers' websites. I am proficient in HTML and database-analysis software and have taught peers how to use Microsoft Word, Excel, Access, and PowerPoint.

When I'm not dashing around reporting, I'm sharing my expertise on a variety of topics as a guest on local and national TV and radio newscasts and as a speaker for groups ranging from the National Association of Asian American Professionals' Boston Chapter to Harvard University's Crimson Summer Academy.

Nicole C. Wong’s Specialties:

Spotting trends. Collecting and analyzing data. Brainstorming and conceptualizing unique approaches to deal with common challenges. Discerning consumers' needs and interests. Reaching out to the community and developing mutually fruitful relationships. Collaborating with multifunction teams internally and externally. Managing multiple large projects simultaneously with little supervision. Recruiting and assessing job candidates.

Nicole C. Wong’s Experience

  • Business Reporter

    The Boston Globe, part of The New York Times Co.

    (Public Company; NYT; Online Media industry)

    September 2007 — Present (1 year 6 months)

    Cover workplace issues and the business and culture of travel, transportation and tourism. Craft nationally appealing articles that have been reprinted across the country and in the International Herald Tribune (the overseas edition of the New York Times). Contribute to the Globe-trotting and Passport travel blogs, host online chats, and post live reporting updates through Twitter. Shoot and edit photos and videos to accompany my stories on our website.   • Technology and Workplace Issues reporter

    San Jose Mercury News

    (Privately Held; Newspapers industry)

    January 2005 — July 2007 (2 years 7 months)

    Covered technology companies, tech culture and workplace issues. Produced the most A1 stories of all the business reporters during my first year on the staff, ranging from data-driven daily articles about Silicon Valley’s job market to enterprise articles I pitched on valley workers’ culture of corporate cafeteria hopping and employees who fly small planes to work to escape the expensive housing, irritating commute traffic and lack of leisure time. Helped design a first-of-its-kind survey examining regional underemployment and wrote a 1A article about the toll this underutilization of work abilities took. As the youngest reporter on the business staff, covered the world's largest tech company (Hewlett-Packard) and uncovered how it was subtly reshaping its worldwide workforce as opportunities arose from technological advances and pressures mounted from global competition. Recorded and edited audio to post online accompanying select articles. Blogged during the Consumer Electronics Show.

  • Assigning Editor and Journalism Instructor

    Mosaic Urban Journalism Workshop

    (Primary/Secondary Education industry)

    March 2005 — July 2005 (5 months)

    Picked by the San Jose Mercury News to help run its two-week summer reporting institute while I continued working full time as a business reporter. Helped recruit the largest applicant pool of high school students in the program's history. Collaborated with other committee members to select a diverse group of 20 students to participate in the institute that's also sponsored by the Dow Jones Newspaper Fund, San Jose State University, and other Bay Area media outlets and foundations. Taught reporting sessions. Assigned and edited stories. Coached students on improving their writing and storytelling skills. Proofread production sheets. Organized evening and weekend professional development and social events, including recruiting a dozen working journalists with various backgrounds to speak on a career panel.

  • Education and City Hall reporter

    San Jose Mercury News

    (Public Company; 501-1000 employees; Newspapers industry)

    December 2001 — January 2005 (3 years 2 months)

    Primarily covered Palo Alto’s city hall and school district, plus 18 other school districts. Wrote a weekly education column highlighting creative efforts inside classrooms and enterprise articles examining dilemmas facing schools that bridged socioeconomic chasms. Created a sub-beat on youth health issues focusing on student stress, depression and suicide. Pitched 1A stories on issues including laid-off techies alleviating the substitute-teacher shortage that plagued California schools a year earlier; high school teachers eliminating D’s from report cards to motivate students who slack off; an item buried in the Palo Alto city council’s 44-point proposed code of conduct discouraging body language that showed “disagreement or disgust” – a free-speech controversy sparking follow-up stories at the New York Times and dozens of news outlets and dooming the proposal.
