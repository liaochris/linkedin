Barbara Freedman’s Summary

* Teacher of Electronic Music & Audio Engineering at Greenwich High School in CT
* President of the Music Educator Technologists Association (META)
* Consultant to schools and districts on implementing music technology in the general music classroom
* Adult Educator of music technology and software
* Clinician/Presenter at conferences and conventions
* Timpanist and Percussionist working regularly with the Ridgefield, Bridgeport and Norwalk Symphonies and has worked with other Tri-state orchestras, opera and ballet companies
* Music Director of the Sound Beach Community Band, Greenwich, CT’s and one of the nation’s oldest community bands

  • Teacher of Electronic Music & Audio Engineering

    Greenwich High School

    (Primary/Secondary Education industry)

    September 2001 — Present (8 years 4 months)

    Basically, I teach kids about music through composition and they use MIDI keyboards interfaced with computers. We us GarageBand and Logic Studio in a classroom with 25 iMac G5s. The name Electronic Music is kept to honor the original class that began in 1968 and offered continually since. We offer four levels of Electronic Music for students with different music backgrounds. My Intro class is the most requested single class a building with 2800 students with about 350 requests every year.
