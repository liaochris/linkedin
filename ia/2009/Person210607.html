Sau Sheong Chang’s Summary

I am currently managing the engineering team for Yahoo! South-East Asia. My team develops and maintains Yahoo! Internet properties for 6 countries including Singapore, Malaysia, Thailand, Vietnam, Indonesia and the Philippines. I also run developer outreach programs for the South-East Asia region under the Yahoo Developer Network.

Before that, I managed a software development team for a global loyalty and payment software company (http://www.welcome-rt.com). We developed retail payment-related loyalty software for large consumer banks.

Sometime back I started up a software company (http://www.elipva.com) with Thomas, and plugged at it for 4 years. We developed all sorts of web-based e-business applications for enterprises like banks, insurance companies and tertiary educational institutions. I was both the VP for Product as well as Chief Architect.

I used to write technical articles for Javaworld, Dr. Dobb’s Journal, Java Report, Computer Times but nowadays I stick mostly to my own blog (http://blog.saush.com). I've just published a book 'Ruby on Rails Web Mashup Projects' with Packt Publishing (http://www.packtpub.com/ruby-on-rails-web-mashup-projects/book) and I'm working on a second book.

I run some open source projects like the Ruby-PayPal library (http://ruby-paypal.rubyforge.org), Jaccal (Java Card Communications Access Layer) (http://jaccal.sourceforge.net), Screensvr (http://screensvr.rubyforge.org) and Chinese-English Dictionary (CED) (http://ced.dev.java.net).

I have a Bachelors in Computer Engineering, a Masters in Commercial Law and a Graduate Certificate in International Arbitration. I'm a Fellow in the Singapore Institute of Arbitrators and a fully qualified arbitrator in Singapore. More importantly, I have a passion for creating useful software and is interested in a variety of subjects including mathematics (esp statistical analysis), economics, artificial intelligence, electronic payment, linguistics and mobile networks.
