May Zayan’s Summary

Simply put, I'm an architect-turned-actress that has delved head-on into the rich and complex world of expression through performance.

Having had formal training in design, and a (thankfully) nurturing musical upbringing, it was only a matter of time before my passion for performance would take over. I've studied theatre at the Tische School of the Arts at NYU in New York City, taken many specialized classes throughout South Florida and have been a principal player in an accomplished improv troupe at the University of Florida. My experience extends from independent films to television hosting and commercials to internet webisodes.

My repertoire encompasses an array of dramatic and comedic roles and my yearning for innovative character development is always keeping me on the lookout for opportunities to explore myself.

Born in Cairo, my Egyptian background has definitely helped sculpt my identity as a performer. I truly believe there are many stories still left to tell, and many misconceptions to clarify about the Middle East--one of my sincere wishes is to be part of telling those stories, illuminating the truth and perhaps helping to change some minds.

I look forward to any project that will allow me to truly express myself as an artist -- and to share a piece of myself with the world in a way that can make an impact for the better.
