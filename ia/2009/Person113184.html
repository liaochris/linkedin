Jim Malcolm’s Summary

Jim Malcolm is a bottom line business development and marketing executive committed to improving shareholder value using a unique taxonomy. His 20+ years experience span entrepreneurial, B2B, emerging and consumer sectors, with more than 12 years devoted to consumer brand experience. His accomplishments cross all vital success measures including top line growth, cost containment, internal controls, efficiency improvements and sustained NIBT contribution.

He has been recognized for his straight forward, process driven methodology which has earned significant praise through numerous corporate and industry awards as well as many other complex and business critical projects he has led. He is well rooted in an industry agnostic business and market philosophy that is inclusive of research, strategy, product development, branding, advertising, customer retention, talent development and P&L management. Jim is uniquely qualified to consistently deliver on shareholder value.

Professional skills include:

- Extensive consumer brand experience

- Annualized marketing plans

- Development and execution of creative platforms

- Retail and point of purchase activation

- Customer Relationship Management (CRM)

- Customer and market segmentation

- ROI metrics and measurement

- Product management and technology launches

- Marketing resource management architect

- International business experience

- Talent retention and development

Jim Malcolm’s Experience

  • Director, Corporate Marketing

    Sony Electronics, Inc.

    (Public Company; SNE; Consumer Electronics industry)

    2004 — 2009 (5 years )

    While at Sony Jim had full responsibility and accountability for directing the development and implementation of marketing and business strategies across a broad spectrum of product categories. He overcome a complex organizational structure by combining Product Management needs, Sales Channel requirements and Voice of Customer criteria in a single unifying Charter for each assigned business resulting in improved business continuity. Through his efforts the team achieved outstanding business and execution results leading to awards for product categories in the portfolio. Further Jim was personally recognized for his innovative communications strategies including the 2.2 million hands-on trials for the Reader Revolution campaign; global standardization giving audio products one consistent voice; several new product launches and improvements in retail merchandising. Throughout his tenure Jim sought the opportunity to work on a wide variety of diverse sub-brands including Mavica, Cyber-shot, Walkman, VAIO, Xplod, nav-u, Reader and others. In addition to his core responsibilities Jim led a long-term Marketing Resource Management initiative to restructure the 130 person U.S. Corporate Marketing team including process mapping, identification of capability gaps, system requirements, business case development, vendor selection and execution. His vision and foresight was accepted and adopted by Sony’s CMO and Sr. VP of Marketing and remains the underlying infrastructure for execution of marketing communications at Sony Electronics.

  • Sr. Product Marketing Manager

    Sony Electronics

    (Public Company; SNE; Consumer Electronics industry)

    2000 — 2004 (4 years )

    When appointed Sr. Product Marketing Manager to lead consumer digital still camera categories Jim was chartered with the development of business and marketing strategies for all Sony digital cameras. His marketing mix supported a diverse portfolio of products that spanned FD Mavica, CD Mavica, Discam and Cyber-shot products. Given the explosive industry and steep technology curve at the time his strategies not only addressed short product lifecycles but also planned obsolescence. Jim overcome many challenges through tight alignment with Japanese based engineers, product planners, business planners and the U.S. Sales team combined with big box retailers such as Best Buy, Circuit City, Sears, Wal-Mart, Good Guys and many others who pioneered the digital camera revolution. Through his efforts Jim was awarded the “Top Performing Campaign”, “Individual Performance Award”, special recognition for Cyber-shot T, Cyber-shot U and CD Mavica launches and shared awards for “Best Performing Marketing Group” and “Best Year over Year Performance”.

  • Product Marketing Manager

    Sony Electronics

    (Public Company; SNE; Consumer Electronics industry)

    1997 — 2000 (3 years )

    Jim was recruited from Scitex specifically to launch Sony Electronics into the commercial photography marketplace because of his experience in digital photography and core photographic knowledge. Using $35,000 state of the art digital camera platform he successfully built the companies first commercial digital photography business through technology, industry innovation and partner relations. His efforts resulted in camera and printer adoption at Sears Portrait Studios, Christies Auction House and hundreds of commercial photographers across the U.S. and Canada.

  • Marketing Manager

    Scitex / Leaf Systems

    (Printing industry)

    1995 — 1997 (2 years )

    Jim joined Scitex / Leaf Systems to apply research learning from Rochester Institute of Technology specifically to develop practical business solutions and digital imaging technologies. He successfully cultivated medical and scientific applications using scanning array and mosaic sensor technologies ultimately providing a significant ongoing revenue stream, new sales channels and business infrastructure supporting OEM partners such as Nikon Instruments, Olympus, ElectroImage, Sinar Bron, Hasselblad and other optics manufactures.

  • Owner - Photographer

    Keepsake Photography

    (Photography industry)

    1988 — 1994 (6 years )

    As an Entrepreneur, dedicated to the growth of his business, Jim nurtured his vision from start-up to sustainable company through effective business planning, creative sales techniques and extensive photographic experience. He successfully authored initial business plans, secured funding, managed capital and realized a positive cash flow within the first two years of operation. In recognition of his hard work Jim was hired by an independent studio to consult and open non-competing studios in multiple locations.
