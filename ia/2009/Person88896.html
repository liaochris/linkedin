Graham Morgan’s Summary

Graham is an expert in the field of geospatial information management, spatial data warehousing, and geospatial system architectures. His specialist GIS knowledge has been demonstrated through a number of diverse consulting contracts for government and utility customers worldwide.

Graham Morgan’s Specialties:

Geospatial systems integration, data management, GIS, spatial data warehousing, enterprise architecture, business consulting, project management, business analysis, systems analysis, government and utility operations, oil field exploration

Graham Morgan’s Experience

  • Founder / Solution Architect

    Spatial Consultants Ltd,

    (Privately Held; Information Technology and Services industry)

    June 2007 — Present (2 years 7 months)

    Graham is a founder of Spatial Consultants Ltd; a consulting practice that provides in-depth expertise to support organisations in the deployment and optimization of geospatial capabilities within their business and IT frameworks. Services include:
    - Geospatial needs assessment
    - Strategy development
    - Project definition
    - Business & Systems Analysis
    - Project Management
    - Request for Proposal development & vendor evaluation
    - Data Modelling
    - Application Development
    - Project review & evaluation

    We have designed and developed geospatial solutions throughout the world for Electric Utility, Water and Waste Water Utility, Oil and Gas, Transportation, Local Government and National Government organizations.

  • Managing Partner/Solution Architect

    Forte Consulting Ltd., Canada

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Information Technology and Services industry)

    August 2004 — June 2007 (2 years 11 months)

    Graham joined Forte as managing partner of the Victoria office and provided consulting services to the provincial government of British Columbia. This included business and systems analysis for the Integrated land and Resource Registry (ILRR), solution architecture of the North West Territories Spatial Data Warehouse. Graham also provided business consulting and implementation of spatial data publishing/replication to the Land Resource Data Warehouse and the development of Logical Data Model and data quality verification environment for the Integrated Cadastral Fabric.

  • Solution Architect

    Atos Origin, United States

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; SAX; Information Technology and Services industry)

    December 2003 — July 2004 (8 months)

    Developed the Solution Architecture for Citywide GIS data management. ArcGIS and Intergraph GeoMedia environments were integrated via an Oracle Spatial database.

  • Solution Architect

    SchlumbergerSema, United States

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; slb; Information Technology and Services industry)

    July 2001 — December 2003 (2 years 6 months)

    Led a team to develop a series of ArcGIS Geodatabase data maintenance environments. These environments were integrated with master business systems to provide collaborative GIS support. All systems fed a central Spatial Data Warehouse via a replication scheme.

    Developed the Solution Architecture support for a state-wide enterpise Spatial Data Warehouse and GIS Portal.

    Participated on a team to develop a concept of Integrated Criminal Justice Management based upon the FileNet P8 platform, a J2EE based aplication platform suite.

    Led the GIS Special Interest Group within the Eureka knowledge management community.

  • Project Engineer

    Convergent Group, United States

    (Privately Held; 201-500 employees; Information Technology and Services industry)

    January 1999 — July 2001 (2 years 7 months)

    Developed a multi-platform GIS Hub to integrate geospatial data in a variety of formats from multiple city bureaus. Employed ArcSDE, SQL Server and FME. Database replication was customized to synchronize spatial data in local warehouses.

  • GIS Developer

    Perot Systems Europe

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; Information Technology and Services industry)

    April 1997 — December 1998 (1 year 9 months)

    Smallworld GIS development for East Midlands Electricity

  • GIS Systems Analyst

    Spatial Knowledge Engineering, Canada

    (Privately Held; Information Technology and Services industry)

    September 1995 — December 1996 (1 year 4 months)

    Designed the software architecture for GIS maintenance environment for the Ontario Ministry of Natural Resources.

  • GIS Teacher

    Stockholm Royal Institute of Technology, Sweden

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    February 1995 — July 1995 (6 months)

    Developed and taught a GIS course to Civil Engineering Masters students.

  • GIS Consultant

    Callahan Fox & Associates, Australia

    (Privately Held; Information Technology and Services industry)

    June 1993 — November 1994 (1 year 6 months)

    Consulted to Australian Federal government organizations in the areas of natural, environmental and cultural geospatial information management.

  • Senior Logging Geologist

    Sperry Sun Drilling Services

    (Public Company; 5001-10,000 employees; Information Services industry)

    September 1989 — June 1992 (2 years 10 months)

    In two separate stints with the company, Graham worked at rig sites offshore in the North Sea as well as in the deserts of North Yemen. He was responsible for monitoring the drilling operation with emphasis upon pressure control and rig safety; logging geological cores; and producing complete computerised geological logs of sections drilled; to evaluate possible hydrocarbon bearing formations.
