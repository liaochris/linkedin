Kevin Paylow’s Summary

I'm a collaboration nut, spending WAY too much time reading, thinking and talking about innovation, knowledge management, change management and a wide range of absurd ideas. It's a good thing I enjoy myself!

I spent the last eight years working in marketing, operations and knowledge management roles in the upstream oil industry. Many of these roles presented GREAT opportunties to learn and stretch... and work on neat projects and efforts like championing the KM effort with multiple stakeholders (up, down, across and outside the organization), aligning the KM effort with organizational strategy and finding new ways to ensure KM remains relevant and provides value to numerous stakeholders.

Innovation is a particular passion. So it's no surprise my most fulfilling accomplishments at work were those that leveraged KM tools and communities of practice to drive and improve innovation. I oversaw the creation and iterative improvement of an enterprise-wide online system to collect ideas and opportunities from employees. I continue to work with similar-minded colleagues to improve and test the way we make decisions on submitted ideas and identify stage gates in the RD&C process where KM tools (and more importantly the ‘wisdom of the crowd’) can be applied to identify stakeholder needs and improve product/service design and commercialization. Beyond the ‘bits and bytes’, I continue to explore methods like ‘innovation games’ to improve collection, assessment and testing of insights, prototypes and new business models.

At times (okay, most of the time) I drive my colleagues and managers a bit crazy with my unorthodox perspectives, constant questions and passion for open, candid dialogue. I believe these traits are what make me a valued member of the team.
