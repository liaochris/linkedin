Gregorio Drayer’s Summary

Gregorio is an electrical engineer and magister in systems engineering graduated from Simon Bolivar University, in Caracas, Venezuela. He has been the delegate of the Space Generation at the United Nations Commitee for the Peaceful Uses of Outer Space and the chief engineer of the Mars Desert Research Station in Utah, with the crews 28, 33 and 34. He has been awarded two SURF scholarships from Caltech, the 2004 annual award from the School of Engineers of Venezuela, the 35th anniversary medal from the Metropolitan-Area Center of Engineers, and the "Sowing the Future Award" of the World Future Society Venezuela, in its first edition (2002). He has been invited lecturer at the Space Biosciences Division at NASA Ames Research Center and is Assistant Professor (with tenure as of april 2009) for the Department of Processes and Systems at Simon Bolivar University.

In his research he is developing tools and techniques for agent-based integration and control of dynamic systems. His main interest into the future is the development of biospheric systems that involve recirculating aquaculture, for sustainable development and spaceflight.

He is member of the IEEE Control Systems and Computational Intelligence societies, and has served as referee for the journal IEEE Transactions on Fuzzy Systems.

Gregorio Drayer’s Specialties:

Agent-based diagnostic and automation of systems in engineering.
