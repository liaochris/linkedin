Alexandra Lajoux’s Summary

Senior-level writer, editor, and researcher with broad knowledge of business, including accounting, finance, governance, and management. Involved in communication, education, and relationship-building. On-the-job experience as educator and corporate secretary. Author/coauthor of Art of M&A series for McGraw-Hill, and currently coauthoring a book on securities valuation. Broad interests in humanities and sciences. Multilingual. Involved in performing arts. Active support for veterans' causes. Goals: to continue learning and serving in an environment that values education and welcomes diversity.

Alexandra Lajoux’s Specialties:

Business: governance, finance, HR, M&A.
Arts/Education: music/drama/literature; special education.
Languages: English, French, German, Spanish; some Italian, ancient Latin.

Alexandra Lajoux’s Experience

  • Chief Knowledge Officer

    National Association of Corporate Directors

    (Non-Profit; Management Consulting industry)

    2005 — Present (4 years)

    CKO can mean many things. For me, it means working collaboratively with other skilled professionals (such as my colleagues in research, education, and information technology) to ensure that NACD thrives as a learning community with the sharing of useful knowledge to the benefit of our members. Key activities for our department: FAQs and ExpresSource (on-demand research). As CKO, I expect myself to be current on all subjects that matter to the members of our association (including trends in accounting, finance, and law). NACD encourages me to serve on advisory boards, and I serve on three: M&A Partners (Dallas, Texas), E-know (Arlington, Virginia), and the Corporate Governance Center at Michael J. Coles College of Business, Kennesaw State University (Kennesaw, Georgia). Past advisory councils or boards include Pritchett (Dallas, Texas), Arlington Little League (Arlington, Virginia), and Cherry Tree Music (Dale City, Virginia).

Alexandra Lajoux’s Honors:

Princeton University awarded me a full fellowship to study Comparative Literature from 1972 until graduation. In 1975-1976, I received a scholarship from the French Government to study for one year at the Centre d'Etudes Superieures de la Civilisation Medievale in Poitiers. Performed at Mason Festival of the Arts and the National Theatre, among other venues. Listed in Marquis' Who's Who (WW)--WW in Finance, WW in American Women, WW in America, and WW in the World.
