  • Managing Director

    Tiny Stars

    (Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    March 2009 — Present (6 months)

    We stop child predators. The Protect Act 2003 made it a crime for an American citizen or resident to sexually exploit children overseas. If a US citizen is caught doing so, such as on a “sex holiday”, they can face up to life in prison when they return to the US. The problem is that because of jurisdictional conflicts, the US law enforcement agencies have difficulty in going overseas to catch the predators and the local law enforcement officials aren’t necessarily that interested. Tiny Stars contracts with former FBI, CIA, Delta Force, even KGB field operatives whom we send into other countries to gather evidence – taking photos, making recordings, etc. We then coordinate with the FBI, State Department and ICE (Immigration and Customs Enforcement Agency) so that when these monsters return to the US they can be arrested and prosecuted. So far, Tiny Stars has been instrumental in putting 12 of these predators behind bars. The model works well and now I am restructuring the organization in order to build it out to its full potential. Given that one sexual predator is estimated, on average, to impact 430 children, we can make a very broad and positive impact on a great number of kids around the world.
