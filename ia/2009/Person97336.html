Itzik Ben-Bassat’s Summary

Internet and Games industry Executive with a wide range of strategic, operational and growth management experience. During his career, Ben-Bassat has planned and launched several innovative online strategies and projects including some of the world’s first Games on Demand services (Games Streaming) with global partners such as Yahoo, T-Online and Comcast among others and the plans for the launch of World of Warcraft by Blizzard Entertainment.

At Blizzard Entertainment Ben-Bassat was a key member of the management team while the company transitioned from a game development studio to a global leader in online entertainment. He was responsible for a wide range of planning and business activities and spearheaded the company’s global presence; he initiated, established and managed Blizzard’s operations in Europe and Asia, supervised more than 1500 employees, was accountable for more than $500MM in annual revenue and managed Blizzard's global business development activities and its relationship with the Vivendi Universal Board.

Late in 2007, Ben-Bassat Co-Founded GameGround (www.gameground.com), a stealth-mode startup building the world's first web-based gaming console (backed by Sequoia Capital) and NetAlign Software (bootstrap) which has developed an ad targeting service based on proprietary and advanced semantic and social analysis technologies. Ben-Bassat is also an active board member at Wix.com (backed by Bessemer Venture Partners).
