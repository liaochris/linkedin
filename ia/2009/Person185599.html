Peter Bashaw’s Experience

  • Director, Manufacturing Operations - PCSB Electrical Components Operation

    Eaton Corporation

    (Public Company; ETN; Electrical/Electronic Manufacturing industry)

    2003 — Present (6 years)

    Planned and executed strategic and tactical operational leadership within a $500M+ business with more than 8 global manufacturing sites and regional centers.

    • Program Management – Planned, launched, and led a global, multi-site integration program moving over $300M in sales with a budget of $18M generating $14M in annual, sustained savings.
    • Transition Management / Organizational Development – Effectively and efficiently trained and certified >1300 employees in numerous manufacturing fabrication and assembly operations in <18 months.
    • Strategic Negotiation & Alliances – Identified, negotiated, and commissioned 4 cost effective leased sites in 2 countries for assembly and logistics operations. Each site was commissioned in 90 days or less avoiding significant capital investments and operational delays. Grew profit more than 100% by outsourcing assembly while protecting intellectual capital.
    • Operational Re-engineering – Bolstered production capacity output year over year for key product lines by 45% to 65% with focused investments and application of lean methodologies. Consolidated common, capital intensive processes for optimized service and economies.
    • Capacity Rationalizing – Decommissioned a 160,000 sq ft fabrication and assembly manufacturing site. Eliminated 100,000 sq ft of manufacturing space as part of a global footprint optimization strategy.
    • Capability Expansion – Augmented subassembly production output ~50%, to fulfill internal and external customer demand growth, thru unprecedented outsourcing of >30% of fabrication operations and executing standard work. The results significantly boosted production flexibility and avoided excess capital investments.

  • Manager, Vehicle Controls Business Unit - PCSO Electical Components Division

    Eaton Corporation

    (Public Company; ETN; Electrical/Electronic Manufacturing industry)

    2000 — 2003 (3 years)

    Provided full profit and loss responsibility for an autonomous business of >$60M in sales. Included sales, marketing, design engineering, finance, materials, supply chain, logistics, quality, human resources, and manufacturing. Manufacturing was substantially vertically integrated with multiple assembly sites including Mexico. Produced for and sold to domestic and international markets serving heavy and medium duty trucks, agriculture, construction, and specialty vehicle industries as a full service Tier 1 and Tier 2 supplier. Also served a compliment of commercial OEM’s and licensed Distributors.

    • Financial & Operational Turnaround – Improved cash flow by 47% through especially developing and leading an inventory reduction program, jumping velocity up 2X and taking out 34 days of on-hand. Reduced the supplier base from ~250 to ~45, improving business relationships and reducing operating complexity.
    • Acquisition Due Diligence / Product Rationalization – Increased net return on investment thru due diligence acquiring 2 high value market opportunity product lines and selling 2 non-strategic product lines.
    • Customer Negotiation – Negotiated extended agreements with the businesses top 5 customers minimizing concessions and competitive advances. Upheld margins thru a market downturn and intense price competition with effective communications, making lean the way work was done, and applying six sigma disciplines.

Shrunk customer returns and internal failures 400% by promoting total quality management and automating repetitive operations. Earned a State Department of Labor Safety Award.
