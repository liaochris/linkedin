John Schulte’s Summary

• Creative development of original and licensed intellectual properties; from ideation/conceptualization to design and production.

• 25 years of experience in writing and producing entertainment (first film produced, "Four Stars," by Garry Marshall in 1986, writer/developer at Dic Entertainment for sundry animated properties, including "New Kids on the Block").

• Hands-on designing, directing product development for consumer products, from video software to toys, interactive platforms and games.

• Unique experience as liaison between animation, licensing and product development. Part of the original creative development team for the Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles. Designed, product managed major toy lines from soup to nuts, including Speed Racer, Zorro, Biker Mice from Mars, Gormiti, The Mask, Mirmo, Toonsylvania, The Mummy, Zorro: Generation Z, Freedom Ops Net and a plethora of others.

• Progressive, successful experience with new technologies: turning blue sky concepts into viable, marketable product; developed innovative, interactive product, including the Amazing dolls from Playmates, Tamagotchi and Nano line extensions, Brian the Brain, Yano, Wittley and others.

• Pioneered internet community-building with AOL and online content development, management company and publisher for AOL (Toy Network) and Apple's eWorld (ToyNet); launched debut websites for many major corporations (Bandai America, Speed Racer, DSI, Blockmen, Whac-A-Mole).

• Always ready to launch original properties for both mainstream and new platform media. Prepared to assume product/project management for new property launches, serving as an outsource solution.

• Have taken licensed and non-licensed product from concept to the shelf; have assumed risk positions as licensee (and licensor), manufacturer, and developer.

• Ongoing strategic development projects -- harnessing assets between entities and negotiating a positive relationship that traffics in new revenues.

John Schulte’s Specialties:

Creative foundation; marketing plan and positioning; brand creation; international negotiations; project management; strategic and tactical business analysis; writing, creative direction and production (scripting, storyboards, commercials, sizzle pieces, design/copy for packaging, collateral, entertainment IP bibles, et cetera).
