Keith Legaz’s Summary

I am a Senior TV Media Research Analyst from a spot cable rep firm exceeding a billion dollars in annual revenue, who has worked with National Cable Communications for five years and Telerep for three years. In addition, I possess expert knowledge of the television media, various ratings methodologies, Nielsen software, multiple research applications, excellent communication and computer skills.

I worked on a research team that provided direction for the entire company as the spot cable rep firm was evolving rapidly and learning how to capitalize on its unused inventory. I played a critical role on corporate research and industry driven projects like Time Shifted Viewing Analyses, Nielsen Ascription model on local cable and posting under-delivery analysis, which enabled NCC to capitalize on its unused inventory. I was the specialist for all LPM Analyses, and developed a company-wide internal tool for sales to easily access research collateral.

Keith Legaz’s Experience

  • Senior TV Media Research Analyst

    National Cable Communications

    (Privately Held; Marketing and Advertising industry)

    April 2004 — January 2009 (4 years 10 months)

    Analyzed and provided NCC with critical quantitative data on both a global level and on an individual company-wide transactional sales request basis, while providing substantial assistance to NCC Programming.

    • Analyzed quantitative data for corporate research and industry driven projects like the effect of DVR viewing in time shifted data streams, the impact of Nielsen’s ascription model on local cable and posting under-delivery analysis.

    • Continuously researched and identified rating trends for all new LPM markets, which involved analyzing Ad Supported Cable vs. Broadcast when a market switches from Meter-Diary to LPM.

  • TV Media Research Analyst

    Telerep

    (Public Company; Broadcast Media industry)

    March 2001 — April 2004 (3 years 2 months)

    Provided Telerep and client salespeople with the marketing material they need to sell advertising time on stations.
