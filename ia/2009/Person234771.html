Thomas Osborne’s Summary

I have over 15 years of development experience with several languages, OSs and DBMS systems. Since 1999, my career has been focused on web site development and related projects to support sites and features.

The web development chapter of my career began as an initial member of the Techrepublic.com development team (start up in Louisville, KY). Techrepublic was purchased by the Gartner Group and later by CNET in 2001. From 2001 to 2009 I served in various roles at CNET Networks from outbound email delivery for all brands to development on CNET Business sites (BNET.com, Techrepublic.com and ZDNet.com). In my later years at CNET I focused on services providing email alerts based on RSS, voting, personalization, caching and platform development for all of the CNET Business brands.

In September of 2008 I left CNET to start an office in Louisville for a San Francisco/Palo Alto based consulting firm specialized in providing Jive Clearspace Community customizations.

In 2006, I started work on a social networking aggregation site and have maintained an ongoing interest in social media and social networking.

My recent experience is centered around Java based web applications running in Tomcat 4.x-6.x. Supporting technologies include JSP/JSTL, Velocity, memcached, Spring, iBatis, BerkeleyDB and many of the jakarta commons libraries.

Thomas Osborne’s Specialties:

Java 1.4-1.6, SCJP, Jive Clearspace Community, Tomcat 4.x-6.x, JSP/JSTL, Velocity, JMS (ActiveMQ), memcached, jakarta commons libraries, MySQL, Oracle, shell scripting
