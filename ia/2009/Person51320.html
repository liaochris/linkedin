Rob Curedale 罗伯特’s Summary

Rob Curedale was born in Australia and worked as a designer, design director and design educator in London, Sydney, Vevey, Switzerland, Portugal, Los Angeles, Silicon Valley, Hong Kong, China and Detroit.

Rob has over 25 years experience leading design teams to achieve transformational improvements in operating and financial results. He has extensive experience in forging strategic growth and creating a competitive advantage, and a background in expanding and driving business into new and emerging markets.He has extensive cross cultural industrial experience and expertise.

Curedale has designed furniture, medical, technology and consumer products in global markets including Europe, Australia, Asia and North America. During this time, Curedale designed hundreds of products as a consultant and in-house design manager at corporate offices and consultancies. Clients include HP, Philips, GEC, Nokia, Sun, Apple, Canon, Motorola, Nissan, Audi VW, Disney, RTKL, Government of the United Arab Emirates, The Royal British and Australian Navy and airforce, Steelcase, Hon, Castelli, Gensler, Haworth, Honeywell, NEC, Hoover, Packard Bell, Dell, Black & Decker and Harmon Kardon.

Rob has lectured and taught widely throughout the United States, Europe, Asia and Australia including at Yale, Cranbrook Academy of Art, Art Center Europe; a faculty member at Sydney College of the Arts and the UTS Sydney; and as Chair of Product Design and Chair of Furniture Design at the College for Creative Studies in Detroit, the largest Product Design school in North America, Escola De Artes e Design Matosinhos in Oporto Portugal, Pratt Institute, Instituto De Artes Visuals, Design e Marketing, Lisbon, Southern Yangtze University, Jiao Tong University in Shanghai and Nanjing Arts Institute in China.

    The Industrial Design Department was part of The school of Environmental Design. I initially undertook studies in architecture and in my second year entered the Industrial Design stream. I completed my undergraduate degree in 1978.
