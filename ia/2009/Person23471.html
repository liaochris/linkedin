Ross Orr’s Summary

BacTech is a pioneering environmental technology firm that has developed a proprietary technology to remediate highly toxic areas resulting from abandoned mining operations. This groundbreaking technology, called “bioleaching,” employs naturally occurring bacteria to oxidize the sulphide materials left behind after years of mining in an area.

These locations include discarded crushed rocks known as tailings, which generally contain ores and related materials contaminated by arsenic and other substances that are poisonous to humans and animals, as well as harmful to the local environment. The sulphides in the tailings react with the atmosphere to create an acidic solution referred to as acid mine drainage (“AMD”), which leaches into the surrounding area over time.

BacTech’s bioleaching process can stabilize these toxins from minerals in as little as five to six days and prevent additional harmful AMD. BacTech has been designing, building and operating bioleaching plants for over 15 years.

BacTech is a publicly-traded firm based in Toronto, Canada, and has been listed on the TSX Venture exchange (ticker: BM) since 1997.

In September 2008, the company signed a Memorandum of Understanding (“MOU”) to investigate the use of bioleaching to reprocess arsenic-laden tailings in Cobalt, Ontario.

Ross Orr’s Specialties:

Tailing clean-up, arsenic remediation (as well as other harmful toxins from tailings), the recovery of precious and non-precious metals; working with mining companies, local and federal jurisdictions, and environmental firms to properly identify, address and solve the arsenic problem.
