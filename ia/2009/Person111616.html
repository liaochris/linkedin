Jessica H. Faust’s Summary

Jessica Faust is a literary agent with BookEnds Literary Agency where she represents represents a number of award-winning and bestselling authors in the areas of romance, mystery, women’s fiction, thrillers, and nonfiction including New York Times bestselling Maggie Sefton, author of A Killer Stitch, New York Times bestselling Angie Fox, author of The Accidental Demon Slayer, USA Today bestselling Sally MacKenzie, author of The Naked Earl, international bestseller Cynthia Shapiro author of Corporate Confidential and What Does Somebody Have to do to Get a Job Around Here? as well as popular authors Kate Douglas, Bella Andre, Lewis Maltby, Betty Hechtman, and Sharon Page (among others).

Prior to starting BookEnds, Jessica was an editor for five years, working at both Berkley Publishing (a division of Penguin) and Macmillan. In those years, Jessica edited a number of award-winning authors including mystery authors Rhys Bowen and Victoria Thompson.

In addition to her responsibilities to her clients, Jessica is an adjunct professor for New York University’s Publishing Program, has been recognized as Agent of the Year by the NYC Romance Writers of America chapter and is asked regularly to speak at writers’ conferences throughout the world. On average she is a guest speaker at eight conferences each year and maintains daily blog posts on www.bookendslitagency.blogspot.com where she regularly dispenses advice on publishing. She is a member of RWA, MWA, and AAR.

Jessica H. Faust’s Specialties:

Areas of expertise include historical, contemporary, fantasy, paranormal, and erotic romance, women's fiction, mysteries, urban fantasy, suspense, and thrillers. In nonfiction, Jessica specializes in business, finance, career, current affairs, parenting, psychology, women's issues, self-help, health, current affairs, and general nonfiction. While open to anything, Jessica is most actively seeking unique fiction with a strong hook, and nonfiction with creative ideas and large author platforms.
