Steve Foerster’s Summary

I have over a decade of experience in politics, communications, information technology, and management. In 2003 I switched my professional focus to applying that experience to higher education and am interested in initiatives in that area.

Steve Foerster’s Specialties:

Process management, information systems, university administration, training, education, educational technology, e-learning, distance learning

Steve Foerster’s Experience

  • WikiEducator

    (E-Learning industry)

    2007 — Present (2 years)

    WikiEducator is a project of the Commonwealth of Learning that serves as a platform for planning of education projects linked with the development of free content and development of open educational resources. It is focused, but not exclusively, on building content for use in the developing world.

    I was on the Interim International Advisory Board from 2007 to 2008, then was elected to the Community Council once that open governance model was adopted. I have traveled to make presentations about WikiEducator as a way of increasing awareness of and participating in the project, and advocate expanding the project to be multilingual, and increasing ease of use to encourage participation from non-technical educators.

  • Marymount University

    (Educational Institution; 201-500 employees; Higher Education industry)

    2006 — Present (3 years)

    Oversee the administration of the university’s course management system, the operations of the campus computer labs, and administration of technology training programs. Concentrate on the important tasks of training students, faculty, and staff in their use of technology. Special emphasis on supporting pedagogical uses of technology and the associated equipment and software, and assisting faculty and staff members in increasing their ability to use technology productively in their work.

  • Free Curricula Center

    (Non-Profit; 1-10 employees; Higher Education industry)

    2004 — 2007 (3 years)

    The Free Curricula Center sought to do for textbooks and other educational materials what the open source movement has done for software: provide free, quality alternatives to commercial products. It did this by developing textbooks, courseware, and other materials that are free for all to use, copy, and modify.

    I was the founding Executive Director of the group until April 2006, and built it up to where a colleague could take on that role. Then as Director of Instructional Technology I maintained the organization's web presence and consulted on technology strategy. Ultimately the organization's activities were folded into those of WikiEducator.

  • Southeastern University

    (Non-Profit; 51-200 employees; Higher Education industry)

    2005 — 2006 (1 year)

    Serve as Primary Designated School Official (PDSO) liaison between the university and U.S. Citizen and Immigration Services. Advise administration on academic policies and marketing to international students. Draft credit transfer tables and propose articulation agreements.

  • Keiser College

    (Educational Institution; 201-500 employees; Higher Education industry)

    2003 — 2005 (2 years)

    Liaison between university's online campus and its largest brick-and-mortar campus. Focus on developing institutional processes and tools for identifying and assisting students at risk of dropping out. Design and build instructional technology tools for use by faculty and staff for student retention and success. Reported to Vice-President of largest brick-and-mortar campus.
