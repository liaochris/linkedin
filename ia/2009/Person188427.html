Paul K. Graham’s Summary

Certified Genealogist (SM) with proven research results in all U.S. regions, primarily the Southeast. Six years professional experience. Currently pursuing a master's degree in Heritage Preservation.

CG, Certified Genealogist is a service mark of the Board for Certification of Genealogists, used under license by board certified genealogists after periodic evaluation, and the board name is registered in the US Patent & Trademark Office.

Paul K. Graham’s Experience

  • Independent Historical Researcher

    Self-employed

    (Research industry)

    March 2004 — Present (5 years)

    Provide historical research services for authors, media companies, home owners, and family historians. Compile and analyze evidence, provide clients with written reports of findings.

    Notable projects:

    1) Researched the maternal ancestry and family land ownership history of actor-comedian Chris Tucker for PBS television series African American Lives, which premiered in February 2006.

    2) Researched the early life and family secrets of Marine Corps Lieutenant General Victor H. Krulak for upcoming biography.

    3) Documented complete land title history of 750-acre tract of land in Montgomery County, Georgia, identifying and dating three nineteenth-century home sites.

    Books:

    1) 1805 Georgia Land Lottery Fortunate Drawers and Grantees. Decatur, Ga.: The Genealogy Company, 2004.

    2) 1805 Georgia Land Lottery Persons Entitled to Draws. Decatur, Ga.: The Genealogy Company, 2005.

  • Archivist

    (Non-Profit; 1-10 employees; Museums and Institutions industry)
