Peter Kuchnicki’s Summary

Peter is President of the Spark Design Awards, a new approach to encourage fresh thinking and innovation across all disciplines and levels of expertise. Spark is now conducting their 3rd competition, promoting “Design by and for Everyone” and emphatically believes “…that higher aspirations are a necessary element of design planning and process.”

Peter has created and/or produced over 70 major events, seminars, conferences and competitions in the areas of design, communications and creativity. He produced the GOING Conference on alternative transportation, the WonderBrands, Wires & Tires, Business UnUsual, Net@Work and Metropolis West conferences, LatinExpo—the First Hispanic Marketing Expo, the Adweek Creative Seminars and Creative Shootout Competitions, Creativity (Canada), the Home Office Magazine Conferences, Creative Media Awards, Malta Creative Marketing conferences, Creativa Awards (collaboration in Mexico) and over 1,200 workshops in the Adweek Seminar Series. In 2008 he launched the Spark Creative Seminar, a collaborative workshop composed of students, creative professionals and business executives.

Peter began his career in 1977 with the launch and publication of Cinegram Magazine, concerning pioneering coverage of New Media. He moved to NYC into positions at Electronic News and MISWeek and participated in the launch of USA Today. At the invitation of legendary editor Clay Felker, he moved into new business development at Adweek, MarketingWeek and MediaWeek magazines. As Special Projects Executive Director for 8 years, he immersed himself in the rapid changes taking place in the wide world of communications.

On the design front, Peter has collaborated with many renowned talents including Kit Hinrichs, Paula Scher, Richard Wilde, Walter Bernard and Milton Glaser, to help create beautiful and award-winning productions and publications. He founded Designing Worlds, a sustainable design and architectural competition and the 9.11 Tribute Archive
