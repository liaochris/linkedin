  • Founder\Chairman

    (Philanthropy industry)

    November 2007 — Present (1 year 10 months)

    Chairman of expedition planning and preparations (in progress) to commemorate Captain Robert Falcon Scott's 1910-1913 expedition and race to the South Pole. The expedition to Antarctica in early 2012 will retrace part of Scott's original journey, and joined by descendants of Scott's men, hold a memorial service at Scott's last camp on the Ross Ice Shelf. The expedition is in aid of the Scott Polar Research Institute (SPRI) and will mark the centenary of the ‘Terra Nova’ Expedition 1910-1913 and the deaths of the Polar Party in 1912.

    The expedition team of a dozen will consist of a few well known polar explorers as well as a few ordinary people chosen from different walks of life. Our current Service patron is Admiral Sir Jonathon Band, First Sea Lord and Head of the Royal Navy. The expedition has been given full support by Ranulph Fiennes and Mike Stroud, both of whom will endeavour to be available and participate in the commemorations.
