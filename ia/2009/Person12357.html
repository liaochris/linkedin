Amy Tierney’s Experience

  • Owner

    f8f11 Images

    (Photography industry)

    May 2008 — Present (1 year 4 months)

    Since 1999 Amy’s work has been published both nationally and internationally, from print to web to broadcast television, such as: Meryl Streep’s Lifetime Achievement Awards ceremony, the Independent Spirit Awards, the World Pro of Surf Championships, the opening of Frank Gehry’s Walt Disney Concert Hall, Lionel Richie for Bosendorfer Pianos, Al Gore at “An Inconvenient Truth” LA Film Fest, comedian Dave Chapelle for HBO, WIRED Magazine’s NextFest, the Roots Grammy Jam, Cindy Crawford for Bentley Motors, Ben Silverman’s NBC Emmy Party, Jane Goodall for the Jane Goodall Institute, and backstage at the NAACP Image Awards.

    Amy Tierney started her work as a celebrity event photographer in 1999 when she joined Lee Salem Photography. In 2004 contracted with WireImage, the celebrity/music/entertainment photography wire service, to additionally shoot for them.

    Most recently she crafted a program for Step Up Women's Network called
    "Photojournalism for Girls"/the "I Dream to..." Project which taught 75
    under-served teen girls the skills of photojournalism as a tool to meet
    with the very woman they dream to be. Those images were distributed in Self Magazine, GettyImages held a show for the girls at it’s Varick St. studio, and Crystal Light/Kraft Foods displayed the images on it’s mobile tour, and NBC broadcast a piece about the program. Thanks to the success of the “I Dream to…” program, Crystal Light put up additional funds to keep the program going in Los Angeles in 2008 and 2009, as well as extend the program to Chicago and New York.

    Amy shoots and processes with an entirely digital workflow, backed by a thorough knowledge of both PC and Macintosh platforms. The Canon 1D Mark II N and 5D Mark II digital SLRs are her current cameras of choice. She looks forward to working with you.
