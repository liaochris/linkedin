Tom Butler’s Summary

Transforming the wastewater industry to both reduce pollution and decrease energy demands. Designing the energy efficient and non polluting farm of the 21st century.

We can save or produce six percent or more of the electricity produced in this country by changing how we process wastewater.

I try to keep on top of the state of the art advances in anaerobic digestors, engine generators, low energy dewatering methods, algae to energy process engineering design, species selection for differential applications and production methodologies.

Currently consulting on two wastewater to energy projects in both Michigan and Indiana and on the design of a new agricultural technology center in Michigan.

Tom Butler’s Specialties:

Interdisciplinary science and engineering including plastics engineering, environmental process engineering, wastewater treatment and algae to energy production.
