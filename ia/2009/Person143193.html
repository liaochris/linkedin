Craig Peters’s Summary

I've developed and implemented award-winning content and successful online marketing programs for a wide variety of companies -- including some of the best-known entertainment brands on the planet.

My online content has received A and A+ ratings from Entertainment Weekly. My e-mail marketing programs for Feld Entertainment delivered returns that typically tripled industry averages. My online marketing plans are devoid of smoke and mirrors and built to succeed.

I've been living online since Prodigy was hailed as the Next Big Thing. Before that I did everything and then some during my 15 years in newsstand magazine publishing: writing, editing, advertising, marketing and even some on-camera television work that proved conclusively there's nothing left to upload to YouTube.

I've written more copy than I care to think about. Most of it's been published, including about three dozen books (mostly for the grade school market), hundreds of magazine articles, press kits, DVD scripts and a boatload more ... even a bunch of instruction manuals for videogames you've probably played.

As director of online marketing and communications for kajeet I took a ride on the startup rollercoaster. It was definitely an E-ticket attraction and would make a great business case study for someone someday.

In 2004 I founded CKPcreative, an agency specializing in custom editorial, Internet marketing and digital project management. I serve a broad range of clients in a diverse range of industries. Learn more at http://www.ckpcreative.com

I blog daily at http://www.lohad.com and for the last several years have blogged ad:tech New York for Adrants. I've spoken at conferences like Kid Power and Virtual Worlds and have served as a judge for the ad:tech Interactive Awards.

Jerry Seinfeld pretty much nailed it when he said: "Once you start doing only what you've already proven you can do, you're on the road to death."

(last updated: July 2008)
