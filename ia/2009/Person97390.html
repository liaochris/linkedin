Ibrahim Habib’s Summary

Ibrahim Habib received the PH.D. degree in 1991 from the City University of New York, the M.Sc. degree from Polytechnic University of New York, and the B.Sc. degree from Ain Shams University, Cairo, Egypt all in Electrical Engineering. From 1981 to 1983 and from 1984 to 1988, he was a computer networks engineer involved in the planning, system engineering and installation of several IBM SNA networking projects in both Egypt and Saudi Arabia. In 1991 he joined the Faculty of the City University of New York where is now a tenured Professor and Director of the Advanced Networking Research Laboratory. From 1998 to 2001 he also worked in the industry with AT&T Labs and Telcordia Technologies Research Labs. His industrial experience spans different areas of data networking including architecture design of IP optical networks, metropolitan optical networks, IP quality of service architecture and traffic engineering. He has published more than 120 technical papers and reports in those areas. He was a Guest Editor of the IEEE Journal on Selected Areas in Communications (JSAC) in 1997, 2000, and 2004 and is currently a Guest Editor for a special issue in 2009. He was a Guest Editor of the IEEE Communications Magazine in 1995, 1997, 2006 and 2007. He also served as an Editor of the same Magazine from 1994 till 1997. He is Guest Editor of a special issue of the IEEE Wireless Communications Magazine to appear in 2008. He was Guest Editor of the John Wiley Journal on Wireless Communications and Mobile Computing in 2002; and is currently an Editor of the same Journal. He served as the co-chairman and chairman of many symposia and workshops in various IEEE conferences.

Ibrahim Habib’s Specialties:

IP Networks Architecture, Next Generation Internet, Wireless Networks, Ethernet and Optical Networks, Operation Support Systems, Network Management.
