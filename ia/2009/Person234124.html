Tim Scanlon’s Summary

Co-founder and CEO of W4C Ltd, Tim has 24 years of business leadership in strategic marketing, business development, user experience (UX) design and evaluation, international business, vision & strategy development, and operations. His teams have helped introduce new software and technology solutions for industry leaders Rockwell Automation, General Electric, Westinghouse, and SDRC (Siemens PLM) focusing on innovations in customer experience and usability.

Tim was responsible for designing, staffing, and leading four teams at these businesses, and has helped many other CXO’s improve their customer experience while during his tenure as President Alucid and Vice President of ThoughtForm.

A Carnegie Mellon University Adjunct Faculty, Tim teaches the course "Practicing Design" to School of Design students preparing for professional careers in interaction design, communication design, and industrial design.

A 1984 graduate of Carnegie Mellon University’s School of Industrial Design, he studied Strategic Marketing Management at Harvard Business School and Strategic Marketing of Technology Solutions through California Institute of Technology among many other professional level courses. He is a 2004 graduate of the Rockwell Leadership Institute and 2007 Graduate of Leadership Pittsburgh (LPI Class XXIII).

Tim serves on the Advisory Board of MoGoes, and as board Chairperson of the MIT Enterprise Forum Pittsburgh. Tim participates in several leadership, design, entrepreneurial, marketing, and technology strategy professional organizations.
