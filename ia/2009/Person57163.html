David Crowe’s Summary

David has been involved in innovative software development since the late 1970s, real-time software development in the 1980s and early 1990s and, since the late 1980s, the development of telecommunications standards. He is also an expert on telecommunications numbering resources such as MIN, IRM, IMSI, ESN, MEID, UIMID and SID. He has a side interest in software and database development, both in computer protocols and speciality systems to manage data and enhance productivity. He was written extensively on wireless issues, with a unique ability to put complex technical issues in language anyone can read and understand.

David Crowe’s Specialties:

Wireless network standards, architectures and protocols. Government mandates including number portability, E911, priority calling and emergency alert services. Number resource management. Database design and implementation. Software development in C, FileMaker, Javascript, HTML, CSS and AppleScript.

  • Senior Consultant

    Qualcomm

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; Telecommunications industry)

    August 2007 — Present (1 year 6 months)

    Assisting Qualcomm and the CDG in the transition of cdma2000 systems to new hardware identifiers known as MEID (Mobile Equipment ID) and Expanded UIMID (User Identification Module ID). So far this work has involved meetings with carriers from China, Japan, India, Indonesia and Thailand.
