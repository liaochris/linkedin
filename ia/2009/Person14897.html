Andrew Schwartz’s Summary

Andrew Schwartz, President, MPI Coin, Inc.
For more than 10 years, Andrew Schwartz’s drive for continual career growth and his ability to identify and act upon business expansion opportunities have played an integral role in the success of MPI Coin.
Andrew Schwartz has worked for various companies, including Milwaukee Journal Sentinel, Yellow Pages of Skiing and Yellow Net. This background provided a solid framework for Andrew’s entrance into MPI Coin in September of 1995. In 1996, MPI Coin expanded to include the coin division, founding MPI Coin, Inc.
Since his joining, MPI Coin has grown from 250 accounts in three states to servicing 11,000 wholesale customers and 34,000 consumers in all 50 states. One of Andrew’s main responsibilities for MPI Coin was to create an inside sales department starting from the ground up. Consequently, he became the leading sales associate for MPI Coin and developed a sales system and team to penetrate the financial services industry on a national level. With the growth of the sales team, Andrew expanded his position, becoming responsible for ensuring a high level of customer service. Thus, he built a customer service team to support the growing sales department.
Andrew’s success in these specific roles provided him the opportunity to become Vice President in 2000, and through mentoring and succession plans, he became the President and major shareholder of MPI Coin in 2002.
In 2008, along with Thomas Multerer, Andrew was instrumental in launching a nationwide $1 Coin distribution and circulation program contracted with the United States Mint and rolled out to 11,000 financial institutions in all 50 states.
Andrew resides in Grafton, WI and is a current member of the Young Presidents’ Organization.
