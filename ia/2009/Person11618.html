Amit K. Sinha’s Summary

I have extensive experience in modeling and analysis of complex systems and have developed novel methods for network analysis.

I have developed a package of network analysis tools, called Integrated Network Analysis of Complex Systems (INACS). INACS brings, under one umbrella, a number of network analysis product lines geared towards various industrial verticals.

INACS has already been applied for analysis of patent citation networks, physiological signal processing for identification of pathological states, genomic-proteomic, and pathway analysis for identification of complex disease types and their signatures.

I am currently tailoring my network analysis methods for developing dynamic road traffic routing protocols. This product will enable individual subscribers/ cars with routing options to their destination as per specified priorities of speed, costs, etc.

I plan to expand our operations, one by one, into a number of different domains including a gprs enabled detection and prediction of cardio-vascular disorders, health care systems, and in development of personalized medical protocols.

The underlying idea of the technical side of my work is that systems are generated by complex interactions between system players, and a comprehensive analysis of these interaction networks can identify sub-networks that are responsible for distinctive functional and dysfunctional system behavior. With this general concept, several real world problems can be addressed.

I hope to contribute to the fundamental understanding of how systems work, and can be controlled in order to achieve desired system behavior.

I plan to always subject our fundamental research to the litmus test of practicality and potential for successful real life applications.
