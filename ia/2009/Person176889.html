Norma Wincker’s Summary

After a biology degree, I obtained a PhD in Biochemistry and Cellular Biology dealing with the mechanisms of intracellular protein degradation in the parasite Trypanosoma cruzi (Tc).
I was engaged into my Postdoctoral training immediately after. First I moved to Brazil and then to France to perform studies in the Molecular Biology and Enzymology of Tc.

These experiences lead me to acquire a wide experience in a) Molecular cloning, b) Protein expression in bacteria and eukaryotic cells, c) Biochemistry of Proteins and d) Enzyme kinetics.

Later as a research team leader I joined the gene therapy field applied to neuromuscular diseases with emphasis in improving gene delivery to skeletal muscle cells both in vitro and in validated models of the diseases to demonstrate the proof of principle for several vectors and gene delivery methods.

In the meantime, I had the great opportunity to gain experience as Project Leader in basic research Project Management with gene therapy medicinal products moving from bench to bed and the application of Good Laboratory Practices (GLP) guidelines to Process Development and Assay Development of plasmids and viral vectors.

This led me to look for new challenges in Clinical Research Development acting as Global Trial Study or Project Leader.

During the last 5 years I have gained a significant experience in International phases 1 to 3b studies in HIV-therapeutic vaccines, infectiology and oncology. As part of a cross-functional management, reporting and process analysis, I have the responsibility to ensure that trials are undertaken according to ICH/GCP guidelines, local regulatory and company requirements and within the time and cost framework agreed upon.

Norma Wincker’s Specialties:

1) Participation to generation of the Plan of development Standard for gene therapy medicinal products. 2) Planning phases of the Project and Cross-functional project team management including CROs and other subcontractors. 3) Knowledge of Regulatory requirements and FDA/EMEA regulations. Regulatory tasks including submissions to both BoH/MoH and IRB/IECs. 5) High level of initiative, ability to multitask and to communicate with a broad spectrum of people.
