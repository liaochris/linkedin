  • Marketing Director

    (Broadcast Media industry)

    1997 — 2000 (3 years )

    Worked with a team of amazing talent (programming, marketing, and sales) to build on a great heritage as the best radio station in Boston as we approached and celebrated KISS 108’s 20th anniversary. We planned and executed many major, award-winning events together, including three annual KISS Concerts (1998-2000), Acoustic KISSmas 1997, Jingle Ball 1998/99, Matty World Tours, Merry Matty 1997-1999. Additional promotions, advertising and sales marketing and “street” programs resulted in an increase of Arbitron ratings from #4 to a consistent #2 in our top demo (M-Su, 6A-12M, Persons 25-54 and 12+) and our morning show became a consistent #1 (from number 2).

  • Marketing/Creative Services

    SportsChannel

    (Broadcast Media industry)

    1993 — 1997 (4 years )

    At SportsChannel New England, I worked with a creative business and programming team. Our business creativity resulted in repositioning SportsChannel New England from a premium channel to the basic line-up resulting in 2.9 million households receiving SportsChannel on their basic cable by June, 1997. SportsChannel had 1.5 million households in 1996. Our marketing and programming creativity allowed us to build and promote SportsChannel programs to support our teams and fulfill our cable affiliates’ goals. Among these successful opportunities, we launched two feature on-air campaigns: Street Beat, with host “Coach” Willie Maye, asking the person-on-the-street their point of view and the TEAM Project. TEAM Project allowed us to build our relationship with the Kraft family, owners of the New England Patriots and the Revolution, that resulted in part to SportsChannel airing 12 MLS New England Revolution games and two pre-season NFL Patriots replays in Summer 1997.
