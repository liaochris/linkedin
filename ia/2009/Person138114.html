  • Student

    New Media Institute at UGA

    (Media Production industry)

    August 2008 — May 2009 (10 months)

    As a student in the New Media Department at the University of Georgia, I was immersed into the digital media world.

    In order to complete the certificate program, I took Intro to New Media, New Media Topics 1 and 2, New Media Production, Rich Media, Graphic Design (also for PR) and the New Media Capstone course.

    My coolest experiences were developing an actual Web application, iEvent, in the Capstone class (NMIX 4510) and a concept Web app in the Intro class (NMIX 2020).

    I served as the leader of 18 students in developing the Web application concept called "The GreenLife." This application was conceptualized to be a "green guide" when purchasing products. Here, using QR codes, anyone could scan a product and compare it to other similar products to find out which one was the most environmentally friendly by comparison of locality, packaging waste, environmental responsibility, etc.

    The second Web application can be found at www.mynmi.net/twilight. This was developed to help an event-host communicate with the event-goer by providing maps, updates, tweets, schedules, pictures and video. As a promotype, we catered the application toward the annual bike race in Athens, Ga., the Twilight Criterium. iEvent could be formulated toward any event experience by choosing the various appropriate platforms. Although accessible on any computer or smart phone, this application works best on the iPhone - it even has an icon to add to your homepage! During the development of this digital interface, I served as the main developer of the application itself. The next step toward development would be turning this into an iPhone application. For more information on iEvent, please visit http://www.mynmi.net/twilight/website.html.

    My favorite projects in Rich Media (NMIX 4310) are "4. Flash project" and "6. Final Project," a video on Athens' Grassroots Fashion. Check it out beginning to end! Don't forget to click on the links either. http://mynmi.net/student/lrausch/NMIX4310/
