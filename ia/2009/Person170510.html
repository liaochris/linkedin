My Tam H. Nguyen’s Summary

My work and community service for the ten years of my working life reflects changes I hope for the world and our human family: more support and resources for the youth, the elderly, the arts, women's rights and liberation, health equity, and poverty alleviation, race and social justice, and the partnerships, interconnection, communication, and sharing of resources between these industries and community organizations to better our world. From pharmaceuticals to journalism, urban planning and microfinance, I have a wide breadth of experiences which I hope to have the opportunity to continue doing internationally.

  • Young Advisory Board

    Seattle Art Museum

    (Non-Profit; Museums and Institutions industry)

    July 2009 — Present (4 months)

    Advise SAM in its outreach, cultivation and messaging for the 18-35 year-old demographic.

    More from the Seattle PI:

    Although 500,000 visitors passed through its doors last year when SAM reopened with a more-than-doubled downtown space, the museum's admittedly incomplete records suggest a hole where it would like people ages 18 to 35 to be.

    Thanks to a $750,000 grant from the Wallace Foundation, SAM plans to tackle the problem head on. First, like any institution run by academics, it will hunker down in study for a year. That study will produce real numbers: Who's coming, who's not and why.

    Only after many sources are tapped and everybody who's anybody at SAM agrees to changes and new programs will the youth-friendly SAM be ready to burst upon the consciousness of the young.

    That last step is called marketing, and its messages will appear not just through traditional media (newspapers, radio and TV), but through social networking sites where the young tend to congregate: My Space, Facebook, Twitter, YouTube and blogs.

    The Wallace Foundation, based in New York, gave $6.1 million in Excellence Awards in Seattle this year, with $1.6 million more for audience development. Grants range from $500,000 to $750,000 spread over four years. Besides SAM, grants went to the Experience Museum Project/Science Fiction Hall of Fame, One Reel, On the Boards, Pacific Northwest Ballet, Seattle Opera, Seattle Repertory Theatre, Seattle Youth Symphony and SIFF (Cinema Seattle).

    "I want the museum to be part of youth consciousness in this city," she said. "Visiting art museums can be a daily life experience, like going to the movies. People ask each other all the time if they've seen a movie. They discuss it and compare it to other movies. I want those conversations to be going on about SAM."
    Sandra Jackson-Dumont, SAM's deputy director for education and public programs

  • Youth Venture Changemaker Coach

    Ashoka

    (Non-Profit; Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    March 2009 — Present (8 months)

    Youth Venture enables young people to learn early on in life that they can lead social change. One of the most effective ways to improve the lives of youth is to empower them to realize their own ability to make positive social change. Youth Venture thus inspires and invests in teams of young people to start and lead their own social ventures, and is building a powerful network of young changemakers across the world. It is currently operating in the US, Mexico, Argentina, Brazil, India, South Africa, Thailand, France, Germany, and Spain.

    Youth Venture seeks to create impact by transforming: the youth participant, through the enabling experience of starting a social venture, the youth team, as they learn important life skills and realize that they can create change, the community, as growing numbers of Youth Venture teams tip Society at large, by fundamentally redefining the role of young people as leaders of social change.

    http://www.ashoka.org/youthventure

  • Alumni Leadership Committee, Class of 2009

    Leadership Tomorrow

    (Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    September 2008 — Present (1 year 2 months)

    Leadership Tomorrow prepares, challenges and engages emerging and existing leaders through leadership development for the benefit of the Puget Sound region. The organization's programs enlighten, inspire and transform participants, who come from a diverse array of professions and communities, by convening productive conversations around critical issues that impact the region.     (Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    July 2008 — Present (1 year 4 months)

    The Asian Pacific Islander Community Leadership Foundation builds individual and community leadership which promotes greater economic, social and political justice; develops and mentors ethical, highly-skilled, and caring API leaders; and strengthens the unique and diverse voices of the united API community in the democratic process.   • Community Partner

    Tasveer Independent South Asian Film Festival

    (Media Production industry)

    June 2008 — Present (1 year 5 months)

    Join us for the 6th Annual Tasveer Independent South Asian Film Festival Oct. 2-4 at the Broadway Performance Hall!
    http://isaff.tasveer.org/2009/

    TASVEER, "picture" in Hindi/Urdu, is a grassroots, community-based organization that is committed to bringing independent progressive films from South Asia and the South Asian Diaspora to the Pacific Northwest.

    We provide an alternative to the commercial films of South Asia. Many people only know about Bollywood but we explore the vast film expressions that come out of South Asia - experimental shorts films, political documentaries, narrative art films from the subcontinent, indy narratives by Diaspora, classic silent films, music videos, and more. Whether aspiring or a veteran, we support all independent filmmakers working with South Asian themes.

    By screening independent films, we engage the community in dialogue and action around sociopolitical and cultural themes that are specific to South Asia - Afghanistan, Bangladesh, Bhutan, India, Maldives, Nepal, Pakistan, Sri Lanka, and Tibet.
