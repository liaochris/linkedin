Van Horgen’s Summary

Van graduated from the University of Minnesota with a degree in English Literature. His senior dissertation was a chapbook of poems, a visionary work destined for obscurity. After realizing poetry doesn’t pay, Van pursued the lucrative career of a corn dog stand manager and then moved on to the even less profitable profession of a juvenile detention officer.

Van got his start in brand building as the supply clerk at Campbell Mithun. At night, he attended Miami Ad School. He eventually got the chance to leave hauling paper on a dolly for writing copy on a laptop. At Campbell Mithun, Van worked on clients such as Verizon Wireless, H&R Block and Toro.

After experiencing big agency life at Campbell Mithun, Van worked at Lindsay Stone & Briggs, a small shop in Madison, Wisconsin. During his time there, Van helped Lindsay Stone & Briggs become a first-time finalist for the O’Toole Awards as Small Advertising Agency of the Year.

As a copywriter with an affinity for the visual arts, Van’s next stop was at Carmichael Lynch Thorburn, the design agency of Carmichael Lynch. Here, Van had the opportunity to work on assignments for Aveda, Disney Cruise Line and Toys R Us.

After a short stop at Little & Company, where he worked on Target and Microsoft, Van wanted to explore his passion for interactive and digital creative. He is currently at space150 helping Starz, Dairy Queen and American Express grow their businesses.

Outside space150, Van is a magazine junkie and enjoys running, listening to music and exploring the World Wide Web. He is married to Jody and together they have a daughter, Malia, who makes him laugh every day.
