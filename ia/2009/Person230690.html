Terrie Wheeler’s Summary

Terrie founded Professional Services Marketing, Inc. (PSM) in 1996 - a consulting firm dedicated exclusively to working with professional services firms and the individuals within those firms. Terrie has over 20 years of business planning and marketing experience at all levels including firm-wide, regional office, practice group, industry, and individual marketing initiatives.

In addition, PSM offers two unique programs to assist individuals in the development of results-oriented marketing strategies:

Your Personal Marketing Coach program offers one-on-one personal coaching to individual professionals in law, banking, accounting, financial services and consulting.

www.MarketYourLawPractice.com - PSM has launched a unique web-based tool for lawyers. Through this website, subscribers to the service can access hundreds of tools, tips, checklists, templates and other practical information to design, develop and implement a marketing plan.

Other Professional Experience
Prior to founding PSM in 1996, Terrie directed the marketing function at three major Twin Cities law firms.

Speaking Engagements - Terrie is a frequent presenter on topics related to professional services marketing and has trained for business groups, educational institutions, and professional services firms in Minnesota and throughout the country.

Education - Terrie holds a Master of Arts degree, magna cum laude, from the Graduate School of Business (MBC), University of St. Thomas, St. Paul, Minnesota, where she also earned graduate-level certification in public and media relations. Terrie completed her bachelor of arts, magna cum laude, in Organizational Management and Communications, from Concordia University in Saint Paul, Minnesota.

Terrie Wheeler’s Specialties:

Strategic Marketing Planning and Implementation for Professional Services Firms including various services supporting Professional Services Marketing's Four Pillars of Marketing:
- Expanding Relationships with Existing Clients
- New Business Development
- Increasing Name Recognition and Awareness
- Utilizing Targeted and Effective Communications
