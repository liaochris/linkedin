IPMA Singapore’s Summary

Prof Sattar Bawany has over 25 years’ international business management experience, including 15 years in executive coaching, group facilitation, and leadership development and training with global management consulting firms.

In addition to his business and consulting career, Sattar has over 10 years of concurrent academic experience as an Adjunct Professor teaching senior executives international business strategies and human resource courses at various leading universities.

He is currently a Council Member and Honorary Academic Advisor of International Professional Managers Association (IPMA) as well as Adjunct Professor of Strategy of Paris Graduate School of Management (PGSM).

He is also the Head of Transition Coaching Practice, DBM Asia Pacific. DBM is a leading global outplacement, coaching, and career management firm providing services to private and public companies, not-for-profits and governments.

IPMA Singapore’s Specialties:

Executive Coaching, Transition Coaching and Executive Onboarding
Organisational Development & Change Management
Strategic Human Resource Management

IPMA Singapore’s Education

  • University of Worcester

    PhD, Business Administration (Human Resource Management), 2009 — 2012 (expected)

    Title of Doctoral Research: The Impact of Executive Coaching on Personal and Professional Development of Leaders.

    The aim of the doctoral research is as follows:

    How can Executive Coaching impact on the Personal and Professional Development of Leaders and as a result how this could assist organisational executives at the "C-Suite" in addressing the pervading obstacles and dilemmas, in both their private and professional lives, as they go about their duties as organisational leaders and more often than not includes roles in a family as well as friend, community member, etc.?

    Practically, this research will provide justification for the use of executive coaching in organisational settings, which is important given the high level of growth in the multi billion dollar executive coaching industry. From a theoretical perspective, the research outcomes will augment the limited body of knowledge in this area.
