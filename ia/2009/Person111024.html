Jerome Osselaer’s Summary

1983 : when my passion started. First season as a football player. One year later, France won the Euro and I completed my first Panini album.

2009 : as an MBA ESSEC graduate, a few years of experience allowed me to enrich my course of study with an operational and international dimension. After three years running a sports management company I founded, I spent another three years in a consulting company, where I developed competencies such as analytical skills, priority management, day-to-day CRM, market research, team-working experience, or computer skills.

After graduating from the MBA in Football Industry at the University of Liverpool, I write analytic articles for footballpartnerships.com in English and Sport Stratégies in French. I strongly desire to join the sports industry, as a perfect professional achievement mixing my consulting and marketing background with my passion for sports, in a challenging environment.

I’m interested in strategic marketing, including brand strategy, CRM strategy and sponsorship strategy, in order to generate new revenue streams or increase existing ones. The football industry, and clubs in particular, is financially TV-dependent and has been growing tremendously in the last 15 years, along with satellite and cable TV, especially in Europe. Consequently, there are still huge opportunities to develop other financial drivers through marketing and commercial levers, together with the redevelopment / construction boom of new stadia considered as profit centres.

I am open to relocate wherever needed for the right job.

According to me, 3 of the best Marketing Managers in Football : Edward Freedman, Peter Kenyon, and Lars-Christer Olsson.

Outside football, 3 of the best Marketing Strategies : Nespresso, iPod, Wii.

My motto : “Give a man a fish he’ll eat for a day. Teach a man how to fish, he’ll eat forever”.
