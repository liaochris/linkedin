Vanessa Fonseca Tumilasci’s Summary

I completed my Undergraduate and Masters Degrees at the Universidade Federal do Rio Grande do Sul (UFRGS) and Universidade de Sao Paulo, respectively, in Brazil.
I have been working with Dr. John Hiscott at the Lady Davis Institute to explore the therapeutic potential of oncolytic viruses as a new strategy for cancer treatment that specifically targets and kills cancer cells, while sparing normal tissues. I have specifically focused on the potential of one such virus – VSV, in the treatment of primary chronic lymphocytic leukemia (CLL), one of the most common forms of leukemia. There is no cure for CLL and all accepted treatment regimens are neither curative nor associated with prolonged survival CLL; therefore, development of new treatment strategies for CLL remains an important challenge. Although the mechanisms responsible for CLL development are still unknown, impaired apoptosis has been correlated with disease and overexpression of pro-survival BCL proteins is often detected in CLL. I set out to test the hypothesis that inhibition of pro-survival proteins may sensitize CLL cells to VSV-induced cell death. Primary CLL cells were resistant to VSV-induced cell death but the use of a BCL-2 inhibitor in combination with VSV acted synergistically to trigger cell death in primary ex vivo (ie from the patient) CLL cells but did not render healthy peripheral blood cells sensitive to VSV-oncolysis, demonstrating the safety and efficacy of this combination. This work has been successfully published at Journal of Virology (J Virol. 2008 Sep;82(17):8487-99). As a continuation of this project I am looking in more detail in the mechanisms by which VSV and BCL-2 inhibitors induce the mitochondrial apoptotic pathway and I am also evaluating the potential of this combination in in vivo models.
