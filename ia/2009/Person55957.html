David Lombardo’s Summary

I have had good entry level experience in three fields: aviation, computer science, and music. For music, I won the Louis Armstrong Jazz award in High School for drum set, and played percussion in the Empire State Youth Orchestra. I formed the band Springload in 2006 @ Purdue, and the band went on to win awards in Indiana, as well as play various gigs in the midwest. I have auditioned for Amanda Overmyer, done sessions for a few local musicians in Albany, NY, and have owned an operated a music venue in Lafayette, Indiana with my old band, Springload. I am a Purdue Tour Guide. I am currently majoring in Aviation Management and self taught in some electrical and computer engineering.

I had an internship with the Federal Aviation Administration and am currently studying aviation technology with an emphasis on airport and airline management. I worked on 'Hangar of The Future', at Purdue University (directed by Professor Denver Lopp) a project that was featured in Popular Mechanics magazine. The project aims to improve hangar operations in airports: decrease aircraft turnaround time, lower risks associated with preventative maintenance standards, and establish data keeping standards using RFID cards. The project will continue to grow and expand as the years go on.

On the computer side of things, I have experience working at the State of New York's Energy Research and Development Authority doing database filing and data entry. I also have extensive experience with the IT field, and am currently working on getting my A+ certification for computer repair. I am self taught in a number of programming languages, and I ran a small computer repair operation at one point when I was younger. I am a licensed ham radio operator in the United States.
