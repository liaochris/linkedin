Ric Miller’s Summary

Link to a resume: http://www.triplescreenmethod.com/ProcessOptimization/Resume.asp

PROFESSIONAL EXPERIENCE – Monsanto Fellow and Solutia Fellow

In most of the following cases, I picked the team, designed the strategies and led each to its final accomplished goal. Here is another example.

Solutia’s 2-Step BCF Yield Bulk and PDL Variation Reduction Team (Greenwood, S.C.) 2007

Business Case: 2-Step BCF represented a key ~120 million lbs annual market for Solutia, and yarn quality (bulk/dye streaking) remained an issue at its largest carpet yarn customer. As a consequence, Solutia was perceived to have inferior BCF yarn quality relative to Invista, the market leader. Route: (1) form a team whose goal was to reduce both yarn bulk and dye variance by 44%; (2) conduct a brainstorming session to identify and rank order opportunities for variance reduction; (3) meet monthly to monitor the implementation strategy.

• Identified 122 potential root causes and championed 25 of them;
• Reduced bulk and dye variation by 44% and identified major process causes: bulk (spun yarn package gradient) and dye (process excursions in multi-position processes);
• Introduced a positional audit strategy to ensure proper process conditions were maintained;
• Implemented positional variation control strategies for this multi-positional process.

Ric Miller’s Specialties:

Developed a process improvement strategy utilizing directed brainstorming, root-cause championing, process optimization, measurement system validation. The strategy can improve any manufacturing process. Other competencies: fiber, yarn and carpet physics, analytical method development/validation, and statistical quality control, traditional and non traditional (nonparametric, robust regressions, resampling) methodologies

Identified 78 opportunities, championed 45 of them and recommended implementing changes that were projected to save $876 thousand.
