Ben Turney’s Summary

I set my first company up when I left University in 2001. My career from then until 2006 was mixed, but I learnt a great deal at the sharp end of entrepreneurial life. Surviving with not enough money and with no experience to rely on, taught me a great deal about what it takes to set up and run a business. This experience is vital to what I do today as it gives me a real insight into the challenges my clients face.

I now specialise in start-up investments. Venture Point provides services and support to start-up entrepreneurs. Our main offerings include one to one guidance and support and entrepreneurial training programmes. Epiphany Capital is our FSA-authorised corporate finance company. This allows us to advise and arrange on funding packages for start-up companies as well as making direct investments into our clients of up to £75,000.

We currently run a 4,500sqft venture centre in Manchester. Our landlord is the University of Manchester and we have a long term exclusive deal for the space we are in.

Our goals during 2008 are to establish our investment model, make our first investments and find additional grow on space.
