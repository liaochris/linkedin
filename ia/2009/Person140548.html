Laurence Wood’s Summary

I am a polymath and autodidact. Two decades ago I was the lead research scientist and an engineering department manager at GTE Government Systems (now divisions of General Dynamics) where I was considered the most prolific filer of patents based on novel technologies of my design. While at GTE my award winning research generated more than 250 million USD. I have been a consultant to many classified government agencies and military commanders at the highest organizational levels. I have been an invited lectured at MIT, Harvard, Los Alamos National Labs and the Santa Fe Institute to list a few places. In 1994 at the close of the cold war I left the corporate world behind to fulfill my dream to work with my wife Lisa Wood and apply all my energies to changing the life sciences by obsoleting molecular orbital (MO) theory, the current basis for chemistry science, i.e. MO theory is the standard explanation of how electrons are situated in stable organic compounds. Along the way I have explored and developed goal supporting orthogonal technologies under related companies my wife and I have formed. These orthogonal technologies are: synthetic motion and performance capture plus high assurance swarm based cloud computing, both of which are currently applied operationally to the implementation of our semiclassical quantum field femtochemistry drug discovery science and technology. Semiclassical quantum field (SQF) chemistry is a revolutionary non-molecular orbital theory based chemistry science and technology that simplifies and unifies organic chemistry for precision analysis, design, and synthesis of highly selective, biologically active therapeutic compounds.

Laurence Wood’s Specialties:

Advanced mathematics, quantum physics, general relativity, low temperature physics, high energy physics, nuclear physics, thermodynamics, organic and medicinal chemistry, molecular biology, computer science, microelectronics, optoelectronics, CGI, patent law (I am a US Patent office registered inventor), and super conducting electronics.
