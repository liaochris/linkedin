Richard Hsu’s Summary

Mr. Hsu is a Partner at Townsend and Townsend and Crew LLP and Chair of the Technology Transactions Practice Group, where he counsels and provides companies advice on all licensing transactions and associated intellectual property issues. He has extensive experience in negotiating license, development, acquisition, service, purchase and other partnering agreements for a variety of industries and clients, including:

Semiconductors: Applied Materials, Fairchild, Hynix, nVidia, Synaptics, MicroUnity;

Life sciences: Baxter Healthcare, Covidien, Connetics (acq. by Stiefel Labs), Oxigene, Li-Cor, Prometheus Labs;

Medical devices: Visx (acq. by Abbott), Intuitive Surgical, Fox Hollow (acq. by ev3), Cardica, Durect;

Computers/Internet: Selectica, Merced Systems, WebEx (acq. by Cisco, Yahoo! , myShape; Oracle; Insight;

Nanotechnology: Evolved Machines, NanoSys, Inc.;

CleanTech: Soraa, Kaai, Stion, Mobius Power;

University/Gov't: Caltech, CSIRO (Australia), Exploit Tech (Singapore);

Financial services: Visa, Goldman Sachs;

Retail: Brooks Sports, Zappos, LeapFrog, Dreyer's Ice Cream;

Entertainment: Walt Disney, Electronic Arts, Konami, LivePlanet, Zynga, Sony Entertainment.

In addition to structuring and negotiating technology transactions, Mr. Hsu also provides strategic intellectual property advice and advises clients on innovative business strategies. Mr. Hsu has assisted numerous clients to develop and implement strategies to acquire, protect, enforce and commercialize various forms of intellectual property.

A frequent lecturer on licensing and intellectual property issues, Mr. Hsu has published a book on patents (Fairmont Press 1997) and written articles for the San Francisco Daily Journal, New York Times, Communications of the ACM and the Los Angeles Business Journal. He has also been quoted in numerous publications, including the Caltech News, Corporate Counsel and the Los Angeles Times.

Richard Hsu’s Specialties:

Intellectual property, technology and intellectual property transactions or licenses, patents, patent strategy, intellectual property strategy, intellectual property purchase and sales, license and other commercial agreements or contracts
