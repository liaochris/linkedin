  • Chief Executive

    Savekers Limited

    (Furniture industry)

    April 1994 — April 2009 (15 years 1 month)

    After graduating from Teesside University with a BA Hons in Product Design & Marketing I joined Savekers soon after on a temporary contract. During my 15 years at the family business I had worked in every job in the company, from the shop floor to customer services and sales, learning the skills and aptitude necessary for each role, including welding and polishing. I led Savekers through an MBO (valued at £2.4M), restructured the factory and Board of Directors, made some of the Board (family members) redundant, directed it through the acquisition of West Bromwich-based company Pride in Design – which specifically added the Cantel range of retail products to Savekers’ portfolio – and the acquisition of TTC Limited of Nottingham.

    Through a proactive approach to business, growing Savekers and developing its customer service and manufacturing capabilities, I gained a reputation for entrepreneurship. The company was unable to weather the recession of 2009 and I voluntarily placed it in administration on 10th March 2009 putting an end to its 106 years of trading.
