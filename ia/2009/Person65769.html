  • Central Regional Manager

    Delrina

    (Public Company; 51-200 employees; Computer Software industry)

    March 1992 — June 1994 (2 years 4 months)

    Initially I was responsible for pioneering and building out the entire US territory. Later I assumed the role of Central Regional Manager where I was responsible for a team of Systems Engineers and Sales Representatives, capable of supporting Fortune 500 companies. The Central North American Region consisted of all states and provinces bounded by Texas to Alabama and north to Saskatchewan and Ontario. My team handled direct corporate sales and developed a Corporate Reseller strategy, under the umbrella of the Major Accounts Division. Delrina's products were sold as enterprise wide solutions and as a result I was responsible for negotiating Enterprise Corporate License Agreements. At that time the Central Region was the strongest region and produced the highest sales.

  • Corporate Sales

    MicroAge

    (Public Company; 51-200 employees; Computer Hardware industry)

    April 1985 — March 1992 (7 years)

    At MicroAge Toronto my responsibilities where two fold, first to develop an account base of new accounts and second to effectively manage those accounts and the MicroAge staff involved with them. I later assumed the role of Advanced Server Marketing Representative. My corporate sales made up half of MicroAge's annual revenue. In addition to these responsibilities I also played an important role in making strategic business decisions that influenced the corporate direction MicroAge followed.
