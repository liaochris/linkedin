Timothy Akimoff’s Summary

I spent several years working for various humanitarian aid organizations in Eastern Europe, especially in Bosnia and Albania, before returning to the United States to finish my university studies. I interned at the Kyiv Post, an English language newspaper in Kiev, Ukraine, just months before the Orange Revolution. After graduating from the University of Oregon with a degree in journalism, I interned at the Statesman Journal in Salem, OR., and then at The Oregonian in Portland, OR. I worked as a county reporter, community blogger (the subject is microbrew: Will Blog For Beer) and videographer at the Statesman Journal. I worked as a writer, online producer and videographer at the Missoulian in Missoula, MT., where I started the regional beer blog GrizzlyGrowler.com. I started the video program and helped create a strong online presence for the Missoulian. I am now the digital director of the Missoulian, a role in which I oversee all the digital content on our websites. I innovate, implement and train others to do the same.

Timothy Akimoff’s Specialties:

Video/audio editing, Russian language, cultural knowledge from extensive travel. Internet technologies such as web site construction, rss feeds, editing video and creating and monetizing blogs. Setting up content management systems.
