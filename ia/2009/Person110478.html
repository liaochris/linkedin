Jeremy Deedes’s Summary

Dear Reader,

You may like to know that I am over 50 but nowhere near retirement. I live and work in beautiful North Yorkshire, with my wife Caroline, my 12-year old son Alexander, one fat ginger cat, one Madagascan Ratcatcher (a dog), and assorted tropical fish. You can see photos and read about us at our web diary, www.me.com/caroline.deedes (we love our Macs).

I run Planning for Life, which was born out of a desire to help you in a way that conventional financial advice seldom achieves. Getting the company up and running has been a hard slog with a big investment in new skills whilst servicing an existing client base. I became the first UK Kinder Institute Registered Life Planner and owe much to my valuable relationship with George Kinder for blowing away a powerful integrity block.

I have also had to learn how to run a company, the practical stuff the MBA didn't teach but which I picked up from the E-myth. My team - Mary, Rowena and Annette - have been a fantastic support and help in building Planning for Life.

As a client of Planning for Life, my team and I would work closely with you to:

➢ Enable you to lead an authentic life by developing a sophisticated and mature approach to your relationship with money
➢ Discover your deepest and most profound goals
➢ Identify the obstacles (of all types) preventing you achieving your goals
➢ Develop a plan to achieve your goals
➢ Restructure your financial resources, if necessary, to achieve your goals
➢ Change your life in the deepest and most dramatic way

I would help you achieve yoir most profound life goals, to live a life of freedom and integrity without the suffering that so often surrounds our dealings with money.

I am actively involved in helping other advisers become planners, and promoting the financial planning profession. I am a Board Member of the Institute of Financial Planning, where I chair the Membership Committee and sit on the Conference Committee.

Jeremy Deedes’s Specialties:

I am a catholic, educated at Ampleforth in the Benedictine tradition, and one of my interests is to build a framework, based on Buddhism and Benedict's Rule, for dealing with money in the world as we find it today that can be followed by many, irrespective of their faith. I have put down some of my thoughts on my money and spirit website, and I would really welcome ideas and feedback.
