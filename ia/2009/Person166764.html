Marc Lewin’s Summary

Marc Lewin is licensed to practice law in Maryland and completed his LL.M. at Georgetown University Law Center to specialize in tax law in May '08. Marc hopes to secure a position as a tax attorney.

In his legal studies, Marc has proven that he is dedicated to succeed and can learn quickly, rising from a ranking in the top 51% of his class after his first semester at John Marshall to a final ranking in the top 24%. By placing special emphasis on developing strong research, writing, trial and appellate skills, he earned four A’s and one B+ in his five lawyering skills courses.

Marc has succeeded outside of the law school also. As the Peggy Browning Fellow at the Chicago Newspaper Guild, he contributed to an appellate brief that convinced Region 13 of the NLRB to reconsider its dismissal of an unfair labor practice. At the Illinois Labor Relations Board as an extern, he drafted a decision for the Board and a decision of an administrative law judge.

Marc Lewin’s Specialties:

Excellent writing and research skills, including LexisNexis and Westlaw Certification.
Strong knowledge of tax, labor, and employment law.
