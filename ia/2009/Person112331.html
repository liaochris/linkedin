Jean Guy Rens’s Summary

Jean-Guy Rens is an information-technologies consultant specializing in public policy analysis and marketing assistance for the introduction of new products and services. As such, he created the consulting firm ScienceTech Communications Inc. in 1992. Mr. Rens is Executive Vice President Mobility & Telecom of the Canadian Advanced Technology Alliance (CATA).

Jean-Guy Rens recently widened his scope of action to the photovoltaic industry and supports actively the development of a consistant solar public policy in Canada. He is a member of the Canadian Solar Industries Association (CanSIA).

Jean-Guy Rens conducted in 2007 a study on the mobile content industry in Canada. Prior to that, he had produced a landmark orientation plan on the coordination and centralization of all government telecommunications networks for the Government of Quebec (in cooperation with the University of Quebec in Montreal).

Mr. Rens and his team opened the way to the analysis of the IT industry in Quebec. He was the project manager and main author of the first studies on the Internet, the profile of the new industry (access providers and content developers), its social impacts and its applications (e-commerce).

Mr. Rens wrote several books and studies on IT. He co-authored a book on Quebec e-commerce, covering Electronic Data Interchange (EDI) and Web-based business-to business (Le Cybercommerce au Québec, 2001). As well, he is the author of a 1,000-page book of history of Canadian telecommunications covering the management, political, technological and social aspects of this industry (L'empire invisible,1993). Volume one of this work was published in English at McGill-Queen’s University Press (The Invisible Empire, 2001). Volume two is in preparation.
