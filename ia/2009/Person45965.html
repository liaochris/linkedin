Chuck Sanchez’s Summary

I was fortunate enough to begin my career shooting for (and placing stories in) the top echelon of media outlets during my work with a high-profile relationships expert.

I was also deeply immersed in the industry during a trial-by-fire position with a start-up PR agency in Milwaukee. Since there were no support staff at first (or even other colleagues!), I leapt head-first into the role of an account executive by running two accounts from campaign conception to ongoing execution and analysis.

In fact, I may be one of the few PR professionals of my generation to know what it was like for our forebears who - gasp! - only ever had giant Bacon's Directory books to flip through in order to call journalists!

After some business-to-business and nonprofit organization experience, I was recruited to Chicago to help create a new PR agency all over again. At this point I began dabbling in the use of social media and Web 2.0 in marketing communications...and I've been hooked ever since.

After a two years of spectacular growth I moved on to a large, international PR agency with the recommendations and blessings of some of my closest media contacts at CNN, Newsweek and the CBS Early Show.

Although I soon found that this particular agency was not a good fit, it was easy enough to pick up multiple freelance clients and strike out on my own.

Today, I assist in the management of PR, marketing communications and social media with the Illinois Student Assistance Commission (ISAC) to ensure that every higher education student has access to funds to make it happen!

    Illinois Student Assistance Commission

    (Government Administration industry)

    August 2008 — Present (6 months)

    The Illinois Student Assistance Commission (ISAC) provides students of all ages and backgrounds with the resources and support to obtain financial aid for higher education.

    A state agency, ISAC has paved the path to post-secondary education with innovative programs for more than 50 years. Last year alone, ISAC continued its mission to make college affordable for Illinois students by issuing 185,000 grants and scholarships totaling more than $448 million.

    The commission coordinates educational funding with student loans, as a loan guarantor and via numerous public programs such as the successful Monetary Award Program (MAP), the Illinois Veteran Grant and College Illinois!sm the commission’s groundbreaking 529 prepaid tuition program.

    Students and families have free access to a wealth of financial aid and college planning information at ISAC’s flagship website: www.collegezone.com.
