Kevin Ferguson’s Summary

I've spent the past 25 years covering business, science, technology and politics. Ten of those years have been for national mags, including Forbes.com, BusinessWeek, Inc., Entrprepreneur, US News and World Report.

The magazine work continues, but I'm also spending time researching, writing and editing reports for rights-based NGOs, including Physicians for Human Rights (reparations to Darfurians), Water For People (access to water and sanitation in Honduras and Guatemala), Management Sciences for Health (medical supply-chain logistics in Afghanistan and HIV/AIDS treatment in South Africa), CARE (women's rights), and Oxfam America.

In 2007, I earned my master's degree from Harvard University's Kennedy School of Government, where I was given the opportunity to study these and other topics in depth.

In January 2008, I returned from Ethiopia where I had the opportunity to experience in a very intimate way the daily struggle of orphaned children in Addis Ababa and in the Silte region of the SNNP State.

In March 2009, I travelled to India to report on water and sanitation in the slums of Delhi for BusinessWeek and Grist magazines, and to Himachal Pradesh for The New York Times.
