Raymond (Ray) Peacock’s Summary

Principal at Roundhouse Advisors, a consulting firm creating value for non-profits in a wide range of vertical markets, industries, and stability experiencing a need to re-organize, develop leadership, implement vision and action, and increase visibility and resources.

Raise awareness, increase resources (including funds & friends) for non-profit organizations whose missions are transformational. Consultant and coach specializing in leadership and organizational development, change strategies, and exit planning. Primary interest is individual and organizational improvement, discovering and improving strengths, and finding innovative solutions in turbulent times.

Progressive experience colors a career in designing, evaluating, leading, and resourcing non-profit programs. I hold a bachelor’s degree in sociology, master’s degree in social work, and doctorate in leadership development. I consult at local, regional and national levels and represented The Salvation Army, which Peter Drucker call’s “America’s Most Effective Organization”, to the White House and National Press Club. I served as CEO overseeing hundreds of employees and $40+ million annual operating budgets.

My community service efforts focus on enhancing the lives of the elderly and at-risk children and families including: the homeless, incarcerated parents and those in addiction recovery.

One major achievement was designing the leadership succession planning model for four major categories of leadership in the 13-state Western Region. I instituted a "two up, two down" plan looking at individuals and their next two positions in terms of what those two down need to learn and experience to move up. This is just a glimpse of what my experience and foresight allows me to offer my clients.

I am passionate about leadership development, social work, and public speaking. I am aware of the complexity of projects, budgets and political issues and am able to identify and manage these concerns.
