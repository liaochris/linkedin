Chuck Cohen’s Experience

  • Operations Manager

    GSD Services, Inc.

    (Security and Investigations industry)

    August 2004 — Present (4 years 11 months)

    Responsibilities include (GSD) oversight of design, installation and maintenance of customer burglary, fire, access control, surveillance/CCTV, and infrastructure monitoring systems, and (SCS) oversight of Central Station operation & administration.

    GSD Services is a full-service unrestricted low voltage contractor located on Atlanta's perimeter providing implementation of various security-related projects for 30+ years. Clientel includes companies such as Galileo, CEIA, Piedmont Medical Group, Forsyth County Schools, Sheltering Arms day care centers, and the Ed Voyles family of car dealerships.

    Security Communications Services Inc. (SCS) is the company's Central Station, providing 24x7 monitoring of signals received from systems all over the Southeastern United States. SCS operates their main center in Atlanta GA and their secondary (redundant) center in Omaha NE. Both UL compliant centers feature T1 connectivity & mutiple DID/DOD lines, UPS & generators for backup power, and digital 24-channel telephone voice recording.

  • Data Center Systems Specialist

    Interland, Inc.

    (Internet industry)

    September 2002 — April 2004 (1 year 8 months)

    Main responsibilities included oversight of the Atlanta data center's physical infrastructure & security systems, cabinet layout & cable-plant management, and preparations for all incoming server relocation/consolidation projects as well as new server installs.

    Interland (now WEB.com) is the leading provider of solutions for websites and web services with more than 4 million websites built using proprietary tools, services and patented technology.

    Interland's physical infrastructure is now part of Peer1 Networks, the leading IT infrastructure provider to over 10,000 startups and businesses, delivering managed, self-managed, and co-location hosting services via 15 data centers in 12 cities across North America.

  • Data Center Operations Manager

    NetRail, Inc.

    (Internet industry)

    December 1998 — May 2001 (2 years 6 months)

    Responsible for the daily operations and continuous upgrade & build-out of all NetRail operated co-location data center facilities.

It utilized an OC-48 network using OSPF, BGP, & MPLS in 60+ cities and a dozen exchange points throughout the US, and 96% of all its internet traffic was exchanged over private peering. Network equipment included Lucent 500 & 9000 series carrier-class switches and Juniper M40 backbone routers. NetRail operated co-location facilities were located in Atlanta, Chicago, and Arlington VA.
