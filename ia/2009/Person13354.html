Andrea Moretti-Adimari’s Summary

Andrea was part of trying to launch four early stage ventures Through these experiences he developed a better feel for how to try to overcome the challenges of introducing new technologies which represent a significant break from a sector's established norms.

While not all of these technologies eventually broke through, while Andrea was part of the team all thankfully "missed the rocks", and transitioned to their respective next stages.

These experiences developed an interest in the drivers of cooperation and non-cooperation in pressurized circumstances, often with remote teams.

Together with a well respected psychologist coach and his software development team, Andrea recently helped co-design and launch Premier Mentor, a remote team coaching system.

  • Research into unlocking greater group effectiveness

    New Venture Architects

    (Market Research industry)

    July 2007 — Present (2 years)

    In Ancient Greece, the Sophists were a group of teachers of philosophy and rhetoric. Rhetoric can be used to confuse in order to dismantle mental barriers for coaching, mentoring, therapy and even helping people on their individual spiritual journey (the personal "Route to Love")

    An economic framework is proposed for the various disciplines that "Give Advice for Making the Most of Now"

    The segmentation of mindsets is based on two axes:

    - How information is assessed: (a) based on codified set rules or (b) based on manifested best practice of freely chosen role models

    - How information is projected into future scenarios: (a) logically built up from within like a PC (with whatever biases may lie there) or (b) disproportionately swayed by external data like a network terminal, and capable of reaching decisions that appear illogical to the PC

    Based on this framework, a methodology has been proposed to "Dismantle the Barriers to Right Person, in the Right Place, at the Right Time".

    In essence this is about how to try to resurface trust in complex multi-player Prisoner's Dilemma.

    Several programmes to test this are currently running, judged by a small group of Kellogg faculty members, which began in May 06 and will end on Aug 31 2009:

    1) Testing the effectiveness of scaling the Sophist approach with technology:

    (a) Can Gestalt/Paradoxical Intentions be softened with technology (and a more sustained and tactical approach)?

    (b) Can a "family" be helped across the chasms in the Cleese/Skynner functionality spectrum?

    (c) Can elite sports-style coaching be adapted to business?

    2) Manifesting the mechanics of group persuasion in seemingly impossible settings ("How to get funded to sail West in 1492")

    3) Exploring the persuasive mechanisms for launching a new brand in an entrenched and potentially explosive market place. ("1st Century Cola Wars. Best practice guerilla brand launch?")
