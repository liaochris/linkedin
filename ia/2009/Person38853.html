Casey Golden’s Summary

Casey started his first company at age 11 (biodegradable golf tees), got two patents, traveled in the US and Japan to speak about invention and promote entrepreneurship for students, sold product in K-Marts across US, and was also featured on NBC, CBS, The Disney Channel, and the Discovery Channel, and in numerous publications including People Magazine. Since then started 3 companies, most notably, Parature, www.parature.com, an on-demand, software as a service CRM company, which has grown to 120+ employees.

Current social venture, Small Act Network, is merging the worlds of social media and philanthropy, helping nonprofits develop sustainable fundraising platforms through the cultivation of awareness, passion and action. This includes both consulting nonprofits on how to effectively leverage existing social media (facebook, twitter, etc.) and also creating new social media sites and applications to enhance relationships and build new ones.

Has written chapter "Technology with a Heart" in the BusinessWeek BestSeller, "Do Your Giving While You Are Living.", www.DoYourGiving.com , (Released Holiday '08), and is currently writing book, "Small Acts. Big Impact. : How You Can Change The World 5 Minutes At A Time" (to be published Fall '09). Chosen as one of the D.C. Region's top young entrepreneurs, named in the "35 Under 35 Top Entrepreneurs" in 2008.

    (Privately Held; Philanthropy industry)

    August 2007 — Present (1 year 11 months)

    The Small Act Network seeks to help people find and substitute every day habits for ones that are more socially responsible or help them in their personal lives/relationships. Our core philosophy is that it is the small acts that we can all do that are create the biggest impact and are the true sources of positive social change.

    In line with this, one of our first steps is that we are developing websites and technologies to help raise funding for charities from corporate sources and gain awareness for charities from internet and mobile phone users. These sites and technologies all focus on niche areas to provide the public with alternatives to their every day habits that are both beneficial to them and the charities they care about.
