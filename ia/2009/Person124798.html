Justin Mclean’s Summary

Justin has 10+ years experience in developing web based applications and has 15+ years experience in the IT industry.

Prior to 1998 Justin worked as full time programmer for several companies including Telstra, Open Technology and Logica and was involved in many large scale and complex development projects.

In 1997 Justin formed his own company, Class Software, and since then has managed and developed more than a 100 web applications, database driven web sites, multimedia applications and semiconnected desktop applications.

Justin has worked on web applications for clients such as Fairfax, Telstra, News Limited, MTV, Terraplanet, SBS, The University of Technology, Panasonic, Harcourt Publishing, Guide Dogs, The Department of Health and many other large and small companies. Other development and consulting companies such as Rocketboots, Red Square, Firmware, NetHead, Mort Bay and Gruden have used his skills and expertise on projects.

Justin has a wide range of skills and experience, including;
• In-depth knowledge of ColdFusion, Flex, ActionScript and AIR.
• Database design and development including enterprise databases such as Sybase, Oracle and SQL server.
• Web application development including usability, accessibility and interface design as well as detailed knowledge of HTML, XHTML, CSS, Javascript, web standards, web services, XML, E4X, Spry, AJAX, microformats, RIAs, CMSs, workflow systems, web hosting and security.
• Software and hardware development with the open source Arduino platform.

Justin has a degree in Computer Science and continues to develop his skills through further studies and regular attendance of conferences both here and overseas. Justin was a speaker at WebDU 2009 and is speaking at cf.Objective(ANZ).

Justin is a certified Adobe trainer for Flex, AIR and ColdFusion.

Class Software is a silver level Adobe solution partner.

Justin Mclean’s Specialties:

Web application development, web development, database design, ColdFusion, Flex, Flash and AIR development.
