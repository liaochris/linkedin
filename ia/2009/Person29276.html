Bjorn Book-Larsson’s Summary

Holding the dual role of COO and CTO, responsible for K2's overall operations, as well as technology solutions behind some of the world's largest MMOs.

Bjorn Book-Larsson’s Experience

  • COO & CTO

    K2 Network

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Computer Games industry)

    September 2008 — Present (9 months)

    As Chief Operating Officer and CTO at K2 Network - one of the world's largest operators of MMOs - responsible for the overall company operations and technology solutions.

    Through our consumer brand "Gamersfirst.com" we have 23 million unique registered MMO players across our 5 major product lines. With our publishing operations in North America, Europe, Turkey, Brazil and India - we also have a direct-to-consumer sales presence in 1000's of global internet cafes.

  • CTO and VP Development

    Gameshastra pvt / Gameshastra Inc.

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Computer Games industry)

    April 2007 — September 2008 (1 year 6 months)

    CTO and Head of the Game Development business unit, one of Game Shastra's three business units (the other two being Testing and Animation). Manages the entire development team and oversees overall technical decisions in the company in the Hyderabad, India office as well as El Segundo, California, USA.

    Game Shastra was India's largest outsourced game services company. Customers included several leading casual game and core game publishers, on projects ranging from simple game components to complete end-to-end games on PC, Web, XBox360, Wii and PS2.

  • Chief Technology Officer

    FUN Technologies

    (Public Company; 201-500 employees; FUN [LSE & TSX]; Computer Games industry)

    May 2004 — April 2007 (3 years)

    In 2004 FUN Technologies aquires SkillJam from Intermix Media. Retained by FUN as part of the transaction and promoted to CTO in the new FUN group of companies (LSE:FUN, TSX:FUN).

    Continued to expand products offerd to our network of white-label partner systems (powering the tournament game backends of MSN, AOL, Real, Lycos, Virgin + 160 partners) as well as focusing on re-architecting the platform for alternative delivery methods via Mobile, ITV and kiosk/on-location systems.

    FUN expands to include Sports Information(http://www.donbest.com), Fantasy Sports (http://www.fanball.com), Skill Games (http://www.skilljam.com and http://www.worldwinner.com), Mobile and Game Studio (http://www.octopi.com with online MMO http://www.poxnora.com) and Casual Games (http://www.teagames.com).

    March 2006 Liberty Media (NASDAQ:LINT) aquires 51% ownership in FUN. FUN is listed in London and Toronto under the symbol FUN. June 2006: the company reached 360 employees and a market cap of 350m USD.

  • CIO SkillJam

    Intermix Media / EUniverse Inc.

    (Public Company; 201-500 employees; MIX; Internet industry)

    November 2001 — May 2004 (2 years 7 months)

    During purchase of EGamesGroup, became CIO of skill games division of EUniverse (NASDAQ:EUNI), later known as Intermix Media (AMEX:MIX). Shortly after purchase skill and tournament games division is renamed "SkillJam"

    Key to architecting custom white label skill game platforms launched by 150+ partners including Microsoft, AOL, Real Networks, Excite, IVillage, and network grows to over 8 million registered tournament players.

    June 2004, Intermix sells SkillJam to FUN Technologies (LSE:FUN) for 11m USD.

  • CTO

    EGamesGroup

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Internet industry)

    November 1999 — November 2001 (2 years 1 month)

    Developed the one of the world's first commercial skill gaming systems. Eventually expanded to include all forms of casual online games in competitive skill-based tournament format. We sold the company to Euniverse / Intermix Media - Nov 26, 2001 and relocated key personell to Los Angeles, CA.

  • VP Technology

    USAMEDIA, inc.

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Broadcast Media industry)

    January 1997 — November 1999 (2 years 11 months)

    Multi-media production company, clients included Duke University (web sites), Mattell (television commercials starring Mia Hamm), Glaxo-Smith-Kline (commercials, videos), Telcordia (XML courseware), Lucent Technologies (XML courseware), AT&T (training)
