Daniel Wigdor’s Summary

My research lies in the general area of Human Computer Interaction. My recent research has focused on understanding perceptual limitations which influence the design of present and emerging technologies. I have particular expertise with the design of multi-touch and mobile devices and interfaces.

Additional interests include Computer Supported Collaborative Work, Information Visualization, Macro-Human Computer Interaction, Social Implications of Computing, and Ubiquitous Computing.

Daniel Wigdor’s Specialties:

Expertise in the design, implementation of novel interaction hardware, software

Experience with research, development, and business of software and hardware

Multiple patent applications in two different organizations

Team management experience

User centered design experience, laboratory and ethnographic user studies

Accomplished speaker: university lecturer, invited talks

High attention to detail, broad focus
