Chris Jones’s Summary

I have a broad range of technical and sales experience.

A common thread through my career has been identifying, analyzing, and ultimately solving challenging technical and business issues.

My combined three years at Ivara Corporation first as a Systems Administrator and then Technical Implementation Consultant laid the foundation for my next four years as an Independent IT Consultant, contracted primarily to Nerds On Site Inc, an
international network of independent contractors.

In that time I tackled many conventional and not-so-conventional technology problems for a variety of residential and corporate clients. My people and sales skills were significantly improved as a result of taking Dale Carnegie Training and the follow up Dale Carnegie Sales Advantage courses.

In my fourth year as a consultant I was looking for a new challenge, and found an opportunity with Recovery Force, a small family-run home-based data recovery business located in Guelph. I worked with Recovery Force in a sales and technical capacity during the day, and continued my consulting business on a smaller scale on evenings and weekends.

My skills and experience have put me in the unique position of being able to understand all of the technical aspects of software and hardware, while simultaneously being able to communicate with non-technical people looking for a solution to whatever problems they may be having.

I am now leveraging both my technical and sales skills at Nortek Computers Ltd. in my home town of North Bay, Ontario. Most of my work involves interfacing with new and existing customers, understanding their problems, and ultimately delivering solutions. My experiences with computer forensics, advanced data recovery including RAID reconstruction, UNIX systems (Linux, Mac OS X and BSD) have proven to be valuable.

My current position allows me to maintain my career goals of constant growth in terms of both engineering and sales proficiency.
