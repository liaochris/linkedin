Dave Whittle’s Summary

I've been passionately involved in the technology industry since '78 and have been online since '79. In '80, I started perhaps the first retail store selling personal computers at a shopping mall.

In '88, I started marketing in cyberspace, with my Personal System/2 Reference Tables, which saved IBM a documented $22 million by '90. In '92, IBM tapped me to represent OS/2 online, at which time I started one of the worlds first and largest virtual teams and online social networks - Team OS/2. As IBM's Online Advocate, I represented IBM online when the web was first coming into prominence.

In '93, I co-authored "Dvorak's Guide to OS/2" with John C. Dvorak and authored "Cyberspace: The Human Dimension" in '97 - a work I'm still proud of for its visionary perspective on social networking and Internet business / marketing

My firm has served selected companies in marketing online and offline - using our trade secret marketing methods to help companies rise above the fray with quality products and offerings. We help bridge the gap between people and any technology that has the power to increase individual enjoyment, strengthen families, and enhance personal fulfillment.

Examples: from '97 through 2001, we helped MGI Software define and establish PhotoSuite as the best-selling digital photo application. From 2002 through early 2007, we developed a popular Build-Your-Own-PC seminar for Intel and helped them popularize the Centrino Mobile Technology. Over the past 5 years, we have popularized muvee's outstanding lineup of slideshow / video software and the innovative, industry-leading services from INVISUS Direct.

I'm currently working with a team of over a dozen talented individuals to create CoolHotNot.com - a social networking site dedicated to tapping the wisdom of the masses and experts alike in choosing quality tech products and avoiding the losers. Join us as we dramatically improve the information available to those making tech decisions!

Dave Whittle’s Specialties:

Internet marketing and business development, strategic niche marketing, viral marketing, online communications, social networking, new media, analytics, and copywriting. Named by Working Woman Magazine as "one of America's most original technological thinkers."
