Diane Mack’s Summary

Diverse, multi-disciplinary, multi-jurisdictional and multi-level (hierarchical) experience in homeland security and emergency management - preparedness, prevention, response, and recovery. Ultimate goal at the end of every day is forward progress in protecting the safety, health, and security of others, and adding more solid bricks to the path of a smooth road to recovery.

Diane Mack’s Specialties:

Strategic planning, capabilities expansion, emergency response, critical infrastructure protection, safety and security, industrial hygiene, occupational safety and health

  • Emergency Preparedness Director

    Includes but is not limited to: campus security, critical infrastructure protection, risk assessment, Emergency Operations Center development and management, interoperable communications, Continuity of Operations planning, Comprehensive Emergency Management Plan development, and National Incident Management System implementation.

  • Strategic Planning Director

    Indiana Department of Homeland Security

    (Public Safety industry)

    March 2005 — December 2007 (2 years 10 months)

Operational Planning; Project Management; Response Planning; Continuity of Operations and Continuity of Government (COOP/COG); Geographic Information Systems (GIS); Critical Infrastructure Protection (CIP); National Incident Management System (NIMS); Chemical Stockpile Emergency Preparedness Program (CSEPP); Interoperable Communications (both data and voice); Evacuation/Evacuee Planning; Earthquake Program.

  • Assistant Director

    Indiana Counter-Terrorism and Security Council

    (Government Administration industry)

    April 2003 — March 2005 (2 years)

    Homeland security initiatives, projects, policy, funding, and training efforts. Second in command of the three-person team that led and managed homeland security efforts in the State of Indiana prior to the creation of the Indiana Department of Homeland Security. Initiated and conducted the initial stages of research for the project that has come to fruition after the creation of the IDHS as the Indiana Firefighter Training System. One of the founders of the homeland security district system that is currently in use, and participated in the initial stages of the development of what is now the Indiana Intelligence Fusion Center.
