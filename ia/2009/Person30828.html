  • co-Founder

    Cheapnet Ltd (POBOX Internet)

    (Internet industry)

    March 1996 — November 2004 (8 years 9 months)

    Co-Founder of UK's oldest Independent ISP. Responsible for all Sales, Marketing and PR aspects of the company. Also designed the company logo.

    Cheapnet was conceived as a result of an argument taking place over an MSNetmeeting session between the three founders (one being in London, the second in Nottingham, and the third in Leicester) in 1996, on how cheap an internet service could be whilst maintaining high standards

    The name Cheapnet was originally 'The Cheap Network' a shopping exchange, the view was that business would work on the internet if only it was possible to bring potential customers online for as little cost as possible - and so was created the £50.00 per annum dialup access account, which boasted a number of email addresses and unlimited online time (something that was not readily available at the time - nearest pricing being a 'Tenner a month' ).

    Once it became obvious we could attract potential customers to businesses operating on the internet we started looking at the possibility of getting vendors online - the main problem was that most vendors didn't see the value in an online presence. So once again we were challenged with the task of getting a product so cheap that people would take it on without being concerned about the success of their venture. The main problem here being that for a vendor to get online at that time, they were looking at spending in the region of £1000.00 per annum, at least.

    So after designing the technology, and many arguments with the bank about online transactions, we built a fully automated signup service - allowing a customer to signup for an account and (in the case of .uk domain registration) was actually able to post content within 4 minutes. So was introduced the Cheapnet Plus account ('fondly referred to as just the 'Plus Account'). In October 1998 Cheapnet was incorporated.

    Left my full time role to establish e-Banx, however maintained a hands-on role at the company
