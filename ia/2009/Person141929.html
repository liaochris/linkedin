Lisa Christine Fahey’s Summary

After graduating with a BFA in musical theatre and touring with the Equity National tour of "Joseph and the Amazing Technicolor Dreamcoat," I've decided to put my performance career on hold to focus on my true passion -- teaching kids and teens to sing.

My goal is to share the gift of music with my students in an exciting, positive learning environment. Most children, whether they have professional aspirations or not, want to take voice lessons simply because they love to sing. As a teacher, I want to nurture that love and inspire my students to be their best.

Each lesson I teach is tailored to the individual student. I am just as passionate about the seven-year-old who wants to be the next Miley Cyrus as I am about the high school senior auditioning for college musical theatre programs. The differing wants and needs of my students keeps my job challenging and fun!

As I work to improve the technical aspects of the voice -- pitch, intonation, projection, and diction -- my goal is to keep each student’s sound unique. It is important to have idols in the music industry, but imitating can be very dangerous to vocal health. Instead, I like my students to keep their singing voice consistent with the sound of their own speaking voice. I favor a bright, focused, age-appropriate sound that is relevant and lends itself to contemporary musical styles.

In my mind, what makes Lisa Christine Studios special is the attention to performance values. While my primary function as a singing teacher is to improve the overall quality of the voice, I want my students to be a complete package! Good vocalists are a dime a dozen. What audiences crave are performers who understand what they’re singing about; who make us feel the lyrics, not just hear them. I spend a good deal of time staging each song and coaching acting values. I want my young singers to look and feel comfortable in front of any audience or audition panel, in addition to sounding great!
