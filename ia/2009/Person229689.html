Taylor Vogt’s Summary

I am the Founder and President of the Wave of Green, a non-profit environmental advocacy and activist group which operates with a simple principle philosophy: Do whatever you can to help. We are based out of Pace University and do work in Northern Westchester County. Our primary community service activities are community cleanups, where the group will clean up a local town or city. The overarching goal of the group is to provide a fun and easy way for the student body and the communities we work with to come together and help the environment. This simply activity works to proverbially 'shake-off' the apathy that clouts the minds of today's youth regarding the damage we do by littering and polluting every day.

I am an associate member of Pace University's Sustainability Committee. The collective group is made up of committed staff, administration and students who work towards bringing sustainable measures and practices to the University. We have joined the Bloomberg Challenge and have pledged to cut our carbon emissions by 20% by 2015. Our work is very important. I am the only student member of the Waste and Recycling and Energy and Water Conservation subcommittees; as well as a member of the Campus Culture and Academics and Student Stewardship subcommittees.

I have been doing work looking into sustainable practices for local businesses. My current clients are the Crabtree's Kittle House in Chappaqua, New York and Grouchy Gabe's Grill in Croton-on-Hudson, New York.
