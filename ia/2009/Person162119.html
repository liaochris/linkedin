Michael Scalise’s Summary

My life in Broadcasting began in 1980 while I was a starving student at Grossmont College. It was near the end of my term there that I secured an internship at KGTV. My many duties at KGTV included mail room clerk, a brief stint in the business office (how/why??), assignment desk assistant, assistant news photographer, video editor, and various other assignments. You might say it was a well rounded experience.

The next several years had me doing some channel changing of my own...between KGTV and KFMB-TV...working part-time at one and vacation relief at another...all while attending San Diego State University. Some in management said a "conflict of interest"...I said a "monetary opportunity!" In 1988 I took a break from television to work at Voice and Video as a video technician. The break did not last long...and television called again.

Although I was re-hired at KFMB-TV in 1989 as a video editor I have taken on other roles as well. During the past twenty years I have also written, produced, edited, and made a cameo appearance in many entertainment reports. Seeing a project from start to finish is what drives and motivates me to make the next one that much better. While I am an editor by trade it is by no means the limit of my skills.

In the summer of 2001 I started my own video production company, Black Tie Productions in Temecula, CA. Our specialty is wedding and event videography...but we also produce photo and video montages, custom design DVDs, and promotional videos. Located in the heart of Temecula's Wine Country there is never a shortage of beautiful locations to shoot video.
