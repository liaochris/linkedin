Kristina Scott’s Summary

The amazing world of microbes has fascinated me since a child. The thought that something a billionth our size, can find a way to take over one cell; multiply; and decimate the body is captivating to me. I started researching behind the microscope and pursed a degree in microbiology. After working with the Anthrax Vaccine in Michigan, and Kidney failure though the University of Michigan my feet were wet with research, and I wanted the opportunity at the bedside rather than behind the microscope. This is what fueled my nursing degree. I was working happily in an OB department after nursing school. I loved OB predominantly because it was one of the only happy places in a hospital. However, I missed my passion with microbes, infectious dieases or research. I always wanted to end my career in infection control. When I saw there was a position opening up with the government to work specifically with one microbe in infection control and prevention…I jumped on it and was offered the job. I have not looked back since. I would like this position to expand and include all Multi-drug resistant organisms and not focused solely on MRSA.

Future goals: Career: Infection Preventionist/Generalist, Certified in Infection Control, Education: MPH or DNP or MS
Personal: Marriage and babies….at some point
