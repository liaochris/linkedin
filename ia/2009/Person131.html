Amy K. Walsh, CES®’s Summary

Not too many people will admit that they love details, but Amy K. Walsh is proud of her affinity for fine print. She would have done well at the Internal Revenue Service, but she chose to wear a white hat instead: her life's work is helping her clients avoid taxes.

She's carved a niche for herself overseeing 1031 tax-deferred property exchanges. If you own investment or business property that you want to sell - and you plan to buy another property - you should call Amy now. The IRS has very strict rules, and after 10 years in the business, Amy knows them backwards and forwards (it's the fine print thing again).

She helps you figure out how to reinvest most of your profit, while making sure you don't get into trouble with the folks in Washington. And best of all, she'll be glad to do the paperwork for you. Don't think she's here to strike and disappear into the shadows; Amy has built her business on solid relationships, making certain that her clients understand they are her highest priority.

Amy is a Certified Exchange Specialist®, the first one accredited in the Upstate of South Carolina, and one of only two first-ever accredited in the entire state. She is on the governing council for CES®, is a Series 7 and CCIM candidate. Amy has also published articles regarding 1031 exchanges, is a licensed continuing education provider, has authored numerous approved continuing education courses in several states for realtors, brokers, CPAs and attorneys, and speaks around the Southeast spreading her message of enlightenment and deferral. She'll be glad to talk to you or your clients about how you can take advantage of this nifty little area of the tax code, and show you how to make your investment or business property work smarter for you.

So, give her a call if you have a question, want to start an exchange, schedule a seminar or have her speak at your next function, or if you just want a good recipe for fried chicken.
