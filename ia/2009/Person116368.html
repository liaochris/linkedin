Johan Ljung’s Experience

  • Online Program Manager

    Eniro

    (Public Company; Marketing and Advertising industry)

    February 2009 — Present (3 months)

    In my current position at Eniro AB, I act as the chief liaison officer between Eniro Group IT and Business Unit Online (comprising eniro.se, eniro.dk, kvasir.no, gulesider.no, eniro.fi, etc.). I'm a member of both organisations' management teams, reporting to the group CIO. My primary responsibility is to manage the overall IT development plan for Eniro's online services - ensuring that IT understands the business unit's requirements, finding synergies between initiatives, and optimizing the throughput of IT development.

    Eniro is the leading search company in the Nordic media market. Eniro makes it easy to find people, businesses and products using directories, directory assistance, Internet and mobile services. Eniro has operations in Sweden, Norway, Finland, Denmark and Poland.

    Eniro is listed on the Stockholm Stock Exchange and has some 4,650 employees. In 2007, revenues amounted to SEK 6,443 M, with EBITDA of SEK 2,266 M.

  • Project Manager, Product Development

    Eniro

    (Public Company; Marketing and Advertising industry)

    March 2007 — January 2009 (1 year 11 months)

    As project manager at Eniro Sverige Online, I led a couple of high profile product development projects at eniro.se, most notably the launch of "Eniro Super Search" and the integration of Eniro's maps with the leading Swedish real estate listings site, hemnet.se. I also managed the final phase of a major outsourcing project, whereby all of Eniro Sweden's IT operations and support were transferred to HP.

  • Product Manager

    Cision

    (Public Company; Public Relations and Communications industry)

    January 2005 — February 2007 (2 years 2 months)

    My last position at Observer was as the Nordic product manager for "Monitor" - the largest of Observer's five product areas, representing well over half of the company's revenues. As product manager, I was a member of the Nordic Business Development department, focusing comparatively more on product development than on product marketing. The single product I worked most with, was Observer's WebAgent, an appreciated and fast-growing module within the Observer Online platform, now rebranded CisionPoint.

    The Observer Group changed its name to Cision worldwide in April 2007. Cision operates within reputation management and media intelligence. Cision has nearly SEK 1.8 billion in sales and 2,700 employees in the US, UK, Sweden, Canada, Germany, Norway, Finland, Denmark, Portugal and Lithuania.
