Stephanie Henderson’s Summary

Licensed attorney, consulting executive, and business leader with over eight years experience of demonstrated success working in fast-paced competitive environments. I specialize in blending technology tools and candidate skill sets to immediately add value to my clients' unique business goals and work environment. I'm heavily involved in local technology and legal communities including Game HR, Internet Product Management, Disney, CloudRecruiting.net, Int'l Assoc of Software Architects, Washington State Bar Association (WSBA), and Puget Sound Association of Legal Administrators (PSALA).

Prior to joining BluWater, I was the regional Division Director of Robert Half Legal (RHL); awarded #1 in Leadership for top growth of any RHL office world-wide (2006 & 2007). Prior to my time at RHL, I spent three years litigating construction, employment and appellate cases with the Seattle law firm Carney Badley Spellman. A 2002 graduate of Gonzaga Law School, I was privileged to be invited by Chief Justice Gerry Alexander of the WA State Supreme Court to serve as his Senior Law Clerk. During law school, I was honored to be awarded “Best Legal Oralist” in the state-wide law school competition (2001), “Best Brief Writer” for National Moot Court competition (2001) and was four-times awarded the “CALI award” for receiving the top grade in law school. In addition to my work with BluWater, I enjoy a busy volunteer schedule with the WSBA: elected to the WSBA Editorial Advisory Board (2007-2009); elected WSBA Board of Trustees (2004-2005); elected Chair of WSBA CLE Committee (2003-2007); and WSBA Professionalism Committee Member (2002-2007).

Stephanie Henderson’s Specialties:

Strategic planning, sales & contract negotiation, building new teams, technology contracts & projects, staff augmentation. Skill sets I typically work with include project/program managers, iPhone app & Cocoa developers, 3D animators, security & infrastructure professionals, business analysts, quality assurance and testers, software engineers, network engineers, system administrators, and database developers.
