Olga Smith’s Summary

My name is Olga Smith and I graduated from Asbury Theological Seminary in December 2005 with a Master of Arts degree in Counseling. Over the course of the program I completed three internships, two in drug and alcohol addiction counseling and one with children who have emotional, behavioral and learning difficulties.

I graduated summa cum laude from Emory & Henry College in 2002, with a Bachelor of Arts degree in Religion and a minor in Teaching English as a Second Language. While in college, I participated in various activities such as tutoring Hispanic immigrants, volunteering at a local elementary school, working as a tutor at a college language lab, and as an administrator at local church. All these activities helped me develop strong interpersonal, organizational, and administrative skills.

Note: I passed the National Counselor Examination for Certification and Licensure (NCE) on February 10, 2007. I now aspire to gain Licensed Professional Counselor status.
