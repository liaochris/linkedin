  • President, Cleveland Chapter and National VP of Chapter Development

    The National Association of Black Sports Professionals (NABSP)

    (Non-Profit; 11-50 employees; Sports industry)

    January 2006 — Present (3 years 5 months)

    In January 2006 I became the president of the Cleveland Chapter of NABSP: The National Association of Black Sports Professionals. In January 2008, I became the national vice president for chapter development.

    NABSP has been built with a mission of connecting and empowering Black Sports Professionals to positions of influence throughout sports and sports-related industries. The goal of NABSP is to increase the pipeline of diversity within sports.

    In two years, the Cleveland chapter has grown from 15 to over 150 members. We've been able to establish thirteen chapters across the country with fifteen others targeted. We are on our way to creating a national network of Black Sports Professionals.

    I believe NABSP is going to become a very influential entity within sports and a great resource to help expedite diversity. My goal is to become a leading executive within the sports industry and be a model for other African-American sports professionals.
