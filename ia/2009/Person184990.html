Periklis Charchalakis’s Summary

Experienced in vetronics (vehicle electronics), primarily working with middleware for military vetronic systems and networks, such as real-time system design & development and bridging of mixed-criticality heterogeneous networks.

Expertise in electronics and communications, embedded systems, deterministic and real-time systems, hardware and PCB design and manufacturing, embedded software design and development, high performance computing, and computer networking.

Participating in a number of programmes such as:
- MilCAN protocol conformance
- Verification and Validation of Complex Vetronic Systems
- MilCAN middleware for Fault Tolerance integration
- Active Avionics and HUMS Integrated System Architecture
- Vehicle Technology Integration Demonstrator (VTID)

Consultancy work on behalf of QinetiQ Ltd, which involved the development of a MilCAN stack on an embedded platform and working with Thales Air-Defence Ltd (TADL) to operate this platform as a network gateway. The work was part of a Vetronics sub-system within the Electronic Architecture (EA) Technology Demonstrator Programme (TDP) of the Future Rapid Effect System (FRES) programme.

Co-investigator in a number of programmes exceeding £1.2m.

Contributed to 8 refereed journals and conferences, 4 invitations, and 18 confidential reports and briefings to the UK Ministry of Defence and QinetiQ Ltd.

Developmend and teaching of an M-level (MSc) course (Advanced Microprocessor Systems) between 2004 and 2006.

Participating in a Knowledge Transfer Programme (KTP), training on Vehicle Electronics to a Belgium Ministry of Defence representative.
