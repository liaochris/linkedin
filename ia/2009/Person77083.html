Daniel A. Guglielmo’s Summary

For 25 years Dan has served successful families and business owners who possess both the resources and passion to make a difference. Dan began his professional career at the SEC as a Finance Attorney in the Division of Enforcement. As much as he enjoyed learning about the public markets in this position, he discovered that his passion was working for owners of privately held companies. When Dan left the SEC he became a tax attorney working primarily with business owners as their estate planning counsel. In this capacity, Dan earned a reputation as a creative and forward-thinking planner.

In 2001, with his partner Ronald B. Ware, Dan founded TrustDesign, a planning-based wealth management firm, to focus more resources on the problems faced by wealthy families, especially those who own businesses. In this capacity, Dan designs and facilitates the implementation of complex wealth strategy/wealth transition plans, which invariably include business succession plans and strategies to remove and diversify capital from the privately owned business. Often, a core component of Dan's responsibility is to assist in the evaluation and coordination of the team of professionals required by an owner to successfully execute and maintain these complex plans.

In 2008, TrustDesign founded a Boston-based, professional social network called the Exit Planning Exchange (XPX), whose mission is to help coordinate and support the many and various professionals required by a business owner to successfully exit his or her business. In the rapidly emerging Exit Planning field, Dan is considered a national thought-leader.

The Dan’s goal is to continue expanding the influence and deepening the relationships within two significant communities: Owners of closely-held businesses, and the community of advisors with whom these families must rely upon to realize their goals.
