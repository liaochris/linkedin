  • Owner

    RMT Spectrum Associates, Inc.

    (Defense & Space industry)

    2000 — Present (9 years)

    Worldwide use of Unmanned Aircraft Systems (UAS) is expected to increase significantly. Safe operation of UAS requires use of many radio frequencies across the radio spectrum. Unmanned aircraft will need to need to fly in the same areas as piloted aircraft, so there is recognition that there is a critical need to provide globally harmonized spectrum to UAS for that purpose.

    The international spectrum community has placed UAS spectrum issues on the agenda of a World Radio Conference (WRC) in 2011. The WRC tasked the International Telecommunications Union (ITU) to analyze the technical aspects of UAS and other systems operating near each other. The ITU must also find places in the radio spectrum for reliable communication links for the safe flight operation of UAS, including the uplink command and downlink telemetry necessary to control the flight and to relay the air traffic control communications.
