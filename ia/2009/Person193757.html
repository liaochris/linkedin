Randy Shear’s Experience

  • Ideation Manager

    ShearDesigns

    (Design industry)

    March 2009 — Present (7 months)

    Mr. Shear has established teams of talented people/thinkers from urban planners to designers, who are assembled into a 'super team' that work collaboratively with clients to acheive 'big idea' solutions for projects worldwide.

  • Senior Design Lead

    RTKL

    (Privately Held; Architecture & Planning industry)

    April 2003 — March 2009 (6 years)

    Long recognized as a creator of ‘one of a kind’ iconic designs, Mr. Shear most recently served as served as a senior designer for the commercial sector at RTKL Associates Inc. in Dallas Texas where he was charged with producing high quality hospitality and entertainment projects for the global market.

    In 2008, Mr. Shear’s design for a convention hotel in Tucson was shortlisted from a 114 projects submitted at the annual AIA Dallas awards, and he was published in RTKL’s annual world-wide “WIP (Works In Progress)” journal, juried by his peers and chosen among hundreds of entry’s.

    In 2007 he contrubited to a project for Parcel 03 of the Coati Strip in Macau China winning the commision in a closed competition with KPF architectural firm and received an AIA ‘Jury Accommodation’ award.

    Later in 2007, he collaborated with the Shanghai office and was senior designer for a signature office/hotel tower at a major crossroads in Tianjin, China.

  • Senior Designer

    HKS

    (Privately Held; Architecture & Planning industry)

    2002 — 2005 (3 years)

    In 2004, he worked directly with legendary team owner Lamar
    Hunt forming a design for Pizza Hut Park, a multi-purpose facility featuring a 20,500-seat stadium built and owned by the suburb of Frisco in Collin County, Texas. This stadium, the third stand-alone MLS stadium built in the United States, set a new standard for all league stadiums to follow by having a ‘U’ shaped bowl that incorporated a permanent concert stage to broaden fan entertainment options and expand revenue opportunities.

    In 2005 he produced the winning design for the Lucas Oil Dome, a 64,000 seat stadium for the Colts in Indianapolis, Indiana. This design was chosen over proposals from two other notable sport facility design firms. Mr. Shear subsequently steered his winning
    design through to contact documents. A signature design element of the 1.8 million square foot stadium is its unique roof design that breaks from common usage by having two panels move to the sides of the stadium, creating an unusual sense of openness when retraqcted. A second special feature is a huge 80-foot window to the city that provides a seating bowl to view out toward downtown Indianapolis.

    The building was highlighted on the cover of HKS in-house publication ‘Innovations’, which is distributed to the firm’s clients, prospects, and consultants but later went on to received a McGraw Hill ‘Best of 2008’ award for construction innovation and in 2009 the prestigious Sports Facility of the Year by Street and Smith’s Sports Business Journal.

  • Big Idea Guy

    Populous formally HOK Sport SVE

    (Architecture & Planning industry)

    1996 — 2003 (7 years)

    From 1996 to 2003, Mr. Shear worked in Kansas City with industry giant HOK Sport Venue Event, specializing in sport and entertainment complexes word-wide. He was instrumental in giving form to some of country’s most notable MLB ballparks, including both Pac Bell Park (now AT&T Park) in San Francisco and the Great America Ballpark in Cincinnati.

    In 2000, Mr. Shear began working with the firm’s then-new Design Marketing Group (DMG), a ‘think tank’ studio which worked directly with clients and consultants to study physical design at the leading edge of industry thought on revenue generation, fan experience, and technology application. During his time as the longest running member of the DMG at HOK, Mr. Shear worked directly with Rod Sheard to produce a design for the New Jersey Metrostars stadium, the stadiums main features was its affordability using a kit of standard parts, simple industrial aesthics and a roof that could be expandable as to fully enclose the stadium in the future.

  • Senior Designer, Senior Associate Position

    HOK Sport

    (Architecture & Planning industry)

    1995 — 2002 (7 years)

    From 1996 to 2003 Mr. Shear worked in Kansas City with industry giant HOK Sport Venue Event, specializing in sport and entertainment complexes word-wide. He was instrumental in giving form to some of country’s most notable MLB ballparks, including both Pac Bell Park (now AT&T Park) in San Francisco and the Great America Ballpark in Cincinnati. In honor its versatility, innovation and beauty, AT&T Park was recently chosen as the 2008 Sports Facility of the Year by Sports Business Journal and Sports Business Daily as part of the inaugural Sports Business Awards
    program.

    In 2000, Mr. Shear began working with the firm’s then-newly created Design Marketing Group (DMG), a ‘think tank’

An original member of the group, and by 2003 DMG’s longest serving member, the group was charged with the initial design from the Great American Ballpark to the design for The Church of the Ressurection in Leawood Kansas.

  • Senior Designer

    Loebl Schlossman & Hackl

    (Privately Held; Architecture & Planning industry)

    1985 — 1995 (10 years)

    Following completion of his post graduate education, Mr. Shear worked in Chicago at Loebl Schlossman Hackl under
    the supervision of Donald Hackl.

    While there he was involved in several mixed-use developments in China including a closed competition for a 64-story AVIC tower and mixed use complex in Shenzhen, which was chosen in a closed
    competition.

    He was on the design teams of two built mixed-use projects in Chicago ‘City Place’ including a 40-story
    tower that included an Omni Hotel at was sited within the famed ‘One Magnificent Mile , and 633 St. Clair, originally
    completed as an office building but later converted to a Wyndham Hotel. He worked on several retail project, the historical renovation of the base to One North State, a project that matched reinforce concrete panels to original terra cotta to created an urban gateway together with the Carson Prairie Scott Building and Westfield Old Orchard Mall which is now the third largest mall in the state of Illinois. The renovation in 1995 redeveloped the plans to include
    anchor the stores Bloomingdale's, Macy's (formerly Marshall Field's), Lord & Taylor and Nordstrom The mall is well
    known as one of the few originally designed open-air malls in the country.
