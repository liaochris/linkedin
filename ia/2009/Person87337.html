George Kallis’s Summary

Lead by film composer George Kallis CINEMATIC SOUNDSCAPES is a dynamic music house specializing in scoring original music for feature films / TV & video games. George's music possesses a combination of several stylistic influences, which are adapted perfectly for each production. In his film scores, he particularly enjoys using unusual instrumentation, whilst still keeping a commercial and individualistic appeal. His style can be described as warm, melodic and distinctive but also of moments of explosive, dramatic power.

Bio & Credits

George has recently delivered the music for the feature film Highlander The Source (LionsGate 2008), directed by Brett Leonard (Virtuosity, The Lawnmower Man) with the score receiving much acclaim by various soundtrack critiques. He has just finished scoring the new romantic comedy Right Hand Drive (Pineroad Films)

Previously, he composed the score for the WWII drama Joy Division, directed by Reg Traviss, which is out now on worldwide DVD release, following it's theatrical release in early 2007. George also orchestrated the music for Ultimate Avengers II and Dr Strange both produced by Marvel. Other past credits include the title music for the BBC2 documentary series Horizon as well as The Travel Channel series It Happened Here, produced by Principal Films. In addition, he has just finished composing music tracks for ITV's music library and KPM.

George has worked his way up by composing music for commercials, multimedia and theatre. He has been published by BMG, EMI and Galaxy Records, while his first personal album (Where There's a Child) became gold. He has won many songwriting awards and also composed the Cyprus entry for the Eurovision Song Contest in 1999.

George earned a music scholarship of outstanding talent at the well-renowned Berklee College of Music where he completed a dual BM in Music Composition degree . In 2002 he followed it up with a MMus in Composition degree at the Royal College of Music in London
