Shannon Austin’s Summary

Senior-level marketing professional with expertise in online product development, branding, email marketing, advertising copywriting, internet recruitment strategies, and more. Successful "Type A" entrepreneur with utility infielder tendencies. Strong background in marketing to and for financial institutions with assets of $300 million plus, including some of the largest U.S. banks (NationsBank/Bank of America, Wachovia, Bank One, Regions Financial Corp., SunTrust, among others).

Shannon Austin’s Specialties:

branding, identity, marketing, internet marketing, email marketing, negotiation, print production, copywriting, news writing, publicity, promotion, non-profit media and publicity management, internet recruitment strategies, online advertising planning, direct mail, executive recruiting/headhunting.

Shannon Austin’s Experience

  • President/CEO/Co-Founder

    Bankjobs.com

    (Marketing and Advertising industry)

    1996 — Present (13 years)

    I started this company with the former Chairman of Private Business, Inc. back in 1996, and have been in and out of involvement with it since that time. Bankjobs.com was the nation's first online job board for the banking and financial services industry, and over the years has served as an "electronic outplacement" and "online recruiting" resource to many of our country's largest banks (Citigroup, NationsBank/Bank of America, Wachovia, Regions Financial Corp., SunTrust, Bank One, Wells Fargo, PNC to name just a few). We also serve as an important resource to independent and corporate recruiters across the U.S. -- we are often their secret weapon when it comes to finding and generating amazing resumes for their available positions.

  • Media and Publicity Director

    Chicago Filmmakers

    (Motion Pictures and Film industry)

    2005 — 2006 (1 year)

    While living in Chicago, I worked for non-profit Chicago Filmmakers as Media and Publicity Director for one of the nation's oldest film festivals. Key sponsors included Showtime, Comcast and Orbitz, and I worked with established sponsors while also pitching new sponsorships, writing and managing e-blast campaigns, and obtaining publicity from all relevant Chicago media outlets, as well as serving as liaison with government offices in the city regarding the festival.

  • Marketing Consultant

    StrategyCorps

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Marketing and Advertising industry)

    2002 — 2005 (3 years)

    StrategyCorps works with community banks across the U.S. on the retail side of the bank to generate recurring fee income for banks through well-marketed checking programs for consumers. This company was founded by two of the original founders of Private Business, Inc.

  • Vice President, Marketing

    Private Business, Inc. (NASDAQ: PBIZ)

    (Public Company; 201-500 employees; GFSI; Financial Services industry)

    1995 — 2001 (6 years)

    Private Business, Inc. worked with community banks across the U.S. on the commercial lending side. The company offered a software program called "Business Manager," which enabled community banks to purchase the outstanding accounts receivable of small businesses on an ongoing basis with the benefit of an interest earning reserve account to mitigate risk, empowering the businesses with the benefit of a shorter receivables cycle, so their businesses could grow. We worked with several thousand banks, and many thousands of businesses, with this commercial product. Various positions held, including Director of Corporate Communications, then VP of Marketing, Internet Services, and also Director of Investor Relations for company's IPO.

  • Production Assistant

    Independent Projects

    (Entertainment industry)

    1994 — 1995 (1 year)

    I served as freelance Production Assistant for the documentary "True Love Waits" by Director Witold Starecki. Starecki is a Polish film director perhaps best known for his many BBC documentaries, and this film was funded by BBC and later shown on HBO in the United States. Filming took place in Nashville, TN with an international crew. (I took a leave of absence from my Advertising Agency job to work on this film.)

  • Senior Copywriter

    Burroughs & Associates Advertising

    (Marketing and Advertising industry)

    1992 — 1994 (2 years)

    Wrote print ad copy for this ad agency (now defunct) which specialized in the automotive industry. Ads appeared in Car & Driver, AutoWeek, and MotorTrend, among others.

  • Editorial Assistant/Contributing Writer

    Nashville Scene

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Newspapers industry)

    1991 — 1992 (1 year)

    First job after college. Served as editorial assistant and contributing writer. I wrote a bunch of cover stories during my two years at this alternative newsweekly, primarily on the "danger" beat because those were the stories no one else wanted to do -- riding Harleys with outlaw bikers, going back to high school undercover, spending 72 hours in a wilderness program for troubled youth. Beyond that, my job was to do anything asked of me, from copyediting/proofing pages of entertainment listings, to writing a dining guide, to making coffee or picking up lunch. Great friendships were made, and tremendous experience was gained (notably, I had second and third jobs to support myself while holding down this one, which paid only slightly more than minimum wage).
