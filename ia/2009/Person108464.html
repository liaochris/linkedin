Jeffrey Gene Kaplan, MD, MS’s Summary

JEFFREY GENE KAPLAN, M.D., M.S., C.M.C.M

Dr. Kaplan is a Board-certified pediatrician, teaching and practicing in a multispecialty, medically underserved ultra-orthodox community health center in the Hudson Valley of New York, at Pace University, and at New York Medical College and Westchester Medical Center (Valhalla, NY).

Post-residency (1975) as Assistant Chief-resident physician, the Children's Hospital of Philadelphia, he served as Lieut. Commander in the U.S. Public Health Service, National Health Services Corp. and was stationed near Windsor, Vt. where he also taught at Dartmouth. From there, Dr. Kaplan, having been recruited by Dr. Frank Oski (then Chairman of the Pediatrics Department of SUNY Upstate Medical Center and later, Johns Hopkins) returned to Syracuse, NY. For the next 19 year, he was the Corporate Medical Director of Health Services Medical Corp./ Health Services Association helping build a series of successful community health centers, wellness programs, group practices, HMOs and IPAs in upstate New York.

Subsequently, Dr. Kaplan was appointed to be Travelers' National V.P. for Research Development and Utilization Management. Later, he became Corporate Medical Director of Blue Cross and Blue Shield of Western New York and then, shortly before returning to practice full time and after over three decades in medical management, he joined the now defunct New Alliance Health Plan in Erie, PA as their Chief Medical Information Officer.
