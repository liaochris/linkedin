Demba Diallo’s Summary

Demba Diallo is Partner with Innhotep, a fast growing professional service firm dedicated to innovation (www.innhotep.com/en/).

For over 11 years, Demba has advised top level managers in a large set of business lines. He is particularly recognized for his strong expertise in Energy and Utilities as well as in innovation development. Demba has demonstrated a distinctive ability to impact clients and has recently taken on a leadership position within his various activities as an entrepreneur and as a Director within Colombus. Prior to joining Colombus, Demba worked as a financial auditor for Arthur Andersen and as a senior consultant for Cap Gemini Ernst & Young Consulting Services.

Alongside his professional career, Demba earned a PhD in Innovation and IT Economics from Telecom Paris, France‘s reference institution in the field of information technology. As part of his research, Demba has significantly contributed to the area of innovation cluster development strategies in a context of globalization and knowledge based economy. A key focus of his work has been to analyze the linkages between clusters, ICTs and economic performance. His research includes a deep analysis of the performance of six world renknown successful IT clusters: the Silicon Valley, Bangalore, Kuala Lumpur-Cyberjaya-Putrajaya (Malaysia), Singapour, Helsinki and Stockholm. Demba was a visitng researcher at MIT, Harvard, UC Berkeley and Stanford. Demba is graduated from "Sciences Po Paris"- Institut d’Etudes Politiques de Paris ("eco-fi" concentration) (eq.to Master in Economics and Finance).

Combining both academic mastery and professional expertise, Demba is a regular panel speaker in conferences in France and abroad, where he shares his passion for innovation technology and economic performance for firms and territories.

Demba Diallo’s Education

  • Ecole nationale supérieure des Télécommunications

    PHD , Economics , 2001 — 2006

    Abstract of my PHD dissertation :
    In the recent years, a growing number of country and cities have wished to develop or emulate a model along the lines of Silicon Valley. In the same time, the current globalisation process has gradually but critically shifted the focus away from the national level towards the regions and the world as the minimum level playing field. The development and diffusion of Information and Communication Technologies has emphasised the key role that innovative industrial clusters play as engines economic competitiveness.
    The approach of the dissertation is to present the results of an intense and original field research I made in the following clusters: Bangalore-India, Singapore, Kuala Lumpur, Putrajaya and Cyberjaya-Malaysia, Helsinki-Finland, and Stockholm-Sweden. The purpose here is to analyze the key linkages between clusters, ICTs and economic performance. Towards this goal, interviews involving a hundred key-actors of each cluster were conducted.
