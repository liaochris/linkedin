Geppy Parziale’s Summary

Geppy Parziale received the academic title of Dottore in Ingegneria Electronica from the Polytechnic of Bari (Italy) in 2000. He majored in Electronics and Computing Systems with a dissertation on Computer Vision and Image Processing.
In 2000, he began working as IC Digital Designer for Infineon Technologies in Graz (Austria). He was mainly involved in the development of a CMOS fingerprint sensor. His main responsibilities were: digital design, IC synthesis, time analysis and testing. He was also involved in the development of a flash memory for a smart card controller.
In 2002, he moved to the Institute of Digital Image Processing within Joanneum Research (Graz). He was involved in the R&D of image processing algorithms for real-time industrial inspection, quality control, landscape analysis and video surveillance. He founded and leaded the Biometric Solutions Group and he specialized his research activities in fingerprint recognition. He leaded a work-package on Biometric Systems for BioSecure a large European NoE, in which he was involved with the major European biometric experts.
In 2006, he moved to Zurich (Switzerland) where his experience in the field of biometrics was fundamental for TBS Holding AG, a small start-up company nearly in the bankrupt. He worked as CTO and reorganized the structure of the company, the product line and road map and the business and financial plan. He leaded an R&D project for the US NIJ to develop an innovative touchless fingerprint device. He closely worked with FBI, DHS, NIJ, DoD. He was invited as speaker in many important events.
In 2007, he helped some Spanish companies to enter the biometric market. He was hired as Sales Manager by Cogent Inc, the leading provider of biometric identification systems and he was responsible for the deployment of an Automated Fingerprint and Palmprint Identification System for the Spanish Ministry of Interior.
