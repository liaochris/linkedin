Adam King’s Experience

  • Principal & Director

    Unnormal · Branding & Media

    (Marketing and Advertising industry)

    July 2003 — Present (5 years 7 months)

    Communications management and design consultancy based in Brantford, Ontario, specializing in web media, campaign management, and brand strategy, serving a variety of not-for-profit and for-profit clients.

  • Council Appointee

    City of Brantford - Brantford Cultural Network

    (Government Agency; Government Administration industry)

    2006 — 2008 (2 years)

    Sat on board responsible for developing policy and advising city council on all matters impacting cultural and arts issues in the city. Internally appointed as coordinator for the Artist Community Development Subcommittee, which organizes events series, for instance, “Revitalizing Cities Through Culture” with Glen Murray.

  • Council Appointee

    City of Brantford - Heritage Committee, Planning Dept.

    (Government Agency; Government Administration industry)

    2006 — 2008 (2 years)

    Initiated a process to increase organizational efficiency by creating a subcommittee structure for delegation of the committee’s onerous responsibilities. Was instrumental in creating policy advocacy tools.. In general, was responsible for advising city council on built fabric design concerns and quality control on heritage revitalization projects, as well as ensuring new development in city core areas complemented city planning and policy, which requires interaction with architects, planners, developers, politicians.

  • Co-Founder, Co-Director (Part-time)

    Ethical Coffee Chain

    (International Trade and Development industry)

    2007 — 2008 (1 year)

    Still in startup planning and research phase, this social enterprise will match third world coffee farmers with North American markets. Proceeds from sales will go toward community projects in coffee farming communities. Traveled to Nicaragua in early 2008 to do an educational film and ground research. Now developing communications campaign and operations plans.

  • Co-founder

    Students For Good Governance/Unis pour une saine gestion

    (Political Organization industry)

    2004 — 2007 (3 years)

    Started a bilingual grassroots campaign to expose fiduciary and constitutional crimes by student union government at York University which inadvertently brought about a $2.7 million lawsuit against administration, which was then up-ended through our efforts, also resulting in impeachment of Union President and criminal charges for embezzlement. Following this, we organized a slate of responsible union candidates and created a campaign to restructure the following government.

  • President & CEO

    Brant Federal Green Party Association

    (Non-Profit; Political Organization industry)

    November 2006 — June 2007 (8 months)

    Saw the association completely transform with large growth in membership, activity, and organization during my brief stint as CEO, with various events and fundraisers organized, including a high-profile nomination contest which saw record attendance, media coverage, and high-caliber candidates (including a former township councillor and a two-time former MP). Established a brand and local recognition for the association in it's own right.

  • Ward 5 Candidate

    City of Brantford Municipal Elections

    (Government Relations industry)

    September 2006 — October 2006 (2 months)

    Ran whirlwind, last-minute campaign for Brantford City Council, producing a newspaper and DVD for campaign materials, coordinating large teams of volunteers to distribute 10,000 newspapers in space of 3 weeks, and being endorsed by an outgoing incumbent. In the end came in 15% short of being elected.

    John Tory (of the Ontario PC Party) after reading the campaign materials said via email, "One of the best local election brochures I have seen. I would like to have an opportunity to meet Adam sometime... he is clearly very impressive."

  • Parliamentary Candidate

    Green Party of Canada

    (Non-Profit; Political Organization industry)

    July 2005 — February 2006 (8 months)

    Ran for Canadian federal parliament as candidate for the Green Party of Canada in Brant; held in high acclaim by local media throughout campaign. Participated in TV and Town Hall debates, saw unprecedented coverage, fundraising levels and community support, and orchestrated what was by far the largest Green campaign in Brant to date.
