Jeff Goldman’s Summary

I believe:
1) Customers are your life's blood and should be referred to in that way.
They are not users.
2) Software should solve a problem and make someone's life better.
If it does not it serves no purpose.
3) Web applications are not monuments, they are temporary structures to facilitate a task and serve a common goal.
4) As a developer who takes stewardship of an existing application it is important to learn the way that the prior developer thought.
I try to get inside that persons head so that you can match or mimic the coding style in the application.
I always assume that the developer who proceeded me was smarter and had a reason for what they did.
I go into the situation expecting to learn something cool, not to place my mark and refactor.
Once I have come to that understanding with the code I may suggest changes where I think performance or usability can be enhanced.
5) Bugs need to eradicated.
Software needs to run cleanly.
6) There is always a way to improve what I do.
There is also a point of diminishing returns that must be respected on any given task.
But tomorrow is an other day..

I like to work with creative people who strive for a common (yet uncommon) goal.
