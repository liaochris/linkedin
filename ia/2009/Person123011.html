  • International School of Mathematics

    postgrado, Free boundary problems in mathematics and industry, 1995 — 1995

    This year's school was devoted to the theory and practice of Free Boundary Problems. The school dealt with some mathematical problems of free boundaries and their applications to industrial problems. This field has matured enormously in the last 20 years, both in the width of its applications and depth of its mathematics. The courses were designed to introduce a number of the most fundamental topics to young research candidates in applied mathematics. The main topics covered were: Stefan problems, nonlinear filtration problems, variational inequalities, mean curvature flows and Hele-Shaw cells. The school tried to combine a genuine interest in the applied parts of the science of free boundaries with a strong emphasis on the mathematical development. It has been co-sponsored by the Programme of the European Science Foundation.
