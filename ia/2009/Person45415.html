Chris Hansen’s Summary

I've always felt strongly that good writing is a commodity in the business world. That rings truer than ever in an environment where so much of business today is represented online. From Web site content to marketing copy to blogs, businesses strive every day to increase their presence and prominence in the cacophony that is the World Wide Web.

I didn't start out with that Web-centric view, but it has come very much into focus for me—as it has for so many—in the past several years. I began my career as a freelance writer for publications within the ski and snowboard industry (Transworld Snowboarding, Snowboarder, Powder) and followed my bliss to a small mountain town where I sought a desired way of life first and a career second. Along the way I've learned how to tailor the written word for many aspects of the business world. And in business in a small town, one is often forced to fill many roles.

Tourism marketing, non-profit arts communications and marketing, public relations, editorial management, blogging and blog management, and online product marketing. These are all competencies that have and continue to forge my career direction.

And by the way, that small town is Jackson, Wyoming—aka Jackson Hole—and not Pocatello, as LinkedIn has forced in my profile. It's a small town environment with a real-world self-awareness that continues to draw as many experienced professionals as ski bums (so often one in the same).

Where to next? That's like asking where the Internet is going next. For now I've started a blog called MarketGreener. The focus is on green marketing and green business practices. It has a local bent but I also take a look at what's happening in the wider world for perspective and to help local businesses capitalize on global practices. Check it out at marketgreener.com.

Thanks for taking the time to read my profile. Help me build my network by linking to me, and maybe we'll have an opportunity to work together in the future.
