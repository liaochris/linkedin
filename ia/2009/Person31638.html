Bradley Ericson’s Summary

Bradley is an aspiring entrepreneur and zealous undergraduate student, majoring in Business Administration and dual-concentrating in Marketing and Entrepreneurship at Drexel University. Not only that, Bradley is ranked as one of the top 5 College Entrepreneurs in the nation, by Entrepreneur Magazine. He co-founded 3SecondReceipts LLC with his mentor and older brother, Timothy Ericson. 3SecondReceipts aims to solve the inconviences caused by paper receipts by developing a solution that enables digital receipts. Bradley expects nothing less than perfection and a positive attitude in his academic, athletic and professional endeavors. Bradley lives by the famous quote by Emmerson, “Nothing great was ever done without enthusiasm.” Aside from his professional endeavors, Bradley is one of the top business students at Drexel University and an avid golfer, field hockey player and fencer.

Bradley Ericson’s Experience

  • CEO and Co-Founder

    3SecondReceipts LLC

    (Consumer Services industry)

    May 2009 — Present (3 months)

    3SecondReceipts offers a simple software that enables the use of a digital receipt. A consumer can simply walk into any retailer employing our software, scan a bar-coded membership card and later access all their itemized receipts on one central website.

    By replacing paper receipts with digital receipts, retailers will save billions of dollars in paper and printer costs, decrease delays at the point of sale, and most importanly consumers will no longer have to deal with unmanageble paper receipts. The production of paper receipts cause the destruction of 400 million trees, the use of 10 billion gallons of oil, 500 billion gallons of water and 30 million tons of paper waste.

    There have been efforts for digital receipts in the past however 3SecondReceipts is the first company to have a sustainable business model: we will earn revenues from the sale of aggregate consumer data to online retailers such as Amazon. Amazon has as much as 40% of their revenues tied directly to recommendations.
