Edward Hopkins’s Summary

MANTRA

Be necessary.

SNAPSHOT

U.S. Air Force Academy alumnus, veteran, entrepreneur, and future lawyer with accounting and finance expertise; international business operations and startup experience; an ENTP personality type; and an abundance mentality.

NARRATIVE

Before founding Criterion LLC, a defense industry-focused firm, Ed had been a U.S. Air Force Finance Officer for 6 years and a U.S. Air Force Academy Cadet for 4 years. Long workweeks were customary. After working 70 to 90 hours per week as Criterion's leader for 3.5 profitable years, he retreated from what had become a lucrative but imbalanced lifestyle. In 2004, Criterion completed its final engagement.

During the next 3 years, Ed spent his days investing in and working with community organizations and small businesses. He also took a few dozen university courses and read hundreds of books, obtaining a princely liberal arts education based on the Western Canon.

This enriching, low-stress lifestyle enabled him to be fully present, physically and emotionally, for his family. He started and finished many good conversations with his son and daughter, shared many cups of coffee with friends, and took his wife out on many more dates.

Once his son and daughter had left for universities in 2007, Ed began law school. He'll graduate in May 2010.

NEXT MOVE

No later than August 24, 2009, Ed will select the city in and law firm with which he'll begin practicing law next year.
