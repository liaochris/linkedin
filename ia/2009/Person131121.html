Kevin Maistros’s Summary

Having been immersed in film making since childhood, Kevin Maistros has developed a name for himself as a bright new talent, with drive and innovation to spare. Getting his start in the world of directing early, as a young professional skater, Kevin began shooting skating videos at various extreme sports events at the age of 14. Under the guidance of Nigel Dick, Kevin began directing music videos for unsigned bands in Cleveland, OH.

He continued to travel around the country for the next 7 years, making many videos for local bands, often on a very limited budget, allowing for the development of his own personal approach to directing. It was this sort of innovative self-sufficiency and boldness that make Kevin’s style and vision completely unique to the music video industry.

Kevin holds that his vision as a director extends far past the music video itself, and into the bands and labels he works with. He steps into the ring with his videos keeping in mind the image of the band and the plan of the label, combining his ground-breaking film techniques with the image of the bands he works with, making the video a vital piece in the musical puzzle of a band. With regards to style, he stays on the edge and stretches boundaries, often trying new technical ideas or production processes that have not been attempted before. He is most known for using the latest technology in filmmaking, or even inventing obscure solutions to creating the visual extension of his vision.

Aside from the usual music video outlets, his music videos have screened at several film festivals and music conferences. He continues to be active in the online filmmaker's community. Kevin is the founder of the MySpace Film Makers group, boasting over 30,000 active filmmakers. He is helping to build the Film Community on DeviantART, an international online art community with over 7 million active members, as the Film Gallery Director.
