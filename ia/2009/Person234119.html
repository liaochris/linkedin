  • Multimedia Developer

    EMBARQ

    (Public Company; EQ; Telecommunications industry)

    January 2006 — November 2008 (2 years 11 months)

    Turnkey video/audio/Flash/graphics/web producer for internal training organization at EMBARQ – an organization of approximately 150 people, serving over 15,000 employees.

    * Completed over thirty short videos, capturing various work-related ethical dynamics.
    * Produced OSHA-approved training videos based on subjects including aerial lift trucks, working with electricity, working in confined spaces, and more.
    * Created Flash-based training courses and interactions outlining products & services, company ethics, employee diversity, and new employee orientation.
    * Instigated & implemented an organization-wide social network including podcasts, blogs, and document sharing.
    * Conceived & managed the company's media library.
    * Designed & directed the installation of a large-scale corporate art piece.
    * Led discussion sessions on creativity, innovation, and new media.
    * Documented and roadmapped a new Brand identity for the training organization.
    * Consulted nearly every organization within the company (as well as some outside vendors) on various multimedia-oriented issues.
    * Submitted two user-facing technologies for patent.

  • Marketing Manager

    (Electrical/Electronic Manufacturing industry)

    * Created the company's brand identity. Designed & implemented their web/eCommerce presence, which contained thousands of products from a variety of different manufacturers. The web sales garnered much acclaim from the electronics industry and went on to be a major avenue of sales for the company.

  • Multimedia Instructor

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    1998 — 2000 (2 years)

    * Taught classes on a variety of subjects, including the history of multimedia, Flash, Photoshop, and other digital media design applications. Each semester ended with team-created prototypes for websites and other multimedia-oriented projects. The teams were required to present their ideas in front of a panel of industry professionals, who would then judge and critique the students' work based on a list of real-world criteria.

  • Producer

    (Broadcast Media industry)

    1992 — 1999 (7 years)

    * Managed teams and worked independently on over 40 software titles ranging from screen savers and desktop customization utilities to interactive CD-ROMs for children and full-fledged console games – mostly based on major Hollywood titles.

    * Collaborated with programmers to assure that interpretation from concept to final version was maintained.
    * Researched hardware and software options, middleware technologies, and Q/A processes for the creation of user interfaces and fulfillment of user experience and end-user requirements.
    * Led several Q/A cycles for various game titles that received acclaim and helped to launch the popularity of “Edutainment.”
