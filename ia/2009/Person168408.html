Moti Weizman’s Summary

During my diverse career, I held senior technical and managerial positions in leading companies such as MRV communications, Zoran microelectronics, Dune networks and others. I was responsible for the development of pioneering FPGA based products such as the world’s first 100Mb FDDI, a breakthrough motion JPEG video decompression and full featured 40 Gigabit network processor, all in a FPGA.

Moti Weizman’s Specialties:

Video: Jpeg, mpeg2, DVI, HDMI, SMPTE, VoIP
High-Speed Interfaces: Serdes, LVDS, Ethernet, XAUI/10GbE, PCIe, PCI, SPI4.2, SPI-S, Fiber channel, DDR controllers (DDR1/2/3 SDRAM, RLDRAM, QDR), TCAM

Moti Weizman’s Experience

  • CEO & Founder

    Allowtech

    (Semiconductors industry)

    2005 — Present (4 years)

    Allowtech's mission is to accelerate time to market by streamlining FPGA development time and cost.

    AllowTech services includes:
    * System architecture
    * Component selection
    * FPGA/CPLD Design and RTL coding (Verilog & VHDL)
    * Firmware and Software drivers
    * Design verification including test bench design and development
    * System integration and troubleshooting
    * Comprehensive documentation and training

    Allowtech has over a hundred commercially deployed products including complex, high speed (over 200Mhz), large scale projects. We leverage the latest cutting-edge technology to produce optimized, cost-effective solutions. With our system architecture capabilities and high-speed timing expertise, we can shrink an existing system to a single FPGA while achieving optimized fit and timing closure.

  • Co-Founder & COO

    Dune Networks

    (Privately Held; Semiconductors industry)

    2000 — 2005 (5 years)

    Participated in defining the company technology and business plan that led to a 24M financing round from major VC’s in year 2000

    Contributed to the definition and design of its high-end scalable switching and routing ASIC based products.

  • GM, Communication division

    MRV Communications

    (Public Company; MRVC; Telecommunications industry)

    1993 — 2000 (7 years)

    Designed Nbase first gigabit speeds router while demonstrating deep knowledge in system, hardware, software, ASIC design and testing.

    Developed the first gigabit Ethernet interface in the world and made a technical contribution to the gigabit Ethernet standard definition.

    Lead the definition and design of the first economical 10/100 switch in the world, that lead to many OEM relationships including Intel and DEC. This product boosted Nbase sales from several $M to $100M and positioned MRV as a technology leader

  • HW Design Manager

    Zoran

    (Public Company; ZRAN; Semiconductors industry)

    1991 — 1993 (2 years)
