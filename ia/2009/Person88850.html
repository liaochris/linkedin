Graham Coates’s Summary

Antal is the leader in EMEA & Asia Executive Search & Selection with a network of over 58 fully-staffed offices in 30 countries, identifying talent in a range of industry sectors & skill functions. Since inception in 1993 Antal has placed executives in over 75 countries and offers clients a choice of selection & staffing solutions from Retained Executive Search, Search & Selection to Contingent Selection in Permanent, Interim & Contract / Temporary staffing.

Antal's executive headhunters combine their industry knowledge, search skills, proven headhunting & selection methodologies with experience of your industry to deliver a truly value added service. This approach ensures Human Resources Managers, Hiring Managers & Candidates all deal with specialists who know the talent market, the top performers in it and importantly, understand your business. Your Antal Headhunter actively shares information, knowledge & experience across our global network adding extensive cross sector and multi-geographic reach when identifying with the efficiency of a single contact point.

Antal's global network has physical offices in UK (London HQ + 10 UK offices), Belgium, Bulgaria, China (Beijing, Shanghai & Hong Kong), Croatia, Czech Republic, Egypt, France, Germany, Ghana, Hungary, India, Italy (2), Luxembourg, Netherlands, Nigeria, Malta, Philippines, Poland, Romania (6), Russia (Moscow, St Petersburg, Ekaterinburg), Spain (Madrid & Barcelona), South Africa, Switzerland, Turkey (3), UAE/Dubai.

In each country sector-focused specialists are aligned to a discipline, enabling us to deliver seamless solutions in Sales, Marketing, HR/Human Resources, Accountancy & Finance, Banking & Law/ Legal, Information Technology, IT Technical, Engineering, Logistics, Operations, Construction, Supply Chain, Consulting, General Management, Healthcare & Public Sector.

Graham Coates’s Specialties:

The headhunting business shares many similarities with the retail industry. Both are highly competitive, fast-paced and customer-centric in its demands.
