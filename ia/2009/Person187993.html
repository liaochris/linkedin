  • Visiting Scholar

    University of California, Berkeley

    (Research industry)

    April 2008 — July 2008 (4 months)

    For my bachelor assignment I am running a research project in material science. Prof. Ramesh is in both the department of Physics and the department of Material Science and Engineering.

    The goal is to explore the consequences of hole doping in BiFeO3. I will perform a systematic study on structural, electric, and magnetic properties for nominally hole-doped BiFeO3. This will be obtained by partially substituting the Bi3+ ions by divalent ions (such as Ca2+). The divalent ion doping will weaken the ferroelectric properties but will increase the magnetization, hopefully leading to an improvement in the magnetoelectric coupling.

    During this study, I will grow high-quality thin films of doped BiFeO3 by pulsed laser deposition (PLD). Further, the structural characterization will be performed by X-ray diffraction, scanning microscopy and transmission electron microscopy, while the magnetic and electronic properties will be investigated as a function of magnetic field and temperature.
