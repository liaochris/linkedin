Gary Laguna’s Summary

Gary Laguna is a computer scientist at Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory, in the Science and Technology Computing Division (STC), where he is the group leader for the Environmental Remediation And Monitoring Support (ERAMS) group. His interests include web-based applications, systems integration, visualization, and GIS. Gary currently supports a variety of projects for the Environmental Protection and Environmental Restoration Departments, including environmental monitoring and restoration, real-time radiation monitoring, heat-stress awareness, and meteorology applications.

Gary joined LLNL in 1981. His past assignments have been with Biomedical Research, Mechanical Engineering and Electronics Engineering, where he worked in the fields of computer graphics, solid modeling, product definition standards, mesh generation and visualization. He earned his B.S. in Computer Science from Abilene Christian University in 1981 and his M.S. in Computer Science from the University of California, Davis, in 1983. Gary is a former vice-chair of the IEEE CS Technical Committee on Computer Graphics. He has also served on the program committee for the IEEE CS Visualization conference series and was the first co-chair of that conference in 1990.
