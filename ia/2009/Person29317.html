  • Chairman for the non-profit and pro-bono sports adventure initative in Telemark

    "Telemarkshelten" - Project: Telemarksbirken

    (Sports industry)

    November 2008 — Present (11 months)

    The "Telemarksbirken" or "Telemarkshelten" is supported by Innovation Norway in Telemark, Vinje and Tinn municipality. The project aim to see if it is possible to establish an sport event and adventure company owned by the local sports clubs to position Telemark via the creation of three great sports events (a cross country ski race in the end of February, a mountain bike off road race in the early July and a mountain run in the end of August) on the top of mountains of Telemark; by the national park Hardangervidda; and utilise the beautiful distance between the town Åmot and Rauland in Vinje and Rjukan in Tinn (www.visittelemark.no, www.visitrauland.com and www.visitrjukan.com). The concept will be based on the fantastic nature, the history of skiing that originated in Telemark and the famous story of the “Heroes of Telemark!. The project objective is to conclude within the early fall of 2009. If the conclusion is positive, the project aim to start of with it first events as of 2010. Other members of the board of this project, managed by project leader/project employee Kjell Gunnar Dahle out of Rjukan; Bernhard Spidsø, Jack Waitz, Aud Irene Kittelsen, Harald Tiedemann Hansen, Erlend Larsen, Birger Hovden and Nils Jostein Helland. External resource; Terje Bakke, Ernst & Young.

    Iceland Crisis Mangement Organization, Republic of Iceland

    (Government Relations industry)

    October 2008 — December 2008 (3 months)

    Helped to establish, and played the role as a facilitator and coordinator of an interim Iceland Crisis Management Organization from early October until the International Monetary Fund, IMF, agreement was agreed in the end of November. The interim crisis management unit's aim was among other to avoid breakdown of the key institutions in the republic during the chaotic weeks with collapse of the countries three major domestic and international banks and financial institutions. The coordinator played an key liaison role between the countries “Situations Room” (political advisory group across government/ministries), the “Control Tower” (the countries key institutions), as well as towards the Prime Ministers Office and these groups daily status meetings. The role also focused on assisting with international media and public affairs relations. The temporary function reported to the Prime Ministers office.
