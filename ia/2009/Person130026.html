Kenneth Ford’s Summary

I recently retired from NASA after 36 years with the agency. This includes a solid record in System Engineering and Project Management positions with the Space Shuttle, Space Station, Tracking and Data Relay Satellite System (TDRSS), and science programs. While with NASA, I worked extensively with governments and companies in the U.S., Europe and Japan. I also have BS, MS and MBA degrees.

I’m available for short-term contract positions as a Program Management Consultant. I also offer my services for international teaming and liaison representation within the U.S. This may include tasks such as: strategic planning, proposal preparation, project development, requirements definition, technical & management studies, independent/red team analysis, field office initiation, risk management, compliance, recruiting, and training. I have strong project experience and I’m a team leader who effectively meets goals through strong leadership, interpersonal communication, and analytical abilities. I also have an active TS/SCI security clearance. I'm immediately available to work remotely from my home in Virginia.

  • NASA Goddard Space Flight Center

    (Government Agency; 10,001 or more employees; Defense & Space industry)

    January 1999 — April 2003 (4 years 4 months)

    Special Assistant for Program Integration for the Earth Observation System (EOS) at Goddard Space Flight Center (EOS). Integration Manager for EOS projects including Landsat-7, Terra, Aqua, Aura, ICESAT, and SORCE.   • NASA Headquarters; Washington, DC

    (Defense & Space industry)

    December 1993 — July 1996 (2 years 8 months)

    Earth Probes Program Manager in the Office of Mission to Planet Earth (MTPE). The function of this position is to serve as manager of the technical, programmatic, and budgetary aspects of Earth Probes Programs. My involvement in these programs culminated in the successful launches of the TOMS-EP in July 1996 and ADEOS in August 1996.
    In addition to the above MTPE responsibilities, I accepted a voluntary assignment as the technical chairman of the Gravity Probe B (GP-B) Independent Annual Review (IAR). I managed a team of individuals to review the technical and programmatic progress of the GP-B Program. The results of this review were briefed to the Program Management Council (PMC) in July 1995.

Managed development of joint technical requirements documents with each international partner in which the applicability of program requirements were baselined.
