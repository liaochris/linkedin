Marina De Santis’s Summary

I was working as a sales adviser on the shopfloor for Stanfords, the independent travel bookshop on Long Acre, Covent Garden, when I was offered a job as an editor for the company's website. I had been looking after the travel literature section and started off by writing reviews and synopsises of the titles we wanted to promote on the site. I was gradually given more work and more responsibilities.
In the meantime I attended a part-time course at Birkbeck College in Web Design and Development.
I never looked back. At the time, 2001, I was convinced the web would become an essential part of our lives, despite what had happened the previous year to the dot-com bubble.
I enjoyed working for a media owner such as Stanfords. It exposed me to all the different issues the running of a website poses on a day-to-day basis and in the planning of a long term strategy.
It is great to work for an agency too, as I did at Siris, because it meant working on different types of web projects with different goals and consequently different content.
I firmly believe in usable and fully accessible websites, with sound and effective content.
I relocated to the UK in May 2008 and from the beginning of June I have been working for Study Group. I covered a maternity leave for nine months, editing the corporate website, www.studygroup.com and the agents' extranet/staff intranet.
I started at a very exciting time as the new agents' extranet was being redeveloped, Partners. The site will be launched in June 2009 and it will allow agents to enrol online. The existing site has become an agglomerate of different layers of information, without a usable and flexible architecture.
The project presented the huge challenge of having two teams working on different parts of the site in Australia and the UK.
