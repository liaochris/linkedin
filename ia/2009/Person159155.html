Melanie Shelby’s Summary

Melanie is a 14-year professional versed at exceeding business objectives. She amassed leadership experience in Fortune 200 companies, impacting public affairs and diversity. In 2008, Melanie started Gray, Greer, Shelby & Vaughn LLC an advocacy firm specializing in diversity, government relations and strategic partnerships.

In 2006, former PG&E President & CEO Thomas B. King appointed Melanie to the newly created senior position of Director of Supplier Diversity to revitalize PG&E’s 25-year supplier diversity program. That year, PG&E spent $491 million with diverse and small businesses. The 2006 results underscored her ability to provide leadership, create infrastructure and align resources which positioned PG&E for accolades from national media. In 2007, PG&E eclipsed its achievement and spent $598 million with diverse firms. Melanie’s leadership was recognized by the National Association of Regulatory Utility Commissioners as 2007 Advocate of the Year.

In 2005, Melanie served as Manager, Federal Governmental Relations for PG&E Corporation in Washington, DC. Her responsibilities included oversight for the corporation’s national philanthropic program for diverse consituencies. Melanie also advocated on energy issues, working closely with the California delegation, Congressional Black Caucus and other Congressional Members. She was a leading advocate for an unprecedented $3.2 billion appropriation for the federally funded Low Income Home Energy Assistance Program (LIHEAP) during the 2005-2006 fiscal year. California’s allocation of that funding increased from $95 million to $175 million. The largest state and federal allocation in the program’s history.

Before DC, Melanie held a wealth of public affairs positions throughout Northern and Central California. This UCLA graduate continues to serve on a diverse range of boards.
