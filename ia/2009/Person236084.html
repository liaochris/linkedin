Tom Moody’s Summary

From the last year of my undergraduate studies, to late 2007, when I moved to Canada, I worked in biophysical chemistry labs in the United States. I now look forward to putting my background and experience to new uses.

Tom Moody’s Specialties:

Membrane-confined electrophoresis, analytical ultracentrifugation (AUC), AUC with fluorescence optics, and nonequilibrium thermodynamics.

Tom Moody’s Experience

  • Research Scientist, Research assistant professor

    University of New Hampshire

    (Educational Institution; 1001-5000 employees; Research industry)

    April 2000 — August 2007 (7 years 5 months)

    Most recent work at the Center to Advance Molecular Interaction Science (CAMIS) involved applications of capillary electrophoresis (CE), membrane-confined electrophoresis (MCE), analytical ultracentrifugation (AUC), and AUC with fluorescence optics (AUF) to study a set of selectively charge-modified proteins in complex solutions.

    Previously, contributed to developing practical methods for the detailed electrostatic and electrohydrodynamic characterization of macro-ion systems without comparison to standards. Toward that end, focused on the nonequilibrium thermodynamic theory applicable to macro-ion systems in MCE and AUC experiments.

  • Visiting scientist

    Amgen

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; AMGN; Pharmaceuticals industry)

    July 2006 — September 2006 (3 months)

    Used AUC to study protein sedimentation as a function of several solution conditions. Data analysis by sedfit (analyticalultracentrifugation.com) permitted the use of pseudo-absorbance methods to increase through-put.

  • Postdoctoral research associate

    University of New Hampshire

    (Educational Institution; 1001-5000 employees; Research industry)

    January 1995 — April 2000 (5 years 4 months)

    Within the Department of Biochemistry and Molecular Biology (BMB), taught physical biochemistry at the senior undergraduate/graduate student level. Investigated transport phenomena, with a focus on MCE. Designed MCE methods and experiments. Wrote and used computer programs to simulate a wide range of MCE and AUC experiments.

Tom Moody’s Education

  • University of New Hampshire

    Ph.D., Biochemistry, 1990 — 1994

    Doctoral research involved Limulus coagulogen, coagulin and coagulin gel formation. Techniques used included turbidimetry, velocity and equilibrium sedimentation, fluorescence spectroscopy, circular dichroic spectroscopy, SDS-PAGE, column chomatography, FPLC, electron microscopy, and MCE.

    Activities and Societies:

    I served as a channel for communication between graduate students and the UNH Board of Trustees, and kept the public informed of graduate students' concerns.
