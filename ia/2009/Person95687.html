Hope Keller’s Experience

  • Editorial Director

    J-Lab: The Institute for Interactive Journalism

    (Online Media industry)

    December 2008 — June 2009 (7 months)

    J-Lab is a center of American University's School of Communication.

  • Project Manager

    The Center for Nursing Advocacy

    (Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    September 2008 — December 2008 (4 months)

    I did a final edit of a book co-written by Sandy Summers, the center's executive director, and handled several other projects for the Baltimore-based nonprofit. After seven years running The Center for Nursing Advocacy, Summers early in 2009 created a new organization, The Truth About Nursing (www.truthaboutnursing.org).

  • Editor in Chief

    Baltimore SmartWoman magazine

    (Writing and Editing industry)

    2006 — 2008 (2 years)

    The name sounds silly but the magazine was smart – and popular with readers: street boxes in Baltimore City and County were empty within days of a new issue's appearance. The professionally designed, bimonthly magazine, published by Media Two Inc., had a circulation of 25,000 and featured the work of local reporters, photographers and essayists. I used InDesign software to correct and adjust pages in the final stages of production. The print magazine folded after a year and a half, in July 2008. An online-only version of SmartWoman exists today (www.smartwomanonline.com) and features content from the hard-copy issues as well as new material.

  • Mother & Work-From-Home Journalist

    Freelance

    (Writing and Editing industry)

    September 1998 — 2008 (10 years)

    Work-from-home journalist for 10 years. (See job details.) My daughter, Emma Angeline, was born in early 1999.

  • Editor and Reporter

    Freelance (Self-employed)

    (Self-Employed; Writing and Editing industry)

    1998 — 2006 (8 years)

    From 2001-02 I worked as a freelance reporter for The Baltimore Sun, writing news stories and features for the Sunday real estate section. Before that I was a freelance editor for The Center for Policy Analysis on Palestine, based in Washington, D.C. In 2004-05 I did an in-depth consulting job for the Washington-based Center for Public Integrity. Until mid-2006 I also worked frequently as a freelance reporter for Baltimore magazine and for Physican magazine (published at Washington Hospital Center) and edited the NPR ombudsman's weekly website column.

  • Copy Editor and Reporter

    The Philadelphia Inquirer

    (Privately Held; Newspapers industry)

    1988 — 1991 (3 years)

    I was the copy editor of a series on the national blood supply that won the 1990 Pulitzer Prize for public service for reporter Gilbert Gaul. I was also a copy editor on the prize-winning series "America: What Went Wrong?" by Donald Barlett and James Steele. In addition, I worked as a staff reporter for the Northeast Philadelphia Neighbors section.

  • Editor, Reporter & Writer

    Freelance (Self-employed)

    (Self-Employed; Writing and Editing industry)

    1980 — 1985 (5 years)

    Paris (1982-83 & 1985), Tel Aviv (1983-84), New York (1980-82). In Paris I was the associate editor of the monthly English-language journal Israel & Palestine and three other monthly publications, in English, French and Hebrew. In Tel Aviv I ghostwrote a book with the Israeli politician and publisher Uri Avnery about the assassinated PLO diplomat Issam Sartawi; I was also the style editor of Poetics Today, a quarterly journal of semiotics published at Tel Aviv University. In New York I edited and wrote horror and romance novels for teenagers while working as a temp secretary during the day. (Temp secretary = worst job ever.)

  • Editor, Reporter & Writer

    Freelance (Self-employed)

    (Self-Employed; Writing and Editing industry)

    1980 — 1982 (2 years)

    New York. I did many things, most of which I don't remember. Mainly I hung out at the Ansonia Hotel on the Upper West Side, working from a friend's high turret studio with a view south down Broadway. We typed, edited and rewrote on a pair of matching IBM Selectrics IIs: books, doctoral theses, beauty-school training manuals – you name it we cleaned it up.

  • Associate Editor

    Harcourt Brace Jovanovich Inc.

    (Writing and Editing industry)

    1979 — 1980 (1 year)

    New York. I started as a secretary in September 1979 (age 21) and was promoted within three months to editorial assistant and then to associate editor, in charge of line-editing coffee-table books about history and nature. $10,000 a year and my own office with a door.
