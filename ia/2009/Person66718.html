Joanne Taylor’s Summary

After a decade of climbing up the Corporate Ladder, I resigned from the IBM Corporation, climbed back down to earth, hung up my blue suit, put on my blue jeans, and started a successful gardening business on Philadelphia's Main Line. As a self-taught gardener for over 20 years, the outdoors became my office. The sky is the ceiling; the trees are the walls; dirt paths and stepping stones make up the hallways; evergreen hedges serve as partitions; and my office is the garden itself. My co-workers are the birds and the bees, rabbits, squirrels, and sometimes even snakes. As an avid gardener in both hobby and profession, I have had the privilege of being exposed to the things of nature on an almost daily basis. I work in a building that is designed and constructed by God himself. During those years of working outdoors, I discovered lessons in nature that we don’t recognize that can teach us how to live, to grow, and be wise. THE EARTH SPEAKS!!

Did you know that an oak tree doesn't produce acorns until it's at least 50 years old? You’re never too old to bloom. Have you ever walked down a city sidewalk and observed a green plant growing in the middle of the concrete or noticed a flower springing forth in the midst of brick wall? It signifies that, we too, can survive and even bloom in the most difficult circumstances.
