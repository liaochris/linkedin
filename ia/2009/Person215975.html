Shawn Steiner’s Summary

I was employed by Cine-tal Systems! They are the developers of Cinemage, DAVIO and CineSpace! More later...the following is my old BIO...I am on my 12th year at Autodesk's Media & Entertainment division. Prior to the acquisition by Autodesk in 1999, we were a separate company called Discreet Logic, based in Montreal. November 2008 Autodesk aquired Softimage from AVID. Three years ago Autodesk acquired Alias (based in Toronto), and has since added their products to our software product offerings. Last year, I moved into a new position at Autodesk working with Systems Clients in our Western Region of the United States and Latin America. The prior two years I have been the Sales Development Manager launching a new enterprise level compositing production tool called Toxik. More information is available about Toxik on our website at http://www.autodesk.com/toxik. I started my career back in 1983 as a Software Engineer for a little known company called Truevision, and received PC Magazine's award for Technical Excellence for our work on the TARGA+ project. I was one of the founders of AT&T Graphics Software Labs working on software products called RIO and TOPAZ. I later moved on to help start several companies in this industry: Electric Image, MetaCreations and Denim Software, which was acquired by Discreet Logic in 1997 and the product is now called combustion.

Autodesk (ADSK) is one of the largest software companies in the world at an estimated $2+ billion in annual revenue. Our M&E division can be found at http://www.autodesk.com/me
