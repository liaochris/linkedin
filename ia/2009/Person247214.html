Wilfrid Estève’s Summary

Wilfrid Estève is a French photojournalist and portraitist. Since 1995 he has worked with various publications, such as ELLE, Liberation, Le Monde, Geo France, Marie-Claire, National Geographic France, Paris Match or VSD, and was awarded a special distinction during the prix Nadar 2005, for his writings in the book "Photojournalisme, à la croisée des chemins".

Focused on testimonies and investigations, his photographic work is carried out in the field of action or in areas of hostility (West Africa, Europe, Middle East). In parallel he continues a societal work on France and immigration.

In 2005 his takes part in the creation of the photographic collective Picturetank and takes on the role of vice-president.

In 2008 he becomes co-director of the photo agency MYOP, and takes part in the creation of the website Photojournalism.fr, dedicated to news photography and photojournalism.

Wilfrid Estève’s Specialties:

Spécialisation en photojournalisme, évolution des formes de représentation de la photographie sur les nouveaux médias (web, téléphonie, PDA et Iphone)

  • Owner & managing director

    Hans Lucas

    (Animation industry)

    November 2006 — Present (2 years 11 months)

    Hans Lucas is a design and audiovisual production agency based in Paris, committed to discovering and adopting all means of visual expression and broadcast. The studio is specialized in the production of short films and has last developed since 2006, the production of the “POM”, literarily “little multimedia object.”


    The POM is a video montage with a soundtrack, which animates a fixed image and gives it a third dimension. The addition of the soundtrack allows a direct and sensitive approach to the subject. Modern and communicative, the POM is by nature a web object. A bridge between media and creation, this short animated film is a perfect tool adapted to today’s technologies and means of communication.



    We collaborate with a pool of a hundred of professionals in the design and media world. A platform of creation and production, Hans Lucas also aims at providing a think-tank around the evolution of new technologies in the audiovisual field.

  • Educational director

    EMI

    (Education Management industry)

    September 2006 — Present (3 years 1 month)

    Since 1996, Wilfrid Estève has been teaching photojournalism in many renowned schools, specialized training centers as well as in the form of workshops taking place in festivals.

    In 2006 he becomes the educational director within the photographic division of l'École des Métiers de l'Information, also know as the Other school. In 2007, he puts together the Prix Tremplin Photo, the first scholarship awarded during the festival Visa pour l'image and dedicated to photojournalism.

    The technical and professional higher education cursus in photojournalism counts among its former students : the 2008 recipient of the prix Mark Grosset ; the 2007 recipient of the award Visa d'or Magazin au Festival Visa pour l'image and the Festival du scoop et du journalism d'Angers Prix Special du Jury, the two recipients 2004 and 2002 of the Prix Kodak de la critique, the 2003 recipient of the Prix International du Reportage Humanitaire CARE and two recipients of the World Press Photo, 2001 and 2002.

  • President

    FreeLens-France

    (Photography industry)

    February 2004 — Present (5 years 8 months)

    Since 2004, Wilfrid Estève has been presiding over Freelens-France, formerly know as l'Association nationale des Journalistes Reporters Photographes et Cinéastes-FreeLens (The National Association of FreeLens Journalist Reporters, Photographers and Filmmakers).

    Since its creation, the association strives to assert the place, the importance as well as the role of photography within a democratic debate.

    This pole of reflection, founded in 1962, has united over the past four decades some of the major protagonist of photojournalism, such as Henri Cartier-Bresson, Raymond Depardon, Gisèle Freund, Marc Riboud, Sebastião Salgado.

    The association was president over by Robert Doisneau, Roger Pic, Patrick Bard and Lorenzo Virgili. In July 2008, FreeLens-France launched the website www.Photojournalisme.fr, dedicated to news photography and photojournalists.

  • Owner and management

    MYOP

    (Photography industry)

    May 2008 — May 2009 (1 year 1 month)

    Wilfrid Estève shares the management of MYOP along with Guillaume Binet and Lionel Charrier.

    This photo agency was created in 2005 and counts 13 independent photographers ; its originality lies in its ability to show a diversity of points of views.

    The members of the agency come from various backgrounds including conceptual photography, photojournalism and documentary, thereby, they bring about a pertinent questioning of the contemporary world.

    Anchored in the political photography of the newspaper Liberation where most of the members of the agency do freelance work, the photographers of MYOP have done some assignments for the most notorious newspapers such as Le Monde and magazines such as Elle, Géo, Marie Claire, National Geographic, Paris Match or The Times.

    MYOP's offices are in Paris, and its field of expertise covers a wide range, from information to venture communication to the development of cultural projects.

    M.Y.O.P stands for "Mind Your Own Photography"

  • General manager

    Territoires de fictions

    (Arts and Crafts industry)

    September 2005 — March 2009 (3 years 7 months)

    In 2005 Wilfrid Estève develops the project Territoires de fictions.

    The exhibition resulting from this project takes a look at France through one hundred artists, 13 sounds designers, 2 graphic designers, 28 filmmakers and 57 photographers. This collection includes contemporary artistic productions in both fields of photography and video. By exploring different forms to represent photography, Territoires de ficitions has developed a concept called POM, Petite Oeuvre Multimédia (Small Works of Multimedia).

    The exhibit combines different mediums (photography, video, sounds and graphics creations) ; over a period of 24 months it was shown over 30 times in France, Belgium, Italy and Poland.

    Territoires de fictions has gathered around a million visitors since 2006 through a social project that combined 600 photographies and 62 videos (presented in the form of a giant mosaic or giant posters).

  • Owner and photojournalist

    L'Oeil Public

    (Photography industry)

    January 1996 — April 2004 (8 years 4 months)

    Co-founder of the photo agency l'Oeil Public which he leaves in 2004, Wilfrid Estève was involved during 8 years with the creation of the broadcasting structure and the rise of the collective.

    The agency was distinguished by many prestigious photography awards (including the World Press Photo and Prix Kodak de la critique and contributes to renowned international publications.

    According to Michel Guerrin, journalist in charge of the cultural department of the newspaper Le Monde, specialized in photography "L'Oeil Public is a structure that fits in a well defined linage, like Magnum as a reference for independence and aesthetic, but also like Viva, a superb team of rabble-rousers created in the aftermath of may 68, that indicated a revival of the societal issue (French) as opposed to the far away "phenomenon", a taking part, a simplicity in the photography, the right of the photographer to his "own poetry", as Le Querrec used to say.
