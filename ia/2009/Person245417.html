Wally Van Sickle’s Summary

I have over 20 years experience working in my area of passion, the conservation of the planet's biodiversity. My goal is to promote national and international biodiversity conservation efforts through research, nonprofit, and educational activities.

I work with conservation biologists, educators and environmental leaders in 82 countries by providing them with the equipment they need to conduct their conservation efforts. For example, we get items like binoculars, digital cameras, laptop computers, two-way radios, recording devices, microscopes, GPS, and telemetry equipment to the people using them in the field. It's estimated that the number of people currently using equipment and supplies provided by Idea Wild is close to 7,000. Projects range in cost from $50 to $1,500, with the average amount given to any one project at around $750.

We are currently looking to form and strengthen relationships with organizations and individuals with connections to international conservation efforts, be they researchers, equipment suppliers, or potential funders. One thing we are always on the lookout for are people traveling to areas where we work who can deliver equipment for us, or who can bring back from that country a bag full of handicrafts that we can sell in fundraisers. If either of these interests you, please contact us.

We realize that we can also be a resource to our friends and are willing to share our knowledge of biodiversity as well as of travel in the many areas where we work. We look forward to working with others to protect this planet's precious biodiversity.

Wally Van Sickle’s Experience

  • Executive Director

    Idea Wild

    (Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    January 1991 — Present (18 years 10 months)

    Idea Wild is about empowering conservation biologists and educators on the front lines in the Developing World to help them conserve the biodiversity of the planet, particularly in the biodiversity "hot spots." These scientists lack the financial resources or basic tools and supplies they need. Idea Wild is the only organization on the planet that focuses on providing just that.

  • Research Biologist

    USDA Forest Service

    (Government Agency; Government Administration industry)

    1993 — 1996 (3 years)

    Rocky Mountain Forest and Range Experiment Station. Collected ecological information on American Martin in the Sierra Madre mountains, immobilized and radio collared martins, monitored martin movement with radio telemetry equipment, located dens and rest sites of females with kits, operated activity recorders collected and recorded forest habitat data at den sites.

  • Research Biologist

    Smithsonian Conservation and Research Center

    (Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    1991 — 1993 (2 years)

    U.S. migrant bird research project in Belize, captured birds in mist nets, identified resident and migrant birds, leg-banded and recorded morph metric data, located and established trapping grids in various habitats, collected and recorded habitat data, interacted with local people, and established field camps.

    Great Plains Wildlife Institute

    (Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    1991 — 1992 (1 year)

    Conducted 1-day wildlife expeditions and research projects with paying clients, prepared and presented lectures, interacted with personnel from various agencies, and prepared summary reports for government agencies based on project data.
