Marta Hurst, CLSSBB’s Summary

Estimates are as much as 40% of the cost of business is due to waste with 80% of processes in healthcare candidates for increasing efficiency, profitability, and even safety.

I have been involved in the creation, implementation, streamlining, and successful completion of many healthcare projects, programs, and accreditation. ENTJ leadership includes serving as the Vice-President of a chapter of the Associate Practitioners of Infection Control, and serving on the state boards of the Oklahoma Association for Healthcare Quality, and the Colorado Association for Healthcare Quality.

Journalism is a life long passion, first in high school, and then working on the campus newspaper and for our university radio station as a Journalism major. I have participated in organizing many events, some conventions and seminars, formal presentations, as well as writing Policies and Procedures, manuals, reports, feasibility studies, and proposals.

Risk Management workshops held by corporate attorneys, college courses on Risk Management, seminars by industry experts, and successfully defending company positions in response to Department of Insurance inquiries, have helped me understand processes, and how to mitigate possible risks to result in successful outcomes.

My Healthcare IT experiences include direct use of Electronic Health Records (EHRs) in the Acute Care setting, selling and installing EHRs and billing systems in physician offices, and investigating and writing feasibility studies on using tele-medicine in delivering care in rural areas. More recently, adding the Bachelor of Science in Computer Information Systems gave me additional IT and database skills, and becoming a Certified LEAN Six Sigma Black Belt provides metric and analysis tools.

Throughout my career, I continue to expand my education, experience, range and abilities. My passion for the win/win solution is real.
