Alexandra Watkins’s Summary

A recent winner of the Make Mine a Million $ Business Program, sponsored by American Express, I am the founder of Eat My Words, a wildly creative boutique naming firm recently featured in The Wall Street Journal.

When you see or hear a "Eat My Words name," you'll never ask what it means, how to pronounce it, or "What were they smoking?!" Our super-likable names make powerful emotional connections, build instant brand affinity, and make our clients rich. We are the only naming firm that MONETIZES names. For instance, the frozen yogurt franchise we named "Spoon Me" is making a fortune selling Spoon Me sportswear, pajamas, booty shorts, etc.

MOONDANCE is an iPod clock radio with mood lighting
FRIGID is a coming-soon gourmet ice cream store
NEATO is a robot that cleans your house
SPOON ME is a chain of frozen yogurt stores
POTION is a new PR firm with a unique formula
MONKEY DUNKS are gourmet dips for kids
MIXIN' VIXENS is an all-female bartending service
EAT MY DUST is a new commercial cleaning service
SECOND WIND is a "sex-over-sixty" business
CAKE FINANCIAL is a social networking site for investors
DIZZYWOOD is a virtual world for kids
VENUE is a website offering luxury goods on installment
STUFF A SOCK IN IT is a laundromat
WAVELENGTH is a forum for top companies and social entrepreneurs

Our clients include Frito-Lay, InterContinental Hotels, Guthy-Renker, Altec Lansing, THX, SIGG, Del Monte, and leading branding firms (including Landor) and ad agencies who put their good name on our great names.

Prior to becoming a full-time namer, I was an advertising copywriter for 15+ years. In 1998, I jumped on the dot com gravy train, and rode it until it crashed in my backyard. I broke into the naming business when Gap hired me to create names for their first line of body care products and I weaseled my way into branding powerhouse Landor, who started sending business my way and helped me build Eat My Words.
