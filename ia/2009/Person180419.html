João Palma’s Summary

Dr. Palma carries research for the development of scientifically sound methods to support forest ecosystems management by the public administration, non industrial forest owners, industry, non governmental organizations and concerned citizens. The main focus is in forest and agroforestry modelling, process (phisiological) and empirical (statistical) models with an emphasis on the development of user-friendly interface development, including web-based.
The research program includes topics such:
- Development and implementation of forest models able to cope with global challenges, including environmental changes as well as changes in forest management alternatives, society requirements and land use.
- Modelling the occurrence of risks – fire, pest and diseases – and its effect of forest structure and growth
- Modelling multiple ecosystem services from timber and non-timber products, to bio-energy, water balance and tourism
- Analysis and development of new methodologies for ecosystem management in a way to take into account: intensity of management, from intensive biomass production to close-to-nature forestry and nature conservation, the consideration of multiple objectives, risk and uncertainty, regime of landownership
- Development of decision systems that take into account participatory analysis and that allow for real time 3D decision support tools and web-based decision support

João Palma’s Specialties:

forest and agroforestry models, process based and empirical models, Geographical Information Systems application and model development, Soil erosion modelling, Nitrate Leaching modellling, Carbon sequestration modelling, Landscape diversity modelling. Artificial weather generation
Programming languages: PHP, Javascript, VB.NET, ArcObjects. Interface development for forest models.
