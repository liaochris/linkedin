Dean Krimmel’s Summary

I wandered into the museum field more than 25 years ago, and, recognizing a good home when I found it, never left. Several years ago, I went out on my own as a consultant, an independent museum professional, a hired gun.

My passion for museums and public history comes from many satisfying, and yes, frustrating, years spent looking for creative and exciting ways to engage different audiences through exhibits, programs, bus and walking tours, lectures, workshops, publications, and the media. I was lucky enough to build on those rich experiences over the past decade through my involvement in the planning and opening of three new museum facilities. A personal and professional finishing school, as it were.

Today, I work with clients, large and small, to create visionary strategic and interpretive plans, meaningful exhibitions, great visitor experiences, and compelling grants. My affiliation with Qm2—a community of consultants—gives my clients access to additional expertise and affords me the opportunity of continuing my museum education forever.

Dean Krimmel’s Specialties:

Exhibition planning and project management, interpretive planning, strategic planning, public engagement, improving the visitor experience

    (Museums and Institutions industry)

    2003 — 2005 (2 years )

    Like a kid in a candy store.

    I placed myself "on loan" to a $5.5 million, 22,000sf museum project on regional sports history. It's Cooperstown meets the Smithsonian (located next to Baltimore's celebrated ballpark).

    I coordinated content development (researched, wrote, edited, managed writing team), design process, hiring of fabricator, collecting, graphics acquisition. Curated several galleries. Liaison to building construction contractor.
