Keith Jack’s Summary

Highly creative, 23 years experience marketing multimedia semiconductors for the consumer electronics market. Extensive experience includes tactical and strategic marketing, business planning, financial analysis, press tours, seminars, customer presentations, sales training, trade shows, literature, standards forums and new product definitions.

Keith Jack’s Specialties:

- strategic and tactical semiconductor marketing
- consumer electronics industry
- trade shows and publishing
- financial and competitive analysis
- knowledge of Blu-ray standards
- knowledge of broadcast and cable standards
- knowledge of video compression standards
- experienced with handling licensing of 3rd-party technology
- author of "Video Demystified"

Keith Jack’s Experience

  • Director of Product Marketing

    Sigma Designs

    (Public Company; 51-200 employees; SIGM; Semiconductors industry)

    March 2001 — Present (8 years)

    Develops and markets highly-integrated System-on-a-Chip (SoC) semiconductors for IPTV set-top boxes, Blu-ray players, HDTVs and digital media adapters.

    Manage group responsible for product definition and management, web sites, trade shows, sales literature, technical documents and sales training.

    Initiated joining 3D@home (Board of Directors representative), ATSC, Blu-ray Disc Association (contributing representative), DLNA, DVB, DVD Forum (contributing representative), OpenIPTV, SCTE, SD Card Association, SMPTE and OpenCable. Managed licensing with the organizations.

    Worked closely with tier-1 Blu-ray player manufacturers to help secure first-generation design wins. Initiated turn-key reference design to enable Chinese Blu-ray player manufacturers to get into production quickly.

    Designed demo room and front lobby to showcase our solutions and customer products, and to impress investors, press and customers.

  • Director of Product Marketing

    Innovision Corp.

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Semiconductors industry)

    June 1999 — March 2001 (1 year 10 months)

    Designed and marketed semiconductors to enable interactive- and internet-ready televisions. Joined the company when it was a start-up; company was bought out early 2001.

    Performed market analysis, competitive analysis, pricing and forecasting for the business plan. Assisted with obtaining financing.

    Developed content for corporate brochure, product brochures, product datasheets and competitive white papers.

    Determined product features and roadmaps with feedback from major television manufacturers. Selected which television manufacturer to work with for custom OEM opportunity.

  • Product Line Marketing Manager, Module Compiler

    Synopsys

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; SNPS; Computer Software industry)

    June 1998 — June 1999 (1 year 1 month)

    Developed and executed business plan to offer high-value multimedia IP (MPEG-2 decoder, 1394 link layer, etc.) that was Module Compiler specific. It was eventually decided to include the IP within Foundation Library.

  • Strategic Marketing Manager, Multimedia

    Harris Semiconductor

    (Public Company; 5001-10,000 employees; HRS; Semiconductors industry)

    June 1995 — June 1998 (3 years 1 month)

    This marketing-driven start-up group designed and marketed multimedia semiconductors for the consumer electronics market.

    Introduced 7 new chips to the market, becoming their top-selling products.

    Initiated offering Verilog models of products, and determined licensing structure, to increase product revenue and to allow investments using our intellectual property.

    Performed technical and business analysis of NxtWave Communications to determine if Harris should invest. Determined the value of intellectual property that could used as an investment. Resulted in an investment in the company.

    Speaker at press conferences in Taipei, Hong Kong, and Singapore to announce the new multimedia focus for Harris. ~25 journalists attended each session.

    Presented at customer training seminars and trade shows to position Harris as a market leader.

  • Strategic Marketing Manager, Multimedia

    Brooktree Corp.

    (Semiconductors industry)

    June 1985 — June 1994 (9 years 1 month)

    Designed and marketed semiconductors for the computer and consumer electronics markets. Joined the company when it was a start-up, and went through the IPO. Company was later bought by Conexant.

    Developed and executed strategy for entering the multimedia semiconductor market, enabling Brooktree to become a leader in multimedia semiconductors for consumer electronics. Introduced 15 new products to the market.

    Architected world's first single-chip NTSC/PAL video encoders and decoders, and many other digital video and high-end RAMDAC products. Resulted in positioning company ahead of the competition and opening new markets.

  • Senior VLSI Design Engineer

    Rockwell Semiconductor

    (Semiconductors industry)

    June 1982 — June 1985 (3 years 1 month)

    Designed various CPUs and peripheral chips, and custom semiconductors for customers. This division of Rockwell International was later spun out as Conexant.
