Kristin Boekhoff’s Summary

I am currently developing a sustainable boutique resort in southern Bangladesh where savvy travelers can have a beautiful, authentic, cultural experience while preserving the social and environmental integrity of the area. Panigram will be the first in a collection of resorts that focus on destination stewardship.

I recently completed my Fulbright Fellowship in Bangladesh where I researched the feasibility of a sustainable, replicable low-income housing real estate development program for female migrant laborers in Dhaka.

Prior to my departure for Bangladesh, I was the Chief Information Officer at CharterMac, one of the US's leading full-service real estate finance companies. My team worked closely with the asset management, underwriting, acquisitions, accounting, lending, and construction groups to improve the firm's ability to service its developers and investors and to continue the corporate goals of streamlining workflow efficiencies and improving communication between internal and external groups. Previously, I ran the Fund Management Group where I was responsible for developer servicing, cash management, and investor reporting. I managed over $7 billion of equity invested in the firm's 100+ real estate funds and oversaw developer relations for over 1,200 new and stabilized properties.

Before joining CharterMac, I had six years of project management experience, most recently as a business analyst at Neuberger Berman, a financial services firm, where I was responsible for managing large information technology and business reorganization projects. Previously, I worked as a management consultant for PricewaterhouseCoopers in the Marketing and Customer Management Group.

I received a B.S. in Apparel Design and Management with concentrations in Computer Science and Information Systems from Cornell University and an M.S. in Real Estate Development from New York University.

  • Owner

    Panigram Resort

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Real Estate industry)

    December 2007 — Present (1 year 6 months)

    I am developing a sustainable boutique resort in southern Bangladesh that provides relaxing, beautiful, luxurious accommodation for guests while maintaining a commitment to social and environmental sustainability. Located in a village just 70 km from the famous Sundarban mangrove forest, Panigram will be a modern interpretation of vernacular Bangladeshi architecture.

    Themes of harmony, community, and cultural sharing will run throughout the resort, from the green design which minimizes the resort’s energy footprint, to the long tables that encourage guests to interact, to the live Bangladeshi folk music performed in the evening.

    Features will include: produce organically grown on site, alternative energy, an environmental education program in a nearby school, support of women’s cottage industries and local artisans, and a training program for local contractors.

    It is my hope that Panigram will be the first of many boutique resorts focused on destination stewardship.
