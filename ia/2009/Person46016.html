Chung-Ying Cheng’s Summary

Professor Chung-ying Cheng

Email: iewu.edu@gmail.com

BA 1956, National Taiwan University
MA 1958, University of Washington
PhD 1964, Harvard University
President of the International East-West University（IEWU）, from August 28, 1995 to today.

A member of the Department since 1963, Professor Cheng has become a senior scholar in Chinese philosophy. With a broad and deep background in the traditions of classical Chinese philosophy and Neo-Confucianism, he received his doctorate from Harvard University in the field of analytical philosophy and logic. He has received fellowships and grants from the National Science Foundation, the Pacific Cultural Foundation and the Stanford Institute in the Philosophy of Science. He is the founder and a past president of the International Society of Chinese philosophy. He also founded and serves as president of the International Society for Yijing Studies. Cheng has edited the Journal of Chinese Philosophy since its founding in 1972 and has received an Honorary Doctorate from the Far Eastern Institute of the Russian Academy of Sciences. Professor Cheng has authored and edited 15 books and over l50 articles in Western, Chinese, and comparative philosophy. He is currently working on a book on onto-hermeneutics and a book on contemporary Chinese philosophy.

Chung-Ying Cheng’s Education

  • Harvard University

    PHD at Harvard University, Philosophy Department, 1956 — 1964

    Prof. Chung-ying Cheng graduated from National Taiwan University with a B.A. in 1957. He received Ph.D. in Philosophy at Harvard University in 1964. He has been teaching analytical philosophy, philosophy of science and Chinese philosophy at the University of Hawaii at Manoa since 1964. He has been a full professor of Philosophy at the same university since 1973. He has been a founder and presently Honorary President of International Society for Chinese Philosophy. He is editor of Journal of Chinese Philosophy and author of 12 books in philosophy and over 120 articles in both English and Chinese. He is also president of the International Society of the Yijing studies.

    Chung-ying Cheng is a Chinese philosopher. He received his BA in 1956 from National Taiwan University, his MA in 1958 from University of Washington, and PhD in 1964 from Harvard University.

    Professor Cheng's research interests are in the areas of Chinese logic, the I Ching and the origins of Chinese philosophy, Confucian and Neo-Confucian Philosophy, the onto-hermeneutics of Eastern and Western philosophy, and Chan (Zen) philosophy. Recently he has specifically worked on the philosophy of c-management and Confucian Bio-Ethics as they relate to the Chinese tradition, and on how Chinese culture relates to world culture.
