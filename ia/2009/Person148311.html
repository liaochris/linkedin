Marc Drees’s Summary

My professional ambition is to transform cutting-edge information technology to the true benefit of the (online) user.

During my corporate life and, since 2000, as an independent consultant, entrepreneur and investor I have always worked at the intersection of strategy and IT. While in very different roles, the direction of my work has always been to humanize technology to the benefit of those who have to use it: real people.

Too often new applications that see the light of day are doomed from the very start, simply because no attention was paid to the only stakeholder that really counts. The user. This is much better said in Alan Cooper's book "The inmates are running the asylum". But the bottom line is that most applications available today are terrorizing normal people.

Focusing on the Dutch labor market I founded RecruitmentMatters (Dutch weblog on online recruitment) and RecruitmentLab (online assessments for online recruitment website quality, available December 2008). I have also have co-founded Kangarooster (job board) and Digitaal-werven (researching jobsections of corporate sites).

  • Founding partner

    Digitaal werven

    (Partnership; 1-10 employees; Internet industry)

    October 2007 — Present (1 year 8 months)

    Digitaal werven is an initiative to assess the quality of the recruitment sections of corporate websites.

    Recruitment sections are assessed on 50 criteria, using a standardized method. Criteria are grouped into the following viewpoints:
    – Site design
    – Ease of use / usability
    – Breadth of functionality
    – Content
    – Vacancy structure
    – Application process
    – Innovative elements

    Companies can subscribe for free to have their recruitment section assessed. Companies can also choose to purchase a detailed report, consisting of assesment findings and advice on key improvement areas.

    Assessment results will be used to determine the best recruitmentsite on an annual basis.
