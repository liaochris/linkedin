Victoria Bowman’s Summary

Victoria Bowman discovered the power of marketing in Kenneth Johnson’s 1983 TV mini series masterpiece, “V”. Seeing the persuasiveness of the alien “friendship” posters, she knew then that she was destined to be in marketing or military propaganda.

A typical teenager’s obsession with fashion waylaid those plans and sent her packing for the big city and F.I.T. Ever the clever gal, she mastered the multiple disciplines of Fashion Merchandising, Product Development and Management, and General Marketing to produce one big ol’ fancy university degree.

Hitting the road running, Victoria held multiple product development positions for the venerable 145+ year Butterick Company. Looking for a new challenge, Victoria was on the hunt. She pursued an opportunity in the Software industry joining Wise Choice Software as a Sales & Marketing Manager. She has steadily grown with the company and is currently a Vice President.

You can contact Victoria at VBowmanMrktg@verizon.net

As the product evangelist I set the direction for brand development. Oversee market and competitive analysis including segmentation, market sizing, trends and competitive intelligence. Drive sales tool development, marketing collateral production and outbound marketing efforts.

  • Product & Marketing Director

    Wise Choice Software

    (Computer Software industry)

    2003 — 2005 (2 years)

    As Director I moved into a more strategic role. During this time we expanded our lead generation programs to incorporate more digital media including email campaigns, banner advertising and web marketing, geared towards gaining new clients and up selling the existing client base. As well as increasing our web presence, taking site from “under construction” to full featured, 150+ page site encompassing corporate branding, software demonstrations and product support.

  • Sales & Marketing Manager

    Wise Choice Software

    (Computer Software industry)

    1999 — 2003 (4 years)

    Initially hired to facilitate VAR sales efforts, Wise Choice Software’s explosive growth made it evident that we needed to launch an in-house sales department quickly. I spearheaded the development of the Sales & Marketing department, establishing lead generation methods, sales approach and marketing objectives. During this time the company introduced RightClick Velocity, a business management software for jewelry retailers. I worked closely with the programmers developing product features based on market needs and competitive analysis.

  • Account Manager

    Butterick Company Inc.

    (Publishing industry)

    1997 — 1999 (2 years)

    If it’s Tuesday, it’s Tupelo. As an Account Manager I traveled nationwide to key accounts to present cooperative consumer marketing programs to their senior management. Working with national retailers and fabric manufacturers we developed P-O-P displays, product packaging and advertising pieces to support the product programs. In addition, I was the media contact for trade publications providing product forecasts. The Butterick Company Inc. brands are now part of The McCall Pattern Company.

  • Merchandise Manager

    Butterick Company Inc.

    (Publishing industry)

    1996 — 1997 (1 year)

    If someone in your family has ever sewn a Halloween costume, window valance, prom dress or wedding gown; chances are likely it was a Butterick home sewing pattern. An integral part of everyday American life, home sewing patterns gave generations of women (and men) the ability to express their creativity.

    The Butterick Company gave me a creative opportunity when I was hired as a Design Assistant while attending FIT. Rising through the Merchandising department, I became the Merchandise Manager for Butterick and See & Sew brands in 1996. As part of the Product team I merchandised 10 catalogs annually, shopped the R-T-W market, and managed the See & Sew brand product development.
