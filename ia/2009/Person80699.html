Franci McFarland (previously Steinmuller)’s Summary

Research, design, and evaluate technologies in order to maximize users' enjoyment, understanding, strategy and skill.

Franci holds a BA in Psychology and a Master's Degree in the Learning Sciences from Northwestern University. She has also completed post-graduate work developing multimedia educational software and curriculum for public school classrooms. Her 10+ years of working in cognitive science and user experience research have been driven by a passion to help clients improve their technologies through better understanding of customers' needs. Franci's career spans studies of technologies designed for both individual and collaborative use, for products as varied as: software for wireless handheld devices to be used by museum goers in a hands-on science museum, website information architecture and branding strategy for a global credit card company, tutorials and games for web-based "casual" multi-player games developed by PlayFirst, tutorials and games for the Nintendo Wii system, tutorials and games for children's "edutainment" hardware and software products developed by Leapfrog Enterprises, and multiple software products for Intuit (i.e. Quicken, Quickbooks, Payroll services) and Adobe Enterprises (i.e. Photoshop, Dreamweaver, Creative Suite, etc). In addition, Franci was a founding member of the faculty at Carnegie Mellon University’s West Coast Campus where she was in charge of evaluation and delivery of e-learning solutions for their graduate school curriculum and where she was also teaching courses on educational technology and interaction design. Franci has taught classes, published articles, and presented papers at conferences on teaching, design, and technology.
