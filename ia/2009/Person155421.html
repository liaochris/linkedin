Matthew Messina’s Summary

I recently completed a Master of Arts degree in Global Leadership and Sustainable Development from Hawaii Pacific University. Today, I am seeking a career in the sustainable energy sector, and more specifically with the direct development of solar industries in the state of California.

Prior to completing my MA, I attended the University of California, Davis to study Political Science and Communication, and received a dual Bachelor of Arts. There, I was a Founding Father of Lambda Chi Alpha's UC Davis chapter, and an active member.

After attending UC Davis, I was hired on at the University of California, San Diego, as the Assistant to the Student Conduct Coordinator. This position allowed me to develop and maintain professional relationships with high-level university employees such as College Deans and University Provosts. My professionalism and work ethic with such individuals eventually led to a full-tuition scholarship offer from Hawaii Pacific University to attend graduate school.

As a graduate student/ assistant, I quickly became an active participant in student organizations, and in Student Life in general. As president of the GREEN club, I led the effort to give the organization a complete "make-over", including a name change, mission re-draft, and enhancing recruitment strategies. These efforts allowed GREEN to become the first student club on campus to implement a cost-effective paper recycling program- returning an average monthly revenue stream of $200. In addition to acting as President, I served part-time as a Graduate Assistant to the VP of Student Affairs.

In my final semester, I was nominated for the Robert E. Black Award for Excellence, as well as honored with Academic Distinction merits at the graduation ceremony in May 2008.
