Thomas Schumacher’s Summary

Thomas Schumacher started his career in 1986 doing software quality assurance for entertainment software. He was part of the teams credited with shipping such diverse products as the Atari Lynx, The Learning Company’s Interactive Math Journey, and Adobe Photoshop version 4. After ten years experience working in SQA, he was successful in making the transition to R&D software development. As a programmer at Canon he started writing test harness code and was soon writing production code. Over five years at Canon he was a major contributor to six large scale projects with over ten thousand lines of code each. Projects included a COM based imaging library, a UI front end to digital camera TWAIN library, an OOP based digital Camera SDK, a USB WIA device driver, and an SDK of profiling tools for color science research. Mr. Schumacher holds an Associates of Science Degree from Foothill College in Los Altos Hills. He was awarded three US patents the areas of color science, image compression, and image authentication.
