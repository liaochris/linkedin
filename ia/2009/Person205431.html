Russell Dyas’s Summary

I have worked in education since leaving college and I have also worked as a consultant. I have worked in various settings from colleges and youth centres to schools.

I am recognised as a leading public commentator on ICT Support in education. I have gained a professional reputation from being a Manager at EduGeek.net - the leading website for ICT support staff in education. I have negotiated several opportunities for EduGeek.net to work with organisations and to improve the working life of our members and ICT Support Staff.

I have long been a public supporter of RM PLC, and feel that the software that RM provides is useful and correct for some educational establishments. During my work with Limehurst High School, I have negotiated us first becoming an RM Partnership School and then becoming an RM Pioneer School. I have also worked with RM, providing them with the customer’s views on such things as RM product design to the RM Customer Community, and I have been an Moderator on the RM Customer Forums. I was also part of the panel which was involved in designing of the new version of their Community Connect branded product.

Working in education often provides an unusual approach to networks and network security (education networks are among the most secure networks due to the nature of education) and also the unusually large number of users - often going into the 1000+ range.

Also, I have been a beta tester for companies such as the BBC, McAfee, Symantec, AOL, RM and Microsoft.

Russell Dyas’s Specialties:

beta testing, network design, network management, ICT support, training, hardware maintenance, website design, website module coding for CMS websites, conference and event organisation, new technologies, evangelism, new-product introduction, education, IT Support, new technology and helping companies break into UK education market.
