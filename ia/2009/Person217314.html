Sica De St Jeor’s Summary

I am the District Manager for the Aortic Intervention SBU for Cook Medical in the Utah and Las Vegas areas. I am involved in training several surgeons and their staff members in endovascular procedures. While studying for my degree I worked in the operating room at the University of Utah Hospital where I was able to closely interact with surgeons and their patients. I am well organized and self motivated. I enjoy working in fields where I can make a difference in the lives of others and myself. It is important to me to see results from my hard work.

• I have set up new successful endovascular programs with groups of physicians in my territory and did the complete training of them and their operating room hospital staff, as well as working with Hospital Administration.
• I started the Aortic Abdominal Rupture program at Ogden Regional Medical Center which has subsequently saved the lives of patients from outlying Salt Lake areas and as far away as Wyoming.
• I travel wherever I am needed to lend a hand to my team members and enjoy providing input at meetings.
• I have led several in-services, written power points and used hands on tools to teach the professionals I work with so they can successfully perform in their career fields.

While at the University of Utah:
• I was one of the five employees (including nurses and surgical technicians) who could assit in vascular and cardiac procedures out of a staff of over 120.
• I taught new hires, surgical technology students, and medical students how to assist in surgical procedures and function appropriately in the operating room.
• I know how to use the other equipment in an operating room, how to protect the sterile field, and often led in-services on these topics.
• I was also in charge of the call shift scheduling for the staff every month and for holidays, and took extra call shifts to make sure we were always covered.
