John Pisapia’s Summary

Dr. Pisapia brings over 23 years of management experience as a principal, state commissioner of education, University Department Chair, and Research Director to his academic podium and consultancies. He previously held tenured faculty positions at West Virginia University and Virginia Commonwealth University.

His book, The Strategic Leader promising a new direction for leading in a globalized world was published in 2009. His articles have appeared in the Leadership Review, International Journal of Leadership Education, The International Education Journal, Phi Delta Kappan, Government Union Review, Journal of Collective Bargaining and NOLPE Law Review.

His work in strategic thinking and execution has received national and international attention. His Strategic Thinking Questionnaire (STQ) measures the leader’s use of systems thinking, reframing and reflection. His Strategic Leadership Questionnaire (SLQ) measures the leader’s use of five sets of action in leading their organizations: managing, transforming, bonding, bartering, and bridging.

His client list is headed by the U. S. Department of State, and includes, the Slovenian Ministry of Commerce, The Chinese University in Hong Kong, The Canadian Ministry of Education, as well as Ritz-Carlton, Playtex Corporation, the Greenbrier Resorts. His collaborations with the National HRD Network of India, The China Executive Leadership Academy Pudong China, American Association of Educational Research, the International Leadership Academy of James MacGregor Burns, The University of Malaya, and the Chinese University at Hong Kong have exposed his ideas throughout the world.

John Pisapia’s Specialties:

Professor Pisapia’s orientation is leading organizations and people from a global perspective. In a 24/7 world leaders at every level of the organization and society must work in a strategic way, meaning creating an actionable plan and implementing it.

John’s core competencies are working with organizations by running the strategic thinking protocol to develop an actionable plan, and the strategic execution protocol to see that intitatives to reach that intent lead high performance.

John Pisapia’s Experience

  • Professor

    Florida Atlantic University

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    August 1998 — Present (11 years 6 months)

    John Pisapia is a Professor of Leadership and Policy at Florida Atlantic University in Boca Raton, Florida and founder of the International Circle of Strategic Leaders. John joined the faculty at Florida Atlantic University as Professor and Chairman of the Department of Educational Leadership. At Florida Atlantic University, Dr. Pisapia led faculty efforts to redesign doctoral programs that focus on a common core of leadership in a global perspective. He now serves as senior research professor in leadership and policy studies and supervises the research of PhD students. He currently serves on the University's Research Priorities Steering Committee.

    He has been called the “global professor,” having consulted with leaders in education and business in India, China, Malaysia, South America, and Europe as well as in the United States. His book, The Strategic Leader promising a new direction for leading in a globalized world was published in 2009. His articles have appeared in the Leadership Review, International Journal of Leadership Education, the Journal for School Effectiveness, Phi Delta Kappan, Journal of Law and Education, Government Union Review, Journal of Collective Bargaining, NASSP Bulletin and NOLPE Law Review.

    Professor Pisapia’s contributions, have led to many significant honors. In 1980, The American Association of School Administrators presented him with its first ever Professor of the Year Award. Other honors include (i) the Gerald Howard Read International Seminar Scholarship Award, (ii) the WVU Alumni Association Award for Significant Leadership, (iii) an NCATE Recognition for exemplary work in founding the Metropolitan Educational Research Consortium (MERC), (iv) a Fulbright Scholarship, (v) the Glenville State College 2007 alumnus of the year, and (vi) recently the International Schools Association’s 2009 distinguished service award for profound leadership in international education.

  • Professor

    Virginia Commonwealth University

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    January 1989 — August 1998 (9 years 8 months)

    At Virginia Commonwealth University, Dr. Pisapia served as the Founding Director of the Metropolitan Educational Research Consortium (MERC) a joint venture between the University and surrounding school districts. From 1992 to 1998, he published 33 major research reports which led to state, national and federal acclaim as an exemplary model of school/university collaboration.

  • Chief State School Officer

    State of West Virginia

    (Executive Office industry)

    August 1980 — December 1989 (9 years 5 months)

    As West Virginia’s Chief State School Officer he was responsible for an $800 million dollar budget. During this public service commitment, he led the development of a common core of learning and installed a system of public accountability. He also launched three major capacity building efforts: (a) statewide delivery of satellite delivered instruction, (b) and nationally recognized Principal and Teacher Academies. Nationally, he chaired the Chief State School Officers’ efforts to produce a blueprint for the creation of a national accountability system using the National Assessment of Educational Progress (NAEP).
