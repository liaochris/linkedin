Ralph Martin’s Summary

Experienced in complex litigation, investigations, and supervision of litigation, including:

• Conducting and supervision of multimillion-dollar investigations and litigation on behalf of private clients, including individuals, corporations, and financial regulators.

• Representation of members of the foreign affairs and intelligence communities in administrative, Inspector General, and criminal matters.

• Counsel to securities brokerages, investment banking firms, and individuals in disciplinary matters, securities arbitration, and preparation of supervisory procedures.

• Service as counsel to the United States Delegation to the United States General Assembly and as associate representative of the United States in the International Court of Justice (EMRO case).

• Service as counsel for Financial Industry Regulatory Authority in securities-related investigations and litigation and in bankruptcy litigation.

• Conduct of high-profile investigations and serving as lead counsel in criminal trials, including civil rights and political corruption prosecutions for the U.S. Department of Justice.

• Supervision of investigations and litigation worldwide for the U.S. Department of State.
