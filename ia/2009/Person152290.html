Coordinated world-wide provision of over $300 million (wholesale) worth of medicines/medical supplies in 130 countries on five continents through health development programming via five regional offices and more than +40 NGO/IGO partners. Managed procurement, inventory, logistics, distribution and field assessment processes in support of health development initiatives designed to build capacity of constituencies to attain self-sufficiency in healthcare. Coordinated international NGO's emergency relief and rehabilitative medical responses to natural and man-made disasters including information-gathering, medical needs assessment, medicine and medical supply availability, consignees/partners, assessment and funding. Ensured organization executed all relief and rehabilitation services according to international performance standards. Represented MAP in strategic fora including Interaction, UNOCHA, UNHCR, UNWHO/PAHO, USAID/OFDA, ECHO. Active in many of the major complex humanitarian emergencies of the last 15 years: Democratic People's Republic of Korea (famine); El Salvador (democratization/reconciliation); Former Yugoslavia [Bosnia-Herzegovina, Croatia, Kosovo] (wartime refugee crisis); Panama (post-war rehabilitation); Rwanda (refugee crisis including activities in Tanzania, Uganda, Democratic Republic of Congo [formerly Zaire]); Sudan (Hunger Triangle); Vietnam (post-U.S. embargo humanitarian airlift); operated in +30 countries over seven years.
