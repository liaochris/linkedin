Danny Garcia’s Summary

Conducts digital forensic examinations within the Miami-Dade Police Department, Forensic Computer Laboratory; supervises and reviews examinations, hardware and software validation testing reports, and manages overall operations of the laboratory; manages and approves all laboratory expenditures from regular funding and grants; maintains current certification as a Certified Forensic Computer Examiner (CFCE) through the International Association of Computer Investigative Specialists (IACIS); Is an official member of the United States Secret Service (USSS), Miami Electronic Crimes Task Force (MECTF).

Has established and maintains relationships with other County department personnel, and outside agencies on behalf of the laboratory and the Department. Is involved in the development, implementation and approval of policies for laboratory management and forensic laboratory procedures. Teaches and organizes courses related to digital and multimedia forensics for the Department, national organizations, and other entities as requested and approved through the Department.

PRIOR ASSIGNMENTS:

Assisted in-house developers and programmers/engineers to developed in-depth software design specifications to include data field usage and user experience issues for a $2M records management project.

Conducted peer reviews of digital evidence examinations conducted by other examiners assigned to the forensic computer laboratory.

Attended review board hearings to determine the continued training and employment of probationary officers. Identified potential liability issues, possible program enhancements, and prepared changes to procedures as required.

Conducted investigations on cases involving crimes related to domestic violence. Prepared detailed investigative reports, search warrants, and arrest warrants. Authored the Domestic Violence Supplement Report used for all domestic violence cases within Miami-Dade County.
