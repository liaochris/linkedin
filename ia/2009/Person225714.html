Stu Goldberg’s Summary

Stu Goldberg - Composer
Composing credits include the five-time Emmy-winning "The Amazing Race" TV series, CBS, Jerry Bruckheimer producer; scored over 100 episodes of the CBS reality series "Rescue: 911", hosted by William Shatner; "Passion's Web" ("Uncaged Heart") for Peace Arch - Lifetime, Allan Harmon, director; "Impulse" for Sony Screen Gems, Charles T. Kanganis, director; "Dancing Trees", Anne Wheeler, director, and "Paparazzi Princess: the Paris Hilton Story", Terry Ingram, director, for Nasser Entertainment; "Fatal Kiss", Jason Bourque, director, for Insight Films and Nasser Group North, "Desperate Hours", George Mendeluk, director, for Insight Films and Nasser Group North; "Christmas Crash", Terry Ingram, director, for Insight Films and Nasser Group North; "The Mrs Clause", George Erschbamer, director, for Insight Films and Jeff Schenck productions; "Silent Venom", Fred Olen Ray, director, for Cinamour Entertainment & ARO Entertainment; "Desperate Escape", George Mendeluk, director, for Insight Films & TF1; "Trust", Allan Harmon, director, for Really Real Films, Insight Films, TF1; "Angel and the Badman", Terry Ingram, director, for Nasser Group North; "The Building", Terry Ingram, director, for Nasser Group North; "Courage", George Erschbamer, director, for Nasser Group North . (credits as of May 2009).

View hours of movie clips and MP3s from Stu's scores at his virtual reel (see above for link).

Toured and recorded worldwide, including 5 years as Jazz keyboardist with John McLaughlin as a member of the legendary Mahavishnu Orchestra. Additionally, performed with Al Di Meola, Freddie Hubbard, Wayne Shorter, Billy Cobham, Jack Bruce and many others. As a recording artist Stu continues to produce and record his own CDs which are released on his Dedication Records label.

Stu Goldberg’s Specialties:

Film composer, recording studio, orchestration, music arranger, record producer, recording engineer, music and sound mixer, audio post production, 5.1 surround recording and mixing, piano, keyboards, hand percussion, computer sampling and sequencing

    "Live" (MPS-68291, recorded 1982). This was a concert recording of my touring trio with myself on piano, Palle Danielsson on bass and Jon Christensen on drums, recorded in the Landesmuseum, Muenster, Germany.
