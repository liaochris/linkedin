Stephen Lawton’s Summary

Steve Lawton brings twenty years of private-sector experience to the redevelopment plan of a small California city.

As Director of Community Development for the City of Hercules, Mr. Lawton oversees the day-to-day operations of the Planning, Engineering and Building Inspection Divisions. Reporting to the City Manager, he manages key elements of the physical and commercial redevelopment program for this fast-growing Bay Area city.

A third-generation Californian, Mr. Lawton's understanding of innovation, organizations and governance, and his passion for great places, led him recently to redirect his career to urban design and the complex processes of urbanism. In 1997 he joined the Congress for the New Urbanism, a national movement that advocates the restructuring of public policy and development practices. In 1999-2000, as Chair of the Planning Commission, he led the effort to create the Central Hercules Plan, which became the first form-based code to be adopted into the law of a California city.

Previously, from 1982 through 2000, Mr. Lawton held management consulting and business development positions with private and public firms providing systems engineering, technology and business innovation services to global corporations. Mr. Lawton managed systems engineering and integration engagements for corporate clients including Goldman Sachs, Federal Express, Bank of America, HongkongBank, United States Postal Service and Burlington Northern Railroad. In 1992, he co-invented an image-based electronic commerce system, awarded US Patent 5832100. In 1986, he supported a business startup that installed the first UNIX workstations on Wall Street.

Steve began his career in 1977 at the Southern Pacific Railroad, in operations and marketing. Mr. Lawton holds a BA degree in Economics from the University of California.
