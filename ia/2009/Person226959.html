  • Licensed Massage Therapist in Private Practice

    Susan Epperly, L.M.T.

    (Health, Wellness and Fitness industry)

    July 2006 — Present (3 years 5 months)

    I am a Licensed Massage Therapist in private practice, specializing in pain relief through Clinical Massage Therapy. I incorporate a unique and powerful combination of therapeutic techniques into my practice, including Clinical Massage Therapy; Trigger Point Therapy; Myofascial Release; Postural & Gait Analysis; Therapeutic Taping; "Ice & Stretch" Muscular Release Techniques; Negative Pressue Cupping Massage; and the use of a Deep Muscle Stimulator (D.M.S.) vibrational device.

    My clinic is in Austin's "SoCo" district at 1902 South Congress Avenue, Austin, Texas 78704. (At the corner of South Congress Avenue and West Mary Street).

    As an independent contractor, I have provided table and chair massage services to various Austin area companies including Book People, Dell, KB Home, Austin Massage Clinic, BFG Communications, Performance Wellness, The Tower Health Club & Spa at the Hilton Hotel Downtown Austin, The Trinity Spa at The Omni Hotel South Park, The Renaissance Hotel at the Arboretum, The Dell Jewish Community Center, and Crown Castle Communications. I have provided sports massage services at sporting events such as the Valero MS 150 Bicycle Race, The Motive Buffalo Stampede Half Marathon, and The Decker Challenge Marathon.

    Before I ventured into the world of massage therapy, I earned a Bachelor of Arts degree in Social Science from Portland State University in Portland, Oregon. My studies there emphasized sociology, psychology, social psychology, and minority & gender studies. (G.P.A. 3.28)

    My massage therapy training began in 2005 at Austin’s Lauterstein-Conway Massage School and Clinic where I completed 300 hours of training in Swedish Massage (G.P.A. 4.00). I then went on to complete the 290 hour Clinical Massage Therapy Program at Texas Healing Arts Institute, where my studies focused primarily on trigger point therapy and myofascial release techniques (G.P.A. 4.00).

    I am currently licensed in Texas and Arkansas.
