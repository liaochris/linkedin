    Phd Candidate, Innovation Technologique - Java Technology, 2008

    This thesis will explore the use of independent mobile and intelligent agents (referred to as smart agents) to solve problems in wireless and mobile ad hoc networks.

    Our research works will focus on two major problems: (a) wireless mobile ad hoc network management (specifically faults management) and (b) content, knowledge, information and data collection and sharing. We will study the combination of the following three major mobility patterns and identify when it is appropriate to use each one individually:
    (i) in a stationary mode, smart agents execute statically their tasks without being impacted by the mobility of their hosting node,
    (ii) a fully mobile agent needs to travel continually to adapt to the ad hoc and dynamic nature of the network,
    (iii) in a hybrid mode, a smart agent will travel (mobile) only when it is necessary and stays locally to run static tasks (stationary) whenever resources are available.
