Richard Litman’s Summary

Helping universities, innovative businesses and creative people comes naturally to Richard Litman. After finishing college at 15, he graduated from law school with honors. He has two advanced law degrees, including a LL.M. in Patent & Trade Regulation Law from The National Law Center of George Washington University, where he was the Food and Drug Law Institute Fellow.

He has been a lawyer for 30 years. His expertise is in protecting and profiting from Intellectual Property (patents, trademarks and copyrights), and in business formation, growth and financing. He has the highest peer review rating: he is AV rated in the Martindale-Hubbell Law Directory and is in the Bar Register of Preeminent Lawyers.

Over 25 years ago he formed Litman Law, an Intellectual Property law firm strategically located near the U.S. Patent & Trademark Office (USPTO). Guided by his vision, the law firm’s clients include universities, companies of all sizes, and innovators from around the world. The staff includes many former U.S. Patent Examiners. The Chief Patent Officer was a USPTO Director.

He is Chairman of Freedom Bank of Virginia, a commercial bank in the U.S. national capital region. He was a director of two other banks that were acquired.

He was Chair of the Patent Committee and Co-Chair of the Intellectual Property Law Section of the DC Bar (Washington, DC) and Chair of the Economic Development Commission in Arlington, VA.

He was commended by U.S. President Clinton for his "deep concern about our future" and his "exemplary commitment to improving our world." He is in Who’s Who in America, Who’s Who in American Law, Who’s Who in Finance and Industry, and Who’s Who in the World.

He is Managing Director of AIM (Arabic Innovation Management). AIM helps governments, universities and research centers with economic development locally and through proactive global technology transfer. Its focus is on relationships involving the MENA region..

Richard Litman’s Specialties:

Intellectual Property Protection, Licensing and Commercialization; Technology Transfer for Universities and Research Centers; Formation and Growth of Technology Companies; Debt and Equity Financing.
