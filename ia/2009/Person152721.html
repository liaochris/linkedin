Mark Stubbs’s Summary

Hardware Engineer with experience in custom ASIC design, embedded system design, PCB design, firmware, and some application software design. Interested in short term contracts to develop custom devices. Currently working full time with Bibaja, LLC to develop next generation irrigation and landscape lighting controls.

Mark Stubbs’s Specialties:

ASIC design using Verilog and Synopsys, custom PCB design using Eagle Layout Editor including component selection and designing for low cost high volume applications, Plastic enclosure specification and development, Inventory Management and Purchasing, Firmware development using assembly, C, and application development in C, PERL, and TCL/TK

Mark Stubbs’s Experience

  • Owner

    Bibaja Landscape Automation, LLC

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Computer Hardware industry)

    June 2002 — Present (7 years 6 months)

    Developing irrigation and landscape lighting control systems. Bibaja was founded to create intelligent irrigation systems using power line communications technology (LonWorks based) to monitor a network of sensors and reduce water waste by delivering only the water required.

  • Sr. Engineer

    Echelon Corp.

    (Public Company; 51-200 employees; ELON; Computer Hardware industry)

    December 1997 — January 2006 (8 years 2 months)

    ASIC Designer for Power Line Communications (PLT-22, PL 3120, PL 3150) modem involving DSP algorithm development. Also responsible for migrating an 8-bit microcontroller design from schematic to Verilog RTL and adding features to create the "Smart Transceiver" line of products. Echelon LonWorks FieldBus, Neuron C programming, LonMaker, LNS.

  • Engineer

    AMD

    (Public Company; Computer Hardware industry)

    November 1996 — December 1997 (1 year 2 months)

    ASIC Engineer, worked on North Bridge design for AMD K6 with Socket 7, SDRAM 100, AGP and PCI interfaces.

  • Engineer

    Apple Computer

    Worked on various Power Macintosh designs from the 8500/9500 series where I helped verify the PCI bridge and developed the PCI Arbiter. Moved on to work with PCI South Bridge, integrating various IP cores with Apple's DBDMA architecture for other PowerPC Macs.
