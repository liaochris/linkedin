Benjy Myaz’s Summary

The vision of My’ Werks Music/Productions is to become Jamaica’s premier, professional and creative recording company within the music industry. Our mission is to initiate programmes which will promote the understanding and appreciation of music, in all its forms. We also intend to assist children with learning disabilities. A large percentage of these children are musically inclined; and in the Jamaican context, the resources are extremely limited. Sponsorship and grants should be offered to these less fortunate children in our society, whose parents/guardians cannot afford to cater to their special needs. "In November 2000, I had the privilege, as Musical Director & Bassist for Jimmy Cliff , to perform as part of a humanitarian concert, "World Food Day", hosted by the the United Nations at James Bond Beach, Oracabessa, St. Mary, Jamaica, W.I. International acts, such as: Miriam Makeba, Hugh Masekela (South Africa), Gilberto (Brazil), Sounds Of Blackness (USA), & Jimmy Cliff (Jamaica) - to name a few - were some of the performers at this star-studded, benefit concert in order to raise funds for the purchase of seeds for starving children around the globe. While we were eating back stage, the reality of starving kids impacted on me. Then, and there, I made the committment to get more involved, even as I was preparing to pursue my solo career." The Save Darfur movement have become my passion for many years. "It is my intention to donate funds from my new CD album, "You've Got Me" The Jamaica Autism Support Association (J.A.S.A.) & UNICIEF to assist where we can."
