Stephen Cleary’s Summary

o Created The Lifestyle Performance Category. As the first company that combines thermal and lifestyle-clothing categories for snowsports enthusiasts, Eesa created a new way to combine two separate and distinct categories.

o Alternative Marketing. Strategic use of low-cost, high-impact viral and guerilla marketing.
• Eesa has created brand awareness through the use of it’s direct mailing campaign to targeted lifestyle retail accounts in North America which rapidly grew virally to include consumers requesting stickers and information about Eesa. Eesa capitalized on the viral buzz by creating and maintaining sites on Myspace and Facebook, which in turn created more global viral buzz around the brand.
• Eesa has also capitalized on guerilla marketing at events such as the US Open (attracting 10,000+ spectators) and the Empire Shakedown an industry event held in Quebec (attracting 5,000+ spectators). By attending these events and distributing promotional items such as stickers, scarves, bandanas and socks, Eesa has created brand awareness with its key demographic in the action snowsports market.
• Eesa also possesses strong presence at the three key ski & snowboard summer camps: Windell’s at Mount Hood, Camp of Champs at Whistler and at South American Snow Sessions in South America.

o Brand Credibility. The industry experience of the principal includes management and execution of product development, marketing and sales. In knowing that the founder came from Burton Snowboards and launched Anon optics, market participants immediately recognize brand credibility.

o Management’s Proximity to the Market. Eesa’s executive is a pioneer-era snowboarder and action sports enthusiast who has actively participated in the evolution of the sport, culture and business over the last 20 years. The result is management that is better equipped to make decisions well-suited for the market.

Stephen Cleary’s Experience

  • Owner

    eesa

    (Apparel & Fashion industry)

    November 2004 — Present (4 years 10 months)

    eesa lifestyle layering was created to remove the shackles of the action-sports industries incredibly static first-layer offerings. If you’re still rocking a long-sleeve cotton-T and hooded sweatshirt when you ride; eesa is the brand for you. eesa makes design-driven innerwear and apparel with a sophisticated focus on functional fabrics engineered with your style in mind. The results are lux-layering pieces that can be worn from Snowflake–To–Beachbreak. eesa’s fall/winter collection hits select specialty snowboard shops this fall in the US, Canada, Europe and Japan, in addition to online distribution through eesa.com

    Founded on 11.11.04, eesa is the brainchild of former Burton Snowboards anon optics manager, Stephen Cleary, who along with eesa enablers are creating the lifestyle performance category. For stickers and free random eesa shit please e-mail enabler@eesaclothing.com

  • Manager

    Burton Snowboards

    (Privately Held; 201-500 employees; Sporting Goods industry)

    June 1997 — October 2003 (6 years 5 months)

    As a founder of Learn-To-Ride, Mr. Cleary was instrumental in turning an industry failure rate of 90% to a success rate of 30% through product innovation.
    Mr. Cleary developed and launched Anon optics, a new snowboard goggle company for Burton Snowboards. Mr. Cleary was hired as a Manager responsible for all product development, timelines, budgets and P & L statements as they pertained to the brand. In addition, Mr. Cleary was responsible for all marketing and sales efforts on a global basis, including timelines, budgets, marketing campaigns, sales pricing, programs, discounts, delivery, warranty and re-order business. During his tenure, Anon optics grew from a $2.2 million to a $3.9 million global company, with limited resources in a highly saturated and competitive goggle industry. During this time Mr. Cleary managed yearly budgets in excess of $1.5 million.
    He holds three snowboard goggle patents.
