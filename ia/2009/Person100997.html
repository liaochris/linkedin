Jackie Honikman’s Summary

Jackie Honikman has always had a real passion for creating meaningful and entertaining interactive experiences. What started as just interacting with an audience in a theater has grown to include work for Fortune 50 companies, engaging international audiences online. As a Project Manager she has spent most of her time developing new creative strategies and Rich Media solutions. In the last year, Jackie’s has managed projects for ABC, Harpo, Comcast Networks, Warner Bros., Microsoft, and Target.

Like many in the interactive industry, Jackie comes from a somewhat eclectic background. Before Schematic, she was one of the original ten employees of Gorilla Nation Media where she helped orchestrate the first online ad campaign for The Lord of the Rings. Prior to her years online, she was a writers’ assistant to screenwriters Dana Fox (The Wedding Date) and Allison Burnett (Autumn in New York and Resurrecting the Champ). Jackie has also worked as a freelance graphic/Web designer and was the Creative Director of the Berkeley Jewish Journal.

Jackie has studied comedy at The Groundlings and the Upright Citizens Brigade in Los Angeles where she still performs every Monday night. In January 2007, Jackie finished the 8-week run of her one-woman satire JACKIE LIVE at the Working Stage Theater in Hollywood, which was produced by Rachael Leigh Cook. The production was described as “A clever original” and “entertaining and imaginative” by Variety and LAWeekly respectively.

Jackie is an award-winning speaker, a member of Mensa, and was Santa Barbara's Young Woman of the Year in 2001. She received her BA in 2005 (Dean’s Honor List all semesters) from the UCLA School of Theater, Film, and Television.
