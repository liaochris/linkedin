Tom Brennan’s Summary

Tom started with technology in 1986 as a SYSOP for a 516/BBS and writing code for fun when Pascal and CP/M was cool <grin>. He has over a decade of cross-platform hands-on work in Application, Network and Wireless Security as a employee and consultant in industries such as DoD, Government, Banking, Entertainment & Manufacturing worldwide.

Tom holds industry certifications including: Certified Information Systems Security Professional (CISSP #54039), National Security Agency, INFOSEC Assessment Methodology (NSA-IAM), Certified Ethical Hacker (C|EH), ISACA Certified Information Security Manager (CISM) #518267 and is a member of ISSA #602855. In addition, he also holds many product focused certifications for products and tools that he proven his experience with building compartmentalized mousetraps.

He volunteers his spare cycles as a Board Member to the OWASP Foundation (www.owasp.org). He is part of the team at WhiteHat Security (www.whitehatsec.com) a leader in web application security assessment for some of the largest brands on the internet.

Tom has been featured as a subject matter expert for the Wall Street Journal, NBC & ABC News in NYC, USA Today, CMP Media/Dark Reading and others on Information Security. He has demonstrated vendor agnostic technical content as a speaker before the DHS/FBI Infragard, ISSA, OWASP, SC World Congress, ISACA, Shmoocon, 2600's Hackers on Planet Earth (HOPE) and before the United Nations.

Can be reached the old fashion way by calling 973-795-1046
