Don Crocker’s Experience

  • CEO

    Support Center

    (Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    1998 — Present (12 years )

    Don is the Executive Director/CEO of the Support Center - a nonprofit Management Support Organization - providing professional development training, executive transition management, and consulting services, including turnaround services to the nonprofit sector.

    Don is nationally recognized as a high-energy facilitator, public speaker, trainer and consultant in the areas of nonprofit leadership, board performance, and executive transition.

    He has raised millions of dollars for nonprofit organizations Don has been an associate consultant to BoardSource, and has been trained in facilitation through the American Management Association.

    Don has served on the selection committee for the New York Times Nonprofit Excellence Awards.

  • Adjunct Professor

    NYU Heyman Center for Philanthropy and Fundraising

    (Education Management industry)

    April 2008 — February 2009 (11 months)

    ROLE OF THE NONPROFIT ORGANIZATION IN SOCIETY
    (Spring 2009 Course)

    The nonprofit sector is a major economic and social force. In New York alone, it is the fastest-growing source of jobs and will most likely shoulder much of the responsibility for closing the city’s multi billion-dollar budget gap. Explore the history of organizing, community building, and philanthropy in America;responses of nonprofits to 21st-century social, political, and economic changes;motivations for giving; ethics—expectations, violations, and regulations; management efficiency; realization of goals;and the future of the nonprofit sector. Veterans and newcomers become more efficient and effective organizers and leaders of nonprofits.

  • Executive Director/CEO

    Alliance for Disabled in Action/State Association of Centers for Independent Living, NJ

    (Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    1993 — 1998 (5 years )

    Responsible for the leadership and operation of both the State Association and the local center. State budget for CILs grew from $6 million to $11million during this time and the Alliance budget grew from $350K to $ 800K during this period. Activities and services include local, statewide and national advocacy, peer support services, I&R, and Home Health Aide services.

  • Executive Director

    Midwood Development Corporation

    (Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    1985 — 1993 (8 years )

    Responsible leadership and operation of the local development corporation. Expanded annual funding, expanded the number and type of funding sources, expanded after-school programs from three sites to seven sites and expanded the number of employees from 45 to more than 125. Agency activities included housing, economic development and crime prevention programs, youth, crime prevention and anti-drug efforts, and local and State-wide advocacy for community development.
