Hal Hassall’s Summary

As vice president and general manager, Hassall helped launch MOR Music Television, the first 24-hour music video-based home shopping channel, reaching 20 million U.S. television households. After working in the information technology and television industries, he made the jump to the Internet in 1994 with the launch of MORMusic.com, one of the first e-commerce sites for recorded music on the Web. In 1999, he joined Country.com (now CMT.com) as manager of e-commerce to create an Internet e-commerce business unit within CBS Cable and later MTV Networks.

While at MTV networks, he was involved in music programming and talent relations for digital platforms, in addition to overseeing e-commerce, search engine optimization, and promotion to expand CMT’s brand presence on digital platforms. Later his involvement expanded to include CMT/MTV Networks music download service, URGE; CMT’s Mobile video; and CMT.com’s broadband channel, CMT LOADED.

During late 2007, Hal joined the echo where he leads the Marketing Services Group. His team is composed of four interrelated disciplines which enable entertainment brands to attract and monetize their digital audiences: Ecommerce, Advertising & Promotion, Street & Viral Marketing and online Community Development (website and social network publishing and fan management). Clients include: Beyonce', Rascal Flatts, Keith Urban, Diddy, Brooks & Dunn, Yanni, Sesame Street Live, Faith Hill, Dolly Parton, Kelly Clarkson, Kellie Pickler, Sugarland, Alicia Keys, and Kanye West.

digital marketing, brand management, mobile, web, consumer behavior, online marketing, digital content creation, digital content distribution, entertainment industry relations, search engine optimization (SEO), search engine marketing (SEM), public speaking, training and development, strategic planning, business development, online advertising, loyalty marketing, sweepstakes and promotions.
