  • Ammado.com

    (Internet industry)

    November 2007 — Present (1 year 4 months)

It connects nonprofits, socially responsible companies and engaged individuals dedicated to positive change on a global and local level. ammado was founded as a mission-based, for-profit enterprise in Dublin by serial entrepreneur Peter Conlon and Dr. Anna Kupka.

  • Draiocht LLC

    (Broadcast Media industry)

    February 2004 — Present (5 years 1 month)

    In 2004 I set up my own LLC as well as to handle consulting work as well as to hold my collection of small web properties. After resigning as a fulltime Wohler employee, I continued with Wohler as a consultant undertaking business development, market research, marketing, management & product consultancy during a period of great transition in Wohler which included many tumultuous events such as both moving, and selling the company. Draiocht is on hold right now as I'm busy with ammado.

  • CoolTouch Monitors

    (Privately Held; Broadcast Media industry)

    August 2006 — June 2007 (11 months)

    Having worked with Jose Guillen at Wohler in the past, I choose to work with him again in CoolTouch Monitors, a startup LCD monitor manufacturer that he founded in 2005. My primary roles at CoolTouch were to handle international business development by creating an international dealership network, to handle marketing and promotion with the media, and to help provide product development guidance. I became a victim of my own success - due to growth in the business my presence was needed more and more at CoolTouch’s base in Los Angeles. I am not a fan of L.A. and wasn’t willing to move there, but I knew that I needed to be there so I chose to leave CoolTouch.

  • Wohler Technologies, Inc.

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Broadcast Media industry)

    November 2000 — September 2004 (3 years 11 months)

    I joined Wohler with the initial role of fixing up their website, and this evolved through various titles to finish at Director of Marketing. When I joined, Wohler was engineering lead and had no marketing team, virtually no printed collateral materials, and significant inconsistencies between what was designed, built and sold. As I was introducing our dealer network to a wide variety of products they did not even know existed, it also served to concentrate minds on product inconsistencies as I formed bridges between Sales, Engineering and the market at large. I was responsible for producing the tradeshows, marketing, brochures and datasheets and the advertising. As we grew, I was tasked with setting up and managing a growing team to assist and collaborate with me in handling Wohler’s global marketing. I also assisted with a variety of other business areas such as working on conceiving new products, and working to keep everyone on the same product script.
