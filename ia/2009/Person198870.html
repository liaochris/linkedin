Ride-Away Handicap Equipment Corp.’s Summary

Starting in 1986, Ride-Away began modifying vehicles for people with disabilities and quickly became the dominant leader of the vehicle modification business. From the early stages of carpeting and paneling to full-service wheelchair lifts and ramps, raised and lowered floor van conversions, and full primary and secondary control modifications, Ride-Away has been a pioneer in the vehicle modification industry and continues to lead this growing and emerging market.

We built our industry-leading position on the expertise of our employees, the commitment of management and the passion of our owner. By adhering to Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standards (FMVSS), becoming a Quality Assurance Program (QAP) facility and creating the Adaptive Driving Alliance (ADA), Ride-Away improves the quality of life for people with disabilities everyday and with every turn of the odometer. Today, we are the largest provider of vehicles for people with disabilities in the United States with offices from Maine to Florida. The company contributes over 10% of its earnings to non-profit organizations and has an 85% employee volunteerism rate in charitable activities.

Our goal is to deliver you freedom and provide you with the best service that this or any other industry has to offer. We want to get you back on the road in a safe and timely manner, so you and your loved ones can enjoy the freedom of the road.

Ride-Away Handicap Equipment Corp.’s Specialties:

We specialize in modifying wheelchair and scooter accessible vehicles from Braun & VMI, as well as installing adaptive equipment for people with disabilities from manufacturers such as Bruno, EMC, Sure-Lok, & MPD.
