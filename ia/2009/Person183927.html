Paul Wozney’s Summary

Network Experience

My experience is grounded in both theory and in practical use; I am able to recommend, design and configure stable and cost-effective solutions for any network requirement. I have experience designing campus networks from both green-field, and logical/physical redesigns of existing network systems. I have experience examining WAN providers and documenting a selection process for vendors. I have implemented STP, VLANs and VTP to great effect in campus network designs in the mining industry. My routing experience is primarily with Cisco’s EIGRP, and with BGP; I have managed a network with both protocols active, passing and filtering network routing updates between protocols. I have hands on experience with the administration of Cisco VoIP hardware.

Application Experience

I have implemented enterprise capable wireless systems, and tested this hardware with security systems like 802.1x. I have worked primarily with switching and routing, and I have a good knowledge of integration with higher-layer protocols like SMTP, SMB, and NIC-teaming. I have designed network systems for load-balancing hardware, and implemented these designs into what are now production systems. I have designed and administrated Checkpoint firewall systems and Cisco PIX and ASA products, and I have lab experience with Cisco’s IDS products.

Troubleshooting Experience

I have an intimate knowledge of lower layer routing and switching protocols, and I am very comfortable with using protocol decoders to assist with network troubleshooting in a complex environment. My troubleshooting ability stems from an ability to see problems in their entire scope, and to come at problems from an outside-of-the-box mentality. I am a constant learner, and if in any situation I find my understanding lacking for a particular protocol, piece of hardware or software, I thoroughly enjoy delving into the details of systems to gain a higher understanding.
