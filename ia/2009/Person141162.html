Linda Arroz’s Summary

I am a former magazine editor and freelance writer, as well as a former Hollywood stylist and TV personality. All of these professions actually connect in Los Angeles. I would write an article, turn it into a TV segment concept, pitch and produce the segment, and appear as the expert.
Armed and dangerous with a certificate in management from General Motors, I worked in management and later as a spot welder at the largest assembly division under one roof in the country.
I networked myself into a three year contract as a spokesmodel with the Spiegel catalog...
Under the tutelage of Jody Donahue and Associates, whose PR firm had the account, I learned the essence of a good public relations campaign and the nuts and bolts to generate good results. Traveling the country, I learned to package and pitch the story, produce and style the segment, as well as be the "talent" and get the plugs in in a very effective but subtle way. That led to writing and styling assignments in Los Angeles and Beverly Hills, where I became a magazine editor, heading up two specialty fashion magazines. An advertiser lured me away and I became the marketing director for a multi-million dollar "department store" brand. I produced all the photo shoots, the monthly training video for the 50+ in store merchandisers, wrote copy for advertisements and the catalog, did numerous in store events, and managed the public relations for the two special size divisions- petite and plus. Two years later, I went back to styling, working on over 150 commercials and a few films as well as TV appearances, with over 450 daytime talk and news shows to date. And a large percentage of them I pitched, produced and styled as well.
I am now a principal at Makeover Media, a think tank consulting firm offering public relations, business development and project management services in upscale lifestyle and entertainment.
