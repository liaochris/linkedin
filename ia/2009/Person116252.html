Joey Nerenberg’s Summary

My utlimate goal is to see the "lightbulb go off". At the end of it all, I want my clients to see the value of something, then take it to their daily life to make it a bit better!

Where does this come from? In my many years in large corporations, I was lucky enough to have managers who allowed me to fail and learn! I was also lucky enough to move across the business spectrum, learning not only the skills, but the business - I walked in cold to each new job, but was able to rise to the task. This holistic experience provides the insight - and skill - to create solutions, big and small, that focus on both the individual while providing profit for the organization!

What about the culinary! Mid-life crisis or savvy business move? Actually, a bit of both. Turns out - no surprise - working a kitchen is the ultra-compressed version of almost any business or organizational project, requiring the same techniques and skills.

So what do I do now? Run a company or two, pop-out a speech here and there, just to get people excited, and always looking for the company or person that understands the value of moving people forward, not boxing them into the traditonal. Oh, yes, on the horizon are books, blog and better tools to help you along your road to success and sanity.
