China's foreign trade has grown faster than its GDP for the past 25 years. China's growth came from the private sector instead of exports.   • Leader / Chef

    Social Credit Party of Canada / Parti Credit Social du Canada

    (Political Organization industry)

    January 2006 — June 2008 (2 years 6 months)

    Social Credit governed Alberta from 1935 to 1971. During this time: the Social Credit government eliminated Alberta's debt; established the Credit Union Act and Alberta Treasury Branches to provide low-interest loans; kept Alberta debt-free and income tax-free; and encouraged local ownership and control of hospitals, schools and utilities.

    In British Columbia, Social Credit continued as government, with an election held every 3 years, until 1972. During this time BC Hydro was developed from B.C. Electric, the B.C. Ferry fleet was established, the Universities of Victoria and Simon Fraser and BCIT and regional technical schools and colleges were developed throughout the province. The highway system was expanded and upgraded; Hospital insurance and Medicare became realities; the Columbia River Treaty and the Bennett and Keenlyside Dams were built and the whole province prospered.

    Socreds have been known as the Ralliement des Creditistes in Quebec. Socrial Credit elected as many as 30 M.P.s to the federal House of Commons and had one Senator, Ernst Manning, father of Reform Party Leader Hon. Preston Manning.

    Social Credit ideas have spread from England to Canada, Australia, New Zealand, and South Africa.

    The founder of Social Credit, C.H. Douglas, presented this idea as early as 1917. Keynes actually referred somewhat favorably to Douglas's basic idea in the General Theory. The Social Credit movement still exists in Canada, Australia, and New Zealand.
