Dr. Oren Fuerst’s Summary

Dr. Fuerst brings over 20 years of experience, combining a career as a senior strategy and valuation advisor to Fortune 500 and other companies and investors, an academic lecturer and researcher, an investor and entrepreneur.

He is the Managing Director of Strategic Models, an advisory and investment boutique. He was the initiator and the co-director of the Technology Valuation Executive Program at Columbia Business School, where he also lectured on technology and global strategy and valuation. Former faculty at Yale University School of Management and Yale International Center for Finance, focusing on International Valuation and Management Systems.

Oren is the inventor of numerous software and medical devices innovations which were commercialized by leading companies, including technologies acquired by Becton Dickinson (NYSE:BDX).

Dr. Fuerst is an investor in numerous technology companies, and is also the co-founder of Tevel Angel club, a NY based angel club focused on Israeli technology companies. He founded Imagine Global, a not for profit for the adoption of healthcare technologies to disadvantaged communities.

He was with the advisory group of KPMG Israel and a feature columnist for leading financial newspapers (including the Financial Times, and the Israeli newspapers Globes and Haaretz). He was a commander in the elite Technology unit of the Israeli Defense Forces.

Oren is the author and co-author of numerous publications, including the books "From Concept to Wall Street" (Financial Times- Prentice Hall, 2003) , “Startup Companies and Venture Capital” (Tel Aviv University Press, 2001), "Mastering Investments" (FT,2003) and the online course "e-Valuation"(Columbia University Digital Ventures, 2003).
Holds B.A. degrees in Economics and Accounting (Tel-Aviv University, dual Magna Cum Laude), and a Masters and Ph.D. from Columbia Business School, on the topics and linkages of game theory, valuation, public offerings and global equity markets.
