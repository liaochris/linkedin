Geska Helena Andersson’s Summary

A kaospilot and cineaste, who works as creative director and producer within the arts, film and media. Currently working for the Interactive Institute (http://www.tii.se) – a national Swedish R&D institute, performing interdisciplinary research and innovation within the field of digital media. Together with Robert Brecevic, she has embarked on a three-year exploration of artistic R&D under the name PERFORMING PICTURES, where she acts a creative partner and producer. Her interest lies in exploring possible new grammars and syntaxes for responsive movies, and to work with moving imagery in relation to “elasticity” in narrative forms.

Geska has a degree in film and drama from the University of Lund and Stockholm University alongside with studies in philosophy, modern literature, ethnology and women studies. She is also trained in entrepreneurship and leadership, focusing on the professional spheres of creativity, innovation and intercultural understanding at the experimental Kaospilot University in Aarhus, Denmark and San Francisco, US.

Prior experiences include work as cultural editor, film and drama critic and stage- and costume designer. Teacher within the Imagineering programme at K3, Malmö University. In a commercial context Geska has gained extensive experience within the field on new media and worked with research as well as management and creation at Scandvision Mediahouse and at Moonwalk Sthlm. Project-based work include for example the Exit99 Outdoor Performing Arts Festival, the contemporary dance company ccap - cristina caprioli artificial experience (http://www.ccap.se/), the Swedish Educational Broadcasting Company and others. Geska has been working with the Interactive Institute since 2001, most recently as director of the Trans-Reality Game Lab – an arts research laboratory specialising in the development of new game forms and technologies integrating virtual and physical modes of game play.

  • Performing Pictures, Art & Technology Program, Interactive Institute

    (Non-Profit; 51-200 employees; Research industry)

    January 2006 — Present (3 years 3 months)

    Performing Pictures is Geska Helena Andersson and Robert Brečević – a Stockholm-based artist duo, working in the area of moving images and new technologies for media delivery as part of the Interactive Institute. In our artistic practice we explore and develop responsive film art from a number of formative limitations in time and space.

    In order to create participatory and responsive art works, we design and implement systems for digital video play out. Included functionalities are realtime editing as well as realtime compositing of full motion video (single- and/or multi-channelled) - control mechanisms are interfaced by sensorial data, computer vision or other input.

    During four years of production, Performing Pictures have developed methodologies for catering public spaces with responsive video experiences. What we deliver is a set of tools, conceptual as well as technical, for the production and distribution of responsive/reactive works.
