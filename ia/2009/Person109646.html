Jennifer Kushnier’s Summary

After nearly 10 years in publishing, I decided to make a radical career change. Partly, I found it increasingly difficult to be passionate in a passionless environment; partly, I had achieved what I'd set out to achieve in the industry (short of having Sonny Mehta's job).

Though I find myself doing the odd freelance project from time to time (I've been fortunate to have a wonderful connection who's given me the opportunity to pen a few recipes for two books.), I did manage to finish my culinary degree. I envisioned myself as either a personal chef and/or the owner of a catering company or sweet shop, with the goal of following the farm-to-table approach as much as possible and being 100% organic, down to the cleaning products I use on my tools & equipment.

Turns out I didn't stray too far from that mark, having landed at one of PA's premiere farm-to-table restaurants. Every day's a new adventure: What fruit will the farmer deliver? Will I make a tatin, a cobbler, or a sorbet out of it? Mmmmm..... I feel like I have to pinch myself everyday just to be sure I'm not sleep-baking. Who knew a job could be so rewarding and absolutely lovely?

Jennifer Kushnier’s Specialties:

As an editor, my specialties included relationships, pop psych, cookbooks, pets, health & wellness, and general nonfiction. I'm most adept at helping authors bring clarity to their texts using their own voices (as opposed to my rewriting their verse).

As a cook, I'm best known for my desserts (seasonal pies, in particular), but I can whip up a pretty mean soup or lasagna. I'm also the go-to person for cooking emergencies, off-the-cuff substitution suggestions, and simple kitchen tricks.
