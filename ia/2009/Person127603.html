Kathleen Janson’s Experience

  • Executive Director & CEO

    Janson Group/Janson PR

    (Marketing and Advertising industry)

    February 2000 — Present (9 years 6 months)

    Kathleen founded Janson PR in 2000, a results-driven practice with a strong and focused commitment to creatively executing on business communication strategies, high-velocity visibility and corporate social responsibility (CSR) for entrepreneurial- spirited companies. In late 2008 she joined forces with long-time associate Andrew Janson to form Janson Group.

    With a shrewd eye for business, branding and communications, Kathleen has successfully served as vice president of global corporate communications for several publicly traded companies, including multi-billion dollar technology distribution giant, Ingram Micro.

    Throughout her career, she has assembled award-winning professional teams, and successfully created and implemented strategic corporate communications plans for companies ranging from start-ups to Fortune 50 in various markets. She is recognized for her proven ability to boost worldwide awareness and reach key infuencers for her clients spanning the technology, political, publishing, healthcare, environment, education and non-profit markets.

    Kathleen brings a wealth of corporate experience to the group following more than 25 years in communications and general management, as well as hands-on knowledge of the administration of corporate foundations.

    Kathleen is personally active in the advocacy/non-profit sector, and proudly serves on the board of Mercy House Living Centers, providing housing and services to the homeless, and is chair of the organization’s annual fundraising event. She also provides strategic communications services for Break the Barriers, Children’s Hospital of Orange County (CHOC) and Soka University of America, among others.
