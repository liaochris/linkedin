Stephen James Joyce’s Summary

Stephen James Joyce is an accomplished bestselling author, coach and speaker on the topics of collaborative intelligence, collaborative leadership and employee retention.

Stephens’s overall goal is to be a more effective human being. He sees no difference in the importance of personal and professional development, each enhances the other.

A PROFESSIONAL SPEAKER – has delivered hundreds of presentations across Canada, USA and in Europe. His clients include the Project Management Institute (PMI), Canadian Marketing Institute (CMI), Ideas Arabia Conference and Public Relations Professionals Conference.

A WRITER/ AUTHOR – the author of the best seller 'Teaching an Anthill to Fetch: Developing Collaborative Intelligence @ Work' and many articles for professional journals and newsletters. His book is written that anyone in any part of the organization can read and understand - this makes it useful for any organization that wants a common message / attitude to be distributed across the employee's population.

A CONSULTANT – has acted as the 'resiliency content expert' for one of Canada largest health care regions. He has also redesigned and implemented the overhaul of a customer service program for a large asset management company. He is always looking for the practical application of knowledge rather than just knowledge for knowledge's sake. He walks his talk - i.e. He talks about changing the way we relate to knowledge and the effects this can have on our creativity and flexibility as a species. He backed this up by publishing his book under a creative commons licence.

A FACILITATOR: has extensive experience in group facilitation. Long (3 days +) intensive events as well as 2-4 hour sessions. Has planned and executed events for groups in excess of 700.

CERTIFIED TRAINER: using methods and processes from many areas including; improv, group dynamics, NLP, accelerated learning, communication theory, psychotherapy, story-telling and visual facilitation.
