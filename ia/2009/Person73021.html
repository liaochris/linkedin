Emmanuel Bresson’s Summary

Currently I work for governmental teams as a research engineer in cryptography. I am involved in the design, validation, analysis of cryptographic algorithms and in charge of securely integrating them into soft/hard components (programs, libraries, chips, OS, networks). More specifically, I developed expertise around public-key cryptography, digital signatures, PKI and X509 certificates, pseudo-random number generators (PRNGs), anonymity and identification over wireless networks, VoIP security, cryptographic libraries. As an illustrative example, I worked on the specifications of BAC & EAC passport. For security products, I made evaluations of Protection Profiles (PPs), Security Targets (STs), according to the Common Criteria (CC) (ISO/IEC 15408). I am also familiar with ISO 27000. For risk analysis, I use the French scheme EBIOS.

In parallel, I also maintain an strong academic research activity, and have published my works in tens of journal and international conference papers. I have participated to many international committees and have been invited in various international workshops and seminars. My favorite topics are about cryptpographic schemes for GKE (group key exchange), signatures, provable security, multi-party protocols and hash functions.

As additional activities, I work as an expert for the French ANR (Agence Nationale pour la Recherche) in order to evaluate projects to be funded by the agency. I also teach in the university of Paris XII (master level), in the European student exchange program "Athens" (research level) and in seminars for professionals.

Before this, I received my engineer degrees from Ecole polytechnique in 1998, and my research degree at University of Paris VI and completed my PhD at ENS (Ecole normale supérieure) in 2002. In addition to cryptography, I got mastering information theory, complexity and probabilities (Markov, stochastic processes, martingales, ...) and topics like PDE, statistical tests and optimization.
