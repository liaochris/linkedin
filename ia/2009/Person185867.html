Peter Macaulay’s Summary

Peter is passionate about the ability of technology and in particular the Internet to enhance many aspects of our lives, and works to make it happen.
The NZ Digital Strategy fits this vision, and Peter became the initial Programme Manager. From 2003 to 2005 Peter was Executive Director of InternetNZ. In July 2007 Peter was elected President of InternetNZ.
Peter joined IDC in November 2008 to develop the End User Practice and in particular the inTEP program.

Moving from aircraft engineering into Air NZ Computer Services, Peter became an analyst programmer in 1972. This technical background is the foundation for management, sales and marketing skills. Generalization has allowed Peter to work internationally in roles as broad as developer of operating system components, through to CIO of one of the world’s largest electronics distributors.
Peter has an interest in intellectual property, and was a founder of the NZ Business Software alliance. To emphasize a commitment to the industry Peter served on the board of ITANZ (The Information Technology Association), and was elected president in 1997.
Trevor Eagle and Peter Macaulay formed The No1 IT Group as a subsidiary of Eagle Technology in 1983, and it became an industry leader in software distribution and technical service. The business provided award winning support.
The technical support division was sold in 1998, leaving Peter clear to run the consulting unit.
Outside of ICT, Peter's personal interests include Christianity, motorcycles, rugby, music, film, reading, food, real ale, boats, fishing,and almost anything that involves risky combinations of velocity, wetness and altitude.
Peter is married to Diana since 1968, with adult children.
Following the death of Matthew, their 26 year old son in the Paddington rail disaster in October 1999, Peter and Diana moved to the UK to focus on the issues of safety and corporate accountability arising from the disaster. They returned to NZ in 2003.
