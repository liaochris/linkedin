Edward Mandla’s Summary

From leading Technology companies, my Presidency of the Australian Computer Society, managing Alt-U Team and Talent Solutions, running for Parliament, continuing involvement in the Liberal Party, Chairing and participating in government panels and work groups, and my popular weekly radio show, I have lots of experience and you would hope I'm not boring!

My passion is sitting down with executives and helping them reach their goals and raise their revenue performance. Whether its planning executive careers, raising funds, helping executives to better understand the motivations and weakness of their staff, training them and their staff or helping with my leading strategies to secure the best talent not looking for work, my team and I continue to help top executives and love doing it.

  • Past President

    Australian Computer Society

    (Non-Profit; 51-200 employees; Information Technology and Services industry)

    November 2003 — Present (5 years 6 months)

    Established in 1966, the ACS is the representative body for ICT (information and Communications Technologies) professionals in Australia with over 15,000 members. A member of the Australian Council of Professions, the ACS is the public voice of the ICT profession and the guardian of professional ethics, education and standards in the ICT industry, with a commitment to the wider community to ensure the beneficial use of ICT.

    The principal object of the society is to foster the growth of ICT resources in Australia for the benefit of members, the profession and the nation. The ACS has branches in each state, regional chapters and a national head office in Sydney. The organisation has 35 paid staff, a large number of contractors/consultants and 200+ volunteers.
