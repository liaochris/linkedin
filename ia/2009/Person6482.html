Alastair Upton’s Experience

  • General Manager, Broadband/Components

    RFMD

    (Public Company; Semiconductors industry)

    November 2007 — Present (1 year 11 months)

    General manager of a high performance business unit with product lines in broadband and fiber (cable TV infrastructure, RF passives, fiber optics), ISM band transceivers and general purpose integrated RF components.

  • Managing Director

    RF Micro Devices (RFMD)

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; Semiconductors industry)

    April 2005 — November 2007 (2 years 8 months)

    Performed strategic marketing for the cellular products group, coordinating the market research, market models and strategic direction for the group. Helped establish RFMD design centers in Shanghai, China and Hyderabad, India. Led two successful proposals for LTE RF and GPS systems. Assigned to the due-diligence team that resulted in the acquisition of Sirenza Microdevices, announced on August 13, 2007.

  • General Manager, Digital Cellular Products

    RF Micro Devices (RFMD)

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; RFMD; Semiconductors industry)

    April 1999 — April 2005 (6 years 1 month)

    General manager of a product line developing RF components and chipsets for cellular handsets. Transformed the group from developing CDMA components to complete GSM/EDGE chipsets. Led the team that created the Polaris brand at RFMD which sold over 100 million chipsets and helped RFMD become a $1B revenue company.

  • Product Manager

    RF Micro Devices (RFMD)

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; Semiconductors industry)

    August 1997 — April 1999 (1 year 9 months)

    Started as the first product manager at RFMD with a focus on CDMA RFICs and ISM band transceivers. Coordinated most of the non-custom prototype products through assembly, test and product launch. Led the successful proposal development for a dual-band CDMA LNA/mixer for a leading handset manufacturer.

  • Program Manager

    Lockheed Martin

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; LMT; Defense & Space industry)

    September 1991 — August 1997 (6 years)

    Group leader of a team developing solid-state phased array T/R modules for Navy and Army applications. Worked on numerous proposals (GBR, MAFET, and other classified programs, interacting with large multi-company teams).

    (Note: While I was in this position GE announced it sold the Aerospace business to Martin Marietta on November 24, 1992 and Martin Marietta announced it would merge with Lockheed on August 30, 1994. Here I saw good and bad parts of mergers and acquisitions.)

  • Microwave Engineer

    GE Aerospace

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; GE; Renewables & Environment industry)

    August 1983 — September 1991 (8 years 2 months)

    GaAs MMIC design engineer specializing in low noise receivers (LNAs and mixers). Designed microwave (X-band) and millimeter wave (Ka-band) amplifiers using MESFET and HEMT transistors. Led the development of a C-band T/R module for an active phased array radar.

  • Research Scientist

    Philips Research Laboratories

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; PHG; Consumer Electronics industry)

    October 1980 — July 1983 (2 years 10 months)

    Research scientist working on millimeter wave (26.5 - 40.0 GHz) instantaneous frequency measurement receivers. Designed 3dB directional couplers and other microstrip structures.
