Hans Gieskes’s Summary

Hans Gieskes is President & CEO for Cision AB (listed on the nordic stock exchange, CSN) working from its offices in Stockholm and Chicago.

He also serves on the board of directors of H3.com, Retirementjobs.com, SciLink.com and Intelligize.com and is the Honorary Consul for the Netherlands in New England.

Previously he was Chairman, CEO & President of Houghton Mifflin Company, a $1.3 billion publishing company in Boston, MA. He led the company through the $1.6 B divestment process for then parent Vivendi-Universal. The transfer of the Company to new owners (Lee, Bain Capital and Blackstone) was completed in 2002.

Previously Hans was President of Monster.com worldwide. During his tenure, annual revenue grew from $100 million in 1999 to $350 million in 2000.

From 1979 to 2000, Hans had a distinguished career with the global Reed-Elsevier Publishing group, culminating in his appointment as President and CEO of the $1.3 billion LexisNexis Group in 1997, at age 43. Hans led the company through complete transformation from a proprietary technology to a web centric technology base, while rapidly expanding into the small law firm market, into European markets and regaining preference leadership among US law school students.

Other key Reed-Elsevier assignments included:
• SVP Global Sales for Elsevier Science
• CEO Elsevier Science UK
• CEO of Elsevier's Dutch B-2-B / consumer division
• Deputy Chairman legal publishing division (19 companies in 15 countries)

The common theme for all these assignments was the need for urgent “change-management”, whether it concerned technology, major product re-launches, major outsourcing or major M&A activities. Hans successfully managed some dozen acquisitions both in Europe and USA, incl. two deals of more than $1,6 billion.
