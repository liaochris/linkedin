Michael Makowski’s Education

  • The University of Akron

    PhD, Polymer Science, 1989 — 1991

    Dissertation: “Dilute Solution and Photophysical Characterization of Rigid Rod Poly(Pyridinium Salts)”. Research Advisor: Prof. Wayne Mattice, Alex Schulman Professor of Polymer Science and Ohio Eminent Scholar.

    The electronic structure, internal chain dynamics and rotational diffusion modes of fully rigid rod poly(pyridinium salts) were studied in the dilute solution regime and in glassy PMMA using steady state and time correlated single photon counting fluorescence spectroscopy. Molecular dynamics simulations were used to explain the unusual anisotropy of fluorescence emission.

  • The University of Akron

    MS, Polymer Science, 1987 — 1989

    Thesis: “Dilute Solution and Hydrodynamic Behavior of Rigid Rod Ionomers”.
    Research Advisor: Prof. Donald McIntyre.

    The intrinsic characteristics and solution behavior for a series of fully rigid-rod ionomers were studied using the techniques of quasi-elastic light scattering and dilute solution viscometry. The unusual non-Newtonian response of the viscosity in capillary flow was studied as a function of shear rate, molecular weight and supporting electrolyte concentration.
