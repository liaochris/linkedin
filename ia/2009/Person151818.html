Mark Radcliffe’s Summary

I work with technology companies and companies that use technology. I have worked in this field for over 25 years and enjoy applying the law to the issues raised by new technologies. My clients range from large companies such as Sun Microsystems, Inc. and Sony Corporation to unknown startups. Currently, my team and I are working with over 40 technology startups. I have experience across a wide variety of industries, including software (in particular open source software), cleantech, semiconductor, consumer electronics, content licensing (from music to characters), and medical devices. I have advised companies and investors on hundreds of venture capital transaction. I assist companies both in their funding and intellectual property matters. The following is example of some of my projects:

Assisted several large companies in developing open source strategies for making their proprietary software available under an open source license

Assisted cleantech company in developing intellectual property protection strategy

Assisted in international corporate structuring for solar company

General outside counsel to the Open Source Initiative on a pro bono basis

Assisted Network Solutions in developing the initial domain dispute resolution policy in 1994 (which continues to be the basis for the current ICANN Dispute Resolution Policy)

Assisted startup in how to use patents, trademarks, copyrights and trade secrets to protect a unique semiconductor product

Assisted Dr. Suess Estate in determining what words, characters and symbols to register as trademarks (yes, we did protect the Cat in the Hat)

Assisted major corporation in obtaining a license from a financially troubled company with a $40M prepaid royalty and ensured that the license would survive bankruptcy

Assisted large Asian company in establishing an intellectual asset management program for over 50,000 patents

Mark Radcliffe’s Specialties:

Developing and implementing strategies to protect and exploit intellectual property for clients from startups to Global Fortune 50; venture capital financing; joint ventures; licensing; particular experience in software, cleantech, Web 2.0 and semiconductors. I was recently named one to the IAM 250: The World's Leading IP Strategists by Intellectual Asset Management magazine.
