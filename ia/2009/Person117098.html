John Evan Frook’s Summary

Selected by Steven Spielberg as youngest member of four-person expert media panel, providing marketing recommendations for ``American Tale: Fievel Goes West’’ and formation of Amblin's animation arm. Wrote first story about David Geffen as a billionaire, Quentin Tarantino as a filmmaker and Cameron Diaz as an actress. One of first journalists to cover Internet full time. Researched and reported at journalism's highest standards, providing fair exposure to all when few bothered with credit-card toting entrepreneurs. Was there for advent of eBay, Yahoo! and Google, among others. Hosted many of Internet's first broadcasts, including those produced by Mark Cuban before he owned the Dallas Mavericks. Originated proof of concept and Web analytics methodologies for CMP Media's first commercial website resulting in corporate portal strategy that produced $728 million in corporate shareholder value in about four years. Microsoft chair Bill Gates recognized work in ``Business@The Speed of Thought'' as one of two sources for the chapter about Internet adoption and Web analytics. At Crain, originated online content strategies for 70-year-old flagship brand, resulting in one of today's most valuable business-to-business websites of its size and scope.Built Internet retailer that ranked among top in customer service consecutive years. Instituted key customer service policies designed to improve buying experiences for products ranging from $1 buyers to $7,000. Managed tracking strategies, standards and Web analytics applications, and helped developed the fair, principled and ethical business practices that enabled the Internet to become the buying machine it is today. What's next? Another big hit.
