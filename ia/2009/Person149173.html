Marco Palazzo’s Summary

In the April of 1999 I have graduated from University Of Lecce as engineer in computer science with vote 110/110 and since the July of 2002 have been registered at Order Of The Engineers of Provincia of Lecce (n.2205).

At present, I am an employee of Edinform S.p.A. ( 8° level, GIS Area Manager, http://www.edinform.it ) and I am collaborator of the research area "Physical technologies for the environment and the territory" of the Department Innovation Engineering of the University of Salento (http://www.dii.unile.it/index.php?page=home/settore_ricerca).

I have a 8 year experience in geospatial technologies (GIS/WebGIS system development and integration, remote sensing, mapping, geospatial related information technology and imagery analysis). I have Experience in digital cartographic production, imagery analysis and production of standard satellite imagery mapping products.

In my professional experience in Edinform S.p.A I have contributed, in the role of GIS Area Manager, to constitute a new business unit who had as principal object the supply of Geographic Informative Systems. Edinform today have very important references and supplies GIS systems for various organizations belonging to the Public Local Administration (Provincia of Lecce, ASL Rieti,ASL Agrigento).

In my applied research experience I have realized important and complex remote sensing projects of innovation of the territorial and environmental relief for several municipalities of the Salentina Peninsula (Trepuzzi, San Donato, Porto Cesareo, Tricase, Brindisi).

I have published papers on scientific journal, have been teaching in national and international masters and have kept several interventions in conferences on GIS and Remote Sensing.

Marco Palazzo’s Specialties:

- Design and development of GIS and WebGIS applications (Tools: MapServer, GRASS, Quantum GIS, PostgreSQL/PostGIS, ArcGIS 9, ESRI ArcObject framework, ERMapper).

- Design and development of Geodatabase (Geographic Data Model: ESRI Geodatabase Data Model)

- Analysis of Very High Resolution (VHR) Multi-spetral Satellite Data (Quick Bird, Ikonos, Landsat)
