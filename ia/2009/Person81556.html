Frazer Thompson’s Summary

CEO – English Wines Group plc

I graduated in 1981 but started my marketing career with Denby Coloroll in 1984, before joining Whitbread Inns in the North of England.
I became Marketing Manager-Ales in 1987. At 29, in 1989, having acquired and integrated the Boddingtons brand and a successful strategy as response to the Beer Orders, I was made a director of Whitbread plc. It's still one of my proudest moments!
I led the team that created the Boddingtons Cream of Manchester advertising and draughtflow widget before taking the lager portfolio of Heineken and Stella Artois. I then moved into business development and strategy reporting directly to Managing Director, Miles Templeman.
I joined Heineken in Amsterdam in 1995 as Global Brand Director. I did well and enjoyed it - the brand outpaced growth in all significant markets.
I left in Sept 2001 to further the education of my three sons and moved into English Wines to prove something to myself. Since then the profile of Chapel Down wines has risen to such an extent that demand vastly exceeds supply (a complete reversal of fortune), the company is profitable, and we've successfully secured three rounds of external fund-raising and floated on Plusmarkets. The last round (in March 2004) raised £1.95m. The wines have now won many International awards as an obsession with quality and brand pays dividends. I have created a board almost unparalleled for a company of this size including Paul Brett (former CEO Thomson travel), Nigel Wray (Britain’s Buffet), Richard Balfour-Lynn (CEO of MWB) as non-executive board members.
