Jack Lynch’s Summary

Entrepreneur with experience in internet gaming, corporate strategy, media and private equity developing a new platform for online therapy.

Jack Lynch’s Experience

  • Co-Founder

    Presence TeleCare

    (Education Management industry)

    April 2009 — Present (4 months)

    Presence TeleCare is a distance therapy platform for children who have difficulty accessing speech language pathology (SLP) services. We connect SLPs seeking flexible schedules with children at schools struggling to find full-time SLPs. We provide them with the educational curriculum, the practice management tools and the technology necessary to deliver high quality remote speech therapy.

  • Director of Finance & Operations

    Lumos Labs

    (Privately Held; Health, Wellness and Fitness industry)

    July 2008 — April 2009 (10 months)

    Lumos Labs, a.k.a. lumosity.com, is the leading online provider of scientifically tested brain training games that help reduce cognitive decline among the elderly. I had responsibility for a range of financial, operational and business development initiatives.

  • Marketing Intern

    Rapt

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Computer Software industry)

    August 2007 — September 2007 (2 months)

    Rapt, which was recently acquired by Microsoft, provides pricing and inventory management analytical tools for digital media companies. I helped them develop their go-to-market strategy.

  • Summer Consultant

    The Boston Consulting Group

    (Partnership; 1001-5000 employees; BCG; Management Consulting industry)

    June 2007 — August 2007 (3 months)

    London office. Cabin crew efficiency project for major European Airline. Received full-time offer.

  • Associate

    Bear Stearns Merchant Banking

    (Investment Management industry)

    August 2004 — June 2006 (1 year 11 months)

    BSMB was the captive private equity arm of Bear Stearns & Co. The firm has since spun out as independent firm, Irving Place Capital. BSMB's most recent fund is $2.7 billion, dedicated to investments in a wide range of industries, especially retail, financial services, consumer products and health care. In the associate role, I evaluated over 20 investments in a range of industries. I helped close a $106 milllion investment in Stuart Weitzman Holdings, a high-end women's shoe retailer and wholesaler. In the portfolio management aspect of my job, I took a hands-on role with the senior management of Balducci's, New York & Company and Stuart Weitzman in developing strategic and operational initiatives.

  • Corporate Strategic Planning Analyst

    The Walt Disney Company

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; DIS; Entertainment industry)

    July 2001 — June 2004 (3 years)

    The Corporate Strategic Planning Department at Disney was a 35-person internal consulting group reporting to Disney's Chief Strategic Officer. The group had broad oversight over high impact strategic initiatives. I focused on our international and consumer products businesses. Projects included the turnaround of the Disney Channel Latin America, the analysis of European acquisition targets, the sale process for the Disney Store North America and the launch of the first mass-market video-on-demand service outside the U.S.
