Bill Burger’s Summary

Innovative, strategic-thinking marketing executive with track record in early-stage companies and established market leaders. Consistent external focus with a passion for growing businesses through research, innovation and customer engagement. Proven experience developing and managing brand strategies, launching products and supporting customer and supplier relationships. Passionate believer in the value of social networking tools to build and engage communities. Strong, cohesive leader of operational units and creative and editorial teams. Extensive business development and international experience. Frequent speaker at publishing and information industry events.

Bill Burger’s Experience

  • Vice President, Marketing

    Copyright Clearance Center

    (Non-Profit; Information Services industry)

    May 2005 — January 2009 (3 years 9 months)

    CCC is the world’s largest provider of content licensing services to corporations and academic institutions.
    --Responsible for the strategic development of the CCC brand, product identities and all externally facing communications and outreach. This included all sales-support and communication programs for the company: the corporate and e-commerce web site (www.copyright.com), trade shows, advertising, collateral and marketing campaigns. Spoke or presented at dozens of industry events, including keynote talks to large audiences of customers, business partners and industry executives.
    --Oversaw the company’s suite of products and services that generated $200 million in annual revenue. Led a product management team that was responsible for market research, strategic marketing plans, product roadmaps, new product and feature development. Led the company into new business lines involving content delivery as well as licensing of new content types, including images and video.

  • Senior Vice President

    Rogers Medical Intelligence Solutions

    (Public Company; Pharmaceuticals industry)

    2002 — 2004 (2 years)

    Rogers MIS was a medical information services company focused on publishing Continuing Medical Education (CME) programs to physicians and clinical information services to the pharmaceutical industry.
    --Managed content distribution partnerships with publishers and medical societies. Created content licensing model and established initial relationships.
    --Proposed and oversaw launch of new publishing system, redesign of the content workflow process and hiring of new editorial team. These changes significantly reduced costs, shortened timelines and improved quality of published reports. Oversaw redesign of the company’s suite of publications.
    --Repositioned the company’s brand and launched its first marketing initiatives, including trade shows and collateral materials.
    Identified and managed numerous business partnerships and business-development relationships. Primary liaison with the company’s outside law firm.
    --Developed and wrote the company’s 3-year strategic plan.

  • Vice President and Lead Analyst

    Outsell, Inc.

    (Management Consulting industry)

    April 2002 — August 2002 (5 months)

    Provided strategic and tactical consulting services to publishing and information industry clients, both independently and as a Vice President of Outsell, Inc., an independent research and advisory firm focusing on the information content industry. Services offered included business development, product strategy, marketing, licensing, pricing and corporate positioning.

  • Vice President

    iCopyright

    (Information Technology and Services industry)

    April 2000 — December 2000 (9 months)

    iCopyright was an early-stage company that created a web-based content-licensing service that makes content easy to license and distribute while protecting the publisher’s copyrights.
    --Responsible for the development of strategic alliances with leading publishers and other content providers and distributors worldwide.
    --Responsible for continual review and enhancement of service offerings to content partners and for developing new services to meet their needs and the needs of the market.

  • Vice President, Content & Media Services

    Infonautics

    (Public Company; INFO; Internet industry)

    January 1997 — January 2000 (3 years 1 month)

    Infonautics was a 190-employee, publicly held company that increased revenues from $1.4 million in 1996 to more than $23 million in 1999, becoming the largest provider of online reference and research services to K-12 schools. I was one of six executive officers of the corporation.
    --Directed the company’s overall content strategy and led the effort to increase the number of content partners for the company’s core Electric Library service from 50 to 400, eliminating the company’s reliance upon competing content aggregators.
    --Led a successful turnaround of the company’s e-commerce Online Publishing business by reducing expenses, increasing sales bookings and overseeing the transition to a new, more efficient and flexible technology platform. Customers included: Business Week, Associated Press, Newsday, Atlanta Journal Constitution and Dallas Morning News.
    --Upgraded content licensing, database management, royalty payment and publisher relations programs and processes.

  • Director of New Media Development & Educational Services

    AT&T

    (Public Company; T; Telecommunications industry)

    July 1995 — December 1996 (1 year 6 months)

    --Planned and directed AT&T’s effort to provide “Homework Hotline” voice-messaging services to U.S. primary and secondary schools.
    --Negotiated an AT&T investment in a company to provide sales support and on-the-ground management for school-based messaging services.
    --Appointed to the company task force that created the AT&T Learning Network, a $150 million program to benefit American schools.

  • Senior Editor

    Newsweek

    (Public Company; WPO; Publishing industry)

    September 1981 — May 1995 (13 years 9 months)

    1993-1995: European Economics Editor, London. Directed all European business and economic coverage for Newsweek International. Reported and wrote numerous special reports and cover stories.
    1990-1993: Senior Editor, New York. Oversaw coverage of business, economic and Asian affairs for Newsweek International, directing and editing the contributions of more than 30 writers, researchers and correspondents around the world. Approved layouts and selected all photos and graphics.
    1989-1990: Senior Editor, Tokyo. Built an editorial team and process to create original content for Newsweek’s Japanese-language edition. Wrote and edited stories for the magazine and coordinated coverage with Newsweek’s editorial staff in New York.
    1981-1989: Senior Writer, General Editor, Associate Editor, Editorial Assistant, New York. Reported and wrote hundreds of stories for Newsweek and Newsweek International, including more than two dozen cover stories.
