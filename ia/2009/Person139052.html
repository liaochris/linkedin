Lee Acosta’s Summary

When I finished active military service I wasn't sure what I wanted to do as a profession. I had always enjoyed the outdoors and after a trip to Oregon where I visited the Lewis & Clark Fort near Astoria decided to go into Land Surveying, the second oldest profession. Since I first began surveying in 1994 I have worked for a couple smaller firms and 2 large ones eventually working my way to Party Chief and obtaining a professional license (PLS No. 37943). In 2006 I moved into the office to take over the drafting portion of surveying as well as get a better understanding of post field data reduction and boundary analysis. After the retirement of my boss in 2008 I took over the duties of project surveyor handling all survey requirements in the office as well as some occasional field work. The slow down in the economy resulted in the loss of our last dedicated field surveyor in late 2008 at which time I handled all surveying from initial client meetings to the delivery of the finished product. June of 2009 brought about the end of the Survey Department in the inter-mountain region and the opening of a new opportunity for me, along with one of the displaced draftsman we created Front Range Land Surveyors. Our goal is to provide professional, timely and accurate Land Surveying services at a competitive price.

Lee Acosta’s Specialties:

ALTA/ACSM Land Title Surveys, Right-of-way determination/delineation, Boundary surveys, Land Divisions, Change of Zoning Plats, Annexation Plats, Easements (Conservation, Access, R.O.W., Temporary), Topographic, Quantity and As-Built surveys, Legal descriptions, Construction surveying, Dam Movement Monument Surveys, Improvement Surveys, Improvement Location certificate (ILC), Flood Plain Certificate
