Michael Chung’s Summary

The path and pace of innovation is eminently foreseeable.

The object of innovation should be to free humans from all consciously involuntary efforts. Wherever involuntary exertions (especially cognitive!) are found, that is the path where innovation needs to be applied. For the pace, simply compute: the inverse square of the current state of art to the future value of the subject innovation factored to the relative nth power of the prevailing conventions.

The purpose of innovation is to increase voluntary time. The destination is 100% voluntary time, all the time.

In the meantime:

1. Webunch will enable people to connect, post, and chat based on search query(s). It will provide the fastest and the shortest link among people and the creation of new first person information knowledge-base. (www.webunch.com)

2. Mamomail will take half generation to reach its full stride - worldwide. However, it will be profitable w/i 1 to 2 yrs of its start:

> Half (2nd) of it - it's goal is to take over some 50% of the current "responsibilites" of the postal mail by uniquely replicating in email, top mail's attributes, namely: failsafe security and reliability and its variety of sender customizable "special handling and delivery" services.

In a digital communication and global warming age, how can the average USA home continue to get 1500 pcs of mail p/year?! (Only 3.5% is personal.) It is wasteful, costly, inefficient and unnecessary to mail CRM notices, billing, statements, etc.

Thus, what improvements does email need for the society to reduce its dependence on postal mail? I have the answer - both the method and the revenue drivers and a huge bonus: an immediate huge new revenue lure to the ISP industries to monetize their free email (loss leader) services.
