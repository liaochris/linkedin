Pedro A. Ramos’s Summary

Pedro Ramos applies a diverse background as an attorney and a manager in the business, government, and the nonprofit sectors on behalf of clients across a broad spectrum, including but not limited to educational institutions, government agencies, vendors for educational institutions and government agencies, and organizations involved in emergency/disaster preparedness.

In addition to representing national clients for more than nine years on employee benefits, executive compensation, and fiduciary matters in private practice, Mr. Ramos has served as the chief of staff to the former president of the University of Pennsylvania, the chief legal counsel for the City of Philadelphia, the chief operating officer for the City of Philadelphia, and the President of the Board of Education of the School District of Philadelphia.

COMMUNITY SERVICE & AFFILIATIONS

Mr. Ramos has received several civic awards, including the Leon J. Obermayer Award for 2000, the annual Puerto Rican Festival’s Citizen of the Year Award for 2000, the Philadelphia Education Fund Giving Forward Award for 2002, and the 2008 J.T. Canales Distinguished Alumni Award by the University of Michigan LLSA.

Mr. Ramos is an alumnus of the Eisenhower Exchange Fellowships (EEF), having focused on early childhood education policy in France, and is the U.S. representative on EEF's Alumni Advisory Council. A survey by Leadership Philadelphia identified Mr. Ramos as one of the region's top "connectors," defined as those who connect for the common good. He is also participating in the 2008 Philadelphia Leadership Exchange, sponsored by the Pennsylvania Economy League.

Mr. Ramos is co-chair of the Philadelphia Bar Association's Legislative Liaison Committee; and serves on the boards of directors of the Kimmel Center, Inc. (Chair, Human Resource & Compensation Committee; Member, Strategic Planning Committee); Project H.O.M.E., and the Pan-American Association of Philadelphia.
