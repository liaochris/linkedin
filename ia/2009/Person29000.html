Srividya Akella’s Summary

I have over 9 years of experience in molecular biology research and during these years I have developed a broad skill set. While my core subjects in school were plant biology, I have also gained few years of experience in microbial genetics and human genetic disorder studies. Having the opportunity to work in both academic laboratories and industry settings has given me flexibility to adapt to different work environments.
To elaborate a little on my research experience, my work has spanned multiple areas giving me insight and depth into many areas of molecular biology and genetics in addition to the skills I have in laboratory techniques. I especially want to draw your attention to my experience in Streptomyces genome annotation, analysis of gene expression profiles, sequence analysis and genotyping analysis of genetic loci involved in human neuro-degenerative disorders. In addition, I have recent training in programming languages C, JAVA, PERL and R.
Based on these strengths and skills, I'd like to further grow my career in the field of bioinformatics research.

  • Consultant

  • Senior Research Associate

    Diversa Corporation

    (Public Company; 201-500 employees; DVSA; Biotechnology industry)

    April 2001 — October 2004 (3 years 7 months)

    Gene expression profiling: Used Affymetrix microarray technology to generate gene expression profiles of Streptomyces samples to study stress response, developmental and antibiotic production pathways. GeneSpring analysis software.
    Streptomyces Genomics: High throughput PCR to fill gaps in Streptomyces diversa genomic DNA sequence and participated in a team effort to annotate the genome.
    Identification of genes involved in regulation of antibiotic production in S.diversa: Putative regulators were identified by sequence homology to known regulators from closely related species. The regulators were PCR amplified and cloned. The effect of these putative regulators was examined in antibiotic production strains.
    Development of S.diversa as a host for protein production: Several high-GC encoded proteins including amylases, xylanases, proteases and lipases were tested for production in S.diversa. Several signal peptides, terminators, RBSs, were evaluated for increase in production.

  • Research Associate

    June 2000 — March 2001 (10 months)

    Used DNA microarray technology to generate gene expression profiles of Arabidopsis plants to identify genes involved in various signaling pathways. Particular tasks accomplished were:
    Target DNA fragments were generated by high-throughput PCR and printed on glass slides using a robotic workstation.
    Arabidopsis plants were administered various treatments, RNA was isolated, cDNA was generated by reverse transcription, tagged with a fluorophore and used as probe.
    After hybridization of probe to targets, slides were scanned to generate analyzable image of colored spots.
