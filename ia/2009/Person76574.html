Evan Bauer’s Summary

25 years of experience in the architecture, design, implementation, and maintenance of complex information systems. A consultant advising both public and private sector organizations on technology innovation.

Current and recent projects include:
Enterprise architecture assessment and re-design for an asset management firm, application alternatives assessment for securities lending, data and compute grid design and architecture, introduction of Linux and other open source technologies, derivatives trading system re-design, technology risk assessment and mitigation, venture capital due diligence, and product strategy consulting to technology firms.

As Chief Technology Officer for Global Infrastructure at Credit Suisse First Boston, Evan provided overall direction and oversight for the re-architecting of CSFB's network, server, and desktop environments. Previously he built a global Internet/Intranet hosting organization and infrastructure for CSFB that managed 77 external and over 1,400 internal sites and applications.

Evan served as Principal Architect for both Global Operations and Sales and Trading Technology at Bankers Trust Company where he had responsibility for the business and application architectures for foreign exchange, money market, and listed securities systems.
Evan was Vice President and Senior Research Advisor at the Giga Information Group where he established the data management and web software research teams.
Previous positions: Vice President, Client-Server Engineering for Bankers Trust; Principal Database Consultant for DEC; and Vice President of Operations for SEED Software Corporation.
At the start of his career he held a variety of technical positions at ManTech International, CDC, International Database Systems, First Pennsylvania Bank, and the staff of the United States Senate.

Evan is a member of the Brooklyn Leadership Committee of Habitat for Humanity NYC and a Founding Member of HFHNYC's Community Builder Society.

Evan Bauer’s Specialties:

Technology strategy. Technology architecture for complex, high-volume, mission-critical systems. Mobile Computing. IT management. In-depth application expertise in the financial services industry. Linux and open source software. Database management. High-Performance Computing.
