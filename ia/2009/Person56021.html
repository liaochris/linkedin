Dave Bryce’s Summary

• Founded and developed ADAM Audio's professional US distributorship into a multi-million dollar business.
• Own and operate state of the art professional recording facility.
• Founded and still moderate Keyboard magazine's online forum community
• Worked with Dave Smith Instruments to improve branding, grow and refine sales network, develop products and significantly increase market presence. Tripled sales in one year.
• Increased Alesis synthesizer division sales by over 1000% in 18 months.
* Play live music with several LA based bands.

Dave Bryce’s Specialties:

Product evangelism, practical market analysis and outreach strategy development, especially fond of problem solving...and anything relating to music.

  • Moderator/Administrator

    musicplayer.com

    (Non-Profit; Information Services industry)

    October 2000 — Present (8 years 7 months)

    In the fall 2000, I was tapped by Craig Anderton to found and moderate Keyboard magazine's online forum, which has grown to be one of the world's most well known internet keyboard communities. I still moderate the forum and am also a site administrator at musicplayer.com. I also write a corresponding column for Keyboard magazine culled from the forum, and have organized and produced thirteen compilations of stellar music submitted by the members of the forum from all over the world.

  • Voice Over Artist

    Independent (Self-employed)

    (Self-Employed; Myself Only; Entertainment industry)

    June 1993 — Present (15 years 11 months)

    My closet passion is doing voices. In the course of any one day, I can go through as many as 20 or 30 different voices, mostly for my own amusement and (hopefully) the amusement of those around me. Luckily for me, doing so seems to frequently land me all sort of different voice over projects. I've done VO for radio and TV commercials (including a series of spots for Norman Lear), industrial projects and (my absolute favorite) video games. I specialize in accents and character voices - the more bizarre and unusual, the better.

  • CEO

    ADAM Audio USA

    (Consumer Electronics industry)

    May 2001 — February 2009 (7 years 10 months)

    ADAM Audio is a high end German professional and consumer speaker manufacturer. I founded and ran their US distributor operation, having been the sole proprietor for the first four years of its operation. As such, I introduced the brand to the US, implemented and ran all sales functions, organized and managed dealer and service networks, planned and executed all US marketing including several sales promotions and all US trade shows and advertising campaigns. I also oversaw all import/export functions, all domestic shipping and receiving and ran the warehouse.

  • North American Sales Manager, Marketing/R&D Consultant

    Dave Smith Instruments

    (Consumer Electronics industry)

    August 2004 — December 2007 (3 years 5 months)

    During the early ADAM days, synth guru Dave Smith was getting started with his new company Dave Smith Instruments. After buying one of his new Evolver synths and falling in love with it, I was lucky enough to have Dave ask me to come and work with him to increase public awareness about his new synth line. During the time I worked with Dave, I was responsible for managing his North American sales network, developing and implementing sales and marketing strategies (e.g. rebranding the company from Dave Smith Instruments to DSI), and materials (training docs, ads, etc), doing demos around the country and at trade shows, coordinating reviews as well as actively participating in product development, beta testing and sound design. Sadly, ADAM and DSI grew too big for me to be able to work for both...

  • Synthesizer group Marketing Manager

    Alesis Studio Electronics

    (Consumer Electronics industry)

    1995 — 2000 (5 years)

    What started as a simple clinician gig quickly turned into one of my favorite jobs ever - synthesizer marketing manager for maverick manufacturer Alesis. In addition to traveling all over the world training, demonstrating and performing on Alesis synthesizers, I managed all marketing aspects of the group for five years, including advertising, POS displays, promotions, product rollouts and trade show presence.

    I also worked very closely with engineering on product planning and development - especially the sound development group, which I ended up managing for a brief period. During my tenure at Alesis, synthesizer sales increased by over 1000% during the first 18 months, and we had the best selling synthesizer in the US for three years straight (QS8). I was also Alesis' representative to IAEKM (International Association of Electronic Keyboard Manufacturers).
