Allen J. Brown, J.D., Ph.D.’s Summary

Allen J. Brown, J.D., Ph.D. is the Director of the Center on Workplace and School Violence and Associate Professor of Psychology and Criminal Justice. He is a psychologist who specializes in clinical and forensic psychology, and an attorney.
He has consulted with corporations, law enforcement, government agencies, law firms, hospitals, schools, and colleges on the prevention and management of workplace and school violence. He developed and teaches specialized courses on workplace and school violence. In addition, he has developed assessment instruments, specifically designed to address issues unique to each type of organization, to aid schools, colleges, and businesses in determining their vulnerability to violence, allowing them to develop appropriate measures to deal with the vulnerabilities identified.
Following completion of his J.D (law) and Ph.D. (Clinical Psychology), he completed post-doctoral training at M.I.T. and Harvard Medical School at Massachusetts General Hospital. Dr. Brown was a Clinical Instructor in the Harvard Medical School Department of Psychiatry (1992 – 2005) and served as the Director of Adult Forensic Training for the Law & Psychiatry Service at Massachusetts General Hospital for four years. He was the Co-Chair of the American Psychological Association's Presidential Working Group on Law and Psychology, a consultant for the Governor's Special Advisory Panel on Forensic Mental Health (Massachusetts), and a member of the Advisory Council of the National Center for State Courts.
Dr. Brown has taught at the undergraduate and graduate levels, published on issues at the interface of psychology and law, and lectured on these issues to judges, attorneys, psychologists, psychiatrists, social workers, educators, administrators, and business executives. He has served as a consultant or expert witness in state and federal courts, and court martial proceedings in the United States and Europe.
