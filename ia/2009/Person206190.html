Ryan Salmon’s Summary

Manage and direct operations through dock staff, dispatchers, routers, yard, shop, data entry, drivers, and afternoon/night traffic staff for effective management of operations. Second in responsibility to terminal manager. Responsible to monitor and maintain all aspects of the terminal operation. This includes supervision, hiring and discipline of personnel, communication with staff, following occupational health and safety procedures, annual staff performance appraisals, dealing with employee issues and concerns, handling customer inquiries, employee orientation and training, managing the movement of freight and generally overseeing the terminal, specifically the operations of the terminal. Work closely with regional dispatch and other terminals to insure operational problems are resolved. Co-ordinate equipment with shop personnel in relation to the terminal operation. Move freight according to delivery schedules and appointment times. Responsible to plan, monitor and schedule staff to ensure all positions are covered. Have the ability to fill in for positions when necessary, including vacations. Responsible for complete knowledge of dispatch system, routing procedures, driver bid runs, tractor assignments, paperflow etc. Work in co-operation with Head Office personnel and other departments when information is requested. Assist in developing operational guidelines with the Terminal Manager. Recommend changes to operations, and follow through with any changes implemented. Be available for special projects as designated by terminal manager. To work in co-operation with all staff and communicate daily with the terminal manager regarding the night operation. Forward any customer requests for rates to the Sales Department and/or Pricing Department. Investigate accidents and claims when required.

Ryan Salmon’s Specialties:

Personnel management. Fluent in Windows and spreadsheet based programs. Time management. Knowledge of maintaining costs as it relates to operational profitability. Strong practical and theoretical knowledge of all aspects pertaining to the transport industry. Fork Lift operators license. Class 3 license. Knowledge of LINK2000 software and operation. Knowledge of AS/400 based programs. Strong computer skills, hardware & software.
