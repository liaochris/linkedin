Danilo Salvego’s Summary

Danilo Salvego is a Brazilian visual artist and graphic designer, with a degree in Visual Arts offered by PUC University (Pontifícia Universidade Católica de Campinas), Brazil.

Danilo Salvego was born in 1978 and was strongly influenced by his father Antonio Salvego who was a painter and introduced the technique of Photographic Serigraphy in Piracicaba in the early 70’s.

He started his career in 1996, acting as a freelancer designer for the press media. From 1998 to 2001 he worked for several digital advertising agencies as an art director, taking part directly in the conception and production of projects for major corporations such as Liz, Terra Networks, Biosintética, Basf, Bayer, Manah, Caterpillar, Davene, among others. He played an essential part at the writing about web-multimedia production techniques for the book “Flash 4 Professional”, which was included in the “Ten Bestsellers List”, according to an evaluation published by the newspaper "O Estado de São Paulo".

Took part at the elaboration and the teaching of such courses as Macromedia Flash, Adobe Photoshop and Adobe Illustrator, all of them focused in digital design production.

In 2002 and 2003 participated in art contests and exhibitions, being selected and awarded in the cities of Piracicaba, Campinas and São Paulo.

His recent activities involve various areas of graphic design, having developed projects for meaningful institutions and Brazilian companies, such as: ABC71, Agripoint, Anglo, Dr. Roger Moreira, Dra. Jocelem Salgado, Drogal, MB Associados, Minerthal, Paragon, Rádio Educativa FM, Rosfrios, Supricel, Universidade Metodista de Piracicaba, Varixx.

As a founder and major partner of Salvego & Salvego Ltd., he works in his own studio as an independent professional in the areas of visual arts and graphic design.
