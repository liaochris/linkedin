Claudia Sampson’s Summary

Claudia's career began as a freelance writer, media & entertainment consultant in D.C. Building on her robust network, she honed her business talents by working in the US and abroad, providing consulting and training services to prestigious firms including the Bank of New York and Marsh & McLennan, Inc. before turning to academia. Claudia returned to her alma mater – Columbia University – to market the program to the next generation of business leaders and entrepreneurs. As an Assistant Director of Admissions at the Columbia Business School, Claudia helped significantly increase the number of MBA candidates. She returned to the private sector as a Corporate HR Manager for Pfizer and Recruiting Manager at PricewaterhouseCoopers. She joined Deloitte as Director of Diversity, Women's Initiatives and Work Life Balance. Some of her executive coaching clients include Best Buy, Guidant. Johnson & Johnson as well as the Stern School of Business Career Development Office, and Yale SOM Career Services Office. For four years, she was the Lead Coach at Management Leadership for Tomorrow where she prepared over 200 fellows for entry level careers and admissions to top graduate business schools. Claudia also founded LEEDS, a leadership development and coaching practice, to help clients develop pathways to success in corporate and entrepreneurial careers. Combining her passion for recruiting, coaching, and leadership development with her corporate HR and non-profit consulting dossier, Claudia graduated Columbia University with a B.A. in Liberal Arts with an emphasis on French and Philosophy. She has received professional certification through the Society of Human Resource Management and the Institute of Executive Development (EDI). She is also an Advisory Board member of Harlem World Magazine. and volunteers wirh RiseTo College and College Summit to give back to others.
