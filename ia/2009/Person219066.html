Aly Diabate’s Summary

I began slaving at the age of 11 to produce cocoa used by major chocolate manufacturers in the United States.

I worked 80 - 100 hours per week.

The bags of cocoa beans that I carried were taller than my body. They were heavy. When I fell I was beaten with a bicycle chain until I got back up again.

This was very difficult work, but I survived for 4 years, until I was freed.

    I never worked directly for Nestle, but because 43% of the world's cocoa comes from Cote d'Ivoire (Ivory Coast, in West Africa), the plantation I slaved at sold cocoa to Nestle (as well as all other major chocolate companies in the United States).

  • child slave

    plantation that sells to Hershey's

    (Food & Beverages industry)

    1999 — 2003 (4 years )

    When I was 11 years old, I was taken by a slave trader and sold to a farmer in the Ivory Coast. For 4 years, I worked 80 - 100 hours per week. I was beaten. At night we were locked into a windowless shed - 18 boys in a 20' x 25' room. We had a bucket to use instead of a toilet.

    We were let out only to work.

    I grew tired of the beatings, and so did the other children. One boy escaped, and got help for the others in the farm.

    The US State Department estimates that there are 15,000 children, between the ages of 9 - 12 who are enslaved in cocoa farms in the Ivory Coast.

    This cocoa is purchased by Hersheys, Nestle, Mars, and other chocolate companies.

    I guess you can say that I was employed by these companies as a child slave.

    The next time you eat a piece of chocolate, or chocolate ice cream, or cafe mocha - please remember that child slaves, like I was, have made that chocolate.

    And that the company that sells it to you is paying the slavemasters.
