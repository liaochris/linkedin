Robin Roberts’s Summary

Robin Roberts is co-anchor of ABC News' "Good Morning America." As an anchor of the morning news program, Roberts brings viewers around the country interviews with newsmakers and celebrities. Roberts also travels around the world bringing firsthand reports on breaking news stories as well as feature reports on everything from the devastating effects of Hurricane Katrina - to First Lady Laura Bush's breast cancer mission in the Middle East - to traveling on the campaign trail interviewing presidential candidate Sen. Barack Obama and co-anchoring "GMA"'s coverage of the Democratic and Republican National Conventions.

Robin Roberts’s Specialties:

Anchoring, reporting, inspirational speaking, sports reporting

Robin Roberts’s Experience

  • ABC News

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; DIS; Broadcast Media industry)

    May 2005 — Present (3 years 9 months)

    Roberts was named co-anchor of ABC News' "Good Morning America" in May 2005. At "Good Morning America," she continues to report on a wide array of topics and issues for the morning broadcast. She's covered numerous special events ranging from the homecoming of soldiers aboard the USS Roosevelt to the Golden Jubilee at Buckingham Palace to the 25th anniversary of the papacy of Pope John Paul II in Vatican City.

  • ESPN

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; DIS; Broadcast Media industry)

    February 1990 — January 2005 (15 years)

    From 1990 to 2005, she was a contributor to ESPN, where she was one of the network's most versatile commentators. Her assignments included hosting "SportsCenter," contributing to "NFL PrimeTime" from 1990-1994, and providing reports and interviews from the field.

    At ESPN, Roberts was a play-by-play commentator and host of the network's WNBA (Women's National Basketball Association) games and specials, from 1997 to 2000. She was also the primary reporter for ESPN's coverage of the Winter and Summer Olympics and served as host of ESPN's prime-time interview program. "In the SportsLight," ESPN Classic's Vintage NBA," provided coverage of the men's NCAA Final Four, the NCAA Women's Basketball Tournament and of LPGA events. She also hosted ABC Sports' "Wide World of Sports."
