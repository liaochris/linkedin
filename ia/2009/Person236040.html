Tom Koppel’s Summary

Pam was a stay-at-home mom and Tom owned his own businesses and participated in raising our children. But our quality of life, other than higher income, was no better than if we had been employees. We had the hassles, expenses and liabilities associated with having employees and we were stuck to the business without the opportunity to even take a day off. There had to be a better way.

We started by setting goals so that we would have a vision of our destination before we started our journey. Maybe our goals are similar to yours. These were ours:

• no boss, work with whomever we want, build for ourselves, not for some company
• set our own flexible work hours without having to commute
• live where we wanted to live and be able to relocate as we wanted
• build our own business with low and attainable start up costs and minimal overhead
• retire early and continue to make money on our previous efforts during our retirement years
• work in a positive environment, with positive, outgoing people who have similar goals
• control our own future and determine by our work how much we would earn
• thrive in a recession and be protected against possible inflation
• pay no royalties or franchise fees to anybody; be able to leverage our time with the efforts of others.

If that seems like a lot to ask for, we were determined to find it. We were also determined to work hard for what we wanted and we knew we could get to the point where we wanted to be.

What we knew for sure was that if we didn’t make changes, if we weren’t the agents for change, then nothing would change for us in our lives. We knew we had choices. Our choice was to either accept things the way they were or proactively choose to focus on achieving a balance of "work", "live", "learn", and "give".
