Grant Goldsmith, CIC, PGD-MA’s Summary

I am an international insurance broker and consultant with domestic expertise in the US markets and regional expertise in the Middle Eastern, African and London markets. Prior to my insurance career I spent 10 years of active military duty with the United States Army. I have lived in Korea, Germany, Bosnia and various locations in the US. I currently live in Kuwait. I am a 1991 Graduate of the United States Military Academy, West Point.

My insurance experience is varied along all lines of commercially available insurance with specific expertise in Property & Casualty, International LIfe and Health Benefits, Marine Insurance and Defense Base Act Insurance. I am also familiar with specialty insurances such as Environmental Liability, D&O, EPL, Kidnap & Ransom and various Professional Liability Policies.

I have worked extensively with the US Department of Defense Contracting with respect to insurances required by the DoD in Iraq and other areas. I have also worked with the United Nations putting together insurance for the UN Mission in Sudan and other UN projects.

Grant Goldsmith, CIC, PGD-MA’s Education

    Humanitarian Logistics Support Operations 2008 — 2008

    The Humanitarian & Emergency Logistics Training is a course established and taught by Agility to prepare volunteer employee logisticians to support the UN Logistics Cluster during a Humanitarian crisis. The purpose of a HELP (Humanitarian & Emergency Logistics Preparedness) team member is to bring commercial logistics management experience and donated resources to a disaster area in order to enhance the efforts organized by an individual nation's emergency response plan as requested by the United Nations Logistics Cluster. HELP Team members are volunteers who agree to go to unimproved areas of the world on short notice to assist with establishing the needed logistics framework to support a Humanitarian Relief Operation as part of a United Nations' request for assistance via the UN Logistics Cluster.
