Nick Cooper’s Summary

Available immediately and contactable at work@nscooper.com or via mobile: (+44) 07764 186864

I have gained thorough experience designing and developing financial systems, for the investment banking/ securities and retail banking industries. My technical skills have been complemented by formal financial training and certification, coupled with exposure to projects concentrating on fixed income, interest rate derivatives and credit risk. I can offer a keenness to gather business requirements, prototype/concept-prove designs and drive through solutions to implementation. I am keen to continue to develop my interpersonal skills, having benefited from a lot of experience dealing directly with traders, business analysts and project stakeholders.

I have experience of agile development methods, including pair programming (both new development and refactoring for around two hours per day) and iterative development/small frequent releases with a CruiseControl based continuous build process. I have developed systems using TDD; designing interfaces, creating JUnit tests and then building class implementations.

My latest project concerned enhancing a JSP/Ajax web-based admin tool and a number of developments on the Swing based dealer’s trading application. The latter has involved significant development to add level 2 pricing facilities (provided by Reuters price feeds) to the “trade-ticket” used by dealers to accept inbound trades from online clients. My last activity concerned development of a JavaWebStart/Swing based CFD margin monitor tool for dealers to identify clients with diminishing capital resources in light of real-time price and FX-rate (from Currenex) fluctuations. The system was hosted on a JBoss (v5) application server and used EJB3, Spring and Hibernate (with annotations). JBPM was used as a rules engine for processing trade lifecycles. Subversion was used for the code and design-document repository.

    RBS Global Banking & Markets

    (Public Company; Investment Banking industry)

    November 2006 — November 2007 (1 year 1 month)

    Contracted as a Senior Developer, I was a member of a small team who designed, developed and supported a global application which delivered trade booking, static and market data services to C#.Net based client trading systems. These services are provided by exposing web services using Axis. Trades were booked for all IR derivatives, most commonly IR Caps/Floors, IR Swaps, Swaptions, Bond Options and Forward rates.     The Rates Services application is deployed as a Spring application onto JBoss 4 application servers. It has an internal caching capability for local static data and also accesses an Oracle Coherence cache (also deployed on DataSynapse) for booking data. Data is persisted via Hibernate 3.1 to both Sybase and Oracle databases.

    Amongst othe roles, I developed new web services to expose functionality for the client applications to capture trading risk information, particularly options expiry and position reports for European, Bermudan and American style options. This has allowed me to collaborate with colleagues in RBS’ Indian-based development teams, who were responsible for exposing web services to provide my application with part of the data, which was captured and included in the data provided to the trading client.
