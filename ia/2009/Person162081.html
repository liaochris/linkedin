Michael Rodriguez’s Summary

I started Ample Realty in 2003 with my partner, Franco Ferrari. Franco has over 20 years as a Business Broker, and he also owns Ferrari Sunbelt Business Brokers. We launched the sales and marketing for 196 units of the Townhomes at Avalon Lakes in East Orlando, and we currently have a new project of 78 properties to develop near the University of Central Florida.

We am currently marketing two developments. Lake Price Estates is a 35 acre project of 75 luxury estate homes over by the University of Central Florida, the third largest university in the U.S. There we are selling the residential lots and homes to be built by ICI Homes. Colonial Greens is a 10 acre commercial project by the 417 Expressway and Colonial near downtown Orlando. Our vision is for 7 commercial office buildings with approximately 140,000 s.f., and a 140 room business suites hotel.

I've particpated with numerous new construction projects since 1979. I've also worked in the resort condominium, and single family home development in Puerto Vallarta, Mexico. I envision the growth we are experiencing here in Central Florida to continue for many years to come, and our goal is to eventually extend our services into Mexico. Mexico is the natural progression of migration for North America baby-boomers who desire warmer temperate climates and less density to enjoy retirement. The southern half of the U.S. is currently experiencing this trend, but it will continue expanding into Mexico, if it remains safe. Safety is being threatened by the instability and violence from the drug wars.

I serve on the board of the National Entrepenuer Center in Orlando. Previous posts included Director for the Puerto Rican Chamber of Commerce of Central Florida, and City of Orlando Parks & Recreation Board.

Michael Rodriguez’s Specialties:

By understanding the construction process, I have been able to help construction personnel and sales people deliver the best product to customers on a timely basis. Change will always happen throughout the process, and it is important to prepare our customers for the unexpected.
