Jeffrey Kaditz’s Summary

I have Bachelor's of Science in Physics and Computer Science from Carnegie Mellon University in Pittsburgh Pennsylvania.

After graduating I began pursuing a PhD at MIT while doing research at Lincoln Labs.

I decided to join a friend starting a company in Silicon Valley (http://www.cpacket.com) . Our goal was to build an innovative new kind of deep packet inspection ASIC. I was responsible the architecture and implementation of a proprietary embedded OS for the chip as well as the compiler and programming language that controlled the ASIC's behavior.

I did this for over two years before moving to Microsoft as a Program Manager for an Enterprise Security product. After shipping the v1.0 of Forefront Client Security, and helping provide the vision for v2.0, I decided I wanted to move out of enterprise products and move more towards building products for the consumer space. In reality, I wanted to start building products that I wanted to use myself. I then accepted a job in the Live! Search Platform team where I was the Program Manager responsible for the index serving platform's performance and efficiency.

Next I moved back to the Bay to help a friend start http://www.touchrev.com. Find out about Nimble by searching for "touch revolution nimble".

Most recently I have founded a small software company named Levitas. We are currently licensing a server for embedded Android platforms that provides system updates, crash reporting, performance monitoring as well as other device management features. The server is currently used for internal development as well as limited customer pilots and field trials.

My long-term goals are to continue to deliver innovative products to people that solve real world problems or enrich the way people communicate and interact.
Right now, I am primarily interested in innovative web services and their intersection with touch-based applications for mobile and non-mobile electronic devices.
