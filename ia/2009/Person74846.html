Eric J. Steiner’s Summary

Eric J. Steiner is currently studying for the Virginia Bar exam to be held July 28-29, 2009.

Mr. Steiner recently served a semester clerkship with the U.S. Election Assistance Commission (EAC), Office of General Counsel, from January to May 2009.

Prior to working for the EAC, Mr. Steiner was appointed by President George W. Bush to serve as Chief of Staff for the United States Department of Agriculture's (USDA) Food, Nutrition and Consumer Services (FNCS). Mr. Steiner supervised 1,300 employees and managed a budget exceeding $60 billion. He was responsible for improving the health and well-being of Americans by developing and promoting science-based dietary guidance and administering USDA's 15 nutrition assistance programs. FNCS programs work to end hunger in the United States and provide nutrition assistance, dietary guidance, nutrition policy coordination, and nutrition education.

Before being named FNCS Chief of Staff, Mr. Steiner served as Associate Administrator for Special Nutrition Programs at the USDA Food and Nutrition Service. Prior to his work at USDA, he served 4 years as professional staff of the U.S. Senate Committee on Agriculture, Nutrition, and Forestry, under the leadership of then-Chairman Thad Cochran of Mississippi and the Committee's current Ranking Republican Member, Saxby Chambliss of Georgia. Before working for the U.S. Senate, he served a year as a Bill Emerson National Hunger Fellow with the Congressional Hunger Center.

Mr. Steiner earned a Bachelor of Science degree in agricultural education from Purdue University, was licensed to teach agricultural science and business in Indiana high schools from 2002-2007, and earned a Juris Doctor degree from the George Mason University School of Law in May 2009.

Eric J. Steiner’s Specialties:

Federal nutrition assistance programs (both domestic and international), program management, Congressional testimony, policy development, facilitation, legislative drafting, technical writing and editing, public speaking, and graphic arts and design.
