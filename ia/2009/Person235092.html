Todd Borghesani, Esq.’s Summary

Todd specializes in bridging the cultures and capabilities of our federal agencies with the interactive entertainment industry. Bridging these worlds, he works across the areas of entertainment, education, training, prototyping, product development and research. He serves the interests of federal agencies, non-profits, interactive entertainment studios, interactive entertainment technology companies, and modeling and simulation enterprises. He has worked domestically and internationally with state and federal governments and has worked in entrepreneurial, large corporate and federal agency settings.

Todd Borghesani, Esq.’s Specialties:

Specialties include opportunity identification and acquisition, business plans, technology proposals, competitive intelligence reports, new product development, federal partnerships, strategic alliances, technology transfer and interdisciplinary team development.

Todd Borghesani, Esq.’s Experience

  • Chief Creative Officer

    REDGLU

    (Privately Held; Computer Games industry)

    April 2009 — Present (4 months)

    We specialize in the convergent space of modeling, simulation and interactive entertainment. Our production solution creates opportunities for game studios, entertainment technology companies, modeling and simulation enterprises as well as federal agencies. The "federal world" is both beneficiary and driver of this convergence.

    The modeling and simulation industry powers innovation across every industry, including aerospace, defense, energy and life sciences. The interactive entertainment industry offers unparalleled creativity and opportunities across hardware technologies, cloud computing, middleware, virtual worlds, games and animation. Increasingly, the interactive entertainment industry needs the "real world" expertise and technologies of federal agencies, their labs and contractors.

    Our production solution offers game studios and publishers the ability to leverage the federal world as a dynamic source of creativity, expertise and authentic content. We work with studios and publishers to execute (1) federal agency partnerships, (2) unique approaches to in-game advertising and (3) structured financing.

  • Partner, Business Development

    Ballyhoo Studios

    (Computer Games industry)

    2008 — Present (1 year)

    Ballyhoo Studios, Inc. develops computer games and training simulations, also known as "serious games". Ballyhoo has extensive experience in the research, development, and creation of serious games focusing on the training concerns of military, medical, and homeland defense partners. We specialize in modeling real-world challenges and environments.

    Ballyhoo core team members have been instrumental in the creation of many successful, and very creative, commercial strategy games, ranging from the original Civilization (MicroProse) to the more recent Rise of Nations (Microsoft) and Ubisoft’s The Settlers: Heritage of Kings. We also have extensive experience in the creation of seriously successful strategy games, including BreakAway’s mōsbē™, a real-time, operational-level wargame created for use by the U.S. Army, and the Integrated Gaming System and Entropy-Based Warfare Model, an operational-level simulation and toolset used extensively by the U.S military, including the U.S Army’s Training and Doctrine Command, Office of the Secretary of Defense - Net Assessment, National Defense University, NATO War College, and Joint Forces Command.

  • Research & Development

    IncredibleMinds Nonprofit

    (Primary/Secondary Education industry)

    2007 — Present (2 years)

    As a career education program, youth will have the opportunity to explore, discover and become scientists, technologists, engineers and mathematicians (STEM). In an afterschool setting, students will roleplay "characters" that face real world challenges. They will confront and solve these challenges as interdisciplinary teams using industry modeling and simulation tools. Our goal is to create an extensible career roleplaying framework where challenges scale in complexity across a wide range of domains (e.g. aerospace, molecular biology, nanotechnology, etc.). Cross-industry and inter-agency application is the centerpiece of our growth strategy.

    Because of their incredibly high appeal to youth, we use playing commercial video games as the conceptual link to “playing” with professional, industry modeling and simulation tools. Modeling and simulation tools offer powerful, hands-on ways to re-create reality, visualize engineered systems and scientific phenomena, model complex systems, and engage youth in the practices of STEM professionals. We design each roleplaying challenge using authentic career pursuits and real world problems.

    The Federation of American Scientists is the research lead. Advisors and supporters include AEgis Technologies, Boeing, Colgate University, Department of Energy, Federal Aviation Administration, George Mason University, Marquette University, National Aeronautics and Space Administration, Parametric Technology Corporation, Raytheon and State of Washington.

  • Education Program Development Manager

    Parametric Technology Corporation

    (Public Company; PMTC; Computer Software industry)

    2007 — 2008 (1 year)

    PTC has over 49,000 customers across 30 countries. The company's software solutions include mechanical computer-aided design (CAD), engineering calculation and Internet-based collaboration. The PTC Global Education Team brings PTC’s unique product development solutions into schools and universities in 30 countries. Globally, PTC has taught 4.0 Million K12 students using Pro/ENGINEER CAD.

    -- Invented the Department of Energy's Real World Design Challenge with PTC's Director of Worldwide Education. Each annual challenge is a live action role playing game that uses authentic scientific and engineering pursuits to form the back story. Working as global engineering teams, students take on real roles to solve real problems using real simulation and modeling tools. Their efforts are supported by mentors from Oak Ridge National Laboratory using a professional, global engineering backbone.

    -- Led the game-based learning project for PTC’s Director of Europe, Middle East and Africa Education. The Federation of American Scientists was contracted to help develop the means for students to design and build mechanical solutions in Pro/Engineer and then work with them in game worlds. The goal was to strengthen the bridge between game worlds and simulation tools.

    -- Created the high level architecture for PTC's Education Portal with PTC’s Director of North American Education. The portal uniquely combines content management and learning management systems with a social network and PTC's Windchill collaboration software. Using the social network with Windchill allowed students to share ideas, build global engineering teams and then work in private, digital project spaces.

  • Business Development Outsource

    Serious Games Consulting

    (Computer Games industry)

    2005 — 2007 (2 years)

    Serious Games is an emerging sector within the entertainment video game industry ($12 billion) that uses video game technologies and entertainment industry creativity for education, training and social change. There is a need to bridge myriad industries, federal agencies and academia to create unique game-based solutions that have educational, social and commercial value.

    -- Invented the concept for the "NASA Official Space Exploration Game." The game platform uniquely bridged NASA Communications, Education and Operations by promoting international space exploration; advancing the state of science and engineering education and offering a collaborative simulation platform, respectively. The game concept was built around a new type of play: First Person Exploration. FPS used the first person perspective to visualize scientific phenomena and engineered systems; and ties that visualization to career development and gameplay in team-based exploration missions. Concept was presented at the National Academies.

    -- Developed a math and science game opportunity for America's Army contractor with the internationally renowned Ewing Marion Kauffman Foundation. “GameLab” explored the use of lateral and systems thinking in game-based learning. The opportunity brings together learning scientists and game developers.

    -- Developed a novel concept for the DoD with the founder of America’s Army Future Applications to advance science and engineering education. “SimChallenge” is an ecosystem of digital design challenges, which combine real-world simulation tools with thematically linked adventure mini-games.
