David J. Mulvihill’s Summary

David J. Mulvihill is Assistant General Counsel with Bank of America Corporation's Legal Department. Mr. Mulvihill joined Bank of America through its acquisition of Chicago-based LaSalle Bank Corporation, where his practice concentrated on legal process and litigation management, estate, trust and probate law, fraud and risk management, banking procedures, compliance and regulation. His current role focuses on support of Bank of America's deposit and legal order processing operations in 17 states and its military bank. Mr. Mulvihill received his B.A. in Politics, Magna Cum Laude, from the Catholic University of America, Washington, D.C., his J.D. from the Chicago-Kent College of Law, Illinois Institute of Technology, and his LL.M. in Financial Services Law with Honors from Chicago-Kent. He has been a featured speaker at the Illinois Institute for Continuing Legal Education, the Northwest Suburban Bar Association, the DuPage and Cook County Bar Associations, the Chicago Bar Association, the Land Trust Council of Illinois and the National Association of Elder Law Attorneys on various topics related to bank policy and estate, trust, probate law.

David J. Mulvihill’s Specialties:

The Supreme Court of Illinois does not recognize certifications of specialties in the practice of law, and I do not hold myself out as a specialist. However, I concentrate my practice in the following areas: Bank Compliance and Regulation, Fraud Investigations and Risk Management, Estate, Trust and Probate Law, Legal Process, Discovery and Litigation Management.
