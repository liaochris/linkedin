Julian Medina’s Summary

Julian Medina is Founder and CEO of Sourcing Up.

Entrepreneur and Executive with a degree in System Engineer and more than 10 years of experience in the offshore/outsourcing business, Julian combines his strong technical background with his business administration knowledge and variety of contacts in the Argentinean market.

Sourcing Up specializes in software development using Ruby on Rails, PHP and Flex.
We have great graphic designs ready to work close with you to make your site success.



We have also a division that creates any kind of content (original text, merge, audio, video) to your site.

Before creating Sourcing Up, Julian was Vice President, Argentine Operations at Quick Software. From 2002 to 2007, made the company grow from 1 employee to more than 40. Quick Software Solutions has developed solutions for the energy and healthcare market with clients like Walmart, Energy Savings, ISTa and many others.

Previous to Quick Software Solutions, Julian worked on two companies and cofounded another one.

He has worked on software and content solutions for North America, Latin America, Europe and India.

He is also teaching "Marketing, Internet and Economy" in the National Technology University (UTN).

    Quick Software Solutions

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Computer Software industry)

    April 2002 — February 2008 (5 years 11 months)

    Information technology managers who believe that there is a niche market for small project development founded Quick Software Solutions in April 2002. It has become evident that while most companies focus on large projects or staff augmentation, few companies focus on aiding business analysts with small projects. The reason for this is simply cost. It is very difficult for a company solely based in the US to carry the marketing and development costs associated with small projects. Although using offshore development would seem to be a good way to defer the development cost, most countries offering lower labor costs are in a different time zone. Therefore, our challenge was to find a lower labor cost within the same time zone to allow us a competitive advantage with projects with tighter deadlines. The solution is to use developers in South America. We have found a well-educated work force and reliable broadband connectivity.
