  • Marketing Representative

    Decision Data Computer Corporation - fmr NASDAQ sy DDCC

    (Computer Hardware industry)

    March 1981 — June 1984 (3 years 4 months)

    Based just outside of Philadelphia, in the IBM middle and mainframe markets, I am still in awe that I was a part of this company and a part of computer history. Looking back and realizing just how incredible this firm was, it absolutely moves me. DDCC was NOT the first of its kind. Memorex, Telex and Raytheon (not to mention IBM) all competed in the same market space. On the verge of extinction, this company was taken over by Richard J Schineller as CEO whom pushed DDCC to become one of the most reputable and respected middle market peripheral manufactures on the planet. As a sales rep., I saw incredible paychecks. As a shareholder, I saw my stock go from pennies to over $20 per share in less than 18 months. I cannot begin to thank everyone at DDCC enough for everything you did for me and taught me. My only regret is that I left you and that I should have held my stock and bought more. Hind sight is always 20/20. To everyone I worked with and helped me, THANK YOU...SO MUCH!
