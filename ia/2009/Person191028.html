Jeff Purser’s Summary

Jeff Purser has an entrepreneurial bent. He enjoys examining opportunities to find strategic solutions, and has a keen ability to research, create, and execute campaigns to both launch new businesses and reinvigorate global brands.

His first job out of college was with a startup residential design/build firm; he quickly became an indispensible asset by designing and implementing a successful marketing strategy over a four-year period. In his third year with the company, he was promoted to vice president of marketing and construction operations.

Two years later, Jeff moved to Boston. As a business school student, he spent a year working full time at Dunkin’ Brands where he investigated and exploited opportunities within the competitive space. Jeff’s recommendations to blunt McDonald’s launch of lattes, cappuccinos, and mochas were implemented after his findings from field-based research convinced executives to prioritize a response.

Jeff has consulted with a number of companies and nonprofits including Mars, Reebok, and Habitat for Humanity. He has a real passion to find sustainable solutions to eliminate extreme poverty. Jeff currently volunteers as a neighborhood organizer for Fair Trade Boston and lives near Central Square in Cambridge.
