Dan Brazil’s Summary

Passionate about computers and music from an early age, my digital career began over a decade ago in the music industry as a qualified sound engineer before moving into record production. Running my own studio and releasing a clutch of successful underground remixes under my own label enabled me to collaborate with some big names in the Dance Music scene as well as hone my skills in the art of delivering a digital product. The greatest commercial success was in September 2003 with a number 3 hit in the UK National Charts with the 'So Solid Crew'.

Having achieved my goals, and excited about the digital possibilities outside of music, I transitioned into multimedia and web development. Following successful completion of an MA in Computer Arts in 2004, I joined the embryonic Digital Media Services (DMS) as their New Media Manager and fulfilled the roles of designer, programmer and technological evangelist for a client base grounded in the film distribution sector - initially UIP but later including SRP, Warner, Disney and Fox. As the business developed I recruited a production team around me and set the company up as a fully-fledged digital agency/media monitoring operation that grew to over twenty-five strong when I joined FullSIX in 2007.

My role as Studio Production Manager in FullSIX and Six & Co saw me responsible for the scheduling and management of an extremely busy studio environment producing eCRM and a wealth of other digital communications for the likes of Proctor and Gamble, Orange, Sky and Hertz.

The offer of joining Delete London as their Senior Producer/Project Manager seemed a very natural career progression to make since it drew on the sum total of my digital production experience to date. I survey the client's requirements, appreciate the complexities and pitfalls of undertaking the work, contribute to quotations and, together with my fellow producers, deliver the work on time and on budget through effective studio resource management.
