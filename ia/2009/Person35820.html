Burns Cheadle’s Summary

I am a geologist with 23 years of experience in the Canadian domestic oil and gas industry. My interests are in the identification and development of unconventional resource plays. To date I have been involved in both the technical and management aspects of oil sands, coalbed methane, oil shale, tight gas and frontier gas plays.

My goal is to continuously broaden my technical expertise as a geologist, while participating in energy development projects as a key decision-maker.

Burns Cheadle’s Specialties:

clastic sedimentology and stratigraphy, data mining, mapping, prospect risk analysis, petrophysics, hydrodynamics, bypassed pay evaluation, assessment and basin analysis

  • President

    Outrider Energy Ltd.

    (Oil & Energy industry)

    April 2003 — April 2009 (6 years 1 month)

    Outrider was a private junior E&P corporation founded in 2003 to pursue unconventional resource plays. Outrider's assets included conventional oil and natural gas production, as well as substantial potential resource plays in coalbed methane and oil shale. Outrider was acquired in a private transaction in April 2009.

  • Manager, Geology

    Koch Petroleum Canada

    (Oil & Energy industry)

    August 1997 — December 2002 (5 years 5 months)

    As Koch Petroleum Canada's Chief Geologist, I provided technical oversight for exploration and development work in Koch's heavy oil and oil sands programs. During this time, I was the exploration lead for the early stages of the Fort Hills Oil Sands Project, as well as originating Koch's Leismer and Christina Lake SAGD properties.

  • Senior Research Geologist

    Imperial Oil Limited

    (Oil & Energy industry)

    May 1986 — August 1997 (11 years 4 months)

    The first half of my time at Imperial, I was involved in conventional exploration in Western Canada, Beaufort Sea exploration and operations, and research (reservoir quality) support for Deep Basin and Triassic teams. The second half of my life at Imperial was focused on oil sands research leading to the development of a new reservoir model for the Clearwater Formation at Cold Lake, as well as involvement in development of follow-up recovery processes to succeed CSS at Cold Lake.
