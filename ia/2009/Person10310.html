Alta Cannaday’s Summary

Over 25 years experience in private sector and nonprofit organizational management with proven record of accomplishment in fundraising for academia and non-profit organizations. Expertise in corporate and foundation relations, grantsmanship, major gifts, design and transformation, and diversity planning and implementation. Serves client institutions in the US/US Virgin Islands, Papua New Guinea, Sweden and the Republic of South Africa.

CEOproviding consultation services to nonprofit organizations, serving primarily HIV/AIDS, educational, women’s, legal justice organizations and museums. Services include but are not limited to: Starter or Transition Services; Fundraising Plans; Fundraising Technology; Annual Giving, Major Gifts; Proposal Development; Grants writing and Proposal Writing Workshops/Training; and, Gift Acknowledgement. Two-decade career in executive management included the SEED Foundation, Delta Research and Educational Foundation, National Minority AIDS Council, Howard University, WAVE, Inc., Urban League, Avon and Sears. Uses metrics approach and in-depth industry knowledge for client partnerships. Cannaday’s client list includes: PROCEED, Inc., Center for Disease Control, Smithsonian Institution, Connecticut Department of Health, Advancement Project, Altria Group, Bank of America, BET Foundation, Eli Lilly, Executive Leadership Council Foundation, Institute of Women and Ethnic Studies, General Mills, Life & Liberty, Inc., Lucent Technologies, PepsiCo, Raytheon, Total Services Solutions and Tuskegee University,

Currently serves as Region II Chair, Howard University Alumni Association and as President of the Board of Directors of Health Education Resources Organization (HERO), Baltimore, MD, and volunteers with numerous community organizations. A graduate of Howard University, member of Delta Sigma Theta Sorority, Inc., Prince Georges County, MD Chapter, Reid Temple AME Church, and the Association of Fundraising Professionals (AFP).
