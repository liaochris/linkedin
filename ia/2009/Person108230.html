Jeff McFadden’s Summary

Technology-savvy, entrepreneurial executive with a passion for creating and marketing great products based upon innovative technology and business models. Veteran of four start-up ventures including three Internet companies focused on consumer online services, advertising, direct marketing and marketing analytics.

Jeff McFadden’s Specialties:

General management (CEO/GM), product strategy, product management, business development, new venture formation.

Jeff McFadden’s Experience

  • Principal

    McFadden Associates

    (Internet industry)

    2008 — Present (1 year)

    Provide management and strategy consulting services to internet and technology companies. Specialize in Product Management/Marketing, Venture Management (interim or part-time CEO), Market Development, and Business Development.

  • CEO

    M-Factor, Inc.

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Information Technology and Services industry)

    2006 — 2008 (2 years)

    Hired as CEO by the founder and board of directors of this venture-backed start-up, whose software-as-a-service offering optimizes the brand marketing spend and product pricing for large consumer goods and services enterprises. Raised $12.5 million in equity and debt financing from Norwest Venture Partners, US Venture Partners, Bay Partners and Pinnacle Ventures. Doubled revenue run rate and grew team from 15 to 35, recruiting entire sales and services staff. Closed major new accounts including Kellogg’s, Campbell’s Soup, Heinz, Alberto Culver and Dean Foods (Horizon milk). Migrated M-Factor’s software products from client/server to a hosted web service architecture.

  • Board member

    ClickShift

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Information Technology and Services industry)

    2007 — 2007 (less than a year)

    After informally acting as an advisor to ClickShift's founder/CEO for a year, I joined the board of directors as the industry expert. Clickshift uses complex statistical models to optimize web advertising. Acquired by Webtrends, Inc.

  • Entrepreneur-in-residence

    U.S. Venture Partners

    (Partnership; 11-50 employees; Venture Capital & Private Equity industry)

    2005 — 2006 (1 year)

    Assisted the firm in sourcing and evaluating new investment opportunities in the online services and media sectors

  • CEO

    Claria Corporation

    (Privately Held; 201-500 employees; Online Media industry)

    1998 — 2005 (7 years)

    Co-founded Claria Corporation, widely recognized as both the market leader and thought leader in the online behavioral marketing business. Raised $60M in financing from leading venture firms (US Venture Partners, TCV, Greylock, Crosslink, and Investor Growth Capital) and prominent angel investors, including Andy Bechtolsheim. Grew revenue to $120MM+/year. Recruited a staff of 250 employees in the US, Europe, and Asia.

    Recognized as one of the “Top 10 Companies to Watch” by Industry Standard magazine.

    Grew Claria’s online audience to 40+ million users. Created one of the world’s largest consumer profile databases (150 Terabytes), and a data mining/processing infrastructure consisting of over 2 thousand servers.

    Secured direct relationships with 1000+ advertisers, including Expedia, United Airlines, Travelocity, Procter & Gamble, Sears, Mastercard, Bank of America, Citigroup, Daimler/Chrysler, Toyota, Honda, Universal Studios, AT&T, FTD, and Federal Express.

  • VP Business Development

    excite.com

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; Online Media industry)

    1995 — 1997 (2 years)

    Joined Excite.com as the first non-founder and seventh employee. Authored Excite’s first product plan and business plan, transitioning the company’s business model from a search engine software company to a free, advertising-supported “portal.”

    Recruited product management, distribution, and content acquisition staff. Established distribution relationships with Netscape, Microsoft, Apple, and WebTV, securing sufficient traffic to propel Excite from last place to second place in the search engine market, enabling a successful IPO in April, 1996.

    Originated the product concept for Excite NewsTracker, the first personalized news service to be launched by a portal company. Initiated Excite Channels, a restructuring of the Excite service into News, Sports, Entertainment and similar “channels,” that directly yielded $85 million in new sponsorship transactions within six months. Secured the lead position in Fortune Magazine’s annual “25 Cool Companies” feature.

  • Director, Product Marketing

    Sun Microsystems

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; SUNW; Computer Hardware industry)

    1990 — 1994 (4 years)

    Responsible for the product planning, pricing, positioning, and promotion of Sun’s $2.5 Billion/year “Sparcstation” product line which generated well over half of the company’s revenue. Defined a new product line (the SPARCstation 5 and SPARCstation 20) whose launch ended four years of flat stock market performance and declining market share for Sun. Within 6 months of their launch, these new products yielded a 49% increase in workstation volume and a 131% increase in corporate profits, fueling a 50% increase in Sun’s share price. Reported to Vice President of Product Marketing and managed a staff of 21.

  • Regional Director, San Francisco Bay Area

    Sun Microsystems

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; SUNW; Computer Hardware industry)

    1987 — 1990 (3 years)

    Managed Sun’s largest sales region. Grew business from $31 million to $154 million while building and managing a staff of 145 sales, support, and consulting professionals. Exceeded revenue goals by an average of more than 20% per year. Selected as Western Area Regional Director of the Year in 1988 and 1989.

    Led the company’s entrance into and eventual domination of the electronic design automation (EDA) market, growing Sun's EDA business from near zero to $32 million in two years. Developed Sun’s partnerships with Oracle, Sybase, Informix and Ingres that resulted in Sun’s leadership position in the database server systems market.
