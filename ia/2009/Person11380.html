Amira Annabi’s Summary

Background and Academic achievements:

I have just finished my Ph.D degree in finance at HEC Montreal. My major research interests are in credit risk and more specifically financial distress and capital structure, as well as the application of game theory to corporate finance.

During my Master of Science in financial engineering, I acquired strong quantitative skills that allowed me understanding the modeling and the valuation of complex financial securities. My doctoral classes and various seminars that I attended in the last few years further deepened my ability to abstract complex models and relate them to empirical issues in financial markets. In my Ph.D. dissertation, I theoretically investigate the reorganization process under Chapter 11 of the bankruptcy code. Despite extensive empirical studies on Chapter 11, very little has been done to incorporate all the features of public bankruptcy into a credit risk model. This line of research increased my interest in working on practical and complex issues, and providing tractable solutions.

Conferences and teaching:

I had the opportunity to present my research at several conferences which enabled me to improve my presentation skills. Teaching has also been an important part of my training during my PhD. I have taught several courses at the undergraduate levels and much enjoyed the experience.

Languages, skills and interests,

*I am trilingual French, English and Arabic. I also speak intermediate Spanish.

*I am a very entrepreneurial person with a strong sense of initiative. For instance, I managed and coordinated a team to raise funds to support the Canadian Breast Cancer Foundation. More recently, I joined ‘Learning Leaders’, a non-profit organization that provides free tutoring and support for New York public schools’ students.

*I have also been involved in Spanish and pottery classes.
