    (Self-Employed; Myself Only; Publishing industry)

    August 2001 — December 2005 (4 years 5 months)

    Wrote numerous freelance articles for Travel & Leisure Golf, Golf For Women, Discover, Departures, Food Arts, New Jersey Life, New Jersey Monthly.

    In September, 2002, wrote a New York Times Home Section article about people (including my son) whose birthday is 9/11. The most unusual was an orthodox Jewish woman who gave birth that day in a hospital within sight of the Twin Towers. Going into the delivery room, she was as shocked and horrified as anyone, but she is not disturbed about her son's birthdate, because she and her husband go by the Hebrew calendar, meaning her son's birthday will fall on a different day each year in the regular calendar.

    A 2002 story I wrote for Food Arts about one Manhattan restaurant's response to 9/11 was later reprinted in "Best Food Writing, 2003," edited by Holly Hughes.
