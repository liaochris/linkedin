Randy Dunaway’s Summary

The Educational Technology market has been my passion for over thirty years , I've witnessed the evolution from Mainframe/dumb ASCII delivery (Control PLATO), to floppy based delivery (MECC), to LAN and Internet content Delivery.

I have designed and managed development of Curriculum Management systems, Spearheaded development of revolutionary products, such as the first LocalTalk to EtherTalk router (the JDL Excellerator), and designed delivery mechanisms, such as the test delivery system for Achievement Technologies.

I've been engaged with the delivery of Educational content from Basic Ed, to Certification delivery, to technical trainers for HVAC and Electronics (Innovative Technologies in Education).

Over my career I have worked in several different capacities, primarily serving in Technology Management roles, from; Product, Project Management to People Management of organizations. Most recently I served as a Field Engineer for Plato Learning, Inc. which gave me hands-on experience integrating technology products with new and existing network environments. Dealing with Real-World integration/product cohabitation issues. It has provided me with an incognito opportunity to see how many of the products with which I have been involved were utilized (and too often not utilized) in schools.
