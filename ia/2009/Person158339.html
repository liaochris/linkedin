  • Risk Analyst

    Dalhousie University

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    July 2004 — June 2006 (2 years)

    Developing risk model and formulation of Formal Safety Assessment framework for Large Passenger Vessel Risks (in adherence to International Maritime Organization’s standards)
    for large passenger vessel incidents in Atlantic Canada.

    Client: Canadian Coast Guard

    Facilitated in organizing an Expert Opinion Elicitation Workshop, conducted to gather experts’ consensus on the estimates of marine risk factors
    Analyzed statistical summaries of 75 marine risk factors to quantify the risk profiles of marine accidents

  • Graduate Research Assistant

    Dalhousie University

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    September 2002 — June 2004 (1 year 10 months)

    Produced a thesis on “Probabilistic Site Characterization”, a project funded by Natural Sciences and Engineering Research Council of Canada, Government of Canada

    Characterization of spatial soil variability:
    Programmed Monte-Carlo simulations to analyze the Markovian and fractal correlation models
    Statistically analyzed six soil sites for determining the nature of spatial soil variability
    Established a relation between the effective scale (and variance) and the size of soil site
    Recommended generic values for practical use

    Limit State Design of single footing under settlement:
    Demonstrated probabilistic site characterization using CPT data (NGES, USA)
    Designed shallow footing (under settlement) using Load Resistant Factor Design criteria

  • Senior Research Fellow

    Risk analysis of soil slopes and landslides

    Modeled and analyzed the influence of spatial soil variability on the stability of slopes
    Examined case studies (slopes in Himalayan ranges) to investigate the isotropic spatial correlation effects on the probabilistic failure of slopes
    Developed a hazard map by integrating a GIS model with earthquake and rainfall induced slope failure models for a typical landslide (Mangalore, India)
