Ephraim Luft’s Summary

Working on a start up, Circle of Moms, that I founded in August 2007 with Mike Greenfield, a classmate of mine from Stanford.

I have spent my career in developing technologies and services in the digital media sector. Previously had leadership roles in product management and business development at Massive Incorporated, an in-game ad network acquired by Microsoft in May 2006. Prior to that worked on products for the cable industry at both Microsoft and Comcast.

Interested in exploring potential partnerships for my company, Circle of Moms, and meeting other people that share the same professional interests as me.

Expertise: entrepreneurship, building and managing next generation ad networks, consumer internet, social networking, product management, and negotiations.

    Circle of Moms

    (Internet industry)

    August 2007 — Present (1 year 7 months)

    Circle of Moms is a San Francisco-based start up backed by some of the top angel investors in Silicon Valley. With over 850,000 users, it is the fastest growing and second largest social network for moms on the Internet. It helps moms connect and address the challenges of motherhood together. This includes activities such as advice on parenting and purchase decisions. Check out our site, http://www.circleofmoms.com, and our Facebook application, http://apps.facebook.com/circleofmoms.

    I am interested in meeting marketers, brand managers or BD folk interested in reaching our audience of mothers. This could be for advertising or partnership purposes. For brands, we offer an array of sponsorship packages that allow moms to embrace and interact around the brand's core messaging.

    We also run Circle of Friends, a product which allows people to build small groups of friends who have common interests. It is one of the largest applications on Facebook (over 10 million installs).
