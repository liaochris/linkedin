Antony Brydon’s Summary

Most recently, Antony Brydon served as chief executive officer of Visible Path Corporation, a corporate social networking company funded by Kleiner Perkins and acquired by Dun & Bradstreet in January 2008.

Prior to Visible Path, Antony was vice president and general manager at eMusic, acquired by Vivendi-Universal in 2001.

Prior to eMusic, Antony was chief executive officer of IUMA, a digital music company acquired by publicly-traded eMusic in 1998.

From 1995 to 1998, Antony was an analyst at R. B. Webber & Company, a strategy consulting firm and venture fund founded by principals from McKinsey, Bain and Boston Consulting Group.

Antony received a BA in psychology and philosophy from Yale University in 1995, where he was the founder and executive director of the Habitat Bicycle Challenge, a non-profit cycling group that coordinates cross-country bicycle trips to fund low-income housing development.
