Paul Ruffino’s Summary

Delivering the daily newspaper to legendary San Franciscan & Mark Hopkins Hotel owner, Louie Lurie was my “foot in the door” at 14 years old. As it turned out Mr. Lurie quit school in the third grade to sell newspapers on the corners in Chicago..the rest was a colorful history. So what job did a 14 year old Italian/American city kid get at a world class hotel in a union town? Two days a week after school and weekends I sat in the basement washing coins…getting the grime off so guests of the hotel wouldn’t sully their fingers on “dirty money”.

During educational respites at UC Berkeley, Cleveland Chiropractic College, St. Marys in London and Sorbonne in Paris I went between working in hospitality (Raphael Hotel, Alameda Plaza, Excelsior Rome, Clift Hotel & The Canterbury) and film & TV (Fantasy Records – Saul Zaentz, Martin Poll and Arnold Kopelson).

My friends and colleagues know the rest of the story…and I’m happy to share the journey with anyone that wants to know.

Oh…a word of warning, if the Aga Khan invites you to his yacht in Sardinia to assist him in the negotiations for the purchase of a Luxury Hotel chain and he tells you it’ll just take a week or two…don’t believe it. You’ll be buying socks for $40 a pair at the gift shops in Porto Cervo and your per diem will be shot in no time. I'm convinced that's how all the no socks with loafer trend started...sort of like George Costanza eating a candy bar with a knife & fork.

  • Partner - Sales Manager

    Pegasus Travel

    (Partnership; 11-50 employees; Retail industry)

    1976 — 1985 (9 years)

    For $15K purchased this small, dingy retail travel agency located on Van Ness Ave. in San Francisco. My partner was a foreign student studying at UCB and wanted a green card. With it came a 68 yr. old Manager, Avis Tierney. She taught me the technical aspects...we had no computers back then just OAG's & tariff guides...and I went door to door selling travel services. In two years we purchased 2 agencies, opened up 2 branch offices and had over 40 outside sales people selling travel. Innovative for the time! I took a 2 year hiatus to work at the Excelsior in Rome and unbeknownst to anyone oversaw the business from abroad...remember, no cell phones or fax machines. In 1980 opened branches in LA & San Diego. Went with Le Hermitage ( a killer offer)...my LA agency was two miles away...and did a stellar job for them while taking the Co. into the world of Entertainment Travel. Sold to AMF in 1985 for the opportunity to open a Hotel in Acapulco & HMS was born.
