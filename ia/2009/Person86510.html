Gilad Zlotkin’s Summary

Dr. Gilad Zlotkin, has twenty years of experience in the software and the telecommunication industries as an entrepreneur, innovator, developer, senior executive and leading scientist. Dr. Zlotkin has a Ph.D. in Distributed Systems from the Hebrew University in Jerusalem and was a Research Fellow at the MIT Sloan School of Management's Center for Coordination Science focusing on distributed resource management. Gilad is the founder of Xeround Systems and was President and CTO. Prior to founding Xeround Systems, Gilad was a Director, Engineering in Cisco Systems (Nasdaq: CSCO), managing the QPM (QoS Policy Management) development division. Gilad was VP Engineering at Class Data Systems that was acquired by Cisco. As a Co-founder Gilad served as a CEO of Picatel Systems, a Multimodal wireless data services company and the CTO of Radview Software (Nasdaq: RDVW), an e-business systems performance, scalability and integrity verification software company. He was also founder and President of AgentSoft, an Internet Agent software company that was acquired by Genesys/Alcatel (NYSE:ALA). Dr. Zlotkin has an extensive experience in building compilers for parallel and distributed computing environments. He has published numerous scientific articles in the field of Multiagent Systems and Distributed Artificial Intelligence, and has co-authored “Rules of Encounter: Designing Conventions for Automated Negotiation Among Computers” published by MIT Press in 1994

Gilad Zlotkin’s Specialties:

I am a serial entrepreneur, with proven track record of founding, managing and leading successful technology software companies. My field of expertise is distributed computation, high performance computing and networking.
