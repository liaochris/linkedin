  • Senior Analyst

    H&R Block

    (Public Company; HRB; Financial Services industry)

    June 2004 — July 2006 (2 years 2 months)

    As a Senior Analyst, I developed statistical models and Excel based tools, which together with recommendations drawn by data analysis I did helped the Wait Less Project to be one of the most successful projects at H&R Block during Tax Seasons 2004 and 2005. Also, I participated in interdisciplinary teams that saved the company millions of dollars. Some notable accomplishments during this assignment include:
    • As the technical expert in the cross-functional Forecasting Tax Professional Wages Team, I was instrumental in developing a labor cost forecasting methodology in 2005 that helped business leaders to achieve business goals by dramatically reducing the labor costs forecast error from $20 million in 2004 to minus $1 million in 2005.

    • Developed from the ground up of the queuing algorithms embedded in Scheduling Projection Tool (SPT) provided to the District Managers to help them plan their staffing needs for 2005, and later on 2006. I used Time Series to forecast client demand used as input for this tool, which was the first tool based on Mathematical Optimization used at H&R Block.
    • Analyzed customer-loss and developed a Negative Binomial Statistical model in order to find measures to reverse that loss.
    • With a peer developed time series forecasts to support bad debt collections of H&R Block clients.
    • By combining data from several datamarts produced a data driven process that enabled effective field labor management in 2005.
    • Determined testing procedure and analyzed the H&R Block marketing campaign “15 minutes or it is Free.”

  • Operations Research Analyst

    UPS

    (Public Company; UPS; Transportation/Trucking/Railroad industry)

    September 1996 — January 2003 (6 years 5 months)

    Developed Operations Research tools and served as Statistical Analyst in a variety of projects, which improved operations and saved money for the company. I developed innovative solutions applying and implementing mathematical models, which have been the basis for other systems currently used at UPS Supply Chain Solutions. Some notable accomplishments during this assignment include:
    • Developed from the ground up of the Sleeper Scheduling System for the long-haul UPS “Sleeper” trucks. These are the big UPS trucks seen on US highways driven by teams of two drivers. One driver drives while the other sleeps. They just stop to load, unload and for regulated rest breaks. The Sleeper Scheduling System satisfied Time Windows, Union Regulations, and balanced equipment. The Column Generation developed for this system has been the basis for other systems currently used in UPS Supply Chain solutions. I gathered Business Requirements and developed Project Management documentation. Determined data requirements. Developed Mathematical Model (Multi-commodity Flow Model) and prototyped it using OPL (Ilog) Software. One of the techniques I used to solve this problem was Column Generation. Together with a Senior Programmer coded the system in C++ and used CPLEX. Developed testing plans and completed documentation.
    • Developed statistical predictive models and scoring algorithms (CHAID, Regression Trees) to support business decisions related to UPS volume distributions: One Day Air, Second Day Air, and Ground.
    • Operations Research consultant in the team that developed a new “Departure Sequence” for the UPS planes that departed from the Louisville Airport for the “First Day Air” and “Second Day Air” services. This new sequence produced 30% cost savings over the old one.
    • From time to time, I helped different UPS departments on how to organize or analyze data in a more meaningful way.
