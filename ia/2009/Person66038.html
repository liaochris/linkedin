Don Zeppenfeld’s Summary

My expertise is with startup and turnaround opportunities. I joined the Atwork Corporation in 1992. I took over a sales organization that was selling Operating Room and Nurse scheduling systems. I introduced strategic selling to a sales force of 12, which allowed the team to focus on real opportunities and better predict their business outcomes. I grew the organization from 12- 32 sales people with 4 regional sales managers. Sales revenues grew from $5.6 M to $17.1 M from 1992-1996.

I joined HealthVISION in late 1996 after a failed IPO. I hired a world class sales organization, reinforced that team with a clinical support organization and took on Cerner and IDX. An entirely new marketing and sales program was born by early 1997. I grew sales and marketing organization from 3 people to 21 people in under 3 years. The company became cash flow positive in late 1998 and was sold to Eclipsys in November 1998. Most notable sales were Sutter Healthcare and Memorial Sloan Kettering.

I co-founded scheduling.com in 1999. This sales and marketing organization was built from the ground up. We established a business plan that would emphasize an ASP based solution for scheduling This sales and marketing organization was built from a team of individuals who had worked for me before in one of my previous companies. The sales organization was grown to 15 people with three regional managers.

Lastly I took over the sales organization at Addressograph Healthcare. This is a company who for the last 45 years was selling equipment and now wanted to sell software. I hired a software sales organization of 5 people and reinforced that team with 5 inside sales representatives. We closed 5 hospitals from September to December in 2005 and 22 hospitals in 2006. I was also able to increase the average selling price from 25k to 150K during that period.

Don Zeppenfeld’s Specialties:

I thrive in startup or turnaround opportunities. My strengths are conviction, creativity, honesty and strategy. I have been a co-founder of a business that is still viable where my partner and I raised over $44M in venture funding.
