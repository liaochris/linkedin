Gwyn Jones’s Summary

A case study in MBA startups, combining both technology & strategic vision, VistaPrint (co-founded by four INSEAD alumni & $2 billion market cap in December 2007) is one of the most successful startups from INSEAD, Europe's top business school. (Or from any European business school.)

Early stage practice helps: I joined my first tech startup (a pioneering printing company that had one of the first British tech IPOs), as a junior programmer at 18. Subsequently, I've worked in several fast-growing software and web-based startups, most notably Serif with 6 million software users, and VistaPrint with 17 million ecommerce customers. The success of these companies was driven by low cost, high value technology products, and extensive viral promotion using FREE Software! and FREE Business Cards! My latest startup, iSyndica, is another mass-market web service, this time operating "in the cloud" with Amazon Web Services.

I have an engineering backgound (with an MS in Information Systems) combined with product/marketing insight (with a top-tier MBA). My emphasis is always on rapid release cycles, and constant poduct iteration... together with free-based viral marketing. I am a speaker on entrepreneurship and ecommerce, and latterly Cloud Computing. I have lectured at several universities, and started my PhD in Management at Said Business School, University of Oxford, before deciding that I much preferred startups to academia. Over 20 years experience as a senior (C-level or VP) entrepreneurial executive, and as a company director in both private and public companies.
