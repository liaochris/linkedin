John Becker’s Summary

Seeking to serve as leadership development and performance improvement manager and facilitator with others seeking organizational performance excellence. John has been successful achieving dramatic organizational improvements using a wide variety of tools and techniques such as formal accreditation, Baldrige, Sterling and WSQA organizational performance criteria, process mapping, performance measurement and benchmarking, strategic planning, and all with a keen eye toward improving the true backbone of organizations' financial, human resources and technology improvement approaches (the three-legged stool approach to organizational success). John's goal is to continue to support and assist others who are like-minded, value leadership collaboration, and share these same sorts of ideas for organizational improvement and innovation.

John Becker’s Specialties:

Areas of special interest include: local government and not-for-profit governance systems, leadership, facilitation, internal consulting, team/train-the-trainer/cadre-focused teaching, internal consulting systems, strategic planning, process management, performance measurement, benchmarking, and all ways possible to demand the best from those with whom we choose to work.

  • Community Indicators-Performance Measurement Marketing Committee Chairperson

    Community Indicators Consortium

    (Non-Profit; Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    July 2008 — Present (1 year 6 months)

    As a representative of the Florida Benchmarking Consortium (FBC), working with select leaders from the Community Indicators Consortium (CIC: http://www.communityindicators.net/) to facilitate the improved alignment between Community Indicators (CI)-focused organizations and governments' evolving performance measurement and performance management efforts. Like minded leasders from around the country are targeting CI organizations that have been establishd to identify and celebrate CI-PM best practices. The CIC wants to also support and encourage CI organizations that still need to be established and otherwise improved. This far reaching effort is aimed at aligning all such CI organizational efforts with the performance measurement initiatives of local, regional and state government organizations that impact their communities. This is the purpose of, and focus for this Alfred P. Sloan Foundation supported work. CI-PM partner organizations include: CIC, FBC, GASB, NAC, ICMA, AGA, NLC and several more (see complete listing of CIC Working Group partner organizations at): http://www.communityindicators.net/CIPM/partners.html )
