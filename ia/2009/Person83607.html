Gary Blosser’s Summary

Acquiring an early interest in technology and computers Gary has been working with and helping others on computers through his entire life, from writing programs in BASIC on an Atari 800 at 9 years old to setting up the computer lab at his junior high school to his professional careers and education. Throughout his career he has become proficient in a broad range of skills including network administration, programming, system architecture, research, cost justification, and presentations.

In his research role, he performed in both a management and collaboration role by leading research meetings when the lead researcher was overseas and developed experimental applications to prove the viability of co-workers theories. He also attended and presented papers at conferences around Asia, sharpening his existing skills.

As a student, Gary learned the information technology and management skills taught through Carnegie Mellon University's master of science in information technology and information security degree getting the second highest score in his class, with a perfect 4.0 on his masters project entitled "Privacy Preserving Network Visualization". He was also hired, and is still working, as a paid part-time teaching assistant for the students due to his understanding of the material and ability to present the material.

When programming, he has performed all parts of application development from flying to the client and gathering requirements to patching bugs and adding features in operational systems. He is knowledgeable about most programming languages and can learn new ones in days with only a basic reference book. His most heavily and recently used languages include C, Java, and C#.net.

In his network administration role, Gary has set up wide ranging WAN systems, secured networks, built mail servers, repaired co-workers computers, and performed the other day to day tasks of ensuring the security and reliability required of a corporate network.
