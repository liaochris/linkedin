Steve Curati’s Summary

I am a highly experienced digital strategist and online/project manager. I have successfully provided innovative, creative and technical digital solutions, in a managerial capacity and as a consultant. Both articulate and literate, I’m as happy presenting my work as I am doing it.

Having gained 5 years of experience in the commercial/retail sector, I then worked across a number of charities before moving to D&AD. Over a period of 3 years, I overhauled D&AD’s digital presence through the use of social media and was instrumental in taking their unique content to a global online audience.

Steve Curati’s Specialties:

Digital strategy, social media, digital project management, online communications, writing/editing for web, SEO.

Steve Curati’s Experience

  • Senior Online Manager

    D&AD

    (Non-Profit; Design industry)

    March 2006 — July 2009 (3 years 5 months)

    During financially difficult times, I steered D&AD to huge growth in digital engagement, through our own sites and throughout the extended web.

    Notable achievements included:
    Formulating D&AD’s online strategy through the main D&AD website and other microsites.

    INtroducing the D&AD blog and reaching out into several social networks including Flickr, Youtube, Facebook, LinkedIn, Twitter, Vimeo.

    I was instrumental in getting D&AD President’s Lectures filmed, online and on to iTunes, clocking up 25,000 views as of June 2009.

    I conceived, briefed and project managed the new 2009 D&AD Awards and Student Awards sites.

    I conceived, wire-framed and oversaw development of the universally acclaimed Awards judging iPhone app.

    I conceived, briefed and project managed the 2008 D&AD Online Annual (featuring 900+ pieces of the best creative work of the year – a huge technical challenge and a first for D&AD).

  • New Media consultant/contractor

    Refugee Council

    (Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    January 2006 — March 2006 (3 months)

    Created online content in various written and visual forms. Consulted on the Refugee Council's future digital strategy and budgets.

  • New Media consultant/contractor

    Barnardo's

    (Non-Profit; Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    August 2005 — December 2005 (5 months)

    Consultancy and development of Barnardo’s future web strategy (focusing on web-user interactivity and use of social media)

    Project-managed launch of www.6appeal.net, a campaign/brand to raise awareness of Barnardo’s within the student community.

    Implementation of a new Content Management System

  • Head of Online

    JJ Fox (St James) Ltd

    (Retail industry)

    February 2000 — June 2005 (5 years 5 months)

    As Online Manager for London’s oldest cigar merchants, I conceived, designed and built a website that was clean and usable while communicating JJ Fox’s 200-plus year history, taking them from the 18th to the 21st century.

    This entailed selling my vision to the often conservative board of directors, briefing and project-managing the integration of a bespoke e-commerce solution with internal systems. I also researched/wrote/ commissioned/edited content for the site to tell the story of JJ Fox's uniquely rich heritage.

    Once the site was live, I was responsible for its management, creating new, engaging content, creating sales and marketing campaigns, SEO and web stats analysis.
