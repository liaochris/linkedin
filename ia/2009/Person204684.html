Rick Rasansky’s Summary

As an entrepreneur, technologist, and innovator, I have over two decades of leadership and management experience in planning and implementing business strategies and policy in technology, software (development and network ops), venture capital, telecommunications, real estate, aviation and hospitality. Having served as founder, c-level officer and a principal partner of public and private entities engaged in a variety of activities spanning software, telecom, financial and consumer services, I have proven experience, expertise and success in building or re-focusing companies using strong and effective collaborative management teams.

Rick Rasansky’s Specialties:

leadership and motivation, collaboration, raising capital, innovation, branding and identity, sales and marketing, m&a, software development, network ops, mobile and wireless, internet, start-ups, strategy, logistics and generally disruptive thinking.

  • Co-Founder | Board member

    Network Acquisition Company LLC (NAC)

    (Telecommunications industry)

    June 2008 — Present (1 year 5 months)

    In May 2008, I identified a unique investment opportunity, led the
    formation of NAC and raised the funds necessary to acquire and gain control of the former EarthLink Philadelphia Wi-Fi network. In less than four weeks, under my guidance and leadership, NAC successfully concluded the high visibility, complex, tri-party transaction between the City of Philadelphia, EarthLink, and the not-for-profit, Wireless Philadelphia. The network offers fixed wireless PTP/PTMP broadband services to commercial customers in addition to wholesale backhaul services to carriers. Currently, NAC also provides a free “best efforts” community Wi-Fi offering that will explore location-based advertising, sponsorships and revenue sharing with specialized consumer application providers.

    Local non-profit organizations including Wireless Philadelphia and Knight Foundation will continue to use the network to deliver their important work of education, training and digital inclusion for area residents.

  • Founder | Thought Leader

    Famology LLC

    (Internet industry)

    January 2008 — Present (1 year 10 months)

    Famology was conceived as a personal desire to design a secure, closed-loop social networking web platform and tools focused specifically on the needs and requirements of the family - actually, an "anti-social" network for just your family. With a primary focus on storytelling, Famology will facilitate the secure communication, collection and preservation of your family’s history. This is an ongoing technical and social project that continues to evolve around the current state of social/cultural anthropology and web 2.0 initiatives. It is also a personally important and meaningful topic that continues to intrigue me daily.

  • CEO | Co-Founder

    MPOWER Mobile Inc. (formerly Sapphire Mobile Systems Inc)

    (Financial Services industry)

    July 2005 — January 2008 (2 years 7 months)

    Sapphire was acquired by MPower Ventures, just two years after the successful development of its patent pending mobile payment infrastructure designed to deliver mobile financial services to the underserved/un-banked demographic. Now known as MPower Mobile™, the SMS based system allows consumers to securely send cash in real time to anyone with a mobile phone, to check balances, transfer money between accounts and pay bills, all without requiring the consumer to upgrade their hardware or install new software.

  • Chief Marketing Officer

    TRM Corporation

    (Public Company; 51-200 employees; TRMM; Information Technology and Services industry)

    August 2004 — June 2005 (11 months)

    TRM is consumer services company that provides convenience ATM and photocopying services in high-traffic consumer environments. My role included leading the sales and marketing initiatives of the company, including the oversight, management, and direction required to integrate the sales and marketing operations of a significant acquisition that resulted in more than a 500% increase in deployed ATMs. I served on the Executive Committee of TRM’s senior management team and was also a member and invited speaker of the ATM industry Association (ATMIA). Further, I was appointed to the ATMIA’s Executive Committee, Government Relations Committee, and Debit Card Committee – in all of which he was actively involved. When I departed TRM in 2005 to start Sapphire, the Company’s ATM and copier customer base had grown to over 46,000 locations worldwide. TRM’s multi-national ATM network was one of the world’s largest, with over 22,000 terminals deployed.

  • Chairman | CEO | Founder

    eCal Corp

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Internet industry)

    June 1997 — May 2002 (5 years)

    eCal was the leading SaaS provider of carrier scalable, event-driven, collaborative software infrastructure that enabled communications service providers to deploy calendaring, scheduling, and notification services, accessible through any Internet-enabled device. eCal’s patented technology unified time and location dependent information generated by disparate groupware applications and other data sources. eCal’s customer base exceeded 100 ASP customers (2 million end-users) including market leaders such as AT&T, Reuters, Nokia Networks, British Telecom, Sprint Canada, Deutsche Bank, Starwood Hotels, ZDNet, and others. eCal grew from one employee in 1997 to over 275 in 2000. With a market cap in excess of $350 million, eCal filed for an IPO in April of 2000, which was subsequently withdrawn due to adverse market conditions that became apparent later that month. Can you say "bubble'? Although eCal was acquired in 2002, the culture and family of eCal still remain today.

  • CEO | Founder

    Millennium Media Group

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Entertainment industry)

    January 1993 — April 1996 (3 years 4 months)

    Millennium Media Group (MMG) wsa a Philadelphia-based international digital media publishing company that primarily focused on the family entertainment and early-education markets. In 1995, MMG acquired the Grammy award winning children’s production company, Rabbit Ears Productions, Inc. thereby expanding its content production and distribution to all forms of media, including, CD-ROM, Video, books, audio, television and radio. Such outlets ranged from the award winning weekly radio program Rabbit Ears Radio syndicated on National Public Radio (NPR), hosted by Mel Gibson and Meg Ryan - to an extensive licensing transaction with Microsoft Home for CD-ROMs. MMG was subsequently acquired in 1997.

  • President | Co-Founder

    Intronics Corporation

    (Consumer Electronics industry)

    January 1991 — March 1993 (2 years 3 months)

    Intronics Corporation was formed to develop innovative electronic products, the first of which was its wireless POWERMID infrared remote control extender. This product is still sold today at Radio Shack Stores and through numerous license and OEM arrangements. Subsequent to the launch of the Powermid product line, while working on a contract for Bogen Communications, I directed the design and development of the first commercially successful stand-alone, multiple mailbox, digital telephone answering device. Bogen marketed and sold the device under the name "Friday", in addition to entering into several OEM agreements, which included AT&T. Friday earned an Innovation Award at the 1994 Consumer Electronics Show.

  • Principal

    The CoreGroup

    (Venture Capital & Private Equity industry)
