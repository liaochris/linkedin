Tom Turley’s Experience

  • Missionary in Brazil

    Global Outreach Missions, Inc.

    (Non-Profit; 501-1000 employees; Religious Institutions industry)

    February 1986 — Present (23 years 9 months)

    Diana, and I have been missionaries 21 years in Brazil - 14 years in the city of Manaus, Amazonas (7 in camp ministry in the jungle) and 7 in church planting.

    One year traveling throughout Brazil, and now, the past six years we are in church ministries in Sao Jose dos Campos, Sao Paulo state. (where Embraer Regional Jets are manufactured)

    Primeira Igreja Batista em São José dos Campos is our base church (www.pibsjcampos.org.br). I pastor the International English venue.

    Help begin Purpose Driven Ministies in Brazil. Currently we travel with Walk Thru the Bible Ministries - BRAZIL, teaching family seminars - Effective Parenting in a Defective World; Biblical Portrait of Marriage; Understanding the Love of Your Life.

    We also speak to youth groups teaching abstinence as a means of producing Happy Families.

    In the very near future we will be directly involved in the P.E.A.C.E. plan in Brazil. <http://www.purposedriven.com/en-US/PEACE/PEACE_Plan.htm>

    (Non-Profit; Religious Institutions industry)

    August 1985 — January 1986 (6 months)

    We were on deputation for almost six months with Liberty Baptist Missions when it was decided that we should change mission agencies. The transition from Liberty to Global Outreach Mission, Inc., was smooth, and God honoring.

  • SMITE director

    Liberty University

    (Educational Institution; 1001-5000 employees; Higher Education industry)

    June 1983 — July 1984 (1 year 2 months)

    Became SMITE (Student Missionary Intern Training for Evangelism) director, under leadership of Dr. Robert Knutson.
    Lead team to Brazil in summer of 1984. Six week tour. During that tour I was invited to join the staff as a missionary with the Igreja Tabernáculo Batista in MAnaus, Brazil. Left Liberty in July 1984, worked PT until Sept. 1984.

  • Station Manager

    CHO with Air Virginia Airlines

    (Privately Held; 201-500 employees; Airlines/Aviation industry)

    May 1979 — May 1983 (4 years 1 month)

    Worked with Air Virginia my senior year at Liberty. Was part-time ramp rat (baggage handler) and moved up to Station Manager in Charlottesville, VA (CHO) the week of graduation in 1981. Talk about a nice graduation present.

    As Station manager I was responsible for all ground operations for Air Virginia at the Charlottesville - Albemarle Regional Airport, CHO, in Charlottesville, VA. This included personnel, public relations, general day to day flight, passenger, and freight operations.

  • Student (5 1/2 year plan)

    Liberty University

    (Educational Institution; 1001-5000 employees; Higher Education industry)

    January 1976 — May 1981 (5 years 5 months)

    Student in bachelor program at what was then Liberty Baptist College.
    Traveled as singer with LBC Chorale Road Team from June 1976-May 1977.

    From June 1977- July1979 sang with SMITE Singers. Traveled to twenty countries, sang in eleven different languages.

    From August 1979 - May 1981 worked with the aviation team on a Convair 240 twin prop - 38 pax (N8327C and N4993G) that was a part of the SMITE ministry.
