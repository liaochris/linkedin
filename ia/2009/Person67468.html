Dr Mani’s Summary

Dr.Mani Sivasubramanian is a heart surgeon using his infopreneur Internet business to fund heart surgery for under-privileged children in India.

Thousands of business owners have benefited from his teaching. Dr.Mani has received worldwide recognition as an Internet marketing specialist. He is one of '99 Purple Cows' named by business guru and bestselling author, Seth Godin, and is ranked among the world's top 50 online marketers in the Gurudaq.com index.

The heart surgeon for children is now also well known as an e-business coach and mentor.

Behind it all is a unifying thread that links his vast and growing social network together. A desire to help unfortunate children born with heart defects.

The same desire fuels his supporters. In many ways, with tireless energy and enthusiasm, they have embraced this purpose bigger than any individual or group, and grown it into a global movement.

  • Narayana Hrudayalaya Institute of Medical Sciences, Bangalore, India

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Health, Wellness and Fitness industry)

    April 2002 — January 2003 (10 months)

    The Narayana Hrudayalaya Institute of Medical Sciences is India's largest specialist heart center, performing over 5,200 heart operations every year, including over 1,200 pediatric cardiac surgeries in a year. It is a referral institution for patients all over India, as well as other countries in South East Asia. With a strong bias towards charitable support to patients from under-privileged families, the institution combines top quality with a compassionate social mission to deliver high quality heart health care to Indian children.

  • Royal Childrens Hospital, Melbourne, Australia

    (Educational Institution; 201-500 employees; Health, Wellness and Fitness industry)

    June 2001 — March 2002 (10 months)

    The Royal Childrens Hospital in Melbourne, Australia is a regional center of excellence in pediatric healthcare and especially in pediatric cardiothoracic surgery. In addition to performing over 400 heart surgeries every year, the institution offers advanced specialist training to surgeons from around the world who often return to establish units of excellence in their respective countries.

  • The Great Ormond Street Hospital for Children, London, U.K.

    (Government Agency; 201-500 employees; Health, Wellness and Fitness industry)

    June 2000 — May 2001 (1 year)
