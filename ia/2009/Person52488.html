Damon Danieli’s Summary

Founded Zero260, Incorporated with the Madrona Venture Group in October of 2008. Zero260 leverages the game changing mobile platforms and new application frameworks to create new user experiences.

We just launched PhotoFeedd, our first iPhone application.

You can see it at http://www.feedd.com.

Prior to Zero260, I'd been at Microsoft 14 years and during this time I worked on many interesting and technically complex products.

I was one of the core designers of the original Xbox Live. There I designed and wrote the functional specification for many of the marquee features (voice chat, voice commands, friends, notifications, etc); managed the feature teams of very hard technical challenges (networking and security, authentication and authorization, etc); and managed a team of PMs. As an interesting side note, the Xbox Live IANA Port is registered in my name (http://www.iana.org/assignments/port-numbers) and my Xbox Live Gamertag is 'd'. Invite me if you're online.

I left the Xbox Platform Team to be the Technical Lead of the Xbox Entertainment Network. This was an incubation effort to bring downloadable, episodic content to the mass market using the Xbox and Xbox Live as the venue and then later worked briefly on the launch of Xbox Live Arcade.

There were a few other fun projects such as SideWinder Game Voice (http://www.microsoft.com/presspass/features/2000/aug00/08-24gamevoice.mspx) where I was the architect and lead developer and Microsoft ActiMates (http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Actimates) where I was lead developer.

I work best when given a blank canvas and organizational support to create new concepts, build new teams and launch products. I have written and submitted over 20 patents. Some of the granted patents include: 7389153, 7370194, 7311608, 6935959, 6928329, 6905414, and 6807562 (the longer list of granted patents is below). There are more patents pending.
