  • Ambiance Director

    High Sierra Music Festival

    (Events Services industry)

    September 1998 — Present (11 years 5 months)

    From the very beginning, the amazing artwork that graces the High Sierra stages and Late Night venues has been created by hand and installed with love by fans and friends of the festival. Lisa Brown has managed the Ambiance crew and created artwork for the festival stages since 1998. This year Lisa has designed another blacklight "Flower Garden" but has moved it to the High Sierra Music Hall.
    In years past Lisa designed "Winter Wonderland" "Under the Sea" and "House of Mirrors" in the Music Hall, "Blacklight Flower Garden" in the Funk'n Jamhouse, and "Love Den" in the Tulsa E. Scott. Lisa has also painted many of the banners which have framed the Grandstand Stage over the years, including "12 Mandalas", the 15th anniversary HSMF banners, "Psychedelic Flowers", and "Tree of Life". Most of these decorations, as well as many others Lisa has created, reappear each year to help enhance the beauty of the Plumas County Fairgrounds during High Sierra.
