Alex Blagg’s Summary

After starting out as a stand-up comedian and freelance comedy writer in San Francisco, I took a position in New York as one of the founding editors of Vh1's "Best Week Ever" blog, and subsequently became the site's Managing Editor, winning two consecutive Webby Awards during my tenure.

I went on to help Gail Berman and Lloyd Braun launch Wonderwall.com in partnership with MSN, serving as the founding Editor-In-Chief of one of the fastest-growing and most acclaimed celebrity sites in recent history. Within a year of launching, Wonderwall was near the top of its category, receiving more than 11mm unique users and over 350mm page views per month.

I now serve as the Head of Celebrity Programming at Buzz Media, the Internet's leading social media publisher. Our network includes Celebuzz, The Superficial, What Would Tyler Durden Do, Stereogum, Videogum and the official sites and blogs of Britney Spears, Nicole Richie, Kim Kardashian and many other fabulous, wonderful, special celebrities.

Additionally, my freelance writing has appeared in SPIN magazine, The Huffington Post, Cracked magazine and Gawker.com, where I maintained a long-running weekly column called "Blue States Lose". I also occasionally appear on television as a guest panelist on Vh1, the Fox News Channel and several other media outlets.
