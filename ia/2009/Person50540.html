Crispin Garden-Webster’s Summary

Crispin Garden-Webster is a leader in the field of organisational development. He is a Registered Psychologist and a Fellow of the Human Resources Institute of New Zealand (HRINZ). Crispin is also a certified HR Professional (CP) through the International Personnel Management Association. Crispin can be contacted at crispin@gardenwebster.com

Crispin began his career in 1983 with the New Zealand Army after graduating from Massey University with Honours in Industrial and Organisational Psychology. He served as a Field Psychologist in a range of assignments in New Zealand and overseas including tours of duty in Singapore, Germany, United Kingdom, Australia, Fiji and Bahrain.
In 1994, Crispin entered the Telecommunications industry and joined Telecom New Zealand Ltd. Crispin served in senior HR Development roles and was accountable for the design and deployment of organisational development frameworks, talent management and online processes.
In 2001, Crispin became an independent consultant completing a range of projects and assignments across both the public and private sector.
In February 2003 Crispin was sought out by the Asian Development Bank in Manila, Philippines where he worked on a review of the Bank's HR Strategy, recruitment and selection processes and career management frameworks. Crispin also supported capacity building operations, and facilitated workshops in both at the Bank’s Headquarters in Manila and in Islamabad and Lahore, Pakistan. During this time Crispin also served as the HRINZ’s delegate to the Asia Pacific Federation of HR Management (APFHRM) working with other HR professional membership organizations across the region on capacity building and collaborative knowledge sharing ventures. He participated in workshops in Sri Lanka, Indonesia, Australia and the Philippines.

In August 2005 Crispin returned to New Zealand to continue the development of his consulting practice.
