Doug Mandell’s Summary

Doug Mandell is the founder and managing partner of the Mandell Law Group, PC (MLG) (www.mlawgroup.com). The Mandell Law Group is a boutique law firm specializing in complex technology transactions and employment law. Doug founded MLG in 2001. Clients of MLG include Fortune 100 companies such as Seagate Technology LLC; venture backed emerging growth companies such as Orb Networks and LinkedIn Corporation, as well as entrepreneurs and executives.

Doug specializes in negotiating complex international technology transactions; representing clients in employment law; and serving as an outsourced general counsel. Most recently, Doug served as the first General Counsel of Linkedin Corporation, a company he has represented as outside counsel since the Company was founded.

Doug also regularly advises companies and executives in all aspects of employment law. Doug is recognized as one of the leading attorneys in San Francisco specializing in representing senior executives (including CEOs, CFOs, founders, entrepreneurs and managing directors) in complex negotiations and disputes.

Doug was recently honored as a 2008 Northern California Super Lawyer (www.superlawyers.com). Super Lawyers identifies the top 5 percent of attorneys in Northern California, as chosen by their peers and through the independent research of Law & Politics. Doug also received this honor for the past two years and is one of the youngest Super Lawyers in Northern California. Doug was also recently recognized as an AV rated attorney by Martindale Hubble, the highest rating awarded to attorneys.

Doug Mandell’s Specialties:

Employment law, employment law counseling, internet law, privacy, technology transactions (including software license agreements and international supply agreements), international transactions, international employment law, providing General Counsel services to companies, sexual harassment, discrimination, executive compensation and counseling senior executives (chief executives, entrepreneurs and managing directors) in complex employment issues.
