Harlan Baum’s Summary

As a second-generation development professional (until 2006, my father was Senior Vice President Community Relations, Cedars-Sinai Medical Center; my brother was Major Gifts Director, United Jewish Fund in Los Angeles). I left the non-profit field in 1997 after having been recruited to serve as West Coast Corporate Advertising Manager for a national publishing company.

In 2006, I returned to the the not-for-profit field to help launch my father's development consulting firm in Los Angeles. The scope of our work includes, but is not limited to, feasibility studies, client representation, board development and corporate, foundation and individual donor prospecting. With the firm now well-established, I can focus on a return to my sales career or furthering the fund raising efforts of a local charitable institution.

As Director of Development for the Los Angeles County Natural History Museum's Petersen Automotive Museum, I provided leadership and direction for the quiet phase of a $25 million capital campaign. I simultaneously directed the Foundation's development staff, in a complex array of development programs dedicated to the History and Automotive Museums' long-term health.
