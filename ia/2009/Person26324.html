  • Research Master (Mphil) student

    Leiden University

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    September 2006 — January 2009 (2 years 5 months)

    My research, published as a monograph called Built Environments, Constructed Societies (Sidestone Press, Leiden, 2009), focuses on the beneficial connnections between archaeology and social geography, and explores how interdisciplinary ideas on social theory, a sense of place, social evolution and spatial organisation contribute to the becoming and inference of a socio-spatial identity. The theoretical framework developed to this end is called 'the social positioning of spatialities'.
    My research master programme focused on Native American archaeology, especially Mesoamerica.
