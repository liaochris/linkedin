Matt Pope’s Summary

I am currently finishing up 8 years active duty service in the United States Navy. I was a helicopter pilot by trade but have performed many roles other than flying during my tenure as an officer and NROTC instructor. To further broaden my skills and business acumen I have been pursuing an MBA from the University of Illinois over the past 2 years.
While I have enjoyed my time in the Navy, I am now excited to begin my transition into the private sector. I bring a wide variety of talents and experiences to the table, but most importantly I bring my action-oriented work ethic and leadship style. I am relentless in the pursuit of success and constantly demand the most from myself. I have had a successful military career and I have performed superbly in pursuit of my MBA. I believe I can leverage these unique skills to succeed in corporate America as I have demonstrated a record of being able to succeed in areas in which I previoulsy had little exposure.

Current Responsibilities:
Responsible for various project management efforts within the NROTC unit, including:
*Coordinated global summer training requirements for over 150 officer candidates by liasing with ship, shore, submarine, carrier, aviation and Marine units.
*Managed over 50 midshipmen to ensure students met academic and ROTC requirements.

Conduct personnel management and coaching:
*Counsel parents and prospective students, to discern academic and career objectives and advise them regarding alternative paths available to meet those objectives.

Design and deliver training and instruction:
*Delivered over 150 instructional hours as a University Assistant Professor, to 160 students -- consistently receiving the highest marks on student critiques.

Developed innovative and unique engagement techniques, to ensure effective transfer of knowledge, including novel lesson plans, tailored exercises, and student interactive projects.
