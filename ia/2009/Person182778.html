Paul Chantler’s Summary

PAUL CHANTLER is an award-winning radio programmer with 25 successful years in the UK radio industry

He has worked in a variety of formats including CHR, Hot AC, Adult Contemporary and Newstalk.

He was responsible for launching two UK regional youth format stations, Galaxy 101 and Vibe FM, as well as Dublin-based FM speech station NewsTalk.

He has experience in radio presentation, production and programme management and has been Group Programme Director at three of the UK's biggest radio groups.

Paul is now a radio programming consultant advising companies throughout the UK, Europe and Asia as well as helping with hands-on training of presenters and journalists.

He is a founder shareholder and non-executive director of KCFM, the radio station broadcasting to 480,000 people in Kingston-Upon-Hull which opened in August 2007.

He also works with selected individual radio personalities to mastermind career strategy and develop their skills through coaching.

Some of the talent Paul has coached and worked with in recent years include:-

- Tony Blackburn, Smooth Radio London
- Fiona Phillips, Smooth Radio London
- Graham Dene, Smooth Radio London
- Alan Brazil and Mike Parry, TalkSport
- James Whale, TalkSport
- George Hook, NewsTalk, Ireland
- Tony Horne, Metro Radio
- Big John, Hallam FM
- Salty, Century Radio Manchester
- Scott & Lisa, Century Radio Newcastle
- Jonathan Morrell, Century Radio Newcastle

Paul is co-author of the handbook Essential Radio Journalism published by A&C Black and used by colleges and universities in the UK, Ireland, USA, Europe and Australia.

Paul appeared in the TV series Return To Lakesiders on BBC2 in August 2008 and is a regular contributor to trade publications.
