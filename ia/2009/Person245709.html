Warren Stevens’s Summary

My life hasn't been on a career track, but I have excellent geek credentials.

My dad was a 'horn-rimmed glasses and pocket-protector' technician at UNIVAC before I was born. Instead of bed-time stories (and in between 'the beatings'), I got descriptions of nand/nor gates and magnetic bubble storage. I had a 16k computer and tried all the programs in the back of PC Magazine. I took a typing class to shorten the time for my code not to fit on the tape drive.

The US Army taught me how to string ideas together in mil-speak by writing non-judicial punishment and award narratives. I found the language better structured and more powerful than BASIC. Plus I got access to free weapons and tracked vehicles during many "camping expeditions" at a fun place we called Graf. (http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grafenwöhr)

College (3.84 cum), or 'a variety of collegiate experiences' was a progression of conflict between Brit Lit and Comp Sci, in which a 300 baud modem was the deciding factor. I caught bbs fever from citadel, installed Slackware on my PC, and had a new found fascination with the shell prompt.

After life in real estate sales, small-business ownership, polo-shirt corporations, and at-home software development, I have experience which is very broad ('accounting', 'advertising', 'marketing' (as in, real marketing not 'sales', 'technology', and 'sales'), where the technology pieces were in the past either the product, or a means to further some business purpose.

Lately (last 5 years), the technology has been the software I'm writing for someone's specs, so they can do their thing. The platform is not a religious issue for me, although there is a lot I like about LAMP and I have the most experience with it.

I once got mad when someone told me PHP was out of the mainstream. So maybe it is a religious issue.

Goals: a). Go to Heaven b). Understand user prior to a).
