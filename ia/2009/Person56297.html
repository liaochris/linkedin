Dave Lieber’s Summary

One of my favorite words is epiphany — a sudden insight that changes the way you view the world. Speakers and writers like me better have these quite often, or we aren’t worth a darn.

Mine came when I realized that 21st century journalism must be different from the kind I practiced during the early part of my career. Readers don’t care what’s on my mind. They want a newspaperman like me to work for them, to be on their side, to help them win fights they can’t win on their own.

My bosses at the Fort Worth Star-Telegram figured this out before I did when they created a new job for me as The Watchdog investigative columnist, which combines column writing narrative storytelling and investigative reporting. Newspaper ads announced the new position with this tagline: “Finally, you’ve got somebody in your corner.”

From the first day, this column was incredibly popular with readers. The mail is never-ending. The problems never stop. I strive to discover solutions.

As part of this, I have developed the concept of WATCHDOG NATION, an ever-growing group of Americans who defend themselves against false promises, frauds and other deceptions. Through extensive public speaking, I show audiences how to become a part of the consumer rights revolution in their own home. WATCHDOG NATION gives inside tips you won't find anywhere based on what I've learned helping others.
- Handle stubborn customer service representatives.
- Fight the electric company, the phone company, banks and credit card companies
- Stand up to scammers of all stripes
- Understand your rights under law

Watchdog Nation also helps businesses and other organizations give their employees a rationale for engaging in stronger customer service procedures. How? By sharing a series of funny and sad stories about customer relationships gone sour, I show organizations how much easier it is to do the right thing. And for laughs, "This is how you stay out of my book."
