Jason Grubb’s Summary

I see myself as a photojournalist, capturing the story of your wedding day through crisp images that communicate the spontaneity, authenticity and emotion of the moment. Wedding photography documents one of the greatest expressions of love in a person's life. But, each love story is as diverse as its participants. That's why I build a personal relationship with each couple. No two couples are the same and their photos should reflect their uniqueness. I want tell your story through memorable images that you will cherish for a lifetime.

When I'm not snapping pictures, you'll find me playing Wii with my son, on coffee date with my daughter or sampling a new wine with my fiancée. A Denver native, I enjoy the playground that the Rocky Mountains offer, frequently fly-fishing, camping and backpacking. I love telling a good story, Starbuck's Pike Roast coffee, watching movies, playing poker, tinkering on the piano, and Saturday mornings spent reading the newspaper on the back patio.

Over the span of my professional career, I've (among other things) been a counselor, a high-stakes poker player, a construction worker and an IT consultant. While my diverse resume always brought home the bacon, it wasn't until I transformed my amateur photography hobby into a career that I found the fulfillment, fun and the artistic outlet I'd been looking for.
