Ken Yanes’s Summary

Mr. Kenneth Yanes is the new Coordinator for the Study Abroad Program at The City College of New York, CUNY. Mr. Yanes has been working in the field of international education for over three years, a career that started when he returned from his semester at the Université François-Rabelais in Tours, France. Before serving as the full-time Program Adviser and Marketing Coordinator at the Rutgers Study Abroad Program, Mr. Yanes’ scholarship focused in Medieval Europe, Romance linguistics, and language pedagogy. He earned his BA in French Literature and Medieval Studies, earning highest honors for his thesis in Menippean satire during the French Religious Wars and his studies in Fin-de-siècle music, art, and theater. He also taught several freshman seminars in foreign language studies at Rutgers. Thereafter, he held a research fellowship in Medieval art history and theory at Rutgers University – New Brunswick. Mr. Yanes is fluent in English, French, Spanish and Portuguese, and is currently pursuing independent studies in Scandinavian languages. His interests within international education include service learning programs, new program development, fundraising, globalization within CUNY, and, furthermost, the convivial exchange he has with his eager advisees. Mr. Yanes is enthusiastic and ready to bring global education to the forefront here at the culturally-diverse and dynamic campus of City College.
