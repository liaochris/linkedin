Sarah Barnes’s Summary

Sarah R. Barnes has been designing websites since 1996. She holds a "day job" working for West Virginia University, but also does freelance web design consulting, and teaches part-time for the WVU School of Journalism.

Although on hiatus from her studies, she is slowly working on a Masters in Journalism.

Sarah has an extensive web design portfolio including sites designed for small businesses (Allegheny Treenware, LLC, Things Gone By Antiques), colleges and universities (Fairmont State C&TC, WVU Center on Aging, WVU Office of Information Technology), conferences (WVNET 2000-2002 conferences, WV Higher Education Technology Conferences, Statewide Technology Conferences), state organizations (WV Statewide Addressing and Mapping Board), non-profits (Arthurdale Heritage, Arts Council of Preston County), programs and special projects (WVSeniors.org, WV Celebration 2000, West Virginia’s Veterans History Project).

She is a member and officer of the Statewide Internet Group (SWIG) – a committee of West Virginia state web developers. In 2007, she spearheaded an effort by the group to recognize excellence in web design on WV state websites and started the Swiggy Awards for Web Excellence.

Sarah also has extensive experience with conference planning. She has served on the conference planning committees for the WV Statewide Technology Conference, the WV Higher Education Technology Conference, WVNET Conferences, and many others.

Sarah blogs on her own at http://sbahns.com, on The Reenactor Post http://thereenactorpost.com (her dad's blog), and the WVU Office of Information Technology Blog: http://blog.oit.wvu.edu.

In April 2004, Sarah was inducted into Kappa Tau Alpha a college honor society that recognizes academic excellence and promotes scholarship in journalism and mass communication.

Sarah is also a member of the Arthurdale Heritage, Inc. Board of Directors.
