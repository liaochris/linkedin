Robert Clay’s Summary

Started business at 19 with no capital. In 3 years achieved 40% penetration of initial market potential compared to 17% nationally for all other brands combined. Achieved much press coverage in Sunday Times, Sunday Telegraph, Guardian, Observer, many regional newspapers & industry publications.

At 23 started franchising. Ran successful marketing campaigns which took business to No.3 in UK within 3 years, with other franchisees worldwide.

Invented the glass sunroof as we know it today, creating from scratch a market in the UK for more than 250,000 units a year within 3 years, when it became third largest sunroof manufacturer in Europe.

Eventually sold both businesses to one of the largest companies in Europe and was instrumental in taking that business to No.1 in its field in the world within four years.

Started consulting with other entrepreneurs and business owners, clarifying and de-mystifying marketing and transforming their thinking and business results.

Set up DSP in 2000 as an experiment and have since helped hundreds of businesses to transform their marketing strategies and business fortunes using low-risk/high-return marketing strategies.

Successes include helping a number of business start-ups to achieve market leadership in their field, or in the UK or in Europe or in the world. One was national Ernst & Young “Entrepreneur of the Year” award winner 2002, who a year later appeared in the Sunday Times Rich List and sold out in Dec 07 for £75M. Another was the “Entrepreneur of the Year” runner up the same year. Another award winning client went from start-up to £100M revenues in 7 years.

Over 10 years have developed a unique and priceless 250,000 page + knowledgebase detailing the best marketing practices worldwide in every conceivable industry.

Writes books and regular columns in magazines and newspapers. Highly regarded keynote speaker, Meeting or exceeding 99.7% of audience expectations. runs regular workshops for entrepreneurs.

Robert Clay’s Specialties:

Specialist in designing low-risk/high-return marketing strategies for entrepreneurial businesses. Provider of knowledge and facilitation in the areas of customer acquisition, better conversion of leads to clients, customer retention, increasing transaction value and increasing transaction frequency. Runs seminars and workshops. Masterminds, creates, implements and sustains all marketing activities for a limited number of clients, without charging a fee, but being paid out of increased profits.

  • Founder and MD

Developed new application methods which revolutionised the industry and were subsequently adopted by virtually every car, truck, bus and aircraft manufacturer in the world, and are still in use today. Used an array of self-taught marketing strategies that rapidly turned the industry on its head and took my company to prominence in its field.

  • Founder and MD

    Skyport Sunroofs UK Ltd (Skyport and Panarama brands)

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Automotive industry)

    1976 — 1985 (9 years)

    At age 22 invented the glass sunroof as we know it today. Manufactured Skyport and Panarama Sunroofs in the UK and California. The design of these units leak-proof units transformed sunroof fitting from a 2-day skilled operation to a 45 minute semi-skilled operation. Within 3 years of launch, created a market from scratch for 250,000 sunroofs a year in the UK alone, and also sold them worldwide. Supplied the sunroofs to many car manufacturers for production line installation, or fitted at point of import, or sold as branded accessories for dealers to fit. Introduced innovations on factory fitted models like radio antennas screen printed onto the glass panel eliminating vandalism etc.

    (Privately Held; 10,001 or more employees; Automotive industry)

    1981 — 1985 (4 years)

    Tuff Kote Dinol, then part of the Multi-Billion Dollar Swedish Axel Johnson Group (second largest company in Scandinavia, owners at the time of Astra Pharmaceutical, Wallenius Shipping Lines, Nynas Oil Company and many others), bought my businesses and I was given the task of convincing car, truck, bus and aircraft manufacturers to adopt our corrosion prevention techniques during production and/or at point of import. Until then they had largely resisted the idea but I developed a very successful strategy and approach to this and in four years secured 100% of contracts we went after. Not one competitor got a single contract in that time. Developed innovative products and application techniques that are still in use today. Literally "wrote the book" on the subject. The company became the largest in its field in the world, absorbing many of its competitors in the process. Brands included: Tuff-Kote Dinol, Dinol, Dinitrol, Tuff-Kote, Bodyshield, Protectol, Endrust and Dual-Gard.
