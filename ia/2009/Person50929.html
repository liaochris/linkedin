Crystal Lauderdale’s Summary

My experience in the news industry has primarily been in highly-converged newsrooms working closely with print, television and online journalists.

While my degree is in print writing, I started my career as a still photographer for The Tampa Tribune. I quickly picked up multimedia and video production skills, becoming one of the first fulltime cross-platform journalists at The News Center (comprised of The Tampa Tribune, WFLA-TV and TBO.com).

Currently, as the Video Product Manager for the New York Times Regional Media Group, I am working to build successful web video operations in 16 newsrooms. As part of that process I am training reporters, photographers and managers in video production, researching developments in video technology and exploring new forms of advertising and promotion.

In addition to my on-going training program for NYTRMG, I have developed and taught multimedia classes for The Tampa Tribune, the Florida Society of News Editors, Freelance Success and the Poynter Institute. I have also worked as a freelance multimedia journalist for MSNBC.com.

I am extremely passionate about my work and excited about the future of journalism! It is my goal to always be learning and teaching, and through collaboration with others devoted to good journalism regardless of medium, I hope to help shape and define the way we tell stories in the 21st Century.
