Consuelo Valverde’s Summary

I am a S&T business expansion professional with more than 20 years of business experience, not counting that I started my first business when I was 6 years old. I am thankful to have grown up in a family environment that fostered my entrepreneurial spirit.

I have focused in science entrepreneurship, business development and innovation best practices.

I am also experienced in creating and growing businesses in recession economic environments. My father founded over 5 very successful businesses in Mexico, in the bakery, cattle, distribution, restaurant and agriculture industries. I founded 3 businesses and advised dozens during recessions and economic crisis. I also experienced being an entrepreneur in the public sector in one of the most dynamic periods of change in the history of modern Mexico. It was very challenging but with determination and drive, got to achieve goals that impact generations.

I currently work with smart S&T business owners and their teams, innovation centers and executives of large corporations and help them with their expansion strategy. I do this in a coaching format with a set of tools and methodologies that provide the best practices in business expansion and innovation.

Have worked with some of the top 50 fastest growing companies in the Bay Area and helped clients double the targeted revenue goal.

Was granted an O-1 Visa for extraordinary abilities in business and innovation.

Consuelo Valverde’s Specialties:

Science and Technology entrepreneurship, Business expansion and innovation best practices.

    (Management Consulting industry)

    August 2008 — December 2009 (1 year 5 months)

    Responsible for implementing the business strategy and establishing operations of Swiss Avenue Partners in Silicon Valley/Bay Area. New product development, develop channel relationships, coaching in the areas of Strategy, Financial Modeling and Revenue Generation, manage new client engagements.
    O-1 Visa: applies to aliens who have extraordinary ability in the sciences, arts, education, business, or athletics which has been demonstrated by sustained national or international acclaim and who are coming to the U.S. to continue work in the area of extraordinary ability. In my case it was granted for outstanding achievements in business and innovation.

  • Vice President of Product Development and Business Research

    Farmacia Remedios Inc.

    (Pharmaceuticals industry)

    January 2007 — July 2008 (1 year 7 months)

    Since Farmacia Remedios began operations in 2004, the store chain has been growing steadily but responsibly. The Company has opened pharmacies that target the Hispanic community in California. The first pharmacy opened in June, 2004, in Oakland, California. In 2005 we opened an additional pharmacy in San Francisco’s Mission District and in 2006 the expansion continued opening several locations in San Jose. We then established in Southern California.The concept was initially tailored to meet the needs of Spanish-speaking customers.
    Assisted the CEO in planning and implementing the business strategy. Headed the development and commercialization of the company's private label, the IT systems, store planning and product layout statistical analysis, launch Farmacia Remedios in the Southern California market.

  • General Coordinator of Modernization, Science & Technology

    Gobierno del Estado de Morelos

    (Pharmaceuticals industry)

    2000 — 2006 (6 years )

    With the strong conviction that Morelos had the potential to become Mexico’s Knowledge Capital – through the combined efforts of effective leadership, public policy, society’s participation, and commitment from all sectors, as the Head of the Office of Innovation, Science & Technology, promoted the strengthening of a State System for Science and Technology; the establishment of the first State fund dedicated to science and technology; the creation of the Morelos Museum of Sciences; the Center for Innovation and Technology Transfer; the creation of technology based enterprises and technology transfer between the academic and business sector; the National Science & Technology Week 2005 -with more than 50,000 participants; international science, technology and innovation exchanges, including STiEX – the first science and technology exchange for enterprise creation between the United Kingdom and Mexico; the first state magazine encouraging the spread of science “Hypatia”; the television program “With Science... Towards Knowledge”, and a considerable number of educational programs for radio and television. I also promoted innovation and process improvement within the State Government.

  • Undersecretary of Information and Systems

    Government of the City of Cuernavaca

    (Government Administration industry)

    June 1997 — September 2000 (3 years 4 months)

    It was a very exciting period in the history of modern Mexico. I wanted to be part of the change that Mexico needed. Therefore I got involved in politics and was part of the campaign who candidate for Mayor of Cuernavaca won. He was the first Mayor to be elected from a party different from the PRI who had been in power for over 70 years.

    We revolutionized internal procedures and service strategies, starting with a systems implementation that modernized the municipal administration. From the years 1998 to 1999, I was elected Vice President of the Information Systems Committee for the State, Public, and Municipal Administration (CIAPEM). In this role, I organized the establishment of the National Information Systems Forum for the State, Municipal and Public Administration. In the year 2000, I became its President.
