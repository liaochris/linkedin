Linda Ruck’s Summary

I'm an Australian residing in Singapore. In 1998, I resigned from the Australian Government to move to Vietnam. For four years, I worked in children's charities in Ho Chi Minh City earning and raising funds as an international writer on human interest and conservation. From Vietnam, I moved to Singapore with Wilbur my Vietnamese cat who I rescued on his way to the cooking pot.

I continued with my writing and was Editor in Chief of an entrepreneur magazine and interviewed some of the world's most high profile people. From interviewing Rudi Giuliani, asking questions to Minister Mentor Lee Kuan Yew and entrepreneur Ho Kwong Ping, bouncing ideas with Robert Kiyosaki, sharing personal thoughts with Anita Roddick and having a tête-à-tête over a coffee with Al Ries and Tom Peters.

In nine years in Asia I have built up an extensive network of business contacts.

The language of commerce goes beyond just saying, “G'day mate!” In Chinese there is a word for business connections. It is called, "Guanxi" which means, “In business, it is not about what you know. It is about who you know." In Indonesian and Malaysian it is, "Abang, ada baik?" which means, "Brother, how are you, and how can I serve you?" In Thailand, it is "Sawadee Kap!" In the Philippines it is, "Mabuhay. Selamat Po!" And Singapore, "Ni hou! How are you?"

If your looking for business contacts in the Asia Pacific or need a creative PR or media campaign contact me www.lindaruck.com

In the meantime stay tuned for my first book “Travels with Wilbur”

About Me: I am passionate about philanthropy and in Dec 08 went to the Ukraine as part of a global fundraising team of business women with the WIN Foundation. We raised more than USD $100,000 for abandoned children. To me its about making a positive difference to a child, animal or our beautiful planet.

Collector of Australian and Russian fine artwork.

At the time of writing this both Linda and Wilbur were hoping to be adoped by Bill Gates.

Linda Ruck’s Interests:

LRC is committed to contributing to society focussing on organisations that support underprivileged children and endangered species. A committed conservationist, Linda is an Executive Committee Member of Hemisphere Foundation and provides complimentary media support for select organisations. Hemisphere Foundation was established to create environmental awareness for children in Singapore. To date more than 100,000 children have benefited from the initiative.

Linda Ruck’s Groups:
