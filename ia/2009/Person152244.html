  • Howard R. Marsh Professor of Journalism

    University of Michigan

    (Educational Institution; 5001-10,000 employees; Higher Education industry)

    September 2005 — May 2007 (1 year 9 months)

    The Marsh professorship is an endowed chair for working journalists to teach at Michigan for a one-year stint. It was so rewarding and it fit me so well that the position was extended for a second year. I taught two senior seminars, one on covering war, conflict and terrorism, the other on New Media and the future of the news. One semester I also taught an Independent Reading class on narrative nonfiction and literary journalism. The two years on campus amounted to a wondrous, profound, exhilarating experience. A special thanks to the department chair, Susan Douglas, and my academic colleagues.

  • Foreign Correspondent, Bureau Chief

    Knight Ridder

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; Newspapers industry)

    April 1998 — May 2005 (7 years 2 months)

    Southeast Asia bureau chief, then Moscow bureau chief, with extended combat-zone deployments in South Asia and the Middle East. Most recently, from 2005-2007, Howard R. Marsh Professor of Journalism at the University of Michigan, Ann Arbor.

  • Deputy sports editor, national/feature writer

    Dallas Morning News

    (Public Company; 501-1000 employees; BLC; Newspapers industry)

    September 1985 — April 1998 (12 years 8 months)

    Hired as deputy sports editor to Dave Smith, then moved to sports feature writing and nearly full-time Olympic coverage. Texas Sportswriter of the Year (Harold Green Award), plus various APSE, APME, GLAAD and Dallas Press Club awards. Three years as national/feature writer in the paper's award-winning Today section. Three individual Pulitzer nominations, and involvement on the paper's Pulitzer-winning team that produced the series "Violence Against Women."

  • Backfield editor

    Boston Globe

    (Public Company; 501-1000 employees; Newspapers industry)

    June 1982 — April 1985 (2 years 11 months)

    Created the backfield concept of copy-desk management with editor Vince Doria, a template now used nationwide. Co-authored, from scratch, the first Boston Globe Style Book.

  • News assistant

    New York Times

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; NYT; Newspapers industry)

    December 1973 — March 1977 (3 years 4 months)

    My first job out of college! Did all the great and glorious grunt work of a clerk,from cleaning paste pots, proofing galleys and editing the CGO to making the beer-and-whiskey runs across 43rd Street on Saturday night for the rim editors. Subbed as a desk editor, got some writing assignments, worked in hot-type makeup and wrote the occasional feature. Took history and fine arts classes at NYU and Columbia. Went to the opera, the Garden, the MOMA, a zillion sports events, Sly Stone's wedding and every half-price show on Broadway. Won a NYT publisher's award for covering --- somewhat accidentally --- the Christmastime airport bombing at LaGuardia. A new miracle every day.
