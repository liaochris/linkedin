Ludwig van Beethoven’s Summary

Travelling around from Germany and Austria in my position of independent musc professional I gained lots of experiences in International Affairs. I have mostly been attracted to "unattainable" women (married or aristocratic), and I never married. My only love affair with an identified woman began in 1805 with the Austrian Josephine von Brunswick, young widow of the Graf von Deym. For some reason, people believe the relationship ended by 1807 due both to my own indecisiveness and the disapproval of Josephine's aristocratic family. In 1812, I wrote a long love letter to a woman I identified only as "Immortal Beloved". Several candidates have been suggested - but guess who it was!

Ludwig van Beethoven’s Specialties:

My music and my reputation inspired — and in many cases intimidated — ensuing generations of composers, musicians, and audiences. I am one of the greatest masters of musical construction, sometimes sketching the architecture of a movement before I decide upon the subject matter. Remarkable is my use of "source-motives," which recurred in many different compositions and lent some unity to my life's work. I made innovations in almost every form of music I touched.

    I am one of the greatest composers in the history of music, and was a crucial figure in the transitional period between the Classical and Romantic eras in Western classical music. While primarily known today as a composer, I was also a celebrated pianist. Despite gradual hearing loss beginning in my twenties, I continued to produce notable masterpieces throughout my life, even when I was totally deaf.

    I was also one of the first composers to work freelance — arranging subscription concerts, selling my compositions to publishers, and gaining financial support from a number of wealthy patrons — rather than seek out permanent employment by the church or by an aristocratic court. I would therefore consider myself one of the first neo-liberals.
