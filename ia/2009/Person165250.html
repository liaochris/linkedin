Milan Maksimovic’s Summary

Dr. Milan Maksimovic received the Dipl.-Ing. degree in electrical engineering from the University of Belgrade, Serbia (2003) and the Ph.D. degree from the University of Twente, The Netherlands (2008).

Currently he is with the MECAL BV, Enschede, The Netherlands working as Project Engineer for Optronics and Imaging. Role includes design and engineering and development tasks related to the implementation of the optical systems and machine vision technologies for effective, practical and innovative measurement system solutions for industrial applications.

Previously he was researcher with the Institute of Chemistry, Technology and Metallurgy, Department of Microelectronic Technologies and Single Crystals, University of Belgrade from 2003 till 2006 and with the MESA+ Institute for Nanotechnology, Applied Analysis and Mathematical Physics Group, University of Twente from 2006.

His research interests include theoretical, computational and application aspects of the nanophotonic systems and structures, negative refractive index metamaterials and wave propagation in aperiodic and fractal optical systems. He has authored and co-authored over 50 publications in journals and conference proceedings. He is a member of SPIE and IEEE.

Milan Maksimovic’s Education

  • Universiteit Twente

    Ph.D., Applied mathematics (Photonics), 2006 — 2008

    PhD thesis title: “Optical resonances in multilayer structures”

    In the thesis an approach toward semi-analytical mathematical models for the field representation and the perturbation theory techniques in multilayer structures using quasi-normal modes and a variational principle is outlined. Further, the wave propagation in non-periodic multilayer structures containing negative index metamaterials has been investigated. Also, concept of the thermal radiation antennas made with NIM-based multilayer systems which show enhanced performances in both spectral selectivity and directionality have been proposed and theoretically investigated.
