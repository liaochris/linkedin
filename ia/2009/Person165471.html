Milo van der Linden’s Summary

Ever since I was a little boy, I have been fascinated by maps. My addiction started reading Michelin maps in the car for my dad at the age of 10. Years later, after serving in the Dutch Navy, I decided I wanted to work with maps. I signed up for the study of Geodesy/Geo-Information technology at the Hogeschool van Utrecht and in the year 1997 I graduated with excellent grades.

My career has always involved GIS. Being autodidact I learned myself to program, first in Delphi and Visual Basic, later in PHP, Javascript, Python, Perl and C++. My programming has always been practical; It was required to enhance GIS and make it adapt to business processes.

Although I am not a programmer, I am particularly good at reading and refactoring code and in a team of programmers, my skills always proof to be extremely useful in keeping a team on target and maintaining clear sight at the goals of a project. With my hands-on get things done mentality, great communication skills and am a good project lead.

After 9 years of working in commercial GIS software, I decided to step into the world of Open Source GIS. The mentality, speed of creating solutions and the fact that I have strong believe that collaborating world wide in software projects thrives innovation, make me feel really at home with open source and the people working with and creating it.

What I bring to open source is knowledge of businesses, their processes and their needs, translation and testing skills and project management experience.

And of course, I don't mind getting my hands dirty coding every now and then.

Milo van der Linden’s Specialties:

Implementing business solutions based upon WMS, WFS, and geoJSON standards using OpenLayers for web based solutions and Mapserver, PostGIS and GeoServer for server solutions.
