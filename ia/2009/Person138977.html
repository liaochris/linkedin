Laurie Smithwick’s Summary

Laurie is obsessed with design.

That could be the beginning and end of this bio, but we'll tell you a little more. She's been working as a designer for over 15 years, but her parents and brother will tell you that she's been designing things (for better or worse) since she was a little kid. One thing led to another and voila -- designer.

She launched her career at the jazz label GRP/Impulse where she got to hang out with (and design CDs for) cool cats like Diana Krall, Dr. John, George Benson, Groove Collective, Dave Grusin, Chick Corea and others. She also got to work on re-release packaging for pretty much all the jazz greats you can think of.

Later, as senior designer at Red Herring Design in NYC she expanded her repertoire to include publishing, interactive design and corporate identity, working with MTV, Simon & Schuster, Reader's Digest, Island Records, and many more.

North Carolina came calling in 1998, and Laurie founded LEAP design and hasn't looked back. Well, actually she has looked back some. LEAP is great, but largely because she had such fantastic mentors in NY. Plus, NY was awesome.

In 2007, she and two friends co-founded Kirtsy.com, a user-generated content aggregator for online news (also known, lovingly, as "Digg for Chicks"). Today, Kirtsy is a thriving community, profitable and a whole lot of fun.

Laurie's work has received many design awards, including a 1998 Grammy nomination for package design. Her work has been recognized by the Art Director's Club, AIGA, Graphis, Society of Publication Designers, Print Magazine and the Association for Independent Music. She graduated from Duke with a BA in English/Creative Writing and later earned an AAS Degree from Parsons School of Design.
