Paul W. Reidl’s Summary

Paul Reidl is a California lawyer with nearly thirty years of experience in litigation and other contested matters. I spent the first decade of my career with the Washington, D.C. firm of Crowell & Moring. I spent the next eighteen years as Associate General Counsel of a multi-billion dollar global company, E. & J. Gallo Winery. Although my practice concentrated on intellectual property and international law, over the years I represented Gallo in a variety of disciplines, including regulatory, contractual, and public policy matters. I was responsible for Gallo’s global trademark program and I represented Gallo in litigation throughout the globe.

My current practice involves both trademark and wine law for clients in Canada, Italy and the United States. In the trademark area, I counsel clients on the full spectrum of global trademark-related issues, from strategic branding through dispute resolution. My wine law practice counsels clients on legal and business matters in the United States and abroad. Recent litigation includes:

E. & J. Gallo Winery v. Mira Enterprises, Inc.
E. & J. Gallo Winery v. The Spanish Table, Inc.
FWI, Inc. v. Perfecta Wine Company
CytoSport, Inc. v. PVL, Ltd.

I have also represented clients in numerous proceedings before the Trademark trial and Appeal Board and have been retained as an expert witness on wine branding and marketing.

I am a frequent speaker and writer on trademark-related issues. I am a member of the Practicing Law Institute faculty, the International Trademark Association (INTA), and the Association Internationale des Juristes du Droit de la Vigne et du Vin (“AIDV”). I was the President of INTA in 2006.

Paul W. Reidl’s Specialties:

Trademark law, trademark litigation, wine law, contracts, general intellectual property counselling.
