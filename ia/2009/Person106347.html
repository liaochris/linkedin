Jay Simon, Ph.D.’s Summary

I am currently seeking a technical position where I can bring to bear my problem solving skills and technical expertise. My passion and my objective is to create innovative solutions to challenging problems arising on the path from project inception to market.

I have experience in a broad spectrum of electrical engineering disciplines: I am essentially a problem solver. The problem may entail a mathematical analysis or it may involve determining the cause of a system anamoly - the system could be an elaborate computer code, a sensor system, or a test procedure for a system of systems. I am most stimulated by problems that stretch my experience and know-how. In this sense, the physical nature of the problem does not matter - all challenging problems are inherently interesting.

I initiated and managed my own government funded research project at MIT Lincoln Laboratory and have supervised small groups of engineers and technicians during my tenure as the Antenna Laboratory manager at MITRE Corp.

Jay Simon, Ph.D.’s Experience

  • Lead Sensor Engineer

    MITRE Corp

    (Non-Profit; 5001-10,000 employees; Information Technology and Services industry)

    2004 — 2008 (4 years)

    - Lead radar analyst supporting government team’s effort to develop and validate srequirements and design documents for the Global Hawk MP-RTIP airborne surveillance radar program. I have developed a number of Matlab- and Fortran-based simulation packages to assess radar performance.

    - System analyst supporting the MITRE sensor simulation team supporting the SBI-Net border surveillance system program. Developed Matlab-based pulsed Doppler ground surveillance radar simulation package supporting real-time XML data interface with MITRE’s netted sensor simulation testbed and real-time human motion Doppler signature analysis.

    - GPS Antenna Development and Research lead – responsible for design, development and delivery of airworthy 4-element 7-channel polarimetric dual-channel (L1/L2) stacked patch GPS array antenna with anti-jam capability for GPS Joint Program Office.

  • Sr. Engineer II

    Raytheon

    (Defense & Space industry)

    2000 — 2004 (4 years)

    · Developed analysis software in support of test/integration of Raptor EO/IR airborne reconnaissance system including verification and validation of pointing accuracy, resolution, and region-of-interest capture achieved with the EO and IR camera subsystems. Supported development of test fixtures and baseline test procedures.

    · The Raytheon team for the Navy’s DD(X) Multi-Mission Surface Combatant destroyer program was tasked to evaluate the electromagnetic interference between deckhouse radar antenna apertures. Developed computer codes incorporating several advanced theoretical techniques to solve this problem. The agreement between my theoretical predictions and measurements later taken at the Navy’s China Lake test facility were excellent.

    · Performed various RCS analyses employing various tools including GO/PO, HFSS finite-element package, moment method, and OSU’s NEC-BSC and PMM codes.

  • Member of Technical Staff

    MIT Lincoln Laboratory

    (Educational Institution; 1001-5000 employees; Defense & Space industry)

    1989 — 2000 (11 years)

    · Invented the Super-resolution Post-Adaptive Coverage Enhancement (S-PACE) satellite-based adaptive antenna-nulling algorithm. My initiative resulted in a one-year $500K Air Force research contract to develop/investigate this concept further as a candidate nulling algorithm for the USAF Milstar II satellite fleet.

    · Analyzed the effects of traveling-wave tube (TWT) dispersion and memory on achievable bit-error rate (BER) for advanced M-ary modulation schemes. Developed Volterra and nonlinear ARMA models. Investigation led to the development of mitigation techniques employing adaptive compensation.

    · Lead investigator and analyst participating in numerous diverse technical initiatives during my tenure at Lincoln.
