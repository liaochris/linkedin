Kai D. Wright’s Summary

As a communications architect, Kai helps build bridges between consumers and brands. He is passionate about the digital space and joined Organic to play an active role in creating exceptional consumer experiences for Bank of America. His marketing experience encompasses a diverse range of audiences including business decision-makers, entrepreneurs, boomers and Gen Y. At the end of the day, he prides himself on “getting people.”

Prior to joining Organic, Kai was an analyst at OgilvyOne Worldwide. At Ogilvy, he was responsible for consumer research and insights. He was a member of Ogilvy’s Employee Advisory Council working with senior executives to introduce more diversity into the advertising industry. For corporate service, he worked with middle school kids for 10 months to develop an advertising campaign for the NY Mets.

Before Ogilvy, he spent three years in not-for-profit at the Collegiate Scholars Program. In his role as Assistant Program Manager & Program Advisor, he was responsible for managing an entrepreneurship program sponsored by the Goldman Sachs Foundation and the University of Chicago Graduate School of Business.

Kai first gained exposure to marketing and advertising through business plan competitions. While in college, he won a national competition through the US Department of Commerce and was a semi-finalist in another.

As a former military brat, Kai considers himself a global citizen. He has lived in four states as well as Germany and France. To date, he has traveled to seven countries; Italy is his favorite. In his spare time, he volunteers for the United Nations Association, an international organization that teaches diplomacy to teens through simulations at the United Nations in New York. He has visited the European Union & European Commission in Brussels, also.

He holds a bachelor's degree in Economics from the University of Chicago and is currently finishing a master's degree in Strategic Communications from Columbia University.
