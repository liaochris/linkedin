Nando DiFilippo’s Summary

Fernando Di Filippo, Jr. was born and raised in Palm Beach, Florida (U.S.A.). He is both an American and Italian citizen. He graduated in 1970 from Georgetown University in Washington D.C. where he received his Bachelor of Arts degree.

Nando received his law degree in 1973 from the Georgetown University Law Center. After graduation he worked for the prestigious Wall Street law firm Sulivan & Cromell. In 1977 he joined Baker & McKenzie and became the youngest ever Head of a Department in the firm’s history.

In 1985, Nando became one of the founders of Home Shopping Network, Inc. and joined his good friend and client, Roy M. Speer, the founder of HSN, and one of America’s most important entrepreneurs. Under his leadership, HSN, which was the first national TV channel in America dedicated to television shopping, which went from $10 million to $ 1 billion in sales in only five years. Nando was responsible for the very successful quotation of HSN’s shares on the American Stock Exchange where the value of HSN’S shares increased over 1000% on the first day of trading.

From 1992 until 2000, Nando lived in Miami, Florida and managed his own investments and experimented with direct response television projects in several countries.

In 2001 Nando moved to Italy where he founded The Direct Marketing Company specializing in the sale of consumer goods and services to private clients. Since its commercial launch, The DMC has increased its annual sales an average of 55% each year. The DMC projects very aggressive growth in 2008-2010 including international expansion.

The DMC is considered Italy’s top Direct Marketing Companies with a client database of over 1.1 million clients. In 2007 DMC Holding generated €55 million in net sales with an EBITDA of 20%. In 2008 DMC Holding expects to increase its top line to €120 million through primarily internal growth.
