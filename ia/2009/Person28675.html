  • Tier Technologies, Inc.

    (Public Company; Computer Software industry)

    June 2004 — January 2008 (3 years 8 months)

    Tier Technologies purchased EPOS Corporation in June, 2004. I continued with my IVR responsibilities using both the FirstLine Encore and I3 products. Most notably, I wrote a Weekly Claim system for the Vermont Department of Labor.

    Accounting difficulties and an economic downturn led Tier Technologies to a decision to phase out the product lines on which I had worked and to terminate most of its operations in Duluth, Georgia and Auburn, Alabama.

  • EPOS Corporation

    (Computer Software industry)

    December 1999 — June 2004 (4 years 7 months)

    The merger of CCS and Thermo Electron did not work out very well. After an economic downturn, Thermo Electron sold CCS to EPOS Corporation of Auburn, Alabama. EPOS had been a CCS VAR for some years. Their big market was selling the CCS system to colleges.

    EPOS had added HTML and credit-card functionality to the CCS product. I worked on a web server for the Jefferson County Human Resources system. I worked on credit-card processing for Santee Cooper.

    I learned the SMS protocol for the Motorola cell-phone text message receiving system.

    I used EPOS' MQiLink product to connect to IBM MQSeries servers for the McLane Company.

    The Virginia Employment Commission decided to establish call centers in Grundy, Virginia and in South Boston, Virginia, using the I3 technology. I wrote their Initial Claim system and upgraded their existing Weekly Claim system for the I3 tools.

  • CCS TREXCOM, Inc.

    (Privately Held; Telecommunications industry)

    1996 — December 1999 (3 years)

    CCS was eventually sold to Thermo Electron Corporation. It was renamed CCS Trexcom. I continued programming IVR systems as before. In addition to my IVR responsibilities, I became system administrator for a Novell file server, a Meridian SL1 PBX and an HP3000 system.

    I learned SQL programming for my work with the Virginia Employment Commission's Continuing Claims System (weekly unemployment claims).

  • Computer Communications Specialists, Inc.

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Computer Software industry)

    March 1985 — 1996 (11 years)

    Computer Communications Specialists (CCS) would create virtually any custom system it could sell for the IBM Series/1 EDX platform. As time went on we shifted to the IBM PC. I started work there doing Series/1 programming for Consolidated Edison of New York (ConEd). I later shifted from the Series/1 to the PC and I learned C programming.

    The systems I wrote for ConEd underwent several changes over the years. Originally there had been five programmers working on their PET system and as many working on their FPET system. Eventually I assumed responsibility for all parts of both systems, including code written in assembler, Nantucket Clipper and C, all of which I had modified as ConEd's needs changed.

    ConEd is only one of many customers I have worked with. I worked on many ACCA receiving systems for wholesale companies.

    C is one just one of the languages I have used. I have done many systems with CCS' ScriptWrite. As such, I am primarily an IVR programmer.
