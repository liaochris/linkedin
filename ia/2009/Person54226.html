Dan Sims, CMP, MBA’s Summary

The founder of three businesses in the meetings industry, Dan currently serves as Director of Sales at the Worldwide Speakers Group.

A CMP with an MBA in Entrepreneurship, Dan believes that relationships are the key to business and personal success. This philosophy, and a deep desire to understand the speakers he represents, has taken him on wonderful adventures around the world including the summit of Mt. Kilimanjaro with a famed mountain guide, along the Great Wall of China with an academic thought leader, Pearl Harbor with a POW, cave diving in the Yucatan with a multicultural expert, skiing the Canadian Rockies with a communications guru, being body-slammed by a professional wrestling champion, serving as a pretend bodyguard for a George W. look-a-like on the Capitol lawn, drinking too many vodka tonics with a political pundit and many other incredible experiences.

After working at a speakers bureau for several years, Dan left to start The Sims Management Group which provided advocacy and administrative support for well-known professional speakers including Capt. Gerald Coffee (7-year POW), Stacy Allison (first American woman to summit Everest), celebrity singer/speaker Willie Jolley and many others. Soon thereafter The Sims Management Group became THE AGENCY Speakers, LLC which represented hundreds of high-profile speakers including NFL Hall of Famers Steve Young and Troy Aikman, Olympic Champions Dan Jansen and Bonnie Blair, Hollywood celebs Alec Baldwin and Whoopi Goldberg, business guru Pat Lencioni and others. In early 2009, Sims was drawn away from THE AGENCY to become Director of Sales at the Worldwide Speakers Group where he now provides his clients access to thousands of speakers around the globe ranging from very specific content experts to the most well-known individuals in the world.

Dan earned a B.S. in Marketing from Penn State and an M.B.A. from George Mason University.
