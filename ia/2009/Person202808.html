Roland Clarke PhD’s Summary

My preparation includes 24+ years experience in renewable energy, energy efficiency and electric utilities. My skills include concept engineering and analysis, economic analysis, project management and project development. My experienced was gained in electric utilities in Connecticut, Massachusetts and Pennsylvania USA, as well as Barbados.

At present, I am CEO of Clarke Energy Associates.

Until recently, I was the Project Manager of the Caribbean Renewable Energy Development Programme (CREDP), which is a region wide market transformation project to introduce commercial electricity produced from renewable energy resources. This project is executed by the CARICOM Secretariat in Georgetown Guyana, South America, with funding from the Global Environment Facility and the United Nations Development Programme (UNDP) of US$4.7 million.

My credentials include the PhD, energy management and policy, University of Pennsylvania, and the MSc Alternative Energy for Developing Countries, Department of Mechanical Engineering, Reading University, UK. I am also a Senior Member of the Association of Energy Engineers, USA.

I am an active invited speaker and panelist to a number of conferences on energy in the Caribbean. I am also a trainer in the use of RETScreen, a popular project analysis tool for clean energy projects.

Roland Clarke PhD’s Specialties:

Renewable Energy and Energy Efficiency. Project analysis and economics, deal origination, project preparation for financing, green house gas analysis. Electric utility avoided costs analysis, production cost modelling. DSM benefit cost analysis, evaluation, market transformation. Speaker and advocate for clean energy.
