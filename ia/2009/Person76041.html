  • Music Producer

    Espion

    (Music industry)

    January 1994 — Present (15 years 10 months)

    Stephen Arnold began producing electronic music in 1994. In 1997 he moved to Glasgow to study sound, audio engineering & music technology, and began to network with influencial producers & promoters from the industrial city. This fuelled the fire to produce darker, more experimental music. After several years as a promoter from 2001 and resident at various club nights, Espion made his first mark on the underground scene in 2005, with sell-out releases on Transparent Sounds' label Orson Records, followed by remixes on two of the best selling records on Billy Nasty's Electrix Records; Transparent Sounds' 'Cider Club ep' & 'Spot the Difference'.

    Since then, his diversity in his music has seen him progress into a well established artist, releasing with pioneering electronic artists from around the world, with frequent plays on Dave Clarke's White Noise radio show.

    Espion has brought his dark and meticulous sound to venues all over the UK, Prague, Berlin, Udine (Italy) and Shenzhen (China), while also doing sound recording, sound design, post production & music for short film.
