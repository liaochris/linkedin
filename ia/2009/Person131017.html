  • IPTV IMS Architecture and Standards

    Nortel

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; NT; Telecommunications industry)

    April 2007 — August 2008 (1 year 5 months)

    Responsible for developing IMS-based IPTV architecture and transition plan from non-IMS IPTV deployments to fully integrated IMS IPTV systems. Teamed with other Nortel IPTV architects to develop proposals for customer trials and deployment. Represented Nortel in Alliance for Telecommunications Industry Solutions (ATIS) IPTV Interoperability Forum (IIF), rapidly becoming one of the top contributors to the ongoing work. Received ATIS IIF Outstanding Contributions award. Appointed IIF liaison to the MultiService Forum (MSF). Attended MSF as representative of IIF. Helped coordinate work between IIF and MSF to achieve interoperability testing at Global MSF Interoperability event, to be held in October 2008.

  • Multimedia Protocol Standards

    Nortel

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; NT; Telecommunications industry)

    2002 — April 2007 (5 years)

    Nortel prime for all international standards work for the H.248/Megaco protocol in the International Telecommunications Union (ITU) and the Internet Engineering Task Force (IETF). Author/editor of vast majority of the H.248 sub-series of standards published by the ITU. Member of United States delegation to the ITU-T Study Group 16 on several occasions. Drafted and presented United States policy on behalf of the State Department. Widely regarded as one of top two world experts on H.248/Megaco protocol. Responsible for developing and coordinating multi-body (ITU-T, IETF, 3rd Generation Partnership Project (3GPP), European Telecommunication Standards Institute (ETSI) Telecoms & Internet converged Services & Protocols for Advanced Networks (TISPAN), MSF) standards strategy for H.248. Nortel prime for ITU-T SG16.

    Nortel representative to CableLabs for PacketCable 2.0 IMS SIP Residential Services. Responsible for drafting text and providing input on PC 2.0 standards documents

  • H.248 Architect

    Nortel

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; NT; Telecommunications industry)

    2000 — April 2007 (7 years)

    Responsible for architecture and implementation of the H.248/Megaco protocol in all seven Nortel Succession products. Owned actual H.248 implementation code in Nortel’s flagship VoIP product, taking over from the initial pre-standard implementation and turning it into the industry-leading H.248 Media Gateway Controller. Responsible for detailing interoperability requirements and negotiating joint interoperability design for and with third parties. Nortel H.248 products had established interoperability with products from at least ten third party vendors. Taught H.248 to customers at various global locations on numerous occasions.

  • Member of Scientific Staff

    Nortel

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; NT; Telecommunications industry)

    1996 — 2000 (4 years)

    As part of R&D team, responsible for design, implementation and support of several customer deliverable features, including adapting CLASS display features for ISDN BRI lines. Key member of team responsible for designing, implementing and providing proof of concept for product that would eventually become Nortel’s flagship VoIP product. Coordinated staff from several geographically diverse locations to achieve project goals. Technical project leader for release that led to Nortel’s landmark $1.1B contract with Sprint to deploy VoIP in their network.
