Christine Kavanaugh’s Summary

Dedicated to providing data driven insights into customer behavior and an actionable plan for meeting user and business needs online, Christine is the defacto champion for user experience design as well as evolving trends and best practices. She has successfully developed and integrated user experience methodology into a number of interactive agencies and wins UX advocates and supporters everywhere she goes.

With over 9 years of experience, Christine brings a creative mindset and the ability to mentor and lead multidisciplinary teams. She specializes in business and brand strategy, process and methodology, personae creation, usability testing as well as a unique method for quickly and effectively identifying target audience requirements and the resulting feature set.

A graduate of NYU Tisch School of the Arts, Christine brings an eclectic background with her to the wonderful world of interactive including, directing off-Broadway theatre for the Obie-winning New Georges, designing auction catalogues for Sotheby’s, documentary film production for the award-winning American Masters series on PBS, production assistance for Saturday Night Live, stand-up comedy in clubs such as The Improv and Catch a Rising Star as well as feature film development for MGM.

So, how the heck did she ever get into user experience design? One of the world’s great mysteries, for sure. Christine likes to think that she is good at her job because she offers a unique perspective and skill set that comes from outside the bubble with her goal being to create relevant interactive experiences that make people’s lives more interesting and fun. This effort includes work for MetLife, Microsoft, GE, Epson, Adobe, American Express, Bristol-Myers Squibb, Glaxo Smith Kline, IBM, M&Ms, Yankee Candle, Garnier Fructis, Old Navy, Citigroup, Deutsche Bank, Toyota, Mitsubishi, sanofi-aventis, and Scholastic.

Christine Kavanaugh’s Specialties:

connecting the dots, translating insights into tools you can use, business and brand strategy, social anthropology, information architecture, interaction design, content strategy, usability, personae, user scenarios, process and methodology, writing, presentations
