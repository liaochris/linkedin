Hartley Millar’s Summary

"Impossible" consulting tasks get me excited: developing network relationships that really change users' behaviour, devising cunning marketing approaches and delightful bid propositions; managing confrontations in politics or clashes in technical insights; providing an "anchor in reality" as non-exec or "business angel"; bridging interfaces - public/private sectors, marketing/logistics, governance/management.

Having survived a raft of conundrums and built bridges in all sorts of niches, I have experience to spare.

Sceptical and radical yet friendly; so I like to probe deeply - "why", "how" and "who"? I am keen to convert this into practical and understandable solutions; I try to be the consultant who is constructive and creative rather than confrontational and looks to the situation rather than the textbook for ideas and answers. Curiosity and a rapid grasp of new areas have led to a wide spectrum of roles and diversity of knowledge - many industries, big companies, SMEs, government, European Union, UN.

With homes and offices in the UK and the Netherlands, the challenges that appeal will make use of decades of varied experience and/or a logician's trained intellect (still reasonably intact).

My colleagues in Management Partners and I perform best assisting intelligent senior executives who have strong customer orientations/priorities - but face obstructions that are hard to overcome because of too little time, lack of access to the facts, or too many "yes-men" around them.

I recently led writing and publication of guidelines for the corporate governance approach to multi-organisational/JV projects. I chair a dialogue "task force" of journalists from around the Med (including Israel). Development areas include online identity, electronic games, social security software, trusted third party services for consortia, market/culture development in central and east Europe and Turkey, and European Union initiatives and R&D projects (including FP7).

Hartley Millar’s Specialties:

Governance of project management, multi-organisation projects, market assessment and modelling, non-executive directorship, IT systems strategy, international business strategy, network development and maintenance, facilitation and dispute avoidance.
Publishing (electronic, hard copy), games, IT, software sales, government services, stationery, high-tech SMEs, journalism and EU relations.
Netherlands, Poland, Slovenia, Turkey and the Middle East, and with international politics.
