Chloe Feinberg’s Summary

An entrepreneur and intrapreneur, I am at Ashoka working on transforming healthcare in rural areas. I am helping support a start-up that will launch in Summer 2009 in India. I am part of the Ashoka team that is promoting change within and outside of the organization. My responsibilities include finding, evaluating and testing diagnostic and medical tools for use in resource-poor settings, with a focus on base of the pyramid markets. I also train end users on these technologies and will help support an initiative to provide ongoing training of these tools.

I recently completed my master's degree in International Science and Technology Policy at the Elliott School of International Affairs at the George Washington University. I focused on appropriate technologies, entrepreneurship, and international development.

Through that program I worked with a team of students to evaluate in March 2009 ICT technologies for use in the Ministry of Health in Kenya. This work was done as an external evaluation for DataDyne, in collaboration with the United Nations Foundation - Vodafone Group Foundation Technology Partnership.

Prior to joining Ashoka, I worked as project manager at the CUBRC Center for International Science and Technology Advancement (CISTA), where I managed international collaborative biological research (CBR) projects in the former Soviet Union. Additionally, I have provided project management training to scientists in this region. In this position I worked with US and foreign government agencies, scientific collaborators, and contractors.

On another note, I am interested in starting a green coworking space in Washington, DC and expanding to capital cities in emerging markets, to help foster entrepreneurial successes.
