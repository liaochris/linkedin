Lynda Elliott’s Summary

Glass Partnership was a full service digital agency. I was hired during a period of growth and expansion as the lead Information Architect (a newly created role), with the opportunity to grow my own team in the future. My role was to develop strategy, processes and documentation on UX, IA and accessibility.

I acted as a mentor to work placements and graduates, and worked closely with all members of a multi disciplinary team through full project life cycles, from requirements gathering, competitor analysis and content audits to paper prototype walkthroughs and testing staging server websites.

I was involved in pitching and developing pitching strategies, facilitating stakeholder engagement sessions with clients to draw out information on all users (internal and external), and using this information to create information architecture, user personas and journeys, process flows etc.

As part of ongoing self development, I researched emerging social trends and user groups, developments and current consensus and standards within accessibility and usability, and wrote articles and blogs to promote thought leadership for the agency.

I was also given the task of looking at how Glass could develop a strategy/rationale to approach NHS Primary Care Trusts to redesign their digital offerings. I assembled a team within the agency and we created a series of criteria for five sections (SEO, Content, Visual Impact, Accessibility and IA/Usability) to assign scores for each website.

The analysis was rigorous and objective, following best practice and NHS guidelines. The output of this research was a series of individual reports giving scores and explaining the research and the importance of the five sections. All of the score data was kept within Excel spreadsheets.

The reports proved to be compelling marketing collateral for Glass, and opened the door for the agency to promote itself to the NHS and subsequently bring in new business.
