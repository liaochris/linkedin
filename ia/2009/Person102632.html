James J. Gillespie’s Summary

James J. Gillespie is an assistant professor of business at the University of Illinois at Chicago (UIC) and a former assistant professor of law at the Illinois Institute of Technology (IIT). He specializes in healthcare policy, strategy, and technology. James has extensive background as a scholar, teacher, and consultant, and he grew his own small business from $0 to almost $1M in annual revenues within 5 yrs. His research has appeared in Academy of Management Review, European Journal of Social Psychology, Harvard Business Review, and Strategic Management Journal. James serves on the Advisory Board of the Center for Performance Management at Stonehill College, the Board of Trustees for La Rabida Children’s Hospital, and the Board of Directors for i.c.stars, a non-profit technology organization. His educational background includes: Northwestern, MA, PhD; Harvard, JD; Princeton, MPA; Massachusetts Institute of Technology, BS; Carnegie Mellon University, graduate coursework in public policy. James is also President and Founder of the non-profit Center for Healthcare Innovation.

    (Research industry)

    July 2009 — Present (5 months)

    .
    Description
    • The Center for Healthcare Innovation will be a non-profit, non-partisan, and independent organization committed to serving as a catalyst for stimulating ideas, people, companies, and institutions to achieve excellence in healthcare innovation.

    Vision
    • Preeminent center for the practice, research, teaching, funding, and discussion of healthcare innovation.

    Mission
    • Support and stimulate development of healthcare innovation by encouraging collaboration among academia, corporations, entrepreneurs, professional firms, governments, investors, and non-profit organizations.

    Goals
    • Increase the quality and quantity of healthcare innovation at the local, national, and international levels.
    • Serve as a neutral, good-faith connector for the leading actors and institutions in the healthcare innovation space, particularly in regard to sharing best practices.
    • Serve as a source of wisdom, information, and knowledge for leading healthcare executives, entrepreneurs, and policymakers regarding healthcare innovation issues.

    Principles
    • Operate CHI for the benefit of others rather than for the benefit of the organizers.
    • Collaboration and partnership, rather than competition, with existing individuals and institutions in the healthcare innovation space.
    • Avoid duplication of events, products, and services already being effectively provided by other firms and organizations in the healthcare innovation space.

    Organizational Structure
    • 501(c)3 non-profit organization.
    • Governed by the Board of Directors.
    • Presidency is and will remain an unpaid position.
