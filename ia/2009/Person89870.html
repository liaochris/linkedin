Greg Pfister’s Summary

I am a designer/engineer/scientist in the general computer area who is typically described as a "systems person." This apparently refers to someone who knows just enough to be dangerous in multiple areas: Processor architecture, I/O architecture, operating systems, database systems, J2EE, interpretive languages, etc. Most recently I've been getting involved in PCI SIG (as a rep from IBM) and Virtual Worlds, so go figure. Hardware people tend to think I'm a software guy, and software people think I'm a hardware guy. I'm no help resolving this; I've never fully understood just what it is that I do, a situation which is occasionally mildly uncomfortable.

Goals? To continue being usefully confused.

Greg Pfister’s Specialties:

Here are some specific areas that I've worked in: clusters, InfiniBand, I/O, parallel processing in general, computer graphics, virtual worlds. Really good at explaining things that many consider confusing and/or black arts, leading me to publishing two editions of the book "In Search of Clusters," called the Bible of Clusters by reader reviews.
