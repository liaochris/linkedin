Rick Spitz’s Experience

  • CTO

    ReachLocal

    (Privately Held; Marketing and Advertising industry)

    2004 — Present (5 years )

    One of the original founders of ReachLocal. Serves as CTO. Reachlocal now has over 800 employees in 40 offiices and does business in 4 countries.

    Personally implemented proof of concept proto type in early 2004. Coauthored patents on local advertising and proxy technology. Hired and directed technical teams to develop automated software platform to support a network of affiliates and sales agents. Created fully automated virtual campaign provisioning, reporting and keyword bidding optimization process with the scalability to manage many thousands of advertisers. Added support for multi-lingual & multi-currency to support Canadian, European, and Australian environments.

    Deployed corporate IT infrastructure for over 40 worldwide offices and four datacenters.

    Major technologies utilized include Apache, XML, SOAP, HTML, Java, Perl, PHP, VOIP, Javascript, MySQL & Oracle databases, on Linux platforms and Windows platforms.

  • CTO

    WorldWinner

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Internet industry)

    2000 — 2004 (4 years )

    CTO/Vice President Development for WorldWinner.com, an online games site specializing in skill-based cash games. Led technology development and site operations for Web, game, and financial backend services. Helped grow Worldwinner from a startup to a successful business serving over 5 million players.

    Coauthored patents on online games and antifraud technology. Directed the technical specification, deployment, and operation of data centers. Hired development teams to develop game families, automated tournament management, back-end financial services, and automated reporting systems to support a network of hundreds of partner sites. Major technologies utilized include C++, HTML, Perl, Java, Active-X, Informix Databases, on Windows & Linux platforms. Created rapid cycle formal development methodology & Q/A processes.

  • Vice President Development

    SunGard - Decalog

    (Privately Held; 10,001 or more employees; SDS; Information Technology and Services industry)

    1997 — 2000 (3 years )

    Vice President, Product Development for Decalog Inc. (a division of Sungard). Headed development for financial applications used by major financial services companies including Citicorp, Warburg Pinkus, and AXA. Distributed development teams in Boston, Paris, Israel.

    Delivered support for web and thin client user interfaces, higher transaction volumes, FIX electronic transactions, real-time pricing and valuation, and next generation server based compliance and performance services. Delivered formal year 2000 compliant release and support for Euro currency. Created formal Q/A organization utilizing automated problem tracking and regression testing systems.

  • Vice President Software

    Apple

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; Computer Hardware industry)

    1991 — 1996 (5 years )

    Vice President, Software for Apple Computer in Cupertino, California, leading the Macintosh system software organization. Delivered key products including System 7 series, PowerPC, and Quicktime multimedia. Refactored MacOS hardware layer to allow independent delivery by various product lines (portables, desktop systems, servers). Initiated cross licensing of Truetype font technology & Quicktime multimedia to establish industry standards. Oversaw prototype MacOS port to the intel platform.

    Subsequently directed technology strategy for Apple’s education market. Worked with major research universities such as Stanford, University of Michigan, MIT to expand usage of computer technologies into environments for higher and ongoing learning. Speaker at numerous technology conferences. Participant in PBS television series "Schools of the Future".

  • Group Manager VAX/VMS Software

    DEC

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; DEC; Computer Hardware industry)

    1977 — 1991 (14 years )

    Led the VAX/VMS software team at Digital Equipment Corporation.
    Speaker at numerous industry and DECUS technology conferences. Delivered several VMS operating system releases to support: many new VAX hardware systems, improved VAX Cluster operation, symmetric multiprocessing, and transaction processing. Initiated port of VMS to the Alpha architecture resulting in industry’s highest performance commercial grade computing at that time.

    Worked with Dick Hustvedt & Gordon Bell to pilot the VAX Station line. Managed development of VAX workstation software and X11 graphics standard.

    Previous to that held various technical positions at DEC involving design, development, and support of VAX/VMS systems. Technical supervisor for VMS communications subsystems. Designed high performance distributed terminal architecture.
