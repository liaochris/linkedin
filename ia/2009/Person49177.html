Cord Pereira’s Summary

BrandEntertain assists brands to create, package and drive their own media platforms allowing them to control and leverage the direct selling motion and branding around their content wherever it is consumed. (live, local and on three screens) Our end-to-end approach blends together local/live events, touring, web, mobile and television elements, to create a vibrant brand ecosystem that historically is reserved for media networks. With web 2.0 and new distribution opportunities, the rules are changing daily for brands to become proactive in shaping and finding new sales channels for products and services. We assemble and collaborate with the finest marketers and producers in the sports, entertainment and technology industries to form a branded entertainment team to work alongside brand management and their agencies.

Cord Pereira’s Experience

  • Preferred Licensing Agent/Consultant

    Arena Racing USA

    (Entertainment industry)

    February 2009 — Present (5 months)

    Arena Racing was founded in 2002 and has been developing and refining their proprietary (patent-pending) professional, indoor arena racing format featuring real ½ scale stock cars, that race on banked oval tracks inside arenas.

    The official sanctioning body is called Arena Racing League.
    Each local market operates their own league with local driver teams, competing in 15 local race events each season. The overall local points winner from each market moves on to regional and national competitions. At the end of each season, a national champion is crowned.

    Arena Racing has been developing its business model in three Licensed Operation markets including Charlotte (NC), Hampton Roads (VA) and Grand Rapids (MI). Over the next five years Arena Racing expects to launch a minimum of 25 Licensed Operation markets. All three leagues are currently operating.

    Arena Racing League format allows each Licensed Operation market to sell up to 56 local driving team medallions. The number of medallions is capped and transferable, so they have the potential to build value over time. All Drivers are certified by the league and can be as young as 14. Teams must buy cars, tires and accessories through the local franchise licensee. Teams can sell their own sponsors, in accordance with branding standards as determined by Arena Racing League and the local licensee.

    Arena Racing has an experienced board and management team, which includes HUJoe GibbsUH (former NFL Redskins coach and owner of Joe Gibbs Racing for NASCAR) and Gary Becker (former Pace Motor Sports) as board members. Arena Racing was founded by league president Ricky Dennis, whose father won the Daytona Grand National three times.
