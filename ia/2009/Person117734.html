John A. Norris, JD, MBA’s Summary

I graduated with high honors from Cornell's law and business schools (where I was a class officer, a federal health-planning fellow, a 2nd-place champion of the first-year Moot Court competition, editor-in-chief of the international law review, graduated near the very top of my business school class, and chaired two business school reunions) and Harvard's Kennedy School of Government, was 2nd-in-command of the US FDA in Washington (where as COO I reformed the FDA and made it much more cost-effective and timely in its new product deliberations and enforcement actions), and have served as the CEO and GC or a board member or consultant and rainmaker to seven healthcare, life sciences, and healthcare-IT companies, law firms, and consulting firms (where I achieved very positive results). I was, for example, for more than a decade a major player at Summit Technology, the founder of the laser eye surgery medical device industry which included diagnosis and treatment of serious eye problems and which my teammates and I sold to Nestle for a billion dollars, and also for more than a dozen years, as a second job, founder and faculty-editor-in-chief of the American Journal of Law & Medicine, a leading health law, policy, and regulatory journal published by MIT's University Press which my colleagues and I contributed to Boston University Law School. I also simultaneously taught health law, policy, regulation, and management (including FDA, healthcare reimbursement, healthcare-IT, and healthcare reform) at Harvard for a dozen years, as a third job. Finally, in addition to my executive and lawyer experience, I have substantial regulatory affairs and business development experience, both key parts of my jobs during the past two decades, I was a key player in taking a company public and then private, and I was a corporate officer, a worldwide managing director, EVP, and a chief rainmaker at Hill and Knowlton, one of the world's largest marketing and management consulting firms.
