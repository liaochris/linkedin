Kristan Tetens’s Summary

Kristan Tetens is the director of communications for two major academic units at Michigan State University: the College of Music and the Residential College in the Arts and Humanities.

She has more than 20 years of experience in planning, implementing, and evaluating integrated communications and marketing strategies that have measurably enhanced public support of MSU. For the last five years, her efforts have focused on the university's arts and humanities programs.

An active member of MSU's Cultural Engagement Council and local, state, regional, and national arts advocacy organizations, Kristan is a co-founder of the One Book, One Community program (a collaboration between MSU and the City of East Lansing) and the creator of Cool U, a one-day university in the arts and humanities for adults.

Kristan has extensive experience in public relations planning and in creating arts programming that builds bridges between cultural organizations and the publics they serve. She believes that the arts and humanities play a unique and intrinsically valuable role in creating vibrant communities and in fostering self-expression, the appreciation of beauty, and cross-cultural understanding.

An award-winning copywriter and project manager, she will earn accreditation by the Public Relations Society of America during the coming year.
