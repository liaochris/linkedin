Alfred Huger’s Summary

(Current company bio) Alfred Huger is a well-known fixture in the Internet security industry. A two-time veteran of launching successful start-up companies, Alfred sold SecurityFocus, a leading provider of enterprise threat management systems, to Symantec in August 2002. In his role at Symantec Alfred has lead the development of technologies in Symantec’s Managed Security Services, Security Response and SaaS lines of business.

Currently Alfred leads a series of technology teams which build products and security content for Symantec's Consumer and Enterprise security business lines.

Alfred is frequently called upon to speak to security issues in the media with common coverage in such outlets as The Globe and Mail, Washington Post, New York Times, CNN and many others.

Early stage startups, computer security product development

  • VP Engineering / Founder

    SecurityFocus

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Computer & Network Security industry)

    April 1999 — August 2002 (3 years 5 months)

    SecurityFocus Inc. was acquired by Symantec in August 2002.
    Funded by: ArrowPath Venture Capital & Mobius Venture Capital

    As a co-founder of SecurityFocus Inc., a Security Intelligence Product company, Mr. Huger’s responsibilities covered a broad range. In addition to raising initial funding and building out the nucleus of the company (in terms of hiring and structuring) he was responsible for defining and then building the company’s multiple product offerings. SecurityFocus Inc. brought to market a series of successful security offerings including the world’s first Early Warning System for Internet based attacks (patent pending) based off a global sensor network as well as worlds first commercial Security Vulnerability Database and Security Risk Alerting Service (tms.symantec.com & alerts.symantec.com). These products experienced significant success in the commercial and government sectors leading to SecurityFocus’ acquisition by Symantec in August of 2002.

    April 1998 — April 1999 (1 year 1 month)

    As Director of Engineering for Network Associates Total Network Security Division Mr. Huger oversaw product engineering for the company’s Vulnerability Assessment product (CyberCop Scanner) in addition to it’s Host Intrusion Detection and Unix based Network Intrusion Detection offerings. While at NAI Mr. Huger also brought to market the industries first Network Emulating Honeynet product, CyberCop Sting.

  • VP Operations / Founder

    Secure Networks Inc.

    (Public Company; 11-50 employees; Computer & Network Security industry)

    May 1995 — May 1998 (3 years 1 month)

    Secure Networks Inc. was acquired by Network Associates, Inc. in May 1998.
    Funded by: Privately held

    Mr. Huger was the initial founder of Secure Networks Inc. a Vulnerability Assessment company. As with Security Focus Mr. Huger’s responsibilities were diverse. In addition to securing initial funding and employees Mr. Huger was responsible for Product Engineering and Delivery. SNI’s primary product offering was Ballista, an award winning vulnerability assessment scanner. Ballista employed new (and patented) technology to assess and test the security postures of TCP/IP connected networks and experienced strong customer adoption in global commercial and government marketplaces which led to the eventual acquisition of SNI by Network Associates in May 1998.
