Joel Lanz’s Summary

Joel is a member of the American Institute of Certified Public Accountant’s IT Executive Committee. Joel is the Chair of the New York State Society of Certified Public Accountant’s Information Technology Committee and the immediate former Chair of the society’s Technology Assurance committee.

Joel’s articles have been published in many peer-reviewed accounting and financial industry publications. He serves on the Editorial Board of Bank Accounting and Finance and is on the editorial review board for The CPA Journal. He currently teaches graduate accounting courses at Long Island University. Previously, Joel taught graduate courses at New York University and Pace University. He frequently presents at regulatory, industry and professional conferences.

Joel’s client base includes regional and community banks, electronic funds transfer networks, health insurance companies, technology service providers and boutique consulting and accounting firms serving the financial services industry. Recent engagements have included conducting IT audit outsourcing on both a retainer and project basis, design and development of an information security program, development of a vendor management program, facilitating a technology governance self-assessment, performing GLBA compliance testing including relevant independent security testing, leading a privacy impact and security risk assessment, reviewing outsourcing contracts for relevant vendor management provisions, performing due diligence over current and potential technology-related vendors, benchmarking information security practices against best practices, and conducting a strategic evaluation of products offered to the financial services industry.

Joel Lanz’s Specialties:

Service specialties include technology risk management, governance, FFIEC regulatory compliance, information security, technology auidt and IT Vendor Management.
