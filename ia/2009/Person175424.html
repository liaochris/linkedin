Nikhil Varma’s Summary

My professional career has evolved from the level of a developer to those of an architect and manager over a period of time. I started off as an embedded software developer working for Samsung Korea (client) in India and then NEC, Australia. My primary work was related to developing Fax and VOIP solutions. On the basis of my performance and ability to meet the targets set by my Indian employer, I was appointed onsite in NEC, Australia to co-ordinate with the client and the offshore team.

Following that, I came to Canada to pursue my Masters in Computer Engineering. I collaborated with my professor to work in the area of Mission critical system design and Real time Communication and presented two research papers in international conferences. I jointly wrote a book review on the use of Java in Embedded technologies.

After graduating, I set out to my entrepreneurial venture Volansa Technologies.Following this I had another valuable working experience with Net-Integration Technologies. I worked in the Montreal R&D center as an Evil Death Ray where my primary job was to identify, design and execute their automated testing strategy for quicker product roll out.

I was then involved with Talkio and SarvGoona Technologies. Talkio is a web 2.0 solution directed to capture the web traffic and form social networks dynamically. My role was a technical architect and project manager, and the product is set to roll out in Early 2009. The patent application is complete and was filled in Feb 2009. SarvGoona was a business process analysis company where I acted as a consultant for various mid size companies in Montreal to strategize their development and support strategies. Responding to the requirements and feedback from the market, I started Inteligan Technologies for Software development and services. The business operated on a cost effective model as the project management and software design was done locally while the majority of the work was done in India.
