Michael Bromley’s Summary

Having completed the concentration requirements for an Economics degree at Brandeis University in 1996 and then graduating from the University of Michigan in 1988, with a degree in Philosophy, I began my career with the Investment Banking firm Lehman Brothers in Boston Massachusetts. After four years at Lehman Brothers an opportunity to become Vice President of the Junior Broker Training Program led to a move to Bear Stearns and co. In 1993 I left Bear Stearns to become Vice President and Branch Manager of Coburn and Meredith’s Investment Banking office in Boston. While at Coburn and Meredith I also founded MSB Financial, a private placement investment firm.

In 1997 I left Wall Street to become Executive Vice President of Marketing and New Business Development for the startup syndicated research and consulting firm World Research Advisory. A successful relationship with America Online eventually led to my joining the company in 1999 as Executive Director of the AOL Devices group. At AOL I developed initial business strategy and subsequent partnerships for the “AOL Anywhere” program. Among other initiatives, I was responsible for bringing the AOL experience to wireless devices including the AOL Communicator built by Research In Motion. In 2002, after leaving AOL, following the acquisition of Time Warner inc., I joined Research In Motion which brought me to Sydney as Director of Emerging Markets.

In 2004 I began the process of becoming a permanent Australian resident and by 2005 was granted a permanent visa allowing me to live and work in Australia. As Executive Vice President of bCODE I oversaw the development and launch of the bCODE mobile ticketing solution before joining Telstra in October 2006.

Currently I am Head of Online for Telstra Business and oversee strategy, development, and management of the Telstra Business web presence and related online services.
