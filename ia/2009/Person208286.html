Samuel Wolfe’s Summary

Samuel Wolfe’s Specialties:

Environmental regulation, especially air pollution, emissions trading, greenhouse gas regulation, water pollution, chemical safety, financing of environmental infrastructure projects; energy regulation and planning

Samuel Wolfe’s Experience

  • Chief Counsel

    New Jersey Board of Public Utilities

    (Government Administration industry)

    March 2006 — Present (3 years 2 months)

    • Helped write NJ Energy Master Plan; worked with FERC, PJM, and USDOE to coordinate electric transmission planning with NJ energy & environmental goals.
    • Working with NJ energy utilities to invest $500 million in energy efficiency and economic stimulus.
    • Developed plan adopted by BPU to mitigate “leakage” from increased imports of carbon-intensive electric generation.
    • Co-authored Nat'l Academies/Nat'l Research Council report, "Coal Research and Development to Support National Energy Policy."
    • Testified before U.S. Senate committee & briefed staff on need to auction emissions credits in greenhouse gas cap and trade programs; testified before state legislature on offshore wind, other energy issues.
    • Wrote rules to transition BPU solar program from rebates to market incentives; helped improve program to support financing of solar projects..
    • Worked on PJM forum on long-term contracting for electricity supply.
    • BPU staff lead on Regional Greenhouse Gas Initiative

  • Member

    Nat'l Research Council Cmte. on Coal Research & Technology to Inform Energy Policy

    (Research industry)

    2006 — 2007 (1 year)

    Co-authored National Academies/National Research Council report, "Coal - Research and Development to Support National Energy Policy." See http://www8.nationalacademies.org/cp/projectview.aspx?key=189, http://dels.nas.edu/dels/rpt_briefs/coal_r&d_final.pdf

  • Assistant Commissioner

    NJ Department of Environmental Protection

    (Environmental Services industry)

    March 2002 — March 2006 (4 years 1 month)

    Managed Air Quality, Water Quality, and Environmental Safety programs; 570 employees, $39 million annual budget.
    • Cut air pollution permit backlogs by more than 90 percent.
    • Led streamlining of air permits for R&D operations, and for combined heat & power facilities..
    • Guided pharmaceutical, chemical, and petroleum refining facilities through difficult permit processes.
    • Worked with cabinet members, other states, stakeholders and the Governor’s office to help develop RGGI.
    • Negotiated nation's first law to require retrofit of diesel engines with air pollution control devices.
    • Led enactment of state rules to cut mercury emissions from coal-fired power plants.
    • Brought strict phosphorus limits into wastewater discharge permits.
    • Served on board of NJ Environmental Infrastructure Trust, and as Commissioner on Delaware River Basin Commission.
    • Ran controversial public hearings; testified before Congressional and State legislative committees.

  • Environmental Policy Manager

    PSEG Services Corporation

    (Public Company; 5001-10,000 employees; Utilities industry)

    November 1998 — March 2002 (3 years 5 months)

    As Environmental Policy Manager for diversified energy company, managed environmental aspects of governmental relations, transactions, and corporate policy.
    • Led environmental due diligence to evaluate potential acquisitions of power plants; forecasted environmental policy developments and incorporated forecasts into plant valuation.
    • Managed team efforts to resolve difficult environmental permitting and enforcement issues with regulatory agencies.
    • Developed corporate guidance and procedures for determining whether maintenance to facilities, and facility upgrades, would trigger New Source Review requirements; crafted and presented proposals to Environmental Protection Agency, environmental groups, and other stakeholders to reform New Source Review program.
    • Persuaded state to make air pollution regulations consistent with nuclear safety needs.
