Yogesh Malhotra’s Summary

AACSB International Impact of Research Task Force recently recognized Yogesh Malhotra's research among others such as Nobel laureate work of Black and Scholes in "exemplars" of business faculty pursuing basic research and having “considerable impact on actual practice.” His research impact is ranked among the top four, six, and three worldwide MIS scholars on Knowledge Management in studies published by the ASIS&T, University of Minnesota, and Drexel University. He has lectured on IT, e-Business, and Knowledge Management at Kellogg, Carnegie Mellon, INSEAD (France), and Queen's (Canada) as invited faculty. Worldwide organizations that have reviewed and recommended his IT innovation ventures include AACSB, ACM, AICPA, ASTD, Harvard, HP, IEEE, ISACA, McKinsey, Microsoft, MIT, NASA, Princeton, Stanford, UN, US Army, US Navy, US Air Force, and, Wharton. His contributions to pioneering global managerial education and research on Business Technology Management and Knowledge Management are recognized in Business Week, CIO Magazine, Computerworld, Fast Company, Forbes, Fortune, Inc., Information Week, InfoWorld, KM World, New York Times, and, Wall Street Journal.

As invited advisor, he has guided national policies and global corporate strategies of some of the world's largest governments and $100 Billion corporations across N. America, Europe, and Asia, and the UN. Prior to that, he founded award-winning IT Management ventures whose corporate clients and patrons across six continents included CMP Media, Doubleclick, Foote Cone & Belding, Goldman Sachs, Google, HP, IBM, IDG, Intel, McKinsey, Microsoft, MIT, Ogilvy & Mather, Oracle, and Xerox. Prior to that, as a software engineer with some of the largest global financial institutions and consulting firms across the U.S., Hong Kong, and India, he was responsible for advising corporate executives on global IT strategy and leading programming of currency arbitrage systems and global financial systems for worldwide banks.
