Nancy Peacock’s Summary

Prior to partnering with Joe Mattis to build Slugfest Records Nashville, I was actively involved in the Nashville music industry as a songplugger and publisher. I represent catalogs ranging from country, pop, rock, R&B to Christian,inspirational and instrumental music. Currently I am working with Robb Royer, founding member of the band Bread, #1 songwriter Lisa Palas, Warner Bros. artist Jeff Middleton, famous vocal coach and producer Jan Smith to name a few.
My first number one single came in the summer of 2006 with Lucie Diamond's cut of "Did He Mention My Name". The goal for my publishing company is to expand its horizons by licensing songs to film, television, commercials and musical theater.
Slugfest Records Nashville released its first artist Brittini Black in the fall of 2008. Her video “Good Happens” has been in the Top 20 on GAC for six strait weeks in 2009. Joe and I co-manage our newly signed artist Scott Lindsey. His first single and video will be released in early summer.
