Stefan Doll’s Experience

  • Design Lead

    Freescale

    (Privately Held; 10,001 or more employees; Semiconductors industry)

    August 2002 — Present (7 years 1 month)

    Design of embedded microcontrollers for automotive applications.

    Responsible for the architecture of a PowerPC-based family of devices.

    Design Lead for MAC7106, MAC7202 and MAC7201. Senior designer for the MAC7102.

    Design of an SCI with built-in LIN support. Design of a configurable timer module.

    Special Interest in configurable IP, currently investigating the use of SystemVerilog for design.

  • Senior Design Engineer

    Intrinsix

    (Privately Held; 201-500 employees; Semiconductors industry)

    July 1998 — May 2001 (2 years 11 months)

    Development of a very large multiplier/divider with 1024 bit length operants, implementation of SHA hash algorithm and design of a TCP/IP packet router directing IP traffic between 2 ethernet interfaces and 2 bus interfaces. (Altera Apex FPGA ~600k Gates).

    Development of a DPLL generating various output frequencies and capable of locking to T1, E1, STS1, DS3 and STS3 frequencies. Also monitors clock signals to determine out of frequency range and loss of signal conditions. (Implemented in a Lucent Orca device).

    Development of a module which interfaces T1 payloads from SONET (STS-12) to ATM.
    The module identifies the deviation of the source frequencies of 336 T1 payloads (+/- 200 ppm) and represent these deviation by producing SRTS time stamps for transmission in the ATM cells.

    Testbench for an ATM scheduler chip using components from the Synopsys ATM workbench.

    Development of a RAM-based FIFO controller for 42 ATM FIFOs using Verilog and Coverscan.

  • Lecturer

    Engineering School of Itajuba, Brazil

    (Government Agency; 501-1000 employees; Semiconductors industry)

    May 1999 — May 1999 (1 month)

    Teaching a VHDL Design Methodology Course which covers the language elements, VHDL synthesis techniques and advanced verification methods. The Duration was one week with 6 hours training per day (lecture and lab work).

  • Design Engineer

    Racal-Datacom

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; Telecommunications industry)

    August 1997 — June 1998 (11 months)

    Using Lattice PLDSs for timing critical interfaces between PCI bridges and device specific busses.

    Design of a protoboard (w Altera FPGAs=to aid software testing of the PCI bridges including the possibility to interconnect two prototype boards. Implementing a TimeSlotAllocator device (XC4028EX) to distribute and merge data between two 8 MBit/s TDM busses and 24 TDM busses at 2 MBit/s. The device also implemented several loopback modes for use in diagnostic SW and a 68360 interface.

    Implementing a Time Slot Allocator with an Altera MAX7064 and a MTSS2046 time switch. The TSA interfaces four 2 MBit/s TDM busses to 32 TDM busses (32 modems).

    Design of an interface FPGA connecting an I2C, 2 Octarts and an RTC to a 68360.

    Participation in board level simulation and development of simulation models for standard devices in VHDL (MC68360, generic TDM transactor).

    Lab debug and verification of PCBs and manufacturing diagnostic code. Support for diagnostic software development.

  • Design Engineer

    Toucan Technology

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Semiconductors industry)

    January 1995 — July 1997 (2 years 7 months)

    Timeslot multiplexer and demultiplexer for four 8 MBit/s TDM busses and 11 TDM busses with 2 MBit/s. Also adapts the processor interfaces of four Brooktree T1 and E1 framers and two MUNICH HDLC controllers to the MC68360 bus, generates and receives (analyses) PRBS 511 test patterns with up to 8 MBit/s. Implemented in an Altera Flex10K30.

    Development of a synthesizable T1/E1-Framer VHDL core. Supporting B8ZS, HDB3 and AMI line coding, ESF, SF and E1 (CAS and CCS) frame formats. Compliant with ITU-T standards and supporting alarm conditions as in Dallas and Mitel framers. Verified by simulation and using a Xilinx proto board & Dallas eval board.

    Participation in system design and development of the main FPGA of a V110 PCB for a Cisco Router. The design interfaces 11 Siemens ITAC devices to a backplane and to a 68302 processor. (Implemented in a XC4008).

    VHDL testing of Toucan designs (ASICs and FPGAs) especially participation in testing of the PCI interface of a RAID type ASIC.
