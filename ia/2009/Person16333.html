    The average recruiter spends 7 seconds (or less) reviewing a resume. Here's what's required to get and hold attention:

    1) An instant overview of your abilities, demonstrated through a 2-3 sentence "Professional Summary", as well as a keyword-friendly "Core Competencies" section. These build interest in a reader, and invites close analysis of a candidate's work history.

    2) Effective job descriptions. The key here is identifying and prioritizing your most important duties, while excluding tasks taken for granted such as "attended meetings," etc. Correct wording is essential to impart a sense of leadership and industry knowledge.

    3) Bulleted accomplishments for every position. Results matter more than anything else on a resume. Statistics should be included wherever possible.
