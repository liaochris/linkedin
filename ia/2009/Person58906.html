David Rose’s Summary

My current focus:

Assure success of SharePoint project for key customer Barge, Waggoner, Sumner and Cannon. Develop business processes for techStrategists to include: Billing, Customer Relationship Management and Marketing efforts.

Also, Improve Kudzu Consultancy business processes and provide product management for internally developed software. My initial focus is on developing Dealer Ignition, http://dealerignition.com application for Co-Op Marketing programs.

Background information:
In 2001 IdleAire Technologies was ripe with opportunities for product development. I was engaged to manage an outsourced relationship with Cadre5, a software development team. IdleAire’s goal was to define and build the software required to deploy IdleAire services. The team began software development in May of 2001 and had the first site grand opening in June of 2002.

In February of 2003 IdleAire chose to bring all software development in-house. I transitioned into a role responsible for hiring and managing the developers in continuation of the project. This group developed many new unique products, including payment systems (credit and fuel cards), reporting systems, a custom door entry system, cash machine, movies-on-demand, embedded VoIP, automated equipment health checks, and an expansive corporate intranet. Our challenge was to develop software to support a growing list of physical locations and over a quarter million users, while continuing to provide tools and improvements for the ever increasing corporate staff.

Historically my role has been to assemble the right mixture of technical expertise and coordinate solution sets for the enterprise as a whole.

My strength is in managing relationships and strategy for the customer and technical staff. I have focused on software applications, development, and technology trends. I have a broad knowledge of current challenges in sales, marketing, accounting, supply chain and corporate development.
