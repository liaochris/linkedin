Russell Myerson’s Summary

I have been fortunate to have played a key role in a number of the evolutionary changes that have taken place in the television business. My greatest personal accomplishments have involved creating new templates for traditional or emerging companies. My passion for the business has been a great motivator. I have a reputation as a problem solver with a great fiscal track record. I have been very successful as a brand builder and an innovative and creative programmer while at the same time staying ahead of the technological curve. What truly defines me, however, are my leadership abilities.

Russell Myerson’s Experience

  • Executive Vice President

    The CW Television Network

    (Partnership; 201-500 employees; Entertainment industry)

    January 2006 — Present (3 years)

    Charged with building the CW PLUS division from its inception in January 2006 through and including the launch in September 2006. Responsible for all aspects of the business and stewardship of the P&L. Includes affiliate acquisition, national unwired advertising sales, creation of local and regional advertiser sales initiatives, programming acquisition, on-air promotion and marketing, traffic, operations, accounting and physical distribution. The CW Plus is a national television network providing programming to analog and digital broadcast (DTV) televisions stations exclusively in the 100+ DMA’s. The CW Plus launch was the largest simultaneous deployment of DTV multicast in America. The service features the programming of the CW Television Network along with demographically compatible acquired product. The CW Plus service has three distinct regional feeds and services 110 domestic television markets. The CW Plus is centralcast out of studios in Los Angeles.

  • Executive Vice President

    The WB Television Network

    (Public Company; 201-500 employees; TWX; Broadcast Media industry)

    August 1997 — September 2006 (9 years 2 months)

    Created the WB 100+ Station Group. Responsible for all aspects of the business and stewardship of the P&L. Included advertising sales, cable carriage (MSO agreements), programming acquisition, promotion and marketing, traffic, operations, accounting and physical distribution.
    The 100+ Station Group was a cable network providing audience coverage and network distribution for the WB Television Network in the 14% of the country where terrestrial over-the-air television frequencies are limited or unavailable. The 100+ network consisted of satellite fed wireless cable interconnects that are personalized by head-end and by market. This entity represented the largest central-casting operation deployed in America and employed a staff of fifty.

  • Senior Vice President--Programming and Operations

    Game Show Network--Sony Pictures Entertainment

    (Public Company; 51-200 employees; Broadcast Media industry)

    March 1994 — June 1997 (3 years 4 months)

    Charged with launching the network and managing the functional areas of programming (creation, production, acquisition, and scheduling), operations, engineering, traffic, and on-air marketing. Program development activities included original long-form and live interstitial programming, many layers of telephone based participatory and interactive elements plus a web component that was developed in 1996 which featured the first on-line application for “Wheel of Fortune” in partnership with AT&T. Launched a special production unit which created weekly programming for state lotteries as well as a separate live unit which produced 8 ½ hours of live game show programming each week.

    To “future-proof” the capital investment, a serial digital television origination and production facility and telephony based interactive unit was designed and constructed, the first of its kind for Sony Electronics in the United States.

  • Director of Programming

    WFLA-TV

    (Broadcast Media industry)

    1989 — 1994 (5 years)

  • Chairman of the Board and CEO

    NATPE International

    (Non-Profit; 11-50 employees; Broadcast Media industry)

    January 1994 — December 1994 (1 year)

    Spent seven years as part of the board of the National Association of Television Program Executives and was elected by my peers in the business as its Chairman and CEO for the year 1994. This was the first cable executive ever elected Chairman of the organization. Co-authored the initiative to expand NATPE’s international scope and moved the organization to a broader more content based focus. Some of the organization’s most successful years occurred during my participation.

  • Corporate Director of Programming

    Media General Broadcast Group

    (Public Company; 201-500 employees; mega; Broadcast Media industry)

    March 1987 — February 1994 (7 years)

    Supervised all program acquisition activities and program scheduling for a group of network affiliated television stations in the southern United States. During my tenure the group had the highest margins and the lowest programming costs in their respective markets. Hundreds of thousands of dollars in annual income were generated tied to the exhibition and marketing of key syndicated television programs.

    Numerous non-traditional revenue streams were created including a variety of local programming initiatives to garner incremental dollars. Partnerships included joint ventures with radio stations for the telecast of major local events and a long-term initiative with Anheuser-Busch in Tampa which resulted in a weekday half-hour strip television show which generated a six-to-one ROI in its inaugural season. Launched a radio network in Tampa to simulcast local news programming during afternoon drive time.
