  • Founder & CEO

    OnlineHR Corporation

    (Internet industry)

    September 2002 — December 2007 (5 years 4 months)

    Approximately 42% of employees return home early with unfinished assignments primarily due to spouses who, because of unresolved isolation, have difficulty assimilating into their new hometowns. We were a seed funded company developing an online social network that would save corporations billions of dollars by reducing the high costs of failed corporate relocations by connecting transferees and their family members, before they moved, to residents in their new areas ( professionals, small business people and other family members) who initially played strong helping roles. Our unique method of building communities created social capital between corporations and their local small business communities and small business people and their peers - while integrating their employees into their organizations and families into their new hometowns.

    inventor - 2 patents pending, titled: "A system and method of establishing and using social networks to facilitate people in life issues"
