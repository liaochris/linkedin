Don Wrege’s Summary

Don Wrege is a results-oriented producer with a record of professional success in media design and production management ranging from the beginnings of multimedia to the emergence of interactive television and the Internet. Wrege builds upon a background in critical aesthetics, coupled with extensive large-scale multimedia and multi-million dollar website production experience, managing both creative and technical teams. Clients served include Pepsi, Whole Foods, Franklin Covey, Mattel, and ABC Television.

Don Wrege’s Interests:

A professional photographer in another life, a multi-instrumentalist with 24-track digital recording studio in his basement, Wrege’s self-produced, self-directed and self-performed music video was picked up by MTV in 1981 and scheduled in normal rotation for two weeks netting an ASCAP Special Award. Don composed and recorded parody songs for KHOW Radio’s Peter Boyles for a period stretching over two years. Wrege appeared on Boulder’s Eclectic Radio Co.’s GoGaGa online radio network with his own regularly scheduled program (ZappaTrax) and as host of the live webcast coverage of The Annual Digital Storytelling Festival. Don videotaped interviews with the festival’s digerati and oversaw the streaming online. One of Wrege’s annual responsibilities at The Kentucky Derby is to videotape short interviews with celebrities for streaming on the Derby website. And Don recently completed his first book, “My Year As Ozzy.”
