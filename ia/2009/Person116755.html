John Censoplano’s Summary

Strong experience in electronics development and design and currently working in the IT infrastructure specialization.

Designing, managing and maintenance of sophisticated IT infrastructure involving Gigabit fiber optics and wireless networking. Large multi-terabyte storage area networks and multi-terabyte tape storage libraries. Large telecom voice networks involving Nortel Meridian mainframe PBXs and Octel voice mail systems integrating these systems with local public networks offered by ATT.

Also, the security aspect of protecting these networks from attack and exploitation.

Lastly, website design and implementation using XML, HTML, CSS and WYSIWYG layout tools.

John Censoplano’s Experience

  • Network Coordinator

    Central Contra Costa Sanitary District

    (Government Administration industry)

    March 1997 — Present (11 years 11 months)

    I worked with Robert Hath and was hired on contract originally and when the position opened I applied and was accepted. I have had the privilege of taking CCCSD from an antiquated single ring Token Ring infrastructure and transformed it into a modern high speed 2GB fiber backbone running 100 Mbits out to the workstations. We have 40 terabytes of tape storage and 20 terabytes of disk storage. Wireless networks and remote networks out to over 24 remote sites. We have Cisco technology for our layer switching and PIX Firewalls and network security. We use Websense web filtering and Linux based email filtering for SPAM blocking. I manage all of the voice and data networks and provide direct support for our Plant Control Systems and our GIS systems.

  • Senior Computer Systems Specialist

    Los Medanos College

    (Educational Institution; 51-200 employees; Higher Education industry)

    1995 — 1997 (2 years)

    Together the Supervisor and I provided regular PC and peripheral support and maintenance both on hardware and software for entire campus.

    The college had a large number of Macintosh PCs in the classrooms from the Mac Plus to the Power Macs that were new on the market. The administration had mostly PC's running MS-DOS and some OS/2 and networked in segregated StarLan networks using Ethernet hubs.

    Special projects:
    1. Installation of a 100 Mbit Copper backbone connecting brand new 10 Mbit switches to all IDF's within the campus.
    2. Upgrading older Novell 2.x servers to Novell 3.x and networking HP Printers, upgrading PC's RAM-Harddrive-OS, networking all MAC's and PC's and creating print servers.
    3. Installing T1 1.5 Mbit Cisco Router and connecting it to the Internet to provide Internet access to the whole campus.
    4. Installation of Mac's in Library and certain classrooms and added special desktop filtering apps to restrict and audit usage.

  • Engineering Liason / QA Lead Engineer

    Media Vision Inc.

    (Consumer Electronics industry)

    1990 — 1995 (5 years)

    I was one of the founding members and was instrumental in putting together much of the initial IT infrastructure as well as the Wildcat BBS which eventually became the company website. I also worked as Lead Technical Support Rep working with our International and Domestic OEMs I worked directly with Media Visions top accounts in the OEM realm such as Microsoft, Leading Edge, Fry's Electronics, Sierra Software and Electronic Arts and many others. I put together training seminars which I took to Microsoft, Leading Edge, Fry's Electronics and others to train their support people on how to use our products to integrate them into systems and how to get Windows 3.0 to have Multimedia Extensions to provide audio and video support. Eventually I transfered into QA and became the Senior QA Support Engineer working with Marshal Lewis who was the original QA Manager. We worked on the Thunder Board, the ProAudio Spectrum Plus and the CDPC which had inherrant design flaws in the ASIC.

  • International Technical Support Specialist

    Everex Computer Systems

    (Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; Computer Hardware industry)

    1989 — 1990 (1 year)

    As the only International Technical Support Specialist for Everex I worked with many of Everex's large OEM customers that were located in the USA, Canada and Europe.

    I managed to learn a little French which helped with those customers located in French speaking countries like France, Canada and Switzerland.

    My specialization was working with Everex storage products such as hard drive and tape drive technologies and the related software for both and PC operating systems such as Xenix and OS/2 as well as DOS (MS DOS & PC DOS)

    I also worked with several domestic companies who bought the Everex PC's and placed their Logo on the case. We also developed a couple of special PCs for certain companies towards the end of Everex's life. (They declared Chapter 11, I got out before thanks to Tim Bratton!)
