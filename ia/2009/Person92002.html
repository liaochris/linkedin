Hannah West’s Summary

Until I can prepare images of my clients' homepages and galleries or stores, please see my work by using the list of client links at my website, www.hannahwestdesign.com.

I hope to continue to create beautiful sites that enable artists to promote and sell their work online while giving them more time to KEEP CREATING! as well as developing a regional resource site to connect members of the arts community in order to facilitate the promotion, expansion and utilization of local business connections.

I also plan to expand my line of Flash media services (both content creation and publishing to the web) with family members who are experienced in video cinematography and electronic music creation.

    2000 — 2007 (7 years )

This was my primary business between 2000 - 2007, when web design took the forefront.   • Assistant Producer

    Babylon Motion Pictures / Revelation Cafe Productions

    (Motion Pictures and Film industry)

    1990 — 1992 (2 years )

    In 1990 I met my husband, who was acting, coaching several young actors and teaching acting classes with his son, who had recently graduated from high school. The young one was a natural talent and his dad had a vision of independent filmmaking that foreshadowed what it has become today. In those days, though, things weren't so easy, but when the desire to make their own movie arose, they went at it like they were seasoned professionals. I basically pitched in wherever a third person was needed, including capacities as talent coordination, set acquisition & production, props, makeup and wardrobe, production scheduling, acting, and graphic design. Together we completed the project with great (pre-personal computer editing) production value and outstanding local response, if not a true "release" or distribution. We have been involved off and on in various independent productions since then.

  • Head Therapeutic Horsebackriding Instructor

    Camp Easter Seals, Washington

    (Medical Practice industry)

    1987 — 1988 (1 year )

    Though I was till a working artist, I started taking temporary office jobs to make extra money, and wound up on an extended assignment at a non-profit called TERI, Inc, an organization that ran several houses and a care and therapy center tailored for severe to profoundly retarded adults. While I was here, I learned about the existence of therapeutic horsebackriding, and that one of only two certification centers in the US was located less than an hour's drive from my home. As an accomplished horsewoman and one who was looking for a more personal service-oriented position in life, I decided this was what I wanted to do. The list of things I needed to do to be accepted in tot he program all came together easily, including a scholarship, and I earned a coveted NARHA (North American Riding for the Handicapped Association) Instructor's certificate under the instruction of Fran Joswick, the founder of this organization. This was the last group she taught before retiring. Upon completion of my certification I was approached by Camp Easter Seals of Washinton and subsequently hired as their Head Instructor. My responsibilities including recruiting, training and organizing a team of 20 volunteers, the care and training of 15 horses, the care of the other animals on the camp's mini farm, and teaching horsebackriding to about 50 students with disabilities ranging from severe cerebral palsy and autism to MS and closed brain trauma. I loved the work, and Washington was beautiful, but learned how much I loved California while I was away and returned to work as an artist, with another solid round of horse portraits commissioned over the next year.
