Carol Carlan’s Summary

Carol Carlan is CEO of Carlan Consulting, LLC. As a motivational speaker, facilitator, and advocate for women’s financial education and success, Carol is known for her extensive involvement and commitment to education, business, economic and social issues within the local community and throughout the state. As the first female bank president in the panhandle, surviving numerous mergers during her 32 years in banking, she took early retirement as the West Panhandle President for Wachovia to form Carlan Consulting, LLC in 2007.

As a Certified LifeSuccess Consultant, Carol works with individuals and corporations taking them from the brink of possibility to the path of infinite probability. Her company empowers individuals through awareness and education with a variety of educational products and tools.

Carol’s “heart work” is far reaching which includes guiding not for profit groups and corporations to grow their mission. She is known for her extensive work in education as a founding member of the PACE Center for Girls Escambia/Santa Rosa Counties and chair of the PACE Statewide Board of Trustee which has been recognized as a national leader in advocacy for at risk girls.

Involvement:
Carol was one of 25 gubernatorial appointees by Governor Crist to serve on the Department of Juvenile Justice Blueprint Commission 2007. Appointed by Governor Bush, Carol has served as Trustee since 1999 for Pensacola Junior College, as well as past president-Emeritus for the Pensacola Junior College Foundation. Former Governor Bush appointed Carol as inaugural chair of Florida Institute for Human and Machine Cognition.

She has been recognized as one of the Panhandles Top 5 Most Powerful Women by the St. Petersburg Times and “A Power Couple” with husband Charles by the Pensacola News Journal Business Journal. She was also honored as Business Leader of the Year by both the Pensacola Business Journal and the Pensacola Bay Area Chamber of Commerce PACE Awards.
