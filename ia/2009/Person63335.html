Diane Wolfe’s Summary

By the triumphant closing hour of MAIN ST. Fort Worth Arts Festival 2009, Diane Wolfe had used every minute of the experience gained in a 25-year career, all in the course of just four extraordinary days.

The fine arts festival, one of the nation's top three, makes its host city a destination and draws on experience in hospitality and destination marketing as well as advocacy of mass transit and environmental initiatives. It demands placements in all media, whether the metro page, nightly news and entertainment blogs or tabloids.

Then, too, stormy weather had threatened briefly, bringing out an issues management tool kit that has safeguarded public sector, nonprofit and for-profit enterprises.

After 15 years' freelance work in one of the nation's top five media markets, this public and media relations specialist is seeking new opportunities.

Her career achievements include:

• Drew crowds generating record revenues for MAIN ST. Fort Worth Arts Festival and crafted issues management strategies that turned a potentially negative media focus on the economy into stories celebrating an enduring market for fine art at the MAIN ST. festival in 2009.

• Made Habitat for Humanity a household name in North Texas.

• Made North Texas air quality issues front-page news on a shoestring budget.

Diane Wolfe’s Specialties:

Crafting the story and message to safeguard brand identity and integrity, managing media relationships, pitching the story and coordination of coverage, issues management and crisis communication.
