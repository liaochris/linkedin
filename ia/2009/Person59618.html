Daniel Doubrovkine’s Summary

Thirteen years of hands-on in-house and project-based software development experience for both client and 24/7 server applications. Over seven years leading development teams up to 35 developers in a co-founded small venture, one of the world’s largest software development corporations, a Kleiner Perkins start-up and a technology firm in NYC.

Integrally involved in every phase and aspect of the system development life-cycle, primarily in technical, but also in business arenas.

On the technical side, designed and implemented components within distributed online applications serving millions of customers. Was particularly involved in the areas of security, authentication and authorization for personal data and highly liable financial applications. Designed version one large scalable distributed systems and front-end user-interfaces. Coordinated cross-team initiatives involving hundreds of developers and affecting thousands of internal customers. Lead ground-up refactoring of complex enterprise-size solutions.

On the business side, sought project sponsors after defining project goals, scope, and team makeup, developed project plans and timelines, overseen scheduling, task tracking and release management. Interviewed and hired developers, reviewed their performance and managed their career paths. Currently managing a team of 35, including 4 managers.

Daniel Doubrovkine’s Specialties:

Expert knowledge of a broad scope of programming languages. Twelve years of C++, in particular server-side 24/7 apps. Six years of t-SQL in a distributed and replicated environment. Seven years of enterprise scale .NET (C#). Three years of commercial x86 assembly, some knowledge of MIPS and IBM processors. Four years of Object Pascal (Delphi) client-side development. Extensive experience of C and C++ programming on Windows and cross-platform Unix systems (Solaris, SunOS, FreeBSD, Linux, etc.).
