Linda Morton’s Summary

Linda P. Morton spend 33 years in higher education, practicing and teaching primarily in public relations.
• 19 years in full-time teaching,
• 5 years in full-time administration (two with adjunct teaching assignments),
• 8 years with dual assignments in administration and teaching.
All but three years of her administration experience were in public relations. The remaining three was as coordinator of an extension office.

Her higher education experience has included:
• managing budgets and personnel,
• raising funds,
• leading faculty through professional development activities,
• participating in strategic planning and evaluation,
• maintaining beneficial relationships with media, civic organizations, and other units/administrators across campus,
• working on two ACEJMC accreditation self-studies and participating in site visits,
• serving and chairing numerous committees at unit through university levels, and
• performing a full-range of public relations activities.

She has a national reputation for her research and was recently ranked the fifth most published public relations researcher. Her scholarship includes:
• 1 textbook
• 2 instructors' manuals
• 20 refereed articles
• 11 refereed presentations
• 50 professional articles
• 39 professional presentations
• 6 professional workshops


Her experience outside of higher education includes:
• a year as publisher and advertising manager for a community newspaper,
• a year as administrator of gift and grant programs for a charitable organization (volunteer),
• and two public relations positions.

Linda Morton’s Specialties:

writing news releases, conducting special events, designing and producing publications, designing and administering advertising campaigns, designing and administering public relations campaigns, conducting public relations and marketing research.
