Kathryn Whitehead’s Summary

The Community Alliance for the Ethical Treatment of Youth (CAFETY), is a youth driven, grassroots organization dedicated to the protection of the human rights of youth placed in residential programs. Charles King and I co-founded CAFETY in 2006 after we both experienced dehumanizing care at a residential program and recognized that we were but a few among many. We worked to create a centralized network of survivors, seeking to empower and to create substantive cultural, policy and legislative change, for the purpose of increasing youth protection, ensuring accountability and increasing transparency of residential programs. I serve as the Executive Director. My responsibilities include fundraising, program development, managing staff, presenting at conferences, and speaking to the press.

I will be attending Law School in the Fall of 2009.
