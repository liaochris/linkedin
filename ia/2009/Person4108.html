Architecture is a key component to support and enable the other value levers in the DCT (Digital and Communication Technology) Strategy. Planning for architecture implementation in 2006 including development of a Service Catalogue, Architecture Blueprint and Architecture Roadmap and review of principles.

  • Enterprise Architecture Consultant

    Shell - E&P

    (Public Company; Oil & Energy industry)

    April 2004 — September 2005 (1 year 6 months)

    Supporting the development of both a short-term (2 years) Enterprise Architectures and a long-term (5-10 years) target Enterprise Architecture vision for the Smart Fields programme. In the short-term the architecture is required to support the integration of automation/control systems, real-time production monitoring, data historians, and production systems modelling applications (including reservoir, well, and production facility models). In the longer term the architecture is aiming towards a service oriented architecture approach to integration with enterprise application integration and enterprise information integration tools.

  • Enterprise Architecture Consultant

    European Central bank

    (Government Agency; Banking industry)

    January 2004 — March 2005 (1 year 3 months)

    Developing an Enterprise Architecture framework based on the best industry practices, TOGAF, Zachman Framework, FEAF, COBIT, ITIL and Archimate. The Enterprise Architecture Framework is designed with six main Architectural Models representing the Information, Process, Organisation, Application, Technology and Performance perspectives of the organisation, structured into several layers of abstraction, with multiple dimensions and views. The Enterprise Architecture Framework also includes an integrated process framework based on integrating the best practice processes such as the TOGAF ADM (Architecture Development), COBIT (Governance and Control), RUP and Select Perspective (System Development), ITIL (Service Management).
    The Enterprise Architecture content is being developed from the top down, with the establishment of an Architecture Review Board, Architecture Principles, Governance and Compliance Processes, a Product Standardisation Process.

    IBM

    (Public Company; 10,001 or more employees; Banking industry)

    January 1995 — December 1997 (3 years)

    Providing consultancy and developed the Financial Services Object Model as part of IBM’s Enterprise Architecture Framework called the Information Framework (IFW). Presentation of IBM’s IFW enterprise architecture strategy to customers across Europe, Middle-East and Africa conducting workshops and education sessions leading to successful sales. Performing assignments with IBM customers to help define their IT Strategy, plan their migration to object oriented development and customize the Financial Services Object Model, Financial Services Data Model and Financial Services Workflow Model for their needs. Wrote several papers that contributed to the further development of the IFW, with special emphasis to the object modelling of Financial Products and Securities. Contributed to the development of an Insurance Object Model based on IFW.
