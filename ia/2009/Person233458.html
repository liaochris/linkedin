Tim Bratton’s Summary

I started my first high tech company in 1990 and have never looked back.

Media Vision was my first start up. I wrote the business plan while still getting my MBA at Santa Clara University and quit my job at National Semiconductor to be employee #1 in July 1990. We did $7 million in sales in 1991, $70 million in 1992, and did over $200 million in 1993. The company went public in 1992.

In 1994 I started the Silicon Valley office of Spatializer Audio Labs. As VP of Sales and Marketing, I had P&L responsibiligy and licensed the company's 3-D audio technology to over 30 OEM such as Panasonic, Sharp, Hitachi, Toshiba, Compaq, and HP. While at Spatializer I grew the company's sales to $6 million, growing the company's stock price by 500% and resulting in a public market valuation of $100 million.

In 1997 I began working on a new technology for delivering music on the Internet, with the idea of creating a celestial jukebox where you could get any song you wanted any time you wanted it. This idea eventually turned into the Rhapsody music service. After raising $6 million in venture capital, I sold the company to Listen.com for $25 million in 2001. I stayed on with Listen to develop Rhasody to it's full potential and helped the Listen.com team sell the company to Real Networks in 2003 for $35 million.

Personal Life Media is my latest high tech start-up. We started in 2006 and the company is growing rapidly. By promoting the principles of conscious business, eco-friendly corporate practices, health and wellness, personal growth, we are authentically bringing helpful information to the world!
