David Kusumoto’s Summary

** WHO? -- I'm a friendly but brutally honest "inward-facing" (internal) and "outward-facing" (external) communications expert specializing in mainstream and guerilla publicity. I've had just 3 career jobs before becoming a broad-spectrum communications pro - (I DON'T job-hop) - 1st as a news director/editor/reporter - 2nd as a Fortune 500 rep handling public, media, investor and community relations - and 3rd as a natl. communications strategist. If you disproportionately REACT to queries (waiting for things to happen) - instead of being aggressively PROACTIVE, e.g., pushing big-picture ideas to stakeholders - you AREN'T practicing blue-chip PR. Consultations are free with fees starting as low as $100.

** RESULTS -- My conventional (traditional media) and guerilla (social media) tactics - lead to stakeholder buy-in of disparate goals, e.g., acceptance by employees, with placements in the WSJ, NY Times, Barron's and many local papers and TV stations.

** IDEOLOGIES - P.R. MUST BE INTEGRATED into your business plans - otherwise it's just "overhead." Pitch themes, not clichés to internal & external stakeholders; frame everything as addressing/resolving a problem or conflict; stakeholder "buy-in" is a daily competition, requiring you to be fearless to debate the benefits of your services/products to increase market share/revenues/funding and political and industry prestige.

** SAMPLE TECHNIQUES -- "Issues du jour wedges" enable you to repeatedly comment as an expert in a primary or secondary position. This is a long-term way to stay visible and credible - vs. a one-shot profile. My tactics to place you in the best light - are based on being on the end of many dumb PR pitches when I was in the news biz. I spin themes with conflict or human interest too compelling to ignore, I demand minimum standards before stakeholders are hit with ANY outreach effort.
