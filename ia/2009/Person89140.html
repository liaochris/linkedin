Grayson Steding’s Summary

My education at Purdue through Electrical Engineering Technology (EET) has focused primarily on practical knowledge as opposed to theoretical. Thus, I have gained a lot of hands on experience designing circuits with programs such as MultiSim and then testing them in a lab. I have also been studying in Aeronautical Engineering Technology (AET) which takes a similar approach to EET. Topics for AET include aircraft physics, material science, various aircraft systems (avionics, hydraulics, pneumatics, etc.), and piston and turbine power plants.

I plan to specialize in electrical power generation and process controls within the EET program. Through the AET program I will obtain my Airframe & Powerplant Mechanic's license (A&P).

I have also been pursuing various ratings as a pilot. I am currently an Instrument rated Private pilot in Single Engine aircraft and am working on a Commercial rating. I currently have 240 total flight hours in various high-wing, low-wing, and glider aircraft with 30 hours in glass cockpits.

During the summer of 2008 I worked for U.S. Aviation Group in Denton, TX, a medium sized flight school that is quickly on the rise. I worked in their avionics department as an Intern, but was delegated the responsibilities on a full-time technician and even an Avionics Manager on a regular basis. I gained valuable knowledge on avionics systems, installations, and troubleshooting in the aircraft and on the bench for small general aviation aircraft.

In the future I'd like to do something with Flight Test Engineering and possibly even become a Test Pilot. For the near future, I plan to continue working with Avionics and Electronics. Once I have my Commercial rating, I plan to fly for hire and eventually become a flight instructor.
