Michael Fröhlich’s Summary

Michael is an Attorney-at-Law (registered with the Munich bar). He has a Ph.D. in Copyright and was educated at the Universität des Saarlandes (1st German State Examination), the Université de Montpellier (Diplôme d’Etudes Approfondies) and the Kammergericht Berlin (2nd German State Examination).

Michael has practised as an Attorney-at-Law for several years at the Düsseldorf office of Preu Bohlig & Partner in the fields of contentious and pre-contentious matters in all areas of Intellectual Property Rights, with a particular focus on patents. In this function he has represented clients from technology-driven industries and acquired significant experience in domestic and cross-border patent infringement litigation. Michael shows also work experience in Brazil, where he was active for Dannemann Siemsen Bigler & Ipanema Moreira.

Michael further acted as the Legal Affairs Director at the European Telecommunications Standards Institute (ETSI). In this role he has been counselling the Board, the General Assembly, the Director-General as well as other senior executives of the ETSI Secretariat on legal issues with a main emphasis on IPRs and Standardisation. He has actively participated and contributed to the work of the ETSI IPR Review ad hoc group and the standing ETSI IPR Special Committee. He is the author of prominent changes in the ETSI Directives, such as the implementation of the mechanism of ex-ante disclosure of licensing terms, the ETSI Antitrust Guidelines or the review of the procedure of Clause 8 of the ETSI IPR Policy.

Prior to joining Research In Motion, he was in charge of defining, driving and representing Nokia's IPR position for various wireless technology standardization activities. He has also been involved in licensing negotiations and patent infringement proceedings.

Michael has written and presented on issues at the intersection of Intellectual Property Law, Antitrust and Standardisation in Europe, the United States, China and Japan.

Michael Fröhlich’s Specialties:

Intellectual Property Rights, with a particular focus on patents and copyright; Competition Law; Standardization
