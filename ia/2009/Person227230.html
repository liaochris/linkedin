Susan D. Strayer’s Summary

OVERVIEW: Susan is a talent acquisition executive and career guru with 10+ years of corporate experience and an MBA, MA and BA. Her latest book is The Right Job, Right Now and she's an expert in social networking and tools for recruiting, leadership and career development.

DETAILS: Susan is the Corporate Director of Talent Acquisition for The Ritz-Carlton Hotel Co. In this role, she is responsible for the global deployment of talent acquisition strategies, processes and tools. Previously, she served as Director of Talent Management working on a number of talent, performance and leadership initiatives. Prior to The Ritz-Carlton, Susan founded University & Career Decisions working with individuals, companies and universities in career management and recruiting strategy. She is the author of "The Right Job, Right Now" (St. Martin's, 2007).

Her experience also includes working in Corporate Staffing for The Home Depot and as Director for the Corporate Executive Board (CEB) serving Fortune 1000 executives in research programs focusing on HR, recruiting, training and learning. Prior to working for CEB, Susan worked in corporate HR for Arthur Andersen.

Susan has an MBA from Vanderbilt, an MA in Human Resource Development from The George Washington University and a BA in Communication from Virginia Tech.

She is certified as a Senior Professional in Human Resources (SPHR), and as an administrator of the Meyers-Briggs Type Indicator (MBTI) and has spoken for hundreds of conferences and organizations including Deloitte, Harvard University and Oprah and Friends radio. Her expertise has been quoted globally: www.susanstrayer.com.

Susan D. Strayer’s Specialties:

Business Strategy: Employment Branding, Performance Management, Recruiting Strategy, Full Life-Cycle Recruiting, Human Resources Communication, Employee Engagement and Retention and Career Development Programming.

Individual coaching and consulting: Career Transition, Job Search Strategies, Career Growth and Development; Public speaking on career development and management for Gen X & Millenials (Gen Y)
