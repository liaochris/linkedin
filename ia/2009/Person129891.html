Ken Homer’s Summary

The Purpose of Collaborative Conversations is to include more voices - even if there are dozens or hundreds of people involved - in the conversations needed to create a mutually desired future, and to make better decisions as a result of the broadened participation.

The Value of Collaborative Conversations is that people can:
~ Arrive at a clear idea of what is important to them regarding specific issues.
~ Explore what is possible based on the constraints they are operating under.
~ Determine and coordinate effective actions that produce desired outcomes.
~ Critically reflect and learn in order to improve their future conversations.

The Results of Collaborative Conversations include:
~ Higher levels of productivity.
~ Less wasted time.
~ Increased sense of ownership and accountability for results produced.
~ Smoother functioning in and among teams and workgroups.
~ Serious fun - the work is enlivening even when it is challenging.

    (Management Consulting industry)

    September 2007 — Present (1 year 7 months)

    I provide coaching and consulting services to both individuals and organizations interested in collaboration and creating environments where people can thrive. My work focuses on building the competence to design and convene conversations that include all relevant voices, address complex issues and make wiser decisions that enjoy widespread support.

    My client collaborations draw from a wide variety of traditions - both ancient and modern - and blend together elements of Appreciative Inquiry, Future Search, Integral Coaching, Open Space Technology, Positive Psychology, Theory U and World Café. Poetry, mythology, storytelling, art, movement, somatic awareness and indigenous wisdom are also regular features in my client partnerships.

  • Director of Community Outreach

    The World Cafe Community Foundation

    (Non-Profit; 1-10 employees; Management Consulting industry)

    June 1997 — February 2007 (9 years 9 months)

    The World Cafe is a dialogue process that can be used in groups ranging in size from a dozen to many hundreds. Properly applied, it can make accessible the knowledge and wisdom of the entire group.

    During the decade I worked with the World Café, it grew from an unknown practice among a few dozen people to a worldwide community of dialogue practitioners numbering in the tens of thousands. I contributed in many ways to the development the Café process itself, as well as the growth of the practitioner community.
