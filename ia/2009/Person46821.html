Clare Rayner’s Summary

I am the Founder and Managing Director of Retail Acumen and The Retail Conference. With over 20 year's practical retail experience I am considered to be a leading authority on retail commercial planning, from range and space to forecasting, replenishment and supply chain management.

Passionate about retailing, my experience spans fast food to fashion, from supermarkets to consumer electronics and general merchandise. My career has afforded me roles across the most commercial and operational functions in retail, including store / operations management, merchandising, supply chain and category management. This comprehensive experience lead to roles in projects, change management, solution selection and implementation, and most recently, management consulting.

Those who know me regard me as an ambitious, confident, highly motivated and innovative thought leader who thrives on challenge and variety. I am known for my considerable passion, energy and enthusiasm. The work value that is of most greatest importance to me is reputation, my own and that of my businesses. To that end I am dedicated to ensuring that all of my relationships with clients, suppliers, colleagues and with my wider network are based on honesty, openness, courage to speak out and authenticity - and that these relationships add value.

I set and expect high standards from my team, creating a high performance culture among those I work with, initiating and driving forward new ideas and change, engaging key stakeholders and creating a sense of excitement about the future.

I am regarded as an excellent communicator with a very open and direct style, which ensures that those I work with have complete clarity and can trust what I tell them. Whilst they might not necessarily like what they hear, I have found that this open, honest approach achieves greater trust - and in my experience trust is fundamental to gaining the buy in that leads to the delivery of exceptional results.

  • Retail Acumen: Managing Consultant

    Woolworths

    (Public Company; WOW; Retail industry)

    October 2006 — January 2008 (1 year 4 months)

    Retail Acumen were engaged by Woolworths as the leading authority on retail commercial planning, specifically merchandise planning, demand forecasting and replenishment.

    The objectives of the assignment were:

    1. To understand the current state of the commercial supply chain, including causal analysis to identify the most likely contributors to low in store availability.

    2. To evaluate an end-state supply chain that utilised sku-store-day data to create an accurate demand forecast, driving out a bottom-up sales and stock flow plan (WSSI). Assessing the change requirements to achieve that end state. Determining if a viable business case existed for the change.

    What we discovered was that whilst process change could drive benefits, underlying systems constraints would prevent delivery of full benefits unless replaced or improved.

    As a result Retail Acumen supported Woolworths in the delivery of a vendor selection exercise for systems to support the commercial supply chain aims, providing all content for the functional assessment. This included an RFI, an extensive ITT and a series of scored presentations by shortlisted vendors.
