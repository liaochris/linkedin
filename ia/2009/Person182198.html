Patrick Power’s Summary

I have spent the better part of my professional life involved with photography and the graphic arts. In the past two years, I've become devoted to my own photography... particularly as an art form; as a way of documenting my life; as a way of communicating the paths my eyes take from day to day.

My professional goals are simple... I want to be able to use my "personal vision" to create photographs for others.

Along with photography, I also help to coordinate the Great Lakes Folk Festival in East Lansing, Michigan (this year from August 7 through 9). I have been active in the Lansing-area folk music community since about 1994.

Patrick Power’s Specialties:

My personal photography can often be quirky. I like spending time with a subject (whether a person or something inanimate) in order to discover its appeal. I have come to prefer working in natural light, although I am also adept at handling my lighting gear. I am particularly drawn to portraiture but very much enjoy photographing events, products and places. I like discovering the not-so-obvious aspects of whatever I might shoot.

  • Music Coordinator, Great Lakes Folk Festival

    Michigan State University Museum

    (Photography industry)

    2002 — Present (7 years )

    The Great Lakes Folk Festival showcases the traditional cultural treasures of the nation's Upper Midwest and a sampling of the best of traditional artists from around the country and the world.

    The festival encourages cross-cultural understanding of our diverse society through the presentation of musicians, dancers, cooks, storytellers and craftspeople whose traditions are rooted in their communities.

    As Music Coordinator since the festival's inception, I select the music for the festival with the help of a local advisory panel, as well as advice of folklorists, ethnomusicologists and other musical experts from around the country. I negotiate contracts, schedule the music stages and serve as the primary liaison between the festival and musical artists.

  • Booking Manager

    Lansing Area Folksong Society / Ten Pound Fiddle Coffeehouse

    (Non-Profit; 11-50 employees; Music industry)

    January 1996 — August 2005 (9 years 8 months)

    For nearly ten years, I scheduled and booked the Ten Pound Fiddle Coffeehouse concert series and served on the executive board of the parent organization, the Lansing Area Folksong Society.

    Because the "The Fiddle" is an all-volunteer organization which has no physical home, booking shows also meant deciding upon and contracting venues (in addition to the performers) for approximately thirty concerts a year. In addition to the booking, I would often serve as master of ceremonies at concerts, and help set-up sound and perform other concert-specific tasks. I also was a contributor to the series' publicity efforts, regularly contacting the media and serving as public spokesperson. For most of my tenure, I maintained the organization's website.

  • Supervisor, Photographic Services

    Michigan State University Instructional Media Center

    (Educational Institution; 11-50 employees; Photography industry)

    May 1984 — August 1994 (10 years 4 months)

    I scheduled and supervised as many as ten full-time employees and five student employees in a full-service photographic unit.

    Services provided were: film processing (b/w, E-6, C-41); b/w and colour printing; special-effects slide production; slide duplication; copy photography; graphic arts photography; studio and on-location photography. As a working supervisor, I also was responsible for any and all types of lab work and photographic assignments, and was primary customer service representative and quality control agent.
