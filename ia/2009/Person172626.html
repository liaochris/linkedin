Ned Desmond’s Summary

I started in journalism at local Massachusetts newspapers: The Cape Cod Times and The Middlesex News. In 1984 I joined Time magazine; in 1988 became bureau chief New Delhi and in 1992 moved to Tokyo. During those years I also wrote for National Review, The New York Review of Books, and Foreign Affairs.

In 1996, I moved to Silicon Valley, where I joined Infoseek and founded the Infoseek subsidiary in Japan in a JV with Joichi Ito's Digital Garage.
I returned briefly to writing at Fortune magazine before going back
to Infoseek/Go.com

In 2000, I returned to Time Inc. to start a new monthly business
magazine and web site in San Francisco called eCompany Now, which later acquired and merged with Business 2.0.

In 2003 I shifted to New York to run Time Inc. Interactive (TII), a group at Time Inc. charged with corporate digital strategy. TII's role was to oversee a
deal that assigned most of Time Inc.'s Web assets to AOL.

In 2006 Time Inc. dropped the AOL partnership and decided to invest heavily in its online properties. TII took the leading role, running Web technology as well as product development, user interaction and visual design, audience development (seo, sem, distribution), ad operations, mobile products, and staffing.

By the end of 2007, the combined audiences of Time Inc. sites reached more than 26 million consumers (Nielsen) and Time Inc. was consistently among the top twenty online media companies.

At the end of 2008, Time Inc. decided to shift most of TII's responsibilities to three new operating groups. As Ann Moore (Time Inc.'s CEO) noted in a 10/30 memo: "Ned and his TII team succeeded so well that, well, there’s not much revolutionizing left to do. The time has arrived to move all the digital responsibility to the new teams in our new Business Units, where, to no surprise, many of the key leaders are folks Ned brought into the company." At this crossroad, I decided to leave the company and look to new frontiers.

Ned Desmond’s Specialties:

Creating new media and service businesses online. Online product development and delivery. Audience development. Inventing online complements to print properties. Digital staff recruitment and organization building. Angel investing.
