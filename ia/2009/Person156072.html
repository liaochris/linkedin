Matthew Rock’s Summary

I'm an experienced business journalist and co-founder of Caspian Publishing. Caspian is a B2B publisher and event organiser. These days, my official role is group editor & communications director.

Some of Caspian's main titles include:

* Real Business (award-winning UK magazine for entrepreneurs).
* Real Deals (pan-European private equity magazine; again, won lots of awards).
* Business Voice (official CBI magazine).

Between 1997 and 2003, I was a founder and editor of the entrepreneurs' magazine Real Business.

As part of the founding team of Caspian, I've had to practise what I preach about entrepreneurship. The business was launched in 1997 and today has a turnover of £10m and employs 65 people. It's backed by private equity firm LDC.

My specialist subject, unsurprisingly, is entrepreneurship and the issues that face SMEs (small and mid-sized businesses). I also have a keen interest in social enterprise and the emergence of Asian economies.

I've done tonnes of media and conference work, on Newsnight, 5Live, CNN and in the FT, Wall Street Journal etc. I chair the "Inspiring Entrepreneurs" series at the British Library, and have interviewed many top business leaders – Sir Richard Branson on a train journey with the Brand New Heavies (weird); 6am purple power drinks with Microsoft boss Steve Ballmer (scary). I reckon I've probably met and interviewed more entrepreneurs than any other British journalist.

Alongside journalism, I've also developed, judged, moderated and spoken at countless business conferences and awards ceremonies - such as the Asian Women of Achievement awards and Growing Business Awards (held with the CBI).

  • Editor, Real Business

    Caspian Publishing

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Publishing industry)

    1998 — 2003 (5 years)

    I was part of the launch team of Real Business in 1997, editing the title for more than five years. Until Real Business, business magazines were unreadable, stuffed full of captains of industry. Real Business played a big part in making the business press more exciting, reader-focused and user-friendly.

    The magazine seemed to touch a nerve with its entrepreneur-readers, such as Edward Perry, who runs a great little retail business called Cook. Edward said (unsolicited): “I can honestly say that part of our small but growing success is directly attributable to ideas that we have acted upon after reading articles in the magazine. Real Business is inspiring, practical and thoroughly entertaining”.
