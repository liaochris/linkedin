Tony Bowden’s Summary

I have been envisaging and delivering internet based products since 1991, and was building professional web sites since before Netscape existed. With a strong background in rapid development and Free Software, I am able to provide significant business advantage quickly and cheaply. My mix of commercial and technical skills means I am capable of operating at numerous different levels within an organisation, and of identifying inefficient processes and methods that can be cheaply augmented with simple technology improvements.

Although I am still capable of creating something from nothing, my primary interest is in improvement through a million small steps, with a focus on maintainability, scalability, automation, testing, abstraction, modularity, reusability, deployment and development practices etc., with a particular focus on long-term development productivity.

Tony Bowden’s Specialties:

Making things better, envisaging change, product conception, product management, process improvement, tactics-led strategy, managing exponential growth, contrarian thought, incremental improvement, whipuptitude, getting things done, doing more with less, databases, spreadsheets, software maintenance and deployment, automated testing, Perl, Ruby, SQL, XP/Agile methods, refactoring, LAMP.

  • Product Manager / Developer

    Socialtext

    (Privately Held; Computer Software industry)

    February 2007 — November 2007 (10 months)

    Socialtext had bought Dan Bricklin's wikiCalc product (which, despite the name, was primarily a tool for constructing table layouts in blog posts) over a year earlier, but had not yet given any significant attention to what to do with it. I was brought into to bring the product to the next level. With a team consisting of just one existing Socialtext programmer, Dan (on a part-time basis), and myself, we hit our first quarter's objective (getting wikiCalc functional inside the existing Socialtext product) within two weeks. I then spent considerable time working out what a true spreadsheet wiki could and should be (see http://nothing.tmtm.com/archives/3571).

    I was also involved in the creation of the Common Public Attribution License (CPAL), now approved by both OSI and FSF, and led the partitioning and relicensing of SocialCalc under both CPAL and the Artistic License 2.0, in an attempt to make the core engine Spreadsheet Engine usable in other open source projects (e.g. a Drupal plugin). I subsequently also released the core engine to CPAN, significantly refactoring much of it, and adding comprehensive test coverage.

  • Turnerarounder

    UNITE Solutions

    (Privately Held; Staffing and Recruiting industry)

    May 2004 — February 2007 (2 years 10 months)

    Took over the running of Ireland's oldest ISP when it was making losses of approximately 50% of turnover, and spent almost three years hands-on turning it around and bringing it to consistent profitability for the first time in its ten-year history.

    This involved significant work reducing costs by more than half, largely through the automatisation of almost all business processes and the creation of a fully-integrated customer management, billing, credit control, and accounting system allowing for remote management and fully transparent real-time operational and financial reporting.

  • Founder / Product Visionary / Lead Developer

    Twingle

    (Internet industry)

    November 2002 — May 2004 (1 year 7 months)

    In the days before Gmail, historic email was a black hole. A search in Outlook took 15 minutes, and if you didn't know the exact search term, you'd find nothing. As a small five-person team we envisaged, developed, and shipped a web-based system that made it easy to find any information buried within your email.

    Despite glowing feedback from all our early testers, and a good write-up in Business 2.0, we were unable to gain sufficient traction to withstand the onslaught of Gmail when it launched, even though we did much more (see http://blog.simon-cozens.org/post/view/527)

  • Founder / COO / CTO

    BlackStar.co.uk

    (Internet industry)

    March 1998 — June 2001 (3 years 4 months)

    Founder of one of Europe's longest lived ecommerce ventures. Grew from zero to £10m turnover in 3 years, with two rounds of VC funding, and one of the lowest technology spends in the industry.

    Most of the infrastructure was built in-house on a LAMP platform, enabling us to provide a completely integrated service, and deliver on our key objective of fanatically great customer care. Through our combination of surprisingly fast delivery and response times (target: 90% shipped in 24h, 90% email response in 4 hours), we maintained the highest customer retention rate in our field and won numerous awards including the BVA's “Online Retail Success of the Year” and Trade International’s “Online Exporter of the Year”.

    I grew from being the sole developer, building the initial version of the site myself in 10 days, right up to managing all day-to-day operations (including site development, customer care, logistics, and MIS) with almost 150 staff at three locations.
