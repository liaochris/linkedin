Steven Romalewski’s Summary

My expertise lies in the use of geographic information systems (GIS) to understand, visualize, and analyze data sets for a variety of urban planning issues including demographic change, land use trends, social service availability, educational needs, public health, and environmental quality.
Through the CUNY Mapping Service, I engage directly in projects to provide maps and spatial analysis, as well as online interactive mapping applications that leverage open source and proprietary technologies.

Skill sets:
• Firm grasp of evolving Web 2.0 techniques for online mapping.
• High level of proficiency with ESRI’s desktop and server product line (through 9.3).
• Facility with other GIS applications (MapInfo and MapBasic), database software (SQL Server 2005), and statistical packages (SPSS).
• Advanced cartography, extensive geocoding experience, and basic spatial analysis techniques.
• Familiarity with web design/architecture, Microsoft Visual Studio, IIS, and Windows Server management.
