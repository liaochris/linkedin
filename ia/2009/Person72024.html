Ellen Jong’s Summary

I've been shooting since 1997. I started working with a production company, nobody creative, doing in-house print and video production and design for packaging, promotional and marketing materials. Between 1999 and 2002, I directed and produced EPK's, CD covers, catalogs and special events. In 2002, I curated an 18 artist designed hotel room installation at The Creek South Beach for a soft opening during Art Basel Miami Beach- artists include Shepard Fairey, Justin Luke, Jose Parla and Oliver Lutz. It was the most challenging and rewarding project I had contributed to which attracted a new school of creatives to Miami Beach for Art Basel in 2002 and following fairs. The entire property was being renovated- lobby, pool deck, pool, landscaping and paint job- while I managed the artists rooms and installation. I started shooting again in 2003 for Seventeen, CosmoGirl and Nylon magazines. As well, my exhibition work from my first monograph, Pees On Earth, surfaced in a variety of group shows in NY, Miami and in March 2009, in Nantes France for The Vice Photo Book group exhibition. While looking for interesting ways to show my own work, I seek opportunities to work with other artists. In 2004 I worked with BlackBook Magazine to produce a show for John Arsenault by Volkswagon for Art Basel Miami at the Marlin Hotel and again in 2005 in NYC for an event with Dos Equis celebrating Cinco de Mayo as a curator of 5 contemporary artists of Mexican decent. Open Eat is a roving concept space which debuted at Photo NY in 2005, and presented itself as a booth. It is an independent, collaborative and ongoing venture to seek and create platforms to present new works of artists who work in various media and themes, worldwide.
