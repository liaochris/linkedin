Mike Concannon’s Experience

  • Vice President Marketing

    Schering Plough

    (Consumer Goods industry)

    December 2003 — May 2008 (4 years 6 months)

    Senior marketing executive responsible for Schering Plough's consumer skincare business. Led the development and execution of a multi-faceted and quick turnaround of the Coppertone franchise, posting the best financial results in the brand’s 63 year history in 2005, 2006 and 2007. Established a growing rate of financial return, growing net sales $91MM, +73% and EBIT $38MM from 1% to 19%, while holding advertising and promotion spending flat. Responsibilities: Full P&L responsibility, including all key elements of the marketing and business mix: talent identification, people development, strategy, product development, creative development, public relations, media, retailer relationships, business development, multi-year business planning.

  • Vice President of Marketing

    InterCure Inc.

    (Medical Devices industry)

    November 2001 — December 2003 (2 years 2 months)

    Recruited from Drugstore.com to develop business strategy, lead marketing and raise capital for a medical device startup company, pioneering the application of drug-free respiratory treatment for cardiovascular and respiratory disease. Developed consumer and medical marketing strategy and led all marketing initiatives. Developed the business plan and participated in fund-raising. Reported to CEO.

  • Vice President of Merchandising

    Beauty.com

    (Internet industry)

    June 2000 — November 2001 (1 year 6 months)

    Beauty.com is a wholly-owned subsidiary of Drugstore.com. Simultaneous to my role at Drugstore.com, also led Beauty.com prestige cosmetic and skincare business, responsible for marketing and merchandising strategy, product assortment, negotiating product distribution agreements, purchasing, site design and features, merchandising, pricing, profitability, public relations and promotion. Also responsible for managing marketing relationship with renowned make-up artist Kevyn Aucoin.

  • Vice President of Merchandising

    Drugstore.com

    (Public Company; 501-1000 employees; dscm; Internet industry)

    November 1998 — October 2001 (3 years)

    Recruited from Johnson & Johnson during the early startup phase of the company (15th employee). Key member of executive team that created the leading e-commerce site for health, beauty and prescription products (2007 sales: $450MM). Responsible for 100% of company revenue ($146MM in 2001) and product margin for drugstore.com and subsidiary beauty.com. Led a 35-person team, accountable for all Rx and OTC product categories. Developed strategy for product revenue and margin, product marketing, customer conversion, category management, Web store operation and supplier relations. Member of Executive Management Board, reported to CEO.

  • Vice President of Sales

    Johnson & Johnson

    (Consumer Goods industry)

    October 1991 — October 1998 (7 years 1 month)

    Senior sales executive responsible for a national sales organization, generating $650MM in sales, with three major consumer product franchises. In a turnaround situation, responsible for channel marketing, category management, trade marketing, retailer relationships, a $70MM channel marketing budget and a $32MM operations budget. Positioned sales organization for long-term success, which contributed to the achievement of the first net profit in over 10 years, in 1998. Member of Executive Management Board, reported to President.
