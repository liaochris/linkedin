Mark Trudeau’s Summary

As Lou Holtz says, "If what you did yesterday looks big, then you haven't done much today!" Following this philosophy for 20 years is what brought me to an exceptional Medical Device manufacturing company.

I am always interested in discussing Medical Device processes and technology. Also, if you are someone who would be interested in adding us to your Preferred Supplier base, contact me, we're always looking for good customers!

Mark Trudeau’s Specialties:

Medical Devices and Validations (DQ IQ OQ PQ), Injection Molding, Statistics, Six Sigma, Scientific Molding, Process Optimization, Getting Things Done! Personally passionate about finding and supporting talented people who make an organization work.

Mark Trudeau’s Experience

  • Director of Operations

    Empire Precision Plastics

    (Privately Held; Plastics industry)

    July 2009 — Present (7 months)

    Director of Operations includes responsibility for direction in the following: Project Management (Project Engineering Group, New tools and parts, APQP, PPAP), Scientific Molding Principles (Process Engineering Group, Process Optimization), Medical Device Validation (DQ/IQ/OQ/PQ, New Equipment, New and existing tooling, New and existing Parts), Statistical Methods (Design of Experiments, Process Optimization, Capability), Process Monitoring and Control (Machinery, Auxiliary Equipment), and Six Sigma Lean Manufacturing and Methodology (Infrastructure, Engineering, Quality Group).

    Training in all these areas is also a core competency of Empire, not only for our own employees, but Customers and Suppliers who are interested in learning these types of disciplines. For example, Medical device customers and suppliers show a high interest in Medical Device Validation (DQ/IQ/OQ/PQ), one of Empire's strongest attributes, and we enjoy visiting them and sharing our training and thoughts on the subject.

    Joined the Empire Precision team due to both a career and personal goal of working with a well established (1992) and exceptional medical device manufacturer, and delivering not only cutting edge medical devices for improving lives, but to do this while helping our customers keep quality high and costs low. With company growth in medical manufacturing as a key goal, I am part of a very strong team whose responsibility is to drive exceptional quality, optimization, and certification into all aspects of the company (Empire's Tool Build facility, and Injection Molding Part Manufacturing facility), under the umbrella of cGMP, FDA, and ISO 13485 Medical certification. Over twenty years of experience and tools will now be used to simply add to the already established system, only to help drive continuous improvement even further.

  • VP Quality

    Val Tech

    (Plastics industry)

    May 2004 — July 2009 (5 years 3 months)

    As the companies only Black Belt, Statistician, Lead Auditor, and ASQ Certified employee, I was responsible for driving improvement by developing metrics, training, RoHS, Process Engineering, Lean Six Sigma, Process Monitoring, DMAIC, Customer Satisfaction, Corrective/Preventive Action (CAPA), APQP, PPAP, On-Time Delivery, Statistical Analysis, Designed Experiments, and all the standard Quality related duties. The company was a start-up (2003) which eventually merged 4 existing companies, and was in need of a standardized approach. Had 8 direct reports, and oversaw the Quality Lab, which included Supplier certification, Receiving inspection, In-Process Inspection, Final Product Inspection, Calibration system, and all procedures associated.

    Significant Accomplishment: Brought together four companies into one Quality Management System (resulting in ISO 9001:2000, TS 16949 Automotive registration, ISO 13485 Medical registration), including development of new metrics for the entire company (Quality, COPQ, Costs, Cycle Time, Purchasing, Manufacturing, Shipping and Receiving, etc). Developed similar system at partner company in Indiana.

  • Worldwide Quality Manager

    (Public Company; EK; Consumer Goods industry)

    January 1999 — July 2004 (5 years 7 months)

    Tool Build and Injection Molding for Single Use Camera, and Kodak Medical (Blood Analyzer Tips that were sold to Johnson & Johnson) and Kodak Dental Products (X-ray equipment and mouth pieces). Responsibilities included evaluating current practices worldwide, and driving standardization worldwide in areas such as Six Sigma methodology, Quality, Metrology, Inspection Methods, Programming, Process Monitoring, and Statistical Methods. Product included consumer goods, cameras, printers, copiers, and medical devices. Jointly responsible for the Low Volume Outsourcing currently underway in production. Had 24 direct reports in the Quality Assurance Group and the Business Planning Group, for which my responsibilities included leading team projects, mentoring Black Belts, employee development plans, scheduled work hours (24/7), and personnel issues. Research and Development teams with Owens-Illinois, Boston Scientific, and Kodak Medical/Dental products.

    Significant Accomplishment: Developed a networked worldwide quality database used to share quality data between the Worldwide Tech Center (Rochester, NY) and the production satellites worldwide. Also developed a networked inspection system, comprised of a database (used internationally for tracking inspection orders and measuring inspection performance) and a structured filing system for completed inspection work. Responsible for standardizing 6 satellite areas for manufacturing (China, Japan, Singapore, Hungary, Mexico, U.S.) with direct reports at each site.
