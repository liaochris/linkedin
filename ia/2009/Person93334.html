Heather E. Eves’s Summary

Wildlife conservation and capacity building of wildlife professionals. 20+ year focus on African wildlife conservation, collaboration-building for conservation, information management system development and improving practice of conservation. Nearly ten years working in Africa followed by ten years partnership building based in the DC metro area for African wildlife conservation.

Heather E. Eves’s Experience

  • Adjunct Professor - Natural Resources Graduate Program National Capital Region

    Virginia Tech

    (Educational Institution; Higher Education industry)

    January 2009 — Present (7 months)

    Teaching graduate courses in wildlife management and conservation project planning, advising graduate students

  • Consultant/Advisor

    Bushmeat-free Eastern Africa Network (BEAN)

    (Higher Education industry)

    January 2008 — Present (1 year 7 months)

    Providing capacity building training in conservation project and communications design and planning (adaptive management) including field research to 8 professionals in the MENTOR [www.mentorfellowshipprogram.org] fellowship program. Program resulted in building a bushmeat network for East Africa (BEAN) and led toward a post-graduate degree for participants at the College of African Wildlife Management, Mweka, Tanzania. Training included conducting and analyzing a bushmeat field assessment, developing a professional network, formal Adaptive Management training with Foundations of Success [www.fosonline.org] using Miradi Software [www.miradi.org]. Developed communications materials (12 fact sheets) and strategy for distribution. Funding support provided by grants from World Wildlife Fund - US and Fulbright Scholar Program (Council for International Exchange of Scholars)

  • Director

    Bushmeat Crisis Task Force

    (Renewables & Environment industry)

    January 2000 — December 2008 (9 years)

    Senior officer in charge of 30+ institutional collaborative to build capacity and resources for better understanding and addressing the unsustainable overharvesting of wildlife across Africa. Partnership building, creating unique information management systems, fundraising, administration, government relations, policy development, scientific evaluation and publication, capacity building.

  • Consultant

    Conservation International

    (Non-Profit; 501-1000 employees; Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    July 1999 — December 1999 (6 months)

    Evaluation of commercial bushmeat trade on wildlife populations across West Africa in preparation for workshop and planning toward building an effort to address the bushmeat issue in the region.

  • Doctoral Student and Sangha River Network

    Yale University

    (Educational Institution; 10,001 or more employees; Higher Education industry)

    September 1994 — June 1999 (4 years 10 months)

    Fieldwork in Republic of Congo, Central Africa focused on evaluating the commercial bushmeat trade associated with villages surrounding a National Park. Worked with colleagues to develop an initial gathering of researchers and practitioners working in the Sangha River Region of Central Africa in the September 1997 international Sangha River Conference which launched a research network.

  • Research Consultant

    Wildlife Conservation Society

    (Non-Profit; 1001-5000 employees; Non-Profit Organization Management industry)

    January 1995 — June 1996 (1 year 6 months)

    Developed system to evaluate the socioeconomics of wildlife use and bushmeat trade in villages associated with the Nouabale-Ndoki National Park in Republic of Congo. Provided capacity building training for Congolese wildlife professionals for data collection, analysis, evaluation and report writing.

  • Masters Student and Fulbright Researcher

    New Mexico State University

    (Educational Institution; 1001-5000 employees; Higher Education industry)

    September 1991 — July 1994 (2 years 11 months)

    Developed monitoring system for gamebird populations in Kenya in addition to modifying gamebird hunting management system managed by government. Developed pilot gamebird management system that provided improved benefits to local communities from hunter fees and improved management of gamebird populations. Research supported by a Fulbright Research Grant and provided training and capacity building for Kenyan wildlife professional who took over long-term management of the program.

  • Education Coordinator

    William Holden Wildlife Foundation

    (Environmental Services industry)

    January 1989 — December 1990 (2 years)

    Developed and implemented wildlife education programs for Kenyan schoolchildren and adults. Managed education center operations, library, staff, and programs. Developed internship program. Identified alternative energy sources and other demonstration projects for student knowledge and experience.

  • Volunteer

    Peace Corps

    (Government Agency; 5001-10,000 employees; International Affairs industry)

    October 1986 — December 1988 (2 years 3 months)

    Education volunteer in Kenya teaching high school biology, physics, chemistry and English. Also developed a wildlife club.
