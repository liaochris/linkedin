Donal O'Connell’s Summary

I am the Managing Director of Chawton Innovation Services. My company offers coaching and training in the areas of innovation and intellectual property management.

Many face challenges developing an IP strategy and with organising their IP activities, whilst also ensuring the interfaces between their IP function and Senior Management, R&D Management, inventors (both internal and external) and Patent Agencies and IP Service Providers are working well, and putting proper quality, cost, efficiency and value add metrics in place.

My company can help resolve such challenges via coaching sessions, formal training classes and the creation of white papers addressing specific issues.

I graduated from the National Institute of Higher Education, Limerick, Ireland in 1985, with a Degree in Electronic Engineering. I have had a long career at Nokia for 21 years and have wide and varied experience in the wireless telecoms industry, having worked for periods in The Netherlands, UK, USA, Finland, and HK.

I am formerly a VP of R&D and a Director of IP at Nokia. In the US I led and managed the Nokia R&D Centre in Texas from 1997 to 2003, growing the site from scratch by recruiting and developing more than 600 engineers and designing, developing and launching numerous successful products. I was also member of the Nokia Americas Management Board.

More recently, from 2003 to 2009 I was a Director of IP at Nokia. I was a leader and manager of Nokia's Patent Creation team and during this time period I successfully managed a number of major change projects.

In recent years, I was involved in a number of special IP projects. I was a core team member of the Eco-Patent Commons initiative. I have written a book on Patent Creation called “Inside the Patent Factory”, which was published in 2008 and I have another book due out in 2010, this time on the subject of external innovation and IP.
