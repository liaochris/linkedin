Marcos Pontes’s Summary

Marcos Pontes is the first professional astronaut with single citizenship of a south hemisphere country. Selected as a mission specialist, he reported to the Johnson Space Center, in August 1998, as a member of the 17th Class of Astronauts. He launched with the Expedition-13 crew from Baikonur Cosmodrome, Kazakhstan, on March 29, 2006, aboard the Soyuz TMA 8 spacecraft, docking with the station on March 31, 2006, where he lived and worked for the next 8-days. Pontes returned to earth with the Expedition-12 crew, landing in Kazakhstan on April 8, 2006.

Pontes has 25 years of experience in the Air Force, working as a fighter pilot, test pilot, and engineer on operations, technical and management positions. He worked 21 years on flight safety and accident investigation for the military and civilian aircraft. He logged more than 2000 hours of jet and test flight in 25 different aircraft. He retired from the Air Force in 2006.

As an astronaut, Pontes worked on technical duties in the International Space Station Branch in NASA JSC for 8 years, including the Japanese Experimental Module (JEM), Multi-Element Integration and Testing, ISS Software Loads Testing, and the Centrifuge Accommodation Module.

Currently, while standing-by for training for possible future Brazilian space flights assigned by the Brazilian Space Agency (AEB), Pontes serves as Director of Space Research at the National Institute for Space and Aeronautics Development (IDEA), as President and CEO of MP Engineering, a R&D company for aerospace projects, as visiting Researcher at the Advanced Studies Institute (IEA-SC) of the University of São Paulo (USP), as Executive Coach for several companies in Brazil, as visiting Professor of Project Management courses at FANOR AREA1 University, in Salvador, Bahia, and as a Motivational Speaker.
