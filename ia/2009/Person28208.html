  • Prepress Production Artist

    Color Graphics

    (Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Printing industry)

    October 2003 — November 2004 (1 year 2 months)

    I joined "CGI" as a production artist and soon learned the company was in crisis. It had coasted since the retirement of its founder. Although he had a retirement plan, the company didn't. If it was going to live, it needed help.

    I made suggestions to that effect and was empowered to apply them into real solutions from the shop floor through upper management.

    My efforts helped CGI reduce it's required floorspace to 1/5 of it's original footprint, and Eliminate 90% of its unused infrastructure while improving it's level of service, all without purchasing equipment or software. By simplifying operations and ensuring the shop ran well, I also freed up the owner so he could pursue new business.

    On a personal level, my efforts resulted in the satisfaction of improving a business as well as recognition in the form of praise from clients, and raises and promotions from pre-press production to design and shop operations directorial duties.

  • Creative Director

    DeepOutdoors

    (Sporting Goods industry)

    September 2002 — April 2003 (8 months)

    Upstart DeepOutdoors claimed a vision of doing for SCUBA what Burton did for snowboarding: Bring the sport into the mainstream. My job was to help realize the vision through branding.

    DeepOutdoors had a great name, some ingenious products, a good logo, and a national magazine ad going for it. My accomplishments include extending that limited presence into a cohesive multi-ad campaign, set of brochures, manuals, packaging and the comprehensive company website: A recognizable cross media identity.

    I developed their icon system, named several products, executed the majority of the art, photo and creative direction, production, design, writing, illustration, and photo-styling to express the vision into a premium brand across print, web and retail channels.

    I left DeepOutdoors positioned to become the company that took SCUBA mainstream. Although it seems their vision has changed, they continue to use many of the elements I developed for them to this day.
