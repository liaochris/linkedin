Garr Godfrey’s Summary

In 1998, I co-founded a company that would eventually become GameHouse, Inc. One of the original goals was to use the web to provide a simple, casual computer gaming experience that had been lost in the big budget video game titles. The games we wanted to build could be learned in under a minute and enjoyed for a few minutes or a few hours, whatever you had time for.

For several years the company survived by taking on an endless variety of creative and engineering tasks, mostly related in some way to casual game playing on the internet. In time, an actual business grew out of the original idea, and we were able to create and sell downloadable, casual games directly to our visitors at GameHouse.com, as well as through partnerships we made with Yahoo, MSN, RealNetworks, iWin.com, Earthlink, AOL and many others. Some of the most popular early titles included "Super Collapse!" and "TextTwist." Our solitaire, jigsaw and Mah Jong games also sold very well.

In 2004, GameHouse was acquired by RealNetworks, creating a powerful combination of casual game creation and distribution though RealNetwork's "RealArcade" product. I continue to act as the studio head for GameHouse Studios, which is now a division of RealNetworks.

In the past year I have also been involved with film production. Stay tuned for details.
