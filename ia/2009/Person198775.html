Rick Wolff’s Experience

  • Owner/Author

    DutchNewYork.com

    (Internet industry)

    September 2008 — Present (5 months)

    "A multimedia Web magazine that rediscovers and celebrates New Netherland, the lost Dutch colony of greater New York and the Hudson Valley." I intend this blog to pick up where author Russell Shorto left off in his 2003 book "Island at the Center of the World." Residents of eastern New York and New Jersey should refer to this region as New Netherland as readily as the six states to the northeast refer to New England — and for the same reason, and with the same pride.
    My intention is profitability, with [1] extra-value-added advertising (beyond a mere rectangle of pixels), and [2] affiliate marketing of artisans, either local or historically appropriate, preferably both.
    Advertisers would be [1] the destinations: museums and historic sites, and the surrounding tourist economy; [2] real estate, especially of historic buildings; [3] dealers of actual antiques and makers of period reproductions.

  • Multimedia Designer

    Gannett / The Journal News

    (Public Company; 501-1000 employees; GCI; Newspapers industry)

    January 2000 — October 2008 (8 years 10 months)

    l was senior member of a pool of designers that handle the graphic aspects of any and all contacts with readers (or potential readers) of their various products. This has included outdoor advertising, vehicle signage, in-print promotion, Flash animated Web banners, and storyboards for television commercials. In a previous configuration, the shop also handled material for advertising sales, from individual sheets to the master sales kit. I advanced the adoption of Flash, interactive PDFs, use of a Wacom tablet for Photoshop work, overall graphic identity, and systematic file archiving. I was the designated "What's-this-font?" guy.

  • freelance graphic artist

    Vanguard Graphics Staffing, Darien, Ct

    (Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Staffing and Recruiting industry)

    August 1997 — January 2000 (2 years 6 months)

    Vanguard placed me in various businesses in Westchester, NY and Fairfield, CT, ranging from package design houses to advertising agencies. I used the top graphic design applications from Adobe and Quark, invariably on a Mac. I had maybe a 5% to 10% downtime where I was not placed, so I was in demand.
