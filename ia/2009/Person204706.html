Reed R. Heustis, Jr., Esq.’s Summary

Reed practices as a Workers' Compensation Defense Attorney for an Orange County law firm; and also markets Life Events Legal Plans for a professional company that is publicly traded on the New York Stock Exchange.

In 2006, Reed earned the Claims Adjuster Designation certificate through California's State Compensation Insurance Fund, the largest provider of workers' compensation insurance in the Golden State. Reed now utilizes his claims experience for an aggressive private professional corporation, the Law Offices of Daniel P. Goggins, defending large and small employers, homeowners, insurance companies and third party administrators throughout California.

Meanwhile, Reed serves as an Independent Associate for Pre-Paid Legal Services, Inc., a pioneer in the North American legal plan industry that provides access to quality legal services at cost effective rates and a reputable business opportunity for its entrepreneurially inclined members.

Reed earned his Juris Doctor from San Diego's oldest law school, California Western School of Law (accredited by the American Bar Association in 1962); and his Bachelor of Arts in Political Science from University of California, Irvine.

In 2001 Reed graduated from the National Litigation Academy with a scholarship endowed by the Alliance Defense Fund, a servant organization that provides resources that empower the spread of the Gospel of Jesus Christ through legal defense and advocacy of religious freedom, the sanctity of human life, and biblical family values.

Reed and his wife raise two daughters, and are members of Trinity Reformed Baptist Church of La Mirada, an affiliate of the Association of Reformed Baptist Churches of America. He is also a member of the American Heritage Party. His interests include baseball, fantasy sports, golf, politics, classical music and network marketing.

Reed R. Heustis, Jr., Esq.’s Specialties:

Defending against excessive and unreasonable claims; negotiating fair settlements with legitimately injured workers; deposing applicants and witnesses; litigating in all venues in Northern and Southern California; drafting monthly diary letters to client claims administrators; advocating at Status Conferences, Expedited Hearings, Mandatory Settlement Conferences, Lien Hearings and Trials at Workers' Compensation Appeals Board and other tribunals; disputing medical provider liens; and much more.
