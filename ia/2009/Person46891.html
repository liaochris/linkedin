  • Executive Vice President

    BGC Brokers Inc.

    (Privately Held; 501-1000 employees; Financial Services industry)

    May 2005 — July 2006 (1 year 3 months)

    Euro Brokers operations and the public holding company, Maxcor Financial Group Inc. (symbol MAXF), were sold and merged into BGC Partners, a subsidiary of Cantor Fitzgerald in May 2005. While continuing my simultaneous role as President of the Euro Brokers Relief Fund, I maintained a role in negotiating brokerage commissions with customers, maintaining relations with regulators (such as the Federal Reserve), training new employees and coordinating special projects (such as the restructuring of the BGC Brokers 401-k plan).

  • President

    Euro Brokers Relief Fund, Inc.

    (Non-Profit; Myself Only; Philanthropy industry)

    September 2001 — July 2006 (4 years 11 months)

    On 9/11, Euro Brokers lost 61 of its 280 New York employees at the World Trade Center. I was asked to be President of a new 501(c)(3) charity that would financially support the families of our 61 victims. Primarily from company 'charity days' and generous donors (such as Wall Street competitors) over $5.2 million was raised and distributed before the fifth anniversary of 9/11. This was a very satisfying position as I interacted with all these families as well as their legal and financial representatives.

  • Executive Vice President

    Euro Brokers Maxcor Inc.

    (Public Company; 201-500 employees; MAXF; Financial Services industry)

    October 1996 — May 2005 (8 years 8 months)

    After the sale to Maxcor, I continued my responsibilities for Marketing, Training, and Commission rate negotiations with customers. Euro Brokers lost 61 of its 280 New York employees on 9/11/2001. Following that event, I was involved in the successful relocation/rebuilding efforts and subsequently served as President of the Euro Brokers Relief Fund, a 501(c)(3) charity. I spent considerable time with regulators in Beijing and Shanghai successfully establishing an office in China as it began to liberalize its financial services industry. MAXF was sold to a subsidiary of Cantor Fitzgerald in May 2005 and all shares were retired for cash.

  • Executive Vice President & Secretary

    Euro Brokers Investment Corp.

    (Financial Services industry)

    January 1987 — September 1996 (9 years 9 months)

    One of seven individuals who completed a management buy-out of the money market and capital market operations in New York (headquarters) and London. During the following decade our team expanded globally and established offices in Tokyo, Hong Kong, Sydney and Geneva while the company grew to over 800 employees. Member of the Compensation Committee. In 1996 we sold the company for cash and a 49% interest in a public special-purpose acquisition corporation later renamed Maxcor Financial Group Inc.

  • Executive Vice President

    Euro Brokers Inc., New York

    (Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Financial Services industry)

    May 1981 — December 1986 (5 years 8 months)

    One of three former partners signed to a five-year retention contract to manage the New York office for the new UK owners. Continued as an InterDealer Broker of Interest Rate Derivatives (Swaps, FRAs, Options). Also responsible for managing company's growing dependence on technology as PCs began to dominate the trading floors in international financial products (pricing algorithms, pricing distribution networks, etc.). In mid-1986 I was one of seven New York employees who negotiated a purchase of the New York and London operations from the UK owners.
