Trevor Thieme’s Summary

Demonstrates flexibility and skill as both a magazine editor and radio producer, navigating diverse topic areas to engage a wide range of audiences: health and fitness, food and travel, business and style, and science and technology. Accomplished writer with 10 years of experience contributing features, profiles, and reviews to top national magazines, working effectively with art and production staffs to generate award-nominated results. Established record of success in expanding brand recognition of publications through television and radio appearances.

Trevor Thieme’s Specialties:

Educated as a science journalist, experienced as both a science and men's lifestyle editor, and trained as an expert in exercise physiology.

Trevor Thieme’s Experience

  • Senior Associate Editor / Best Life Magazine

    Rodale

    (Privately Held; Publishing industry)

    November 2004 — March 2009 (4 years 5 months)

    EDITORIAL RESPONSIBILITIES: Oversaw The Best Advice, a front-of-book question-and-answer section consistently rated “most popular” by readers. Oversaw and wrote features on health, fitness, travel, and nutrition. Oversaw and wrote front-of-book health, fitness, career, and nutrition pages, and edited associated columnists. Recruited and managed a team of 22 professional advisors in fields ranging from psychology and nutrition to investing, career planning, and travel. Oversaw the publication of Best Life’s first book, “Flat-Belly Summer” (Rodale, June 2008).

    WRITING CREDITS: Contributed regularly to feature well, most recently with “The New Powerfoods” (October 2008), a collection of 10 little known, must-have health foods for men. Other notable features include “It Tastes Better If It’s Still Squirming” (March 2008), which chronicled my tour of the wildest markets and finest restaurants in Asia with three star Michelin chef Jean-Georges Vongerichten; “America’s Best Body Shops” (March 2006), an award-nominated review of the nation’s top healthcare facilities in specialties ranging from cardiology to fertility; and “Out Race Heart Disease” (October 2005), an investigative look at the latest advancements in cardiovascular health. Also contributed front-of-book articles spanning the subjects of business, travel, style, health, and fitness.

    TELEVISION & RADIO CREDITS: Extensive media training through Mary Mayotte + Associates, a New York City-based communications consulting firm. Appeared regularly on ABC Eyewitness News, CW11 Morning News, Good Day New York, and several nationally syndicated radio programs to discuss topics ranging from the threat of corporate surveillance to how to decode a sushi menu.

    Time Inc.

    (Public Company; TWX; Publishing industry)

    December 2001 — November 2003 (2 years)

Developed lineups, assigned stories, recruited writers, edited manuscripts, and wrote display copy. Also created and oversaw the magazine’s first fact checking department.

    WRITING CREDITS: Regularly reported Popular Science Adventure stories from the field, piloting deep ocean submersibles, training with wildland firefighters, learning to skeleton with the U.S. Olympic team, and flying twin-prop weather planes into 40,000-foot thunderheads to fight hailstorms. Also contributed several features, including “Building a Better Pest,” an investigative look at how scientists plan to use genetically engineered insects to protect the American food supply.
