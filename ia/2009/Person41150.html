Charisse McAuliffe’s Summary

After graduating from College of Charleston in South Carolina with a degree in mass media communications, she headed off to Los Angeles, CA to pursue her career in television production. Charisse worked as a segment producer and field producer for Warner Brothers/Telepictures where she created television show pilots, then worked as a productio n coordinator and production manager for ASAG Productions on shows for MSNBC, A&E, MTV and CBS. Following a position as a broadcast producer for various ad and production companies, as well as a freelancer for The Travel Channel, Charisse got her real estate license and began brokering commercial real estate projects including a large scale condominium conversion project where she also worked as the project manager. It was at that time that she discovered her passion for all things green. A self-taught novice in green building practice, she further explored other areas of sustainable lifestyle changes and found this knowledge to be life changing. In 2006, desperate to be surrounded with more like-minded individuals, Charisse and her family moved to Ft. Collins where, upon her arrival, she found her calling, later to be known as GenGreen. She made it her life mission to lay the groundwork to make it easier for people in Colorado, the U.S., and hopefully one day the world, to live their sustainable lives easier.
