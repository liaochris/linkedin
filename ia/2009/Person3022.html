Adam McGreggor’s Summary

First and foremost, I'm an opinionated (based on experience) blunt, straight-talking generalist. I *hate* corporatese and salesbollocks.

Whilst mainly getting my fingers involved in SysAdmining, I've been known to manage projects, and number-crunch in the last few years.

Whilst I will use Linux, I prefer, on the whole to use BSD-based operating systems: they seem more mature and stable to me. Linux Distros: mainly Debian, but will touch others, although I prefer not to deal with RedHat. I don't believe Umbungo has a place on server-platforms. And now am questioning its use as a desktop operating system.

SysAdminery wise, I'm a weirdo who *likes* email systems: Exim, Mailman, RT are where I spent time, along with SpamAssassin, Clam, all that sort of thing. I'm moderately active on mailman-{users,developers}. I'll get my fingers involved in Apache, but I am not a webdev. I've been known to write the odd bit of Perl, and knock up scripts (I am a lazy sysadmin who will script as much as possible).

Also familiar with Microsoft Products, I do like (and know) Exchange: and by know I don't mean being able to press 'next' 9 times, and hope for the best...

I'm neither a Mac-hater, nor a fanboy :)

If you're using old versions of Netware, I may be able to help you there, too. Although I may be more inclined to advise you to upgrade the network to something a bit more modern.

Consultancy, management (people/systems/projects), analyses, second-opinions, infrastructure rejigs, negotiations, strategy, migrations & upgrades, procurement, advice, are roles I'm content (and competent) in.

Elsewhere, I'm (mainly) a Commercial Analyst, Policy Draftsman, Negotiator, Strategic Thinker/Directionalist.

My general-knowledge stretches quite far: in various interests.

Currently, I'm mainly working with out-of-the-tarball web-solutions: Wordpress, Mediawiki, Drupal &c.
