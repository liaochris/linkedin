Lance B. Taylor’s Summary

A vet of television programming and production, Lance established himself as an independent producer to acquire rights, develop, and produce books, pilots, and original concepts as television series. He also consults with the industry, advising networks, cable services, and others.

In January, "Professor" Taylor begins teaching multi-camera production & direction at Cal State LA's Master of Television-Film-Theatre program and looks forward to again presenting his class to UCR's MFA writers on developing, producing and marketing a successful television show. Thanks to industry colleagues, the class gains insights through guest lectures.

Lance continues to write a book "on a certain aspect of Hollywood celebrities that my lawyer thinks would make a bizarre reality television series" but he wants to try it as a book first.

Lance's career includes ABC Sr. Vice President, Current Programming contributing to the network’s resurgence, supervising the production of "Desperate Housewives", "Boston Legal", "Lost" and "Grey’s Anatomy" among others. Recognized by AFTRA as Executive Producer of the innovative “Micro Mini” series.

At Fox, SVP Current creatively overseeing successful series "That ‘70s Show", "Malcolm in the Middle", "The X-Files", and "24" and animated series "Family Guy", "King of the Hill", "The PJs" with Eddie Murphy, "Futurama" and "The Simpsons".

Taylor founded a non-profit regional theatre company, Capitol City Theatre, in Boise, Idaho creating “The Emerging Playwrights” for the city schools.

Earlier, VP Creative Affairs at Shelley Duvall's Think Entertainment producing series and movies for Showtime, TNT, and Disney.

Prior, VP Current, Columbia Pictures TV; then VP Creative Affairs, Coca-Cola to develop/produce live action, animation, and a package of international co-productions.

Taylor began as a Page, promoted to Unit Manager for ABC daytime, news, and sports, then onto programming becoming Director, Comedy Development.
