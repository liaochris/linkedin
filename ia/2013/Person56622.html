Founder, CEO

Once Magazine, Inc.

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Publishing industry

January 2010 – February 2012 (2 years 2 months) San Francisco

Two months before the iPad was announced, I drafted a business case for a photography magazine to be built exclusively for "slate" computers, as the rumor mill had pegged Apple's mystery device. I spent the next two years bringing it to life with the help of, at its peak, a team of 22 journalists and designers.

Highlights? We outsold The New Yorker (briefly). We pulled $30,000 in revenue. We were the highest rated paid magazine on the App Store for over four months. TechCrunch called us "Life Magazine for the tablet age." We were in our early 20s and collaborating with some of the best living photojournalists.

Director of Design

San Francisco Public Press

Nonprofit; 11-50 employees; Writing and Editing industry

March 2010 – January 2011 (11 months) San Francisco

I oversaw the redesign of the SFPP website into a print newspaper. Seriously. After interviews with a dozen stakeholders, including a volunteer team from IDEO, I led a ground-up effort to reimagine the SFPPs website as a quarterly, printed news product.

After designing and building consensus around the debut look of the paper, I managed the production process for the first two issues.

Photo Editor

McSweeney's Publishing

Publishing industry

June 2009 – December 2009 (7 months) San Francisco

I was the photo editor for issue 33 of McSweeney's Quarterly Concern, aka: The San Francisco Panorama. Most of my days were spent reporting alongside McSweeney's writers and journalists in the field.

I also oversaw a team of 3-6 photographer-interns (it varied), wrote and edited captions, and drafted the magazine's freelance photography contract. In the end, I personally shot 19 assignments, eight of which were published. (This was actually a great ratio, as dozens of stories were ultimately cut.)

Editor and Publisher

The Catalyst Newspaper

Nonprofit; 11-50 employees; Newspapers industry

December 2006 – December 2007 (1 year 1 month) Colorado Springs, CO

I oversaw both the business and editorial sides of my college's student run newspaper. My time was primarily consumed by recruiting and managing the growth of the team from 22 to nearly 60 students. Under my direction, the paper's circulation grew from 1100 to 1900 through widened coverage of off-campus communities.

In the newsroom, I phased out the paper's centralized editorial system and designed and implemented a series of subject-focused desks. I commissioned a redesign of the paper; my byline appeared on about half a dozen articles; and I co-edited a story about students in black-face that gained national attention.
