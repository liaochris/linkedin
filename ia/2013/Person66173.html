John Carter McKnight's Summary

As a May 2013 PhD graduate, I am seeking a tenure-track appointment or joint appointment in STS, Law, Communications or related field at an institution committed to high-profile interdisciplinary research at the intersection of emerging technologies and innovation in science education and governance.

I study social relations in new or unusual technological environments: online games and virtual worlds, cross-platform social networks of science and technology fans and professionals, informal public science communication, and space exploration advocacy communities. My particular interests are in the rhetorics of identity (including gender and power), space and place, and imagined futures.

I specialize in internet-based ethnography, and as a member of the Ethics Committee of the Association of Internet Researchers, aim to contribute to evolving standards of ethical research and effective methods.

As Co-Chair of the Consortium of Applied Space Ethics (CASE) this past year, I was able to unite my legal and policy gained over 10 years as a securities regulation and corporate finance attorney in international private practice with my work on ethical behavior in the use of new technologies, imagined futures, and governance of emerging technological communities. CASE, a pilot project of the Lincoln Center for Applied Ethics at Arizona State University, sought to broker conversations among stakeholders in a new age of space exploration and commercialization, foster public debate at an early stage of deployment of space technologies, and aid in the development of consensus standards for new space activities.


Specialties: Nonprofit management; qualitative internet research; teaching (Law, Science & Technology Studies, Anthropology, Qualitative Research Methodologies, Games Studies); curriculum design; marketing and social media strategies for education and nonprofits; games for education, training and social change
