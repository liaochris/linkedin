Jeff Christian's Summary

Jeff Christian is considered one of the pioneers in Talent Equity. Talent Equity mobilizes the extraordinary leadership talent and ideal financial resources required to transform an idea or a company into a market leader. Jeff partners with Private Equity firms and Backable CEOs to consolidate fragmented markets as well as to build market leading companies from the ground up. Jeff has utilized the Talent Equity model throughout his career to build hundreds of early stage companies as well as secured the top leadership teams for large corporations. He placed the CEO at HP, F5 Networks, Hyperion and many early stage start ups as well as conducted Board work for Adobe, Biogen, HP and the last 2 Board searches for Steve Jobs. Jeff was Founder and CEO of Christian & Timbers (Now CTP) which was the fastest growing search firm in the industry, growing the company to $80 million revenue and the market leader in technology as well as one of the market leaders in CEO & Board work. CTP was the first firm to start a VC company in 1984 with the launch of Technology Ventures making it the first true Talent Equity firm. Jeff was also the Founder and Managing Partner of CT Access Partners, a talent based venture fund. Jeff was the Founder of Peoplescape, a software company specializing in the staffing industry. Jeff orchestrated the merger with Hire.com in 2003. Jeff is a deal guy who loves building companies. He was listed on the Forbes Midas list of 100 Top Deal Makers for 4 consecutive years Jeff was named on the Forbes Midas list as a top deal maker four years in a row and has been on the Inc. 500 list as well as a finalist in the Ernst & Young Entrepreneur of the Year. . He is the author of ‘The Headhunter’s Edge’. He has founded 2 non profits, SIE (Scholarship In Escrow) and The Number 12 Foundation and has sat on a number of corporate and philanthropic Boards including Cleveland Art Museum and Cleveland Ballet.

CEO and founder

CT Partners (formerly Christian & Timbers)

1979 – 2004 (25 years)

Jeff founded Christian & Timbers as a retained search firm specializing in technology in 1979. At the time the company had one office in Cleveland and in order to compete in Silicon Valley and other technology meccas, such as Boston, the company was forced to develop the most advanced systems, methodologies and processes in search execution in the industry. Jeff found that the industry in general employed antiquated methods (relying on personal networks and database contacts) and did very little in analyzing a company's culture and unique dynamics necessary in helping to build Talent based organizations. His first three clients were GE, Westinghouse and IBM working in the fields of robotics and factory automation.

He ensured his company took the time to reinvent what is most important in finding, recruiting and hiring the very best Talent. As a result, the company grew very quickly and soon dominated the high tech industry. He extended the practice areas to include computing, software and semi-conductors, and began to work heavily with Start Ups, Early Stage Companies, Venture Capitalists and Private Equity firms.

Some of the work included CEO/HP, CEO/F5Networks, CEO/Network Solutions, and Board work for Apple, Biogen and Adobe. Jeff also managed strategic relationships where his company did most of the work for Apple, Microsoft and all the high end work for Cisco.

Jeff led the company through its fastest periods of growth, averaging a 70% growth rate over a nine year period of time. His strategy included not only the best methodologies in search execution, but superior branding and marketing, and an intense commitment to finding and developing the best Talent for our own firm.

By 2004, Jeff had built Christian & Timbers into a 350 employee organization with 15 offices globally billing $80 million in revenue.

Managing Partner

Technology Ventures, Managing Partner

1983 – 1987 (4 years)

TVI focused on building early state start ups where we could provide the marketing strategy, business plan development, funding and leadership necessary for success.

Our first deal was a spin off from Case Western Reserve University, Endot, a systems level design automation software company used for the design of IC's. We came in, brought in one of our partners as acting COO, wrote the business plan, enhanced revenue and raised two rounds of funding in addition to rebuilding the board. The company was successfully wold at a significant profit for all investors to Data I/O Futurenet.

TVI's second deal was Dental Research where we found the inventor of the Interplak toothbrush, raised several rounds of funding, built the leadership team and wrote the business plan. The company was successfully sold two years later for $2 million. The Interplak toothbrush became the top selling item ever in The Sharper Image catalog.

Our third deal was Thermoscan, the instant thermometer. We built the company around a prototype, built the management team and board and secured several rounds of funding. The company was sold 2 1/2 years after inception for approximately $200 million.

Jeff Christian's Publications

  • The Headhunter's Edge

    • Random House
    • August 26, 2002
    Authors: Jeff Christian

    One of the world’s top headhunters reveals his most valuable techniques for getting the best jobs and finding the right people.

    The most important thing you’ll ever do if you are trying to build, rebuild, or even turn around an organization is hire the best people—and keep them. Jeffrey E. Christian has learned this lesson by working on hundreds of executive search assignments and building his own headhunting firm into a nationally recognized company, one of the top ten in the nation. In The Headhunter’s Edge, he reveals his secrets for excelling on either side of the desk—as a leader trying to build a great company, or as a job seeker in search of the next big position.

    In this practical manifesto, Christian shows how essential it is to have the most talented people on your side. But how do you find the best? And how do you become the best? Christian’s solution: Think like a headhunter. He gives readers the benefits of his twenty years of experience interviewing thousands of CEOs and potential CEOs, and tells you

    • how to conduct an interview and spot great leadership qualities in job candidates
    • exactly what to do and say to keep a valuable employee from resigning
    • how to expand your network to find the best emerging talent
    • key strategies and instructions for choosing and getting the most out of a search firm
    • what it takes for ambitious and talented people to get noticed and get the next big job or promotion

    Practical, impassioned, and wise, The Headhunter’s Edge is an indispensable guide to advancing your career—and making your business more successful and profitable.
