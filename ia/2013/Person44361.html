Ben Feist's Summary

Associate Director of Technology

dthree

Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Internet industry

December 2004 – May 2006 (1 year 6 months)

Head of dthree's client-side development group. This was the heyday of the Flash microsite.

Working at dthree was really an experiment for me; one that unfortunately failed. When I joined dthree it was a struggling sole proprietorship. I joined with several others in an attempt to right the ship and create a functioning digital agency. We made some positive progress but unfortunately were outnumbered and out-lasted. After approximately a year of pushing to improve agency operations, positive momentum waned and previous progress began to erode. One need only look at the roster of great talent that entered and exited the company in the short time I was there to assess the health of the company.

The best thing that came out of being there for me was connecting with some fantastic people who I have since worked with in healthier environments to great success.

Senior Project Engineer

Organic

Privately Held; 201-500 employees; OMC; Marketing and Advertising industry

January 2000 – December 2004 (5 years)

One of the three first employees in the Organic Toronto office. I built a number of Organic's more technically complex projects during their "golden age" as both a technical team leader and a developer.

2000 - 2001 - A period of expansion at Organic. I focused on building the development team--often when projects were already in flight. I lead the architecture and development of several multimillion dollar web sites such as a complex J2EE site for BMO's ill-fated insurance offering, olgc.ca, olgclotteries.ca

2001 - 2002 - Because of new interactive market realities (bubble burst) I expanded my role to cover gaps created by downsizing. I maintained the quality of Organic's technical output with a much smaller team.

2002 - 2004 - Through this period I continued to operate in an expanded role. I was given a great mixture of unusual project challenges, and met each challenge with creative thinking and flexibility. I used a wide variety of technologies to build a large number of projects ranging from Bell Canada’s McDTV narrowcast television delivery system in 2002, to Benjamin Moore’s Canadian internet presence in 2004 (benjaminmoore.ca).
