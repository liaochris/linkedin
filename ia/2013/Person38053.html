Dr. Rick Kirschner's Summary

Best selling author, communication and change speaker, trainer and coach, primarily working in the areas of positive change (making a difference), conflict resolution (dealing with difficult people) and persuasion (increasing your influence). Interested in making contacts with mentors, colleagues and angels, and in beginning and developing relationships with key people in organizations that can benefit from my work. My previous book, published by Hyperion Books, is 'How To Click With People: The Secret To Better Relationships In Business And In Life.' My latest book is the coauthored bestseller, Dealing With People You Can't Stand:How To Bring Out The Best In People At Their Worst' (Brinkman, Kirschner), third revised edition released summer of 2012.

I deliver keynote speeches and communication training at conferences, conventions, workshops, in house events.

Rick Kirschner, N.D.

Private Practice of Naturopathic/Nutritional Medicine

Health, Wellness and Fitness industry

January 1982 – June 2011 (29 years 6 months) Ashland, OR

Following 3 years of conventional pre-medical education, I attended a 4 year naturopathic medical school, where I learned about healthcare options, like specific alternatives to giving people petroleum derivatives and cutting off troublesome body parts. I learned that an ounce of prevention really is worth a ton of cure. I learned that if all you do is treat symptoms, they may eventually overwhelm the patient. And I learned that if you treat the cause of a problem, you can often eliminate the problem.

Naturopathic medicine was born in the United States at the end of the 19th century. Though popular early in the new century, it fell out of favor with the advent of antibiotics, and the idea that the solution for every health problem is found in a pill or procedure. But it is back now, and expanding.

When I graduated in 1981, there was only 1 four year naturopathic medical school in the entire country. Today, it is estimated that well over half of U.S. healthcare consumers are now using some form of natural medicine, and there are at least 5 four-year natural medicine colleges in the U.S and 3 more in Canada. These schools are developing world class natural medicine experts to meet today's demands, and tomorrows as well. You can learn more by visiting the American Association of Naturopathic Medicine, at http://naturopathic.org
