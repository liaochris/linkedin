Jean Robert Opgenort's Publications

  • A Grammar of Jero

    • Leiden: Koninklijke Brill
    • 2005
    Authors: Jean Robert Opgenort

    The book A Grammar of Jero is the main spin-off of my descriptive research on the Wambule language of eastern Nepal. This book offers the first-ever published detailed analysis of the phonology, morphology and syntax of Jero, the previously undescribed and endangered Kiranti language most closely related to Wambule. The presentation of this short grammar owes a great deal to my exhaustive grammar of Wambule, allowing easy comparison between the two closely related languages.

    The book also contains a historical comparative study of the Kiranti languages, the branch of the Tibeto-Burman language family to which both Jero and Wambule belong. This systematic reconstruction is the most recent and complete study of Proto-Kiranti to date and supersedes all earlier lexical and phonological comparisons of Kiranti languages.

  • A Grammar of Wambule

    • Leiden: Koninklijke Brill
    • 2004
    Authors: Jean Robert Opgenort

    The book A Grammar of Wambule is the revised and enlarged version of the doctoral dissertation The Wāmbule Language. The book consists of ten chapters and five annexes with sixteen analysed texts (including a 52-page long sacred text of the oral tradition chanted by wedding negotiators), a Wambule-English-Nepali and English-Wambule lexicon, verbal paradigms and an overview of the first material published on the language; an exhaustive reference work for Wambule/Tibeto-Burman linguistics, language typology, linguistic theory and Wambule society and culture, and as such indispensable for any linguistic and anthropological library.This book was sponsored by the Netherlands Organisation for Scientific Research (NWO), the Niels Stensen Stichting and the International Institute of Asian Studies (IIAS).

  • The Wāmbule Language

    • Dissertation, Leiden University
    • 2002
    Authors: Jean Robert Opgenort

    The book The Wāmbule Language offers the first-ever published analysis of the phonology, morphology, syntax and lexicon of the previously undescribed and endangered Wambule language (Hilepānī dialect). The book consists of ten chapters and four annexes with twelve analysed texts, a Wambule-English-Nepali lexicon, verbal paradigms and an overview of the first material published on the language.

  • Basiscursus Noors

    • Praktischoling Vertalingen V.O.F.
    • 1996
    Authors: Jean Robert Opgenort

    Op een eenvoudige manier maakt u kennis met de uitspraak en grammatica van het Noors en u leert al doende de meest gebruikte woorden, zodat u zich al snel in het Noors kunt redden. Bij de twee cursusboeken horen zes cassettes met daarop Noorse teksten, voorbeeldzinnen, oefeningen en gesprekken.
