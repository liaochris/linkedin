Dr. Rob Anderson's Summary

Dr. Rob uses a "whole person approach". This approach to wellness means looking for underlying causes of any disturbance or disruption (which may or may not be causing symptoms at the time) and make whatever interventions and lifestyle adjustments that would optimize the conditions for normal function. Using this unique approach, Dr. Rob is able to help you to accelerate and/or maintain your journey to good health.

Dr. Rob is a Colorado native. He grew up playing baseball, BMX racing and wrestling. He went to undergrad at CU and studied kinesiology and human biology. Then he moved to Portland, to attend chiropractic college. He has a wonderful family including: his wife, Kimberly, a daughter, Marissa and two sons, Cole and Ryne. During his professional education he was captain of the Sports Chiropractic Club. He cared for many of the Nike World Campus athletes, hundreds of Iron-Men, athletes competing in the Nike World Masters Olympics and numerous marathoners. He graduated Cum Laude and was honored with receiving the class award for Clinical Excellence. He and his family moved back to Colorado and he has been in practice since 1998. Since then he has volunteered his time and skills to provide thousands of toys to less fortunate children at Christmas. In 10 years he has donated over 6000 adjustment visits to athletes at Highlands Ranch and Valor Christian High Schools. He has provided care to the School of Mines football team, current and retired Denver Broncos, pro and amateur golfers, Iron Men, MMA fighters, Stanford University’s track team, Olympic Training Center Athletes and hundreds of local youth athletes. Currently, he is the team doctor for Valor Christian High. He was recently honored to serve in partnership with Sportsbridge Sports Complex, and HitStreak as their Chiropractic Health Provider.
