Gregg Townsley's Publications

  • Lady of the Lake

    • Two Bears Books
    • July 2013
    Authors: Gregg Townsley

    Does the "Lady of the Lake," a large grey stone whose lower half resembles a woman's body, hold the key to who murdered two Washoe Indian men up at Lake Tahoe? W.W. Ronin, an ex-Episcopal priest turned bounty hunter, is about to find out. With help from his Washoe shaman friend, Happy Hands -- interpreter of dreams, earth-bound spiritualist and stranger to the white man's rituals -- Ronin is easing closer to an answer as the pair meanders up and down the Carson Valley in 1880s Nevada.

    It will take a plethora of people, including Emma Naumann, guardian of children at the local Christian mission ... his best buddy and occasional partner in crime Deputy Marcus T. "Dustsucker" Slade ... and the contrary but capable U.S. Marshal Augustus Ash ... to form an unlikely posse good enough to unravel the mystery.

    As Ronin's continuing adventures find him engaged in more gun battles and heartaches than any aging but still formidable former reverend ought to endure, he begins to discover what makes his own life shimmer even as he pursues the desperadoes who took the lives of the Indians at the lake.

    The second in the W. W. Ronin series of Westerns, by Gregg Edwards Townsley's: "Real people. Real places. The way the West was really won."

  • East Jesus, Nevada

    • Saint Helens, OR
    • November 2012
    Authors: Gregg Townsley

    Native American children turn up missing from a white man’s Christian Gospel mission, and two fast friends—brought together by passion and persistence during Nevada’s 880s western expansion—are hell-bent on finding them.

    Meet W.W. Ronin, a former Episcopal minister turned bounty hunter, and his imposing but congenial buddy, Carson City deputy sheriff, Marcus T. “Dustsucker” Slade, in the first
    of the W. W. Ronin series of Westerns.

    Together, the men fight graft and greed with pure guts as they wind their way through the Washoe Valley to solve the mystery of the missing Indian children. And along the way, they discover not
    only a faster friendship, but a deeper meaning to life in the wild, wild West.

    The W. W. Ronin series of westerns: real people, real places, how the West was really won!

  • A Description of the Unchurched American

    • Academic Press
    • June 1983
    Authors: Gregg Townsley

    Out of print revision of my D. Min. research on finding, understanding, attracting and incorporating new church members from the growing number of disaffiliated and unchurched individuals in the United States. The final chapter was titled, "Ten Steps Toward a Church for the Unchurched," prefiguring the decline of the mainline (now correctly referred to as "old line") church while proposing changes that would finally become the "emerging church" movement.
