Lisa Brewster's Summary

Part developer, part marketer, and part strategist, I build genuine relationships that make technology more meaningful.

I co-founded BarCamp San Diego and Ignite San Diego, two pretty awesome tech events that facilitate the rapid transmission of ideas. I was also one of three organizers for preDevCamp, a grassroots “hack day” to kick-start webOS development in 80 cities around the world.

In 2011, I was named one of LAPTOP Magazine's 20 most influential women in mobile tech. I've also been covered in TechCrunch, Mashable, MAKE, Adbusters Magazine, and the Washington Post.

In my time running the app review program for webOS, I've worked with independent developers, name brands, content producers for kids and "adults," open source, and homebrew communities, and navigated through a broad range of challenges with minimal drama. It starts with having clear policies, but the real success comes from being relatable and trustworthy. It's not the relationship of a company controlling users, but of a real person helping your friends be awesome.
