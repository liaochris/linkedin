Madelon Evers' Summary

I build a bridge between leadership and innovation, facilitating top tier teams to lead change for a better future. Today, a company's success in innovation depends on responsible leadership, as practiced in teams. Responsible leaders know WHY they do what they do, working in a conscious manner to make courageous, ethical decisions. Responsible leaders design the future, find synergies and move people from idea to success. Trust your potential and start doing great things, rather than just dreaming about them!

I focus on responsible business leaders who wish to achieve their highest potential. You are a talented professional, manager, or entrepreneur, 8+ years in business, aiming for responsible leadership. You need an experienced executive/team coach to reach superior results in less time. Use my 20+ years in international business to your benefit. Responsible leadership can only be developed with structured support and time to learn in a personal and collective space, about what you are doing, finding ways to do things even better. In my Responsible Leadership programs, we work with you to:
• Become aware of your natural strengths
• Leverage personal creativity and vision
• Embrace the cutting edge principles of adaptive and ‘whole systems’ thinking for management
• Understand how group dynamics impact teamwork
• Know how to leverage co-creation within groups as a sustainable source of power
• Be comfortable with taking up power responsibly in challenging business settings.

As a result of my programs, leaders say they discover what it takes to be a role model to others, walking their talk as a responsible leaders. This makes a huge difference for companies requiring talented, empowered and creative managers to tackle the complex issues of our times.

I can work with you in English, French, Dutch, and with business level German, Italian, Spanish.

Madelon Evers' Publications

  • Learning from Design

    • Eburon, Delft NL
    • November 1, 2004
    Authors: Madelon Evers

    Most strategic information systems (IS) projects fail, as teams struggle to understand complex design problems, manage large-scale design processes, and negotiate design decisions in organisations. Multidisciplinary design teams need support to learn how to improve their skills and knowledge in design, and to manage complex processes more effectively. Dr Madelon Evers proposes an approach called the Design and Learning Methodology (DLM), as a practical way to facilitate stakeholders involved in IS design to learn collectively from their design experience, using this experience to build quality results. The added value of the DLM is that it has been shown to transform team-based learning into appropriate design decisions. The study described here involved participatory action research in eleven design projects at European financial services companies and at an international engineering institute, over a period of five years.

  • Leading change for sustainable business

    • September 2011 to Present
    Team Members: Madelon Evers, Joe Lafferty, Michael Lindsay

    The Diploma in Sustainable Business is a joint program of the Institute for Economy and the Environment at University of St. Gallen (IWÖ-HSG) and BSL. It is endorsed by the World Business Council for Sustainable Development (WBCSD).
    The Leading Change track is 1/3 of the total program, and is designed by Dr. Madelon Evers and Joe Lafferty. Leading Change is a team facilitation process focused on how leaders develop their strategy and competencies to embed sustainability into a company and manage risks in a responsible way.
    This innovative program provides participants a unique opportunity to work directly with, and reflect deeply on, the major challenges that our planet, society and businesses face.
    Leading Change is part of a 10-month program including a project focused on sustainability, sponsored by Swiss-based international companies. The final project and Leading Change reports replace the traditional thesis, making it a highly practical education for business professionals. For a brochure go to: http://www.bsl-lausanne.ch/programs/executive-education/diploma-in-sustainable-business/home
