Steffen Bartschat's Summary

I love to bridge gaps - for example, the gap between start-ups and global corporations - and within companies, the gap between sales and engineering - or more generically, the gap between creativity and delivery.

To start-ups, I provide an executive-level perspective on how to best reach commercial scale. Activities include business modeling/planning, supply chain creation/optimization, building engineering teams, organization-level planning & recruiting, generating sales, and putting in metrics and processes for weekly decision making to drive the business forward. Key technical areas of expertise include consumer software, digital video, and wearable devices.

For corporates, I act as ambassador to Silicon Valley, the world's innovation capital. As a Silicon Valley insider, I help companies create mind share within the Silicon Valley ecosystem, as well as provide access to deal-flow. I supply a "forward base" to visitors - you give me a list of corporate innovation challenges and I find startups, university researchers, and investors that focus their energies within this area, but most likely never thought of your industry as a target market. My corporate experience ranges from enterprise software to consumer productivity and entertainment software, to transportation (automotive, airline, freight) and spans on-the-ground experience in USA, Europe, and Asia.

Before founding my own business in 2010, I held a variety of engineering executive management positions at IBM, Maxis (Electronic Arts), Broderbund, and Sonic Solutions / Roxio (now Rovi).

I have degrees in both business and engineering. Recently, I earned an MS in Business Management from Stanford Business School as an Alfred P. Sloan Fellow, Class of 2011.
I also hold B.S. and M.S. degrees in Electrical Engineering from Carnegie Mellon University.
