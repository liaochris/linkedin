Marilyn Krieger's Summary

Professional experience:
Certified Cat Behavior Consultant, MA, CWA Professional Member, co-chair of the cat division and former member of the BOD of the International Association of Animal Behavior Consultants (IAABC), owner of The Cat Coach, LLC(SM) (www.thecatcoach.com.

Marilyn solves cat behavior problems through on-site, Skype and phone consultations. Marilyn works directly with clients as well as through veterinarian referrals. She uses a combination of positive reinforcement methods, including behavior modification, training, management and education.

Her award-winning book, Naughty No More! focuses on changing unwanted cat behaviors through clicker training, environmental management and other positive reinforcement based methods. She writes the monthly cat behavior column for Cat Fancy Magazine and the behavior columns for Catster.com.

Marilyn has appeared on Animal Planet's Cats 101, demonstrating clicker training cats and discussing cat behavior and was the cat behavior advisor for a program aired on National Geographic. Additionally, she has received attention in such prominent media outlets as USA Today, MSNBC, San Jose Mercury News, The San Francisco Chronicle and others. She frequently guests on television and radio programs nationally, providing valuable tips and insights on how to modify cat and their human companion’s behaviors.

Marilyn has articles in many publications, including Cat Fancy Magazines, USA Today, Catnip, the Tufts University journal, as well as others.

Based in Northern California, Marilyn donates her expertise in cat behavior to the Peninsula Humane Society.

In addition to helping people with cat behavior challenges, Marilyn speaks nationally & is certified through the International Association of Animal Behavior Consultants.

For more information about her, check out www.thecatcoach.com

Specialties

A sample of some of the issues The Cat Coach can help resolve are:
Adjusting in a new home; Inappropriate elimination; Spraying; Aggression towards other animals; Aggression towards people; Excessive vocalization; Destructive behaviors; Furniture scratching; Fear-based behaviors; Door darting; Biting; Fear of people; Compulsive behaviors.
Training cats through clicker training and other positive based techniques
