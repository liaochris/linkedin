Martin Parker's Summary

A successful Engineering Director, Senior Project Management Professional and Prince2 Practitioner, whose 23 years’ significant and progressive experience and expertise within the defence industry have equipped him with all the necessary skills to achieve. With a commitment to quality and the use of structured project management tools such as work breakdown structures, schedule and resource planning, progress tracking, and risk and change management, he consistently ensures project deliverables and milestones are met on time and to budget. With his strong leadership, negotiation and persuasion skills, he motivates his multidisciplinary team and ensures productivity and maintains morale in high pressure situations.

An engineering graduate with proven technical skills, he is a competent problem solver who has managed the development of software and hardware throughout the project lifecycle.

He is confident, firm and fair, and is able to establish excellent working relationships with colleagues, internal customers and external customers alike. He has excellent presentation skills and has provided project management training courses.

Previous projects include sonar, command and control systems, fibre-optic communications, vibration monitoring equipment, aircraft power converters/inverters and tram management systems.

Consultant Project Manager (Contract)

Thales Underwater Systems

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; HO; Defense & Space industry

November 2011 – April 2012 (6 months) Cheadle Heath, Stockport

Reporting to the Transmitters and Sonar Receivers Competence Centre (UK) Manager, I assessed the impact of Her Majesty’s Government Information Assurance Standard No. 5 (IS5), Secure Sanitisation, on the company's products.

This involved the detailed analysis of all in-service equipments in order to establish the hardware used, the interaction between hardware and software and therefore the sanitisation options available. Documents sets were then produced which detailed immediate actions to be taken, actions to be taken before delivery of equipment or return for repair, and future recommendations.

Achievements
• Devised a generic document structure to be used for the products
• Analysed and produced document sets for the key products
• Developed a secure sanitisation process which be rolled out across the company. This process ensured that no equipment leaves the company without having the appropriate level of sanitisation applied.
• Highlighted areas of the design process which need to change in order to ensure ‘Design for Sanitisation’.

Senior Project Manager (Contract)

Thales Underwater Systems

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; HO; Defense & Space industry

January 2011 – October 2011 (10 months) Templecombe

Reporting to the Programme Manager, I was responsible for managing the integration and test of a large, complex Command and Control System product for mine hunting ships. The role involved planning, leading the integration and test team, the generation of tests scripts, the reporting and management of defects, and providing concise reports to the senior management. Presentation of the product at customer factory acceptances and the management of ship installation activities were other key activities.

Achievements:

• Introduced a suite of metrics to monitor testing performed and problems raised
• Stabilised the test platform in terms of configuration management and control
• Created detailed test schedules, using historical data to improve time and cost estimates
• Presented the system for factory acceptance on schedule
• Managed a subcontract with an overseas supplier which involved agreeing requirements allocation, the technical specification, interface specification and the scope of work. The supplier delivered on time.
• Planned and managed the successful installation of equipment onboard ship

Senior Project Manager (Contract)

Thales Underwater Systems

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; HO; Defense & Space industry

August 2008 – December 2010 (2 years 5 months) Stockport

Reporting to the Acquisition Sensors and Receivers Manager, I was responsible for the development of a number of analogue and digital transmitters and receivers for a submarine sonar upgrade programme (Project 1). The transmitters/receivers interface with the submarine’s acoustic arrays, process the data and pass it through to the signal and data processing software. The scope of work included the management of the development of the hardware and software. The project had undergone a number of significant cost increases and my objective was to evaluate the status of the project, estimate the real cost to completion and then to deliver to budget. My contract was extended to manage two further projects. One was for the development and supply of hardware for a sonar upgrade (COTS) programme (Project 2), the other was the development and supply of the hardware for a complex mine hunting command and control system (Project 3).

Achievements:
• Re-estimated cost to completion of Project 1 and delivered to budget after 18 months
• Delivered hardware for Project 2 on time with 14.5% cost saving
• Delivered hardware for Project 3 on time with 15% cost saving, partly through risk management
• Was requested to re-locate to Templecombe to manage the integration and test of Project 3
