Juanita Harb, Ph.D.'s Summary

I am a Clinical Psychologist who enjoys facilitating constructive change with clients who are experiencing life's transitions, whether personal or health related. I am currently seeking full-time work in a hospital setting.

My experience includes working with all age groups; children through adults. I have worked in hospital, educational, and private practice settings conducting crisis and non-crisis assessments, in conjunction with providing short- and long-term therapy modalities with individuals, couples, and families. Juanita Harb, Ph.D.'s Publications

  • How Anger is Displayed in Sandplay: A hermeneutic-phenomenological study.

    • ProQuest. UMI Dissertations (Reference #3345844).
    • September 3, 2011
    Authors: Juanita Harb, Ph.D.

    How anger is displayed in sandplay; a depth-psychological, phenomenological-hermeneutic comparative study on how anger is displayed by clients in sandplay. The origins of anger are examined from the perspectives of the psychoanalytic theory of Sigmund Freud, the school of object relations by Melanie Klein and furthered by Winnicott and Otto Kernberg, and the theory of self-psychology developed by Heinz Kohut. The affect of anger is also explored from the perspective of Carl G. Jung's "analytical psychology." Post-Jungian theory on development is drawn from Jungian theory by Erich Neumann and Michael Fordham and compared. Donald Kalsched's theory on trauma, the archetypal defenses, and aggression is explored in relation to anger.

    The work of several sandplay practitioners is reviewed in relation to understanding how anger is displayed in sandplay thorugh representations of symbols and archetypes of the collective unconscious, demonstrating how the psyche's symbolic language reveals anger, rage, and aggression in sandplay and allows its transformation. The concept that the developmental phases of clients and their anger,rage, and aggression are displayed in the symbols, archetypes, and defense mechanisms they use in sandplay. These same symbols and archtypes of fairy tales, dreams, and myth are the original prototypes of human behavior.
