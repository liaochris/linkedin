Gregg Zegarelli's Summary

Excellent client service. That is the reason my law firm has been in business for almost 25 years. Your prosperity is serious business.

I work with entrepreneurs and growing businesses, bringing energy, passion and enthusiasm to achieve client goals. During my 25 years as an attorney, I have represented a diverse mix of public and private companies, INC. 500 companies, startup and established, doing business horizontally through many industries. Moreover, because I litigate, arbitrate and mediate disputes, my advice and deal-making has a unique perspective for positioning clients through an entire deal life-cycle.

I have a superb clientele, and we keep it interesting.

I was lead counsel in Borings v. Google, the first case of its kind for Internet-related trespass. I achieved a successful overturn against Google at the United States 3rd Circuit Court of Appeals that resulted in the only final judgment against Google for *intentional* trespass regarding its Internet mapping.

I was also successful at the U.S. Patent and Trademark Office against Facebook overruling Facebook's motion to dismiss an opposition to the registration of "Facebook" as a brand.

With an accounting education, and years of legal and business experience, I am faculty for multiple quality organizations teaching attorneys and CPA’s (see below).

Core disciplines include mergers, branding and trademarks, intellectual property, commercialization strategies, joint ventures, corporate structuring, equity grants, contracts, social media and workplace rights and policies, real estate and wealth transfers, and raising capital.

Law exists in a practical framework. Because I have owned and operated my own law firm for almost 25 years, I have personally experienced the risks, challenges and rewards that are applicable to my clients. I bring my experience to you to achieve your goals. Seasoned experience.

Gregg Zegarelli's Publications

  • The Sunshine Act, 135 P.L.J. 39, 1988

    • Pittsburgh Legal Journal
    • 1988
    Authors: Gregg Zegarelli

    Authored while I was a Councilman. There was cause for me to argue that Council discussions were occurring in private, not at public meetings. To develop the authority for my argument, I researched the law and authored the article. Cited by Purdon's Statutes. Addresses the "open meeting" law whereby citizens have the right to observe elected officials making decisions, not "behind closed doors."

  • Computer Fraud and Abuse Act of 1986

    • MacMillan (Gale Group) Encyclopedia
    • 2004
    Authors: Gregg Zegarelli

    Technology tends to move a step ahead of the law. I was selected to write this article for inclusion in the encyclopedia because of my background in technology and law. The article addressed the need for new federal laws to meet the new risks of pervasive technologies, more particularly, computer viruses.

  • ONE®: The Unified Gospel of Jesus

    • OUG Press, Ltd.
    • November 30, 2006
    Authors: Gregg Zegarelli

    I wrote this book following recognition that none of the four Gospels contains the entire testimony of Jesus. Being an attorney, by some compulsion, I unified the testimonies for presentation, authoritatively documented with a 3,000 item index that tracks the process back to each verse of the extant works. Now cited as a reference and used as a framework for other works.

  • U.S. Supreme Court Petition: Borings v. Google

    • [.pdf format may take a moment to download]
    • June 1, 2010
    Authors: Gregg Zegarelli

    I served as counsel for plaintiffs in the Google trespass/invasion of privacy case for its Street View program. The original dismissal was overturned by the U.S. Third Circuit and resulted in a judgment against Google for *intentional* trespass. When filed in April, 2008, this case was the first of its kind. This is the Preamble to the U.S. Supreme Court Petition regarding privacy in our new world.
