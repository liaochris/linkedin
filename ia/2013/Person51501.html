Hannah Chung's Summary

Hannah Chung is the Co-founder and Chief Creative Officer of Sproutel, creator of Jerry the Bear – an interactive teaching toy for children diagnosed with type 1 diabetes. Sproutel was one of "Top 5 Startups that Use Tech To Do Good" by Mashable.

She illustrates everything from ideas to business model canvases. She focuses on the character development, and manufacturing, making sure that the product is intuitive to the users.

Hannah also co-founded Design for America when she was a Mechanical Engineering and Design freshman student at Northwestern University. Here, she found her passion–designing for social impact. DFA is an award-winning national initiative whose mission is to create a network of student-led studios that generates local and social impact through interdisciplinary design.

In 2012, Hannah was listed as one of "Top 15 Women to Watch in Tech" by Inc. She is currently located in Providence, RI.

Hannah Chung's Experience

Sproutel

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Health, Wellness and Fitness industry

January 2012 – Present (2 years) Providence, Rhode Island Area

Sproutel creates interactive games for children with a chronic illness. Our first product is Jerry the Bear, Jerry the Bear, helps kids with type 1 diabetes master their medical procedures - from monitoring glucose levels to maintaining a healthy diet. Using games, positive reinforcement, and real world scenarios, Jerry allows children to learn the skills necessary to take care of themselves for the rest of their lives.

Mashable listed Sproutel as one of "5 Startups to Use Tech To Do Good" in 2012.
Sproutel received "People's Choice Award" at Health 2.0 SF in 2012.

Along with Mashable, Sproutel was featured in GOOD, Chicago Tribune, Huffington Post, MIT Technology Review, Inc, NPR, USA Today, INC, and many others.

Co-founder, National Board Advisor

Design for America

Partnership; Myself Only; Design industry

February 2009 – Present (4 years 11 months)

Design for America is an award-winning national initiative using design with the mission to be a network of student-led studios creating local and social impact through interdisciplinary design.
DFA teaches human centered design to young adults and collaborating community partners and implements innovative solutions based on insights into human needs, rapid prototyping, user-testing, and interdisciplinary team work. Design for America currently addresses national challenges in education, health, and environment.

Founded at Northwestern University in 2009 we now have 18 studios across the country with over 4,000 students in our network and 350 core team members. DFA is a pipeline of innovators, change makers, and social entrepreneurs.
This year, two of our start-ups SwipeSense and Sproutel have gone on to raise nearly $1.5 million in start-up capital, with more projects in the pipeline ready to make an impact.
