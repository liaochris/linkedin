Clive Zickel's Summary

I am a Partner at B2B CFO®. We take companies to a higher level of success with our unique, proven six-step process – the GamePlan™.

B2B CFO® is the USA's Largest CFO Firm™.

Every company, regardless of its size, needs a Chief Financial Officer. You can now afford a CFO with B2B CFO®

We serve owners of emerging and mid-market companies with revenues up to $75 million who want to increase cash, profitability, sales and company value.

OUR SPECIALITIES: Cash Flow Issues, Accounting Problems, Banking and Lending Relationships, The Gameplan

MY BACKGROUND:
I have 38 years of financial and operations management, including 10 years in public accounting in Europe , 20 years in key financial management positions in Europe and the United States at a major International Bank, Chief Financial Officer at an international law firm, plus 12 years as a company founder, entrepreneur and advisor to many start ups and growing companies, adjunct professor at Stony Brook University Graduate School of Business and Loyola Marymount University.

With core values of Honesty, Integrity and Objectivity, I am a trusted, long-term business advisor bringing Financial & Goal Clarity and Increased Profit & Cash to clients (as an independent contractor and not an officer, employee or agent of, or partner or joint venturer with, the companies I serve, nor am I an independent CPA to them).

I am a Fellow of the Institute of Chartered Accountants in England & Wales, graduate of the Harvard Business School Program for Management Development and graduate of the Executive MBA Program at Stony Brook University Graduate School of Business.

MY SPECIALTIES
Early stage business planning and funding, financial and general management advice, business modeling, financial management systems, performance measurement,business intelligence, balanced scorecard, business process improvement, exit planning and execution.

In 1986, the firm founded Urbach Hacker Young International, which is now an international network of more than 45 independent member firms with Hacker Young as the primary UK member. Chantrey Vellacott DFK

Partnership; 201-500 employees; Accounting industry

1972 – 1977 (5 years)

Hill Vellacott

It is believed that Hill Vellacott was the only accountancy firm that could trace its ancestry back 200 years. Based on this history, Chantrey Vellacott DFK is regarded by the Institute of Chartered Accountants in England and Wales as one of the five oldest firms in the country.

In 1788 Edward Thomas Jones set up in business and his grandson later went into partnership with Arthur J Hill and William Edward Vellacott. Hill was taken into the partnership in 1867 and Vellacott in 1878. It was not until 1967 that the firm became known as Hill Vellacott.

Arthur Hill’s son, Lancelot, became very involved with the Council of the Institute of Chartered Accountants and served as president twice. During the 1930’s Sir Henry Peat who later went on to Peat Marwick and David McClintock of KMG Thomson McClintock, both served their articles with Hill Vellacott.

By 1967 Hill Vellacott had offices in London, Birmingham, Canterbury, Croydon and a presence in Northampton and Leicester. At this time their London offices were at Hanging Sword House, Whitefriars.

Hill Vellacott merged with Chantrey Wood King in 1988 and both firms moved into Russell Square House as the firm of Chantrey Vellacott.
