David Wood's Summary

I have spent 25 years envisioning, architecting, implementing, supporting, and avidly using smart mobile devices, including ten years with PDA manufacturer Psion PLC, then ten more with smartphone operating system specialist Symbian Ltd.

My background includes: many years building and integrating UI system software and application frameworks in 16-bit and 32-bit versions of “EPOC” software (later named “Symbian OS”); growing and directing the technical consulting teams that worked with leading phone manufacturers to create the world’s first successful smartphones; and defining and running development programs to stimulate and nurture the Symbian partner ecosystem.

From the first half of 2008, I was involved in preparations and planning for the independent open source Symbian Foundation. I served on the Leadership Team of the Symbian Foundation as Catalyst & Futurist until October 2009. I continue these same roles from within Delta Wisdom:

*) As 'Catalyst', my task is to enable communities to discover and explore innovative solutions for the many challenges and opportunities posed by disruptive changes in technology and society;
*) As 'Futurist', my task is to distil compelling visions of the future of technology, business, and society – visions that provide the energy and inspiration for deeply productive open collaboration.

I have an MA in Mathematics from Cambridge University and an honorary doctorate in science from the University of Westminster.

My first book was published in 2005: "Symbian for software leaders - principles of successful smartphone development projects".

In 2009 I was included in T3's list of "100 most influential people in technology".

In 2010 I featured in the world's first Augmented Reality CV: http://arcv.cwjobs.co.uk/augmented_reality_cv

Outside of work, I am chair of the London Futurists – a meetup organisation with over 1500 members.

Chair

London Futurists

Nonprofit; 1-10 employees; Research industry

March 2008 – Present (5 years 7 months)

London Futurists is a meetup dedicated to serious discussion of the likely impact of technology on human experience. The acceleration of technology means that the near future may bring radical changes to all of us. Major developments in technologies like anti-aging, nanotech, synthetic biology, personalised genetics, computing, robotics, and geo-engineering are going to make the next few years very exciting - and possibly also very dangerous. At one extreme, we might witness a "singularity" convergence of new technologies, via the emergence of artificial intelligence that significantly exceeds human capability and comprehension.

To make the best of these opportunities, we arguably need a new guiding philosophy - known as "Humanity+".

Since March 2008 I have been arranging for interesting speakers to make presentations at our meetings, and chairing these meetings.

By May 2013, the membership of London Futurists reached 1,500. See http://www.meetup.com/London-Futurists/.

Technology Planning Lead

Accenture

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; ACN; Management Consulting industry

June 2010 – March 2013 (2 years 10 months)

As Technology Planning Lead / CTO for Accenture Mobility, my role was to provide technology foresight: to ensure that Accenture Mobility could take best advantage of fast-evolving technology, and that the technology perspective was properly represented in Leadership Team deliberations.

I oversaw a network of more than 40 technical authorities, spanning every technology discipline, product domain and geographical region. Via the Technology Planning Review (TPR) process that I initiated, this network operated as a community of best practice that collectively originated, evaluated, and prioritised ideas for investments to develop or enhance Mobility offers.

I also co-led Accenture’s Mobility Health business initiative, acting as technology coach, in the fast-moving intersection between two of the world’s most powerful and significant industries – Health and Mobility.  Accenture’s Mobility Health offers cover strategy consulting, application development and maintenance, device and platform engineering, managed services, and workflow transformation.
