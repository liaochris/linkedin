Liliana Matos' Projects

  • Integrative Design Studio: Cultures of Play

    • January 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Liliana Matos, Chris McDonnell

    Currently engaged in a research-based design course / interaction design studio that explores how "play" facilitates cultural exchange and social interaction.

    I am part of a three person team asked to re-imagine LEGO's charitable activity and study the concept of "play". At the present time, LEGO collects used LEGO bricks from families, cleans and polishes them, and sends them packed in LEGO Charity Boxes to communities around the globe. Many of these communities have never before heard of or interacted with the LEGO system. Research by the LEGO Learning Institute indicates that the recipients of these donated materials tend play with the LEGO bricks independently. We are designing collaborative objects and experiences that encourage "necessarily social" play.

    After the creation of a number of prototypes our team created a finalized test-ready prototype. We packaged the design within a standard LEGO Charity Box and prepared for a one week design ethnography in Turkey.

    Between March 3 and March 10 my team tested our design with children ages 5 to 7 in both a home context and a school context in Ankara, Turkey and Eskisehir, Turkey. Our testing methods included free play with LEGO bricks (as part of their first introduction to LEGO; something they had never before played with), play with our collaborative construction object, and design exercises with our object.

    The project was extensively documented with photos, videos, notes. More on this to come.

    Our design was highly successful in facilitating collaborative play. Our group hopes that the design will have a positive impact on:
    *The development social and collaborative skills in young children, particularly those ages 5 to 7
    *Fostering creativity and imagination
    *Alternative construction logics, display possibilities, and creation preservation techniques
    *Cost-effective solutions to problems in the fields of Education and Play
