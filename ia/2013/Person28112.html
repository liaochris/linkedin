  • Communication and hearing protection for airboating: A review of best practices

    • Airboating Magazine
    • September 2013
    Authors: Chris Hawley, Julie Steding, Michael Walsh

    Clear communication is essential to creating a safe, effective, and productive work environment. Unfortunately, excessive levels of background noise often make clear communication difficult or impossible. An estimated 22 million Americans are exposed to hazardous levels of workplace noise each year — four million receive constant exposure every day. If you work on or around airboats, you are already one of those four million.

    Prolonged exposure to excessive noise leads to a condition known as “noise-induced hearing loss” (NIHL). NIHL is the most common work-related illness in the United States, costing an estimated $242 million in workers’ compensation annually. Even worse, NIHL is often difficult to detect until it’s too late. Hearing loss typically takes place slowly and painlessly over years of exposure. Ironically, while NIHL is generally considered to be 100 percent preventable, it is also 100 percent incurable. Once hearing has been lost to excessive noise exposure, there is no way to “undo” the damage. . . .

  • "Artisan Dental Laboratory: The art and skill of dental prosthetics."

    • Doctor of Dentistry magazine
    • September 2008
    Authors: Chris Hawley

    Before there were factories and assembly lines there were people known as artisans: craftsmen who dedicated their lives to creating beautiful and functional works of art. “Artisan” is derived from the Italian words for “art” and “skill” — precisely what Karl Koch and his partners had in mind when they founded Artisan Dental Laboratory in 1973.

    “We chose the name Artisan because that’s how we wanted our work to be known,” Karl said. “Artisans are meticulous craftsmen who pride themselves on perfection. We work hard to maintain that tradition of art and skill today — one case at a time.”

    That commitment to excellence has served the company well in its 35-year history. With nearly 100 employees and a 40 percent growth in business over the last four years, Artisan is one of the largest and most successful dental laboratories in the country.

    • Today's FDA

    Authors: Chris Hawley
