  • Visit Sweden

    • March 2012 to April 2012
    Team Members: Jonas Linder

    Type: Scamp Ad
    Medium: Print Ad + PR-event and Brochure
    My Role: Copywriter / Concept
    Brief: To attract Western Europeans 18–25 years to visit northern Sweden.

    This was an assignment during my course at Berghs SoC. I came up with the line: The Last European Wilderness, and created a concept with a bit of Into the Wild feeling. I also tried to write long copy for the first time. Since I always saw myself as more of a conceptual copywriter this was a big step for me. I’m actually pretty happy with the result and it’s a great feeling getting into the actual craft.

    In a later part of the course I chose to develop this further with a PR concept and a brochure for that concept.

    PR-Event / Strategy

    The plan is to show off threatened wildlife like the arctic fox, wolverines, the sea otter and other. This campaign will show off, first hand, what we are about to lose. Norrland might be the last European wilderness and some of the unique wildlife is almost lost. The tour will travel Europe and show the animals off in different Zoos around Europe.

    I chose this campaign because not only is it PR worthy, it's also relevant. New arrivals at big Zoos always get press, if it's for a limited time with a special message it should get even more. The last European wilderness, it's the last of it's kind and disappearing. Just like the animals that we will display and raise awareness for. I see this as a scalable campaign that can continue for years and even go outside Europe.

    For the events I created a brochure. The thought is that the bottom half of the first page will be see-through. So that you’ll really see how the fox disappears from the landscape when you open the brochure. A campaign site will back it up with more information on both the wildlife of the last European wilderness and the travels there.

  • Master Beat

    • September 2011 to October 2011
    Team Members: Jonas Linder, Elias Liedholm, Amina Kesraoui, August Levinson, Nils Helmersson

    Master Beat was the project I worked on during the Exploring Technology module on Hyper Island. We were given 700 swedish crowns (about $100) per group to get technology for and the next instruction was to use this technology and build something cool to show on an exhibition barely one month later.

    Our idea was to build a device that would allow you to play music only by pressing your fingers together. The original thought was to have guitar chords assigned to each finger to make it kind of like an air guitar. But we later on discovered that it was even more cool to have various loops and beats so that you could DJ in a whole new way. So we did that.

    My role: Circuit bender, I made the technology work for us.
