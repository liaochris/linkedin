Jim Riviello's Summary

Having worked with hundreds of companies across the USA, I have found most business leaders to be extremely successful people. They seem to have this natural ability to see things that others do not. They have a unique gift and set of skills that separate them from others. Yet, consistently something is missing like a piece of a puzzle they can’t find. On the surface, it may be a specific person to fill a required role or a skill-set to mask an inherent weakness. In many cases, the burning question of "how are we going to grow the business" ways heavily on their mind. Many know something needs to change, professionally and personally, but struggle to articulate what and how. This discovery sparked my true calling—a deeply rooted desire to help others find a better way—a sustainable way and why I founded the Leadership X University.

The Leadership X University provides business leaders with the programs and tools to drive sustainable change for themselves, their business and their network.

Through personal experience, I have found that the little, everyday decisions we make will either move us closer to the life we desire or take us off the beaten path, making it harder to find our way. With clarity and discipline, I believe anything is possible. Without these attributes, our desires become hope and hope has never been a successful strategy.

Please contact me if you or the organization you work for is tired of getting the same old results; tired of being frustrated and recognize there has to be a better way--a more sustainable way to achieve the results you desire.

General Electric

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; GE; Electrical/Electronic Manufacturing industry

June 1985 – May 1990 (5 years) Syracuse, New York Area

Early in my career, I was fortunate enough to work for what was in my opinion one of the greatest companies ever–General Electric. One of my favorite Jack Welch quotes from that era is, “An organization’s ability to learn, and translate that learning into action rapidly, is the ultimate competitive advantage.“ This laid the foundation for my interest in personal leadership. I became a firm believer that our desire to be a “willing student ” along with our ability to translate what we learn into action is the single greatest thing that separates those who are successful and those who are not. Being at GE at a time when Welch was in his prime was a remarkable learning experience.
