Director, Corporate Compliance (1 year Contract)

August 2005 – August 2006 (1 year 1 month)

The role of the position was to establish licenses, service level agreements and multiple layers of contacts with the Ministry of Energy, Ontario Energy Board, 93 electric utilities, 2 gas utilities and all other Energy Sector affiliates. Once these relationships were established, the role was to improve and/or stabilize our relationships with these entities in a competitive and often misunderstood direct selling environment (door-to-door).

Major achievement: As a start-up company no corporate policies and procedures were in place. With my direction, we were able to fully document a compliance program for an independent sales force of approximately 800. Along with this documentation a full monitoring and communication strategy was implemented that was so successful, the majority of the original strategy was implemented by the Ontario Energy Board as a standard for all other energy marketers.

Business Development Manager (Contract)

Responsible for recruiting commercial and residential sales representatives within the Energy Sector. This position involved overseeing the recruiting and hiring process, as well as the Administration/Payroll function that included OEB compliance.

Major achievement: Between July 2002 and December 2003, we successfully developed the most weekly number one sales representatives on both the commercial and residential platforms. Even with the strictest rules set out for OEB compliance, our office consistently was in the bottom 25% for overall complaints and the bottom 10% for severe complaints. I attribute this to strong leadership and training.

National Account Executive

Responsible for developing sales and marketing strategies for the "Companion" wireless product to large warehousing and manufacturing facilities across Canada.

Major achievement: I was responsible for managing the pilot project and later the national installations of the "Companion" wireless product into Home Depot stores. This project allowed for cost effective wireless communication between all Home Depot staff members within each store. Other projects included Toys 'R Us, Chrysler, and the Sky Dome.

Corporate Recruiter

DGS Inormation Consultants

October 1991 – January 1993 (1 year 4 months)

I was responsible for developing relationships with the Human Resources and Information Technology departments of large companies for the purpose of placing full-time and contractual Information Technology personnel.

Major achievement - In 1990 when Pizza Pizza was having union difficulties with their internal call centre, I approached their senior management with a solution which today is still in place. This solution was to de-centralize the call centre, thus allowing the originating call to be handled by the switch and be passed to the next available operator. This operator would work from a home-based office that was fully equipped with the necessary technology to produce the order and send it to the appropriate store. This solution resulted in a reduction in cost of order transactions that was a direct savings to the franchisee. My original proposal included the placement of the home-based staff, which would be made up of physically disabled personnel.
