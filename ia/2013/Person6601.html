Andre Durand's Summary

When I'm not running towards fear, I feed my passion for culture and technology at Ping Identity.

I am a walking example of how persistence can pay off. Said another way, I can be stubborn when I believe.

I've discovered the truth is often the opposite of conventional wisdom. Sometimes slower is faster, sometimes function follows form and sometimes to lead one must support.

With age I've become patient and with failure, I've become obsessed with improvement.

My purpose isn't a casual pursuit.

I make a lot of mistakes, only some of them twice.

I started my career as an "RPU - Revenue Producing Unit" at KPMG.

In 1993 I created Durand Communications with Bryan Field-Elliot.. While thoroughly exploring every conceivable business mistake, my team built some incredible technology dispite me, and we did quite well right up until the moment we did't. We sold the company to Webb Interactive in 1998 in what would symbolize for me the intersection of serendipity and fate. While I didn't retire from the transaction, I did land my wife, Kim Durand.

In 1999, I founded Jabber Inc. to commercialize the Jabber open source instant messaging project created by Jeremie Miller. Jabber was a great ride. Brilliant people, vision and product. The company was later sold to Cisco (2008) but it's tech lives on within Google Chat, iChat and Webex.

In 2002 I experienced my aha moment with identity and left Jabber to start Ping Identity.

After 11 years as Ping CEO, I'm proud to serve 300 PingIdentians & 900 enterprises, including 45 of the Fortune 100. Our mission: Enable & Protect Identity, Defend Privacy & Secure the Internet.

When I'm not thinking the future real, I'm spending time with my awesome wife and daughters, all quick to remind me they are the true bosses of the Durand clan.
