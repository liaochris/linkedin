Adam Hayes' Summary

After 29 years in customer-centric sales positions, addressing other’s needs has been absolutely second nature for me, whether it was the restaurant business, the automobile industry or now in Real Estate.

Little did I know that all of my negotiating and people skills would be required, in fact demanded, and just for the seemingly simple act of moving Dad into a Skilled Nursing Facility.

My wife and I spent 3 months and several thousand driving- miles dealing with concerns, repairs, cleanouts and other assorted loose ends.

Years later I thought, I’ve been in training my whole adult life for that Olympian task and nothing truly prepared me for the energy drain of helping Dad.

Additionally, as the father of a fantastic 17 year old son with special needs, my understanding of true negotiating has been honed with doctors, hospitals, health plans and school systems.

So, what I bring to the table is not only an “insider’s empathy” for clients and their families when faced with downsizing and moving, but also a high level of enthusiasm for making the transition as smooth as possible. For me this is visceral.

I have dedicated my work at Milestones Realty in my Mom's memory and I choose to work with seniors as I see this as doing the most good for a deserving and woefully under-served population. By keeping my client consistently at the center of the sales universe, I'm able act as the leader, guide and source of strength through the arduous and unglamorous downsizing process: hence forth our tag line: More Than Just Another Transaction.

Specialties: I am a walking billboard for collaboration and am happy to share my thoughts and resources to improve anyone's life. Humility is my hallmark and I'm fully committed to life-long learning in hope of sheparding my son, clients and even acquaintances through the ebb and flow of life.

WE UNDERSTAND IT'S NEVER EASY TO SELL THE FAMILY HOME.

The real estate industry overall is focused on statistics and numbers rather than on the people and reasons WHY they are selling their homes. Many elders have owned their homes for 30,40 or 50 years, raised their families there and the process of sorting through decades of family photos and possessions and all that they represent can be physically and emotionally daunting even though they will have a better quality of life by making the move.

I have the ability and resources to coordinate all of the people and services necessary to alleviate my clients' stress.

There are many milestones throughout life-the purchase of a first home, upgrading after having a baby, downsizing, etc. While Milestones Realty was founded as a result of my work with elder law attorney Judy Flynn, Milestones Realty is not limited to serving elders, we are a full service brokerage.
