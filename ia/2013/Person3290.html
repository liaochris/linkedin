Richard Alberg's Summary

I founded and ran PSL, a firm of occupational (I/O) psychologists that developed sophisticated assessment solutions. This involved psychometric expertise and advanced technology. In November 2006 PSL was acquired by Kenexa and I became Senior Vice President responsible for sales, marketing and strategy in Europe. I left Kenexa in 2008 upon completion of my earn-out.

Whilst I decided what I wanted to do next I joined a small consortium investing in a snacks and drinks distribution company that was in Administration. I became interim Chief Executive and helped the management team deal with the issues that had led to the failure of the company whilst also significantly cutting costs.

In December 2008 I took a few months off, something I had not been able to do in the first 20 years of my working life. I am now the founder CEO of Myworksearch Ltd (www.myworksearch.co.uk), an online service that helps an individual secure employment. We offer our services to employers, government, further and higher education institutions, welfare to work providers and vocational training organisations. Since launching in Q3 2009 we have provided our services to over 60,000 people and achieved over £3 million in sales. We have demonstrated that online employment assistance is an effective model and that it overcomes three of the major flaws of offline delivery, namely its lack of consistency, scalability and affordability.

We are delighted to have had our successes recognised through winning the following awards:

My Worksearch Ltd is a startup launched in June 2009. We have developed a comprehensive self-service online offering for the job-seeker. This includes e-learning materials on effective job-seeking, a comprehensive range of tools from CV building to vacancy aggregation, workflow to ensure efficiency and focus and reporting to provide progress tracking. Through using technology the service is massively scalable and can thus be provided at an affordable low cost.
