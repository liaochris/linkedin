Jonathan Sekerak's Projects

  • GoPro Quests - Brand Extension Case Study

    • January 2013 to March 2013
    Team Members: Jonathan Sekerak

    Humans have always been athletes. Born to push their limitations. As structures grew so did organized competitions. Gatherings would be held to witness human achievement. Live events turned into shared photographed memories. Videos added more depth to these past events but still were limited by sidelines. What was it like to be flipping, rushing, soaring against mother nature?

    The GoPro camera let everyone experience what most could not. It broke the division between athlete and enthusiast becoming the number one selling action sports camera. On the flip side, because it captured extreme moves, people began to think their life wasn’t ‘cool’ enough to capture.

    To expand the market from extreme athlete to active, athletic working professional, GoPro has created GoPro Quests: extreme sports vacations located at adventure hot spots around the globe.

    To launch GoPro Quests, professionals in multiple cities around the united states have encountered extreme sports installations during their lunch break. They had the opportunity to ‘rock climb’ a skyscraper, terrasail the sidewalk or bungee jump from the observation deck of their office building.

    The GoPro Quest's Basecamp - minimalist accommodations - differs depending on location. In Alaska members are greeted with an industrial rustic-style Basecamp. While in Costa Rica, participants stay in thatched-roof bungalows. During their stay, each day will bring a new guided extreme adventure captured by multiple go-pros and edited back at the Basecamp's AV studio.
