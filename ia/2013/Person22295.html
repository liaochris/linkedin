Charlie Pellerin's Summary

Inventor; Research Astrophysicist; Leader of the NASA team that built Hubble Space Telescope; Director, NASA Astrophysics; CU Business School Professor;Small-business President; Author of How NASA Builds Teams (Wiley, 2009); PMI Presenter; Team and Leader Developer, World-wide

My driving purpose (purpose is everything to me) is to improve technical team performance (and lower risk) by developing and supporting "4-D Network Providers," World-wide. (Well into my second million miles on United Airlines)

Specialties: Simultaneous development of leaders by managing the "Fifth Force," Team Social Context

Charlie Pellerin's Experience

NASA Headquarters

Government Agency; 10,001+ employees; Aviation & Aerospace industry

Research Astrophysicist

Charlie Pellerin's Patents

  • Two-Axis Fluxgate Magnetometer

    • United States Patent 3,571,700
    • Issued December 10, 1968
    Inventors: Charlie Pellerin, Mario Acuna

    Magentic sensor initially developed for sounding rocket aspect sensing. Received the Goddard Space Flight Center's highest patent-related monetary award. Co-inventor Mario Acuna subsequently flew this design on every planetary mission!

Charlie Pellerin's Publications

  • A Miniature Two-Axis Fluxgate Magnetometer

    • IEEE Transactions on Geoscience Electronics Volume GE-7, Number 4
    • October 10, 1969
    Authors: Charlie Pellerin, Mario Acuna

    I used the programming language "APL" to model and optimize the performance of a magnetometer that flew on many hundreds of Sounding Rockets, and nearly every mission to the planets.

  • Elemental Abundances in the August, 1974 Solar Flare

    • Solar Physics
    • April 7, 1975
    Authors: Charlie Pellerin

    I flew Sounding Rockets up the North Pole Magnetic filed lines onto one of the most intense solar flares in history. I measured the elemental abundances of the solar photoshpere with unprecedented precision, confirming that our Sun is totally ordinary.

  • Large Scale Galactic Magnetic Field Structures

    • Astrophysical Journal
    • April 3, 1975
    Authors: Charlie Pellerin, Many other co-authors

    We flew a large (size of a small car) electron-positron instrument across Canada (twice) suspended below a 50 mcf balloon during solar minimum at an altitude of 150,000 feet. Everything worked!

  • How NASA Builds Teams

    • Wiley, and Sons
    • June 3, 2008
    Authors: Charlie Pellerin
