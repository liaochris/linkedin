Lester Hightower's Summary

Multi-talented and experienced technology executive with many years of successful, diverse and progressive experience in positions such as CTO, Business Unit VP, VP of R&D, Director of Software Solutions, Manager of Information Systems, Technology Entrepreneur, and Lead Developer. Possesses a deep understanding of macro- and micro-economics, generally, and Software as a Service (SaaS) business models, specifically. Founded and grew RailDOCS, a prominent SaaS platform to the rail transportation industry that is now a property of GE Transportation Systems.

Proven abilities to plan objectives, determine strategies and implement methods; staff (recruit, hire, train and mentor); direct; organize; coordinate; and evaluate business and IT policies, projects and procedures. Exemplary oral, written, and presentation skills with the ability to communicate effectively with all levels of audience. A participating member of several rail industry trade associations and standards development organizations. Has authored numerous highly technical documents and industry white papers and presented to large public audiences.

Specialties

Software as a Service, Open Sourced Software, Railroading.

• SaaS: Helped found, grow, and sell a very successful SaaS business with both web and mobile technologies and per-transaction and per-user pricing models.
• OSS Technologies: Linux, Xen, Apache, Perl, Javascript, Python, PHP, MySQL, PostgreSQL, and many more.
• Railroading: Design, Build, Operate, and Maintain processes (DBOM) for Signals, Communications, Positive Train Control, Track, plus AEI and EOT devices and protocols.

10East Corp. / RMI (now GE Transportation Systems)

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Information Technology and Services industry

10East Corp. was a software as a service (SaaS) provider to the North American railroad industry providing services under the RailDOCS brand name. My role at ABC-NACO (NASDAQ:ABCR) was two-pronged: 1) I managed all IT for ABC-NACO's Rail Systems Division (~350 employees in 11 locations) and 2) I managed a startup, for-profit subsidiary of ABC-NACO called The AIMS Group, Inc. and served as its Vice President and the Chief Technology Officer until the assets of ABC-NACO were sold to a private equity firm through a bankruptcy court auction, after which 10East Corp. was formed from the assets and human resources of The AIMS Group, Inc.

VP and Manager of Information Systems

United Railway Signal Group

Privately Held; 501-1000 employees; Transportation/Trucking/Railroad industry

September 1995 – October 1997 (2 years 2 months) Jacksonville, Florida Area

United Railway Signal Group was an independent signal design contractor to the North American railroad industry. I joined URSG's team in 1995 as the Manager of Information and Communication Systems and ultimately became a Vice President. During this time, the software as a service business that became known as RailDOCS was born. URSG was sold to ABC-NACO (NASDAQ:ABCR) in October of 1997.

Sole Proprietor

Computation Integration Technologies

January 1995 – June 1996 (1 year 6 months) Tallahassee, Florida Area

CIT provided small and mid-sized companies with (mostly) Internet-related consulting and software development services. Most engagements involved doing substantially all of the "inside the wall" work of connecting companies to the Internet for the first time. In this time-frame, that often meant moving LANs from Banyan VINES, LANtastic, Netware IPX, etc. to TCP/IP, or overlaying TCP/IP. CIT routinely installed routers, firewalls, SMTP and POP/IMAP email servers, web and FTP servers, etc. Additionally, we did a lot of custom software development work, some web-site related, but much of it more business centric (Intra/Extranet).

Two notable and non-Internet-based projects were an emergency 911 call response software system and a jail management system, both done for the Sheriff Department of Gadsden County, Florida, on subcontract to Delphi Enterprises, and both designed to integrate with Delphi's public safety software named Code3.

In addition to myself, CIT had two part-time employees. CIT was wound down after I accepted employment with United Railway Signal Group.

Lead Developer of the WANE Project, and other assignments

The Supercomputer Computations Research Institute

Educational Institution; 5001-10,000 employees; Higher Education industry

January 1990 – November 1995 (5 years 11 months) Tallahassee, Florida Area

The Supercomputer Computations Research Institute (SCRI) was a federally funded supercomputer research center that was started during the Ronald Reagan presidency and housed on the campus of the Florida State University. Federal funds were lost during the Bill Clinton presidency and that ultimately led to SCRI's demise, with FSU taking over the facility and turning it into FSU's Department of Scientific Computing. I held several positions at SCRI and worked for many different research scientists. The last position held was as the Lead Developer of the Wide Area Network Environment project where the fledgling and open-sourced Linux operating system was enhanced to support very large numbers of simultaneous users on commodity x86 servers.

Part-time Instructor of Computer Technology

North Florida Christian School

August 1994 – December 1994 (5 months) Tallahassee, Florida Area

For half of a year (two semesters) I taught two computer science courses at the high-school level, five days per week and in the afternoons after attending my college classes at FSU. Those two courses were "Advanced Computer Programming" and "Introduction to Computer Graphics and Design." The advanced programming class was only for advanced students and was taught at a collegiate level. The computer graphics course was available to the general student population but we still accomplished very impressive results.
