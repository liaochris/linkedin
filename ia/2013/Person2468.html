Andrew Hill's Summary

Respectable.rebel

I had a good education and a successful career found me running my own consultancy firm in the early 1980s, primarily advising senior management on the intricacies of pension funds. Suits, smart offices, secretaries and a fondness for Switzerland may have had something to do with the respectable bit. Or maybe it was because I had, and still have, a tendency to open doors for ladies, get annoyed at people who throw litter in the street and paid more attention than most to grammar and spelling - what now seem terribly old-fashioned things.

civil.service

My work brought me into close contact with many civil servants, and officials in large institutions where I was quite incapable of hiding my views on the amazing amount of unecessary form-filling, procedures created almost for the sake of creating procedures and the quite extraordinary effort that had to be made to get someone to do something that was slightly different to the way that they had been doing it for years before. That's when I got the rebel bit. I made a pretty respectable living from translating jargon into something we can all understand without losing any of a document's specific legal intentions.

first.computer

Computers first entered my life in 1980 in the form of a Dictaphone Dual Display system which used massive 8" floppy, very floppy, disks. An Amstrad PCW helped me produce some short stories and novels in the early 1990s until I began lecturing on the wonders of Windows in 1995.

still.here

Now, in the 21st Century, I'm still lecturing at College and Middlesex University, chair a JISC RSC E-learning Forum and on the Academic Board of an FE College. Remarkably, I also survived five years secondment to NLN/LSDA/LSN as an E-learning Co-ordinator trying to make sense of government initiatives too. Now it's also dogs, design and writing on which I work ridiculously long hours - but I do thoroughly enjoy it all.

Goals? To help friends achieve theirs.

Specialties

I love BLT - brilliant learning technology (or I suppose I'd better add e-learning and ILT to catch the tags). See my new web tools site at http://andrewx.com/webtools

Web design - a growing portfolio of interesting clients in a world where a flair for making words and images work well together is useful

Writing - as well as my own novels, if you write something I'm happy to edit it and make it even better!

Stamps - expert in GB Machin definitives. Sad but true.

Andrew Hill's Experience

Consultant

Learning and Skills Improvement Service

Government Agency; 51-200 employees; Professional Training & Coaching industry

June 2012 – Present (1 year 3 months) Astcote, Towcester, Northants

Development of a Third Sector Toolkit and encouraging organisations to trial a range of web tools at selected stages of The Learner Journey.

From initial discussions with interested representatives from this sector I have worked with an LSIS team to create a web site providing information and guidance in how a range of free and mostly ad-free applications could be utilised by teachers, administrative staff and students.

Organisations have been invited to apply to trial this and a selection will be offered a grant to cover staff time and resources during March 2013 when it is hoped they will be illustrating and sharing their progress on a blog I have set up at http://educationS3.blogspot.co.uk

The applications and support material can be seen at http://andrewx.com/webtools and there is also a version of parts of this on the LSIS Excellence Gateway.

These are small projects and designed to be simple both to participate in and report progress with, participants generally being staff who may not be familiar with new technology or how it can impact upon their activities and for whom grants and support may not have reached before. It should be fascinating to see just what can be achieved by the individuals taking part and the extent to which they feel inspired and more confident to continue using some of these tools in future.

This is me in one of my favourite fields of activity so, whilst there may not be any more money available from LSIS, if anyone would like to take a closer look at what can be achieved I'd be happy to make suggestions and advise options.
