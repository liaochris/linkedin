Carla Lynne Hall's Experience

"Internet for Artists" Course Leader

Creative Capital

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Philanthropy industry

April 2011 – Present (2 years 9 months)

The Internet for Artists workshop helps participants expand their online presence and learn to harness the power of the internet.

The workshop explores how internet resources can be used to build audience, expand community, amplify marketing and extend administrative resources. Participants develop a step-by-step holistic strategy for using a range of internet applications to achieve specific career goals.

Additionally, the workshop will help artists quickly recognize and implement the possibilities of new technologies as they develop. This workshop is designed for groups of up to 24 artists.

Workshop Leader

Workshop in Business Opportunities

Nonprofit; 1-10 employees; Higher Education industry

February 2011 – Present (2 years 11 months)

Workshop in Business Opportunities (WIBO ) is a private non-profit organization that supports men and women with the drive to become successful entrepreneurs.

WIBO 's mission is to enable small business owners and budding entrepreneurs from under-served communities to obtain financial success by starting, operating, and building successful businesses that develop economic power, provide jobs and improve communities.

WIBO offers a myriad of services including its flagship 16-week workshop, How to Build a Growing Profitable Business. Other services include personalized business coaching, legal and accounting clinics, monthly seminars, networking events, and other support services. Supporters include Price Foundation, Bank of New York Mellon, Citigroup Foundation, and New York Community Trust.

Blogger & Podcaster

Rock Star Life Lessons

November 2007 – Present (6 years 2 months) New York, NY

Rock Star Life Lessons is the blog aimed at music entrepreneurs interested in creating a business along with their musical practice.

The Rock Star Life Lessons podcast features interviews with music luminaries such as Mary J Blige, Tina Knowles (Beyonce's mom), rapper Eve, and Kelly Rowland, as well as successful music business people such as Steven Zelin, "The Singing CPA"

Owner

Moxie Entertainment

February 1998 – Present (15 years 11 months)

Moxie Entertainment is an independent record label which has released four CDs, plus an additional CD released under a subsidiary.

Moxie Entertainment is also the umbrella company that oversees Soulflower Selections, a music publishing company under ASCAP (American Society for Composers and Publishers).

Moxie Publishing released "The DIY Guide to the Music Biz" by Carla Lynne Hall in 1999. Moxie Entertainment also organizes performance booking, music business speaking and writing engagements for principal, Carla Lynne Hall
