Keith's Story:

Keith's career started at an early age in the rural Eastern Oregon town of Helix—population 150. He identified his first business opportunity when the town’s only store started selling fresh sandwiches. His negotiations with the store’s owner landed him a contract to provide her with fresh homemade chocolate chip cookies to be sold at the new deli counter. Keith then invested the profits from his cookie business into purchasing a used lawnmower, which he used to build a respectable yard-care business. His work ethic and reputation then earned him the job of maintaining his local Little League field. But the ‘local’ field wasn’t actually in Helix. It was 9 miles away in the next closest town. Keith rode his bike both ways to mow the field on Saturdays, while playing on the same field during the week for Helix’s Little League team. When Keith graduated from Junior High School, he had already owned and operated two successful businesses for more than three years. By the time he reached young adulthood, his drive to succeed had given him experience as a screen-printer, baker, truck driver, home loan processor, janitor, school bus driver, and even railroad conductor. However, after surviving a head-on collision with another train, Keith made the life-changing decision to return to the entrepreneurial roots of his youth—it was this pivot in Keith’s life that really marked the beginning of his career in sales.
