Jon Bischke's Summary

Over the past dozen years I've been a part of the founding team of five companies, three of which have been acquired (two by publicly traded companies) and one that has been bootstrapped to profitability. Our current company, Entelo is building powerful tools to help growing companies better discover talent.

Specialties

CEO/Founder

eduFire

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; E-Learning industry

February 2007 – June 2010 (3 years 5 months)

I co-founded eduFire. eduFire was a pioneer in the MOOC (massive open online course) movement. The company raised ~$2MM from high profile investors such as Battery Ventures, Presidio Ventures and early Google and Yahoo! employees and hosted over 10,000 live classes in 2009. In June of 2010 eduFire.com was acquired by Camelback Education Group, a higher education holding company based in Phoenix, Arizona.

Founder

LearnOutLoud.com

January 2005 – February 2007 (2 years 2 months)

I founded LearnOutLoud.com, an online portal for audio and video educational content. LearnOutLoud.com has over 25,000 titles including audio books, podcasts and streaming lectures in areas such as history, philosophy, religion and self-development. LearnOutLoud.com has not raised outside capital and became profitable in 2007.

President/Founder

Zaadz

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Internet industry

January 2004 – February 2007 (3 years 2 months)

I was a co-founder and early investor in Zaadz, a social networking company focused on using technology and online community to change the world. The company raised ~$3MM from investors such as John Mackey (Founder of Whole Foods) and billionaire Sam Wyly. Zaadz was acquired by Gaiam, a publicly traded company, in 2007.

CEO/Founder

The 2000Tutor.com Network

September 1998 – March 2003 (4 years 7 months)

I founded The 2000Tutor.com Network in September of 1998 and sold The 2000Tutor.com Network to Penton Media, a publicly traded company, in January of 2001. I was retained as a consultant to Penton Media after the acquisition was complete and continued to grow the 2000Tutor.com network of sites (renamed CertTutor.net) until my departure in 2003.
