Julie Rabinowitz's Publications

  • The Use of Condensation Symbols to Develop Presidential Leadership

    • Master’s thesis. University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill
    • August 1997
    Authors: Julie Rabinowitz

    Within the discipline of rhetoric, presidential rhetoric has become a significant area of study. Not only do presidents of the United States influence discussions of public policy, they both symbolize and wield the power of the U. S. government domestically, and the power of the state internationally. My interest in the presidency has increasingly focused upon the relationships between the symbol use of a president and the development of presidential leadership and authority. This thesis explores this intersection as it is manifested in Bill Clinton’s use of Franklin Delano Roosevelt as a condensation symbol for presidential leadership and vision.
