Dan Grossman's Summary

Dan has been building and investing in successful technology companies for the past 15 years.

Most recently, Dan has operated an advisory business, providing capital and advice to early stage companies.

Prior to that, Dan served as a portfolio manager for several hedge funds, ultimately responsible for a $1 billion portfolio of global technology companies (after beginning with $25m) as one of two managers covering the tech sector.

Dan spent six years at Venrock, the venture capital arm of the Rockefeller family office. During that time, he worked closely with founders and management teams to develop disruptive products and scale their companies through periods of high growth. He joined as a Kauffman Fellow.

Dan was previously a board director or observer at Inrix, Teranetics, Spotlight Solutions/Profit Logic (acquired by Oracle), White Amber (acquired by Taleo), Lab Velocity (acquired by Infotrieve), MaXXan, and Neoconix, among other private companies. He is currently a board director at Friends of Island Academy and the Partnership for Palliative Care.

Before Venrock, Dan spent three years at McKinsey & Company, including thirteen months helping to launch a then-new location in Jakarta, Indonesia, and six months in Sydney, Australia.
