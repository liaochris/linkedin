Mike Potter's Summary

HumdingerPGH.com

"Digital Since 1995 • Creative Since Always"

The Elevator Pitch: I'm a Digital Creative Director with 18+ years of experience, brilliant original ideas, stellar experience and, oh yeah, an MBA. I'm driven to inspire users, thrill clients, build brands and push envelopes mercilessly.


Full bio: In the world of digital marketing and advertising, I like to think I'm a true veteran thanks to my 18+ years of experience. In 1995, I was the 4th employee hired by Y&R's fledgling New Technologies group, where I worked on accounts such as Dr Pepper, 7UP, Molson Golden, Advil, The U.S. Army and Sony Electronics. I also notably led UX development for the first two USPS e-commerce ventures.

In 1999, I became ACD, Interactive at Margeotes | Fertitta, where I spent almost 3 years working on Bombay Sapphire, Condé Nast, CNA Insurance, McGraw-Hill, Stolichnaya Vodka and Godiva. Thanks to a lucrative freelance opportunity to design and build a digital project for Forbes magazine, I took off on my own in 2001, working for agencies such as J. Walter Thompson (NY) and Y&R Irvine, as well as numerous corporate clients like Paramount Classics and The Hollywood Reporter.

In 2007, I joined The Cimarron Group in Los Angeles where, as Digital Creative Director, I developed record-setting campaigns for the multi-billion dollar Twilight franchise, helped win Mitsubishi's North American advertising account for Cimarron's Traffic start-up and saw Cimarron Digital grow from 3 to almost 30 employees.

The birth of my niece and a desire to be closer to family led me back east in January 2011, where I first worked at Smith Bros. (who I'd been freelancing with starting in 2010) as Digital Creative Director and then as a freelance creative at local agencies like This Is Red and LA-based agency Addison Interactive.

Since November 2012, I've been freelancing with 2009 and 2011 AdAge Agency of the Year mcgarrybowen, helping out on their JPMorgan Chase and United clients.
