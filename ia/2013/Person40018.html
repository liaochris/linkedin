Marc Effron's Summary

Marc Effron helps companies to radically simplify their talent management practices.

Marc has consulted to the world's largest and most successful companies across industries and continents. He consults using the One Page Talent Management methodology, which emphasizes simplicity, transparency and accountability. His innovations in the field include Talent Philosophy, Success Models, Rapid Cycle Design and Experience Maps among others.

As a speaker to HR and executive audiences worldwide, he provides clear, inspirational and practical advice on talent issues.

Marc's prior experience includes serving as VP, Talent Management for Avon Products, and starting and leading the Global Leadership Consulting Practice at Hewitt Associates, where he created the iconic Top Companies for Leaders study. Marc was also SVP, Leadership Development for Bank of America, Director of Organization Effectiveness and Learning for Oxford Health Plans, and a compensation consultant for a global consulting firm. He previously served as a political consultant and a congressional staff assistant.

His latest book is One Page Talent Management: Eliminating Complexity, Adding Value (Harvard Business Press) co-authored by Miriam Ort and Harvard's best-selling talent management book. He co-authored Leading the Way, co-edited Human Resources in the 21st Century, has written chapters in eight management and leadership books and numerous professional journals.

Marc is widely quoted in the business media on talent issues and is a sought after speaker at industry events. He has been recognized as one of the Top 100 Influencers in HR. He earned a M.B.A. from the Yale School of Management and a B.A. degree in Political Science from the University of Washington.

In 2007, Marc founded the New Talent Management Network (http://www.newtmn.com/), a non-profit networking and research organization that is now the world's largest talent management organization with more than 2,700 members.
