Kim Noele Brice's Summary

I started my consulting practice in June 2006 in order to pursue advising, coaching, advocacy and journalism projects. I am particularly interested in working with “drivers of change” and in implementating and evaluating “change” projects locally and internationally.

For the past 20 years I have worked as an activist, journalist, manager and organisational and personal advisor dedicated to combating injustice in societies and unlocking potential.

I have served in middle and high management positions for an array of international organisations working in the fields of freedom of expression, human rights and good governance. I have worked for organisations as varied as the Committee to Protect Journalists, the United Nations and the Open Society Institute. I have experience working both at corporate headquarters and in the field in over a dozen countries, many of them in or emerging from conflict and in the midst of turbulent political change. I speak English, French, and Dutch fluently and have written extensively for international publications and for NGOs.

My experience includes research and campaigning on freedom of expression and human rights issues; establishing the Southern and West African branches of the Open Society Institute; managing multi-million Euro grant-giving programmes and developing and overseeing politically sensitive media projects. During the last few years, I have developed a growing interest and experience in evaluation, organisational learning and leadership development.

Whether I’m supporting others in their projects or serving as a team leader, I bring an entrepreneurial spirit and a results-driven attitude to what I do. I find I am able to cut to the core of what is required and quickly ascertain patterns and trends. I love embarking on new ventures and working in multi-cultural contexts.
