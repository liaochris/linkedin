Lisa Sams MSN, RNC's Summary

In 29 years I moved 23 times for my husband's Air Force career...and at one point held six positions in nine years. These transitions taught me that variations in how people received care in the hospital were often arbitrary. Today we know from research that variations drive up cost and can even harm patients. When I landed in the DC area and worked with AWHONN, the world of "evidence based medicine' was becoming more prominent and through multiple opportunities I began to learn how dramatically the work environment affects patient care. I hope this sheds light on my passion to see evidence informed care offered to all our patients....and on my varied career path.

That said:

Did you know the urinary catheter infects 440,000 people every year because it is not removed in <48 hours? Or that many hospital patients who received the wrong medication realized there was a problem but were too afraid to say anything?

When infections and errors happen, it costs the hospitals money and, most important, the patients pay a price with their health. The same can be said for a host of other common safety issues such as bedsores, falls and confusion about care following hospital discharge.

We must create an environment where patients and their families feel comfortable speaking up about a potential problem. In fact, we must go beyond that—hospital staff, patients and their families must begin partnering together to ensure the best possible hospital stay.

I also created Your Hospital Care, Expect the Best so busy hospital staff can quickly encourage and give patients the tools to get involved. The guidebook features user-friendly information about basic prevention practices for the problems listed above and more—including questions patients should ask and tips for working with their care team. The guidebooks can be easily customized to meet your needs.

Lisa Sams MSN, RNC's Publications

  • Your Hospital Care: Expect the Best A Guidebook for Patients and Families

    • Clinical Linkages Press
    • August 2010
    Authors: Lisa Sams MSN, RNC

    The guidebook offers a wealth of tips, charts, checklists and worksheets to help patients and families navigate life after hospital admission. It tells you what to look for and questions to ask about 19 common issues such as managing pain, preventing infections, falls, medication errors and more. Colorful and spiral bound it is easy to use between patients and the clinical team.

    Being a "good patient" who is afraid to ask questions and leaves all the decisions to others is outdated and dangerous way to think. Research shows patients and families who actively work with the clinical team to get best care are not only less anxious during the hospitalization--they also have shorter, more comfortable stays.
