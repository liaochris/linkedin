Chandlee Bryan's Summary

As a child, my answer to the question "What do you want to be when you grow up?" was always the same: "I have a lot of time to think about it."

As an adult, I've made a career out of helping people answer this question. This has been my primary work interest since my first job at the American Institute of Aeronautics and Astronautics – where I discovered that rocket scientists (aerospace engineers) and English majors ask the same questions about careers and life. Since that time, I’ve earned an M.Ed. in Counseling and have obtained certifications as a resume writer and career coach. I've worked as a career advisor, recruiter and as the “resume writing specialist” for a national Microsoft campaign for new college graduates. I’ve also led one of New York City’s largest job clubs, the NYC Job Seekers Meetup Group and am a co-author of the Twitter Job Search Guide. I enjoy talking to employers and researching industry trends to learn about careers and jobs in high demand -- and sharing information about the training and education that it takes to fill openings.

I currently work as a career advisor at Dartmouth College. In my spare time, I'm an avid knitter and work as a freelance writer and personal storyteller for a small number of private clients. I also facilitate workshops and webinars on job search strategy, social media and reputation management.

Enough about me. To borrow a line from my favorite poet Mary Oliver, “Tell me, what do you plan to do with your one wild and precious life?”

Specialties: Community Management, Writing, Certified Professional Resume Writer, Career Coach, Social Media and the Job Search, Public Speaking, Training
