The Caregiver’s Voice is the independent voice for family and professional caregivers of adults with brain impairment or dementia caused by Alzheimer’s, stroke, related illness, or trauma.

Founded in 1998 by Brenda Avadian, MA, The Caregiver's Voice mission is to bring hope and strength to caregivers of adults with brain impairment through knowledge, support, and humor.

Brenda Avadian, MA's Education

University of Wisconsin-Milwaukee

MA, BA, Communication & Business

1976 – 1982

As a Wisconsin native, I wanted nothing more than to go to Hollywood to be an actress. After enrolling in the Professional Theatre Acting Program at UW-Milwaukee, I realized I was too young (just turned 17) and too much of a ham. I turned to Communications--figuring I could still perform, but be myself.
What a tough program--yet a solid Midwestern education that still inspires me to reach higher while keeping grounded. The business courses I took in graduate school with my organizational communications specialty have been key in my success for many years as a leadership and communications consultant to corporations across America.
It's not so much education we receive, but what we do with the education we receive.
THANK YOU, UWM Letters & Science faculty.
