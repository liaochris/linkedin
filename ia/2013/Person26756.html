Connye Griffin's Summary

I thrive upon facilitating excellence in myself and in others. I am attentive to the talents, strengths, and needs of others. I enjoy helping others believe in those talents, hone their strengths, and meet their needs.

To this end, I have successfully collaborated in developing ideas, reshaped and edited drafts, and refined messages, both written and spoken. Such coaching has contributed to others' receiving opportunities for continual growth and to personal satisfaction, including my own.

In 37 years, I have worked effectively with students ranging in age from 15 to 60 in public schools, two and four-year colleges, and individual tutorials. I have also earned the respect of colleagues in all these venues.

Specialties

In the words of more than one colleague, I have inspired others to become "better" teachers and writers. Another described me as "the electric company," making the light of others shine.

Teacher

Moore High School

August 1989 – May 2008 (18 years 10 months)

In 1989, I began my secondary career as the Speech and Debate teacher and coach. In 1991, sophomore and junior English became my full-time assignment. In 1992, Advanced Placement English literature for seniors was added to my record of courses taught and developed. Soon, however, I taught only seniors in on-level English and Advanced Placement. In my final three years at Moore High, I was responsible for instructional leadership as department chair.
