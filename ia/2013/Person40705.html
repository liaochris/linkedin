Elizabeth Kanna |Business Producer |'s Summary

Elizabeth Kanna is a widely recognized business "producer" currently restaging one of America¹s greatest icons, Joe DiMaggio, into a movement with social impact.

Kanna transformed multi-million dollar funded research into a new standard for business practices being adopted by Microsoft, Intel, P&G and other billion-dollar powerhouses. Partnering with Dun & Bradstreet, JetBlue, Hilton, and The Grammy Foundation, Kanna created multimedia business makeover contests that took winning companies to the next level.

She has created and marketed New York Times, Wall Street Journal and Business Week best-selling books, and showcased her virtual schooling book at Apple stores across the country.

She has been featured on TV networks, including Fox, ABC, CBS, CNBC, CW, and in many magazines and newspapers, including Forbes, Wall Street Journal, New York Post, Kiplinger’s Personal Finance, U.S. News and Review and Woman’s Day.

As an emeritus advisor at an annual elite business retreat since 2003, Kanna has consulted with more than 500 business owners on transforming their companies. She has also consulted and held advisory roles for world-renowned scientists Sally Ride and Bob Ballard.

She is co-author of two market-defining books on virtual learning (first book ever written for parents on virtual schooling) and homeschooling and served as school board president for a public virtual school.

Gifted in seeing the unseen, Kanna has a knack for giving shape and weight to amorphous concepts. She has expertise in devising unique strategies and reframing conversations to help clients dominate and lead their industries by building engagement and direct relationships with consumers and potential clients. She has an enviable track record of transforming brands; composing vetted teams to identify and solidify goals; identifying transformational market displacements; and building category-killer brands that dominate those new markets.

Kanna has appeared on TV networks Fox, MSNBC, ABC, CBS, CW, and has been quoted in magazines including Forbes, Kiplinger’s Personal Finance, U.S. News and Review and Women’s Day.

A senior advisor at an annual exclusive business retreat since 2003, Kanna has performed consulting services for more than 500 small- and mid-size business owners who sought guidance to take their companies to the next level of prominence. She also has consulted and held advisory roles for billon-dollar companies, world-renowned scientists Sally Ride and Bob Ballard, authors on The New York Times, The Wall Street Journal and Business Week best-seller lists, philanthropists and well-known entrepreneurs and celebrities.
