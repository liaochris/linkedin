Maria Brazda's Summary

My expertise focuses on strategic communication, marketing, digital multimedia, training and organizational change management.

During my 28-year professional career, I have spearheaded successful marketing and business development campaigns within multiple industries including the federal government, associations and corporations.

I am the principal of Leopard Media Group, a strategic communication, marketing, digital multimedia and public relations company designed to help clients understand, leverage and reach their stakeholders through a variety of proven communication processes. These processes cover the full spectrum of OCM, media and marketing strategies. They may include the use of film, video, web and social media outlets.

Current and past clients include: PWC, Armed Forces Retirement Home, U.S. Department of Education, CACI, Air Force Sergeants Association, American Council of the Blind, Wildlife Habitat Council, Women’s Bar Association, Disabled American Veterans, U.S. Department of Interior, Museum of the US Army, Liuna Training and Education Fund, and IHS.

Maria Brazda's Projects

  • Disabled American Veterans (DAV)

    • 2011 to Present
    Team Members: Maria Brazda

    Working with one of the most influential Veteran Service Organizations in America (DAV), Leopard Media Group was tasked to support their continued efforts in telling the Veteran story. We developed an exhibit display and produced media programs for the National Women Veteran Summit (U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs), in Washington DC. The stories reflected on the women veteran’s sacrifices and achievements from WWII – the War in Afghanistan. The key focus was on veteran women health issues and the ongoing challenges they face today.

    • 1995 to 2010
    Team Members: Maria Brazda

Managed all aspects, including talent recruitment, marketing and promotion, and execution. Acted as liaison to prime corporate sponsor, HSBC Bank, among others.

Proceeds of the campaign are shared with participating dioceses for local communications programs and to aid Catholic communication efforts in developing nations.
