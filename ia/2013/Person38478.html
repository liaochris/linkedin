David Schairer's Summary

As a technologist and engineer, a product- and company-builder, and above all an autodidact and thinker, I specialize in finding solutions to big technology human problems and then constructing the teams to build them.

My cursus honorum started in building core ISP infrastructure in the early days of the commercial internet, when everything on the software side still had to be built from scratch. As the lead technologist at Concentric, I built big -- big mail and DNS platforms, big hosting and application systems, big adaptive learning engines, always with an eye to linear scalability and security by design rather than by serendipity. Our service products always achieved a high margin due to limited costs and aggressive scalability and provided services to millions of users under Concentric's own brand as well as being the private-label business/SaaS technology behind Netscape, Microsoft, SBC, and others.

Concentric was acquired in 2000 for $2.5B by Nextlink to form XO; I then continued to run the Concentric technology group and extended its product platform into email security, cloud hosting (with an early all-virtual, Xen-based distributed platform), and other enterprise products.

I have also founded, designed technology for, and/or advised a number of early startups, including:

RelayRides -- distributed, peer-to-peer car sharing, working out initial models and deploying MVP technology
Trapit -- massive machine-learning-based platform for adaptive personal information discovery, based on DARPA/SRI technology
Kuato Studios -- cutting-edge educational gaming backed by natural-speech AI, also based on SRI technology
New stealth startup

I also advise startups and will consult on any area of interest -- past consulting experience has covered SaaS and hosted infrastructure, team management (large and small), ecommerce platforms and applications, mobile systems and services, enterprise technology teams and organization, and the like.

Specialties

Building startup businesses; scalable Internet architectures; adaptive/machine learning; natural language processing; massively-threaded programming; high-performance distributed platforms; agile-style dev; Internet protocols -- email, web, DNS, &c; server virtualization & cloud computing; systems security; systems, network, data center ops; high-performance team mgmt; technology M&A/business development; product lifecycle, from idea to deployment to operations; C/C++/Python/PHP/Java/SQL/JS, &c
