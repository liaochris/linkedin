Kendall Allen's Summary

Now serving numerous media and data technology clients for WIT Strategy as a Senior Associate in corporate affairs and media relations, Kendall previously ran collaborative pursuits through her company, Influence Collective, LLC. -- advising and supporting media and tech entrepreneurs.

Past Influence Collective clients include Gramercy Group; Motivity Marketing; Howard-Sloan-Koller; 20nine; Catalyst S+F; Carolyn & Co. Media; Cult360/Rust Company; Circus Marketing; Red Bricks Media; Linett & Harrison; PumpOne. The company also serves a dedicated passion of Kendall's: Special Spectators, providing services to the charity. Current WIT Strategy clients highlighted below.

Allen’s 20 year path includes print and digital, spanning publisher and agency sides. She first covered emergent media tech in the SF Bay Area as a moonlighting, independent journalist obsessed with the broadening landscape.

Moving from print to the digital publishing realm during its most formative years, Allen helped found Thrive, an early play in content/community, and AOL/Time Inc. joint venture. She then moved to the agency world on top posts for USWeb/CKS and Euro RSCG Circle. She ultimately would spend five years supporting and building independent agencies or divisions in New York, before establishing Influence Collective.

A highly regarded thought leader in media circles, she was a frontrunner on Search, as original executive at one of the first SEM agencies, Fathom Online, a post that originally brought her to New York to open the East – and then as regional Director of Client Services for iCrossing. She contributes heavily to the industry, as a trainer; speaker, moderator and panelist; popular editorial contributor to Media Post, AdAge and MarketingLand; and numerous advisories. She served the 212NYC Board of Directors for several years, 2 of them as Vice President and sits on the Producers Guild of America, as national delegate, New Media Executive Council, Vice Chair for the East.

Specialties

Blended management profile, with broad and deep business background on several sides of the media business. Avid connector, with stellar reputation with reports, clients, partners, within industry. Innate business developer. Converged media background, with integrated marketing services bent, due to progression from print to digital, in various capacities, during industry evolution. Unique breed of consultant -- providing strategy but also trench-level support on execution of recommendations.

Special Spectators

October 2010 – Present (2 years 11 months) East Coast

Special Spectators creates magical days for seriously ill children and their families at sporting events.

We pursue this mission, in partnership with college and professional organizations, by providing tickets to games and designing days filled with special surprises and experiences in and around the stadium that are not accessible to most fans. Special Spectators is designed to create fun for the entire family and provide a pause from the difficulties they face battling a loved one’s illness. Special Spectators also coordinates visits by athletes to children’s hospitals or pediatric units in order to expose them to the inspiration and reward of making a powerful contribution to the community.

Our game day events provide the ultimate fan experience complete with tailgate parties visited by mascots, cheerleaders and marching bands. Special Spectators doesn't just take the kids to the game, but makes them a part of the game by providing special locker room tours, meeting coaches and walking on the field during a time out.

The field visit is a great moment for everyone in the stadium. Not only does it create incredible memories for the parents and their children, but it also provides an opportunity for the fans to make a powerful impact.

While the kids are standing on the field, a stadium announcement tells the fans why these kids are attending the game, explains Special Spectators and asks the crowd to give the children a warm welcome to the stadium. Typically this results in a standing ovation.

Millions of fans have discovered Special Spectators thanks to national coverage from Fox Sports, ESPN.com, Big Ten Network and ESPN2's Cold Pizza (segment was also shown as in-flight entertainment on Delta flights). We've also received regional and local TV/Print/Radio coverage in such markets as Chicago, Philadelphia, Orlando, Cleveland, Dallas, Denver, Tucson and many more.

To donate or volunteer, contact blake@specialspectators.org.
