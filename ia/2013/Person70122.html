James Tilbury's Summary

I enjoy setting up and leading organisations and projects designed to solve important social and environmental problems. I am currently working on developing a tool to estimate the risk posed by climate change legislation to investment portfolios. It is my opinion that these risks are currently not priced properly, resulting in inflated valuations of some assets and inefficient allocation of resources to polluting industries. This opinion is shared by Oxford University's Smith School of Enterprise and the Environment, where I do related research work in the Stranded Assets Programme.

In the past I have helped set up and led a chapter of Engineers Without Borders, an international development organisation; Project Survival Pacific, a climate change advocacy organisation; and Veratas, a sustainability consultancy focused on facilitating a shift towards a form of capitalism that produces better outcomes for society.

I have worked as an engineer, a sustainability consultant at Ernst and Young, and a project management assistant at CARE International in Cambodia. My undergraduate degree was in engineering, specialising in economics and the environment, and I have a masters degree in environmental management from the University of Oxford.

If you want to get in touch please feel free to email me at james.tilbury@gmail.com.
