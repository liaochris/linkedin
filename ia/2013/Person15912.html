SEN Administrator

Ramillies Hall School

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Education Management industry

November 2009 – Present (3 years 10 months) Cheadle Hulme

Ramillies Hall School is a small, independent school (ages 4 - 16) that specialises in teaching students with learning difficulties, namely Autistic Spectrum Conditions, ADHD, Dyslexia and Dyspraxia. We are the only CReSTeD registered school in the Greater Manchester area and are BDA accredited.

I joined the school in 2009 and originally worked as a Learning Support Assistant/Teaching Assistant. I worked with students from the age of 6 – 16, in both small group and one-to-one capacity.

Over the years my role expanded some what and I took on an element of teaching, primarily our specialist learning support Assistive Technology sessions but also Year 7 & 8 Religious Studies.

My role also includes administration of the SEN department, IT & technical expertise (including the school's website) and marketing (organising open days, meeting parents & Local Authorities, Newsletters, Printed & Online advertising).

I've been fortunate to sit on various school planning teams/strategic planning groups, including the development and opening of a new infants department. I've also been involved with the fundraising, development and opening of a new multi-sensory library for the school.

As administrator for the SEN department this has involved working with our Head of Learning Support/Deputy Head, to ensure provision matches pupil needs, responding to professional requests for information, scheduling and holding of Annual Reviews, staff timetabling, everyday office management and much, much more!

In September 2013, my role was formalised into the SEN Administrator position and this forms an integral part of the school's management.

I love working at Ramillies. It has tremendous advantage in being a small school, and truly offers unique teaching for those with learning difficulties. It also offers great challenge and satisfaction to me and because we are small, my role is ever-changing and flexible - but I think it's great!
