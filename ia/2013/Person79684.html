Lee Corkett's Summary

University graduate educated in the social sciences with professional experience in marketing and a passion for the development of community and knowledge through objective research and progressive education. A forward thinker who holds a bachelor’s degree in Anthropology from UCLA, and has more than two years’ experience in research, outreach and administration in an educational institution. Exceptional skills in data management, academic writing, public relations and interviewing. My goal is to work with scholars and educators in the greater LA area toward socially oriented projects.

I am both an academic researcher and creative professional. I work in photography, graphic design, prepress and printing, and have recently completed an international research project which focused on visual culture and photographic production.

Lee Corkett's Experience

Assistant to President

Norman Seeff Photographic Archives

June 2012 – Present (1 year 5 months) Hollywood, CA

Assist in compiling and maintaining a vast archive of vintage silver print photographs from the 1960s and 1970s. My immediate tasks are: addressing issues concerning print to database integrity through examination of image detail; researching production dates of unidentified photographs; and preparing presentations for potential buyers and curators. I also provide technical computer support for the president where needed and offer perspective on file management problems.

Photographer, Designer, Commercial Printer

Lee Corkett Photography

January 2006 – Present (7 years 10 months)

Lee Corkett Photography is a full service imaging team in Ventura County, offering Creative Portraiture, Product/Sculpture Photography, Fine Art Photography, Graphic Design and 4-color printing and print brokering. We work in the Ventura and LA County area with retailers and business owners from varying industries and commercial enterprises creating visual designs and marketing tools that form the foundation of their current marketing efforts, online and in print.

Primary Researcher

UCLA

Educational Institution; 10,001+ employees; Research industry

October 2010 – June 2012 (1 year 9 months)

Completed during my undergraduate career, but representing Master’s level course and fieldwork, my research focused on contemporary photographic history and production in Indonesia. I was one of fifteen students selected for the program and worked under the auspices of Dr. Sherry Ortner. I carried out this two year project through the UCLA Anthropology Department and was responsible for grant writing, logistics and qualitative analysis of my data collected in Central Java.

Outreach Coordinator

Undergraduate Anthropological Association

October 2010 – June 2012 (1 year 9 months) UCLA

Facilitated academic and cultural activities for the UCLA undergraduate anthropology student body. Working closely with the Chair of the Anthropology Department, I assisted in faculty projects, acted as liaison between the department and student body, recruited professional speakers for club functions and navigated the university’s bureaucracy to secure event funding.

Photography Instructor

Agoura Hills Homeschool Network

November 2011 – February 2012 (4 months) Agoura Hills, CA

Designed a curriculum for and instruct teenaged students in photography and visual art in a homeschool district in the Conejo Valley. Continuously develop new strategies to incorporate and integrate the students’ individual strengths and weaknesses into educational exercises that coax them to consider the personal value of their practice of photography. The goals of my lesson plans are to inspire new ways of using their skills beyond the lessons presented to them.

Gallery Educator

Fowler Museum of Cultural History

September 2010 – June 2011 (10 months) UCLA

Hired as the only undergraduate member of the otherwise graduate student Fowler Museum Education team. Researched the cultural and academic history of selected works in the museum to design and lead tours for high school and undergraduate students. Compiling information and making culture and art accessible to both academic and non-academic audiences was at the core of this position.

Prepress/Graphic Designer

Various Print Shops in Southern California

June 2006 – February 2009 (2 years 9 months)

Managed digital assets and worked as the primary graphic designer in various printing companies. Duties ranged from free-form creative design to digital recreation of existing analog art, print setup, and conferring with customers to assess and meet design goals. I often ran multiple digital presses simultaneously, working with color management profiles on-the-fly to adjust color output to the desired appearance. Worked directly with offset and digital print teams and applied my knowledge of both output mediums to produce consistent results.
