Genevieve Whitaker, M.P.M., J.D.'s Summary

Genevieve's overall desire is to have a direct, positive impact on the way the international community views the Caribbean and to ensure that the U.S. Virgin Islands assumes an essential role in the development of a new global perception of the region.

Genevieve Whitaker is a human rights activist,lobbyist researcher, professor, radio personality and blogger. Genevieve has developed a strong human rights acumen, having received a Certificate from Oxford University's Summer Program in International Human Right Law and also serving as a lobbyist for Amnesty International USA. She is also a trained political consultant as the Graduate of The White House Project training program; admitted participant in the 2011 Women's Campaign School at Yale University; and current student of Political Management (master's program) at The George Washington University School for Professional Studies (Graduate School of Political Management).

Genevieve currently serves as the Deputy Supervisor (St. Croix district) for the Elections System of the Virgin Islands (U.S.). Genevieve is also a licensed consultant, serving as the Chief Executive Officer & Owner of The Whitaker Consultant Group, LLC, a legal and political analysis, non-profit administration and new media marketing company.

Genevieve has served as an Assistant Professor at the University of the Virgin Islands (College of Liberal Arts and Social Sciences) specializing in courses in criminal and juvenile delinquency law, communication and leadership skills. Additionally, Genevieve dove into the communications field during her term as an Associate State Director of Communications at AARP Virgin Islands. She led the social communications agenda for the state office.

Genevieve is also the Co-Founder of Virgin Islands Youth Advocacy Coalition, Inc., a youth civics education organization.

    Mission
    VI Youth Advocacy Coalition, Inc. (VIYAC), is a broad based coalition of organizations and individuals committed to increasing political and civic involvement and overall voter participation of Virgin Islanders, ages 14-30. VIYAC is youth led and dedicated to educating young voters who have not been full participants in the political processes, namely the electoral and legislative systems. VIYAC aims, through civic education, voter literacy, and leadership development training, to educate youth and young adults on how to identify issues, to influence public policy and how to become a more effective participant in the democratic governance of the Virgin Islands (U.S.).
    Overview
    VIYAC is a civic organization run by youth who strive to promote youth empowerment in the Virgin Islands and by extension, the world.

    The Women’s Coalition of St. Croix provides advocacy, programs and services to end all forms of violence and oppression by promoting equality for all.

    We implement our mission in the community by providing crisis intervention services and counseling for victims in both English and Spanish. We train volunteers as advocates for victims in crisis, provide a 24-hour, year-round advocate program to assist men, women, and children victims of rape, domestic violence and other violent crimes and conduct self-help support groups for women and teen girls in the second stage of crisis and adult survivors of incest.

    We provide temporary safe housing, emergency aid, food and clothing to victims and their children and conduct specialized training seminars to sensitize police, hospital and social service personnel to the needs of victims.

    We offer referrals, advocacy and support for victims involved with the hospitals and other community agencies and provide community education and public speakers on the subjects of domestic violence, rape, child molestation, conflict resolution, date rape, and women’s health issues.
