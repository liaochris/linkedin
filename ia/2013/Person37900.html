David Hiler's Summary

DAVID HILER decided young to enlist in the hospitality industry. He has since amassed 20+ years of Restaurant and Inn operations and consulting experience, which he now brings to the Whetstone Station Restaurant and Brewery, Hiler Hospitality, LLC and InnConcierge. Five of those years were spent building the Three Mountain Inn, in Jamaica, Vermont, from a poorly performing business into a profitable, 4-diamond AAA, Wine Spectator award winning Inn.

David moved to San Francisco in the mid 80's, choosing a life of “enlightenment” in the sunshine over the chilly weather of New England. After graduating from San Francisco City College Hotel/Restaurant Program, he became the youngest manager in the Hard Rock Café Corporation, where he remained throughout the late eighties into the early nineties.

Returning to a "quieter" life in the Inn-business, he dedicated himself to the pursuit of exceptional guest service and to discovering a path to a fulfilling career in the Hospitaltiy Industry. To that end, David has owned, leased and managed properties, developed Innkeeper training materials, designed and implemented Internship programs, performed speaking engagements for leading industry organizations such as the Professional Association of Innkeepers (PAII), Select Registry, Distinctive Inns of New England and Passport to New England, published articles in hospitality publications and worked to promote the industry overall.

David and his family live in Brattleboro, Vermont. The Hilers are active in the community and David recently resigned from the board of PAII in order to focus his efforts on his home town and state. He is a founding board member of the Brattleboro Arts Initiative and the Latchis Hotel and Art Center. He enjoys traveling, the outdoors, live music and visiting the occasional restaurant or B&B.
