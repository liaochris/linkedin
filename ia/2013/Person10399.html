Asian Focus NC's Summary

Asian Focus is a non-profit organization formed to promote, sponsor and support programs to help Asian Americans and Asian immigrants of all ages.Asian focus strives to help individuals and their families address the daily cultural and linguistic challenges to better acclimate into American society. We hope to foster synergy and encourage participation from agencies and skilled professionals; promoting education, diversity and cultural exchange services for all underserved groups towards the betterment of tomorrow.

Asian Health Fair 2010

Asian Focus

Nonprofit; 10,001+ employees; Nonprofit Organization Management industry

March 2006 – September 2010 (4 years 7 months)

Asian Health Fair
Location: Brier Creek Community Ctr, 10810 Globe Road, Raleigh NC
Time: 1:00 P.M - 5:00 P.M, September 18, 2010

Asian Health Fair Program: http://dl.dropbox.com/u/8938681/Asian_healthfair_broch_ToddParham091310.pdf

Asian Health Fair Postcard: http://dl.dropbox.com/u/8938681/AHF%20Postcard%207-26-10FN.pdf

Asian Americans represent a wide variety of languages, dialects, and cultures as different from one another as from non-Asian groups. Asian Americans have historically been overlooked due to the myth of the model minority, the erroneous notion that Asian Americans are passive, compliant, and without problems or needs. The effects of this myth have been the failure to take seriously the very real concerns of this population.

Asian Americans represent both extremes of the socioeconomic and health indices: while more than a million Asian Americans live at or below the federal poverty level, Asian-American women have the highest life expectancy of any other group. Asian Americans suffer disproportionately from certain types of cancer, tuberculosis, and Hepatitis B. Factors contributing to poor health outcomes for Asian Americans include language and cultural barriers, stigma associated with certain conditions, and lack of health insurance.
Data source: CDC Asian Health Disparities

Join ASIAN FOCUS and other Asian American community-based groups (coalition partners) in the Triangle area as we deliver an annual health fair with literatures, exhibitors, speakers and screenings geared for the Asian American population in the Triangle area. We hope to bridge the gap of health disparities by organizing the Asian Health Fair that will promote wellness and prevention. We anticipate the Asian Health Fair to include under 50 exhibitors with speaking seminar sessions that will focus on the top health concerns affecting Asian Americans.

Interests:

Asian Focus is a 501c3 nonprofit organization registered to operate in the state of North Carolina. Our purpose, to help bridge disparity gaps for Asian Americans and Asian Immigrants and their families. Why: The founder of Asian Focus (AF) and the AF team understands the struggles and difficulties first generation immigrant faces. Asian Americans are recognized as the "model citizens". Unfortunately this recognition also creates an erroneous belief that Asians are without needs. This perception can easily create an atmosphere to overlook the segment of population whom are linguistically and culturally challenged. Asian Focus hopes to promote and encourage participation from skilled professionals and agencies to sponsor programs to bridge disparity gaps for the forgotten/under-served group(s).
