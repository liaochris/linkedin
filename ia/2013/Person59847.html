Jay Hennessy's Publications

  • Biomass energy – implications of increased use for Sweden and beyond

    • October 25, 2012
    Authors: Jay Hennessy

    Biomass is commonly seen as the ‘carbon-neutral’ panacea of fuels that can replace everything from coal to natural gas as well as transport fuels. However at the point of combustion, wood for example, generates twice as much greenhouse gas as burning natural gas. So can it really be the solution it is claimed to be for mitigating climate change? This essay looks at the policies driving the increased use of biomass and questions whether they add up. The findings, with particular reference to Sweden, demonstrate that as with all renewable technologies, there are many considerations to weigh up. Even in a country with a bioenergy resource per capita that is three times as abundant as that of France, Sweden would need at least 93 % of its available resource to cover existing use and heating needs by 2020.

  • What role can new dwellings in Sweden play in achieving goals for greenhouse gas emissions? What factors are preventing this?

    • October 26, 2012
    Authors: Jay Hennessy

    Sweden estimates that it will achieve less than half of its greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions target by 2020. With a housing shortage and a growing population there will be a need for significant growth in new dwellings. This essay shows what contributions new buildings in Sweden can make towards meeting emissions targets set by the EU and nationally. It shows that stricter building standards can significantly improve emissions from new buildings with little extra cost, but this must be driven by policy changes.
