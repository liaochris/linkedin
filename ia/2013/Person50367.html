Greg Ottensmeyer's Summary

Greg’s career in telecommunications dates to 1974 when he purchased a Telephone Answering Service business. Today, Ottensmeyer serves as President of TelephoNET Corp., a technology company he co-founded in April 1996. TelephoNET is a privately-held company providing turn-key telephony solutions worldwide.

Early Business History (excerpt from Wikipedia.com)
Gregory Vernon Ottensmeyer, Sr., b. 15 October, 1952 in Baltimore, MD is an American entrepreneur and businessperson. Ottensmeyer’s early business history helped pioneer competition in the US telecommunications industry. His actions along with MCI and others[1] contributed to the U.S. Department of Justice mandated breakup of AT&T, (the Bell System monopoly) in 1984, by decree of the Honorable Harold H. Greene, encouraging a climate that would fuel development of the competitive telephone industry.

References:

Ottensmeyer's interview is featured in "Genius on Hold" 2011, Top Cat Productions LLC, a 90-minute docu-movie by Director, Gregory Marquette, chronicling historical events leading to the 1984 break-up of the Bell Telephone System and AT&T - the world's largest monopoly. The 1984 Divestiture is a significant event because it marked the dawning of an era of competitive telecommunications and nurtured an environment allowing rapid development of technologies leading to the proliferation of cellphones and the public Internet.
