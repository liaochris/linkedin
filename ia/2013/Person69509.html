Joyce Murton's Summary

Nearly 10 years of professional software development experience with a focus on application development for embedded platforms.

I am primarily a coder, although many of my projects have required me to take various leadership roles. I have been responsible for the entire product lifecycle from design and development to deployment and maintenance. I've managed small teams or worked with external contributors to ensure all assets were delivered on time according to specification. In this capacity, I've acted as UX to design UI, set up project build tools, assisted graphic artists with asset management, produced project plans to communicate important milestones to the team and higher up management, and done whatever else it took to complete a given project.

I am also familiar with agile software development practices such as Scrum and XP, and use these to manage the output of my game development hobbyists group.

I have professionally used the following languages and technologies to a substantial degree: HTML5, CSS, JavaScript, Adobe Flash, ActionScript 2 and 3, Java, Java2D, Swing, AWT, Python, Ruby, C++, etc.

I am somewhat familiar with a large number of other languages and technologies as well, having frequently done one-off projects in a new language.

Specialties

user interface design and development, embedded software development, agile software development, technical writing, project management, Java, ActionScript 2/3, HTML5

Lead Control Systems Engineer

Kaleidescape Inc.

Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Consumer Electronics industry

May 2007 – August 2010 (3 years 4 months)

At Kaleidescape, I was responsible for developing and maintaining remote control systems used for controlling our company's home media server.

This required expertise in everything from design, testing, and implementation of entire software applications, to augmenting limited development environments with various scripts and tools, to designing and defining user interactions for present and future control devices and control concepts.

Since every project required a completely new technology and design environment, I had to become incredible at rapidly becoming an expert with unfamiliar technologies, development environments, and hardware.

Projects were on embedded and non-embedded platforms such as Windows CE, Linux, and others. Applications were written with Flash/ActionScript, JavaScript, Java, C++, Python, Crestron SIMPL+, AMX NetLinx, Lua, and other languages.

Software Architect

S.M.I.L.E. Inc.

September 2005 – April 2007 (1 year 8 months)

As software architect, I designed a series of modular software applications that could be easily swapped in and out of individual client systems to customize their installation. These applications required full front to back design and implementation, from the user interface to the underlying back end code that made it work.

However, I was responsible for more than just overseeing the general direction of software design for the company. I was also responsible for overseeing all installations for our clients, and ensuring that their systems were reliable and fully operational. As part of this, I implemented system monitoring and trouble detection software, as well as scripting tools to permit more rapid deployment of custom software packages.

I was also responsible for the company's internal IT, including server management, internal tech support, networking support, and telephone system support.
