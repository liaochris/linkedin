Ingrid Sturgis' Publications

  • Teaching Converged Media Through News Coverage of the 2008 U.S. Presidential Election and Inauguration

    • Asia Pacific Media Educator
    • January 1, 2010
    Authors: Ingrid Sturgis, Yanick Rice Lamb, Charles Fancher

    This commentary provides insights into how the journalism faculty at Howard University in Washington, DC tested the efficacy of its approach to teaching converged media techniques during the US presidential election on November 4, 2008 and the Inauguration of President Barack Obama on January 20, 2009. Coverage of both events were conducted by students supervised by faculty mentors.

  • Are Traditional Media Dead: Can Journalism Survive in the Digital World?

    • International Debate Education Association
    • April 2012
    Authors: Ingrid Sturgis

    In the 21st century, the Internet has made publishers of anyone with a laptop or mobile phone. In response, many observers have said that traditional media—newspapers, radio, television, cable TV, magazines, and other print publications—are in a death spiral if not already dead. In a series of articles from academic journals, popular print media, opinion surveys, and government reports, Are Traditional Media Dead? investigates this question, exploring the problems facing traditional media, the media’s response to these challenges, and whether the responses might prove successful.
