Dallas Thornton's Summary

Dallas Thornton is the Division Director for Health Cyberinfrastructure at UC San Diego’s San Diego Supercomputer Center and Vice President for SD Technology. Dallas’s team works to bring together the right data, tools, and people to enable discovery and informed, strategic decision making. Mr. Thornton received his masters in management of technology and his MBA from Vanderbilt University. He is currently working on his PhD in healthcare fraud detection at the University of Twente in the Netherlands.

Dallas’s SDSC division is focused on the health domain and responsible for delivering secure cyberinfrastructure to support government, academia, and industry. Mr. Thornton leads UCSD’s participation in the Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services’ MIG Data Engine program, which combats fraud, waste, and abuse through analysis of Medicaid data. In 2011, Dallas led the effort to architect and deploy SDSC Cloud, the largest and highest-performance known academic cloud storage repository, including over 5 petabytes of disk at inception. His team subsequently deployed SDSC GovCloud, offering FISMA- and HIPAA- compliant cloud compute and storage resources in support of researchers and federal sponsors.

SD Technology (San Diego Technology, Inc.) provides expertise and solutions to government and prime government contractors in areas such as data mining, cybersecurity, health informatics, high-performance computing, technology research and development, IT, systems engineering, C4I, program management, and acquisition support. The company is able to support defense and other classified efforts not possible within UCSD’s open research mission and restrictions. The company is a HUBZone-certified, woman-owned (WO), service disabled veteran owned small business (SDVOSB).
