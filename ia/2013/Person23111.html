Chris Brough's Summary

Hi. I am Chris Brough. I'm currently a Programming Intern at Square Enix Montreal. I recently graduated with a Bachelor of Science in Game Programming from Champlain College in Burlington, Vermont.

I've had an immense interest in technology and video games for as long as I can remember. When I'm not developing my knowledge in programming, I enjoy drawing and playing video games.

Among the many interests I have in programming, I'm currently focused on systems, data-oriented design, networking, and 3D graphics.

Specialties: C, C++, C#, Objective-C, GLSL, HLSL, Python, Lua, JavaScript, ActionScript 3.0, HTML, CSS, Unity3D, XNA, 2D and 3D Graphics

Chris Brough's Projects

  • Heightmaps with Google Elevations

    • November 2012 to November 2012
    Team Members: Chris Brough

    A heightmap implementation that uses Google Elevations API to acquire real height data from locations around Earth. This is built with C++ and OpenGL in a data-oriented design pattern.

  • Quadtree Level of Detail for Heightmaps

    • April 2012 to April 2012
    Team Members: Chris Brough

    As a final project for my 3D Graphics Programming course, I chose to implement level of detail (lod) for terrains (heightmaps) using a quadtree, C++, and OpenGL.

  • Sagittarii Run

    • January 2013 to April 2013
    Team Members: Chris Brough, Grant Parker, Ethan Akey, Jason Rauck, Kyle Marchev

    Sagittarii Run is an arcade racing game set in the future on the edge of a black hole. The game is being developed by a team of 12 individuals including a producer, programmers, artists, and designers.

  • Beatpad Defender

    • January 2012 to April 2012
    Team Members: Chris Brough

    Beatpad Defender is a musical tower defense game. The goal is to stop the incoming creeps (enemies) from taking control of the beatpad. The player must place guitar (projectile), drum (area of effect), and cymbal (splash) towers to defend the beatpad controls. The placement and upgrade configuration of the towers will create a unique musical experience on every play through.

  • Light

    • November 2011 to November 2011
    Team Members: Chris Brough

    Light is vertical scrolling platformer with focus on the preservation of light. The goal is to reach the end of the level before the light reaches zero; along the way, the player can pick batteries which will recharge the light's battery.

  • Harry Pitcher

    • March 2011 to April 2011
    Team Members: Chris Brough

    Harry Pitcher is a 2D platformer built in C++ using the Allegro Game Programming Library. The gameplay consists of navigating platforms, climbing ladders, shooting pitchforks, and dodging enemies. While the design may be less appealing than other platformers, this project was great mechanism for apply concepts that I learned during the game architecture course at Champlain College.
