Matt Rocheleau's Summary

As a reporter for Boston.com’s Your Campus and Your Town sections, I cover 15 area campuses, four of the city’s busiest neighborhoods and MBTA news, regularly breaking stories and writing in-depth features on an array of topics. Over the past three years, I’ve led major citywide projects for the sites and taken on numerous beats. For the past two years, I’ve also worked a weekend reporting shift at The Boston Globe’s Metro Desk covering general assignment and breaking news, including several front page stories about complex topics, often under tight deadlines.

In the summer of 2010, I was an editorial intern at The Christian Science Monitor writing distinct stories on major national issues – including multiple, in-depth pieces on the Gulf oil spill – and assisting with research for the weekly print cover page feature.

Most of my work has been covering news in and around my home state – from Boston to western Massachusetts.

Working at the Globe West bureau as a co-op for six months, I covered a wide range of news – from breaking crime, court and late-night election reports to lengthy, detailed features – and was frequently commended for my aggressive work ethic in digging to find and report on unique, local stories. Midway through the co-op, my editors asked me to be the correspondent for a new hyperlocal site covering Framingham, one of New England’s largest towns.

During my final college semester, as managing editor of The Massachusetts Daily Collegian, I was integral in daily print, web and multimedia production. I worked closely on special investigative projects and series, fostered partnerships with other media entities and launched a business news section and a smartphone application.

While working as a Collegian editor and juggling a full course load, I interned at The Daily Hampshire Gazette’s sports and news desks. I earned two scholarships that semester. Over the summer of 2008, I wrote for The Sun Chronicle where my news writing began.
