Abla Hamilton's Summary

Abla Hamilton has 12 years of experience designing solutions for eCommerce across web, desktop, kiosk, and interactive television platforms.

She is currently a senior UI designer for Redbubble. She is responsible for performing customer research, interaction design and usability testing for the website.

Before joining Redbubble, Abla has held positions as a senior user experience designer and lead interaction designer at Snapfish-HP, ZillionTV, Rhapsody America and Bank of America.

Abla holds a B.A. in Studio Art from Pomona College, and a M.S. in Digital Design (formerly known as Information Design and Technology) from Georgia Tech.

Specialties

Information architecture, information design, user research, ethnographic studies, persona development, user interface design, complex web application design, software design, kiosk design, interactive television, project management, Six Sigma

Abla Hamilton's Patents

  • Online payment system and method

    • United States Patent US20120116973 A1
    • Issued May 10, 2012
    Inventors: Charmaine Klein, Chris Gagliardo, Peter Nies, Abla Hamilton, Hitesh Bajaj

    System, method, and computer program product are provided for a user to send and receive P2P payments using the Internet. Through the use of a network, a user may access accounts the user has at a financial institution and direct payments to other individuals or entities using the other individuals or entities alias. In this way, the user may ensure a secure payment to a third-party through the network associated with a financial institution. The payments may be directed to individuals the user may input into the system by providing the user's alias. A user's alias may be a unique identifier of the user and tied to a financial account of that user. In this way, the sender of the P2P payments may input the recipient's alias and transfer funds via the P2P payment system without having to input the recipient's account information.

Abla Hamilton's Publications

  • Challenges and Realities of Merging Online Bank

    • AIGA: American Institute of Graphic Arts
    • 2005
    Authors: Abla Hamilton, Suzan Revah

    The recent merger of Bank of America and Fleet Bank presented unique challenges that arose from the effort to combine two online banking applications. Different functionality and different navigation systems, combined with different and sometimes conflicting business goals and stakeholders' views, significantly impacted the user interface design process.This study will focus on one particular project aimed at facilitating this transition (or merger), the Online Banking & Online Investing integration project. Employing user-centered design techniques, the project team was able to validate user-focused design decisions that contradicted the initial direction of the line of business. However, by collaborating with our business partners and stakeholders, we were able to determine and develop design compromises that supported business goals and ultimately provided an acceptable customer experience.
