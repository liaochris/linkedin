Esther James' Summary

Experienced, effective fundraiser skilled in winning grants from foundations, government agencies and corporations. Partners with nonprofit organizations to identify new funding prospects; develop strategy to approach each prospect; and write technically accurate and persuasive grant proposals and reports, fundraising letters, and other development materials.

Specialties

Grant proposal writing to raise funds from family foundations; community foundations; and county, state and federal agencies. Specific services include grant proposal writing, editing and review; grant prospect research; teaching grantwriting training workshops; and grant report writing.

Atlantic Cape Community College

Educational Institution; 201-500 employees; Higher Education industry

July 2009 – Present (4 years 3 months) Mays Landing, NJ

Working closely with faculty and staff of community college, develop competitive grant proposals for submission to federal, state and local grant programs as well as private foundations. This work includes:
- Researching and developing relationships with prospective grant funders for projects that match institutional objectives; closely monitoring multiple streams of information about prospective funders’ priorities and deadlines; and managing database of information about funding prospects and active grants.
- Continually informing college program managers and leaders about new and upcoming grant opportunities, providing summaries and recommendations related to each opportunity.
- Delivering technical assistance to college personnel seeking grants in following proposal guidelines and strategically cultivating relationships with grantmakers.
- Drafting proposal documents (e.g., letters of inquiry, proposal narratives and budgets, project budgets and cover letters), as well as reviewing and editing all proposal documents created by other college personnel.
- Collaborating closely with college’s other development functions (especially individual donor development program).
- Providing appropriate information and guidance to other college personnel charged with implementing necessary processes for management of granted funds.
