Jeff Macharyas - Graphic Design Expert's Summary

237 endorsements • 15 recommendations (thank you!)

I come from a long line of printers, so it was only natural to pursue a career in visual arts. As a teenager, I worked in the family printshop, doing bindery work, running presses (I was terrible), deliveries, and typesetting/layout. I was really good at the typesetting stuff, so I went to Florida State University and earned a degree in Communications and Visual Arts.

I've worked as a creative service manager, art director and graphic designer. I love print! I admire—and scrutinize—everything I see in print. I even smell magazines and catalogs (my wife thinks I'm weird). Then, I heard about this Internet thing. It seemed like it would stick around, so I earned my certification in HTML and have been designing websites as well as print. And, wouldn't you know it, along comes social media. So, I embraced that form of communication and got a miniMBA from Rutgers in Social Media Marketing.

Getting words and pictures—and pixels and palettes—to all work together can be challenging. That's where I come in. Whether it's print, digital or whatever—if it contains a message, with words and images, I make it happen.

See some cool stuff I've worked on: www.macharyas.com

    I'm going to check on other states/provinces to see if the history follows the same pattern.

    Are Idaho's Potatoes really "Famous"? What is a "Peace Garden" in North Dakota? What is it they want to "Show-Me" in Missouri? Can there really "10,000 Lakes" in Minnesota?     Letter to the editor:

    Jeff:

    I read your article about Florida's License Plate History in the Jan edition of the Pineapple Post with great interest.

    As you may or may not know Dept of Motor Vehicles can approve the use of one of the plates on classic cars. The plate has to be the year of the car. I have a 57 T-Bird with a 57 plate on it. I was aware of the county prefix code numbers.

    My question is I have a plate with the county code of "27" on it. I've been told it might be from Highlands County which is around Sebring but nobody knows for sure. Do you have any information on this.

    Also there is a letter designation after the county code which I was told had to do with the weight of the vehicle.

    I Thank You in advance and appreciate any info you might have.

    (Answer: 27 is Highlands County, and the letter designates weight.)
