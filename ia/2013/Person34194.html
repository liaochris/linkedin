Dennis Elbers' Summary

I'm a freelance curator, editor, writer and lecturer specializing in contemporary graphic design and it’s social relevance. I have frequently curated exhibitions for the Graphic Design Festival Breda, KOP, and MOTI (Museum of the Image, formally known as Graphic Design Museum). Recently I contributed to design media like 360°, Etapes: and gestalten.tv. I'm also an advisor for various governmental funding and policy organizations. In 2010, I received the Rabobank culture award for my contributions to the cultural climate in Breda.

Specialties: curating, branding, start-ups, policy, networking

Dennis Elbers' Experience

director (founder)

Graphic Design Festival

Nonprofit; 1-10 employees; Design industry

January 2008 – Present (5 years 8 months) Breda

In 2007 I founded the Graphic Design Festival Breda (NL). This biannual festival goes into present developments in (typo)graphic visual culture and largely takes place in the public space. The festival offers insight in current developments to a professional graphic design audience. In a surprising though perceptible manner the festival enables talented international designers to connect graphic design, technological developments, social context and the individual perspective by means of exhibitions, lectures, and workshops. By showing the communicative powers of graphic design within our daily life the festival inspires professionals, and also confronts a large general audience with the influence of images on their behaviour and opinions. With surprising graphic interventions in public space the festival creates awareness on the audiences personal relation to visual culture.
As a director I am responsible for the artistic and corporate policy of the festival. I manage a team of 16 professionals and interns. I'm also responsible all funding of this >€400k event.

In 2003 I graduated from the Fine Art department at AKV|St.Joost (Breda, NL). During my study I also worked as a festival promoter in The Netherlands and Australia. curator

MOTI

January 2011 – March 2012 (1 year 3 months) Breda

MOTI, Museum Of The Image belongs in a visual culture where disciplines merge and an explosive amount of images have become a part of our daily lives. The world of visual communication has become a sanctuary for text, images, dynamism and change. By broadening its horizon from graphic design to visual culture the museum attempts to show the dynamic world of film, design, photography, fashion, visual arts, archi­tecture, science and gaming. In the previous century the field of graphic design has laid a foundation for the present-day visual culture. The craft used to be a niche and avant-garde, now its everywhere and for everyone.
As curator I was in charge of exhibitions like Comix,Hotpix & Tragix by Peter Pontiac, Crafts Matter @DDW, Pop-Up Generation (curated by Lidewij Edelkoort), Grapic Detour in Berlin (curated by Erik Kessels), Another show about promotion and advertising material by Stefan Sagmeister and lectures like Overwerk and Masters of Amateurism (icw Premsela.org).

Dennis Elbers' Publications

  • Search/Find/Like/Share Perspectives in visual storytelling

    • Graphic Design Festival Breda
    • September 18, 2012
    Authors: Dennis Elbers, Sven Ehmann, Robert Urquhart, Ellen Lupton

    By using visual storytelling as a tool, designers communicate events and opinions in a way that stands out from the continuous stream of images we encounter everyday. As there is always more than one side to a story, there are different perspectives from which the visual storyteller can work. By exploring various perspectives designers show the influence they have on a society dominated by images.

    The projects in this book display how a socially relevant attitude combined with these various perspectives on storytelling extend the designers role to:
    Journalist – who creates compact and comprehensible reflections on the news.
    Scientist – who dissects complex systems so as to find a transparent view.
    Agitator – who questions and our personal opinions by provoking public debate.
    Poet – who shares their personal reflections on reality to trigger the imagination.

    Through exploring these various roles, designers prove how visual storytelling will raise an audiences visual literacy. It’s all about Search, Find, Like, and Share.
