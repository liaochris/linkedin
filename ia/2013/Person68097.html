Jonathan Sisk's Publications

  • Reality Pocket Guide

    • JES Publishing
    • August 20, 1981
    Authors: Jonathan Sisk

    The first independent book in the Pick space, this convenient (large) pocket-sized reference incorporated every command in the Reality Operating System in an easy-to-find format. The first edition of 200 copies sold out at the Micru Conference in August 1981.

  • Pick Pocket Guide

    • JES Publishing for Pick Systems
    • May 1, 1982
    Authors: Jonathan Sisk

    This collection of every command and token in the Pick Operating System became the gold standard for defining the Pick System. Licensees used it as a check off list over the following decade to assure their implementation was complete. It went through five editions and ultimately became the basis of Encyclopedia Pick.

  • Exploring the Pick Operating System

    • Hayden Book Company
    • March 1, 1985
    Authors: Jonathan Sisk, Steve VanArsdale

    The first independent textbook for the Pick System, this became the gold standard for people entering the Pick Space and the basis of JES' popular training courses. It was also the first into the commercial bookstores.

  • Pick/BASIC: A Programmer's Guide

    • Tab Books
    • November 1, 1987
    Authors: Jonathan Sisk

    The first textbook devoted to the Pick/BASIC programming language, it also became the entry point for countless thousands of programmers entering the Pick community. Put up as a freebie on the jes.com web site in 1995, it is still downloaded hundreds of times a month, much to the amazement of the author.

  • Encyclopedia Pick

    • JES Publishing for Pick Systems
    • June 1, 1992
    Authors: Jonathan Sisk

    Commissioned personally by the late Dick Pick, this all-encompassing reference became the basis of all future documentation as well as the online help for what was then known as Advanced Pick and subsequently renamed D3 after Dick's death. It included hyperlinked text just like modern HTML, several years before the Web was invented. In the fullness of time, the later generations of documentarians conspired to kill it and returned to the old job-protecting ways of folding it all into a single Word document they could control for job security, killing the ability for egalitarian input and maintenance. Brief remnants still appear in printed docs from the company, which have fallen into a state of incompletion and are maintained by people without a shred of Pick experience.

  • Pertec/Pick Pocket Guide

    • JES Publishing for Pertec Computer Corporation
    • August 1, 1983
    Authors: Jonathan Sisk

    The first of many spinoffs of the Pick Pocket Guide, this was the first produced in the foldover format, which fitted much easier into a pocket but was a little unwieldy.

  • The CIES Pick Pocket Guide

    • JES Publishing for CIE Systems
    • May 1, 1989
    Authors: Jonathan Sisk

    This version of the Pick Pocket Guide was by far the most extended, given that it was written for the version of Pick enhanced by Ian Sandler, of GA and CIES fame.

  • The Ultimate Pocket Guide

    • JES Publishing
    • June 1, 1988
    Authors: Jonathan Sisk

    This unauthorized version of the Pick Pocket Guide for the Ultimate version was met with displeasure by the Ultimate Corporation, but sold out all of its editions to happy Ultimate customers.

  • Microprocessor Operating Systems, Vol III

    • Microcomputer Applications
    • February 1, 1984
    Authors: Jonathan Sisk, Each chapter covering a specific Operating System of the time was written by a leading expert.

    This book compiled a single chapter on each of multiple popular operating systems of the day. Pick Systems commissioned Jon Sisk to write the Pick Chapter.

  • Pick For Professionals: Advanced Methods & Techniques

    • Tab Books
    • January 1, 1990
    Authors: Jonathan Sisk, Harvey Rodstein

    Harvey was listed as the author on this one.
    Jon edited it as part of the Pick Library series and it was based on the material developed for JES' advanced (Pick) training course.

  • Programming With IBM PC BASIC and the Pick Database System

    • Tab Books
    • January 1, 1990
    Authors: Jonathan Sisk, David L. Clark

    This was singularly written by David Clark of Los Angeles.
    Jon edited it as part of the Pick Library Series.

  • The Pick Perspective

    • Tab Books
    • January 1, 1989
    Authors: Jonathan Sisk, Ian Sandler

    Singularly written by Ian Sandler, of CIES and GA fame, and edited by Jon Sisk as part of the Pick Library for Tab Books. This was the first "philosophy" book on the Pick Database, offering unique perspective from the brilliant mind of Ian Sandler, who made significant enhancements to it over the course of his career.
