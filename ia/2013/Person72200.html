Karen Rowinsky, LSCSW's Summary

I have been working with individuals and families for over 30 years. As a Clinical Social Worker, my private counseling practice is the culmination of my life goal to live, and help others live, a happy, healthy, and meaningful life.

Specialties

Individuals who have experienced pregnancy loss, grief and loss, family caregiving, chronic disease, parenting issues, adoption and infertility, life transitions, previous trauma, or who want to be happier.

Couples, including same sex couples, who are not as happy as they would like, are having difficulty with communication, dealing with trust issues, having parenting challenges, are in blended families, or who are contemplating separation or divorce.

Karen Rowinsky, LSCSW's Experience

Owner,

Rowinsky Counseling Services providing counseling for individuals, couples, and families.

2008 – Present (5 years)

I work with individuals and couples to create the life they desire. This may be in helping you, or someone you know, who is going through a life transition, has experienced a loss, or is having trouble coping with the life's challenges. I work with couples who are contemplating a long term commitment, in a marriage or relationship that is not working, and those who can't figure out how they can stay together. I have special expertise in working with blended family issues, caring for ill or elderly relatives, and dealing with past traumas.

I have an eclectic approach in counseling and make use of a variety of theories and practice methods. I work especially well with people who have been afraid to seek help in the past or who never thought they would ever need counseling. I always tell new clients that it takes time to develop a relationship with a helping professional and that if at anytime they feel like I am not a good fit, I will help them find someone who is.

President

Come Alive! Presentations

1999 – 2004 (5 years)

Karen worked as a full-time professional speaker and trainer for four years. She was a nationally recognized expert in women's health education and the author of the book, Come Alive! 50 Easy Ways to Have More Energy Now.

President

The Family Center for Childbirth and Family Life Education

1983 – 1999 (16 years)

The Family Center was a 501(c)3 non-profit corporation in Topeka, Kansas. It's major client was Stormont-Vail Regional Medical Center. While there Karen created, planned, and executed hundreds of community education programs for The Birthplace and the Jane C. Stormont Women's Health Center. Karen also acted as the Kansas Family Planning State Training Manager from 1986-1988 and the Director of a Teen Pregnancy Prevention project from 1988-1990.
