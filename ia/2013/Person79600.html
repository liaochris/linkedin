Laurie Smithwick's Summary

Laurie is obsessed with design.

She’s been working as a designer for 20 years, but her family will tell you she’s been designing things as long as she could hold a crayon.

She launched her career at the historic jazz label GRP ⁄ Impulse (now Verve Records) where she designed CD packaging for Diana Krall, Dr. John, George Benson, Dave Grusin, Chick Corea, and virtually all the legendary jazz musicians you can think of.

Later, as senior designer at Red Herring Design in NYC she expanded her repertoire to include publishing, interactive design and corporate identity, working with MTV, Simon & Schuster, Pocket Books, Pfizer, Reader’s Digest, Elektra Records, Island Records, and many more.

North Carolina called her home in 1998, and Laurie founded LEAP design and hasn’t looked back.
Laurie’s work has received many design awards, including a Grammy nomination for CD Package Design.

Her work has been recognized by the Art Director’s Club, AIGA, Graphis, Society of Publication Designers, Print Magazine and the Association for Independent Music. She graduated from Duke with a BA in English ⁄ Creative Writing and later studied at Parsons School of Design. She lives in Charlotte, NC with her creative, left-brained husband, and her identical, 10yr old, twin daughters.
