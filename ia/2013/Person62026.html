Jennifer Yankopolus' Summary

I am a freelance editor specializing in architecture and design, historic preservation, garden history, sustainable/green design, and general non-fiction---as well as working with first-time authors. I offer my clients a supportive, collaborative author-editor relationship.

I have graduate degrees in architectural history and historic preservation and experience editing books, articles, reports, websites, blog posts, resumes, and newsletters. I also frequently work with non-native English writers from Asia, South America, and Europe, helping them prepare their manuscripts for English-language publications.

Specialties

Copyediting (polishing grammar, punctuation, spelling, and consistency), substantive and line editing (enhancing clarity, flow, and organization), proofreading, writing coach, manuscript critiquing, developmental editing (helping authors to develop their book ideas).

Subject areas: Architecture and design (including landscape and industrial design), garden history, historic preservation, sustainable/green design, history, business, reference, and general non-fiction.

Jennifer Yankopolus' Projects

  • Crossroads of Conflict: A Guide to Civil War Sites in Georgia.

    • 2006 to 2010
    Team Members: Jennifer Yankopolus

    Edited the manuscript written by multiple authors to ensure a unified voice and cohesive format. Created a user-focused design to help readers navigate the 350 history-related sites in this glossy full-color guidebook. Established a visual hierarchy so that the address and contact information is in a logical, consistent place and the overview text is distinct from the explanatory text for each site. Used professional and historic photographs to enhance the visual appear.

    Published by the University of Georgia Press in November 2010, Crossroads of Conflict received an Award of Merit in the Leadership in History Awards from the American Association for State and Local History as well as the Award for Excellence in Documenting Georgia’s History from the Georgia Historical Records Advisory Board, Office of the Secretary of State. The authors were nominated for the 2010 Author of the Year award for creative nonfiction by the Georgia Writers Association.
