Jim Loter's Publications

  • Cathal Black's "Pigs": Ambivalence, Confinement, and the Search for an Irish Sense of Place

    • Contemporary Irish Cinema: From The Quiet Man to Dancing at Lughnasa
    • 1999
    Authors: Jim Loter

    Irish culture is defined by a paradox: it is credited with a strong sense of place, yet it allows those places to go to ruin. Cathal Black's 1984 film "Pigs" operates within that paradox and creates a filmic sense of place that exists between stable placehood and the tumult of unsettledness. The ambivalence toward place breaks with the Western tradition that equates place with stability and, thus, defines a uniquely Irish sense of place.
