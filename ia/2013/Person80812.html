Paul Marsh's Publications

  • HR and Other Swear Words

    • Austin and Macauley
    • April 2009
    Authors: Paul Marsh

    Chosen to work in HR but worried about its reputation? Looking to make a positive impact as quickly as possible? Concerned about how you can really 'add value'? To a lot of people in business 'Human Resources' (HR) are a necessary evil, seen to be sitting at the top of a proverbial ivory tower and having very little influence on company results. But it doesn't have to be this way. "HR & Other Swear Words" is aimed at practitioners new to the profession and intent on breaking down the stereotypes that exist around HR and its value. It will also benefit established professionals who are reflecting on their own impact and are looking for different perspectives. Quite simply, this guide and its practical, easy to read format gives you all the practical advice and tips you need to really make a difference and avoid your role becoming another swearword or obstacle to be overcome in your organization. It's ultimately about having credibility, using expertise and delivering results; here are the simple lessons that will help give you that magic formula for success.
