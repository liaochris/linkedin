Christopher A. Crane's Summary

Chris Crane is President & CEO of Edify, a humanitarian organization he founded in September 2009 to make loans to Christian schools educating impoverished children in Africa and Latin America. These schools are owned by local entrepreneurs and provide strong education and life skills training including English, computers and entrepreneurship/business training. The schools teach children integrity and to love others. The schools charge $5 to $20 per month tuition and are financially sustainable. The schools repay the loans which are then recycled to other schools to build more classrooms, computer labs, buy busses, etc. As of Dec. 2012, Edify has made loans to over 700 schools.

Specialties: Financing low-fee, financially sustainable schools in the developing world. Improving learning at developing country schools. Managing non-profits with best practices learned at for-profit businesses. Scaling non-profit and for-profit organizations. Education technology.

Chief Executive Officer

COMPS InfoSystems, Inc.

August 1992 – February 2000 (7 years 7 months)

Acquired COMPS InfoSystems, Inc. in San Diego in 1992 and served as its President & CEO until he sold the company in February 2000. Under Crane’s leadership, COMPS grew from a small, print-based publishing firm with coverage of commercial real estate sale information in four Western states into an electronic database publisher with 420 employees and coverage of the 50 top real estate markets nationwide. During his eight-year tenure, Crane oversaw the acquisition of 13 companies. He raised four rounds of venture capital and listed the company in an initial public offering (IPO).
