Jordan Barnett MD FACEP FAAEM's Summary

I am a practicing Board Certified Emergency Medicine physician. I provide Emergency Medical care and lead the teams responsible for stabilizing individuals suffering from acute illness and trauma.

My career arc has transitioned me from being an emergency worker with New York City Emergency Medical Services, to obtaining my medical degree, providing emergency medical care as a physician at receiving hospitals ranging in specialty from Level I and II trauma centers, to regional Rape Crisis Centers,as well as regional Spinal Cord Centers, to providing educational and keynote lectures, teaching, and now providing medical command services to multiple basic and advanced life support services in the greater Philadelphia area. This role has recently been extended by my opening my own EMS consulting firm.

I am hoping to expand my career into a role with either business or government, leveraging my experience as a lecturer, teacher, medical director, EMS manager, political advisor, and my prior field experience, as well as my roles with management consulting and informatics. Opportunities in research, review and analysis, and promotional medical education are also of interest.

I love working as a Emergency Medical Physician but I am also open to other opportunities.

If you are interested in tapping me as a resource or knowledge base for the management of trauma, pharmacological treatment and medical management of acute illness, or 911 systems and operations, please don't hesitate to contact me.

I also publish a blog discussing the politics and current state of emergency medical care, located at http://theemsblog.com/

I look forward to speaking with you!

Jordan Barnett MD FACEP FAAEM's Publications

  • Nitric Oxide Metabolite Levels in Acute Vaso-occlusive Sickle-cell Crisis

    • Academic Emergency Medicine
    • December 1996
    Authors: Jordan Barnett MD FACEP FAAEM, Bernard L. Lopez MD, Samir K. Ballas MD, Theodore A. Christopher MD, Linda Davis-Moon RN, Xin-liang Ma MD, PhD

    Keywords:
    Pharmacologic Research: nitric oxide;NO; sickle-cell anemia;vaso-occlusive crisis;sickle-cell painful crisis;emergency department
    ABSTRACT
    Objectives: 1) To measure nitric oxide (NO) metabolite levels in patients presenting to the ED in acute vasoocclusive sickle-cell crisis (SCC), and 2) to determine whether a relationship exists between NO metabolite levels and pain.
    Conclusion: NO metabolite levels vary in SCC patients. Elevated levels are associated with lower pain scores, while lower levels are associated with higher pain scores, indicating that NO metabolites may potentially represent a marker for compensatory mechanisms in SCC tissue ischemia. Further work is needed to delineate the usefulness of NO metabolites in assessing the seventy of SCC.
