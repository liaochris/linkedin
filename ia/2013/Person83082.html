Lucia Mauro's Summary

For the past 25 years, I have devoted my career to writing, speaking and educating others about topics that continue to inspire me: dance, theater, music, the arts; Italian history and culture; and fascinating people from many walks of life. In addition, my Italy photography visually complements my writing and reveals the country's endlessly intriguing layers.

My goal is and always has been to share my stories, my experience and my knowledge with a wide audience.

As my career trajectory continues to evolve, I am engaged in two major projects that center on Italy, film, dance, history and technology:

1) My independent film, Anita: Mother of War, is entering its pre-production phase. I wrote the screenplay, which chronicles the life of 19th century Brazilian revolutionary Anita Garibaldi -- wife of Giuseppe, Italy's hero of Unification -- and explores the personal challenges faced by individuals committed to a cause. It's propelled by an encounter between Anita and outspoken American journalist Margaret Fuller. Stephen Baldwin is signed as Giuseppe Garibaldi, and Brazilian actress Leticia Sabatella is Anita. Rick Snyder of Chicago's renowned Steppenwolf Theatre is directing, and Italian cinematographer Giulio Pietromarchi is director of photography. Filming is scheduled for Spring 2014 in Sardinia, Italy. More info: http://www.anitathemovie.com

2) In response to ever-changing education models, I created and served as host of Think You Know Dance, a 52-webisode on-line dance history series that is digitally archived and available to enhance arts programs in schools, libraries and more. It consists of (52) 35-minute sessions on the development of Western concert dance (ballet, modern, post-modern, music theater) and includes my audio lecture, visuals and video clips -- all providing a clear sociopolitical context. More info: http://ambientstream.com/landing/lucia/

Lucia Mauro's Experience

Owner & Host

Think You Know Dance On-Line Dance History Webinar Series

November 2011 – Present (2 years)

Created and completed 52-webisode on-line dance history series, which originally aired live from November 2011 to October 2012. Archives of the entire series and individual webisodes are available for libraries, colleges, universities, dance schools, dance troupes, and individuals wishing to enhance their knowledge of dance's multifaceted lineage. The engaging 35-minute webinar program highlights pivotal milestones in dance history (ballet, modern, post-modern, music theater, and more). Each webisode incorporates my audio lecture, visuals, and spectacular video excerpts. Each era and its evolution are placed in a clear sociopolitical context. Adjunct Professor of Dance History

Loyola University Chicago

Educational Institution; 1001-5000 employees; Higher Education industry

January 2008 – Present (5 years 10 months)

My core course, Dance History: Renaissance to the Present, is offered through Loyola University's expanding Department of Fine and Performing Arts. It provides students with a solid foundation for understanding the origins of ballet, modern dance and other styles over the ages; how they are part of a continuum of crucial developments within the performing arts; and their importance within a larger sociopolitical context. I also teach a Writing Intensive Independent Study, titled Dance Research, Writing and Criticism, for serious students interested in honing their arts writing skills in a variety of formats. In Summer 2010, I taught Dance History at Loyola's John Felice Rome Center in Rome, Italy.

Original Story & Screenwriter

Anita: Mother of War Film

January 2005 – Present (8 years 10 months) Chicago and Italy

Inspired by Anita Garibaldi's bold equestrian monument on Rome's Janiculum Hill, I've spent a decade researching and writing an independent feature film on this unsung Brazilian revolutionary's life. This is an impassioned collaboration between American, Brazilian and Italian artists, including Stephen Baldwin, Leticia Sabatella, Carol Lawrence, Paolo Bonacelli, Giulio Pietromarchi and Rick Synder (of Steppenwolf Theatre). Shooting is targeted for 2014 in Sardinia, Italy. Anita: Mother of War imagines an invigorating encounter between gaucho guerrilla fighter Anita Garibaldi and uncompromising American journalist Margaret Fuller in a battered Rome under siege. Rooted in the 19th century revolutions of South America and Italy, the film probes the motivations and sacrifices of historic figures, including Anita’s famed husband Giuseppe Garibaldi, determined to fight for social justice.

Writer-Speaker-Photographer-Educator

luciamauro.com

1988 – Present (25 years)

I have been writing about the arts in Chicago since 1988 for many publications, including the Chicago Tribune, Chicago Magazine, Playbill. I am the former dance critic for Chicago Public Radio, and the former Chicago Dance Examiner for examiner.com I've written three arts-career books for McGraw-Hill. Italy is the focus of my writing and photography. I have traveled to every region and speak frequently on Italian culture and life. I am the National Italy Culture & Travel Examiner for examiner.com. and have published two books of photography: "Frieze Frame: Textures & Colors of Italy" (2004) and "Frieze Frame II: Textures & Colors of Italy" (2006). I host many live conversations with artists, including the Auditorium Theatre's FireSide Chats, the Arts Club's InConversations, and American Ballet Theatre's Spotlight Seminars in Chicago and at New York's Metropolitan Opera House.
