George Gramelspacher's Summary

George is an Operations and General Manager with a solid technical knowledge in the manufacture of Veneer and Hardwood Plywood, both flat plywood and curved or molded plywood.
George has experience in the lamination of curved (molded) plywood using Radio (High) frequency and electrical resistance heat. He is familiar with the capabilities and limitations of the various adhesives used in the manufacturing of hardwood plywood, as well as with the properties of particleboard, MDF, lumber core, veneer core and other raw materials used in the manufacture of hardwood plywood. He is familiar with the machining of plywood and lumber products. He is well versed in the fabrication of veneer fancy faces and decorative veneer faces. He is experienced in the manufacture of Sliced and Rotary Cut Hardwood veneer.

He served on Board of Directors of two manufacturing companies, and as the General Manager of a small business. George has an interest in getting involved with manufacturing operations as a consultant, investor, in management, or in any manner in which he can be helpful.

Specialties

Comprehensive knowledge of veneer and plywood used to find profitable solutions to manufacturing problems in Hardwood Veneer and Plywood Manufacturing operations.

June 1999 – Present (14 years 7 months)

Jasper Veneer Mills has been manufacturing Hardwood Veneer since 1895. Our roots are in the Commercial Rotary Veneer business. However, for the past 25 years, we have been manufacturing Fine Hardwood Veneer for the Office Furniture, Residential Furniture and Kitchen Cabinet manufacturers, and Hardwood Panel Manufacturers.
Responsible for all operations of JVM, which include:
· Sourcing, measuring, grading, selecting and purchasing veneer logs.
· Arranging the shipment of the logs to the mill.
· Overseeing the slicing of the logs into veneer.
· Grading and pricing the veneer, and selling veneer.
· Receiving the veneer into the warehouse, loading sold veneer onto the trucks for delivery to customers.
· Invoicing, and maintaining the financial records of the company.

Jasper Veneer Mills, Inc.

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Paper & Forest Products industry

June 1974 – June 1999 (25 years 1 month)

Jasper Veneer Mills, Inc., was originally a manufacturer of rotary veneer that is used as the core and crossband in hardwood plywood, and also used in boxes, crates, baskets, and brick dividers. JVM also manufactured crating lumber used in packaging office furniture. JVM employed about 75 persons.
Accomplishments
· In 1983, while employed at JWP, George joined the Board of Directors of Jasper Veneer Mills, Inc. George encouraged and materially participated in the modernization of JVM's green end and other operations in an effort to lower the costs of veneer. JVM had a reputation for well manufactured veneer that was especially useful for curved plywood. Late in 1984, Jasper Veneer Mills ceased operation as a rotary veneer manufacturer.
· As a member of the Board of Directors, in 1985, George developed the methods and systems to move JVM from producing Commercial Rotary Cut veneer to the production of sliced Hardwood Veneer. He began buying logs and contracting with veneer mills to have the logs sliced into Fine Hardwood Veneer.
-Upon completing his college studies in 1974, George worked as a supervisor in rough mill and veneer mill, and as a log buyer. He received an excellent education in the fundamentals of Rotary Veneer Manufacturing.
-As a student he worked as a laborer in the lumber yard, cleaning the cooking vats and the veneer dryers, and rafters, helping to manually fire the boiler, and off bearing machines.
