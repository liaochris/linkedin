Matt Hendrick's Summary

Awareness -> Conversion -> Activation -> Retention.

Although I own a myriad of different responsibilities related to both product and marketing, at the end of the day the underlying goal of anything I'm working on inevitably falls into one of the 4 buckets above.

I'm the voice of the customer as we develop and evolve the product, and I'm the voice of the company as we market said product to potential customers. I prefer a channel-based strategy as the foundation for any good marketing plan, but have no shortage of ideas for ways to "hack" the customer acquisition process and deliver low cost/high growth opportunities.

I love what I do, and I gravitate toward startups for two primary reasons: I thoroughly enjoy the challenge of building something out of nothing, and I greatly appreciate the potential value that having an equity stake at an early stage holds.

Director of Product Marketing and Analytics

ChargeSmart, Inc.

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Financial Services industry

October 2009 – January 2012 (2 years 4 months) San Francisco, CA

I'm passionate about building great products, developing brand strategies and key messaging, and using a combination of SEO, SEM, and social media to acquire users and sustain long term, profitable growth. As such, the lines between product management, marketing, and the metrics analysis of both can become quite woven together, and my goal is to use every available resource to move the business forward.

- I <3 metrics and data. I live in Google Analytics, Marin Software, and our own proprietary metrics dashboard. I manage a massive Adwords and Microsoft AdCenter campaign with our SEM partner, and have delivered consistent, large scale growth over an extended period of time.

- Customer Acquisition. SEO. SEM. Social. Word of mouth. PR. This isn't just about "spray and pray" - it's about understanding who your customers are and how best to reach them. I love watching our customer base grow.

- Conversion. We're getting people to the site, but are we converting them into paying customers? Conversion funnels and rates, on-page copy, exit points, A/B tests, ROI, etc, etc, etc; I love them all, and live them daily.

- Retention. Customers don't disappear after they complete their first transaction - so how can we extend their lifetime value?

- Trust building. Every transaction at ChargeSmart requires a customer to "take out their wallet" to make a payment, and this takes trust. We're building trust with consumers by increasing our visibility on the major search engines and display networks, building brand awareness in the general marketplace, and pursuing ongoing public relations and media campaigns.

- And...what else? I love to write, have a deep understanding of social, and possess a passion for building and marketing awesomeness. ChargeSmart has been one heck of a ride thus far, and I truly believe we have a unique opportunity to be part of something big in the payments space. Keep an eye out for what we're up to next.

Senior Market Manager

BACARDI USA

Privately Held; 5001-10,000 employees; Wine and Spirits industry

April 2004 – October 2008 (4 years 7 months) Northern California

In this role, I led the local marketing and distribution efforts for BACARDI in Northern CA. My goal was twofold: 1) to develop local marketing programs and sales promotions that enabled our distributors' salesforce to increase distribution of our product line at accounts across the region; and 2) to work directly with restaurant/bar/nightclub management, developing marketing programs and promotions that increased sell-through of our products in their account.

One of the keys to my success in this position was my understanding of the operational challenges faced by many on-premise establishments, and my ability to use that knowledge to develop marketing programs that met and overcame any objections. Many owner/operators simply don't have the time to think about what "marketing" means for their business, much less how it affects their bottom line. As a key local marketing partner, I was able to translate BACARDI's national marketing objectives into unique, account level programs that increased our total sales while also positively impacting my customers' revenue and bottom line.

I excelled quickly in the BACARDI organization, and only left because I had a drive to pursue new business opportunities that were more entrepreneurial and internet-related in nature. The experience taught me an incredible amount about what it takes to develop successful marketing programs that speak to both B2B (BACARDI to distributor) and B2C (BACARDI to bar/restaurants) channels, and those years were a great jumping off point for my career.
