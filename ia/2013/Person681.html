Aron Art Lasky's Summary

A native of sunny Southern California. Aron grew up in the Palm Springs area and moved to the San Fernando Valley in 1971.

Since 1979, Lasky has designed and fabricated solutions for a multitude of entertainment and industry challenges. His unique career essentially began at the birthplace of innovative design: Walt Disney Productions in Burbank, California. Some of his first creations there were animatronic characters for the Tokyo and Florida EPCOT theme parks. Lasky’s mechanical design capabilities, critical thinking, and paradigmatic skills, led him to successfully interface with a wide variety of companies within the entertainment, product development, and manufacturing industries.

Other than Walt Disney Productions, these companies include;
• Dittos of California (fashion design)
• Stan Winston Studios (movie props, special effects)
• Worlds of Wonder (prototype toys, product development, R&D)
• On-Time Inventions (Product development, R&D)
• SGB Enterprises, Inc. (Avionic simulators)
• NCB Entertainment (Marketing, P.O.P.)

Themed Entertainment Industry leaders;
• Technifex, Inc. (Theme park, casinos, special effects, attractions, trade show booths)
• Thinkwell, Inc. (Themed Experiences)
• Lester Creative, Inc. (museum, hotel, casino projects)
• Creative Presentations, Inc. (museums, interactives)
• LA ProPoint, Inc. (theater, themed venues)
• The Attraction Services Company, Inc.
• SEGA Entertainment Group, Inc.

Lasky has served as Project Lead, Show and Set Designer, Art Director, and Senior Mechanical Designer on hundreds of projects and operates his own mechanical design, computer graphics and consulting business.

His clients include commercial and private businesses and the services he provides range from mechanical design, show and set design, computer-aided design, proof-of-concept visualizations, and 3D modeling, corporate presence, website development and hosting, video editing, photography, photographic manipulation and special effects.

  • Harrah's Multi-Media Tower

    • 2004 to 2006
    Team Members: Aron Art Lasky, Technifex, inc.

    New Orleans, USA (2004-2006)
    A 360° rear-projection tower, 40 feet tall. 20 feet in diameter at the top, 13 feet at the circular bar. This spectacular center-piece for the Masquerade Bar utilizes 20 high-end projectors mounted on a center structure surrounded by custom-fit acrylic glass panels for the rear-projection surface. Over the bar is a circular stage with Fauxfire™ around it's edge. Fauxfire™ is a Technifex-original product, incorporating steam, air and high-intensity lighting to simulate an all-too-real fire effect. Dancers on this circular stage appear to be dancing within 3-foot-tall flames. A truly breathtaking and eye-popping show.

    Built in California, all the structural elements had to be designed to be disassembled, shipped, and transported on freeways, through the casino lobby and gaming floor, then reassembled on-site. Since there was no room for cranes, the mammoth structure was carefully erected using a pair of heavy-duty forklifts working in unison.

    Air'n Art Designs was brought on-board from the incept to head the 3D structural and mechanical design, as well as the production effort. Engineering assistance from Entertainment Engineering helped to make this Technifex project an inspired success.
