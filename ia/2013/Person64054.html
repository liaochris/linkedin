Blood Bank Technologist

Willis-Knighton Health System

Nonprofit; 5001-10,000 employees; Hospital & Health Care industry

April 2007 – April 2009 (2 years 1 month) Shreveport, Louisiana

Duties included ABO and Rh typing, antibody screens, antibody identification, crossmatch testing, DAT, Rhogam work-ups, and preparation of neonatal aliquots for transfusion. Also responsible for preparation of monthly blood utilization reports, maintaining readiness for inspections by CAP and other regulatory agencies, preventative maintenance and quality control testing in the areas of Blood Bank and Serology.

Responsible for instruction and demonstration of prenatal and neonatal immunohematology and serology testing to medical technology students.

Responsible for validation and correlation studies for implementation of Immucor Galileo Echo automated Blood Bank instrument for pretransfusion testing; including ABO and Rh typing, antibody screens and antibody identification.

Instructor - Part Time Temporary Position

LSU Health Sciences Center

Educational Institution; 1001-5000 employees; Hospital & Health Care industry

August 2008 – December 2008 (5 months) Shreveport, Louisiana

Immunohematology Laboratory Instructor in Department of Clinical Laboratory Science. This was a part time, temporary position.

Provide instruction and direction to medical technology students for completing laboratory assignments which included ABO and Rh typing; including discrepancies, antibody screens and identification, elutions, and problem crossmatches. Prepared lectures to complement laboratory tasks.

Medical Technologist - Immunohematology Reference Laboratory and Product Qualification Laboratory

LifeShare Blood Centers

Nonprofit; 501-1000 employees; Nonprofit Organization Management industry

March 2005 – April 2007 (2 years 2 months) Shreveport, Louisiana

Performed all expected duties in an AABB accredited Immunohematology Reference Laboratory (IRL): ABO and Rh typing, antibody screens, crossmatch testing, antibody identification techniques such as adsorptions and elutions, transfusion reaction investigation, platelet antibody screening, washing of red blood cell units, freezing and deglycerolization of rare units of red blood cells, maintenance and quality control.

Product Qualification Laboratory duties included CMV testing, quality control testing of blood products to ensure suitability for transfusion and adherence to FDA standards of purity and potency, flow cytometric analysis to ensure adequate leukoreduction of red blood cell and platelet products.

Medical Technologist - Transfusion Services and Hematology Departments

LSU Health Sciences Center

Educational Institution; 1001-5000 employees; Hospital & Health Care industry

March 1999 – March 2005 (6 years 1 month) Shreveport, Louisiana

Performed all expected duties in a large academic institution transfusion services department: crossmatches, types and screens, antibody identification, elutions, irradiation of products, transfusion reaction investigation, maintenance and quality control. Also performed Hepatitis and HIV serologic testing to ensure suitability of organ donors. Responsible for monitoring and operation of Cobe SPECTRA during therapeutic apheresis procedures.

Hematology duties included specimen processing, automated CBC and differential analysis and review of automated analysis and performance of manual differential if indicated. Performed manual cell counts and differentials on various body fluids. Duties also included automated coagulation analysis and quality control and preventative maintenance for hematology and coagulation analyzers.
