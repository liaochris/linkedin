Kevin Mershon's Summary

I am a software developer, primarily focused on web technologies. Most of my career has involved using Java, PHP, and the HTML/CSS/Javascript trifecta in the design of data collection systems for the oil industry. Since entering the field in 2003 I have worked a consultant most of the time, having finally made the jump to regular employment in 2011.

I work comfortably in every major operating system. I frequently use OS Virtualization software such as VirtualBox, Xen, and Parallels. I am experienced with several nuanced approaches to software engineering such as functional programming, object-oriented design, reflection, and normalized relational database design. I'm fond of pair programming, and always use a bug tracker and code repository. I am a firm believer in self-documenting code and test-driven development, and prioritize the user experience above all else. I am an experienced team leader, and have personally trained several people in web development from a novice to competent intermediate level.

In addition to the specialties listed, I also provide general-to-specialized levels of technical support, depending on context. My programming expertise is web applications, clean and intuitive GUIs, socket networking, and custom integration. I am also completely fascinated with cryptography.

I work best under a little pressure, and in a comfortable environment where I can take a break when I need, but still retain my focus. Beginning in 2013 I will only be accepting part-time consulting work where I am permitted to telecommute (work remotely). Exceptions such as commissioning projects and sales meetings are negotiable.

Kevin Mershon's Experience

Lead Software Developer

Ramsgate Engineering / ProGauge Technologies

January 2008 – Present (5 years 9 months)

Ramsgate Engineering and ProGauge Technologies are sister companies, and I work under both titles depending on the circumstances.

I was initially hired to build an application layer to utilize an existing MySQL database model, which focuses on oilfield exploration and statistical analysis. This statistical analysis application is known as ProGauge Prime, and is composed of C#, ActionScript 3 (Flex charting), and JavaScript/AJAX (Bing Maps 3D, HTML5/canvas). The application itself is actually a webservice thin client, which communicates to a Java EE5 server layer running on GlassFish.

After developing ProGauge Prime to a fully-featured release version, I was joined by two novice programmers. Together, we designed a new data-collection device called the ProGauge ICP (Intermediate Communication Portal). The device functions very similarly to a cellular phone, in that it possesses the ability to perform limited tasks on its own, but relies on an Internet connection and the computational power of our server cluster at the main office for heavy lifting. Our clients claim that this service matches, and in some cases surpasses, the functionality of high-end SCADA (Supervisory Control & Data Aquisition) systems, for a fraction of the cost. Data is collected from PLC's and primitive sensors, and synchronized across encrypted connections to our server cluster for post-processing and analysis within ProGauge Prime. The device also plays host to an easy-to-use operator interface for manual data collection and realtime alarm reporting.

Currently, we have about a dozen deployments throughout California, the middle-east, and Africa. As of August 2nd, my team consists of 4 junior programmers, and 2 seniors (counting myself), with 4 additional part-time consultants participating in side-projects as needed.
