Don Green, LEED AP's Summary

Don has spent his career in environmental assessment, water quality, policy development, developing partnerships, stormwater, watersheds, environmental education and other environmental/policy groups. He started out chasing turtles in graduate school at MTSU and worked as a field biologist with TDOT then jumped to TN Department of Environment and Conservation's 319 Nonpoint Source Program as the staff biologist/GIS/stormwater monitoring (stay away from trying to catch storm events!). The program was moved to the TN Department of Ag where Don became Assistant Manager; where he developed and managed projects (grants) such as Project Wet, WaterWorks! and TN Growth Readiness and was responsible for contracts, grant oversight, employee supervision, etc. Opportunity hit and he moved to develop the Stormwater Program at Franklin, TN, where he served as Stormwater Coordinator for 5 yrs. Don is currently a LEED AP environmental consultant interested in green infrastructure and how energy (alternative energy) and water quality nexus and how 'we' can reduce our impact on the planet we inhabit. The environment has always been his avocation and vocation...in his spare time, he is past board member of Keep Williamson County Beautiful, Cumberland River Compact, and Southeast Stormwater Association. He is presently on the board and VP of TN Stormwater Association. (past member of Tree Commission of City of Brentwood); Franklin, TN Sustainability Committee, member of Program and Advocacy committees and past Chair of Statewide Advocacy of Mid TN USGBC Chapter along with merit badge counselor with his son's Boy Scout Troop.....As of July '09, he is Water Quality Supervisor City of Chattanooga, a Phase I Stormwater program. He supervises 5+ employees in their industrial Inspections, illicit discharge, water quality program. He works closely with the sustainability program and aids in development of policy.

Specialties

Environmental Policy, LEED AP, Low Impact Development, Green Infrastructure, Energy/Water Nexus, water quality, erosion and sediment control, stormwater, environmental education, web site development, writing.
