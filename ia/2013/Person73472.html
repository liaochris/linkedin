Katie Eastman, Psy.D, MSW's Summary

Dr. Katie Eastman, founder and CEO of Children's Palliative Care Community is a key figure in the lives of those struggling to understand death, the process of dying and how it informs living. An advocate in the field of hospice and palliative care for over 25 years, Katie is a transition coach, consultant and writer supporting others in living today better than yesterday. Katie holds a Doctorate in Clinical Psychology and a Masters in Social Work with a Health Care specialization. Katie lives with her husband and daughter in Washington. Visit www.katieeastman.com for more information.


Specialties: Transition Coaching, Palliative Care, End-of-life Care, Oncology, Writing, Consulting, Advocating, Public Speaking, Media, Contributing Author, "Percolate" (Hay House) 4/4/14 Strategic Planning, Program Development, Non-Profit management.

Katie Eastman, Psy.D, MSW's Projects

  • Executive Producer, Fighting Heart: The Jason DeBusk Story

    • February 1991 to Present
    Team Members: Katie Eastman, Psy.D, MSW

    A film project about a teen struggling with Cancer who discovers life's purpose is to make a difference.
    When cancer strikes a young football phenomenon in gridiron-crazy Texas just as his high school sports career should be launching him into some kind of teen status stratosphere, the heartbreak is palpable.
    But instead of a standard "kid-with-cancer" afterschool special that collapses from the weight of melodrama or descends into an overly sentimental spiral, Fighting Heart soars.
    Based on the true, uniquely inspirational journey of 15 year-old Jason DeBusk, the Fighting Heart script brilliantly conveys the uplifting spirit of his battle in a way that vanquishes conventional views of contemporary adolescents as self-absorbed, gadget-obsessed, social climbing party animals.
    Jason responds to his diagnosis with refreshing grit, grace, humor, faith, and near supernatural personal strength, becoming a beacon of positive change for everyone around him: his family, friends, fellow patients, and especially a newly minted social worker just starting her career.
    Award-winning screenwriter James Flamberg (Nurse Betty) has rendered a magical retelling of Jason's life. His powerful narrative walks a tightrope between edge and empathy, depicting the raw emotion, the sleepless nights, the agonizing decisions, the deep relationships, and the vomit, hugs and kisses, deftly balancing the philosophy and reality of battling cancer.
    Fighting Heart is about the tidal wave of compassion that results when a truly heroic adolescent lives as much as he possibly can until he dies. It serves as a reminder to all of us to love our lives every day. As it fills the screen in theaters across the country, the story brought to life in this script will inspire like few others.
