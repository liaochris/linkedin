Douglas Hopper's Summary

Douglas Hopper is a writer, editor, multimedia storyteller and communications specialist with more than 10 years experience creating and managing interactive media programs and initiatives. As a recognized expert in global health, international development and HIV/AIDS, he has produced and edited content for NPR, the National Press Foundation, the Harvard School of Public Health and a broad range of leading global health organizations.

After graduating from the University of Oregon School of Journalism and Communication, Douglas was selected from a national applicant pool for the inaugural NPR Kroc Fellowship. During his time at NPR, he was a founding producer for Tell Me More, a groundbreaking program about diversity and global issues. He recently served as the lead producer and editor for Harvard’s landmark AIDS@30 International Symposium and subsequent academic journal publication.

Douglas is currently a Communications Officer at the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation.

Douglas Hopper's Projects

  • AIDS@30 International Symposium

    • October 2010 to April 2012
    Team Members: Douglas Hopper, Martha Henry, Mansour Farahani, Jonathan Schatz, Orla Kennedy, Tamera Kingston, Christen Reardon, Kristin Brown

    To commemorate the 30th anniversary of AIDS, the Harvard School of Public Health convened an international symposium to assess what we have learned from AIDS and how to effectively apply those lessons toward ending the epidemic. 500 global health leaders from around the world participated in the two-day meeting on Dec 1-2, 2011. The interactive conversation was advanced further in a special supplement of the Journal of Acquired Immune Deficiency Syndromes (JAIDS), which was presented at the International AIDS Conference, AIDS 2012, in Washington, DC.

  • Tell Me More with Michel Martin

    • March 2007 to September 2010
    Team Members: Douglas Hopper, Monika Evstatieva, Jennifer Longmire-Wright, Michel Martin

    "From the opinions of global newsmakers to listeners...personal experiences of life-changing travel...the wisdom of renowned thinkers, activists and spiritual leaders...and intimate dispatches of daily life around the world from NPR News correspondents on the ground...the NPR talk show Tell Me More brings fresh voices and perspectives to public radio. Capturing the headlines, issues and pleasures relevant to multicultural life in America, the daily one-hour series is hosted by award-winning journalist Michel Martin. Tell Me More marks Martin's first role in hosting a daily program. She views it as an opportunity to focus on the stories, experiences, ideas and people important in contemporary life but often not heard." - NPR.org
