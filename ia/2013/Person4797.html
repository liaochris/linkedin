Allan Suarez's Summary

A Serial entrepreneur Allan successfully secured funding from SOFTBANK LATIN AMERICA VENTURES principally funded by SOFTBANK to startup his vision - LatCom Communications in 2000. Latino actor/comedian John Leguizamo subsequently joined Allan as a partner and spokesperson for iCaramba.com - LatCom's social network property geared toward the New Generation Latino.

LatCom Communication became a leader in New Generation Latino media and entertainment with properties that generated millions of targeted impressions including: iCaramba.com, iCaramba College Tour, iCaramba Comedy Series, iCaramba U. (Latino University Magazine), LatinoGreeks.com (fraternity / sorority social network), Latinos Step (a live collegiate event series), and the animated series Lugar Heights. All properties exclusively targeted the New Generation Latino market making LatCom one of only a handful of companies that specialized in this demographic.

In addition to Allan's corporate duties at LatCom he aIso managed all digital design, web programming, and printing initiatives. He lead the development and production of multiple in-house websites through all stages from concept, documentation, design, production, testing, and launch. He was also the Creative Director of LatCom's National Latino college publication - iCaramba U. (later re-named Latino University Magazine) which reached a circulation of 200,000 (audited by BPA). Allan also launched Piragua Productions which produced the first Latino animated TV series, ‘Lugar Heights’ airing on mun2 (the national cable broadcast network owned by NBC Universal) and on MTV's Latino bi-cultural cable channel Tr3s.

LatCom Communications was successfully sold in 2005 to Davidson Media Group - one of the largest multicultural radio groups in America with a focus on serving the country’s emerging Hispanic markets. In 2006 Davidson Media Group sold LatCom to Batanga.com - the premier online destination for U.S. Hispanics.
