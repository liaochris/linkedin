Beth Bridges' Summary

Over the last 10 years I have attended over 2,300 networking events. That’s not even counting one-on-one coffee meetings, client get-togethers and speaking to groups. Why would I do so much networking? Because there was no budget – and very few other marketing resources – available to me when I began working at the Chamber.

I had to get the job done with what I had. And that was networking. You cannot overestimate the importance of getting outside the office, meeting new people, strengthening connections and giving as much value as you can.

Along the way, I also developed a great appreciation for social media. It’s simply a tool for social networking. All the principals are the same as in person networking. You still do the same things and treat people the same way; you’re just using the computer as your communication channel.

I've developed and run networking events to make it easier for more people in my community to connect with other people including Speed Networking, Virtual Networking and leads clubs. I've also conducted live, hands on Facebook and in-person networking training for the same purpose of sharing information and strengthening other businesses.
