Todd Anderson's Experience

Software Engineer

Civinomics

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Civic & Social Organization industry

November 2011 – Present (1 year 10 months) San Francisco Bay Area

We have created a platform that empowers communities to crowdsource their wisdom. Having reached beta in April of 2013, we still have many important features to implement. When I first joined the team, I developed an iOS application that we were hired to use for interviewing 5,000 people over the course of a year in Santa Cruz and San Francisco. Since then I've created a functional testing framework for our web platform, trained my colleagues how to add to it and I am now tackling social networking integration for it.

Software Engineer

Wireless Social Networking, Inc.

December 2007 – February 2010 (2 years 3 months) San Diego

Wireless Social Networking got its start as the website T9space.com, the first way people with mobile phones could use myspace over the mobile internet. When I came on board, I cleaned up what was a bit of a tangled assortment of translation scripts for enabling these people to use any one of about 10 makor social networking sites from old school Nokias, for example. We were very popular in Southeast Asia. My best achievement was in creating a generalized engine based on these translators that allowed our users to collectively decide what parts of the web page were needed for viewing and what parts were not. This was a very handy feature in the early days of the mobile internet, and coupled with our website moniker we added to messages sent with the help of our platform, we achieved viral growth for a period of at least a year. It was a great experience to help fine tune the server to be able to handle this growth in traffic, ultimately migrating to an amazon cloud instance.

Assistant / Web Designer

Henry Miller Library

April 2005 – October 2005 (7 months)

I was on my way to Seattle to crash with a friend who was going to help me get my foot in the door of the tech industry, and wanted to camp out in Big Sur for a couple days before heading north. On day 2 I'm winging the juice on the Library's internet and Magnus offers me a job there.
It took me about 10 seconds to think to myself, I can live in Big Sur rent free, about a mile away from one of my favorite surf spots ever. "I WOULD BE HAPPY TO WORK HERE MAGNUS."
That moment was the start of what turned out to be a wonderful 6 months living in the heart of Big Sur. The people of the Henry Miller Library and the Big Sur coast blessed me with an energy I've carried with me ever since.
