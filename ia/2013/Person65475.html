Joel Kessel's Summary

Why Joel Kessel is on LinkedIn:
I'm on LinkedIn to connect with and help resource-constrained organizations—nonprofits, small businesses, start-ups—gain third-party credibility, get their story on TV, radio, online and in the newspaper, and communicate with impact for greater awareness and more sales/fundraising. I do this by:
► Building a compelling value proposition, message, and story.
► Creating a steady stream of content about your organization, programs and services.
► Identifying and building relationships with journalists, trade associations, investors, employees, customers.
► Positioning you and/or your organization as experts in your industry and contributors to your community.

I help organizations with the following:
► Create news for your organization—whitepapers, surveys, special reports, events, community engagement.
► Pitch your organization’s news to media—news releases, press kits, interview preparation.
► Develop content to fuel your social media—blogs, posts, tweets.
► Build a strategic communications plan—ensuring integration in your business and marketing plans.
► Write copy—for all of the above and for websites, advertising, newsletters, brochures.

Specialties: Public relations and communications for nonprofit organizations, small businesses, start ups and early stage companies, corporate communications, media relations, marketing communications, marketing public relations, public communications, publicist, press releases, news releases, writing, business writing, speech writing, online writing, writing letters, white papers, copy editing, strategic communications, communication plan.

Joel Kessel's Experience

President

Kessel Communications, LLC

2000 – Present (13 years)

Joel Kessel is a strategic communications and public relations consultant and the founder of Kessel Communications.

For more than 18 years, Kessel has been working with organizations of all sizes from start-ups to nonprofits to national brands who desire a SMARTer way to strategically communicate their story. He does this by helping organizations define and deliver their message, urging leadership and decision makers to think differently about their organization, and challenging their perceptions about how they communicate their story to those who matter.

Current and past organizations Kessel has worked with include BringShare, National Runaway Safeline (formerly National Runaway Switchboard), Ohio Association of Advanced Practice Nurses, Solid Waste Authority of Central Ohio, Brighten Hall, Venture Highway, National Forum on Disability Issues, and Weber Grills.

Manager, Public Relations/Communications

Chicago-Greater Illinois Chapter, National Multiple Sclerosis Society

1998 – 1999 (1 year)

Joel Kessel planned and implemented public relations campaigns in support of 10 fund-raising events which annually raised more than $6 million. Kessel's responsibilities also consisted of managing and implementing all internal and external communications of the chapter, including producing, writing and tracking budgets for its website, annual report, brochures, videos and other publicity/promotional materials, serving as spokesperson for special events and crisis situations, and managing an annual public relations and communications budget of $100,000.

Senior Account Executive

Golin/Harris Communications

1996 – 1998 (2 years)

• Managed grant and donor special events for Ronald McDonald House Charities; managed national public relations program for AirLifeLine

• Coordinated press events with celebrities such as President and Barbara Bush, Dustin Hoffman, Dorothy Hamill
• Handled the production, writing and budgets of video news releases, b-rolls, client videos, brochures, annual reports and other publicity/promotional materials
• Attended AirLifeLine’s quarterly meetings and presented 20-member board of directors with update of publicity efforts and strategic direction

Account Executive

Chris Ruys Communications
