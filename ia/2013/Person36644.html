Don Freeman's Publications

  • TouchPoint + SEES: A proven retention strategy

    • CAAHE Chronicle - Consortium for the Advancement of Adult Higher Education
    • April 2012
    Authors: Don Freeman

    Student retention is one of the most important issues facing higher education. Students that drop cost the institution thousands of dollars in lost investment and revenue. Student attrition is caused by a number of different variables outside of academic rigor. The challenge is establishing effective retention strategies to retain students and control costs. In June 2011, IPD developed and combined the Student Enrollment Experience Survey (SEES) with the TouchPoint retention calling service. We are excited to see positive retention results from this initiative. An effective retention program like TouchPoint and SEES focuses on activities that increase student retention rates and assist students in overcoming barriers to success. Student retention improves when focusing on the following key areas:

    ◦Early identification of student concerns and issues
    ◦Timely response and intervention promoting a student-centered culture

    Team Members: Don Freeman, Yvette Harrold

    Presentation title: Improving Quality in Recruitment and Retention.

    In light of the new Department of Education regulations that go into effect on July 1, 2011, all colleges and universities need to carefully assess the manner in which they set goals and evaluate their enrollment representatives’ performances. Students receiving exemplary service and accurate guidance during the enrollment process are more likely to begin degree programs and be successful in their programs. IPD’s retention solution combines feedback from students on the enrollment representative’s performance with a knowledge database and proactive student contact. This retention program has resulted in an average student retention rate of 92% within the first three courses of enrollment.
