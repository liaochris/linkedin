Ellen Beldner's Experience

Cofounder & Director of Product and UX

Magneto Inc.

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Internet industry

June 2012 – Present (1 year 7 months) Palo Alto, CA

Magneto is a stealth-mode seed-round startup funded in 2012. We're reinventing the calendar to help you build a better day.

I'm primarily responsible for Magneto's product and user experience. This includes product strategy and prioritization, product definition and design, UX design, product marketing, qualitative and quantitative user research, and detailed interaction design on web and mobile platforms. I manage relevant work of our UX-related employees and contractors, including visual/brand design, writing & voice, web engineering, and mobile UI engineering. Like any cofounder I do whatever it takes to get the job done, from email HTML to beer purchasing (although I'm better at the former than the latter).

Director of Consumer UX

Groupon

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; Internet industry

June 2011 – May 2012 (1 year) Palo Alto, CA

I built out Groupon's user research team, including a strong UX manager, and coordinated a partnership with the separate market research team. I directed design work for Groupon's mobile apps, including managing its mobile design team. I set up a classic iterative design and usability testing program that allowed the team to do a fast, relatively painless, and hugely successful redesign of Groupon's iOS and Android mobile apps. Additionally provided design direction for various Groupon.com programs, emails, and new products and features. Managed a team of 10.

Principal Designer

LinkedIn

Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; LNKD; Internet industry

September 2010 – June 2011 (10 months)

As Principal Designer, I was the UX lead for core LinkedIn product areas: homepage, groups, inbox, emails, LinkedIn Today, Signal. I conceptually designed the current signed-out LinkedIn homepage and saw the project through to launch; the conversion rate more than doubled to 9.1%. I oversaw the launch of LinkedIn Today, LI's flagship news product, and its "usefulness-based" marketing position on the LinkedIn signed-in homepage. (Rather than advertising LI Today we created space in the feed for top stories for that user, and advertised the product by providing great, relevant content to the user.)
