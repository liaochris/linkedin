Laura Schwartz's Summary

Communications wunderkind Laura Schwartz networked her way from answering phones for President Clinton’s press office at age 19 to producing more than 1,000 events as the White House Director of Events. In her book, Eat, Drink & Succeed! Laura discusses the power of partnership and positive networking to create both professional and personal success. As a professional speaker she takes those same details to stages and boardrooms around the world in her speaking series to empower, motivate and inspire each audience to Eat, Drink & Succeed!

Beyond the stage, Laura is on your screens as a commentator discussing leadership, networking and current events. In 2008 she covered the Presidential Campaign for the CBS Early Show, prior to joining CBS, Laura was the on air contributor for the Fox News Channel 2004-2007 and was a Special Correspondent to Larry King Live and CNN. Laura is also seen internationally on the BBC and Sir David Frost’s “Frost Over the World.”

In 2001 Laura founded White House Strategies, headquartered in Chicago, through which she travels domestically and internationally speaking to Fortune’s Top 50 and 500 companies, independent businesses, industry associations, universities and nonprofit organizations, teaching people of all ages how they, too can: EAT, DRINK & SUCCEED!

Laura serves on the boards of the AHA, Illinois Institute of Art and Event Solutions Magazine and is a member of the National Speakers Association.

Awards and Honors Include:

2012 Awarded the Steve Kimble Leadership Award for Excellence
2011 Named one of the ‘100 Most Influential Women’ by Today’s Chicago Woman Magazine
2010 Named one of Chicago’s Five ‘Most Bold and Beautiful’ for her contributions to the community by Chicago Magazine

2008 Recipient of the American University Excellence in Education Award
