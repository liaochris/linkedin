Dominga Martin's Summary

Dominga Martin, originally from Boston, Massachusetts, attended Clark Atlanta University where she studied Radio, TV, Film and Journalism.

In 2001, Dominga branched out to other creative endeavors and formed House of Ming, a boutique motion picture company that has developed two short films; bum in 2001 and Yellow in 2005, both of which she directed. bum received acclaim at the Roxbury Film Festival (2001). Yellow was featured in the 9th Annual Reel Sisters Film Festival (2006), the Hollywood Black Film Festival (2006), BET’s The Best Shorts (October, 2006) and a host of other festivals from NYC to LA. Additionally, the film was the winner of the “Best Short Narrative” category at the 5th Annual Hip Hop Odyssey International Film Festival (2007). Most recently, Yellow screened at BAM Rose Cinemas in Brooklyn, NY in their “Creatively Speaking Series” (May, 2008) and at the new MIST Theater in Harlem (Feb. 22-24th 2013).

Dominga has also directed and produced Fiche d'Identite, a documentary which follows the first President of Senegal’s – Leopold Sedhar Senghor – grand-daughter and her friends on their journey through the American immigration system, while portraying their life in the entertainment industry. It was invited to screen at the United Nations for the 11th Annual African American Women in Cinema Festival.

Dominga’s most successful music video production “Forever” (The Floacist & Musiq Soulchild) has been featured on MTV Music, VH-1 and BET, it has surpassed the 650,000 viewer mark on YouTube. "Forever" premiered on VIBE.com to over 1 Million viewers.

Over the last 14 years as a freelance writer, Dominga has authored numerous magazine articles ranging from lifestyle & culture to health and fitness.

Specialties: Journalism, creative writing, directing, producing, content creation, webi-sodes, digital, online publishing, creative development, branding and marketing, freelance writer.
