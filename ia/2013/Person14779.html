Nicole Leedham - Copywriter's Summary

With nearly 25 years in the communications field, I bring a broad experience to any project. While my current focus is business communication - copywriting, annual reports, news articles and more - my background includes almost every facet of PR and communications. These include media and stakeholder management to publications, internal communications and communication strategy.

Those who work with me know they are getting much more than a writer - they are getting a pragmatic business person who can delve deep into any organisation's culture, people and target market and come up with words that work.

Nicole Leedham - Copywriter's Experience

Word Barista

Black Coffee Communication

March 2011 – Present (2 years 6 months) Adelaide

If you want a marketing message that is as stimulating as black coffee and just as hard to sleep after, you have come to the right place.

With more than 20 years in the communications field, I can help you rock your business communications as well as project manage all facets of your branding - web site, logo, design, trademarking and more.

I specialise in taking technical jargon and translating it for the layperson. So for anything from one-page flyers, to detailed annual reports, you need Black Coffee Communication - where your message never sleeps.

Manager Communications

SafeWork SA

June 2007 – July 2011 (4 years 2 months) Adelaide Area, Australia

Managed the communications unit of SafeWork SA, the South Australian agency responsible for OHS and IR education, regulation and enforcement.

During my time there, staff numbers ranged from 9-12 and the team was responsible for the agency's publications; media; advertising campaigns, website; graphic design and the annual Safe Work Australia Event and Awards.

As manager, I led the team, as well as represented SWSA on national bodies and represented the communications unit on various internal working groups. I reported directly to the Director of Field and Frontline Services and was answerable to the SWSA OHS Advisory Committe - made up of external representatives from unions, business and WorkCover.

Writing, editing, production (Quark) of fortnightly staff newsletter.

Manager Community Relations, Riverina

Country Energy

Government Agency; 1001-5000 employees; Utilities industry

2001 – 2005 (4 years) Wagga Wagga

When Great Southern Energy merged with Advance Energy and Northpower, there was a spill and fill of all positions. The company was set up with both a corporate structure, responsible for setting overall objectives, and eight regional teams, with day-to-day operational responsibilities. As Manager, Community Relations, I was responsible for stakeholder management in the Riverina region. Working closely with the Regional General Manager and the senior management team, I ensured Country Energy was seen as a good corporate citizen in the region. This involved managing our relationship with councils, business groups, community organisations and the media, as well as heading up two customer service centres (in Wagga Wagga and Young) and dealing with high-level customer complaints.

Manager Public Relations

Great Southern Energy

1999 – 2001 (2 years) Queanbeyan

I was initially brought on board at Great Southern Energy to work on the promotion of the company to the ACT community and media in readiness for a potential merger with the ACT-owned ACTEW. When this merger fell through due to political reasons, I was hired as the Public Relations Manager. This role involved media and stakeholder relations, project management and writing of the annual and other reports, high-level customer complain management and crisis and issues management.
