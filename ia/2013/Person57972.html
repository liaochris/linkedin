Product Line Director Salesforce Ideas

Salesforce.com

Public Company; 5001-10,000 employees; CRM; Internet industry

January 2007 – March 2009 (2 years 3 months)

In the Summer of 2006 a member of my team (Kingsley) discovered a company called CrispyNews. We approached them to see if they were willing to work with us to adapt their product the enterprise. Within about a month the IdeaExchange was born. The response was overwhelmingly positive. Over the next several months we worked through an acquisition. Less than a month later, in February 2007 Michael Dell approached Marc Benioff to create a similar community for Dell. On February 16 we launched IdeaStorm, Dell’s customer community, and a couple months later we launched Employee Storm, Dells employee community. Both have been tremendous successes. In less than a year IdeaStorm has received 8k ideas, 564k votes, 57k comments. In addition to supporting Dell, I started the new Salesforce Ideas product line where in we productized this new technology. In December 2007 we launched in limited release, with GA scheduled for February.

Director SMB Strategies and Programs

Salesforce.com

Public Company; 5001-10,000 employees; CRM; Internet industry

February 2006 – July 2007 (1 year 6 months)

While in the corporate marketing I was also chartered with SMB Strategy and Programs. One of the products I oversaw was called Team Edition, salesforce.com’s entry level product for very small businesses. Given the low price point it had received very little attention since its launch in 2002. Realizing the value of seed and grow we created a sales team focused on this segment and began to market the product more aggressively. In the course of two years we grew subscribers 400%. During that period we also acquired a company called Kieden which integrated Google AdWord Campaigns with Salesforce Leads and Opportunities. In June of 2007 we struck a global alliance with Google where in we marketed Team Edition (re-named Group Edition featuring Google AdWords), selling the joint value proposition of “Advertise your business online with Google. Turn those leads into customers with Salesforce CRM.” This announcement was among the biggest in Salesforce history with 749 press clippings.

Customer Marketing

Salesforce.com

Public Company; 5001-10,000 employees; CRM; Internet industry

April 2005 – February 2006 (11 months)

My initial charter in customer marketing was to figure out how to onboard customers and drive adoption. This challenge was particularly important for our corporate sales customers. My first project was an automated email called the personalized account review. It was sent out each month to 50k administrators and showed customers areas being used today, areas to explore, and available upgrades. It also made personalized recommendations based on their stage on the CRM Success Ladder. The email was complex with over 60 integration points, but a very scaleable way to improve adoption. Along with the Personalized Account Review I also built a website called CRMSuccess.com. The goal was to create a step-by-step framework to help companies through their CRM implementation. Over time the CRM Success site grew from a one page website to thousands of pages of best practice content.

Sales

Salesforce.com

Public Company; 5001-10,000 employees; CRM; Internet industry

June 2002 – April 2005 (2 years 11 months)

I worked in sales for just under two years consistently hitting my number and making club. I started on the inside sales team and worked my way up to an account executive role. While in sales I spent a considerable amount of time moonlighting, building PowerPoint decks, engineering processes, writing specs for de-dup engines and lead routers, and designing dashboards. While I loved my experience in sales, in April of 2004 George Hu asked if I was interested in taking a role in Customer Marketing. Salesforce had grown to over a hundred thousand subscribers and we needed a way to reach customers in a one-to-many fashion. It was a great opportunity so I jumped at it. In November of 2004 the sales organization was in need of an interim manager for the inside sales team while they sought a replacement. For a two month period I managed a team of 15 inside sales reps. I had a great team and had the chance to implement a number of innovative changes.
