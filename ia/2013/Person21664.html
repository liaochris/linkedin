Chris Grundemann's Summary

I solve problems. Typically computer network problems.

I'm a network architect with deep engineering roots, people skills, business sense, and a broad base of deep knowledge built on formal training, a wide array of experience, and many ‘trial by fire’ lessons. I have direct experience with enterprise, telecommunications, and cable environments; including new deployments and cutting edge technology.

I leverage my business analysis and relationship skills, along with my experience as a network engineer (JNCIE #449) to focus primarily on strategy, design, implementation, standardization, and operations in Internet Protocol (IP: Core / Backbone, Access, Edge, etc.), Ethernet (Metro Ethernet / Carrier Ethernet), and wireless Ethernet (fixed broadband, wireless backhaul, etc.) network projects.

I'm also extremely interested in the impact and future of the Internet. Which is leading to becoming more and more active in the policy and politics surrounding it, in addition to engineering the bits.

My specialties include network design, protocol design, consensus building, research and development (R&D), leading collaborative groups, communicating abstract ideas to diverse audiences and generally getting stuff done.

My dream job would have a title like Principle Architect, Distinguished Engineer or Internet Evangelist.

    The goals of the task force are to:

Chris Grundemann's Publications

  • Day One: Exploring IPv6

    • Juniper Networks Books
    • February 2010
    Authors: Chris Grundemann

    As you read this booklet and work through the topics in your lab, you’ll progressively gain a fuller understanding of IPv6 configuration and operation in Junos. The layered, methodical progression provided will get you up to speed on this crucial networking technology quickly and easily.

  • Day One: Advanced IPv6 Configuration

    • Juniper Networks Books
    • April 2011
    Authors: Chris Grundemann

    It’s day one and you need to enable BGP routing in your network. Where to start? Start here and get it done with Junos. You’ll configure, test, and verify your IPv6 BGP configurations with best practices and rock-solid techniques.

  • The Experience Gap: Coping with the Looming IPv4 Address Shortage

    • Society of Cable Telecommunications Engineers
    • November 15, 2011
    Authors: Chris Grundemann, John Berg

    This paper presents the results of CableLabs’ architectural analysis and lab testing of CGNs and provides a discussion of the various methods for optimizing the cable subscriber experience during the IPv6 transition.

    Published and presented as part of SCTE's Cable-Tek Expo 2011.

  • Leaping Forward: How IPv6 is enabling the Next Generation of Home Networking

    • Society of Cable Telecommunications Engineers
    • October 17, 2012
    Authors: Chris Grundemann

    This paper presents both the challenges and many of the solutions for tomorrow’s home networks and how to make them a reality today. It covers the work completed so far and the work still being done (at CableLabs, in the IETF, within CEA, UPnP, and DLNA, etc.) to ensure the proper application of IPv6 technology to current and emerging home networking requirements. The paper also provides best practices and recommendations for MSOs.

    Published and presented as part of SCTE's Cable-Tek Expo 2012.

  • HIPnet: A self-configuring multi-router Home IP network

    • National Cable and Telecommunications Association
    • June 10, 2013
    Authors: Chris Grundemann

    This paper presents a novel approach to home router architecture, which applies many of the tools and protocols within the IPv6 framework in new ways in order to enable a completely self-configuring dual-stack (IPv4 & IPv6) multi-router home network capable of supporting the full range of in-home IP services.

    Published and presented as part of The 2013 Spring Technical Forum at The Cable Show 2013.
