Greg Cannon's Summary

Greg Cannon is a published Expert Author with 17 years of home building and insurance restoration sales experience. He earned his Navy Achievement Medal, commendations from the White House and two Fleet Admirals as an Advanced Systems Analyst in the U.S. Navy. He attended Angelo State University with a major in real estate finance, and holds nine construction trade certifications including: HAAG Engineering and Building Performance Institute. Greg is the President and CEO of Kingpin Marketing LLC, which has helped transform numerous roofing and construction sales companies throughout the United States. Greg’s team has over 10 years of hands-on experience guiding their consulting clients to outstanding profit margins. They focus on optimizing the business strategy, growing and maintaining a productive sales force, streamlining project production, and more. The home improvement and construction industries are constantly changing, and Kingpin Marketing LLC teaches companies to adapt and overcome contemporary obstacles through a combination of proven old school methods and new technological advances.

Specialties: Commercial and Residential Roofing Sales, Specialty Siding and Gutters Sales, Solar Photovoltaic and thermal, Building Energy Auditing

Greg Cannon's Publications

  • How to Avoid Getting Screwed by a Roof Salesman

    • HailandWind.com
    • July 2, 2013
    Authors: Greg Cannon

    Hail and Wind storms are most common in the Midwest, but in recent years they have been more frequent, and they are happening all over the country. You have seen it on the news! More and more people are getting scammed, ripped off, stolen from, and in some cases losing their homes and savings because of unscrupulous gypsies and con artists posing as contractors.
    Unfortunately there isn’t much the authorities can do once these thieves have skipped town, after wrecking homeowner’s lives, and the normally publicized tips you see on the news and internet are nowhere near good enough. The best way to avoid getting screwed by a dishonest contractor is to be proactive in the beginning, but how? This book has detailed tools and steps to protect you, your family, and your home; and an abundance of new material from inside the construction industry, and not yet shared in previous books from the “Storm Damage Secrets” series.
    This book has a cynical tone throughout to accentuate the gravity of the decision you will have to make in choosing a contractor to work on your home, yet it is mixed with positive ways to wade through the chaos. I use examples from all over the U.S., but mostly Oklahoma and Texas. These two states consistently have more hail and wind storms than anywhere else, yet have the loosest consumer protection laws and regulations when it comes to the roofing industry.
    A large number of storm chasing contractors will come from these states when hail and tornadoes hit your area. The principles found here are useful no matter what state you live in and can even be applied to other tradesman such as plumbers, electricians, window salesman, solar panelists, etc.

  • The Single Mom's Guide to Dealing With a Roof Salesman

    I was raised by a single mom, for much of my childhood. And the fact is, single mothers are considered easy prey to some scumbag salesmen. This book is quick and to the point without any unnecessary fluff, and it is a quick must-read for the single mother who already has a lot on her plate, besides having to deal with a hail and wind damaged roof.

    The information is unique to the industry, and is based on over a decade of my residential roof contracting and insurance adjusting experience nationwide: dozens of roofing companies, hundreds of roof salesmen, and thousands of homeowners’ insurance claims. The inside info here is true of 95% of the roofers in the industry (the other 5% either aren't in business for long, or eventually join the dark side).

    The principles found here are useful no matter what state you live in and can even be applied to other tradesman such as plumbers, electricians, window salesman, solar panelists, etc.
