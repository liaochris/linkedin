  • Patented Invention - The Cleaning Station

    • June 2004 to May 2007
    Team Members: Doreen Savran

    As a manager of a health club I was searching for a solution to the problem of using reusable rags and spray bottles to clean fitness equipment. Through years of researching and testing a variety of elements, I created the design and utility that worked. This had to have an organizational component, be very sleek, attractive and a complete system for the habit of hygiene. During the design phase, keeping up with industry trends and staying ahead was extremely important. Therefore, The Cleaning Station had to be versatile enough to take both a dry or wet towel, shelves, hand sanitizer and a hidden trash receptacle. We experimented with many features to make this product one that could wipe and santize hands without compromising the sleek design. Using many prototypes, I then went through the patent process to protect my invention. The process proved to be very exciting and it was a great accomplishment once awarded both a design and utility patent. Now, The Cleaning Station can be seen in health clubs, educational institutions, libraries, hospitality, healthcare, retail, at work and at home.
