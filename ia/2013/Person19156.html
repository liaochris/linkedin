Byron Small, OHST, CHST, CRIS's Summary

Byron Small is a Project Health Safety and Environmental Manager with Jacobs Engineering, contracted on the Sempra Pipeline Safety Enhancement Project. Mr. Small has nearly a decade of success overseeing safety on multi-billion dollar construction and petrochemical projects with a combined value of over $15 Billion in scope.

A proven safety professional, with extensive experience on integrated OCIP’s (Owner Controlled Insurance Program.) Mr. Small holds three professional designations, CHST (Construction Health and Safety Technician), OHST (Occupational Health and Safety Technician) and CRIS (Construction Risk Insurance Specialist.) He currently sits as the 1st Vice President for the American Society of Safety Engineers, Los Angeles Chapter.

Specialties: Corporate Safety Management, Strategic Business Development, Safety in Design Review, Risk Management Strategies, WC/GL Claims Management, Long Range Safety Planning, Pre Operational Planning, Safety Performance Analytics, Risk Assessments, OSHA Inspection Preparation, HSE Expert Witness, Loss Control Services, Accident/Incident Investigation, Program Safety Development and Integrated Risk & Safety.

Byron Small, OHST, CHST, CRIS's Projects

  • Sempra Energy - Pipeline Safety Enhancement Project

    • February 2013 to Present
    Team Members: Byron Small, OHST, CHST, CRIS

    The California Public Utilities Commission (CPUC) requires the state’s four natural gas transmission pipeline operators -- Pacific Gas & Electric and Southwest Gas, San Diego Gas & Electric and Southern California Gas Company -- to submit, for its consideration, a plan for replacing or pressure testing all natural gas transmission pipelines that have not been pressure tested. Regulations specifying pressure testing were implemented after many of the transmission pipelines were installed.

    The order is part of a pipeline safety rulemaking proceeding that the CPUC launched following last year’s natural gas pipeline rupture in northern California. The intent of the proceeding is to build on lessons learned from this event and to modify existing regulations to enhance natural gas transmission pipeline safety.

  • Los Angeles Community College District- Sustainable Building Program

    • May 2009 to Present
    Team Members: Byron Small, OHST, CHST, CRIS

    In 2002, the Board of Trustees for the Los Angeles Community College District saw an opportunity to become a leader in sustainability. The Board brought in leading environmental organizations to help develop a sustainability policy that balanced costs with the District's commitment to modernizing its colleges while protecting the environment. Today, the LACCD is at the helm of a $6 billion Sustainable Building Program – one of the largest such public-sector construction programs in the nation.
