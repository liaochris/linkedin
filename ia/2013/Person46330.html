Frank (Francesco) S. Adamo, DTM's Summary

Frank S. Adamo, is a communications skills specialist, an international instructor/trainer, a published author of 31 Tips to Becoming an Effective Presenter; a contributor to two books, Discover your Inner Strengths with Brian Tracy, Ken Blanchard, and Stephen Covey, as well as Yes You Can! with Dr. Warren Bennis and Jim Rohn; and a writer of several articles.

Since 2002, Frank has received enthusiastic reviews for his workshops, presentations and training in the United States, Canada, and as far away as Georgia in Eastern Europe and Indonesia in Southeast Asia. Reviewed highlights include “very skillful communication coach,” “great presenter and very organized,” “well prepared with moral lessons,” “extremely motivational,” “truly trained and professional,” “very dedicated person,” “talented writer,” “made me feel great and not inadequate,” and “caring and compassionate.”

A detailed and results-oriented professional with a strong ability to work with diverse groups, both locally and abroad, Frank has now has fostered a new enterprise, Quality Communications & Training. In essence, Frank believes that even though technology in communications has expanded exponentially in the past few decades, actual communications among people has significantly deteriorated during the same time period.

His vision is to strive for world peace through effective, positive, and qualified communications by understanding and accepting each other’s differences. By achieving “malice towards none,” the only phrase Frank remembers from his childhood comic books.

Yet if we can’t achieve world peace, we can achieve his mission; to provide the tools to personal peace by demonstrating how to effectively, positively, and qualifiedly communicate and accept differences of opinion among each other. In turn, perhaps, one day if everyone can achieve personal peace, we can achieve world peace.

Frank (Francesco) S. Adamo, DTM's Experience

Communication Skills Specialist

Quality Communications & Training

January 2013 – Present (8 months) Cypress, CA 90630

Technology in communications has accelerated exponentially in the past few decades. Today we have instant communications. We can watch events across the globe in real time. We have smartphones with thousands of available apps. We can text, talk, or video conference nearly anyone in the world—instantly.

Yet, are we really communicating better today? Today, there seems to be more rampant violence, mass murders, bullying, single parent families, and verbal, physical as well as sexual abuse. More children and young adults are dying on city streets than in war zones. In fact, a few veterans who have served in combat have returned home only to be shot and killed on their home turf.

We appear to lack respect, loyalty and integrity. Even younger children talk back to their parents and the parents allow it. We hardly seem to debate our differences without getting deviant. We don’t seem to know how to resolve our differences rationally and respectfully. Most importantly, and though the vast majority of Americans believe in God, it seems like we have left God behind, and we seem more focused on receiving than giving. We’re more concerned about what’s in it for me rather than how we can serve others.

Quality Communication's vision is to strive for world peace through effective, positive, and qualified communications by understanding and accepting each other’s differences.

Yet if we can’t achieve world peace, we can achieve our mission; to provide the tools to personal peace by demonstrating how to effectively, positively, and qualifiedly communicate and accept differences of opinion among each other. In turn, perhaps, one day if everyone can achieve personal peace, we can achieve world peace.
