Charles Amann's Summary

Author of The Princes and Princesses of Dance/The Follies of Fame, my first book, to be published on The Philadelphia Years of American Bandstand, with particular focus on the dancers on the show, the "Regulars", and their fame and how it influenced their lives.

Specialties: Philadelphia American Bandstand expert/historian. Comprehensive knowledge of the dance and music of the period -- late Fifties to early Sixties.

The Book

http://theprincesandprincessesofdance.com/

April 2004 – Present (9 years 9 months) http://www.theprincesandprincessesofdance.com

In 2007 I was a principal contributor and advisor to the Fiftieth Anniversary of American Bandstand, among which was my core concept of the dancers’ and musicians’ historical roles portrayed on the mural which was unveiled at the old studio on August 2, 2007.

I can be seen in the forthcoming documentary, The Philly Music Sound, being interviewed in the old Bandstand studio (an excerpt of which appears on YouTube: http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jZeWaqFoUI4.)

In 2008, I brought Bandstand to Broadway. On June 30, 2008, and for the first time in history, the American Bandstand Regulars, with my collaboration, performed on stage. The revue was titled The Radio Hits of 1958 and was a roaring success. Performing among the Broadway guest stars of today and Bandstand dancers of yesterday was Constantine Maroulis of American Idol fame.

June 2009 I am currently involved with the fund raiser titled "I Saved the Bandstand Floor" to repair the aging dance floor in the Bandstand Studio in West Philadelphia.

IN 2012 adviser, fund raiser and concept designer of a sidewalk plaque outside the original Bandstand studio in Philadelphia honoring the American Bandstand Regulars and Dick Clark for the national years of Philadelphia American Bandstand.

Along with my ongoing contributions and involvement, I have amassed one of the most significant and comprehensive collections of American Bandstand memorabilia existing today.

Charles Amann is the author of The Princes and Princesses of Dance, his first book, to be published on the Philadelphia years of American Bandstand, with particular focus on the dancers on the show, the "Regulars” and their fame and how it influenced their lives.

Over the past seven years, Mr. Amann has developed a close kinship with the Bandstand and rock 'n' roll community, and has become an authority on that period - the music, the dance and the "Regulars" who played an extraordinary role in the influence of dance, and a newly born music called Rock 'n" Roll, to a nation.
