Anson Sowby's Summary

Never say die...that's my manifesto.

* That's how I built a $500MM toy division in only 3 years at JAKKS Pacific.
* That's how I co-founded, scaled and sold Rocket XL, an award-winning global advertising agency.
* That's how I, together with my partners with no agency experience among us, built a new type of digital marketing agency across LA, NYC, Toronto, Sweden and London.

I've been lucky enough to work on some pretty amazing brands over the years as we've achieved AOR relationships with brands like PepsiCo, the NHL, Dove, Samsung and Ikea.

Read my latest articles and interviews for Forbes, Venture Beat and others at www.ansonsowby.com

Anson Sowby's Experience

CEO

Rocket XL

Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Marketing and Advertising industry

2007 – Present (6 years) LA, NYC, Toronto

Rocket XL is an award winning creative digital agency with over 75 employees across LA, NYC and Toronto. I co-founded the agency and we later sold the company to Cossette Communications/Vision7 and now operate as the North American social media offering.

* Co-founded and sold one of the first pure play global social media agencies with offices in LA, NYC, Toronto, London
* Expanded the agency's capabilities from project based campaigns to a full services digital branding agency with agency of record agreements with Fortune 200 Brands.
* Achieved agency of record relationships with major Fortune 100 companies (Unilever, Kraft, Coke, PepsiCo, Samsung, IKEA, NHL)
* Evaluated and implemented social media revenue and referral generation technologies (F-Commerce store fronts, Social CMS platforms, Social event based sales platforms, etc.) on behalf of multiple agency clients.
* Rocket XL is proud to be a member of the Society of Digital Agencies.
* Currently run Rocket's global operations with such agency of record clients as Unilever, LinkedIn, PepsiCo, Kraft, NHL and others.

Mentor

Amplify.la

* A hands-on startup accelerator and entrepreneurial campus in Venice, California.

Director of Global Marketing

JAKKS Pacific

Public Company; 201-500 employees; JAKK; Consumer Goods industry

November 2003 – April 2007 (3 years 6 months) Malibu, CA

At JAKKS, then the world's 3rd largest toy company with revenues over $600 Million, I helped build one of the best selling toy categories of the decade - plug and play tv games. I established the international division and oversaw global marketing. By expanding our distribution across the globe and developing products for some of the hottest licenses, we generated over $500 Million in global sales during my 3 years.

* Expanded the interactive division of the world's 3rd largest toy company from 10 products to over 1,000.
* Oversaw teams in the US and China consisting of marketers, designers, distributors and manufacturers.
* Ran global advertising and retail budgets in excess of $30 Million across 35 countries and developed a distribution network across 6 continents.
* Built one of the hottest toy categories of the decade working with such licensors as Disney, Nickelodeon and Sesame Street.
* Generated over $500 Million in global sales while I was with JAKKS.

Sr. Brand Manager

Vivendi Universal Games

Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; V; Computer Games industry

May 1999 – November 2003 (4 years 7 months) Los Angeles

I joined a small team of 8 people at the video games division of Universal Studios producing a handful of games licenses. Within 3 years, we grew to over 200 people producing hundreds of games for such major licenses as the Lord of the Rings, Hulk and Fast and The Furious.

* Joined as a marketing assistant and quickly rose the ranks to a sr. brand manager.
* Played a key role in the division's growth from a start-up (Universal Interactive Studios) to a global games publisher (Vivendi Universal Games)
* Developed over 50 global marketing campaigns for major franchises, many of which sold more than 1 million units.
* Executed the rebranding of Universal Interactive Studios including website, logos and style guide.
* Managed external agency relations and marketing budgets in excess of $10 Million.
* The profitable and fast growing division was acquired by Vivendi during my tenure.
