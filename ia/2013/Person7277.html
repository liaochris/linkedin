Developer

Driverstext Services Inc

2006 – 2008 (2 years)

Driverstext was one of the major projects of Adjec. We designed, developed, and ran a nationwide road hazard alert service, utilizing VoIP and SMS (text messages). This took around a million lines of code and a large amount of coffee. We ran a toll free number for each state we had subscribers. When a call came in, it was routed to an answering service where they took down the information for the call (what did you see and where was it?) and the alert was sent to all of the subscribers in the county via text message. The service used custom VoIP software to route the calls properly, databases to manage subscribers, web servers for the answering service and web site, and lots of code to send text messages. We ran all of this from two newish servers and one really old one. All of our servers were housed in a datacenter in Jackson TN (3 hours away) where I remotely managed them.

I started my own company and this was quite the educational opportunity, everyone should do this sometime in their life. To get this off the ground we also did the old standards of a small tech business like telephone and data network design and deployment along with troubleshooting and desktop support for local businesses and residents. I was the only technical person on the staff (2 people), so I learned many lessons on how to communicate with the non techies. We also renovated an old building down town for our office where I did most of the construction and called upon friends for the demolition work.

Developer

Home Tour Systems LLC

2005 – 2006 (1 year)

This was my first big consulting gig. I was hired to rewire their office as it’s a bit important for the phones and computers to work at a mortgage company. They had recently purchased some expensive software to do telephone tours for real estate and were very unhappy with it. I had played a bit with Asterisk, which is an Open Source PBX and offered to look into it for them. This lead to me designing and developing their nationwide service with Asterisk and lots of custom code. I managed all networking (office and collocation), desktops, code, databases, web servers, backups, and of course my PBX’s (computers that think they are telephones). As I understood the system better than anyone (I did build it all) I had the opportunity to dress up and demonstrate in sales meetings with banks and mortgage companies.
