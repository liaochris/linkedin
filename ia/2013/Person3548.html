Aleksei Anikin's Projects

  • Yota PCRF

    • September 2010 to Present
    Team Members: Aleksei Anikin, Anastasia Kazakova, Vladimir Romanov, Denis Tikhomirov, Alexey Bakin, Yury Bocharov, Sergey Malygin, Evgenia Martynyuk

    Yota PCRF (Policy and Charging Rules Function) is an advanced policy management solution enabling the operator to dynamically control network elements and apply real-time policies based on service, subscriber and usage context.

    Yota PCRF allows the operator to succeed at a turbulent IP market by quickly responding to changing consumer data habits and outpacing competitors in introducing new services. It facilitates making flexible policy decisions based on a wide variety of parameters including the following:
    subscriber profile and services,
    application, protocol (Skype, P2P), content type (Video, MP3),
    subscriber device (Mobile, iPad, TV, PC),
    subscriber location (home, roaming, specific cell),
    accumulated usage (per month, week, day, hour),
    cell congestion.

    Script-based logic of Yota PCRF allows taking into account all available parameters with any combinations of the parameters and conditions, thus enabling CSPs to launch new services without a need for any software development with shortest time to market.

    Yota PCRF optimizes the network utilization and protects the operator’s key revenue-generating services from being cannibalized by growing data consumption. It can provide subscribers with increased bandwidth when extra network capacity is available, and reduce bandwidth when network is congested to prevent overload. It can also balance QoS based on service and subscriber priority.

    Yota PCRF enables the operator to monetize Over-the-Top services, like Internet video, which generate a heavy load on the mobile network, but do not directly produce any revenue for an operator. The operator can offer a bundled QoS option for partner’s streaming Internet video service and optimize the delivery based on the service, type of device and the network subscriber is using. Thus operators can generate additional income from OTT services while improving user experience for subscribers.
