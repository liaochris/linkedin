RAJAN GS's Experience

Chairman

ART INDIA

June 1989 – Present (24 years 7 months) New Delhi Area, India

Founded in February 1989 by GS RAJAN and Anjana Rajan, both alumni of Kalakshetra, Madras, and Ashutosh Sundriyal, Art India was established in New Delhi in 1989 with the aim of putting into practice their beliefs concerning the arts. Art India’s aim was – then as now – to offer, on the one hand, programmes of genuine quality to audiences, and, on the other, to true artists, a forum where their talents could be exhibited most effectively – through proper organisation and aesthetic presentation.

World music composer - Indian classical flautist - Arts Administrator

www.malabarian.com

January 1989 – Present (25 years)

GS RAJAN started composing right from his school days. Today he is an outstanding music composer with decades of experience in composing music for films, documentaries, albums, ballets, classical and world music compositions both acoustic and electronic. He is one of the first composers to introduce world music orchestral compositions in India. His compositions received appreciation from none other maestro Zubin Mehta, Sufi singer Abida Parween, Film maker Muzafar Ali, Dr. Raghava R Menon, Subbudu and many others. Many top ranking vocalists including playback singers Vani Jayaram, Mano, Srikumar; Classical singers Unnikrishnan, Anooradha Parasuram, Madhup Mudgal and others have sung Rajan's popular music compositions.

For his thematic orchestral compostion titled 'oasis' he received the Akashwani Award from All India Radio, Government of India in 1996 and in 2005 Rajan was awarded with a special fellowship by the Government of France to compose Raga-Symphony which he composed living in France and presented with French orchestra at Cite de la Musique and other venues in Europe.

Honors and Awards:

JUNE 1995
The then CEC of India, TN Seshan honoured GS RAJAN on 10 June 1995 when Rajan composed and presented a two-hour, first of its kind multi-media musical entitled 'Reflections' in Kamani Auditorium, New Delhi.

FEBRUARY 1996
The national jury of Akashwani Award of All India Radio was bestowed upon RAJAN in the year 1996 when Rajan's unique production entitled 'OASIS' containing seven thematic orchestral compositions was selected as one of the best productions of All India Radio.

NOVEMBER 2004
GS RAJAN received the "Best Music Critic" award for the year 2004 from the Minister of Information and Broadcasting. Sangam Kala Group instituted this national level award.

JANUARY 2005
In 2005 Rajan was awarded a special fellowship by the Government of France to work with French musicians and to compose "RAGA-SYMPHONY".

JANUARY 2007
