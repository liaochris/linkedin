  • Mobile MWR

  • IMCOM iOS App

    Team Members: Evan Dyson

    Building upon lessons learned since the release of the Mobile MWR application, the IMCOM product features integrated third-party SDKs such as Facebook and JSON parsers. The universal application is fully compatible with the Apple iPhone and iPad.

  • Online Campaign: 2012 IMCOM-level Best Warrior Competition

    • April 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Evan Dyson, Keith Smith, William Bradner, Neal Snyder, Ray Johnson

    In April, 2012, the Public Affairs Office for Army IMCOM was tasked with providing support to the command-level 2012 Best Warrior Competition. Top Soldiers and Non-commissioned Officers from four IMCOM regions met for a week to determine who would advance to the U.S. Army-level Best Warrior competition in October, 2012. PAO sought to create a fast, efficient mechanism to aide garrisons who were following their specific contestant. While serving as a photo editor and social coordinator for the project, I developed the procedure to efficiently consolidate imagery, stories, videos and news stories produced by the team.

    - After receiving images from the field, I processed, cropped and toned all photos. Adjusting metadata for every photograph enabled the storage of caption details, photographer credit, and the participant keywords. Every photo was then uploaded to the IMCOM Flickr and SmugMug image hosting websites.

    - While images began to upload, I created small image sets to natively load to Facebook and promote through Twitter soon after each event.

    - Videographer Keith Smith simultaneously generated and posted his video products to the IMCOM YouTube channel, adding them to a Best Warrior-specific playlist.

    - Having added proper metadata, images could be sorted based on specific competitors. On Facebook, I established a note on the IMCOM page allowing the garrison Public Affairs Officers to see all resources pertaining to their competitor in one location. I then highlighted this note in all social media presences to increase awareness.

    As news stories emerged, the post was edited to capture any additional products.

    The resulting note capturing our products is available here: http://bit.ly/BW2012Note

  • Report Automation

    • September 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Evan Dyson

    Recognizing possible areas for increased efficiency, I built a native Cocoa application in Objective-C to streamline a daily reporting requirement. The third-party website ifttt.com (if-this-then-that) was configured to generate text files within a specific Dropbox account with all social content posted by my department, as well as released videos, stories and photography. The automation program draws from this data to populate a daily report email template, saving several man-hours daily. In addition to the automated sources of content, the program also contains input fields to provide additional daily accomplishments that are also integrated into the report.

  • Social Media Campaign: 2012 AUSA

    • October 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Evan Dyson, Amanda Rodriguez

    In October, 2012, Public Affairs for Army IMCOM was tasked with promoting our organization's involvement in the annual Association of the United States Army's 2012 annual meeting. I developed a social media campaign surrounding the event and mentored a colleague who aided in the process.

    By live-tweeting four sessions of family-based forums, as well as other relevant sessions and news stories pertaining to the event, we achieved 4.5 million views of AUSA-related social content in three days.

    Facebook was selectively updated with major highlights to enable maximum visibility per post and allow opportunities for community engagement. Collective efforts on Facebook yielded more likes, comments and shares than was common for our presences.
