Alan J. Zell's Summary

When I started my business, Attitudes for Selling in 1983 I knew that there were four things that all businesses were interested in improving – Sales, Profits, Customers, Growth. While consulting firms and books have been written covering how to do this, none address this by looking at the four selling situations which are common to all businesses and organizations.

External Selling is the title I give to the traditional use of the term “Sales” It is obvious that sales, profits, customers, growth come from good External Selling.

The other three selling situations not usually seen as affecting Sales, Profits, Customers/Clients, and Growth but do. They are:

Internal Selling, the selling of ideas, information, skills, services that takes place within a business or organization.. It is from poor Internal Selling that Profits are lost.

3rd-Party Selling is a technique that is seldom, if ever, used. However, it is 3rd-Party where Sales are consummated or rejected.

Non-Sales Selling is where Customers are lost.

Hence, not matter how good External Selling is, the other three selling situations can negate what is expected from External Selling.

I also knew, early on, that most people, even some of those who are “in-sales” do not want to admit that what they do is, in reality, selling. Attitudes for Selling’s consulting, workshops, seminars are designed to help our clients develop effective and efficient selling in all their four selling situations as a way to help increase Sales, Profits, Customer acquisition, and business Growth.

The tenets of the Attitude for Selling approach to selling are in our book, "Elements of Selling."
