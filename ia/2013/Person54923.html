Ilham Al-Qaradawi's Summary

Profile:

I am a professor of physics at Qatar University. I Received my Ph.D. from University of London, UK in 1991.
Established a well-equipped positron laboratory at Qatar University and built the first variable energy slow positron beam in the Middle East.
Established a radiation measurement laboratory at Qatar University carrying out research in the area of environmental radiation physics.
Involved in many research projects and collaborations on local, regional and international levels.
Fellow of the Institute of Physics of UK and a member of many international and regional scientific societies in the field of physics, nuclear physics and physics education.
Lectured in the World Nuclear University Summer Institute since 2007. A member of the WNU School on Radiation Technology advisory committee. Member of World Council on Isotopes; WCI.
A very enthusiastic physicist, researcher, and a keen educator always working to raise the standards of science awareness and teaching particularly in physics.
Founder of the Qatar Physics Society; through which I regularly hold training workshops for physics teachers and other activities in an effort to spread physics knowledge, nuclear awareness and research culture as well as improve physics education.

Specialties

Research is mainly in the field of positron physics working on various types of materials, and is involved with CERN in the new Antihydrogen experiment; AEGIS. Also involved in physics education research and environmental radiation physics research.
