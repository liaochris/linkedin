Cindy Pavlinac's Summary

I travel as an artist, a seeker tracking alignments back to source, questing towards expressions of the extraordinary.

As a photographer, I lure images out of wisps of time, revealing a deeply shared vision of beauty, mystery, power and presence of place.

I write, mapping terrain, exploring the convergence of inner and outer landscapes, spinning complex tales of pilgrimage, ancient and modern, imaginative and real.

Creations are shared in galleries, on location, and at events, blending digital projection to weave images, words, and music into multimedia extravaganzas that return to center along quiet paths of peace.

I am called to the journey. By traveling, I find my way home.

Cindy Pavlinac's Experience

Resident Studio Artist

Art Works Downtown

Nonprofit; 1-10 employees; Fine Art industry

1997 – Present (16 years) Art Works Downtown

I am a founding studio artist at Art Works Downtown in fine art photography, placing my work in the public stream of possibility. Current subjects include ancient sites, archaeoastronomy, labyrinths, classic cars, aerospace, tech events, Ren Faires, and digital photo composites. I mentor photography enthusiasts in my studio and on location, photograph artwork for artists, and host interns through the Marin County School to Career and Work-Readiness Certification programs.

Owner/ Operator

Sacred Land Photography

1984 – Present (29 years)

I specialize in images which convey the beauty, mystery, and power of place. I work on location throughout Europe and North America photographing ancient sanctuaries and modern expressions of the sacred. Since my first photo exhibit in 1974 my fine art photography has been collected, won awards, and appeared in over 800 publications. I produce multimedia presentations featuring historic and prehistoric sites, rock art and labyrinths. I am an independent multimedia producer, content provider, and creative consultant. My Labyrinths of Spirit and Grace Image Library and photography of historic and prehistoric sites provides editorial, rights managed stock photos for publication, print, web, and AV production. I also design book covers and CD artwork for authors and musicians.

I’ve been visiting special places since Apollo whispered to me in Delphi, Greece in 1977, charging me to bridge the ancient with the modern. I aim to inspire people through images, words, music, and performance towards a hope filled future.

Pro Bono Labyrinth Photographer

Grace Cathedral/Quest/Veriditas

Nonprofit; 11-50 employees; Religious Institutions industry

1990 – 2012 (22 years)

Professional documentation of the birth of the worldwide Labyrinth Movement at its epicenter, the Chartres Labyrinths installed in Grace Cathedral, San Francisco. Photographed monthly walks, concerts, 24 hour New Years vigils, weekend pilgrimages, overnight Dream Quests, Friday noon Peace Walks, Grace After Hours, Veriditas events, School of the Sacred Feminine. Provided press photos, created Gift Shop post cards, supplied countless images for publicity, bulletins, videos, fund raising. Produced multimedia presentations with hundreds of photos, created labyrinth walk installations projecting images onto 30 foot silk veils for labyrinth walks. Published photos appeared in national newspapers, worldwide websites, Time Magazine’s Pictures of the Year. I walked with the Grace Cathedral labyrinths from their first bold painting on canvas through the installation of the wool tapestry rug and outside terrazzo terrace to the celebration of the magnificent cut stone floor installation.

Vice President

California Writers Club, Marin Branch

June 2007 – November 2009 (2 years 6 months)

The California Writers Club is the nation's oldest professional club for writers, founded in 1909 by San Francisco Bay Area writers, including Jack London. We are a nonprofit education corporation with branches throughout California, dedicated to educating writers of all levels and disciplines in the craft and practice of writing. Our branches hold regular meetings with informative speakers and opportunities for networking with fellow writers and publishing industry specialists.

I am one of the founding members of the Marin Branch, chartered in 1999. We meet at Book Passage in Corte Madera, and our events have featured authors, publishers, agents, lawyers, bloggers, publicists, and other writing professionals. Our branch has a critique group registry (I’m in three) and plans writing outings and informal gatherings during the year. We're here to inspire, inform, encourage, network, and remind writers to have fun while improving their writing and building a career.
Marin Branch: http://www.cwcmarinwriters.com
California Writers Club: http://www.calwriters.org
