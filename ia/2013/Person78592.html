Lars Vilhelmsen's Summary

(Se evt. www.larsvilhelmsen.dk for mere og for dansk tekst)

I have a lot of experience directing (creative) projects with high ambitions and very tight budgets. I like the challenge of trying to make the most of limited ressources, as the limitations often necessitate new and simpler solutions.

As a project lead I have managed production and economy, creative and ludological design on several projects. From early conceptualization and research through pitch/sales, final design, production, launch and overseeing post-launch operation.

Budgets of past projects: from 30.000 - 500.000 € per project

I have designed games and fictions for both entertainment and learning.

I have filled the role of Project Lead, Conceptualizer and Writer on diverse projects since 2000. More/details here: www.larsvilhelmsen.dk

Interactive products are my specialty. I am very interested in the good experience and the psychological play involved in creating that, whether it is a single-user or a multi-user experience. I have also been able to utilize some of the same skills in creating new methods for effective idea-generation, pitch-writing and arranging large workshops.

My main field is that of game design and story structure. I am a good project lead because of my ability to focus on the end-users experience and communicating the project goals, tasks and essence to both creative and technical personnel. I have a good understanding of the possibilities and challenges programmers have and face, of how data can be used or how the interplay of simple mechanics can create a very complex experience. Years of working with good programmers have given me great insights, that I draw upon when leading the digital productions.

I draw heavily on a personal interest in games and stories. 20+ years of role-, card-, board- and computer-gaming and game/story creation provides a solid knowledge of the many facets of games and fiction.

Learning Lab Denmark

Educational Institution; 51-200 employees; Research industry

February 2005 – September 2007 (2 years 8 months) Copenhagen Area, Denmark

I worked at Learning Lab Denmark creating the game Drabssag/Melved (ENG: Homicide) and on various other projects on a freelance basis until 2006, when I was employed full time to manage and direct two game projects. I stayed at Learning Lab Denmark (Danish University of Education, Aarhus University) producing the 2 games and a few other interactive productions and recieved a special bonus for innovative, experimental work.

At Learning Lab Denmark we experimented with employing games and game mechanics to teaching. I had a close collaboration with researchers in the field of learning games, with the responsibility to find and create game projects (externally financed) where we could test theories and gather data from the effect of teaching with games. This was a great environment, where game design theory, psychology and teaching methods were regular subjects at discussions and meetings. Perfect for a game developer like me, who is really interested in practical application of game design theory and the user/players psychological response to games. At LLD games had to be experimental and all choices in production were made while focusing on the players state of mind and how this could help both illustrate the use of the subject matter from class, but also how we could engage students emotionally in matters they normally found too abstract - such as math, biology, litterary analysis, physics, etc.

Late in 2007 I was approached by DR, who offered me the opportunity to direct and manage two large productions. I accepted that offer.

Lars Vilhelmsen's Publications

  • Tankestreger - Tværvidenskabelige Nybrud

    • Aarhus University Press
    • 2007
    Authors: Lars Vilhelmsen, Morten Misfeldt

    Article on social learning games for the book Tankestreger - a book on crossdisciplinary science and the worlds that open when professionals from different disciplines cooperate on research and experiments. The article was co-written with Morten Misfeldt.

  • Drabssag Melved (Eng: Homicide)

    • Malling Beck
    • 2004
    Authors: Lars Vilhelmsen, Max Møller, Anders Skovgaard-Petersen, Svend Ask Larsen, Jacob Jaskov

    A groundbreaking learning game, where students solve murder mysteries by solving realistic forensic tasks using math, biology, litterary theory and physics. Developed in cooperation with forensic experts and teachers to create a simulation of practical, realistic work, where students experience the school subjects being used to solve real problems. The authors (above) designed the game elements, narratives, web interface, wrote the texts and worked with film crew, assisting writers, university researchers and other expert groups to make Homicide the experience, product and experiment that it is.

  • Fabula - Eventyrfortælling med flere fortællere

    • Fabula
    • 2002
    Authors: Lars Vilhelmsen

    A method for telling stories in groups from 3-33, guided by simple rules and assisted by illustrated cards depicting and describing typical characters and places in a fairytale. Written by me and published privately, with technical assistance from Anders Skovgaard-Petersen and illustrations by Palle Schmidt.
