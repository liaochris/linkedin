Lowell Nerenberg's Summary

I coach chief executives, business owners, and executive teams to perform and produce the winning results they desire and need.

Why might you want or need a coach?

Many executives go it alone, with occasional conversations in confidence with a trusted friend. Not a bad idea. At least they know they don't know it all. But do they write up a success plan with that friend who helps them stay on track? A person who intervenes when they get stuck? Who presses them to go on? Usually no.

Even the most successful executives have ideas they've been thinking about implementing. But they drag around limiting beliefs - both unconscious and conscious, "insurmountable" blocks, and self-defeating behavioral habits which hold them back.

My intention is to guide you to achieve profound and enduring breakthroughs in leadership effectiveness and team performance, such that you are invigorated by your progress and delighted with the results.

I conduct candid, confidential, one-on-one executive coaching sessions, both face-to-face and by phone. I help you establish and practice new patterns of thought > emotion > behavior which accelerate the changes you want to bring about.

BIO

I was the founder and CEO of Computer Graphics Distributing (CGD), a master wholesale distributor in the Mid-Atlantic region and an INC. 500 company (#144). I also co-founded a similar firm, New England Computer Graphics (NECG). After 11 years as CEO, I sold CGD in a merger and roll-up with five other regional distributors. We went public, as Tekgraf, Inc., shortly after the merger, and then we acquired NECG.

In 2005 I became a Vistage Chair, where I led chief executives and key executives in monthly, all-day peer group meetings. I also coached each member in one-to-one sessions.

Besides my leadership and coaching experience, I have extensive training and education in human development, interpersonal communications, neuroscience, and coaching strategies and techniques.

Specialties

One-on-one leadership and team coaching.
Establish a viscerally inspiring vision for an organization.
Identify the core values an organization agrees to live by. Facilitate off-site strategy contracts.
Mediate challenging intra-company disagreements.
