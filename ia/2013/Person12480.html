The Grassroots Action Network provides capacity building services, mentoring, training opportunities and technical assistance to organizations that are actively forging new ideas to transform communities and end hunger and poverty. Together with a network of more than 8,000 grassroots organizations, we share innovations, mobilize resources and connect them to each other in order to support their work to build healthier, sustainable communities that develop local food systems and strengthen local economies. Beatriz Beckford's Projects

  • Brooklyn School Food Network

    • June 2011 to Present
    Team Members: Beatriz Beckford, Nancy Romer, Adriana Velez

    BFC’s large and organized Brooklyn School Food Network
    brings together parent activists from 20+ schools from many neighborhoods in Brooklyn,
    with a focus on Brownsville, East New York, Bushwick and Bed-Stuy. Parents empowered with knowledge of the school food system and with support from BFC organizers have won a number of concrete victories, including salad bars, water jets, more fruits and veggies, school gardens, changing the chocolate milk formula to exclude HFCS. The New York City Council Resolution on School Food Change references specifically “Brooklyn Food Coalition’s Roadmap for Healthy, Sustainable School Food Change”.

    • January 2011 to Present
    Team Members: Beatriz Beckford, Jalal Sabur, Joe Jordan, Moriba Jackson

    The Malcolm X Grassroots Movement is an organization of Afrikans in America/New Afrikans whose mission is to defend the human rights of our people and promote self-determination in our community.
