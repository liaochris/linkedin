Husani Oakley's Experience

Director of Creative Technology

Wieden + Kennedy

Privately Held; 1001-5000 employees; Marketing and Advertising industry

February 2011 – Present (2 years 8 months) New York City

I am responsible for overseeing development of digital projects across all clients in our New York City office. While at W+K, I've grown the technology department from one sad, lonely developer to seven (hopefully) happy coders and quality assurance folks — ensuring that we keep 90% of digital development in-house. I’ve acted as a tech/digital evangelist and have played a key role in selling creative-focused but technically-impressive digital work.

Clients include ESPN, Heineken, Southern Comfort, Delta, Jordan, and One Kings Lane.

Founder / CEO

Oakley and Partners

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Marketing and Advertising industry

January 2010 – Present (3 years 9 months)

While Oakley + Partners has morphed into a holding firm for my non-advertising side projects, it started out as a production shop. We worked with a number of more traditionally minded agencies to help them extend and execute their digital work; our direct client relationships allowed us to create our own.

Clients included ExxonMobil, Google, Luxottica, Disney, Ubisoft, and Cutwater.

VP, Technology and Digital Production

EVB

Public Company; 11-50 employees; OMC; Marketing and Advertising industry

May 2008 – January 2010 (1 year 9 months)

Forever the agency that created “Elf Yourself” for OfficeMax, Omnicom’s EVB was (and still is) an envelope-pushing digital shop. I ran both the technology and the digital production (project management) groups developing immersive, sharable, creative-focused (and award winning!) digital work.

Clients included PayPal, Adidas, Kodak, Levi’s, Intuit, VSP, and JC Penney.

Director, Technology

Euro RSCG

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; HAV.PA; Marketing and Advertising industry

February 2006 – May 2008 (2 years 4 months)

Now known as Havas Worldwide, I ran the interactive development department at Euro RSCG Worldwide. My team was ultimately responsible for all technical tasks (development, quality assurance, maintenance, and analytics) across all clients, including management of Volvo’s primary US website.

Clients included Volvo, Charles Schwab, ExxonMobil, Dos Equis, Kraft, IBM, and Intel.

Founding Partner

Squaretangle LLC

June 2004 – January 2007 (2 years 8 months)

Squaretangle was a company that made companies. I wanted to call it CompanyFactory, but my partners thought that was a terrible idea. I was responsible for all things technology and overall brand strategy and execution.

Our most notable achievement was creation and management of Atlantico, a modern furniture company outlined below.

Co-Founder and Co-President

Atlantico

January 2005 – July 2005 (7 months)

Atlantico designed, manufactured, and sold warm minimalist, solid wood furniture. A joint venture with noted furniture brand De La Espada, we designed our products in London, built them in Portugal, and sold them in an apartment-style store in Williamsburg, Brooklyn.

A successful buyout concluded my direct involvement; Atlantico pieces are still sold today via various resellers.

Director of Technology

De La Espada

Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Furniture industry

November 2003 – January 2005 (1 year 3 months)

De La Espada was a noted, high-end modern furniture designer and manufacturer head- quartered in London, with stores in New York City and San Francisco. I created a global information management system called “Project Apollo”. Apollo allowed us to track orders as they made their way through a very manual production process, allowed salespeople to keep track of client communication (and cross-reference that tracking with website usage), and managed our email marketing campaigns.

Partner, Director of Technology

Flavorpill Productions

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Online Media industry

January 2001 – October 2003 (2 years 10 months)

I was partner and employee #3 at Flavorpill, the cultural events newsletter/website now in more than nine cities across the globe. I was the primary developer on all software used in the first three years of Flavorpill’s existence, including custom content management tools, email deployment systems, and subscriber tracking and analytics platforms. I played a key role in finding and pitching investors, selling media placements to agencies and brands, and managing our writers.

I remain an equity holder.

Producer / Project Manager

Datek Online

Privately Held; 501-1000 employees; Financial Services industry

April 2000 – October 2000 (7 months)

In the midst of the dot-com-boom’s explosion of daytrading, I was a producer / project manager on the marketing side at Datek Online, one of the first (and most powerful) consumer stock trading platforms. I led a team of four developers and designers who managed the non-trading website.

Lead Web Integrator

T3 Media

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Internet industry

November 1998 – May 1999 (7 months)

Ranked by both Forrester and US News and World Report as one of the top 10 interactive stop in the late 1990s, T3 Media was an O.G. digital agency. I was a lead programmer, responsible for building front-end code for a number of client websites and digital marketing materials.

Clients included AlliedSignal (now Honeywell), Hearst, and Cameron Mackintosh.

Site Builder / Site Manager (UK)

AGENCY.COM

Privately Held; 201-500 employees; OMC; Marketing and Advertising industry

February 1998 – November 1998 (10 months)

Back in the day, if you wanted to make websites for a living, you probably worked at AGENCY.COM. My time at the agency was spent as a front-end developer, one of three who built and managed British Airways’ global website. Later, I moved to London, where I managed (and hands-on participated in) the technical side of BA’s website redesign.

Clients included British Airways, Oppenheimer Funds, and Texaco.

Founder and President

DigiSync Technologies

May 1995 – December 1997 (2 years 8 months)

Small, youth-run consulting firm servicing small businesses. DigiSync's specialties included building small local area networks and intranets. At it's peak, the firm employed four people and had quarterly revenues of more than enough money for a teenager.
