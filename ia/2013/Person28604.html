Team Lead

Ubisoft

Public Company; 5001-10,000 employees; FR0000054470; Computer Games industry

September 2009 – December 2010 (1 year 4 months)

In charge of a cross-platform C++ application framework that was providing all the services required to develop, debug and optimize a multithreaded game engine, such as high- performance containers (including lock free containers), memory allocators and debuggers, file streamers, optimized math routines (SSE, Altivec), managers for input controllers (including Move, Kinect and touch screens), a visual profiler and performance analyzer, a job scheduler (presented at GDC 2010), a network library, etc.

This framework was the most established middleware at Ubisoft and had been used in more than 100 games since its creation. It was running on Microsoft Windows (32/64bit) and Xbox 360, Sony PS3 (PPU/SPU), PSP and Vita, Nintendo DS, 3DS and Wii, Apple OS X and iOS. We provided expertise and technical support for tens of production teams worldwide, while optimizing the framework and implementing new features.

In addition to my senior programmer duties, I was in charge of the day-to-day management of six programmers and the prioritization, tracking and assessment of their tasks, in an Agile environment. I was responsible for their performance appraisals and the recruitment of new team members. I also wrote the release notes and the newsletters sent to our numerous customers, and filled the SR&ED claims.

Software Developer

CAE

Public Company; 5001-10,000 employees; CAE; Aviation & Aerospace industry

July 2001 – January 2008 (6 years 7 months)

As a member of the Rehost group, I implemented and integrated CPU software emulators, utilized in the simulation of complex avionic boxes. The emulators run on single-board PCs, embedded in the full-flight simulators, and are designed to execute third-party binary code originally compiled for another architecture (PowerPC, 68k), by interpreting in real-time the machine language instructions.

I also wrote many communication libraries to control and monitor remote, asynchronous hardware (avionic boxes, Ethernet switches) from a real-time simulation process, using compression libraries, proprietary protocols and custom-built Ethernet and 1394 links.

I installed, troubleshot and debugged my code on site with the other engineers and regularly travelled to Japan, China and the United-States to provide field engineering assistance and training.

I made use of on-the-job training to demonstrate troubleshooting, maintenance and customization. I was also on call 24/7 to ensure the highest availability of the simulator.
