Jennifer Keliher-Venegas' Experience

Graduate Student & Research Assistant

Moss Landing Marine Laboratories

Educational Institution; 51-200 employees; Higher Education industry

March 2013 – Present (8 months) Moss Landing, CA

I will be conducting invertebrate metagenomic analyses as part of an international, collaborative project funded by NSF's Office of International Science and Engineering (OISE) through the PIRE with San Diego State University, UCLA, the Smithsonian Institute and NOAA scientists to measure marine biodiversity gradients across the Indonesian Archipelago (otherwise known as the Coral Triangle). Our objective is to determine what organisms are present on these reefs and how this biodiversity is impacted by human caused environmental stress. We will be deploying a novel monitoring tool called Autonomous Reef Monitoring Structures (ARMS). The information we collect from these structures will enable us to better understand the processes shaping marine biodiversity within the Coral Triangle (a region that contains the world’s most biologically diverse coral reefs), thereby improving the ability of resource managers to develop strategies to promote sustainability of these locally and internationally valuable marine resources. Lastly, the project will also bring an international collaboration to Indonesia (working closely with Indonesian scientists and students), one of the world’s most populous developing countries, increasing the nation’s scientific capacity. We expect that this work will have important implications for improving the sustainability of commercial and subsistence fisheries.

SWC Biotechnology Education & Training Sequence Investment (BETSI)

January 2013 – Present (10 months) Southwestern College, various local high schools

The SWC Biotechnology Education & Training Sequence Investment (BETSI) Project is an educational and training program designed to train high school biology teachers and to educate Sweetwater Union High School District students in biotechnology with the objective of recruiting them into the Biotechnology Certificate Program at Southwestern College. I work as a TA during high school outreaches facilitating molecular experiments such as the analysis of food items to check for genetic modification by polymerase chain reaction.
