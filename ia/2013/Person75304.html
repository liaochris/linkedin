Kev Webber's Summary

I have worked with large scale IT for 14 years, working in many different disciplines including:

● strategy development and implementation,
● IT service development;
● project management
● systems operations and administration
● desktop service design & implementation
● website and application development
● computing facilities management
● 1st, 2nd & 3rd line IT support.

My experience lies in running highly available and complex services to large HE environments with user-bases ranging between 35,000 and 45,000 users, 5,000-6,000 desktops, and several thousand laptops and other network connected devices. I therefore have a strong technical understanding, diagnostic abilities, and incident management skills.

I have experience in running large-scale Microsoft solutions, and also with running multi-platform services (Macs, LAMP servers, etc) in a hybrid environment. In recent years, I have migrated some major on-premises services into the cloud, and then have successfully run co-located or outsourced services as part of a hybrid service portfolio.

I'm a believer that the most successful IT solutions in a company-wide context are delivered via business-led IT portfolios, which requires a highly communicative and expert IT function.

I am a creative thinker and I'm able to communicate, advise and influence at many different levels inside or outside of an organisation, and I build strong, empowered teams.

Please do feel free to connect :-)

Kev Webber's Experience

Operations and Maintenance Team Manager

Plymouth University

Educational Institution; 1001-5000 employees; Higher Education industry

2012 – 2013 (1 year)

In this role I assisted with the application of a university-led plan to apply best practice frameworks (ITIL v3, PRINCE2, Agile, SFIA measurement) to a recently restructured department. I also continued to develop and formalise my expertise in the area of enterprise Service Operation, achieving an ITIL v3 intermediate certification in Operational Support and Analysis.

Additionally, I continued to lead a multi-disciplinary technical team in the provision of enterprise class services to around 35-45k users, while altering the boundaries of my team's duties in order to align with the new departmental structures - transferring knowledge, procedures and service responsibilities into and out of personnel in different departments.

Systems and Middleware Manager

Plymouth University

Educational Institution; 1001-5000 employees; Higher Education industry

2006 – 2012 (6 years)

In this position I was a team manager of a multi-disciplinary technical team responsible for design, implementation, development, operation and support for enterprise class services to all students and staff (~35-45k). Some of the larger services provided included: Microsoft Exchange (2k3/7/10), Live@edu, Exim, Active Directory (2k3/8/12), Office Communication Server 2007r2, Internet Authentication Services, ISA, enterprise print services (several hundred networked MFDs), DHCP, BIND DNS, Radius, MS Forefront Identity Management.

I also engaged with university departments and 3rd parties to negotiate and form successful working partnerships for IT provision (Plymouth University International College; Royal Statistical Society; Plymouth University Student Union), and likewise collaborated with other internal IT department heads to achieve a coordinated set of actions, outputs and strategies - often as primary technical advisor.

I also supported the Human Resources department into allegations by conducting investigations of staff or student misconduct, acting under internal regulations, the Regulation of Investigatory Powers and Data Protection Acts, collating data for either HR / police or governmental services.

In addition I have completed responses for numerous audits, and provided and declined to provide data in response to Freedom of Information requests.

In order to formalise some of my experience of managing services, I completed the ITIL v3 foundation certification.

Principal Computing Officer (Desktops, Policies and Operating Systems)

Plymouth University

Educational Institution; 1001-5000 employees; Higher Education industry

2003 – 2006 (3 years)

Here, my principal role was the design, provision and support of the student desktop service for Plymouth University and for the Peninsula College of Medicine and Dentistry. Development and use of multicast-imaging and unattended installations in order to standardise and reduce installation resource footprint. In addition, my responsibilities included the operation and maintenance of Active Directory and Group Policies.

While I was in this in role, the company formally adopted Macintosh services to support business requirements and as a result I worked with a mac specialist to help create a hybrid environment.

In this role I also influenced strategy in order to support aggressive expansion of customer-facing IT services - for example, extending services to laptops and increasing wireless provision.

Senior Computing Officer (Applications Team)

Plymouth University

Educational Institution; 1001-5000 employees; Higher Education industry

2001 – 2003 (2 years)

In this role, I was responsible for the technical design and implementation of the move from company owned desktops to a 4 year lease cycle - including transitioning data between hardware and between platform (NT4 to XP). At the time, this was the largest move to a lease model in UK Higher Education. I also designed the first desktop IT services for newly formed Medical School.

During this time I also made use of VB6 / VB.net, TSQL, IIS5/6, ASP, vbscript and similar to write bespoke applications, scripts and websites. I was also responsible for repackaging software for distribution and inclusion on the OS images that I created.

Additionally I assisted in setting up one of the largest grid computing environments in the country (3000 nodes at peak). This grid was used by a PhD student to solve a 19 quadrillion number, 40-year old problem, and validate the use of Quadratic Double Circulum Code in the analysis of satellite-gathered deep space photography.

Senior Computing Officer (Development Team)

Plymouth University

Educational Institution; 1001-5000 employees; Higher Education industry

2000 – 2001 (1 year)

Here I was part of a team that introduced Active Directory 2000 and Exchange 2000 alongside an existing Novell Directory Service-based architecture, and subsequently migrated our directory services to Active Directory in their entirety.

I also assisted in the introduction of a custom-coded intranet, liaising with technical and non-technical stakeholders in order to capture requirements and shape services based on requirements. Other technical achievements included exploration and implementation of Windows DFS services, Network Load Balancers and SQL Clusters in order to provide highly scalable and available services.

Computing Officer (Open Access Facilities)

Plymouth University

Educational Institution; 1001-5000 employees; Higher Education industry

1999 – 2000 (1 year)

Further, I organized and ran the Student Computing Advisory Service (1st and 2nd line support) with 6 staff plus 20 on a rota, for a potential 30,000 user base. To increase service efficiency and customer satisfaction, I developed tools and services to promote quick, first-time first-line fixes, and was able to successfully obtain funding for additional posts due to the successful service I was running.

I was also responsible for health and safety in the areas from which the Student Computing Advisory Service was provided.
