James Cross' Experience

Education Manager

MediaCore Technologies Inc.

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; E-Learning industry

March 2012 – Present (1 year 6 months) London UK / Victoria Canada

I've been involved in the MediaCore story since it was an open source platform, and was heavily involved in the company's relaunch as an education-focused product in early 2012. Since then, I've played a key role in MediaCore's dramatic growth worldwide.

My role at MediaCore is a mix of business development, evangelism, technical and educational input - ranging from writing thought leadership pieces for national press, to high-stakes product demos and sales development, to developing partnerships with key industry players.

MediaCore is the best way for schools, universities and businesses to harness the power of the video learning revolution.

MediaCore is an easy to use, secure, cloud video learning platform with comprehensive mobile support - underpinned by exceptional design and usability.

It's a disruptive product with the potential to change education forever - doing for education what YouTube and Netflix have done for the world of entertainment.

Founder

Schooleo

Public Company; 1-10 employees; E-Learning industry

December 2010 – May 2012 (1 year 6 months) Sheffield, United Kingdom

Whilst working as a teacher and using video in my own classroom, and through my experience working with schools since leaving the classroom, I realised that a simple, easy to use video sharing tool, which capitalised on the increasing use of mobile devices in schools, could make a huge impact on students' learning.

Working with the developers of an open source video platform, Schooleo was born - an online platform, customised to the needs of schools and teachers, that allowed them to safely share video, including mobile streaming to iOS devices and other modern smartphones. Schooleo received positive press, and positive feedback from school users.

The materials and educational concepts that I prepared for Schooleo have now been incorporated into MediaCore's education offering.
