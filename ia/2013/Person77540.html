Kurt Oberloh's Summary

I have been an entrepreneur for over 10 years in Eastern Washington and have owned, operated, or had ownership interest in: a small business support company, an IT firm, an entertainment company, a bowling center, a music venue & cafe, a live sound company, short term lending company, real estate investment company, and a marketing consulting firm; specializing in social media marketing, entrepreneurial support and asset building strategies.

I have also held positions as an Advertising Representative for two mainstream publications, General Manager of a rescue animal shelter, and sales positions for organizations both big and small. I was also employed at Central Washington University, where I managed the daily technical tasks of the radio station and served as a student adviser. While being at the station I have created and developed the station’s new digital image and improved their on-line presence. Implemented the station's mobile app and increased over all on-line listenership and interaction.

I have served on philanthropy and civic boards and committees serving both in leadership roles and committee member. I also have a huge passion for microenterprise development and co-organized a support group for new and hopeful entrepreneurs where I gave lectures series on “Starting Your Own Business,” “Business/Marketing Plan Writing,” “Internet Marketing and Web 2.0 Strategy,” and “Knowing Who Your Customers Are”; as well as, many smaller breakout workshops to answer individual questions. I have also been able to help and mentor many of my students to accomplish their goal to becoming successful business owners.

One of my proudest business moments was in 2007; I was a Washington State Young Entrepreneur of the Year (Under 30) Nominee.

I live by the motto from Thomas A. Edison “I have not failed. I've just found 10000 ways that won't work.”
