Jason Brown's Summary

Prior to founding the Brown Law Offices, P.A., Jason clerked for Timothy R. Bloomquist, retired Chief Judge of Minnesota’s Tenth Judicial District, and practiced with a well-known Minneapolis personal injury law firm. He has tried a wide variety of matters, ranging from divorce and personal injury to civil commitment and criminal matters. Many of these cases received significant media attention, both locally and nationally. He has successfully litigated against some of the most prominent attorneys in the Twin Cities.

Jason enjoys lecturing at continuing legal education seminars, including the annual Minnesota Family Law Institute, and has authored many published articles. Local media appearances include the Star Tribune, WCCO Television, KMSP Television, KARE 11 Television, WCCO Radio, KTWIN Radio, City Pages and Minnesota Lawyer. National media appearances include NBC News, Fox News, USA Today, Time Magazine and the Huffington Post.

Jason has volunteered as a high school mock trial coach with Legacy Christian Academy, and serves on the Board of Directors at Northgate Church.

Jason graduated magna cum laude from Minnesota State University Mankato and earned his Juris Doctorate, with honors, from the William Mitchell College of Law. During law school, Jason served as a published staff writer and associate editor of the Law Review and as a member, and later coach, of William Mitchell’s International Moot Court.
