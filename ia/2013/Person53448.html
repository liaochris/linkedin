Holly Eburne's Summary

Holly Eburne is a Physiotherapist (32 years) and a Life Coach. Her career as a physiotherapist has involved years of working (and traveling Internationally) with elite and recreational athletes. It wasn't long before Holly recognized the connection between their physical health, injuries, pain--and stress. She realized that without taking care of a person's 'inner' game, she was only putting a bandaid on their symptoms.

In Holly's Life Coaching practice she helps people of all ages, but her primary focus is women who are on the edge of Adrenal burnout/exhaustion: waking up at night worrying about finances, business, family, or relationships; never feeling like there is enough time in the day; often feels tired but wired; has no time for self; may have digestive issues; feels a sense of panic often; doesn't usually ask for help; and can't say no easily because she feels guilty.

Using a variety of releasing and coaching strategies, Holly helps these women find 'calm among their chaos', that allows them to have free time without feeling guilty, sleep better, have more energy and improved health, and as a result have more success in their personal and professional life.


"changing lives by helping people see their lives differently"

Holly Eburne's Experience

Owner

Holly Eburne Coaching Services

June 2009 – Present (4 years 5 months)

Early in my physiotherapy career I recognized a correlation between the stresses in people's lives and their physical symptoms. Scientists have now proven that over 90% of pain, illness, and disease has a root in stress.

Although I work with all people of all ages, my primary focus is helping women (just like where I was a few years ago) who are on the edge of Adrenal burnout. They are waking up at night worrying about finances, family, business, or whatever. They wonder how they can keep managing...and doing it all for everyone, but themselves. Their days are go-go-go and they want off this treadmill but don't know where the 'off' switch is.

Using a variety of releasing and coaching techniques, and strategies, I help these women find peace among their 'busyness', or chaos, so they sleep better, and as a result have better health, more energy, and therefore achieve more success in their life--personally and professionally.

I started working as a physiotherapist in 1981 at RIH in Kamloops. I was an outpatient therapist with a focus on post-surgical, athletic, and arthritis. In 1995 I opened my private practice and I felt like a bird released from a cage. I continue to work on a part-time and after 32 years I still LOVE helping people to live their best life. It is about giving them the tools (holistic) to help themselves and that will last a lifetime.
