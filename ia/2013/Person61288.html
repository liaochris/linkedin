Jeffrey Rollins' Summary

Highly skilled, resourceful litigator with 22 years proven track record of successful results in of all cases tried or negotiated. AV peer review rated. Precise, prepared and innovative strategist able to manage multiple complex matters in tort and civil litigation. Solid communicator with talent for building and maintaining trust-based alliances with diverse clients, professionals, support staff and key corporate decision makers. Committed, involved community leader elected to serve as Board Member and officer of several prominent Palm Beach and Treasure Coast organizations affording a broad base of influential contacts. Recognized and honored with Awards for “Outstanding Leadership” and “Distinguished Service.” Juris Doctor with B.A., University of Michigan, J.D. Duquesne University. Admitted to Florida and Federal Bars.

Specialties

Injury Law, Auto, Truck and Motorcycle Accidents, Boat Accidents, Medical Malpractice

Probate Litigation

Litigated in cooperation with and against experienced probate litigators from prestigious firms such as Quarles & Brady, Boose, Casey and others, achieving a consistent record of successful results. Represented the firm in a high six figure attorney’s fee dispute opposing a former circuit court judge of some prestige. Obtained a favorable ruling.

Senior Associate

Roberts & Reynolds, P.A.

Advanced quickly, earning role as Senior Associate in this insurance defense firm which grew from three to seven attorneys during my tenure. Major clients were Liberty Mutual, CIGNA Property & Casualty, Florida League of Cities, Southern Fire Insurance Co., and others. Gained immediate “hands on” exposure to all aspects of litigation as first chair in seven jury and bench trials opposing seasoned trial lawyers and recognized clients. Credited with saving hundreds of thousands of dollars in defense costs and negated potential multi-million dollar verdicts. Identified as first attorney in firm to handle workers’ compensation appellate matters based on experience gained in clerkship at Daniels & Hicks. Single-handedly managed 12 cases settling and winning over 70%. Two major cases were reported in the Southern Second Reporter. Learned excellent case management techniques and built well-rounded foundation of analytical, negotiation and presentation skills.
