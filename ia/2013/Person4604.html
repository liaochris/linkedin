Alin Jacobs' Summary

Without a doubt, 2010 was the most thought-provoking year of my entire career.

Emerging technologies had already transformed the business landscape. Social media continued to challenge direct marketing as the breakthrough strategy of choice. And, the great recession was unrelenting with its impact on marketing budgets. If that wasn’t distracting enough, today’s technology shifted power to the consumer.

Like it, or not – we live in an always connected, email addicted, text messaging, TiVo dependant, social media driven world. Welcome to the (r)evolution…

It wasn’t a revelation that made me realize that my continued growth and success wouldn’t be the result of 25 years of experience as a relationship marketer. Sure, some may be impressed that I had spearheaded a variety of programs for companies such as Xerox, XMPie, Cox Communications, Kodak, Volkswagen of America, Seminole Casinos, Toyota Motor Sales, and the Trump Network. But the real differentiator would be my knowledge and understanding of the next evolutionary step in customer communications, and my expertise in integrating this groundbreaking methodology with traditional data-driven marketing disciplines.

Realizing that marketing is dynamic, a continuum of components that are forever transforming and developing, my focus for 2010 was on information, education, and networking. With a goal to "reimagine" – I devoured dozens of books, more than twice as many newsletters and blog reads, and attended a variety of virtual conferences on subjects ranging from social media to story telling.

Now, "reimagine" has evolved into "synergize". Though still deeply rooted in the fundamental principles of art, science, communication, and technology – success dictates that marketing communications evolve as quickly and dynamically as the marketplace they serve.
