Lee Evans' Summary

Lee Evans (LeRoi Evans) is a Gold Album-award winning Producer/Songwriter/Engineer/Musician. He is a veteran in the music industry with over 30 years experience. In the late '70s and '80s Lee was a band member and musical director performing with top recording with acts such as “The Jimmy Castor Bunch” “Brass Construction” The Force MDs, “New Edition” “Afrikaa Bambaataa”, “Willie Colon” “Johnny Gill” “Kleer” “Bionic Boogie” “Joe Simon” “The Coasters”.

Locally, he worked with artists: Johnny Kemp, Me'lissa Morgan, and Keith Sweat.

Lee Evans is a natural born musician. He brings passion, authenticity and timelessness to his musical creations. Lee Evans' many accomplishments include his work with singer "Marc Anthony" in the early stages if his career, "Cindi Lauper" , "Harry Belafonte", "The Force MDs", "Brass Construction", "Afrikaa Bambaataa" and a long list of dance Hip Hop and “latin freestyle” favorites from the ‘80’s and 90’s. During this time, Mr. Evans became a familiar face in the most prominent NYC studios and earned recognition as a top NYC’s multi-keyboardist/producer/songwriter/engineer.

Lee Evans started a small 4 track recording studio in a tiny basement in the Bronx. “It was always tight and hot as heck down there. Guys would say it felt (and smelled) like a sweatbox but man, we used to Jam!”. Thus, the name JAMBOX was born. Some of today’s music pioneers that came up to record at his studio include, latin artists Marc Anthony & Willie Colon, Afrikaa Bambaataa Jazzy Jay,, Queen Latifah, Doug E. Fresh Lori Michaels, Sa-fire, The Cover Girls, TKA, Amoretto, Cyre, The Cold Crush Brothers, The Force MDs, Brass Construction, Rainy Davis, Glen Jones, DJ Jose “Animal” Diaz, and many more

In 1989, JAMBOX relocated to midtown Manhattan, expanding to a 3 room recording facility. Lee Evans and Cathy Palmisano co-own JAMBOX Entertainment and Recording Studios. JAMBOX Studios has become a major player in the current NYC music recording scene.
