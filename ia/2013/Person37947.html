James Moore's Experience

Activist, technologist and strategist

GeoPartners Foundation

2000 – Present (13 years) Cambridge, Massachusetts, USA; Concord, Massachusetts, USA

Long-time student of collaboration, competition and growth in business ecosystems. Interested in sharing insights with people experimenting with new models of creative capitalism. Like many others, focused on the pressing issues of climate change and global warming, sustainable economic and social development, and establishing economic alternatives to war ("peace economics").
Fascinated by science, interested in nanofabrication, nano imaging and nano/biotechnology, part-time graduate student at the Center for Nanoscale Systems at Harvard.

Academic research on innovative business and technology leadership

[various affiliations]

1993 – Present (20 years) Cambridge, MA

1. Study of health and health care systems, to go beyond current approaches to reform by examining the complex systems relationships among consumer behavior, health care coordination and process improvement, information technology, and law and public policy.

2. Action-research study of the Internet and political and social change, focusing on the role of citizen participation and new forms of interactive media.

3. Longitudinal study of the evolution of the computer and communication sector, with primary concern for innovation, business strategy and leadership, industry structure, and the role of volunteerism and popular movements such as Open Source Software. Currently Jim is engaged with the development of personal media networks and new types of groups and communities.

Co-director

Visionary Lincoln

2007 – 2008 (1 year) Lincoln, MA

Co-directed with three others a 300-member environmental coalition to block destruction of a strategic piece of property located on Cambridge Reservoir at Hobbs Brook, in Lincoln, MA at the border with Waltham. Initiated an online campaign of information and membership, rallied key people and groups, participated in government sponsored planning processes, carried out political action including voter outreach, and prepared for extensive legal action. Success was achieved after two years of effort.

Co-founder and co-leader

Sudan, the Passion of the Present

2004 – 2006 (2 years)

Co-founded and co-led the blog campaign and web community Passion of the Present with Joanne Moore, to bring attention and resources to the genocide in Darfur, Sudan. Helped rally the community of bloggers and other online activists. Active in the formation of other organizations and campaigns including SaveDarfur. Personally blogged daily for more than a year on the issue as a personal testimony as well as to help bring together others. Got to know many truly wonderful people. Tragically, no movement was able to stop the genocide in Sudan, which continues today.

Senior Fellow, Berkman Fellow

Harvard Law School

Educational Institution; 1001-5000 employees; Higher Education industry

2000 – 2005 (5 years) Cambridge, MA

Policy research and advocacy aimed at bridging the digital divide and promoting digital entrepreneurship and digital democracy in the developing world, with special attention to Africa.

Senior Fellow at Harvard Law School, in the Berkman Center for Internet & Society.

Helped Hewlett-Packard form its e-Inclusion initiative and business unit with Barbara Waugh and others. Recruited, organized and chaired its board of advisors.

Founded the Open Economies Project with philanthropic support from Hewlett-Packard, to address the interaction of law, technology and human capital in promoting social and economic entrepreneurship in developing economies. Created active partnerships in Ghana and South Africa.

Advised the G8 Group of Nations on technology and economic development, as a US business representative to the G8 Digital Opportunity Task Force, appointed by the President Clinton.

Advised the government of South Africa as a member of the SA government's e-strategy initiative, supported by the Markle Foundation program for South Africa, led by Ira Magaziner of the Clinton Foundation.

Advised the United Nations, as a member of the UN Information Communications Technology Task Force.

Advised and presented at the World Economic Forum on conditions necessary to promote digital entrepreneurship and digital development worldwide.

Became particularly optimistic about grassroots campaigns for economic and political development. Wrote online essay "The Second Superpower Rears its Beautiful Head" to outline ways in which citizen action might develop in combination with laws and technology. The essay went viral, and was extensively republished online and in print, in English and in multiple translations.

Presidential Campaign National Director of Internet and Information Services

Dean for America

Nonprofit; 201-500 employees; Political Organization industry

2003 – 2004 (1 year) Burlington, Vermont Area

National Director of Internet and Information Services for Howard Dean's presidential campaign. Proud member of a community who changed presidential politics and provided the first mass scale use of the Internet for community organizing, communication and fundraising for a presidential campaign.

Founder & Chief Executive Officer

GeoPartners Research, Inc.

1989 – 2000 (11 years)

Management consulting, business strategy, technology strategy, and organization/implementation services to clients including Jim Henson Productions, AT&T, AT&T Bell Labs, Hewlett-Packard, Intel, Qualcomm, Motorola, Computer Sciences Corporation, Royal Dutch Shell, ABB, Softbank, GE Capital, AT&T Ventures.

Developed and diffused the concept of "business ecosystems" through consulting, training and publications.

Upside Magazine (Silicon Valley) special issue on business ecosystems in 1991;

Harvard Business Review article in May/June 1992--winner of the McKinsey Award for best article of the year;

1996 book "The Death of Competition: Leadership and Strategy in the age of business ecosystems" excerpted in Fortune, reviewed and selected by BusinessWeek as one of the top ten books of the year, reviewed and selected by Wall Street Journal as one of the top books of the decade for entrepreneurs. Multiple translations including European languages, Brazilian Portuguese, Chinese.

Close working relationships with leading academics including Donald Schon of MIT (author of "The Reflective Practitioner").

Invested in small and growing technology companies. Served on two public company boards of directors.

Retired from consulting in 2000 to turn my attention to public service activities.

Long-range planner for computers and education, Senior Research Associate

Conducted research on strategy making, strategic thinking and the use of software applications to enhance management performance and learning.

James Moore's Patents

  • Enhanced syndication

    • United States Patent 8,200,775
    • Issued September 10, 2005
    Inventors: James Moore

    A variety of tools and techniques are disclosed for managing, viewing, publishing, searching, clustering, and otherwise manipulating data streams. Data streams such as RSS data feeds may be searched, aggregated, and filtered into a processed feed. The processed feed, along with rules used to process the feed may be shared in a number of ways. A data feed management system may provide an integrated user interface through which a user may manage feeds, including searching for new feeds, managing and filtering current feeds, modifying a user profile, and sharing feeds and feed configuration data with other users. A server may provide a complementary search engine to locate new feeds and to store and/or index items or posts in known feeds. Together, these technologies may provide a richly-functioned feed management system and greater ease of use for individuals in managing large numbers of feeds and large amounts of data in feeds. Additional functional layers may provide for authentication, security, and privacy, metadata creation and management, and social networking features. Using the management tools and additional functionality, a syndicated data stream system may provide a platform for a wide array of useful consumer and business applications.
