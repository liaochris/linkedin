Adam Worrall's Summary

I am a Doctoral Candidate at Florida State University’s School of Library and Information Studies (SLIS).

My broad research interests include social informatics; digital libraries; collaborative information behavior; scientific collaboration; social media; online communities; and social and community theories in information science. These fall under the common thread of studying information behavior within and around the social and sociotechnical contexts—groups, communities, worlds, and other collaborative environments—of information and communication technologies (ICTs). My current focus is on the sociotechnical, community, and collaborative contexts and roles of digital libraries as boundary objects within and across social worlds, information worlds, and communities.

You can read more details about me at http://www.adamworrall.org/

Adam Worrall's Experience

Principal Investigator

Florida State University School of Library and Information Studies

Educational Institution; 5001-10,000 employees; Higher Education industry

December 2012 – Present (8 months) Tallahassee, Florida Area

"The Roles of Digital Libraries as Boundary Objects Within and Across Social and Information Worlds" is my dissertation research, focusing on the roles that two digital libraries, LibraryThing and Goodreads, play as boundary objects in translation and coherence between existing social and information worlds and in the potential convergence and emergence of new worlds around their use. Draws on Star’s boundary object theory, Strauss’s social worlds framework, and Burnett and Jaeger’s theory of information worlds. I am collecting data via interviews, content analysis, and surveys to examine and interpret these roles and the communities, information behaviors, and activities of users in sociotechnical contexts. Prospectus for dissertation successfully defended 11/2012.
