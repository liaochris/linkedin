Michael Smith's Publications

  • Rural Development in the Crossfire: Grassroots Support Organizations in Situations of Political Violence in Peru

    • Peruvian Graffiti Press
    • 2010
    Authors: Michael Smith

    In the 1980s, Maoist guerrillas were taking aim at advocates of change that were trying to lift marginalized populations out of poverty in the Peruvian Andes. The armed conflict dominated Peruvian politics for the next decade and shaped society for years to come. This book examines the complex factors that led to this confrontation and the military response that the Lima government unleashed in 1983. After providing background information and describing the main political and military forces, the book examines two case studies of non-profit development promotion centers that were attacked by the Shining Path (Sendero Luminoso in Spanish but also known as the Communist Party of Peru) guerrillas: the Allpachaka agricultural station associated with the local university in Ayacucho in 1982 and the Waqrani rural development program associated with the Catholic Church in Puno in 1989. These incidents are thoroughly documented for the first time.

    The author made frequent field trips to interview local leaders, government authorities and other observers to acquire a unique perspective on political conflict at the bleeding edge of development in the Third World. It underscores the social tensions that can be stirred up by "progress." This study was originally published in 1991 in Canada and then in 1992 in Peru. It is currently available on Amazon as a Kindle e-book.

  • Why People Grow Drugs: Narcotics and Development in the Third World

    • Panos Institute, London
    • 1992
    Authors: Michael Smith

    Journalists from Pakistan, Thailand, Colombia, Peru and Bolivia contribute accounts of how the global illicit drug trade has affected local communities, distorted their prospects for development and leaves them vulnerable to trafficking organizations.
