Joseph Hogue, CFA's Summary

** Now taking new clients. Contact me through this site or through EfficientAlpha.com with your writing and analysis needs.

I provide client communication and investment research solutions for small and medium-sized firms in the investment management industry. Core products include: Newsletters & Blogging, Equity & Investment Analysis, and Ghost-writing.

Current Projects include:
• FinQuiz Blog (Administrator)
• Street Authority (Feature Article Writer)
• Emerging Money (Weekly Economic Analysis)
• BCU Investment Advisors (Newsletter Contributor)
• Seeking Alpha (Weekly Investment Analysis)

As an independent provider of investment writing and research, I have developed the skill to provide quality and insightful analysis that draws investor interest. Over 450 reports, articles and flash notes published by Morningstar, the Colombian Securities Exchange, the International Economic Development Council, and other financial websites.

With between one and four posts per week, the blog I administer (FinQuiz) consistently attracts visitors to the site. It appears in the top ten search results for more than 20 popular search terms and in the top three for several terms relevant to the subject matter.

I finished my degrees after service in the military and got into commercial real estate. After working as an economist for the State of Iowa, I moved back to Colombia where I have previous experience in trade and real estate development. I am a former Communications Director on the Board of the CFA Society of Iowa and hold the Chartered Financial Analyst designation.

Specialties: Fluent Spanish and English, Research Economics, Client & Investor Communications, Emerging Markets with emphasis on Latin America

Joseph Hogue, CFA's Experience

Founder/Analyst

Efficient Alpha

Privately Held; Myself Only; Financial Services industry

June 2011 – Present (2 years 4 months)

I use this website as a conduit to present my work in financial analysis and investment research, and to secure writing and analysis projects. My normal clientele are small to medium-sized firms with analysis or reporting needs but whom may have insufficient staff or topic expertise.

Labor Market Research Economist

Iowa Workforce Development

Government Agency; 501-1000 employees; Government Administration industry

July 2008 – August 2013 (5 years 2 months)

I collect, analyze, and disseminate information about Iowa's labor market. I work mostly with employment growth functions of the labor market, but also research how other areas affect our employment market as well.

I am working on a survey measuring the state's job vacancies, retirement outlook, and projected changes in payroll and how they relate to occupational demand. I have just started a project to regionally measure the skill and knowledge based clusters and perform a gap analysis for the needs of knowledge-based industries.

I have just completed a VBA macro that is being applied as part of a ten state project to assess the skills gap among manufacturing workers. The macro does in three minutes what would normally take three months.

Senior Financial Reporting Analyst

Marsh

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; MMC; Insurance industry

February 2003 – December 2006 (3 years 11 months)

I worked as a senior reporting analyst for three years. I collected and analyzed data from the general ledger, accounts payable, and financial reporting systems to reconcile receivables and payables. The best part of the job was researching the account variances between the different departments within finance/accounting, reconciling them with various reports, and making adjustments. It's a bit like being a detective, though (unfortunately) nothing like the stuff on tv.
