Michel Vanhoonacker's Projects

  • Flanders Fields Memorial Garden 1914-2014 in London

    • January 2011 to Present
    Team Members: Michel Vanhoonacker, Piet Blanckaert, Andrew Wallis, Nic Vandermarliere

    “The Memorial Garden should honour all those who fought and died in the war
    and it should be a symbol of a better future for all.”


    The creation of a Memorial Garden ‘Flanders Fields 1914-2014’ is an initiative of The Guards Museum and is supported by Flanders House in London. The garden will be located at Wellington Barracks alongside the Guards Chapel which is adjacent to Buckingham Palace.

    The new garden will be a unique opportunity not only to commemorate the 100th anniversary of the start of the Great War but also to thank the British people for their sacrifice in liberating our country.

    The project will involve specially collecting soil from the Flanders Fields battlefields in ceremonies of remembrance. It will be transported to London where it will be laid into a bed in the newly constructed garden.

    The Memorial Garden has been designed by internationally acclaimed landscape architect Piet Blanckaert from Bruges, Belgium. Every aspect has been carefully considered so that it becomes a very special memorial to all those who lost their lives or were injured in the battlefields of Flanders.

    The project is supported wholeheartedly by The Flemish Government, The House of Lords, the City
    of Westminster, the British Army, English Heritage, the Belgian-Luxembourg Chamber of Commerce in Great Britain and many individual and corporate supporters.

    The Garden will be opened in July 2014 during a very special ceremony which will be attended by members of both countries’ Royal Families.
