Executive Director

Virginia Solar Energy Association

March 2009 – Present (4 years 6 months)

We host a tour of facilities with solar energy power production and solar energy management features built in as an inspirational and educational experience. We advocate for positions not covered by other more mainstream solar advocacy orgs that are complimentary and supportive of managing the solar resource that is available to all residents of Planet Earth. For example, Shade Cloth is a very cost-effective and underutilized way to cut down 4-% to 95% of the sun's heat on a window in the summer BEFORE that heat even touches the window! Its more effective than overhangs and is removable and as cheap as a curtain (because that's really what it is).

Synergy Radio Project

January 2007 – Present (6 years 8 months)

I'm the engineer on the Board of Directors of Synergy Project. Under my technical and consultant direction we have offered to host and build translators for WRIR-LP as well as applied for two full power FM community radio stations. This is because WRIR-LP is a "secondary status" radio station and if a Full Power station wants their frequency, WRIR loses that frequency and all that work is lost. Becoming a Full Power station prevents that loss.

Founder

WRIR Richmond Independent Radio

Nonprofit; 51-200 employees; Music industry

1992 – March 2007 (15 years) 1621 W Broad St. , Richmond, Va. 23220

Built USA's largest community LPFM radio station with no debt, grants or loans by joining thousands of people and organizations across a full political spectrum and the nation to help Don Schellhardt (successfully) persuade the Federal Communication Commission to create the Low Power FM (LFPM) service category in 2000. Then inspired hundreds of Richmonders to apply (successfully) in 2000 for permit to construct an LPFM radio station. Finally got on-air in 2004, January 1st with hundreds of volunteers and thousands of locals donating $15,000 and no debt, no corporate funding, no government funding or grants. Big thanks to Liz Skrobishevsky-Humes for also persuading many of her contacts from her time as editor of Punchline to underwrite shows that didn't exist yet!

Founder - Recycling Program of Virginia Commonwealth University

Virginia Commonwealth University

Educational Institution; 10,001+ employees; Higher Education industry

1991 – 1995 (4 years) Richmond, Virginia Area

I took over the VCU Recycling Coop from Ann Fletcher then persuaded VCU's visionary Physical Plant manager, Ted Pelikan, to hire me as a student worker so I could use their equipment. While taking a full class load of 12 to 18 credit hours I expanded Recycling from 38 tons in 1992 to 305 tons in 1995 and made a profit of over $5.20/ton. I heard at a recycling conference that making a profit in an urban university with taller buildings is considered nearly impossible. Luckily nobody told me that! Then President Eugene Trani let the program die when I moved to South Florida to work at WPBR. The outraged staff and students pressed Trani to reinstate Recycling - and Mr Swistock hired a bureaucrat from CVWMA who unfortunately did not reinstate much of the community outreach I had innovated for Richmond's immediate community right around VCU.
