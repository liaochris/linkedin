Michael Renz's Summary

I am a generalist. I work with software, hardware, networking, as well as metalworking, woodworking, chemistry, biology, electronics - anything I can get my hands on, really.

I have goals. Some of these goals include constructing my own lasers, etc. and testing them in DoE tunnels, but they're still goals. I do not believe that this little box could list the hierarchy of my goals properly.

I don't care about money. I don't care about credit. I don't care about having a cellphone, or if Britney got a haircut, or when and where the next party is. There's a very high probability that you will never meet me in public. I have better stuff to do on my own, like researching on how to map and block Tor networks, or running a better ground around here so that the power conditioners I use for my comp and audio equipment actually work well.

How are these related to my professional experience and goals? Well, they make up a very large picture of who I am. Wherever I work, it should be understood that you can't offer me more money to get me to do something differently - I put a very high value on a logical discussion, and will sacrifice my time out of work to learn more about the subject matter if necessary.
