Alberto Labarga's Summary

For the past years I have pursued a successful career in bioinformatics and laboratory software development. Coming from the field of telecommunication engineering, I started at university teaching and developing research in the field of biomedical engineering.

Along my scientific career I have worked at TECNUN and later on at the European Bioinformatics Institute where I led the Service Oriented Architecture strategy of the Institute, coordinating the development of a new data and services framework, that included a revamped web site and a unified search engine.

I have also teaching experience at University of Navarra, Public University of Navarra and IE University.

My entrepreneur character and my management skills also enabled me to achieve high performance level as Executive Director of several Spanish technology start-ups.

Concepts such as information architecture, user interface design, content strategy and visual design are the main drivers of my work. Also I have a major interest in the introduction of new software development methodologies (agile development, continuous integration) and open technologies.

Specialties

bioinformatics, scientific data management systems (LIMS,LIS,HIS,ELN), data mining, big data, open data, SOA, information architecture, visual design, project management, team building,

Alberto Labarga's Projects

  • Bionotate

    • January 2008 to Present
    Team Members: Alberto Labarga, Carlos Cano Gutierrez

    BioNotate-2.0 is a web-based open source system for supporting such distributed collaborative annotation efforts. Key assertions are extracted from the literature based on a user query, automatically annotated and mapped to external resources. These annotated extracts can be further corrected and enhanced through collaborative user manual curation via a simple web interface. The results are then converted into Semantic / Linked Data format and made accessible via a SPARQL endpoint.

Alberto Labarga's Publications

  • The semantic web and the social web heading towards a living document in life sciences

    • Elsevier Grand Challenge 2008
    • 2008
    Authors: Alberto Labarga, César Montaña, alexander garcia, Leyla Jael Garcia Castro

    This project focuses on facilitating three aspects central to the semantic web vision: organize, share and discover. These three milestones require massive live knowledge elicitation that is feasible within the context of digital libraries and, more importantly, it is viable with the existing resources. In order to deliver an environment where users can manage papers and related information, share their knowledge and discover hidden associations the elicited knowledge must be effectively managed.
