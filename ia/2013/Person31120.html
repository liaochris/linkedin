David Hite's Summary

David Hite is an accomplished Organizational Learning and Development Technologist who has helped organizations, such as Eastman Chemical Co., Tennessee Valley Authority, and Univ of TN, enhance their operations through the use of HR strategies and web technology. He has helped design, develop, implement and evaluate employee training programs for diverse types of employees as well as mentored small businesses when transitioning to online operations.

Currently, David is the program coordinator for business programs at Virginia Intermont College. David has taught courses over the past ten years at several area colleges in addition to online courses for other universities. These courses include: Management, HR Management, International HR, eCommerce, Entrepreneurism, Consumer Behavior, and online courses in Marketing, HR, and Mgt. Information Systems.

He received the Business Journal's 40-Under-40 Leadership Award in 2007, as well as being the keynote for their 2008 awards.He has been fortunate to have studied business in over 25 countries, including Greece, China, and Europe and is able to incorporate diverse viewpoints into most of the projects he works on.

David has an associate degree in Computer Science, a Bachelor's degree in Workforce Education and Training, and a Master's degree from the University of Tennessee in Human Resources where he focused his research on Organizational Development and Instructional eLearning technologies. His graduate work in HR focused on virtual learning organizations that use web technologies to improve organizational effectiveness.

He received a full-scholarship to help implement the first full online B.S. program in HR in the country. He led a project team at Eastman Chemical Company to create an international learning portal to help improve and centralize training and development initiatives for over 10,000 employees.

Specialties

Organizational assessments, group facilitation, performance improvement methodologies, Web 2.x eLearning, curriculum design, organizational development, human resource and workforce development strategies
