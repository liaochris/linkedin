I am combining my 10+ years of planning, operations, and training experience in emergency management and response with my experience in innovation and technology to improve resilience for both organizations and communities.

I have a particular focus on policy and management challenges related to:

► Technology and Innovation in Disaster Management and Resilience
► Management and Coordination in Large-Scale and Complex Emergencies
► Data, Information and Decision-Making for Disaster Management
► Building Organizational and Community-Based Resilience

As an "outside-the-box" thinker, I affect change by combing my expertise in policy, management, and entrepreneurship at the macro, meso, and micro levels of resilience.

I am currently a PhD student in Crisis, Risk and Disaster Management at The George Washington University where I am studying the impact of technology on disaster operations. I have a Masters of Public Administration from NYU's Wagner School of Public Service and hold a Graduate Certificate in Community Preparedness and Disaster Management from the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill.

Brandon Greenberg's Projects

  • Open Corporate Registration Data

    • September 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Brandon Greenberg, Alexandros Hatzakis, Michael Swigert

    Imagine a comprehensive and accurate global directory of information about public and private companies that regulators, investors, civil society, the media and researchers could easily and freely access and combine with other datasets to promote corporate accountability, economic growth, and the public interest.

    To advance this vision, we were engaged by two of the world's leading company registers, to investigate alternative business models (considering financial, technological and political factors) to inform their consideration of how and why they might make the information about business entities they collect more freely accessible and open. The team interviewed experts and innovators in open data, other company registries, and current and potential users of company registry data and leveraged social media to generate public dialogue among stakeholders. This research informed the final analysis and recommendations to both the Delaware Secretary of State Division of Corporations and the UK Companies House.

  • #CodeforChange - NYC OEM Crowdmapping Challenge

    • October 2012 to November 2012
    Team Members: Brandon Greenberg

    Worked with developer to customize a crowdmapping platform based on Ushahidi called Taarifa. Using the Ushahidi codebase, we added an HTML 5 Webapp for mobile browser reporting and added workflows for reports. This mapping solution was delivered to NYC OEM in November 2012.
