Brent Skinner's Summary

As marketing manager for Ceridian HCM, I help to spread word of the division's full range of products, with special emphasis on Dayforce HCM, Ceridian Corporation's SaaS application.

In 2004 I launched STETrevisions, a communications consultancy whose project work guided me, ultimately, to my present role. Previously tech editor for HRO Today and main contributor to TalentManagementTech, I co-founded and used to co-host #HRTechChat. Under the auspices of HRO Today and #HRTechChat, my focus was technology for human resources, talent management and recruiting. Quoted in a Wall Street Journal article exploring the compensation of executive search professionals, I am, additionally, a former contributing editor to Executive Recruiter News. Through it all has been my mainstay position at Boston University's College of Communication, where, since 2003, I have been an adjunct instructor of writing for the communications industry.

Brent Skinner's Education

Boston University

Master of Science, Communication, Public Relations, Writing

1997 – 1999

As a graduate student at Boston University's College of Communication (COM), I completed a thesis that explored how the source credibility of online environments might affect traditional news media gatekeepers' control over information.

Surprisingly, the data revealed no statistically significant correlation between online sources' perceived credibility and people's propensity to believe posted content. The implications were obvious, at least in an abstract sense, suggesting that traditional news gatekeepers might one day see their influence wane. Blogs and other nontraditional, seemingly less credible online news sources have since knowingly, or unwittingly, exploited the new ground rules, becoming examples of the profound possibilities.

Activities and Societies: Editor in Chief of "The COMmunicator," an internal newsletter for COM

Bachelor of Arts, Music Performance with a minor in Spanish

1990 – 1994

A classically trained musician, I played principal clarinet in UNH's various wind ensembles and orchestral groups.
