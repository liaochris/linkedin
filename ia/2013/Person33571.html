Deborah Pardes' Summary

From talk to tangible -- I launch ideas.

A veteran presenter, writer, producer, I leverage my understanding of concise messaging (what fits in a 3 minute songs) into everything I do.

My big love and talent is building community through creativity. I collaborate with teams and individuals to bring powerful words and imagery to their campaigns and initiatives. People hire me to write and think through communication strategies. I get people out of silos and into circles.

We've just launched "Cool the World". It's a project of Artists for Literacy - the org I founded 13 years ago. Cool the World is like 'we are the world' but this time - it's about kids and the environment. It's a powerful project led by those young kids who stand to gain the most if we safeguard this planet. www.cooltheworld.org

If you need some fun @ work - think about Show & Tell. I'm bringing it into the corporate world, after 7 years of running them as social events (which I continue to do!) I'm now working with businesses and organizations to better connect their community members to each other. It's such fun, important work. www.showandtellcorp.com

Deborah Pardes' Experience

Founder

Artists for Literacy

January 2000 – Present (13 years 8 months) Greater Los Angeles Area

I founded Artists for Literacy, In 2000, after gaining national recognition for a song I wrote & recorded inspired by Frank McCourt’s novel, Angela’s Ashes. We have mobilized over 1000 songwriters and musicians nation-wide to promote literacy, including Bruce Springsteen, David Bowie, Tom Waits, Aimee Mann , Steve Earle & Roseanne Cash. Our Songs Inspired By Literature (SIBL) Project garnered me and AFL's mission coverage in the New York Times, Wall Street Journal, Rolling Stone, All Things Considered, (NPR) and more.

Owner

Show and Tell Corp

February 2007 – Present (6 years 7 months) Santa Monica

Since 2007, I have been running these privately, for social events. Now I am taking Show & Tell into businesses. Show & Tell brings a new appreciation of who we are to each other. It fosters understanding, by shedding light on a deeper part of ourselves. It allows us to break from regular convention and gives a moment up for story telling of the most basic kind – oral, spontaneous, eye-to-eye. A rare picture, a watch, a mask from Thailand — objects we cherish can help people learn about who we are and what makes us tick. And from that understanding, comes greater potential for things to grow between us. www.showandtellcorp.com

Deborah Pardes' Projects

  • Cool the World

    • April 2013 to Present
    Team Members: Deborah Pardes

    Think "We are the World" circa 1985, except now it's kids singing and we're melting. We have set out to take one song 'Cool the World' and make sure it's heard all over the world, in as many languages as possible. Why? Because art and storytelling of this sort might be - in the case of climate change - the very thing that helps us reach a tipping point of change. Viral happiness = impact.

  • One Million Moms view Hyland's Video

    • January 2010 to Present
    Team Members: Deborah Pardes, Rusty Howes

    I've been the senior writer and advertising creative for Hyland's Homeopathic for over 6 years. They were a need of a cool way to re-launch their teething tablets. I wrote a jingle on Ukulele..and the rest is self-explanatory. I'd love to do more of these!

  • Producer, Engineer of NPR Syndicated "Experience Talks"

    • April 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Deborah Pardes, Alicia Sedwick, tim carpenter

    I co-produced a two part series that shares stories told by older adults in Southern California who participated in the Talk Story: Democracy program. Funded by the California Council for the Humanities, Talk Story: Democracy was a storytelling class offered by the non-profit EngAGE that taught older adults to write and present personal stories about democracy. The stories focus on the perspective gained by people who come from other countries to experience freedom for the first time later in life.

  • Get Smart Radio

    • January 2008 to Present
    Team Members: Deborah Pardes

    This show is recorded both in SF and in LA in front of a live studio audience. It was created to bring together complimentary experts to discuss a compelling topic. The audience has a chance to engage, ask questions, create the vibe of the show. We archive them for podcast download. My goal is to grow this project in scope and frequency. I also taught a class to kids - to empower them with great listening and talking skills.

  • Show and Tell

    • January 2007 to Present
    Team Members: Deborah Pardes

    It's an night that puts people back stories up front. It started at social parties - now I am bringing it to corporate environments. It's a great way to connect people to each other in new ways - ways that are memorable and meaningful.
