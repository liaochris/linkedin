Alaina Browne's Summary

I'm passionate about building platforms that humanize the internet, and enable personal, one-to-one connections that transcend the online experience.

I was the founding General Manager of Serious Eats, one of the largest and most respected independent food publications and communities on the Internet. I oversaw business operations and product development since before the site's launch to its 2013 audience of millions of visitors per month and its recognition in the form of two James Beard awards. Prior to helping launch Serious Eats, I was the first employee at Mule Design, where I helped the company grow from a smart partnership to an industry leading design studio. I led key projects including the relaunch of Chow.com as well as significant projects for Intuit/Quickbooks, Jotspot (later sold to Google) and more.

I have been a blogger since 1998, and in 2003 my food blog A Full Belly (then NYC Eats) was the first food blog ever recognized as Yahoo's Site of the Day. My earlier career includes work as an independent consultant and as a project and product manager at Mindspring through the acquisition by EarthLink.
