Lee Quessenberry's Projects

  • Kitty Hawk Kites Online Store Upgrade

    • June 2013 to Present
    Team Members: Lee Quessenberry

    The store website for Kitty Hawk Kites needed an upgrade to the current system it was using to allow for a mobile interface for smartphones as well as added improvements to the inventory system for allowing products to comply with Google Base Feed requirements for product listings in Product Listing Advertisements and Google Shopping. The site was rebuilt for SEO effectiveness, clean mark up, ease of use, and better inventory management.

    The design was overhauled to incorporate a bright but easy to use interface that also worked well with tablet devices to compensate for the more than 40% traffic visits from iOS and iPad devices. The interface was made easier to use for finger tip navigation and organization of content.

  • Global Ag Equipment

    Team Members: Lee Quessenberry, Cody McGrew

    This project was built as a tool for agricultural equipment dealers to easily locate "white labeled" used implement and equipment for sale overseas and in other networks outside of northeast Arkansas. It has a largely extensive amount of items for review and for sale by several vendors and manufacturers.

    This project illustrates the facility of Drupal as a data management and data parsing tool. The items in this site are fed into the system from several different data feeds and is parsed into Drupal fields and views by using the Feeds module and submodules to create an interface that categorizes and organizes each item by several taxonomies.

    I also created the logo for the brand.

  • Commemoration Project

    • August 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Lee Quessenberry, Charles Carter, Sherry Caldwell, Chase Patton

    The Commemoration Project is an effort built with honor, courage, and remembrance for those who have given the ultimate sacrifice to protect our interests as American citizens both domestically and abroad. The project is a nationwide hiking expedition where the members of the team will place batons in remembrance of fallen soldiers of all divisions of the US military from several different operations including Desert Storm, Iraqi Freedom, and Afghanistan.

    I was instrumental in the design of the logo and the site theme to be used for the website and all marketing and branding efforts used by the team. Along with help and input from team members Chase Patton and Sherry Caldwell, I was able to develop a logo that will stand to show unity, freedom, and the resolve of the American Soldier.

    The crew will take a trek across the United States placing commemorative plaques every half mile for the soldiers who made the ultimate sacrifice.

    My duties were to create a website that tracks their progress along the journey and provide a portal for donations, outreach for families, and interaction between members of the crew and friends and families of those fallen soldiers. This project was started as a professional endeavor when I was a self employed web developer, but I have since become a member of the crew and am working to keep the site up for the crew as they make the trek.

  • DeClerk LP Gas

    • June 2011 to Present
    Team Members: Lee Quessenberry, Jennifer Clay, Abbi Siler, Bill DeClerk

    What started as a simple website for a propane company in Pocahontas, AR became the passion and dream of Bill DeClerk, owner of DeClerk LP Gas. I was influential and instrumental in developing a new look for the company, including an all new color scheme, logo, and website design. From there, with help of Abbi Siler, we were able to develop strategies to allow DeClerk LP Gas to interact with existing clients and new clients all over Northeast Arkansas by inviting them to participate in online marketing activities including a large giveaway of propane gas to a randomly drawn winner. After building the site, providing safety tips on the site, and providing general information on the advantages of using propane, we took things a step further and created informational and comical videos for the continued promotion of propane use inside and outside of the home. Bill DeClerk was very involved and even took the video production on his own to record messages about general use as well as specialized uses for propane and propane accessories.

    The logo was a smash success and was placed on an 80 ft long holding tank visible from the highway entering Pocahontas. It is the largest application of a single logo that I have created to date.

    Jennifer Clay, of Pocahontas, AR, was able to take over some of the management of content on the site, and is currently working with me to keep the site relevant, full of content, and has taken over the responsibilities for marketing DeClerk LP Gas online via Facebook and the website.
