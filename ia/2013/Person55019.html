Charlotte Reznick PhD's Summary

As a licensed Child educational Psychologist and Associate Clinical Professor Emeritus of Psychology at UCLA, I specialize in helping children and adolescents develop the emotional skills necessary for a happy and successful life.

My focus and philosophy is one of self-empowerment. Rather than give children answers, I teach them the tools to find the answers for themselves through the positive power of their imagination. Children and adolescents learn the skills needed to express their feelings appropriately, gain self-confidence, and develop more positive social skills.

Youth have been successfully helped to: reduce stress, cope with the losses of divorce and death, the worries of parental and sibling conflicts, deal with anger and frustration, heal somatic complaints, overcome sleep disorders, increase concentration, and compensate for learning disabilities.

Notes of appreciation from families:

“I wanted to let you know that our son is now a delightful, straight A, 8th grade student. He is so organized, self-motivated, self-assured, thanks in no small part to your time with him and the tools he learned from you. Thank you ever so much!”

"I have often heard it said, 'If you make a difference in one person's life, then you have made a great accomplishment.' Well .... you have certainly made a difference in our daughter's life. You have given her so many tools to use with which she can overcome life's challenges. You have helped to shape a wonderful young person, who truly believes in herself and knows herself.

We are most grateful to you and all the life's lessons we have learned as a family because of you. With gratitude and admiration."

"Thank you for everything, especially this year as my teen transitions to college. You have done so much to support and shape the health and resilience of my family."
