Meg E. Cox's Summary

For 14 years as a freelance writer and editor I have been helping service providers and academics to make their message more accessible to the public. My clientele has varied widely--from the Environmental Law Institute and an association of nonprofits to the City Colleges of Chicago and multiple university presses.

The growing edge of my practice is online content production for nonprofits and professional-service providers. I help clients identify their audience, develop an editorial calendar, and create a steady flow of content. I am also doing more work with authors from around the world, particularly in the human rights sector.

As an online content manager I have been the client's voice on Twitter and have produced a steady stream of blog content by writing articles and soliciting and editing content from staff members and outside contributors.

My written work has appeared in the Chicago Tribune, Sojourners, the Christian Century, the newsletter of the Christian Community Development Association, and elsewhere. In 2008 one of my articles on voting rights was published in a Norton anthology of writings on politics. In 2009 Cascade published my edited collection of addresses from a conference on cynicism and hope. I have also written curriculum and successful grant proposals.

My editorial clients value my expertise in untangling particularly challenging manuscripts, and I have developed a reputation for effective author diplomacy and exceptionally clean edits. Since 2005 I have been the copy editor for the award-winning book-review section of the Christian Century. I am also the top-of-the-list freelance book indexer for a major university press.

Specialties

As a writer: careful research; clear, analytical writing; clean finished products. As an editor: preserving the author's voice and meaning while streamlining the presentation and improving clarity. As a consultant to nonprofits: experience as a practitioner, manager, and fundraiser for nonprofits; skill in writing engaging and respectful client and volunteer stories.
