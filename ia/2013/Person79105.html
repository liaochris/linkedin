Lauren Moccio's Summary

I am a young woman with an entrepreneurial spirit, an eye for detail and a passion for the written word. I am well-versed in both expository and creative writing, having studied both in college--though my degree concentration was in expository writing and the mechanics of language because I believe that these are necessary skills for both fiction and non-fiction.

Upon graduating college in 2008, I launched my own small affiliate marketing business, Laurie Beth's Grotto, while working part-time at my local library (a job I still adore) and working freelance to help market a local entertainment company that specializes in young, up-and-coming artists. While marketing both businesses, I learned to write advertising copy and to use social media for marketing and community outreach. My passion for politics and social progress blossomed into a passion including new media and blogging. I connected with several non-profits through Twitter and have used my Twitterfeed and blog to further their message. I've learned how to communicate more efficiently with people from all walks of life.

After leaving my position with ADHD Entertainment in 2009, I began to concentrate more fully on writing freelance editorials for outlets such as Break Studios and Gather.com. I recently re-connected with ADHD Entertainment in order to proofread and ghostwrite several manuscripts, among other projects.

My objective is to create effective marketing campaigns for businesses, freelancers and non-profits, using copywriting, blogging and social media, and to use new media for community outreach and social progress. I am available for social media, general PR, creative writing and ghost writing, speech writing, data entry and blogging.

Specialties

Expository writing, creative writing, copywriting, blogging, social media and web content, data entry and clerical duties, proofreading, customer service. Proficient in MS Word and PowerPoint.

We children of Baby Boomers are often separated into nonsensical categories like Generations X, Y and Z (or Generation "Next," the "Me"generation, "Yuppies," the "heroin chic," the "Google" or "MySpace" generation, the "New Silent" generation, etc.)...all designed to depict us as divided, selfish and devoid of compassion. In actuality, we should be a united front resisting such division and pushing for social and scientific progress. I firmly believe that rather than being deadened by our new found technology, we can use it to connect with one another and share information. This website is intended to be a gathering place for younger people to find resources tailored to them.
