Jaime Palmera II's Summary

Studio Palmera pursues excellence in contemporary design by best resolving programmatic, spatial and structural challenges artistically and creatively to achieve superior solutions for the clients' needs. I enjoy exploring the potentials of form, space and order. I study the design’s context and the relationship between the elements of architecture.

Well beyond mere design skills and technical prowess, I reflect on the inhabitants psychological responses to the built environment and strive to transcend by incorporating these responses into successful design studies. This integration matures into a holistic architectural experience. The holistic approach greatly enhances the pragmatic substance of a structure and its space. I’ve always believed in the power and influence of design. Through design one has the special ability to transform something mundane into something inspirational, the normal into unique, the structural into sculptural, a room into an environment. A philosophy that instruments my ability to design. Leads me to pursue architecture as more than just a job and profession, but as a vital tool to create better architecture, better habitat.

By being passionate for design and abiding to an ethical commitment to it rather than commercialism Studio Palmera has built its reputation and gained the trust of professional colleagues.

Drawing and designing buildings comes naturally to me. As a child sitting in my father's architectural office and "helping" him design buildings, through my school days when I would spend hours examining and "red-marking" the lavish Manhattan properties of the real estate pages of the New York Times Magazine, and when I see structures with great potential that are lacking design, were poorly executed, or are in need of repair. My academic education began at the Universidad de Los Andes in Bogota, Colombia, and culminated with a degree in Architecture with Honors from The Catholic University in Washington DC.

Fine Art Photographer

Palmera Fine Art Photography

June 2009 – Present (4 years 8 months) Miami Beach, San Francisco, New York City, Hawaii

I am a self-taught photographer. My abstract photography mostly depicts landscapes, architecture, still life and some nude subjects. The photos are conceived through the interaction of time, movement and light exposure in spirit with the Dadaist principals which embraced part calculation and part chance to capture impressionist images beyond reality.

I paint with the camera, it becomes my brush. I go beyond what is commonly seen with the naked eye by manipulating the camera to achieve the desired effects while respecting the integrity of the captured subject. The photographs become an exploration of light, movement and form, a graphical poetry of shapes, shades and colors producing surprising and unexpected imagery. Some photographs divert into a study in strong colors as they speak further of the sensuality of color and our attraction to it, breaking with our conventional perceptions or our predictable reality.

I want my photography to draw us into the image and transport us to that place as in within a hazy memory, a vision, a dream. Some images are also dynamic, vibrant, with a force that blurs details but succeeds in capturing the essence of the subject. I feel these images have a soul, a feeling of that captured subject, which I want to project to the viewer.
