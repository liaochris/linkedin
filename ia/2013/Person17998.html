Brittany Kay Hanson's Experience

Graphic Designer

ZGF Architects LLP

Privately Held; 201-500 employees; Architecture & Planning industry

October 2012 – Present (1 year) Portland, Oregon Area

As a newer addition to ZGF Architects, I have been working on large marketing pursuits, including RFP and RFQ responses, interview prep and specialty graphics for client recognition.
Working on a larger team with bigger deadlines and more complex marketing efforts, I'm learning to prioritize quickly while minding all the details.
One thing I've learned thus far, is that Everything Matters; The Design is in the Details.

Graphic Designer & Coordinator

SRG Partnership

Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Architecture & Planning industry

August 2010 – October 2012 (2 years 3 months) Portland OR

As the graphic design and visual communications point person, I supported firm-wide marketing efforts including proposals (answers to RFPs and RFQs), firm interviews, award submissions, public relations and social media. While at SRG I coordinated and lead a new brand launch with Portland's own "the Felt Hat" http://felthat.com/. This included updating the website, all collateral materials, job signage, and the face of SRG on all social media sites; in addition to communicating with the firm's partners at monthly meetings and across multiple offices.
As part of a small, but fierce marketing team environment I was given the opportunity to lead a handful of proposal efforts and firm-wide award submissions. I also helped create a strong set of clean, user friendly templates for all marketing collateral including proposals and interview materials.
During my 2nd year, in addition to my other tasks, I took on the coordination of SRG's firm-wide architectural photography. This including setting up shoots with the architects, clients and photographer in addition to attending the shoots and art directing specific shots.

I thoroughly enjoyed my time at SRG and am grateful for all that I have learned and experienced while working with the SRG team.

Volunteer

Together Gallery

October 2009 – March 2011 (1 year 6 months) Alberta Arts District, Portland, OR

As a volunteer at Together Gallery I was given my first foray into the Portland art scene. Surrounded by highly accomplished, quirky fine artists I took part in gallery openings, show hangings, and art selling. Every Sunday for just under two years I worked as a gallery attendant and salesperson, responsible for all transactions. This opportunity was a wonderful way to meet local artists, cultivate my knowledge of art, and inspire some of my personal illustration work.

Freelance Design

Coty Hogue, Senva Inc, & Good Seed

June 2010 – September 2010 (4 months) Portland, OR

Given a break in-between full-time positions, I worked diligently as a freelance graphic designer out of my small studio in Portland, OR. These names are just a handful of projects I worked on:
Coty Hogue, a talented folk artist, asked me to create her website announcing the launch of her new album, To The West.
Senva, Inc, a specialty sensor manufacturing company, worked with me to create new email campaigns and update the product inventory on their website.
Good Seed, which is a food product created by Carte Blanche's Jessie Aron, worked closely with me to create a logo, utilitarian packaging and a brand-promise book to use while pitching to potential buyers.

Graphic Designer/ Marketing Communications

Cerus Industrial

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Industrial Automation industry

September 2008 – June 2010 (1 year 10 months) Hillsboro, OR

As the marketing coordinator and sole graphic designer at Cerus I played a key role in all marketing efforts including publication design for multiple catalogs, website edits and updates, email campaigns, flash landing pages for sales campaigns, product photography, photo editing, and print production management. I also coordinated press packages for new product releases, created custom submittal forms in Adobe Livecycle, vector illustration for advertisements and other collateral.
My experience at Cerus gave me further invaluable training working with the entire Adobe Creative Suite and gave me the opportunity to develop a seamless branded look and feel appropriate for a major corporation.

Freelance Design

Portland Organoponico Project

November 2008 – January 2009 (3 months) Portland, Oregon Area

POP is a local, farming cooperative bringing the Organoponico farming traditions to our neighborhoods. Dan Bravin, the owner & creator, needed a unique identity and a few simple collateral pieces to get started. The package I put together for him included a final logo with multiple process phases, t-shirt designs, signage and a look and feel for collateral and a website.

Graphic Production Coordinator @ Publicity Center

Western Washington University

Educational Institution; 1001-5000 employees; Higher Education industry

September 2007 – June 2008 (10 months) Bellingham, Washington Area

As the production designer, I juggled tight deadlines producing graphic banners, posters and digital media campaigns for music shows and lectures coming to campus. I was the lead designer for all production activities and took on larger specialty projects including the Labyrinth Book Project + Art Show.
With the little downtime I had I decided to collaborate on additional revenue streams including greeting card sales, and creating one-of-a-kind graphics for several other Associated Student's Departments.
