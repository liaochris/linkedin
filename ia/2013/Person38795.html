Dustin J. Burnett, MS, RD's Summary

Dustin's skill set is diverse. Before attending college, he developed people skills by working in an array of industries: grocery stores, coffeehouses, restaurants, bakeries, a convenience store, a locksmith, and literally being a clown. These practical skills -- and hard work -- eventually carried him through college, graduate school, and a dietetic internship where he further gained experience in clinical research, lab work, subject recruitment, and producing nutrient-controlled meals. Not only did Dustin gain practical skills through hands-on experience, but he also developed intellectual aptitude through academic study.

Today, Dustin supervises the Metabolic Kitchen and Human Feeding Lab at the USDA Western Human Nutrition Research Center at UC Davis. He works closely with nutrition scientists to identify an efficient and cost-effective means of implementing behavior change and/or testing diet interventions. He uses his culinary skills to transform dietary vehicles into foods that study participants will eat while also maintaining rigid control of nutrient composition. Dustin also prepares homogenates of foods and study menus to validate nutrient calculations through biochemical assays. He assists in writing the dietary components for publishing manuscripts. He also mentors undergraduate students and dietetic interns by offering volunteer opportunities through his unit.

Specialties: In addition to the Microsoft Office programs -- Access, Excel, Outlook, PowerPoint, Publisher, and Word -- Dustin uses NDSR, ProNutra, and ASA24 software programs for designing nutrient-controlled meals, determining production needs, and performing diet assessments. Dustin also applies linear programming when formulating placebos and other dietary vectors. He has elementary experience with InVivo software.

Dustin mentors undergraduate students and dietetic interns who volunteer under his supervision (over 15,000 logged hours since 2009).
