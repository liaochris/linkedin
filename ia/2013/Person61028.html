Jeff Loehr's Projects

  • Investing in Africa

    • November 2009 to Present
    Team Members: Jeff Loehr

    One challenge with expanding business in developing nations is to understand and value the risk of this investment. The risk is often buried in a discount rate calculation. This has the effect of making risky projects more difficult to approve but does not help the organization understand or value individual risks. Adjusting discount rates does not help with mitigating risks - a project with a risk, even a sovereign risk, that you can manage but your competitors cannot is a mis-priced opportunity for you. We developed methods for thinking through these sovereign risk challenges.

  • Creating a Shared Services Organization

    • January 2008 to Present
    Team Members: Jeff Loehr

    Large managing companies must share services - this is critical to realizing the benefit of a corporate manager. Shared services removes the duplication in individual operation, can provide better service and allows the operating organization to focus on operations. We implemented shared services in South Africa - both realizing benefits and uncovering challenges.

    Technology today allows more services to be centralized, even if the individuals working together are not in the same place. But making this work requires clear direction, a definitive mandate and tacit support from senior management. Without this, creating shared services just adds cost and complexity to the organization.

  • Strategy for Gold Miner

    • July 2009 to July 2010
    Team Members: Jeff Loehr

    Developed a strategy for an African Gold company that included redesigning the regional management structure and a plan for growth. Growth options focused on developing innovation initiatives, initiating M&A transactions and driving operational improvement. We developed new models for understanding performance and initiated a number of value adding processes including an acquisition that added hundreds of millions of dollars in value.

  • Innovation for new technology

    Team Members: Jeff Loehr, Roby Stancel

    Anglo Gold Ashanti faced serious challenges with their deep underground mines and we worked with them to develop completely new methods of mining as well as the technology to make these methods a reality. This involved creating a consortium of supplier companies and large scale open innovation workshops that broke the mould of the current approach to create something entirely new.

  • Energy Scenarios and Strategy

    • May 2009 to October 2009
    Team Members: Jeff Loehr

    A company in the energy industry in the US faced a large challenge in terms of changing fuel sources, competing products and developing long term strategies for the future. We developed long term energy scenarios to help frame up the future and developed scenario dependent strategies for growth. In order to explore how a transition from one fuel source to another might develop we created an agent based simulation model. We also quantified the value of options through real option analysis, adjusting valuations based on scenario outcomes.
