Jim Boxmeyer's Summary

James William Boxmeyer of Flemington, N.J., formerly of Lake Hiawatha, N.J., died following a brief illness, surrounded by his loving family and friends, on July 27, 2013. A memorial Mass was celebrated in St. Timothy's Church, 650 Nichols St., Norwood, Mass., on Friday morning, Aug. 2, at 10 a.m. Relatives and friends were invited to attend visiting hours on Tuesday, July 30, 2013, from 4 to 8 p.m. in the Holcombe-Fisher Funeral Home, 147 Main St., Flemington, N.J. Interment will be private. Please visit www.holcombefisher.com for further information or to send condolences. As the director of cyber intelligence and incident response for AT&T, Jim lead the AT&T Cyber Intelligence, Advanced Security Research, and Tier 3 Incident Response team of senior security experts who gather, correlate, and analyze cyber intelligence related to cyber-attacks. This team also acts as the tier three subject matter experts for critical incident response events for AT&T, commercial customers, and government agencies. Jim attended Sussex County schools and later earned a bachelor's degree in information technology specializing in information security from the University of Phoenix. Through the course of his 21-year career at AT&T, he was awarded several technology patents related to his work. Jim was sustained through his illness by his family and friends who will dearly miss him and who know that they are forever blessed for his presence in their lives. Jim was the devoted and loving son of Frances and Jeffry Raynes of Flemington, and William 'David' and Connie Boxmeyer of Beaufort, N.C., and devoted brother to David Michael Boxmeyer and his wife, Kate, and extra special uncle to William, Abigail and Owen Boxmeyer of Medfield, Mass.; his sister, Barbara and Michael Williams, and nephews, John Michael and Alex, and his brother, William David Boxmeyer Jr., all of Southern Shores, N.C. In lieu of flowers, memorial contributions may be made to the at donate.cancer.org.

Jim Boxmeyer's Patents

  • Method and apparatus for recursively analyzing log file data in a network

    • United States Patent 7,451,145
    • Issued December 13, 2005
    Inventors: Jim Boxmeyer

    Method and apparatus for processing log data produced by a network is described. In one example, entries in the log data are filtered using a plurality of filters to select first entries from the entries. The first entries are filtered using a plurality of false positive filters associated with the plurality of filters to select second entries from the first entries. Unique IP addresses are identified in the second entries. The entries in the log data are then filtered using the unique IP addresses to select third set entries. The third entries are analyzed to detect one or more patterns.

  • Method and apparatus for generating a reconnaissance index

    • United States Patent 7,590,113
    • Issued December 29, 2005
    Inventors: Jim Boxmeyer, Dan Hurley, Brian Rexroad, Michael Singer

    A method and apparatus for generating a reconnaissance index for a communications network is described. In one embodiment, scan detection data is initially received. A number of scan flows and a number of unique source Internet protocol (SIP) addresses initiating said number of scan flows is then identified from said scan detection data. Afterwards, a reconnaissance index is generated by utilizing said number of scan flows and said number of unique SIP addresses.

  • Security information repository system and method thereof

    • United States Patent 8,055,682
    • Issued June 30, 2006
    Inventors: Jim Boxmeyer, David Gross

    A method and system for maintaining a self-updating security information repository. A plurality of types of security information are stored in a corresponding plurality of information storage units, such as database tables. A plurality of configuration files respectively associated with the information storage units are stored, and each configuration file includes update parameters of the associated information storage unit. Each information storage unit is periodically automatically updated based on its update parameters. The information storage units can be searched to return security information of different types in one search.
