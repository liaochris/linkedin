Dan Griffin's Summary

I am the author of A Man's Way through the Twelve Steps (Hazelden) and Helping Men Recover (Wiley) - the first trauma-informed resources written specifically for men. II am nationally recognized as an expert on men's issues, particularly in helping addiction and mental health professionals more effectively engage men in services and deal with men's trauma. I am also an expert on criminal justice/drug courts, and the culture of recovery. I am a consultant and trainer focused on providing services for organizations, individuals, and couples. I do retreats for men in recovery and couples in recovery. I will custom design retreats for your organization. I write a blog for Addiction Professional magazine and am a regular contributor to The Good Men Project.

Specialties

Training small and large groups on various topic in addiction and recovery, public speaking, team development, recovery retreats, systems change, tilting at windmills, challenging status quo and established dogma and doctrine, and bringing humor to all occasions.

Dan Griffin's Publications

  • A Man's Way through the Twelve Steps

    • Hazelden
    • October 2009
    Authors: Dan Griffin

    Using interviews with men in various stages of recovery, excerpts from relevant Twelve Steps, and his own experience, Griffin offers the first holistic approach to sobriety for men. A Man’s Way through the Twelve Steps offers practical advice and inspiration for men to define their own sense of masculinity and thus heighten their potential for a lifetime of sobriety.

  • Helping Men Recover

    • Jossey Bass/ Wiley & Sons
    • January 2011
    Authors: Dan Griffin, Dr. Stephanie Covington, Rick Dauer

    Helping Men Recover is the first gender-responsive, trauma-informed treatment program for men. The materials, based on the widely used, evidence-based women’s curriculum, Helping Women Recover, are grounded in research, theory, and clinical practice.
