  • Last Chance for Parents of High School Juniors to Shape Base Year Financials for College Planning

    • Motley Fool
    • December 2012
    Authors: Diane Bucka

    Just as important as year-end tax planning – and, admittedly, just as distasteful – is the need to look ahead and put your college funding ducks in a row.

    Unlike tax planning, however, the opportunities presented – but missed – this year will not appear again next year.

    Parents of current college-bound high school juniors need to know that this year is the base year that informs college financial aid applications. The base year refers to the tax year prior to the academic year for which aid is requested. For example, if the student matriculates in September 2014, the base year is from January 1 through December 31, 2013. It is among the most determinative time to enhance your position for financial aid consideration, and there are some concrete steps to consider.
