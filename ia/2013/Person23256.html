Chris Ennis' Summary

Chris is an engineer and entrepreneur who has built a wide array of successful web and mobile products. He has a passion to improve lives daily through products that connect people to support and services. His recent work at SoberCircle and RxAssurance are prime examples of this mantra.

Chris's first product, inventory management software, was acquired by a major logistics company in 1998. He is a pioneer in web-based healthcare solutions, building the first purely web-based electronic health record (EHR) management system for behavioral healthcare in 2002. Chris was the co-founder of SoberCircle, one of the earliest social networks for addicts and alcoholics. SoberCircle was acquired in 2009 and lives on today in the form of OneHealth. He built one of the first localized Twitter aggregators, NashMash, which aggregated tweets for users located in the Nashville, Tennessee area.

Chris grew up in Tennessee where he spent summers working on a farm, building a strong work ethic and an appreciation for agriculture entrepreneurship. He currently lives in Denver with his wife Jessica and two sons, Reed and Dean.
