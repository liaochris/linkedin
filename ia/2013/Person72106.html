Principal Device Interaction Designer

Jawbone

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Consumer Electronics industry

September 2010 – June 2013 (2 years 10 months)

First as the sole Device Interaction Designer, and then as manager of a small team, I was responsible for designing usable and intuitive audio and physical experiences on Jawbone devices and companion software. This included:
- Working with the Product team to define products and features
- Designing interactive, physical and audio experiences across Jawbone devices
- Collaborating with engineers to create seamless hardware and software experiences

Senior User Experience Designer/UX Strategist

Microsoft/Tellme Networks

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; MSFT; Computer Software industry

January 2005 – August 2010 (5 years 8 months)

At Tellme then Microsoft, I led complex voice and multi-modal design efforts from inception to deployment. This included up-front user research, brainstorming, experience prototyping, usability testing and translating design and technical requirements into design briefs and detailed specifications. My focus spanned creating audio-only, speech recognition experiences to multi-modal (audio+visual) experiences.

▪ Led a team of UX designers to design and integrate Tellme’s consumer applications into “the steering wheel” for Ford Sync's Traffic, Directions and nformation offering.
▪ Lead designer for Tellme for Windows Mobile 6.5 – a multi-modal consumer product implementing Voice-Activated Dialing, Voice-to-SMS, and Web Search - the precursor to the speech experience on Windows Phones.
▪ Lead design consultant for large enterprise clients to define their multi-channel strategic visions for next generation contact centers, Natural Language, enterprise
mobile applications, and channel-convergence.
