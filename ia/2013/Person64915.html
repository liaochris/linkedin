Joanne Cantor, Ph.D.'s Summary

I am an expert on the psychology of media and communications.

My Evaluation Research helps organizations develop programs that demonstrably achieve their educational goals and change attitudes in positive directions.
My Academic Research on cognitive and affective responses to media advances theory, while having immediate practical applications as well.
My Presentations to corporations, professionals, and students help people get more done, boost their creativity, and reduce stress and burnout.

I was a professor at the University of Wisconsin for 26 years (now Professor Emerita) and am the author of three books and more than 90 scholarly articles and chapters.

I have testified on numerous occasions before US Congressional committees as well as the Federal Communications Commission. I'm frequently cited in the national press (most recently The New York Times, Chicago Tribune, and Newsweek) and have been a guest on numerous radio and television shows including "Oprah," "Good Morning America," and "The Daily Rundown" with Chuck Todd and Savannah Guthrie.

I am Outreach Director of the Center for Communication Research at the University of Wisconsin–Madison, and my consulting firm maintains an active program of evaluation research for educational projects.

I'm a member of the Authors Guild, the National Speakers Association, Madison Area Business Consultants, the American Psychological Association, the Association for Psychological Science, the International Communication Association, and the National Communication Association.

Specialties: child development, child learning, child emotion and motivation, STEM learning, productivity, creativity, stress reduction, time management, educational achievement, multitasking, information overload, attention, brain exercises, adjustment to college, getting things done, cyberoverload, facebook, twitter, email, declutter, priorities, college success, taking breaks, strategic sleeping, burnout prevention
