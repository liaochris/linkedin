Katharine O'Moore-Klopf, ELS's Experience

Owner

KOK Edit

January 1995 – Present (18 years 10 months) East Setauket, NY

As a copyeditor, I make good books and good journal articles great. I get rid of inconsistencies, point out errors of fact, eliminate wordiness, smooth transitions between topics, and correct grammar and misspellings. You need a passionate advocate to make your book or journal manuscript the best it can be. I am that advocate. If you are not a native speaker of English, I will work with you to remove the language barrier that can keep book publishers and biomedical journals from considering your manuscript. I specialize in editing biomedicine manuscripts written by ESL (English as a second language) and EFL (English as a foreign language) authors.

    member, Social Networking Subcommittee
    • 2006 to Present

    CSE was established in 1957 as the Council of Biology Editors. Renamed the Council of Science Editors in 2000, it serves editorial professionals in the sciences by creating a supportive network for career development, providing educational opportunities, and developing resources for identifying and implementing high-quality editorial practices.

    http://www.councilscienceeditors.org

  • World Association of Medical Editors (WAME)

    associate member
    • 2006 to Present

    Established in 1995, WAME is a nonprofit voluntary association of editors of peer-reviewed medical journals from countries throughout the world who seek to foster international cooperation among and education of medical journal editors. Membership in WAME is free and all decision-making editors of peer-reviewed medical journals are eligible to join. Membership is also available to selected scholars in journal editorial policy and peer review.

    http://www.wame.org

  • American Copy Editors Society

    member
    • December 2012 to Present

    The American Copy Editors Society is a professional organization working toward the advancement of editors. Its aim is to provide opportunities through training, discussion and advocacy that promote the editing profession.

Katharine O'Moore-Klopf, ELS's Publications

  • Taking Charge: Your Education, Your Career, Your Life (2nd edition)

    • TSTC Publishing
    • 2010
    Authors: Katharine O'Moore-Klopf, ELS, Karen Mitchell Smith

    Typically, college success textbooks focus on traditional higher education academic skills: note taking, test strategies, and research. However, in response to the increasing trend--especially at two-year colleges--of students immediately entering the workforce upon graduation, Taking Charge directly contextualizes these academic skills with the soft skills necessary to succeed in the workplace. In addition, all the subject areas covered in the book are related to personal growth so that students may see that these skills are not used in isolation but are, rather, an integral part of the ongoing development everyone experiences in his or her life.

    In this edition, customized for use specifically at TSTC Waco, features in Taking Charge: Your Education, Your Career, Your Life include personal, educational, and career applications relating to each chapter's subject; chapters focusing on goal setting, time management, financial planning, critical thinking, research, written and interpersonal communications, the diverse and non-hostile work environment, and much more; profiles at the beginning of each chapter of TSTC students, graduates, faculty, and staff; and additional suggested resources, as well as individual and group exercises designed to increase student comprehension and internalization of concepts presented.

    More information about Taking Charge may be found at its companion web site: http://tstcpubtakingcharge.wordpress.com

  • Getting Started as a Freelance Copyeditor

    • KOK Edit
    • 2008
    Authors: Katharine O'Moore-Klopf, ELS

    Want to become a freelance copyeditor? Maybe you’re ready to leave the corporate world or you’d like to have the flexibility that working for yourself can offer. How do you market yourself to establish and build your business? Do you know what skills you need, and what resources are available for you to develop those skills? What computer resources will you need? And what about money--how do you charge for your work, budget for the slow periods, and deal with invoicing and contracts? Getting Started as a Freelance Copyeditor, a 16-page onscreen booklet from KOK Edit, will tell you what you need to know. It’s expertly written by Katharine O’Moore-Klopf, ELS, who has been working in publishing for almost three decades, spending most of the last two decades as a freelance copyeditor.
