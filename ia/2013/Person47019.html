Garth Schmalenberg's Summary

An international consultant (MA in Human Security and Peacebuilding) my most recent ventures include post conflict Uganda, where I worked for 6 months with several CSOs (CARITAS Gulu, Gulu District NGO Forum and the National NGO Forum of Uganda) developing proposals, processes, policies, systems and strategies while researching partnership and governance, measurement and evaluation, accountability and development effectiveness, and meetings in Chile with actors in an ongoing conflict related to land ownership between the government, forestry industry and the Mapuche people.

Formerly an IT Executive, Executive Coach and IT Specialist, I combine an array of skills and focus on learning from the bigger picture.

My search is for opportunities to contribute my skills to global development.

While seeking consulting opportunities, I continue my work as Executive Director of Partners for Prosperity assisting the development of community projects which encourage learning through action and work with the Board of Faith and the Common Good, I also hope to promote education and action related to the advancement of sustainable development through faith communities.

Specialties: Leadership, Human Security and Peacebuilding, Sustainability, Cultural development, Capacity building, Measurement and Evaluation, Resilience, Influence, Communication, IT, Coaching

Garth Schmalenberg's Education

Royal Roads University

MA, Human Security and Peacebuilding

2011 – 2013

Human Security involves securing human rights, ensuring physical security, food security, access to health care and education, and in the broadest definition, development for everyone.

It focuses on understanding the interactions between economics, national and international politics and policies, globalization and the impact of multinational companies.

It investigates issues related to sustainability, including the distribution of energy and resources, and researches the strengths and weaknesses of security approaches which utilize police, national security, the UN Security Council, NATO and the various judicial forces including the ICC and national courts which seek to enable the rule of law (in its many definitions) and to establish order and peace.

It focuses on the work of international, national and local Government agencies and ministries as well as Civil Society Organizations (CSOs) and their effectiveness as they strive to improve the situation of people in all nations.
