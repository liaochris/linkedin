Daniel G. Gensler, MS, CFP®, RLP®'s Summary

Dan has extensive experience advising individuals, families and corporations alike. He has instructed the Certified Financial Planner curriculum and earned recognition as a Five Star Best in Client Satisfaction Wealth Manager for 2007, as seen in San Diego magazine (To learn more about the selection criteria visit www.fivestarwealthmanager.com/wmresearch). He has also been recognized as one of the top 100 advisors in the US by Registered Rep magazine in 2008 and 2009 based on assets under management. Dan's goal is to help his clients achieve their dreams and be able to do those things in life that they are passionate about.

Dan has a proven track record of delivering sophisticated financial solutions designed to nurture the core life objectives of his clients: “My greatest reward is helping clients fulfill their dreams and live their best life."

The Gensler Group

December 1995 – Present (17 years 11 months)

Daniel G. Gensler entered the financial planning industry in 1984 with a vision of providing comprehensive financial planning to improve the lives of his clients. Dan’s ability to combine compassion with financial expertise has been the foundation of his success ever
since. In 1995, Dan founded The Gensler Group, a thriving financial planning and wealth advisory firm with over $125 million in brokerage and advisory assets (as of July 31, 2011).

At the Gensler Group, client service is our highest priority. Every point of contact with a client is a reflection on our company and therefore is handled with integrity, knowledge and attention to detail. Our commitment to outstanding client service is at the center of our unique financial planning and investment management processes. We strive to help you merge the money with the meaning in your life, and sincerely believe you deserve a financial and life planning partner to get you where you want to be, now and in the future.

Our investment philosophy at The Gensler Group is grounded in our belief in efficient markets. Some investment managers practice “active investment,” trying to time – and beat – the market. While this may seem like a reasonable, proactive approach, too often it results in costly mistakes. At The Gensler Group, our investment strategy is based on a efficient investing approach that is based on exhaustive financial research and science. We take advantage of the way markets naturally behave. We define potentially profitable asset classes, diversify investments across those asset classes and strategically rebalance client portfolios as necessary. No strategy assures success or guarantees against loss.
