Ken Cheney's Summary

Kenneth C. Cheney is an associate in the law firm of McDermott Will & Emery LLP and is based in the Firm’s Orange County office. He focuses his practice on intellectual property related matters.

Prior to joining McDermott, Ken worked in an intellectual property boutique law firm specializing in IP litigation and transactional matters. His transactional experience includes preparation and prosecution of patent applications in the areas of computer hardware and software, internet systems and processes, integrated circuits, and medical devices and methods; opinions regarding infringement analysis, patent validity, and freedom to operate. His litigation experience has involved patent and trademark infringement, trade secrets, fraud, and breach of contract.

Ken received his J.D. from Loyola Law School, Los Angeles, and his B.S. in computer engineering from the University of California, Irvine. While in law school, he was a Senior Production Editor of the Loyola International & Comparative Law Review and a moot court competition semi-finalist.

Prior to attending law school, Ken worked as a IT project manager and software engineer, and has developed broad expertise in software and computer architecture, as well as a deep understanding of the technical goals and business thinking behind software and computer engineering-related technologies. His programming expertise includes the development of software applications using C++, Java/J2EE, C#, ASP.NET, XML, HTML, SOAP, BASIC, and 8086 Assembly.

Ken has authored several articles on intellectual property related issues, including Patentability of Stem Cell Research Under TRIPS; Patenting Virtual Worlds; and, Computer Games and Virtual Worlds: A New Frontier in Intellectual Property Law (contributing author), published by the ABA IP Section in 2010.

Ken is a member of the California State Bar and is registered to practice before the U.S. Patent and Trademark Office.
