Maurice Levin's Summary

At Jurassic Garden, we grow, propagate and sell rare plants called cycads. Cycads are living fossils, sculptural plants that are drought-tolerant and when someone puts them in their garden, they are preserving an endangered species. We sell these plants to home gardeners, rare plant enthusiasts, landscapers and retail nurseries.

If we can get enough of these living fossil sculptural plants into peoples' gardens, we will all preserve these endangered species for future generations, and reduce and eliminate poaching of them from their habitats in the wild.

Specialties

Landscape Plants, Rare Plants, Endangered Species Preservation

Maurice Levin's Publications

  • Growing Cycads in Field and Garden

    • Cycad Society Newsletter
    • September 30, 2006
    Authors: Maurice Levin

    This article, by Maurice Levin, will help you grow healthy cycads. It is written for part-time home gardeners as well as plant enthusiasts and landscapers. You will learn how to:

    • Transplant and grow mature cycads and cycad seedlings successfully.
    • Prepare your site for a cycad.
    • Prepare your cycad for planting.
    • Provide necessary drainage.

  • "Encephalartos horridus, Eastern Cape Blue Cycad"

    • Cycad Society Newsletter
    • March 31, 2008
    Authors: Maurice Levin, Lara Loewenstein

    Encephalartos horridus is the classic South African blue cycad. Its look is absolutely distinct among all cycads, combining an incredibly blue color with unusual three-dimensional convex leaflets unique in the cycad world.

  • Encephalartos turneri -- Turner's Cycad

    • Cycad Society Newsletter
    • March 1, 2007
    Authors: Maurice Levin

    For years, the distinct leaves and relative inaccessibility of Encephalartos turneri have made it a true collector's item among cycad afficionados. Located in hard-to-access habitats in previously war-torn Mozambique, Turner's Cycad only became well-known in gardens in the last ten years, since political stability returned to Mozambique.
