Aireen Arellano's Summary

Aerial Ardour, LLC was founded by Aireen Arellano. Some of Aireen’s favorite things include design, deadlines and very happy clients.

Aireen has seven years of professional experience in art direction, print, web design, rich media, packaging, illustration and social media branding and has used these skills to develop an expertise in the beverage and hospitality industries.

She dove into the art world headfirst by studying Fine Arts at The School of the Art Institute of Chicago before attending Northern Illinois University to study Illustration. It was there where Aireen found her true talent in creative branding, so she transferred to Columbia College Chicago and earned a Bachelor of Fine Arts degree in Advertising Art Direction.

Aireen creates smart, visual branding and just plain cool design. But it’s about the big picture – not just making a pretty one.

Aireen is a quick, personable and dependable gal with a compulsion for detail, and she exemplifies all these attributes through her company, which demonstrates her Passion on Paper and Pixels™.

The Hum Spirits Company

2008 – 2010 (2 years) Chicago

There's nothing like seeing it. Watch the video here: http://www.monvera.com/twbb-hum-botanical-spirits/

I designed the bottle and packaging for hum botanical spirit, the brainchild of world renowned mixologists Adam Seger of Chicago and Joe McCanta of London. Another designer created hum's trademark logo, and I took the project to completion.

Designing a bottle for a spirit requires a unique and experienced set of skills. Designing hum's bottle wasn't my first bottle-design rodeo, so I remained keenly aware of the process.

One must be aware of legal implications of every minute design element right down to the height of the tiniest font and the placement of the legal statement. One must also consider the label printer's capabilities and needs.

The process becomes a team effort between the client, the label printer, and me, with the legal and monetary consequence of every design element on the bottle being taken into account.

hum is now world renowned and widely featured in Oprah's finale star-studded after party, bon appétit magazine, and received 5 stars in F. Paul Pacult's Spirit Journal being described as, "Like nothing out there and lap-up luscious."

front desk receptionist

Alison Andrews Day Spa

October 2003 – September 2010 (7 years)

I greeted clients and made them feel welcome at the spa, kept client files in check, confirmed appointments, sold product, backed up data, handled POP with clients, and managed inventory.

Here, I learned the front desk becomes the face and first impression of a company, making it imperative to maintain a balance of warmth and professionalism. The front desk also is the last touch point for a client, making it the responsibility of the receptionist to encourage the client to return.

I also learned additional nuances of time management, not only for myself but for the rest of the staff. It was my responsibility to ensure estheticians kept within the allotted time for each client.

This position taught me to get the work done, all with a sincere smile.

art director

Rain

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Marketing and Advertising industry

September 2005 – May 2009 (3 years 9 months) Chicago

Rain threw me into the fire, so to speak. At my point of hiring, I was a green designer with little direct client contact and campaign design and pitching experience.

All within a short number of years, I grew tremendously here.

Working with a highly talented staff of other art directors, motion graphic artists, copywriters, and designers, I grew as an artist and solidified my feet in the profession.

I learned the nuances of balancing projects and appeasing clients and creating pitches. With my teammates, I reveled in the triumph of winning business and learned the tough lessons that came with losses.

At Rain, I was able to forge relationships that have effectively changed the course of my life, personally and professionally, for the better.
