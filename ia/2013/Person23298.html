Chris Gattis' Summary

With 30 years experience in branding, marketing strategies, corporate finance, site selection, business coaching, business start-up, crisis management, turnarounds, writing business plans and managing budgets, I understand what it takes to run a successful business. I bring that knowledge and experience to work for my clients to help them understand how to achieve financial success while never losing focus on their customer.

Ad4! is a branding, marketing strategies and communication services boutique. We work with clients looking to update their brand, more effectively communicate their brand promise, create a comprehensive marketing strategy and communication plan, or want to improve their market visibility and presence. Chris Gattis' Publications

  • Business Start-up 101: From Great Idea to Profit...Quick!

    • Blue Point Publishers
    • September 13, 2010
    Authors: Chris Gattis

    An Amazon Best Seller! You don’t need an Ivy League MBA to understand how to successfully start a new business; you just need to know the rules. Chris identifies the rules and explains how to use the tools that help insure success. Business Start-up 101 is a how-to guide for entrepreneurs.

    This guide helps potential owners understand the world of business. The steps allow an owner to fine –tune their plans to insure a profitable business.

    The book walks potential business owners through a five-stage process of starting a business from exploring entrepreneurship, managing a small business, conducting business planning and financial analysis to finally writing a business plan.
    1. In part one, potential owners learn about how their personal financial situation and family affect the business start-up process.
    2. In part two, they learn about small business management including accounting and management systems, legal entities, intellectual property and how systems will help them run their business more efficiently.
    3. Part three teaches how to use market research, competition and customer analysis to develop a marketing plan to promote the business.
    4. The likely success of the business model is tested in part four as Chris shows entrepreneurs how to use the Three Tools of Financial ViabilityTM to determine if their plans will result in success.
    5. Part five discusses the business plan and how entrepreneurs can use different business plan types to satisfy the needs of their banker, investors or management team.
