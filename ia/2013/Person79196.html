Laurie Boettcher's Summary

With years of design and communication experience, I teach, speak, post, and tweet on using social media. As an educator, I have fallen in love with the mission of Career and Technical Education and advocate its concepts through my teaching methods. My students (of all ages) experience social media and marketing through active practical application that exposes them to a variety of technologies. They leave ready to contribute knowledge, embrace new ideas, experiment with a variety of methods, and flow with change.

A frequent speaker at workshops, conferences, and private events throughout the US and Canada, I have been featured in US News & World Report, The New York Times, and numerous other media. I work primarily with municipalities, government agencies, higher education, public libraries, and groups/ associations because I believe you pave the way in educating our communities, workforce, and future generations. My experience and fascination with the use of social media in emergency management and crisis communications, has lead me to conduct research and be involved in that arena as well.

My approach, whether in teaching or speaking, is practical and realistic - if you don't understand and are overwhelmed, you will get frustrated and not use it. I want people to 'get' how it applies to them and their organizations.

I hold a MS in Communication from Northwestern University and BA in Mass Communication-Public Relations from the University of Wisconsin-Eau Claire. In the near future, I hope to pursue an EdD in Learning Technologies.

Specialties:
• Practical application of social media
• Addressing digital technology skill gap
• Speaker/keynote/breakout/trainer/panelist
• Social media for governments
• Social media for public libraries
• Social media in human resources
• Social media privacy protection - personal and professional
