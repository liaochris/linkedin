Thomas Harden's Summary

Thomas Harden's Experience

Chancellor

University of Wisconsin-Green Bay

Educational Institution; 501-1000 employees; Higher Education industry

June 2009 – Present (4 years 7 months) Green Bay, WI

Presided over a surge in enrollment of returning adult students and an increase in the number of students of color — two areas targeted for growth as the institution of approximately 6,600 students seeks to better serve its region.

Achieved a major strategic goal in spring 2011 with creation of the UW‑Green Bay Foundation, Inc., which provides new flexibilities and more appropriate control not only of gifts and investments but also real estate, research and service grants.

Managed steep state budget cuts to maintain current enrollment and services while avoiding faculty and staff layoffs. Anticipating possible reductions, the University held a portion of tuition revenue from a record 2009-10 enrollment in reserve. Ongoing cost cutting in travel, purchasing and review of open positions assumed additional strategic importance.

Initiated a universitywide strategic planning process to guide institutional priorities over the next decade

Assembled a new leadership team.

Accepted the University’s largest single gift for academics with a $5.5 million contribution from Dr. David A. Cofrin

Reached out to UW‑Green Bay employees and solicited suggestions for low-cost and no-cost “Quick Fix” initiatives — simple but solid ideas for improving life on campus and services to students.

President

Clayton State University

Educational Institution; 501-1000 employees; Higher Education industry

June 2000 – May 2009 (9 years)

Served as chief executive officer, chair of the faculty, professor, and administrative head of the University. Worked with a large number and wide range of internal and external constituencies and provide overall leadership and direction for the institution. Promoted improvement, educational experience, fiscal and physical development, and general welfare of the University. Developed partnerships and alliances, generate external funding, and allocate resources to support priorities.

Oversaw and directed the institution’s planning, including strategic positioning, setting priorities, budget planning and completion of the annual financial plan. Articulated the University’s vision and mission to ensure its development as an outstanding comprehensive metropolitan university.

Established and provided a positive, productive, and collaborative climate. Created trust between the administration and all constituents. Formed meaningful, supportive alliances with governmental, civic, educational and other partners.

Provided an example of working for the benefit of students and making decisions based on the impact on student learning and student welfare.

As chair of the faculty, maintained an active leadership role in the development of academic programs, policies, and faculty development.

Provided oversight of seven major divisions of the University; five vice-presidents, and two executive directors.

Maintain active support for Clayton State University’s Spivey Hall, which is widely recognized as one of the world’s most outstanding performance halls and concert schedules, specializing in classical music, jazz, and world music.

Provide active oversight of CSU’s successful NCAA II intercollegiate athletics program. Participate as voting delegate to the NCAA. Current member of the NCAA Division II Presidents Council. Member of the Board of Directors and part president of the Peachbelt Conference, an eleven university conference encompassing three states.

Interim Dean of the College of Business

Eastern Michigan University

Educational Institution; 1001-5000 employees; Higher Education industry

January 1999 – June 2000 (1 year 6 months)

Served concurrently in this capacity after the resignation of the dean. The College of Business included four departments: Accounting, Finance/Computer Information Systems, Management, and Marketing. Oversaw the successful re-accreditation by the Association to Advance Collegiate Schools of Business International (AACSB).

Dean of the College of Technology and Professor

Eastern Michigan University

Educational Institution; 1001-5000 employees; Higher Education industry

June 1993 – June 2000 (7 years 1 month)

Provided leadership in planning, budgeting, and delivery of all college programs. Specific responsibilities included evaluation and development of innovative and traditional academic programs, recruitment and retention of faculty and staff, support and enhancement of faculty research and creative endeavors, expansion of faculty development activities related to teaching, evaluation of faculty and staff, development and support of new program delivery methods, and resource management and development. Developed the Ph.D. in Technology, EMU’s third doctoral program.

Chairperson of the Department of Technology

Northern Kentucky University

Educational Institution; 1001-5000 employees; Higher Education industry

1985 – 1993 (8 years)

Responsible for degree programs in engineering technology, industrial technology, office systems technology, industrial and labor relations, industrial supervision, electronics technology, business education, office administration, construction technology, office systems technology, industrial education, and manufacturing technology. Established Technical Services Institute, obtained numerous industry donations, provided special training programs for numerous companies. Account manager for several budgets, and responsible for budget development each fiscal year. Liaison to local, state, and regional agencies and associations. Associate Professor 1985-1990, Professor 1990-1993.

Acting Dean of University College

Northern Kentucky University

Educational Institution; 1001-5000 employees; Higher Education industry

1989 – 1990 (1 year)

Responsible for all functions of the College including budget, personnel, facility management, and program administration. University College included the (Homemaker) ReEntry Center, the Retraining Center, the ReEmployment Center, portfolio program, telecourses, off-campus course coordination, Community Education, Governmental Services, Professional Development, and Continuing Education. Overall management of the University College campus in Covington. Approximately 1000 students attended University College campus in credit courses. A large number of community and business/industry personnel took advantage of the non-credit courses, seminars and workshops, which were offered through the Office of Community Education and Service. At the request of the VPAA/Provost, I accepted this one year assignment during re-organization with the understanding that I would be permitted to return to my regular position as chair and professor. I did not apply for the position of Dean of University College.

Associate Dean of the College of Professional Studies

Northern Kentucky University

Educational Institution; 1001-5000 employees; Higher Education industry

1988 – 1989 (1 year)

Responsibilities included resolution of all student related problems at the college level, assisting with budget development, preparing management reports and position papers for the college, assisting the chairpersons with problem resolution. Oversaw summer school operation, oversaw preparation of class schedules and catalog copy. Served in the absence of the Dean. The departments in the College of Professional Studies were 1) Allied Health, Human Services, and Social Work, 2) Communications, 3) Education, 4) Military Science, 5) Nursing, and 6) Technology. I left the position when I was asked to serve as Acting Dean of University College.
