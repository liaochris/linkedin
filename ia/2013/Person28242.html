Phil Cullen's Summary

Phil helped kick start community broadcasting in Australia, set out to revitalise the Australian Broadcasting Corporation and once walked for a living.

He is a journalist, communications, media manager & producer who worked as Development Coordinator for ABC Radio, Manager ABC Coast FM, Manager 612 ABC Brisbane, Manager 105.7 ABC Darwin, Manager 774 ABC Melbourne and General Manager TripleJ. He was Program Coordinator 4ZZZ & 2ARM-FM.

He helped develop ABC Radio's early web presence, mentored the establishment of Speaking Out and Awaye and has several awards for program production in News, Community Affairs and Music.

Phil taught Broadcast and On Line journalism and Communications skills for Griffith University and University of Canberra, Radio Skills for the Australian Film Television and Radio School and was marketing manager, web master & a tour guide for interNATIONAL PARKtours. He also launched the inaugural website for Pacific Asia Tourism.

He works as a policy and program manager for the Department of Broadband, Communications and the Digital Economy, now on Indigenous Broadcasting and recently: drafting cyber safety and security policy content for a Digital Economy White paper; assisting with the development of media access legislation; developing case studies in the digital economy and helping frame Australia's digital economy strategy, through events such as the Realising Our Broadband Future forum at UNSW.

Phil has been elected or co-opted to board and community roles including industry training bodies, media, sports and community associations and selection panels for media, music and tourism awards and has been an active member of the rural fire service.

Specialities: Strategic development and positioning of service enterprises, including team engagement, deployment and development. Resource and facilities management. Media production and presentation. Communications and public relations. Research and writing. Negotiation, conciliation and liaison.
