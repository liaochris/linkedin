Enoch Perry's Summary

Hello! My name is Enoch Perry, born and raised in the heart of the Midwest, known as Fort Wayne, Indiana. It was in Fort Wayne where I discovered the determination that I want to be nothing less than the best in the field of broadcasting.

After 3 semesters at Vincennes University, I spent almost 7 years at Federated Media; 3 of those years were spent as THE producer of Fort Wayne's #1 morning radio program on the market's #1 radio station, NewsTalk 1190 WOWO Radio (from November 2005 through March 2009). From February 2009 through November 2009, I produced a live Saturday morning talk show as well as a live & local sports talk program on Saturdays for both WOWO and its sister station, WKJG ESPN Radio 1380. Throughout my tenure at FedMed, I had the opportunity to be a part of the cluster's Promotions department (prior to being a Board Operator and Producer) as well as published podcasts for both AM stations. My greatest experience so far was when I interviewed Tony Dungy in 2009.

In December 2009, I decided to make a change of scenery by leaving my family and countless friends in Fort Wayne for Albuquerque, New Mexico to seek better employment in radio. As of June 2011, I was hired by Citadel Broadcasting (prior to the acquisition by Cumulus Media) as a part-time Promotions Assistant and Board Operator.

What stands out about me, professionally, is my strong work ethic and my sheer determination to stand behind something that I truly believe in.

My current short-term goal is to continue re-establishing myself professionally in the radio marketplace. My long-term goal is to own my own business (relating to broadcast media, i.e. voice-over, radio production). I've come a long way since my days at Vincennes University and realize that I still have a ways to go. My goal is to stay determined & motivated. I'm open to network with other professionals and exchange ideas while I'm on this site; I can be contacted via email or phone.

Thank you for your time!

-Enoch
