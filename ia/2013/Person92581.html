Michael Burroughs' Summary

Author of "Before Onboarding: How to Integrate New Leaders for Quick and Sustained Results." ("Five Star" rating on Amazon/Kindle--Link is below in publications section)

Managing Director (St. Louis) & Executive Integration Practice Leader at Sheer Velocity, LLC, a Denver-based retained executive search firm, and as President of Burroughs International, LLC, an executive integration consulting practice, outplacement and interim executive placement service.

Integrate ("pre-board") executives that Sheer Velocity recruits. Executive Integration begins when an offer is accepted and concludes 90 days after the new leader's start date.

Fifteen years of retained executive recruiting experience, most with a "top five" global search firm, and includes placements from director to public company CEO in the US, Asia and Europe.

Consult with global organizations in designing enterprise-specific Executive Onboarding programs and deliver an onsite, two-day, case-study based workshop to HR management teams to prepare them to create and launch an Executive Onboarding program that is best suited for their organization.

Served as the Organization Development executive for divisions of three Fortune 500 companies.

Sought after keynote speaker and breakout session leader / panel member at professional conferences, speaking on the topic of New Leader Integration.

Board Certified Executive Coach. Executive career coach. Executive outplacement consultant.

Colonel, US Army (Ret.).

Adjunct professor in the MBA program at Lindenwood University.

Specialties: Executive Recruiting / Executive Integration & On-boarding / Executive Coaching / Leadership Development / Organization Development / Public Speaking

Michael Burroughs' Publications

  • Before Onboarding: How to Integrate New Leaders for Quick and Sustained Results

    • CreateSpace (Amazon)
    • July 5, 2011
    Authors: Michael Burroughs

    In this book the author discusses and outlines his proven New Leader Integration "pre-boarding" process that occurs before a new manager's start date. New leaders arrive on "Day 1" with a "Blueprint for Success" that equips them to do the right things quickly; avoid costly, preventable mistakes; and sustain their early wins long term. New Leader Integration accommodates both outside hires and internal promotions and is appropriate for managers at all levels.

  • Courtesy Interviews with Executive Recruiters

    • www.EzineArticles.com
    • March 7, 2011
    Authors: Michael Burroughs

    Most veteran executive recruiters are frequently asked to conduct courtesy interviews. Reasons vary for the rationale of these requests. In this article, a veteran executive recruiter outlines some more common reasons for these requests and how he typically responds to them. While he does not attempt to speak for all recruiters, he shares his own views regarding these common requests.

  • Why Take a Recruiter's Call If You Are Not In a Job Hunt

    • www.EzineArticles.com
    • February 20, 2011
    Authors: Michael Burroughs

    Chances are you have received more than your share of calls from a recruiter. The author, a veteran retained executive recruiter from a "top five" firm shares with you some reasons to take those calls and welcome them.

  • Good Candidates Don't Get Better With Age

    • www.EzineArticles.com
    • February 7, 2011
    Authors: Michael Burroughs

    This article is not about age discrimination in the executive recruiting process. Instead, it focuses on the risks organizations take in not moving swiftly on hiring candidates they want.

  • Getting Quick Results From the Executives You Recruit

    • www.EzineArticles.com
    • February 7, 2011
    Authors: Michael Burroughs

    In the VC/PE world executive changes abound. The firm's partners retain a search firm, hire a new executive, and the new "hard charging" leader is off and running. Studies show that the first 90 days will determine ultimate success or failure. Other studies suggest that 40 to 50% of outside hired executives will not succeed in their jobs. This is an expensive proposition. Everything should be done that can be done to ensure the executive makes the right decisions and gets desired results quickly away. The author is a veteran executive recruiter and coach who has developed a process for doing that called, New Leader Integration.

  • An Alternative to On-boarding Healthcare Executives

    • www.EzineArticles.com
    • February 6, 2011
    Authors: Michael Burroughs

    Many healthcare systems and hospitals have implemented on-boarding programs. Any structured process that occurs once a new leader is on-board is a step in the right direction. Most on-boarding programs begin on the start date and are primarily an administrative and orienting process. By the time a new leader arrives, many months have transpired in the search for that individual. The organization's need to see quick results is a given, yet few organizations have a process to ensure that the executives they recruit are fully prepared when they arrive to get the right results quickly. Research shows that what happens to a new executive in the first 90 days will often determine ultimate success or failure.

  • Disclosing Compensation History: What to Tell an Executive Recruiter

    • www.EzineArticles.com
    • February 7, 2011
    Authors: Michael Burroughs

    It's an old debate: Do I or don't I disclose my compensation history to an executive recruiter who calls? The author is a veteran executive recruiter who provides his perspective in this article.

  • Executive Search Status Reports: How to Get Them

    • www.EzineArticles.com
    • February 14, 2011
    Authors: Michael Burroughs

    If there is any single point that irritates candidates when working with executive recruiters, it is the issue of getting updates or status reports on their candidacy. It ranks high in the literature as a major issue. How best should you deal with this as a candidate? What is acceptable and what is not? The author is a veteran retained executive recruiter with both major international firm and boutique experience. He has placed executives from CEO to director level. In this article he guides you through a typical search process and advises you as to when you can expect to hear back from recruiters and what you should do if they have not contacted you again in a timely manner.
