Carrie Ching's Summary

I'm an award-winning independent journalist and producer based in Oakland, California. My new storytelling series, OFF THE RECORD, is in development and will launch soon with a new partnership! Journalists tell personal stories about the crazy s#@t that happens to them. Follow on Twitter @OTRstories.

I led digital storytelling projects at the Center for Investigative Reporting for six years as Senior Multimedia Producer. I pioneered and developed CIR's animated and graphic journalism initiatives, and directed and produced the online hits "The Price of Gas," "The Hidden Costs of Hamburgers," "In Jennifer's Room," "The Shooter," and "High on the Job." My projects drove more than half of the internal traffic (more than a million views) on The I Files YouTube channel during the first nine months. My specialty is narrative multimedia storytelling and exploring ways to use video, audio, photography, animation, and interactive graphics to push the boundaries of storytelling on the Web and other digital platforms.

My multimedia reports have been featured by NPR.org, The Huffington Post, Rolling Stone, Grist, Time.com, Fast Company, the Los Angeles Times, KQED, PBS NewsHour, Salon.com, Mother Jones, Public Radio International, Poynter, and Columbia Journalism Review, among others. My work has been honored with several awards—including prizes for Journalism Innovation, Explanatory Journalism and Feature Storytelling.

Before I got into multimedia I was a magazine editor, newspaper reporter, and video journalist. I have a master’s degree in journalism from UC Berkeley.
