Daniel Kenning's Summary

A chartered engineer and Fellow of the Institution of Mechanical Engineers, with 24 years experience and a strong focus on sustainability.
My focus has been on using sustainability thinking as a design strategy, and on helping clients seize the business opportunities of "doing more good" rather than avoiding the penalties of just "doing less bad".
I apply engineering to work towards my vision of a future with the capacity for continuity into the long term future.
I have conceived and led several innovative projects, including the Ford/Visteon Sustainability Forum, the "Splendid Eco-Car", and the Energy Environment and Sustainability Group of the IMechE.
My goal is to find and realise the biggest opportunities for moving towards sustainability.

Specialties

Sustainability, energy, energy strategy and management (ISO 50001), climate change mitigation and adaptation, resilience to peak oil, energy descent, sustainability for planning, sustainable development, sustainable transport, eco-design, environmental management systems (ISO 14001), training, stakeholder engagement, support for transition initiatives. Registered Carbon Trust Low Carbon Consultant, qualified Code for Sustainable Homes, BREEAM Retail Assessor and BREEAM Accredited Professional

IMechE

Nonprofit; 51-200 employees; Mechanical or Industrial Engineering industry

2007 – Present (6 years) IMechE London

In this non-executive role I lead a board of 22 IMechE members, and up to 100 volunteers in project working groups. The Board represents about 10,000 IMechE members worldwide with an interest in energy, environment and sustainability.
I use the role of the learned society to enable engineers to apply engineering and sustainability principles to deliver the IMechE mission of "improving the world through engineering".
To this end I initiated a new 3-stage process of learning comprising inward learning from established experts, new learning to find answers that we need to know but that we do not, and wide dissemination of new learning.
I liaise with other Divisions and Groups within IMechE and with external organisations (including trade bodies, private companies, NGOs, government and universities) to enable engineers to engage in the technical challenges of critical behaviour changing actions as well as technological actions.
I have initiated links with our Japanese colleagues at JSME to share learning on national energy plans, with the Energy Institute to share learning on peak oil, and with other professional associations.
