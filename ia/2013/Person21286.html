C. Daniel Miller's Summary

Award-winning author of Copyright Clearance for Creatives, independent publisher, consultant on the business of independent publishing, researcher in large unstructured data using the RelevanceLogic platform, developer of training in the RelevanceLogic platform.

Co-founder and co-owner of Integrated Writer Services, LLC; former producer of Mario the Peddler Productions; former marketing and distribution manager for Rockaway Press; founder, president and CEO of C/V LAN, Inc., consultant on computer and telecommunications systems to architects; president of the Center for Educational Technologies; executive director of NASA's Classroom of the Future; university professor; university department chair, educational researcher, classroom teacher, US Air Force radar technician.

Executive Director

NASA Classroom of the Future

January 1992 – December 1997 (6 years)

The Classroom of the Future was designated as NASA's premier research and development facility for applied research in the uses of advanced computer and telecommunications technologies in education. Responsibilities included providing the vision for the program, as well as overall program development, financial planning and project management. In addition to directing a combined annual COTF operations budget of just under $3 million, the position included overseeing the operation of a new 48,000 sq. ft. facility that includes a world-class integrated computer/video local area network running over an ATM fiber optic backbone network. Program development included increasing the staff from five in 1992 to the 50 in 1997. This staff created new multimedia products, provided teacher education and training, provided Internet access to NASA resources, conducted research on effective ways to learn and teach, and shared their knowledge gained on uses of technology in education. The position reported directly to the President of the university. The incumbent served on the President's Executive Council with senior university administrators.

President, Center for Educational Technologies (concurrent position)

Responsible for the administration of funded programs obtained through the Center for Educational Technologies. The total operations budget for sponsored programs funded during 1997 exceeded $4.2M. This position ran concurrently with the NASA Classroom of the Future Executive Director’s position.

C. Daniel Miller's Projects

  • University of Alaska Listening Sessions Report

    • May 2012 to June 2012
    Team Members: C. Daniel Miller

    The University of Alaska’s (UA) “Shaping Alaska’s Future 2017” is a strategic planning effort designed to solidly position the UA as a premier educational system by the 100th anniversary of its founding, 2017. In an effort to include UA constituents in this planning effort, University leaders invited students, faculty, staff, business leaders and employers, elected officials, alumni, donors, K-12 partners, and community members to Listening Sessions. A total of 80 Listening Sessions (LS) were conducted over 6 months at 17 locations spread across the UA system. The sessions allowed constituents to discuss how the UA system can become more productive and aligned with priorities of students, employers and the people of the state.

    During these Listening Sessions, a collection of 2,527 comments was amassed. RelevanceLogic (RL), a Littleton, Colorado research firm equipped with unparalleled proprietary technology, analyzed the unstructured LS comments in a way that incorporated the information’s context. By doing so, RL researchers were able to glean new insights and discoveries from the body of comments made during the Listening Sessions.
