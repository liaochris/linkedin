John K. Berrigan Jr., P.E.'s Summary

John Berrigan has over twenty-eight (28) years of experience in project engineering management, sales management, business development, & technology development in groundwater, drinking water, and facility water management.

John is a recognized specialist in water treatment technology, and regulatory affairs. He has specific expertise in water treatment, wastewater treatment, water reuse facilities design and regulatory consulting for municipal & industrial Clients.

Specialties: Facility Water Management - drinking water and water reclamation, with experience in pharmaceutical, OCPSF, oil & gas, minerals and other industries.

Water Treatment Technology - process selection, filtration, RO, NF, UF, ion exchange, activated carbon, and others.

Project Management - facilities planning, design management, process consulting, public financing.

Regulatory Affairs - SDWA, CWA compliance, litigation support & expert witness experience.

John K. Berrigan Jr., P.E.'s Projects

  • Confidential Industrial Client, Georgia

    • July 2013 to Present
    Team Members: John K. Berrigan Jr., P.E., Nicholas Tisch

    Delivery to the Client in Q4 of 2013, this project is a water treatment plant delivered to the site "complete." The building includes iron and manganese filters, a chemical feed room, delivery of treated water to external clearwell storage, withdrawal by installed high-lift pumping with hydropneumatic system pressure management and associated plant controls in a pump room. The filters include the best available backwash system with sustained simultaneous air/water backwash operating in a sub-fluidized mode for the best available media cleanliness. Limited project information on this project is available.

  • Shiloh Springs Water Treatment Plant, South Carolina

    This water treatment project provides an alternative to traditional construction by controls, instrumentation, piping and actuated valving delivered to the utility in a finished building.

    Water treated by this system will adjust naturally occurring elements emitting alpha-particles of the source water to meet state and federal requirements.

  • Arcadia, FL Water Treatment System

    • January 2010 to January 2013
    Team Members: John K. Berrigan Jr., P.E., Julie Karleskint, Chuck Hlavach, Emily Woo

    This innovative project strategy developed by Julie Karlskint and the team of professionals at Hazen & Sawyer provided a holistic solution for the City of Arcadia addressing land application, wastewater treatment and incorporating very selective use of innovative technologies dismissed by other consultants. The result was lower impact on rates and a treatment process requiring little maintenance. Tonka Water provided the innovative biologically active ion exchange processes to address sulfide, disinfection byproduct precursors and hardness.

  • Hutchinson, Minnesota - Biofiltration and Reverse Osmosis WTP

    • January 2003 to Present
    Team Members: John K. Berrigan Jr., P.E., George Bender, Dale Armitage, Craig Schuenemann

    Low pressure RO and biofiltration for ammonia removal was piloted by AECOM using equipment from Tonka Water and Infilco Degremont. John Berrigan served as project manager for the design and bidding of this facility including well rehabilitation, ground storage, and a 6 mgd treatment plant replacement to facilitate treatment of ammonia, hardness, iron and manganese in both the RO system and in sidestream treatment using two stage biofiltration. George Bender provided construction related services. Dale Armitage provided mechanical design. Craig Schuenemann provided structural design services.

    (Note, this project has many other vital contributors to this project that are very deserving of recognition, but are not currently within our LinkedIn network.)

  • Mankato, Minnesota Softening and Membrane Filtration Plant

    • January 2003 to June 2006
    Team Members: John K. Berrigan Jr., P.E.

    Mankato, Minnesota added a radial collector well, new ground water well, storage facility, raw water transmission mains directional drilled under the Blue Earth River, and a treatment facility upgrade adding high rate lime softening, recarbonation, membrane filtration, and chlorination. John Berrigan provided permitting, SRF application assistance, and design coordination through equipment selection, procurement and preliminary design.

  • Creston, Iowa, 12-Mile Water Treatment Plant, Creston Water Works

    • February 2007 to Present
    Team Members: John K. Berrigan Jr., P.E.

    Creston Water Works retained Fox Engineering to upgrade their 6 mgd surface water treatment plant. The selected improvements included conversion of the standard rate clarifiers to high-rate units, modification of five of the filter cells to accommodate a submerged ultra-filtration membrane system (one of the first of its kind in Iowa), conversion of the remaining seven filter cells to granular activated carbon contactors, SCADA improvements, pumping and hydraulic modifications, and general plant rehabilitation to a potential of 9 MGD.

    John Berrigan provided assistance in preparation of preliminary design information for Fox Engineering, bidding as a potential supplier for the project, and design and construction consulting assistance through 2008.


  • Council Bluffs, Iowa UF/RO WTP Piloting, Process and Design Assistance

    Team Members: John K. Berrigan Jr., P.E., Jami Cerone, Phil Brandhuber

    Working with HDR, and pilot equipment from Tonka Water and GE, John lead the process engineering and competitive design for the UF/RO plant in a 30-day "run-off" then a 90-day pilot study as required by the State of Iowa, stamping and sealing the pilot study and report submittal as a registered engineer in Iowa. Iron oxidation equipment was provided by Tonka water, manganese pre-oxidation, UF and RO membranes and controls were provided by GE. Three UF trains provided 5 MGD of capacity, expandable to a future 10 MGD at a recovery of 95 percent. Eighty percent of the UF filtrate was process through RO, meeting finished water hardness criteria. Three RO skids were initially provided with the ability to expand to 10 MGD of finished water capacity at a 75 percent recovery.

    The plant, CBWW Council Point Water Treatment Plant, was bid in November 2009 for $22.2 million, which included $4.5 million for the UF and RO integrated membrane system.

    Construction was completed in 2012.

    "Integrated UF and RO membrane treatment processes were
    selected for the new plant. The process performance, small footprint, operational flexibility, ability for future expansion and level of automation of the integrated system offered are expected to allow Council Bluffs Water Works to meet its treatment and operational goals with challenging raw water, now and in the future."

    Credits to HDR Inc. Waterscapes Magazine, Spring 2012 for the information provided in this project profile.
