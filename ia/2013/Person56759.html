Jacob Fox's Summary

I have always been driven to learn new things. I got top grades at McGill University. Instead of applying to law school, though, I decided to pursue a master's degree in environmental management specializing in energy systems. I wanted to continue to work in multiple disciplines across the social sciences.

Working as a graduate student and research associate, I’ve since learnt many things: how to model energy-economy systems, how analyze public policy, how to become a rapid and effective learner, how to manage a long-term project, and how to communicate complex ideas to a general audience.

I’ve also taught myself a lot outside of school: how to make money designing and self-publishing books, how to illustrate, how to navigate blogging and social media, and how to produce profitable live entertainment.

Most importantly, though, I’ve learnt a lot about myself and how I work best: my specialty is execution. While I’m very good at generating ideas, I’m great at figuring out how to get something done. I am also excellent at thinking strategically in stressful situations. I see gaps and mistakes before they happen, and make others around me look good.
