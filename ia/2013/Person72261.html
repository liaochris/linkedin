Kari Berit's Summary

Kari Berit brings years of experience and a passion for the subject of aging and caregiving.

An author, speaker, radio show host and regular column contributor, Kari is known for her wit, energy, kindness and insight. She is, as KPNW radio hosts proclaimed, “bubbly, effervescent fun.”

Kari is the author of "The Unexpected Caregiver: How Boomers Can Keep Mom & Dad Active, Safe and Independent," as well as a popular "Mental Fitness Guide" (both published by Wisconsin-based Attainment Company), hosts a weekly radio syndicated radio show, “The Unexpected Caregiver,” produces Caregiver Minutes for distribution online, and contributes caregiving articles and columns to magazines and newsletters nationwide.

Recently Kari has developed a peer-to-peer caregiver support group, designed specifically to allow family caregivers a scripted and directed approach to running a support group. The program includes support and education and is an evidence based model. (http://kariberit.com/caregiver-support/)

Specialties: Keynote presentations, starting a conference, meeting or event off with just the right tone, incorporating what you're all about into a dynamic, humorous and educational presentation.

I'm that type of keynoter--I will help you kick off your day as part of your team-connecting with your group and incorporating your theme.

If you're a financial or insurance or senior housing company are are looking for those Baby Boomer clients, I will talk about the hot topic of giving care to aging parents. Let me tackle the soft issues and you can help fill in the details to help them make tough decisions about their loved one's care as well as their own care. It's not always easy for people to talk about aging, cargiving, and making final plans. I'll make that conversation easy.

Speaker: A nationally recognized expert on the issue of aging and the family dynamics involved across generations as Boomers and their parents come to terms with caregiving issues.

Maybe your audience needs reassurance that not everyone gets Alzheimer's disease as they age. Or that it's not a foregone conclusion to look and act like their mom. Or maybe you've got a stressed-out work force who needs not only laughs, but to learn from the down and out moments in life. Tears, laughter, Learning is what you'll get when you hire me.
