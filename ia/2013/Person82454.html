Lori Bandi's Summary

Lori Bandi began her career in photography while teaching a high school yearbook class, as an elective to her English classes, in Oklahoma. When teaching wasn't working anymore, she decided to do something creative with her career and went back to school to become a professional photographer. When finished with school, she moved to Dallas, TX, where all the commercial action was happening and she's been there ever since.

Her first step in getting started was to be an assistant and learn as much about the business as she could, from as many professional photographers as possible. One major influence in her work was Greg Booth & Associates. While assisting there, she soaked us as much technique as she could from all the talented photographers there.

After a year of assisting freelance, she was hired at a small studio in Dallas, HealeyGrisham. Soon after being hired, she stopped assisting and became a full-time shooter. She worked there for nearly three years and then decided to go freelance again, as a shooter.

The latest chapter in her life puts her back in a full-time position. She is a photographer at Neiman Marcus working mainly on Horchow and Neiman Marcus Home. She is excited about what opportunities this new change will have in store for her and is optimistic about the future.

Her versatility extends to all formats: 35 mm, medium & large in both film and digital. She has experience in the darkroom and is highly trained in the digital darkroom as well. She takes pride in her efforts at continuing her education by participating in workshops and classes in both photography and Photoshop as well as other software. She is also an active board member in ASMP (American Society of Media Photographers).

Specialties: Still-life commercial photography including food, jewelry, home, and off-figure fashion. Editorial photography of food and products.
