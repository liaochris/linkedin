Janie McQueen's Publications

  • Hanging On By My Fingernails: Surviving the New Divorce Gamesmanship, and How a Scratch Can Land You in Jail

    • Burning Sage Publishing, Atlanta
    • April 2012
    Authors: Janie McQueen

    HANGING ON BY MY FINGERNAILS: Surviving the New Divorce Gamesmanship, and How a Scratch Can Land You in Jail unveils the truth behind the "new divorce gamesmanship"--vicious tactics that thrust victims into complex webs of legal tangles that destroy spirits and hobble divorce cases. These surprisingly common--and legally deadly--ploys can and often lead to criminal charges and trials, lingering records, heartbreaking child custody battles, embarrassing distortion campaigns, and even unemployment as employers increasingly trawl the Internet for background checks. Women who are anywhere in the divorce process will find here powerful information with which to defend themselves--keeping heart and soul intact. With bonus guide: How To Spot a Set-Up and What To Do.

  • The New Magic Bookshelf: Finding Great Books Your Child Will Treasure Forever

    • Fine Lines Publishing
    • October 2008
    Authors: Janie McQueen

    This all-new, completely revised and updated Anniversary edition brings back the popular parents' resource guide that 10 years ago won national recognition for showing parents how to weed through confusing, over-hyped juvenile book merchandising and turn their children on to the very best in modern children's literature. Packed with more practical, easy to implement advice than ever, The New Magic Bookshelf offers resources including fully annotated book lists that help parents choose exciting and appealing books for their children's specific interests and needs, from picture books to quality series to nonfiction to young adult novels. Numerous special sections include "Book-Lovers From Babyhood," "Luring the Noninterested Reader and the Junk Book Junkie," and "If Your Child Does Not Live With You." Bonus sections include guides to literary award-winners, electronic media and Internet sites, children's book club and magazine info, and review sources for new books.
