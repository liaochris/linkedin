Courtney-Rose Dantus' Summary

Courtney-Rose Harris has been involved in issues of civil rights and social justice since she was elected as President of the NAACP Colorado/Montana/Wyoming State Conference Youth and College Division at age 16. She led voter registration drives and voter education efforts for the NAACP and was a co-founder of the Diversity Alliance at her high school.

Courtney-Rose has leveraged her public affairs work for the greater good. She worked as an organizer in the Southern Colorado Campaign of Barack Obama for President and as a staff intern in the office of U.S. Senator Ken Salazar. As a student at Colorado College she successfully organized others in support of of equal opportunity, opposing Colorado Amendment 46 which would have ended affirmative action and equal opportunity in the state.

Previously, Courtney-Rose was a member of NAACP's Communications and New Media Department. While there she supported the creative development and management of NAACPConnect - The NAACP’s social networking hub for youth and college members of the Association along with other NAACP Web Campaign and New Media initiatives. She has also been a featured speaker and trainer at Regional NAACP Civil Rights & Advocacy Institute’s in North Carolina, Maryland, Iowa and Alabama, training young civil rights leaders on the power of social media as a tool for advocacy and civic engagement.

Currently she works as a Digital Campaign Coordinator at SEIU -- the fastest-growing union in North America focused on building a fair economy, providing workers a voice on the job, fighting for equality and ensuring that all working people can live with dignity. Courtney-Rose leads online organizing and strategy efforts for SEIU's Public Services Division and the union's Retirement Security campaign.

As "The Blacktivist", a blog persona, she is creating an online community that promises to bring together young women leaders, utilizing social media, powerful stories and other technology.
