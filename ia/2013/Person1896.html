Andrew D. Nystrom's Publications

  • Top Trails Yellowstone & Grand Tetons: Must-do Hikes for Everyone

    • Wilderness Press
    • July 15, 2009
    Authors: Andrew D. Nystrom

    "There are several very good guidebooks to the trails of Yellowstone [and Grand Teton National Parks]. This one is great." – Tim Cahill, author of Lost in My Own Backyard: A Walk in Yellowstone Park, Jaguars Ripped My Flesh, and Hold the Enlightenment

  • The Lonely Planet Guide to The Middle of Nowhere

    • Lonely Planet
    • October 1, 2006
    Authors: Andrew D. Nystrom

    This coffee table pictorial fosters the spirit of exploration and travel. It's about appreciation and seeking out adventure in all travel experiences, whether in the remote regions of Russia or the chaotic streets of Shanghai. The first-person accounts weave a story about the journeys as much as the destinations. I contributed an essay about the most remote inhabited seasonal outpost in the Lower 48 US States, in Yellowstone National Park.

  • Lonely Planet South America: On a Shoestring

    • Lonely Planet (BBC)
    • March 1, 2004
    Authors: Andrew D. Nystrom

    "The backpackers' bible for South America." I wrote the Bolivia chapter and contributed to Paraguay, Antarctica and Argentine and Chilean Patagonia chapters. "Best for curious and independent-minded travelers." --Wall Street Journal

  • Lonely Planet Guide to Experimental Travel

    • Lonely Planet
    • May 1, 2005
    Authors: Andrew D. Nystrom

    Essay about letting a stray dog take me for a walk in Montana. "Those aching for a relief from packaged tours or Club Med vacations would do well to pick up this out-of-the-ordinary guide. It's a manual for 'experimental travel,' a 'playful' and 'pleasingly vague' style of vacation, 'where the journey's methodology is clear but the destination may be unknown.'" "For the adventurous traveler who wants to live like a native." --Real Simple Magazine

  • Lonely Planet The Career Break Book

    • Lonely Planet
    • December 1, 2004
    Authors: Andrew D. Nystrom
