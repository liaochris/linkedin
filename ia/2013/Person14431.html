Bill Russell's Summary

Bill Russell creates images and design that enhance the printed page and in the digital sphere. As an accomplished web designer, illustrator and visual journalist, he has contributed his creative expertise, design and picture-making to a multitude of projects, companies, forums and online media throughout the United States and Canada. His voracious appetite for artistic expression keeps Bill busy creating abstract paintings and collages in his San Rafael studio. He enjoys spending time with his family in Marin County, California.

Specialties

illustration, web design, visual journalism, writing, teaching,

Bill Russell's design company provides expert interface design, content management, marketing and front-end programming for websites, blogs and smart devices with a quality assurance that's both professional and personal.

Bill is looking for small, creative businesses that require these custom services.

Visual Journalist

Profile Reportage

Design industry

April 2005 – Present (8 years 5 months)

Bill specializes in an illustration style he calls "profile reportage", an on-site, sketchbook-style approach to tell a visual story using drawings and text. With this approach BIll brings people, situations and events to life.

Recently Bill's portraits have been appearing in a series of newspaper ads for the Marin Humane Society. As well, he created a web-based ad campaign called 'Shine' for Golden Gate University in San Francisco. Bill applied his brand of visual journalism at Recology in San Francisco as their artist-in-residence. Some other clients include Chow.com where he drew various dishes being prepared for a bi-weekly, on-line feature called The Basics. For TODO magazine he drew cab drivers in sitiu. He also illustrated small business owners for the San Francisco Chamber of Commerce's monthly newsletter. Bill created over 80 illustrated profiles of unique Bay area people for a Saturday feature called the Bay Folk Sketchbook in the San Francisco Chronicle.

Bill is seeking a few robust corporate and non-profit clients to produce visual profiles on an on-going basis.

Illustrator

Billustration

Design industry

June 1976 – Present (37 years 3 months) San Rafael, California

As a freelance illustrator Bill Russell uses an art medium called scratchboard which produces a very graphic, iconic, woodcut-like style images for corporate and editorial clients, book publishing companies, magazines and newspapers, licensing, logo design and package design.

Bill works in a graphic, woodcut-like style of illustration using scratchboard.

He has been commissioned by a plethora of editorial and publishing clients, including Apple, Simon & Schuster, Esquire, Reader's Digest and The New York Times. Some highlights include interior drawings for three Stephen King novels and a complete illustrated brand identity for MetroWerks, the Austin-based software developer.

He has received awards for his work from The New York Society of Illustrators, The San Francisco Society of Illustrators, The Toronto Art Director's Club and the Communication Arts Award Annual.

Bill taught illustration at California College of the Arts (formerly CCAC) for eight years.

His illustration agent is the Toronto-based Reactor Art + Design.
