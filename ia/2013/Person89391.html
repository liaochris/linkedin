Matt MacDonald's Summary

Matt MacDonald is the Chief Creative Officer at JWT New York, one of the world’s largest global advertising agencies. As the head creative for brands such as Macy’s, Smirnoff and JetBlue, Matt has created award-winning and groundbreaking campaigns that go far beyond the traditional thirty-second commercial.

For Macy’s, Matt helped create “Yes, Virginia”, an animated Christmas special that premiered on CBS in 2009. Since then, he has overseen its development as an entertainment brand, spawning a best-selling DVD, book, iPhone and iPad app, and even a Macy’s Thanksgiving Day Parade balloon.

For Smirnoff, Matt helmed the Nightlife Exchange Project, an interactive experiment that brought together the online and offline worlds in a global celebration of nightlife. Now in its second year, the Nightlife Exchange has expanded from 14 countries to 50.

In 2009, his JetBlue “Welcome Bigwigs” web series lampooning CEOs and their private jets made headline news around the country—including the front page of the New York Times.

Matt’s work has been recognized in every major creative competition in the industry, including The One Show, Cannes, D&AD, Clio and The Effies. His work is a part of the permanent collection of the Museum of Modern Art. He lives with his wife Sandy and twin daughters in Rowayton, Connecticut. 

Specialties: I actively avoid the phrase "at the end of the day."
