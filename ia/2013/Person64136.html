  • Your Career Ladder is a 3D Cube

    • Boulder Daily Camera
    • May 1999
    Authors: Jim Rettew

    Graduation season is full of metaphors. Are you on the road to success or at a dead end? Are you a fly on the windshield or a caterpillar turning into a butterfly?

    The metaphor many college graduates are dealing with now has to do with jobs -- time to climb the infamous career ladder. That's fine if ladders were inspirational. But when I think of ladders, I think of work. Ladders are used to clean the gutters, paint the house, get the frisbee off the roof. And more than that, they're limiting. You can only go in two directions, and one of them is down.

    What if we changed that metaphor? What if your career ladder was a three dimensional cube? You could move up and over, zig right, zag left, turn up and in. You could not only make lateral jumps, but three-dimensional ones, always moving along the way.

    For some, this is an uneasy scenario because it doesn't provide a lot of direction. This is true. A ladder may give a lot more direction but not a lot of inspiration. Instead, the 3D cube allows for leniency, for creativity. As long as you point in the right general direction, it doesn't matter that you're not going directly up, just as long as you're pointed magnetic north.
