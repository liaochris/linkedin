Ed Rodriguez's Summary

Ed Rodríguez is a versatile Corporate Development executive with a consistent record of inventing, planning, launching, and managing new market mobile and information solutions businesses. His leadership skills have been proven in market leading, high-growth enterprises from start-ups to the Fortune 100 across a broad scope of roles including corporate strategy, general management, strategic marketing, operations, business process reengineering, finance, and consulting. Strategically, Ed created and presented new market business cases resulting in more than $3 Billion in annual sales. Tactically, Ed is known for building and leading high-performing cross-function teams including managing a $MM start-up business unit overseas.

Preferred business model : eco-system lock-in model in a blue ocean network economy growing via concentric diversification.

Business style: an "amiable driver",i.e., driven to meet or exceed important milestones but highly values business interpersonal relationships

First wireless-related role: 1993

Professional interests: Emerging Market opportunities; profitable and highly relevant content, marketing, and solutions for mobile users

Specialties

- Sound business planning and execution for emerging mobile and information market opportunities

- Latin America / international, and hispanic US marketing / bus dev / management

- Bilingual Spanish / English and some French

Founder, CEO

PrivatePlanet.com

1999 – 2001 (2 years)

I started PrivatePlanet.com in order to provide a private ecommerce option to the 2/3rds of online shoppers who were not buying online due to privacy concerns. This was especially important for the teen and healthcare market segments. We put together a great team, plan, and had a wonderful group of angel investors. After one "pivot", the timing of the dot com collapse killed us at the starting line, but we did not lose a dime of investor capital. I am still a big believer in the transaction processing business model where one's business value is embedded into an ecosystem.

Key learning: uncontrollable / unpredictable macro events happen, so getting out to market / launching asap reduces everyone's risk

Director, Emerging Markets

EDS Corporation

Privately Held; 10,001+ employees; Information Technology and Services industry

1996 – 1998 (2 years)

I directed the team responsible for all corporate new market planning at EDS, the #1 independent provider of professional services and IT solutions worldwide. Our #1 achievement here was creating the original Business Process Outsourcing plan that I presented to senior leaders. This became a multi-Billion dollar core line of EDS business.

Key learning: massive companies shifting gears during an industry transformation should think configuration strategy

General Manager

EDS Colombia

Privately Held; 10,001+ employees; Information Technology and Services industry

1995 – 1996 (1 year)

After implementation of an EDS PC billing system at Celumóvil, Colombia's first cellular carrier, I started up and managed EDS Colombia. This was EDS' first in-country organization in Colombia. We provided a mission-critical solution to Celumóvil during its own start-up period which set world subscriber growth records for penetration. Despite extraordinary challenges, we were very successful in large part due to a great team of sharp, hard-working local hires, a few well-placed experienced ex-pats, good integration with mother ship development, and an aggressive and collaborative customer. EDS built off of its success in the mobile carrier vertical to later expand its client base in other vertical markets in Colombia.

Key learning: a team that works diligently together can produce incredible results under the most challenging conditions

Business Planning Specialist, Strategy and International

EDS Personal Communications

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; Hospital & Health Care industry

1993 – 1995 (2 years)

EDS PC was the fastest growing small business in the USA, and was focused primarily on billing systems for wireless carriers and inter-carrier information solutions - a true eco-system lock in model for a network economy. I established the Strategic Planning process here as part of BPR. A lot of the advancements just now being introduced in wireless were part of our planning as far back as the early 1990's.

Later I supported the International side of the business with regional marketing. My only regret was not spending more free time in Buenos Aires.

Key learning was ...
- the real pace of change in wireless versus the hype
- the continuous need to manage client expectations is universal, but relationships matter most
- the cultural shift that inevitably happens when an entrepreneurial org is swallowed by a massive company must be managed
- the only perfect strategy requires having senior leaders in lockstep
