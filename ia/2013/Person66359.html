John Fries' Summary

For three decades, I've been leading teams and projects in a wide and diverse range of innovative marketing, advertising, branding, public relations, and digital communications initiatives. My approach combines a high degree of innovation and creativity with a sharp focus on results. I'm also a prolific writer and graphic designer across all media.

In addition to holding communications leadership positions at such organizations as UPMC Passavant, UPMC Shadyside, the Port Authority of Allegheny County, and The Washington Hospital, I served as manager at Burson-Marsteller, one of the world's largest PR and advertising agencies, where I worked on a range of initiatives.

My diverse portfolio of strategy, writing and design includes work for such corporations as Heinz, Highmark, UPMC Health System, PNC Bank, Giant Eagle, Duquesne Light and Schering Plough, as well as dozens of mid-sized companies, small businesses, non-profit organizations, entrepreneurial ventures and entertainment projects.

I also write, produce and direct television, videos and commercials. "Blue Myst Road," my original film about Pittsburgh urban legends, received significant media coverage. I also wrote the short comedy "A Day in the Life of Gene Simmons," which was featured on the Kiss founder's website. I've since established Blue Myst Films, which creates original and client-contracted film, video and digital online content.

As a contributing journalist, my writing has been published in a wide variety of media. In 2013, I published the e-book "Rock & Roll Business School," which presents 15 proven marketing and communications strategies from which any company can benefit.

I sometimes speak at professional gatherings and occasionally guest lecture at colleges and universities.

My website is at www.johnfries.com.
