Gonçalo Andrade's Publications

  • Subjective Timepieces - a personal approach to the notion of time

    • ESAD
    • November 27, 2010
    Authors: Gonçalo Andrade

    Ever since ancient times, Man has used timepieces to map time. These worked in the most suitable way to serve the needs of its users.

    A long time ago, important things to measure were years and seasons and religious events, so that was what timepieces measured. However, these needs changed along the centuries, and so did the timepieces along with them, so as to better serve the people who used them.

    Today the value of time has increased exponentially, becoming more valuable than ever. As such, our need to measure it and control it mush better and precisely has led to a proliferation of timepieces. But the timepieces we use still use the same principles as ever. Even the ones on our computers and cellphones and other gadgets work the same way their great-grandads used to work.

    Is there no better way for timepieces to work other than the old ones? Aren't there more systems more suitable to the needs of an accelerated culture? This is what this project intends to answer, by analysing what already exists, and experimenting into new grounds.
