Brad Howarth's Summary

I'm a freelance journalist, author and speaker with close to 20 years' professional experience, including roles as marketing editor and technology editor at the Australian weekly business magazine BRW, and as a technology writer for the national newspaper The Australian. In 2003 I authored my first book, 'Innovation and Emerging Markets', which was a study of entrepreneurial Australian technology companies and the process of commercialising technology globally. In February 2011 I released my second book, ' A Faster Future', co-authored with Janelle Ledwidge. A Faster Future is an investigation of the future of broadband applications and services and the impact these will have on business, society and individuals. More information can be found at www.afasterfuture.com.

I'm currently working on a freelance basis for the following publications:

The Australian (IT section)
BIT.com.au
BRW
CIO Australia
Government Technology Review
Sydney Morning Herald (features and IT Pro section)

I've also been engaged to write numerous white papers and research reports for a variety of organisations.

And finally, I am also a regular speaker on topics relating to technology-based change within business and society, and how these changes are impacting business sectors and society. I've included more information below.

Brad Howarth's Experience

Co-author

A Faster Future

January 2010 – Present (3 years 7 months)

This website has been created as a companion site for the forthcoming book, A Faster Future, by Brad Howarth and Janelle Ledwidge. The National Broadband Network (NBN) presents an unprecedented opportunity for the development and expansion of the Australian economy. It has the potential to deliver a step-change in the way that individuals and businesses interact through online services and to reshape numerous industries, from entertainment and education to healthcare and utilities, changing business models and creating new opportunities. This website is dedicated to delivering news and information on high-speed broadband applications in Australia and around the world, and to exploring the challenges and opportunities that will emerge as Australia moves to faster broadband services.

Speaker, facilitator, moderator

Saxton Speakers Bureau

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Entertainment industry

2011 – Present (2 years)

Two decades in journalism have given me a knack for taking complicated topics and scenarios and presenting them in a straightforward and compelling manner, and demonstrating the interconnectedness of developments. It's also given me a chance to investigate multiple industries, so that each presentation is tailored to the specific needs of the audience with information that is relevant, entertaining and thought-provoking.

My key message - the future is important, but it is what is happening today that is vital. The world is full of ideas and concepts that are ready to be harnessed. We don't need to wait for tomorrow - there is plenty we can work with today, if we know what to look for and how to adapt ideas. After all, we create our own future through the actions we take today.

In addition to delivering keynote presentations, I'm also available for hosting events and round tables, and for leading discussions on the impact of change and the possibilities that it opens up.
