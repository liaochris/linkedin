Michael Haupt's Summary

How wonderful to meet you!
I’m a firm believer in synchronicity. That’s just a fancy word for “meaningful coincidences.” It’s no coincidence that our paths crossed today, and I hope you’ll take the time to connect with me, particularly if you're running a business that is in service to a higher purpose.

My guess is that:
- You have an incredibly powerful message, but it often feels to you as if it's the world's best kept secret.
- You've been told that social media is the way to get your voice heard, but you're being drowned out by others who make more noise than you (and you don't have the time anyway).
- You dislike typical sales and marketing methods, because they don't feel authentic.

Here's a question for you: What if it were possible to use existing platforms like Facebook, YouTube and Twitter in a radically different way to gently and authentically reach only the people who most need your message - in a non-salesy way?

Set that intention, and you'll be surprised by what can be done.
If you need any pointers, start by downloading my "Rock the Planet Guide" at ShiftPublishing.com. I don't even ask for an email address, because that's just too same-same, isn't it?

On a personal level, I've had the great privilege of living in 16 cities on 6 continents, but right now I'm connecting with nature in a tiny mountain village 30 minutes from the nearest bank and 90 minutes from the nearest city. Most of the clients I work with recognize that a setting like this is conducive to radically different levels of thinking. I hope you'll come visit some day to experience it yourself.

Michael Haupt's Publications

  • Radical Marketing Strategies for Difference Makers, Disruptors and Other Troublemakers

    • Shift Publishing
    • May 31, 2013
    Authors: Michael Haupt

    This book is not about religion, social media or marketing. And yet it’s about all three, since all of life is interconnected.

    “Would Jesus Tweet?” is about trends, convergence and future memes. It’s what you need to know if you want to rock the planet with your message, nurture your disciples and leave a lasting legacy for your followers.

    There are literally thousands of titles available for Twitter, Facebook, LinkedIn and other social media tactics, and yet none sufficiently explain how social media fits into Western civilization’s development, and what the future of communication looks like. This book does.

    “Would Jesus Tweet?” is the ideal reference for time-strapped, overwhelmed and confused difference-makers who want to make as big an impact as Jesus did in his time, but have been frustrated by their social media results and experience to date. It’s perfect for you if all of the social media advice you’ve received so far just doesn’t cut it.

    Inside these pages you’ll learn:

    - The major trend communication has followed through the ages – from letter-writing to tweeting – and how to future-proof your communication style and methods;
    - The historical cycle affecting all Western civilizations of the past and how you need to adjust your marketing to prepare for the coming changes;
    - The radical shifts marketing leaders in forward thinking companies are preparing for;
    - How to craft the essence of your message, keeping these trends in mind;
    - How to achieve consistency across all social media platforms;
    - How to get social media working for you to gather your disciples and enthuse them with your energy;
    - How to use technology to stop selling and start serving;
    - Case studies of quiet revolutions using social media, which you can use to model and create your own loyal following.

  • Noetic Business Manifesto - The Blueprint for Business Success in Times of Radical Change

    • October 1, 2010
    Authors: Michael Haupt

    This is a Manifesto for Noetic Business — a challenge for business leaders in every sector and in every corner of our planet to work together to inspire, encourage and support as many employees, customers and suppliers as possible to create a global shift of our “operating system” to solve our major planetary crises, without negatively affecting quality, profits, turnover or shareholder returns.

    By the end of 2012, everyone of working age should understand the need to transition from a worldview built on separation and independence to one of inter-dependence, based on a global sense of oneness. No one should retire from business without having contributed to a truly global culture that is sustainable, peaceful, healthy, and prosperous.

    My vision is for modern business to create a world that we will be proud to leave to our children. Business-as-usual hasn’t achieved this. This new business model is one possible answer.
