Beck Tench's Summary

Beck Tench is a simplifier, illustrator, story teller and technologist. Formally trained as a graphics designer at the University of North Carolina’s School of Journalism and Mass Communication, she has spent her career elbow deep in web work of all sorts – from the knowledge work of information architecture and design to the hands dirty work of writing code and testing user experiences. Currently, she serves as Director for Innovation and Digital Engagement at the Museum of Life and Science in Durham, NC where she studies and experiments with how visitors and staff use technology to experience risk-taking, community-making and science in their everyday lives.

Beck Tench's Experience

Director for Innovation and Digital Engagement

Museum of Life and Science

Nonprofit; 51-200 employees; Museums and Institutions industry

July 2008 – Present (4 years 10 months)

I create novel ways for people to spend time with the science museum, most often using technologies like social networks, data visualization and mobile devices. In addition to my home museum, I have worked with the Exploratorium in San Francisco, the Science Museum of Minnesota, Michigan State University’s Writing in Digital Environments Program and the Cornell Lab of Ornithology.

Usually, the experiences I create are web-initiated, but designed to bring people together in a face-to-face, materials-based learning environment. In the words of meetup.com: I use the Internet to get people off the Internet. To design and deploy experiences quickly, I almost always turn to free, lightweight, third-party or open source technologies. To support community, I rely on pre-existing social networks, choosing to go where they people are instead requiring them to come to us.
