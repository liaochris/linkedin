Andrea López-Portillo's Summary

I was born in Mexico City and I currently live in Salzburg, Austria, teaching at an international school.

In the past, I have volunteered in film festivals, photographic archives and art galleries, achieved a work experience placement at BBC Mundo in London in 2010, and worked as a photographer for the Salzburg Global Seminar. Some of my work and articles have been published in Mexican and English newspapers, Austrian magazines, and websites such as BBC Mundo and GlobalPost.

My contact with photography started when I was very young, with a father who would not leave the camera alone. I inherited my dad's 35mm camera when I was still a teenager and I had my first digital camera when I turned 23. I have gone from animation projects and assisting photographers, to creating artistic interventions for companies, and have experimented with diverse creative areas such as painting, sculpture, engraving, design, film and writing, my other passion.

In 2009 I exhibited my first collection 'De uno a otro' (One to another), which travelled to three different states in Mexico for a total of five exhibitions; it was also showcased in London in 2010. The collection 'Home' was exhibited several times in London between 2011 and 2013. 'Memories of Schloss Leopoldskron' may be the last digital photography collection that I develop, as I look towards experimenting with other mediums.

Although I aspired to become a known artist for a long time, this ambition changed the moment I learned about global citizenship. I am now interested in using photography and art as an educational tool, and as a means for social change.

I am currently developing a Polaroid collection and, in collaboration with Adam Beeson, a photobook and research project titled 'Marked by Trade'.

www.andrealopezportillo.com
