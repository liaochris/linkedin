David M. Lilenfeld is an intellectual property attorney and founder of Lilenfeld PC in Atlanta. He represents clients in intellectual property disputes involving trademarks, copyrights, patents and trade secrets.

David is a graduate of Hunter College with a Bachelor of Arts in philosophy. He earned his J.D. at The Ohio State University Moritz College of Law in 1996 where he was a member of The Ohio State Law Journal. David was named a Rising Star in 2009 and 2010 by Georgia Super Lawyers, which recognizes the best up-and-coming attorneys in the state. He serves as the chairperson of the Small Firm Section of the Atlanta Bar Association and was the chairperson of the Litigation Committee of the Technology Law section of the State Bar of Georgia.

A trusted resource for the media on intellectual property and business litigation, David has been published in The Atlanta Journal-Constitution, Atlanta Business Chronicle, IPFrontline.com and other business and legal outlets. At business meetings and conferences, he speaks on intellectual property law and business litigation.

Honors and Awards:

In 2009 and again in 2010, David M. Lilenfeld was selected as a Georgia “Rising Star” by Georgia Super Lawyers. This listing of the “best up-and-coming attorneys” represents only 2.5 percent of Georgia attorneys.

Rising Stars are selected by Georgia Super Lawyers through a nomination process, peer evaluation and internal research conducted by Law & Politics. The most recent group of "Super Lawyers" first votes on the best attorneys who are either 40 or under or who have been practicing 10 or fewer years. Then, the attorney-led research team reviews the credentials of potential "Rising Stars" and assigns points based on a set of defined evaluation criteria. After the scores are tallied, only 2.5 percent of the best up-and-coming attorneys in the state remain.
