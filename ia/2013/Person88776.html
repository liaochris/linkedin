Matt Heller's Summary

I have spent 24 years in the amusement/theme park industry. Starting as a ride operator at Canobie Lake Park just out of high school, I have enjoyed a robust career around the industry with leadership positions at Canobie, Knott’s Camp Snoopy, Valleyfair and Smiles Entertainment Center. Pulling from my previous leadership experience, in 2003 I joined Universal Orlando Resort as the Senior Trainer of Leadership Development. I also served as the Chair of the IAAPA Human Resources Committee from 2009-2012, and am currently serving on the IAAPA Education Committee.

In 2011, I reduced my role at Universal to pursue additional independent training and consulting opportunities. I created Performance Optimist Consulting and now I help leaders across the hospitality industry make sure they are getting the most out of themselves and their teams. I do this through my blog (leadertips.wordpress.com), on-site training, and individual coaching.

In 2013, I released my first book entitled, “The Myth of Employee Burnout”. In it I outline my quest to find out what truly causes employees to lose motivation and what leaders can do to overcome it. www.performanceoptimist.com
