Lazaro Wolf's Projects

  • Jeff Sutherland at BCS

    • October 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Lazaro Wolf

    BCS Agile SG organises an inspirational talk with the co-creator of Scrum, Dr. Jeff Sutherland.

    The leading expert will be speaking about the Origins of Scrum, how these values and influences appear today and where he sees that Scrum is headed.

    The methodology that Jeff developed in 1993 and formalised in 1995 with Ken Schwaber has since been adopted by the vast majority of software development companies around the world. But Jeff realised that the benefits of Scrum are not limited to software and product development. He has adapted this successful strategy for several other industries including: finance, healthcare, higher education and telecom.

    Jeff sets the vision for success with Scrum. He continues to share best practices with organisations around the globe and has written extensively on Scrum rules and methods. With a deep understanding of business process -gleaned from years as CTO/CEO of eleven different software companies- Jeff is able to describe the high level organisational benefits of Scrum and what it takes to create hyper-productive teams.

    The event is kindly hosted and sponsored by ExactTarget, London.

  • E-Tourism: Filling the Gap between Knowledge Demand and Supply

    • November 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Lazaro Wolf

    E-Tourism has significantly changed tourism industry and tourist behaviour by creating new services, fostering international exposure and providing access to local knowledge through its enabling role.

    The quality of e-tourism content is significant and frequently forces the establishment of national knowledge providers to centrally manage the national brand and facilitate collaboration amongst various stakeholders.

    The lecture focuses on successful implementation approaches, which fill the gap between knowledge supply and demand through the innovative integration of tools, services, human resources management and knowledge management.

    Discussion includes the case of New Zealand, highlighting how an integrated portal transformed the online tourism presence and attracted millions of visitors worldwide.

    Phuket Rajabhat University, Phuket, April 2013.
