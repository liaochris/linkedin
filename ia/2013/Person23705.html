Chris Romano's Summary

Chris Romano is a sales champion and natural leader. His MBA in Entrepreneurial Studies from Xavier University is a nice complement to his BS in Sociology from LaSalle University.

Chris served 10 years as a U.S. Marine, and has led award-winning sales teams in the recruiting and pharmaceutical sectors. An entrepreneur at heart, Chris founded a social venture called Bunch! which empowers members to find information and motivation to better their lives.

Today, he is a partner at Applaud, an innovative company that uses patent pending methods and technology to help groups (like families, teams and clubs) collaborate to preserve and share the memories of their important times together. Building a great company with a very cool mission has been the culmination of a lifetime honing skills and knowledge in leadership & management, recruiting & HR, sales & marketing, complex math & brain science.

Chris lives with his wife and five children in Deerfield, a fast-growing Cincinnati suburb. Elected to the town council in 2005, Chris is intimately involved in the strategic leadership of 120+ employee, $20 million government.

Chris Romano's Experience

Vice President

Applaud

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Internet industry

March 2009 – Present (4 years 6 months) Cincinnati Area

Applaud is an interactive digital media company that uses science, math and technology to help people have better lives through better memories. Our innovative technique, uses social software to crowd source input, then blends content to create valuable and personalized output that makes for better memories.

Our premier offering is a service for families to preserve and share important group memories from the teams and clubs they spend their lives in. We’ve also invented a novel approach to delivering what’s called Collaborative Reminiscence Therapy for people impacted by memory disorder. We're exploring ways to use this method and toolset to better the lives of recovering combat wounded veterans and people with early stage dementia.
