Aaron Davis' Summary

Telling a compelling story, exciting an audience, heightening a brand, making a person say "I have to go see that" or "I'll take it" is the focus of my work.

My goal is making your messages meaningful to your audience. By asking myself better and better questions throughout the design process I discover new insights.

The end product is a concept and execution of a movie poster, an ad, etc. that reinforces your brand and motivate people to buy.

Specialties

My strengths are: Type: creating typefaces and title treatments; designing logos and marks. Photoshop: I have been acquiring advanced techniques since 1.0 (circa 1993). I have extensive experience retouching and compositing images that are absolutely believable.

Art Director

Victoria's Secret PINK

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; LTD; Retail industry

May 2007 – June 2011 (4 years 2 months)

My position was to support the creative director with Illustrator, InDesign & Photoshop work for his concepts on magazine ads and direct mail pieces while creating and presenting my own concepts for store signage, gift cards and window vinyls. After a year my position evolved into a more managerial focus, overseeing our teams workload. I developed a management style and worked with the team members to help them reach their development goals. I became an effective project manager saving the department time, money and resources. I traveled regularly between Ohio, New York and Las Vegas gathering inspiration, attending press checks and managing both in person and from a distance. In the 2010 fiscal year we reached $1 billion in sales. On this high note I went freelance to create the same type of success for a variety of companies.

Art Director / Production Manager

Indika Entertainment Advertising

Public Company; 1-10 employees; Entertainment industry

May 2006 – October 2006 (6 months)

Indika is an Entertainment Ad Agency with offices in NY & LA with their primary clients being Warner Brother, Paramount and The Weinsteins.

As production manager at an entertainment ad agency with only eight employees I maintained the file structure and workflow procedure for our artwork as well as a hands on graphic designer 90% of the time. We worked on home entertainment, i.e. Jackass & Ghost Whisperer. We mostly worked on feature film key art, i.e. Oceans 13, Goya’s Ghost, Perfume, etc. We would successfully produce 15-18 concepts for a project in a 1-3 day period. My forte was title treatments and Photoshop compositing. People refer to work at Indika as guerilla warfare on artwork. They average a grueling 10 hour day with lots of stress and pressure. I enjoyed the momentum and importance of it all. I would not have left this position, but a family tragedy took precedence and I left to support my father.

Retoucher

Warner Bros.

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; TWX; Entertainment industry

September 2004 – October 2005 (1 year 2 months)

As a graphic designer I do a fair amount of retouching to my work and I always thought it would be great to be a full time retoucher. I received an opportunity at Ivyhill to become a prepress retoucher for Warner Home Entertainment. Learning the print shop side of design was an invaluable experience. I excelled in this position and was moved up to Warner Features doing the final retouching to Warner’s new releases. I finished films like Kiss Kiss Bang Bang, Corpse Bride & Batman Returns. This experience made me a better designer by far and my feeling was that I wanted to go back to designing so I refocused and left to freelance as a graphic designer.

Post Production Supervisor

eVox Productions

Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Automotive industry

December 2003 – August 2004 (9 months)

eVox is a photo studio that shoots automobiles in a standardised way to license the photography back to the auto manufacturer for use on their website. For instance, if you’ve ever seen a 360º view of the inside of a car or the car spin or change color on a website then that was eVox’s work. I managed a team of ten; teaching them advanced retouching techniques. I kept track of our vehicle inventory and pushed the team to up their productivity. I managed the library of digital assets and prepared deliveries to the client. This is the position was an excellent learning experience in managing both people and a large library of digital assets. My reason for leaving was to pursue the opportunity at Warner Bros.

Creative Services Supervisor

Metro Goldwyn Mayer Studios

Privately Held; 201-500 employees; MGM; Entertainment industry

May 2000 – June 2002 (2 years 2 months)

This position was the lead designer in MGM Worldwide Television Group. It was concepting key art for televisions series and pilots both domestic and intl. In addition to MGM’s assets we also worked on the artwork for NBC and Showtime. Posters, trade ads and billboards were our main vehicles. I created bar campaigns where we would brand everything in a bar with our show, i.e. Stargate & Outer Limits glasses, coasters, napkins, bathroom posters and sidewalk stickers. I would carry the key art across large media buys for the top eight markets for shows like SexWars & She Spys. I monitored the departments equipment and ordered software packages. I guided projects through completion with vendors and freelancers like dvd menus and soundtracks. This position ended with MGM’s restructuring.

Senior Graphic Designer

The Ohio State University

Educational Institution; 10,001+ employees; Higher Education industry

June 1998 – 2000 (2 years)

I had been working full time as a graphic designer since I was nineteen years old, but this was my first salaried position at twenty-four. The university hired me to work on a project to double bus service. We had three months to evaluate the need, structure the routes, create maps and collateral, and educate the public through an ad campaign. I benchmarked the design work against the NYC Metro, The London Underground and other successful transportation systems. This was information design as opposed to the typical entertainment design I had been doing. I operated on a more objective set of parameters than with entertainment. I applied myself fully and met our goals. We submitted the process to the USA Today/RIT Quality Cup contest and won. With this award I relocated to Los Angeles to pursue greater opportunities.
