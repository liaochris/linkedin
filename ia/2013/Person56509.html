Jackie Lee's Summary

In 2000, I founded Artemis with a view to helping young people engage with their history and heritage. Through the business I have developed a unique way of presenting Scottish history based on solid topic research both to the education world and heritage sector.

My passion for history and the heritage and culture of our country and its interpretation to the widest possible audience is evident in the work of my Company and has been the primary focus of Artemis since its foundation.

Specialties

Innovative in solutions to heritage interpretation; creative and passionate about excellence at all levels of service; meticulous attention to detail.

Jackie Lee's Experience

Director and owner

Artemis Scotland Ltd

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Museums and Institutions industry

March 2000 – Present (13 years 6 months) Glasgow, United Kingdom

Niche provider of high quality services to the cultural, heritage & tourism sector and educational institutions.

We have recently been joined by Dr Chris Lee who brings with him over 25 years of museum interpretation and exhibition experience.

While costumed intepretation has long been our focus for delivery, the Company now provides a variety of other resources to those working in the field of heritage and museum education through our resource packs, CPD training and skill based development programmes for professionals in museums, galleries and heritage sites as well as volunteers working within these sectors.

Our team includes museum professionals who advise us on our sets and props for our costumed work and who also deliver the training sessions. We also provide costumed interpretation training for those in the profession.

The business has been commended for its meticulous attention to detail and is committed to providing its clients with an excellent professional service.

Our excellence in heritage education has been recognised by the prestigious Sandford Award.

As part of our costumed interpretation work we provide well researched performances and presentations at heritage sites as part of the visitor attraction of the site. We specialise in informal encounters with the public which encourage them to engage with the surroundings in which they find themselves in a fun and informative way. As with our other work we provide handling objects which provide a tangible link to the period presented. All our work is site specific and we are able to provide appropriate period props and handling material to compliment all the costumed presentations.

Our clients include Historic Scotland, The National Museums of Scotland, The National Trust for Scotland, Dundee Heritage Trust, the Museums Association, Tullie House, MUSA and schools across Scotland and northern England.
