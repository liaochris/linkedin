Alain Burrese, J.D.'s Summary

Alain Burrese, J.D. is a speaker, writer and mediator. Alain combines his military, martial art, and Asian experiences with his business, law, and conflict resolution education to provide programs on Staying Safe, self-defense, and effective communication for conflict resolution, negotiation, and leadership. He also teaches how to use the Warrior's Edge to Take Action and Achieve Remarkable Results. Alain is the author of Lost Conscience, the Tough Guy Wisdom series, and Hard-Won Wisdom From The School Of Hard Knocks. He's also starred the DVDs Hapkido Hoshinsul, Streetfighting Essentials, Hapkido Cane, the Lock On Joint Locking series, and Restraint, Control & Come-A-Long Techniques. Alain has also written numerous articles and reviews. You can find out much more at http://www.burrese.com and his other websites.

Specialties: High intensity speaking for businesses and organizations on safety, self-defense and effective communication. Also teaches to live with the Warrior's Edge. Safety, Self-Defense, and Hapkido instructor. Mediator and Conflict Resolution expert.

  • Campfire Tales from Hell: Musings on Martial Arts, Survival, Bouncing, and General Thug Stuff

    • CreateSpace Independent Publishing Platform
    • May 16, 2012
    Authors: Alain Burrese, J.D., Jesse Alcorta, Rory Miller, Marc MacYoung, Win Demerre, Barry Eisler, L.A. Kane, Dr. Drew Anderson

    Life out at the edges can be rough, scary and at times dangerous. Campfire Tales From Hell is a collection of essays from people who have been there, done that -- some of whom had to fight for their title of ‘survivor.’ Some are professionals, calmly going to work knowing a bad day means someone dies -- and that ‘someone’ could be them. Others came through by being smarter, more aware, better trained or just having it more together than the other 'team.' Some of the authors looked into the abyss and spit in its eye when it looked back. Still others were normal people thrown into extraordinary circumstances. Campfire Tales is not a collection of macho stories. It's a primer for what you'll need to know to make it out there, on the edge. It's what to remember when you're dealing with dangerous people and difficult situations. It's what attitudes, knowledge and perspectives you'll need to get through. It's 'what I wish someone had told me when I started.' It's how Hollywood and fiction can -- and will -- get you killed or thrown into prison. It's what you need to know to fill the holes in your self-defense or defensive tactics training. In a different direction, writers can learn from the people who actually do what they're writing about. But most of all, Campfire Tales From Hell is a collection of stories and wisdom that tells you, 'you too can get through.'

Alain Burrese, J.D.'s Projects

  • Campfire Tales From Hell

    • April 2012 to May 2012
    Team Members: Alain Burrese, J.D., Marc MacYoung, Lawrence Kane, Rory Miller, Barry Eisler, Drew Anderson

    I like to tell people that I wrote my first book for the scared seventeen year old I was. It was all the things I wished someone had told me about facing a violent and dangerous environment. Campfire Tales From Hell exceeds that.

    Campfire Tales is from people who speak from first-hand experience about what it takes to function and survive in extreme environments. Nowhere else will you find such a wide collection of topics and diverse perspectives. Essays from law enforcement officers are next to essays by ex-criminals. Articles on PTSD by a psychology Ph.D share space with an article on how to get along on a mental ward. Martial artists sit next to streetfighters. Essays on police work are next to stories about being in a foreign prison.

    This is knowledge, insight and understanding that you cannot get anywhere else. Topics covered include

    Recognizing predators
    Dealing with the police after an incident
    What the veterans know that can save a rookie's life
    Simple (and profound) truths about violence
    Dealing with trauma (physical and psychological)
    The inner workings of Martial Arts cults
    Lessons from a lifetime of martial arts
    ... and other valuable insights.

    Also included is a fiction piece where Barry Eisler’s ‘John Rain’ talks shop with me (Marc ‘Animal’ MacYoung) over scotch. The topics discussed are fiction. But it’s fiction any high level operator will understand.
