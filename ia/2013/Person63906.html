  • Cerebral Palsy Career Builders Blog

    • October 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Jim Hasse, ABC, GCDF

    As a youngster with cerebral palsy, I was lucky to have a mom who was trained as a grade school teacher and believed that nurture (career builders) had more power over nature. She is the reason I have had a meaningful work life.

    My story shows what you do today can have a lasting effect on your youngster’s future well-being. You can set the stage. You can build the foundation for a happy, independent, meaningful, productive life that your son or daughter with cerebral palsy can enjoy.

    To do so, all you need is a basic understanding of contemporary career management and how to blend those principles with some of the hard disability employment lessons I’ve learned personally through trial and error and through the experiences of other accomplished individuals with cerebral palsy.

    That’s what you’ll discover on the Cerebral Palsy Career Builders Blog.

    This blog is all about what my mom would have wanted you to know.

  • 7 TRANSFORMATION STORIES: Little Book 1 (Career-coaching Series) for Parents of Elementary Students with Cerebral Palsy

    • June 2013 to Present
    Team Members: Jim Hasse, ABC, GCDF

    In this 45-page transformation book, Jim Hasse identifies seven career builders parents can use to help their elementary student with cerebral palsy grow in self-confidence, a key factor in disability development and career readiness. Jim, a Global Career Development Facilitator (GCDF) who has athetoid cerebral palsy, illustrates those seven bits of career advice with a transformational story from his own experiences. For each short story, he defines the issue at hand for learning how to live and work well with cerebral palsy, shows how his parents and others helped him personally resolve the situation, and provides a potential path parents can follow as mentors for their own youngster. (13,000 words)

Jim Hasse, ABC, GCDF's Summary

Here are two examples of how my personal experience in coping effectively with a disability helped me to develop my planning and implementing skills.

Planning

Positioned organization to take advantage of dairy industry restructuring.

* Developed, monitored growth strategy, based on benchmark research.

* Promoted organization as first choice among dairy farmers.

* Created complete corporate marketing program.

* Planned communication strategy for 15 mergers and acquisitions.

Result:

Aligned 3,000 new dairy farmers, 300 new employees, 80 new truckers and 40 new customers with organization’s vision, values and direction.

Personal Experience:

I was an “outsider” in four different family homes during seven years of grade school.

I needed to form mutually beneficial relationships with each of my week-day “house” families while attending an orthopedic school for students with disabilities 60 miles from home.

As a result, at an early age, I learned how to win friends and influence people -- and get along with "week-day" brothers and sisters.

Implementing

Developed cross-disability online forums about disability employment issues.

* Launched online discussions about disability employment in 1997.

* Assigned, wrote, edited 1,300 rich articles about disability employment.

* Created 150 dialogue boards, weekly newsletter to generate user content.

* Developed an online course, “Online Networking as Job Search Tool.”

Result:

Compiled user-generated insight about managing a career from a disability perspective and repurposed it for recruiters in AMACOM book.

Personal Experience:

My experience in gathering and summarizing user-generated insight about disability employment issues prepared me for the opportunity to compile and edit the material for Perfectly Able: How to Attract and Hire Talented People with Disabilities (AMACOM, 2010).

Specialties

Human resource management, communication management, communication measurement, online facilitation, online instruction, career management, career coaching, disability employment, writing, editing, online publishing
