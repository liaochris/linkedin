Developer

Python

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Computer Software industry

September 1994 – December 2009 (15 years 4 months)

I first became aware of Python by using it for a class project while getting my BSc at McGill, and after a while began contributing to the documentation and to the core interpreter. Past activities:

Director

Python Software Foundation

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Computer Software industry

March 2005 – March 2008 (3 years 1 month)

I was elected as a member of the Board of Directors when I took over the organization of the PyCon conference (http://us.pycon.org), and remained a director for three terms.

PyCon was my primary PSF-related activity; I co-chaired the 2006 and 2007 conferences and did much of the work of organizing them.

I later returned for a single-year term as a director from March 2009 to March 2010. As part of this term, I inventoried and analyzed the Python contributor agreements, and carried out the voting duties of a board member.

Developer

Advanced Simulation Technology Inc.

May 2003 – June 2007 (4 years 2 months)

I worked as an R&D developer on ASTi's Telestra product line, which was a system for simulating radio and audio communication, digital aural cue and communications systems for flight simulators and other real-time simulation and training devices.

* Assisted with the software design of a new generation of ASTi's Telestra platform. The changes in the redesign included: going from a multi-file XML-based model format to a single-file S-expression format; introducing the use of an object database to store models; rewriting a GUI editor from C++/Qt using Python/GTk+; rewriting the web-based management application (~20,000 lines of Python code) from Zope into Quixote; writing new model-checking tools.

* For the original generation of the software, designed and implemented a standard library for performing system and model-related tasks, along with a comprehensive test suite (22,000 lines of Python code.)

* Helped point-of-contact engineers trace down system problems, often fixing bugs affecting them.

* Helped with system administration tasks for the corporate web and e-mail servers.

Co-chair

PyCon

Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Electrical/Electronic Manufacturing industry

March 2005 – March 2007 (2 years 1 month)

I volunteered to be the co-chair of PyCon in 2005, and was responsible for the 2006 and the 2007 conferences, both held in Dallas, TX. The 2006 conference attracted almost 400 attendees, and 2007 grew to 600 attendees. Both events were well-liked by the attendees and did not lose money for the Python Software Foundation that backed them.

In 2008 and 2009, I reduced my role to organizing the registration process, and in 2009 also ran a small one-day sub-event, a Language Summit for the core developers to informally discuss plans for the next year of Python development.

In 2009, I also gave a presentation as part of the main conference, titled "How to Give a Python Talk", that discussed how to decide upon a topic, how to prepare, and tips for better delivery. Video is available at http://pycon.blip.tv/file/1947177/.
