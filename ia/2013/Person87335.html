Marlon Torres' Summary

Self-starter working in diverse fields.
Experienced leader who plays well with others.
Speak English and Spanish fluently.
Project Management Institute certification.
Proven ability to define and drive project tasks and milestones within necessary timing.

In my former role as vice president of the modern dance ensemble Galumpha, I led regional and international marketing efforts, conducted auditions and hired new dancers. I managed tours in the United States and Latin America and collaborated with public agencies like the Office of Cultural Affairs of the U.S. Embassy in Caracas. My choreography has been showcased on stages throughout America, Europe and Asia, including the Kennedy Center in Washington, DC, the ARTE Television Network in Germany and the Singapore Arts Festival. I have created educational materials for public schools and conducted over 300 workshops at universities, including Binghamton University, Ohio State University, Vanderbilt University and Ithaca College.

As part of the A+ Schools Program of North Carolina, an active member of the International Dance Council, CID UNESCO, and a grant review panelist for the Durham Arts Council and North Carolina Arts Council, I work toward promoting a learning community that embraces and supports the arts. My experience in both in-schools and after school program implementation and evaluation has taught me that the positive impact of dance in children is measurable.

As a project manager and leader in the arts, I helped establish Crescendo Cultural, a national task force of Latino arts professionals brought together by the National Museum of Mexican Art to participate in shaping the future of the arts and envision ways to increase arts exposure in at-risk communities. I co-presented the group’s findings and recommendations at the Grantmakers in the Arts Conference in October 2012.
