Elana Hayden's Summary

An in-demand session singer in NY, Elana written and recorded numerous songs, jingles and voiceovers for radio and TV, including spots for the NBA, WNBA, Maury Povich, The Montel Show, and several other shows. Elana has appeared and/or recorded with Mark Rivera, Harry Chapin, Patti LaBelle, Bob Hope, Tony Orlando, The Pointer Sisters, Willie Nelson, Boomer Esiason, and others. She recorded a jingle for German radio that was produced by Klaus Doldinger, leader of the internationally renowned jazz-fusion band “Passport.”

Most recently, Elana did a Breakfast and Morning Show jingle for The Phil Ray UK radio show and several radio identification spots for M-Radio, a web station in Germany.

As a member of the group Q-Club, Elana sang lead on the release of “I’ll Be There.” She continued recording in the United States and Europe as the featured vocalist on Force of 2’s single “Victory is Ours” (Tommy O Records), and appeared on Teddy Kumpel’s 1994 CD, “Nome Sane” (Hooney Records). In 1997, she sang lead vocals for the group “Push” on their single for Blank Frank Records and released a cover of the ABBA song, “The Winner Takes it All.”

Elana has recorded with the bands “Stiletto” and “Phillips Head,” as well as numerous other New York-based bands and has toured Europe with the band “Renegade."

She appeared in the National Touring Company of the Off-Broadway show Beehive, and in New York productions of Gym, Carrie, Starmites and Goose.

Film and TV credits: 20th Century Fox’s Unfaithfully Yours, starring Nastasia Kinski, Armand Assante, and Dudley Moore, and the ABC-TV “After School Special,” The Accident, which aired on network television in the U.S.

Elana is the author of a children's book, is working on a CD of beautiful lullabies and is a vocal coach and instructor at Bach to Rock: America's Music School - and a mother.

Most recently, Elana is featured in Wendell Hanes' new book, "Making 30 Grand in 30 Seconds! Vol 4. - Singing on Major TV Commercials"

Specialties

Elana is co-owner of At The Helm Music, a New York based music production company co-founded with Mike Leslie.

Founding member of the Al D. Rodriguez Liver Foundation, a non-profit organization in memory of her friend Al D. Rodriguez. The ADRLF educates people about chronic viral hepatitis & liver cancer. (http://www.aldrodriguezliverfoundation.com)
