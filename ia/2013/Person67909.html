Jon Heilman's Summary

As an Art Director, Marketing Director, Graphics Production Supervisor and Graphic Designer, I have a diverse background covering the graphic arts, photographic, digital imaging and printing industries. I have handled a wide variety of design projects including corporate identity packages, magazines, print ads, catalogs, newsletters, trade show displays & collateral, web banners, 3D graphics & animation, Flash applications and html. In addition to my years of Mac expertise, I have worked as a designer in a Windows environment and MS Word and PowerPoint.

My experience covers concepting and layout right through finished production. I have worked as a one-man art department and in team environments and have excelled in both.

As Marketing Director at Firehouse, it's an exciting time as I'm leading the charge in marketing and re-branding a company that has never embraced marketing. I have restructured our brand as a retail-friendly graphics provider with a global presence. We are aggressively e-marketing, creating print collateral, producing informational videos and getting our name in front of clients like never before. The results have been very exciting to see.

Specialties

Brand concepting, corporate identity packages including brand standards guides and compliance. Favorites of mine include logo design, animation and Photoshop compositing and image manipulation and retouching. I have done extensive innovation in workflow streamlining and automation.

Graphic Designer

5MetaCom (formerly The Majestic Group) is the largest agency in Indiana specializing in marketing technical and scientific products.

At 5MetaCom, I ran the gamut from concept and layout to production and proofing. I functioned as art director on projects that fell within my specific areas of expertise such as web, html and Flash. Freelance Art Director

WoodWorks Films

2003 – 2006 (3 years)

WoodWorks was the brain child of Indianapolis native Gary Wood. Along with production partner Justin Escue, they put together a completely volunteer crew and shot their first film in the summer of 2003. The result was the award-winning Saving Star Wars. A parody about a father trying to fulfill his terminally ill son's wish to contact George Lucas and personally ask him to continue making Star Wars films. Comedy ensues as the father and his best friend "accidently" kidnap George Lucas. Dave (Darth Vader) Prowse also has a leading role.

I heard about the production on local radio and volunteered my design services. They readily accepted my offer and I was soon inundated with requests for everything from logos and business cards to multiple websites, actor photo shoots, on-location consulting and one-sheet posters.

I continued in that role for WoodWorks' second production, OPEN MIC'rs. It followed a varied group of stand-up comedians through a fictional comedy competition. The ensemble cast included Cindy (Caddyshack) Morgan, Hank McGill, Etta May and Dave Dugan. That led to the creation of a website for Cindy Morgan's Caddyshack Celebrity Golf Tournament in 2006.

Art Director

PanAngling Travel

January 1999 – September 2001 (2 years 9 months)

PanAngling was a full-service travel agency specializing in outdoor adventures. The driving force behind this 26-year-old company was Pat Galyan who blazed a trail to the top of the retail sporting goods industry with stores bearing his name. I was personally hired by Mr. Galyan to facilitate the production of all design work in-house.

During my time as Art Director at PanAngling, I revamped the graphic identity of the company to the status of an industry leader. That plan was reflected in company logos, letterhead & business cards, numerous full-color catalogs, a bi-monthly adventure newsletter, print ads, website design, trade show displays, direct mail and corporate proposals.

As the one-man art department, I took all projects from concept to completion. My responsibilities included executing multiple design projects simultaneously, writing copy, scanning photos, purchasing and maintaining hardware & software, overseeing press checks, managing and archiving graphics files and constantly working miracles to meet deadlines!

PanAngling was forced to close due to the sharp and sudden decline in travel following September 11, 2001.
