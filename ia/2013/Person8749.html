Anthony Fasano, PE's Experience

Executive Director

New York State Society of Professional Engineers

Nonprofit; 1-10 employees; Executive Office industry

November 2012 – Present (1 year) Albany, New York Area

After being a very active member of the New York State Society of Professional Engineers for more than 10 years, the opportunity to become the Executive Director came about and I jumped at the opportunity.

As the Executive Director, as I have done the past few years in other endeavors, I am focused on building a strong online presence for the organization to communicate and transmit the value that this organization provides. To summarize, among other benefits that the organization provides to engineers in the State of New York, some of the key ones include:

• Make engineering students in New York aware of the licensure process,
• Assist engineers in maintaining their license by providing affordable continuing education,
• Provide top of the line special inspections training for engineers in and around the New York City area,
• Lobby on behalf of the engineering license in the State of New York to ensure that engineers can continue to protect the health and safety of the citizens of New York,
• Maintain a blog/newsfeed to keep NYS engineers up to date on the most important engineering issues.

To find out more about the New York State Society of Professional Engineers,
visit ► www.NYSSPE.org

Engineering Career Coach

Institute for Engineering Career Development

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Professional Training & Coaching industry

February 2012 – Present (1 year 9 months) Worldwide

When I realized that many engineering companies stopped paying for career development training, I founded the Institute for Engineering Career Development (IECD) to help engineers develop their careers on their own. The IECD is an online community focused on providing affordable career coaching, training, and most importantly a forum which acts a 24-7 support community where members can chat with other motivated engineers around the world.

The IECD offers membership fees for as low as $11 per month and serves engineers of all ages and experience levels from soon to be college graduates, to management level engineering leaders. There are 2 calls per month, mostly off work hours to make it easy for members to fully participate and the online private forum is always accessible to members. Your engineering career team is waiting for you.

To check out our IECD community or some of the free career resources we have available to non-members like our blog for engineers, visit ► www.EngineeringCareerDevelopment.com

Civil Engineer ♦ Associate Partner ♦ Engineering Career Coach

Maser Consulting

Privately Held; 201-500 employees; Civil Engineering industry

July 2000 – July 2010 (10 years 1 month) West Nyack, New York

Maser Consulting acquired JC & A when I graduated college, which worked out well for me because it gave me opportunities that I wouldn’t have had at a smaller company. For example, Maser supported me in obtaining my M.S. in Civil Engineering from Columbia University.

I struggled early on in my career as many engineers do, just trying to figure out where I wanted to go in the industry and how to get there. One day, I had an epiphany and realized that most successful engineers have good technical skills and dynamic soft or interpersonal skills. From that moment on, I focused on developing my non-technical skills like communication, networking, public speaking, etc. by reading every book and taking every possible training course on these subjects.

I was promoted to an Associate Partner at the age of 27 (the youngest in the company’s history). After my promotion, the company asked me to develop seminars to give to other younger engineers and project managers to facilitate their development. The enjoyment and success of this new venture lead me to go to executive coaching school and subsequently start my own engineering career development company – Powerful Purpose Associates.

Key Take Away: Engineers develop your non-technical skills as early and often as possible if you desire a management/leadership role in your career.

Engineering Intern ♦ Field Land Surveyor

Joseph Caruso & Associates

June 1997 – June 2000 (3 years 1 month) Nanuet, New York

Throughout college I worked as an intern for a very small civil engineering company of less than 5 people.

I spent a lot of time in the field performing land-surveying services. It was through this field experience that I gained a very practical perspective of engineering, which would prove to be invaluable in my work as a design engineer.

The size of the company also gave me the opportunity to start to converse with clients at a very young age, which would help me to develop extraordinary customer service skills, which I utilize to this day in my own business.

Key Take Away: Younger engineers and students get field/practical experience as early as possible in your engineering career.
