Konstantin Brazhnik's Projects

  • Seymour Deeply

    • August 2011 to May 2012
    Team Members: Konstantin Brazhnik, Jimmy Gorham, Michael Annetta, CheWei Chang, Andres Ramirez

    eymour Deeply is the MFA thesis project of interactivity designer, Michael Annetta. The project is an immersive interactive storybook using stereoscopic 3D combined with Microsoft’s Kinect for gestural interfacing. The story of Seymour is a retelling and continuation of the Jack and the Beanstalk fable, but from Seymour’s point of view.

  • NEVERMIND Game

    • August 2011 to May 2012
    Team Members: Konstantin Brazhnik, Erin Reynolds, Andres Ramirez, Erick Moen

    NEVERMIND is Erin Reynold‘s Graduate Interactive Media Thesis Project at the USC School of Cinematic Arts. It is a psychological horror puzzle game that utilizes a heart rate sensor to make your own stress level a crucial part of the game play mechanic. You play as a “Neuroprober” who dives into the minds of patients with PTSD and helps them uncover the truth that their mind has repressed for many years. Only after confronting these hidden realities can the patients begin to feel in control of their own lives. The only catch is that you must learn to keep your calm amidst the horrors you see or the patients’ minds reject your presence.
