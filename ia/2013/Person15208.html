Robert Atkinson's Publications

  • Mystic Journey: Getting to the Heart of Your Soul's Story

    • Cosimo Books
    • 2012
    Authors: Robert Atkinson

    "In this exquisite exploration of the spiritual craft of soul-making, Robert Atkinson illumines the journey as well as the art and discipline that leads to spiritual transformation. This is as deep and profound a study that ever was of our double nature - finite and Infinite... To read this magnificent study is to remember our original birthright and to commit again to following the path leads us home to who and what we really are."
    ~ Jean Houston, Ph.D., author of "A Mythic Life," and "The Search for the Beloved"

  • Remembering 1969: Searching for the Eternal in Changing Times

    • Baha'i Publishing
    • 2008
    Authors: Robert Atkinson

    "Remembering 1969 is a real autobiography in that it is the story of a soul. It tells of incursions of the timeless and the magical as a sensitive life unfolds. I found it profound, friendly, inspiring, and nostalgic. I loved it and hope others will discover it and take heart."
    ~ Thomas Moore, author of "Care of the Soul"

  • The Beat of My Drum: An Autobiography

    • Temple University Press
    • 2005
    Authors: Robert Atkinson, with Babatunde Olatunji

    "Olatunji taught this country - white and black - the glories of West African music and dance... It is good to have this book, with his words, to tell his story more completely."
    ~ Pete Seeger

  • The Life Story Interview

    • Sage
    • 1998
    Authors: Robert Atkinson

    The standard multi-disciplinary methodology for helping people tell their complete life story, with over 200 possible questions to guide the process.

  • The Gift of Stories: Practical and Spiritual Applications of Autobiography, Life Stories, and Personal Mythmaking

    • Bergin & Garvey
    • 1995
    Authors: Robert Atkinson

    The stories we tell about ourselves are guided by cultural patterns and enduring elements. This book makes evident how the classic hero's journey represents a theme not only common to all the world's myths, but also our own lives today. It enables individuals to begin writing about the important aspects of their lives and to understand how and why the universal elements of stories we tell contribute to our continuing growth.

  • Latino Voices in New England

    • SUNY Press
    • 2009
    Authors: Robert Atkinson, with David Carey

    "The personal tone of the narratives and the compelling, engaging stories are a strong complement to scholarship about Latinos in the United States."
    ~ Daniel D. Arreola, author of "Tejano South Texas: A Mexican American Cultural Province"

  • The Teenage World: Adolescents Self-Image in Ten Countries

    • Springer
    • 1988
    Authors: Robert Atkinson, with Daniel Offer, Eric Ostrov, and Kenneth Howard

    "... this is an important book... it provides extremely interesting data and insights, and a useful understanding of the world of many teenagers in ten countries."
    ~ From the Commentary by Harry C. Triandis, University of Illinois, Champaign

  • Songs of the Open Road: The poetry of Folk Rock and the Journey of the Hero

    • Signet/New American Library
    • 1974
    Authors: Robert Atkinson

    The poetry that has transfigured the consciousness of a generation is embodied in the songs of Bob Dylan, and John Lennon, and Joni Mitchell, and Paul Simon, and many other poets who have come of age with Folk Rock. They are songs of awakening, songs of love, songs of eternity voiced with a spirit of freedom and searching - a spirit that speaks directly to a new pioneering generation out to create a new world. Here are those voices, those songs, that spirit in the finest collection of Folk Rock lyrics ever assembled.
