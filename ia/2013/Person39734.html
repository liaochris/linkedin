Edmund Tori's Publications

  • Seduction Artists, Cult Leaders & Con Men & How They Can Save Healthcare

    • Innovations In Thinking Differently Forum
    • October 9, 2012
    Authors: Edmund Tori

    A different kind of health innovation... well-intentioned learning of rapport and influence strategies from not-so-well-intentioned people like pickup artists, cult leaders, and con men.

    This talk explores the role of emotions, body language, word choice, and more on our influence skills in healthcare. If we are truly about the health of the patient, then we will communicate and connect in such a way that affords improving health and maintaining wellness.

  • Competencies & Choosing Consciousness

    • Innovations In Patient Safety Forum
    • August 12, 2010
    Authors: Edmund Tori

    The people who are best at performing tasks and procedures are not necessarily the best at teaching them. Find out why your best-performing nurse is not necessarily the best one to teach nursing students. Find out why your organization's best heart surgeon make be completely incompetent in another role. Find out why creating appropriate moments of mindfulness are are the sweet spots for health innovations and reducing medical error.

  • Bugs on Scrubs

    • GiggleMed.com, LLC
    • November 29, 2010
    Authors: Edmund Tori

    Bugs on Scrubs is a medical humor text that rhymes like a kids book, but instructs better than nursing competencies and doctor journal reviews. Infection control topics from scrubs outside to hand-washing, from eating at the nurses' station to wearing ties... it's all there. But this time as an argument between a doctor and a nurse... with an interesting (but all too familiar) twist at the end.
