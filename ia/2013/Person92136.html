Michael Nilsen's Summary

Since graduating from Virginia Polytechnic Institute and State University with a Bachelor of Architecture, Michael have been working for local design firms, architecture firms and freelancing as a designer. His expertise includes conceptual design, design development,project management, construction documents, graphic design, environmental graphics, three dimensional modeling and presentation graphics.

He excels as providing clients with unique and creative solutions to challenging design, technical and construction issues. He has worked on projects ranging in size and scope from single tenant retail build-outs (<5,000 s.f.) to regional mall renovations (1,000,000 s.f.). When not working directly on projects, he finds time to keep busy with things like graphic and presentation standards, IT issues, processes and procedures and even a bit of marketing.

At Virginia Marti, he taught two courses in Interior Design and three in Fashion Merchandising. All of the courses focused on using design software within the context of each major. He strove to teach students the computer cannot design for them, rather it is only a tool, like a pencil or paint brush, and understanding its capabilities and limitations is critical to using the computer as an effective design tool.

He is a licensed architect in Ohio, a member of the American Institute of Architects (AIA) and an National Council of Architectural Registration Boards (NCARB) certificate holder. He lives in Brecksville with my beautiful wife and adopted dog. As a proficient daydreamer and amateur inventor, he finds inspiration in architecture, music, art, graphic design, motion graphics, film, fashion design and industrial design. He believes design is an ongoing process and is a part of everything he does.
