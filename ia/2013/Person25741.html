Ross Rosenberg, M.Ed., LCPC, CADC's Summary

Since 1988, Ross has been an administrator, professional trainer, addiction specialist & psychotherapist in the mental health, social service and child welfare fields. Ross provides local, statewide, & national professional training on a variety of psychotherapy & addiction based topics, including sex & Internet addiction, Narcissism, & Codependency. He is currently a PESI seminar speaker, touring the USA with his "Emotional Manipulators & Codependents, Understanding the Attraction" training.

Ross is a Huffington Post blogger. He is the author, "The Human Magnet Syndrome: Why We Love People Who Hurt Us. Emotional Manipulators,Codependents, & their Dysfunctional Relationship. The book's website is: www.humanmagnetsyndrome.com


Ross is considered an expert in the field of Sex Addictions, Love & Internet Addictions, for which he provides comprehensive counseling services, training, & consultation. More about these services: www.Help4SexAddictions.com

Over the span of his career, Ross has provided psychotherapy & consultation services for a broad spectrum of issues including: substance abuse, addictions, codependency, ADHD, teen issues, trauma recovery, anxiety, depression & others. His services have helped his clients to heal traumatic wounds of the past, achieve emotional & personal balance. Ross's psychotherapy style has been described as solution focused, analytical, healing & educational while also being warm, intuitive, & effective.

Ross is the owner of Clinical Care Consultants, an Arlington Heights based counseling agency. CCC counselors are experts or specialists in a wide variety of counseling & addictions based services. CCC's staff are seasoned, experienced, & highly trained. Some of CCC's clinicians hold specialized certifications which enable them to understand & work with specialized populations. CCC clinicians are highly skilled & talented in individual counseling, marriage counseling / therapy & group counseling.

The Human Magnet Syndrome: Why We Love People Who Hurt Us. Codependency, Emotional Manipulators and Dysfunctional Relationships.” Available in March of 2013. The book is based on my PESI/CMI training entitled, "Emotional Manipulators and Codependents: Understanding the Attraction.” More info: www.humanmagnetsyndrome.com

Book Description:
When individuals with healthy emotional backgrounds meet, the result is a loving, reciprocal and stable relationship. However, when Codependents and Emotional Manipulators meet, they are enveloped in a magnetic and seductive “love force,” that begins like a fairytale, but later unfolds into a painful “seesaw” of love/pain and hope/disappointment. This book will help the reader make sense of the ubiquitous attraction that affects each and every person who desires to find the romantic partner of their dreams – and answers why patient, giving and selfless individuals (Codependents) are predictably attracted to self-centered, selfish and controlling partners (Emotional Manipulators). This unique, fresh and innovative relationship model will explore the traits, symptoms and origins of both Codependency and various Emotional Manipulation Disorders.

"Born in the cauldron of personal experience of suffering and healing and honed through years of professional experience, this book will help anyone understand the attractors of love and consequent suffering. I recommend it to couples who are mystified by the depth and repitition of their pain and joy and to therapists whose destiny is to help them."
~ Harville Hendrix, Ph.D., co-author with Helen LaKelly Hunt of Making Marriage Simple: Transform the Relationship you Have Into the Relationship you Want

    An article about ADHD:

    ADHD is neither a “new” mental health problem nor is it a disorder created for the purpose of personal gain or financial profit by pharmaceutical companies, the mental health field, or by the media. It is a very real behavioral and medical disorder that affects millions of people nationwide. According to the National Institute of Mental Health (NIMH), ADHD is one of the most common mental disorders in children and adolescents. According to research sponsored by NIMH, estimated the number of children with ADHD to be between 3% – 5% of the population. NIMH also estimates that 4.1 percent of adults have ADHD.
