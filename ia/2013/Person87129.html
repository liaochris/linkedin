Mark Donohue's Summary

Mr. Donohue is a serial entrepreneur. In June 2008, he was appointed Entrepreneur-in-Residence at Babson College, which offers the number-one rated entrepreneurship education. He served in this role until September 2013, when he returned his focus to partnering with CEO's to build enterprises, via his role as Managing Partner of Catalyst Insight Partners.

In January of 2011, Mr. Donohue was honored as one of the "Top 100 Thought Leaders in Trustworthy Business Behavior" by the organization Trust Across America.

In February 2013, TripAlertz, where he was CEO, was acquired by Media Market, which is a $100M online marketer. He served as President / Board member until October.

In February of 2013, he was appointed to the Board of Faria Instruments, which is a leading provider gauges/controls in the C&I and marine sectors. Faria employs 200.

In April of 2013, he was appointed Chairman of FW Telematics, which offers remote monitoring and diagnostics to convert big data into action.

In September of 2013, he was appointed Chairman of Sheffield Pharmaceuticals. Sheffield manufactures private label, contract packaging and Sheffield Brand products for partners like Walmart and CVS. The company employs 140.

In October of 2013, he as appointed a Senior Fellow in Social Innovation by Babson College's Lewis Institute.

Previously, in 2001, he was the sole founder of Expansion Capital, a pioneer in Cleantech investing, which manages $100,000,000. He has been Chairman Emeritus since April of 2008.

He has served in many nonprofit roles: Chair of Membership Committee for Social Venture Network, (the leading org of socially progressive CEO's), Trustee of Temple of Understanding (interfaith work), and committees for the Threshold Foundation (since 1995).

Mr. Donohue earned his BS, with honors, from Babson in 1988. He served on the Board of Overseers from 2002-2011. In September, 2010, he was honored by Babson with an annual "Distinguished Service to the College" award.

Mark Donohue's Publications

  • ENERNOC: TURNING ENERGY SAVINGS INTO SALES

    • ECCH & Harvard; Click header to buy the case for your teaching
    • September 15, 2010
    Authors: Mark Donohue, Mark P. Rice, Michael Lelyveld

    With respect to this teaching case study, it is used primarily in MBA Marketing & Sales Courses: EnerNOC - a clean energy company - sells energy-monitoring, management and efficiency services to utility customers, who agree to reduce consumption during peak-period emergencies in exchange for payments throughout the year. Utilities sign long term contracts with EnerNOC for delivery of 'negawatts', the reduced consumption of electricity during peak periods, as a way to avoid adding power generating capacity. EnerNOC is undergoing explosive growth and must manage the build out of its energy management system, as well as the growth and evolution of its sales force. This case can be used in a variety of courses. In an entrepreneurship course, it can be the basis for a discussion of entrepreneurial opportunities in the clean energy sector, as well as the challenges of managing rapid growth. In a marketing class, it can be used to discuss the concept of adjacent markets. It can also be used in a sales management class to stimulate a discussion on a broad range of issues such as: (1) rewards systems; (2) identification of sales skills in potential employees; (3) entry into a new market; and (4) sales training. It can be used in MBA level courses and in upper level undergraduate courses.
