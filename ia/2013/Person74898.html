Kevin Chiu's Projects

  • Photo Tourism

    • March 2006 to June 2007
    Team Members: Kevin Chiu, Noah Snavely, Andy Hou, Steven Seitz, Richard Szeliski

    Photo tourism is a system for browsing large collections of photographs in 3D. Our approach takes as input large collections of images from either personal photo collections or Internet photo sharing sites, and automatically computes each photo's viewpoint and a sparse 3D model of the scene. Our photo explorer interface enables the viewer to interactively move about the 3D space by seamlessly transitioning between photographs, based on user control.

  • Barcodes as URLs

    • September 2007 to Present
    Team Members: Kevin Chiu

    Built and released the first Hacker SDK for iPhone by pulling together several other resources into an automated installer. Used this to create the first QR code reader for iPhone before applications could officially be written for iOS. Asked by Google to use this code in production.

  • Slow Display

    • July 2010 to Present
    Team Members: Kevin Chiu, Daniel Saakes, Tyler Hutchison, Biyeun Buczyk, Naoya Koizumi, Masahiko INAMI, Ramesh Raskar

    We introduce a high resolution, low frame rate display, a ”slow display.”

    Lasers and monostable UV-reactive materials provide a programmable surface. This refreshable, high resolution display exploits the time decay of monostable materials, making it attractive in terms of cost and power requirements. Our effort to repurpose these materials involves solving underlying problems in color reproduction, day/night visibility, and optimal time sequences for updating content.

  • Spotwatcher

    • May 2009 to Present
    Team Members: Kevin Chiu

    An introduction to how normal people might use computer vision. Launched website that hooks into your webcam to provide remote motion alerts to your cellphone, twitter, email etc. Implemented in two weeks while visiting the MIT Media Lab.
