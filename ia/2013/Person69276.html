Joshua Gray's Experience

Editor in Chief

Journal of Toxicological Education

October 2013 – Present (3 months) New London, CT

The purpose of the Journal of Toxicological Education is to facilitate the distribution of high quality, peer-reviewed materials for use in the teaching of Toxicology to students of all backgrounds. For more information, see our journal's website at www.jtoxed.org.

Associate Professor of Chemistry

United States Coast Guard Academy

Government Agency; 1001-5000 employees; Military industry

July 2008 – Present (5 years 6 months) New London, CT

As a tenured faculty member in the Department of Science, I teach General Chemistry, Toxicology, Biochemistry, and Microbiology, and co-teach the Science of Terrorism. My research focuses on the role of environmental toxicants in the regulation of pancreatic beta cell health and insulin release in the context of diabetes. I also investigate the mechanism of action of the vesicant, sulfur mustard (mustard gas).

On campus, I serve as Director of the Center for Advanced Studies, an organization dedicated to the support and publicity of faculty scholarship and research at the U.S. Coast Guard Academy. I also serve as a faculty senator for the Department of Science and co-coordinate the Peer Tutor program.

Adjunct Associate Scientist

Marine Biological Laboratory

Nonprofit; 201-500 employees; Research industry

June 2008 – Present (5 years 7 months) Woods Hole, MA

Adjunct Associate Scientist in the Cellular Dynamics Program at the Marine Biological Laboratory. I work here during the summers with my collaborator Dr. Emma Heart, researching mechanisms of insulin secretion.

Adjunct Full Professor

University of Maryland University College

Educational Institution; 1001-5000 employees; Higher Education industry

September 2005 – Present (8 years 4 months) Online (Adelphi, MD)

The University of Maryland University College is a premier distance education school. As a professor in the Biotechnology Management program, I teach courses to students around the globe. Courses I teach include:
- Strategies for Interagency Cooperation, Verification, and Global Countermeasures in Biodefense
- Biological and Chemical Weapons - focuses on reading the primary literature dealing with medical countermeasures and their mechanism of action.

Assistant Professor

Rutgers University

Educational Institution; 10,001+ employees; Higher Education industry

September 2006 – June 2008 (1 year 10 months)

Assistant Professor in the Pharmacology & Toxicology Department of the Ernest Mario School of Pharmacy at Rutgers University. My research focuses on the toxicology of sulfur mustard and the development of therapies to enhance wound healing following exposure to this chemical weapon. I am also interested in redox cycling chemicals and their effects on various tissue systems including the brain (paraquat, Parkinson's Disease), lung (paraquat, pulmonary fibrosis), pancreatic islet (alloxan, diabetes), and tumor cell (menadione, various cancers).
