Martine Ehrenclou's Summary

I am an award-winning author, health writer, health care advocate and speaker. I am the author of the multiple award-winning book, The Take-Charge Patient: How You Can Get The Best Medical Care (Lemon Grove Press) and the award-winning Critical Conditions (Lemon Grove Press).
I currently write and publish articles on health, patient safety, patient advocacy, patient empowerment, patient engagement, the collaborative relationship between patients and medical providers, patient centered care, health literacy,and other health/medical related issues for magazines, newspapers, health websites, and more. I have been interviewed on TV and radio, including NBC Nightly News, ABC News, KCAL 9/CBS News, Good Day Dallas, NPR, and published in Prevention Magazine, Prevention Magazine/Australia, KevinMD, Family Circle, MORE Magazine, Woman's Day, Los Angeles Times Magazine, The Alzheimer's Foundation magazine, ParentGiving, Alliance Health Networks and more.

I lecture to health care organizations, hospitals, universities and others.Topics include patient advocacy, patient safety, patient empowerment, patient centered care, the collaborative relationship, communication strategies for medical providers and patients, patient engagement and the patient experience.

I have a masters degree in psychology from Pepperdine University.
I am currently enrolled in the Patient Advocacy Certificate program at UCLA.

Specialties: I am a health and medical writer, health care advocate and speaker specializing in medical and health issues. I write articles for newspapers, magazines, health organizations, health publications, and online health communities.
I lecture on patient advocacy, patient safety, patient empowerment, health literacy, patient centered care and the collaborative relationship between patients and medical providers.

  • The Take Charge Patient: How You Can Get The Best Medical Care

    • Lemon Grove Press
    • May 15, 2012
    Authors: Martine Ehrenclou

    Become a take-charge Patient with insider information from 200 medical professionals. The book is about how to take charge of yourself as a patient.

After eleven doctors failed to diagnose correctly, Martine found her own diagnosis and the correct surgeon to treat her. She is now pain free.
