Laura Lee Carter, M.A. Counseling Psychology's Summary

Laura is addicted to personal transformations. Her passion is teaching others how to deal with the new emotional rite of passage we now call 'midlife.' Her life purpose is to offer information, support, guidance, and inspiration to those in midlife transition.

After 25 years as an academic librarian, Laura Lee became a freelance writer and blogger with her Midlife Crisis Queen blog in 2007. There she discusses issues related to midlife emotional change. She also presents lively presentations on how healthy, normal and natural midlife change can be.

Her new book "Find Your Reason to Be Here: The Search for Meaning in Midlife" first offers an analysis of what boomers share psychologically as a generation. She then offers strategies to confront midlife change head-on, and finally become the person you want to be.

Her previous titles include: "How to Believe In Love Again: Opening to Forgiveness, Trust and Your Own Inner Wisdom", "Midlife Magic: Becoming the Person You Are Inside" along with a companion workbook. Her popular e-book: "Feel like a Loser? What To Do When You Don't Have a Clue" came out in 2010.

Laura Lee believes everyone has the power to transform their life and become the person they are inside. Midlife is the perfect time to begin to blossom into the person you always knew you wanted to be.

Specialties: Psychotherapist, Author, and Professional Speaker

Laura Lee Carter, M.A. Counseling Psychology's Experience

Writer, Speaker, Psychotherapist @ Midlife Crisis Queen

www.midlifecrisisqueen.com

January 2006 – Present (8 years) Fort Collins, Colorado Area

After 25 years as an academic librarian, I experienced a divorce at age 46, and then job loss at 49. So I started my own match-making service, met a spanking new husband, and then whipped up enough courage to begin my new career as a psychotherapist, writer, blogger and professional speaker.

In late 2007 I crowned myself the "Midlife Crisis Queen," began publishing the first of many workbooks and books, and now I have hundreds of thousands of readers on Midlife Crisis Queen, and my other popular blogs.

Through Midlife Crisis Queen magazine, I provide my own thoughts and information on all aspects of surviving the many emotional changes brought on by aging, and what I now lovingly call “midlife madness.”

With an M.A.in counseling psychology, and years of experience as a psychotherapist, I know how to help you transform your life quickly and efficiently!

Please consider a few sessions of one-on-one therapy to receive the encouragement, support and instruction you need to change your internal dialogue, your feelings about yourself, and how you see yourself and your world!
