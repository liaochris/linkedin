Ann Sachs' Summary

Since 1969 I’ve been a theatre professional, working as an actor, writer, director, business owner and mentor. I think of myself as a creative collaborator.

I graduated from Carnegie-Mellon’s Drama Department, and made my Broadway debut opposite Frank Langella in the Tony Award-winning DRACULA. I played dozens of leading roles on Broadway, Off-Broadway, and in our country's fine resident theatres in premieres of plays by Wendy Wasserstein, Jules Feiffer, Horton Foote, Gunter Grass and Eugene Ionesco.

As a member of the Ensemble Studio Theatre (EST), I collaborated with four other women to write MAMA DRAMA, a play about motherhood published by Samuel French, performed by professional and amateur groups nationwide. I am (still!) the voice of Princess Leia on the NPR Radio Series STAR WARS, which has had a cult following for 30 years.

From 1992 though 2012 I collaborated with my husband, Tony Award winning designer Roger Morgan in our company Sachs Morgan Studio: Theatre Design Specialists. The Studio designs theatre buildings (not stage sets, buildings). Studio projects range from Broadway to Hollywood, from Disney to the Kennedy Center, and just about everything in between. Our clients tell us that our projects work for theatre owners, patrons, and professionals and that we design theatres people love.

My newest venture is Theatrical Intelligence® , a system I've developed that uses the theatre production model to impact business performance, and serves as a model for collaborative leadership. It's the fun part of being smart! I’m writing a book on the subject that is taking much too long to write.

Roger and I have been personal and professional partners since 1970. Our favorite roles are as proud parents of two grown children, Abigail and Sam; parents-in-law to Dave; and as grandparents to Gabriel and Lucy.
