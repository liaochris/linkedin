Ben Harris' Summary

For over 10 years, I've worked with content strategists, interactive designers and developers to create compelling and usable online experiences. My approach to interactive design is to be mindful of the context of the user experience. How did the user get to this website or application? What device are they using to view it? Why are they here? What do we want them to accomplish before they leave? These are all things that should be considered before the words "best practices" are considered.

Once a strategy is in place, I believe what separates a memorable user experience from a forgettable one is all in the details. Subtle placement, colors and textures can make or break a design. My job is to help make every online experience an easy and memorable one for the user.

I graduated from SUNY Alfred in 1999. Prior to joining Centerline, I was designing and developing online games and promotions for Nickelodeon, Sony Pictures and Dreamworks.

In my spare time I freelance under the name 'Big Splash" (http://www.bigsplashstudios.com) and I experiment with bringing interactive design out of the computer and into the real world with interactive art using the electronics platform, Arduino.

Ben Harris' Projects

  • Google Drive Spreadsheet Animation

    • February 2012 to February 2012
    Team Members: Ben Harris

    A code experiment to see if it's possible to use Google Drive's Apps Script and PHP to render an animated gif in a Spreadsheet. Each cell of the spreadsheet is used as one pixel of the animated gif.

  • Gravy Restaurant Website

    • 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Ben Harris, Bret Kruse, Ryan Keefer, John Lane, Rachel Rooney

    A website for one of Downtown Raleigh's premier Italian American restaurants. The design strategy for Gravy Restaurant was to put their delicious food front and center. The home page serves as a full-screen gallery of their beautifully arranged dishes and the restaurant itself. This site includes a content management system so the restaurant staff are able to update site content, including menus and news, themselves. The site includes desktop and mobile versions.

  • Raleigh Typographic Map

    • 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Ben Harris

    This "typographic map" uses hand–drawn typography to showcase the historic neighborhoods of Raleigh, North Carolina. The name of each neighborhood is drawn using the boundaries of the neighborhood as a guide.

    A few weeks after printing the first run, the city of Raleigh got wind of the design and a representative from the city's Public Affairs department contacted me to order a custom run of 30 prints to give away as awards to people who perform services for the city.

  • Ramones Screenprint

    • 2010 to Present
    Team Members: Ben Harris

    A 2 color screen print depicting a scene from the Ramones song, "53rd and 3rd." I was inspired by the punk-comic style that is well known to Ramones fans. I believe this style is the perfect compliment to the high contrast aestetic of their music.

    I designed, illustrated and printed these myself. The prints were featured in the grand opening of a music art gallery in Raleigh, North Carolina.
