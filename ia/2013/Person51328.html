Haley Elkins' Summary

Haley's management style is a blend of bravery, kindness, optimism, creative thinking, and old-fashioned sweat. She blames her West Texas roots (which grow deep) for her agrarian work-ethic: rise with the sun and work until everything is done, rain or shine. She had parents who encouraged her early on to make a difference, and she proudly adheres to the Girl Scout Law of "leaving the world a better place than you found it."

Much of Haley's life is wrapped around text, either reading or writing. She is one of those women who will read anything: philosophy, biographies, satire, poetry, magazines, journals, or the back labels of household products.

When she plays those "describe-yourself-in-one-word" games, she always picks "writer, " although writing picked her - not the other way around.

She has a substantial body of essays on feminism, etiquette, and entertaining. When she writes fiction, she focuses on turn-of-the-century New York City. (A contemporary fiction professor in college once told her that most writers carry a map of New York around in their head, even if they've never been there, and she finds this to be true.)
