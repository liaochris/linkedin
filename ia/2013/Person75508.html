Kieron Smith's Experience

Managing Director

The Book Depository

Public Company; 201-500 employees; Retail industry

August 2008 – Present (5 years) Windsor

The Book Depository is the UK's largest dedicated online bookseller - striving to offer the largest range of titles in the world.

Founded in 2004 to make 'All Books To All' we focus on selling 'less of more' rather than 'more of less', differentiating ourselves from other retailers who increasingly focus on bestsellers.

Having forged unique publisher and supplier relationships, we have the ability to dispatch over 9 million titles within 48 hours, with free worldwide delivery, enabling customers to find the books they want.

Kieron has led the strategic direction of the business, developed the brand and key supplier relationships, global pricing management, customer proposition (& service), digital marketing, design and overseen customer oriented web development.

The Book Depository won both the British Book Awards - Direct Bookseller of the Year 2009 and The Bookseller Retail Awards - Direct to Consumer Bookseller of the Year 2009 and 2010. In addition the company is listed in the Sunday Times Fast Track 100, and won a Queen's Award For Enterprise - International Trade in 2010.

Turning over in excess of a hundred million pounds sterling, The Book Depository was acquired by Amazon.com in November 2011.

Down Syndrome Education International has been at the heart of educational research and support for young people with Down syndrome for the last 30 years.       • Politics
      June 2012 – present (1 year 2 months)

      The main objectives and purpose of the All Party Parliamentary Group on Down Syndrome is to raise awareness of issues affecting people with Down syndrome, and the prospects for improving life outcomes; campaign for changes to government policy to improve the lives of people with Down Syndrome. This is particularly relevant, as Down syndrome is a common human condition affecting one in every 860 babies born in the UK today. Furthermore, Down syndrome research in the UK is very limited, with current public research funding wholly insufficient to deliver improvements and is disproportionately low compared to funding for other conditions.

  • The Politics of Down Syndrome

    • Zero Books
    • September 30, 2011
    Authors: Kieron Smith

    "This is a timely book that is as important as it is unusual. The 'problems' of people with Down syndrome are often discussed - less so those of the world they find themselves in. Political decisions that profoundly affect (or even prematurely end) the lives of people with Down syndrome are taken by policymakers with little knowledge of the condition and almost always without asking people with Down syndrome what they think. This book highlights many of the prejudices behind these decisions, and many of their consequences. In so doing, it provokes a debate that is urgently needed - one that is not just about Down syndrome but about human differences, human diversity and the defence of individual human rights." Frank Buckley, CEO Down Syndrome Education International
