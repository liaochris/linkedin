Blair Barnhardt, APM's Summary

70,000 miles a year in RV. An expert in pavement management, distress evaluation, preservation, asphalt recycling, MicroPAVER, StreetSaver pavement evaluation, and asset management. I design and deliver online university certificate programs on pavement management, in-place asphalt recycling and pavement preservation for Auburn University, University of Kansas and UC Berkeley, NWETC and IPMA Academy. When not teaching, our TBG crews are performing expert pavement management training and services for agencies across the Country.

With Pacific and Atlantic Divisions, I fly across the country to teach APWA, LTAP & NHI Classes on Asphalt Recycling, Pavement Preservation and Pavement Evaluation to State DOTs and local agencies.

Past experience has seen me generate 11.5 million in revenue bidding hundreds of millions worth of asphalt recycling, soil stabilization, concrete and asphalt paving work for medium sized paving firms. I am one of few that has worked for CP, CIR, HIR and FDR firms in one lifetime. Proud members of ARRA, ISSA, AAPT, APWA, ASCE, Charter Members of IPMA and a Founders Circle Lifetime Member of PRRC.

On the municipal side, I also bid, managed, and administered the set up of the complete public works crews for three privatized cities, Sandy Springs, Milton and Johns Creek for our client at that time, CH2MHill.

I have taught college level classes for 17 years and spent the last 6 years with colleagues from Asphalt Institute and Applied Pavement Technology teaching the NHI/ FHWA Asphalt Pavement Recycling Technologies and Pavement Preservation workshops to State DOTs from AK to CA to DC, and FL and everywhere in between.

In class we teach State DOTs CIR, FDR, HIR, slurry, micro surfacing, thin overlays and ultra-thin overlays in detail. The Barnhardt Group has performed comprehensive MicroPAVER and/or StreetSaver pavement evaluation surveys for high end gated HOAs, as well as US Army Corp. and countless cities and counties across North America.

Specialties

MicroPAVER & StreetSaver Pavement distress evaluation, KU, Auburn, UC Berkeley University Course Design & Delivery, Pavement Preservation Education, Asphalt Recycling Education, Full Depth Reclamation (FDR), Cold in Place Recycling (CIP), Hot in Place Recycling, (HIR), slurry & micro surfacing, concrete, asphalt paving, education, consulting, internationally published author, seminar speaker, Micro Paver StreetSaver Pavement Management, Retroreflectivity, University Professor.

About

The Pavement Recycling and Reclaiming Center (PRRC) was established at CAL POLY POMONA in August 2010. The Center is a partnership between agencies, academia and industry. Seed funding for the Center was provided by the California Department of Transportation (Caltrans) through a $1million, three-year contract.

Mission

The PRR Center is dedicated to the advancement of pavement recycling and reclaiming technologies through applied research, training and technology transfer. The purpose of the PRRC is to provide pavement design professionals and public agencies with the knowledge and tools necessary to use pavement recycling and reclaiming as a feasible and competitive alternative to traditional pavement maintenance and rehabilitation strategies.

Conitnuing Education Professor Certificate Program

University of Kansas

Educational Institution; 10,001+ employees; Higher Education industry

October 2009 – 2012 (3 years) Lawrence, Kansas Area

The Asphalt Pavement Management, Recycling & Preservation Online Certificate Program consists of three modules covering pavement evaluation, management, recycling and preservation. Those who are faced with the challenges of maintaining roadways on a shrinking budget will learn cost and energy—efficient strategies to extend the useful service life of pavement. The instructor, Blair Barnhardt, has spent over 30 years building roads and implementing the technologies presented in this class. He has taught his innovative asphalt pavement recycling and preservation procedures to thousands of agency engineers, resulting in millions of dollars in savings.

Participants in this training program will learn:
• to evaluate pavement distress and recommend repair strategies
•to use techniques such as crack sealing/filling, patching, chip seals, slurry seals, microsurfacing, thin overlays
•to couple the pavement management program to the recycling and preservation components
• to identify strategies to get more years of service from newer pavements

The learner will be awarded 96 PDH credits upon completion of this certificate program
