Colleen Zea, CEDS's Summary

Technology and the law are, and forever will be, intertwined. Thus, evaluating early and often how technology plays its part in various legal scenarios is imperative.

In an effort to bridge the gap, I work with a computer forensic specialist. Together, we assist with the exploration of viable legal arguments and defenses by comparing the digital data to the factual scenario. Our teamwork approach with lawyers that have hired us has found success in cases ranging from criminal defense to family law to employment law and practically everything in between.

In addition, the increase in technology has a direct influence on what may be discoverable in a legal matter. To better serve our clients that need assistance with any part of the electronic discovery process, I have obtained the designation of Certified E-Discovery Specialist (CEDS) by passing the CEDS certification examination covering 15 major areas of electronic discovery and accumulating numerous hours of continued education focused on different aspects of electronic discovery.

My career goals can be summed up by following objective: to have extensive knowledge regarding technology and the law that is indispensable to the success of fellow lawyers that seek my assistance.
