Hari Khalsa's Summary

Hari Kaur Khalsa,BSN,MSN,CNP is a family nurse practitioner with 30 years experience in the healthcare system. She received her NP degree from the University of New Mexico, Albuquerque.

She has worked in a variety of settings, from hospitals to holistic practices to rural settings overseas.

She has worked in the area of HIV/AIDS, community and global health, and family practice. She was co-founder of Southwest AIDS Care in Santa Fe ,NM treating patients in northern New Mexico. She worked with other AIDS organizations in the late 1990’s including UNAIDS in bringing HIV antiretrovirals to the third world. She was a delegate to the UNAIDS conference and General Assembly.

She switched to family practice in 2000. She started Healthcare Whisperer, a patient advocacy and healthcare navigation business, in 2007 after watching the frustration of her clients manage the changes in the healthcare system and the complexities each change created. In her years of practice, she has developed an extensive network of medical practitioners and care centers. She is certified from Harvard Medical School Continuing Education in medical acupuncture. She has studied Kundalini Yoga and meditation for 38 years. Her goal in all her years of practice has been to help her patients achieve optimal healing.

She lives in Massachusetts with her husband and is an avid cyclist.

Specialties

One of her specialties is working with insurance companies with refusal and grievances. She has an excellent success rate in negotiating and reducing bills, securing positive grievance results and reversing denial of care.

Hari Khalsa's Experience

Owner

Healthcare Whisperer

Sole Proprietorship; 1-10 employees; Hospital & Health Care industry

January 2007 – Present (6 years 9 months)

Healthcare Whisperer was founded in 2007 by Hari Khalsa, MSN,C-NP with the notion that everyone deserved quality, accessible and equal care. Her experience in HIV/AIDS and family practice had opened her eyes to the frustrations and suffering her patients experienced when trying to navigate the healthcare system. She was frustrated with her patients waiting weeks for proper radiological testing, biopsy or specialty appointments. She started advocating and spending more time on the phone helping her patients with whatever was needed from insurance problems to securing timely appointments. It was difficult to see twenty five to thirty patients a day and make the proper phone calls. She also noticed when her patients were sent to specialists for acute or chronic problems like cancer or diabetes, there was no coordination of care. She felt no one was looking out for the patient’s best interest. The tipping point came when one of her patient’s required a CT Scan for abdominal pain. After fighting with the insurance company for prior approval, the test was done. The result was astounding. This person had not just a bowel infection but a mass on the kidneys and an aortic aneurysm. With this information several specialists came into play but nobody was talking to each other and the patient felt lost. She began coordinating the care and being a patient advocate. Several months later she left family practice to start Healthcare Whisperer.

Since starting Healthcare Whisperer, she has continued to help people navigate the system. She has worked with hospitals, doctors, specialists, nursing homes and pharmacies. There is no area of health and wellness off limits at Healthcare Whisperer. She is passionate about helping her patients receive optimal health in whatever situation they find themselves. The Healthcare Whisperer slogan is “Your Champion for Healthcare Solutions”.
