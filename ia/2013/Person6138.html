Ana Livingston Mural Fine Artist's Summary

I am a self-taught artist with over 30 years of experience in the arts.

At the start of my career—at age six—I created framed works which were exhibited in various fairs and festivals throughout the Pacific Northwest region, receiving multiple Best of Show, Grand Champion and First Place awards.

My works in oil on canvas, watercolor, acrylic, and charcoal can be found in private collections across the country.

Additionally, I have solely, or on a collaborative basis, completed close to a dozen murals between Clearwater, Florida and Seattle, Washington.

I have, as a strong basic purpose, to actively contribute to a new Artistic Renaissance on Earth.

Specialties: I am a fine arts artists and muralist. I specialize in oil on canvas works, and interior and exterior murals and their restoration.

    he Making of the Clearwater Mural - Part 1
    by Fine Artist, Ana Livingston

    September 3rd, 2012


    The 55-foot long wall stood barren at Honka Automotive for years. It was painted a stark white. Randy Meyers, the shop owner, decided to change all that.

    He knew of my history as a fine artist, as well as my mural restoration projects in downtown Clearwater. I was contacted and assigned a mission: to give our city something special. Over the years I had formulated an idea of what this wall should be: bright, playful, and able to transmit just the right message.


    I tasked the creation of the computer design to my son, Ian Phoenix, local graphic and web design extraordinaire. Ian currently multi-tasks as the Creative Director at Visual Edge Design here in Clearwater, as well as running his own successful original design t-shirt & accessories company, Shadefyre. I knew he could create exactly what I had in mind.

    The concept he came up with exceeded both Randy's and my imagination. This would be THE most colorful and vibrant mural in this area. Perfect.

    After receiving design approval from the City of Clearwater, my first order of business was to take this concept (8.5”x11”) and translate it onto a 55-foot wall, all the while keeping everything in proportion. The sketching phase took four mornings.

    From there I was armed with nothing more than scaffolding, my paint and brushes, some skill, and a whole lot of fortitude. When it comes to spending 4-6 hours a day painting outdoors in subtropical Florida in the summertime...well, you soon learn a deeper meaning of the word “endurance”.

    Beyond the infinite joy of creation for any artist there does exist that facet of perseverance. I wrote a post on just that during this mural project: “Exterior Mural Creation: An Article on Endurance”. But these factors, quite simply, are nothing more than small, negative aspects of the mural-painting game; the goal is to overcome them and actualize your vision.

    • The Comm Line Magazine, Los Angeles

    Authors: Ana Livingston Mural Fine Artist, Sally Heath, Editor, The Comm Line Magazine - Los Angeles

    The Making of the Clearwater Mural - Part II
    by Fine Artist, Ana Livingston

    September 3rd, 2012


    Over the course of 45 days, and in spite of two tropical storms, I brought our concept to life. I wrote daily chronicle posts on my online blog, as well as posting day-to-day evolution photos on my websites.


    We received an incredible influx of phone calls, emails, and blog posts about how the mural was so uplifting and appreciated—all in addition to people with broad smiles taking the time to stop by when they saw me up on scaffolding, or shouting from their cars as they passed by: “It's beautiful!” and “Thank you!”.

    In Clearwater's East Gateway District, where Honka Automotive is located, many hundreds of passersby are treated daily to this radiant scene as they cross over Missouri Street in their travels east. The mural depicts a landscape of rich hues that features the famous Memorial Causeway bridge out to Clearwater Beach, a group of seagulls flying high, and everyone's favorite: a mother and baby dolphin frolicking in the waves.

    This is our vibrant gift to Clearwater!

    I had someone ask me yesterday if I learn things from my projects. Absolutely. Above all I gain a broader understanding of myself, aesthetics, and the human spirit. I acquire a deeper discernment of all the good in others, in myself, and how all the beautiful effects we create in the world are reflected and magnified in others.

    What could be a grander lesson than that?

    ~Ana

    The Clearwater Mural by Fine Artist, Ana Livingston

    Over the course of 45 days, and even through the threat of two tropical storms, local fine artist and muralist, Ana Livingston, brought to life Clearwater's most intensely-vibrant mural to date.

    The west-facing wall is located at Honka Automotive (1266 Court Street) in the East Gateway District, and many hundreds of passersby are treated daily to a brilliant, whimsical scene as they cross over Missouri Street in their travels east.

    The original design concept was the brainchild of local graphic and web design extraordinaire, Ian Phoenix. Ian also runs his own successful original design t-shirt & accessories company, Shadefyre.

    Armed with nothing more than scaffolding, paint, her brushes, and talent, she's brought Ian's concept to life as a broad landscape of rich hues that portrays the famous Memorial Causeway bridge, seagulls flying high, and everyone's favorite: a mother and baby dolphin frolicking in the waves.

    Bring the kids and stop by for a closer look—it's her beautiful gift to Clearwater!
