Diandra Archibald's Summary

The Immigrant’s Journal Legal & Educational Fund, ( IJLEF), Inc., through its founder, attorney Brian Figeroux, began its history by assisting immigrants to the United States with their citizenship applications (deportation inoculation), free immigration consultations and free seminars on immigration issues throughout New York City, in 1996. These free services were important, because of the anti-immigrant climate that was prevailing at that time and the passage of IRAIRA in Congress, a law curtailing the rights of immigrants in the US. Additionally, the anti-immigrant rhetoric espoused by various individuals and organizations that opposed the increased influx of Latinos and Blacks immigrating to the United States, then and now, did not help.

To this day The Immigrant’s Journal continues to combat anti-immigrant laws, and signs of injustice in the immigrant community through our membership program. Membership benefits now include:

Membership ID, Discount on Tax Returns
Free assistance with Citizenship Applications, Tax ID Application, Opening a Bank Account, Visa Lottery Annual assistance, as well as Free Individual Membership with NACC (New American Chamber of Commerce)
FreeConsultations on unlimited topics, legal, financial or business conditions, 24/7 Emergency Assistance (Leave a message, we will get back to you as soon as possible)
After-hours legal consultation for covered legal emergencies such as: if you're arrested or detained, if you're seriously injured, if you're served with a warrant, or if the state tries to take your child(ren).
Legal Letters and Phone Calls on Your Behalf
Available at the discretion of Figeroux & Associates
Up to three per year on your behalf (up to two for the same legal subject matter)
Legal Document Review (up to 10 pages each)
Contracts/documents up to 10 pages each
Standard Will Preparation
Standard Will with yearly reviews/updates
Available to covered family members at no additional charge
