Marc Boucher's Summary

I'm an entrepreneur, technologist, programmer, writer and publisher and have been online and developing content since 1991.

Currently I'm CEO at @SpaceRef, a new media company focused on the space systems sector.

My first online service was SportsWorld BBS (1992), a bulletin board system. Some of you might remember those those ancient 2400 baud modems using dial-in systems of the early 90's. SportsWorld morphed into aTerra Technologies Corporation. After that I went on to create Maple Square Inc. - Canada's Internet Directory (1996), which I sold, I then co-founded SpaceRef Interactive Inc. (1999). My most recent endeavour was Hyperix (2007).

I have also help build several non-profit organizations including the Mars Institute (2002) and the Canadian Space Commerce Association (2007) for which I serve as a director.

I started developing online content in 1991 and honed my software development skills in the 90's which has served my businesses well since. I still program almost daily along with my other responsibilities listed below.

Through my 14 years of working within the space sector and with my natural analytical skills I've also become a space policy analyst able to interpret current policy primarily in the U.S. and Canada and with enough data other regions as well. I've made media appearances on CBC Radio, CTV, CNN (Web), the Space Show, in the Globe and Mail, MSNBC, USA Today and other outlets.
