Executive Director & CEO

National Court Reporters Association

December 1998 – June 2012 (13 years 7 months) Vienna, Virginia

Chief executive officer of this 23,000, member, professional society and related 501(c)(3) foundation, with combined assets of $10.7 million; $9.9 million in annual revenues and 40 person staff.

Key NCRA Accomplishments:

Modernization: Brought NCRA's signature programs, including publications, education delivery, certifications, advocacy, communications, governance and professional networking, into the digital age through aggressive and innovative online strategies.

Rebranding/Repositioning: Created and facilitated a membership-wide dialogue process to bridge deeply divisive controversies within a closely divided membership over the future direction of the profession and role of NCRA.

Certifications: Maintained and expanded NCRA’s multi-level, multi-discipline professional certification programs, which include applied skill and written knowledge tests and are the basis for professional licensure in more than 30 states

Schools and Education: Expanded NCRA's national level school certification program and secured Federal funding for education system improvements.

Business and Financial Model: Consistently developed and implemented balanced budgets that continued valued programs and initiatives while expanding and introducing needed new products and services, even during periods of economic recession.

Maintained a strong balance sheet and increased level of reserves, preserving the association’s capacity to respond quickly to unexpected events.

Executed innovative new business models and approaches to member service and delivery.

High Performance Staff: Created staff development, performance management and performance reward systems to attract, retain, empower and motivate staff, achieving high levels of productivity, effectiveness and strategic focus.

International: Established NCRA as a major force in the International Federation for Information Management (INTERSTENO).

Senior Vice President, Industry Affairs/Acting President

Personal Communications Industry Association (PCIA)

1991 – 1998 (7 years) Washington DC/Alexandria, Virgina

$20.8 million, 110 person staff trade association, representing the wireless industries. Held direct responsibility for Government Relations team of 15 and annual budget of $4.2 million. Served the needs of six autonomous membership sections.

Key PCIA Accomplishments

Directed the association’s advocacy and industry development activities and successfully:

1) built consensus across a diverse membership with directly conflicting, individual interests over legislative, regulatory and legal policy, strategies and tactics; and

2) achieved the regulatory, legislative, judicial and international standards outcomes needed to create the business environment that allowed for the introduction of ubiquitous, affordable wireless telephone and texting services in the United States.

As Acting President, successfully concluded negotiations for and implemented a merger of PCIA with a related organization, insuring the continued viability and growth of an association able to support the needs of both legacy constituencies.

Identified new, emerging industry segments, created membership sections within the association to represent them, and successfully recruited key industry players.

When members faced a need for specialized engineering information and technical support services in order to achieve market growth:
- identified the opportunity,
- performed initial business planning,
- secured necessary regulatory approvals, and
- successfully launched service operations that both created a new revenue stream for the association and supported members in achieving their business objectives.

Appointed to several Federal Advisory Groups, including those on year 2000 conversion (Y2K), implementation of Telecommunications Reform Act provisions governing access to telecommunications services by persons with disabilities, management of the North American numbering plan, and increasing the reliability of the nation's telecommunications networks.

Vice President

Association of Telemessaging Services International (ATSI)

Initiated research and development activities and improved educational support for the industry, as it coped with fundamental changes in technology, service capabilities and the competitive environment

  • "7 Measures of Success: What Remarkable Associations Do That Others Don't"

    • 2003 to 2006
    Team Members: Mark Golden

    As chair of the ASAE Foundation's Research Committee, I served on the task forced that designed and conducted the research reviewed and analyzed the data, and prepared the conclusions in this landmark study, conducted in cooperation with Jim Collins and applying his research methodology from "Good to Great" to the association sector. Based on 15 years of data and original, objective research tailored to the association community's needs "7 Measures of Success" provides empirical data and seven success factors common among visionary nonprofits.

    Upon completion of the study, I made the initial presentation of its findings during the opening general session of ASAE's Boston convention in 2006, and went on to conduct numerous speeches and workshops on the study for ASAE member organizations (the Pittsburgh Society of Association Executives, CFP Institute, Pittsburgh Technology Council, Radiological Society of North America, Society for Neuroscience, National Association of Secondary School Principals, National Association of Elementary School Principals, among others) and at various ASAE conferences.
