Jeff Allen's Summary

I believe that regardless of the product you peddle your success will always be proportional to the combined strength of three aspects of your business - 1) your ability to deliver the market a competitive and quality product; 2) your ability to communicate and differentiate the value of your product to the market; and 3) your ability to quickly empower sellers to tailor your message to the unique needs of every individual prospect. In short, if your product development, marketing and sales houses are not order, you will underachieve or fail.

Like a lot of people, I began my career in high-tech in sales. Recognizing that the tools I needed weren't the tools I had, I transitioned into a series of marketing roles. Upon discovering disconnects between what my customers wanted me to sell them and what I had to sell them, I undertook a series of product development roles. Each time I've changed roles, it's been because I discovered (and in many cases the management of the organization I was in saw) that one of the three components of this formula was lacking.

Having gone through this cycle multiple times, including a complete revolution at Novell, I consider myself a "migrant marketer" - or someone who can be comfortable and successful whether they're developing, marketing or selling their wares.

From that perspective, I believe the most valuable things I bring to my employer are:

- I'm a highly-adaptable, versatile marketing professional - I have experience performing nearly every function under the marketing umbrella as a top-performer

- I am a skilled communicator and am able to quickly assimilate new, technical concepts, and present them within the context of higher-level strategies

- I understand by experience how to navigate and maximize the resources of the marketing, product management and sales organizations in large and small companies

- I am a hard-working, professional business person who's always looking for new challenges
