Henry de La Garza's Summary

Mr. de La Garza has served as a public information officer for the responsible party at maritime and energy oil spill incidents, including the only US Spill Incident of National Significance. His career spans more than 40 years as an award winning television news reporter and news producer, a trouble shooter and public spokesman for AT&T, and the director of communications and press secretary for the Mayors of Houston and El Paso. He served as a media advisor to the 1990 Economic Summit of Industrialized Nations.

He serves as the Public Information Officer for American harbor pilots that are responsible for safely guiding ships and vessels in some of the most congested ports in the United States. He enjoys connections with foreign harbor pilots. As The Pilots' PIO, he oversees media and public communications as well as issues management.

Mr. de La Garza is a FEMA certified National Incident Management System Public Information Officer (IS-00702.a) qualified to effectively manage public information at any incident, regardless of size and complexity of the situation or the number of entities involved in the response. He also is certified by FEMA in the National Incident Management System (IS-00700.a NIMS) and Social Media in Emergency Management (IS-00042). He presents communications lectures to harbor pilots.

On September 6, 2010, he completed a 125-day assignment as a PIO and spokesman for BP and the Unified Command response to the Deepwater Horizon oil spill. His crisis assignments include three of the worst oil spills (Cosco Busan – 2007, Eagle Otome - 2010, and Mississippi Canyon 252 - 2010) in the United States since the Exxon Valdez.

A '71 graduate, he is an Alumni Ambassador for the University of Texas at El Paso. He serves on the Board of Directors for the UTEP Alumni Association and was appointed by the President to the UTEP Centennial Celebration Planning Committee. He delivered the Commence Day Address to the 2007 UTEP Graduating Class.
