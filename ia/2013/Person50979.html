Gus Murray (@ideasoutloud)'s Projects

  • Maersk Group Social Media Strategy

    • September 2011 to Present
    Team Members: Gus Murray (@ideasoutloud), Mads Bøcher Brandt

    Our mission: not simply to generate Likes, but to turn conversations into qualified candidates, while generating socially-driven brand exposure.

    By designing a digital ecosystem that fostered dynamic dialogue and social participation around the brand, Maersk became one of the leading digital brands in the B2B sector. The Maersk Group Facebook Page functioned as the main hub for content and conversations, and tied in more than 12 social media profiles and microsites. This helped people discover the organization behind the Maersk brand.

    As an ‘always-on’ strategy, we mixed branded and fan-generated content to keep communication lively, relevant, and true to the Maersk culture and personality. As conversations evolved, our Facebook Page gained more than 172,000 engaged users per month. Several Facebook apps reinforced the user experience by allowing people to interact with new projects, engage in challenges, receive career advice, and apply for positions within Maersk.

    One year after the campaign was launched in October 2011, the strategic objectives have been met and exceeded several times. By the end of 2012, the Maersk Facebook Page had grown into a community of more than 700,000 fans and the Maersk brand was exposed to millions of stakeholders around the world.

    Social media has provided Maersk with multiple ways of sharing its story, and now there are over 172,000 unique interactions a month on Maersk's social platforms, generated largely by its Facebook fans.

    Of the 15 key markets that were the focus of the recruitment effort, the number of candidates has increased by 116%. 87% of visitors to the Facebook Page click on the Job tab.

    You can see more here: http://mrsk.co/XaoFie
