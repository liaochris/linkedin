Rob Reed's Summary

Entrepreneur with an emphasis on product development, marketing, strategy, and business development. More than 15 years of startup experience in media, technology, and alternative energy. Served as corporate spokesperson for public and private companies. Wrote a book on the history of snowboarding and worked with Julia Roberts, Willie Nelson, and Morgan Freeman on promoting clean, renewable energy.

Specialties

All-around entrepreneur

Rob Reed's Experience

Founder

MomentFeed

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Internet industry

March 2010 – Present (3 years 9 months)

MomentFeed is the first social marketing platform built for the local opportunity. The MomentFeed solution enables large brands to manage Facebook, Foursquare, Instagram, and Twitter across hundreds or thousands of locations from a central resource.

Founder

Max Gladwell

April 2008 – January 2011 (2 years 10 months)

MaxGladwell.com is an independent blog on social media strategy, sustainability, and social entrepreneurship. Rob is the visionary and sole author of Max Gladwell, which has become synonymous with the nexus of social media and green living.

In 2009, Rob pioneered a new, distributed style of publishing, where an article is posted simultaneously across multiple blogs. His post on "10 Ways to Change the World Through Social Media" was published on more than 85 blogs including Mashable, TreeHugger, and The Huffington Post and occupied two spots among Twitter's trending topics.

Rob also initiated the #EcoMonday Twitter meme and is ranked among Cision's "Top 100 Social Media & Internet Marketing Bloggers."

VP of Marketing and Government Relations

Zumbox

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Internet industry

January 2009 – March 2010 (1 year 3 months)

Developed Zumbox positioning as the first paperless postal system and "paperless mail" as a new product and product category.

Developed and lead the company's highly successful government (B2G) strategy. Launched Zumbox in in the Cities of San Francisco, Newark, New York, and Minneapolis with key support and participation from Mayors Newsom, Booker, Bloomberg, and Rybak respectively.

SVP of Marketing

Earth Biofuels

Public Company; 11-50 employees; Oil & Energy industry

March 2005 – January 2008 (2 years 11 months)

Composed original business & marketing plans. Served as key adviser to the CEO. Saw company through a reverse merger and initial public offering (IPO) on the OTCBB. Developed and marketed Willie Nelson's "BioWillie" biodiesel brand. Hired and managed PR/IR firms for corporate, brand, and crisis management. Served as corporate spokesperson. Lauched "clean school bus" cause marketing campaign. Recruited actress Julia Roberts to chair the company's advisory board and serve as spokesperson. Succeeded in raising the company's market cap by $200 million and earned a feature on the Oprah Winfrey Show.

Founder

Reed Content (Sole Proprietorship)

April 1999 – March 2005 (6 years)

Producer and distributor of multi-media content in the adventure-travel, action-sports, celebrity lifestyle, and automotive categories. Webcasting production company.

Aggregated and signed more than 200 independent content producers/ owners to revenue-sharing contracts for content syndication. Built a top-notch video production team.

Produced live-via-satellite webcasting events for Yahoo! Broadcast.

Wrote the definitive book on the history and people of snowboarding, "The Way of the Snowboarder", published by Harry Abrams Books (2005).

Worked on assignment for leading national magazines: National Geographic Adventure, Men’s Journal, Outside, Men’s Fitness, Stuff, SKI, Complex, and others.

Editor

Bike Magazine

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Publishing industry

September 1997 – April 1999 (1 year 8 months)

The preeminent magazine of mountain biking. Also served as Senior Editor of Powder Magazine (skiing).
