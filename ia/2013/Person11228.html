Axel Leblois' Experience

President and Executive Director

G3ict - The Global Initiative for Inclusive ICTs

Nonprofit; 11-50 employees; Nonprofit Organization Management industry

October 2006 – Present (7 years)

G3ict is a public-private partnership dedicated to facilitating the implementation around the world of the Digital Accessibility Agenda defined by the Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities, one of the fastest ever negotiated Human Rights treaties. Its Article 9 creates the first universal framework for accessible Information and Communication Technologies - ICTs - and assistive technologies, benefiting an estimated 650 million persons living with disabilities worldwide. G3ict’s participants include policy makers and public institutions, ICT industries and the private sector, international standards development organizations, associations of persons with disabilities, and academia.

Its main activities are to:
• Raise awareness on effective public policies, private sector initiatives
• Facilitate the sharing of solutions and good practices
• Promote harmonization and standardization
• Support policy makers with capacity building programs and benchmarking.

Founder and Trustee

CIFAL Atlanta (United Nations)

2004 – Present (9 years)

Introduced UNITAR, the United Nations Institute for Training and research, to the City of Atlanta and the State of Georgia, conducted negotiations and started up CIFAL Atlanta with the support of Hemisphere, Inc., a public-private partnerhip promoting Atlanta as an international center. Developed capacity building programs for local government officials responsible for the largest urban areas of the Americas. Topics covered economic and social development issues most critical in the Americas, such as fighting AIDS in inner city environments, managing water resources, developing landfill methane production programs, leveraging airports or academic centers for economic development and managing immigrant populations.

Participants included mayors, governors, senators, city administrators and other locally elected officials. Over 4,000 government officials have benefited fro CIFAL Atlanta programs since inception.

Conducted all fund raising activities from 2004 till 2006.

President

W2i, the Wireless Internet Institute

February 2001 – Present (12 years 8 months)

W2i was spun-off from World Times, Inc. in 2001 to conduct capacity building program for public sector executives involved in the deployment of broadband wireless infrastructure. W2i has done extensive work for the United Nations ICT Task Force, and produced programs in cooperation with InfoDev (World Bank), Intel, IBM, Motorola and a number of local governments.

W2i assisted UNITAR, the United Nations Institute for Training and Research, to launch its North American affiliate CIFAL Atlanta which I ran as pro-bono executive director from 2004 till 2006.

In 2006, W2i started G3ict, the Global Initiative for Inclusive ICTs on behalf of the United Nations Global Alliance for ICT and Development.

President and CEO

ExecuTrain

Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Information Technology and Services industry

April 1996 – May 2000 (4 years 2 months)

First served as a director of the company and then as its CEO for three years. One of the largest computer training companies in the world, ExecuTrain has operated since inception in 1984 as a franchise system led by quality entrepreneurs. Between 1996 and 2000, ExecuTrain rapidly diversified its portfolio of training activities to take advantage of the growing need for technical training and the new opportunities opened by e-learning while traditional desktop applications training were less in demand.
