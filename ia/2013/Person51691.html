Haril A. Pandya, AIA LEED AP's Summary

Haril A. Pandya, AIA, LEED AP is a Principal at CBT Architects in Boston. While at CBT, he has managed and led several significant projects and project types. Among them, one of the large air-rights parcels at Columbus Center in Boston, a $500 million mixed-use urban redevelopment project, and several office repositioning projects totaling $150 million for The Blackstone Group, Equity Office, Brookfield Properties and Pembroke. Several of these projects have won prestigious interior design awards. Recently, Haril wrote and produced a clever marketing short film for and about CBT, called “CBT Delivers.” This short film won a national SMPS Marketing Award in 2010.

In 2009, Haril was one of eight recipients nationally of the American Institute of Architects’ (AIA) Young Architects Awards – the highest recognition for architects who have been licensed for 10 years or less. Additionally, in 2008, he was recognized by Building Design + Construction as a top “40 under 40” in the design and construction industry. Haril has been featured in 2010 and 2011 in Banker and Tradesman and Boston Business Journal as an upcoming “Emerging Leader.” As part of his professional experience he served as the youngest elected member on the Board of the Boston Society of Architects, President and Chair of YPAC, the Young Professional Advisory Council, and currently on the Board of Directors of the Boston Center for the Arts.

Haril has toured the U.S. as the lead guitarist in an indo-jazz fusion group Karyshma, produced the band’s album, designed the band’s website and marketing materials. He has been featured in numerous articles in the Boston Globe, News India, India Today, and has made several TV appearances for his musical accomplishments. Haril’s musical audiences have included such notables as Senator Hillary Clinton, Dr. Deepak Chopra and the Hon. Prime Minister of India. At the local level, Haril can often be seen and heard playing in the cover band Red Square at local clubs.

Specialties

Haril has written recorded and produced music for several independent films and recently begun producing entire films. In 2009, Haril co-wrote and produced his first 30 minute short film and earlier this year, he participated in Boston’s 48Hour Film Project. They had 48 hours to write, shoot and edit their films. Haril wrote and directed the film “ex.man” which placed in the top 15 of 82 teams and won an audience favorite award
