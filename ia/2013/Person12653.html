  • Styleguide Rails

Joe Nelson's Experience

Developer

bendyworks.com

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Computer Software industry

February 2012 – Present (1 year 7 months) Madison, Wisconsin Area

Being a consultant at Bendyworks has taught me good habits. First and foremost is communication with peers and clients. Between our pair programming, client stand-ups, documentation, and preventing duplicate team effort, I end up writing as much English as code.

Code quality is another good habit. Before Bendyworks I hadn't practiced test-driven design, nor used a continuous integration server. Now I've seen a variety of projects, from maintenance to greenfield and gained experience in making them better.

Finally, I have been able to take responsibility for projects' success. I get to advise clients, and help to define and prioritize work. Being trusted to guide projects, participate in hiring, and improve my company has been the best part of working in a small, tight-knit consultancy.

Co-Founder

Gymput

October 2011 – July 2012 (10 months) Madison, Wisconsin Area

I pitched a daily goal achievement web app at a startup weekend, built it and won first place. Over the next few months my team and I learned the importance of customer validation first hand. We hit the streets and demoed prototypes to potential customers in several industries. Eventually the idea pivoted to an iPad fitness CRM, which was commercially viable but ultimately not interesting to me.

Senior Developer

Academic ADL Co-Lab

Educational Institution; 11-50 employees; E-Learning industry

June 2006 – February 2012 (5 years 9 months) Madison, Wisconsin Area

Not only do I love coding, I love to teach. The Academic Colab is a research lab for improving online-learning technical standards and curricula. They make experiments and prototypes involving simulation, grading, and UI. I designed and led an online course which earned the lab thousands of dollars and reached students in ten countries.

I also took the initiative and wrote what became the most popular open-source library for recording e-learning (SCORM) data. It was later adopted by the US Department of Defense.

Co-Founder

Flyerside.com

January 2008 – December 2011 (4 years) Madison, Wisconsin Area

This was my first exposure to full-stack web development and startups. My business partner and I consistently spent twenty hours per week during nights and weekends to to create a national grocery sale search engine. We learned the hard way how to make a beautiful, usable, scalable site. We handled all aspects of engineering from caching and content delivery to database design and deployment. This project taught me a lot about engineering, user experience and project management.

The business ultimately lost out to well-funded competitors in what we discovered to be the somewhat dismal, low-margin industry of grocery advertising. However, the project payed off in the grit and confidence I gained to handle all aspects of web development.

Research Experience for Undergraduates in Mathematics

UW Madison Research and Sponsored Programs

Higher Education industry

February 2006 – May 2006 (4 months) Madison, Wisconsin Area

I got a paid semester-long research position to study provable program correctness at UW-Madison. I learned that you can mechanically derive an algorithm from a proof of its correctness using the so-called Curry-Howard Isomorphism. Though I haven't applied this to real software development, it might be an interesting project some day.

Statistics Programmer

Wisconsin Longitudinal Study

Educational Institution; 11-50 employees; Research industry

June 2005 – January 2006 (8 months) Madison, Wisconsin Area

I was hired for a student position to tidy up the data in a fifty-year survey and create statistical variables. I invented ways to automate the detection of statistically-revealing data and top-code it to protect respondents' privacy.

Programmer

Condor High Throughput Computing Project

Educational Institution; 10,001+ employees; Higher Education industry

May 2003 – March 2004 (11 months) Madison, Wisconsin Area

This is a UW-Madison project that coordinates distributed computing. Scientific projects like the Large Hadron Collider use it to crunch staggering amounts of data. I noticed that the data format the system uses to match job requirements with available resources was expressive enough to represent a functional language. I wrote an interpreter for this language and used it to concisely express more complicated job-matching criteria.

Software Architect

TIE Kinetix

Public Company; 51-200 employees; Computer Software industry

2000 – 2002 (2 years) Greater Minneapolis-St. Paul Area

I joined this e-commerce company out of high school to maintain their B2B data-translation product. It was a legacy C codebase and I noticed some substantial code duplication. I surprised my managers with a class diagram for an object-oriented rewrite. They loved it so I implemented it in C++.
