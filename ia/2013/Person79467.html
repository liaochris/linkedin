Kathleen Leach's Publications

  • Focus to Drive Growth

    • My Take
    • December 20, 2012
    Authors: Kathleen Leach

    I often find professional services firms dilute their business development efforts, leading to lackluster growth and discontent among professionals and staff. Most often the situation can be attributed to a lack of laser focus on a firm's business objectives and the commitment to making the hard decisions that come with declaring a firm’s target-market strategies.

  • Focus on One Objective

    • My Take
    • January 2, 2013
    Authors: Kathleen Leach

    The first step in a strategy to grow revenue is focusing on one objective. The objective could be for the firm as a whole, an industry or practice group, geography – whatever segment of the business you are trying to develop. Note however, if you are focusing on something other than the firm as a whole, your objective should support and complement the firm’s objective.

  • Focus Your Practice Offerings

    • My Take
    • January 21, 2013
    Authors: Kathleen Leach

    If you're following my Focus to Drive Growth series, you've put into words, in one or two sentences, your main business objective and how you will measure its achievement. Your business strategy will encircle that objective so that everything you do goes toward achieving it. So far, so good. Now it's time to take that second step toward focusing to drive growth -- Focus your practice offerings.

  • Focus on a Few Industries

    • My Take
    • February 11, 2013
    Authors: Kathleen Leach

    For the most part, people will give you the benefit of the doubt that, if you work for a firm they have heard of, you are credentialed in your field. With that assumption, what they really want to know is do you understand their industry. So Step 3 in a Focus to Drive Growth is recognizing that deep industry knowledge is important to clients, and you will want to select a few industries to focus on. Why only a few? Because real industry specialization is a significant commitment.

  • Focus on a Few Strategies

    • My Take
    • March 5, 2013
    Authors: Kathleen Leach

    Moving on in the ten-step Focus to Drive Growth process is Step 4: Focus on a Few Strategies. The basic outline I use in developing marketing and business development plans has three components: objective, strategies and tactics. As discussed in Step 1, you commit to one objective to really focus in on your end goal. Shifting to strategies, less is better (don’t worry; I’ll let you go pretty broad with tactics.) In my experience, three to five strategies usually cover the core of your high potential business.

  • Focus Your Tactics

    • My Take
    • March 26, 2013
    Authors: Kathleen Leach

    Tactics are often the section of the marketing plan that people jump to first. All kinds of suggestions are forthcoming when it comes to tactics. However, without considering those “good ideas” against a solid agreed upon objective (Step1) and your select strategies (Step 4) your tactics will be anything but focused for success.

  • Focus on Your Rainmakers of Tomorrow

    • My Take
    • April 15, 2013
    Authors: Kathleen Leach

    In most organizations there is a bell-curve when it comes to professionals’ abilities and desires to develop new business. Typically 20 percent of your people already know how to bring in business. They are comfortable doing what it takes to expand your client base – these people are your current rainmakers. You’ll likely have another 20 percent who may be wonderful subject matter experts, but not good at social interaction. No amount of training and cajoling will move them to network with the goal of developing new clients. That leaves you with 60 percent of your people who are flexible and ready for opportunities if given the chance. Training and development programs should be focused on these individuals.

  • Focus on Measuring ROI or ROO

    • My Take
    • May 13, 2013
    Authors: Kathleen Leach

    Conventional wisdom says that multiple touch points will be needed before you’ll be awarded work and essentially create a client. This makes it difficult to allocate revenue to any specific marketing touch or tactic. Furthermore, some initiatives could be measured but not without a vast amount of effort that may take more resources than the tactic itself. Nevertheless, that doesn’t mean you shouldn’t try to measure return on investment (ROI), return on objective (ROO) or both.

  • Focus on Your Brand

    • My Take
    • June 10, 2013
    Authors: Kathleen Leach

    In my experience, I’ve found that clients ultimately buy the expertise of professionals after a relationship has been established. To get to the point of engagement faster, you can pave the road by enveloping your professionals in the firm’s brand.

  • Step 9: Focus on Your Culture

    • My Take
    • July 1, 2013
    Authors: Kathleen Leach

    Your professionals are your client-facing ambassadors; they breathe life into your brand and are the most trusted referral sources for your firm. Focus on your culture for a consistent presentation to the market.
