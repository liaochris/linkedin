Alex Fotios' Summary

(Former name is Fotios Basagiannis)

Alexander is an entrepreneur with more than 10 years of hands-on IT experience. Through his work at Domino Enterprises he is currently heading the effort of bringing to market a patented product that could improve the everyday life of people in Brazil.

He has a keen interest in IT security, Internet applications and embedded development. His research interests have included secure networks, models of trust, trusted agents, information exchange, and software development methodologies.

He holds a BSc and MSc in Computer Science. In the past he has worked as a freelance consultant and senior developer in the private sector in the UK as well as the Joint Research Centre of the European Commission in Italy.

His past work has included leading the design, development and final successful deployment of ERCA, the European Root Certification Authority for the European Digital Tachograph project as well as the conceptualization, design, and proof-of-concept prototype development of SecNet-IE, an e-platform for Critical Infrastructures Information Exchange among governments.

Specialties: C, C++, Javascript, Flash/Flex ActionScript, Sockets, Multithreading, Cryptography, COM, DOM, HTML/XML, SQL, Software Development Methodologies, Hardware Security Modules (HSMs), UML.

IT Systems Architect

JRC, European Commission

November 2003 – November 2009 (6 years 1 month)

I worked as a systems architect for the SCNI (Security of Critical Networked Infrastructures) action at the European Commission's Joint Research Centre.

The last project I worked on is SecNet-IE. SecNet–IE work involved conceptualization, design and prototype development of a platform for the secure exchange of security information on European Critical Networked Infrastructures.

The exchange comprises government and industry stakeholders at an international level (EU). Some models, standards, frameworks and technologies being explored are: SOA, MDR (ISO 11179), FEA (Federal Enterprise Architecture), EDI, ebXML, EDXL, XDI, CAP, Rains-Net. The system is basically a secure P2P network of trusted agents, embedded in Hardware Security Modules (HSMs). Ref: http://sta.jrc.ec.europa.eu/index.php/competitive-projects-/21-scni/7-secnet-ie

During my time at the JRC I have also worked on such systems/projects as: EEJ-Net, PIPS, ERCA and MEDI. ERCA in particular has been a project of major significance and is today part of the EU IT infrastructure that increases safety on the roads of Europe. Ref: http://dtc.jrc.it/erca.html

Senior Software Engineer

NKD Webmedia

March 2002 – July 2003 (1 year 5 months)

During my time with the NKD crew I was the front-end developer for Soq, one of the very first SaaS CMS (Software as a Service, Content Management System). During this time I had the opportunity to become a real expert in javascript and IE based development (e.g. ActiveX) and wrote some world-class proprietary code including an XML-driven Javascript engine for loading complex web interface elements. I have also created several Flash based components that were routinely used by the company's web developers for building various sites. I have later released some of those as freeware on the Adobe Flash Exchange site (http://www.adobe.com/cfusion/exchange/index.cfm?event=productHome&exc=2) - search for "Fotios".
