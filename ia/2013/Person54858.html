Stewart David Ikeda's Summary

Well-rounded new media professional and business consultant with expertise in online and offline communications strategy, brand and content development, editorial management, community-building and partnership outreach in a 24/7 multichannel publishing environment.

Diverse foci range from significant site design to feature writing, analytics to ad network planning, social media channel development to email marketing, staff management to business partnerships in an international telecommuting environment.

Managed the now-defunct IMDiversity.com network of multiple online community content sites and diversity recruitment e-business areas featuring high-quality, daily-updated content and advanced jobs database search technology for non-traditional and multicultural niche audiences. Also edited The Black Collegian Magazine, DiversityRegistry.com, and Diversity Employers Magazine.

Experienced editor, award-winning book author, diversity consultant, former college professor (English and Ethnic Studies) and public speaker featured in conferences, presentations and media nationwide.

Novel, What the Scarecrow Said (HarperCollins Regan Books), winner of the Avery & Jule Hopwood Award for Major Fiction, finalist for the Barnes & Noble Discover Great New Writers Award.

Co-founded Asian American Creative Coalition to support multicultural / APIA voter outreach in 2004 elections; past Board member of APAs for Progress, 80-20 Initiative, Asian American Writers Workshop; works with numerous national and local NPOs dedicated to promoting civil rights, civil liberties, equal opportunity, diversity, the arts and progressive political values.
