MARK WHITFIELD's Summary

Currently employed as an IT Project/Product Manager (PRINCE2 Practitioner, Agile - Scrum, ITIL) and Principal Consultant at Insider Technologies Limited in Salford Quays, Greater Manchester (UK).

My main area of expertise is HP NonStop (formerly Tandem Computers & COMPAQ Himalaya). This current role involves me with RTLX, Reflex, XPNET, BASE24, BASE24-eps (formerly BASE24-es) & the certified integration of ITL's primary product set with leading Electronic Payment/Transaction applications & Enterprise Managers such as BMC Patrol / Performance Manager (formerly COMMAND/POST), TIVOLI & HP OpenView Operations (now HP Operations) (for Windows). Also IBM WebSphere MQ Series queue monitoring, management, tracking and MI reporting.

RTLX (Real Time Log Extraction) is an ATM, POS & EMS monitoring product that enables multiple browser based views for real-time alerting and querying of transaction & interchange SLA issues, e.g. MasterCard, VISA & LINK. RTLX deploys lean extractor agents to the HP NonStop platform(s) to relay TLF, PTLF & EMS data to a Windows Server running MS SQL Server.

Previously employed at Deluxe Data International Operations (now FIS), Runcorn, Merseyside (see NOTE below) in Electronic Banking & developing the sp/ARCHITECT (sp-architect) product, host interfaces and Barclays Business Master II (BBMII) project. I also supported the Periphonics Voice Processing systems (VOS) (bought by NORTEL in 1999) & interfaces developed for GiroBank in Denmark.

The Software Partnership (TSP) was bought out by Deluxe Data to become Deluxe Data International Operations in 1994 (based in Runcorn in the UK). This in turn then became eFUNDS (EFD) in 2000 which recently became Fidelity National Information Services (FIS) in 2007. The UK based wing of FIS is based in Daresbury in Warrington (UK).

MARK WHITFIELD's Projects

  • PCI DSS Compliant Payment & Transaction Monitoring & Querying

    • January 2010 to Present
    Team Members: MARK WHITFIELD

    ITL have created a Sentra module, RTLX Reactor, to provide PCI DSS compliant, real-time monitoring and archiving of the transaction flow information created by payment and transaction applications. This also includes the ACI BASE24™ and BASE24-eps™ (including Faster Payments) ATM/POS application coupled with the NonStop EMS subsystem.

    RTLX Reactor does not inflict a fixed look-and-feel for monitoring live applications. RTLX provides a completely secure, tailorable graphical canvas to enable you to view your payment and transaction processing real-estate at a system level, e.g. PATHWAYs, Middleware, XPNET and Logical Networks, or at a business level, e.g. top 20 retailers with drill-down to card type broken down by transaction volumes, approvals, denials etc. There is no programmed limit to the number of views that can be constructed and so each department can have their own customised monitoring screen(s) and management reports.

    With RTLX, it's easy to plug-in another file, log, payment, transaction or message feed by deploying a new lean, optimised extraction client. The result is a standard approach to monitoring and querying live payment environments. Data can be masked, encrypted or omitted for PCI DSS directives and the live application and platform is secure as all transaction queries and rules analysis takes place on Windows Server technology against a local MS SQL or Oracle database.
