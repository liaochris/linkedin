Bob Stewart's Summary

Currently focused on delivering the convergence of physical and cyber security. Identifying and creating hybrid hardware and software solution systems for federated identity and access management designed to secure and ensure transactional integrity. Designing systemic accountability through encrypted attribution signatures and escrowed audit capabilities. Leveraging data toward finite improvement in signal to noise attention vectors that identify deviation from expected patterns thereby maximizing security resource effectiveness.

Co-Founder of Maine State Licensed Contract Security Company Maine Guard, LLC. focused on physical, logical and cyber security and privacy. Leveraging years of special forces and emergency management service experience including their VitalLock information privacy and public safety personal emergency notification system.

Bob formerly led boutique RIA development services for high-profile, media and consumer sites including; Adobe, Boeing, BT, CBS, CNET, Disney, EMC, Facebook, Fidelity, Gomez, Hyundai, Intuit, Kodak, NFL, Ribbit, SearchMe, Times Mirror and others.

Stewart served as Internet Chief Technology Officer of Motorola's $1B Internet and Networking Group where he earned Motorola's highest I.T. technical ladder rank of Strategic Adviser Technical Analyst and Webmaster.

Bob has performed technology due diligence for a number of venture capital firms in North America and Europe and at times served as an interim Chief Technology Officer to select portfolio companies. Bob has also been involved, both as an investment adviser and a transition manager, in substantial M&A activity including a $220M merger with BellSouth and a $290M acquisition from Compaq.

Bob also earned global recognition as Chief Technology Officer for CMGI portfolio companies Planet Direct, CMGI Solutions, Zip2 and MyWay.com and is the author of OpenSDLC.org
