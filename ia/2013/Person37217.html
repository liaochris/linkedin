Douglas C. Myers, PhD's Publications

  • ALASKA - Fishing The UNTAMED

    • Adventure Alaska Ltd
    • October 30, 2009
    Authors: Douglas C. Myers, PhD, Dan F. Barr (Foreword)

    A wilderness fishing ebook written by Alaskan author Doug Myers, adventurer, and sport fisherman. A former remote lodge owner in Southwest Alaska, Myers writes of his fishing/wildlife adventures on wild rivers of the Bristol Bay Region, Kodiak Island, and Alaska Peninsula, and less distant Kenai Peninsula. It is a book for the sport fisherman, outdoor enthusiast, and all who embrace the suspense in wild and reverence for the pristine.

    To read the book is to take a virtual trip to America's 'last frontier', a timeless treasure experienced by a fortunate few. For sport fishermen it provides the vicarious realization of an often elusive dream. All 5 species of Pacific salmon (chinook/king, chum/dog, coho/silver, humpy/pink, and sockeye/red) are included in the illustrated narrative. Dolly Varden char, halibut, northern pike, rainbow trout, and pursuit of the prestigious Arctic grayling are included in the adventure narrative.

    The writing includes visits with Native friends at their subsistence fish camp site on a designated 'wild and scenic' river within the Bristol Bay Region of Southwest Alaska. From ancient privilege to the running of the Iditarod Sled Dog Race, wildlife observations to sport fishing highlights, the book emphasizes Alaska's greatest resource - the spirit of adventure. Introductory photo is included for each chapter.

  • PURSUING ALASKA'S UNTAMED

    • Adventure Alaska Ltd
    • May 5, 2009
    Authors: Douglas C. Myers, PhD, Colonel Norman D. Vaughan (Foreword)

    The ebook illustrates with personal narrative and photos Alaska's greatest and most cherished resource - the spirit of adventure. It is written for the outdoor enthusiast, culturally curious, and wildlife romantic who embrace the suspense in wild and reverence for Nature's pristine and historic. For some it provides the vicarious realization of an elusive dream. The book is about Alaska's heart and soul.

    The 'Foreword' is authored by Colonel Norman D. Vaughan, member of the first Byrd Antarctic Expedition. A brief bio of his illustrious life is included.

    The writing includes visits with Native friends at their subsistence fish camp site at the edge of a designated 'wild and scenic' river within the Bristol Bay Region of Southwest Alaska. Each chapter is photo illustrated, and a reference link is available for viewing additional photos from the companion hard copy. A link is also available for viewing photos of oil paintings by the author that depict his experiences in pursuing Alaska's scenic and wildlife treasure, the untamed.

    The book's contents include: Spirit of Adventure (Prologue), Katmai's Other Eruption (Best and Worst of the Alaska Dream), Message of Goodnews (Promised Land), Seeking Subsistence (Ancient Privilege), River Wildlife Potpourri (Adventures of Father and Son), Streams of Dreams (Alaska Peninsula's Silver-Lining), Sourdough Legacy (Prospecting Harbinger Herring), Island Extreme (Emerald Gem in Alaska's Gulf), Highway to Heaven (From Hope to Heaven's Gate), Dogged Determination (Heart and Soul of Alaska), Unity in Diversity (Winter's Allure and Summer's Prolific Embrace), Surviving the Surreal (Moose Twin's Torment), and A Last Frontier (Pleasure's Freedom Song).
