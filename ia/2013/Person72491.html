Karri Ojanen's Summary

I’m a user experience designer, strategist and creative lead, multi-skilled and versed in creative, strategy and technology. I'm particularly excited about making sense of emerging technologies and creating seamless connections between the digital and physical worlds. I’m also an avid world traveller, an electronic musician, and a long distance runner.

I earned my experience as art director, concept designer and creative director in Scandinavia, praised for its award-hoarding digital agencies, then went on to work in the Middle East, the United States, and Canada. Most recently, I worked as an Interaction Design Director at R/GA in New York leading a large team of interaction designers. I'm now based in Canada, working as director of user experience and creative design with a global IT consulting organization. I'm also an independent user experience consultant, designer, speaker, and strategist.

My work has been awarded nationally and internationally. I also participate in and speak at events and design community events, including Slush 2012 in Helsinki, Finland, the UXLx conference in Lisbon, Portugal in 2010, and IxDA events in Toronto, Canada.

Karri Ojanen's Projects

  • VillageLife

    • May 2004 to February 2005
    Team Members: Karri Ojanen, Anders Wikström, , Juha Javanainen

    A game-like digital fundraising platform for a Finnish charitable organization, in which virtual villages represent actual locations in Asia, Africa, and Latin America.

    Individual or group donors choose a village and a specific item or service to donate via easy, immediate online payment or by SMS billing on their mobile phones. The result of their donations, such as constructing part of a school or supporting a local social worker for the village, is immediately visible to the donors. The site also includes a news section where the organization’s workers in the real villages can post updates from the ground.

    Sponsored by the Finnish Ministry of Culture, Village Life launched in 2004 with much attention from the media. It won several awards including first prize for The Best Consumer Service at the Grand One digital industry awards in Helsinki in 2005.
