Andre Gardner's Summary

Major Market radio broadcast professional since 1977. Experienced in all aspects of Radio Programming and formats (Classic Rock, AOR, Top 40, AC, Alternative) including positions as Air Personality, Program Director, Music Director and Production Director.

Andre Gardner's Experience

On-Air Personality

102.9 WMGK Radio (Greater Media) - Philadelphia

Privately Held; 501-1000 employees; Broadcast Media industry

April 2002 – Present (11 years 4 months)

Hired by Buzz Knight as afternoon drive personality (2-7pm) where I have consistently been top 10 (and in most cases, top 5) Arbitron-rated with adults 25-54. I also host "Breakfast With The Beatles" - a syndicated Beatles specialty show - which currently airs on WMGK/Philadelphia and KSLX/Phoenix.

Censor, Howard Stern Show

WXRK, New York (92-3 K-Rock) - CBS Radio

October 1998 – April 2002 (3 years 7 months)

An interesting position, indeed, monitoring the content of the Howard Stern Show for national broadcast, and editing out objectionable material that did not suit the FCC's guidelines.

Commercial Production Director

WXRK, New York (92-3 K-Rock) - CBS Radio

January 1996 – April 2002 (6 years 4 months)

Produced, voiced and oversaw commercial production for K-Rock Radio. Also recorded and mixed band performances when they dropped by the station (REM, Sean Lennon, The Cranberries, Muse, Coldplay, etc.)

Program Director

WXRK, New York (92-3 K-Rock) - CBS Radio

October 1994 – January 1996 (1 year 4 months)

Oversaw Programming, Promotion, Music and Talent departments of Classic Rock Station in New York. Biggest achievement while I was Program Director was overtaking longtime rival WNEW in adults 25-54 between 10am-6pm (music hours) to become the highest rated rock station in New York. Station flipped to alternative in January, 1996.

Assistant Program Director/Music Director

WXRK, New York (92-3 K-Rock) CBS Radio

December 1993 – October 1994 (11 months)

Shortly after Tim Sabean (for whom I worked at WYSP) left to take the Program Director job at WXRK, I was hired by him at WXRK to serve as his assistant until his departure in 1994. Upon his departure, I was made Program Director of WXRK by then-GM Tom Chiusano. Thanks for the shot, Tom!!

Music Director - On Air Personality

WYSP/Philadelphia -- Infinity Broadcasting

December 1988 – January 1994 (5 years 2 months)

Hired by john Roberts at Music Director/Nights. Worked for John, then Andy Bloom, then Tim Sabean. It was during this time that WYSP enjoyed the best ratings success in the station's history. Stayed at that position until Time hired me at WXRK (K-Rock) in New York.

Assistant Program Director/Music Director

KZEW Radio (The Zoo) - Dallas, TX

September 1988 – December 1988 (4 months)

Spent three months at this heritage rock station as assistant to PD John Roberts. When John left for a position at WYSP in Philadelphia (my hometown) he took me with him.

Air Personality

KEGL Radio (Eagle 97) - Dallas, TX

September 1987 – September 1988 (1 year 1 month)

Started as 10pm-2am jock, then promoted to middays before leaving for a position at KZEW, working for PD John Roberts.

Air Personality

WARM-FM (Power 99) - Atlanta

August 1986 – September 1987 (1 year 2 months)

Evening air personality for Susquehanna Broadcasting Top 40 station.

Air Personality

WZGO (Z-106) - Philadelphia

August 1984 – May 1986 (1 year 10 months)

Started doing weekends, then promoted to late nights and eventually afternoon drive at Cox Broadcasting Top 40 station.

Production Director

WSNI (Sunny 104.5) Radio - Philadelphia

April 1983 – June 1984 (1 year 3 months)

Production Director for AC station in Philadelphia. Also did some fill-in air work.

On-Air Personality

WIFI Radio (WIFI-92)

January 1981 – April 1983 (2 years 4 months)

Returned in January, 1981 to do nights, working for PDs Tom Bigby and Don Cannon. Promoted to mornings at the short lived "I-92," one of the first alternative stations in the country, from February to April, 1983. One of the highlights of my time with the I-92 phase of the station was the opportunity to assist the legendary Rick Carroll in signing on the New Wave format.

Morning Drive Air Personality

WIZD (Wizzard 99) - Ft. Pierce, Florida

August 1980 – January 1981 (6 months)

Morning Drive Personality for Top 40 station owned by a really nice guy named Ron Crider. Worked there with the legendary Jonathan Doll. Got homesick and returned to WIFI, Philadelphia.

Air Personality

WIFI Radio (WIFI-92) - Philadelphia

December 1979 – August 1980 (9 months)

My dream job! Landed a weekend gig, then was promoted to fulltime nights in the spring of 1980. Left briefly to take a job in Florida.

Part-time on-air personality

WPST/Trenton, NJ -- Nassau Broadcasting

July 1979 – December 1979 (6 months)

Toward the tail end of my time at WZZD, I returned here to do weekends/swing. Left for a similar position at WIFI-92 in December, 1979.

Air Personality

WZZD (WIzzard 100)/Philadelphia - Fairbanks Broadcasting

November 1978 – September 1979 (11 months)

I was shocked when Larry Knight, PD of Wizzard 100 in Philly, hired me for weekends. To work in the old WIBG building in Lafayette Hill, where my brother Bill Gardner, Joe Niagra, Hy Lit, John "Records" Landecker and Joey Reynolds had all practiced their craft, was surreal. Worked late nights and was promoted to afternoon drive. The station started out as a Hot AC when I got there, and flipped to disco in January, 1979.

Air Personality

WPST/Trenton, NJ -- Nassau Broadcasting

July 1977 – March 1979 (1 year 9 months)

My first radio gig, living the dream as a young radio geek still in high school. Then-PD Tom Taylor (now writes an excellent daily radio column, TR-I, at radio-info.com) gave me the shot when I was just 16 years old and my Dad had to drive me to the station! Started as a weekender, then moved to afternoons and, at my request, nights.
