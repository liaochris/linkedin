James Abbott's Summary

Dr. Abbott's extensive experience and capabilities in factory test methods, measurement physics, failure analysis, acoustic and vibration engineering, and sensor and actuator technologies, stems from over 25 years of work in these and related fields.

Recently, he applied these diverse skills as a Senior HW Test and Acoustic Validation Engineer at Apple. In this role he led test programs for high-volume manufacturing of personal mobile devices via direct and broad-based factory support in Asia.

His professional career started with a two-year position as an Associate Scientist at Bolt Beranek and Newman, Inc. in the Sonar Acoustics group. He later worked as a Senior Research Physicist at SRI (Stanford Research Institute) where he led numerous projects related to acoustic and vibration engineering. Subsequently, while a Principal at Charles Salter Associates, he co-invented the first broadband, compact, low-cost, multi-channel active noise control system applied to the problem of human snoring noise (Patent US5844996.) Later he joined Exponent/Failure Analysis as a Senior Scientist and Founding Director of the firm's Manhattan office. He then served on the faculty of the Albert Nerken School of Engineering at The Cooper Union, where he advised five Masters students.

He received his Ph.D. in Physics from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT) in 1991, working under the advisement of Prof. K. U. Ingard on a thesis related to acoustical test methods. He was awarded the Buechner Prize and the Goodwin Medal at graduation, the highest institute award for teaching by a graduate student. Also while at MIT, he served for two years as a Research Assistant in the MIT Media Laboratory where he conducted research and development projects involving active control of room acoustics.

Specialties

Personal mobile products, factory test methods and devices, failure analysis, in-region support, vendor and manufacturer relationships, acoustics, audio engineering, vibration, transducers, data acquisition, technology assessment.
