Kris Vann's Summary

Marketing is my first love. It was what I studied and where I started my career. For over 20 years I went onto management consulting, practicing law, and building technology industry experience in areas such as strategy, operations, product development, project management, and partnership/ business development.

Throughout that time, I’ve always been involved on the marketing side of the business and am (quite happily) back full-circle in a marketing and strategy role, but now with a vital understanding of organizational structure and cross-functional areas across an enterprise.

I’m most at home in high-growth technology companies where I get to collaborate as part of a strong community of smart people, all dedicated to using their talents to the best of their abilities to solve complex problems – in business and in the community.

I’m most at flow when dealing with the intersection of big data, law, regulatory compliance, digital media, and mobile technology. It’s when I can best leverage my hybrid legal/technology experience.

-----------------------------------------------------
Kris has a B.S.B.A. in international business and marketing from American University in Washington D.C. and studied product management and marketing at UC Berkeley Extension. She earned her J.D. at Northwestern University School of Law and Southern Methodist University. She is admitted to the Bar in Illinois, is a member of the American Bar Association, and former program co-chair of the Section on Science and Technology Law.

Specialties: technology marketing, product management and marketing, partnership/ business development, strategy consulting, business process consulting, mobile telecom, IT, digital media/publishing, cloud, SaaS

Legal: regulatory investigations, case management, depositions, records retention, commercial litigation, products liability, intellectual property, employment, bankruptcy, anti-trust, environment, electronic discovery process

Kris Vann's Publications

  • "Counsel or iCounsel? Practical and Ethical Considerations of 'Artificial Intelligence' in E-Discovery," ABA Annual Meeting, Chicago

    • ABA Science and Technology.
    • August 7, 2012
    Authors: Kris Vann, Julia Brickell, Jeffrey Fowler, Dera J. Nevin

    As corporate and personal technologies merge, the escalating burden of exploding data volumes affects litigation as never before, inhibiting access to justice, increasing regulatory and litigation risk, and threatening the quality of internal investigations. This program addresses the ethical implications and legal consequences of using “artificial intelligence” technologies to review and search electronic information and social media in the context of litigation and investigations. Do they work? Are they defensible? In addition to understanding ethical considerations and judicial perspectives regarding the use of technology to search for and produce electronic information, the audience will emerge better prepared to maximize defensibility and help clients contain costs in the face of electronic data challenges.

  • The Progress of Tanzanian Women in the Law

    • Southern California Review of Law and Women's Studies
    • 2000
    Authors: Kris Vann, Jennifer Rakstad, Charlotte Kaiser

    The hot button legal issues for women in Tanzania are marriage laws, inheritance laws, land laws, and sex offense laws. It was only last year, for example, that women in Tanzania were allowed to own land. Even with this reform, because there are no laws that specifically relate to the inheritance of property, upon the death of a husband, all property reverts to his family under customary law and many women are left destitute. This article explores the laws that impact the progress of women in Tanzania. 10 S. Cal. R. L. & Women's Stud. 35 (2000-2001).
