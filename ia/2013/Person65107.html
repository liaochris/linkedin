Jo Coddington's Summary

Jo Coddington - Bio
www.jocoddington.com

As comfortable whether stylishly dressed and speaking in front of a crowd or sliding out from under a hot rod in my coveralls, I am continuing to make my own mark in a male dominated industry.
"I was napping on a creeper as a kid!" My father and other family members had been in the automotive business world my whole life. I raced go-karts and, with the aid of two large pillows, could drive the family's '53 Chevy between home and the shop at a precocious 12 years of age. For some 10 years I raced sand-rails in hillclimbs and other events, where my combination of skill and light weight make me quick and popular. My enthusiasm for all things motorized has taken me down a wonderful path!

Looking back on my time with Boyd, I was an integral part of the operational success of the business, the knowledge and education I gleaned off of Boyd really fine-tuned my understanding and accomplishments in the Hot Rod industry. I learned so much from the King of Hot Rod design that it fills my head almost as much as his memory warms my heart. It was an amazing time in my life.
Moving forward, I continue to wear many hats. First and foremost I am dedicated to being the keeper of Boyd’s Legacy. I travel the country on Boyd’s behalf for speaking engagements and such events as being inducted into one of the many Hot Rod Halls of Fame. I continuously, yet selectively, find opportunities to keep Boyd’s Brand alive.
My entrepreneurial spirit has me moving in many exciting directions. One that I am perhaps most passionate about is design work. Working with Boyd, I always had a voice and influence in design. Now, working on my own designs I find Boyd’s voice and influence to be at the heart of my work which in turn helps keep Boyd’s legacy alive.
Making my own way in the industry, my integrity and understanding as a businesswoman coupled with my philanthropic outlook allows many opportunities!
