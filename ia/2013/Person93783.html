Miffa Salter's Summary

Miffa is an experienced facilitator, trainer and performance coach with a well-earned reputation for energy, rigour and results.

Over the past twenty years, she has led projects across the public, private and voluntary sectors with commissions extending from board and 'top tier' leadership programmes to front line team building.

Miffa's work currently involves her in the design and facilitation of high profile events for national bodies and pan-London agencies including: NHS England; the Greater London Authority (GLA); the London Development Agency (LDA); Future of London; and, the Homes and Communities Agency (HCA). Recent clients include senior teams in central and local government, housing, health and the arts.

Miffa also provides one-to-one performance coaching alongside her work as a facilitator and trainer. She has a keen interest in psychological assessment and is licensed to use a full range of psychometrics covering both individual aptitude and personality profiling. She is also accredited to run a number of bench marking frameworks including both 360-degree Feedback and Skillscope.

Miffa holds a first class honours degree from the London School of Economics and was successfully nominated in 2000 for the British American Project – an independently funded leadership exchange programme for internationally recognised young leaders. She is an advisory board member for Rising Minds - a social enterprise providing coaching and mindfulness training in London.

Specialties: Team building, leadership development, small and large group facilitation, public consultation/stakeholder engagement, one-to-one performance coaching, self and group motivation, psychological assessment.
