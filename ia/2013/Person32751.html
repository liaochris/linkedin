Co-Founder, Software Director

1985 – 1989 (4 years) San Francisco, California

I co-founded Blank Software in 1985 in order to integrate the emerging new technologies of the Macintosh, MIDI, and digital audio sampling. Created powerful new tools for musicians with intuitive user interfaces, including four commercially successful products: SoundLab, DrumFile, SoundFile, and most notably Alchemy. Alchemy was the first stereo sample editor for the Macintosh and supported a large variety of samplers through a Distributed Audio Network. In 1989 Alchemy won the Macworld Editor's choice award for best music and sound production software.

David Willenbrink's Patents

  • Meters for dynamics processing of audio signals

    • United States Patent 20070195975
    • Issued July 6, 2005
    Inventors: David Willenbrink

    A gain reduction meter for dynamics processing provides information describing which part of a dynamics curve is affecting the input signal. In particular, the gain reduction meter displays an amount of gain reduction due to applying a dynamics processing operation in a manner that differentiates between gain reduction achieved by the different parts of the dynamics curve for that dynamics processing operation. The gain reduction meter has a plurality of display elements. Each display element corresponds to an amount of gain reduction. The illumination of the display elements is controlled such that the appearance of each illuminated display element is dependent on where on the dynamics curve the amount of gain reduction corresponding to that display element is produced.

  • Method and system for editing digital audio information with music-like parameters

    • United States Patent 5792971
    • Issued August 11, 1998
    Inventors: David Willenbrink

    The present invention provides a method for editing digital audio information, such as musical material. Original musical parameters are extracted and/or inputted from recorded original digital audio material. The original musical parameters are then edited. The resulting edited musical parameters are compared to the original musical parameters to provide time varying control functions. The original digital audio material is then processed with signal processing algorithms which are controlled by the time varying control functions. This processing changes the original digital audio material into new digital audio material having musical characteristics which correspond to the edited musical parameters.
