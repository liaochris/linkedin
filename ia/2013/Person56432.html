Jack Carman's Summary

Jack Carman, FASLA, president of Design for Generations LLC, is a Landscape Architect with 20 years experience in the analysis, planning, design and management of outdoor spaces.
• Designs, develops and manages therapeutic exterior environments for senior communities and healthcare facilities
• Created over 100 Landscape Plans for Assisted Living and Alzheimer Residences, Adult Day Health Care Centers, Skilled Nursing Facilities and Continuing Care Retirement Communities and Hospitals in over 18 states throughout the US
• Designed over 30 memory care/Alzheimer's gardens
• Speaker at national conferences on Therapeutic Gardens
• Edited and contributed to “Re-creating Neighborhoods for Successful Aging” published by Health Professional Press.
• Contributing author to "Interaction by Design", and "Complementary and Alternative Medicine for Older Adults" and numerous technical papers, national elder care journals, and related publications
• Adjunct faculty at Temple University - Landscape Architecture Department and the Chicago Botanic Garden School - “Healthcare Garden Design” Certificate Program.

Specialties

Landscape Architecture expertise in Therapeutic Landscapes for healthcare facilities, senior living/elder care communities, and commercial/residential environments.
