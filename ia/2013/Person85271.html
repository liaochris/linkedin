Marcos Pontes' Summary

Born on March 11th, 1963 in Bauru, SP, Marcos Pontes is the first professional astronaut with single citizenship of a south hemisphere country of the Planet. Selected as a mission specialist, he reported to the Johnson Space Center, in August 1998 (17th Class of Astronauts). He launched with the Expedition-13 crew from Baikonur Cosmodrome, Kazakhstan, on March 29, 2006 to perform a 10 days space mission to the International Space Station (ISS). Pontes returned to earth with the Expedition-12 crew, landing in Kazakhstan on April 8, 2006. Currently, he is in stand-by for his second space flight assignment.

Pontes has 25 years of experience in the Air Force, working as a fighter pilot, test pilot, and aeronautical engineer on operations, technical and management positions. He logged more than 2000 hours of jet and test flight in 25 different aircraft. He retired from the Air Force in 2006 to continue with his activities for the Space Program as the only Brazilian Professional Astronaut. He has been working on Flight/Work Safety for 25 years.

In NASA and JAXA, Pontes worked on technical duties in the International Space Station Branch for 8 years, including Project Management and Systems Integration for the Japanese Experimental Module (JEM), Multi-Element Integration and Testing, ISS Software Loads Testing, and the Centrifuge Accommodation Module.

Currently, Pontes serves as President of Astronaut Marcos Pontes Foundation, as President of MP Engenharia e Eventos Ltda, a R&D company, as visiting Researcher at the Advanced Studies Institute (IEA-SC) of the University of São Paulo (USP), as Life & Executive Coach, as visiting Professor of the Aeronautical Engineering Department of the University of São Paulo (USP), as Motivational Speaker and Author with 2 published books.
