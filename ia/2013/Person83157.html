Luc Levesque's Summary

When traveling in the late 1990s, Luc knew there was a better way than email to share travel stories with friends and family back home. Luc's penchant for problem solving, building, and efficiency led him to found TravelPod.com, the world’s first travel blogging website. At the age of 21, Luc was on the start-up tech scene and spent ten years growing the company. In 2006, TripAdvisor/Expedia acquired TravelPod and him.

Today, Luc is Vice President of Search Engine Optimization (SEO) at TripAdvisor where he leads a North American-wide team to produce outstanding SEO and viral marketing results. Luc has also worked with his team to develop two award-winning web-based travel products: TripWow (“WebAward for Outstanding Achievement” – Web Marketing Association) and Traveler IQ Challenge (Excellence in Social Media – CNMA). TravelPod was named by PC Magazine as “Top 100 sites you can’t live without,” the 24th most valuable blog by 24/7 Wall Street, and was ranked the #12 most popular blog on the Internet by technocrati.com.

Luc can hold his own as a coder, but now focuses on development and management – the big picture stuff that requires connecting-the-dots and leading people. Luc is big on speed. He works fast and hard, and has fun because he is passionate about producing solid results in record time. This is one reason why Luc received the Ottawa Business Journal Top 40 under 40 award in 2008. It is his use of creative tools and technology for management, specifically by "Boss’s Blueprint", that landed him in the New York Times in 2013.

Giving back to the start-up world remains a passion. Entrepreneurship is in Luc's blood. He speaks at various global, national, and local events, and acts as an advisor to companies such as MyMusic and 360pi. He is also a mentor at the start-up incubator FounderFuel. While his home and heart are in Ottawa, Canada, Luc travels North America and the world for work and play as often as possible
