Matthew Capala's Summary

Matthew Capala is a global inbound marketer, SEO strategist, blogger and speaker. By day, Matthew is the Head of Search at Profero, a leading global digital ad agency, and Adjunct Professor at NYU, where he teaches a graduate course on search marketing. By night, he is a Blogger decoding insider inbound marketing strategies via SearchDecoder.com and broadcasting tips via @SearchDecoder.

Matthew helps businesses attract website traffic and develop sustained audience online to generate leads and sales through integrated inbound marketing solutions, including:

- Content Marketing
- Search Engine Optimization (SEO)
- Pay-Per-Click Advertising (PPC)
- Social Media Marketing (SMM)
- Blogging & Community Building
- Website Monetization Solutions
- Site Analytics

With a decade of global experience in building both iconic brands and small businesses online, Matthew spent most of his career at global ad agencies, including Mediacom, Mindshare, and Zeta Interactive. He managed search marketing campaigns for high-profile clients, including Dell, LG, Prudential, Hilfiger, Sotheby’s, Boehringer Ingelheim and Allergan. He also advised a number of emerging start-ups on business development and launched numerous online ventures. Currently, Matthew heads the Americas search group at Profero New York, where he started the SEO practice signing clients such as Western Union, TRESemme, Smirnoff, Mohegan Sun, NYT, and Circa. Within a year, Matthew grew the search group in Profero’s NY office from 2 to 9, and is currently leading global SEO expansion and inbound marketing product development.

Matthew is frequently featured at conferences, seminars, talk shows and universities. His most recent speaking engagements include keynote address at the 40th anniversary of CEANY, and talks at MarketingWeekNYC and the Great Ideas at C-Level in Miami. Matthew teaches a graduate course on search marketing at NYU, where he was appointed an Adjunct Professor and founded Inbound Marketing Clinic.

Matthew Capala's Publications

  • Does eBay’s Research Reflect The Effectiveness Of Paid Search?

    • Search Marketing Standard
    • April 15, 2013
    Authors: Matthew Capala, Daniel Kastalsky

    The paid search medium has long been a cornerstone of successful media campaigns, typically driving highly relevant traffic at a fraction of the cost of awareness tactics. As a “pull” channel, search capitalizes on strong user intent in translating clicks into cost-effective conversions.

    However, the efficacy of this channel has been recently called into question in a research study conducted by eBay and presented at a National Bureau of Economic Research conference in March 2013. In that study, eBay insists that their PPC efforts are ineffective in driving a positive ROI, arguing that their paid clicks are replaced by organic clicks when pausing such initiatives. These findings, while undoubtedly accurate from a data collection perspective, may not correctly portray the entire story behind the value of the paid search channel – if it is used effectively.

  • Planning a Tablet Commerce Strategy

    • Multichannel Merchant, Cover Story
    • March 1, 2012
    Authors: Matthew Capala

    When you formulate your tablet strategy, you really need to think of a tablet as an integral part of your multichannel marketing strategy.

    First, you need to understand how tablets are used in your customers’ buying cycles, and which demographics have the highest tablet usage. Studies show that tablet usage is highest at home on weekdays during the eve­ning, prime-time hours. That is a very telling statistic for those who buy television ads.

    Second, you need to better understand the role of tablets in your media mix. Your customers are con­suming media across the four screens—television, PC, mobile and tablet—and expect a seamless experience when switching devices.
