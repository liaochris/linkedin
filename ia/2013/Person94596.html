Mike Sommers' Summary

For more than 15 years I have created and led digital product organizations for companies large and small at the forefront of digital technology.

I have a track record of building high-performance teams, developing innovative technology solutions and driving customer engagement and satisfaction. Over my career I have built successful ad-driven web and mobile products for the mass market, cloud-based social software platforms for small, medium and enterprise businesses, video management and distribution systems for large media companies and consumer mobile applications based on a virtual currency. I have experience successfully evaluating and integrating companies as part of M&A transactions and understand the challenges of operating a company at every stage, from pre-revenue to publicly traded.

I began my career writing code in startup environments and have been a proponent of the lean approach to software product development since before it was a movement. I believe that winning is about listening to consumers and remaining agile. Software products are never "done."

Mike Sommers' Patents

  • Indicating recent content publication activity by a user

    • United States Patent 60757397
    • Issued January 10, 2007
    Inventors: Mike Sommers, Chris Armstrong, Alan Keister, Lark Dunham, Steve Greenberg, Rakesh "Rocky" Agrawal, Carol Glover

    My contributions to this wide-ranging patent appear in sections 1300 - 1600, which describe a "buddy powered search engine" that is capable of searching the web for content that was put on the web by a given user's online friends. For instance a search on "Athens, Greece" might return a set of results containing photos on Flickr, blog posts on WordPress, shared links on Facebook, videos on YouTube, status updates on Twitter, saved links on Delicious, etc., all related to the query "Athens, Greece," and all contributed to those sites by my friends.

    Check out Figure 13 in the Drawings to see a mock up of the interface for a search on the term "Creme Brulee." The results are filtered to just show content added to the web by the searcher's friends. Profile updates, message board posts, photos on Flickr. The interface also includes a feature that allows the user to check a box to be notified if any of their friends contributes anything to the web in the future, they want to be notified. And another checkbox that says, "Broadcast this question to my buddies," to notify their friends in real-time. This application was submitted in January, 2006. Facebook announced their "Graph Search" in January, 2013.
