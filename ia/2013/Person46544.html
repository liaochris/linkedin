Gabriela Bohm's Summary

Gabriela Böhm is a documentary producer and director. She is founder of Böhm Productions, which makes films that captivate the heart by exploring cultural identity.

She is currently in post-production on In Raquel's Footsteps, the story of Raquel Liberman who was one of more than 30,000 Jewish women lured from Europe to the New World and forced into Argentinean prostitution rings. The documentary is about Raquel's journey from naïf to defiled to defiant.
http://www.indiegogo.com/raquelfilm/x/1697888

Böhm is the producer/director of several award-winning films. The Longing: The Forgotten Jews of South America was honored with the “Best Documentary” Award, Long Island Latino International Film Festival, “Best Latino Film” Award, Santa Fe Film Festival, “Telly Award” for Religion & Spirituality and “Honorable Mention”, Orlando Hispanic Film Festival.

She produced and directed Passages, which won “Best Documentary” at the Woodstock Film Festival and “Jury Award”, at the Tambay Film Festival. She produced, directed, wrote and edited her first film, Voice-less, which screened at film festivals throughout the U.S.

A native of Argentina, she studied art and photography in Israel, prior to coming to the United States, where she received a BFA at NYU Tisch School of the Arts and a MFA at Maine Media College. A US citizen, Böhm speaks Spanish, English and Hebrew.
