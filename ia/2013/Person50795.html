Gui Ambros' Summary

TL;DR: internet pioneer, 2 startups, 17 years of experience in the digital space – 12 of which leading digital media, technology, mobile, social and digital marketing for tier-1 brands. Global citizen, hacker, gamer, passionate about technology and digital mkt.

Boring version:

I've been working with digital technology and the web since… well, since it wasn't called “web”. At the time HTML 1.0 was still in beta, and I was spending my weekends on IRCs or reading usenet news. I'm lucky enough to say I was there when the web was born.

I'm currently responsible for Marketing Operations & Tech Innovation at Wunderman New York. My role is to connect the dots between clients' needs, team structures and agency resources, and directly oversee Project Management, Technology, QA and Creative Production.

Most recently I led the Coca-Cola account, developing campaigns and managing several projects for Coke's world's biggest loyalty program. Prior, I was the Digital practice lead at Y&R Miami, where we built one of the most sophisticate e-commerce demandgen programs for Dell (with SEM, display, email, social, trading desk).

Before moving to Miami, I spent 6 years at Wunderman Sao Paulo, where I was part of the original Team Nokia, coordinating 60+ people across 6 countries. Our work was recognized at Cannes, NY Festival, El Ojo and more. It was also the start of what is today the most sophisticate digital agency in Brazil.

Making justice to my entrepreneurial spirit, I lived in 5 cities, 2 countries, created two startups (one exit, but not quite Instagram-level), and sometimes participate as angel investor and advisor in the areas of product, marketing and tech operations. In my spare time, I continue to code (Python, Go, Ruby), hack electronics (robots, FPGA, OpenCV), sysadmin for my wife's startup (AWS, Vagrant, Chef automation), and hardcore COD player. Also used to be a DJ many moons ago, but I'm now officially retired.

If you share common interests, say hi @GuiAmbros.

Co-founder, Engineering Lead

Plug-In Internet Provider

April 1995 – December 2000 (5 years 9 months)

I was working in the university's computer lab while doing my bachelor's, when a friend invited me to start a new company. "Internet access", he said. "uh? I thought that was illegal..."

That was 1994, and we were both at the university and had privileged access. The next year internet would became open to the public, and it was the end of the world as we knew it.

Plug-In was one of the first commercial ISP in Brazil. While there's no clear record of who was the very first, probably we hold the 3rd or 4th spot, by a difference of a month or two. It was a great experience to grow a bootstrapped company from my bedroom into a multi-million dollar company, during a period where nobody knew what the web was going to be.

We had lots of firsts: the first internet connected company, the first webhosting company, the first radio streaming over the web, the first 3D MMO-like world (Words Chat, the Snow Crash-inspired predecessor of Second Life), the first commercial company to run entirely on Linux (kernel 0.9.5).

I knew nothing about how to run a company. That's the beauty of startups: you have to show up, and do it all. From sysadmin to buying staples.

My main roles:

- Engineering Lead: from scalalibility, C/C++/Bash development, Apache configuration, site development
- Product Lead: defining the company's offering and market positioning, pricing, quotes, pre-sales
- System Administration: Apache, system optimization (I've been using Linux since the first Slackware)
- Solutions Architect: designing web and network solutions for our corporate clients

I left the company in 2000 to create another startup with StarMedia, but continued as an investor, until the company was acquired by UOL Host.
