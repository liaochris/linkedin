Brent MacKinnon's Summary

Social Media Tools for Work & Learning (SMT) works with organizations and individuals interested in integrating social web tools to improve work flow, learning, innovation, cooperation and collaboration.

My "word fingerprint" is Listening, Engaging, Encouraging and Energizing. I strive to bring these key attributes and skills to my work with individuals and organizations.

I have an extensive experience in the non-profit sector, particularly within the youth and community development field. I utilize private/public collaboration web platforms to support project development, communities of practice and organizational change processes. I combine my community work and social media expertise to enhance personal knowledge management skills.

Brent MacKinnon's Experience

Consultant/Owner

Social Media Tools for Work & Learning

January 2009 – Present (4 years 11 months)

My consultancy’s purpose is to introduce social media tools, showcase their benefits and support their use by practitioners and organizations who recognize the value of social media for bringing positive change to people, organizations, networks and community. My services center on supporting and using appropriate social media tools with organizations and individuals so that communities are strengthened, learning is enhanced, networks are stronger and workers are engaging their clients with the most effective tools possible.

Program Manager

Street Kids International

Nonprofit; 11-50 employees; Nonprofit Organization Management industry

September 2006 – April 2009 (2 years 8 months)

Street Kids International partners with youth-serving organizations around the world to deliver entrepreneurship and employment programs. As Manager of Canadian Programming I was hired to initiate this pilot project that sought to create new approaches to address youth poverty in Canada. My project was called Streetjibe - addressing youth poverty in York Region. The project brought together youth workers, teachers, mental health professionals, outreach workers to learn from each other, build new skills and increase their networking and collaboration effectiveness. We used a learning community approach based on community of practice model to support this process. To complement the learning process we used a private webspace (blog) to promote ongoing dialogue and sharing resources among members. The project demonstrated significant success in bringing about cross sector collaboration and developing new practitioner skills.

Program Manager

Community Resource & Learning Room

June 2003 – June 2006 (3 years 1 month)

I designed this community based program and launched it with the support of the York Region District School Board. The program connected youth who where not "thriving" within the high school learning structure with youth run projects centered on community (civic) development opportunities.

Executive Director

Operation Catch Up

September 2002 – September 2003 (1 year 1 month)

Hired to transition the organization towards greater governance and operational accountability. This program offerred social and academic support for young people at risk who were struggling within the school system.

Program Manager

Square One Youth Centre

1996 – 2002 (6 years)

I was the founding Manager of this collaborative initiative that brought together youth serving organizations, young people, business representatives and the United Way of Peel Region. The organization delivered a range of services to at risk youth in a multi service facility within the largest shopping and retail establishment in Ontario.

Community Development Coordinator

Jane Finch Community & Family Centre

Nonprofit Organization Management industry

1992 – 1995 (3 years)

The Jane Finch Centre offered community development, settlement, mental health, local administrative services and general support to residents living in the Jane Finch area of North York, Toronto. I shared the Executive Director role with two other Coordinators.

Program Manager

Kinark Child & Family Services

Nonprofit; 501-1000 employees; Mental Health Care industry

1991 – 1992 (1 year)

I was hired to manage a residential treatment program for young people who had experienced significant losses throughout their lives. In this role I designed a treatment approach that utilized a unique grief counseling model.

Executive Director

Operation Pride

1987 – 1989 (2 years)

I was hired to redesign this employment training program for severely employment disadvantaged youth. Our program gave high risk youth an opportunity to gain life and work skills so they can successfully move to wards greater independence. The program delivered life skills, summer landscaping work and winter snow removal services along with ongoing support.

Instrutor/Field Placement Suppervisor

Grant MacEwan & George Brown Communit College

1984 – 1986 (2 years)

I helped design a course in community based youth work at Grant MacEwan and eventually taught the course over several semesters. At George Brown I supervised a group of Child and Youth Workers completing their field placement obligations.

Program Manager

Inner City Youth Program

1982 – 1986 (4 years)

Operating within MacMan Youth Services, I was hired to design, launch and manage Edmonton's first street work program. Our street work team connected with inner city youth engaged in the sex trade and criminal sub cultures. Many of these youth were transient, running from broken unsupportive families and detaching themselves from school and social service systems.

Counselor - Child & Youth Worker

Youth Development Centre

1977 – 1982 (5 years)

I was part of a treatment team working with 12 - 15 youth in a residential facility serving young people from all over Alberta and the North West Territories. These were extremely hard to serve youth with a long history of significant losses along with criminal charges. The Youth Centre was a large (over 125 female & male youth) controlled treatment setting were youth were detained under the Juvenile Delinquents and the Child & Family Services Act.

  • Social Artistry - A Virtual Knowledge Cafe (private space for learning about social artistry)

    • February 2012 to June 2012
    Team Members: Brent MacKinnon, Michele Martin

    Our initiative, co-created by Michele and Brent, was an experiment in learning about social artistry. Social artistry is the art of enhancing human capacities in the light of social complexity. It seeks to bring new ways of thinking, being and doing to social challenges in the world.

    We attracted over 50 individuals from around the world to join this private platform so they could share how they practice social artistry in their lives. We used a "cafe" structure to organize our discussions into cafe topics.

    …Social Artists are leaders in many fields who bring the same order of passion and skill that an artist brings to his or her art form, to the canvas of our social reality.

    We see our Cafe as an experiment in using social artistry to explore social artistry. It is a space that we will be co-creating together as we ask questions, share our knowledge and interact with each other.

    Michele Martin & Brent MacKinnon
