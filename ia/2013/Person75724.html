Kim Elmose's Summary

Kim Elmose has been an online professional since november 1999 where he started at the first dedicated editorial online team at Computerworld Online (in Denmark) - started previous that year.

Kim Elmose took part in the consolidation of the online team that in the years to come established Computerworld Online as the most powerfull news-team in Denmark - when it came to tech-news.

In 2003 Kim moved on to Engineering Weekly (Nyhedsmagasinet Ingeniøren ) to be senior editor at the online team. One of the milestones he left at the online media was starting the first journalist-blogs in Denmark.

In 2006 he was hired as the first blogeditor in Denmark - to build a blog-community - both editorial blogs and later on , userblogs.

In 2009 he moved on to The Danish Society of Engineers (IDA). He's introduced the organisation to the strategic use of social media tools and Facebook. He's also initiated a major make-over of the site, ida.dk, including building of a new IA.

In the spring of 2012 he headed the research in finding out which cms would be the best for the organisation - and recommended the flexible open source cms Drupal.

Kim was during the years 2003 - 2011 active in Danish Online News Association (DONA) - an organisation dedicated to knowledgesharing about online communication. From 2008 - 2011 he was president of the organisation. People like Steve Outing, Kelly Goto, Rebecca Blood and Dan Gillmor has been speakers at DONA seminars through the years.

Kim has also been a part of the conference board of the Journalist- and communication Festival 14. - 15. november 2010 in Odense - and setup workshops and seminars at the festival.
