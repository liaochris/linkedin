Martin Wilkes' Publications

  • Hydrodynamic Variability in the Physical Biotopes of a Trout Stream

    • 9th International Symposium on Ecohydraulics, Vienna
    • September 1, 2012
    Authors: Martin Wilkes, Fleur Visser, Michael C. Acreman, Ian Maddock

    Conference paper showing that river habitats may be classified on the basis of hydrodynamics.

  • Incorporating hydrodynamics into ecohydraulics: The role of turbulence in the swimming and habitat selection of river-dwelling salmonids (in press)

    • In Kemp, P., Harby, A., Maddock, I. & Wood, P. J. (eds.) Ecohydraulics: An Integrated Approach, Wiley
    Authors: Martin Wilkes, Ian Maddock, Fleur Visser, Michael C. Acreman

    Research-level book chapter reviewing recent trends in environmental fluid dynamics, the use of acoustic Doppler velocimetry and the energetics of fish swimming in turbulent flow. The work identifies future research priorities and provides a reference for those engaged in river management. It focuses on salmonids but many of the topics covered are relevant to a range of other freshwater biota.
