Jake Huber's Summary

I am a young entrepreneur with consulting experience serving clients ranging from solo entrepreneurs to Fortune 100 corporations.

I am currently a co-founder of Gigawatt, which is an online crowdfunding platform that is going to completely change the way colleges approach fundraising and how young alumni donate. I'm also pursuing my MBA with a concentration in Corporate Entrepreneurship at Lehigh University.

In the past, I worked as a business analyst for Lehigh University's Small Business Development Center (SBDC). I've worked one-on-one with clients to help test new business propositions, draft business plans, develop financial cash flow projections, conduct industry and market research, and produce product-to-market strategies. I also worked at Ernst & Young within its audit practice serving public and private companies across several industries, including manufacturing, publishing, telecommunications, and power utilities.

Jake Huber's Projects

  • "Management 3.0" Sponsored Research Project for Lehigh University Enterprise Systems Center

    • May 2012 to August 2012
    Team Members: Jake Huber, Robert Gustafson, Michael Martinez, Catherine Ridings, Tom Brinker, Stephen Senkowski

    Computer Aid, Inc. in partnership with Lehigh University and the Enterprise Systems Center (ESC) has undertaken a research initiative to identify and deliver concrete solutions to the various systemic problems of the current management control systems. During the course of the research study, the ESC has been commissioned with the task of redefining and dramatically improving the current management systems. The ESC team will interview Fortune 1000 C-level executives concerning their experiences with current management systems, propose a structure for an ideal management system we are calling Management 3.0, and obtain their opinions about the impact of such a new system capability. The intent is to publish the Management 3.0 aggregate research results in the Harvard Business Review or similar publication.

  • Nestle Waters KWH Standards Project

    • May 2013 to August 2013
    Team Members: Jake Huber, Robert Gustafson, Jon Schlegel

    Cost analysis/energy efficiency project at Nestle Waters to determine actual KWH usage and update cost standards. Provided an activity based costing analysis of electrical energy usage that established the basis for more accurate energy accounting standards by package. This analysis identified 93% of the electrical energy bill allocation and explained 85% of the 2013 YTD unfavorable usage variance.
