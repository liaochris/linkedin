Angus Davis' Summary

I helped start a small New England ISP in 1995 as a teenager. Moved to California to become Netscape's youngest employee in 1996. Co-founded Tellme with Mike McCue in 1999; acquired by Microsoft in 2007. Founded Swipely in 2009 (back on the east coast). In addition to my entrepreneurship, I am an active angel investor.

Outside of work, I am active in education reform, having served on the state board of education in Rhode Island and on the Board of the Center for Education Reform in Washington, DC.

Specialties

Leadership, product/technology strategy, sales, early stage technology investing, product marketing, etc. The stuff you learn over a decade of start-up life.

Angus Davis' Experience

Founder & CEO

Swipely

Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Internet industry

August 2009 – Present (4 years 1 month) Providence, RI

Swipely helps local merchants accept payments, understand customers and grow sales. We crunch the big data behind every transaction to provide analytics and marketing tools that help local restaurants and retailers grow with online technology. Named to the Forbes 100 list of "America's Most Promising Companies" and "Innovator of the Year" (PBN), Swipely is backed by great investors like Index, First Round and Greylock, who have made the largest private investment in a software company in Rhode Island's history.

Co-Founder

Tellme Networks

Privately Held; 201-500 employees; MSFT; Telecommunications industry

January 1999 – May 2009 (10 years 5 months)

Founded Tellme in 1999 with Mike McCue. Went through many ups and downs, learning experiences and different roles over our journey. Grew business from zero to profitability with over $100 mm in annual revenue from Fortune 100 customers. Shaped software-as-a-service that redefined the industry and changed the way people use their telephone with pioneering speech interfaces. Raised $238 mm from investors including Kleiner Perkins, Benchmark Capital and Jim Barksdale. In 2007, Microsoft acquired Tellme in their largest-ever acquisition of a private company. Integration was a significant exercise, but successful: 304 of Tellme's 312 full-time employees stayed on with Microsoft following the acquisition.

Product Management

Netscape Communiations

Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; NSCP; Computer Software industry

October 1996 – January 1999 (2 years 4 months)

Led prototyping efforts as part of a small technology strategy group led by then-CTO Marc Andreessen. One interesting "special project" was serving as the technical / product resource to the legal team pursuing the DOJ case against Microsoft (ironic since I would later work for Microsoft). I was product manager for the technology today known as Gecko, the core technology inside the Mozilla Firefox Web Browser. Worked on the AOL merger and the launch of Mozilla.org before leaving to start Tellme.

Product Management / Development

Led Web site development for medium-sized businesses like radio stations, TV networks and car dealerships (common customer quote: "We need to get on the information super-highway!") that were establishing their initial presence online at the dawn of the Web. Raised the financing for the company from angel investors. Notably, we had a Class B IP address allocation, which was sort of cool looking back nowadays (155.212).

Angus Davis' Volunteer Experience & Causes

  • Volunteer Experience

    • Regent

      Rhode Island Board of Education
      • Education
      June 2007 – March 2011 (3 years 10 months)

      The 9-member Board of Regents is the chief policy-setting body overseeing elementary and secondary education in Rhode Island, shaping the course of public education to ensure all the state's children receive the best possible education. I co-chaired the search committee to hire our new Commissioner of Education, Deborah Gist, the first change in leadership in 17 years. I chaired the state and charter schools committee, opening the first new charter schools in Rhode Island in more than five years and later raising the cap. I brought Teach for America to Rhode Island. During my tenure, we strengthened graduation standards, introduced meaningful educator evaluation, eliminated the ban on charter schools and passed the state's first "money follows the student" funding formula. As a result of our strategic plan, in 2010 we won a $75 million Federal Race to the Top grant, the largest competitive federal grant ever received in Rhode Island history. My goal in this work is to close the achievement gap between low- and high-income children.

BV Prep has successfully reversed the achievement gap and its students consistently demonstrate among the highest achievement levels in the state. Achievement First is one of the nation's top-performing CMOs as measured by student achievement.
