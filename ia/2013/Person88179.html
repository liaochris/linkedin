Mary Parke's Summary

Instructional Designer and eLearning expert specializing in distance learning program structure, technology, and faculty professional development - from accreditation including curriculum & legal compliance, to establishing and delivering faculty training & certification programs in best practices for creating and facilitating online & hybrid courses. Additional proven experience in implementation, systems analysis, and project management for LMS platforms. I'm an advocate for eLearning solutions that help diverse communities understand current technologies that support efficient, scalable, measurable content delivery and assessment. I am frequently hired to analyze existing systems, transition communities to new systems, create program processes and procedures, establish knowledge management, and then move on to the next project/location - while leaving the client in a self-sustaining position for the next 2-3 tech years.

    Authors: Mary Parke

    This paper investigates the history of the Ohlone along with government policy and legislation that shaped the formation of this Native American nation of people and continues to impact their existence. At the time I wrote this paper I was an Assistant Teacher in a third grade classroom at Live Oak School in San Francisco, CA. Part of the curriculum for third grade is teaching the history of California and San Francisco, and the Ohlone were the Native people of San Francisco. I used this research in my classroom teaching. The paper was a requirement for EED 678.4 - "Cultural and Linguistic Diversity in the Elementary Classroom" as part of the California Multiple Subject Teacher Credentialing Program at San Francisco State University.

    • University of Illinois at Chicago

    This paper was a requirement for my Social Policy Analysis course at the University of Illinois at Chicago for the requirements of the Bachelor of Social Work program. However, the topic represented my personal interest in the Native American tribes of the United States and was also relevant to work I was doing in my internship placement.  The analysis was used by my internship supervisor in court to aid in the reuniting of a Native American family caught up in the Department of Child and Family Services (DCFS) system in Chicago, and aided in their subsequent relocation as a family to tribal lands. The paper also includes copies of the legislation with my annotations. All comments/markup on the paper are courtesy of Dr. Dubey.
