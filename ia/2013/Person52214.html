Heather Desurvire's Summary

The work I do ensures your game, software, website, smartphone, or product becomes as successful as possible by ensuring the user's experience is highly enjoyable, accessible, engaging,usable, immersive, and fun!

I have been working in the industry for over a decade on several games (as well as software and websites). I've worked for Fortune 100/500 companies such as Microsoft, LucasArts (Star Wars, Indy), Electronic Arts (Lord of the Rings, CnC), AOL, THQ (Full Spectrum Warrior, Tak, Dawn of War, Cars, Wall-E and many other titles), Disney (Girlfriends, Friends), Sega (Medieval, Universe At War), Fandango, Citibank, Symantec, the department of Defense (Strategy Game), Activision (Game Interface) among many others.

The work of playability/usability has assisted many design teams in ensuring the customer's expectations and experiences are highly enjoyable, fun, accessible playable, and usable. Thus, increasing the game's likelihood of success.


I have presented papers on Playability/Usability at the highly esteemed professional group, the ACM Special Interest Group CHI (Computer Human Interaction) and (INTERACT) on research work on playability and usability on Good game design evaluation methodologies, as well as game Accessibility and Approachability for Casual Players. I have also published articles in professional journals and books. Please see the website www.behavioristics.com

Specialties

I do Playability/Usability Evaluation for Video Games, as well as for software and websites. I ensure your customers are having the best user experience, which helps ensure your product or game's success.

Heather Desurvire's Publications

  • Game Princples: Change, Choice, & Creation: Making Better Games

    • ACM SIG CHI 2013
    • March 20, 2013
    Authors: Heather Desurvire, Dennis Wixon, Ph D.

    Demonstrating through a user study, the effective use of Heuristics PLAY and GAP on improving the game designs' player experience. Of note, the study shows evidence that the Heuristics are effective in not only finding player experience problems, but finding fixes as well as even more interestingly, what is working well as well as enhancing the design beyond it's current working state.

  • Desurvire, H., Wixon, D. (2013). Game Principles- Change, Choice and Creation: Making Better Games. CHI Extended Abstracts.

    • ACM SIG CHI Abstracts 2013
    • May 7, 2013
    Authors: Heather Desurvire, Dennis Wixon, Ph D.

    Heuristics or player experience principles have evolved to assist designers and evaluators in creating better games. Heuristics are intended to provide a structure for improving game design. The technique of using heuristics to review designs has gained wide acceptance in productivity products. The PLAY heuristics [2] is the further iteration of HEP [1], general principles of optimal player experience. GAP is another set of principles, focused on first time player, tutorial use and initial game play. We conducted a study to assess the benefits of using heuristics for games. Results showed that heuristics are more effective than "unassisted intuition" not only in identifying problems, but also in inspiring recommendations for enhancements to the games' player experience. Future analysis of the data will take this analysis further, examining the quality of recommendations.
