Chris Nickols' Summary

Everything used to be simple.

Drums and guitar were my passions for as long as I could remember. Writing songs and gigging around town with the band had completely dominated my thoughts, dreams and focus. Then I got my first taste of web development...

Things didn't seem so simple anymore.

I don't know what it was about coding that connected so deeply with me, but I became completely obsessed. Strangely, writing code seemed just as artistic as any song I had ever written. I could create! Gradually, my focus shifted entirely from music to development. My poor wife can attest to all the nights I told her I'd be to bed soon - only to wake and find me still coding away in the early morning.

Complexity became natural.

Over time I found myself doing more and more custom e-commerce apps for small to mid-sized businesses. So around 15 years ago, it made sense for my wife and I to start our development company, Upside Down Design. Our clients' businesses grew and we starting doing more complex applications like complete inventory management control and automating almost every aspect of their business workflow that we could. The challenge (and I LOVE a challenge) was to make the user interfaces as easy and intuitive as possible even while the underlying systems were ever so complex.

We used this discipline as a foundation for our growing niche of developing custom personalization applications. Interchangeable jewelry lines, photo baby announcements, content management systems are but a few of the apps we've built. This led my wife and I to launch our latest startup Whobaloo.

Whobaloo is an online graphic design platform that makes it easy to create digital and printed cards and images. You have complete control over the colors, font styles and layouts. Add as many of your own photos and images as you want as well as anything from our growing library of independent art. We make it easy for you to make it awesome.

And now it's all simple again.
