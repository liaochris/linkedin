Joanne Martin's Experience

Director

The Therapeutic Writing Network

October 2011 – Present (2 years 3 months)

The Therapeutic Writing Network is the portal/hub/centre for expressive, reflective, and therapeutic writing practitioners and their clients. We offer clients and practitioners alike the broadest access possible to therapeutic writing information, training, methods, professional networks, research, resources, tools, techniques, workshops, etc.

Director, Counsellor, Facilitator

C-Change Counselling

1996 – Present (17 years)

Some change we seek - like career change. Some change comes with time--like aging or retiring. We may not like it; but we know it's inevitable. Then there are those unexpected changes that often cause great upheaval. In every case, change shifts the narrative of our lives. At C-Change through writing, reading, journaling, and story-telling, we help people to create the new story of their lives. Amidst the turmoil of change, you too can discover how to live your life with meaning and with purpose.

Employment Advisor, Centre for Career Education

University of Windsor

Educational Institution; 5001-10,000 employees; Higher Education industry

September 2007 – April 2008 (8 months)

Advised students and graduates relative to employment and career planning.

Engaged students in experiential learning relative to job search strategies, interview techniques, etc.

Worked in cooperation with other members of the Career Education team to develop materials, program and strategies to promote the employment readiness of students and graduates.

Lecturer, Centre for Higher Education & Professional Development

Macquarie University

Educational Institution; 1001-5000 employees; Higher Education industry

January 1996 – September 1996 (9 months)

Developed and delivered staff development seminars for Macquarie University academic staff in "Teacher as Facilitator," experiential learning, problem-based and resource-based teaching methods.

Under the direction of the Pro-Vice-Chancellor, conducted research into the Assessment and development of generic skills in higher education: The aim of this project was to develop a means of assessing the level of generic skill among university students at entry level and at periodic intervals during their degree program. it also recommended specific teaching and learning assessment methods and strategies to facilitate the development of generic skills.

Developed and delivered a "Train the Tutor" program for a Superannuation Management program offered by the Macquaire University Centre for Open Education in conjunction with the Association of Superannuation Funds of Australia limited (ASFA).

Coordinator - Co-operative Education

University of Waterloo

Educational Institution; 5001-10,000 employees; Higher Education industry

1986 – 1996 (10 years)

Placed an average of sixty university students with "co-operative" employers per semester and, through coaching and counselling, assisted those students with their career planning and development; maintained liaison with students under personal placement mandate, on campus and through on-site visits to the workplace, to facilitate experiential learning and to provide life skills training, coaching and career counselling.

Developed marketing and communications strategies and programs designed to recruit both students and employers to participate in Co-operative Education programs at the University of Waterloo; Contributed significantly to the growth of these programs, particularly through the successful launch of such programs as the multi-disciplinary International Trade Program.

Provided liaison between the university and employers in both the public and private sectors to provide experiential learning opportunities for university students, to meet the real recruitment and productivity needs of employers and to ensure that academic programs continue to meet their needs for skilled human resources.

Developed and delivered workshops, seminars, and learning materials to prepare students for Career Planning and Job Search, and to facilitate their learning through dialogue, reflection, and processing of their work experience; Designed and delivered staff training programs in Meeting Facilitation, Communication Skills, Conflict Resolution, and Team Building.

Maintained liaison with faculty and administration of the university, providing input relative to admissions, academic standards, program content and design.

Human Resources Advisor

Imperial Oil Limited

Public Company; 5001-10,000 employees; IMO; Oil & Energy industry

1975 – 1986 (11 years)

Developed, implemented, documented, communicated and administered national corporate relocation policies, programs and procedures to facilitate the relocation of Imperial Oil Limited and Esso Resources Canada employees and their families between company locations (approximately 500 domestic transfers per year at that time), and updated such policies, as required, in response to changing economic factors.

Consulted to senior Imperial Oil Limited executives and to regional managers, advising them relative to appropriate compensation packages for the relocation of key personnel, recommending changes to existing policies, and encouraging consideration of relocation costs in human resources development and succession planning.

Provided support to the Management Development Committee of Imperial Oil Limited’s Marketing Department, integrating the training needs and career interests of employees with the succession and business plans of the corporation to develop professional development and training strategies. Documented Human Resources succession planning and candidate selection processes within the department.

Monitored the Employee Performance Review Programs of the Marketing department on a national basis, to ensure that all management, professional and technical employees received timely and thorough performance reviews.

Conducted salary and benefits surveys, and provided support for labour negotiations and development of contingency planning.

Documented discussions of the Joint Industrial Council (employee/management labour relations committee) for distribution to all levels of the Imperial Oil Limited organization; documented discussions of the Work Error Review Committee in determining the cause of industrial accidents, and in recommending appropriate remedial and/or disciplinary action.
