Matt A. Getz's Summary

As of May 2012, I began working as a paralegal in the International Arbitration department of Curtis, Mallet-Prevost, Colt & Mosle LLP in New York. At Curtis, I manage documentation, perform legal research, and conduct translations pertaining to international commercial and Investor-State arbitration in institutions around the world.

At Columbia University, I was a double major in Political Science and Hispanic Studies. My coursework also covered the classics, philosophy, literature, anthropology, and languages. I graduated summa cum laude in May 2012 and was elected into my school's Phi Beta Kappa chapter.

My strengths include researching complex issues or subjects and integrating broad tracts of research into concise presentations; drafting or editing any piece of writing; developing efficient ways to multitask; and interacting with people in collaborative situations.

Matt A. Getz's Publications

  • The Privilege of Normality: Latin American Fiscal Space, Countercyclical Policy and the 2008 Financial Crisis

    • Journal of Politics & Society (Columbia University)
    • October 2012
    Authors: Matt A. Getz

    An extended version of "Rainy Day Politics" (see below), this article presents the complete picture of the critical choices made before and after the 2008 financial crisis in the seven largest economies of Latin America. It poses and attempts to answer the following question: what can governments do to weather the storms of peaks and troughs in the global economy?

  • Rainy Day Politics: Latin American Fiscal Policy and the 2008 Financial Crisis

    • Columbia Economics Review
    • September 2012
    Authors: Matt A. Getz

    The 2008 global financial crisis presented Latin American economies with serious socioeconomic and political challenges. This paper examines fiscal policy in the run-up to the crisis and concludes that countercyclical fiscal policy in good times can enable countries to better handle severe economic downturns.

  • The Chile Winter: Education Reform and Lessons for Global Protests

    • Columbia Political Review
    • December 19, 2011
    Authors: Matt A. Getz

    The ongoing student protests in Chile represent the most sustained social mobilization in Chile in over a decade. In this article I discuss the state of education in Chile, the reasons for the movement's contingent success and its prospects for the future.

  • Acknowleding the Americas: Charting a New Course for American Foreign Policy

    • Columbia Political Review
    • October 24, 2011
    Authors: Matt A. Getz

    Despite Obama's intention to refocus U.S. policy toward Latin America, he has thus far failed to create a consistent or coherent policy in the hemisphere. This article discusses the sources of this deficiency—most notably, a debilitating lack of understanding about new political actors in Latin America—and identifies areas where Obama can push for progress.

  • Moving Beyond Football: A multi-image analysis of the 1969 Salvadoran invasion of Honduras

    • Publius
    • February 2011
    Authors: Matt A. Getz

    This paper presents the underlying causes of the 1969 Honduras-El Salvador conflict, including oligarchic resource allocation, domestic politics, the security dilemma and preemptive warfare. This paper seeks to understand the populational and domestic factors at the heart of conflicts and to demonstrate how these types of conflicts may reoccur in the future.
