Meg Stolt's Summary

My experience encompasses a variety of modalities and practice. I started out as a lifeguard and have worked at many pools and beaches. I still maintain my certification, as I continue to work in and around pools. I have taught (and continue to) teach swimming technique to children through adults, for water safety and skill development. I also coach runners and triathletes from a 5K to ironman. The pool environment has provided a tool to train deep water running, as well as work with post-rehab protocols for return to play. This is my primary passion-- running, training and competing.
I also work in athletic development: or preparation for sport. This encompasses determining where an athlete is, and where she needs to be to perform. This is not about competing more, but competing efficiently.

Through a series of events, I was forced to reflect and refocus my goals and objectives. In this, I have returned to coaching. I currently serve as the Head Field Hockey coach for Sturgis Charter School, East as well as with several groups of runners and triathletes. These experiences have shown me how much I missed coaching. I look forward to continuing in this pursuit, and expanding to a track and field team.

My writing includes Continuing Education for the AEA, and my presenting includes the Internation Aquatic Fitness Conference. I have also taught physical education on the college level at John Jay College for Criminal Justice and New Jersey City University.

I am interested in doing more work in baseball and/or softball. I have worked with several players and I love the sport.

It is also my personal goal to run the marathons in both polar regions, and run on the bottom of the ocean on the North Shore (HI) and compete in the iron man (again) before I am 50.

Specialties: running, deep water running, swimming, return to play, cycling, cycling, and triathletes, motivational speaking, educational seminars and workshops.
