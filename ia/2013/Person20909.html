Catherine Garceau's Summary

Catherine Garceau, who was recently featured on Entertainment Tonight and Canada AM, believes that physical, mental, emotional, and spiritual wholeness occurs by adopting lifestyle practices that honor it all while above all, embracing life’s lessons with faith and courage. Garceau shares insights of her journey of self-discovery to empower audiences to think outside the box, create new thought patterns and adopt behaviors that lead to personal breakthroughs and self-care practices, no matter what life circumstances come your way. Her energy and enthusiasm will inspire you to dive in and find life’s pearls of wisdom and use them to pursue your life’s incredible possibilities.

The release of Swimming Out of Water will be followed by Catherine’s EAT 360: Emotional Awareness Training for 360 Degrees of Nourishment program. This program helps women to free themselves from emotional eating as it addresses the emotional, physical, mental and spiritual intricacies of our relationship with food and body, so that we can understand and free ourselves completely.

Catherine Garceau's Projects

  • EAT 360 - Emotional Awareness Training for 360 Degrees of Nourishment

    • January 2013 to Present
    Team Members: Catherine Garceau, Johnny Blackburn

    Catherine joined forces with Johnny Blackburn, a certified Integral Life Coach, to co-create EAT 360. This 8 week home study program covers beliefs, emotional intelligence, conscious eating, the power of slowness, movement, and spirituality through a variety of audio tracks, study guide exercises, and EFT (emotional freedom techniques) training. The programs follow through and personal accountability is enhanced with the EAT APP, a phone application designed to apply the concepts learned through the program.

    Johnny Blackburn earned the M.A. in Psychology from Saybrook University, the B.A. in Business Management from the University of Southern California and a professional license and certification in Clinical Nutrition from the Natural Healing Institute. He has extensive clinical experience, having worked in various centers for alternative holistic healing and addictions. Catherine and Johnny have combined their unique insights to create a long lasting way for women to get back into the driver’s seat of navigating their relationship with their mind, body and food.

Catherine Garceau's Publications

  • Swimming Out of Water

    • Morgan James
    • July 17, 2012
    Authors: Catherine Garceau

    Swimming Out of Water takes you through Garceau's life-altering experience of being caught alone in the Red Rocks of Las Vegas for 24 hours. Weaving through the many life stories that lead her to the Olympic podium in Sydney 2000 in synchronized swimming, she reveals the secret struggles she had with eating disorders and the many wellness discoveries as she took on her own healing journey. Studying mind-body nutrition and realizing the negative effects of chlorine byproducts for swimmers, Swimming Out of Water is only the beginning of her plan to advocate and educate for better applications of the information she so passionately shares not only on the subject of Chlorine Byproducts, but also for applicable solutions to emotional eating.
