Julia Yong-hee Park's Summary

Julia is the Managing Director of Advantage America New York Regional Center (AANYRC), a federally designated EB-5 Regional Center. AANYRC was approved by the USCIS in March 2013.

In addition, Julia is an immigration attorney focusing on EB-5 Immigrant Investor Visas. The combination of Julia's past business/securities law experience at a major Wall Street law firm and her current immigration practice has placed Julia in a unique position to focus on the highly-specialized area of EB-5 immigrant visas.

Julia is a frequent speaker on the topic of EB-5 law. Most recently she spoke at:

--Where’s the Money? Crowdsourcing and Other New Financing Alternatives (October 10, 2012)(Sponsored by the New Jersey Entrepreneurial Network.)

--The EB-5 Investor Vista Program (September 17, 2012)(Sponsored by AREAA New Jersey Chapter)

-- Alternative Financing Options for Real Estate Clients (December 6, 2011) (Co-sponsored by Citrin Cooperman, Signature Bank, Johnson Capital and Think Forward Financial.)

-- 2011 AREAA (Asian Real Estate Association of America) National Convention, Make the World Your Market: Working with International Clients panel (October 1, 2011).

-- The Art of the EB-5 Real Estate Transaction (June 2011) (Co-sponsored by Akerman Senterfitt, Urban Land Institute, and NYU Schack Institute of Real Estate)

-- Alternative Capital: EB-5 Opportunity, a New Frontier for Financial Industry - Regional Center Development and Investors Procurement Seminar (January 2011)

-- HSBC EB-5 Seminar for Premier Korean Clients (January 2011)

Please email her at jpark[at]juliaparklaw.com if you would like to invite her to speak at your organization or event.

In addition to her main EB-5 practice, Julia provides special-education legal services to Korean immigrant parents and special-needs children in New Jersey. This practice grew out of her personal passion and experience as a parent of a special-needs child.

Julia Yong-hee Park's Organizations

  • IIUSA

    Member, Public Policy Committee
    • August 2013 to Present

    Founded in 2005, the Association to Invest In the USA (IIUSA®) is the national membership-based 501(c)(6) not-for-profit industry trade association for the EB-5 Regional Center Program (the “Program”). IIUSA’s primary mission is to make the Program a permanent and successful part of 21st century U.S. economic development policy. Federally-designated, active EB-5 Regional Centers – the public and/or private entities approved to serve as the conduits of foreign direct investment (“FDI”) and regional economic development in the Program – are IIUSA’s core members. As a reflection of the international and interdisciplinary makeup of the Regional Center industry, IIUSA also has Associate members from numerous professions and from around the world, including: attorneys, broker-dealers, consultants, developers, economists, financial advisors, migration intermediaries, etc. To date, IIUSA represents 99 Regional Center members and 180+ Associate members, with our Regional Centers accounting for over 95% of all the EB-5 capital formation (billions of dollars), resulting U.S. job creation (tens of thousands of jobs), and tax revenue (over a billion dollars).

    The State of New York provides only a portion of the LaGuardia Community College’s annual operating budget. Private support enables LaGuardia to provide scholarships to deserving students, fund quality education and research programs and develop new and innovative programs.

    The mission of the LaGuardia Community College Foundation is to raise, manage, invest and distribute funds for the College in support of its immediate and long-term student, academic and facility needs, goals and priorities. Funds are used primarily for scholarships, emergency support for students, textbooks, transportation, faculty research and programs.
