Christopher Nahil's Summary

With 25 years of communications and marketing experience in the B2B technology arena, I have developed communications programs and campaigns for all manner of companies from venture-backed start-ups to high-growth mode organizations to international market leaders. As an experienced communications pro, I am an expert in positioning, messaging, content creation and management, media/analyst/influencer relations (via print, electronic, online and social media), and PR program measurement. At the same time, my experience as an entrepreneur allows me to bring fresh ideas and new approaches to communications problems that don’t rely on the tired tactics of “traditional” high-tech PR.

I am proud of my reputation as an excellent mentor, coach and team builder, and I am very comfortable counseling senior executives. I also enjoy the role of corporate spokesperson, in good times and in crises.

A writer at heart, I am passionate about content development and content marketing across a range of media and platforms. I also volunteer with the PEN/New England Prison Writing Program as a writing workshop teacher.

Specialties

Communications, public relations, social media and digital marketing, executive coaching, content marketing, content creation and curation, leadership, mentoring, teaching.

Christopher Nahil's Publications

  • A Home on Haven Street: 50 Years of MIT Endicott House

    • MIT
    • 2005
    Authors: Christopher Nahil, Joseph C. Nahil

    The book was commissioned by MIT to mark the fiftieth year of the Institute’s stewardship of the former Endicott and Weld family seats in Dedham, Mass. "A Home on Haven Street" celebrates the rich history of the manor home — now in use as an elegant conference center — by tracing the impact of the three influential New England families who have created, expanded and maintained the estate and its grounds since the property’s conception just after the Civil War. The book is divided into three sections, each dedicated to one of the three families who have left their mark on the property: the Weld family, the Endicott family and the MIT “family.”
