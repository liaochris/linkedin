Beth Taylor's Summary

Beth Taylor is an award-winning communications expert. She has earned awards in journalism, public relations, marketing, advertising, political consulting, video production, and photography. She is also an award-winning businesswoman - named one of Mississippi's 50 Leading Businesswomen - having successfully owned and operated a full-service communications agency for nearly 20-years.

Taylor's honors include a PRisim Award and Best of Show from the Public Relations Association of Mississippi, plus awards from the College Public Relations Association of Mississippi for work on the 2010 Deepwater Horizon, Gulf of Mexico oil spill. She also holds awards from the Associated Press, is a multiple Telly Award winner, has several ADDY awards, and awards from the Mississippi Association of Broadcasters. She has the distinction of being the first South Mississippi Ad-Fed member to earn the American Advertising Federation Silver Medal® award.

Beth Taylor recently authored and published her first book - "Bless Them Father, for They Have Sinned" - which chronicles her case of sexual abuse in the Catholic Church, and repressed memories which caused her to "forget" the traumatic events for roughly 40 years before the memories resurfaced as horrific flashbacks.

Taylor holds the distinction of being Mississippi's first female television sportscaster, having filled the "anchor" seat at NBC affiliate, WDAM-TV.

She is a civic leader who has been recognized for her community service by the Gulf Pines Girl Scout Council, the Domestic Abuse Family Shelter, the Salvation Army, and DREAM of Hattiesburg. For more than 25-years, she served in leadership positions on the Boards of Directors of numerous non-profit agencies; working with grants and managing teams of professionals. She is also a graduate of the prestigious "Leaders for a New Century" community program.

Beth Taylor is a New Orleans native and a 1977 graduate of Louisiana State University in Baton Rouge.
