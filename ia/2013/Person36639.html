Don Estes' Summary

Specializes in the unique challenges of legacy modernization projects: how to ensure that maximum business value is secured, the new system works correctly in all aspects, and that expensive cost overruns or outright project failures are avoided. With our approach, the success of your modernization efforts are assured.

There is a tension between gaining the greatest value from a legacy modernization, and avoiding the risks that are all too frequently attendant on these projects. In general, the lowest risk projects deliver the least business value, and the highest business value projects are accompanied by an unacceptable project risk profile.

Our specialized consultancy has developed a methodology to optimize between risk and business value. The current project at the United States Patent and Trademark Office demonstrates that it is possible to drive optimal business value through a business architecture approach, while simultaneously ensuring that the legacy business rules are extracted and re-implemented completely and correctly. There are multiple tactics to employ in this endeavor, with different recommendations depending on the unique circumstances in each organization and technical details of the modernization challenge.

Our modernization solutions derive from diverse technical and business experiences across the range of industries and technical environments, including extensive public sector and international experience with both vendors and end-users.

Data Migration Solution Architect/Contract

USPTO

Government Agency; 5001-10,000 employees; Government Administration industry

August 2012 – Present (1 year 2 months) Alexandria, VA

This project is a variation on the Federal Reserve project discussed below. Instead of a test driven approach, this project uses a form of automated production parallel testing. Since all transactions are submitted to both legacy and modernized systems, the results can be compared in real time. If a discrepancy is detected, then diagnostic procedures provide for an efficient resolution of the incorrect implementation. The project is on-going, with the first results expected later this year.

The original challenge was to provide a practical solution for a complex data migration challenge as part of the modernization of the trademark applications. The business requires that the legacy mainframe application and the replacement Trademark Next Generation application must run with their databases synchronized for 3 years. Synchronizing the databases creates the infrastructure to also perform automated production parallel testing.

The solution to this challenge provides a unique opportunity to ensure that the legacy business rules are correctly extracted from the COBOL and ALGOL programs. The synchronization methodology submits equivalent transactions to the other system for each transaction received on either system, and then compares the results of processing to ensure equivalence. If the results are not equivalent, then the business rules were not correctly implemented. An integrated diagnostic capability will allow for rapid pinpointing of the divergent implementations. In this way, the incorrect implementation (which might be legacy or the Next Generation system) will be rapidly and efficiently corrected.

This unusual project, which is derived from business architecture principles to elicit the greatest business value for the USPTO, also is able to leverage the value of the legacy assets to ensure a satisfactory outcome to the project.
