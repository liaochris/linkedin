Director of Technology

Denver School of Science and Technology

Educational Institution; 51-200 employees; Education Management industry

June 2004 – July 2009 (5 years 2 months)

I am a founding member of the staff of the Denver School of Technology. DSST was the first public high school in Colorado to roll out a 1 to 1 laptop program; every HS student is presented with a laptop or tablet computer for use in and outside the classroom. Andrew Zucker, nationally known researcher on educational technology was commissioned to do a study of our program in the fall of 2007. Glowing results of that study are available on our website.

This study, coupled with the documented and much touted success of DSST's academic achievement, indicate the power of technology in education when used as a orchestrated tool in a school culture and context strongly oriented toward student learning, not as an end in itself, or a panacea for education's problems.

In the fall of 2008, DSST added a Middle School and serves as a model school for the Texas TSTEM project.

This fall DSST will use approximately 650 wireless HP laptops and tablets and operates on a Cisco network.
