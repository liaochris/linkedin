Brenda Bence's Summary

Founder & President of Brand Development Assocs (BDA) Int'l. With offices in the U.S. and Asia, BDA specializes in helping companies and individuals build successful, growth-oriented corporate and personal brands. As a Certified Speaking Professional, Certified Executive Coach, and experienced trainer, Brenda has worked with hundreds of executives, leaders, and entrepreneurs around the world to help them define and communicate their corporate and personal brands.

With an MBA from Harvard Business School, Brenda spent the first 20 years of her career building mega brands for Fortune 100 companies like Procter & Gamble and Bristol-Myers Squibb where she was a senior executive responsible for businesses across four continents and 50 countries.

In 2002, Brenda left the corporate world and founded BDA. Now doing business across 30 countries and six continents, BDA clients include BCG Consulting, Credit Suisse, Danone Int’l, Deloitte, KFC, Kraft, Lilly, Mattel, Microsoft, Pizza Hut, Radisson Hotels, RBS, Sheraton Hotels, Standard Chartered Bank, UBS, U.S. Department of State, and the Young Presidents Organization.

Brenda travels intensively to present her unique approach to branding at conferences, conventions, and corporations all across the globe.

An avid writer, Brenda is author of four award-winning branding books. As a columnist on executive coaching, leadership, and corporate & personal branding, Brenda's articles have been featured in more than 300 media outlets all across the world including Reader's Digest, Financial Times, Investor's Business Daily, Entrepreneur Magazine, Kiplinger's Personal Finance, SmartMoney, TheStreet.com, and The Los Angeles Times. Brenda is a frequent guest on radio and television shows.

Brenda sits on boards of both public and private companies as well as not-for-profit organizations. Having traveled to 75 countries, Brenda enjoys studying foreign languages, and is a keen Mahjong player. See www.BrendaBence.com.
