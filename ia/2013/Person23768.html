Chris Solarski's Summary

I'm an artist video game designer and author of 'Drawing Basics and Video Game Art: Classic to Cutting Edge Art Techniques for Winning Video Game Design' (Watson Guptill 2012).

I'm interested in classical art and design theory, and how video game interaction has changed the way in which we interpret and engage with images. My particular focus is on iconography and primary shapes, and their ability to convey complex emotions.

I received a BA in computer animation and began working as a 3D character and environment artist for Sony Computer Entertainment in London. Eventually, I enrolled in art classes at the Warsaw Academy of Fine Arts, where my interest in applying classical art techniques to video games began. I currently develop my own video games under SOLARSKI STUDIO, with the aim of exploring new forms of player interaction and creating more expressive and varied emotional experiences in games. I also work as art director at Gbanga—a location-based gaming start-up—and lecture at SAE in Zurich, Switzerland.

My unique experience has given me the opportunity to talk on the topic of classical art techniques and video game art at events including, The Art of Video Games Exhibition at the Smithsonian Museum in Washington DC.; Fantoche International Animation Film Festival; Toronto Digifest; and Develop Conference in Brighton, UK.

Chris Solarski's Experience

Author

Watson-Guptill Publications

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Publishing industry

March 2011 – Present (2 years 10 months) Zurich

I've written a book on drawing fundamentals for game art and design, to be published by the art imprint of Random House. The book defines the aesthetics of video games by illustrating the medium's visual links to classical art. Distinguished video games are studied alongside artworks by the Old Masters to demonstrate techniques that will enable game designers to develop games that convey meaningful, and more varied emotional experiences.

The book features a foreword by Tristan Donovan—author of Replay: The History of Video Games—and an endorsement from John Romero—co-founder of id Software and CEO of Loot Drop, Inc.
