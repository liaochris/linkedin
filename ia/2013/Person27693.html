Graphics Manager

Exhibitgroup/Giltspur

Public Company; 501-1000 employees; VVI; Marketing and Advertising industry

December 2000 – May 2002 (1 year 6 months) Fremont, CA

San Francisco Division

Exhibitgroup/Giltspur was one of the largest trade-show exhibit companies in the world. I was contacted by senior management in the spring of 2000, and was invited to interview for an opening as the Manager of the Graphics Group in the San Francisco Facility. I had just signed a letter of intent with a start-up, so no interview took place. But, 8 months later, the start-up crashed and burned, and shortly thereafter I was hired by EG/G to manage their Graphics Group. In the beginning, I had only one direct report. But, within 6 months, I was given responsibility of Graphics Production, which included 3 more direct reports, all union employees. Then, a few months later, I was given another direct report, the Graphic Design Department. Everything was working out great; design was hitting the mark of satisfying the clients with an increasingly higher rate, the graphic artists were setting-up files for print with little or no problems, and the production department was outputting wide-format graphics with very few reprints. Then 911 happened. If no one would fly, then trade-shows suffered the consequences. The Graphics Department dried up, I was promoted to VP Operations, and eventually helped the President close the Facility. I left in the Spring of 2002.

Graphic Artist

Crocker Bank

October 1976 – September 1986 (10 years)

Crocker Bank was as exciting as it gets. And for a bank, that's saying allot. Banking can be a very dry subject. But, the department I worked in was anything but dull. Originally started in 1974 by the new incoming Chairman, Tom Wilcox from Citi Bank, the Crocker Graphics Department handled all of the graphics for the Shareholders, Board of Directors, Executive Committee, Policy Committee, and all members of the senior staff. Departments included; Economics, International Banking, Domestic Banking, the Money Market Dept., Electronic Banking, Corporate Banking, the Advertising Group and Corporate Affairs. In February of 1986, the Brit's sold Crocker to competitor Wells Fargo Bank. I was an Officer of the Bank and Assistant Manager of the Graphics Department, so I was asked to stay on to help in the transition. In May of 1986, Wells announced the layoff of 7,500 former Crocker employees.

Graphic Artist

MARCOR

September 1973 – June 1974 (10 months)

This was my first full-time "paying" job. While attending San Diego State, I started at MARCOR doing "paste-up" of Yellow Page Ads for military base magazines. In a short period of time I was advanced over 6 other artists to work directly with the Snr. Graphic Designer on the full-color, editorial pages. Everything was performed on drawing boards using rapidiograph pens, straight-edges. triangles, t-squares, velum & acetate. The only computer at the company was a Compugraphic Type-Setter. Base pay was $1.25/hr. After advancement, I received $2.10/hr. Prior to leaving, I was offered $3.35/hr.
