Martin Franklin, PMP's Summary

MOSIMTEC, LLC is founded with the core focus to leverage modern advancements in modeling and simulation science and technology to solve complex issues for clients across various industries. Modeling and Simulation (M&S) is a mostly untapped resource in the Industrial Engineers toolkit and we firmly believe that it could serve as an engineering and science discipline all its own. We refer to this new discipline as Modeling and Simulation Engineering (MSE).

The MOSIMTEC concept is different from traditional engineering and consulting services. It is based on the paradigm that technology will be instrumental in decision making. Sophisticated computer simulation software in combination with ever increasing processing speed and memory will enable decision support tools to deliver deep insight in a complex, dynamic, and real-time world.

The MOSIMTEC founders have extensive experience working with leading computer simulation software and solving complex issues for clients across various industries. We are uniquely positioned to support clients in making smart decisions by applying Modeling and Simulation Engineering (MSE) to predict outcomes, prescribe actions and improve operations.

MOSIMTEC SERVICES INCLUDE:

Inventory Optimization
Material Handling
Supply Chain Management
Queuing
Risk-based Planning and Scheduling (RPS)
Data Analytics


Martin has earned the PMP credential and serve as Vice President of Institute of Industrial Engineers National Capital Chapter (2006 to current).

Martin Franklin, PMP's Publications

  • Case Studies on the Application of Dynamic Simulation Tools in the Mining Industry

    • Proceedings at the 6th International Industry Summit on Mining Performance in Pittsburgh, PA
    • May 2013
    Authors: Martin Franklin, PMP, Peter Fordham

    Simulation technology is evolving rapidly and the mining industry is realizing the value it can add. New and existing mines (underground and surface) across multiple commodities are finding new ways to identify opportunities and improve performance from the mine face to the customer. Benefits include process improvement, visualization of how operations and activities work at a detailed level, reduced capital expenditures, lower operating expenses, use for long-term/annual planning, and other improvements.

    This presentation includes a selection of mining industry case studies highlighting business process improvements through the use of simulation. These case studies include green- and brownfield operations across multiple commodities, including mining operations and supply chain from mine to port. Attendees will walk away with a better understanding and insight on how simulation could potentially help their organization improve performance.

  • Risk-based Planning and Scheduling through Simulation

    • Annual Industrial Engineering Conference and Expo, Applied Solutions Track
    • May 23, 2012
    Authors: Martin Franklin, PMP

    Traditionally, plans and schedules are drawn on a deterministic approach and are essentially obsolete given any deviation from the original drafted plan. Simulation provides a means to incorporate inherent system variation in the plans and schedules, through highlighting schedule risks and allowing for a means to analyze and mitigate those risks. The end result is the creation of more robust and feasible plans.

  • Process Improvement and the Application of Modeling Tools in the Mining Industry

    • Workshop at the 5th International Industry Summit on Mining Performance in Rapid City, SD
    • May 2011
    Authors: Martin Franklin, PMP, Peter Fordham

    Quantitative modeling tools and the means of analyzing and identifying process improvement opportunities have come a long way over the past few years. This is especially important in the mining industry when new ideas are met with today’s challenges. This workshop will highlight the value that modeling provides in a mining context, review different modeling tools available, and discuss best practices. A number of case studies will be covered to showcase modeling tools. Interactive discussions will facilitate the application of potential modeling needs and opportunities in a wide range of mining situations and environments.

    The goal of this workshop is to have participants walk away with a better understanding of the latest developments in modeling and how this capability can be effectively applied to improve processes and performance on a day-to-day basis in their own mining environment.
