Lisa Johnson's Summary

I have always worked with food: planting and harvesting, cooking, waitressing (wow, that was a long time ago!), buying and retailing, and helping other people find food that was grown by a farmer near them.

Now, all that work has led me to this mission:
Help busy people eat better.

And the way I'm doing that right now is:
Delightfully bring sweetpotatoes to the nation.

I am a serial entrepreneur: I haven't had one career or interest that sustains me for decades, but instead naturally migrate from new idea to new idea, creating something new about every 10 years or so. For twelve years I dedicated myself to the food coops I ran, building those businesses toward a vision of having the cooperatively owned stores become the finest stores in the community. I also love the visionary aspects of community leadership. That led me ten years ago to the nonprofit Vital Communities, where I held several leadership positions over the ten+ years dedicated to helping community members find community solutions.

My current goal is to take outstanding care of my body and soul, my children and family, and to help you do the same. To that end, I've recommitted to extraordinary nutrition, boxing, and down time. It's an imperfect, daily practice. And I'm building Yummy Yammy, company dedicated to helping busy people eat better by delightfully bringing sweetpotatoes to the nation.

Specialties

Vision. I can sometimes feel when a certain idea is coming. In late 80's I felt the Coop I managed had a chance to ride a great wave of natural foods. In 1999 I launched a nonprofit program to help people find local farmers, because I just knew people would be ready soon. When I told people in 1999 we were building the local food system, no one had any idea what I was talking about; today everyone "buys local". Now I feel sweetpotatoes are coming. And I want to be the one to help bring them.

Upper Valley Food Coop

March 1989 – February 1997 (8 years) White River Junction, VT

Running this Coop was a way of life for me at the time. I lived, breathed, and ate the cooperative way of life as married to the natural foods ideal.

In the 8 years I was the General Manager (stepping up after a year of being a cashier and then Assistant Manager for 6 mos), we increased sales from $300,000/yr to $1.5 million/year, an increase of 400% in 8 years. I was pleased with that accomplishment. Even more importantly, we had great communication with our members and expanded services significantly by relocating to a larger space with 5 times the retail square footage. This was a rewarding, intense 10 years.
