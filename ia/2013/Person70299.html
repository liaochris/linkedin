Judd Treeman's Summary

I help businesses protect and enforce their rights to confidential information and competitive advantages in all types of business disputes and negotiations, including trial and appeals of cases involving trade secrets, breach of fiduciary duty, breaches of confidentiality and noncompetition agreements.

I also counsel and present to businesses on protecting their confidential information and avoiding litigation over the use of confidential information.

My trial and appellate experience also extends into other business litigation matters and forums, including alternative dispute resolution, product liability, business torts, contract disputes, bankruptcy litigation, and consumer litigation defense.

Judd Treeman's Publications

  • Blessed Be the Name of the Code: How to Protect Churches from Tithe Avoidance Under the Bankruptcy Code’s Fraudulent Transfer Law

    • 25 Emory Bankruptcy Developments Journal 2009
    • May 1, 2009
    Authors: Judd Treeman

    (See http://www.law.emory.edu/fileadmin/journals/bdj/25/25.2/Treeman.pdf.)

    The Bankruptcy Code treats debtors’ tithes as constructively fraudulent transfers per se and disgorges them from unsuspecting churches drawn into debtors’ bankruptcies. Unlike most creditors, churches rely on tithes for their financial wellbeing and thus have unique and important interests at stake when a tithing debtor files in bankruptcy. Courts and Congress have tried to prevent the Code’s fraudulent transfer law from disgorging tithes, but they have failed to protect the unique church interests most sharply affected in such cases. Their approaches leave church interests dependent upon debtors’ conduct and subordinate to creditors’ interests. This Comment explains how courts and claimants should use existing federal religious liberty law as a simpler and more direct approach to protecting churches’ interests in tithe avoidance cases.

  • Health Care Reform and You: A Presentation on the Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act and the Health Care and Education Reconciliation Act for the 2010 National Meeting of the Foundation Repair Association

    • Foundation Repair Association
    • April 23, 2010
    Authors: Judd Treeman

    The presentation begins first by explaining where we are now with the health reform laws. it lays a foundation that makes sense of some potentially confusing or disjointed provisions in the law and considers the problems legislators sought to remedy. It also examines the major provisions of the laws---first from a general perspective, and then in some detail. it identifies the laws’ implications for employers---both large and small ones---and for employees, beginning first with clear the implications and then proceeding to educated guesses for less certain implications.
    The presentation then turns to where the new laws are taking us. it examines specific provisions, and especially the effective dates of those provisions.

    Finally, it briefly examines some of the pending challenges to the legislation and review the potential for substantial changes to the legislation.
