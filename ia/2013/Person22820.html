Jeff Denton's Summary

I have been blessed with a great many loves in my life. My current profession is the result of a passion discovered at 13 years of age with a Timex Sinclair 1000. From the Sinclair I quickly graduated to a Commodore 64, C-128, BASIC, IBM PC, C, and finally internet development. I enjoy my work a lot. I love the creative process that it inspires. Graphic design, coding, data modeling - I enjoy all of it.

I am proficient in a number of technologies including PHP, CSS3, HTML5, AJAX, JavaScript, and mobile. But I believe that my greatest skill is not technical at all - bridging the gap between technology and the end user.

You see, I'm primarily a web application developer. Many of the projects I build are used by people on a daily basis as part of their jobs. I believe that the user should not be forced to conform to an app, but that an app should conform to the user.

Achieving this takes more than a deep knowledge of user interface design. It also requires empathy, patience, the ability to listen, a willingness to discard "great" ideas in favor of workable ones, and a strong desire to hear your client say, "This is exactly how I thought it should work." I love that!

I founded UpStart Productions in November of 2000 with the desire to help small businesses build truly effective internet strategies without paying exorbitant fees. I have been a full-time employee of The Teaching Research Institute at Western Oregon University since March of 2005. I'm in a great community of professionals and the projects I work on continually push my skills forward.

Aside from my day job, I have created and nurtured two SaaS apps since 2005: CreativePro Office, a project management app, and StuffSafe, a home/business inventory app. Since their creation, these apps collectively have been used by over 50,000 people and have been featured in Lifehacker, GigaOm, Smashing Magazine, PC Magazine's AppScout, and Kim Komando.

Jeff Denton's Publications

  • Lessons Learned Modeling "Connecting Teaching and Learning", Games and Simulations in Online Learning (pp. 206-222)

    • Hershey, PA: Information Science Publishing
    • 2007
    Authors: Jeff Denton, Gerald Girod, Mark Girod

    While designing a web-based simulation to provide practice for teacher education students as they sought to master the complex skills expected of them as they develop work samples, the authors learned eight important lessons during the development of Cook School District. Work samples are a methodology for helping students learn to analyze their teaching by seeking connections between their work and student achievement. Cook School District serves as a site where teacher candidates begin the arduous process of learning to determine which strategies of instruction and assessment will result in greater student growth. The four years required to develop the simulation brought home eight lessons, often painfully acquired, that are shared with readers. Learning and teaching are not inherently linked. Much learning takes place without teaching, and indeed much teaching takes place without learning (Wenger, 1998).

Jeff Denton's Projects

  • National Consortium on Deaf-Blindness Census Maps

    • June 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Jeff Denton

    The NCDB is required to maintain an annual census of all deaf-blind children in the United States and territories. Until 2009, the census was compiled and provided in table form. Then we moved to demographic maps using Excel to render the census data. By mid-2010, we wanted a better, more portable way to display the demographic maps on the NCDB website. So, the maps were rebuilt using Adobe Flex and a third party mapping solution for Flash. The census data was housed in a MySQL database and a Flash/AJAX JavaScript bridge was used to pull data from the server and render it within the Flash maps.

    From the moment we launched the new Flash maps we realized that the iPad was going to be a really BIG deal and, of course, the iPad could not render our maps. We had to convert the maps to SVG. The Raphael JavaScript vector library proved to be exactly the thing we needed to make this work. A few hours of JavaScript tweaking later we had some maps that work beautifully on iOS tablets.

  • NCDB Mobile Resources App

    Team Members: Jeff Denton

    The National Consortium on Deaf-Blindness (NCDB) offers a vast array of technical assistance resources to state projects, teachers, and parents through their website at http://www.nationaldb.org. The NCDB iPhone app was designed to provide these resources, Selected Topics, People & Programs, and Conferences, in a mobile friendly format.

  • CreativePro Office iPhone App

    • May 2011 to Present
    Team Members: Jeff Denton

    CreativePro Office is a project and office management application for creative professionals. Features include time tracking, invoicing, expenses, calendar, and tasks. Our mobile app allows CPO users to track their time and access their tasks from their iPhone. It was built with the Appcelerator Titanium platform.

  • Promotion Portfolio

    • April 2013 to Present
    Team Members: Jeff Denton

    Every year, staff at The Teaching Research Institute are given the opportunity to apply for promotion. Successful candidate portfolios must address and show growth in several domains including Scope of Work, Contribution & Impact, Service to the Agency, Presentation, and Management. A mentoring committee is put together by the candidate to help them craft a portfolio. After submission, the portfolios are judged by a portfolio review committee. Successful candidates must be approved by the portfolio review committee, the TRI director, and the Western Oregon University president.

    On the merit of this portfolio, I was granted promotion to the rank of Assistant Specialist in April of 2013. This was the first all digital portfolio in the agencies history.
