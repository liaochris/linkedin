Kimball Packard's Summary

22 years in the music industry, including concert promotion, label and artist management. I have also been production coordinator on various recording projects.

Our management company works with two acts from New Orleans, trombone player/vocalist Glen David Andrews, and an all-star collection of Crescent City musicians, The New Orleans Social Club, and the Grammy-nominated Mali Griot, Cheick Hamala Diabate, now living in D.C.

We also provide affordable short-term PR for touring bands and solo artists through promotemytour.com, getting their music, their tour, and their story into the hands -- and ears -- of the right people, so that the musicians can focus on what they do best: play amazing shows.

All of our artists are available for special events, conferences and corporate retreats. We can help you create the perfect New Orleans vibe with any number of Crescent City-based musicians that we also work with for corporate bookings.

Specialties: We provide artist management services primarily for independent musicians. PR, label negotiations, song placements, and web design and maintenance are just a few of the services handled by our company. We also provide these services on a contract basis to artists as well as small businesses looking for a web presence, html email campaigns, PR services etc.

We have a strong catalogue of artist-owned music available for Advertising, TV and Film placement.

1987 – 1988 (1 year)

General manager for this legendary 275 seat live music venue in Cambridge MA, during it's second year of operation. Venue included a 75 seat restaurant and hosted a wide variety of national and international headliners.

Owner/Innkeeper

Eagle Mountain House (Sole Proprietorship)

February 1982 – December 1985 (3 years 11 months)

Owner/Innkeeper for the 110 room Eagle Mountain House in Jackson NH. 9 hole golf course, 235 seat fine dining restaurant, 2 lounges, live music on weekends. Created a popular artist concert series that included performances by David Crosby, Flora Purim and Airto, John Hammond, John Martyn, Leon Redbone, to name just a few. The inn had 65 employees and operated year-round.
