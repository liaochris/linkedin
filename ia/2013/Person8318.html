Tutor - Adult Education

Kerry Education Service

October 2009 – June 2013 (3 years 9 months) Tralee

Stepping Stone is an innovative part-time course for adults who left school early and are currently unemployed. As co-ordinator, I have developed the course with the Adult Literacy Organiser in Tralee. I teach the eight students, covering the content of Career Preparation (FETAC Level 3) in one class per week.

At a family resource centre in Tralee, I teach a weekly class on Communications and Internet Skills (both FETAC Level 3). The group of women comprises asylum seekers as well as Irish residents.

I have previously designed and taught courses in Office Procedures (FETAC Level 3), "Read, Write, Spell" and "Writing for Work".

Chief Executive

National Confidential Enquiry into Perioperative Deaths

1988 – 1998 (10 years)

I took this project from a three District pilot to cover all acute hospitals in England, Wales and Northern Ireland, with a team of ten staff. We worked with consultant anaesthetists, surgeons and pathologists to review an annual sample of deaths in hospital within 30 days of surgery. Reports were published, with recommendations, many of which changed the practice of surgery and anaesthesia.
