Robin Snow's Summary

In November 1977, Charles Oropallo authored and implemented a Bulletin Board System (BBS) called Access-80. It operated on a Radio Shack TRS-80 Model I, Level II microcomputer. There were only a handful of such systems online for public use on the entire planet at that time. It provided personal messaging for local computer enthusiasts. It went online in East Greenbush, NY with a 300 baud modem and home brewed auto answering device. Relative speed and technological changes have been immense - today's dialup at its fastest speed is 53,000 bits per second compared to 300 then!

Access-80 was also concurrently placed online in Schenectady, NY.

In 1982, Charles moved from the Albany, NY area to Nashua, NH, and so did Access-80. Charles's original Nashua Access-80 setup is shown at http://Access80.com.

Before long Access-80 was concurrently online in Londonderry NH as well as Nashua. Access-80 remained online until 1987.

Charles founded CharlesWorks in June of 1998 in order to provide reliable and affordable hosting solutions. At that time he was only providing Internet connectivity for several personal and organizational websites at very low speed. I am proud to have been working with Charles since May 2005.

Robin shares Charles's desire to provide reliable (as well as still being affordable) Internet solutions. This moved us into more commercially oriented markets. Robin and Charles still help individuals get personal sites on the web.

Let CharlesWorks get you out here onto your own web site today! Email Robin or visit http://CharlesWorks.net for an affordable solution coupled with the best customer service in the field.

To get you started, CharlesWorks offers .biz, .com, .info, .name, .net, .org, .us, and .ws domain names with full privacy for only $15.00 per year! More specialty domains are available as well with varying price structures.
