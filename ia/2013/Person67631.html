Jonathan Pimento's Summary

There are jobs, there are careers and then there is a calling. I answered the calling and begun my journey four years ago. I share an unbiased passion for design and technology. In 2009, I had to pick one of my passions to pursue academically. I opted for the latter and enrolled at the University Of Mumbai to study computer engineering. Outside the classroom, I freelanced as a designer and took small steps up the big ladder.

2009
At the age of 17, I landed my first client project via Twitter. As the opportunities came, I took up several clients and personal projects with a view of combining my formal education with my self-taught design skills. I started working with print design and code simultaneously.

2010
By then, I had successfully ventured into Web and UX. I joined a freelance startup in Mumbai which worked on websites and mobile applications. This gave me the experience of converting design into functional code and collaborating with professionals in the industry. While the work experience was valuable, it was the chance to work with a team that excited me the most.

2011
I built my own website and was working with a handful of startups, nonprofits and entrepreneurs on print, web and user experience. Magazines such as Photoshop Creative (UK) and Creative Gaga took notice and featured some of my work. I also started curating my thoughts on my blog, Scotch Egg Media. I grew into the driving design force for my college magazine and festivals.

2012
I joined forces with ‘Rock For A Cause’, a non-profit youth organisation, to learn how non-profits work and how design can drive change. I did a set of Creative Suite CS6 Review Sessions as a part of Adobe’s User Review Program. As 2012 drew to an end, I geared up as the design partner for my first TEDx event – TEDxMasala 2013.

2013
Four years later amidst all this I have managed to maintain progressive results with my computer engineering degree and land my first job with Adobe
