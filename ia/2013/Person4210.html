Alex Nobert's Summary

I have 15 years of industry experience, an obsessive compulsion to always have the right answer, and a perhaps unhealthy love of technology—by which I mean Apple products.

My specific expertise is in the growth and maintenance of systems, infrastructure and teams required to support high-volume web applications and the engineering teams that build them. I have experience with vendor and datacenter relations, operational budgets, recruiting and hiring, and mentoring junior team members.

I strive to be the friendliest, most helpful and most knowledgeable operations leader possible, and I expect, encourage and enable my team to embody those same qualities.

Alex Nobert's Experience

Manager of Operations

Vox Media

Privately Held; 201-500 employees; Online Media industry

January 2012 – February 2013 (1 year 2 months) Washington D.C. Metro Area

My operations team and I were tasked with keeping our properties—The Verge, Polygon, and SB Nation—running and responsive, and we strove to improve the lives of developers by arming them with better tools, by giving them access to powerful and flexible infrastructure, and by being decent human beings who were fun and easy to work with.

I managed multiple production, staging and development environments across physical and cloud infrastructure, staffing and hosting budgets, capacity planning, and communications with management, developers, and editorial.

During my tenure, the operations culture evolved from expecting regular hiccups and downtime, and quickly treating symptoms to demanding 100% uptime, and diligently solving problems. What originally struggled with traffic spikes and DDoS attacks was made to handle Apple product launch and Super Bowl coverage with ease.
