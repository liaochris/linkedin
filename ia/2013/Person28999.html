Damon Lynch's Summary

I am a PhD student in sociocultural anthropology at the University of Minnesota.

In my doctoral research, I plan to investigate how Tajiks use ideas about time to frame their knowledge of war and peace; I also propose studying how they use that knowledge to inform their conflict transformation and peacebuilding efforts. I hope my research findings will help peacebuilders better incorporate an appropriate sense of time in their theory and practice. I further discuss my research here: http://www.damonlynch.net/research.html

I also have research interests in Iran, Pakistan, the Philippines, and Israel/Palestine.

The biggest inspiration in my life is Khan Abdul Ghaffar Khan (1890-1988). Khan was a nonviolent soldier who fought for freedom and justice for more than 70 years. Incredibly, Khan raised a large nonviolent Muslim army to fight British colonial rule from the midst of a proud and largely tribal people, the Pukhtuns. I am inspired by Khan's selfless service, dedication to truth, and his passionate peace work.

Specialties: Anthropology, photography, nonviolence, peacebuilding, computer programming in python, Linux.
