Evan Greenwood's Summary

Running the IT Infrastructure for The Warehouse Group (http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Warehouse_Group), - The Warehouse Limited and Warehouse Stationery is a challenge, but surrounding yourself with a great team is definitely a help.

Starting here made me realise the level of under investment in IT infrastructure, and some poor decisions in the past. But that's the challenge, right?

New store networks! Design it right, and it's a SARS network (Simple, Agile, Resilient and Secure - this is my team's mission statement)

The design is WAN agnostic (I wasn't sure what WAN vendor we'd end up with) and flexible enough to meet the challenges of all the business requests!! PoC is currently kicking off with Aerohive equipment - they seem to be the innovators in the Wireless arena.

The challenge of this design was to make it ubiquous across the now 4 brands - The Warehouse, Warehouse Stationery, Noel Leeming & CES

VoIP stage two is kicking off - pushing it out to the stores, and distribution centres. Which means a new WAN at least for the stores.

Getting the core right is still on-going, current focus is replacing all the cheapo switches with stackable switches - the only way to get failover right. We're also moving off our iSCSI Left Hand storage on to 3PAR. iSCSI hasn't lived up to expectation, and let's face it, Fibre isn't hard to understand, and it's reliable with seamless failover. Simply the right way to go

Commvault project is done & dusted. This is the best backup product on the market at present!

Focus on the core, get that right, then do the rest! That's how we're working. Still.

Evan Greenwood's Additional Information

Interests:

My interests are infrastructure design with particular interests in Doc Management, mail infrastructure, Security - particularly intrusion prevention & desktop security, and programming - favorite language C#. I'm interested in developments within the 'cloud' - services & IaaS. I'm personally using the cloud, and I'm watching with interest how the industry deals with potential security issues of the public cloud. Personally, I see the hybrid public/private cloud the next logical step. I've been investigating opensource solutions for mail, file store, single instance solutions etc. Motivator is frustration with the Microsoft licensing model, which does not scale well to SMEs - 98% of NZ companies. At present my conclusion is that what you save in licensing, you spend in expertise. There's no real point-and-click alternative to the mighty Microsoft juggernaut...yet. SSO is also a big seller of MS products - something that can be done with difficulty using Open Source
