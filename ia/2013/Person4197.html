Alex Morel's Experience

Managing Director

ALX Training Ltd

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Professional Training & Coaching industry

August 2009 – Present (4 years 5 months) Jersey

We are the leading providers of MS Office training in the Channel Islands. We deliver a wide range of courses on Excel, Word, PowerPoint, Visio, Project & Outlook from beginner to advanced level, from 1-1 sessions to large audiences.

Our sessions are tailored to meet the exact requirements of our clients. We have an extensive range of equipment which means we can deliver any of our sessions at our clients premises. We also have access to state of the art training suites on the Esplanade in St Helier.

Ultimately we aim to provide highly effective consultancy, materials, delivery and learning environments to help companies maximise their investment in their employees and IT systems.

Centre Point Trust is the largest childcare provider on the island. As a registered charity we have been working in the local community for 30 years to provide quality childcare and a safe place for children to play and spend their leisure time.

Founder and Chairman

Supporting African Schools

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; BBC; Broadcast Media industry

July 2003 – December 2007 (4 years 6 months) Jersey

Following numerous trips and working experience in East Africa, I founded Supporting African Schools with the view of covering the teaching salaries of the staff at Nakulabye Junior School in Uganda. Over 5 years we raised in excess of £10,000 through musical events but more importantly we helped to raise the profile of the school which culminated in other much larger organisations providing long-term support. Nakulabye Junior School is no longer based out of a few ranshackled shacks in the heart of a slum but has a new building with greater security, kitchen area, toilet facilities and vocational classrooms.

Executive Officer

2001 – 2003 (2 years) Jersey

Helped manage the Family Literacy Project, later re-named the Community Development Project at St Paul's Centre.
This project provided non-native English speakers with the opportunities to access local facilities & health information. English language & literacy classes were run alongside childcare provision so that mums could attend who would otherwise be unable to improve their skills.

English Language Teacher
