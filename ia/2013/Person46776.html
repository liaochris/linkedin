Gale Borger's Experience

Author

galeborgerbooks.com

November 2003 – Present (10 years)

Whoever said murder could not be hilarious never met the Miller sisters.

"Totally Buzzed" is the first in a series of murder mysteries taking place in the Southeastern Wisconsin town of White Bass Lake. Little old ladies with police scanners and jello salads, an ex-detective and a high school biology teacher, drug lords and other really, really bad guys and a Sheriff with deputies named Moe, Larry, Curly and Shemp, bring police procedures to an all new low, and belly laughs to an all new high!

Also available is Book two, Totally Fishy. Buzz Miller really has her hands full when she and her sister Fred take a wild ride through the Venezuelan jungle looking for answers. Meanwhile, two bumbling hit men keep knocking off the wrong guys, and a pole dancing granny has the town up in arms!

If you are in the mood for a quick read, try Totally Decked. Available from OmniLit.com, "Decked" finds trouble brewing at the new coffee shop next door to Fred Miller's pet store. A shady Santa is stalked by an even shadier elf, and Christmas in White Bass Lake may never be the same!

Totally Buzzed and Totaly are available on Amazon.com, Barnes and Noble.com, OmniLit and Echelon Press.com

All are published by Echelon Press, LLC.

Co-owner

Captain Bob's Fishtales

January 2000 – Present (13 years 10 months)

At Captain Bob's, our main goal is to educate the public about raising tropical fish, and to preserve the hobby for future generations.

We currently have over 40 tanks of 33 varieties of Cory Cat fish, Plecostomus, Angel fish, Bolivian Rams, Discus Fish, various tetra, Endler live bearers, guppies-you name it, we might have it!

We breed, trade, raise fry, make our own food, caves, plants(live and silk/plastic)and support the hobby.

Correctional Officer

Walworth County Sheriff's Office

July 1996 – Present (17 years 4 months)

I work in a direct-supervision facility, which means that the correctional officer is locked in the housing unit with the inmates. This hands-on, in-your-face approach allows inmates to ask questions, find answers and vent emotions.

Aside from the obvious "Don't let the bad guy out" duties, I tutor inmates for their GEDs, I answer hundreds of questions a day I teach inmates how to read, write, find jobs, and obtain identification. My theory is that if inmates have more education, they create more options for themselves. More options might lead to better decision making in the future, and subsequently lower recidivism rates in the future.

I am also a part of a small group of correctional officers trained in crisis intervention. Walworth County is the first in the State of Wisconsin to put into place a program which is pro-active in the identification and prevention of suicides in correctional facilities.

It works. It is a valuable tool in maintaining the safety and security of inmates, officers and facility.
