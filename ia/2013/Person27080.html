Corinne Tippett's Publications

  • When No One Else Would Fly

    • The Westchester Press
    • May 17, 2013
    Authors: Corinne Tippett, Colonel C. J. Tippett

    “When No One Else Would Fly” by Corinne Tippett introduces Colonel C. J. Tippett, an extraordinary aviation pioneer who took himself from working class origins to one of the highest leadership positions in international civil aviation. Tip trained the earliest Flying Tigers, certified the first class of Alabama students who would become the Tuskegee Airmen, made record-setting flights over the Amazon Jungle in 1943, and fished for black marlin with Ernest Hemingway in Cabo Blanco, Peru in 1956. He lived in an elite world of political leaders, millionaires, socialites, and celebrities during a pivotal time in aviation. In his own words, he tells how he made history.
