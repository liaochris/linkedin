Kingshuk Das' Summary

Kingshuk Das leads the Healthcare Practice at Jump Associates, a strategy consulting firm. He has a track record of innovating for healthcare in the US, and has led a wide range of health-related innovation programs - from surgical systems to care delivery, patient experience and facilities design. Within the industry, he has worked closely with care providers like the Mayo Clinic, equipment suppliers like Medtronic, and not-for-profits like the American Red Cross.

Kingshuk was a key member of the teams that helped Mayo and Kaiser Permanente design and build their Innovation Centers and run pilot initiatives. He has conducted in-depth observations of daily life and complex procedures like brain surgeries at major hospitals around the country, including Stanford, Massachusetts General and Texas Children’s.

This research helped him understand the US healthcare delivery system at a gut level – including the experiences of patients, families, physicians, nurses, staff and hospital leadership. It paved the way for institutions like Packard Children’s Hospital to rethink their care delivery models, and for manufacturers like Medtronic to reinvent the products and services they offer hospitals. In all cases, the focus was on reducing medical errors, lowering costs, and improving the quality and experience of care.

Kingshuk holds a Master’s degree in Strategic Design Planning from the Illinois Institute of Technology, Chicago, and a Bachelor’s degree in Architecture from the Birla Institute of Technology, India. He has won international awards for the design of environments based on human interaction patterns, including recognition from the Commonwealth Association of Architects, London. He has been published by leading innovation journals like the Design Management Review, Rotman Magazine, and Fast Company, and speaks at a range of international forums on the challenges of innovating in traditional organizations and cultures.
