    • Senior Board Member

      National Centre for Suicide Prevention & Training
      • Health
      March 2005 – present (8 years 8 months)

      The National Suicide Prevention Lifeline is a much-needed organization for those in need. There are many people today that deal with suicidal thoughts, and the community needs to do as much as they can in order to help and counsel them in their times of need. Jack Cola, born John Colaiacovo has been a contributor to this organization and has done many things in order to help this organization grow. He was able to strike a deal with Centel Voice in order for them to donate thousands of VOIP lines to the cause.

      Cola took developers from across the country to help develop a PBX system, which is a private telephone network that is used within the company. It is used to make external calls, which connects the internal telephones within a business. This was done so that when a possible candidate for suicide calls in he can be routed to the correct bureau, or area. This makes it much easier for the Suicide Hotline responders to react accordingly by sending police or medical associates when needed.

      The method was highly successful about two years ago, when it was first implemented. If a person called seeking counseling, they were offered anywhere from 45 to 60 minute sessions, which is very good considering the time and effort that is needed in order to put this forth. However, that has greatly decreased, but there is still an effort to work with Centel Voice in order to increase the amount of time again.

      Cola has always been part of golf outings to raise money to help train counselors so they can offer the best help for those who call. It is very important to have a strong staff, considering that one of these counselors can help prevent a person from taking his or her life. They have to be trained extensively in order to provide this help and this, of course, takes money. Cola has shown that he is aware and keen to the needs of the community, and has shown great heart in dedicating his time to better organizations such as the Suicide Prevention Lifeline.

    • Board Member

      Women's Resources
      • Disaster and Humanitarian Relief
      January 2013 – present (10 months)

      Jack Cola is involved in many community outreach programs that help provide for those less fortunate than others. One particular charity that Cola is very heavily involved in is the Women's Resources of America. This charity was created to aid women who have been victims of abuse.

      The program is unique because it offers a cell phone recycling program. Used phones are refurbished and mailed to the charity, where they are distributed to women in the program. With these phones, women can dial 911 if they find themselves in a bad situation.

      Conditions of the phones are not important. The phones are recycled and restored back to working order and given to these women in need. Often times when we get a new phone we discard our old phones or forget about them. Now your old phones have a purpose and they could potentially save lives.

      Often, women who have been abused leave and don’t take anything with them, let alone a device to communicate with. Iif they find themselves in trouble, it can be harder to call for help. The initiative to participate in this charity is mainly about saving someone’s life and giving her a chance to survive and live another day.

      There is also a global initiative as well. Maybe you never knew what to do with your own phones and you didn’t want to throw them away. Now you have a place to send them to be properly recycled and taken care of. You can come away with two feelings: one in knowing you helped the environment and the other being the potential to save a life. John Colaiacovo, or Jack Cola as he is commonly called, aids this charity because he believes in giving back to the community in effort to help better someone else’s life.
