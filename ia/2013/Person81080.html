Linda Parkinson-Hardman's Summary

“Give a man a fish and you feed him for a day; Teach a man to fish and you feed him for a lifetime”.

My job is to remove the mystique and fear from social media, I strive to make it very common sense and easy for people to get the solutions they need to solve the problems they have. I am in the solutions business.

Social Media Coach - I work with clients, one on one; taking them through a six month professional development programme that designs their strategy, their content marketing, how they use the networks and implements tools to help them monitor and engage on a sustainable basis.

Social Media Trainer - I provide organisations, groups and companies with bespoke courses that introduce them to the specifics of social media marketing and social networking. Let me set up your LinkedIn Training Programme, allow me to help you fit Facebook into your Business, give you the skills to broadcast powerful messages on Twitter and show you the benefits of Blogging.

Social Media Strategist - I create sustainable and achievable social media strategies for companies enabling them to build relationships with their future customers and other stakeholders using the most appropriate social marketing tools and techniques.

Best Selling Author and blogger - I’ve written seven books, two are Amazon Bestsellers including LinkedIn Made Easy. I self publish and I show authors how to market their books through my blog at womanontheedgeofreality.com

Social Entrepreneur - I founded the Hysterectomy Association in 1997. It has supported millions of women over the years with impartial, appropriate information through a huge network of thousands of women all helping each other.

You can find me sharing everything I know at conferences, seminars, presentations and workshops.

  • Losing The Woman Within

    • The Hysterectomy Association
    • 2007
    Authors: Linda Parkinson-Hardman

    The title of this ebook is a response to the 1000’s of women who have contacted us over the years who feel that their essential womanhood will be profoundly affected when they lose their womb.

    It is a full and frank account of the range of possible emotional impacts that a hysterectomy can have. It covers the emotional upset that can be caused by our hormones as well as the psychological ones of anxiety, stress, anger, grief, guilt and depression; it talks about the possible effect on relationships and our sexuality and it is all done with the sensitivity and understanding by someone who has ‘been there’, and who has emerged happy and healthy at the end of it.

    Written as an informative guide to what you may experience, it is also interspersed with the authors own experiences and story, which she has used to illustrate the emotions and situations she is talking about.

    It has a clear and easy style of writing, that will appeal to every woman who is about to embark on a hysterectomy journey.
