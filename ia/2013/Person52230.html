Heather Hettrick's Summary

Dr. Hettrick joined the Physical Therapy Faculty at the University of New Mexico in 2012. She is the Director of Clinical Education and is responsible for wound, burn and lymphedema curriculum development. As a faculty member, she will provide community outreach, continue professional writing and publications, conduct research and collaborate with health providers throughout the Southwest.

As a physical therapist, her clinical specialties reside within wound, burn and lymphedema management. She is a Certified Wound Specialist by the American Academy of Wound Management and is also certified as a Manual Lymph Therapist by the Academy of Lymphatic Studies.

Previously, she was the Vice President of Academic Affairs & Education at American Medical Technologies. Prior to that, Dr. Hettrick spent 6 years as a Clinical Assistant Professor in the Department of Physical Therapy at New York University. Additionally, she was the Program Coordinator for Burn Rehabilitation Research at the William Randolph Hearst Burn Center at NY Presbyterian Hospital and a Master Clinician at the Hospital for Joint Diseases at the Diabetic Foot and Ankle Center. Dr. Hettrick is a Past President of the American Academy of Wound Management and actively involved in numerous professional organizations. She publishes and presents nationally on integumentary related issues.
