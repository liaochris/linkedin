Daniel Ponech's Summary

SUMMARY
Daniel Ponech has been responsible for integrating usability, researching human factors, as well as creating and implementing user interface design standards in a variety of environments. He has also over-seen the establishment of appropriate user experience practices for successful projects at both the pre- and post-sales stages.

Daniel brings fifteen+ years of experience combining technical, creative, and content elements on corporate image, portal, intranet, and e-commerce sites, in addition to defining usability, navigation, and content flow in a variety of media delivery environments (Web, desktop, mobile).

BACKGROUND
Trained as an anthropologist, Daniel applies practical research skills, like ethnographic field methods, to the identification and integration of user needs into interface development.

PROFESSIONAL LIFE
In addition to teaching Information Design in the Faculty of Information Studies at the University of Toronto, Interaction Design at Centennial College, Information Architecture at Humber College, and Interface design at the University of Guelph-Humber, Daniel has served as the Founding President of Toronto Interacts, the Toronto Chapter of the Usability Professional’s Association, and Founding Co-Director of the Canadian Usability Summit Society. As such, Daniel maintains an active professional life and is in demand as a workshop leader and presenter.

Specialties: usability, interaction design, information design, user experience
