Kate Scott's Summary

Currently anchoring sports for KNBR 680 AM by day and reporting from the sideline for the San Jose Earthquakes and San Jose Giants by night, I tend to think of myself as young, versatile, and just getting started.

My journey began during my sophomore year at Cal, when I made history by becoming the school's first female yell-leader. The exposure led to my first industry jobs, hosting the info-tainment segment on The Cal Sports Magazine and blogging about Cal basketball for the Nike website. The following year, I signed on to write for The Bear Insider magazine and its corresponding ESPN affiliated website, becoming the youngest and lone female writer on staff.

Upon graduating from UC Berkeley in 2005, I took a chance on another aspect of the broadcasting industry, radio. Intrigued by the emphasis on the voice, I soon turned an internship at KLLC-FM (San Francisco) into an anchor/reporter/producer position at Metro Networks. In my five years as a member of the Bay Area staff, I anchored the news for KSFO-AM and KFRC-AM, talked traffic on KCBS-AM and KFOG-FM, and updated sports for KNBR-AM.

In 2007, I returned to television to co-host Cal's highlight show on CSN California. Once again, the exposure paid instant dividends. Within months, I was writing and hosting a webcast for the San Francisco Giants, video blogging for Fanhouse, and hosting Cal’s post game football show.

In 2010, I broadened my focus to include a new passion, voice over. You can currently hear me on your smartphone, voicing spots and imaging for Stitcher Smart Radio.

I was born in Fresno, CA and grew up in nearby Clovis, CA. I currently reside in San Francisco.
