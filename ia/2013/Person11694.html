Barbara Kazdan's Experience

Founder/Principal

Achieving Change Together

Sole Proprietorship; Myself Only; Nonprofit Organization Management industry

September 2009 – Present (4 years) Washington D.C. Metro Area and national

Solutions-oriented consulting, connecting, coaching and collaborative writing tailored to social innovators. I care about, listen deeply to and work closely with clients to map the way forward. Clients include leading social innovators,those who invest in their success, and individuals seeking vibrant non-profit careers. Offer workshops tailored to change agents, fellowship and membership organizations. Joined Parents Alliance 1-13 as Strategic Development Officer to help founder Jose-Pablo Fernandez launch expansion plans.

Board member

Humane Farm Animal Care

September 2009 – Present (4 years)

A national non-profit 501(c)3 organization created by Ashoka Fellow Adele Douglass to improve the lives of farm animals by setting rigorous standards, conducting annual inspections, and certifying their humane treatment. Offers consumers healthy choices at stores, markets and restaurants carrying "Certified Humane" products. Gives small producers a competitive edge over agribusiness.

Steering Commitee and Diversity Committee

The Transition Network

2009 – Present (4 years) DC Metro area

Strategic planning and leadership of voluntary organization offering professional women 50+ a variety of networking, personal growth and social opportunities.

Standing member, Diversity Committee event team, developing events to engage women across cultures in exploring and sharing life experiences.

Director, Ashoka-U.S.

Ashoka

Nonprofit; 501-1000 employees; Nonprofit Organization Management industry

August 2003 – April 2009 (5 years 9 months) Arlington VA

Responsible for developing programs to support the success of leading U.S. social entrepreneurs elected to Ashoka's global association. Grew the US Fellowship 4X. Led the global organization in identifying and guiding Fellowship candidates to election; directed an intensive multi-phase selection process. Pioneered programs and interactive meetings to engage Fellows in a dynamic, collaborative community. Mentored and linked Fellows to human/financial/technical resources. Created/directed signature annual event (e.g., 350 guests at Google campus). Cultivated/sustained strategic partnerships and donor relations. $4M annual budget.

Founding Executive Director

Interprofessional Fostering of Ophthalmic Care for Underserved Sectors (InFOCUS)

November 1994 – July 2003 (8 years 9 months)

Developed programs to bring affordable basic eye care to the world's poorest people, in the U.S. and in developing world. Designed the "vision station" model which was replicated with success in 24 sites in Texas and became the model for programs in remote villages in East Africa, India and Central America.

Founder

Trisector Enterprises

January 1992 – December 1994 (3 years)

Consulting services to promote and assist in developing multi-sector partnerships among public, private and non-profit organizations; assist organizations through start-up or transitions.

Founding Executive Director

Houston READ Commission

1987 – 1991 (4 years)

Spearheaded the organization of the Houston READ Commission, leading this mayoral literacy agency from inception to prominence among urban literacy coalitions. As founding executive director, forged creative public-private sector partnerships, raising $3 million in 3 years to pioneer new instructional strategies in 13 demonstration projects and neighborhood learning centers. Recognized by First Lady Barbara Bush, appointment to the Governor’s Literacy Task Force, and induction into Leadership Houston. Elected V.P., National Urban Literacy Coalition, which helped emerging urban literacy collaboratives, and influenced federal policy development.

National Board/Field Service Director

Women's American ORT

1982 – 1986 (4 years)

As V.P. of the Southwest District and then National Board member, provided field service to help local chapters establish or revitalize their chapters. Created ways for chapters to share best practices.
