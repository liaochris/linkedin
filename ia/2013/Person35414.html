Didi (Blanca) Davis' Summary

23 years experience in the healthcare industry with emphasis in the implementation, development, and management of information technology solutions and healthcare workflow requirements. Nationally known as a subject matter expert for healthcare standards, interoperability design and strategy for healthcare stakeholders deploying health information technology to improve patient safety and quality of care. Founded Serendipity Health in April 2009 to provide consulting services for the advancement and deployment of interoperable healthcare by connecting people, processes and technology. Serves on the Tennessee HIMSS Board as the Health Information Exchange (HIE) liaison, is a member of the Health Information Partnership for TN Technology Workgroup and the Co-chair for the Quality, Research and Public Health Domain for IHE International. Mrs. Davis has contracted as the Interim CEO for CareSpark, Inc., a Health Information Exchange in Tennessee nationally known for its standards based architecture. She currently has contracts with an ONC contractor, the State of TN and a Not-for-Profit 501(c)3 Health Information Exchange located in Knoxville, TN known as the East TN Health Information Network (etHIN).

Prior to founding Serendipity Health, Mrs. Davis was senior director of Informatics at the Healthcare Information and Management Systems Society (HIMSS). Her responsibilities included execution and direction for the standards activities of IHE International and its related projects and committees such as the IHE North American Connectathon and the Interoperability Showcase demonstrations in the US, Europe and Asia. Prior to joining HIMSS in May 2006 she was employed by Eclipsys Corporation as Director, IT Product Strategies. From 1994-1997, she was the Vice President of Implementation for Omnicell Technologies for the Southeastern Territory. From 1990 – 1994 she was a Senior Budget Analyst for the University of Tennessee Medical Center in Knoxville, TN.

Specialties

Interoperability, strategic planning, budgeting, clinical work flow redesign, architecture, databases, project management, senior leadership, management, implementation, consulting, pharmacy, physician contract negiotiation, accounting, healthcare and informatics.
