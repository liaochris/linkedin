IOANA LAZAROV's Education

BSc (Hons) Psychology

2007 – 2010

Dissertation: ‘The Impostor Phenomenon: Academic Motivation, Fear of Negative Evaluation, Perceived Stress and Proactive Coping’

A new area of research which intends to expand on current research into the above areas and to assess the prevalence of the Impostor Phenomenon in Undergraduate students, explore the facets of achievement acceptance, ownership and academic motivation, the aspects and implications of fear on negative evaluation, the levels of perceived stress and proactive coping strategies that students might employ to deal with the stresses of “feeling like a fake” or “out of place” in academic settings.
A longer term objective is to facilitate new research which will further out understanding of a complex area of human psychology
