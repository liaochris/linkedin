Jamie Kravitz's Summary

Patient, calm, and confident, Jamie Kravitz motivates people to reach beyond the status quo.

As UX Lead for Franklin Templeton's Global Web Digital Marketing department, Jamie manages the UX team members, creates UX artifacts, supervises research efforts, and oversees the establishment of UX standards.

As Sr. User Experience Architect, Jamie led the redesign of the company's retail website (from the UX perspective). He created wireframes, designed and conducted usability studies, and built the styleguide. He created interactive prototypes for the design of international websites. He has advocated for user-centered design and represented the UX Team in companywide initiatives.

Previously a Usability Engineer for Interface Analysis Associates in San José, CA, Jamie worked on interaction design, usability testing, and evaluation of software applications, medical products, and other consumer products.

In 2007 Jamie finished his Master's degree in Human Factors and Ergonomics at San José State University. A version of his Masters' Thesis, "Effects of Input Device on Different Age Groups Performing Goal-Oriented Tasks," was published in the 2007 Meeting of the Human Factors and Ergonomics Society (see http://www.digivitz.com/research.php). Jamie also presented a poster on balancing business goals with user needs at the UPA 2008 conference (see http://digivitz.com/blog/?p=74).

Jamie worked as an independent filmmaker, multimedia producer, and cable TV program coordinator. He founded West Hollywood Public Access, the community television station for the City of West Hollywood, teaching and supervising video productions, as well as designing the educational curriculum and running the day to day operations of the facility. He produced and directed an award winning documentary "Into the Streets" (2001) and edited another award-winning documentary "Hearts Cracked Open" (2004) which has shown in international film festivals.
