Bill Protzmann's Summary

Bill Protzmann is a man on a mission: to raise awareness of the power of music as self care.

Encouraged by a therapist to bring his unique advocacy into closer connection with care for Service members and Veterans, Mr Protzmann premiered a new version of his one-man show, “Connected!,” in San Diego on Memorial Day weekend, 2007. In 2011, he launched Music Care Inc, a for-profit corporation dedicated to providing education in music as adjunct treatment for post-traumatic stress and the many other harmful issues related to military service, and to advocacy of more self-reliant means of relieving depression and anxiety.

Mr Protzmann offers “Connected!” as a 30-minute short-format talk, a keynote address, a daylong seminar and a concert-based performance. In addition to recent performances in San Diego, Palm Desert, Redlands and Fresno California, Connected! has been presented:

• at the La Jolla VA Healthcare System;
• for the VA Chaplaincy couples program “From Warrior to Soulmate;”
• as a weekly music program of Episcopal Community Services of California’s “Friend to Friend” outreach for homeless Veterans;
• for members of the San Diego Veterans/Families Forum;
• for groups of “civilians” looking for a better way to cope with depression and anxiety;
• and in short-format settings for groups such as a Rotary Club featured speaker.

As a volunteer, Mr Protzmann serves in the San Diego Veterans Coalition, as a contributing member of San Diego Vet/Fam Forum, and as San Diego facilitator of Guitars for Vets, a nationwide non-profit corporation that provides guitars and instruction to Veterans for relief from post-traumatic stress. He is an Associate member of the American Music Therapy Association and a member of the Healing Music Organization. When he talks about music and self care, he speaks with gentle conviction and from real experience.

Specialties

Teaching the use of music as self-care for military Veterans and active-duty service members dealing with post-traumatic stress and other issues unique to military service

Empowerment of corporate audiences through "intentional listening" -- using music to build skills, memory and intellect -- and relieving depression and anxiety
