Andrea Shapiro's Summary

Andrea's consulting practice focuses on nonprofit management, affordable housing and community development. Andrea has broad experience as a consultant, staff member and funder in the nonprofit, public and private sectors in areas including homelessness, affordable housing, community and economic development, philanthropy, domestic violence prevention, women's issues, immigration and immigrant services, disability issues, civil rights, anti-poverty programs, early childhood education, and others. She has worked as a management consultant in the private, public and nonprofit sectors; been a successful Interim Executive Director in several organizations facing significant transitions; founded an innovative community-based nonprofit organization (Second Chances); been a leader and senior staff member in the nonprofit and public sectors; managed the restructuring of several departments and organizations; consulted with nonprofit boards, CEOs and managers, as well as public agencies and businesses; and served as a board member and executive committee member.

Andrea holds an MBA from Boston University's Graduate School of Management with a focus on public management, finance and real estate, and a BA cum laude from Smith College in psychology and sociology. She maintains consulting affiliations with Third Sector New England's Executive Transitions Program, NeighborWorks America and Community Reinvestment Associates.

Interim Executive Director (consultant)

The Second Step

October 2012 – June 2013 (9 months) Newton, Massachusetts

Served as The Second Step's Interim Executive Director for eight months through a partnership between Andrea Shapiro Consulting and Third Sector New England. This is one of several interim leadership projects I've worked on since 2006.

The Second Step is a comprehensive program for survivors of domestic violence. Located in Newton, Massachusetts and founded in 1992, The Second Step offers transitional housing for up to 16 families as well as advocacy, safety planning, mentoring, children’s programs, legal case management, and an array of supportive services for families transitioning away from abuse.

As Interim Executive Director I worked with the agency's Board of Directors, staff and partners to strategically address long-term challenges resulting from changes in client and community needs combined with the availability of funding resources. Worked closely with staff and Board to close a multi-year budget deficit (of approximately 15% of the agency's budget) by streamlining operations, prioritizing services with the most impact for clients, and implementing spending controls. Assessed organizational operations including financial management, resource development capacity, service delivery and outcomes, governance, public and community relations, and more. Prepared the organization for its new Executive Director a sustainable future.

Interim Executive Director (consultant)

Greater Lowell Community Foundation

December 2010 – July 2011 (8 months) Lowell, Massachusetts

Through Andrea Shapiro Consulting, in partnership with Third Sector New England's (TSNE) Executive Transitions Program, I served as Greater Lowell Community Foundation's (GLCF) Interim Executive Director during an eight month transition. This is one of several interim leadership projects I've worked on since 2006.

Managed the day-to-day operations of the foundation and assessed GLCF's organizational capacity, policies and procedures, provided recommendations to the agency's Board of Directors and implemented changes to improve operations and public relations. During this time, the agency's Board of Directors, a Transition Consultant and stakeholders reviewed GLCF's strategic direction, defined the desired qualifications and experience for a permanent Executive Director, searched for and hired GLCF's next leader. Supported the Executive Director for several months after he began his new position.

Greater Lowell Community Foundation is a $22 million philanthropic institution serving donors and supporting nonprofits and their clients in Lowell and the surrounding communities.
