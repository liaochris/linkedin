Alessandro Rossini's Summary

During the latest years, I have conducted extensive research in the field of software engineering, with particular emphasis on model-driven engineering (MDE).

Currently, I am doing research on the application of MDE in the field of cloud computing. My contribution is the the design of techniques and methods for the specification and enactment of provisioning, deployment, monitoring, and adaptation concerns for multi-cloud systems. My research is carried out together with international partners within EU-funded projects.

During my Ph.D. programme, I did research on the formalisation of aspects of MDE. My contributions were the consolidation of two core techniques in MDE, namely metamodelling and model transformation, as well as the formalisation of two novel techniques in MDE, namely model versioning and multi-level modelling. The outcome of my research led to several publications in highly ranked conferences and journals.

Besides my primary research in the field of software engineering, I conducted research in the interdisciplinary field of technology and society, especially on the impact of information technology on society.

Specialities: software engineering, model-driven engineering, cloud computing, web technologies, free/open source software, technology and society.

Alessandro Rossini's Projects

  • PaaSage

    • November 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Alessandro Rossini

    PaaSage is a EU FP7 funded collaborative project which started in October 2012. The main goal of PaaSage is to deliver an open and integrated platform to support both design and deployment of cloud applications, together with an accompanying methodology that allows model-based development, configuration, optimisation, and deployment of existing and new applications independently of the existing underlying cloud infrastructures.

  • MODAClouds

    • November 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Alessandro Rossini

    MODAClouds is a EU FP7 funded collaborative project which started in October 2012. The main goal of MODAClouds is to deliver methods, a Decision Support System (DDS), an Integrated Development Environment (IDE), and a run-time environment for the high-level design, early prototyping, semi-automatic code generation, and automatic deployment of applications on multiple clouds with guaranteed QoS.

  • Broker@Cloud

    • November 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Alessandro Rossini

    Broker@Cloud is a EU FP7 funded collaborative project which started in November 2012. The main goal of Broker@Cloud is to deliver a brokerage framework for continuous quality assurance and optimization. The Broker@Cloud framework aims at proving future enterprise could service brokers with advanced means to monitor both the obligations and the opportunities pertaining to the delivery of enterprise cloud services to consumers.

  • Diagram Predicate Framework (DPF)

    • January 2008 to Present
    Team Members: Alessandro Rossini, Adrian Rutle

    Diagram Predicate Framework (DPF) is a diagrammatic specification framework founded on category theory and graph transformation which has been adopted to formalise aspects of model-driven engineering.

  • ZNF

    • August 2005 to May 2007
    Team Members: Alessandro Rossini, Graziano Liberati, Tomasz Kuter, Christian Kassab

    ZNF is an open source framework for building PHP5 enterprise web applications.
