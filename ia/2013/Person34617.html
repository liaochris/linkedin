Heather Frenette's Summary

Ms. Frenette is a Registered Nurse who focuses her practice on the care needs of elderly, disabled and mentally ill persons. She received her Bachelor of Science in Nursing from Baylor University, a Master of Science in Nursing from The University of Texas at Arlington and a Master's level Certificate in Gerontology from Arizona State University. Ms. Frenette is also a Certified Care Manager (CMC) through the National Academy of Certified Care Managers. She is actively involved in the National Association of Professional Geriatric Care Managers on both the regional and national levels. In 2006, Ms. Frenette was recognized as a Fellow of the Leadership Academy, a distinction awarded to those professional care managers with comprehensive experience. That year she also received the Rose Kleiner Award for excellence and achievement in the field of professional geriatric care management. As an advocate for the elderly, disabled and mentally ill, Ms. Frenette believes that every person deserves a chance to live his/her life at the highest functional level in the least restrictive environment possible.

Specialties

A Care Manager has advanced degrees in nursing, social work, gerontology or a related field. Care Managers are trained to assess, plan, coordinate, monitor and provide services for the client and their family. I focus my practice on dementia, elder care, the mentally ill and the developmentally disabled.

Desert Care Management provides services for persons with cognitive impairments, physical or developmental disabilities or mental illness. We are a guide, advocate, and resource, offering options to provide support for persons requiring assistance. Proactive care planning for someone you care about can help prevent emergencies and accidents, thus allowing your loved one to remain safe and independent for as long as possible. After a comprehensive care management needs assessment, our staff will design an individualized plan of care with recommendations to create a comfortable, healthy, and safe environment for your loved one.
