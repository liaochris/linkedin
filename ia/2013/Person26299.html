Colin Goedecke's Publications

  • The Speed of Sight

    • Xlibris
    • November 2, 2007
    Authors: Colin Goedecke

    In this debut collection of poems, inspired over a decade, readers enter a series of lyrical landscapes; to see and experience the places, people and poetry-at-play appearing and unfolding in the Poet's deeper field of vision.

    As a watercolorist might work, and often plein air, he renders them freshly, spontaneously, indelibly. From a tranquil harbor in Maine to a fanciful farmhouse in Holland. From observing a lunar eclipse, or being shaved in a Tuscan barber shop, to making family wine below the brownstoned surface of New York's Upper West Side. From living portraits of friends, and magnesium flashes of memory.

    The world-roving writer often brings native language into various pieces written abroad; stippling them with Italian, Spanish, French, Dutch. To infuse a vocal flavor into the scenes and scenery he's writing in, and musing about.

    The constant thread in these 55 poems is the immediacy of seeing; not just with the eyes but with other senses; with the emotions and imagination. Color and texture are abundant; as are qualities and plays of light, and atmosphere; sometimes sound, or silence; motion; music; clouds; the sea.
