David Hardesty's Summary

Consultant, author, educator - financial reporting, taxation, corporate governance

David Hardesty is a CPA, a consultant, author, and educator in the fields of financial reporting, taxation and corporate governance. His financial reporting and tax consulting and accounting practice concentrates on the challenges facing small and medium size public companies, and technology companies.

David is also an expert on audit committee operations (see his book, Corporate Director's Deskbook), and is available to serve on audit committees of public companies.

In 1999, David completed his first major book, Electronic Commerce: Taxation and Planning, a pioneering effort to create an authoritative treatise covering all aspects of taxation related to electronic commerce. He has gone on to write seven books for Thomson Reuters on financial reporting, taxation and corporate governance.
The following books by David are available from Thomson Reuters (for more information, see http://www.davidhardesty.com):

Financial reporting and taxation:

• Electronic Commerce: Taxation and Planning

• Accounting for Stock Compensation

• SEC Expert: Domestic Filers

• Choice of Entity – Title 1 of Tax Advisors Planning System

• Taxation of Electronic Commerce – Title 43 of Tax Advisors Planning System

Corporate governance:

• Practical Guide to Corporate Governance and Accounting: Implementing the
Requirements of the Sarbanes-Oxley Act

• Corporate Director’s Deskbook

David began teaching in Golden Gate University's Graduate School of Taxation in 1999. He currently teaches a course taxation of e-commerce. David also teaches international taxation in at the D'Amore-McKim School of Business, at Northeastern University.

Accounting Today voted David Hardesty one of the “Top 100 Most Influential People in Accounting” for 2004, 2006, 2007, and 2008, and CPA Magazine named him one of the “Top 100 Most Influential Practitioners,” for 2005 and 2007.

David Hardesty's Publications

  • Electronic Commerce: Taxation and Planning

    • Thomson Reuters
    • December 31, 1999
    Authors: David Hardesty

    The first and only authoritative treatise on electronic commerce taxation and planning, this invaluable resource addresses the special strategies and solutions needed by electronic commerce enterprises.

  • Practical Guide to Corporate Governance and Accounting: Implementing the Requirements of the Sarbanes-Oxley Act

    • Thomson Reuters
    • December 31, 2002
    Authors: David Hardesty

    This authoritative treatise includes analysis, explanations, sample client letters and interoffice memoranda, checklists and tables to help you understand exactly how the Sarbanes-Oxley Act and the related SEC and PCAOB rules and regulations affect you and your company or firm. The Guide also offers cross-reference tables grouped into who-is affected categories so you to can determine what actions to take.

  • Accounting for Stock Compensation

    • Thomson Reuters
    • December 31, 2010
    Authors: David Hardesty

    Accounting for Stock Compensation provides a comprehensive treatment of the financial reporting, accounting, and taxation of stock compensation.

    Stock compensation covered by this book includes all forms of share-based payments to employees for services, and to nonemployees for either goods or services. Share-based payments are payments made in the form of (1) the entity's equity instruments, including equity shares and share options; or (2) a liability payable to the supplier, where the liability is either based, at least in part, on the entity's equity instruments, or can be settled by issuance of the entity's shares.

  • SEC Expert: Domestic Filers

    • Thomson Reuters
    • December 31, 2008
    Authors: David Hardesty, Various co-authors.

    SEC Expert: Domestic Filers is an end-to-end compliance solution for US corporations filing with the SEC, and the accountants, auditors, and attorneys who advise them. It provides complete SEC reporting guidance, along with frequent updates to keep them on top of the latest SEC, FASB, IASB, AICPA and PCAOB developments affecting their filings.

  • Corporate Director's Deskbook

    • Thomson Reuters
    • December 31, 2004
    Authors: David Hardesty, Paul Hilton

    Directors, corporate executives, auditors and lawyers face many challenges in the post-Sarbanes-Oxley world. Perhaps no challenge is greater than understanding the role that directors now play. Shareholders, judges, Congress, and the public have all made it clear that investor protection should be the order of the day; to further this goal, directors have been given a mandate to vigorously perform their oversight role.

  • Taxation of Electronic Commerce - Title 43 of Tax Advisors Planning System

    • RIA
    • December 31, 2002
    Authors: David Hardesty

    Practical guide to all aspects of the taxation of electronic commerce.

  • Choice of Entity - Title 1 of Tax Advisors Planning System

    • Thomson Reuters / RIA
    • December 31, 2004
    Authors: David Hardesty

    Explanation of essential tax features and differences between sole proprietorships, partnerships, limited liability companies, C corporations, and S corporations.

  • Online tax course: Taxation of Electronic Commerce and Remote Sellers

    • Golden Gate University, Graduate School of Taxation
    Authors: David Hardesty

    This is sixteen week online graduate level tax course covering federal, state, and international taxation of electronic commerce. It focuses primarily on the tax problems associated with online sales of products and services. It also covers the tax issues associated with creating online companies and building websites.

  • Online tax course: Limited Liability Companies and S Corporations, a Choice of Entity

    • Golden Gate University, Graduate School of Taxation
    Authors: David Hardesty

    This is a sixteen week online graduate level tax course that covers the taxation of S corporations and limited liability companies, and analyzes the advantages and disadvantages of choosing one of these entity forms over the other.

  • Online tax course: International Taxation - Inbound Transactions

    • D'Amore-McKim School of Business at Northeastern University
    • April 15, 2011
    Authors: David Hardesty

    Created the syllabus and all of the course materials for the online tax course, International Taxation - Inbound Transactions, offered in the Masters in Taxation program at the D'Amore-McKim School of Business at Northeaster University.
