Questions about my work are encouraged.

The first thing I do is listen to you — no talking.
What I learn about your sales goals, your competition, your successes — and your disappointments — determines the details of what I will present to you as a visual solution.

Direct mail is my foundation. It demands focused, targeted, well-conceived design and I understand it extremely well.

In close collaboration with clients, I have developed a wide breadth of graphic design in response to well-thought-out marketing strategies. You will see success with your promotional materials: packaging, collateral, direct mail campaigns, advertisements, and in-store displays.

I use my collaborative approach — it's YOUR company — to achieve success with your marketing and design goals. My natural curiosity about you and your business makes the process fun for both of us!

Specialties: I am currently developing marketing plans and design for small business — specifically, tactics that will grow my clients' business; in some cases quite aggressively. I am focusing attention on brand development; understanding the competition, executing the tactical design; monitoring results; doing everything possible within budget to promote sales and understand the market(s) as they evolve.

I have also sponsored a young gentleman through the "Young Entrepreneurs" Program at the Midcoast Chamber of Commerce. A very talented young man, we both learned from one another.

Martha Dillard's Experience

Owner, Designer and Marketing Consultant

Martha Dillard Design

June 2004 – Present (9 years 4 months)

I am a one-woman design group!

My primary experience is in catalogs, at the VT Country Store, NEBS, DeLorme and Crestline. I also have done illustration work using Adobe Illustrator CS6. I added this to my repertoire recently.

My focus right now is helping very small businesses grow thoughtfully by understanding their clients better. I use basic principals of direct mail marketing which works——always. I try to educate my clients when necessary. This is a fun business and always will be. I work hard and jump through burning hoops to help them, whether it be identity, catalog, presentation or collateral. . .

Two huge projects that I will always take away lessons from:
1) With of R&D colleagues, we handled the entire project of opening
three retail stores in the Boston area for NEBS; again I was a one-woman advertising dept.
2) Increasing the number and frequency of catalogs at Vt Country Store. I led my group through that experience, which at first seemed insurmountable. But we did it. I moved on, but keep track of the VCS catalog program.

DeLorme Mapping

September 2000 – January 2003 (2 years 5 months)

Maintaining the brand is the single most important responsibility of the design team and I take that responsibility seriously. While at DeLorme, we handled several product launches. It was essential to present the new product “look” consistently, while at the
same time maintaining the DeLorme Brand — otherwise we ran the risk of confusing the customer. Product launches extended into the Map Store, where a new product had to stand out. All material was absolutely consistent.

Art Director

Crestline (division of Geiger Brothers)

September 1998 – September 2000 (2 years 1 month)

At the Crestline division of Geiger Brothers, I redesigned and produced the catalog and at the same time shortened catalog production time significantly. Increased time gave Product Management and Marketing more ability to devote to strategy and product selection. There were also fewer last minute changes. These catalogs performed 10% over plan.

Catalog Production Manager; Senior Art Director

The Vermont Country Store

Privately Held; 201-500 employees; Retail industry

July 1994 – August 1998 (4 years 2 months)

Promoted to Senior Art Director, my team and I designed and produced three new spin-off catalogs featuring successful product lines to address sales that were under plan at the Vermont Country Store. We did not add to full-time staff and maintained design and production of the existing catalogs. Catalog program contributed to a success rate of 40% over plan.
As Catalog Production Manager at The Vermont Country Store, my role was to ensure schedules were met and press runs of 50 million or more were managed correctly. I was responsible for the smallest detail and the biggest picture. Often, with tact, I had to get a better product from very tired people that were already working very hard for us. That diplomacy gained a lot for all of us, both teams: the end product was better, and they were proud of a job well done.

Senior Graphic Designer; Advertising Project Manager

New England Business Service

October 1987 – June 1994 (6 years 9 months)

To promote a retail channel for NEBS, I became a one-woman advertising department — completing all research design and production to promote three start-up NEBS Printing retail stores in the Boston area. Design included direct mail, newspaper ads and
placement, and special in-store promotions. I also developed in-store design and supporting graphics. In the first year, gross revenue surpassed anticipated plan by 15%.
Prior to being selected for this management position, I was a senior graphic designer in the advertising department primarily designing and producing the NEBS direct mail B-to-B catalogs.
