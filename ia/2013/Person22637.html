Cheryl Antier's Summary

As a ghostwriter, best-selling author, book publisher, licensed Law of Attraction/Re-ignite Your Life TM coach and ‘Marketing Magician’, my mission is to work with women who are entering the second half of their lives and help them create well-written books that clearly communicates their most authentic, powerful and passionate messages, along with individualized publishing and law of attraction publicity, promotion and targeted marketing services.

I'm a certified Law of Attraction coach, the founder of a business academy for writers (who want to learn to build the business side of their writing businesses), a book publishing company, and a publicity firm for ‘slightly shy' indie authors.

I also offer small group masterminds and one-on-one personalized consultations to give my clients the tools and strategies to become an irresistible magnet for their perfect customers, employees, business partners, vendors, funders, or any relationship that is pertinent to them being able to re-ignite their lives and live with passion, joy and magic…

My clients are changemakers,thought leaders and heart-centered professional women who are bright, passionate, and successful… They know they’ve got much to offer to the world - whether it’s through a project or program that will change the Status Quo, build a lasting legacy and create something meaningful and significant and life-changing.

They're not willing to settle for retirement homes and rocking chairs. Instead, they want to re-ignite the divine spark they were born with.

They want to show up differently. To play big. To create meaningful connections with their ideal tribe...

For them, the first step in that process is to finally get their book written and published. The book they’ve been wanting to write for a while now… The book that’s going to showcase their expertise, their talents and finally let them reveal who they really are - no masks, no games, no more hiding their light under a bushel or playing small.

Specialties

Marketing Strategist: If the circus is coming to town and you paint a sign saying 'Circus Coming to the Fairground' -- that's advertising.

If you put the sign on an elephant and walk him into town -- that's promotion.

If the elephant walks through the mayor's flowerbed -- that's publicity.

If you can get the mayor to laugh about it -- that's public relations.

And if you planned the elephant's walk -- that's marketing."

Let me "plan the elephant's walk" for your business.

I work with small business owners, consultants and coaches who are uncomfortable with selling because they think that means that they have to turn into a high-pressure, pushy phony and become someone they're not. They often equate the word selling with lying or coercion or manipulation.

So first of all, let's get this straight - effective sales copy is honest and compelling and comes from the gut. It's a reflection of who you really are and what you have to share with the world.

It uses the power of persuasion - not lies, not hype, not high pressure. It's about your honesty, integrity and expertise, and the reason that you're in business doing what you do.

Effective sales copy informs and educates the reader. It stimulates their interest and makes them want to know more. And it explains "why you" and not someone else.

Development Director

J.E.D.I. Women

November 1995 – August 1996 (10 months)

How do you describe being a part of Camelot? Of watching low-income women step outside their comfort zones and find and wield their true power? Fight for what mattered, what was right and what was seldom easy? Like the street law project, so battered and abused women could find justice and protection under the law? The employment resource center, so low-income parents could take classes, look for work and improve their skills? The free onsite child care space, so those same parents knew their children were safe and secure while they made a better life for their kids? Or "My Sister's Closet" so that with the support of professional businesswomen, women had business clothing for interviews and new jobs? We challenged unfair legislation, worked for new laws, and helped to educate, inform and engage a community. "Don't let it be forgot...that once there was a spot...For one brief, shining moment that was known as Camelot."
