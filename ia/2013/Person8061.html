Anita Statman, CEM's Summary

I'm honored to have been apppointed by the Martinez administration to serve New Mexico. I look forward to working across state agencies, with county and city governments, as well as the public in increasing ongoing prevention and preparedness activities in the State as well as improving our response capability to support local governments and our citizens' safety and property.

I've been working at the nexus of public safety and public policy issues in emergency management and homeland security issues for more than 25 years. I have worked as a government employee and as a consultant across all the hazards we plan for.

My goal is to continue to be part of the discussion and the process; to continue to help decision makers make decisions about protection, prevention, and preparedness with the best information about risk and consequences in hand as possible. That was my goal as a consultant and that remains my goal serving in the Martinez administration.

Anita Statman, CEM's Experience

Chief Executive Officer

CONOPS Consulting LLC

October 2008 – September 2012 (4 years) Reston, VA

Ms. Tallarico started and served as the CEO of CONOPS Consulting LLC a professional services firm specializing in emergency management services and headquartered in Reston, Virginia. CONOPS helped dozens of government clients improve preparedness through planning, training, and exercises consistent with contemporary DHS doctrine (HSEEP). We also provided support during disasters by offering field experienced senior level emergency management professionals to support public assistance and individual assistance programs. CONOPS employees are proud of those we served and thank our clients for the opportunity to provide assistance.

Senior Vice President/Vice President

ICF International

Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; ICFI; Management Consulting industry

March 1999 – October 2008 (9 years 8 months)

Ms. Tallarico was a Senior Vice President with ICF International where she led its Emergency Management and Homeland Security business for 10 years. Ms.Tallarico had operational and sales responsibility for up to 100 staff and about several multi-year, multi-million dollar contracts at DHS, DOD, and DHHS, in addition to over 30 other contracts around the country in UASIs and major urban areas. Under her tenure the business grew at an average rate of 18% annually.

Project Manager & Senior Associate

ICF International

Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; ICFI; Management Consulting industry

September 1992 – March 1998 (5 years 7 months)

Prior to becoming the Practice Leader of the EM & HS business in 1998, she managed projects for FEMA, DOE, EPA as well as state and local governments. For eight years she served as the Project Manager for ICF support to the National Response Team (NRT) through EPA, as the chair of the National Response Team. She directed support of tasks including the preparation of guidance, reports, and analyses, and assistance in coordination of meeting and workshops. ICF supported the four standing committees of the NRT: the Executive Secretariat (a management committee), Preparedness, Response, and Science and Technology Committees, as well as several ad-hoc committees. She also directed the development of a Public Health CONOPS and Emergency Management Planning for Georgia. For FEMA Ms. Tallarico managed a project for the Eastern Territorial Close-Out Team to identify the current and past problems affecting the timely close-out of disaster recovery activities in FEMA Regions I through IV for events declared from 1989 – 1993.
