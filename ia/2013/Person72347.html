There is a reason why this phrase appears on my business cards and my marketing materials…because it’s crucial to your business’ success! Businesses that focus all of their time on attracting clients will find that it’s fun and exciting to bring in new customers. However, that excitement will fade after they realize that it takes a lot of time, energy and resources to constantly bring in new business to replace what they’ve lost. Likewise, companies that focus solely on retention will find that their handful of clients are very loyal to them, but they can’t survive on just a handful of clients. This is why it’s important to have a comprehensive marketing plan that incorporates both attraction and retention.

If you’re not tracking with me yet…check out these retention statistics:

-The probability of selling to an existing customer is 60-70%: and there’s only a 5-10% probability that you will sell to a new prospect. Source: Marketing Metrics

-Just a 10% improvement in customer retention results in a 30% increase in the value of the company. Source: Bain & Co.

-A 5% increase in customer retention can increase business profits by 25% to 125%. Source: Gartner Group and “Leading on the Edge of Chaos,” Emmett C. Murphy and Mark A. Murphy, 2003
