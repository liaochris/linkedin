Bernell Ingram's Summary

Bernell King Ingram is a professional coach, corporate trainer, certified Japanese and English language coach, author, speaker, and president of Visions International. Bernell has excelled at language and intercultural coaching to global executives and expatriate families for over 13 years. She has coached and trained fellow coaches, managers and global executives of AFL Telecommunications (Fujikura), Michelin, AFCO (Denso), BMW and other small businesses and multinational corporations of USA, Japan, Korea, Kuwait, Mexico, Germany, France, Austria, Spain, Brazil, Colombia, Venezuela, Ecuador, and Argentina. Her love for languages and cultures has led her to Mexico, Canada, and as far as Japan on a study abroad and as a professional scholar with Rotary International.

Visions International has made noteworthy contributions to the work of small business and microbusiness development by partnering with and volunteering for economic development organizations to start and development microfinance and business coaching programs. Visions International has directly assisted businesses in securing over $1 million in financing. Partnerships with lenders have provided entrepreneurs access to over $1 million in new financing opportunities.

Bernell Ingram's Projects

  • SC Commission for Minority Affairs Minority Business Development Program Advisory Committee

    • November 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Bernell Ingram, Greg Davis, Nika White, Rogie Nelson, Evelyn Logo, Nivia Hayes, Sophia Vickers, Dexter Sharp, Karen Nichols, Floyd Johnson

    The mission of the Minority Business Development Program Advisory Committee is to help guide and direct policies relevant to the Commission’s Minority Business Development Program which supports the development of strong and effective minority businesses in South Carolina. The committee will develop robust programming to address the needs of the citizens of South Carolina.The South Carolina Commission for Minority Affairs was signed into law to study the causes and effects of socio-economic deprivation of minorities in the State and to implement programs to address inequities confronting minorities in the State. A core mission of the Commission is “to serve as a liaison with community leaders, businesses, and elected officials to build the infrastructure needed to foster economic prosperity among members of the minority population.”
