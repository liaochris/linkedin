  • A Potentially Fatal Situation: PR crisis management

    • December 2007 to May 2009
    Team Members: Inga McVicar

    Brief:
    The client, a high profile national organisation, was publically linked to an individual who had been arrested, charged and subsequently found guilty of crimes of a serious nature.

    The client organisation had no awareness that this individual had been committing such crimes, and the Police publically stated that the crimes had no connection whatsoever to the organisation.

    However, public perception (both individually and through the media) could make incorrect associations between the organisation and the crimes, especially considering the nature of the organisation’s work.

    The arrest, charging and subsequent trial was reported in the National (UK) media.

    - Advised the client organisation’s National Board on all external communications regarding this external issue
    - Inputted into crisis planning procedures
    - Advised the staff team on handling media enquiries, will all enquires signposted onto McVicar Walden
    - Acted as the official spokesperson on all media enquiries
    - Issued statements to the press
    - Attended and report back on the ensuing court case, which was a commitment undertaken over a ten week period
    - Managed press relations at the court case
    - Submitted corrections to the press to ensure fair and accurate reporting
    - Monitored and briefed the National Board on all relevant media coverage
    - Liaised with key partners and government agencies

    Result:

    - 100% of all media enquiries were handled correctly by the staff team, ensuring such enquiries were dealt with promptly and efficiently
    - 65% of media reports did not mention the organisation
    - Of media reports which did mention the organisation, 91% correctly reported the true nature of the link between this individual and the organisation, which, in-turn, emphasised that the organisation was not associated with the crimes committed by the individual

  • JK Rowling world exclusive launch

    Launch of the 5th Harry Potter Book – The Order of the Phoenix – with JK Rowling, 2003.

    ... without being able to tell anyone (including partner, friends, family, colleagues, branch staff members, or even the school children attending the event) that JK Rowling herself would be appearing at 12.01am to celebrate the 5th instalment of her world-famous series.

    That meant arranging a very high-profile event, which required precision and strict care of every detail, without anyone realising what was about to take place:
    - High level security
    - Co-ordination of media attendance with JK Rowling’s PR Company (Colman Getty) – without them guessing where the event was about to take place

    The results:
    - 20 school children met their hero, and had their copies of The Order of the Phoenix signed (which, I understand, were the only 20 books to be signed of that edition by JK Rowling)
    - JK Rowling celebrated the launch of her new book with her fans, in a safe, calm and enjoyable atmosphere
    - Media coverage was truly blanket – from UK newspapers to TV and radio, to American, Australian, Indian etc press. Quite simply, everyone reported on this event, and every photo used had the Waterstone’s branding in it
