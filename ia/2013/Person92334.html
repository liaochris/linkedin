Michael Eric Berube's Summary

I used my hard earned GI Bill money and obtained the Sheepskin in Marketing (everyone said I shouldn't waste my time on the Philosophy Degree that I really wanted) and I then pursued the Great American Corporate Dream (tm) for a little over a decade in Market Research and Sales & Marketing.

I really believe in taking care of my clients, so I was a fairly successful and a very busy 'sales guy'. What left me unfulfilled were the demands of working for corporations who weren't always able to be as client focused as I felt we should be able to be. While the last corporation I worked for was a great experience, it was a casualty of the Great DotCom Implosion of 2001. After the dust cleared, I reevaluated where I was, compared that to where I wanted to be and realised that yet another startup wouldn't get me to where I wanted to go so I left Sales & Marketing to come back to being a happy, successful, self employed photographer, dad and husband full time.

Career wise, I am right where I want to spend the rest of my 'working' life. I get to work with the creative and interesting people whom I chose to work with and who value my product and services. I can be as customer oriented as I want to be, without answering to a new kid on the block CEO or VCs about what good customer service should cost. Other than when I'm actually making photographs with clients on site, I have a very flexible schedule and can balance my work and family life now instead of waiting until I retire. (IF I choose to retire someday.)

My profession meets my needs financially and spiritually and enables me to take a much larger role in my family's life than I would have ever been able to had I continued to be salaried from 8am-6pm (plus a two hour commute) in a white shirt and red tie from Monday to Saturday.

Life is not just good, life is great and I want to continue to work with clients who realise this as well until the day I am no longer able to hold a camera up.

Specialties

Event Photography, Family Portraiture, Business Portraiture, Real Estate Tours, Product Photography, Photojournalism and Fine Art are my photographic specialties.
