Douglas van Dyke's Summary

I suppose I should’ve known that I’d be involved in education as a career. As early as 1st grade I was “teaching” my younger sister what I’d learned in school that day – complete with using her blackboard to do math & making my own hand-made dittos!

While the first 20something years of my life would be marked by a passion for acting and a BFA in theatre, a random need for a sociology credit (& a desire not to get up for class before 9am), I took a philosophy of education class. I was fascinated & hooked. Before I knew it, I had completed a teaching certificate and had my first teaching job.

After 15 years in the classroom, I became a curriculum AP. Turns out I’m a curriculum & process geek! Getting into classrooms, watching colleagues at their craft, mapping curriculum, coaching peers, facilitating professional development all deepened my career. I had moved from being a teacher of adolescents to being a facilitator of adult learning.

Common Elements, an educational consulting company, is the next step in that evolution. Our name stems from the understanding that no matter the field, there are common elements necessary for success. These elements include professional growth, life-long learning, positive collegial relationships, a creative professional workplace & continuous reflection.

Now Common Elements is engaged in professional development efforts in over a dozen schools across Chicagoland. We provide independent consulting for a national curriculum improvement company. & we serve a variety of non-profits. Not your run-of-the-mill consulting company, our processes involve more listening & less talking. We ask questions to elicit our clients’ particular experiences. Then with a deep toolkit we tailor leadership & professional growth solutions to the specific needs of individuals. We are the resource most clients don’t even know they need. That is, until we identify the essentials for crafting their effective workplace, until we engage their Common Elements.
