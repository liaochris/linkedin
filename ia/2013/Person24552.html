Enterprise Architect

University of Kentucky

Educational Institution; 5001-10,000 employees; Higher Education industry

July 2010 – November 2011 (1 year 5 months)

As the Enterprise Architect for Academic Technologies & Process, I work to more closely integrate the student and faculty perspective on teaching and learning technologies into the development of the enterprise technology architecture of the University of Kentucky. I lead our Experience Analysis and Design process to drive more effective design of faculty and student experiences and technology interfaces to improve the strategic teaching, research and retention goals of the university. I also work to promote awareness and integration of Web 2.0/Social Technologies into the architecture supporting these missions.

Social Technology Strategist

University of Kentucky

Educational Institution; 5001-10,000 employees; Higher Education industry

August 2009 – November 2011 (2 years 4 months) Lexington, KY

As the Social Technology Strategist for Office of Undergraduate Education at the University of Kentucky, I work with faculty, staff and students to develop, maintain and expand the use of social technologies in the promotion of undergraduate success and retention. This includes managing our private undergraduate social network, maintaining a personal and institutional Twitter presence (and working with other campus offices to do the same), building engagement in Second Life, and exploring the use of other social technologies such as ARGs, games, Facebook, LinkedIn, social bookmarking and online video.

Lecturer

University of Kentucky

Educational Institution; 5001-10,000 employees; Higher Education industry

August 2005 – August 2009 (4 years 1 month)

As a Lecturer in the Political Science Department at the University of Kentucky, I developed and taught multiple courses each semester ranging from Kentucky and Appalachian Politics to Political Theory to American Government. In every course I worked to teach not only the substantive material of the course, but also to push the limits of conventional pedagogy by radical incorporation of social technologies (such as blogs, microblogging, wikis and Second Life). I also served as Director of Undergraduate Studies for the department from 2007-2008 and as Internship Director from 2007-2009.

Program Director

MACED

Key member of MACED management team responsible for management of budget and activities of non-business development programs, including sustainable forestry, technical assistance research and community change capacity building. Worked directly with President to develop new programs and grant proposals. Conducted policy, economic and demographic research.

Research Director

University of Kentucky Appalachian Center

Educational Institution; 5001-10,000 employees; Higher Education industry

August 2002 – July 2003 (1 year)

Key member of UKAC management team responsible for design and management of research projects, recruitment and supervision of internal research team (4 RA’s, 1 intern), providing data and client service to University and Center constituencies, and managing metadata and budgeting systems. Coordinated, contributed to, and authored research and production of Center research reports, presentations and website and newsletter pieces. Directed UKAC Common Knowledge Network program, a participatory research project involving the networking of grassroots citizen groups focusing on creation of a Citizen Research Center in Pike County, KY. Successfully lobbied for State funding for the project. Directed UKAC Civil Society Project, including research design, conducting primary and secondary research on nonprofit organizations, updating and expanding a proprietary database of 450+ civil society organizations in a six-state area of Central Appalachia, and writing grants for support of the project.
