Lawrence Watkins' Summary

Lawrence Watkins is the Founder & CEO of Great Black Speakers, the largest African-American owned speakers bureau in the nation. He works with celebrities, entertainers, and thought leaders to book them for speaking engagements at top universities, corporations, and civic organizations. Lawrence is experienced in internet marketing, public speaking, business development, and consumer behavior.

Lawrence's most recent startup is Ujamaa Deals, where he is the co-founder and Chief Operating Officer. Ujamaa is a daily deal site that promotes black owned businesses. In 2011, Lawrence was featured as "Entrepreneur of the Week" by Black Enterprise through his work with Ujamaa. Also, Lawrence won a $10,000 grant through 100 Urban Entrepreneurs by winning their pitch competition in 2011.

In 2012, Lawrence added additional responsibilities to his plate and became the small business expert for TheGrio.com (a division of NBC News). Here, he writes a weekly column on entrepreneurship, motivation, and general business principles.

On top of his responsibilities for TheGrio, Lawrence has been recognized by top organizations and media outlets for his work as an entrepreneur. Lawrence was named a 2011 honoree, at The White House, of the Kauffman Foundation’s Empact100 Award which recognizes the top 100 entrepreneurs under 30. He has also written for Forbes, US News, and Inc. while being featured in the Huffington Post, Venture Beat, EBONY, Mashable, Mixergy, and dozens of other publications.

Lawrence is an active member of The Young Entrepreneur Council, Alpha Phi Alpha Fraternity, and 100 Urban Entrepreneurs. He is an alum of The Johnson School at Cornell University, Management Leadership for Tomorrow, and the Public Policy and International Affairs Fellowship.

Specialties: systems thinking, consumer behavior, internet marketing, entrepreneurship, public speaking, branding, online business operations

Lawrence Watkins' Experience

Founder & CEO

Great Black Speakers Bureau

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Marketing and Advertising industry

January 2007 – Present (7 years) Greater Atlanta Area

Great Black Speakers' aim is to be an information disseminator whose goal is to deliver powerful messages to the masses. We do this in three main ways:

1.) GBS connects with event planners to help them find the perfect speaker for their event and audience. We rid them of the logistical nightmare of booking and managing multiple speakers, which saves many hours of time.

2.) We recently launched GBS University, which is an e-learning platform that provides an opportunity for our speakers and audiences to connect virtually. This is a way for people to digest the knowledge from our speakers at a fraction of the cost of a live speaking engagement.

3.) Through Great Pro Speakers, we offer courses to aspiring speakers to teach them how to market and brand themselves, which helps them compete better in the marketplace of ideas.

Some of our company highlights include:

1.) Represent and broker speaking engagements for over 200 professional speakers.
2.) Developed a web platform that makes it easy for speakers to produce content and share it with our media partners.
3.) Established a network of recommended suppliers to help speakers grow their brands.
4.) GBS has grown to become the largest African American owned speakers bureau in the nation.
5.) Helped organize many marketing campaigns for Fortune 100 companies and regularly books speakers in Lagos, Nigeria.

The Public Policy and International Affairs Fellowship (PPIA) is a national program that prepares adults for an advanced degree and ultimately for careers and influential roles serving the public good. The flagship program of this organization is the Junior Summer Institute (JSI), a rigorous seven-week training focused on quantitative, analytical and communication skills.

From a highly selective and competitive national application pool, twenty college juniors were invited to participate in the Heinz College’s PPIA JSI program. The composition of the PPIA class reflects academic, ethnic, regional, sexual orientation, gender, religious belief and age diversity, as well as the potential and demonstrated leadership necessary to positively impact the public sector.
