Jason Hoeppner's Summary

I am a consultant to the insurance industry on matters of agency-management and technology, with experience in Information Technology Project Management and Lean Six Sigma principles.

As an Information Technology Project Manager, I led initiatives that enabled a national insurance company to enter new markets, specifically the independent-agency channel; to conduct business more efficiently; and to compete more effectively across the country using real-time technologies.

I have been an independent-agency consultant since 2005, and have led technology transitions, implemented standardized workflows, and held management system training seminars for every level of insurance agency employee — from Principals, Producers, and CSRs to new staff members.

I am also active with local users’ groups, conducting training seminars for agents that allow them to better utilize their technology and management system and to improve their agencies’ bottom line. I also consult with agencies on their use of Social Media, a topic on which I present at users’ groups and other industry conventions.

I was on active duty with the U. S. Navy for 8½ years and during that time I was certified as a nuclear engineer, and I also served two years as an exchange officer with the Chilean Navy in South America. I continue to serve in the Navy Reserves and completed a ten-month mobilization in 2009 to the Combined Joint Task Force – Horn of Africa, where I ran the Joint Operations Center, overseeing humanitarian assistance, civil affairs, and security cooperation missions for 13 nations in East Africa.

I am a Certified Insurance Counselor (CIC) and is affiliated with the Professional Insurance Agents Connecticut (PIACT), and the Applied Systems Client Network (ASCnet), for which he is a certified presenter. He is also a volunteer with ACORD Users’ Information Exchange (AUGIE) and the Agents Council on Technology (ACT) as well as being an ACORD-certified POWER of Change® facilitator.

Specialties

Technology and agency management platforms - Applied, Vertafore. System conversions. Insurance agency operations and management. Agency processes and workflows. Information Security Programs and breach notification laws. Social Media. Basic accounting. Fluent in Spanish (and English!)

Jason Hoeppner's Experience

Consultant

B.H. Burke & Co., Inc.

January 2008 – Present (5 years 10 months) Westbrook, CT

Specializing in technology, workflows, and agency operations with a focus on productivity, Jason works with independent insurance agencies to help them reach their potential. He is an experienced trainer with extensive leadership experience from his time in the U.S. Navy. Jason is a Certified Insurance Counselor (CIC), a certified master presenter for ASCnet (Applied Systems Community Network) and participates regularly in AUGIE (ACORD Users Group Information Exchange) and ACT (Agents Council for Technology).

Jason implements workflows at agencies, and has guided several through complete technology transitions. He is experienced in Applied Epic, TAM, & DORIS; Vertafore AMS360, Sagitta, & AfW.

Jason has been in the forefront in helping agencies ensure they are in compliance with personal information security laws and breach notification requirements (such as 201 CMR 17.00) by providing agency owners and managers a clear approach to addressing the requirements of the laws of Massachusetts and other states. He was a member of the MAIA (MA Association of Insurance Agents) Data Security Panel of Professionals in September 2009, at which he illustrated how to find and fix gaps in compliance while improving agency operations and marketing. Jason was also on the Data Security Panel at MAIA’s Big Event in October 2010 and wrote about the operational and business implications of the new Massachusetts law in an article for the (December 4, 2009 issue of) The Standard.

Jason has presented the topic of information security and security breach requirements to numerous industry groups, including the NY Hudson Valley Users’ Group (ASCnet), the Connecticut Professional Insurance Agents (PIA), the Big I of Connecticut (IIAC) Young Agents. He is a member of the ACT Information Security working group and has guided more than 200 independent agencies in MA, CT, and NY through the implementation of a written security program to ensure compliance with the MA state regulation.
