Donna Shannon's Summary

As a professional career coach, my goal is to empower the job seeker.
Whether you are seeking a job in private service or the traditional corporate world, I can help you capitalize on your most relevant skills and abilities to land your target job.

Since 2004, I have been teaching modern job searching techniques. As a former recruiter, I know what employers want, from nailing the right key words to acing the interview. Everything is based on real-world HR practices and feedback from hiring managers.

From targeted, effective resumes to comprehensive workshops, I want my clients to understand how the job search process works - and help them find a job they love as quickly as possible.

Our services include:
♦ Resumes and cover letters
♦ LinkedIn profiles and biographies
♦ My book: "How to Get a Job Without Going Crazy"
♦ Workshops on resume writing, interview skills and job search strategy
♦ Interview coaching
♦ Job search coaching

My staff resume writers and myself work with a wide variety of industries, including Finance, HR, Pharmaceutical/ Healthcare, Sales, Non-Profit, and Federal/ Government. We have helped everyone from top Executives to entry-level workers land a job.

Plus, we are one of the few career coaching services in the world that works specifically with the private service industry. If you are an Estate Manager, Household Manager, Personal Assistant, Private Chef or Nanny, we understand your unique job and how to capture those elements in a professional resume.

  • The Job Seekers' Guide to Private Service Agencies

    • The Personal Touch Career Services
    • January 2013
    Authors: Donna Shannon

    In the luxury lifestyle management industry, agencies are a key factor in finding some of the best jobs. However, not all agencies are the same. Some specialize in specific areas or in certain types of positions. Years of experience required may vary from one agency to another. Some agencies even provide mentoring to the most promising candidates. With all of these variations, how can a job seeker determine which agencies fit his needs best?

    The Job Seeker's Guide to Private Service Agencies is a listing of over 20 of the most recognized firms in the United States. Over a year of development went into the book, contacting agencies and asking them the same core questions to highlight their target candidates and standard procedures. Contact information is included for every agency listed. Bonus material includes How Agencies (Generally) Work and Making the Right Impression.
