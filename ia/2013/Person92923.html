Michael Grant's Summary

I started out a journalism major and have since become a designer, front-end developer and information graphics artist.

It all started with visual communications professor Sandra Lee, at Grambling State University, where I became Graphics Editor and Managing Editor of The Gramblinite student newspaper.

Fresh from course work I was trained by the prestigious New York Times Student Journalism Institute. The Orange County Register then taught me daily graphics production. The Baltimore Sun custom-carved for me a development plan in graphics and features.

In 10 weeks the Sun-Sentinel made a features designer of me. A couple months later, I had a command of monster graphics production, using programs like Flash and Lightwave.

Though the Atlanta Journal-Constitution supported my graphics development, I learned more about the declining print-journalism industry. As a result I set out to learn web languages such as HTML, CSS, Javascript, PHP and MySQL.

I attended and studies design and web programming languages at Savannah College of Art and Design, while also working as a lead designer at Appen Newspapers.

After leaving the Atlanta market, I began consulting and have been hired for special projects - both print and web - working with The LA Watts Times, the Los Angeles Wave, and News In Black.

My skill set has earned me contract work for non-profits, producing sites for The Lavelle Center, BMX National, and promotional work for Crystal Stairs.

I'm back to working full-time print-journalism. My last job was at a Tribune Co.'s Forum Publishing Group in South Florida. My current job is fantastic. I enjoy a significant roll as a designer in the Gannett Asbury Park Press Design Studio.

My metamorphosis has paused at an interesting stage - part web designer, part information architect; all involve communicating visually. I'm currently learning jquery, design for iPads and epub formats, and have embraced Wordpress as my website development platform of choice.

Specialties

Layout design, information graphics, video editing, web design, presentation design, and systems planning.
