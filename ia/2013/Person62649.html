Jerome Gilmer's Summary

Jerome Gilmer has lived and worked in Denver, Colorado since 1976. Since settling in the area, Jerome’s music has been heard in markets across the country and internationally. His song, "This Is Colorado," has become a staple in the state's school music programs. His work for KCNC-TV helped the station forge an image as a leading news channel. He was musical director for the Inaugural Gala of Denver's Pepsi Center, featuring the Arapahoe Community Orchestra, jazz recording artists Dotsero, the Eternal Life Singers and an aerial ballet by Debra Brown, choreographer for Cirque du Soliel.

He has composed and produced theme packages for the Colorado Rockies and the Denver Nuggets.. When the state bid for the Winter Olympics and later hosted the Olympic Festival, when it introduced the new convention center and the new international airport, Jerome’s works were prominent in the presentations. In 1998 his work was featured in both the Olympic Winter Games in Nagano, Japan, and the Goodwill Games here in the US. He produced the theme for the BBC’s "Summer Sunday" series and his theme and underscore for NBC's award winning "News For Kids" played in over 150 markets throughout the US. As a contributor to the OmniMusic Production Music Library his work has been heard in programs around the world, including the David Letterman Show.

He has been a contributing composer on Animal Planet's "Breed All About It" series. He has scored two of Chrysler Corporation's "Mission" films and one for Intelsat. He produced news and promotional music from 2002 through 2011 for the 28 station Hearst-Argyle Television Group and has received eight regional Emmy’s from the National Academy of Television Arts and Sciences.
