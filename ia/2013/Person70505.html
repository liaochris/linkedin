Julia Cantor's Summary

I lead social influence marketing at Engauge, a full-service marketing agency for the digital age. I have ten years of experience in marketing communications, primarily in social strategy and public relations. Julia Cantor's Projects

  • Pinterest: Social Media's Newest Sweetheart

    • October 2011 to March 2012
    Team Members: Julia Cantor, Drew Hawkins, Teresa Caro

    In the fall of 2011, we recognized a huge surge in interest in Pinterest. The user base grew exponentially, and the users joining the network weren't the typical beta users. Instead, they were the 25-40 year old women who were taking time out of their day and out of their time previously spent on Facebook. They were flocking to the newest shiny social media platform, Pinterest. Pinterest thrives off of the natural instinct to appreciate photos and multimedia content.

    We created this report to educate brands about the opportunities of the platform. To get a handle on the opportunities, we needed to sift through the buzz, explore the usage patterns and analyze the data. So we did primary research in the form of surveys as well as social listening through Crimson Hexagon. Most notably, we had the opportunity to work with HPCC Systems from LexisNexis Risk Solutions – an open source, enterprise-proven Big Data analytics provider, which gave us quality insight into the true data behind the platform.

  • Social Networking to Social Niche-working

    • April 2012 to April 2012
    Team Members: Julia Cantor, Drew Hawkins

    In today’s online world, most people and brands are consumed with thoughts of hitting a “critical mass.” As time goes on, it’s the “credible mass” that will be the most powerful. This presentation, part of Fuel Innovation, an event at Engauge, walks through primary research and firsthand experience with online platforms such as Pinterest, Untapped, Instagram, Path and much more.

  • Influencer Marketing: 6 Steps to Gaining the Attention of Top Brands

    • October 2012 to October 2012
    Team Members: Julia Cantor

    Digital and social marketing has flipped traditional marketing on its head. Today, everyday people with serious passions for their interests are those with the most power. With their authenticity and credibility, they garner attention of consumers, which is very valuable to brands.

    Even with a closer eye on the relationships between blogger and brand, many content creators have partnered with brands to receive free trips and high value items in exchange for exposure.

    How can you be included in these campaigns? This presentation covers six things social influencers can do to get the attention of brands.

  • ENFLUENCE: Social Influence Marketing Report

    • February 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Julia Cantor, Teresa Caro, Noah Echols, Stephanie Critchfield

    ENFLUENCE is a report on social influence marketing. The report leverages findings from Engauge-conduced primary research with both influencers and fans of influencers. This report helps brands understand the behaviors, interests and motivations of influencers in order to create more engaged and fruitful relationships. It is also an excellent report for influencers to glean information about their audiences.
