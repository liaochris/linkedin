Alex Kuang's Summary

Software Developer based in the Greater Boston area. Graduated from Worcester Polytechnic Institute in 2012, and currently working as a developer at athenahealth.

While in school, I interned for a small startup building web applications with python. My work there involved both research for the core recommendation engine as well as web application work with django and google appengine. After school ended, I started a full-time position at athenahealth, where I deal mainly with Perl and Oracle, with a smattering of HTML/CSS/jQuery.

Academically, I have been exposed to C/C++, Java, and Scheme, along with a variety of applications and concepts.

Outside of work, I play around with Scala as a hobby. I like Lift in particular since its "view-first" philosophy differs drastically from the approach of MVC-style frameworks, and I believe it's more suited for the development needs of the modern webapp. Baking in features like Comet and stateful form wizards that traditionally involve a lot of manual labor to implement is definitely a plus as well. And of course, Scala runs on the JVM which means that it's blazing fast and has access to all of the Java ecosystem.
