Adam Lowe's Summary

Adam Lowe is a writer, publisher, producer and live artist from Leeds, who now lives in Manchester. His most recent chapbook, Precocious, was a reader nomination for the Guardian Book Award.

Adam was 2013 LGBT History Month Poet Laureate and 2012 Olympic Poet for Yorkshire. He was one of the 2010 young writers in residence at the I Love West Leeds Arts Festival.

In 2009 he received four Lambda Award nominations, in both poetry and prose. In 2008, his magazine, Polluto, was awarded the Spectrum Fantastic Art Silver Editorial Award for an illustration of his story 'Singer' by artists Kurt Huggins & Zelda Devon.

Adam is a graduate of Street Voices 2 and an active member of Young Inscribe. In the past he has been a part of Critters.org and Orson Scott Card's Hatrack River Writers' Workshop. Adam regularly delivers workshops and runs an annual mentoring and masterclass programme for new writers in the North who specialise in science fiction, fantasy, horror and cross-genre writing: the Dog Horn Masterclass Programme. Because of his work in the region, he is a Youth Ambassador for the Cultural Olympiad in Yorkshire.

He has appeared in Word Riot, Unlikely Stories, The Cadaverine, Chimeraworld 5, The Leeds Guide, WAMACK, Saucytooth's, Kaleidotrope, Ex Plus Ultra, PoetCasting.co.uk, Cake, Chorus: A Literary Mixtape (ed. by Saul Williams), The Guardian, Electric Literature's The Outlet, and literaturedevelopment.org. In 2009, his debut novella, Troglodyte Rose, was also released in limited edition hardback by Cadaverine Publications. An expanded novel-length paperback is due out the other side of the Mayan apocalypse.
