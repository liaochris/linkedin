Andrés Manso's Summary

The turning point of my life was in 2000, when I began to work in a start up founded by a Stanford student. Working there inspired me to become one of the crazy individuals who will make the world different, and better.

In particular, I am very interested in bridging the digital divide for non-technological people. In this I am motivated by personal experience. When I turned 20, I founded a company based on the home automation industry. To put my resolve into practice I have applied myself to acquiring multiple skills: technology, real-world problem solving and execution.

First of all, I have acquired a solid technological background as a computer science major. As well as the core curriculum, I have taught Masters in 5 different Spanish universities. I am engaged as leader in the residential gateways normalization group in Spain and I was vice-chair and founder of AIDA.

Secondly, I am committed to sharpening my problem solving skills, which will be critical in introducing experimental innovations to the real world. As project manager and founder of very varied businesses such as media portals, search engine marketing. Feeling comfortable with uncertainty gives me confidence in the problem solving process, which allows me to stay focused on the task and to come up with innovative solutions.

Last but not least, I have been honing my execution skills in order to deliver greater impacts. I am not content to be just an engineer; I want to be like one of the people featured in "Think Different!" and to explore, create and inspire. I have published scientific articles and I was co-author of two books. I have submitted a number of proposals to the European Union to get funding for R+D projects.

I possess the leadership and passion to utilize technological innovations to achieve my goal. I have never doubted my ability to make a difference and I firmly believe that Those who are crazy enough to think they can change the world, are the ones who do.
