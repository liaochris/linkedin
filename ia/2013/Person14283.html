Bill Fulton's Summary

Bill Fulton is known throughout the Los Angeles area as a composer, arranger, orchestrator, music producer and pianist/keyboardist. Bill has been very active in the Southland vicinity in and around Los Angeles, CA since 1987 when he moved there from the San Francisco Bay Area. As a matter of fact Bill’s only real intention on moving to Los Angeles was to attend the Dick Grove Music Workshops to study with the master himself, Dick Grove. Bill had a tremendously wonderful time while attending the school and playing local gigs at clubs and private events. At the end of his year at Dick Grove, Bill received a recommendation from Paul Goldfield (the engineering instructor at the school) to MCA-TV (now NBC Universal) to work as a staff composer. That was the start of Bill’s career as a film and tv composer. My music can be seen all over the world in syndicated television (tv) shows and films. I even have a three second sound-bite in the movies “The Ring” with Naomi Watts and “Unbreakable” with Bruce Willis and Samuel L. Jackson (look for “I Am Weasel” playing on a tv set in both films. My film and tv music is published in part by my publishing company Fulltone Music and in part by the various production companies I have worked for. BMI has been the PRO (Performing Rights Organization) of my music since 1989.

Bill has also worked as an orchestrator and copyist and is known for his big band writing which recently (2008) has become more in demand from high school and university music departments, music camps, and established big bands throughout the world. Bill also started the Bill Fulton Band in 2002. The Bill Fulton Band is a jazz fusion octet that features some of the greatest talents in Los Angeles. The Bill Fulton Band without a doubt represents LA’s (Los Angeles’) top players and musicians performing jazz fusion, smooth jazz, funk, pop, and classical styles.
