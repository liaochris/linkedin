Director of Technology

I directed and implemented the long-term IT vision of the university's 300-member, student-run TV studio, and served on the executive board.

* Solely responsible for the continued performance of 8+ virtual servers and 4 physical servers (running ESX Server 4, Windows Server 2008, and Mac OS X Server), 20+ Mac and Windows workstations, 4+ video production appliances, and six core firewall/router/switch devices.

* Collaborated with university and contracting engineers, architects and project managers to design, install, and test all technical aspects of the ~$250,000 studio construction project. Focused on data connectivity, power, spatial, and HVAC needs.
* Developed technology-related portions of the annual line item budget proposals, for operating needs, enhancing system stability, improving production and video distribution workflow efficiency, and preparing to edit and store high-definition video.
* Trained staff from each show on software and broadcast equipment, regularly seeking expanded background and "best practice" information.


NOTE: I first held this position from Dec '09 - Dec '10, when the role was officially termed Computer Systems Manager. From Dec '10 - Dec '11, I no longer officially held the position but continued to fully carry out its responsibilities as General Manager when the position was vacant. I officially returned to the position in Dec '11 to continue advancing the studio's technology setup and workflows, as the studio needed new technical systems and solutions due to a facility expansion and more demanding production requirements.
