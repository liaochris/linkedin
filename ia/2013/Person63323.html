Jewel Ward's Summary

An analyst by nature, my doctoral research interests include the management, stewardship, curation, and preservation of (usually "big") data for use and re-use, as well as understanding and implementing the technology that makes all of that possible.

I also explore the issues that arise from the data explosion. In my world view, content is king, and the wonderful technology that houses it is temporary and ever-changing.

I have defended my dissertation proposal topic. It is, “Machine-level Policy Implementation in a Preservation Repository”. I expect to graduate in May 2014.

Interests/Specialties: machine-level policy implementation, data and metadata transfer, preservation systems and infrastructures, including: networked information, preservation data grids, the integrated Rule Oriented Data System (iRODS), the Open Archives Initiative Protocol for Metadata Harvesting (OAI-PMH), digital preservation, mark-up languages, information and web design, system and information processes, digital curation, digital stewardship, digital libraries and data archives.
