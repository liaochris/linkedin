Mike Flood's Summary

I compose and produce music in a diverse range of styles, drawing on influences from a varied musical career. I studied classical piano and music theory from the age of 7 (aargh, so long ago!), with Patrick Redmond, a Star Pupil prize winner from the Paris Conservatoire.

I worked as a keyboard player, pianist and songwriter in my own band projects, Cursory Glances, Sound Advice and After The Flood, and also worked with Dave Purdye (Fischer Z, The Labrettas), James Newton (The Lambrettas) and Steve Fennimore (The Gino Washington Band, Desmond Dekker) in bands ranging in style from Rock through New Country to Brit Pop.

In contrast, I'm also the Organist at an 11th Century Church! I compose for, and play keyboards in, the Christian Rock band 'Heaven's Heights' (check out www.heavensheights.com).

I work from my own studio just outside Brighton (UK).

Some examples of projects I've worked as the composer for:
The Blonde - music for Phoebe Wild's short comedy (2013)
Hotcakes - short film with New Orleans-based director Jo Custer (2012)
Say I Can with iKan - short competition film with Director Caroline Pires (2011)
7 Deadly Sins - short film with Director Samantha Roper (2011)
And The Oscar Goes To - short film with Red Carpet Films (2010)
Ongoing - Adventure Game soundtrack for STS
Dead Or Alive - a futuristic crime mystery film, by London Project Ltd.
My Baby - a Metropolitan Productions short thriller, premiered in London's Leicester Square as part of the BBC's British Short Film Festival.
Nigel The Plonker - another Metropolitan Productions short comedy, also shown in the BBC's British Short Film Festival.
The Warehouse - with director Elena Lario. This film was premiered in London's Leicester Square as well.
My Name Is Sally Jones? - a musical play co-written with Clara Armand - premiered on the London Fringe.
The Week That Changed The World - dramatic tableau performed in Churches in Sussex during Holy Week.
