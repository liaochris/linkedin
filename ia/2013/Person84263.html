Mahbuba Khan NCARB, AIA, LEED AP's Summary

Mahbuba founded MnKhan Architects PLLC in 2011 formerly known as MnK Architects. MnKhan Architects PLLC is a minority/women owned full service architectural firm with focus on commercial projects and sustainability consultancy. Before starting her own firm she worked at various nationally and internationally recognized firms for 12 years. She built and maintained relationships with her clients, peers and industry professionals.

Mahbuba serves as Chair of AIA Dallas Small Firm Roundtable. Working with the AIA Dallas chapter she stated "Under the Radar- best of small firms" Juried Exhibit and had a great participation this year. Last year she was selected as an Emerging Leader by AIA Dallas. Mahbuba is still staying involved with the “City Walk Play Ground” charity project her Emerging Leaders group started in 2012. She has been an active member of American Institute of architects and United States Green Building Council, regularly attending events and volunteering many hours at both of these organizations.

Background:

In February 2010, Mahbuba became a licensed architect in the State of Texas and is able to practice architecture in all 50 US states through reciprocation using her NCARB (National Council of architectural Registration Boards) certification. She is also a LEED Accredited Professional with specialty in Building Design and Construction. Out of her passion for architecture she has been doing small projects under the DBA “Noksha” since 2000. Mahbuba earned her 5 years Bachelor of Architecture Degree from Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology with Honors. She practiced Architecture in Asia before immigrating to the U.S.

  • Snowtex Outerware LTD

    • September 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Mahbuba Khan NCARB, AIA, LEED AP, Aaron McEwin, P.E., LEED AP,, Jose Montoya

    The 500,000 sq ft,7 storied Garments Factory is under construction on 3.86 acre land in a designated industrial zone with community connectivity. Most workers will walk or ride a cycle rickshaw or ride public bus to work. The entire building is designed to be mechanically ventilated with operable windows along the south side and exhaust fans and windows on the North side. The goal is to reduce energy consumption 40% from baseline. On site power by installing panel mounted small wind turbines right next to the exhaust fans and solar panels on the roof and gas operated generator. Rainwater will be harvested during the heavy Monsoon season and stored in huge concrete reservoirs at the semi-sunken basement. no municipal water will be used for irrigation. The building's 50% vegetated rooftop is designed with recreational ares. This industrial building is seeking LEED Platinum as New Construction under LEED 2009. This building will raise the bar in standards in Bangladesh's economic powerhouse garments industry sector. We are very excited to work on this challenging international project.
