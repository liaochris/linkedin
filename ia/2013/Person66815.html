Johnny Rice II, Dr.PH.'s Experience

Senior Program Associate - Center on Victimization and Safety, Supervised Visitation Initiative

Vera Institute of Justice

Nonprofit; 51-200 employees; Public Policy industry

October 2010 – Present (3 years 3 months)

Background:
The Vera Institute of Justice, a private non-profit organization, combines expertise in research, demonstration projects, and technical assistance to help leaders in government and civil society improve the systems on which people rely for justice and safety. Through its Center on Victimization and Safety (CVS), Vera works to promote innovative practices that address violence against women and has launched an initiative to enhance the capacity of supervised visitation programs to address issues of domestic violence.

Responsibilities:
As a Senior Program Associate based in Washington, D.C., provide technical assistance and training to federally funded Safe Haven’s supervised visitation grantees funded by the Office of Violence Against Women (OVW). Manage projects, contribute to the management of the center, and work closely with the Center Director and Project Director to achieve organizational goals.

Director - Office of Grants Management

Maryland Department of Human Resources

Government Agency; 501-1000 employees; Law Enforcement industry

July 2008 – October 2010 (2 years 4 months)

Administered and oversaw $27 million budget consisting of the following federally and state funded programs: victim services, food and nutrition and homeless/transitional services, and community-based projects consisting of father-focused grants and contracts. Ensured that the six core business processes guiding office and related activities were implemented as prescribed by the senior leadership team: (1) funding allocation/grant acquisition, (2) procurement, (3) monitoring, (4) fiscal payments, (5) reporting, and (6) communications and other activities (i.e., problem solving).

Stabilized newly established office during major organizational restructuring and managed diverse group of 12 senior and mid-level analysts and fiscal personnel charged with programmatic and fiscal oversight of grants, contracts, vendors, and consultants conducting business with OGM statewide to ensure compliance.
Served on boards, committees, and workgroups on behalf of agency secretary as required. Member of Governor’s Family Violence Council, Maryland State Board of Victim Services, and Interagency Council to End Homelessness.

Routinely conducted troubleshooting and complex analysis to achieve agency objectives. Possessed discretion to expedite flow of work as deemed necessary. Researched problems of considerable consequences to the agency and made recommendations using position papers, decision documents, and other communication tools. Specifically charged with developing the Continuity of Operation Plan (COOP) for the Office of the Secretary. Submitted plan approved by Maryland Emergency Management Agency (MEMA) in 2008.

Interests:
