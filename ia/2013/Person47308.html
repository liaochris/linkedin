Gaurav Nilakantan's Summary

Dr. Gaurav Nilakantan is the Editor of the Journal of Multifunctional Composites. He is currently a Senior Research Associate in the Mork Family Dept of Chemical Eng & Materials Sci at the University of Southern California, and the Founder of Nilakantan Composites. He was previously a Research Associate at the Center for Composite Materials at the University of Delaware. He holds a PhD degree in Materials Science and Engineering from the University of Delaware and a Masters degree in Mechanical Engineering from the University of Cincinnati. Dr. Nilakantan is a recognized expert in the field of ballistic impact modeling of woven fabrics and textile composites used in soft body armor. He has developed probabilistic and multiscale fabric finite element models with membrane, yarn, and filament level architectures that can predict the entire V0-V100 impact curve (www.drgaurav.org). His other research work includes modeling the fiber-matrix interface, occupant survivability during mine blasts underneath infantry vehicles, occupant safety during automobile crash, anti-whiplash technologies, and the design of energy absorbing structures. He has over 8 years of experience working on large scale impact simulations using LS-DYNA. Dr. Nilakantan's company is engaged in the development and marketing of DYNAFAB (www.nicomposites.com), an automated preprocessor that creates finite element models of 2D and 3D textile fabrics/composites. Dr. Nilakantan has one U.S. patent and one patent pending invention, authored over thirty five journal and conference articles, and won many awards for his work.
He is the owner of the LinkedIn group "A2E - Armor, Ballistics, Composites, and Defense Experts".

Specialties: Expert modeling and simulation using LS-DYNA, Ballistic impact of woven fabrics and composite structures, Experimental and statistical characterization of material properties, Occupant survivability during vehicular IED/mine blast, Energy absorbing seats for armored vehicles

2009 – Present (4 years)

Nilakantan Composites (www.nicomposites.com) is primarily engaged in the development and marketing of DYNAFAB, an automated finite element (FE) preprocessor to create FE models of 2D and 3D textile composites such as plain weave fabrics and unidirectional composites. DYNAFAB has a user friendly GUI that accepts geometrical data describing the fabric architecture, cross sections and undulations of the yarns/tows; and mesh scheme data that describes the density of hexahedral elements along the three axes. The output of DYNAFAB is an LS-DYNA (or Abaqus) keyword inputfile that is ready to use in a simulation, or can be opened / imported into other commercial preprocessors such as LSPrePost and Altair Hypermesh. DYNAFAB is capable of creating complex meshes of fabric architectures in only a few minutes. More details as well a picture gallery of some of the FE models created using DYNAFAB are available on the Nilakantan Composites website.
