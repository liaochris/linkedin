Dominique Dennery's Summary

I am a fluently bilingual consultant and coach who brings more than 25 years of experience supporting and engaging individual and group potential. Through my professional and personal experiences around the world, I have developed a deep understanding of human growth and development, group dynamics, and leadership. I focus on those who are overwhelmed or in need of guidance, and with their permission, help them unlock both their personal career potential and the potential of their group(s) in the workplace.

Mobilizing career potential and maximizing group potential are vital to my work and permeate the culture of my business—whether I’m working in the public or private sector, on a cross-functional team, or with a group of peers. Because every organization, group or individual is unique, I ensure that every session is tailored to my clients’ culture, norms and challenges. This includes addressing their core issues, fostering a safe, supportive environment for those involved, and effecting real, lasting change.

The delivery of these sessions is of the utmost importance. Drawing from my own diverse, cross-cultural experiences, I ensure that all individuals are treated with the respect, kindness and encouragement they deserve. Individual and group transformation is possible under the right circumstances. I strive to create these circumstances and facilitate the positive changes that are essential in building sustainable and productive organizations in today’s economy.

Please visit my website, www.dominiquedennery.com, to learn more about my approach, services and best practices for mobilizing career potential and maximizing group potential.

Dominique Dennery's Projects

  • Facilitation of Planning and Implementation of Strategies for the Subcommittee on Francophone Immigration in Ontario

    • March 2007 to March 2012
    Team Members: Dominique Dennery, Claire Paris, Eloïse Bellemont-Neef, Mireille Apollon

    The project involved the on-going coordination, design, facilitation and documentation of the meetings of the SubCommittee on Francophone Immigration in Ontario to promote the recruitment and economic integration of francophone immigrants in the labor market through stakeholder engagement.

    Dominique’s Role:

    Dominique Dennery was charged with facilitating the creation of the planning and implementation process that would lead to productive dialogue, consensus decision-making and the harmonization of efforts on the ground.

    Results and Additional Positive Developments:
    As a result of the project, the Subcommittee was able to:
    • mobilize existing knowledge in the fields of community governance and immigration;
    • facilitate the development of a strategy for the recruitment and economic integration of francophones in the labor market;

    • support the coordination of the implementation efforts through the planning and reporting activities of the 3 regional networks; and
    • support CIC in achieving cost savings through the leadership of stakeholders in leveraging their services and programs at the federal, provincial and local levels.

    The sub-committee was identified as a model and best practice of multi-stakeholder
    engagement by federal decision-makers accountable for immigration.

  • Leading a Successful change management initiative for the Office of the Commissioner of Official Languages

    • November 2011 to March 2012
    Team Members: Dominique Dennery, Lisa Anna Palmer

    Roles: Design and Facilitation

    Description:
    The Director of IM-IT of the Office of the Commissioner of Official Languages (OCOL) wanted to prepare a team of employees and managers for a major transition in their roles, responsibilities and work processes. This transition was compounded by the need to introduce new systems OCOL-wide, with no interruption to client service.

    The goal of this mission was to transform an ordinary team into an extraordinary team by focusing on its collective potential and making each member a change leader.

    We held a series of facilitated transition workshops were held over four months. Each experiential workshop was specially designed for two defined groups (employees and managers), and focused on each of the three transition phases defined by William Bridges. The goal was to facilitate interactions and ongoing dialogue between participants to help them manage their own internal transition process, while providing them with additional tools to facilitate the transition their clients would be experiencing once the IM-IT system changed.

    Results and Additional Positive Developments:
    • Team spirit and self-esteem were restored.
    • Client service improved.
    • Internal teamwork became more effective.
    • Use the tailor-made IM-IT organizational development initiative as a launching pad to develop an organizational-wide change management initiative.
    • Coordinate IM-IT implementation and the delivery of new HR programs and policies to support clients in a smooth transition.

    Ultimately the project ended with Dominique Dennery and the Office of the Commissioner of Official Languages were named recipients of a Gold award level at the Facilitation Impact Awards from the International Association of Facilitators.
     

  • Industry Canada – Developing a Respectful Workplace Model

    • April 2013 to Present
    Team Members: Dominique Dennery, Lauren Evans

    Role: Design and Facilitation

    Description:
    Developed a strategy through consultations and engagement of Industry Canada stakeholders in all branches and sectors on how to create a respectful workplace. Instead of focusing on what harassment is not, we emphasized instead what a respectful workplace is and how to get there. Engagement led to the development of a maturity model and implementation model. In order to accomplish this, we facilitated stakeholders meetings, and coached HR leaders in presenting to senior leaders and committees.

    Results and Additional Positive Developments:

    Staff members and senior leadership are excited about the maturity model and the implementation model and see the potential for it going viral. Members of the focus group felt that the model was useful, applied to their work and they could see its potential in helping to create a respectful workplace.

    Going forward we are focusing on implementing suggestions and recommendations that will enable the model to become part of the culture of Industry Canada, and used as a way for managers and employees to have conversations.


  • Health Canada– Middle Manager Engagement Strategy and Community Forum

    • November 2012 to February 2013
    Team Members: Dominique Dennery, Marilyn Domagalski

    Role: Design/facilitate a Bilingual Middle Management Engagement Strategy

    Description:

    Middle Managers were identified as key to the success of Branch transformation efforts that had begun the previous year. We Championed the development of an Engagement Strategy for the Middle Management (MM) Community to help accelerate implementation. The Strategy centered on the creation of a Design Team comprised of 25 MM from all directorates who were ready to get involved in the operationalization of the Branch Strategic Plan and Priorities. The first role of the Design Team was to support the design and delivery of a Community Forum with their 250 peers that would identify concrete ways of moving forward as a Branch.

    We helped design, plan and facilitate the Forum to engage the whole MM community in the implementation of the transformation agenda. With the support of a fellow consultant and writer, we used best practices in Change Management, Participatory Processes and the building of Communities of Practices to facilitate Design Team meetings, train and coach Design Team members, develop the Forum agenda and design the large group and small group processes. Electronic polling questions were developed to create baseline data that could be monitored moving forward. These questions assessed the Community’s awareness of the need for change, its desire to participate and support the change, its knowledge on how to change and its ability to implement required skills and behaviours.

    Results and Additional Positive Developments:
    The Forum was a resounding success. It involved MM Community members in all aspects of the organization, hosting, and facilitation of the event. The Forum also included the sharing of experiences, stories and best practices and innovations. The ideas put forward and gaps identified are being used to further develop the Engagement Strategy and create momentum for the implementation of the Strategic Plan and transformation agenda.
