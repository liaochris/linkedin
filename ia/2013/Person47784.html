Geoffrey Graham's Summary

I am a husband (2003), the father of a son (2004) and a daughter (2007), the founder of GuildQuality (2002), and a part-owner of the I'On Company (1998).

Prior to starting GuildQuality, I worked in the homebuilding and real estate development industries in the lowcountry of South Carolina (1996-2003). I’m also a partner in the ongoing development of an award-winning mixed-use traditional neighborhood development in Charleston, South Carolina.

I grew up in Atlanta and graduated from Davidson College. I now live in intown Atlanta. In my spare time, I enjoy hiking, running, movies, and reading.

Specialties

general management, project management, process creation, financial modeling, design, marketing strategy, sales, residential real estate development

Geoffrey Graham's Experience

Founder & CEO

GuildQuality

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Internet industry

April 2002 – Present (11 years 7 months) Atlanta, GA

While working with I'On's development and building team, I became interested in the characteristics common among great building companies. Through surveying our customers and our builders' customers, I observed that service quality was something that was both measurable and predictive of a company's success. That realization, and it's potential to improve the building profession, led me to start GuildQuality.

Today, the most accomplished residential real estate and construction companies in North America rely on GuildQuality's customer satisfaction surveying and performance reporting to help them monitor, improve, and articulate the quality of their work. Our mission is to elevate the stature of the building profession to a level commensurate with its importance.

Partner

The I'On Company

March 1998 – Present (15 years 8 months) Mount Pleasant, SC

Part owner of The I'On Company (www.ionvillage.com), a nationally acclaimed real estate development in Mount Pleasant, SC.

Project Manager

The I'On Company

April 1997 – April 2002 (5 years 1 month) Mount Pleasant, SC

During the first five years of I'On's development, I managed all construction in the community. That included five phases of land plan refinement, engineering, permitting, site work, and the development of a number of civic buildings, including the neighborhood's swim and tennis club and the creek club.

As a fun distraction from operations and execution, I designed several of the notable structures in the community: the dock pavilion, the boat house, the first postal pavilion, and my own home (which was featured as Coastal Living Magazine's "Idea House" in 1999).

I was instrumental in developing I'On's original design guidelines and review process, and served on the plan review committee for the community's first few years. I also recruited the builders that would form the neighborhood's building team (The I'On Guild) and created the guidelines governing ongoing participation. This was the first "Builders Guild" in community development.

While guiding the development of our built environment was my primary responsibility, I was heavily involved in sales, and worked with our marketing team, homeowners, and prospective purchasers on a daily basis.

General Manager

Broadstreet

January 1996 – March 1997 (1 year 3 months) Beaufort, SC

Oversaw land development, launched marketing, and managed design and construction of the first several homes in a 50 homesite, 11 acre infill neighborhood in Beaufort, SC.
