Donna Coxon-McCory's Summary

I have built my career where Technology, the Internet and Media converge. I am a mixture of Entrepreneur, Creative Strategist, Client Whisperer, Producer and Mentor. This combination allows me to own and lead execution on digital projects of all shapes and sizes for my client partners and deliver with a calm, focused demeanor and a dose of humor. Capable of sourcing, hiring and managing teams both locally and remotely, I seek out quality designers, developers and producers while build lasting relationships with them and often mentoring them in their own career path.

I am Founder and Producer at The FlashBrigade Collective and Revel-R Apps. I also have a passion for building community. I founded the creative tech communities Converge-SF and San Flashcisco. And I was a founding member of SF ArtsFest.

My current focus is creating unique digital experiences utilizing or emphasizing new Technology. I am interested in products/projects that use creative technology in a new way to effectively blow my mind.

Open to contract or permanent F/T positions.

Founder, Community Leader

Converge-SF

January 2012 – Present (1 year 9 months) San Francisco Bay Area

I kicked 2012 off with a new creative tech community and a broader vision when I settled on the new moniker 'Converge'. Ultimately, the best products and projects fuse art+tech or design+development. My Converge technical co-manager and our steering committee helped forge the details for this monthly event, allowing for multi-disciplinary mingling, music, mentorship and a platform for engaging people and teams to showcase their coolest digital works, all in a very non-threatening setting. I secured ongoing sponsorship with Onward Search and finally settled on a regular meeting space courtesy of WeWorks Labs. All events are live-streamed and recorded using Adobe Connect. I really enjoy putting on Converge-SF events, learning about great new technology and connecting people. As of today, our email list is about 700 Convergers strong.

Ted Patrick on Sencha Animator
Ray Villalobos and the Easel Framework
