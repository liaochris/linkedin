Software Design Engineer

Amazon.com

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; AMZN; Internet industry

March 2006 – January 2009 (2 years 11 months)

As an SDE at Amazon I first worked on the build team. We owned source control and a large home-grown build system with impressive characteristics for ensuring reliability, consistency, reproducibility, and change management of hundreds of millions of lines of code used by hundreds of teams with tens of thousands of edges in their dependency graph. We were also frequently the "go-to" team for ushering through large shifts across the code base, such as when we helped migrate a huge part of the stack from from Red Hat Enterprise Linux 3 to 64-bit Red Hat Enterprise Linux 4, on a tight deadline, in time for Q4 scaling, saving the company 20 million dollars in hardware costs due to performance improvements. We primarily used Perl and C++.

I later worked on the BASALT team, where I played a role as the technical lead on a project to replace the existing system of localization (string translation). I collected requirements, designed, and implemented the system using Java, working on a team with 3 others. The system was delivered to internal customers in January 2009.

Researcher

Purdue University

Educational Institution; 10,001+ employees; Higher Education industry

2005 – 2005 (less than a year) West Lafayette, IN

I worked as a researcher at Purdue's Rosen Center for Advanced Computing in the math building. There I helped build and maintain large compute clusters, mostly racking and wiring up new nodes. I helped build super compute clusters which appeared on the top-500 list at the time, as well as machines involved in the massive TeraGrid project. Clusters included Copper GigE, fiber optic, and the Proprietary Infiniband interconnects. I also did research on creating a "bootable cluster on a CD" based on the Knoppix Linux distribution.
