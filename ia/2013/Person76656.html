Dena Kouremetis' Summary

Behind every movie, book, magazine.newscast, play, advertisement, TV show, comedy routine, speech, web site or well-crafted executive profile is a writer. Today's business owners are called upon to be writers each and every day in the 24/7 cyber-world, many finding themselves ill-equipped or time-challenged in dealing with writing their own bios, blogging, newsletters and marketing emails.

Having been a professional journalist, copywriter, ghostwriter and freelance scribe since 1997, Dena Kouremetis considers it vital to help business owners as well as their employees to get a handle on their communications. In addition to being a professional writer, she offers workshops, coaching and keynote speaking to help you or your company become more adept at business writing.

Specialties: Kouremetis covers topics and creates web site content ranging from legalese to health, fitness and beauty, and from dentistry to real estate and design. She also loves to address business owners and groups about how to create their own "rock star" bios and online profiles.

She loves to get out from behind her Mac to speak to business groups on the topics of business blogging, effective email marketing tactics and newsletter content creation.

  • Project Yiayia: Tell Me Your Story

    Team Members: Dena Kouremetis, Calliope Iconomacou

    They are strong, they are beautiful and they are Greek.

    Famed Greek artist Calliope Iconomacou features Project Yiayia: Tell Me Your Story, a series of portraits of Greek mothers, daughters and grandmothers in native dress, representing the stock of women who left Greece and traveled to foreign lands to pursue more promising futures for themselves and for their families.

    For the global series exhibition of these stunning portraits, Iconomacou assigns names as well as stories to each “yiayia” (grandmother) – stories that may have been told countless times in an effort to pass on Hellenistic ideals and timeworn traditions to succeeding generations, lending both grace and meaning to their challenges, their joys and their legacies. Through their stories, these women become timeless symbols of the Greek diaspora.

    Combining the old and new worlds, Iconomacou presents Byzantine-like images while using digital technology to tell the tales of the hard-working women of the modern-day Diaspora. Each yiayia’s tale will be professionally narrated, telling stories of fear, hope, discovery, tribulation and most of all, love.

    Iconomacou Productions seeks stories from all over the world – stories that inspire, stories that entertain and stories that illustrate the path Greek immigrant women have taken throughout the past century. Do you have a story to tell, or do you know a Greek yiayia who does? While there is no guarantee it will be used in this exhibition, all submissions are both encouraged and welcomed. If not written, recordings and interviews will also be used to gather information. For more information, contact California-based project writer/ editor Dena Kouremetis at dena@communic8or.com.

  • Voices of Hellenism Project

    Team Members: Dena Kouremetis, Annamarie P. Buonocore, Betty Rozakis

    The Voices of Hellenism Project is a pending nonprofit literary journal and literary society for the Greek Communities of the San Francisco Bay Area and beyond. The journal encourages members of the Greek community to reflect on their rich histories and exciting personal experiences in a way that is creative and idiosyncratic. The project truly values diversity and multiple perspectives and moves away from the textbook-history approach of many other community organizations and history projects. As a nonprofit literary society, proceeds will benefit other Hellenic organizations and societies to create a friendship and partnership that will ultimately strengthen the Hellenic-American community in the Bay Area and elsewhere. The project works with Greek Americans through personal, targeted invitations, presentations, and online correspondence. We hope you will submit, volunteer, and get involved with the project soon.


    The mission of the Voices of Hellenism Project is to create a publication for the Greek communities of the Bay Area and beyond, to create a community project for every person to contribute to, to preserve history for future generations, and to disseminate Hellenic perspectives, histories, and traditions to the community and the general public.

    We envision an in-print publication, an online publication, a time capsule, and a historical DVD all connected through a nonprofit Hellenic literary society.
