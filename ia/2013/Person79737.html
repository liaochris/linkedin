Leela Srinivasan's Publications

  • The Employer Brand Playbook: Five Steps to Crafting a Highly Social Talent Brand

    • February 21, 2013
    Authors: Leela Srinivasan, Kate Reilly

    Our new e-book shares the 5 steps you need to build a highly social talent brand. Incorporating a mix of strategic and tactical guidance, this playbook is packed full of tips and best practices garnered from our customers and in-house experts.Topics covered include:

    - Why talent brand is the new employer brand
    - Tips on getting executive buy-in for the need to focus on your talent brand
    - How and why to listen to your talent audiences, internal and external
    - What metrics to track to show impact
    - Countless tips on how to promote your talent brand and engage your target audiences with it

    Download it, read it, and feel free to send me your comments and questions!

  • The Job-Seeking Status of the Fully-Employed

    • December 2010
    Authors: Leela Srinivasan, Lou Adler

    Worked with Lou Adler on this whitepaper, which sheds light on fully-employed professionals' attitudes to career opportunities and who's really in the active candidate pool. Provides compelling reasons why recruiters everywhere should be dialing up their resources to focus on passive candidate recruiting.

  • The State of Employer Branding

    • October 11, 2012
    Authors: Leela Srinivasan, Sohan Murthy, Kate Reilly

    A deeper look at one of the hottest topics in talent acquisition, based on survey responses of over 3,000 recruiting leaders in 15 countries. The report confirms how critical a strong employer brand is in attracting great talent, and that companies are prioritizing employer brand appropriately... but it also shows that there's a clear gap between organizational focus and strategy/measurement. And we all know that if you don't measure it, you can't manage it.

  • Top Down, Bottoms Up: Rethinking Corporate Recruiting

    • May 2011
    Authors: Leela Srinivasan, Lou Adler

    This whitepaper explains why we need to rethink corporate recruiting, and provides a couple of valuable frameworks to help get you there. By following Lou's guidance you can up-level your entire organization's recruiting efforts. Includes some data from March 2011 on hiring trends and what skills corporate recruiting leaders wish their teams had more of.
