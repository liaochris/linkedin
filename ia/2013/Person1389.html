Tellart

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Design industry

November 2011 – August 2012 (10 months)

For Google Chrome Web Lab, a yearlong exhibit visited by millions online and at The Science Museum, London, co-designed and implemented the Universal Orchestra, a unique mechanical musical ensemble playable in realtime collaboration by museum and online visitors.

Proposed and prototyped software innovations, including the physics behavior of the playing interface and functional solutions to the synchronization challenges involved in global, networked music collaboration.

Wrote 5k lines of production code in 9 languages, including: the HTML5 museum touch UI; a controller interfacing a remote WebSockets server and hardware actuators; a render farm that produces musically-precise custom videos for each visitor; a generative musical backing track; and attractive maintenance dashboards.
