Jim Ratcliffe's Summary

Author, design thinker, and educator, Jim loves empowering individuals and organizations to be design-centered and creatively confident.

Jim has:
• Led workshops using design thinking to promote creative confidence in Palo Alto, India, Bhutan, and Taiwan.
• More than 10 years experience in the publishing industry.
• Taught graduate-level classes on design and social entrepreneurship at Stanford University.

Combining ethnography, user experience research, lateral thinking, and rapid prototyping, design thinking is a methodology made popular by design firms such as IDEO and Stanford University’s d.school. At the d.school, Jim focused on developing international partnerships with design-minded schools and educational organizations throughout the world.

He has a Masters degree in Learning, Design and Technology from Stanford's School of Education. At Stanford, Jim designed a breath-controlled digital lamaze trainer for pregnant couples and a mobile-phone based projection system for the classrooms in the developing world. He also co-led Brainstorming India-a class that paired student teams with social entrepreneurs in India.

Jim’s skill set is strengthened by more than ten years experience as a publishing professional and has worked closely with writers and designers to craft high-quality publications on deadline.

A native of the Blue Ridge mountains, Jim has a lifelong passion for mountain cultures. A part-time inhabitant of the himalayas, Jim has hung his boots outside yurts, yak herder's huts, and homes throughout the himalayan range.

In his spare time, Jim is an amateur mycologist—he is often found rooting around the woods in bay area, hunting for edible mushrooms. He considers this one of the few pursuits where "freedom to fail" is not an advantage. Jim also holds the record for the world's highest altitude musical saw concert- 19,300 feet!

Jim Ratcliffe's Education

Stanford University

MA, Education

2007 – 2008

Jim graduated from the Learning, Design and Technology Master's Program in the School of Education at Stanford University in the Summer of 2008. It is a four quarter intensive master's program that focuses on developing scholars and innovators in the field of educational technology. The program features intensive coursework in education, design, and computer science as well as three internship quarters at bay area businesses or nonprofits. The program culminates in a Master's Project exposition.

Michigan State University

Educational Psychology and Educational Technology

2006 – 2007

Jim completed one year of doctoral work in the Educational Psychology and Educational Technology Ph.d program in the College of Education at Michigan State University. The program prepares graduates for scholarship and teaching in the study of human learning and development in varied educational settings and the study, creation, and use of diverse technologies supporting learning, design, teaching and communication. At MSU Jim was awarded Research Assistantships with MSU's Environmental Literacy Project and the Confucius Institute.

Activities and Societies: Research Assistantship with MSU's Environmental Literacy Project (NSF-funded), Research Assistantship with the Confucius Institute to design a Chinese language learning environment in Second Life, Presented keynote at 23rd Annual Educational Technology Conference of the College of Education-All Things Google: Thinking Across Software Modules, South Asia Panel Presentation at 2007 CIES Conference-The Ashokan Blog- Social Technologies in Indian Primary Education

Bates College

BA, Anthropology

1991 – 1995

Jim graduated from Bates College in the Spring of 1995 and was awarded a B.A. in Anthropology with a minor in South Asian Studies from the Department of Anthropology. Coursework focused on ethnographic field methods and critical approaches to the construction of ethnic and religious identities. His thesis was entitled "Contested Histories-Representations of Partition in Modern Indian and Pakistani TV Serials."

While at Bates, he also attended The School for International Training in Kathmandu, Nepal.
