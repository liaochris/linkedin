Chaz Mena's Summary

Chaz earned an MFA in Drama from Carnegie-Mellon University and a BA in English Literature from Barry University in South Florida. His one-person pieces on the lives of José Martí (1853-1895) Pedro Menéndez de Avilés (1513-1574), and Bernardo de Gálvez (1746-1786), travel the country at any given time. All were commissioned with a grant from the Florida Humanities Council and University of West Florida. He was recently awarded the John C. Pace Eminent Scholar Award from the University of West Florida for his cross-disciplinary work in History and Drama. PBS plans to air his one-person play, "Yo Solo: Bernardo de Gálvez on the Stage of the American Revolution" treating the life of that long-neglected Revolutionary War hero who wrested Florida from the British in 1781.

He is a hard-working actor having played in many theatres of national repute, off-Broadway, dozens of films and T.V. shows, and commercials.

Visit www.chazmena.com for more information. Along with his beloved wife and partner, Ileana Margarita, and his lovely daughter Anabela, he lives in Coral Gables, FL.
