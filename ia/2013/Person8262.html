Ann Douglas' Summary

I'm the creator of The Mother of All Book series, a bestselling series of pregnancy and parenting books. The lead title, The Mother of All Pregnancy Books, has sold 250,000 copies in North America while the series as a whole has sold over 500,000 copies in North America. The books have also been published in a variety of other markets (China, Greece, Indonesia) and excerpts of various titles have been adapted for use in online pregnancy and parenting courses.

I'm a highly versatile writer. I'm just as comfortable writing brochure copy as I am writing feature-length articles, radio scripts, online articles, or 800-page books. And while I specialize in writing about social issues, women's health, pregnancy, parenting, and motherhood, I have developed a knack for giving a fresh twist to evergreen stories on a variety of other health and lifestyle issues.

I'm known for sharing lively anecdotes and real-world advice -- and for being a radio or television guest who can be counted on to pinch-hit for a last-minute cancellation. (Hosting and producing two radio shows for Trent Radio has given me insights into what a radio or television producer looks for when booking a guest -- someone flexible, engaging, and hard-working. That's the kind of guest I aim to be.)

And I love developing workshops and keynote presentations that are tailored to meet the needs of a particular audience. A highlight for me in 2012 was working on behalf of The Writers' Union of Canada to deliver workshops on social media and online publicity in Vancouver, Victoria, Calgary, Winnipeg, Toronto, Ottawa, Kingston, Montreal, and Halifax, as well as via webinar.

If you are an editor, producer, meeting planner, creative director, or someone else I might be able to assist, either now or down the road, I'd love to hear from you. (And if you're someone I've worked with in the past, I'd love to reconnect.)

- Ann

Ann Douglas' Publications

  • The Mother of All Pregnancy Books

    • John Wiley and Sons Publishing Inc
    • July 3, 2012
    Authors: Ann Douglas

    250,000 copies sold. Unlike those other bossy, tell-you-what-to-do pregnancy books, this funny, entertaining guide presents expectant parents with all the facts they need to know about conception, birth, and everything in between. Celebrating the 10th anniversary of this favorite guide to pregnancy and birth, this new edition is easier-to-use, fully updated, and has a fresh and appealing new design.

    The Mother of All Pregnancy Books features a friendly, fresh, and fun approach to the greatest adventure life has to offer. Based on the best advice from over 100 parents and a panel of experts, it is packed with tons of nuts-and-bolts information and presents expectant parents with all the facts on such perennial hot topics as pain relief during labor, episiotomy, and circumcision, and empowering expectant parents to make informed personal choices.

  • The Mother of All Pregnancy Books: An All-Canadian Guide to Conception, Birth, and Everything In-Between

    • John Wiley and Sons Inc.
    • May 2011
    Authors: Ann Douglas

    If you're looking for the inside scoop on what it's really like to have a baby, you've come to the right place. This comprehensive guide features a friendly, fresh, and fun approach to the greatest adventure life has to offer. Based on the best advice from over 100 Canadian parents and panel of experts, this information is served up with a uniquely Canadian spin. The Mother of All Pregnancy Books is funny, entertaining, and packed with tons of nuts-and-bolts information and presents expectant parents with all the facts on such perennial hot topics as pain relief during labor, episiotomy, and circumcision, empowering them to make informed personal choices.
    Unique and innovative, this book is packed with helpful tools readers won't find anywhere else, including charts highlighting the risks of using various over-the-counter drug products during pregnancy, tables summarizing the functions of the 10 most important nutrients during pregnancy, lists of the 10 best-and worst-baby products, and much, much more. Featuring a glossary of pregnancy-related terms, a sample birth plan and a set of emergency childbirth procedures, The Mother of All Pregnancy Books isn't afraid to tackle difficult subjects, like infertility, high-risk pregnancy, and pregnancy and infant loss -- topics that most other pregnancy books shy away from.

    Authors: Ann Douglas
