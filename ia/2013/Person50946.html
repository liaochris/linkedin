Nemo Mermer's Summary

Hello! I’m a software engineer, professional since Y2K. My biggest passion is to build user interfaces that are as simple as the nature itself, intuitive, efficient, unobtrusive and enabling.

I was first introduced to programming in 1989, through a Commodore 64 and a BASIC manual for kids. In 1999, I was hired as a front-end developer and got paid for the first time.

Today, I enjoy building user experiences for a living and I specialise in interface engineering, specifically on the web.

Details are very important to me and I care deeply about usability. I also tend to take code quality very seriously. I believe that a well-maintained, high-quality codebase is a minimum requirement for any project – not a luxury.

Previously, I've had the chance to work on projects for well-known labels – Nike, Asics, Sony, Volkswagen, Bing, Currys, Kingfisher, Cadbury, GT1, Rizla, Bank of Scotland, zeebox – to name a few.

I've been working with JavaScript since 1996 and it's still the language I work with the most on a daily basis. I adore SASS.

In my spare time, you can find me fiddling around with Processing, Node.js, working on my big-history project or rebuilding a part of one of my robots.
