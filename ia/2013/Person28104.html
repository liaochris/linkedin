Chris Serico's Summary

An award-winning entertainment reporter and analyst, Chris Serico has deconstructed pop culture on national TV, interviewed A-listers, and written offbeat commentary that’s been highlighted by The New York Times and "Late Night with Jimmy Fallon."

As a full-time reporter for Newsday and weekly on-air contributor to News12 Westchester, Serico covered celebrities, breaking news, music, movies, TV, comedy, pop culture and red carpets in 2012-13. Prior to joining Newsday, his 11 years as a reporter at The Journal News allowed him to provide entertainment analysis for TV Guide Network and RNN-TV; co-host a TV-themed podcast; post hundreds of updates to that paper's entertainment blogs; produce and edit nightlife content for Metromix; pen weekly sports-humor columns; and be honored by the New York Associated Press.

He's interviewed an eclectic set of celebrities, including directors J.J. Abrams and John Waters; actors Susan Sarandon, Glenn Close, Mandy Patinkin, Patricia Clarkson and Rooney Mara; comedians Joan Rivers, Chevy Chase, Jerry Stiller, Bobby Moynihan, Artie Lange, George Lopez, Aziz Ansari and Amy Schumer; and musicians Elvis Costello, Yoko Ono, Brian Wilson, Daryl Hall, Paul Shaffer, "Weird Al" Yankovic, Ani DiFranco and Buddy Guy.

The New York Times, "Late Night," Entertainment Weekly, Comedy Central, BuzzFeed, The Huffington Post, Gawker, SiriusXM, Best Week Ever, Cracked, Splitsider and the Buffalo Bills are among those to cite Serico's exclusive interviews, pointed commentary and general silliness.

Serico's the creator of the social-media-skewering @EveryTweet_Ever (42,000+ Twitter followers) and Pop On The Clock, his irreverent pop-culture podcast featuring entertainers and entertainment experts.

In addition to co-founding Unsung Heroes — a vocal band recruited for NBC's "The Sing-Off" — Serico has performed comedy at the Upright Citizens Brigade Theatre and Gotham in New York City, and provided voice-overs for video games, Gannett and other clients.
