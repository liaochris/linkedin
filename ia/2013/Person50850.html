Guillaume Thoreau's Summary

For the last 18 years, Guillaume has been devising User Experience designs, architecture and strategies for a variety of products, including world-class gaming products on mobile, tablet and Web, professional software, Web 2.0 apps, TV interfaces, large E-commerce and service portals.

Guillaume began his career in France, where he was born, as a visual designer for interactive and print products. He quickly developed a passion for an emerging discipline: User Experience design. He worked in various companies from Toulon, Bordeaux and Paris.

Arriving in Montreal in 2005, Guillaume joined LVL Studio as lead UX designer on various TV and Web project for Canadian telco TELUS.

In 2006, Guillaume founded his own UX Architect studio: Thoreau Interface Design. The studio quickly distinguishes itself as an important player in the burgeoning Montreal startup scene.

In 2010, Guillaume was recruited by EA as senior UX architect at POGO.com, then he joined EA Mobile as UX Design Manager. Guillaume was responsible for everything UX in mobile and social games. Guillaume built a centralized UX team able to service multiple game teams (including hit franchises like SCRABBLE, TETRIS, and World Series of Poker). The UX team contributed in promoting the quick adoption of Agile by embedding quick iterations of wireframes, prototypes and tests into the sprint cycles (lean UX).

Guillaume is also a founding member of UXMTL, an organization that aims to synchronize UX practices with the needs and realities of the Montreal market.
