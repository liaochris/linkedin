Jennie Levine Knies' Experience

Manager, Digital Stewardship

University of Maryland, College Park

Educational Institution; 10,001+ employees; Higher Education industry

January 2012 – Present (1 year 6 months)

Digital Stewardship (DS) is the primary conduit through which all University of Maryland (UMD) Libraries’ staff communicates their ideas regarding projects with a primarily digital focus. Digital Stewardship encourages creative thinking among UMD Libraries’ staff with regard to digital initiatives. By acting as a clearinghouse, Digital Stewardship serves as a central location that can compile and compare ideas across the UMD Libraries, help to create partnerships, and endeavor to endorse and create tools and services with a wide appeal and applicability. Digital Stewardship’s primary function is as a data manager and project manager, developing, in collaboration and communication with other UMD Libraries divisions, plans and roadmaps for digital initiatives within the UMD Libraries. Once Library-wide decisions are made on priorities relating to digital initiatives, Digital Stewardship will launch and manage projects to ensure that those priorities are realized. While Digital Stewardship fills a project management role within the UMD Libraries, several large digital initiatives live permanently within the Digital Stewardship unit, including Digital Collections, the Digital Repository at the University of Maryland, the Research Data initiative, the ePublishing initiative, and digital preservation policy.

The head of DS is the first point of contact for all new digital projects and digital initiatives and works with all stakeholders to develop project charters and project plans. It is the role of the head of DS to keep current on technology trends, to identify possibilities for collaboration and partnership within the UMD Libraries and externally, and to be proactive in ensuring that the UMD Libraries remain current and relevant with their digital repository and digital systems. In addition, the head of DS serves as a public face for digital initiatives.

Manager, Digital Collections

University of Maryland, College Park

Educational Institution; 10,001+ employees; Higher Education industry

February 2008 – December 2011 (3 years 11 months)

Under the direction of the Dean of the Libraries, manage and administer projects in the Office of Digital Collections and Research (DCR). Responsibilities include creating specifications for creating digital objects in the Fedora digital asset management system; providing mock-ups and specifications for public interface; creating and maintaining documentation; testing implementations; and public relations and outreach relating to projects. Together with the technical co-manager, supervise DCR librarians, technical staff, and graduate assistants. Collaborate with the Technical Services Division (TSD) to create metadata for digital objects. Oversee the digitization and description of image and text content for particular projects. Explore opportunities for new digital projects. Serve on Library-wide teams and committees.

Manager ("Webmistress")

The Sugar Quill

January 2001 – September 2008 (7 years 9 months)

The Sugar Quill is a website whose main goals are to improve and encourage creative writing through the use of "fan fiction" based on J. K. Rowling's Harry Potter book series. I co-conceived and created this website, and my role has included design, forum moderation, story "beta-reading" (editing), and administration. My involvement in this website has greatly contributed to my understanding of web design, online communities and behaviors, and emerging technologies. I still own the domain and the site, although the forums officially closed in 2008.

Curator for Historical Manuscripts

University of Maryland, College Park

Educational Institution; 10,001+ employees; Higher Education industry

July 2000 – February 2008 (7 years 8 months)

Curatorial responsibility for archival and manuscript holdings relating primarily to the history of the Maryland region; labor union archives; women's history collections; and faculty papers.

Special projects included the design of and user testing on an online guide, "Research Using Primary Sources" (with Joanne Archer and Ann Hanlon); development of a database in Microsoft Access to track digital images; and the development and ongoing maintenance of "ArchivesUM" (http://www.lib.umd.edu/archivesum), which provides online access to collection descriptions, finding aids, and resource guides for special collections the University of Maryland Libraries through the use of Encoded Archival Description (EAD).

Vice-Chair

Mid-Atlantic Regional Archives Conference

1996 – 2008 (12 years)

The primary role of the MARAC vice chair is to obtain hotel contracts for the twice-yearly meetings and to coordinate the work of the Local Arrangements and Program Committees for every conference. The vice chair is the ex-officio chair of the Meetings Coordinating Committee.

Director of Operations

Integrated Systems Research Corporation

July 1997 – July 2000 (3 years 1 month)

Integrated Systems Research Corporation (ISR) is a technology company that specializes in providing Global Positioning Satellite (GPS)-based mapping and tracking products for use in both the government and the commercial sectors. Responsible for quality testing of mapping software and relational database applications. Trained clients on GPS systems, provided technical support, and facilitated hardware installations. Attended conferences and trade shows and assisted in development of marketing materials. Designed company's first website.

Western Fellow

Open Society Archives

June 1996 – June 1997 (1 year 1 month)

The Open Society Archives (OSA) is an archives and a center for research and education that is part of the Central European University in Budapest, Hungary. Its collections and activities relate to the Cold War, the history of former communist countries, human rights, and war crimes. Curatorial responsibility for two research collections from the former Research Institute of Radio Free Europe/Radio Liberty: the Soviet "Red" Archives and the Soviet Samizdat Archives. Prepared materials for relocation, served on the reference desk, assisted with exhibits and special events, and created bilingual finding aids (Russian and English). Developed an understanding of international archival practices. Served as guest lecturer on archival topics to students participating in the Summer Archives Institute at the Central European University
