Ethan Hein's Summary

I'm a masters candidate in NYU's Music Technology program. For my thesis, I'm developing a software tool for self-guided music education. Read more about it here: http://www.ethanhein.com/wp/2013/the-radial-drum-machine-background-and-inspiration/ I expect to graduate in December 2013, at which point I'll be seeking a product design position with a music software company.

My coursework at NYU includes composition, film music and audio editing, music production and recording and software programming. I work as a recording engineer in the state-of-the-art James L. Dolan studio, and have also taught music production for NYU's IMPACT program.

Specialties: Music composition, performance, production and teaching; writing, research and editing; software product management; internet marketing and public relations; web and graphic design.

    This past November, I had the privilege of performing in the fourth Disquiet Junto concert of 2012 in the apexart gallery.

    We each did two pieces, one connected to the apexart exhibit “As Real As It Gets,” organized by Rob Walker. The other musicians performing were Arcka (Shawn Kelly), Brian Biggs, Kenneth Kirschner, Tom Moody, and Roddy Schrock with Joon Oluchi Lee.

    The project for the evening involved making music made from the sound of field recordings of large-scale retail spaces, and that music was intended to approximate the mechanistic fury inherent in Zola’s novel Au Bonheur des Dames(1883), which was a touchstone for the overall exhibit.
