Chris Landau's Summary

Chris Landau is an artist and designer who creates organic, abstract systems which resemble both the city and the cell. Using parametric modeling, animation and digital fabrication, as well as traditional approaches like drawing and printmaking, he explores themes of decay and regeneration.

Landau currently works for OLIN, a Philadelphia-based landscape architecture and urban design firm. Over the past four years at OLIN he has explored ways to integrate 3D approaches into OLIN's traditional design process. Recently this has included parametric design and 3D prototyping. In his studio Landau is creating laser cut woodblock prints and etchings of his digital creations, cut at NextFab and editioned at Second State Press. His increasing interest in the life of cities has led him to participate in Philly Works and Design Philadelphia. Landau was also recently part of Mural Arts and Breadboard "muraLAB" artist-in-residence where he utilized the facilities of NextFab studio to create a proposal for an urban public artwork. 

Landau has a BFA in printmaking and received his MFA in Interdisciplinary Art and Design from The University of Michigan. He has shown in several exhibitions including Emergence at Bahdeebahdu Gallery in Philadelphia and From Here to Infinity and The Big Bang at Spaces Gallery in Cleveland, OH. Landau also exhibited in and co-curated Endless Forms: Engaging Evolution at Work Gallery in Ann Arbor, MI. Chris has also lectured and exhibited in Beijing, China in the Tsing Hua University Design Department. In Philadelphia Landau co-founded and was lead designer of N Formation Design, a company creating 3D, interactive, and visually rich web content for museums, education, and the life sciences.

Chris Landau's Education

University of Michigan

Master of Fine Art, Interdisciplinary Art and Design

2003 – 2006

My Thesis was a creative speculation of nature and technology in the form of a online digital narrative (www.theflockingparty.com). This cautionary tale encompasses drawing and new media in a wholey new form that is a premonition of Bird Flu and the iPad Era.

The 3 year Masters of Fine Art program in the Department of Art and Design was supposed to have been a long time (usually these programs are 2). But I did a lot and learned more than I can even say in that time that was over far too quick. A highlight of my time there was working in GROCS on an interactive informatics project. that's where I met my current business partner. Another important experience was going to Beijing, China for 5 weeks, where I made a great friend and assisted with the installation of an International New Media Exhibition. Last, check out my job under "Grdauate Student Instructor", which will elucidate another important experience aside from the research and creative work I was doing while I was there.
