Deborah Lee Marlow's Summary

Deborah Lee Marlow is an internationally recognized, Award winning Serial Entrepreneur, Non-Exec Company Director, EIR and popular Motivational Speaker who’s brought her business-building and corporate governance ‘know-how’ to New Zealand.

As a teenage single-mom Deborah co-launched her first business; a reforestation co-op in the Pacific Northwest USA growing it to 200+ members, it stayed profitable for 22 years. She planted over 1-million trees and as an initial attack fire-fighter fought in 175 campaign forest-fires.

When her knees gave out, she grew Award Software International Inc. from a Silicon Valley start-up into the world's 3rd largest systems software house. Today over 300 million PCs use the Award Software BIOS. As the CEO & Director of a wholly owned company within ‘First-e Bank’ (ENBA group), one of Europe’s first online banks, Deborah was instrumental in garnering over 200,000 customers, 281 employees and helping raise £381mio investment capital.

Deborah was the commencement speaker for the University of Oregon’s graduating class of Computer Scientists & winner of the 2006 O2 Ability Awards for Leadership in Ireland. She launched a popular call-in radio show in Ireland, combined with public speaking & business panel engagements her voice became nationally recognized as “The Business Whisperer’. She’s a self-taught entrepreneur who typically 'boot-straps' her businesses and believes that “growth thru sales” is the way to build a successful company. “Happiness is a Positive Cash-flow”. A sign on the wall of her office simply says: "Nothing happens until somebody sells something!" No sales? No Business! She combines a solid no-nonsense style of sagacious management experience with frankness and (lots 'n lots of) good humour. Her interest leans toward women & minority owned start-ups but any smart business with equally smart people at the helm can grab her attention. She actively supports organizations involved in ending violence against women.

Specialties

Motivational Speaking, Sales, Corporate Governance, Negotiations, Sales training, Writing fund-able Business Plans, Managing Start-ups, Creative Problem Solving, Raising Capital, Sensible Board of Director member, Skilled at keeping a Good Sense of Humour during difficult moments, Fearless Dealmaker, Effortless Public Speaker.

Deborah Lee Marlow's Experience

Chapter Chair New Zealand

Women Presidents Organization

Nonprofit; 11-50 employees; Nonprofit Organization Management industry

March 2013 – Present (7 months) Wellington, New Zealand

Deborah is the NZ Chapter Chair of the WPO; a non-profit membership organization for women Presidents, CEO's & Managing Directors of multimillion-dollar companies. The members of the WPO take part in professionally-facilitated peer advisory groups in order to bring the 'genius out of the group' and accelerate the growth of their businesses.

MISSION: Women Presidents’ Organization, Inc. is formed to improve business conditions for global women entrepreneurs and to promote the acceptance and advancement of women entrepreneurs in all industries. There are currently 101 chapters in the United States, Canada, United Kingdom, Peru, Turkey, Mexico, South Africa & New Zealand. Chapters are moderated by professionally-trained facilitators, all of whom own their own businesses. Each chapter serves as a peer advisory group based on the 4 C’s of WPO: Collaboration, Confidentiality, Commitment and Connections. WPO members address both strategic and operational issues focusing on financial concerns, organizational development, hiring and firing and other issues defined by chapter members, to drive their businesses to the next level.

WHO: More than 1,600 accomplished women entrepreneurs at the multimillion-dollar level who aim to increase their business success.

MEMBERSHIP REQUIREMENT: $2mio in gross annual sales or $1mio for service-based business; ownership at some level and senior management responsibilities for attorneys/accountants

INTERESTING MEMBERSHIP STATISTICS:

* 3% of women-owned businesses gross over a million a year and qualify for WPO membership.
* 70 % of WPO members do business with each other.
* 11.5 % of its members pay themselves a salary of more than $300,000 a year.
* WPO members collectively generate $17 billion in annual revenue and represent about 28,000
years of collective business experience.
