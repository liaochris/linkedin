Filiz Efe's Summary

Offering a unique combination of innovative creative vision, technical savvy, and solid professional grounding in strategic communications, Filiz excels at distilling complex projects and events into engaging narratives. Her work ranges from professional development videos focusing on the demonstration of academic techniques and practices, to public information videos that introduce a project or activity to a specific audience or convey a message to the community at large. She can work in a fast-paced journalistic environment as she did with her daily video coverage of the 2012 Consumer Electronics Show and her recent work with the international consortium LINGOs in Guatemala. She is also a very capable long-term visual storyteller and researcher as evident with her work on the Knowledge in Action Project for the College of Education, UW.

During her graduate studies, Filiz interned with Pacific Science Center and the UW Media Relations and Communications Department where she created a storytelling website initiative and took a lead role in creating media for the UW YouTube Channel. Recognized by her superiors and the media community at large, she won a 2011 Fund Raising and Alumni Relations Videos CASE District VIII Communication Award. Post graduation, Filiz has maintained her ties to the UW MCDM by repeatedly serving both as an Adjunct Instructor for the Advanced Multimedia Storytelling course and as a Multimedia Producer.

Boasting an international education that broadens her skillset as well as her worldview, Filiz holds a Bachelor of Fine Arts in Cinema-TV (1996) from Dokuz Eylul University in Izmir, Turkey; a Master of Arts in Communication Strategies and Public Relations (2008) from Galatasaray University in Istanbul, as well as a Master of Communication in Digital Media (2010) from the University of Washington in Seattle.

Specialties

Video Production, Strategic Communications, Multimedia Storytelling

    • May 2012 to May 2012
    Team Members: Filiz Efe, Marisa Nickle

    UW faculty use lecture-capture for both practical and pedagogical reasons—to maintain course continuity when classes can’t meet, to make lectures accessible for remote or absent students, or to make complete recordings available to students for review lectures after class, a technique that students find helpful. Some faculty create stand-alone lectures for students to watch at home, freeing up in-person class time for more interactive and engaging activities.   • Introducing Canvas: UW's preferred course management system

    • October 2012 to February 2013
    Team Members: Filiz Efe, Marisa Nickle

    The University of Washington is moving to a uniform, system-wide learning management system (LMS) called Canvas. Students asked for and expect a single platform, while faculty appreciate features that save time and support grading, feedback, and interaction, such as online office hours. We chose Canvas after conducting a pilot, which found that students and faculty preferred Canvas over other LMSs such as Moodle and Blackboard. Students liked the modern look and feel, faculty said it was easy to use, and administrators like that it’s free to their units through UW-IT.
