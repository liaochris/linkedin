John R Daley's Projects

  • Top Line Growth: How we use digital technologies to help the Daley Group gain customer insights, build brand awareness and top line revenue

    • January 2007 to Present
    Team Members: John R Daley, Robert Kiehl, Laura Wiley

    After 2008 it became clear we had to increase our brand awareness. We had to identify new clients around the world, gain client insights and increase client engagement though shorter sales cycles, reduced marketing cost and increased closing percentages.

    Situation:
    2008 was a life changing event for business and their markets including the Daley Group, LLC. Capital was stressed and cost cutting was in order to survive. Risk and survival were the two orders of the day. Those two things make being successful at consulting difficult. So how do we stay in touch with our market and current with their needs?

    Action:
    We started to study social media to define ourselves, develop our brand to build and maintain a relationship with our markets. LinkedIn allows us to connect and stay in touch with our market. Between our LinkedIn business page and our individual profiles we have defined our brand. LinkedIn also allows us to stay in touch with them as individuals and get to know them professionally. Facebook seems to be diminishing but does allow us to get to know our market on a personal level when appropriate. We work hard to provide content of interest for clients and respect their time. Our clients can opt out of our market at any time and we appreciate their staying with us.
    We have grown our database from a loose 1500 to a focused 7000. We have a global reach with diverse industries that we appreciate. Our team of experts share their knowledge and experience and learn from our market. We know we do not know what we don’t know and we are fine with that.

    Result:
    We do not make cold calls, we have colleagues that know us and work with us. We have shortened our sales cycle and increased our close rate. We now teach this to our clients so they can take advantage of today’s digital technologies to increase market penetration at a lower cost. We have grown sales from 0 to over $300,000.00 for one start up service client in less than two years.

  • Grow sales of a 1 billion $ Life Sciences Company from 1% to 8%

    • 2013 to Present
    Team Members: Bob Gilot, John R Daley, Mike Gray, Marcia Mednick

    Three-quarters of US companies are prioritizing innovation, with growth as their top objective. Is your company among them

    Situation:
    There were 4 sales forces all with their own management team and goals. Each sales force was primarily made up of bench scientists. Growth was at about 1% of this billion dollar life sciences company.

    Action:
    We combined all sales teams globally under one management team. We built the College of Sales and Science with over 160 courses designed and built internally with the help of marketing and R&D. We build and facilitated a career path that encouraged the sales team members to move up in their career ladder. The global team was required to take online courses, workshops and working with their managers to grow their sales and technical skills in order to move up. We designed and built a online transcript that was well received. Sales goal success was built into the ability to move up the ladder. The sales team actually pushed the management to help them improve. We built and facilitated our own on boarding process for sales reps and management. We also designed and built a private Team Sites where teammates globally could share competitive information with each other, Marketing and R&D.

    Result:
    We actually started sourcing sales team talent from competition due to the College of Sales and Science and the career ladder. Our sales growth went from 1% to 8% in about 2 years.

  • Dealing with a new era of threats.

    Team Members: John R Daley

    Private companies are rethinking their approach to risk against a backdrop of globalization, economic realignment, and rapid technological change.

    Situation:
    We routinely see companies that are risk averse but need to grow. Market’s are not growing, so growth is at the expense of your company or competition. They know they must spend and take on risk but are adverse to failing. Global events are having a direct financial effect on private companies such as: 92% cited recession/weak global economy; 74% cited rising costs; 68%, unstable capital markets, heightened competition was cited by 68%, increased taxation to address public deficit, 64% and over regulation was cited by 63%. 47% of companies see talent shortages as a key risk and 41% worry about information security breaches(PWC, 2012) Making no change at all may be the biggest risk to organizations.

    Action:
    To minimize the risk and give our clients confidence we perform a thorough evaluation of the risk inside the company, the supply chain, the customers and the competition. We provide "new eyes" for our clients . During that process it is crucial to identify and evaluate risk and opportunity. As part of the strategic plan we mitigate risk and identify opportunities and minimize total cost. Our team includes subject matter experts in Sales, Operations, Finance, M&A, Organizational Development and Talent Development. Our global perspective gives our clients a competitive edge, we have successfully grown sales cost effectively.

    Result:
    A multi-billion dollar company increased growth from 1% to 8%. A 2 million dollar company grew to 6 million. Usually we see at least a 10% growth.

    Situation:
