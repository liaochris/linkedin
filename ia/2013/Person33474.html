Deborah Hartmann Preuss' Summary

My goal is to bring more joy into the workplace ... and all of life!

What is important to you? And what's keeping you from hitting high gear? Have you got an opportunity you'd really like to launch into? Or a stumbling block that keeps tripping you up? I offer you a safe space in which to design shifts that bring your life more in line with your strengths and passions ... as well as accountability along the path you choose.

I energetically support individuals and teams to get better outcomes, to reflect on their values and goals and make adjustments to produce more of what matters. This coaching takes place on the phone and in person. I am also happy to teach these skills, at unconferences and in public and private workshops.

Prior to my move into pure work-life coaching, I worked as an agile trainer, coach, team lead and analyst, helping to address teams' most pervasive problems, collaborating with management and team members to apply and improve practices such as Lean Thinking, Scrum, Extreme Programming, Action Meetings, Agile Work, as well as the Open Space un-conference methodology.

In the past I have worked with both traditional software lifecycle methods and Agile approaches: Scrum, RUP, FDD (Feature Driven Development), XP and Test-Driven Development.

Since 2009, following my marriage and a move to Germany, I work around Europe and occasionally travel to the US and Canada.

Specialties: Professional Co-Active Work-Life Coach, Newfield "Coaching in Effectiveness", Training/Coaching in Agile and Lean Software Process Improvement, Preparing and Facilitating Open Space / BarCamp-style unconferences, Excellent Analytical Skills, Broad Business Domain Knowledge, Object Oriented Analysis and Design for Java, Data Modeling, Scrum Agile Project Management Methodology.

Deborah Hartmann Preuss' Education

The Coaches Training Institute

Co-Active Certified Professional Coach

2007 – 2013

Grade: CPCC

The Co-Active Coach Training Program is one of the oldest and best respected in the international coaching profession. The program consists of five in-person experiential workshops (the Intermediate program, 103 hours), followed by a six month virtual Certification program (92 classroom hours, 100 coaching hours, 20 hours supervision).

Upon completion, CPCC recipients are immediately also eligible for International Coach Federation PCC status.

The Co-Active model views the client as creative, resourceful and whole, and focuses on evoking transformation of the whole person to create a more effective and satisfied (work) life.

Activities and Societies: 2007-2009 Coaching Intermediate Program, 2012-2013 Professional Coach Program, 2012 Completed Written Exam, 2013 Practical Exam

Agile Coach Training Institute

The Coaching Stance, (assistant trainer)

2012 – 2012

Grade: Teaching Assistant

Designed as a collaboration between masterful Agile, Co-Active and ORSC coaches, the Coaching Stance is meant for ScrumMasters, agile coaches, project managers in transition to agile and managers of agile teams.

Day One: learn and practice basic skills of the work-life coaching profession: deep listening, powerful questions, articulating what’s going on, acknowledgement, clearing, self-management, values work, designed alliance and more.

Day Two: explore how these pure coaching skills help in applied coaching situations of the agile coach, and creation of a personal plan for further development.

Includes:
* anatomy of a coaching session
* clear requests and accountability
* agile coach competencies framework
* dealing with complaints
* resistance and strong emotions
* reframing to move from being stuck
