Dr. Charlie Motzko's Summary

Dr. Motzko is Senior Partner in C. A. Motzko & Associates. His consultancy is built on the premise that the real challenge going forward is the training and education of technicians, engineers, managers and executives for success in implementing the Total Test Management Process.
As a State of California Registered Professional Engineer in Quality, Dr. Motzko helped shape the direction of test equipment management during the industry's critical early years. Dr. Motzko began to concentrate his work in test equipment management starting in the early 1980s when, as Vice President of ElectroRent’s Equipment Information and Management Group, he helped develop the company's strategic information system to support the full spectrum of product management and delivered services. This integrated system presented a compelling vision of the enormous opportunity and sustainable benefits available in the proper employment of logistics, service and support of test equipment.

Upon receiving his PhD, Dr. Motzko started a second career in education, as adjunct faculty, by teaching Quality, Management, Human Resources, and Logistics at the graduate and under-graduate level. In his curriculum, Dr. Motzko incorporates "Lessons Learned" over the past four decades of dealing in management. Dr. Motzko's instructional philosophy is focused on student engagement.

Specialties: We offer metrology consulting, design and implementation of our software-based metrology, calibration and test equipment management solutions. We focus on meeting regulatory compliance and internal audits requirements.

We primarily service the technology-based communities such as the aerospace, defense, manufacturing, communications, electronics, life sciences and healthcare.

  • Evolution of NCSLI Recommend Practice: RP-5 – Equipment Specifications

    • Presented and Published in the Proceedings of the 2011 Measurement Science Conference (MSC).
    • March 2011
    Authors: Dr. Charlie Motzko

    Presented and Published in the Proceedings of the 2011 Measurement Science Conference (MSC).
    Manufacturer specifications are an important element of cost and quality control for testing, calibration and other measurement processes. They are used for equipment selection or establishing equipment substitutions for a given measurement application. In addition, manufacturer specified tolerances are used to compute accuracy ratios and estimate bias uncertainties. Ideally, measuring or test equipment is calibrated periodically to determine if they are performing within manufacturer specified tolerance limits. In fact, the elapsed-time or interval between calibrations is often based on in-tolerance or out-of-tolerance data. Therefore, it is important that manufacturer specifications are properly interpreted and applied. Specifically how manufacturer specifications are obtained, interpreted, and used to assess instrument performance, reliability and selection. The session will open with a paper that discusses the evolution of the NCSLI RP-5: Measurement and Test Equipment Specifications. RP5 was submitted to the editorial review committee in September 2010, and the current status of that process will be covered as part of this presentation. Following the paper presentation, the panel consisting of review committee representatives will discuss the process, procedures and outcomes of the formal editorial review of the 200 Page Recommended Practice. The panel will then outline and start the process for acceptance of public comment on RP-5. Presenter and Moderator: Dr. Charles A. Motzko, P.E., RP-5 Chairperson Panel Members: RP-5 Writing and Editorial Committee Members

  • Social Media and Metrology Organizations: Why?

    • Presented and Published in the Proceedings of the 2010 NCSL International Workshop and Symposium.
    • 2010
    Authors: Dr. Charlie Motzko, Patti Hill


    This paper will provide a high level survey of the phenomena designated "social media" and how social media will affect the metrology community. The questions these endeavors need to answer are; "what is social media and how best to integrate this new phenomenon into the organizational strategy" and "what are the possible benefits?" An excellent example of an early adaptation is the National Aeronautics and Space Administration (NASA) use of social media. One of the primary elements of the NASA social media initiative was developing a comprehensive social media plan and strategy for implementation and control. NASA's approach started with what social media elements worked towards fulfillment of the overall NASA strategy, while optimizing the time, resources, and effort involved. The end result was that NASA's activities in social media was recognized in February, 2009, when the agency received the "Shorty Twitter Award" as the best producer of short content on Twitter during 2008. This paper will provide a synopsis of the professionally focused social media continuum by providing an outline for implementation, including resource links.

  • The Benefits of Automated RF & Microwave Test for Calibration, Compliance, and Customer Satisfaction

    • Cal Lab, The International Journal of Metrology, Vol. 15, No. 3. pp. 45-49.
    • 2009
    Authors: Dr. Charlie Motzko, Jim Davis

    This article examines one case of the long-term benefits derived from Automated RF and Microwave testing for calibration, compliance, and customer satisfaction. The results from the Equipment Management Technology’s installation of several automated RF and Microwave test systems. These test systems showed a reduction in overall cost, increased profits, shortened turnaround times, and improved customer satisfaction. Changes in ROI, expenses, reliability, rework, and staffing requirements were some of the positive and measurable outcomes.
    While the long-term benefits of testing/calibration automation are generally agreed upon, there remains the question of quantifying of the elements of the performance measurements (the measurands) to validate profitability, shortened turnaround times, improved quality, and customer satisfaction. Many factors used today do not have well defined determinants. One reason may be that, typical performance measurements (the determinants) are typically selected to maximize efficiency or effectiveness. What may be missing is establishing the optimal mix of performance measurands to provide the best results. [i.e. what you measure - is what you get!]
    This case study outlines some options that may be useful to justify and manage automated testing. The goal is to increases throughput, reduce overall operating cost, and still meet the demands of increased volumes, and reporting timelines coupled with improved consistency and reliability of data. Automated RF and Microwave testing appears to satisfy all of these requirements.

  • Leadership and Determinants of Organizational Effectiveness for National Measurement Institutes

    • Presented and Published in the 2008 Proceedings of the NCSL International Workshop and Symposium..
    • 2008
    Authors: Dr. Charlie Motzko

    Leadership and Determininants of Organizational Effective
    This exploratory mixed methodology research study examines the relationship between the National Measurement Institute’s (NMI) leadership and that leadership’s preference criteria for selecting measures of organizational effectiveness. The target population consisted of the 142 recognized NMIs in the world. The study comprised of structured field interviews with a population sample, a pilot survey with a population sample, and an Internet distributed survey to the target population. The findings revealed some statistically significant relationships between leadership and certain measurands. These relationships varied depending on the goal of effectiveness or efficiency. Other findings revealed information on the selection process, including the unexpected emergence of a third and fourth measurement dimension involving NMI relations with their political sponsor and the point-in-time of the performance measurement. All of these findings suggest rich areas for future research

  • A Proposal for Focused Organizational Effectiveness Measures by National Measurement Institutes.

    • Institutes. Presented and Published in the 2005 Proceedings of the NCSL International Workshop and Symposium.
    • 2005
    Authors: Dr. Charlie Motzko

    National Measurement Institutes (NMIs) throughout the world perform essentially the same functions yet have significant differences in terms of leadership style, use of effectiveness measures for management, and the ultimate output of public goods. Public goods are those services whose benefits are uniformly spread across the economic base. While NMI’s perform the same functions, there are some key differences that influence each NMI’s operational performance and output, and ultimately the international perception of the individual NMI’s measurement integrity.
