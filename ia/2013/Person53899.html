Heather Sutton's Summary

I serve as Media Relations and Project Director for the Metropolitan Drug Commission, a non-profit organization specializing in substance abuse prevention. I implement and oversee all of the organization’s local and regional media initiatives, including print, television, radio, online and social media. I also manage two state grants aimed at reducing underage drinking in Knoxville. I work closely with local partners and government officials to mobilize the community against adolescent alcohol use. Additionally, I am an advisory board member for the Senior Companion Program.

My past experience includes work in communication as a member of the public relations and events staff at Big Brothers Big Sisters of East Tennessee. Prior to that, I interned for Moxley Carmichael public relations firm and WBIR-TV. During college, I served as copy editor and reporter for the Tennessee Journalist, the online news website for the University of Tennessee.

You may view samples of my work online at www.heathersutton.net.

In my free time, I enjoy taking Zumba classes at the West Side Y; volunteering with Big Brothers Big Sisters; discovering new books, movies and music; online shopping and spending time with family and friends. I am an active member on Facebook, Twitter and LinkedIn.

I am a graduate of the University of Tennessee with a degree in Communication.
