Massimiliano Lenardi, PhD's Publications

  • RSVconf: Node Autoconfiguration for MANETs

    • ITS Telecommunications Proceedings, 2006 6th International Conference on Telecommunications
    • June 2006
    Authors: Massimiliano Lenardi, PhD, Robert Bredy, Osafune T.

    A mobile ad-hoc network (MANET) is a self-configuring network of mobile nodes connected by wireless links, which can form an arbitrary topology. We have worked on a new protocol, RSVconf, to ensure the IP address self-configuration of MANETs, with a special focus on the ITS (intelligent transportation system) context which is indeed the most mobile scenario. The RSVconf protocol manages the creation, merger and remerger (merger after part of the network temporarily separated) of networks at IP layer. It is stateful, distributed and routing independent. The simulation results show its capability to react fast and correctly to the rapid topology changes of mobile networks without a waste of bandwidth. In this paper we present the current status of our research, open aspects and future directions.

Massimiliano Lenardi, PhD's Projects

  • eCo-FEV project (Coordinator)

    • September 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Massimiliano Lenardi, PhD, Andrea Tomatis, Lan Lin, , Yuichi Kobayashi

    eCo-FEV aims at fulfilling the integration of the Fully Electric Vehicles (FEV) in the cooperative transport infrastructure.
    An integrated IT platform will be developed that enables connection and information exchanges between multiple infrastructure systems relevant to the FEV, such as road IT infrastructure, EV back-end infrastructure and EV charging infrastructure.
    Over this platform, multiple advanced electric mobility services are able to be provided to FEV users to improve the energy management efficiency and usability of the FEV.
