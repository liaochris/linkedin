Colorado Governor's Council for Physical Fitness

January 2011 – Present (3 years) Colorado

Http://www.coloradofitness.org The mission of the Colorado Fitness Foundation and the Colorado Governor’s Council for Physical Fitness is to empower lifestyle change in citizens of all ages and abilities through promoting and sponsoring physical activities, and to act as a liaison for the Governor on fitness-related issues.



Established in 1975 by Governor Richard Lamm, the Colorado Governor’s Council for Physical Fitness established The Colorado Foundation for Physical Fitness as its 501(c)3 not-for-profit organization to be the funding arm of the Council's efforts to promote physically active lifestyles throughout Colorado.



The Council is a bipartisan physical activity promotion and advocacy group comprised of approximately 18 Governor appointed members and numerous affiliated volunteers representing a wide range of Coloradans. The Council is funded through private donations, registration fees, and merchandise sales and receives no funding from the state for Council activities and personnel.


We believe that adults and children need at least one hour of moderate physical activity a day to stay healthy and keep from gaining weight. Regular exercise reduces the risk of heart disease, stroke and other chronic illnesses.
