David Kirkpatrick's Summary

I've been a journalist writing about tech for more than two decades. Now I'm building a conference and media company called Techonomy Media. We organize both invite-only and public events and create text and video content, all of it devoted to explaining and understanding the extraordinarily important intersection of technology and progress. We aim to be a nexus where business leaders can meet and better understand tech innovators, so both sides can better understand one another. We organize the annual Techonomy Conference, this Nov. 11-13 near Tucson, Arizona. We're also planning a half-day event on the Internet of Everything in Menlo Park May 16 and a one-day Techonomy Detroit, focused on U.S. economic and technology issues, especially jobs and education, Sept. 17.
In 2010 Simon & Schuster published my book The Facebook Effect: The Inside Story of the Company that is Connecting the World, and continue closely to track developments at Facebook and on the global Internet. At Fortune Magazine, where I worked for 25 years, I covered technology and wrote a weekly column called Fast Forward (www.fortune.com/fastforward for archives). I also created and hosted Fortune's Brainstorm conference, beginning in 2001. I'm a believer in the power of technology as a critical tool to change the world for the better. In addition to writing and producing video for www.techonomy.com, I write occasionally for Forbes and Vanity Fair. I also spend time speaking and moderating, with engagements focused on technology's intersection with the economy, the Internet, Facebook, social media, and how all those things connect.

Specialties: technology, business, Internet, Facebook, international development, technology and development, digital divide, technology journalism, global issues, technology industry competition, innovation, competitiveness, technology competition.
