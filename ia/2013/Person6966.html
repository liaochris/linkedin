Andrew Faulkner's Summary

Andrew bases his career on his beliefs that potential of architecture must be realized through the creation of unique and sustainable places to live, work, and inhabit, and that successful design must work across scales from the room to the city beyond. His experience includes projects in contexts ranging from non-profit community design and commercial redevelopment to speculative commercial, production residential, custom residential, and furniture design.

Andrew's belief in the transformational power of design vision to mobilize residents, attract investment, and revitalize identity has also led him to work in the public interest; in graduate school he began work with initiatives that became proposals for a community market in the Ville neighborhood of St. Louis and an orchard in Pagedale (http://samfoxschool.wustl.edu/news/2553). After graduation, he built on these efforts as a founder and vice-chairman of the City to River advocacy group (http://citytoriver.org) where he developed strategy, worked to build stakeholder support and leveraged political buy-in for improved access to the Mississippi River and Gateway Arch.

A graduate of Washington University in St. Louis (dual M.Arch/MaUD) and the Ohio State University, (B.S. Arch/minor City and Regional Planning) Andrew was appointed to the Planning, Landuse, and Zoning committee of the Mayor's Vanguard Cabinet of St. Louis and served from 2010-2012. While collaborating with the Planning and Urban Design Agency to advance legislation enabling Form Based Code overlays for neighborhood commercial districts, he also developed a concurrent partnership with the Mayor's Office to develop place-based community design strategies to deter crime at nuisance locations.

Andrew relocated to San Francisco in 2012. He has assisted with the Urban Prototyping SF festival and served as a mentor for Creative Currency; he has also contributed articles to NextSTL (http://nextstl.com/) and TraceSF (http://tracesf.com/).
