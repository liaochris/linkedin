Leanne Waller's Summary

My working life began when I joined my family's woodcarving business in the mid-80s. My role was teaching woodcarving, meeting new people and expanding the business. I started branches in Western Victoria which I really enjoyed, particularly the customer service and promoting the business through local media.

In the fourth year I wanted more so I returned to Adelaide to start a singing degree (B.Mus.) at the University of Adelaide.

During this time I supported myself as a food demonstrator in various supermarkets around Adelaide. I did well because I loved it: meeting people, selling and being organised. I was the highest selling demonstrator due to my ability to read and engage the customer, my selling skills and going beyond the call of duty to build rapport with department managers.

Following Uni, I owned and operated my own company: a singing studio providing singing lessons. I employed up to 8 teachers, we taught up to 200 students per week and held countless concerts and gigs, large and small, plus numerous trade shows. Prior to closing the business, we won a number of Messenger Newspaper Local Business Awards, recognising my attention to detail, management and customer service skills.

After 16 years of growth and expansion of the singing studio, the time was right to move on. I wanted a career change into Event Management, an industry to which I had plenty of exposure while running the studio and the woodcarving business.

So I closed the studio in mid-2011 and began an Advanced Diploma in Event Coordination at TAFE.

Seeking more, I also started a Masters Degree in Management at UniSA which is enhancing my management knowledge, while continuing TAFE studies. Both courses finish in Dec 2013.

I'm ready to explore opportunities in the Event Management industry. I am seeking a role with responsibility and challenge to use my organisational, management and customer service skills. I am focussed, capable, seize opportunities and produce results.
