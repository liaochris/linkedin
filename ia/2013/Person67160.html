John Kennedy's Projects

  • Pocket Universe

    • 1999 to Present
    Team Members: John Kennedy

    Award-winning Astronomy App for iPhone and iPad. This app was the first Astronomy app to support the gyro and compass on the iPhone to create a 3D model of the night sky that updated as the user moved their device. It also was the first to use the camera to provide an augmented reality view, and the first to provide 'game achievements' to encourage learning. I still update it regularly with new features and to support new hardware, and it has generated $1.1m in sales and almost paid 600,000 downloads.

    Pocket Universe was awarded "Educational App of the Year 2009" by Mac World magazine, and has won awards from Apple in 2009 and 2010, and numerous positive reviews.

  • Zeitgeist

    • January 2013 to Present
    Team Members: John Kennedy

    With the simplest possible graphics, and equally simple but addictive gameplay, Zeitgeist is a good test of your spatial awareness.Taking the currently in vogue non-skeuomorphic approach, this is a game written using UIKit / CoreAnimation in about 2 days.

  • SkyQ

    • September 2011 to September 2012
    Team Members: John Kennedy,

    Working with the astronomical telescope manufacturer Celestron, I wrote the app SkyQ to communicate via Wifi with the telescope and provide advanced alignment and 'go to' features. Celestron developed the hardware/networking interface and I created the app.

  • Cosmic X-Ray Background Nanosatellite: Unbridled Spirit

    • October 2012 to November 2012
    Team Members: John Kennedy, Wayne Hall

    "Unbridled Spirit," the Cosmic X-ray Background Nanosatellite (CXBN), was launched on Sept. 13, 2012 from Vandenberg Air Force Base.

    The mission is led by students and faculty at the Morehead State University Space Science Center. Partnering on the project with Morehead State University are Kentucky Space LLC, Lawrence Livermore National Laboratories, Noqsi Aerospace, Black Forest Engineering and Little H-Bar Ranch.

    I was asked to write this app to track the location of the satellite as it orbits the Earth, using the latest available orbital elements downloaded over the internet.

  • Eagle Lake

    • December 2012 to Present
    Team Members: John Kennedy, Matt Johnston, Aidan Rogers

    I did the bulk of the coding, UI design, graphics and sound for this retro-inspired shoot-em-up, developed as a 'toe in the water' for a fledgling games company based in the UK and US. This app is iPad only, and makes use of OpenGL|ES 2 for graphics (sprites and particles), and GameKit for networking play. Although apparently simple, the focus was on pure gameplay and the next phase would move to a true 3D textured rendering engine.
