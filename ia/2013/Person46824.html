Guillermo Antonio Amaral Bastidas' Summary

I am a software engineer with 9+ years of professional experience. I've worked on a wide array of open-source and proprietary projects, from small scale web based applications to large scale high-availability airline booking systems.

I'm very active in the open-source software and hardware communities; I'm best known for producing and starring in numerous podcasts, my involvement with The KDE and PC-BSD projects and more recently, for creating the Marshmallow Entertainment System, a DIY game console kit based around the Raspberry Pi.

In addition, I created and ran a hosting company in the early 2000s, making me highly familiar with how many UNIX system internals work. I've both maintained and installed load-balanced LAMP deployments, replication based MySQL clusters and high-traffic e-mail hosts, et al; I've also designed custom web kiosks complete with NFC and credit card processing.

My hobbies include electronic engineering, kernel hacking and game development. I also enjoy listening to operas and taking long walks on the beach.
