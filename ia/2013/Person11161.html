Alan Wallner's Summary

Alan is the founder and Creative Director of W Creative.

Alan gets up close and personal with your brand. He discovers what makes it tick, from your core values, your beliefs, your culture, your services, products, and the experience you want your customers to have. He helps distinguish you from the crowd and leverage your brand to build relationships with your customers. By working with you to consistently deliver your brand experience, Alan and his team will unleash your full brand potential.

Alan leads the creative vision for all client work at W Creative and has more than 25 years of experience with national and international clients in branding, corporate identity, logo design, website design, packaging, print, retail and environmental graphics. He brings a hands-on and open approach to creative problem solving that fosters a high level of collaboration and focus. His breadth of experience and approachable style delivers results for clients. Alan’s strength is his ability to solve complex problems with simple, resonating solutions.

Specialties: Alan has directed creative initiatives that include: brand strategy, positioning, tone and voice, naming, logo creation, brand identity and integration, website design, packaging, company and product literature, sales kits, signage and environments, video.

We ask our clients to breath deep and examine from within, because building a brand from your core values gives you authenticity. As the Creative Director for the Minneapolis office of JWT, Alan managed the Minneapolis and Toronto creative teams that provided integrated marketing solutions focused on building dynamic relationships between the target audience and the online brand experience for key accounts. Working with the traditional creative teams out of New York, Atlanta, and Chicago,  Alan worked to build collaborative, internal, long-distance relationships that led to award-winning creative.

While working at JWT, Alan had the chance to evolve my creative process into an approach that is scalable and can be easily adapted to a variety of project types. This approach is focused on setting up a common visual and verbal vocabulary brand foundation and core messaging to be leveraged for concept development. The process is focused on creating, building, and growing brands.

Creative Director

iPares

1999 – 2001 (2 years)

In the quest to create a truly integrated marketing approach, W design and five other companies merged to form iPares. Alan was the Creative Director and had a team of 13 talented individuals. We provided creative marketing solutions for print, online, and interactive solutions.

Creative leadership was fulfilled daily through educating and training the creative team about process, creative problem solving, team values, and creating an ideal work environment that fostered productivity, creativity, and collaboration.

As an active leader, Alan facilitated the creation and implementation of a company-wide process syncing multiple practice areas and disciplines to increase efficiencies and accountability, quality and affordability of the deliverable, and quality of the customer experience.

Creative Director

W design

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Design industry

January 1991 – August 1999 (8 years 8 months)

As a first business venture, W design excelled in graphic design for corporate and venture companies. W design, grew to be an award-winning design company, with a drive for creating unique, effective, non-traditional creative solutions that helped clients stand out from their competitors. These solutions included marketing and communication tools for print and online media generating an integrated system for customers. W design was an early adaptor of leveraging websites combined with traditional print for marketing businesses.

While building W design as a creative boutique known for a non-traditional creative approach, Alan developed a philosophy for creating an ideal creative culture. This philosophy includes, focusing on innovation not failures, accountability for making something of your opportunity, and having a mindset for setting yourself and the team up for success.

Senior Designer

Barsuhn Design

February 1985 – December 1991 (6 years 11 months)

For a first job in the real work environment, Alan focused on applying problem-solving techniques he studied in college and exploring developing unique creative concepts. While at Barsuhn Design, Alan was recognized by AIGA as a leading designer under 30 and awarded recognition by national design contests and local shows.

A unique feature of Barsuhn Design was that all the work was created by hand—no computers were used to create or prepare artwork. Alan takes pride in this fact because this practice set the stage for some of the creative problem-solving techniques he has developed and uses today.
