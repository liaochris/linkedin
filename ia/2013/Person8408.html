Annet Scheringa's Summary

TheStoryConnection's promise to managers/teams who are responsible for organizational change: we help you to be more succesful, to achieve your goals faster and with less 'resistance to change'.
TheStoryConnection's promise to communicationprofessionals is:
we help you to empower you in your role as an advisor in organizational change.

We consider organizational change to be the result of a permanent dialogue between all the partners and parties that are involved. This places communication in the heart of organizational change. Therefore our approach focuses on both the change manager and the communication professional. Organizational storytelling is key element of our program.

Annet Scheringa has over 20 years of experience as a professional trainer, consultant and writer. She is known for her competence, her enthousiasm and her reliability.
Annet is the author of three books, two on organizational storytelling: Storypower - verhalen die je organisatie veranderen (Adformatie Groep, 2013), Storytelling Handboek (coauteur, Boom 2008)

Scheringa has worked/works for both big and small organizations, public als well as private. Several (some well known) leaders have trusted her as a speech writer.

Specialties: - ConnectedChange: succesful implementation of organizational change.
Keywords: connection, dialogue, commitment change with less 'resistance to change'.

Storytelling Center (Self-employed)

Self-Employed; 1-10 employees; Public Relations and Communications industry

April 2009 – April 2010 (1 year 1 month)

Storytelling Center helps organizations to systematically and methodically use stories that simply exist in and around organizations ('lived stories'). In order:
- to turn something abstract (mission statement, goals) into something tangible by providing context;
- to establish a strong connection between management and employees;
- to communicate a company's core values: who are we and what do we stand for;
- to share knowledge and understanding;
- to ignite action.
For short: to restore connections that have been lacking. And by doing so, restoring the organization's effectiveness.
