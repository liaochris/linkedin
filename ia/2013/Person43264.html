Eugene Fram's Summary

PRACTITIONER AND PROFESSOR OF MARKETING AND MANAGEMENT, AUTHOR & CONSULTANT. ALSO SIGNIFICANT EXPERTISE WITH BUSINESS & NONPROFIT BOARDS OF DIRECTORS.

* Honored by an anonymous RIT grad in 2011 who donated $3 Million to RIT for the The Eugene Fram Endowed Chair in Critical Thinking

* Well over 50 consulting engagements: Identifying customer needs, improving marketing functions, improving operational functions and realigning business and nonprofit organizations & boards. Many consulting relationships have lasted 10 years or longer

* Have worked with start-ups, midsize firms and Fortune 500 firms such as Eastman Kodak, Gannett and Finland’s Outokumpu Copper, Caring.com

* Published over 125 articles in professional and trade journals such as Harvard Business Review, MIT’s Sloan Management Review, The Wall Street Journal, Business Horizons, Sales and Marketing Management, Nonprofit World, Leader to Leader & The Chronicle of Philanthropy. (Nine articles and one book since 2011) About 50% of articles in peer reviewed journals,

* For the last 10 years, have had about 100 business media placements annually in regional outlets and nation-wide example outlets New York Times, Baltimore Sun, Cleveland Plain Dealer, San Francisco Chronicle and NPR

* Extensive experience assisting senior mangers. Work well orienting marketing concepts to scientific and engineering personnel.

* Headed RIT management development center for about 15 years - designed and delivered programs for mid-level and senior managers

* Decades of successful undergraduate and graduate teaching experience. Received RIT outstanding teaching award in 1997.

* Have contributed to about 13 for-profit & nonprofit boards, holding leadership positions, including board chair and audit committee chair

* Developed a nonprofit organizational structure described in book, “Policy vs. Paper Clips,” (third edition March, 2011, http://amzn.to/eu7nQl Blog Site: http://bit.ly/yfRZpz . 400 followers.

Specialties

Developing marketing strategies, customer satisfaction studies, customer segmentation, direct marketing, expansion to new markets, marketing channel planning, brand management strategy, product development, public relations, marketing negative products, advertising campaign optimization, sales force motivation, retail location studies, industrial partnerships, nonprofit management, nonprofit boards
