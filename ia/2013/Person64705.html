Jason M. Kueser's Summary

Jason M. Kueser has focused his practice on representing investors and consumers who have been the victims of securities or investment fraud. He has represented individual investors, groups of investors, financial advisors, and stockbrokers in securities arbitration, and has represented clients in securities and consumer litigation, including class actions. He also provides estate planning and business planning services to his clients.

Jason is admitted to practice law in Missouri (2005) and Kansas (2006). He is also admitted to practice in the Eighth Circuit Court of Appeals, the United States District Court for the Western District of Missouri, and the United States District Court for the District of Kansas. He is a member of the Private Investors Arbitration Bar Association (PIABA), the American Bar Association (ABA), the Missouri Bar Association, and the Kansas City Metropolitan Bar Association. In addition, he is a member of Beta Gamma Sigma for his post-graduate studies at UMKC in the area of Finance, as well as a member of the UMKC Law School Alumni Association.

Specialties

Securities arbitration, securities litigation, class action litigation, consumer protection litigation, estate planning, business planning

The Kueser Law Firm represents investors in securities arbitration and litigation. The firm also represents financial professionals in employment disputes, represents clients in civil and commercial litigation, and provides estate planning and business formation/planning services.

The firm takes a client-centered approach to resolving clients' problems. Whether a client is involved in a lawsuit, needs to establish a corporation, LLC, or other business entity, or needs a will or trust, the firm works with the client to identify their needs and to provide the appropriate solution(s).

The Kueser Law Firm has a variety of fee options and attempts to work, as reasonably as possible, to make sure that costs do not prevent a client from taking the appropriate action.
