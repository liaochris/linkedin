Chris Ronan's Experience

Communications Manager

Crown Center Redevelopment Corp.

Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Consumer Services industry

March 2001 – Present (12 years 6 months) Kansas City, Missouri Area

I came to Crown Center for a position that focused mostly on public and media relations. Fortunately, as years have passed, I've taken on "other duties as assigned," as the old saying goes. Today, I still handle PR and media relations. But my world also includes social media, website, email marketing, video production (a nod to my old TV life), various other marketing and advertising initiatives, crisis communication planning, and a host of other fun stuff.

Projects I'm most proud of include:

* Redesigns of Crown Center's website (http://crowncenter.com) in 2006 and 2012.

* Leading Crown Center's ventures into Facebook (http://facebook.com/crowncenter), Twitter (http://twitter.com/crowncenter), YouTube (http://youtube.com/crowncenter), Foursquare, Instagram, Flickr, and other social channels.

* Communications planning for major Crown Center projects, including LEGOLAND Discovery Center, SEA LIFE Kansas City, the Sheraton Kansas City Hotel.

* Management of Crown Center's digital touchscreen kiosk system, the only one of its kind in our area.

Sports Director

Sunflower Cablevision

July 1997 – March 2001 (3 years 9 months) Lawrence, Kansas Area

For nearly four years here, I anchored the sports each night on 6News and handled play-by-play duties on Sunflower Cablevision's productions of University of Kansas football and basketball games. We did some great things here, including:

* Coverage of Kansas City Chiefs, Kansas City Royals, University of Kansas, several local high schools, and other local sports happenings.

* On-site coverage at several NCAA men's and women's basketball tournaments.

* Introduced Chris' Sunflower Sports Challenge, a feature that gave viewers the chance to challenge me to the sport of their choice. I swam against a 10-year-old (and lost, decisively), beat KU's soccer goalie at penalty kicks, surprised a former KU basketball player in a game of H-O-R-S-E, and much more.

* Recognized as the Best Small Station in the Country by the National Cable Television Association.

Sports Reporter/Anchor

KSNT-TV

May 1996 – July 1997 (1 year 3 months) Topeka, Kansas Area

A college classmate of mine was a sports reporter at KSNT and introduced me to the Sports Director as an intern candidate. By the time I'd left, three years later, I'd graduated from intern to sports reporter/anchor, to weekend anchor. It was a tremendous experience, with highlights that included:

* On-site coverage at Kansas City Chiefs training camp, several NCAA basketball tournaments and many Chiefs and Kansas City Royals games.

* The 1996 Atlanta Olympics. We were fortunate to have several athletes in our viewing area qualify for the Games. Plus, our station was the NBC affiliate. So that meant a lot of fun work. And while I didn't get to visit Atlanta, Georgia, we did travel to Atlanta, Kansas for a story. (Which was picked up nationally.)

* Grunt work. This was where I learned to handle a camera, beat nightly editing deadlines, and all the other stuff that helps you become a better reporter, videographer and anchor.
