John Leighton's Summary

John Leighton is a board certified civil trial lawyer with emphasis on catastrophic personal injury and wrongful death cases. He has 28 years of experience in litigating and trying serious personal injury and complex civil cases in Florida and throughout the United States involving medical malpractice, inadequate security/violent crime, product liability, aviation, admiralty/maritime, Resort Torts, auto/trucking negligence, legal malpractice and commercial litigation. Mr. Leighton has litigated and tried cases in Florida, Illinois, Wisconsin, Indiana, North Carolina, Texas and Georgia. Mr. Leighton's law firm, Leighton Law, P.A., has offices in Miami and Orlando, Florida to serve clients throughout the state.

Specialties: Complex and catastrophic injury litigation, including inadequate security and violent crime cases, trucking/vehicle crashes and product liability. Mr. Leighton is the author of the two volume text "Litigating Premises Security Cases" (West 2006). He is a national lecturer in trial advocacy. Expertise on Resort Torts throughout Florida. Extensive expertise in medical malpractice and catastrophic injury and death cases, having obtained some of the largest personal injury and wrongful death verdicts and settlements in Florida and the U.S.

John Leighton's Publications

  • Litigating Premises Security Cases

    • West Publishing
    • October 2006
    Authors: John Leighton

    This two-volume text covers all aspects of a premises security case, helping plaintiff attorneys achieve full compensation for innocent victims injured when business proprietors and property owners fail to protect them against foreseeable crime. This practice guide provides a detailed road map and complete resources for handling these cases from the moment the client walks in the door through successful settlement or verdict. Its complete methodology provides all the resources needed to win a premises security case, including expert legal analysis to help determine and prove your liability case and establish foreseeability of criminal conduct. This text is updated annually with a focus on most recent issues and case law related to the field of premises security and safety.
