Anton Kapliy's Experience

PhD research assistant

Enrico Fermi Institute, University of Chicago

Educational Institution; 10,001+ employees; Higher Education industry

September 2007 – Present (6 years 1 month) Greater Chicago Area

I work with Professor Melvyn Shochet, who was instrumental in discovering the top quark at Tevatron in 1994-1995. I've been involved in a number of projects, outlined below.

1. Developed in collaboration with ~10 colleagues a comprehensive distributed simulation framework for the FastTracker trigger upgrade project at the Large Hadron Collider.
- Size of codebase: >100k C++ lines, 50k C lines, 40k Python lines
- Pattern recognition, principal component analysis, and regression on very large datasets
- Over a million CPU-hours of simulation, both locally and on the cloud

2. Achieved the world’s most precise measurement of the differential production cross-section of intermediate vector bosons at the Large Hadron Collider (ATLAS experiment)
- Statistical analysis, classification, and interpretation of noisy, multi-terabyte datasets
- Extensive experience working with Hadoop-like storage (XRootD)

3. Designed and implemented a semi-automated heuristic framework to retrieve, parse, and classify thousands of daily test logs from the nightly control system, which was officially adopted by the collaboration. Reduced the length of a typical software validation shift from 8 hours to 1 hour per day.

4. Led a small team (1 engineer, 4 students) to design, build, test, install, and commission a set of data transmission cards for the Large Hadron Collider. Wrote FPGA firmware and Linux-based data acquisition tools.
