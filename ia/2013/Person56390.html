Jacco van der Kooij's Summary

Do you know a large mid-west family? If you do, then you know me, youngest of eight, raised in a farmers village in the Netherlands. If I picked an apple from the wrong tree on my way home from school - my parents would know about it before I got off my bike.

For years I thought that my name was "one-of-the VanderKooij's". My family name was like a bank account around town, and my parents had been putting their savings in it for 50+ years. My family was not rich, but known for their hard work, and willingness to help others out. So we did not need money, for my family the reputation was our most valued asset and trust was the currency we traded in.

Add 20 exciting years of building, leading and operating all aspects of sales, mostly working for Silicon Valley in start-ups. Combining these two - very distinct experiences - it all starts to makes sense to me.

All around me, I notice how every part of marketing & sales is impacted by social media. With the new goal being to make your clients successful, to focus on earning a word-of-mouth reference by exceeding a client's expectation. Not make money with some secret black art to sell something they don't use. These changes are the result of the increasing role the internet plays, and they are re-introducing me to the norms & values I was raised on.

Welcome to my village, pop. 2,400, est. 2005.
