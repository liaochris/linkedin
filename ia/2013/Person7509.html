Andy McCue's Experience

Journalist and Editor

Freelance

March 2012 – Present (1 year 6 months)

Freelance journalist and editor with 13 years experience in business-to-business, consumer and customer publishing across print and digital platforms. I do a wide range of print and web writing and editing work, ranging from rock climbing features for Climber magazine to business executive interviews and profiles for the B2B business technology media. Currently involved in an ongoing digital corporate content project for a leading telco creating thought leadership blogs for its chief executive and conceiving and writing discussion material for a senior executive group on LinkedIn. I also do a variety of content marketing writing and editing project work for Collective Content (collectivecontent.co.uk).

Editor

Climber Magazine, Warners Group Publications

Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Publishing industry

April 2009 – February 2012 (2 years 11 months)

Editor of Britain's leading monthly climbing magazine, Climber, responsible for the direction of the magazine, managing a team of contributors from around the world, managing the editorial budget, commissioning, writing and editing news, reviews and features and helping exploit commercial opportunities for the publication. Successfully reduced the editorial budget in an extremely challenging niche market whilst overseeing a complete redesign of the magazine and increase in pagination. Also responsible for driving forward the climber.co.uk website and increasing traffic (more than doubled unique visitors and page impressions), expanding use of videos, photos, user generated content and unique web content that ties in with the overall vision and direction of the sister magazine. Also instigated the incorporation of social media into the Climber brand, successfully building from scratch our Twitter and Facebook presence and growing activity on those channels rapidly to complement the magazine and website.

Editor

Redwood Publishing

Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Marketing and Advertising industry

December 2008 – April 2009 (5 months)

Initially worked as consultant editor on a pitch for a new customer magazine for IT company Fujitsu, which Redwood won. I then worked as launch editor for the publication, a high-quality serious business quarterly magazine called 'I' and microsite (www.i-cio.com) for European Chief Information Officers. The position involved liaising with the client to develop a content plan, commissioning articles from a range of writers and contributors and managing an internal art director and deputy editor. Shortlisted for Launch Editor of the Year at the British Society of Magazine Editors Awards 2009 for 'I' magazine.

Journalist and Editor

Freelance

September 2008 – November 2008 (3 months)

I worked for a range of clients writing feature articles, blog postings, managing online commercial microsites and producing video content. Clients included CBS Interactive UK, silicon.com and Greenbang.com.

Deputy Editor

silicon.com

May 2003 – August 2008 (5 years 4 months) London, United Kingdom

Spearheaded silicon.com's Chief Information Officer (CIO) coverage and built extremely strong relationships with that audience over a number of years through a string of exclusive interviews and content. Was a key part of launching the successful annual CIO50 special report and associated event, which profiled the top 50 CIOs in the UK every year. As Deputy Editor I was responsible for the day-to-day running of the news operation of the silicon.com website - setting the agenda, running the daily news meetings and line managing a team of four reporters. Also led a lot of silicon.com's trademark investigative reports and campaigns, helping the publication win Online Business Publication of the Year at the 2006 Work Foundation WorkWorld media awards with a high profile campaign into the costs and security risks of the government's controversial ID card scheme.
