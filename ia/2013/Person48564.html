Chris Swatty's Experience

Founder of a Game Changing Veteran Employment & Educational Service to Support Our Returning Heroes

C3 Technology Solutions LLC

February 2008 – Present (5 years 11 months) Scottsdale Arizona

Being a former Army officer with experience in two combat zones, after spending my previous eleven years enlisted I decided to make a change in the way veterans transition into the civilian workforce. That change entailed the development of a world class solution to assist our brothers and sisters in arms to bridge the gap between honorable service and corporate, higher educational success.

Life in the red zone of Baghdad ('05 - '06) and while working with surveillance teams on various FOBs around RC East in Afghanistan ('10 - '11) taught me a ton about "Duty, Honor, Country" (referring to the speech Gen McArthur made to the USMA grads of 1962). that all of us Officer Candidates have since been asked politely (?) to memorize.

The "Duty", included patrolling Baghdad during the referendum and their first General Elections and flying all over RC East in Afghanistan. The "Honor" was obtained by being re-called. The "Country" just happened to include helping more than ours, and yes we have. Ironically, the "Duty" was was at times measurably less stressful than facing the job market when I returned home. That sort of homecoming is not what our Brothers and Sisters in Arms deserve.

Gigseer.com (http://g2.gigseer.com) is the result of experiencing the fractured support while transitioning from Active Duty military. We as Veterans and patriotic Americans need to be doing a lot more for those who have served.

GigSeer is primarily focused on providing all necessary support through a broad community of "Seers" comprised of former military leaders who have already landed in the civilian corporate world. We are also in the development stages of creating an engine to automate and "civilianize" their value statements.

There is a huge population of tremendously talented, driven and trained military and former military who are oddly enough experiencing the largest demographically adjusted unemployment rate @ 12-29%. More to follow...

Director, Corporate Computing

CNA National Warranty

May 2011 – July 2012 (1 year 3 months) Scottsdale, Arizona

Recruited while still in Afghanistan to bring about technology change by leading software development and migration initiatives including enterprise email, intranet web presence, Customer Relationship Management (CRM) suite and multiple modules of an HR Information System (HRIS). HRIS required managing the vendor (Ultimate Software) from inception through go-live.

Team developed and launched both an an automated and collaborative internal help desk as well as linking it to make it available for all external customers including Harley Davidson. That initiative resulted in an increase of responsiveness to calls by over 80% while also allowing us to track adn trend to collect best practices thereby avoiding future most common calls.

My team also developed the company's first ever intranet. It was developed in Liferay and augmented by data storage and process flows via SharePoint. That resulted in both a technological shift as well as a cultural shift for the company removing the need for 95% of internal memos.

Planned, developed and lead migrations from Oracle’s Beehive email system to Microsoft Exchange 2010 as well as the conversion from UNIX based thin clients to a mix of PC and Wyse terminal services virtual Microsoft 2007 machines. Migrations increased productivity, decreased cost of supporting legacy systems while experiencing zero loss of data.

Planned, developed and have successfully tested Disaster Avoidance/Recovery for all of the 74 servers within our environment. My team has achieved availability for our production servers to within standards of 99.99 or just under an hour downtime with no data loss.

Prepares and manages annual IT operating and capital budgets in excess of $500K.

Net result of efforts resulted in improving intra-corporate communications as well as solidifying customer facing networks.

I learned a TON from working within every component of a multi-BN international company
