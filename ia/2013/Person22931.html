James Chisholm's Summary

Shortlisted for the CBI’s Young Finance Director of the Year, I am already a Pension Fund Chairman and Charity Trustee, as well as the Chief Financial Officer for a major industrial membership body. Chief Financial Officer of the British Glass Manufacturers’ Confederation since 2003, I was instrumental in ensuring the survival of British Glass as both a representative and research organisation during the 2008-9 financial crisis through strong cash management and leading a cultural turnaround and downsizing programme. Since then the organisation has grown. Alongside this role, I am also the Company Secretary of the British Glass Manufacturers’ Confederation and the Finance Director of Glass Technology Services Ltd.

A published author on the pensions’ crisis, I am used to working with the Managing Directors of global corporations, am an Executive Director, and manage a multi-professional team including HR, IT, facilities and other support functions. I am now seeking to add other Non-Executive Directorships to my portfolio, for Social Housing Associations (RSLs), major charities (Third Sector), NHS Trusts and Foundation Trusts and Central Government Non-Departmental Public Bodies (NDPBs).

James Chisholm's Experience

Chief Financial Officer and Company Secretary

British Glass Manufacturers' Confederation

Nonprofit; 11-50 employees; Glass, Ceramics & Concrete industry

November 2003 – Present (9 years 7 months) Sheffield, United Kingdom

British Glass is the representative body for one of the UK’s leading industries. Its member companies include Pilkington, BOC, Rio Tinto and Saint Gobain. I manage the financial and legal aspects of British Glass as a company and report directly to the board of directors in my role as Company Secretary. I am also Secretary to three British Glass Sub-committees: The British Glass Council, the Finance and General Purpose Committee and the Pensions Sub-committee.

My achievements include:

• Building a close working relationship with British Glass’ member companies’ board, including the Managing Directors of global companies, ensuring a constructive partnership between these rival firms.

• Saving the organisation during the financial crash of 2008-9, reshaping the relationship with member companies, improving cash flow management, closing the defined benefits pension scheme and working with Pilkington to create a defined contribution scheme.

• Safely managing a redundancy programme in this highly-skilled, scientifically educated organisation, in a fair and ethical manner with no resulting employment tribunals.

• Making the group much more commercial, taking lessons from the MDs of our member companies and transferring them to British Glass, recently boosting turnover by around 33% in a single year.

• Successfully developing the support infrastructure for this group of companies, leading not just finance but legal, IT, facilities management and other staff, cutting costs while improving quality.

• Project managing an accounting system transition from the Opera to Sage systems, leading the whole programme from procurement through data transfer to the switching off of the legacy system.

• Designing and implementing a thorough, professional, training and development programme for British Glass’ support services.

• Leading on British Glass’ involvement with, and implementation of, the MacLeod Report on Employee Engagement.

    Through an external provider we had personality profiling to assist with working more effectively as a business.

    My natural styles are:

    Thruster Organiser
    Concluder Producer
    Controller Inspector

    Thruster-Organizers are people who enjoy making things happen. They are analytical decision-makers, always doing what is best for the task, even if their actions sometimes upset others. Their great ability is to get things done, and for this reason they are often found working in project management positions.

    Concluder-Producers are practical people who can be counted on to carry things through to the end. Their strength is in setting up plans and standard systems so that outputs can be achieved on a regular basis, in an orderly and controlled fashion.

    Controller-Inspectors are quiet, reflective people who enjoy the detailed side of work, such as dealing with facts and figures. They are usually careful and meticulous and can spend long periods of time on a particular task, working quietly on their own.
