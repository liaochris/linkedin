Katie-Leigh Lubinsky's Summary

Journalism, and how we communicate overall, is rapidly changing. Intertwined with this change are users' increasing ability to control and demand what type of content they want and receive from communicators. New age media involving user interaction, visuals, blogs and social media is becoming more and more popular. I am fascinated by this rapid change from traditional print and televised journalism to a more tech-saavy and real-time journalistic style. I am interested in new media specifically integrating and communicating different types of communication styles in online and mobile formats.

I am originally from Southport, N.C., a small beach community, where my passion for journalism began in high school with the school's newspaper as well as interning at a professional local newspaper. I was accepted into UNC - Chapel Hill's School of Journalism & Mass Communication as a multimedia undergraduate. After working at various news outlets, volunteering and freelancing some, I graduated in May 2011 with two bachelor's degrees and a load of fun experience (go Heels!).

Currently, I assist in communicating media items from the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency's Office of Research and Development; volunteer as the social media and website coordinator for the non-profit Citiwide Vocational Center; am pursuing a certificate in web design from Wake Technical Community College; and freelance from time-to-time.
