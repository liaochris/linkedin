Katie Skeen's Summary

I'm an editor with a background in teaching and research. What this means is that:

I bring to your project a passion for purposeful, effective writing. For 15+ years I have engaged directly with the written word in its many forms (literary, creative, descriptive, persuasive, technical, professional, practical, academic), as a teacher of writing, as an author, as a literary scholar, and as a sympathetic but clear-eyed editor.

I bring analytical, communications, and research skills honed in high-profile institutional settings.

I can make sense of complex information and turn it into something that will appeal to your target audience.

With an eye for detail and an ability to see the "big picture," I can spot errors and potential problems at all stages of a project.

I am accustomed to handling varied projects with multiple and varying deadlines and adept at keeping other people on track.

I have the resourcefulness to work independently, allowing you to concentrate on other aspects of your project -- while always maintaining open lines of communication.

To date, my writing and editorial work has included web and social media content, marketing materials, e-newsletters, grant proposals, résumés, instructional materials, historical/biographical notes, academic and trade books, journal articles, book reviews, and conference presentations.

Specialties:
--Learning new subject fields, new lexicons, new technologies, FAST
--Writing for niche audiences
--Structuring content for maximum persuasive impact
--Targeted research, fact-checking
--Leading and enabling collaborative conversations
--Web and social media content
--Recognized expertise in the humanities, writing instruction, Irish art/history/culture
--Specific knowledge of many general-interest fields (e.g. health, cooking, fashion, parenting, gardening, travel, green living, consumer finance)

    • Dublin: Four Courts Press
    • September 2010
    Authors: Katie Skeen, Catherine Skeen

    More than simply a new edition of a rollicking 1746 poem, this is the definitive book on William Dunkin, eighteenth-century Irish poet and friend of Jonathan Swift. Combining careful biographical and historical research with attention to context and meaning, the Introduction and Notes (by Catherine Skeen) shed light on Irish and literary history. Terry Eagleton calls it "a brilliant edition of the Dunkin poem, which has inspired me to read it again. It's done with great love and devotion."
