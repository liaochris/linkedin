Barbara Christensen's Summary

Barbara Christensen works with people who speak English as their second language to improve their English pronunciation. Ideal candidates have a moderate to excellent understanding and command of the English language but need assistance with their pronunciation to communicate more efficiently and effectively in English. Clients are seen individually or in small groups of up to 5 people. Training can be arranged onsite. Additional services: presentation skills training, interview skills, communication coaching.


Speech Advantage’s founder and president is Barbara Christensen. Ms. Christensen received a Bachelor of Science Degree in Speech Communication from Iowa State University as well as a Master of Arts degree in Speech Pathology from Northeast Missouri State University (currently Truman State University.) She worked as a speech-language pathologist in the schools, medical settings, and private practice until she started her corporate work in 1999 in Des Moines, IA.

Today, Ms. Christensen’s specialty area is accent modification and is based out of Madison, WI. She has authored numerous published articles as well as spoken at state and national conventions about accent modification techniques and corporate speech practices. She is a former national trainer for the Institute of Language and Phonology - Training Division, where she traveled the U.S. to teach accent modification workshops to other speech professionals who wanted to offer this specialty area as part of their individual businesses. Ms. Christensen served on the Executive Board of the Corporate Speech Pathology Network (Corspan), an international corporate speech organization, for five years during which time she served in the offices of president and secretary. Other organizations in which she is involved include Society of Human Resource Management and International Professionals Association.

Ms. Christensen lives in Madison, WI with her husband and two children.
