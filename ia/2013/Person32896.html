Dawn Goldberg's Summary

A long time ago, I became an English teacher because I wanted to help students communicate well and gain meaning and insight from the written word. While the experience of teaching wasn't what I'd hoped it to be, it was the foundation for all I would do in my life. I just didn't know it then.

I went on to start my own business as a virtual assistant. I helped my clients make their already-successful businesses into thriving ones. I managed their businesses, handling invoicing, travel arrangements, website design and maintenance, and all things words. I began to see, or maybe just to remember, that there was a real need for people to learn how to communicate with words so that what they wrote was what others understood.

It's not a surprise, then, that I completely shifted from one path to another; I created an entire company around helping others become stronger writers. I took everything I had done, as a teacher, as a virtual assistant, and as a business owner, and applied it to this new company, which is now over six years old.

My biggest gift and skill is my ability to see the big picture, the puzzle, as well as the details and pieces that make up that puzzle. Whether I'm creating a marketing campaign or helping a coaching client create a Book Writing Action Plan, I keep the big picture in mind at the same time as I help plan how to get there.

I'm an online business manager for just a small handful of high-end clients, and for them, I create systems, procedures, and, oh yes, lots of marketing material.

Through Write Well U, I help women entrepreneurs unleash their authentic selves into their businesses through their words. I created the Writing From Your Soul system to help business owners connect more powerfully, reach more people, and make a difference.

Specialties: Exceptional writing skills, the vision to see to the heart of someone's challenge, systems and procedures creation (I swear I do this in my sleep!), and the creativity process.

Dawn Goldberg's Projects

  • Relationship Inventory

    • September 2013 to Present
    Team Members: Dawn Goldberg, Mark Shuler

    What we do in the Relationship Inventory Workshop

    Basically, we help people go through a three-step process:

    1. Identify – Evaluate major relationships in their lives and where they fall on that Positive-Neutral-Negative scale. Analyze the dysfunctional communication mechanisms at work.

    2. Correct – Phase out, deal with, act, and intervene with toxic and negative relationships

    3. Prevent and Increase – Create boundaries and early detection systems to help us avoid and recognize negative relationships and thus have more energy to accentuate and increase positive ones.

    While this sounds like an oh-so-technical process, the results are what is really important.

    What we get out of dissecting and exploring our various relationships (and the working on those that need some help) is more of an ability to be here and now.
