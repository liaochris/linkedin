Michael Stone's Summary

Michael has covered politics from city councils to presidential candidates, crime, courts, education, business and employment, wildlife and the environment, health and fitness, military, travel, weather, celebrities, and several large music festivals, most notably Austin City Limits.

His work has resulted in numerous journalism awards and scholarships, an assistantship and a fellowship. It has been picked up by the Associated Press, CNN, MSNBC, the Huffington Post, Publishers Weekly and other media. The website he launched in 2010 began without any content or hits but grew to attract tens of thousands of unique visitors a month.

His biggest proficiencies are writing, reporting and editing. But he is also greatly experienced in photography, videography, multimedia, page design, public relations, social media, open records and meetings, content and freelance management, and more.

Michael started with Middle Tennessee State University’s student newspaper in 2007, eventually holding the position of editor in chief. During college, he also completed two internships for the Daily News Journal in Murfreesboro, Tenn., and had several freelance articles published in between internships.

After graduating with his bachelor’s in journalism from MTSU, he was selected for a prestigious six-month internship at the Chattanooga Times Free Press. Upon completion, he moved to metro Atlanta to launch and direct a news website covering two counties for AOL.

Having always planned to go to graduate school, he left AOL after two years and is pursuing his master’s in environmental communications. He also teaches two sections of an undergraduate journalism lab, and reports and photographs for the PBS affiliate WUFT.

Among Michael's passions are conservation and wildlife. Along with time volunteering in zoos, he was named the first-ever visiting media specialist for White Oak Conservation, a premiere facility for 20-plus rare and endangered species.
