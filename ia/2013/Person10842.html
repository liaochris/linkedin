Audrey Dimola's Summary

The first thing writer/poet/curator Audrey Dimola ever wanted to be was a trapeze artist, but acrobatics of the written word have always been closest to her heart.

Her poetry and prose is infused with visceral images, crackling energy, and sometimes even song – live, it becomes a kind of spoken mixed-media of life experience, the alchemy of ache into art.

Born and raised in Astoria/Long Island City, Audrey has been writing about, participating in, and helping to promote Queens culture since her college years. She is the author of "Decisions We Make While We Dream," an original collection of work spanning 2000 to 2012, guerrilla sticker poet of the Compass Project, founder of the roving reading/live writing series Nature of the Muse, and curator of the first ever Queens Literary Town Hall.

Audrey has been featured on NY1 and in the New York Daily News, DNAinfo New York, Flavorpill’s Flavorwire, and many Queens publications such as BORO Magazine, LIC Courier Magazine, and It’s Queens Magazine. She has performed at venues around NYC including the Brooklyn Museum and at the NYC Poetry Festival. Her poetry/prose, articles, and photos have been published in Newtown Literary Journal, Missfits Magazine, Words Dance, Creations Magazine, Whiskey Dregs Magazine, QRO Mag, Ins&Outs Magazine, and more.

She devotes her life to the power of words and aims always to stay wild and stay grateful.
