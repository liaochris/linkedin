Jeanine Broderick, CFIRS, AIRC, CHC's Summary

Jeanine Broderick is the Founder of Happiness 1st Institute where the knowledge and skills necessary to become adept at utilizing one's emotional guidance system are easily learned. Ms. Broderick has completed more than five years of self-directed study on human thriving across many scientific disciplines and philosophies with advanced research in philosophy, positive psychology, sociology, quantum physics, and other sciences as they relate to what cultivates human thriving. Her education is rich, broad, deep, thrilling, and exciting. The analytic skills that brought her to an executive position from which she is retired facilitated her research. Her burning passion to find answers that will help others thrive has created a depth and breadth of knowledge of how to increase human thriving beyond common psychology practice and theory, where the focus continues to emphasize what can go wrong.

Happiness 1st (Happiness 1st Institute) was created from a passionate desire to help others understand and enjoy the vast benefits of being happy. Science has proven that increased positive emotions and optimism provide many advantages to the individual who cultivates these emotions.

See the company description for scientifically documented benefits of positivity and optimism. Science has also proven that happiness and optimism levels can be increased and the increases are sustainable.

A masterful combination of information that helps students have a clearer perspective about topics such as their own beliefs and the impact of current emotional state on behavior is utilized. The course includes more than 50 processes, many evidence based skills, to help students deliberately impact their emotional state and develop new habits of thought that are more beneficial to thriving.

We are also available for public speaking engagements,private coaching, and as consultants for projects addressing social issues.

Happiness has become an industry.

It is time for the industry to have organizations that support individuals working in the industry.

There are many ways to provide such support.

Some examples include:

+ Professional standards
+ Continuing Education
+ Networking
+ Professional publications
+ Conferences with other professionals in the industry that provide new and relevant information
+ An organization that creates seminars with presenters from the organization for clients that increase the draw by offering many quality professionals in one location with resources that individuals may not be able to provide alone
+ A forum for communication
+ Regional seminars and webinars
+ Sharing of best practices

There are many kinds of individuals working in the Happiness industry performing many types of activities.
