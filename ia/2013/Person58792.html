Latino Outreach and Education Coordinator

National Alliance on Mental Illness Greater Milwaukee

August 2013 – Present (5 months) Greater Milwaukee Area

Jeanette Arellano is currently involved with the National Alliance on Mental Illness Greater Milwaukee as the Latino Outreach and Education Coordinator. Jeanette's position enables her to assist and inform the Latino Community on strategies in avoiding the stigmas of mental illness and help build stronger methods of engaging the spanish speaking community about mental health. This method is delivered through a series of Creative presentations,events, workshops and support groups for the Latino Community, while recognizing the importance of cultural awareness in the Spanish speaking community. Jeanette currently as the MHAT Co-Chair for the Milwaukee Latino Health Coalition, and sits on the Board as a student Rep. American Civil Liberties Union and the Riverwest Artist Association. And has been recognized as Wisconsin's first Bilingual Wellness Recovery Action Plan certified trained Facilitator.

  • Milwaukee Latino Health Coalition

    • September 2011 to Present
    Team Members: Jeanette Arellano, Leslie Davis

    Goals
    Advocate for quality and accessibility of mental health services for Latinos.
    Support and hold accountable the agencies that provide mental health services for Latinos.
    Reduce stigma surrounding mental health issues.
    Address the need for more bilingual mental health providers.
    The Mental Health Action Team started out as the Preventive Education Action Team. Under this topic, we began with four areas of interest: workforce development, cultural competency and sensitivity, patient engagement, and a holistic approach to health. We engaged in a few activities to determine which area of interest we wanted to focus on first, and we came to the consensus of focusing on a holistic approach to Latino health. Thus, we became the Mental Health Action Team.

    The membership of our Action Team includes organizations such as Sixteenth Street Community Health Center, Walker’s Point Community Clinic, Council for the Spanish Speaking, Sojourner Family Peace Center, Mental Health America of Wisconsin, NAMI—Greater Milwaukee, UMOS, and Disability Rights WI.

    Some of our collaborations include working with the Milwaukee Mental Health Task Force, the Milwaukee Latino Mental Health Institute, and the “Community-Based Behavioral Activation for Latinos with Depression” project with UWM and Sixteenth Street Community Health Center.

    We welcome any interested parties to attend our meetings. We usually meet on the third Thursday of the month at 9:00 a.m. Come join us!
