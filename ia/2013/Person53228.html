Hiroshi Wald's Summary

As Managing Director of Austral Capital, Hiroshi Wald leads US operations for an early-stage ($30M) international venture capital fund, focused on identifying and developing successful entrepreneurs in high growth industries in the US and in Latin America. Founded in 2008, Austral has offices in Santiago, Chile and Palo Alto, California and currently has 11 companies in its portfolio of IT and life sciences ventures.

Hiroshi also serves on the Executive Board of the MIT/Stanford Venture Lab, which hosts the largest ongoing gathering of Silicon Valley entrepreneurs, venture capitalists and thought leaders at monthly events at the Stanford Graduate School of Business. VLAB convenes leading minds from the Valley to identify disruptive technologies and innovations that will have a significant impact upon the tech industry in the coming 18 to 24 months and serves an ecosystem of 25,000 Valley influencers.

Prior to joining Austral, he was Principal at The Wald Group, a consultancy focused on technology development, management strategy and customer insights, analysis and intelligence. His diverse background includes Managing Director at Competitive Insights, a business intelligence firm focused on natural language processing and semantic analysis of web content; product development executive at Knowledge Networks where he built and managed teams in India; CEO and Founder of a wireless startup, Zingdata, which was acquired by Knowledge Networks; directing market intelligence at Hill and Knowlton, one of the world’s largest public affairs and relations firms; selection as a Coro Fellow in New York City and serving as Staff Assistant in The White House Office of Intergovernmental Affairs.

Hiroshi is a graduate of the University of California, Los Angeles and is game for any gastronomic adventure, anytime.

Hiroshi Wald's Projects

  • Geek Camp

    • October 2011 to Present
    Team Members: Hiroshi Wald

    Geek Camp is a twice yearly, 3 month long startup acceleration program which gives student and professional founders the opportunity to be mentored by leading innovators from around the world to bring ideas from prototype to startup.
    Geek Camp starts with a 5 day selection hackathon on the campus of the top university in Chile and results in the selection of a small handful of companies which receive $20,000 in funding and an invitation to join an intense, 3 month long program focused on product design and development, user traction, business models and investor presentations.
    The final month of Geek Camp culminates in a one month incubation in the Silicon Valley where founders connect with local investors, entrepreneurs and advisors. Several Geek Camp alumni have gone on to raise seed rounds from investors, been accepted to top US accelerators and have also established US-based companies.
    Now in it’s fourth cohort, Geek Camp 4 attracted over 600 entrants from all over Latin America from which up to 10 will be selected to spend one month in the Silicon Valley in July 2013.
    Geek Camp is sponsored by Incuba UC the technology incubator at Universidad Católica de Chile (Chile’s top university) and Austral Capital.
