Harold Boeck's Summary

Harold Boeck is a professor at the business faculty of the Université du Québec à Montréal where he focuses on Innovation Management and Marketing Technology. His current research interests focus on the use and impact of RFID within open-loop Supply Chain environments: the factors leading to its adoption, its influence on buyer-seller relationships and how it can lead to a competitive advantage. He is also a member of the editorial board of the "International Journal of RF Technologies: Research and Applications", a new research journal focused on the business applications of RF technologies.

Harold is a regular guest speaker for several industrial organizations, a CompTIA RFID+ Certified Professional, the founder and leader of Canada’s first local chapter of the RFID Tribe as well as a founder of Academia RFID, the first bilingual RFID training and certification center in Canada and is a judge at the RFID Journal Awards.

Harold Boeck's Publications

  • Coercive Power in an Interdependent Dyad: A Study of Interfirm Relationships in the Canadian Broadcast TV Industry.

    • In Annual Paris Business and Social Sciences Research Conference
    • May 22, 2013
    Authors: Harold Boeck, Jessica Labre, Vincent Levis

    This exploratory study focuses on the presence and relationship between 3 key constructs namely (1) coercive power, (2) interfirm relationships and (3) compliance in a highly interdependent dyad that operates in the Canadian Broadcast TV industry.

    Very interestingly, the field research found that all participants used coercive power. This contrasts with previous studies that usually present coercive power being used by a powerful organization in a dominant/dependent dichotomy. When used, coercive power was presented in an indirect manner, regarding price, linked to the end consumer’s satisfaction and presented as a natural consequence of complying or not complying. Its use was rather soft and was beneficial in contributing to the dyad’s goals. Its effectiveness has been demonstrated in the case study as it will generally influence the business partner to comply with the demands being presented. The use of coercion did not seem to generate strong negative sentiments among the participants.
