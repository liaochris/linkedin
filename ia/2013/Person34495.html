Derek Rudnak's Summary

With virtually a lifetime of exposure to mass media, Derek Rudnak has masterfully corralled his diverse experiences to become a writer, editor and designer with a chameleon-like ability to adapt to any medium, audience, industry, subject or environment. Coupled with his management savvy and and entrepreneurial spirit, he has enjoyed success both as a staff employee and freelancer.

Rudnak paid his dues in print and traditional media. More recently, he has almost exclusively transitioned into online communications, and although he appreciates the unique differences between print and online, he believes that core fundamentals of strong writing will always remain.

"'Good writing' has always been a subjective concept," said Rudnak. "Along with grammar, style and mechanics, there's always been a need to write for the audience and the medium.

"Online writing--Websites, SEO, e-mail campaigns, etc.--still requires excellent fundamentals, but it also requires an open and inquisitive mind that is willing to embrace new rules and adapt them to constantly evolving media and platforms."

But whether for print or online, Rudnak's communication style is always razor sharp. He has earned a reputation for an ability to slice through convoluted concepts and aim directly for the core message. Yet, although there is a fierce sense of efficiency in his writing and work styles, there is also a very warm, humorous and organic spirit in his style and personality.

Citing many of the "New Journalism" pioneers and other related writers as inspirations (e.g., Hunter Thompson, P.J. O'Rourke, Doug Kenney, Chris Miller), Rudnak often flirts with creative writing and non-fiction/journalism techniques, even in his marketing and advertising writing.

"My father and brother are painters--both in commercial and fine art--and I'm also a musician and former media scholar, so I've been exposed to a fantastic range of ways to express ideas," said Rudnak.
