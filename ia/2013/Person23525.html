Dr. Christina McCale's Summary

Capitalizing on her lengthy marketing expertise, Dr Christina McCale provides organizations, departments, and individuals data / decision making assistance to form strategic plans, devise creative communications, implement marketing campaigns, and create strategies to serve customers better.

As 17 year veteran of corporate marketing, McCale has also developed significant experience teaching more than 6,000 students through more than 240 classes across 30 subject areas.

Her teaching ability, coupled with professional “real world” knowledge is a style undergraduate and graduate students enthusiastically respond to, appreciate, and benefit from.

Her research and writing agenda stretches across nine subject areas, writing with 11 co-authors, generating more than 40 articles, conference presentations and proceedings. She also has contributed to eight text books, written five books, and is the co-editor of 12 books.

Corporate Specialties: Marketing planning, strategy, research; Team management and leadership; remote management, content development, marketing communications, results, ROI and tracking, creativity, public speaking, media spokesperson, and problem solving.

Academic Specialties: Experiential education; marketing; integrated marketing communications; marketing management; services marketing; promotions; research; consumer behavior; corporate marketing; management/leadership.

    Authors: Dr. Christina McCale, Dick Moody, Ed.D., Willie Crawford, Jane Mark, Rachel Rofe, Reed Floren, Connie Ragen Green

  • Waiting For Change: Impacts on Life, Family Work and the New 99% Reality

    • Book
    Authors: Dr. Christina McCale

    The premise for this book was simple: what is the real, human experience of those so adversely affected by the economy – the human face? More than “a catalogue of pain," Waiting for Change provides the twin understandings -- the mind boggling numbers of the Great Recession and the real human experience behind the numbers. In an effort to profile and understand the long-term unemployed or under employed, Dr. Christina McCale has gone beyond the media sound bite statistics of the Great Recession and profiled the very personal realities of those living with the economic impacts to their housing, sustenance, employment, children, and our social support systems: exploring how these areas of one’s life can be so drastically impacted – irrevocably altered – by job loss and the continuing drag of the Great Recession we’ve all experienced in a myriad of different ways.

  • Self Marketing Plan: A research based overview, integrating marketing and career planning

    • Book
    Authors: Dr. Christina McCale

    Many college students put off looking for their first "real" job after graduation until it's too late. Career Services administrators know this. Professors see this. And parents worry about this. But how do you change this cycle? It's simple: through experiential education. Experiential learning - or, "learning by doing" -- helps students learn not just the "theory" they are supposed to gain from the classroom, but the all important "why" - why does this matter to me?

    The self marketing plan, as discussed in this book, helps integrate career building skills and soft skills such as writing skills, critical thinking skills, and communications skills -- the skills a student will need the rest of their lives -- with a marketing or business class; but realistically could be done in just about any discipline.

    In this book, McCale shares the core concepts that create the foundation for both experiential education, the self marketing plan, and why education owes it to our students to teach them these skills. Grounded in human development, psychology, learning theory, educational process, marketing principles, and career management, this book provides the foundation any teacher, professor, administrator or educational leader may need to understand the principles behind the self marketing plan, know the core concepts of experiential education, know how these activities benefit an prepare students, and develop a strategy for including such activities in their programs.

  • Self Marketing Plan: Research Results, Implications and Considerations for Integrating Marketing and Career Planning

    • Book
    Authors: Dr. Christina McCale

  • Self Marketing Plan: Teaching marketing concepts for career planning

    • Book
    Authors: Dr. Christina McCale

    This book is the result of a broad, over-arching question:
    How should universities best prepare undergraduates to be street-ready productive individuals in the workplace?

    Education does not have to be an either/or situation where it is entirely lecture/classroom oriented OR practical and hands on based.

    The inclusion of an experiential learning project, such as the Self Marketing Plan, can meet the needs of several constituencies when teaching marketing concepts for career planning. First, the Self Marketing Plan can assist faculty in educating students about the core marketing concepts being reviewed in class. Second, the project can help students develop the key skill sets that employers/researchers say are important. Lastly, the project can provide the impetus for students to begin career planning, a key factor in career entry success.
