  • The MisterHouse/APRS Tracking System (MHATS)

    • February 1999 to Present
    Team Members: Brian Klier, Bruce Winter

    This project was Slashdotted in May 2003 (http://developers.slashdot.org/story/03/05/02/1128209/misterhouse---a-home-driven-by-perl-scripts).

    The software decodes GPS information sent wirelessly, and provides visual and audible notifications of station bearings and distances from both a base station and other points of interest. It also allows remote control of home lights and appliances through X-10 protocol through these wireless signals.

  • Minnesota Tiger Census Map Conversion

    • February 2011 to April 2011
    Team Members: Brian Klier

    I spent a considerable amount of time converting Minnesota Tiger Census map files into a GIS format compatible with GRLevelX, a weather radar viewer used by most Skywarn agencies in the state of Minnesota, as well as the National Weather Service. The map files allow agencies to zoom in and get street-level mapping detail directly on the radar display.

  • MailTalk Voice-based E-Mail Client

    • 2001 to 2007
    Team Members: Brian Klier

    I wrote a homebrew E-Mail client that allows visually-impaired people to have their incoming E-Mail read to them through a text-to-speech interface. Many pieces of software available on the market are difficult to use without the aid of a person with sight. The focus of this software was to provide an easy-to-use interface to let individuals with little or no sight to communicate with friends and family on the Internet.

  • Network Systems Design for Mobile Incident Command Trailer

    • June 2000 to August 2000
    Team Members: Brian Klier

    I designed and implemented a LAN and telephone system for the Rice County Emergency Communications Command Center. This is a mobile 30-foot trailer that is wired for both voice and data, and contains computer, phone, and radio communication systems that Police, Fire, and the Sheriff's Department will use during emergencies and special events. The trailer was designed with emphasis given on fault-tolerance of the communications systems.
