Jason Pamental's Summary

Jason Pamental has worked on the web since 1994. With a background in design and an intimate understanding of technology and its application on the web, he has led creative and technical teams for NFL and America's Cup teams, Fortune 25 and technology corporations and many other clients, serving in both creative and technical roles. A passionate advocate of web standards and accessibility, Jason embraces new developments in web typography and responsive design as the next logical evolution in usability, search and maintenance of complex web systems.

In addition to his consulting practice, Jason speaks about Responsive Design, Web Typography and Web Strategy at conferences and pretty much anywhere people will listen. When that’s not happening he’s usually hanging out with his family or following @aProperCollie around Turner Reservoir in the morning, posting photos on Instagram.

Specialties

Background in graphic design and over a decade's experience in print and online design, HTML, CSS, XML, XHTML, Javascript, numerous development platforms (.NET, PHP, Witango and others). Extensive experience in usability and information architecture. Have designed and developed numerous websites, eCommerce applications, complex web-based applications and complete hardware/software solutions for end users and within data centers.

Creative Director/Platform Architect

Schoolyard, Inc.

Public Company; 1-10 employees; Internet industry

October 2009 – October 2012 (3 years 1 month) Providence, RI

Starting with helping to relaunch Schoolyard in early 2010, I was responsible for initial prototype and all platform development of the Schoolyard platform. Developed extensive feature list including athletics, news publishing, integrated calendars, multi-role publishing, extensive private group content (teacher pages, parent community groups, Board of Trustees, etc) and numerous data and Single-Sign-On integrations. This in-depth knowledge of capabilities and technology helped in the business development process, as I was usually brought in to consult with schools during the sales process to evaluate technical needs and answer questions about mobile access, technology integration, marketing/SEO and more. I also provided mentoring, training and creative direction to the design team after developing our design process.

After seeing it go poorly at so many other web companies, I set up a flow that pushed visual design much later in the process: we conducted discovery research with each school which led to new Information Architecture and (within days) a fully-built out 'sandbox' site. This was not a sample but a fully functioning instance of Schoolyard's Drupal platform, complete with a base responsive theme. We could then proceed with both content collection and import and begin the visual design process.

That process was also tweaked to start with Style Tiles rather than full page mockups. This allowed more creative exploration with less wasted time and also invites more and better collaboration with the client. From there we would design a single interior page, which would then usually only require one round or so of iteration. From there we could approach the home page and focus on getting the right content instead of wrangling over design language. It's proved to be a very success process, in use since early 2011 - helping launch about 30 sites by late 2012.
