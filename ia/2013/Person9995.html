Human Resources Senior Consultant, Employee Relations

University of Minnesota

Educational Institution; 10,001+ employees; Higher Education industry

September 2012 – Present (1 year) 100 Donhowe Building, 319 15th Avenue S.E., Minneapolis, MN 55455

The Human Resources Senior Consultant serves as a member of a dedicated service team of HR professionals who work together to advise and support human resource leaders, managers, and employees in a defined portfolio of collegiate and administrative units on a broad array of critical HR issues, including but not limited to:
- recruitment and selection
- organizational effectiveness
- compensation and classification
- on-boarding and orientation
- performance management
- employee and labor relations
- employee benefits
- policy and compliance
- conflict resolution and management
- rewards and recognition

Responsibilities include:
- Coach collegiate and administrative units in human resource management, identify options for their consideration, and translate creative ideas into sound workplace practices, in full compliance with federal and state laws and University policies and procedures;
- Provide consistent interpretation and application of employment-related policies, rules, contracts, and practices for all employee groups;
- Assist collegiate and administrative units in managing difficult workplace situations through effective problem solving strategies and sound conflict resolution services;
- Learn the organizational and operational facets of the units supported (i.e., strategic objectives, budget overview, personnel matters, culture) in order to deliver the most effective and customized consulting services possible;
- Work collaboratively with the vice president for human resources, other service teams, and the OHR centers of excellence to identify and apply best practices, recognize recurring issues and trends that may require a recalibration of policy and practice across the human resources spectrum, and ensure units are receiving the highest level of OHR experience and expertise possible; and
- Facilitate union grievance hearings; participate in labor management committees, and activist meetings.

Creative Life Coach

Artist Coach Wendy Lane, LLC

Professional Training & Coaching industry

February 2009 – September 2012 (3 years 8 months) Saint Paul, Minnesota

“Identifying what’s in your heart, awakening your spirit, and living creatively”. Wendy Lane is a creative life coach, visual artist and human resources professional, trained in the Co-Active coaching model through the Coaches Training Institute (CTI). Wendy has completed 196 training hours, has coached over 100 hours, and is a certified professional co-active coach (CPCC). CTI was the first organization accredited by the International Coach Federation (ICF). Wendy is a member of ICF and the Minnesota Coaches Association (MCA). Wendy adheres to ICF’s Standards of Ethical Conduct. Wendy is also certified to administer and interpret the Bar-On EQ-i® (Emotional Quotient Inventory) through Multi-Health Systems, Inc. Wendy received her certification from Crescendo, Inc.

The CLA human resources consultant serves as a member of an HR team that provides HR consultation, support, and services to the college community and leadership. The HR consultant provides one-on-one consultation and coaching to academic department chairs and directors, managers, supervisors, and other employees to identify solutions to problems and resolve issues. The consultant must be highly knowledgeable of HR policies and practices in order to provide timely and accurate information to administrators, faculty, other academic employees, civil service and bargaining unit staff. The college's HR team works in close partnership with the CLA Office of the Associate Dean for Faculty and with the University's central Office of Human Resources.
