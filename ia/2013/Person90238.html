Michael Beblo's Experience

Director of Private Equity

Alfred I. duPont Testamentary Trust

May 2003 – February 2011 (7 years 10 months)

Responsible for managing a private equity portfolio that exceeded $1 billion in commitments for a privately-held $4.7 billion charitable trust. Sat on the Advisory Boards of a number of private equity limited partnerships, and was very successful in gaining access to several “invitation only” top-tier funds over several years.

Besides covering traditional private equity areas such as venture capital, buyouts and growth equity, was instrumental to the Trust’s ramp-up in areas such as energy, natural resources, and distressed securities.

Responsible for conducting all meetings with prospective managers, go/no-go decisions to invest, follow-on due diligence, performance monitoring, benchmarking, legal review process of terms and conditions, and third-party analytics. Spearheaded the transformation of a US-centric private equity portfolio into a global portfolio by initiating private equity investments in Europe, China, India and other regions.

Contributed regularly to discussions with the Chief Investment Officer and other Directors on which active tactical bets made sense with regard to both the private and public markets. Played a key role in overall portfolio strategy discussions, and in talks dealing with prospective traditional and non-traditional manager candidates.

Chief Financial Officer

Tredegar Investments / Perennial Ventures

November 1999 – April 2003 (3 years 6 months)

Served as the focal point for organizing deal closings with attorneys and executing the sale of securities coming off of IPO lockup.

Provided oversight to the entire external due diligence process.

Accomplished all responsibilities related to financial modeling, scenario testing, and portfolio deal attribution analysis.

Accurately created monthly and ad hoc reports, which included valuation analysis, liquidity projections, cash flow modeling, and performance tracking.

Principal

Hambrecht & Quist LLC

Public Company; 501-1000 employees; Investment Banking industry

May 1998 – November 1999 (1 year 7 months)

Served as an effective Chief Financial Officer for a +$300M later-stage venture fund with more than 300 limited partners.

Ensured accuracy in producing quarterly and annual reports for the fund on a timely basis.

Rendered exceptional assistance in preparing the fund’s offering memorandum and term sheet throughout the legal review and fundraising processes.

Manager, Private Equity

California Institute of Technology

Educational Institution; 1001-5000 employees; Research industry

1996 – 1998 (2 years) Pasadena, CA

Key investment position supporting the California Institute of Technology’s endowment fund. Worked directly with the CIO and Treasurer in jump-starting a new alternative investment program.

Held the primary responsibility for making recommendations on new investments in private equity funds (venture capital, buyouts, growth equity, and distressed securities).

Served as the main point of contact for reviewing all prospective private equity funds; performed due diligence on all proposed limited partnerships; negotiated partnership terms; reviewed both established funds as well as “emerging manager” funds for possible inclusion in the portfolio.

Associate Client Executive

Frank Russell Company

Presented the most appropriate gatekeepers to clients based on their specific private equity program needs and constraints.

Functioned effectively as the main point person for questions regarding private equity within the Russell Consulting Group, and served as member of Russell’s Private Equity Practice Group.
