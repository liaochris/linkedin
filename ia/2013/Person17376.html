  • Iconstructors.com PlanRoom - Ushered In Cloud Computing

    • June 2007 to September 2007
    Team Members: Brian Galvan, Sean Pascoe

    I was originally hired to design the website for Solutions Inc. a Class A General Contractor in Tampa, Fl. During my constant visits to their offices I learned about the blueprint/drawings process of printing them, mailing them and receiving a return bid for that sub contract job segment. I learned that in the old days a GC would have to print and send out 10 sets of blueprints for examples to 10 electricians if they wanted to build a Wal Mart in Jacksonville. So now imagine the different trades involved and in one build out alone you may have nearly 1000 pages of blueprints for a project. Then the energy of FedEx or Postal Charges is also another factor of time.

    In 2007 I clearly saw the unnecessary effort and energy waste this process represented and proposed a solution. Why not place all the pdf drawings online and have the subs login and download the sets they need? Sounded simple, as FTP is relatively simple but they needed protection of files, username password protections etc...

    Using some custom server software and with my server level expertise, I launched a web application that allowed subs to login via a browser and download the needed files all while protecting the rest of the files from not being seen. They did not need access to everything, just what they needed to get.

    The end results were that the GC no longer had to print the thousands of drawings and they did not have to waste money on FedEx to send it to them either. There was an immediate cost savings that GC's typically would run in past times because that's the way the industry did business. Now they saved not just in dollars but in time. When asking Sean Pascoe, the Director of Corporate Development that if the cost savings was the best thing from it, he said no. That the best thing that came from it was that the company could bid more efficiently and rapidly from the time savings and allow them with the same staff pursue more major build projects and potentially make more money.
