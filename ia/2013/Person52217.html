Heather Esposito, IIDA Industry, LEED AP's Summary

I grew up with a mother who was an Interior Designer and can remember playing with books of wallpaper, fabric, and paint samples; however, I never imagined having a career in a related industry.

My educational background was in Accounting and Business Administration, but my true desire was to go to law school and specialize in tax law. Along the way, I discovered a true love for children and veered hard left into a career in special education and working with young children with all levels of disabilities.

In the educational field, I quickly discovered that it is hard to be a single woman on a teacher's paycheck and turned to a second job in cosmetic sales to supplement my income. I never thought I could be successful in sales, but knew I could teach. A sales person was born! In this position, I learned motivation and discipline, but most of all, that I am driven to achieve. I also became a great student in the discipline of understanding behavioral styles which led me to become a certified DISC instructor.

Through the circumstances of Hurricane Katrina, I was offered an introduction into the design industry. I thought this would be temporary, but apparently God had other plans.

Through a series of referrals, I worked my way through the industry into my current position with Herman Miller. I love consulting with architects, designers and end users to create a workplace that is not only healthy and beautiful, but increases productivity, as well. My job requires understanding a business enough to be able to help them design an environment that fits their corporate culture, while also helping them to budget, plan and manage those environments.

My life experiences give me a unique perspective with my clients. I approach every opportunity with the mind of a teacher. My goal is to teach my clients how to design a workplace that is healthy, efficient, and makes a statement about their corporate brand.

Let me show you how to make where you work, work for you!
