February 2012 – November 2013 (1 year 10 months) Canton, MA

This is an ongoing personal project. The Windows Passive Crossover Designer (WinPCD) is a complete re-write from scratch of a long-standing and widely used Excel/Visual Basic spreadsheet. It is used to design loudspeaker systems. Electrical and acoustic measurements are imported, then the user designs the crossover and can do some basic box design. David Ralph's Publications

  • Finding Relative Acoustic Offsets Empirically

    • SpeakerBuilder (now audioXpress)
    • January 2000
    Authors: David Ralph

    Describes a method for determining the relative acoustic offset between any two drivers on a panel of a loudspeaker system. This is required for accurate system modeling and design in 3-D space.

  • Diffraction Doesn't Have to be a Problem

    • audioXpress
    • June 2005
    Authors: David Ralph

    Loudspeaker systems always have an element of diffraction in the response. This article examines the typical diffraction on small drivers on a typical baffle. Various tests with measurements are presented to demonstrate a method of reducing the impact of diffraction and improving the overall acoustic response.
