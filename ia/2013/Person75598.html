Kimberly Blessing's Summary

My passion since 1994 has been building great experiences on the web. In those nearly 20 years, I've held a number of roles from developer to architect to manager and I've come to learn that great experiences aren't simply the result of good design and strong code but of the cooperation and balance of three things: people, process, and technology. I seek to work on challenging projects where all of these areas are in focus for evolution and change in order to make both teams and products better for all.

I am an advocate for an open and accessible web, and my work experience reflects a strong focus on collaborating on web-related specifications and implementing web standards in a variety of environments. Along the way, I have gained skills in mentoring, training, and developing technical teams, in designing and implementing publishing systems, and in redesigning and optimizing product and engineering processes.

Outside of my "day job", I have continued to evangelize for the open web, by participating in the Web Standards Project, by authoring and editing articles and books, and by speaking at conferences. I also write and speak on topics such as managing change and career development.

Specialties: standards-based web development, progressive enhancement, accessibility, cross-browser and platform support, adaptive and responsive web design/development, client- and server-side programming, site speed performance analysis and graphics/code optimization, search engine optimization, strengths/skills assessment and coaching for individuals and teams, process and workflow evaluation and design, curriculum development and training delivery.
