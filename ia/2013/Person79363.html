  • Hanes - Soften The Blow

    • May 2013 to Present
    Team Members: Layne Harris, Aaron Mosher, Sarah Kapoor, Nick Panayotopoulos, Roberto Max Salas

    To promote the ultra-soft quality of Hanes’ new ComfortBlendTM line of T-shirts, socks and boxer briefs, we tapped into Gen Y males’ love affair with destruction videos to show how the super soft apparel could “soften the blow” for most any type of physical destruction. The “Soften the Blow” campaign comprises of four separate web videos sharing demolition-style content that leverages the power of digital in a creative way.

    To lead the effort on camera, we brought in ‘Al Lenderson’ – a quirky guy so obsessed with ComfortBlend that he can’t wait to share it with the world – and engaged him in extreme scenarios of "smashing stuff up," asking him to wrap delicate items in Hanes ComfortBlend products to see if they’ll survive falls, crashes and even catapulted hurls at hard objects. To help Al further spread the word about his passion for ComfortBlend, the effort is being supported by paid media.

    *Curiosity Spark: "We needed to figure out a way to get men talking about Hanes – a big challenge since our research showed us that men don’t typically talk about their underwear online. We found a way to both communicate classic product benefits as well as further bring the Hanes brand into contemporary culture through a branded online video series of content that not only resonated with our target's content consumption habits, but also aligned with their content sharing culture – thus, humorous destruction videos."

  • Oreo Grab and Go Interactive Display at SXSW

    • March 2013 to Present
    Team Members: Layne Harris, Adam Gloo, Nick Bayne, Amanda Kwan

    This year, for the first time ever, Oreo headed west to SXSW to promote the brand’s new “Grab & Go” snack packs in the most dynamic way possible. 360i helped the brand build and execute a fun and unique “Grab, Guess & Go” interactive display. The experiential game utilized an interactive green screen to digitally transport Oreo snackers – with their “Grab & Go” packs in hand – around famous landmarks and hot spots in Austin.

    Here’s how it worked: Oreo fans that were able to guess the notable venue on the green screen within 30 seconds got the chance to win gift vouchers to the hottest restaurants and notable stores in town. We also engaged SX-goers to tweet a photo to be entered for the chance to meet with the conference’s biggest celebs, such as Guy Kawasaki.

    Over 1,155 attendees checked out the display, and the Huffington Post named Oreo as “the hot brand in Austin.” The effort drove 54 million earned media impressions and especially resonated with the conference’s influential crowd. People that mentioned the #OreoGrabGo effort had 25,000 Twitter followers on average.

  • H&R Block Dreamfund Sweepstakes

    • January 2013 to Present
    Team Members: Layne Harris

    H&R Block may be a well-known brand, but it was lacking awareness around its top-notch At-Home tax preparation software.

    Solution: The Dreamfund campaign invited the H&R Block Facebook community to share what they would do with their refund since they’d have more time to spend it after using the At-Home software. The campaign was designed to maximize participation and encourage social sharing to get people actually talking about At-Home and how they would spend their refund. Paid media and influencer marketing supported the effort.

    In bringing At-Home to the forefront of social conversation, 360i successfully engaged the community and fielded more than 928K sweeps entries. Not only did the campaign get people talking, but it also got people sharing – to the tune of 236K social shares.
