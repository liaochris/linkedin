Matt Raithel's Summary

I joined Black Lantern after serving the Missouri Fine Arts Academy as a concept art instructor while running an independent web development business. Since arriving at Black Lantern in 2004, I have served as an Art Director, Director of Art and Design, Vice President of Operations, and Studio President before serving in my current role as the Studio Director for the St. Louis office - Graphite Lab.

Within the past 10 years, I have held a production role in over 40 titles while helping to grow a company which includes three game development studios (Springfield, MO; Austin, TX, St. Louis, MO) with portfolio of games that span numerous platforms (PC, GBA, DS, 3DS, Wii, XBOX360, iPhone, Android, Facebook and Kindle).

I've had the pleasure of bringing some of the most famous brands to life through video games including but not limited to My Little Pony, Play-Doh, Sesame Street, Dora the Explorer, Ben 10, Garfield and the Zhu Zhu Pets.

In developing these titles I've had the honor of working with the top publishing teams in the world including Hasbro, Disney, Konami, Cartoon Network, Activision, PopCap, Universal Studios, Nickelodeon, Mattel, Hasbro, High Voltage Software, THQ, 2KPLAY, Warner Brothers, ValuSoft, GameMill, Dreamcatcher Games, Destineer, ZOO Games and Tecmo.

As an advocate for the video game industry, I speak on topics such as gaming violence and game production at major universities throughout the state. I hold a BS degree in Electronic Art from Missouri State University, and have taught classes in animation on both the college and university level.
