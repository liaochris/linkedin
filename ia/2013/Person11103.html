Avil Beckford's Summary

Ambeck Enterprise produces content and expert interviewing.

___________________________________________________________________________

Avil Beckford, Chief Mentoring Officer and founder of Ambeck Enterprise, is a skilled writer, blogger, researcher, analyst and published author. She is also the founder of The Invisible Mentor http://theinvisiblementor.com a learning spot for professionals.

Her ability to conduct interviews, find, evaluate and synthesize data from a variety of sources, has served her clients well over the years. Avil’s calmness makes her interviewees immediately relax. Her clients also appreciate her content production skills because she is detail-oriented.

Knowledge management is top-of-mind these days for leaders. For companies facing the departure of a large number of senior executives who are retiring in the next three to five years, Avil can interview them to capture institutional knowledge.

Avil works with CEOs to give them a start on writing their memoirs by conducting in-depth interviews with them. They can pass on their knowledge, wisdom and deep insights to the world, and build a legacy. She's interested in working with CEOs who want to write their memoirs and need help with starting their writing project.
__________________________________________________________________________

She is a voracious reader who loves to read books off the beaten path written decades and sometimes centuries ago. Avil loves Goethe’s quote that “Everything has been thought of already but the problem is to think of it again.” She is an advocate of continuous learning and loves to teach others what she has learned. Avil also loves to travel and has been to China, across the US, Caribbean Islands and Canadian cities.

Originally from Jamaica, with stints in Calgary and New York, I now live in Toronto, Canada. I am the President of Ambeck Enterprise, a firm that specializes in producing a variety of content. An expert interviewer, researcher and writer, with 18 year of experience using interviewing as a data gathering and knowledge management tool, I combined my skills to craft Tales of People Who Get It and its companion workbook, Journey to Getting It.

An advocate for people taking control of their professional development, I founded The Invisible Mentor (http://theinvisiblementor.com) to provide an alternative mentoring model where professionals get the information and resources they need to mentor themselves. Through SummaReviews – hybrid book summaries and reviews, profiles of wise people, and interviews with highly accomplished people, I create a safe place for self-learning. I also founded Leaders Are Great Readers to share my love for reading.

Recognizing that relationships are the foundation for success, I have facilitated the process on several occasions by interviewing members of a community so they can share their stories with each other. I also conduct expert interviews as a way to create a legacy, transferring knowledge from one generation to the next. I use interviews as a knowledge management tool for future leaders to benefit from the experiences and deep insights of present leaders.
