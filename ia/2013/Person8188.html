Annalisa Burgos' Summary

Multimedia journalist with 13+ years of experience as a field reporter, news writer, features writer, on-air talent, show producer, video editor, copy editor, assignment editor and managing editor. Proven record of launching profitable products and businesses on time and within budget under intense deadline pressure and designing and implementing production efficiencies. Strong commitment to innovation and calculated risk taking, complemented by excellent leadership, team building and negotiation skills.

My story: I started off as a local news reporter and editor in the newspaper industry and took on roles in magazine writing and editing. I studied broadcasting at Columbia University's Graduate School of Journalism and learned to tell stories for radio and TV, from short-form 1:30 news packages to long-form documentaries. In 2004, I became one of the first online video anchors and field reporters in the industry, booking and interviewing celebrities, senior executives and newsmakers for Forbes.com and later CNBC.com.

I took on increased management responsibility in a senior role at Scripps Networks, where I developed and produced award-winning digital content for the company's home category and served as the editorial lead for HGTV's real estate site FrontDoor.com and HGTVRemodels.com. I honed my on-air, live reporting skills at Bloomberg TV, where I produced and anchored business news updates for local markets across the country and India's UTV. Now a mom of two and a frequent traveler, I am an independent writer and producer/host, covering topics I love, such as food, tech and real estate.

Career highlights: Chasing gangbangers in SoCal. Telling President Bill Clinton he's "da bomb." Interviewing a convicted murderer in prison. Talking movies with Morgan Freeman. Busting myths of the "Sound of Music" with a von Trapp family member. Reporting from the floor of the New York Stock Exchange.
