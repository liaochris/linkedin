Dr. Nancy O'Reilly, Psy.D's Summary

Claim your power. I am passionately committed to helping women find the power to live their life -- which can be obtained by feeling more personally secure, connected, belonging and loved.

I have training and experience as a Clinical Psychologist, Certified Employee Assistance Professional, Traumatologist, Crisis Responder, Mediator, and Life Coach. All this experience has helped me understand that mentoring and empowering women is the best thing we can do for the women themselves, for their families, their countries and the world.

Through my radio podcast interviews, website, speeches, philanthropy, mentoring, fundraising, and other volunteer work I focus on mentoring, connecting and empowering women to change the world for the better and to move women into more powerful leadership roles.

I post Internet podcast interviews with smart amazing women, which are increasingly devoted to linking together women who are using their time, talents and treasure to change the world.

Founder and Director

Women Connect4Good Foundation

January 1998 – Present (16 years 1 month) Internet and Springfield, MO

"Nancy has managed to do so much in such a classy way! Watching her as she worked with first the Backpack Program and now Suit Yourself has been inspirational! She probably can't even imagine the lives she has touched in such positive ways. From the little boy excited and relieved to have a backpack full of food to tide him over the weekend to a very relieved woman trying to rebuild her life and yet not having the appropriate clothing for a job interview, she has indeed made hundreds of people's lives better. Her presence for women of all ages through her written work and radio work has crossed many barriers and opened many subjects that women have needed to talk about and express. She is leading the way to better lives for so many people."
Roseann Bentley, former Missouri State Senator

In 1997 I was concerned about the lack of information and mentoring available to help women have a good experience as they were growing older. My research as a clinical psychologist established benchmarks for women's expectations and perception, and I'm proud to have been published in the Journal of the American Society for Health Behavior and to have presented my results at an American Psychological Association Annual conference.

In recent years, however, WomenSpeak has become a nonprofit service dedicated to helping women of all ages to thrive as they pursue their joy and share their time, talents and treasure to make the world a better place. Our website includes a Women Connect4Good section, profiles of success, personal stories, helpful articles and tips, research findings, surveys, and more. It is my intention to make connections among the many smart, amazing women I meet and to help them share information to empower and mentor other women.
