Content Designer

38 Studios

Privately Held; 201-500 employees; Computer Games industry

February 2011 – January 2012 (1 year) Providence, Rhode Island Area

After moving North along the East coast, I assumed a position as a Content Designer working on the Kingdoms of Amalur MMO. There I joined a passionate and enthusiastic team comprised of over 150 developers (that would grow to nearly 300,) which was a huge change compared to the smaller teams I had worked with previously.

As a Content Designer I was responsible for helping develop overland zones from concept to completion; performing as either a supporting designer or the zone principle based on the needs of the team. The quality of my work, as well as my growing familiarity of our proprietary state machine scripting system, led me to eventually transition to the Events Team.

Key Tasks:
Zone Design, Quest Design, Populating Environments, Gameplay Scripting

Project:
Kingdoms of Amalur MMO (PC - Shelved)

Lead Game Designer

n-Space

Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Computer Games industry

June 2009 – October 2010 (1 year 5 months) Orlando, Florida Area

After completing MUA2, our former Lead Designer Tim Schwalk stepped into a Producer role which created an opening for Lead Designer of which I was appointed. It was an early point in my career and there were many other worthy designers on our team, so taking over as lead was exciting as well as daunting.

Only a few years prior I had created a tribute to the original Discs of Tron, so having the opportunity to help develop an actual sequel to the original Tron Arcade game really was a dream come true. I worked diligently to scope the game realistically and for our limited resources we delivered an exceptionally fun split screen experience without having to endure excessive overtime.

Unfortunately, a number of projects wrapped up around the same time and n-Space was unable to secure additional contracts to keep us employees working. After a few rounds of partial layoffs, the owner was forced to release everyone from the company. After eventually recovering, n-Space was able to bring back a number of employees; I was offered a position but had already agreed to terms with 38 Studios.

Key Tasks:
Systems Design, Level Design, AI Tuning, Scheduling, Tasking

Title(s):
Tron: Evolution - Battle Grids (Wii - Released December 7th, 2010)
Golf Cart Ranger (iPhone - Released November 5th, 2010)
