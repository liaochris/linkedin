VP Software Development

Multiply

Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Internet industry

2011 – December 2012 (1 year) Boca Raton

Subsequent to Naspers’ acquisition of controlling interest in Multiply, I became responsible for architecting ecommerce functionality on top of the existing social network platform. The site currently supports approximately 200,000 merchants in Indonesia and Philippines. Key features include a multi-vendor shopping cart, multi-payment transactions, multi-currency support, asynchronous offline transactions and fully customizable merchant sites.

My development staff grew by eight as I led the team in an effort to integrate numerous payment systems in Indonesia and the Philippines. These systems include mobile payment systems GCash, SmartMoney and XL Tunia and credit card processors Infintium, AsiaPay and PayPal.

Additional efforts included the design of direct integration between the ecommerce platform and four major South East Asian banks (BDO, BPI, Mandiri and BCA) which allowed for automated collections using ATMs and OTC, as well as automated disbursals to merchants.

Further responsibilities include the generation of accounting reports, business metrics and KPIs to facilitate corporate level accountability with Naspers’ global requirements.

Director of Technology

Multiply

Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Internet industry

December 2003 – October 2011 (7 years 11 months) Boca Raton, FL

As one of the founders and primary developers of Multiply.com, a social networking media-sharing web site, I designed and implemented the sites’s architecture from scratch. It grew to support 20 million registered users with 30 million page views and 1.6M unique visitors per day. At peak our users uploaded 2.5 million photos and 20,000 videos daily.

I reported directly to the CEO, and managed six developers.

I was responsible for site wide performance and management of key subsystems:

Fast disk array storage: 12TB customer meta-data
Media storage: 2 PB nfs farm of dell 2950's and Dell md1000 shelves
Web Application: serving 100 million images day, 30 million pages of dynamic user-driven content..
Uploads (~2.5 million photos, and 20,000 videos daily),
Internal messaging: delivering 500M messages a day w/ 80% <10s latency
Media caching: cycling 1.4TB of data per day
API (REST based, ~1 million unique sessions per day

The startup nature of the business also required me to serve as the lead mysql DBA, managing and monitoring an installation comprised of three major components. First a multi-master/slave layer for user meta-information that averages 2000 queries per second and 200 writes per second. Second the user inbox databases, segmented across 200 hosts containing 6 billion messages. And finally a series of reporting and report roll-up databases that contain business related and systems performance metrics.

Owner

TriMeros

July 2000 – January 2008 (7 years 7 months) Minneapolis, MN / Washington, DC / Fort Lauderdale, FL

I started, self-financed and managed this company that provided voter database management software for political parties.

I developed, implemented and maintained a mod_perl/mysql application that allowed users to data mine state-wide and national voter registration databases using web browser and integrated maps. The application output data in “walkable” PDF format, meaning chunks of data were organized into reports that would allow volunteers to walk a door to door route.

My clients included the Clark Presidential Campaign, Dean Presidential Campaign, DNC, Minnesota DFL, North Dakota NPL, South Dakota Democratic Party, Arizona Democratic Party, Kentucky Democratic Party, State of California, Iowa Democratic Party, Washington State Democrats, South Carolina Democratic Party and the DSCC.
