Fred J. Bourgeois, III's Summary

Fred J. Bourgeois, III is the creator of Open Source Space Exploration, and the Founder & CEO of "Team FREDNET, The Open Space Society Inc." He began planning for space exploration from the age of two. Growing up in a "NASA family" probably had a lot to do with that. Originally from Waveland, Mississippi, Bourgeois spent most of his childhood and pre-college years in Huntsville, Alabama, where he attended Virgil I. "Gus" Grissom High School along with scores of other NASA kids. Bourgeois was early recognized for his mathematical talents, and went on to receive his BSCS degree from Tulane University's School of Engineering and continued his graduate education at the Tulane Graduate School. His academic career began with teaching Computer Science at the University of Minnesota, then at Eaton Corporation's Information Management Systems Division in Westlake Village, CA, followed by several University and extension programs. Bourgeois' work at Eaton and as a contractor at NASA's Jet Propulsion Laboratory included satellite systems, networks, and command, control & communications (C^3I) programs. He re-entered the commercial software world as a Senior Compiler Developer for MetaWare Inc. in Santa Cruz, California (near Silicon Valley), and later branched into his own software development and consulting business, FREDNET. Now operating for more than 20 years, the company was renamed Applios Inc. (Applied Open Source Technology) in 1999 and continues to provide Software Development, Systems Engineering, Networking, and Information Security solutions today. Applios' current and former clients include AMD, Lockheed Martin, NEC, Resilience, SJSU, Sun Micro, Transmeta, Web Side Story, as well as many Silicon Valley startups, and local, regional, and national Network Service Providers.

Fred J. Bourgeois, III is the Founder and Leader of Team FREDNET, a NASA Innovative Lunar Demonstrations Data Contractor and a leading contender for the Google Lunar X PRIZE. Ad lunam!
