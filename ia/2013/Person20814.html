Casper Bodewitz's Projects

  • Contracting and Billing for Gas (CoBi-G)

    • January 2004 to December 2005
    Team Members: Casper Bodewitz, Ben Tels, Rudi Hendrix, Erik Van Berkel, Victor Morassi, Michelle Maloy, Sander van Loon, Marcel Hundt, Oliver Paatz, Robert Scholz

    GasTerra B.V. (formerly GasUnie) is the major gas shipper in The Netherlands. Founded as a cooperation of Royal Dutch/Shell, Exxon and the Dutch Government, it supplies all the Dutch utilities companies and major industries with gas.

    As a result of European deregulation of the utilities market, GasUnie was split into a network management company (GasUnie Transport Services (GTS)) and a gas seller (GasUnie, later GasTerra) in 2004. As part of this split, the newly formed GasTerra decided to implement a completely new billing system to charge customers for their gas.

    The Contracting and Billing for Gas (CoBi-G) project implemented this contracting and billing system in the Lodestar Customer Choice Suite (now owned by Oracle). This product is aimed specifically at the utilities industry as a contracting, billing, prediction and balancing solution.

    My role on the project was as a junior and intermediate level developer. During the project I:

    * developed several billing modules within the Lodestar Rules and Write language;
    * developed core system code to allow billing modules to be associated dynamically with contract types and executed on contract basis;
    * developed core system code to introduce variable and module scoping to the LR&W language to support module development;
    * developed several utility programs in Visual Basic to enable automating maintenance tasks around the core system.

    In addition I had my first experience as a coach for other developers on this project.

    Within my role I also learned the basics of the gas industry, which gave me enough common ground with the GasUnie business personnel associated with the project that I was able to collaborate with them on module design and development through a common domain language.
