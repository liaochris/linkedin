Donald Bottelsen's Projects

  • IBM System/z IMT level analysis

    • January 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Donald Bottelsen

    Problem
    IBM’s largest profit engine, the mainframe/System Z was having difficulty gaining new customers. Traditional sales/marketing techniques weren’t working creating significant revenue and profit gaps.

    Solution
    Developed and ran 21 individual country exercises consisting of analytic models, statistical analysis, competitive intelligence and 2 day strategic workshops in each country to identify barriers and actions. Each workshop was attended by the GM’s in each country. 78 sub-projects were created.

    Result
    “Revenues from System z mainframe server products increased 56 percent. Total delivery of System z computing power increased 66 percent representing the largest quarter in the company's history.” “Revenue in the growth markets improved 68 percent.” IBM’s 4Q Earnings report.

  • Pure Systems Category Development and Launch

    • June 2011 to Present
    Team Members: Donald Bottelsen

    Problem
    IBM could be a huge player in a maturing market we identified early called converged systems.

    Solution
    Worked with marketing and product dev. to test competitiveness, value propositions, configurations, pricing, positioning, messaging, and naming for the new product in preparation for the launch, all through numerous, and quick turn-around qualitative and quantitative surveys. We also identified first touch prospects through analytic models.

  • Revenue Concentration analysis and Segmentation
