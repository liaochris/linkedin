Alexandra Kosteniuk's Summary

Chess Grandmaster & Model, holds title of 12th Women's World Chess Champion. Also Podcaster, Blogger & Educator. Author of “Diary of a Chess Queen”.

♕ Holds with her husband the Trademarks to the brands CHESS QUEEN and CHESS KING and CHESS KILLER TIPS

Won the 2008 Women's World Chess Championship in Nalchik Russia and received world's top chess award.

Since 2006 holds the title of Women's Chess960 World Champion (won in 2006, defended successfully in 2008).

Earned several top spots and medals playing for the Russian teams in Chess Olympiads, European and World Team Championships, and Mind Sport Games.

US Open Blitz Champion 2009.

In 2004, became the 10th woman in history to earn the Men's Grandmaster title, highest in chess.

Top World Chess Promoter. Awarded the Chess Journalist of the Year prize in 2009.

Acted in full-length feature movie "Bless the Woman" by director Stanislav Govorukhin.

Has appeared on the cover of over 60 chess and non-chess publications.

Modeling photo shoots for magazines such as Vogue, Elle, Officiel, thousands of articles in worldwide press

Advertising contracts with LG Electronics, Balmain Watches, Zimaletto.

Alexandra Kosteniuk's Publications

  • Diary of a Chess Queen

    • Mongoose Press
    • January 15, 2009
    Authors: Alexandra Kosteniuk

    Women’s World Chess Champion Alexandra Kosteniuk chronicles her rise to the top of the chess world in this introspective autobiographical work. Drawing from personal diaries kept during her youth, Kosteniuk takes the reader from the very dawn of her career as a child star in Russia, through triumph and disappointment, and finally to the pinnacle of success on the black-and-white battlefield. Along the way, we are treated to much more than an inside look into how a grandmaster approaches the royal game: we also learn the unique challenges posed to a young woman pulled at once by the diverging demands of professional chess, the glamour of the modeling lifestyle, and the joys of love and family life.

    Part memoir and travelogue, part game collection, Diary of a Chess Queen features a selection of 64 annotated games with a wide range of world-class competitors, including male super-GM Sergey Karjakin and former female world champions Zhu Chen and Antoaneta Stefanova.
