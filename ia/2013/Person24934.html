Cindy Battisti's Summary

Opera Bracelets: Opera Gifts and Jewelry
cindy@operabracelets.com
cindy@dyzines.com

More than just bracelets, my pieces are wearable works of art that symbolically tell the stories of operas. Each bead or charm represents a character, aria or event in the plot. Opera Bracelets are perfect keepsakes or gifts for passionate opera lovers seeking to "Remember the Moment" they fell in love with opera.

For more information please visit our website at www.operabracelets.com

Specialties: memory wire bead jewelry, opera gifts, opera jewelry, opera

Customer Service

1989 – 1996 (7 years)

Answer phone...Find out what customer needs...pull off shelf and put in box...ship. It was a lot more fun than it sounds. Some of the happiest days of my life were hanging out in their basement, chatting with regular customers, getting their stuff, listening to the radio and shipping it out. But the art industry didn't see computers coming and the need for commercial art supplies dried up. When the job became more retail it was the end of happy days.

Cake Decorator

Decorated cakes and pastries- now that WAS FUN! Would have stayed forever but pins and needles in my forearms were a sign it was time to stop. But the Wegman's policy of ripping managers out just when you had proved what a wonderful employee you were to them was really too emotionally difficult to put up with forever also...

Cindy Battisti's Education

Nazareth College of Rochester

BS, Studio Art

1985 – 1989

I had a great time, lovely teachers, a few good friends... but probably the wiser move would have been to go to one of the Art Institutes to get an employable skill. Nazareth was into ART but ART doesn't feed you by itself.

Activities and Societies: ha...who had time.. I was working too

Richmond, The American International University in London

History, Architecture, Northern Renaissance Painting

1988 – 1988

Simply the best experience of my life. Everyone should do at least a semester abroad someplace...maybe a year...get a visa that lets you work a little so you can be a part of everyday life. Do not spend your days hanging out with other Americans go hang out at bars and cafes to get to know locals, pick a couple places and be a "regular".

Mercy High School

1981 – 1985

Pretty much everyday I am shocked to discover how much I don't know: the Chinese Cultural revolution; the Civil War; World War 1 and so much more. I have a hard time remembering what I did learn (Except for Japan-so many of my teachers seemed to be obsessed with it). I am so disappointed in this overly expensive private school education. I wish I could get my Dad his money back.

AND WHY READ WERTHER WHEN YOU COULD READ FAUST????
