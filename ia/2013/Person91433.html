Menekse Gencer's Summary

Experienced executive in mobile payments working with banks, alternative payments companies, MNOs, OEMs, mobile wallet platform companies, governments, and development agencies. Formerly, executive at PayPal Mobile. Expertise in mobile financial services for the unbanked in developed and developing markets. Dynamic background in driving success from 3-person startups to Fortune 500 firms. Multiple awards for previous performance. Chosen by State Department as 1 of 38 TechWomen mentors to support President Obama's vision for greater collaboration with the Middle East. Chosen as an Advisor for the Bill and Melinda Gates Technology Partner Network for her expertise in Mobile Financial Services. Advisor and consultant to startups: bKash, KopoKopo, Simpa Networks. Media-trained, spokesperson, and conference speaker. Fluent in Turkish. Wharton M.B.A. and Harvard B.A. in Economics.

Menekse Gencer's Publications

  • Amplifying the Impact: Examining the Intersection of Mobile Health and Mobile Finance

    • The World Economic Forum
    • January 26, 2011
    Authors: Menekse Gencer, Menekse Gencer

    This report was written by Menekse Gencer of mPay Connect which identifies commonalities between mobile health and mobile finance and how each sector can benefit from the other. It was published for the Davos Summit of The World Economic Forum in 2011.

  • Advancing the Dialog on Mobile Finance and Mobile Health

    • The mHealth Alliance
    • February 11, 2012
    Authors: Menekse Gencer, Menekse Gencer, Jody Ranck

    This was the second report around mobile finance and mobile health written by Menekse Gencer of mPay Connect for The mHealth Alliance and published for Mobile World Congress in 2012. It examines four country case studies which have used mobile finance to advance healthcare in emerging markets.

  • The Mobile Money Movement: Catalyst to Jumpstart Emerging Markets

    • Innovations Journal- MIT Press
    • January 7, 2011
    Authors: Menekse Gencer

    This report was written by Menekse Gencer of mPay Connect and analyzes the forces of mobile money and the reasons why mobile money will positively impact the per capita GDP of emerging countries.

  • Mobile Money: The Foundation for Food Security

    • Innovations Journal, MIT Press
    • April 5, 2012
    Authors: Menekse Gencer

    This article was written by Menekse Gencer of mPay Connect and was published in MIT Press' "Innovations Journal" in 2012. It describes the reasons why mobile financial services are vital to food security efforts and highlights a few players in the market. Ms. Gencer has subsequently provided consulting services to enable organizations to provide mobile financial services to small holder farmers in Africa by taking into account the agricultural supply chain, partners (banks, MNOs, COOPs), and vendor providers in various geographies.
