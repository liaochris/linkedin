Andrew Benson Greene Jr's Experience

Founder and CEO

B-Gifted Foundation

October 2007 – Present (5 years 10 months)

Andrew Benson Greene has set himself apart from others in his field. He is Founder and CEO of B-Gifted foundation of Sierra Leone. Andrew Greene has 10+ years experience in the non-profit sector, youth and technology and the issues of child soldiers and human rights of children in areas of conflict. He has traveled world-wide, from Beijing to Moscow, to Washington, to share his vision of using information technology amongst children and war scarred youth. A referee once noted that: 'Mr. Greene's efforts have had a profound effect on the minds of young people around the world, from Sierra Leone to Cambodia to Afghanistan. His efforts attracted the United Nations to his project as a model to be used in countries affected by war". Mr. Greene contributed enormously as a Guest Speaker to the CIDA in 2004 during the International Corporation Days Millennium Development Goals: Moving from Consensus to Momentum. Mr. Greene has gathered multiple awards, citations, and bursaries for his peace building efforts and Internet-based innovations. For several years in Sierra Leone, he has overseen and coordinated the successfully implementation of War Child Canada DFAIT funded project No War Zone. Andrew was a distinguished Sauvé scholar at McGill University, Montreal Canada. Mr. Greene was contracted twice by the World Bank in DC to produce video documentaries for the World Development Report 2007 focusing on youth unemployment in Sierra Leone and the ‘Next Generation. In 2005, he made a significant contribution to the book, 'Raise Your Voice, Lend A Hand, Change The World' written by Susan Hughes and produced by Scholastic Canada. He holds a BA in English Civil Law and International Relations and is currently a double Masters Candidate for an MA Program in Coexistence and Conflict in US. Andrew is a real trailblazer. Another referee noted: "It’s not just that Andrew Greene is mold breaking. What’s most important is that he is a powerful model for other young African leaders".

Andrew Benson Greene Jr's Projects

  • Support to Survivors of Amputation

    • August 2010 to Present
    Team Members: Andrew Benson Greene Jr, Abass Victor Osman Kamara

    The Project Support to survivors of amputation is developed to enhanced self-esteem and body image restructuring, and create synergies between group participants . The program was further designed to enhance the ability of amputees to participate in the creation of a peaceful society.

  • Digital Hope

    • January 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Andrew Benson Greene Jr, Abass Victor Osman Kamara, Regina M. Sipos

    The project's aim is to use ICT as tool to creating an inclusive society. The project is innovative because war victims whose arms and limbs were chopped off during the war are for the first time given new ease of hope of access through access to technology which was not the case before. The project is helping to bridge the ‘digital divide’ and contribute to an inclusive information society by targeting an approximately 20,000 amputee war victims marginalized from information technology. The project is creating impact as it is giving war amputated victims a new voice through ICT tools and a way to learn skills with a potential for employment and empowerment, and a chance to bear witness and share their war time stories to the world thereby creating attention to their plight, hope and healing.

  • Child Soldiers Project

    • November 1999 to September 2007
    Team Members: Andrew Benson Greene Jr, Anita Townsend, Connie Linder

    The child soldiers project encourages children and youth affected by war to come into contact with other young people and exchange ideas on peace by means of telecommunication technology. The child soldiers project has its root in a friendship between to educators from opposite ends of the Earth. Andrew Benson Greene from Sierra Leone and Canadian teacher Bill Belsey of iEARN Canada. The two never met in person until July of 2000 when Andrew Presented his ideas on peace building, conflict resolution and human rights in the 21st Century at an annual international conference hosted by IEARN in Beijing China. Throughout Andrew's workshops and presentations which Bill attended at the China Hall of Science and tecnology, the two were able to discuss what they can do to help the youth of Sierra Leone transformed from the agonies of war. the result was the collaborative and successful 'child soldiers project' which till date has touched the lives and heart of so many people. In 2002, the UN office for Displaced persons in NY considered it as a potential model to be used in four other countries affected by war.
