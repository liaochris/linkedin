Kevin Cullis' Summary

Kevin is the Chief Business Geek at MainStreetpreneur.com, independently published author of "How to Start a Business: Mac Version," Entrepreneur, and Startup, Small Business, and Technology Evangelist and Author.

Kevin is:

1. Analytical - able to see patterns and connections and their affects.
2. A learner - thrives in dynamic work environments for short project assignments to learn a lot in a short period of time.
3. Loves ideas - fascinated by ideas, always looking for connections, intrigued by disparate phenomena that can be linked by an obscure connection. Loves to “connect the dots” of situations.
4. Action orientated - make a decision, take action, and then your next action.
5. An extrovert - enjoys the challenge of meeting new people, strangers energize Kevin, loves breaking the ice and making a connection. There are no strangers, only friends Kevin has not met yet.

Kevin loves helping startups, entrepreneurs and businesses integrate, utilize, and optimize the use of their IT technology in their social media, marketing, sales, business, and financial management processes. The combination of business and computer process perspectives makes him unique in both making and saving money using IT and Internet technologies for businesses.

Specialties: B2B, B2C, Business Development, Sales, Technical Writer, Technology Evangelist, Social Media, Sales and Business Operations, Mac/Linux/Windows experience, WordPress blogging, business and computer process improvement, book publishing.

Unfortunately, many entrepreneurs resist technology and never fully maximize what their computers can do. From creating a business model to tracking cash flow ... from designing presentations to recording and publishing videos ...     How to make money with your Mac.
    How to set up your business the right way the first time.
    How to implement your business model and get your first customer.
    How to start a business at home or college, or open a medical or law office.
    How to use a Mac’s built-in software to advertise and attract new customers.
    And much, much more!

Aircraft Maintenance Officer

US Air Force (Air Combat Command)

Government Agency; 10,001+ employees; Defense & Space industry

January 1984 – 1992 (8 years)

In 1992, Kevin left the US Air Force as an Aircraft Maintenance Officer (Tactical Air Command and later the Strategic Air Command to become the Air Combat Command) where he monitored, managed, directed, and controlled Wing maintenance production and resources for 19 B-52 and 18 KC-135 aircraft using 1100 personel to meet mission requirements at Wurtsmith AFB, MI during the first Gulf War. As the Logistics Quality Assurance Officer and later the Wing's foremost authority in Quality Assurance principles and practices he was instrumental in reducing costs and saving valuable tax dollars as well as improving the overall mission capabilities in 6 squadrons totalling 1500 people in 12 different work specialties while communicating with all levels of management. Previous to his time at Wurtsmith, he was stationed at Bitburg, Germany where he was responsible for 24 F-5C/D aircraft valued at $1.5 Billion and oversaw 840 personnel and equipment valued at $15 Million. His squadron won the coveted USAF Maintenance Effectiveness Award in 1989, the top award.
