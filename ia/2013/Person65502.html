Joel Litman's Summary

Joel Litman is Managing Director at Equity Analysis & Strategy, Inc. and chairs the EAS Group which includes EAS Digital, EAS Incera, and the Institute of Strategy & Valuation. He advises clients in fundamental credit and equity analysis and the investment process. Litman has held Manager/Director positions with Credit Suisse, Diamond Technology Partners (now PwC), Deloitte Consulting, HOLT Value Associates, and American Express. Litman is a member of the Board of Directors of COL Financial Group (PSE:COL) a high-growth publicly-traded financial services firm in Asia.

In addition to co-authoring the book, “DRIVEN,” he has served as editor or author of articles in Harvard Business Review, Strategic Finance, and other publications.

Litman has taught and/or guest-lectured at Harvard Business School, University of Chicago, MIT Sloan, Wharton, London Business School, SAIF Jiang Tong, and others. He has conducted seminars and keynotes for organizations such as CalPERs, Fidelity, AON, CSFB, HSBC, PwC, Oracle, CFA CPA NYSSA and NIRI trade groups, and the US State Dept. and US Armed Forces.

In curriculum development, he helped build Credit Suisse's HOLT University for investors and executives, the S.E.V. Concentration for DePaul's Kellstadt MBA Program, and the recently launched C.S.V. designation - Certified in Strategy & Value-Creation.

Intellectual property has included the Signal Flag patent as well as trademarks/copyrights of Return Driven Strategy©, Genuine Assets™, and Strategic Valuation™.

Litman is a member of the Global CFA Institute for Chartered Financial Analysts. He received a B.S in Accounting from DePaul University. He is a CPA (Certified Public Accountant) and holds an MBA/MM from the Kellogg Graduate School of Management at Northwestern University.

Litman’s most important work is in assisting under-developed communities through life-cycle programs with multi-level scholarships, job training, and microfinance lending, particularly in the Philippines.

Joel Litman's Publications

  • DRIVEN: Business Strategy, Human Actions, and the Creation of Wealth

    • S&E LLC
    • 2007
    Authors: Joel Litman

    Book describing the Return Driven Strategy framework for business strategy, planning, and evaluation. Highlights the decade-long research project studying over 10,000 companies' cash flow performance and sources of good and bad cash flow results.

  • Give My Regrets to Wall Street

    • Harvard Business Review
    • February 2004
    Authors: Joel Litman

    Case study investigating the benefits, problems and issues of companies going public - or going private.

  • When Strategy and Valuation Meet

    • Strategic Finance
    • August 2004
    Authors: Joel Litman

    An article highlighting the importance of business strategy skills for the valuation analyst, and the importance of valuation skills for business strategists and leaders.

  • When Cash Is Not Cash and Why

    • Institute of Strategy & Valuation
    • January 2010
    Authors: Joel Litman

    Cites the many issues and total unreliability of the Statement of Cash Flows for virtually any user of the financial statements.

  • Can Higher Free Cash Flow Be a Sell Signal?

    • Institute of Strategy & Valuation
    • 2010
    Authors: Joel Litman

    Discusses the many problems with using "Free Cash Flow" as a single period performance metric when its primary purpose is in DCF valuations. Very often, higher free cash flows do not signal an improving business, but one that has lost its growth options.

  • Genuine Assets: Building Blocks of Strategy and Sustainable Competitive Advantage

    • Strategic Finance
    • 2000
    Authors: Joel Litman

  • Why Great Stocks Aren't Always Great Companies...

    • Strategic Finance
    • 2002
    Authors: Joel Litman

    Highlights the all-important difference between a great company and a great stock. Stock prices go up when investors' expectations of the company's future go up, regardless of the quality of the firm.
