Brian Troiano's Projects

  • Order Motion (OMX) Order Management/CRM Suite

    • September 2011 to December 2011
    Team Members: Brian Troiano, David Kaplan, Lynn Higgins, Gene Godick, Fred Swain

    It was a grueling system selection to find a replacement for Tafford's legacy Ecometry ERP system to enable our marketing team to expand the types of promotions offered and allow for the addition of 50k stock units to our product offerings. We selected Order Motion (OMX), which is a SaaS platform that allowed us to move away from the traditional in-house client-server environment to a cloud based solution. OMX also allowed us to stretch our marketing wings to offer better controls over promotions and the reporting of each campaign.

    Another business initiative that OMX was able to perform was the ability to drop ship products that traditionally we needed to bring in-house and assume an inventory position. Having the ability to drop ship products added roughly 50K stock units to our product offerings, thus increasing our sales volume
    .
    Moving off of our legacy system was no easy task. Given the age disparity between OMX and our legacy ERP, required both teams to get creative around the data needed to populate OMX, which would interface directly with a new eCommerce platform to go into production at the same time.
    In a little under 4 months, we were able to interface OMX with our new eCommerce platform and legacy ERP which would handle the warehouse management (WMS) functions, to be replaced in the months to follow.

  • ASPDOTNET Storefront eCommerce Platform

    • September 2011 to December 2011
    Team Members: Brian Troiano, David Kaplan, Lynn Higgins, Gene Godick, Fred Swain

    After selecting a new cloud based order management system (OMX) we needed a simple, flexible eCommerce platform. We had experience with ASPDOTNETSF and a development company (Enet Partners) which helped us build a second website in the past, we re-engaged Enet again with the new Tafford.com.
    OMX drove category content and product management for all products listed on the site. Integrating our legacy warehouse management system (WMS) to OMX for inventory and order information, which then integrated to the eCommerce platform was crucial.
    The new eCommerce platform allowed marketing to generate successful onsite & offsite promotions, track conversion rates in greater detail. The platform also allowed marketing to test new 3rd party marketing programs aimed at increasing conversion rates and creating an optimal customer experience. In addition to greater controls over marketing promotions, the site allowed us to offer over 50k products to our brand savvy customers.
    Hosted offsite at Rackspace in our own cloud environment, all images served using Rackspace's Cloud(CDN) Network. Increased page load times due to hosting images "in the cloud" lead to a better customer experience and higher conversion rates. I chose Rackspace due to the ability to scale the infrastructure quickly and easily as business dictates. Rackspace also left the burden of maintaining the environment outside of our internal infrastructure and support staff.
    The result of simultaneous implementations of the eCommerce platform and order management platforms meant keeping the projects moving along together for a single go live date was critical. Project management and internal/external collaboration was the key to the success of our new ecommerce site. Having the right team in place to manage each step of the project played a big role in hitting the go live target date, start to finish in less than 3 months. Upon go live we saw a significant increase in conversion rates and customer satisfaction.

  • Relocate Tafford's Corporate Offices & Warehouse Infrastructure

    • January 2012 to March 2012
    Team Members: Brian Troiano

    In a short period of time, I was tasked to acquire the hardware and services necessary in order to move Tafford's offices and warehouse infrastructure to a new building. For business continuity reasons, the new infrastructure needed to be fully functional before anything was physically moved. The new building was a complete shell needing data wiring, office build outs and warehouse racking installed.
    Knowing our new environment was not going to new be very complex, I chose a stack of Dell Layer 3 switches for VOIP phones, workstations, servers, printers, and Cisco wireless access points to run the warehouse devices. Each network segment was in its own VLAN for optimum control over network security and performance. A Sonicwall NSA firewall/VPN device connected the new location to our old data center (legacy ERP mainframe) in order to allow us to setup the warehouse racking before any products were brought over. This connection also stayed in place once we moved as we were still utilizing our old WMS system for warehouse inventory, picking, packing, and shipping.
    After just implementing two new 100% cloud-based business applications and a third new cloud-based WMS implementation underway, I chose two ISP's and setup WAN Failover/Load Balancing between the two to keep our applications running. We chose a cloud based VOIP solution which required its own ISP which I beefed up to utilize in the event of our primary data connection was offline.
    All installations and configurations occurred over a few short days while we moved one building to another without interrupting the normal flow of business.

  • Deposco - Cloud-Based Warehouse Management System Integration

    • January 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Brian Troiano, Gene Godick

    Tafford's aging legacy Ecometry warehouse management system was the last application we needed to replace. For the past 10 years Ecometry (MPE/IX) had been customized to suit our unique processes for picking, sorting and packing orders. Traditionally picking is done on a “by order” basis, whether it was via paper (ouch) or RF/WIFI handheld. Tafford employed a slick method of grouping orders together into a single wave to be later sorted down to individual orders and packed. Another key distinction in the way Tafford stocked units was that instead of each item having its own primary location, Taffords’ receiver’s are able to randomly put items to any location, thus speeding up the receiving process and reducing replenishment tasks.

    In steps Deposco, a SaaS model warehouse management platform that has the flexibility to satisfy the needs of Tafford's unique warehousing processes. After a few onsite information gathering visits, Deposco's team needed to interface with our new order management platform (Order Motion) for product data and orders. I needed to provide them with the design and process flow of our order/product lifecycle to mimic what was currently in place. Along the way we eliminated some processes while streamlining others. Overall the core functionality was successfully mirrored reducing the training time needed.

    To capture the value of our drop ship business, Deposco introduced a cross-dock process which would allow us to simply receive orders through the back door from the drop shipper and immediately ship them to our customers.
    We elaborated on this cross dock process, improving on the intelligence over the number of shipments an order will take to decide whether to fulfill the order in house via cross dock or drop ship the order entirely. This process dubbed "The Brain" is currently under development and has yet to be tested.
