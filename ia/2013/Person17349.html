Brian Farkas' Publications

  • Third Circuit Hears Arguments on Controversial Delaware Chancery Arbitration Program

    • Alternatives to the High Cost of Litigation
    • July 20, 2013
    Authors: Brian Farkas

    The Third Circuit Court of Appeals is considering a controversial arbitration program established in the Delaware Court of Chancery that allows sitting judges to arbitrate disputes. The case raises important issues of court access, judicial power and dispute resolution systems design. This article outlines the underlying case, questions on appeal, and potential outcomes.

  • Silver Linings: Reimagining the Role of ADR Education in the Wake of the Great Recession (co-written with Professor Lela P. Love)

    • 6 NORTHEASTERN LAW J. (forthcoming, Summer 2013)
    Authors: Brian Farkas

    This forthcoming article for Northeastern's Law Journal, co-written with Professor Lela P. Love, surveys arbitration, negotiation and mediation teaching in American law schools. The paper highlights best practices, and suggests specific reforms to meet the demands of the rapidly changing legal market.

  • Old Problem, New Medium: Deception in Computer-Facilitated Negotiation & Dispute Resolution

    • 14 CARDOZO J. CONFLICT RESOL. 161
    • December 10, 2012
    Authors: Brian Farkas

    This piece, published in the Cardozo Journal of Conflict Resolution, examines the prevalence of deception in the emerging areas of computer-facilitated negotiation and dispute resolution, and offers coping strategies for attorneys to safeguard their clients' interests. This piece won First Prize in the 2013 Writing Competition of the Association for Conflict Resolution of Greater New York.

  • Fixing Law Review Critics

    • Inside Higher Ed
    • November 30, 2012
    Authors: Brian Farkas

    This essay rebuts much of the recent criticism of law reviews, arguing for the continuing importance of student-edited legal scholarship. The piece also suggests ways of moving legal publishing into the digital world in ways that would dramatically reduce costs and shorten publication times.

  • SSRN Page

    • November 1, 2012
    Authors: Brian Farkas

    SSRN author page containing links to articles written for the New York State Bar Association's Law Student Connections publication. Topics include media, art and intellectual property law.

  • Covering the Campus: A History of The Miscellany News at Vassar College

    • March 2009
    Authors: Brian Farkas

    This book tells the story of The Miscellany News, the student newspaper of Vassar College since 1866. Among the country's oldest student publications, the Miscellany News began as a literary magazine and evolved into a contemporary newspaper. The book includes extensive interviews with former editors, placing 150 years of student reporting against the backdrop of American politics and collegiate life.
