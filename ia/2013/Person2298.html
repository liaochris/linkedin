Agatha Nolen, Ph.D.'s Publications

  • Sailing Into The Wind: Ten Years of Public Policy in Health Reform to Improve Patient Safety with Health Information Technology,

    • Lambert Academic Publishing, Cologne, Germany
    • February 9, 2012
    Authors: Agatha Nolen, Ph.D.

    PATIENT SAFETY AND HEALTH INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY
    - includes 136 references

    SUMMARY:
    You’ve read To Err Is Human (2000) which showed that deaths from medical errors may be as high as 98,000 per year. In 2001, Crossing the Quality Chasm, called for a restructuring of the U.S. health system with advanced health information technology (HIT) to address quality and cost concerns.

    But has there been any real change in ten years?

    Sailing Into the Wind examines federal and state health care policies from 2000 to 2010, and chronicles their impact on the implementation rates of three HIT patient safety strategies: Barcode Medication Administration (BCMA), Computerized Prescriber Order Entry (CPOE) and Electronic Health Records (EHRs). Significant new findings explain continued low implementation rates in acute care hospitals; personal interviews with Chief Executive Officers, Chief Medical Officers, and Chief Nursing Officers provide recommendations for needed public policy changes within health care reform to improve patient safety through information technology.
