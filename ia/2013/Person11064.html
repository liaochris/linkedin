Tony Verguldi Jr's Summary

Starting with my first company, I have worked in the software engineering field developing top tier software systems. Graduating college, I believed I would be a heads down developer, but that was not the path I was destined to travel. With over 17 years of software engineering experience, I have been a developer, architect, designer, consultant, product manager, installer / trainer, business analyst, project manager and development manager. I have had the opportunity to write business requirements, design and architecture specifications, and support documentation. In addition, I have been the go to guy when a client is having issues to the point where I have travelled to their site to debug, train, and install fixes and upgrades. My diverse background has given me the experience I need to be a software engineer and solution provider. Ultimately, being able to solve the client's pain is how you meet clients’ needs and expectations.

My career began by being the 50th person hired at J&B Software and within 3 weeks I was on a plane to visit one of their largest clients to learn how the client used and expected to use the software. This was the best thing that could happen to me and I am grateful my expectation of being a heads down developer did not happen. By being put in front of the end user, I was able to not only know how something technically could be done, but why – what was the business need. From that point forward I wanted to know the bigger picture, the business need and how this would improve the product, benefit the end user, and help my company.

My expectations and goals have changed over the years. However, one thing has remained consistent – striving for excellence in the finished product. I want to build client relationships, understand their businesses, needs, and the problem to be solved, not just slog through code. My aim is to make the end-user happy which in turn makes my company happy, and in turn benefits me.

Fiserv - DataSafe

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; FISV; Information Technology and Services industry

June 2009 – November 2009 (6 months)

The primary project I worked on during my time as a lead was the Cash Device Interface (CDI). I was the architect, designer, and lead developer over a team of on- and off-shore developers. CDI provides functionality for tellers to use cash recyclers, cash and coin dispensers, and the administration of those devices. CDI provides a user-interface for the device administration, an API to communicate with the devices from the teller applications, and abstracts the Lutzwolf API to allow for other cash device APIs (if needed). CDI was developed with C# (client and middle tier components), Caché (database), and SQL Server scripts (to create tables and stored procedures). CDI is used by the other Fiserv credit union divisions in their applications.

DataSafe provides various functionality for all aspects of running a credit union; however, the system was developed for individual (member) processing as opposed to business (commercial) processing. I was one of three architects to design the new commercial member and loan service products to be used by DataSafe. We restructured the DataSafe database from being account-centric to entity-centric (member) to allow for commercial processing and future enhancements in the DataSafe system. This was a far reaching and deep change to the DataSafe system involving many different applications and technologies, such as Visual Basic (version 6 and .NET), C#, and Caché.
