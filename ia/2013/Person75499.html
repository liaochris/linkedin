Kieran Delaney's Summary

I am a freelance web designer who knows what it's like to run a business, understands the technical stuff and most importantly, makes brilliantly designed websites and apps that keep your users happy and engaged.

I started designing around about 14 years ago and I've been lucky enough to work with brands like Nike, Apple, Ray Ban, X-Box, Vauxhall, EA Games, Stella McCartney and NME Magazine. I've also run a successful events business so I know exactly how much work goes into making a business thrive, and I understand how to meet the needs of both my clients and their users.

If you'd like to talk about what I can do for you – whether it be a mock-up for a client, wireframes for a future project or a complete, responsive Wordpress site – please feel free to get in touch.

Kieran Delaney's Experience

Web Designer

Freelance

1999 – Present (14 years) London, United Kingdom

A freelance designer with over 14 years of commercial experience, I have worked with the likes of Adidas, Ray Ban, X-Box, Vauxhall, EA Games, Stella McCartney and NME Magazine, as well as websites and branding for smaller brands and startups.

My skills lay predominantly in design for web – from wireframe to final delivery, but I have years of experience in print, development (including PHP/HTML/CSS), planning, hosting and publishing platforms, particularly Wordpress.

A full portfolio can be found at http://kierandelaney.co.uk

Digital Designer

IPC Media

Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; TWX; Publishing industry

2009 – January 2013 (4 years) London

NME.COM is the most popular standalone music website in the world, attracting 7 million unique users per month.

After overseeing a redesign in 2009 I retained NME as a client, overseeing all design and art direction on the site, from editorial to commercial to marketing, including brand extensions like NME Awards, NMETV, NME Video and web apps.

The main consideration with a site like NME.com is the sheer amount of content delivered on each page, and the need to combine editorial and commercial needs with the needs of the user. Since first working with the site in the early days, priorities have shifted towards driving social engagement and video content, but one goal has stayed key – create engagement with the product that means users will keep coming back.

Art Director

Emap

Privately Held; 1001-5000 employees; Publishing industry

2005 – 2008 (3 years) London, United Kingdom

Employed as Commercial Art Director for FHM.com, one of the largest entertainment websites in Europe with 2 million unique users and 35 million page impressions a month. The role involved producing websites and campaigns for high profile companies including Fiat, L’Oreal, Paramount Pictures and Pepsi. Alongside commercial work I designed editorial content for FHM.com and solus emails promoting the magazine. I also art directed shoots for Vauxhall and was client-facing with all brands and agencies.

  • Offset Festival

    • 2008 to 2010
    Team Members: Kieran Delaney

    A festival I put together in my spare time that grew to a 5000-capacity weekend-long event with camping, 6 stages and over 100 artists each year (including The xx, Mystery Jets, The Maccabees, Biffy Clyro and Gang of Four).

    As well as running the event I took care of all branding, art direction, web design and development and online marketing.

    Branding and Art Direction: The festival had to reach a cool young audience, and branding reflected this. Ads appeared in the likes of NME, Artrocker, Stool Pigeon and the Guardian Guide. I art directed shoots for these advertisements, designed the logo and each year’s overall theme, which stretched from flyers to outdoor advertising to branding at the event, working alongside sponsors including Last.FM and Guitar Hero. All in my spare time.

    Web Design and Development: The main website was the portal for all information on the event and was built by myself in PHP using WordPress as a back-end, allowing multiple users to update news, information and band biogs. This tied into an e-commerce system allowing ticket purchase, which I put together using Paypal.

    Online Marketing: Targeted at a young audience, running the event gave me a strong knowledge of online marketing and building a strong email database. The event grew rapidly from a small outdoor gathering to an event that attracted thousands from across the world – a large part of this was due to strong social networking.
