Michael Santos' Summary

My name is Michael G. Santos. On August 12, 2013 I transitioned back into society after longer than 26 years as a federal prisoner. Three weeks later I began teaching a course titled The Architecture of Incarceration at San Francisco State University. As I wrote on my website at MichaelSantos.com, I began preparing for that transition in 1987, while still locked inside of a county jail.

Rather than wasting a quarter century in confinement, I worked every day to develop values, skills, and resources that would translate into success. Because of my deliberate adjustment strategy, I emerged with options and opportunities that few would expect. Now I'm building a career around all that I learned.

Adversity is a fact of life, something each of us endures at some point on our journey. My adversity was self-induced because of bad decisions I made during a reckless transition between youth and adulthood. Those decisions delivered me into a lengthy prison term, but I'm defined by the way I conquered it. While inside I educated myself, I contributed to society in meaningful, measurable ways, I nurtured a strong support network. Now that I've returned to society, I'm determined to show others how the strategies I relied upon to overcome a quarter century in prison will help them triumph over struggles of their own.

I strive to add value to four distinct market groups:

1) Organizations and agencies that work with at-risk populations.

2) Educators who work to help audiences understand more about our nation's prisons and the people they hold.

3) Business leaders who want motivate and inspire team members to pursue higher potentials.

4) Defendants: Providing products and services that help individuals who face criminal charges make better decisions.

Please visit my website at MichaelSantos.com or contact me directly if you would like to review the products and services I offer.

  • Straight-A Guide Program

    • January 2011 to Present
    Team Members: Michael Santos, Jerron Jordan, Justin Paperny

    We’ve designed the Straight-A Guide reentry curriculum as a video-based program, complete with lesson plans and literature. Our 10-module program provides facilitators with the teaching resources they need to help offenders understand why they should reject criminal lifestyles and associations to pursue law-abiding, contributing lives.

    Facilitators teach the Straight-A Guide program over a period of between 10 to 15 weeks, in one-hour segments. Administrators may assign elective credit toward the GED for successful participants of this values-based, goal-oriented program.

    Each module includes five videos, five lesson plans, and PDF files for the following books:

    Earning Freedom: Conquering a 45-year Prison Term
    Success! Straight-A Guide for At-risk Youth
    Prison! My 8,344th Day
    Triumph! Straight-A Guide to Conquering Imprisonment and Preparing for Reentry
    In addition, we provide facilitators with five fully developed lesson plans for each module. And we provide staff training for those charged with the responsibility of introducing our Straight-A Guide reentry curriculum.

    The curriculum itself begins with an overview module, then modules on values and goals. Those introductory materials lead into the Straight-A Guide, which begins with attitude.

Michael Santos' Publications

  • Inside: Life Behind Bars in America

    • St. Martin's Press
    • August 1, 2006
    Authors: Michael Santos

    In his book, Inside: Life Behind Bars in America, Michael Santos shows readers what it’s like to live in America’s jails and prisons. Using the voice of the penitentiary, he graphically describes a system infested with gangs, drugs, beatings, shanks, and extortion. From meth-crazed prisoners raping the weak, to female guards who prostitute themselves with the help of gang leaders, and murders in protective custody, Santos brings readers as close as they’ll ever want to come to confinement.

    It is not the sugarcoated version of “corrections” and “rehabilitation” that administrators want taxpayers to swallow. This book describes a $60 billion industry designed with policies and infrastructure that perpetuate failure.

    Santos succeeds in introducing readers to the characters, values, and growing subculture festering inside America’s prison system. With the soaring costs of keeping more than two million prisoners inside, this hard-hitting book is timely for all nonfiction readers.

  • About Prison

    • Cengage (formerly Wadsworth) Publishing
    • 2003
    Authors: Michael Santos

    In this unique and extraordinary text, Michael G. Santos helps others learn about the abnormal way of life behind the walls and fences of prisons. To provide readers with a more complete and realistic picture of the growing subculture that exists in prison, the author provides both his own experiences and observations of living as a prisoner, as well as dialogues, vignettes, and profiles of other prisoners and workers within the prison environment. This text addresses the unprecedented growth in the prison system over the past two decades, and asks future correctional professionals to critically examine the current prison system.

  • Profiles From Prison

    • Greenwood/Praeger Publishing
    • 2003
    Authors: Michael Santos

    An in-depth view living behind bars from the perspective of prisoners themselves. Sections of the book are based on length of imprisonment. Some describe the actions that lead to their confinement, or detail the complexities of living in all-male communities. Others reveal the ways they cope with their terms, or the expectations they have for life after prison. Santos offers the gripping stories of men serving a variety of terms, providing commentary and analysis as he guides readers through the prison experience. Through these often-difficult accounts, readers gain a greater understanding of what it means to be a prisoner, and how the system itself can contribute to both positive adjustment and negative outcomes alike.

  • Earning Freedom: Conquering a 45-Year Prison Term

    • APS Publishing
    • 2011
    Authors: Michael Santos

    Spanning more than two and a half decades, Michael Santos has written the extraordinary story of his lengthy passage through the criminal justice system. This memoir brings readers on Michael’s journey as he carved his path to redemption through years of introspection, education, and his efforts to build a strong network of support in a never-ending, ceaseless effort to earn his freedom and rejoin society.

  • Triumph! Straight-A Guide: Conquering Imprisonment and Preparing Prisoners for Re-entry

    • APS Publishing
    • 2011
    Authors: Michael Santos

    In Triumph!, Michael Santos details his strategies for thriving through more than two decades in continuous confinement. Triumph! outlines Michael’s seven-point Straight-A program for offenders that–when followed–leads to successful reentry and positive, life-changing outcomes.

  • Success! The Straight-A Guide: A Guidebook for At-Risk Youth

    • APS Publishing
    • 2010
    Authors: Michael Santos

    In Success!, Michael Santos applies his Straight-A strategies with the purpose of guiding at-risk youth toward better decisions using a clearly-defined path to success rather than imprisonment. Success! The Straight-A Guide describes Michael’s seven-point process that—when followed—leads to positive, life-changing outcomes.

  • Prison! My 8,344th Day

    • APS Publishing
    • 2010
    Authors: Michael Santos

    In this short, easy to read book, Michael Santos provides an inside look at his typical day. Prison! is the astounding story of how one man, after serving 23 years in institutions that are known for extinguishing all hope, will soon leave prison filled with hope and dignity intact. Readers of all ages can learn from Mr. Santos’ description of discipline, commitment, and values-based, goal-centered decisions.
