Laura Scott's Summary

I am a designer and web developer working primarily in Drupal. I've been designing websites since 1995. I have also been involved in video production and post-production since 1990.

I currently lead an Online Strategy and Interaction Design studio. In the past I led a Drupal software development company, and worked freelance in print design and layout, video and television production/postproduction, and web design.

My goal is looking towards the convergence of media (Internet, television, handheld), the growing understanding and parsing of information that is increasingly available, and how these things affect how we relate to each other. I'm especially interested in ways Open Source can transform markets and cultures.

We are creating our own new world. The information technology sphere is just one part of the big picture. These are exciting times.

Laura Scott's Projects

  • Dwell.com

    • January 2013 to Present
    Team Members: Laura Scott, Katherine Lawrence, Jen Schultes, Brian Vuyk, Evan Donovan, Garrett Albright, Lin Clark, Julia Sifers, Alexei Rayu

    This was a complex project with many challenges across the board, from inventing a responsive mobile presence to improving UX, all as a lead-in to the main task of moving Dwell off of a SaaS CMS to their own bespoke Drupal 7 implementation. Case study: http://pingv.com/portfolio/dwell

  • California Watch

    • January 2010 to Present
    Team Members: Laura Scott, Katherine Lawrence, Jen Schultes

    For the Center for Investigative Reporting, we architected, designed and developed the premier launch site for this new initiative to provide in-depth investigative reporting, combined with guides and resources for citizens to get involved, lights a path towards "new journalism." From design to back-end functionality, the California Watch site had to embody the "new journalism" ethos that reflected their goals and expertise in pushing for more and better community-powered journalism, while emphasizing the team's outstanding investigative reporting on important issues in California. In November 2010, California Watch won the ONA 2010 Award for General Excellence in Online Journalism. More: http://pingv.com/blog/california-watch-launches

  • The National Interest

    • August 2010 to Present
    Team Members: Laura Scott, Katherine Lawrence, Jen Schultes, Brian Vuyk

    With an archaic website that was virtually non-functional, The National Interest magazine needed to (re)invent its online presence.

    Our goal was to make a clean, sophisticated site that accurately reflected the paper magazine's quality and intellectual appeal, while increasing its accessibility to a larger audience. The site had to have the gravitas of a leading foreign affairs publication with A-list diplomatic, political and academic thinkers writing its articles. Flashy, pushy, noisy approaches used so often in publishing sites just would not do for TNI. We needed something cleaner, simpler, with a bit more elegance.

    Case study: http://pingv.com/portfolio/the-national-interest

  • Science & Diplomacy

    • January 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Laura Scott, Katherine Lawrence, Jen Schultes, Brian Vuyk, Evan Donovan

    The publishers of Science magazine came to us with a challenge: Design and develop a web-based application for a new online journal comprised of longform, in-depth articles.
