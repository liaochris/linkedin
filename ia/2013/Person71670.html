Kurt Ackermann's Summary

I’ve been a consultant working at the confluence of communications, strategy and technology for more than twenty years.

For the past decade I’ve worked independently and with partners to develop and implement marketing, communications, branding, strategy and business planning projects in tourism, conservation and sustainability, energy and climate change, arts and culture, and other fields. I have a strong personal interest in responsible tourism.

I draw on my years of work as a management consultant and strategist across a number of sectors including government, healthcare, ICT and financial services.

I also have a passion for storytelling, particularly stories about South Africa’s cultural, natural and historical heritage, and have worked as a writer, researcher and content producer for documentary film and television as well as for magazines.

Recently, I co-founded a non-profit urban farm in the neighbourhood where I live, combining many of my professional and personal interests and networks in a volunteer capacity.

Specialties: Strategy development, competitive analysis and positioning, feasibility studies, business requirements, business planning, content development and production

Kurt Ackermann's Volunteer Experience & Causes

  • Volunteer Experience

    • Director

      Grandmothers Against Poverty and Aids (GAPA)
      • Civil Rights and Social Action
      February 2012 – present (1 year 8 months)

      Grandmothers Against Poverty and AIDS (GAPA) is a non-profit organisation that harnesses and develops the latent power of older people to turn the tide of poverty in their communities made worse by the HIV/AIDS epidemic in Africa. Founded in 2001, GAPA began as a self help project in Khayelitsha, outside Cape Town, South Africa. Today all over South Africa there are grandmothers holding together families affected by HIV/AIDS and poverty. In Tanzania, Kenya, Zimbabwe, older persons have been stripped of their ability to take up leadership roles in their families and communities due to ongoing political upheaval and its devastating effect older person’s ability to cope with modern challenges. In South Africa older persons also bear the scars of apartheid and suffer from low self esteem. GAPA boosts self confidence through education and psychosocial support so that older people are able to draw on dormant and emerging skills to embark on money-making activities within their families and communities, thus reducing financial poverty as well as bolstering self-worth so that participation in community affairs can be confidently resumed. Bringing knowledge to older people, and dispelling myths and misinformation in favour of facts is done in workshops by trained grandmothers in their mother tongue and at a level that all understand. African grandparents are thus able to confidently care for their surviving family members. The formation of peer groups enhances individual skills and, as a collective, grandmothers present a formidable force. www.gapa.org.za

      Oranjezicht City Farm (OZCF)

      October 2011 – present (2 years)

      The Oranjezicht City Farm (OZCF) is a non-profit urban farming project celebrating local food, culture and community. It is located in Cape Town, South Africa in the suburb of Oranjezicht. OZCF seeks to re-connect the Oranjezicht neighbourhood and the rest of Cape Town to the neglected farming, food and cultural heritage of the historic national monument site on which it operates through design, gardening activity and outreach, and to use it as a catalyst to build social cohesion across communities, to develop skills among the unemployed, to educate residents and their children and others about food, environmental and related issues, to beautify public space and to champion unused or under-utilised green spaces in the City Bowl. www.ozcf.co.za

Kurt Ackermann's Projects

  • A Country Imagined

    • June 2009 to February 2010
    Team Members: Kurt Ackermann, Tracy Murinik

    A Country Imagined is a South African documentary series produced by Curious Pictures that goes on a journey through South Africa with legendary musician Johnny Clegg, who explores the landscapes that have inspired artists, writers, musicians and dancers for centuries. Featuring work by more than 400 artists, it explores the landscapes that have inspired artists, writers, musicians and dancers for centuries, set against a backdrop of spectacular South African scenery and a vexed and dramatic history. The 13-part series premiered on SABC2 on Sunday 25 April 2010. I wrote 4 of the episodes: Northern Cape, Cape Town, KwaZulu-Natal and Mpumalanga. A Country Imagined won a 2011 SAFTA award (South African 'Oscars') in the Best Factual / Educational Programme category.
