Arya Goudarzi's Summary

I work where I feel I am at the epicenter of change.

Have been programming since the age of 12, three years later found myself hosting a Bulletin Board System during the early text based web days with very limited resources. Migrated to US, and pursuit a degree in Computer Engineering. School was not challenging enough. Started working as a System Administrator. Did all that while attending school. Nevertheless, it was not challenging. Got excited about web programming. Started a consulting business helping several small businesses to expand their online presence and marketing. Got a few tens of clients, and it became the side gig. Did an internship at Yahoo! and was fascinated with its scale and impact of work. Graduated. Got a full time offer from Yahoo! Started as a QA Engineer, and then worked on search data aggregation processes, tools and web services. A friend needed some help. Started a social media company that would reward blood/marrow donors. Built a Facebook App for it. Couldn't attract enough donors in the demographic that we wanted, so terminated the business. Meanwhile, moved to Gaia Interactive. Worked on heavily sharded MySQL infrastructure. Introduces Cassandra to the company and built an ORM as well as migration paths for some games. Helped build the backend of Monster Galaxy. Even did some of its front-end modules. Abstracted a social game framework. Felt the need for a fast and more challenging startup. Joined CardSpring. Indulged in designing and building an automated, cost effective, multi-datacenter, fault tolerant infrastructure that today serves several of our publishers including 30M Foursquare users across many national merchant chains.
