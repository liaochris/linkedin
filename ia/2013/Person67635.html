Jonathan Poland's Summary

Since 2002, my investment research has documented over 4,000% in total cumulative returns highlighting only NYSE and NASDAQ stocks for a network of clients. In that time, I've developed strategic methodology, borrowing from top money managers and investment analysts, that can be taught to most people fairly quickly.

I operate on the premise that performance is the bottom line, and that result should always be tied to a standard benchmark. In the stock market this means that investment returns need to be judged against the S&P 500 or comparable index over a fair period of time.

Sadly, with over 90 million people invested in stocks, the majority fail to outperform the S&P 500 year after year. According to ICI.org there more than $13 trillion are currently invested in mutual funds. Yet, according to Morningstar, less than 3% of mutual funds outpaced the gains on S&P over the last 5 years. At what rate is your money growing?
