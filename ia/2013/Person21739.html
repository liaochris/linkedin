Chad Green's Summary

I am a Firefighter/EMT from Linden, Michigan. My career started while enlisted in the United States Air Force as a Fire Protection Specialist. The USAF brought me from Texas, to Dover, with overseas duty in Italy. After my enlistment, I began working for the Department of Defense, in Dover, Florida, Arizona, and finally back in Michigan. I am happy to now be living near my hometown, in the greater Flint area, with a great job with the Detroit Arsenal Fire Department.

During my career as a firefighter, I recognized a need to provide infants and small children better protection while being rescued from structural fires. I designed a fire protective air supplied carrier, to be used by firefighters and rescuers when these situations arise. The device is called a Positive Pressure Pediatric Rescue Carrier (P3RC). I started Figure 8 Rescue as the company to bring this product to the fire service.

Chad Green's Patents

  • Rescue Bag

    • United States Patent 8,505,789
    • Issued September 4, 2009
    Inventors: Chad Green

    A fire and heat resistant rescue device designed to offer a safe and effective means to protect and remove, during an emergency, infants, toddlers, children or other persons from hazardous atmospheres which may include heat, flame, toxic gases and fumes, hazardous materials, and other dangerous exposures. This design was developed to give a firefighter/rescuer a way to safely move a potential victim through a dangerous environment found during a house or structure fire, but also has broader rescue and situational uses when protecting and moving a victim from a hazardous area. This device uses compressed air, when required, to supply slight positive air pressure inside the bag, thus excluding entry of heat, smoke, and other hazards. It is designed to function as a raising or lowering device when needed. It is also designed to allow the rescuer to perform CPR in non hazardous atmospheres, when required.
