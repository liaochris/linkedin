Aaron Stampa's Summary

I'm an Associate Art Director with 8 years worth of experience who specializes in, but not limited to, youth market based graphic design. I'm experienced in bringing a project from concept to completion, working with clients demands, tight deadlines, photo shoot art direction, and a fast paced work environment. Also, I have designed printed and interactive pieces for b2b, non-profit, and corporations. My goals for the near future are to learn more of the interactive and web development areas of the industry. My long term goals are to become a creative director in an agency that creates stunning design work for clients that want to be the first to do something different and daring.

Specialties

Extensive experience in Photoshop, Illustrator, Indesign and QuarkXPress. Some experience with Adobe Dreamweaver and Flash. General Knowledge of MS Office, Extensis Suitcase, and Mac & PC proficient.

Aaron Stampa's Projects

  • Journeys Digital Catalog

    • August 2010 to September 2010
    Team Members: Aaron Stampa, Bob Myers, Nick Purifico

    The Journeys digital catalog was developed
    to bridge a gap between the printed catalog and the Journeys website. It was a more interactive approach to the lifestyle of the customer with pictures and videos of their favorite professional extreme athletes. The intro page promoted the Journeys Back Yard BBQ Tour and demonstrated how to use the digital catalog as well as allowing you to shop by brand. Each Brand Page featured two brands, a video and pictures of one of their professionally endorsed athletes and shoes which, when clicked upon, would bring you to the product page which gave you all of the shoe's information as well as four different viewable angles.

  • Amway Global - Fulton Street Market Ads

    • November 2011 to November 2011
    Team Members: Aaron Stampa, Nick Purifico, Sue Naylor

    Amway Global is a bulk goods distributor that sells a variety of products ranging from food to beauty and cleaning supplies. Their catalog boasts an impressive array of national and international brands who specialize in making these types of products all under one roof. Amway wanted to create a simple unifying brand for their own line of products to compete with these larger, well known brands and boost both repeat recognition and customer loyalty. These ads appeared in their Spring 2011 catalog to introduce Fulton Street Market to their customers and making them aware that the old products weren't discontinued but rather repackaged and rebranded. The first full page ad introduces all three divisions of the brand: Personal, Home and Pantry. The second full spread focuses on the Pantry division as this was the area where the majority of products had changed.

  • Interact 2012 Brochure

    • April 2011 to May 2011
    Team Members: Aaron Stampa, Maureen Vidulich

    Interact is a small play house in Center City Philadelphia that centers on highly political and thought provoking plays. This season's run included three plays and one series of short plays. The How and the Why is about women working in the field of Evolutionary Science, which happens to be a predominately male profession. It highlights two women's struggle for acceptance in the field while feuding amongst each other, only to reveal thatthe two are mother and daughter. Microcrisis is about a mannamed Bennet who takes advantage of small bonds developed for third world countries' businesses and how the chain of events lead him to collapse the entire world's economy. An extensive understanding of these plays was critical in the direction of their design.
