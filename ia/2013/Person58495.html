Jan Jonak's Publications

  • Your City Guide to Prague

    • Garp CZ
    • September 2000
    Authors: Jan Jonak, Ondrej Gottwald, Mgr.A. Martin Krpec

    A guide book to the city of Prague I wrote and produced for Visa International.

    Introduction:

    Walk No.1 Going Back in Time
    See where Vaclav Havel first spoke to the people during the Velvet Revolution. This introductory excursion starts in the present and takes you into Prague's metaphysical past.

    Walk No.2 Stepping Into the Legend
    The Premyslids. Explore an ancient keep shrouded in myth that formed the Czech national identity. Later, the walk will take you into the New Town. Chochol and unique Cubist architecture.

    Walk No.3 Strolling by the Riverside
    Find out how Janacek and Dvorak got their start and why Smetana became a national hero even though Czech was not his strong language. Czech Surrealists. How does Frank Gehry fit into all this?

    Walk No.4 Exploring Jewish Prague
    Rabbi Loew and the Kabbala. Kafka, Freud and Einstein under the same roof. Did you know that Albert Einstein was also an excellent violin player?

    Walk No.5 Entering the Old Town
    Imagine the town as it was a thousand years ago. Then, at a leisurely pace, look around the Old Town Square and uncover the secrets of time.

    Walk No.6 Following the King's Road
    Trace the steps of Bohemian monarchs. Hear the tale of King Wenceslas's daring escape from rebelling burghers and look into the theatre where Vaclav Havel drew inspiration for his plays.

    Walk No.7 Crossing the Bridge
    The most beautiful medieval bridge in Europe? You be the judge.
    Then, visit the secluded side streets and gardens of the Lesser Quarter. Learn how General Wallenstein's power-mongering caused his demise.

    Walk No.8 Taking the Castle
    Cafe windows give a view of the palace where Thirty Years' War began. The royal citadel. Alchemists and the Golden Lane where Kafka wrote The Castle.

    Walk No.9 Visiting the Outskirts
    The best views of the magical city. Josip Plecnik, the president's architect, Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart and Stalin all on one tour. Impossible? Not in Prague.

  • Production Guide Czech Republic

    • Czech Film Commission
    • May 2005
    Authors: Jan Jonak, Ludmila Claussova

    Welcome note:

    You have in your hands the first edition of the Production Guide Czech Republic, a publication for filmmakers planning a film shoot in the Czech Republic. This guide is designed to save filmmakers time, and to provide them with information and local contacts. When we set out to produce this guide, we asked the following question:
    Why do filmmakers keep returning to the Czech Republic?
    Prague and the Czech countryside attract a large number of international film productions. Visiting filmmakers appreciate the top-quality skills and international experience of local film professionals and crews, are inspired by the country's stunning locations, and take advantage of the considerable production value offered by the Czech Republic.
    Producing this handbook and its online version has been one of our main goals since the founding of the Czech Film Commission in 2004. Our thanks go out to all the people and organisations that have made it possible. We would like to thank the Ministry of Culture of the Czech Republic, the Audiovisual Producers' Association and CzechTourism, to name just a few.
    We look forward to working with you!
