Bruce H. Young's Summary

Twenty-four years experience in the field of disaster mental health and over 40 years experience in crisis intervention -- I help develop, guide, and evaluate disaster mental health practices & national emergency behavioral health systems in the United States. This involves working with federal agencies, state departments of mental health, non-governmental agencies, and educational institutions. Recently stepped down from my position with a world leading PTSD knowledge source, the National Center for PTSD (1989-2013).

Area of Expertise: Teaching agency staff and large groups of allied mental health practitioners the spectrum of disaster mental health/emergency behavioral health services based on best-practices and field experience; developing disaster mental health-related operational guidelines, policies, clinical guidelines, national and community field training exercises, workshops, online courses, and phone apps.

As an emergency responder familiar with the research literature, I have many publications (select listing below) and videos in circulation, including the national-award winning video "Children and Trauma: The School's Response (Co-producer) and the critically acclaimed video "Hope and Remembrance: Ritual and Recovery" (Co-Director).

Created, and currently serve as group administrator, for online LinkedIn.com "Disaster Mental Health Provider Network". This group serves as a social network and information resource for allied mental health professionals, chaplains, and graduate students involved or interested in the emergency behavioral response to catastrophic events.

Bruce H. Young's Publications

  • The immediate response to disaster: Guidelines for adult psychological first aid

    • Guilford Press. New York
    • 2006
    Authors: Bruce H. Young

    Book chapter on how to provide psychological first aid (PFA) to adults. PFA is defined here as the use of pragmatic-oriented interventions delivered during the immediate-impact phase (first 4 weeks) to individuals who are experiencing acute stress reactions or who appear at risk for being unable to regain sufficient functional equilibrium by themselves, with the intent of aiding adaptive coping and problem solving

  • Disaster mental health training: Guidelines, considerations, and recommendations

    • Guilford Press. New York
    • 2006
    Authors: Bruce H. Young, Ruzek, J.I., Wong, M., Salzer, M., Naturale, A.

    Unique chapter to help disaster mental health administrators select training programs and trainers to meet learning objectives of mental health practitioners and school personnel.

  • Emergency Outreach: Navigational and brief screening guidelines for working in large group settings following catastrophic events

    • National Center for PTSD Clinical Quarterly
    • 2002
    Authors: Bruce H. Young

  • Cognitive-behavioral group treatment for disaster-related PTSD

    • Taylor & Francis Publishers
    • 1999
    Authors: Bruce H. Young, Josef I. Ruzek

    Chapter describing manualized 15-session group treatment including skills training, trauma scene identification, direct therapeutic exposure, cognitive restructuring, affirmation technique, and problem-solving training.

  • Disaster mental health services: A guidebook for clinicians and administrators

    • Department of Veterans Affairs, National Media Center
    • 1998
    Authors: Bruce H. Young, Josef I. Ruzek, Julian D. Ford, Matthew J. Friedman, Fred D. Gusman

    Originally published in 1998, the guidebook was among the first to detail helping survivors, helping the helpers, and helping organizations. Largely relevant today, it remains a useful resource.

  • Group Treatments for PTSD

    • Taylor and Francis, Philadelphia
    • 1999
    Authors: Bruce H. Young, Dudley D. Blake (Co-editor)

    Article outlining several varieties of group intervention. Addresses issues related to patient selection, and consideration in establishing and managing group services.

  • Disaster Mental Health Services: Implementation, Training, and Sustainability

    • In J. L. Framingham & M. L. Teasley (Eds.), (2012). Behavioral Health Response to Disasters (pp. 351-369 ). Boca Raton, FL: CRC Press, Taylor & Francis Group.
    • 2012
    Authors: Bruce H. Young, Joe Ruzek

    Clarifies important considerations related to services implementation; delineates content and processes in the training of managers, supervisors, service practitioners and teams, and identifies strategies for achieving program sustainability.

Bruce H. Young's Projects

  • Psychological First Aid

    • January 2002 to Present
    Team Members: Bruce H. Young, Josef Ruzek, Gilbert Reyes, John Tassey, Jeffrey West, John Zinck, James Taylor, Mara Davis, Robert Smith, Terri Julian

    PFA is a subject-matter-expert endorsed set of disaster behavioral health interventions designed to support survivors of community-wide catastrophic events during the first 14-21 days following onset.

  • Psychological First Aid (PFA)

    • 1998 to Present
    Team Members: Bruce H. Young, Josef Ruzek, Gilbert Reyes, John Tassey, Jeffrey West, Robert Smith, John Zinck, James Taylor, Terri Julian

    PFA is a subject-matter-expert endorsed set of disaster behavioral health interventions designed to support survivors of community-wide catastrophic events during the first 14-21 days following onset. The abbreviated material appearing here served as the guiding outline used by the Committee to publish the PFA Field Operations Guide and accompanying online course.

  • PFA Phone apps for Android and Blackberry Platforms

    • 2010 to Present
    Team Members: Bruce H. Young, Robert Smith

    Phone apps such as these two are useful in the field to refresh thinking about psychological first aid core concepts and strategies of intervention. Also may serve experienced mental health professionals as "just-in-time training' when no training is available during emergencies.
