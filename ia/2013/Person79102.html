Art has always been an important part of my life. From the time I was a small child I remember using the back of a plastic knife as a palette knife to paint mountains just like Bob Ross on PBS and layering color pencils to bring portraits of my favorite athletes to life. Through my eyes the world is full of beautiful aesthetic and clean design.

Following graduation, I bought a camera and started shooting. I consider my style to be an intimate portrait of my subjects, be them family, food, or fashion. The objective is to draw you into an experience with my imagery. I’m always looking to expand upon my skill set and absorb new techniques.

Senior Server, Banquet Captain, Training, Bookkeeping

Richard Sandoval Restaurants [Zengo, Masa14, El Centro]

Events Services industry

September 2006 – September 2012 (6 years 1 month) Washington D.C. Metro Area

In Richard Sandoval's Latin-Asian restaurant, Zengo, I started as a fresh faced server in his Chinatown location, only one year removed from its grand opening. The blended cuisine was a little daunting to grasp at first, but I diligently studied and made it past their thorough training program.

My ability to absorb, dissect, and create a more efficient workflow was quickly noticed by my superiors. The selfless, team oriented work habits I displayed established esteem with my fellow FOH coworkers. Soon I was asked by my superiors to represent our company at Capital Food Fight. And among our guests, I built a group of loyal clientele from first time diners that came to the restaurant specifically to enjoy my service as well as our delicious food.

In time, my managers relied more on my detail orientation, professionalism, wine, and food knowledge to train the incoming servers. I offered insight and constructive changes on a more comprehensive training program.

In addition to my customer service, I was able to utilize my graphic design skills to collaborate with the Chef de Cuisine on redesigning the lunch, dinner, and dessert menus. I used my problem solving skills often when assisting the Events Manager with spatial layout of full dining room buy outs. I was asked by the General Manager to review and restructure our service sections to foster a fair dispersion ratio of seats per servers creating more efficient restaurant operating.

In the summer of 2011, two of our sister restaurants in the U St Corridor abruptly lost their bookkeeper. While searching for a replacement I filled the position, learning QuickBooks, payroll, and restaurant accounting protocol on the job. I did this full-time in the morning while maintaining my full-time serving schedule in the evening.

Private Events, Hostess, Server, Bartender

B. Smith's Restaurant

June 2001 – November 2005 (4 years 6 months) Washington D.C. Metro Area

Hosting was the first job that I was ever hired to do. Although the job is seemingly easy, I quickly understood it takes a certain finesse and level of efficiency to properly multitask with constantly changing variables. Within months, I was recognized for my superb time management and customer service by becoming the lead host and often relied upon by management and staff to keep a busy shift under control without micromanagement.

Always willing to help where needed, I translated my ability to multitask into working directly in the office directing phone calls, taking reservations, booking large group dinners, transferring calls to voicemail, updating and printing menus for service, faxing private event contracts, ordering office supplies, meeting with potential clients, updating online site content and the alike.

After a few years, under management encouragement, I trained as a bartender and finally became a server over my four and a half year span. I was happy to work all of the positions interchangeably.
