R&D engineer

Atos

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; Information Technology and Services industry

January 2003 – August 2004 (1 year 8 months) Toulouse Area, France

On behalf of Airbus, I had fun developing simulators used for validating software to be run on Airbus A380 calculators.

By hooking on the main arteries of the Linux kernel, a colleague and me have implemented a full ARINC653 API, allowing to test, in near real-time, software written for still to come A380 calculators.
After the first success, we wrote a second version 100% user-space using real-time POSIX threads, the result was 100% functional (but didn't cover the full specs), unsurprisingly, the timing performance had not been met.

HW & SW engineer

The system was based on a DSP, a PowerPC and a Gigabit ethernet switch, to provide telephony, video and various automation and control of passenger's room
I have also ported the linux kernel to this PowerPC based system, wrote drivers for loading firmware into and communicating with the DSP.
