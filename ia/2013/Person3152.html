Alan Lamberg's Publications

  • Reasons and Rationale for Historic District Inclusion: A report to the Kirkwood City Council

    • Office of Landmarks Commission, City of Kirkwood
    • October 14, 2010
    Authors: Alan Lamberg

    Responding to a request by the City Council, I was tasked with writing reasons and rationale supporting the decision of the Landmarks Commission to include certain homes into North Taylor Historic District.

    Consulting with the Commission, its procedures, and prior case history, I outlined the following topics, citing research and analysis:
    • Background on Kirkwood’s policy on historic preservation;
    • Initiative for historic district designation;
    • Providing sufficient public notice to affected owners;
    • Using criteria for determining inclusion;
    • Eligibility for listing on the National Register of historic places;
    • Regulations that apply to this house and all places in the district;
    • Activity exempt from regulation;
    • Owners’ use of their property;
    • Owners’ maintenance of their property;
    • Market value is not adversely affected by preservation programs;
    • Inclusion does not cause economic hardship;
    • The effect of exclusion from historic district;
    • Written testimonials

    Having read the report and heard from both the Landmarks Commission and aggrieved petitioners, the Council decided to uphold the decision. The Council noted that the decision to include the home was "based on a studied analysis following a detailed survey of all the properties," and the reasons and rationale supported the Council's affirmation of "the benefits of a united, cohesive historic district."
