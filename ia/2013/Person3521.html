Alejandro Martín Clemente's Publications

  • Lightweight Magnetorheological Based Clutch Actuator for quick response times

    • Actuator 2012, 13th International Conference on New Actuators, Bremen, Germany, 18-20 June 2012
    • 31 de marzo de 2012
    Autores: Alejandro Martín Clemente, Luis Enrique Moreno Lorente

    Standard clutch actuators use the friction between different surfaces for transmitting movement from one shaft to the other. MR based clutches add the possibility to "unlock" or free the output torque instantly. Furthermore the rotational inertia is decreased and left in a liquid medium, when there is not any magnetic field applied to the
    MR fluid, so this viscous medium makes a soft dynamic response which is able to be used for example in medical purposes.

  • Elbow Functional Compensation using a Lightweight Magnetorheological Clutch

    • 33rd Annual International Conference of the IEEE EMBS Boston, Massachusetts USA
    • 30 de agosto de 2011
    Autores: Alejandro Martín Clemente, Luis Enrique Moreno Lorente

    There are many applications for which a patient needs functional compensation due to motor disorders in daily activities. Classic research has focused on robotics solutions in terms of actuators or motors, but the point of this paper is to analyze new solutions combining both biological and artificial structures, in order to improve standard developments. The designed device is based on a lightweight magnetorheological (MR) clutch which is able to transmit torque from a motor to the injured joint. Though it is intended to work in human upper limb (elbow mainly), other future designs will also be studied for other human joints. Simulation results using Simulink®, MSC Adams®and MSMS®are reported to illustrate the viability of the proposed device.

  • Técnicas de Paralelización para Visión Estereoscópica utilizando NVIDIA CUDA

    • 14 de junio de 2011
    Autores: Alejandro Martín Clemente, Jorge García Bueno

    Actualmente la tecnología de procesamiento de imágenes orientada a la obtención de mapas de profundidades, dispone de una serie de inconvenientes, la gran mayoría de ellos radican en los altos requerimientos computacionales para llevarlos a cabo.
    El objetivo de este trabajo se centra en la mejora de las técnicas de cálculo de mapas de disparidades para aplicaciones a distancias lejanas, para las cuales la tecnología ToF (tiempo de vuelo) no es una solución posible. Para ello se ha propuesto una solución basada en la paralelización de los cálculos mediante el uso de una GPU habilitada para el uso de la tecnología CUDA. Ésta permite desarrollar algoritmos escalables a cualquier hardware disponible de manera transparente al usuario.

  • Robot Teleoperation using multisensorial hand data

    • 2 de diciembre de 2010
    Autores: Alejandro Martín Clemente, Jorge García Bueno

    There are many systems which requires to be teleoperated only by using hand gestures. This paper presents a new way of teleoperating the Lego Mindstorms using natural hand moves. Real-time visual recognition of the hand images is performed by a previous quick training of the system.
    To finish the work, a functionally demonstration of the idea will be provided, in order to focus on the practical utility of the application at the same time of making it more attractive to the readers.
