Jill Lena Ford's Experience

Organic Mixed Media Painter

Artist

2000 – Present (13 years)

I am an abstract artist working in the unique form of painting that I developed called ‘Organic Mixed Media.’ This multifaceted process uses natural materials such as shells, stones, leaves, flowers and sand in combination with oil and acrylic paints. The final result is an earthy and energetic work of art that is invigorating to the senses and inviting to the touch. With nature as my primary source of inspiration, my work has an organic unity that reflects the beauty of both nature and the human spirit, and shines a light upon the interconnection that exists between them.


I travel the world, collecting unique and exotic materials to create globally inspired artwork. Many of the natural materials used in my paintings have been collected during my global travels from places such as India, Morocco, Hawaii, Thailand, Indonesia, and Laos. I find much joy in exploring diverse lands and cultures, and create art as a way to celebrate, honor, and share the amazing beauty that I find in this world.
.

Program Coordinator / Community Art Facilitator

Creative Reuse Pittsburgh

January 2010 – September 2010 (9 months)

I worked as an independent contractor for Creative Reuse Pittsburgh, a local non-profit organization with a mission of promoting resource conservation, creativity, and community engagement through the creative reuse of business discards.

I established and maintained creative hands-on arts projects, programs and events in the community which use recycled materials from local businesses.

I also assisted and inspired participants in community arts projects.

Program Developer/Art Instructor

KidzArt Flagstaff

I established and maintained class locations and programs, working with the community and educational administrators to provide quality arts programs to the community.

I taught art classes to students ranging from 3-17 years old and provided positive service to directors, parents and students.

Art Instructor
