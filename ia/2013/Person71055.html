Jurriaan van Rijswijk M Sc's Summary

Jurriaan van Rijswijk, MSc, is the founding chairman of Games for Health Europe and CEO of Monpellier Venture, leading applied games solution in the Netherlands and beyond. For more than fifteen years producing and publishing applied games is Jurriaan’s main professional focus. He is the digital architect of applied games.

He knows what’s going on in the mass consumer on-line gaming market and in the industry for applied games. He is an independent producer of more than 300 (serious) games, serial entrepreneur and board member of the Dutch Games Association. In 2010 he also founded the Games for Health Europe Foundation of which he is the chairman and responsible for organizing and programming the successful annual Games for Health Europe Conferences.

Pinpointed as the first game consultant in the Netherlands he started in 1981 with gaming by programming Pong on his Sinclair ZX81 spectrum mounted to a black and white TV. He focused during his study at Wageningen University on climatologically models and Geographic Information Systems. In his first assignment he let professionals literally play with business and process models. The first serious game he was involved with developing was for the second World Water Forum organized by the UN in 1996.

  • ParkinsonNeXt

    • June 2013 to Present
    Team Members: Jurriaan van Rijswijk M Sc, Daan Dohmen, Thea van Kemenade, Bas Bloem, ParkinsonNet, Marten Munneke, Tessa van de Zande, Laura Visser, phd, Nijmegen School of Management, Hans van Baaren, CEO of Simultrainer, Wim Smit, MRDM, Hugo Brand, Andre van der Veen, de Praktijkindex

    ParkinsonNeXt is a public private project and partnership trying to improve the self-management and selfcare of patients suffering from Parkinson Disease. A series of selfmanagement products is developed to support patients to live at home for a longer period of time with an improved quality of life and to empower patients in creating their own treatment plans together with their physicians, home carers and family. Products that are developed are a self care portal with personalized health and diagnostic data and treatment plans + advices, a medicin cabinet with feed back system and personalized apps, physical trainers with applied games and virtual reality in combination with health monitoring feed back and applied games to stimulate cognitive health, again with personalized feed back. With this set of support we aim to create a platform where the quality of care and health impact is monitored. This can lead to a optimized treatment plan, with high impact on health treatment to be insured. Overall, the project aims to reduce health care costs by optimizing between several health care treatments and products and avoiding hospitalization. ParkinsonNeXt has the support of several insurance companies and is created out of the Foundation ParkinsonNet. The project is created with the financial support of GO-EFRO.
