Leo Neumeyer's Summary

I studied electrical and computer engineering in Argentina and Canada. In 1992 I joined the Speech Technology and Research lab at SRI International (formerly Stanford Research Institute). My work at SRI included the development of speaker adaption and noise-robust speech recognition algorithms. These technologies were tested in the highly competitive DARPA-sponsored speech recognition competitions and made it into SRI’s spinoff companies Nuance Communications, and Siri. In 1999, I co-founded Mindstech International, a startup that developed technology to teach spoken English in Asia over the Internet. The Mindstech product was deployed in Japan and Taiwan and was used by hundreds of thousands of students. In 2006 I joined Yahoo! Labs where I led the search advertising optimization sciences group. More recently I have been working and studying independently to help large and small companies develop data-driven algorithms with applications in search, advertising, finance, and personalization. I have published over 24 research papers and 8 patents.

Open Source Contributions: While at Yahoo! Labs, I championed the development of distributed real-time systems to personalize Yahoo!'s search advertising system. As a result of this effort, I co-founded the Apache S4 project, which was the first open source, distributed, stream computing software platform.

Research Interests: real-time distributed stream processing architectures, decentralized distributed computing systems, online machine learning, bio-inspired algorithms, agent-based models.

Specialties: signal processing, speech recognition, information retrieval, machine learning, search advertising.
