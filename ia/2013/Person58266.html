Janet Barclay's Summary

Shortly after discovering the Internet in the late 1990s, I created my first website, “Jan’s Helpful Home Page,” but it would be several years before I considered starting a business, and even longer before I learned about blogging.

Since launching Organized Assistant, I’d never dream of going back to a “real job.” I guess you might say that entrepreneurship is in my blood, as my father and grandfather both owned successful businesses.

I have strong roots in the organizing industry; I served for over three years on the National Board of Directors of Professional Organizers in Canada (POC), spearheaded the redevelopment of the POC website, and was named a Silver Leaf Member, before realized my true calling was in the area of web design and virtual assistance.

As the only website designer and virtual assistant who specializes in helping professional organizers to maximize their online presence through blogging, I can handle every aspect of maintaining a successful blog, including proofreading and editing posts, creating an editorial calendar, and communicating with guest bloggers, as well as WordPress technical support and theme design.

When I take on the tasks you don’t know how to do, don’t have time to do, or don’t want to do, you can focus on serving your clients and growing your business.

As Founder and Director of the Golden Horseshoe Virtual Assistants Group, I’ve enjoyed supporting the professional growth of other virtual assistants since 2008. I’m also a Volunteer Facilitator for Welcome Inn Community Centre’s bizPLAN program, where I share social media tips with individuals in Hamilton, Ontario who are in the process of starting or expanding a small business.
