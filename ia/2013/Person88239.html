Mary Zimnik's Summary

For more than two decades, I’ve worked in creative communications as an educator, designer, writer and photographer; in public relations, brand development and marketing; and as a filming industry expert.

Since 2004, I’ve taught Web and Graphic Design and Internet Marketing in four certificate
programs at Emory University’s Continuing Education in Atlanta and also for the past year in Southern Methodist University’s CAPE Graphic Design program.

Prior to founding Studio-MZ in 1999, I managed creative services in higher education for Agnes Scott College and was a location agent representing universities for filming projects, including feature films such as Scream 2 and a wide variety of documentary, television and commercials projects.

In addition to creating hundreds of printed and digital pieces for clients in education and the corporate and commercial sectors, I’ve judged national design contests, won national contests, taught design in national conferences and taught English as a Second Language to students from all over the world.

Mary Zimnik's Experience

Contract Instructor, Graphic Design Certificate

Southern Methodist University, CAPE

Educational Institution; 1001-5000 employees; Higher Education industry

September 2012 – Present (1 year 4 months) Dallas/Fort Worth Area

I teach the Graphic Design Capstone Course (online via GoToMeeting.com). In the Capstone, students, who have completed all their requirements in the program, come to this final course to work on a comprehensive real-world client project. The project involves selecting a client, designing/redesigning their brand (to conclude with the development of a graphic style manual/brand book and signature pieces: business card, letterhead, stationary), then a full advertising campaign, direct mail marketing campaign and portfolios (online and in print) focusing primarily on this client case study. Students learn throughout the course about strategizing messaging, effecting business objectives and creative planning, and they explore design theory (color, type, layout and use of images, textures, etc.) In the course, they also develop relationships with vendors and other Dallas/Fort Worth area print resources.

Contract Instructor, Web, Internet Marketing and Graphic Design Certificates

Emory Continuing Education

Educational Institution; 10,001+ employees; Higher Education industry

July 2004 – Present (9 years 6 months) Greater Atlanta Area

I teach several classes in the Web Design, Web Development, Internet Marketing and Graphic Design certificate programs at Emory Continuing Education, including advanced courses in HTML5, CSS3, Dreamweaver, Social Media Marketing, WordPress development and dynamic site and front-end design development. In the Graphic Design Certificate program, I teach the graduating Capstone course, lead on the Directed Studies courses that focus on various aspects of design theory, teach advanced lessons in InDesign, PhotoShop, Illustrator, communication strategy and design concepts (which involve selecting a client, designing/redesigning their brand to conclude with the development of a graphic style manual/brand book, then a full advertising and direct mail marketing campaigns and resulting case study portfolios, online and in print). In the Internet Marketing Capstone Course, students develop a comprehensive Internet Marketing Communications plan for a real world client (which will ultimately involve a combination of both traditional sales and marketing strategies and that which fully utilizes new medias such as social media, Web analytics, email marketing, online advertising, etc.) In the process of helping to develop and grow these 4 programs, I've established co-hort program requirements, designed lesson plans and created exams, conducted the information sessions for these 4 programs to recruit new students, and evaluated final projects for all graduating students in these programs.

Location Agent

Studio-MZ and Agnes Scott College

October 1995 – April 2004 (8 years 7 months)

I acted as Location Agent for a handful of academic clients, and, for some time, was the only person anywhere specializing in academic locations (there are now a handful who have followed in my footsteps). I represented clients like metro-Atlanta's Agnes Scott College for major film projects (feature films, documentaries and some corporate work). The most recent project was in 2004, "Stroke of Genius: The Bobby Jones Story," and others included Wes Craven's "Scream 2," HBO's "Women in Sports," A&E's "Solomon's Temple," pilots such as The WB's "Young Americans" and the television mini-series "A Season in Purgatory."

According to The New York Times, I twice negotiated the largest per-day location fee in the history of college and university on-location filming. My responsibilities included negotiating (and authoring) film location contracts, supervising film crews on my sets from pre-production through wrap and scouting on a variety of projects (perhaps 50 in all).
