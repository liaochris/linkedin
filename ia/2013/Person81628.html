Lisa-Natalie Anjozian's Organizations

  • US Forest Service, Cleveland National Forest

    Ranger--Volunteer Mounted Assist Unit
    • February 1992 to March 2001

    Patrolled backcountry on horseback; assisted visitors in land navigation; natural history interpretation; first aid; and CPR. Educated visitors on wilderness use; Leave No Trace; fire prevention; and multi-use trail etiquette. Trained and conditioned horses for rugged backcountry terrain, and to develop quiet attitudes when facing loud noises and hazards. Wrote grants and secured funding for equipment, radios, uniforms, etc. Loved every minute of providing service to the Forest Service, our public lands, and the people who visit our wild places.

  • UC Berkeley Hastings Natural History Reserve

    Research assistant
    • June 1993 to August 1993

    At this biological field station of the University of California, located in Carmel Valley, California, I worked with the reserve manager on a project studying native perennial California grasses. I gathered site data and implemented a range restoration project on Rana Creek Ranch--an operating cattle ranch--including the harvesting of seed, propagation and planting of native perennial California grasses. I learned a lot about what restoration can accomplish, and how I always want to be doing something that makes a positive contribution.
