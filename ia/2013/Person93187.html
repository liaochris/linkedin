Dr. Michael Y. Brenner's Summary

In his 13-year career as an organizational and leadership consultant, facilitator and educator, Dr. Michael Brenner has helped both new and experienced leaders strengthen the essential skills needed to foster healthy, creative, collaborative teams and organizations. His expertise and academic background -- he holds a doctorate in Adult Learning and Leadership from Columbia University and master's degree in Adult and Organizational Development from Temple University -- help his clients improve performance, increase engagement, and promote more effective working relationships.

Michael engages and integrates both the intuitive, holistic "right brain" and the logical, analytical "left brain" to achieve optimal results for his clients. By tapping the wisdom of artists, business leaders, researchers, and workshop participants themselves, he delivers highly interactive learning experiences that foster collaboration, creative decision making, and personal accountability.

Michael has worked successfully with leading companies in a variety of industries, including QVC, SAP, the University of Pennsylvania Health System, Hahnemann University Hospital, Drexel College of Medicine, Children's Hospital of Philadelphia, Boeing, and the Federal Reserve Bank of Philadelphia. His leadership programs and consulting services have helped clients increase their engagement survey scores, obtain promotions, lower turnover, and reduce operating costs.

Michael currently teaches organizational behavior at Immaculata University and is president-elect of the Philadelphia chapter of ASTD. He has been a featured speaker at many industry events and conferences.

In addition to serving as president of IdeAgency, Michael is a professional saxophonist who currently plays with the Philadelphia funk band The 9's.

Specialties

Workplace engagement, interpersonal communication, persuasion and influence, emotional intelligence, arts-based learning, creativity and innovation, presentation skills, organizational cultures, building effective teams, conflict resolution, employee engagement.
