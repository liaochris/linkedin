James Wesson's Projects

  • UCSF Osher Center For Integrative Medicine New Building Move-In

    • March 2010 to January 2011
    Team Members: James Wesson

    Between 2003 and 2011 the UCSF Osher Center grew significantly. By 2010 they were spread out over four building at two distinct geographical locations. During 2010 the physical construction of a new building to house all of the Osher Center units began construction. Throughout 2011 I worked with the UCSF Facilities Coordinator, the UCSF Design and Construction project manager, the Osher Center clients and the UCSF Medical Center Network Architecture group to determine the network needs for the Osher Center units. I worked with each group to assure that the Osher Center employees would have sufficient, functional and reliable network access for their needs upon move-in. I worked with the Osher Facilities Coordinator to gain access to the building before move-in to test and verify network resources such as printer and workstations. During each unit's move-in phase, I was physically present to connect and verify connectivity and functionality of network resources. I worked with the UCSF Medical Center Network Architecture group and the UCSF SOM Server team to troubleshoot and isolate network problems that arose during the move-in phase. By the end of January 2011, all network connectivity issues had been resolved except for known issues with SOM ISU's older print servers and ad-hoc local print queues were created to address this issue. By the end of 2011, SOM ISU's migration to a Windows 2008 Server based print queue server resolved the print server issues and server based print queues were created for all Osher printers and links to these servers were distributed to all employees and are now used by all new incoming employees.
