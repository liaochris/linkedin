Alexandra Landeros' Summary

I have nine years of experience in marketing, public relations, and communications with independent retail and non-profit organizations. I also have an MFA in Creative Writing and have been a freelance writer for over ten years.

At Mexic-Arte Museum (2008-2011), I helped land several cover stories in The Austin Chronicle and the Austin American-Statesman, and I launched the museum's social media program. I also led the rebranding of Dia de los Muertos Festival as Viva la Vida Fest - attendance doubled, there was an increase in vendor and sponsor participation, and we integrated green practices.

I worked at Descuento Libre from March 2011 through June 2012, where we launched the first daily deal site for the Latino community / Hispanic market.

I also have experience as professional editor, having managed and edited a children's book project for the Texas Higher Education Coordinating Board, which was published by UT Austin press.

I've worked as a freelance writer for LatinoMetro.com, AustinPost.org, PopularHispanics.com, and Latino Magazine (quarterly print magazine). I have a monthly column in the monthly newspaper TODO Austin.

Currently, I am Communications Director for Farm and Ranch Freedom Alliance and Content Wrangler for Sync Digital Marketing. I also handle a few clients through my own company, Roots Public Relations, including Salud de Paloma and Las Comadres para las Americas.

Specialties: writing, editing, public relations, marketing, event planning, social media, basic video editing and graphic design, research, and Spanish translation.
