Adam Lindsay's Summary

Adam Lindsay is the technical co-founder behind Social Genius, Inc, which introduced a social music streaming application with the novel aspect of presenting members of a user's social graph as first-class musical objects: not only could you "play" a Facebook friend's musical preferences as a playlist, you could combine them with each other and with other factors. He has been responsible as the core developer, architect, and technical co-founder for two years of effort from earliest prototype to enterprise-scale, crafting a responsive, horizontally-scalable back-end with a consistent API presented to both native iOS and HTML5 clients. The core infrastructure he has built is architected to serve not only music preferences, but any profiling data.

Adam Lindsay was also a Research Fellow at the Computing Department of Lancaster University, UK. He had been the university's technical research lead on four significant projects (funded within both the EU and UK), as well as a consultant on other ventures. He came to the UK following 3.5 years at Starlab NV/SA in Belgium as a Principal Research Scientist leading involvement in its multimedia research projects and ventures.

Although consistently active in contributing to open-source projects, in recent years, he has also become a passionate builder of systems, most visibly exemplified by early and prominent participation in Music Hack Days. It is that passion that connected him with his co-founders at Social Genius.

Throughout his time at both Lancaster University and Starlab, Adam was involved in MPEG standardization. He was co-editor of the MPEG-7 Audio standard, leading the MPEG-7 Audio standardisation effort for several years, and then moved on to be co-editor of the MPEG-7 Systems standard. Much of his work forms the foundations in parts of the MPEG-21 and MPEG-A standards.

Previous to this, Adam received two Bachelors of Science from MIT and a Masters in Media Arts and Sciences from the MIT Media Lab.
