Julie Hansen, Speaker, Author, Sales Trainer's Summary

Julie is the founder of Acting for Sales and an international speaker and trainer. Her book, ACT Like a Sales Pro was a finalist for Top Sales and Marketing Book of 2011.

Julie offers fresh, unique and proven skills for gaining a competitive edge, creating authentic urgency and delivering compelling sales presentations by harnessing the power of the performer.

Julie has been featured on the cover of Ken Blanchard and Stephen Covey’s “Sales and Service Excellence” Magazine (Oct 2011) in Selling Power Magazine, Sales Management Digest, Entrepreneur’s ThinkSales Magazine, South Africa and many others.

As a speaker and trainer, Julie works with leading-edge companies like Oracle, SAP and IBM on how to successfully apply strategic performance-based techniques to create compelling, memorable sales presentations that win business.

“Julie brings a whole new approach to Sales Training by incorporating the latest in acting techniques into the “Art” of the Sale.” Jackie Purmort, Colorado Public Radio

Prior to founding Acting for Sales, Julie was an award-winning sales producer in radio, publishing and real estate, as well as the Director of Sales for The National Enquirer and STAR Magazine. She credits her success to applying the skills she used to win roles in national commercials, television (including HBO’s Sex & The City) and New York stage productions to the Sales Model.

Specialties: Training and speaking topics include:
*Memorable and persuasive presentations
*Getting the meeting with audition techniques
*Handling objections with the rules of improvisation

Julie Hansen, Speaker, Author, Sales Trainer's Publications

  • ACT Like a Sales Pro!

    • Career Press
    • June 2, 2011
    Authors: Julie Hansen, Speaker, Author, Sales Trainer

    In today's economy, a salesperson must have the performance skills of an actor in order to stand out in an increasingly crowded business environment. Just as an actor wins the audience's applause by delivering a phenomenal performance, a salesperson must deliver an equally memorable buying experience to win in business.
    Act Like a Sales Pro takes its cue from acting, the ultimate form of persuasion, and shows you how to apply the proven methods and techniques of the acting professional to every step of the sales process, from getting the appointment to closing the sale. This innovative, entertaining, and insightful guide offers unique strategies and concrete examples from business and Broadway, to help you develop your own unique sales talent and take command of the business stage.
    Act Like a Sales Pro shows you how to:
    Gain the appointment using audition techniques

    Draw interest from reluctant prospects using secrets of the performer

    React to the unexpected and maintain control using the rules of improvisation

    Deliver memorable and persuasive presentations

  • Sales Columnist, Colorado Biz Magazine

    • Weisner Publishing
    • June 2, 2011
    Authors: Julie Hansen, Speaker, Author, Sales Trainer

    As a panelist on ColoradoBiz's Social Media event last month I got to hear firsthand what was paramount on people's minds when it comes to social media: how do I take all of this information and put it into a practice that's going to help my bottom line? For salespeople in particular, there is a particular sense of urgency. "I need sales now. I don't have months and years to nurture relationships," are common refrains. If traditional sales methods don't work, what's an enterprising seller or business owner to do?

    Social media can lead to sales-just not in a predictable Point A to Point B fashion. I've discovered that much like sales and acting, social media is about relationships. Placing the focus on those relationships makes the path much clearer-and leads to the third social media mistake in sales:

    Failing to transition cyber relationships into real world relationships
