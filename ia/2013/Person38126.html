Mary Hoke's Summary

Dr. Mary Hoke holds a Bachelor of Arts in Psychology, a Masters in Social Work (MSW), a PhD in Psychology with an emphasis in Industrial/Organizational Psychology and an advanced graduate certificate in nonprofit management. She has more than 20 years of teaching in higher education and nonprofit administration as well as clinical psychotherapy, case management and testing experience. Prior to teaching at Kaplan, she has held positions in psychiatric treatment centers, community mental health centers, family service organizations and had her own private practice. In addition, Dr. Hoke has had her own radio show “Ask Your Therapist”, has appeared on TV discussing various mental health topics, has written for the “Open Mind” newspaper column responding to readers mental health-related questions, and been published in both a national woman’s magazine and local city magazine on the topic of therapy with stepfamilies.


Dr. Hoke has presented at national conferences on the topics of violence prevention, addictions, program evaluation, group psychotherapy with children, and collaborative models for integrated health and mental health delivery systems. Recent publications include a book, Effects of Compensation Strategies on the Culture of Nonprofit Organizations, published in 2009.


Dr. Hoke's current professional associations include membership on the research advisory board of a university, chair of the performance and quality improvement committee of a nonprofit organization and membership on the steering committee of a 5-organization mental health collaborative. Dr. Hoke has been with Kaplan University since 2007.

Specialties

Dr. Hoke specializes in non-profit management/supervision, evidence-based and outcomes-driven treatment interventions, multi-agency collaborations and program evaluation.
