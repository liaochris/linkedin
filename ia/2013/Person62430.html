Jeremy D. McVicar's Summary

I am currently a full time GS-level employee working in the Department of Defense/United States Air Force as a Contract Specialist.

I started my career in federal service with four years as an Infantry-Marine and served in Operation Iraqi Freedom (OIF) in 2003. After the military I co-founded an international trading company based in Pennsylvania. I exported American products (approximately 200 containers annually) to Asia over a seven year period. I returned to federal service in January of 2012 as part of the "Vets to Feds" career transition program; coming to work at Hill AFB under the Student Career Experience Program (SCEP) while I completed my studies at Penn State University. I was transitioned into the Pathways internship program and most recently upon graduation, was selected for the Copper Cap program.

Specialty: Negotiation, implementation, and administration of Government defense contracts spanning across a wide range of services and products intended to meet the needs of the Department of Defense and US Air Force in support of the warfighter.

Description:The LAR battalion performs combined arms reconnaissance and security missions in support of the ground combat element (GCE). Its mission is to conduct reconnaissance; security and economy of force operations; and, within its capabilities, limited offensive or defensive operations that exploit the unit’s mobility and firepower.

An LAR battalion may function as an independent maneuver element or as an element of a larger unit such as a regimental combat team, or its subordinate companies may support other tactical units in the GCE. Infantry is the central component of Marine ground forces. Infantrymen are trained to locate, close with and destroy the enemy by fire and maneuver, or repel the enemy's assault by fire and close combat. Riflemen serve as the primary scouts, assault troops and close combat forces within each infantry unit.
