Researcher - Search, Network and Analytics

LinkedIn

Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; LNKD; Internet industry

May 2011 – August 2011 (4 months) Mountain View, CA

At LinkedIn I worked on two projects. The first was rebuilding rebuilding the search recommendation engine for related search suggestions. When a user performs a search on LinkedIn, she gets recommendations for related searches. These recommendations are generated from analyzing terabytes worth of search data. We use a number of signals to determine relatedness among searches. Related searches are computed on a Hadoop MapReduce cluster, and served from LinkedIn's NoSQL database. The system we built currently supports tens of millions of searches a day. I led the team that built the system, and our algorithms were published at the 2012 ACM International Conference on Information and Knowledge Management: "Metaphor: a System for Related Search Recommendations". Our technologies included Java, Hadoop, HDFS, Pig and Voldemort (NoSQL).

I also worked on analyzing social networking growth, impacts and characteristics in developing countries and emerging markets based on LinkedIn's data. The goal was understanding patterns in social networking growth in new markets. You can find the details of our results in our paper titled "Social Networking in Emerging Markets", published at ICTD 2012.

At LinkedIn, I also did multiple 'hackday projects', where you hack together a new product in a day, including a product for importing academic publications into the profile and a plugin for the LinkedIn Android app.

Research Intern

Microsoft Research

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; MSFT; Computer Software industry

January 2011 – April 2011 (4 months)

I spent a few months at Microsoft Research India in Bangalore. While in India, I got to work on two projects:

One was understanding personalized web usage in developing country contexts. This is important when thinking about web acceleration mechanisms that are designed to improve end user experience in the face of limited internet connectivity. As the next billion people come online, they are doing it on devices connected to networks with low bandwidth and high latencies. I built a browser plugin to collect usage data and analyzed them to generate usage patterns that can be used to build a better browsing experiance for users. This work was published at NSDR 2011: "Towards Improved Web Acceleration: Leveraging the Personal Web"

Another project was designing an efficient remote attendance tracking system that works over a simple mobile phone. To accomplish this, we use voice biometrics as the underlying identity system. This is necessary for deployment in areas where only voice and SMS are commonly used. Our experiments were conducted with voice data collected over the phone. This work was also published at NSDR 2011: "Hyke: A Low-cost Remote Attendance Tracking System for Developing Regions"
