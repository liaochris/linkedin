David Thomas' Honors and Awards

  • Jack Cornwell

    Scouts Canada
    • November 1988

    The Jack Cornwell Decoration is the highest award in Scouts Canada and is presented to youth who display a high level of character or courage. It was named after John Travers Cornwell, who at age sixteen, served as a Boy (1st class) on board H.M.S. "Chester" at the Battle of Jutland in 1916. Mortally wounded early in the action, he remained at his post awaiting orders until the end of the action, with the dead and dying around him. Transfered to Grimsby Hospital, he died a few days later. He was posthumously awarded the Victoria Cross, Jack had been a Scout in London, England.

    Recipients of this award are recognized as true heroes of this organization and through their actions are considered to be role models to others. Recipients receive an emblem, which is presented to them by their nominator at an appropriate time and place. They are also recognized at both the Adult and Youth Recognition Ceremonies. Finally, a certificate and the decoration itself is presented to the recipient at an Investiture Ceremony held annually in Ottawa, which is normally presided over by the Governor-General of Canada.
