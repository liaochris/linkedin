Gernot Ortmanns' Summary

Qualified physician with master degrees in Health Management and Public Health and over 12 years experience in consulting and project management including 7 years international exposure within blue chip organisations.

Parallel to clinical studies and research in neurophysiology, developed an IT business - GEO Computer - in 1996. Following completion of medical degree (University of Bonn, Germany) focus shifted towards the health & life sciences market, which was developed through my role with Accenture.

Excellent experience as a change agent in process optimisation and strategy consulting projects and as a medical expert in eHealth projects. As medical expert involved in the development of the Accenture Care Management Services, an offering for health insurance organisations aiming to lower reimbursement costs through early identification and intervention of costly conditions.
Following this undertook role as lead in the Clinical Development Department and Analytics Department of DxCG Health Analytics, the German subsidiary of the U.S. market leader in health analytics and predictive health risk and cost modelling. There I used data mining and statistical methods to predict health risks.

Relocated to Australia in 2010 and most recent with a role within the National e-Health Transition Authority (NEHTA) with responsibility for the management of clinical safety and design assurance or the Personally Controlled Electronic Health Record (PCEHR), the largest health IT project in Australian history, ensuring the risk to a patient’s health from the new system is as low as reasonably practical.

Specialties: - Change Management

- Clinical Risk Management
- Clinical Usability for new products (hardware & software)
