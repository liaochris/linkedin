Darrell Dawson's Experience

Marketing Copywriter

The Columbus Dispatch

Privately Held; 501-1000 employees; Newspapers industry

August 2006 – Present (7 years 2 months) Columbus, Ohio Area

• As this metropolitan daily newspaper's lead copywriter, I work with all departments to create print, electronic, online, television and radio ads to promote The Dispatch for both internal and external use. Sales flyers, direct mail and e-blasts, too.

I also write copy, as needed, for our many affiliated properties, which include dispatch.com and the Dispatch Digital network of websites; alive!; Capital Style; Crave; 97.1 The FAN and WBNS 10-TV.

I even get to write the “This Day In History” nuggets that run on the scoreboard during Columbus Clippers games at Huntington Park, and come up with Clippers- and Blue Jackets-themed billboards that go up in the Arena District. Pretty cool!

• A television campaign for our classifieds Web site, OHGetIt.com, won an Ohio Valley Emmy award in 2010. My work has also twice been nominated for an International Newspaper Marketing Association award (2010 — ”OHGetIt.com”; 2012 — ”What’s In It For YOU?”)

• From time to time, I also work on campaigns for organizations The Dispatch is close to, like the YWCA of Central Ohio and Columbus Commons.

• Finally, I write a humor column for the Dispatch Life & Arts section from time to time.

Editor, writer, graphic designer

Columbus Sports Publications

Privately Held; 501-1000 employees; Newspapers industry

1998 – 2006 (8 years)

• As editor of CSP's Blue Jackets Beat, which was the official magazine of Columbus' National Hockey League team, I oversaw a staff of 10 writers and photographers. I did a little bit of everything for this publication, but I most enjoyed copy editing the stories I received each month from my freelancers.

• As the primary graphic artist for Buckeye Sports Bulletin, I directed the layout and design for each of the 30 issues that are published every year. I also wrote headlines, edited copy and composed graphics.

• During my time with CSP, we operated www.BuckeyeSports.com. For our Web site, I wrote a daily compendium of links to other Ohio State stories on the Internet, and uploaded stories, graphics and photos.

• Finally, I helped create self-promotion advertising and collateral as needed.

Sports Editor

The Madison Press

Newspapers industry

January 1997 – January 2000 (3 years 1 month)

• I was this daily paper's sports editor for three years, during which time I organized all aspects of the sports section's production. These tasks included assigning coverage to a staff of two sportswriters and a photographer, editing copy, designing and laying out the pages and making sure all of this was done each morning on deadline.
