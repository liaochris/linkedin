  • "DyeWorks: A Commerce in Colour" Video Game Development

    • September 2010 to June 2011

    About the Project

    This computer game is the result of a University project led by Dr.Alma Barranco-Mendoza, Dr. Kevin Schut and Dr. Sean Ho that ran from September 2010 through June of 2011 at Trinity Western University in Langley, BC, Canada. An assorted team of software engineers, musicians, marketers, artists, writers, businesspeople and game designers got together to produce this educational computer game.

    The release of this game represents a continuing endeavour at Trinity Western University that aims to give students a real-life work experience. Creating a product to be put out in the market and doing it within a multidisciplinary team prepares students in a way a typical classroom experience never could.


    About the Game


    "DyeWorks: A Commerce in Colour" is an educational game targeted to grade 4-8 students simulating the late 19th century post-Civil War American dye and textile industry. The player plays the role of a textiles tycoon. The object of the game is to make as much money as possible by producing dyed fabric and selling lots of it at a high price and wasting little on manufacturing costs. The game begins in 1870 and goes until 1890. It is broken up into 5 consecutive scenarios, each lasting a maximum of 4 years. Each scenario has 16 turns and every time the player plays one of 6 minigames, each representing a process of dye development, a turn is used. Each turn represents 3 months elapsing. The better you play, the earlier you complete the game. The overall score for the game is based on year of completion. DyeWorks is comprised of mini-games:

    - A strategy mini-game in order to capture markets
    - A platform mini-game collecting dyestuff while avoiding hazards
    - A reflex mini-game keeping pots from boiling over
    - A puzzle mini-game in order to construct a specific dye

    Become effective at all mini-games in order to create an overall profit and advance to the next scenario.
