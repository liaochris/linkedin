Barbara Jaurequi's Summary

Barbara Jaurequi is the owner and principal operator of Barbara Jaurequi Counseling, a private counseling practice in Rancho Cucamonga, CA, about 30 miles east of Los Angeles, and is the author of A.C.E.S. - Adult Children Entitlement Syndrome. Her practice serves individuals, couples, families and groups and develops tailored workshops and seminars for large corporations and small businesses. Ms. Jaurequi is a Licensed Marriage and Family Therapist through the California Board of Behavioral Sciences and is a Nationally Certified Master Addiction Counselor through NAADAC - The Association for Addiction Professionals. Ms. Jaurequi is an energetic and intuitive Executive Coach and Business Consultant and is a weekly blogger for CEO Magazine, an international online magazine for business leaders and entrepreneurs. She is a former Adjunct Professor of Psychology at Mount St. Mary's College in Los Angeles where she taught and developed numerous courses for graduates and undergrads. Ms. Jaurequi is a vibrant and entertaining keynote and educational speaker at private and public events.

Specialties: Providing direct counseling services to individuals, couples, families, business leaders and groups, Barbara Jaurequi holds a specialized national certification in addiction counseling. Her expertise in relationship coaching makes her an authority on the treatment of Codependency and work-related interpersonal conflict resolution. She is a motivational speaker who teaches on psychological and practical topics and is a conductor of specialized workshops and seminars for business leaders and their staffs.

Barbara Jaurequi's Experience

Author

A.C.E.S. – Adult-Child Entitlement Syndrome

2012 – Present (1 year)

In her galvanizing new book, A.C.E.S. – Adult-Child Entitlement Syndrome, Barbara Jaurequi provides a thorough and enlightening description of A.C.E.S., a widespread family dilemma in the United States today. Ms. Jaurequi developed her theory of A.C.E.S. through her successful work with hundreds of married couples and their Living-At-Home adult-children. Her book delivers an easy-to-understand, explicit step-by-step guide on how to compassionately compel adult-children to move out of their childhood homes and into the world of personal responsibility once and for all!

Through the application of a thoughtfully crafted program that will empower their adult-children to discover and achieve personal independence, couples will ultimately learn how to re-focus their attention away from their adult-children and onto other neglected areas of married life, thereby enabling them to enjoy their marital relationships as never before. This is a provocative, compelling, and particularly timely work that is sure to intrigue readers as they recognize the presence of the syndrome in their own families. A.C.E.S. – Adult-Child Entitlement Syndrome is surely one of the most important contributions to Family Systems Theory to come along in decades.
