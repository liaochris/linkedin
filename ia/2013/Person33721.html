Declan Dunn's Summary

This unusual skill set allows him to bring people together in business settings large and small, enhance their relationships, and then empower them to monetize those relationships.

Declan Dunn's Projects

  • Pivot and Profit

    • March 2010 to Present
    Team Members: Declan Dunn, Jody Colvard

    Real Entrepreneurial Training Based on Results Improving Real Businesses in 90 Days or Less

    Pivot and Profit is part of a growing entrepreneurial educational wave sweeping businesses worldwide. The goal is to create thriving small businesses and entrepreneurial employees, generating new jobs and economic growth, through applied training -- learning applied to a real business or startup as it is in progress – why wait to learn when you learn best by doing?

    We're one of the first certification programs for Entrepreneurs based on practical experience and results, changing passive education models into learning applied on the job and in the business. We do this by providing experience and facts through online education and live training, instead of theories often taught in university courses.

  • Remember.org

    • March 1995 to Present
    Team Members: Declan Dunn, Joey Korn, Alan Jacobs, Krysia Jacobs, Samuel Edelman, Carol Edelman, John Ittelson, Geoffrey Laurence, Harold Gordon, Chuck Ferree, Sidney Iwens

    Remember.org is an educational, social network built by contributors and students studying the Holocaust worldwide. Created to fill a need for learning materials ni 6-12 grade US schools, Remember.org has reached over 50 million students since 1995, in every single country in the world.

    The original foundation of development, based on user generated content, curation, and moderation of a volatile and argumentative subject online, serving entry level students trying to find out about the subject.

    Adobe awarded this social community the Social Impact in Media award in 2009, against strong competition in schools with funding. This has been and continues to be a grass roots site that leverages social methodologies and user generated content, search positioning, and a strong focus on curating content suitable for grades 6-12.

    What makes Remember.org different is its focus on story telling, sharing stories of survival, and not just positioning this as victim based learning with gory photos - which of course are shared in abundance.

    The work of Alan Jacobs and Krysia Jacobs, two of the creative spirits who share their content via Remember.org, is featured, with a Virtual Tour of Auschwitz - you can get 360 degree views of the camp today - and their "Then and Now" presentation, showing photos today based on paintings from a Holocaust Survivor.

    "As time passes, memory can fade. The Cybrary of the Holocaust uses art, discussion groups, photos, poems, and a wealth of facts to preserve powerful memories and to educate scholars and newcomers alike about the Holocaust.... The Cybrary is stunningly effective in its service to memory." (2/26/97)
