Gregory Hurcomb's Summary

My work is situated along the borders of art, architecture, and design through the development of mediascapes that utilize both analog and digital techniques in virtual and real space through projections, sound, and fabrication processes.

Specialties: Rhino, Autocad, Maya, 3dsmax, Vray, Photoshop, Illustrator, InDesign, Office Suite, AfterEffects, Premier, Aperture and Lightroom.

Lecturer

California College of the Arts

Educational Institution; 1001-5000 employees; Higher Education industry

December 2010 – August 2013 (2 years 9 months) San Francisco Bay Area

Lecturer in the Architecture and Interior Design Departments at California College of the Arts with course descriptions listed below:

Fall 2012 - The Future Digital Mural, Interdisciplinary Studio in Interior Design and Architecture: A Studio focused on the nature of surfaces through the design and fabrication of an installation on the glass facade of CCA's nave.

Spring 2012, 2013 - Radical Representation, All Tomorrow's Parties: MArch & BArch: Advances in the capabilities of modeling tools radically change how architectural representation and design are conceived and executed. These tools are informed by and inform the architecture we create. Structured as a critical evaluation of the integration of architectural intent and representation methods, this advanced course will both teach specific methods and digital tools as well as explore the connections between 2D, 3D and 4D representation and the physical spaces they represent.

Fall 2011 - Studio 1, BArch: Studio 1 introduces students to architectural design as a process of research, iterative development and investigating relations between concepts and constructions. The studio emphasizes methods that are rooted in discovery and learning, in lieu of models that illustrate preconceived images.

Spring 2011, 2012, 2013 - Visual Digital Media, BArch: This class is required and runs concomitantly with BArch Studio 2. This class emphasizes architectural communication, representation and design methodology through the use of visualization techniques with a focus on graphic representation, 2D drawing, 3D modeling, and graphic design for portfolio development. Students will be introduced to 3D digital media concepts and techniques with a focus on the fundamental aspects of the computer aided design and digital fabrication processes.

  • Distress Signals: Maurizio Cattelan's "All"

    • Architizer
    • January 2012
    Authors: Gregory Hurcomb

    It’s a dangling deconstruction, full of parodies of power and pomegranate seeds of discovery. I get lost in the dangling taxidermy, Hitler’s cartoon visage, the pope’s downfall, all swaying above me, the walls blank canvases, art’s artifice. I mingle with it all on the spiral ramp, up to the sky, visit the pigeons that roost on the art, all is surreal, all is the magic of the now. Visitors gawk, dazzled, enthralled, impaled on the edge of the balcony, beckoned by Cattelan. Be sure to catch this one.

  • Modernist Athens: A Look at the Benaki Museum

    • Architizer
    • November 2011
    Authors: Gregory Hurcomb, dafni matziaraki

    The red stoned building at 138 Peiraios Street is one of the most successful contemporary buildings in Athens, Greece. Located in the neighborhood of Rouf, and designed by Maria Kokkinou and Andreas Kourkoulas Architects, it houses a branch of the 80-year-old Benaki Museum. Rouf is undergoing a “regeneration” that will be familiar to any city-dweller. Grandiose nightclubs and tacky restaurants have colonized this once industrial, working-class neighborhood. The Benaki stands in opposition to the influx of neo-bourgeoisie culture, offering the public access to Greek art ranging from pre-history to the present. Small galleries, artsy restaurants and bars, furniture boutiques, and architectural and art studios have settled into the streets behind it, gravitating towards this center of culture.
