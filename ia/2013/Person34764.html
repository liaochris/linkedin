  • Earth Steward

  • Affordable Colour Management within Reach

    • Asian Geographic Photo Annual
    • December 2003
    Authors: Christopher Lee, Des Paroz

    Overview of digital colour management techniques using the ColorVision Spyder.

  • Great Shore Dives of Australia

    • Scuba Diver Australasia
    • September 2003
    Authors: Des Paroz, Belinda Briggs

    Eleven page feature article coordinated, compiled and edited by Belinda and I, with contributions from Micheal Aw, Greg Blair, Andrew Bowie, Anthony Cowan, Glen Cowans and Dave Harasti.

    This feature article was based on a concept I developed to combine online and offline communications in the dive industry. We started by bringing together a team of photographers/writers around Australia who submitted nominations for the best shore dives in their states/areas. We then had a poll at the SDAA website for divers to vote for their favourites. The top 10 sites were chosen, and the respective authors pulled together photography and text for those sites.

    Aside from developing the concept, coordinating the contributors and running the online component, Belinda and I wrote the introduction, edited the contributions and provided the text/photos for two of the entries. Published in Issue 2003:5.

  • Sydney Downunder

    • Scuba Diver Australasia
    • April 2003
    Authors: Des Paroz, Belinda Briggs

    Seven page feature article in Issue 2003:2 of Scuba Diver Australasia magazine covering the best scuba diving in and around Australia's largest city, Sydney.

  • Diving Southern NSW

    • Scuba Diver Australasia
    • October 2002
    Authors: Des Paroz, Belinda Briggs

    Nine page feature article including photographic spreads about diving in the temperate waters of southern NSW. Covering all areas from Wollongong to Eden, and dives from shore or boat.

  • The PLACEBO Principle for Underwater Photography

    • UwP Magazine
    • August 2002
    Authors: Des Paroz

    Article about an mindset approach to photography (particularly underwater) based on the mnenomic PLACEBO. I developed this approach to assist underwater photographers in particular who are challenged with time and equipment limitations in their quest to get good results.
