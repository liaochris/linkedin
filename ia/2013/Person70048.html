John Stoops' Summary

14 years experience gathering business requirements, leading analysis/design, and developing software. I'm a very motivated individual who enjoys solving complex problems and designing elegant solutions, including knowledge management systems (KMS) for the U.S. Navy, scientific data analysis systems for the Environment Protect Agency (EPA) and State Water Boards, and a wide range of commercial, publicly accessible Web sites with e-commerce and content management solution (CMS) capabilities.

For the last few years I've run my own company building high quality, reliable custom CMS websites and software applications. The main thing I have learnt in managing agile teams and producing quality software is mindfulness. This includes a preoccupation with failure to anticipate and become aware of the unexpected, a reluctance to simplify because we live in a complex world and standard procedures can't replace thinking people, a sensitivity to operations by being attentive to the analysts, developers, designers and content managers who do the work, a commitment to resilience by learning to detect, contain and bounce back from failure, and deference to expertise by giving the experts the power make decisions.

I focus on the deployment of an operational system as early as possible as the primary goal using timeboxed iterations, decentralizing control of the team so technical people have control of their own decisions, emphasizing reliability with stringent quality control (stop-the-line culture for mistake-proof systems), and encouraging teamwork and commitment.

This means eliminating as much waste as possible from the development process and encouraging relentless improvement by ensuring the structure is in place to fix problems or annoyances and encouraging everyone not to ignore or complain about problems but to find the root cause and fix them.
