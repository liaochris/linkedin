Kim Vandenbroucke's Experience

Brainstorming Innovator • Game Inventor, Developer and Trivia Writer

Brainy Chick, Inc.

March 2006 – Present (7 years 10 months)

I provide brainstorming services, including facilitation, to a myriad of different organizations including innovation consultancies. The brainstorming workshops I develop foster creative, strategic thinking through dynamic exercises and discussion. Working as a facilitator and innovator-for-hire has given me the experience to help stretch even self-professed “non-creatives’” thinking to new and exciting areas. I have worked in a variety of industries including packaged foods, food service, hotel service, paper products, juvenile products, furniture and hardware.

I also specialize in the invention and development of mass-market and specialty toys and games. Since 2002, I have helped create successes for world-class marketers such as Mattel, Pressman, Hasbro Games, Winning Moves and Briarpatch. These successes include blue-sky invention as well as revitalizing classic games and developing new games based on licenses, movie titles and existing brands. I also have experience writing content and trivia for game use.

Regular Columnist

Games for Educators, Global Toy News

May 2010 – Present (3 years 8 months)

Games for Educators is a website dedicated to finding ways to make learning fun through bringing games in schools and classrooms.

Global Toy News is one of the top online magazines covering the toy and game industry.

Chief Game Reviewer

TheGameAisle.com

July 2009 – Present (4 years 6 months)

At The Game Aisle I review only good games. I forget about the bad ones and only mention the ugly ones if they are worth playing. Games are a favorite pastime of millions of people around the world, but unlike movies or books they are not reviewed so many feel lost when they are looking to purchase a new game. The Game Aisle solves their problem by informing readers not only which games are worth their money, but what kind of groups and ages they're appropriate for. I also highlight the people behind the games; the inventors who are often unknown but definitely deserving of praise.

Innovator

Cloverleaf Innovation

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Marketing and Advertising industry

2006 – 2009 (3 years)

Cloverleaf Innovation is a world-class innovation firm focused on the innovation and renovation of brands products and services. As apart of the Cloverleaf team I worked with their clients and partners to envision new strategic solutions to innovation challenges using the Cloverleaf process.

Designer • Inventor • Lead Brainstormer

Meyer/Glass Design

2002 – 2006 (4 years)

As a designer with Meyer/Glass Design I invented and developed exciting new toys and games for mass market. My responsibilities included product conception and design, prototyping, managing each concept from initial idea through playable prototype development and presentation to the client. I also was the lead brainstormer, which required developing and facilitating regular idea generation sessions for the other designers. Dozens of toys and games I worked on made it to market, please visit my website to see some of the items in my portfolio.

Collections Assistant

Spurlock Museum

2001 – 2002 (1 year)

This was the best college job ever! Working as a collections assistant I got to see all the cool stuff that sat in storage like a baby seal that was made into a floaty by a North American indigenous tribe, necklaces made of brilliantly colored beetles, money from countries that no longer exist. I was brought on to use my industrial design and problem solving skills to build display and storage mounts for artifacts in need very special, individualized care.
