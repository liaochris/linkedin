Bill Good's Summary

The entire focus of Bill Good Marketing is designing and implementing systems to enable financial advisors to have the time and money to pursue the important things in life.

Our primary product is the Gorilla CRM System for Financial Advisors, a computer-based, client marketing, prospecting and office management system.

We provide specialized training for assistants and computer operators. Financial Advisors can receive specialized training in high net worth marketing and investment risk management through our "No More Pies" series of workshops.

We encourage you to visit our website at www.billgood.com or feel free to call us at 888-495-7303. Ask for Jill Webster.

Our ideal client is someone who has survived a few years in the industry, most likely has over 100 clients and has the financial capability to invest in his or her business. Most importantly, he or she understands the vital necessity of building or getting on a team.

My latest book is Hot Prospects. It is available on Amazon.

Bill Good's Education

University of Virginia

AB.d (All but dissertation), Economics

1965 – 1968

I spent 2 1/2 wonderful years at "The University." Anyone who went there or even lived there knows the "The University" is UVa. I had a full ride Ford Foundation fellowship. I lived about 15 miles out of town in what would have been the servant's quarters of an antebellum home. It's name is Glentivar. It was a spectacularly beautiful home. Probably still is. I haven't seen it in a long time.

I completed all the coursework for a Ph.D. in Economics. When time came to write a dissertation, my faculty advisor told me, "What you want to write about does not exist. Pick an easy topic like factory conditions in England in the 1750's." My response: bye-bye. By that time, I had peeked behind the curtain of academia and decided "not for me."

Activities and Societies: While a second year graduate student, I chaired a group that brought in Alan Greenspan as a speaker. When I later decided not to continue my graduate school, I was able to use that connection to get my first job our of college. For a number of months, I was a number cruncher for the man who would become one of the most powerful men in the world. Success does depend, in part, on who you know.

University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill

BA, Economics

1961 – 1965

I graduated Phi Beta Kappa and with Highest Honors in Economics. For my Honors Thesis, I interviewed Milton Friedman, who would later win the Nobel Prize in Economics and Ludwig von Mises, one of the great free market economists. Both interviews were life changing.
