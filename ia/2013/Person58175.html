Jane Falter's Summary

Academy Certified Resume Writer (ACRW) * Senior Professional Human Resources Manager (SPHR) * Certified Professional Coach (CPC)
===========================================================================
Our careers are journeys that many times lead us to unexpected destinations. Perhaps many of our experiences simply get us from where we are to our next opportunity!

Like many people, I know what's it's like to be laid off. Although I had many fears about how I was going to survive without a regular paycheck, I knew this was my chance to finally pursue career coaching--something I had wanted to do for more than 10 years. I had lots of excuses why I couldn't go forward, but many of those excuses were gone when I lost my job.

Little did I realize how much my career journey would continue after I lost my "traditional job." In addition to a coaching certification, I would attend a well-respected resume writing certification program. I always want to give the best to my clients and I also continue to keep up-to-date with trends.

My clients seek me out because:

I have a 20+ year career in human resources. I have worked in manufacturing, distribution/sales, pharmaceutical, and non-profit organizations. I started my career journey as a teacher, so I can relate to the world of education as well. In my HR career, I supported warehouse workers, CEOs, administrative coordinators, line supervisors, Ph.D. scientists, social workers, teachers, IT professionals and machine operators--just to name a few.

I am quick to understand "your world" and where you want to go. I use my creativity and HR experiences to design a perfect resume, give you confidence for the interview, and help you discover ways to find your perfect job. I bring all of me to get you where you want to go! And we can walk together as you search for "what's next" for you.

Want more information? Check out my web site at www.janefalter.com. Email me at jane@janefalter.com or call at 828-712-8837.

Professional Training & Coaching industry

January 2007 – Present (6 years 9 months) Asheville, North Carolina Area

I am PASSIONATE about equipping individuals to find a new career they are PASSIONATE about.

Here is what I can do for you:

* I write career marketing materials that you'll need to get your job search underway...your resume, cover letter, LinkedIn profile. My clients tell me that my resumes open doors for interviews. One of my clients mentioned that several recruitment agencies commented that his was the "best resume they have ever seen."

In addition to a sharp and targeted resume, you need to be go beyond the usual job boards to get a job. I will work with you to develop a networking and job search strategy that gets you in the door.

Human Resources Business Partner

AstraZeneca

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; AZN; Pharmaceuticals industry

January 1996 – August 2006 (10 years 8 months) Wilmington, Delaware

I started with Astra AB in Rochester, New York, soon after they acquired a part of another pharmaceutical company. I created the HR function of this new site for Astra which included about 175 Discovery scientists and a small administrative staff.

Within 2 years, a decision came to relocate our Rochester site to Massachusetts. I developed the policies for the Group Move and Severance Policies for those who didn't move with us. Just as we finished our move to Massachusetts, the company merged with Zeneca and yet another relocation for many of us.

When I moved to Wilmington, Delaware, our new US Headquarters, I continued to support the same group of Discovery scientists, plus other functions. AstraZeneca was a multi-billion dollar company and I became involved in global teams. I discovered what it was like to work for an employer with 55,000 world-wide staff. Working in a large environment meant the way I worked and influenced change was different. Not better or worse, just different!

Human Resources Manager

Staples Business Advantage

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; SPLS; Business Supplies and Equipment industry

August 1985 – July 1995 (10 years) Rochester, NY

I created the HR function for Spectrum Office Products, a privately held company, before it was acquired by Staples in 1995.

Spectrum was a distribution center with an outside sales team. We primarily sold office products to companies directly. We had 6 sales offices in 3 states (NY, PA, OH). I was a key person who introduced our company and welcomed new staff to our newly acquired companies.

After a few years in developing and implementing policies and procedures, I observed that there was a need to educate the management team on how to find the right people, create a work environment that motivates people, and increase awareness regarding rules and regulations.

The management training series I created was considered extremely valuable by the Leadership Team and it continued for many years. We were a medium sized company with not a lot of extra funds for education, so I developed the training by researching and writing my own curriculum. In addition to the topics we covered, it facilitated conversations between departments so we could share ideas, successes, and challenges with each other candidly.

In the early 90's, as Vice President of Human Resources and Total Quality, I championed a Total Quality Management process company-wide. It incorporated both training and team facilitation for all levels of the organization--with an emphasis on discovering ways to improve the process and do our work more efficiently and effectively.

I created all aspects of the process without the use of a consultant. I developed the training, facilitated the first quality improvement teams, and developed measurement vehicles. It was a company-wide effort and expanded beyond my previous HR experience. I got to know more aspects of the company, their challenges, and was excited to make a significant contribution to seeing financial benefits for the bottom line.

Director Human Resources

Hillside Children's Center

1980 – 1984 (4 years) Rochester, New York Area

This was my first management position and I learned a lot! I developed the HR function from the ground-up for this non-profit organization. Little did I know that I would start 3 more HR functions in my career!

I discovered a great deal about the non-profit world. I created all the HR systems, wrote the employee handbook, created a performance development system, and upgraded the benefit program.

There was a lot of burnout among those who worked directly with emotionally disturbed children, so I did a considerable amount of interviewing. Additionally, I worked with managers on performance and employee relations issues.

I was the staff member who attended board of director meetings and made recommendations for changes in our policies and benefit packages.

Jane Falter's Education

Rochester Institute of Technology

Master of Science, Careers and Human Resources Development

Grade: GPA 3.7

I had always wanted my masters degree. But like many people, there was always something that came up--like life. My 3 daughters were young adults when I finally kept that promise to myself. I was working for a smaller company that didn't have education benefits, so I paid for the degree myself.

Although I had been in HR for many years by the time I started my program, I wanted to "formalize" my credentials. Plus I loved the world of Organization Development and had a commitment to helping managers create the best environment that would motivate staff. So I took all the OD courses that were available.

It took me 7 years to finish my degree, since I was working and was a single parent. It was a proud day when I got my degree in front of my 3 daughters and a wonderful group of friends.

William Paterson University of New Jersey

BA, Education

When trying to decide what to major in for college, my mother encouraged me to "be a teacher," you'll have your summers off. It wasn't until I started college that I truly got excited about teaching. I taught for 4 years, but then reluctantly ended up in business when I moved out of state with my husband and teaching jobs were hard to come by.

When I finally accepted that I needed to change careers, I compared my talents and skills, and discovered Human Resources.

Looking back at my initiation into the career world and where I have ended up, I realized that...a) I have always used my teaching talent throughout my life, no matter what job or company I was in...b) The passion I felt when I taught school drove me to find that "something more" in a career. I knew what it meant to feel that spark and I was not willing to settle for "just a way to earn money."

That spark has remained with me throughout my life!
