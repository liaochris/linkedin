Karen Friedman's Summary

I'm the Chief Improvement Officer at Karen Friedman Enterprises, Inc. in the Philadelphia region where we teach people how to become more compelling connective communicators who can impact and influence important listeners. Since leaving my post as a television news reporter for nearly two decades, my firm has helped thousands of people on four continents overcome communication challenges and make the most of public appearances, media interviews, presentations and meetings.

Many of our techniques are outlined in my best-selling book "Shut Up and Say Something: Business Communication Strategies to Overcome Challenges and Influence Listeners". We pride ourselves on helping companies prepare internal and external spokespeople for product launches, FDA hearings, medical meetings, media interviews, high profile events and assist clients with message development, management and delivery. When I left ABC TV in Philadelphia to start the firm, I was honored that a U.S. delegation led by former First Lady Hillary Rodham Clinton tapped me to provide media and political training for women in South and Central America. We continue to counsel key opinion leaders across the globe and have rolled out speaker and communication training programs in numerous countries including China, England, Singapore, Malaysia, Uruguay, Argentina, Colombia, Trinidad, Canada and Puerto Rico.

I also co-authored Speaking with Success: World Class Experts Share Their Secrets with leading experts including best selling author Stephen Covey and write a monthly column for the Philadelphia Business Journal which appears in business journals nationwide. I developed and taught a journalism course at the University of Wisconsin and once ran for a hotly contested seat in the PA House of Representatives. I am also a professional speaker and long time member of the National Speakers Association. I'm also a faculty instructor at Smith College's prestigious executive education programs.

Specialties

business communications, leadership communications, executive presence, presentation and speaking skills, media interview training, crisis management, facilitator and moderator, speaker bureau training, key opinion leader coaching, message development, FDA Advisory Committee meeting preparation and patient advocacy storytelling

Karen Friedman's Publications

  • Shut Up and Say Something: Business Communication Strategies to Overcome Challenges and Influence Listeners

    • Praeger Publishing
    • October 14, 2010
    Authors: Karen Friedman, Stephen Covey, Ken BlanchardJack Canfield

    Shut Up and Say Something unveils an entirely new “Z–to-A” approach to business communications showing readers how to keep the end result in mind when faced with daily communication challenges that are frequently met with resistance and uncertainty. The author demonstrates how to dodge loaded questions, think on your feet, express yourself clearly and provides practical problem solving strategies to twenty-six different communication topics to engage listeners so they care about what you have to say. Brimming with heartwarming and humorous stories, try-it-this-way techniques, best practices, and a wealth of personal review notes, examples and evaluations from coaching sessions, Shut Up and Say Something shows readers how to think and speak differently to advance agendas in a fast-paced attention challenged 21st century workplace.
