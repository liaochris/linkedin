Eve Broadis' Summary

Current
After a challenging two years, we finally have our licence to import Fair Trade sugar - concentrating on LDC countries such as Malawi and Zambia.

A seasoned Fair Trade campaigner, and passionate trainer, with over 25 years experience of working at grassroots and board level within the Fair Trade movement.

Information sharing about the ethos which underpins the Fair Trade movement is crucial to understanding the plight of small marginalised farmers and how CSR procurement policies really do help sustainable development through Fair Trade.

As an independent company we are able to give an overall picture of the players, structure and diversity of the Fair Trade Movement in the UK/Europe and internationally. We are registered with the Fairtrade Foundation as an importer/distributor and Eve is an Associate Member of WFTO (currently applying for Company Membership).

Core to our business practice are the ten WFTO Fair Trade Principles and The Charter of Fair Trade, which are both written into our constitution.

Past Experience
Owner/manager of a Fair Trade Retail Business
Project Co-ordinator with a Commercial Fair Trade business.
Grassroots activist/supporter of Fair Trade since 1984, where I became a Fair Trader for Traidcraft and later joined their Speaker Scheme.
Trained as one of Scotlands first Youth Information Workers on the Isle of Arran, with a remit for 13-25 yr. olds.
Life Skills Trainer working with young adults at Argyll Training, for 16-18 yr. olds.
Project Coordinator, with Youth Connections in Inverclyde, with responsibility for two area youth schemes.

Specialties

Delivering interactive workshops/presentations on the two complimentary but very different approaches to Fair Trade/Fairtrade - using the 10 WFTO Principles and the Charter of Fair Trade Principles.
Works with young people, in a variety of settings, to broaden their understanding of what it means to be a Fair Trade business/town/Nation.
Delivering workshops on the benefits to businesses of engaging with Fair Trade as part of their CSR procurement policies and sustainable development.
