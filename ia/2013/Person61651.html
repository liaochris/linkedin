Jen Barker's Projects

  • Client Success Case Study: Education Next

    • June 2011 to May 2013
    Team Members: Jen Barker

    +From June 2011-May 2013, I served as Education Next's social media manager. When I took over social media management, @EducationNext’s Twitter following was 6,000, when I departed, the account had over 40,000 followers. In the non-profit world, the number of followers has a lot of meaning, as grant writers often use these numbers to help determine whether to continue or even increase funding. Following one 2012 funding meeting, I received an email from the managing editor of the journal praising my work as she and others on the board felt the growth in our Twitter following was a significant portion of the reason grant funding was increased.

    +Education Next was host to a conference August 2012 at Harvard’s Kennedy School, “Globally Challenged: Are U.S. Students Ready to Compete?” from which I tweeted live. This coverage gained the attention of Huffington Post Education Reform journalist, Joy Resmovits, who tweeted about the event and then wrote about it in the Huffington Post. You can find the article here: http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2011/08/18/harvard-conference-education-reform_n_930875.html

  • Client Success Case Study: Healthworks Fitness Centers for Women

    • December 2011 to June 2012
    Team Members: Jen Barker

    I managed Healthworks' social media networks during an interim when they were searching for someone to fill an internal marketing position that included the social media management. Here are some successes I had while managing their networks:

    +From the time I managed their Facebook page until the completion of my contract, their Facebook page "Likes" increased by over 40%.

    +As part of my page management, I assisted in several contests including a large one called the “Bride-Camp Challenge.” Over 50 engaged women in the Boston area submitted photos and wrote us as to why they should win entry into this bride-focused bootcamp which normally costs $1,250. It was quite a successful in bringing in new interest to the gym and leading to women signing up for classes and bootcamps when they didn't win. Also, the winner went on to create content for the Healthworks website by blogging about her Bride Bootcamp experience.

    +Every week we had a “Freebie Friday” giveaway. I created the criteria and handled all aspects of these small contests. Here is a link to one I especially like: https://www.facebook.com/12130647119/posts/10150465133137120
