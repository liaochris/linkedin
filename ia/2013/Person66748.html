John M. Wills' Summary

A former Chicago police officer and retired FBI agent, John is a freelance writer and novelist. He has published more than 150 articles on officer survival, training and ethics on Officer.com, LawOfficer.com, and several print magazines. John has short stories published in True Blue,To Protect and To Serve,Grant Wolf's Devotional: Battlefields and Blessings Stories from Cops on the Street, and American Blue by Ed Nowicki, and Felons, Flames and Ambulance rides by Marilyn Olsen. He is an award winning author of novels,short stories and poetry.

Creator of the Chicago Warriors Thriller Series: "Chicago Warriors, Midnight Battles in The Windy City, Gripped By Fear," and "Targeted" (1st Place Award fiction novel 2011). Also, Lessons From The Street, an anthology of John's most popular articles, Women Warriors Stories from the Thin Blue Line, a collection of true stories about women in law enforcement, and The Nightstand Collection, a collection of prose and poetry.

John has just released a new novel, The Year Without Christmas. Also, this fall marks the release of a novella titled: Dancer, the story of a young woman's journey into the adult entertainment industry.

John writes book reviews for the New York Journal of Books. He is a member PSWA (Public Safety Writers Association), National Book Critics Circle, FBINAA 175th Session, The Virginia Writers Club, The Riverside Writers and International Thriller Writers.

Specialties: Writing fiction and non-fiction.Book reviewer. Public Speaker for NCAA on steroids & drugs. Holds Personal Trainer Certificate. FBI certified: Firearms Instructor, Police Instructor, Sniper, Defensive Tactics, Tactical Instructor, Physical Training Instructor
