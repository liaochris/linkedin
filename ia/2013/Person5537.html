Amber Cook's Projects

  • Kana

    • January 2013 to February 2013
    Team Members: Amber Cook, Andrew Diamantoukos

    A collaborative iOS app designed to teach the Japanese alphabets, hiragana and katakana. The app features a dictionary for both alphabets, which give the sound and associative words for each kana. We also threw in a few reference guides for basic Japanese spelling rules. The main focus for the app was teaching through memorization, which we facilitated through flash cards and a small memory game.

  • Scripsi

    • September 2012 to February 2013
    Team Members: Amber Cook, Victoria (Tori) McCaleb, Emily Halbert, Tiffany Tsoi, Mark Koepsell, Patrick Downey, Pierce Wolcott, Zachary O'Neill

    An interactive learning program which teaches kids how to read and write music through play in the physical world. The project was a collaborative effort between designers and developers as part of a capstone project for each of our majors.

  • moodspring

    • October 2012 to November 2012
    Team Members: Amber Cook

    A mash-up web service that creates a mood board of colors, patterns, palettes and images related to the user’s location, randomly generated words, and queries. The user can also save the mood board they generated via Canvas, which was an interesting problem to approach because all data was cross-domain.
