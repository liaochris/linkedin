Chibuike Mbakamma CBAP, PMP, ITIL, MCITP(DBA,DBD)'s Publications

  • KNOWLEDGE DISCOVERY IN DATABASES (KDD)

    • Presented to the Department of Computer Science, University of Lagos.
    • August 2012
    Authors: Chibuike Mbakamma CBAP, PMP, ITIL, MCITP(DBA,DBD)

    These are tough times. Organizations are trying to cope with the effect of inconsistent government policies, insecurity, rising operational costs and low consumer spending. This trend has driven management at all levels to seek strategies and solutions for addressing these challenges in order to compete effectively.
    Envisage the following scenario: a bank is rebranding and as part of the exercise it intends to launch new products. In order to develop effective channel strategies and targeted sales campaigns, it needs to analyze thousands of available customer data in order to create profiles of its customers and classify them accordingly.
    Knowledge discovery is a concept in the field of computer science that describes the process of automatically searching large volumes of data for patterns that can be considered knowledge about the data (Wikipedia, 2012).
    One attribute of this technology-driven era is the availability of data and information from diverse sources and in different forms. The prevalent use of databases has created a need for a means to analyze data to see meaningful patterns and extract useful information or knowledge.
    Knowledge discovery from databases leverages on database technologies, statistics, business intelligence, data extraction, transformation and loading (ETL) as well as pattern recognition.


    This seminar discusses the process, benefits, challenges and future of Knowledge discovery in databases.
