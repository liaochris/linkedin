Brian Costa's Summary

I always want to learn something new and exciting. My goal and passion has always been the medium of film and telling stories. While I currently am not in that field, film/video production is a place I'd very much like to end up.

I've been editing for about 10 years now and it never gets old for me. I've been a computer junkie for even longer. Technology and the medium of film/video and that fusion of technology and story drive me. I love it.

I’m a big believer in teamwork. I don't think much of anything can be accomplished in this world without a good team, leaving the egos out (or at least trying to) gives you a better product. I like to play hard (sometimes harder than some) and bring my A game to everything I do.

I’m also a big fan of technology theory and bridging the gap between the computing world, and the film world. Added to that, even just technological workflows for the betterment of whomever. It sounds crazy I know, but technology is moving at such a fast rate that everyone’s trying to keep up. One of the things I love is learning about new technology and how it can help the medium of film/video, enhance it, make it better, more streamlined. I dig stuff like that, even more so is dreaming up ways to impalement into existing media frame works and workflows.

And teaching people new things about anything always interests and excites me too!

Specialties

Experience in using Avid and Final Cut Pro. In addition to an array of other applications that go along with those. Photoshop, Sorenson Media, QuickTime, DVD Studio Pro, Dreamweaver, Sound Forge, Wave Lab.

In addition to software, I love big picture ideas, media and technology theory. Implementation of those big ideas and making them work. This includes technological infracure and workflows in computer systems and editing systems.

Brian Costa's Experience

Digital Media Asset Technician

Meredith

Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; MDP; Publishing industry

January 2013 – Present (8 months) Beaverton, OR

To be honest, my job comes down to basically being a Media Alchemist for the station. In workflow design and implementation, especially with media, it very well could be a matter of tossing in everything but the kitchen sink to get something to work. Or, it could be something very simple. You're not going to know until you have the time to tinker, make some educated guesses, maybe fail once or twice, but you'll always come out with something to show for it! Media today is tricky because everyone is doing their own thing it seems. NLEs can have issues catering to everything and even then, fancy solutions like AMA can be it's own headache. It's my job to make sure that any media processed and used in our station can get to a verity of destinations. Be that on air, FTP, digital delivery or tape, it's my job to make sure that people can use these types of methods without having to think about it. In other words, I don't care how complex it becomes for me, I want the end users to not be afraid their going to brake something.

I'm also partially a conduit between the departments in the station and Engineering. Since I can speak both "languages," having me dispel a lot of technical talk down to something digestible for other people is a good thing. This allows everyone to be on the same page during complex technical upgrades, additions or quick progress meetings.
