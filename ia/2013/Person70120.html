James Thornburgh's Summary

My doctoral dissertation is focused on how employers evaluate product engineeres, and specifically how they determine which applicants will be offered jobs. This research will help educational instutions to use instructional methods and experiances that employers value, people know the be best way to present information in applications, and employers to see what is being used across industries, and in different parts of the state.

I have a wide variety of profesional experiences which give me a broad understanding of many topics including teaching, product design, research, and business.

I believe that engineering skills are critical to the knowledge economy that we live in. It is essential that people learn problem solving skills, and the ability to find a solution to a problem when it is not apparent.

Aditionaly, I believe that formal, non-formal, and informal learning are all critical to the educational growth of individuals. Many times people get caught up in formal education and do just enough to get a grade, but this is not enough to grasp the full concept or apply the material. Non-formal experiences are critical to building expertise in an area of study and are stating to become more mainstream.

I have posted many learning videos to http://youtube.com/jthornj
