Justin Wardell's Projects

  • Mars Rover Art Car - 2013

    • October 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Justin Wardell, Ray Cirino, Charles White, Reba Valentine, Danger Bunnie, Tina Merrie, Jason Saunders, Susan White, and more

    The Mars Rover Art Car is a labor of love for science, art, playfulness, education, and a desire for community to DARE MIGHTY THINGS.

    On August 5, 2012, the employees at the Jet Propulsion Laboratory pulled off the most amazing technological feat in space exploration by safely landing a 1 ton rover on Mars. To pay tribute to NASA's achievement, we want to build an artistic version of the Mars Rover that will also double as an educational tool.

    Our mission to spread knowledge about space, wonder about Mars, conscious thought of environmental impact on Earth, and spark the desire to inspire for those that dare mighty things.

    While Burning Man is the first stop on our mission, we plan make appearances year-round at schools, science fairs, tech conferences, and environmental talks. Our dream is to provide tangible access to what humans dream of...space exploration, permaculture, sustainability, unity through community, and the strength of self-expression.

    One of our core tenets is the power of the individual, and how, when multiplied through community, anything can be accomplished.

    The Desert Wizards of Mars and this project are radically inclusive, working to help every person interested realize their ability to be involved and contribute through an "open-door" build ethos. We further this by extending virtual access to our project via internet collaboration tools and a growing network of portals for online access. We also like to top this off with a healthy dose of fun, a whimsical nature, and the ability laugh and not take ourselves too seriously.

    Like the real rovers, a project of this magnitude requires many different people with many different skills.

    Come one, come all... you've got a place with us.
