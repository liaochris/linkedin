Contract professor in Data Analysis.
The main goals of the course are to introduce the students with the basic techniques of data analysis: descriptive statistics and inferential statistics. Special emphasis has been placed on both preliminary methods of data analysis (graphic methods and distributional analysis), and on the choice of the most suitable statistical models for psychological disciplines.

The idea of the course is that of representing the practice of data analysis as a multi-phase decisional process, hierarchically organised so as to glean useful information from data gathered. This hierarchy, in any case, is not to be intended as static, but rather as something dynamic and rich in interconnections between the different decisional stadium.

Stefano Bussolon's Publications

  • From PHR to NHR? An UCD challenge

    • December 12, 2011
    Authors: Stefano Bussolon, Manuela Corradi, Giampaolo Armellin, Dario Betti, Annamaria Chiasera, Jovan Stevovic

  • Card sorting, category validity, and contextual navigation

    • J Inform Architecture
    • December 16, 2009
    Authors: Stefano Bussolon

    The aim of this paper is twofold: the first purpose is to propose some measures of the fitness of a taxonomy as a whole and of each item. Three measures will be presented: a) the Consensus Analysis; b) an index called Auto-correlation; c) a new measure, called Category Validity, conceptually similar to the Cue Validity. All these measures can be calculated from the results of the Card Sorting.
    The second goal is to present a contextual navigation that could ameliorate the findability of those items whose classification has been proved to be problematic, and to increase the information scent of the whole domain.
