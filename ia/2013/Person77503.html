Kurt Christopher's Projects

  • Rocky Mountain PBS Kids Fun Fest

    • August 2006 to Present
    Team Members: Kurt Christopher

    When I took over outreach events for Rocky Mountain PBS in 2005, I decided the Kids Fun Fest – an annual “open house” at the station for kids and their families – had the most potential for growth. Previously, the event was only held in the parking lot of KRMA-TV. I envisioned a downtown block party that could become a major event for downtown Denver.

    I met with the Denver Office of Cultural Affairs about my idea. The response was very positive. No other downtown event on the calendar was targeted just for kids and families. The DOCA assisted in providing an outdoor stage for entertainment. I worked with the Denver Public Works department on a permit to close down Bannock Street to expand to a full block party. I met with RTD to reschedule bus routes the day of the event.

    I coordinated with dozens of organizations to provide booths, activities and food, including the Denver Police Department, Colorado Railroad Museum, Denver Children’s Museum, and Wild Oats Market. I coordinated with PBS headquarters outside Washington, DC, to obtain official character costumes for Clifford the Big Red Dog, Arthur, Curious George and others. I was even able to get the national host of the PBS Kids programming block to make an appearance.

    I had to ensure all parties invested in the event were fully represented in marketing. Multiple television spots were produced to give equal time to the major stakeholders. Press releases had to be written in ways to recognize multiple organizations. Custom messages were crafted for various groups to share with their members via email.

    The combined efforts paid off with a record crowd of over 4,000 families in attendance at the 2006 event. Today the Rocky Mountain PBS Kids Fun Fest continues to be an annual fixture in downtown Denver.
