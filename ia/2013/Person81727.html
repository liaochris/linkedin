Lisa Torrence's Summary

With over 20 years of experience, Lisa is an award-winning, marketing lead creative who has a broad range of experience from web to direct, print to video. Lisa doesn't throw things up on the wall to see if they stick, she asks questions and digs deep to find what makes your product or service different. She can help you find a voice and more importantly find an audience your voice will resonate with. She is passionate about working with people and creating teams that don't just get the job done, but forges new frontiers and exposes new opportunities.

Specialties: Branding, re-branding, marketing strategy, email marketing, project management, creative direction, art direction, logo design, advertising, graphic design, web design, print design, PowerPoint presentations, info graphics, production management, marketing automation, Marketo, process work flow engineer, budgeting, team management, team building, coaching, listening, a get 'er done type of gal.

Lisa Torrence's Projects

  • Results Matter Campaign

    • January 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Lisa Torrence, Paul Suttell, Heather Bechard, Rachel Jackson, Scott Wilson, John Gallagher

    The goal of this award winning campaign was to improve global awareness and image perception of Intermec through an impactful campaign, reinforcing a newly created brand positioning statement that was part of this project. The campaign was highly differentiated in the marketplace and utilized customer results as key metric. The Results campaign featured print and digital advertising, email nurturing, videos, partner co-branding and a microsite. The campaign has generated significant leads and sales opportunities as well as nearly 2M impressions and was viewed as a global success as well as being recognized as a B2B campaign of the year from the American Business Awards for 2013.

  • SuperPrinters Campaign

    • January 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Lisa Torrence, Paul Suttell, Heather Bechard, John Gallagher, Scott Wilson, Rachel Jackson

    The goal was to create a global campaign to support three simultaneous tier-1 product launches representing substantial revenue opportunity. The Super-Series campaign help tie innovative yet highly technical product features together for partners, distributors and end users through a digestible, creative and memorable approach. The Super-Series campaign featured print and digital advertising, email nurturing, videos, partner co-branding and a microsite.

  • CK3X Mobile Computer Product Launch

    • January 2013 to Present
    Team Members: Lisa Torrence, Amy Hilliard, Heather Bechard, John Gallagher, Scott Wilson, Rachel Jackson

    CK3X is a next generation, mid-range mobile computer based on many of the technologies first pioneered in the 70 Series. It replaces the existing CK3, a critically important product for Intermec forecasted to drive more than $60 million in revenue next year. The CK3 is the leading product in Intermec’s mobile computer product portfolio and is universally well regarded by partners and end users. Global B2B target audience, C-level executives, directors and managers in IT and operations positions in the supply chain space, with a special focus on the DC deployment environment and retail.

  • Own Your Segment: Highlands Kids Dentistry

    • September 2009 to April 2010
    Team Members: Lisa Torrence, Nick Bean, Mimi Bean

    When a strong emotional benefit marries functional differentiation, good things happen. Highlands Kids Dentistry was the first in its region to offer pain-free pediatric laser dentistry. Throughout the branding process, we never lost sight that parents were the natural target, not their children. The hope was for parents to provide a markedly different and more positive experience at the dentist than their own a generation ago. The first step was to address parents’ questions directly through strong headlines and body copy worth reading, all with a design aesthetic that acknowledged children without being childish. One of their initial launch promotions provided bookmarks to private schools around Halloween and we coined “Unscary Dentist” for the event. Parents responded, and it stuck. UnscaryDentist.com swiftly became the domain parents remembered and the kids themselves began to ask for the Unscary Dentist, a connection we hadn’t anticipated.
