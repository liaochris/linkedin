Ingrid Edmunds' Summary

IN A NUTSHELL: After years honing my sales and marketing skills in media, including print, broadcast and Internet, I embarked on my current career as an Events Producer for CyberVision Marketing & Events Inc. (CVM Events).

During the last 14 years at CVM Events I have project managed many marketing initiatives and produced a significant number of major events in Vancouver, events ranging from 30 to 30,000 people.

The budgets for these projects varied considerably ($5000 - $275,000), were unique and demanding, requiring the highest organizational skills and competency in order to achieve successful results.

I am committed to every marketing project and special event, from the moment of inception to completion simply because I care about my clients and I LOVE what I do! (Except weddings...I don't do weddings :~).

Specialties: Creativity. Vision. Branding. Copywriting. Project Management. Sales. Spretzatuka; the art of making difficult look effortless.

  • Davie Day Street Festival

    • June 2008 to September 2008
    Team Members: Ingrid Edmunds

    CVM Events was hired by the West End Business Improvement Association (WEBIA) to create the 5th Annual Davie Day Street Festival, Sept. 8th, 2008. The organization of this annual 4 block street closure was a fairly late start (June 1) considering the multitude of coordination with the City of Vancouver (fire, police, ambulance, transit, and health). Prior to coordination of all day-of-event logistics, CVM Events was also responsible for the creation of all necessary contracts (booking forms for buskers and entertainers, contracts for sponsors and vendors), creating & selling sponsor packages, media liaison, graphics, copywriting, promotional material, website, advertising, data capture and merchant liaison.

    The 4 block Festival area included numerous individual vendors (food and retail), existing merchant outdoor involvement, street patios and several themed zones (Kid Zone, Heritage Zone, Doggy Zone, Comfort Zone, Green Zone, Artisan Zone) complete with entertainment and activities (dancing classes, games, buskers, bouncy castle, canine fashion show, doggy was, blacksmith demonstrations, carriage rides, etc.). The Main Stage included 8 hours of high energy, top-notch scheduled entertainment suitable for the whole family. All aspects of the event were produced by CVM Events.

    We were very pleased to work closely with the WEBIA businesses by encouraging their involvement with the Festival while keeping them and the local residents well-informed of all street closure activities. The 2008 Festival was the most successful Davie Day to date; receiving excellent attendance, many accolades and eventual distinction as Vancouver's 2nd Best Festival, WE Reader's Choice Award.
