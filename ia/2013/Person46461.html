Fredi Walker-Browne's Summary

Fredi has been blessed with an abundant career having achieved success as an actress, director, writer and teacher.

For her Broadway debut, she was granted the incredible experience of creating the role of JoAnne Jefferson in the smash hit musical RENT which won the 1966 Pulitzer Prize for Drama as well as four Antoinette Perry (TONY) Awards and for which she personally garnered an Obie and a Fanny.

Fredi has performed in many hit plays including: THE LION KING, LOVE-LOSS & WHAT I WORE - ONCE ON THIS ISLAND - LITTLE SHOP OF HORRORS and many others. Television appearances include LAw & ORDER and a recurring guest spot on the Emmy Award winning series THE BIG C

As a director, Fredi is currently working on a new musical CAGED BIRD - written by Myla Churchill-Barret and Joan Delahunt. Her most recent productions include - SCANDALOUS PEOPLE (also by Myla Churchill-Barrett) and KING ALIVE - The People and Music of the Movement - co-written by Fredi and Craig Alan Edwards - who stars as Dr. King.

Fredi is a staunch advocate of Arts Education. She has taught for several prestigious institutions including NYU (her alma mater) and LONG ISLAND UNIVERSITY. She is also a Teaching Artist for several Conservatories in the NY Tri-State area - specializing in Vocal Performance.
