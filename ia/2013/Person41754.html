Ed Costello's Summary

I enjoy solving difficult information technology problems.

I enjoy making technology solve our problems, instead of the traditional sense that we must warp our businesses to the whims of technology.

I was IBM's first Corporate Webmaster. It was a role that was parts systems engineer, network engineer, application developer, project manager, and herder of corporate cats. Today it would likely be called a CTO. I was responsible for managing the technology behind www.ibm.com and IBM's presence on the web, as well as overseeing application development and operations for a variety of IBM corporate web properties. I was responsible for security, for brand enforcement, for deployment standards, for education of what it meant to "run" a web site. My team and I pioneered web site operations as a discipline, developing procedures, practices and routines to run a 7x24x365 web presence across multiple data centers in multiple countries. I directly managed over a dozen technical professionals, and lead a worldwide team of technologists through the first explosive growth period of the web.

After I left the role was split up into multiple departments.

In 1999 I was recruited to manage the content and applications teams for the official web site of the 2000 Sydney Olympic Games. In this role I managed 35 people across the US, Spain and Australia for the year leading up to the Olympic Games, as well as technology operations oversite of the olympics.com site during the Olympic Games.

Since 2001 I have worked with a number of startups, either as an employee or as a consultant through my consulting practice.

I have direct experience designing, developing and managing web applications taking into consideration security, scalability, maintainability and manageability.
