Dennis Devlin's Summary

I provide "Consumer Clarity in Marketing to Millennials".

My professional purpose is to deliver greater consumer clarity to corporate, agency, and nonprofit marketers in their marketing to Millennials. I do so through uncovering deep insight about the Millennial consumer, generating clear marketing strategy to reach. engage, and motivate them to act, and creating from the strategy effective execution in Millennial consumer marketing programs and campaigns.

* I am a consumer thought leader in the marketing to Millennials arena, serving as an in-demand public speaker and blogger of MarketingToMillennials.com..

* I currently serve as the President of the Cincinnati Chapter of the American Marketing Association (Cincinnati AMA).

* Since January 2007 I have served as an adjunct instructor at Cincinnati area colleges and universities.

* As a life-long learner, I support through volunteering my time educational programs such as the University of Cincinnati College of Business Mentor Program and Junior Achievement.

Prior to launching Consumer Clarity in October 2009, I built a successful career spending nearly 25 years in agency leadership and management roles. I have had the good fortune of working on many of the most successful consumer brands in the world during my career.

Specialties: Millennial consumer, market research, consumer insight, consumer understanding, marketing strategy, consumer strategy, communications strategy, brand strategy, digital marketing, mobile marketing

- Attended 2012 AMA Leadership Summit in Chicago in April, 2012 as incoming President-Elect.
- Attended 2013 AMA Leadership Summit in Chicago in April 2013 as incoming President.

With approximately 550 members in the Greater Cincinnati/Northern Kentucky area, the Cincinnati AMA is considered THE destination to tap into marketing know-how, learn what’s hot in marketing today and spur professional development. Cincinnati AMA members represent a wide array of marketing roles in the public, private, academic and not-for-profit sectors.

The Cincinnati AMA is dedicated to the growth, development and success of marketing professionals in the Greater Cincinnati business community by providing tools and resources necessary for increasing knowledge and marketing expertise, promoting the best practices for acquiring, satisfying and keeping customers, providing networking opportunities for making business contacts with fellow marketing professionals within lively social settings and leveraging the internationally-established brand of the American Marketing Association to create greater visibility and prestige within the business and professional community

Prior to my current role as President, I served as the Group Leader of the Market Research Shared Interest Group (SIG) from October, 2009 until May 2012 before becoming the President-Elect, The SIG is a monthly facilitated discussion group and knowledge exchange for marketing and market research professionals who enjoy discussing current issues/trends and increasing their knowledge/skills in market research and consumer insights.

Dennis Devlin's Projects

  • Researchers For the Arts Cincinnati (RFA Cincinnati)

    • July 2013 to Present
    Team Members: Dennis Devlin, Matt Valle, Will Krieger, , Ric Sweeney

    ArtsWave has reached out to Cincinnati area marketing researchers to volunteer to support community arts organizations. The program is called Researchers for the Arts Cincinnati (RFA Cincinnati). The program supports the needs of the grantee arts organizations around community impact measurement.

    Eligible requests might include survey design, data analysis, development of infographics and other data visualizations, focus group facilitation, and national or regional benchmarking, depending on the volunteer’s skill sets. The program looks for interested research and data professionals who have a passion for or interest in arts and culture, and a willingness to provide pro bono assistance for area arts nonprofits. Projects can range from a single consultation to several months in length depending on the nature of the project and need.

    Volunteers are expected to provide assistance to at least one RFA Cincinnati client each calendar year in order to remain affiliated with the group. ArtsWave will periodically convene the Researchers for the Arts participants to provide updates on the collaborative metrics work and enable the volunteers to network and share ideas.
