Gwen Clarke's Experience

President

People First Consulting, LLC

September 2004 – Present (9 years 3 months)

I founded People First in 2004 and it is the culmination of all of my various work experiences. I have learned that whether it be a start up, entrepreneurial, mid-size or Corporate environment, it really does get down to people in terms of the differentiating success factor.

People First helps companies increase profits by maximizing their human capital. We help our clients "clone" and retain their Top Performers and also help them to align their employees in order for the company to be more productive and profitable. We provide information and systems that will increase sales and productivity, reduce costs and provide an ongoing basis for more effective management. Our clients are more profitable because they hire smarter, promote better, manage more effectively and retain their Top Performers.

There is broad application for the work we do, but I have a
passion for helping clients build, develop and retain a high performing sales organization. This involves sharing and applying expertise in four key strategic areas:

1. Selective Hiring – replicating, developing and retaining more Top Performers
2. Optimization – doing more with fewer resources
3. Alignment – effective integration resulting from mergers and other restructuring activities
4. Management Development – hiring, developing and retaining effective sales managers

I have been particularly successful in developing systems and processes that allow successful sales practices of Top Performers to be duplicated across teams with widely varying sales abilities.

In the words of one of our clients, we offer a "rare quantitative lens into the performance of a sales team". Our systems, tools and processes are helping our clients effectively address the four areas mentioned above during what has been a critical time for many companies.

Regional Vice President

Digital Insurance

Privately Held; 201-500 employees; Insurance industry

June 2000 – August 2004 (4 years 3 months)

Digital Insurance (DI) is the fastest-growing national employee benefits broker in the small business market. DI is reshaping the small group benefits arena by partnering with brokers, agents and financial services companies to profitably expand their small business customer base, and to provide their clients with the price, product and service advantages experienced by large employers.

I was recruited by the CEO at the time to join the start-up team responsible for Digital Insurance's launch. Responsibilities included participating in the development of the strategic marketing plan and the generation of new business through partnering with national and regional insurance agencies to acquire and/or manage, and subsequently grow, their existing block of small group benefits business.

Vice President, Business Development

Vivra Specialty Partners - Vivra, Inc.

1995 – 2000 (5 years)

Vivra Specialty Partners was originally a division of Vivra, Inc., but later was a privately held national physicians practice management company. The company was acquired by Magellan Health Services in February, 2000.

I had the honor of being part of the team responsible for Vivra Specialty Partner's entry into the specialty care business. We grew to be the largest independent manager of specialty health care at the time, serving over 5 million members through contracts with health care organizations, employers and other payers.

My responsibilities included the successful development and implementation of strategic partnerships with clients such as CIGNA, Aetna, Blue Cross, etc. involving 40+ specialty networks. The initiative involved first time entry into four major markets, requiring negotiations with physicians, hospitals, employers and health care companies. I also had the responsibility for centralizing and standardizing the implementation process for strategic partnerships, subsequently resulting in the major turnaround of business development function’s performance.

Vice President, Business Development

Premier Asthma and Allergy

1991 – 1995 (4 years)

Premier Asthma and Allergy was a national physicians practice management company specific to asthma and allergy. I was a member of the original start-up team and my responsibilities ranged from physician recruitment to negotiating managed care contracts (professional and disease management) for 12 major markets. Premier's name was changed to Asthma and Allergy Care America in 1994 and we became a subsidiary of Vivra, Inc. at that time.

Marketing Consultant

Response Marketing Group

1989 – 1991 (2 years)

From 1996 to 1998 RMG grew to be the second largest privately held direct response marketing company in the U.S., with $43 million in revenue, 300 employees and offices in 5 countries. The company was sold to Snyder Communications in 1998. During my tenure at RMG, I opened the Atlanta office with responsibilities for developing new business in eight financial markets through a direct marketing approach.

Director of Developer Relations

Rocking Horse Child Care Centers

1986 – 1989 (3 years)

I was recruited by the CEO to be a part of the management team driving what was, at the time, a unique approach for the childcare industry. The aspect of the business I was involved with was working with Developers to develop childcare centers as an integral amenity within upscale corporate park environments. Responsibilities included initial demographic analysis, site selection, zoning procedures, proforma operating analysis, lease negotiations, space and floor plan design and supervision of construction and/or renovation. The company was later acquired by Nobel Learning.

Account Executive

Reported to Vice President, Sales and marketing, of this start-up HMO. Responsibilities included the then groundbreaking marketing of HMO contracts to major employers. I achieved 180% of annual sales quota. The company operates as CIGNA HealthCare of Virginia today.
