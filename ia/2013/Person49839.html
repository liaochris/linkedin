Kitty Wojcik, GPC, CGMS's Summary

Kitty Wojcik is one of a hand-full of grant specialists in the United States that holds both the Grant Professional Certified (GPC) and Certified Grant Management Specialist (CGMS) credentials.

Ms. Wojcik’s expertise in grant development for healthcare, workforce development, education, human services, transportation, environmental protection, community development, animal welfare, and national & community service are based on extensive experience in the nonprofit sector and more than twelve years of professional grant writing, grant making and grant peer review success.

Kitty Wojcik served on peer review panels for the U. S. Department of Health and Human Services, the U.S. Department of Labor, the Center for Disease Control, the Corporation for National and Community Service as well as for private foundations.

Ms. Wojcik is a designated subject matter expert for the Grants Professionals Certification Institute and the National Grant Managers Association. Kitty holds the Nonprofit and Civil Society Studies Certificate from the Graduate Institute for Policy Studies, Johns Hopkins University. Kitty served on the Board of Directors, Grant Professionals Association and the Grant Professionals Foundation. Currently she sits on the Board of Trustees, University of Bridgeport.

As president and chief grant officer for KWG Grant Consultants, Kitty oversees KWG’s client roster of municipalities, universities, colleges, healthcare systems, for-profit firms, non-profit organizations and private grant making foundations with grant review, grant staff development, grant writing, grant writing training, subject matter expert facilitation, and grant project management services.

From the many grant applications she worked on over the past three years Kitty helped clients to obtain more than $20,000,000 in program and project assistance.
