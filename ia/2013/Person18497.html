Bryan Haywood's Summary

A safety professional with over 20 years of experience in safety and emergency response. During my career, I developed, implemented, and managed chemical process safety management systems for five Fortune 500 companies, of which four facilities achieved OSHA VPP status. I have held positions ranging from Safety Engineer to Corporate Safety and Health Manager with Westvaco, Great Lakes Chemical, General Electric Plastics, BFGoodrich Specialty Chemicals, and SUMCO Phoenix Corporation.

I have extensive experience with Emergency Planning and Response in Fire, HAZMAT, Confined Space, High-Angle Rescue. I maintain all my certifications in these area and have obtained over 6,000 hours of technical training in Industrial Firefighting, Hazardous Materials and Technical Rescue (Confined Space, Trench, High-Angle), and Incident Command from recognized training organizations such as Texas A&M’s National Emergency Response and Rescue Training Center, Louisiana State University Fire and Emergency Training Institute, U.S. Fire Administration’s National Fire Academy, Security and Emergency Response Training Center, and the U.S. Department of Energy’s Nonproliferation Test and Evaluation Complex.

By means of my website, SAFTENG.net, my work is utilized on a weekly basis by over 25,000 safety professionals in more than 75 countries around the world, including staff members of recognized government agencies such as OSHA, NIOSH, EPA, the U.S. Chemical Safety Board (CSB), United Kingdom's Health & Safety Executive, Australia's Work Safe Victoria, and British Columbia’s WORKSAFE.

I am also an adjunct instructor at the University of Cincinnati Fire Science Program, where I teach Safety Management and Emergency Management courses.
