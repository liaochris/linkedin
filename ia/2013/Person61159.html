Jeffrey Brookner's Summary

I counsel individuals and small businesses regarding their legal needs. Whether you are buying or selling a business, negotiating a contract, trying to avoid a lawsuit (or engaged in one), working on estate or succession planning, facing burdensome administrative regulations, or simply seeking advice on how to handle a difficult situation, I am here to help.

For 15 years, I worked at three large law firms handling commercial litigation matters, including banking, franchise, real estate, legal and accounting malpractice, business dissolution, consumer class-action defense, estates and trusts, and many other areas. This background exposed me to a wide array of business issues and helped me learn how to keep problems from spiraling out of control.

In January 2010, I brought my experience home to the Somerset County community where I live and play. My office is on Route 22 in Bridgewater, 3 minutes from downtown Somerville and 5 minutes from my home.

Over the past three years, I have handled a broad variety of matters. I have represented individuals who were forming, purchasing, or selling businesses, or bringing on new partners. I have helped companies refinance their debts or secure other forms of financing. I have counseled both companies and individuals regarding employment relationships. I have drafted wills and related documents, and have helped individuals resolve disputes in that arena. This, and much more.

Meanwhile, I have continued my litigation practice. My philosophy is that litigation is a necessary evil. Evil, because it is expensive and burdensome, diverting both capital and manpower away from the litigants' core mission. Necessary, because without litigation (or the threat of litigation), it can often be difficult to protect what is rightfully yours. Forgetting that litigation is necessary can make you a pushover, but forgetting that litigation is evil rarely works out well.
