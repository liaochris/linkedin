Michael Naman's Projects

  • The Snow Machine

    • December 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Michael Naman, Shaban Siddiq, Yiannis Poulakas, Paul Stockdale, Alex Dunning, Pete Giblin

    Weapon7's 2012 Christmas Card. A one day event held in Covent Garden, London, with a 8 tonne Mercedes-Benz, a snow machine and a lot of fake snow. Users could tweet #snow @thesnowmachine to trigger a 10 second flurry of snow, have their twitter handle displayed on a large LCD screen and have a photo of their snowfall tweeted back to them. All photos were pulling into thesnowmachine.com microsite (now shows wrap-up video and professional photos) during the course of the day. Tweet harvesting through the Twitter API > processing app > arduino circuit board connecting to a relay switch allowed the control of tweet to activate the snow machine. Project managed the whole project, which included event planning with Westminster Council and Covent Garden, prop, vehicle and equipment planning and hire, and website + technical project management. Received 1,000 tweets over the course of the day, coverage in the London Evening Standard newspaper and Metro online and have been voted top of PSFK's top 5 Google Creative Sandbox campaigns.

  • Realtime Man

    • November 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Michael Naman, Paul Stockdale, Shaban Siddiq, Salome Van Leuven, Joel Lim

    Weapon7's presence at Internet Week 2012 was to challenge an individual to attend over 100 events in just 5 days, build up a social network and share as much information as possible about those events with that social network. Time-stamped and geo-located twitter and instagram integration brought all the content together on a single microsite hub. Project managed the entire project from event planning to website production.
