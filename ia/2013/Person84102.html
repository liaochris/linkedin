Mads Nissen's Summary

Solution architect, developer and entrepreneur. Mads has experience from most roles involved in software development. As a certified ScrumMaster he has been responsible for management of 25 developers at 10 concurrent projects. Mads is also an experienced speaker with multiple sessions held at community and Microsoft events, and takes great pride and passion in his work.

At the core Mads is a Microsoft platform technology expert, focusing on SharePoint with more than 10 years of experience in this field. In 2005 he was awarded the Microsoft Most Valuable Professional Award for his community efforts for SharePoint. With experience from delivering projects like large-scale integration, portals, publishing, advisory, coaching and mentoring he is a very versatile resource. In working with these projects Mads has gained experience within domains such as banking, shipping, legal, media, industrial, public sector, technology and telecom.

At the moment Mads is focusing entirely on the startup of Puzzlepart and changing the face of enterprise software with innovative Business Apps for SharePoint. The first business app is "Did - The calendar is the timesheet" which removes the need for traditional timesheets by analyzing the content of your Outlook calendar.

You can make contact on mads dot nissen at puzzlepart dot com.

Mads Nissen's Projects

  • Did - The Calendar is the Timesheet

    • August 2010 to Present
    Team Members: Mads Nissen, Milan Chauhan, Mikael Svenson, Indika Rathnasekara, Andreas Eide, Inoka Silva, Sachintana Dissanayake, Christian Manholt, Kjell-Sverre Jerijærvi, Øystein Garshol

    Did™ simply leverages what you already use: your calendar in Microsoft Outlook. Did knows about your customers and your projects and uses the information in your calendar appointments to build your timesheet. Did™ does not make use of any custom plugins for Outlook, but supports simple conventions to allow you to make sure the timesheet is completed automatically.

    Did™ builds on standard Outlook functionality such as categories and private marking. Your privates will never be seen in Did, and personal appointments you'd like your colleagues to see, you'll just tag it with a category to have Did™ will automatically ignore it.

    With Did™ the Outlook calendar is now your primary tool for completing your timesheets. All you need to do is to make sure that your schedule is accurate.​
