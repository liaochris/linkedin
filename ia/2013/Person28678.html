David Driscoll 07703009736/01473727134's Summary

Homestead PDS Ltd Commercial Painters & Decorators

Once I completed my apprenticeship with qualifications I registered self employed (715) at 18 that was a big step back then in the eighties sub-contractors really were the productive side of skilled/professional labour the PAYE employee was becoming extinct and today is not much different.
Insurance and an accountant were on board and today we still stick by these two credentials.

Working for Countryside Properties gave me a insight into the mechanics of site work and competence. The opportunity of building my knowledge was far easier than someone who had no skills and no experience due to the aprenticeship. Working with sub-contractors was by far the best route as many left to create contacts and businesses. This lead me to the second stage of sub-contracting which was the privilege of working for two sub-contractors which had found their own projects for the Lord Chancellors. Believe you me when these sectors decide to spend money they spend money. This doesn't seem to happen today. We worked from the second week in January to the last week in March.

This period in business is a gold mine, today it seems to be road works. Please take light hearted. The money should be spread all over the public and private sectors even if it means only five days a week. With my own domestic work growing and private work I/we completed over the weekends the next three to four years were quite good fun. Had around five years on good standard new development, three to four years in the public sector learning and completing refurbishment projects all as a sub-contractor, working in teams of two and teams of fifteen. At times we caught the train the congestion charge wasn't around and it cost a fair few quid for parking if a permit or underground car park wasn't available

Specialties: Refurbishment. New Development. Period Properties. Organising & Planning.

Director

Homestead PDS Ltd

April 2007 – Present (6 years 8 months) Ipswich, United Kingdom

Started in the industry 8th September 1986 as an apprentice for Countryside Properties PLC now (UK) Ltd. completed the apprenticeship on 24th May 1989. Achieved advanced craft in theory and practical. Went into self employment on the old 715 scheme and started sub-contracting 25th May 1989. For the next 3 years approximately I stayed and developed my skills in new build developments, having the opportunity to work for Mr Tinsley the better of my two mentors. Taking great steps and in a gang of fifteen worked for The Lord Chancellors Department in the City of London covering local boroughs. This work lasted for around four years and in that time I started to buy a two bedroom purpose built maisonette. This was the eighties and the thing to do. A hindsight view; no it wasn't. From The Lord Chancellors I had a difficult spell with work as the recession kicked in and cuts were made probably information not required but this environment back in the nineties put me in good stead for the recession of the 2000s.
Domestic painting and decorating became a part of my resume and I started to think for myself trying very hard to grow my skills and my contacts. Still enjoying my twenties and building a social network. Client focus is paramount and we provide a sustainable painting and decorating service. To be continued.................

Apprentice

Countryside Properties

September 1986 – May 1989 (2 years 9 months) Billericay

Apprentice Painter/Decorator Course: Basic and advanced skills in the decorating industry, practical and theory.
Spent the first six months on site working in conjunction with other skilled professionals with the added bonus of responsibility to organise and plan exterior site work and communicate with contract/site managers foreman and other such like operatives.

I worked on many high standard builds all round the Essex area, also had the opportunity to learn paper hanging on and away from site. Day release and evening classes were the format for the next two and a half years, yes we studied and had exams about health and safety, scaffolding was then allowed to be erected by a competent person and machinery was in the students remit. We learnt scumble glazing, glazing (window panes), marbling, gold leafing, even how to change a gas bottle.

Quotations and estimates were written and drawn up for us to measure and calculate also how to mix colours, add products to change paint finishes. Paint finishes as a whole. Sign writing was also a major part of the course plus most of the painting was by brush not rollers. Spray painting was in the agenda and much more. Practical skills were my strongest however I earned a credit for theory which was more than acceptable. If I could do it again I'd study theory and practical to achieve the highest qualifications. I use many of the skills I was taught at college for that I'm thankful. Over 22 years in the industry.
