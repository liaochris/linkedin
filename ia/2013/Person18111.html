Brooke Hall's Summary

I'm an entrepreneur who founded and currently leads two Baltimore-based startups. What Weekly is “Baltimore’s Best New Magazine,” which works to rebrand the city by documenting the Baltimore Renaissance. What Works Studio (previously Brooke Hall Creative) is a small, full-service creative agency that helps its clients get noticed in a noisy world.

I strive to create remarkable change by doing great work at the powerful intersection of art and technology and believe microbusinesses have the potential to empower individuals, strengthen the community, and revive the economy.

My writing and photography has been published in New York Times, Spin.com, Baltimore City Paper, Closer Magazine, SmartCEO Magazine, Washington Business Journal and others.

With a background in dance, theater and painting, I earned a B.A. in Philosophy and Literature from the Wilkes Honors College of Florida Atlantic University.

At college, I founded and managed a photography collective and was a producer, director and actor with Theater in the Raw. I previously worked as a travel writer, managing editor, freelance journalist, professional photographer and marketing/PR professional, where I found a front row seat at the collapse of old world media and was inspired by the approaching digital revolution.

I am focused on designing a life of freedom and adventure - and doing work I love. I'd also like to leave the world a little better place than how I found it.
