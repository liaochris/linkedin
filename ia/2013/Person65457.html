joel gratcyk's Summary

Joel is a community manager with broad travel experience, online fundraising success, and viral marketing skills. He was instrumental in bringing the once defunct ska band back to life in 2011 with a Kickstarter campaign that was, at the time, the most funded Music Kickstarter Campaign ever at almost $208,000. The band’s online base has grown, under Joel’s guidance from, less than 15,000 fans to over 310,000 fans in just two years.

Fuel International is where Joel started his career in 1997. He spent 9 years volunteering with the international humanitarian aid non-profit. That time was spent traveling to over 15 countries and overseeing the organization’s websites, technology and various volunteer efforts.

In 2006 Joel became the Project Manager at the Union Church of Hinsdale. His position there includes editorial, graphic design & content work on newsletters, emails, blog posts, podcast, website and social media. He is also responsible for various technical support and organizational planning efforts.

Dad blogging was added to Joel’s portfolio in 2012. His parental storytelling continues to grow in reach as he recently has done some work with Starbucks, The Breakfast Project, Life Of Dad, and The Good Men Project. Joel has also worked with the 10,000 Days web series on a social media launch campaign that passed 10K fans in 30 days.

He has done some strategic website and social media consulting for the Indiana National Guard on how to keep communication effective and helpful for families at home while troops are deployed. Several other one off projects include websites, Kickstarter and social media consulting for Babe Scott (The Lazy Hostess), The OC Supertones, Chris Fason, and She Bears Brewing.
