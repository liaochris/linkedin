James / Jim Decker's Summary

If a product is created and no one buys it, have you been branded?

Gordon Cox proclaimed in Variety: "Many legiters attribute the development of the current merchandise marketplace to Dewynters, which 20 years ago was one of the first companies to realize souvenirs could act as an extension of a show's marketing campaign when emblazoned with a production's instantly recognizable logo, such the feline eyes of "Cats" or the bedraggled waif of "Les Miz."

For over two decades, I have been designing, manufacturing, and retailing souvenir merchandise for live event and entertainment properties. I'm a recognized expert at building / enhancing brand recognition and generating supplemental revenue for clients. As head of Dewynters merchandising division, I create unique retail spaces and themed sales points while developing novel and innovative products for sale and promotional “give aways.”

Ever since I was I young boy...I dreamed of creating and selling souvenir merchandise, since then I have directed merchandising for more than 65 properties including the ING New York City Marathon, Federation of Gay Games, and Paul Simon’s Concert in Central Park; worked with licensed brands such as Garfield and Looney Tunes; and constructed retail stores from New York to Las Vegas. I have operated in hundreds of theatres across North America and venues as diverse as Central Park, the Pacific National Expo in Vancouver Canada, and Yankee Stadium.

In addition to the ING NYC Marathon, last year Dewynters became the official offsite retailer for the US Open and merchandise operator for USTA Jr. Team Tennis & League National Championships.

Our work in theatre ranges from blockbusters such as Phantom of the Opera to not-for-profits including The Roundabout Theatre.

US productions include:

ANYTHING GOES
CHICAGO – THE MUSICAL
THE COLOR PURPLE
LA CAGE AUX FOLLES
GODSPELL
THE PHANTOM OF THE OPERA
YOUNG FRANKENSTEIN

We also operate the PHANTOM BOUTIQUE at the Venetian in Las Vegas.
