Kevin Galvin's Summary

My company, Connecticut Commercial Maintenance, began in 1985 as the original “handyman” service serving residential clients in the Greater Hartford area as "Colonial Handyman." In the mid 90's we evolved into "Connecticut Commercial Maintenance" serving the areas commercial/retail markets with a wide range of repair services. We presently provide services to more than 140 national retail brands doing business in the region. We are the professional "Handyman" for national retailers with a presence in Connecticut.

We offer:

→ An extraordinary wide range of repair services
→ Personally trained technicians
→ Fully-equipped service vehicles
→ Amazingly equipped shop facility
→ Service the entire State of Connecticut and beyond

In addition to our service work, we are sought out for training, consulting, emergency management and dispute mediaton.

Our service area includes the entire state of Connecticut, Central Massachusetts,selected locations in Eastern New York State.

Serving nonprofit organizations throughout the state of Connecticut.

Kevin Galvin's Experience

Small Business Advocate

Small Business For A Healthy Connecticut

2007 – Present (6 years) Connecticut

Kevin Galvin is the founding chair of the Small Business for a Healthy Connecticut advisory committee and is best known for his efforts as an advocate for the interests of small business in the reform of health care systems. Galvin participated in the national health care reform debate that culminated in the passage and signing of the Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act in March 2010. He and his coalition also played a role in the passage of Connecticut’s SustiNet health care reform plan in 2009.

In August 2009 Kevin Galvin hosted Secretary of Health and Human Services Kathleen Sebelius, Small Business Administration Administrator Karen Mills, Senator Chris Dodd and Representative Rosa DeLauro at his Connecticut Commercial Maintenance shop facility for a discussion of health care reform and business. In October 2009, he was invited to the White House with a group of small business owners from across the nation to share his ideas about what small business need in order to grow and strengthen the economy and communities.

Galvin continues to advocate for Small Business in the area of Health Care Reform in Connecticut working with partners on the implementation of CT's Health Insurance Exchange.

In 2012 Galvin was elected Board Secretary of Connecticut's first non-profit Health Insurance carrier HealthyCT.

Most recently Galvin founded www.CTJOBSNOW.biz a grassroots organization to call for Connecticut's State Legislature to actively engage Small Business to create a strategies to improve Connecticut's economy.
