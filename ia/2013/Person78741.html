Laura Cerrano's Summary

Thank you for stopping by and taking the time to review my profile. My name is Laura Cerrano, I have been a Certified Feng Shui Ascension expert for over 13 years. I've been honored to study with some of the finest Masters in Feng Shui, Ascension and Shamanism to combine with my Native American background. Mindfully blending Classical, Black Sect, Ba-gua, Form, Mystery School teachings, and Western Feng Shui methods allows for various insights to help empower each client with the true knowledge and deeper understanding of Feng Shui.

My love of Feng Shui was born when I began working and assisting with my mother (Carole Provenzale) during Feng Shui consultations at the early age of 16. I saw the profound impacts Feng Shui was creating in our client’s lives and eventually opened my own practice called Feng Shui Manhattan in 2000. Since that time I have had the privilege and honor of working with hundreds of residents, local businesses owners, Corporations and New Construction projects.

In March of 2013 I teamed up with two other Top Feng Shui Experts, various spiritual healers, believers and skeptics to collaborate with PMA Productions Inc a Documentary called, "The Upside to Superstition." The goal of this film is to help to share insight into the world of Feng Shui along with other cultural practices from around the world. The Film is due to come out October 31st, 2013 airing in Canada first.

  • Bless This House

    • The Real Deal
    • December 1, 2012
    Authors: Laura Cerrano

    For the past few years, it’s become increasingly popular for apartment buyers in New York to use the ancient Chinese practice of feng shui to evaluate potential apartment buys. But the Vista is taking the feng shui craze to the next level: The building is one of the only new condos in the city to be designed fully in accordance with feng shui principles, which aim to change a space’s energy through blessings and placement of rooms and furniture.

    Cerrano advised the developers on every facet of the building. For example, the Vista does not have a fourth floor — since the number four can be considered bad luck — and the apartment entrances are configured to avoid facing other interior doors, which is believed to allow energy to escape. Craig Axelrod of the Emmy Companies, which is developing the 48-unit building along with Lions Group, said he doesn’t know of any other buildings that have gone so far to incorporate feng shui into their design.
