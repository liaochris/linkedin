Justin Henceroth's Summary

Justin Henceroth is a Henry Luce Scholar with the Thailand Environment Institute where he helps local partners and cities develop solutions for development in the face of urbanization and climate change. Prior to his work in Thailand, Justin was an Associate and Fellow at Meridian Institute, where he designed and managed multi-stakeholder engagement and collaborative decision making processes.

Justin is recognized as a committed and talented collaboration professional with the ability to help stakeholders develop novel solutions to long-standing and complex challenges. He has expertise in collaborative process design and project management with a focus on stakeholder engagement, program development, consensus building, and strategy planning processes. He has worked with local, national and international governments, leading international NGOs and foundations, local stakeholders and community organizations, and the private sector ranging from small businesses to Fortune 500 enterprises.

His work has led to enduring solutions that continue to have a positive impact. In addition, he and his projects have been recognized with a number of awards, including one project being named a Flagship Open-Government Initiative by President Obama. In 2012, Justin was awarded the National Environmental Collaboration and Conflict Resolution Award for 'innovative and exemplary' collaborative efforts.

  Justin is also an accomplished mogul skier and scientist. In 2011 his undergraduate thesis research utilizing chemical patterns in dinosaur teeth to infer migratory behavior was published in Nature. Between 2004 and 2009 he competed multiple times as a finalist at North American World Cup Events and US National Championships.

Justin Henceroth's Projects

  • Asian Cities Climate Change Resilience Network (ACCCRN)

    Team Members: Justin Henceroth

    ACCCRN is developing, testing and demonstrating practical strategies for responding to the impacts of climate change and urbanization in ten cities in Thailand, Vietnam, India, and Indonesia. While the work of ACCCRN partners focuses on building resilience at the city level, the Network is also regional and global in its outlook and outreach. One of the key objectives of ACCCRN is to share success stories and encourage cities around the world to replicate effective strategies and activities. The Network aims to expand and deepen the base of urban climate change resilience work to achieve greater scale beyond the existing cities.

  • USAID Mekong Building Climate Resilient Asian Cities (M-BRACE)

    Team Members: Justin Henceroth

    M-BRACE is a regional project working in four cities in Thailand and Vietnam to better understand the impacts of climate change and urbanization and to build resilience to address those challenges. M-BRACE is also working with academics in both countries to expand research on resilience and to build a body of knowledge for applying the concept locally.

  • US Forest Service Planning Rule Revision

    Team Members: Justin Henceroth

    Meridian Institute' served as the lead contractor assisting the US Forest Service in conducting an open, collaborative process to create and implement a new regulation governing forest planning in the National Forest system. The planning rule revision was one of the flagship projects of President Obama’s Open Government Initiative and connected with over ten thousand stakeholders across a variety of mediums. The multi-year collaboration process ended in early 2012 with the publication of the new Planning Rule. However, the US Forest Service is currently building on the successful collaboration to implement the new rule and use it to write new forest plans.

    Justin Henceroth served as the project manager, led youth outreach, oversaw online collaboration, and managed the production of all reports for the effort.

  • Colorado Smart Grid Task Force

    Team Members: Justin Henceroth

    In 2010, Meridian Institute's facilitated and supported the deliberative process of the Colorado Smart Grid Task Force and the preparation of a final report for the Governor of Colorado, the Colorado General Assembly, and the state’s Public Utilities Commission. The report contained recommendations for the transition to a secure, resilient, and technologically advanced electric grid – a “Smart Grid.”

    Justin Henceroth served as project manager, handled all logistics, documented the process, and drafted and oversaw production of the final report.

  • Community Resilience System

    Team Members: Justin Henceroth

    The Community Resilience System (CRS) is a web-based system that can help communities assess its capacity to withstand and recover from significant disturbances. The CRS helps communities create a vision for the future and establish the necessary actions to improve overall resilience to disasters and other disturbances. The system will help people prepare for - and recover from - any challenge.

    Justin Henceroth was a key member of the team that developed the system. Justin helped with the early stage research, system design, and development of specific elements of the CRS.

  • Volunteer Technical Community Strategy Planning

    Team Members: Justin Henceroth

    Meridian Institute provided strategy planning services for two start-up nongovernmental organizations supporting global networks of technical and technological volunteer efforts to support disaster responders and disaster response. Humanitarian OpenStreetMap Team (HOT) conducts open-srouce mapping to provide reliable and up-to-date geolocation data for emergency responders. Crisis Commons serve as a central support and knowledge sharing network for volunteer technical responders with a variety of skills and the groups and organizations that can employ them in response efforts.

    Justin Henceroth co-led the strategy planning processes for both groups.

  • Asia-Pacific Disaster Risk Reduction and Resilience Network

    Team Members: Justin Henceroth

    Meridian Institute supported the discussions of the Asia-Pacific Disaster Risk Reduction and Resilience Network (AP DR3 Network) with a focus on establishing a formal structure for a secretariat. The AP DR3 Network, which believes public-private collaboration on disaster risk reduction and resilience can help to save lives, ensure economic vitality, and enhance human well-being across the region, was formalized under joint statement of intent at APEC 2011 and counts US Pacific Command, the US Chamber of Commerce, University of Hawaii System, US Agency for International Development, National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration, Ford Foundation, Rockefeller Foundation, US-Japan Council, and Federal Emergency Management Agency.

    Justin Henceroth provided research, proposal writing, and report development support for the project.

  • Bill and Melinda Gates Foundation: Agriculture Development Strategy Refresh

    Team Members: Justin Henceroth

    Meridian Instituted served as the lead contractor for the strategy refresh of the Agricultural Development program at the Bill and Melinda Gates Foundation. The Gates Foundation is second only to the United States in funding agricultural development in Africa, and the process aimed to find ways to leverage this large investment for greater impact.

    Justin Henceroth documented and helped facilitate the effort.

  • Microsoft Global Security Strategy and Diplomacy Planning

    Team Members: Justin Henceroth

    Meridian facilitated an internal strategic planning retreat for Microsoft’s new Global Security Strategy and Diplomacy team. Microsoft team members from around the world came together to develop a strategy and roadmap for their work going forward, and discussed issues relating to targeted customers, trustworthy computing initiatives, critical infrastructure protection and resiliency, cyber security, the role of diplomacy, and global security, among other topics.

    Justin Henceroth documented the deliberations and prepared a summary report of the meeting.

  • America's Nuclear Future Blue Ribbon Commission

    Team Members: Justin Henceroth

    Meridian Institute designed and convened five meetings for the Blue Ribbon Commission on America’s Nuclear Future (BRC). The 15-member Commission was mandated with conducting a comprehensive review of policies for managing the back end of the nuclear fuel cycle and recommending a new plan to the U.S. Department of Energy. The meetings invited citizens and key stakeholders to comment on the findings of the Commission's draft report and to offer additional feedback on addressing nuclear fuel waste.

    Justin facilitated discussion groups at the meetings and prepared summary reports of the input.

  • America's Great Watershed Initiative

    Team Members: Justin Henceroth

    America’s Great Watershed Initiative (AGWI) is a collaboration that seeks solutions for meeting—for present and future generations—the multiple demands placed on the vast and complex Mississippi watershed system by integrating issues, partners, and ideas at the full watershed scale. The AGWI process began in early 2010 with an assessment of key stakeholders from diverse perspectives to hear their thoughts about the need for a shared vision and a coherent management strategy for the watershed. Since then, a steering committee has been working to build a basin wide structure that identifies comprehensive solutions while also respecting and encouraging local efforts throughout the basin.

    Justin Henceroth was involved with AGWI since the beginning, helping conduct the assessment, stand up the steering committee, and provide ongoing process support.

  • Meridian Internal Strategy team

    Team Members: Justin Henceroth

    Meridian's strategy team tracks emerging issues, trends in collaboration and problem solving, and developments across a number of sectors in order to provide ongoing advice to the organization as it expands and develops projects and project areas. Justin Henceroth was a founding member of the Strategy Team.

  • Colorado Roadless Rule Public Meetings

    Team Members: Justin Henceroth

    In the summer of 2011, Meridian Institute supported a public engagement process on the Colorado Roadless Rule for Region 2 of the U.S. Forest Service. The Colorado Roadless Rule aims to provide management and conservation direction for roadless areas in Colorado’s National Forests. Meridian Institute, in consultation with the U.S. Institute for Environmental Conflict Resolution, helped to design and facilitate a series of nine public meetings throughout the state of Colorado. A total of more than 300 stakeholders attended.

    Justin Henceroth served as the lead facilitator for the public meetings.

  • University of Wyoming Hydraulic Fracturing Forum

    Team Members: Justin Henceroth

    Meridian Institute worked with the University of Wyoming’s School of Energy Resources, Ruckelshaus Institute, and Haub School of Environment and Natural Resources to convene, manage, and facilitate a large public forum on the practice of hydraulic fracturing in oil and gas development. Meridian facilitated a Steering Committee composed of representatives from industry, regulatory agencies, and the environmental community, who guided the development of the forum agenda and selected speakers to represent a diversity of expertise and perspectives on hydraulic fracturing. Meridian also facilitated the forum itself.

    Justin Henceroth documented the forum and helped draft the final report.

  • Community Conversations on H1N1 Vaccine

    Team Members: Justin Henceroth

    In 2010, Meridian worked with Seattle-King County Washington to conduct a series of community and high-level stakeholder workshops to review the H1N1 Influenza vaccination efforts in 2009 and 2010.

    Justin Henceroth facilitated discussions on the project and prepared the final summary report.

  • Meridian Fellowship

    Team Members: Justin Henceroth

    The Meridian Fellowship is a two-year professional fellowship intended to provide young professionals with core skills in writing, project management, and collaborative processes. The program also provides mentoring and professional development support. Justin Henceroth led the team that proposed and implemented the Fellowship and led the Fellows selection process in 2011 and 2012.
