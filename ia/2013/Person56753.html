NOTE: Hey, I'm not currently looking for any full time positions. Please do not contact me. I am also booked as far as contracts are concerned.

Another NOTE: I'm actually not the best professional writer. I have edited this many times to be concise and technical, but that isn't me. Plus, it is really hard to reproduce quality summaries from better written profiles.


My geek programming life started when I was 11 learning HTML and later CSS. I briefly studied Java, C, C++ prior to picking up PHP in 2000. My focus may have shifted from learning to develop video games to web development, but it has always been learning how to be a better programmer.

My professional programming life started in 2007, when Branson Tourism Center invited me into their family. That was a lot of fun and very different from what I expected. At BTC, I worked primarily on PHP, HTML, CSS and JavaScript. I briefly worked with Python and Filemaker. It was a fun run, but it was no longer challenging and we parted ways.

During 2007 through 2009, I contributed to the WordPress open-source project adding documentation and fixing bugs. I introduced the WP_HTTP class and various other small features still used today, albeit with many modifications and improvements by other community members.
