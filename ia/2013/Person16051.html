Providing expert real estate advice is expected of any Realtor. My skills are enhanced by my unique experience as a licensed builder and over 20 years of experience from a previous career. I am dedicated to my real estate clients, offering them a commitment to unparalleled customer service. These additional skills and a drive to exceed expectations led me to become the top selling new agent in the Keller Williams East office in 2011 & I was named “Rookie of the Year” for the entire State of Michigan and Northern Ohio region of Keller Williams Realty, with sales exceeding $8M. In 2012, I ranked in the Top 10 in sales volume at Keller Williams GR East office, out of approximately 162 Realtors (Including all Teams / Groups). My path into real estate truly began over ten years ago, when I obtained my builders license and began renovating homes. A passion evolved, as I personally purchased, renovated and sold seven homes. I was able to manage all of this while working as a full time police officer in the city of Grand Rapids. This required dedication and a superior work ethic. Throughout my 20+ year career, I worked equally hard in a wide variety of roles including Road Patrol, Training Supervisor (Lead Firearms & Defensive Tactics Instructor), SWAT Team, Vice, Internal Affairs and Patrol Supervisor. I received numerous awards / recognition as a result of my actions, including two separate nominations for Police Officer of the Year. Critically important to my continued development is the vast experience gained from this life altering work. This lends to my character and credibility and has carried over to help manage a myriad of challenges that all buyers & sellers face, as I seek to sell your home or find you a place to call home.

Former Police Sergeant

Grand Rapids Police Department

December 1991 – May 2012 (20 years 6 months) 1 Monroe Center N.W. Grand Rapids, Michigan 49501

A 20 year career in Law Enforcement, in the City of Grand Rapids, taught me a great deal about life. I don't know if there is another job that can prepare someone for the many ups and downs that life throws at you. It instilled a work ethic and drive in me to succeed in anything I choose to do. My law enforcement career in Grand Rapids allowed me to work in many different assignments. I worked in the Neighborhood Patrol Unit (currently the SRT - Special Response Team). This team had specialized training to manage critical incidents (barricaded gunmen), the execution of high risk search warrants, and other tactical situations. As a member of this team, I was also assigned as a police sniper. While a member of the NPU and during an assignment in our Vice Unit, I was involved in numerous undercover drug buys, typically purchasing crack cocaine from street level dealers. I also managed many informants that would provide reliable information on criminal activity in and around GR. For a period of six (6) years, I was the lead instructor in the Grand Rapids Police Department, for both Firearms and Defensive Tactics training. I was an active instructor in both for 17 years. In 1999, I was promoted to Sergeant and assigned as a Patrol Supervisor. After several years on both 2nd and 3rd shift, working the southeast side of town, I was reassiged to the Internal Affairs Unit (IAU) as an Investigator. In IAU, was called upon to investigate various complaints against all employees working at GRPD. Investigations are initiated and completed for minor procedural violations, up to and including, major criminal acts. IAU also conducts procedural reviews for all officer involved shootings. My most memorable investigation surrounded the death (homicide) of one of our police officers. The courage and teamwork displayed by officers attempting to rescue their fallen comrade, was nothing short of heroic.
