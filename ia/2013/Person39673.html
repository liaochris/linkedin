Ed Noyes' Publications

  • Small Business Electronic Commerce Implementation: A Case Study

    • Journal of Internet Commerce
    • 2006
    Authors: Ed Noyes, Ian MacInnes

    This case study outlines experiences of a small stationer with electronic commerce implementation that should be valuable for many similar companies attempting to provide Internet services to their customers. It describes the origin of the organization and the factors that prompted its CEO to add electronic commerce to its business model. The company's objective was to grow at several levels. The study describes the many challenges that the company had to overcome, including handling growth while maintaining the same staff size, integration of old and new databases, personal service in an online environment, potential change in business solutions from custom-made to packaged solutions, and limitations in terms of budget and expertise. The case outlines the steps taken to achieve these goals, including IT decisions such as outsourcing, software, and database choices.

Ed Noyes' Projects

  • Lennox Capital Management - Financial Services Website

    • September 2010 to December 2010
    Team Members: Ed Noyes

    ennox Capital Management provides investment planning services along with group and individual insurance and benefit programs.

    This site provides fast self-service access to client accounts. It also gives easy-to-use links to relevant information on external provider websites. One challenge was to embed a secure log-in feature on the home page to take clients directly to remotely hosted investment accounts. Since they are licensed financial advisers the site also had to meet strict regulatory guidelines.

  • Nale Law - Elder Care Law Firm

    • March 2011 to May 2011
    Team Members: Ed Noyes

    The Elder Law Offices of John and Mark Nale needed a website that not only described their services, but also showcased their involvement in the community and issues affecting the elder population. The solution was to create an automated slide show that highlights the Nale brothers' support for local services. Each feature can be clicked to explore more information. The site had to also be friendly to older users who might have disabilities.

  • Riverbend Condos - Vacation Condo Website

    • March 2010 to April 2010
    Team Members: Ed Noyes

    Riverbend at Sunday River is one of Maine's top 4-season vacation condo destinations.

    Our client needed a new site to promote rentals and condo sales, as well as improve online reservations. Customers (returning and new) are using mobile devices more and more, so optimizing the site for mobile users was another priority. With that in mind navigation and important sales copy appear in the upper left of the page to minimize scrolling and making navigation easier across a variety of platforms. The slide show features views of unit interiors, while the string of static photos highlights vacation and lifestyle possibilities. The site has many other features including integration with a robust reservation system.

  • Stained Glass Express - Ecommerce Website

    • May 2012 to July 2012
    Team Members: Ed Noyes

    Stained Glass Express is the largest distributor of stained glass and supplies north of Boston.

    In business for 24 years the company needed to revamp and upgrade its website to incorporate advanced SEO features and better showcase its many products and services. The database used by the old website was out of sync with the retail POS system. Thousands of products had to be brought in line to establish consistency between the two main channels. The project also involved launching the new site in parallel with the old site, creating new content, transferring email accounts, and migrating data. The site runs on X-Cart e-commerce software and includes calendar and multiple slideshow elements.

  • O & P Glass - Commercial and Residential Glass Company

    • August 2012 to September 2012
    Team Members: Ed Noyes

  • Winslow-Oakland-Unity-Fairfield Pharmacy Group

    • November 2011 to April 2012
    Team Members: Ed Noyes

    Winslow Pharmacy, Fairfield Pharmacy, Unity Pharmacy, and Oakland Pharmacy all share common ownership. Yet each has established its own unique community brand. The challenge was to create a new online presence without the benefit of a unifying brand. The solution was microsites. Each site has its own URL to optimize search engine results. Since the keywords are in the name of each store four domain names were purchased - winslowpharmacy.net, oaklandpharmacy.net, fairfieldpharmacy.net, and unitypharmacy.net. The .net top-level domain was chosen since .com was taken for some of the names. Each site was built with a consistent look and feel and customized content. The navigation links all four sites together creating the impression of a single site regardless of which URL was used to reach the group.

  • RMS Ornamental Iron - Steel Fabrication Firm

    • July 2010 to August 2010
    Team Members: Ed Noyes

    RMS Ornamental Iron is Western Maine's premier custom steel fabricator.

    This creative client's work can be found throughout Western Maine and beyond. Products include railings, gates, ring signs, and custom logo park benches. In business for over 10 years the client needed a basic website to showcase his work, provide general information, and solicit inquiries form potential customers. The home page has a slider, featuring examples of the wide range of product designs. Visitors can then follow links to view a more detailed gallery, to get directions, or to send a message.
