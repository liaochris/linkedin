Gary Belk's Summary

I find hidden potential in homes so you can profit.

I often hear, “I’ve been trying to buy a home but, competition is so fierce and every time I make an offer I get overbid.”

I know what it’s like to be in your shoes too. I've bought, renovated and sold over 20 properties of my own and have overseen the renovation of many more. I understand your frustration. We are in a booming market in San Francisco, yet I have a knack for locating properties that are flying under the radar. Often these are properites hiding their potential. Hidden because of the condition, improper use or, because they are not marketed properly.

I can locate homes in a tight market. With 25+ years as a Realtor and general contractor combined with a degree in architecture, I am very good at illuminating the hidden potential in houses and forming a winning strategy to buying them. I use the techniques and tools I’ve learned over the years to create a unique solution for every buyer and property.

For example, a client, who I helped purchase a Noe Valley cottage. A cute little place that was under the radar due to poor marketing. We wrote a very clean offer, $50k under asking and we got the house! Unfortunately, as soon as the deal closed, her job changed and she had to sell. With just a light renovation, we sold the property 6 months later for a $150K profit. She doubled her renovation dollars. Renovation Consultant

Home Visionaries

July 2011 – Present (2 years 4 months) San Francisco Bay Area

As a Renovation Consultant I help you create an action-plan for your home renovation project that makes sense. This plan will include establishing a budget and developing a prioritized list of needs and wishes. What’s more, the process of creating the plan will help you understand what to expect, both before, during, and after, your home renovation project is completed.

If needed, I can also become part of your permanent home renovation team, ensuring that communication, quality, and scheduling are maintained. Acting as your agent on site, they can be your eyes and ears while you continue to live your life.

Renovation consultants also can assist you in the purchase of a property. In some cases – for example, if you are looking in a specific location where there are few choices within your budget - buying a “fixer-upper” may be the best option for you. If so, spending $150 to $300 on a consultant before finalizing the purchase of a property can prevent surprises down the road. By carefully examining the layout, current condition and zoning limitations, and then overlaying your needs and wants, the consultant can advise you about the design potential and the functional limitations of the property.

While every home renovation project is unique, the items a good renovation consultant will help you with will typically include at least the following:

Pre-Purchase Advice
Fix or Move – Completing a Move vs. Remodel analysis to help you determine if you should move or do a home renovation
Feasibility Study - Assessing existing conditions
Renovation Planning
