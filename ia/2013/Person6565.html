Andrea Visak, RD, CSSD's Summary

Physical activity, exercise, and athletic performance are enhanced by optimal nutrition. The daily types, quantities, and timing of foods and fluids assist athletes to maintain health, delay fatigue during activity, improve recovery from illness or injury, and enhance the ability to train effectively. Proper nutrition promotes adaptation to the stimulus of training and contributes to improvements in athletic performance. Andrea as a Sports Dietitian counsels athletes regarding food and fluid choices to meet their nutrition needs throughout the various phases of the annual training and competition plan. In addition, her role as a Sports dietitian is to evaluate, educate, and advise pertaining to the safety, advocacy, and efficacy of dietary supplements and ergogenic aids.

The physiological demands of exercise and sport and their effects on nutritional status vary during individual phases of training and competition. Maintaining nutritional adequacy in response to varying requirements for energy, fluid, and nutrients can be challenging. Formulating optimal nutrition guidelines for athletes and active individuals is dependent on understanding the physiological demands of exercise and the impact of training/competition phase on energy and nutrient metabolism.

Interest in sports foods, dietary supplements, and purported nutritional ergogenic aids is common among athletes seeking to gain a competitive edge. Evaluation of these products relative to legality, safety, efficacy, quality, and application to sports is an essential skill.
