Linda Hartley's Summary

Linda C. Hartley has worked with major New York institutions in education, the arts, advocacy, and social services for over thirty years. She and Hartley Consulting, Inc. have helped over 40 nonprofits raise over $400 million.

Hartley was previously Vice President for External Affairs at The Cooper Union for the Advancement of Science and Art. She led development and campaign efforts at The Cooper Union, Bard College, Columbia University, and New School University. Earlier, she managed campaign and major gift programs at The New York Public Library and special projects at New York University, where she earned a Masters of Business Administration in 1986. Much earlier, she co-founded The Cutting Edge Theatre.

Hartley served two terms as president of Women in Development, New York. She currently serves as a Vice President on the board of directors for the New York Women’s Agenda, and as a member of the boards of the Association of Fundraising Professionals of Greater New York and the Heyman Center for Philanthropy and Fundraising at New York University, where she is also an adjunct assistant professor.

She teaches NYU’s ten-session course, Fundraising Concepts and Practices and a summer intensive session on major gifts. She has lectured on ethics and the role of leadership and advocacy in the nonprofit sector at NYU, Columbia University, American Technion Society, and AFP’s annual conference, Fundraising Day in New York. Recent leadership trainings and planning retreats include El Museo del Barrio (sponsored by the Rockefeller Brothers Fund), Sakhi for South Asian Women, and The Bronx Museum of the Arts.

Specialties

Hartley’s expertise encompasses nonprofit governance, management, communications and planning; staff and board development and training; recruitment; capital campaigns; major, planned, and annual giving programs.
