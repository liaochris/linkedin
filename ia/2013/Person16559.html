Brandon Schatsiek's Summary

My name is Brandon Schatsiek and I am a 2010 graduate from the University of Missouri's School of Journalism with a major in convergence journalism and a minor in religious studies.

Currently I am the Marketing/PR Coordinator for Special Olympics Missouri, based in Jefferson City.

In 2012, I served as a campaign ambassador for the Central Illinois United Way. I was in charge of ~40 accounts where it was my job to help those businesses raise as much money as possible for the 2012 United Way campaign. My responsibilities ranged from coordinating agency tours to giving presentations about what the United Way was and how the donations coming in were being put to good use.

From 2010-2012, I was the editor of the Washington Times-Reporter in Washington, Ill. Not only was I the editor of the newspaper, but the writer, photographer and layout designer as well.

I have experience writing and producing news/content for six professional newsrooms. I have experience with writing stories at the Columbia Missourian, KMOV.com in St. Louis, the Boulder Daily Camera and for World Vision Appalachia. I have shot and edited video stories for KOMU-TV in Columbia, Missouri as well as CurrentTV.com and World Vision Appalachia. I have also reported and edited stories for KBIA-FM in Columbia as well as produced print graphics with Illustrator and interactive graphics with Flash for the Missourian. I have experience in many different aspects of journalism and even know XHTML coding. I also have an eagerness and willingness to learn new and exciting things.

I am very interested in finding jobs where I can utilize my journalism degree in the non-profit sector.

Specialties

I have experience with: Illustrator, Flash, Actionscript 3.0, XHTML coding, Photoshop, Final Cut Pro, Avid Media Composer, Audacity, Soundslides, Dreamweaver, InDesign, Microsoft Access
