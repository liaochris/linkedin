Manoj Kandasamy's Summary

“Fascination”- I can’t find any better word to describe my career.

Advertising fascinated me. Not making more money. That made me work without a pay for first 6 months of my career. Crazy? Nope. I enjoyed it. Really. That enthusiasm landed me in a job that paid well and sharpen my skills. Technology fascinated me. That made me to quit a safe job and join a technology start-up during the worst recession in the history of my generation. It paid. I learned how to market technology to ad agencies and using social media for PR. Bingo!. Digital advertising fascinated me. So did Travel. Both at the same time. So I took a break for 3 months and traveled across Tamilnadu in local buses, trains, met interesting strangers. When I returned to my job hunt, a crazy idea fascinated me. So I do this social campaign called “who is my next boss”. Got crazy buzz. Get 10 calls. 8 meetings. 4 offers. Took one. Pitching ideas fascinates me. Though some see that as a PPT, for me that's an opportunity to beat myself. 2 rocking pitches. One for a new brand. Another for new social idea to an existing client. Both a roaring success. I love the job. I’m happy with the job. Here comes mobile. I’m fascinated again! Now i'm part of the best mobile team you can find in India (Mobile agency of the year according to MMA) - Maxus Digital. Can't ask for a better team!

What fascinates me now?

Not campaigns or awards.

Is there a way where we can challenge the marketing norms?

How can we move the industry forward?

Can we able to help CMOs use digital data to take informed and disruptive marketing decisions?

Can ZMOT be the backbone of our approach? (Well, Google coined this term for their advantage. But what the hell, I love the concept. This opens up immense opportunities for a marketer.)

My career has thought me some life lessons! There are lot of things that will fascinate us if we stay curious. Next time you fly, think this for a minute - "I'm SITTING in a chair 1000ft from the ground!"
