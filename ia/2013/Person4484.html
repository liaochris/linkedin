Alice Morgan's Summary

Jack of all trades? Yes probably.

A freelance digital marketing professional with a highly pragmatic approach, I have worked on both agency and client sides for 22 years - mainly within the automotive sector (I love cars).

Here's what I mainly do for my two main clients, Chevrolet Europe and Opel Vauxhall Europe:

- Content strategy - including copywriting and usability consultancy
- Digital media strategy development
- Pan-European Search
- Data leverage
- Digital training to various stakeholders at different levels
- Consultancy to other GM regions and stakeholders

I started out in corporate events, moved into retail advertising at BMP DDB in London and from there over to the client side at Chevrolet Europe, initially based in Zurich. I'm now in London.

I speak German fluently and can read several other European languages with relatively passable spoken French and Italian.

Specialties: Content strategy development, SEO consultant and strategist, web copy writer, website management, search engine marketing, search engine optimization, dealer advertising and marketing, digital strategist, performance analytics strategist and presentation expert.

Alice Morgan's Experience

Digital marketing strategist and SEO consultant

Opel Vauxhall

January 2009 – Present (5 years) Ruesselsheim, Germany

Opel Vauxhall Europe has a complex semi-centralised structure and it's here that a pragmatic approach is key. My role is to support the OVE team to define the backbone of the central digital media support. Clear communication, the development of easy-to-understand strategic pillars, setting objectives and getting the buy-in of a large stakeholder group are all part of this. My main focus here is search, specifically SEO, but I also advise on web content and usability, as these affect media (and business) performance.

Content Strategist and Search Consutant

Chevrolet Europe

Public Company; 501-1000 employees; Automotive industry

August 2004 – Present (9 years 5 months) Zurich, Switzerland

I've been working freelance for Chevrolet Europe for 9 years. For the last 7, I have supported my client Tamara Warren as a member of the B2C website and digital team. We centrally manage 39 European markets (44 websites).

When Tamara first asked me to help her, back in 2006, the two of us did everything - including many things we had never done before. So we learnt fast and read a lot. Together we set up the business processes (since been adopted by many other GM global entities), worked on metrics, defined budgets and allocations per market, oversaw creative development, defined the digital media strategies and much more besides. In short, we've always had to be innovative, pragmatic and entrepreneurial.

I still I get involved in many aspects of the business, but now my main responsibility is for central content strategy and development. Together with our agency partners, I plan and produce (or obtain from other GM entities) revelant, resonating content that meets the needs of consumers (compelling, scannable, benefit-led and, if appropriate, also sharable) while also driving sales. On a shoe-string. What's new is that this content now has to render correctly on whatever device our site visitors are using. Working with our experience planner, I have just re-written all the websites' product pages so they work in Responsive Web Design.

In 2008 I designed, set up and then - until recently - ran the Chevrolet SEO programme. I am now its strategic consultant. Serving 20 markets, we try to keep our objectives simple and achieveable. I have also done a lot of work with the digital marketing manager and data manager to maximise efficiency and effectiveness through the closer integration of natural with paid search. To this end, we have done a lot of testing to understand the symbiosis of the two search channels. Rina Patel and I addressed the Adobe Summit on our interim findings last year. And there's a lot more to discover in this increasingly interesting area.
