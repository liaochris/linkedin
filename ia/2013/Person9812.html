Angela Robar's Summary

Mission: Provide a marketing program for wineries and wine importers that includes strategy, execution, measurement, and ongoing maintenance. It shouldn't be consuming, or complicated; it needs to have a return-on-investment quickly, and provide information to use in decision making.

Why? Technology enables us to do a lot for not a whole lot, but it's important to use it to its potential, which means incorporating proper marketing planning, brand differentiation, and execution. We need businesses to succeed the good old fashioned way - by providing something the market desires. We know there's an appetite for wine, it's a matter of connecting to the consumers who want our products specifically.

How I arrived here:
Ten plus years marketing in the technology sector, working across all industries.
Design and execution of hundreds of multi-tier channel marketing programs.
Experience helping companies of all sizes improve their marketing and sales.
Appreciation and vision for the potential of technology.

Interests in wine, food, travel, and a look ahead in life to ask "Where do I really want to be, working so hard?"

Foray into the wine industry. Enough professional training and experience to quickly pick up on the unique challenges and see the exciting opportunities for an improved marketing approach that leverages technology.

An inherent drive to shake up the status quo when it's not working, and help businesses succeed with a balanced combination of proven plus different.

Angela Robar's Projects

  • Ambrosia Wine Group & Maestri Vignaioli

    • June 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Angela Robar

    When seven small estates from Italy collectively go-to-market in a highly competitive wine marketplace like Seattle, it's a great thing, but also a big challenge to organize and leverage every opportunity to make an impact. New importer and distributor Ambrosia Wine Group knew they needed something different--yet very cohesive--to help this group break into the marketplace and get traction. Cost is always a factor, so they signed up for the Vinette marketing services platform which includes: the mobile and web infrastructure to deliver each wineries' unique story, provide insight for each wine, and collectively introduce the group; QR codes to make access to the information highly available and relevant; and the point-of-sale marketing materials to make the information available to on- and off-premise buyers and consumers alike. Vinette provided in-language (Italian) submission forms, and completed all translations--extracting the unique details that make each producer special, but also ensuring a consistency of information is available across all producers and wines.

  • LEAP Licensee National Rollout

    • December 2011 to Present
    Team Members: Angela Robar

    LEAP is a 12-month cohort-based leadership acceleration program that develops mid-career managers into leaders who help differentiate their organizations and drive these organizations forward to a successful future. The program includes individualized coaching, a curriculum that is customized to participants' specific development needs, and action-based skill building activities for their unique environment. Unlike many programs, progress is tracked. Participants and their managers are able to see measurable increases in skill and confidence from the start of the program to the finish. As an alumni of LEAP myself, I welcomed the opportunity to help build the plan and package to roll out to other markets through a network of certified professionals, extending the opportunity to others and helping improve workforce leadership nationwide.

  • Willie's World Cycling Content Refresh

    • August 2012 to December 2012
    Team Members: Angela Robar

    When you have as much passion for what you do as Willie Joffroy of Willie's World Cycling, you're naturally going to have a lot to say. You're going to captivate people with the stories of your adventures cycling the ancient hill-towns and vineyards of Piedmont and coastal vistas of Tuscany. You'll have their mouth watering when you tell of the perfectly paired foods and wines from the wonderful small-estate wineries. But ask them to read this depth of information on a website, well, that's a whole lotta work! Having the good fortune to have worked with Willie before, I've been captivated by his personal accounts and stories of adventure; I was intrigued by the challenge to consolidate his prose into snippets of content, enhanced with paired images, more suitable for web reading.

  • Walla Walla Valley Wine Alliance - Traveling Event Series

    • January 2012 to March 2012
    Team Members: Angela Robar

    45 Wineries travel together to showcase the gorgeous wines they produce in Walla Walla, WA, to the trade and consumers. Showing up to four wines each means there's a lot to try in a short window of a few hours--what can be done to improve retention in the minds of buyers and consumers? Enter Vinette. We loaded up two wines each and all the info on the winery and winemaker to our platform and then created QR codes, tasting cards, and a sticker for each wine so that tasters could scan and learn more, and then take the information with them to revisit it again later (once it's on your mobile device, it's accessible anywhere!). We saw more than 800 scans during the event period and visitors were super excited at the use of technology to help them learn more about each wine, winery, and winemaker.
