Senior Software Developer

BT

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; BT.A; Telecommunications industry

April 2009 – August 2012 (3 years 5 months) London, United Kingdom

Working for Osmosoft, the Open source innovation arm of BT, I was a software developer specialising in rich client side web applications written in JavaScript.

I worked primarily on TiddlySpace (http://tiddlyspace.com), an extensible note taking application, based on TiddlyWiki and designed to allow rapid prototyping and development of new web applications (among other things). You can find the source code on GitHub (https://github.com/tiddlyspace/tiddlyspace).
In the past I have worked on a number of other projects, the first of which was a website for the International Lesbian, Gay Trans and Intersex Association (ILGA) (http://ilga.org). It was successfully launched a couple of years ago and makes use of a number of innovative new technologies. It was designed to make use of the TiddlyWeb platform and provides up to date information about laws throughout the world, viewable through an interactive map. It currently proves to be popular within the LGBT community, and helped BT to win the Most Gay Friendly Company award when the trial was launched in 2009.
In addition to this, I have also worked on creating reporting software, most notably for the BTID Research department, to enable them to better surface the work that each team member accomplishes. This has led to a dramatic increase in productivity, with the time taken to report dropping from hours each week to minutes.
