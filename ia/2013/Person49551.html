Gordy Pratt's Summary

Expect hysterically funny comedy and mesmerizing Spanish guitar playing in Gordy’s stand-up musical comedy show. Gordy’s family friendly baby boomer humor is guaranteed to make you laugh, make you cry … make you laugh so hard you cry.

A veteran entertainer, extraordinary guitarist, comedy writer and published songwriter, Gordy has performed his stand-up musical comedy show for hundreds of corporations, associations and other organizations, large and small. Gordy also does a full-length concert, performed throughout the United States. He has appeared on ABC’s Good Morning America and opened for Kenny Chesney, the Nitty Gritty Dirt Band, The Beach Boys and many more.

Called the “Victor Borge of the Guitar,” Gordy’s unique performance style brings laughter to music and music to laughter. Gordy mesmerizes audiences as he plays Bach and Bluegrass in the same breath, Mozart on “12 cups of good, strong cowboy coffee,” and a foot-stomping turn of “Classical Gas.”

In Gordy’s character-driven comedy, you’ll meet Orville T. Saddlesore, “a chewed up old cowboy” who offers up Words of Western Wisdom; Joe California, a cool dude struggling to survive his first winter; Joe Prosciutto, a New Yorker suffering culture shock; and, of course, Gordy, a character in his own right.

A theatre and music professional for more than 30 years, Gordy grew up in summer stock theater and studied classical guitar and ancient music at Ithaca College, Ithaca, NY; Royal College of Music, London; and the San Francisco Conservatory of Music. The founder of Deadwood Production Company, Gordy wrote 12 history alive shows, including “SETH BULLOCK: The Spirit of the West,” which he continues to perform for a variety of organizations.

Gordy has produced several CDs including his most recent, “rusty old american dream,” and his classical solo guitar album “String Theory.” Gordy’s CDS are available for sale on CDBaby, iTunes, Amazon.com and on Gordy’s website, www.gordypratt.com.

Specialties

Stand-up musical comedy; classical guitar; singing/songwriting

Gordy Pratt's Experience

Owner

ONE GUY

March 1994 – Present (19 years 10 months) Spearfish, SD

An accomplished writer and seasoned performer, Gordy has appeared on ABC's Good Morning America and numerous Public Television and radio shows. He has opened for Kenny Chesney, Michael Martin Murphy, Williams & Ree, the Beach Boys, the Nitty Gritty Dirt Band and others. Gordy performs for corporations, associations and non-profit groups. He performs at holiday parties and other special events.

As a child, Gordy's family moved around a lot. He learned early on that the quickest way to make friends was to make people laugh. With you or at you. A foot taller that anybody else, he became a kind of a conversation piece. "My second grade classroom was a sea of little desks with this huge desk in the back ... mine. I was very lucky at the age of 12 to spend summers at a theatre company in the Black Hills of South Dakota. I was hooked -- on the entertainment business and the Hills.

"I got got hooked on the classical guitar at 19 after a friend said, 'Listen to this guitar music.' I said, 'Hey, those guys are great.' And he said, 'That's one guy.' That was the beginning of "The ONE GUY.'" Gordy studied guitar at Ithaca College, Ithaca, New York; the Royal College of Music in London; and the San Francisco Conservatory of Music.Gordy spent five years in New York City where he toured with Pirates of Penzance, starring Jim Belushi and Peter Noon, and "Ain't Misbehavin'" (in Europe), doing everything from sound to selling merchandise to stage managing.

"I came back to the Black Hills to raise a couple of great kids and ended up starting a little production company in Deadwood, in the Black Hills. One summer we did 42 shows a week, which included barking people into the shows.

"I started working solo in 1994 and the "ONE GUY" was born: Gordy Pratt, The original Fabulous ONE GUY ("You know, that one guy!?!"). It's a great job. It's a great feeling to make people laugh. I'm very lucky to be able to make a great living doing something I enjoy ... DRIVING!"
