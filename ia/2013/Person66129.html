  • Music Venue Analysis: Using Real Feedback to Develop the Ideal Venue

    • August 2009 to May 2011
    Team Members: John Bradley Jackson, Mike Dittman

    ABSTRACT

    Music, movies, and pictures are more inexpensive and easily accessible than ever before as a result of the digital revolution, digitized media sources, and the trend of file sharing. This has led to an increase in the importance of live performances from bands, both famous and unknown; while the price for gaining access to music decreases, the necessity to make income off of performing live increases. As live performances become more essential to surviving in the music industry, it would seem equally imperative that the venues providing locations for those live performances understand how to best attract and please performers and audience members alike. In order to recognize the wants and needs of these three groups, Michael Dittman has conducted first hand research through band and audience member surveys and interviews with the management and promoters of music venues. His research is limited to music venues and local bands located within Orange County, CA. From the information gathered from these surveys and interviews, Michael describes the “ideal” music venue for that area and discusses the difficulties he encountered throughout the course of his research.
