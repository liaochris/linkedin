Clint Alexander's Summary

To summarize my experiences is a challenge in and of itself. I am a self-educated Internet Technologist. I've acquired certifications through MySQL (CMDBA/CMCDBA) and a Brainbench Certified Internet Professional (BCIP) in Web Design, Development, and Administration; I have learned through personal and professional experiences in development and production networks.

Currently operating within LAMP environments, strengths and focus are in custom scale-out techniques to manage high usage load averages and maintain high-availability.

I am an Internet developer using PHP, Perl, Flash, Flex, Director/Shockwave, Etc. I do not use .NET or ASP program environments. I had made most use of Open Source frameworks/applications as they were readily available as well as fully supported.

I play the role of Sys Admin and Network Architect, Database Administrator and all-around Engineer. As stated, I create high-load networks with scalable and redundant solutions -- modular; cost-effective; extremely flexible, Zero Points of Failure.

Database technologies are among the favorites. I have designed a few data warehouse solutions. I am a community volunteer developer in MySQL focused on Cluster, Replication and MySQL-Proxy. You Oracle guys out there should give a bit of respect to the performance metrics by comparison to MySQL! <wink>

I am also a multimedia developer. I use tools such as Adobe Creative Suite and 3D modeling software such as Alias 3D Studio Max and Maya. I enjoy creating interactive Shockwave games and working with virtual world applications. I don't create models so much, just enough to create what I need.

As you can see, I am a 'jack-of-all' in the field of Information Technology. I know this field well. I am confident, success-driven, team oriented, and a real problem-solver using out-of-the-box solutions. My professional goals are open ended and I am still hunting for my final "resting place" to live out the rest of my professional career.
