Emma Özsen's Summary

For the past 6 years, I have worked on a variety of buildings and masterplans in Europe and Mauritius, with a focus on passive design for energy, thermal comfort and daylight, and Low and Zero Carbon technologies. I also worked extensively on preparing documentation for planning applications in the UK, including undertaking Energy Performance assessments and Sustainability Assessments and Labelling. I have been providing services independently in the field of sustainable building design and analysis in Mauritius since January 2010. I have been involved in a number of different activities and projects, some of which include carrying out solar/daylighting, thermal and natural ventilation analysis, developing site-wide sustainability strategies for residential & commercial projects. I was also part of the team of consultants working on the development of the new Building Control Bill, regulations and Energy Codes for Mauritius in the period 2010-2011.

Specialties: Bioclimatic design / thermal modelling, site-wide environmental strategies, energy / water consumption analysis, RET feasibility studies, BREEAM / LEED / GREEN STAR (SA), Code for Sustainable Homes (UK) assessments.

Emma Özsen's Experience

Sustainability Consultant & Building Design Analyst

trading as Build-Green Consulting

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Renewables & Environment industry

2009 – Present (4 years) Mauritius

My work consists of the application of passive design strategies to buildings, in order to minimise or eliminate their dependency on mechanical systems. Where building rely heavily on mechanical systems, I am able to carry out energy performance analysis and provide advice on how to reduce the building's energy consumption. I also produce outline environmental design reports, advising on how to maximise the use of natural elements, minimise the use of fossil-fuel energy, optimise solar control, building orientation, natural ventilation, reduction of mains water use, reduction of waste, etc. The key objectives are to address ecological concerns and achieve economic viability in any building project.

I am also a LEED Accredited Professional, a licensed BREEAM Bespoke International Assessor and Green Star (South Africa) Accredited Professional.

I was also part of the team of consultants involved in producing the new Building Control Bill, Energy Efficiency Building Codes, and Passive Design Guidelines for Mauritius. This has being carried out under terms of reference set by the UNDP Mauritius, on behalf of the Ministry of Renewable Energy and Public Utilities and the Ministry of Public Infrastructure, in accordance with the Government's initiative to promote more energy-efficient buildings.
