Kristin DeRamus' Summary

My name is Kristin DeRamus and I am a product design-focused professional. I started my digital career working as a web designer for the United States Army under Department of Defense contractor L-3 Communications. My time with the Army’s website taught me the value of using digital products to communicate transparency in our government.

I have since moved on to the private sector at USA TODAY where I lead the interactive design team for Gannett Digital. It’s a challenging time in news media consumption, but my job allows me the opportunity to tackle some of those challenges through designing beautiful, well-thought out solutions.

Kristin DeRamus' Projects

  • Ghost Factories: Smelting and Lead Contamination

    • January 2011 to April 2012
    Team Members: Kristin DeRamus, Alison Young, Anthony DeBarros, Stan Wilson Jr, John Hillkirk, Denny Gainer, Chuck Rose, Corey Greeneltch, Shannon Rae Green, Erin Sansone

    This 14-month newsroom investigation revealed government regulators did little to protect the public from lead "smelter" factories that operated in the U.S. from the 1930s to 1960s. The interactive component of the series breaks down the complexity of the issues in a user-friendly way on both desktop and iPad. The response of the findings prompted at least 14 states and Congress to take action (as of May 1st, 2012). In addition, the Centers for Disease Control revised its lead poisoning guidelines in children (as of May 16, 2012). Winner of an Associated Press Media Editors association award in the "Digital Storytelling & Reporting" category (July 2012). EPPY Award finalist in both Best Investigative/Enterprise Feature and Best Enterprise/Investigative Video categories (October 2012). EPA opens investigation to reexamine all 460 former lead factory sites across the U.S. for health hazards (November 2012). Winner of an Alfred I. duPont-Columbia University Award for excellence in broadcast and digital journalism for "outstanding investigative multimedia report" (January 2013). Society of News Design (SND) Best of Digital Design silver medal for use of multimedia (February 2013). Society of American Business Editors and Writers (SABEW) Best in Business Innovation winner (February 2013). Sidney Hillman Foundation Prize for Web Journalism (May 2013).

  • The Princeton Review 2012 Best Value Colleges

    • January 2012 to February 2012
    Team Members: Kristin DeRamus, Stan Wilson Jr, Juan Thomassie, Brian Price, Destin Frasier, Chuck Rose, Robert Berthold, , Emily Brown

    USA TODAY partnered with The Princeton Review to compile a data-rich interactive that highlights the 150 best value colleges across U.S. Using data provided by The Princeton Review, we created an easy-to-understand interactive while embracing modern technologies such as jQuery DataTables and Modernizr on both desktop and iPad.

  • Martin Luther King Jr. Witnessing the Dream

    • August 2011 to October 2011
    Team Members: Kristin DeRamus, Jerry Mosemak, Shannon Rae Green, Melanie Eversley, Eileen Blass

    The Martin Luther King Jr. “Witnessing the Dream” interactive package, with stories by Melanie Eversley, video by Eileen Blass and video editing by Shannon Green and art direction by Jerry Mosemak was named a finalist in the Salute to Excellence awards of the National Association of Black Journalists.

  • USA TODAY/Twitter Election Meter

    • July 2012 to July 2012
    Team Members: Kristin DeRamus, Pim Linders

    USA TODAY teamed up with Twitter to launch the 2012 Political Election Meter, which measures real-time sentiment about President Obama and Mitt Romney. Twitter & Topsy provided the data and we designed and developed the project in-house. Featured on Elearning Examples: http://elearningexamples.com/usa-todaytwitter-election-meter/

  • Where the Jobs are

    • September 2012 to September 2012
    Team Members: Kristin DeRamus, Juan Thomassie, Barbie Hansen, Anthony DeBarros, Ray Goldbacher, Doug Carroll

    Where the jobs are presents an overall picture of how the U.S. job markets are doing in the top 200 metro areas. The interactive allows users to toggle between various job sectors and manipulate data points on the U.S. map with a timeline slider that represents change over time.

  • Tropical Storm Tracker

    • October 2013 to Present
    Team Members: Kristin DeRamus, Maureen Linke, Andrea Fuller, Juan Thomassie, Anthony DeBarros

    Live-tracking Atlantic and Pacific tropical storms.
