Michael Locklear's Summary

I'm a recent Ohio University grad with a good deal of hands-on experience reporting, anchoring and producing. For three years or so, I shot, scripted and edited a package once a week for the live 5:30 p.m. newscast at WOUB. I also stacked, wrote and boothed Newswatch each week, and during last fall's election season, I reported from the Ohio Statehouse when I wasn't chasing presidential candidates around the Buckeye State. In my bureau chief-like capacity, I turned multiple packages and VOSOTs each week for WOUB and for "The State of Ohio," a public affairs program that airs statewide on PBS affiliates.

As for my news philosophy, it's no secret story-telling is the driving force behind any station. I'm all about chasing the stories and getting the information people care about... but just as important is finding great characters in the community to help tell those stories best. I've interviewed tornado survivors, exotic animal owners and locals who've won national contests in the last few years, and I'm ready to meet the cast in a new area.

I should also mention that I prize accuracy above all else. Being first means little if you're wrong.

You can get to know those southeast Ohioans I mentioned in my TV Reporting Portfolio at http://michaellocklear.com/. You'll also find my reporter montage and a live newscast I produced below.

As far as skills are concerned, I'm very comfortable with ENPS, Adobe Premiere Pro and Final Cut Pro. I have some experience working with iNews, and I can no doubt learn your newsroom systems. I'm ready to pack and hit the road, so give me a ring.
