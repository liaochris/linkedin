Barbara Bowen, PhD's Summary

My deep expertise, gained in 25+ years of professional experience, in facilitating knowledge creation,  sharing and transfer, knowledge-based collaboration and meaningful learning enables my work to be highly effective, cost effective and efficient.

The purpose of the work I do through SolutionsMapping is to facilitate the design & implementation of transformational solutions that address critical challenges we face at this time.

Virtually all such solutions require the seamless integration of multi-disciplinary knowledge. They also require those involved to access & share their most profound knowledge, know-how and insights, including tacit & emergent knowledge. These are capacities I have developed in the course of my professional career & it is my privilege to apply them inservice of our common good and the good of the environment.

The department created and managed collaborative research partnerships between Apple's engineers and researchers and leading university researchers for a broad range of technologies and research areas.

External Research partnerships generated new technologies for Apple's portfolio, including: human interface, manufacturing, object oriented databases, operating systems and advanced learning technologies.

The partnership strategy was researcher-to-researchers collaborations, an approach that was very popular with both University researchers and Apple employees.

External Research's policy of providing unrestricted fellowship support for graduate students led to highly productive internships and major reductions in the cost of recruiting researchers and engineers.

Barbara Bowen, PhD's Projects

  • Web 2.0 and Lawyers

    • February 2005 to June 2005
    Team Members: Barbara Bowen, PhD, Patrick Hindert, Denham Grey

    Web 2.0 and Lawyers is a hypermedia visual knowledge model. It was created via tacit knowledge elicitation with Patrick Hindert, an attorney, and his colleague, Denham Grey, an expert in social media. Patrick used the knowledge model in executive presentations to industry & professional organizations to explain the new challenges and opportunities web 2.0 creates for lawyers.

    In spite of the changes changes in Web 2.0 and social media since 2005, the conceptual structure of the root map, and the sub-concept maps, is still valid.

Barbara Bowen, PhD's Publications

  • "Concept Maps: Tools to Enhance Executive and Team Effectiveness" - chapter in Applied Concept Mapping: Capturing, Analyzing & Organizing Knowledge

    • Routledge/Taylor & Francis Grp.
    • February 11, 2011
    Authors: Barbara Bowen, PhD, Book authors: Moon, B, Hoffman, R., Novak J., & Canas, A.

    This chapter describes case studies of the use of concept maps to reduce ramp-up time for new employees in a small business, to facilitate shared vision and enhance team effectiveness in a public health organization, for executive communication using Web 2.0 tools and as a capacity building infrastructure for a geographically distributed team. Drucker's definition of effectiveness as a "contribution to the organization as a whole" is a thread through all the cases.
