Jason Chesebrough's Summary

Jason Chesebrough is an artist, designer and creative that embraces all forms of media for the express purpose of making things that people can experience.

A digital wise and seasoned art director drawing on a multi-disciplinary perspective, his work often embraces a transmedia platform approach to enhance a users engagement with a brand, and make their experience more intuitive and rewarding.

Named as one of 'Canada's most influential innovators in the diverse field of media innovation' through his global collaborative initiative "the Fusion Project", he has recently revisited this project undertaking its next iteration, through a group sourced art experiment conducted as anAmalgam.

Creative Lead, Owner

Custom Murals and Design

November 1996 – December 2000 (4 years 2 months)

Way back in the day, I needed money to live. My talent was as a painter, but I only sold occasional gallery work, so I began to knock on doors and generated an income painting Christmas and seasonal temporary window displays.

As time went on, this turned into more permanent work in restaurants and nightclubs, allowing for work with clients such as Molson, Labatt, Bacardi, Keith's& Carlsberg - thru to Canadian Club, Jack Daniels and Vex.

Those who know Oshawa know the famous Teddy's Restaurant & Deli, and every inch of that place has been touched by me, from the portraiture work in the lobby through to the 20 food teddy bear platter signage outside.

Work expanded to restaurant mural-space design for clients such as Bobby C's Dockside, Tollbooth Willys and the Fieldhouse. While the fundamentals of the seasonal work was never lost with a steady flow of art produced for Mickey Finn's, IGA, IDA, La Cantina, Trader Sam's, Joe's Pizza, Color Your World and Marlin Travel Thomas Cook.

Most notable however, is the work I produced for Barklay's The Outdoors Specialist on the Oshawa/Whitby border. The largest work I've ever done single-handed, transforming a 7,000 square foot white siding building into a log cabin, it took over 6 months and brought me into the world of digital (there had to be an easier way to be creative and be successful than baking and freezing alone on a scaffold)

I continue to paint, though personal, to this day.
