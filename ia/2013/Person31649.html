David Cox's Projects

  • Indian River Lagoon Greenway

    • January 2013 to Present
    Team Members: David Cox, Indian River Land Trust, Indian River County, St. Johns River Water Management District

    DCC provided environmental planning, design, permitting and project management services for a greenway trail system along the Indian River Lagoon. The greenway is located in Vero Beach, Florida from south of the 17th Street Bridge to behind McKee Botanical Garden. This first phase of the project is focused on creating a 2-3 mile Greenway along a series of private and public lands for walking and bicycling through maritime hammock and viewing the lagoon from a mangrove forest. Boardwalks and an observation pier will be added in the near future.

  • North American Wetlands Conservation Act Grant

    • April 2012 to Present
    Team Members: David Cox, Indian River Land Trust, US Fish & Wildlife Service

    DCC created this successful grant proposal on behalf of the Indian River Land Trust (IRLT). The US Fish and Wildlife Service awarded $1 million to the IRLT to assist with acquisition and permanent protection of Bee Gum Point, a 111-acre estuarine wetland located along the Atlantic Flyway in the Indian River Lagoon. This was the largest remaining parcel of unprotected wetland on the barrier island

  • Lagoon Waterfront Initiative

    • January 2009 to Present
    Team Members: David Cox, Indian River Land Trust, CivaTerra, US Fish and Wildlife Service

    David Cox Consulting has helped the Indian River Land Trust identify ecologically significant lands to preserve and protect. GIS-based maps, environmental site assessments, grant writing, and management plans are among the services DCC has provided. Since 2009 the IRLT has now acquired more than 625 acres and over 6 miles of frontage on the Indian River Lagoon. This project is continuing and will bring additional estuarine wetlands under protection by the IR Land Trust.
