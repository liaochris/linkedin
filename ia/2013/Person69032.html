Josh Ochs' Summary

With a background in brand marketing at Disney and a love for all things technology, Josh Ochs advises some of the world’s best brands on how they can use social media to find new customers, nurture existing relationships and tie each campaign back to the bottom line. As the grassroots founder of MediaLeaders.com, the direct response team that manages Facebook, Twitter & LinkedIn for over three dozen brands, Josh is leading the industry with customer centric campaigns that blend traditional sales methods with new technology.

Josh outlines many of his counter-intuitive marketing techniques in his bestselling book: “Light, Bright and Polite” In his book, Josh shows brands how to keep their social media messaging safe and effective.

Josh has worked with some of the best brands in the business, including advising Disney ABC on marketing, managing social media campaigns for Neutrogena skiniD, Palms Casino, and developing a nationwide social media formula for publicly traded Emmis Communications (Parent company to a dozen leading radio properties). Josh prides himself in helping brands focus less on technology and more on messaging that resonates with a customer. There’s no talk of “buzz” or “strategy” with Josh, instead he focuses on building tactical plans and making each campaign contribute to the bottom line. Josh frequently speaks at marketing conferences nationwide and shares his unique grassroots approach with businesses of all sizes.

In 2009, Josh was nearly elected to city council in Hermosa Beach, CA, gaining 70% of the votes required to win a seat in the local election. As the youngest person on the ballot with limited resources, Josh assembled a grassroots team to help him walk to over 3,000 homes to meet voters on their doorsteps and followup using social media.

Josh has been quoted and featured as a source in Forbes, CBS News, KTLA, KFWB Radio and is a guest lecturer at USC and UCLA. He lives and works in Los Angeles where he was born and raised.

Former Candidate for City Council November 2009

Former Candidate for Hermosa Beach City Council 2009

May 2009 – November 2009 (7 months) Hermosa Beach, CA

Unfortunately, I was shy about 350 votes to win a spot on city council. This is a huge victory for our younger generation (and maybe the start of another race).
----
In May of 2009, I made a decision to run for Hermosa Beach City Council. After living there for several years, I knew we needed to make changes to help businesses & residents in these tough times. My decision to run was not an easy one. At the start, I was relatively unknown by community leaders. I was up against eight year incumbents and candidates with experience in three past races. Essentially: all of them were well known.

I set out with three campaign goals:
1) To be honest and positive
2) To listen to the needs of residents and businesses
3) To work harder than any other candidate

On November 3rd, 2009, we proved that you can make a positive impact in a community following these three rules.

HERE’S HOW MY CAMPAIGN MADE A DIFFERENCE:
- Every afternoon and evening I walked door-to-door in Hermosa and spoke with 50+ voters per day; getting to know their concerns and giving them a copy of my goals for the city. Voters thanked me every day for taking the time to shake their hand on their doorstep.
- I met nearly all of the 3,000 key voters in the city on their doorstep (If they weren't home, I left a brochure that says “Sorry I missed you, visit my website.”)
- My campaign team followed up with phone calls to almost every voter reminding them that “We are voting for Josh Ochs and here’s why you should too…”
- My campaign signs are the OPPOSITE color scheme of our competitors (Most people agreed, ours are way more visible and memorable)
- Our campaign website had 70+ endorsements from local community members. No other candidate in this race has such an extensive site that involves the community.
- We made social media easy to use for residents and included it on our campaign website and Facebook page.

This campaign became the foundation for my bestselling marketing book "Light, Bright and Polite"

Manager of Marketing

The Marcus Buckingham Company

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; E-Learning industry

January 2005 – December 2005 (1 year) Beverly Hills, Ca

Marcus Buckingham is a British-American New York Times bestselling author, researcher, motivational speaker and business consultant best known for promoting what he calls "Strengths."

I first met Marcus in October of 2002 when I won the opportunity to assist him at a business leadership conference at the Staples Center in Los Angeles. I told Mr. Buckingham "While you're prepping for your speech, perhaps I should get your number so I can alert you if your speaking time changes." He reluctantly recited his 10 digit cell number and I left him to his practicing. Two weeks after the speaking engagement I left him a voice mail..."Hello, Mr Buckingham, I'd love to buy you lunch and tell you more about some of the ideas that your competitors are writing in their books. I've read all their stuff and I have tips for your next book to set it apart." I never got a returned call, but I was persistent. I kept leaving voice messages for Mr. Buckingham, every two weeks. Three months would go by, six, then 11. "Mr. Buckingham, I'm not looking for a job, in fact, I'm working full time at Disney Studios and things are great. However, I'd love to buy you breakfast someday near your office. I have ideas for your next book. In one of your books you said the world's best managers are persistent. It's been almost a year since we first met. You know my name, you know my number. I'd love to buy you lunch."

That same afternoon (almost a year to the date that we originally met) Mr. Buckingham called me back with news that he left the Gallup Organization. He was looking for a new team member...and there was my voicemail. We met for lunch and began to discuss some marketing projects that he needed help with.

While working for Mr. Buckingham, I traveled with him and watched him speak to crowds of almost 15,000 people. His speaking fees were in excess of $40,000. I helped him to develop his marketing materials and assist with some of his newest books that he was writing.
