      Henry Booth House
      • Education
      April 2012 – present (1 year 6 months)

      Henry Booth House is a greater Chicago community based Not for Profit established in 1898. Their mission is to improve the quality of life for culturally economically diverse families by providing comprehensive early childhood education, health and social services families across the Chicagoland area. Their continuum of services includes:
      • Critical educational support from the “Cradle to the Classroom “ through their system of 41 Head Start programs children ages 3 to 5
      • Family Health Services to expectant mothers and their babies through the Healthy Moms program and further supporting their young babies through WIC and Family Health Service including a site based family clinic run by Mercy Hospital
      • GED Classes to those individuals seeking to better themselves, better their child’s lives resulting in a brighter future for all of us
      The organization is primarily funded through grants from the City of Chicago and State of Illinois. Despite recent budget cutbacks, they have found a way to thrive in this struggling economy servicing over 2000 children and families across the city. I joined their Board of Directors a little over a year ago and have found the work of trying to re-establish this 111 year old community based Not for Profit as a premier social services agency as one of my most challenging yet fulfilling tasks.
