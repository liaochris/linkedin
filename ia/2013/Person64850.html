Joanes Rosó's Summary

I was born in Elgoibar (Gipuzkoa) in 1987. Life in the village has shaped me as a citizen that cares evolve and glocality.

I started working very young, from bottling cider until lumberjack but I have always believed that the most powerful weapon to change the world is education and I started as a teacher of Euskara in “Bilbo zaharra Euskaltegia“ of the old part of Bilbao while studying Tourism in Deusto University. Before tourism, I study kitchen because I did not know where to direct the life, and as I knew I wanted retire in a cozy farmhouse, thought to spend two years of my life preparing and reflecting… Chef, teacher, educator, bottler, photographer… I think this defines my multidisciplinarity. I have worked and led in many projects in various associations, companies and NGOs. For this, one of my main desires is to create a more common concept of these entities that they are still groups of people working for a common goal.

The aim of our company is to create a constant learning company. We think that the co- creation with the customer using the “learning by doing” philosophy is the best way to give  an answer. According to this, we believe that our company has to be in the intersection of  two circles, the circle of education and the circle of business we provide  consulting services, but we want to transform this concept. We promote projects, our commitment goes further, we work implementing them, from a innovative standpoint, so we want to work in INNpulsory,  implementing Innovation consultancy.

  • High-tech Sealing Solutions

    • febrero de 2013 a Actualidad

    For hydraulic, pneumatic, automotive... In the last 30 years, we have always been available with a unique sealing solution for those engineers who are struggling to find the answer to an equation, which involves so many parameters like space, pressure, temperature, noise, EMC, type of fluid... plus cost.

    The introduction of EgaprintTM into the automotive and industrial sector is one of those best answers. And surprisingly you will find that the solution is so simple as they are backed up by great ideas and comprehensive engineering work.

    The principle production process is silk-screen printing of a polymeric compound in the shape of a gasket beading over a base-support.
