Robert Pickard's Summary

Winner of "PR Agency Head of the Year" at the Asia-Pacific PR Awards in 2012, for nearly three years served as leader of a 38 office organization across 19 markets with more than 1,000 people in the Burson-Marsteller regional network. An Asia-based global corporate communications executive with two decades of experience focused on providing counsel for senior multinational decision-makers and creating campaigns for their organizations. Co-founder and leader of successful award-winning public relations consultancies in Canada, the United States, Korea, Japan, and Singapore. Social media community builder and digital storyteller.

Specialties: Corporate, crisis, and change communications; public relations counsel for the c-suite; stakeholder relationship management; social media marketing; corporate social responsibility consulting; executive communications coaching and media training; sustainability strategy; public affairs and government relations. Developing and sustaining a worldwide network of relationships with key opinion leaders and media. Image development and reputation management. Digital storytelling. Publicity.

Robert Pickard's Experience

President & CEO, Asia-Pacific

Burson-Marsteller

Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; WPPGY; Public Relations and Communications industry

January 2010 – January 2013 (3 years 1 month) Singapore

In his first year as chief executive, Pickard led the firm to record financial performance with the highest revenue and profits in Burson-Marsteller Asia-Pacific's history. He since continued the firm's development and expanded B-M's geographic footprint, with new offices in Shenzhen, China, Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia and a joint venture in Vietnam. Known for his recruitment prowess was the catalyst for the creation of the firm's Asia-based global energy practice, its first credible digital capability, as well as its proactive program to meet the PR needs of the next generation of Asian multinationals. Regularly quoted in media including on television and in the world business press, under Pickard B-M in Asia generated considerable intellectual capital and won major industry awards - including his "PR Agency Head of the Year" trophy in 2012 - which achieved a degree of visibility that the resurgent firm hadn't enjoyed for years.

President, North Asia; Representative Director, Japan

Edelman

Privately Held; 1001-5000 employees; Public Relations and Communications industry

August 2004 – June 2008 (3 years 11 months)

Grew Edelman’s North Asia revenues ten times larger in a very hands-on way during six fiscal years, representing a plurality of the entire Asia-Pacific region’s non-acquired growth during that time. In 2005 - with continued responsibility for Edelman Korea - founded Edelman Japan, the global firm's first successful subsidiary in the world's second-largest economy. The Tokyo operation grew from nothing to 30 people within two years (in the operating black after year one). In a 50 office international network, Tokyo and Seoul were two of the agency's top 10 offices for quality as scored by clients (2007). Personally provided senior client counsel to leaders at AstraZeneca, Boston Scientific, Goldman Sachs, Merck, Microsoft, Nissan, Pfizer, sanofi-aventis, Standard & Poor’s.

Managing Director, Korea

Edelman

Privately Held; 1001-5000 employees; Public Relations and Communications industry

July 2002 – August 2004 (2 years 2 months)

Led the Korean operation to record revenues (+309%) and profits (38%), taking it from being one of the smallest offices in Edelman's Asia-Pacific region to the largest within two years. During his term, the Korean operation was twice named Edelman’s ‘Office of the Year’ and won ‘Consultancy of the Year’ at the Asia-Pacific PR Awards (2004). Personally led and managed the Kia Motors Corporation global account from Seoul. Appeared across a full spectrum of national media outlets, covered and quoted on television and radio, newspapers and magazines. Recruited, trained and mentored a successor who continued the organization's winning ways.

Executive Vice President & Founding Partner

Environics Communications

Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Public Relations and Communications industry

August 1994 – May 2000 (5 years 10 months)

Co-founder of one of Canada's premier communications consultancies (1994 at Toronto). Led the establishment of the first Canadian-owned PR agency in the United States market as founder and general manager of the firm's New York-area office (1996 at Stamford, CT). Focused on banking, energy and advanced technology clients.

Vice President

Hill & Knowlton Canada

Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; WPPGY; Public Relations and Communications industry

September 1990 – August 1994 (4 years)

Based in Toronto, founded the agency's technology practice in Canada and led the firm's largest account team (Microsoft). Specialized in international communications, including client projects in Brazil (at the United Nations Earth Summit) and South Korea (global PR via satellite at Seoul for a major energy sector announcement).
