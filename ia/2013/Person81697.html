Lisa Schwartz's Publications

  • Guidebook To Local Search Marketing

    • Book
    • January 15, 2013
    Authors: Lisa Schwartz

    The bottom line is that we need to give local consumers what they want and
    not what we want them to have. That’s probably one of the most neglected
    principals of internet marketing or any marketing in general. We really have
    to think about what we’re giving people and why we’re going after the terms
    that we target.
    It is also important to understand local marketing because many searches
    made online are “localized” by the search engines and affect the rankings of a
    website. The search engines know what geography a search is made within
    when a potential Customer or Prospect types in their request into the search
    bar of their favorite search engine, say Google search. This gives your
    Business the opportunity to target your audience based upon where you do
    business. We will need to “get into the head of the local consumer.” What
    exactly is it that the local consumer is looking for and how are they searching?
    Are they ready to buy or are they going to be doing research? These are the
    things that you need to think about because it will make a big difference in
    the end result for your profit and return on marketing investment.
