Joao Frigerio's Summary

A Sports Business Professional with over 7 years of experience in the industry. Joao is a creative, communicative and versatile individual, capable of working well both in a team as individually. He has MBA education, experience in a variety of sectors and is capable of working in 5 languages.

Currently João Frigerio consults with a focus on helping foreign companies that wish to do business in the Brazilian sports marketplace. He acts as a ‘business facilitator’, assisting them in various areas. In the same way, he assists Brazilian companies that wish to expand in Europe. He is a Vice President of the FIFA Master Alumni Association and a consultant for FGV Online's FGV/FIFA/CIES Course in Management, Marketing and Law of Sports.

Brazilian/ Italian national, he graduated as a journalist in 1999. He has a Master degree in Management, Law and Humanities of Sports and a MBA in Investor Relations, and he has been active in the Sports Industry in Europe since 2004.

He is co-author of ''Mobile Sports Rights in Europe 2006'' and ''Sports on Broadband 2007: Video Rights, the Internet and IPTV''. Before starting his consulting role, he was General Manager of Sportcal Europe for four years. Previously he had been the senior editor for Fifaworldcup.com in Portuguese before and during the 2006 FIFA World Cup.

Joao Frigerio's Publications

  • Sport on Broadband 2007: Video Rights, the Internet and IPTV

    • Sportcal Global Communications
    • July 1, 2007
    Authors: Joao Frigerio, Martin Ross, Callum Murray

    Sports websites worldwide could be attracting $2 billion a year in advertising revenues by 2009, if current trends for the growth of the online advertising sector and the growth of sports coverage online continue.

    The prediction is contained in Sport on Broadband 2007: Video Rights, the Internet and IPTV, a comprehensive and in-depth new 328-page management report available now from Sportcal.com, the business site for sport.

    The figures represent a huge potential market for sports federations and other rights-owners seeking to find new ways of commercialising their sports properties and content.

    About the Report

    How do you share in the expanding broadband sports market?
    How are rights holders delivering their sports online?
    Who are the key players in the market?
    What are the commercial pricing models?
    What are broadband rights worth?
    What is IPTV and how can it help you?
    All these questions and more are answered in Sportcal.com’s latest 328-page report, Sport on Broadband 2007: Video Rights, the Internet and IPTV.

    The report offers a comprehensive reference guide to this expanding market, using interviews with key industry players, case studies, graphs, charts and tables providing the trends, issues and business opportunities involved, whether you are a rights holder, an events organiser, an internet service provider or a company servicing the new media market.
