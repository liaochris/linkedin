Maksim Sorokin's Projects

  • HELMUT Film Scanner

    • April 2013 to Present
    Team Members: Maksim Sorokin, Kostas Rutkauskas

    Helmut is a photographic film scanner on your Android phone.

    It can be used to digitize your (old) negatives, share your (lomo) shots easily without format or film type restrictions, use it as a fast contact sheet tool. No additional hardware is needed.

  • Girls in IT

    • March 2013 to Present
    Team Members: Maksim Sorokin, Kostas Rutkauskas, Olga Marcenko,

    Girls in IT is a social initiative to empower young women to pursue a career in tech and engineering.

    The initiative uncovers everyday female IT professionals and their success stories. We believe that such an action tackles deprecated stereotypes and contributes to the commonwealth of the sector.

  • Cultural Heritage app KULTURARV

    • November 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Maksim Sorokin, Kostas Rutkauskas

    Kulturarv is an open source augmented reality app on Android. It guides you through Denmark's protected and preservation-worthy buildings. The app was developed in a cooperation between CodeUnited and Dansk Kulturstyrelsen (Danish Agency for Culture).

  • Exif4Film

    • July 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Maksim Sorokin, Kostas Rutkauskas

    Exif4Film is a tool for film photographers.

    It comes as a pair of programs: an Android app helps shooters store specific details as soon as photos are captured, and a desktop utility that takes the Android app data and automatically adds it to the film scans.

  • Roskilde 2013

    • May 2013 to June 2013
    Team Members: Maksim Sorokin, Kostas Rutkauskas, Marius Žostautas, Indre Rusyte

    Roskilde 2013 is the first ever augmented reality app created for the legendary festival.

    Find all the bars, food/coffee shops, showers, wi-fi areas, and many other placemarks through an augmented view. Every stage is represented with a detailed schedule, Twitter-powered chat, and even a set of pictures taken by artists behind the scenes!
