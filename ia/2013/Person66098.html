John Biesnecker's Summary

I'm a product developer, and I know how to hustle.

I live to meet, greet, and devour problems.

I've taken a non-traditional path. Over the last six years I've been involved in small startups where I've been ask to play a variety of roles, and thus have gotten my feet wet in more than one field. It's always come back to software, though, and writing code that solves problems and produces value is what I do.

I'm pretty damn good at it, too.

These days I mostly speak Ruby and Objective-C, though I once was pretty fluent in PHP, and I've taken a liking to Clojure, Haskell, and Scala over the last several years. I also speak Mandarin Chinese fluently, and can hold my own in Cantonese, too.

Because of the career path upon which I've embarked, I have experience with marketing and communications, project management, and product management and design, too. I've run my own consulting firm, I've worked for customers from both sides of the Pacific, and I've managed agile processes in multiple languages simultaneously.

If you need someone who is as comfortable with business as he is with technology, who can talk about marketing ROI and then write a system to track that ROI himself, and is as comfortable in East Asia as he is in East Texas, let's talk.
