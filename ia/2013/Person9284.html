Aprill Allen's Summary

I believe great knowledge management involves collaboration of colleagues and community. I’ve worked in IT and network support for many years. Over that time I came to appreciate how useful a well-maintained knowledge base is, but it’s becoming more than that. It’s a platform for customer engagement, too.

I left IT in 2010 to build a career in freelance copywriting, but wanted to leave a legacy for all those years in the trenches of support. So I wrote an eBook about how to create a knowledge base, and I set up this blog to wax lyrical on knowledge management and collaboration. Through marketing and social media efforts, I managed to find a niche—IT professionals struggling with knowledge management.

So I took a left turn and then another one, and now I advise those IT service management professionals through this blog, the eBook, in person, and conference presentations.

I still write for money, too.
