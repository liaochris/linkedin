Applications Development

Universal Imaging

1988 – 1989 (1 year)

I worked helped Ted Inoue and his team develop, demo, and sell a system that connected research grade high resolution cameras to a high quality optical microscope, grab a frame, and then apply analytical filters to the image to automate the detection, counting and recording of live cell observation. Super cool gear for great science work. A big user was the Marine Biology Lab and the European Molecular Biology Lab. I loved that job. I left it to start CoSA/The Company of Science & Art, which made PACo and AfterEffects. Great very early pre-QuickTime digital video and what is now an industry standard for motion graphics.
