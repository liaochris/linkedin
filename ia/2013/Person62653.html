Jerome Jewell's Summary

In 1995, Jerome formed Jewell Consulting Group, a management consulting firm helping organizations improve productivity and leadership. He has provided consulting and leadership training to a broad range of clients, including NASA, AMGEN, Lucent Technologies, the United Nations, COMCAST and the U.S Department of Homeland Security.

His background includes two decades with global industry leaders, including IBM and Chase Manhattan Bank, where he developed operations and marketing expertise in the airline, banking, systems integration and satellite telecommunication sectors.

Jerome’s work has taken him to France, Greece, Argentina, Mexico, Hungary, Belgium, the U.K, Canada and Bermuda and he has conducted considerable primary research in higher education, financial services, professional sports management and national defense.

He has scaled Mt. Kilimanjaro and Mt. Rainier, run the Marine Corps Marathon, swum the Alcatraz Challenge in San Francisco Bay twice and broken bread in another 11 nations...all part of his outlook.

Jerome coaches Executive MBA and MBA students at Columbia Business School, has coached in Georgetown University's MBA Program and is a former faculty member at Howard University's MBA Program, the AMA and Management Centre Europe - Brussels. He has also provided career transition and outplacement coaching as a subcontractor at DBM and Right Management.

He has an MBA from Columbia University and continues to study Spanish, French and German. He’s certified in the Emotional Competency Inventory, a 360-degree assessment tool developed by Dr. Daniel Goleman (author of “Emotional Intelligence”), to help leaders understand and improve their emotional intelligence.
