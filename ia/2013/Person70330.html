Judith Field's Experience

Director, speaker, coach, facilitator

Direct Speech (Self-employed)

Self-Employed; Myself Only; Professional Training & Coaching industry

June 2004 – Present (9 years 4 months)

Judith Field is an outstanding presenter, trainer, facilitator, author. Having been a brilliant English teacher for over 30 years, she understands the importance of communication skills. In 2004 she set up Direct Speech and turned her passion and talents to empower managers in the corporate world, eductors and students with powerful presentation skills.
One of her top achievements was her role as Literaacy Coordinator for secondary teachers in the state of Victoria. Whilst a teacher, she also helped found King David School, a leading Liberal school in Melbourne and led the team that chose the philosophy, first principal and teachers. This experience gave her tremendous experience as a leader, educator and communicator.
Now in her workshops for corporates and schools as well as the public, she combines intuition, humour and tricks to speak to the voice in the head and shift any limiting beliefs around speaking and presenting.
She has recently launched a DVD on all the skills needed to prepare, present and speak confidently. It is interactive and very practical. Judith is currently writing four books caless Speech Matters for students and teachers on speaking. Theses four books have many practical applications for the public as well as specific guides for teachers. they are connected to the new Australian Curriculum. These books and the DVD will allow her to share her skills far beyond where she can physically be.
One of the best in the business, Judith can guarantee shifts in her clients in the most feared area of life: public speaking,
Her unique style includes a Master of NLP. This can help shift individuals from where they are to where they can be. The feedback from clients and schools has been outstanding.

Head Of English

Educational Institution; 201-500 employees; Education Management industry

This elective became so effective, that one participant who had been shy, went on to win the school's public speaking competition. Another came second in a Victorian public speaking competition.
Judith also introduced many fun activities in the school, including DEAR (Drop Everything and Read), English Week, with professional writers and games. Judith has brought all these creative and visionary ideas into her speaking busienss.
