The Alpha Delta Phi Society is a literary society and one of North America's foremost coeducational Greek-letter institutions, with five active chapters. The Alpha Delta Phi was founded by Samuel Eells in 1832 to encourage free thought and to supplement a rigid classroom education. In 1992, after years of controversy over the status of women within the organization, the Alpha Delta Phi formally separated into the Alpha Delta Phi Society, which could accept women, and the all-male Alpha Delta Phi Fraternity.

Craig Cheslog's Projects

  • Alpha Delta Phi Society - 2012 National Convention

    • March 2011 to March 2012
    Team Members: Craig Cheslog, Kristen Soule, Ann Ford, Terrie Eastmade, Chad Wolfsheimer, Michael Rose, Michael Blitstein, Jennifer Ragsdale, Vihang Mehta

    The Alpha Delta Phi Society is coeducational organization dedicated to cultivating character, scholarship, and enduring friendships. Our literary tradition fosters life-long intellectual growth, mentoring, and achievement among our members. The annual Convention and Leadership Training Conference provides an opportunity for the Board of Governors, Officers, and representatives from all of our chapters to guide the organization's operational and policy decisions, to attend training sessions for personal and professional development, and to recognize high achievers. Planning and hosting this event, including local excursions and social activities for the participants, requires a dedicated team of organizers who begin a year in advance. In 2012, we hosted the event in "the Renaissance City"; Providence, RI.
