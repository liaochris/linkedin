Griet Dehandschutter's Summary

I am an international fundraising consultant. I specialize in advising large and small nonprofits around the world on how to raise funds in the United States.

I never expected to live anywhere else than Belgium, where I grew up and studied law and where my entire family still resides today. All that changed, when I went to study at the Johns Hopkins University Center in Bologna, Italy, and I met my future American husband. International relations turned from a pure academic study subject into my life’s journey.

Starting with my first dream job in New York City as Managing Director of the first pan-European Chamber of Commerce, I discovered my twin passion for fund development & entrepreneurship, as I helped to raise the funds for this new start-up.

Over the next 20+ years, I worked on a wide range of international projects, tackling pan-European communications issues at a global PR firm in Brussels and establishing a new investment office for the Flemish government in Boston. In all cases, my focus was on strategically helping organizations to develop their message, build consensus among senior leaders and bridge different cultures.

Today, I am still in the US, still married to the man I met in Bologna, and still bridging the gap between Europe and America in both my family and my work life.

Every day fills me with wonder and excitement, as I work with nonprofit clients around the world, and help them to discover new horizons, while building internal and external support for their cause.


Specialties:

Fundraising Consultant, Fund Development (annual, major, capital), Donor Communications (creative case statements), Staff and Board Development, Tailored Coaching, Prospect Research and Network Strategies, Fundraising Program Evaluation, Capacity Building, and Public Speaking.
