Alison Anderson Holland's Summary

A rural Mora, Minnesota native and resident, I’m a process-based Integrative Teaching Artist and Social Practice Choreographer. I’m usually teaching things like fractions through chance dance theory and line in the visual arts though creative movement, but I’m also a studio dance teacher, musical choreographer, the writing specialist for a standards-based digital curriculum project, and a freelance news and feature writer for the local paper.

In my creative practice, I’ve danced at Franconia Sculpture Park and presented a “chance dance” in a church basement -- anything to bring modern and post-modern dance into rural communities in accessible, constructive, and meaningful ways. My work is grounded in a desire to improve the quality of rural life and encourage others to be active in their communities. I utilize vacant, underutilized, and unique spaces, activating them with movement events that both surprise and engage.
