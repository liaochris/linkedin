Mathieu Carenzo's Projects

  • REDIVE - Recruitment for Effective European Cultural Workforce Diversity

    • August 2012 to September 2014
    Team Members: Mathieu Carenzo, Ana Barroca, Catarina Neto, Gonçalo Meireles, Dirk Van Dierendonck, Milton Sousa, Małgorzata Mikłosz

    REDIVE is a project financed by the Erasmus Programme under the Lifelong Learning Programme from the European Commission.

    Based on an initial extensive and in-depth research in partnership with several European Business Schools and HR professionals across Europe, we propose developing and running an extensive training program for effective recruitment across cultures. This will ultimately help ensuring the effectiveness of workforce diversity, enhancing competitiveness and reducing recruitment and retention costs. It can also support the effectiveness of programs like Erasmus and induce workforce mobility and successful diversity in EU.
    The project will start in October, 2012.

    PROJ. Nº 528362-LLP-1-2012-1-PT-ERASMUS-ECUE

Mathieu Carenzo's Publications

  • Trovit S.A.

    • IESE Publishing
    • February 2, 2011
    Authors: Mathieu Carenzo, Jeffrey Berry, Luis Martin Cabiedes, Antonio Davila

    It was early summer of 2010, and CEO Iñaki Ecenarro had just hung up the phone. He had spent the last hour speaking with a venture capitalist from London. A London based venture capital firm had made a formal offer of €30 million for 100% of the Trovit’s equity. The next day, the partners would meet with the principal investor to discuss what to do. The offer was certainly attractive for the young entrepreneur who had founded the company just four years earlier.

  • The First 10 Questions for Would-be Searchers

    • IESE Publishing
    • September 30, 2011
    Authors: Mathieu Carenzo, Ian Nieboer, P.Eng, Antonio Davila

    This note answers ten of the most common questions asked by people who are relatively new to the search fund model, or have never heard of a search fund before. The content of this note is based on the experience of search funds from around the world, and will help prospective entrepreneurs become familiar with the search fund model and the important questions that should be answered before considering the model more seriously. At the end of each question, the reader is provided with additional resources to deepen his/her understanding of the issues discussed.

  • Considerations for Entrepreneurial Acquisitions

    • IESE Publishing
    • September 30, 2011
    Authors: Mathieu Carenzo, Ian Nieboer, P.Eng, Antonio Davila

    This note synthesizes interviews with entrepreneurial acquirers and financiers from across the globe. The discussion and recommendations that follow reflect the varied experiences of entrepreneurial acquisitions practitioners and attempts to encapsulate the considerations any would-be entrepreneurs should bear in mind as they embark on the journey of acquiring an entrepreneurial opportunity.
