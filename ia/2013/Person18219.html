Bruce Burtch's Experience

Win-Win for the Greater Good

Author

June 2013 – Present (4 months)

"Win-Win for the Greater Good provides the “how to” blueprint for organizations of any size and from any sector to build highly productive partnerships. It reveals the true essence of success - focusing on the business objectives of your partner, while striving together to create a greater good."

Casey Sheahan, CEO, Patagonia, Inc.

Win-Win for the Great Good, http://amzn.to/18MGnhU, is the first complete step-by-step guidebook on building highly successful partnerships between the for-profit, nonprofit, education and government sectors. Learn how to impact the revenue and reputation of all your stakeholders while creating a greater good for society. With over 35 years of in-the-trenches work in creating innovative, record-breaking cross-sector partnerships, Bruce has helped Fortune 500 corporations and local businesses, national, regional and local nonprofits, education districts and governmental agencies. Called the "Father of Cause Marketing" Bruce is internationally-recognized as a leading expert in building cross-sector partnerships.

This book takes the reader by hand and walks them through the entire process of finding the best partners and developing partnerships with other sectors that address their individual business objectives while always focusing on the greater good.

CEO

Bruce W. Burtch, Inc

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Marketing and Advertising industry

2009 – Present (4 years) San Francisco Bay Area

Designs and directs highly effective cross-sector partnerships, primarily between nonprofit and for-profit organizations, to generate increase economic and social benefit. Provides workshops, trainings and seminars under the trademark of "Glowing Your business", Bruce is considered one of the most respected subject matter experts on developing cause marketing in cross sector partnerships in the country. He is credited with designing the first cause marketing program, a partnership between Marriott Corporation and the March of Dimes, in 1976.

Bruce is sought out as a speaker and presenter for his insightful approach, deep experience and heartfelt passion for creating good through cross-sector partnerships.

Executive Director

10,000 Partnerships

2011 – Present (2 years) San Francisco Bay Area

10,000 Partnerships will give like-minded individuals and organizations a platform to discuss the design and implementation of highly successful cause marketing and cross-sector partnership programs between any combination of nonprofit, for-profit, education and government sectors. We look forward to sharing, collaborating, and building a deep knowledge base from everyone's participation in this discussion. Our ultimate goal - to motivate the development of 10,000 cross-sector partnerships, resulting in a greater good for society.

June 1985 – December 1992 (7 years 7 months) San Rafael, CA

Founded this product design and development company which produced cosmetic and personal care organizational products. Under the trademark "Reina Alexis Barone", the company's patented products were hailed by the beauty/retail industry as the finest in the industry. They were the first products in their category to be accepted into over 1,200 specialty retail stores, including Neiman Marcus, Saks, Bloomingdales, Nordstrom, Macys, I. Magnin, and in catalogs such as Horchow, Touch of Class, Paragon and Spiegel.

The company and/or its products were the subject of over 100 major media articles including features in the Good Housekeeping, Los Angeles Times Magazine, Shape, San Francisco Business Journal, Packaging Digest and many more.

Founder, CEO and President

The William Bentley Agency

October 1980 – May 1985 (4 years 8 months) San Francisco, CA

Founded this full service integrated marketing company. The agency directed the market introduction of over $2 billion of prime California real estate projects for such clients as Chevron, Marriott Hotels, Mobil, Campeau Corporation, Lincoln Property, Tishman Realty and The Lurie Co. The agency designed the highly successful California introduction of cellular technology for AT&T; marketed Soviet Trade Expo '91 and '92 (the largest business trade delegations ever to visit the US from the former Soviet Union); promoted the largest beauty show on the West Coast for Glamour Magazine; directed media relations for the California State Special Olympic Games; created San Francisco's largest Holiday Festival to benefit Toys For Tots and many other award-winning cause marketing campaigns.

Director of Public Relations and Special Events

United States Olympic Committee

Nonprofit; 201-500 employees; Sports industry

September 1977 – October 1980 (3 years 2 months) Squaw Valley, CA and San Francisco, CA

Hired on special assignment reporting directly to USOC Executive Director. Directed corporate sponsorship and fund raising, public relations, large-scale special events, celebrity liaison, and media relations. Developed the opening promotion of the country's first Olympic Training Center, a Today Show national network feature on the Center, the Kenny Rogers Celebrity Tennis Tournament and created a 56-city simulcast radio benefit entitled Salute to America's Amateur Athletes. Designed two major national events, benefit concerts by Neil Diamond and the Beach Boys, which were canceled due to the U.S. boycott of the 1980 Olympic Games.

Public Affairs Manager

Marriott's Great America

Designed the award-winning, record-breaking opening promotion of the $200 million family entertainment center, the largest development to date of Marriott Corporation. Youngest member of management team, which built one of the world's leading entertainment complexes. Hired and managed staff which smashed existing theme park publicity records. Cited by Billboard Publication's Amusement Business for "bringing a new level of professionalism in public relations" to the family entertainment industry.

Bruce Burtch's Projects

  • 5th Annual Health and Wellness Fair, September 15, 2012

    • January 2012 to September 2012
    Team Members: Bruce Burtch, Ramsell Corporation, Alameda County Health Department, California State Assemblymember Sandré R. Swanson, Center for Elders’ Independence

    Partners: Ramsell Corporation, Alameda County Public Health Department, California State Assemblymember Sandré R. Swanson, Center for Elders’ Independence

    Sponsors: Kaiser Permanente, Oakland Mayor Jean Quan, Nate Miley, Pres. Alameda County Board of Supervisors, One Pacific Coast Bank

    5th Annual Health and Wellness Fair was held September 15, 2012 at Oakland’s Eastmont Town Center. 90 San Francisco Bay Area health and wellness providers offered free health and wellness assessments and services to over 600 low-income residents of Oakland and the East Bay. This free-to-the-public Health Fair is designed for ethnically-diverse, low income and underinsured populations, and is the result of a unique community partnership to do what none of them could have accomplished on their own.

    Assemblymember Sandré R. Swanson stated, ““This unique partnership is a perfect example of what can happen when concerned community-focused organizations see a common goal. Combining the abilities of these diverse for-profit, nonprofit and governmental organizations means that far more Oakland and East Bay residents will benefit from the services provided at the Health Fair. This innovative partnership stands as a promising model for others to follow.”
