James Garrow's Summary

Jim is a disaster planner who specializes in emergency public information, crisis communication and risk communication. An accomplished public speaker and noted thought leader, Jim has been invited to and spoken at conferences across the US on social media use in public health and emergency response. He also provides media relations support for the Philadelphia Department of Public Health and has served as Public Information Officer for a hazardous material Unified Command response. Jim manages a variety of Department social media accounts and writes the Face of the Matter blog.

Health Communications Specialist

Philadelphia Department of Public Health

Government Agency; 1001-5000 employees; Government Administration industry

February 2007 – November 2009 (2 years 10 months)

I developed and coordinated the Philadelphia Department of Public Health’s (PDPH) H1N1 influenza public information and outreach campaign. This work has continued into my current position.

For the last three years, I managed the Philadelphia Health Alert Network (HAN) and distributed 78 Health Alert messages to more than 3,000 Philadelphia-area health care providers, while maintaining and keeping up-to-date the HAN contact database. In addition, I managed the internal emergency notification system used by PDPH. Under my direction, PDPH received perfect scores in the Tactical Communications section of the annual CDC Local Technical Assistance Review for each of the last three years.

I was in charge of designing, developing and maintaining a website, the Health Information Portal (hip.phila.gov), intended for use by local health care providers. This website contains the latest local disease surveillance, information on disease reporting and diseases of public health importance, and a library of Health Alert messages. I was tasked, during the H1N1 influenza pandemic, with redesigning the PDPH Division of Disease Control website.

During my time as Health Communications Specialist, I participated in all public health preparedness planning efforts, exercises and emergency activations. Those efforts that I am proudest of include my participating in Joint Information Center (JIC) during the multi-agency full-scale exercise testing the distribution of Strategic National Stockpile materiel by the United States Postal Service to three zip codes in Philadelphia, and writing a plan for a public health call center designed to support emergency medication points of distribution (PODs) as well as developing and running the exercise of that plan.
