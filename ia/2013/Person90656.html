Matt Turner's Projects

  • Gravity gradiometer

    • April 2010 to Present
    Team Members: Matt Turner

    I am developing a wirelessly-powered active-mass torsion balance for low-drift gravitational field measurements. The device will address the leading systematic uncertainty in our test of the equivalence principle.

  • Rapid prototyping & fabrication tools

    • 2011 to Present
    Team Members: Matt Turner

    I initiated, wrote, and managed successful grant proposals for the purchase of a high-power CNC laser cutter system and a 3D printer for our lab’s student shop. I oversaw the purchase and installation of this equipment and have developed and conducted the training for student users.

  • Interferometric quasi-autocollimator

    • 2009 to Present
    Team Members: Matt Turner, Charlie Hagedorn

    I developed, built, and tested an optical instrument for angular measurement with picoradian sensitivity to support future experiments by our group. A patent application for this device is pending.

  • “Segfault” balancing scooter

    • 2010 to Present
    Team Members: Matt Turner

    As a personal project, I designed and built a Segway analogue for a final cost of under $300.

  • Low-energy electron gun

    • 2009 to Present
    Team Members: Matt Turner

    I designed and built an LED-based device to controllably deposit electrons on an isolated body in vacuum.
