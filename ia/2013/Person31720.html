David Harris' Summary

David was born and raised in Detroit, MI. Growing up he had many mentors and people in his life that contributed to his learning and experience with education. After graduating from the University of Michigan in 2006 with a Bachelor’s degree in Electrical Engineering, he went to work for Microsoft as a software test engineer in Redmond, Washington. After college he got a chance to be a mentor while working with Kappa League, a youth program of Kappa Alpha Psi Fraternity. Realizing the impact that mentors and tutors had on him in the past, he started Dream Allies, a life skills and tutoring program. Witnessing the achievement gap steadily increase between minorities and their counterparts gave David a sense of urgency to take action.

After three years at Microsoft, he decided to go down another road on his journey and help bridge this gap. On his path he is concentrated on serving his community through education and technology. He has since worked as an Apple Genius, helped numerous people and businesses with their technological endeavors, and works with Technology Access Foundation (TAF) working on their “Teacher/Scientist Partnership”. The mission statement of TAF aligns perfectly with David’s personal goal to serve his community through education and technology. In addition to the work David does throughout the day, he also loves finding new music, learning about new technology, golf, and having fun on the water.
