PhD Student in Information Systems

NTNU

Educational Institution; 1001-5000 employees; Higher Education industry

October 2007 – August 2012 (4 years 11 months) Trondheim Area, Norway

I have studied the development of national e-service infrastructures supporting collaborative development and provisioning of public services. The objective is to contribute to the delivery of citizen-centric and demand driven services, and to the establishment of a hypothetical My Processes citizen's portal. This portal will provide process-oriented multi-channel personalised interaction for all public services. Using the design science for information systems approach, the presented papers are based on the analysis of the existing knowledge base, the modelling of design artefacts, and case studies in the problem environment.

The five following contributions are presented as part of the thesis: 1) A model describing the dynamics of e-government, 2) future scenarios for citizen-centric and demand driven public services, 3) requirements for an e-service infrastructure for public service provisioning, 4) a conceptual design for a national e-service infrastructure, and 5) a conceptual e-service governance model. In addition to a general focus on process-oriented service provisioning, overviews of the current use of process models and the flexibility supported by process-aware information systems are also provided.

The contributions are targeted towards system developers and researchers that are working with governance or development of e-government systems providing public services, and those that are working with holistic aspects of e-service infrastructure development.
