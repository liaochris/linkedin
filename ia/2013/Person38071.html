Laura Jana's Summary

My professional experiences are all focused on improving the lives of children and parents - whether via national media, pediatric practice, the community, the child care setting, or in my writing as a early childhood, pediatric and parenting expert.

Having served as a consultant for Dr. Benjamin Spock, I went on to co-found the national parenting company that bears his name. I became increasingly involved in not only working with children and their parents, with a focus on behavior, development, health and safety and early childhood, but also in communicating with/helping parents on a wider scale through various forms of media - including national radio, television, and print.

My first book, entitled Heading Home with Your Newborn; From Birth to Reality (American Academy of Pediatrics, 2005) received the Parenting Book of the Year Award. Food Fights: Winning the Nutritional Challenges of Parenthood Armed with Insight, Humor and a Bottle of Ketchup (AAP, Oct, 2007) deals with the everyday challenges of feeding young children and received the 2008 Gold Medal Mom's Choice Award.

I have served as content editor, reviewer, contributor, including serving as head of the Medical Advisory Board for the Walt Disney Internet Group and on the advisory board for American Baby Magazine. I frequently contribute to or am quoted in the national parenting magazines and for national media outlets and have reviewed, edited, or contributed to several parenting books.

I also have more than a decade's worth of experience in corporate consulting - with extensive experience both on and off camera helping ensure that marketing and/or corporate educational content aimed at reaching parents/children is both accurate and effective.

Ultimately, my goal is to continue pursuing new and rewarding ways to extend my reach and impact as a health communicator and advocate for children - especially vulnerable children - and improve the lives of all children and their families.

Specialties

Health communication, media experience & training, small business ownership, and writing/editing skills.
