David MacGregor's Experience

Founder

MacGregor Media

July 2011 – Present (2 years 4 months) Auckland, New Zealand

MacGregor Media is a media product development vehicle. I create commercial concepts and content across all media (TV, web, magazines, apps for tablets and phones and mobile marketing).

Emphasis is on marketing insights, persuasive storytelling and high levels of engagement across media.

Creative Director Product Development

BrandWorld

February 2008 – December 2012 (4 years 11 months)

Developing proprietary media concepts for New Zealand's largest brands.

BrandWorld is the leader in masthead marketing. Family Health Diary, Eating Well and Discover are award winning properties that deliver exceptional results for clients.

I was a founding partner in BrandWorld, selling my stake in 2002 to develop other media interests.

I returned to BrandWorld as Creative Director responsible for ideation and new product concept development such as The Mix - a groundbreaking shopper marketing programme in the liquor sector that has revitalised the spirits category.

Founder/Creative Director/columnist/blogger

Idealog Magazine

November 2004 – June 2012 (7 years 8 months)

I developed and launched Idealog magazine in partnership with HB media.

The purpose of the magazine is to describe, promote and celebrate the creative economy. The sponsorship model I developed accelerated our early success. Idealog is now the most widely distributed business magazine in New Zealand and has received praise for its content and presentation from around the world. Idealog has won the Magazine Publishers Association (MPA) Business Magazine of the Year every year of its publication.

Aside from concept development of the publication - including design direction and editorial direction - I functioned as art director and designer of the first year of publication. I was intent on commissioning the most interesting editorial photography and illustration to create a unique title whose brand position was clearly differentiated from all other titles in a cluttered market.

Creative Director-Owner

BrandWorld

Lipitor was a major launch into a heavily defended market. Developed an innovative dual strategy to promote the brand and general cholesterol lowering campaign to stimulate early testing by doctors.

Creative Director

Creative Director

Young & Rubicam

Public Company; 5001-10,000 employees; WPPGY; Marketing and Advertising industry

January 1990 – February 1992 (2 years 2 months)

Young and Rubicam was established in New Zealand but struggled to achieve creative recognition. It's stalward clients were international alignments. During my tenure we were able to revive many client brands like Coruba Rum for Allied Liquor Merchants - setting the brand back on course with a revival of the famous Do Da Coruba theme.

Senior Copywriter

Hutcheson Knowles Marinkovich

1988 – 1989 (1 year)

HKM was a highly regarded ad agency in the late 80's - mid 90's.
The firm was noted for its thoughtful approach to tasks (not many dancing girls and jingles in their ads). While I was there (both times) I developed many successful campaigns, but I think the pharmaceutical work was the most notable - with government and banking not far behind.
With art director James Blackwood we produced some very early work using digital imaging to launch the New Zealand Employment Service - a campaign so successful - it did its self out of a job.

Copywriter/Art Director

Rialto Advertising

1986 – 1988 (2 years)

Rialto was part of the Clemenger/BBDO group. During the time I worked in the creative department there it enjoyed a period of stellar success - one of the top three firms in New Zealand in creative award rankings. I worked under the creative direction of Brian Harrison. Together we produced a string of successful campaigns for the likes of Mitsubishi Motors, Montana Wine, Tip Top Ice Cream, Movenpick, Pepsi…Rialto was later folded into sister agency HKM

David MacGregor's Publications

  • Vanishing Act - A Novel

    • Brainforest Press
    • February 10, 2009
    Authors: David MacGregor

    A simple yarn: Vanishing Act is the story of marine biologist Dr Hinemoa O'Reilly; smart, telegenic, Maori. Her television show is syndicated across the globe, earning her fame and millions in royalties and endorsements. When she visits the waters around New Zealand, filming the Humpback whale migration her team discover a sub-species of the world's rarest dolphin. The world's media, politicians and environmental activists all converge on the small fishing town of Kawhiamatu for their moment in the sun. A tale of love and loss, money and murder and, ultimately true discovery.
