Anchises Moraes' Summary

As an active member of Brazilian Information Security community with about 20 years of experience in Information Technology, I have been working in the Information Security field for about 15 years.

I've been acting as an Information Security advisor providing assessment, modeling and contributing to achieve company's goals on creating a future-state IT infrastructure where Information Security is a key component of business strategy. Strong experience designing and integrating complex enterprise-wide solutions for large companies, interfacing with senior executives and technical departments.

Skills include security policies, technologies and project management. Used to contribute thought leadership both internally and externally, and network with peers from the industry actively. Able to assume a creative leadership role to implement the business and/or technology strategy efficiently and effectively.

Before current position as Threat Intelligence Analyst at RSA, I worked as Cyber Intelligence Analyst at iDefense, Verisign. I also worked about three years as an IT and Information Security independent consultant for leading Brazilian companies and also for small business. Former security officer at leading Brazilian Telecom and ISP companies. Early professional experiences include working as Security Product Manager and Internet & UNIX System Administrator.

Bachelors Degree in Computer Science from IME-USP (www.ime.usp.br), Master Degree in Marketing from ESPM (www.espm.br). Previous certifications include CISSP, ITIL Foundation and SANS GIAC GHTQ certified. I've been authoring security papers and articles, working as Infosec instructor and speaker at conferences in Brazil and abroad.

President of ISSA Chapter Brasil-SP from 2008 to 2009 and Membership director from 2006 to 2008. One of the founder members of Cloud Security Alliance (CSA) chapter Brazil and Garoa Hacker Clube, the first Brazilian HackerSpace. Organizer of Security BSides São Paulo conference.

Conference Organizer

Security BSides São Paulo

January 2011 – Present (2 years 8 months) São Paulo Area, Brazil

Security BSides São Paulo (BSidesSP) is a one-day conference on information security and hacker culture, organized by Brazilian hakerspace Garoa Hacker Clube as a way to promote the hacker culture and the integration between security researchers, professionals, and students in the country. BSidesSP is organized twice a year, usually featuring two tracks with talks and various workshops.
BSides (www.securitybsides.com) is a global series of community-driven conferences for and by information security community members. The goal is to expand the spectrum of conversation by creating opportunities for individuals to both present and participate in an intimate atmosphere that encourages collaboration. It is an intense event with discussions, demos, and interaction from participants. It is where conversations for the next-big-thing are happening.
