Dan Wallace's Summary

I enjoy working with leaders and teams to learn, create and grow.

The ideal client is facing dramatic change and/or in search of transformational growth. When it is time to execute, I can draw upon a network of more than 1500 talented colleagues and specialists.

Depending on the client need, I can work as an marketing strategist, creative director and writer. During each engagement I work to transfer knowledge, skills, insights, ideas and contacts.

Dan Wallace's Experience

Marketing Strategist & Creative Director

Idea Food, Inc.

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Marketing and Advertising industry

June 1986 – Present (26 years 9 months) Greater Minneapolis-St. Paul Area

Idea Food is all about helping companies create and grow. It began as "Dan Wallace, Copywriter" and I wrote everything from direct response TV ads to brochures to fortune cookie messages. During these years I worked on more than 500 brands and collaborated with dozens of talented designers, art directors, illustrators, photographers and videographers.

Messerli & Kramer asked me to represent them as an ad agency, and "Dan Wallace, Copywriter" became the virtual agency "Dan Wallace, et al." Other clients included The Bureau and Palomino Euro Bistro.

I started Idea Food in 2001 to provide food for thought and creative direction for and with companies, agencies, design firms and consultants. Idea Food is also the brand name for several products that help people learn, create and grow.

I also work with Jeff Brown of Catalytic Innovation to help leaders and teams find their WHY.

VP Programming

American Marketing Association

Nonprofit; 51-200 employees; Marketing and Advertising industry

2000 – 2001 (1 year)

Over seven years with the AMA I heard more than 100 speakers and met many new colleagues. I still try to attend two learning events every week.

Account Management

Fallon

Public Company; 201-500 employees; Marketing and Advertising industry

1984 – 1986 (2 years)

Worked on The Wall Street Journal, Coleman, MedCenters and Murray's Steakhouse. Fallon had just won Advertising Age Agency Of The Year, and I sat across the hall from the most recognized writers and art directors in the industry. It was a heady time. Working there inspired me to switch from account management to copywriting.
