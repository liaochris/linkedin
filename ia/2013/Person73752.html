Keith A. Yount, DDS, MAGD's Summary

Dr. Keith A. Yount graduated from the UNC Chapel Hill Dental School in 1979 and completed an fellowship Academy of General Dentistry in 1985. While in private practice in NC, he was often perplexed by cases involving chronic facial pain.

Dr. Yount completed a 3 year fellowship in orofacial pain from University of Florida at the Parker E. Mahan Facial Pain Center at the University of Florida. His time there included rotations through ENT, Anesthesiology, Psychology, Pharmacology and Oral Medicine, Oral Surgery and Physical Therapy. He successfully completed the educational and clinical requirements to earn the title "Diplomate of the American Board of Orofacial Pain" in 1996.

When his wife began to experience chronic daily headaches, Dr. Yount became even more committed to finding solutions that would help people suffering from chronic pain of the head and neck area.

In 1997, Dr. Yount moved to Raleigh, NC and established a practice dedicated to orofacial pain, which is dedicated to caring for patients with chronic muscle or joint pain, or dysfunction of the trigeminal system. When the joint muscle complex of chewing system is inflamed (painful) or dysfunctional (restricted opening), we manage the pain with orthopedics. The same orthopedic receipe used for damaged knee joint.

In 2005, Dr. Yount completed his Mastership in general dentistry from the Academy of General Dentistry.

Orofacial Pain Specialty: Includes the treatment of jaw joint pain, restricted opening, atypical toothache, atypical earache, tension headache, and burning mouth.

Keith A. Yount, DDS, MAGD's Publications

  • Appliances Design & Application

    • Academy General Dentistry
    • November 2012
    Authors: Keith A. Yount, DDS, MAGD

    Upon graduation from dental school, I discovered that my knowledge of dental appliances was quite weak. Dental school taught us that the soft nightguard was adequate for tooth protection in grinding teeth. Unfortunately, the knowledge about parafunction was all but missing from our dental school training, also.

    As you know, there are numerous appliances on the market from OTC to professionally made, from in-office fabricated to laboratory fabricated, from upper to lower, from hard to soft, from passive to active, repositioning to centric relation appliances, and segmental to full coverage. With the limited exposure to the science of parafunction, jaw joint problems, and appliances, we are left with little ability to decide what appliances to use in our practices and on what problems. Most of the time we are left with song and dance of an appliance company guru. For all due purposes, the manufacture guru says there is only "my appliance" for all purposes. The “one appliance cures all” selling point should be the one point that causes you the most skepticism of such a product.

    We want to provide the best care for our patients. We want to solve their problems. We want to be appreciated by our patients. Providing the best clinical care based on best science provides the best success. Apply science to the proper selection of appliances in your practice not the fastest, the cheapest, the easiest to deliver, but the right appliance for the right job.

  • Burning Mouth Syndrome

    • Practical Pain Management
    • December 2007
    Authors: Keith A. Yount, DDS, MAGD

    Unrelated and dIverse collection of mouth pains in which burning is the primary symptom. The medication induce dry mouth is one most common causes in elderly due to the multiple medications for heart, thyroid, and diabetes. The second most common cause is significant up-regulation of sympathetic system (protection system of body) set off by environmental, genetic, biochemical, and repetitive negative thinking habits (worrying, fretting, and perfectionism).   • Atypical Earache

    • Practical Pain Management
    • December 2004
    Authors: Keith A. Yount, DDS, MAGD

    The referral of pain into the ear is the 2nd most common pain seen by medical doctors and ENT. In some cases the ear is still suspected and antibiotics are administered, but fail to reduce the ear pain. THe ear is the next door neighbor to jaw joint with multiple interconnects and relationships. The most common condition is the jaw joint referring pain into the ear. Another relationship is in 6% of the population there is a ligament that attaches inside the ear on Malleus and connects to medial aspect of articular disc of the TMJ. IF the disc displaces enough and the pull on the ligament is significant, a person can get ear ringing, ear discomfort, and even dizziness. Majority of the causes of dizziness and tinnitus (ringing) are associated with ear.
