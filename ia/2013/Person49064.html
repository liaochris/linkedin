Glenn Parker's Publications

  • MEETING EXCELLENCE: 33 Tools to Lead Meetings That Get Results

    • Jossey-Bass/Wiley
    • 2006
    Authors: Glenn Parker, Robert Hoffman

    Meeting Excellence is a comprehensive resource that provides a wide range of ready-to-use tools that have been developed and tested by a meeting initiative within Novartis Pharmaceuticals. It is based on years of research observing team meetings, examining existing meeting documents, and conducting a number of intensive individual interviews in the U.S. and Europe. This important book offers the information and tools needed to prepare, facilitate, and follow up on all your meetings.

  • CROSS-FUNCTIONAL TEAMS: Working with Allies, Enemies and Other Strangers

    • Jossey-Bass/Wiley
    • 2003
    Authors: Glenn Parker

    In this completely revised version of his best-selling book, Cross-Functional Teams: Working with Allies, Enemies, and Strangers, author and consultant Glenn Parker updates his definitive practical guide to include his recent work in team rewards and recognition, communications technology, and multicultural and virtual-team issues. This new edition contains fresh examples and additional case studies of successful cross-functional teams from IBM, Parke-Davis, Xerox, Boeing, BOC Gases, government agencies, and more. Parker offers concrete advice and inspiration to team leaders, team members, and senior management. Cross-Functional Teams delivers a team operating manual to executives, team leaders, human resource professionals, and students of organizational behavior and provides a tool kit of assessment surveys, worksheets, checklists, and even sample training programs to help launch and sustain effective teams.

  • TEAM PLAYERS AND TEAMWORK; New Strategies for Developing Successful Collaboration, Completely Revised and Updated Second Edition

    • Jossey-Bass/Wiley
    • 2008
    Authors: Glenn Parker

    Since the first edition of Team Players and Teamwork was published in 1990, there have been significant and dramatic changes in the business environment in which teams now operate. Teams are now cross-functional, cross-cultural and often virtual. As a result, team trust is more vital than ever and communications technology is critical. With team players often serving on multiple teams, leadership has become important and more challenging. In this thoroughly revised and updated edition of his groundbreaking book, Parker analyzes these trends and shows how his concept of effective teamwork is even more relevant today. Team Playes and Teamwork provides specific, practical tools that will help both leaders and members identify their team player style ---contributor, collaborator, communicator or challengers. The book explains how each style contributes to the five key leadership functions --- planning, communication, risk-taking, problem-solving and decision-making. It includes and shows how to use the Parker Team Player Survey and the Team Development Survey to increase team effectiveness.

  • REWARDING TEAMS: Lessons from the Trenches

    • Jossey-Bass/Wiley
    • 2000
    Authors: Glenn Parker, Jerry McAdams, David Zielinski

    Using 27 actual case studies from a variety of leading companies, Rewarding Teams provides a blueprint for building team reward programs that spur development and success. The book focuses on the three most important types of team-based rewards programs--recognition plans, project team incentives, and group incentives--offering readers detailed advice on how they can create and implement such programs themselves. Twenty-seven profiles of team reward and recognition plans from today's top companies give readers an in-depth look at how these plans work in actual practice. They also provide the basis for the set of best principles included in the final chapter.

  • Teamwork and Team Play: Games and Activities for Building and Training Teams

    • Jossey-Bass/Pfieffer
    • 1999
    Authors: Glenn Parker, Sivasailam Thiagarajan

    The world's two best-known team-building facilitators bring you over thirty-five cutting-edge activities.The game formats are varied: some short, some long; some icebreakers, some closers; and much more. A game selection matrix enables you to find a game that suits your situation. Plus, training legends Thiagi and Parker share with you their proven insights on effective teamwork and facilitation.

  • TEAM DEPOT: A Warehouse of Over 585 Tools to Reassess, Rejuvenate & Rehabilitate Your Team

    • Jossey-Bass/Pfieffer
    • 2002
    Authors: Glenn Parker

    Team leaders of all types will put the contents of this book to good use. With chapters formatted as "aisles" you can pick and choose the tools that you have on your shopping list. Aisles include tools for Revisiting your team's goals; Redefining team members' roles; Reestablishing ground rules, Rejuvenating team meetins; Rebuilding a climate of trust and much more. The book begins with a stop at "customer service" where you will find the "Lost in Team Depot" directional assessment that will help guide you to the aisles that provide solutions geared to your team's issues.

  • PARKER TEAM PLAYER SURVEY

    • CPP
    • 1991
    Authors: Glenn Parker

    The Survey is an 18 item, easy-to-use, self-assessment instrument that helps individuals identify their team player style. The results and the related exercises give people insight into their strengths and to address their weaknesses. The Survey booklet includes complete scoring information, interpretation of results, action planning guide and a team score grid. Organizations use the Survey as part of a new team kick-off, an ice breaker in a teams class, an exercise in a team training class and as a team building intervention with an intact team. A companion survey provides each person with feedback from teammates on their style.

  • 50 ACTIVITIES FOR TEAM BUILDING, Volume 1

    • HRD Press
    • 1992
    Authors: Glenn Parker, Richard P. Kropp, Jr

    A series of practical exercises designed to help the busy trainer create and sustain a high-performing team. For each activity the authors provide a clear purpose, the ideal group size, time required, room set-up, materials needed (handouts are provided), a step-by-step process for accomplishing the goals as well as some possible variation. The activities cover team diagnosis, skill building, conflict resolution, decision-making, communications and leadership development, All materials in the volume are fully
    reproducible for use in training programs. A detailed index facilitates activity selection.

  • PARKER TEAM BUILDING PROGRAM FACILITATOR'S GUIDE

    • CPP
    • 2003
    Authors: Glenn Parker

    Designed especially for users of the Parker Team Player Survey and the Parker Team Development Survey, this loose-leaf binder includes training designs, team building designs, lecture noes, 17 team activities, Power Point masters, pre-course assignments as well as back-up materials such as a reliability/validity report and a normative data report for the Parker Team Player Survey.
