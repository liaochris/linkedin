Gregory James Blount's Summary

I am an independent producer who works for companies such as Disney and Universal Studios. I have produced projects, concerts, fashion shows and photo shoots for companies such as CosmoGIRL, ELLEgirl, Rampage, MasterCard. I manage several Disney casting directors, set and live audience coaches, as well.

I own Peter Glenn Publications (www.pgdirect.com). Since 1956, PGP is the leading entertainment publishing company for the model, talent and fashion industry for nearly 60 years. Our MODEL & TALENT AGENCY DIRECTORY lists over 2,000 legitimate agencies all over the globe. We are also well know for publishing the famous Madison Avenue Handbook for nearly 40+ years. Currently I travel and teach talent and parents about what it takes to break into the entertainment and talent industry, discuss expectations, career opportunities and how not to get scammed.

My next project is working to build a state of the art, Charter School for the Arts in Palm Beach County for kindergarden through 8th grade. The school will be called the Eagle Arts Academy, as I am an Eagle Scout. I believe in the importance of arts education and preparing our children for a future of promise. The school will educate children on both sides of the camera. In addition, we have just established the EMPPAC Foundation to help serve our cause.

I have discovered amazing talent including actress Amber Heard (The Rum Diary, Pineapple Express) and work with stars such as Jake Short (ANT Farm) and Olivia Osteen, niece of Pastor Joel Osteen. Recently, while producing a project at Lakewood Church, Jake and I had the privilege to speaks to the youth ministry at Lakewood Church and also support their Circle of Hope ministry.

I believe in the importance of giving back. I work with several charities including Blessings in a Backpack and TOMS Shoes, to name a few. My team and I support the Village Academy in Delray Beach.

My complete bio is available at: http://www.soundtreeentertainment.com/about.asp

Founder

EMPPAC Foundation

January 2013 – Present (9 months)

The EMPPAC Foundation purpose is to change the way we look at American education. For decades we have facilitated industrial education and it is now time for the American educational system should make a dramatic change to support a more direct informational educational system.

For years, schools have taken arts education out of schools. The EMPPAC Foundation's purpose is to instill arts education into core curriculum, into core classes such as reading, writing, math, social studies and science. By combining auditory and visual stimulation, we can inspire the next generation of students.
