John Sheppard's Experience

Director, Customer Programs & Experiences

REI (Recreational Equipment Inc.)

Privately Held; 5001-10,000 employees; Retail industry

2007 – Present (6 years) Greater Seattle Area

I lead our customer programs and experiences division (200+ employees), toward the objectives of customer acquisition, increasing brand loyalty, and diversifying revenue channels. My team brings the REI brand to life in very real ways every day. Research shows customers engaged by our experiences are twice as likely to transact and spend three times more than an average REI customer.

+ REI Adventures, REI's global adventure travel company, one of the largest in the industry operating over 150 different adventure programs on all seven continents and in over 40 countries. Manage over 80 business partners around the globe. I led the business unit to a 78% revenue growth over past four years and a 330% growth over the past seven years.

+ REI Outdoor School, REI's national outdoor education program, which inspires and instructs outdoor enthusiasts through day and weekend classes and outings. Our objective is to deepen loyalty by helping customers accomplish their aspirations. I originated the business unit in 2007 and it is now the nation's largest outdoor education program, offering over 11,500 classes serving over 250,000 program participants annually.

+ Community Development, bringing together partner organizations and customers to build relationships that connect customers to the brand, the outdoors, and one another.

+ Event Marketing, engaging customers in unique and memorable ways at the local and national levels through activations, sponsorships, partnerships, and produced events and tours. Developed comprehensive program to engage millions of customers and potential customers in brand experiences.

+ Localized Social Media, developing customer relationships through locally relevant content and experiences.

+ I am a member of REI's Retail Leadership Team and represent the retail division on several senior level cross-divisional groups focused on integrating REI's brand, sales, and marketing strategy across all our channels and communications.

General Manager, REI Adventures

REI

Privately Held; 5001-10,000 employees; Retail industry

2004 – 2007 (3 years) Greater Seattle Area

Drove an average annual sales growth of 26% and increased profitability by 216 basis points. I am most proud of the work I did to re-organize the business unit and the difficult decisions I made early in the transformation process regarding product assortment strategy, organizational structure, financial planning, and marketing strategy. During this process we developed new innovative programs, such as Weekend Get-aways, Family Adventures, and the REI Outdoor School, which supported REI’s core business strategy, extended REI's market penetration, drove REI brand engagement, and delivered significant incremental product revenue.

Business and Operations Manager, Seattle REI Flagship Store

REI

Privately Held; 5001-10,000 employees; Retail industry

1997 – 2004 (7 years) Greater Seattle Area

I led the complex operations of REI’s largest store ($65MM in annual revenue) and most visible brand experience by providing leadership to seven managers, 37 professional roles, and 140 service and support staff. Promoted 17 individuals into leadership roles around the company. Led the development of an enterprise Retail Dashboard business analytics tool and the required organizational change to ensure its use and success. I am most proud of creating a strategic plan which resulted in a renewed commitment to the Flagship Store customer experience. The vision and foundation of this plan were adopted as REI’s core purpose statement and remains the foundation of REI's strategic plan today.

Retail Specialty Shop Manager, Seattle REI Flagship Store

REI

Privately Held; 5001-10,000 employees; Retail industry

1995 – 1997 (2 years) Greater Seattle Area

I served on the new management team charged with transitioning REI’s original and iconic Capital Hill Store to a new Flagship Store location and brand experience. I led the $26MM apparel and footwear business. In this role, I am most proud of integrating two distinct employee cultures, the long tenured Capital Hill Store staff and the new Flagship Store staff, into a cohesive 145 member team recognized for its selling culture and quality customer service. In year one, drove a 22% sales increase.
