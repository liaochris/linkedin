Isaiah McGee's Summary

McGee is owner of McGee Strategies LLC, a training & consulting, and coaching firm specializing in generational behavior, social capital, work engagement, and social networking. McGee Strategies focuses on seeking resources and solutions for individuals, organizations, and businesses that lead to growth, engagement, functionality and fulfillment.

Originally a Los Angeles native. Isaiah attended Cornell College, where he was a two-sport athlete in football and wrestling, hosted a popular radio program, and served in many leadership roles. Isaiah also received a Master’s degree in Education from Drake University in Des Moines.

McGee has established relationships with numerous business leaders, change agents, and officials at every level and party affiliation of government. Two newspapers have called him "One of Des Moines' Movers and Shakers under 40” and he has successfully worked on numerous issues from local ballot initiatives to campaigns for local, state, and federal offices. At age 26, Isaiah was elected to the city council of a Des Moines Suburb, one of the fastest growing communities in the nation.

Isaiah has also received recognition for his leadership and efforts in a number of organizations and non-profit boards including the Greater Des Moines Partnership, Greater Des Moines Community Foundation, the African-American Chamber, Leadership Iowa and other civic and leadership groups.

Isaiah writes a popular article or the Des Moines Business Record and host a weekly radio show, both dealling with engaging the next generation.

Isaiah has also been featured in numerous national, state, and local press and media, including Time Magazine, the New York Times, Associated Press, NPR, MSNBC, FOX News, Des Moines Register, local TV and radio.
