Lois Phillips, PhD's Summary

My background combines academic research with executive administrative experience. In addition, I've co-hosted two Cable Television programs and one NPR program. I enjoy the art and craft of communication, from the inception of an idea to the implementation of the message. I've achieved success and recognition in terms of honors and awards for my work in diversity, gender, the field of communication, and also higher education, and feel gratified in that regard.

Specialties

Public Speaking, Training and Consulting to Institutions and Companies, Coaching.

Lois Phillips, PhD's Experience

Author of "Women Seen & Heard," Conference Speaker, Founder of SCA

Strategic Change Associates

January 1988 – Present (25 years 10 months)

I provide consulting services to schools, college, and universities as well as governmental agencies, commissions, and the non profit sector with regard to strategic planning processes. This involves forging consensus around a common VISION of the future and identifying the strategies to get there. First, an 'environmental scan' identifies trends, demographic shifts, and challenges as well as opportunities to succeed in achieving goals. As important is the identification of internal and external stakeholders who have a stake in the organization's future. I speak at conferences and coach executives to deliver dynamic presentations and speak to the media.

Founding Board and first President

Association for Women in Communications -Santa Barbara Chapter

2007 – 2010 (3 years)

I established the Santa Barbara Chapter of Women in Communications as its first President and built the Board to produce a stellar event to honor "Women of Achievement" who are outstanding communicators.

consultant

Antioch University Santa Barbara

Nonprofit; 11-50 employees; Higher Education industry

2006 – 2010 (4 years)

I worked with the Chancellor and then President to build AUSB's first Board of Trustees.

President

Communications Consulting

1988 – 2010 (22 years)

I assist individuals, groups, organizations in branding themselves and communicating their message to various publics in the most effective way. Sometimes that involves training, coaching, Board Development, or speechwriting. I can also provide evaluation services when a Board or foundation requires an assessment of an agency or program's quality and impact. I work best with "C suite" executives in developing and implementing a communications campaign and where appropriate, coaching an individual so that she or he is a dynamic presenter, to include Media Skills Training.

Learning Team Leader

Linkage Womens Summit

2007 – 2008 (1 year)

Professional women are chosen to facilitate groups of women who are attending the LINKAGE CORPORATION WOMEN'S SUMMIT, held in San Francisco or Boston twice a year. The theme of my Learning Team was "Women Negotiating." We meet twice during the 3 day conference to debrief about what was learned from the speakers and workshops and discuss our focus in terms of research and practical experience.

Founding Executive Director of local campus

Antioch University Santa Barbara

Nonprofit; 11-50 employees; Higher Education industry

June 1977 – June 1988 (11 years 1 month)

As the founding CEO of the local campus, my role was to build enrollment, hire faculty, mount the BA and MA Psychnology Program, and find adequate space. I needed to maintain fiscal stability and quality control in line with accreditation requirements. I reported to the Provost of Antioch University West at first and later to the Chancellor of Antioch University in Yellow Springs Ohio.
