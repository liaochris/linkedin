Matt Nestor's Summary

As an Engineer with Boston Device Development, Matt is responsible for the implementation of complex mechanical systems. Over the course of his 10 year career in engineering and manufacturing, Matt has pursued a range of projects from medical devices and consumer products, to robotics and large-scale capital equipment.

Prior to joining Boston Device Development, Matt worked for Cytonome/ST, and Mass Bay Engineering. Some of his notable projects included an optical detection system for micro-fluidic cell purification systems, a high speed splicing device for the printing industry, and multiple products for both the consumer and military firearms industry.

In addition to Matt’s professional experience, he was the recipient of the 2011 ProtoLabs Cool Idea! Award for his magnetic hex key case, or “QuicKaddy,” which improved the speed and usability of one of the most common tools in the world.

Matt studied mechanical engineering at Daniel Webster College and Benjamin Franklin Institute of Technology.

QuicKaddy

July 2011 – Present (2 years 6 months) Greater Boston Area

QuicKaddy, recipient of Proto Labs 'Cool Idea! Award', is a must have for any toolbox. QuicKaddy eliminates the unnecessary steps found in traditional hex key cases by allowing the user to access the tool they want without fiddling with any other tool in the set. By replacing the through holes found in standard cases with slots QuicKaddy allows the user to access and store tool in a more efficient manner. Many people don't realize how much time is wasted when using standard hex key cases but the time and frustration add up quickly. QuicKaddy allows users to get the tool they want up to 200% faster!

Matt Nestor's Patents

  • QUICK RELEASE HEX KEY CARRYING CASE (QuicKaddy)

    • United States Patent Application xxxx2260
    • Filed 2011
    Inventors: Matt Nestor

    This is a brand new Patent Pending carrying case for Hex Keys. I have been using the prototypes for just under a month and cannot understand why this hasn't been done before. In traditional plastic Hex Key carrying cases the tool is secured to the case by being placed in a hole running the length of the case. This requires that in order to remove most tools in the set, another tool must be spun out of the way to avoid interference. Typically the shorter right angle side of a hex key overhangs the hole(s) in which other tools are placed. This means that in order to remove most of the tools in the set other tools must be touched, moved or somehow altered to allow access to the one you want. This is an annoying and a waste of time. The new case allows the removal of a hex key both vertically as well as horizontally relative to the case where all existing ones allow you to only remove the tool vertically relative to the case. My case eliminates the need to move or alter any other tool in the set to remove the one you want by replacing the through holes found in a standard case with slots running the length of the case. There is a magnet(s) in a cavity in the case in between the two banks of tools. This retains the hex keys in the case securely while also allowing easy removal. The magnets are strong enough to prevent the tool from being knocked out when dropped or juggled around a tool box while at the same time not overpowering and difficult to use. I feel as though this is a case that should and will replace all existing cases. Everyone is looking to make their lives easier and that exactly what this product does.
