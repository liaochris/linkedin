Jean Marois' Summary

Brings 20 years of experience delivering effective solutions to the challenges facing researchers in the drug discovery space. Spent the last six years as an Associate Director at Merck & Co., where during those years had responsibilities as Global In vitro Sciences IT Account Manager, Global External Basic Research IT Account Manager, Montreal Research IT Site Account Manager, and led the New Technology Research Licensing Committee for IT ventures.

From 2007 to 2009, he was a member of Merck's effort to redesign its basic research global operating strategy, and played a lead role during the current state assessment activities, mapping out the drug discovery processes from Target ID Validation to Lead Optimization. He was also a core member of the task force reviewing the operations for Research Informatics, IT, & Automation. In 2010, was a member of the Merck/Schering-Plough task force rationalizing the set of In Vitro IT systems.

Obtained a Bachelors of Computer Science - Honours Program with distinction from Concordia University, Montreal, Quebec, Canada, and later as a graduate student of V.S. Alagar at Concordia Univ. conducted research into Formal Specification Languages for Describing Concurrent Systems.
