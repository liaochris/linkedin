Dan Callahan's Experience

President/Manager

Live Cell Assays, LLC

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Biotechnology industry

July 2008 – Present (5 years 6 months) Walnut Creek, CA

The President/Manager is responsible for developing, sublicensing, and commercializing Visual-Servoing Optical Microscopy (VSOM) a patented method for vision-based automated decision-making during live cell imaging processes, procedures and experiments. The VSOM Method is a practical implementation of next-generation automated scientific experimentation. VSOM can be implemented on commercially available automated live cell imaging instruments (i.e., High Content Analysis (HCA) and High Content Screening (HCS) imaging instruments). These instruments can be VSOM®-enabled via plug-in VSOM® Application Software. VSOM®-enabled live cell imaging instruments have the ability to (repeatedly) make real-time automated decisions during live cell imaging processes, procedures and experiments. These real-time automated (software-based) decisions are based, in part, on real-time live cell response analysis. This adaptive and responsive mode of automation (VSOM® Automation Mode) equips HCA imaging instruments for next-generation biological complexity and next-generation fluorescent protein technologies. VSOM® Automation Mode provides long-term, vision-based, adaptive and responsive automated experiment control during live cell imaging experiments. This innovative approach makes automated discovery and optimization of new VSOM® Assays, bioprotocols, bioprocesses possible. VSOM® Application Software makes HCA and HCS instruments more versatile, efficient and productive. Our software will soon be offered as a Cloud-Based service. Cloud-based remote control of live cell imaging instruments is a key feature of the patented VSOM Method. (Note: VSOM® is a Registered U.S. Patent and Trademark Office trademark belonging to Live Cell Assays, LLC)
