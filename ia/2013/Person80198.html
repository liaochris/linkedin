Leonie Driessen's Summary

I'm a multimedia designer specialized in concept design and e-learning. I come up with idea's, work them out to be solid concepts and love seeing them work in the real world. I also enjoy teaching students about concepting, media psychology and media design, which I have done in the past and am now doing again at Fontys Academy for Creative Industries. I have also worked in e-learning, where it's always my goal to not only make (digital) learning material, but to make the students (whatever their age) enjoy working with it. If possible, have them enjoy it so much that they almost forget they're learning.
Other things I do and have done contain: experience design, logo- and multimedia design, marketing and photography.

Leonie Driessen's Projects

  • !nspiratieLab

    • February 2008 to February 2010
    Team Members: Leonie Driessen, Wies Noest, Wouter Buning, Lolkje van der Kooi, Merel Laferte, Sjef Smeets, Patrick van Aalst, Michel Van Galen, Jacco De Weerd

    !nspiratieLab (InspirationLab) is a platform for creative thinking, authenticity and design, created because we felt these subjects did not get enough attention within the study of Communication & Multimedia Design. We took every Wednesday morning and filled it with lectures, workshops and trips on subjects requested by students. Students were free to join whenever they were interested in the subject and many teachers participated in sharing they're knowledge on these diverse subjects.

    !nspiratieLab is still running. In the year of the kick-off I was still a student myself, about to start graduating. For three years I've been one of four workgroup members. Making sure there was a Lab Session every week. Also this is where I started teaching myself. Through the years I've done sessions on concepting, design, media psychology and serious gaming.

  • Library Mysteries

    • March 2010 to January 2011
    Team Members: Leonie Driessen

    The "Library Mysteries" are a concept I created around the assignment I got to create two digital teaching packages for the Library of Eindhoven. These short digital courses were meant to use in school for kids ages 10 up to 14 and were mainly about cultural subjects.

    The concept that could be used on every thinkable subject in short is about two teens, working in the library in a part time job as researchers. Whenever something strange happened (like a really old letter found in a book or a painting stolen from the library) these researchers were the ones figuring it all out. This way a mysterious story was woven around every subject and the user would be asked to help out as an actual researcher and solve the mystery, while learning about the subject in the process.
    In fact, the whole idea is to lure kids into learning. They're having fun solving the mystery and sometimes forget that they are actually learning a lot in the process.

  • Fries Straat Festival (Street Theatre Festival)

    • December 2005 to May 2006
    Team Members: Leonie Driessen, Rienk Jan Schurer, Karin Gorter, Yu Yi Wu, Janneke ten Rouwelaar

    While studying Communication & Multimedia Design I've worked on numerous projects. Still this one still stands out, because it was so different than the others and surely a great experience.

    For a street theatre festival we were asked to create part of the festival involving digital media. Our client wanted a big projector screen in the middle of the city centre, showing pictures that visitors of the festival would make and bring to us. Also they wanted us to interview visitors, asking them how they were experiencing the festival. We as creative students found this plan quite boring and decided to lift it to a higher level. We set up some crazy concepts and they were all turned down... We figured out they just didn't know what to think of it, because they'd never seen anything like it before and so we decided to go through with the ideas anyway, because we were sure they would work out well on the festival.

    In the end we created several colorful wooden stands. One where we had some computers to upload the pictures of visitors, which would be projected on the big screen. We build a tiny theatre with a fabric tent-like top and red curtains, in which a visitor could push a red button and then record a video message of 15 seconds, talking about the festival, while seeing themselves on the screen in front of them. These video's also got projected on the big screen. Last but not least we thought of an extra use to the big screen: a giant computermouse game! We created a huge mousepad with a gigantic mouse on it that actually worked. Kids (almost the same size as the mouse itself) would move the mouse around, controlling a character on the big screen that they had to steer through a maze (looking like the city we were in) and bringing in to the festival. Touching a wall meant starting over.
    All elements were populair and the theatre ("15 Seconds of Fame) has even been used for similar purposes later on, but the (at first turned down) "Mega Mouse Game" was a huge hit.
