Brandon Keim's Summary

I am an attorney with the IRS Office of Chief Counsel in Phoenix, Arizona practicing in the area of tax controversy. I represent the IRS in a variety of federal tax cases in the United States Tax Court, including corporate, partnership, collection, and estate and gift tax matters. I am responsible for identifying desired legal theories, developing trial strategies, and advocating on behalf of the IRS. I also provide legal advice to IRS audit teams on all areas of tax law, including international, corporate, and partnership matters, and tax procedure. I coordinate the IRS's litigation position with the Department of Justice in the handling of collection and refund cases.

As a CPA and former financial statement auditor, I bring a strong understanding of accounting and business concepts to my tax practice.

A summary of my recent article, Schedule UTP: An Attempt to Side-Step the Work-Product Doctrine, 44 Ariz. St. L.J. 343 (2012), can be found here:
http://taxprof.typepad.com/taxprof_blog/2012/08/schedule-utp.html
