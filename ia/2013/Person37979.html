Dr. Jenni Silberstein's Summary

Jenni, Ph.D., M.F.A., L.M.F.T. is a licensed psychotherapist as well as a professional writer and media expert (Bravo, MTV, Fox) specializing in relationship issues. Some of her television appearances have included appearing as a relationship expert on a national network talk show and as a regular psychotherapist on a television series. Her educational and clinical experience is extensive. For over fifteen years she has been assisting individuals in overcoming dysfunctional behaviors which have kept them from flourishing in their lives as well as their relationships.

In her private practice her innovative techniques and personalized approach have helped individuals, couples and families reestablish essential connections and transform their difficulties into opportunities for healing.

Her articles have been featured in magazines such as The California Therapist and LA Parent and she has frequently lectured around Orange County and Los Angeles on relationship issues.

Jenni currently lives in Los Angeles with her husband Jeff, her three children Jake, Bella and Lily, her chocolate lab Talula and her cats Rocket and Moonie.

Specialties

Relationship issues, children and adolescent therapy, anxiety, depression, divorce, eating disorders

Dr. Jenni Silberstein's Experience

Owner/Psychotherapist

Center for Creative Psychology

January 2000 – Present (13 years 8 months)

The Center for Creative Psychology offers each client the opportunity to enter their own unique path towards healing and growth. Through a safe, therapeutic relationship, individuals are assisted in confronting the obstacles and difficulties which have kept their lives from reaching full potential.

The philosophy of CCP is that we are all on a journey back to ourselves. Within all of us lies a core of wholeness and well-being which has been covered up by painful past experiences. The process of growth involves a releasing of old patterns and an opening to present possibilities.

We consider it an honor at CCP to assist you in this return back to yourself.

Artist

Art in Mind

January 1985 – Present (28 years 8 months)

Ever since she was a little girl, Jenni has been interested in art and writing. As a child, she could always be found drawing on anything and everything, creating hundreds of magical and fanciful creatures and images.

As Jenni grew up, these enchanting images were combined with words as her journals overflowed with her daily experiences, feelings and never-ending stories.

After graduating with a B.A. in English where she studied the classics, Jenni was accepted to many prestigious art schools around the country. While deciding whether or not she wanted to enroll, Jenni worked for an international art company in San Francisco, (Art Group International) helping place world-renowned artists’ work in private homes and hotels around the world.

Inspired by her mother, who worked in the field of psychology Jenni’s interest in the human mind grew and she decided to advance in the field of psychology, receiving an M.A. in counseling psychology and a Ph.D. in depth psychology.

While practicing psychology for over fifteen years in various settings, including her private practice, the Center for Creative Psychology, Jenni’s passion for art and writing grew. While treating hundreds of clients struggling with relationship issues, anxiety, depression, divorce and eating disorders, Jenni observed that all of her stories and artwork were universal messages.

Her simple, child-like characters (He and She) represent the yearnings of all human beings – to find true connection and understanding. Their flat, surface-like appearance mirrors our everyday existence in a mundane, earthly world.

Her more complex versions of He and She depict our journey to the depths, the unconscious, the dream-world where nothing is as it appears and everything is full of marvel, complexity and wonder.

Author

The Gossamer Thread

January 1985 – Present (28 years 8 months)

In college, Jenni decided to major in English, where she deepened her love for literature. After receiving her B.A. from UC Santa Barbara, it was her mother who influenced her to embrace the field of psychology. Jenni went on to receive an M.A. in counseling psychology from the University of San Francisco, an M.F.A. in creative writing from the University of Southern California and a Ph.D. in depth psychology from Pacifica Graduate Institute.

Jenni is now a licensed psychotherapist, professional writer, speaker and media expert (Bravo, MTV, Fox) specializing in relationship issues. Some of her television appearances have included appearing as a relationship expert on a national network talk show and as a regular psychotherapist on a television series. Her educational and clinical experience is extensive. For over fifteen years she has been assisting individuals in overcoming dysfunctional behaviors which have kept them from flourishing in their lives as well as their relationships.

In her private practice, the Center for Creative Psychology

Throughout her career in psychology, Jenni continued to write stories and dabble in the arts. The more clients she saw in her private practice, the more she recognized the universality of all human beings yearnings and desires. She knew that eventually she would need to translate these yearnings into a creative medium.

During her classes at U.S.C. Jenni laid the groundwork for her novel, writing a memoir entitled Angels in the Snow. In her Ph.D program, Jenni began writing The Gossamer Thread, which she included in her research-based, artistic dissertation, An Angel and Her Feather; how Fact and Fiction heal and Explore the Archetype of Home.

Jenni has written many psychological articles for professional magazines (The California Therapist, LA Parent.) Most of her “Art in Mind” stories were written in her early twenties.
