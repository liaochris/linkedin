Fernando Covello's Summary

Fernando Covello has been involved in the information security industry for more than fifteen years. He is a primary advisor and consultant regarding the implementation of control systems cyber security and industrial network risk analysis to support critical infrastructure protection.

A history of success delivering measurable value to a myriad of clients in multiple industries, from Fortune 500 technology environments to small, high-growth companies. Experienced in managing risk as organizations apply technology to complex business processes.

Fernando is currently focused on the cyber threat in the oil and gas industry, developing a cyber security policy framework for the secure use of industrial control systems and other critical digital assets at oil and gas industries.

Involved in multiple cyber security projects covering a spectrum of oil and gas industries, he is leading a several security assessments for two oil and gas companies operating in South America, as well as the largest power and water utilities in Brazil. He also helped devise some industrial security projects internationally.

In South America, he serves on and supports public/private advisory working groups and task forces including the Brazil’s Security Advisory Telecommunications Committee and the Committee for Critical Infrastructure Security; Cyber Security and Industrial Control Systems Working Groups.

Familiar with industrial security standards: ISA/IEC 62443, ISO 27001 (compliance/certification process), BS 25999-2, CPNI UK, NERC-CIP, NIST, API 1164, and CFATS.

In his free time (using time management techniques) he enjoys golfing, sailing, traveling, working out, badminton, and rocking out with a ‘garage Jazz band’.

Specialties: Advanced Persistent Threats (APT), CISM, CISA, CISO, IT Audit, emergency preparedness policy, critical infrastructure protection, SCADA security, and penetration test.
