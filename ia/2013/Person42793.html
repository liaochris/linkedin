Erin Powers M.Ed. BCET's Summary

Erin Powers has been a practicing Educational Therapist for over twenty years. She currently coordinates Learning Services for The Carey School and has a thriving private educational therapy practice. Ms. Powers also provides parent education through the Morrissey-Compton Educational Center, most noted for her talks titled, "Homework 911" and "Attention - Understanding Attention and How it Impacts Student Learning." Five years ago Ms. Powers began teaching courses in Educational Therapy through the University of California Extension Program at Santa Cruz, with varied class offerings ranging from "Introduction to Educational Therapy," to "ADHD in the Classroom- for teachers" to her current role as Coordinator of the internship program. Ms. Powers recently added "Educational Consultant" to her already impressive resume as she began conducting research with the founding team of  the "Goalbook" website. Ms. Powers is also the proud parent of an astonishing, inspiring, perfectly imperfect son with ADHD.  

Erin Powers M.Ed. BCET's Experience

Product Development Consultant

Goalbook

June 2012 – Present (1 year 4 months) Palo Alto

Parents of students with special needs often have difficulty getting everyone on their child's team collaborating and on the same page.  Parents also receive overwhelming amounts of paperwork, oftentimes complex and difficult to understand.  Goalbook is an exciting new product designed by a former special education teacher.  Goalbook provides a simple and fully secure online solution to help parents enable collaboration, keep paperwork organized, better understand their child's IEP, and discover additional resources and supports available to them.

Here's a short video that shows our existing collaboration product that's being used in districts and schools.  We want to essentially provide a similar product for parents to use so they can loop people in and better communicate / coordinate.
