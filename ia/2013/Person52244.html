Heather Kinzie, SPHR, GPHR's Summary

I know the value of quality Human Resources!

With expertise in recruitment and selection, training and development, job analysis and design, labor and employee relations, and investigations/risk assessment, I provide business leaders, Human Resources (HR) professionals and management staff practical, relevant and valuable HR consultation.

I offer a variety of engaging facilitation services to include workforce planning, strategic planning, team-building, and process analysis and redesign.

My training courses include topics to enhance Leadership, Supervision, Communications, Productivity, Customer Service, and Cultural Awareness and Diversity. In addition, I can create and deliver a variety of Human Resources courses to ensure your HR staff is well prepared to lead your organization to success.

I am a member of the Society for Human Resources Management and proudly served on the Board of Directors for the Anchorage Chapter for eight years.

Finally, I serve on the Board of Directors for the Alaska Performance Excellence (APEX) Foundation. Although small, APEX is mighty and it gives me more than I give it for sure! It is Alaska's version of the Baldridge Quality Improvement Program and I am committed to learning as much as I can to help my clients discover the value in continued learning and quality improvement.

Specialties: Training and Development, Organizational Design and Development, Employee Relations, Investigations, Process Improvement, Presentations and Keynotes, and FUN!

A Leading Solution

I hung out my shingle as a Sole Proprietor in 2007.

I love process improvement projects and years ago, started using the LEAN methodology and improvement process to help my clients save thousands in operating costs each year. Past President 2011

ASHRM (Society of Human Resource Management, Anchorage Chapter)

2004 – Present (9 years) Anchorage, Alaska Area

The Anchorage Chapter of SHRM is an incredibly active Chapter, offering multiple development and networking opportunities to HR Professionals each year. ASHRM has received numerous awards for its service to the Anchorage community and is proud to say its Board Members are some of the most dedicated HR professionals in the state!

I served on this Board for eight years in a variety of capacities, the latest of which was the 3 year commitment of Incoming President, President and Past President Positions. This was hard work, much harder than I thought and more time consuming than I had realized! But it was fulfilling and I'm proud to have done it.

At this time, I serve as an ad hoc volunteer for the Board, filling in when they need some help here and there...I love the Board too much to step away completely!

I worked in two different capacities for the Consortium, serving as a Human Resources Manager for two different programs and then again as an internal consultant performing various projects aimed at improving its organizational performance.

Working for The Growth Company was an interesting career move and served as a stepping stone to A Leading Solution.

There is always a time in one's career when a professional looks for some validation of his/her talent and expertise. The year at The Growth Company served this purpose. I learned that consulting was my "path" and hence, A Leading Solution was created.

Various Titles beginning with Personnel Assistant and ending with Human Resources Manager

State of Alaska

Government Agency; 10,001+ employees; Government Administration industry

1994 – 2003 (9 years)

When I moved to Juneau in 1993, I quickly realized that steady employment in the little city would mean government work.

Having a training and development background naturally led me to the State's personnel department. As was typical at the time, I had to pay my dues, working in support positions performing (dare I say) "grunt work" as I gained the years necessary to move up in the organization.

The career ladder to management positions promoted a diverse background in all areas of HR; I worked in classification, recruitment, training, organizational design, labor and employee relations, etc. While I can't say I loved every minute of it, I must say I learned more in these years than many professionals learn in a lifetime. I believe it is through this experience that I have a "global" approach to workforce improvement and development.
