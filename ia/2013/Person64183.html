Jim Spadaccini's Summary

I am the Founder and Creative Director of Ideum. Our company develops innovative hardware and software and is keenly focused on the future of Human Computer Interaction (HCI). Our multitouch tables are used by Fortune 500 companies, research laboratories, government agencies, universities, museums, and other design and technology firms around the world.

Ideum has also designed and developed Gestureworks, the industry-leading engine for multitouch and motion-tracking development. Gestureworks works across platforms and supports hundreds of 2D and 3D gestures. As part of this development, we introduced the world’s first markup language for HCI, GestureML.

Along with hardware and software products, Ideum collaborates with museums, educational institutions, and socially responsible companies to develop software, dynamic online communities of practice, and large-scale exhibits.

Before Ideum, I was the Director of Interactive Media at the Exploratorium, where I was responsible for managing the Website and for developing media exhibits for the floor. I currently teach a course for the Cultural Resource Management program at the University of Victoria and previously I taught for the TEC-CH program at the University of Lugano in Switzerland. I am the Principal Investigator of Open Exhibits, a National Science Foundation (NSF) funded educational software initiative. I am also the co-Chair of the NSF-funded Human Computer Interaction in Informal Science Education (HCI+ISE) conference.

Specialties: project management, design, multitouch UI, new HCI technologies, motion recognition, interaction design, art direction, creative direction, exhibit design, museum studies, multitouch tables,
