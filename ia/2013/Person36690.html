Donald Macleay's Summary

I was a journeyman machinist/millwright (what the anglo-aussie crowd call a "turner-fitter" in Canada, Nicaragua, Italy, Germany and here in the USA from 1980 to 2000. I still have a metal shop. My profession became computerized using "computer numeric control" programing. I can program in "G-Code" on Fanuc and Cincinnati machines.

During the 90's I also went to college earning a Liberal Studies degree at SFSU. During that time I became an early adopter of the desktop computer for word processing and telecommunications. I used the early internet with Procomm Vt100 terminals for IGC. At SFSU I had a Vax/VMS account and became an early user of GOPHER, Elm, Pine, Lynx etc.

In the late 90's I took training in MS Networking at LANOP earning an MCP/MCSE in NT4. At this point I also started to be self employed.

From 2000 to 2005 my role at work was that of the "hands on" tech manager. At that time I had my "bad business partner" experience. My reaction to it was to find better partners and pick ourselves up where we were.

From 2005 to today I am more involved in the business side of the business.

I like the field I am in and have no desire to leave it. There are some related fields I think my company can branch out to given the opportunity to do it and maintain our quality level.

My goals are to grow the current company using the standards we have developed over the last few years.
I am very happy to leave the hard core tech development to others and focus on client satisfaction, quality control and staff development. I very much enjoy the contact with the clients. I also enjoy the financial planning, business budgeting and attention to accounting and book keeping that many other business owners do not like.

Specialties

Lathe, mill, surface grinding, cylinder grinding, large VTL's.
Gas, arc with stick, MIG and TIG welding.
CNC G-Code Programing on Fanuc and Cincinnati
Core Microsoft networking using NT technology Domains.
TCP/IP configurations on network computers and routers.
Subnet planning. DHCP / DNS schema
Cabling, layout, computer mounting and layout in server rooms and collocation facilities.

Director of the Cua-Bocay Micro-Hydro and Appropriate Technology Development Project

Proyecto Cua-Bocay

January 1985 – December 1987 (3 years)

I was the founding Director of a development project in rural Nicaragua. My first job was to finish the installation of a micro hydro system using a Banki cross flow turbine and hydrolic regulation. We have a 3 phase Y distribution system over 11 KM that served 3 small rural towns.

Once the electric service was working we needed to set up an electric company. Following that we started adult education programs in line work, internal house wiring and coffee processing equipment maintenance, repair and use.

The project also involved itself in other Appropriate Technology projects, included one lead by Mira Brown on the LORENA cook stove (first started in Guatemala) We continued to involve ourselves in coffee and rice growing and processing. Supporting basic grain production (rice, beans and corn) and finding ways to use the electricity we were providing to support local education, health and economic needs.

During the course of this work our lead engineer, Ben Linder was murdered by the Contra forces. I had already left the project at that time, but returned for a short while to help recover from the loss of this key man. The exact dates I was working on this project are not so clear. We worked on setting up the power generation before a project was started under the auspices of the Nicaraguan Energy Institute (INI) and some of my work on the project was unofficial, as a project supporter.
