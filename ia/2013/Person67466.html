Jonathan Caines' Projects

  • HIGHWAYS AGENCY / SERCO (Appius) - Traffic England

    • December 2007 to July 2009
    Team Members: Jonathan Caines, Greg Pike, Simon Annicchiarico, Ben Aston, Ash Clarke, Oliver Gitsham, laura cannings

    Traffic England is a public website provided by the Highways Agency through the National Traffic Control Centre, providing live traffic information about the motorways and A-Roads in England (gathered for their operations centre via various road sensors and cameras). The prime-contractor had a long term contract to operate the National Traffic Control Centre, including the website. The project involved a tender process and subsequent implementation, test and maintenance phases and an overall service contract of 3 years. The project team had to demonstrate to the client and the prime contractor, extensive skills and a proven track record in the areas of project management methodology, user centred design, technical architecture, bespoke development and integration, application design, GiS mapping.

    Project objectives:

    - Ensure the site is designed for a varied audience from business commuters to tourist users
    - Improve usability through strong layout and design
    - Integrate with various real time traffic information feeds from the NTCC
    - Enhance the presentation of the traffic information on the maps
    - Better use new types of traffic information such as average speed and road side camera shots
    - Specify a robust hosting infrastructure capable of withstanding massive ‘crisis day’ website visitor levels
    - Design and build associated applications required for the website
    - Install, support and enhance the infrastructure across a 3 year contract period
