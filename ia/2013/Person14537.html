Billy Parrish's Summary

In 1981 I was fortunate to be a part of the national rollout of the Main Street® Program, as one of the original thirty Main Street® Managers in the six state pilot program. Main Street® redefined the strategy and structure for downtown revitalization in America, and for over 30 years Main Street® has provided the foundation for thousands of downtown development programs across the U.S. Over the ensuing years I have managed the development of the National Main Street® state programs network, served as the principal executive manager for a national educational foundation, served as the COO of the nation's largest nonprofit statewide historic preservation organization, managed an affordable housing development nonprofit with 2,000 units under management, and run the downtown development programs for the state of Georgia for almost a decade. Now I have returned to my first love - working with communities to renew their downtown and neighborhood commercial areas. In this next phase of my varied but interconnected working life I am working closely with the people and programs that are bringing new life to America's cities and towns. My niche consulting practice focuses in the areas of downtown and neighborhood development, with clients including the Georgia cities of Augusta, Brunswick, Covington, Madison, and Toccoa, the Alabama Main Street Program and The Alabama Historical Commission, and Upstate Forever, a Greenville, SC-based sustainability nonprofit. I am also a regular contributor to the Georgia Municipal Association’s “People Place Purpose” blog site and am a member of the City of Dunwoody Sustainability Commission.

Billy Parrish's Experience

Principal & Senior Downtown Advisor, BillyParrish Consulting

BillyParrish Consulting

January 2013 – Present (10 months) Dunwoody, Georgia

BillyParrish Consulting is a Catalyst for Downtown and Neighborhood Development, providing expertise in Downtown Management, Downtown and Neighborhood Commercial Center Renewal, Main Street Program Development, Applied Historic Preservation and Nonprofit Management. As the co-creator of "The New Community Initiated Development Approach" to Public/Private real estate development, BillyParrish Consulting provides training and consulting for downtown and neighborhood development projects specifically targeted to create marketplace value not only in the project itself, but in the surrounding real estate. In addition, BillyParrish consulting created the concept of "The Cityness Continuum", a way for all cities to capitalize on the growing market for affordable city life - from big city, to medium-sized metropolitan statistical area central cities, to small cities that serve as the hub for a micropolitan area, to small towns and rural villages.

Ensured a high level of quality, commitment and effectiveness in field services, conferences, product development and a network membership program.

Work History at the NMSC:

After a national search, I was hired in 1983 by the NMSC as a Program Associate, one of two initial field staff hired to begin the national program expansion. Over my time at the NMSC I provided direct fee-for-service consulting services to over 30 communities in state programs in Oregon, South Carolina, Arkansas and Virginia. I then became Manager of State Programs, overseeing the NMSC's state program consulting services, expanding the reach of the program from 15 states and a $500,000 annual budget to a 28 state initiative with a $1 million annual budget. In addition, I continued to provide technical consulting services to programs in South Carolina, Arkansas, Tennessee and Virginia. As the national program grew I was promoted to Program Manager for Field Services, managing the NMSC's highly successful Field Services Division. In this role I was responsible for 12 field staff and a services consulting budget of $1.4 million through fee-for-service contracts with statewide programs, urban area programs and independent small town programs. During this time I also continued to provide technical assistance to states and communities as a senior-level professional. In 1988 I became the National Director.
