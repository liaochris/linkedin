Adrienne Dell's Summary

A member of the Santa Clara County Bar Association, Adrienne Dell received a B.A. in History from UCLA and earned her Juris Doctor at Loyola Law School in Los Angeles. Adrienne Dell’s earliest work in the legal field included serving as a Superior Court Clerk and working for the firms of Weitzman & Re and Brodey & Price PC as a Law Clerk. After being admitted to The State Bar of California in 1985, Adrienne Dell became an attorney for Brodey & Price, and later for the Office of the Public Defender in Los Angeles County, managing full caseloads and handling a number of jury trials.

For many years, Adrienne Dell served as a sole practitioner, first in Los Angeles and later in San Jose. In 1999, she became a partner in the law firm of Vertner & Dell, representing both juveniles and adults accused of crimes, from pre-filing to post-conviction. Following that partnership, Adrienne Dell returned to working as a solo attorney, establishing a criminal defense practice and focusing on pre-conviction matters for her clients.

Adrienne Dell’s professional affiliations include California Attorneys for Criminal Justice, California Public Defenders Association, National Legal Aid & Defender Association, and Death Penalty Focus, an organization advocating for the abolishment of the death penalty. She brought her legal expertise to the world of television in 1991, when she became a technical advisor for the CBS drama The Trials of Rosie O’Neill.

Active in her community, Adrienne Dell enjoys coaching soccer and volunteering for the Marine Toys for Tots Foundation. The American National Red Cross, St. Jude Children’s Research Hospital, and the American Society for the Prevention of Cruelty to Animals (ASPCA) are also among her favorite charities. In her free time, she likes hiking, bird watching, fishing, and reading.
