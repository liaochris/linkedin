Laura Clarke's Summary

Laura has just started her own freelance filmmaking business, making films for a variety of ANU initiatives in addition to her first feature documentary "A Farmer Abroad."

Laura underwent her undergraduate degree in New Media Arts at the ANU. During this time she fulfilled several roles within her residential hall as First Year Representative, Communications Officer and then President of the Residents’ Committee. In addition, Laura interned at the National Film and Sound Archives (NFSA), where she curated a collection of fashion films for the Mediatheque at the Australian Centre for the Moving Image. During Laura’s Honours year she continued her involvement at Fenner Hall as Sub-Dean and Coordinator of the Aid and Development Learning Community. Laura graduated with First Class Honours in New Media Arts majoring in film theory and practice. Her thesis entitled Documenting the Rural Way: Filmmaking Style, Practices and Ethics in Representing Rural Australia included commentary on her own filmmaking process during the making of her Honours short documentary Chatting With Bill. Upon graduating, Laura was awarded the Tillyard Prize, the oldest and most prestigious prize available to bachelor degree students of the University (see more at http://cass.anu.edu.au/story/tillyard-prize-awarded-laura-clarke). She is Conference Coordinator for Visible Evidence 2012, an international documentary conference to be hosted at the ANU and NFSA (see more at http://www.visibleevidence2012.com/). She is also currently Research Assistant to Professor David MacDougall. As Visiting Fellow, Laura is making a short documentary film on the fiftieth year of relations between Australia and the EU and the relating events being hosted by the ANUCES (http://ces.anu.edu.au/australia-and-europe-conversation-50-years-eu-australia-relations).
Research interests include the role of film as an advocate and the ethical implications of documentary filmmaking.

Laura Clarke's Projects

  • Innovation ACT 2012

    • September 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Laura Clarke, Sajal Jain, Shweta Mariwala

    Each member in team Excelsior has a story to share from the most significant aspect of their life — University and Career. We, as graduates from a University in Canberra, share a common notion formed through our journey as students. Universities have quite strongly positioned themselves in assisting international students towards the inward transitioning process, that is, enabling students to adjust to the move into university. But what the universities fail to provide, in our opinion, is the “Outward transitioning of International Students” which implies equipping students with enough tools and connections before they graduate to enter the Australian Job force.

    Team Excelsior aims to create a web portal for International Students in Canberra to connect them with Career Mentors comprising University Alumni (within Australia and overseas) and professionals in the Canberra community. The web interaction will lead to networking sessions between mentors and students to guide the latter towards their professional career.
