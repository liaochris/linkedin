Edgar Pitts' Summary

Edgar C. Pitts III is an expert in all things web and uses his vast knowledge base to consult clients on how to streamline Internet business models and integrate automated systems. He founded DTCS on the premise that technology should be embraced and leveraged to your strategic advantage.

Edgar is well-schooled in many disciplines including management, marketing, design, programming and IT. Mr. Pitts founded his first Internet business in 1998, and over the next decade he honed his many skills establishing multiple successful companies.

In 2006, Edgar set out on an entirely new adventure by producing his first feature film, the gothic western Shroud (2009). Being involved with every aspect of the project, the next two-years were a whirlwind. He emerged a lean, mean producing machine with deep film software, hardware and technology knowledge base.

Edgar was recently married to his beautiful bride, Ima Pitts. They live happily in Irving, Texas and are expecting to begin a family soon.

Specialties

Internet marketing, database programming, film producer, post-production supervisor, research & development

September 2005 – Present (8 years 1 month)

Jetrefilm Entertainment is an independent film group dedicated to restoring excellence in film by creating highly imaginative, dramatically compelling and marketable films through sheer excellence of storytelling, entrepreneurial spirit and bold technological innovation.

Following the organizational models of such successful studios as Pixar, the filmmaking team at Jetrefilm are qualified film makers – not accountants, mid-level managers or profit-crazed executives. Conversely, we are not deeply scared or militant iconoclasts who refuse to respect the fact that filmmaking is a business, and as such must be profitable.

Our model is simple: find the best vision + write the best stories + hire the best people + give them best environment to work + let them share in the film’s success = the best movie. The fact our films will be extremely profitable is simply inevitable.
