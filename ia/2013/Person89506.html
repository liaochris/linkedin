Matt Olpinski's Projects

  • ARTI: Art Rendered Through Identity

    • September 2011 to Present
    Team Members: Matt Olpinski, Jesse Thoman, Jake Gross, Michael Higham, Andrew Kiproff, Glen Cheney

    A.R.T.I. is my senior thesis, or at least my major's equivalent. A team of 6 seniors (3 developers and 6 designers) were involved in its creation. ARTI is an interactive installation that allows visitors to create unique artwork using their body and its movements. Using camera tracking software, the system will capture the visitors biometric data that will then be used to generate specific to them. The visitors artwork will then be uploaded to an online public gallery where they can download it at a later time. Microsoft’s Kinect captures the data using the Kinect SDK and C#. It is then stored in a Redis database. My major role was to create the brand identity, the promotional video, and setting up the exhibit. The final step was to have Processing use the user’s data to create the art.
