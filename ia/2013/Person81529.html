Lisa Guernsey's Summary

I'm the director of the Early Education Initiative at the New America Foundation, a non-partisan think tank and incubator for explanatory and investigative journalism on pressing policy issues. I'm a regular contributor to the Early Ed Watch blog. Prior to joining New America, I had been writing about education for nearly 15 years as a staff writer at the Chronicle of Higher Education until 1999 and the New York Times until 2002. I've written articles on the intersections of social science, education and technology for many national publications over the past several years, including Newsweek, Time.com, the Los Angeles Times, the Washington Post, the American Prospect, Ladies Home Journal, Consumer Reports, and more.

My most recent book is Screen Time: How Electronic Media -- from Baby Videos to Educational Software -- Affects Your Young Child (Basic Books). It will be out in paperback in March 2012. It is updated version of the hardcover book, Into the Minds of Babes: How Screen Time Affects Children From Birth to Age 5, published in 2007.

I've also contributed a chapter to Mobile Technology for Children: Designing for Interaction and Learning (Morgan Kaufman, 2009) titled "It's Mine: Kids Carrying their Culture Wherever They Go."

From 1996-2009, I wrote and updated College.Edu: A Guide for the Cyber-Savvy Student (Octameron Associates), which is a guide for high school students and parents seeking college information online.
