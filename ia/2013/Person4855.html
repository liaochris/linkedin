Allen Cochran's Summary

ABOUT

I'm designing the future. As ridiculous as that sounds, I'm working each and every day to revamp the way that education is delivered to the masses with careful consideration on how educational systems are adapted for users seeking a customized approach to learning. I work for McGraw-Hill Education in Columbus, Ohio as a user researcher, service designer and user experience analyst.

In 2013, I graduated from Ohio State University's Department of Design with a Master's degree in design research, service design, and interaction design. My research focus was centered around the services and interaction that graduate student organizations have with their constituents. My research path lead me in many directions including being the President of the graduate student body and Executive Officer of the Council of Graduate Students.

I am a Masters student pursuing my degree in Design Development. Though my interests vary from interaction design, visual communication and technology, I am primarily researching topics pertaining to design management, team building, and ideation as it relates to design.

For more information, visit my personal website at www.allenjcochran.com. Information regarding my OSU Master's thesis is also available online at www.allenjcochran.com/thesis/thesis.html.


GOALS

Collaboration is my constant goal. Together, teams make a difference. I strive to lead and bring my own creative interests to each group and professional role that I am apart of. I endeavor to always remain critical of my own methods and adapt to new projects based on those assessments.

Specialties: Professional Skills: Project management, event production, graphic design, video editing, artistic advisor pertaining to painting and sculpture processes.

Technical Skills: Adobe Creative Suite CS4, Final Cut Pro 7, Microsoft Office Suite 2007, iWork 2009, iLife 2008, Microsoft Access 2003, FileMaker Pro 10 and both Windows XP and Macintosh OS X Snow Leopard operating systems.
