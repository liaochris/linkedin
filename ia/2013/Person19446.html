Cameron Dearlove's Summary

Cameron is pleased to be bringing his community development focus to The Family Centre. With lots of enthusiasm and a short attention span, Cameron has worked and studied many places. He’s studied at Wilfrid Laurier University, Aarhus University in Denmark, and Nipissing University, has been a teacher here in Kitchener and in South Korea, has been an Employment Counsellor, Job Developer, and Workplace Language Coach for Newcomers to Canada through The Working Centre in downtown Kitchener. He was also the lead developer of The Waterloo Region Internationally Educated Teachers Pilot Project, a 2010-2011 project which seeks partnerships with local School Boards to improve access opportunities for Internationally Educated Teachers.

Cameron is also active in our community:
• as a member of Kitchener’s Downtown Advisory Committee;
• as a board member of Opportunities Waterloo Region, while also representing OWR on the Equity and Inclusion Advisory Committee of the Waterloo Region District School Board;
• representing Family and Children’s Services and The Family Centre on the Early Literacy Alliance of Waterloo Region;
• as a member of the Waterloo-Wellington Local Health Integration Network's Community Council
• as a member of the Leadership Waterloo Region class of 2013-14
• as a contributor to the Waterloo Region Record and the Kitchener Post;
• as a volunteer with Dreamcatcher Mentoring where he mentors northern youth;
• as a past candidate for public office and as a politically engaged individual with a focus on poverty and prevention.

Opportunity, inclusion, engagement, community, empathy, and earnestness are some of his favorite words. One of Cameron’s strengths is connecting people and groups, one of his biggest fears is public speaking, and one of his many weaknesses is talking about himself – which makes writing a simple bio a formidable task.
