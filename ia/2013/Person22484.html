craig lefebvre's Summary

I am an architect and designer of public health and social change programs.

Have led efforts to create and refine social marketing strategy and organizational technical capacity in the private sector and with an international NGO.

Have worked as senior strategic counsel with all levels of government (US and international), nonprofit organizations and corporations to apply marketing principles to improve public health and social conditions.

My change framework blends research and consumer experience to engage people’s imagination and passions in the design, implementation and evaluation of social change programs.

Some current projects include applying design thinking to organizational change and public health programs; mobile and social technologies for public health and community development; development of community-based social marketing models for policy implementation to prevent obesity; social media to combat health disparities; strategic planning and marketing around health IT and meaningful use; and development of a global social network site for social marketing practitioners and organizations involved in environmental, public health and social change projects.

Specialties

Audience Empathy & Insight; Design Thinking; Social and Mobile Technologies for Health; Partnership Development; Program Design and Evaluation; Public Health Branding; Social Marketing Planning; Social Media; Sustainability.

craig lefebvre's Publications

  • Social marketing and social change: Strategies and tools for improving health, well-being and the environment.

    • San Francisco: Jossey-Bass
    • March 4, 2013
    Authors: craig lefebvre

    How can we facilitate more effective, efficient, equitable and sustainable solutions to the problems that confound our communities and world? The author weaves together multi-level theories of change, research and case studies to explain and illustrate the development of social marketing to address some of society’s most vexing problems. The result is a people-centered approach that relies on insight and empathy as much as on data for the inspiration, design and management of programs that strive for changes for good. This text is ideal for students and professionals in health, nonprofit, business, social services, and other areas.

  • Social marketing (a six volume series)

    • London: Sage Publications
    • March 1, 2013
    Authors: craig lefebvre

    The theory and practice of social marketing has steadily been gaining significance following its conception over four decades ago, and has since been adopted by an ever expanding group of practitioners, academics, researchers and policymakers in governments around the world. A key feature underlined in this work is how social marketing stands apart from other approaches to health promotion and disease prevention, environmental sustainability, safety and injury prevention and other topics, by searching for population level impacts that can be achieved by:

    " Using marketing techniques such as audience segmentation

    " Implementing product and service development

    " Realigning incentives and removing barriers to behaviour change

    " Increasing opportunities to practice healthier and more socially beneficial behaviours

    " Creating communication strategies to promote, encourage and support positive behaviour changes that ultimately benefit society as a whole.

    Bringing together seminal texts from diverse sources, this six-volume set - framed by a newly written introductory chapter - seeks to organize the field of social marketing, highlight its global scope and contributions, and present its current growth and dynamism.

  • Transformative Social Marketing: Co-creating the Social Marketing Discipline and Brand

    • Journal of Social Marketing
    • 2012
    Authors: craig lefebvre

    The paper reviews new insights and understandings from modern social marketing practice, social innovation, design thinking and service design, social media, transformative consumer research, marketing theory and advertising practice and develops a model for transforming social marketing thought, research and practice. A three dimensional model is presented that includes: scope – co-creation, conversations, communities and markets; design – honoring people, radiating value, engaging service and enhancing experiences; value space – dignity, hope, love and trust.

  • An Integrative Model for Social Marketing

    • Journal of Social Marketing
    • 2011
    Authors: craig lefebvre

    Social marketing has evolved differently in the developing and developed worlds, at times
    leading to different emphases on what social marketing thought and practice entail. This paper aims to document what those differences have been and provide an integrative framework to guide social marketers in working with significant social and health issues. Five challenges to social marketing are identified – achieving equity, influence of social networks on behaviors, critical marketing, sustainability, scalability and the need for comprehensive programs (a Total Market Approach) – that may serve to focus and coalesce social marketing research and practice around the world
