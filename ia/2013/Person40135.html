Eileen Lenson's Summary

I have an expansive combination of education, clinical training, and experience in the field of psychology. I have a Masters degree in Clinical Social Work. Prior to being a Life and Business Coach, I was a practicing psychotherapist for over 20 years, working with individuals, couples, families and groups. I've authored the book, Succeeding in Private Practice: A Business Guide for Psychotherapists, written a chapter in the book, Grief and Bereavement in Contemporary Society, and write the column, 'Life According To Lenson', for Beverly Hills Canyon News and San Francisco News papers.

My experiences include past appearances as a frequent radio guest. In 2013 I was a guest on KABC. Past invited speaker at national and international conferences, and teaching ethics at George Washington University Medical School.

I developed programs for at-risk teenagers, for birth mothers relinquishing their newborns, and for new residents adjusting to life changes in a senior citizen assisted living facility. I developed therapy groups for parents who suffered the loss of a child, divorcing couples, and teens with social adjustment problems, and now lead a women's support group.

I assist businesses with motivation, direction and focus, time and stress management, and conflict resolution.

My professional training from graduate school, sitting on Boards, community and hospital ethics committees, forensic expert testimony, two decades of work in medical and psychiatric hospitals, and an active private practice in three states and Wash., D.C., provides me with the skills and experience necessary to be able to coach people and businesses desiring personal growth and development.

As a Life and Business Coach, I work with each client to clearly identify and define their needs and goals, and then develop a plan of action with them. Through my structured yet supportive sessions, assignments, readings, and interactive exercises, I help clients determine and reach their goals.
