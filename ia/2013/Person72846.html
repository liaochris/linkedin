Kathleen March's Summary

Attorney Kathleen March earned her law degree from Yale Law School in 1974, where she served as the editor of the prestigious Yale Law Journal. After serving as a Federal Prosecutor at the U.S. Attorney's Office for the Central District of California from 1978-1982, Attorney March was sworn in as a United States Bankruptcy Judge for the Central District of California in 1988, where she served for the next 14 years. In 2002, Kathleen March marked her return to private practice with the creation of The Bankruptcy Law Firm, P.C., located in Los Angeles, California.

The Bankruptcy Law Firm, P.C. represents individual and small business debtors in Chapter 7 and Chapter 13 bankruptcy cases, in all counties that are within the United States Bankruptcy Court for the Central District of California (Los Angeles County, Orange County, Ventura County, San Bernardino County, Riverside County, Santa Barbara County, south San Luis Obispo County).

Attorney March is admitted to practice law in both California and New York. She has published numerous articles on Bankruptcy Law topics, and is the lead author of both the Rutter Group's 4 volume California Bankruptcy Guide (first published in 2007 and updated annually) and the Rutter Group's 4 volume Bankruptcy Practice Guide, published in 2008. Additionally, Attorney March has taught bankruptcy law subjects to lawyers, as well as other judges, in CLE programs presented by bar associations, judges' associations, and the Rutter Group.

Kathleen March is a member of numerous professional organizations, including the Los Angeles Bankruptcy Forum, the National Association of Consumer Bankruptcy Attorneys, the Women Lawyers Association of Los Angeles, and the Federal Bar Association (Los Angeles chapter).

Specialties

Representing Debtor Rights and Creditor Rights in the Los Angeles Bankruptcy system.
