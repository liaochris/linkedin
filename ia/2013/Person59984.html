Jayson Minard's Projects

  • Summon web-scale discovery service

    • January 2008 to Present
    Team Members: Jayson Minard, John Law, Andrea Michalek, Mike Buschman

    Key technologies and concepts: Solr, Lucene, ElasticSearch, Cassandra, MongoDB, MySQL, Java (and most class libraries under the sun), Ruby, Groovy, XQuery, Gradle (and formerly Maven). Linguistics, Big Data, Search, Distributed Processing, Grid Computing.

    Architect and development team leader for this Billion+ record document ingestion and search system. From the first proof of concept, through rapid time to market for v1, and onwards to mature versions: Consistently created break throughs for the team in areas of scalability and problem solving difficult features such as full rights managed per-user to per-document relationships without any query-time performance penalty, de-duplication and merging of related documents (total corpus daily), high ingestion indexing rates (total corpus daily), parent-child documents, compound documents, spotlighting and grouping, custom full-text and blob storage with minimal cost cleaning, distributed processing, and other technical challenges.

    Other technologies researched or used, but not retained during the project: Hadoop, ActiveMQ, Voldemort, Postgresql

  • AbeBooks Re-Architecture / Re-Build

    • June 2003 to April 2007
    Team Members: Jayson Minard

    Key Technologies: Java, Endeca, Oracle RAC, SonicMQ, JavaScript, CSS+HTML

    During my tenure, I moved the company from a problematic position of failing to deliver services reliability at 30 million inventory items to an open-ended scalability model passing 6 million searches a day over 100 million inventory items that churn daily at a rapid pace. I provided similar improvements across the board in other services such as order processing, financial systems and general batch and online systems. Rebuilt the technical teams to be agile, nimble and effective as the company moved into a more professional and higher growth mode by improving the team skill levels and recruiting new senior talent.

  • MyBoeingFleet Search

    • May 2007 to December 2008
    Team Members: Jayson Minard

    Key technologies: Endeca, Java, C# / .NET, Oracle.

    Originally brought in by vendor (Endeca) to solve a difficult problem of rights management of users connected to documents through a very complex and constantly changing relationship of a user-plane-part-documents which exceeded the capabilities of the search engine at the time.

    I later continued in the project to continue solving issues with integrating 50 disparate content providers for search within Boeing who also had differing needs for the same search data. Created a meta-layer for Endeca to unify the content while still allowing differences at the provider level without resorting to federated search. Also created a desktop development tool for managing the schema development of the loosely related group of indexes.

    In addition, guided the Boeing development team on Java architecture, the introduction of open-source projects into Boeing, and the introduction of Agile development into the company.

  • JBuilder (project JAyVA, metro, raven, latte, ...)

    Team Members: Jayson Minard

    Also worked with SUN to debug and fix crashes, deadlocks and other mystery bugs in the Java VM and class libraries from alpha through 1.x releases.
