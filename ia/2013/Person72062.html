Karen E. Pellegrin's Summary

Currently, I am owner and designer of ChromaKi. I execute brand design solutions for clients, from start to finish, managing their brand from the visual message of color, logo and business identity to how that brand is managed on the web.

ChromaKi is a Colorado based design studio offering socially conscious, brand savvy solutions for web, print, social media, e-marketing and more.

My ultimate goal is to provide a highly personalized experience that speaks the language and brand of my clients, while solving all of their branding needs. I collaborate with my clients at every level of the process to create the best brand elements for them and their business. I believe that success is at least as important to me, as it it to them.

I also give a definite nod to how much I love this planet (and it's inhabitants) by using green products, utilizing universal design techniques (like scalable type, responsive web design and color friendly graphics for the vision impaired), and sharing my time & talents with the community. Bringing social responsibility to my business makes me feel great and my clients love being a part of that spirit.

Specialties

Graphics for web, social media, brand development, print marketing, e-marketing and custom stationary. Microsoft technologies including Visual Studio, Expression, Office, Skydrive, Windows 7 mobile and more. Also, Adobe Creative Suite, Wordpress customization and 3D environment modeling.

Karen E. Pellegrin's Projects

  • DenverGov.org Re-design

    • March 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Karen E. Pellegrin, Chani Goering, James Lindauer, Juliana Munoz, Marsha Evans, Ram Krishnan

    As part of the City and County of Denver's Technology Services department, our team completed a re-design the existing denvergov.org website to fully integrate contemporary cross-channel communications, html5/CSS3 design and a completely updated, user friendly (section 508 compliant) interface.

    My part in this project included the design of a large collection of icons, badges, agency/department banners, landing pages and social media integration. In addition, I completely developed and designed the new 'City and County of Denver' Facebook page. You can see the new Facebook page at: https://www.facebook.com/CityandCountyofDenver.
    Future UI/UX projects with the city will be released throughout the rest of the year and, with the mayor's new "e-denver" initiative (recently announced in Mayor Hancock's 2012 State of the City Address), constant improvements to the city's technology and digital communications will continue to be nurtured and developed in the coming years. I'm looking forward to being a part of this new growth in the city's technology and sharing our new releases with the public.

  • DenverGov.org Re-design

    • March 2012 to July 2012
    Team Members: Karen E. Pellegrin, Chani Goering, Rich Beaudoin, James Lindauer, Joe Harker, J.D. Ready, Susan Ingle, Juliana Munoz, Dawn Miguel, Ram Krishnan, Marsha Evans

    Denvergov.org went through a complete redesign to update the look, feel and function of the entire site. This was done with the City and County of Denver's Technology Services Custom Application and Web teams and it is the initial project that is helping to drive the website into the 21st century with user friendly interface and HTML5/CSS3 W3C standards, as well as the most up-to-date 508c compliance.

  • City and County of Denver Facebook page

    • May 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Karen E. Pellegrin, Chani Goering

    This project was essentially a reboot of the city's inactive Facebook page. A new, completely customized page with custom agency tabs and a variety of cross-channel communication integration was implemented. it includes embedded YouTube and Flickr galleries, Twitter and RSS feeds and rich html content. Additionally, custom banners were created for each agency tab that link to the agency's web portal on Denvergov.org.
