Jen Wang's Summary

Though my longstanding love of books, art, and graphic design seems the furthest thing from digital technologies design, my curiosity about how the two potentially intersect has led me to pursue my masters at the School of Information, UC Berkeley.

At the I School I learned the process of answering complex problems, creating stories through design thinking, and transforming data and research into insights with which to implement user-friendly solutions. I developed concrete skills in rapid prototyping, user research, user interface design.

My group final project at the I School, called Mapestry, involved creating a digital suite of tools for collecting, organizing and sharing oral histories. My role in the project primarily involved user experience, user interface and visual design, while also participating in user testing and research. Our group went on to receive the school's James R. Chen Award for outstanding master's final project.

I have worked with Python, HTML and CSS, with some exposure to JavaScript. I have strong skills in the Adobe Creative Suite (Illustrator, InDesign, Photoshop, Fireworks) and have used Balsamiq, and Keynote for prototyping applications.
