Frank Lamb's Summary

Machinery and Factory Automation Business Consultant specializing in systemization, machine building and controls. Background includes over 300 automation design and build projects. In addition to specializing in the factory automation business I am experienced in Product Development, Project Management and Lean Manufacturing/Six-Sigma.

My book, Industrial Automation - Hands On was published in June of 2013 by McGraw-Hill Professional. Books on panel building and business systemization are in process. My blogs at www.automationprimer.com and www.automationllc.com are also educational resources.

I am currently involved in a large commercial software development project that will be presented to venture capital this year or early next year. This will require a top quality management team. I am looking for people who are interested in being part of this venture, please contact me directly for details.

The same tools that I have used in my successful engineering practice can be applied in the business world. Above all I am a problem solver. My added training in Six Sigma/Lean Manufacturing as well as experience in the management of large capital projects provides a unique perspective as a solution provider.

Frank Lamb's Publications

  • Industrial Automation - Hands On

    • McGraw-Hill
    • 2012
    Authors: Frank Lamb

    This book is meant to serve as an outline for automation and industrial machinery concepts and terminology. It is suitable as a guidebook for newcomers to the field of automation as well as a reference for the seasoned automation professional. This book emphasizes control systems, but many other subjects – including machine building, mechanical engineering and devices, manufacturing business systems and job functions in an industrial environment – are also covered extensively.

    Mechanical engineers who want to know more about controls and business, electrical engineers and technicians who need more information on mechanical concepts and components, and factory management employees needing more background on technical subjects should find this book helpful. Machine operators hoping to move into the maintenance field and maintenance technicians needing more information on engineering techniques will also find subjects of interest.

  • Mastering The Machine

    • Automation Consulting, LLC
    • April 2013
    Authors: Frank Lamb

    A complete tutorial on the processes of procuring, designing and fabricating industrial machinery with an emphasis on documentation.
