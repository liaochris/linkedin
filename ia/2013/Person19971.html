Carl E. Moore's Summary

++
I was born in Canton Mississippi and currently live and work in Memphis, TN as a artist and designer. I consider my work to be a form of visual communication using simplicity and depth to express current, social and economic conditions that effect our lives on a daily basis. By using common themes and media based content, a connection is developed between the personal and the public by using color and composition to express mood, situation and setting. This style is also presented in a series of drawings that relay a more surreal set of ideas and concerns designed to evoke discussions about the environment and social classes. This work explores topics such as living space, environmental adaptation and alternative communication.

My process of development combines complex content with simple forms and figures creating a narrative between the art and the viewer. I don’t think art should be directive, nor an attempt to answer questions or solve the problems of the world, but left up to the viewer to form their own opinion on how to accept or interpret the work.

I’m constantly exploring different methods of production while maintaining a core style that reflects a consistent body of work. My art is influenced by artist: Jacob Lawrence, Stuart Davis, Salvador Dali, Charles White, Michelangelo and John Biggers

Carl E. Moore's Experience

WREG-TV Senior Graphic Designer

News Channel 3

January 1997 – Present (16 years 8 months)

Design and develop daily on-air graphics for broadcast news shows, special in-house programming, in-house produced shows, and various print, web and station signage. Project development involved designing Special supers, series logos, special backgrounds, animated opens, set designs, presentation graphics, commercial graphics and maps. Special publications are produced for talent recognition, newspaper ads, weather almanacs and calendars.

Modern Contemporary Artist & Painter

Memphis & Mid-South Area

1983 – Present (30 years)

Artist Statement: I use art as a form of visual communication, I try to maintain simplicity and depth in my work. I don’t think art should be complicated; my work is based on modern contemporary, social themes to reflect the world around us. Thick black lines and bold colors are used to convey emotional strength and balance in a surreal environment.
