Christopher Grant's Summary

I've led user experience teams in the US and Europe for over a decade. Today, I head Tuenti's design team in Barcelona, where we build commercial and entertainment products.

Previously, I worked with Credit Suisse, Emagister and innovative startups. While SVP for User Experience at Sclipo, this education site was named to Red Herring's list of the "World's Top 100 Most Innovative Companies".

My true passion is user psychology, especially how it affects online behavior. I first came up with the concept of "digital altruism" while organizing an online community of 2,000 expert users.

  • e³ - Learn what you need to learn

    • January 2011 to November 2011
    Team Members: Christopher Grant, Alberto Almansa Monguilot, Joaquim Falgueras, Tomy Pelluz, Ricardo Valiente, Lasse Johnsen

    I designed Emagister's new flagship learning product, at the time code-named e³. Together with a top human resources expert, we developed a complete skills questionnaire, a 360-evaluation system and an innovative profile that highlights what users have learned, rather than what they have done. This project taught me valuable lessons on how to manage stakeholder expectations, on how to stay lean and avoid endless iterations and how people acquire and master skills. The product went live in late 2011.
