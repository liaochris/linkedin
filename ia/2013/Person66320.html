John Evans CSP's Summary

John Evans is an International Strategist, Speaker, Trainer and Thought Leader working with organizations who want to become more successful by improving their businesses practices and processes while developing their people to do more and better.


His individualized customized presentations and programs range from groups of hundreds to as small as ten.


He is the author of “EMBRACE THE TIGER”, Relentless Pursuit of Priorities. This book will ensure you become more competitive overnight by completing the steps indelibly impressed in the mindset you will develop implementing the plan.


President of a Full Service Training, Consulting, and Professional Speaking Firm. John worked with over 900 US Corporations and International Corporations, Department of Defense, the Intelligence Community.


Adjunct Professor for the Army Management Staff College is highest rated leadership program, Personnel Management for Executives (PME) delivering over 220 half-day presentations on leadership, from seven Regions, all over the world under Government contracts since 1984.

He worked with Dale Carnegie Course Sponsors for Five years as an Area Manager and Certified Instructor for their six courses. John Evans CSP's Publications

  • Embrace the Tiger

    • USA
    • 2001
    Authors: John Evans CSP

    I never liked Cats. When I completed the manuscript of a book I had written the title Embrace the Tiger, How to be Certain in Uncertain Times first edition. I was conducting a Leadership program for a Major Command several hundred participants at Redstone Arsenal in Huntsville Alabama. The Director read my manuscript he said that he knew the owners of a Zoo right outside of Huntsville the Limestone Zoo.

    A woman named Ann Case founded the Zoo and her husband who worked for NASA. They had a passion for Big Cats and started the Zoo for Orphaned retired entertainers abused and abandoned large animals. With the Directors introduction, I met the owners and asked if there was any way I could get a picture with one of their three Tigers. They were actually working to mate the male with either of the females. When Introduced to the Tigers to see how they responded to me two did not like me at all but the Beautiful Raya was docile towards me. After several visits, quietly talking to her it was time.

    Two Trainers were working together to get her in the right frame of mind for me to get behind her and stroke this magnificent beautiful Tiger. When I got in position with me behind her and above her it made her look somewhat smaller even though she weighed over 400 pounds to my 225. That was truly an optical illusion.
