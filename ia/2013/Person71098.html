Justin Hall's Summary

I first used the internet in 1988. Since then I've been inspired to help connect people through technology: web pages, video games, mobile apps and beyond.

I am now on sabbatical starting 1 July 2013 - traveling, and experimenting with video and electronics in San Francisco.

In 2013 I worked for mobile gaming titan DeNA as a Recruiter in their San Francisco office. In March 2010 I joined ngmoco:) to produce freemium social mobile games. My first title for the company was Touch Pets Cats, a 2010 pet simulator for the iPhone. In late 2010 ngmoco:) was purchased by Japanese mobile entertainment network pioneer DeNA. In 2011 and 2012, I worked for ngmoco:) / DeNA as Director, Culture and Communications - ensuring that employees felt connected across the enterprise.

In 2007, I co-founded and served as CEO of GameLayers, an online social games company. We raised $2.1 million from venture capital firms O’Reilly AlphaTech Ventures and Shasta Ventures to make PMOG/The Nethernet - a game played by surfing the web. I led the growth of the company from three to nine people. We published three innovative social games on Facebook and in Firefox before closing in 2009.

Between 2001 and 2003, I lived in Japan, reporting on mobile technology and video games for various print and online publications. In 2007, I earned an MFA in "Interactive Media" at the University of Southern California's School of Cinematic Arts. My masters thesis on "Passively Multiplayer Online Gaming" evolved into GameLayers, Inc. In 1998 I received a bachelor's degree from Swarthmore College in a special major "Meaning Context and Media."

In 1994, I launched a personal web site "Justin's Links from the Underground" which came to host thousands of pages about my life. In 2004, Jeffrey Rosen writing for the New York Times Magazine said I may be "the founding father of personal bloggers." Born and raised in Chicago, Illinois, now living in San Francisco, California.
