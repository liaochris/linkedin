Chris Thomson's Experience

Software Developer

Desire2Learn Incorporated

Privately Held; 501-1000 employees; E-Learning industry

May 2012 – August 2012 (4 months) Kitchener, Ontario

I developed server-side systems to support Desire2Learn's mobile applications during my first co-op work term. I also had the opportunity to work on developing an Android application that interacted with the company's popular learning environment product.

The vast majority of my time was spent perfecting a platform-agnostic push notifications server-side framework, to allow for other systems within the company to send arbitrary push notifications to users' devices, regardless of which mobile platform those users use. I developed, tested, load-tested, and continuously refined the push notification system until we felt it was performant enough to be ready for primetime.

The majority of my time at Desire2Learn was spent developing and testing with Node.js and utilizing various Amazon Web Services such as S3, EC2, ElastiCache, Route 53, and DynamoDB.

Web Application Developer and Maintainer

Fusion Ads

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Marketing and Advertising industry

December 2008 – December 2011 (3 years 1 month)

Over my time at Fusion, I continually improved the ad system to meet their needs. Fusion's a small company, and I was in charge of all technical aspects of it.

At first, that meant deploying an open-source ad system called OpenX. OpenX had many features that Fusion didn't need or didn't work the way they wanted, so I removed unnecessary features and built custom features independently around the ad database. A statistics system was implemented independent of OpenX. I also implemented several APIs (XML and JSON, generated through PHP) that allowed desktop and mobile applications to serve Fusion advertisements.

In the summer of 2011, I decided it was finally time for a change. I built an entirely new ad management and delivery system to meet Fusion's specific needs. I used Ruby on Rails for the ad management side (for rapid feature changes and additions). For ad delivery, Node.js and Redis (acting as a temporary datastore, interacting with a persistent MySQL datastore) were used because they're very performant. As a bonus, I find all of these technologies to be more interesting to work with compared to some others.

(Part-time.)
