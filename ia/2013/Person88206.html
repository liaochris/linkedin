Mary Shafer's Summary

My career in the book publishing industry began in 1990, and at this point I've worked in every facet except distribution. Freelance editorial and commercial copywriting, marketing and public relations have also occupied me for 30 years. I've written three published books and contributed to two published anthologies, while sometimes teaching marketing, copywriting, commercial art and illustration in a formal college setting. I continue to teach writing, drawing and how to navigate the modern publishing industry.

My career as a marketing consultant and commercial copywriter has tended over the years toward working with small and micro-businesses. Part of this work includes Word Forge Books, our publishing division. Technological innovations have created undeniable synergies between the marketing and publishing industries, and there are more to come. My marketing clients have often expressed interest in writing their own books or other publications, so I offer services such as ghostwriting, editing and custom publishing to help get their messages out in a format that complements their other channels.

My publishing colleagues often need help in marketing, so I consult with them from the sympathetic standpoint of another publisher and author who understands the unique pressures they face in this industry, currently undergoing incredible upheaval. Consulting with authors and indie publishers is the fastest-growing segment of my business, which often involves public speaking at conferences, in classrooms and libraries.

Specialties:
• Consulting – marketing: branding, positioning, corporate identity
• Messaging -- concept development, campaign messaging & content development
• Speaking & Teaching -- Speeches, presentations, seminars & workshops
• Custom publishing -- Turnkey project management for print, audio & eBooks

Mary Shafer's Publications

    • Word Forge Books
    • August 18, 2010
    Authors: Mary Shafer

    The only comprehensive documentary of this historic weather disaster in the Delaware Valley between New York, New Jersey and Pennsylvania, which killed nearly 100 people and changed life in the region forever.

  • Almost Perfect: Disabled Pets and the People Who Love Them

    • Enspirio House, an imprint of Word Forge Books
    • August 2008
    Authors: Mary Shafer, Sue Bertrand, Vicki Tiernan, Roberta Jacobson

    Almost Perfect contains essays by eleven contributors from three countries. Some veteran writers join with several new voices to bring you inspiring, uplifting and thought-provoking stories of domestic pets unlike any you’ve ever met. Allow yourself to believe once again in the power of the positive as you follow the many ways in which helping and loving “less-than-perfect” critters helps their human counterparts appreciate what it means to be fully alive.

  • Rural America: A Pictorial Folk Memory

    • Willow Creek Press
    • 1995
    Authors: Mary Shafer

    An affectionate look back at agrarian America before the industrial revolution, profusely illustrated with more than 100 historic photos from the Library of Congress, many never before published.

  • Wisconsin: The Way We Were, 1845-1945

    • Heartland Press, an imprint of NorthWord Press
    • 1993
    Authors: Mary Shafer

    A light popular history of the first century of statehood for the Badger State, covering the period starting three years before statehood through the Second Word War in the heart of America's Midwest.
