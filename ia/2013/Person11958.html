Barry R Bowen's Summary

I’m an AutoCAD developer and AutoLISP programmer who makes using AutoCAD easy for Architectural, Structural, Piping, Mechanical, HVAC and Contractors who use AutoCAD.

After using AutoCAD as a drafter in 1983, I sought out solutions to automate the daily repetitive tasks that were bogging down my productivity. I learned the AutoLISP programming language and started SoftDraft, LLC to deliver my low cost, high impact software solutions to other AutoCAD users.

I worked for over 14 years as a production drafting professional – doing the work by hand – and across several different disciplines. It gave me the firsthand experience in creating contract documents and the drafting process. I’ve combined this experience with CAD programming knowledge in order to develop applications that are quick and easy to use use without upfront training – after all, CAD was supposed to make us more productive!

I began developing programs to automate the tasks that were used on a daily basis, and it was only then that I realized what AutoCAD could do for the drafting and designing process.

My AutoCAd software solutions include:
• Joist2K
• SteelPLUS Steel Detailing
• EZ-Pipe PLUS
• EZ-Duct
• And more!

At SoftDraft, we develop low cost Architectural, Engineering and Construction software applications for 2D and 3D, which work inside all versions of AutoCAD. The applications have an intuitive graphic user dialog interface – which shortens the learning curve and doesn’t require any expensive training.

Barry R Bowen's Experience

Owner

SoftDraft LLC

April 1992 – Present (21 years 9 months)

Software developer specializing in AutoCAD / AutoLISP / Visual LISP applications for the AEC industry. Applications for Structural Steel include SteelPLUS Structural Steel Detailing Bundle, Bolts/Nuts/Washers, Steel Floor & Roof Deck, Joist & Girder Series, and Welding Symbols. Other applications include EZ-Pipe PLUS for the process piping industry and EZ-Duct HVAC program. These applications are used by a large number of Architects, Structural Engineers, Steel detailers and Constructions companies.

Contributing Editor

CADalyst Magazine

1988 – 2003 (15 years)

Barry started writing “The AutoLISP Tutorial” for CADalyst magazine in 1988 and continued writing different AutoLISP programming articles for the next 15 years. He began using AutoCAD software with Release 1.2 in 1983 and has been an AutoCAD / AutoLISP instructor in the continuing education programs at Memphis State University and State Technical Institute in Memphis. He also wrote several articles on AutoLISP programming for Cadence and Technical Design Solutions Magazines.

VP of Technology and Partner

OnLine, Inc.

November 1991 – April 1992 (6 months)

A company specializing in Facility Management (an outgrowth of ANFW). Responsible for developing FM / GIS applications with interface to AutoCAD using AutoLISP / Visual LISP programming. Designed and developed automated parts book information systems for maintenance planners. Links between AutoCAD drawings, scanned parts views, database info, main frames and video images. Developed AutoLISP applications software to input as-built architectural floor plans for hospitals.

CAD Systems Manager

Askew, Nixon, Ferguson & Wolf, Inc.

January 1986 – November 1991 (5 years 11 months)

Systems Manager for 25 AutoCAD stations and 15 support systems on Novell network, responsible for research and development, customizing, AutoLISP programming, trouble-shooting, repair, cad standards, hardware/software installations and employee training programs. Analyzed every aspect of CAD operations to implement more efficient and productive techniques. Increased CAD production by 30% to 40% with custom AutoLISP add-in applications. Increased plotting efficiency with overnight spooling techniques. Developed CAD system standards manual for Memphis Shelby County Airport Authority, Federal Express, St. Jude, The MED Hospital, Baptist Hospital and others.

Researched, designed, and developed interface to MSCAA McDonald Douglas main frame-Land Acquisition Database, for automatic updating of AutoCAD Land Acquisition maps and monthly FAA Grant Maps. Designed and redeveloped an AutoLISP application, “Airport Airspace Analysis Program,” to generate a complete 3D model in AutoCAD, using AME, for Part 77 surfaces for airports of all sizes. Conducted seminars, workshops and training programs on AutoCAD, Auto Architect, Archibus and others.
