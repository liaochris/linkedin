Dusan Krstic's Summary

DUSAN KRSTIC

Born in 1971 in Belgrade. Finished Medical College in Belgrade. In 1990 joined 63rd Airborne Brigade, the JNA Special Operations unit. During his service finished Basic and Advanced Airborne Course, and number of courses for Paramedics and Nuclear/Biological/Chemical Protection; finished Counter-Terrorism, Counter-Insurgency, Intelligence and Counter-Intelligence courses as well.
Due to multiple nature of the prolonged and still brewing conflict in former Yugoslavia, gathered an extensive scope and experience in counter-insurgency, counter-terror operations with knowledge of field problems of interaction between the Middle-Eastern terror hotbed and its Balkan incarnations.
During the mandatory service and in extensive active reserve service was engaged in training of Special Forces, with educational accent on intelligence and field intelligence.
Educational and training capabilities were broadened with undergraduate studies of Psychology and Law at the University of Belgrade, and graduate studies at the International School for Holocaust Studies at Yad Vashem in Jerusalem (2004) and Israeli International Security Academy (2009). as SSO - Ship Security Officer. Made Aliyah in 2008. with residence in Jerusalem.

Specialties

During that time cooperated with several international missions and peacekeeping forces including KFOR and UNIFIL.Two years CEO of «Electronic Department», a company with a certificate allowing the trade with controlled goods (military arms and equipment). Dusan Krstic's Projects

  • MVNO - Mobile Virtual Network Operator

    • January 2010 to Present
    Team Members: Dusan Krstic, FEUERSTEIN DANNY, Yaniv Vaknin, Nir Levi

    A mobile virtual network operator (MVNO) is a mobile phone operator that provides services directly to their own customers but does not own key network assets such as a licensed frequency allocation of radio spectrum and the cell tower infrastructure.[1] Instead these assets are leased from a mobile network operator in the region where the MVNO operates.

    MVNOs are roughly equivalent to the "switchless resellers" of the traditional landline telephone market. An MVNO works independently of the host MNO and sets its own retail pricing structures to its customers. However, the MVNO must also pay a wholesale fee to the host in proportion to the usage by their customers.

    Although the MVNO does not generally own core network infrastructure, they sometimes do have a home location register, or HLR. This allows more flexibility and ownership of the subscriber's mobile phone number (MSISDN). Some MVNOs run their own billing and customer care solutions known as business support systems (BSS). Many use a Mobile Virtual Network Enabler.
