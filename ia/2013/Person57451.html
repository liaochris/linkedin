James Gannon's Summary

James Gannon is an analyst of current affairs and politics. He formerly worked as a producer and writer at NBC news, where he produced four one-hour documentaries. Two of these documentaries won Champion Media Awards for excellence in economic reporting.

James Gannon's Projects

  • The Reckless Presidency of George W. Bush

    • June 2010 to January 2012
    Team Members: James Gannon, Worked alone.

    Countless books have been written about Bush's presidential career, but few avoid blind, emotion-fueled criticism of the man. The Reckless Presidency of George W. Bush offers a fresh critique--not of the president, but of his policies. The book highlights key failings of Bush's administration such as the deceptive campaign to justify war on Iraq, the barbaric torture of detainees, the overt upper-class economic favoritism,the censorship of climate scientists, and the ideological embrace of deregulation that led the nation to deep recession, presenting each with the clear, articulate language of a journalist and scholar.

  • Obama's War

    • 2009 to 2010
    Team Members: James Gannon, Worked alone.

    Analysis of the American military presence in Afghanistan, justified by the 9/11/01 terrorist attack on America in the context of America's global military power and the rise of radical Islam. Since the United Nations adopted the principle of self-determination in 1945, great powers have found that military strength is no guarantee of success in small wars fought against insurgents who use guerrilla and terrorist tactics. It is well past time for Americans to understand that military victory is usually beyond their reach in this kind of warfare. American and NATO forces should withdraw as soon as can be done responsibly. President Barack Obama realizes that such long, drawn-out wars waste lives and drain resources far out of proportion to any possible gain.

    The dual-track strategy in Afghanistan now is to apply the lessons of Iraq’s Anbar Awakening by negating the Islamists’ influence, driving them out of population centers—with military force, if necessary—and building trust between the tribes in the hinterlands and the central government. At the same time, the American and NATO commanders must encourage negotiations between the Afghan government and the Taliban. The goal is a stable, peaceful Afghanistan. The fight against such Islamist fanatics as al Qaeda and the Pakistani Taliban requires a different strategy—a relentless campaign using special operations forces and high-tech weapons such as drones to disrupt insurgents’ operations. Only when Afghanistan has become stabilized and anti-government operations disrupted can America and NATO safely withdraw. Nobody should think it will be quick or easy.

  • Military Occupations in the Age of Self-Determination

    • 2003 to 2007
    Team Members: James Gannon, Worked alone

    Drawing on historical antecedents to the occupation of Iraq, this book examines events such as the British struggles in Palestine, French war in Algeria, the Soviet debacle in Afghanistan, and other instances which demonstrate the futility of great-power occupations in the face of determined resistence.

  • Stealing Secrets, Telling Lies: How Spies and Codebreakers Helped Shape the 20th Century

    • August 2001 to August 2001
    Team Members: James Gannon

    This book examines the impact of many major incidents, such as the Zimmerman telegram interception, deciphering the German Enigma machine, deciphering the Japenese diplomatic code called "Purple," the Allies' great D-Day deceptiion, the Soviets' damaging penetration of the British Foreign Service through the "Cambridge Five" spy ring, the U.S. counterintelligence coup known as Operation Venona (classified until 1995), and the hidden inteligence that tipped the balance for America in the Cuban Missile Crisis.
