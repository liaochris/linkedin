karl grant's Summary

Born in London to an English Father and and Irish Mother, we moved around various places in my early years including Dublin & Milan and then settled in Manchester, where I spent my formative years, discovering amongst other things Punk Music, MG Sports Cars and the photography of Man Ray.

I attended Art School in Salford and returned to London to study photography at Harrow College of Higher Education (now the University of Westminster)

I left Harrow in 1989 with a Degree in Film Photography & Television and started to shooting Fashion & Music Portraits, in my first year out of college I photographed the then new face model Rachel Weiss for Zoo Clothing and my first magazine cover, Betty Boo for Record Mirror.

Working ‘pre-digital’ all my work was hand printed from colour negative. Later, digital technology offered increased creative freedom (and the joy of working without chemicals). I became an early adopter of the technology, initially scanning negatives until digital cameras finally became of age, since then I’ve adopted a purely digital workflow.

In 2003, I opened a studio in Mallorca and now divide my time between bases in London and Spain.
