Jacob Jay's Summary

Agile 'full-stack' architect, designer, developer and entrepreneur. Passionate about branding, marketplaces, platforms and resilience. Previously with a London agency throughout the dotcom era, subsequently independent based in India and France, now orbiting from…wherever. (Pitch me an interesting opportunity and I'd relocate/travel/telework…)

I've built and supported multitudinous projects involving everything from APIs to typesetting—across varied sectors with a total of 16 years experience. I've undertaken two key self-funded projects: a (now EOL) desktop photo app built closely with many photosharing services, having a wide scope of end users both paid and free; plus more recently a multipurpose low-latency webapp framework born out of an e-Commerce platform concept. I've also dabbled in some social enterprise endeavours around 'open' communities—incorporating coworking and coliving.

Jacob Jay's Projects

  • Moonstalk

    • April 2009 to Present
    Team Members: Jacob Jay, Deependra Tewari

    An efficient web development and hosting system built upon Lua. Created from the ground up to provide high-performance hosting of web-applications, with easy integration into a development flow. Achieved the key goal of greatly improving page rendering and scalability over common e-commerce to <5ms (e.g. deployments using Magento are commonly >250ms).

  • PictureSync

    • October 2004 to June 2009
    Team Members: Jacob Jay

    A desktop application initially providing sync services between digital asset management applications (e.g. Expression Media, iPhoto) on the Mac, and online hosting services (e.g. Flickr, Webshots). Latterly developed into a multipurpose annotation manager, and online browser with an initial version developed for Windows. First application of its kind to aggregate disparate APIs and use a rules engine for processing.

  • Moonlighting

    • November 2010 to Present
    Team Members: Jacob Jay, Yatin K Thakur

    An experimental coworking and cohousing network, initially under trial in a large villa in New Delhi, having 9 rooms and common spaces. Break–even after 18 months on a social-enterprise model.
