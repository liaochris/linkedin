Carl Killian's Summary

My name is Carl Killian, and I am a Game Designer.

Professionally, I have worked for Method Solutions as the Lead Designer on "House M.D.: Critical Cases" for Facebook, a game based on the hit television series. My responsibilities included creating, documenting, and balancing social and single-player features, as well as scripting in various languages. My efforts have helped make “House” one of Ubisoft's most successful Facebook games to date, retaining over 1,000,000 MAUs as of 9/10/2012. Before "House", I worked with Black Lantern Studios on "Zhu Zhu Pets 2: Featuring the Wild Bunch" (Wii/DS) in a Level Design and Writing capacity. By crafting Adobe Illustrator "Paper Prototypes", updating design documents, and developing dialog delivery features, I put to use many of the lessons learned in earning my Masters Degree at the University of Central Florida.

At UCF's Florida Interactive Entertainment Academy, one of the standout titles I worked on was "Drifters" (PC). Working on "Drifters" gave me the discipline to work within an Agile Scrum team environment, arrange the game-play spaces and events (specifically using Gamebryo Scene Designer and XML scripting), craft a game's fiction, and most importantly, iterate on design features from conception, to prototype, to release.

On a personal level, to be a Game Designer means to always improve one's self; to broaden one's knowledge, and hold one's self to a standard of no less than excellence. I do this in experimenting with and developing for various level editors (Unreal, GECK, Aurora, Galaxy, etc.), pushing myself beyond my existing skill sets (scripting for XNA in C#, modeling in Maya, learning business management skills), and improving myself as a human being through perusing new experiences. I have a zeal for game development, and would love the opportunity to direct that creative energy into your next project.

My name is Carl Killian, and I am a Game Designer. It would be my pleasure to speak with you.
