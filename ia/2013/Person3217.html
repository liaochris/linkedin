Alan See's Summary

Alan See is a senior executive with the rare ability to speak Web 2.0 and Sales 101 in the same sentence. He is also currently ranked as one of the most followed Chief Marketing Officer’s on Twitter by Social Media Marketing Magazine and a Top 1% Influencer by Kred. This means his grown children still wonder "what does dad really do all day at work?" He has also served as an associate faculty member at the University of Phoenix where he loved to teach, but hated to grade papers! Alan is an active blogger and frequent presenter on topics that help organizations develop marketing strategies and sales initiatives to power profitable growth. He has performed for and interacts with some of the world’s most respected brands including; IBM, Cap Gemini, Teradata, SAS Institute, NCR Corporation and AT&T. Alan holds BBA and MBA degrees from Abilene Christian University and currently serves as Interim Chief Marketing Officer for DocuStar.

Specialties: B2B, Social Media Marketing, Digital Marketing, Content-based Marketing, Directional Advertising, Strategic Planning, Marketing Strategy, Lead Generation, Published Thought Leader & Conference Speaker, Change Agent, Sales Management, Strategic Alliances and Business Development, Channel Development, Product Marketing and Product Management. Budgeting, P&L Accountability, Customer Relationship Management, Business Intelligence, Data Warehousing, Application Lifecycle Management, eLearning and Talent Management Expertise

Alan See's Projects

  • Building a Local-Ready Marketing Organization

    • May 2013 to Present
    Team Members: Alan See, Tyler Munn, Annette Schultz-Walker, Martha France, Kandi O'Connor, Jay Brokamp, Liz Schaefer

    By definition, a local market includes customers located within the region the product or service is produced or made available. That means an effective local marketing strategy should engage field sales agents and distributors - those who know the local market best - to create highly targeted marketing messages. To do this effectively and be considered “local-ready”, organizations must focus on four key competency areas:

    1. Marketing Asset Management: The ability to create and manage your marketing material inventory, including logos and images.
    2. Campaign Management: The ability to schedule one-time or ongoing multi-channel campaigns, as well as manage lists.
    3. Workflow: The ability to manage customization approvals. Manage and track QR codes and measure campaign results.
    4. Budgeting: The ability to manage and control budgets. Automate the allocation of marketing funds across sales/distribution channels.

    The DocuStar Local-Ready Marketing Assessment focuses on your unique marketing organization, its current state and where it must evolve to become local-ready.
