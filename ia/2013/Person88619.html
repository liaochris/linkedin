Matt Costanza's Summary

Matt Costanza is a fulltime faculty member of the Digital Filmmaking and Video Production department at The Art Institute of Washington. Matt has also worked as a producer, videographer, video editor, special effects animator, and photographer.

In December of 2007, Matt went to the Mathare Valley slum in Kenya to film a documentary and to teach a youth group how to use video technology. Some of the students showed exceptional potential and went on to work for a local news station. In 2008, Matt graduated from Rochester Institute of Technology with an MFA in Live Action Filmmaking. In 2012, Matt began working on a feature length screenplay which he intends to film in 2013.

Matt’s films, videos, and animations have won numerous awards and have collectively garnered 60+ screenings in festivals, galleries, theaters, and universities. International screenings of his work include: Canada, Germany, Italy, Costa Rica, Columbia, New Zealand, and Australia.

Specialties

Project Management, Photography, Cinematography, Curriculum Design, Educational Media, Higher Education, Teaching, Content Management, Producing for Film and Video, Video Editing, and Motion Graphics Design.
