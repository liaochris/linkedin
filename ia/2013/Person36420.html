Domanique Alicia's Projects

  • Hewlett Foundation Grant Visualization.

    • January 2012 to September 2012
    Team Members: Domanique Alicia, Andrew Winterman, Jacob O'Brien, Brett Johnson, Andrew Witherspoon, Dino Citraro, Pamela Statz

    Their goal was to make a tool with three functions: provide insight into where the Foundation has made the largest impact; provide grant seekers context for their applications; and help the Foundation’s officers make decisions about new grantmaking efforts, based on their existing portfolio. They had one request: No maps.

    After working with them for several months, we developed a visualization tool that includes a series of filters for sorting through $3.86 billion in grantmaking efforts over the last 12 years. We also include a “key findings” section that allows visitors to jump to interesting sortings of the data, and allows the Hewlett Foundation to highlight the stories they felt were most compelling.

  • The State of the Polar Bear

    • August 2011 to Present
    Team Members: Domanique Alicia, Zach Krausnick, Kim Rees, Jacob O'Brien, Dino Citraro

    The State of the Polar Bear is the authoritative source for the health and status of the world’s polar bears. This multipart data visualization was developed through an international partnership with the Polar Bear Specialist Group, a scientific collaboration of the five polar bear nations: Canada, Denmark, Norway, the USA, and Russia.

    The most comprehensive online visualization of polar bear information to date, the tool provides a detailed look at each of the 19 polar bear subpopulations, and explores the factors that have influenced their IUCN health status ratings.

    The tool includes visualizations of abundance figures, harvest rates, circumpolar pollution levels, the shrinking Arctic sea ice, and other factors. In the next version of this tool, visitors will also be able to watch over two dozen videos of the world’s top scientists explaining the key issues that threaten the survival of this iconic species.
