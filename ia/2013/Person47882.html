George Dy Jr.'s Summary

I'm a product designer by trade, bridging both interface and interaction design. I co-founded a startup called GetContact.Info, aimed at making meeting and remembering people simpler. Our company folded and I joined a team called Post+Beam to help build their development and design practice in San Francisco. I'm a strong advocate for the lean startup principles and agile development methodology. I like downloading cool apps, appreciating innovative new designs and interactions, and spend most of my time working on new experiences for personal and client projects.

When I design, I believe in simplicity of design and function and I intend on making technology more accessible and useful for people across generational gaps. I use my formal experience in UI/UX design, project management, product marketing, and business development to learn and create better products.

Specialties: User experience design, interaction design, user interface design, internet marketing, SEO/SEM, social media, project management, business development, building partnerships, making things shiny.

GetContact.Info

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Internet industry

November 2010 – December 2011 (1 year 2 months) San Francisco, CA

We launched GetContact.Info in 3 months from idea to MVP and released our beta build to the public at SxSW 2010. Separately, Alex and I had multiple experiences going to conferences and faced a constant struggle collecting and evaluating the sheer amount of contacts we'd made in a single day at an event.

Our goal with GCI was to build a mobile tool that enhanced the first interaction two people had with each other. By building a web-based platform, our users were able to change and update their contact information on the fly and provide a medium of exchange through a printed QR code on a business cards, flyers, and other printer materials.

We aimed for avid conference goers (as the first audience), built a growing customer-base and continued to grow outside of paid conferences and events. Following our conference-circuit, we pitched our services to enterprise clients that looked to bring GCI as a standard for contact exchange in cases ranging from real estate to law practice.

At SxSW 2011, we promoted our digital business card product by producing QR code stickers that contained a users' digital identity. Attendees would scan each other, triggering the GPS component to our site, which would show who, where, and when individuals met and exchanged contact information. It augmented the experience by providing a "save-for-later, note-taking, and interactive" component to exchanging business cards.
