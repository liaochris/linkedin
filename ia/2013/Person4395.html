Alfred Abel's Summary

For more than 30 years, Alfred M. Abel, Esq., has provided legal advice and litigation services in Philadelphia and the surrounding suburban counties. Clients arrive in the office frustrated, confused and at a loss to solve their financial issue. The lawsuits, court notices and other documents they get are hard to decipher. He knows the community, understands the procedures involved, and knows the law and can explain it in plain English. If you're heading into court, that's a depth of experience you want on your side.
The problems he solves for clients include litigation of debts, loan workouts, private business dissolutions, bankruptcy cases for consumers and businesses, business matters, and claims for insurance for personal injury and property damage.
The mission of our office is to listen to our clients, understand their objectives and provide strategic, cost-effective solutions.

Specialties

loan workouts, real estate, business, personal, and corporate bankruptcy, personal injury, wrongful death, and diminished value and property damage claims.

Alfred Abel's Experience

Judiciary Committee of the Montgomery County Bar Association

Montgomery county bar

January 2011 – Present (2 years 8 months)

The Judiciary Committee reviews the candidates for judicial elections and makes public recommendations on the fitness of each candidate. The candidates are vetted on criteria including trial experience, health, breadth of experience and others. The committee consists of roughly 18 Montgomery County Bar members who are appointed by the directors for a term of three years. The recommendations are published widely to help the general public make informed decisions when they vote for judges.

Co-Chairman Bankruptcy and Creditors Rights Committee

Montgomery County Bar Association

2008 – 2009 (1 year)

The Montgomery County Bar Association Bankruptcy and Creditors Rights Committee consists of lawyers in Montgomery County Pennsylvania who work in this area of the law, and who represent both debtors and creditors, and both personal and business interests. The committe meets at regular monthly meetings to discuss current topics in this area, make proposals for changes, and hosts educational seminars for lawyers and other interested individuals This years educational seminar for lawyers examined the intersection of bankruptcy and family law practice in Pennsylvania.

Chairman, Bankruptcy and Creditors rights committee

Montgomery County Bar Association

2008 – 2009 (1 year)

As chairman of the Bankruptcy and Creditors rights committee, I presented seminars on topics of interest to lawyers, accountants, business owners and other community members. These topics included how a business or individual can file bankruptcy, how a business can protect their interest if a customer files bankruptcy, how a business owner can protect their assets if their business is failing, how to resolve debts without using bankruptcy laws and other. I also held monthly meetings to discuss current topics.
