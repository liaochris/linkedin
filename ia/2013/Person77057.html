2009 – Present (4 years) Sunnyvale, CA

BioCurious is the first ever "hackerspace for biotech" where members come to learn, share ideas, collaborate on projects and eventually, start up companies.

We believe that innovations in biology should be accessible, affordable, and open to everyone. We are a not-for-profit community biology lab for amateurs, inventors, entrepreneurs, and anyone who wants to experiment with friends.

BioCurious is…
* a complete working laboratory for entrepreneurs to cheaply access equipment, materials, and co-working space

* a meeting place for citizen scientists, hobbyists, activists, and students

I took a small amount of money we raised on Kickstarter, found a building, convinced the landlord to take a chance on a rag-tag team of people with an idea, and very little money, and we opened to the public a few short months later. I handle all things operational, financial - basically, I make the engine run.

I also developed and ran several workshop series. "The Business of Biotech" brought in industry experts to focus on slices of the big, broad catalog of things we call Biotech. "Founders' Tales" had real-life founders telling their stories from the road - what worked, what didn't, and their best advice for new and seasoned entrepreneurs.
