Mayordomo

Acequia el Molino

October 1988 – September 1992 (4 years)

In New Mexico today there are about 1000 acequias, or ditches, used for irrigation. Many are hundreds of years old and originally dug by hand, and most are still maintained primarily by shovel and sweat. A communal system has evolved over time for the administration of acequias, including the election annually of a "mayordomo," or ditch boss, who is responsible for the allocation of water among the member properties along the course of the acequia; recruiting, supervising, and paying laborers to maintain the ditch; and, along with "commissioners", negotiating water allocation issues with neighboring acequias. The Acequia el Molino runs off the Rio Pueblo in the community of Ranchitos, NM, part of Taos, NM. At least 160 years old and just north of town, Acequia el Molino runs through a variety of small and large land tracts, providing water to about 30 member properties with surface water rights -- gardens and large grazing pastures alike. I was a commissioner of the acequia for four years and Mayordomo for the last of those years before moving away from the property that had water rights on the acequia.
