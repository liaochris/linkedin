Dan Goldman's Summary

Dan Goldman writes graphic novels and videogames, draws comics and editorial illustrations, and crafts transmedia experiences for such companies as AMC Television, Random House, IDW Publishing, Time Magazine, Hachette Books, Entertainment Weekly and New York Magazine. He has been profiled by The New York Times, NPR, Sky News, BBC Radio, The Village Voice and Advertising Age.

His recent work includes the comic series RED LIGHT PROPERTIES — a paranormal dramedy following a husband and wife's exorcism/realty business selling "previously-haunted homes" — published across all digital platforms by Monkeybrain Comics and in print by IDW Publishing. Dan also serves as Story Sync Experience Architect on the second screen applications for AMC Television's flagship original series Breaking Bad, The Killing and The Walking Dead (for which he also wrote The Walking Dead Social Game).

Beginning his career in DIY web publishing, Goldman co-founded the Brooklyn-based ACT-I-VATE webcomics collective in 2006 as an "online laboratory for experimental comics" which went from a group LiveJournal blog to become a New York Times-featured online anthology hosting long-form comics works by over 50 international cartoonists.

His 2006 webcomic SHOOTING WAR (with Anthony Lappé), which Forbes called “the Apocalypse Now of the War on Terrorism, told in the form of a brilliantly-rendered graphic novel," was acclaimed by publications from Wired and Brazil's Folha de São Paulo to India's Business Standard and Tokyo's Daily Yomiuri, earning an Eisner Award nomination for Best Digital Comic.

Goldman stands as an industry thought-leader, presenting on digital comics at tech/media/entertainment conferences such as SXSW Interactive ("Comics on Handhelds: Taking Webcomics Mobile"), New York Comic-Con ("Going Paperless: Using Computers to Draw Comics" 2008-2009), Mostra 3M de Arte Digital, DIY Days ("The Right Now of Comics"), PixelShow São Paulo 2011 and M.I.T.’s Futures of Entertainment 4.
