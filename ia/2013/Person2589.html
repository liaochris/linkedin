Frank Aiello's Summary

I am CEO of Aiello Studios, award winning commercial photography studio, in business since 1985. We service food, apparel, and pharmaceutical industries with commercial advertising & corporate identity photography, web development, infomercial productions, and digital enhancement. Known for photographic and creative excellence, we specialize in launching new brands and repositioning market plans.

My strength is my extraordinary ability to quickly resolve issues at crunch moments, to be creative, and to remain positively productive. At Aiello Studios, we offer a wealth of diversified experience with a concentration in post production as well as pre-production.

As the son of a classical painter, I knew from an early age that my gift was my innate eye for light and its enhancement for the subject. It’s been “my eye” that has allowed my photography studio to survive, flourish and support clients for the past twenty years.

I also teach college classes in photography, I truly enjoy sharing my passion with every student. My goal is to connect with each student and spark their creativity.

CAREER MILESTONES:

Frank Aiello's Projects

  • Men Mothering

    • January 1985 to Present
    Team Members: Frank Aiello

    When my late wife Loretta Morris Aiello died on July 25, 1990, after a long, grueling battle with breast cancer, so many visions went through my head.
    Scenes from our wedding day flooded my mind…young, carefree, full of hope and promise. I daydreamed of a life of children, work, home, vacations, watching the kids play soccer or baseball, with all the excitement of a new husband embarking on family life. After the births of our children Frankie and Laura, the dream became a reality, and life took off in the flurry that comes with small children. Never once did I stop to consider, or let my mind even touch upon, the possibility of raising my children without my wife.

    For me, growing up in Brooklyn, the ocean represented a reprieve from the crowded frenzy of the city. I could look out at the vast immensity, see the waves as they rose up to kiss the awaiting sky and get a feeling of serenity,
    a lift from my daily cares. So, when I searched for the right place to tell
    six-year-old Frankie the worst news of his life, my car found its way to Manhattan Beach. But, there would be no serenity on that day.

    Frankie’s two-year-old sister Laura, too young to understand anything about what was happening to her life, played in the sand next to us, as I gently took my son’s hand and whispered that Mommy had gone to heaven. The look on his confused, panicked face and his words, “Mommy’s dead?” which he spoke with involuntary repetition, continue to haunt me to this day, and the helplessness I felt will stay with me eternally.

    From that moment on, my life changed forever. To be a nurturing parent took on a new meaning…Now I had to be a not only a father but also a mother.

    I am looking for four very special men telling the stories of their role not only as a father but as a mother to their children. It is a photographic book including short stories of the journey, the struggles and the rewards.
