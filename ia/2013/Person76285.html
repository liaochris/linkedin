K'Lee Banks, M.Ed.'s Summary

I am an experienced educator and lifelong learner – and a strong proponent of online learning. I earned a Master of Education degree in Instructional Technology from AIU Online in 2008, with a 4.0 GPA, at the age of 50!

I am now on my journey toward earning my Doctor of Education degree, with a dual concentration in Educational Technology and E-Learning, at Northcentral University. I'm anticipating a 2016 graduation, at which time I'll become Dr. K'Lee Banks!

My ultimate GOALS - to become:
*Online instructional facilitator/tutor/professor
*Curriculum developer
*Autism/PECS consultant

I have many years of experience in various forms of writing, acquired through academic, professional, and life experience. I'm currently offering the following services from my home office in Maine: writing, editing, online tutoring, instructional design and course authoring/review.

I also have a background in social services and special education, having served in the following positions:
*Speech and developmental therapist (SLPA) for Griffiths Therapy Associates
*FSA (Family Service Advocate) for Head Start
*BHP (Behavioral Health Professional) for Providence Service Corporation and Atlantic Home Health
*Youth Crisis Worker for Rumford Group Homes

Some completed projects:
*EditFast: (1) rewriting/ghostwriting book of 12 chapters (approx. 360 pages, ~78,000 words); (2) proofreading book of about 200 pages for a fitness celebrity; (3) editing multiple academic college and graduate level papers

*Llumina Press: (1) Editing fantasy/horror fiction manuscript of approx.130,000 words; (2) Editing non-fiction manuscript of approx. 35,000 words

*FYI Online Learning: Completed course authoring/review and instructional design work for the following five courses: English 1 & 3, Creative Writing, Personal Fitness, and Research

*Completed samples or single lessons/modules for SMH eDevelopment, Strategic Learning Solutions, The Entrepreneurial Woman, Training Improvement, and Training Made.

Specialties

Academic writing, blogging, forum posting, editing and proofreading, instructional design, course authoring/reviewing, e-tutoring

  • WebQuest: Lighthouses in Maine: Why is Maine Known as the Lighthouse State?

    • Zunal.com
    • April 22, 2012
    Authors: K'Lee Banks, M.Ed.

    I created this WebQuest as one of my final projects for one of my Ed.D. degree courses.

    It is a virtual journey along Maine's coast to learn more about the state's lighthouses, for Grades 6-8 Social Studies curriculum supplement in WebQuest format.

    Introduction: You are about to take a journey along Maine’s coast to learn more about the state’s lighthouses. You will learn some of the history behind Maine’s lighthouses, how many lighthouses there are, how long the state’s coastline is, and what purpose Maine’s lighthouses have served and still serve today.

  • Special Resources for Special Education

    • SchoolRack.com
    • April 22, 2012
    Authors: K'Lee Banks, M.Ed.

    I created this website as my final project for one of my grad courses entitled, "Instruction and the Internet." This is for my Ed.D. degree program, with a dual concentration in Educational Technology and E-Learning.

    I have a background in social services and special education, including working briefly as a speech and developmental therapist (SLPA) here in Maine.

    Since early 2008, I have been a freelance writer and instructional designer, and have now written hundreds of articles - and about 100-150 of those are on various educational topics.

    I plan to regularly update this site with articles, resources, and other information that will be useful to those who teach special needs students, as well as typically learning students.

    As one who is a lifelong learner, a current doctoral student, and one who is working toward being a reputable educational consultant, I hope this site will be helpful for many educators, students, and others.

  • Fatherly Reflections

    • Amazon
    • June 17, 2012
    Authors: K'Lee Banks, M.Ed.

    Fatherly Reflections is inspirational, as well as a thought-provoking look at the importance of being a good father and leaving behind a legacy worth remembering. Fatherly Reflections is encouraging for Dads, those who may be fathers some day, as well as those who love/d their Dads!

    Prepare to laugh, shed a tear or two, and probably nod your head in agreement along the way as you read Fatherly Reflections. Readers will enjoy this warm collection of short stories, poems, and reflections about Dads, and also benefit from suggestions to Dads or to those men who may be Dads some day.

    Fatherly Reflections is primarily a tribute to the author's father, Ben Willis, who passed away in 2002, and about fatherhood in general. Banks has included her own tribute and memories about him, plus compiled contributions from other family members and friends who knew him.
