Mari Huertas' Summary

I built my first website in 1998 and began working as a professional web designer and developer shortly afterward to produce full-scale, hand-coded websites for clients.

I joined the publishing field in 2003 and brought World Book, Inc.'s, initial digital curriculum offerings to life from 2008-2011, crafting an entire suite of commercially and critically successful web-based education products in social studies, science, and literacy.

In early 2012, I joined President Obama's re-election campaign in the Technology sector, drafting and managing the development of software tools across the Digital, Finance, and Voter verticals to microtarget, message, and register voters; raise money and sell campaign merchandise; and move our community up a ladder of engagement to purposeful actions.

My expertise rests in communicating openly across people and projects and connecting dots creatively and effectively. I'm highly passionate about my projects, work tirelessly to deliver them on goal, and thoughtfully evaluate what I can do to remove friction and strip out waste at every step.

I plan never to retire because I love creating too much to stop, and I always appreciate a good problem to solve. If that sounds good to you, we should jam.

Mari Huertas' Projects

  • Targeted sharing / microtargeting

    • February 2013 to November 2013
    Team Members: Mari Huertas, Rayid Ghani, Matthew Rattigan, Christopher Coté, Will St. Clair

    The Technology team (engineers and data scientists both) developed an infrastructure that targeted messaging more efficiently and effectively for voter outreach and registration, message sharing, and more. This helped the team reach a broad audience with pinpointed talking points and rallied the public to the polls.

  • Payment Processor

    • February 2013 to November 2013
    Team Members: Mari Huertas, Nick Leeper, Ivan Indrautama, Kyle Rush, Dan Ratner

    The Payment Processor software handled campaign donations, at one point clipping $2 million / hour. My colleague Kyle Rush has great write-ups on the software on his website, kylerush.net.

  • Call Tool

    • September 2011 to Present
    Team Members: Mari Huertas, Derek Brooks, Bridget Liberty Halligan

    Call Tool is a phone canvassing web application used by both Obama for America and the DNC. In its simplest form, it provides volunteers with a relevant script to read and a voter to Call. The volunteer records the answers of the voter, which are used to learn more about how the campaign should be operating or targeting individuals.

    Underneath its shell Call Tool had a fairly complex architecture that proxied all of its data through our Narwhal API and interfaced with a Voter Checkout Service API and synced with 2 of the campaign's vendors.

    I architected and built the Call Tool web application and its communication with Narwhal API from the ground up. I also extended several parts of the Voter Checkout Service to make it more performant, tighten protection against fraudulent callers, and better integrate with our vendors. As I took on other tasks on the campaign, I worked with other engineers and oversaw all development on the application.
