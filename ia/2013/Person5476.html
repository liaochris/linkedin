Coby Perkins, D.C.'s Summary

Our mission is to improve the health and long term well-being of our patients by providing healthcare solutions that are surprisingly gentle, safe, and effective. Imagine a clinic that puts the CARE back in HEALTHCARE, and where we emphasize a gentle, personalized approach for each patient. We are dedicated to helping people in the Amarillo area find relief from pain, while experiencing greater mobility and function, and enjoy long-term well-being. It's our greatest pleasure to make a difference in people's lives!

As a family chiropractic clinic, we offer solutions to help your active kids and teenagers in sports stay healthier, as well as adults and seniors who enjoy an active lifestyle. On any given day, we are helping people find effective relief from back, shoulder, or neck tension, or find relief from shoulder pain, sports injuries, sciatica, headaches, and more! We offer a range of clinical techniques that allow us to provide a more personalized approach to healing and recovery.

One of these approaches is through the use of Cold Laser / Low Level Laser Therapy. We use the same laser technology now being used by professional teams including the New York Jets, the Texas Rangers, and a wide range of professional athletes. In a nutshell, the laser therapy works on a deeper cellular level to promote faster tissue healing and repair, while also reducing inflammation and the pain response. Other benefits may include increased mobility or strength in areas that have become weakened from lack of use. It can be highly effective before as well as after orthopedic or spinal surgery - so if you suffer chronic post-surgical pain there is hope for improvement.

By consulting our office, you're taking an exciting first step toward better health. Perhaps we can help you discover safe, gentle, non-invasive options that are surprisingly effective!

Coby Perkins, D.C.'s Projects

  • Shoulder Pain Relieved - After Suffering for 6 Years

    • September 2013 to Present
    Team Members: Coby Perkins, D.C.

    Shoulder pain can result from overuse or repetitive motion (tendonitis), as well as various injuries (rotator cuff tear for example), and arthritis. It can even persist after undergoing orthopedic / arthroscopic surgery. In this case, a 27 year old teacher came in with chronic severe pain in her left shoulder. 6 years earlier she had been in a serious car accident where she was rear-ended so strongly that she was knocked unconscious and does not remember the wreck. She sustained severe whiplash, a herniated disc in her neck, and developed severe pain in her neck, left shoulder, upper chest, and her left arm. As a result, her sleep quality has suffered and use of her left arm has become more limited over time. Despite going to her regular doctor and an orthopedist, having physical therapy, and seeing another chiropractor, the pain in her left shoulder never resolved. After she began care with us, she was pleasantly surprised at the relief and improvement she felt in just 2 visits. In less than 10 visits, she had regained full and pain-free range of motion in her shoulder. A welcome change after having unrelenting pain for 6 years!!

  • Back Injury Success

    • August 2013 to Present
    Team Members: Coby Perkins, D.C.

    Back injuries can occur at the gym as well as on the job or at home, and we've seen great success helping people recover! Recently a 36 year old patient injured his low back while working out at the gym. He tried resting, massage, and just giving it some time to recover. Well, almost a week later his pain and ability to move comfortably had still not improved - a sure sign this was no simple back strain. When he came in he was not able to stand or walk very well due to severe pain, he was unable to bend at the waist, and had to have assistance at home getting dressed. Every movement was difficult and brought increased pain. Within 4 hours of his first treatment he was starting to feel very welcome relief and was beginning to move better already. By the next day his pain level had dropped 80 to 90% from the day before, and was he moving and bending with only mild pain / soreness. This was probably the most dramatic turn-around with a serious back injury that we've seen! Naturally, each patient is unique and response to care will vary from person to person.
