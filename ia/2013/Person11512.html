John Bakos, CRO | Careerist | Thought Leader's Summary

Nothing works unless you have an effective resume. Unfortunately, job seekers believe that resumes are biographical career histories that are unchangeable, hoping and relying on employers and recruiters to give them the opportunity to be a candidate. In fact, and unknown to job seekers, their resumes are used to disqualify them. Employers and recruiters are very grateful that their jobs have been made easier … in disqualifying you! For instance:

★ ENTREPRENEURSHIPS / SELF-EMPLOYMENT (your ambitions, courage and independence can backfire in a job search)

★ JOB HOPPING APPEARANCE (even if you're not) Most job seekers believe that they have to rely on employers and recruiters to give them the 'benefit of the doubt' and hope that the appearance of job instability will not be used to reject them. You suffered when you lost your job that was outside your control; now you're suffering again. Doubly unfair. Creative presentation techniques can resolve this 'red flag' issue.

★ UNEMPLOYMENT (an unfair hiring disqualifier) Some employers are actually stating that 'only employed' people can apply.

★ UNRELATED JOBS (your career resourcefulness and strong work ethic are used against you)

★ AGE (real age v. resume age ... a big difference) Great confusion exists. Many job seekers actually make themselves older in the resume, believing they will look younger.

★ TYPECASTING HALO (assumption of industry and talent transferability is incorrect) You know that your talents and industry-specific skills can be transported or transferred.

★ INCORRECT or WEAK TITLES (most titles are weak, incorrect or sending the wrong message, affecting your career) Your employers gave you a title that, at the time, was appropriate. Now, it's being used to reject you, or at least weaken your marketability. You have done more than the title conveys; the wrong message is being sent; and the theme of your resume is 'out of kilter'.

All of these issues can be fixed! And they should be.
