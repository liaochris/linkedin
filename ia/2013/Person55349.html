Andrew Ingkavet's Summary

Andrew Ingkavet is a renaissance soul with boundless curiousity and passion. This has led to a rather unusual career path with deep dives in the areas of Music, Design, New Media, Film, Theater, Television, Journalism and Education.

In the early 1990's Andrew was one of the first VJ's for MTV-Asia. As an actor with Pan-Asian Rep, he starred in the original Off-Broadway production of "Letters To A Student Revolutionary." You may also see him in the background of several Cosby Show episodes. Simultaneously, he was writing scripts for MTV, and freelance writing for several music trades.

In 1994, Andrew co-founded award-winning interactive agency Lemon in Hong Kong, securing their first clients Cathay Pacific, NBC-Asia, CNBC, and MSNBC.

Upon returning to the States in the mid 90's, he quickly rose to VP/ Creative Director for advertising giant Foote, Cone & Belding's fledgling interactive agency. Whilst there he scored many projects, several of which won awards.

As a Composer/Music Producer he has credits in advertising, television, film, television, multimedia/web and even at the United Nations. His film scoring includes independent feature "Noelle" for which industry trade magazine VARIETY says "Obviously a labor of love for all parties involved...Andrew Ingkavet's delicate musical score are the film's standout production values."

Currently, Andrew divides his time as Executive Producer and Head Monk of 300 Monks, a global music collective/audio agency providing audio strategy, original music scoring and music licensing services and the owner of a highly successful music teaching studio Park Slope Music Lessons. He has authored an interactive music book for parents of young children "Play Piano For Kids".

Specialties: Creativity across the disciplines of Broadcast, Interactive, Music/Audio, Theater and Television
