ASP Franchise's Summary

ASP - America's Swimming Pool Co, the nation's most respected swimming pool maintenance and repair franchise, was established in 2001 by Stewart Vernon of Macon, Georgia. The company was built on the belief that providing the highest-quality pool maintenance with competitive pricing and a high level of dependability will attract customers. Since 2005, ASP has expanded into 110 franchised locations servicing over 200 cities across the country in 12 states. At this point, the company manages over 80 million gallons of water per week and renovates or remodels more than 250 pools each year.

Specialties

ASP - America's Swimming Pool Co, the nation's largest swimming pool maintenance and repair company, provides professional service with a reputation for excellence unmatched in the industry.

Since 2001, ASP has been dedicated to serving pool owners throughout the United States. Our professionals are experienced in maintenance, equipment diagnostics & repair, & pool renovation. Stewart C. Vernon - President, Chief Executive Officer

Stewart C. Vernon is our president and chief executive officer, positions he has held since our inception. Mr. Vernon also is the founder and former owner of our affiliate, ASP of Macon, Georgia, a position he held from January 2002 until January 2008. At that time, Mr. Vernon sold the franchise to his partner in that business, Matt Dayton.

Thomas P. Swift, Jr., JD, LLM, CFP® - Vice President, Chief Operating Officer

Thomas P. Swift, Jr. is our vice president and chief operating officer, positions he has held since January 2009. Mr. Swift is also the owner of two franchises, ASP of Warner Robins and ASP of North Fulton County, positions he has held since their inception in 2006. Prior to 2006, Mr. Swift was a corporate tax attorney for James, Bates, Pope and Spivey, LLLP, specializing in corporate tax and estate tax planning. Mr. Swift has earned his Juris Doctorate, a master's degree in tax law and a certified financial planner’s license.

Many of these pools are owned by people who have little time to spend maintaining and servicing them. Instead, the owners want to spend their downtime enjoying their investment.
