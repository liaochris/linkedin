Geoffrey Klecan's Projects

  • Weather Fred'Neck

    • January 2005 to Present
    Team Members: Geoffrey Klecan

    For many years, I've had a strong passion for meteorology. Since college, I've been developing a not-for-profit entity called Weather Fred'Neck to promote local weather forecasts, phenomena discussion and live/historical data collection to the Frederick, MD community. Some notable accomplishments include:

    2013:
    - Began developing a custom e-blast forecasting platform for Web users to sign up and receive customized forecasts, based on National Weather Service XML data for locales throughout Maryland, Delaware, Northern Virginia, the Eastern Panhandle of West Virginia, and southern Pennsylvania.
    - Began developing an automated process of pulling live NWS forecasts and posting to a twitter feed (@wxfrnx) 4 times daily (still working some bugs out of the background processing).
    - Currently posting data to a separate twitter feed (@wxfrnx_report) every 15 minutes with current weather observations.
    - Planning on beginning to autonomously collect data for archival purposes which will be accessible from the future development of the Weather Fred'Neck Web site.

    2012:
    - Successfully completed training with the National Weather Service to be a Weather Spotter within the Baltimore/Washington DC Forecast region.
    - Began autonomously feeding data to meteorological services including Weather Underground, WeatherBug, and the Citizen Weather Observer Program.

    2011:
    - Setup a new Davis Instruments weather station in my backyard.
