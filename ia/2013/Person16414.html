Brandi Moore's Summary

I am an information security professional with extensive experience solving a variety of security problems, devising and implementing information security policy for both users and employees, managing compliance obligations such as RIPPA, PCI and SOX, running large multi-million dollar projects that span international borders, negotiating with foreign governments on compliance obligations, and maybe most importantly!! managing difficult personalities across teams to get things done.

I have studied computer crime for the government, lead programs at software companies, sold services at incident response, static analysis and PCI focused firms.

I have a Masters in Forensic Science with a focus on Security Management. This program taught me how to think through complex security problems and offer business-oriented solutions to organizations.

I spent the last three years working on a variety of projects to pursue my passion for international understanding. Some of this was consulting. A lot of this work was writing. My writing has been featured in The Christian Science Monitor, Forbes, Wall Street Journal, ABA Journal, Outsource Magazine, and many other locations.

I am the author of The Little BRIC Book: Cracking the code of global management across Brazil, Russia, India and China, a SIMPLIFIED guide for people engaged in complex projects across the BRIC that need tactics for getting started or reversing project failures.

I am a professional speaker. My talks help Americans rethink their approaches to managing teams in India. I experience delivering presentations and keynotes to audiences over 600. I have trained more than 10,000 people.
