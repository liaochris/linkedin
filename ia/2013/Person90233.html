Matthew Campbell's Summary

As a developer I pride myself on creating clean, valid, accessible, and semantically correct markup. I follow the design methodologies of progressive enhancement and responsive design to ensure all users will have an enjoyable browsing experience. I also focus on optimizing my sites to minimize bandwidth requirements. When creating server side code I strive for readable code produced within a strong MVC framework so that it is both efficient and scalable. I have an extensive background in code design techniques such as Object-Oriented Design, UML, and IDEF1X which I leverage to produce well-documented, extensible, and functional code.

I am also a certified Ontario High School teacher with qualifications in Physics, Computer Science, and Mathematics.

Matthew Campbell's Publications

  • All the News That's Fit to Blog

    • STAO Crucible
    • September 2010
    Authors: Matthew Campbell

    A blog (short for web log) is a type of website that permits online journaling. Blogs can be used in the classroom to encourage literacy, record new ideas learned in science, to examine science in real-life, and as a lab notebook. This article will explore how to create a blog and will examine uses of blogs in the science classroom.

  • Quick, Quick it's a Wiki

    • STAO Crucible
    • June 2010
    Authors: Matthew Campbell

    A wiki is a website that allows the reader of the site to edit, add, or remove content on the site. Wikis provide an ideal medium for collaboration, as all users are able to alter the content easily through their own web browser. In this article, I will look at current popular uses of wikis, how to make a wiki, and why you may want one for your classroom.

  • Podcasting in the Science Classroom

    • STAO Crucible
    • March 2010
    Authors: Matthew Campbell

    Personal MP3 players have become as ubiquitous in schools as pencils and pens. However, unlike the latter, MP3 players provide an educational resource that too often goes untapped. Thanks to the portability and memory capabilities of modern MP3 players, the podcast has become one of the fastest growing communication mediums on the Internet. In this article, I will introduce podcasting, explain how to create podcasts, and provide examples of classroom activities that take advantage of the strengths of podcasts.
