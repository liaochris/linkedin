Programming Intern

Tag Games

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Computer Games industry

June 2010 – August 2010 (3 months)

I worked as the sole programmer porting and extending Astro Ranch, an iPhone farming-adventure game, for the WiiWare platform.

The position involved quickly familiarising myself with an established codebase in order to add features and adapt the game to use motion controls.

Laboratory Demonstrator

University of Abertay Dundee

Educational Institution; 501-1000 employees; Higher Education industry

September 2008 – May 2010 (1 year 9 months)

Assisted in first year lab classes, helping new students with C++ programming. This includes going over the basics of the language, encouraging good development practices, and helping to solve problems with programming coursework.

Through this position I also assisted during the Dare Schools Challenge in 2008. During this short project I helped high-school programmers and artists create some of their first games using Flash Actionscript.

Student Ambassador

University of Abertay Dundee

Educational Institution; 501-1000 employees; Higher Education industry

June 2007 – May 2010 (3 years)

Representing the university, I welcomed and assisted new students and potential applicants. This ranged from giving tours relating to my course to interview candidates to helping international students arriving in Scotland for the first time.

For this work and my involvement with the peer mentor and class representative schemes I was awarded the U. B. Henry Prize for making a difference to the lives of others at University.

Programmer

Digital Colony (Dare to be Digital)

June 2009 – August 2009 (3 months)

Developed "Pollen", a 2D puzzle-platformer set in a 3D environment which challenges the player to guide a swarm of bees to their new home.

Part of a team of five with only ten weeks production time, I worked with the team leader to organise our iterative development process and oversee programming tasks.

I worked on a range of areas including the game framework, shaders and full-screen effects, audio and game-play such as gesture recognition and swarming motion. Development was completed in C# with XNA.

Programmer

BBC Prototype

September 2008 – June 2009 (10 months)

As part of a joint project between the University of Abertay Dundee and the BBC, my team of ten developed a 3D platform game from conception to a completed demo level, using the Ogre engine.

As well as overseeing our iterative development process, I worked on a range of areas including the initial framework, gameplay, and physics.
