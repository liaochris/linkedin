Christy Olsen's Summary

"I love to draw or paint using my observation skills and then as part of the aesthetics in each piece, I emphasize the beauty in all of my subjects. This approach allows me to express my individuality, exercise my imagination and gives me the freedom to edit, alter or improve my original design."

Professional Artist & Owner

Olsen Fine Art

December 2010 – Present (2 years 10 months) Tucson, Arizona Area

Artist Christy Olsen creates the utmost soft, soothing and serene imagery in which to contemplate. Her spacious compositions, the use of soft edges and her neutral palette of earth tones combine into stunning works of art which are both timeless and elegant. Olsen developed her unique style by studying the early American Impressionist of the 19th century and the Old Masters of the Renaissance. Olsen prefers to work in oil or soft pastel and is also an excellent drafts person using charcoal, graphite or pastel. Olsen is the sole author of "The Art Appreciation Blog.com" and "The Art Verve.com" and she serves as a well respected art instructor.

Teaching Artist

Outreach Art Tutoring for Seniors (OATS) Program

2011 – 2013 (2 years) Tucson, Arizona Area

Outreach Art Tutoring for Seniors (OATS) is a program that brings professional artists, into the senior community of southern Arizona to teach the practice of studio art. The program places practicing artists within the community to teach the basic fundamentals of drawing and painting. Students draw objects from life and develop their observational skills. Soon after, they quickly realize that anyone can improve their ability to draw at any age or any level. They often have the powerful experience of “I am not my own limitation” which also increases their self-confidence in other aspects of their lives.
