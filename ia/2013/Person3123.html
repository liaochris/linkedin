Alan Goldberger's Summary

ALAN GOLDBERGER is a member of the New Jersey, New York and Maryland Bars. His practice is concentrated in the areas of sports law and sports and athletic risk management, officiating law, business and chancery litigation, sports insurance defense, and association law.

Alan Goldberger represents business entities, insurance carriers, sports officials' associations and other sports organizations, referees, coaches, and administrators on a local, state, and national scale. Alan represents clients in business related disputes, defense of liability claims, risk management consulting, administrative hearings, and contractual relations. He is frequently retained by insurance carriers to defend sports officials, coaches, athletic facilities and other sports organizations.

A nationally recognized authority on sports law and sports officiating, Alan is the author of "Sports Officiating: A Legal Guide" -- now in its second edition; and coauthor of "Sport, Physical Activity and the Law," a college textbook now in its third edition.

Alan has been a featured speaker at national conferences, including the National Association of Sports Officials, International Association of Approved Basketball Officials, Inc., American Bar Association, National Intramural Recreational Sports Association, National Parks and Recreation Association, Professional Association of Volleyball Officials, Police Athletic League, US Lacrosse, Medical Society of New Jersey, New Jersey Council of Administrators of Health and Physical Education, Athletic Trainers Society of New Jersey, Inc., and many state and local high school athletic associations, colleges, universities, and school districts. His work has been featured in national publications, and in numerous newspaper, radio and television appearances.

ALAN GOLDBERGER is AV®_Rated by Martindale-Hubbell and is an A. M. Best Recommended Insurance Attorney.

Alan Goldberger's Publications

  • SPORTS OFFICIATING: A LEGAL GUIDE 2nd Edition

    • Referee Enterprises, Inc.
    • December 1, 2007
    Authors: Alan Goldberger

    This book is the essential guide for sports officials and those who supervise, manage and use their services. Its contents are a clear indication that officiating in today's world is not like the "old days." Contemporary officiating practice now must be guided by the legal realities officials are confronted with when they take to the floor, field, diamond or pitch. This book is the definitive exposition of these new realities. Legal rights and responsibilities of game officials and officiating associations are featured, as well as expanded reviews of legal duties of officials in ten sports. In addition to completely new and expanded "Legal Checkups" for Baseball, Softball, Basketball, Football, Wrestling, and Soccer officials, four new sports have been added to the Second Edition. Officials who work Lacrosse, Volleyball, Track & Field, and Swimming will find their own "Legal Checkups." The first edition of SPORTS OFFICIATING: A LEGAL GUIDE has been named one of "The 17 Essential Books of the Well Read Official" by Referee Magazine.

    • BestConnect Newsletter
    • October 29, 2008
    Authors: Alan Goldberger

    BestConnect, an e-newsletter created to keep you up-to-date on key events and happenings associated with Best's Directories of Recommended Insurance Attorneys and Adjusters, the Best's Directories of Insurance Professionals Web site and the insurance industry in general..

  • Sport, Physical Activity, and the Law (Third Edition)

    • Sagamore Publishing
    • November 1, 2007
    Authors: Alan Goldberger, Neil Dougherty, LInda Jean Carpenter

    Sport, Physical Activity, and the Law, 3rd Edition, by Neil J. Dougherty, Alan S. Goldberger, & Linda Jean Carpenter
    is a new edition of the popular college text that takes a practical approach to using the law as a day-to-day management tool. The reader is introduced to the law and the American legal system in terms of practical applications to sports and recreation. The basics of the law for those working, or intending to work in the sports and athletic industries are explained in clear and concise fashion. Included are practical discussions of principles of risk management, liability for negligence, legal issues regarding violence, codes of conduct, responsibilities of game officials, breach of contract, product liability, and the legal rights of athletes, coaches, and administrators, This new edition features supplementary case studies, management guidelines, and discussion questions to help clarify each concept.
