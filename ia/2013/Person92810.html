Michael Orban's Summary

Who would imagine or even think that a soul can be destroyed? Mine was and I was witness to the event.
My book, ‘Souled Out, A Memoir of War and Inner Peace’ http://michaelorban.com/bookandreviews.html details 4 decades with Combat PTSD and finally experiencing love again.
I was drafted into the army in 1969. A lot of work and a year in the jungles of Vietnam made a good soldier of me. That year doomed me to a deep emotional and spiritual darkness. You see, I remember the day I was to leave Vietnam. I remember believing I was returning to life just as it had been before war. I did not expect nor understand the reactions that consumed me. I did not know who I was which led to many failures, vices and emptiness. I was not aware of combat ptsd, pts, combat stress or other names for what was ravaging my mind and soul.
“Peace Corps, Toughest Job You’ll Ever Love’! I felt life return and was filled with excitement. Gabon? Never heard of the place but it is in the jungle and that’s where I’m going. Africa saved me, it’s that simple. What the village people, pygmies and majestic nature of remote Africa did for my soul and mind would leave the wisest western psychiatrist envious and, most likely, perplexed. I did return home and PTSD was waiting for me and attacked ruthlessly. I admitted myself to the PTSD, Post Traumatic Stress Disorder program at the VA Hospital. A list of twenty six common reactions to trauma made me furious, my book explains why. Today I have devoted my life to helping those who struggle with PTSD, PTS, Post Traumatic Stress Disorder or whatever name you want to give it. My book, my writing and our presentations on PTSD are designed to fearlessly confront PTSD with a goal to help provide resolutions.
I would like to join with individuals and groups who have similar interests and invite them to join me.

Michael Orban's Experience

Author-Speaker on Combat PTSD

Author

February 2010 – Present (3 years 11 months) nationally

I write, speak on Radio and give presentations on Combat Post Traumatic Stress Disorder PTSD. Combat veterans are the greatest resource for education on combat PTSD.
I believe many mental health care professionals are entrenched in practices and theories (on combat ptsd) they cannot break out of. This limits their ability to treat PTSD. I recognize there are professionals who have successful treatment therapies. We must recognize that Combat PTSD is complicated and complex and not be afraid to change our thinking. Education and honest
conversation are critical to improving understanding and treatment for Combat PTSD. Letting go of treatments, theories and thinking that are antiquated and ineffective for Combat PTSD is critical.
Fearless development of new thinking and strategies should be exciting not fearful.
In news reports we mainly hear from mental health professionals who
speak of PTSD in percentages. It is impossible to arrive at a percentage of soldiers
and Veterans who suffer PTS, these percentages give us no understanding of PTSD nor a treatment plan only expose our lack of understanding.
Speaking in percentages increases the stigma in Veterans who suffer PTSD
and discourages them from seeking treatment.
The prevalent and heavy use of medications to anesthetize Veterans shows the failure old, worn out treatments. Many claim it is the Veteran with Combat PTSD who is resistant to treatment rather than confront the failure of the treatment. Treating Combat PTSD requires qualified trauma specialists. The VA Hospitals in Tomah and Milwaukee helped me. The most important element to successful treatment is for Veterans with Combat PTSD to participate in their healing. By honest participation in treatment they will find healing and add to the knowledge that will help improve treatment for Combat PTSD for other Veterans/ families who suffer combat PTSD and seek help. Be a warriors not a victim and help!


establishing successful strategies for healing.

I speak and give presentations on combat PTSD to inpatient combat Veterans at the VA Hospital in Milwaukee. In addition combat veterans are sent to me for mentoring. I have the ability and desire as a combat Veteran to communicate honestly on topics concerning readjustment from war. I know about creating a facade to hide true feelings after war, I did this for several decades. I know how damaging and empty of life that facade is. Michael Orban's Projects

  • Understanding Combat PTSD

    • 2012 to 2013
    Team Members: Michael Orban, Combat Door Gunner Jim Hackbarth, Combat Navy Corpsman Mark Foreman, Combat Medic Michael Maurer, Yon Christensen Vietnam Veteran

    For our public presentation on Understanding Combat PTSD we have assembled 5 Combat Veterans who have each experienced over 40 years of Combat PTSD. Our goal is to expose Combat PTSD and help remove the stigma and fear associated with Combat PTSD. Through honest sharing describing years of substance abuse, divorce, isolation, darkness of mind and spirit, inability to feel any new joy in life (along with other reactions to war) we explain how we came to again experience love for life and joy in our different healing paths. The healing path is unique to each individual as is PTSD unique to the individual. This powerful presentation provides hope and demonstrates the resilience of the human spirit. Each Combat Veteran shares the love and joy in a discipline he came to find healing, stone sculpting, golf, writing, writing poetry, Rieki, and energy stones.
    We discuss the importance of taking control of our PTSD after years of PTSD controlling us. Understanding and acknowledging the PTSD responses to war allows us resolve those experiences that can be resolved and accept those that are part of the experience of war and life.
