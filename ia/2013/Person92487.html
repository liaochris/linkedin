Michael Hugos' Summary

Delivering elegant solutions to complex problems with focus in supply chains, cloud computing, and use of game mechanics to redesign and reinvigorate business organizations. Application systems delivered and enhanced in 30-60-90 day cycles. Clients such as Starbucks Coffee, Microsoft Xbox Group, US Navy Medical Logistics Command and numerous smaller companies. Published author (see publications below), engaging speaker and panel moderator (see video clips on my website).

Recent examples:
-- Consulted with two startups providing healthcare supply chain services; mapped out business processes, defined technical architectures, and designed modular business application systems for roll out every 30 - 90 days to support growth in business volume and expansion of service offerings.

-- Designed and built interactive supply chain design and simulation application. It combines the fun of a video game with a serious learning experience for students and instructors of supply chain management. Currently used in graduate and undergraduate courses worldwide. New features and releases rolled out in 30-60-90 day cycles.

Award-winning chief information officer - CIO 100 Award, InformationWeek 500 Award, and Premier 100 Award. MBA from Northwestern University's Kellogg School of Management with joint major in finance and management information systems.

Specialties: Mentoring companies in bold and resourceful use of people, process and technology to deliver business agility and competitive advantage in a timely and decisive manner. Accomplished speaker and writer on the topics of agility, supply chain management, cloud computing and game mechanics in business.

Michael Hugos' Publications

  • Enterprise Games: Using Game Mechanics to Build a Better Business

    • O'Reilly Media
    • September 20, 2012
    Authors: Michael Hugos

    For the last 100 years the assembly line has been the dominant model for organizing business operations. But now high rates of change and the unpredictable nature of our real-time economy calls for something more flexible and responsive. Games offer field-tested models for people to organize and focus their actions and continuously learn and succeed in fast-paced and unpredictable environments. See pragmatic examples of how to use game mechanics to improve business operations from sales to supply chain management. Get ideas you can use in your own company. It's not a game, it's the future of work!

  • Business in the Cloud: What Every Business Needs to Know about Cloud Computing

    • John Wiley & Sons
    • October 20, 2010
    Authors: Michael Hugos, Derek Hulitzky

    A pragmatic discussion of how cloud computing can transform your business model. The spread of cloud computing is driving a cycle of creative destruction not seen in business since the introduction of the assembly line at the turn of the last century. Learn how to "variablize" your operating costs, lower your risk and increase your success rate in rolling out new and innovative products and services to keep up with your ever changing markets and customers. Appreciate both the technology and the business opportunities opened up by cloud computing.

  • Essentials of Supply Chain Management, 3rd Edition

    • John Wiley & Sons
    • July 26, 2011
    Authors: Michael Hugos

    "In clear and concise prose, this lean book outlines the most crucial tenets and concepts of supply chain management." -- Supply Chain Management Review. Reported by Amazon.com as best selling book on supply chains worldwide. Understand what a supply chain is and how it operates. See how to use information technology and emerging new technologies and practices to make your supply chain efficient and responsive to changing market demands.

  • CIO Best Practices: Enabling Strategic Value with Information Technology

    • John Wiley & Sons
    • October 15, 2010
    Authors: Michael Hugos, Joe Stenzel, Gary Cokins, Karl Schubert

    This practical resource provides best practice guidance on the key responsibilities of the CIOs and their indispensable executive leadership role in modern enterprises of all sizes and industries. It is the most definitive and important collection of best practices for achieving and exercising strategic IT leadership for CIOs, those who intend to become CIOs, and those who want to understand the strategic importance of IT for the entire enterprise. For anyone who wants to achieve better returns on their IT investments, CIO Best Practices, Second Edition presents the leadership skills and competencies required of a CIO addressing comprehensive enterprise strategic frameworks to fully leverage IT resources. Filled with real-world examples of CIO success stories.
