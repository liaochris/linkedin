Chris Klug's Summary

I am proud to have helped pioneer the sport of snowboarding and participate in its progress the past thirty years. After twenty years on the World Cup Snowboarding Circuit and finishing as the top American Snowboard Racer in seventh place in my third Winter Olympic Games in Vancouver, Canada in February 2010 and winning my eleventh US National Snowboarding title in March 2010, I retired from snowboard competition. As a ten year liver transplant recipient I am grateful to be alive and am committed through the Chris Klug Foundation to promoting life-saving organ and tissue donation in all that I do to help the more than 110,000 people currently awaiting a second chance across our country today. After twenty plus years living out of a suitcase on the road pursuing my Olympic dreams I am happy to be home in Aspen focusing my energies on my real estate business with Aspen Snowmass Sotheby’s International Realty and balancing my snowboard freeriding and mountaineering activities. I'm serious about my real estate business and I want to be serious about yours! I continue to challenge myself athletically. I am passionate about demonstrating what is possible after a transplant by participating in events like the Leadville 100. This past winter I competed in the Elk Mountains Grand Traverse and the inaugural Power of Four Randonee Ski Mountaineering Race and Power of Four Mtn. Bike Race. One of my future goals is to become the first transplant recipient to climb the Seven Summits. My motto is “Make the most of every turn and enjoy the ride.”

Chris Klug's Publications

  • To the Edge & Back; My Story from Transplant Survivor to Olympic Medalist

    • Avalon Publishing
    • January 2004
    Authors: Chris Klug, Steve Jackson

    Snowboarder Chris Klug battled for years to have his extreme sport accepted in the mainstream, rose through the World Cup ranks, and won a bronze medal at the 2002 Winter Olympics less than two years after undergoing a lifesaving liver transplant. Already an inspiration to millions worldwide, Klug, with award-winning journalist Steve Jackson, gives us his remarkable story of survival and triumph.
    During snowboarding’s 1996 World Cup season, Klug was diagnosed with primary schlerosing cholangitis, the same disease that would cause the death of football great Walter Payton. In April 2000, Klug’s liver began to fail so rapidly that doctors, fearing the worst, moved him up on the transplant list. Through surgery, recovery, and Olympic glory, every setback and small victory in Chris Klug’s ride is riveting. To the Edge and Back is a must-read for the millions of people in the organ donor community as well as all Americans looking for a story of an athlete who represents the best of the human spirit.

    “Chris Klug is a special inspiration to every person on the waiting list for an organ. He is a true American hero—as are all the individuals who decide to be donors and talk to their loved ones about their decision to donate the gift of life.” –Tommy G. Thompson,
    Secretary of the US Department of Health and Human Services

    Chris Klug is the first athlete ever to win an Olympic medal after successful organ transplant surgery. He has compiled four World Cup victories, three Grand Prix wins, five national titles, a U.S. Open victory, a bronze medal in the 2002 Winter Olympics, and is in training for the 2006 Winter Olympics in Turin, Italy. He was one of the eight Olympians chosen to carry the Ground Zero flag into the 2002 Winter Games Opening Ceremonies. He lives in Aspen, Colorado. Steve Jackson is a journalist from Evergreen, Colorado. His books include Lucky Lady, which won the 2003 Colorado Book Award for History.
