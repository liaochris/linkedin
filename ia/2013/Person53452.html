Holly Flesh's Experience

Chief of Operations

CIC (fka Cambridge Innovation Center)

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Commercial Real Estate industry

June 2012 – Present (1 year 5 months) Cambridge, MA

In the heart of Kendall Square, CIC is the largest flexible office facility for growing startups in the Greater Boston area. CIC offers emerging companies award-winning facilities and state-of-the-art business and technical services in a package designed to meet the needs of growing businesses. Currently, over 500 companies are located at CIC.

Principal

Cleantech Catalyst Advisors

February 2012 – Present (1 year 9 months) Greater Boston Area

We work with leadership teams to identify obstacles in their business model and go-to-market strategies. Additionally, we connect up-and-coming companies with strategic partners to reach a global audience and provide access to funding opportunities.

Organizing Committee Member

Global Cleantech Meet-Up

July 2012 – October 2012 (4 months) Boston

The Global Cleantech Meet-Up is an "unconference" connecting all pillars of the cleantech ecosystem: major energy users, strategic partners, startups, leading companies and investors. I recruited large corporate strategic partners and international startups to present at the Global Cleantech Meet-Up.

Sponsors included the Mass. Clean Energy Center, Fraunhofer Center for Sustainable Energy Systems, Mass. Technology Transfer Center, and the New England Clean Energy Council.

President

Innoventé USA

Renewables & Environment industry

January 2011 – January 2012 (1 year 1 month)

Innoventé transforms local feedstocks of organic waste from a liability into distributed energy. The Canadian-based company has a 25-year power purchase agreement with Hydro-Québec for electricity produced at its combined heat and power plant in St. Patrice, Québec.

• Strategic partnerships: Sought out potential Fortune 500 partners with complementary expertise and global distribution channels including engineering, oil and utility firms. Leveraged existing relationships to position Innoventé in US market as a mature company with production-ready technology.

• Investment: Built personal relationships with top private placement firms financing renewable energy projects in the US and Canada. Kept investors engaged with Innoventé’s progress.

• Regulatory pathways: Advocated on MA Organics Task Force Committee to increase use of organic residues for electricity production and to minimize landfilling.

• Messaging: Promoted Innoventé at biomass and clean energy conferences, accepted as presenter four times. Sought out as panelist due to passion for cleantech, willingness to mine experiences at previous company and deep knowledge of industry. Shaped public relations efforts through press release and website content.
