Matt Haines' Summary

For more than two decades Matt has held management and technologist positions with several leading companies including HP, Motorola and Inktomi where he helped to bring innovative solutions to market in the areas of Cloud Services, streaming media, digital rights management, and Internet infrastructure. Prior to that, Matt held a number of academic faculty and research positions with NASA Langley Research Center, the College of William and Mary, and the University of Wyoming where he taught Computer Science and ran research labs in the area of parallel and distributed computing. Matt holds an MBA in Finance and Entrepreneurship and a PhD in Computer Science.

Matt Haines' Experience

Vice President, Engineering

HP Cloud Services

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; Information Technology and Services industry

October 2011 – August 2013 (1 year 11 months) Fort Collins, Colorado Area

Matt led a global group of devops-oriented service teams to build, deploy, and support HP's new public Infrastructure as a Service (IaaS) offering called HP Cloud. HP Cloud currently includes HP Cloud Compute, HP Cloud Object Storage, HP Cloud Block Storage, and HP Cloud CDN, all tied together with HP Cloud Identify Service. Matt's teams leveraged OpenStack IaaS projects (Nova, Glance, Quantum, Cinder, Swift, and Keystone) in developing the HP Cloud through core contributions and HP-differentiated extensions.

Senior Director of Engineering, Consumer Software Applications and Services

Hewlett-Packard

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; HPQ; Information Technology and Services industry

April 2010 – October 2011 (1 year 7 months)

Matt provided strategy and direction for all software programs within the Consumer Applications and Services GBU at HP. This included responsibility for Windows and webOS software programs spanning notebook and desktop platforms.

Director of Engineering, Desktop Software Organization

Hewlett-Packard

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; HPQ; Information Technology and Services industry

October 2009 – April 2010 (7 months)

Led the formation of the Desktop Software Organization to unify all software efforts for the Desktop Organization (DTO). Responsible for establishing strategy and procedures for effective software development with teams from Fort Collins, Houston, Philadelphia, Cupertino, and Shanghai. Re-organized in April to lead webOS engineering for the Consumer Software Applications and Services business unit.

Engineering Lab Manager

Hewlett-Packard

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; HPQ; Information Technology and Services industry

October 2006 – October 2009 (3 years 1 month)

Matt oversaw consumer product engineering and development activities including hardware design, software development, quality assurance, and program management for the HP Home Server group, producing a number of storage-related products including the award-winning Mediasmart server. In a welcome departure from his software background, Matt also led the hardware R&D team that designed and built several custom consumer hardware platforms.

Co-founder

Quattro Vino Inc.

October 2005 – October 2006 (1 year 1 month)

Matt teamed up with his wife Stefanie to help her launch an online wine company called Quattro Vino. The concept was to provide simple wine pairings in a four-pack configuration that aligned wines based on grapes, regions, or taste profiles. Matt leveraged a then-emerging web applications framework called Rails to build a custom e-commerce site that supported both online and in-store sales. The software also provided custom inventory and shipping management. By building, deploying and monitoring the software on a continual basis, Matt saw the early stages of a devops-oriented approach to application development. In addition to the site, Matt also helped to optimize the Adwords spend through common Search Engine Optimization (SEO) techniques. Stefanie continued to run the company for a full 5 years.

Principal Architect

Haines Technology Partners

May 2002 – October 2005 (3 years 6 months)

Matt started and run a consulting group focused on emerging web application architecture and security concerns. Most engagements started with an evaluation of problematic web application architectures and evolved into helping companies transition to lightweight web application frameworks and agile software development methodologies. Matt helped one notable customer, The World Bank, build a web-based application for helping small and medium businesses in emerging countries (smetoolkit.org). Matt also worked with a number of venture capital companies to perform due-diligence on funding targets.

Director, Systems Architecture

Motorola Mobility

Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; MMI; Consumer Electronics industry

April 2001 – May 2002 (1 year 2 months) Greater San Diego Area

Matt joined a Motorola-backed startup called Aerocast to design and deliver high-quality, digitally protected streaming media to cable broadband users. Matt's architecture team focused on the underlying designs for two of the system's critical components: streaming media and digital rights management. In each case, Matt's team (in partnership with architects in Motorola's set-top box division) provided innovative yet realistic designs for the company to develop.

Principal Software Architect

Inktomi

Public Company; 501-1000 employees; Computer Software industry

June 1997 – May 2001 (4 years) San Francisco Bay Area

Matt joined Inktomi during the formation of the original Traffic Server team, working with a small team of talented engineers to design and build the largest and fastest Internet caching proxy server using an advanced, high-speed network architecture. Once the product was built Matt moved into the mode of evangelizing the technology by working with outside partners and customers to leverage the technology into new areas. Results included streaming media caching, content syndication, and mobile phone integration. Worked with Real Networks, AOL, Nokia, and other partners.

Assistant Professor

University of Wyoming

Educational Institution; 10,001+ employees; Higher Education industry

August 1995 – July 1999 (4 years) Laramie, WY

Following a post-doctoral research program at NASA, Matt joined the Computer Science faculty at the University of Wyoming. In addition to teaching at both undergraduate and graduate levels, Matt led a successful research program backed by several national grants including the prestigious Career Young Investigator Award from the National Science Foundation (NSF).

Research Scientist, ICASE

NASA Langley Research Center

Government Agency; 1001-5000 employees; Aviation & Aerospace industry

July 1993 – June 1995 (2 years)

As a Research Scientist with the Institute for Computer Applications in Science and Engineering (ICASE), Matt led a research program focused on distributed software systems and high-performance thread libraries. Matt collaborated with academic and government researchers from around the world and led a team of research interns from the College of William and Mary.
