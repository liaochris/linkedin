Kimberly Whitfield's Summary

I am a Business Systems Analyst and Instructor with a Masters degree in Instructional Design. I have over 14 years of experience which include applying analysis, design, development, implementation and evaluation principles of instructional technology to provide software and training solutions for state judicial personnel. I develop instructional materials and provide group, one-on-one, on-site and remote instruction. I analyze, test and document hardware and software applications, and write technical and training manuals for software applications. I also provide 24 hour helpdesk and training support to personnel.
My goal is to work in Instructional Technology and Distance Education Programs.

Specialties: I have worked with Learning Management Systems and online collaboration software such as Blackboard, Moodle, Web CT, WebEx and GoToMeeting. I have a Web Design Certification and am a Certified Business Systems Analyst. I have also worked with Adobe Captivate, Fireworks, Flash, Dreamweaver and Camtasia, All Microsoft Office products including Project, Access and Publisher and many other types of software applications.

    This article describes the methods that were used to evaluate the “Blackboard Basics” and “Teaching in Blackboard” courses that are currently being offered by the Office of e-Learning at a southeastern university in the United States.   • Involuntary Commitment Application: An Online Training Module

    • Information Systems Education Journal, 10(3) pp 18-34. http://isedj.org/2012-10/ ISSN: 1545-679X
    • May 1, 2012
    Authors: Kimberly Whitfield

    Abstract: The use of Web-based technology has enabled many government and corporate training divisions to reach more learners than ever before. Institutions are restructuring their budgets, obtaining funding from governments and foundations to fund resources needed to increase online learning offerings. While online learning is increasing, questions arise as to the quality when compared to traditional face-to-face instruction. Research indicates that online learning can be more effective than face-to-face instruction. The purpose of this project is to analyze training sessions used by a state judicial system in southeastern United States for their Involuntary Commitment (IC) training to determine if self-directed online training is a viable solution to replace the current face-to-face training program and if so, design, develop and evaluate a pilot program for online training material.
    Whitfield, K. Y. (2012). Involuntary Commitment Application: An Online Training Module. Information Systems Education Journal, 10(3) pp 18-34. http://isedj.org/2012-10/ ISSN: 1545-679X. (A preliminary version appears in The Proceedings of ISECON 2011)
