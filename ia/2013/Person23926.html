Christian Jorg's Summary

Entrepreneur, C-Level Executive and Board Member. Has developed and implemented successful growth strategies in multiple companies and been several time founder and start-up CEO. Has significant deal and money raising experience. Leader in developing new business models around content enabling both publishers and brands by pioneering content marketing. In addition to day to day responsibilities as CEO, Opentopic, serves on a number of Boards/Advisory Boards for start-ups and venture and investment firms and is frequent speaker at conferences on Digital Media.

Specialties: Start-ups, operating experience/P&L responsibility in public and private companies, developing new business models, partnership and business development deals, international business expansion, M&A transactions. SaaS business models.

Christian Jorg's Experience

CEO

Opentopic

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Internet industry

February 2012 – Present (1 year 11 months) New York

Opentopic is a fast growing SaaS enterprise company providing an individually updated and relevant content hub and distribution platform. It empowers companies of any size to be efficient and innovative marketers and communicators by allowing them to find, curate and distribute the most relevant content and putting it to use for their business goals in a cost-effective way. The company has built significant traction with customers in five countries and offices in New York, Paris and Munich. Visit opentopic.com.

Senior Vice President, Head of Digital

Universal Music Group

Public Company; 5001-10,000 employees; VIVEF; Music industry

July 2007 – January 2010 (2 years 7 months) New York

Oversaw all Island Def Jam business activities in the online and mobile space. Also responsible for expanding the company’s business beyond its traditional focus by building new revenue streams and working with UMG on setting corporate digital strategy. During tenure IDJ launched a number of high impact new major initiatives and created a full digital media platform of websites and social media including iPhone apps, video, widgets and more to monetize content through e-commerce, advertising and brand integration on the web and mobile. Was named one of Billboard’s Top Ten Mobile Executives in 2008.

Executive Vice President

Acotel Interactive

Public Company; 201-500 employees; Media Production industry

October 2005 – July 2007 (1 year 10 months) New York

Executive Vice President at Acotel Interactive, one of the world’s leading direct to consumer mobile entertainment companies, based in New York.
As part of its founding team, built the company from start-up to one of the top players in the mobile entertainment industry. Its 2006 growth was the major reason for tripling its public parent company’s market cap (Acotel.MI). Responsibilities included product and marketing strategy including production and marketing of original content in music, film and games as well as licensing deals with third party content providers. Also involved in negotiations with telecom operators such as Verizon, T-Mobile, at&t etc. and in working with the compny's gateway provider MBlox on optimizing subscriber sign up and retention. Acotel Interactive is now exceeding $150 m in revenue and is significantly profitable.

Co-Founder and Co-CEO

E-Magine Entertainment

May 1999 – December 2004 (5 years 8 months) New York

Co-Founded E-Magine Entertainment, a New York based record label with an internet marketing, on-line community and e-commerce focus.
Raised three rounds of equity financing from private and institutional investors for a total of $ 4.5 million and developed the business, responsible for operations, finance, administration, business affairs and development. Negotiated company’s artist, distribution and marketing agreements.

Senior Vice President, Business Affairs and Development

Imax Corporation

Public Company; 201-500 employees; IMAX; Motion Pictures and Film industry

August 1995 – April 1999 (3 years 9 months) New York

Hired to turn around Imax subsidiary Ridefilm Corp. and build Imax’s Business Affairs and Development departments. Served on Imax’s R&D Committee and on the Board of Directors of Imax subsidiary Sonics Corp. Reported to Imax's CEO.
Developed the Imax Business Affairs and Development department from scratch as well as Imax’s theme park business overseeing a team of BA/legal employees. In addition had first approval for all deals Imax sales force was working on before final CEO sign-off. Engineered turnaround of Ridefilm division from a substantial loss in 1995 to profitability in 1997.
Negotiated film, theatre and product development deals for Imax with Hitachi, Sony, ITT, Sega, Universal, Mainframe Entertainment and many others.

Vice President, New Technologies, BMG Entertainment

Bertelsmann

Privately Held; 10,001+ employees; Publishing industry

August 1990 – August 1995 (5 years 1 month) New York

Spearheaded BMG’s participation in new technologies, founding a New Technologies division. Responsible for developing new businesses related to new technologies including interactive entertainment, video games, on-line services, and internet related activities.
Targeted and completed BMG’s equity investments of $12 million in technology start-ups. This included the initial funding for ION, later part of Gracenote which was sold to Sony Corp. in 2008 for $260 m.. Served on Board of Directors and as strategic advisor to top management. Had key role in building BMG’s multi-media distribution and international infrastructures. Initiated BMG’s strategy for Internet presence leading to the development of Web pages for both the record club and labels. Served as advisor on new media to Chairman of BMG and CEO of Bertelsmann AG. Served as BMG’s representative on the RIAA’s (Recording Association of America) Technology committee.
