David Stark's Summary

Primary Contribution Areas
To supply exceptional educational experiences to diverse undergraduate and graduate students in various subjects within Judeo-Christian Studies, including: Biblical Theology, Hermeneutics, New Testament, Old Testament, and Second Temple Judaism. To provide excellent administration for courses and degree programs offered in non-traditional formats. To advance scholarship in Judeo-Christian Studies by consistent and leading-edge publications.

Specialties

Primary research interests include hermeneutics (especially in the Second Temple period), reception-historical study of Jewish and Christian scripture, Greek grammar, Pauline studies, and the discourse grammar of biblical literature. Some other interests include excellence in academic writing, the use of technology in the academy, pedagogical development, and theological interpretation.
