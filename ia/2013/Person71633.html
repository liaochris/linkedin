Under The Grape Tree (Self-employed)

Self-Employed; Myself Only; Wine and Spirits industry

April 2008 – Present (5 years 9 months) Fort Mitchell, Kentucky

I started this blog as a way to document the assorted goings-on of my job in conjunction with my ever-burgeoning need for self-expression. I rant and rave, and track all my tasting notes, as well as make snap commentary on all the various hulabaloo that is the wine industry.

Senior Wine Buyer

D.E.P.'s Fine Wine & Spirits

September 2002 – July 2012 (9 years 11 months) Covington, Kentucky and Fort Thomas, Kentucky

For ten years I was the Senior Wine Buyer for two “bridge” stores in the Greater Cincinnati/Northern Kentucky metropolitan area. These stores grew exponentially under my watch, from roughly 400 selections to now, boasting over 3,000 selections. I was an integral part in the day-to-day operations within my department as well as providing direction and support in the areas of marketing, staff training, social media, customer resource management and inventory control. I was responsible for 95% of all wine buying, and it was my vision that created one of the region’s best selections of Spanish, Italian and South American wines alongside our domestic offerings, the area’s best pricing, and the friendliest and most knowledgeable staff in the Tri-State.

WINE BUYER/MANAGER

Chateau Pomije

April 2001 – September 2002 (1 year 6 months)

My first real foray into wine beverage retail, I came on board this longstanding tradition in Cincinnati as the manager of Chateau Pomije’s retail aspect of their business. A restaurant whose wine list was a store next door featuring over 500 offerings from around the world, I oversaw the selection of these wines, along with coordinating all store and restaurant wine-related events with the collaboration of the restaurant manager and chef, inventory management, implementation and supervising of the store’s Wine Club, coordinating deliveries to customers, customer emails, staffing and training the retail aspect of the store and wine training for the restaurant personnel.

ASSISTANT MANAGER

THE LITTLE STORE

August 2000 – March 2001 (8 months) Vandalia, Ohio

During my transition period from restaurant to retail, I came on board The Little Store as an assistant to the owner, Eric Jerardi as well as daytime manager of the deli and inventory manager for the wine. I assisted the owner in stocking and maintaining the shelves and warehouse, helped train staff with all wine related issues, and supervised the staff while the owner travelled.

BAR MANAGER

Cafe Boulevard

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Restaurants industry

July 1997 – August 2000 (3 years 2 months) Dayton, Ohio Area

I was one of the original hires at Café Boulevard, a new French Bistro-themed restaurant in Dayton, Ohio’s historic Oregon District located in Downtown Dayton. I was initially hired as a bartender, yet was promoted to bartender after only 2 months on the job. I was then tasked with creating and managing the restaurant’s wine list, as well as supervising and training the front-of-house staff, meeting with sales reps, managing the restaurant, processing end-of-day reports, scheduling, and implementing a Web presence to promote our business online. I was also present and helped usher in the expansion of the restaurant with the addition of Le Monde, a high-end cocktail bar adjacent to the restaurant. The restaurant became Boulevard Haus in 2010.
