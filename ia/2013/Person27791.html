John Berard's Summary

Credible Context is a communications consultancy based on the insight that effective marketing -- of products, services, even the company itself -- requires more than commitment, authenticity and transparency. Advocacy and persuasion must be contextually credible.

Consumers and business customers want to rely on companies they can trust and trust is linked to consistent, beneficial performance.

Credible Context seeks to help companies reveal their context and power market-making messaging. The result is the ability to identify, align, arm and activate supporters.

Trust built on a strong foundation -- a credible context -- is increasingly important as more and more businesses are built on information.

My more than 30-year career has been forged in three "company towns," each the center of its own universe. I began in the financial and corporate communications landscape of New York City, then spent 15 years in the legal and issues-driven Washington, D.C. and since 1996 have been based in San Francisco supporting the marketing, primarily, of technology companies.

Credible Context's goal is to build a company that breaks new, customer-centric ground. I seek to help clients win, keep and expand market share. My skill set is a mix of positioning, messaging, personality management, influencer endorsement, media attention and customer success.

My methodology is to help companies tap into the market power of who they are; creating communications in context.

Specialties

advertising and public relations, corporate and marketing communications for technology, health care, financial services, consumer and consumer electronics companies.

Credible Context (Sole Proprietorship)

Sole Proprietorship; Myself Only; Public Relations and Communications industry

March 2009 – Present (4 years 10 months) San Francisco Bay Area

The intense interest in the 2008 presidential election accelerated a key change in the way candidates - and companies - need to communicate.

Beyond the 24-hour news cycle, the ability for individuals to be heard on the Internet and the blurred line between information and entertainment, what is said is now judged without mercy against the context of what has been said and done.

More than frequency, authenticity and empathy, effective communications must be built on credible context.

This new era requires a new approach. Messaging needs to be more consistent; it is better to run counter to the current news agenda than to change too often to meet it. Story lines can't be drawn from thin, raw material. Missteps cannot be seen as matters of policy, but as personal failings.

Even though credible context cannot be established retroactively, it can be uncovered. Acknowledging and moving to meet the changed communications environment will help companies avoid being undone by history.
