Dick Fleming's Summary

Chairman, Community Development Ventures, Inc., a 20-year old professional services consulting firm in public/private partnerships, community & economic development, entrepreneurship, resource & organizational development, housing, arts & cuture, and urban education/charter schools. Also currently Chair Board of Trustees of a national economic development and environmental initiative, Clean Economy Solutions, based in Washington and funded by the Rockefeller Brothers Fund and other foundations.

Over 30 years of CEO and practitioner experience in private/public partnership initiatives in Atlanta, Denver & St. Louis in public policy, regional strategy and problem-solving, community and economic development, as well as sub-cabinet grant-making roles with U.S. Department of HUD in Washington and a developer with The Rouse Company in Baltimore, MD.

Served as President and CEO of Saint Louis RCGA (Regional Chamber & Growth Association) from 1994 to 2012, and two similar CEO roles in Denver from 1980 to 1993. Also served in philanthropy as Executive Director of the State of St. Louis Foundation from 1994 to 2012, and founding President and CEO of Denver Civic Ventures, Inc. from 1980 to 1987.

Success-driven, action-oriented leader and "civic innovation broker"with superb strategic thinking and planning skills, who builds collaborative relationships and makes genuine connections with people. Proficient at problem-solving and navigating through obstacles and pursuing opportunities to achieve results.

President & CEO

St. Louis Regional Chamber & Growth Association (RCGA)

Nonprofit; 11-50 employees; Nonprofit Organization Management industry

1994 – 2012 (18 years) St. Louis

Served as President and CEO of St. Louis Regional Chamber & Growth Association (RCGA), the 16-county bi-state chamber of commerce and regional economic development organization, from 1994 to 2012. Concurrently served as Publisher of award-winning monthly business magazine, St. Louis Commerce Magazine, and as Executive Director of the State of St. Louis Foundation, a multi million dollar regional charitable public-purpose foundation:

•Revitalized/restructured 175-year old organization, enhancing the organization's funding base from $3 million to over $10 million per year, with highest member & investor retention rates in the nation.

•Conducted 3 successful multi-million dollar, 5-year regional economic development campaigns, raising over $70 million.

•Recruited and developed top senior executive team to lead both the chamber and regional economic development initiatives.

•Worked with a diverse set of 125 St. Louis CEO leaders to revitalize and recruit the Saint Louis RCGA Board composed of business, education, labor and civc leaders.

•Undertook long term community-wide Regional Strategic Planning efforts in 1995, 2000, and 2009, leading to major regional initiatives by both the chamber and economic development arms of RCGA.

•Designed and implemented Greater St. Louis, Inc. as the affiliated regional economic development arm of the St. Louis RCGA, served as its CEO since 1995:

-Negotiated collaborative regional economic development approach to economic development in Regional Economic Development Network, recruiting over 112,000 new jobs.
-St. Louis RCGA named one of Top Ten Economic Development Organizations in the Nation in 2006, 2007, and 2011.
-Launched successful BioBelt initiative attracting hundreds of millions of dollars in venture capital and developing a wide range of entrepreneurial mentoring, funding, and commercialization initiatives.

•St. Louis RCGA won Top Regional Brand Award from Public Relations Society of America, 2007

President & CEO

Metro Denver Chamber of Commerce

1986 – 1993 (7 years) Denver, CO

Assumed role of president & CEO of the Denver Chamber during Colorado’s massive economic decline in late 1980s. Business and civic leadership pursued multiple strategic actions to turn around the economy of the State and region. Helped move Denver and Colorado economies from crisis to leading the nation in economic and civic development.

•Played lead private sector role, in partnership with Mayor Federico Pena and Governor Roy Romer, driving major economic development initiatives, including two successful airport elections, negotiating with airlines, and obtaining federal funding to develop $5 billion Denver International Airport.

•Led Chamber’s and Greater Denver Corporation’s successful efforts for:
-New Colorado Convention Center, centerpiece of the Downtown Area Plan
-Passage of a quarter-billion dollar city bond initiative
-Metro-wide referendums establishing 6-county funding for Scientific & Cultural Facilities District; 6-county Stadium District; and the recruitment of Major League Baseball to Denver.

•Designed and developed the Greater Denver Corporation, Inc. (GDC), serving as its CEO from 1987 to 1993.

•Designed and implemented the 52-member Metro Denver Network to improve intra-regional competition in economic development. Denver Post called the Network “the economic development equivalency of a disarmament agreement.”

Chamber and GDC won several IAMC recognitions at Top Ten Economic development Organization in nation. GDC netted record 240,000 new jobs over its two multi-year campaigns.
