Jon Paul's Summary

Jon Paul functions as the right hand for entrepreneurs, enabling them to do what they do best. Four ventures, with credit to terrific leaders and teams, exceeded beyond even “hockey stick” projections:

• A pharmaceutical firm that the bank wanted to liquidate for $5 million sold 20 years later for $5 billion.
• A toy manufacturer grew from $2 million to $30 million in 3 years; the parent used the profits to buy a much larger competitor and merge them together.
• A telecom wireless provider climbed from falling to $2 a share to sell for $65 a share in 2 years.
• A disease state management firm sold for $75 million less than a decade after launching.

With a finance background, but wired like a marketing person, Jon works well in dynamic environments where change is needed. Recently as interim CFO, he helped Brook Furniture Rental emerge stronger from the recession, refinancing their bank debt and helping take out $3 million in costs to boost profits.

Jon gets to the heart of numbers quickly to glean insights. He is a creative thinker, drawing from experiences where companies made dramatic shifts in strategy and disrupted industries. Jon was the fastest to earn the black belt in sales training by the Samurai Business Group. He is an avid reader, looking for ideas he can share to help people develop their God given talents.

Jon is a connector willing to help others. Clients and nonprofits benefit from his network. Jon feels very strongly in giving back and devotes time to a variety of nonprofits either on the board or as a member, including his church, Christian groups, social service ministries and associations.

While each situation is unique, Jon finds there is often a common process to success:

- Listen- to people (good questions) and numbers (where and how to look)
- Light- uncover hidden opportunities to grow or cut costs
- Lift- people and organization to higher results
- Life- enable leaders to do what they enjoy that gives them energy
