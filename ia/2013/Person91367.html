Melodie Shrader's Summary

Melodie Shrader is an independent legislative consultant with an extensive background in non-profit management and Kentucky politics. In all, Ms. Shrader provides her clients with 18 years of management and political experience. Today’s lobbying effort requires the skills to build coalitions, develop grassroots advocacy when necessary and above all approach every legislative issue with a business plan for success. Of course, a thorough knowledge of the governmental process and a familiarity with key players are benefits that she brings to each of her clients.

Ms. Shrader has served on several state and local boards including the Governor's Advisory Commission on Tourism during two administrations, United Way, the Greater Paducah Economic Development Council and West Kentucky Corporation Board of Directors.

Ms. Shrader received an associate degree in communications from the Henderson Community College, a baccalaureate degree from the University of Evansville and is currently completing her Masters in Business Administration at the University of Kentucky. She is the mother of two adult sons, Johnathon and Brandon. She and her husband, Horace, currently live in Henderson, Kentucky and maintain an office in Frankfort, Kentucky.

Specialties

Non-profit Association Management, Government Relations, Private Sector Health Care, Medicaid, Deficit Reduction Act, Government Business Development

Big Rivers Electric Cooperative

October 2003 – December 2004 (1 year 3 months)

Big Rivers Electric Corporation is an electric generation and transmission cooperative (G&T) headquartered in Henderson, Kentucky and owned by its three member system distribution cooperatives— Jackson Purchase Energy Corporation, headquartered in Paducah; Kenergy Corp., headquartered in Henderson; and Meade County Rural Electric Cooperative Corporation, headquartered in Brandenburg.

Big Rivers supplies the wholesale power needs of its three member systems and markets surplus power to non-member utilities and power markets. These members system cooperatives provide retail electric power and energy to more than 110,000 residential, commercial, and industrial customers in portions of 22 Western Kentucky counties.

Big Rivers owns a generating capacity of 1,459 megawatts (MW) in four plants. Total power capacity is 1,854 MW, including rights to Henderson Municipal Power and Light (HMP&L) Station Two and contracted capacity from Southeastern Power Administration (SEPA).
