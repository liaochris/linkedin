E. L. (Betsy) Smith, PhD's Summary

I enable leaders to lead courageously and strategically for maximum results. My desire is for leaders to acknowledge and employ their strengths to insure their followers have the same opportunities. I motivate leaders to energize others by relating to them on a personal level instilling the philosophy that a positive attitude creates ongoing results.

Whether wearing my Coach, Speaker, or Author hat – or all of them at once – when I speak, people do a whole lot more than just listen. The Founder and Principal of E.L. Smith Consulting, I not only understand what people need to achieve short- and long-term success, my innovative and fun (yes, FUN) style motivates them to actually learn – and apply – those strategies. Her clients have included entrepreneurs, senior executives, emerging leaders, and the executive leadership and boards of several non-profit organizations.

How can I help you? Good question!

As a certified leadership coach I have a fast-paced, purpose-driven style to help you achieve personal and/or professional goals.

As a Speaker & Seminar Leader, I engage and inform you and/or your audience(s) on a range of topics, including: Leadership Development, Team Building, Human Performance Management and Change Management.

I co-authored Second Blooming for Women: Growing a Life That Matters After Fifty and have numerous articles in well-respected magazines and academic journals, including one for which I and my co-author earned a Bronze Award in the 2009 National Mature Media Awards.

A graduate of Leadership Florida, Leadership Pensacola, and the Cornell University Institute of Executive Leadership.I served in numerous leadership roles in Pensacola FL; chaired the Pensacola Symphony, Health Initiatives Task Force of the Greater Pensacola Area Chamber of Commerce, the Pensacola Women’s Alliance; and was the Lead Organizer of 2006 International Coach Federation’s Research Symposium.

Texas A&M University

Educational Institution; 10,001+ employees; Higher Education industry

September 1983 – August 1985 (2 years)

When I decided to pursue my doctorate, I quite my job, withdrew all of my retirement funds and entered Texas A&M as a full time student. My dissertation topic was to assess The Levels of Satisfaction of Community Education Students. My research partner and I surveyed community education participants and got a 93% return of our surveys.

I defended my dissertation almost two years exactly from the day I took my first class. My wish is that everyone can enjoy their doctoral work as much as I did.
