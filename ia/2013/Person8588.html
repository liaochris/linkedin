Annya Stoddart's Summary

I have worked in the health and wellness industry for over 20 years. I started off training as an aerobics and gym instructor and then running my own classes and personal training for about 10 years (alongside full time jobs at Center Parcs and then at Otley College).

After finding that I was losing my enthusiasm for teaching Step Classes and pumping iron I decided to train as a Yoga teacher. I found that this was much more satisfying to teach and actually built up my energy rather than drained it.

With the Yoga course in its final stages I launched into four years of Acupuncture studies. I started up my own Clinic in Stowmarket on 2008 and I now specialise in treating pain.

Specialties

Pain relief. Stress reduction. Fertility Support. Movement therapy. Meditation. Giving talks. Workshops.

Annya Stoddart's Projects

  • Life Changing treatment

    • 2011 to 2012
    Team Members: Annya Stoddart

    A WAR veteran who was in so much pain he had to take 19 painkillers a day has been taken off all his medication by his GP and feels he has got his life back.

    Bernard Martin, 85, fought for 3 years in Malaysia where he was blown up twice, losing a toe on one occasion.

    He suffered from continual headaches and woke up at 3am in pain, but is now pain free after a course of acupuncture and alphastim. Alphastim does not use needles, but a very mild electric stimulation, very similar to that which the body itself uses. Like acupuncture it communicates with the brain to switch off pain.

    Great grandfather Bernard, said: “Acupuncture has literally given me my life back.

    “Before I went I would drag my right foot, now I can bend, it made so much difference to me. I used to be on so many painkillers a day I would rattle! Now I take none, my GP is happy for me to stop the painkillers, and my pain is virtually non existent.

    “Some people think acupuncture could be mumbo jumbo, but it is has changed my life. I was not wasting money as a pensioner, it was money very well spent. You can’t put a price on health, it’s no good being the richest man in the cemetery.”

    Bernard, a talented amateur wood carver, found it hard to continue his hobby before treatment because of the pain and trouble moving. Now he enjoys working in his workshop, creating modern art, totem poles and pretty wooden animals.

    Annya Stoddart, who runs the Pain Relief Clinic in Stowmarket town centre, said: “One of the things I love about acupuncture is seeing and hearing the change in patients, sometimes after just one session, sometimes after a few.

    “Having lost a toe in a bombing Bernard’s posture was out of alignment, and regular episodes of malaria had weakened him. He was suffering from shoulder, back, arm, leg pain, and had headaches 24/7.

    “The pain has gone, there are no malaria re-occurrences and no headaches. What I love to hear is when he says “I am as fit as a butcher’s dog!”

  • Blooming in old age

    • March 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Annya Stoddart

    One of my patients, Jackie, used to teach flower arranging until arthritis in her hands made it too painful to continue. She’d heard about my acupuncture practice from some friends, and as the arthritis impeded more and more of her day-to-day activities she was willing to try anything to achieve a good quality of life.

    Arthritis had changed the shape of her fingers and thumbs, which impeded the flow of blood around her knuckles. Once we’d had a good chat about her symptoms and what she would like my treatments to improve, we decided on a combined course of Alphastim and Acupuncture.

    Alphastim is a treatment that uses microcurrent electrotherapy to calm down your nervous system when it is sending out lots of pain signals. The very gentle current is just like the natural electricity used in your body to make your muscles and your heart work properly – so it’s particularly effective at providing natural pain relief, without any need for chemical painkillers and their unwanted side-effects such as drowsiness. And if you’re a bit afraid of needles, it’s a good alternative to get the similar benefits of acupuncture, such as pain relief and extra energy.

    After a couple of sessions, Jackie was amazed to find all but two of her most painful fingers were now pain-free. With further sessions, we’re working on ridding the pain from these fingers as well.

    Arthritis also meant walking was also a problem for Jackie before she came to me. So week before she was about to set off for a holiday in Cornwall, we did a quick acupuncture treatment (each session takes just 45 minutes) to relieve the pain in her feet. When she came back I was really pleased to hear that, thanks to the acupuncture, she was able to go walking without any pain whatsoever.
