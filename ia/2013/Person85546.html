Margaret Lulic's Publications

  • Living Your Fullest Life After Fifty: Powerful Questions & Intimate Conversations

    • April 22, 2013
    Authors: Margaret Lulic

    You deserve the fullest possible life after fifty. Too often our culture steers us towards asking limited practical questions about money and wishes for those next years. There is a lot more to a full life than those two important but limited items. Those questions are like looking at trees and missing the forest. This short free ebook explores how to see the forest and the trees for a full enriching life. It is featured on my homepage at www.mlulic.com. Email me with questions about related coaching options at Margaret@mlulic.com. May you live to the fullest.

  • Home - Inspired by Love and Beauty

    • Blue Edge Publishing
    • October 2010
    Authors: Margaret Lulic

    After decades of coaching and consulting within business, I have found that I wanted to also talk about a philosophy of home. We are so stressed by our workplaces, that to maintain health and creativity, we really need a restorative home. Homes change lives. They can heal and inspire. This is a comprehensive insight into your home and what it can tell you about yourself. Stories, reflective questions, exercises, and practical suggestions say it all.

    "Few things are as critically important to our health and happiness
    as our homes. This book should be required reading for all adults.
    It will transform how you deliberate, decorate, and dwell in your home."

    Bill Manahan, MD, Assistant Professor Emeritus
    University of Minnesota Academic Health Center
    Past President, American Holistic Medical Association

    In a review by Mary Ann Grossman she said, “So, how do we rekindle our love of our homes and create spaces that nurture our souls? Lulic tells us how in her book, part memoir, part spirituality, part common sense, part decorating guide, part exercises that help you make decisions with a little discussion of our culture thrown in. I've rarely read a book that incorporates so much in just 280 pages.”
