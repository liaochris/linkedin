Christopher Herring's Summary

Effective July 1, 2013 - Christopher C. Herring is the new President - Executive Director of the Alamo City Chamber of Commerce leading one of the nation's oldest historically black chambers of commerce. He assumes his duty as the organization is celebrating 75 years of service within San Antonio, Texas, the nation's 7th largest city. Additionally, Christopher is the Mayor of San Antonio's appointee to the Small Business Advocacy Committee (SBAC) overseeing the Small Business Economic Development Advocacy (SBEDA) Program subcontracting goals. He serves as the Chairman of the SBAC's 5-Yr Contracting Diversity Plan Accountability Subcommittee .

Christopher is retired 20-year military officer; business owner, business deveoper and community organizer. His family are the owners of New Horizons School, LLC, a San Antonio Preschool, helping to grow America's next generation of leaders. He is a past Board of Director for the West San Antonio Chamber of Commerce, past Chaiman of the Boards for Joy Keeper (Breast Cancer Patient Support) and Alamo Arts Ballet Theatre.

Christopher is the National Director for MatchRate PLUS' nonprofit division. He created the merchant account marketing company's "We Give Back USA" fundraising campaign to help our nation's wounded warriors. He also fundraises and serves locally the wounded warrior nonprofit, Operation OASIS.

Christopher was recognized by the US JAYCEES, with a lifetime achievement as one of Ten Outstanding Young Americans Award (TOYA) for his positive impacts to America. He joined an elite group of a little over 600 Americans to include former Presidents Bill Clinton, Gerald Ford and John F. Kennedy, civil rights leader Rev. Jesse Jackson and corporate tycoons Ross Perot, Henry Heinz and Nelson Rockefeller.
