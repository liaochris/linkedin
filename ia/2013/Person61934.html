Reporter

New York Newsday

Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; Newspapers industry

August 1984 – March 1986 (1 year 8 months) New York, NY

Worked as Police Bureau Chief for New York Newsday starting in the fall of 1984 until I moved to the Albany Bureau in January of 1986 for a couple of months before starting my new job as a reporter in the paper's City Hall Bureau. As police bureau chief, I worked with Jane Fritsch on an award-winning series of stories about the use of deadly force by off-duty New York City police officers. We found that almost a third of all use of deadly force was by off-duty officers involved in bar fights, traffic disputes or personal matters, prompting the department to change its policies and rules.
