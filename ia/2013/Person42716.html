Erin Miller's Summary

Policy analyst with extensive experience evaluating federal, state, and local policy related to the Affordable Care Act, Medicaid, Health Exchanges, discretionary health programs, and public health. Experience with economics and political analysis.

Erin Miller is currently serving as a budget analyst for health for the Democratic staff of the House Budget Committee. She previously worked at HHS as a Special Assistant to the Assistant Secretary for Planning and Evaluation. Prior to joining HHS, Erin worked on public health preparedness at the National Association of County and City Health Officials (NACCHO). During graduate school, she served as an analyst intern at the Government Accountability office where she contributed to a report on Medicaid Preventive Services. Erin has also worked as an educator and local area retail coordinator for the Special Supplemental Nutrition Program for Women, Infants and Children (WIC) for Denver Health. She received her Master of Public Health from Columbia University and holds a Bachelor of Arts in History from the University of Colorado at Boulder.

Specialties: Affordable Care Act, Program Planning and Evaluation, Insurance Coverage Policy, Federal Budget Process, Legislative Process, Strategic Planning, Fiscal Policy, Medicaid, Discretionary Health Programs, Government Contracting, Health System Data, Health Policy Communication, Public Health Policy, Policy Implementation, Health Systems Research
