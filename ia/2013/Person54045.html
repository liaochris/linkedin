James Israel's Summary

I founded the Humor Times, originally the Comic Press News, in April, 1991. It was the tail end of the first gulf war, and the first issue featured a four page summary of the politics surrounding it, in editorial cartoon form. Since then, we've covered all the big issues, using political satire to lighten up the otherwise somewhat depressing news. We want to help people laugh at the news, rather than cry about it.

We are now working on increasing our visibility nationally, in an effort to increase subscriptions. We are looking for venture capitalists willing to invest a relatively small sum into something unusual, but set up for success. There is a huge audience out there hungry for political humor, as the popularity of shows like Comedy Central's Daily Show and Colbert Report, and publications like the Funny Times and The Onion attest. We feel their success proves there is a huge market for this type of publication, and plenty of room for growth.

Meanwhile, we continue to grow more slowly and organically, as lovers of fun, incisive political satire discover the Humor Times. Why not order a free sample issue for yourself?

Subscriptions make great gifts, by the way ;)
