Alison Naomi Holt's Summary

I am the author of the Psychological Crime Thriller The Door at the Top of the Stairs, Credo's Hope, book 1 of The Alex Wolfe Mysteries, and Credo's Legacy, Book 2 of The Alex Wolfe Mysteries.

I'm also a retired Lieutenant with twenty years experience on the Tucson Police Department. I enjoy speaking at writer's conferences, teaching authors about writing realistic police characters, police procedures, and anything else writers want to know in order to improve their police related writing skills. I have taught many different subjects to law enforcement officers including hostage negotiations, mobile field force tactics (riot control), undercover techniques and much more.

Supervisor K9 Unit (Sergeant – Voluntary demotion from Lieutenant)

Tucson Police Department

2004 – 2006 (2 years)

Supervised eleven K9 handlers- 7 narcotics specialists and 4 explosive specialists. Oversaw all K9 deployment throughout the City of Tucson. Restructured all training aspects of the unit, oversaw all training including tactical deployment techniques, narcotics detection and explosive detection, building searches and handler protection. Organized and coordinated the 2005 Tucson Area Police K9 Trials where K9 teams from 30 local, state and federal agencies, including the United States Air force, United States Army, the Drug Enforcement Administration and teams from more than 20 states competed in various types of police dog handling skills.

Commander in charge of maintaining CALEA certification for the Tucson Police Department 2003-2004

Tucson Police Department

2003 – 2004 (1 year)

Assigned directly to the Chief of Police overseeing the department’s National Certification by The Commission on Accreditation for Law Enforcement Agencies (CALEA). CALEA Accreditation requires an agency to develop a comprehensive, well thought out, uniform set of written directives, provide the necessary reports and analyses a CEO needs to make fact-based, informed management decisions, requires that a preparedness program be put in place so an agency is ready to address natural or man-made unusual occurrences, limits an agency’s liability and risk exposure because it demonstrates that internationally recognized standards for law enforcement have been met, as verified by a team of independent outside CALEA-trained assessors.

Assistant Division Commander (Lieutenant) Operations Division East

Tucson Police Department

2002 – 2004 (2 years)

Management of 4 patrol squads of 12 officers and 1 sergeant each, 1 Division Detective Squad of 7 detectives and one sergeant and 1 division bicycle unit of 10 officers and 1 sergeant. Responsibilities included call load management, supervision of all major crime scenes assigned to my squads, Force Commander duties (1 24-hour period/week I was the commander in charge of all calls for service within the city of Tucson), investigated and ruled on all disciplinary decisions regarding officers assigned to me and was responsible for writing performance evaluations.

Mobile Field Force (MFF) Commander (Riot Control)

Tucson Police Department

2000 – 2004 (4 years)

Concurrent with other commander duties during this time period, I was a tactical commander of one of the department’s 5 mobile field forces. I commanded 4 riot control squads of 10 officers with 1 sergeant each plus four grenadiers. My MFF participated in Y2K preparedness, suppressing 2 NCAA Final Four Riots and 1 NCAA Championship riot. I was also selected and trained as a Departmental Mobile Field Force Trainer and I helped train all departmental personnel in riot control techniques.

Division Commander (Lieutenant) Planning and Research Division

Tucson Police Department

2001 – 2002 (1 year)

Assigned directly to the Chief of Staff. Responsible for maintaining the department’s Rules and Procedures manual, for investigating innovative programs of law enforcement agencies throughout the country and implementing those programs that would benefit the Tucson Police Department, taught all departmental commanders how to use a management program used by the Chief of Police that monitored all active programs throughout the department.
Assigned to research and implement a police foundation for the Tucson Police Department. Began the Tucson Police Foundation, wrote bylaws and articles of incorporation, recruited board members and participated in fund raising activities. In 2006 the Tucson Police Foundation had revenues in excess of $180,000.

Assistant Division Commander Special Investigation Section (Lieutenant)

Tucson Police Department

2000 – 2001 (1 year)

Management of 1 undercover Vice Squad made up of 6 officers and 1 sergeant, 1 undercover Intelligence Squad made up of 6 officers and 1 sergeant assigned to investigate organized crime, Illegal motorcycle gangs and various subversive groups and 1 Gang Tactical Unit responsible for monitoring all of the local gang activity throughout the city.
