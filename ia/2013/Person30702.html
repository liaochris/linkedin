John Darlington's Summary

Graduated from the University of Leeds in 2011 with a BA in Japanese Studies (2:1). Currently living and working in the Japanese countryside, teaching English, introducing British culture, and promoting internationalisation.

John Darlington's Experience

Assistant Language Teacher

Shimonoseki City Board of Education

August 2011 – July 2013 (2 years) Hohoku-cho, Shimonoseki, Yamaguchi

Working at 14 schools (10 elementary, 3 junior high and 1 kindergarten) across the Hohoku, Yasuoka, Yoshimo and Yoshimi areas of Shimonoseki city, Yamaguchi, assisting with the instruction of the English language and the introduction of international culture. Also teaching at a weekly English Conversation Club for adults, as well as occaisional classes for other community groups. Many aspects of this job require me to be proactive and take a leading role.

Introduced a year long RPG based activity to motivate junior high students to use English outside of the classroom. (www.hohokurpg.wordpress.com)

Introduced an English Licence system to 9 elementary schools, giving students a motivational reason to try and remember the English they learn. At the end of the year students will take a 5 question quiz to determine whether they are awarded a gold (perfect), silver (4 or 3 correct), bronze (2 or 1 correct) or blue (no correct answers) licence.

Created a visual novel style computer game with a story that makes the player use English. It's similar to the 'Choose Your Own Adventure' books, only it's your answers that will affect the story. (www.outlandishmove.wordpress.com).

With the help of equipment donated by the Japan Cricket Association (日本クリケット協会), I introduced the sport of Cricket to my elementary and junior high schools. Using English as a tool to explain the rules, many students had the opportunity to try a new sport.

Assistant webmaster of the Yamaguchi chapter of AJET (Association for Japan Exchange and Teaching) (http://yamaguchi.ajet.net/).

English Tutor

Konan University

October 2008 – January 2009 (4 months)

Once a week I was available to help correct Japanese students work and assist in improving both oral and aural communication skills.
