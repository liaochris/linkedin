Mary Sullivan Esseff, Transformational Author

June 1965 – Present (48 years 4 months)

Transformational author Mary Sullivan Esseff writes spiritual fictionalized stories that can make you laugh or bring tears to your eyes. She draws on her tenacious wit and rare humor even when difficulties arise. She always searches for what is good, true and lovely. Her characters exhibit many of these same qualities.

Her husband, Peter's Lebanese culture has inspired her series of "Khalil Khoury" stories, including, "Love Made Visible," published in the anthology "Three Naked Ladies Playing Cellos" and "The Evil Eye," published in an e-zine.

During her year of treatment for breast cancer, she published an e-journal, "Amazing Grace, Notes From a Breast Cancer Survivor."

Her latest book, a fictionalized spiritual memoir, "The Butterfly & The Snail," recalls the events that took place in 1964-1965 beginning with Rebecca Butler and Khalil Khoury's initial encounter in Salzburg, Austria and developed into an intense love. It is being prepared for publication.

Mary Sullivan Esseff, Ph.D.'s Publications

  • The Butterfly & The Snail

    • Balboa Press, March 21, 2013
    • March 21, 2013
    Authors: Mary Sullivan Esseff, Ph.D.

    "The Butterfly & the Snail," a fictionalized spiritual memoir, is set in the same time and place where another celebrated romance, "The Sound of Music," is being filmed (summer 1964, Salzburg, Austria).

    Based on actual events, "Butterfly" brings together a shy college senior and a soon-to-be-ordained Jesuit priest. A series of heart-filled encounters transforms their lives forever. For example, while relaxing on Gaisberg Mountain, Rebecca divulges to Khalil the turmoil she’s had to overcome to gain inner peace. Her heart’s desire—love—has eluded her. But at that moment, she discovers it in Khalil. Gathering her courage, she asks him, “How can I love like you?”

    The Butterfly & the Snail reveals a deep love that transcends time and place. The magic travels from the hills of Salzburg to the glittery stage of New York where Khalil appears on The Ed Sullivan Show; from the halls of a Catholic girls’ college to the bowels of the Jesuit Theologate. All great love stories abound in conflict and this is especially true in The Butterfly and the Snail. The struggles to resolve these conflicts are not only engaging, but entirely riveting, from the first page to the last. And yet, in this tale of two souls inexplicably drawn to each other, there is no guarantee of a happy ending. It is a story that will make readers want to ask: “How can I love like they do?”
    "The Butterfly & the Snail" entices readers to share the spirituality and intimacy of this unique couple.

    The inspirational love story evokes universally shared recollections of childhood grief and pain; pride, anger, disobedience; life decisions; the courage and sacrifices love demands; and the healing, transformational power of love.
