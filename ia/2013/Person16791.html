Brendan Sheehan's Summary

Brendan Sheehan is a business consultant, strategy analyst and leading expert in executive compensation, public company governance, and compliance. He engages with boards and management, advising them in the areas of executive compensation, human resource strategy and shareholder engagement.

As Principal with Global Governance Advisors, Brendan specializes in the strategic review, valuation and innovative design of compensation and corporate governance programs that support organizational strategy and objectives, as well as shareholders' interests.

In addition, he lectures on governance, risk and compliance issues and is a regular contributor to the debate on shareholder rights, financial reform and the strategic impact of Dodd-Frank. Stanford University recognized Brendan's achievements in the governance and journalism fields by voting him one of the Top 15 business journalists in the US and awarded him a Stanford Media Fellowship for 2010.

He was formerly Editorial Director at NACD Directorship, where he oversaw editorial content for a broad range of publications and website activities and was responsible for development of content-rich multimedia platforms. He was also Executive Editor at Corporate Secretary magazine and worked in London as a senior analyst for BoardEx, where he was responsible for analyzing and reporting on corporate governance and board dynamics at UK, US, and international companies.

His other professional experience includes being senior capital markets analyst at MCM CorporateWatch, specializing in high-yield bonds and asset-backed securities.

He is a former professional rock climbing and mountaineering instructor and has participated in expeditions around the globe. He holds a Bachelor's degree in Economics from the Queensland University of Technology and has worked professionally in Australia, Germany, UK and the USA.
