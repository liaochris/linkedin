George Warshaw's Summary

To be successful in any field, profession or venture is not hard: plan with foresight, pay attention to the details, anticipate problems before they occur, get things done on time, and never abandon common sense.

I'm fortunate to have the best group of attorneys and staff working with for me that I have ever had. Positive people with great energy, hard-working, detail oriented and a delight to talk and work with.

I founded my law firm in 1979 focusing on litigation and real estate. I added specialized training in wills, trusts and estate planning to provide a more complete capability of responding to the core needs of my clients.

Along the way, I squeezed in the time to write a legal textbook used by lawyers throughout the state on Massachusetts Landlord-Tenant Law, now in its second edition, Lexis Law Publishing.

I am a member of WealthCounsel, a national group of estate planning attorneys, and the Real Estate Bar Association (including the Condominium Law, Leasing and Technology Committees).

Lastly, I am pioneering a field of law that protects pets from unnecessary abandonment and destruction when an owner becomes unable to care for them or dies. Through the use of "pet trusts" or "small private foundations" a person can care for his or her pets or care for pets on a larger charitable scale.

George Warshaw's Experience

Principal

George Warshaw & Associates, P.C.

January 1989 – Present (24 years 10 months)

I've been an attorney in Massachusetts for over 30 years. I have a diverse legal and practical background. I practiced in many areas of law before focusing on real estate, estate planning and business ventures.

REAL ESTATE: I represent buyers and sellers of homes, condos, town houses and investment properties, and provide real estate services and representation to international buyers of US real estate and professional athletes relocating to the Boston, Massachusetts area, among many other related aspects of real estate.

ESTATE PLANNING: I prepare wills and trusts for traditional and modern relationships, trusts and guardianship documents to protect one's children and pets, create small private foundations to accomplish a client's charitable desires, and utilize many other estate planning devices.

BUSINESS VENTURES: I advise and organize corporations and LLCs, and serve as general counsel to software startups hoping to ultimately merge or go public.

We have offices in Boston, Hingham and Nantucket/Martha's Vineyard, Massachusetts.
