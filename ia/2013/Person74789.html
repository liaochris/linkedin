Kevin Bury's Summary

Design professional with over 15 years of experience.

Currently managing team responsible for the cross-platform design (web, tablet, and mobile) of all Content and Community products at LinkedIn. I have also directly designed numerous products, including: LinkedIn Influencers, Company Pages, Product Pages, Career Pages, Groups, Jobs, and Recruiter.

Prior to LinkedIn, I was Director, User Experience and Design at Yahoo! where my team was responsible for the design of all of Yahoo's "Front Door" products.

I have also held senior design and design management positions at Ariba, WebMD, Apple, and IBM.

Specialties: Design innovation. Iterative design. Design Management. Rapid prototyping of designs in HTML, JavaScript, and CSS.

Kevin Bury's Experience

PRINCIPAL USER INTERFACE DESIGNER

LinkedIn

Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; LNKD; Internet industry

April 2008 – Present (5 years 6 months)

• Led the 2013 redesign of LinkedIn Groups.

• Part of the team that conceived, designed, and launched the LinkedIn Influencer program in 2012. 150 thought leaders from a variety of industries were recruited to publish articles on LinkedIn (people like Richard Branson, Barack Obama, and Arianna Huffington). LinkedIn members were given the ability to Follow these thought leaders, as well as Like, Share, and Comment on their posts.

• Led the 2012 redesign of LinkedIn Company, Career, and Product Pages. Companies use these pages to showcase their brand, products, and career opportunities. Previous versions had been largely text based and static. The redesign gave companies the ability to communicate their brand via images, as well as the ability to post targeted status updates about the company. The primary call to action on company pages is "Follow". Members who follow a company receive status updates from the company as well as insights about the company - such as how they are connected.

 • Between 2008 and early 2011, I was the lead designer (actually the only designer) for LinkedIn Hiring Solutions – Corporate Recruiter, Hiring Manager, and Job Seeker. This was an exciting time and a period of rapid growth for this business.

  • Led the 2009 redesign of LinkedIn site navigation - a total revamp of primary, secondary, and tertiary navigation across Linkedin. This was a massive effort that touched every page on the site. In addition to simplified navigation, the redesign updated the visual style of the site and freed up an additional 30% of screen real estate for page content. The new design was very well received by the press and by users.
