AJ Juliani's Summary

AJ Juliani is a K-12 Staff Developer in the Wissahickon School District. Juliani taught middle school and high school English at Wissahickon and is the co-founder of Education Is My Life. He also founded Project: Global Inform and Collaborative Education Solutions.

AJ also currently serves as an ISTE Faculty and Instructional Consultant with the Verizon Innovative Learning Schools project. He has been an invited speaker to the ISTE Leadership Forum (2012), the VILS Virtual Conference (2013), and presented at a number of education conferences and workshops. As co-founder of Education Is My Life and CES, Juliani is working towards solving some of biggest issues in education through collaboration.

Juliani spent two summers in South Africa and Swaziland, volunteering at an AIDS clinic, running kids camps, preparing a community structure to be used for a medical clinic, church, school, and food shelter. Since then Juliani has taken on the role of Webmaster and education consultant for Swaziland Relief (which just started their six-building school this Winter!).

His first eBook “The 2.0 Teacher” was published by Six Voices and is available on iTunes (in the App Store) and Amazon. The book explains why using 2.0 resources is necessary in a 21st century classroom, and how to implement this technology in the classroom immediately. Juliani has since edited and contributed to "Education Is My Life: Stories From The Field", "Intellectual Curiosity in Education", "Education By Design", "The Best and Next in Education", and "Education Leadership 101" eBooks.

AJ received his MS Degree from Drexel University in “Global and International Education”. He founded – Project: Global Inform – a project that gives students the power to do something about human rights violations. AJ is also involved in the “Flat Classroom Project”, and is a Microsoft Innovative Educator. He's currently running the "20% Time MOOC" and writing about inquiry-driven learning.

Learn more at ajjuliani.com.

AJ Juliani's Projects

  • 20% Time MOOC

    • July 2013 to Present
    Team Members: AJ Juliani

    This MOOC offers two outcomes. Teachers will learn about the research behind Google's 20% policy and how it can be applied in K-12 education. Learners will also participate in their own 20% project throughout the course and present as a final product.

  • Education Is My Life

    • January 2011 to Present
    Team Members: AJ Juliani

    Founder and Chief Editor at EducationIsMyLife.com. We are a group blog in which educators offer varying perspectives on the same topic each month. We also produce free eBooks each month for our readers!

  • Summer of Twitter: Teacher Challenge

    • May 2013 to Present
    Team Members: AJ Juliani

    As a staff developer I realized that many teachers don’t communicate or collaborate with other educators. Sometimes they’ll talk to a peer down the hall or in their department at school, but rarely do they venture out into the world and connect. Twitter changed all of that for me, and it has made me SUCH a BETTER educator. My community of peers has inspired, motivated, and challenged me to really care about students and innovate in the classroom. The “Summer of Twitter” Challenge is a way for teachers to get started with Twitter over the summer. In an easy to follow 7-step program, each teacher will reach out and connect with educators from around the world. Check out the wiki.

  • Project: Global Inform

    • January 2009 to Present
    Team Members: AJ Juliani

    Project Global Inform (PGI) is an in-school project where students use media to spread awareness about human rights violations. PGI came out of the idea that we too often “teach” our students about genocide and human rights violations, but never “do” anything about it. This project’s main objective is to create awareness about current human rights violations in our schools, communities, and abroad. Through the use of media and technology students have the power to make a difference.
