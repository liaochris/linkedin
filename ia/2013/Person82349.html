Lorayne Blacka's Summary

Technology product and sales training specialist with extensive background in design, development, and delivery of motivating, high-quality training. Highly experienced in Instructional Systems Design (ISD), development of classroom (ILT) and web-based training (WBT) materials, and delivery of live and virtual learning engagements including Microsoft Office applications training. Can get up to speed quickly on content to be taught, completely comfortable in the language of technology and working with Subject Matter Experts (SMEs).

Lorayne Blacka's Experience

Owner/Principal

LHB Consulting

September 1995 – Present (18 years 2 months)

Since 1995, LHB Consulting has provided design and development of high-tech training, documentation, and web content to Businesses, Government/Defense, and NGOs. We believe in continuous learning for ourselves and our clients, and take pride in the quality of our products and services. Grounded in the principles of adult learning and instructional systems design, we specialize in the latest eLearning, New Media, software demo/simulation, and online Help technologies. We provide all the traditional modes as well, including Instructor-Led Training and Self-Paced study materials. Our consultants are experts in a variety of content areas -- in particular, end-user desktop applications, systems technology, digital printing systems, publishing technologies, media production, and ESL/foreign language learning. Clients have included Xerox Corporation, Pearson Performance Solutions, FedEx Kinkos, U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD), Federal Aviation Administration (FAA), GE, ESI International, GTE, American Red Cross, and a major Computer-Telephony Integration (CTI) provider that offered telephony products and services to call centers, large and small. Lorayne also has partnerships with other independent consultants who are experts in a variety of content areas – in particular, end-user desktop applications, systems technology, digital printing systems, publishing technologies, media production, and ESL/foreign language learning. Other LHB Consulting services are storyboarding and scriptwriting, media production, train-the-trainer, implementation/rollout planning, testing/QA, and maintenance to serve a client’s full training and documentation needs.

Assistant Director (Volunteer Non-profit Organization)

Annandale Family History Center

2005 – Present (8 years) Annandale, VA

Assistance with administration of family history / genealogy research services rendered by 30+ community staff volunteers and made available to anyone in the community who wants to access them. Creation and administration of the Center’s Website at http://www.annandalefhc.org/ . Administration and training of staff on Center support technologies, including local computer and Internet services used by Center patrons and staff to do genealogy research, order microfilms and microfiche from the central Family History Library distribution center in Salt Lake City, scan document page images from film and fiche to digital format to make them more accessible to patrons, and perform other research tasks.

Training Consultant (Xerox Vendor)

Xerox Corporation

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; XRX; Information Technology and Services industry

December 1995 – 2010 (15 years)

As Owner/Principal of LHB Consulting, Lorayne has designed and developed web-based courses to introduce Xerox Analysts and Sales Reps to major components of new digital production printing software product launches (DigiPath and FreeFlow) – the look-and-feel, features, benefits, underlying technical concepts, and typical customer workflows. This involved working with software on-site and participating in Subject Matter Expert (SME) project meetings and training sessions, publishing courses to the Xerox server, and testing/revising as needed. Lorayne has also managed, designed and developed other self-paced training and reference materials for Xerox Analysts and Sales Reps. Materials provided training in key marketing messages, value statements, and major technical features of Xerox digital production printing software and printing system hardware products in a print shop environment. Lorayne and associates used MS Word, MS PowerPoint, Adobe Acrobat, Adobe Captivate, Visio, Paint Shop Pro, WebMentor (a web courseware authoring shell), Front Page, HTML and other tools.
