Social Projects Vice-President

Enactus Southampton

Nonprofit; 10,001+ employees; Nonprofit Organization Management industry

May 2011 – May 2012 (1 year 1 month) Southampton

Enactus Southampton is part of the global network of Enactus. The University society enables students to gain real life business experience whilst creating social enterprise projects that help people in our local and global community.

I was responsible for the management of our social enterprise projects. In my role I increased our portfolio from six to nine projects, strengthened our engagement and impact in the local community and established team training events.

I worked directly with the nine project leaders to guide, motivate and encourage in the development of their projects. These projects included working with unemployed youths, struggling local businesses, farming communities in Sierra Leone, and solar lamp entrepreneurs in Kenya and Madagascar. Under my management and leadership, our nine social enterprise projects improved the lives of 3,325 people.

In April 2012, Enactus Southampton won the Enactus UK National Competition presenting four of our nine social projects, and went onto compete at the Enactus World Cup in Washington DC in October 2012.

Enactus Project Leader: Right Light

Enactus Southampton - (formerly SIFE)

Nonprofit; 10,001+ employees; Nonprofit Organization Management industry

January 2010 – May 2011 (1 year 5 months) Southampton

Right Light is an Enactus Southampton social enterprise project which sets up small scale solar lamp entrepreneurs with business training and micro-finance loans. These solar lamp businesses rent the solar lamps to the community, eradicating the need for expensive, toxic and dangerous kerosene lamps and provide an income to the entrepreneur.

I established the project in early 2010, and lead a team of fifteen students to realise the project idea into a successful social enterprise. Right Light won 'SETsquared Best Social Enterprise 2011'.

In my role as founder I formed professional contractual relationships with two international organisations, developed a business plan, and financial forecasts. As project leader Right Light improved the lives of 1,164 people in Madagascar through increased disposable income and clean and safe light at night.

In April 2011 I presented Right Light as part of the Enactus Southampton's presentation at the National Competition, in front of a panel of fifty senior business judges and an audience of 500 students. As National Champions we attended the Enactus World Cup 2011 in Kuala Lumpur, achieving the status of one of the Top 24 teams in the world.

Since passing on the leadership of Right Light, the project has continued to be student led within the Enactus team. In April 2013 Right Light worked with 27 entrepreneurs across Madagascar, Kenya and Uganda and improved the lives of 2,577 people.
