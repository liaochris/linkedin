Kevin Ryan's Summary

Kevin has more than fifteen years commercial experience in the design and development of enterprise scale software applications. He is the author of both the Dynamic Range and Limited Days estimation techniques and is principal contributor to the IT Kanban Framework. He works with companies to support their transition to lean and agile methods from team to enterprise scale.

Kevin provides independent consultancy and delivers courses in lean, Kanban, and agile practices, employing real world examples and experience to support organisations on their journey to continuous improvement. Recent clients include: Financial Times; Pearson; BAA; and Barclays. His website and podcast are a source of information for thousands of people around the world with readers and podcast subscribers in over 75 countries. The IT Kanban linked in user group is also a lively source of debate and information with an active community of over 2500 members.

Over the course of his career Kevin’s skill set and experience expanded into the architectural and design areas of software development and then into agile project management, training and agile practices consultancy. He now works with companies to support their transition to Lean and Agile methods from team to enterprise scale.

Both articulate and numerate with a logical approach to problem solving Kevin is an excellent communicator with the ability to understand business issues. This has resulted in a proven track record of driving technical delivery to deliver business value. If you believe that Kevin may be able to support your organisations transition to agile practices or are already implementing agile and want to further enhance your organisations productivity please get in touch. Kevin is also available for public speaking engagements, training and workshops.

Specialties

Agile Project Management, Scrum, XP, Agile Unified Process Software Engineering, Application Architecture, Enterprise Application Development, .net, JEE, Agile Team Mentoring
