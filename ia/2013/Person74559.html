Kenneth Trump's Summary

Kenneth S. Trump, M.P.A., is President of National School Safety and Security Services, a Cleveland (OH) national firm specializing in preK-12 school security and emergency preparedness training, consulting, crisis communications, expert witness and litigation consulting services.

Ken and his team help superintendents, school boards, principals and first responders strengthen school safety, security, emergency preparedness and communications by:

- School safety, security, emergency/crisis preparedness & crime prevention assessments
- Evaluating & developing emergency plans, facilitating tabletop exercises & training
- Keynote presentations, conference & workshop speaking
- Post-crisis consultations after shootings & other crises
- Developing crisis communications plans, social media strategy, parent/media communications skills
- School safety expert witness & litigation support
- Consulting & training on school climate, psychology & related safety support
- Advising policymakers, legislators and other elected officials

Ken served as an officer, investigator and youth gang unit supervisor for the Cleveland City Schools' safety division. He also served as a suburban Cleveland school security director and assistant gang task force director. Ken has nearly 30 years experience working with school and public safety officials from all 50 states.

He authored three books and more than 80 articles. Ken is one of the most widely quoted school safety experts in national networks, cable, print, radio and online news.

Ken is a four-time invited Congressional witness on school safety and crisis issues. He testified on bullying before the U.S. Commission on Civil Rights and briefed Israeli educators on behalf of the U.S. State Department. Ken provided testimony to the Sandy Hook Advisory Commission, Oklahoma Commission on School Security, National Association of Attorneys General and more.
Ken was an invited attendee at the 2006 White House Conference on School Safety.

Specialties

School safety, security and emergency preparedness consulting and training, preK-12 school security and emergency preparedness planning, expert witness and litigation consulting. School security assessments, school emergency preparedness planning, professional development training for educators and public safety officials, tabletop exercises, bullying, crisis communications, and related school safety consulting. Freelance writer, news researcher,content consultant, digital news producer.

    Authors: Kenneth Trump

    Proactive School Security and Emergency Preparedness Planning has been described by readers as a one-stop shop addressing prevention, security, emergency preparedness and planning, parent and media communications, and more.

    This latest edition introduces new dedicated full chapters on managing bullying, preparing schools for terrorism, managing school safety on tight budgets, parents and school safety, and managing media and parent communications in the postcrisis stage of school emergency planning. These five new chapters offer practical, common-sense frameworks and steps school leaders can take to proactively manage and respond to highly visible, emotional, and political aspects of school safety leadership in today’s security-sensitive school community. The guidance in these chapters will help school leaders navigate complex school safety issues while operating under unprecedented budget constraints.

    Educators and safety officials will also benefit from new subchapter sections on hot topics including administration building and board meeting security, after-hours school security; athletic and large event security; cell phones; Election Day security; elementary school security; Federal Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA) and school privacy exceptions; Tasers and school police; training staff on school security and emergency preparedness; transportation security; diversifying emergency drills; tabletop exercises; and financial and continuity of operations plans are among the new and expanded subchapters.
