Harry Tucker's Summary

I have spent over 25 years in the IT industry, having served as a business strategy advisor, global technology adoption advisor and large-scale technology architect to Wall St. and Fortune 50 companies. I have won multiple awards for excellence while working for large consulting firms in New York; as a principal consultant with Cap Gemini and as a senior enterprise strategy advisor with Microsoft Consulting Services.

I cofounded and was CTO of an award-winning software company in New York that specialized in human capital optimization, the capture and mathematical expression of human productivity. This company was successfully sold in 2003 – the acquiring company went public shortly after the deal.

I currently provide guidance to clients in the areas of large-scale technology architecture and implementation, technology best practices adoption, corporate strategy, M & A due diligence (buyer and seller sides) and startup guidance. I am also a contributing author to O’Rielly’s “97 Things Every Project Manager Should Know: Collective Wisdom from the Experts”.

I have deep and broad business and technology experience in the following verticals:

Financial services (investment, commercial, institutional, retail)
Insurance (property, casualty and life)
Retail
Predictive analytics (human capital optimization)
Management consulting (public and private corporations)

Specialties

I focus on providing strategic, tactical and technology guidance to companies ranging from not-for-profits and startups to Fortune 25 companies in the following areas:

Business strategy
