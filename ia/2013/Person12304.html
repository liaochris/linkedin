Brendan Borrell's Summary

Brendan Borrell is a journalist whose writing has appeared in Smithsonian, Slate, Bloomberg Businessweek, The American Prospect, and the Washington Post. He is a 2013 Alicia Patterson Foundation fellow and has reported from around the world on science, business, crime, health, and natural resources.

Previously, he held stints as a reporter for Nature, Scientific American, and The Oregonian. He also spent two years as a correspondent for the life sciences magazine, The Scientist.

A native of Texas, Brendan spent several years conducting research in Panama and Costa Rica, publishing more than a dozen scientific papers on frogs, snakes, and insects. He now lives in Brooklyn.
