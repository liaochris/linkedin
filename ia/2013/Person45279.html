Heidi Forbes Öste's Summary

Forbes Öste is a Visual Practitioner and Global Social Strategist. Incorporating visual thinking into communications and social strategy for organizations, business leaders and entrepreneurs. Founder of the Art of Social Strategy methodology, which she presents in keynotes and workshops releasing the ROI potential of social tools for individuals' and organizations' success and sustainability. Currently working on a doctoral dissertation on the impact of social technologies on leadership.
** Relocating to Bay Area, open to new opportunities and collaboration **

She lives by the philosophy that Knowledge is Power: Sharing is Powerful. Her passion is empowering change makers to achieve their untapped potential through clarity in vision and communications globally.

Specialties: Social Strategy, Leadership Development, Organizational Social Strategy, Networking, Organizational Development, Strategic Visualization, Visual Communications, Social Entrepreneurship

Heidi Forbes Öste's Projects

  • Research on Value of Visual Practice

    • May 2012 to November 2012
    Team Members: Heidi Forbes Öste

    This was a mixed methods study, in two parts, combining interviews with experts in the field with surveys of clients experiences. The survey goal was to measure and understand the effectiveness of the visual practice in meetings versus traditional meetings. The resulting data showed the visual practice, specifically graphic facilitation, to be over 80% more effective as a meeting format for process, content, delivery and results.

  • Looking forward: Collaborative Spaces

    • May 2013 to Present
    Team Members: Heidi Forbes Öste, Kerry Mitchell, Angela Zimmermann, SPHR, Sergej van Middendorp, Dottie Agger-Gupta, Lidija Nikolic, CTP

    The study was part of a four day intensive looking at the future of collaborative spaces in Malmö, Sweden. The team was compiled of students and faculty from Fielding Graduate University. Contributors included representatives from the city of Malmö, Media Evolution (our hosts) and a few other guests. The resulting report (linked) is not a collaborative piece, but rather is a reflection of the collaboration and discussions that took place during the "Malmö Jam." Pieces of this report are being used for the "Future of Work" component of Media Evolution's "The Conference" in August 2013 and their publication.
