Andrew Nebus' Summary

Andrew Nebus has a history of transforming government systems. Since he joined public service in 1996, he has been challenged to open processes and innovate solutions for the public sector. With a background in technology development, command and control systems, government records, and information security, Andrew has a genuine passion for Government 2.0 and spoke at Gov 2.0 LA 2012 and 2013.

Andrew is a Certified Information Systems Security Professional (CISSP), a member of the FBI Infragard’s New Jersey chapter, and an alumnus of Western Governors University. He considers himself a lifelong student – gripped by technology, social media, government, and real world change.

Specialties: Accomplished professional skilled at learning new concepts quickly, transforming strategic plans into workable solutions, and communicating ideas clearly and effectively. Results-oriented attitude with proven success building relationships with business leaders and holding vendors accountable. Strong focus balancing user’s needs with organization’s goals and regulatory requirements within an appropriate financial framework.

  • Clipper

    • July 2009 to Present
    Team Members: Andrew Nebus

    In a Public Safety environment, dispatchers and mobile personnel often have information in disparate systems to add to a Computer Aided Dispatch event. This may be unstructured, or differently structured than the dispatch system requires.

    Noticing this workflow issue, I designed and programmed a tool to take output from systems and scan for relevant data to transfer into a CAD or MDT terminal.

    Clipper works either by using inserting code dynamically into an HTML report where possible, or a bookmarklet when a system is web browser based to pass selected information. In either case, a new protocol was designed to pass data to the parsing engine. A lightweight application indicates findings and provides a control to simulate typing into the desired input form.
