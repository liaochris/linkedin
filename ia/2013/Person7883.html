Àngels Masó's Summary

I am a passionate about communication. I started my career in Peru, where I worked as a correspondent for the Spanish News Agency EFE and then as a producer of a TV program. Later, I worked in a newspaper "Prensa Libre" of Guatemala, where I specialized in health and education.

I have written about ethnic minorities, victims of armed conflicts, the consequences of mining & petroleum exploitation harming the environment and the consequences of child sex tourism, among others.

In addition, I have participated as a volunteer (in Peru, Guatemala and Uganda) on cooperation projects related with preventing pregnancy and AIDS among youth, providing educational support to underprivileged children and violence prevention.

For some time now, I have been interested in the potential found in social media, which has led me to work as a community manager and a social media strategist for companies, individuals and NGOs.

My passion for the humanitarian issues and the international organizations brought me to Washington, worked in the Knowledge, Management and Communications department of the Pan American Health Organization (PAHO) as an intern since last January to July 2013.
