  • From Principle to Practice: Bugs, Drugs & Attachments

    • Biotech Pharma Solutions
    • August 31, 2013
    Authors: Mark Brian Anderson, PhD, RAC

    From Principle to Practice: Bugs, Drugs & Attachments. When Time Matters, Where to find it! is a compact easy-to-carry reference handbook on US Regulatory Affairs.

    Navigating the US Regulatory pathways from initial application to the FDA to market approval or clearance is a highly demanding process that is complete with many technical and regulatory challenges that sometimes leaves us exclaiming ‘What the Muffin!’

    ‘What the Muffin!’ is a humorous rendition of what so many people had thought or uttered when presented with unanticipated program challenges; busted timelines; wrong turns; or a Form FDA 483. The phrase ‘What the Muffin!’ makes us smile and cringe all at the same time.

    ‘Bugs, Drugs and Attachments’ summarize the many sources of therapeutic agents, medical devices and combination products that we work with on a regular basis. This spirited title was chosen to stir the imagination and result in an opportunity for results-oriented discussion. Working with bugs, drugs and attachments is done in concert with the US FDA, regulatory agencies and our clinical development teams entrusted with our most precious deliverables.

    ‘From Principle to Practice’ is an encapsulation of the concept of transforming scientific principles and opportunities into clinical practice and marketable products by focusing on key deliverables in a work plan.

    The global biomedical industry and regulatory requirement environment is continuously evolving. We as professionals and industry leaders need to adapt to these evolutions if we want to gain market approvals or clearances for our intended products.

    Use this handbook as a portable reference handbook to stir discussion and highlight where to find the authoritative sources. Identifying the intended use and appropriate regulatory pathway is one of the important keys to success in developing meaningful therapeutic products to market approval or clearance.

    I hope you enjoy it.

  • NeutroPhase® in chronic non-healing wounds.

    • International Journal of Burns & Trauma
    • December 2012
    Authors: Mark Brian Anderson, PhD, RAC, J Crew, R Varilla, T Rocas, D Debabov, L Wang, A Najafi, S Rani, R Najafi

    Chronic non-healing wounds, such as venous stasis ulcers, diabetic ulcers, and pressure ulcers are serious unmet medical needs that affect a patient’s morbidity and mortality. Common pathogens observed in chronic non-healing wounds are Staphylococcus including MRSA, Pseudomonas, Enterobacter, Stenotrophomonas, and Serratia spp. Topical and systemically administered antibiotics do not adequately decrease the level of bacteria or the associated biofilm in chronic granulating wounds and the use of sub-lethal concentrations of antibiotics can lead to resistant phenotypes. We show 5 representative examples from our more than 30 clinical case studies using NeutroPhase as an irrigation solution with chronic non-healing wounds with and without the technique of negative pressure wound therapy (NPWT). NeutroPhase® is pure 0.01% hypochlorous acid (i.e. >97% relative molar distribution of active chlorine species as HOCl) in a 0.9% saline solution at pH 4-5 and is stored in glass containers. NovaBay has three FDA cleared 510(k)s. Patients showed a profound improvement and marked accelerated rates of wound healing using NeutroPhase with and without NPWT. NeutroPhase® was non-toxic to living tissues.

  • NVC-422 Inactivates Staphylococcus aureus Toxins.

    • Antimicrob. Agents Chemother. published ahead of print 3 December 2012 , doi:10.1128/AAC.01945-12.
    • December 3, 2012
    Authors: Mark Brian Anderson, PhD, RAC, A Jekle, J Yoon, M Zuck, R Najafi, L Wang, T Shiau, C Francavilla, S Rani, C Eitzinger, M Nagl, and D Debabov

    Bacterial pathogens have specific virulence factors (e.g. toxins) that contribute significantly to the virulence and infectivity of microorganisms within the human hosts. Virulence factors are molecules expressed by pathogens that enable colonization, immunoevasion, immunosuppression, obtaining nutrients from the host or gaining entry into host cells. For example, in systemic Staphylococcus aureus infections virulence factors such as Toxic Shock Syndrome Toxin 1 (TSST-1), Staphylococcal Enterotoxin A (SEA) and Staphylococcal Enterotoxin B (SEB) cause sepsis or toxic shock by uncontrolled stimulation of T-lymphocytes and by triggering a cytokine storm. In vitro, these superantigens stimulate the proliferation of human peripheral blood mononuclear cells (PBMC) and the release of many cytokines. Using mass-spectrometry, we demonstrate here that NVC-422 oxidizes methionine residues of TSST-1, SEA, SEB and Exfoliative Toxin A (ETA). These results show that, in addition to its well-described direct microbicidal activity, NVC-422 can inactivate S. aureus virulence factors through rapid oxidation of methionines.

  • N-chlorotaurine and its analogues N,N-dichloro-2,2-dimethyltaurine and N-monochloro-2,2-dimethyltaurine are safe and effective bactericidal agents in ex vivo corneal infection models.

    • Acta Ophthalmologica. doi: 10.1111/j.1755-3768.2012.02501.x PMID: 22971221
    • September 12, 2012
    Authors: Mark Brian Anderson, PhD, RAC, Teuchner B, Eitzinger C, Lutz M, Hager T, Schmid E, Bechrakis NE, Zuck M, Jekle A, Debabov D, Nagl M.

    N-chlorotaurine (NCT) and its analogues N-monochloro-2,2-dimethyltaurine (NVC-612) and N-dichloro-2,2-dimethyltaurine (NVC-422) are new anti-infectives for topical treatment for conjunctivitis. The aim of this study was to show that these compounds are safe in an EpiOcular model and effective in corneas infected ex vivo. Bacterial accumulations were detected histologically both on the corneal surface and also in the anterior third of the stroma of incised corneal buttons. All three test compounds at a concentration of 55 mm (equals 1% NCT) reduced the bacterial counts of P. aeruginosa and S. aureus by approximately 5 log10 after 60- and 120-min incubation, respectively. Significant killing was observed as early as after 5-min incubation. Also intrastromal bacteria were inactivated. In the EpiOcular™ tissue model, NCT, NVC-422 and NVC-612 had no or very low potential to irritate corneal tissue. Conclusion:  N-chlorotaurine, NVC-422 and NVC-612 are non-irritating in cornea and kill P. aeruginosa and S. aureus, even following penetration into the deeper corneal stromal layers.
