Adam Winsor's Summary

I'm a digital artist with a focus in animation, illustration, and interactive media.

I've worked on visual assets for applications ranging from mobile devices and websites, to games and custom interactive augmented reality installations. I've also worked on e-learning applications, game design, character design and animation, 3D mock-ups for technology proposals, and video production and editing.

Specialties: 3D modeling for video, games, and simulations using Maya. Illustration using Photoshop, Illustrator, and traditional media. Web design using Dreamweaver and Microsoft Expressions Web. 2D animation using Adobe Flash. Video editing/composition in AfterEffects. eLearning course design using Storyline. General technology integration.

Adam Winsor's Projects

  • Augmented Reality Playroom

    • February 2011 to September 2011
    Team Members: Adam Winsor, Charles Rinker, Jeff Stanley, Robert Murphy

    Designed and created animated sea creatures to populate a children's playroom for Florida Hospital's flagship location in Celebration, FL. Designed and built Augmented Reality view scope which layered these characters on a live video feed of the room. Projected characters on the windows to create a cartoon aquarium effect. Additionally created art assets for several custom themed arcade games, and assisted in creating projection screen layouts.

  • Digitanks

    • July 2010 to July 2011
    Team Members: Adam Winsor, Jorge Rodriguez

    Created 3D in-game assets and 2D user interface for Digitanks, an indie tank combat game.

  • Augmented Reality Power Relay Station

    • March 2012 to April 2012
    Team Members: Adam Winsor, Charles Rinker

    A small iOS app for ABB, one of the leading providers of power grid infrastructure, showing off one of their compact GIS relay stations.

  • nuV-Host Virtual Spokesperson

    • November 2010 to Present
    Team Members: Adam Winsor, Charles Rinker, Robert Murphy

    I was the lead designer on this project at nuMedia Innovations, and was in charge of designing and fabricating the physical interactive display system (the concept, software development and videography were handled by others).

    From the concept given to me by the project lead, I designed a base unit that could be attached to interchangeable custom-cut projection screen silhouettes. This unit needed to look nice, support the silhouette, and contain a wireless video server, wireless keyboard, remote sensor receiver, audio amp with embedded speakers and audio in/out, a full assortment of video and usb ports, a sonar proximity sensor, and needed to be plug-and-play ready.

    I was able to take the project from my initial concept sketches, through the fabrication of the custom enclosure and sourcing the internal components, to the assembly and installation of the completed unit in the client's showroom.
