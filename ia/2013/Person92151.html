Michael Burcham's Experience

President & CEO

The Nashville Entrepreneur Center

Nonprofit; 11-50 employees; Capital Markets industry

July 2010 – Present (3 years 4 months)

The mission of the Nashville Entrepreneur Center is simple: connect entrepreneurs with critical resources to accelerate business creation and growth. The EC serves as a gateway that fuses training, relationships, and resources in order to cultivate economic development and drive Tennessee’s future as the epicenter of entrepreneurial endeavors.

Special Venture Partner

Galen Partners

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Venture Capital & Private Equity industry

Venture Partner with Galen. Assist in evaluating new opportunities, due diligence of potential investments, serve on Boards, advise CEOs.

Professor for the Practice of Management

Vanderbilt University - Owen Graduate School of Management

Educational Institution; 10,001+ employees; Higher Education industry

October 2006 – Present (7 years 1 month)

Teaching Healthcare Innovation, Capstone Strategy Projects Course and Director of Accelerator - The Summer Business Institute for the Owen Graduate School of Management.

Executive Director

Onlife Health

Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Health, Wellness and Fitness industry

January 2010 – June 2010 (6 months)

Onlife is a wellness and health coaching firm owned by BCBS of Tennessee. I had the opportunity to work with the CEO, Chris Hunter, as a consultant to help him develop new products and services for their clients and re-engineer the organizational structure to improve profitability and create scale.

Executive Director

Accelerator - The Summer Business Institute

January 2007 – June 2010 (3 years 6 months)

Accelerator is a "Business Boot Camp" exposing bright young people to "what's possible" - it's an ideal place for a liberal arts graduate to discover their passion and launch an incredible career by partnering with Fortune 500 businesses to learn critical thinking skills and solve problems.

President

ParadigmHealth

January 2001 – December 2007 (7 years)

ParadigmHealth was a specialty care support and disease management firm - focused on the sickest 1% of individuals (which account for almost 35% of all healthcare costs). We provided valuable information regarding treatment options, individual and family support, coaching, access to spiritual and financial support for those facing life-limiting and life-threating situations. Having the opportunity to be President of a firm that did so much for so many was one of the highlights of my professional career. ParadigmHealth was a venture-baced firm and was sold to Inverness in December, 2007.

Founder & President

Theraphysics Corp

1993 – 2000 (7 years)

Led business model development, capital funding, building of the management team, and growth of the business in the Rehabilitation space.

VP, Managed Care

Hospital Corporation of America

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; Hospital & Health Care industry

1987 – 1992 (5 years)

At HCA, I had the opportunity to establish the relationships between our hospitals and the healthplans / payers. In the late 1980s - early 1990's, it was an amazing time in the healthcare space. These early agreements that were forged shaped much of the network landscape of the healthcare system. It was a highly entrepreneurial time - and a ton of fun.
