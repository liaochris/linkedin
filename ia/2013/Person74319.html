Ken Berger's Summary

An audio and music product industry pioneer with more than 30 years of experience, Ken Berger currently runs a product development and marketing consulting firm. During those 30 years, Berger distinguished himself as one of a very small handful of entrepreneurs to create and develop a high-end professional audio brand, EAW, and take it to $50M annual revenue.

Prior to co-founding VUE Audiotechnik, I held the Senior Vice President of Marketing and Product Development position at LOUD Technologies Inc., one of the world’s largest professional audio and musical instrument manufacturers and distributors. At LOUD, he was responsible for the strategic direction and marketing for iconic brands including Ampeg, Alvarez, EAW, Mackie, Crate and Tapco.

Immediately prior to LOUD, Ken was founder and CEO of UCI, a web portal company which created ProSoundWeb.com and Live-Audio.com, the largest online community serving the live and recording sound industries.

However, Berger’s greatest contribution to the professional audio industry was his founding of EAW (Eastern Acoustic Works) in 1978. The company grew to become one of the world’s leading suppliers of loudspeakers for concerts (Erik Clapton to Motley Crue) and major installations (Fenway Park to St. Peters Square). As founder and CEO, Ken was responsible for all aspects of the operation — including finance, sales, marketing and manufacturing — up until his sale of the brand in 2001.
