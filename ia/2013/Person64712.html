James M. Maloney's Experience

Solo Practitioner

Law Office of James M. Maloney

Practice currently limited to litigation in federal trial and appellate courts and in state appellate courts. Call (516) 767-1395 for more information.

Editor

Fort Schuyler MARINER

July 2003 – April 2008 (4 years 10 months)

Follow "Fort Schuyler MARINER archives" link in "Websites" sections (above and below) for links to pdf copies of the magazine and of selected articles and/or source material.

Associate Attorney

Kirlin, Campbell & Keating

1996 – 1999 (3 years)

Together with Charles C. Burlingham of the firm listed below, J. Parker Kirlin of this firm represented the Titanic interests in the American limitation of liability case that went to the U.S. Supreme Court, Oceanic Steam Navigation Company v. Mellor, 233 U.S. 718 (1914) (opinion by Oliver Wendell Holmes, Jr.). Kirlin's was another "venerable maritime law firm" (see below) that opened its doors in the 19th Century, and closed them during the first years of the current millennium.

Associate Attorney

Burlingham Underwood LLP

1995 – 1996 (1 year)

As one partner at Burlingham Underwood during its final years (and with whom I worked during my year there) described it: "A venerable maritime law firm that represented The Titanic, The Andrea Doria, and other parties in some of the most significant maritime cases for over 175 years until the firm was dissolved in 2002."

Paramedic

St. Vincent's Hospital & Medical Center

Nonprofit; 1001-5000 employees; Hospital & Health Care industry

1987 – 1995 (8 years)

Provided emergency medical care (endotracheal intubation, intravenous medication, advanced cardiac life support, etc.) and transportation (via ambulance) within the New York City EMS 911 system, as well as on-site at the World Trade Center and Jacob K. Javits Convention Center. St. Vincent's was founded in 1849 and closed in 2010.

Licensed Deck Officer

International Organization of Masters, Mates & Pilots

Maritime industry

1980 – 1987 (7 years)

Served as Second or Third Mate aboard oceangoing American merchant ships (container and tanker vessels) through above-named union. During same time period also served occasionally as Chief Mate aboard smaller tonnage vessels, including tugboats and seismic research vessels.
