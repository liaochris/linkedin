Shelter Manager @ God's Family Community Church

American Red Cross Great Plains Chapter

August 2005 – September 2005 (2 months) Lawton, Oklahoma Area

Initially volunteered & worked in the Eye Clinic as a receptionist/ file clerk at the Reynolds Army hospital located at Ft. Sill, outside of Lawton, I was requested to run 2 shelters being put together to receive evacuees from Hurricane Katrina. Due to the magnitude of the catastrophy, paid members of the Red Cross were overwhelmed & had to take on an influx of untrained volunteers and there was a large degree of chaos during the first few weeks after Katrina. Since I had shelter experience on my resume, from Philadelphia 25 yrs earlier working with battered women, I was asked to organize how to help this mass amount of people coming in from the gulf & was placed in a position to have to micromanage certain aspects in regards to creating the needed documentation of evacuees as well as resources offered in the surrounding areas that were being overlooked & told by Red Cross Inspectors from Tulsa, that I had the best operating shelter in all of Oklahoma within the first couple of weeks after the storm. I stayed long enough to organize and put teams together to better service the evacuees and compiled resource listings to help some evacuees find new homes and jobs in less than 24 hours of arriving at the shelter. Once organized, I was able to leave for Washington and on to Pennsylvania for other personal priorities.
