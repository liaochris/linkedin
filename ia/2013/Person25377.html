Clarke McAllister's Summary

As founder and President of ADASA, Clarke leads this IP company to provide RFID solutions for leading retailers, consumer brands, and global supply chain trading partners. A key contribution to the industry is the now globally adopted technique of assuring that each RFID tag has a unique SGTIN number. GS1 member companies and tag serialization working group leaders chose ADASA’s number pre-allocation scheme to overcome the inadequacies of the Internet to distribute numbers on a real-time basis. Assurance of globally unique numbers is as critical to RFID as unique IP addresses are to the Internet; it is fundamental.

ADASA serialization licensees have the option of adding RFID transponder security. Important security features that are built into the EPCglobal standard RFID inlays only find practical use when our cryptography patents are utilized. FIPS140-2 security is at the core of our mobile devices, enabling secure deployments that withstand even the most aggressive cryptographic attacks.   

ADASA’s patent portfolio includes robotic inventory reading solutions that provide overnight accuracy beyond what is attainable from workers using handheld readers, thus eliminating the final weakness of RFID automation.

Omni-channel retailing is a major driver of our work, enabling brick-and-mortar retailers to compete with online retailers like Amazon. Traditional retail stores must also serve as stocking locations for online sales; a challenge that only can be met with daily automated inventory updates.

Specialties: RFID serialization and security; preventing the cloning of legitimate RFID tags. Robotic RFID inventory scanning for accurate labor-free inventory visibility.

Clarke McAllister's Experience

President

ADASA Inc.

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Information Technology and Services industry

August 2004 – Present (9 years 2 months)

ADASA Inc. develops intellectual property and solutions using radio frequency identification (RFID). Brick and mortar retailers are striving to compete with online retailers like Amazon for customers, competitive prices, and same-day delivery. To win they must use in-store inventory to fulfill online orders. That requires efficient and accurate inventory counts, preferably on a daily basis. RFID-reading robots that roam the stores at night perform that function without incurring errors or labor costs. All of this requires RFID tags with globally unique serial numbers that are based on GS1 standards; which is entirely based on the ADASA ‘198 patent.

ADASA has more technologically advanced tagging solutions than traditional printer vendors and service bureaus. In addition to assuring globally unique numbering, ADASA enables brand owners to protect their RFID tags from counterfeiters by preventing the outright cloning of legitimate tags. This is especially important for brand owners that have offshore contract manufacturers tagging goods for them – in regions of the world where counterfeiting runs rampant.
