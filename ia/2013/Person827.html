Al Berg's Experience

Chief Security & Risk Officer

Liquidnet

Privately Held; 201-500 employees; Financial Services industry

December 2004 – Present (8 years 9 months)

I am responsible for information security, physical security and enterprise risk management for Liquidnet, an institutional equities broker/dealer employing 400+ people in offices in the United States, United Kingdom, Hong Kong, Japan, Singapore and Australia. Security and risk are core to Liquidnet’s value proposition as its customers entrust the firm with highly confidential trading information.

On my arrival at Liquidnet, I built the Security & Risk Management function from the ground up, assembling and mentoring a team of top notch professionals who handle everything from infosec testing and assessment, infosec policy development, investigations, and physical access control systems.

I introduced the SAS70 (now SSAE 16) audit process to Liquidnet and have implemented annual reviews which are published to our Members and business partners.

I have also developed and implemented Global Enterprise Risk Management Framework which provides company management with insight into the risks faced by the business and provides guidance on corporate priorities.

My work at Liquidnet has taken me all over the world and has put me in contact with financial regulators here in the US and abroad.

Tech Director - Information Security

SIAC

Privately Held; 1001-5000 employees; Financial Services industry

2001 – 2004 (3 years)

SIAC (the Securities Industry Automation Corporation) is the technology arm of the New York and American Stock Exchanges (since merged). In addition to developing information security policies and coordinating the Data Security Review Board, I was responsible for performing security assessments on a variety of systems and networks.

Information Security Practice Director

Mentor Technologies

Privately Held; 201-500 employees; Computer Networking industry

1998 – 2000 (2 years)

As the head of this technology consulting company’s Information Security Practice, I provided consulting services to the firm’s clients as well as management of the nationwide security practice.

I performed security consulting, assessments and testing for a wide variety of private and public sector organizations, including the US Department of Defense. Projects included design, installation and maintenance of firewalls and other network components, penetration testing of networks, systems and applications, and the delivery of security training and education to internal and external clients.

I provided thought leadership and management of the firm’s Information Security Practice.

Director of Strategic Technologies

NETLAN

1991 – 1998 (7 years)

NETLAN was a network systems reseller, consulting and training firm. In this position, I was a jack of all trades, doing everything from installation and troubleshooting of Novell NetWare networks, to design of Internet access and firewall solutions, to building web sites, to fighting the earliest computer viruses.
