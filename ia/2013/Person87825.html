Martin Willis' Summary

Currently the Director of Decorative Arts, Boston for James D. Julia which is one of the nations top ten auction galleries and he is also an appraiser/consultant for Worthpoint.com which is the leading worldwide resource for collectors and dealers.

Having a sincere passion for auctions, antiques, history, fine & decorative arts ~ Martin Willis founded the Antique Auction Forum in August 2009 which is a weekly podcast show on the antiques and arts. His ongoing efforts are to do his part in keeping the antique business alive and share knowledge to all.

As a second-generation auctioneer, Martin graduated from Missouri Auction school in 1977 and has over 40 years experience working at auctions in Maine, New Hampshire, Massachusetts, Colorado and California. He has been involved in cataloging and auctioning tens of thousands of antiques, collectibles and works of art.

In 1989 he held the Josiah Bartlett Auction, signer of The Declaration of Independence with attendees from around the world. Among other attributes, he was the exclusive appraiser for TV mogul Merv Griffin’s Estate including all of his California properties, and appraiser for the talk show host Tom Snyder’s estate.

He formerly served as a board member of American Decorative Arts Forum, of Northern California. Charter member of the Board of Director’s National Antiques Council, past board member of the National Auctioneers Association, past Vice President and Board Member of the New Hampshire Auctioneers Association.

He is a past columnist for What’s It Worth in the Sunday San Francisco Chronicle, a contributor to Warman’s Antiques & Collectibles 2014, Lamp & Glass Section, Antique Trader, Woburn Daily Times Chronicle and WorthPoint as well as a blog writer on the AAF website.


Specialties: 19th & 20th Century American & European Fine Art, American Period Furniture; American and European Decorative Arts, Ephemera and Historical Manuscripts and Documents.

Martin is a painter of landscapes as well.
