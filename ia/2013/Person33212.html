Dean Batson's Summary

Burgers turned me into a data addict.

At 16, I worked the highly prestigious graveyard shift at Jack in the Box. In the middle of the night, around the time everyone left the bars during our frequent $0.49 cheeseburger promotions we’d be slammed as the drive-through lane snaked around the building.

In order to quickly accommodate our buzzed, short-tempered customers’ late-night cravings and eliminate product waste, I counted cars and people, reviewed receipts, tracked order quantities and order preferences. My "database" was written on burger bags and everyone thought I was nuts. Then I developed a schedule of when to toast buns, cook burgers, fry fries and make shakes. It worked... very well.

Now... many years later, marketing is my life and data is still king. The only changes are hints of grey in my hair and the enormous amount of data we can now analyze.

By using data and market strategy, I help companies learn, tell their story and profit by focusing on what they’re good at, what to say and who to say it to....then execute.

The amount of insight from data analytics that’s available is remarkable. The trick is to know what data is actually out there and find the right balance of analysis and execution.

So, if you’d like some fries with this profile or if you’re interested in marketing data stories and how they apply to your company, email DeanBatson@gmail.com.
