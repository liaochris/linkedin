Kimba Livesay's Experience

Creativity and Life Coach

Kimba Livesay Coaching

January 2008 – Present (5 years 10 months) California

I am the creator and founder of Kimba Livesay Coaching. I help people create purpose and wellness through life and creativity coaching. My coaching programs are a magical blend of action based coaching, education, creativity and holistic mind, body and spirit resources that empower clients to heal their lives. I offer four creative and healing coaching programs. The Art of Life is designed to help people create a life they love. Get Your Art On helps artist move forward in their creative process. Inner Rhythms Activity Program provides healing play programs for elders and coaching for activity directors. Healing Play for Women’s Wellness supports women as they create healthy habits for well-being.

Certified Activity Consultant

Inner Rhythms Activity Program

April 1997 – Present (16 years 7 months) Stockton, California Area

I am the founder and creator of the Inner Rhythms Activity Program The activity program is designed for activity professionals who work in long-term care settings. The program is delivered to activity professionals through a series of on-line classes that I developed. Classes offered include Aromatherapy and Activities, Art and Soul in Long-Term Care and Good Vibrations for Well-Being. IRAP serves activity directors throughout the United States and Canada.

Director of Religious Education

First Unitarian Universalist Church of Stockton

September 2002 – June 2004 (1 year 10 months) Stockton, California Area

As the Director of Religious Education I provided curricula and professional leadership to support education programs for youth, adults and multigenerational groups in church congregations.

Aging Program Analyst II and Training Coordinator

California Department of Aging

June 1998 – August 2002 (4 years 3 months) Sacramento, California Area

I believe that I made a difference at the California Department of Aging in that I brought new ideas and resources to the Long-Term Care Ombudsman Program. One of my first tasks was to revise the core training curriculum. This was an enormous undertaking. However, after working with my teammates and acquiring outside resources we developed a comprehensive training manual. I also developed several training conferences and programs for the Ombudsman State Office and their 35 Representatives.

I researched and beta tested several software data programs. After a year of complex issues I was able to secure a new database for the department and their 35 Area Agency offices.
I was appointed the Liaison to the Department of Social Services and as a representative to the Governor's LTC nursing assistant task force.

In 2002 I received the employee of the month award for outstanding state service.

Area Marketing Director

Sunrise HealthCare Corporation

January 1997 – October 1998 (1 year 10 months) Stockton, California Area

At Sunrise I managed four area marketing managers that served Stockton, Ca. I developed, established and maintained marketing strategies to meet Sunrise HealthCare Corporation objectives that included direct marketing and promotional activities.

Director of Program Development

Atlas Senior Care

October 1997 – June 1998 (9 months)

Atlas Senior Care is an assisted living company that serves elders in northern California. As Director of Program Development I created manuals, training programs, tools and resources for each of the inter-disciplinary departments. I also was responsible for marketing plans and materials.

Actvities Coordintaor

South Haven Nursing Home

April 1991 – September 1997 (6 years 6 months) Birmingham, Alabama Area

I managed three activity staff and one social service staff member. Together we provided an ongoing program of activities designed to meet the interests and the physical, mental, and psychosocial well-being of 110 residents.
