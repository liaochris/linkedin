Special Projects Manager, Software Development Manager

RLG, Inc

Nonprofit; 51-200 employees; Information Technology and Services industry

April 2001 – February 2005 (3 years 11 months)

I led in the development of "Red Light Green." This was RLG's first open web product that would not be licensed by libraries for library patrons. Through techniques in user driven development, we developed an interface that users would find intuitive, despite the complexity that bibliographic record data, which puts importance on the distinctions between editions, imposes on a user who isn't sensitive to the distinctions between the first run on the university press versus the latest trade paperback printing. We also wanted to go beyond traditional library search ordering, and worked with a RecomMind, which was developing a probabilistic latent semantic indexing engine to improve the search experience.

While in this role, i also worked on managing the development of a fine arts digital image database and lead the team designing the relational database structure for the Union Catalog and migrating it from SPIRES to IBM's DB2.

Judith Elaine Bush's Summary

My training is in physics, my experience is in technology, but what I and my employer have discovered is that I manage people well. I enjoy creating a team and working with then to analyze problems, troubleshoot, or design new systems. I've broad experience in web application design, database design, and data center management. I'm passionate about our creative culture, the creative commons we share, and the balance of protecting the commons as well as creators. I'm excited about GIS and GPS technologies: the data, metadata, analysis, and distribution. I'm engaged with identity and privacy issues, first as an end user in the growing social web and now as a technology manager building an enterprise IDM system with SAML based on user centric identity principles.
