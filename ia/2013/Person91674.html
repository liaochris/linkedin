Mevlut "Hilmi" Cinar's Summary

Mevlut “Hilmi” Cinar is the Vice President of the Niagara Foundation, where he worked since 2007. Hilmi is a Turkish native and moved to the U.S. for his college education and stayed afterwards. The love he has for his home country of Turkey, as well as his adopted home of the United States, inspires him to work towards Niagara’s mission of fostering civic conversations and sustained relationships between people of different cultures and faiths.

Hilmi is a President’s Circle member of the Chicago Council on Global Affairs, and is also a member of the City Club of Chicago. He has a history of volunteer service through his work with the Big Brothers Big Sisters organization in Fairfax, Virginia. He is proud to serve on the NATO Welcoming Committee during the NATO Chicago Summit in 2012. He is also proud to have received multiple acknowledgements from the IL House of Representatives, one for his role in the NATO welcoming committee, and one for his work at Niagara Foundation towards building friendship, peace, and harmony in the State of Illinois. Hilmi is one of the government and civic leaders representing Chicago, selected by the U.S. State Department, for the Countering Violent Extremism Program in Dusseldorf, Germany in November, 2013.

Hilmi majored in International Business in his undergraduate studies at Strayer University in Virginia. He received his Master of Public Administration degree from Roosevelt University. He is also an alumnus of the Northwestern University Kellogg School of Management as a Kellogg Executive Scholar in Non Profit Management, and he holds a certificate from Cornell University Johnson School of Management. Hilmi is currently working towards his Master of Arts degree in Islam and Global Studies at the American Islamic College and his Master of Liberal Arts degree in International Relations from Harvard University.

He and his wife Ayse Arslan-Cinar have two sons. They reside in the Museum Campus neighborhood in Chicago.
