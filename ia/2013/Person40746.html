Elizabeth Shell's Summary

In short: I'm a data journalist, creative multimedia producer, wanna be hacker and designer of webness.

More detail? OK. I'm happiest when deep in a color choice decision for an infographic I'm working on to explain some wonky dataset. I've a Swiss-Army knife of skills, from video production to typography and even a bit of code. I'm not a CS expert but I like to get dirty with JavaScript. I enjoy using (and getting better at) these skills by finding interesting and underreported stories and creating compelling ways to tell them at the PBS NewsHour. How about an interactive graphic that explains five gun policies even NRA members would support? Or an infographic on beer to help explain global consumption trends over the last 40 years? Or a video interview with a Muppet to respond to a parody by Stephen Colbert? I'm a journalist, aspiring code geek, producer, editor, videographer, photographer, graphic artist, motion animator, wrangler of CMS fuddyduddyness, researcher, analytics translator, community engagement monitor, schedule organizer and consumer of copious amounts of popcorn.

I earned a master’s degree in multimedia and innovation journalism from the Walter Cronkite School of Journalism and Mass Communication at Arizona State University. I have two cats and am powered by purrs.

Specialties: Data-driven storytelling. Interactives. Multimedia production, including video, audio and graphics. Design. Storytelling. Photography. Print, web and audio.
