Michelle Lauer's Summary

Michelle Lauer (miche on D.O) started her Drupal adventures in 2006 and quickly became known for her incredible sense of detail while possessing the ability to see the big picture. These innate skills allowed Michelle to cultivate her specialty of site architecture and multi-phased deployments.

In addition to custom module development and theming, she develops and implements the strategy for complex content architecture from the end-user experience to the manageability by website administrators.

Michelle's resume includes presentations at DrupalCon Paris and San Francisco, DrupalCamp Montreal and coordinator and curriculum author for DrupalCamp NH Training Day. Her dedication to the Drupal community awarded her a scholarship to DrupalCon Copenhagen.

Michelle is a contributing author for the highly acclaimed book, The Definitive Guide to Drupal 7. She is also currently co-managing the New Hampshire Drupal Group.

In addition to her public appearances, Michelle contracted for Stonyfield.com as the sole person responsible for architecting and building a site with a focus on the content administrator experience.

Most recently, she was the smiling face teaching Drupal via Drupalize.me, Lullabot's archive of training videos.

When Michelle isn't fulfilling her passion as a Drupal developer and community advocate, she is working on NodeMaker a free distribution targeting new Drupal site builders allowing them to launch fully-featured sites very quickly.
