IT-014 Health Informatics (Standards Australia)

Nonprofit; 51-200 employees; Nonprofit Organization Management industry

2012 – Present (1 year) Australia

IT-014 has been in existence for 20 years and is the preeminent Standards Australia Technical Committee responsible for health information technology standards. These standards are developed to facilitate better health outcomes for all Australians. They aid information exchange. They are a critical foundation for the Personally Controlled Electronic Health Record (PCEHR). They create opportunity for future cost reduction as vendors and systems converge on Australian and international standards. They ease effort required for systems integration.

The work of IT-014 is vast and covers: quality of systems and data; health information structures and terminologies; information security and privacy; messaging and communication including clinical documents; patient administration; prescriptions; diagnostics; collaborative care; electronic health records; supply chain, telehealth; and clinical decision support systems.

IT-014 has over 200 dedicated Committee Members representing 42 major health related government departments and industry groups. IT-014 has published 88 documents and currently has 71 projects under development.

Additionally, the committee takes a leading role in ISO, HL7, CEN and many other major international standards bodies involved in health information technology standards. This ensures Australia’s health system is well aligned with emergent international best practice.

Participation in Standards development work generates personal, corporate and national benefits. The advantages you will gain from involvement include: access to knowledge and leading thinkers; network and career development; and experience working in groups, presenting and negotiating. Additionally, your organisation benefits from having a say in industry shaping standards and from your ever expanding social network and knowledge. IT-014 would welcome your involvement. If you are interested in participating please contact Naomi Ryan at naomi.ryan@standards.org.au.

Australian Technical Infrastructure Alliance

January 2010 – Present (4 years) Australia

The Australian Technical Infrastructure Alliance is made up of the CEOs of The Joint Accreditation System of Australia and New Zealand (JAS-ANZ), The National Association of Testing Authorities (NATA), Standards Australia and the National Measurement Institute (NMI).

Australia's Technical Infrastructure is the systems, organisations and resources that operate in the background: improving everyday life; supporting and fostering new technologies; underpinning commerce; assisting productivity and growth; facilitating international trade and providing the basis for confidence.

Everyday commercial transactions and international trade could not take place without the support of an effective technical infrastructure.

Such infrastructure, comprising metrology, standards, accreditation, and conformity assessment also impacts on our everyday lives such as having confidence in the accuracy of a kilogram of meat or a litre of petrol, getting correct results from a blood test, the way seatbelts in a car operate and the safety of our food.

National Centre for Security Standards

January 2003 – November 2010 (7 years 11 months) Australia

In December 2003, in an effort to support the Commonwealth Government and industry in addressing issues related to the security of critical infrastructure, Standards Australia established the National Centre for Security Standards.

The vision of the National Centre for Security Standards is to provide efficiencies to industry and government by developing and adopting standards and other documents about the protection of critical infrastructure.

Listed below are the objectives of the National Centre for Security Standards.

Facilitate the timely identification, development, and adoption of standards responsive to the needs identified by key stakeholders.

Provide a forum to define needs, determine work plans and establish priorities for updating standards or creating new standards.

Solicit participation from national security-related sectors.

As appropriate, coordinate with other national, regional, and international efforts addressing national security standards.

Provide guidelines in a holistic and integrated approach to security.
