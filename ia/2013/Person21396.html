Cédric Laurant's Summary

Owner of Cedric Laurant Law Firm, a sole proprietorship law firm that works on projects in Latin America, the European Union and the United States, and also more globally in the areas of data protection, online and consumer privacy, telecommunications, information governance and information security for international organizations, governments, trade associations, NGOs and the private sector.

With 15 years of experience under my belt in those fields, I handle legal research and public policy projects, government affairs and public advocacy missions, and offer technical assistance, training and capacity building services.

I am frequently invited to speak at conferences around the world on data protection and online privacy issues, especially in Europe, the United States and Latin America, and have testified before the European Parliament, US Congress and governmental bodies.

Between 2007 and 2008, I was Visiting Law Professor at the University of los Andes in Bogota in Colombia, and between 2001 and 2006, the Electronic Privacy Information Center's International Privacy Director in Washington, DC.

I am an attorney, member of the District of Columbia Bar (US), and graduated from the University of Louvain (UCL) in Belgium, and Columbia University Law School in New York.

Specialties: global data privacy compliance, privacy, data protection, international data privacy, Internet policy, information privacy, online profiling, consumer protection, consumer privacy, information security, data breaches and breach notification, public advocacy, behavioural advertising, telecommunications privacy, government affairs, government surveillance, public policy, e-commerce, cybersecurity, Internet law, social media, Internet governance, civil society, fundraising, capacity building & training, teaching.

  • EU-VIETNAM MUTRAP III (Multilateral Trade Assistance Project III) (VIET NAM)

    • April 2012 to June 2012
    Team Members: Cédric Laurant,

    European Union-funded project to support the Ministry of Industry and Trade of Viet Nam ("MOIT") on Studies and Researches for Drafting a New Decree on E-Commerce.
    This activity is part of the second component of "MUTRAP III", aiming to increase the coordination of the MOIT with the business sector, training and research institutions to develop a coherent, social and environmental sustainable trade integration strategy. The activity aims to support MOIT in drafting a new Decree providing an efficient regulation for e-commerce.
    Its main objective is to support MOIT in improving the regulation of E-Commerce through the production of a new Draft Decree substituting the existing Decree 57/2006/ND-CP. Specifically, the activity aims to provide MOIT with the relevant information and best practices regarding the regulations of e-commerce in other countries, with particular focus on the legal framework and on the provisions for ensuring the adequate protection of intellectual property rights and consumers’ rights. The activity also aims to provide MOIT with inputs for the formulation of specific provisions on certain aspects of e-commerce, such as electronic contracting, electronic payment, personal information protection, data security, and regulation of special e-commerce business models.

    Team Members: Cédric Laurant

  • BLOG: "Information Security Breaches & The Law"

    • Cédric Laurant & Marie-Andrée Weiss
    • June 1, 2010
    Authors: Cédric Laurant, Marie-Andrée Weiss, Armando Becerra, Joseph Santangelo, Farid Bouguettaya, Renato Monteiro, Annie C. Bai

    This blog is a project that I started with Marie-Andrée Weiss in June 2010. It is written in English, French, Portuguese and Spanish, and includes opinions, comments on recent news, laws or other developments, research notes and conference reports in the area of information security breaches, mainly in Europe, the United States and Latin America.

  • European Privacy & Human Rights 2010

    • Privacy International, Central European University & EPIC
    • January 28, 2011
    Authors: Cédric Laurant, Matteo E. Bonfanti, Gus Hosein

    Research Director, and Editor with Matteo Bonfanti, of this collective work to which more than 90 experts from all over Europe, as well as the Privacy International and Central European University's Center for Media and Communication Studies' research teams contributed.
    Full report available at <https://www.privacyinternational.org/sites/privacyinternational.org/files/ephr_full.pdf>.
    "European Privacy and Human Rights" (EPHR) investigates the European landscape of national privacy and data protection laws and regulations, as well as any other laws or recent developments with an impact on privacy. The research field encompasses jurisdictions of all 27 EU Member States, 2 EFTA countries (Norway and Switzerland), 3 EU accession candidate countries (Croatia, Macedonia and Turkey), and the EU itself as a jurisdiction. The study consists of 33 reports, an overview presenting a comparative legal and policy analysis of main privacy topics with a privacy ranking for all countries surveyed.
    It has been translated into the language of all countries surveyed.
    EPHR builds on the legacy of EPIC and Privacy International's "Privacy and Human Rights" report. It is probably the most comprehensive report on privacy and data protection in Europe ever published.
    This report was funded by the European Commission's Special Programme "Fundamental Rights and Citizenship," 2007-2013.
