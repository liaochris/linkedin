Bertil Hatt's Summary

I help companies and institutions make sense of the data they have access to, and how to use them to answer their business questions.

I am specialised on complex interaction graphs but I’m familiar with atypical (non-structured, text-based, bayesian or machine learning) and more classic statistical techniques (component analysis, recommendation, regression, actuarial estimates).

I started a PhD to understand the success of social services (messaging, telephone, night-clubs, contact-based sites, etc.) based on the structure of the underlying network. I have published on privacy concerns, data gravity, graph complementary and data informed product management and algorithms to forecast a diffusion patterns. I haven’t been able to access large scale data with my competition-enforcement based agenda, though—hence my switch back to consulting.

I used to work as a strategy and energy consultant, and worked for financial institutions. I’ve tough more class to list them all, lately Digital economics.

My speciality include Economic modeling (Social network, innovation valuation); Decyphering the added value in communication services, and measuring Simplicity of use and the benefit of Open platforms.
