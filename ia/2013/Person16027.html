Brad Canham's Publications

  • Positioning an Online Niche Sport Magazine for Success

    • University of St. Thomas
    • May 15, 2009
    Authors: Brad Canham

    Internet Use Theories and Internet Niche Audiences

    Although little readily accessible research has been published on the
    subject of online niche magazines (Ingham, 2008), scholars have begun to
    examine how and why people use the internet to find in-depth, specialized
    information online (Rife, Lacy, and Varouhakis, 2008).
    In general there appears to be a great deal of effort describing the growth
    of the Internet and the types of information accessed, but little research into the
    theoretical “frameworks” (Rife, et. al., 2008) for exploring how the Internet is
    integrated into individuals lives.
    However, an article, (Chyi and Lasora, 1999) discusses theories involved
    in the adoption of innovations in communication mediums, including the Internet.
    The article cites “complexity” as an impediment to adoption of online
    communication tools. However, even at that time (1999) Chyi and Lasora noted
    that an initial hesitancy to adopt the Internet was fading away as online
    publication become ubiquitous, the audience was becoming familiarized with the
    Internet, and the medium itself was increasingly viewed as simpler and simpler to
    use.

    In fact, at the time of the Chyi and Lasora, 1999 study the authors
    correctly posited that an Internet user’s impression of the medium’s complexity
    was likely to fade quickly.
    A few researchers, such as both (Chyi and Lasora, 1999) and (Rife, et al.,
    2008) applied “uses and gratifications” theories to the study of Internet use.
    While there continues to be extensive experimentation in applying research and
    theory to developing successful online niche magazine models (Ives, 2008), in
    general, identifying individual motivations for the use of online media content
    remains an rapidly evolving area of study.

    Theoretical models exploring the use of media readers of online niche
    magazines, include Media System Dependency Theory (Rife, et al., 2008) and
    Time Based Accounting of Value (Gershuny, 2005) and (Ingham 2008).
