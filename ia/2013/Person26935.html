Corbin Norman's Projects

  • Independent Cost Estimate for Affordable Care Act (ACA)

    • September 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Corbin Norman, Kevin McKeel,

    Enacted on March 23, 2010, the Patient Protection Affordable Care Act (PPACA) involves major health care stakeholders, including federal and state governments, employers, insurers, and health care providers, in an attempt to reform the private insurance market and expand health coverage to the uninsured. To implement PPACA and its large set of tax law changes, the Client had to work closely with partner agencies to develop information technology systems that can share data with other agencies.

    The Client internally developed a cost estimate for PPACA in October 2010 however it did not fully meet best practices for a reliable cost estimate, including no timely updates to ensure accuracy. The Client tapped Logapps LLC to provide independent expertise in cost estimation to aid its very own in conducting a structural analysis for all Information Technology (IT) resources and associated costs of all system and applications of ACA from development to deployment.

  • Financial Management System Hosting – Review of Strategic Business Options

    • January 2013 to Present
    Team Members: Corbin Norman, , Kevin McKeel

    At the request of the Client, Logapps LLC was tasked to conduct a study composed of two primary objectives: 1) provide a business case analysis (BCA) that can be used to determine the most advantageous system architecture and acquisition strategy; and 2) provide cost and price analysis that can be used as an Independent Government Cost Estimate (IGCE) and budget baseline.

  • Weather Radio Improvement Program 2 Independent Cost Estimate (ICE2)

    • December 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Corbin Norman, Kevin McKeel

    At the request of the WRIP-2 Program Manager Logapps LLC was tasked as a sub-contractor to Compumatics Group to provide two Independent Cost Estimates; 1) Threshold requirements and 2) Objective requirements, using cost estimation techniques that are consistent with the GAO Cost Estimating and Assessment Guide. Compumatics and Logapps LLC delivered two cost estimates (IOC & FOC) to the client that supported the program’s budget leading to the Milestone Decision Authority for project implementation approval.

  • Improving Service Deliver and Efficiency: Emily Couric Clinical Cancer Center System Analysis

    • May 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Corbin Norman

    This study called for an evaluation of the University of Virginia Health System Emily Couric Clinical Cancer Center, focusing in particular on the scheduling, communication and patient service aspects of therapeutic treatments and the complexities of all related demands services that support these treatments. These demands produce stress upon the UVa Cancer Center’s ability to provide efficient and customer-focused care that leads to patient satisfaction, better employee morale, ease of access to required treatment services, and effective staff/provider engagement. The main goal of this study was to increase the patient experience while maintaining probability and competitiveness, taking into account patients and caregivers, staff/faculty, and operational environment.

  • Software Safety Analysis: An Integrative Approach

    • July 2009 to Present
    Team Members: Corbin Norman

    Every significant safety violation of a system has the potential for great harm and monetary loss. By identifying and preventing even one of these from occurring, catastrophe and millions of dollars in damage can be avoided. Taking into consideration that software can fail in any critical system and that the impact these failures have is substantial, in this study the team’s goal was to develop and refine an improved process for identifying and eliminating as many failure modes in a system as possible. We intended to integrate the results of root cause analysis with functional specifications. UML behavioral state machines were utilized exclusively for the functional model of the chosen system along with fault trees taken as input for root cause analysis.

  • Identifying Methodologies and Processes Used to Compare Programming Languages: A Systematic Literature

    • August 2010 to Present
    Team Members: Corbin Norman

    Software engineering researchers previously have compared the capabilities and characteristics of different programming languages. This method of comparison does not provide enough empirical evidence to validate the notion of which language is most useful for a given task. This literature review was aimed at understanding the current methodologies used by the research community in comparing programming languages so better processes could be created to aid these researches in language comparison.
