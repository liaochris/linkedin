Greg Vatt's Summary

Greg graduated from Colorado State University in 1975, with a BSEE, and received his MSEE from Arizona State University in 1979, specializing in Communications Theory.

Greg is an author of many technical papers and articles, and an inventor, with 13 U.S. patents issued.

Greg is an international lecturer, having made presentations world-wide to audiences including both technical and business forums, universities, regulatory bodies, corporate boards, as well as to the ITU, ETSI, and FCC.

Greg has developed and taught graduate level courses on Receiver Design Techniques and on Wireless Communications System Design.

Greg was also the 4th engineer to become involved in the conceptual development of what later became known as the Iridium Personal Communications System, where he made significant contributions to that communication system, most notably the time-division multiplexing within the system and especially the “floating” time-division technology used in the inter-satellite links. Greg also devised a significant portion of the paging concept which has been part of the system since its early conception.

Greg also was the first person to create the Motorola concept for broadband communications with a constellation of low-earth satellites in what publicly came to be known as Celestri, Millennium, and M-Star. Greg led both the business and technical teams which created the first FCC filings for these systems.

Greg distinguished himself while working on several technical and business teams while at Motorola, and was inducted into the Motorola Science Advisory Board.
