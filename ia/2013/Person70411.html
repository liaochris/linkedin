Judy Pagano's Summary

I have specialized in residential real estate sales for many areas of Boston including Back Bay, South End, Beacon Hill, Fenway, the Waterfront, and surrounding areas since 2003. Striving to provide my clients with superior results is a passion of mine. My hard work ethic, honest attitude, knowledge of the real estate market, and attention to detail will guide each sale with ease.

Having been a resident of the Back Bay and The Fenway for seventeen years, I have extensive knowledge of all Boston neighborhoods and the historic homes located within.

Prior to my real estate experience I was hired by business owners to reorganize their business operationally and redevelop their marketability. I was extremely successful at improving their exposure, business operations, and financial growth. Marketing is a specialty of mine and I have relished each opportunity to bring a new level of success to each business.

My loyalty to my clients is a true asset to anyone I represent.

      The White Aisle Foundation

      Our Mission:

      The White Aisle Foundation was established to honor the life of Rebecca Garrison-Sokoloff, founder and owner of The White Aisle, who died of melanoma on November 13, 2011. Through the foundation, her family and friends raise funds for melanoma research and patient support, as well as for other charities that Rebecca held dear during her life.

      The White Aisle Foundation is a support-driven organization based in Baltimore, Maryland. We have applied for and are awaiting 501(c)3 not for profit status. The foundation helps fund research that will lead to effective treatments and, ultimately, a cure for melanoma; provides education on melanoma prevention and treatment; and supports organizations that provide help with medical expenses to those with melanoma. Honoring the love and concern Rebecca had for the people of Cambodia, the foundation also supports charities that provide housing, education, and health care for children and young women in that country.

      For more information and to donate go to
      www.whiteaisle.org
