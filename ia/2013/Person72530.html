Karyn Romeis' Summary

I am currently working on my second contract as LD Lead for Houghton Mifflin Harcourt on a project producing digital learning materials for the US GK-6 market.

The current contract has been extended a few times and now comes to an end in August 2013 (or that's the plan at the moment, anyway, but you know how these things can change), whereafter I will be available for the next interesting project.

This is a bit about me and what I like to do:

* I am passionate about learning, and passionate about learners. I will go to great lengths to help people reach a new place in their journey, whether it be personal or professional. Can there be a better way to end a day than to know you gave someone a leg up to something they couldn't access before?
* I can see myself helping an organisation streamline its L&D provision, un-bottle-necking things, and embedding learning in the workflow. Taking learning to the point of need. That would be my dream job!
* I'd love to work with organisations who are venturing out into the realms of using social media, either for corporate/commercial identity purposes, or as learning tools.
* I have worked across a wide range of sectors and have no objection to going back to any of them. I have never actually worked for a university, but, during my Master's degree studies often thought how much I would love to help faculty members move into the spaces their students already occupy (and some that they don't), to harness the learning potential.
* I enjoy working with people who love what they do. I have claimed the term 'workafrolic' for myself, and prefer working with others who could be described in the same way.

Karyn Romeis' Publications

  • What has been the impact of the use of social media on my professional practice as a corporate learning professional?

    • Wetpaint
    • September 2009
    Authors: Karyn Romeis

    Social media are increasingly becoming part of everyday life, and the term web 2.0 is now a common feature of any conversation about technology. This project sets out to explore the extent to which social media and web 2.0 tools have impacted the learning experience of a workplace learning professional.

    Using connectivism as a theoretical framework and first person narrative enquiry as the methodology, I present my project in the form of a wiki, inviting the wider community to engage in the discussion.
