VP and GC

Beal Aerospace

Privately Held; 201-500 employees; Banking industry

1998 – 2001 (3 years)

Beal Aerospace was a privately funded effort to develop a new, heavy launch capability for commercial payloads to space. My role at Beal Aerospace was much more than being the GC; it included responsibility for governmental relations, public relations, environmental compliance, contracting, and much more. Specific examples of unique opportunities I enjoyed included negotiating an agreement with the country of Guyana to give Beal Aerospace the right to build a very large launch facility in that country, and managing all of our activities in the U.S. Virgin Islands as well as within the country of Anguilla.

Special Assistant to Assistant Secretary of Defense (P&L)

United States Department of Defense

Government Agency; 10,001+ employees; Military industry

1990 – 1993 (3 years)

My job was to help ensure that the Assistant Secretary's goals and priorities were achieved. As one example, I had primary responsibility for reforming the National Defense Stockpile. That effort was successful, saving the DoD an estimated $8 billion and for which I received the Secretary of Defense Medal for Outstanding Public Service (the highest medal that can be awarded).
