Andrew Herbert's Projects

  • Washington Warriors (Redskins Redesign)

    • January 2013 to Present
    Team Members: Andrew Herbert

    Washington, D.C. is a city of equality, all aspects of this great city should reflect that characteristic. This was a self-initiated project to rebrand the Washington Redskins with the goal being to achieve not only a more politically correct mascot name, but to also provide DC with a stronger more modern logo and color scheme (holding true to the traditional colors). Adding enhanced uniform detail with more visually intriguing icons and logo marks for DC's beloved team was a priority.

  • Halcyon Tea Company Branding & Packaging

    • April 2010 to September 2011
    Team Members: Andrew Herbert

    Halcyon Tea package design was a project for Halcyon Tea of San Diego, CA. The entire idea of this project was to create unique special edition packaging with a separate branding concept from their existing logo and identity to showcase four of their most rare and exotic tea blends.

  • Gibson Annual Report 2008

Andrew Herbert's Publications

  • The Fractured Road - Trilogy

    • Britt Holewinski
    • January 1, 2013
    Authors: Andrew Herbert

    I was the graphic designer for author Britt Holewinski on her upcoming books two and three of the trilogy.

    And so it begins...a vision of life without supervision, rules, or elected leaders. Disorder will become the "new normal" in places where civility once existed. Knowledge of generations past is omnipresent and always beyond reach, and technology a haunting reminder of times that will never return. For the survivors, they each must decide: fight for order and peace, or give in to the hopelessness.

  • GrowthThinking: Building the New Growth Enterprise

    • The Growth Strategy Company
    • October 1, 2012
    Authors: Andrew Herbert

    I was the graphic designer for authors, Keary Crawford and Wayne Simmons of The Growth Strategy Company. This book was written for companies that are facing the demand to reaccelerate growth and also those that may be successful today but wish to take preemptive measures to avert any decline and ensure continued growth. GrowthThinking® provides an antidote to the concept that industry is doomed to failure, slow growth, and decline. This book counters that negative ideology by equipping companies with an alternative view that business growth and renewal can be achieved and sustained.
