Eleanor Feldman Barbera, PhD's Summary

Author of The Savvy Resident's Guide, Speaker, and Consulting Psychologist in Nursing Homes, specializing in humanizing the medical experience. Founder of My Better Nursing Home, a forum designed to raise awareness of the mental health needs of residents and to expand the team model to include residents, staff, families, and governing bodies everywhere. Let's work together to make nursing homes better.

Specialties

Advocate for residents, nursing homes, and the use of psychology and technology to improve nursing home care.

Eleanor Feldman Barbera, PhD's Experience

Speaker, Author of The Savvy Resident's Guide, Psychologist, Founder

My Better Nursing Home

1996 – Present (17 years)

My Better Nursing Home is devoted to sharing the insights gathered during my years as a consulting psychologist, speaking with residents at their bedsides. I'm convinced that by better integrating mental and physical health care, we can reduce costs and improve the care and satisfaction of residents, families, and nursing home staff.

Clinical Care Manager and Service Provider

Managed Mental Health Care

1993 – 1996 (3 years)

As a mental health and substance abuse care manager for two different insurance companies, I had a fascinating window into how mental health "carve-outs" work. Through computer access to both the medical and mental health/substance abuse benefits, I could see that the substance abusing clients were high utilizers of medical benefits. Due to confidentiality, however, I was unable to notify the medical providers that the person they were treating had a substance abuse problem requiring intervention. This experience no doubt contributes to my passion for integrating mental and physical health care.

Psychologist

Manhattan Psychiatric Center

1989 – 1993 (4 years)

I loved working with the seriously mentally ill and MICA (mentally ill chemical abusers) patients at this inner-city State psychiatric center. The education, teamwork, and innovation were unparalleled, and the experience informs my work in nursing homes. Many of the techniques are applicable to the nursing home setting, such as the focus on the overall tone of the unit and attention to small, but important, behavioral changes in individuals.

Eleanor Feldman Barbera, PhD's Projects

  • The Savvy Resident's Guide: Everything You Wanted to Know About Your Nursing Home Stay But Were Afraid to Ask

    • October 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Eleanor Feldman Barbera, PhD

    In The Savvy Resident's Guide, psychologist Eleanor Feldman Barbera explains how nursing homes work and how people can make the most of their stays. This large-print guide is the only book available that is directed specifically toward residents. Its calm, pragmatic approach will reassure readers and give them practical tools for handling a nursing home stay.

    Families will find answers to many of their own questions about nursing homes in The Savvy Resident's Guide. Reading the stories of the resident narrators will help family members understand what Mom or Dad worries about after they've left for the day.

    Staff can use The Guide as a helpful adjunct to their work since it answers questions without requiring staff time. It's a ready reference book that makes an ideal addition to the admissions package.

    Students of many disciplines -- nursing, psychology, social work, rehab, medicine, etc. -- will benefit from this overview of life in long-term care. They'll gain insight into the typical concerns of nursing home residents and absorb a person-centered approach to managing these concerns.

    Join Dr. El and her group of resident narrators in learning how residents can thrive in a nursing home. Visit MyBetterNursingHome.com to find resources to create long-term care where EVERYBODY thrives.
