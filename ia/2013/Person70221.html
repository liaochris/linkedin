Juanita Hardy's Summary

Juanita Hardy is Managing Principal of Tiger Management Consulting Group, LLC, a global business consulting and training firm, which she established in 2006, after retiring from a 31 year career with IBM. Ms. Hardy has led many large scale business transformation initiatives for public and private sector clients and trained business professionals worldwide in the US, Asia, Australia, and South America. For Tiger Management, she developed the I3 (I-cubed) methodology that underpins the firm’s offerings. She has served as an Executive Coach for Right Management since 2006.

Ms. Hardy was featured among 13 women in a book, Montgomery County Women Making It (2011). She is a 2010 recipient of the Washington Business Journal’s Minority Business Leaders Award. She serves on the Board of Women's Council on Energy and the Environment, Maryland China Business Council, and Mentor Foundation USA. Ms. Hardy’s papers and articles have been featured in "The Roadmap to 2020" published by Quantum Leaps and sponsored by IBM, Enterprising Women Magazine, and The Peking University Business Review published by Peking University, Beijing. She is a sought after speaker, recently speaking on "Culture and Business" at the Global Conference of the World Future Society. She has been featured in Smart CEO and the Gazette News of Business and Politics. Ms. Hardy has a passion for art and forging cross cultural connections. She serves on several non-profit art boards, including the Art Advisory Board for the University of Maryland University College and Millennium Arts Salon, which she co-founded in 2000.

Ms. Hardy received a Bachelor of Science Degree from Livingstone College. She did graduate work in Operations Research at George Washington University. Ms. Hardy is a 2006 graduate of the inaugural program on entrepreneurship, ACTiVATE, at the University of Maryland Baltimore County.

  • The Future of World Culture and the Role of Business

    • September 2011 to Present
    Team Members: Juanita Hardy, Research Assistant

    There are several trends that are shaping our world culture of tomorrow, and have implications for our global economy.
    •Demography: The world population is 6.9B today and will swell to 9.5B by 2050, with most of this growth occurring in urban cities of developing countries. At the same time, world fertility rates are declining, and mortality rates are increasing, suggesting that a predominant portion of the workforce of developed countries will need to be fed by immigration from developing countries.
    •Economy: The main driver for economic expansion will come from developing countries. For example, the combined GDP of US, Europe, and Canada is projected to double by 2050, whereas the GDP of developing countries will increase by a factor of 5. Local markets are being replaced by global markets for goods, services, and labor.

    •Technology: The pace of technology breakthroughs will accelerate faster than ever before. This will not be foreign to “Millennials” (born between 1977 and 1997) who by 2015 will make up 47 per cent of the [US] workforce. Most have routinely used technology from their early years and will expect it to be part of their work and learning experience. Technology advancements are facilitating cyber-connections and giving birth to virtual relationships by individuals and businesses and thereby fostering a more tightly integrated, interdependent, and linked world.

    What are the implications of these trends to world culture?
    What role should businesses play in shaping world culture while fostering economic growth?
    How can “cultural dexterity” be an advantage in an increasingly competitive global market?

    These questions and more will be explored through interactive discussion and presentation, enabled by a video case study, to demonstrate how honoring differences and bridging cultural gaps in business can impact a company’s bottom line and promote a healthy world culture for our future.
