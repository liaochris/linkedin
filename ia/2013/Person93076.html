Michael Taube's Publications

  • Development of a priori Level Controller Tuning for Vertical & Horizontal Vessels

    • Self Published, White Paper
    Authors: Michael Taube

    Development of controller tuning is usually done by performing step tests on a running plant or creating a dynamic simlation of the process in question, requiring considerable time & effort. A vast majority of "support loops" - such as level controllers - can be tuned without such efforts as they are strictly a function of volume, geometry & flow. This white paper provides the mathematical derivation of Process & PID Controller Gain for Horizontal & Veritcal Tank Levels. A guide for tuning is also given in the appendix that illustrates robust tuning response for SP changes & unmeasured disturbances.

  • Dynamic Analysis: The forgotten side of Process Design

    • Presentation: Process Plant Debottlenecking & Optimisation Conference, Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia
    • July 2010
    Authors: Michael Taube

    Plants built in the 1960s and 70s could achieve up to 50% (or more) additional throughput with only nominal equipment upgrades. Increasing fidelity of computer-aided design software (steady state process simulators) resulted in squeezing the “fat” out of every element of new plant designs. Foregoing a dynamic analysis of key control points could result in suboptimal design, poor performance and lost opportunities.
    The presentation addresses the following key points:
    •Process design before digital computers required many engineers performing tedious calculations with “mechanical computers”.
    •The impact of process dynamic characteristics on process behavior was never considered with little or no consequence.
    •New plants designed via simulation software sometimes encountered instability or poor control at design rates.
    •Dynamic analysis of key control points early in the design process ensures that equipment sizing and capacity are sufficient to handle nominal process disturbances at design rates.

  • Regaining Operating Excellence through Enhanced Training

    • Presentation: Gulf Publishing's International Refining & Petrochem Conference, New Delhi, India
    • July 11, 2013
    Authors: Michael Taube, Joe Bonem, Andy Gibbins

    Given the number of engineers around the world, one might conclude that company performance would be on the rise. However, when examining actual performance, one is confronted by the rampant existence of knowledge & experience gaps. The reasons for these deficiencies are multiple. This presentation addresses some of the underlying causes and proposes an approach to solving them.

  • Distillation Control Primer

    • Presentation: Auckland University Chemical & Materials Engineering Plant Design Class
    • September 27, 2013
    Authors: Michael Taube

    This presentation reviews current design practices affecting distillation columns and provides fundamental guidelines for proper MV-CV pairing, including a non-mathematical relative gain analysis. Real-world examples of improper controls design are reviewed and how to correct same. Lastly, a design challenge of a real-world multicomponent distillation problem is given for the attendees to solve.
