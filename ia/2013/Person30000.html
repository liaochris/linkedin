Founder & Director

Music Studio on Harrison Hill

January 2010 – May 2013 (3 years 5 months) 2800 SE Harrison Street Portland Oregon

What do you do with a large but nearly empty church and lots of music teachers in a family-friendly, arts-loving neighborhood? I could not resist the opportunity to combine my interests in private music instruction and arts administration in developing this music education cooperative, a one-of-a-kind alternative to the traditional music school.

I oversee the Cooperative Teaching Artist program, which provides beautiful teaching spaces, collective promotion, community collaboration opportunities, and professional development to privately contracted music teachers. We collectively develop fundraising opportunities, obtain grants, promote the cooperative, and arrange the building schedule.

In 2011, I collaborated with our host, St. David of Wales and JOIN, a non-profit working to support the efforts of homeless individuals and families to transition off the streets and into permanent housing, to obtain a program grant from SE Uplift and create an outreach branch of our cooperative. We created a free guitar class where "homed," "recently-homed," and "homeless" music-makers learned basic chords together through familiar folk and rock songs to strum and sing. In 2012, we added Sound & Rhythm Drumming into the mix and ran a similar program for ten weeks with the infections rhythms and movements of African drumming.

Curator for Community and Music

Saint David of Wales Church

September 2009 – May 2013 (3 years 9 months) 2800 SE Harrison Street Portland Oregon

I have learned more than possible to list at this jack-of-all-trades position managing the growing community of non-profits, meetings, and special events filling the halls of a very large church in inner-SE Portland with music, dance, children, educators, and more. My role as Chief Operating Officer for this small non-profit organization with huge dreams requires that I flex both my mico (communication with building partners, financial management in Quickbooks, public relations, volunteer recruitment and management, website and social media development) and macro (managing staff and interns, grant writing and reporting, strategic planning) muscles with equal amounts of enthusiasm, ingenuity, and attention to detail.

The church has now completely transformed in the past three years from an empty brick building into a vibrant community hub thanks to the hard work of our small but dedicated staff.

Office Manager

Metropolitan Youth Symphony

October 2012 – April 2013 (7 months) 4800 SW Macadam Avenue, Suite 105 Portland Oregon 97239

I am a versatile leader helping to educate, advocate for, and promote 400 young musicians in orchestral and jazz ensembles. My primary responsibilities are managing ticket sales and being the primary point of communication with families, but I am most proud of my collaborative work with my colleagues in fundraising, grant writing, financial management, and production within our small staff.

I am very excited to bring my expertise and experience as a music educator, successful track record helping new and old organizations grow, and knack for community outreach to this wonderful non-profit.

Accompanist

Portland Peace Choir

October 2009 – March 2012 (2 years 6 months) 2800 SE Harrison Street Portland Oregon

It was a true pleasure to collaborate with this new but growing community choir. I accompanied their weekly rehearsals and seasonal performances, including wonderful collaborations with other musicians and was an active member of the music planning committee. I was also very luck to also share my love of choral music education as their substitute conductor, helping this un-auditioned ensemble learn more about reading music, utilizing their breath, vocal relaxation through movement, and basic conducting patterns.

Middle School Music Specialist

Cathedral School

October 2010 – June 2011 (9 months)

Added music to the middle school for the first time but the first month was less than successful with your elementary music educator as the teacher? Sign me up!

I deftly helped rescue this new program for grades 6-8 by expanding use of Orff percussion instruments in sound explorations, improvisations, and ensemble repertoire. Introduced Music Ace Maestro software to allow differentiated instruction in music theory, listening, collaboration, and composition. To top it all off, I created a Middle School Music Showcase to help bring the parents, rising middle school students, and classroom teacher together to celebrate this monumental year with performances and compositions.

Music Educator

Holy Trinity Catholic School

Education Management industry

August 2010 – June 2011 (11 months)

Following in the footsteps of a highly successful and very popular music educator at this school was a daunting tasks. However, I worked closely with the already established traditions while infusing my instruction with the National Standards for Music Education, tools for healthy singing, and games to encourage creativity, ensemble, and improvisation.

I also developed new middle school music electives, including an Orff jazz ensemble, mixed chorus, and main stage Broadway, Jr. production and led several formal and informal school-wide performances.
