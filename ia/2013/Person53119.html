Hildy Gottlieb's Summary

Hildy Gottlieb is an activist, teacher, explorer, TEDx speaker http://ow.ly/94FO2 and catalyst for social change.

Hildy is co-founder and Chief Boundary Pusher at Creating the Future, a living laboratory for accomplishing social change. Find out how you can make visionary social change the result of ALL social change efforts at http://creatingthefuture.org/

Her book, "The Pollyanna Principles: Reinventing 'Nonprofit Organizations' to Create the Future of Our World," details her groundbreaking approach to aiming social change groups at their highest potential for creating the future of our world. (Download the first 4 chapters of the book here: http://PollyannaPrinciples.org )

Hildy helped develop the Masters degree program in Community Leadership at Duquesne University, where she and her partner, Dimitri Petropolis, taught as professors. Hildy’s books are used as texts in undergraduate and graduate programs around the world.

Hildy’s numerous awards include a Points of Light Citation from President Bill Clinton. Her writing appears in various publications including the Chronicle of Philanthropy, where she currently hosts the monthly podcast, Making Change – interviewing change leaders from around the world. In addition to writing for Creating the Future's blogs, Hildy is a regular blogger for The Huffington Post and the Fearless Revolution Blog.

When not working, Hildy can be found in the garden, at the movies, shooting photos, or watching the Daily Show.

Hildy's books include:
• The Pollyanna Principles: Reinventing 'Nonprofit Organizations' to Create the Future of Our World
• FriendRaising: Community Engagement Strategies for Boards Who Hate Fundraising but Love Making Friends
• Community Engagement Step-by-Step Action Kit
• Board Recruitment & Orientation: A Step-by-Step, Common Sense Guide

Specialties: Accomplishing social change.
