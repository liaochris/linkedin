Lorenzo Brusattin's Publications

  • Candidate Visual Appearance as a Shortcut for Both Sophisticated and Unsophisticated Voters: Evidence from a Spanish Online Study

    • International Journal of Public Opinion Research - Oxford University Press
    • November 13, 2011
    Authors: Lorenzo Brusattin

    An experiment was designed to ascertain if political sophistication helps the voter focus on the available information about the ideology of candidates as opposed to drawing inferences on the candidates' personality based on their photos. With this purpose in mind, 937 participants in a web questionnaire were presented with two unknown hypothetical candidates and were asked to vote for one of them. Information on the candidates was presented (and successively manipulated) in the fashion of electoral posters showing both the candidates' pictures as well as a set of policy statements. According to the findings, the photograph of a candidate significantly influences voter behavior at the ballot box. Moreover, a higher level of political sophistication does not prevent or reduce the possibility that such visual information may have a bearing on a candidate's electoral success.

  • Late Anti-Communism as a Shortcut: The Success of Forza Italia in the 1994 Italian Election

    • South European Society & Politics - Taylor & Francis
    • December 12, 2007
    Authors: Lorenzo Brusattin

    The article undertakes an analysis of Forza Italia's success in the 1994 Italian election. While drawing from the insights of political psychology, it puts forward an explanatory hypothesis based both on the historical legacy of the strong polarization of the Italian electorate and on the use of anti-communism as a cognitive shortcut by relatively uninformed voters. The hypothesis, which is supported by the data, offers new insight into the consequences of the lack of sophistication in the electorate, and lends substance to some scepticism about the remedial effect of heuristic reasoning on poor political knowledge.

  • Evaluación de programas de televisión a través de grupos de discusión Online: Oportunidades y limitaciones

    • Investigación y Marketing - AEDEMO
    • June 30, 2006
    Authors: Lorenzo Brusattin

    Este artículo analiza las ventajas e inconvenientes que se hallan en la investigación cualitativa online, más concretamente en los
    focus group realizados a través de un software de chat. Para ello se sirve de una experiencia real en la que se investigaba la
    opinión de los televidentes ante un programa del prime time televisivo.
    Como puntos fuertes de esta novedosa técnica se destacan los costes reducidos, la trascripción automática de los datos, la
    reducción de los efectos de social desirability que la proximidad física de los participantes puede causar y la mayor libertad de
    expresión derivada del anonimato.
    Como puntos débiles se detectan la distancia entre los participantes, su consecuente falta de interacción y el sesgo derivado
    del reclutamiento de usuarios de Internet. No obstante, se consideran infundadas las dudas sobre la falta de privacidad y la
    necesidad de la presencia del moderador para ﬁltrar las intervenciones.
    En deﬁnitiva, cabe decir que, pese al escepticismo de algunos, los grupos de discusión online pueden tener un gran futuro como
    herramienta de investigación de mercados.
