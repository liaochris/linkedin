Ann Oliveri's Publications

  • What's Really the Matter? How to Ask the Right Questions

    • ASAE The Center for Association Leadership
    • March 26, 2013
    Authors: Ann Oliveri

    When a consultant begins work with an association, the task at hand may only scratch the surface of the association's deeper challenges and dynamics. Asking the right questions is key to finding the right solutions. Six association consultants share the questions they ask to get to the heart of an issue.

  • Visions for the Future essay: Associations as Engines of Change

    • Associations NOW
    • January 2010
    Authors: Ann Oliveri

    Long known for what we are not—nonprofit—associations in 2030 are now known for what we are: trusted communities where leaders come to grow and learn, improvise and innovate, contribute and prosper.

    Leadership is our purpose, not just the name of our governance groups.

    And, by creating and sustaining mega-communities of business, government, and service-sector leaders, associations align the most influential to take on the most significant public policy issues.

  • Shared Responsibility: Collective Action for a Sustainable Future

    • Journal of Association Leadership
    • April 2009
    Authors: Ann Oliveri

    This article synthesizes what I learned from the Boston College Center for Corporate Citizenship's 25 years of research, notably the competencies of corporate social responsibility officers and a field-tested framework for assessing the stages of development. The balance of the article speculates on how these concepts apply to associations and the synergies and leadership opportunities available.

    What I learned reinforced my appreciation for the competencies of the best association executives and my conviction that mission-driven associations will restore our planet and secure our children's future.

  • Breaking Down Barriers to Diversity

    • Associations NOW/Volunteer Leadership Issue
    • January 2009
    Authors: Ann Oliveri

    Wondering why your industry or profession isn't more diverse? Sometimes, structural barriers are to blame. In the commercial real estate industry, three associations have joined forces to change that.

  • The Power of Many

    • Journal of Association Leadership
    • January 2005
    Authors: Ann Oliveri

    Can too much communication render a group ineffective? Can too much consensus or compromise negate collective wisdom? Yes, says James Surowiecki, author of The Wisdom of Crowds: Why the Many Are Smarter Than the Few and How Collective Wisdom Shapes Business, Economies, Societies, and Nations. Drawing on an exclusive interview with the author and her own experience at the Urban Land Institute, Ann Oliveri explores why large groups of independent people seem to make consistently better decisions than individual experts — and examines what this means for associations.

  • Recalibrating Normal

    • ASAE, Association Management Magazine
    • December 2003
    Authors: Ann Oliveri

    Has white-water rafting through turbulent times lost its appeal? Jolted by power outages and terrorist threats, computer viruses and health crises, corporate scandals and countless reorganizations, many association executives and boards of directors are holding their collective breath awaiting a return to normal.

    The rest of us are looking for a way to generate our own energy or create a force field of influence to neutralize external threats, attract the powerful, and change lives for the better. Doing that, however, requires a different set of questions than the passe "When will things return to normal?" It requires a new series of assumptions of what people working together in an association can do. Perhaps the most fundamental question is "Can a cooperative membership organization work in this new era?"
