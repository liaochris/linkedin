Bianca Gendron's Experience

PatternMaker & Fashion Illustrator - Freelancer

Bianca Gendron

July 2010 – Present (3 years 2 months) Montreal, Canada Area

PatternMaker:
- Analyze the patterns in the collection.
- Determine the number, shape and dimensions of each pattern pieces.
- Determine the outline of different parts of the pattern, digitize the on-screen and develop on paper drawing.
- Verify the lengths, the curves and the proportions of the design.
- Indicate the position of cuts and lines of adjustment, modify the shape of the sleeves and establish the form of collars.
- Execute the modification of quantities of the pattern parts to reproduce the clothing in different sizes.
- Label all parts of the patterns, the size, the number, the style and any other necessary information.
- Execute the modification of the pattern following the trial or the recommendations from the designer.
- Perform the controls of the quality before initializing the production or manufacture of the prototype.
- Complete the specification sheets.

Fashion Illustrator:
- Design the pattern layout as the illustration of Apparel/Fashion
- Study different techniques to learn how to apply them to artistic endeavors.
- Maintain portfolios of artistic work to demonstrate styles, interests, and abilities.
- Integrate and develop visual elements, such as line, space, mass, color, and perspective, in order to produce desired effects such as the illustration of ideas, emotions, or moods.
- Study styles, techniques, colors, textures, and materials used in works undergoing restoration to ensure consistency during the restoration process.
- Trace drawings onto clear acetate for painting or coloring, or trace them with ink to make final copies.
