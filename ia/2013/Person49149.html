Global Potential's Summary

Since 2007, the 501(c)3 global non-profit, Global Potential (GP) has provided transformative life experiences for 388 inner-city youth from New York, Boston, Paris, Dominican Republic, Nicaragua and Haiti. GP's 1.5-year leadership program increases youth's self-esteem, civic participation and enhances their college and job entry rates.

GP provides youth from low-income and marginalized communities the opportunity to travel internationally and live in small villages where they are working on collaborative service projects such as building classrooms and gardens, teaching literacy and increasing health awareness. After these mind-opening adventures, GP youth then create exciting projects in their own urban neighborhoods, including documentary films and social enterprise ventures.

• GP is based in Boston, New York, Paris, Haiti, Dominican Republic and Nicaragua

• On an annual basis, GP directly serves 500 youth a year, including 40 new competitively-selected Fellows. These youth serve an additional 500 people locally in their own neighborhoods through their volunteer work. GP youth fellows have created 15 independent Social Enterprise Ventures funded with seed grants by Ashoka's Youth Venture, 12 documentary films (3 of them award winning by Tribeca, Human Rights Watch and Plural +), and done dozens of internships, activism projects and community service engagements.

• Our youth receive Fellowships, Film Festival Awards, and Posse and Gates college scholarships. More importantly, they become mentors for other youth.

• GP is a volunteer-run organization and 100% of our revenue goes towards the organization's mission, which has a quadruple impact -- changing youth and communities locally and internationally
