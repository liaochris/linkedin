Linda Crill's Summary

--Author of Newly Published Book: Blind Curves: A Story of Reinvention & "What Now?"
--Keynote Speaker on Reinvention, Route to Aha & Navigating the Unexpected
--Corporate Facilitation & Training Seminars on Reinvention, Resiliency & Chaotic Creation

The world is changing faster than companies can keep up. Before we finish implementing, there's already something newer. As leaders, we used to create long-range plans, emulate best practices from best-in-class organizations, predict results from past performance and develop contingency plans fairly easily.

As we becomes more globally connected, innovations are happening at an accelerated rate moving us through a challenging state of hyper-change. We must master the new leadership skills: reinvention, resiliency and chaotic creation – or risk falling further behind.

Captivating audiences through powerful storytelling, engaging visuals and actionable examples, Linda is a speaker and trainer to executives around the globe and a sought after reinvention expert. A former Fortune 100 executive, nonprofit executive, small business owner and public servant, her 30-year career has centered on helping leaders manage change and build a culture of innovation.

Now as an author and speaker, I help audiences:
• Answer "What Now? when the current ways no longer work
• Turn crisis into opportunity & navigate the unexpected
• Erase old boundaries & open doors labeled "Not Us"
• Adopt a new mindset for “Catching Up & Keeping Up”
• Head into “Blind Curves” & discover a zest for life, work & play

Specialties: Reinvention, innovation, change management, leadership, training, resiliency, manage change, transformation, chaotic creation, navigate the unexpected, answer What Now?, discover a zest for life, work & play

Linda Crill's Experience

Author, Blind Curves: A Story of Reinvention & What Now?

Blind Curves

March 2013 – Present (7 months) Available online Amazon, B&N, BAM, Indie Bound, Kindle, Nook, iBook

Blind Curves: One Woman’s Unusual Journey to Reinvent Herself & Answer “What Now?”

After 18 months of one-size-fits all advice for a 57 year-old-widow, Linda was still miserable. In a moment of rebellion, she traded her corporate suits for motorcycle leathers and committed to a 2,500-mile road trip down America's Pacific Northwest coast from Vancouver to California wine country—with only 30 days to learn how to ride. This out-of-character leap became a catalyst for discovering answers to "What Now?" Heading into "blind curves," she faced fears, redefined beliefs and discovered a broader horizon of possibilities for reinventing herself and the fuel to make it happen. Funny, irreverent and extraordinarily honest, it's the perfect read when you're looking to reinvent yourself and answer: "What Now?"
