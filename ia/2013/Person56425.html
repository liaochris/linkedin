Jack Brewer's Summary

Highly successful global business executive and philanthropist possessing a unique combination of expertise in the fields of global consulting, sports consulting, sports business, finance, foreign affairs and sustainable global economic development. Currently the CEO of The Brewer Group, Chairman of the United Nations Millennium Development Goals Awards Athletes and Artists Committee, Executive Director of The Jack Brewer Foundation, Senior Advisor to H.E. President Joyce Banda of Malawi and a host/sports analyst for South-South News, Fox Sports and KFAN. Former National Football League player.

Specialties: Dynamic and innovative entrepreneur and devoted philanthropist, Motivational Speaker, Senior Advisor, Financial Advisor, Sports Business Expert, Sports Analyst, Consulting Expert, Financial Expert

Founder and Executive Director

The Jack Brewer Foundation

April 2006 – Present (7 years 6 months)

The Jack Brewer Foundation strives to “Empower From Within” through our self-sustaining initiatives, offering educational opportunities and economic development in partnership with underprivileged people around the world.

Our mission is to help alleviate extreme poverty through our self-sustaining socioeconomic initiatives: JBF/JBF Microfinance Program, Partnership to Protect Children and Young Leaders Initiative.

JBF Worldwide focuses on the empowerment of women and children living within impoverished and underdeveloped communities through the implementation of initiatives that enable food security; provide access to educational resources; assist with disaster relief and medical aid; and promote peace and development through sport. Work in partnership with the President of Malawi to help impact the economy of Malawi.

JBF Worldwide is thoroughly dedicated to providing opportunity to the world’s most underserved populations.

Chair Board of Advisors

Montessori Model United Nations

January 2013 – Present (9 months)

Internationally, MMUN is the largest of the Model UN programs servicing the needs of students age 9-15, which is the age when they are developing their sense of social justice. We are a 501 (3) (c) compliant not-for-profit organization with an international focus on youth education through our Global Citizenship Program. MMUN's NGO Forum provides them with mentors and infrastructure to help them accomplish their goals. As an example, this April, students at the MMUN will help package 100,000 meals for Malawi families in collaboration with the Jack Brewer Foundation and Outreach International.

In addition to curriculum and conference programs, MMUN supports the development of Montessori schools and the training of teachers in developing countries in collaboration with other organizations including Rotary International, Association Montessori Internationale, Educators Sans Frontiers, Kids Can Free the Children and the Jack Brewer Foundation.

In developing countries, MMUN also supports and/or implements Preparation for Parenthood programs that help reduce childhood mortality while guiding new parents on how to prepare their children in the earliest stages of their development to become cognitive learners. MMUN's goal is to reach as many children and parents as possible.

Jack Brewer's Projects

  • MDG Athletes and Artists Committee

    Team Members: Jack Brewer, Emily Chalk, Alissa Cross, Danielle Berman, Nikki Rugh, Michael Jacobson

  • Official Launch of the MDGAAC

    • April 2013 to Present
    Team Members: Jack Brewer, Alissa Cross, Emily Chalk, Danielle Christine Hyman, Danielle Berman, Nikki Rugh, Michael Jacobson

    On April 13, 2013 The Jack Brewer Foundation (JBF Worldwide) will celebrate its 7th Anniversary with the Millennium Development Goals (MDG) Awards Committee and launch the MDG Athletes and Artists Committee (MDGAAC).

    An event hosted in New York City, a delegation including numerous Global Ambassadors of JBF Worldwide, Goodwill Ambassadors of the MDG Awards Committee and Members of its Athletes and Artists Committee (MDGAAC) were\ recognized for their exceptional dedication and ongoing commitment to philanthropic work on a global level.

    At a press conference held at the United Nations (UN) Mr. Brewer and Mr. Jacobson introduced the newest members of the MDGAAC, who are also Global Ambassadors of JBF Worldwide.
    The work was recoginzed of world renowned entrepreneur and philanthropist Rohan Marley, former New York Knick John Starks, former international basketball star Eric Dailey as well as former and current NFL athletes Akin Ayodele, Lance Johnstone, Corey Chavous, Sidney Rice, E.J. Henderson, Ronnie Cameron, Kenderick Allen and Darrell Reid. Also honored are up and coming pop stars the BQ Girls including former Miss Universe Wendy Fitzwilliam, Miss USA Chelsea Cooley and teen queen Haley Seibert.

    In the evening a reception was held at the Eli Klein Fine Art gallery, located in the SoHo neighborhood of Manhattan. This high-profile event featured a performance from the BQ Girls as well as words from our non-profit honorees, Rohan Marley, and recognized the JBF Worldwide partnership with Millennium Development Goals Awards.

    Non-profit honorees included Doc2Doc (D2D), One World Futbol Project (OWFP), Chevrolet, AmeriCares, Outreach, Inc., Elevate New York (Elevate NY) and the China World Peace Foundation (CWPF). These selected organizations received Special Recognition Awards presented jointly by the MDG Awards Committee and JBF Worldwide in recognition of the outstanding efforts each organization is making towards achieving the eight MDGs.
