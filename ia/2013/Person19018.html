Julie Chen's Summary

Up until 2010, Julie had been a professional architectural model builder for over a decade. After attending the Studio for Interrelated Media at MassArt, she has reshaped her career as an interdisciplinary artist, designer, and communications consultant in the New England region.

When she is not working on her art, she looks for work that involves civic engagement in the arts and education.

Currently, she is designing for the Connecticut Science Center, a non-profit science and technology museum with hands-on exhibits and educational labs for all ages.

Julie Chen's Projects

  • Scaled-Up

    • November 2012 to April 2013
    Team Members: Julie Chen

    I won a contract to design and install two interior window displays at the Boston Children's Museum for their project "An Alphabet of Inspiration: Artists Celebrate 100 Years of Collections."

    The concept is called "Scaled-Up", blowing up hi-res scans of the Museums collection items to be displayed beside the items themselves. "U is for Urchin" displays the Museum's sea urchin shells. "J is for Jade" displays the Museum's jade jewelry collection.

  • An Alphabet of Inspiration: Artists Celebrate 100 Years of Collections

    • July 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Julie Chen, Rachel Farkas, Brooke Scibelli, Dyllan Nguyen, Alice Vogler, Bren Bataclan, Dan Moynihan, Diane Fiedler, Peter Agoos, Maggie Carberry, Kerri Schmidt, Rachel E. Mello, Philippe Lejeune, Judith Klausner, Janet Kawada, Hilary Zelson, Wen-Hao Tien, Carolyn L., Kyle Larabee, Margaret Bellafiore, Donna Rhae Marder, Danielle Freiman, Andrea Zampitella

    To celebrate the Boston Children's Museum's 100th Anniversary, artists have been commissioned to create installations for vitrine window displays inside the museum, each representing a letter of the alphabet. Installation will be complete in the Spring of 2013 and will remain open to museum guests for one year.
