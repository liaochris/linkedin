Heather Steinberger's Summary

I have been a professional writer for almost 20 years, with experience as a journalist, magazine editor, advertising copywriter and public relations specialist. I launched my freelance writing business, Write On LLC, in 2004; today, I write for regionally and nationally circulated print magazines and newspapers as well as for websites.

As a writer and editor, I specialize in watersports, adventure travel, the outdoors, the arts and Native American issues. As a PR writer and consultant, I focus on the arts and not-for-profit work, and I very much enjoy ghostwriting projects. I have great passion for using communications tools to help individuals and organizations achieve their goals — and find their own distinct voices along the way.

In January 2012, I added book publishing to the mix. My husband, professional photographer Richard Steinberger, and I are in the process of producing a fine-art photography book showcasing "beauty in unexpected places" on the Cheyenne River Sioux Reservation in South Dakota. We formed a new business, Timberline Media LLC, to handle this new direction in our creative lives.

Specialties: Feature writing, news reporting, personality profiles, product reviews, book/art/film reviews, public relations and ghostwriting.

Contributing Editor

Boating Magazine

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Publishing industry

January 2012 – Present (2 years)

I started contributing to Boating Magazine in early 2012. I write eclectic news items for "Making Waves," Q&A pieces with interesting people for "On Board With" and a variety of feature stories. To date, my features have included pieces about attending the American Power Boat Association's tunnel-boat driving school, the history and influence of Baja California Sur's pangas (which won first place in its category in the Boating Writers International annual writing competition) and three intrepid retired firefighters from California who took part in a long-distance, offshore cruising rally aboard a 28-foot Skipjack. I love my job.

Public Relations Manager

Cheyenne River Youth Project

June 2006 – Present (7 years 7 months)

I first became involved with CRYP, located on the Cheyenne River Sioux Reservation in South Dakota, as a volunteer; I helped run The Main Youth Center for two months during the summer of 2006. Shortly after I returned home, I joined the staff as public relations manager. I prepare and write all the organization's press releases; I create and distribute a monthly e-newsletter; I regularly update CRYP's Facebook group and cause pages; and I work closely with the executive director on PR planning and strategy. And I try to go back to the rez at least once a year... it's a special place that, once it gets inside of you, never lets go.

Owner / Writer / Editor

Write On LLC

Writing and Editing industry

April 2004 – Present (9 years 9 months)

Write On LLC is my freelance writing/editing business. In the nine years since I started the company, my articles have appeared in Backpacker, Boating Magazine, Boating World, Door County Magazine, Indian Country Today, Islands, Lakeland Boating, Motor Boating, Sailing, Sea Magazine, Showboats International, Southern Boating, Sport Diver and other publications.

On the PR side of the business, I have represented a wide variety of businesses, organizations and individuals, including a contemporary art center, a popular regional winery, a state-level Senate campaign, well-known marine industry manufacturers and a variety of independent artists. I also regularly contribute to national and regional blogs as a ghostwriter.

In addition, I have volunteered my time as a member of the Door County Maritime Museum and Door County Humane Society marketing committees, as a Boating Writers International Annual Writing Competition judge, and as a judge for the Denver Woman's Press Club's "Unknown Writers Contest." Most recently, I have thrown my hat in the ring as a BWI board member and as the chairperson for the DWPC "In-House Writing Contest."
