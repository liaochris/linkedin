Kim Usey's Summary

Communications expert with substantial experience in daily newspapers and magazines and long exposure to inner workings of higher education and nonprofits. Editor and writer with excellent design eye and understanding of how technology and social media can enhance journalism, communications, and public relations. Long record of proven leadership, initiative, and organizational support. Great creativity and intuition brings strength to collaboration, brainstorming, and planning sessions. Interested in putting creative, collaborative, and communications skills to work for good, as in nonprofit work, education, & civic/green/social marketing.

Bylines as Kim Usey, Kim Koster, Kimberly Bartlett.

Specialties: Nonprofit communications & marketing, higher education communications & marketing, daily journalism, editing, freelance management, interagency collaboration, leadership, magazine journalism, news management, quick communications response, social media, strategic planning.

Owner and Principal

Usey Communications

November 2005 – Present (8 years 2 months)

Strategic communications, creative communications, small business communications. Director of Communications

Texas Learning and Computation Center, University of Houston

Educational Institution; 10,001+ employees; Higher Education industry

November 2008 – January 2010 (1 year 3 months)

The Texas Learning & Computation Center was an interdisciplinary academic center whose mission was to foster research, education, and outreach at the University of Houston and beyond. TLC2 provided high performance computing support for researchers in the sciences, technology, social sciences, and humanities; communications, marketing, and event support for faculty; and educational and outreach programs for various faculty, groups, and the community at large.

As Director of Communications, I was responsible for long-term strategic communications planning and meeting short-term daily communications needs. Long-term plans included an increased use of social media and the creation of a magazine focusing on research, education, and outreach by TLC2-affiliated faculty and the center itself. Short-term responsibilities included public relations and marketing, graphic design, proposal design and editing, press release production, event marketing coordination, and social media/web content updating.

Director of Communications

St. David's Community Health Foundation

Director of communications for a foundation whose mission is to improve health and health care for all Central Texans through grants, programs, and community collaborations; responsible for external and internal publications, including policy reports, annual reports, and newsletters, in traditional media forms and new media. Provided staff leadership and support for executive during separate processes of strategic planning with foundation staff and board and organizational values assessment with foundation staff. Began providing communications assistance to SDCHF’s 46 grant-recipient organizations and established a quarterly communications & marketing meeting for those agencies. Began establishing social media presence for SDCHF. Duties included writing, editing, designing, planning, public relations, providing leadership as a foundation director, and representing the Foundation in community collaborations (including the Health Alliance for Austin Musicians, the Mayor’s Fitness Council, and steering the “Critical Condition” health care town meeting planning committee).
