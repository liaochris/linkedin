Denise Crute's Summary

With an extensive, prestigious record of advanced studies, neurosurgeon Dr. Denise Crute began her secondary education at Duke University. After being named to the Dean’s List several times, Dr. Denise Crute graduated cum laude with a Bachelor of Science in Zoology. During her undergraduate years, Dr. Denise Crute also received critical acclaim at juried art shows and symposiums for her artistic talents with paint and other mediums. Dr. Denise Crute had carefully developed these artistic abilities throughout her childhood and had offered painting lessons during high school.

Dr. Denise Crute soon began her medical studies and earned her M.D. from the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill School of Medicine in 1988 while simultaneously continuing the university’s graduate curriculum in Neurobiology. Recognized and awarded for outstanding abilities, Dr. Denise Crute received a Kenan Scholarship. Throughout medical and graduate school Dr. Denise Crute sold her paintings, using the proceeds to buy the medical texts required for her studies and to support herself.

Moving to Northwestern Memorial Hospital in 1990, Dr. Denise Crute began her general surgery internship. After its completion, Dr. Denise Crute immediately continued on into her residency in neurosurgery. Throughout her postgraduate work, Dr. Denise Crute earned numerous scholarships, grants, and honors, including a postdoctoral fellowship in Neurobiology at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill, an Individual National Research Service Award from National Institutes of Health, and the Physician’s Recognition Award in Continuing Medical Education. She also completed a Fellowship in Neurocritical Care at the prestigious Mt. Sinai Medical Center in New York City.
