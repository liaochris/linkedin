Christian Hansen's Summary

A perpetual learner and professional developer. I combine deep technical knowledge with deft use of abstraction and patterns to make systems that are reliable, maintainable and as simple as can be. And I inspire others to do the same.

Too much crappy code is being written, and I want to do something about that.

I have built a BI and reporting system with self-healing properties, and optimised it to effectively handle hundreds of millions of data-points. I have written the authorisation back-end to a custom single sign-on system, and a number of open source software projects, including Fabric (pythonic remote automation) and Stormpot (fast and thread-safe object pooling for Java).

I enjoy social life both on and off the job, and like going to conferences, meet-ups, events, etc.
I also occasionally give talks, and I am co-organizing the Copenhagen JVM Interest Group.

Specialties: Back-End/Server Programming · Concurrency & Multi-Threading · High Performance & Low Latency · Mechanical Sympathy · Self-Healing Systems · MySQL · TDD · Test Automation & Unit Testing · Clean Code · Maintainable Software Design · Hard Problems · Simplicity & Complexity · Java & JVM Languages

Christian Hansen's Projects

  • Stormpot

    • March 2011 to Present
    Team Members: Christian Hansen

    A fast and thread-safe object pooling library, written in Java. A great deal of care have been taking to make this not only one of the fastest object pools in Java, but also one of the most well-tested. Benchmarks have shown it capable of over 20 million operations per second on a 4 core system, with near-linear scalability.

  • Fabric

    • January 2008 to November 2009
    Team Members: Christian Hansen

    Fabric is a pythonic remote shell automation tool that I wrote, because I needed something like that. It is now maintained by the community under the stewardship of Jeff Forcier.
