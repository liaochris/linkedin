heather parlato's Summary

whether you’re a new business starting from scratch, an existing organization looking to pull collateral together into a tighter system, or an established company ready for a refreshing way to punch up brand communications, identity-focused design finds the personal twist that connects you more meaningfully with your constituents.

powerful branding is not about simply making things pretty. the message drives the system, and informs the look, feel, and voice of how your materials tell the story of who you are, what you do, and highlights your unique contributions to the market.

as a brand & identity specialist, i want to give you business cards you're excited to hand out, support materials that are a veritable extension of your brand promise, and leave-behinds that keep the conversation going long after you've left the meeting. in a nutshell, i want you to be armed and dangerous, with a communication strategy that knocks your clients socks off.

i’ve been doing design in los angeles for more than a decade, helping my clients share their individual greatness with the world through brand & identity design, and marketing strategy services. when i’m not whipping up ideas or beating back deadlines, i’m most likely developing recipes, tending the garden, or teaching my cats to do web updates at the studio in echo park, ca.

expert capabilities: brand & identity development, creative direction, and marketing strategy. parlato design studio helps robust small businesses translate their visions to all phases of a strong brand & identity system that connects them more meaningfully with their constituents.

heather parlato's Experience

creative director & owner

parlato design studio (Sole Proprietorship)

Sole Proprietorship; Myself Only; Graphic Design industry

April 1996 – Present (17 years 9 months)

A Los Angeles-based brand design studio specializing brand discovery & development, graphic design design, identity collateral and marketing strategy. We can help with businesses starting from scratch, looking for a rebrand, or working with you to pull all your positioning pieces into one clear strategy. please see parlatodesign.com for more information.

senior graphic designer

earthlink

Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; ELNK; Telecommunications industry

August 2005 – November 2007 (2 years 4 months)

As a senior print designer, I worked with the print team to create fresh looks for new products as well as uphold longstanding brand identity for existing services. Projects encompass brochures, magazine ads, posters, in-store displays, product packaging and many times work together as full campaigns for new products and services.

art director

natpe

Nonprofit; 11-50 employees; Entertainment industry

July 1998 – August 2003 (5 years 2 months)

As the Art Director for this non-profit television association, I brought several diverging resident styles of print, online and promotional vehicles into a cohesive corporate identity and brand strategy for optimum industry presence while meeting the budgetary constraints of a changing economy. I conceived and executed creative for all printed matter and online concepts including ad campaigns, industry publications, newsletters, brochures and large-format trade show graphics. In addition, I photographed all seminars and special events, managed production artists, freelance designers and print production, collaborated with all freelance designers and copywriters, and wrote advertising copy.

graphic designer and iris printer

muse [x] editions and imaging

November 1996 – June 1998 (1 year 8 months)

As in-house Graphic Designer and Iris Printer, my responsibilities included graphic design and production of all gallery-related print materials, collaboration and consultation with digital artists, digital retouching, color correction, fine art and commercial Iris printing, Iris maintenance, management of assistant Iris printers and client/vendor relations. Extensive knowledge of fine art papers, inks and archival processes.

graphic production artist

electronic publishing specialists, inc.

October 1994 – November 1996 (2 years 2 months)

EPS was a full-service prepress studio where I provided graphic design services, drum scanning, color correction and retouching for litho as well as art-quality Iris [ink jet] and LightJet [photographic] prints. Additional responsibilities included client and artist liaison, print and prepress consultation, 4-color film separation, project management and customer relations.
