Glen Gilmore's Summary

A Forbes Top 50 "Social Media Power Influencer", Gilmore is an attorney and social media strategist. He also teaches Digital Marketing, Crisis Commns, and Social Media Law at Rutgers. He has been called a “man of action" by TIME magazine, a "Twitter Powerhouse" by the Huffington Post, and Memphis Daily News has written that “Gilmore is well-versed in all things Web-related.” He is also a Google Glass Explorer.

Gilmore has developed and provided customized training to members of the Fortune 500. He regularly teaches executive-level social media marketing classes for the Rutgers MBA marketing program.

As an attorney, he handles a diverse variety of special cases. He is the author of "Social Media Law For Business".

Gilmore advises clients on issues ranging from digital trends, brand management, social business, community building, influencer mapping and relations, content marketing, social media compliance and governance. Startup consultation. Problem resolution.

Gilmore served eight years as mayor of Hamilton, NJ, a city of nearly 100,000. He chaired NJ's League of Municipalities Economic Development Task Force. Glen led Hamilton to achieve its greatest period of economic growth, job creation, and open space preservation, as well as recognition by Money magazine as “one of the best places to live in America,” and by CNN as “America’s 10th safest city.”

Lauded a “national hero” by The New York Times for his leadership during America’s anthrax crisis, he appeared on Good Morning America, CNN, Fox News, and BCC. As an adjunct instructor with Texas A&M University, he assisted in the development and delivery of crisis leadership training funded by the US Dept of Homeland Security.

A former prosecutor, he completed a judicial clerkship with the NJ Superior Court, where he was also a certified mediator. He served as Communications Director to a NJ Legislator.

He also served on the RWJ University Hospital Board of Directors.

Glen is also a restauranteur.
