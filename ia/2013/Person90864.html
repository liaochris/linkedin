Megan Welsh's Summary

Good work shouldn't be complicated to find. Our company is a great choice for small and growing businesses and associations who need big firm creative from a scrappy, smaller firm. Although the public affairs, and issue advocacy are the bread and butter (or is it bread and water?), we are excited to work with a wide range of clients; restaurants, foundations, non-profits, and small business like mine who want to be bigger.

Specialties

Branding and identity, Print concepts and design, from integrated campaigns to simple, singular solutions. A wide range of good ol' fashioned know-how paired with forward-thinking ideas and door-to-door results. And lots of hyphenated phrases.

Megan Welsh's Experience

Owner

Spring Creative

December 2002 – Present (11 years 1 month)

Spring Creative is a fresh, small, collaborative practice that specializes in the concept and creation of design, print and electronic communications. ie: ads, logos, brochures, direct mail, presentations, the world wide web, transit, you name it...

Our goal is to keep the creative bar set high in spite of limitations to time and budget -- and nail it -- all while looking great and staying cool.

And sometimes, we manage just that.

Creative Director

Goddard Claussen

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Public Relations and Communications industry

May 1998 – December 2002 (4 years 8 months)

Handled or managed all print and design projects, worked with writer/partner to generate ideas for advertising for print and broadcast. Presentations, storyboards, logo design. Helped to bring high end advertising aesthetics to a political environment and by successfully raising the bar, our firm enjoyed increasing enthusiasm from our clients and account managers, increased ad buys and awards; all in an environment that values hyper speed over great ideas. This job tested my resolve to do great work regardless of the deadline.

Senior Art Director

Milli Smythe Design

December 1996 – June 1998 (1 year 7 months)

The main design and marketing animal behind The Simpson's, working in tandem with Fox's marketing machine, Milli and her long time friend Matt Groening held the fox at bay to preserve the charm of the Simpson's and reign control over all print advertising, publications, collateral and merchandising. When Bart wasn't front and center, Warner Brothers and Universal picked up the slack with merchandise design, collateral, endless fun with Rocky & Bullwinkle, Woody Woodpecker and Frankenstein. Quonset hut, pinball machine, very hard work and some equally hard play. Great job that went from 60 to 5 mph in a couple of months, and evaporated completely a few months after I left. Best job ever.
