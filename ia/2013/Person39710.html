Ed Robertson's Summary

Ed Robertson is an award-winning author and journalist who divides his time between collaborative writing and editing, and hosting and producing his radio show, TV CONFIDENTIAL. He has had eight books published covering such genres as biography, media and entertainment, including MAVERICK: LEGEND OF THE WEST (“a notable work of television journalism,” according to the Nassau Herald) and THIRTY YEARS OF THE ROCKFORD FILES, a behind-the-scenes history of James Garner's groundbreaking, award-winning TV series. Ed has also written for The New York Times, Media Life Magazine, The Wave Magazine, Columbia House Video Library, Highbury House Communications and other media venues. For five years he was the monthly TV analyst for The Tony Gill Show (AI Radio). He currently appears as a regular guest on The Ronn Owens Program (KGO-AM, San Francisco) and The Gary Snyder Show (Indiana Talks, Marion, IN).

Ed is also a successful ghostwriter and editor with a proven track record for producing books, articles and other content on behalf of publishers, agents, authors and companies. His collaborations include THE ETHICS OF STAR TREK, which he co-authored with Indiana State University professor Judith Barad; VOICE OF REASON, a best-selling book of political commentary by National Radio Hall of Fame nominee Ronn Owens; the memoirs KEEP ON KEEPING ON and UNFULFILLED DREAMS; and ONCE UPON A TIME: ELVIS AND ANITA, which he edited on behalf of the book's author, Jonnita Brewer Barrett.

Since 2008, Ed has produced and hosted TV CONFIDENTIAL, a radio talk show about television featuring lively conversation about network and cable programming, plus interviews with special guests. The show is broadcast every week on nine stations, with archives available at TVConfidential.net.

Ed Robertson is a member of the Authors Guild, the American Society of Journalists and Authors and the Association of Ghostwriters. For more information, visit www.edrobertson.com.

Specialties

Nonfiction books and articles, ghostwriting/expert collaborations, short-form writing (plot summaries, reviews, side bars), research, editing, media coaching, speech writing, web content, custom publications, press releases, business profiles, copywriting (for print and radio), video liner notes, blogs, radio broadcasts and podcasting
