Lene' Alley DeRudder's Summary

Lené Alley DeRudder is a civil litigator and credentialed mediator with over eight years of prior professional experience in assisting small businesses and corporations in their media and marketing strategies. Her law practice primarily consists of representing and counseling business clients on a variety of of legal matters, such as entity formation, business divorces, and contractual transactions, as well as persons facing family law matters, such as divorce, child custody or support modifications, and adoption. Lené brings a level of practical understanding to her business and family clients in that she strives to view her client’s needs from their point while simultaneously providing legal guidance in their best interest. Prior to forming Alley DeRudder Law, PLLC, Lené did her externship and clerkship at BNSF Railway Company in Fort Worth, Texas.

~ Represented members (plaintiffs and defendants) of limited liability companies in business divorces as well as other lawsuits, such as breach of contract, breach of fiduciary duty, and conversion.

~ Advised on and/or handled a variety of issues small businesses operationally deal with on a daily basis, including creating, reviewing, and negotiating contracts, debt collection, property matters, and tax concerns.

~ Formed numerous Texas Limited Liability Companies for businesses in a variety of industries such as Food & Beverage, Consulting, General Contracting, and Retail.

~ Formed a Texas non-profit as well as assisted in obtaining 501(c)(3) exempt status.

~ Represented parties in a variety of family law matters that involved children, property division disputes, and/or highly sensitive matters, and effectively obtained supervised visitation, spousal support, and Court-ordered treatment programs, where appropriate.

~ As a mediator, obtained settlements agreements in 70% of civil cases mediated, including breach of contract, collections, and forfeiture cases.
