Jeff Abrams' Summary

I have been a registered architect in the state of Colorado and have designed light construction projects for a vast array of clients. Some of our projects include large custom homes, restaurants, a solar-heated church, a private school, medical/dental facilities, multifamily projects, merchant housing, commercial/industrial facilities, commercial space planning, and renovations, remodels, and additions for private clients. Our early work in the developing field of "passive-solar" design/build led to many innovations and contributions in the area of energy conservation and off the grid heating and cooling technologies. In 1975 I founded the Energy Conservation Group, a tough sell back then but pretty mainstream in today's carbon conscious "green" world. We have worked with many wonderful private clients, builders and developers and are most sought after as problem solvers to redeem and add value to an owner's property.
Our professional services cover mostly the Rocky Mountain region and the West; however, we are currently completing an institutional project in Fujian, China.
Our goal is to create meaningful, comfortable, efficient and profitable places in which to live and work.
Our best project will be yours!

Jeff Abrams' Projects

  • Buchanan Residence Addition

    • February 2009 to August 2010
    Team Members: Jeff Abrams, Henry Lopez Structural Engineer, Kurowski Development - builder

    Having outgrown the limited space of their early 20th century Victorian country house, this young and growing family was in desperate need of children's bedrooms and a large garage/storage space to accommodate 3 young children and their 15 acre ranchette. A tacked on addition was an impossible challenge as the existing stair could not service a lateral building. We found the solution in a new "water tower" style stair building that bridges old and new and allows for additional bedrooms above a new over-sized garage. This tower serves at once as an exclamation point and connector between old and new and perfectly completes the stylistic reference.

  • Gross Residence Addition

    • January 2011 to Present
    Team Members: Jeff Abrams, Tom Berry, Mark Booren

    Lori Gross owns an outdated 1960's semi-raised ranch house of medium size where she lives and works. We were tasked with many phases of remediation to correct issues such as negative drainage, energy in-efficiency, pest control, and general livability. Additionally, we designed a new front entry area to improve interior circulation, enlarge the Living Room, improve privacy and garage entry. Other areas of design work incorporated a reworked Kitchen/Living Room space, new enlarged deck and incorporated deck roof, reworked Master Suite taking over a guest bedroom, and improvements to the existing garage. Energy upgrades include new reworked state of the art wall sections and roofing overbuild with 2' rigid and blown-in insulation at bedrooms, as well as, upgraded mechanical systems.

  • Asti d'italia Restaurant

    • June 2008 to February 2009
    Team Members: Jeff Abrams, Tom Berry, Kay D'mato, Henry Lopez - structural engineer, Frank Banta - general contractor, The Boulder Engineering Co.

    A contemporary upscale Italian restaurant serving classic, traditional, and updated northern Italian dishes with an emphasis on wines of the Lombardi region.
