Jed A. Reay's Summary

Over the last 30+ years, I have dedicated myself to improving the lives of others, both through my entrepreneurial work in the healthcare sector and as a coach and mentor to companies and fellow entrepreneurs. Underpinning all my experiences to date is an overwhelming belief that positive change can be achieved by unleashing the power of communication and dynamic interaction.

I have always demanded excellence of myself and am passionate about encouraging other people to expect more from themselves, enabling them to realize their full potential and achieve their destiny. This has been achieved through my work with LifeStyles Unlimited Co, a transformation training company that provides consulting, training and coaching to individuals and companies. In this capacity, I have helped many thousands of people all over the world by empowering them to make life-changing decisions.

I am the author of a book on business communication, “Why Can’t We Say What We Mean?” and my second book, entitled “Breaking Free of Self-Imposed Limitations: Beyond Positive Thinking” is due to be published mid 2014; a third book, “Transformations of an Authentic Man” is currently being researched.

To find out more about what I do, and what I can do to help you or your company to enjoy future success and fulfillment, please email me on jed at jedreay.com with your contact details and I will call you back to discuss how I can meet your needs.

Jed A. Reay's Publications

  • "Why Can't We Say What We Mean: Developing Meaningful Businesses Relationships Through Effective Communication."

    • Create Space Self Published
    • July 2008
    Authors: Jed A. Reay, Cynthia Bull, Editor

    This book will be both provocative and informative, and I truly encourage dialogue when the subject is featured. I have amassed an eclectic group of individuals from the business world to provide their own thoughts and opinions on the subject of communication in the current business climate.

    This book is a series of transcribed interviews with very successful entrepreneurs from the off line and on line world. Each one of these special entrepreneurs plays a unique role in the guidance, development, delivery, and education of the individuals they work with. Their commitment to quality relationship building and business development through effective communication is the pivotal point in business success or failure.

    Each one of the interviewees has a very special role to play and provide the reader with solutions to common issues facing entrepreneurs and business owners in the current business environment.

  • Unethical Business Practices

    • Self
    • March 4, 2008
    Authors: Jed A. Reay

    Bad press and lawsuits are things that every business owner fears. Bad press can ruin your reputation, as well as your business, and lawsuits can bankrupt you. The easiest way to avoid both of these situations is to avoid unethical business practices. There are very easy ways to avoid unethical business practices.

  • Global Business and Ethics

    • Ezine Articles
    • April 21, 2008
    Authors: Jed A. Reay

    With the advent of the Internet, everything from personal relationships to business has become 'global' for all intents and purposes. Today, you can talk to people across the world just as easily as if you were talking to your next door neighbor. Businesses can exchange documents of all sorts with the push of a button - without having to wait days, and often weeks, for those documents to be delivered by hand. We are global, and this has had a profound effect in the area of business ethics.
