Everyone loves a show or fair, whether it's a winter "Cabin Fever" event, an outdoor music festival, a home & garden trade show, a flea market, an agricultural show with pies, animals & tractors, or one designed to showcase products & services such as bridal events or the healing arts.

I founded RHMHS, a for profit state-licensed mental health agency. The company started with one employee (myself); at its peak we employed 230 people in 10 counties, and billed $8.7 million annually.

As a start-up, I developed the company’s infrastructure including all initial licensing & contracts and policies & procedures, and managed all aspects from service design to human resources, scheduling staff, budgeting & accounting, and client relations.

As RHMHS grew and became one of the largest employers in Maine (in the top 5% state-wide), my duties & responsibilities changed. I was less involved with day to day operations, concentrating instead on CEO-level duties of developing strategy & vision, meeting the infrastructure needs of a rapidly growing company coping with a difficult reimbursement environment, setting programmatic goals & budgets (which included eliminating non-performing but popular divisions such as children’s behavioral services), and supervising executive level and senior management staff.
