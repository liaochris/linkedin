David Hablewitz, PMP's Summary

It's not about the software.

Lance Armstrong said it well in "It's Not About the Bike." The details of knowledge of specific software are secondary to all the other skills that make someone stand out. It's the skills that cannot be learned from a book that set one apart from the rest and why some people are successful no matter what job they set out to do.

My passion is in bringing people and technology together, applying divergent thinking to find innovative ways to solve business needs, giving your company a dominating competitive advantage and helping it be agile and adaptive to changing conditions. I have a degree in computer science and 25 years of experience in the industry. My skills include providing leadership, managing projects, administering systems, designing and implementing infrastructure architecture, and designing and developing software. Beyond that are the social skills in teaching, mentoring, marketing. I am always working with the goal to seek out ways to make people more effective, efficient, and satisfied in their jobs.

Everyone has a passion. If they're lucky, they do it for a living and if they do, it will show. In my case it shows in my involvement in the I.T. community. I maintain a professional blog. I speak at conferences and user groups. I volunteer at the local college. I participate in web communities. As an example, I am consistently in the Top Innovators list at Ideajam.net
http://ideajam.net/IdeaJam/P/ij.nsf/topinnovators?readform

I'm a firm believer in paying it forward. I find success in helping others be successful.

Specialties

Helping organizations use social business software to their advantage. Customer service, communicating, teaching, public speaking, problem solving, designing software, finding ways to make software fail (testing) so it won't fail when you need it, technical and creative writing, computer infrastructure design and administration, building rapport with peers, nontechnical people, and business leaders alike, finding ways to use technology to help people succeed.

The Notes Guy in Seattle

The Notes Guy in Seattle

Information Technology and Services industry

July 2000 – Present (13 years 6 months) Seattle

Deployment Engineer Lead

Microsoft - Office 365 (Vendor)

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; MSFT; Computer Software industry

May 2011 – June 2012 (1 year 2 months) Redmond, WA

Cloud computing is all about scalability and efficiency. When done right, what would be a major project for others, like a server upgrade, is just daily operations for us.

I am currently contracting at a large provider of cloud services. I was originally hired by the engineering team to update the instructions used to build the BlackBerry Enterprise Server (BES) environment for new customers and to identify and reduce the sources of errors in the build process.

Later I moved to the operations team responsible for actually building and deploying the infrastructure. In this new role, I am primarily responsible for managing the work done by our team to build and deploy the SQL and BES Servers, though I work with all aspects of the infrastructure. I coordinate with the customer representatives, schedule and allocate the work, monitor the builds and address problems and escalate them as needed. I still spend a large amount of my time actually doing build work which enables me to validate the documentation and give feedback to the engineers writing it. I have also taken the initiative to drive an effort to streamline and automate the processes, saving many man-hours for each server built and improving quality. I have scripted much of the automation myself including a quality control script to verify the accuracy of each build in a fraction of the time of the old process.

While working with the documentation, I came up with a new layout, making them more functional and further reducing the possibility of errors during the build process. This new design is being adopted by the technical writing team for all procedural documentation. The impact of these efforts is staggering when you multiply it over all our customers. This environment is orders of magnitude larger than any single company.

Though I moved to the operations team, I have stayed on engineering as a member of the strategic team that plans the future architecture of the infrastructure.
