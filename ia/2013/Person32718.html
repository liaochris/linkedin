David Walmsley's Summary

I'm responsible for M&S.com, trading the business and developing our future multichannel platform.

I previously ran Dixons Retails' online and multichannel business - managing a £500m P&L, and before that I ran John Lewis online, including John Lewis' customer contact centre operations.

I have been working in the digital space for the last 19 years having built my first web site in 1994.

I've worked across retail, publishing, advertising, and financial services. My approach is to focus on building high-calibre teams who are relentlessly focused on the customer, are comfortable crunching large amounts of data to get to insight, and have huge amounts of fun turning that insight into action and commercial payback.

My multichannel retailing experience has seen me lean more and more into supply chain, merchandising and ranging strategy. I already feel pretty comfortable on all things relating to ecommerce and digital strategy - customer experience stuff in particular is sort of my backyard. I think we're pretty close to finding much smarter ways to run retailing businesses.

Specialties: Business transformation and turnaround; general management; digital strategy; multichannel strategy; international strategy; ecommerce; retail; analytically-driven trading; IT and eCommerce platform strategy; programme development; operating model development; logistics; supply chain; retailing; web merchandising; search; e-retail; online marketing; sem; brand strategy and development; partnerships; ebusiness; web site development; commercial development; content.

David Walmsley's Experience

Director M&S.com

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; MKS.L; Retail industry

Customer-facing deliverables have included the launch of next day Click and Collect to all our UK stores; the launches of M&S online in China, France, Germany, Spain, Belgium and Ireland; international Click & Collect; the launch of M&S Outlets Online, and user-driven enhancements to the shopping experience across our web, mobile and instore channels.
