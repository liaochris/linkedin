Caleb Mercer's Projects

  • Pwning N00bs

    • April 2011 to April 2011
    Team Members: Caleb Mercer

    Call of Duty: Black Ops (COD:BO) is a popular online first-person shooter (FPS) video game available on multiple gaming consoles. Online FPS gameplay in titles such as COD:BO creates unique communities for gamers to interact in. Such environments could positively affect gamers by encouraging teamwork or creating for them a sense of belonging. Conversely, online gaming communities could foster bullying, taunting, and harassment.

  • A Theater of Self and Family Mobile Device Use

    • January 2007 to May 2007
    Team Members: Caleb Mercer

    While the literature on mobile devices has described variations in behavior and use, the cultural resources that make these behaviors intelligible and creditable have received less attention. This study illustrates the role expressive elements of culture (e.g., idiom and performance) can play in mobile phone behavior. This study found that mobile phone behavior not only reflects but can help to redefine central social institutions like the family. The students that were studied also used mobile devices to work through issues like autonomy and responsibility – ones that tie individuation, maturation and self directly to central issues in American culture. This research also suggests that the success the mobile industry has had in the United States with family pricing plans has much to do with the industry’s ability to capture and market key elements of American culture, such as kinship, autonomy, and financial independence.
