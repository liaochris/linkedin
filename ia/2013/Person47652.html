Gene Norman, CBM's Publications

  • "Alex’s Houston Impact"

    • June 2011
    Authors: Gene Norman, CBM

    Presentation made at 2011 AMS Broadcast Conference in Oklahoma City, Oklahoma

    Abstract: On July 1 and 2, 2010, the remnants of Hurricane Alex combined with a stalled front to deluge parts of Southeast Texas. I outline the meteorological conditions that led to this as well as show portions of the KHOU coverage. I also illustrate some of the ways the station applied a multi-media approach giving viewers interactive information. Conclusion indicates the lessons learned from this episode.

  • "Out On A Limb"

    • June 2010
    Authors: Gene Norman, CBM

    Presented at the 2010 AMS Broadcast Conference in Miami, FL.

    Abstract: On Nov. 30, 2009, computer models indicated that Houston could receive several inches of snow four days later. Successive computer models continued to reinforce the scenario of a surge of moisture arriving south of Houston as arctic air pushed in from the north. Indeed, large (for Houston) amounts of snow fell on Dec. 4, 2009. I showed portions of the KHOU coverage for that week day as well as illustrated the meteorological conditions that produced this very rare event.

  • "Hurricane Ike: Before, During and After"

    • June 2009
    Authors: Gene Norman, CBM

    Presented at the 2009 AMS Broadcast Conference in Portland, OR.

    Abstract: Hurricane Ike devastated the Upper Texas Coast in mid-September, 2008. I traced the history of the storm and showed how KHOU mobilized resources across multiple media platforms to serve viewers before, during and after the storm. I showed how the station implemented new technology as part of their continuing coverage commitment.
