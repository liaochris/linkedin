Kirk Jeffries' Summary

Industrial Solutions USA is a designer, fabricator and provider of protective solutions for physical assets incorporating these main product areas: elastomeric linings/coatings, flexible foam and heavy duty metal storage systems.

Industrial Solutions USA's protective systems are designed for long term performance in severe service environments.

Since 2005, Industrial Solutions USA provides innovative:
1) Protective spray and cast elastomeric lining/coating formulations: polyurethane, polyurea, polyaspartic, hybrids & primers.

Our COTS and customized formulations are designed for corrosion resistance, abrasion resistance, chemical attack, fire resistance, liquids containment, sound attenuation and vibration isolation.

2) Fabricated flexible foams with/without exterior cases/packaging: Polyethylene foams, cross-linked polyethylene foams, polyurethane foams, reticulated polyurethane foams and specialty foams.

We utilize CNC routing, compression molding, water-jet cutting, wire saw cutting, die cutting and thermoforming to obtain precise tolerances unavailable through most fabricators.

3) Heavy duty modular storage systems: Shelves, drawers, bulk racks, mobile aisle carriages, heavy weight capacity roll-out shelving, mezzanines, workbenches, etc.

We provide weapons storage cabinets with National Stock Number (NSN). The weapons racks are certified by TACOM for military use.

Member of SSPC and NDIA.

SAM, WAWF registered.

Specialties: Identifying/Integration of very robust "tough" components for specific protection environments.

Kirk Jeffries' Projects

  • CAMEL Prototype

    • February 2006 to December 2010
    Team Members: Kirk Jeffries, Primary Company Defense Contractor

    Unit Water Pod System (CAMEL) that will be the primary system for receiving, transporting, storing and issuing potable water within a unit.
    CAMEL has a water capacity of 800 gallons and is capable of preventing its water payload from freezing.
    ISUSA developed the lining/insulation system for four prototypes; all were tested and the lining/insulation portion of the CAMEL passed all tests.

  • Secondary Containment

    • September 2004 to Present
    Team Members: Kirk Jeffries, V&V Industrial Linings

    Colonial Pipeline had a pipeline trap area where the original concrete containment pad had cracked and heaved over time breaching the integrity of the concrete containment.

    Colonial Pipeline had to decide whether to replace the concrete containment area or repair it. They decided to repair the containment area.

    The Solution:
    Our polyurethane lining was sprayed 1/4” thick onto the primered concrete conforming to all the various shapes, contours and reducing transition elevations creating a monolithic, impervious protective lining with tenacious adhesion.

    The job was completed in 12 hours and back in service in 24 hours.

    EPA - SPCC compliant Title 40, Part 112.
