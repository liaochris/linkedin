Leland A. Core's Summary

Reverend Leland Core, CLC, currently serves as the President of ACS Communications. He brings well over 30 years of technical operational, engineering management centered on manufacturing computer main frames, robotic engineering, electronics, satellite technology, Hybrid Fiber Coaxial network design and telecommunication experience.

Reverend Core has served in several Senior Executive level positions for the following companies Comcast, MCI, DirecTV, AT&T Broadband, Time Warner, Cox Communications, and Control Data Corporation. Reverend Core's career and passion for people has afforded him the opportunity to lead and empower people in the following cities around the United States.

He completed his studies at Temple Business College in Computer Technology, Maryland Drafting Institute in Electrical/Mechanical Drafting Technology and subsequently received his Bachelor of Science Degree in Engineering Management. His completion in Dale Carnegie's multi-level training, as well as being a Certified Life Strategies Coach, has enhanced his gift to develop people.

Headquarted in Washington, D.C. ACS Communications is a high-end digital telecommunications company specializing in installations, maintenance and end-to-end IT network applications. ACS Communications provides a comprehensive array of core telecommunication services, and our information technology capabilities cover a wide suite of application, data, network, program and management products and services.

ACS Communications has over 70 years of expertise providing communications and information technology solutions to commercial and government clients. Whether a copper, fiber-optic or wireless solution is needed, ACS is capable of designing and building a network that meets the demands of your application. Our team of experienced and professional telecommunications technicians are capable of installing cable and structured wiring to include analyzing, engineering, implementing and maintaining new and existing communications systems.
