Chief Executive Officer

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Information Technology and Services industry

January 2005 – August 2008 (3 years 8 months)

As CEO, Bill's time was split between cultivating strategic relationships with clients, partners, advisors and investors as well as driving the functional direction of m2vc's products and services - managing the balance between short-term imperatives and long-term challenges.

Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; PWAV; Telecommunications industry

August 2002 – December 2004 (2 years 5 months)

Bill was recruited to ‘upgrade’ the IT Organization in this company. In early 2004, the company acquired a global enterprise ($300MM+) headquartered in Sweden and China, doubling his scope of control. He led an ERP Evaluation project, choosing Oracle e-Business Suite 11i for global deployment and completed a successful SOX-404 (IT Controls) internal and external audit for 2004.

VP, IT and Business Operations

Western Digital

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; WDC; Computer Hardware industry

September 1991 – September 1999 (8 years 1 month)

Bill was responsible for Customer Service, Technical Support, Account Management and Sales Support functions on a global basis. He had direct reports at the Vice President level. Bill devised and institutionalized information systems for improving forecasting, product allocation and cash projections on a weekly basis - realizing accuracy improvements of more than 50%.

In 1995 he was charged with the responsibility of replacing the entire Corporation's disparate worldwide information systems with a single, integrated ORACLE ERP / CRM system. He staffed the project with key Western Digital employees from all functional areas; chose the hardware, software and infrastructure solutions; engaged more than 150 external consulting resources and directed the entire ($50 million+) project through successful, on-time implementation.

Vice President and COO

Emerson Technologies, LP, Irvine, CA

December 1987 – August 1991 (3 years 9 months)

Emerson Technologies was a startup PC Company funded by Emerson Radio. Bill was instrumental in developing the 1990 business plan, positioning PC products for the OEM marketplace, hiring and developing a staff in the areas of Purchasing, MIS, Operations, Quality and Production Control. He led all manufacturing operations, from contract negotiations with prospective sub-contractors, to parts procurement, financial reporting, inventory control, production scheduling, production and quality assurance, as well as being the key overseer of product engineering. The company manufactured 130,000 PCs in the first year of operation.

Director, OEM Product Marketing / National Systems Manager

McDonnell Douglas, St. Louis, MO

January 1984 – November 1987 (3 years 11 months)

Bill was recruited into the position of National Systems Manager, responsible for the provision of pre- and post-sale assistance to the McDonnell Douglas Sales Organization across Canada. He hired a nationwide staff of systems analysts and programmers. Bill was transferred from MD Canada to the US parent organization as the Product Marketing Director for its minicomputer product line sales to ADP. He worked with the customer in defining custom hardware and software platforms for its Automobile Dealer Services Division, working with all customer and in-house disciplines to ensure the successful design, manufacture and implementation of the platform.

Vice President

June 1977 – December 1983 (6 years 7 months)

Bill was recruited into this organization as Director of Client Services and was quickly promoted to Vice President. He grew the staff of hardware and software professionals from 8 to 50+ during his tenure, leading the company from a diversified provider of system solutions to various business segments, into a focused leader in the Canadian hospital systems business. His efforts led to the acquisition of the company by USA leader - Shared Medical Systems.
