Aaron Orner's Summary

When thinking back on my career, I often visualize it as photography in an art gallery. Each sequenced frame chronicling risks taken, lessons learned and the rush of success. It makes sense as my career began in photography, film and television.

My career art gallery is packed with experience. The last 10 years of my career has focused toward online strategies and solutions. I take a “big picture” approach to improving the effectiveness of my Client's online marketing communications. I draw on my deep expertise, unique industry perspective, and comprehensive methodology to create fully-accountable solutions for specific situations.

With diverse consultancy, agency, and client experience across major verticals, I understand the opportunities and dynamics of online marketing.

I’ve developed a set of respected best practices that help create successful online marketing programs for leading brands like Pizza Hut, Foot Locker, Dell, and more.

Based in central Texas, I work with clients around the world. From New York to San Francisco and London to Berlin, location is not an obstacle. I love to travel and experience different cultures. However, the Texas hill country is my home and playground.

Aaron Orner's Projects

  • McCoy's Building Supply - Web Analytics

    • May 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Aaron Orner

    The McCoy's website is a "drive-to-store" experience. Users are guided to build a materials or shopping list. After submitting the list to the nearest store, the user can pick-up and pay for the materials locally. McCoy's has made great strides in providing its customers useful tools to improve their shopping experience.

    After moving their analytics from Omniture to Google, the marketing team began to question the accuracy of their new reports. In addition, the team was challenged to deliver "actionable" analytics to the merchandising and other internal teams.

    The project began with a comparative analysis between the Omniture installation and the new Google installation. Once the Google installation was aligned with the teams goals, the next step was developing Google Dashboards for each of the internal teams.

    Developing successful dashboards requires well defined goals and needs. All the teams were consulted to define those goals and needs. The McCoy's dashboard solutions required over 10 dashboards and 12 custom reports.

  • Empire Today - National Website

    • August 2006 to May 2007
    Team Members: Aaron Orner

    Empire Today provides shop-at-home convenience or on-site consultation, next day professional installation on many products and services to more than 55 metropolitan areas in the United States.

    The Client's website was not performing as a lead generator and SEO initiatives were plateauing. They were looking for a complete overhaul of the?online experience.

    The project began with a series of quantitative and qualitative research. The research included?a comparative lab-based usability study. The four day study, analyzed how target customers interacted with the Empire website and competitors' websites. ?The research initiative also included surveys and secondary research guiding communication strategies.

    A full set of research-backed UX documentation, SEO strategy, and communication strategies drove key business decisions. Once the new Empire website was in beta, another series of lab-based usability studies and communication focus groups confirmed the project's goals were met.

  • Pizza Hut - E-commerce

    • August 2001 to January 2005
    Team Members: Aaron Orner

    Delivery revolutionized the Quick Serve Restaurant (QSR) industry. Phone in your order and in 30 minutes a pie is at your door. 2001 was the year QSR revolutionized delivery. The phone was no longer the fastest way to order pizza. Pizza Hut executives knew online ordering was coming and decided to pave the way.

    In 2001, there were no best practices for ordering food online. This was truly a new frontier. With a big brand like Pizza Hut, there could be no mistakes. The project required months of research and technical requirements gathering.

    Parallel to the ordering system development, the launch campaign strategy called for an 200% media budget increase, new branding, new point-of-purchase (POP), new emails, new website, new coupons, and more.

    Pizza Hut's online ordering started the QSR delivery revolution. Today, brands like Domino's, Chipotle, PF Chang's, and more use the technology and user experience defined by Pizza Hut's 2001 online ordering system.

  • BMC Software - SEO

    • October 2011 to May 2012
    Team Members: Aaron Orner

    BMC's international SEO initiatives focused on three primary solutions; Control-M, Cloud, and ITSM. The Marketing Managers were missing critical insights to gauge visibility and optimization initiatives. They required a more focused data-driven SEO strategy.

    An initial analysis provided baseline metrics and clearer visibility into BMC’s organic search landscape. The analysis included a keyword audit, target URL audit, visibility report, and site optimization review. In addition to the baseline analysis, BMC's content development and distribution process was audited.

    The analysis determined BMC competitors were leading in the organic search results. BMC was producing volumes of content; however, website optimization issues and a silo'd distribution process prevented BMC from improving their visibility.

    Automated monthly and quarterly visibility reports were implemented. Quarterly analysis of the reports provides the Market Managers a clear picture of BMC's SEO landscape. Additionally, website optimization and centralized content development strategies laid a solid foundation for future link building initiatives.

Leveraging the touch-based user experience, customers can intuitively access critical business best practices. The mobile media budget focused on "catfish ads", text messaging, geo-messaging, email, and more.
