Jay Fenster's Patents

  • State-Aware, Conditional Mobile App Navigation System

    • United States Patent Application 61763304
    • Filed February 11, 2013
    Inventors: Jay Fenster

    The inCompass Cardinal uses a combination of administrator-defined business rules and flags to indicate points of segmentation, where an app would present different content or different navigation options based on specified variables. When a screen in the app renders, it checks the status of relevant flags and presents whichever variation of the content or navigation corresponds to that flag status.

    Feature overload on mobile applications renders them intimidating, unwieldy and difficult to use. Complicated navigation systems and overwhelming choices can cause a backlash against a bloated app, ultimately resulting in swift abandonment by its user base, especially older users who may be more easily frightened off by that complexity.

    By presenting the right options and content within the app at exactly the right moment, the GUI subtly encourages users directly toward the options app creators most want them to access, at the moment of maximum impact.

    To ensure that the deployment of inCompass Cardinal apps aligns with overall business goals, the combination of flags, business rules and conditional if-else statements allows for intricate segmentation on the back end that is completely undetectable to the end user.
