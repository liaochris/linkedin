Corey Dutson's Projects

  • Clarksons PLC

    • June 2012 to April 2013
    Team Members: Corey Dutson, Andrew Dyton, Jonathan Cousins, Neil Cooper, Tom Corser, Chris Stanley

    Clarksons wanted a new website to replace their existing, tired-feeling one. Our team came up with a variety of designs, and convinced the client to go bold. The end result was a website that was re-thought from the ground up, from the IA and UX, to the design, messaging, and branding, as well as the technology under-pinning the whole thing.

    My role was to architect the entire website from a technology stand-point, as well as do much of the development. We built the system using Umbraco due to it's flexible nature, low cost, and ease of integration with their existing server architecture. From there, I developed the overall HTML and CSS framework the website would use, as it had to be fully responsive.

    I constructed much of the JavaScript and jQuery around ease of expansion, and we took a progressive-enhancement angle on the whole project. We wanted to make sure that while the website would be at its best when viewed with the latest technologies, it had to still meet business requirements when viewed in older technologies.

  • Vodafone - Year in Review 2012

    • January 2012 to March 2012
    Team Members: Corey Dutson, Sam Scott, Andrew Webster

    Vodafone wanted to step away from the typical annual report, and Radley Yeldar were happy to pitch a very different idea. We went in with the idea of trimming out a lot of the typical overhead, and make their annual report more of a story telling people what they're all about, and less about feeding the figures to people. There was a large focus on video, as well as making the site easily accessible. The end result was a fully responsive, progressively enhanced website that worked cross-platform.

    From a technical standpoint, I developed the website using traditional a .Net setup, but as the end result was living in a CQ5 environment, I also had to make sure the site was correctly processed to a flat HTML build that worked within itself. Custom fonts, high-resolution images for retina displays, and javascript that changed depending on resolution are only some of the highlights and challenges that this project offered me.

  • Visa Europe - Future of Payments

    • February 2013 to March 2013
    Team Members: Corey Dutson, Andrew Webster, Waigwa Gatamu

    Visa opted to present a slimmed-down annual report that focused more on their investments in the future of customer payments and transactions. They wanted clear story-telling, they wanted it it accessible, and they wanted it to work on mobile devices without a separate mobile site.

    My role was primarily one of mentorship and correction for the other developer on the project. I gave direction on what responsive framework to use, how the javascript should be structured, and advised on how things should work in a responsive way. I did some of the more complex javascript items such as the mobile navigation, as well as all of the tightening-up of the pages with the designer to really make it sing.

  • Ahold - Year in review 2012

    • January 2013 to March 2013
    Team Members: Corey Dutson, Adam Mills, Waigwa Gatamu, Joe McLewin

    Ahold wanted to simplify their annual report experience and really focus on telling their story. They also wanted something that could easily translate to mobile. We came up with something that really showcased their major stories without sacrificing any of the experience. We also wanted to make sure that the website worked across a variety of platforms and systems, so a progressive enhancement view was taken to make sure all of the content was accessible and searchable.

    My role was mainly a mentorship and correction lead for the other developer on the project. I gave direction on how the responsive breaks should work, how the javascript should be structured, how to best integrate the video elements, as well as how to best translate the website between accessible and mobile views. I was also tasked with last-minute amends and corrections to make the project the best that it could be.

  • Flöff - web presence

    • October 2012 to December 2012
    Team Members: Corey Dutson, Andrew Webster

    I was commissioned to create the online presence of Flöff, a german winter beer. Working alongside the designer, we had to meet client requirements in both cost and timing, and still produce high-quality work. The website also had to work in multiple languages, and be accessible for indexing, whilst still maintaining an age-wall.

    I developed all of the code for the website, as well as a few of the visual assets. I also acted as an advisor to the designer and client to make sure they got the most out of their project for the budget allotted. I had to work smart in order to keep the time down on this project, which meant some smart component and asset re-use.

  • eClerx office wall scroll

    • August 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Corey Dutson, Matthew Glynn

    I was commissioned by JAC Interiors to create a wall scroll for their client eClerx. This design was to be installed in their new office, and so had to follow closely to designed specifications.

    The project called for creating a merged cityscape that demonstrated the global reach of the client. I was required to source all imagery, produce comps, get sign-off, and to work with the printers to make sure the quality of the end-product was as good as it could be.

    The design has since been installed, and everyone involved is happy with the end result.

  • Navig8 - Transparent overlay for glass wall

    • March 2013 to Present
    Team Members: Corey Dutson, Matthew Glynn

    I was commissioned by JAC Interiors to create a vector map design that would be applied to a large glass wall within Navig8's new office space.

    A straight-forward project that went really well. The client has a large brand palette, so the hardest part of this project was deciding what colours would not only best represent the client, but also work best given the transparent nature of the end substrate. I had to work with the client through proofs, test-strips, and sign-off to come up with a design they would be happy with.

  • The Children's Safety Village of Brant - Bookings Calendar system

    • July 2010 to Present
    Team Members: Corey Dutson, Bartek Ciszkowski

    The original booking system for the Safety Village was an arduous phone and fax system, where everything lived in a binder. It was cumbersome and difficult to educate others on how to use. In addition, it was difficult for support officers to know when they were working, as well as schedule when they could help.

    Bart and I proposed an online booking system that allowed for admin and support workers to access the system, see bookings at a glance, schedule classes, and print out details. Teachers could easily book their class(es) into the system using a 4 step system.

    The project featured an email notification and reminder system, and ease of use was a primary concern.

    The solution is still in use today.
