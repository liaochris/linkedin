Kathryn Britton's Summary

After 29 years as a software engineer, my interests shifted from computers to what makes people flourish in life. In 2006, I graduated from the University of Pennsylvania with a Master of Applied Positive Psychology degree. I opened a personal development coaching practice to put to good use the wonderful things I learned in the program. Positive psychology is an empirical discipline based on the concept that the “good” in life is much more than absence of the “bad.”

People hire coaches when they want to accelerate progress toward major goals, make positive changes in their lives, or feel happier with their lives just as they are.

To these ends, I listen intently, ask questions that broaden perspective, suggest actions that can lead to greater flourishing, and help people hold themselves accountable for their feelings and actions. Explaining opportunities and challenges out loud to an unbiased listener helps people think more clearly, and often my clients hear the best solutions coming out of their own mouths

Specialties

I have experience in IT that makes it easy for me to understand the challenges and opportunities in that field. I have also personally dealt with a chronic illness for 28 years, which gives me an interest in how to live well with physical limitations.

  • Resilience: How to Navigate Life's Curves

    • Positive Psychology News, LLC
    • 2009
    Authors: Kathryn Britton, Denise Clegg, Kirsten Cronlund, Senia Maymin, Eleanor Chin, MAPP, Nicholas Hall, Jeff Dustin, Timothy So, Aren Cohen, Sherri Fisher, Sean Doyle

    Do you ever lie awake worrying at night? Do you worry about how your boss is unhappy with your work or how you failed an exam or how your son may get swine flu or how your parents are old and need attention? Yes, some things are bad. We don't deny it. The biggest misperception about positive psychology is that it is positive thinking. It is not. Positive psychology is the study of what makes people grow and flourish, no matter what circumstances surround them. Life includes grief and loss. As a prominent professor says, "Positive psychology is the permission to be human." But worrying about things that may never occur takes a huge toll on people. People may also attach more suffering than necessary to negative events by creating interpretations that make them feel helpless. This little book explores an important topic in the field of positive psychology: Why do some people go through hard times and be-come stronger and more appreciative of their lives? Do they think differently than other people? Do they behave differently? What can we learn from them that can make other people's lives better? What tools and techniques emerge from research that can help all of us deal more effectively with the challenges of our time?

  • Gratitude: How to Appreciate Life's Gifts

    • Positive Psychology News, LLC
    • 2010
    Authors: Kathryn Britton, Kirsten Cronlund, Senia Maymin, Sherri Fisher, Jen Hausman, Miriam Akhtar, Sean Doyle, Angus Skinner, Yee-Ming Tan, Nicholas Hall, Timothy So

    The second book in the Positive Psychology News series explores the benefits and practices of gratitude and looking at the world with an appreciative eye. Like the earlier book on resilience, it conveys compelling research results with stories, examples, and suggested actions that are immediately useful. Topics include talking to children about gratitude, giving gifts that increase happiness, fresh ways to celebrate holidays, and experiencing gratitude in unlikely situations.

    • United States Patent 6535896
    • Issued March 18, 2003
    Inventors: Kathryn Britton, Steve Ims, Brad Topol

    Systems, methods and computer program products are provided for utilizing XML-based tools to tailor HTML-based Web page content for display within various client devices. Content portions of a requested Web page are converted to an XML format and then modified using an XML content-tailoring tool. Other content portions of the Web page are masked so as to be "hidden" and are, thus, not converted to XML format.   • Systems, methods and computer program products for assigning, generating and delivering content to intranet users

    • United States Patent 6564259
    • Issued May 13, 2003
    Inventors: Kathryn Britton, David L. Kaminsky, Stephen C. Baber, Ann Marie Fred, Brian Blount

    Systems, methods and computer program products allow intranet administrators to assign, generate and deliver content to users of an intranet. Intranet users are defined and assigned to various defined user groups. Units of content available to users of an intranet are also defined and assigned to various defined content groups. The defined content groups are then associated with the defined user groups such that each of the defined user groups has at least one of the defined units of content associated therewith. A content page creation profile is provided for each defined user and is configured to control how content is displayed within a user's customized content page. Through identified relationships of user groups and content groups, the units of content assigned to a user can be determined and a content page containing the assigned units of content can be created and delivered to a user.
