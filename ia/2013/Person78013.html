Laia Cabrera's Summary

Laia Cabrera is a filmmaker and video artist born in Spain and based in New York since 1997. As a filmmaker and visual artist she uses a variety of media merging cinematic arts; dance, music; photography; theater; visual arts; voice; writing. She is the recipient of several awards including the grant KrTU to Young Creators and the Kodak and Color Lab award for Best Cinematic Film for “Under Influence”. Her work as a filmmaker includes “Singularity” (inaugurated the International video animation festival “Animac”, Spain), “Is There an Edge of Belief?” (International multidisciplinary art festival “Jaen en Femenino”, Spain and Bienal Internacional ULA 2010, Merida, VENEZUELA, etc,) Her latest creation, “Shifting Gaze”, a Film-Art-Music Installation, premiered at “Spain Art Fest’10” in Times Square, NY, (October 2010) and closed “Region Ø Latino Video Art Festival of New York” (March 2011). Her visual artwork credits includes “Mapping Moebus”, a multimedia theater play presented at La Mama theater (NY, December 2010); “Monegros-Nueva York” video installation held at La Lonja (Zaragoza, Spain, 2011); “Walk Pasa Bouge V1” Video-Performance- presented in New York and Brussels (2009-2011); “Playing Equality” (Dance-theater-Video), Boricua College of New York, (November 2009), Festival Internacional 'ReACT'” Valencia, (Spain. May 2010); “Claim your Place”, a large scale video-installation-performance presented in the USA and Europe. “For Feather” live cinema with live musicians performed at BAM (NY, November 2008) and throughout the USA, She is also an award winning visual effects artist, director, editor, writer, playwright and composer. She edited the Docu-Concert “Cachao” with Andy Garcia, the documentary “The World of Vija Vetra”, the Feature Film “Shut Up and Do It” and the upcoming film “Reinventing Kate Carew”. She is currently working on a new large sitespecific visual installation at the Cathedral Saint John The Divine in New York, November 2011.
