✔ David E. Fowler's Summary

Rosarnach Media provides web site consulting, management and training so that small and medium-sized businesses can make the most of their internet marketing budget.

Among our services we provide advice and execution of site make-overs, ongoing content management, search engine optimization and pay per click advertising.

Leading the company is David E. Fowler, B.A., B.Ed., an internet pioneer. With a local radio station David started an dial-up internet service provider in 1995 and quickly grew it to be the largest in town only selling it to a larger company at the height of the dot com boom in 2000.

In 2003 David made headlines with his unprecedented online coverage of Kelowna's 2003 Okanagan Mountain Park Fire as he provided 24/7 news and updates on the fire on his company's blog. David's efforts were widely recognized and credited as being a core part of the emergency response to the fire. The City recognized David's efforts by having him accept the thank you award on behalf of the city's media.

In 2003 David took the reigns of a local struggling online newspaper Castanet.net and turned it around to be a serious player in its local advertising market. When David left the company its margins were 30% and site traffic continued to grow.

In 2012, David founded his internet consulting practice to share his expertise: Rosarnach Media.

David is a collaborative leader and a long range thinker with creative and innovative ideas. Integrity and respect are important core values to him.

Specialties: internet marketing, web site design, website content management, project management, social media management (SMM), search engine optimization (SEO), WordPress, Joomla, Google Analytics, Ad Words, IT software and hardware support for small business

General Manager / Content Manager

Castanet.net (Castanet Media Ltd.)

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Marketing and Advertising industry

May 2003 – July 2010 (7 years 3 months) Kelowna, BC

As General Manager and on separate occasions as Content Manager, I was responsible for increasing traffic to the online newspaper site Castanet.net. Over the first six months of my tenure, I doubled the site's traffic and subsequently oversaw a complete overhaul of the site which resulted in Castanet becoming profitable after four years of operations.

Under my tenure news coverage was increased, and new content management, classifieds, and advertising management software was introduced. Under my direction, sales changed their strategy of selling individual ad positions on the site to less confusing and more lucrative “pooled” positions.

The parent company of Castanet, Silk FM Broadcasting Ltd, was sold in January 2007. As Castanet and Silk shared office space, an accountant and sales staff, I had to reinvent each of these for Castanet in a very short time. In Castanet's first year away from Silk, three new sales people were hired and revenue almost doubled. With the addition of a full-time technical person, a reporter and an automall photographer, the company grew to 15 staff, $2 million in revenue, 200,000 unique visitors a week, and 6.5 million page views a week.

In 2010 I moved all of Castanet’s internal system resources to Google Applications, switched the web servers to a more scalable blade environment, updated the classifieds front end, created a radio page, daily email newsletter and an iPhone application.
