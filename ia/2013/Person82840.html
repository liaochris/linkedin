Colin Lowry's Publications

  • Role of Parkinson’s Law on Productivity in a Timed Environment

    • Irish HCI Conference 2013 – 7th Annual Irish HCI Conference Proceedings
    • June 13, 2013
    Authors: Colin Lowry, Simon Finney

    An experiment was designed to test the effects of Parkinson’s Law on productivity rates in a virtual reality environment. Two groups were asked to complete a series of tasks. The first group was given a set time to complete the tasks with a countdown timer displayed on the user interface. The second group were not given a set time and no timer was displayed on the user interface. The experiment was designed to examine the differences between the groups’ time taken to complete the tasks.

    In order to carry out the study, a 3D virtual environment was designed and developed using Autodesk 3DS Max and Vizard VR with Python Programming Language. The environment can be easily rolled out into a Cave Automatic Virtual Environment (CAVE), a high-tech immersive virtual reality system.
