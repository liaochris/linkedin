Eric Brunt, LEED AP's Summary

I love making things!

Growing up I had an idea that I wanted to design products, but no idea how to go about doing so. With a lack of information on such a career path I found myself drawn to the related field of Architecture for its foundation in design and making. It wasn't until a furniture design studio in my last quarter of Architecture school that I learned of the Industrial Design profession.

I decided to shift my focus to Industrial Design after gaining valuable work experience in the Architecture industry. Studying both disciplines has provided me a unique perspective on design and user experience, and how they relate to people at all scales.

I welcome design challenges of all types. Although my focus is in Industrial Design I am a multidisciplinary designer that also excels in Architecture, Furniture, Photography, Visual Communication Design, and Interaction Design.

  • Flux Snowshoe

    Team Members: Eric Brunt, LEED AP

  • Goldmyer.org (Goldmyer Hot Springs)

    • January 2011 to Present
    Team Members: Eric Brunt, LEED AP, Serena Li, Weijia Wang

    Goldmyer Hot Springs is a wilderness preserve managed by Northwest Wilderness Programs, a Washington State non-profit corporation. I managed the creation of a new website that showcases this natural wonder of the Pacific Northwest and incorporates a reservation management system for communications between our office staff in Seattle and live-in caretakers at the Hot Springs located 30 miles outside of North Bend. The process of creating this new website brought up many questions that facilitated clarification of old and creation of new policies among fellow board members. I am responsible for design direction, photography, creating and editing the website content, and designing downloadable/printable documents.

  • Multi-Stool

    • September 2011 to December 2011
    Team Members: Eric Brunt, LEED AP

    A stool consisting of two parts that separate into two chairs whenever extra seating is needed. Height adjustable from 18” to 27”, it adapts to fit a variety of uses: freestanding seat(s), desk chair, drafting stool, bar stool, side table, laptop stand, etc. The Multi-Stool is made from 100% recyclable materials.

  • Wine Table

    • April 2008 to June 2008
    Team Members: Eric Brunt, LEED AP

    A wine table providing storage for 6 wine bottles and an 18-piece stemware set. Inspired by Celtic knot work, the table's steel frame weaves in and out of itself, creating a never-ending line of structure. The front edges of the frame are polished to highlight this feature and invite the user's eye to explore the different components of the table. The composition of the steel and wood elements, as well as negative space, further explores this idea of knot work and the relationship between these two distinct materials.

    DIMENSIONS: 36" tall x 51" long x 18" deep

    MATERIALS: Jatoba (Brazilian Cherry) and Steel Angle.
