Max Buriak's Summary

Max began his career in the late 90’s as a Presentation Architect for USWeb/CKS. Max used his relationships to help found their first Vignette CMS Practice.

In the early 2000s, Max accepted a partner position at Ironbridge Partners – a venture capital incubator group. While there Max filled the role of Technology Architect. He evaluated business plans and worked with startups to launch their products and become self-sustaining and profitable. In 2001, Max left Ironbridge for Cepstral, a startup just out of the incubation stage.

Cepstral, a text-to-speech synthesis company, offered many challenges. Max created relationships with software and technology partners, helped to spearhead Cepstral’s product design/marketing, and re-branded the company’s image. The Cepstral Voice Store (One of Max's innovative projects) was a valuable stream of revenue for well over 8 years.

In 2003, Max left Cepstral to consult full time with startups and small to medium size businesses. During this time Max focused on building brands, markets, and technology products for his clients.

In 2005, Max joined the Buzzhoney team as their Senior Technology Architect managing projects for Eaton, Leeds and GE.

In 2006, Max left his position with Buzzhoney to start his own consulting group called Up Up Results. Working on a virtual agency model Up Up assembled teams from the best of Pittsburgh's freelance community.

In early 2008, Max was asked to fill the role of Director of Technology at Merging Media, a boutique agency focused on video production and interactive projects. Max was responsible for managing all interactive initiatives, partnerships and new business.

In 2013 Max left Pittsburgh for New York City. He currently works at the Dow Jones as a Technology Director managing online projects for Wall Street Journal and Barron's.

Working with senior Usweb/CKS management he created the first CMS group inside Usweb/CKS. In the past Max and his developer counterparts had created thier own small-scale CMSs, but a standardized CMS like Vignette offered a chance to work with large corporate websites requiring full integration with other internal technology services.

    After much research Max settled on Vignette because of its open platform and versatile programing base. In addition to Vignette being deployed for large enterprise websites, the CMS group also consulted on medium to small size content management integration.

  • Usweb/CKS Vision Suite

    • 1999 to Present
    Team Members: Max Buriak, Matthew Portune, Louis Manning

    Working in partnership with Microsoft and Panasonic DVD, I helped to develop a product called Vision Suite, a multiple purpose DVD based sales tool. In the late 90s most businesses were still far away from having broadband accessible in their offices. This was also true for public meeting places like airports, hotels and coffee shops. Vision Suite was designed to be a "broadband bridge" providing large (unfriendly to download) company assets like power point presentations, images and high quality video to be accessible on a single DVD carried by a Usweb/CKS sales associate or consultant. The Vision Suite product was funded internally by Usweb/CKS and could support gigs of material. To access the contents a sales rep would simply insert the dvd and watch Vision Suite's front-end interface (that I developed) load. It would then use proprietary programming calls (given to us by Microsoft's IE development team) to access dvd video content and play it inside the browser's html interface. Vision Suite at its time was cutting edge and led to a number of breakthroughs. Because of our work on IE's experimental DVD features both Microsoft and Panasonic wrote case studies about it.
