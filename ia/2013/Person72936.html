Kathy Brandt's Summary

As Principal of the kb group, llc, Kathy helps organizations identify and maximize their capacity to grow and expand their services through business development, marketing, engagement, planning and system design. Kathy loves helping organizations identify their “sweet spot,” that unique opportunity to leverage organizational assets to achieve their goals.

For more than 16 years Kathy helped lead the National Hospice and Palliative Care Organization, working her way up through the organization to become the Senior Vice President, Office of Innovation and the Executive Director of NHPCO’s Mary J Labyak Institute for Innovation.

After her first 7 years at NHPCO, Kathy left to work for Suncoast Hospice, nationally recognized as a trend-setting program. At Suncoast she created a community engagement model, Project DECIDE, which led to Suncoast being named at Rallying Points Regional Resource Center, a Robert Wood Johnson Funded initiative.

Kathy led NHPCO’s efforts to help hospices evolve beyond the Medicare Hospice Benefit, expanding services in a fiscally-prudent, mission-focused way to help people access palliative care sooner in their disease process. Kathy spearheaded NHPCO’s forays into online education, authored grants and contracts generating more than seven million dollars of funding, and created Caring Connections, an award-winning consumer engagement initiative that has helped millions of people learn more about advance care planning, palliative care, hospice and grief. NHPCO’s go-to-person for strategic planning, Kathy facilitated strategic planning for the boards of NHPCO, the National Hospice Foundation, the Hospice Action Network, and Americans for Better Care of the Dying and chaired the planning committee for the American Society on Aging.

Kathy currently serves on the American Society on Aging's strategic planning committee and is chair of ASA's membership committee.

    Advance care planning activities frequently focus on the need to complete the two documents comprising an advance directive - the living will and designation of a healthcare agent or surrogate. This article presents a new paradigm, encouraging advocates to lead discussions with the importance of naming someone to speak for them in the event of a crisis. This article sparked the creation of NHPCO's Speak4me Social Media Campaign http://www.nhpco.org/resources/speak4me-social-media-campaign
