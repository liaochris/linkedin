Mihai Corlan's Summary

I have been working with web technologies since late '90: from web design to web development, from PHP to ColdFusion and Java, from DHTML to Ajax/HTML5 and Flex/AIR.

These days I split my time between these activities: writing technical articles, talking to product teams and customers, building prototypes or coding, speaking at conferences, organizing conferences, and traveling around the world to do all the above. I have presented at more than 200 events and spoke to tens of thousands of people at events across the world - South America, North America, Europe, Middle East, and Asia.

This is how everything started: in June 2008, I took a position as a Platform Evangelist with Adobe. My job was to "evangelize" the Flash Platform in Europe. In order to accomplish this objective, I wrote articles, presented at conferences and events, and visited customers.

In my current role I wear, sometimes simultaneously, a technical marketing hat, a developer/designer hat, and a professional speaker/trainer hat. One goal I have is raising awareness for the work we are doing at Adobe on web standards and tools/libraries for web development and the workflows we are providing for creative professionals in the form of the Creative Cloud offering. Another part of my job is to work close with the product management team and make sure I pass the feedback and insights I get from the field.

Before being an evangelist I worked as a computer scientist on the Flash Builder team. Flash Builder is Adobe's IDE for Rich Internet Applications, based on the Eclipse platform.

Specialties: Programming in JavaScript, HTML(5), CSS, PhoneGap/jQuery/jQuery Mobile, PHP, Java, SQL, Flex/ActionScript
