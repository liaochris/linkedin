Grzegorz M Michalski +48503452860 grzegorzmichalski@gmail.com's Summary

Asistant professor of finance at the Wroclaw University of Economics. The main area of research are business finance and entrepreneurial liquidity management. The research is aimed at understanding the determinants and dynamics of financial enterprise liquidity and working capital management. That research examines the enterprise value and cost of capital results of working capital and corporate liquidity management policies and results of demand for cash, accounts receivables and liquidity by enterprises. Investigation of the effects of liquidity on portfolio choice and corporate current assets levels and he studied the liquidity decisions made by nonprofit organizations, current business investment in accounts payable, inventories and operating cash. Recent grants and projects examine the effect of liquidity constraints on nonprofit organizations and forprofit small enterpises decisions to level of current assets investments, and on whether or not to use such information on cost of capital level and results on business valuation results. In ongoing work, are studies the unique risk characteristics of business organization capital, and documents the high expected returns which enterprises heavily invested in organization capital earn. His works have been published in ISI academic journals such as the Romanian Journal of Economic Forecasting, the Journal of Economic Computation and Economic Cybernetics Studies and Research, and the Agricultural Economics. He is an author or coauthor of over 80 papers and 10 books. He is part of Editorial Boards in international journals:
Australasian Accounting Business & Finance Journal (AABFJ) ISSN: 1834-2000,
Journal of Corporate Treasury Management (JCTM) ISSN 1753-2574,
Journal of Problems and Perspectives in Management (PPM) ISSN: 1727-7051, and some other.
He holds MA and Ph.D. degrees from the Wroclaw University of Economics (MA in finance and banking in 1998 and PhD in economics in 2002).

Specialties

Corporate finance decisions, corporate liquidity management, value based current assets investment strategies
