Fabio Bier's Summary

I specialize in the design and development of clean and smart user interfaces for the web. I think there is lots to be learned from the ever changing way people interact with web- sites and applications.

We have seen some major shifts in the way people interpret and navigate websites, 'touch' being one of the more upcoming and revolutionary of those.

I take pride in delivering not only clean designs but clean code as well. I always strive to conform to the latest, most strict standards and guidelines. If necessary (which I find is almost always) I use a framework to get me started in developing.

As a (mainly) Front-end developer I don't like code logic to interfere with my presentation layer so using a Framework with an MVC design pattern naturally appeals to me. Some examples of Frameworks I like working with are:
- CodeIgniter (My framework of choice for most PHP based projects).
- Django (Python framework).

Being the geek that I am I'm also nuts about all things technology. Open Source software being my favorite of casual interests. I have extensive knowledge of Linux based Operating Systems and the Linux system in general.

I have set up hundreds of LAMP stacks and know my way around the command-line.

Needless to say I am also a great Star Wars fan (mandatory geek thing I guess).

I strive to be the golden combination of being a Web Designer and Developer all in one combo pack.

Even if I might not succeed to be as efficient on all disciplines I figure it can never hurt to have a better understanding of each role involved when it comes to building a website from scratch to launch. This helps me think through the approach and strategy from start to finish and therefore having less surprises along the way.

Quote to live by: "Responsive Design is a way of life".

Interaction design

2004 – 2008

My school works by a method of teaching called "project based education" this means that they realy don't teach you a whole lot. In fact, this method is based on working experience, the school comes up with several assignments (for real industries or fake ones) and we get to work.

During this cycle we are responsible for our scedules and It doesn't quite matter how you do it, as long as you manage to make the deadline. This system is working out just fine for me, since I've really learned to scedule my projects to do so. We bear complete responsibility of our own actions and mistakes if any.

In the third (semi final) year I went on a whole years training period. I got to work the real way and this is where all my knowledge realy added up.
