Cory David Alexander Evans' Summary

Political Advisor to the Deputy Consul-General at the Consulate-General of Japan in New York. Extensive experience in quantitative political analysis, psephology, government affairs, legal research and international protocol.

Adjunct Lecturer in the Department of Philosophy at Baruch College and Adjunct Instructor in the Department of Social Sciences at the Fasion Institute of Technology. Principal focus in jurisprudence and political philosophy.

Cory David Alexander Evans' Experience

Political Advisor

The Consulate-General of Japan in New York

March 2010 – Present (3 years 2 months) 299 Park Avenue, New York, New York

The Consulate-General of Japan in New York provides consular and diplomatic representation for the Japanese government. The jurisdiction of the consulate is limited to the American Northeast. During my time at the Consulate I have served under Ambassador Shinichi Nishimiya and Ambassador Shigeyuki Hiroki.

As political advisor, I assist the Consulate in navigating the American political system, primarily focused on New York City and New York State.

Since June of 2011 I have also served as a consultant to a small group of Japanese private firms, working to improve their familiarity with the American regulatory process. My clients are primarily in the financial services, capital markets and securities sectors. A list of clients is available on request.

I previously held the positions of research assistant (2010) and political analyst (2011) at the Consulate.

Cory David Alexander Evans' Publications

  • The Desolation of Smog

    • OpEdNews
    • October 3, 2012
    Authors: Cory David Alexander Evans

    In this article, I discussed some of the problems with air pollution in New York City, especially the pollution caused by the use of dirty heating oil. I finished by arguing in favor of Assemblymember Dan Quart's legislation to provide tax credits for the replacement of older, dirty heating units.

  • Will Japan's New Prime Minister Last?

    • Dissent Magazine (Blog)
    • September 26, 2011
    Authors: Cory David Alexander Evans

    In this article I explore the crisis of leadership in Japan's highest office. I argue that Japan's Prime Minister squandered a golden opportunity to turn the corner after the Great Earthquake.

  • A New Translation of Vanini's Dialogues

    • The Philosophical Forum
    • September 10, 2011
    Authors: Cory David Alexander Evans

  • Innovations in the Mikenas Benoni

    • Chess Life
    • September 1, 2011
    Authors: Cory David Alexander Evans

    In this article my father and I analyze new ideas in the Mikenas Benoni and demonstrate a significant improvement for the black side. My research suggests that, with accurate play, black achieves dynamic equality in this particular variation.

  • The Future of the Japanese Labor Movement

    • Dissent Magazine (Online)
    • August 13, 2011
    Authors: Cory David Alexander Evans

    In this extended essay, featured on the home page of Dissent Magazine, I trace the history of the Japanese labor movement and contemplate its possible future. I argue that Japan's unique "union-as-HR" model led to various benefits but ultimately stunted the growth of an independent labor movement.

  • The Development of Ethics: A Review

    • The Philosophical Forum
    • August 1, 2011
    Authors: Cory David Alexander Evans

    I contributed a short section analyzing Terrence Irwin's take on Suarez for this special issue of the Philosophical Forum. I argue that Irwin's exposition of Suarez is excellent, although highly technical, but he fails to give a compelling argument on the importance of Suarez in philosophical history.

  • Eric Cantor Bets Against the Treasury

    • Dissent Magazine (Blog)
    • June 30, 2011
    Authors: Cory David Alexander Evans

    Eric Cantor, the House Majority Leader, claims a financial wager he made against U. S. Treasuries is not a conflict of interest because he was hedging his portfolio. But a basic financial analysis of his equity position reveals glaring inconsistencies, calling his objectivity as a negotiator into question.

  • No Guarantees for Japan after 2012

    • The Japan Times
    • July 8, 2011
    Authors: Cory David Alexander Evans, Humza Ahmad

    Historically, the Japanese political elite have always been more comfortable with Republicans. However, a look at the field of Republican Presidential candidates for 2012 shows that there is no guarantee that a future Republican administration be more beneficial than the Obama administration.

  • Rational Intuition in the Art of Chess

    • Phenomenology and the Cognitive Sciences
    • June 1, 2011
    Authors: Cory David Alexander Evans, Barbara Montero

    In this peer-reviewed academic essay, Barbara Montero and I argue that Herbert Dreyfus' account of expertise in chess as arationally intuitive is in error. We argue that chess players do frequently deliberate and, even when they do not do so, their intuitive decisions are fundamentally rational.

  • What Keeps the Japanese People Together

    • Dissent Magazine (Blog)
    • May 11, 2011
    Authors: Cory David Alexander Evans

    In the wake of the terrible Great East Japan Earthquake in Japan one positive headline has emerged: the near-total absence of looting and crime in the Tohoku region. I argue that traditional aspects of Japanese culture along with structural improvements in law enforcement account for this superlative record.

  • The Next 25 Years: A Review

    • The Advocate
    • December 3, 2008
    Authors: Cory David Alexander Evans

    In this review of "The Next 25 Years" by Martin Garbus, I criticize the argument that judicial outcomes in the American legal system are purely political. I argue that whatever one might think of the arcane legal arguments employed by the Supreme Court, cases of consensus despite political controversy indicate that there exists at least a weak set of shared legal principles.

  • In Re: "Moderates, Schmoderates"

    • Rocky Mountain News
    • September 25, 2008
    Authors: Cory David Alexander Evans

    In this Letter to the Editor, I criticized the argument that moderates lack political principles. I draw from history to illustrate that a rich variety of value perspectives might lead one to a principled yet still moderate ideological stance.

  • In Re: "Come Back, GOP"

    • The Los Angeles Times
    • August 25, 2008
    Authors: Cory David Alexander Evans

    In this letter to the editor, I criticize an unstated premise in the LA Times editorial "Come Back, GOP." I argue that the Times conflates discretionary and non-discretionary spending in their editorial, resulting in a muddled account of fiscal policy during the Reagan administration.

  • Against Residency Restrictions

    • Christian Science Monitor
    • August 14, 2008
    Authors: Cory David Alexander Evans

    In this essay I argue that residency restrictions violate Article I, Section 9 of the United States Constitution. Since the Constitution bars punishment ex post facto, and since residency boundaries cannot be legislated in advance, I argue that society must select an alternative form of punishment.

  • Non-Linear Modal Semantics

    • Intuitions (Student Journal)
    • May 3, 2007
    Authors: Cory David Alexander Evans

    This essay was my undergraduate honors thesis. My goal was to square skeptical intutions about the metaphysics of modal systems B and S5 with a new approach to iterated model operators. I propose a hierachical approach to reading phrases like 'LMP', arguing that the best interpretation is: "in all logically possible universes there exists a metaphysically possible world such that P."
