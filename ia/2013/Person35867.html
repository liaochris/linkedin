Dita Firdiana's Publications

  • Minimal Intervention as a Unity of Dental Treatment in Indonesia

    • 29th Asia Pacific Dental Congress Journal
    • April 2007
    Authors: Dita Firdiana

    The concept of Minimal Intervention (MI) in dentistry can be expected to improve the level of dental health in the general community. The primary aim of preventive dentistry is the improvement of oral and dental health and the prevention of the occurance or formation of any disease in a patient’s mouth. MI has principal concepts. The principal concepts are problem identification, prevention and so rehabilitation and control of existing caries. The concept of MI can be applied to ill-equipped areas. This concept is done without invasive action but using Atraumatic Restorative Treatment (ART). By understanding the principles implied in MI, and understanding field and authority of dentist and also preventive points that could be done by society. Dentists can fully carry out their roles towards society. The society can understand the development of oral and dental diseases in simple terms, and apply various means to prevent them. With the advancement of the MI concept, it is hoped that there will be improvements in health quality of Indonesian, especially oral and dental health. Therefore, we will achieve INDONESIA SEHAT 2010.
