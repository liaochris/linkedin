Bob Stanke's Summary

Bob Stanke is a recognized professional in the field of digital strategy and interactive technologies. Stanke is currently the Director of Interactive Services for the Minnesota Timberwolves and Minnesota Lynx, and is a published blogger, speaker and panelist.

As an experienced Digital Strategist and Community Engagement Management with 15 years of experience, Stanke specializes in the design, delivery, and day-to-day execution of enterprise digital content and social business strategies, including online community development. Stanke understands the tools, tactics, and techniques needed to design, develop, and direct the intersection of content, community and communications, both online and off, to successfully help companies navigate customer experiences, their brand, products, and/or services across the web.

In this free time, Stanke enjoys spending time with family, is an avid basketball fan, and can regularly be seen on one of the many beautiful golf courses in Minnesota and Wisconsin.

Specialties

Content Lifecycle Strategy, Community Management, Community Architecture, Community Design, Community Development, Moderation Strategy, Project Management, Business Analysis, Project Coordination, Business & Technical Research, Social Media, Social Media Policy Development, Social Networking, Social Marketing, Database & Application Development, CRM, Business Operations, Google AdWords, Google AdSense, SEO, Squarespace Development, Reputation & Brand Management, Analytics, and Entrepreneurship.

Database Developer, Microsoft Application Developer, Project Manager

Montage, Inc.

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Internet industry

December 1998 – September 2005 (6 years 10 months) Greater Minneapolis-St. Paul Area

Over the course of almost seven years at Montage, I held a variety of different roles, supporting clients such as 3M, Medtronic and Blue Cross Blue Shield.

My first role with Montage was as a database developer and systems analyst. In this role, I helped the company design and build business critical systems to help with the growth of the firm. Examples of these systems were: a lead generation system, eCommerce system, and a fulfillment and distribution system – all critical components to the firm’s service offerings. Towards the end of this role, I had established key processes needed to facilitate the company to moving to a common ERP system using the module development strategy which had led to much success and cost savings.

In my role as the Reporting Manager, my main responsibility was to gather business intelligence reporting requirements, execute a development strategy, perform quality assurance and testing, and automate delivery of said reports. To achieve this, I worked as a team lead and helped other IT departments establish data requirements, processing rules, and data quality standards. The reports that my team produced were often delivered to high-ranking decision makers at Fortune 500 companies.

As a Project Manager for the organization, I led many different types of IT and business projects spanning in areas of marketing, advertising, sales lead processing, fulfillment and distribution. Specifically, I managed one of the largest system development projects in company history, involving the complete overhaul and re-development of the firm’s flagship information system – a global consumer warranty database. The original system was in a legacy database platform and lacked a user-friendly interface. I successfully led a team of internal developers and outside consultants to convert the system (entailing millions of business critical records and thousands of tables) into a relational system within a SQL Server platform and VB.net front-end.
