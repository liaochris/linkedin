Matthieu Dallon's Summary

Managing director and co-founder of Oxent, gaming oriented interactive agency, that owns and develops online and offline eSports assets:

• ESWC (www.eswc.com), Electronic Sports World Cup, pioneered and iconic global competition of video games, starting with qualifying on all continents, to end with a World Final designed as a live show.

• Gamersband (www.gamersband.com), geo-social network providing tournaments and rankings engine to leagues and video games publishers.

• Cyberathlete Summit (www.cybsum.com), live events dedicated to one game highlighting champions’ talent.

• Esports France (www.esportsfrance.com), online daily news about video games competitions.

Prior to Oxent, Matthieu Dallon created the Electronic Sports World Cup (ESWC) in 2002, and has been its producer in the Futuroscope Park, the Carrousel du Louvre, at Paris Bercy, Paris Expo, San José Congress Center, and during the Paris Games Week. Under Matthieu Dallon’s leadership, ESWC has federated gaming organizations in over 70 countries and designed all modern esport’s touchstones, gathering more than 200,000 live spectators during Finals and offering more than 1,500,000$ to video games champions.

Founder of Gaming Show of Paris (Mondial du Gaming) in 2006, the Fnac Trophy of Video Games (Trophée Fnac) in 2004, the Video Games French Cup (Coupe de France des Jeux Vidéo) in 2003, the Cyberleagues platform in 2001. Co-founder of Ligarena in 2000, producer of the Lan-Arena events from 2000 to 2002, and of the WCG launch in 2001. Co-initiator with Nadeo in 2006 of Trackmania Nations ESWC, the first Free-to-Play designed for eSports.

Electronic Sports World Cup (ESWC) is the pioneered and iconic global competition of video games, starting locally with qualifiers on all continents, to end with a World Final designed as a live show.

The ESWC was created in France in 2003. From its first editions at Futuroscope, the World Cup has federated video game tournaments on the 5 continents. In 2005 at the Louvre of Paris, then at Paris Bercy in 2006, the event has become a reference for the organization of video game competitions and live match showcases. In 2008, the Grand Final was organized in San José, California. In 2011, the ESWC won over the audience at Paris Games Week with 5 days of shows staged in front of more than 150,000 visitors and an Internet audience of 2 million. In 2012, the ESWC takes up the challenge again at Paris Games Week, satisfying both purists of electronic sports and video game fans, in a festive and sportsmanlike atmosphere.

ESWC federates companies as non-profit organizations all over the world; all convinced that video games champions are genuine athletes, precursors of their generation and the new sports of the XXIst century.
