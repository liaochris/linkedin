Michael Rambach's Summary

I’m Michael Rambach, a freelance designer, web designer, and social media manager. My job is to make start-up small businesses look good on a shoestring. You can contact me via my website www.GraphicAirDesign.com

"A life spent making mistakes is not only more honorable but more useful than a life doing nothing."

I started as paste-up artist at Auto Trader Magazines, moved into circulation there and at Metro Community news. On to owning a lawn mowing/light landscape company and then working for a top water garden center where eventually I was GM of both divisions. I grew overly anxious to stand on my artistic talents and now operate my own design, social/online marketing, web design agency (I call it an agency because of my broad reach.)

I am interested in live music, great art and learning about how everything works and works together.

Michael Rambach's Projects

  • Holiday Twin Rinks

    Team Members: Michael Rambach

    The project brief asked to design a logo for the new "Power Play Points Club" offered at Holiday Twin Rinks and Leisure Rinks. The logo would be used on promotional stock, posters and used as the image in the loyalty card.

    The concept was to give an edgy feel, incorporate an ice surface and utilize some of the color seen within the rinks. Make the poster something to capture the eye of not only the players visiting the rinks but the parents as well.

    What was created is a reversed player in an ultimate action pose, on a grunge background. Power Play in bold with an icy texture and a reverse of the title points card to finish the message.

    The posters and rack cards supporting the promotion of the card were equally as grungy and included information on sign up details. The poster included a QR code to direct clients directly to the web signup page. The Rack card has a pre-printed application on the back for rink staff to fill in to gain participants easily.

    The logo is used in print and on the web as well as in promotional emails.

  • Gear Guard Inc.

    • July 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Michael Rambach

    The design brief included current power point materials as well as a promotional brochure that the owner created. Graphic Air was charged with developing a clean rack card to promote and inform for an upcoming hockey gear cleaning partner event at Holiday Twin Rinks and Leisure Rinks.

    What was created was a modern feel card that promoted the event on one side and explained the benefits on the reverse. I also modernized the logo with a gloss affect and subtle highlighting. The rack card utilizes Gear Guard's official red and blue colors and adds a clipped note design to it.

  • Grace Stumberg Band

    • September 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Michael Rambach

    Graphic Air is pleased to announce our latest client, the Grace Stumberg Band, Grace Stumberg is a creator of clearly accessible original music that grabs ears of all ages. Singing Buffalo’s “Good Feet” jingle and opening for bands such as Rusted Root, Hawksley Workman, and Haiku.

    Graphic Air Design inadvertently designed the new logo for the cover of Grace’s 2nd CD “Affect” released September 22nd, 2012 (Available on iTunes). After chatting with bassist of the band Mike Petrino, I got into a creative groove and started whipping up sample artwork that I felt visually represented the fun, folky and rock sound that I heard in her Music in Action benefit performance at the Sportsmans’ tavern back in February 2012. I designed a sample promotional poster for the CD release show, and didn’t know that at the time the band was seeking an image to represent the sound and attitude of the music. After hours of deliberating the band decided to “steal” the graphic I created for the poster to use on the CD cover. I of course was flattered, the work is paid for and the CD, autographed poster giveaways, various promotional items and stage banner all carry the developed image.

    Find Grace online at graceStumberg.com, follow her on Twitter, socialize on Facebook, watch her perform on her YouTube channel – oh and of course buy her music on iTunes
