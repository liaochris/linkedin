Davin "Hutch" Hutchins' Summary

Davin Hutchins is an award-winning journalist, manager, motivator, digital strategist, video producer and consultant specializing in the Middle East, international social issues, media training, social media, video and digital platforms.

Currently, Hutchins is Editorial Director at MBN Digital - the digital division of Middle East Broadcasting Networks which includes Alhurra and Radio Sawa, part of the the BBG family of international broadcasting. Before this, Hutchins was Managing Editor of Middle East Voices at Voice of America / BBG where he conceived of an Arab Spring social journalism site and supervised a team of multimedia reporters covering grass-roots democratization in the Middle East. The site won the Topical Reporting category in the 2012 Online Journalism Awards (ONA) beating CNN and the New York Times (Nate Silver's Political Calculus Blog).

Before VOA, Hutchins was the Director of IndiesLab at PBS & ITVS. In this role, Hutchins helped define PBS and ITVS strategies to help documentary work in the digital age. He worked with Independent Lens, iTunes, Amazon, Hulu, SnagFilms, Netflix to raise profile of independent documentary titles.

Hutchins has worked for and with CNN, CNN International, BBC World, Headline News, TechTV, Witness, Internews and Huffington Post. His projects and assignments have taken him across the Middle East and Africa.

In 2008-09, he was Managing Producer at American News Project in Washington, D.C. He produced investigative video reports on U.S. politics and the 2008 presidential elections which appeared on Huffington Post, Al Jazeera English and CNN. His video reports have tallied more than 2 million views.

Among career highlights, Hutchins directed The Art Of Flight in 2004-05, his feature documentary debuted at AFI Fest Hollywood, International Documentary Festival Amsterdam and Bangkok International Film Festival. He also trained journalists in the West Bank, Syria and Jordan in 2007-09.
