Jonathan Aizen's Summary

Nothing fascinates me more than the intersection of technology and society. The power of technology, and specifically the web, to shape our world has been a draw for me since I was a child. I first sat in front of a computer at the age of 18 months, learned to code when I was 8 years old, and built my first web company when I was 13. My mission is to positively transform the world around me through technology.

My recent work has focused on general management, corporate strategy, positioning, go-to-market planning, and product definition. In prior roles, I focused on evangelism and public speaking, sales, sales development, account management, marketing, and operations (legal, finance, etc.).

The team serves as the in-market subject matter experts on these complex offerings.

Founded, designed, implemented, and launched the Dapper service (http://www.dapper.net), which was acquired by Yahoo! in October 2010. Responsible for day-to-day operations of the company, evangelism, account management, human resources, and strategic partnerships.

Dapper (www.dapper.net) is an advertising technology platform with a mission to change the way that advertisers and agencies create, traffic and optimize dynamic display ads and the way that consumers interact with advertising content. Leveraging its core technology - built on machine learning, intent exploration, sophisticated matching algorithms, and real-time automated optimization - Dapper can deliver dynamically assembled, intent-driven display ads that drive increased relevance and performance.

The company is headquartered in San Francisco, CA with offices in New York and Tel Aviv. Dapper is a privately held company with funding from Accel Partners. For more information, please see our demo (http://bit.ly/cpKma) or subscribe to Dapper’s blog at blog.dapper.net.

Dapper was acquired by Yahoo! in October, 2010.
