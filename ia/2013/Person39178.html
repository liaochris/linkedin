Earon Davis' Summary

I am a generalist, with backgrounds in sustainability education, journalism, holistic health, integrative health practice and law. I have a broad, varied view, from years as a writer, teacher, award-winning consumer advocate, environmental health lawyer and consultant, nonprofit manager, integrative body worker and member of a large integrative medicine team.

My book project integrates much of what I've learned in my life and career. "Divine Primates: Hope for Our Stressed-Out Species" presents a unique view of human nature that allows new hope as we attempt to change our collective awareness in ways that foster sustainability. "Divine Primates" helps us sharpen our "human systems thinking" and "public anthropology" so that we can re-direct some of our culture's diffuse energies towards sustainability and peace.

GOAL: My current professional goals include finding a university faculty or administrative position related to interdisciplinary human sustainability studies and practices, getting some articles published and finding a publisher for my book.

---------------------------------------------

Specialties: Earon is a teacher of sustainability and resilience. He works with individuals, groups and organizations to promote value-driven strategic/systems analysis, based upon intercultural and partnership models of cooperation. Earon's work is informed by in-depth study of spirituality and wisdom traditions, experience in nonprofit management, environmental advocacy, university teaching and as an integrative health practitioner. The recommendations below provide a glimpse of Earon's effectiveness.

Bloomington Rotary Club

Educational Institution; 5001-10,000 employees; Higher Education industry

July 2013 – Present (3 months) Bloomington, Indiana Area

"Service Above Self" is the motto of Rotary. Rotary is an International service organization dedicated to friendship, peace and service to others. There is no creed and no secret handshake or secret membership requirements. Rotary is open to people from all backgrounds, faiths, creeds, ethnic groups, national origins, genders and gender identities and political views. ( I didn't mention race, because I don't believe that there is such a thing - just one race - humans.)

Rotary is a volunteer organization of over 1.2 million business and professional leaders united worldwide to provide humanitarian service and help build goodwill and peace. About 34,000 local Rotary clubs in more than 196 countries and geographical areas meet regularly for friendship, good will and service. They conduct projects to address today’s local challenges here and around the world — including illiteracy, disease, hunger, poverty, lack of clean water, and environmental concerns — while encouraging high ethical standards in all we do.

Most people are unaware of the vast array of service organizations and nonprofits that help those of us who lack resources to provide everything we need. Governments are not able to provide for all of the unmet need in this country or any other nation. People are helping people in countless ways, and this "service above self" is an important part of what makes us human. Liberals and conservatives, women and men, religious people and atheists all play important roles in Rotary. It is an organization through which everyone can come together and pitch in to help their communities and the world.
