Arrash Nekonam's Publications

  • Agent-Based Workflows for Pipeline Design

    • University of Aberdeen
    • September 1, 2007
    Authors: Arrash Nekonam

    In most organisations, human expertise is perceived as an essential resource and needs to be used efficiently. Existing workflows, in both the scientific and business domain, fail to maximise the potential of human expertise, simply due to the frequency of repetitive and monotonous tasks being carried out by domain experts. The detailed engineering process involved in the design of pipeline systems, is a key example of a scientific system which follows a stringent workflow in order to achieve a final resolution. Currently this process involves expert contributions from groups of engineers at each stage of the development process, often delegating particular design tasks to engineers with particular expertise in a certain aspect of pipeline engineering. The existing system is inefficient due to the fact that expert engineers waste valuable time on extensive low-level spreadsheet calculations, when they can assign that time to more critical high-level design decisions. Agent-Based Workflows introduces the concept of automating workflow enactment through the utilisation of intelligent agents in a multi-agent system. The key objective of the Agent-Based Workflow project is to propose an agent-based solution to the workflow problem described above. Given a standard workflow specification for a typical Pipeline Design process, consisting of both ‘workflow automation’ elements and ‘workflow management’ elements, the proposed solution will enact the workflow by utilising the combined capabilities of both human experts and intelligent software agents, in order to produce a final design outcome.

  • Visual Constraint Problem Solver

    • University of Aberdeen
    • June 1, 2006
    Authors: Arrash Nekonam

    "Constraint programming represents one of the closest approaches computer science has yet made to the Holy Grail of programming: the user states the problem, the computer solves it."

    - Eugene C. Freuder, 1997

    Constraint Satisfaction Problems (CSPs) are mathematical based problems which aim to find solutions that satisfy a certain number of constraints or criteria. CSPs aim to provide solutions for a variety of hard combinatorial problems such as Timetabling, Scheduling and Planning. Constraint programming has been successfully applied in numerous domains such as electrical engineering (locating faults), molecular biology (DNA sequencing), database systems (to ensure and/or restore consistency of data) and many more. The aim of this project will be to further enhance current CSP techniques by representing the constraint-based problem solving process as a high-level visual representation.
