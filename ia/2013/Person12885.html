Ben Gomes-Casseres' Summary

I specialize in alliance strategy and management, based on 25 years of research, teaching, and consulting on the topic.

As a professor at Brandeis International Business School, I lead the MBA program and direct the Asper Center for Global Entrepreneurship. Previously, I was a professor at the Harvard Business School and an Economist at the World Bank. My book "The Alliance Revolution" pioneered the concept of alliance constellations; my co-authored book "Mastering Alliance Strategy" gives advice on all aspects of alliance management. I'm working on a third book, to be published by Harvard Business Press on strategies to leverage resources external to the firm. My work has appeared in Harvard Business Review, Financial Times, Strategy & Business, Sloan Management Review, and in academic journals, and has been cited in The New York Times, Wall Street Journal, Forbes, and Associated Press. Most of my writings are available at www.alliancestrategy.com.

Through Alliance Strategy Consulting, I offer speeches, workshops, and focused consulting on all aspects of alliance and joint venture management. Recent clients have included General Electric, Chevron, Yahoo, Johnson Controls, BASF, Weyerhaeuser, Daimler, Battelle, and NASA.

At Harvard, Princeton, and Brandeis I received degrees in business, economics, history. A native of Curacao, I speak four languages.
