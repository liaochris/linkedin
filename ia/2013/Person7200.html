Andrew Simes' Summary

Andrew has over twenty years experience successfully delivering strategic business and technology programmes and projects in a wide range of industry sectors. Over the last fifteen years, he has furthered his management experience and capability through providing management consulting advice and skills to the executive and senior management of a number of New Zealand, Australia, and China’s foremost organisations.

As a programme and project manager, Andrew brings a strong, persuasive yet resilient management style to ensure that the portfolio of projects he is involved with are focused upon delivering their defined benefits within their stated constraints. As a management consultant, Andrew is purposeful in managing and delivering to the expectations of his clients. He is an experienced people manager, effective at leading and managing teams to succeed within challenging environments and cultures.

Andrew has a Master of Project Management (MPM) degree from the University of Technology, Sydney and has been independently accredited as an Information Technology Certified Professional (ITCP) by NZCS, the professional body of the IT sector in New Zealand. He holds MSP Foundation Practitioner and PRINCE2 Practitioner certificates and has additionally been trained in, and worked with, a number of formal programme, project and risk management methodologies including SCRUM, TCM, PMM, ITPM, SIIPS, P-Plus, and PRISM. Andrew’s Myers-Briggs type indicator is ENTP.

Andrew's experience includes technology solution delivery approach exposure (including and not limited) to:
• Agile software development approaches including Scrum-based, XP and; Peer Programming;
• Agile delivery approaches including User Story, FDD, TDD, and Continuous Integration
• Non-Agile delivery approaches including RUP, Waterfall, Off-shoring and vendor delivery management
• Solutions integration partnering
• Lead integrator for partner to partner relationships
• Supplier for customer solutions

Specialties

Complex Project Management, Technology Programme Management, IS/IT Strategic Planning, Technology Management Consulting, Economic Analysis, Risk Management, Mentoring & Coaching, Systems & Solutions Integration, Enterprise Architecture, Business Continuity Planning, Disaster Recovery Planning

Andrew Simes' Publications

  • Project Initiation Definition Processes “Starting right, right from the start”

    • University of Technology, Sydney, Australia
    • September 1, 2002
    Authors: Andrew Simes

    Abstract – Project failures have the potential to create a negative impact in the public’s eye on the profile of project management and the profession of the project manager. For Project Management to gain and maintain acceptance by the public as an integral part of business and commerce organisations need to mitigate the likelihood for problems to occur and reoccur. Common themes that appear in reviews and post-mortems of projects that have been perceived by the layman as failing often relate to a lack of consistency and rigour in the processes employed at the project’s initiation. The focus of this dissertation is upon providing insights into the initiation phase definition processes used by a number of significant New Zealand organisations within their methodologies for managing projects. By sharing these insights there is an opportunity for gains in the consistency of project success to be made through seeking to better understand projects, specifically their management, outcomes, processes and the environment within which they execute.
