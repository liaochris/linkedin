Provided the wind resource is adequate, before a wind turbine can be erected, they must be sited correctly—this is especially critical in the built and/or urban environment.

  • Installing Photovoltaics on California K-12 Schools [Abstract]

    • Rahus Institute
    • March 2005
    Authors: Kimberly L. King

    Solar contractors, experienced in installing residential and commercial systems, experienced frustration with the unfamiliar California Division of the State Architect (DSA) process when trying to install photovoltaic (PV) systems at schools—leading to very few installations of school solar systems, despite a very active solar market in California.

    To ease this frustration and to help facilitate more installations, a simplified procedural design guidelines and approaches was composed for parties interested in purchasing and installing photovoltaic systems at California schools. Utilizing site examples, this paper illustrated the DSA process for four different approaches: New Construction, Pre-Check (PC), Exempt and Retrofit systems.
