Systems Specialist

Michigan State Police

Government Agency; 1001-5000 employees; Law Enforcement industry

August 2012 – May 2013 (10 months) Lansing, Michigan Area

I specialize in managing the operations of the Michigan Law Enforcement Information Network (LEIN) message switch and serve as the primary point of contact for agencies requesting access to the network or encountering system-level issues that cannot be resolved by the helpdesk. I also work closely with the Information Security Officer to review proposed solutions from local agencies for connecting to the LEIN or State Network, and determine compliance with state and federal policies.

LEIN is a legislatively mandated system used statewide by criminal justice agencies (such as police, courts, prosecutors, and corrections) to share information between themselves and state/federal systems (such as the National Crime Information Center, the Michigan Department of State, and Nlets). LEIN also contains the state “hotfile” databases, which stores information on wanted people, missing people, abandoned/impounded/stolen vehicles, personal protection orders, and mental orders.

LEIN has approximately 30,000 users statewide, an average response time of 300ms for query and hotfile transactions, and maintains a minimum monthly uptime of 99%. Over one billion transactions are sent thru the LEIN system annually.

Project Manager

Michigan State Police

Government Agency; 1001-5000 employees; Law Enforcement industry

May 2011 – August 2012 (1 year 4 months) Lansing, Michigan Area

As a Project Manager in the Office of the Director, my primary function was to apply project management techniques to ensure completion of agency-wide projects assigned by the Director. I also monitored significant projects across the agency to help ensure success.

Governor Snyder placed a significant emphasis on using data to drive decisions. I assisted on a number of projects related to the use of data and technology to improve processes, including:

* Participating on the Regional Policing Plan, which consolidated the number of physical worksites statewide from 62 to 29, and moved enlisted members out of offices and onto the road through the implementation of new technology in the patrol cars.

* The implementation and collection of executive level metrics to measure the health of key agency functions.

* Co-Chairing a subcommittee to implement the Data Driven Approaches to Crime and Traffic Safety (DDACTS) model in Michigan’s four most dangerous cities, to advise enlisted members on where the data shows the highest levels of activity and ideal locations for deploying resources.

As part of the Regional Policing Plan and post consolidations, more than half of the enlisted members of the agency requested transfers to different worksites. In the past, the transfer process was done by submitting a transfer request in writing, determining the seniority of each transfer to a given worksite, and then making a selection based on union rules. I developed a website to automate this process, which reduced process time for the Human Resources Division from an estimated six months to less than one month.

I frequently worked with internal stakeholders and the Department of Technology, Management and Budget (DTMB) to prioritize the agency’s information technology needs and requests.

LEIN Operations Analyst

Michigan State Police

Government Agency; 1001-5000 employees; Law Enforcement industry

September 2006 – May 2011 (4 years 9 months) Lansing, Michigan Area

As Operations Analyst, I was responsible for the day-to-day technical operations of the Law Enforcement Information Network (LEIN). I managed station and agency access controls, monitored system performance, and initiated/coordinated technical changes to the system. I also served as a liaison between other state departments and our federal partners.

In 2006-2007, the LEIN hotfiles and the logic for processing LEIN transactions, were moved from the Unisys Clearpath Mainframe environment to a new CPI OpenFox message switch. This required extensive testing between the old system and the new system (and review of thousands of lines of ALGOL code developed over the prior 40 years) to ensure that all transactions types would accept the same input and return the exact same responses as the system being replaced.

IT Manager

Container and Pallet Services

March 2006 – September 2006 (7 months) Livonia, Michigan

As the Information Technology Manager at a “startup” company, I was responsible for all information technology systems and software at the main office and all the software and hardware associated with the real-time tracking of over 100,000 containers/pallets located at depot and customer locations. I also managed a team of contract developers tasked with modernizing the CAPSTRAC container tracking software.

Programmer / Analyst

Michigan Department of Information Technology

Government Agency; 10,001+ employees; Government Administration industry

February 2005 – March 2006 (1 year 2 months) East Lansing, MI

As a Programmer/Analyst with the Department of Information Technology, I worked on a number of projects for the Michigan State Police. My primary project was the re-write of the Personal Protection Order (PPO) "Update", "Respondent Notification" and "File Transfer" programs in Java, and extracting the associated data to import into Oracle. These programs were originally written in ALGOL, running on a UNISYS ClearPath Mainframe.

I also assisted in the deployment of the Mobile LEIN pilot and the development of the ISERVICES Gateway. The Mobile LEIN application allowed police officers access to criminal justice information for detectives and undercover officers from their BlackBerry devices. The ISERVICES Gateway project connected disparate law enforcement information systems across the state, to allow criminal justice users a single point of access to any or all connected systems in a single query.

Contrator

Michigan State Police (via Kelly Services)

Government Agency; 1001-5000 employees; Law Enforcement industry

August 2004 – February 2005 (7 months) Lansing, MI

In 2004, the Michigan State Police was under a federal deadline to implement an encryption solution between local criminal justice agencies and the State of Michigan network for any network traffic containing federal criminal justice information.

I was contracted to assist their Information Security Officer in reviewing network diagrams for compliance with the federal encryption policies and to walk approximately 400 criminal justice agencies through the process of purchasing the approved encryption solution, installing the software, and configuring their server to utilize the encryption software.
