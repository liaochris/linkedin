Senior Member of Technical Staff

Sandia National Laboratories

Government Agency; 5001-10,000 employees; Defense & Space industry

July 2002 – October 2008 (6 years 4 months) Albuquerque, New Mexico

Sandia National Laboratories is a multi-program laboratory managed by Lockheed Martin for the US Department of Energy. Its vision is to "become the nation's premier science and engineering laboratory for national security and technology innovation" (source: Sandia.gov). I gained knowledge in two different areas: Flight Avionics & Sensor Subsystems from Feb 2007 to Oct 2008 and Neutron Generator Manufacturing from July 2002 to Jan 2007.

In Flight Avionics & Sensor Subsystems, my work included basic research, production agency liaisonship, environmental (radiation, acceleration, vibration) testing, and Matlab programming for telemetry data analysis. My key accomplishment was recognition from the US Department of Energy for exceptional teamwork on a weapons recertification program.

In Neutron Generator Manufacturing, my work included systems design, project management with emphasis on lean manufacturing, high-voltage circuit design, user experience, and VB.Net & LabVIEW programming. My key accomplishments were completing a Master's Certificate in Project Management from The George Washington University and being the first in the department to obtain greenbelt certification in the Lean Six Sigma corporate initiative.

I left Sandia Labs to be with family in Ohio.

My work included project management, high-speed PCB design, and VHDL coding for FPGA implementations. My key accomplishments were recognition by getting progressively larger project roles, from tester to designer to technical lead, and acknowledgement for an outstanding effort in supporting a customer's urgent capability need.

I left Appsig to pursue full time a master's program in electrical engineering at UCLA.
