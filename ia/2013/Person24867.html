Christopher Hurtado's Summary

Christopher Hurtado is a self-taught entrepreneur with a passion for technology, languages, learning, and teaching. He has more than 22 years of experience in translation, foreign language instruction and cross-cultural training. He has taught English as a second language (ESL) as well as Spanish and Arabic for English speakers. He is coauthor of Vacation Spanish: A Survival Guide for Mexico, the Caribbean, Central & South America (including a Brazilian Portuguese chapter). He earned a BA in Middle East Studies/Arabic with a second major in Philosophy at Brigham Young University (2010) and an MA in Nonproliferation and Terrorism Studies at the Monterey Institute of International Studies (2012).

Since founding Linguistic Solutions in 1991, Hurtado has been using high tech/high touch methodologies to manage customer relationships, translation projects and a worldwide network of freelance translators and translation managers while serving a variety of international clients, including Fortune 500 companies and high-tech startups. Linguistic Solutions breaks down language and cultural barriers through translation, interpretation, foreign language instruction and cross-cultural training.

Hurtado was born in Baltimore, Maryland. He grew up in Venezuela, his father’s native country. He’s a childhood bilingual (English and Spanish) and also speaks Brazilian Portuguese, French and Arabic. He has lived in the United States, Venezuela, Jordan, and Syria. He has traveled extensively in the United States, Venezuela, Egypt, Jordan, Israel and the Palestinian Territories, and Syria and less extensively in Mexico, Puerto Rico, Colombia, and Canada. He is married to children’s book author Alysia Gonzalez. They have nine children and are active in their church and community.

  • “U.S. Hispanic consumer e-commerce preferences: expectations and attitudes toward web content”

    • Journal of Electronic Commerce Research
    • May 2008
    Authors: Christopher Hurtado, Nitish Singh, Daniel W. Baack, Sumit K. Kundu

    ABSTRACT

    The U.S. Hispanic Internet user is an emerging and relatively unexplored consumer segment in the marketing literature. In this study an attempt is made to gain phenomenological insights into the web site content expectations of U.S. Hispanic consumers. The study uses the focus group method to generate an experiential account of U.S. Hispanic consumers' web design expectations. The themes and insights generated from focus groups then form the basis for further ideation using the brainwriting technique. The findings from the study will help web marketers to develop U.S. Hispanic-centric web sites that speak to the U.S. Hispanic market.

    Keywords: U.S. Hispanic consumer behavior, online consumer behavior, e-commerce, culture, adaptation
