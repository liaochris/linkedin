Michael Richardson's Summary

This innovative technology executive actively assists growth-stage product companies as a strong contributor in developing and delivering market-leading enterprise software solutions. A proven leader with a track record of helping businesses expand through his experience in effectively managing engineering teams, leading technical product strategies, establishing pivotal alliance and sourcing relationships, while also attaining aggressive business goals under the most challenging time and resource constraints. Michael is a highly ethical professional, and a motivational leader with a rare balance of applied technological vision and insightful business judgment; delivering practical strategic guidance and hands-on solution architecture.

Specialties

Michael brings over 25 years of experience in a variety of senior management and entrepreneurial positions with leading-edge technology companies. He holds 19 US patents with others pending, and maintains an interest in many technologies and emerging industries.

Michael received dual BSME./CSci in Mechanical Engineering & Computer Science from the U of MN, Institute of Technology.

In addition, Michael holds Dual USA + EU/French Citizenship and enjoys extensive global travel.

  • Open API’s for secure social enterprise computing drive innovation

    • ZD Net: from Presentation at 2009 RSA Conference
    • April 23, 2009
    Authors: Michael Richardson, Chet Kapor

    Guest editorial by Chet Kapoor and Michael Richardson

    As the “Chief Innovation Officer” now for most enterprises, the CIO needs a new portfolio of social computing capable methodologies to enable collaboration between empowered users inside and adjacent to the organization. Social networking is an obvious way for people to connect and collaborate outside of work, but what happens when companies harness this social computing inside company walls to foster collaboration and innovation? The possibilities are astounding when you consider the power that personal connections have in finding employees’ skill sets and matching them to where they are needed within the organization. Move social networking inside and across a corporation and you’ll be able to encourage more relationship building and collaboration to organically happen where it wouldn’t otherwise. This is the key to enterprise 2.0 adoption throughout an organization.

  • Member Profile: Michael M. Richardson, SMART System Technologies

    • Smart Card Alliance
    • June 2005
    Authors: Michael Richardson

    What obstacles to growth do you see that must be overcome to capitalize on these new opportunities?

    Consumer and retailer education and adoption are probably the biggest obstacles all smart card-related technology companies face in North America. However, in Europe and Asia we have seen and continue to see ever-growing customer acceptance. We have to continue to educate retailers and consumers on the respective value propositions we bring to them through the effective application of contactless smart card technology so that they feel motivated and secure in adopting it. European and other trends also continue to generate relevant proof-points and learning for the North American market.
