Director of Digital Initiatives

The Harvard Common Press

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Publishing industry

January 2010 – February 2011 (1 year 2 months)

My role as Director of Digital Initiatives involves overseeing and advancing the digital goals of The Harvard Common Press. These initiatives extend across a variety of media and platforms to include development of ebooks (including associated video, audio and ancillary media content); in conjunction with the Production department, the acquisition of a content management system and development of an XML workflow that facilitates conversion to ebook formats as well as chunking of content; development of partnerships across both online and mobile platforms for the purpose of content licensing and digital distribution; as well as management of in-house systems necessary for the tracking and dissemination of information across departments. The goal of the position is not only to setup these various initiatives, but also ensure their long-term success by implementing sustainable best practices across departments that anticipate, adapt to and capitalize on changing digital conditions.

    • February 2013 to Present

    • February 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Adam Salomone

    The Roger Smith Cookbook Conference 2012 was the first-of-its-kind event to explore the past, present and future of cookbook and recipe content. Bringing together 24 panels on a variety of topics, including enhanced ebooks, app development, cultural and historical influences in cookbooks, social media best practices and more, the conference allowed for diverse and engaging debate on the question of where recipe content has been and where it's headed. The conference sold out with over 250 attendees, and including culinary luminaries such as Barbara Haber, Grace Young, Molly O'Neill, Amanda Hesser, and more.
