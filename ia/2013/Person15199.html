  • EMR Thesis

    • August 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Boaz Gurdin

    ABSTRACT
    How do primary care physicians follow up on health issues at visits?
    Patient history in medical records plays an important role connecting visits.

    MOTIVATION
    Primary care physicians provide care for patients, and patients keep coming back. How do they pick up where they left off at the last visit? Following up on patients' on-going health issues is part of what the medical community calls "continuity of care." Doctors have too many patients with too many health issues to remember every detail, so medical records play an important supporting role in continuity of care, documenting the history of patients' health issues for future follow-up. By improving medical records, we can increase continuity of care, leading to better healthcare outcomes.

    RESEARCH QUESTIONS
    * How can electronic medical records help primary care physicians provide more efficient and effective continuity of care?
    * What are their information needs?
    * What are their current practices for reading and writing the information?

    METHOD
    * Ethnographic observation of 6 primary care physicians, 68 patient-visits, 50+ hours at clinics
    * Grounded theory analysis of fieldnotes to inductively discover patterns in work practices and information usage
    * Analysis of information needs, breakdowns due to information fragmentation, and design implications
