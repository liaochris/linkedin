Johann van der Merwe's Summary

Johann van der Merwe is Group Head of Information Security for De Beers. Johann is responsible for leading information security and reports to the Group CIO. He is tasked with establishing and maintaining the enterprise information security vision, strategy and programme to ensure information and technology assets are adequately protected.

Johann's experience includes information security governance, management and technical information security consulting in complex business and high risk environments. This includes projects for major organisations spanning the financial services, telecommunications, mining, retail and government sectors. Johann has advised organisations on information security matters in countries such as the UK, Canada, Sudan, Botswana, Namibia, Cote-d'Ivoire, Nigeria, Zambia, Uganda, Mauritius and South Africa.

Johann’s industry involvement includes leading South Africa at the ISO meetings on information security management. Lately, he has been involved in major revisions of the two main information security standards, namely ISO 27001 and ISO 27002. He is also co-editor of the revision now being undertaken on ISO 27003 "Information security management system implementation guidance".

Johann is a regular public speaker on emerging information security topics and security in the context of IT Governance, Enterprise Risk Management and Corporate Governance.

Prior to joining De Beers he worked as a Senior Manager in the Advisory division of PricewaterhouseCoopers (PwC) and as a Manager in the Security & Privacy Services team of Deloitte.

Johann received his Bachelor of Science, Master of Science and PhD degrees in Electronic Engineering (specialising in information security) from the University of KwaZulu-Natal.

Johann holds the CISM and CISA certifications from ISACA.

Specialties: Information security governance, information security management and technical information security consulting in complex business and high risk environments.
