    • Real Estate Law Journal (Thomson Reuters)

    Authors: Matthew Kirsch

    41 Real Est. L.J. 4 (2012). This Note will argue that a change in banking activity restrictions and incentives will prevent the further collapse of housing markets, ease neighborhood-level problems associated with vacant properties, and provide banks with the chance to recover a larger return from the sale of REO properties.3 Specifically, this Note will advocate a three-pronged proposal in which 1) capital-adequacy requirements are modified to reduce banks' incentive to rapidly sell REO property; 2) the Comptroller of the Currency (the “OCC”) allows banks to establish REO holding companies to manage, lease and eventually sell REO inventories at responsible prices; and 3) newly established REO holding companies are permitted to apply for a blanket extension of the five-year holding limitation proscribed by 12 U.S.C § 29.
