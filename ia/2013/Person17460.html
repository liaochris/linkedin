Brian Duggan's Summary

I have been in the Building Industry 38 years, starting as a Carpenter, I went to Darwin after Cyclone Tracy in 1975 and after working there for a couple of years, went to Jabiru, in Arnham Land, in the Northern Territory, where I worked as a carpenter on the Ranger Uranium Mine. After that I moved to Perth and subbied as a Roof Framing Carpenter. It was now when I obtained my Western Australian Builders License. I then became a Building Supervisor for Taylor Woodrow building forty houses in a lot for the Navy in Rockingham, just South of Perth. After completing 4 x 40 lots of homes, I was promoted to Project Manager for Taylor Woodrow Group, where in 1986 I built 500 houses, Shopping Centre, School, Police and Ambulance Stations in Karratha, WA, for BHP, Woodside Petroleum and KJR. I successfully brought this Contract in on time, and under budget. To hot up there for me and left back to Perth, where it was the lead up to the Americas Cup Yaucht Race, and I renovated quite a few hotels in Freemantle, Perth and Mount Hawthorn. Family brought me to Queensland and I settled on the Gold Coast in 1989, Started my Building Company and after a good while, I went back to study with MBA and gained my Certificate 1V Diploma in Building. Now I am Renovating Houses and love it. I am also a Member of Master Builders. htttp/www.bjconstruct.com.au
