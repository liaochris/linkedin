Mark Hannah's Summary

Mark Hannah is a doctoral student at the University of Southern California's Annenberg School for Communication, where he studies emerging information and communication technologies and their impact on political knowledge, opinion and behavior.

Mark previously studied at the University of Pennsylvania (B.A.) and Columbia University (M.S.). He is a contributor to PBS’s MediaShift blog, The Huffington Post and Politico's "The Arena," and appears as a political analyst on CNBC, Fox News and Bloomberg.

He is an adjunct professor in the media studies department of The New School and an advisor to #Waywire, the social media site founded by Cory Booker.

With ten years as a communications practitioner, he's conducted sensitive public affairs campaigns for many well-known clients, including: AstraZeneca, MetLife, the Monsanto Company, Anheuser-Busch, Hilton Hotels, the NCAA, New York Presbyterian Hospital, Dannon, the American Petroleum Institute, and Yale University. He most recently served as the Director of Academic Communications at Parsons, one of the world's oldest and most respected colleges of art and design.

A veteran of the Kerry-Edwards presidential campaign, his on-site advance work included the presidential announcement event, the Democratic National Convention, two of the three presidential debate preps and the senator’s residence on election night. He's more recently conducted advance work for the Obama for America campaign, Presidential Inaugural Committee and The White House.

Mark serves on the Board of Directors of the National Association for Media Literacy Education, is a member of the Public Relations Society of America, and has been a research fellow at the Society for New Communications Research. He served as an awards judge for these three organizations. As a former researcher at the Annenberg Public Policy Center, Mark analyzed the network news treatment of terrorist threats immediately following the 9/11 attacks.

Specialties

Issues and reputation management, crisis communications, political messaging, online communications, and large-scale event production
