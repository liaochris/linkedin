Bjorn Bertoft's Summary

CREATING IDEAS FOR GOOD REASONS.
I have always worked with the creation of ideas and brands. Nowadays I am focusing on projects based on a sincere ambition to change things for the better. Helping out to make them successful is my way to make the world a better place.

CROSSING BORDERS, LEARNING MORE.
In 2003 I grabbed a chance to work for the iconic pop singer Wei Wei in China. It turned into a role as an international manager with a global brand assignment in the field of music. I was given the opportunity to dive into another corner of the world. An ocean full of challenges and cultural differences. Insights and experiences I simply couldn't have found elsewhere. For that I am deeply grateful.

Still learning and still having the fortunate opportunity to travel the world, my days offer endless challenges while crossing borders, time zones and bureaucratic formats. I see things that keep me motivated to focus on matters that make sense on a global scale: To work with projects, brands or phenomenon with an ambition to correct any of the mishaps that we – the humans – have created on planet earth. May it be to develop education, peace, health, fair play, cultural bridges or environmentally sustainable solutions.

SCALING DOWN ON STUFF. SCALING UP ON ACTION.
I have lived my fair share of life in the developed world, collecting stuff end experiences. I am spending the rest of it scaling down to the needs of a nomad, in order to get more things done. The less-is-more lifestyle generates independence, space and time. Allowing me to move around and work harder with people, businesses and projects that make global sense first of all, and money as a necessary number two – an order of priorities I believe will be the life line for most businesses very soon.

Thank you for the attention.

Bjorn Bertoft's Experience

Creative Manager

bertoft.com

January 2010 – Present (3 years 8 months)

CREATIVITY OUTSIDE THE BOX. AND BORDERS.
Who are we in one corner of the world to say that we know the answers, if we do not push ourselves to even try to understand and learn from the mindsets of others? How can “think outside the box” be creative enough if we do not even dare to think outside our own cultural- and linguistic borders?

Diving into China, closely connected to a national icon, teaches me new things every day. Over time it has opened up an understanding for the importance of global perspectives. Leading into border crossing assignments and projects in other fields:

NON-VIOLENCE, EDUCATION, PUBLIC HEALTH, ANTI-DOPING, CULTURAL BRIDGES, PLANTING FRUIT TREES, GREENTECH SOLUTIONS…

These experiences has contributed to the development of a new business direction. These projects will always need helping hands in terms of marketing- and brand management. These causes makes more sense than many others to work with, and for. These projects deserves to be successful.

These kind of issues motivates me to roll up my sleeves.

Owner/Manager

Wei Wei International Management

2004 – Present (9 years)

WEI WEI AND CHINA – A CROSS CULTURAL ADVENTURE
Wei Wei became a Chinese superstar already in 1984. She closed the Beijing 2008 Summer Olympics and opened the Shanghai 2010 World Expo with her voice and spirit. These two moments were historical milestones for her and for China. And for me, to say the least.

Being a national icon in China with a foot in the West, makes Wei Wei a natural link between the two. It was upon that simple fact we started to build a global brand back in 2004.

ASSIGNMENT: International management of the Chinese pop vocalist Wei Wei, her brand and music worldwide, in trust with her local management in Beijing. Using these cross cultural experiences to develop creative brand solutions with the tools of marketing-, music- and brand management. Aside from Wei Wei's scheduled touring in China and in the rest of the world, and continous song production, a number of musical productions, campaigns and events were created:

- The book was produced
- The 20X20 albums were created, recorded, published
- The FIFA Women World Cup campaign, song and video in collaboration with FC Barcelona's Messi, Deco, Puyol and Marquez and EMI.
- The IAAF World Championship/DN Galan theme song
- The Beijing 2008 Sailing City Qingdao theme song
- An Olympic song and campaign for Beijing 2008 Olympics
- A mobile platform marketing partnership with Ericson

Bjorn Bertoft's Projects

  • The War On Doping

    • December 2008 to Present
    Team Members: Bjorn Bertoft, Mats Omne

    Initiated and produced the independent documentary film about the story and dilemmas of doping in sports, featuring the world's foremost doping hunter Professor Arne Ljungqvist. Covering the big names, the big scandals and the sensitive aspects.
    Dec 2008 Meeting Professor Ljungqvist
    May 2009 Starting filming
    April 2012 Distribution Agreement Mercury Media, London
    June 2012 Avant Premiere at UNESCO in Paris
    July 2012 Screening at London 2012 Olympics
    Sept 2012 Sold in Sweden, Finland, Russia, Poland, Latvia, Japan, China, USA (PPV)
    Nov 2012 Awarded by FICTS – Fédération Internationale Cinéma Télévision Sportifs

  • Non-Violence Project / IOBFFP

    • February 2010 to Present
    Team Members: Bjorn Bertoft, Rolf Skjoldebrand, Jan Hellman, Ivana Busljeta, Janjarang Pat Kijtikhun, Fanny Davidsson, Leslye Lacroix, Therese Engström, Eddy Balina, Mauricio Moralez, Markus Schruf

    Initiated the global call Imagine One Billion Faces For Peace as an incorporated online campaign connected to the educational outreach program being developed for the Non-Violence Project Foundation. The Non-Violence Project Foundation is a non-profit organization with the mission to inspire, motivate and engage young people to understand how to solve conflicts peacefully. The NVP has educated more than 5 million young people, teachers and sports coaches around the world and the objective is to reach out to 100 million young people via local educational activities connected to the global call for peace and non-violence Imagine One Billon Faces For Peace – IOBFFP. The NVP symbol, the iconic knotted gun – Non Violence - was originally created as a memorial tribute to John Lennon after he was shot and killed. One of the original Non-Violence sculptures is displayed outside of the United Nations Headquarters in New York City. Sir Paul McCartney, Lionel Messi and Yoko Ono are, among others, ambassadors of the Non-Violence Project and IOBFFP. Join the global peace movement on www.nonviolence.com and www.iobffp.com.

  • Clean Sports Symbol

    • March 2011 to Present
    Team Members: Bjorn Bertoft, Sassona Norton

    Initiating a symbol for clean sport in order to gather the movement for health and fair play around the world as a gesture to honor the world's foremost anti-doping advocates, Professor Arne Ljungqvist.The symbol is developed together with the sculptor Sassona Norton (the 9/11 Memorial sculpture) and is a registered trademark. The Clean Sport symbol was unveiled at the 80 year celebration reception for Professor Arne Ljungqvist, in Stockholm, May 2011. Wanna support, drop an mail: bjorn@bertoft.com
