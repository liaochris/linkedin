Leah Nichaman's Summary

I founded Everyday Money Management in 2006 to bring peace of mind to individuals and families by helping them keep their financial lives in order. As the Outreach Coordinator at the National Alliance on Mental Illness of Montgomery County, I knew first-hand that people with mental illness struggle with money. Once I launched Everyday Money Management, I found that others, including seniors and people with physical disabilities, needed assistance as well. My vision for my clients is that they will be able to maintain their independence and achieve their life goals through the organization and control of their personal finances.

As a personal bookkeeper, it’s a pleasure to work with seniors and people with disabilities to keep them organized, feeling in control, and safe from scammers and identity thieves. Our clients and their families have less stress because my dedicated staff of daily money managers efficiently manages mail, bills, and financial data. Clients can make better decisions about their money with accurate financial information, and family members are relieved to know that a professional is managing the finances. Not to mention that tax time is easier than ever!

I am a certified Professional Daily Money Manager (PDMM) through the American Association of Daily Money Managers and serve on the board of directors. I serve as a bill paying volunteer and sit on the Advisory Committee for the Representative Payee Program of Montgomery County.

Specialties

Personal financial management including budgeting, cash flow analysis, and Quicken.
File organization (paper and computer files).
Working with individuals with memory loss, dementia, mental illness, other disabilities.

      Representative Payee Program, Mental Health Assoc. of Montgomery County
      • Social Services

      I volunteer to pay bills for a low income senior citizen with mental illness who lives in a group home. I manage his Social Security benefit in accordance with guidelines from the Social Security Administration. Once his rent, food and medicine are paid for, I give him the rest of his benefit as spending money. This helps him maintain stability in housing and health. It is an honor to work with him.
