Daniel Harrison's Experience

Channel Producer

Holiday & Cruise Channel

Public Company; 1-10 employees; Entertainment industry

June 2011 – Present (2 years 4 months) Liverpool, United Kingdom

Sole Producer of a lifestyle channel broadcasting on Sky Channel 284 – 6am to 10pm.

Since launch, I've been responsible for outputting all in-house content, a mix of pre-recorded & live editorial and teleshopping programming, making up at least 12 hours of the channels daily schedule.

Sourced equipment and built the channels multi-camera studio as well as designed studio sets.

Devised a number of studio based programmes, developing them from my own initial concept to going on air including The Real Love Boat, The John Scott Show, Inside Cruise & The Travel Book Club.

A gallery multitasker, my role involves producing, directing, audio mixing, operating VT's, graphics & autocue as well as liasing with the channels playout provider in and out of any live broadcasts.

Producer

DM Digital TV

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Broadcast Media industry

November 2007 – February 2011 (3 years 4 months) Manchester, United Kingdom

Working for the UK’s leading British Asian television channel, I headed up the English programming department which initially comprised of news output only. With my ambitions and ideas, this was expanded to include a live magazine programme which saw the team growing from 3 to more than 10 employees.

On a daily basis, I was responsible for setting the news agenda, sourcing studio guests, creating themed segments & shows, developing regular lifestyle features with guest experts, seek sponsorship for competition prizes plus write scripts and brief presenters.

During live broadcasts, duties included technical directing (camera, audio, auto cue, graphics and VT) as well as gallery producing to ensure running orders were kept up-to-date with any latest developments or breaking news stories whilst always ensuring that all content remained fair, impartial and unbiased as well as adhering to Ofcom regulations.

Production Assistant / Technical Operator

Price Crash TV

June 2003 – February 2007 (3 years 9 months) Manchester, United Kingdom

Initially employed as assistant to the Managing Director with duties including scheduling and planning of what was a pre-recorded shopping channel.

During 2004, helped plan the introduction of live broadcasting which once launched, saw me move into production as Production Assistant where my duties were split covering either the Assistant Director or Assistant Floor Manager.

In 2006, after undertaking an intensive training course, I was promoted to Technical Operator. This role involved rotating between three key roles in the studio and gallery – camera, sound and racks operator.
