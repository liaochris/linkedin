Cari Weston, CPA, MST's Summary

Carianne (Cari) Weston has over 18 years experience in tax services, accounting services and small business consulting. Her experience includes a broad range of industries including architecture, computer services, engineering, legal, medical, non-profit, professional services, real estate, and many others.

Ms. Weston worked full time as a bookkeeper and tax preparer while earning a Bachelor of Science in Accounting. She went on further to earn her Masters of Taxation while working full time for Ernst & Young in Phoenix, Arizona. Both degrees were obtained from the W.P. Carey School of Business at Arizona State University.

Cari has extensive experience in working with both individuals and businesses on tax planning, tax return preparation, and IRS representation. In addition, she works with small business owners on their accounting system implementation, analyzing and reviewing financial results, budgeting and forecasting, cash flow management, internal controls, staff training, and general accounting and tax advisory services. She is a QuickBooks Pro Advisor and has worked with the program for close to 20 years. While most of Ms. Weston's career has been in public accounting, she spent two years working on the client side as an accounting manager for a large publicly traded company.

Cari is licensed as a Certified Public Accountant in the States of Arizona and Texas. She is a member of the American Institute of Certified Public Accountants and the Texas Society of Certified Public Accountants.

Her interests include spending time with her husband and three children, church, gardening, home projects, and community activities such as being the Treasurer of the Canyon Creek Elementary School PTA and a YMCA volleyball coach.

    Tax Practice Management Committee
    • November 2011 to Present

    The Tax Practice Management Committee develops products and services for AICPA Tax Section members that improve the quality, efficiency and profitability of members’ tax practices, and help members maintain high practice standards by:

  • IRS Issue Management Resolution System (IMRS)

    Liaison for the TSCPA
    • October 2012 to Present

    One of two CPAs in the State of Texas appointed to serve as a liason on the monthly calls with the IRS Stakeholder Liason office for the Southwest. Works directly with the Stakeholder Liasons to identify and solve systemic issues within the IRS.

    The IRS Communications and Stakeholder Outreach function has established the Issue Management Resolution System, a streamlined and structured process that facilitates stakeholder issue identification, resolution and feedback.

    IMRS captures, develops and responds to significant national and local stakeholder issues. When stakeholder organizations notify the IRS of concerns about IRS policies, practices and procedures, analysts research and respond to the issues.

    IMRS also identifies nationwide trends in the reporting, filing and paying requirements that may necessitate changes to IRS processes and procedures. Progress on stakeholder issues is closely monitored to assure proper response and communication to the initiating stakeholder and all impacted stakeholders.
