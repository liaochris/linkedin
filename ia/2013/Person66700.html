John Metta's Summary

Starting at childhood, I was a programmer. I later became a cultural anthropologist, and then later, a Hydrologist and mathematical modeler. I've spent the past 20 years at the junction of software development, human culture/policy, and mathematics. I've spent that entire time wondering where it will lead me.

Now I know.

There's never been a more magical time to be a software developer. The tools available now allow us to do simply amazing things. From Python to Scala to JQuery to Redis, the tools we have are a dream.

At the same time, we are at an unprecedented time in human culture, with human knowledge, experience, and action shared, cataloged, and visualized in ways never before imagined. Build on that the tools that we now have to encode mathematics and model that human information, and we have something we've never dreamed of before.

The nexus of software, human culture, and mathematics is upon us in the visualization, modeling, and analysis of today's data.

It's a magical time.

Specialties

Software development and architecture (numerous languages and technologies), ecological and socio-economic modeling, systems modeling, statistical analysis, data mining, visualization, and analysis.
