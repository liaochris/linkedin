Bill Stahlman, PE's Experience

Port Engineer

America's Central Port

Government Agency; 11-50 employees; Transportation/Trucking/Railroad industry

July 2011 – Present (2 years 2 months) Granite City, Illinois

Appointed by the Port’s Board of Commissioners to serve as their Port Engineer in July of 2011. At the time of appointment, Mr. Stahlman was the youngest professional engineer to serve in this key position since the Port was created by the Illinois State Legislature in 1959. As Port Engineer, Mr. Stahlman is responsible for managing all engineering and construction projects within the Port’s jurisdiction. Responsibilities include:

- Supervise the day-today operations of the Engineering Department and prepare a monthly report to the Board of Commissioners on progress of all engineering and construction activities at the Port;

- Advise the Executive Director and Board of Commissioners on public safety, all infrastructure requirements pertaining to roads, rail, utilities, drainage, and buildings;

- Negotiate, draft and manage all outside engineering services contracts and work performance;

- Oversee permitting and construction activities within the Port’s jurisdiction and serve corrective notices or stop work notices on construction activities;

- Develop and approve plans and specifications for Port engineering and construction projects;

- Develop testing and inspection standards for infrastructure improvements and develop appropriate policies for managing said improvements;

- Development of the Port’s Annual Capital Improvement Program;

- Manage the Port’s internal railroad infrastructure and coordinate rail operations between Port tenants and rail carriers serving the Port;

- Coordinate with city, county, state and federal agencies as required;
