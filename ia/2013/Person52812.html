Henry Nahurski's Summary

Hi. My name is Henry Nahurski.

I believe great art and design discovers compelling stories of the human condition and uses those stories to improve people's lives.

I graduated from the University of Minnesota with a double major in Art and Graphic Design. I've worked in brand positioning, print design, web design, illustration, animation, web design, and interaction design, but what really interests me is coming up with creative solutions to difficult problems.

I'm not afraid of a challenge. In fact, I'm always looking for new ways to push myself to learn and grow, not only as a designer, but as a person. I believe in originality, variety, and finding that fine line where art meets design.

Apprentices gain a holistic understanding of the UX process by studying business discovery, user research, information architecture, interaction design, and visual design—and by utilizing techniques such as interviewing, empathy maps, card sorting, process flows, wireframing, prototyping, and user testing.
