Kevin Kingsbury's Summary

In January of 2009, with Glenn Grant, they founded G2 Technology Group which is an IT consulting firm that provides technical services and expertise to small and medium business while being committed to helping businesses implement "Green IT" technologies and best practices that reduce operating costs while contributing to the global environmental movement.

Before starting G2, Kevin had been working for the Smithsonian Astrophysical Observatory in the day to day operations of NASA's Chandra X-ray Observatory. As the lead Technical Analyst in the Mission Planning group for the Science Operations Team Mr. Kingsbury was the manager responsible for software development and the lead security analyst for the group.

In addition to the everyday scheduling, programming, simulation and anomaly support activities Kevin Kingsbury was also involved in the formation of an incident response team (HEAD SMART) at the Harvard Smithsonian Center for Astrophysics as well as implementing the Chandra Radiation Module which resulted in increased scientific efficiency.

Mission Planner

Smithsonian Astrophysical Observatory

August 2002 – August 2011 (9 years 1 month)

The Chandra X-ray Observatory is one of NASA's Great Observatories, launched on STS-93 by NASA on July 23, 1999. Mission Planning provides computer-related support of x-ray astronomy research and space observatory operations using the Chandra X-ray Observatory. The structure of Chandra scheduling is derived from the need for efficiency as part of the process of maximizing the scientific return while, at the same time, minimizing risk to the spacecraft. In concert with scientific staff, Mission Planning will plan and schedule observations, and are also involved in, coding, modifying, debugging, and documenting software for mission planning; developing and maintaining databases; drafting and maintaining materials for websites; providing user support; and participating in the daily operations of the Chandra Observatory.
