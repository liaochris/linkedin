Kate Gardiner's Summary

I am a digital strategy consultant working with clients across the United States on national and globalfacing audience development. I have worked with media organizations
including Al Jazeera (in Doha, Qatar & Washington, D.C.), Voice of America / the Broadcasting Board of Governors, CBS Interactive, New York Public Radio, PBS NewsHour, the Natural Resources Defense Council and many journalism-oriented startups. I speak at conferences about social and digital strategy for nonprofit organizations. In the past I have contributed to various websites, including Huffington Post Live, Poynter and PBS Mediashift.

As of May 2013, my principle project is Years of Living Dangerously, a climate change documentary series to air on Showtime in February 2014. Follow us on Twitter (@yearsofliving), on Facebook, (http://facebook.com/yearsofliving), or via our site, http://yearsoflivingdangerously.com for more information, or contact me to work with us: kate@theyearsproject.com.

  • I am a journalist; ask me anything

    • Columbia Journalism Review
    • December 14, 2012
    Authors: Kate Gardiner, Sarah Laskow

    “It’s a very challenging community to get used to, but it’s one of the most rewarding places to have conversations in the longer term,” says Kate Gardiner, a writer and digital strategy consultant, who helped set up one of the first journalist IAmAs, for PBS NewsHour’s Hari Sreenivasan, back in December 2010. It was a little bit of an experiment: The show’s website was getting a notable amount of traffic from Reddit, and it seemed like a good idea to try to talk to the audience there. Gardiner set up another one about a year later, for a reporter at Al Jazeera, who’d just gone on a road trip through Texas. “Since then, it’s gotten crazy popular,” she says. “I’ve noticed that it has less of an impact than it used to, but it’s a good piece of social strategy for news organizations.”
