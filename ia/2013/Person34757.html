Desmond Pieri's Summary

I help companies get started, restarted, or grow to the next level. I have run technology-based organizations from pure startups to $25 million in revenue.

Though not by nature a short-term type person, because of a decsion to live for a few years in Europe, since 2000 I've completed 15 interim CEO and interim COO roles in the US and abroad. Sometimes I am the interim CEO. Other times I am the interim COO, helping the founding CEO take their company to the next level.

My industry expertise is broad, including consumer ecommerce, mobile devices, wireless communication, medical devices, software, middleware, computer-telephony, speech recognition, electronic ink, computer hardware, video games, electronic monitoring of devices (such as fire extinguishers and medical oxygen tanks), neighbor-to-neighbor car sharing, and a not-for-profit start-up accelerator.

Specialties

Whether interim or long-term, I'm always an agent of change. I do well at bringing focus and order to organizations, at creating products that leapfrog the competition, and at driving revenue. I hire well and I lead people to achieve aggressive goals.

Interim CEO or Interim COO

For the last decade, I've completed over a dozen interim CEO/COO assignments, as noted at my blog:

http://changeagentdes.com/

People say the post where I tell how the first thing I did at one turnaround was to clean the kitchen says a lot about who I am.

In some cases the founder asks me in, while in others it is the investor that initiates the assignment. Engagements generally run 3 to 12 months, though three were for two years. Each has been a hands-on, operational role. And each has been found via word-of-mouth by people who are familiar with what I've done.

CEO vs. COO

CEO: Many times I was brought in as interim CEO to fill the gap between an exited CEO and a new, industry-specific CEO. In each case I lead the company to a new level so it could attract a CEO from the industry. Companies ranged from pre-reveune startups to $25 million in revenue.

COO: Several times I was brought in as interim COO to help the existing CEO move the company to the next level. Often the CEO is a founder, is very technical and / or very young, and wants help in executing the CEO role. Working hand-in-hand with the CEO, I help organize and focus the company, instilling a culture of action and results..

FOUR SWEET SPOTS

1. Product / Market Fit: I determine the fit between the company’s product(s) and the needs of the market(s). Sometimes I change the product and/or the target market. Sometimes I focus the company on fewer products and/or fewer markets.

2. Management Team Changes: I determine what changes are required in a management team, and make those changes. In most cases fewer changes are required than anticipated.

3. Fund Raising: I determine - and raise - whatever cash is required, given all the other changes (product, target market, team, sales plan.)

4. Business Development / Distribution: I determine if the sales / distribution plan is appropriate, making any changes required. I turn suspects into prospects, customers, and cash.
