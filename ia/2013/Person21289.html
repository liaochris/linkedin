C. David Clauss' Summary

Serving in the practice of law since 1973. Law Clerk for Supreme Court of Kentucky from 1973 until 1974. Private practice in Kentucky from 1974 until 1977. Assistant Attorney General, Kentucky, from 1977 until 1981. Actively practicing law in Jackson, Wyoming, since 1981. Practice is concentrated in estate planning, wealth preservation, probate and estate administration, and business strategies for successful families and individuals.

Adjunct Professor, Academy of Multidisciplinary Practice and a Senior Faculty Member of the Estate and Wealth Strategies Institute at Michigan State University. Frequent lecturer and speaker to professional organizations, C.P.A.s, lawyers, financial advisors, insurance professionals, charitable organizations, and the public, on wealth preservation strategies, estate planning, business planning, and related topics.

Admitted to practice by: United States Supreme Court (1978); Wyoming Supreme Court (1981); Kentucky Supreme Court (1973); United States Court of Appeals for the Sixth Circuit; United States District Court, District of Wyoming; United States District Court, Eastern District of Kentucky; United States District Court, Western District of Kentucky.

Specialties

My practice is concentrated in estate, wealth, and business planning, including limited liability companies, family limited partnerships, and private trust companies.
