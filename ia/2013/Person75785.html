Kimberly Kohatsu's Summary

I don't love advertising. I find it a tad disconcerting when people say they do. After all, no audience would say "I love advertising," and they're the one's you're really after.

Still, advertising is something I've found I'm rather good at. I have a knack for taking smart insights and communicating them in a way that is meaningful. What I create goes beyond a simple ad or article or brochure. What I create, above all, is a voice.

Specialties

• Online marketing including PPC, social media, blogging, email marketing, video and screencasts, press releases, article marketing, and community management
• Advertising including writing, editing, advertising concepts and copywriting (all media including TV, print, outdoor, guerilla, radio, web, social marketing), branding and identity

Directing marketing efforts in a startup environment means I have my hands in everything: copywriting, PPC, branding, SEO, event planning, networking, brand strategy, blogging, and probably a bunch of stuff I missed.

Some results:
• On the day I started working at CallFire, conversions through PPC were not being tracked, CTR was a meager .1%, and roughly $80,000 was being spent every month on paid search.

One of my first initiatives was implementing Conversion Tracking. I cut out a lot of the waste by reorganizing our campaigns, testing ad copy, and increasing the number of negative keywords. I also introduced Remarketing to increase efficiency of the Display Network.

Today, our campaigns are converting at 7.55%, with an overall CTR of 1.91%, all the while spending less each month (approx. $70K).

• Revamped our email marketing efforts, moving from a broken and difficult-to-use open source software over to MailChimp for usability and reporting.
