Kendra D. Link's Experience

Director of Contracts and Procurement

Early Learning Coalition of Miami-Dade/Monroe

Nonprofit; 51-200 employees; Individual & Family Services industry

July 2012 – Present (1 year 4 months) Coral Gables, FL (currently remote from Greater New York City Area)

Responsible for the overall management of the Contracts Department of the Early Learning Coalition of Miami-Dade/Monroe with an annual budget of nearly $180 million. Enable the Coalition to comply with all statutory and contractual obligations in relation to early education programs in Miami-Dade and Monroe Counties. Ensure accomplishment of goals related to monitoring of Central Agencies and sub-contractors for compliance with federal and state regulations and contractual agreements. Responsible for managing contract actions, business proposal development, and the procurement of goods and services. Lead the Contracts Team both strategically and operationally in a government contracting environment.

Determine effective cost structures, establish contracting procedures, identify and execute universal contracting vehicles, support the strategic initiatives of the operating centers, manage relationships with government auditors and key contracting organizations, and enhance the overall financial and contractual acumen of project managers and staff. Head the procurement function which oversees solicitation of bids from Invitations to Negotiate (ITNs), Request for Quotes (RFQs), and Request for Proposals (RFPs), ensure compliance with state and federal purchasing guidelines, and support a wide variety of projects and functions under the direction of the Senior Vice President & Chief Administrative Officer which can encompass management reports, organizational reviews, alternatives analyses and financial analyses.

As of September 2013, working remotely from the Greater New York City Area during transition to new Director of Contracts and Procurement.

Attorney

Kendra D. Link, Esq.

March 2011 – July 2012 (1 year 5 months) Coral Gables, FL

Provided legal advisory services to the U.S. government. Services included: advising the federal agency on the legal implications of policy decisions and program transactions, interpreting statutes and regulations, and assessing legal options when enforcing legislation, regulations, and agency agreements. Services also included: review, revision, and update of agency due diligence guidelines, including recommendations on implementing such revised guidelines and training agency staff and contractors in use of such guidelines; as well as other legal research and document review services.

Managing Member

LCG International

February 2011 – July 2012 (1 year 6 months) Coral Gables, Florida

LCG International addresses some of the world’s most complex issues in international development in order to improve the economic, political, and social well-being of individuals and communities in emerging economies. Supported domestic and foreign governments, corporations, nonprofit organizations, development banks, and multilateral institutions in devising effective strategies to address global development challenges. Provided strategic advisory and management services in the areas of economic development, trade, privatization, democracy and governance, rule of law, energy and the environment, and infrastructure.

Assistant General Counsel

U.S. Trade and Development Agency, Washington D.C. Metro Area

Government Agency; 11-50 employees; International Trade and Development industry

February 2005 – January 2011 (6 years)

Kendra D. Link joined the U.S. Trade and Development Agency (USTDA) in 2005, where she most recently served as an Assistant General Counsel. Ms. Link represented USTDA, an independent agency of the U.S. government, in connection with international infrastructure development projects in emerging economies spanning numerous sectors, including transportation, energy, water and the environment, mining and natural resources, and information and communication technology.

Functioned in high-profile, multifaceted role as a key member of the Office of the General Counsel with program and administrative responsibilities. Coordinated and managed all legal affairs affecting USTDA projects in Latin America & the Caribbean and Southeast Asia (representing approximately 33% of USTDA’s program budget and encompassing over 35 countries). Advised senior management and administrative staff on all legal issues affecting the internal operation of USTDA.

Counseled regional program staff and senior management regarding design, implementation, monitoring, and audit of international infrastructure development projects. Structured, negotiated, and executed international transactions and agreements by which the agency delivers its program commitments – grant agreements with foreign governments and foreign private entities, contracts with U.S. firms, interagency agreements with other U.S. government agencies, and agreements relating to the use of trust funds at multilateral development bank groups.

Managed legal matters relating to administrative law and procedures applicable to federal government agencies, including government contracts under the Federal Acquisition Regulation, international grants, due diligence, audits, disputes, and agency administrative reporting requirements.
