Cliff Tyllick's Projects

  • Making Accessibility Easy

    • June 2007 to September 2010
    Team Members: Cliff Tyllick, Buddy Allison, Debra Cerda

    In 2007, the TCEQ formed its Accessibility Coordination Group and charged it with the mission of finding out what needed to be done to make electronic information accessible to everyone.

    One of our greatest issues was with PDFs. Hardly any of our PDFs met even the most basic aspects of the accessibility guidelines.

    Realizing that most PDFs began life as Word documents, I looked into whether the Word documents had been accessible to begin with. In brief, the answer was no.

    Instead of using heading styles, people were slapping formatting on lines that should be headings. Instead of using Word's features for creating columns, people created invisible tables and dropped their content into them.

    Why? Because it was the easy thing to do. Those "features" were on Word's default toolbar. Features that helped Word help you—and that made documents accessible—were buried.

    So I created my Accessibility toolbar for Word 2003. It put heading styles front and center, made it easy to generate a table of contents, and made it easy to open the document map. It also made it easy to edit styles.

    Then, working with a subgroup, I developed training to show folks how to use the toolbar to create accessible documents—and how to make their jobs easier, too. A number of people told me that this approach to learning Word helped them turn projects that had taken days into tasks that took less than a minute.

    Creating and updating a table of contents.

    Changing the font and margins throughout to satisfy their boss. And then again to satisfy *his* boss.

    And so on.

    I taught the classes for 15 months—twice a week at first; by the end, twice a month. Many of the people I taught went on to teach their peers.

    On Jan. 1, 2010, management was able to say that no new documents were to be inaccessible—and know that it was a practical, achievable demand.
