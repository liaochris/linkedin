  • EMPLOYER’S GUIDE TO MEDICAL TOURISM BENEFIT DESIGN: Cost containment and value-based health travel benefit programming for group health plans

    • MHI Press
    • March 13, 2013
    Authors: Maria K Todd MHA PhD

    Health travel for the group health benefit sector is an established cost containment option that was, for years, used primarily by reinsurers and case management firms and limited to rare, high-cost, tertiary cases.

    In recent years, this decades old benefit program has become increasingly popular when applied to a number of more frequently-encountered, elective health procedures alongside other principles of
    value-based health purchasing.

    Through the use of cost-saving benefit design incentives, employers are testing the receptiveness of plan participants and encouraging plan members to consider bespoke, narrow networks of high-performance healthcare providers in targeted locations that may be located further from home.

    The author addresses how to design and launch a health travel benefit pilot program, plan funding options, quality, safety and logistics considerations, provider selection criteria, and bundled case rate contracting in the USA and abroad.

  • Physician Integration & Alignment: IPAs, PHOs, MSOs, ACOs and beyond

    • CRC Productivity Press
    • November 5, 2012
    Authors: Maria K Todd MHA PhD

    Today, with physician and hospital reimbursement being cut and tied to quality incentives, physicians and health plans are revisiting the concept of integration. Payers demand that providers do more with less without sacrificing quality. As a result, physicians again find themselves integrating and aligning with hospitals that have the resources they lack or must develop together.

    Written by respected expert in the field of physician integration and managed care, the book examines physician integration and alignment in the current healthcare market. It outlines the common characteristics of integrated groups and various organizational structures, and also explains how to avoid making the same mistakes of the past. Filled with suggestions and ideas from successfully integrated practices, the book:

    - Identifies industry drivers for the resurgence of integrated models and the need for aligned models

    - Provides a look at the common characteristics of integrated and aligned groups and how they can work together

    - Discusses antitrust and other regulatory concerns present when considering the right organizational & management structure

    - Offers time- and money-saving checklists, lessons learned, models, and templates—saving thousands of dollars in consulting fees

    Maria Todd provides readers with the vision and practical tools needed to organize their business entities in a manner that will maximize economic clout and provide quality of care for both the hospital and physician group. This much-needed resource includes helpful insights on topics such as declining physician reimbursement, declining margins, physician shortages, physician-hospital competition, rising practice investment requirements, the return to capitation as a payment mechanism, and recent changes in the relationships between physicians and health systems.

    National Release Date: November 5. 2012
    ISBN-10: 1439813086
    ISBN-13: 978-1439813089
