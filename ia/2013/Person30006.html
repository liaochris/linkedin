Daniel Marashlian's Summary

I started out at going to one of the top ranked Public Universities for Computer Science, the University of Arizona.

From there, my career started at one of the leading online colleges in the world, Rio Salado College. They've won many awards in education and technology and are considered pioneers in online education. Our custom LMS RioLearn and various in-house applications aided our college to be the most cost effective college in the largest community college district in the world, Maricopa Community Colleges.

While working at Rio Salado, I had an opportunity to get my Masters in Leadership. While this was a 180 degree turn from my Computer Science background, I knew it would help me build towards the person that I wanted to be.

After my Masters, I got a job at Epic Cycle Interactive in downtown San Diego. From high profile clients like the Raiders and CBS, work dedication and tight deadlines were key! Working at Epic sparked my love for "the start up".

Troy Malone, the founder of Pelotonics was looking for a CTO to lead the technology within the company. With my education and work experience, it was a perfect fit! With blood, sweat, and some tears, Troy and I built out an amazing tool for small and medium sized businesses alike to help them get things done.

While working on Pelotonics, I got put in touch with Rodney Rumford and Sean Callahan to help them maintain TweetPhoto, a new photo sharing website for Twitter that was becoming very popular. The rest is history, we renamed it to Plixi and I became the CTO when we raised our Series-A round. At that time, around ten months into starting the company, we were one of the fastest growing internet companies EVER!

We eventually sold Plixi to Lockerz in January of 2011 and now I'm innovating at Lockerz.

More recently, I started Picks+ and I'm going to be building the smartest sports prediction software in the world. More on that later...

Specialties: Everything that it takes to run a top website in the world.
