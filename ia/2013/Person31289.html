Dave Sansom's Summary

My passion for photography, nature, golf and golf courses has presented me with exciting creative and business opportunities, and Dave Sansom Photography is doing exceptionally well. My client base continues to grow and life is good, indeed.

I photograph golf courses for a very simple reason. I love the game, and I'm a fan of courses of all kinds. Public, private, old golf courses and new, coastal, mountain, parkland… I enjoy them all. If a course is well-designed and well-maintained, there’s no place I’d rather be. I have great respect for the men and women who design, build, maintain and manage golf courses, and I consider it a privilege to help make each property I shoot look its best. My philosophy, when it comes to golf course photography, can be boiled down to a brief phrase: Your Golf Course… Only Better.  Every time I step onto a new course, my goal and my singular focus is to provide my clients with the finest photographs I've ever taken, and hopefully, the best ever shot of their property. 

Shooting non-golf landscapes requires a different mindset, but I feel the same responsibility to capture the power of a moment, and not just to take a picture. I work hard to bring the emotional impact of nature's astounding beauty to my photographs.

My life has tilted in a variety of creative directions, but photography is my obsession and my love. To borrow a phrase from Charlton Heston, "They can have my camera when they pry it from my cold, dead hands."

You can download my latest golf course photography portfolio, including new images of Liberty National Golf Club and Peachtree Golf club, at:

Dave Sansom's Projects

  • Liberty National Golf Club, Images for The 2013 Barclays

    • July 2013 to Present
    Team Members: Dave Sansom

    As The Barclays, the first FedEx Cup Playoff event of the 2013 season, gets underway the Official Souvenir Program features 26 of the images I shot at Liberty National Golf Club in early July, plus another 4 of my images in my full page ad in the program. This beautiful golf course is made more spectacular by its proximity to Manhattan and The Statue of Liberty, which stands a mere 1000 yards away. My photos of the course and surrounding environs will also be sold in the merchandise tent at The Barclays.

    I'm very grateful to the folks at Heritage Charity Auctions and Awards, of Cumming, Georgia, a company I'm now associated with as Senior Consulting Photographer, who arranged for me to shoot Liberty National. I've known Dennis, Doug, Simon and Mark for a few years, but they've only recently added me to the team. As a licensed PGA Tour vendor, they will be giving me many more PGA Tour venues and TPC courses to shoot.

    I also want to thank Mike Mazur, Steve Slocum, Ken Dao and Robin Daily, at Golfweek Custom Media, who approached me about using my images in the Program, which they published. They got in touch to ask if they could use my photos for the book, and I gladly agreed to their generous offer.

    But most of all, I want to thank noted golf course architect, Bob Cupp, for designing the amazing Liberty National course, along with Tom Kite. It truly is a remarkable golf course, and the story of it's creation is completely unique in golf. My photos of his work have served as an introduction to Bob, and we've discovered that we have many things in common. His is a new friendship that has great meaning to me, and his kind words about my work are nothing less than inspirational.

    You can see the Official Program online, at http://tinyurl.com/ktr48yf.

  • Utah State Parks Project

    • June 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Dave Sansom

    Since the Fall of 2011, I've had the pleasure of photographing the State Parks of Utah. The first assignment was to photograph Soldier Hollow, the Utah golf course that hosted the 2012 USGA National Public Links Championship. That assignment resulted in images used for the official poster, program and more for the Publinks, as well as being hired as Utah's official photographer at the event.
    That project blossomed over time into an ongoing assignment to provide new photos of all 43 of Utah's amazing state parks. I'm not quite half way there, yet, so there are many more trips to Utah and many more weeks in the studio until this amazing project is completed. It's a tough gig, but somebody has to do it!
