Elizabeth Galindo Roberts PhD's Summary

Dr. Galindo Roberts is a renowned couture designer, historical research and costume design consultant for cinema, and conservation and preservation expert in textiles and accessories. Dr. Galindo Roberts brings extraordinary enthusiasm and energy to pass on her vast and broad knowledge to students wishing to pursue these important fields of fashion, design, theater and film, and costume making.
• Known as a leading expert in designing and creating hand-made clothing patterns, unique textiles, burnt- out velvets, stenciled velvets and hand-painted silks using “originary craft practices” dating to the 18th century.
• Brings expansive knowledge of historical textile construction methods and hands-on skill in embroidery, imprinting, weaving, and leather dyeing, using these methods to design original costumes and crafts for Hollywood films. These techniques add greater credibility and depth to the character in each scene, helping the actor to strike a deeper emotional connection with members of the audience.
• Skilled in the delicate art of cleaning, repairing, and preserving priceless articles, costumes and fabrics, preparing each piece for display in museums.

Historical Costume Research for Film and TV

Galindo Couture and Consulting

January 2005 – Present (8 years 9 months) World Wide

Dr. Galindo Roberts collaborates with film directors, costume designers, and leading actors to create one-of-a-kind historically accurate costumes and crafts for motion pictures. She thoroughly researches the eras in which the scenes are set to capture the design styles and textile construction methods from the period. She presents overall costume concepts to directors and production designers using storyboards with sketches, fabric samples, color palettes and photographs of the actors wearing the costumes.
• Worked as historical costume design consultant for numerous full-length films such as Savages, pre-production, 2011, Director: Oliver Stone; The Tree of Life, 2011, Director: Terrence Malick; The Girl With the Red Riding Hood, 2011, Director: Catherine Hardwicke; Fair Game, 2009, Director: Doug Liman; The Curious Case of Benjamin Button, 2007, Director: David Fincher; and was Assistant to Costume Designer Mark J. Bridges for There Will Be Blood, 2006, Director: Paul Thomas Anderson.

Textile Conservation, Preservation, and Valuation Expert

Galindo Couture and Consulting

January 2000 – Present (13 years 9 months) Carmichael California

Dr. Galindo Roberts directs a team of experts to evaluate environmental and physical conditions to properly store and display collections of textiles and accessories for museums and private collectors. She and her team thoroughly document the provenance and historical context for each item in the collection, write detailed narratives along with digital photographs, and compose complete and accurate entries into the catalogue.
• Currently researching, archiving, appraising, and preserving 15,000 pieces for two private collectors to certify and document accurate replacement value.
• Provided preventive and remedial conservation treatments for de Young Museum in San Francisco, and Victoria and Albert Museum in London.
• Worked with vintage couture and textile artifacts including gowns by Paul Poiret, Madeleine Vionnet, Cristóbal Balenciaga, Christian Lacroix, Jean Patou, Mariano Fortuny and Elsa Schiaparelli.

Couture Designer and CEO

Galindo Couture and Consulting

January 1990 – Present (23 years 9 months)

Dr. Galindo Roberts leads a creative team to produce unique, imaginative and hand-made fashion designs, and, as CEO, is key decision maker for P&L, budgeting, marketing, business development, and client relations.
• Galindo Costume and Couture designs have been featured in HBO’s series Sex in the City, Charlie’s Angels—Full Throttle in 2003, and Charlie’s Angels in 2000.
• Designed and managed Galindo Couture collections for leading fashion retailers such as Neiman Marcus, Saks Fifth Avenue, Bergdorf Goodman, and Wilkes Bashford Boutiques from 1997 to 2004.
• Sold out all 20 of Galindo Couture’s one-of-a-kind gowns featured in Saks Fifth Avenue’s gown shows touring the country in 2000
• Numerous one-of-a-kind Galindo Couture gowns have been worn by leading actresses including Sophia Loren, Cameron Diaz, Uma Thurman, Cate Blanchette, Melanie Griffith, Sharon Stone, Minnie Driver, and Selma Hayek at Hollywood Red Carpet Events such as the Oscars, Golden Globes, Emmys, Grammys, and Screen Actors Guild award shows from 1999 to 2009.
