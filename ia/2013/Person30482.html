Dan White's Summary

I've had the funtastic, yes that's right, funtastic privilege of being able to make a career out of graphic design. Not too many people get to do something they love while getting paid for it.

My training in graphic design had a real solid foundation from the start. Unlike a lot of designers today, I was able to have some actual hands on design experience with physical layout, art classes, typography, drawing, camera and print work. Combining this with the technology of the digital age, I feel allows me to have a bit of an edge and appreciation for true graphic design many don't get the privilege of experiencing.

Graphic/Web design is a vital and crucial part of any corporation or client’s professional marketability and I approach my work with the mind-set of being successful and instrumental in producing outstanding design materials that make an impact. My goal is always to be a positive, valuable, go-to, or team player type creative professional.

I strive to adhere to a life philosophy of being someone who exhibits a character of integrity, reliability, hard working, one who encourages, develops potential, seeks to understand, who can take the initiative, while maintaining a sense of humor.

Being current and up-to-date on the latest design trends is important to me, and so I consistently expand my professional development in all areas of print and web design. For example, I went back to school and learned at the foundational level, learning how to hand code, program and style. This has benefited me greatly in that I now posses not just the WYSIWYG knowledge but also the "behind-the-scenes" knowledge of making changes to the code or styling. I'm also in the process of learning Java to enhance my skill set.

An enjoyable part of being a graphic designer is providing professional creative solutions that bolster, enhance or drastically increase a client's or organization's public business presence. I have had the exciting privilege of doing that many many times.
