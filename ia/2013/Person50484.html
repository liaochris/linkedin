Ron Perry's Summary

A Virginia native, Ron has a broad background in research and development of technology, including robotics, media production, technology education, telecommunications, and entrepreneurship. After starting and managing two successful CAD/CAM and graphics companies, he developed and instructed the advanced technology program for the Shenandoah Valley Governor's School, including a very successful electric vehicle racing program. Ron joined Ntelos in 1995 where he created and managed the Internet business unit, later overseeing the engineering and technical development of data products as the manager of technology innovation.

In May 2002 he started grep Innovation LLC, which provides a wide range of technology services including broadband architecture design and strategy, technology strategy, web development, information security and forensics, virtual director of IT/CTO services, and photography. Ron authored the technology portion of the Town of Shenandoah’s successful 2003 USDA RUS broadband grant application. Known informally as the Shenandoah Valley's mad scientist, he is a co-founder and past vice chair of the Shenandoah Valley Technology Council, and also serves on curriculum advisory boards for Woodrow Wilson Rehabilitation Center and Blue Ridge Community College. In addition from 2001-2010 Ron served as vice chair and technology chair, Wildlife Center of Virginia board of directors, which was awarded a 2004 Computerworld Honors 21st Century Achievement Award and the 2004 SVTC CIT Award for Technology Application.

An avid middle-distance runner, Ron has survived many adventures in parenting, car and motorcycle racing, mountain biking, basketball, coaching and as an impatient orthopedics patient.
