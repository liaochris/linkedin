Gregory G. Desierto, M.A.'s Summary

Hello there! Thank you for taking your time and visiting my LinkedIn profile. I am a psychologist in training. I love helping children, adults, and families discover awesome parts of themselves and overcome their struggles. I understand people in a deeper level, adapt to people's needs, empathize with difficult emotions, respect their individuality, and help people find meaningful connections in their lives.

I am a clinical psychology doctoral candidate at the California School of Professional Psychology, Alliant International University, San Francisco, and a pre-doctorate intern at Access Institute for Psychological Services, where I am receiving training in psychodynamic/psychoanalytic psychotherapy. I am also working on my dissertation, "Kumibo Ka Naman Dyan: Childhood Sexual Abuse Disclosures of Filipino American Men." In addition, I am a registered psychological assistant for Dr. Stephen H. Chou, and a Research Associate for NCIRE/SFVAMC.

I have worked at various schools, hospitals, and mental health agencies such as Richmond Area Multi-Services, Inc., New Leaf: Services for Our Community, San Francisco General Hospital/Child and Adolescent Services, Alternative Paths Training School, and CrisisLink. I have provided psychotherapy services to and completed psychological/neuropsychological evaluations for children, adults, and families.

In the future, I plan to apply my clinical skills in community mental health settings, supervise students, maintain a private practice, write papers to contribute to the psychology field, and continue to disseminate psychodynamic/psychoanalytic thinking in the public.

Specialties and Clinical Interests:
-Psychodynamic and Psychoanalytic Psychotherapy
-Psychological, Psychoeducational, and Neuropsychological Assessments
-Child, Adult, Couples, and Family Therapy
-Multicultural and Social Justice Issues
-Gay Men
-Borderline, Narcissism, and Psychosis
-Trauma and Childhood Sexual Abuse
