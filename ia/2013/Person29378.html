Daniela Miotto's Summary

Daniela has worked across various creative digital media platforms as an art director, producer and content developer for 15 years. She manages commercial creative projects from the idea's conception to product completion. Her client list is long, varied and full of great people! Currently she lives in Tasmania, Australia.

She is passionate about fostering confident creative business methods suiting to creative industry practices and practitioners; where new art practice and traditional business modes influence each other hand-in-glove. She's a true cross-discipline creative practitioner and a determined businesswoman.

While she was an undergraduate in Melbourne in the 90's she cut her project management teeth working for a reputable multimedia contemporary dance company and multiple freelance theatre production gigs. She graduated with a double major in professional writing (factual) and contemporary arts (dance) with a minor in romance languages (Italian).

Next a 2.5 year stint in Tokyo working in multimedia educational publishing as chief editor finished with a return to Australia and self-employment.

Under one roof she directs, produces and manages a multi-discipline commercial creative studio that houses 3 interdependent, yet separate, products & services:

Brownjames | Illustrator
Brownbread & Butter Studio | Multi-media Production and Design) and
Mio Brown | Textile & Surface Design


She is 42, no-nonsense, happily married and mother to two heart-throbbing children.

Chief Editor

LADO International College, Tokyo

August 2000 – August 2002 (2 years 1 month)

LADO was an English College based in Tokyo. They had a 3000 page English text book that required editing in preparation for publishing. I co-ordinated the appraisal of the text to determine what state the manuscript was in pre-edit.

I had a brilliant team of readers and none of the original authors were available to us. We determined that a re-write would be faster and cheaper.

With a team of writers on board, I co-ordinated the proposing, planning writing, editing and publishing of a new multiple volume version. The project took 2 years.

I was in charge of 10 people. It was a very complex, challenging and rewarding project. Begin a cross-cultural and mixed-media project was incredibly good fun and the people were one of the best groups I have ever worked with.

My primary tasks were the creation and management of the systems that held together the budget, schedule, project accountability to upper management, hiring of freelancers, choosing suppliers, artistic direction, style direction, the structural development and edit and management of the team.

Great project! Great People.

Daniela Miotto's Projects

  • WP Holman Clinic; an introduction to your treatment

    • March 2010 to September 2011
    Team Members: Daniela Miotto

    The W.P Holman Clinic staff identified a crucial need for a more pragmatic and sensitive approach to treatment introduction so they sought and received a sizable grant from the Tasmania Community Fund to produce a video.

    Brownbread and Butter Studio was commissioned to produce the video.

    The video guide bridges the gap between home life and life during cancer treatment by familiarising the patient and their carers/families with the whole clinic.

    It softens the edges of the unknown by offering straightforward advice on how to access the full breadth of clinic treatments and services and shows where patients will be treated and introduces patients to who will treat and support them.

    Daniela produced and co-wrote this project.

    Since it's entry into the clinic, we have had excellent feedback about the positive outcome amongst patients and families.

  • The Adventures of Captain Sparks

    • March 2010 to December 2010
    Team Members: Daniela Miotto

    Brownbread & Butter Studio wrote, directed and animated this 8.27 minute animation to be exhibited at Launceston's Queen Victoria Museum and Art Gallery, Launcston. The project was conceived by and funding was sought by the Sustainability Officer at the Launceston City Council.

    The museum and the Sustainability Officer, Jim Taylor, had worked very closely to make the building more energy efficient by creating some nifty switches, timers and doo-hickeys AND employing some good old common sense.

    Our brief was to tell the all-ages general public about these changes in a way that would inspire them to follow suit.

    Great project! More great people!
