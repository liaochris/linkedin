Douglas Buchanan | General Contractor's Volunteer Experience & Causes

  • Volunteer Experience

    • Volunteer

      Relay For Life, Central Phoenix
      • Health
      March 2012 – present (1 year 6 months)

      Relay for Life is part American Cancer Society's way to help provide education and awareness that Cancer is a disease that never sleeps and effects all people no matter what race, creed or color. Funding goes to help the victims, their families and to curing this terrible disease. Relay's matto is Celebrate, Remember, Fight Back.

    • Volunteer

      Grand Canyon Hikers Backpackers Association
      • Environment
      February 2011 – present (2 years 7 months)

      A week of hard work and hiking. A group of four volunteers teamed with Grand Canyon rangers and park ecologist to work on what is commonly known as the "Akin house". We were part of an ongoing work project to clear out vegetation and old debris from the area and get it ready for a new solar composting toilet facility. Our work day started off and ended with a mile plus hike from Cottonwood Campground to Roaring springs ranger house (the old Akin house). After 4 days of work we had accomplished the goals set by the park, a day early, so they set us free. But three of us just couldn't leave Grand Canyon a day early, so we helped out another group on another project for a day and then hiked out in rain and snow. It was the perfect way to end a week of work in the Canyon. Yes I love this stuff!

    • Volunteer

      AZ Game and Fish
      • Animal Welfare
      April 2011 – present (2 years 5 months)

      Antelope relocation. Joined up with about 40 others and participated with the AZGFD in a two day project (for us) of building a temporary capture facility outside of Prescott, AZ. The purpose of the project was to capture, tag and relocate approximately 120 antelope to two different areas of the state to help re-establish a stronger antelope presence in areas where their numbers were declining. Following the capture and transfer we released the 30 plus antelope that just happened to avoid the transfer and removed the capture pins. I met some great people, captured and wrestled with antelope and had a great experience. I'm glad to be part of helping preserve nature so others can enjoy it in the future. Now if I could only get drawn for an antelope hunt.

    • Volunteer

I thank them all - I really needed a hair cut! Fourteen inches was the length of hair I sent in - for those who are wondering.
