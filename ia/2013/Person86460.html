Mark Dennehy's Summary

I've spent the last few years working for several small companies and startups, usually as the generalist on the team. I enjoy working on the back-end fundamentals of a system, and the further down the software stack the better. I've maintained legacy systems, developed existing systems, installed new systems and architected entirely bespoke solutions. I've worked in the back office, I've worked facing the client, and I've pitched to VCs. I'm happiest when working on problems that are more substance than hype, more technical than marketing, and where the problem pushes me to new technical limits.

I currently work in IBM, developing High Availability and Disaster Recovery solutions for DB2, and also as part of the sysadmin team managing the DB2 development infrastructure for the Dublin lab.

Specialties

Systems administration, Low-level development in C, LAMP-stack administration, design and application programming.


Languages I've worked in over the years include C, Python, PHP, SQL (MySQL, Postgresql), C++, unix shell script and Java.


Systems I've worked on include RADIUS (configuration and writing custom modules), Apache, MySQL, Postgresql, Moodle, and several company products from DeviceAtlas to mobile work order systems to bespoke educational systems.
