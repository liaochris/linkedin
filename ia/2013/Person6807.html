Andrew Burg's Summary

IMPORTANT NOTE

I am friends with another Andrew Burg in Southern California. The two of us have been in contact off an on for years and we just wanted to make it confusing for everyone else. You're simply going to have to figure out which one of us is which.

OBJECTIVE

I want to build stuff. Big stuff. To work as a project owner or contractor, developing and building renewable, green-energy projects. Or continue in the water resources and environmental restoration discipline. Enjoying travel and new cultural experiences, I am available for relocation and am available immediately.

SUMMARY

During the past 25 years, I have developed a solid reputation providing creative solutions to environmental and infrastructure needs --- combining technical know-how with out-of-the-box thinking to meet challenges while resolving engineering / construction / funding problems. Strong visualization, organizational, leadership and consensus-building skills. Excellent oral, written and presentation abilities adaptable to various industries and project types. Accomplished negotiator that was solely responsible for creating working relationships with government officials that secured grants of $7+ million. Typically entrusted with resolving the “unusual” elements of a project. Created two of my own businesses.

Specialties

With 30 years of experience, I am passionate about the renewable “green” energy industry as well as environmental restoration and preservation. My skills, however, were obtained in the development and management of environmental, hazard mitigation and water resource projects; more specifically the real estate, land surveying, planning, feasibility, finance, client / business development and permitting aspects of system corridors and project sites.

PROJECT DEVELOPER / ENGINEER / OWNER

Mecklenburg County Storm Water Services

January 1997 – January 2004 (7 years 1 month)

As a manager of capital projects and demolition services, flood hazard mitigation and environmental restoration efforts came together in the form of numerous riparian corridor projects ($1-2 million typical) --- I coined the phrase "Little Sugar Creek Environmental Restoration Initiative" valued at approximately $50 million to tie together a series of inter-agency projects as a sales tool to secure grant funding. Re-engineered and integrated my civil engineering skills with "green" techniques to construct "living, breathing, evolving" systems rather than stagnant, mono-discipline projects. Rather than working with architects, planners and other "traditional" allies, I learned to work with geomorphologists, biologists, fisheries experts, botanists, floodplain managers.

Comprehensive water resources planning using turnkey management included project development, economic feasibility analysis, hazard mitigation studies, prioritizing sites using geo-spatial software tools, managing design consultants & construction contractors, permitting (Corps, NEPA, SEPA, SWPPP) and long-term maintenance and monitoring. Emphasis on public relations and creating educational materials and raising capital from state and federal grants.
