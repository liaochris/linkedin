Jeff Sanpore's Summary

DEAR RECRUITERS: Thank you for your interest; however, I am currently not available or even interested in considering new opportunities. Please DO NOT contact me.


As a seasoned software engineer and consultant I have broad experience in software engineering, project management, and software security. I’ve worked with many clients, in different domains, with many different tools and methodologies.

I'm looking for challenging engineering problems that need a creative solution.I see the big picture but know that its the details that count when a system goes live in production. I like to create well documented, bullet-proof, production grade code, not hastily thrown together "craplets".

I ran my own software consulting company for fourteen years and tried my best to lead with "Peopleware" as my guide. I'm always surprised at the number of software managers (and developers) who've never read this classic! I'm more productive in a culture where the corporate leaders treat and value software (and developers) as a vital corporate asset, not merely as a necessary evil. I'd much rather work with leaders that can share their product vision than for managers who can only share their bureaucracy, office politics and endless meetings. I'm wearing big boy pants now, I like to be inspired and led, not simply managed.

I believe that a good software process will transform a team from being "hero-centric" to being "process-centric". I like to invest time in doing things right the first time, not fixing mistakes.

My career spanning favorite projects (so far) include: automated trading systems at several hedge funds, a patented fraud detection system for cellular networks, and expert system prototypes to diagnose "transient excursions" in nuclear power plants. I like working on software that talks to other systems.

Specialties

Almost everything I’ve done has involved several of the following:

• Real-time data acquisition
• High volume, low latency storage and retrieval
• Pattern Analysis and Data Mining
• Automated Decision Making

Jeff Sanpore's Patents

  • Method and apparatus for fraud control in cellular telephone systems

    • United States Patent 5,950,121
    • Issued September 7, 1997
    Inventors: Jeff Sanpore, William Kaminsky, Mary Kennedy, David Daniels

    A method and apparatus for fraud control in cellular telephone systems. The invention identifies relative fraud probabilities for call records from the cellular telephone system, associates the call records with radio frequency (RF) signatures, and identifies the radio frequency signatures as being fraudulent or valid based on the relative fraud probabilities for the associated call records.
