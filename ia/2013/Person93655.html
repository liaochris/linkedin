Michiel Hulshof's Experience

Founder / Director

Tertium

Public Company; 1-10 employees; Events Services industry

January 2012 – Present (1 year 9 months) Amsterdam

Tertium is a strategic consultant for communication and research. We engage citizens in complex, future-orientend topics such as sustainability, Biobased Economy, the future of the EU, financial sector or bottum-up urban development. We give our clients strategic advice, moderate brainstorm sessions, organise public events and citizen dialogues, do reserach and offer a full media & communication strategy.

Founder / Director

Go West Project

March 2009 – Present (4 years 7 months)

The Go West Project is a multidisciplinary research lab focusing on emerging megacities. The body of work produced by Go West Project over the past years is extensive and varied: lectures, blogs, (photo) essays, installations, performances, events, architectural designs, urban plans, books and policy proposals. The work has been widely published in both China and Europe and won various awards.
In 2011, Go West launched the book How the City Moved to Mr Sun – China’s New Megacities, which was well received by the international press. The Dutch edition of the book was launched in spring 2012, the Chinese edition is due spring 2013.

Michiel Hulshof's Publications

  • How The City Moved To Mr Sun

    • SUN Publishers
    • September 1, 2011
    Authors: Michiel Hulshof, Daan Roggeveen

    Over the next two decades around 300 million Chinese villagers will move to the city, creating the largest urban society the world has ever seen. Small towns in Central and Western China are transforming at breakneck pace into huge metropolises with many millions of inhabitants. In size they rival global cities like Rio de Janeiro, London and Moskow, but their names are unknown in the rest of the world. How the City moved to Mr Sun lifts the veil from these unexplored megacities in the heart of China.
