George Soules' Summary

George Soules is the President and co-founder of AvantLogic Corporation, which owns MapsAlive and George Soules Photography. He is a software architect with over 30 years experience in software engineering, architectural design, and mission-critical applications. He is also a professional photographer.

George holds a degree in Information Sciences from the University of California, Santa Cruz. After college he worked in the high tech industry in Massachusetts for 10 years and then moved to Vermont to start a consulting company. He has worked with ABC Television, Bombardier, DaimlerChrysler, IBM, New York Power Authority, The Art Institute of Chicago, and the State of Vermont to name a few.

Specialties

Interactive Maps, Software Architecture, Object Oriented Design and Development, C#, ASP.NET, SQL Server, JavaScript, Flash, HTML5. Headshots, portraits, food and product photography, architectural photography.

George Soules' Projects

  • Art Institute of Chicago interactive floor plan and virtual gallery tour system, "Pathfinder"

    • November 2009 to May 2010
    Team Members: George Soules, Cheri Cross, Tim Toffoli, Janice Kenyon

    Interactive Kiosk 2010 MUSE Award Winner: Pathfinder, the Art Institute of Chicago’s interactive touch-screen floor plan, is a wayfinding tool designed to help on-site and online visitors discover and locate artworks, exhibitions, amenities, and daily events at the museum. To assist visitors in planning their visit, Pathfinder provides directional paths, offering the option to choose the most accessible route. Pulling information from the museum’s internal information management system and online calendar, Pathfinder enables visitors to find answers to the most common questions: What does the museum have for me to see and do? Where are the galleries with artworks that interest me? How do I get to these places?

    Additionally, Pathfinder currently supports screen navigation for both English and Spanish speakers. Online visitors have the additional opportunity to virtually “wander” through many galleries via high-definition 360-degree panoramic views of the galleries and can access online artwork records for more information. With these features and more, Pathfinder offers a new a way of accessing and exploring the museum.
