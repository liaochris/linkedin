Drew Lentz's Summary

I am a highly experienced wireless network and applications engineer with a background in core network fabric and a focus on wireless strategies. Through a career of over 20 years in the data delivery industry, a decade of which is focused on wireless data deployments, I am able to build unique solutions for clients looking to enhance their existing infrastructure or build out new networks. As an established network engineer with a fundamental understanding of routing and switching, and concerning the way that it relates to the RF characteristics of wireless networks, I am positioned to design comprehensive structured solutions of all scales for prospective clients. My ability to explain and simplify puzzling technology concepts and theories into real-world examples, in a meeting room or as a trade-show panelist, has garnered recognition from industry press, analysts, and most importantly customers. It is with an undying passion for the industry that I am able to speak candidly about technology and help drive business through problem solving and solutions because at the end of the day, I do what I love and I love what I do.

I was hand picked as the sole network engineer to design and build out a multi-county Next-Generation Non-Line-of-Sight Broadband Internet Service Provider. This deployment was the largest deployment of NLOS technology in the United States. I was required to develop the entire network, high-speed backhaul solution, server systems, and all surrounding technology to allow for over 20,000 users to access the internet via next-generation wireless broadband. The network build-out consisted of 8 Cisco 7500 series routers, 10+ Cisco 2500 series routers, 15+ Cisco Catalyst 5000 and 2900 series switches, Sun Netra and Dell PowerEdge servers, APC Symmetra and SmartUPS power devices, as well as 16 Navini 2.6GHz MMDS Base Stations. The backbone of the network is all fully licensed Stratex Altium OC-3 (3) and XP4 DS-3 (5) microwave radios. Also installed were 16 Alvarion BreezeAccess VL basestations and 4 Motorola Canopy Access Point clusters. I was responsible for administrating, maintaining, and operating all of the above listed devices. Alongside my normal day-to-day duties, I was also a Special Projects Developer responsible for coming up with and implementing new projects to generate revenue, as well as add usability and service to our customers and potential customers.
