Boyd Willis' Summary

I have 32 yrs experience in process engineering, including 25 yrs of metallurgy & hydrometallurgy, & 15 yrs operations experience in continuous process plants. My experience includes 16 yrs of engineering design & 3 yrs of plant commissioning. I have a METSIM license (metallurgical process simulation, heat & mass balance software) & I am a very experienced user.

I became a freelance consultant in July 2008, specialising in nickel laterite project development work. In this time I have been heavily involved in 12 nickel laterite studies, for 2 heap leach projects, 9 HPAL projects, & 3 atmospheric leach projects. Two of these projects intend recovering scandium as a by-product. For the 12 yrs prior to becoming a consultant I worked for Aker Solutions (prev Davy McKee & Kvaerner Metals) in Brisbane. I have been involved in many base metals projects, & have extensive knowledge of nickel laterite flowsheets, contributing to more than 30 nickel laterite projects. My involvement has extended to all facets of project development covering flowsheet development, testwork planning & management, mining, ore preparation, metallurgical processing, services, utilities & infrastructure, enabling me to bring a whole of project perspective to the table. My nickel laterite experience includes high pressure, atmospheric & heap leaching, & a variety of downstream recovery processes.

PreviousIy I spent 6 yrs at Kwinana Nickel Refinery, where I developed expertise in aqueous hydrogen reduction of nickel in high pressure autoclaves, and detailed knowledge of complex hydrometallurgical processes and chemistry.

I am enthusiastic about becoming involved in project definition, process development, testwork management and METSIM modelling work.

Boyd Willis' Publications

  • The Mindoro Nickel Project: HPAL Piloting and Process Development

    • ALTA 2009
    • May 2009
    Authors: Boyd Willis, Niels Verbaan, James Brown, D. Marshall

    The Mindoro Nickel Project is located in the Philippines and is being developed by Intex Resources. SGS Minerals was contracted by Intex in April 2008 to provide testing services in support of a Definitive Feasibility Study. The metallurgical testing program commenced with laboratory and continuous pilot plant beneficiation programs, followed by a laboratory hydrometallurgical program. The program culminated in the successful 8 day operation of an integrated continuous pilot plant consisting of: high pressure acid leaching (HPAL), primary neutralisation (PN), counter current decantation (CCD) and secondary (solution) neutralisation (SN). The pilot plant provided feed (SN thickener overflow) for a mixed sulphide precipitation (MSP) laboratory program. This paper discusses the design, operation and results of the integrated HPAL pilot plant. Key findings of 98% nickel and cobalt recoveries at 30 minutes residence time and >60% CCD underflow densities, are highlighted. In addition, this paper discusses the interpretation of the pilot plant data and how it was used by Aker Solutions Australia in the development of process design criteria and a METSIM® model to facilitate the design of the full-scale plant.

  • Developments and Trends in Hydrometallurgical Processing of Nickel Laterites

    • ALTA Nickel/Cobalt/Copper 2012
    • May 2012
    Authors: Boyd Willis

    To date the only nickel laterite acid leaching plants constructed have been based around high pres-sure acid leaching (HPAL) technology and the only other nickel laterite hydrometallurgical plants constructed have employed the Caron process. Several downstream recovery routes have been employed, including mixed sulphide precipitation (MSP), mixed hydroxide precipitation (MHP) and direct solvent extraction (DSX), with commercial products ranging from intermediates (MSP/MHP) to nickel oxide, nickel briquettes and nickel cathodes. Over the last decade considerable effort has been expended on the development of atmospheric and low pressure leaching processes (heap leaching and agitated tank leaching), as well as integrated flowsheets that re-generate the lixiviant and produce value-adding by-products. Much progress has been made towards the development of alternative downstream recovery processes, particularly in the field of DSX.

    This paper examines trends and new developments in hydrometallurgical processing of nickel later-ite ores. Block flowsheets for the established and developmental processes are presented and the relative merits of each flowsheet are discussed.

  • The Agata Nickel Project – Project Update - A Potential Low Cost Nickel Producer in the Surigao District of Northern Mindanao, the Philippines

    • ALTA Nickel/Cobalt 2011
    • May 2011
    Authors: Boyd Willis, Jon Dugdale, TONY CLIMIE

    Mindoro Resources Ltd (MRL) is undertaking feasibility studies into the development of an integrat-ed nickel-cobalt laterite project at Agata in the Surigao District of Mindanao, the Philippines. The Agata deposit has a National Instrument NI 43-101 Measured and Indicated Resource of 32.6 million DMT at 1.04% nickel and 0.05% cobalt, and an Inferred Resource of 1.7 million DMT at 1.04% nickel and 0.04% cobalt. An Exploration Target of an additional 50 to 70 million DMT grading 0.9 to 1.2 percent nickel has been identified for other Surigao District tenements within 30 km north of the Agata resource.

    A scoping study completed in 2010 considered processing options employing a combination of high pressure acid leach (HPAL) and atmospheric leach (AL) technologies. Preliminary metallurgical testwork has been performed on selected ore blends, demonstrating excellent leaching performance for HPAL of limonite and transition ores and AL of saprolite ores.

    The project is conveniently located about 47 km northwest of Butuan City and 73 km southwest of Surigao City. The Surigao District is an historic and established mining district for copper, gold and nickel and is a politically stable and supportive jurisdiction for mining in the Philippines. These strategically located resources and exploration targets have strong competitive advantages: close proximity to established infrastructure and deep ocean port sites, no inhabitants or forest in the resource area, large regional population base, close proximity to markets, abundant limestone for acid neutralisation on site and plentiful fresh water. These attributes, combined with the quality of the resource and outstanding metallurgical characteristics, offer high potential for the establishment of a world class nickel and cobalt processing operation.

    This paper reports on the findings of the scoping study, the metallurgical testwork results to date, and the selection of plant capacity and configuration for the PFS.

  • The Agata Nickel Project - A Potential Low Cost Nickel Producer in the Surigao District of Northern Mindanao, the Philippines

    • New Caledonia Nickel
    • November 2010

    Mindoro Resources Ltd (MRL) is undertaking feasibility studies into the development of a major, integrated, nickel-cobalt laterite project at Agata in the Surigao District of Mindanao, the Philippines. The Agata deposit has a National Instrument NI 43-101 Measured and Indicated Resource of 32.6 million DMT at 1.04% nickel and 0.05% cobalt, and an Inferred Resource of 1.7 million DMT at 1.04% nickel and 0.04% cobalt. An Exploration Target of an additional 50 to 70 million DMT grading 0.9 to 1.2 percent nickel has been identified for other Surigao District tenements within 30 km north of the Agata resource.
    The project is conveniently located about 47 km northwest of Butuan City and 73 km southwest of Surigao City. The Surigao District is an historic and established mining district for copper, gold and nickel and is a politically stable and supportive jurisdiction for mining in the Philippines. These strategically located resources and exploration targets have strong competitive advantages: excellent infrastructure, close proximity to deep ocean and markets, abundant limestone for acid neutralisation on site and plentiful fresh water. These attributes, combined with the quality of the resource and outstanding metallurgical characteristics, offer high potential for the establishment of a world class nickel and cobalt processing operation.
    MRL commissioned a scoping study to consider a project commencing with a Phase 1 selective direct shipped ore (DSO) operation to provide early cash flow and a Phase 2 processing operation using a combination of high pressure acid leach (HPAL) and atmospheric leaching technologies, either as a staged or single step development.
    This paper reports on the exploration progress and the preliminary findings of the scoping study for the mine and processing facility.

  • Downstream Processing Options for Nickel Laterite Heap Leach Liquors

    • ALTA Nickel/Cobalt 2007
    • May 2007
    Authors: Boyd Willis

    Growing worldwide interest in the application of heap leaching technology to nickel laterite ores heralds a need to explore compatible nickel recovery flowsheets. Alternative approaches to downstream processing of nickel laterite heap leach liquors are described and reviewed. Block flowsheets for established and conceptual processes are presented and each flowsheet is assessed in terms of strengths, weaknesses and product quality. The impact of ore type and grade on process selection is examined. Considerations related to project location and infrastructure needs are also discussed. Generic examples are given where appropriate.

  • Precipitation of Nickel Metal by Hydrogen Reduction – A New Perspective

    • ALTA Nickel/Cobalt 2000
    • May 2000
    Authors: Boyd Willis, John Von Essen

    The history and application of nickel precipitation from solution by hydrogen reduction are presented. Key factors affecting the performance of a nickel reduction operation are discussed in detail, outlining measures for achieving average nickel reduction rates of 100 kg/min. and increasing the capacity of typical installations by 30 to 60%. Emphasis is placed on the importance of adequate seeding and seed suspension. Other issues addressed include gas-liquid mass transfer, autoclave piping configuration, hydrogen quality, nickel concentration, fill factor and autoclave turnarounds. The importance of agitator design with respect to seed suspension and hydrogen mass transfer is emphasised. Impeller configuration and positioning, including a discussion of dual versus single impellers, and the importance of scale model testing are discussed. Recommendations on Froude number and tip speed for the upper mass transfer impeller and torque requirements for the lower solids suspension impeller are given.
