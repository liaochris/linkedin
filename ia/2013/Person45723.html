Franklin Kielar's Summary

Franklin Kielar was born in Upstate NY in 1977 where he continues to live. Even at an early age he studied animation and began writing stories and poetry inspired by Walt Disney, Brian Lumley, and Robert Frost. Graduating with a Bachelors of Science Degree in Computer Animation from the Art Institute of Fort Lauderdale in 1999, gave Franklin a solid background in the fundamentals of animation and art. He brings those talents to every production he is involved in. In 2001, finding full-time work in art became increasingly difficult requiring him to work in technical support jobs for a few years while continuing the artwork and productions on the side. During those years he learned a wide array of problem solving skills and management skills that are useful in every aspect of art and film production. Franklin then took a senior artist position with a local screen printing company in Rochester NY where he worked on designs for BMW CCA National, Rochester General Hospital, Pacebootlegger Volleyball, and many colleges, schools, and clubs. His free time was spent working with Rochester Movie Makers and local film makers in over 20 short films and independent features such as Saberfrog (2009) and Bunker nVa (2011). Franklin continues to work with multiple mediums including digital designs along with hand drawn art in order to create compelling stories and visuals.

Specialties

Animation and Art, establishing a good work dynamic between individuals in order to create an environment for highly motivated members with high creativity.
