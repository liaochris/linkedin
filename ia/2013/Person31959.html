David Weinberg's Summary

I spent 19 years as a senior level scientist at Merck and Co., Inc., where my focus was on early stage drug discovery, including target identification and validation, tool generation, assay development and project advancement with particular expertise in CNS GPCR targets. Drug development during that time relied on cross-functional project teams, and my experience as both leader and member of such teams developed my leadership, communication, and collaborative skills, while providing me with broad exposure to all aspects of drug development leading into Phase I. I am familiar with the pitfalls of new target advancement and experienced in the development of reasonable but aggressive timelines with well-defined Go/NoGo decision points.

In 2009 I moved to NIH as a Scientific Review Officer, first at the Center for Scientific Review, and then in 2011, at the National Institute for Child Health and Human Development. My role is to manage the initial phase of the grant review process, through identification of expert reviewers and management of review meetings. The position is vital to insuring that NIH funds the most meritorious science, and makes the best use of taxpayer dollars. Being a top review officer requires not only a rapid understanding of new scientific areas, but strong organizational, communication, leadership, and time management skills. These skills were forged during my tenure at Merck, and have enabled me to be highly successful. In addition, I have developed a strong understanding of the extramural grant application process, and the relevant governmental policies.

I continue to watch for new opportunities and challenges which would leverage my skills, both inside and outside the government.


Specialties: Project leadership, strategic design, cell line development, Molecular Biology, In vitro Pharmacology, Compound screening.
