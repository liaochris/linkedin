Chris Senior's Summary

An enthusiastic bloke, I've worked in the environmental and conservation fields for most of my adult life. Practical conservation work on school nature areas, local nature sites, and such led me to do a Countryside Recreation Management degree at Sheffield Hallam back in 1997. This further led me to develop specific interests, which mesh together rather well, as you can see under my Ecoscape section.

This mix of skills allows me to stay fresh, with projects combining various aspects of these. I could launch into paragraphs of management-speak and techno-babble about what I do, but I’d rather just say: If you want GIS work, commercial photography, a website, or any of the other various things I do, then you should give me a try. I'm really rather good at what I do, and get a real buzz from helping someone find a perfect solution specific to their need, and delivering great results which I have enjoyed spending time achieving.

I'm always happy to chat about my work, and offer advice, so please get in touch and we can see how I can help you. Distance no object, as they say, rates extremely competitive, lots of happy clients. What more do you want?

I work in the environmental and conservation fields. This general heading covers my varied portfolio of skills and interests, which intermingle rather well, to keep life fresh and interesting…

One aspect is GIS computer mapping: I work on projects ranging from small, local woodland, through regional spatial strategies, and even country-wide, when considering something like the wolf population in Bulgaria. I will assess your needs and recommend the best approach for your specific project, and will support you as much or as little as you require. I also offer OCN-certified MapInfo training, should you need it.

I also offer commercial photography, again with the environmental slant, covering landscape photography as defined by me: This includes both unspoilt, desolate beauty, but also the urban landscape which the majority of us inhabit. It also includes derelict sites and buildings, which fascinate me. Also, people and creatures interacting with the landscape are all part and parcel of my definition. Plenty of variety for me to explore!

Another area is website design: I work with different packages, but if I think that a free blog will meet your needs, then that’s what I will recommend, rather than try to sell you an expensive design-and-hosting package. I also do graphic design work, to help turn your ideas into something stunning and appropriate.

In addition, I sometimes write articles to go with my photos for various magazines, further feeding my love of travel: Working internationally is now feasible, with all the new technology, but as with all the other software I use to achieve my work, this is simply a tool: People are what matters and sometimes, you simply need to meet face-to-face too. Again, not a problem for me.

So, if you would like me to advise you on any of the above, please get in touch and I hope to add you to my varied client-list, which includes including local authorities, community forests, charities and NGOs, with much repeat-custom.

Brownfield Sites of South Yorkshire - contract

South Yorkshire Forest Partnership

Partnership; 11-50 employees; Environmental Services industry

June 2000 – May 2008 (8 years)

Investigation into brownfield, derelict and underused public open space, consisting of desktop research to create list of candidate sites, site visits to assess potential for tree planting, and collation of results into custom Microsoft Access database linked with MapInfo GIS system, designed by myself. Revisited in 2005. Since 2007, this work has been used to create a portfolio of new planting sites, with the aim of increasing the tree cover of South Yorkshire still further. In the year 2000, 138 brownfield sites were identified as having tree planting potential in South Yorkshire, an area totalling 2860 hectares. Over the next 6.5 years, the New Planting element of the FRG has enabled over 450 hectares of trees to be planted, which represents over one million new trees planted in South Yorkshire: Tree cover here is now approximately 10.3% of the land area, compared to a European regional average of 28%, and the county has a much greener appearance than at the start of the work.

Tree Canopy Cover Audit - contract

Red Rose Forest

October 2007 – March 2008 (6 months)

Manchester City Council & Red Rose Forest commissioned a study of their tree canopy cover, using the methodology of the Wakefield Groundwork project. This data was integrated with Urban Morphology Type data, produced by Manchester University, to allow a detailed analysis not only by electoral ward, but also by these 29 landscape designations, such as high / medium / low density housing, formal and informal open space, and disused and derelict land. The total canopy cover was found to be 15.50%, and a detailed report was produced breaking these figures down by both UMT and wards.

Tree Canopy Cover Audit - contract

Wakefield Groundwork

September 2004 – February 2005 (6 months)

Woodland canopy mapping for Wakefield Groundwork project to determine accurately the tree canopy cover for the district, by high resolution plotting based on aerial photographs and existing data, covering all woodlands down to 0.04 hectare, individual trees of three metre or above canopy and hedgerows greater than three metres in width. This new methodology resulted in a rise in measured woodland cover from 3.2% to 10.16%, and allowed identification of trees by any of six pre-defined categories. With two other contractors working under me, I was also team-leader for this project.
