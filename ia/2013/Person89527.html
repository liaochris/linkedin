Matt Petronzio's Summary

Matt Petronzio is the assistant features editor at Mashable, a leading source of news, information and resources for the connected generation. He helps guide the site's features content from editing to publishing, while continuing to write longform articles and cover social good. Based at the New York City headquarters, he previously worked as a features writer.

Prior to joining Mashable in January 2012, Matt worked for Guernica Magazine, W. W. Norton & Company and CURA: A Literary Magazine of Art & Action. He received his bachelor's degree in English literature and creative writing from Fordham University, where he graduated magna cum laude, and he is currently an MFA candidate in poetry at Hunter College of The City University of New York.

Matt's journalistic work has been quoted in Forbes, Nonprofit Quarterly, GOOD, the Huffington Post, ABC Action News, American Journalism Review and the Poetry Foundation's blog, Harriet; syndicated on Tumblr's Storyboard, American Express OPEN Forum, Yahoo! News and Metro; and cited in a University of California, Berkeley, study about online political advertisements.

His poetry has earned him several awards and honors, and has appeared or is forthcoming in Breakwater Review, InDigest, NAP and [PANK].

Specialties: Strong background in writing and editing.
