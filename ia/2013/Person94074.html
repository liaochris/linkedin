Mike Crittenden's Projects

  • Stanford Engineering

    • September 2011 to July 2012
    Team Members: Mike Crittenden, Tim Loudon

    Stanford's School of Engineering is one of the largest and most important schools within Stanford Unviersity. I, along with Tim Loudon, migrated the 1000+ page legacy HTML site into Drupal and implemented content sharing to share profiles/news/etc. from the main Engineering site base to the various department sites to eliminate double entry or outdated info.

    We also overhauled the information architecture and implemented it within Drupal, merging Stanford's design standards with the Engineering school's unique problems and messaging.

  • Waste Management

    • September 2010 to September 2011
    Team Members: Mike Crittenden, Tim Loudon, Hanns-Oskar Porr

    Waste Management is a Fortune 500 company in North America dating back to 1894. WM contracted Drupal Connect to assist in their Greenopolis/Think Green Rewards program which rewards users with points for recycling, which can in turn be used to buy real products.

    Our team completely overhauled the Greenopolis and Think Green Rewards web applications and automated huge parts of their workflow to reduce their monthly man hours required by them from 50+ to about 5. We added large features such as the Scan@School initiative and implemented a complete retheme which was extremely successful.

  • Smosh

    • November 2012 to February 2013
    Team Members: Mike Crittenden, Tim Loudon, Rob Decker, Vanessa Turke

    Fun! Smosh is currently the #1 subscribed channel on YouTube and their site showcases their funny videos and blog posts, along with a forum and some social networking features.

    We got tasked with upgrading their Drupal 6 site to Drupal 7 along with doing a theme overhaul to match a new (much better looking) design. I built much of the new site and handled the migration of all content and all 2 million users from the old site to the new site.

    I also helped Tim Loudon out with making the site able to withstand the insane traffic that Smosh gets. The interesting thing about Smosh is that much of its traffic is authenticated users which makes traditional methods like Varnish a little trickier since we had to handle the dynamic elements. We used edge-side includes for that as well as MongoDB for some data.

    All in all, a very fun and successful project, and one that I'm proud of.

  • Olympus

    • July 2012 to January 2013
    Team Members: Mike Crittenden, Chad Hester, Ross Nunamaker

    Olympus is one of the largest medical manufacturers in the world. They came to us looking for a site revamp built on Drupal with some fairly complex requirements for product migration and workflow.

    This project was unique in that rather than handing over design mockups, the Olympus design team handed us hand-coded HTML and CSS, which we then took and Drupalized. So theming on this project meant making Drupal match the markup they sent as opposed to theming what Drupal spits out. This ended up being a significant time saver for us as it basically eliminated browser testing/QA and cut down on development time as well.

    The project also came with a non-trivial data migration. Chad Hester did a great job with mapping up the ~100 fields associated with Olympus products to Drupal fields in a way that the node add/edit form wasn't completely overwhelming.

    This was a great project and a great client to work with.
