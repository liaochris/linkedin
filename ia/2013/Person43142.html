Ahmed Tausif Aijazi's Publications

  • Evaluation of Screen Printed Inductive Coils for Wireless Power

    • International Journal of Science and Technology
    • October 2012
    Authors: Ahmed Tausif Aijazi, Sooman Lim

    From small appliances such as a toothbrush to powering a kitchen counter appliances, wireless technology seems to be a power transmission alternative of the future. Although there have been many advances, the technology is not where researchers would like it to be. Cost of the materials and the design of the inductive coils can be improved to make the technology even more viable. Making the technology more cost effective will also have the benefit of expanding its use into lower cost applications. One way to lower the cost of the technology is to print the coils using conductive inks. Further reduction in costs can be achieved by using less expensive metallic inks such as copper instead of silver. Silver is the preferred metallic ink currently being used because when oxidized it is still conductive, whereas copper is not. The objective of this study is to compare the resistivity and inductance of three different printed coil designs for wireless power. The coils were screen printable using copper inks. The printed coils were cured using a Xenon® lamp system. The print attributes of the printed coils were also measured and the contribution of print quality to performance of the ink and coils are presented.
