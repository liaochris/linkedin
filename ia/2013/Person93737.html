Michigan Coalition Against Homelessness' Summary

Michigan Coalition Against Homelessness is a statewide association of housing, shelter, and service providers to the homeless, as well as concerned citizens. MCAH provides information, training and advocacy on issues involving housing and human services. Their mission is to decrease the incidence of homelessness, improve the quality of services provided to the homeless, effect systematic change into the root causes of homelessness and to increase communication amount Michigan's difference homeless services.

Michigan Coalition Against Homelessness' Projects

  • Michigan Statewide Homeless Management Information System

    • 1991 to Present
    Team Members: Michigan Coalition Against Homelessness

    In collaboration with Michigan State Housing Developement, MCAH adminsters MSHMIS which uses Internet-based technology to assist more than 600 service organizations and Continuums of Care. MCAH manages the system and helps to capture necessary information about clients to assit local service agencies.

  • Homeless Awareness Week

    Team Members: Michigan Coalition Against Homelessness

    Homeless Awareness Week occurs every November in an attempt to raise public awareness of issues relating to homelessness in Michigan. MCAH assists local partners in hosting events, reaching out to local officials and generating media coverage.

  • Project Homeless Connect

    Team Members: Michigan Coalition Against Homelessness

    Project Homeless Connect days occur throughout the year, and are one day community events that offer those who are homeless or at-risk of homlessness the opportunity to connect with various service providers in their community. Attendees are often provided with various free services such as meals, haircuts, dental check-ups and others.

  • CTEH AmeriCorps Program

    • 2008 to Present
    Team Members: Michigan Coalition Against Homelessness

    MCAH manages this program that places members with agencies that offer services to homeless or at-risk individuals. They are placed statewide and fufill various critical functions to further the mission of their local host sites and Michigan's Campaign to End Homelessness.
