Debra Yergen's Projects

  • Cardiology growth at Yakima Regional over a 3.5 year period

    • June 2009 to Present
    Team Members: Debra Yergen, Veronica Peery, Corinne Murphy-Hines

    Since 2009, I was responsible for creating and implementing a strategy that ultimately increased market share in cardiology, our top revenue-generating service line, as measured by an independent research firm, in four key areas: In 2010, when residents of a two-county area were surveyed about their preference for cardiac services, 26% preferred Yakima Regional (my hospital) and 43% preferred the competition. By the close of 2012, 37% preferred Yakima Regional and 33% preferred the competition. Drilling down to seniors 50+ (our key demographic), in 2010, 29% of respondents said they preferred our hospital while 40% preferred our competitor. By the close of 2012, 48% reported preferring our hospital while a mere 25% preferred our key competitor. In the market of those new to the area, and not affected by years of local politics, in 2010, 23% picked us and 26% picked the competition. By the close of 2012, 37% reported preferring our hospital while 22% reported preferring the competition. And finally in the Hispanic market, which we have traditionally scored low because we do not offer a family maternity center or pediatrics, and the average age of Hispanics in Central Washington is 26, in 2010, just 19% of respondents said they would prefer our hospital while 46% said they would prefer the competition. By the close of 2012, we were statistically even, with 35% of Hispanics preferring our hospital for heart care and 37% preferring the competition. This was achieved by a combination of media, public relations, grassroots events and activities, and ensuring corporate marketing program pull-through on the local level.
