Barbara Bruce's Summary

I am a facilitator - a mid-wife - helping people to "birth" their new learning. My objectives are to help people live a long and healthy life and to learn life long. My areas of expertise fall into two main categories: Successful Aging and Teaching Teachers to teach.

SUCCESSFUL AGING:
As a Boomer turns 65 every 7 seconds, the world is changing. More people are alive over the age of 65 than in any time in human history. The numbers grow daily. People are more concerned about SUCCESSFUL AGING, particularly in the area of MEMORY ENHANCEMENT and MAINTAINING BRAIN HEALTH. I incorporate a "holistic" approach including Mind/Body/Spirit as we cannot separate these issues out.

In the area of SUCCESSFUL AGING I facilitate seminars on many aspects of brain health including, but not limited to: Care and Feeding of Your Brain, Dealing with Memory enhancement, Stress reduction, the advantages of an Attitude of Gratitude, Multiple Intelligences and the aging brain, creative and critical thinking and a host of other areas ALL linked to maintaining and enhancing brain health. I teach locally and nationally. I teach for Lifespan, Oasis, the JCC, MVP, Road Scholar (formerly Elderhostel) many assisted and independent living facilities near Rochester, NY and around the country. I lead retreats and seminars on these very current and hot topics.

TEACHING TEACHERS:
Teaching is and always will be my passion. I teach teachers and pastors to teach. Currently I teach at the Colgate Rochester Crozer Divinity School and in the Course of Study program through Wesley Untied Methodist Seminary.

I am the author of ten books and countless articles on successful aging, brain health and helping teachers to use proven tools and strategies to enhance their teaching methods to enrich learning at all levels from children to college and seminary.

  • Antioxidents

    Authors: Barbara Bruce

    We hear a great deal about antioxidants, but we may not be sure what they are or why they are important.

    What happens when you cut an apple in half and lay it on the counter? Within minutes it begins to turn brown. The Oxidation process has begun. You can prevent this from happening by simply dipping the cut side of the apple in lemon juice - high in vitamin C. In a similar process your body uses vitamins called antioxidants to help fight the damage caused by oxidation. Your brain produces energy from oxygen and glucose and must get rid of extra oxygen molecules that are known as free radicals. Many scientists think these free radicals are partially to blame for your aging body and brain as well as some diseases including Alzheimer's, cancer and heart disease. Three big ones to be sure!

    There is no right or wrong way to work through a dilemma. How you approach this problem solving task depends on how your brain is wired. For many people a sampling of both approaches offers the best solution.
