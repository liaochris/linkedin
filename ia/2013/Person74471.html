Ken Ramsley's Summary

In Brief:

Using systematic methodologies, I render order out of chaos.

Bio:

Ken Ramsley is a design engineer and planetary geoscience researcher with thirty years of product development, engineering consulting and management experience across multiple industries including medical devices, automotive accessories, scientific instruments, industrial controls, audio and noise-canceling systems, food processing, high-power lasers, aviation and spacecraft communication, avionics, and power systems (five spacecraft on orbit).

Before joining Brown University as a visiting investigator in the School of Engineering, Mr. Ramsley most recently served as chief engineer for electronics and software at AeroAstro, Inc. of Ashburn, VA, a maker of space satellites and related subsystem components.

Activities at Brown University:

In the School of Engineering, I lecture and mentor on subjects related to spacecraft electronics, systems configuration, and orbital mechanics. I also lecture on entrepreneurship, commercial product conceptualization, marketing and corporate culture.

With sponsorship from NASA Space Grant, I organize emerging space technology workshops focused on developments related to micro-spacecraft engineering including miniaturization, flight propulsion and stability, newly enabled missions, and cost sustainability.

In the Department of Geological Sciences, I bring a systems engineering perspective to solar system exploration and contribute to ongoing planetary geological research.
