Matthias Weidlich's Projects

  • NisB - The Network is the Bussiness

    • September 2010 to Present
    Team Members: Matthias Weidlich, Mor Sagmon, Ran Bittmann, Victor Shafran, Avi (Avigdor) Gal, Zoltan Miklos, Roman Maharshak, Eliezer Levy, Boris Otto, Michael Noble, Michele Sesana

    The main goal of the NisB project is to provide ICT support for value networks of SMEs, namely hierarchical supply chains or dynamic business ecosystems, thereby rendering them the primary facilitators of innovative networked businesses. The novelty in NisB is using the network itself as a distributed repository and dispenser of the information and knowledge needed to conduct the networked business. The project provides the scientific basis for this novelty, the business incentives for using it, and a software platform to demonstrate the concept on real use cases.

    The NisB software system provides enterprises a continuum of business networking capabilities that are offered without upfront investment in a ubiquitous and seamlessly usable manner. The basic capability is that of sharing and reusing fragments of interoperability information for the purpose of establishing user-centric understanding of diverse business schemata and vocabularies. This capability is not based on limiting the heterogeneity of the network members by forcing them to adopt some standardized solutions or common ontologies. Instead, the NisB system identifies and exploits bits and pieces of past experiences and practices which will be re-composed and orchestrated to solve new problems. Leveraging this foundational functionality, NisB provides higher level capabilities that promote the dynamic evolution of value networks, thereby enabling business agility in adapting to change and in seizing opportunities. The sharing paradigm of the Future Internet is tapped in both levels. At the basic level, interoperability information is shared and reused along the links of the value networks. In turn, seamless interoperability promotes higher-level collaboration and knowledge sharing. The ultimate goal is to make the value network a tangible asset that in essence ensures business sustainability to the individual enterprise.
