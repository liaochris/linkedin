Dan Sloat's Summary

I am a User Experience designer and manager, delivering compelling product experiences using the latest methodologies, trends, and best practices in the industry. My unique blend of knowledge from multiple domains allows me to synthesize solutions that look beyond the boundaries of one specific topic area.

With 15+ years of professional experience, my deep understanding of user research, visual design, and complex systems has provided organizations such as Lockheed Martin, IBM, GE Healthcare, VerticleNet (now Bravo Solutions), and most recently Roundarch Isobar with highly compelling and technically savvy user-centered solutions.

I have a strong background in project management, collaboration tools, social media, interface design, interaction design, mock-ups and wireframing, educational tools, usability testing, visual design, and software development.

My design philosophy is very much grounded in the user-centered experience; without proper user context, requirements, and empathy a good design cannot emerge. I believe that design is a way of being and an essential means of communication and cultural growth. Experiencing the human condition from a physiological, cognitive, and humanistic perspectives allows us to gain new insights and see the solutions that lie in the negative spaces that exist between all of us.

I am also very interested in the notion of Empathic Design as well as the application of gaming mechanics and narrative as modes of motivating and guiding users. I have recently begun a project co-founding a Healthcare Gamification think tank (Room201.org), dedicated to addressing specific health related issued through the application of creative patient-centric design and gaming techniques. More to come on that!

Specialties: Functional design, visual design, information architecture, usability testing, nursing informatics, creative problem solving, project leadership, web design, visual design, UI/UX, gamification.

Dan Sloat's Publications

  • Indirect Collaborative Evolution for the Facilitation of Group Intelligence in Nursing Care Plan Development

    • ProQuest, Doctoral Dissertation
    • August 1, 2009
    Authors: Dan Sloat

    This research focused on the application of Genetic Algorithm (GA) based methodologies to collaborative problem solving. Direct mechanisms for online asynchronous group work were compared to an indirect constrained group interaction model which was modeled after recombination used in traditional roulette wheel style GAs.

    This new methodology contributes to the existing literature by extending the work in the areas of Interactive Evolutionary Computation (IEC) and Human Based Genetic Algorithms (HBGA) by creating a hybrid approach, Indirect Collaborative Evolution (ICE), employing some of the methodological features found within IEC with the collaborative human-centric features of HBGA. This contribution is important as it presents a new means of facilitating group intelligence through indirect interactions (i.e., ‘cold collaboration) and provides new tools to collaborative rich fields, such as nursing.

    Groups were compared for overall quality of solutions produced relative to tool set and group size as well as the subjective experience of the participants within each group. Cross generational ratings for solutions within the experimental condition was also examined. Findings indicate that the ICE methodology did not perform significantly different from the direct methodology and that group size played an important role in group performance. ICE outperformed direct collaboration with regards to the subjective measures of group efficacy and participant satisfaction. This finding has several implications, including support for ICE as a valid tool. Advantages for indirect collaboration are discussed.
