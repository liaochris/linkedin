Andrew Hoffman's Experience

Executive Director

NeighborLink Fort Wayne

Nonprofit; 1-10 employees; Civic & Social Organization industry

February 2008 – Present (5 years 7 months) Fort Wayne, Indiana Area

I'm responsible for connecting marginalized home owners to community servants. NL's mission is practical, neighbor-to-neighbor expressions of God's love.

Our goal is to mobilize church congregations throughout the city of Fort Wayne to meet the household related needs of our city's vulnerable home owners by tangible acts of kindness and mercy.

I network with social service agencies and local non-profits that serve this population and provide them with a resource that allows them to pass on the needs they are not able to meet. I then network with area churches and local community leadership looking for ways to get involved in serving. We make that connection via our web platform. www.nlfw.org

NeighborLink is one of the easiest and most sustainable ways for anyone to connect to real needs and make an immediate impact on our neighborhoods.

Executive Director

NeighborLink Network

Nonprofit; 1-10 employees; Civic & Social Organization industry

August 2008 – Present (5 years 1 month) Fort Wayne, IN

In 2008, NeighborLink Fort Wayne started licensing our web platform to any interested city or existing community development organization that could sustain it.

The goal of the Network is to help new cities and organizations understand how the system works and help them select the best model for their needs.

I oversee all new NeighborLink developments as well as develop and distribute resources that are relevant to all affiliates and non-affiliates.

The courses focused on how utilizing web-based and creative communication tools are vital to individual and organizational success at compelling us to make a difference in our world. We covered topics such as Guerilla Marketing, social media, web 2.0, and how personal branding all works together towards accomplishing the established goals. The course gave a fundamental understanding of social media onto which students can develop their own understanding of how it applies to their specific major focus.

In a competitive marketplace for college graduates, having the knowledge of how to do a lot with very little by using these accessible technologies will help differentiate them from their competition.
