Christian Behrenbruch's Summary

My professional experience is primarily based in the healthcare domain. I have been involved in starting small companies, medium-sized public company activity as well as large corporate organizations (mostly through acquisition).

I believe we need new models for development of healthcare technologies that more effectively bridge the private/public and for-profit/non-profit divide. To this end, I am a non-executive director of the Brussels-based Oncidium Foundation (www.oncidium-life.org), which is exploring new ways to make cancer therapeutics avaialble to developing economies. I also recently became a non-executive director of Cell Therapies Pty Ltd - a private/public partnership with the Peter McCallum Cancer Center.

I'm passionate about entrepreneurs - they are the change agents in the world.

You can also find my personal web site / blog ("The Modern Mendicant") at : www.behrenbruch.net.

Christian Behrenbruch's Experience

Founder / Chief Executive Officer

ImaginAb, Inc.

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Biotechnology industry

October 2007 – Present (5 years 11 months)

ImaginAb’s mission is to transform the clinical management of cancer and autoimmune diseases via targeted molecular imaging. We re-engineer therapeutic antibodies against biologically important targets into small proteins optimized for diagnostic imaging with Positron Emission Tomography (PET). We believe that in the era of targeted molecular therapy, there is a major unmet need for true companion in vivo diagnostics to help realize the promise of personalized medicine.

ImaginAb received $12.5m Series A financing in March 2012, led by Novartis Venture Funds, syndicated with Merieux Developpment, Nextech and Cycad. The company is based in Los Angeles (Inglewood) with an R&D subsidiary in Singapore.

Fibron Technologies, Inc.

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Nanotechnology industry

June 2008 – December 2011 (3 years 7 months)

Fibron was an early-stage nanotechnology company based on excellent intellectual property developed jointly between the Kaner lab at UCLA and Aerospace Corporation (a non-profit FFRDC company affiliated with the US Air Force). The company's mission was to develop nanostructured forms of conducting polymers that are useful for a variety of cleantech and industrial applications. Operations included a small manufacturing footprint in Finland.

Despite receiving a signed term sheet from Pangaea Ventures and a follower VC, Fibron failed to complete syndication of Series A financing. Both the US and Finnish subsidiary were wound down in late 2011.

Founder and Non-Executive Director

Radius Health Ltd.

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Medical Devices industry

May 2009 – February 2011 (1 year 10 months)

Materials science and nanotechnology has transformed virtually every area of electronics from consumer technology to avionics. Just as solid state technology has enabled flat screen TVs and advanced radar technology, Radius Health (www.radius-health.com) will revolutionize healthcare by replacing bulky, expensive, inefficient and fragile X-ray tubes with a new solid state emitter "on a chip".

Based on groundbreaking patented technology developed at the University of California, Los Angeles, Radius is developing a lightweight x-ray emitter employing Microemitter Array X-rays (MAX) technology - an x-ray source on a chip capable of delivering the same x-ray spectrum as traditional x-ray sources. Our technology delivers x-ray beams from microfabricated structures enabling MAX to be produced using the current generation of semiconductor foundry processes at high volume, quality and low cost.
