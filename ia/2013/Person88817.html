Matthew Bugg's Summary

Qualifications: BA(Hons), DipArch, BArch(Hons),
Registered Professional Bodies; ARB, RIBA

I work for Make architects an employee owned architectural practice based in London. I have experience in office buildings including trading and financial facilities and mixed use schemes including residential. I have worked on a diverse range of projects including the design of stadia, auditorium and laboratories. I am currently working on 5 Broadgate a new headquarter office building for UBS in the City of London.

I have experience leading design teams from feasibility and planning through to construction. I have acquired extensive site experience while working on 5 Broadgate (Make) and HM Treasury (Foster and Partners). I also have international project running experience while working on such projects as the Southern Cross station in Melbourne.

The diversity of my experience illustrates an ambition to innovate and produce new and exciting building design which meets the clients expectations and end user requirements.

I bring excellent excellent project running skills and collaborative thinking to the design process which have been tested on large scale projects with demanding commercial targets.

I also encourage the use mixed media in the design process including animation, photography,sculpture and model making.

Specialties: Office including financial and trading facilities, Masterplanning, Feasibility studies, Private and Multi Residential, Urban design, Historic integration and refurbishment.
Video creation and editing, Website creation, Photography, Architectural systems development.

Matthew Bugg's Experience

partner

Make Architects

Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Architecture & Planning industry

May 2007 – Present (6 years 5 months)

I joined Make in May 2007 and I am currently working on 5 Broadgate a new headquarter building for UBS located in the City of London. I am one of the lead architects on the project responsible for delivering this new building typology which will accommodate super trading floor plates.
I have also contributed to a wide range of mixed use developments including Noho Square for Candy and Candy, a major development on the site of the former Middlesex Hospital in central London.

I have also worked on several international competition schemes, including masterplan proposals for a 20,000-seat arena and entertainment complex for Jerry Bruckheimer in central Las Vegas, and the Taipei Performing Arts Centre, a new state-of-the-art cultural facility in the heart of Taiwan’s capital comprising an opera house, two theatres and a public square.

In addition I have overseen the development of a competition-winning mixed use scheme where the former Bath Press building sits in the UNESCO World Heritage Site. The project retains the existing historic facade in the new design.
