John Horton's Summary

Currently, my focus is on moving LegitScript forward. As the only third party whose standards for online pharmacy verification and investigation are recognized by the National Association of Boards of Pharmacy (NABP), we have unique abilities and credibility in the areas of Internet compliance research and investigations, and online pharmaceuticals market research.

Specialties

Internet compliance, online pharmaceuticals market, prescription drug online marketing, Controlled Substances Act, Food Drug and Cosmetic Act.

John Horton's Experience

President and Founder

LegitScript

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Internet industry

May 2007 – Present (6 years 9 months)

LegitScript reviews and verifies the operations of pharmacy websites (such as Internet pharmacy websites) and the entities controlling those websites to determine compliance with the law and accepted standards of medical and pharmacy practice and ethics. LegitScript is the only organization in this sector whose standards are recognized by the National Association of Boards of Pharmacy for this purpose. LegitScript reviews a broad range of activity touching on issues of prescription drug counterfeiting operations, prescription drug abuse, and pharmaceutical security.

President

Whibse, Inc.

August 2011 – Present (2 years 6 months) Portland, Oregon Area

Whibse is an Internet intelligence and analytics company that bulk-retrieves, parses and outputs Whois, DNS (domain name system) and website reputation information. I lead the company's business and product development.

Associate Deputy Director

Office of National Drug Control Policy

March 2002 – April 2007 (5 years 2 months)

At the Office of National Drug Control Policy, I served as the primary staff person responsible for advising the "Drug Czar" and coordinating federal policy on several issues, including prescription drug-related issues (including Internet pharmacy policy) and several chemical control issues. I authored the Administration's National Synthetic Drug Control Strategy and co-authored the President's National Drug Control Strategy in the years from 2003 until 2007.

Deputy District Attorney

Multnomah County District Attorney's Office

Government Agency; 51-200 employees; Law Practice industry

September 1997 – January 2002 (4 years 5 months)

As a Deputy District Attorney, I worked on cases ranging from homicide, burglary and robbery to narcotics offenses, theft, and other felonies and misdemeanors. The bulk of my work was in the narcotics division.
