Steve Bell's Summary

Steve is the founder of Lean IT Strategies, LLC, an executive coaching firm focused on helping deliver value through strategic alignment, continuous improvement and innovation of IT products, projects and services.

Steve Bell is a faculty member of the Lean Enterprise Institute (www.lean.org).

Steve’s latest book, "Run Grow Transform: Integrating Business and Lean IT" (Productivity Press, 2012) offers a vision of how your enterprise can leverage the transformative capability of Lean IT principles and practices (including Agile, Scrum, ITIL, DevOps, and Lean Startup thinking) to promote operational excellence, accelerate collaboration and learning, enable efficient growth, and drive innovation and customer value.

Steve is co-author of "Lean IT: Enabling and Sustaining Your Lean Transformation" (Productivity Press, 2010) which was honored by the Shingo Prize for Operational Excellence, with their 2011 Research and Professional Publications Award.

Steve's first book "Lean Enterprise Systems, Using IT for Continuous Improvement" (Wiley, 2006) was the first publication to explore the emerging discipline of Lean IT.

In addition to his work leading Lean IT transformation, Steve is the founder of www.Lean4NGO.org their mission is to help make Lean Thinking accessible to humanitarian aid organizations, improving NGO operational efficiency (use of scarce resources) and effectiveness (improved outcomes) to benefit the three billion people living at the "bottom of the pyramid" on less than $2 a day.

Specialties: Lean IT, Strategy Deployment, Kaizen, Value Stream Mapping, Coaching the coaches, Lean Transformation planning and measurement, Continuous improvement of IT service delivery
