John Wilhoit, Jr.'s Publications

  • How To Read A Rent Roll

    • WIN Publishing
    • September 21, 2013
    Authors: John Wilhoit, Jr.

    Do you know all the elements of a rent roll? This book is intended for those who are on the verge or in the throes of buying residential income-producing property. One of your foremost tasks in the acquisitions process is to validate contractual rental revenue. Rental revenue is most often provided to the prospective investor under the terms of a confidentiality agreement. This agreement prohibits from you sharing the information with anyone unrelated to the transaction. The seller will provide you with certain financial data, including the rent roll, for your inspection during the due diligence period. It is up to you to validate this information.

  • Multifamily Insight Volume 1

    • WIN Publishing
    • February 9, 2012
    Authors: John Wilhoit, Jr.

    Multifamily Insight Volume 1 delivers hard hitting facts about how to buy and operate multifamily apartment assets. Multifamily Insight Volume 1 teaches its readers how to apply techniques to increase revenue and control expenses in today's volatile market. It is a reference guide delivering educational content about how to implement real world strategies in professional property management and execute multifamily operations at their highest level of efficiency. Multifamily Insight Volume 1's focus is on multifamily acquisitions, property management, market analysis and real estate finance. It also includes a category discussion about how multifamily and the American economy relate with each other.

    Multifamily Insight Volume 1 readers will learn:
    How to realize gains in incremental income that can make or break profit potential.
    How to implement real world strategies in professional property management.
    How to execute multifamily operations at their highest level of efficiency.
    How to apply techniques to increase revenue and control expenses.

  • Strategic Management of Commercial Real Estate Assets Utilizing Geographic Informations Systems Technology: A Case Study

    • May 5, 1995

The objective of the GIS is to enhance the overall management of real estate assets by providing accessible information for making timely decisions about existing and proposed real estate assets. This GIS represents a cost effective use of technology for leveraging real estate decisions which impact upon the strategic plan.
