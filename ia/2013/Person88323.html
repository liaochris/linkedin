Bob O'Hare's Summary

BOB'S CAREER SUMMARY

Out of college with an engineering degree, Bob O’Hare developed digital computer systems, which included changing vacuum tubes the size of 12 ounce water bottles to repair early Univac computers. Later, he developed communication terminals so we humans could interface directly with computer power. When computers were still physically large, he created the concept of a small, low cost, fully integrated desktop computer, one of the first PCs. Bob is proud to have participated in the development of digital technology which has made the world a smaller, better place.

After a career in digital technology, which utilized his bachelor’s degree in engineering and master’s in computer science, Bob completed post graduate studies in counseling, group process and organization development. He founded Performance Improvement Technologies, Inc. to address the human side of corporate leadership. He spends much of his time as a confidential sounding board for executives, sharing ideas and experience in pursuit of better results. Upper management’s concern about information overload and productivity loss encouraged Bob to seek a solution for these issues.

Bob’s attention moved to the proliferation of Smartphones, iPads, social networking and email management. Through his research, he saw the ever present Smartphone, social desire to be connected all the time and expectation for immediate response in the workplace as too stressful and upsetting work-life balance. He concluded that processing email takes too much of our precious time and email distraction is costing companies millions of dollars. Addressing these issues, Bob developed the MasteringEmail™ methodology to improve the way we process email, unload the overload and empty our inboxes. Bob’s book, Unload Email Overload, enables working professionals to benefit from email and still get home on time for dinner.
Bob and his family live and work in the Philadelphia area.
