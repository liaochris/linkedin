James Jackson's Summary

James has more than 20 years of experience in project management including start-up operations, corporate and military budgeting processes, and advanced skills in human resource management and training. As an enlisted member of the U.S. Navy, James began his professional career managing projects such as the creation of a comprehensive relational database management system for the security detachment on the Rodman Naval Station in the Republic of Panama which included a network system that connected over 25 computer systems and was the first of its kind on the Naval Station.

His efforts earned him a Navy Achievement Medal which he followed up with a second Navy Achievement Medal two years later when he was involved in the creation of a secure communications command center for the United States Southern Command. After his service in the Navy, James worked as a lead project manager on projects for the United States and Panamanian governments as a systems analyst, Geographic Information Systems (GIS) Specialist and lead training manager. After the completion of several successful projects in the Republic of Panama,

James returned to the U.S. and promptly was hired by Harrison College, formerly Indiana Business College and provided project management leadership in the creation of the Technology Programs of the college, creation of a Center for Technology Resources, and the Online Division. He holds a Masters of Education (M.Ed.) in Occupational Training and Development and is working on his doctorate (Ph.D.).

James holds several technology certifications including Microsoft Certified Trainer (MCT), Microsoft Certified Systems Engineer (MCSE), Microsoft Certified Systems Analyst (MCSA), CompTIA A+ Certified Technician, CompTIA Network+ Certified Technician, and is an authorized IC3 Instructor.
