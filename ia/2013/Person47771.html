Geoff Pearman's Summary

I know what it means to have to rethink my career in my fifties having twice lost jobs. I know the experience of applying for jobs, of wanting to regain a job similar to the one I lost so I could to prove I could still do it. The opportunity to rethink the contribution I wanted to make for the next period took me down a new pathway.

I established Partners in Change in 2009 as an organisational and workforce development consultancy. I now work with organisations in Australia and New Zealand who are serious about addressing the risks and opportunities of the ageing workforce. I enjoy the variety of clients I have in sectors as diverse as health, financial services, utilities, transport, aged care and local and central government.

I had also noticed that there was not shortage of reports on the ageing of the population, but what was missing were approaches that addressed the real business issues faced by companies. So was born Staying On, a unique approach that helps organisations understand their risks and develop practical strategies focused on retention, productivity and wellbeing. I am now extending that approach to working with regional and city governments to address the economic risks and opportunities of an ageing population.

My focus is in three areas
1. Ageing Workforces. Assisting organisations to understand the risks and develop practical strategies. Check out Staying On on my website.
2. Ageing and Economic Development. Working with local governments and economic development agencies to address the risks and maximize the opportunities for regional economic growth.
3. Stakeholder Engagement. Assisting organisations to engage more effectively with their stakeholders.

I am an experienced manager, trainer and facilitator, recognised for my strategic and future thinking skills, my ability to initiate and implement complex organisational change and to develop innovative approaches to address complex challenges.
