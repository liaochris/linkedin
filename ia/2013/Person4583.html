Mohamad Ali's Summary

Mohamad Ali is Chief Strategy Officer at Hewlett-Packard.

Previously, Mohamad was CEO of Aspect Workforce, which provides software to optimize people-intensive processes.

Mohamad also served as SVP and President, Avaya Global Services and prior to that, SVP, Corporate Development & Strategy. In this later position, Mohamad drove Avaya's strategy, led acquisitions and divestitures, established key partner relationships, and managed the company's research labs and emerging product business unit.

Before joining Avaya, Mohamad was Vice President of Business Development & Strategy for IBM's Information Management Division where he acquired and integrated key acquisitions, including Cognos Incorporated ($5 billion), FileNet Corporation ($1.6 billion) and Ascential Software ($1.1 billion). Additionally, as IBM's senior state executive, he was responsible for the welfare of IBM's 5,000 employees in Massachusetts. He also served as Vice President of IBM’s EDA software business unit, Vice President of IBM’s Information Applications software business unit, Director of IBM’s E&TS systems services business unit, and Program Director of IBM’s GSM semiconductor business unit.

Prior to joining IBM, Mohamad held engineering and leadership positions in companies including Adobe Systems and Neural Applications Corporation, an artificial-intelligence start-up.

Mohamad holds a bachelor’s degree and a master's degrees in electrical engineering, both from Stanford University. He serves on the board of City National Bank, on the Leadership Council of Oxfam America, and on the board of the Massachusetts Technology Leadership Council. He was named to Boston Business Journal’s 2008 “40 Under 40” list, and recognized by Massachusetts High Tech magazine as a 2011 All-Star.

Specialties: General Management, Mergers and Acquisitions, Acquisition Integration, Strategy, Engineering, Operations, Venture Capital, Startups, Alliances
