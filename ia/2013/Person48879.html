Store Manager

Circuit City

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; CC; Retail industry

August 2001 – December 2003 (2 years 5 months)

As the Store manager of Circuit City I had full accountability for the profitability of the store. This was an end to end position and basically required leadership and strategic execution with the sales, operational and customer service teams. I found that creating a leadership style set the tone for the entire store. The goal was to manage people and processes while inspiring to do their best at providing a customer a experience they would come back again and again and tell their friends about. I also had a responsibility to develop associates into mangers and team leaders all the while focusing on employee and business results, always with the understanding the customer came first. The position also included the following: Lead employee engagement and development efforts, ensuring that employees feel valued, safe and empowered to serve customers, Partner with district leadership to recruit and hire world class employees and cast them into appropriate roles, ensuring they are fully trained and equipped to serve customers, Analyze store performance indicators against company business strategies and goals and lead the team in developing plans to improve the business, Lead regularly scheduled store meetings, attend District/Territory meetings, participate in special projects/initiatives and perform other duties as assigned. The term end to end meant all aspects for the business were under my control, Profit, Pricing, recruitment, Loss prevention, Employee moral and education on our products and services.
