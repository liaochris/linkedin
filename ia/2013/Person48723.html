Ginger B. Collins' Summary

I split my time between the Blue Ridge Mountains of western North Carolina, and Cape Breton Island, Nova Scotia. After a 20+ year career in marketing, sales, and PR, I started writing for pleasure, contributing articles to newspaper travel sections and sailing magazines. Then I switched to fiction, publishing short stories and creative non-fiction online and in anthologies.

In 2010, a side interest in jewelry design took hold and I found myself exchanging the linear art of the written word for the playful art of creating necklaces, earrings, and bracelets as wardrobe accessories. Files with drafts and finished stories have now been replaced by clear boxes of colorful glass beads and polished stones. I dig for treasure at thrift stores and vintage shops, and hunt old jewelry boxes at auctions, searching for unique pieces to repurpose into one-of-a-kind accessories.

I have established a home base for creative projects in a corner cubbyhole inside my husband’s 2,000 square foot hot-rod workshop, and participate in local craft shows and village markets. I’m exploring all the creative things I've never done but have always wanted to try.

I understand the power of personal relationships, the importance of networking, and the way solid marketing can create top of mind awareness. Although retired from the business world, I still participate in community activities and contribute time as a consultant on marketing and advertising projects.

My fiction credits include stories in SilverBoomer Anthology, Freckles to Wrinkles, LunchHour Stories, and the latest Scratch anthology. The personal essay, REASON TO BELIEVE appears in the Voices of Alcoholism healing companion. The Atlanta Journal Constitution, Cincinnati Inquirer, Cruising World, and Living Aboard have published my “slice-of-wave” sailing tales, and I have contributed to Reunions and Real Simple. Read excerpts at www.gingerbcollins.com.
