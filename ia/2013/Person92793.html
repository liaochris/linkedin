Michael Nittmann's Summary

I create technology solutions that are out of the box but right on the money. In doing so I only use common off the shelf (COTS) components, with publicly available documentation.
There is never a 'secret sauce', the customer can take full ownership of the solution.

This helps companies do more with less in a more reliable way.

Questions I do ask:

Frequently found ways to do the 'cannot be done' things.


Consistent trail:

implemented first 'real industry use' VPN over the Internet for The Trane Company in 1994: capital and operational savings compared to a fully owned leased line network of same service level: $4M, $2M/Y operational savings. However, they never had to pay the steep fully owned network...

network architecture: Built two Internet Service Providers from scratch, brought IDT from unusable to perfect (customers called 'what happened'). Implemented natively switched backbone: IP mapped to backbone trunks and circuits, no backbone IP hops, switch latency instead of router latency (factor 100 faster); today only XO has a comparable architecture.
major process re-engineering and database 'revamp' for National Steel: optimized and re-tuned the 'heart of the corporation' database without downtimes (rearranging table spaces, analyzing wasteful queries, etc.)
some 'skunk' security work, in particular one where I did a live break-in (laptop to dial-up IP via phone) at an executive presentation.
'one of a kind' designs, e.g. dual Linux cluster with instantaneous replicating file system, flash signing solution
