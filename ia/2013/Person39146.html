Emily Paulsen's Summary

I love to tell health improvement stories. Over the years, I've told the stories of organizations and individuals working to expand access, improve quality, and increase effectiveness of healthcare for minorities, children, women, senior citizens and others. For example, I recently wrote a series of impact stories for the Indian Health Service’s Improving Patient Care (IPC) Collaborative. I interviewed physicians, nurses, and administrators at 10 different sites from North Carolina to Alaska. Based on what I learned from these interviews, I told the compelling stories of how staff developed “Indian health medical homes” to meet the needs of their communities.

I also love to help people improve their health through clear and practical information that helps patients take charge of their care. I write educational materials for a variety of healthcare organizations, government agencies, and publishing companies.

Having written about health and healthcare for more than 20 years, I understand how challenging it can be to deliver quality care in this fast-changing environment. Working with leading clinicians and experts in their fields, I develop guides to help physicians and nurses integrate best practices into their busy schedules. I also write articles and white papers that discuss important topics in healthcare, such as transitioning to electronic health records, improving patient communications, and fostering greater patient engagement.

My favorite projects are those that combine patient education with tips to improve clinician-patient communication. I developed a series of three toolkits that combined patient education brochures with clinicians guides to how to use those brochures to increase awareness of certain conditions or adherence to recommended treatment. Harvard's Center for AIDS Research sent copies of one of these toolkits to their clinics in Africa to help increase adherence to HIV medication.

Emily Paulsen's Projects

  • Impact Stories from the Improving Patient Care Collaborative of the Indian Health Service

    • March 2011 to November 2011
    Team Members: Emily Paulsen

    In 2006, the Indian Health Service launched an ambitious project to overhaul the way it delivered healthcare to American Indians and Alaskan Natives across the country. Based on the Institute for Healthcare Improvement recommendations, the IHS' Improving Patient Care program aims to reduce health disparities and improve care by delivering high quality, responsive, patient-centered care in "Indian health medical homes" that reflect the needs and the culture of the tribe and the community. In 2011, the IHS hired me to write a series of Impact Stories that illustrate the importance of this program and the profound impact it has made on the health of American Indians and Alaskan Natives.
