Catherine Smith's Summary

“It’s not what you say, it’s the way you say it.”

Little did my mother know when uttering those words to my young self that she was propelling me into a career in voiceovers.

Thanks to that wisdom (sometimes delivered in a scolding tone) I became attuned at an early age how important delivery was to language. I realized that I could say the same words and it could be interpreted in an entirely different manner.

This lead to a degree in performing arts where I learned to add the shading – aging my voice to become a wicked witch, changing pitch and speed to portray a flighty young girl or a seductress, or adding layers like accents or gravelly tones to season my characters. This laid all the right groundwork for a career as a voice talent.

But, let’s face it; being an actress is a tough gig, especially if you don’t want to move from city to city, and job to job. So instead, I soon found myself thriving in the ‘real’ work-a-day world.

A stint in public radio exposed me to the simple, straightforward story telling of that medium and also gave me an appreciation for people who love language and craft as much as I do. My on-air work also brought me my very first job as a voice actor.

Moving to a corporate position with Time Warner allowed me to dabble in marketing, public relations and special event planning and hosting, further expanding my repertoire and helping me understand the client side of the business. It also allowed me to develop my customer service skills.

Throughout my corporate journey, I continued performing on stage, doing on camera acting gigs and working in the new-to-me business of voiceovers. One day it was time to go for it and in March of 2003, I launched into VO work full-time.

Since then, my home-based digital studio has kept me hopping with commercials, narrations, on-hold messaging and e-learning projects along with character work. And the most important lesson I learned is…..

It’s both WHAT you say and HOW you say it.
