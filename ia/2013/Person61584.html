Jen Deaderick's Summary

Using my background in performance, writing, film, and computers, I am working on several projects.

Currently, I am researching my family's relationship with Andrew Jackson for a future book, and side articles along the way.

In addition, I am writing, along with co-author Sarah-Jane Stratford, a graphic novel history of the Equal Rights Amendment. Each chapter to be illustrated by a different artist, in the style of the chapter's time period. We're having a ridiculous amount of fun working on it.

Specialties: Networking and smart-aleck comments.

Junior Navigators

September 2001 – May 2003 (1 year 9 months)

Developed curriculum and structure of the Junior Navigators, a group in Harlem, NY for 7-10 year old girls and boys learning to explore their world. Originally conceived as a more inclusive alternative to the Cub Scouts, the focus was on teaching the kids to become independent agents in a city that could seem overwhelming and intimidating. We always traveled by public transportation, to give a sense of the neighborhood's easy connection to the rest of the city. Among our destinations were museums, movie theaters, parades and landmarks. There was a memorable day touring ABC News, where the Navs got to do their own newscast sitting in the anchor's chair. We also had visitors, including prominent chefs and comedians. The good work continues under my former co-director, Russell Williams , and the head of the umbrella Navigators group, Robin Bossert.

Jen Deaderick's Projects

  • Graphic Novel History of the Equal Rights Amendment

    • October 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Jen Deaderick, Sarah-Jane Stratford

    A linear history of the conception of the Equal Rights Amendment, and the efforts -- still ongoing -- to get it into the US Constitution. Scripted by Ms. Stratford and myself, each chapter to be illustrated by a different artist, in the style of the chapter's time period. Sidebars, written by guest experts, will further explore the Constitution, and the connections between the women's movement and religion, civil rights, and labor organizations.
