Chip Conrad's Publications

  • Capitol City Theater finds new space

    • Statesman Journal
    • May 1, 2013
    Authors: Chip Conrad

    For the past three years, Capitol City Theater was nestled away in the second floor of the Reed Opera House, but as of Wednesday it’s streetside at 210 Liberty St. SE.

    “After the first year, we realized we should be on street level,” said owner Chip Conrad.

    Conrad noticed the group’s new space in February 2012 when it was occupied by Gilgamesh Brewing’s The Lounge. When The Lounge closed in January, Conrad set the wheels in motion to secure the spot for his theater, and for the past month, Conrad and his crew have been renovating the space to suit their needs — adding a lobby, stage and parlor-style seating.

    Conrad started performing in middle school as a puppeteer and skit performance in high school before discovering improv in college.

    He was looking to teach improv after he moved to Salem when he came across the Reed Opera House theater space and thought the location would not only allow him to teach, but also perform.

    Although he was told, “You are crazy, Salemites will never come,” Conrad persevered.

    “When we opened, we had auditions. Ten people maybe showed up, but Julie was one. She was the best in Salem,” Conrad said, referring to Julie Harrison, who has been dubbed the theater’s “Queen Bee of Improv.”

    After a few months, Reverend Buerge, originally from Idaho, joined the crew, then Ripley Sugden came aboard to round out the cast of four.

    Biannual auditions help fill the stage cast, as well as the theater’s “farm team.” The next auditions are in early September.

    “The whole cast has to re-audition every time, even if you are a main stage actor. It’s kind of a big deal,” Conrad said.

  • Salem Sharks

    • June 2013 to Present
    Team Members: Chip Conrad, Michael Hill, AJ Nash, MBA, Tyson Pruett CPA PFS, Jason Brandt

    CCT Events is proud to announce the launch of “Salem Sharks”, a 90-minute live investment event taking place on Saturday, June 1 beginning at 7pm at the Grand Theatre in downtown Salem, Oregon. The “Salem Sharks” event will give budding entrepreneurs the chance to make their dreams come true, and possibly make a business deal that will start them on their way towards success in business.

    Up to 10 entrepreneurial finalists will be given the chance to give their elevator pitch to a panel of handpicked investors willing to invest a minimum of $30,000 in entrepreneurial business plans and individuals they believe in. Official event partners include the Salem Area Chamber of Commerce, SEDCOR, Salem Area Young Professionals, and Chemeketa Center for Business and Industry.

    Each featured entrepreneur will start with a 30 second pitch followed by a chance for handpicked investors on stage to tear into the idea to determine its investment value. Entrepreneurs will also be vying for the people’s choice award which will be given at the conclusion of the event through a mobile vote of event attendees. The winner will receive $25,000 worth in complimentary business start up services to assist them in turning their pitch into a viable business start up.
