Mark J. Blotcky, M.D.'s Summary

Dr. Blotcky has been in the private practice of child, adolescent and adult psychiatry and has taken care of children and their families in Dallas, Texas since 1977.
Dr. Blotcky's Clinical Practice consists of approximately one third children, adolescents, and adults. He has seen thousands of patients with a wide variety of disorders. He provides comprehensive outpatient pschiatric evaluations and multimodal treatments including individual psychotherapy, psychopharmalogical treatment, parental guidance and family therapy.
Dr. Blotcky also conducts Forensic Psychiatric Consultations in civil tort matters and in family custody and possession matters. He is regularly appointed by the Dallas and surrounding area family Courts.He provides consultation and expert opinion in civil litigation regarding a wide variety of tort suits and is retained by both defense and plaintiff attorneys regarding liability and damages.He teaches the psychiatry residents Forensic Child Psychiatry and Evaluation of Young Children at UTSWMS.

Clinical and Forensic Psychiatry

Mark J. Blotcky M.D.

July 1977 – Present (36 years 3 months)

My practice is about 1/3 each children, adolescents and adults and 20% additional time in forensic consultation. I have spoken in many professional organizations over the years and published over 40 papers, chapters and books. I have extensive experience in forensic psychiatry consultations primarily in civil actions and custody evaluations for the family courts. Forensic consultation is only undertaken after the client understands the methodology and data to be used, my professional and ethical neutrality, and a discussion of my thinking about how a psychiatrist would study the relevant issues in the matter. These litigation matters involve a wide variety of serious and disturbing issues including medical malpractice of psychotherapists and psychiatrists, sexual molestation, sexual harassment and other employee allegations of abuse, emotional abuse, hospital malpractice and RICO issues, toxic substances and their psychiatric/health effects, child-proof lighters and children's ability to handle them, damages associated with death of a parent and the alleged wrongful burial of a loved one.

Mark J. Blotcky, M.D.'s Publications

  • Understanding the Forensic Child Interview

    • TCDLA
    • December 3, 2010
    Authors: Mark J. Blotcky, M.D.

    This material reviews the forensic psychiatric interview especially in matters of alleged child sexual abuse. It addresses issues of child credibility within the context of civil or criminal litigation and summarizes the extensive literatuure regarding children's testimony, memory reliability, factors suggestive of true and false allegation as well as reviewing child development regarding memory and recall. An extensive list of the relevant professional literature is provided.

  • Visitation under the Texas Family Code for Children Under 3 and Other Young Children

    • Univ Of Texas School of Law,Frontiers of Family Law
    • January 8, 2010
    Authors: Mark J. Blotcky, M.D.

    “Visitation under the Texas Family Code for Children Under 3 and Other Young Children,” Blotcky, M.J., LaMorgese, B., 2010 Infant Mental Health Advocacy Conference, Texas Association for infant Mental Health, January 8-9, 2010, Dallas, TX deals with the complex needs of young children and their parents. This is an incredibly relevant topic because the Texas Family Code does not adequately guide the Courts regarding their needs.More frequent visits are often indicated.
