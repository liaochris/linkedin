Ed Hasan, MBA, SPHR's Summary

Ed Hasan is a results-driven Human Resources Executive with an impressive record of advancement and achievement. He draws on a unique business-oriented leadership approach to align HR activity with broader corporate initiatives and maximize ROI on all efforts. Ed is an HR leader with a firm grasp of all core HR functions; restructuring organizations, refining business strategy, recruiting topflight talent, enhancing internal communication, and guiding workforces through periods of major change. He has experience in various industries such as Retail, Finance, Hospitality, Healthcare, Transportation, and Education within both for-profit and non-profit sectors.

Ed currently serves as the Executive Director of Human Resources and Administration at the Immune Tolerance Network (ITN), supporting ITN staff at the University of California San Francisco (UCSF), Benaroya Research Institute: Seattle, Washington, Beth Israel Deaconess Medical Center: Boston, Massachusetts, and Bethesda, Maryland. The ITN is an international clinical research consortium funded by the National Institute of Allergy and Infectious Diseases (NIAID), part of the National Institutes of Health (NIH), whose mission is to advance the clinical application of immune tolerance through a unique development model.

Ed also serves as an instructor for George Mason University’s Office of Continuing Professional Education teaching its Human Resource Certificate Program in partnership with the Society for Human Resource Management (SHRM).

Ed received a B.S. in Decision Sciences and Management Information Systems at George Mason University, an MBA from the University of Scranton's Kania School of Management and is a Certified Senior Professional in HR (SPHR).

Ed Hasan, MBA, SPHR's Experience

Immune Tolerance Network

Nonprofit; 51-200 employees; Research industry

January 2008 – Present (5 years 8 months) Bethesda, MD

The Immune Tolerance Network is a National Institute of Allergy and Infectious Diseases (NIAID) funded research consortium designed to advance the clinical application of immune tolerance therapies by evaluating and researching new and improved therapeutic strategies in human disease. The ITN conducts clinical trials and associated studies of the mechanisms for tolerance in kidney, liver and islet transplantations, as well as autoimmune diseases, asthma and allergic diseases. The ITN is comprised of leading scientific and medical faculty from over 50 institutions in nine countries worldwide. It also employs staff between its offices at the University of California San Francisco (UCSF), Benaroya Research Institute: Seattle, Washington, Beth Israel Deaconess Medical Center: Boston, Massachusetts, and Bethesda, Maryland.
