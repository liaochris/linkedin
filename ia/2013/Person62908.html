Jesse Freeman's Summary

For more than 13 years, Jesse Freeman has been on the cutting edge of interactive development with a focus on the Web and mobile platforms. As an expert in his field, Jesse has worked for VW, Tommy Hilfiger, Heavy, MLB, the New York Jets, HBO, and many more. Jesse was a traditional artist for most of his life until making the transition into interactive art, and he has never looked back.

Jesse is an active leader in New York's developer community. He is also involved in the online community as a writer for several development sites including O'Reilly Media, Inc., Adobe Developer Connection, and Activetuts+. He can be found on twitter at @jessefreeman engaging with his 3,800+ followers.

Jesse is a seasoned speaker who has presented at over 15 conferences in the past 3 years. He is comfortable speaking to any size crowd and also does hands on workshops/video tutorials in addition to speaking. Attendance at Jesse’s talks is always high and he has received excellent reviews allowing him to return to each event year after year. You can see a schedule of upcoming conferences and workshops on his site at http://jessefreeman.com.

Jesse is also the author of Introducing HTML5 Game Development: Developing Games with Impact (http://amzn.to/html5gaming), which is published by O'Reilly Media.

Specialties

My specialties are in technology strategy, enterprise application architecture, technology evangelism/speaking, writing, development and art/design.

Jesse Freeman's Projects

  • The Johnny Cash Project

    • 2010 to 2010
    Team Members: Jesse Freeman

    In 2010 I had the privilege of leading development on The Johnny Cash Project website which was built by RadicalMedia in collaboration with Chris Milk, Mr Doob and Aaran Koblin. This was one of the most exciting sites I have ever built.

    The Johnny Cash Project is a living tribute to the Man In Black. To celebrate the release of the last recorded Johnny Cash album they decided to create a special music video in which people are invited to redraw a single frame from the actual video footage so that every night a new music video can be generated based on the user submissions. This way the music video is always changing and evolving based on the community around it. It is something you have to see to believe!

  • HBO Website/Android App

    • 2010 to 2011
    Team Members: Jesse Freeman

    As a Technical Architect at Roundarch I was involved in rebuilding HBO.com‘s online schedule along with helping to maintain and create additional features into the site. Working on HBO was a great opportunity to put my open source component framework, Flash Camouflage, to good use. The Schedule and Feature Menu both take advantage of this framework.

    In addition to my work on the dot com property, I also was involved in architecting and developing HBO’s Android app. I built the UI components along with styling the application.

  • New York Jets Site/Command Center

    • 2005 to 2010
    Team Members: Jesse Freeman

    I have had a long history working with the New York Jets ever since I was brought into help redesign their kid’s website, Generation Jets, in 2004. After that I helped work on the online material for the West Side Stadium campaign. Then in 2006 I coded all the of the Flash components in the NewYorkJets.com website which was redesigned by AKQA before the NFL took over the site in 2010.

    Recently I helped bring the Jets in as a new client for Roundarch where we were engaged to build a 42″ touch screen powered Command Center to help the owner, Woody Johnson, monitor his new stadium during games. This project has been written up by Fast Company, Forbes, Fox Business News and the Wall St. Journal.
