G. Kevin Baker's Summary

Kevin was born in Oklahoma City, Oklahoma. He grew up in the Nazarene Church where his dad was a pastor for over 27 years and met his wife Denise at undergraduate school at Southern Nazarene University.

While attending Duke Divinity School, Kevin and Denise transferred into the UMC and Kevin later entered into full membership in the NC Annual Conference in June of 1997. Having grown up in the extended Wesleyan family, Kevin describes the move to the United Methodist Church as “coming home.” The Bakers have three children: Sarah, Zachary, and Rachel.

During his first pastorate in Creswell, NC, he served as the Vice-President of the Human Relations Council and taught courses on racism and race relations. In 1997, Kevin was one of two pastors appointed to help start a new, multi-ethnic congregation in Durham, NC. On August 1, 2004, Kevin became the Lead Pastor of RUMC. Reconciliation grew to become a bilingual ministry (Spanish and English) that had over 10 nationalities present in the congregation. In June of 2011, Kevin was appointed to First United Methodist in Graham, NC where he presently serves as lead pastor.

In addition to being a full time pastor, Kevin also serves as adjunct faculty for Duke Divinity School, writes curriculum for the UM Publishing House, and has experience with coaching, facilitating outreach and evangelism, integrating technology in ministry, and implementing strategies for short and long term planning in various ministry contexts.
