Mihai Paraschiv's Publications

  • Summarization Methods for Clusters of Geo-Referenced Objects

    • Politehnica University of Bucharest
    • July 2009
    Authors: Mihai Paraschiv

    Exploring large collections can be simplified by creating summaries based on subset characteristics. In the context of geo-referenced objects, an area would be represented by those items which best approximate its distinguishable properties. This paper describes the most important factors influencing the quality of summarization methods. Some of the aspects are studied by evaluating a prototype application.
    Like many applications that focus on creating region overviews, the one described in this paper uses a hierarchy of clusters. On this structure, a ranking algorithm which considers textual patterns and user scores is applied to find the best items.
    The analysis is focused on photo collections because the summarization methods are most easily applied to them. Other types of sets which can benefit from the algorithms described in this paper are those made up of events, videos, places and news articles.

  • News Topic Detection and Tracking

    • Politehnica University of Bucharest
    • February 2011
    Authors: Mihai Paraschiv

    Today, the news publishing landscape goes through major transformations. On one hand, we see that the number of subjects is increasing dramatically. On the other hand, there is a significant shift toward expressing points of views and consuming content using social interaction services.
    These changes require new means to sift through vast quantities of information. We propose a new strategy for news exploration that is based on tracking topics over long periods of time. The core of our approach is a modeling scheme that gathers news articles and social reactions in a two level hierarchy: stories and topics. The purpose of the first level is to centralize views on an event or a discussion theme. The second level is used to study the evolution of subjects by discovering and following trends.
    We assess the characteristics of the clustering scheme with a distributed system that can be extended for real life use. At the center of this system, there is a processing pipeline which applies the grouping methods in an online fashion.
    Our evaluations show that the proposed mechanisms are very promising and can be applied to a wide range of purposes, such as personalized topic summarization, trend discovery and novelty detection.

  • A Topology - Based Directed Acyclic Graph Generation Strategy

    • Politehnica University of Bucharest
    • 2010
    Authors: Mihai Paraschiv

    Directed Acyclic Graphs (DAGs) are used in various fields of research. Generating these types of graphs has been studied mainly from the perspective of node and link constraints. This paper describes a strategy for building DAGs that focuses on their global structure. First, we look at constructing a node net. Starting from the desired properties of the graph’s topological ordering, we build an envelope in which to place the nodes. Dependencies are then associated based on a set of given criteria. Afterwards, we focus on ways of recursively applying the algorithm in order to create a wide range of graphs. Finally, we discuss the implementation of a proof of concept application and possible improvements.

  • Agent-Based Implementation of a P2P Publish/Subscribe System

    • Politehnica University of Bucharest
    • 2010
    Authors: Mihai Paraschiv, Alexandru Stefanescu, Adela-Diana Almasi

    The publish/subscribe paradigm offers many advantages for communication in unstructured decentralized peer-to-peer networks. One of the models that fit into this paradigm is topic-based event dissemination. This paper presents an agent-based system for publish/subscribe inspired by TERA and implemented on top of JADE. We describe the system’s design and implementation and show several performance results obtained in a real-life setting.

  • Analyzing Topic Evolution in News Collections

    • Politehnica University of Bucharest
    • May 2010
    Authors: Mihai Paraschiv

    The extraction of information from news articles has been studied in a large number of projects. Usually, these were focused either on discovering and tracking topics or on assessing the importance and novelty of content sections. This paper describes a framework on which both type of systems can be built and proposes a feature scoring method based on trend detection.
