Ismail Hameduddin's Summary

I am an engineer, researcher and scholar-in-training. I am currently at the Johns Hopkins University doing my PhD work under the guidance of Prof. Dennice Gayme. My current research interests are in hydrodynamic stability and transition to turbulence. Specifically, I am interested in using control-theoretic approaches to model and predict various transition phenomena in fluid dynamics.

I have previously been associated in a research capacity with KAUST (with Prof. Christian Claudel), Purdue Unviersity (Prof. Kartik Ariyur) and King Abdulaziz University (Prof. Abdulrahman Bajodah). My work at these institutions included traffic control via the LWR/Burgers partial differential equation, distributed flight control for aeroelastic systems, and developing novel nonlinear flight control laws. At King Abdulaziz University, I also played a significant role in setting up an advanced research aerospace controls lab.

I graduated (BS) cum laude from the University of Missouri (Columbia) with departmental honors in Mechanical Engineering and also a minor in Mathematics.

Specialties: fluid dynamics, 1D conservation laws, traffic systems, flight dynamics and control, aeroelasticity, fluid dynamics, technical writing/communication.

Certified LabVIEW Associate Developer (CLAD), MATLAB/Simulink, LaTeX/BibTeX, Fortran 90+, Linux/UNIX.

Ismail Hameduddin's Publications

  • Nonlinear generalised dynamic inversion for aircraft manoeuvring control

    • International Journal of Control
    • February 1, 2012
    Authors: Ismail Hameduddin, Abdulrahman H. Bajodah

    The nonlinear control problem of aircraft trajectory tracking is tackled in the framework of multiple linear time-varying constrained control using the newly developed paradigm of generalised dynamic inversion. Numerical simulations of an aggressive multiaxial aircraft coordinated manoeuvre verify the efficacy of designing nonlinear flight control systems via this methodology.
