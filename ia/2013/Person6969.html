Andrew Kahn's Summary

Andrew was eight years old when his family got its first computer, which instantly gave Andrew's writing a sense of legitimacy. He awoke at hours he now finds ungodly to write recaps of entire New York Yankees seasons (yes, the Yankees -- their seasons were typically longer than his beloved Mets').

Since, he has founded his own blog and written for several major publications, such as The Wall Street Journal and ESPN the Magazine. He has covered games at Madison Square Garden and Citi Field.

In his current full-time job as Editor at the Dana Foundation, Andrew reads and writes about neuroscience, occasionally making connections to the sporting world when appropriate -- such as concussions in the NFL or the neural mechanisms behind "choking" golfers.

Specialties

Profiles, Interviews, Features, Game Stories, Human Interest Pieces
