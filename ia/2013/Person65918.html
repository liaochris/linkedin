Johan Ljung's Experience

Head of PMO

Klarna AB

Privately Held; 501-1000 employees; Financial Services industry

September 2012 – Present (1 year 1 month) Stockholm, Sweden

Building a world-class PMO at a world-class company. Come chat with me in San Francisco on September 26!

Head of PMO

Eniro

Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; Marketing and Advertising industry

March 2011 – August 2012 (1 year 6 months) Stockholm, Sweden

When Eniro's Group Service Delivery division was formed through the merger of Group IT and Business Operations, I was given the task of building a new Program Management Office, responsible for the tactical planning and operational management of Eniro's corporate development portfolio. We were initially 13 project, program and portfolio managers, in Stockholm, Oslo and Copenhagen, with me as department head. I was a member of the divisional management team, reporting to the SVP Group Service Delivery.

Member of the Board

AlltOmMotor Bilweb Eniro AB

Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; Marketing and Advertising industry

June 2010 – June 2012 (2 years 1 month) Stockholm, Sweden

AlltOmMotor Bilweb was a wholly owned subsidiary of Eniro, set up to publish the Swedish classifieds site for new and used cars, www.bilweb.se.

Online Program Manager and Head of PMO

Eniro

Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; Marketing and Advertising industry

February 2009 – February 2011 (2 years 1 month) Stockholm, Sweden

As Online Program Manager, I acted as the chief liaison officer between Eniro Group IT and the Products & Services Division (responsible for the development and marketing of eniro.se, krak.dk, kvasir.no, gulesider.no, eniro.fi, etc.). I was a member of both organisations' management teams, reporting to the group CIO. My primary responsibility was to manage the overall IT development portfolio for Eniro's online services - ensuring that IT understood the business requirements, finding synergies between initiatives, and optimizing the throughput of IT development.

From March 2010, I was also Head of the Nordic Program Management Office at Group IT, then comprising 11 senior project managers in Sweden and Denmark.

Senior Project Manager

Eniro

Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; Marketing and Advertising industry

March 2007 – January 2009 (1 year 11 months)

As project manager at Eniro Sverige Online, I led a couple of high profile product development projects at eniro.se, most notably the launch of "Eniro Super Search" and the integration of Eniro's maps with the leading Swedish real estate listings site, hemnet.se. I also managed the final phase of a major outsourcing project, whereby all of Eniro Sweden's IT operations and support were transferred to HP.

Product Manager

Cision

Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; Public Relations and Communications industry

January 2005 – February 2007 (2 years 2 months)

My last position at Observer was as the Nordic product manager for "Monitor" - the largest of Observer's five product areas, representing well over half of the company's revenues. As product manager, I was a member of the Nordic Business Development department, focusing comparatively more on product development than on product marketing. The single product I worked most with, was Observer's WebAgent, an appreciated and fast-growing module within the Observer Online platform, later rebranded CisionPoint.

The Observer Group changed its name to Cision worldwide in April 2007. Cision operates within reputation management and media intelligence. Cision has nearly SEK 1.8 billion in sales and 2,700 employees in the US, UK, Sweden, Canada, Germany, Norway, Finland, Denmark, Portugal and Lithuania.

Business Development Manager

Observer AB

May 2001 – December 2004 (3 years 8 months)

Project manager

Romeike

Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; Public Relations and Communications industry

November 2000 – May 2001 (7 months) London, United Kingdom

After the Observer Group acquired the UK's leading media monitoring agency, Romeike & Curtice, I was relocated to the London office to lead and support various process integration and harmonization projects. I reported to the MD of Romeike.

Software Developer & Project Manager

Sifo Group Guidance

1997 – 2000 (3 years) Stockholm, Sweden

Sifo Group Guidance was formed as a joint venture between Sifo Group (later Observer Group) and Guide Konsult. My first project there concerned automated digital monitoring of TV broadcasts, for which I developed a prototype system, using MATLAB. I then worked as systems architect and technical project manager. In particular on a big long-term project implementing scanning, OCR and electronic search technology at Observer Norway
