Bonnie Gabriel's Experience

Founder/Director

Words for Lovers

February 1995 – Present (18 years 8 months)

Bonnie Gabriel is a published author, sex educator, radio show producer/host and former college counselor. She is best known for her acclaimed Bantam/Doubleday book, "The Fine Art of Erotic Talk: How to Entice, Excite and Enchant Your Lover with Words". (The book has also been published in German as "Worte Der Lust: Die Kunst des erotischen Sprechens" Schwarzkopf and Schwarzkopf,, publishers)

Bonnie holds an MA in Counseling from New York University and has taught at Baruch College, The New School for Social Research, New York University, U.C. Berkeley Extension, San Francisco State University, and other educational institutions. She currently is a columnist/writer/reviewer for relationship issues and the performing arts as well as a professional speaker.

Bonnie Gabriel's Publications

  • The Fine Art of Erotic Talk: How to Entice, Excite & Enchant Your Lover with Words

    • Random House
    • July 1, 2010
    Authors: Bonnie Gabriel

    “A whispered term of endearment, a flirtatious phrase, a secret suggestion, a cry of passion – words can be the most intimate gifts that lovers share. This book pays tribute to their erotic power and shows how to use words to arouse desire, to communicate wishes, to reveal and fulfill fantasies, and to infuse lovemaking with romance and fire. Based upon counselor Gabriel’s popular workshops, it is designed both for singles and couples: to help singles navigate the shoals of attraction, budding intimacy, and safe sex; and to inspire couples to creative expressions of love and ardor – from offering and enjoying pleasure to “talking dirty” to playing out new erotic roles – through explicit examples, sample fantasy scripts, “naughty” games, and techniques for nurturing and sexual healing. It also offers help for those too shy or fearful to give full voice to their intimate desires. Inspiringly sensual and warmly encouraging, THE FINE ART OF EROTIC TALK will help lovers craft an ongoing amorous conversation – both to deepen intimacy and to keep the thrill alive.”
