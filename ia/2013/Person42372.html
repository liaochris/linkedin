Eric Miller's Summary

I have deep experience creating and managing software organizations, played key business development and executive management roles in several successful start up companies, and have been a leader in energy software and hardware product development, sales, wholesale energy trading, retail electricity, marketing, energy project development, finance, and public advocacy for many years.

I am currently focused on growing my successful development services business and investing in early stage companies that are able to leverage our software development, enterprise architecture, and energy market expertise

Specialties: Software Development, Enterprise Architecture, Marketing and Sales, Executive Management, Project and Venture Finance, Entrepreneur

1999 – 2008 (9 years)

Itron is a leading global provider of gas, water, and electricity metering, data collection systems, and software for data collection, data management, and analysis.

Mr. Miller was responsible for the global software business. This included over 20 software products, serving more than 2000 customers in markets on 5 continents. His team exceeded 350 people in 5 development centers world wide. Vice President Strategy

Silicon Energy, Inc.

1999 – 2003 (4 years)

Silicon Energy was a successful, venture back energy management software company for large companies. It grew quickly from its founding to over $20 million in revenues and served top global clients with advanced energy data collection and analysis software. The company also conceived and pioneered one of the first energy curtailment management and Meter Meter Data Management systems, both of which have become new core elements of utility infrastructure world wide. The company was purchased by Itron in 2003.

As employee 15, Mr. Miller was responsible for product strategy, product management, sales support, and led their successful entry into the global utilities market, and led the due diligence and integration for 2 acquisitions.

Founder, CEO

Foresight Energy

July 1996 – March 1999 (2 years 9 months) Larkspur, CA

Foresight Energy was an early entrant into the then newly deregulated retail electricity sector. Foresight was a leader in establishing the green retail energy industry, first as a direct retailer and then as a wholesale power trader serving green energy retailers with wholesale energy supplies including customer such as PG&E Energy Services and the Northern California Power Authority. Foresight played a pivotal role in the establishment of green energy power definitions, certification processes, and business models.

Mr. Miller co founded the company and served as its first CEO.

Vice President, Business Development

Kenetech

August 1990 – June 1996 (5 years 11 months) San Francisco, CA

Kenetech at the time was the largest developer and manufacturer of wind turbines in the US. Mr. Miller was responsible for all aspects of sales, regulatory affairs, land acquisition, permitting, community affairs, and permitting in the southwestern US and California. He developed and sold a 60 MW project to the Sacramento Municipal Utility District, then the largest direct utility owned wind project in the world, he also led an $80 million redevelopment of the core resource area of Palm Springs as well as numerous acquisitions in the California wind energy market. At one time Mr. Miller had over 25 projects comprising 1100MW under active development, with a team of over 30 people. Contributing significantly to the Companies successful IPO. Mr. Miller also played a key role in the development of renewable and energy efficiency policy in California for over a decade.

Vice President, Environmental Services

AES

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; AES; Utilities industry

June 1984 – August 1990 (6 years 3 months) Arlington, VA

Mr. Miller served a variety of roles as employee 25 of AES. These included a project manager in the energy consulting group, building advanced forecasting models for long range strategic planning for electric utilities; as assistant project director lead all on site development (real estate permitting, community relations, political affairs) for a $450 million power facility located in Hawaii. The development also required completion of a deepwater harbor and conveyor system, also led by Mr. Miller. The project remains one of the companies most successful projects; and founded an internal start-up division focused on landfill methane recovery projects.
