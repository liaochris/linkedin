Helena Richardson's Summary

Helena Richardson a native of New Orleans; moved to Richmond Virginia with her husband, daughter and loving family pet, after the aftermath of Hurricane Katrina, in August 2005.

Mrs. Richardson received her bachelor's degree in Computer Information Systems from Loyola University New Orleans and continued to further her education by acquiring several certifications in the Information Technology (IT) industry. Prior to Hurricane Katrina, she worked at NASA’s Stennis Space Center in Mississippi, the nation’s largest test site for rocket engines as a Senior Level Application Developer.

As an IT professional with with over 20 years of experience and having the passion to become an entrepreneur, she often provided computer services to family and friends. Both the storm and the recession provided her the opportunity to start her own business and make money doing something she loves.

She also designed and developed complex system applications from Cobol/CICS Legacy Systems to data-driven Web Application Platforms. Her clients ranged from small to large businesses, government and non-profit institutions.

"The recession has caused individuals and businesses to be more selective than ever when choosing services" says Mrs. Richardson; to her each client brings a unique set of needs that requires her to stay focus and provide the most excellent service large or small to her clients with integrity and efficiency.

Her main goal and focus is to be a positive resource for her clients and she feels this will make people want to do business with her.
