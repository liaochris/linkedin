Daniel Simon's Publications

  • The Timeless Racer ®

    • Design Studio Press
    Authors: Daniel Simon

    The Timeless Racer™ is a large-scale picture book series about a racing world that could have existed between 1916 and 2615. Simon will show racing machines in impeccable detail, including their heroic drivers and tales. It kicks off now, with the over-sized book ‘Episode 1 – year 2027.’ At a 12″x14″ (30.5cm x 35.5cm) and 72 pages, it offers the best way to see Simon’s maniacal attention to detail, with rendering up to 50 megapixel. With a foreword by racing legend Jacky Ickx, praises by Top Gear and GQ magazine, text in English, French and German, and three cars in full detail, it is a nifty launch into an unseen world. Episode 2 and more products are planned.

  • Cosmic Motors™

    • Design Studio Press
    Authors: Daniel Simon

    Cosmic Motors changed my life. It compresses seven years of passionate design work into 176 pages. It is a blend of my experience as senior designer at VW and Bugatti and my passion for drawing, science fiction and 3D modeling. It sold over 30,000 copies and paved my way to Hollywood and other seductive projects. Click this link to have a look at amazon.
    My second book project launches 2013.

Daniel Simon's Experience

Founder & Creative Director

Daniel Simon LLC

2011 – Present (2 years) Los Angeles

The mother ship of my creative adventures, with the current focus on finishing my newest book publication, in stores May 2013.

Workshop Leader

Audi AG

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; VWV; Automotive industry

November 2012 – November 2012 (1 month) Ingolstadt

Enrolled by Head of Audi Exterior Design, Achim Badstübner, I ran a one week workshop for a group of Exterior Designers at Audi Design, Ingolstadt. Theme: Fast and unorthodox shape development in Alias and fast photo-real visualization in Bunkspeed. It concluded with a presentation of the team results to a large number of Audi Design Team members. A powerful week, thank you Audi Design for the trust.

Livery Designer | Lotus LMP2 Le Mans Team

Lotus Cars

Privately Held; 1001-5000 employees; Automotive industry

2012 – 2012 (less than a year)

An enjoyable collaboration with the Lotus LMP2 Team and their team manager Colin Kolles. I developed the race livery design of their race cars, including digital press release images, for the 2012 World Endurance Championship, with the highlight race being the 24 Hours of Le Mans. The car managed a pole position at the final race in Japan. The color theme is a homage to the iconic JPS livery and carries the Daniel Simon logo on mirror and cabin. A new car is in development for 2013.

Vehicle Designer | Turbo

DreamWorks Animation

Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; DWA; Animation industry

2011 – 2011 (less than a year)

Director David Soren appointed me to design a very realistic yet animation film fitting version of an Indycar, to be featured in various colors in the 2013 Dreamworks Animation feature film 'Turbo'. It came at a point when Indycar design was about to change, so we based my design on a prototype car that was provided by the race organization. Thank you David and Dreamworks.

Lead Vehicle Designer | TRON: Uprising

The Walt Disney Company

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; DIS; Entertainment industry

2010 – 2011 (1 year) Burbank

Appointed Lead Vehicle Designer, I was responsible to create some and oversee all vehicles in Disney's TV animation Tron:Uprising. Working directly with director Charlie Bean, I started by myself and gradually supervised the vehicle artists joining the show. What a great project, thank you Charlie and all artists for your vision.

Vehicle Designer | Oblivion with Tom Cruise

Universal Pictures

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; GE; Entertainment industry

2010 – 2011 (1 year) Burbank

My second assignment by director Joe Kosinski. I started designing directly for him for his pitch to studios in 2009, as part of a graphic novel illustrated by Andrée Wallin. When an art department was formed after that, I continued under the lead of production designer Darren Gilford.
When Tom Cruise signed on as lead actor, his experience as a pilot went first hand into my design process. During several meetings we adjusted details and functions. A full-size version was built under my design supervision by Wildfactory, whom I previously work with on Tron: Legacy. Best shop ever. I additionally contributed the exterior design of the Drones. All work delivered in 3D Alias data.
Thank you to director Joe Kosinski for this extraordinary work experience.

Vehicle Concept Designer | Ridley Scott's Prometheus

Fox Filmed Entertainment

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; NWS; Entertainment industry

2010 – 2010 (less than a year) Bevery Hills

Hired by Oscar-nominated production designer Arthur Max, I was responsible to develop early package and chassis studies for functional vehicles in 20th Century Fox's feature film "Prometheus". Director and role model Ridley Scott was regularly involved, it was an honor to work with him on one desk.

Lead Vehicle Designer | Captain America: The First Avenger

Marvel Entertainment

Public Company; 201-500 employees; DIS; Entertainment industry

2009 – 2010 (1 year) Los Angeles

Appointed Lead Vehicle Designer under the lead of production designer Rick Heinrichs, I created from first concept sketch to final 3D seven vehicles for the Marvel feature film 'Captain America: The First Avenger': The Schmidt Coupe, the Flying Wing, the Flying Bomber Drone, the Hydra Train, the Submarine, the Hydra Motorcycles and a Mini-Tank.
I worked at the Raleigh Studios in Los Angeles hand in hand with director Joe Johnston and Oscar-winning production designer Rick Heinrichs.

Vehicle Concept Designer | TRON: Legacy

The Walt Disney Company

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; DIS; Entertainment industry

2008 – 2009 (1 year) Burbank

Hired as concept vehicle cesigner by director Joe Kosinski and production designer Darren Gilford, I created vehicle designs from sketch to the final detail refinement in post-production, and oversaw some vehicle set builds for this feature film, including the Light Cycle, the Vintage Light Cycle, the Light Runner, the Light Jet, the Big Light Jet, and a few background vehicles. I was involved in hand-over meetings with post production company Digital Domain to ensure the perfect look of the vehicles in the final film, and participated at press junkets in Los Angeles, Paris, London and Berlin, helping to promote the design aspect of this project.
