Larry Weintraub's Summary

What do I do?
I am CEO and co-founder of Fanscape, the leading and longest running pure-play Social Media Marketing Agency. Nearly 15 years ago we started this company with a mission to connect consumers with the brands they love. We had a simple idea, make people feel a part of the marketing process and they will do the marketing for you.

I'm a customer-centric marketer with a strong belief in listening to the customer to know what they want. 'm a fan of music, movies, sports and many brands and products and I love it when my favorite brands look to me for my input. Which is the basis for the company I co-created. As a result, we're leaders and innovators with a great reputation for helping our clients better connect with their customers.

What did I do before?
Prior to founding Fanscape, I was in the music business. I spent 8 years as VP, Artist Development and A&R for A&M Records. I helped launch and participate in the careers of artists such as Sheryl Crow, Gin Blossoms, Blues Traveler, Soundgarden, Sting, and, MxPx. I oversaw am amazing staff and a multi-million dollar annual touring and recording budget for all the label's artists. Additionally, for over 20 years I managed and helped guide the careers of numerous musicians including Korn, The Offspring, Social Distortion, and Taking Back Sunday.

I was born an entrepreneur and started a food service business at 14, promoted my first concert at 15 and created a merchandise company at 18. I attended the University of California in San Diego where I majored in Economics and Literature and booked concerts for 4 years.

How can you reach me?
Just send me a note at LarryW@Fanscape.com.

Specialties: Social Media Marketing, Digital Word of Mouth Marketing, Online / viral / engagement marketing, event management, personnel management, finance, brand identity

Larry Weintraub's Experience

CEO

Fanscape

Public Company; 51-200 employees; Marketing and Advertising industry

August 1998 – Present (15 years 3 months) Greater Los Angeles Area

Fanscape is a leading Social Media Marketing Agency with nearly fifteen years of experience helping clients reach and activate consumers and influencers to foster positive word-of-mouth sentiment about their products. Fanscape’s social marketing experts have in-depth understanding of niche audiences and consumer behaviors and are driven to increase our clients’: brand identification, share-of-voice, and sales. We specialize in the online marketing of products and brands. Our clients include established brands, top advertising agencies, and every major entertainment media company.

We provide highly measurable, earned media strategies for major corporations and leading entertainment, gaming, travel, automotive, and consumer package goods companies. Our mission is to lead the field of Social Media Marketing, keep developing innovative and creative methods of inspiring word-of-mouth conversations, foster positive sentiment for our clients, provide detailed measurement and analysis, generate the highest Return on Investment (ROI) possible and to listen, educate and activate consumers. This results in forging trusted relationships between our clients and their customers.

Along with my business partner, Terry Dry, I co-oversee the entire operation of Fanscape. This includes all marketing, sales, finance, personnel, and growth.

Fanscape is also the social media practice for The Marketing Arm, an Omnicom marketing and promotions agency.

Vice President, Artist Development and A&R

A&M Records

Privately Held; 201-500 employees; Music industry

January 1991 – January 1999 (8 years 1 month)

As Vice President of Artist Development I oversaw a department of 8 people that was responsible for all personal appearances and touring-related activities of every act on the A&M Records roster.

As Vice President of Artists and Repertoire (A&R) I was in charge of signing acts to the label and the making and marketing of albums.

Partner

Rebel Waltz Management

January 1991 – December 1994 (4 years) Greater Los Angeles Area

I started managing bands when I was 16 years old (mainly because I couldn't play an instrument, so this was the next best thing to being on stage!). In 1991 I started working with music business legend Jim Guerinot and together we ran a personal management company responsible for the careers of Social Distortion, Korn, and The Offspring. Let's just say it was quite a bit different managing multi-platinum world-wide mega stars than managing my friends' band in high school. And I'd have to say, I was really good at it.

Assistant Manager

Music Plus

September 1985 – December 1987 (2 years 4 months) Westwood and Santa Monica, CA

My desire to work in the music business started at a very young age. So as you can imagine, my dream job at the age of 16 was to work in a record store. And during the 1980s, Music Plus was one of the largest music and video (VHS and Beta thank you!) chains in Southern California. I worked here when I was in High School and during college summers and holiday vacations. I started as a clerk and quickly rose to becoming assistant manager of both the Westwood and Santa Monica stores. I'd probably have become a super successful store manager too if that whole college thing hadn't gotten in the way.
