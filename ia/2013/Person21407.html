Cindy Edwards' Publications

  • An Evaluation of Wiki Implementation in a Teacher Education Course

    • Teacher Education Quarterly
    • May 2010
    Authors: Cindy Edwards, Cynthia Gautreau, PhD

    The use of web based collaborative technologies, such as wikis, in pre-service teacher education programs continues to evolve. As new technologies emerge, faculty design instruction to integrate technology resources to promote technology skills among new teachers through practical application strategies and concrete experiences. This paper provides insight to an assignment completed by pre-service educators who used wikis during their first semester of credential coursework. This paper reveals the integration process, and benefits and drawbacks of wiki implementation in a hybrid course. The authors make recommendations for faculty who are considering the integration of wikis into teaching education courses.

  • The Use of Wikis to Support Informal Learning for Geographically Dispersed Project Teams

    • CSU Fullerton
    • May 2009
    Authors: Cindy Edwards

    In a U.S. workforce heavily populated by ready-to-retire baby boomers, knowledge obtained only through years of practical experience is becoming increasingly scarce, and the need to capture that knowledge is becoming paramount to ensure smooth operational transitions to a new generation of experts. This paper assesses the feasibility of using wiki applications to support project team collaboration in an informal learning forum. Introducing new generation social software tools as a knowledge management system can bring resistance from an aging workforce. Meeting that resistance with Web 2.0 technology will provide flexibility and agility needed to gather, manage and nurture rich expert knowledge to pass on to future generations of experts.
