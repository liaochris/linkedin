Mathew Ingram's Summary

In 2010, I left the paper to become a senior writer at GigaOm, one of the leading U.S. technology blog networks. Prior to that I was the new media reporter for the Globe, and then the paper's first "communities editor," with responsibility for helping writers and editors learn how to use social-media tools to connect with readers.

I started writing for the Globe in 1994 and wrote about the stock market for the Report on Business section, then moved to Calgary in 1996 and wrote about Western Canadian business -- primarily the oil and gas industry -- and in 2000 moved back to Toronto to become the Globe's first online business columnist for the relaunched globeandmail.com. I started the Globe's first blog in 2004 and began writing primarily about technology and the Internet.

I'm also a co-founder of the mesh conference, one of Canada's top Web conferences.
