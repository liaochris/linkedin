Laneth Sffarlenn's Summary

"Imagination is more important than knowledge..." -Albert Einstein.

I would be what you'd call a 'Jack-of-all-Trades', though I much prefer the term Renaissance Man. Someone who seeks not to specialise in any one field but rather become adept across a broad range of topics.

I take great pride in my ability to learn very quickly and my attention to detail and I seek to employ these qualities in everything I do.

I've a burning desire to help people and so often find myself fulfilling an administrative role, either professionally or privately, doing anything from web design to social media management to proofreading to letter writing.

My dream job would be to work as a virtual assistant for someone that works in new media or the creative arts.

Acting, singing, writing, content curation and creation on YouTube, meditation and photography are just some of the things that occupy my private life.

Laneth Sffarlenn's Experience

Director

SMY Studios Pty Ltd

October 2012 – Present (1 year) Melbourne Area, Australia

SMY Studios Pty Ltd was designed and created as an entity to grow the Melbourne YouTube community.

The goal is to become a base for formalising and learning the best practices of the video and Internet platform and community effort that is YouTube.

Operations and Marketing

Rocket Registrations

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Legal Services industry

September 2012 – January 2013 (5 months) Melbourne Area, Australia

Primarily focused on developing a social media presence and marketing plan for Rocket as a new startup, I was tasked with assisting the marketing director, specifically in creating a blog-based marketing platform.

Rocket takes the idea of a 'paperless' future and applies it to the corporate registry / compliance sector of Australia's business world, with the aim of assisting everyone from the work-at-home small business owner to the high-end entrepreneurs and investors.

With custom-built technology and documentation designed specifically for Rocket, registering a company or forming a trust fund couldn't be simpler, or faster!

My daily tasks involved the monitoring of our social media assets, working on creating content plans and also developing that content, projecting potential future partnerships and associations with businesses and charities and conceptualising marketing hooks for specific industries. Having left due to personal reasons, I was unable to see much of my projected work through, though I believe we will see it come to light across the next couple of years of Rocket's future.

Administration

Castle Corporate Pty Ltd

August 2011 – September 2012 (1 year 2 months) Box Hill, VIC

"Castle Corporate provides business structures of all types, including companies, trusts and superannuation funds, using the latest electronic facilities backed up with complimentary support and our highly-regarded post-formation service." - http://www.castlecorp.com.au

When Norfolk Corporate Support was acquired by Castle Corporate, I jumped at the opportunity presented to work for a larger corporate service provider. Seeking to grow my skills and diversify my knowledge base, I saw Castle as a way to do so given their reputation in the industry.

Assisting with the merge of the two businesses and overseeing the integration of the NCS client base into the Castle family was a task I happily performed, ensuring as smooth a transition for the NCS clients as possible.

My primary role in Castle was as a Formation Team member, one of six specialist staff who oversaw the formation and production of company, trust and superannuation products for all Castle clients.

The processes and team dynamic were quite different to NCS, given the size differences and this was another hidden opportunity given the relative autonomy my previous position afforded me.

My time at Castle was a rewarding one with many fond memories and a wealth of experience gained.

Corporate Secretarial Assistant

Norfolk Corporate Support (Sole Proprietorship)

Sole Proprietorship; 1-10 employees; Government Administration industry

June 2006 – August 2011 (5 years 3 months)

A position for a multi-tasker, my role at NCS was to cover both reception and the more in-depth formation duties of a corporate secretarial assistant.

As an outsource service for accounting and legal firms, NCS was positioned as a well respected provider of company and trust formation services as well as their unique secretarial services that further assisted professionals in maintaining their clients' corporate files and filing obligations.

Liaising with numerous government departments and international government consulates made up our daily bread, with each staff member handling tasks from minor administrative issues to major corporate structural registrations / changes.

Working for five years with Sue Norfolk and her team was a great honour, helped shape my work ethic and gave me a skill-set and understanding of Australian business that I otherwise might never have known.

Editor in Chief - Whispering Grey Matters

Grey School of Wizardry

March 2008 – March 2009 (1 year 1 month) http://www.greyschool.com

As Editor in Chief of the student newsletter "Whispering Grey Matters", it was my job to oversee the whole operation and production of each issue, including the planning of future issues and distribution of articles to our team of writers.

For a student publication, the level of organisation and dedication was incredible. I was the first EiC for the publication, despite it having been active for two years prior, which gave me the freedom to explore just how creative I could be with the direction of the e-zine.

Having gone from a writer and a desk editor for the opinion desk to the Editor in Chief was a great honour. One that helped shape my outlook on not only working within a team but leading one as well.

A challenge and an incredibly rewarding experience.

Sales Assistant

EB Games

Public Company; 5001-10,000 employees; ELBO; Retail industry

June 2005 – June 2006 (1 year 1 month)

Providing customer service and advice to parents, kids and hardcore gamers alike, working at EB Games was a dream job for a geek like me.

I love video games and got to spend my shifts discussing upcoming titles, previously released games and helping mothers decide on the purchase of an MA15+ game for their ten year old.

Sales, customer service, stock control, store management - it was an all-rounder job in the retail industry.
