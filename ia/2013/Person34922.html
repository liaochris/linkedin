Gena Rotstein's Summary

Serial social venture entrepreneur. Focusing on creating businesses for making money and doing good at the same time.

My company focuses on the conversation about strategic philanthropy. Clients include:
Single Family and Multi-Family Offices and their advisors (financial and legal); SME's who want to have corporate citizenship as part of their overall approach to business; and charities seeking out support in operational effectiveness (note: due to the nature of my consulting practise, we do not offer fundraising services, but I can happily recommend fund development consultants).

Place2Give technology - This is a database of Canadian (soon to be North American) charities that have been profiled against donor types in order to find the best fit between a donor and a charity. There is a free version of this website and a licensing version that is available to SFO's and MFO's as well as other financial and legal firms to facilitate the social capital conversation and manage the reporting of the philanthropy that clients are undertaking. API's are also available for developers looking to build software using our data and the matching algorithms.

My passion - identifying new ways of using profit to drive social change. One project that I am most proud of is the reframing a food-related, poverty organization to a food production and transportation company thereby creating a new channel for revenue generation (funds used to offset the charity arm).

Rotarian.

Specialties: A skilled consultant to the non-profit sector, specializing in providing services on philanthropic decision making, Board governance, strategic planning, and organizational effectiveness. Senior management experience in cultural arts, religious institutions, social services and informal education, combined with diverse experience in Board and leadership roles for charitable organizations. A facilitator and educator who brings a well-rounded perspective on the community to as a consultant.
