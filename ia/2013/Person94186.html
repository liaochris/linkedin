Michael Keller's Summary

Mike Keller is founding partner, President & CTO of International Direct Selling Technology Corporation (www.IDSTC.com), the premier software technology provider to the direct selling industry. Over the past decade, IDSTC has completed more than 500 successful implementations, supporting millions of users.

Mike is a U.S. Army veteran of Operation Iraqi Freedom (FL-ARNG), and a graduate of the University of South Florida. He has earned a Certificate in Executive Leadership from Cornell University, a Six Sigma GB Certificate from Villanova University, and is an ISACA Certified Information Security Manager (CISM).

Mike is a published author, with his memoir "Torture Central" receiving wide critical acclaim. His other writings have appeared in more than a dozen different periodicals, including: Time Magazine, The Hill, American Thinker, Tampa Bay Times, etc. He has participated in numerous interviews and public appearances, including: NBC, ABC, Fox, AuthorTalk, Radioactivity (WMNF), etc. He was the Story and Production Adviser for the movie "The Boys of Abu Ghraib."

Mike has served as a Delegate (alt) to the Republican National Convention, an Ambassador to the Republican State Convention, and is an active member of the Hillsborough County Republican Executive Committee.

Mike was appointed by the Governor as Commissioner to the Florida Commission on Human Relations, the state agency charged with enforcing the Civil Rights and Fair Housing Acts. He was also appointed to the Public Library Board, which oversees the operation of the public library system.

Mike has completed multiple Savage Races and the Tough Mudder, as well as Tampa Bay's Dancing With The Stars 2013. He enjoys boating and spending time with his wife (Kendra), nine-year-old daughter (Morgan), six-year-old son (Trent), and two-year-old daughter (Reagan).
