Ken Follett's Summary

We are currently looking for a small project where Ruredil cementitious fiber reinforced matrix materials would be an optimal engineered solution. We have hands-on mechanics and project management staff in place to undertake this work. Please inquire.

I have been active as a tradesperson and contractor in the heritage conservation sector of the construction industry in excess of forty years. I was raised on a hill of glacial till in the Finger Lakes region of NY State and my early passion was to play and build with rocks, mostly in the local crick-bed where they were plentiful.

Currently I am, with my son and business partner, engaged in providing support services to structural engineers, architects and conservators in their investigations of historic structures. These services range anywhere from logistics, to probes, to cores, to sample extractions to making sure everyone on a site is hydrated, mock-ups, feasibility of craft, conceptual budgets and construction logic. We engage as 'consultants with tools' as a member of the design investigation team. My hands-on and project management experience is diverse and includes involvement on very small and rather large heritage projects. As well I have a widely diverse experience in masonry restoration, roofing, waterproofing, carpentry and window restoration.

We also team with and work directly in the employ of a small number of associates with whom we provide project management services, hands-on craft and/or engage with them to undertake larger restoration projects on a selected basis.

Our geographic range is that we will provide service wherever there is a pay check.

Recent projects have included consultation on a terra cotta silo in Cape Cod, exterior restoration of a private residential structure (including roof work, carpentry, window work, masonry and painting), consultation on a paint strip project (removal on the interior of layers of paint over a natural cement board-formed concrete).
