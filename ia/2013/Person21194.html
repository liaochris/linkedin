Christopher Jones' Experience

Publisher

Trek.fm

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Broadcast Media industry

April 2010 – Present (3 years 6 months) Tokyo

In April 2010 I founded Trek.fm, an information resource and community for Star Trek fans that combines traditional written content with a family of audio and video shows.

Under my guidance, and with the help of a talented group of writers, artists, and on-air talent, Trek.fm has quickly grown from a new, completely unknown entity into a popular destination for fans. Trek.fm now receives more than 80,000 views and 30,000 unique visitors per month, along with 12 different shows and an average of 300GB of podcast downloads monthly through iTunes and Stitcher. Our shows have been cited by many as amongst the best Star Trek-related programming on the Internet, and Trek.fm produces more Star Trek-related content each week than anyone in the world.

The rapid growth of Trek.fm has been achieved exclusively through word-of-mouth marketing using Twitter, Facebook, Google+, and App.Net.

Despite being an unknown startup in 2010, Trek.fm has attracted many of the biggest names in Star Trek and science as guests on our shows, including Larry Nemecek, Anthony Montgomery, Kirsten Beyer, Keith R. A. DeCandido, Seth Shostak, David A. Goodman, James Swallow, Athena Andreadis, Dan Madsen, Dayton Ward, Dave Rossi, David Mack, and Rod Roddenberry, the son of Star Trek creator Gene Roddenberry.

My creative and managerial hand can be seen throughout Trek.fm, as creative director, designer, writer, and producer. I also host two Trek.fm shows—The Ready Room and Matter Stream—and co-host Literary Treks and The Orb.

Trek.fm and our shows can be found at http://trek.fm/

Principal + Creative Director

Stellar Debris

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Design industry

July 2002 – Present (11 years 3 months) Tokyo

In 2002 I founded Stellar Debris, a studio that combines experience in print and web design, magazines, fine arts, illustration, sound design, video production, and writing to create comprehensive and engaging approaches to marketing and communications.

Our focus has evolved over the years along with technology and the needs of our clients. With the evolution of the Internet and social media, our current focus is on the creation of engaging multimedia content and the publication, distribution, and integration of this content into broader marketing strategies that leverage social media.

Ongoing work includes:

• Design and illustration for clients in the technology industry
• Audio and video podcast production for Trek.fm
• Social media marketing for Trek.fm
• Design and marketing assistance for clients in the scientific and medical industries
• Interactive design using HTML5 to replace Flash

Stellar Debris work can be found at http://www.stellardebris.com/

Director of Marketing

Bytware

Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Computer Software industry

2002 – 2008 (6 years)

Starting in 2002 I directed the design, marketing, and media buying for one of the oldest software companies in the IBM midrange server industry. Bytware’s clientele includes at least one division of every Fortune 100 company. As Director of Marketing for Bytware I planned and executed global marketing, targeting corporate IT departments whose operations are built upon the IBM AS/400, iSeries, System i, and IBM i platforms. Our solutions address automated operations, systems management, notification, security, and anti-virus needs for corporate IT.

Managed annual marketing budget of $500,000+

After formally leaving my position with Bytware I have continued to assist the company with design- and marketing-related tasks to this day through their relationship with my studio, Stellar Debris. I also perform the same type of work for several other companies owned by Help/Systems, the parent company of Bytware.

Communications Manager

Tokyo American Club

Nonprofit; 201-500 employees; Hospitality industry

2000 – 2002 (2 years) Tokyo

I worked both as a designer and the Communications Manager for The Tokyo American Club, a large private club serving the expatriate community with banquet facilities, family dining, library access, meeting facilities, social events, and recreation.

As head of the Communications department I managed a five-person in-house design team that supported five other departments and three Member event committees/organizations with design and marketing services. I reported to the Member Services Director and the Assistant General Manager.

Some of my major projects at The Tokyo American Club included:

A redesign of the Club’s 37-year-old full-color monthly magazine. I organized and streamlined the production process and brought full production in-house, saving the Club money and allowing full control over the look, feel, and quality of the magazine. The new format was called “a classy publication by world standards” by the General Manager of the Hong Kong American Club.

I led a team that developed and designed the Club's new website. At a time when the Internet was still largely a static information source, we integrated interactive features and online registration capabilities. These changes saved the Club time and money by shifting portions of three information sources to the Web. The new website provided greatly improved organization, ease-of-use, and consistency over previous site.

Also as Communications Manager I increased department productivity by redistributing staff responsibilities, restructuring workflow, and identifying and correcting deficiencies in tools and operational reliability of equipment.

Art Director and Managing Editor

Business World Corporation

January 1998 – January 1999 (1 year 1 month) Tokyo

At Business World, a publishing company specializing in English-language magazines, I edited and designed two monthly publications: "Tokyo Day & Night," and "The Nippon View" for distribution in the Tokyo metropolitan area. These publications covered a wide variety of subjects including entertainment, community events, and fashion.

In addition to the monthlies I also edited and designed the semi-annual restaurant guide "Wining and Dining in Tokyo."

As Art Director I established the design of the magazines and also did layout. As Managing Editor I commissioned and edited articles from five regular contributors and a field of freelance writers, and also wrote feature articles about Japanese culture and tourism. Lastly, because of my prepress experience I developed a system for proofing and production that improved accuracy and reduced film costs.

Prepress

Randall Publishing Company

April 1996 – September 1997 (1 year 6 months) Tuscaloosa, Alabama

I gained my prepress experience working for a large publisher of the nationally distributed magazines "Overdrive" and "Equipment World" for the owner-operator, transportation, and construction industries. I was a member of five-person in-house prepress department handling scanning, film output/stripping, and proof preparation for two 150-page monthly magazines and annual catalogs for Caterpillar and other major manufacturers.

My primary role in prepress was operating the drum scanner and performing color correction tasks for the art department. I scanned and color corrected an average of 400 photos in each 3–4 week period using a Howtek drum scanner and Photoshop.

I also assisted in outputting/processing film and creating color keys/4-color matchprints for more than 300 pages each month.

I had the privilege of performing with some of the world's great musicians including Itzhak Perlman and Yo-Yo Ma.
