Fer Isella's Summary

Fer Isella is a serial cultural entrepreneur, music producer, composer, pianist and a digital music strategist, innovation and production.

After producing albums for Sony Music (+2 million records sold and +15 platinum albums), Fer was awarded by the U.S. Department of State with a full Fulbright Scholarship to study at Berklee College of Music, Boston. In 1999 was awarded with the "Clare Fischer Award" for composer of the year, then earning a cum laude diploma in Jazz Composition.

Fer moved to New York in the year 2000 to perform with his own band as well as other artists like Joy Askew (keyboard player for Peter Gabriel and David Bowie), Miho Hatori (Cibo Mato, Sean Lennon), and to produce records.

In 2005 Fer moved back to his homeland, Buenos Aires, and founded Limbo Music, an integrated innovation and music production hub.

In 2009 an 2011 was awarded by the British Council and the Argentinean Cultural Entrepreneur of the Year.

Currently, Fer coordinates the music industry sector at the Argentine Cultural Industries Market (MICA), organized by the Secretariat of Culture of Argentina.
