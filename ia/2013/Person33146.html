Darren Day's Summary

Artistic and creative exploration of image production with emphasis on altering reality just enough to make people, and product, appear more appealing than we may usually see them. Also like to push the envelope on how far one can take those alterations...

Specialties

Keen sense for the dramatic lighting. Good composition of subject to surrounding background. excellent people skills when coaching the posing of even the most novice of models.

Darren Day's Education

Real world experience.

No degrees at Real World Experience, only satisfaction at receiving a useful, unforgetable eduation., Thinking out "of the box" and "on my feet".

1983 – 2012

Self taught in the art of composition and presentation of the photographed subject. Began studies in lighting and composition at age 9 with a small camera and two instructional books purchased with saved up allowance.

Real world experience as apprentice to a nationally known Commercial Product and Food photographer, Jim Parks. At that time Mr. Parks had seven instructional books published in the art and technical mechanics of photographic studio lighting and subject composition. I was accepted as 1st. assistant in apprenticeship with Jim in the year 1989 and maintained that position until 1992 upon leaving the Chicago area to venture out on my own.

Independentaly operating my own business in photography by age 18. Locating model hopefuls and producing high quality portfolio images from film. Learned techniques in processing and developing my own film to ensure the quality and style I wanted. Self taught in printing processes for the same reasons.
