He also obtained a Bachelors in both electrical engineering and economics from the University of Western Ontario in Canada in 2000.

Ian R. Smart's Projects

  • Tobago Solar Outdoor Lights

    • November 2011 to March 2012
    Team Members: Ian R. Smart

    Remote areas or areas that are not connected to the electricity grid have no power due to the high cost of running electricity lines. This has been a major barrier for providing lighting for security and basic safety that helps to reduce crime as well as prevent accidents. Solar powered lights have the potential to address this issue and if done correctly, also have an added benefit of creating a cottage industry. The Smart Energy Approach to solving the problem was achieved through the use of solar powered park lights that were designed in Germany but assembled locally and mounted on site using indigenous labour. This resulted in transfers of knowledge and wealth to different small businesses and provided an additional benefit to the environment as a result of using energy that does not have continuous carbon dioxide emissions. Analysis of the project demonstrated that a solar economy has a high job creation capability with strong emphasis on the creation of decent, sustainable jobs. Scaling up such projects therefore, has the potential to accrue significant benefits to society in terms of improving the quality of life economically, environmentally and socially.
