  • My bank account for charity

    • 2010 to Present
    Team Members: Clayton Correia, John Bromley, Crystal Henrickson, Jeffrey Golby, Clif Bromley, Kareem Mayan, Arif Bandali, Daniel Wintschel, Chris Shorrock

    We use a bank account to organize the money we interact with every day, whether that is 'money in' (paycheck, friend paying you back...) or 'money out' (paying rent, buying food...). Having a bank account makes my life easier to manage.

    Why don't we do the same for charitable dollars? I couldn't think of any good reasons why not, so I created Chimp (from charitable impact) as a project to simplify and centralize my interactions with charity.

    Now I have a single place to hold and organize my charitable dollars. I can add money to my Chimp Fund as a donation and have the company I work for match it. Even friends can send me money to give away as a 'thank you' for the dinner I paid for.

    With money in my account, I can send money to any charity I want when I want. I can also send money to a friend for them to give away or pool money with others so that a bigger amount can be sent to the charity we choose together.

    I'm still working on this project I call Chimp. Check it out and lmk what you think.
