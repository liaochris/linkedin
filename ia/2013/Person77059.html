Holdon Log, was founded by performers who realized the need for organizational tools specifically designed for entertainers. The company launched with Actors' Holdon Log. They have since expanded their paper based product line to include Young Actors, Background Artists, Models, Live Performers and Pageant Contestants, along with Submission Tracker, Events & Training and Booked Projects logbooks. In 2003, the company revolutionized the Performing Industry by releasing the one-of-a-kind software, ActorTrack which was the #1 selling performer-related software program. In 2008, they launched PerformerTrack webware which they continue to build upon the PerformerTrack with unique, cutting-edge enhancements -- while simultaneously continuing to bolster their paper-based product line.Holdon Log is dedicated to providing both technological and non-technological organizational solutions so that performers can better maximize their potential in "the business of show".

2001 – Present (12 years)

PerfomerTrack takes the pain out of the mundane, left-brained tasks and makes doing the "business of show" simple, effective and, believe it or not, fun to do! We believe in creating simple, intuitive, and easy to use solutions for performers.

While being simple, everything we build inside PerformerTrack is with the User in mind. The Moveable Theatre Company

April 2010 – Present (3 years 6 months) Greater Los Angeles Area

The Moveable Theatre Company is a group of like-minded actors, directors, producers and writers who share a love of theatre and a desire to share enriching theatrical experiences with audiences throughout the Los Angeles area. Since most theatre companies that fail, do so due to high costs of renting and maintaining a “home” facility, TMTC operates differently: rather than attempting to lure the audience to us, we take the theatre to where the audiences are. The result is a creative, innovative experience in which we perform in spaces not normally thought to be “stages”: libraries, private homes, churches, museums, senior centers, even cemeteries – wherever there is a space where a story can be told – we perform and create theatrical magic! Because we are not limited by the physical constraints of a small stage, our audiences can be as large or as small as the space allows and because of the varied spaces in which we perform, we incorporate the physical surroundings into our staging.

  • I Am Neda

    • 2010 to 2012
    Team Members: Kristina Hughes, SAG-AFTRA, Nicole Kian-Sadighi, Mary Apick, Max Bartoli, David Bartlett, Brian Vermeire

    The film the Iranian government doesn't want you to see...

    From first time Director Nicole Kian Sadighi who has written & produced this award winning movie 'I Am Neda' the true story of Neda Agha Soltan who fearlessly pursued the cause of freedom in defiance of tyranny & oppression.

    June 2009 marked another turning point for Iranians, when mass anti-government protests erupted across major cities in Iran, following the June 12 rigged Iranian presidential election against the disputed victory of Iranian President Mahmoud Ahmadinejad, which came to be known as the Green Movement. These events provoked the government to declare war on its own people & the world became a witness to some of the most horrific acts that were shown daily through major news outlets as well as Twitter, Facebook & YouTube posting. Many innocent Iranians were, imprisoned, tortured, raped & killed for standing up for their basic human rights in nonviolent civil disobedience.

    This film is centered on one ordinary yet courageous young woman on the backdrop of extraordinary circumstances, who has since become the symbol & face of this movement

    News reports regarding Neda soon went viral across the globe through leading news outlets & online social media. Everyone from CNN, Fox, BBC, Reuters, AP, AFP, London Times, Life Magazine, the musical group U2 &President Obama commented & paid homage to Neda's story - the girl whose face will always be remembered from YouTube. She was one of the 2009 Time Magazines Person of the Year, 2010 London Times Person of the Year. Additionally The Neda Agha Soltan Graduate Scholarship is a scholarship for post-graduate philosophy students at The Queen's College, Oxford, with preference given to students of Iranian citizenship or heritage. Neda's impact on the Green Movement in Iran which launched the Arab Spring in the Middle East, has made her the face & symbol of Iran's freedom movement & with it a historical legacy that must never be forgotten.
