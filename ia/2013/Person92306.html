Patch Operations and Delivery

The Patch Operations and Delivery system was the System of Record for all patches provided by Sun Microsystems. It provided patch life cycle tracking, machine consumable meta-data, secure long-term storage and delivery mechanisms to the public Internet and Sun’s Corporate Intranet.

As the Founder and Technical Lead of the Patch Operations and Delivery system, I was responsible for the technical direction of Sun Microsystems' patch repositories and delivery infrastructure; serving as a Division representative at Corporate level planning sessions and committees as well as a Corporate representative in customer feedback sessions.

Within the team, some of my principal contributions included; proposing the project to upper management, recruiting the development staff, leading the team through 16 development cycles within a 4 year period, architecting a solution that reduced the world wide patch release time from > 48 hours to < 3 minutes (nominally), working cooperatively with SMI Operations staff to architect the system’s production hardware infrastructure, and serving as one of the team’s principle Software Development Engineers. In this capacity, contributions included functionality such as the global distribution of patch state invariant data, support for non-SVR4 patch types, the production of patch cross-reference metadata as well as the project’s database creation and build automation code.

I had Primary Development and maintenance responsibility for Osmos’ Data Abstraction Layer (DAL) as well as secondary development responsibility for Osmos’ SQL and Call Level Interface (CLI / ODBC).
