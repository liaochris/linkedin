Andy Dennie's Summary

I'm an independent software developer and consultant with 25+ years industry experience in roles ranging from individual contributor to Vice President of Development/QA, at companies of all sizes.

In 2011, I started a company called Fizz Buzz LLC, focused on Android applications and cloud-based web services.

At Fizz Buzz, I created News Valet, a family of mobile applications serving two constituencies: content producers looking to expand their audiences and traffic, and content consumers seeking an easier way to discover and read content on topics they care about. You can read more about it at http://www.newsvalet.com.

I also enjoy working in a contractor/consultant role to help others with their projects. Ping me if you'd like to talk.

Fizz Buzz is a solo effort at this point, meaning I'm basically Chief Everything Officer. I define the product strategy, plan and prioritize features, architect, implement, build and test the code, handle all marketing and advertising activities, design the web site and manage its content, create digital graphic assets like logos, banners, and icons, and provide customer support.

===============

Prior to my current endeavor, I accumulated extensive experience both as a hands-on and detail-oriented manager/director/VP of software development and QA teams, and as a developer working on a wide variety of technologies, encompassing two decades in the design, architecture, development, testing, documentation and maintenance of software products in both startup and larger corporate environments.

Andy Dennie's Patents

  • Method and system for providing a networked collaborative work environment

    • United States Patent 712750
    • Issued October 24, 2006
    Inventors: Andy Dennie, Jeffrey Beir, Melissa Leffler, Glenn McDonald, Paul Kleppner, Neal Finnegan, Craig Morrissey, Ala

    A system and method for providing a collaborative work environment includes servers and client workstations. Client workstations receive data objects from one or more servers and combine the received data objects with stored templates to render HTML pages representing at least a portion of a common project. Users may view, edit, and create common documents for the projects and upload them to the server using a drag-and-drop interface. Additionally, since all file transfers take place using HTTP, project groups can span corporate organizations as well as time zones and geographic boundaries.
