Co-designer

Project Littlefoot

August 2008 – June 2010 (1 year 11 months) Lawrence, Kansas

Design for a more complete education. Project Littlefoot is a children's program that was developed in response to gaps identified in current education trends. The goal of the program is to validate creativity through learning about teamwork and narrative skills. The program encourages design thinking at young age, by reinforcing the child’s confidence and ability to solve problems. During the program kids work together to create a story including all characters, scenes, and sounds of their story. Once the story is complete, Project Littlefoot volunteers help to bring their story to life through animations and local premieres. The program ran at the Edna A. Hill Childhood Development Center in Lawrence, Kansas and again at the Topeka and Shawnee County Public Library.

Project Littlefoot began as my thesis project for Industrial Design, along with my partner Patrick O’Neil. We were awarded a scholarship that gave us the ability to continue developing the program, at which point Carey Spesard joined the team. Project Littlefoot was recognized at the IDSA midwest conference 2009 as a merit scholar presentation by Patrick O’Neil. I worked to develop this program with my team from ideation to a complete realization of the idea. The entire project serves as a prototype for the impact that design thinking can have in education, a topic I am very passionate about.

Design Intern, Exhibits Team

Kansas State Historical Society, museums

June 2008 – August 2008 (3 months) Topeka, Kansas

I worked as an intern with the exhibits design team to enhance interactions between the Kansas Museum of History and its visitors. The work included retrofitting exhibits by providing new ways to understand the artifacts and stories laid out in the museum space. I was tasked with designing an informative display that would need to withstand the next 30 years of wound-up kids on field trips visiting the museum. I worked with the design team on a variety of smaller projects including graphic design work promoting visiting exhibits, and planning implementation strategies for larger renovations to the space.

The museum’s tagline was “Real People, Real Stories”, this motto resonated with me. During my time working at the museum I had the pleasure of hearing many stories of life in Kansas throughout history from curators and history enthusiasts that truly were realistic and inspiring. This experience taught me the power of storytelling through design.

Skills used during this internship included collaborative concepting, sketching, rapid prototypes, infographics, and installations.
