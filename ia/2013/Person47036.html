Gary Arndt's Summary

In March 2007 I sold my house and have been traveling around the world ever since. Since I started traveling, I have probably done and seen more than I have in the rest of my life combined.

So far I have visited all 7 continents, over 145 countries/territories, all 50 US states, 9/10 Canadian provinces, every Australian state and territory, over 100 US National Park Service sites and over 250 UNESCO World Heritage Sites.

I have also:

- Bungee jumped in New Zealand
- Floated in the Dead Sea
- Crossed the arctic circle in the Yukon
- Rode in a Formula 1 car at 180mpg (300kph) in Spain
- Been in the water with great white sharks in South Africa
- Dove in the ruins of the Great Lighthouse of Alexandria in Egypt
- Had an entire remote white sand beach to myself in Fiji
- Swam with whale sharks in Australia
- Been spelunking in Borneo
- Rode the world’s highest zip line in Puerto Rico
- Rode out a tsunami in Hawaii
- Visited an active war zone in Cambodia
- Resisted the temptation to take a $5,000,000 moon rock in the Solomon Islands
- Been kicked out of the nation of Kiribati
- Experienced the Thai New Year (Songkran) in Bangkok
- Experienced Holy Week in Jerusalem
- Got caught in the middle of a political protest in Thailand
- Swam with jellyfish in Palau
- Visited many of the greatest museums on Earth
- Traveled by plane, train, boat, car, bus, motorcycle, tuk tuk, bicycle, camel, elephant, horse, hot air balloon, and helicopter

Specialties: Blogging, photography

Gary Arndt's Experience

Blogger

Everything Everywhere

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Leisure, Travel & Tourism industry

October 2006 – Present (7 years) Nomadic

I have been blogging at Everything-Everywhere.com since October 2006. Since then Everything Everywhere has become one of the most popular travel blogs in the world. It was named by Time Magazine as one of the 25 Best Blogs on the internet in 2010 and has won awards from the Society of American Travel Writers and the North American Travel Journalists Association.

Co-Host

This Week In Travel

August 2010 – Present (3 years 2 months) Global

This Week in Travel is a quasi-weekly podcast co-hosted by myself, Jen Leo and Chris Christensen. We cover travel news and whatever other subjects pique our fancy.

CEO

Stomped

August 1999 – May 2003 (3 years 10 months) Greater Minneapolis-St. Paul Area

Stomped.com was one of the larger network of video game websites in the late 90's and early 00's. In addition to the flagship Stomped.com, we also had in our network the official site for Counter Strike and the most popular EverQuest site: Allakhazam.com. At its peak it was delivering 50,000,000 pageviews per month.

We also opened up The Stomping Grounds, North America's premier LAN gaming center in Minneapolis, with 30 state of the art gaming PC's.

Manager

Syntegra

1998 – 1999 (1 year)

After I sold my company to Syntegra, I basically was on the payroll for a year with no one above or below me in the org chart. I was responsible for no projects and did nothing but plan my next company.

CEO

Creative Internet Solutions

June 1994 – August 1998 (4 years 3 months) Greater Minneapolis-St. Paul Area

I started Creative Internet Solutions as a vehicle to do freelance work on a platform written by my college roommate: Cold Fusion.

Over time it evolved into a custom web application company working in both Cold Fusion and ASP, with approximately 50 employees by the time it was sold.
