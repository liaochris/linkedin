Jay Timms' Summary

As an author and award winning researcher in the field of communication, habit development and motivation, Jay Timms has been helping individuals and organizations move from “good” to “better” and “best” for almost two decades.

Jay’s experience in the business world and in the field of psychology has been fed by a passionate desire to answer the question, “Why do we do the things that we do?” This question has lead him into studies in brain development and anatomy, multiple disciplines in psychology, quantum physics, serious mental health disorders, non-verbal communication, the unconscious mind, and the art of communication.

In everything he does, Jay’s goal is to challenge the status quo. To do things differently, and to challenge others to do the same. Whether he is working with Life Coaches and therapists, major corporations, or even with his private clients, Jay’s focus is on helping them learn how to literally change their own brain anatomy and patterns of thinking. This training allows anyone the opportunity to do more with the resources that they already have with less effort. His philosophy is that “Life doesn’t need to be hard. It just needs to be intentional.”

Jay has spoken to and trained organizations around the world, and continues to be in demand by groups ranging from Nationally ranked Universities, Fortune 500 companies, sales teams, Financial institutions, hospitals, and many more.

Jay Timms' Experience

Certified Personal and Corporate NeuralStrategist, Award Winning Researcher, Author

Empower Training and Development

January 2004 – Present (10 years) International

Most people strive for a "good" life. Career, family, financial situation, health...you know, life. Chances are, "good" probably isn't cutting it for you, or, maybe "good" only describes a portion of what you have.

My experience is that there are simple things that you can do to move past "good" in all areas in your life. In short, they are 1) know who you are, 2) know why you do what you do and create strategies to allow you to do what you want to do, and 3) understand how to communicate in a way with others that allows you to get past "good".

The training I deliver to individuals and teams live and virtually, simply put, gets you past "good" and into "great". Great workplaces, great relationships, great health, great...whatever. You can continue to live in "good" if you would like, but my bet is that you want more. I am here because life doesn't come with a manual.
