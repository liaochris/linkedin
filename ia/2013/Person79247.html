Laurie Van Zandt's Summary

I attended Cal Poly San Luis Obispo School of Architecture and was an architectural designer for approx. ten years. This experience and training combined with extensive continuing education in landscape design makes me uniquely qualified to design creative, functional landscapes. I have been on the education committee for Utah Nursery & Landscape Assoc. annual Green Conference for several years and led seminars on landscape design at this conference. I am an active member of the international organization, Association of Professional Landscape Designers and helped organize their annual conference in Salt Lake City.

Specialties

Incredible backyards that will bring your life outdoors, swimming pools, unique waterfeatures, creative stone work, herb gardens, drought tolerant plantings, textural plantings, garden rooms, beautiful welcoming entries. Project co-ordination to ensure that your landscape experience is more than you expected

Laurie Van Zandt's Volunteer Experience & Causes

  • Volunteer Experience

    • Landscape/Garden Design

      Daughters of Charity, Give Me a Chance
      • Civil Rights and Social Action
      July 2013 – present (7 months)

      Design landscape features, pergolas, ponds, paths, patios, industrial kitchen/book store/dining area/lounge area, all plantings for 'Guadalupe Garden'. A one acre parcel, Guadalupe Garden is intended as a gathering place for events such as weddings, a private retreat, a meditation garden, play area, and botanical learning center for the immediate neighborhood - a lower income area of Ogden, Utah. The immediate use is for outside play for the children of the abused women who are being helped vocationally and emotionally at the adjacent center. It will also function as a place for meditation and prayer for those women. It is a very spiritual place designed with symbolism waiting to be discovered, and incorporates a shrine to Our Lady of Guadalupe.
