Christos KK Loverdos' Publications

  • Steps in Scala

    • Cambridge University Press
    • 2010
    Authors: Christos KK Loverdos, Apostolos Syropoulos

    Scala is a highly expressive, concise and scalable language. It is also the most prominent method of the new and exciting methodology known as object-functional programming. In this book, the authors show how Scala grows to the needs of the programmer, whether professional or hobbyist. They teach Scala with a step-by-step approach and explain how to exploit the full power of the industry-proven JVM technology. Readers can then dive into specially chosen design challenges and implementation problems, inspired by the trials of real-world software engineering. It also helps readers to embrace the power of static typing and automatic type inference. In addition, the book shows how to use the dual-object and functional-oriented natures combined at Scala's core, and so write code that is less 'boilerplate', giving a genuine increase in productivity.

  • "Olympic Agents"

    • Spinger Verlag LNCS Series
    • 2006
    Authors: Christos KK Loverdos, Aris Tzoumas, Alexandros Basakidis, Dr. Nikos Skarmeas, Dimitris Livas, Katerina Tsiara

    Agent-oriented middle-tier architecture deployed during the realisation of the Athens 2004 Olympics results internet broadcasting. The system involved the online processing of messages (XML in nature) and their publishing to the www.athens2004.com internet site. Those messages were containing the Games intermediate and final results and were originated from the Olympic venues. For the accomplishment of this task a number of systems and applications needed to be integrated. Also the domain posed some unique problems regarding the fact that for the first time in the history of the Games a real time approach for broadcasting results was deployed and furthermore due to the reliability and performance requirements of the system. Various enterprise application integration patterns were used in conjunction with an agent oriented design approach. Asynchronous intercommunicating agents were deployed for realizing the architectural components of the system.

Christos KK Loverdos' Projects

  • Aquarium

    • August 2011 to Present
    Team Members: Christos KK Loverdos, Georgios Gousios, Panos Louridas

    Aquarium is a charging platform, initially targeted to IaaS cloud environments. It features extensible resource definitions and charging configuration based on a DSL. Developed in Scala, serves as the charging backend for GRNet's Okeanos cloud.

  • Athens2004 Games-Time Olympic Portal

    • September 2003 to September 2004
    Team Members: Christos KK Loverdos, Dr. Nikos Skarmeas, Alexandros Basakidis, Aris Tzoumas

    Member of the core team that designed and implemented the largest Olympic portal until 2004, ATHENS 2004 Games-Time Portal. Budgeted as high as 12.000.000 €, was considered, by the time of its implementation and operation the most successful Olympic portal ever made.
    For the first time in the Olympic portals history, real time results were
    delivered over the web in more than 5.000.000 unique visitors per day, through a highly redundant infrastructure, even at the CDN level. The development team staffed of 80 JAVA and systems engineers worked closely under precise and restrict plans for less 10 months and delivered a high quality, robust application on time and bellow budget.
    The innovative software design led to the publication of the team’s work under the title "Olympic Agents", Published by Spinger Verlag LNCS Series.
