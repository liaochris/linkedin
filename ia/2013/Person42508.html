Erika Grace Powell's Summary

Erika Grace Powell is a Vocal Performance graduate of Furman University in Greenville, SC with musical interests including opera, classical crossover, jazz, theatre, and pop. Erika was crowned Miss South Carolina 2005 and advanced to the 2006 Miss America Pageant, where she won the Bernie Wayne overall talent award and placed as a top-10 finalist. In 2007, she was engaged for Lyrique en Mer/Festival de Belle Ile, the largest Opera Festival in Western France. Erika won The Metropolitan Opera National Council auditions for the District of SC in 2009. She was both the state and regional winner of the National Association of Teachers of Singing (NATS) Competition for SC and Southeast five times. Erika made her solo debut at Lincoln Center’s Avery Fisher Hall in 2010 and was seen in The New York Philharmonic’s 2011 production of Janáček’s The Cunning Little Vixen.

Residing in NYC the last three years, Erika continues to develop her career with a diverse calendar. She received SAG eligibility for work in the movie, “Something Borrowed”, featuring Kate Hudson and is excited to pursue acting for film, TV, and modeling. Erika has been spotted on “Ugly Betty”, “White Collar”, “Royal Pains”, “The Good Wife”, and in commercials for Kohl’s, Lowe’s, and others.

Thrilled to partner with platinum-selling singer/songwriter, Edwin McCain and producer/engineer, Noel Golden, Erika's first Classical Crossover CD is available on iTunes and Amazon.

Erika was selected out of over 6,000 submissions as a Top-25 Global Semi-Finalist in the Avon Voices Talent Search with Avon Cosmetics. Erika was coached and performed alongside celebrity judges, including Fergie, Natasha Bedingfield, David Pack, Lea Solanga, and Diane Warren.

In November 2011, Erika was crowned Miss South Carolina USA 2012; a rare occurrence that a woman should win both the Miss America and the Miss Universe state titles in her lifetime. She was thrilled to place in the Top-15 at The Miss USA Pageant in June 2012.
