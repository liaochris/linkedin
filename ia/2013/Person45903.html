Fabio Rapposelli's Summary

I’m a Virtualization and Storage Architect with a solid UNIX background, my ultimate goal is to make every IT infrastructure I touch run smooth.

My main focus is on infrastructure consolidation and automation through virtualization, SAN design and their implementation using both IP and FC technologies, I’m also quite experienced with geographically dispersed application clustering. My strongest technical skill set revolves around VMware and virtualization in general (Xen and KVM) and storage where I’m fluent on almost every vendor and I can deep dive easily on every storage architecture in the market. On the Networking side I have keen knowledge of everything up to Layer 7 and I’m currently refreshing my networking skills studying for CCIE R&S, on the FC side of things I can work my way through both Brocade and Cisco equipment with several large-scale deployments under my belt.

I maintain a solid and prolific social media presence through blogging and Twitter, founded the Italian VMUG in 2011, I’m also frequently invited by vendors to attend their industry events as influencer, including but not limited to: HP, Dell, Tech Field Day, VMware and Hitachi Data Systems. Vendors like Solarwinds and Veeam also engage me for paid work as Subject Matter Expert for content creation and thought leading. I also routinely participate in Technical roundtables like the Tech Field Day roundtables and I’ve been video interviewed by the Cube as well as HP for their internal and external Social Media team.

I also speak at public events revolving around virtualization and storage, I presented at VMUG, DatacenterDynamics and other industry events as well as at an itinerant tour I co-organized called Juku Unplugged which offered independent content and advise on hot topics such as Data Storage, Virtualization and Cloud Computing.

    La virtualizzazione dei server è uno degli argomenti più caldi di questi anni.

    Molte aziende stanno già affrontando la loro seconda ondata di virtualizzazione che spesso si traduce in una rivisitazione radicale di quello che è stato fatto negli anni passati per correggere errori e, magari, aggiornare l’infrastruttura con l’espressione degli ultimi trend tecnologici.
