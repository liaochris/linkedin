Michel Holper's Summary

I am an industrial designer with a strong passion for integrating aesthetics with function. Meaning in products is created by the interaction people have with that product, and not the product by itself. I believe that beauty can be found in the way people interact with products as well as the design itself.

Be sure to check out my website if you're curious to find out how this fuzzy statement translates into actual products :-)

www.parap.lu

Graduation project at VitaLight department

Philips Consumer Lifestyle

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; PHG; Electrical/Electronic Manufacturing industry

October 2009 – June 2010 (9 months)

During my graduation project at Philips I worked on the development of an innovative infrared warmth device. The extensive process carried out encompassed all phases from the fuzzy-front-end, ideation, sketching to prototype building and concept validation. The final stage included the development of necessary electronic circuitry and programming of the Arduino microprocessor in C. A set of five validation sessions were carried out to observe ordinary consumers using the prototype at the Philips homeLab simulation living room.

Master Student IPD (Integrated Product Design)

Faculty of Industrial Design Engineering

Educational Institution; 5001-10,000 employees; Research industry

September 2008 – May 2010 (1 year 9 months)

In the Master's Programme in IPD, the student learns to adapt the design process to the demands of specific design problems and design contexts, focusing in particular on the design of innovative products and product-service combinations for consumers and professional users. The student learns how to perform complex design activities with modern methods and advanced design technologies, and how to plan, execute and manage complex product development processes in multidisciplinary teams and distributed environments. (Quote adapted from the faculty website)
