  • Missile Fight Simulation: Surface to Air Missiles

    • September 2010 to January 2011
    Team Members: Jeffrey Strickland, Ph.D., CMSP, ASEP, MCTS

    First book I have authored using MATLAB and Simulink as a tool. Surface-to-air missiles are designed to defend a land area against an aerial or theater ballistic missile threat. The size of the defended area and the capabilities of the threat have great influence on the speed, maneuverability, and lethality requirements of the missile system. Simulation of the missile flight path can provide valuable information about these requirements. A missile flight simulation is a computational tool that calculates the flight of a missile from launch until it engages the target. The simulation is based on mathematical models of the missile, target and environment. This book provides instruction for the preparation of these mathematical models to simulate the flight of a surface-to-air missile. This book may be used as a reference or as a textbook, although it is devoid of exercises. However, the reader is encourages to perform the simulation of Charter 12 using MATLAB® and SimulinkTM, or a programming language such as FORTRAN (see Chapter 10).

    The primary goal of this book is to assist the student to develop the skills necessary to effectively employ the ideas of mathematics to solve military problems. At the simplest level I seek to promote an understanding of why mathematics is useful as a language for characterizing the interaction and relationships among quantifiable concepts, or in mathematical terms, variables.   • Missile Flight Simulation - Surface-to-Air Missiles

    • Lulu.com
    • August 2, 2011
    Authors: Jeffrey Strickland, Ph.D., CMSP, ASEP, MCTS

    Surface-to-air missiles are designed to defend a land area against an aerial or theater ballistic missile threat. The size of the defended area and the capabilities of the threat have great influence on the speed, maneuverability, and lethality requirements of the missile system. Simulation of the missile flight path can provide valuable information about these requirements. A missile flight simulation is a computational tool that calculates the flight of a missile from launch until it engages the target. The simulation is based on mathematical models of the missile, target and environment. This book provides instruction for the preparation of these mathematical models to simulate the flight of a surface-to-air missile. This book may be used as a reference or as a textbook, although it is devoid of exercises. However, the reader is encourages to perform the simulation of Charter 12 using MATLAB® and Simulink, or a programming language such as FORTRAN (see Chapter 10).

    • 2009 Interservice/Industry Training, Simulation, and Education (I/ITSEC) Conference
    • November 30, 2009
    Authors: Jeffrey Strickland, Ph.D., CMSP, ASEP, MCTS

    In discrete-event simulation, the operation of a system is represented as a chronological sequence of events. Each event occurs at an instant in time and marks a change of state in the system. A common exercise in learning how to build discrete-event simulations is to model a queue, such as a single service queue. In this example, the system includes entities, an arrival-queue, and a service process. This tutorial will discuss system events, system states, and random variables that need to be characterized to model a system, and will introduce simulation design of experiments (DOE). Tutorial examples will use a commercial simulation software package, ExtendSim, and discuss similar software. Topics to be addressed include:
    • Introduction to simulation modeling and discrete event
    simulation
    • Event sequencing and scheduling
    • Introduction to ExtendSim and DOE
    • Demonstration of a simple Ballistic Missile Defense System
    (BMDS) end-to-end model
