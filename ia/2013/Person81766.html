Lisl Dennis' Summary

As an international travel and decorative arts photographer, Lisl Dennis began her career immediately after high school while living and traveling in Europe. Day hikes and adventurous excursions by herself in the Italian, Sicilian and Swiss countryside confirmed that she thrived as a photographer in the creative discovery and direct experience with people in foreign cultures. To be confined within walls of art studios was not her life-path: at sixteen, Lisl's ambition to be a sculptor or stained glass artist gave way to a socially responsible creative experience in destination photography.

Lisl was the first woman staff photographer on the Boston Globe newspaper from 1968-1970. A freelancer based in New York City, a 40-year worldwide career unfolded as a photographer, writer, workshop facilitator, and presenter on Creativity in Culture. Often in collaboration with her writer husband Landt Dennis, Lisl worked on assignments for magazines and book publishers, and guided VISUAL JOURNEYS photo-tours focused on Creativity in Culture.

Today, Lisl is guiding STORYSHARDS: Re-form the Vessel of Your Life. The upcoming STORYSHARDS: Gathering for Women is hosted at the Upaya Zen Center in Santa Fe, NM, September 14-16, 2012. The Friday-thru-Sunday retreat will invite conversations around Personal Storytelling, Engaged Creativity, Spiritual Practice, and the Science of Surprise (Complexity Theory). Based on a vibrant multimedia presentation, the program is a timely new fusion of themes supportive of womens' engagement and leadership in all domains.

A natural evolution of her career – dedicated to creativity in relationship to all cultures – Lisl's intention in STORYSHARDS is to create conducive contexts and evocative atmospheres that support a grounded and practice-based engagement with the world – How Your Story Reveals Your Mission in service to the world-at-large.

Outdoor Photographer

2005 – 2011 (6 years)

It was a priviledge – and loads of fun – to be the token "culturist" for Outdoor Photographer, a magazine devoted to wildlife photography and to the great outdoors more broadly. I love writing, and as the travel editor, I wrote about my far-flung experiences engaged with humankind – in creative relationship to humankind in all cultures. (Prior, I had been the Travel Editor for Popular Photography.) Loving all things colorful and exotic, I produced articles from around the world. After 911, much changed...

Staff Photographer

Boston Globe Newspaper

1968 – 1970 (2 years) Boston, MA

What an experience to be the first woman photographer on the staff of the Boston Globe newspaper from 1968 to 1970. With the credit line Beth Jones – yes! – I covered all manner of assignments throughout New England: hard-hat construction sites and the Big Dig; ribbon cutting ceremonies and the ladies-who-lunch at the Ritz; North Shore lobstering and evidence of deadly disappearances on the Cape; fires, floods and drugs – all in the midst of the Students for Democratic Society (SDS) uprisings, and the Feminist and Civil Rights movements.

At the age of twenty one, I was covering several assignments with deadlines on a daily basis. Thrown into diverse contexts and cultures that required the appropriate response in a timely fashion, my training on the Boston Globe formed the basis of my career as a photographer in the world-at-large.

In my teaching, I've often stated that one is not a bonafide photographer without a grounding as a photojournalist engaging multiple narratives simultaneously – just an opinion. Anyway, I'll never forget my colleagues – those Bean Town guys with the "gars" – and how much they supported me – the girl "rookie".
