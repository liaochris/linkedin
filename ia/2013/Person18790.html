Benjamin Weisman's Summary

What I do:

In my role at MRM Princeton, I play the role of equal parts Creative Director, Digital Strategist, Product Designer & Motion Theorist. I'm continually seeking out new & innovative ways to approach User Centered Experiences in both physical & synthetic spaces. My work embodies a respect for, & a focus on, end-user needs, direct marketing, mobile & social media integration.

I love finding new creative ways to define experiences using emerging platforms & methods. It’s a part of what enables marketers to connect & collaborate with audiences. I'm driven to produce work with my clients & teams to define these new authentic experiences around curated content & utility that really impact peoples lives. From looking under the hood of CRM to diving into how motion can tell a story.

I've honed my skills at top digital shops including Deep Focus, Tribal DDB, Ogilvy, iris & HBO, — driven by entrepreneurial curiosity that has earned me a reputation as an industry 'trailblazer'. I can’t help but be fascinated by culture & tools. It’s part of our Human roots. It’s only natural that I’m drawn to digital social media tools & services. I work to help migrate users/consumers to new & pre-commoditized landscapes, and consider how Brand Experiences live there.

I have been lucky to have the opportunity to bringing Voice & Engagement to Brands like, IHOP, Hertz, AVIS, Applebee's, adidas, US Army, Vitamin Water, Staples, Office Depot, Chase, Mastercard, COTY, HBO, MGM, Miramax, Weinstein, Nike, SONY, Sony Pictures, AT&T, Sony Ericsson, Cuervo, Dewars, Russian Standard Vodka, ETS, GRE, TOEFL, PRAXIS, TOEIC & the NRDC. I have also been privileged to help drive Pharmaceutical Brands like, BMS, Sunovion, Boehringer Ingelheim, Orencia, Plavix, Lunesta, Latuda, Humira, Brovana, Alvesco, Xopenex, Trajenta, Yervoy & Sprycel.

Specialties

Management Style
My management style takes different approaches depending on the team & project. Basically, I fluidly shift between four basic teaching styles depending on the team and work: Formal Authority, Demonstrator, Facilitator and Delegator.
