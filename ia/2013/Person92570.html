Michael Kahane's Summary

I'm a rising second year MFA student/game designer in the Design and Technology program at Parsons the New School for Design in New York City. Before I moved to NYC I was working with a Maryland nonprofit called Moving Maryland Forward where I assisted with the ACLU of Maryland and several other Maryland non-profits in using social media to increase awareness of their campaigns. I'm interested in both games for change and games for fun, assistive technology, interaction design, and the intersections of technology and social justice.

Before Parsons, I graduated from the University of Maryland - College Park. I majored in English Language & Literature with an emphasis in New Media and Film, and I received my degree in May 2011. I was a teaching assistant for the Writing for Blogs course at UMD.

January 2013 – Present (10 months) New York, NY and Guatemala City, Guatemala

•Led a group of students in the Spring 2013 semester on the continuation of a code system to help the Guatemalan government obtain the information they need to provide rural health clinics with proper supplies. Rather than the current system which uses paper forms four times per year, our system allows community leaders to send SMS messages which report the lack of medication, equipment and other concerns, as frequently as necessary, with the goal of strengthening communication between the rural communities and the Government.
•This summer, I am in Guatemala City, Guatemala, working with Centro de Estudios para la Equidad y Gobernanza en los Sistemas de Salud (CEGSS), an organization that works with indigenous populations throughout Guatemala in health and human rights, social participation, accountability and participatory monitoring of public policies and services.
