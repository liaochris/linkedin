Ian McGee's Summary

Seasoned games industry veteran of 20+ years with extensive experience across the spectrum.

As an executive and consultant, I’ve been focused on the mobile and social game spaces since 2009, with a particular focus in free-to-play monetization.

Highlights of my career include:
• Expresso Fitness: Creating the most successful virtual reality commercial exercise bike ever
• AndNow: Learning from Ed “Ecco the Dolphin” Annunziata and great teams in Russia and Hungary
• Sony: Helping to launch the original Playstation in 1995
• Malibu Interactive: Founding a company in the UK with incredible local talent

I thrive in the challenge of startups and companies that act like startups. I love to dive into technical and gameplay details, and really enjoy working with specialists in all areas.

I have a keen appreciation for user experience issues, and regularly play a wide range of current games. Mobile-Local-Social is a current passion.

When not playing or making games, I like to race sailboats on the San Francisco Bay.

Specialties: Mobile Games, Social Games, Production, Production Management, Producing, Producer, People Management, Product Manager, Gaming, Games, User Engagement, Monetization, Analytics, Game Design, Startups, Scheduling, Budgeting, Consulting.

Ian McGee's Experience

Mentor

YetiZen

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Computer Games industry

2011 – Present (2 years) San Francisco Bay Area

YetiZen is the only games focused accelerator, bringing together 150 advisors, 50 investors, and 10 VC firms to mentor accepted startups. YetiZen provides an incredible fire-hose of information that founders will draw on for the life of their company and beyond.

As mentor I give workshops and one-on-one advice to a select group of game startups. Typical topics include MVP feature selection, A/B testing, User Experience, team management, fundraising and elevator pitches. I also help them build and maintain industry connections.

Captain

SailByte Productions, LLC

November 2009 – Present (3 years 11 months) Half Moon Bay, CA

SailByte was born into the maelstrom of the social and mobile free-to-play movement that is still completely transforming the game industry. I saw it as a return to game industry roots with smaller teams building games quickly, and learning from the new markets. The huge advantage of analytics meant we could watch our players as never before, and develop games as an ongoing relationship.

As captain I could help navigate these troubled waters by consulting on mobile and social game design, production, monetization, and user experience.

VP Product

Casualing

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Entertainment industry

2011 – March 2012 (1 year) San Francisco Bay Area

Casualing was one of the first real-money gaming companies focused completely on internet casual and casino games.

As VP of Product helped develop product strategy and drove the movement to mobile. Managed the highly technical issues involved in bringing our HTML5 games to Facebook and smartphones. Redesigned the extensive back-end database and analytics systems to provide platform-independent support. Designed the mobile user experience to support shorter session times and adhoc play.

Co-founder & COO/CTO

Brighter Future Challenge

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Computer Software industry

2010 – 2011 (1 year) Cupertino, CA

Brighter Future Challenge had a mission to help people with diabetes. We envisioned a system to help patients, families and friends understand the implications of living with diabetes, and model successful strategies for living with the condition.

Designed an online game using social game mechanics to encourage education and behavior modification for people with diabetes. Redesigned as a mobile game. Successfully won funding from Start-up Chile.

VP of Software Development

Expresso Fitness

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Health, Wellness and Fitness industry

April 2006 – November 2009 (3 years 8 months)

We grew Expresso to 90 people and sold over 10,000 commercial quality exercise bikes complete with 3d virtual worlds, hundreds of miles of roads, and internet connections supporting over 1 million riders. Amazing stories of personal health earned on our bikes drove the team.

As VP of Software Development created smooth systems for continued software development, built an analytics system from scratch, dealt with scaling issues (just ahead of social games), and dove into Customer Service systems. An amazing software team regularly did the impossible, delivering significant improvements to the user experience, a wholly original game to extend beyond simple riding on roads, and tremendous enhancements to the user website supporting features such as racing against your friends. We even changed graphic engines and moved from Windows to Linux on the bikes.

Created and delivered online contests for riders and clubs, culminating in giveaways of Expresso bikes to the best riders. Improved data collection and processing lead to discoveries about user behavior and motivation, which we rolled into product updates.

Developed issue-detection techniques to proactively identify hardware problems in the field and provide Customer Service with the information needed to repair bikes before riders noticed. Worked closely with CS to identify failure patterns and communicate back to hardware production and assembly.

Continued to actively raise VC money, especially during 2008 when the world was ending and we did dozens of pitches. Ultimately we raised $42.5 Million.

Executive Producer/Designer

Expresso Fitness

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Health, Wellness and Fitness industry

October 2003 – April 2006 (2 years 7 months)

Expresso started as 5 people in a small borrowed room with a white board. The dream was to combine current 3D computer game experiences with commercial-quality gym equipment. Shockingly at this point most gyms looked the same as they did in the 1980’s, with only minor enhancements to the user experience. We spent months beating our heads against the wall of industry stagnation before deciding to build our own bike to support the dream.

Helped develop and deliver pitches eventually resulting in our first round of $1.2M from Enterprise Partners, and then later rounds. Directly managed and contributed to the prototype bike built on a used bike and early FarCry game engine. Hired and ran the software team and designed the art pipeline for creating worlds. Hired firmware and back-end database engineers and drove development of both systems. Installed bikes in health clubs and developed installation/repair processes.

Director of Production

AndNow

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Management Consulting industry

February 1997 – April 2002 (5 years 3 months)

AndNow was a small startup providing original game design, artwork and development. Founded by Ed Annunziata of Ecco the Dolphin fame, the studio drew on significant development resources in Russia and Hungary.

As Director of Production, managed the overseas development teams to design and produce PS1, PS2, windows and web-based games. “Tiny Tank” was a Sony first-party game supported by Sony TV advertising.

Helped design and produce Smallball Baseball - a web-based free-to-play massively multiplayer baseball game released in 2001 and run as an online service. Monetization was by selling tickets that provided extra training and services for team members.

Executive Producer

August 1996 – November 1996 (4 months)

Started development of 4 original titles over 12 SKU’s, including design approvals, technical design reviews, weekly visits and monthly milestones. Worked with VP, Finance to classify last 2 years PC and 32-bit console game titles and sales figures. Helped plan product strategy. Studio was owned by Acclaim.

Senior Producer

Sony Computer Entertainment America

Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; SNE; Entertainment industry

February 1995 – May 1996 (1 year 4 months)

SCEA was a startup of 50 people with the resources of a world-wide company. Industry view was that Sony couldn’t build games, but the early teams were all industry veterans with solid track records. The hardware was constantly changing and the early software libraries were unusable, but somehow we persevered.

Helped build internal development, policies and procedures. Evaluated and worked with external developers and concepts. Participated in troubleshooting hardware/software for PlayStation launch. Designed and helped implement SGI/PC 100Mbps network solution to support 32-bit development. Worked extensively with Alias to streamline art path. Coordinated internal R&D efforts to push the PlayStation hardware. Level 2A+ Alias trained. Installed the first web browser at SCEA, designating it an open terminal.

The US launch of the Playstation on September 9, 1995 was an amazing experience. I happily joined those thousands of people lined up at midnight to buy the first consoles.

Malibu Interactive

Responsible for founding the UK operation, including hiring, locating and leasing premises, purchasing equipment and tools, budgeting, establishing procedures, etc. Trained new hires in production and testing methods. Grew company to 35 staff over 3 locations in UK. Produced titles, coordinated information flow to/from publishers, prepared cost analysis and bids, represented company and solicited new project orders.
