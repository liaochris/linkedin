Karla Porter's Experience

Director of Program Development & Marketing

The Arc of Luzerne County

Nonprofit; 1-10 employees; Nonprofit Organization Management industry

September 2011 – Present (2 years 2 months) Wilkes-Barre, PA

My labor of love here is the program design, development, implementation and growth of TRACE - TRAnsition to Community Employment, a two-year 30 hour per week, employability skills training certificate program, for individuals with intellectual and developmental disabilities, and autism, who aspire to successfully enter the workforce. The program went into the implementation phase September 2012. TRACE is hosted at Luzerne County Community College.

The program focuses on self-determination and skills to succeed as independently as individually possible in the workforce and community. Students develop individual career profiles based on personality, aptitude, interest and ability. They learn about business cultures, policies and protocol, identify occupations that are a potential fit, job shadow, intern, network, and receive job placement assistance upon graduation. Other critical components of the program are work life balance, full community participation, socialization, and soft skills, financial and digital literacy skills training.

Marketing, brand and social media management/ external communications and community building for programs and the organization.

Advocate / participant for regional and state level forums and discussions on inclusion and diversity in the workplace.

The Arc of Luzerne County in partnership with PA Department of Public Welfare ODP organized the PA Disability Employment Summit held October 4-6, 2012 at The Hershey Lodge, again slated for fall 2013 as the PA Disability Employment and Empowerment Summit. http://padesummit.org
