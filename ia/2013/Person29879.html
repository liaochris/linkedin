Daniel Dus' Projects

  • Town of Southbridge, Massachusetts

    • August 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Daniel Dus

    3.4MW utility interconnected solar asset providing power via long term Power Purchase Agreements to the towns of Southbridge and Dudley Massachusetts. The asset is financed, owned and operated by Washington Gas, the project was developed by EPG Solar.

    Roughly 20 acres of solar installed on driven pile and ballasted ground mounted racking, the asset is comprised of Tianwei New Energy modules and SatCon inverters connected to National Grid's 13.8kV distribution system.

  • Jefferson County Public Schools for Panasonic's Coronal Management

    Team Members: Daniel Dus

    4.2 MW installed simultaneously on 30 locations across Colorado's largest school district, the project was recognized by the White House for its innovative public-private partnership. Many schools are more than 50% powered by clean solar electricity. The installations consist of SolarOne and Schuco solar modules, Schuco rack and PVPowered inverters.

  • Town of Orange, Massachusetts

    • September 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Daniel Dus

    2.4MW utility interconnected solar asset providing power via long term Power Purchase Agreements to the town Orange, Massachusetts. The asset is financed, owned and operated by Washington Gas, the project was developed by EPG Solar.

    Roughly 15 acres of solar installed on a screwed-pile, ground mounted racking system, the asset is comprised of Tianwei New Energy modules and SatCon inverters connected to National Grid's 13.8kV distribution system.

  • The Westfield Group

    • April 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Daniel Dus

    2.4MW installed for Westfield Malls, the largest mall-owner, and one of the largest real estate owners in the world by market capitalization. These installations were often performed at night, spanning the sensitive holiday shopping season, in order to limit impact to the ongoing operations of the facility.

    Consisting of carport and rooftop assets, the project portfolio was completed on four sites and offset a majority of the properties' common area meters.

  • CBS Studios

    • December 2011 to Present
    Team Members: Daniel Dus

    Over 1.7MW of solar are offsetting grid power consumption for CBS Studios, including assets powering the Television City facility in the Fairfax district, as well as Studio Center in LA's Studio City district. Carport structures not only power the location's studios and offices, but also provide shade for executive, employee and visitor parking.
