Co-Founder & CTO

Print3D Corporation

January 2008 – November 2011 (3 years 11 months) Las Vegas, USA

Print3D is a platform which gives CAD (Computer Aided Design) users the ability to get live prices for 3D printed parts of their designs right within their CAD software by simply clicking a button. When they are ready to place an order, they click another button in the CAD software and their designs are securely uploaded to Print3D servers. Orders are managed using a back office 3D printing management system. The parts are 3D printed and are shipped to the customer. All along the customer can keep track of the status of his parts from withing the CAD software itself.

I designed, developed and implemented all the pieces of desktop and web software of the Print3D platform. In April 2011 3D Systems (NYSE:DDD) acquired Print3D Corporation.

Deelip Menezes' Publications

  • OpenCAD - A Step by Step Guide to Developing a Professional CAD Application

    • Deelip.com
    • March 14, 2009
    Authors: Deelip Menezes

    The book is written as a short story describing the evolution of a CAD application called OpenCAD. The reader starts with an empty Visual C++ project. In each chapter, the reader adds features to OpenCAD, first making it a DWG reader, then a viewer and finally an editor. Finally the reader ends up with a professional CAD application complete with an AutoCAD-like command prompt and plug-in architecture, thereby giving it the ability to be extended by means of DRX plug-ins.
