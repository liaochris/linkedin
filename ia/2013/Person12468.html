Beatrice Benne, Ph.D.'s Summary

SENSE-MAKER – STRATEGIST – ARCHITECT OF A BETTER FUTURE

Beatrice Benne founded Soma Integral Consulting in 2010 with a mission to facilitate the resolution of adaptive challenges while focusing on the well-being of social and environmental ecosystems. Beatrice brings to her clients a broad range of skills and expertise including a systems approach to organizational management and change, strategic management, leadership capacity development, a creative and collaborative approach to problem investigation and resolution, and work process performance assessment and improvement. She seeks to combine her skills to facilitate transformative change towards more sustainable business practices.

Beatrice co-founded Transformative Sustainable Solutions, Inc (TSSI)—a Portland, OR start-up—where she served as Product and Marketing Manager for ProjectDX, an innovative web-based service enabling governments and NGOs to meet their environmental and sustainability goals through a community-wide engagement approach. At TSSI, Beatrice worked with new customers to understand their stormwater management, energy efficiency and renewable energy programs and advise them on how to prepare for the launch of these programs in their community.

Beatrice worked 10 years at Bechtel Corporation—San Francisco, CA, in the Information Systems & Technology group, where she was responsible for the definition of short- and long-term strategies for diverse web-based technologies. She also served as a Six Sigma Black Belt for two years supporting engineering and construction projects in the U.S.

Beatrice has been a fifth-year instructor with Bainbridge Graduate Institute (BGI), a distance learning MBA and Certificate program in sustainable business where she co-taught a Strategy & Implementation course and a Systems Thinking in Action course. She currently teaches the Strategic Management course for the Design MBA at the California College of the Arts in San Francisco.

Beatrice Benne, Ph.D.'s Education

University of California, Berkeley

Ph.D., Architecture, Social Systems, Living Systems

1998 – 2005

Ph.D Dissertation: "Managing AEC Project Organizations at the Edge of Chaos: An Analysis of AEC Projects' Adaptive Capacity from a Living Systems Perspective."

Through the Ph.D. journey I have investigated interdisciplinary fields such as social science; organizational theories; behavioral theory; economics theory; cognitive science; communication science; complexity theory, living systems theory; and evolutionary biology. Certainly, my research does not make me an expert in any one of these different disciplines, but I can apply these findings and knowledge to the solution of complex business problems.

College of Environmental Design, Berkeley, CA, USA

Master of Science, Architecture, Design Theories and Methods

August 1996 – May 1998

The MS program focused on the study of Collaborative Design in the context of multi-disciplinary, geographical dispersed project teams in the AEC industry. Specifically, I analyzed how professionals (e.g., architects, engineers, clients, contractors, and others AEC consultants) involved in the design of a facility work with one another, exchange information, coordinate their work, collaborate, and negotiate to resolve inter-disciplinary conflicts. I also investigated the impact of information technology on the transformation of designers’ work processes and analyzed the requirements for developing conceptual web-based environments to support distributed work.
For my MS thesis I developed a Web-based database of architectural components that could be used in the context of a 3D Computer-Aided design model to assess a building’s energy efficiency.
