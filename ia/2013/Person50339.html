Greg Mulholland's Summary

I am currently pursuing my MBA as a member of the class of 2014 at the Stanford Graduate School of Business. While in school, a classmate of mine and I began to work on an idea for a company that has become Citrine Informatics (http://www.citrine.io). We are continuing to build it over the summer of 2013 and into the school year.

Prior to that, I was the Director of Strategic Alliances at Sageworks, where I analyzed economic data around private companies and oversaw the development of corporate partnerships. Before joining Sageworks in 2011, I worked as Director of New Product Development for Kyma Technologies, a small maker of semiconductor materials, building partnerships to develop next generation LED and power electronics technologies. I earned my MPhil in Materials Science from the University of Cambridge my BS in Electrical Engineering and Computer Engineering from North Carolina State University.

Specialties: GaN materials, III-N materials, LEDs, thin films, oxides, solar cells, green technology, software, web-enabled tools, new technologies, data analysis, marketing of innovative technologies, small business strategy

    Executive Board Member

    The Park Alumni Society (PAS) was formed in March 2003 by alumni of the Park Scholarships program at NC State University. Founded with the notion of ”giving back”—both to the program and the University—it received its 501c3 non-profit status in 2004.

    The PAS was created with the following goals in mind:
    -- serve as a clearing house for information for Park alumni
    -- foster interaction between alumni and current scholars;
    -- be involved in the recruitment and selection process
    -- assist the Park Scholarships program in capacities needed;
    -- promote positive growth within the University culture;
    -- fulfill obligation as members of the general alumni population and
    -- raise/tend funds, for activities to be determined.

    At the heart of its mission is communication—between scholars past and present, with the Park Scholarships administration and with the University at large.

    Park alumni and current Park Scholars are eligible to join the PAS. There are no membership dues. Members enjoy access to an online community and planned events and reunions.
