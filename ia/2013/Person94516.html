Mike Reekie's Summary

Michael Joined IBBS (http://www.ibbs.com) in July 2012. Michael leads three dedicated teams of engineers delivering IT, Systems, and Provisioning services to ISPs with both residential and commercial service offerings.

Michael began his career as a software developer at The MITRE Corporation & Fidelity Investments before transitioning to on-site project management at Boston Technology. There he deployed carrier-class voicemail systems world-wide. Michael moved to American Internet as a Systems Engineer where he supported early data-over-cable inter-operability testing at CableLabs. Michael joined Cisco in 1998 with American Internet.

At Cisco Michael became involved in performance and capacity testing of large scale multi-protocol provisioning systems. Michael played a key role in performance testing the Cisco Network Registrar (CNR) DHCP protocol engine during its redesign including contributing to the design and development of several software test harnesses. He created and taught a three week CNR training class which drew participants from world-wide TAC locations. He wrote to and supported extension points and APIs in the CNR and Broadband Access Center (BACC) products.

Michael became a Cisco consultant and worked with the provisioning engineering team at the largest of Cisco’s cable Internet Service Provider customers. In that role he was the Advanced Services technical lead on a project that decentralized their mission critical customer device provisioning services.

Michael Joined the Cisco Unified Computing Architecture practice in 2009. In that role his clients looked to him as a trusted advisor on the UCS particularly around the chassis internals, the UCS XML API, and operationalization of the system.

Michael was part of a team awarded “United States Patent 7848258: Dynamically transitioning static network addresses” and was a Contributing technical reviewer to “Deploying IPv6 in Broadband Access Networks" ISBN: 978-0-470-50843-5
