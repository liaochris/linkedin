Jillian Maslow's Summary

Jillian is an accomplished milliner, costumer and prop builder who has had her work shown in film, video and television and also exhibited in galleries.

Most recently she has been seen on Lifetime TV and USA Network as a technical expert on reality fashion shows, and her metal armor and crowns can currently be seen on the stage at NYC Opera.

Jillian is also an avant-garde couture artist creating 3-dimensional assemblages blending couture fashion silhouettes, precision craftsmanship and common objects with powerful themes. Two sculptural gowns include 48lbs of bullet shells & dog-tags in ‘TARGET-PEACE’; 2,351 guitar picks and metal in ‘Don’t Fret’.

Jillian has a BA in fashion design from FIT and studied couture design at Parsons in Paris . She is from a long lineage (four generations) of custom furniture manufacturers (NYC & Milan).
Jillian has a well-honed eye for balancing surface designs with classic design silhouettes, and possesses a unique ability to mimic the physical drape of cloth using merely found objects and gravity.

Her sculptural gowns are constructed with great care so that her work can move gracefully on the female form while appearing like cloth when displayed on fashion mannequins in a gallery setting.
