Jason Bramble's Summary

Do not follow where the path may lead. Go instead where there is no path and leave a trail.
~Ralph Waldo Emerson

Specialties: Web and Mobile Application Development, UI & UX Design, & Agile Development

My Background:

I have been programming for a long time. Not to date myself - the first language I learned was assembly on an Apple IIe when I was 10 years old. In high school, I designed and hosted custom door games for dial up bulletin boards like Wildcat and PC Board, and was one of the largest FidoNet nodes in the Northeast. One of my earliest achievements was developing an ANSI based shopping mall for NuTech Online Systems just as the internet was becoming popular in the early 90s.

What I think makes me unique from a lot of developers is that I have always been able to engage in the entire life cycle of a software project - that is going from a concept to a final product. Helping clients get past functional gaps, or under-defined acceptance requirements has always been a talent of mine. It is why I love agile development so much. It focuses on quick delivery, constant customer collaboration, and the real world realization - this project is going to evolve throughout it's life cycle. It fits how I operate.

Another thing that probably makes me different is my art & design background. In a way, it is kind of a detriment because most recruiters who call me want me to either be a code developer or a UI/UX designer... not both. But in a consulting world, it has been an asset, and is probably why I have been given the opportunity to work on most the high profile projects I have worked on.

In summary, application design - whether web, mobile, or pc based - is just something I love to do, and I hope that shows here. Because of that, I have even developed some of my own ideas, which I own and operate today. Below are only a couple examples:
