Danilo Arao's Summary

DANILO ARAÑA ARAO is an assistant professor of the Department of Journalism at the University of the Philippines College of Mass Communication (UP CMC) in Diliman, Quezon City.

He is a member of the Board of Editors of Bulatlat.com (a weekly online news magazine). He writes a column in Filipino ("Konteksto") for Pinoy Weekly (pinoyweekly.org) and another one in English ("Subtext") for The Lobbyist (thelobbyist.biz).

Prof. Arao is managing editor of the Philippine Journalism Review - the only refereed journal on journalism in Southeast Asia - published by the Center for Media Freedom and Responsibility (CMFR).

From February 10, 2011 to October 31, 2012, he worked as assistant vice-president for public affairs and director, System Information Office (SIO) of UP. He also served as associate editor of the UP-based refereed journal Social Science Diliman from 2007 to 2011.

He was on special detail from March 2009 to February 2010 as a visiting professor at Hannam University's Linton Global College in Daejeon, South Korea.

Prior to teaching fulltime at UP Diliman, he was editor-in-chief (1994-1999) and head of research (2000-2001) of IBON Foundation, an independent research think-tank. He also taught Political Science part-time at the De La Salle University (DLSU)-Manila from 1993 to 1995.

Prof. Arao earned a Bachelor of Arts (BA) in Communication (major in Journalism), cum laude, from UP Diliman and was adjudged Most Outstanding Journalism Student upon his graduation. He finished his Master of Arts (MA) in Philippine Studies, with High Distinction, at DLSU-Manila and was awarded a gold medal for academic excellence.

For more information about Prof. Arao, please visit his website at http://www.dannyarao.com.

To know his major book projects from 1997 to 2012, please go to http://faculty.masscomm.upd.edu.ph/dannyarao/publications.

Specialties

print journalism, radio journalism, online journalism, advocacy journalism, alternative journalism, campus journalism, citizen journalism, media criticism, media ethics, media research, statistical analysis, political economy, technical writing, editing, publication design and layout, web authoring, blogging
