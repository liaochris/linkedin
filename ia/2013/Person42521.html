Erik Anderson's Experience

Founder & President

The Healthy Non-Profit

May 2011 – Present (2 years 8 months)

The Healthy Non-Profit LLC is a small consulting practice focused on non-profit management, organizational capacity building, resource development / fundraising, and executive coaching. Our mission is based around the idea that "those we serve deserve sustainability".

Director, RD Club Services

Boys & Girls Club of America

Nonprofit; 501-1000 employees; Nonprofit Organization Management industry

2006 – May 2011 (5 years)

In 2006 and 2007, I worked with local Boys & Girls Club affiliates on development, planning and implementation of their annual campaign focused solely on individual giving. This work occurred with Clubs throughout the 13 state area that Boys & Girls Clubs of America (BGCA) refers to as the Midwest Region (e.g. North Dakota, South Dakota, Nebraska, Iowa, Missouri, Minnesota, Kentucky, Illinois, Wisconsin, Indiana, Michigan, Ohio, and West Virginia).

Starting in 2008, BGCA received a 3-year grant from the Lilly Endowment to help Boys & Girls Club corporations in Indiana grow organizational capacity in the areas of Resource Development and Board Development. While in the same position, the job changed in the sense that my responsibilities broadened. In addition to helping Indiana Clubs with developing/implementing an annual campaign, I helped as an internal consultant with the following: strategic planning, board development planning, marketing planning, resource development planning.

From a job functions perspective, I am a consultant, facilitator and trainer rolled into one.

In addition to my work with local Boys & Girls Clubs, I've played a role with conference planning, managing an exhibitors/vendors program, and even co-chaired BGCA's 2009 & 2010 national Employee Campaign.

Executive Director

Boys & Girls Club of Elgin

Nonprofit; 11-50 employees; Nonprofit Organization Management industry

March 2000 – April 2006 (6 years 2 months)

As the Chief Executive Officer of the Boys & Girls Club of Elgin, I was responsible for finance, resource development, board development, human resources, facility management, planning, and program development & implementation. The following were a few of my accomplishments:

* Doubled the size and diversified the Club's revenue budget
* Developed & implemented the following plans & tools: Strategic Plan, Board Development Plan, Facilities Plan, Marketing Plan, Resource Development Plan, Compensation & Benefits Plan, Marketing Plan, Program Plan, Technology Plan, and Internal Financial Control Policies.
* Opened two school-based programs.
* Doubled individual giving & special event revenue streams
* Received the Illinois Area Council’s 2002 & 2005 Resource Development Award.
* Received the Illinois Area Council’s 2004 & 2005 Marketing Award.
* Oversight of capital campaign feasibility study and experience with capital campaigning.
* Secured $1 million lead capital challenge gift.

District Executive
