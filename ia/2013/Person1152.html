Alan Colmenares' Summary

Entrepreneur, investor and advisor, Alan Colmenares is currently involved with two accelerators in Latin America: the Founder Institute (a pre-idea accelerator) and Socialatom Ventures, which accepts startups from countries such as Colombia, Venezuela, Chile and others, where he and his partners, have also made equity investments in some of the accelerated companies. Previously, he was a Strategic Investment Manager at Intel Capital presenting Mexican and Brazilian deals to the company's investment committee in Santa Clara, California.

He has worked at startups in Silicon Valley. These include companies such as SCO, Madge Networks and NetManage and, also, wrote and edited article for InfoWorld Magazine in Menlo Park, CA. In Latin America, he has a demonstrated history of getting new businesses off the ground for companies such as Intel (e.g., motherboards and servers), Oracle (new technologies) and others. As a consultant he has given workshops on topics such as Software-as-a-Service for Microsoft Corporation and Business Models and Digital Strategies for the Colombian Chamber of Commerce.

he has contributed articles to VentureBeat, NYTimes.com, Computer Reseller News as well as Latin American publications such as Dinero.com and La Republica as well as for his own blog, TropicalGringo (http://www.tropicalgringo.com).

Specialties: Strategic Investment, Business Development, Channel Development, Technology Evangelization
