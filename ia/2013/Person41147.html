Emiel van den Boomen's Summary

In a 10th century Japanese folktale called the Tale of the Bamboo Cutter, a mysterious babygirl was discovered by a bamboo cutter inside the stalk of a glowing bamboo plant. This man found something extraordinary while doing his daily work. As a marketing & communications professional I try to find creative solutions in things that at first glance seem to be common, but have a lot of unexpected hidden potential. In that way I am looking for serendipity value.

That serendipity value is also a clear subject of my travel writing. I travel not to tick off my bucket list but to get inspired by local, daily life. I tend to travel slowly and let unexpected happenings guide me.

Our family is passionate about traveling. I write on my personal travel blog about unique destinations and how to improve your travel experiences. Please do visit www.ActofTraveling.com to get some serious travel inspiration.

When I'm not traveling I work to create marketing communication tools (online and offline) and manage marketing projects in a B2B international environment. My Japanese studies and consecutive stay in Japan allowed me to view people and companies from a different perspective, hence my Japanese folktale introduction.
