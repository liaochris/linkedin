Lara Moffat, ASLA's Summary

Differentiation is key in today’s marketplace…Call on LM Creative Consulting to strategically set your business apart.

Lara’s diverse education and green industry experience makes her approach to strategic marketing, public relations, and branding ideal for any size business. As the former Director of Marketing for a nationally recognized Design/Build/Maintenance firm, she understands the art and science needed to market to clients desiring a luxury good as well as those requesting a service product. With degrees in art, psychology, horticulture, and landscape architecture, Lara brings a unique perspective to the landscape industry. Through her strategic process she will help you analyse your organization, develop an inclusive marketing plan, and align an integral message- both internally and externally- responsive to your comprehensive business direction. Lara tailors each program entirely and welcomes the opportunity to work with firms from fresh faced to established or boutique to broad-based.

Respected both within and outside of the landscape industry; Lara is an author, photographer, speaker, and serves on various boards locally and nationally.

  • Capitalizing on public relations within a marketing plan: Start with planning…

    • PLANET Front Page
    • April 2012
    Authors: Lara Moffat, ASLA

    Plans are nothing; planning is everything. —Dwight D. Eisenhower

    When I hear the statement, “A plan is a means to the end,” I side with Eisenhower. Planning is everything and truly is the means to an end and not an end unto itself. I don’t mean a plan is useless, but that for a plan ultimately to be successful, the planning must be strategic, focused yet comprehensive, relevant to time and place, and allow for free thought. When this occurs, the resulting plan will be effective, achievable, quantifiable, and adaptable.

    Are your marketing and public relations strategies working in tandem with your business model, target audience, and community culture? Within the last year, the Public Relations Society of America (PRSA) has amended the definition of public relations to “a strategic communication process that builds mutually beneficial relationships between organizations and their publics.” So, essentially, PRSA is reinforcing the fact that public relations is a course of action. One that is strategic in nature—creating a platform of communication that relates to and is responsive to both an organization and its public(s).

  • Art And The Built Landscape In The Central Business District Of Dallas: Influences From An Identifiable Era In The Late Twentieth Century

    • Masters of Landscape Architecture Thesis
    • 2008
    Authors: Lara Moffat, ASLA

    In the mid to late 1970s, Dallas’ emergence as a center of culture and art became outwardly evident in the urban landscape. International and local designers were commissioned to make their mark on the gridded environment; Dallas City Hall was the first major development during this period. Internationally acclaimed architect I.M. Pei’s inverted pyramidal structure and
    ceremonial plaza were soon followed by Carpenter Park with its noteworthy environmental art piece ‘Portal Park Slice’ by Robert Irwin.
    Significant human communities typically have a collection of spaces that are both private and public, mostly privately funded but publicly used. In the sites’ development a group of financiers, developers, designers, artists and in some cases, city officials and individual citizens outlined the usage by defining the purpose and function of the site in order to aesthetically achieve their means. Behind this collaboration, amass a series of stories; narratives that explain the who, why and what in the development of each project.
