Julia M. Feltham's Summary

Julia is a pragmatic optimist, obsessed with Atlantic Canada & makes it her job to help people make their living building a better world. Her passion is helping people & organizations connect their principles & values and visions for a better future into their everyday, and navigating complexity.

She finds collaborative ways to meet community needs: Connecting grassroots organizations to bigger institutions, strengthening social capital, hosting spaces to co-create & enrich big picture thinking. Julia surveys the past & present of Atlantic Canada to help build on our strengths & dreams. Whether through events, music, entrepreneurship, curriculum, government, not-for-profits, education or entertainment: Cultivating joie de vivre is her job.

Youth & Community Animator

Falls Brook Centre

Nonprofit; 11-50 employees; Environmental Services industry

May 2012 – Present (1 year 5 months) New Brunswick, Canada

Falls Brook Centre is currently taking our youth entrepreneurship programming
beyond the borders of New Brunswick, to engage youth across the Maritimes in five
entrepreneurship, leadership and knowledge-sharing events. Our intent is that through
peer-to-peer networking, skill-building, and showcasing the successes of local youth the
capacity of young people in Maritime communities will be strengthened and broadened.



We are proposing to provide youth with a creative, diversified environment in
which to take up the challenge of envisioning a new economy for the Maritimes. Skilled
youth and youth with confidence in their own abilities will have new reasons to become
agents of positive change in their local economic system. Perhaps most importantly,
they will share their successes and lessons with each other in an environment of
collaboration, mutual support, and peer mentorship. We propose these skills-training
events in the hopes of inspiring youth to move forward as leaders of a local economy that
is in balance with society and the environment.

January 2011 – Present (2 years 9 months) Halifax, Canada Area

In addition to hosting the co-working space, helping with event-planning and collaborative projects with The Hub Halifax, Julia is helped to compile a survey & white paper for Nova Scotians on Social Enterprise.

The Hub along with Atlantic Council for Community and Social Enterprise are being supported by The Province to explore how organizations in Nova Scotia engage in Enterprise (money exchanged for sale of service) in order to accomplish an environmental, social, economic or cultural mission.
The process itself is helping the community identify itself and support changes needed to create a more vibrant environment for Social and Community Enterprise to thrive and it is the largest & most encompassing study of its kind to be undertaken in Nova Scotia.
