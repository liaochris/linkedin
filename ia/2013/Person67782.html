Jon Cable's Summary

Web professional with over a decade of experience designing, developing and delivering web portable applications and social experiences. Starting in early 1999 I began working full time on e-commerce projects at Able Ammo in Huntsville Texas growing an immediate interest in Web Architecture. l spent the next 9 years developing social media projects for our own ventures slowly teaching myself PERL, ASP, PHP, HTML, CSS, Java, and Flash.

After moving to Austin, Texas I took my hand at working on web projects in the world of online publishing. At PCI I worked on several ventures which included custom development projects for American Society of Interior Designers and a Portfolio of over 15 other web/print projects daily.

Continuing down the same path; I now work for Photon Factory (IC3D.tv) developing online web portals that provide organized data structure, real-time streaming and 3D browser controls that feed directly from our Desktop application suites from offshore operations. Our tools provide project mangers and oil executives on-site communications from offshore operations saving time, money and providing real-time backup and management solutions.

Principle Founder

EverydayJunglist

October 1999 – Present (14 years 1 month) Austin, Texas Area

In 1999 two friends and I founded an underground music organization entitled Everyday Junglist (EDJ) the idea was simply to share our passion for the genre Drum and Bass. Little did we know for the next 13 years we would nurture a community capable of sharing ideas about underground music by empowering artists themselves to contribute news and media about its evolving culture. When we moved to Austin in 04′ interest spiked and grew to almost 17 members/artists, as a collective we quickly started hosting/booking monthly, weekly and headliner performances.

We soon realized many of the talents within the new collective had more to offer then just music. With so many like minded individuals with a passion to deliver underground music and armed with a diverse professional background in web, system engineering and design we started with more challenging ideas. Very quickly we began exploring ways to expand the social experience by adding features to stream our own live remote events, integrate the huge collection of music supplied by our existing audience and eventually allowing the artists themselves to take over the airwaves.

EDJ has since grown to almost 1500 members who have shared over 500GB of media about its culture. It represents a contributed effort of thousands of lines of custom PHP/Perl that has reached millions of artists/fans from across the globe. Its been a truly inspiring journey to lead EDJ’s progression and I feel truly blessed to be surrounded by so many talented artists and friends from across the globe!
