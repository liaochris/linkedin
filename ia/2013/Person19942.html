Carl Johnson's Summary

ARTIST:
I am a full time artist, painting the changing faces and places of local culture.


LARGE-SCALE MOSAIC
With my partner, Liz Nicklus, I design and construct large-scale mosaic murals for corporate and public installations. I am available to teach mosaic workshops, and to create smaller scale installations for private residences. I have a wide range of experience with tile and terrazzo, from basic tiling of kitchens and baths to an understanding of the mechanics involved in erecting large-scale public art.


WRITER, PUBLISHER:
I have written and co-written several non-fiction books, and edited several more. I have more than a decade of experience in desktop publishing, page layout and editing. I offer affordable editing and publishing services to any writer that is interested in self-publishing.

GRAPHIC ARTIST:
I am experienced in Photoshop, and designed industrial equipment catalogs and brochures professionally.

WEB DESIGNER:
I design websites for artists and artistic types. My rates are affordable. In order for an artist to be taken seriously, a facebook page is not enough, and an Etsy page does not allow for enough customization. Free websites such as wix.com are better than nothing, but having your own domain is the only way to ensure that your personal website comes up on internet searches.

ELECTRONICS TECHNICIAN:
I have over two decades experience in electronics and communications troubleshooting and repair.

MECHANIC:
I addition to the electronics experience, I am mechanically adept. I have decades experience troubleshooting and maintaining copier machines, facsimile, and computers and peripherals. More recently I have been involved in the maintenance and retrofitting of extremely large recycling equipment, machines that shred automobiles into 4-inch pieces of metal.

Carl Johnson's Publications

  • INFERNO - burning the bridges behind us

    • Inferno Publishing
    Authors: Carl Johnson, Dr. Debra Miller, Rachel Citrino, "Sitarbob" Price, Larry Ericksen, Morel Pagano, Rob Bilenky, Anna Vosburgh

    Inferno Newspaper began life as a conspiratorial conversation in the back room of a neighborhood bar in the winter of 2002. The first issue ran off the presses in spring of 2003.

    The paper caused controversy from the very first edition. There was no middle ground; people either loved it or they hated it.

    We gathered some of the brightest talent in the south Jersey and Philadelphia region to contribute gratis. We received accolades from New York and Washington for our content and wit. Working on a shoestring budget, every issue was at risk of being our last.

    This book is a recap of our first seven years, with some of our best articles, best humor pieces, best art and best covers. Publisher and writer Carl B. Johnson contributes a new running commentary throughout, taking the reader on a journey of the rise and fall of an underground arts newspaper. Interspersed with some of the best articles, Carl leads you into the quagmire of local politics and puritanism that fueled and inspired the inferno that was INFERNO.

  • Bar Exam - musings from the barstool

    • Inferno Publishing
    Authors: Carl Johnson

    Bar Exam is an art book of Carl B. Johnson's bar series of paintings, and musings about the disappearing neighborhood tavern.

    "I'd rather put a stick in my eye than read this book."
    ~Morel Pagano, Newfield, NJ

  • Bar Exam, too

    • Inferno Publishing
    • 2012
    Authors: Carl Johnson

    Carl B. Johnson takes a journey to the seedy side of town, with this travelogue of pubs and drinking establishments. More original paintings of bars, both famous and unknown. More wisdom from the bar stool. More opinions from your favorite barfly that has no lack of words to say...

    Key West, New Orleans, Cape May, and locales more obscure - any bar stool provides Carl with fodder for his paintbrush.

  • Mosaic Murals: How to tackle large-scale installations

    • Inferno Publishing
    Authors: Carl Johnson, Liz Nicklus

    Artists Liz Nicklus and Carl B. Johnson explain the intricacies of large-scale mosaic mural installations.

    Professional artists by trade, this team has designed massive mosaic murals for both interior and exterior architectural installations.

    They tell you what to do, and more importantly, what not to do. They are sharing from years of personal experience.
