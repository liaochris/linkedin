Keith Cupp's Summary

Keith Cupp serves as President of Gazelles International Coaching Association (CEO Verne Harnish "Mastering the Rockefeller Habits"), is a Certified Gazelle’s coach, and Affiliate Partner with Executive Mentors and Trainers, a premiere Pacific Northwest coaching and consulting group, and is based in the Portland, Oregon metropolitan area. He brings to Gazelle’s clients over 20 years “in the trenches” operating experience as a business executive in the high technology, real estate, aerospace and transportation industries. He has served on leadership teams in the capacity of President, Chief Operating Officer, VP of Sales & Marketing and Director of Finance, all in high growth companies. These leadership experiences create tremendous “connection” with Gazelle’s client executive teams, along with the ability to empathize with the daily challenges and opportunities they face as leaders. Keith served on two executive management teams whose companies were awarded “Fastest Growth Companies” in Washington State and understands how to create award winning results and cultures. He is a talented communicator and proven leader who understands corporate culture, collaborative process and how to assist organizations and their leadership to optimize the balance between people, performance and profit. Keith is a native of Oregon, graduate of Louisiana State University, and a distinguished veteran of the United States Naval Nuclear Power Program, having been awarded the Navy Achievement Medal while serving aboard the USS Enterprise. He serves his community through volunteering as a baseball coach, his local church and contributing his time to children’s causes in the Pacific Northwest and Baja Mexico. In Fall 2009 Keith was invited to join the Harvard Business Review Advisory Council.

Specialties

Executive team coaching & development, strategic planning and accountability, leadership development & transitions in multi-generation family businesses. Specific expertise in Sales & Marketing and Investment (Finance) Development.
