Bob Cooley's Summary

As a strategic and creative lead, my range of expertise stems from a diverse background in digital and traditional media, drawing on three decades of growth and professional involvement as a Creative Director, User Interface Designer/Developer, Photographer and Journalist. I am also adept at project management, able to coordinate the complex sets of technological, architectural, creative and budgetary challenges that face every project.

As a partner, I foster collaboration by empowering stakeholders to become part of the strategic process while carefully managing expectations and cultivating ongoing communications.

As a manager, I engage my team not only as a mentor, but also as a hands-on leader who inspires cooperative innovation and rewards each individual’s ingenuity. With ten years’ experience in managing teams and coordinating projects, I’m happy to roll up my sleeves and be directly involved in the creation of my partners campaigns.

Prior to my work in interactive media, I spent a decade and a half as an internationally published photojournalist and commercial photographer with a wide range of assignments and credits including LIFE magazine, Sports Illustrated, The Economist, Policy Review, The Associated Press, Herman Miller, Haworth, Coca Cola, Pella Windows, and many more.

Now I donate my photography skills to philanthropic organizations like the Orphaned Starfish Foundation, where I travel and document the stories of life in the orphanages of Latin and Central America.

Bob Cooley has been speaking through photography for over two decades.

His work has appeared in Life magazine, Sports Illustrated, Golf Digest, The Washington Post, Policy Review, The Economist, The Detroit Free Press, and Weekly Reader.

He has won awards from The Michigan State Press Association, The Hoosier State Press Association, The National Press Photographers Association, and the Associated Press.

Bob’s photos have also been used in advertising and marketing for companies including The Coca Cola Company, Herman Miller,
Simon & Schuster, Spalding, and Pella Windows.

Born in San Antonio, TX, Bob grew up in southwestern Michigan, where he developed his love for photography. After starting his photography career in the Midwest, Bob moved to New York City to work as an art director and as a creative director for interactive and print business and marketing initiatives. He has worked extensively with major corporations as well as executives and partners of smaller firms.

Bob now lives in Greenwich Village and continues photography work around the United States and abroad.
