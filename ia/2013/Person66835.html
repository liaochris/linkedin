John Ohab, Ph.D.'s Education

University of California, Los Angeles

Ph.D., Neuroscience

2002 – 2007

My work with Dr. Tom Carmichael examined the role of neural stem cells in brain repair and functional recovery after stroke. The studies defined a unique cellular environment in which neural stem cell and vascular interactions induce neuronal regeneration. A better understanding of this brain environment may lead to stem cell therapies for human stroke patients. Funded by NIH Ruth Kirschstein NRSA and Neural Repair Training Grant.

Resulting publications:

• Gregorian et al. (2009) Pten deletion in adult neural stem/progenitor cells enhances constitutive neurogenesis. J Neurosci 29(6) 1874-86.

• Ohab JJ & Carmichael ST (2008) Post-stroke neurogenesis: principles of migration and localization of immature neurons. The Neuroscientist 14(4) 369-380.

• Ohab JJ et al. (2006) A neurovascular niche for neurogenesis after stroke. J Neurosci 26(50)13007-16.

• Tsai PT, Ohab JJ et al. (2006) A critical role of erythropoietin receptor in neurogenesis. J Neurosci 26(4) 1269-1274.

Activities and Societies: Brain Awareness Week, Society for Neuroscience, Johnnie Cochran Jr. Junior High Science Fair, UCLA Graduate Student Resource Center Program Committee, Neuroscience Undergraduate Mentorship Program, Neuroscience Admissions Committee, Neuroscience Council of Classes

University of California, Santa Barbara

B.S. (with High Honors and Distinction), Biopsychology

1999 – 2002

My senior thesis with Dr. Ben Reese examined the geometric properties of the dopaminergic amacrine cell mosaic in the mouse retina. We used spatial patterning analyses and computer simulations to determine how this cell population spaced itself within the retina. The results indicated that dopaminergic amacrine cells are positioned according to an exclusion zone around the cell body, possibly created by lateral inhibition during cell fate determination or local dendritic competition.

Resulting publications:

• Raven MA, Eglen SJ, Ohab JJ, Reese BE (2003) Determinants of the exclusion zone in dopaminergic amacrine cell mosaics. J Comp Neurol 461(1):123-36.

Activities and Societies: Vice President UCSB Habitat for Humanity, UCSB Honors Program, Honors Provost Council, Academic Research Consortium Fellow, Golden Key International Honor Society,Mortar Board National College Honor Society, National Society of Collegiate Honors
