Julien Perronneau's Summary

After earning a bachelor’s degree in marketing from HEC Montréal, I worked for the next two years on the launch of weblocal.ca, one of the first online platforms for researching and evaluating local businesses, founded by Transcontinental Media (Canada) and YellowBot (USA).

In late 2010, I started working in the advertising industry, first at Optimum Public Relations (now Citizen Optimum), followed by Cossette and Revolver 3, where my roles involved defining social media strategies and a diverse range of responsibilities including competitive intelligence, crisis management, contest planning and content marketing.
I gained invaluable experience at all three agencies, working with clients such as Liberté, L'Oréal (Lancôme, Kiehl's and Biotherm), La Maison Simons, Air Transat, Montréal Science Centre, the Business Development Bank of Canada and Transports Québec.

In the fall of 2012, I joined the Business Development Bank of Canada to develop and implement strategic communication plans for its social media initiatives. BDC promotes entrepreneurship by providing highly tailored financing, venture capital and consulting services to entrepreneurs, with a special focus on small and medium-sized businesses.

My main area of professional interest lies in digital brand communication strategies, as well as the mechanics of online relationship marketing (both customer acquisition and loyalty), with a particular focus on performance tracking and measurement.
