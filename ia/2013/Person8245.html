In my fifteenth year working directly with top-ranking executives, managing directors, and company principals in the corporate offices of major organizations such as Chase Bank, Kraft Foods, and United Brands, I opened the doors to Best Resumes of New York in 1993.

I knew instinctively what was needed to transition from a corporate environment to working privately with clients seeking career/job transitions of their own. In short, taking stock in my career, understanding what was needed in my next venture, and learning/mastering the skills needed to "hit the ground running" and achieve continued success.

These are steps I take to help my clients identify what they can bring to their next position. I focus on what they have done to define/brand them; separate them from competing job seekers; and help them to shine while keeping the position's requirements front and center.

I don't take short cuts. I am passionate about what I do. Clients know they have a career partner in me - someone they can contact anytime to ask a question or for a quick confidence booster before an interview. I can listen intently and can "get on my soapbox" to educate clients about what's important and what's not.

No matter the scenario, one thing is certain -- clients appreciate my knowledge and writing talent, and my honest "NY-style" candidness. In fact, it is not uncommon for me to tell a job seeker that their resume is fine and that they do not need my services. If you would like a second opinion about your resume, email it to me using the contact info above.

To learn more, visit www.e-bestresumes.com. Then give me a call to discuss. You'll be glad you did.
