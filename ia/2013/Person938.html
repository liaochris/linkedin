Nancy O'Mallon's Summary

Nancy O'Mallon grew up on an 80-acre family farm in Western Pennsylvania. She wrote produced and directed two documentaries, The Mighty Humble Blueberry and New Jersey's Red October. Her documentaries won awards nationally, internationally and aired on local Public Broadcasting Stations.

Award-winning filmmaker, writer and dynamic speaker, she has discovered, documented and catalogued hundreds of hours of film footage, archival materials and first hand accounts related to food and agriculture.

Ms. O’Mallon works as producer on additional diverse film and projects and volunteers for business, community and historical organizations, including two New Jersey film commissions.

In 2009, after completing her documentaries and determined to continue to educate people about food and agriculture through media, O’Mallon formed the media company, About Harvest, LLC.

Since 2010 she has lead the "Food Science and Technology Group" at The Global Leaders, a business social networking community. The mission of The Global Leaders is to connect senior leaders and facilitate business development. The Food Science and Technology group builds conversations between scientists and the business, philanthropic, and educational communities in the "global virtual room" made available by TGL with a purpose of advancing new projects.

Serving as the 2012 media affiliate for the US organization, Latinos in Agriculture, O’Mallon functioned as the master of ceremonies at their 2012 Latinos in Agriculture Leaders Forum in San Antonio, Texas in October.

January 2013 O'Mallon founded a start-up company, Earth Twine, whose mission is to strengthen global food security through innovation, education and collaboration.

Specialties: Writing, research, social media, video production, public speaking

Earth Twine

Public Company; 1-10 employees; Information Technology and Services industry

November 2012 – Present (9 months) International

Earth Twine is an agriculture innovation incubator and education platform. Our mission is to seek out and develop solutions that strengthen weaknesses in the global food chain, while improving the quality of life for farmers and mitigating our impact on the environment. Earth Twine weaves four socioeconomic strands to obtain our global vision.

Business
Incubate, invest, and market innovative agriculture, food product development, and food distribution solutions, through our Development Lab.

Innovation
Develop creative, adaptable, products, which increase global food security through our Discovery Lab.

Education
Provide proven sustainable agricultural knowledge, renew interest in agricultural careers, bring agriculture, food science and nutrition to classrooms around the world.

Community
Provide a web-based platform for our community to obtain the latest industry news and reports on sustainable agriculture and global food chain issues, share, sell and exchange educational videos, materials, about innovative solutions for agriculture including education and food distribution solutions.

About Harvest, LLC

Public Company; 1-10 employees; Media Production industry

January 2009 – Present (4 years 7 months)

About Harvest is a for profit social enterprise using media in formats dedicated to informing, educating and celebrating agricultural history, development, and food.

We make short and long form programs, documentaries, educational videos and PSA’s that unearth the history, science, and relevance of modern agricultural crops that are grown and harvested in the United States and around the world.

The company earns revenue through direct commissioning of documentary films, public service announcements, websites and broadcast productions, as well as direct sales of DVDs, curriculum tools, merchandise, events tickets and publications from its website(s) and fulfillment centers. who make it happen.
