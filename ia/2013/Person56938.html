Co-Founder

MessageSpace

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Internet industry

June 2006 – Present (7 years 5 months) London, United Kingdom

Online political campaigning and advertising agency that's worked for all the major political parties and campaigns in Britain, and some of the biggest brands on Earth.

MessageSpace is primarily a targeted ad network focused on the most influential and high traffic news/politics/current affairs publishers in the UK. We've worked to help them earn revenue to sustain their operations and the important role they play in their political spheres, and also within the wider political debate.

Launched FringeList.com - a social scheduler for political conferences, and helps publicise conference fringe events via the MessageSpace network.

Founder

YouFund.me.uk

March 2010 – May 2010 (3 months) London, United Kingdom

Britain's first ever online cross-party online fundraising platform, tied into the MessageSpace network (which conveniently is the way to reach over a million politically active users on the Web).

We raised tens of thousands of pounds for candidates during the 2010 General Election cycle.

One of the candidates utilising the platform raised more money than he could spend during the general election.

I built and designed the infrastructure, and handled all aspects of the platform, from legal/regulatory campaign finance compliance, to card/merchant processing and security, to building a scalable database and fund-raising engine, capable of withstanding a media blitz and large-scale traffic surges.

Founder

Labourhome.org

June 2006 – 2009 (3 years) London, United Kingdom

One of the Labour Party's first attempts at digitally engaging with and reinvigorating its grassroots. I built and designed the infrastructure, managed media relationships, grew ad revenues (from zero), and sustained an ecosystem of grassroots support. Labourhome experienced more traffic than the offical Labour Party website.

I exited after a substantial buyout offer from a large publishing firm.
