Cody McKibben's Summary

Professionally, I am a marketer, writer, online business advisor, WordPress tinkerer and web developer, I have done a bit of event planning, and product launch consulting, among many other things. I wear way too many hats, but my greatest skills lie somewhere in the space between connecting incredibly creative people, advocating for new ideas, and building strong, engaged online communities around those ideas.

I'm proud to be a digital nomad and lifestyle designer – in 2007, I started my own web-based freelance business, developed client relationships on five continents, and in 2008 I decided to experiment with location independence, to move and live abroad, and continue to build and run my online business, location-independent from anywhere in the world. Originally from California, I've adopted a permanent traveler lifestyle and have now slow traveled around the world to over 28 countries, living and working on the islands of Koh Phangan and Bali, the beaches of Krabi, the big city metropolises of Bangkok, Kuala Lumpur and Saigon, in the mountains of Chiang Mai, then in the Philippines, and now Chile.

Now my passion is helping others design unconventional lifestyles for themselves and build remarkable businesses that allow them to incorporate adventure, travel, entrepreneurship, giving & fun into their lives! Currently I mentor and consult with 250+ virtual business owners and aspiring entrepreneurs inside Digital Nomad Academy, building new lifestyle business startups, creating new communities, and launching new products & resources.

In the past 6 years I've also helped develop South East Asia Backpacker Magazine, SovereignMan.com, ChrisDucker.com, Untemplater.com, In Search Of Sanuk, and been involved in running the Bangkok Tweetup and TEDx BKK—projects that have been featured by BBC Capital, FreelanceSwitch, Huffington Post, the New York Times, ABC News, Transitions Abroad, the Washington Post, CNN Travel and more.

My primary passion in life is to help others break free from the conventional template lifestyle—to liberate people from traditional ways of thinking about work and life, help other young professionals become entrepreneurs and build their own online businesses, live remarkable lives, and do good for others.

After losing two of my best friends in 2004, I started my own side business, quit my job, and eventually decided to travel and live abroad. I started Thrilling Heroics in 2006 to encourage other young professionals to take risks, not to settle for a life of mediocrity, working in a cubicle and vegetating in front of the TV, but to pursue a passionate, exciting lifestyle, and to make a positive difference in the world!

At Thrilling Heroics, I advocate complete lifestyle design, location independent living, permanent travel, and I discuss my experience living overseas as a digital nomad. The site has grown and evolved in leaps and bounds since 2006: I’ve been ranked among the likes of Tim Ferriss, Chris Brogan, and Chris Guillebeau as one of the top 10 Digital Nomads to Learn From, and the site has been ranked as one of the Top 30 Young Entrepreneur Blogs of 2009 alongside Neil Patel’s QuickSprout, Andrew Warner’s Mixergy, and Ramit Sethi’s I Will Teach You To Be Rich.
