Ed Wargin's Summary

A professional photographer with over 25 years experience of successfully operating Ed Wargin Studios and the founder of The Fresh Coast Project, I am now immersed in the industry of digital publishing where I serve as the Vice President of See North Media, publisher of digital magazines and custom media solutions. I serve as the Visual Director for NORTH Magazine, a niche publication for those who live, travel and explore Duluth and the North Shore of Lake Superior.

Along with my business partner, Kathy-jo Wargin (President of See North Media), we believe that success in any field boils down to these core traits and abilities:

- Remain flexible and focused.
- Commit to quality on every level.
- Lead with purpose, relate with kindness.
- Establish an environment of authentic excellence where achievement is rewarded.
- Never stop learning. Ever.

Here is a glimpse of my past experience as a professional photographer:

More than 25 years experience creating imagery for commercial, editorial, and publishing clients such as GMC Motors, Anheuser-Bush Inc., Caterpillar Inc., Woolrich, Orvis, LL Bean, Arctic Cat and the inaugural award-winning Pure Michigan Tourism campaign and more. My photographic works are held in public and private galleries across the globe, as well as healthcare and corporate environments.

Past specialties: Commercial & Editorial Assignment Photography, Nature Landscape Photography, Book Publishing, Fine Art Printing, Collaborating with Corporate Interior Designers on Evidence Based Health Care Art and a public speaker for photography, preservation and the natural environment.

I am the founder of The Fresh Coast Project which has been an artistic and historic endeavor to archive the beauty and magnificence of the Great Lakes on film prior to the impending cessation of film as photographic medium.

Ed Wargin's Publications

  • Legends of Light - A Michigan Lighthouse Portfolio

    • Ann Arbor Media Group
    • 2005
    Authors: Ed Wargin

    The many moods of Michigan's majestic sentinels are brought to life by the amazing images in Ed Wargin's Legends of Light. Wargin, one of the region's most talented photographers, has been busy shooting Michigan lighthouses, trying to capture each in a manner that exudes the unique character of these popular lakeshore monuments. By land, air, and sea he takes you on a personal tour of his favorite locations-from the remote and rarely seen to the most heavily visited tourist sites. From summer's gentle sunshine to winter's bitter fury, each page of this book reminds the reader why millions of people visit Michigan lighthouses each year. Let Wargin's book help you plan a trip or take you to places of which you can only dream.

  • Voelker's Pond - A Robert Traver Legacy

    • Huron River Press
    • 2003
    Authors: Ed Wargin, James McCullough

    John Voelker was a fly fishing icon - and to avid readers of 1950s best sellers and to classic film buffs, he was also known as Robert Traver the author of award-winning classics such as Trout Madness, Trout Magic and novel turned Academy Award winning movie, Anatomy of a Murder. John Voelker was also a Michigan Supreme Court justice. And either way he was a man who needed little introduction, fr he was the man who Charles Kuralt befriended and in the end called the closest thing to a great man [he] ever met.John Voelker had a fishing spot - a private retreat more or less - in the northern portion of Michigan's upper peninsula. Millions of people are familiar with this place in the woods through his words, but never before has a professional photographer captured with such stunning beauty, the one-room cabin nestled in the woods, the glacial pond where native brook trout remain elusive, the forest that surrounds it all, as well as the rods, the flies, and the small nuances that tell the story of an extraordinary man, and the legacy he leaves behind.

  • The Great Lakes Cottage Book

    • Sleeping Bear Press
    • 2000
    Authors: Ed Wargin, Kathy-jo Wargin

    For centuries we have been drawn to edges of the Great Lakes to create magical places that we escape to for weekends and vacations. Some of these cottages have witnessed the love and caring of several generations, others are newly found or created treasures. Some of our most vivid childhood memories are of summer vacations at the same cottage, on the same lake, where we spent endless hours drawing on the spiritual and purifying power of the water. Nationally acclaimed photographer Ed Wargin, The Great Lakes Cottage Book takes you on a visual visit to some of the most pristine and private cottages on the Great Lakes.

  • Michigan - The Spirit of the Land

    • Voyageur Press
    • 1999
    Authors: Ed Wargin, Kathy-jo Wargin

    This book captures the landscapes, the legends, and the lore that make Michigan such a unique place to live and visit. The stunning, medium-format photography and descriptive text of the natural wonders and historic sites of Michigan truly capture the spirit of the land. From the lower peninsula through the Mackinac straits, to the upper peninsula and Isle Royale, you'll discover all the state's natural wonders and historic sites that convey the spirit of the land that is Michigan.

  • I Spy With My Little Eye Minnesota - A Picture Puzzle Book

    • Sleeping Bear Press
    Authors: Ed Wargin, Kathy-jo Wargin

    From the author of V is for Viking: A Minnesota Alphabet and North Star Numbers: A Minnesota Number Book comes yet another reason to enjoy learning about this north woods state. Kathy-jo Wargin’s poetic puzzles are perfectly paired with dual photos of Minnesota scenery and landmarks. Taken by Great Lakes photographer Ed Wargin, one in each pair of photos has undergone a slight change. Can you spot the difference?

    This visual treat is a charming way to acquaint beginning readers with the great state of Minnesota and its wonders.
