Christa Dyson's Summary

From the first bewildering moments when a spinal cord injury or a stroke is confirmed to the present day and beyond, rehabilitation is an active, life-long process.

No matter how high your level of spinal injury, how severe your stroke there is a positive way forward but it will be, more than likely, a different way to one you had previously envisaged.

Christa comes from 19 years of experience living with a high level spinal cord injury following a spinal stroke. She can share her experience, tips and coping strategies to make your rehabilitation experience as successful as possible and make the most out of your new found life.

She was taken ill just a few weeks after moving to live in The Netherlands. It was a frightening and isolating experience. Despite receiving excellent care and rehabilitation with no English speaking mentor to turn to she had to navigate her own way and rebuild her life, find a way to be a true wife and mother to her two, then very young, children; find purpose to each day.

Her interests are varied from traveling to the arts be it visiting the theater, art galleries, museums and cinema. Pilates and hydrotherapy are the ways she enjoys most to stay fit and healthy. She enjoys singing, reading, dining out and a good glass of wine whilst sitting in her English garden. She has recently had a car adapted so that after 19 years she is back on the open roads again!

She is a Back Up Trust accredited mentor. She is a Trustee for the Spinal Injuries Association, UK.

‘Coming from some very dark early days after my initial injury,’ she says, ‘I would have never thought it possible to come so far’.

Specialties: Christa has particular knowledge in the areas of tetraplygia, women's issues, ageing, raising a family, adaptive technology in support of daily living, re-evaluation of personal interests and travel.
