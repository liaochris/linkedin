Isaac Chua's Summary

Isaac comes from a multidisciplinary background.

First, he is a systems analyst. He started off in information technology since 1999, and has developed applications and software in many areas, including web applications, computer networks, client-server frameworks, information security, 3D games, location-based services (LBS), database applications, optical character recognition (OCR), and statistical analysis. He holds a Dip IT with merit, and several world-recognized certifications.

Second, he is a marketer, specializing in marketing research, consumer behavior and customer satisfaction. He majored in marketing while reading his BBM, and has topped his cohort in the Consumer Research course and graduated magna cum laude. He has promoted the PassWindow security product in trade shows and has written sales copies for the Chinese market. He has also developed, in record time, a customer satisfaction survey and its analysis based on established theories on satisfaction (ACSI, SERVQUAL) for Training Vision Institute (TVI).

Third, he is a researcher/statistician. Given impetus by his statistics-related courses in the university, he has gone on to self-study research methodology and multivariate data analysis. He has written two research papers and has applied the skills into marketing research (such as in TVI). He is proficient in the use of SPSS and R. He has also been teaching university-level statistics since 2010.

Fourth, he is a writer cum editor. He has written many articles on the subject of Christianity, and other subjects including interviews. Most recently, he has edited a book, “Progress and Its (Dis-)Contents”, published in October 2011.

Fifth, he is an entrepreneur. He has been working on startups since late-2009, after an entrepreneurship course. He is currently working on his third startup after having failed two earlier ones. He is also a Circle Leader in the Circle of Punks, a group of 200+ young founders and co-founders in Singapore.

  • GPS Traffic Aggregation System

    • September 2009 to January 2010
    Team Members: Isaac Chua, Melvin Goh

    The GPS Traffic Aggregation System (GTAS) was an idea that came out of an entrepreneurship course in Singapore Management University (SMU). At that time, information on on-road traffic conditions was not available to drivers in Singapore except through infrequent updates on the radio. Having such information widely available, especially in road navigation and route planning systems, will aid drivers in getting to their destination in the shortest amount of time.

    As the number of smartphones per capita in Singapore was increasing with the introduction of the iPhone and Android devices, as well as the continual infrastructural investments by telcos and the government into the nationwide 3/3.5G network, the concept of crowdsourcing and aggregating information given by the in-device GPS chip over the 3G network came up.

    Working software prototypes were created that demonstrated the possibility of collecting and transmitting GPS data from consumers over the network, and crunching them into useful route decision-making information in real-time. A large scale proof-of-concept was not attempted however.

    The team attempted to commercialize the technology by embarking on developing a traffic navigation system that uses the technology for smartphones. The software would collect GPS information from the phone, transmit the data to our servers, and the aggregated information would be fed back to phone for route planning. However, the development was terminated prematurely—a competitor had launched a similar product that provided the same function, backed by the Land Transport Authority (LTA), Google and Garmin. As the technology was essentially infrastructure, the team felt that creating a second one was redundant.

  • Spelling Bee Data Solution

    • April 2007 to June 2007
    Team Members: Isaac Chua

    An organization was holding a spelling bee competition as part of a major conference. Part of the competition involves the generation of word lists for the three categories of contestants. Each of the words had to be delivered with the pronunciation and meaning(s), and each of the words must not be too similar to the others in the list. Hence a solution had to be developed with the following functions:

    1. A database for the entry and storage of chosen words.
    2. A data miner/scraper to collect the chosen words' meanings and pronunciation (in IPA).
    3. A form for the entry of missing pronunciations in IPA format.
    4. A report generator for the creation of the word lists.
    5. A verification tool to ensure that words are not too similar to each other.

    The solution was developed successfully, which led to the successful execution of the spelling bee competition.
