Alan Heisig's Summary

Alan helps companies win business in the federal marketplace. He founded ALANSA International LLC, providing executive level advice for large and medium value contracts, including capture / proposal strategy, pre-proposal and proposal management/writing and color team support.

His customers also received successful business opportunity analyses, white papers, special reports, and case studies. He has provided subject matter expertise in maritime and naval affairs, communications networking management and telecommunications management.

Shipley Associates reached out to him and he has associated with them. Shipley defines the industry standards for professional capture and proposal support and training.

As Enterprise VP for Netco Government Services, he lead the reinvention of the base networks for the Navy Marine Corps Internet (NMCI) as a tier one subcontractor for EDS which held the prime contract for NMCI. He grew the business from 30 personnel to over 600 employees in four years.

He is a graduate of the Northwestern University, Kellogg School of Business and of the Naval Postgraduate School.
His sterling US Navy career included three commands at sea: Commander Amphibious Squadron Six [Commander Amphibious Task Forces Mediterranean], USS Blue Ridge (LCC-19) [flagship of the Commander Seventh Fleet} and USS Spartanburg County (LST 1192).

During his IT focused naval tours ashore, he created a plan for reorganization of the entire Commander Naval Telecommunications Command. He led the team that resolved the naval message crisis attendant to the first Gulf War (and led to the tripled telecommunications throughput without capital investment). He culminated his career as the Deputy Director C4 on the Joint Chiefs of Staff.

Specialties

Strategic and tactical support to U.S. federal government contractors including capture strategy, capture planning, capture and proposal assessment, proposal support, win strategy, and color team reviews.

One of a limited number of professionals worldwide accredited by APMP.

A senior line officer and expert in amphibious warfare and telecommunications management. While the Amphibious Task Force Commander in the Mediterranean, we quadrupled the number of operations plans created and approved from any previous Amphib Task Force Commander through automation efficiencies on my staff and reduced the message error rate for our command from a historical average of 5% per month to a cumulative error rate of 0.75% for a six month period. As Captain of USS Blue Ridge (LCC19) I conceived, funded and accomplished transitioning the entire ship and staff from a paper message culture to messages to the desktop. The entire process had an ROI of six months from savings generated from the cost of message paper alone. As C.O. of USS Spartanburg County (LST 1192) I brought it from a position of no battle efficiency awards to almost perfect performance during the course of my tour. As a telecommunications specialist, on the staff of the Chief of Naval Operations, I spearheaded a team of diverse organizational representatives which solved the Navy's strangling message backlog which occurred during the workup to the first Gulf War and created a throughput which tripled capacity in two months without capital investment.
My career spanned engagements in Vietnam, the humanitarian evacuation of Lebanon to Cypress, the humanitarian evacuation of Cypress to Lebanon, the humanitarian rescue of distressed craft during the Muriel boatlift from Cuba, the conflicts in the former Republic of Yugoslavia and the Cold War.
