Beverly Byrd's Summary

At Byrd & Associates, we exclusively handle U.S. immigration cases. This focus allows us to stay abreast of the constantly changing laws and policies that affect our clients. We handle all types of immigration cases, incuding business immigration, family immigration, and deportation and removal defense cases among other types of cases.

BUSINESS IMMIGRATION CASES
We have the knowledge, experience and resources to handle your business immigration needs, whether large or small. Our business clients include high tech companies, large healthcare organizations, architectural firms, manufacturing companies, engineering firms, dental offices, financial institutions and start up companies.

FAMILY IMMIGRATION
Our family immigration clients have traveled from across the globe to reunite with their family members in the United States. Whether they married U.S. citizens and entered on a K-3 spousal visa or an immigrant visa, whether engaged to a U.S. citizen, entered on a K-1 fiancé visa and later filed for adjustment of status, whether a spouse or child of a person granted asylum status, all obtained permanent residency in the United States.

CITIZENSHIP AND NATURALIZATION
We help our naturalization clients file for and obtain U.S. citizenship. Many of our clients come to us for help in filing their naturalization applications because of prior criminal convictions that complicate their cases. Others contract us to make sure that their applications are in the best of order and are processed as flawlessly and quickly as possible.

DEPORTATION AND ASYLUM
At Byrd & Associates, we help individuals defend against deportation from the United States. We understand the anxiousness that this causes individuals and their family members, and do everything possible to find a successful solution to their deportation.

Specialties

We specialize in U.S. immigration law, handling all types of immigration cases.

Beverly Byrd's Education

Georgetown University Law Center

LL.M., International Law

Continuing my specialization in international law, I obtained a master's (LL.M.) in International and Comparative Law from Georgetown University Law Center in Washington, D.C. While completing my LL.M., I worked for the Office of the Legal Adviser, United Nations Affairs, at the U.S. State Department in Washington, D.C. Samford University, Cumberland School of Law

J.D.

1997 – 1999

I received my law degree (JD) from Cumberland Law School of Samford University in Birmingham , Alabama. I studied international law in Europe, visiting and receiving lectures at the Scottish Supreme Court, the European Court of Human Rights, the European Commission, the European Court of Justice, and the European Parliament.

Several lectures were taught by U.S. Supreme Court Justice Ruth Bader Ginsburg, and the former Bosnian War Crimes Tribunal Chief Prosecutor, Richard Goldstone. After obtaining my law degree, I worked for the United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees in Washington , D.C.
