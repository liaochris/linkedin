David Perry's Summary

To connect, simply add dp@dperry.com as a friend. (Nearly 16,000 direct connections.)

David Perry was the Founder & President of Shiny Entertainment, Inc. (a game development company bought by Atari) known for making hits like Earthworm Jim & MDK, after 30 years in the business Perry remains one of the best known Video Game Industry veterans, winning the Development Legend award in 2012.

Perry has developed or programmed games across 29 video game platforms, including #1 Hits like the Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles and other top brands including The Terminator, Disney's Aladdin & Warner’s Matrix projects, all told they have sold over a billion dollars in retail sales.

Perry has an Honorary Doctorate from Queens University in Belfast and an Honorary Masters in Game Development from Westwood College. He remains one of the longest serving members of the Game Developers Conference Advisory Board, and has spoken at TED, E3, Hollywood and Games, Stanford, MIT, USC, UCI, UCLA, DICE, GTC, GDC, AMD, MGS, Digital Hollywood, What Teens Want, 3D Gaming Summit, Casual Connect etc.

Following Shiny Perry became the Co-Founder & Chief Creative Officer of Acclaim, which was subsequently purchased in 2010 by Playdom, then by Disney.

In 2009 Perry became the CEO and co-founder of Gaikai, a company that developed a cutting-edge video game streaming technology that allows games and applications to run in any browser or on any internet connected device. Gaikai was acquired by Sony Computer Entertainment in July 2012.

Perry has launched a book for students called "David Perry on Game Design", it's the largest non-profit book on Game Design ever written (www.GameDesignBook.org), and funds a free industry map to help indie developers be discovered and to help developers find local jobs. (www.GameIndustryMap.com)

CEO & Co-Founder

Gaikai, Inc.

Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Computer Games industry

March 2009 – August 2012 (3 years 6 months) Aliso Viejo, CA

Gaikai is a cloud gaming company (meaning that it runs video games in the cloud) that places instantly playable games inside major websites. For example to place professional video games in Walmart.com or YouTube.com Gaikai is the only company in the world that does this. Gaikai also powers instant game trials on major video game publisher websites like Electronic Arts' Origin store or Ubisoft's UBIShop.

To make this possible Gaikai had to build the fastest (lowest latency) interactive network in the world. This has been achieved by partnering with major global providers like Level 3, Limelight and Savvis.

Key Investors include Benchmark Capital, NEA, Rustic Canyon, Qualcomm & Intel Capital.

To find out more about Gaikai, just visit www.gaikai.com

CCO (Chief Creative Officer)

Acclaim Games Inc.

Partnership; 51-200 employees; Computer Games industry

March 2006 – December 2009 (3 years 10 months)

As one of the founders of Acclaim, Perry held the Chief Creative Officer title, directing all key Acclaim titles worldwide. Acclaim was one of the first Western companies to embrace and publish massively multiplayer online "free-to-play" games, using in-game advertising, micro-transactions, crowd-sourced localization and state-of-the-art viral marketing techniques.

The Mayfield Fund invested into Acclaim, it then rapidly became the largest American free to play game company.

After Perry started his own company Gaikai Inc., Acclaim was bought by Playdom. Playdom was then subsequently bought by Disney.

President and Founder / Programmer / Game Designer

Shiny Entertainment Inc.

Public Company; 51-200 employees; ATAR; Computer Games industry

October 1993 – February 2006 (12 years 5 months)

Perry was the President and Founder of Shiny, running it for 12.5 years.

He worked with all the technical, development, sales, licensing, legal, PR, finance, marketing and management sides of running a leading video game development studio.

Perry has a technical background also, while at Shiny he programmed the award winning games Earthworm Jim 1 & 2. Shiny also developed other games to like Earthworm Jim Special Edition, MDK, Messiah, Sacrifice, R/C Stunt Copter, Wild 9, Enter the Matrix & The Matrix: Path of Neo under Perry's leadership.

Earthworm Jim became a TV show made by Universal Cartoon Studios, aired on the Warner Brothers Kids Network, a Marvel Comic book series and was licensed for just about every category including fast food promotions by both Del Taco and Carls Jr.

Shiny was partially bought from Perry in 1995 by Interplay Entertainment, then again in 2002 for $47M by Atari Inc. After Perry's departure it was sold to Foundation 9, the largest game development company in the world, in 2006.
