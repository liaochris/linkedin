Mark Maloney's Summary

Mark Maloney is an independent design consultant whose work centers on bringing greater value to the digital experience for users. A biochemist by training, Mark‘s uniquely analytical approach to creative development ensures a best-of-class work product that is on strategy, user-centric and results-focused.

Over his 18-year career, Mark has worked to blur the lines between television and the Web for MTV, provided global interactive brand strategy for Coke and Samsung, standardized the enterprise user experience platform at AOL's Advertising.com and imagined communities of advocacy for the American Petroleum Institute, Walmart and the Embassies of Canada and South Korea. He has helped to flip the classroom by introducing blended learning to over a million college students worldwide with Echo360. In 2013, HP CEO Meg Whitman highlighted Mark’s work in a keynote speech.

Mark served as Executive Creative Director at Grassroots Enterprise, an award-winning grassroots communication and political advocacy firm that was acquired by Edelman in 2009. Mark continues to work closely with both the Grassroots and Edelman teams and serves as the de facto creative director for Edelman Digital Public Affairs.

Mark's career as a freelance designer and web developer began in 1995. In 1998, he joined Gr8, an Adweek Top 100 Interactive Agency, and was quickly promoted to Art Director. Striking out on his own in 2000, he founded the design agency no|inc, a nationally recognized leader in interactive communications and product development. By 2001, he had developed an exclusive partnership between no|inc and Berlin Cameron Partners and was de facto Director of Interactive Services for BCP from 2002-2007.

Mark serves both as a member of the WYPR Community Advisory Board and as a trustee of The Odyssey School.

In 2005, he was named to the Baltimore Business Journal's list of "Top 40 Under 40". He holds an M.S. from the University of Maryland and a B.S. from Roanoke College.
