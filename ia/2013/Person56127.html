Paul Gavin Jorgensen's Summary

I attended University of Illinois for International Business and left to pursue a career as a hip-hop artist, journeying through Nashville and New York City. I quickly became an entrepreneur, building and managing a team to help me reach my music career goal: to attain a major label record deal and achieve worldwide notoriety.

I successfully attracted a single deal offer from Universal Motown three months later and was advised not to sign. I had commercial music and a great team, but I didn't have bargaining power to negotiate a quality deal. I was missing fans, sales, and performances, but more importantly, I was missing my direction. I was blessed to experience failure.

My music career pursuit was over. I worked at nightclubs and golf courses while I began a proactive approach to get involved in social media marketing. I developed and implemented social media campaigns for good friends, startups, and local businesses. My early contributions were free, and my work experience was a great education.

My whole life changed after I lost someone very close. I moved back to Chicago, picked up abstract painting, created personal music, and built deep, intimate relationships. I naturally met people seeking business advice and started consulting for a variety of entrepreneurs. I provided consulting on business strategy, marketing & social media, business development, and more. I transitioned from freelance work into launching a marketing agency called Treerock, Inc. with a great friend in Chicago.

Lifestock, Inc. came next. I partnered with a great friend and a wise man who was on a mission to develop passive large-scale permaculture systems that improve local ecology and bring real food back to local communities. This agriculture system models the natural ecosystem with a diverse group of plants and animals working in synergy with one another. I deeply connected to the purpose and am taking action to create a brighter future.
