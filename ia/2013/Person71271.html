      Los Angeles Game Developer Gatherings
      • Science and Technology
      2003 – 2009 (6 years)

      For a few years I had discussed with industry peers the need for a local gathering of game developers. The International Game Developers Association was not active at all within the Los Angeles area and I found this to be a great oversight. After determining the political situation of the why the IGDA was not addressing the core needs of game developers within the Los Angeles area I decided to organize and promote a new monthly gathering named "Los Angeles Game Developer Gatherings."

      Initially the meetings were scheduled to take place twice a month, first Wednesday of the month on the Westside, in the Santa Monica area, and again, two weeks later, on the third Wednesday of the month, within the Burbank area, to enable game developers on both sides of the city to attend a meeting. I eventually dropped the Burbank meeting after about six months due to lack of attendance.

      The Santa Monica meeting grew rapidly in attendance numbers with a mostly informal get together of game developers occurring. There were occasional meetings with presentations and panel discussions that also took place.

      The Los Angeles Game Developer Gatherings were eventually rolled in to an official monthly IGDA meeting after I was appointed the local IGDA chapter coordinator/president.

      With an established meeting and strong attendance and a family illness I decided that at the beginning of 2009 the stewardship of the meetings should be handled by others with more time to dedicate to the growth of the meetings.

One of the many reasons that Sid Meier (Civilization), Chris Crawford (Balance of Power), and Peter Molyneux (Populous) so easily created genuinely interesting game play and computer opponents was that they hid the simplistic governing rules and variables—really making you think there was something more going on than what the reality was."
