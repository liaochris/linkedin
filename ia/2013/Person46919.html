Gareth Young's Summary

I spent 15 years developing and executing business expansion and rationalization strategies through M&A, partnerships, and other corporate and commercial transactions for BellSouth. This included helping businesses identify strategic needs, establish priorities and plans, and execute on these, as well as all aspects of establishing search criteria for alliances and acquisitions, screening and selecting candidates, and staffing and leading teams to negotiate, execute and close transactions.

Subsequent to leaving BellSouth in 2006, I have been been working with small/medium businesses and start ups to help them drive growth, improve financial performance, enhance cash flow, and create value with a variety of niche services. My area of focus and my greatest value add is through my relationship with Alpharesults, which allows me to bring an operational perspective to bear as I help businesses with Georgia employees take advantage of some significant tax credit opportunities. I also provide M&A advisory and transaction support services as well as financial and operational consulting.

In addition to my core business activities, I am passionate about social justice and interfaith and committed to working on this though my role at the Faith Alliance of Metro Atlanta and elsewhere.

Specialties: Tax credit, RTC, GA RTC, Retraining tax credit, M&A, mergers and acquisitions, due diligence, business broker, business services, exit strategy, acquisition, exit, sale, strategy, information memorandum preparation, start-ups, entrepreneurial skills, accounting and finance (CPA and Chartered Accountant), business cases and business plans. Also novels, publishing-on-demand, and spirituality, zen and interfaith, retraining tax credits, training, facilitation, leadership forums, development of training materials, curriculum, CPE.

The Faith Alliance of Atlanta is a 501(c)(3) organization whose mission is the promotion of understanding, respect, prayer, interaction and unity among the diverse faiths in the greater Atlanta region, and the advancement of the influence of voices of the faith communities for social justice.

We are not a uniform group: rather our board and membership we are pluralistic in practice and diverse with regard to age, race, sexuality, faith, culture, race and tradition. We believe that our organization, by intentionally manifesting and representing this diversity, can contribute positively to the Metro Atlanta community through:

- Communal prayer with those of other faith
- Education on religion and matters of social justice
- Dialogue and conversation, including during projects held in conjunction with our partners the Atlanta Food Bank and Habitat for Humanity
- Cooperation and networking with religious and social justice organizations
- Advocacy and action on matters of local and national importance

It is a personal passion to expand the influence of this remarkable organization as an ethical force for social justice and the common good in Atlanta.

Gareth Young's Publications

  • The Grace of Guilt

    • Dog Ear Press
    • March 30, 2009
    Authors: Gareth Young

    When fifteen-year-old Jessica Jackson’s crush on her pastor's son leads to rape, she finds herself pregnant and alone, rejected by both family and church as an unrepentant sinner. And when fellow-church member, Simon Cassell’s homosexual relationship with his high school friend is discovered, he is beaten by his father and ostracized. Meanwhile, middle-aged Dwight Cook is struggling with the economic failure of his business, the town hardware store, with estrangement from his wife and oldest son, and with uncontrollable lust for a female employee half his age.

    The outcasts’ struggles with underserved guilt intensify when a lynching brings the FBI to the rural North Carolina town and both church and family members are implicated, and when a tormented loved one commits suicide.

    The Grace of Guilt follows the destruction of three families and their quests for redemption, and examines the old prejudice of the new South.

  • An Unexamined Life

    • August 13, 2010
    Authors: Gareth Young

    Gareth Young's second novel spices up echoes of Kafka's The Trial and Nabakov's Lolita with a pinch of erotica as he crafts a compelling tale of the relentless and inevitable decline of a modern day Don Giovanni.

    Joe Kay is released from a career that had long since lost its meaning by inheritance and seeks solace in his marriage, but when he discovers that a decade of neglect had allowed his vows to drain away he allows his libido to lead him astray. He accumulates an online roster of virtual mistresses, starts sleeping with a stripper and plays strip-Twister with a neighbor. Eventually a high school girl who frequents his favorite coffee shop and his daily fantasies exploits his clumsy infatuation and seduces Joe. Has he finally found a home for his faithless heart or would his newfound loyalty falter and lead Joe and his lovers deeper into disaster?

  • Sacred Space: Explore the Sacred Spaces of Five World Religions

    • CreateSpace
    • April 21, 2011
    Authors: Gareth Young, Ben Johnson, Mitch Cohen, Gillian Renault, Plemon El Amin, Kemal Korucu, Tom Buchanan

    Sacred Space offers an introduction to the five major religions of the world: Judaism, Christianity, Islam, Hinduism and Vedantaism, and Buddhism. Beginning with a description of the places of worship, the authors explore what takes place in this space, what it means and what happens in the lives of the people beyond the Sacred Space. This book offers a model for religious groups and communities to explore the sacred space of the other. It aims to promote dialogue, mutual understanding and cooperation in solving the major problems of the world. The authors explain in simple language the faith and the practice of the various religions. This book is unique in that the authors also serve as guides in visiting the Sacred Spaces of the other faiths. The approach is clear; the language is simple; and the effect is clear. Those who will benefit from this text include individuals who want a brief overview of these five religions, searching lay persons, students in reigious studies and teachers.
