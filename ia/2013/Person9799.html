Composer and soundartist

Tape TV Productions

Privately Held; Myself Only; Media Production industry

1983 – Present (30 years)

I am an autodidact composer: in 1983 I started my first musical experiments with modified tapedecks, hacked electronic toys and hifi-equipment.

Around 1986 I was able to professionalize my technical skills and study electronic composition at the then legendary Center for Electronic Music (CEM-Studio).
In 1989 I was one of the techno-pioneers in the Netherlands, working under names as Voltage Control, Spasms, The Implant and in collaborations like Random XS and Urban Electro.

In the years that followed, I made several records and CD's in the emerging electronic dance-scene but, disappointed by the many conventions back than, I turned away and fled into more experimental music of which some was released on the German Mille Plateaux label.
In 1996 I created “AeroSon”, a 40' soundscape as the Dutch admission to the International Rostrum for Electro-Acoustic Music (IREM). It won me the first prize in the category ‘Composers Under 30”.

I've always showed interest with almost any part of sound, which has lead to a rather diverse number of activities. I'm a freelance radioproducer with the Netherlands Program Service (NPS), teach editing,-and recording techniques in various settings and I do both commercial,- and non-profit productions, ranging from remixes, leaders and advertising to experimental music and sounddesign. From time to time I'm involved in recording on location as well: for film/TV, or DVD-productions. I assemble, edit and mix for radio, film and games on a regular basis and specialize in surround sound and audio within interactive applications. My personal interest however still is experimental music and soundscapes. Together with partner and artist Iris Honderdos, I completed several installations, most recently in Ukraine, Vietnam and Finland.

Due to my widespread experience in the area of sound and music, festivals and organizations acquire my help as consultant for audiovisual solutions or as an aid in setting up the program.

Centre for Electronic Music (CEM)

1986 – 2000 (14 years)

The Center for Electronic Music (CEM) was originally one of the oldest institutes in the Netherlands dealing with this genre, way before synthesizers where commonplace: it is said it's earliest incarnation was 1957.
I joined there as a student, learning to work with tape recorders and professional equipment for the first time.
Later I started giving courses in Music on the Mac (we're talking DECKII and Sounddesigner on OS7 here...).
In the late 90's the studio started to decline little by little due to the availability of ever cheaper equipment and a lack of interest of the younger generation to take courses in Electronic Music. By 2007 the studio had moved twice and found residency at WORM in Rotterdam.
