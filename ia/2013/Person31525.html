David Bock's Summary

David Bock is the President and Principal Consultant at CodeSherpas, a company he founded in 2007. In this capacity, Mr. Bock works with clients to help solve their pressing business problems through the application of technology and custom software solutions.

Mr Bock is active in the software development community, co-founding the Northern Virginia Java Users group in 1997, and co-founding the Northern Virginia Ruby Users Group in 2005. Mr. Bock served as the President of the Northern Virginia Java Users Group from October 2000 to February 2010. Mr. Bock is also a co-founder of the RubyNation conference, and serves on its Board of Organizers.

In January 2006, David was awarded the title of Java Champion by a panel of leaders in the Java Community through a program sponsored by Sun. There are approximately 100 active Java Champions worldwide, and the program continues under Oracle's MVP program.

David served as the Editor of O'Reilly's OnJava.com website, has been published in several books and magazines, and is a frequent speaker on technology and team processes at various software engineering conferences.

David has also served on several JCP panels, including JSR-270, the Specification of the Java 6 Platform.

At a previous employer, David served as the Chief Architect of the Export Control Program with the Non-Proliferation and Disarmament Fund at the U.S. Department of State, where he was responsible for the development of software for tracking the licensing process and shipment of nuclear, chemical, and biological material, and for software used by the U.N. for tracking the removal of landmines.

In addition to his public speaking and training activities, Mr. Bock actively consults as a software engineer, project manager, and team mentor for commercial and government clients. If you are interesting in using Dave's talents on your project, he is available for short and long term consulting projects through his consultancy.
