Chris Alpiar's Summary

Christopher Kennedy Alpiar is an award-winning saxophonist and composer for Film, TV, Stage Theater, Advertising and Video Games. His specialization is in large epic orchestral compositions and Jazz large and small ensemble works, and with his background equally balanced in Jazz and orchestral music, he is considered a composer's composer, using his palette with great acumen of his art and craft.

Mr. Alpiar spent the earlier years of his life as a Jazz saxophonist. As an instrumentalist he has had the unique pleasure of recording as a sideman on hundreds of recording sessions and spent a significant amount of time touring Europe and South America. His experiences as a performer encompass every genre of music currently catalogued, including Pop, Rock, Blues, Jazz, Hip Hop, Acid Jazz, Reggae, Souca, Calypso, Salsa, Merengue, Coumbia, Big Band, Grunge, Death Metal, Ska, Avant Garde, and so many others. He has shared the stage with Jazz greats including Joe Lovano, Randy Brecker, Peter Erskine, Eliane Elias, Michelle Petrucciani, Will Lee, and many others.

Through his worldly experiences he spent much of that time in studious awe of different world culture and their natural musics. His education includes performance and sound engineering majors at Miami-Dade Community College, performance and Jazz composition majors at Berklee College of Music in Boston (where he won the Quincy Jones Award for Jazz composition) studying with Jazz legends including Herb Pomeroy, George Garzone and Billy Pierce. And 3 years of study of the Ewe and Dagomba drumming and music from Ghana by master drummer Abubakari Lunna.

Specialties: Orchestral Jazz large and small ensemble and pop music compositions and arrangements, Sampling technology, Digital Performer, Cubase, Gigastudio, Kontakt, Pro Tools, Sonar, Finale

International Alliance of Composers

April 2008 – Present (5 years 6 months)

The International Alliance of Composers is currently forming as a non-profit organization of composers from all over the world. We come together as a guild and a trade organization built of, by and for composers. We provide unique benefits and services designed to empower working composers in their careers and their professional lives. From minimum wage suggestions for composition jobs to free lectures and seminars, we provide a comprehensive set of member benefits and work hard to ensure that the IAC serves as a vital resource for working composers.

There are many organizations that support songwriters and lyricists, but we are unique as we are focused primarily on issues and the quality of life for composers. As a composer specific organization, we have the distinct pleasure and responsibility of addressing issues that affect composers on a local, national and global basis. Come to our website at http://www.iacomp.org to find out more about what we do, benefits of membership, and services we offer.
