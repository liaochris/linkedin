Director of Technology

St. Louis Beacon

Nonprofit; 11-50 employees; Online Media industry

October 2010 – July 2012 (1 year 10 months)

The Beacon is one of the top nonprofit online news organizations in the country. I worked with New York-based Next Street Agency as the Beacon's technical lead on a $288,000 project to move from Joomla to a custom-built content management system codenamed Lantern. Lantern was developed using CodeIgniter (a PHP framework), Alternative PHP Cache, Doctrine (an object relational mapper), MySQL, Subversion and a JavaScript-driven application framework (hashbang URLs). I instituted the use of Bugzilla to manage the project and evaluated, prioritized and addressed bug reports and enhancement requests. I installed and configured ResourceSpace for use as the Beacon's image library, and I was the system administrator for their LAMP servers hosted at Media Temple and Rackspace. I was responsible for analyzing metrics for their site traffic, email campaigns and social media presences. I also provided technical support for the staff and visitors to the site.
