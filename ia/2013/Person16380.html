Bunny Randall's Summary

A strong person who has always had an interest in being creative.
I have been a full time designer since 2000, and have worked on many design projects within jobs before this. With a passion for colour and getting things just so design is my dream job.
Running Brandall Design since January 2010 is a great experience and every day is different. One day I could be designing a large format map and the next designing artwork for a postage stamp.

Bunny Randall's Experience

Owner/Designer

Brandall Design

Self-Employed; 1-10 employees; Graphic Design industry

January 2010 – Present (3 years 8 months) Wiltshire

At Brandall Design - we are creatives who care for business image. We improve your revenue FACT!

Its amazing how much good (in fact, excellent) design can impact your business and help you get known for a business who looks the part. I always think you wouldn't turn up to a job interview looking scruffy so would you market your business without having top notch graphic work? The answer is you wouldn't and that is where we come in.

Covering all types of design work we create and evolve companies images. Designing branding for new or existing businesses, brochures, leaflets, flyers, posters right up to large format banners and vehicle livery. Also running our own email newsletter software BrandallMAIL which lets you create your own newsletters and manage your reports.
Working with some totally fantastic companies that we have spent years building relationships with we also offer web design, copywriting and exhibition production.
We are a one stop shop for everything creative for paper, digital and big!

It is always time to review your companies image - we work on your business image so you have time to do the things you do best.

Adult Cadet Instructor

Army Cadet Force

January 2008 – Present (5 years 8 months)

Joining the Army Cadets as an adult instructor I knew that I would get great pleasure in helping young people to have fun whilst learning army skills and discipline.
The Army Cadet Force is a fantastic hobby for children from the age of 12 to get involved in. Teaching things like map reading, shooting, camouflage and concealment is all really good fun for the kids. Its all about making it as interesting as possible and following a syllabus.
As part of Wiltshire Army Cadet Force we have detachments all over the county from Downton to Marlbourgh including Salisbury and surrounding areas. If you have a son or daughter who is over 12 and loves the idea of learning new skills and having fun at almost no cost then get in touch.

Teaching for my second year at Wiltshire college lets me pass on my knowledge as a designer to Art and Design students keen to learn a trade.

Steelhenge Design

Working with an umbrella from Steelhenge Consulting to launch myself as a freelance designer Steelhenge Design was created. As a graphic designer with many skills and experience brought me to want to run my own company, something I have always aspired to do.
Learning the skills of running a business was a good challenge. Whilst working closely with Steelhenge Consulting on all their internal design, including everything from a set of brochures, symbols and diagrams, flyers, reports, pull up banners and large format exhibition graphics I also worked on building up business for external design work.

RAC Soldier

Territorial Army

2006 – 2009 (3 years)

Joining the TA was something I had always wanted to do! Not growing up in a Military environment I am not sure where this came from but I decided to go for it.
Having goals to reach for fitness and the challenge of completing all the tasks to get in was a buzz and something that will stay with me forever. Nominated 2nd best recruit through my training stage was something to be proud of.
Being with the RAC for 3 years gave me varying roles and one of them was being on the recruiting team for the South West region. This involved meeting, co-ordinating and assessing all the new recruits and guiding and testing them throughout various induction weekends. Moving onto their first weekend out in the field where we would work them hard and give them a tough sleepless weekend to see how they react. Great fun for staff and for the recruits.
Throughout this time I re-badged from RAC to AGC to become the Squadron clerk which I loved. When I wasn't working with recruits this role got me involved with training other soldiers on various systems that were in place and also as the only clerk for the whole squadron it was a busy role.
Leaving the TA in 2006 with a lot of good memories to for-fill my role with the Army Cadet Force.
