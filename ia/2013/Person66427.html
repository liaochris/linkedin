John Hamilton's Summary

Risk tolerant investor and facilitator of CEO peer groups to help small and medium-sized businesses in NH.

Please visit my "People and Profit" Blog at: blog.vestedforgrowth.com

Specialties

Types of financing include: royalty, sub-debt and other non dilutive ways to raise capital. CEO peer groups tackle a range of topics including: strategic planning, lean, self-directed teams, profit sharing, business literacy and role of CEO.

John Hamilton's Experience

Managing Director

Vested for Growth

Nonprofit; 1-10 employees; Venture Capital & Private Equity industry

September 2002 – Present (11 years 5 months)

Vested for Growth (VfG) offers risk tolerant capital, business education and CEO peer learning opportunities for owners of small to medium-sized businesses in NH. VfG provides sub-debt and royalty financing when both banks and equity sources are unable to provide enough capital for the needs of the business. VfG also provides management assistance, industry contacts, CEO peer learning opportunities, and connections to additional sources of bank debt and private equity.

VP Economic Opportunity

NH Community Loan Fund

Nonprofit; 51-200 employees; Financial Services industry

April 2002 – Present (11 years 10 months) Concord NH

John leads the Community Loan Fund’s Business Finance program, which connects business owners with the capital and expertise they need to compete in a changing marketplace. His Business Finance team works hard to get to know its borrowers, then applies creativity and experience to get to “yes” on hard-to-get-done deals, and to find the right type of financing to help its borrowers succeed.

Business Finance recently created a Farm and Food Initiative to improve access to capital for farms, food providers and others that support the growth of healthy sustainable food systems in New Hampshire.

President

Workforce Opportunity Council

1999 – 2000 (1 year)

Established the Workforce Opportunity Council (WOC) to make policy and funding allocation decisions based on demand for and effectiveness of workforce programs. Constituents for the system include both employers looking to recruit and people who are under employed.

President

NH Job Training Council

April 1998 – October 2000 (2 years 7 months)

Developed and implemented a plan to transition all service delivery functions to other agencies.

Founding Executive Director

Concord Area Trust for Community Housing

July 1990 – March 1997 (6 years 9 months)

Founded this nonprofit affordable housing organization. Managed its growth from inception to it becoming a community institution with over $3 million in total assets.
