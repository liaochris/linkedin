Holly Grant's Summary

I'm a happy person who loves a job well done, with a huge enthusiasm for the games industry and an urge to show everyone why I think programming is fantastic.

Up until a few months ago I was looking to become a games developer but, since hearing about the change in national curriculum and the need for passionate computing teachers, I have earned a place on the Teach First scheme and will be introducing secondary school students to the world of computer science from September 2014!

In the mean time I'm looking to get as much real-world experience as possible, both within tech and education. If you are looking for some short term help in either of these fields do get it touch, as I have built a decent portfolio of developer and mentor work and would love the opportunity to do more!

Holly Grant's Volunteer Experience & Causes

  • Volunteer Experience

    • STEM Intern

      Teach First
      • Education
      March 2013 – present (5 months)

      Teach First believe that every child should have the best chance at a great education regardless of their socioeconomic background. It's certainly a worthy cause and over Easter 2013 I was fortunate enough to earn a place on their 2 week internship scheme designed to bring STEM undergraduates into schools.

      Over the internship I got some intense pedagogy training before spending a week in Feltham Community College devising and delivering a technology lesson to both a year 8 and year 9 class.

    • Organiser and Mentor

      Techmix
      • Science and Technology
      July 2013 – present (1 month)

      I found out about the start-up Techmix by chance, but found that just a few impassioned individuals were working to bring digital skills to all school age students, using industry leaders and expert workshops to provide school kids an opportunity they'd never had before.

      For a month I helped co-organise, co-host and mentor students in the Silicon Skills Challenge, a development competition for 75 fifteen-year-olds that was run alongside Digital Summer Camp, the UK's largest Digital Skills event.

      Along with helping to run the Silicon Skills Challenge I was also solely responsible for the programming workshop material we delivered within the SSC which was a very rewarding and informative experience.
