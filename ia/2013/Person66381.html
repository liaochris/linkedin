John Gerzema's Summary

As a strategist and leadership consultant, I utilize data, consumer insights, journalism and marketing to identify social change and help companies anticipate and adapt to new interests and demands.

As Executive Chairman of WPP Group’s BAV Consulting, I oversee BrandAsset Valuator, the world's largest database of brands. I have written three best-selling books on social change, consumer behavior and leadership. My new book, The Athena Doctrine is a New York Times best seller and all have appeared on critics “best of” lists at The Wall Street Journal, FAST COMPANY, Washington Post, Bloomberg Business Week, Inc., Amazon editors review and THE WEEK Magazine.

In The Brand Bubble, I identified changes in consumer attitudes that preceded the financial crisis and was #3 in Amazon’s best business books of 2008 and in the best marketing books by Strategy & Business for 2009. My second book, Spend Shift: How The Post Crisis Values Revolution is Changing The Way We Buy, Sell and Live -- is a Wall Street Journal Best Seller and was voted one of the top 10 TED talks by Ask Men in 2010.

I serve as a board advisor to several tech start-ups and NGO's. As a Fellow with the Athena Center for Leadership Studies at Barnard College, I study emergent leadership strategies. I advise The United Nations Foundation's Girl Up Campaign, for whom all proceeds of my new book support.

I'm also a public speaker. TED called me a “marketing whiz”, which is an overstatement. My talks center on innovation, social change, mindful consumption and ethical leadership. My ‘Trend Watcher’ column for Inc. has been widely read and have appeared on various broadcast media. I've written for McKinsey, Harvard Business Review among others and my essay 'The Trouble With Brands' was voted one the top articles of The Decade by Strategy & Business.

More info on me is at www.johngerzema.com and for speaking enquiries contact www.brightsightgroup.com or contact me at jgerzema@bavconsulting.com and @johngerzema.

John Gerzema's Projects

  • TEDx Talk: The Post Crisis Consumer

    • August 2009 to Present
    Team Members: John Gerzema

    There's an upside to the recent financial crisis -- the opportunity for positive change. Speaking at TEDxKC, I identify four major cultural shifts driving new consumer behavior and shows how businesses are evolving to connect with thoughtful spending.

  • TEDx Talk: The Athena Doctrine: How Women (And The Men Who Think Like Them) Will Rule The Future

    • December 2012 to Present
    Team Members: John Gerzema

    We live in a world that’s increasingly social, interdependent and transparent. And in this world, feminine values are ascendant. As authors, we surveyed 64,000 people in 13 nations and traveled to another 18 countries over the past two years meeting with business leaders, NGO's, start-ups and politicians. And around the world, we found strong consensus for inclusion of feminine values and thinking – a view shared nearly equally by women, men and millennials. Also, countries that think in a more feminine way have a higher per capital GDP and higher reported quality of life and people around the world. And the more developed an economy, more people are likely to think with “both” feminine and masculine traits and behaviors. This suggests economic growth and a greater standard of living is enhanced by including feminine strategies. (81% of people said that man or woman, you need both masculine and feminine traits to thrive in today’s world). We interviewed many extraordinary women as well as men from all over the world. The insights point to essential skills you'll need to thrive as a leader and manager in the years ahead. The book comes out in April, 2013 and I will be presenting the book at the global TED Women's conference in December in Washington, D.C.
