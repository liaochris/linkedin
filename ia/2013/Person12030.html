Bart Jellema | b@rtje.net | LION's Summary

I'm a nerd, entrepreneur and love solving problems. At age 6 my dad bought a Commodore 64 and I soon started programming on it. At the age of 14 I developed and implemented a complete CRM system for a large company and ever since I've been developing new software applications and websites.

In 2007 I co-founded my first company: Tjoos (pronounced ‘choose’). The website Tjoos.com started as price comparison engine, but then specialized in online discount coupons. The site grew by 400 percent year on year to well over 1 million unique visitors in February 2010; offering over 500,000 coupons from more than 180,000 merchants. In March 2010 Tjoos.com was acquired by US based Internet Brands (NASDAQ: INET).

Annoyed with my email, I founded ZeroMail in January 2011 to create the next generation email client.

I'm also mentor at StartMate and PushStart, investor in BlackBird and organizer of Silicon Beach Drinks.

Bart Jellema | b@rtje.net | LION's Patents

  • Tool, method and system for testing of a redemption-value activator

    • Australia Patent 2010100229
    • Issued March 16, 2010
    Inventors: Bart Jellema | b@rtje.net | LION, Herman Bosma, Mark Bradley, Gary Brewer

    The invention provides a MARV testing tool for testing one or more redemption value activators or MARVs (Means for Activation of a Redemption Value) on a merchant website, the MARV testing tool being a computer program product for
    performing a MARV testing method to test validity of a MARV. The MARV testing method includes the step of performing at least one function that a user would normally manually perform to test validity of a MARV such that the function(s) can be performed automatically and wherein performance of the function(s) results in the tool being capable of indicating whether the MARV being tested is valid. The MARV testing tool is suitable for automatically testing MARVs on one or more
    merchant websites, thereby overcoming the problems of manual testing.
