Grigor Nazaryan's Publications

  • Improving routing performance In a small world

    • FreeNet Project (Java)
    • February 21, 2011
    Authors: Grigor Nazaryan

    In this thesis, we study algorithms and techniques which may increase efficiency of routing procedure in Milgram’s “Small world experiment”. We consider FreeNet project which is specific case of this problem. The hypothesis is that the division of all population of peers into different subgroups will increase the routing performance and the probability of successful search.
    On the basis of Cloud Computing with a peer to peer connection we develop following models:
    • The division method • Merging technique
    • Dynamic ID implementation • Artificial Intelligence utilization
    • User friendly approach

    All models will be studied separately and afterwards best one’s will be combined as a final model. We perform thorough comparative analysis of basic and enhanced versions of algorithms, opposing the speed of the algorithms to the size of the searched neighborhoods.
