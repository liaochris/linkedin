Coiya Tompkins, MBA's Summary

Award-winning marketing & public relations executive with more than 16 years of experience in developing comprehensive communications programs for both large and small businesses. Adept at aligning strategic communications plans with essential business goals, core competencies include marketing, media relations, employee communications, executive speech writing, crisis management and public relations.

Specialties

Marketing communications & measurement, customer relationship management (CRM), contract negotiation, change management, branding, board & administrative leadership, media relations, community outreach, public relations, advertising, physician relations

Coiya Tompkins, MBA's Experience

Vice President, Corporate Communications & Public Relations

Sun Health/Banner Health

Nonprofit; 1001-5000 employees; Hospital & Health Care industry

November 2010 – Present (3 years 2 months) Sun City, Arizona

Oversee all internal and external marketing and public relations activities for this non-profit health organization. This includes the Sun Health Foundation, Sun Health senior living communities and community health projects such as disease and medication management.

MEDIA RELATIONS: Increased media placements by 60 percent in less than six months. Media value reached more than 19 million impressions and more than $2 million in "free" coverage. Coverage included prominent placement in both local and national markets, including the Wall Street Journal, New York Times and NBC Nightly News.

BRANDING: Developed and implemented a comprehensive and strategic re-branding campaign for this 40-year-old organization.

COMMUNITY RELATIONS: Launched a nonprofit community education program in partnership with Banner Health and area physician practices that reaches more than 2,500 people each year, and drives leads to the organization's senior living (10 percent of attendees request tours) and philanthropic (attendees donate nearly $20K/year) business units.

BUSINESS OPERATIONS: Trimmed marketing and advertising expenses by more than 50 percent. Replaced outside agency with more robust and cost-effective in-house resources, creating efficiencies that saved more than $100/hour and more than $20K/year in people resource expenses.

Marketing Director

Sierra Medical Center/Tenet Health

January 2010 – October 2010 (10 months) El Paso, Texas

Oversaw all internal and external marketing strategies and tactics for this 300-plus bed facility. This included print and broadcast advertising, CRM, direct mail, social media, Web site development and maintenance, employee communications, physician communications, community relations, executive/ghost writing, publications and collateral development for all audiences. Served as a key member of the facility's seven-person administrative team. Sierra Medical Center is one of three Tenet hospitals in El Paso and one of 50 in the nation.

BUSINESS OPERATIONS: Recommended several cost-cutting measures that streamlined marketing production and trimmed the network (multi-hospital) budget by more than $500K.

MARKETING: Hosted nearly 600 online visitors during the region’s first live remote Web cast targeting abnormal heart rhythms. This was a first for the 52-hospital system.

MARKETING COMMUNICATIONS: Developed and implemented several inaugural communication solutions including a comprehensive neuroscience marketing program that show cased award-winning commercials, an interactive boutique Web site, a bimonthly physician newsletter and other physician-relation tools, a weekly employee newsletter, and patient relations/feedback tools.
