Computer/Information Technology/Internet/E-Commerce Law

• Experience Based on Representing Technology/Software Vendors and Users
• Understanding the Interests of Each
• Knowing the Range of Compromises

Freibrun focuses his legal practice in computer/information technology law, Internet law and intellectual property protection (copyrights, trademarks and trade secrets). His computer/information technology law practice consists of representing and advising software and other IT products and services vendors - and their customers - in a wide variety of contractual and intellectual property matters, including the preparation and negotiation of software license and development agreements, systems acquisition, distribution and hosting agreements; consulting services agreements; nondisclosure agreements; source code escrow and other agreements, as well as the development of corporate software, Internet and e-mail use policies, web site terms of use and web privacy policies.

In the negotiation of technology transactions, Freibrun represents vendors and users. He understands the interests of both sides, and the acceptable range of compromises.

Freibrun’s intellectual property law practice consists of representing and advising clients regarding the protection, acquisition, assignment and licensing of intellectual property, including copyright and trademark registration.

In-House Legal Counsel

Accenture (then Andersen Consulting) & Arthur Andersen & Co.

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; ACN; Management Consulting industry

1986 – 1992 (6 years)

Freibrun was one of the principal in-house intellectual property attorneys for Accenture (then Andersen Consulting) and Arthur Andersen & Co. for nearly 7 years in their Chicago headquarters. His responsibilities included advising on an entire range of intellectual property protection issues applicable to information technology development and the licensing and sale of computer software and related professional services engagements. He also coordinated the firms’ worldwide trademark registration programs and reviewed and analyzed ad copy and marketing programs for their respective I.T. and accounting businesses.

Freibrun negotiated and drafted innumerable critical I.T. agreements related to: acquisitions, distribution arrangements, software/technology development and licensing, consulting and other I.T. services, trademark use, employment, confidentiality and publishing. He also assisted the Andersen Consulting Foundation software organization in cost allocation analysis relating to the development of computer aided systems engineering software.

Legal Counsel (via acquisition of American Hospital Supply Corp.)

Baxter

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; BAX; Pharmaceuticals industry

Freibrun was previously a member of the in-house legal departments of American Hospital Supply Corporation (AHSC) and Baxter Healthcare Corporation through its acquisition of AHSC. While at American Hospital Supply Corporation, he assisted with legal issues associated with one of the first software programs enabling communication between PCs in physician offices and hospital mainframe computers. He was also extensively involved in the Department of Justice review of the proposed merger of American Hospital Supply Corporation with Hospital Corporation of America and the merger of AHSC with Baxter Healthcare Corporation.
