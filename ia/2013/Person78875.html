Laura Neidert's Summary

Asking questions has always been an important part of my life. Some of my earliest memories involve reading the newspaper at age 3, regularly watching Peter Jennings on ABC World News Tonight, and generally soaking in as much knowledge as I could about the world around me. When I majored in journalism in college, I finally found an outlet for my need to ask questions-Who? What? When? Where? Why? How? Career advising is an excellent outlet for continuing to ask these questions of students, employers, alumni, and many others.

In my experience, asking these basic questions lets students begin to contemplate their own strengths, weaknesses, and aspirations during our sessions. As a result, they are able to discover their own career story line, build it, and advocate successfully for themselves in the job market, before graduate schools, or in an opportunity of their own creation. Using technology to achieve these goals comes naturally to me. I thoroughly enjoy managing and instructing students on the most effective use of technology resources in their career development. This specifically includes helping students improve their online presence by offering professional LinkedIn profile photos and providing regular instruction on best practices for using LinkedIn.

As I continue to grow my career at the University of Virginia, I am focused on informing students on the best uses of technology in their job search, how to present the best professional image, and being a guide to the intangible qualities of career success. In my joint responsibilities with our Employer Services team, I am directing my energy towards providing a positive experience for students and employers in On-Grounds recruitment efforts and interpreting the needs of employers for the student audience at large.
