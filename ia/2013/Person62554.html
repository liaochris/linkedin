Jeri Walker-Bickett's Summary

Author of contemporary literary fiction and psychological suspense. Freelance editor of fiction and creative nonfiction. Former English teacher at the secondary and college level. In addition, wrote my own curriculum for a creative writing class. My work as an author, editor, and teacher converge on my twisted book blog: What do I know? located at JeriWB.com.

Jeri Walker-Bickett's Publications

  • Such is Life

    • Amazon
    • January 2013
    Authors: Jeri Walker-Bickett

    These realistic short stories feature characters down on their luck, yet stubborn enough to move on. A tryst between a carnival worker and a pretty high school student begs the question of who takes advantage of who. A young man’s encounter with a drug addict finds him striking out on his own in hopes of a better life. An English teacher publishes literature deemed inappropriate by a Mormon community. A mother goes on a quest to get rid of the family’s aggressive pet. Finally, New Orleans provides the backdrop for a stroll with a psychotic housewife. Such is life!

  • Popular Poe Stories in Plain English

    • Amazon
    • January 2012
    Authors: Jeri Walker-Bickett

    Five of Edgar Allan Poe's most popular short stories have been rewritten in a plain English version made more accessible for today's readers. The content has not been shortened or simplified, but Poe’s complex and long-winded writing style has been toned down. Lengthy paragraphs remedied, meandering sentences simplified, unnecessary repetition curtailed, daunting vocabulary replaced, confusing dialogue addressed, and obscure references clarified.

    A man with murderous intentions lures an enemy into his cellar with a false promise in “The Cask of Amontillado” An uninvited guest arrives at a fancy party held during the Plague in “The Masque of the Red Death.” An artist ignores the health of his subject while capturing her likeness on canvas in "The Oval Portrait." A prisoner endures extreme torture in "The Pit and the Pendulum." Finally, a man guilty of murder lets paranoia get the best of him as he tries to convince the reader of his sanity in "The Tell-Tale Heart."
