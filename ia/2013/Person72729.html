Katie Major's Summary

Katie Major is an award-wining graphic designer living in Cleveland, Ohio. Her work has recieved both regional and national awards and featured in numberous blogs and publications, including HOW Magzine. Katie is also a design partner at Fizz Creative, freelance designer for American Greetings in their properties division, and a part-time professor of design.

Outside of work Katie is actively involved in the Cleveland design and business community as a member of various trade organizations like COSE, AIGA and the Lakewood Chamber of Commerce. She also loves to donate her time to non-profit organzations such as refugee resettlement programs as well as a Haitian women's organization.

Katie Major's Projects

  • Letterpress QR Coasters

    • March 2011 to April 2011
    Team Members: Katie Major, Jasen Melnick

    These QR codes allow smart phone users to quickly link to four unique web addresses on our business website, each detailing a specific wine or beer that answers the question prompted on the printed coasters. We talk about our favorite beverages and some possible food pairings we like for each. Check out the pairing links to the left if you haven't already. A simple example of the potential for QR codes, and beautifully printed by our friends at Cranky Pressman.

  • Holiday Cheers

    • November 2011 to December 2011
    Team Members: Katie Major, Jasen Melnick

    Hand-lettered type and custom snowflakes letterpressed on A9 cards. Our objective with was to create a fun and beautiful piece to welcome the Christmas and New Year's holidays, and to remind everyone that we do kick-ass work! Something to stick on the fridge and never want to take down. The design is sparse on information—QR codes and URLs need not apply—because we only gave these shimmery beauties to people we care about, and who know us personally. Risky, yes, but design comes first! As always, we are forever thankful to Cranky Pressman of Salem, Ohio for making these look amazing on their new heavy heavy white stock.

    Cheers!

  • Local Food Systems Identity

    • July 2011 to November 2011
    Team Members: Katie Major, Jasen Melnick

    Local Food Systems is a startup non-profit dedicated to facilitating business connections and economic networks around the core industry of agriculture. Businesses can be anything from farmers to biomass energy producers to manufacturing and many things in between. We avoided heavy usage of green colors and plant imagery so we could define their brand away from food and farmers markets and more toward networking and community growth. The honeycomb matrix was fitting and we carefully selected colors that were strong but mostly subdued for differentiation. We skinned and designed the website in collaboration with Jeff Schuler at Substrate.
