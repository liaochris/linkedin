Chizoma Nosiri, MA's Publications

  • The Non-Communicative Female Consumer: A Look at Conflict & Confidence

    • ProQuest
    • January 2012
    Authors: Chizoma Nosiri, MA

    With low self-confidence, a dissatisfied female consumer is rendered mute. Although globally, women control about $20 trillion in annual consumer spending (Silverstein and Sayre, 2009. p. 2) and make over 85% of the consumer purchases in the US, the female consumer has yet to be a driving force to the structure of customer relations in corporate consumership.

    In a world infused by the male influenced language and structure, the perspective and notion of corporate dominance and customer relations for the female consumer is obscured. Since conflict is unavoidable during a complaint, the obstacles the female consumer faces mounts as she battles this and many other variables, as she approaches corporate personnel face-to-face (F2F). The intent of this study was to determine if F2F complaint was affected by the female consumer's self-confidence. In addition, the study also determines the factors that influence low self-confidence in the female consumer to become non-communicative (mute) in approaching corporate personnel F2F, and the difference in her confidence level and behavior during a complaint using corporate computer-mediated-communication (CMC) tools.
