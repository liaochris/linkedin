    • Independent Adviser

      North Wales Police
      June 2011 – present (2 years 6 months)

      As an independent adviser to North Wales Police, I have had great opportunities to engage directly with the force. This includes advising on, and equality impact assessing policy and policing strategies and being able to consult on a wide range of community engagement as well as equality, diversity, fairness and inclusion related matters. I am also on call 24/7 to advise on critical incidents with Gold Command level clearance.

      We regularly monitor community tension and act as a critical friend to the North Wales Police. We are effective in pointing out if they are making fundamental errors in their policing efforts, but perhaps more importantly, letting them know when the work they do is having a positive effect.

      It's extremely rewarding.

      I also personally designed the IAG section of the North Wales Police website and was honoured to be a member of an All Wales judging panel for the Harry King Special Constable/Team Awards

    • Advisor/Assessor

      Citizens Advice
      • Civil Rights and Social Action
      December 2011 – August 2012 (9 months)

      Volunteering with the CAB was a very rewarding experience. I started out as a Gateway Assessor, which gave me direct access to a range of clients and problems. I progressed through training as a General Adviser and also sat with the Board of Trustee's as the Volunteer Representative.

      The CAB do valuable work and I enjoyed being able to offer people access to justice and to be able to effectively influence social policy on a national scale. I also found that engaging with clients directly was extremely rewarding and interesting, as opposed to my usual 'behind-the-scenes', strategic work.

    • Offender Mentor/Support

      Community Justice Intervention
      • Social Services
      May 2012 – present (1 year 7 months)

      Working with Community Justice Interventions on their COSA project, is extremely challenging, but also extremely rewarding. The work is based around supporting and helping core members to reintegrate back into the community while they are still on licence from prison. The work also helps core members to discuss their feelings and offenses so that we may monitor any continued threat to the community with which they are being reintegrated.

      COSA is primarily concerned with Schedule 1 offenders who have signed the Sex Offenders Register. The work is interesting, intense and very worthwhile as a successful project would see a core member reintegrate successfully, while keeping the community safe. Thus far, in relation to core members re-offending, the projects have a 100% success rate.
