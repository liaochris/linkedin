Emil "AngryAnt" Johansen's Projects

  • Behave

    • July 2008 to Present
    Team Members: Emil "AngryAnt" Johansen

    Behave is a system for designing, integrating and running behaviour logic using Behaviour Trees. At its core Behave has a drag-drop designer and a highly effective and flexible compiled runtime.

    No funky runtime data formats or interpretation. Designer → Compiler → Your code.

  • Path

    • February 2008 to December 2011
    Team Members: Emil "AngryAnt" Johansen

    An open source (MIT) pathfinding solution for the Unity engine.

  • "Urban Mysteries"

    • March 2005 to May 2007
    Team Members: Emil "AngryAnt" Johansen, Caspar Strandbygaard, Morten Iversen

    (Last known working title). An ambitious third person action-adventure shooter. Based in a steam-punk future, the player is put in the role of Our Hero, who by means of time traveling corrects changes in the timeline made by insidious would-be overlords - in an attempt to restore the world to a better future.

    Core mechanics: branching story-line, time travel visibly changing the simulated world, faction standing and crowd mechanics.

    Over the duration of this project, several technologies were utilized - including: RenderWare, CryEngine(1) and UnrealEngine 3.

    Project failed due to a loss of financial support.
