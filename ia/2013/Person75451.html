Khürt Williams' Summary

I’m a systems thinker. I tend to think about how all the pieces and parts of things fit, and work, together. I’m a big fan of patterns — recognizing them, observing them, creating them.

I am an experienced information security analyst with over seventeen years of computer, software, and information technology experience, with over nine years in senior-level technical positions. I support daily security operations and wide range of IT security activities including network and system security monitoring, incident response, and digital forensics.

I help assess risk and implement enterprise information security standards, processes and policies in coordination with company Information Technology and internal and external audit teams to comply with regulatory requirements, with enterprise information technology and security policies to meet the business and information management needs of the company.



I help develop security strategy, architecture, process and policy to manage the security risks associated with employee access to the Internet and serve as an advisor to the Network Security and Security Awareness programs. I serve as a thought-leader in internal IT peer-groups, and design and drive effective education of IT staff and end users on information security.

I write about technology on several blogs and background includes multimedia research and development, IT security, and consulting. I’ve been a Web Developer, Web Master, Technical Lead, Project Manager, Service Delivery Manger and Systems Security Manager.

I am also active in the Princeton Macintosh Users Group and currently serve as its President.

I am a Certified Information Systems Security Professional (CISSP), ITIL v3 Foundation certified, and Certified in Risk and Information Systems Control (CRISC).
