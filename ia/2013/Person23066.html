Chris Beeler's Summary

My experience's are probably not unique. I began working in a lawn care business with my brother and Dad at age 11 until age 16, when I began employment with Wal-Mart. I stayed with Wal-Mart until I graduated high school and overall had a good experience. When I left Wal-Mart I was the furniture department manager.

After High School & a few years of college I got married and had a babygirl (who at the time of this writing is 16) and I had to put my academics on hold and start working.

Apparently my entrepreneurial experiences in lawn care and the management efficiencies I learned at Wal-Mart made me a very difficult employee. I would go to work for someone and I would find myself having a disagreement with my supervisor about workflow efficiencies, among other things. I went through 15+ jobs in 48 months.

Finally a life insurance manager named Tommy Williams came by my home. He apparently was desperate and needed a go-getter to fill a local vacancy for a debit life insurance route. I saw the opportunity and within 2 years had sold enough life insurance to win a trip to Hawaii.

I stayed with the debit life insurance company until 1998 when I tried the waters with a local State Farm agent. Once again, we clashed and it didn't last long but I got a taste of the P&C business & knew that I could build a book of business.

In mid 1998 I actually began calling on independent agents and interviewing them. I wanted to know if they had a position for a commission only agent but I added the stipulation that they must teach me their business model and offer to partner with me on an agency with-in two years,

I found a good man in Marshall Irwin and the Irwin Agency. I have stated on many occasions that Marshall Irwin has had his own set of issues but he is a good insurance agent and he presented an opportunity to me that I'll always be grateful for.

Without a single customer, I opened the doors of Beeler Insurance 12/2000.

Specialties

Social networking, use of technology, knowledgeable agents, and knowing that the boss around our office is always the client.
