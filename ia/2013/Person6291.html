Anderson Sandes' Summary

Anderson Sandes is a bilingual technical writer, historian, researcher, educator, and higher education professional. He worked on two multinational and multidisciplinary projects of the U.S. National Science Foundation as a technical writer and Geographic Information Systems (GIS) consultant and research assistant. While working with GIS, Anderson created and managed vector and raster GIS datasets, performed spatial investigations, generated mapping products, and developed educational web mapping applications using ESRI's ArcGIS, ArcGIS for Server, and Google Maps.

Anderson has vast experience as a technical writer, educator, and researcher. He wrote a manual aimed at teaching the open source GIS software MapWindow to individuals with little to no knowledge of GIS. These groups were skeptical of GIS. To assist his team and supervisors, Anderson created online demonstrations, delivered lectures, and collaborated with a global team of developers to ensure that their software accurately reflected the needs of the target audience. The project increased awareness of the use of GIS and of MapWindow in the target audience.

Anderson earned two Master's degrees, an M.A in Historical Resources Management (2010) and an M.S in GISc (2012), from Idaho State University, in the United States. He also earned a licentiate (B.Ed) degree in Portuguese (2007) from the Universidade of Fortaleza, in Brazil. He speaks English and Portuguese with native fluency and has professional knowledge of Spanish. When he is not working on his projects, he enjoys sightseeing and stargazing.
