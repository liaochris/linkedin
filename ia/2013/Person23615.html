✔ Chris Paschane, MBA, Fitness Entrepreneur, USAF Veteran's Summary

Chris Paschane, MBA, MWRS, BABC, AA, DT, APT, RES, RTS, AFP, PT, FT, CM, Gulf War Veteran. With 24 years of experience in fitness, strength, and sports performance training, Chris is a proven industry strategist and entrepreneur.

He earned an MBA & BABC in entrepreneurship both with Summa Cum Laude honors; he is a USAF Tech School graduate in Diet Therapy & Combat Medicine; earned an AA in Phys. Ed. with Honors, and MANY fitness certs.

Currently, Chris Paschane serves as:

* Brand Manager, adidas Training– a division of Impex
* Founder & CEO, Veterans Fitness Career College
* Editor in Chief, Patriots' Fitness Magazine

Chris's previous experience includes:

* MANAGING fitness departments as an Area Fitness Director at Gold's Gym in Hollywood & N. Hollywood; Area Fitness Manager at LA Fitness in Anaheim; and Fitness Manager at 24 Hour Fitness in Irvine, Beverly Hills, Sacramento; & Santa Clarita Athletic Club

Cryogenics Specialist

United States Air Force

February 1989 – February 1993 (4 years 1 month)

During my first four years, I served as a Cryogenics and Jet Fuel Specialist.

Served in support of the Panama invasion referred to as "Operation Just Cause" in 1989; the Persian Gulf War from 1990 - 1991 including "Operation Desert Storm" followed by "Operation Desert Shield"; and Somalia from 1992 - 1993.

The primary duties of the Cryogenics and Jet Fuels Specialist include receiving, storing and issuing cyrogenics (e.g. liquid oxygen and liguid nitrogen) and petroleum products purchased for use in Air Force vehicles, aircraft and support equipment used on the flight line and throughout the base. Training covers all duties including the actual refueling of aircraft on the flight line with various types of mobile refueling equipment. Other areas of importance are the Fuels Control Center where all requests for fuel are received, and the operator maintenance of refueling vehicles and flight line hydrant systems. There is also the operation of the bulk storage facility where millions of gallons of fuel are stored, issued, and received daily. My highest paygrade held was E-4 Sergeant.
