Elizabeth (Liz) Handlin's Summary

Liz Handlin, CEO of Ultimate Resumes LLC, has more than 20 years of experience in marketing, communications, and recruiting. Ms Handlin earned her BA from the University of Chicago and an MBA from the University of Chicago Graduate School of Business.

In Chicago, Handlin may be best known for creating and managing Money Smart Week in 2002 while at the Federal Reserve Bank of Chicago. Money Smart Week is an annual financial literacy campaign that brings together banks, public libraries, not-for-profits, financial experts, schools, the media, and government agencies to help consumers learn about personal finance.

Ms Handlin was honored by Microsoft Corporation and featured in Smart Money Magazine (June 2004) for creating Money Smart Week (MSW). She is the author of numerous articles on access to banking issues including: Chicago Fed Letter, Remittances and the Unbanked, March 2002, Chicago Fed Letter, Tapping the Potential of the Unbanked – Private Sector Interest Increases, January 2003. Her book, "The Ultimate Book of Thank You and Sympathy Notes" is available on Amazon.com as a Kindle ebook.

Ms. Handlin has been interviewed for and quoted in numerous articles and publications that include: The Economist, The Chicago Tribune, Chicago Sun Times, Community Banker, and American Banker. Ms. Handlin writes articles on resume creation, careers, and related issues and these articles can be found on a variety of websites. She is a featured entrepreneur in the book, "A Cup of Cappuccino for the Entrepreneur's Spirit: Volume II"

After years helping colleagues to write powerful resumes as a hobby, Ms Handlin launched Ultimate Resumes in June 2004.

Ms. Handlin is a past member of Illinois State Treasurer Judy Baar Topinka’s Advisory Board on Women’s issues. She was a founder of Chicago Street Project. She is a past President of the Austin Chapter of Kappa Alpha Theta Alumnae, is an active supporter of the Austin Lyric Opera, and Austin Cavalier King Charles Rescue.
