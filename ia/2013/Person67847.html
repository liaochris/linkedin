Richard Jones' Organizations

  • BICSI

    Member, Standard Committee, Datacenter and Bonding and Grounding sub-committees
    • January 1991 to Present

    From its inception as the BICSI Standards Committee, the BICSI Standards Program creates standards and guidelines for use in the design, installation and integration of information technology systems (ITS) and related telecommunications fields. BICSI standards are written to define current practices and drive improvement in quality and performance over the spectrum of voice, data, electronic safety & security and audio & video technologies, and encompass optical, fiber, copper and wireless-based distribution systems. The BICSI Standards Program is an ANSI-accredited, consensus-based standards development organization, with membership open to all interested parties. www.bicsi.org/

  • TIA

    TR-42 Standards Committee
    • January 2007 to Present

    TIA TR-42 formulating standards committee is responsible for user premises telecommunications cabling design and installation standards and is American National Standard Institute (ANSI) accredited. Applications are copper and fiber cabling, pathways and spaces, outside plant, bonding and grounding, and administration. Environments are commercial, residential, industrial and data centers. TIA TR-42.3 is the Commercial Building Telecommunications Pathways and Spaces sub-committee and TR-42.16 is the Premises Telecommunications Bonding and Grounding sub-committee. www.tiaonline.org/

  • ASHRAE

    Member, ASHRAE TC 9.9 Mission Critical Facilities, Technology Spaces and Electronic Equipment
    • January 2007 to December 2011

    Technical Committee 9.9 is concerned with all aspects of Mission Critical Facilities, Technology Spaces, and Electronic Equipment / Systems. This includes data centers, computer rooms/closets, raised floor environments, high density loads, emergency/network operations centers, telecom facilities, communication rooms/closets, and electronic equipment rooms/closets. For more info see http://tc99.ashraetcs.org/

  • The Green Grid

    Chair, Thermal Management Task Group
    • February 2006 to October 2010

    The Green Grid is a global consortium of IT companies and professionals seeking to improve energy efficiency in data centers and business computing ecosystems around the globe. The organization seeks to unite global industry efforts to standardize on a common set of metrics, processes, methods and new technologies to further its common goals. www.thegreengrid.org/
