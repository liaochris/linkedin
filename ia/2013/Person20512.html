Carolyn Cotney's Summary

My lifelong skills have served well-known publications with millions of readers (Avalon Travel/Moon, Frommer's, California Home+Design, Sierra) along with advertising agencies (DraftFCB, Resin, Giant), journals, tech companies, and businesses.

WRITER with an angle

“Damn. That's pretty much perfect.” “Great article, you nailed the magazine's tone and voice.” That praise came from two tough-as-nails magazine editors.

A solar-tech genius, a pregnant rock climber, and Lucy Lawless (a/k/a Xena Warrior Princess) are among the extraordinary people I get to meet every day. That's "work"?

EDITOR with an eye

“You really do have a knack for this work. Meticulous from the first sentence to the last.” Those words came to me from a magazine editor with more than twenty years in publishing—I’m beyond flattered.

When I edit, I polish writing, organize ideas, and notice every detail. When I'm done, sentences flow smoothly, key concepts stand out, and manuscripts hold up to intense scrutiny. Balancing a writer's creative spark against style guides and grammar rules ensures reading is effortless, without losing the writer’s voice or the reader's attention. Clients also appreciate that I stick to instructions and never go off course.

    Team Members: Carolyn Cotney

    (Editing samples available upon request)

    UNEDITED: The Pacific Northwest’s enormous range of habitat—from glacial ice to marine waters, to pungent sagebrush plains, to mountains so high that their uppermost zone is Arctic-Alpine—is home to an equally diverse range of wildlife. The states of Washington, Oregon, and Idaho are known for their forests that, whether eastside or westside, are alike in offering habitat for animals small and large, from chattering squirrels to stately deer, from inch-long ice worms to 6-ton orcas. The wildest, shyest animals--the snowshoe-footed lynx; the curious, stalking cougar; the shaggy, sure-footed mountain goat--still have wild places in which to live: the many wilderness areas of the Northwest. Idaho alone provides more than 4 million wilderness acres. Oregon preserves habitat for wild animals in 35 wilderness areas, and Washington in 30.

    EDITED: From glacial ice to marine waters, pungent sagebrush plains to mountains in the Arctic-Alpine zone, the enormous range of habitat in the Pacific Northwest is home to an equally diverse range of wildlife. Washington, Oregon, and Idaho are known for their forests and waterways, offering habitat for animals small and large from chattering squirrels to stately deer, inch-long ice worms to six-ton orcas. Even the most wild and shy animals—the snowshoe-footed lynx, the stalking cougar, the sure-footed mountain goat—still have untamed places to live in the northwest. Idaho alone provides more than four million wilderness acres. Its Frank Church-River of No Return Wilderness is the largest in the Lower 48; through its 2.3 million acres runs the Middle Fork of the Salmon River, federally designated as “Wild and Scenic.” Oregon preserves habitat for wild animals in thirty-five wilderness areas, and Washington in thirty.
