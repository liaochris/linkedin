Lakshman Gugulloth's Publications

  • Efficiency and Effectiveness of SEBI as Security market Regulator

    • March 31, 2010
    Authors: Lakshman Gugulloth

    I have done the Research in the Capital Markets on the present Regulation and working of the Regulators. I have submitted my Research Dissertation to IIM Bangalore. I was also selected to do the Ph.D (Finance) at IIMB on Capital Market Regulators.

  • Microfiannce to eradicate the poverty in India

    • International Conference on E-Governance (Held at IIMB)
    • April 30, 2010
    Authors: Lakshman Gugulloth

    The paper deals with the multi-faceted aspects of the poverty in India and how far the Microfiannce has been able to address it, how fat it can address and what it cannot address. I have presented a base paper for the 'Public Finance' course at IIMB. After that, I have referred more than 250 research papers on the subject to convert it into a hi-fi concept paper on poverty. I have been planning to publish some more papers in this area, which includes commercialisation of microfinace etc.

  • e-GIF Framework for India

    • International Conference on E-Governance (Held at IIMB)
    • April 30, 2010
    Authors: Lakshman Gugulloth

    This research paper deals with the necessity of e-GIF (e-Government Interoperability Framework) for India and deals with what are the salient aspects it should address. Integration of systems across all Departments is essential for integrated delivery of public services. All the prominent e-GIF frameworks existing in various countries and what can be adopted out of them has been enumerated. It recommends a framework for the country.

  • Regulatory Failures in Sub Prime Crisis and Lessons for Emerging Countries

    • Presented at Syracuse University, New York state, USA
    • November 27, 2008
    Authors: Lakshman Gugulloth

    This paper deals in detail the failure of regulation in regulating the sub prime mortgage property market, which has led to one of the worst financial crisis after the famines of 1930s. The regulatory vaccum, regulatory failures and inadequacies of the regulation in the USA capital markets, especially the sub prime mortgage market has been presented in detail. The paper brings forth the salient aspects which are relevan for the emerging markets, like BRIC, ASEAN etc.
