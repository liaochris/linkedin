Daniel Bena's Summary

Dan Bena believes in using the power of the private sector to make the world a better place, through strategic, impactful partnerships that benefit business & society. He currently leads Sustainable Development for PepsiCo, the world’s second largest food and beverage company, which serves consumers in more countries and territories than the United Nations has members.

Dan is a liaison between Government Affairs, Public Policy, Communications and Operations to “connect the dots” for impactful advocacy and messaging. He serves on the Leadership Group of the World Business Council for Sustainable Development (WBCSD); Steering Board of the IFC’s 2030 Water Resources Group; & formerly served on the Steering Committee of the UN CEO Mandate, and the Board of the International Society of Beverage Technologists. Most recently, Bena was elected to the Boards of the US Water Alliance and the Creative Visions Foundation, a non-profit dedicated to developing youth as agents of positive social change. He also serves on the Advisory Board for ArtisticCircles.org and their WaterPressures documentary and program.

Represented PepsiCo as one of three invitees by the Government of Germany to speak about the Food:Water:Energy Nexus prior to Bonn 2011. Delivered the keynote address at a Royal Gala in the presence of TRH Princess Madeleine and Prince Daniel of Sweden, and has spoken at over 100 venues which span the business, government, academic, and non-profit worlds. One of four witnesses invited to testify to the US Senate Committee on Energy & Natural Resources, & was invited by the mayor of his city to serve on a new Sustainability Advisory Board to develop a sustainability plan for the city, one of only three nationwide selected to pilot a tool kit based on the successful PlaNYC for the City of New York. Published his first book, Sustain-Ability: How a Corporate Conscience Sustains a Company’s Ability to Win, to help other companies along their sustainability journeys.

Specialties

Sustainable development, sustainability, integration across multiple functions for collective impact. Effective communication of technical subjects to various non-technical stakeholders--senior management, governments, NGOs, and others. Comprehensive, holistic water stewardship strategies.
