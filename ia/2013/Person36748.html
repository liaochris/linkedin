Donna Fenn's Summary

I'm an expert on entrepreneurship and small business trends, which I've been writing about for more than twenty years. I'm the author of two books: Upstarts! How GenY Entrepreneurs Are Rocking the World of Business (McGraw-Hill, 2009); and Alpha Dogs: How Your Small Business Can Become a Leader of the Pack (Collins, 2005). My work has appeared in Inc. Magazine, The New York Times, Newsweek, The Associated Press, Reader's Digest, The Washington Monthly, Working Woman, Working Mother, Family Money, CFO, Corporate Finance, Parents, and New England Monthly. I'm also a public speaker and have given keynote presentations at several national and international business conferences in the U.S., Canada, South America, and Europe. I lived in Saudi Arabia from 1988-1992, where I worked as a correspondent for the Associated Press and a stringer for Newsweek.

Specialties

Young entrepreneurs, generational trends, social networking. workplace innovation, employee engagement, branding, family business, women entrepreneurs

Donna Fenn's Projects

  • Upstarts: How GenY Entrepreneurs are Rocking the World of Business

    • January 2008 to November 2009
    Team Members: Donna Fenn

    Upstarts deconstructs the DNA of the most entrepreneurial in history -- GenerationY -- and identifies eight traits that characterize their impact on the larger entrepreneurial landscape. Upstarts, along with my involvement in Inc. Mangazine's 30 Under 30 Coolest Entrepreneurs feature, solidified my reputation as an expert on young entrepreneurs.

  • Alpha Dogs: How Your Small Business Can Become a Leader of the Pack

    • January 2004 to September 2005
    Team Members: Donna Fenn

    Alpha Dogs, my first book, profiles eight companies in ordinary industries that have become extraordinarily successful not by virtue of what they do but how they do it. By focusing on customer service, employee engagement, branding, innovation, strategic partnerships, and other key strategies, ANY business can move to the head of the pack by differentiating itself from bigger, more well-resourced companies.
