Katie Beckwith's Projects

  • Yearbook Tagged Photos - Shuffle Viewer

    • January 2013 to Present
    Team Members: Katie Beckwith

    As a product manager at classmates.com, I developed a method of viewing yearbook content online that focuses on the people and classmates.com members pictured in the yearbook. The intent of this project was to increase the number of profile visits generated per user session with the yearbook and, in future iterations, increase the number of photo tags and user generated content related to the books. Upon initial launch, the new yearbook shuffle viewer garnered a 40% increase in KPIs.

  • Invite to Reunion from Facebook

    • February 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Katie Beckwith

    As a product manager at classmates.com, I implemented an integrated inviation feature that allowed reunion invitees to extend an invitation to members of their Facebook Social Graph. By allowing for invitations to be sent via Facebook wall post, Classmates.com was able to generate more social awareness of events being planned on the site and encourage virality through social graph interactions.

  • Tag Yearbook Photo from Social Graph

    • November 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Katie Beckwith

    As a product manager at classmates.com, I implemented a tagging feature for our collection of online yearbooks that allows members to tag photos of their friends and family using their Facebook social graph. By allowing for members to tag people outside of the classmates.com database, we were able to market our yearbook content to a wider, social network of individuals and to build out our content relationship graph. Member-created Facebook photo tags generated a Facebook wall post that displayed a clipped image of the yearbook photo and an inviation to view the photo clip in the context of the yearbook on the classmates.com website.

  • Conversations

    • August 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Katie Beckwith

    As a product manager at classmates.com, I released a new conversations feature that allowed members to comment on various actionable objects. The first implementation of this feature was on Reunions. Conversations on reunions allowed for members to comment on a reunion, reply to an existing comment on a reunion, as well as "like" existing comments and replies on the page. The pre-existing commenting feature did not allow for commenting on the reunion page, itself, nor did it allow for "liking" a comment. Upon launch, a 30% increase in reunion conversation activity was documented.

  • You be the Judge

    • December 2011 to Present
    Team Members: Katie Beckwith

    As a product manager at classmates.com, I created a gamified experience that engaged classmates.com members with the content on our new website, memorylane.com. This feature was built to engage users with nostalgic content located on Memory Lane, generate user generated content in the form of "likes" on that content, and to encourage new connections between members with shared interests. Upon launch, of the members who played, more than 75% answered all 100 questions in a single session, delivering on the gamification objective. Memory Lane content is no longer a company priority and this feature is in the process of being deprecated.
