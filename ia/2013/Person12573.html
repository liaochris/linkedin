Becky Feola's Summary

I have always had a focus on serving others whether it was in my personal life or my career.

I have been involved in the nonprofit sector off and on since 1994 when I was employed by Eden Housing, Inc. Eden is a corporation that develops affordable/safe housing for low income families throughout the San Francisco Bay Area and northern CA. In 1997, I resigned my position and for the next eight years, I was the primary caregiver for my husband until his death in 2005. Since that time, my professional experience consisted of the conceptual design, development, funding and operations of a non-profit, The NJF Center for Caregivers. As Ex. Dir., I planned, directed and oversaw all aspects of NJF Center’s policies, programs and operations.

Currently, I own Assisted Living Advantage that provides guidance, counseling and placement services for those seeking assisted living for their loved ones when they can no longer live on their own. We match your loved one’s care needs, personal/geographic preferences and ability to pay with the best facility available! We also specialize in working with families dealing with Alzheimer’s and dementia care. www.assistedlivingadvantage.com

I have personally seen how difficult it can be to find that perfect care facility without the assistance of someone who understands licensing, levels of care, and appropriate pricing. I view myself as a “matchmaker” and am determined to create the perfect match where your loved one will move one time only to a new home where they will thrive as much as possible during their remainder years.

I also perform free seminars throughout the Greater Phoenix Area to educate the public on assisted living and their options in order to help them make informed decisions regarding their loved one.

Founder/Executive Director

NJF Center for Caregivers, Inc.

April 2005 – April 2009 (4 years 1 month)

The mission of the NJF Center is to support informal caregivers, their families, and the elderly population seeking supportive, accessible, impartial and ethical solutions to finding resources, assisted living facilities and/or in-home care. Our goal is to assist families providing care to a loved one with a chronic and/or terminal illness in planning and managing that care and allowing their loved one to remain on their own or with them for as long as possible. We also work with the elderly, who are living independently, to access resources that will allow them to age in place safely and comfortably. However, in today’s society, people are living longer than before. With this longevity comes increasing care demands, if that client’s care needs rise to a level they can no longer meet on their own, we will offer unbiased, ethical and moral options for them and assist them in that transition.
