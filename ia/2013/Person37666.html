David Fraser's Summary

David Fraser is an author, speaker, and coach or trusted advisor (or interventionist, if you prefer) and a leading authority on leadership and interpersonal effectiveness as key to success in all endeavours. He is originator of the "Relationship Mastery" philosophy. David works with business leaders, corporate executives, SME owner-managers, entrepreneurs and individuals:

• Developing leadership and strategy
• Improving sales and delivery performance
• Enabling organisational learning, change, and growth
• Managing programmes and projects
• Facilitating collaborative working, out-sourcing, and public-private partnerships
• Honing interpersonal relationship skills
• Clarifying personal purpose, direction, and self-belief

As W. Edwards Deming said, “Help must come from outside because a system cannot understand itself”...

David brings out the best in people. He resolves complex problems, deals with change, and helps individuals, teams and organisations improve performance and achieve—in their words—amazing results, through profoundly powerful, and—crucially and unusually—holistic insights, drawing on:

• Principles of systems, organisational learning and change
• Practical psychology, NLP and mediation expertise
• Engineering and projects background
• Board-level business experience
• Various timeless principles

As one client said, "The only one around with the skills to make this happen. Able to interpret what is required, take board level responsibility and make it happen without needing further input, coming forward with options when appropriate. I didn’t need to worry about it from one meeting to the next.”
Craig Lockhart, Managing Director, Babcock Marine

  • The Unreasonable Learners

    • June 2011 to Present
    Team Members: David Fraser, Gordon Hall, Andy Lippok, Fiona Savage, Mike McCarron, Tony Miller, Wendy Chalmers Mill, Colin Campbell, Louie Gardiner

    The Unreasonable Learners is a loose association of changemakers in Scotland - people in various roles and walks of life who believe we could be running our collective affairs with greater intelligence and mindfulness - with our “theories in use” made explicit. This philosophy finds expression among other things in dissatisfaction with the consequences of command and control styles of management in which the targets tend to become the customer rather than the user of the service. The UL is a champion of management styles that recognise the intrinsic motivation of the individual - in short that people inherently want to do a good job.

    The name Unreasonable Learners echoes George Bernard Shaw's dictum that all progress depends on the unreasonable man (or woman), because the reasonable person accepts things as they are. People in the network also recognise their individual responsibility to learn and to be open to not knowing.

    For more information see http://www.unreasonable-learners.com (The heading of this section is a link to that URL). New members are very welcome.
