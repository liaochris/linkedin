Roy Gibson's Summary

I started doing woodwork as a child in my dad's shop at home. When my father died and I inherited his tools, I needed someway to justify keeping all these tools in the house. This was the start of my first business called Crafty Hands. This was in northern California.

Upon moving to Colorado, the business was recreated as Laughing Coyote Woodworks. My earliest products were fireplace bellows and clocks.

My primary goals are to make practical, affordable wooden items that people will enjoy and that they can't find in the Big Box stores. I also want to make a living doing something I like to do.

Specialties

I'm a generalist. I've done just about everything working with wood that you can do, from framing to fine detail carving.

Roy Gibson's Experience

Owner-Furnituremaker-Woodworker

Laughing Coyote Woodworks

1991 – Present (22 years)

I'm responsible for designing and building everything on our website. Nothing is contracted out; there are no sub-assemblies that I buy. Everything on the website starts out as rough-sawn wood from the lumber mill, and is worked on by one person (me) all the way through.

Woodworker

Raimer's Furniture Repair

August 2004 – August 2005 (1 year 1 month)

When my former employer decided to scale back his business as he entered retirement, I started working for another furniture repairman who was renting space in the same building. His specialty was elaborate Victorian furniture and rare antiques. We worked side by side for a year, and it was a valuable learning experience to see how these old pieces were constructed and put together.

Woodworker

Shaker Valley Woodworks

August 2002 – August 2004 (2 years 1 month)

When I moved to New England, it took a little while to get Laughing Coyote established locally. During that time I took a part-time job doing furniture repair for a woodworker who specializes in restoring furniture in Pittsfield, MA. The shop did everything from reglueing chairs to recreating carved parts of Victorian furniture.
