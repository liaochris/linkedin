Dan Morvay's Summary

Have worked extensively with the semiconductor industry as a Plasma Etch Process Engineer, moving up to a Plasma Etch Staff Engineer, to my current position of Plasma Etch Process Manager (Texas Instruments -> Lam Research - Tokyo Electron Limited). Have started with 3 inch wafers in school, transitioned to 6 inch wafers at my first semiconductor job, and as of 4/15/2012 saw my first 450mm wafer and wafer handler, dealing with 300mm manufacturing since 2000, and 200mm manufacturing since the mid 90's! Started working at 1MB DRAMs, learned EPROMs, transitioned to Flash Memories, now working with FinFETs! Have managed projects (trained others with semiconductor software usage on how to reduce downtime, troubleshooting tool problems faster) at multiple customer sites, and have managed web site deployment as a knowledge management tool for multiple sites (SharePoint). Currently manage etch process engineers keeping etch tools running with as high availability as possible, working with the engineers to constantly improve tool performance to 1) match customer expectations, then to 2) go beyond customer expectations.

Specialties Include: Poly etch, Silicon etch, Oxide etch, Metal etch, SEM operation, SPC techniques, DOE optimizations (can perform independent of software), people management.

Also learned HTML, PHP, MySQL and have successfully deployed sites, utilizing decent designs, with everything from static pages through completely dynamic page content containing user friendly back end logins. Specialties here include: Web Site Creation, Search Engine Optimization, Internet Hosting, E-mail, Domain Names.
