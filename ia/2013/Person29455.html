Daniel Cornwall's Summary

An experienced librarian and researcher who has worked in a variety of settings. Strong knowledge of public government information resources and variety of resources purchased by libraries nationwide for their users. Believe strongly in a mixed media future and the need for librarians and other information professionals to make themselves known in online venues. One of my professional goals to is work with colleagues to ensure that our entire user base is aware of both our virtual and tangible services.

I am a published author. I was one of five coauthors of Tapping state government information sources, Greenwood Press, 2003, ISBN - 1573563870. Find in a library at http://worldcat.org/oclc/51647542

Specialties

Researching government information at state and federal levels, research in printed and online resources. Expertise in blogging and wiki creation.

Daniel Cornwall's Projects

  • State Agency Databases Project

    • June 2007 to Present
    Team Members: Daniel Cornwall

    A librarian led effort to locate and annotate publicly searchable databases produced by state agencies in the United States.

  • Alaska Constitutional Convention Recordings

    • 2012 to 2012
    Team Members: Daniel Cornwall, Chris Hieb, Stacy Katasse

    This digitized collection represents approximately 220 hours of all plenary sessions and most meetings of the Committee of the Whole during the Alaska Constitutional Convention held at the University of Alaska, Fairbanks from Nov. 8th, 1955 until Feb. 6th, 1956. The meetings were recorded by Mr. Carroza, Station manager of KFAR in Fairbanks. In 2008, with funding from the Alaska Historical Society, the recordings were digitized.

  • Digitized Alaska Territorial Governor Correspondence

    • 2012 to 2012
    Team Members: Daniel Cornwall, Chris Hieb, Stacy Katasse, Stan Hubbard, Lynn Van Brocklin

    Correspondence of Territorial Governors from 1884-1919, includes extensive files on Alaska's World War I draft boards and detailed accounting for schools of the period. Also contains some missing persons investigations.

  • Healthcare practitioners databases

    Team Members: Daniel Cornwall, Lynn McClelland

    This is one page within the much larger State Agency Databases project, a part of ALA Government Documents Roundtable (GODORT) wiki: http://wikis.ala.org/godort/index.php/State_Agency_Databases
    It's a useful resource, check it out!
