Ben Bradley's Summary

Known for wearing plaid and sweater vests before they were popular, Ben Bradley is managing director of Macon Raine - a B2B marketing agency.

He drives a mini-van, enjoys soup and long walks on the beach. He can prepare 5 minute rice in 4 minutes.

He lives at the intersection of sales, technology and marketing with his wife, two children and a purebred Latvian Goathound named Stella.

As an avid indoorsman, his primary interests include CRM, marketing automation, change management, university recruiting, micro-finance, military public affairs, self-organizing networks, network and physical security, collaboration and groupware.

He frequently lectures his children on a variety of topics.

He is a graduate of the University of Iowa and was a member of the undergraduate Iowa Writer’s Workshop.

Specialties

B2B marketing agency, CRM, strategic marketing execution, PR, marcom, interactive, content development, salesforce.com, marketing automation

Ben Bradley's Publications

  • Illinois Veteran Academy

    • Robert van der Hooning and Ben Bradley
    • August 27, 2010
    Authors: Ben Bradley, Robert van der Hooning

    Robert van der Hooning and Ben Bradley, advocates for veterans' education, proposed to establish an academy for veterans that offered a coop- and night-school model.

    Veterans represent scarce technical and entrepreneurial resources which the State of Illinois' economy could benefit from. The Illinois Veteran Academy could be established as a stand-alone institution or developed in partnership with other public universities and community colleges in Illinois.

    The Illinois Veteran Academy would offer an "inverted" academic model and allow for fast specialization in 3-4 course sequences necessary for veterans to find and retain employment. The curriculum would be limited to only 6-8 disciplines in cooperation with Illinois employers.

    Classes would be competency-based and aided by adaptive learning technologies to accelerate learning and remedy specific knowledge gaps after years of active military service.
