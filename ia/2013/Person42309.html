Eric Oberstein's Experience

Associate Director

Duke Performances

Educational Institution; 10,001+ employees; Higher Education industry

October 2012 – Present (1 year) Durham, NC

Duke Performances, the professional performing arts presenting body at Duke University in Durham, North Carolina, is committed to presenting willfully eclectic, forward-thinking artistic work of the highest quality. Through performances, visiting artist residencies, commissioning and development of new work, and engagement with Duke's campus and the Durham community, Duke Performances celebrates cultural offerings incubated locally, nationally, and internationally, while reinforcing the educational mission of Duke University. Annually, Duke Performances offers a robust season of 60-70 world-class performances, making use of a network of more than a dozen Duke & Durham venues, and presenting artists spanning classical, new music, jazz, Americana, independent rock, world music, theater, and dance.

Author

20UNDER40

2010 – Present (3 years)

In light of the impending generational shift in leadership the field of the arts and arts education is about to experience, there has been much talk about the future: who will be our new leading arts thinkers, administrators, policymakers, and practitioners—and in what social, cultural, and political landscapes will these individuals operate? While there is great concern surrounding this matter, little is being done to provide a platform for tomorrow’s leaders to share their ideas with the larger field.

20UNDER40 endeavors to collect twenty essays about the future of the arts and arts education, each written by an emerging leader under the age of forty. In doing so, this anthology will provide a unique arena for new ideas by formally gathering the thoughts of young artists, teaching artists, administrators, researchers, and other arts and arts education professionals—legitimizing the talent of young leaders by bringing their ideas out of the margins and into the forefront of our dialogue.

Executive Director

Afro Latin Jazz Alliance

Nonprofit; 1-10 employees; Performing Arts industry

August 2010 – September 2012 (2 years 2 months) New York, NY

The Afro Latin Jazz Alliance is dedicated to preserving the music and heritage of big band Latin Jazz, supporting its performance for new audiences, and educating young people in the understanding and performance of this important cultural treasure. The Alliance maintains a world-class collection of Latin jazz musical scores and recordings, provides institutional support for the GRAMMY Award-winning Afro Latin Jazz Orchestra directed by Arturo O'Farrill, and provides education programs for young musicians and new audiences.

*Producer of Arturo O'Farrill and the Afro Latin Jazz Orchestra's "40 Acres and a Burro," nominated for a GRAMMY Award for Best Large Jazz Ensemble Album
