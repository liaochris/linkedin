Kevin Miles' Summary

In business, I believe your value should be based on the power, reach and influence of the ideas you contribute over time on a consistent basis. The ultimate measure of this power, reach and influence are the results attributed to your ideas and the ideas you champion.

Throughout my career, my ideas have consistently achieved remarkable results. Not due solely to my efforts alone, but in many instances, as a by product of working alongside some of the best minds in the business. Scores of individuals vastly more talented than I – who have inspired and pushed me to create not just some of my most effective work, but also some of the work I’m most proud of. Work which has been recognized by organizations such as: Communication Arts, The One Show, Clios, Cannes, The Webbys, Bestadsontv.com, Ads of the World, Campaign Brief, The New York Festivals and the ADDYs.

I look forward to contributing more business-building, award-winning ideas in the near future.

Copywriter

DMB&B

Public Company; 51-200 employees; Marketing and Advertising industry

March 1987 – September 1992 (5 years 7 months) St. Louis, MO

This was my first job right out of college. It was a great place to learn with lots of opportunities to get great work produced. I worked with some of the best people in the business here, many of whom are now at some of the top creative shops in the business. I won lots of awards and was recognized at Cannes and the Clios in television, radio and print, and had my work featured in Business Week, The Wall Street Journal, The New York Times, Adweek, Advertising Age, Art Direction, Print's Design Annual, the St. Louis Post Dispatch and The St. Louis Journalism Revue. My biggest claim to fame however was, along with my partner at the time, Terry Taylor, conceiving the idea that won the Burger King Business for the New York Office of DMB&B – in just 2 days. This accounted for $120 million in new business (the account was split between DMB&B and another agency so the account actually totaled $240 million) which was at the time the biggest account shift in the history of advertising.
