Brandon Woods' Summary

I have worked in the digital design industry for nearly 12 years. I left Florida to enter the Rochester Institute of Technology as a print major, but eventually switched majors to follow my real career path as a web designer. After graduation I lived in the Rochester area for about a year, working as a sales representative for a web company called Grip Media. Grip Media was a small web company, which allowed me a lot of creative web freedom and was my first real web design job.

However, while visiting a friend in Atlanta, I fell in love with the city -- a little bit New York and a little bit Florida, it was the perfect place for me! I moved to Atlanta in late 2004 and took a job at InnoShare, a full-service web shop in Sandy Springs. InnoShare is where I really had a chance to begin my career as a web designer, working directly with customers on requirements and branding. I left after almost six years of building a solid client base and designing / project managing dozens of full websites for many of the most recognizable places in Atlanta, including Park Tavern, Van Michael, Twisted Taco and the Fabulous Fox Theater, among others.

Currently I am with Anderson Press, working with book publishers and product designers in a wide range of fields. I have spent the past year-and-a-half managing and designing the sites for all the Anderson Press properties, which include everything from Dalmatian Press (children’s books) to Whitman Publishing (numismatic books), Burnes of Boston (home décor), and Tutu Couture (children’s costumes). The largest project so far has been moving the Red Book, Whitman’s number-one-selling coin price guide (and at more than 23 million copies sold, the best-selling U.S. book of all time), to the web. I was able to design the interface, design and flow of the website itself, along with its entire mobile application. Our team, was able to complete the entire build inside four months -- well within projections.
