Bret Shefter's Summary

A game writer and designer, Bret unleashes the power of compelling stories and characters to engage and captivate players. His family roots reach deep in the entertainment industry, so storytelling—both good and bad—has been a part of his life from an early age. After he earned his B.A. in English from Yale University, he inexplicably decided to attend law school and become a practicing attorney, using his expertise in storytelling to persuade judges and juries for the next 15 years.

Eventually Bret came to his senses and combined his passion for telling stories with his lifelong love of games and puzzles. He has helped create games for major corporate clients such as Microsoft Corporation, Bungie Studios, Capcom Co., Nestlé, and the BBC; assisted game-design students at the University of Southern California; curated a games exhibit at the Hammer Museum; and collaborated with some of the industry’s top designers. Bret is currently the lead narrative designer on a project at Mega Pickle Entertainment, a global virtual game studio developing its first release.

In his spare time—a rare commodity at best—Bret acts on stage and screen, sings Christmas carols professionally, plays piano and guitar, makes room-clearing puns, consumes more chocolate than the average small town, and of course plays plenty of games. He is a member of the IGDA, TLA, NPL, SAG-AFTRA, and several other acronyms. Got an interesting project or a decadent chocolate recipe? Drop him a line here.
