Founder and President

Clayton Multimedia Inc.

Public Company; 1-10 employees; Media Production industry

September 1985 – July 2000 (14 years 11 months) San Francisco

Leveraged the disparity between strength of unions and strength of technology between Northern and Southern California to help the Bay Area become the center of digital media production and post-production. Moved film post production from film stages to computer-sync based studios. Helped move recording from tape to digital tape to non-linear computer systems.

• Worked on the earliest digital-tape recording systems (PCM-F1, PCM-1600, Mitsubishi X-80, DBX-700) as well as last-gasp analog noise reduction systems (Dolby SR).

• Worked on the earliest digital work stations (from SoundCap with proprietary circuit board to the earliest SoundDesigner and ProTools).

• Pioneered SMPTE-based film post production (Audio Kinetics Q-Lock, etc.) for scoring, ADR, sound-design and mixing.

• Introduced high-quality 8-bit audio production for games, advertising, products...(Worlds of Wonder, Buick, Apple, Spectrum-Holobyte, Broderbund and many others).

• Multiple awards including Grammy nomination (Best Engineered Recording). Mixed Bobby McFerrin's Best Jazz Vocals Grammy award winning track.

• Pioneered Blue-book CD production for David Bowie, Brian Eno and many others.

• Oversaw production for the first tour of Soviet Union after Reagan/Gorbachev reinstated Cultural Exchange between US/USSR...Dave Brubeck Quartet. Resulted in TV special and album.

• Produced the first recordings at Lucas' Skywalker Ranch (featuring the San Francisco Ballet Orchestra). (Last I heard, a photo of that first session is still on the wall in the control room at Skywalker Sound.)

• Worked on many Academy Award winning films, Grammy winning albums and Emmy winning TV shows. Projects with Michael Jackson, Dave Brubeck, The Cure, Brian Eno, David Bowie, Mel Torme, Sam Shepard, David Byrne, Norman Mailer, Apple Computer (Knowledge Navigator, Newton) and many others.

  • Navigation using a search engine and phonetic voice recognition

    • United States Patent 8,380,512
    • Issued March 10, 2008
    Inventors: Gary Clayton, Jeff Bonforte, Victor Chen

    A method and apparatus for incorporating voice recognition into a search engine is provided. The phonetic voice recognition system lacking grammar and spell checking is used. The output of the phonetic voice recognition system is forwarded to a search engine. The search engine performs disambiguation and relevancy analysis based on past similar queries. Search engine user behavior is recorded to improve the accuracy. Recorded statistics are used to rank results pages.

  • Enhanced Signaling For Mobile Communications Devices

    • Filed February 21, 2013
    Inventors: Gary Clayton, John Pollard, Shree Madhavapeddi, Pim van Meurs

    A data communication system routes calls from a mobile communication device associated with a calling party to a mobile communication device associated with a called party. The transmitted audio is supplied to the voicemail server for conversion from an audio to a text format.

  • Voicemail Preview and Editing System

    A voicemail computer system transcribes a voicemail message into text that is presented to a calling party for approval. A calling party is able to approve, disapprove or edit a voicemail message prior to delivery to one or more called parties. The voicemail computer system may analyze a voicemail message to detect errors, omissions, or potentially offensive words. The voicemail computer may analyze a voicemail message to make suggestions as to tone, content or information contained within the voicemail message. The calling party can edit the voicemail message or approve it prior to providing a notification to one or more called parties that they have received the voicemail message.
