Marie Christine Meurs-Gerken's Summary

I am a communications professional, enterprising and energetic, with a high capacity for generating quality results in a team environment. Through my work for Think PR, I have experience with strategic and practical handling of PR and preparation of press materials for large and small brands. Marie Christine Meurs-Gerken's Publications

  • Vi vil ha' noget!

    • Final Thesis
    • February 23, 2012
    Authors: Marie Christine Meurs-Gerken, Mai Lindegaard Pedersen, Lisa Svane Baltzer Parsberg

    Through theoretical and qualitative empiric studies this thesis examines: how and on which terms museums henceforth can communicate with their users through social medias – more specifically on facebook?

    By means of focus group interviews we have focused on the users motives, needs preferences and their view on our two cases – National Gallery of Denmark and the National Museum in Copenhagen – and their effort on social media. Through interviews with representatives for the two museums it was possible to get an insight into their use and position on social media.

    The frame of reference in the thesis is social constructivist, and the focus is on social media, identity theory, processes of individualisation (Anthony Giddens), neo-tribes (Michel Maffesoli), Uses & Gratifications theory, and Creative Man (Klaus Æ. Mogensen). Further more the museum's obligations and communications responsibility in correlation with the Danish ‘Museum Act’ and the Ministry of Culture has been studied.

    On the basis of our analysis and discussions we are able to conclude the following:
    The users of social media construct their identity on several levels via facebook profile-editing – they create a picture consisting of photos, status updates, friends and the pages they like, among other things.

    The users socialise in communities of interests that contributes to an individual construction of identity.

    The museums has to open up, and their traditional hierarchic structure and form of communication has to be replaced by an equivalent flat structure and communication based on dialog.

    Through the use of social media museums can live up to their communication responsibility and obligations in correlation with the ‘Museum Act’ and their individual agreements with the Ministry of Culture.

    Received grade of 12/A+
