Michael Sanford's Publications

  • Click here to comment: the role of intertextuality in blog comments

    • 3rd Annual School of Linguistics and Applied Language Studies Symposium “Navigating currents in linguistic research”
    • April 2007
    Authors: Michael Sanford

    The blog is now a well-established and recognizable web-based tool for publishing information of various types, such as academic papers, news or personal opinion. While, as early as 2004, the weblog had been identified as a genre (Miller & Spehard, 2004; Herring, Scheidt, Bonus & Wright, 2004) research has overwhelmingly focused on the main article, usually paying only cursory attention to the comments. This paper examines the use of intertextuality in blog comments and finds that, overwhelmingly, those who cite choose to do so to give background information for a statement they are making, and that they mostly choose to mention another person or statement or to directly quote to accomplish this goal. Further research is required on a broader range of blog types.

  • Timesheet Web Application

    • July 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Michael Sanford

    Using an existing paper-based timesheet as a base, and in consultation with management to best understand the required business logic, I designed a task-time-and-project-tracking web application using a powerful PHP (MVC-pattern) framework and jQuery + UI for a beautiful user experience.

    The UX design was driven by my desire to give employees of varying technical skill the ability to enter data for a full day in under 90 seconds, and to make the process absolutely intuitive. Similarly, I wanted the generation of comprehensive, multi-axis project reports to be equally intuitive and comparably fast.

    Unit testing routines, custom backup routines, strong (industry-leading) security, URL-parameterized comprehensive multi-axis project reporting, and complete source documentation were integrated from the start.

    The product has officially launched, but is under continual improvement.

  • Integrated Judging Web Application

    • August 2012 to February 2013
    Team Members: Michael Sanford

    Overhaul of an extremely labour-intensive and inefficient process for judging competition entries. I completely redesigned several judging matrices and their associated processes for completion, based chiefly on new standards for classroom scoring rubric design (the whole endeavour is pedagogical in nature) and judge persona development (designed to dramatically improve the user experience for the judges). The new scoring matrix much better describes the reality of the competition, while the new process takes 80% less time to complete. The judging web application is in development and will be released mid-January 2013.

  • Integrated Web Overhaul
