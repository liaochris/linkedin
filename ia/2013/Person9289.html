April Sayler's Summary

All things design - graphics, web, even interior and garden. If I can think it up, I will do it. Working for myself was divine. I was able to support my clients around the clock - work a short day or a [really] long one - whatever was needed. I have however returned to the land of the W-2 employee. I missed working within a team environment, and that's where I'm at today. I still dabble as an independent contract when opportunities arise. Do designer/developers ever give that up?!

My design background stems (like the tie-in?) from the printing industry. Got my foot in thanks to my dad and his 30+ years with Xerox. Learned pre-press and production which is a priceless skill – you'd be surprised how many "designers" don't understand bleeds and impositions.

Started designing websites for fun and liked it, so went back to school and got my AA at the Art Institute of Washington (Arlington, VA) in Multimedia/Web Design. Worked at the Apple Store (Tysons & Clarendon) while I was at school and even after. Life changing experience!

Worked for a couple small businesses - learned some more web development (thanks Seth) and Flash (thanks Ed). Re-enrolled at the Art Institute for Advertising - I started to like the business end of design and thought Advertising would be a fun new outlet. Liked it but didn't love it.

It's a rare commodity, not to toot my own horn but TOOT! – to have a designer truly understand the ins and outs of the print and web world. I do admit that it's limited my free time to excel in one over the other but I'm young and look forward to growing in each industry.

Tech Trend Faves? HTML5. CSS3. Responsive Web Design (RWD). Usability.

Specialties: Corporate Branding, Corporate Identity, Print Design and Production, Website Design, Graphic Design, Illustration, Site Architecture, Web Development, Database Design, Database Maintenance, Technical Support and Training
