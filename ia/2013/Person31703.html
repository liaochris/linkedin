David Dooghe's Summary

David Dooghe works as a researcher, strategist and designer on urban development.

The resulting projects can be temporary or long-term designs and strategies. These projects combine different scales and are characterized by their strong connection with the spatial, cultural, social and economical context of where they occur.

For David Dooghe the city is organic. Questioning the city and understanding its working principles is the path to a sustainable project.

Rotterdamse Academie van Bouwkunst

July 2012 – July 2012 (1 month) Rotterdam

The 2012 summer school is organized together with the Research Centre Sustainable Solutions RDM of the Rotterdam University of Applied Sciences (Prof. Duzan Doepel, Sustainable Architecture and Urban Design) and the province of South-Holland (Zuidvleugel/ South Wing).

A search for attractive, sustainable residential environments

Which living environments are desirable in the South Wing? In comparison with the past few decades, there will be a much greater degree of differentiation in residential areas in the area. Also urban networks will become more important, instead of single cities and villages. Finally densification is crucial, in order to ensure affordable accessibility and to preserve the remaining landscape in the region.

The current urbanization programme of the South Wing focuses on a balance between supply and demand in residential areas, an emphasis on inner-city building, good accessibility and opportunities for outdoor recreation, and a good environment.
During the summer school participants will search for the most promising residential areas in the south wing of the Randstad, where living and quality of life is related to accessibility and of course sustainability – a sustainable home or neighborhood in the wrong place in the region is simply not sustainable.

David Dooghe's Projects

  • The Metropolitan Programme (Het Programma van de Metropool)

    • September 2011 to Present
    Team Members: David Dooghe, Inge Hartkoorn

    'The Metropolitan Programme' is one of the three themes in the work plan 2009/2012 of the Deltametropolis Association (Vereniging Deltametropool). The central question within this theme is what provisions and/or activities Randstad Holland needs in order to compete in global metropolitan urban rankings. With this theme, the Deltametropolis Association aims to emphasise the need for these types of programmes, and the importance of their role within the international context.

  • Water Garden Bridge Amsterdam

    • January 2012 to Present
    Team Members: David Dooghe, Sander van der Poort, Edoardo Costa

    The bridge is the ‘water garden’ of the Hermitage where interaction takes place between the area, the people and the water. It is the place where inhabitants and visitors of Amsterdam come to enjoy the sun, go for a stroll between the green, do sports, sit at the waterside or enjoy various events, such at concerts on the water.
