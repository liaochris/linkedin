Andrea Vidali, MD's Summary

Dr. Vidali had an international upbringing having been raised between Italy and the United States. His academic credentials are stellar: he attended Stuyvesant High School in New York City followed by the Liceo Scientifico Ferm in Padova, Italy. He then completed his medical degree at age 23 at the University of Padova in Italy. After medical school Dr. Vidali entered a postdoctoral fellowship in Molecular Biology at the National Cancer Institute in Italy.
Subsequently he was accepted in the the residency program in Obstetrics and Gynecology at Georgetown University Hospital in Washington, D.C.
After completing his residency at Georgetown Dr. Vidali completed a fellowship in reproductive endocrinology at Columbia University.
Dr. Vidali and Dr. Husami soon joined forces and started a new IVF center called “American Fertility Services”. The center became one of the leading IVF centers in New York City. Dr. Vidali subsequently became the Director of the Division of Reproductive Endocrinology and Infertility at St. Vincent’s Medical Center in New York. At Saint Vincent’s Dr. Vidali developed a division of Advanced Laparoscopic Surgery in collaboration with Dr. Harry Reich.
At that time Dr. Vidali carried out important research both in the surgical and IVF fields. In 1998 Dr. Vidali published one of the earliest reports of egg freezing for fertility. In the same year Dr. Vidali also published one of the earliest papers on laparoscopic myomectomy for fertility patients.
Dr. Vidali’s additional accomplishments include being a founder of the biotechnology company Acceptys, a company focused on the development of a completely new type of treatment for cancer – natural human antibodies. The asset of Acceptys were later transferred to Patrys LTD, and listed on the Australian Securities Exchange (ASX:PAB).
Dr. Vidali recently sold his stake in AFS and , after taking a short sabbatical, he is currently developing new ideas in Integrative Medicine and Reproduction.
