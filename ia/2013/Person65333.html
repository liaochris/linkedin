Joe Halper's Summary

Joe Halper is a 15-year veteran of the commercial and serious games industry. In the 1990s he started as a 3D artist and designer working on military and civilian training applications. His passion for games led Joe and a team of equally enthusiastic hobbyists to create the award-winning modification Desert Combat. In 2002 he co-founded Trauma Studios in New York City and led a team of artists, designers and programmers to successfully research and develop gameplay features for the PC game Battlefield 2, as well as develop original IP that was greenlit by EA. Trauma Studios was successfully acquired by DICE in 2004. Then in 2005 Joe was one of the founding members of Kaos Studios in Manhattan, New York. There he served as Senior Producer for its first title Frontlines: Fuel of War for Xbox 360 and PC.

In late 2008 Joe took the role of Director of Production for the Virtual Heroes Division of Applied Research Associates, which is a serious games developer as well as an international research and engineering company located in Raleigh, NC. He directed and produced multiple award-winning serious games projects (both classified and unclassified) with clients in the military, commercial, government, medical and education markets. Clients included NASA, DARPA, most branches of the US military, Department of Homeland Security, Warner Brothers, Kaiser Permanente, Johnson & Johnson, as well as many hospitals, colleges, and educational institutions.

In 2012 Joe returned to focus full-time on entertainment titles by joining 2Dawn Games to successfully develop and release 2Dawn's first PC multiplayer title Ravaged, delivered in October 2012. He has also established a new studio called Grit Games, LLC in Raleigh, NC, that is focusing on unannounced entertainment titles for both the PC and mobile environments.

Specialties: Game Development, Creative Direction, Art Direction, Design, Marketing, PR, Production, 3D Modeling and Animation, Video Post Production, Serious Games
