Ken Kaplan's Summary

Multimedia journalist who blends broadcast media expertise with new, social media skills to increase awareness of technology and innovation at Intel, ranked 51 on the Fortune list of largest American companies. More than 20 years of corporate communications experience.

Founding member of Intel Free Press, the tech news bureau inside Intel that began publishing online in October 2010.

Joined Intel in 2000 as broadcast media relations manager. Ideas, writing, media production and teamwork help Intel's Global Communications Group better participate directly with top broadcast outlets, tech bloggers and online influencers.

Began leading efforts in 2005 to evolve Intel into a better media company by engaging the chipmaker in social media and establishing trusted relationships with online media producers and bloggers. Helped found the Intel Advisers program in 2007, which brought together social media pioneers and went on to win numerous PR and communications industry awards for “Best Influencer Program.”

Prior to Intel, was the first publicist at KRON-TV, then an NBC-TV affiliate in San Francisco, a top five U.S. TV market.

Specialties: Storytelling through writing, video production and photography. Social computing, blogging, online and broadcast video production and distribution, measurement, and community building. Media relations strategy and technical broadcast experience. Event sponsorship and product integration.
