Michael Astrachan's Summary

My passions and achievements were forged in the Bunsen burner flames of my high school’s science curriculum. From chemistry labs to Biology assignments I was always illustrating complex scientific processes through my drawings and animations for final projects and extra credit.

Today, I continue this mission. I am Creative Director, President and Owner of XVIVO | Scientific animation, a global leader in the medical and scientific animation field. Through my dedication to scientific accuracy and high artistic quality my work has been featured in the New York Times, showcased on NPR.org, displayed in science museums internationally, gone viral on youtube, and is used by speakers at TED and TEDMED. It my commitment to inspiring future scientists that leads to productions worthy of notice that genuinely engages audiences.

One of my proudest moments was when I read Erik Olsen's article on the cover of the New York Times, Science Times. He wrote, “Perhaps the pivotal moment for molecular animations came four years ago with a video called “The Inner Life of the Cell.” Produced by Bio Visions, a scientific visualization program at Harvard University’s Department of Molecular and Cellular Biology, and a Connecticut-based scientific animation company called XVIVO.”

I have been involved in many medical educational projects, including developing work for museums, broadcasters, and universities. Among my clients are Nobel Prize winner Kerry Mullis, Columbia University theoretical physicist Brian Green, and Harvard Professor of the Practice of Molecular and Cellular Biology, Rob Lue, who commissioned XVIVO to develop the ground-breaking “Inner Life of the Cell” for Biovisions. I am currently working on “Protein Packing,” which is the third installment of the “Inner Life of a Cell” series.
