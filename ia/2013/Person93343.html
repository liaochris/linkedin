Michele Simon's Summary

I am a public health lawyer specializing in legal and policy strategies to counter marketing tactics and political factors that threaten public health. With 17 years of experience in food politics I also spent 4.5 years promoting policies that prevent alcohol harm. My work has centered around understanding how marketing interferes with healthful living and exploits children. My research demonstrates why voluntary industry self-regulation is a failure that should (and can) be replaced with enforceable regulations. I also provide advocates practical resources and technical assistance to counter lobbying tactics that undermine health policy efforts.

My professional goals include moving the public health field away from the educational model into an era where business practices such as marketing and lobbying are recognized as a legitimate area of research and advocacy, and helping to level the playing field to make healthful living the norm.

My first book, Appetite for Profit: How the Food Industry Undermines Our Health and How to Fight Back was called "brilliant" by Marion Nestle, who uses it to teach her nutrition class at New York University.

Specialties: Marketing harmful products including by the food, alcohol, and tobacco industries. Lobbying tactics. The failure of industry self-regulation. Legal and policy strategies to improve public health, including economic incentives, and reducing political influence to undermine public policy. Technical assistance to help advocates understand legal concepts such as commercial speech and advertising, taxation regulation, limiting outlet density, and other policy solutions to promote public health.

I have extensive experience putting research into action through powerful report writing. For example, her groundbreaking 2007 report on alcoholic energy drinks led to federal action to ban the dangerous products. I am also an accomplished public speaker.
