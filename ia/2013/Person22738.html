Chester Elton's Summary

Called the "apostle of appreciation," by the Globe and Mail, Canada's largest newspaper, and "creative and refreshing" by the New York Times, Chester Elton is co-author of several successful leadership books and is an in-demand speaker the world over. In 2013 Chester was named one of the top 30 business gurus in America.

Elton’s books have been translated into over 20 languages and have sold over a million copies worldwide. The Carrot Principle by Simon & Schuster has been a New York Times and Wall Street Journal bestseller, and 24-Carrot Manager has been called a “must read for modern-day managers” by Larry King of CNN. In 2006,

As a motivation expert, Chester has been featured in The Wall Street Journal, Washington Post, Fast Company magazine and the New York Times. He has also been a guest on CNN, NBC's Today Show, ABC News MSNBC and on National Public Radio. He has a regular segment on WCBS Radio in NYC and is a LinkedIn "Influencer." A sought-after speaker and consultant, Chester is partners with his long time friend and co-author Adrian Gostick in founding The Culture Works a training consulting company dedicated to creating productive and rewarding cultures at work.

Chester has spoken to delighted audiences from Seattle to Singapore and from Toronto to Turkey. In 2005 he was the highest rated speaker at the national Society for Human Resource  Management annual conference (Bill Cosby was the number-two rated speaker). He serves as a culture consultant to Fortune 100 firms such as American Exptress, AT&T, Pepsi, Wal-Mart and Avis Budget Group.

However, the thing of which he is most proud is that he is the father of four EXCEPTIONAL children!

Specialties: Making work more rewarding through creating a "ALL IN" culture based on recognition, appreciation and engagement. Because when people feel valued and recognized it accelerates business results and creates lasting relationship with clients and employees.
