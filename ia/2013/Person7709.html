Angela Johnson Meadows' Publications

  • Does Race Still Matter?: Moving Toward a New Global Conversation on Race and Colorism (Editor)

    • Diversity Best Practices
    • July 2013
    Authors: Angela Johnson Meadows, Andres Tapia, Katherine Phillips

    The election of the first Black president of the United States has led many to believe that we are now living in a post-racial society. However, at Diversity Best Practices, we strongly believe that race does still matter, though we also believe we have to develop a new and contemporary way to address race. We believe race still matters because wherever we look—educational attainment, health outcomes, financial stability, leadership representation—we find troubling statistics, some as bad as during Dr. Martin Luther King’s time. Yet, at the same time, many things have changed for the better. We need to remain engaged in these complex realities.

    Not only does race still matter in America, the movement towards a global conversation on diversity warrants the acknowledgement that race still matters around the world. To move diversity and inclusion forward, these new times require the ability to engage in a conversation about race and colorism in a contemporary way that takes into account these paradoxical realities. In this report, the authors demonstrate how, while challenging your conventional thinking about race and offering some principles for a new conversation on the topic.

  • Report from São Paulo: Diversity Possibilities, Contrasts, and Contradictions in Booming Brazil

    • Diversity Best Practices
    • February 7, 2013
    Authors: Angela Johnson Meadows

    In November 2012, Diversity Best Practices and its parent company, Working Mother Media, traveled to São Paulo to host a series of events addressing diversity and inclusion and the advancement of women in Brazil. This paper provides a discussion of the key themes that emerged from the week’s sessions.
