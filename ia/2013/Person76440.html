Michael Knox's Summary

Mr. Knox is a forensic science and criminology consultant and former police detective from Jacksonville, Florida, who is a recognized expert in firearms, ballistics, crime scene reconstruction, death investigations, bloodstain pattern analysis, and traffic accident reconstruction.

He became a police officer with the Jacksonville Sheriff's Office in 1994 and served in patrol and DUI enforcement capacities before transferring to the Crime Scene Unit in 2000. While in the unit, Mr. Knox was training coordinator and served as a major case investigator having been responsible for over 350 forensic death scene investigations. In 2007, he transferred to the Traffic Homicide Unit where he investigated over 100 fatal and life-threatening traffic crashes and performed follow-up investigations on serious hit-and-run cases.

In 2010, Mr. Knox took an early retirement from the Jacksonville Sheriff's Office to dedicate his full-time efforts to his forensic consulting business, Knox & Associates, LLC. Through his company, Mr. Knox has provided forensic consulting services in criminal and civil cases throughout the United States for both plaintiff/prosecution and defense. He has testified as an expert in state and federal courts in Florida, Alabama, Mississippi, Texas, and Illinois.

Mr. Knox is an active educator in the field of forensic science. He has taught courses on crime scene investigations and reconstruction in Florida, Georgia, Missouri, Connecticut, Peru, the United Arab Emirates, and the Republic of Georgia.

Mr. Knox earned a Bachelor of Science degree in mechanical engineering from the University of North Florida and a Master of Science degree in forensic science from the University of Florida. He holds current certification as a crime scene reconstructionist through the International Association for Identification and accreditation as a traffic accident reconstructionist through the Accreditation Commission for Traffic Accident Reconstruction.

    • American Society of Mechanical Engineers
    • November 2012
    Authors: Michael Knox
