Jeff Vetterick's Experience

President, Founder

Oh! Space, LLC

January 2010 – March 2013 (3 years 3 months) Oklahoma City, Oklahoma Area

Oh! Space, which I co-founded, started as a hobby and has become a highly unique and innovative real estate repurposing, event-centric brokerage service. It’s founding created a whole new category in its local market.

Oh! Space finds unique and interesting commercial buildings and functional spaces that are unoccupied, for sale, for lease, or otherwise underutilized in the Oklahoma City metropolitan market. The owners of these spaces are approached by Oh! Space to secure the property for event-based rental to private parties, wedding receptions, or corporate events. Building owners win by generating short-term supplemental revenue without having to take the property off of the market. Meanwhile, Oh Space markets its portfolio of unique party spaces to the general public through its website, word of mouth, and other marketing endeavors.

Oh! Space books the event, arranges for party services, clean up, event insurance, and other value-added event services through its vast network of third party providers. Event clients pay Oh! Space, and Oh! Space shares this revenue with property owners under contracted revenue sharing agreements.

Upon deciding to personally relocate to southern California, I gifted my interest in this business to my former partner, and it continues to thrive under her leadership.

EVP / Marketing

Myriad Systems Inc (MSI)

September 2007 – September 2009 (2 years 1 month) Oklahoma City, Oklahoma Area

MSI provides print & mail and electronic account information delivery services as an outsourced service to financial institutions, utilities, and governments, as well as providing a variety of cloud-based (SAAS) applications for bank and corporate financial backroom operations.

When I joined MSI, it was a relatively small, 20-year old local provider of printing and mailing services run by a highly inventive father and son team. It had a number of prominent local banks as customers, but had yet exceeded $6M in revenues. In recent years, MSI had introduced a number of visionary cloud-based software solutions to the company’s product mix to diversify, and to capitalize on the industry’s movement from hardcopy toward electronic delivery of e-services.

MSI had excellent technical and operational know-how, and the ability to execute. They wanted to grow, but were having trouble expanding the business beyond their local geography. They asked me to join them to build a powerful marketing face around their established core value proposition.

I can say that I completely re-vamped this company’s image and marketing from scratch. The fresh new look of the company, the improvement in relations with its clients, and it’s winning of new customers around the country grew its revenues by 50%, to over $9M, in my two years there, and brought MSI to the attention of a much larger national provider as an acquisition candidate. MSI is now part of CSI.

General Manager

Endpoint Exchange

March 2005 – September 2007 (2 years 7 months) Oklahoma City, Oklahoma Area

Endpoint Exchange was the first telecommunications network established for the exchange of electronic check images between U.S. financial institutions. It essentially allows banks to electron-ify any paper check it receives in deposit and treat it as if it was an electronic transaction, increasing security while expediting availability of deposited funds to consumers and business alike.

I helped develop the very concept of the network along with some fellow executives at my previous employer, AFS. I was responsible for all marketing of the endeavor, making it a household name within the banking industry well before its eagerly anticipated launch. After it was launched and spun off to a separate company, I was appointed General Manager, and the network grew under my leadership from a relative handful of customers to be responsible for the daily exchange of $Billions in assets between over 5000 of the nation’s financial institutions.

VP Marketing

Advanced Financial Solutions

Privately Held; 201-500 employees; Computer Software industry

December 1999 – June 2007 (7 years 7 months) Oklahoma City, Oklahoma Area

Advanced Financial Solutions (AFS) developed, sold, installed, and supported image-based check processing solutions to the financial industry, including banks, thrifts, credit unions, and check processing service bureaus internationally.

When I joined AFS, it was an 8-year-old high-tech startup with about $20M in annual revenues. It had developed a market-disrupting, database-driven software product rich in functionality, with a very slick user interface, and was among the first to introduce document imaging to re-engineer a bank’s back room workflow. But it had admittedly under-invested in marketing, and its corporate look and presentation was no where near the caliber of its true value proposition. Its principals desired a major coordinated overhaul.

I was recruited to run all aspects of the company’s marketing mix including corporate and product brand strategy, brand management, product releases, advertising, events and trade shows, sales collateral, promotional web site development and maintenance, social media, facilities aesthetics, client visits, and strategic partner and user relations.

Executing with passion and precision, along with my fellow executives, we grew this business to over $100M in revenues and the undisputed market leader in the Community Bank market space, attracting as a suitor and ultimately executing its sale to Metavante Corporation in 2005.

President

Panini North America

May 1998 – December 1999 (1 year 8 months) Dayton, Ohio Area; and Turin, Italy

Panini SpA, based in Italy, manufactured tabletop sorting machines for check and coupon-sized documents, which it successfully sold in Europe for the scanning, imaging, sorting, and processing of giros and other specialized financial documents popular throughout Europe. Panini North America was the Americas distributorship responsible for dealer and direct sales, installation, and service of the Panini product line throughout North and South America.

Panini SpA wanted to increase its market share in the Americas. They had seen how I had helped an Israeli technology company quickly gain market domination in the U.S. and hired me to get those kind of results for their products. I led a team of 8 in finance, marketing, sales, installation, support, and maintenance of machines in the U.S. and Latin America.

Panini had a couple of hundred machines deployed in the US, relative to it's sizable European market share, and one of Panini's challenges was a perception that product quality was not up to par. This was due to a difference in maintenance protocol expectations between European and U.S. users, but I discovered a number of hidden causes that, if corrected, would dramatically improve US salability. I implemented those initiatives, which included an overhaul of all user and maintenance documentation, quality control testing in both Italy and the U.S., and identified a dozen or so Engineering changes that improved the product globally. Our U.S. resellers were ecstatic, and concerns about product returns gave way to a happier reseller network.

But, I knew the existing product sat in a performance chasm between the two directions the industry was taking, and so I launched a market research endeavor to identify the requirements for an entirely new product line that would knock the socks off of the U.S. Market. That program's legacy is the Panini VisionX, now 400,000 units installed and among the most popular check scanning devices worldwide.

VP Sales & Marketing

Orbograph LLC

December 1995 – May 1998 (2 years 6 months) Greater Boston Area; and Tel Aviv, Israel

Orbograph provides industrial-strength image character recognition (ICR) software solutions to the banking and healthcare industries for automatically reading and recognizing handwritten and machine printed information from checks and other high-value documents. Orbograph’s solutions eliminate the need for human operators in high volume paper scanning environments to manually key data.

When I was recruited to launch its U.S. sales and marketing operations, Orbograph's Engineering team had built a very powerful firmware-based engine that could handle the intensive processing required with incredible accuracy. The technology was a quantum leap beyond all prevailing attempts at ICR. But they had yet no customers and had been struggling to launch their product in the U.S. from their Israel-based headquarters.

One of my first initiatives was to provide the Engineering team with a refined set of market requirements toward porting the proprietary hardware-based approach to software, so as to capitalize on the then-emerging trend of open, off-the-shelf PC server architecture.

Then, I set to work selling one after another the US check processing solution vendors on the technology, combining highly persuasive technical and ROI arguments, sales presentations, and boosting our presence at trade events. I built the company a highly educational public website on the disruptive technology, and organized seminars to educate U.S. software and sales engineers industry-wide on the opportunity and integration. I orchestrated creative attention-getting strategies at trade shows we attended, creating buzz. I was vigorously deploying both push and pull marketing strategies.

Within 18 months, I had signed the vast majority of 3rd party solution providers in the check imaging space as customers, and was coordinating all integration support between customers and the Israeli team. Orders began pouring in for licenses and sales climbed to over $1MM in the second year.

Director, Payment Solutions

Recognition Equipment Incorporated

1987 – 1995 (8 years) Irving, TX and Charlotte, NC

REI provided state of the art, high speed, turn-key solutions for scanning mountains of paper in corporate and government back room operations so as to automate data capture from everything from keyboards, to checks, to pages, to mail for the US postal service; and REI was a pioneer in software-driven workflow within corporate business environments. REI was acquired by Banctec Inc in 1995.

My 8-year span at REI was marked by a fast track rise of successive promotions from entry level system installer, to customer services, to information technology, to product management for a series of legacy hardware products and new hardware and software product launches, followed by Directorship of one of the 3 major market segments the company addressed, reporting the Corporate VP of Marketing.
