Scott Brovsky's Summary

Scott has over nine years of marketing experience in the video game industry with a successful track record defining, launching and managing major franchises such as The Avengers, Tron, Turok, Kingdom Hearts and Medal of Honor for leading entertainment companies like Disney/Marvel, Electronic Arts and Take-Two Interactive.

His current interests focus on the Quantified Self movement and on how we can use wearable tech/activity trackers, mobile fitness apps and game mechanics to make outdoor activities and fitness more engaging and more fun for people while at the same time helping them to be more active and lead healthier lives.

Most recently, Scott was part of the global leadership team at Disney that worked for the last three years to build The Avengers franchise with his focus being the interactive games space. He led the successful global launch of Avengers Alliance on Facebook and Avengers Initiative on mobile and with 35 million plus people having played the games, they have been a hit with the fans and critics alike. Scott also ended up pulling off a big idea and an industry first, premiering The Hulk TV spot promoting the film in the Facebook game before it aired on national television.

Seeing the early promise of the first iPhone, Scott left Disney in early 2008 to identify talented indie developers and support their games as an angel investor. He co-funded the iPhone puzzle game HydroTilt with publisher partner Publisher X and the developer Cogeglue which launched globally in December 2008. The game reviewed well, garnered support from Apple and spent 3 weeks in the Top 50 games on the App Store.

Scott spends as much of his free time as possible outside biking, hiking and exploring the mountains and deserts of the Western US including racing the Nautica Malibu Triathlon with Team Disney in September 2012 which raised money for the Children's Hospital of LA.

LIT’s sensor family analyzes movements 24×7, scoring both activity and action (jumps, rotations, airtime, max-g, etc.). Points become the currency used to measure personal growth and compare action-inspired lifestyles with friends.
