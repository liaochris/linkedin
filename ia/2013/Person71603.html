Jan Willem Wennekes' Summary

I am a goal-oriented project manager with a strong curiosity. My tendency to look at a subject matter from different angles makes that I can see analogies where others do not see them yet. I like to see matters as part of a bigger whole. I love to bring people together in order to achieve a common goal. I aim to think big and, when needed, (re)evaluate existing patterns and structures. I am interested in understanding how changes affect people, organizations and society as a whole. I have a strong fondness for matters sustainable, eco-friendly and green.

I am working as creative entrepreneur and project manager and am always eager to apply my broad base of experience to challenges in new fields!

Maker Faire Groningen

Educational Institution; 1001-5000 employees; Research industry

February 2012 – Present (1 year 9 months) Groningen, Netherlands

Groningen Mini Maker Faire is a DIY festival for resourcefulness. A fun and active festival where kids and grown-ups alike are being invited to use their creativity, to be curious and to seek the adventure. The festival consists of numerous workshops, exhibitions, talks and a genuine scrapheap challenge. In oktober 2012 over 3000 visitors from all over the Netherlands attended this family-friendly festival!

Partner - Business Director

Rabonk!

2008 – Present (5 years)

Machine builders collective Rabonk! is a collective in which design, theatre and construction come together. The collective operates in the world of performing arts and cultural festivals. Its members work together to build crafty and mysterious installations, that often employ mechanical principles. Their inner workings are revealed when visitors start to interact with the installation, and surrender themselves to it. It is this ancient bond between man and machine that Rabonk aims to investigate and use in a playful and sustainable way.

Jan Willem Wennekes' Publications

  • Vormator

    • BIS Publishers
    • September 1, 2008
    Authors: Jan Willem Wennekes, Wimer Hazenberg ⚡, Menno Huisman

    The Vormator book showcases the best submissions from a graphic design contest. The contest challenged artists to create a visual by using a limited palette of shapes and design rules. The goal of the project was to show the importance of limitations on creativity. The results of the contest prove that even with a large number of limitations, a surprising variation of outstanding graphics is possible.

  • Black & White Freedrawings

    • Zeptonn
    • 2008
    Authors: Jan Willem Wennekes

    Black & White Freedrawings is a unique exploration of collaborative drawing. In the book, over 40 international artists collaborate on an illustration with Zeptonn. These collaborations are called "freedrawings" to highlight the lack of restrictions, themes and guidelines. At the same time these drawings are not a preliminary sketch, but the final artwork. In 132 pages filled with freedrawing collaborations the artists take you on a journey through the process of collaboration, showcasing their personal illustrations and working spaces.

  • Stingermania

    • Zeptonn
    • 2006
    Authors: Jan Willem Wennekes

    A collection of work by illustrator Zeptonn, ranging from t-shirts to posters, logos, button badges and books. The work showcases a profound love for colours and is recognized for its simplicity.
