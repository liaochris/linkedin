James McIntosh's Summary

Not sure what a coach is for? Neither was I until I wrote this piece: http://blog.nonsenseatwork.com/archives/3077

Still not sure? Ask me. I think I know: james@nonsenseatwork.com

Coaching Results:
1. Achieved within 18 months successful turnaround from bankruptcy of a retail clothing chain of 185 stores in 5 countries (as consultant and confidant to the CEO; and directly responsible for strategic planning and executive team development.
2. Transformed an outdated paper packaging business into a diversified industrial company with interests in information technology, financial services, and paper packaging (initially as interim managing director and then as executive director with responsibility for strategic planning and the integration of acquisitions.)
3. Directly involved in 10 acquisitions, two start-ups and the sale of a major business unit for a profit in excess of market expectation.

Strategy Consulting:
1. Developed a perceptual analysis tool and a planning process which links the implementation of focused action plans directly to strategy - used since 1990.
2. Developed a formal strategic planning framework for a retail company with a chain of furniture stores and a chain of fashion sports goods and clothing. The same framework, with minor revisions, was still in use a decade later.

Specialties: Executive business coaching; strategy development and execution; off-site facilitation; public speaking.

James McIntosh's Experience

Executive Business Coach

JamesHMcIntosh

January 2010 – Present (3 years 9 months)

James has a uniquely simple approach to solving business problems—increasing the effectiveness of individual executives and management teams. His proven methodology—after more than 25 years of international executive experience—seems like Business 101. But in today’s fast-paced board rooms, many lose sight of a key principle: even executives can have fear and history impede progress. As an executive coach, James works directly with individual executives or teams against measurable business objectives to deliver results and transformative change.

James, a former CEO of two businesses, has dramatically increased the effectiveness of CEOs, leaders and teams in different business environments, including the public sector and non-profits. And, while he focuses on the effectiveness of individual managers or teams, his strategic planning and business acumen enable him to leverage a career that has encompassed diversification strategies, mergers and acquisitions, start-ups, the sale of business units and corporate turnarounds from bankruptcy.

Chief Nonsense Officer

NonsenseAtWork (Sole Proprietorship)

Sole Proprietorship; Myself Only; Management Consulting industry

January 2005 – Present (8 years 9 months) Richmond, Virginia Area

James was born in the middle of South African wine country, earned three degrees drinking red wine, was chased by a lioness, had to run from elephants, got bored, and moved to the USA seeking adventure.

As an executive business coach, he has seen a lot of nonsense at work. That’s why he now shows senior executives how to make the nonsense at work – work for them. When this gets the better of him, he retreats to writing and public speaking until his confidence returns.

An avid writer and proponent of change, James has two books and a regular blog that highlights his general philosophy—that the political nature of business and personal consternation can be overcome by crossing what he calls “The Nonsense Divide.” This philosophy has an avid following, including listeners of regional public radio that hear his weekly spots called “Nonsense At Work.”

His blog, books, and overall philosophy of nonsense can be found at www.nonsenseatwork.com
