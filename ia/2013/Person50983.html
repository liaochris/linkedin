Gustaf Brandberg's Summary

Gustaf Brandberg is a Seed Investor and Business Angel bridging the gap between family, friends and fools and institutional investors by providing capital, competence and contacts to startups in exchange for equity.

Gustaf has a background in programming and agile development and was CEO for Citerus when the company brought Scrum to Sweden in 2003. He has worked 5 years at Tieto as Business Advisor, Team Manager and Sales Manager, helping some of Sweden's largest companies and organisations with web migration projects, social media strategies and digital service design.

Starbreeze

Public Company; 51-200 employees; N/A; Computer Games industry

November 2011 – November 2013 (2 years 1 month) Stockholm, Sweden

Founded in 1998. Starbreeze Studios is an independent game developer of computer games such as The Chronicles of Riddick, The Darkness and Syndicate.

Served on the board during a two year turnover when the company moved from a risky and unprofitable work-for-hire business model with a market cap of ~$18 M to a highly profitable company with ownership of the intellectual property of the games they develop and a market cap of ~$134 M.

Figures from 21 November 2013 when I left the board (compared to 15 November 2011 when I joined it):
- Market cap: 871 MSEK (120 MSEK)
- EBITDA July-September 2013: 81,4 MSEK (-1,5 MSEK for July-September 2011)
- Games with intellectual property ownership: 3 (0)
- Player base to launch new games to: >1.58 M players (0)
- Co-ops with legendary game developer Valve Corporation: 1 (not in a million years)

The turnover included:
- raising 22 MSEK in an issue of shares,
- acquiring independent game developer Overkill (including the franchise "Payday:The Heist" and star game developers Bo and Ulf Andersson and their great team),
- recruiting top talent such as Battlefield 3 Lead Designer David Goldfarb,
- reducing the workforce from 90 people to around 50 and relocating the studio to Stockholm,
- developing and launching two new games ("Brothers - a tale of two sons" which received a metacritic of stellar 90/100 on PC, plus PAYDAY 2 which was the #1 top seller on the world's biggest PC game distribution platform Steam for 16 days).

Shortly after I joined the board the company launched its last work-for-hire-project Syndicate which got a lukewarm market response ("the best game no-one bought in 2012" as one of the critics put it) and the market cap declined to 37 MSEK. A bit more than a year later, shortly after the launch of the success PAYDAY 2, the market cap peaked at 1120 MSEK.
