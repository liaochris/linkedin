Lisa Nichols' Experience

Business Development and Operational Design

Lisa Nichols - Nichols Consulting

November 2004 – Present (9 years) San Francisco Bay Area

I have 25 years of experience working both as a management level executive and entrepreneur, in the areas of business development, operational design, and communications. I'm a skilled senior level project manager and negotiator. Several of my clients have worked with me consistently since 2006 as I've helped them design and grow their businesses.

Project Samples:

Sustainability Assessment and Strategic Planning.
Alameda County hired me to do a sustainability study for their largest non-profit family resource center. I brought in a financial expert for that side of the evaluation and performed the operational audits myself, relying on surveys and interviews with employees, other agencies, clients, Board members, and conducted on-site research to assess the strengths and weaknesses of the agency. My report was very well received and the agency subsequently hired me directly to assist with their 5-year strategic plan, document all operations, and make recommendations for infrastructure changes.

Business Launches.
1) I work with a Bay Area company to make a clinical protocol accessible to medical/therapy professionals. My responsibilities include vetting medical design firms and manufacturers, acting as project manager for taking their medical device through FDA approval processes, and market development.
2) I was hired by a company that produced digital widgets to completely overhaul their business plan in order to launch a second round of funding. I significantly revised the structure and focus of their initial business plan and helped them define/refine functional areas of the business.

Operational Documentation / Procedures.
A large publicly held semi-conductor firm hired me to revise their POS documentation to reflect a newly automated system they had just developed. My audience was a group distributors all over the world - and my task was to take a highly technical process and describe it step-by-step to a non-tech audience.
