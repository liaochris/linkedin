Electrical Engineer

Lockheed Martin Space Systems Company

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; LMT; Defense & Space industry

May 1986 – September 1986 (5 months)

Hungry for something "bleeding edge", I worked on a top secret government project to develop a laser satellite defense system. It consisted of a curtain of X-ray lasers. The curtain was to be deployed either using a series of missiles launched from submarines or via satellites, during the critical seconds following an attack on the US. The satellites would be powered by built-in nuclear warheads – in theory, the energy from the warhead detonation would be used to pump a series of laser emitters in the missiles or satellites, allowing each satellite to shoot down many incoming warheads simultaneously. The attraction of this approach was that it was thought to be faster than an optical laser, which could only shoot down warheads one at a time, limiting the number of warheads each laser could destroy in the short time 'window' of an attack. Also worked on optical laser approach with large satellite-based mirror used to reflect ground-based laser system to shoot down warheads.
