GREG WILLETT BIO:

Greg Willett is a native of Washington DC. Having attended both Georgetown University and the University of Maryland, he has a degree in Marine Biology. After a brief stint of employment with the State of Maryland working on the Chesapeake Bay, he left to pursue a long time dream of; “ professional treasure hunter”.
He has been a full time antiques dealer for 35 years, auctioneer for 25 years, appraiser of antiques and personal property for 20 years and estate sale manager for 17 years. Greg’s first shop was opened in rural Maryland in 1973. Shortly, thereafter he opened shops in both Western Maine and Boulder Colorado. In the early 1980’s, Greg moved to Southern coastal Maine, where he operated Antiques on Nine in Kennebunkport. That shop still exists today.
In 1988, Greg was profiled in the New York Times Sunday magazine as one of four rising stars in the antiques business.
A specialist in North American furniture and accessories, Greg has also, as a direct importer, bought extensively in Mexico, Canada, France, Spain and China, actually going village to village, and house to house to acquire fresh stock.
In 1993 he moved to Neenah to marry a local girl and continue his career in the antiques field. Greg is currently one of the preeminent antique dealers in the Fox Valley, active in the purchase of individual antique items as well as the purchase and dispersal of personal property estates items.
Greg feels strongly about educating the general public with regards to personal property procedures, options, and inherent potential pitfalls. In conjunction with this goal, Greg gives talks to interested groups. One discussion is ” Personal Property Strategies, the Do’s and Don’ts of Estate Liquidation”, the other is the “Greg Willett Antique Show and Tell.”
