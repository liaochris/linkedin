  • DevOps: Solving the engineering productivity challenge

    • PwC Technology Forecast
    • July 23, 2013
    Authors: Alan Morrison, Bo Parker, Bud Mathaisel, Bob Scheier

    Business processes have to be adaptive enough to deliver solutions for today’s problems and opportunities. Today, large enterprises develop ideas that would result in better processes, but they are facing yesterday's legacy IT systems and project management processes that take too long to deliver infrastructure and application changes needed to support process changes. A major culprit is how IT manages change and the risks that system changes create for stable operations. IT reaches for stable operations by minimizing the frequency of code changes.

    Contrast that approach with how things are done now at web-based companies, even very large ones such as Facebook, Google, and Netflix. These companies strive for continuity of operations by embracing change. Some introduce new code to their production systems many times a day. The outcomes for web-scale companies are systems that are much better aligned to what the business needs today and more responsive to what the business needs tomorrow.

    Large enterprises are beginning to learn from what's happening in these web-based companies and trying similar things internally themselves. What they're discovering is that a different "antifragile" definition of stability is emerging that builds on existing agile development principles. The notion of a highly adaptive model applies not only to IT specifically, but the business as a whole.
