Michael Ferrari's Summary

Primary areas of expertise: applied and theoretical climate/biophysical research, biometeorology, environmental/commodity risk, informatics/data science, and stochastic modeling. Related topics include commercialization of scientific research & technology transfer.

Commercial research applications: sensor networks, climate, weather & environmental risk, energy & agricultural commodity markets, derivatives, natural resource economics, environmental finance, sensor technology engineering/design/R&D, industrial ecology, cleantech.

Basic Research: Stochastic processes, Environmental Systems Science & Engineering, Metagenomics, Biometeorology, Complex Adaptive Systems, Atmospheric Science, Network Theory, Climate/Fluid Dynamics, Biogeochemistry, Data Fusion, Ecosystem/Evolutionary Dynamics, Disease Modeling, Remote Sensing & Downscaling, Climate & Society, Physical Resource Theory & Modeling, Synthetic Environments, Artificial Life.

I serve on the Board of Societal Impacts of Weather & Climate for the American Meteorological Society, and I am a judge for the MIT Climate CoLab (agriculture) project.

I build tools and models with sensors & data to examine the above topics from a systems perspective.
