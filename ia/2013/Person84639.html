Mikel Manitius' Summary

Mikel Manitius is an innovative Enterprise Architect and technology evangelist. For nearly 25 years Mikel has specialized in UNIX-based network computing. From the early days of ARPAnet, and now the Internet, Mikel has been exploring and leveraging the growing power of connectivity. He has administered large networks at BellCoRe, ran a USENET backbone site and lead development of a distributed MIS system at AT&T, implemented wide-area communication protocols and written EDA interfaces for a reservations system at AAA, designed an Internet Appliance, and developed solution architectures with Enterprise Wireless Systems Integrators.

Currently at Oracle, Mikel is a SPARC SuperCluster specialist in the Engineered Systems group.

Previously at Sun Microsystems, Mikel has worked as a Lead Architect on a global outsourcing account where he collaborated with the client to define technology standards and best practices, reduce total cost of ownership through server and desktop virtualization and data center rationalization, develop new solutions and pursue new business opportunities.

Mikel has strong problem solving skills and international experience. He conceptualizes and absorbs information quickly, simplifies complexity, and is highly articulate. He is independent, inquisitive and creative with excellent ability in strategy development and realizing new opportunities. He enjoys a solid reputation for challenging existing concepts and pushing boundaries to innovate. He is often most valued for the way he thinks.

Fluent in English, French and Polish. Learning Dutch and Spanish.

Specialties

Data Communications, IP networking, Solaris and Linux system administration & internals, client/server architectures, horizontal and N-tier architectures, desktop virtualization, thin clients, server virtualization, datacenter consolidation, clustering, network security, open systems.
