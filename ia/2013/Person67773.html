Jon Brack's Summary

Energetic, passionate and adaptable photographer with diverse international experiences in unpredictable environments.

Specializes in photographing international organizations, foreign cultures and isolated locations, all with the intent to humanize the foreign.

Possesses balanced interpersonal skills, a consistent hard-working ethic and comfort functioning alone or in a team.

Jon Brack's Experience

Freelance Photographer/Photojournalist

Jon Brack Photography

2006 – Present (7 years)

Kennedy Space Center, Florida – December 2011 to present

• Documenting the decommissioning of NASA’s Space Shuttle program utilizing high-definition gigapixel panoramas and individual still images
• Writing blog posts to accompany photography on National Geographic’s website
• Image chosen for “Mission 26: The Big Endeavour” exhibit at the California Science Center
• Flight Deck panorama included in the introductory movie to the Space Shuttle Atlantis exhibit at the Kennedy Space Center Visitor Complex
• Panoramas and still pictures used by the Washington Post, National Geographic News, NASA HQ, NASA Dryden, California Science Center, Huffington Post and Gigapan


Recent highlights of work with International Non-Profit Organizations – June 2006 to present

> Haiti – February and November 2013
• Traveled on two occasions with AKV through the north of Haiti photographing their school gardens, environmental education, livestock and reforestation programs
• Continued working with SOIL in Port-au-Prince and Cap Haitian and their composting toilets and agricultural development projects
• Also worked with Project HOPE documenting their health care development projects in Port-au-Prince and then in Santo Domingo and Monte Plata, Dominican Republic

> Indonesia – July 2011
• Produced ten blog posts with images for Project HOPE while embedded with Operation Pacific Angel, a two-week US Air Force medical humanitarian mission
• Images also used in promotional materials and Project HOPE’s annual report

> South Sudan – April 2011
• Month long self-assignment documenting Southern Sudan’s progress towards independence and anticipation for democracy
• Spent one week documenting the Duk Lost Boys Clinic, built and operated by the John Dau Foundation, and provided images for their use online and in promotional materials
• Coordinated additional photographic access with the UN, Maltezar International, PSI, the Carter Center and CEFA
