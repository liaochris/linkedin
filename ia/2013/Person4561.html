Mike Alifano's Summary

Mike was born and raised in Half Moon Bay, CA. He attended the local public schools and graduated from Half Moon Bay High School in 1990. Mike pursued a career in technology in Silicon Valley for 10 years before starting his own company, Alifano Technologies in 2000. Alifano Technologies focuses on providing leading-edge technology support to all Coastside home users and small businesses. Over the last 12 years Mike has successfully grown his business from a one-man operation to a staff of 15 employees; he is proud that all his employees can both work and live in our Coastside Community. Mike, his wife Cortney and father Allan, operate their computer sales and service business at 225 Main St. in downtown Half Moon Bay.
Currently he's Vice President of the Coastside Fire Protection District Board of Directors after being appointed as a Director in 2010 and running unopposed for 2011-2015 term. Along with serving as a board director for the Cabrillo Education Foundation, Mike is also President of the Board of Directors for the Boys and Girls Club the Coastside, and past board member of Coastside HOPE. He is also a committee member of the Coastside Young Professionals, past member of the HMB Police Chief’s Advisory Committee, as well as an active supporter in the Police Athletics League. Mike’s political experience began in 2009 when he managed his father successful election to the Half Moon Bay City Council and current position of Mayor for 2011-2012. He also managed the successful election of Rob Pappalardo to the CUSD School Board. He was the Technology Chair for FOCUS (Friends of Cabrillo Unified Schools) for the Yes on Measure E & S campaign. Following the successful passage of Measure E & S he was appointed to the CUSD Measure E Citizen’s Oversight Committee. In 2012 Mike was appointed to both the Measure S Facilities Program Bond Steering Committee and Citizen's Oversight Committee.

Specialties

Hardware sales and support. Computer, Server and laptop sales and service. Data recovery and migration. We are experts in Mac and Windows, Virus and Spyware removal specialists. We complete component level repairs on laptops and desktop computers. We build customer servers, desktop and specialize in High end gaming systems. Same day service calls and turnaround on walk in help desk and computer bench work. Open 7 days a week and offer 24/7 sales, service and support.

      The committee was established by the CUSD Governing Board in order to ensure a transparent and community-based process for assigning projects under the Measure S Facility Bond Program. To this end, the Board assigned two responsibilities to the committee.

      The first is to serve in an advisory role to the CUSD Governing Board. The committee is charged with developing recommendations to the Board concerning which specific projects to move forward with, as well as to prioritize the project list and develop a timeline for the selected projects. During the current year, the committee will develop very specific recommendations for the first issuance of funding ($18 million) and a skeleton framework for the entire $81 million facility bond program.
