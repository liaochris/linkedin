Kathryn Hall's Summary

In 1980 I founded Kathryn Hall, Publicist after serving for one year as Publicity Director for a small publishing company in Marin County. Throughout much of the 80's I continued to promote such emerging New Age luminaries as Shakti Gawain, Dan Millman, Helen Palmer and Angeles Arrien. My career took a new direction when I entered grad school at California Institute of Integral Studies, studying Social and Cultural Anthropology and networked into business authors who were bringing change and transformation into corporations, which became my primary focus to this day.

In the fall of 2007 I began a gardening blog called Plant Whatever Brings You Joy in anticipation of one day publishing a book by the same title, which I had been slowly writing over two decades. In 2009 upon the advice of a kind agent in NYC I wrote the final draft of my book by rewriting chronologically, which brought my own story to the fore. In the summer of 2010 I made the bold move to publish the book under the company name Estrella Catarina, named by my daughter. My worklife now embraces continued publicity services for carefully chosen business book authors and the continued promotion of Plant Whatever Brings You Joy: Blessed Wisdom from the Garden, now distributed by Partners West. My popular gardening blog is now five years old.

I am also a non-fiction writer. I was the contributing health writer for The Financial Times Guide to Business Travel (Financial Times/Prentice Hall, London). My articles have appeared in Science of Mind magazine, Ode Magazine, Bird Watcher’s Digest, Personal Excellence, the San Francisco Business Times, The Edge, Journal for Quality and Participation, Western North Carolina Woman, Training and Development Journal and Garden Writer Association’s Quill and Trowel . I am a regular contributor to OdeWire and I am a Twitter contributor for the San Francisco Chronicle.

I anticipate continued commitment to both venues, as a personal book publicist, and as an author.
