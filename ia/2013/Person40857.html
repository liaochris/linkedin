Ellen Treanor's Summary

Ellen Treanor - MA Communication, specializes in branding and marketing communication. Her areas of expertise include advertising, public relations, impression management, social media (objective setting, implementation and evaluation), visual communication and micro PR campaigns. She earned her Masters Degree in Communication, teaches marketing, organizational communication, advertising strategies, critical thinking and public relations at the university level. She has published academic articles.

She founded ActorsWebmaster (www.actorswebmaster.com) in 1998 catering to the creative community. Earlier in her career, Ellen logged over a dozen years with AT&T -Lucent Technologies/ Bell Labs as a technical marketing manager and holds a Graduate Certificate in Information Systems from USC. Ellen studied graphic design and art history at UCLA, Video Symphony and through graphic industry workshops and seminars as a member of the National Association of Photoshop Professionals.

She was recently invited with 120 executives for the Business Mastery Series with Tony Robbins in Fiji. After the training, she launched Personal Branding Pros (www.personalbrandingpros.com) with Benjamin King Smith and a hand picked team of talented, ambitious creatives. PBP transforms individuals into brands with logo design, public relations, advertising and social media strategies.

She won several awards for directing her short film, WRINKLE. Another short film, GHOSTWRITER (producer and lead actress) was a favorite on the festival circuit garnering several prestigious awards in Washington DC, Houston, New York, and Southern California. Ellen has appeared on network television in half-hour comedy, one hour drama, national commercials and feature films. A recent film premiered at the SUNDANCE Film Festival, directed by John August.

Ellen Treanor's Publications

  • Patriotic symbolism in presidential candidate logos

    • Southern Utah University Press
    • January 28, 2010
    Authors: Ellen Treanor

    The use of patriotic symbols as visual elements in the logos of candidates for the presidential primary in 2007/08 has the power to differentiate the candidates. Using Social Judgment Theory of Sharif, the symbols in the logos will fall into the latitude of acceptance for the viewers who agree with the candidate. The traditional candidates are expected to use more patriotic symbolism while the iconoclast candidates are expected to use fewer. Powerful patriotic symbols such as color, flags, stars and eagles, strike the responsive chord but may not result in votes cast for the candidate choosing high patriotic symbolism. If the analysis, based on research findings is accurate and the candidates using traditional patriotic symbolism are nominated, the presidential match will be between Mitt Romney and Hillary Clinton whose high use of patriotic symbols will portray them as favorable and reward them with the nomination.
