Beach Eye Medical Group's Summary

Our group has well over 100 years of combined experience in diagnosing, managing, and treating a wide variety of eye conditions including cataract, glaucoma, diabetes, macular degeneration, dry eyes, keratoconus, conjunctivitis, flashes and floaters, and blepharitis. We prescribe a full range of high quality glasses and contact lenses for the correction of myopia, hyperopia, astigmatism, and presbyopia. We also offer eye care surgeries like LASIK.

At each visit, you can expect a warm, friendly, and personal touch to your medical care and eye care. We strive to treat each patient as a member of our own family. We have been serving the residents of Orange County and Southern California for over 35 years. We look forward to serving you!

Beach Eye Medical Group's Experience

Samir A. Shah, MD, MS, FACS

Beach Eye Medical Group

September 2010 – Present (3 years) Huntington Beach, CA 92648

Samir A. Shah, MD, MS, FACS is a board-certified, surgical ophthalmologist (eye doctor) with Beach Eye Medical Group in Huntington Beach. Doctor Shah has been repeatedly selected by the Consumer's Research Council as among"America's Top Ophthalmologists". Doctor Shah graduated as valedictorian of his college class at Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute in New York. He then pursued medical studies at the University of Michigan Medical School in Ann Arbor, where he was elected by his peers as President of the Medical Student Body. He then went on to complete his residency at the prestigious Jules Stein Eye Institute at the University of California, Los Angeles (UCLA). He was selected to complete a highly competitive fellowship specializing in Cornea, External Disease, and Refractive Surgery at the Wilmer Eye Institute at the world-renowned Johns Hopkins Hospital.
Doctor Shah also studied Shakespeare at Oxford University in England. He is an avid reader, traveler, and enjoys spending time with his family.
Doctor Shah's clinical expertise lies in advanced cataract surgery, laser vision correction, the latest forms of corneal transplantation, and pterygium excision. He has published book chapters and peer-reviewed journal articles on these topics. He has been involved in the training of future ophthalmologists in the United States as well as abroad. He has performed charitable surgeries internationally at the Aravind Eye Hospital in South India bringing care to some of the most rural and impoverished parts of India. He received recognition for his work by the American Association of Physicians of Indian Origin.
Doctor Shah is a Diplomate of the American Board of Ophthalmology, a member of the American Academy of Ophthalmology, the American Society for Cataract and Refractive Surgery, the Cornea Society, and the California Academy of Eye Physicians and Surgeons.

DruAnn J. McCluskey, MD

Beach Eye Medical Group

DruAnn McCluskey, MD is a Diplomate of the American Board of Ophthalmology and is an eye doctor at Beach Eye Medical Group in Huntington Beach. She was born and raised on a farm in Oklahoma. She graduated in the top one percent of her class at Oklahoma State University where she was named a Top Ten Senior. She subsequently graduated from the University of Oklahoma Medical School and was named to the Alpha Omega Alpha National Medical Honor Society. She completed her ophthalmology residency at the University of Texas. She chose to complete her fellowship training at the prestigious University of Southern California's Doheny Eye Institute where she completed fellowships both in cornea and external disease and in glaucoma. Doctor McCluskey has been on the staff of Hoag Memorial Hospital since 1989 and joined Beach Eye Medical Group in 1998.
Doctor McCluskey and her husband have two children who attend Huntington Beach High School. She has donated her time and skills to charity ophthalmologic work in Mexico and is active as a Girl Scout Leader and in the National Charity League.
Doctor McCluskey 's interests include the medical care of cornea and external disease and glaucoma, including the care of dry eye and corneal ulcer, though she is happy to see all general ophthalmology concerns. She is proud to welcome patients to a practice where real caring and compassion combine with education and experience to provide the best in eye care.

Ira A. Vidor, MD

Beach Eye Medical Group

April 2012 – Present (1 year 5 months) Huntington Beach, CA 92648

Dr. Vidor is a board-certified expert in the diagnosis and management of disorders of the eyelids, eye socket, tear ducts, eyebrows, and midface. He graduated from the Chicago Medical School and then completed a rigorous residency at the University of Texas Southwestern in Dallas. Specializing in facial and oculoplastic surgery, Dr. Vidor completed a prestigious two-year fellowship at West Virginia University that is accredited by the American Society of Ophthalmic Plastics and Reconstructive Surgery (ASOPRS). This fellowship is limited nationally to less than 30 surgeons per year.
Dr. Vidor's expertise lies in providing specialty care for all oculoplastic conditions including eyelid disorders, tear duct obstructions, periocular tumors, thyroid eye disease, orbital diseases, surgical eyelid rejuvenation, and nonsurgical facial rejuvenation such as Botox, demal fillers, and laser resurfacing. Dr. Vidor currently serves as a Clinical Professor of Ophthalmology at Loma Linda University. He is a Diplomate of the American Board of Ophthalmology and a Fellow of the American Academy of Ophthalmology and the American Society of Ophthalmic Plastics and Reconstructive Surgery. Dr. Vidor is married with two children and enjoys hiking and music in his spare time.

Timothy S. Liegler, OD

Beach Eye Medical Group

January 2000 – Present (13 years 8 months) Huntington Beach, CA 92648

Timothy S. Liegler, OD is a board-certified optometrist who has been with Beach Eye Medical Group in Huntington Beach since 2000. Doctor Liegler graduated from the University of California, Irvine with degrees in biology and psychology. He then received his doctor of optometry degree from the Southern California College of Optometry, with an advanced degree in physiological optics. While at school, he was a leader of the American Optometric Student Association, representing his school around the country. After completing his optometric degree, he received additional training in developmental optometry during a summer internship program at State University of New York, Manhattan.
Doctor Liegler was one of the first optometrists in the state to receive his glaucoma certification, and has the highest level of optometric licensure for diagnosing and treating eye disease in the State of California. He has volunteered time with the Flying Samaritans, correcting vision and treating eye diseases in rural Mexico. He also mentors students who are pursuing careers in eye care.
