Anselm Hook's Summary

I'm a software developer and artist with a passion for building software applications that help people "see through walls."

Software can help us make better decisions to collectively navigate and work within our social, political and environmental setting. The work I do is world changing, makes a real difference, and helps leverage the power of many minds to solve issues collectively.

The bulk of my experience is writing video games. This has shaped my skills and philosophy. I am an expert software developer, comfortable with most popular software languages, tools and techniques but my core skill is "shipping". The games industry is fast moving and each product cycle introduced new hardware, software, technologies and techniques. We often would go to academic journals such as SIGGRAPH and USENIX and the Santa Fe ALIFE proceedings in order to find the kinds of algorithms and methods that were necessary to simulate life. We also worked across many disciplines with many different kinds of people: artists, musicians, designers, motion capture. Our art pipeline was often a multi-disciplinary integration tool. We worked under high pressure situations and learning to ship and ship on time was paramount. Many of the processes that are common today such as XP, pair programming and the like are techniques we used to iterate quickly. We learned to decouple dependencies, prototype with off the shelf components, do high risk components first, avoid submarining with the executive, provide maximum transparency to all stake-holders, investors and the like.

I am fascinated by mapping, social cartography and especially Augmented Reality. I started WhereCamp, worked at Parc and at Dekko in this regard. Currently this is where I believe the most impact can be had.

Specialties

Technical osmosis: every project requires a significant number of new technologies or draws upon expertise that is decades old and has to be relearned.

Understanding new algorithms, hardware, software and methods, advocating for those methods and acting as a technical liason to investors or clients as well as managing production staff or simply just coding up solutions.

Anselm Hook's Experience

R&D

Dekko

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Wireless industry

March 2012 – September 2013 (1 year 7 months) SF

We built simply the most amazing technology on the planet; reconstruction of 3d spaces in real time from mobile devices to allow the blending of real and digital together. We used SLAM techniques to sample points in 3d space and used the parallax to extract a point cloud in real time directly on the mobile device. Then I built a polygon hull that approximated the surface and allowed participants to physically interact with that volume (by dropping toy objects such as cars and puppets which they could move around). The total effect was like the real world blended with CGI - you could have computer characters directly interact with objects on your table - in software - in real time. Our goal was to build a voxel map of the planet and to allow compositing of augmented information on top of the real world - just like Iron Man or other popular movies portray.

My role here in this very small team was across the board, largely hands-on and jack-of-all-trades: code, new algorithms, support software, meeting clients or investors, giving talks at conferences, planning and scheduling. I wrote the point cloud polygonizer, wrote the server, wrote documented and packaged our API for third party developers, built and published the iOS app version of our product, wrote numerous algorithms and ported our entire app to Android and to Google Glass as well. We worked with Unity3D, Objective C, C#, C, Java, node.js, parse.com, both client and server side code. It was a widely medley of technologies and techniques as is often the case in small ventures.

VFX

The Zero Theorem

October 2012 – December 2012 (3 months) Bucharest, Romania

Worked closely with Terry Gilliam and Christof Waltz. Worked with several other household name movie industry folks including Matt Damon and Tilda Swinton and an absolutely world class team of artists, animators and designers. Wrote and puppeteered interactive digital effects with the actors: teaching them how to interact with me via the software I built and then live acting the digital effects from off stage. Often meant late nights, even sleeping on set, doing whatever it took to help Terry have a win. Got to see how the movie industry works, great new friends and an opportunity to help out with my usual medley of technical and people skills.

Co-founder

MakerLab

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Internet industry

2008 – 2012 (4 years) San Francisco

Makerlab is where my friends and I play. We are a full service interactive design/build collective. Our common practice is to get together on a weekend and sprint through a project, sharing a table, food and code. We start with high concept and we translate that into a high performance interactive experience. Our expertise includes rapid prototyping, mobile and Web 2.0 dev. We've exhibited at events such as Disjecta [ http://disjecta.org/exhibitions-events/immaterialized ] and prototyped the first visual image search engine and explored the implications of this medium [ http://vimeo.com/2818525 ] . We built Lemonopoly - a place based outdoor game - [ http://lemonopoly.org ] which won the main grant at the ZeroOne Festival. We built a 3d interactive globe that surfaced tweets that was exhibited at the Banff Centre as part of a residency program. We built a full body lightsuit with spatialized RGB lights and programmed it to respond to the environment. Basically many many projects beyond my ability to enumerate here - see http://makerlab.com .

CTO

Meedan

Nonprofit; 11-50 employees; Internet industry

January 2007 – December 2009 (3 years)

Our vision is to bridge barriers between communities by providing real time natural language translation. Part of our offering is to aggregate news and conversation and to offer proximity based search on that conversation. By this I mean that we collect news articles in english and arabic and geolocate (and topic cluster) those articles and place them on a globe that allows users to precisely filter for articles of interest. This role involved quite a bit of travel and managing a remote team of IBM developers in the UK. It also involved developing a nuanced understanding of the Middle East.
