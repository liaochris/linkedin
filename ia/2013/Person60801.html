Jeff Broyles' Publications

  • In a Changing World: Helping Employers Take Control of Benefits

    • Springfield Business Journal
    • July 25, 2012
    Authors: Jeff Broyles, Janet Michel

    With double-digit healthcare increases hitting many of their clients at renewal time, Medical Benefits Group set out to shake-up how healthcare benefits were designed. The group has brought an open-network, partially self-funded group health plan to Southwest Missouri – the first of its kind to provide an array of features not normally found in traditional health plans.

    “With this partially-self funded program, we are seeing two to three months worth of premiums being returned annually, depending on the health of the group for that year,” said Medical Benefits Group owner Jeff Broyles. “This is money that wasn’t used to pay for insurance claims – and all claims money not used is returned to the employer.”

    The plan is designed so that stop-loss insurance provides a cushion for catastrophic claims and protection from any overages for the year. The plan is built on a level, monthly payment plan, just like traditional plans, and can include co-pays and cost sharing based on customer preference.

    “But the big difference in this plan is the potential to receive money-back that wasn’t used to pay claims,” Broyles added. “When has any traditional insurance provider said ‘you had a great year, you didn’t use all your benefits, so here is $30,000 back’? This will never happen in a traditional plan.
