Luis Carpio-Govea's Summary

I am currently consulting for organisations working for social change and sustainable development, in particular on (but not limited to) multilateral organisations and Caribbean affairs. I was Head of Projects at the Latin American and Caribbean Economic System Secretariat until June 2011. From 2005 to 2010 I was Director for Transport and Disaster Risk Reduction of the Association of Caribbean States (ACS). In my time there I had a considerable record of successes working with very limited resources by fomenting meaningful relationships with other organisations. Before Joining the ACS in 2003 as Political Advisor I served for 20 years in the Venezuelan Foreign Service. In 2002, I was a member of the Venezuelan team that chaired the G-77 and China during the WFFD and WSSD, My most cherished achievements whilst in the G-77 Chair, include being personally responsible for negotiating within the Group, as well as with development partners, the entirety of Chapter X (Means of implementation) of the Plan of Implementation of the WSSD during the last year of negotiations, as well as during the Summit itself. I am also proud to have personally negotiated the first NEPAD Resolution on behalf of the G-77 and China. My most significant achievement as Director at the ACS was the organisation (both substantive and logistically, including resource mobilisation) of the High-Level Conference on Disaster Reduction held from 14th to 16th November 2007 in Haiti. My duties included the drafting of the original Conference Framework Paper and the Draft Saint Marc Plan of Action. During my tenure as Director, I wa successful in introducing the concept of gender perspective for the first time in any ACS agenda. On the Transport part of my Brief, I am also quite proud to have successfully carried out (including resource mobilisation) two very important projects: "Map of Maritime Routes of the Greater Caribbean" and "Port and Maritime Database of the Greater Caribbean".

Job Description (Head of Projects) 01/02/11-Present

Established in 1975 and with its Headquarters in Caracas, Venezuela, the Latin American and Caribbean Economic System (SELA) is a regional intergovernmental organization that groups 28 countries. Its mission is to provide the region with a system of consultation and coordination for the adoption of common positions and strategies on economic issues and foster cooperation and integration. The Head of Projects is tasked with conducting studies to gain knowledge on critical areas or issues of common interest for the Member States of SELA, with a high level of quality and opportunities for SELA to achieve recognition as a leading entity in promoting regional integration and cooperation. In this regard the job entails the preparation of analytical documents on various aspects related to international economic relations of Latin America and the Caribbean, the creation and updating of databases on various economic indicators and qualitative information about the region, interpretation and process of statistical information in order to determine trends, patterns and relations and the preparation of drafts for technical and analytical documents on various aspects of the region’s social and economic development.
