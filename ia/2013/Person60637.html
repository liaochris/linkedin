Director of Communications, U.S. Senate Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs Committee

United States Senate

Government Agency; 1001-5000 employees; Legislative Office industry

June 2009 – May 2011 (2 years) Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs Committee, Washington, D.C.

As communications director for the U.S. Senate Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs Committee, I created and executed all media strategies and political messaging for the minority staff under U.S. Senator Susan Collins of Maine, Ranking Member. Accomplishments:
• Ensured coverage of all major committee activities through advisories, press releases, statements, quotes, videos and photographs for broadcast, print and online media.
• Responded 24/7 to media inquiries on the vast array of committee work, including proposed legislation, pending nominations, member-requested GAO and IG investigations and urgent responses to immediate breaking news.
• Translated complex legislation, public policy proposals, and all aspects of national security and terrorism prevention into compelling and accurate press releases and statements for the general public, using all media platforms.
• Wrote opening statements for committee hearings, bill introductions, Senate floor statements, op-eds, speeches, and national addresses.
• Worked closely with the committee’s staff director, general counsel and all policy experts to capture the committee’s wide range of work – from TSA airport security to chemical facility safeguards; from U.S. Department of Homeland Security budgeting to federal contracting; from counter-terrorism to federal oversight issues.

The Committee on Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs (HSGAC) is the Senate’s primary oversight committee with broad jurisdiction over government operations generally and the Department of Homeland Security in particular. The Committee has five subcommittees:
Permanent Subcommittee on Investigations
Subcommittee on Oversight of Government Management, the Federal Workforce and the District of Columbia
Subcommittee on Federal Financial Management, Government Information, Federal Services, and International Security
Ad Hoc Subcommittee on Disaster Recovery and Intergovernmental Affairs
Ad Hoc Subcommittee on Contracting Oversight

Editor and Vice President

Portland Press Herald/Maine Sunday Telegram

Privately Held; 201-500 employees; Newspapers industry

September 1996 – June 2009 (12 years 10 months) Portland, Maine

As editor and vice president, I was the top news executive for this award-winning Blethen Maine Newspaper, family-owned and part of The Seattle Times Co. It was the largest newspaper in Maine, with a daily circulation of about 70,000 and a Sunday circulation of about 110,000. The Sunday Telegram was Maine’s only statewide newspaper. Our Web site, www.pressherald.com, averaged more than 4 million page views per month and growth was robust. Accomplishments:
• Supervised a 90-member news staff, including merger of Maine Today online operation into a single news team.
• Grew newspaper circulation and increase Web site traffic. Our news motto: Break it online, explain it in print.
• Managed a $6 million operating budget and a $5 million newsprint budget.
• Participated in senior executive committee oversight and served on the editorial board.
• Oversaw daily and Sunday coverage plans, built for reader relevance and impact.
• Collaborated with broadcast partners, WMTW-TV, WGME-TV and MPBN, Maine Public Broadcasting.
• Wrote weekly “Editor’s Note” column, designed to connect with our audience and gather feedback. This column also served to make me the newspaper’s ombudsman, or “public face” of the newspaper.
• Initiated and hosted annual Community Council of Readers advisory board.
• Chaired interdepartmental New Products Task Force.
• Launched new products both in print and online. These included Outdoors, Boomers, iHerald, Monday Magazine, Maine Life, Home & Garden, plus “Your Neighbor” and “Close to Home” micro-local news sections.
