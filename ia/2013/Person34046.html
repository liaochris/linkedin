General Manager

ID&T Publishing B.V.

Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Entertainment industry

April 2002 – January 2005 (2 years 10 months) Amsterdam Area, Netherlands

In the music industry, a music publisher (or publishing company) is responsible for ensuring the songwriters and composers receive payment when their compositions are used commercially. The copyrights owned and administered by a publishing company is one of the most important forms of intellectual property in the music industry (the other is the copyright on a master recording which is typically owned by a record company). Publishing companies play a central role in managing this vital asset.

In my role as music publisher I was responsible to link up new songs by songwriters with suitable recording artists to record them and to place writers' songs in other media such as movie soundtracks and commercials.

I also handled copyright registration and "ownership" matters for the songwriters and composers I represented. Further I also supervise the issue of songbooks and sheet music.

As a general manager I ran the day to day operation and was responsible for the P&L, Administration and the relations with songwriters, composers and co-publishers.

• Announce new recorded music acquisitions to the radio station
• Select music to be programmed into the station’s automated music system

Director of A&R/International Affairs

Combined Forces Records B.V.

February 1999 – February 2002 (3 years 1 month) The Hague Area, Netherlands

As a Director of A&R I was responsible for finding new recording artists and bringing them to the record company together with the A&R team. We were expected to understand the current tastes of the market and to be able to find artists that will be commercially successful.

I was authorized to offer a record contract. I maintained contact with entertainment lawyers hired by the musician's manager or the musician himself. Further I oversaw the recording process. This includes helping the artist to find the right record producer, scheduling time in a recording studio and advising the artist on all aspects of making a high-quality recording.

In my role as Director of International Affairs my responsibility was to significantly expand our business to license our master recordings to record companies outside the Netherlands.

Aside from generating additional record sales, my job was also to create relationships with record companies outside the Netherlands who will have various music business relationships in that foreign country and who can connect us with booking agencies, venues, festival organizers, music publishers, etc. in that territory.

Music licensing was my main activity which was intended to ensure that we the owners of the rights on the musical works were compensated for the use of our recordings.
