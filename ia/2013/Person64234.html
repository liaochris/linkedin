Jim Winburn's Projects

  • Clinical Study Resource Optimization

    • February 2013 to Present
    Team Members: Jim Winburn

    Developing an optimizer application for resourcing (monitor and site management) that combines an intuitive visualization identifying current assignments and plan voids with resource assignment based on a validated time distribution model. This model significantly improves accuracy in assignment, allocation, realization, cost control and planning.

  • Site Level Patient Enrollment Rate Forecast Model

    • December 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Jim Winburn, Raymond B Walther III,

    Successfully designed and developed a site level patient enrollment forecast method and visualization. Based on a gamma-poisson process, this model has been validated for correctness and accuracy by region and across multiple therapeutic areas.

  • Variance Reduction Model for Clinical Studies

    • December 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Jim Winburn

    This model is based on an analysis of study variance. It elucidates why many manufacturing methods are ineffective in clinical studies. It also forms a foundation for developing an effective strategy to significantly reduce execution time, while improving quality.

  • Accelerated Patient Recruitment Model

    • December 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Jim Winburn, Raymond B Walther III,

    This model is based on an acceleration method that exploits high probability features from a specific motivational analysis. The model also provides a means to dynamically evaluate "What if" scenario outcomes and costs. It will increase the likelihood of meeting or exceeding recruitment targets while lowering costs.

  • Interim Monitoring Forecast Model

    • December 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Jim Winburn

    This model provides a method to more accurately plan monitoring based on an analysis of IMV's across multiple therapeutic areas and phases, over several years.
    In aggregate, it can be evaluated as a Markov process for total portfolio forecasting, resulting in significantly more accurate resource planning. It can also factor seasonality and regional influences.

  • CRA Recruitment Model for improved Hiring and Retention

    • December 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Jim Winburn, Raymond B Walther III

    This model is based on a classification method that improves decision quality in hiring and is the basis of a high probability, strategic retention model.

  • Intelligent Study Resourcing Visualization

    Team Members: Jim Winburn, Raymond B Walther III

    These are geo-spatial project and portfolio visualizations, layered with intelligence, derived from the application of multiple, advanced methods to answer intangible / intractable questions and provide optimization scenarios.

  • Clinical Architecture & Integration

    • December 2011 to Present
    Team Members: Jim Winburn, Bill Rivers, Gabriele Monetti

    Established PRA's Enterprise Architectural practice. Designed, Developed and Implemented a successful, standards based, foundational fabric enabling business efficiency and improving data quality.

  • Study Rescue

    • June 2013 to Present
    Team Members: Jim Winburn

    The objective of this project was to "rescue" a study that wasn't enrolling patients from the target population. Our analysis produced a previously unrecognized potential population demographic and we helped develop the strategy to find and enroll new patients and geographically identify likely investigator candidates with the requisite therapeutic and performance criteria. We also helped develop an effective marketing strategy including social media methods.
