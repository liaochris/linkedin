Emre Sokullu's Summary

I'm the founder and CEO of GROU.PS, a social groupware platform that allows people to come together and form interactive communities around a shared interest or affiliation. As a free platform, GROU.PS traffic soared from 0 to 8 million monthly unique visitors between 2008 and 2011. Today, as a subscription based business, we're profitable and see 1 million monthly unique visitors. We have raised $5MM over 3 rounds of equity financing.

Prior to founding GROU.PS, I managed the West-Coast and blogosphere-wide marketing efforts of $25MM funded semantic search startup hakia, wrote for one of the world's top 20 blogs, ReadWriteWeb, and took a temporary research position at Wordnet Project in Princeton University.

My first project as an undergrad student, Turkix, a Windows-like Linux distribution for the Turkic desktop OS market, was snapped up by Armador AS and sold bundled with Turkey's leading laptop producer Datron's mainstream models. During my tenure at Armador, I also served in thin client transformation of Tupras, one of Europe's largest energy companies.

I'm a big open source enthusiast. I led the local efforts of Apache Software Foundation in Turkey and participated many international open source projects like KDE, Mandriva, Red Hat, RPM.

I can be found blogging on http://www.emresokullu.com
