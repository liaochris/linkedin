Daniel Taylor's Summary

Mr. Taylor counsels clients regarding all aspects of civil litigation, including trial, and assists clients with managing their intellectual property resources to avoid litigation where possible.

Mr. Taylor received his Juris Doctorate degree from the University of Southern California, where he was the Senior Editor of the Southern California Law Review. Mr. Taylor received a Bachelor of Arts degree in History and Minor in Professional Writing from the University of California at Santa Barbara, graduating with High Honors.

Mr. Taylor is a member of the State Bar of California and admitted to practice before all California courts and the U.S. District Court, Central District of California.

Specialties

Civil Litigation, Protection and Enforcement of Intellectual Property, Copyrights, Trademarks, Trade Secrets, and Entertainment Law
