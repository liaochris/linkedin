National Coalition Against Prescription Drug Abuse

The National Coalition Against Prescription Drug Abuse (NCAPDA) was established to help reduce the number of deaths and addictions caused by prescription drug abuse, through the implementation of a nationwide awareness campaign, the initiation and support of pertinent legislative action and through partnerships with educational, medical, law enforcement and other appropriate entities.

Primary Goals:
Link existing community based entities throughout the U.S. under this organization to share best practices, develop and share useful material resources and to establish and implement a cohesive, nationwide awareness campaign around the dangers of prescription drug abuse.

Foundational Study Facilitator

Women of Grace

January 2009 – 2013 (4 years)

Facilitators are at the heart of the Women of Grace® Study Program. In the Women of Grace Foundational Study we embark upon a journey of discovery and transformation as we explore the great call and gift of our femininity. Along the way each week we meet two great women saints, receive wisdom from Holy Mother Church, unpack the life-changing wisdom of Holy Scripture, and discover how the Blessed Virgin Mary is the exemplar of discipleship and the pathway to her Son, Jesus Christ and the heart of the Father. Lessons are presented in a multi-media format of DVD’s, books, and study guides.
