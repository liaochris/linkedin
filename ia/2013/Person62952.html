Jesse Mullins' Experience

Via Verdigris

2009 – Present (4 years) Abilene, Texas

Founder and Operator of Via Verdigris, a content marketing firm. Currently serving as editor (in a freelance capacity) of Permian Basin Oil and Gas Magazine. Established, accomplished, and widely published editor-in-chief and writer who has been read by millions.

Editor in Chief

American Cowboy Magazine

January 1991 – August 2009 (18 years 8 months)

Employed by Active Interest Media, which in 2006 acquired American Cowboy L.L.C., formerly dba Web Publications Inc.; American Cowboy has a national paid circulation of approximately 100,000 (ABC-audited), with total pass-around readership exceeding a quarter-million and subscribers in more than 50 countries. Dreamed up the magazine in 1993 with its then-owner Bill Bales and was its Editor in Chief since its inception in 1994.
Responsibilities:
Established overall concept, strategy, and direction for the editorial portion of the magazine.
Set the editorial tone, style, and voice.
Supervised all editorial staff, including art director.
Found and/or generated article ideas.
Recruited and retained freelance writers and photographers.
Hired editorial staff.
Assigned stories.
Managed freelance budgets of $14,000 per issue.
Wrote bylined articles and commentaries and compiled or composed un-bylined material. Contributed approximately 1/5 of the written content of any given issue.
Edited manuscripts for substance, style, and mechanics (or directed and oversaw work of other editor[s] in those efforts); made final judgments on all material.
Supervised content, delegated workload, and determined strategy on americancowboy.com, the magazine's website.
Advised marketing department of opportunities; met with them on strategy.
Worked with writers to resolve major questions or revisions.
Worked with publishing/production staff to ensure quality product as well as adherence to publication deadlines.
Worked with design/layout staff to ensure high standards of visual appearance and readability.
Reviewed and gave approval to all pages designed by outside graphic designers, as well as those done in-house.
Traveled monthly to Boulder (company headquarters) for meetings.
Represented the magazine at association meetings, industry events, conventions, and other gatherings; spoke before industry groups; gave interviews to media.

Editorial Director

Collector's Mart Magazine

January 1991 – January 1994 (3 years 1 month)

During this timeframe I was at same company that launched American Cowboy, but these were my responsibilities prior to the launch of that magazine.
Was Editorial Director of Collectors Mart Magazine, Art Today Magazine, and Antique Market Report Newsletter (Web Publications); Collector's Mart had a national paid circulation of 90,000, and carried more than $300,000 worth of advertising in the last issue we published prior to its sale to Krause Publications. Art Today, whose circulation peaked at about 50,000 prior to the sale, was dedicated to fine contemporary painting and sculpture. Antique Market Report tracked antique sales activity-mainly auction results-across the country.
Responsibilities: Comparable to above (with American Cowboy), but with addition of more travel to cover collectibles expositions, art and antiques shows, and other related events.
