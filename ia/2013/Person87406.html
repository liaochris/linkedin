    Mar 11, 2011 - NATIONAL BLACK MARRIAGE DAY 2011

    FAYETTEVILLE, NC (March 11, 2011-) – A Stronger Bond in cooperation with Wedded Bliss Foundation, Inc. will host its 2nd annual Black Marriage Day Celebration in Fayetteville, NC on Sunday, March 27, 2011, with the theme “Married and Proud of It!” Although this is only the second year for Fayetteville, NC, Black Marriage Day has been celebrated nationwide since 2003.

    A Stronger Bond will join activists in hundreds of cities around the country to highlight the benefits of married life and offer a celebration to strengthen and promote marriage in Fayetteville’s Black community. This year’s event is a celebration of love and marriage filled with entertainment including dining, dancing, and giveaways. The highlight of this event will be a special recognition in honor of the couple who has been married the longest in the Fayetteville, NC area.

    “You don’t have to fall prey to the dismal statistics facing the black community. Black love is real and exists beyond the imagination and the pages of romance novels. There are many marriages thriving in the black community worthy of celebrating”, explained A Stronger Bond’s spokesperson LaTricia Smith.

    The event is open to the public. All is welcome, whether you are married, engaged or somewhere in between. Preregistration is required.

    For more information regarding this event, you may contact LaTricia Smith @ (910) 816-9270, e-mail her at info @ astrongerbond.com or visit ww.celebratingblackmarriage.com
