Bjoern Lasse Herrmann's Summary

Bjoern (28) was born in Germany, and has become a recognized entrepreneur and expert on data products, data driven decision making, education, innovation, entrepreneurship and organizational development.

He founded and led 5 for and non profit ventures in Germany, Bangladesh & the US. He also worked as an executive in Russia for one year for a mid sized marketing firm. In his free time he is an advisor to multiple for and non profit (e.g. Startup Canada, Startup Malaysia), a mentor for the Thiel Fellowship, Startup Bootcamp and Spark Labs and a speaker at various conferences (e.g Strata & Hadoop World, Pioneers Festival, Int. Startup Festival) and Universities (e.g. Stanford, Oxford, Cambridge, SU). He is also a member of the Sandbox Network, the WEF GS and the Silicon Valley Leadership group. He is actively engaged into German, European and US politics on the issues of entrepreneurship and immigration.

Most recently his team & him set out to decrease the massive failure rate of businesses by transforming the business world from gut driven to data driven. The first product "Startup Compass" now serves tens of thousands of software businesses around the world. Its success has been covered in WSJ, PBS, ARD, Techcrunch, Wired and Forbes. The young company has been backed by prominent investors including Steve Blank, Allen Morgan, Roger Krakoff, Daniel Recanati, Gianluca Dettori, Erik Jansen and Stefan Glaenzer.

Since its inception Compass has released a series of well-regarded reports on why companies fail and succeed and on comparing global ecosystems. They have been adopted into the curriculum of more than 100 Universities worldwide such as Stanford and Science Po and have inspired numerous businesses and political initiatives.

Bjoern's personal passion or "leitmotif" is: unleashing human potential. He enjoys to see people grow. His higher purpose is to strive to maximize the capitalization of every single individual.

Bjoern Lasse Herrmann's Experience

Founder & CEO

Compass

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Computer Software industry

January 2011 – Present (2 years 11 months) San Francisco

My current venture is Compass. So far we released two projects: Startup Compass and Startup Genome.

We empower small and medium sized companies to leverage the massive competitive advantage of data driven decision making by replacing expensive and fallible consultants & analysts previously only accessible to large companies.

Startup Compass is a dashboard providing benchmarks and recommendations based on Marketing, Sales and Financial data pulled various APIs (Salesforce/Quickbooks/Google Analytics/etc.). The dashboard helps companies to allocate their resources by a) Recommending relevant Metrics b) Benchmarking Metrics against similar businesses and c) Providing automated recommendations on how to improve those Metrics.

The technology is essentially a “data refinery” or an “analyst in the cloud”. The user puts low value data in and gets high value data back. We are among a new class of technology companies automating today’s manual labor of highly educated & expensive analysts, consultants and data scientists.

Our goal is to accelerate the global pace of innovation and reduce the failure rate of businesses.

Supercool School

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; E-Learning industry

November 2008 – January 2011 (2 years 3 months)

This was my first funded Silicon Valley startup.

Our mission is to give people worldwide access to education. The best way to do that seemed to decentralize and democratize education by empowering passionate educators around the world.

Therefore we built a platform that allows everybody to create their own school online. Essentially we are doing for education what blogging has done for publishing.

Each school is a mix of ning and webex. We stripped the learning management component to the minimum and added the functionality to allow people to request classes as well. By doing this we built a simple way for people to build the curriculum collaboratively. Because of its social networking component each school becomes a powerful community that evolves organically over time.

The platform reached more than 2000 online schools and more than 60000 students. The platform is used by various ivy league universities, Fortune 50 companies and passionate individuals to educate the world.
