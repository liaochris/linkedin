Kevin Loker's Summary

Kevin Loker is a journalist and researcher interested in how communities function, grow and obtain knowledge both online and off. He is the Program Coordinator at the American Press Institute, a journalism think tank. Previously he was the Digital Coordinator at the Online News Association, a membership association for journalists. Recently he was a regular contributor at 10,000 Words, a technology and journalism blog.

--

Kevin graduated as "Senior of the Year" (and homecoming king!) from George Mason University in Fairfax, Virginia, in May 2012 with a B.A. in anthropology. Other accomplishments during his undergraduate career include the University Scholars Leadership Award, the Distinguished Quill Award for Student Leadership, and independent research on the role of instant communications technology in the development of college-aged romantic relationships.

At Mason, Kevin oversaw the growth of Connect2Mason, a young online-only news site that acts as both a one-stop shop for the distribution of all student media content and a source for original daily news and updates affecting the Mason community. He played a primary role in the site's redesign, its staff restructure and the increase in reader participation. He also created a large IRL puzzle game event to boost community engagement and awareness for the site.

At Washington Post Live, the paper's events and conferences division, he worked on live event coverage and created engagement strategies that harness social media.

Specialties

- emerging technology and trends
- applied anthropological theory
- web publishing/producing with Drupal and WordPress
- live coverage planning and execution
- event planning
