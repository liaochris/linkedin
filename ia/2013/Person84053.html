madhu jain's Summary

I studied & researched Nihonga and Sumi-e two Japanese techniques of painting for 4 years in Japan, after a tedious study of the Japanese language. Solo exhibitions of my paintings have been invited internationally (Tokyo, Singapore & San Francisco USA, and received wide international acclaim.

Specialties:

1. Nihonga technique involves the use of natural mineral pigments ground to 10 gradations and layered upon by brush on handmade paper (washi) using glue(nikawa) and water.

2. Sumi-e, are the single stroke black ink painting, much like the Zen concept, minimal or only the essence is communicated dropping all unnecessary details. Needless to say, it is beauty condensed and distilled. Even one wrong line, can take away the ‘life’ from the painting. Much concentration and practice is required, before the actual painting is started.
