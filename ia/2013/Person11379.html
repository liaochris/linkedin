Co-Founder | Co-Curator

ProjectDP.org

March 2013 – Present (7 months) http://www.projectdp.org

ProjectDP archives the experience of Armenian Displaced Persons who lived at Fünkerkaserne in Stuttgart, Germany immediately after WWII. The idea behind ProjectDP is to harness the Internet to make your family’s DP experience accessible to more people, and to interactively record your family experience by giving you feedback opportunities.

The founders envision ProjectDP to have three phases. First, we’re publishing the interactive website at http://www.projectdp.org to share and gather information. Second, we plan to publish a coffee table book – a treasure for your home and also for the Library of Congress to preserve the DP experience for posterity. The third phase is to maintain a research archive for scholars to access to facilitate publication of scholarly materials.

The curators are interested in the DP community's photos, videos, sound recordings, research material, works of art, artifacts, historical maps and charts. Feel free to contact us.
