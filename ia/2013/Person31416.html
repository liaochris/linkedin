David A. George's Summary

My passion for technology developed at a young age. I can remember sitting on my livingroom floor as a tiny child, taking apart my parents’ answering machine, VCR, radio - you name it - and trying to put it back together again. K’nex and Legos were the ultimate - I spent many summer hours building towers, roller coasters, and ball machines. Boy, that was the life.

But as I got older, I realized there was something missing with machines and computers - the human element. I’m a pretty strong extrovert, so just getting computers to talk to computers wasn’t going to cut it for me. I wanted to bring humans and technology together to create something beautiful.

My first project happened while I was in college at Virginia Tech. A friend and I had a vision for a wiki-like site structure with a central dashboard to connect students with all the different colleges, clubs, and organizations on campus. I got support from the SGA and acquired 5 students from a senior-level computer science class who helped me turn this idea into a reality.

Next, I worked with two engineering Ph.D. candidates to build a crowdfunding platform for the medical-research industry. We created Dive Into Research - a crowdfunding platform that gives researchers an easy way to gain exposure and funding by allowing people to donate to that individual researcher/project.

Now, I work for a start-up named Heyo, and spend my days writing blog posts on internet marketing, running A/B tests on landing pages, designing and testing email marketing campaigns, digging through in-app and website analytics, working with backlogs and sprints to guide our product development, and even a bit of HTML and CSS work here and there. My motto: "Bacon, not spam."

I absolutely love what I do, and the best part is - it’s exactly what I want to do! Every day I combine my passion for technology and my love for other people by solving their needs through creating software they love - and I'm not stopping anytime soon.
