      San Francisco Bicycle Coalition
      • Environment
      September 2007 – present (6 years 5 months)

      PARK(ing) Day 2007
      I was approached by Rachel Krai, then SFBC Program Manager, and asked to coordinate the building of a park to raise public awareness for the Fix Masonic Campaign.

      The morning of September 21, 2007 a handful of concerned citizens and activists from the Fix Masonic campaign and San Francisco Bicycle Coalition (SFBC) converged on the busy intersection of Masonic and Fulton with the intent to transform an ordinary street parking spot into an open green space oasis for the public to enjoy for the day. PARK(ing) Day an idea originally conceived by REBAR, a San Francisco-based art collective, has grown into a global event since 2005 when the first temporary park was built. In total, 180 PARKS were built in 47 cities worldwide on PARK(ing) Day 2007. Our park, 1 among 58 temporary public parks built in San Francisco that day, fulfilled the mission of PARK(ing) Day - "To rethink the way streets are used, call attention to the need for urban parks, and improve the quality of urban human habitat. ".

      The Masonic and Fulton PARK aimed to increase public awareness and participation in the efforts to improve conditions and safety for motorists, pedestrians and bicyclists who travel Masonic Avenue. Curious passersby stopped to engage in conversation about solutions to the high traffic situation and signed the Fix Masonic petition. Friendly honks and waves from motorists added to the relaxed and amiable atmosphere felt by all who paused to rest lingering in the shaded comfort of the PARK. A successful experiment in how just a little extra open green space the size of a parking spot can slow traffic on a busy transportation corridor and bring people within the community together to openly discuss creating viable solutions.
