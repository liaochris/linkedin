  • Blue Centurion Homes - Web-based Administration Portal

    • March 2013 to June 2013
    Team Members: Jaime Nacach

    Blue Centurion Home's main business is to purchase houses from the foreclosure market, remodel them, and sell them for a profit. "Flipping houses", as this business is commonly known, is very risky and delicate, so the key to being successful relies on having the most up-to-date and accurate information about a house up for sale through a public auction. This includes information about the house such as it's condition (through images), estimated value, rehab costs, loans and trustees, and multiple other costs that must be calculated and analyzed.

    My task and goal was to create a web-based administration portal that would transform the way in which Blue Centurion Homes (BCH) manages information. BCH managed all of it's information through the use multiple Excel sheets and hundreds of un-organized emails with photograph attachments, which took BCH's analysts too much time to go through and analyze information and photos of more than an average of 60 properties per day. This made it too hard to have the correct and latest information in front of them when the auction begins, so they could determine their highest price they're willing to pay.

    Through the use of Intuit's Quickbase online database software, I developed a portal that allows administrators, managers, house evaluators, on-site house inspectors and Title companies to work together as a team through a single web-based system. Each user's role was restricted to only the fields and information each needed, increasing their job efficiency and timing. This system has helped BCH not only work more efficiently in San Diego's real estate market, but has now allowed it to expand to evaluation and buying homes in Orange and LA County. Thanks for this great IT system, everyone has access to information fast and when they need it.

    Blue Centurion's Website: www.bluecenturionhomes.com
