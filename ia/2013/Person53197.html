Beth McMillan's Summary

A PhD student at the University of Oxford, studying at the Systems Biology Doctoral Training Centre. My first degree was in Biochemistry and my second was in Mechanistic Biology.

Specialties

Organisation, ruthless efficiency, biological modelling, mathematics, Python, XHTML, PHP, CSS, working with Linux systems, automation, bioinformatics, structural biology, secretarial work, typing (80wpm speed), unnecessarily precise grammar, scientific writing, skills sharing, public speaking, learning quickly, baking cakes and having boundless enthusiasm!

Beth McMillan's Education

University of Oxford

Doctor of Philosophy (PhD), Systems Biology

2012 – 2016 (expected)

The taught component of this course has included modules on programming in C, Perl, R and MATLAB, vector calculus, organic chemistry, statistics, drug discovery, experimental design, image analysis and biological modelling. In addition, I have had the opportunity to experience some practical organic chemistry including: thin layer chromatography, rotary evaporation, column chromatography, IR spectroscopy, the Suzuki coupling reaction, and synthesis of Fembufen, DEET, and biaryls, as well as practical biology including: agrobacterium-mediated Ti-plasmid infiltration of tobacco plants, confocal microscopy, and qRT-PCR.

I am currently working in the Institute of Biomedical Engineering, creating a mathematical model of the changes in metabolic rate on differentiation of human stem cells into hepatocytes.

Activities and Societies: Oxford University Student Chorus (alto), Somerville College Choir (soprano), DTC Chamber Choir (conductor), Somerville College Settlers of Catan Society (secretary).

The University of Sheffield

MSc, Mechanistic Biology

2011 – 2012

Grade: Distinction

An interdisciplinary course based in the Molecular Biology, Physics, Chemistry, Maths and Computer Science departments. This course includes modules in maths, modelling of biological systems, physical biology, biophysical chemistry, and programming in Python, with a particular emphasis on bioinformatics applications.

Practical lab projects have included determining the structure of a protein from X-ray crystallography data using multi-wavelength anomalous dispersion, using 1D 1H nuclear magnetic resonance spectroscopy to follow the unfolding of lysozyme by urea, and atomic force microscopy of empty Escherichia coli peptidoglycan sacculi.

For my long research project, I created an agent-based model of the contribution of actin filaments to the impetus required for endocytosis in budding yeast, using Python.

Activities and Societies: Wind Orchestra, Chamber Choir

The University of Sheffield

BSc Hons, Molecular Biology & Biotechnology

2007 – 2011

This course covered microbiology, genetics and biochemistry practical and theory, as well as modules in data handling, IT and communication skills.

My final year project was in X-ray crystallography, investigating the binding of inhibitors to a large enzyme. This involved transformation of competent cells, size exclusion and ion exchange chromatography, SDS-PAGE, co-crystallization of the enzyme with its inhibitors, and X-ray data interpretation using iMosflm.

In my final year, I was involved in the "Bring It Don't Bin It!" scheme, which gives leaving students a chance to recycle their unwanted goods. I successfully persuaded the student's union to extend the scheme to involve PAT testing for electrical items, a collection service for non-campus students and a "yard sale" where continuing students could pick up the collected items for a donation. I also joined in with a team of volunteers collecting and sorting items in several university buildings.

Activities and Societies: Symphony Orchestra, Wind Orchestra, Chamber Choir, Bring It Don't Bin It
