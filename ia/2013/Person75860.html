Kim Siler's Summary

Kim Siler brings a diverse background to her role as Mobile Brand Strategist within the Global Connections team at The Coca-Cola Company. In this position, Kim is responsible for defining mobile strategies and platforms into connection plans as well as guiding development and implementation. Kim lead the global mobile strategy for The London 2012 Olympic Games --- the work was awarded a 2012 Bronze Lion for Best Integrated Campaign led by Mobile in the inaugural Mobile category at Cannes and four awards, including Best in Show and Global Mobile Campaign, at the Mobile Marketing Association’s 2012 Smarties awards.

Kim is currently managing the global digital strategy and development of 2014 FIFA World Cup activation for global brand Powerade.

Previously, Kim held design director and product marketing executive roles within Cingular/AT&T Mobility. Kim was given the CEO awarded AT&T Mobility Key Contributor award for her instrumental role in the text HAITI to 90999 to donate $10 to the Red Cross campaign. Prior to Cingular/AT&T Mobility, Kim’s focus was interactive design as an Art Director at AGENCY.COM, Design Manager at Cox Interactive Media’s Atlanta Studio and Graphic Designer at InterZine Productions – AOL’s first greenhouse niche content provider.

In December 2012 Kim was named as one of Mobile Marketer’s Women to Watch in 2013.

Board of Directors: MMA EMEA (Mobile Marketing Association - Europe, Middle East and Africa)
Advisory Board: LBMA (Location Based Marketing Association)
Member: Toastmasters International
