Benjamin Cramer's Summary

I represent small businesses and entrepreneurs in business formation, business operation, and—when necessary—business litigation. I am a firm believer that good lawyers win cases, but better lawyers are able to avoid the fight. For many small businesses, the costs of litigation outweigh the benefits of winning and many fights can be avoided with careful planning and the proper agreements. Nevertheless, some disputes are inevitable—and when they arise I have the expertise to handle them.

As a tax attorney, I help individuals and small businesses in resolving tax controversies, such as disputes over assessments by the IRS and state and local tax authorities.

My litigation practice focuses on business disputes with an emphasis on appellate matters. I have briefed arguments and motions in various state and federal courts, including the U.S. Court of Appeals for the Sixth Circuit and the Supreme Court of Ohio. I have represented clients in both prosecuting and defending original actions in the Supreme Court of Ohio.

I also represent consumers who are feeling the effects of the present economy. My consumer practice involves representing debtors with creditor disputes, bankruptcy, and post-bankruptcy credit issues.

I have extensive government experience, having worked for judges on both a federal trial court and a federal court of appeals. I use this experience to help clients navigate the frequently difficult maze of government regulation. I have particular experience representing governments and private clients in matters involving Ohio’s Open Meetings Act and Public Records Act.

I am a member of the Cincinnati Bar Association and serve as an officer on its Solo and Small Firm committee. I am also a member of the Ohio State Bar Association and the Cincinnati/Northern Kentucky chapter of the Federal Bar Association.
