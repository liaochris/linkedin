Doug Battista's Summary

A resourceful and innovative Senior Human Resources professional with 15 years of diverse experience. A natural leader who excels at building HR organizations. Expertise includes outstanding skills in Talent Management, Change Management and Employee Relations.

Doug Battista's Experience

Vice President Human Resources

Jenny Craig

Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; Health, Wellness and Fitness industry

March 2012 – Present (1 year 10 months) Carlsbad, California

Reporting directly to the Global CEO, lead the Human Resources function for Jenny Craig, a weight management Company owned by Nestlé with over 4000 employees and 700 company-owned and franchised locations. Brought in to overhaul the Human Resources Department and align it with the Company’s business strategy. A member of the Company’s Executive Leadership Team.
• In a short period of time, re-built the Human Resources department from a reactionary group focused on personnel administration to a strategic business partner. Closely involved with the Company’s business strategy while managing the entire HR function including leadership development, succession, employee relations, recruiting, internal communications, payroll, compensation & benefits.
• Lead the organizational design and change efforts for Jenny Craig’s strategy implementation including the transition from a brick-and-mortar retailer to a multi-channel service organization with a revenue-generating e-business model. Plan and execute all facets of the Company’s organization model including business case, organizational design, talent model, and compensation programs.
• Successfully planned and executed HR business transformation initiatives including the closure of the company’s United Kingdom business, consolidation of 100 North America retail locations, and startup of Jenny Craig’s e-business channel.
• Headed the efforts to recruit, select and hire members of the Company’s leadership team including the Chief Information Officer, Chief Marketing Officer and E-business Director.
• Serve as a partner and trusted confidant of CEO.

Head of Talent Acquisition and HR Analytics

Nestle USA

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; NESN; Food & Beverages industry

July 2008 – March 2012 (3 years 9 months) Glendale, California

Lead Talent Acquisition for Nestlé USA, a $12 billion consumer products company. Built a Center-of-Expertise (COE) that supports 19 Nestlé divisions, 6 corporate offices, 23 factories, 6 distribution centers, and 50 sales offices. Guide a team of 40 human resources professionals. Report directly to the Chief Human Resources Officer for Nestlé USA.

• Transformed talent acquisition from a fragmented, reactive model with high agency costs to a COE with a defined service delivery model and metrics-driven approach. Introduced and implemented a new model that enables recruitment through analytics, recruitment marketing, technology and process improvement. .
• Fill 1500 positions yearly. Reduced agency usage from 9% to 1/2% of total hires. Improved time to fill by 32% while achieving 4.5 (scale of 5) quality-of-hire score.
• Developed and implemented a campus recruitment strategy that focuses on a tiered university approach, social media, and diversity. Supported the recruitment for nine functional developmental programs.
• Lead the strategy of the Company’s employment branding as it relates to recruitment. Execute against the entire candidate experience including new employment website, social media, employment collateral, event management and offer process.
• Direct the recruitment integration of two newly acquired companies into Nestlé USA. (Nestlé USA recently acquired Dreyer’s Grand Ice Cream and Kraft’s Pizza business.) Transitioned all hiring processes for field and corporate locations, implemented recruitment technology (Applicant Tracking System), and initiated recruitment services.
• Built the HR Analytics department, a group responsible for the development of systems, metrics, data collection methodologies and reports to enable HR to effectively assess and manage the workforce. Developed and introduced the Company’s first Human Resources Scorecard and a self-service web-based human resources metrics tool.

Human Resources Director, Logistics Division

Nestle USA

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; NESN; Food & Beverages industry

October 2006 – July 2008 (1 year 10 months)

Directed human resources for the Nestlé USA Logistics division, an organization responsible for the planning, deployment, distribution, transportation and customer service of Nestlé’s products. Responsible for six distribution centers, four customer service centers, five private fleet terminals, a centralized transportation department, and a corporate planning and operations research organization. Worked closely with executive team to develop a culture of talent development and sharing.

• Streamlined the HR delivery at 14 field locations through new applicant tracking, timekeeping, and attendance processes and systems. Achieved a .78 year breakeven point and annual cost savings of $380,000.
• Oversaw division compensation project including the compilation of all updated job descriptions, analysis of market data, and execution of strategy to address individuals whose compensation fell well below the market median.
• Developed and implemented the Logistics Training Cycle, a seven course series covering the supply chain process at Nestlé USA.
• Establish a talent assessment and planning program that was later adopted by the entire Nestlé USA company.

• Decreased turnover of Field General Managers from 21% to 4%, Assistant General Managers from 38% to 7%, and Sales Managers from 42% to 6%.
