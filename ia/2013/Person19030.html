Doug Butler's Summary

Dr. Doug Butler's expertise on humane horse foot care is built on his combined educational and work background. Dr. Butler has a Ph.D. in veterinary anatomy and equine nutrition and over 50 years experience as a practicing farrier. He has devoted his life to studying problems associated with horse foot care and simplifying essential information into usable solutions that he shares with his students and fellow horse professionals. He is dedicated to excellence in horse foot care.

Dr. Butler holds many unique distinctions; he is...
- one of less than 1% of farriers in the U.S. to have a Ph.D.
- one of 550 Certified Journeyman Farriers in the U.S.
*He is one of 158 Fellows of the Worshipful Company of
Farriers since 1356 A.D.
*He is one of 35 living FWCF's -- the first American citizen to
achieve it
*He is the only person who is a Ph.D., CJF and FWCF
Doug has won many state and national shoeing competitions, including the North American Challenge Cup Horseshoeing Contest, and he has been a member of the North American Horseshoeing Team 3 times.
His books, including "The Principles of Horseshoeing," are the most widely used in the world on the subject of farriery. A popular speaker, Dr. Butler has spoken at many national horse symposiums and has been a featured speaker at Veterinary Congresses in Argentina, Australia, Canada, Great Britain, and Switzerland. He has addressed the AAEP and the first Florida Association of Equine Practitioners Conference.

Specialties

Teacher, author, speaker, consultant assisting professional farriers and horse owners throughout the country with challenging cases, coach, horse and veterinary magazine consultant, member of National Speakers Association

March 1974 – Present (39 years 6 months)

Dr. Doug Butler is an internationally known farrier and professor with over 45 years of practical teaching experience. He writes about, teaches and practices the farrier's craft. Butler's motivation stems from his commitment to promote the welfare of the horse, especially in regard to humane horse foot care. Dr. Butler has authored over 30 books, including "The Principles of Horseshoeing" -- the most widely used farrier textbook in the world, as well as dvds on horse foot care and horseshoeing. Visit www.dougbutler.com to see current products. He has presented programs in 47 states and 8 foreign countries. He has been inducted into the International Horseshoeing Hall of Fame in Lexington, Kentucky and also named a "Farrier Industry Legend." He is the first American to become a Fellow by examination of the Worshipful Company of Farriers of Great Britain. Only 35 living farriers have the distinction of being classified as a FWCF. Dr. Butler holds a doctorate degree from Cornell University in veterinary anatomy and equine nutrition.
He served on the governor-appointed Colorado Horse Development Authority for seven years. He served on a six-member national committee writing humane horse care guidelines for the "Consortium for Developing a Guide for the Care and Use of Agricultural Animals in Agricultural Research and Teaching."
Works published by Butler include a chapter in "World Animal Science" (a prestigious multi-volume series generally recognized as the leading animal science reference) published by Elsevier; eight chapters in the "Horse Industry Handbook" published by the American Horse Council; and contributor to the seventh edition of Ensminger's "Horses and Horsemanship."
He is the recipient of numerous publishing and teaching awards, including the 3-time Journalism Award and Educator's Award presented by the American Farrier's Association and American Horse Publications' excellence in editing awards. He is dedicated to excellence.
