Barry A. Baker's Summary

• Over 20 years experience as an investment banker, valuation consultant, CFO and entrepreneur.

• Principal of CapVal-ABA, a firm that provides business valuation services for financial reporting and tax compliance, litigation support, and corporate transactions.

• Founder of The Cobble Hill Group LLC, a firm that provides outsourced business development services to private equity investment groups and their portfolio companies.

• Extensive involvement in international and cross-border transactions. Have lived and worked in New York, Tokyo and London and provided M&A and corporate finance services to clients in the U.S., England, Sweden, Belgium, France, Japan and China.

• Financial advisor and shareholder of consumer products company based in Guangzhou, China.

• Co-founded a manufactured home retailer with $75 million in annual sales, built primarily through acquisitions.

Specialties

M&A, business valuation, due diligence, capital raising, transaction structuring and negotiating, financial and strategic advice, planning and budgeting, cash flow management

Barry A. Baker's Experience

Principal

CapVal-ABA, LLC

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Financial Services industry

January 2010 – Present (3 years 9 months)

Principal of CapVal-American Business Appraisers, LLC, a firm that provides business valuation services for financial reporting, gift & estate tax, matrimonial dissolution, litigation support, buy-sell agreement, and corporate transaction purposes. We have extensive experience in the valuation of technology companies, tiered entities and other complex situations.

For private equity firms and their portfolio companies, we can provide financial reporting and compliance related valuation services comparable in quality to the Big 4 accounting and national valuation firms at a significantly lower cost. These valuation services include:
- ASC 718 (FAS 123R) / IRC 409A - stock based compensation
- ASC 805 (FAS 141R) - business combinations (purchase price allocations)
- ASC 350 (FAS 142) - goodwill impairment testing
- ASC 820 (FAS 157) - fair value

President

The Cobble Hill Group LLC

2003 – Present (10 years)

Founder of merchant bank that provides research-driven, outsourced business development services to private equity investment groups and their portfolio companies. Cobble Hill’s clients acquire companies with annual sales between $20 million and $125 million (or smaller if add-on) located throughout the U.S. and Canada. Cobble Hill helps its clients identify attractive industries and companies in which to invest, conducts direct marketing campaigns to contact company owners on their behalf, and screens interested companies to determine fit with financial performance, valuation and other criteria. Cobble Hill has also co-invested in companies acquired by its clients.

Co-Founder and President

American Home Centers Corporation

1998 – 2002 (4 years)

Co-founded a retailer of manufactured homes that operated twenty sales centers in the Midwestern U.S. with combined sales of approximately $75 million. Responsible for the valuation, due diligence, negotiation and documentation of seven acquisitions. Initiated and maintained dialogues with more than 60 other acquisition candidates and completed valuations and preliminary due diligence for the majority of them. Overall responsibility for most financial functions including financial reporting, budgeting, cash management, real estate, insurance, and investor and lender relations. Also served as Executive Vice President of American Home Centers Finance Corporation, a subsidiary of AHC, which specialized in providing mortgage services to buyers of manufactured and modular homes.

Senior Vice President

Prime Charter Ltd.

1992 – 1997 (5 years)

Co-founded corporate finance department that served emerging growth and middle market companies primarily in the healthcare, environmental, information technology and consumer product and service industries. Transactions included private equity placements, public equity offerings and mergers and acquisitions.

Vice President

Dillon, Read & Co. Inc.

Privately Held; 201-500 employees; Financial Services industry

1984 – 1992 (8 years)

Provided M&A, corporate finance and financial advisory services to a client base of multinational corporations. Based in New York for five years, Tokyo for two years and London for one year. Transactions included corporate divestitures, corporate sales and defense advisory assignments, buy-side advisory assignments, fairness opinions, public offerings, and private placements of debt and equity.
