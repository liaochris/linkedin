Dylan Tweney's Summary

Entrepreneurial journalist with 20 years' experience making complicated science and technology topics understandable to general and business audiences.

Editorial leader with a track record of building and growing audiences for online media properties while producing high-quality, award-winning content.

Organized and effective editorial leader, project manager, and operational problem-solver. Experienced developer, launcher and curator of new content products in text, audio and video.

Broad and deep understanding of computer, mobile, and internet technologies. Extensive understanding of the startup and venture capital worlds spanning several boom and bust cycles.

Dylan Tweney's Experience

Executive Editor

VentureBeat

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Internet industry

June 2011 – Present (2 years 7 months) San Francisco, CA

I am the "editorial chief operating officer" for VentureBeat, an independent tech news site that reaches 5 million readers every month.

I directly manage a news team of about 12 reporters and editors, helping them to write and publish daily stories that are speedy, accurate, innovative, informative, contextualized, well-written -- and which break news. I also write and edit a fair amount of copy myself. I also set editorial standards and expectations; organize newsroom workflow and beats; hire reporters and editors; set and implement our editorial calendar; oversee our social media strategy; and collaborate with tech, marketing, business development, sales, and event planning to ensure VentureBeat's editorial success.

Since I started at VentureBeat, I've grown the edit staff about 2X, while our audience has grown by more than 3X and our pageviews by almost 4X.

Publisher

tinywords

September 2000 – Present (13 years 4 months)

tinywords publishes one haiku or micropoem every weekday, on the web and via email and SMS.

I created this site in 2000, built a custom PHP- and MySQL-based content management system for it in 2003, and ran it singlehandedly for its first 10 years. I now manage the technical side of things (now running on WordPress, with some templates and plugins I customized) and help the editorial team.

tinywords delivers haiku to 3,600 subscribers around the world daily via email and SMS, and another 2,000 on Twitter, making it the largest-circulation haiku publication in English anywhere in the world. It has published some of the world's leading haiku poets as well as complete beginners.

Senior Editor

Wired

Public Company; 11-50 employees; Online Media industry

March 2007 – June 2011 (4 years 4 months)

I edited Wired.com's technology and gadget coverage, including the Gadget Lab blog (http://www.wired.com/gadgetlab) and Product Reviews site (http://www.wired.com/reviews). For most of that time, I managed a team of one assistant editor and three to four reporters.

In the first 3 years I was in this position, traffic to Gadget Lab increased 10X, making it the site's most successful blog, during a time when Wired.com's overall traffic increased about 2X. We also designed and launched a successful reviews site that also became one of Wired.com's most popular destinations.

During my tenure, I also edited Wired.com's business and science coverage, on the Epicenter and Wired Science blogs. In my first year at Wired, I built Wired.com's business beat via the Epicenter blog (later renamed Wired Business), tripled the blog's monthly traffic to over 1 million pageviews/month, and led the business team to win a national award for excellence in online journalism.

I frequently appeared in videos produced by Wired and in media appearances on major news outlets. I also produced and appeared in 116 episodes of the site's most successful video show, the Gadget Lab podcast, from 2008 to 2011.

Editorial Director, PCMagcast

Ziff Davis Media

Public Company; 201-500 employees; Online Media industry

May 2006 – March 2007 (11 months)

As part of the startup team, I was responsible for all editorial content of PCMagcast, an online channel for web seminars and online events on technology topics. We produced live webcasts (aka "webinars") and interactive virtual tradeshows, as well as short how-to videos.

As editorial director, I set the editorial vision for PCMagCast, created the edit calendar, recruited speakers, produced video segments, wrote and edited all copy (from event scripts to website text and direct marketing copy), and bore ultimate responsibility for all editorial event content. In addition I moderated many panels and appeared on-screen in our instructional videos.

In my year at PCMagCast, we won two Folio awards for excellence in online events.

Freelance writer and editor

Tweney Media

September 1999 – May 2006 (6 years 9 months)

Science and technology journalist, writing for a variety of publications including Technology Review, Business 2.0, PC World, CIO Magazine, InfoWorld, eWeek, SFGate, the San Francisco Chronicle, and more.

From 2000 through 2002 I wrote "The Defogger," a weekly (online) and monthly (print) column for Time Inc.'s then-startup magazine, eCompany Now / Business 2.0.

I have also performed contract content management, editing, and writing services for clients including Cisco, Kinko's, TeaLeaf, and RLG.

Executive Editor

Mobile magazine

September 2003 – September 2005 (2 years 1 month)

I was the number two editor at Mobile, a monthly magazine covering the whole gamut of mobile technology, including notebook computers, tablets, PDAs, cell phones, digital cameras, and more. I wrote, assigned, and edited news stories, reviews, and features. I also managed the magazine's website during most of 2005. Mobile had a circulation of almost 200,000 nationwide, and was part of Future Network USA (formerly known as Imagine Media).

Editor and Content Consultant

RLG

Nonprofit; 51-200 employees; Information Technology and Services industry

November 2002 – September 2003 (11 months)

As a contractor, I provided editing and content consulting services for the Research Libraries Group, a consortium of research libraries that is now part of OCLC. I edited the quarterly members' magazine (print) and several online newsletters, as well as reporting on RLG events for those publications. I also assisted with the redesign of the RLG website by providing analysis and QA on the content management system, website structure, and navigational text, and by writing and editing web copy.

Co-founder, chief operating officer

Utipia

October 1999 – December 2000 (1 year 3 months)

Created, incorporated, and eventually liquidated a content startup aimed at syndicating how-to content (tips and tricks) to a variety of commercial websites. Raised seed capital, wrote and revised a business plan, and (with cofounders) built a prototype. Demonstrated prototype at Demo 2000, a high-profile conference showcasing startups and new products. Got caught in the bursting of the dot-com bubble and failed to secure necessary additional financing. Closed the company in an orderly fashion and returned a significant portion of investors' money.

Senior Editor / Columnist

InfoWorld

Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Publishing industry

1996 – 1999 (3 years)

Over the course of three years at InfoWorld, I held a number of positions, including reviews editor, news section editor, and content development manager. In addition, I wrote the popular weekly "Net Prophet" column from 1997-1999, which covered the emerging world of e-commerce.

Associate Editor

PC/Computing

Helped plan, deploy, and manage the magazine's online presences on the Web, AOL, and other online services.

Assistant Editor

PC Computing

Edited how-to articles, tips & tricks, and Q&A features for the magazine's "Help" section. Contributed to reviews and features.
