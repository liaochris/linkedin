David Crowe's Summary

David has been involved in innovative software development since the late 1970s, real-time software development in the 1980s and early 1990s and, since the late 1980s, the development of telecommunications standards. He is also an expert on telecommunications numbering resources such as MIN, IRM, IMSI, ESN, MEID, UIMID and SID. He has a side interest in software and database development, both in computer protocols and speciality systems to manage data and enhance productivity. He was written extensively on wireless issues, with a unique ability to put complex technical issues in language anyone can read and understand.

Specialties

Wireless network standards, architectures and protocols. Government mandates including number portability, E911, priority calling and emergency alert services. Number resource management. Database design and implementation. Software development in C, FileMaker, Javascript, HTML, CSS, PHP and AppleScript.

  • Hybridization and agamospermy of Bidens in Northwestern Ontario

    • Taxon
    • November 1981
    Authors: David Crowe, WH Parker

    Four weedy taxa of the genus Bidens are common in Thunder Bay: B. cernua, B. connata, B. frondosa and B. vulgata. Since B. connata is intermediate in gross morphology and ecological preference between B. cernua and B. frondosa, and since it is only found in areas where B. cernua and B. frondosa are [both also found], an investigation was undertaken to determine whether B. connata is of hybrid origin (B. cernua X B.. frondosa).

  • NovAtel’s novel approach to CPU usage measurement

    • Software Practice and Experience
    • May 1991
    Authors: David Crowe

    This paper describes a set of CPU usage analysis tools, named CPU Probes, with the benefits of both hardware and software tools, and few of their disadvantages. CPU probes use a serial line unit or programmable interval timer as a clock to sample the CPU usage of a system, resulting in fast and accurate CPU usage estimates. CPU usage can be monitored for all processes simultaneously or as little as a single machine instruction. CPU probes are well suited for monitoring real-time system performance.

    • Atlantic Free Press

    Any parent’s nightmare is the death of a child. Yet, there is one thing far, far worse – to be accused of killing the child you have just started grieving. While the risk of a child dying is thankfully low, if it does happen, the risk of such horrendous accusations is significant if the death does not have an obvious explanation. The overwhelming influence of doctors on judges and juries, and the widespread belief that forensic evidence is unassailable make a false conviction quite likely.
