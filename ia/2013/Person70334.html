Judith HeartSong's Summary

I am a professional artist with 31 years of experience as a painter and muralist and now reside in the metropolitan DC area. I have painted large murals along the eastern seaboard, including a large mural at the National Zoo. I have maintained studio space at VisArts and a large studio in Glen Echo, Maryland.

I have licensed work with The Company of Women, and my art has appeared on t-shirts and posters to support the Dick Batchelor Run for the Children. Mr. Batchelor also presented one of my paintings to Hillary Clinton at President Clinton’s first inauguration. Since 2008 a series of limited edition prints of my work have been offered on Princess Cruise Line and since 2009 the limited edition prints have been featured on the Cunard Line Queen Mary 2. My painting PEACOCK CRIMSON has been licensed by Transformational Threads to produce a limited edition run of gorgeous thread paintings, and that piece has been featured in a trunk show at the Smithsonian’s Freer+Sackler Gallery.

I have served as the membership chair for the Women’s Caucus for the Arts in Central Florida and juried and hung numerous shows. I worked on the committee to organize the Winter Park Autumn Art Festival, and curated and hung Victims’ Rights Week Fine Art Shows with the Victim’s Services Coalition. At VisArts, I worked as the assistant to the director of exhibitions - hanging shows, maintaining the gallery, and creating collateral for exhibitions. Concurrently I began work as the office manager and then art director for the metro area corporate art consulting firm ArtMatters, where I have worked for more than eight years. I am currently serving on the board of directors of Capitol Arts Network as we bring a brand new arts center to Montgomery County, and have assumed the role of executive director in January 2013.

Specialties: Murals, Paintings, Mixed Media, Fine Art Commissions, Painting Workshops, Decorating and Design, Interiors, Licensing, Art, Artist, Painter
