Chris York's Summary

Chris is an industry recognized leader in delivering IT Service Management solutions. His innovative vision is rooted in deep experience. With over a decade of direct and relevant experience, he has proven capable of producing extraordinary results. He has worked with dozens of IT organizations to successfully help them to create true value for their respective organizations.

Chris has helped companies review, document, and ultimately optimize their IT Processes. The ability to fully grasp the process issues in addition to the technical issues is a key differentiator between Chris and others. As a self described “ITSM Evangelist”, he fully understands the symbiotic relationship between Process, People, and Technology.

Chris York's Publications

  • “Culture Eats Process for Lunch”, Panel

    • ServiceNow Knowledge 2013 Conference
    • May 15, 2013
    Authors: Chris York, Adam Mason, Paul Hardy, Eveline Oehrlich

    Traditional processes and tools are quite capable when used to manage faceless technology resources and the business of IT. But these same processes and tools fail to capture the essence of people and culture for IT success. Fortunately, modern ITSM technologies, combined with forward-thinking ITSM professionals, are adding capabilities to allow IT organizations to personalize what is typically a very impersonal IT experience. This panel discussion will address the growing realization that principles like social IT, advanced knowledge management, team collaboration and gamification will put some culture on the lunch table, and change the face of IT for good.
