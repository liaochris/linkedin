Jamie Morris' Summary

Voice. Heart. Vision.
Get it in writing.

I'm an experienced editor, book-writing coach, and presenter who specializes in developmental editing for novelists, short-story writers, and memoirists; (substantive) editing of non-fiction work; and book-coaching for first-time book-writers.

I can assist you in the discovery of the heart and shape of your story and the organization of your ideas and book-writing process. I can help you complete your book!

I also offer critique groups for book writers and a Training the Trainers program, which helps writing workshop leaders, teachers of creative writing, and critique group peers elicit deeper, more substantive feedback from students and peer writers.

Jamie Morris' Experience

Woodstream Writers (Sole Proprietorship)

Sole Proprietorship; 1-10 employees; Writing and Editing industry

January 2004 – Present (10 years) Southeastern United States

As Director of Woodstream Writers, Jamie Morris provides literary critique, book-coaching, and editing services to clients around the U.S. and across Canada. She also leads writing workshops and critique groups and offers presentations on literary craft and on the writing process at writers' conferences and for writers' groups around the Southeast.

In addition, she is available to facilitate team-building and creative problem-solving workshops based on writing-process and cooperative learning techniques. Consider her for your next corporate, non-profit, or service organization retreat!

Co-Writer

The Portland Tarot

June 2013 – Present (7 months)

As co-writer for The Portland Tarot project, Jamie Morris creates promotional materials, editorial and creative content, as well as ancillary and conceptual work, with deck creator, Theresa Pridemore.

The Portland Tarot, www.ThePortandTarot.com, is currently available in its first edition as an original, 23-card, Major-arcana only tarot deck, complete with Reading Guide and custom-designed three-card spreads. The deck was launched at Portland, Oregon's, 2013 World Domination Summit and reviewed on Aeclectic Tarot, www.AeclecticTarot.com.

Now, Pridemore and Morris are developing the art and companion book necessary for a complete, 79-card version of The Portland Tarot, for mainstream publication in 2014.

Co-Presenter

The Next Level: Weekend Craft Intensives for Dedicated Writers

April 2010 – May 2012 (2 years 2 months) Florida

Over the course of two years of weekend intensives, Jamie Morris, co-presenting with award-winning author Joyce Sweeney, offered writers support, instruction, accurate information, and the opportunity to practice skills that helped them take their work to THE NEXT LEVEL.

Each Next Level Craft Intensive was an engaging, instructive, experiential, market-savvy weekend focused on a particular aspect of the writer's craft, including,

* Understanding plotting and structure
* Revising to a theme
* Marketing to a specific audience
* Incorporating back story and sub-plot
* Building conflict and suspense

Program Presenter

Bloomingdale Life Story Writing Program

2011 – 2011 (less than a year) Tampa, Florida

Over the course of two visits to this Tampa-based writing organization in 2011, Jamie Morris presented intermediate-to-advanced critique and literary response techniques and approaches to both peer writers and a group of twenty writing workshop facilitators, expanding participants' "toolboxes" and offering them new approaches to creative writing and insights into non-confrontational, yet effective, critique for writers.

She also helped workshop leaders understand the purposes of analyzing plot in even the shortest narrative work as a guide to excavating hidden strengths and weak points in a writer's work.

Discussions with workshop leaders included evaluating literature at the macro level, with elements such as story scope, momentum, and architecture; the mid-level, with elements such as voice, flow, and style; and the micro level, with elements such as imagery, phrasing, and dialogue.

Instructor

The Knowledge Shop

January 2007 – December 2007 (1 year) Winter Park, Florida

At The Knowledge Shop, an independent center for education which catered to both the experienced and novice learner with a wide range of programs for personal and professional development, Jamie taught weekly creative writing workshops and literary critique sessions to writers at all levels of experience.

Topics included how writers can consistently generate new writing; support their writing practice; find their most authentic literary voice; evaluate positively and effectively their own work and that of their peers.

They then developed and revised their own short stories, working closely with the instructor and with a workshop "buddy," before presenting a draft for feedback in a full-class workshop.
