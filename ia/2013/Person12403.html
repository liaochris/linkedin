Bryn Hughes' Publications

  • The Use of Websites in Law Firm Marketing - Examining how corporate (B2B) law firms use their websites in marketing and business development

    • LexisNexis Martindale-Hubbell
    • October 18, 2012
    Authors: Bryn Hughes, Stephen Bairfelt

    Jointly undertaken with Stephen Bairfelt at Purple Market Research. The study examines how corporate (B2B) law firms use their websites as part of their online tactical activities to develop new business and retain existing clients. The study examines: the size of marketing budgets and the extent to which marketing professionals are employed, the balance between online and offline marketing activity, general website development and associated expenditure levels, content management, tools used to measure online activity and website effectiveness and future plans with regard to online marketing activity.

  • Global Social Media Check Up - A global audit of law firm engagement in social media methods

    • LexisNexis Martindale-Hubbell
    • October 27, 2011
    Authors: Bryn Hughes, Heleana Quartey

    Jointly undertaken with Heleana Quartey and Burson Marseteller. The study examines how international law firms are engaged in using social media to help reach target audiences and maintain their competitive position. The study covers: An audit of social media tools used by top law firms in key cities across the world, an analysis of differences in use between regions, influence on business development efforts and communications with clients and best practice tips for law firms using social media.

  • The Selection and retention of Law Firms in Western Europe - Exploring selection criteria and the retention of preferred law firms amongst Western European In-House Counsel

    • LexisNexis Martindale-Hubbell
    • May 1, 2012
    Authors: Bryn Hughes, Mary Heaney

    Jointly undertaken with Mary Heaney at The Global Legal Post. The study examines some of the processes involved in the selection and retention of Western European law firms and aims to delve a little deeper to understand the reason behind successful, long-term relationships between external law firms and in-house teams. The study covers: Selection factors and reasons for reviews of panel firms and frequency, factors influencing the retention of firms for future work, top Reasons for the removal of firms for preferred panels/lists, approach taken by in-house counsel to evaluate law firm performance and common themes in feedback and value-adding elements of relationships.

  • The Selection and Retention of International Law Firms - Exploring the selection and retention of foreign law firms by organizations located in North America and Latin America

    • LexisNexis Martindale-Hubbell
    • June 25, 2012
    Authors: Bryn Hughes, Michael Bond

    Jointly undertaken with Michael Bond at The International In-House Counsel Journal. The study examines how companies based in Canada, the U.S. and Latin America select and retain law firms in international (foreign) jurisdictions. The study covers: proportion of external legal work undertaken in international regions, selection factors and number of firms considered, rationale for hiring foreign and local law firms, size of firm and types of work foreign law firms are used for, responsibility for law firm selection and termination of firms.

  • How in-house counsel in China select and retain external counsel

    • LexisNexis Martindale-Hubbell
    • November 1, 2010
    Authors: Bryn Hughes, Richard Parnham

    I have edited, or written, numerous reports for LexisNexis Martindale-Hubbell on how corporate clients select and retain external law firms. This is an example of a report I wrote, investigating the buying decisions of Chinese in-house counsel.

  • Global Client Feedback Initiatives and Best Practices

    • LexisNexis Martindale-Hubbell
    • June 28, 2011
    Authors: Bryn Hughes

    Jointly undertaken with Laura Meherg at Wicker Park Group. The study examines how international law firms are engaged in client feedback efforts and identifies best practices.

  • Canadian Client Feedback Initiatives - Survey on client feedback initiatives and best practices in Canadian law firms

    • LexisNexis Martindale-Hubbell
    • April 17, 2012
    Authors: Bryn Hughes

    Jointly undertaken with Laura Meherg at Wicker Park Group. The study examines how Canadian law firms are engaged in client feedback efforts and identifies best practices. The report examines: the percentage of law firms currently seeking client feedback, objectives and importance of client feedback initiatives, the methods by which law firms obtain feedback from their clients, attitudes towards feedback programmes, how firms share learned feedback and future plans for client feedback.

  • Lawyer to Lawyer Referrals: A Canadian Perspective - Examining the flow of referrals between law firms in Canada and internationally

    • LexisNexis Martindale-Hubbell
    • July 31, 2012
    Authors: Bryn Hughes, Richard Parnham

    Jointly undertaken with The Canadian Bar Association. The study examines trends in referring work between Canadian law firms, both in domestic and international markets. The study examines: the importance of referral work to firms' overall revenues, the type of work most frequently referred, the flow of inbound and outbound referral work, how law firms maintain their referral relationships and future prospects for law firm referrals amongst Canadian law firms.
