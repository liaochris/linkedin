Joshua Korenblat's Summary

I am an art director and writer with over ten years of experience in publishing: I have helped to found an information graphics/ data visualization startup, Graphicacy, and I have a combined seven years of full-time staff experience in the Art Department at National Geographic Magazine and in the Design Department at Science News Magazine. I have worked as a freelance art director, writer, and educator for three years as I pursued graduate school. Since 2007, I have been a faculty member at the Boston University Center for Digital Imaging Arts, where I teach communication design.

In work and school, I have carefully honed a set of creative problem-solving skills directly applicable for text and image-based projects. As an educator, I have learned to manage an array of personalities by identifying individual talents and finding an appropriate way to maximize motivation and productivity. These skills are a prerequisite in the classroom and in the office.

I prefer to work on creative projects that have an educational and storytelling focus. Though I am goal-oriented and well-versed in production and meeting deadlines, I am amiable, flexible, and work well with a team.

My work has been published by Boston Review Magazine, Houghton Mifflin Company, Prentice Hall, Congressional Quarterly Press, National Geographic Magazine, Johns Hopkins Penn Union Magazine, the Consortium for Ocean Leadership, Smithsonian Magazine Online, Science News Magazine, and Scientific American Magazine. At present, I am working as an artist for collaborative animations. These animations and motion graphics will be integrated into an educational film series about psychology, produced by Scientific American and Worth Publishers.

Specialties: Visual journalism, graphic design, art direction, illustration, digital imaging production, writing (expository essays, articles, reviews, literary non-fiction, and fiction), curriculum design for courses, and teaching
