Arlene Hauben's Summary

I have been a marketing writer and public relations professional for more than 30 years in New York City and South Florida. Many of my clients have been in real estate, engineering and construction, and technical disciplines so I am accustomed to writing about complex subjects for industry publications.

Currently, I am the editor of The Prepaid Press, a monthly print and online trade publication covering prepaid payment technologies, including mobile commerce, debit cards and gift cards.

My freelance practice, Scribe Tribe, Inc., concentrates on writing marketing communications for small and mid-sized businesses, and I work with design professionals to produce all types of presentation materials.

My PR blog pertains to getting publicity by creating news and milestone events. It's all about creating persuasive communications to highlight and reinforce a company's expertise. I believe in combining traditional public relations with web strategies and social media marketing and linking it all together. The goal is always to help businesses grow and make them more profitable and attractive to stakeholders and customers.

Specialties

I turn technical information into clear and understandable language for the general public and specific industry groups. For 30 years, I developed and managed business to business public relations campaigns for professional services firms throughout the US.

Arlene Hauben's Publications

  • Telecom Predictions 2013

    • The Prepaid Press
    • January 16, 2013
    Authors: Arlene Hauben, Tony Kumar

    Tony Kumar, CEO, Red Cherry/Sigma

    Prepaid Phone Cards

    Prepaid phone cards are contracting slowly due to several reasons. Rates are lower, people talk less, and fewer immigrants are coming to the US. VoIP enabled devices and Skype usage are increasing, cellular companies offer unlimited minutes to many countries, home phone companies allow customers to choose certain destinations unlimited for a few extra bucks. Don’t forget that the pinless and portals are eating away the phone card market.

    As for the prepaid wireless market, it does better each year. The majority of prepaid carriers now offer unlimited voice prepaid plans to their customers. Earlier we only witnessed smartphones and data plans offered on postpaid plans but now we see even prepaid plans are offering the latest smartphones and providing unlimited data plans. When such plans are available, you can imagine people running away from postpaid two-year commitment plans. About one out of every four wireless customers is on a prepaid wireless plan.

    Over the next year, there will be a price war among the wireless carriers in terms of data plans. People are getting used to using the cellular phones for watching YouTube videos, checking their emails, browsing the internet, and making VoIP calls. This all requires a lot of data bandwidth. Whoever offers unlimited data will keep the bigger slice of the pie.

    As for prepaid phone cards, since the market has shrunk, there needs to be fewer players; therefore, I believe there will be more acquisitions. Bigger companies will take over smaller sub-distributors. We have taken over multiple small size distributions in the northeast region in the last 18 months and I see the trend continuing.

    In addition to selling phone cards, which is our core business, Sigma group has plans to aggressively expand our existing pinless products and portal.
