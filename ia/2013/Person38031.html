Kari Williams' Publications

  • The Journey Back: How To Transition Back To Your Natural Curl

    • self published
    • September 15, 2012
    Authors: Kari Williams

    The Journey Back: How to Transition Back to Your Natural Curl is a quick, detailed read that will give you all of the information you need to feel confident in your transition to natural hair. This book not only covers the transitioning process, but also provides styling options and key product information to help you protect your newly natural hair.

  • Mini Tresses

    • Self published
    • December 15, 2012
    Authors: Kari Williams

    “Mahogany Mini-Tresses is a guide for parents that will change the way you think about your child’s hair. You will learn how to work with your child’s specific hair texture; how to avoid styles and tools that can increase stress and tension on their hair; and the ingredients you should look for in hair care products.This book provides useful hair care regimens with ways to further promote self esteem in children. We can have an infinitely positive impact on how our future teens and pre-teens care for their hair as adults by instilling healthy hair care practices now.”

  • My Hair Grows Up

    • Self published
    • January 15, 2013
    Authors: Kari Williams

    We (Dr. Kari & Carole) wrote this book to provide fun inspiration for children to love their natural hair. Lots of little girls want hair that looks like the dolls they play with, the characters in the shows they watch and like some of their classmates. This hair is typically long, straight hair. So what is a girl to do? Most importantly what is a parent to do when trying to explain to their precious little girl that her curly tresses are just as beautiful as the straight hair of her friend at school?

    The wonderful thing is that we are seeing more women on television wearing their natural curls, dolls are being produced with wonderful afros and now there is this new book, “My Hair Grows Up” that gives a very colorful, fun explanation about the differences between straight and curly hair.
