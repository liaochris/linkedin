Alexander Craghead's Summary

My name is Alexander Craghead, and I am a writer, photographer, and creative specialist from Portland, Oregon. I have a special interest in architecture, urbanism, and landscape, focusing on land use and transportation, industry, the effects of macro-economics on place, Pacific Northwest history, and North American cultural geography.

In addition to pursuing these subject areas, I frequently consult with non-profits, publications, small enterprises, and other creative individuals to help create and impliment projects and processes that get these clients closer to key goals.

Alexander Craghead's Publications

  • The Ghosts of Railroading

    • TRAINS Magazine
    • October 2012
    Authors: Alexander Craghead

    Uses text and photography to explore the railroad-altered landscape, with a special focus on industrial ruins abandoned in place in the landscape of Western Oregon, such as substations, yard offices, and railcar unloading facilities.

  • Portland Transit Challenge: Ride End-to-End and Every Mode

    • Railfan & Railroad Magazine
    • January 2012
    Authors: Alexander Craghead

    How well does a mid-sized city's transit system function when it employs three different modes of rail transport? Ride end-to-end of every line and mode of rail transit in the Portland metropolitan region in this feature.

  • Linking Tacoma: Finding Renewed Pride in a Streetcar

    • Railfan & Railroad Magazine
    • July 2011
    Authors: Alexander Craghead

    In this feature the Tacoma Link streetcar line is examined. First opened in 2003, this 1.6 mile line has helped to give rebirth to the urban core of this industrial city on Commencement Bay.

  • Bit by bit a line becomes a system: Amtrak Cascades has shown the nation how to build higher speed rail -- and do it right

    • Trains Magazine
    • April 2011
    Authors: Alexander Craghead

    This article examines how Washington State DOT lead the development of one of the fastest growing and most successful modern passenger systems, the Amtrak Cascades service between Vancouver, B.C. and Eugene, Oregon. Emphasis is placed on the pragmatic, incremental basis of the system's development, which runs contrary to the opinions of many passenger rail advocates.

  • The Portland Switching District Project: An Overview

    • National Railway Historical Society Bulletin
    • March 2011
    Authors: Alexander Craghead

    How does industry shape a city, and a city shape industry? This photographic project examines the relationship between street grids, urbanism, rail infrastructure, and urban industry. This article provided a condensed overview of the project.

  • Temples for a Forgotten Religion: The American Railway Depot

    • National Railroad Historical Society Bulletin
    • December 2010
    Authors: Alexander Craghead

    An essay on the architectural and social history of the American railway depot. This article examines the role of the depot as the progenitor of the franchised landscape, as a projector of corporate power during the Gilded Age, and as an allegory to cultural change in American society throughout the 20th century.

  • MAX at Night

    • Trains Magazine
    • May 2007
    Authors: Alexander Craghead, Dan Haneckow

    This article examines how the TriMet MAX light rail system operates between the end of service (near 1:36 a.m.) to the first morning trains (at about 4:00 a.m.). This article involved a behind the scenes look at TriMet's overnight line maintenance, the maintenance shops, and the dispatching center, all sensitive areas normally closed to the public. Co-authored with local Portland history blogger and transit observer Dan Haneckow, who originated the story concept.

  • The Grande Dame

    • Trains Magazine
    • November 2005
    Authors: Alexander Craghead

    This large feature story examined the role of Portland's Union Station as a meaningful transportation node for over a century. The role of various government agencies as its caretaker and their diligence or lack thereof became a central element of this story. This is the fullest story about Union Station as a functioning transportation hub written in the last 50 or more years.
