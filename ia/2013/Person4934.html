ALLISON BERGMAN's Summary

As a Theatre Director and Dramaturg, I am excited by plays that acknowledge the stage as a metaphoric space that gives voice to ideas and ways of being through storytelling. I search for plays that use language and action to engage an audience in the working out of human relationships – to others, to ourselves, to our world. I like to experiment with scale and contrast on stage. Genres and styles that attract me include musicals, comedies, and any plays using presentational, Brechtian, break-the-frame elements that also include the theatrical event in the story. I’m drawn to anything that contributes to the development of the form, or reaffirms theatre as a progressive art form that reflects and inspires the collective social consciousness of its audience. I prefer to work with writers who welcome collaboration, but have a strong voice. My most successful collaborations have been with writers who can see their words as a blueprint for the actual play. It doesn’t matter to me what stage of development a project has achieved before I am involved, provided I have something to bring to it, and the writer and I have a common language and clearly communicated expectations.

As an Educator, my general teaching philosophy is to introduce new ways of thinking that empower students to become innovative, collaborative, and focused artists, and to contribute to their overall realization as conscientious human beings. Through application, experimentation and discussion, I offer possibilities and techniques that challenge the students and encourage discourse. My classroom provides a laboratory space where students are given permission to fail and reinvent themselves repeatedly, with the understanding that I will also require them to be fully engaged in class work, and take personal responsibility for their level of participation and the ultimate success of their education.
