'SkyDive for Rhinos' National Campaign SkyDiving and Logistics Manager

African Conservation Trust

Nonprofit; 51-200 employees; Environmental Services industry

July 2012 – December 2012 (6 months) South Africa

Asked to take on the role of organising coordinating and managing the skydiving element of the national campaign, I donated my time and expertise to the cause, and became heavily involved in all aspects of the ‘SkyDive for Rhinos’ 2012 campaign on a full time basis for six months.

The ‘SkyDive for Rhinos’ 2012 Campaign grew into the largest and most successful public fund raising and awareness campaign for South Africa’s endangered Rhinos, and became the largest concerted effort for a good cause in South African skydiving history.

The campaign saw five events in five provinces over two months, with an additional sixth event being added due to popular demand. The campaign also received special permission to be included at the Margate, Virginia, and Africa Aerospace and Defense Airshows.

Numerous public figures, sporting stars, actors, actresses, models, television and radio personalities, presenters, schools, colleges, universities, pensioners, archaeologists, producers, DJs, artists, conservationists, Company Directors and CEOs participated, and three young men even skydived naked for the cause.

The campaign was featured in (and often on the front page of) local, provincial, and national newspapers (including the front page of the Sunday Times); in various magazines; in on-line blogs, websites, and e-newsletters; on numerous local, and several national radio stations; on national television; and live on SABC Morning / Weekend Live when SABC Sports presenter Vaylen Kirtley made a Tandem ‘SkyDive for Rhinos’ on World Rhino Day.

The SkyDive or Rhinos 2012 campaign raised R 7 Million for strategic Rhino anti-poaching efforts in South Africa.

SkyDive Rustenburg

2010 – 2012 (2 years) Rustenburg Area, South Africa

SkyDive Rustenburg is a Parachute Association of South Africa (PASA) Sports Skydiving Operation providing a facility for Sports Skydiving, Tandem Introductory Skydiving, Student Training and Progression.

Introducing a turbine Atlas Angel to the operation I was responsible a total of 6650 individual descents, 73 night jumps, 118 display descents, 653 tandem skydives, and the training of 72 students over 147 days of operation with an average of 40 descents per day of operation.

Expedition Photographer / Digital Mapping / GEO Referencing Data to Location

Boundless Southern Africa Expedition

May 2009 – September 2009 (5 months) Southern Africa

The Kingsley Holgate led 'Boundless Southern Africa Expedition' was an initiative to link and unite ‘Nature, Culture and Community’ through nine Countries, seven Transfrontier Conservation Areas, more than thirty National Parks and Nature Reserves, and the Communities living within or adjacent to these areas.

It was a diplomatic FIFA Legacy Project in the run up to the 2010 South African Soccer World Cup.

Driving a kitted out Land Rover 90 I was the expedition photographer, responsible for digital mapping and GEO referencing data to digital mapping, I compiled 360x360 images for incorporation into Google Earth, and compiled information and route detail for Garmap and Peace Parks.

National Geographic Explorer and Expedition Leader Kingsley Holgate described the route as ‘one of the greatest 4x4 adventure routes in the world’.

Community initiatives included the upgrading of some 30 soccer pitches, community soccer challenges, community art competitions, distribution of water carrying school lap top desks, water purifying life drinking straws, mobile community box libraries, wildlife quizzes, execution of the Right to Sight campaign, One Net One Life malaria prevention education and mosquito net distribution to pregnant women and mothers with young children.

It was a life changing experience.

Jump Garmin SkyDiving Team

2007 – 2009 (2 years) South Africa

A high-profile professional public display parachuting team, The Jump Garmin SkyDiving Team performed displays at major motoring, marine, sport and fitness, and other high profile events from the Dusi, to the Midmar Mile, Round The Island Yacht Race, SA Navy Festival, and Cape Epic, to Airshows, Flyins, Aerobatic and Gliding Championships, Sun City Spring Breaks, Motor Shows, Boat Shows, the KTM Rally Raids, the G4 Challenge, Mountain Biking events, street renamings, and high profile events and launches around the country.

Director

FullHouse Imaging

2000 – 2008 (8 years) South Africa

Armed with an early digital camera and some ground breaking software I brought 360x360 imaging capabilities to market, and was part of the digital imaging revolution in South Africa.

Over a period of 8 years, eventually with 3 partners and a team of 10, we grew FullHouse Imaging into a successful specialist digital imaging and digital media presentations company providing a range of digital imaging products and services from satellite imagery through aerial photography to flat photography; 360x360 imaging; virtual and actual 3d modeling; time lapse creations; animations; virtual fly-throughs; and interactive digital media presentations to an extremely diverse array of clients.

FullHouse Imaging provided specialist services to major agencies and organisations, including South African Tourism; Gauteng Film Office; Sport and Recreation South Africa; National Research Foundation; and a number of South Africa’s top educational institutions.

We 3d modeled the Rooivalk attack helicopter in flight; designed and presented the University of Johannesburg’s first open day presentation; provided specialist digital imagery for Wits, UJ and UP; for KIA, Daimler Chrysler, Toyota, and Harley Davidson; provided imaging, presentations and interactive multimedia presentations for A1GP Team South Africa, Sun International, Protea Hotels, 3 Cities, Wilderness Safaris, Forever Resorts and more. We performed specialist-imaging assignments and completed various projects across Southern Africa for numerous organisations, companies, groups and institutions, including the Reserve Bank, and even Teazers!

Our catch phrase started out as “We immerse ourselves in your environment to give you the best possible visual results”, and developed into “More Information, More Interaction and a Richer Visual Experience…”
