Gerald Klickstein's Summary

Veteran performer, educator, and career coach with more than 30 years of experience in higher education, training musicians to be successful artists and professionals.

Director and Founder of the Music Entrepreneurship & Career Center at the Peabody Institute (since July 2012). Previously, Professor of Music at the University of North Carolina School of the Arts (1992-2012).

Also a sought-after speaker, writer, music career coach, and consultant.

Author of The Musician’s Way (Oxford 2009), The Musician's Way Blog, and diverse articles about music making, creativity, entrepreneurship, collaboration, career development, and living the contemporary musician's life.

Volunteer service includes 2-year terms on the Board of Directors of the American String Teachers Association and on the National Task Force on the Arts in Education organized by the College Board. Currently serving on the College Music Society Committee on Careers Outside the Academy.

Specialties: Music performance and entrepreneurship education; career counseling; workshops on music career development, practice, collaboration, creativity, music curriculum, occupational health, overcoming performance anxiety; classical guitar performance and instruction.

Gerald Klickstein's Publications

  • The Musician's Way - A Guide to Practice, Performance, and Wellness

    • Oxford University Press
    • August 2009
    Authors: Gerald Klickstein

    In The Musician's Way, veteran performer and educator Gerald Klickstein combines the latest research with his 30 years of professional experience to provide aspiring musicians with a roadmap to artistic excellence. Part I, Artful Practice, describes strategies to interpret and memorize compositions, fuel motivation, collaborate, and more. Part II, Fearless Performance, lifts the lid on the hidden causes of nervousness and shows how musicians can become confident performers. Part III, Lifelong Creativity, equips musicians to tap their innate creativity, build careers, and prevent music-related injuries.
