Christopher Cocca's Summary

Christopher Cocca's fiction, nonfiction, and poetry have appeared in/at Brevity, elimae, Pindeldyboz, Geez Magazine, Creative Nonfiction, Generate, and elsewhere. He writes for the religion and media verticals at The Huffington Post, helps conspire the broken liturgy project, and is the author of What Other People Heard When I Taught Myself To Speak.

Chris earned an MDiv from Yale Divinity School in 2005 and an MFA in fiction at The New School in 2011.

If you're interested in talking to Chris about a short or long-term creative project related to the sharing of good news and the spreading of social justice through the artful telling of important stories, contact him here. He also considers requests for speaking and facilitation engagements and has experience in corporate training and adult education.

Specialties: communications, public speaking and teaching, community outreach, writing, blogging, social media, networking.
