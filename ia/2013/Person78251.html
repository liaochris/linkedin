Lane Becker's Summary

tl;dr: Likes the Internet. Fun at parties.

Right now I'm working to support my New York Times bestselling book "Get Lucky: How to Put Planned Serendipity to Work for You and Your Business," written with my business partner and braniac friend Thor Muller, bouncing globeward giving presentations and workshops on the subjects covered in the book. Get in touch if you'd like one or both of us to speak to your organization about bringing more serendipity into your office environment. We have variations on our presentations specific to technology companies, educational institutions, medical companies, retail organizations, and non-profits. We're informative, direct, and have mastered the subtle art of prop comedy.

Prior to the book, I was co-founder and President of Get Satisfaction, a web startup dedicated to fostering new methods of communication and collaboration between companies and their customers. Get Satisfaction pioneered the enterprise social CRM space, and though I'm no longer with the company on a daily basis, it's still going strong with over 67,000 companies using it this very minute.

Before Get Satisfaction, I co-founded Adaptive Path, a user experience strategy, research, and design consultancy, known for, among other things, coining the technology terms "blog" and "ajax." While at Adaptive Path, I ran the consulting business and, as the creator of the New Ventures program, partnered with early-stage startup companies to provide them with UX support. While at Adaptive Path, we also built Measure Map, a blog analytics tool that was sold to Google in 2006 and became part of Google Analytics.

I love, in no particular order, kayaks, fancy cocktails, nonfiction about the current state of the world and where it might be headed, pool parties, scenic vistas, modern neuroscience and the ways it helps us better understand our interior state, front porches, traveling, public parks, mindfulness, and getting to know other people and finding out what they love most.

Specialties

Product development and product management; partnerships and business development; market strategy; user experience research, strategy, and design; investment fundraising; patience; disasters, both creation and recovery.

Lane Becker's Experience

Founder and Chief Product Officer

The Original Internet Company, Inc.

August 2012 – Present (1 year 4 months) San Francisco, CA

Our first product, Original, is a revolutionary new way to share your work.

The way we work has changed radically in the last decade, but the tools we use to represent our work have not kept up. Resumes are outdated, static documents of limited utility. Portfolios are complex to create and niche-oriented. Original shows projects and examples, is real-time and highly social, and recognizes that reputation, not affiliation, is the key driver of a person's success.

Working with Thor Muller, one of my cofounders from Get Satisfaction, and Keith Messick, our VP of Marketing at Get Satisfaction and former CMO and CEO of Keas.

Author

"Get Lucky: How to Put Planned Serendipity to Work for You and Your Business"

April 2012 – Present (1 year 8 months) San Francisco, CA

I coauthored, along with Thor Muller, "Get Lucky: How to Put Planned Serendipity to Work for You and Your Business," published by Jossey-Bass, a imprint available, available as of April 17, 2012 at fine bookstores and e-bookstores everywhere.

"Get Lucky" is a manual for serendipity: What it is, how it works, and how to put it to work for you. As the pace of change accelerates around our businesses and the sheer volume of information explodes, we're under incredible pressure to connect just in time with the people and ideas we need to thrive, even when we don’t know who they are or where they might be. This uncertainty isn’t a problem, however—it brings tremendous opportunities, but only if we embrace one of the most important drivers of success in the modern economy: serendipity.

Serendipity isn’t an abstract, magical notion—it can be planned. Though we can’t know exactly when serendipity will strike, we can foster the conditions for it to occur early and often. Luck isn’t lucky—it's learnable. "Get Lucky" will show you how the world’s most admired businesses have applied the 8 essential skills of serendipity.

It's pretty awesome. You should buy a copy. Or seven.
