James Greenfield's Summary

James Greenfield is committed to the principal of design-by-team through an open and common sense approach that supports the goals of their clients. Listening, consensus building, and the clarification of needs vs. wants are keystones to his client centered approach. His extensive experience provides in depth knowledge for the development of design solutions for education, hospitality, corporate, research and development, medical and professional services clients.

As the President and Owner of Greenfield Architects, Jim leads the firm’s overall management and design of architectural and interior projects. They create people centered designs by utilizing their experience to visualize the whole project before offering an architectural solution.

Clients work with Jim and Greenfield Architects:
- For their creative innovation, solutions based design, and whole building approach
- For their start to finish involvement and service delivery
- For their wisdom, experience, likability, integrity, and common sense approach.

Greenfield Architects has worked with such notable clients as Kiewit Infrastructure, Qwest Diagnostics, Cassidy Turley, Elements, and Red Rocks Country Club and many others.

Jim received his Bachelor of Architecture from Iowa State University and completed the Green Building Certificate Program from Colorado State University. He holds a license to practice Architecture in Arizona, California, Colorado, Nebraska, Oklahoma, and Wyoming. He is a certificate holder with the National Council of Architectural Registration Boards (NCARB). He is a member of several professional organizations, including The American Institute of Architects, The National Council of Architectural Registration Board, The International Code Council, and the Colorado chapter of the United States Green Building Council.

Jim served on The Knolls HOA Board of Directors from 2008 through 2010 and on the Board of Directors for the Veterans Project from 2009 through 2012.
