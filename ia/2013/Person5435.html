Amanda Visconti's Summary

I’m a digital humanist hybrid: a CMS geek, games researcher/designer, and literature Ph.D. candidate with a master’s degree in information from the University of Michigan. I pull my backgrounds in information studies and web design into my literary and textual scholarship by building, testing, and theorizing code, interfaces, and websites towards realizing more participatory online scholarly editions, reimagining reading and learning through visual design, play, pedagogy, and user studies.

I have a diverse but interwoven skillset, including roles as a:

professional web designer and developer
...as the Maryland Institute for Technology in the Humanities (digital humanities think tank) Webmaster (Summer 2011-Spring 2013), an IMLS Model Digital Humanities Intern (Summer 2009), and major CMS geek (Wordpress, Drupal, Omeka... all the time!)

serious games researcher/developer
...as a member of an NSF-funded research team studying alternative reality game (ARG) design and pedagogy, and co-organizing the first THATCamp Games digital humanities game studies unconference (January 2012)

Master of the Science of Information (M.S.I.) from the University of Michigan School of Information
... with a digital humanities focus, where I worked in diverse information science positions including as a special collections librarian, digital archive research assistant, contextual inquiry consultant, and games archive assessor

Ph.D. Candidate in Literature at the University of Maryland
... where I'm coding a set of innovative tools for my digital dissertation

Amanda Visconti's Projects

  • Digital Dos Passos

    • September 2010 to June 2011
    Team Members: Amanda Visconti

    A digital engagement with the media of Dos Passos' U.S.A. trilogy, using the Omeka CMS with a customized non-linear entry into the collection's items.

  • ARGs in the Service of Education and Design

    • September 2010 to Present
    Team Members: Amanda Visconti, Elizabeth Bonsignore, Derek Hansen, Ann Fraistat, Kari Kraus, Marc Ruppel

    Member of an interdisciplinary research team that both researches, creates, and runs alternate reality games (ARGs) in the service of education and design knowledge; our main ARG is the Arcane Gallery of Gadgetry (AGOG). By incorporating counterfactuals and re-imagining the past, AGOG is designed to lead players into a newly enfranchised relationship with history, teach them STEM and information literacy skills, and help them discover the secret stories outside most history books.

  • UlyssesUlysses

    • August 2008 to July 2009
    Team Members: Amanda Visconti

    A prototype participatory edition of James Joyce's Ulysses for first-time readers, containing annotated text and reading lessons for the first two chapters of the novel (Telemachus and Nestor). This site is now a very dated representation of my technical skills, but demonstrates my early interest in participatory digital design, interface, and experimentation with digital edition annotation, toggling, and other ways of moving beyond the variorum with information overabundance and scholarly editions.
