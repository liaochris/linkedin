David Gerster's Summary

As a data guy, I've gotten to work on some pretty cool stuff. At Yahoo, I led the project to collect billions of URL "clickstreams" from Toolbar and record them in Hadoop, data which researchers used to improve web search ranking. I'm proud to have this recommendation from a member of the Y data science team, who worked on this project with me:

"David is an enabler for researchers, and works tirelessly to build systems that can be leveraged in multiple ways. He has the vision and foresight to push for the right things, and the determination to deliver them even when it requires shifting a company's corporate inertia to do so."

At Yahoo, I was also fortunate to work with web performance guru Steve Souders, who asked me to measure page load times as part of the Toolbar project. As a result, Y now has billions of page load times stored in its Hadoop infrastructure--one of the largest repositories of actual page load times measured from the client side. (Interestingly, page load time was also useful as a feature for search ranking, indicating that web sites with better infrastructure tend to have higher quality content.)

At Microsoft, Omar Alonso and I stumbled on the idea of using human computation to create features (instead of just labels) in training sets for supervised learning. Such "faux features" could illuminate which signals are potentially useful, without having to actually code them. This could save significant engineering time, since you would know in advance which features are actually worthy of machine computation. Someday, I'd love to explore this idea further.

Finally, at Groupon I thoroughly enjoyed working on Mobile experimentation. My team created a state-of-the-art A/B test framework that computes statistical significance of metrics "on the fly" along user selected dimensions. Having fast, accurate stats gave the Mobile product team the confidence to launch radical UI improvements to Groupon's iPhone and Android apps.

Specialties

Analytics, Big Data, A/B testing, Mobile, Search, SQL.
