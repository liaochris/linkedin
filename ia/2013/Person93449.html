Michelle Flowers' Summary

Michelle Flowers is an accomplished leader and advisor with experience in K-12, undergraduate, and adult education, and over 18 years of success building support for highly visible programs and managing instructional teams. She offers a record of improving student engagement by implementing cutting-edge media and instructional technologies into classroom environments, with demonstrated skill as a counselor for K-12, undergraduate, and adult learners from diverse backgrounds. Her success in academic environments is informed by a recruitment and management career in the corporate and nonprofit sectors.

  • From the Soil Up: Community Change and Urban Beautification in a "Greene Countrie Towne"

    • Temple University
    • 1999
    Authors: Michelle Flowers

It explores how competing interests of separate community organizations, and different external private interests falsely assume that a "community" consists of one group of people with common goals, and agreed upon shared strategies to achieve those goals. It is not a study of either successes, or failures. Instead, it is an analysis of the social processes involved in a changing community.
