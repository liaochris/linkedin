Jackie Newgent, RDN, CDN's Summary

Jackie Newgent is a Brooklyn-based chef, registered dietitian-nutritionist (RDN), and media personality, and is passionate about plant-based cuisine. She's the author of 1,000 Low-Calorie Recipes, Big Green Cookbook, and the award-winning The All Natural Diabetes Cookbook. Jackie is a recreational culinary instructor at the Institute of Culinary Education, frequent contributor to Health, Shape, and Woman's World magazines, and freelance recipe developer for Food Network Kitchens. Plus, Jackie can be seen as host of healthy cooking videos on eHow.com and her unique recipe makeovers can be found on her blog, "tasteovers by jackie." Stay tuned ... her next book, The With or Without Meat Cookbook, arrives January 2014.

Specialties: Culinary Nutritionist (chef and registered dietitian-nutritionist); Cookbook Author; Recipe Developer; Writer; Speaker; Spokesperson

Jackie Newgent, RDN, CDN's Publications

  • The With or Without Meat Cookbook

    • American Diabetes Associaiton
    Authors: Jackie Newgent, RDN, CDN

    COMING MARCH 2014!

    Jackie Newgent, an award-winning cookbook author, will be releasing her fourth cookbook, The With or Without Meat Cookbook: The Flexible Approach to Flavorful Diabetes Cooking, in March 2014. It will be published by The American Diabetes Association and is perfect for pre-diabetes, diabetes, and heart health. Plus, it’ll be well suited for anyone who is overweight or simply wanting to eat cleaner and leaner. The With or Without Meat Cookbook will make it simple for people to savor the tastes and health benefits of a plant-centered eating plan. Each of the 125 recipes is vegetarian. Then, a non-vegetarian recipe “add-on” will be provided so that every single recipe can be prepared two ways—for both veggie lovers and meat lovers. So really, you wind up getting 250 recipes!

    The highlight: the flexitarian-style “add-on” is just that … it’s added on; so you’ll only need to take time and energy to prepare one full recipe. The add-on is an individual ingredient or extra simple recipe that will be incorporated into as much of the original plant-based recipe as desired. For instance, instead of making two entirely different recipes, such as chicken burritos and bean burritos, one recipe for bean burritos will be provided. The add-on will be thinly sliced pre-grilled organic chicken breast to easily be added to one or more of the bean burritos. That makes this cookbook perfect for couples or families that have both vegetarians and non-vegetarians in the household. The bonus is that the recipes are healthful for all. You don’t need to have diabetes to enjoy this flexible approach to cooking.
