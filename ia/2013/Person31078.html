David Gannon's Experience

Digital Marketing Manager

Fathom Events

Public Company; 501-1000 employees; NCMI; Entertainment industry

September 2012 – October 2013 (1 year 2 months) Centennial, CO

Working at Fathom Events, I digitally help market 65-70 individual, unique in-theater events per year. They range from The Metropolitan Opera Live in HD, a Josh Groban or Bon Jovi concert where fans can text in-theater questions to the band, or a Mayweather vs. Canello Las Vegas boxing event.

The exciting challenges to marketing these events are three-fold. One, you have to consider each of your genre audiences that all hang out on Facebook and your website. You don't market the same to someone that loves the Met Opera as you do to boxing or UFC fans. Two, I work with a fairly tight budget per event. And three, you have to get pretty creative - how do you keep each genre engaged during the times when you're marketing another's event?

Every post, every interaction, every event is a different, challenging opportunity. Our content calendars are not one page for Facebook and Twitter. Our content graphics were not just one size. Some were for highlighted posts, some were for Twitter or G+. The content calendars are 5-8 tabs with 4-8 posts per tab. That's a lot of writing and creativity for each voice, client and social channel.
