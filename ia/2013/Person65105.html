Jock COATS's Summary

I've been all over the place in my various careers so far! From Stock Exchange trading, to IT for investment management and now IT in Higher Education. After not quite getting into Oxford as an undergraduate, I have finally made Oxford my home, where I have now been settled for fifteen years with one short break where I considered becoming a Benedictine monk!

Now thankfully out of that, my busy out of office time is spent developing and promoting a new model for affordable home ownership called Community Land Trusts, and lobbying for such things as a "Wireless Oxford" project to create a socially owned wireless ICT infrastructure for the whole city.

I have been a city councillor, and may want to be again if the Community Land Trusts and other good causes eventually allow me the time to do it justice.

Specialties

Social enterprise, higher education, IT (desktop systems, Windows, Mac OS specialisms but also run my own servers investigating community building and content management solutions), local (in particular) political lobbying.

In content management software I develop using Zope & Plone and Drupal & CiviCRM in particular, so I have a smattering of Python, a bit more PHP and SQL skills as well as being experienced in running my own BSD/Linux based servers.

Jock COATS's Experience

Chairman & Co-founder

Oxfordshire Community Land Trusts

July 2003 – Present (10 years 4 months)

I started Oxfordshire Community Land Trusts, along with Fran Ryan and Tony Crofts and now a small group of others in response to the dire shortage of affordable housing and other kinds of property in Oxfordshire, UK. Drawing on inspiration from the Garden Cities movement and, further afield, from SEK co-operative housing in Sweden and a burgeoning Community Land Trust movement in the US, epitomized by the Burlington Community Land Trust in Vermont, founded by the then Mayor and now independent Federal Senator Bernie Sanders, we are developing and promoting a new form of tenure that allows first time buyers to buy into the home they live in through a mutual ownership structure, and with the freehold of the land being held by the community land trust so that it's future use and affordability remains in the hands of the community.
