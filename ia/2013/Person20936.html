Catherine Burr's Publications

  • Motherhood Moments by Catherine Burr

    • New Line Press
    • May 1, 2011
    Authors: Catherine Burr

    From fall to spring, Catherine Burr, co-author of the world-wide bestselling parenting humor book, Motherhood is not for Wimps, and noted Domestic Humorist, brings her wit and humor to the hardest job in the universe, motherhood. A combination of Dave Barry and Erma Bombeck, Catherine Burr writes these columns that anyone with children will be able to relate to! Motherhood Moments is filled with laughter, poignancy and encouragement, as Catherine writes from her own experiences as a mom and lets us in the world of motherhood that is cherished and flabbergasting at the same time.

  • The Cat With No Name by Catherine Burr

    • New Line Press
    • January 21, 2012
    Authors: Catherine Burr

    A cat with no name is born underneath an abandoned house and she and her mother and siblings live there until someone moves into the house and strange things start to happen, like her mother and siblings disappear! What will happen next to the little cat with no name?

    17 illustrations/pictures in this delightful children's story.

  • The Cat Who Went Lame by Catherine Burr

    • New Line Press
    • February 9, 2012
    Authors: Catherine Burr

    It started out like every other day for the cat with no name, that is until Samantha's best friend Ralph came over with his dog that liked to chase squirrels. Mistaking the cat for a squirrel (or was it on purpose?) the dog chased the cat out of the yard, not to be seen again for several days. Samantha, her mother, and Ralph set out to search for the missing cat. What they find is shocking.

    The Cat Who Went Lame is a coloful children's picture book with 25 illustrations.

  • The Cat Who Would Never Be The Same by Catherine Burr

    • New Line Press
    • March 1, 2012
    Authors: Catherine Burr

    Shadow Cat becomes jealous when Max the dog moves in. Max gets to go places and do things that the cat cannot. Thankfully, Shadow Cat has a second home nearby that she moves into. This picture book is book 3 in the Shadow Cat Series of children's books in the 1-6 age range.

  • The Cat Who Had Fame by Catherine Burr

    • New Line Press
    • May 17, 2012
    Authors: Catherine Burr

    Shadow Cat becomes famous after the Shadow Cat Series of picture books are written about her, she embarks on a publicity trip around the world, including being in a Hollywood movie and being interviewed by newsperson, Anderson Cat-per. Even though Shadow falls in love in Paris, she is lonely for her friends back home and soon decides to choose friends over fame.

    21 colorful pictures in this easy to read children's book, ages 1-5.

  • Silicon Secrets by Catherine Burr

    • New Line Press
    • July 6, 2012
    Authors: Catherine Burr

    Before Facebook, before Twitter, there was Nomadic Software, where stock options were handed out like candy and everyone was hungry. From Lucky, to Heather, and J. J., everyone had their goals and desires in the valley. Everyone wanted a piece of the pie, and everyone had secrets, Silicon Secrets.

    Take a step inside Silicon Valley, where stock options are the name of the game and lust, greed and avarice rule the day. European hunk Lucky is destined for great things, he can feel it in his blood. He just has to sleep his way to the top. Step in, Jennifer Jones aka J. J., who offers him a place in the hot bedroom and an enticing spot in the board room. Nothing will stop Lucky from success, not even love.

    Heather could care less about money but desires love and freedom, when she meets Lucky, she is instantly attracted to him but his desire for riches leads him in another direction. What transpires is a love triangle with many twists and turns and secrets in Silicon Valley as you enter into a world where stock options are king.
    Author Catherine Burr lived the Silicon Valley lifestyle and took notes at every Silicon Valley high-tech social event she attended and every woman’s league meeting she volunteered for. The result, an inside look at the life and world of Silicon Valley from the perspective of a high-tech executive’s wife.

  • Desires and Deceptions by Catherine Burr

    • New Line Press
    • June 9, 2012
    Authors: Catherine Burr

    Take a step back in time to the early days when Silicon Valley was transitioning from the Santa Clara Valley. Changes were happening quickly and those who were not part of the fast-moving high-tech world could easily be left behind. Marisa Clink was one such person who did not want to be left behind in a plowed up field. Even as a young girl growing up in Santa Clara Valley, Marisa could see the valley changing and what impact it had on her family. Her brother was called to the Viet Nam war and her mother was ill. She viewed money as a way out of what she perceived to be an impoverished life. Marisa stops at nothing to get what she wants, even if it means hurting those she loves the most. Desires & Deceptions by bestselling author Catherine Burr
