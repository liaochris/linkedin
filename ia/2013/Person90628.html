Volunteer

University of Wisconsin Hospital and Clinics

Nonprofit; 5001-10,000 employees; Hospital & Health Care industry

August 2010 – April 2012 (1 year 9 months) Madison, WI

I volunteered in the Family Practice/Forensics Unit (D4/6) every Tuesday evening from 5-8pm. My duties varied, but my primary focus was to assist the patients and care providers in any way I could. This typically involved delivering food, drinks, or other items to patients, answering patient call buttons, running errands for unit nurses, restocking supplies, and many times just talking with patients to improve their quality of stay. This weekly opportunity gave me a chance to interact closely with both patients and hospital staff, and furthered my understanding and experience in an inpatient setting.

Volunteer

Grinnell Regional Medical Center

Nonprofit; 201-500 employees; Hospital & Health Care industry

August 2009 – May 2010 (10 months) Grinnell, IA

I volunteered with the Food Services department at the Grinnell Hospital once a week for 4 hours. I helped the staff deliver meals to the patients as well as record their menu choices for the following day. In addition, I served as the liaison for their patient satisfaction department by carrying out short questionnaires. With this position, I had a wealth of opportunity to engage in conversation with the patients in the hospital and hopefully make their stay in the medical center as enjoyable as possible.

Research Intern

Grinnell College

Educational Institution; 501-1000 employees; Higher Education industry

May 2009 – May 2010 (1 year 1 month) Grinnell, IA

I conducted a research project entitled “Mechanical Changes Underlying Allostery in Neuronal Nicotinic Acetylcholine Receptors.” For ten weeks over the summer and continuing into my senior year at Grinnell College, I conducted experiments to determine the mechanism of information transmission between the canonical (Acetylcholine) binding site and an allosteric (Morantel) binding site to the ion channel gating site. As part of this project, I was one of two students to present our research in the poster session at the 2009 Society for Neuroscience Conference in Chicago.

Clinical Information Technician

May 2008 – August 2008 (4 months) Knoxville, TN

I assisted in the development and implementation of a program (MDRounding) used by physicians to create and view notes and charges for their patients during their daily rounds. Along with the technological support aspect of this position, I also assisted in the input of daily lab data and patient histories into the electronic system.

Lifeguard / Swim Instructor
