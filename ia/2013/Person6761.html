Andrew Altamirano's Summary

Andrew Altamirano's Projects

  • Detroit River International Crossing

    • September 2011 to December 2011
    Team Members: Andrew Altamirano, Isaac Epstein, Yi Li, David Coplon

    Conceptualized a new major bridge spanning the Detroit River with a main span of 2000 ft. Based on a review of the site, a curved cable stay solution was developed and analyzed for performance under dynamic loads, including wind, seismic, and cable loss.

  • Stress Analysis of a Gravity Dam on a Soil Foundation Using Finite Element Method

    • October 2011 to December 2011
    Team Members: Andrew Altamirano, David Coplon

    The class final project for Finite Element I involved students writing custom finite element code in MATLAB to model a dam under hydrostatic pressure. In the simulation, a tapered 60m height dam restricts water flow, thereby causing a horizontal hydrostatic pressure on the structure. In addition to the hydrostatic pressures, gravity loads from water and the concrete dam subject the soil foundation to stress.

  • Fracture Mechanics: Computation of Stress Factors in Mode 1

    • September 2011 to October 2011
    Team Members: Andrew Altamirano, David Coplon

    Using ABAQUS finite element software, stress concentrations around crack tips were modeled and numerically calculated to compare against theoretical values.

  • Design of Auto Plant Factory

    • October 2011 to November 2011
    Team Members: Andrew Altamirano, David Coplon, Evram Dawd, Peggy Seabright, Kathleen Walsh

    Class project to design a two-bay auto factory in NJ. Plans included column-free bays with 50 ton capacity cranes using trusses at 25' spacing. Wind and snow analysis was conducting according to ASCE 7-05 criteria.

  • Design of Concrete Box Girder Bridge

    • November 2011 to December 2011
    Team Members: Andrew Altamirano, David Coplon, Evram Dawd, Peggy Seabright, Kathleen Walsh

    Class project to design reinforcement rebar for a concrete box girder bridge (no pre-stressing) using standard AASHTO HSS-20-44 loads. Influence surfaces were calculated using SAP2000, from which total shear and moment envelopes were calculated along the bridge span for calculation of rebar and tie placement and sizing. Rebar placements and cutoffs were detailed in CAD.

  • Columbia Manhattanville Subgrade Master Plan

    • January 2012 to May 2012
    Team Members: Andrew Altamirano, Hillary Cheng, Harrison Chiu, David Coplon, Lacey Reddix, Danielle Rubin, Shoshana Spellman, Richard Strickland, Jane Zellar

    Developed a master plan for the combined subgrade of Columbia University's Manhattanville expansion. Our team developed programmed space for buildings and and support facilities, including central energy, delivery, and parking. LEED credits from the subgrade help achieve Columbia's goal of an overall LEED-ND Silver rating.

  • AISC Steel Bridge Competition

    • November 2011 to April 2012
    Team Members: Andrew Altamirano, Megan Ng, David Coplon, Jane Zellar, Edwin Li, Harrison Chiu, Franz Kogler, Noel R. Flores, EIT

    Designed and fabricated scale-model steel bridge to meet AISC competition regulations
