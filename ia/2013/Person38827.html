Dustin Sommer's Summary

Working as a development analyst at National Heritage Academies, I am involved in the development of facilities for our growing national portfolio of schools. Hired to fill the position of Architect at NHA to build an in-house design capacity, I have a unique opportunity to apply my diverse academic and practice backgrounds in architecture, engineering, and real estate development. I work on project teams at multiple stages conducting site selection/due diligence, conceptual design, space comparison analysis, as well as some construction/operations/maintenance responsibilities.

This past May, I completed my Master of Architecture degree at the University of Michigan as well as the Certificate of Graduate studies in Real Estate Development. My primary focus and interest is synthesizing architecture, engineering, and business in the development of walkable urban places. Most notable to this, I competed as a national finalist in the Gerald D. Hines Urban Design Competition against more than 160 teams from Universities across North America with our teams $400M redevelopment proposal in the Rainier Valley in Seattle WA.

During engineering school, I completed three strong Civil Engineering internships in three different regions of the United States. I have broad experience in leadership through extra-curricular activities leading a campus tradition and expanded it into a university program responsible for generating and expending its $50,000 per year budget. I also was successful at improving campus by raising and implementing over $70,000 of improvements that resulted in a major green space addition to the heart of campus as well as upgrades to the waterfront.

It is my goal to work in real estate development utilizing my backgrounds to implement lasting positive change.

Specialties

Walkable urban development, program management, site engineering, transportation engineering, public transit planning, and asset management.
