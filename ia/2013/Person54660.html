Mark Harlien's Summary

Founded Ideawire in 1998 initially in response to the demand for quality design and savvy web programming in one company. Since then, Ideawire has launched over a hundred websites, authored over 100,00 lines of code, and provided highly-successful, results-oriented print, online and visual communication for a variety of industries.

Ideawire is an award-winning firm. It's metrics for success are:

1) The successes of it's clients (such as USAA and Cargill, whose successes are industry-recognized),

2) The IABC Quill Awards. These awards are results-based honors and require proof and measurement of project successes. Ideawire has garnered over a dozen Quill awards, including three international Gold Quills, considered "the Oscars" in the communication business, and

3) The Ragan Awards - also a results' based recognition contest. Ideawire has won several.

Both the Quill and Ragan contests require proven measurable results; design and creative are secondary measures (though in Ideawire's case, as typically responsible for the measured successes).

Mark started Ideawire in 1995 and coded his first website in 1996. As such, Ideawire is one of the most experienced web development and online strategy firms in San Antonio - few can match Ideawire's longevity in online work. It has worked with both large Fortune 500 companies and small start-ups. Ideawire carries an equal expertise in traditional media, such as video production, print and brand placement.

Specialties

Online/mobile strategy, custom web application programming, marketing strategy and fulfillment, custom print and online publishing (including social media publishing) and all areas of fulfillment, including copywriting, photography and illustration.

Mark Harlien's Experience

President/CEO, Creative Director

Ideawire

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Online Media industry

April 1998 – Present (15 years 6 months)

I started Ideawire as a company equally versed in web, print, technology and marketing, to fill a void in the late '90s. I learned from my experience that there were two groups of people developing websites:

1) designers who couldn't program and
2) programmers who couldn't design.

I had learned both at the very beginning of the Internet explosion. I wanted to offer superior design and sophisticated programming, which was hard to find in the marketplace. I also wanted to leverage my overall talent - marketing strategy - with our services.
Soon, companies such as

• Advantage Rent-A-Car (the largest independently-owned car rental company in the U.S.)
• Cargill (the largest privately-owned corporation in the country)
• USAA

were added to Ideawire's roster of clients.

Another key advantage in using Ideawire is the marketing background. My degree is in Advertising and applies marketing strategy to many projects handled by the company.
Our measure for success focuses on the IABC Quill awards - a contest that focuses primarily on the success/ROI of the work we provide our clients. Since 2007, we have won over a dozen quill awards, including three prestigious Gold Quills. These are handed out only if our work meets or exceeds our clients' goals. We won two more in 2009.

Today, our company is one of the most experienced, if not the most experienced web development firm in San Antonio and Texas. We have authored over 100,000 lines of original programming code, hold the copyright to a significant code base, and continue to excel in areas such as Search Engine Optimization and Social Media - areas many of our competitors claim expertise in.
