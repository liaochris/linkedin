Jennifer (Jenny) Tucker's Summary

Jenny currently serves as Associate Deputy Administrator for the USDA National Organic Program. Prior to this role, she was an organization development consultant and group facilitator serving primarily scientific and technical organizations in public and non-profit sectors. Jenny holds a B.A in Environmental Science, an M.S. in Management, and a Ph.D. in Science and Technology Studies. She is the author of the booklet "Introduction to Type and Project Management," part of the Introduction to Type Series from CPP, Inc, and has been a Certified Project Management Professional (PMP) since 2005. She also coauthored "Reversing Forward," a practical introduction to Reversal Theory.

Specialties

Master Practitioner of the Myers-Briggs Type Indicator (MBTI); also qualified with the Strength Deployment Inventory (SDI), the Leadership Spectrum Profile (LSP), the Work Environment Scales (WES), Reversal Theory / Apter Motivational Styles Profile (AMSP), and FIRO-B.

Jennifer (Jenny) Tucker's Experience

Associate Deputy Administrator

USDA National Organic Program

Government Agency; 11-50 employees; Government Administration industry

August 2011 – Present (2 years 5 months) Washington D.C. Metro Area

The National Organic Program (NOP) is part of the Agricultural Marketing Service in the U.S. Department of Agriculture (USDA), and is a key lead for regulatory activities related to organic agriculture. As Associate Deputy Administrator, Jenny provides leadership for human capital management, organization development, project management, budgeting and contracting, training, and communications activities for the NOP organization.

Consulting Director

OKA (Otto Kroeger Associates)

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Management Consulting industry

April 2006 – August 2011 (5 years 5 months)

Founded in 1977, OKA is a training and consulting small business. While with OKA, Jenny led the company's organization development project portfolio, including organization assessments, work group facilitation, strategic planning, leadership and team development, communications, and training development and delivery. Clients included, among others, the National Cancer Institute, the Almond Board of California, Association for Professionals in Infection Control, the National Recreation and Parks Association, and the Agricultural Marketing Service. She also served as an Adjunct Faculty member for the Federal Executive Institute and Defense Acquisition University.

Associate/Sr. Associate

Booz Allen Hamilton

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; Management Consulting industry

October 2001 – April 2006 (4 years 7 months)

During her time at Booz Allen, Jenny served as a project and program manager for a variety of consulting efforts with the federal government. Favorite projects included acting as Principal Investigator for five year $5-million research study sponsored by the Department of Defense to explore the personality and group dynamics of information technology teams; and leading organization development consulting projects for a Defense information technology policy program, for technology assessment projects at the Census Bureau, and for information management workgroups at the Environmental Protection Agency.

Project Manager

ADI Technology Corporation (Now Dell Consulting)

August 1999 – October 2001 (2 years 3 months)

While with ADI, Jenny served as facilitator and consultant for a variety of workgroups focusing on topics such as environmental data quality, pollution prevention, marine mammal protection, and emergency management information systems. She also led environmental communications and training projects, including website development, web based training development, and outreach program design and production.

Policy Analyst

Versar

Public Company; 501-1000 employees; VSR; Environmental Services industry

June 1998 – August 1999 (1 year 3 months)

At Versar, Jenny facilitated and consulted to interagency data quality workgroups for the Defense Department and Environmental Protection Agency; led the creation of software to help Air Force Project Managers plan for and streamline environmental clean-up projects; and led a research study comparing the long-term cost and risk implications of different hazardous waste management decisions.

Geoscientist

July 1991 – August 1995 (4 years 2 months)

Jenny began her career wearing a hard hat and steel toed boots as a geoscientist for the environmental investigation and clean-up effort at Pease Air Force Base. In this role, she created field data management systems, performed sampling tasks, wrote technical and progress reports, and supervised selected field activities at sites across the base.
