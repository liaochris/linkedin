I teach behavioral statistics, a class covering all of the behavioral methods: descriptives, the normal distribution and percentile rank, odds-ratio, t-test, ANOVA, and chi-square. I assisted in reorganizing the course for the nursing program.

Graduate Student

University of Illinois at Chicago

Educational Institution; 10,001+ employees; Higher Education industry

August 2007 – November 2013 (6 years 4 months) Greater Chicago Area

I studied Behavioral Neuroscience with a particular interest in the visual system. I am adept at using psychophysical methods and can write my own testing software. I have generated models of visual cortex and examined how neurons might carry information given a specific input-output pairing.

I also took a great interest in statistics and at understanding how so much of the perceived world and ultimate decisions are based on the computation of statistics. I have an interest in neural encoding and how the properties of a neuron within a system can ultimately affect our decision (such as when and where we make a visual saccade).

Lastly, I dappled in computer vision and tried to apply what I learned about the brain into useful algorithms for car detection and tracking. I learned that it is an interesting but difficult problem! This research has propelled my interest in understanding the efficient methods utilized by the brain to do such tasks. The brain uses 30 watts of energy yet can do visual search and recognition 10000 times faster than your best supercomputer. I think that the answer lies in nature.

I funded my PhD as a Teaching Assistant. I have extensive experience in teaching Research Methods, Statistics, and upper level Neuroscience coursework.
