Erik Guilfoyle's Summary

In 1998 I acquired a position at Tres Media Group Inc. as an Internet programmer. As an active musician, I have an ear for audio and was able to offer my skills editing and mixing. When I wasn’t busy developing websites, I invested my time learning such tools as Adobe Photoshop and Lightwave for 3D modeling and animation. Later, I would become a partner and vice president.

While with Tres Media Group, I continued working in my family’s business, Guilfoyle Associates, filing personal income tax returns primarily for local residents within the Tri-State area.

At this time, computer games were becoming more mainstream and I took an interest in how they were developed. After producing some of my own modifications, I released an online compendium of tutorials. Now widely known in the modding community, MODSonline.com offers assistance and education to over 100,000 members for games including Quake, Half-Life and Call of Duty.

MODSonline led to a weekly podcast, MODSonair. Since July 2005, I've been hosting and producing a weekly live program about games and technology.

Seeking an authority on game modding, Wiley Publishing Inc. contracted me in 2005 to write a book. In August of 2006, I Published Quake 4 Mods for Dummies. In January 2007, my second book for Wiley, Half-Life 2 Mods for Dummies was also published.

In 2008, I helped create Mission Critical Studios, a boutique game development studio. With the iPhone gaining popularity as gaming platform, Mission Critical conceived Dr. Nano and Dr. Nano X. As an authorized Nintendo developer and having extensive PC development experience, I plan are to expand that portfolio as well as that IP.

Tres Media Group has also become well known as top producers of medical animation work for a variety of channels, making us not only highly regarded as an Internet development company, but as a 3D video production company as well.

Specialties

Games: Dr. Nano (iPhone/Mac), Left 4 Dead 1 & 2, Call of Duty 1 & 2 & UO & 4 & WaW, Crysis, Prey, Quake 4, Half-Life 1 & 2, Doom III, Battlefield 2, Unreal Tournament 2003, Quake III, Red Faction, Serious Sam, Tribes, Soldier of Fortune.

Tools: Worldcraft, Radiant, UnrealEd, Hammer, BF2 Editor, Unity3D, Adobe Photoshop, Adobe Premiere, Adobe After Effects, Adobe Flash, Sony Vegas, Sonar, Goldwave, Lightwave 3D, Camtasia.

Languages: HTML, Javascript, AJAX, PHP, MySQL, AS, C++, C-sharp.

Erik Guilfoyle's Experience

Creative Director, Designer, Programmer

Mission Critical Studios

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Computer Games industry

January 2008 – Present (5 years 9 months)

Mission Critical Studios is a game development studio. During this time, we have developed our own casual 3D game titled Dr. Nano which later lead to the release of an action game titled Dr. Nano X. Offered on the iPhone, this is a fantastic 3D journey into the human body. You are Dr. NanoTM, using future Nanotech to shrink down to the size of a red blood cell and treat your patients from the inside-out. While managing the development team, I designed and programmed various parts of the game.

In late 2010 I programmed a racing game for the ASH medical conference in Orlando, Florida. This game provided both product insight and fun for the thousands who attended and played the game. Uniquely, this was a Windows PC game played with Wii controllers.

I am a member of the International Game Developers Associates (IGDA) and Entertainment Association (ECA).

Owner/Founder

MODSonline LLC

January 2001 – Present (12 years 9 months)

MODSonline is a community of mappers, modders and gamers each supporting the other in their endeavors. Those endeavors however differ for us all ranging from mapping and modding to finding a way into the gaming industry to simply enjoying great games and the custom content others create for it. We are over 100,000 members strong.

In 2005, I began producing a weekly audio podcast discussing the news from a modders perspective. Composed primarily of game enthusiasts from the community, we talk about gaming and how it relates to the modding community.

In 2009, we added video to our weekly live broadcast all of which I host and produce.
