Carol Wiley's Summary

I’m a freelance writer with a background as both a technical writer/editor and a licensed massage therapist. I enjoy creating concise, useful, informative, and accurate written materials for a variety of purposes. My services include articles, website content, blog posts, e-books and books, newsletters, case studies, and technical documents.

My writing career began in 1990 as a technical writer for Boeing Commercial Airplanes, and I provided technical writing/editing services throughout the 1990s for companies in the Seattle area, including about three years at Microsoft. This background means I'm also available for writing or editing technical documents. (I will consider onsite contract work.)

After taking a break from technical writing and becoming a Licensed Massage Practitioner, I began web content writing in 2003, initially as a way to promote my massage business. I still maintain a website about massage and healthy living and another about aromatherapy. I also created a site, which I sold, about DIY home tips. In 2009, I became a full-time freelance writer.

I'm the editor of three traditionally published essay anthologies: "Martial Arts Teachers on Teaching," "Journeys to Self-Acceptance," and "Women in the Martial Arts." (I hold second-degree black belts in both Aikido and Tae Kwon Do.) Also, I was a co-founder, in 1994, of the Seattle Festival of Dance Improvisation (SFDI) and, in 2002, of the non-profit Dance Art Group, an organization that took over production of SFDI.

Please contact me about your writing/editing project or job. Whether you hire me to write short blog posts or edit a 200-page technical manual, your project receives the attention it deserves, and I take special care to work closely with you to ensure you receive what you want.
