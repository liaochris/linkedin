Quality and Regulatory Systems Management for the Production of In Vitro Diagnostics (IVDs) and Biologics for Infectious or Chronic Disease. Supervision of Quality Control and Quality Assurance Specialists. Leading Authority on all Quality and Regulatory Issues Affecting Principal Investigators, Project Managers and Functional Staff. Supervision and Leadership on all Validation Projects, CMC Documents Preparations and FDA Submissions, Qualifications, Metrology/Equipment Specifications, Batch Records, SOPs, Change Control, Non-Conformities & Out of Specification (OOS) Reporting, CAPA, FMEA, Internal and External Audits, Customer Relations, and Trending. Training of Employees in Regulatory Standards and Compliance.

Group Leader, Cell Culture Development

RenaMed Biologics, Inc.

2004 – 2005 (1 year)

Management of cell culture processes and approved materials for the development of a replacement human kidney metabolic device for the treatment of acute kidney failure. Development of the manufacturing process under FDA standards of Current Good Tissue Practice (cGTP) and cGMP for human cell, tissue, and cellular and tissue-based products. Development of serum-free cell culture media. Supervision of upstream cell culture development, process validation, scale-up, vendor qualification, and cost of goods reduction programs.

Senior Project Manager

Charles River Laboratories

Public Company; 5001-10,000 employees; CRL; Research industry

2001 – 2004 (3 years)

Coordinated the activities of 35-40 cross-functional employees in FDA-approved cGMP contract services for therapeutics protein production for human clinical trials for multiple cardiovascular or anticancer products including blood coagulation factors, antibodies, and oral forms of intravenous drugs. Coached three junior project managers.

Led technology transfer from clients to Primedica Labs. Interaction with clients to design and plan process scale up. Coordinated internal cross function groups in the scale-up, optimization, and validation of biomanufacturing processes for preclinical studies and cGMP production for human clinical trials. Approved all project changes and wrote and approved Interim and Final Project Reports. Coordinated with the client to prepare and assembly the regulatory documents needed for the FDA submissions and meetings.

Biopharmaceuticals Manager

Bristol-Myers Squibb Company

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; BMY; Pharmaceuticals industry

1991 – 2001 (10 years)

Coordinated process development activities on the successful develop of two molecules to the multi-billion dollar therapeutics marketplace: Taxol, a small molecule for cancer made in plant cells in culture; and Orencia, a protein-antibody fragment conjugate for rheumatoid arthritis which was manufactured using mammalian cells culture. Quality, and Project Management Experience.

Quality Manager, Development of documents for GMP manufacturing. Preparation of CMCs, SOPs, Batch Records, Trending Reports, CAPA, Change Control, FDA Audits for Biological License Applications (BLA).

Senior Scientist, Management of budget, personnel, and resources of Cell Culture Development Laboratories. Technology transfer from in-house research and outside collaborator for numerous therapeutics. Improved and increased scale-up of multiple process steps through cell line selection and subcloning, gene amplification, metabolic testing, and ingredients selection for serum-free, protein-free media.

Group Leader, Cell Biology

Houston Biotechnology, Inc.

1986 – 1991 (5 years)

Developed monoclonal antibody-based products from start-up phase into phase III Clinical Trials. SBIRs in cell signaling, polypeptide growth factors, extracellular matrix, wound healing in anticancer and ophthalmology areas. Ensured FDA GLP compliance for the use of animals in research, product safety, and toxicology. Supervised six employees in cell line development and cGMP scale-up. Developed inoculum processes for mammalian cell lines in suspension in spinner flaks or roller bottles, scale-up in bioreactors. Developed target cell screening assays with primary and differentiated cells attached to basement membranes, cell substratum, or extracellular matrix components.

Fellow in Laboratory Medicine & Pathology

University of Minnesota Medical School

1983 – 1986 (3 years)
