Aisha Ashraf's Summary

"I have to write. Writing's like breathing. How long can you hold your breath?"

An independent contributor to both print and online media, Aisha delivers cogent, well-constructed articles internationally, for UK broadsheets, high-end glossies and a range of travel and expat websites. Drawing on personal experience of expat life, inter-cultural relationships and mental health, she produces informative writing from a fresh perspective, injected to the eyeballs with enthusiasm.

Her singular voice and willingness to tackle issues that are widely ignored have earned her a loyal readership, while her strong grasp of social media and high Klout score mean her engaging content has widespread reach.

She's the author of an award-winning blog, Expatlog (expatlog.com), where she documents the vagaries and challenges of inter-cultural existence, campaigns to reduce mental health stigma, and records her progress in a research study for CAMH (Canada’s leading mental health research facility and one of the largest in the world), investigating mindfulness in depression relapse prevention.

In between scheduled commitments, she works on an autobiographical project exploring her experiences in the fields of mental health, and cultural and religious transition. Privately educated by Irish nuns, she has ample scope for reference.
