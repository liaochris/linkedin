John Pallister's Summary

Employment: John has taught students of all ages and abilities in an 11-18 school and adults in a Further Education College. John has developed and delivered school based in-service training programmes, and as a national Key Skills Trainer, has delivered training to staff in colleges across the northern region. He is a Functional Skills Trainer and presented on the national Functional Skills launch events in June 2008.

John has regularly delivered presentations and has contributed to, national and regional conferences including: 4th International Conference, ePortfolio 2006, "eStrategies for Empowering Learners", Oxford – October 2006; ePortfolio 2007, “Employability and Lifelong Learning in the Knowledge Society”, Maastricht, October 2007; 4th Edumedia Conference, Salzburg June 2008; Krakow, May 2007 and ePortfolio 2009, Innovation, Creativity and Accountability, London June 2009.

John has managed many successful curriculum development projects. Recent projects have focused on supporting students with the development of Key Skills, vocational skills and employability skills. He has worked closely with the Key Skills Support Programme, the Functional Skills Support Programme, the Learning and Skills Development Agency and the County Durham Business and Learning Partnership. For the past four years, John has developed and piloted E-Me, a video-rich multimedia ePortfolio with students, aged 11-18 and, for the past 2 years, has taken a major role in the European MOSEP ePortfolio project.


John disseminates ePortfolio project findings through his Blog and a Google group. He has an interest in Web 2 Tools and regularly contributes to on-line discussions and activities. He recently delivered an on-line presentations as part of the Trainers in Europe Conference in November 2008.

Specialties

teaching ICT to students aged 11- 19; lecturing in FE on a wide range of vocational ICT courses; In-service Teacher Training both in-house and University based; curriculum development and project management including ICT, Key Skills, Functional Skills, employability skills and ePortfolios, Key Skills and Functional Skills training
