David Witz's Projects

  • File 13

    • 1984 to Present
    Team Members: David Witz

    File 13 was a studio collaboration between Doug DiFranco (aka Double Dee) and myself (aka Arthur Ether). The first single, "Taste So Good", was originally created from audio snippets taken from sex-phone recordings, laid on an original electro dance track. The 12" Profile Records single reached number #39 on Billboard's Disco chart, and has since appeared on numerous anthologies.

  • The Many Men of Martha Manning

    • September 2007 to Present
    Team Members: David Witz, Karen Getz

    "The Many Men of Martha Manning" was the first Philadelphia Fringe Festival podcast play. Episodes were downloadable in sequence over the course of the 2007 Fringe's two week run. Created by Grace Gonglewski, Karen Getz and myself, "Many Men" introduces us to 1957 housewife Martha Manning and her best friend, Dotty. Reviews online at MarthaAndDotty.com).

  • Martha & Dotty: Microwave Mambo!

    • September 2008 to Present
    Team Members: David Witz, Karen Getz

    Thanks to the surprising success of "Many Men" at the 2007 Philly Fringe, Grace Gonglewski, Karen Getz and I got to do another one. The plot of "Microwave Mambo" finds Martha & Dotty in 1958, dealing with the live atomic bomb at the bottom of Tybee Bay, Georgia (which is still in the bay today). Reviews at MarthaAndDotty.com.
