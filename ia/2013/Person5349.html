After 25 years in marketing, I’ve come to realise two things: marketing is misunderstood and marketing is broken. It’s broken because the majority of people misunderstand what marketing is really about and they don’t understand the value of the truth. The antidote, I believe, is a marketing framework I call True Story Marketing. The fact is, what most people understand marketing to be is outputs: ads, brochures, websites and emails for instance. But marketing is much, much more than the message. I believe marketing is actually a real-life story, told in three acts. And the name of your version of this real- life, three-act story, is your brand. Read the True Story Marketing Manifesto for more. OK, I know this is a LinkedIn profile so here's what, I guess, should be here. At Talking Brand, I am the founder, strategy developer, communications director, marketing partner, cocktail-of-talent- manager and devil's advocate, and I behave just like I'm on your management team.

Amanda Falconer's Summary

I've a background in both small and big business. I began in film publicity, founded a clothing business during my 20s, and have had 12 years in senior corporate marketing roles. I was the ANZ Marketing Manager for CSR divisions PGH and Hebel, the Group Marketing Manager for The Allam Group and the National Marketing Manager for James Hardie.

I also led acquisition bid teams and developed new business opportunities in CSR’s corporate strategy department. I have a Masters of Management from Macquarie University, a Certificate IV in Training and Assessment from RMIT, am a certified Net Promoter System associate, a certified SEO Toolset Analyst, and am certified in Value Proposition Development and Email Messaging.

Amanda Falconer's Experience

Director

Talking Brand

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Marketing and Advertising industry

2010 – Present (3 years) Sydney

In a $100 million business there’s budget for an in-house team of marketing specialists; a team of people who develop marketing strategy, coordinate the execution using both in house staff and external agencies, and learn and improve.

I've run departments just like this in the past.

However, if you own or run a medium-sized business – with a turnover of say $3 million to $30 million – then what you really need is access to your own marketing department, without having to pay for one 24/7.

Talking Brand is an outsourced marketing department, set up to handle anything you’ll need. It’s a team of people with a hybrid skillset because in an interconnected world, silo-based specialties just won’t cut the mustard. To deliver online and off, you need people who are not only business and marketing strategists, but also storytellers and video makers and SEO analysts; you need a hybrid marketing department that offers a cocktail of talent in a glass you can afford.

This is the team that I manage at Talking Brand.
