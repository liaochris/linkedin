Charley Han's Summary

Regal headshot of a CEO in a personal library, half empty perfume bottle on a worn writing desk, lone rock, cracked and dried up on watershed, they all tell a story. The photographer's job is to determine how much of that story is revealed, to communicate the aesthetics and essence of the subject.

There are many reasons one makes an image, whether for practical, aesthetic, or even manipulation. But overwhelmingly, the overarching activity of mankind is information gathering and sharing it. And photography is one giant tool that is used to express ourselves and the information that makes us.

I enjoy using whatever it takes to tell your story, and figuring out the 'how' is part of the fun for me. Whether that means to use a rangefinder, a 4x5 view camera with digital back or Kodak film, 35mm Nikon with manual parts, or the latest digital camera from Canon, Nikon, or Red to capture the subject lit by the sun or the moon, flashes of light or a single burning match, my job is in the telling of your story. Video and film making is a natural extension of what I do as a photographer and even so audio production is a natural support for moving images. Please check out my website for the latest projects.
