David L. Bailin, ChFC, CLU, CLTC has been helping clients accumulate wealth and reduce taxes* for the last 23 years. In 1996 David founded his own Financial Services Agency.

Professionally, David has been invited to speak on Finance and Employment issues at Rutgers University, the New Jersey Department of Labor, and was a featured speaker at the Small Business Week Conference sponsored by the City of Trenton. He regularly speaks to Physician groups at Robert Wood Johnson, JFK, Jersey Shore, Jersey City, St. Francis, St. Peter’s, as well as other Medical Centers. David has been interviewed as an expert on WCTC's You and Your Money, and in several periodicals including The Home News, The Asbury Park Press and Advisor Today magazine.

The focus of David's financial practice has been in providing advice to clients helping them to make smart decisions about their money. His investment strategies fully utilize the principles of the 1990 Nobel prize-winning Modern Portfolio Theory.

David is a Registered Representative and Investment Advisor of Securian Financial Services, Inc., Member FINRA/SIPC. Securities and investment advisory services are offered through Securian Financial Services Inc., Member FINRA/SIPC. Personal Wealth Strategeis is independently owned and operated.

The American College

ChFC

What a ChFC® Can Do For You:

A ChFC® (Chartered Financial Consultant) is an educated, experienced, and qualified professional committed to the financial well being of your family, your business, and yourself. A ChFC is required to complete all of the educational elements of the generalist CFP® Certification plus two additional specialized electives. A ChFC® can provide professional advice on a broad range of financial topics and help you:

Create a Sound Financial Solution
Reduce Your Income Taxes
Increase Your Nest Egg for Retirement
Increase Your Investments
Enhance the Value of Your Estate

The Corporation for Long Term Care Certification

CLTC

What does CLTC stand for?

CLTC stands for "Certified in Long-Term Care," a designation granted by the Corporation For Long-Term Care Certification. CLTC graduates have completed a rigorous multidisciplinary course that focuses on the profession of long-term care. The program is recognized by state regulators, through the granting of continuing education credits, as having provided essential information necessary to the appropriate sale of long-term care insurance.
