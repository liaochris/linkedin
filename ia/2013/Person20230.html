Carma Spence's Summary

An award-winning editor with 20+ years experience in marketing, public relations (PR) and science communication, Carma Spence is a certified life coach, multi-dimensional entrepreneur, science fiction (SF) writer and poet.

After earning an MS in Journalism from the University of Maryland College Park (in the nation’s #1 PR program, according to U.S. News & World Report), Carma developed a career as a corporate science writer. Her ability to understand science (she has a BA in Biology) and translate it for a lay audience, coupled with her marketing and PR training, enabled her to help employers such as City of Hope National Medical Center, Blue Shield of California and the National Human Genome Research Institute gain media exposure and improve customer and employee communications.

Carma performed pro bono work for KRCB TV, the Luther Burbank Center and the North Bay Career Resource Center. She has promoted wines from Texas, South Africa and France, and helped pioneer a website for pediatric professionals.

Recognizing the importance of web marketing, Carma has developed an expertise in online strategy, and authored Home Sweet Home Page and 57 Secrets for Branding Yourself Online.

Carma’s love of travel (she grew up in Kenya), along with her passion for SF, propelled her to create the online travel resource for SF, fantasy and horror fans, The Genre Traveler. She is also working on a multi-media history and sociology of SF.

As a speaker, Carma has received several awards, including a lifetime membership to the National Forensic League. A Toastmaster since 1989, she has earned her Advanced Communicator Bronze and Advanced Leader Bronze awards. Public Speaking Super Powers is due Spring 2013.

The wind beneath her clients’ wings, Carma provides creative entrepreneurs with expert advice on mindset, branding and web presence, as well as the emotional support necessary to carve out their success online.
