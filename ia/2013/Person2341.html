Aggie Perilli's Summary

Aggie Perilli Communications International gets to the heart of issues and inspires a world of positive response from specific markets and the general public. Our management and marketing strategists, writers, artists, and technology innovators deliver transformative communication to regional and global nonprofit and for-profit organizations.

We provide clients with strategic planning, writing, art and animation, digital and print publishing services, education and entertainment, foreign language translations, and more.

Our clients have included the Affinity Federal Credit Union, Pfizer, Roche, Johnson & Johnson, X-Factor Communications, Strategic Intelligence Skills, Cotton Gloves Research, REB Consulting, and The Arc of Somerset County.

Specialties: books & magazines, catalogs and other publications; websites & blogs, videos and other online communications; advertising & marketing, and mobile and social media; commercials, films & trailers and 3D micro-visualizations; animation & visual effects for the web, video games and e-devices.

Aggie Perilli's Experience

President

Aggie Perilli Communications International

1990 – Present (23 years)

My family tree is rooted in publishing. My father and his four siblings and mother helped my grandfather, Armondo Perilli, edit and publish an Italian newspaper. Following in their footsteps, I launched my communications career as a newspaper reporter for The Princeton Packet, where I also served as lifestyle editor, contributed stories to their monthly magazine, and occasionally wrote op-ed columns.

To pursue entrepreneurial aspirations of my own, I completed a postgraduate publishing program at Radcliffe College, where a Vice President from European Travel & Life magazine in New York recruited me to help launch an advertising section––profitable from our first issue. Before founding Aggie Perilli Communications International (APCI) in 1990, I played an instrumental role in transforming a biweekly trade newspaper into Housewares Retailing magazine, and, later, managed the production of technical publications for AT&T.

Since our first global newsmagazine, APCI has received accolades from numerous organizations, which include the NY/NJ Association of Graphic Communication, NY Society for Technical Communication, Neographics, The Arc of New Jersey, and The Communicator (worldwide). Before founding APCI, I received a Citation for Meritorious Enterprise Reporting from the NJ Press Association.
