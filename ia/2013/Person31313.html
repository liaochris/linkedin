David D. Sohn's Summary

At SOHN LEGAL GROUP, we have a demonstrated track record of successfully representing our clients in litigation, mediation, arbitration, and trial. We are committed to working harder and smarter than our adversaries to help our clients achieve favorable results. Our aggressive strategies are driven by our ultimate goal: to win through strategic motions or at trial, or to maximize the opportunities for a favorable settlement.

Our practice, which spans both state and federal courts, is focused on prosecuting and defending socially just actions on behalf of individuals, small businesses, and non-profit organizations in a variety of substantive areas. These include: employment disputes involving discrimination, sexual harassment, retaliation, wrongful termination, and/or wage and hour claims; violations of consumer protection laws; commercial and/or contractual disputes; corporate governance disputes, and personal injury matters, among others. Our firm is also well-versed in litigating and trying class actions, in particular, employment and consumer class actions.

We are not afraid to take on a difficult case. However, the issue must be right.

We accept a limited number of engagements at any time. By limiting the number of engagements we accept, we are able to ensure that our clients enjoy the access, responsiveness, and service consistent with the highest standards of the legal profession. We take pride in the fact that our livelihood is a profession. We are counselors, advocates, and officers of the court. We have chosen our profession not just to make a decent living, but to serve the diverse communities we live in. We volunteer our time at clinics throughout the Bay Area, including those hosted by El Centro Legal de la Raza, the Bar Association of San Francisco’s Volunteer Legal Services Program, the Legal Aid Society – Employment Law Center, and the Asian American Bar Association of the Greater Bay Area, among others.

Specialties

Employment discrimination, sexual harassment, retaliation, wrongful termination, and/or wage and hour claims; violations of consumer protection laws; commercial and/or contractual disputes; corporate governance disputes, and personal injury matters.
