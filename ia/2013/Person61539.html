John Tenney's Experience

Lecturer

Weird Lectures

February 1991 – Present (22 years 11 months)

John E.L. Tenney’s columns have been printed in magazines and newspapers world-wide, and he has lectured to numerous public and private schools, universities, organizations and clubs. It is estimated that, over the past 25 years, more than 45,000 people have attended one of John’s signature “Weird Lectures.” Mr. Tenney has also been interviewed extensively on radio and television worldwide. John has been actively involved in the field of anomalistic/conspiratorial research for well-over two decades. Due to his extended time involved in anomalistic research he has acted as a consultant for numerous companies including, Fox, SyFy, A&E, NBC and many more.

John Tenney's Publications

  • Bad Luck Chuck

    • John E.L. Tenney / Lulu Publishing
    • June 1, 2006
    Authors: John Tenney

    An early reader book which tells the tale of a small black cat who is blamed for everday problems because of how he looks. A cute book with a great little lesson for children.

  • Telepathy: Genuine and Fradulent

    • Tenney's Library of Recovered Knowledge
    • May 1, 2011
    Authors: John Tenney

    Not only does this book act as a learner’s guide for modern-day researchers of supernatural phenomena but it holds within it’s pages a documentation of how belief systems can be challenged in the most polite of manners.

  • One Last Thing

    • Weird Lectures
    • December 1, 2011
    Authors: John Tenney

    Over the past two decades anomalistic investigator John E.L.
    Tenney has collected and compiled hundreds of hours of what many people consider voices from another realm of existence.
    Assembled in this publication are a few of the curious, strange, startling and often humorous responses gathered from investigations across not only the country but perhaps various other dimensions.

  • The Sceptical Poltergeist & The Doubtful Phantom

    • Tenney's Library of Recovered Knowledge
    • September 2011
    Authors: John Tenney

    In this short volume you will find The Sceptical Poltergeist, by J.D. Beresford reprinted in complete and unabridged form from the original 1922 text, as well as an all new short story The Doubtful Phantom by John E.L. Tenney printed here for the first time.

  • Sky People, Creatures And Spirits: Folk Tales, Legends and Paranormal Encounters of The First North Americans

    • Tenney's Library of Recovered Knowledge
    • February 2012
    Authors: John Tenney

    The concepts of nature, life and the spiritual beliefs held by the original inhabitants of North America are some of the most insightful and curiously interpreted of any peoples in the world. From star people to bog beasts, ghosts and giants, the tales told by indigenous North American peoples are fascinating in design and illuminating upon reflection.

  • Ghosties, Haunts & Magic

    • Tenney's Library of Recovered Knowledge
    • March 1, 2012
    Authors: John Tenney

    The stories included in this book not only reveal some of the thoughts and beliefs of the former American slaves but also provide a fascinating, and sometimes heartbreaking, account of the lives they led. Collected and edited in this short volume are some of those recollections.

  • All of a Sudden I'm The Bad Guy & Parking for International Departures

    • Weird Lectures
    • September 1, 2012
    Authors: John Tenney

    The words and ideas contained in this book are an attempt to capture the author’s thoughts about everything from candy to heartbreak. For the first time, these two books have been collected into a single edition, for no reason other than to allow the author to throw away all the original scraps of paper.
