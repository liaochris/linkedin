Jennifer Rolfsema's Summary

I love what I do. Design introduces a variety of industries, types of projects and so many people to meet along the way. Whether I'm designing a logo for a small business or system of collateral for themed initiative- each has its unique challenges and rewards. I work hard. Network globally. Think and research.

The value and experience of learning is continual. I was introduced to the field of graphic arts as a teenager. Pursued it through internships, European study abroad and training, and formal degrees from a world-renown educational institution. Today, much of my work is designing for academia and advancing my knowledge in various key disciplines.

Running a small business for over 6-years, and being self-employed for close to 10, I've learned many lessons beyond the desk. Multitasking. Where to pull my strengths. Carrying out projects from an idea in the mind, to production on a mass scale. I appreciate the face-to-face meetings with clients, or working remotely with an author on their 5th historical family memoir, to working on a website for a florist down-the-street.

In my professional life, I am fortunate to share my artistic talents beyond the digital platform. I display and sell my artwork. I am often invited to work or speak at formal and familial events and festivals. It is a joy as well as a job.
