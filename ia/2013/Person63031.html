Jessica A. Bush's Summary

PEOPLE and PEOPLE CONNECTIONS are what make Jessica tick! She is passionate about people, connecting with people and sharing what THEY are passionate about! Before launching Tutor Doctor of North Jersey, Jessica had close to 30 years of experience selling fine art, photography to collectors as well as galleries around the world, along with managing a number of retail Galleries. As Director of Sales for collections such as the LIFE Collection of Photography, she had the amazing opportunity to curate photography exhibitions in photo galleries around the world specializing in fine art photography & photojournalism.

Art speaks to people in a very personal way. Art lovers & collectors have an emotional connection and reaction to a work of art whether it be a painting, a sculpture, or a photograph. The connection and commitment made when one buys a work of art is emotionally charged.

Very similarly, Jessica's connections with the family's and students she works with are very meaningful to her. The connection a family has with their child / student and their commitment to their child’s progress is very emotionally charged. When Jessica works with a family to find the “perfect” tutor for a student, there is a very strong commitment from the family; a desire to see their student succeed and excel in school and in life. Likewise, there is a sincere commitment from Jessica to see each student to reach their full potential. Jessica’s sensitivity, intuition and skills of matching a student to a tutor that provides for the students individual personality, learning style and needs comes from her high level of “people” expertise and sensibility. Tutor Doctor of North Jersey families have come to know Jessica as honest, responsive, very accessible and extremely reliable. The program is competitive and work in the studios and classrooms is rigorous.

Alfred University

Bachelor of Fine Arts (BFA), School of Art and Design

1978 – 1980

The School of Art and Design in The New York State College of Ceramics at Alfred University has a long illustrious history of creative excellence. It is a place where art, science and technology have merged for over a century. In art, your only limitation is your imagination. At Alfred they are committed to assisting both undergraduate and graduate students achieve their greatest creative potential. Their programmatic foundation rests upon these key values: personal and intellectual development, exploration and observation, understanding the symbiotic relationship between the history of art and contemporary art practice, and last but perhaps most important, skill based instruction. A strong work ethic, open-mindedness, and imaginative courage are the touchstones of serious art making. It was a wonderful place to explores concepts and build skills that can be applied to contemporary art practice in any field.
