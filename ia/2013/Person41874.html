Erica A. Carlino's Summary

Erica is a consultant for numerous NGO's in consultative status with the United Nations Economic and Social Council (ECOSOC). She is currently active on the Mining Working Group (MWG) to influence UN Sustainable Development Goals. In the past she was an active member of the NGO Committee on Migration & the Working Group on Girls (WGG).

From 2005 until 2010 she lived in the Czech Republic, where she received a Masters in Applied Sociology and Public Policy and conducted research as part of a joint investigative team for the European Commission on sex-trafficking along the Czech-German border.

Current consulting projects include:

Feminist Task Force of the Global Call to Action Against Poverty (GCAP): Following and reporting on the Post-2015 Agenda and the Open Working Group on Sustainability. Provides support in the areas of official negotiations, written interventions and marketing/visual design.

Loretto Community: Part of the UN Representative team. Provides support in the areas of: representation, written interventions, event organizing, marketing/branding, point of contact for the Youth Delegation to the Commission on the Status of Women.

Franciscans International: Provides support in the areas of creative project management: currently co-authoring and designing a Handbook to teach International Human Rights Law.

Sisters of Mercy: Provides support in the areas of statical visual design.

Specialties: girls' and women's rights, sex-trafficking, human rights, Czech language, Central and Eastern Europe, Roma, art activism, graphic design, community organizing, urban greening

Interests: girl's and women's rights, social movements, civic innovation, environmental sustainability, participatory economics and the take back of public space.
