Christine Fish's Summary

I am an experienced technical communicator, instructional designer, and entrepreneur, but more accurately described by my friends and business associates as an artist with an engineer's mind, a talented teacher, and someone who can turn chaos into order. My passions are learning; designing and facilitating memorable learning experiences; making connections between what on the surface seem to be disparate concepts or facts; solving problems; and creating great team experiences.

It's About People and Processes
"You can make another one," my fused glass instructor offered as I held the pieces of my broken glass bowl--a work of art that fell apart in my hands while I was smoothing it on a lapidary wheel. Through too many kiln firings, I had stressed the various formulas of glass included in the bowl beyond their physical limits. Placing the pieces over a special light box revealed additional stress fractures that were not visible to the unaided eye. "No, this lesson was worth the price of admission to this class," I said. "My art is about people and processes more than the finished art piece." The bowl hangs above the grinding station in my home studio as a reminder of the lesson. Pretty much all I do is about people and processes, with various art forms (traditional and technical) as the tangible end product.

Specialties: Screencasting, instructional design, technical writing and project management, workflow analysis and improvement, team leadership and facilitation
