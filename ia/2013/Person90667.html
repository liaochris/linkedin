Mea Boykins' Summary

I am a graduate of Spelman College, C'11, with a Bachelors of Arts in Psychology. While at Spelman, I established a scholarship fund entitled S.E.A. or the Student Emergency Assistance Fund. This fund is awarded to juniors and seniors at Spelman whom have exhausted all of the financial resources and have an impending balance placing them at risk for expulsion.

Through letter written solicitations to business contacts, family, and friends I was able to raise over $14,000 and award four scholarships students at Spelman. S.E.A. is also platform to uplift the community. I have planned and hosted several events in Atlanta, including two Feed the Homeless Events for Women, Children, and Men; an empowerment event for adolescence, a S.E.A Benefit Concert Fundraiser, and an event entitled "Fashion Playground" to promote confidence and self-love within youth in New York. The events included motivational speakers, prayer, food/clothing donations, testimonies, job fairs, etiquette training, celebrity appearances, live performances & more. I have also been invited to speak on several panels, including the Teen Summit panel at the Women of Worth Conference with Miss USA & Miss Georgia, & a panel with the NAACP, Georgia State Representatives, & Miss Georgia.

I am currently a Masters student in a Child Psychiatry program at Kings College London, an Ivy League University, where I will publishing my first literature in the UK. I plan on pursuing my doctorate in Clinical Psychology, running my nonprofit, fundraising, hosting events & uplifting the community globally. I also plan on starting a Charter school in New Orleans, Louisiana and pursuing a Masters in Theology.

To learn more about S.E.A please visit www.sea-of-love.org and like the S.E.A. FB page here: www.facebook.com/studentemergencyassistance

Specialties: Motivational Speaking, Fund-raising, Event Planning, Public Speaking, Corporate Communication, Leadership Development/Training, Research, Writing, Marketing,Therapy

Mea Boykins' Experience

Child & Adolescent Psychologist Trainee

South London and Maudsley NHS Foundation Trust: Conduct, Adoption & Fostering Team (CAFT)-

Nonprofit; 5001-10,000 employees; Mental Health Care industry

December 2012 – Present (1 year) London, United Kingdom

As part of my university course, I have been a trainee at the South London & Maudsley NHS trust under the Con-duct and Foster Team (CAFT) where I observe cases of adopted children with psychiatric disorders such as Au-tism, Autism Spectrum Disorders, ADHD, and Attachment Disorder. I also participate in facilitation of child and family therapy, and conduct school visits to observe children in different settings and to identify pervasiveness of symptoms. During the school visits I meet with the Principal and teachers of the patient and interview them in order to gain their perspective on the issues, as well as obtain a school report. After obtaining the necessary information from the visit, I write a report on the case and assist with identifying the proper diagnosis. Along with these visits, I observe “Parent-Child games,” which is an intervention used for some children with behavior difficulties. The expe-rience has been an invaluable learning experience as it gave me a clear insight into the clinical application of the theoretical concepts I have learned in my Masters Course. I gained a sense of appreciation for psychology as the field makes a difference in the lives of patients and their families alike.

Founder

Student Emergency Assistance, Incorporated

July 2009 – Present (4 years 5 months) Atlanta, New York, London

In 2009, I founded the S.E.A. scholarship fund, with the objective of providing financial assistance to college stu-dents in need of support in order to complete their education. Minimum requirements, both academic and otherwise were stipulated for eligibility. Raising funds for the organization involved extensive networking and communication with corporations and prospective donors as well as monthly events planned and hosted by the S.E.A. for disad-vantaged individuals in Atlanta, New York and London. Activities mainly included musical performances, celebrity appearances, give-a-ways and job-fairs. Provisions were also made for donations of food and clothing to homeless women, men and children. Thus far I have raised over $15,000 and awarded 4 scholarships. My involvement in the SEA is a difficult yet incredibly fulfilling activity that has polished my ability to communicate effectively, build rapport with ease and has cemented my ability to assume leadership roles effectively. (See www.sea-of-love.org)

Archival Researcher

JPMorgan Chase

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; JPM; Financial Services industry

December 2011 – April 2012 (5 months) Greater Atlanta Area

As a research assistant for the King Centre Imaging project, I worked alongside a team which composed of some of JP Morgan’s supervisors, managers of the Technology for Social Good sector of Chase, veterans and other King’s Center personnel; compiling and digitizing numerous high profile documents and indexing using the Alfresco database. Over 20,000 of Dr. Martin Luther King’s personal hand written letters, speeches, and notes were tran-scribed and collated by the team. The historical data compiled, dating back to the Civil Rights Era, was henceforth used in creating an Interactive website which showcases a digital archive of the words that shaped the time. It was a pleasure to be a part of such an effective team to produce an accessible and available resource giving people a glimpse into such a pivotal period in modern society and human rights. It was also most certainly a valuable expe-rience, which tested and improved my ability to work in a large multidisciplinary team of professionals with structure and a clearly defined role as a team member. From this experience, I learned that the success of a team is not only measured by the quality of the product but by the coherence within it and the ability of every member to understand and play their roles effectively. (See www.thekingcenter.org)
