Doug Gold's Summary

Doug Gold has spent his life seeking simplicity amidst confusion. Whether in business, politics or personal relationships Doug has defined himself a Scribe of Simplicity™. This philosophy extends all aspects of his life, from running a company to interacting with his children to making entries into his blog. Where many see chaos and complexity, Doug identifies patterns and trends by cutting through the clutter. He accomplishes this by insightfully breaking down problems to their most basic elements through common sense analysis, all while using his characteristic charm and levity.

Doug has spent the last 22 years mastering an ability to go from big picture to details and back again in working on diverse transactions, ranging from an IPO to venture financings to acquisitions and operations, in a variety of industries, including banking, software, services, manufacturing, non-profit and law. His entrepreneurial spirit had him involved in a wide range of adventures well before completing his degree. At the tender age of 13, Doug began his apprenticeship by picking up trash, driving a forklift, being a golf caddy, building furniture, working the front desk of a hotel and selling cellular phones. His travels have taken him around the world from India and the Asia Pacific region, to New Zealand and throughout Europe, providing him with an additional perspective on cultural differences in decision making and relationships.

Whether it’s a subconscious desire for self-torment or a more purposeful enjoyment for exercising new muscles, Doug continuously throws himself into the unknown and the unfamiliar with a combination of quiet, sage-like resolve coupled with the innocence of a goofy kid. A good mix that keeps him both grounded and focused.

Doug invests time in his community as a Jewish Big Brother. His past honors include having served as Chairman of the Board of the March of Dimes, Chapter President of Kiwanis International, and coaching AYSO soccer.

Specialties

Branding, mergers & acquisition, IT infrastructure, finance & accounting infrastructure, HR management, fundraising, business plan development, Board development, strategic planning, compensation plan design, government affairs, complex deal negotiation.
