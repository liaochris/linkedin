Eric Holzer's Summary

Eric Holzer harnesses a rare balanced cocktail of skills in product, website, and graphic design to create an outcome that is consistently innovative and exceeds expectations. Eric has been locally and nationally awarded for excellence in design, most recently with two CES Innovation Awards and Best In Show from iLounge.com and BeatWeek Magazine at the 2012 International Consumer Electronics Show in Las Vegas.

He has designed for such notable brands as iHome, Timex, Hotel Technologies, Lutron Electronics and more, with distribution globally in stores such as Apple Store, Target, Wal-Mart, Best Buy, and Fnac (Europe).

New endeavors are always approached with a modern and clean aesthetic, honed from his multinational industrial design education at both Philadelphia University in the US and Nuova Accademia di Bella Arti Milano in Italy.

Eric currently resides in Piscataway, New Jersey in the USA, where he mainly develops new initiatives focused on iPhone, iPad and iPod docking systems to maintain iHome as the market leader in docking products.

Specialties: Quick sketching/ideation, Mockups, Prototype construction, Solidworks concept renderings, Photoshop, Illustrator, Dreamweaver, HTML, CSS

  • Size Convertible Mattress

    • United States Patent 8001638
    • Issued August 23, 2011
    Inventors: Eric Holzer, Nicole Zimmerman, Geoff Quinter, Lauren C. Gatto

    A mattress converts from a crib mattress with side walls and end walls surrounding an infant sleeping surface on a central mattress section therebetween to a twin mattress with a substantially planar sleeping surface to support a reclining child or adult. The end walls and side walls are tethered or hingedly connected to the central mattress section and are moveable from an upright position (crib mattress) to an open position (twin mattress). Sidewall extensions are hingedly connected to the side walls and are pivotably moveable from a first position above the side walls to a second position axially aligned with the side walls when the side walls are in the open position.
