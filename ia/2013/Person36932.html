Don Yaeger's Summary

In my 20+ years as an author and journalist for Sports Illustrated, I've had the incredible fortune of spending time with some of the greatest winners of all time.

While interviewing legends like Michael Jordan, Coach John Wooden, Michael Oher, Walter Payton and countless others, I've always carved out time to discuss GREATNESS -- and what, specifically, it takes to get from good to GREAT.

From these conversations, I've distilled 16 Consistent Characteristics of Greatness, which I share with audiences around the world as a motivational keynote speaker. I also recently released a book called GREATNESS, which outlines these characteristics, some of the Great Ones who embody them and tips on how we can apply these lessons for our own personal and professional growth.

Please stop by my website to learn more: www.donyaeger.com.

Specialties

motivational keynote speaking, writing, reporting, business development, leadership training, crisis communications, media training

Associate Editor

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; TWX; Publishing industry

July 1996 – April 2008 (11 years 10 months)

During my 10 years at Sports Illustrated, I worked on many of the magazine’s most significant and groundbreaking stories, including the first national investigation of steroids and their influence in baseball; the future of Internet sports gaming and its potential to influence the games; as well as the horrific efforts of Saddam Hussein’s son, Uday, in shaping the Iraqi Olympic Committee and Uday’s torture of Iraqi athletes when they didn’t perform up to his standards.
