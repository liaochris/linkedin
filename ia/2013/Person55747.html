Iraklis Hatziathanasiou's Experience

Product Development Manager

COM DEV Europe

Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Defense & Space industry

October 2009 – Present (3 years 9 months) Aylesbury, United Kingdom

Responsible for the development and marketing of a portfolio of products related to the space industry. I was employed by COM DEV Europe to assess the technology readiness, market compatibility and potential product offering for a number of the company’s R&D activities. During the last three years, I was able to help the company develop and sell a number of key products (space equipment) to the international market. Moreover, I established a product roadmap and identified sources of funding for the development of a series of forthcoming products and capabilities. As a result, I helped the company develop into a centre of excellence for small communications payloads.

Independent Expert

European Commission

Government Agency; 10,001+ employees; International Affairs industry

August 2006 – Present (6 years 11 months) Brussels, Belgium

As an independent expert, I have participated in a number of evaluations for funding organised by the European Commission. Since 2006, I have evaluated a moderately large number of proposals. These have provided me with an in-depth understanding of the funding process, what is expected of the applicants and how such projects can add value to the overall policies of the associated directorates. More importantly, it helped me expand my professional network both within the European Commission and with other expert evaluators.

Managing Director / Principle Consultant

Kymatics Ltd

September 2006 – October 2009 (3 years 2 months) Oxford, United Kingdom

During my employment with Kymatics, I worked closely with the European Commission’s Joint Research Centre (under a collaboration agreement) and the UK Border Agency at establishing working models of telemetry and tracking systems for the active management of shipping containers. The project examined the complications of launching technology that was aimed towards an extraordinary high number of users and proposed a number of approaches for dealing with the data management issues and the value proposition of each of the stakeholders in the chain.

WG Expert, Member of the CEN BT/WG 161 Expert Group on Integrated Border Management

European Commission

Government Agency; 10,001+ employees; International Affairs industry

2005 – 2009 (4 years)

Involvement with the pre-standardisation process at European and national levels in the context of European security.
Research on issues that pertain to matters of security in the context of police and judicial cooperation in criminal matters as well as their implication for fundamental rights protection.

Capability Leader / Business Manager

QinetiQ

Public Company; 5001-10,000 employees; qq.; Aviation & Aerospace industry

2004 – 2006 (2 years) Farnborough, United Kingdom

I joined the company as a capability leader and managed a team of technical experts. During this period, I helped the team setup the manufacturing of a range of high sensitivity GPS receivers and launch the products to a high volume market (greater than 100K units per year). At a later stage, I was able to form a separate business group (within QinetiQ) that developed and launched a range of wireless technology platforms and their associated data management service. During my employment with QinetiQ, I also participated in European Commission expert work groups and coordinated consortia and partnerships which involved governmental organisations from various Member States.

Senior Project Manager

OxLoc Ltd

2001 – 2003 (2 years) Oxford, United Kingdom

Being part of this Oxford University spin-out company from its inception, I was able to actively participate in all aspects of the business. I was responsible for the design, manufacture and market launch of an advanced Telematics system, including all aspects of customer support and data management. This award winning (SMART award) project was successfully engaged by a number of scientists, internationally, to monitor and understand the behavioural characteristics of wild animals in their natural habitat.

Consultant

HIS ( Altran Group)

Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Logistics and Supply Chain industry

2000 – 2001 (1 year) Harlow, United Kingdom

Responsible for research development and day-to-day management of a global scale project. Worked on the design of a remote access global database with the aim to revolutionise the way hospitals and microbiology labs tested patients and samples for bacterial infections. This very high exposure project won three awards and was developed with the support of a number of research institutes and hospitals around the world.

Development Engineer

Coral Engineering Limited

1999 – 2000 (1 year) Aberdeen, United Kingdom

Responsible for the design and manufacture of electronic and control systems. During my employment with Coral, I designed control systems for large centrifuges and shaker systems operated remotely in oil rigs, gaining valuable experience in PLC/SCADA systems. I was also involved in the design and testing of underwater sonar communication systems and electronic detection of magnetic units travelling in pipelines. Part of my employment also involved subcontracting and consultancy work with multinational companies.
