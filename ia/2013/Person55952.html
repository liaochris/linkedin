Irvin Schein's Summary

With over 30 years of litigation experience, I have acted as counsel in a vast number of areas including: shareholders' rights and remedies, applications for injunctive relief and judicial review, wrongful dismissal, defamation, corporate and commercial disputes, international litigation, including jurisdictional disputes, negligence and tort claims, sexual abuse litigation, real property litigation including commercial development disputes and commercial leasing disputes, pension and employment benefits disputes, prosecution and defence of solicitors' negligence and professional malpractice claims, and environmental litigation. I am also chair of both Minden Gross' litigation department and Vehiculaw, Minden Gross' motor vehicle dealers practice group.

Specialties

Industries served include financial institutions, commercial landlords, commercial tenants, accounting firms, real estate developers, builders, real estate agents and property managers, franchisors, software developers, insurers, importers and distributors, retailers, automobile dealerships, meat processors, trucking companies, and other entrepreneurs.

I have also developed specific knowledge involving the automotive industry and the grape growing / winery industry.

Irvin Schein's Experience

Partner

Minden Gross LLP

Partnership; 51-200 employees; Law Practice industry

1983 – Present (30 years)

I have acted on a number of high profile cases of national importance and have conducted numerous cases against financial institutions and is particularly well known as having obtained an outstanding result for an entrepreneur client in an action against a financial institution which acted improperly in connection with a loan to the client. Notwithstanding the fact that the actual loan amounted to approximately $200,000.00, the financial institution was ultimately ordered to pay almost $7 million in damages. This case is widely regarded as one of the most important lender liability cases in recent history.

I have also achieved success on behalf of financial institutions, including a judgment of the Court of Appeal allowing his lender client to recover its funds where its mortgage was discharged in error notwithstanding that the provisions of the Registry Act appeared to dictate the opposite result.

My experience in environmental litigation includes having acted for a financial institution whose mortgage security was impaired by toxic chemicals emanating from a neighbouring property.

I have substantial experience with litigation of an international nature and routinely handle matters for companies based in the Unites States and the United Kingdom.

I serve as a co-chair of the litigation section of Meritas worldwide and have been very active throughout his career as an author and lecturer on litigation practice related matters.
