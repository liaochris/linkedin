Laurent Antonczak's Summary

Laurent Antonczak is a multicultural entrepreneur specialising in Digital Strategies (branding, visual communication, social media, transmedia, information architecture) and emerging technologies (web & mobile phones).

“I am deeply interested in people’s experiences, stories and working with new technologies. I value working collaboratively, taking risks and having some laughs while still being professional. My playground is the world with a particular interest in New Zealand, France and Japan.” (Laurent)

Laurent is one of the co-founders of MINA (Mobile Innovation Network Aotearoa), which aims to explore the possibilities of interaction between people, content and the emerging mobile industry. He lectures and supervises students in New Media & Visual Communication mainly at Postgraduate level (on campus and online delivery) at AUT University.

In 2012, Laurent was the successful recipient of both: the Learning and Teaching Fellowship, and the Learning and Teaching Enabled by Technology (LATENT) grants.

Though involved in academia and education, Laurent is concurrently involved in two companies: ATZ119 (Director), specialising in Mobile Content Production (mainly Video) & Visual Communication and VIRTUO (Partner), specialising in Mobile Research & Development, Digital Strategy & Consultancy (Emerging Technologies and Mobile).

Laurent is also deeply involved in building and maintaining strong relationships between France and New Zealand as President of the French New Zealand Chamber of Commerce and Industry.

The range of clients that Laurent worked with include HSBC Republic (Private Banking), Goldman Sachs – JB Were (Private Banking), La Cours des Comptes de Luxembourg, Toyota – Lexus, Ford, RTL9, Telecom, Tango (LUX), Télé2 (LUX), ExxonMobil, Vodafone (NZ), Raffles College of Design & Commerce (NZ), The French Embassy – Wellington (NZ), Lonnie Hutchinson - visual artist & multimedia (Maori -Ngai Tahu, Samoan).

DOC Lab is a three-day incubator to educate, inspire and develop filmmakers with a shared goal of developing and prototyping multi-platform documentary projects. Local and international experts are brought in as mentors to present and help develop the selected projects and teams. Up to 12 teams are selected to participate with each team comprising 1-3 members.

Starting from content ideas or from existing material, DOC Lab 2010, 2011 and 2012 discussed new documentary prototypes that use one or more new media. Workshop topics included:
• a master-class in storytelling
• the new and different forms of platforms and storytelling devices
• how to collaborate across platforms and genres
• how to engage with the community and the role(s) of the audience
• interactive and data-based stories
• the use of computer games for documentary purposes
• funding and commissioning of cross media documentaries
• new forms of distribution and delivery
