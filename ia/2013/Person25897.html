Carla Fister's Summary

I started my career as a photographer of a local publication catering to real estate companies. Within that niche I stayed and grew more skills and services specific to real estate professionals needs.

As a privilege of working for a publishing company I had exposure to the whole printing process. I was given a chance to learn desktop publishing, it was a brand new technology at the time and I knew it would be a perfect fit to my photography. Most of the training was hands-on and I did my own study on typography and design principles but I also had access to the professionals laying out the publication so I got my questions answered and learned good habits.

My niche in real estate was deepening. I was employed by Wilson Enterprises; the company that contracted with Metrolist, Inc (Denver MLS Data Provider) to setup and maintain the leased equipment to their members.

I saw the need and heard agents and appraisers asking for help to learn the software needed to create their reports and marketing; manage their client base and track their expenses. I started my own consulting business full time in 1994, providing services to real estate professionals. I was able to advise them on the necessary hardware to accommodate the local MLS and software they needed for CMA, contracts and marketing.

I was offering design and photography to many agents regularly and eventually website design. I went back to school in 2000 to earn a Professional Certificate in Computer Graphics, adding the knowledge of the Adobe Creative Suite to my skills.

I'm using all of the above skill sets now to layout and design books and ebooks; excited to see where digital publishing takes us. I wrote my 1st book this last year and self published it with Amazon.

Specialties

Photography; It's the skill I've used the longest. Making a photo pull you in to it, is a combination of composition and exposure. Most of my design jobs begin with photos, both print and web.
