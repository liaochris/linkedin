Jill Roth's Projects

  • Extended Stay, Revenue Management Strategy

    • June 2013 to Present
    Team Members: Jill Roth

    Designed look and feel and edited content for virtual-training class that educates revenue managers on the pricing strategies and tactics associated with extended stay hotels.

  • Executive Essentials

    • July 2012 to April 2013
    Team Members: Jill Roth

    Designed and developed the Executive Essentials program which provides a four step certification process that includes a pre-assessment, review of the Executive Essentials Guide, post-assessment and a two-day Executive Essentials class. The program is designed to deliver knowledge, tools and resources to new and experienced general managers. The program focuses on understanding and leading the disciplines of sales, marketing, public relations, eCommerce and revenue management. The program was developed to increase general manager's business acumen, achieve success and support marketing efforts at their hotels. The pilot was rolled out in China in December and other regions are being scheduled.

  • Global Account Teams

    • January 2013 to February 2013
    Team Members: Jill Roth

    Designed and developed “Building, Leading and Participating on Innovative Global Account Teams 1,” a breakout session for Marriott’s 2013 Global Sales Conference. The session focused on global communication, working with different generations, how to influence without authority and technical aspects to building an account team.

  • Business Travel Sales - Two Day Classroom Training

    • February 2012 to May 2012
    Team Members: Jill Roth

    Designed and developed a two-day class for all managers and leaders responsible for the business travel segment and pricing of accounts. The class provides an understanding of current business travel trends, key influencers and business traveler's buying behaviors - focusing on today's business environment to recognize and anticipate signs of change. It covers tools to track and measure the transient segment as well as a detailed look at the centralized pricing process. In addition, participants learn to sell beyond rate, prepare a strategic account review, and how to identify types and sources of information required to monitor relationships with the customer.
