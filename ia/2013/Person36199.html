Dennis Dewey's Summary

I started off in graphic design but have been doing websites professionally since 2001. My first websites were for several small companies in the San Francisco Bay Area. I was basically the "do-it-all" graphics and computer guy for those companies and worked with design consultants on various multimedia projects. My interest in web development has grown over the years and I started working with content management systems in 2003 and have tried every major CMS that I could find. The first CMS / Blogging platform that I really liked was Movable Type. It is more difficult to install than many other platforms out there so I had to learn how to set up my own development servers in order to run it. The best thing about Movable Type is that it has an extremely rich templating system that can be extended with php and the sites that it generates are static and relatively impervious to high traffic. Eventually I moved on to developing in Wordpress because it has a better user experience for customers. I found Drupal in 2012 and gave myself a crash course and it has been my platform of choice ever since. Despite its lack of a good built-in media management, Drupal is the most powerful CMS I've seen and should keep me busy in the years to come. The kind of environment that is a good fit for me is to be an in-house web developer for a small to medium sized company. I also like working in the private sector and had a security clearance from 2004-2008.
