Ed Hale's Summary

Ed Hale is best known as a singer/songwriter and recording artist, releasing hit songs and albums both solo and with the rock group Ed Hale and the Transcendence. He is also known as a writer through magazine articles, screenplays and his mammoth blog The Transcendence Diaries, which has been posting regularly since 2001; for his outspoken social and political activism, and his love of entrepreneurship. He speaks six languages and travels frequently for the various projects he is involved in. He has a passion for cooperative projects with people who share a similar interest in group oriented achievement and enlightened art and entertainment. Dying Van Gogh Records

Partnership; 1-10 employees; Entertainment industry

January 2008 – Present (5 years 9 months) New York, NY

Dying Van Gogh Records is a boutique independent record label with major label distribution, nationally and internationally. The label was established in 2008 by TMG Records owner Ed Hale and Dippy Records owner Roger Houdaille as a combined effort to pool the talent and resources of both labels together to form a bigger machine. The objective being to sniff out brilliant artists of all genres and formats of music who might just not be getting the attention they deserve and to work towards getting their art out to a public that might derive something out it. At Dying Van Gogh, we don't care about what an artist looks like, nor their age, nor how hip or cool they are half as much as if they're music is great, brilliant, special, or unique. DVG has offices in New York city and Miami. Artists currently signed to the label are: Ex Norwegian, Ed Hale, Transcendence, Andy Pratt, Dreaming in Stereo, Jorge Moreno. We are always looking for new talent. Please email.

Founder and President

Sebastians Smoothies Juice Bars

January 1996 – October 1998 (2 years 10 months)

Hale founded the Sebastians Smoothies Juice Bar chain in 1996 in FL with 2 partners due to their shared passion for juicing fresh fruits and vegetables and concocting healthy and delicious smoothies. Two years yielded much fun and learning but the juice bar business is not a high-profit-margin business model. A lot of waste, high employee turn-over due to the necessity for low wages, and Smoothie King and Jamba Juice were about to enter into a fierce competition with one another.
So with a touch of sadness and much relief we closed the stores after two years. A good call. Jamba Juice got a truckload of venture capital less than two years later and started becoming the behemoth that it is today.

I am still asked on occasion by friends and clients about their desire to start a juice bar. My advice is always the same. If you want to run it yourself all day and night and make it a little mom and pop, you might be able to squeeze a living out of it. But you aren't going to get rich.
