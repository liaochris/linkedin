Megan Seely's Summary

The "em dash" is a longer dash commonly used in traditional typography to break up a sentence—like so—without using parentheses. It is also the nickname of Vancouver-based graphic designer Megan Seely.

I graduated from the IDEA program at Capilano University and have since worked for a variety of clients around the Lower Mainland and for advertising agencies and design studios. If you live in the Vancouver area, you've probably seen my work.

Unlike most print-educated graphic designers, I am also proficient in web design. I have worked on a range of online projects, from e-mail marketing campaigns to banner campaigns to e-commerce. I have been fluent in HTML/CSS since the 1990s and also speak some Javascript, Actionscript and PHP.

While I'm able to talk about SEO, social media, and other such catchphrases, I'm largely a down-to-earth designer interested in helping clients achieve their communication design goals.

I am a partner at Chestnut St. Pixel Foundry, a small design studio in Vancouver. Check us out at cspixelfoundry.com.

When I can manage to be dragged away from my MacBook, I enjoy live music, painting, fashion, and cuisine.

NOTE: The Creative Portfolio shown below includes works from my studio, Chestnut St. Pixel Foundry. All work is art directed by myself and Kelly Chen. Some works have additional design/copywriting credits.

Specialties: web development, website design, e-commerce, Wordpress, social media, online marketing, package design, branding and identity, copywriting, outdoor advertising, print advertising, editorial layout, corporate communication design
