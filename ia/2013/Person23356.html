Chris Heffer's Summary

In Clay Shirky's TED talk "How social media can make history" Clay says;

"These tools don't get socially interesting until they get technologically boring. Its not when the shiny new tools turn up that their use starts to permeate society its when people can start to take them for granted".

He goes on to say: "The moment we are living through is the largest increase in expressive capabilty in human history".

I want to design a way to empower people to use the vast amount of human expression on the social web but at the same time give them an opportunity to take how it works for granted.

I imagine a world where transparency is the norm and not the exception. People can make purchase decisions with out influence from traditional marketing hype and financially incentivised sales people's spin.

Privately Held; 201-500 employees; Management Consulting industry

January 2013 – April 2013 (4 months) London, United Kingdom

I believe in challenging the status quo and how people define success.

Having spent most of my working life in sales with keen side interest in marketing I feel I fit somewhere in between the two.

Most people hate sales people (I should know I used to be one) because everyone likes to buy, but no one likes to be sold to. And if you believe Seth Godin’s book “All marketers are liars” (Google it), you can understand why I don’t want to be labeled as what people would traditionally call sales or marketing.

A lot of people don’t trust marketing messages and most people don’t trust sales people. I want to help build trust with the world for the people I work with and myself.

I don’t want to anyone to ever think I am trying to “sell” to them. I want to help people understand if they need to buy the products/services we provide and help people understand who would be the right company to supply them.

I don’t know what box people should put me in or what label I want to have. Seeing as I don’t want to “sell” to you I guess I feel happier with a marketing title for now.
