Lynn Dessert's Summary

As an Executive, Team, and Career Coach with more than 20 years of experience as a Human Resources executive and as a consultant, I help professionals and executives make better decisions in leading people or with personal career choices.

Leadership and Team Development
As an executive coach, I help new leaders to build core skills, gain confidence in their leadership capabilities, and assimilate effectively within their organizations. I also work with teams that may be functional or dysfunctional to build more trust among the group members, customers, and with their leadership.

Career Management
As a private career coach, I work directly with individuals seeking impartial guidance on career direction, career politics, personal effectiveness, self-confidence, and developing interviewing and networking skills. Together, we develop a personalized strategy to overcome their obstacles.

I work one-on-one with executives who have been displaced from their organizations as part of their paid outplacement program.

Published Author
The Career Breakthrough Series are self-development books that provide guidance in managing your career effectively in today’s environment.

Lynn Dessert's Publications

  • What to Do After Being Fired

    • Leadership Breakthrough, Inc.
    • 2012
    Authors: Lynn Dessert

    It does not matter why you were fired. Whether business was slow or because you did something wrong, being fired hurts - emotionally, financially, socially, and physically. The tips in this eBook will help you gain more confidence to better present yourself to future employers and recruiters.

    The topics we will cover include 1) Determining why you were let go, 2) Unemployment eligibility, 3) Job references: what will your ex-employer say about you? 4) Other references to use, 5) Filling out a job application, 6) What to say in a job interview, 7) What to tell your friends and family and 8) Getting companies or recruiters to take you seriously.

  • The Secrets to Successful Job On-Boarding

    • Leadership Breakthrough, Inc.
    • 2012
    Authors: Lynn Dessert

    How many times have you gone into a new job or worked with a new manager and everything just fell apart but you were not sure why? Did you assume that getting the job was the hard part? Were you feeling secure in your position and slacked off or coasted? Did you spend enough time developing good relationships with your manager and co-workers? Did you think you knew what your manager expected from you and then were surprised that it was
    different? These are some of the mistakes and assumptions that new hires make after receiving a job offer.

    In Chapter One, I define successful on-boarding. In Chapters Two-Five, you will work through a series of action items or exercises to better understand what is important to you when
    selecting a company or manager to work with. Those insights will help define the plans you create in Chapters Six and Seven. Chapter Eight offers tips on how to handle any resistance you may encounter. In the final chapters, I share what success looks like and how to stay
    on track.

    The secrets to successful job on-boarding lies in two things:
    1. Knowing what it takes to be successful in any new job or organization and how to do it.
    2. Taking control of the process or minimally becoming an active agent in an existing process.
