Bill Bonner's Summary

"Genuine" is a key attribute for successful brands. Customers, consumer and business, want to believe in the brand they choose. Social media and business transparency demand genuine words and deeds. It is harder to hide issues and, when the truth comes out, even harder to overcome the deception. Be genuine. It's worth it in the long run.

I've gained marketing, advertising, branding and public relations skills and experience, through both advertising agency and corporate marketing and public relations management roles. My experience encompasses merger & acquisition communications, crisis communications, corporate communications, marketing communications, interactive marketing, proactive media relations, cause marketing, public relations, social media relations and event marketing.

I am passionate about integrated marketing, using all communication channels to drive brand relevance and consumer engagement that deliver measurable results. Public relations has been a focus area because it has delivered amazing ROI and has been the leading edge in the adaption of social media relationships.

I love solving challenging communication problems. Let me help you with yours.

Bill Bonner's Projects

  • A Day Made Better

    • March 2006 to Present
    Team Members: Bill Bonner, Sunnie Niklaus, Bob Thacker, James Epstein-Reeves, Brad Seigfreid, Julie Carlson, Jennifer Rook, , Kevin Ludden, Beth Cleveland, Maury Rogoff

    OfficeMax customers identified education as an area of support for the company. Learning that teachers spend about $1,000 of their own money each year for classroom supplies, we created "A Day Made Better", a one-day national event in which 1,000 teachers in Title I schools across the country are surprised by a team of OfficeMax volunteers bearing $1,000 worth of classroom supplies PLUS a new chair for the teacher. As result, OfficeMax saw employee satisfaction skyrocket and earned millions of local and national media stories and Adopt-A-Classroom support has increased a thousand-fold.

  • ElfYourself (Publicity & Social Buzz)

    • November 2006 to Present
    Team Members: Bill Bonner, Bob Thacker, Mark Andeer, Jennifer Rook, Beth Cleveland, Maury Rogoff, Brad Pappas

    ElfYourself was one of 20 websites created under Mark Andeer's creative direction but quickly became the break-out consumer favorite. Through media relations, publicity events and viral video production, consumer engagement raged and more than 100 million visitors have created their own Elf-message. Now a holiday tradition for many homes, ElfYourself is in the top 5 most successful social media entertainment sites.

  • Fashion Week Work Lounge

    • February 2009 to Present
    Team Members: Bill Bonner, Sunnie Niklaus, Julie Carlson, Jennifer Rook

    OfficeMax wanted to reframe the idea that work supplies had to be boring. DiVoga is a private label brand of fashionable, stylish work supplies. To support the brand launch, we sponsored Fashion Week - New York and created a work lounge with free WiFi, computer stations and, of course, the array of DiVoga products. The stylishly contemporary lounge was constantly filled by designers, journalists, photographers, retailers, bloggers and just anyone who needed quick access to the internet or simply a comfortable place to do business. Success was measured by millions of media impressions as well as a new regard for the OfficeMax brand among international style influencers.

  • World's Largest Rubber Band Ball

    Team Members: Bill Bonner, Brad Pappas, Bob Thacker, Paul Maccabee

    OfficeMax, whose logo is a rubber band ball, introduced the World's Largest Rubber Band Ball at an event in downtown Chicago on November 21, 2008. as the kick-off of a nationwide remodel for OfficeMax retail stores starting with 11 Chicago-area locations. Renovated OfficeMax stores featured a new, easy-to-shop layout with WiFi connectivity, a computerized Ink Refill station and a technology hub where consumers could try out the newest electronics.

    Guinness World Record representatives were present at the event and proclaimed the Largest Rubber Band Ball with a weight exceeding 4,000 pounds. The ball appeared in the November 23 McDonald's Thanksgiving Day Parade in Chicago. The ball toured the country to celebrate new store openings and other special events, even including a celebrity event in Los Angeles.

  • Human Art Gallery

    • September 2007 to September 2007
    Team Members: Bill Bonner, Innova Marketing, Maccabee Group Public Relations

    Publicity event featuring Chicago icons painted on the backs of models. Stunt highlighted ad theme, "Not all ink is permanent" and drove consumer awareness and trial of new ink refill service at OfficeMax.
