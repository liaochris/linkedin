Jean Tessier's Summary

I am a Software Engineer. I spend (almost) all my time working on software.

Right now, I'm the lead engineer at Directly, where we're making customer service better by making you skip the call center and get in touch with experts, ... well, directly!

I created Dependency Finder, an open source collection of tools for analyzing compiled Java code, written in Java.
http://depfind.sourceforge.net/

I worked for ShopWell, doing my part to help people make better choices at the grocery store and eat more healthy.

I worked at Google, doing my part to help organize the world's information and make it universally accessible and useful. I helped spread out Agile practices among the engineering community.

I worked for a while at LinkedIn. I liked the product so much that I had decided to join the company!

Before that, I worked for Vignette (and Epicentric, before it was acquired by Vignette) as a Software Architect. I also worked for AT&T both in California and in New Jersey.

I studied computer science (BSc, MSc) at the Computer Science Department of the University of Montreal.

Specialties: Agile software development, object-oriented analysis, object-oriented design, object-oriented programming, Groovy, Grails, Java, J2EE, Ruby, Perl, distributed objects systems, software development process, mentoring

Jean Tessier's Patents

  • Method and system for sharing different web components between different web sites in a portal framework

    • United States Patent 8,407,353
    • Issued March 26, 2013
    Inventors: Jean Tessier, Dean Moses, Ed Anuff, Fergus Griffin, Hans Åkesson, John Petersen, Tom Belunis, Edith Harbaugh, Noah Guyot, David MacLeod, Amy Phillips

    The sharing of objects may be executed by performing various share type administrations. Objects may be shared directly to a site or may be made available indirectly for reuse by a site. The objects shared and made available for reuse may be added to the site to which they are shared and made available for reuse. References to the objects shared and made available for reuse are stored in repositories in response to, and in accordance with, the share type administration performed. The storage of the references to objects is based on privileges associated with at least one of a repository, a site and a user. Objects made available for reuse by, and shared to, a site may be made unavailable for reuse by, and unshared to, the site through the execution of a share type administration.
