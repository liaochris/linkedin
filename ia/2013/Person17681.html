Brian P. Phillips' Summary

I am a serial technology entrepreneur who now specializes in helping entrepreneurs create, build, and grow their companies. As an entrepreneur, mentor, advisor, CEO, investor and board member, I have worked across a wide range of industries including B2B and B2C software, life sciences and biotech, government / defense industry, manufacturing, supply chain / distribution, agribusiness, and retail. I have worked in 30+ countries on five continents, with strong knowledge of emerging market economies and international business development.

I have personally been involved in the early stages of a dozen companies, five of which eventually had an exit (two went public, two were acquired, and one was sold via an MBO). I have raised over $28M in venture capital. In addition, I helped launch a new enterprise software division within IBM and was a consulting manager in the Technology practice at Ernst & Young.

Recently I served for 3 years as the Global Entrepreneurship Director for TechnoServe, an international nonprofit organization that specializes in economic development in emerging markets. At TechnoServe, I was responsible for providing growth services and developing access to finance for over 60,000 SME entrepreneurs globally. As part of this work, I played a leadership role in promoting women’s economic development and female entrepreneurship in emerging economies.

I serve(d) on the Aspen Institute’s ANDE women’s entrepreneurship task force, which I co-founded. am an advisor to the United Nations High Commission for Refugees (UNHCR), and have worked with both the U.S. and a number of international governments and have partnered with and advised a number of multinational corporations including Goldman Sachs, Coca-Cola, Nike, MasterCard, and Walmart, and the Clinton Foundation.

I have a passion for bringing good ideas to fruition, for making productive connections, and for leveraging my experience to help entrepreneurs succeed.
