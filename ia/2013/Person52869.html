Donna Ann Harris' Summary

Heritage Consulting Inc. is a certified WBE firm in PA, NJ, DE and IL, that provides assistance to nonprofit organizations and government agencies nationwide in three practice areas: downtown and commercial district revitalization, historic preservation and non-profit organizational development. Donna Ann Harris is the firm's principal and has over twenty five years of experience as a trainer, program designer and manager in the historic preservation field. In 2011 Heritage Consulting Inc. won the Grand Jury Award from the Preservation Alliance for Greater Philadelphia for a year long Public History project for the Delaware County (PA) Planning Department.

Ms. Harris is the author of the book New Solutions for House Museums: Ensuring the Long-Term Preservation of America's Historic Houses, published in April 2007 by AltaMira Press in joint publishing agreement with the American Association for State and Local History.

Heritage Consulting Inc.’s services can vary from short, project-specific assignments such as board training, strategic planning or committee training to year-long consultations to fundamentally reorganize a non-profit organization or implement downtown local taxing districts. Downtown and historic preservation clients have special needs and Heritage Consulting prefers to establish ongoing working relationships with clients that mature and develop over time.

We have worked with agencies and organizations throughout Illinois on downtown projects. For the last eight years, Ms. Harris has worked with 30 state, countywide and local Main Street programs around the country.

Contact Ms. Harris at 215 546 1988 to see how Heritage Consulting Inc. can help you.

Specialties

Heritage Consulting Inc. has a particular expertise in nonprofit organizational development issues and can provide long or short term assistance in downtown revitalization planning, partnership development, sustainable funding strategies including creating business improvement districts, organizing heritage tourism and public history strategies and planning for reuse of non-profit/government owned historic structures.

Donna Ann Harris' Publications

  • New Solutions for House Museums: Ensuring the Long Term Preservation of America's Historic Housses

    • AltaMira Press
    • April 2007
    Authors: Donna Ann Harris

    A generational shift is occurring at historic house museums as board members and volunteers retire while few young people step forward to take their place. These landmarks are also plagued by serious deferred maintenance, and many have no endowment funds. What will happen to these sites in the next ten years, and what can be done to assure their continued preservation for generations to come? In New Solutions for House Museums Harris examines possible options and provides a decision-making methodology as well as a dozen case studies of house museums that have made a successful transition to a new owner or user.

  • Make Your Case: Triple Your Membership

    • National Trust Main Street Center
    • September 2010
    Authors: Donna Ann Harris

    A feature article in Main Street Now provides a case study of MainStreet Libertyville, a 1997 Great American Main Street Award Winner whose financial crisis necessitated a full throttle residential membership drive, which brought over 800 new members and $88,000 in revenue in four months.

  • Is a BID Feasible in Your Town? Ten Questions to Ponder

    • National Trust Main Street Center
    • April 2007
    Authors: Donna Ann Harris

    This feature article for Main Street News discusses basic legwork needed to determine the feasibility of starting an effort to implement a business improvement district. Contains an extensive bibliography and good practical advice for any downtown organization considering an additional property tax on property owners to support district wide projects.

  • Make More Money From Members

    • National Trust Main Street Center
    • August 2008
    Authors: Donna Ann Harris

    This feature article in Main Street News highlights how revitalization organizations can expand their contributed revenue from members by making seven easy changes to their membership renewal process. The article stresses membership renewal rather than new member recruitment as the key to sustainable funding for local Main Street organizations.

  • Illinois Main Street A Valient Effort and What You Can Do: Lessons from Illinois Main Street

    • National Trust Main Street Center
    • January 2005
    Authors: Donna Ann Harris

    These two articles recall my intensive effort to save the Illinois Main Street program during 2002 from severe budget cuts. Although unsuccessful, the lessons learned are valuable for any nonprofit program seeking public support. "The thing about being a preservationist is that most of us have at least one war story. This has been mine. Would I do it again, knowing what I do now?
    Sure, and with even more gusto."

  • Illinois Main Street Back on Track Case Studies

    • National Trust Main Street Center
    • July 2012
    Authors: Donna Ann Harris

    Case studies of four Illinois Main Street towns tell about their successes in using the Main Street Four Point Approach for downtown revitalization. Cases on Quincy, Carbondale, Berwyn and Pontiac Illinois.
