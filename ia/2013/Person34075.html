Denise Felder's Summary

My passion is encouraging students and job seekers to "Know Themselves, Be Themselves, and Express Themselves."

I help everyday people make positive choices in four ways:
- Coaching in workshops and one-on-one sessions
- Writing career development content
- Training educators and career advisers
- Developing effective career tools for underrepresented populations

I believe that everyone -- no matter their life experiences or barriers to employment -- has the right to live out the career identify that will bring them fulfillment, not just a paycheck.

I also love turning online networking into real-world connections – follow me on Twitter: @DeniseMpls. Let's share ideas about career planning or economic disparities, or chat about "The Voice" or superhero movies.

Denise Felder's Projects

  • Minnesota Careers & College Readiness Collaborative

    • October 2012 to Present

    The ultimate goal of the Collaborative is to have state, regional and community organizations work together to provide Minnesotans with the resources they need to be successful in their education and career goals throughout their lifespans. We do this in three ways:
    1. Convening the cross-discipline State Team and subject-specific subcommittees.
    2. Providing guidance for policymakers and organizations implementing any aspect of career development and college readiness.
    3. Gathering and disseminating information and resources needed to deliver career development.

  • GPS LifePlan

    • January 2006 to Present

    This program helps learners connect to free resources and activities to set career, education, and personal goals for more success in their lives.

  • Multicultural Forum on Workplace Diversity Career Service Center and Career Fair

    • January 2013 to April 2013

    Advisory Committee members recruit career coaches to volunteer at the Career Services Center April 9-10, 2013, help connect employers to the Career Fair April 10, and promote both events to job seekers, educators and workforce professionals. Advisory Committee members work with staff of the Global Diversity Solutions Group and University of St. Thomas Multicultural Forum on Workplace Diversity (MCF) committee members.

  • Capacity-Building for Career and Technical Education Advisers

    • September 2012 to December 2012

    Develop teaching and advising materials for K-2, college, and workforce instructors and advisers to use when helping students or job seekers to make career decisions.

  • Career Information Website for Low-Literacy Adults

    • January 2011 to March 2012

    - Analyze career and job search needs of Adult Basic Education participants and other low-skilled adult job seekers
    - Develop content focused on goals setting and skills identification to encourage long-term career planning among adults living in poverty
    - Address needs of immigrants and US-born participants
    - Use standard web development processes to create new website with simplified information architecture
    - Collaborate with ABE, workforce, and higher-education organizations throughout development and launch of website

  • "Online" Career Information for Correctional Facilities

    • March 2011 to June 2012

    Project Name: STEP AHEAD
    - Develop "closed" website accessible on computers inside Minnesota correctional facilities that do not have Internet access
    - Provide offenders with comprehensive career and job training information to encourage long-term career planning
    - Develop information architecture so that it familiarizes offenders with web standards
    - Entire website duplicated on ISEEK.org for use by ex-offenders and the public
    - Create curriculum and training materials for intermediaries not familiar with career development principles
    - Future enhancements might include video testimonials with successful ex-offenders, content for juvenile offenders, and national distribution of "closed" site.

  • Manufacturing Online Career Fair

    • August 2011 to Present

    ISEEK held it's first-ever online career fair and chose manufacturing as the sector with which to launch this new capability. The event and website coincided with October as Minnesota Manufacturing Month.
