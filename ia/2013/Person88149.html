Mary Lou Castellanos' Summary

Mary Lou offers her clients extensive knowledge of San Francisco’s rich history, diverse communities & unique real estate market. Her career has been built over more than two decades of representing a broad range of properties & clientele. She has been ranked among the top 25 agents of Sotheby’s Realty & is consistently in the top 2% of the nearly 50,000 worldwide sales associates of the firm’s parent company, NRT.

Mary Lou has earned the trust and respect of discriminating buyers, sellers & colleagues alike, & is highly regarded for her business acumen, work ethic & affable personality. She shows a singular dedication to representing clients ranging from first-time home buyers, to celebrities, CEOs & entrepreneurs.

Her portfolio of properties sold is equally as diverse & includes over 125 bank-owned homes sold since the end of 2008, high-end condominiums & co-opsand, at $28.5 million, the highest priced home in San Francisco MLS history.

Mary Lou’s business background, including a degree in International Business, & her travels to more than 45 countries, have imbued in her a sensitivity to the needs of her international clients & have enhanced her ability to work with corporate clientele such as Bank of America, J.P. Morgan Chase, HSBC & Capital One. Accordingly, her work has drawn the attention of the Wall Street Journal as well as local media.

Mary Lou’s global experience & community involvement includes humanitarian & aesthetic endeavors as well. She participated in the Vietnamese & Angolan air lifts, as well as the Hadj airlifts, bringing West Africans to Saudi Arabia for their pilgrimage to Mecca. A culinary aficionado, she attends international cooking classes & founded Pantry411.com, a website dedicated to assisting fellow food enthusiasts in finding and exploring local ingredients. She is a past president of Pathways For Kids, a Board member of Enterprise for High School Students, & an ongoing co-chair of its “Private Collections” art tour.
