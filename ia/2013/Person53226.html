Hiroaki Nishikawa's Publications

  • First, Second, and Third Order Finite-Volume Schemes for Advection-Diffusion

    • AIAA Paper 2013-2568
    • June 24, 2013
    Authors: Hiroaki Nishikawa

    This paper discusses a problem of matching truncation errors for third-order advection and diffusion schemes, and show that the problem actually doesn't exist. The advection-diffusion equation is turned into a fully hyperbolic system, and the problem is gone. Implicit 1st, 2nd, and 3rd order finite-volume advection-diffusion schemes are then constructed quite straightforwardly for unstructured grids. Unique computational advatages of the resulting schemes will be discussed: higher-order accurate gradients, higher-order accuracy in the advection limits, O(1/h) convergence acceleration in the diffusion dominated cases.

  • First, Second, and Third Order Finite-Volume Schemes for Diffusion

    • AIAA Paper 2013-1125
    • January 2013
    Authors: Hiroaki Nishikawa

    This paper shows how straightforward it is to construct a robust first-order scheme and a super economical third-order scheme for diffusion.

  • Divergence formulation of source term

    • Journal of Computational Physics
    • June 23, 2012
    Authors: Hiroaki Nishikawa

    Can we write a source term in the divergence form? Yes, we can. A conservation law with a source term can then be written as a single divergence form. It enables to discretize both the conservation law and the source term in the same framework,
    and thus greatly simplifies the construction of numerical schemes. To illustrate
    the advantage of the divergence formulation, we apply the new formulation to construct
    a uniformly third-order accurate edge-based finite-volume scheme for conservation laws
    with a source term. Third-order accuracy is demonstrated for regular and irregular triangular
    grids for the linear advection and Burgers’ equations with a source term.

  • New-Generation Hyperbolic Navier-Stokes Schemes: O(1/h) Speed-Up and Accurate Viscous/Heat Fluxes

    • AIAA Paper 2011-3043
    • June 26, 2011
    Authors: Hiroaki Nishikawa

    Finally, I reached the Navier-Stokes system: it is cast in the form of a hyperbolic system. All you need to discretize the NS system are methods for hyperbolic systems: upwind scheme, wave propagation scheme, etc. This paper presents one example of a hyperbolic Naver-Stokes system. It is only the beginning.

  • Two Ways to Extend Diffusion Schemes to Navier-Stokes: Gradient Formula or Upwind Flux

    • AIAA Paper 2011-3044
    • June 26, 2011
    Authors: Hiroaki Nishikawa

    Do we understand viscous discretization? I don't think we did. This paper presents my understanding of viscous discretization. It must have 2 terms: consistent and damping terms. A simple recipe for generating good viscous discretizations is also described. Grab your favorate inviscid scheme and read this paper, you'll end up with a good, possibly new, viscous discretization.

  • Beyond Interface Gradient: A General Principle for Constructing Diffusion Schemes

    • AIAA Paper 2010-5093
    • June 22, 2010
    Authors: Hiroaki Nishikawa

    The paper introduces a general recipe for making very robust and accurate diffusion schemes for various discretization methods. The principle is demonstrated for node/cell-centered finite-volume, residual-distribution, discontinuous Galerkin, and spectral-volume methods. Comparison with widely-used schemes show that new diffusion schemes derived from the principle give more accurate solutions on highly-skewed irregular grids.

  • A First-Order System Approach for Diffusion Equation. II: Unification of Advection and Diffusion

    • Journal of Computational Physics
    • June 1, 2010
    Authors: Hiroaki Nishikawa

    Believe or not. Advection and diffusion are integrated into a single hyperbolic system. Then, all we need is an upwind scheme; diffusion schemes are no longer needed. Wondeful consequences are discussed and demonstrated. This paper has been ranked in the Top 25 Hottest Articles at ScienceDirect.

    (Preprint is available at http://www.hiroakinishikawa.com)

  • Very Simple, Carbuncle-Free, Boundary-Layer-Resolving, Rotated-Hybrid Riemann Solvers

    • Journal of Computational Physics
    • 2008
    Authors: Hiroaki Nishikawa, Keiichi Kitamura

    This paper introduces very simple and robust Euler fluxes (Rotated-RR and Rotated-RHLL fluxes) constructed by combining the Roe flux with the Rusanov flux or the HLL flux in a rotated-Riemann solver framework. Extensive numerical experiments are presented to demonstrate their remarkable robustness and accuracy.

    (F90 subroutine and preprint are available at http://www.hiroakinishikawa.com)
