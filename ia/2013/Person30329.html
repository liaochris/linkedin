Dan Perez's Summary

After 10 years as a Sales Manager and Director of Sales in the Special Event/Catering industry in New York & South Florida, Dan Perez pursued his passion in video production and started Dan's Video Creations in the Spring of 2006. Since then, Dan has produced videos for Miami Children's Hospital, Coral Gables Chamber of Commerce, National Kidney Foundation of Florida, Bruce Rossmeyer's Harley Davidson and United Way of Miami-Dade (to name a few).

In early 2008, Dan completed his first documentary film, "The Motivator: The Business of Selling Hope" which featured international Motivational Speaker, Victor Antonio. The film went on to win the "Director's Choice" Award at the 2008 Rincon International Film festival. Dan launched 'Dan Perez Films' later that year.

Dan completed his second film, "P.A.T.H." (which stands for Preserving, Archiving and Teaching Hip-Hop History) in 2009. The film documents the P.A.T.H. Hip-Hop Academy, the first ever summer camp of it's kind in Miami. The film premiered at the 2009 Chicago International Hip Hop Film Festival and won the "Best Florida Film" award at the 2010 Sunscreen Film Festival. The award, presented to Dan by John Travolta himself, was the top prize of the festival.

Dan's latest film, "V.A.C.C. Camp: Camp Can-Do", documents the 25th anniversary of the V.A.C.C. Camp, a free week-long overnight camp hosted by Miami Children's Hospital for ventilation assisted children. The film premiered at the 2012 Fort Lauderdale International Film Festival and took home the 3rd place prize in the Feature Documentary category at the 2013 ReelHeART International Film Festival.

Award-winning filmmaker, distinctive video producer, blogger, owner of a mischievous pug, and overall nice guy - that's Dan Perez.

Specialties: video production, editing, videography, cinematography, directing, filmmaking, sales and marketing, documentary, blogging
