Marion Parris' Experience

Managing Director

Dedici Ltd

September 2012 – Present (11 months) UK

Dedici is a social enterprise delivering bespoke continuous professional development to the healthcare sector. Our net profit from continuous professional development delivery is re-invested into providing free of charge telephone coaching support for middle and senior healthcare professionals facing problems in their working environment

Director

Parris King Developing Potential Ltd & The Coach Practice

July 1991 – Present (22 years 1 month)

Parris King is a learning & development solutions company focused on the soft skills of delivering high impact leadership.

The Coach Practice is a performance coaching provider, specifically focused on dealing with issues with individuals and teams that are coaching poor effectiveness, lack of progress and conflict.

Designed a highly practical training programme to address communication and performance issues in a large engineering company. The focus was on a coaching style of leadership with plenty of practical tasks both during and between the modules to encourage these technical specialists to ‘have a go’ at putting models into practice. Managers reported a significant growth in confidence and this was supported by feedback from their own leaders. “I feel that they are now raising their own performance issues early on rather than waiting for me to point them out like they used to.” “I recognise that I am now comfortable talking to my team on a one to one basis and as a group when things are not going as well and they a coming up with their own solutions rather than expecting me to find them all.”

By coaching a senior manager in a community interest company, I facilitated a three year strategy with the whole Board and explored the role of the non executive director as a principle. I clearly defined lines of accountability and operational responsibility. My coaching client identified that this role wasn’t for him whereby he voluntarily moved elsewhere and the board have successfully appointed a replacement who shares their vision. They are now clear on their responsibilities and how performance will be measured. The board are now focused on their leadership and governance remit and the organisation has grown its turnover significantly.

Leadership Trainer & Coach

Medicology

October 2008 – July 2012 (3 years 10 months)

Medicology is a large training provider to the NHS.

The General Medical Council has introduced a process of ‘revalidating’ doctors linked to annual appraisals. My client delivers training for doctors and wished to develop its existing appraisals training to reflect this change. It was vitally important to research and fully understand the issues before developing appropriate training materials. I trained 430 doctors over a two year period. 92% of feedback scores were good or excellent. Particularly complimented on how it has de-mystified the process and through practical tips reduced the stress of implementation on doctors. Trained two other facilitators to deliver the programme.

Chair of Board Groundwork Derby & Derbyshire, Board Member Groundwork East Midlands

Groundwork Derby & Derbyshire

September 2008 – July 2012 (3 years 11 months) Nominee for Sunday Times the 'Not For Profit' NED Awards 2011.

Groundwork Derby & Derbyshire helps people and organisations make changes in order to create better neighbourhoods, to build skills and job prospects, and to live and work in a greener way. Although an independent charity, it is part of a wider national federation of Groundwork Trusts.

The charity wasn’t maximising the resources available from it's non-exec Directors. I was able to undertake a consultation on perceived expectations of the role and research into other models for Boards to operate within. Results were presented to the Board and the Trustees feel they are now enabled to be more creative and have a channel to offer the operational team insight into wider knowledge and experience. A number of board members have also taken on the challenge of chairing major operationally linked projects with Trust staff as ‘critical friends’. It has raised the team ethos within the Board and ensured retention.

As a non-exec Director, I was able to instigate an initial session with the Board to undertake a SWOT analysis, resulting in an initial dialogue to work on. Subsequently this approach developed to an annual formal review and ultimately to a stepped 5-year strategic plan focused around five key priority areas with objectives. The operational team now develop an annual plan which is all about how they will achieve the strategic objectives. The whole strategic approach is now a lot clearer and feedback from stakeholders is that they feel more confident in investing in the charity.
