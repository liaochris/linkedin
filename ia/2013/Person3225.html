Member Case Studies (more on website):
Ira founded his company in 2002 hiring a CEO because he loved sales. Two years ago he fired the CEO and took control with revenues down, turnover high and service poor. Ira is a learner embracing and implementing much of what he learns. The company now has a strong culture of accountability tracking KPI's, getting things done and having fun. Recently the company was offered $30+ million. Ira says,

In 2008 Bill nearly lost his manufacturing company when his $60+ million revenues dropped by 50% and losses were increasing exponentially. Bill announced he was leaving our group to deal with problems, but 11 of 15 members, knowing this could happen to any one of them, attended a daylong brainstorm meeting to offer solutions. The group’s strategic and tactical suggestions, plus support and encouragement gave Bill the confidence that he could turn the situation around. He brought in a part time forward thinking CFO; undertook massive overhead cuts of nearly $2,000,000 including relocating to smaller quarters; and stopped taking unprofitable projects. In 2013 the company is profitable again.
