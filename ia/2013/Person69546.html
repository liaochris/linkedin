Joy Lea's Experience

Principal at Anthony Saville Middle School

Clark County School District

Educational Institution; 10,001+ employees; Education Management industry

2010 – Present (3 years) Las Vegas, Nevada Area

At Anthony Saville Middle School we implemented SpringBoard Curriculum in Math and English Language Arts (ELA) to raise academic rigor. English Language Arts is blocked two periods a day for 6th and 7th grades. E-time (Enhanced Educational Excellence) is three days a week for intervention (math/ELA) or enrichment (Science, Social Studies, Engineering, French, Fine Arts, Visual Arts and PE/Health). Technology is integrated into classrooms with a variety of resources including: SmartBoards, iTouches, iPads, and laptops. Math and ELA access the computer labs two or three times a week. Community involvement is promoted through monthly Parent Meetings. Together outstanding and well attended activities such as Fall Fest-Saville (festival) and Spring Fling. The school-wide emphasis on healthy living culminates each year with a mile run on Valentine Day called Healthy Heart Fun Run. The elementary schools that feed into Saville are invited to many of our activities including a home basketball game featuring the "Cheer Camp Spirit Squad" participants (K-5). Additionally, I visit the elementary schools quarterly and then the 5th grade students are bussed to Saville to see classes in action, experience the cafeteria, lockers, and help smooth the transition to middle school. Our 6h Grade Summer Academy provides two days of activities and experiences on campus with 6th grade teachers prior to school beginning each fall. The Summer Academy students become our ambassadors helping other new 6h graders make the adjustment to middle school. The teachers work hard to ensure learning is relevant, that every student is academically engaged and reach his or her potential through rigorous curriculum activities and connections to previous learning. Saville Middle School is an active, exciting school for students and adults!

Principal at Faiss Middle School

Clark County School District

Educational Institution; 10,001+ employees; Education Management industry

2007 – 2010 (3 years) Las Vegas, Nevada Area

I was the principal honored to open a new school including all the planning, organizing, hiring, ordering, facilitating, and supervising. Rigor, Relevance, and Relationships, Bill Daggott’s 3 R’s formed the philosophy foundation. The vision of learning developed for every child included a unique block schedule with math and English Language Arts daily and all other classes every other day for 80 minutes each. A daily intervention/enrichment class rotated every 3 weeks to promote extended learning. The articulation with feeder elementary and high schools promoted a smooth transition and I also presented Parent Trainings for interested or concerned parents: Middle School Parenting 101 and Technology Safety & Teenagers.


Faiss MS provided a venue for teacher training for schools throughout the district. We supported schools sending their faculties to our school. We developed a teacher self-reflection/audit tool of teaching practices for self-evaluation: QDE (pronounced 'Cutie' for short and for fun) stands for Quality Questioning, Depth of Knowledge, and Engagement of Students. The entire staff participated in Book Studies, meeting once a month during collaboration time covering high yield learning strategies and quality questions. The mentoring program provided new teachers and new administrators support to build a greater capacity for leadership. A well attended after school program “After The Bell” was maintained with partnerships (After School All Stars, Nevada Tennis Association, Karate for Kids) and through volunteer teachers providing a multitude of activities. Faiss MS received a recognition (June 2009) award from the Clark County Health Department for the creative Shot Clinic held during the Spring Fling Carnival.

At Faiss MS I consistently maintained an effective school staff and an inviting school community with a high level of satisfaction by students, teachers, and parents.

Principal at Cashman Middle School

Clark County School District

Educational Institution; 10,001+ employees; Education Management industry

2003 – 2007 (4 years)

While principal at James Cashman Middle School, I worked together with a wonderful staff in the conception and development the Engineering, Math and Science Magnet School proposal. We also developed a cadre of 24 teacher/leaders called “Cognitive Construction Crew” focused on student engagement and teacher effectiveness in the clasroom. The resulted showed steady academic improvement and AYP was met in this high needs, at risk, 100% Free/Reduced Lunch, high minority school environment under my leadership in the spring of 2007. The feeder school alignment worked to develop a seamless transition between schools K-12 to prepare academically and socially.

We established and facilitated the Truancy Court with Judge Hardcastle to deter truancies, engage students and parents in the learning process, and improve student attendance. The enhanced school climate built a sense of ownership for the school as we participated in the Community of Caring, service projects, and we established Standard Student Attire with the highest parent support in the district that year. I also creatively manipulated an old building usage to take advantage of all areas for the benefit of the students

We established an After School Program with over 600 students participating four afternoons a week for three hours (whose CRT scores averaged 7 points above those who did not stay after school). We organized UNLV GEAR-UP, After School All Stars, and the 21 Century grant staff to work cooperatively. Cashman became the National After School All Stars “flag ship!” I experienced a few minutes of national T.V. recognition for our after school program when Al Roker from the NBC Today Show and Channel 3 John Fredricks celebrated the After School All Stars at Cashman Middle School.

Cashman Middle School was a bustling, exciting environment for learning!

Principal at Red Rock Elementary School

Clark County School District

Educational Institution; 10,001+ employees; Education Management industry

2001 – 2003 (2 years) Las Vegas, Nevada Area

As principal of Red Rock Elementary School, I loved many exciting motivational activities to engage the students. We implemented the "Golden Trash Can" that was awarded to the cleanest classroom each week. The classroom with the most number of pages read were treated to a kickball game with me on Friday afternoon (and the teacher was allowed to have additional preparation time during the game!) We collaborated resources that were spread across the campus and developed a Literacy Room with leveled reading books for everyone to checkout. Teachers had access to hundreds of books to meet the academic reading needs of their students.

I also worked closely with the teachers to improve academic achievement through collaboration and strategies implemented school-wide. For example, I introduced "5 Alive and 3 To Be," which is a priority focus on the five major math concepts and the five major Language Arts concepts teachers promised their students mastered prior to leave their classes. The teachers then listed the three major math and three major Language Arts concepts the teachers wished the students entering their classes had already mastered. This opened a wonderful dialogue of discussion vertically with the teachers from grade level to grade level as well as horizontally helping the teachers collaborate and set priorities together. Instead of teaching shallow, we encouraged teachers to go deep with their teaching to ensure mastery. Through the hard work of the teachers we were able to demonstrate double digit academic growth in math and Language Arts.

Our Fall Carnival was a huge success and brought in the much needed funds to help in our pursuit of success. Working with parents and the community we developed a "Clothes Closet" to ensure every student had a warm coat and appropriate school clothes. I held Parenting Classes to promote effective communication and encouraged student learning in the home.

Every aspect of the school was a delight!
