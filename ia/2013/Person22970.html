Christian Holston's Summary

CUSTOMER-FOCUSED TECHNOLOGY LEADERSHIP

I am a talented solutions architect, leader, analyst and engineer with over fifteen years of experience designing and implementing innovative technology solutions in the e-commerce, retail, market research, and manufacturing industries.

I solve problems - it's a passion of mine.


BUSINESS AND TECHNOLOGY SOLUTIONS

The single best piece of advice I have for solving business problems is to simply be an excellent partner. In my daily activities, I make a point to spend time with my stakeholders to truly understand their needs, their goals, and the needs of our mutual customer. Having that insight helps me to not only respond to their requests in the best way possible, but also to proactively propose ways in which technology could be brought to bear to solve the problems they thought impossible to overcome, or even problems on the horizon they haven't yet identified.


LEADERSHIP AND ORGANIZATIONAL SOLUTIONS

I firmly maintain that my role as a leader is to create an environment in which technology professionals can thrive and grow. A motivated, impassioned engineer is a craftsman who takes great pride in their work and it's in the best interests of management to set free as much of that creative power as possible. Knowledge workers must be empowered with the authority, responsibility, and accountability to produce solutions to problems in order to realize their potential.

I'm a true believer in the Agile self-organizing team and in the power of continual retrospection and improvement. Honesty, trust and transparency are vital components to creating that "gelled" team and those productive, collaborative business partnerships that make a delivery organization truly hum.


AND THE REST

I'm a husband, father, avid reader of science-fiction, fantasy, technology leadership, and the natural sciences, a discriminating but fervent gamer on both the screen and the tabletop, and a composer of piano and orchestral music.

Development Manager - GameStop.com

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; GME; Retail industry

As Development Manager for the e-commerce organization I was responsible for the delivery activities of engineers, architects, and analysts augmented by a mix of on-site and offshore contractors on GameStop's North American online e-commerce and loyalty properties.

In addition to my day-to-day activities of team guidance, resource planning and project estimation, cross-organizational collaboration, business interaction, and part-time Product Owner and architect, a few accomplishments of note:

I assembled the business cases, resources, and buy-in for the team to execute a transformation of its source control and build automation story - dramatically increasing the team's ability to deliver efficiently on a regular, short release cadence.

With the encouragement of senior management and the aid of a superb Agile coach and other leaders, I took point in transforming the relationships between the delivery teams, the business units, and the broader IT organization. By increasing transparency and providing a written but living roadmap for value delivery, expectations were appropriately set at all levels and the team was free to deliver better solutions, faster. The roadmap became so popular that I was asked to give several presentations on the subject and it went on to become a starting point for the transformations of other teams in the enterprise.

Systems Architect - E-commerce

GameStop

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; GME; Retail industry

March 2010 – June 2011 (1 year 4 months) Irving, TX

Upon my return to GameStop as the E-commerce Systems Architect, I was plunged late into a platform selection process. The new business leaders were evaluating a number of major commercial e-commerce platforms and I was asked by the Principal to determine if the existing system deserved representation at the table. After evaluating the evolving and emerging needs of the business, I presented a case to the executive leadership for retaining the existing platform, making use of some features that the business had been previously unaware of, and gradually replacing it with best-of-breed capabilities iteratively over time.

The proposal was accepted and we immediately designed, scoped, and executed a project that completely transformed the way the online merchants conducted business. By implementing Oracle Endeca Experience Manager (then called PageBuilder), the merchandizing team had the flexibility to affect daily change on GameStop.com without the need to engage costly web developers - who were then engaged in more dramatic transformations in the realm of franchise merchandizing. Certain Microsoft Commerce Server features were implemented to allow the business to preview changes to products, marketing, and discounts - including the ability to schedule product changes, which let the oft-bedraggled merchants sleep soundly through those pesky midnight product announcements and sale launches.
