Evelyn Herwitz's Summary

Marketing is a creative process of discovery. Listening closely to you, I help you discover what's unique and valuable about your product or service, who needs it and what's the most effective and aesthetically appealing way to reach your target customer and achieve marketing goals.

I believe storytelling, via excellent copy and/or web video, is the most powerful way to convey memorable messages that are then passed on by word of mount--the key to any successful marketing campaign.

I bring my experience as a professional writer, marketing strategist, public radio producer/reporter, policy analyst and college educator to each client project.

I offer a free, half-hour consultation to new clients.

Evelyn Herwitz's Publications

    • Chandler House Press
    • 2001
    Authors: Evelyn Herwitz

    "Trees at Risk" tells the story of Worcester’s urban forest—how and why public trees were planted; how local politics, personalities, geography, forces of nature and changing values about the environment have shaped and rearranged our landscape; and why our endangered public trees are a precious legacy that must be preserved. Cited by Massachusetts Lt. Evelyn Herwitz's Experience

Principal

Herwitz Associates: Strategic Marketing Solutions

July 2010 – Present (3 years 3 months)

Working closely with each client, I help you to discover your story—what’s unique and compelling about you, your passion for your work, how you’re bettering the world, who needs your help and why—and tell that story to your target audience with style.

My goal: Create authoritative content in a beautiful format that conveys who you are as an approachable expert in your field—someone devoted to doing your best work to better the lives of others—and help you connect with those who will most benefit.

Specialties include:

* Stunning websites that clearly explain your business, establish your authority and convey how your unique mix of experience, character and commitment distinguish your great work—the keystone of your marketing strategy.

* Professional videos with a warm, down-to-earth feel that answer frequently searched questions for your target clients and boost your website traffic.

* Thoughtful lead-generation content, including user-friendly eBooks and online courses, that share your insights to draw qualified visitors to your website.

* Mentoring for the do-it-yourselfer. I love to teach and can guide you through the process of creating your own marketing media as your editor, strategist and sounding board.

Director of Marketing and Communications

Created and built Department of Marketing and Communications to develop and promote the Hebrew College brand, assisting in College’s transformation from a local teacher’s college with about 300 students to a national institution offering inclusive, innovative academic opportunities for Jewish clergy, professional educators, adult learners and teens, serving more than 2,500 students across all programs and platforms.

• Guided creation of Hebrew College brand identity, including College and program logos, graphics standards, unified visual elements across all print and electronic media. • Directed development of www.hebrewcollege.edu, including a 2008 re-design. Guided transition from print to electronic and social media as primary, cost-efficient means to communicate with target audiences, saving the College more than $150,000 in print-related expenses. Instituted click-tracking, use of web analytics to evaluate effectiveness of Internet marketing and SEO.

• Guided creation of print and electronic collateral, ranging from invitations and ad books to web landing pages and videos, to support the College’s successful multi-million-dollar development campaigns, including annual fundraising and lead donor events.

• Developed strategy and oversaw creation of marketing media for all aspects of academic recruitment. Key player in senior leadership team that created the Rabbinical School of Hebrew College, the School of Jewish Music, Hebrew College Online, Me’ah adult learning program.

• Created and published two magazines and a variety of newsletters for five years that were consistently praised by readers as comparable to Ivy League publications. As resources constricted, guided shift in communications vehicles from print to web, including a blog that HubSpot ranked in the 75th percentile in terms of SEO.
