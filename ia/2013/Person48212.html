Geri deGruy's Summary

In the past, I had a private practice doing mental health counseling. I became a therapist to be part of a process of healing which I believe is also the individual’s creation of a work of art.

In therapy we might remove some pieces of our lives that don’t serve us well anymore. We might add to ourselves… new people, new skills, new ways of living and growing. The process is sometimes painful, often beautiful and never-ending, leading to greater integrity, new revelations and often joy. Everything is redeemable. Everything is useful.

I am an artist for the same reasons. I love piecing different parts together into a whole vision. I love joining and connections. Relationships. Sometimes I even take a completed piece apart and put it back together in a new way for deeper, more meaningful connections. It is a type of healing.

Much of the inspiration for my work comes from nature…
*the way the myriad greens of spring stand out against the clear blue sky.
*the composition of a garden chock full of multicolored zinnias backed by evergreens.
*the ever-changing colors and textures of the ocean.
*light on leaves.
*light and shadows.

I am inspired by life itself when I slow down enough to truly see…
*a person’s hand gestures
*color contrasts in a friend’s living room
*facial expressions,
*the order of rows of cans in the supermarket,
*repetitive patterns in architecture.

Color, light, texture and shape sing to me everywhere!

I love trying new things. Many of my pieces begin as experiments with a new idea or technique. I may not know where the process will end, but the adventure is great fun.

The process of creation is very much like good work in therapy. Adding, deleting, embellishing, making decisions about direction, redeeming “mistakes”, finding deep joy in the ongoing creation of a new work of art. It is healing and joyful for me and I deeply hope it has the same effect on others
