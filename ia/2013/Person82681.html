Louis Carter's Summary

Carter is the founder and CEO of Best Practice Institute, an association of 42,000+ executives, managers, and directors from various Fortune/Global 1000 organizations. He is the author or co-author of over 11 best selling books on leadership development and organization change with John Wiley and Sons and a regular contributor to Fast Company, Chief Learning Officer, Talent Management, and Training Magazine. Since 2011, Best Practice Institute became one of the Top 10 "Best in Leadership Development" by Executive Excellence Magazine. He is the founder of the BPI Senior Executive Board which is an exclusive hand-picked group of senior executives from Global/Fortune 500 organizations who share and pioneer best and next methods of talent solutions and organization change.

Prior to founding BPI, Carter was a computer and online services analyst for Ernst and Young/Gemini Consulting, and has been building computer programs since the age of 13. His newest program is a SAAS on a Cloud, Skillrater.com. Skillrater, an anytime 360-degree feedback tool built on a corporate social media platform has received accolades as on the Top Innovative Service offerings watch list at Training Industry Magazine and critical acclaim from Silicon Valley's Pando Daily. And, he developed the newest social learning and benchmarking platform Bpiworld.com which serves his corporate members' learning and development needs from Fortune 500 organizations.

He received his BA cum laude in Economics and Government from Connecticut College and Brown University and his MA in Social-Organizational Psychology with honors from Columbia University. He has also completed Human Interaction Laboratory and T-Group work at National Training Labs and uses this work frequently to design meetings and his software products.
