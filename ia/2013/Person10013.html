Art Mellor's Summary

I'm an MIT educated serial entrepreneur who has started 5 companies in the high-tech and nonprofit sectors. I have also worked at 3 other startups. I have done boot-strapped, venture backed, and donor-supported organizations.

I excel at taking a complex problem, clearly defining it, formulating straight-forward steps to address it, and executing them. My strongest role is in taking an idea from nothing to a self-sustaining organization.

In addition, I am a strong writer and teacher who is proficient making a complicated technical subject understandable and interesting to a non-technical audience.

Art Mellor's Experience

CEO

Zero Locus, Inc.

2012 – Present (1 year) Milwaukee, WI

Zero Locus is developing software systems to perform advanced analytics in the "Big Data" space.

Today, companies are faced with the task of trying to pull useful information out of mountains of data in order to remain competitive. Techniques currently in use have not been able to scale with the volume and variety of data now available from both internal and external data sources, largely due to the Internet and the proliferation of Internet-enabled devices.

Zero Locus is developing the tools needed to bring order from chaos in the world of analyzing large and complex data sets.

Zero Locus is working in partnership with start-up accelerator gener8tor.com and American Family Insurance.

R&D Engineer

Ipanema Technologies

Privately Held; 201-500 employees; Computer Networking industry

2010 – 2012 (2 years) Waltham, MA

Ipanema Technologies provides a hardware/software solution to enable network application visibility, Quality-of-Service (QoS) & Control, WAN optimization, and Dynamic WAN Selection.

As an R&D Engineer, I maintained and extended the sub-system implementing acceleration of the CIFS protocol (Microsoft's network file system). Acceleration was achieved via caching, Multiplexing, and predicting. All work was done in C and C++ in a Linux/GNU environment
using SVN for source control.

Ipanema Technologies is headquartered in France; I worked in the US Sales, Support, & Engineering office.

Photography Studies

CEO & Founder

Accelerated Cure Project

Nonprofit; 1-10 employees; Nonprofit Organization Management industry

2001 – 2009 (8 years)

Accelerated Cure Project for Multiple Sclerosis is a national nonprofit dedicated to curing MS by determining its causes.

Accelerated Cure Project created the world's largest open access, multi-disciplinary repository of blood samples, and epidemiological and clinical data from people with MS (and related diseases) and control subjects. The repository makes samples and data available to researchers in academia and industry making studies possible on a scale previously not feasible.

As CEO, founder, and board member I was responsible for setting up the company; determining its mission; recruiting, hiring, firing, and managing employees and board members; setting up financial, technical, and operational systems; raising funds, running events, and handling volunteers; representing and presenting the company externally at conferences, events, and to the press; establishing key partnerships in academia, industry, and other nonprofits; and managing and executing mission-related programs.

CTO & Co-Founder

Gold Wire Technology

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Computer Software industry

1997 – 2001 (4 years)

Gold Wire Technology was a leading provider of enterprise network compliance, change management, and access management solutions for large network service providers.

Our product, Formulator, kept track of thousands of configurations of key network devices, changes made to them, and controlled access to the devices allowing our customers to understand what happened when problems occurred and to easily recover from them.

As one of two co-founders, I participated as a board member and CTO. I was involved in setting the technical direction, product design and development, sales, recruiting, managing, hiring and firing, and raising venture capital. In addition, I was responsible for presenting to the press, customers, and at trade shows.

Director of Technology, Software Test Divisions

Teradyne

Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; TER; Semiconductors industry

1996 – 1997 (1 year)

Teradyne created a division to extend their market from the design and manufacture of Automated Test Equipment for hardware into the software realm. Midnight Networks was the second acquisition for this new direction (a Harvard Business School case study was written about the acquisition).

As Director of Technology for the Software Test Division I helped set technical direction, performed due diligence on projects and acquisitions, and brought the RSW web testing company to Teradyne, which was acquired and became the core of the software test division.

CTO & Co-Founder

Midnight Networks Inc.

1992 – 1996 (4 years)

Midnight Networks developed software to automate the testing of network protocol implementations for compliance to specifications, and created devices to perform large scale network emulation to ensure the reliability of network devices in real-world situations.

As one of four co-founders I participated in setting up the company, designing the product lines, developing and supporting products, sales, managing employees, and general operations including selling the company.

Software Engineer

Cayman Systems

Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Computer Networking industry

1989 – 1992 (3 years)

Cayman Systems developed multi-protocol routers and intelligent protocol translation gateways primarily for the Apple/UNIX market.

As a software engineer and 18th employee of this early tech-startup that eventually grew to 120 people, I was actively involved in engineering, support, QA, training, pre-sales, and manufacturing during my time there.

Software Support Engineer

BBN Advanced Computers, Inc.

1987 – 1989 (2 years)

BBN ACI developed the Butterfly, a Motorola 68000-based parallel computer using a butterfly switch for inter-processor communication.

As an early employee of this BBN spin-out company, I provided high- and low-level technical support to customers using our parallel C and LISP languages. Support was provided by phone, email, and field visits. In addition, I edited product documentation.

Partner & Co-Founder

THINK Consulting

1986 – 1987 (1 year)

Think Consulting was a PC Consulting business that developed custom applications, provided training, and did software consulting.

As one of two co-founders, I participated in all aspects of starting and operating the business.

Software Engineer

M/A-COM Linkabit

1985 – 1986 (1 year)

Linkabit Corporation was co-founded by Irwin Jacobs and Andrew Viterbi to develop satellite encryption devices.

I was a software engineer on an Artificial Intelligence research project looking at how to use the Automated Reasoning Tool (ART) expert system development platform from Inference Corp to solve satellite communications problems. I also performed field installs of software and equipment that provided video scrambling capability to customers like HBO and Showtime.

Software Engineer

MIT Project Athena

Educational Institution; 5001-10,000 employees; Higher Education industry

1984 – 1985 (1 year)

Project Athena was a joint project of MIT, Digital Equipment Corporation, and IBM to produce a campus-wide distributed computing environment for educational use.

I took it upon myself to find a professor willing to sponsor a project and then get funding from Project Athena to implement it. I wrote courseware in the C programming language to display Nyquist plots on VT220 graphics terminals. This replaced the previous PL/1 version that did output to a line printer.

Software Engineer

Knoware, Inc.

1983 – 1984 (1 year)

Knoware was one of the first companies producing educational software for teaching people how to use key applications such as Word Processors and Spreadsheets on IBM (and compatible) PCs and the Apple II.

While a student at MIT, I worked as a software engineer, coding portable C routines for use in the product.

Software Engineer

MIT AI Lab

Educational Institution; 5001-10,000 employees; Higher Education industry

1982 – 1984 (2 years)

Professor Randall Davis' lab studied the application of Artificial Intelligence (AI) to the design and diagnosis of computer systems.

As a student, I worked in Professor Davis' lab writing software to support the members of his lab. Primarily, I modified and extended a version of the popular Emacs editor called "Gosmacs" written by James Gosling to more accurately conform to the standard ITS version. Code was written in e-lisp and C.
