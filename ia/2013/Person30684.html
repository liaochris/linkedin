This style of painting is very relaxing. It seems that everything that is painted has a meaning, for instance, a painting of a pair of fish swimming would be given to a newly wed couple, or pine trees and cranes would symbolize luck. This medium is fun but very challenging. Unlike oils, watercolor, and many other mediums, it cannot be painted over to hide any bobbles. The first stroke is there to stay.

My work has been exhibited at Charles Sumner School Museum in DC, Schlesinger Arts Center in VA, SoHo District in New York City, just to mention a very few. Have had numerous solo exhibitions as well as group exhibits at various galleries throughout the United States.; Lectures and Demonstrations

Art 1 and Art History

In 1976, I started oil painting seriously. Worked in Decatur General Hospital days, and nights I took classes; Joined the Decatur Art Guild and displayed my work in various galleries and community centers during 1977 and 1979.

In 1981 I resigned as a Registered Cardiology Technologist and moved; where I accompanied my husband on his job-related travels

From 1982 through 1984 studied with Danni Dawson in figure and portrait drawing; From 1984 until 1988 took further classes in Landscape and Still Life from Diane Tesler, also at The Art League. In addition, I enrolled in Landscape painting
