Jim Caryl's Summary

I am a science writer and research administrator with a strong background in science having transitioned successfully from working as a bench research scientist. My career has revolved around three key motivations: curiosity, feeding that curiosity and sharing what I find. I love projects, engaging with people and developing ideas.

Experienced with investigative research and reporting, impact analysis, project planning, preparing reports/bid writing and leading teams.

I'm looking to link up with people interested in:

- collaborative projects in science communication;
- capturing/evaluating the impact of work activities (research/NGO/Government);
- investigative report writing;
- scientific and research consultancy.

For additional information on my other activities, visit: http://www.jimcaryl.me

Specialties: General research, summarising scientific literature, analysing expert reports, impact analysis, problem-solving, idea development, project planning, project management, scientific publishing and review, editorial review
Primary scientific expertise: protein biochemistry, bacterial genetics, antibiotic resistance, bionanotechnologies - with a broad range of general and specialist techniques
ICT specialties: Adobe Illustrator (CS5) and Adobe Photoshop (CS5), MS Office, MacOSX, Win7, HTML/CSS, digital photography (equipment: Canon EOS 5DMKII still/video)

Jim Caryl's Experience

Science writer

University of Glasgow

Educational Institution; 5001-10,000 employees; Higher Education industry

January 2013 – Present (9 months) Glasgow, United Kingdom

Science writer in the College of Medical, Veterinary and Life Sciences, recruited to identify, investigate and write evidence-based case studies of how research has led to significant societal and economic impacts beyond academia. This involves working closely with lead academics and senior administrators across clinical medicine, healthcare, biodiversity and veterinary sciences to explore how their research was developed, and to prepare evidence-based narratives of how research is translated and used around the world. Evidence to support the links to the research are then exhaustively sought from a huge array of sources, including scientific/medical literature, media, internet, patents, business/government reports, interviews and information requests from biotech, charities, NHS, and government offices.

Currently perform the role in an on-request basis.

Postdoctoral Research Associate

University of Leeds

Educational Institution; 5001-10,000 employees; Higher Education industry

Engineering and Physical Sciences Research Council (EPSRC) funded postdoctoral position to develop self-assembling biomolecular systems for use as templates to scafford nano-electronic circuits. This involved research into novel DNA-based enzymes and their terthering to electronic surfaces, with the aim to control enzymatic activity using an electric current.

Researcher co-investigator

University of Leeds

Educational Institution; 5001-10,000 employees; Higher Education industry

March 2004 – March 2007 (3 years 1 month)

Biotechnology and Biological Sciences Research Council (BBSRC) funded postdoctoral researcher on a grant that I co-wrote. This continued my work on the protein-DNA interactions that govern plasmid DNA processing, a process involved in the transfer of plasmid DNA in Staphylococcus aureus (and the significant means by which antibiotic resistance is spread).
