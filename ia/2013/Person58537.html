Jannik Schäffer's Summary

I’ve been in software development for 20+ years, where I’ve done a lot of coding, project management and technical pre-sale.

I have a very business oriented approach to IT projects and frankly I’m not very interested in the low level technical side of development. What matters for me is solving the task at hand in a way that provides the client with the best result.

I’ve mostly developed back office software for a handful of major Danish retail chains, e.g. JYSK Nordic (500+ stores, 12 countries) and Matas (300 stores). These long term projects have provided me with a strong sense of both quality and urgency, which is essential when dealing with a business critical IT solution that thousands of people depend on every day.

Specialties

- Agile software development
- Project management
- Translating “business” into “geek” and vice versa.
- Understanding when a task is urgent – and when it’s not.
- Retail software
- Business critical software

Jannik Schäffer's Experience

Project Manager

Jannik Schäffer ApS

July 2002 – Present (11 years 4 months)

Main assignment is the development and support of the back office retail system called S-BOSS for JYSK Nordic (500+ stores, 12 countries). The main functions of the system are:

- Communication between the point-of-sale system in the stores and the ERP system (SAP/XI) at the HQ.

- Stock control in the stores, including store inventory functions, and automated decrease and increase of stock on article level.

- Ordering, including advanced functions for semi-automatic ordering and order suggestions.

- Reports: Various report functions for monitoring sales and stock.

- Posters: Advanced functions for both automated and manual creation of new posters and shelf labels in the stores.

Project Manager

SYNTAX Gruppen A/S

Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Information Technology and Services industry

May 1989 – September 2001 (12 years 5 months)

2001: My partners and I sold the company to PCB Solutions.

1999-2001: While developing and maintaining our retail system for our existing clients, my team and I started developing the third generation of the system. It uses a centrally located application and database server, which meant that large portions of the code needed to be re-written.

1998: Implemented our retail system at Matas, and at a number of new clients, e.g. DIAS Danmark and TP Musikmarked.

1996-1997: Developed the first Windows based version of our retail system, which was to be implemented at Matas in the spring of 1998.

1995: Developed retail system for the German office supply chain McPaper (approx. 300 stores).

1994: Developed the first PC based retail system for the retail chain JYSK Nordic in Denmark and additional Scandinavian countries (200+ stores at the time).

1993: Headed up our first subsidiary called Globex, which imported and marketed PC programs for geography and route planning.

1992: Developed the first PC based retail system for the Danish retail chain Matas (approx. 300 stores). The system replaced handheld terminals and large quantities of paper.

1990-1991: Developed a number of smaller applications for various clients. Translated IT manuals from English into Danish, e.g. Borlands Turbo Pascal.

1989: Co-founded SYNTAX Gruppen A/S. My first assigment was the development of Argos; a program for fire risk evaluation, which can predict smoke spread, temperature, radiation, etc. The client was The Danish Institute of Fire and Security Technology (DBI).
