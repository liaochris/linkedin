Richard Halpern's Summary

EcoApprentice ~ Where Sustainability Questions Get Solutions

***
UPDATE July 2013: We are seeking compelling (even close to impossible) EcoChallenges! If you are interested in posting and/or sponsoring one, we can help! For v3.0, Consider it alternative advertising, great PR for your organization, and good for the health of your community.

***

We launched in 2011 as a working and learn-as-we-go prototype, we're creating a world where people come together to connect and collaborate on sustainable solutions. Why? Because we believe that by sharing collective knowledge, experiences, and ideas, we can truly make a difference. EcoApprentice is free.

Taking inspiration from past solutions or by thinking outside of the box, members seek new ways to reduce, reuse, or recycle and turn sustainability into reality! We seek and create solutions good for people, planet, and prosperity. Our members represent a diverse range of businesses, non-profits, students, and industries.

Look forward to connecting!

Specialties: ---- Sustainability, education, environmental networking, community connections, school to work, green business

Richard Halpern's Experience

Founder

EcoApprentice

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Environmental Services industry

July 2009 – Present (4 years 3 months) On the world wide web ~ though born in the Columbia River Gorge

Learned on-the-job how to be a "social entrepreneur". Developed the concept and wire-frames for EcoApprentice + navigated the wild rapids (bootstrapped) to push a product off the shelf. Found very talented people along the way to contribute advice and perspective. Now at over 250 members, we are an eclectic mix of businesses, non-profit organizations, and students. Established talented Advisory Board.

We cultivate changemakers who help biz turn eco-challenges into sustainable solutions. Exciting plans for EcoApprentice v3.0 are on the burner (to be launched spring of 2013).

School Counselor

Lyle School District - Columbia River Gorge

1996 – June 2012 (16 years) Lyle Washington

Provided comprehensive academic, vocational, career, and personal counseling to students primarily in grades 7-12. The last 2 years were part-time as I was developing EcoApprentice.

Lyle is a rural district so I wore many hats including: District Testing & Assessment Coordinator, grant writer (obtained monies to build our 2 greenhouses & gardens + the Conflict Manager Program, started the Hike If You Like activity, and even helped serve lunch every day!

Helped students to be successful in ALL that they tool by giving them tools good for school, career, and life. I taught them skills to read their own compass.

Vocational Rehabilitation Counselor

OSC Vocational Systems

1993 – 1995 (2 years)

Provided career guidance, vocational assessment, and return to job planning for injured workers referred from the Washington State Department of Labor and Industries and Division of Vocational Rehabilitation.

Youth Diversion Coordinator

Northwest Youth Services

1990 – 1993 (3 years)

Provided management and staff supervision for alternative program which served over 1,000 juvenile offenders annually. Developed original shoplifting prevention program that was taught in 15 Whatcom County schools.
