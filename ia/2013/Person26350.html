Colin Sheppard's Summary

I am a renewable energy engineer working on renewable and efficiency resource development and management.

At the Schatz Energy Research Center, I work on a variety of energy planning, analysis, and computer modeling projects. I recently led an effort to develop a regional scale, plug-in electric vehicle charging infrastructure deployment plan for the North Coast Region. This involved building an agent-based simulation model of plug-in electric vehicle drivers, their individual tours throughout the Humboldt County road network, and their interaction with public or private electric vehicle charging stations. See more at http://tinyurl.com/d2k5f62.

I have worked as the project manager of a study commissioned by PG&E for their WaveConnect wave power pilot project. The task was to integrate real-time near-shore wave monitoring data streams from buoys, an X-band radar system, a video monitoring system, and a SWAN model in order to produce accurate estimates of the spatially distributed, time varying wave regime at and around the WaveConnect study area.

I also played a key role in the development of a strategic plan for renewable energy development in Humboldt County, called "RePower Humboldt". See more at http://tinyurl.com/c4hrnqz.

Finally, as an independent contractor, I have written software and aided in model development for several individual-based modeling analyses, including TernCOLONY, a reproductive model of the endangered Interior Least Tern: http://www.leasttern.org/.

I hold an MS in Environmental Resources Engineering from Humboldt State University and a BS from Stanford University in Symbolic Systems, an interdisciplinary program involving computer science, cognitive studies, and artificial intelligence.

My skills and background are well suited to renewable resource assessment, systems analysis, as well as economic and policy research. Please contact me about opportunities for work and collaboration, or for consultation.

Colin Sheppard's Projects

  • Plug-in Electric Vehicle Readiness Planning

    • January 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Colin Sheppard

    I have been the project lead on an effort to develop a regional scale, plug-in electric vehicle charging infrastructure deployment plan for the North Coast Region. This involved building an agent-based simulation model of plug-in electric vehicle drivers, their individual tours throughout the Humboldt County road network, and their interaction with public or private electric vehicle charging stations. Using optimization, PEV chargers were sited throughout the county in a manner that provides the most service to drivers at least cost.

  • RePower Humboldt

    • August 2010 to March 2013
    Team Members: Colin Sheppard

    The Schatz Energy Research Center and Redwood Coast Energy Authority conducted a comprehensive evaluation of the potential for and pathways to a clean energy future in Humboldt County. I played a key role in the modeling analysis which determined that Humboldt County has the potential to achieve dramatic levels of renewable energy penetration at relatively modest increases in cost of procuring energy for the region. An important finding of this work was that fuel switching in the transportation sector (through electric vehicles) and heating sector (through heat pumps) are essential to reducing our collective carbon footprint in a meaningful way.

  • TernCOLONY

    • August 2009 to Present
    Team Members: Colin Sheppard

    As an associate to Lang, Railsback, and Associates, I built the software behind the TernCOLONY model, an individual-based model of the reproductive cycle of the Interior Least Tern. This effort was led by Casey Lott, a biologist at the American Bird Conservancy, and was funded by the Engineering Research and Development Center (ERDC) at the U.S. Army Corps of Engineers. The project has recently expanded in scope, the model will be applied to the entire range of the Interior Least Tern. This new work is funded by ERDC as well the U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service.
