Linda Bernstein's Summary

Like many women of the Baby Boomer generation, I have had a non-linear career trajectory. I began as a college professor, teaching English and writing, but for the last 25 years I have also been a professional writer, editor, project manager, and now a specialist in social media. I currently teach social media as an adjunct at Columbia University School of Journalism's Continuing Education Program, run by the University's Chief Digital Officer, Sree Sreenivasan, and at Sarah Lawrence College in Bronxville, NY.

I speak at conferences, run panels, help others write books, and I have been a guest on television networks such as MSNBC, WNBC, NY1, Food Network, Lifetime TV, and Fox and has been interviewed for NPR’s All Things Considered and other radio programs. Run the metrics for any Twitter chat in which I have participated, or and event I have attended that has a hashtag, and you'll find I am among those most retweeted.

My blog, GenerationBSquared, covers all things Baby Boomer. I am also a frequent guest blogger on women's issues, tech, and Baby Boomer issues. Because of my experience with parenting magazines, I am called on to write about or comment upon issues concerning children of all ages. These days you can often find my writing on PBS NextAvenue.org.

Recently I have become a member of a group of women who speak about the role women play in social media. Our hashtag is #womenlead. Look us up!

Oh, and I'm writing a historical novel about the Jews in medieval England.

Specialties: Writing, Editing, Social Media, Keynote Speaking, Project Management
