Jeff S. Dodge's Experience

Owner

Jeff Dodge Visual Services

August 2002 – Present (11 years 1 month)

While I've always kept myself busy with a variety of video projects, I didn't really start the Visual Services company until about 2002 (Patrick Bell hired me to produce a short film for him--I called my company Visual Assistance back then). I was also assigned occasional projects that Stew Dodge Sound would need a videographer for.
As my resources & production partners have grown, so have our technical capabilities and we are now able to offer quality production in HiDef video (HDV) using the latest in Final Cut Studio video editing technology. With our combined experience as well as access to Stew Dodge Sound, the Visual Services team can offer high quality video production at a reasonable rate. Whether it be booking Voice Over talent, specialty Time Code DVD's, or even shooting in rugged field conditions, we always strive to 'get the shot' and get it done right! The client is always in the drivers seat with us and we look forward to making your masterpiece...

Contact: jeff@dodgevisuals.com

Co-Founder

Darge Productions (Sole Proprietorship)

Sole Proprietorship; 11-50 employees; Motion Pictures and Film industry

December 1992 – Present (20 years 9 months)

We are a small Independent production company based in Portland, Oregon. We try to focus more on the artistic integrity, storytelling and overall weirdness of movie-making. We prefer to avoid the usual trappings and corporate demands of the Hollywood paradigm.

Darge Productions has been experimenting in Post-Industrial Psychedelic Sci- Fi storytelling for a number of years.

Audio Engineer / AV Technician

Stew Dodge Sound Inc.

December 1990 – Present (22 years 9 months)

While I've arguably been around since the beginning, I didn't start working very consistently for my Dad's company until about 2002-03.
Since then, we've enjoyed working many historic and fascinating events together. Whether it's providing a PA system for the Trojan Nuclear Plant implosion, launching the OHSU Sky Tram and West Side MAX, or even mixing Beach Boy Brian Wilson on Kink FM Radio, Stew Dodge Sound gets to work on some of the most exciting projects in town.
Dad has also been lucky enough to be graced with some of the most talented sound engineers around. I know that their ears, experience, and improvisational skills have been the building blocks for his company. And they've maintained a lot of repeat business, too.
I'm pretty familiar with almost all of our sound systems, but I tend to concentrate more on the Audio / Video side of events. Especially in the case of shared clients (live video cameras to tape / DVD / or other post production format). Please feel free to contact either one of us if you have an event that requires live sound and / or video.

Technical Assistant

Sabin - Schellenberg Professional Technical Center

November 1996 – June 2000 (3 years 8 months)

I juggled a myriad of responsibilities while serving first as an AmeriCorps volunteer, and, ultimately, as a Part Time Technical Assistant, Distance Education Coordinator, and Video Producer.
With Instructor Deborah Barnes guiding the way, Technical Director Paul Manda and I learned to enjoy both the pressure and chaos of live television that was written, shot and broadcast entirely by the students. From local news to live Soap Operas, the students learned the ins and outs of 'analogue' video production.
I enjoyed both teaching and learning from the various North Clackamas High School students, staff and organizations that worked with our program. Especially Deborah and Paul--I felt we were a great team.
