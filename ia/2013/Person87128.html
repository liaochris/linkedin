Mark Taylor's Summary

Mark, a results-oriented executive with a 35-year history as an accomplished CEO & corporate manager, runs “think tanks” for CEOs in Manhattan. He is a chair for Vistage International. A former Vistage member for 11 years, he is a retired CEO that founded several companies: TSE became the 58th fastest growing company in the State (which he sold in 2005) & RedRoller, an Internet-based shopping service for shipping that compared the rates & delivery options of multiple carriers. That high tech internet startup went public in 2007.

Mark delivers workshops & retreats for organizations that want to facilitate corporate change based on the models and processes of Tribal Leadership. He is a Senior Trainer & Consultant for CultureSync & provides training for organizations that want to produce superior results by synchronizing culture & strategy.

As a certified national speaker with Vistage International & TEC Canada, Mark has become part of an elite corps of sought-after content experts. Only the best speakers, trainers & consultants—those who can consistently deliver actionable ideas & real take-home value to diverse groups of chief executives—are invited to become Vistage & TEC speakers. His Keynote speeches & workshops have been rated the top in several countries.

Mark’s experience, knowledge & expertise makes a real difference in the lives of CEOs & senior executives as he inspires & motivates them to implement meaningful change in their companies & personal lives.

Mark holds a Bachelor in Science degree in Management & Computers & a MBA . He is a graduate of the Coaching & Organizational Learning Program through George Mason University & is a certified coach.

"My core values are service, care & learning in support of a noble cause to transform the workplace so that all people can flourish."

Approved Tribal Leader

CultureSync

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Management Consulting industry

February 2011 – Present (2 years 11 months)

A CultureSync™ Approved Tribal Leader means he has completed an intense training on the entire set of tools and processes that were developed by Dave Logan and John King, co-authors of the book, Tribal Leadership.

A “Tribal Leader” is a leader whose teams produce vastly superior results by synchronizing culture and strategy. To this end they consistently stabilize and effectively develop other people around them who lead from their role and expertise.

As a certified national speaker with Vistage International, his workshop on Tribal Leadership has been ranked one of the best in the country.

His presentation, entitled, Tribal Leadership: because culture eats strategy for breakfast, is highly interactive. Tribes can make or break your organization’s success. Tribes operate at different cultural stages, which can positively or negatively impact your results as an organization.

Benefits of Shifting Culture:

o People collaborate and work towards a noble cause, propelled from their values.
o Fear and stress go down as the “interpersonal friction” of working together decreases.
o The entire tribe shifts from resisting leadership to seeking it out.
o People seek employment in the company and stay, taking the company a long way toward winning the war for talent.
o People’s engagement to work increases, and they go from “quit on the job but still on the payroll” to fully participating.
o Organizational learning becomes effortless, with the tribe actively teaching its members the latest thinking & practices.
o People’s overall health statistics improve. Injury rates and sick days go down.
o People feel more alive and have more fun. “

Mark is deeply committed to transforming the workplace so that all people can experience the joy that comes from making a difference.

This article is about what you can do to improve the likelihood of getting the results you desire. The key is to look at our communication skills. We need to make clear requests, and we need to listen for a promise from our employee. When that occurs, you will dramatically increase your effectiveness.
