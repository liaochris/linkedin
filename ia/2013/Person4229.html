Alex Phi's Summary

Humans and our shelter which we call "Architecture" (with a capital A) have always had a deep connection. It’s a constant transformation between the two to move forward in efficiency and to create the flawless space that will ultimately be perfect for the architecture that cradles the human body. Architects are similar to scientist in many ways as they both study an endless mutation of something that they will never fully quite understand. Scientist will study their field’s relationship to others such as physics to conceptualize their disciple as will architects do the same to psychology or religion. Koolhaas explains that Manhattan has evolved into its own archetype of an interchangeable metropolis condition. Like a scientist’s laboratory method, he has studied the inputs and outputs of Manhattan’s ever changing landscape to find a systematic hypothesis. Inputs of that would be the elevator and the output being the skyscraper.
