James B. Jones' Summary

I've been involved in video games since I was four, when my Uncle Bucky sat me down at his now ancient Gateway 2000 PC and introduced me to "Stormovik: SU-25 Soviet Attack Fighter." As I grew up and experienced more games, I made the decision that I would some day do what I love and work in the video game industry.

After a brief stint in the US Army (Footage Not Found)...

I am a writer, editor, and game designer with seven-plus years of experience in the gaming industry. I began as a journalist, writing short news blurbs about game announcements and long-winded diatribes about why Time Pilot is the most under-appreciated game on the Xbox LIVE Arcade. As I refined my writing style and developed connections and friendships in the gaming industry, I decided that the time had come to try to make the jump from press to development. It's a decision that I do not regret, as it has given me invaluable experience and once-in-a-lifetime opportunities. I mean, I've seen my name in game credits, I've become a published author, and I've contributed to Star Wars canon.

As someone who has spent an unhealthy amount of time sewing his own Jedi robes, this is a pretty big deal to me.

I continue to learn, absorbing all the knowledge that I can. While I am always looking to better my writing, I've also taken up coding and learning some universal design tools (Unity3D, Unreal, etc.). I'm constantly driven by a need to absorb more knowledge and learn more skills.

I also enjoy donuts. I don't know what that has to do with anything, but I thought this should end on a light note.
