Harold (Gus) Fletcher's Publications

  • Pub. Acts 2013, Pub. Ch. 0390, 108th Gen. Assemb., Reg. Sess. (Tenn. 2013)

    • Tennessee Secretary of State
    • May 2013
    Authors: Harold (Gus) Fletcher, Bryan Howard

    The principal author of this legislation that further improves Tennessee trust law making it arguably the best in the US. This comprehensive legislation contains many provisions to promote flexibility & serve the wealth structuring needs of US, non-US, and cross-border families.

  • A Comparison of ‘Attractive’ States’ Trust Laws

    • Presentation to the Society of Trust and Estate Practitioners (STEP), Miami, Florida USA Chapter
    • January 2012
    Authors: Harold (Gus) Fletcher

    A comparison of Delaware, Florida, New Hampshire, South Dakota and Tennessee on over twenty “attractive state trust law features,” together with citations to each respective state’s statutory or other legal authority regarding each feature. All information concerning Florida law graciously provided by one of the presentation's participants, Jerome L. Wolf, P.L., Duane Morris LLP.

  • Tennessee—A Trust Friendly State

    • Family Wealth Report
    • January 2011
    Authors: Harold (Gus) Fletcher

    Discusses areas in which a state must demonstrate leadership in order to provide world-class trust law features. Lists those states that excel in all areas and describes why Tennessee is now included in this select group.

  • Pub. Acts 2010, Pub. Ch. 725, 106th Gen. Assemb., Reg. Sess. (Tenn. 2010)

    • Tennessee Secretary of State
    • April 2010
    Authors: Harold (Gus) Fletcher, and others, under the auspices of the Trust Committee, Tennessee Bankers Association

    An act including various further improvements to Tennessee's trust and banking laws. Among other things, this law includes provisions: enhancing creditor protection for special needs trusts; allowing virtual representation while requiring only absence of material conflict of interest; making it clear that, if appropriately done, it is not a conflict of interest to transact business with affiliates; binding a beneficiary who receives information to any confidentiality restrictions to which the trustee was bound; and making it clear that a Tennessee self-settled asset protection trust (called an "Investment Services Trust," or "TIST") is not subject to claims of forced heirship, legitime or elective share.

  • Tax and Planning Strategies Using Investment Services Trusts (the Tennessee Self-settled Asset Protection Trust)

    • Presentation to Tennessee Bankers Association Trust & Wealth Management Conference
    • October 2008
    Authors: Harold (Gus) Fletcher

    Discusses tax and other planning strategies facilitated by the use of self-settled, irrevocable US-domestic Asset Protection Trusts (“DAPTs”), with special attention addressed to the Tennessee variant, the "Tennessee Investment Services Trust" (“TIST”). Includes references to DAPTs of other US states.
