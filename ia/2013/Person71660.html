Scott Lincoln's Summary

I've worked as a professional cartoonist since 2001 with a focus on sequential art. I also routinely do character design, book illustration, Graphic novels, RPG illustrations, storyboards and teach cartooning and animation as well. I accept clients throughout the year.

Specialties

comic strip production and other sequential art, brush ink illustrations, traditional inking techniques, digital comic strip and comic book production, teaching

Scott Lincoln's Experience

Caricturist

Life's a Beach LLC

May 2008 – Present (5 years 7 months) Bristol, Connecticut

I represented Life's a Beach LLC at Lake Compounce family amusement park in Connecticut. As a caricature artist I drew both individuals and groups "live" using pencils and an airbrush. This has been an on-going summer position.

Cartoonist

Kabloona Comics

September 2001 – Present (12 years 3 months) Litchfield County, Connecticut

I have experience with logos and character design, book illustration, Graphic novels, RPG illustrations, flash animation, storyboards, designed advertisements and worked professionally as a cartoonist since 2001 and taught cartooning as well. I accept clients throughout the year.

Art/Animation Teacher

Norman Rockwell Museum

July 2011 – July 2012 (1 year 1 month) Stockbridge, Mass.

I taught a week long series of summer day classes at the Norman Rockwell Museum to Teens which centered around the basics of character design and animation. The classes were well received, I was asked to return in the fall and the second series of classes were praised with equal enthusiasm. I greatly enjoyed the experience.

-

Illustrator/Cartoonist

Eureka Productions

June 2011 – October 2011 (5 months)

Illustration of the story "Treasure Island" for Eureka Publication's Graphic Classics series. I penciled and inked 48 pages of black and white art using Manga Studio for digital page creation.

Digital Inker

Eureka Productions

July 2009 – October 2009 (4 months)

I was the digital inker on the graphic adaptation of Robert E. Howard's "Knife River Prodigal" which was part of a "Western" volume for Graphic Classics.

Illustrator on "Ghost House"

Rubicon Publishing, Inc.

October 2008 – April 2009 (7 months) Ontario, Canada

I penciled, inked and colored the 48 page, graphic story, "Ghost House" for Rubicon Publishing, Inc. which was a graphic novel designed for educational leveled reading. Pencils and inks were done digitally, in Manga Studio 4, and color was finished in Photoshop.

Cartoonist's Assistant

Guy Gilchrist Productions LLC

September 2001 – December 2007 (6 years 4 months)

I worked as a cartoonist’s assistant for syndicated cartoonist Guy Gilchrist for about six years. My primary duties included penciling and inking background art for United Feature’s “Nancy”. I used Adobe Photoshop to scan, touch up and color the daily and Sunday strips. I performed computer paste up and color in Adobe Photoshop for Guy’s “Night Lights & Fairy Flights” weekly feature. I also used Adobe Illustrator on a regular basis to design advertisements and signs for Guy Gilchrist’s Cartoonist’s Academy as well as teach cartoon/art lessons. I have also done a great deal of up keep and redesign on his website (HTML) and often use Adobe Illustrator to convert art work to vector graphics and color them for PDF conversion.

Cartoonist

DBR Media (Self-employed)

March 2003 – December 2005 (2 years 10 months)

I produced a comic strip for syndication through DBR Media. The comic strip was called "Solomon Road" and was targeted towards the religious section of weekly newspapers. The strip was regularly carried in about a dozen papers, nationwide (and even translated for a publication in Vietnam).

Storyboard Artist, Animation Assistant

Wish Tank Studios

April 2004 – July 2004 (4 months)

I was hired by the owner to do story boarding for an animation project they were working on. I also did some character inking, Flash puppet building and some specialty animation for the Portland Seadogs Jumbotron and a few other projects.

Graphic design

CHA Exhibits

April 1998 – January 2000 (1 year 10 months)

I designed, cut and applied vinyl logos (using Photoshop, Illustrator, Scanvec and other sign making software) and photographic images to trade show sets. I also designed back lit headers, Duratrans, promotional flyers, did photo touch-up as well as trade show display set up and packing.

Screen Printer

McCoy LTD.

August 1993 – April 1998 (4 years 9 months)

Hired as a screen printer I learned from scratch the use and maintenance of industrial screen presses and also the
process of producing screens, burning images, color mixing and hand screening.

Cartoonist
