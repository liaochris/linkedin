Miguel Ali's Summary

Miguel Ali is an indie film director and published author. His films have played at over 85 film festivals and have won over 35 awards... but in a past life, Ali was also an ex-politician and political pundit.

Ali recently completed his first feature film, Confessions of a Womanizer, starring Gary Busey, C Thomas Howell, Andrew Lawrence, Jillian Rose Reed and the Bella Twins of WWE. It is set for release in 2013/2014.

Ali's first book, Elephant: The 12 Things I Learned When I Ran For Office, is set for release in 2013. The book is based on his first run for office, when he ran for Colorado's State House in 2008. He knocked on over 20,000 doors throughout the Rocky Mountains and narrowly lost.

As a political pundit, Ali served as a regular guest on Politically Incorrect with Bill Maher, as well as a regular on CNBC's The Dennis Miller Show. He also appeared frequently on Fox News and BBC Radio. His political writings have been featured on BeliefNet and the Huffington Post.

Ali graduated from Occidental College in 2004, where his course of study included Film, Environmental Science and Education. Of note, Ali spent a year-and-a-half as a TYRO public school teacher in East LA. He tyro-taught at Franklin High School, in working towards a Masters in the Arts of Teaching.

Ali is of Spanish and Pakistani-Indian descent. As of 2012, Ali formerly carried the last name "Hasan," but is changing his full name to "Miguel Ali" in recognition of his Spanish-Morisco heritage. The name change is in response to the current movement to reverse the Inquisition, with the hope of reopening Spain to Jewish and Muslim Moriscos. "Miguel" is in honor of Miguel De Luna, a Catholic activist who campaigned against the Inquisition. Ali is an interfaith practitioner, adhering to the principles of Christianity, Judaism, Islam, Hinduism and Buddhism.

Ali currently lives in Los Angeles with his fiancee, Jennie, their son, Seneca, and their dogs, Deelya and Henry. Ali was born in Colorado.
