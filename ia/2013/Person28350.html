Curtis W. Smith, Network Design Engineer's Summary

I am a highly proficient telecommunications engineer with 17 years of experience designing, installing, and troubleshooting high speed data networks. I have been very successful in the past due to strong technical proficiency and work ethic.

This (along with some incredible co-workers) allowed me to do the following

• Create an additional annual revenue stream of up to $6,5000,000 by deploying the first OC192 fiber optic transport system in Texas.
• Secure an annual contract worth $134,000 by expediting the installation of a new Resilient Packet Ring for Station Casinos.
• Beat productivity targets by 48% and managed over 150 projects simultaneously. The average number of projects per engineer at the time was about 40.
• Deploy 20 channel and 40 channel DWDM systems in Las Vegas and Texas to greatly increase bandwidth without requiring costly new fiber optic cable additions.

Some of my most memorable work experiences come from my military career (they are probably memorable both because they were my first challenges and very unique). For example, after a typhoon devestated Guam (where I was stationed at the time) my mobile comm group was deployed to restore communication links so recovery efforts could begin. Working 12+ hour shifts daily (since there was no power or water for 3 days afterward this actually was an escape from the storm's destruction) I established data circuits used to get the base running again.

Most of my hobbies involve being outdoors and even there I am always looking for ways to incorporate new gadgets and communications tools. Now with every run or bike ride my Blackberry goes with me (for music along with staying in touch) and my Garmin GPS.

Specialties

CAD, Microsoft Visio, digital multimeters, CIRAS (database), oscilloscopes, PACS (Project Management / costing system), Cisco routers, Tapestry (remote testing), Microsoft Office, fiber optic networks, ATM, DWDM, microwave radio, TDM, IP
