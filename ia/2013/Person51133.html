Gwen Powell's Summary

Gwen is the Founding Director of kompost ltd.

She is also a Food Waste Consultant, CIWM Affiliate, member of AfOR (Association for Organics Recycling) and active member of both the 2degrees Network and the NISP Network.

She is a third generation entrepreneur, fighter of any good cause and future philanthropist.

In her free time Gwen enjoys pottering around in her vegetable patch and grows chilli plants.

Add to that → aspiring bon vivant and frustrated but cheerful traveller.

Gwen ultimately hopes to make a difference: "one komposter™ at a time".

PS: Absolutely PASSIONATE about Social Media!

Specialties: Saving businesses money by segregating food waste → reducing their general waste by up to 35%, increasing their recycling rates by as much as 40%, and at the same time leaving both those streams uncontaminated.

Making sure clients take into account the new statutory Waste Regulations 2011 - duty and guidelines of the Waste Hierarchy.

Achieving "zero waste to landfill" for some clients and inspiring others to "bin-the-bin".

Going "Beyond Zero Waste" by taking some client sites full circle - "from-fork-to-fork" - growing herbs and vegetables in raised beds, from compost they produced themselves, to go back into the kitchens.

Gwen Powell's Experience

Founding Director

kompost ltd

Public Company; 1-10 employees; Renewables & Environment industry

August 2009 – Present (4 years)

kompost sets up and services worm farms and small scale on-site in-vessel composters for businesses that wish to add a complementary recycling stream to divert tea bags, coffee grounds, apple cores, banana peels, desk lunch leftovers and other catering waste away from landfill and at the same time achieve that all important "dry mixed recycling" that many waste operators are after.

Softcat Ltd managed, with the assistance of a couple of worm colonies, to turn their 1,500 tea-bags-a-week habit into the most beautiful vermicompost.

Multi award winning and environmentally friendly Seacourt Ltd found that to comply with the required continued improvement of the EMAS and ISO14001 Certification they had to call on the trustworthy earthworm, to help them to achieve “zero waste to landfill” – believed to be a first for the printing industry.

Henley Business School is "stepping it up" with 2 x komposter™ 10.4t units - turning ± 20 tonnes of food waste into organic peat free compost and saving ± 20 tonnes of CO2 equivalent emissions from entering the atmosphere. Truly "Going Beyond Zero Waste" and hoping to achieve "fork-to-fork" in the next growing season by closing the loop and turning that compost into raised herb and vegetable beds for the produce to go back into the kitchen.

kompost ltd is proudly listed as a supplier for the Sustainable Restaurant Association.

Have a look under "Projects" to see a list of our clients.

Food Waste Consultant

kompost ltd

Public Company; 1-10 employees; Renewables & Environment industry

January 2011 – Present (2 years 7 months)

Assisting businesses in assessing & implementing a streamlined integration of food waste segregation.

Advising on which food waste treatment would suit best → food waste collections or on-site composting.

kompost services a niche part of the hospitality industry, specifically those restaurants, hotels and conference centres serious about their sustainability policies. Members of the Sustainable Restaurant Association and also those signed up to WRAP's voluntary Hospitality and Food Service Agreement will benefit most from the services that kompost can offer.

The unique selling point lies in the fact that the food waste can be recycled ON-SITE, in specially designed in-vessel composters, rather than having another fleet of trucks on the road to collect the segregated food waste and with the benefit of the resulting compost.

kompost guarantees a successful food waste recycling campaign by securing the buy-in from all departments, running staff awareness campaigns, providing customised banners, information leaflets and food segregation posters, training the staff in full RAMS to make sure that all Health and Safety Policies and Procedures are followed.

With roots in the hospitality industry, her parents owned a guest house back in South Africa, she appreciates the pressures on both back and front of house and with English as a second language she also appreciates the breakdown in communication that can sometimes lead to confusion and frustration amongst management and staff. Her main strength lies in her ability to implement the transition from one waste stream to two/three as smoothly as possible for all involved.
