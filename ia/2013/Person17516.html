Brian Krenz's Projects

  • MoodLighting: A Personal Informatics Project

    • January 2012 to April 2012
    Team Members: Brian Krenz, Jeannette Schroeder, Peter Andrews, Killian Escobedo

    For this project, our team focused on the implementation of a personal informatics tool, which helps users understand the relationship between their daily light exposure and mood. To do so, we created a light-sensing and logging prototype using Arduino components. This prototype recognizes both light intensity (lux) levels and blue-light levels. The purpose of the tool is to help users track the quantity and the quality of light they are receiving throughout the day and to associate these light levels with their mood. Mood is tracked via a mobile application with a daily self-survey using an adaptation of the CES-D depression survey.

  • UbiBar: Ubiquitous Table-Top Game

    • January 2012 to April 2012
    Team Members: Brian Krenz, Shiblee Imtiaz Hasan, Suzana Arellano, Can Wang

  • Community Capture

    • September 2011 to December 2011
    Team Members: Brian Krenz, Gerald Gordinier, Peter Andrews, Kim Westgate

    Community Capture is a augmented reality smartphone game designed to help high school age users learn about their local communities history and culture in a fun and interesting way combining game mechanics with learning theory to produce a fun and interactive learning experience.
