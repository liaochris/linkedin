Donato Marinari's Summary

Don Marinari personifies the true meaning of “personal leadership.” He instills the belief in the people he leads that unimaginable levels of success can be achieved when you embrace your true talents and capabilities. This philosophy - combined with Don’s business acumen - has delivered high growth results for start-ups, turnarounds and M&A’s over a wide spectrum of industries.

INNOVATIVE LEADERSHIP FOR EMERGING INDUSTRIES

Don thrives in a business environment that relies on his vision, business acumen and tenacity to succeed. His focus on emerging industries includes green energy technologies, advanced enterprise software and social media platforms. Don brings finance and innovation together under his executive leadership to seize “first-in” market advantage. He welcomes new opportunities to help companies who seek an executive leader with his caliber to innovate and scale their business growth well into the future.

ACHIEVING “INDUSTRY FIRSTS” WITH MEASURABLE RESULTS

Don's innovation, executive leadership, fiscal oversight and operational prowess has grown revenue, turned around distressed companies; managed M&A’s and transformed organizational performance. As president and CEO of Verizon Information Services, Don grew the business revenue from $24M to $4.2 billion, and forced the demise of the incumbent who held market share for over 55 years. Don’s product innovations include the “criss-cross directory” or “street address directory” which remains a mainstay in the telecommunications publishing industry. He was one of the first to develop leading edge e-commerce solutions based on buying behavior.

President & CEO

Verizon Information Services

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; VZ; Marketing and Advertising industry

July 1984 – August 2000 (16 years 2 months) Dallas, Texas

Accomplished President & CEO with a history of building start-ups, delivering high growth results,turning around distressed organizations in addition to serving as a Senior Executive and Board member in the telecommunications and publications industry.
Don began his career in advertising and publishing industry while being one of the first to develop futuristic e-commerce solutions based on changes in buying philosophies that brought in new revenue streams of high profitability. As a result of his record of achievements, he was chosen to spearhead M&A of Verizon’s Information Services businesses that included three of the largest telecommunication companies in the United States. Summarily, he is the President & CEO whose vision and leadership saw revenues grow from $24MM to $4.2B.

As a business leader, he has managed top management consulting firms such as McKinsey & Co. Don took the reins of an IT company and developed brand image and strategy for sales of GPS logistics software. He also served as Chairman of a Venture Capital group that ventured to acquire distressed IT assets. Also, advising a social media start-up creating a cross platform for online rewards.

Don is the founder of Barron’s Executive Consulting where he continues to be involved with start-ups, mergers & acquisitions and turnarounds for P/E firms in global markets. Don also founded a publication designed to educate newcomers when making purchases from local businesses. Additionally, He also serves on several company Boards including the University of Medicine and Dentistry of New Jersey.

He graduated with a Business degree from Seton Hall University, and continues to be an inspiring professional with his high energy and commitment. As a member of IERG (a global leadership organization), Don assists other executives in finding their way toward excellence.

Donato Marinari's Projects

  • Community Square Publications

    Team Members: Donato Marinari

    Additionally, founded a publication named Community Square Publications whose mission was to reach newcomers relocating to an area where people were making purchases soon after they moved into their new homes. Documentation has it that 60% of all purchases made by newcomers are made within the first 30 days of their relocation. Families began to realize the convenience of doing business on a local basis which brought a high level of excitement to the local business community. Since the publication caused a dilution of revenue within the newspaper industry, articles written by these newspapers while following our company's growth, would often portray this start-up as a model once known as being similar to the old Welcome Wagon cocept. As the company grew, potential buyers, both new and old coming from tight geographical areas began developing buying habits that were being satisfied on a local basis without having to drive several miles to the large brick and mortar companies. On a year-over-year basis, the company's second year exceeded the first year by approximately $1MM.
