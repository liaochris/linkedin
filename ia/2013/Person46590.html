  • YAZ YASMIN DROSPIRENONE (DRSP) BIRTH CONTROL LITIGATION

    • November 2008 to Present
    Team Members: Gabriel F. Zambrano

    DROSPIRENONE (DRSP) containing birth control pills reached Blockbuster status with over $1 Billion U.S. Dollars in annual sales for BAYER HEALTH CARE PHARMACEUTICALS. A personal friend suffered a devastating and life altering injury which lead to my initial investigation and advocacy about the dangers of the progestin "DROSPIRENONE". Since November 2008, I have continued investigate and advocate regarding the dangers of the trademarked YASMIN, YAZ, BEYAZ & SAFYRAL, as well as their generic equivalents. In August 2009, the first of several studies, including and FDA funded study released in October 2011, found that DROSPIRENONE (DRSP) containing birth control pills present higher risk of blood clots to users in comparison to other pills. Nearly 12,000 personal injury lawsuits have been filed in coordinated Mass Tort proceedings in a Federal Multi-District Litigation and select State court's since the initiation of litigation. To date, published reports indicate some settlements totaling $142 Million to victims as of April 2012 with reports estimating the total collective settlement will reach $2.65 Billion for blood clot cases.

  • PRADAXA BLEEDING LAWSUITS & INVESTIGATIONS

    • December 2011 to Present
    Team Members: Gabriel F. Zambrano

    PRADAXA (DABIGATRAN ETEXILATE) is a blood thinner that was approved for use by the FDA in October 2010. It is only approved in patients with non-valvular Atrial Fibrilation. In November 2011, public health authorities worldwide began reporting deaths from unstoppable or uncontrolled bleeding events. This highlighted the fact that PRADAXA has not antidote or quick reversal agent. During December 2011, the FDA issued an alert regarding "safety signals" and adverse events. Sadly, by January 2012 I had been contacted by two families that had lost a parent from a PRADAXA BLEEDING event. Others have also come forward and this is an emerging Mass Tort. As of April 2012, reports of at least 542 deaths in the United States have been identified in FDA adverse event data. As a result, PRADAXA CASES was launched to assist in raising awareness, provide information and assist in the investigation.

Multiple scientific studies have confirmed higher than advertised, or expected, failure rates requiring revision surgery, explanation of hip joints and leading to complications.
