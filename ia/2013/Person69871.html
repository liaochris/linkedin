JR Sheetz's Projects

  • Playmakers Short Film Series

    • November 2011 to November 2012
    Team Members: JR Sheetz, Brian Waniewski, Katie Salen, Criswell Lappin, Bob Holling, Ilena Parker, Jason Darnell, Rick Brennan, Joel Levin

    Roles: Director of Photography & Editor
    PLAYMAKERS is an exploration of the experiences and innovations that are leading the way for learning design in the twenty-first century. The seven pieces in this series introduce you to a range of people working at the intersection of games and learning, from teachers who happened on the power of play through trial and error, to commercial game designers who set out to make one great game and ended up empowering millions of users to make their own.

  • Play@

    • July 2012 to December 2012
    Team Members: JR Sheetz, Brian Waniewski, Criswell Lappin

    Roles: Director of Photography & Editor
    Play@ Your Org gives you tools to integrate the power of play-based learning into your work through a hands-on exploration of games and design. Over the course of one to three days, workshop participants explore the state of games in the world today as well as innovative models of play-based learning. They acquire a critical perspective on game play and the powerful learning it supports. And they begin to design and prototype their own games, in order to gain an understanding of how the principles of game design can be leveraged to maximize participation and engagement.

  • "Quest Learning in Action" video exemplar series

    • June 2013 to Present
    Team Members: JR Sheetz, Eliza Spang, Shula Ponet

    Roles: Director of Photography & Editor.
    Quest Learning in Action videos bring the Quest Schools model of game-like connected learning to life, providing learning practitioners with a view into the way the model is implemented in Quest to Learn and other schools. Each video shows how the core design principles of the model become classroom practice, highlighting just some of the ways game-like learning can be effectively used by students and teachers.
