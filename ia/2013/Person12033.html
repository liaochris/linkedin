Bart Knols (1050+)'s Summary

I am a malariologist with a MSc (cum laude) in Biology and a PhD in Medical Entomology from Wageningen University, the Netherlands (1996), and also obtained a Masters in Business Administration degree with distinction in 2006 (Open University, UK) for which I won the international ‘MBA Student of the Year 2007 Award’ as well as the Alumnus of the Year Award from the Open University.

With eleven years of working experience in East and Southern Africa I managed large-scale research and vector control programmes when working for ministries, international or national research institutions. I led research groups of more than 100 staff. I worked for the United Nations (IAEA) as a programme manager for three years, served as a consultant for the World Health Organization, and was a Board Member of the UBS Optimus Foundation from 2006 - 2012.

I have published over 140 peer-reviewed research articles, have (co)authored 19 book chapters, and have served as senior editor on a WHO/IAEA sponsored book on implementation research. In 2007 I co-edited a best-selling book titled 'Emerging Pests and Vector-Borne Diseases in Europe'.

I was awarded an Ig Nobel Prize (2006), an IAEA Special Service Award (2006), and in 2007 became a laureate of the Eijkman medal (the highest award in the field of tropical medicine in the Netherlands). I have been a member of the Royal Dutch Academy of Arts and Sciences since 2004. I held an Assistant Professorship at Wageningen University until April '09 with projects in Benin, Kenya, Tanzania, and South Africa. Now I direct K&S Consulting, a firm I founded in 2007. In 2010 I co-founded Soper Strategies, and in 2011 the company In2Care.

Latest book (2010) Olfaction in vector-host interactions (Takken W and Knols BGJ, Eds.). 437 pp.

Specialties

As a medical entomologist:
- consulting in research and control of vector-borne diseases, notably malaria and dengue
- innovation for vector-borne disease control based on the behaviour and ecology of vectors

As Director of a consulting firm:
- Innovation in access to scientific information
- In search of innovative ways to provide medical information to those in need of it, notably in developing countries
- Driving scientific debate to solve vector-borne diseases, notably malaria and dengue
