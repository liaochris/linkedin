Mat Churchill's Summary

Mat Churchill is a producer of theatre and live events who is recognised for his pioneering work combining theatre and visual storytelling. He has conceived, funded, and realised a wide range of productions over the last 20 years – from touring shows to outdoor and site-specific events.

He is currently launching a new theatrical production company that will be touring a series of major experiential theatrical events around the world. The new pop up venue for these shows is currently installed in Chichester England where it is home to Cameron Mackintosh's production of "Barnum" until September.

In 2008, Churchill co-founded Three Sixty Entertainment and served as CEO until 2011. During this time he conceived and produced the international theatrical hit Peter Pan with Charlie Burnell. Blending the world’s first 360 degree surround CGI with live action, Peter Pan opened to critical acclaim. The show went on to become the second largest-selling show in London’s West End over the summer of 2009 and broke box office records for a non-Broadway show in San Francisco and Orange County. It has now been seen by more than 800,000 people in both the UK and USA.

His career began at the Circus Space in London, where he trained as an acrobat in the early 1990s. Then in 1995, inspired by his surroundings, Churchill formed the seminal contemporary circus company Mamaloucos who toured throughout the UK in their own tent with shows that brought together circus and theatre.

He also runs the Theatre Tent; a company who create tented venues for theatrical clients. Over 3 million people have attended events at venues created by the Theatre Tent.

He is a fellow of the Royal Society for the Arts and the Clore Leadership program and is married to Dr Teresa Hoskyns; an architect and academic specialising in the field of public space.

Specialties: Event Theatre, Video Projection, Theatre, CGI,Tents, Theatre Tent, Immersive Experiences,

The Wedding Collective

2003 – 2004 (1 year) International

In 2002 Churchill converted the crypt of St Andrew’s Church, Holborn into a temporary theatre where he produced a sell-out production of Steve Tiller's play Warcrime, receiving international attention and across the board 4 star reviews.
In 2003 he produced a nationwide tour of Warcrime in 5 unique site specific locations including a decommissioned nuclear bomb store and one leg of the Tyne bridge.

Producer "The Birds"

Mamaloucos

2001 – 2002 (1 year)

He returned to the Royal National Theatre in 2002 having conceived and developed a version of Aristophanes’ The Birds with actress and director Kathryn Hunter. This became the biggest selling show in their Lyttleton theatre in 2002 and was their first circus theatre collaboration.

Producer

Mamaloucos and Ninja Tune

2000 – 2000 (less than a year) London, United Kingdom

In 2000 he created a genre defying multimedia club night with record label Ninja Tune. It combined circus with performance, DJs & VJs (video projection DJs). The UK music press described it as “possibly the party of the year” - “a totally new nightlife experience”.

Producer National Theatre Tour

Mamaloucos

1997 – 1998 (1 year)

In 1998 he toured the Royal National Theatre’s sell-out production of Oh What a Lovely War throughout the UK in a theatre tent co-designed with his wife-to-be, Teresa Hoskyns. It was the first time that large-scale theatre had toured the UK in a tent.

Producer

Mamaloucos

1995 – 1998 (3 years)

He was creative producer and founder with Julian Rudd of Mamaloucos a UK contemporary circus company. Mamaloucos devised and toured shows that fused circus theatre and comedy. Mamaloucos began with shows in cabaret format and toured the UK for three years in their own big top.
