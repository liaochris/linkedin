Kelsye Nelson's Summary

Kelsye Nelson is CEO of Writer.ly, a marketplace of independent publishing marketing services. Other roles have included Vice President of Growth and Outreach at Brown Paper Tickets and leading marketing efforts at Hedgebrook, TransACT and Headsprout. She served as a board member of the PS American Marketing Association, Editor-in-chief of ToteMs, a monthly, print newspaper for MENSA and as teacher at Kwansei Gakuin University in Japan. Highly proficient in building online tribes, over 20,000 people follow Kelsye's twitter stream (@Kelsye) and she often speaks at national conferences on how business, non-profits and individuals may use social media to turn online friends to real-life advocates.

Kelsye's greatest claim to fame is helping save the Missouri Prairie Chicken from extinction by chainsawing non-native trees as part of an AmeriCorps project. That was the only time in her life she had arm muscles.
