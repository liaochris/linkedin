Geoff Bristow's Summary

Geoff Bristow combines:

(1) top tier tech background (PhD from Cambridge; Electronics at Imperial; MIEE, CEng)
(2) Division director multinational corporate (Texas Instruments, Fujitsu, ICL)
(3) CEO in hands-on corporate rescues (Wordplex, Compsoft, Alphameric, ARC)
(4) Chairman in VC/Angel (Poqet, Interactive Forecasting, Cashflow Wizard, Cybertill)

Geoff’s initial industry was semiconductors (having had responsibility for TI’s European ASIC development as far back as 1982) but he used that as a spring-board for all forms of software, tools, OEM hardware and mobile consumer devices.

In 1986 he set up a Buy-In fund to mount rescues of ailing technology companies quoted on the London Stock Exchange. He stepped in as crisis-CEO at four mid-size corporates, in each case establishing a new strategy and team and executing an initial turnround leading to an exit… in one case (Wordplex) the rescue having been filmed by the BBC for a dedicated 30-minute documentary.

After the sale to Fujitsu in 1992 of the Poqet Computer Corporation (Californian creator of the world’s first pocket-sized computer), for which he was Chief Operating Officer, Geoff became Executive Vice President for Business Planning at Fujistu plc, which spurred a new stream of expertise for him. Since then Geoff has led 3 start-ups in business planning software.

He now applies this experience by investing his own fund in early-stage start-ups, mainly in the field of business forecasting and decision-making; and in writing a series of books about managing creative people.

He is passionate about the arts, is a keen runner and sailor and loves anything that involves two wheels. He is a Chartered Engineer, a Fellow of the RSA, and a Freeman of the City of London.

Specialties

As a non-exec, Dr Bristow brings not only experience of more than 15 public-company M&A transactions as CEO or Chairman, but in his 10 years as a PLC non-exec, he has had 3 years as a PLC RemCo chairman and 7 years as a UK “SID” (Senior Independent Director).

Having lived/worked in five countries in Europe and three states of the USA, and being fluent in Spanish and French, Geoff has now formed an operating "base camp" in Barcelona, Spain.

Geoff Bristow's Experience

Chairman

Decision Curve Ltd

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Computer Software industry

November 2009 – Present (3 years 11 months)

Decision Curve is the creator of the award-winning Cashflow Wizard and Rugged Logic, which have been used by more than 10,000 businesses around the world and were the first business planning software packages to be accredited by the Institute of Chartered Accountants (www.decisioncurve.com).

Cashflow Wizard is designed for entrepreneurs and SMEs, and delivers robust financial forecasts and editable professional reports that will help you plan and communicate future business strategy.

Rugged Logic is a high-end financial forecasting tool, designed for finance professionals and larger corporations, that incorporates the patented SpreadsheetBuilder® technology.

Model My Future will be launching a new suite of interactive business forecasting models, entirely on-line.

President and CEO

ARC International

Public Company; 51-200 employees; ARK; Semiconductors industry

May 2009 – December 2009 (8 months)

Became CEO at a time of great uncertainty for ARC, despite it being admired for its technology worldwide, with more than a billion consumer devices having been shipped with its intellectual property (CPU designs) inside.

Under the imaginative stewardship of the previous CEO the company had been transformed into a well-respected multimedia focussed solutions-IP company. Nevertheless, against the background of world recession, and in the shadow of a well established competitor more than 10x the size, the company had never made a profit and its cash resources were dwindling.

By taking some previously unpalletable decisions, such as closing the company's flagship UK headquarters where many of the designs had been inspired and brought to life years earlier, the business was brought to a viable ongoing financial basis. Meanwhile the company's future engine of innovation was assured by focussing and further investing in its premium design centres in St Petersburg, Russia; San Jose, California; and Hyderabad, India.

In October 2009 we were able to announce the unconditional sale of the company to Virage Logic via an all-cash offer to all of its 7000+ London Stock Exchange shareholders.

During this period the company grew from a cash-hungry technology VC startup of 20 people to a mature, consistently profitable organisation with a leadership industry position and a growing cash balance.

Based in Santa Clara, California, Poqet invented and manufactured the Worlds first hand-held computer, fore-runner to the Palm Pilot and the Psion organiser.

Subsequent innovations included redesigning the initial Poqet into the Worlds first wireless pen computer, fore-runner to today's PDAs, incorporating world's first 2.4GHz WiFi in a hand-held.

Originally a $60m start-up, Poqet was successfully sold to Fujitsu in 1992.

Executive Chairman

Alphameric

Public Company; 201-500 employees; Computer Software industry

December 1989 – August 1990 (9 months)

In December 1989 this full-list plc on the London Stock Exchange was on the brink of receivership, having hit a cash crisis after making numerous acquisitions.

Working against the clock through the Christmas break, Bristow enlisted the help of renowned industry strategists Robb Wilmot and Alex Reid to put together an emergency package of rescue finance and turnaround strategy.

Following presentation of the rescue proposals in January 1990, Bristow was invited by the board to become Exec chairman on contract to put them into effect.

A new top team was identified, including Rodney Hornstein as CEO, who led the recovery and subsequent growth over many years and subsequently became chairman until 2007. The company is still publicly traded on the London Stock Exchange. (Press cuttings available).

Executive Chairman

Compsoft Holdings plc

1987 – 1989 (2 years)

Database software. Initiatially a classic turnaround based on sales focus, then acquired Multisoft, Mega and Wootton Jeffries. (Press cuttings available.)

Chief Executive

Wordplex Information Systems plc

1986 – 1987 (1 year)

A rescue pre-receivership prompted by a cash crisis. Wordplex, a full-list plc on the London Stock Exchange, was the UK's leading provider of word processing systems, especially to London's large legal practices. However, the company had not responded to the challenge from the newly emerging Personal Computer.

Bristow was introduced to the board by the company's stockbrokers and invited to present a turnaround strategy, following the presentation of which he was asked to become CEO to put it into effect. Following a strategic redirection to refocus the company as a global systems integrator, it was sold to Norwegian public group, Norsk Data, at three times the original share price. (Press cuttings available.)

Director of Network Products

ICL plc

1984 – 1986 (2 years)

At that time ICL was UK's answer to IBM, a $2billion mainframe and computer systems group. Bristow's responsibility was the networking and communications products and software in all their guises, spreading across 10 sites with 650 staff. Projects managed by Bristow's team included the networking technology required for the initial computerisation of the UK government's PAYE (income tax) and DSS (social security) systems.

Profit Centre Manager

Texas Instruments

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; TXN; Semiconductors industry

1979 – 1984 (5 years)

Five years at global semiconductor giant Texas Instruments during which Bristow set up and ran the "Marketing Business Centre" for North Europe and subsequently became the European Profit Centre manager for microsystems and custom silicon devices.

Bristow's responsibility included the TMS1000, at the time the world's best selling single-chip microcontroller, surpassing 60million units already in 1982. In addition the TMS320 Digital Signal Processor was launched, which is now one of the mainstays of TI's portfolio.

Geoff Bristow's Education

University of Cambridge

PhD, Speech recognition

1976 – 1979

A speech training aid for the deaf that Geoff Bristow invented as part of his PhD research was manufactured by Rank Cintel and adopted by the majority of UK schools for the hearing impaired.
The academic specialism required for this invention was the same as for speech synthesis and recognition. Using this knowledge, Bristow subsequently published two successful text books entitled:
Electronic Speech Synthesis: Techniques, Technologies and Applications (1981)
Electronic Speech Recognition: Techniques, Technologies and Applications (1983)
Both books were published by Collins in the UK and McGraw-Hill in the USA, and became foundation texts for developers of the evolving use of speech communication by devices and telecom services.

Activities and Societies: President of Peterhouse Graduate Society

Imperial College London

BSc(Eng), Electronic and Electrical Engineeing (1st Class honours)

1972 – 1976

Activities and Societies: Sabbatical year as Director of West London Nightline (Student crisis line) . Sub-warden of Selkirk Hall of Residence. Royal Society of Arts Silver Medal (for combination of academic achievement with social contribution). Lord Willis Jackson Memorial Scholarship.
