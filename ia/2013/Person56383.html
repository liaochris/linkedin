Jason Buff's Summary

I have always been passionate about presentation. Graphic Design is one of those careers where a lot of people don't completely understand what you do, even though it effects almost every aspect of our daily lives. It moves us to purchase certain products and is one of the subliminal ways in which we makes sense of the world around us.

I started my career in Television and Film, working in San Francisco and Los Angeles, primarily as a Production Assistant. To supplement my income, I also worked as a cameraman shooting corporate commericals. In 1998, my career shifted primarily into Graphics as I worked as an assistant at Formation Creative Media in Atlanta. From there, I dedicated all my time to learning Graphic Design.

In 2000, I landed a job as the Lead Graphic Designer at Orotech (now Sage Island) a small company in Wilmington, NC. With this trial by fire, I quickly learned the ends and outs of project management, as well as dealing with clients. I also became a much better designer.

In 2001, I decided to strike out on my own as a freelancer and started Jabu Studio. Things were slow at first, but with a lot of work I got my first few clients and was able to live off of my income. After a few years, I had around 10 clients who kept me busy...almost too busy.

In 2006, I decided to switch from freelance to working full time as Creative Director of Nebo Design in Atlanta. At Nebo I was responsible for brand consulting, brand development, graphic design implementation as well as in house projects. During my time with Nebo we created a wide range of Award Winning projects and I feel like I created some of my best work.

In 2008, my son was born and I went back to offering services through Jabu Studio and now work for Nebo primarily as a freelance designer. I have been blessed to have a pretty consistent work over the years. I truly enjoy the work I do. I'm always looking for new and interesting projects so don't hesitate to send me a message.
