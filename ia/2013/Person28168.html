C. Sunny Martin's Summary

Described by many as a gifted visionary, C. Sunny Martin is one of America's foremost experts in multicultural marketing. Sunny's 30 plus years of experience and achievements has afforded him face-to-face audiences with (4) four U.S. Presidents.

In 2008, President Barack Obama said of Sunny, "I'd like to congratulate Sunny on all his work to showcase the positive achievements of African-Americans nationwide."

In 2004, former U.S. President Jimmy Carter held a special tribute to Mr. Martin at the Carter Presidential Library in Atlanta, Georgia to honor his outstanding achievements gained throughout the publishing industry.

Sunny is an accomplished nationally acclaimed publisher, having self-published 61 books in a short twenty-three year period.

Mr. Martin has also produced elite events at such prestigious venues as The Apollo Theatre in Harlem, New York, Churchill Downs in Louisville Kentucky, The Fox Theatre in St. Louis, Missouri, Staples Center in downtown Los Angeles, California, The Schottenstein Center in Columbus, Ohio, and The Muhammad Ali Museum in Louisville, Kentucky.

Previously, Mr. Martin was the founder and CEO of Who's Who Publishing Company. (http://www.whoswhopublishing.com) As a result, the company became recognized as the largest African-American directory publishers in the United States. In February of 2009, Mr. Martin sold Who's Who Publishing resulting in a multimillion-dollar deal transaction.

Sunny has now focused his time, energy, and talents in private label publishing projects, online social media engagement, producing multicultural elite events, and producing regional primetime television specials.

http://www.GrapevineColumbus.com is Mr. Martin's new online social media business targeted to Central Ohio's multicultural professionals. His firm owns 30 different social media Grapevine community sites!

Mr. Martin is also the co-founder, and executive producer of the annual http://www.Ohio Asian Leaders.com & Legends Gala.

C. Sunny Martin's Publications

  • 100 Years of African-American Achievement at The Ohio State University

    • Columbus, Ohio
    • April 10, 2011
    Authors: C. Sunny Martin, Paula M. Gray, Melanie Houston, Cory Favor, Jerrian Bell

    I was honored that one of the largest universities in this nation selected me to tell the incredible stories of success of its African-American alumni. The colorful historic book stands testimony to The Ohio State University's investment in diverse partnership with minority suppliers. The book is 223-pages in length, and features rich editorial content and many historical photographs of celebrated alumni, faculty and staff personnel. The publication retails for $39.95

  • Columbus' Black Heritage - The Bicentennial Edition

    • February 20, 2012
    Authors: C. Sunny Martin, Paula M. Gray, Moe Buckley, Terry Gilliam, Kojo, Ira Grahm

    We had the singular distinction of capturing the rich legacy of Columbus, Ohio's African-American population for a two-hundred year period in celebration of the city's bicentennial. The 216-page book lists over 1,300 African-Americans who at onetime called Columbus, Ohio home. The book is filled with photographs and featured stories that document the many stories of achievement and noteworthy accomplishments of those in the Black community. The book retails for $25.00.
