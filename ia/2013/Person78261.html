Lane Rapp's Summary

Reasonable guy. Works hard. Skates for fun.

Failed seeking the Republican Nomination for President of the United States of America - 2012. Open to reforming the party if they become interested. In the meanwhile, I'll continue to align populists of many tribes to reform government. I'm growing a startup monastery to fund this interest.

My experience in customer relations, community development, and persuasion brings more bang for each buck I put into this long term campaign.

I take my savvy from street smarts and practice in developing the art of organizations, formal and informal. Before arriving in Silicon Valley, I joined those in service and play as a backcountry volunteer in our pristine Olympic National Park. Prior to that I was farming in Tumwater, WA with a non-profit helping to bring outdoor labor options to teens and adults with psychiatric or neurological variances. I was appreciated many experiences of camaraderie. During that same year, while gradually dropping out or getting shoved out of medical school, I also helped a great 92 year-old retired Lt. Col. avoid the nursing home by living with him in Panorama City Retirement Community in Lacey, WA. I was able to dig soil and other retirees there too, participating in the garden club.

With twenty years practicing wordplay and with 40 years developing my empathy and experience for this thing the social psychologists call the human condition, I strive steadfastly toward your Presidential poet laureate offer. I think the role of any nation's leaders could better resemble the leadership of a poet.

The tools for self-governance have arrived and it's high time we transitioned toward it. I will offer initiatives that move formalized government on the federal level toward pure protection. Private enterprises, trade guilds, and other networks of citizens in both profit and non-profit ventures can take care of most of our society's service needs if, but only if, we promote fantastic self-governance again.
