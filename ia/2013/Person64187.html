Jim Stagnitto's Summary

Data Warehouse Architect: specializing in powerful designs that extract the maximum business benefit from Intelligence and Insight investments.

Master Data Management (MDM) and Customer Data Integration (CDI) strategist and architect.

Data Warehousing, Data Quality, and Data Integration thought-leader: co-author with Lawrence Corr of "Agile Data Warehouse Design", guest author of Ralph Kimball’s “Data Warehouse Designer” column, and contributing author to Ralph and Joe Caserta's latest book: “The DW ETL Toolkit”.

Hard-won expertise in complex Data and Application Integration initiatives, and in navigating through the technical and organizational challenges that often surround these opportunities.

Significant expertise and experience in the measurement and improvement of Information Quality, and in the empirical correlation these efforts with business performance results.

Experienced Project Manager and Technical Team Leader – effective in bridging the communication abyss between the technical and business communities – maintaining and amplifying a relentless business focus.

Insightful and thoughtful Business Requirements collection, documentation and analysis, and technology assessments – and in the translation of these concepts into crisp, focused and actionable designs, specifications, recommendations, and go-forward strategies.

Deep hands-on experience with the full compliment of contemporary Business Intelligence Tools and Technologies – including general and specialized DBMSs, ETL tools, Reporting and Analytic suites, BI Portals, Dashboard and Scorecards, Data Mining tools, and contemporary programming languages.

Outstanding presentation and writing skills – able to effectively communicate complex scenarios to both technical and non-technical audiences, and in balancing the evangelization of project successes with the need for full disclosure and transparency. Expertise with authoring tools and web based publication.

Specialties

MDM and CDI Strategist and Architect, Enterprise and Data Warehouse Data Modeling, Physical Design, Enterprise Application Integration and Data Integration Architectures, Technical Architectures, Security Architectures, Project Management, Data Warehouse Methodologies, Business Requirement Collection and Analysis, Business Justification, Proposals and Presentation, Project Web Site Design and Implementation, CASE, Meta Data & ETL Tools, Procedural Programming.

Jim Stagnitto's Publications

  • Agile Data Warehouse Design: Collaborative Dimensional Modeling, from Whiteboard to Star Schema

    • Decision Press
    • November 24, 2011
    Authors: Jim Stagnitto, Lawrence Corr

    Agile Data Warehouse Design is a step-by-step guide for capturing data warehousing/business intelligence (DW/BI) requirements and turning them into high performance dimensional models in the most direct way: by modelstorming (data modeling + brainstorming) with BI stakeholders.

  • Agile Data Warehouse Design Sampler

    • DecisionOne Press
    • March 2, 2012
    Authors: Jim Stagnitto, Lawrence Corr

    46 page PDF sample from the book 'Agile Data Warehouse Design: Collaborative Dimensional Modeling, from Whiteboard to Star Schema'. Includes TOC, Introduction and Chapter 1. Describes the rational for agile dimensional modeling and introduces the BEAM✲ BI requirements modeling technique and notation.

  • The Data Warehouse ETL Toolkit: Practical Techniques for Extracting, Cleaning, Conforming, and Delivering Data

    • Wiley and Sons
    • October 2004
    Authors: Jim Stagnitto, Joe Caserta, Ralph Kimball

    Primary author is Joe Caserta - a leading DW practitioner, and founder of Caserta Concepts consultancy.
    Cowritten by Ralph Kimball, the world's leading data warehousing authority, whose previous books have sold more than 150,000 copies
    Delivers real-world solutions for the most time- and labor-intensive portion of data warehousing-data staging, or the extract, transform, load (ETL) process
    Delineates best practices for extracting data from scattered sources, removing redundant and inaccurate data, transforming the remaining data into correctly formatted data structures, and then loading the end product into the data warehouse
    Offers proven time-saving ETL techniques, comprehensive guidance on building dimensional structures, and crucial advice on ensuring data quality

  • Hubs, Spokes and Buses: How to Get to a Better Data Warehouse

    • Intelligent Enterprise
    • March 10, 2008
    Authors: Jim Stagnitto

    Now that data warehousing has become ubiquitous in the corporate world, "clean slate" designs of entirely new decision support systems are becoming somewhat rare. Instead, designs and roadmaps are more often intended to improve, modernize or otherwise evolve existing production data warehouses that may be showing signs of age.

  • Better Storytelling

    • Intelligent Enterprise - Data Warehouse Designer
    • August 10, 2003
    Authors: Jim Stagnitto, Ralph Kimball

    Storytelling is a powerful means of sharing knowledge because it simultaneously and elegantly satisfies several innate human needs, including the need for entertainment, sharing the accumulated wisdom of experience, and providing avenues of self-expression. How, you may ask, does storytelling relate to the job of a data warehouse designer? Design techniques can amplify the dimensional data warehouse's ability to tell the stories encased within the millions or billions of events sitting within its subject areas.
