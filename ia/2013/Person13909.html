Juvenile Judges determine which cases will be appointed CASA volunteers. Once appointed, CASA volunteers serve as the "eyes and ears" of the court. Each case may have multiple children involved.

> Focus on each child's particular needs and be the one consistent, caring person during this very difficult time
> Become a trusted "friend" for each child in need
> Conduct independent investigations including all aspects of the child's life (living conditions, family and school situation, medical needs, special needs, etc.) and ensure the child is safe environment
> Develop written, factual reports with recommendations in the child's best interests. Judges read these recommendations and use them are part of their decisions and rulings for the cases and often influence the judges' final decisions regarding a child's permanent placement.
> Work with others agencies and persons involved in the child(ren)'s case (social services professionals, case workers, attorneys, foster parents, parents, agency case workers.
