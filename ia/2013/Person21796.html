Chad Petrovay's Experience

Project Manager/Data Conversion Specialist

Gallery Systems

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Computer Software industry

December 2011 – Present (2 years 1 month) Greater New York City Area

Assisted new clients with their transition from other collections management software
to The Museum System (TMS), including consultation on system functionality and
recommended best‐practices, the analysis of data and data structures, the extraction,
transformation and ingestion of data from legacy systems into SQL Server, and the
validation of conversions to ensure accuracy. Conducted group trainings. Provided client
care support and advocated for client during feature development. Developed List
Views, Data Views, Crystal Reports, and SQL‐based solutions for clients.

Consulting Research and Content Development Coordinator

Qatar Museums Authority, National Museum of Qatar

Government Agency; 51-200 employees; Museums and Institutions industry

May 2011 – November 2011 (7 months) Doha, Qatar

Developed systems for managing the flow of work and information related to the
development of core exhibitions for the NMoQ. Liaised between Curators, Researchers,
Collections Management staff, Interpretive Planners, Exhibition Designers, and other
stakeholders to produce schedules, process documents, and manage collections
databases. Facilitated the logistics of meetings, project team communications, and
distribution of information and materials to various team members, departments, and
external parties, including meeting minutes, feedback, project documents, and data
hand‐offs. Maintained records of curatorial and research output, as well as potential
items (artifacts, media, and other collections) for use in exhibit installations. Identified
copyright and intellectual property issues, and coordinated permissions and licensing
when necessary.

Collections Database Administrator

Musical Instrument Museum

Nonprofit; 51-200 employees; Museums and Institutions industry

February 2009 – February 2011 (2 years 1 month) Phoenix, Arizona Area

Was part of the pre-opening team for the museum. Developed workflows and systems for the exhibition development process, as well as mechanisms for tracking progress on artifact acquisition, preparing installation schedules, and sharing data between the institution and vendors/consultants. Facilitated content creation through centralized artifact metadata capture, and tools to export the content into appropriate formats for production needs. Maintained collections database using TMS; created custom data entry screens, list views, and Crystal Reports to support above. Planned and successfully executed a major upgrade for TMS (9.35 to 2010 R2). Established cataloging initiative for the institution’s
growing library using Koha ILS.

Associate Registrar / Collections Database Administrator

The Walters Art Museum

Nonprofit; 51-200 employees; Museums and Institutions industry

February 2007 – February 2009 (2 years 1 month) Baltimore, Maryland Area

Maintained collections database using TMS; established data standards; prepared Crystal Reports; managed data scrubbing projects; coordinated staff training, and served on the Technology Initiative Committee. Lead the development team for art.thewalters.org, an online collections interface, which launched in Jan. 2009. Managed the project of digitizing the artifacts on exhibit, completing the project a month ahead of schedule. Developed workflow and consulted on metadata issues for two major grants: an NEH grant for creating digital surrogates of Islamic manuscripts and an IMLS grant for digitizing Japanese metalwork. Launched the TMS HelpCenter, a user support site containing data entry style guides and tutorials. Prepared incoming receipts for temporary deposits and maintained artifact record files. Automated TMS media ingestion. Developed exhibition technology, including technology centers or kiosks for: Maps: Finding our Place in the Universe; Mummified; Faces of Ancient Arabia; and The Special Dead. Served on the Advisory Committee for Waltee’s Quest, an interactive game with child-friendly object information for 5-8 year olds, which received a Gold MUSE award. Assisted with the museum’s Facebook presence, including their participation with ArtShare.

Instructional Technologist

Mother Catherine Spalding School

August 2004 – February 2007 (2 years 7 months) Helen, Maryland

Responsible for the instruction of both faculty and students in the use and application of computer technology. Coordinated faculty training sessions on Inspiration, GradeQuick, and Curriculum Mapper applications. Maintained student records in SchoolMinder and electronic grade books in GradeQuick; spearheaded the initiative for integrating the two systems to generate printed report cards, first in Grades 6-8 and then in Grades 3-5. Renovated the computer lab to make the environment more ergonomic and accessible to the student population, including the implementation of classroom management software (AB Tutor Control), which allowed for instruction via screen broadcasting instead of projection. Developed the school website using Site@School CMS.
