Adrian Sexton's Experience

Chief Evangelist & Business Development

Dynamics

Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Financial Services industry

July 2012 – Present (1 year 4 months) Greater Pittsburgh Area

As Chief Evangelist, I am in charge of Dynamics' market and business development for its content and brand ecosystem, working with major studios, talent agencies, charities, game publishers, lifestyle brands and retailers.

Dynamics produces and manufactures intelligent powered cards such as advanced payment cards like the ePlate. Focused on introducing fast-cycle innovation to top card issuers, the company's first commercial application is the world's first fully card-programmable magnetic stripe for use in next-generation payment cards. Dynamics is recently backed with over $40M by Bain Capital Ventures and Adams Capital Management, and the company is in-market with VISA and other banks.

Founder & CEO

New Medici

Partnership; 1-10 employees; Entertainment industry

September 2008 – July 2012 (3 years 11 months) Greater Los Angeles Area

Sexton co-founded New Medici to advise media, technology and lifestyle brands on long term digital and media strategies in marketing, social, sales/advertising, operations and distribution.

Working with clients including Ogilvy/WPP, Sony Asia, Groupon, the CBC, Vevo, 20th Century Fox, Producers Guild of America/CEA, Austrade (Australian Government), Might Entertainment, NeoClassics and start-ups including Palindrome Media (Happy Madison), Bigstar, Muxlim and 3DTV, Sexton's expertise lies in building, marketing and then monetizing audiences.

New Medici’s clients represent a hybrid blend of traditional and digital media companies. On a go-to-market basis, Sexton and his team handle business planning, modeling, product development, content and social strategy, advertising/ecommerce, strategic marketing, distribution and investment fundraising for client companies and startups. New Medici was awarded the British Airways/Harvard Business Review entrepreneurialism award in 2009.

Executive Vice President, Digital

Participant Media

Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Entertainment industry

September 2006 – August 2008 (2 years)

Sexton consulted Participant overseeing digital via TAG Strategic beginning in September 2006, then was appointed head of Digital (EVP) at Participant Media in January 2007.

He was responsible for running the company's expansion into global media, digital distribution and marketing, and architected TakePart.com, its social action network™. Traffic to TakePart, included its blog network and acquired sites, grew 1000% year over year during Sexton’s tenure. He later consulted Participant during its TakePart beta launch in 2009.

Sexton led the greenlight investment of social marketing portal TakePart.com ($10M+ investment); shared executive committee work involving Summit Entertainment distribution and funding ($1B), and the film investment from Imagenation Abu Dhabi ($250M).

Sexton worked on Participant films’ Waiting for Superman, The Cove, Food Inc., The Visitor, Charlie Wilson’s War, Fast Food Nation, The Kite Runner, and the Academy Award®-winning documentary An Inconvenient Truth.

Founding Partner

TAG Strategic

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Entertainment industry

June 2006 – January 2007 (8 months)

Sexton co-founded TAG Strategic, a digital media and entertainment agency with EMI veteran and digital pioneer Ted Cohen. TAG’s diverse client roster has included Lionsgate, Eyespot, Qualcomm, Sandisk, Muze, LimeWire, Participant Media and LivePlanet.

Sexton strategized digital distribution, marketing and business planning for media companies and interactive startups, including IP licensing deals with Lucasfilm. TAG launched a digital entertainment partnership with Edelman, creating end-to-end business, distribution and communications strategy counsel.

Sexton was hired out of TAG Strategic by entertainment client, Participant Media, to run digital in January 2007.

Vice President, Head of Digital Media

Lionsgate

Public Company; 501-1000 employees; LGF; Entertainment industry

October 1999 – June 2006 (6 years 9 months) Santa Monica, CA

Sexton headed Digital Media as Vice President at Lionsgate, where he oversaw the day-to-day marketing, operations, production, media planning and business development for digital and mobile properties. He also worked in corporate development, supporting the studio's catalog growth and financing.

His duties encompassed theatrical, home entertainment, television, international and physical studios. He oversaw the digital campaigns for such movies as the Best Picture Academy Award ®-winning Crash, Oscar®-winning Monster’s Ball, Fahrenheit 9/11, as well as the SAW and Tyler Perry franchises. He and his team were awarded a Clio for the first SAW film website.

Sexton identified multiple-platform marketing, distribution and sales opportunities for the studio’s 8000+ title film library in such high-growth markets as broadband, mobile, digital cinema, peer-to-peer and IPTV. Lionsgate’s market capitalization rose from $60 million to $1.2 billion during Sexton’s tenure; and he was part of the senior team which secured the $350 million credit facility to acquire Artisan Entertainment.

Sexton was director of new media at MediaPact Capital, a $500M media management and investment company, run by former Sony Pictures Television head, Jon Feltheimer, and Prudential Securities managing director, Michael Burns. In early 2000, Mediapact led a $33M preferred equity position in Lionsgate with strategic partners - Paul Allen's Vulcan Ventures, Capital Research and Fidelity Investments - taking over management control at Lionsgate.

Television Creative Associate

Sony Pictures Entertainment

Public Company; 5001-10,000 employees; SNE; Entertainment industry

March 1995 – October 1999 (4 years 8 months) Culver City, CA

Coordinated creative, marketing and development deals, executive sessions and research with domestic and international networks, agencies and producers under President Jon Feltheimer.

Organized internal pitch, sales and marketing agendas with Columbia TriStar Domestic and International Television, Distribution, Telemundo and the Game Show Network. Facilitated development and distribution on such titles as "Mad About You," "The King of Queens," "Dawson's Creek" and "Seinfeld."

Creative Consultant

HBO

Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; Entertainment industry

October 1993 – October 1995 (2 years 1 month) Los Angeles, CA

Developed 20th Century Fox Television MOWs (Movies of the Week) properties. Handled script analysis across novels, screenplays and magazine articles, including works by Stephen King and Oscar Hijuelos.

Coordinated HBO Pictures domestic and international production and story department development, working closely with creative and senior production on "And the Band Played On," "The Late Shift" and other Pay TV movies.
