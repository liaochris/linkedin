Eric Hilton's Experience

Owner & Internet Marketing Specialist

Hilton Internet Marketing

Privately Held; Myself Only; Internet industry

2010 – Present (3 years) Santa Barbara, California

I started Hilton Internet Marketing with the goal of helping small to medium-sized businesses in getting and converting leads in the still young and constantly changing field of online marketing.

I am happy to be able to say I am actively engaged in doing so every day by applying my knowledge of SEO (search engine optimization), Google+ Local optimization, social media marketing (Facebook business pages, Twitter, Pinterest, Google+ and others), online reputation management, pay per click advertising (Google AdWords), website design, mobile website design, mobile app development and SMS marketing.

Owner

Lingo Institute

2000 – 2011 (11 years) Saitama, Japan

I, and my two partners, ran a language school in Japan at which were taught English, Japanese, French and Italian to students of all ages, reasons for learning their target language and levels of commitment for 11 years.

Because the school has unique programs and services tailored to answer the needs of both individual students and business people who, beyond language instruction, have to master cross-cultural communication skills, most students stay for a long time.

Still, an important aspect of running the school was finding effective advertising strategies to keep a steady stream of new students coming in.

That was where I fist learned something about Internet marketing. In our early years, the most effective form of advertising for us was local town guides that were popular with young homemakers. But then there was a very sudden shift. To the question, How did you find out about us?, virtually every prospective student who came in for a level assessment suddenly started answering, "I saw your website online."

So we stopped advertising in the town guides and hired a company to do SEO for us. I became interested in SEO myself and was able to get our school's Google Places listing to rank second for a couple of high-volume and highly relevant keyword phrases, outranking some big famous chains--Berlitz for one--in the process.

I was also able to get new customers through leveraging social media sites, specifically Facebook, Twitter and Pinterest.

I also became interested in affiliate marketing and found that I had a knack both for getting websites to rank really well in the search engines and online marketing in general.

So, the long and short of it is that I ended up changing my profession from being a language school operator to an Internet marketing consultant.
