Bob Elliott's Summary

Bob Elliott is President/Owner of The Transpective Group, a management-consulting firm located in the New England area. Bob is an accomplished senior consultant in the areas of organizational transformation and leadership development. He has worked with clients throughout the US and in the UK. Client organizations represent industry leaders in health care, health care products, gas and electric utilities, tele-communications, higher education, high-tech, music industry, manufacturing and defense. The Transpective Group has been delivering transformational consulting and leadership development programs for 17 years. Bob is responsible for managing the design and delivery of client engagements - that include interventions and executive coaching.

Before his consulting career, Bob was the General Manger of Cri-Tech, Inc., the leading custom formulator of fluoroelastomer compounds and specialty products. Bob also has several years of Human Resource experience in industry, as well as in the military as a Chief Warrant Officer, with the Massachusetts Army National Guard State Headquarters. Bob has a business degree from Northeastern University, in Boston. Additionally he has two years of studies in Organizational Behavior at Lesley University, in Cambridge. Bob has over 30 years experience in business that has given him a wealth of first hand knowledge and experience “in the trenches” that has proven in-valuable.

Bob co-authored “A World Conversation: “The Context and Content of Sustaining Livelihoods” published in The Journal of Global Transformation, in July 2003. Bob served as the subject matter expert for a recent APQC® consortium benchmarking study “Best Practices in Realizing Change”. As a volunteer in a Massachusetts Correctional Institution, he worked with incarcerated combat veterans serving life sentences, leading the “Combat Veterans Stress Group”.

Specialties

Management consulting. Executive coaching. Productivity workshops and consulting. Stress workshops and consulting. Diagnostic tools and workshops that help people in organizations better understand themselves and others from a behavior model. A unique Leadership Development program that distinguishes: 1) the paradoxical nature of leadership and management; 2) the default nature of culture.
