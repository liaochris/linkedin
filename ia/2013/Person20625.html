Carrie Weiler's Experience

Independent Graphic Design Professional & Owner, Designer & Creative Director

Aura Creative

March 2009 – Present (4 years 5 months) Toronto, Canada Area

I decided to name my company "Aura Creative" for a couple of reasons. The first being that I believe that every brand, if properly designed, can shine in its space. Secondly, many of my clients and colleagues have commented on my positive outlook. I wanted my company name to portray that feeling, an Aura of positivity!

I have over 17 years of experience, specializing in branding, packaging, and promotional design. I am also an Independent graphic design professional. I can work in your studio or off site out of my studio. Some of my current and past clients are; Casman Group, Coco Rogue Chocolatier, First Reliance Financial, Inverness Homes and UR the Star. My two highlighted projects as of late are the Coco Rogue masterbranding project and The Insync Income book cover and promotional creative, check my website for more information.

What Aura can do for you!
Branding, packaging, and promotional design! These are the things I do! Take a look through my portfolio on my website to get an idea of the type of work that I do and can do for you, I'm always up for a new challenge! I welcome the opportunity to speak to new potential clients and design studios. Please do not hesitate to contact me to discuss your design needs!

Senior Designer

Polan + Waski

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Graphic Design industry

July 2002 – January 2009 (6 years 7 months) Toronto, Canada Area

Polan and Waski presented me with a unique situation. With the head office in New York, and with me being the only designer in Canada at the time, I had to adjust to a new way of communication. Briefs were done via conference calls and video chats. Occasional travel to New York was also part of the job.

Nestlé Canada was the big client here and I was introduced to the exciting and ever changing world of confections. I was able to work on many of the big brands such as; Kit Kat, Coffee Crisp, Aero and Smarties to name a few. I spent well over a year re-inventing the Turtles branding and packaging for Canada which was so well received that I was quickly asked to work on the USA packaging as well.

Other clients that I worked with include George Weston Bakeries (Thomas’ line of baked goods) and Phoenix Brands(Sunlight dish soap products for Canada and USA).

My responsibilities included, branding, concept development, new product development, name generation, market research, art direction for illustration and photoshoots, as well as client presentations.

Designer

Strategies International (now OnBrand)

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Design industry

1995 – 2002 (7 years) Toronto, Canada Area

Strategies is where I found my love for all things packaged! I was hired right out of college and quickly proved I was up for a challenge. Within the space of 7 years I worked my way from junior to senior designer.

At Strategies the main focus was branding, package design and corporate identity. I was fortunate to have the opportunity to design a wide range of products including beer, wine, pharmaceuticals and dairy products, just to name a few. Some project highlights include the development of the Davis & Henderson Logo, the very first illustrated Christmas cup for Tim Hortons and Sensational Soy's juice cartons.

My responsibilities at Strategies included: brand name generation, concept development, new product development, illustration and photo shoot art direction, market research and client presentations.

Carrie Weiler's Education

Conestoga College

Graphic Design and Advertising

1992 – 1995

Conestoga's Graphic Design program is recognized as one of the top design programs in the province.

This Graphic Design program allows you to study professional design principles and practices, including typography, photography, layout, illustration, printing production procedures, client presentation techniques and business practices.

In the first year, graphic design students study traditional design techniques and are introduced to electronic design methods. Year Two provides a solid base in Mac-based graphic design software, and the third year allows students to work in professional industry settings and to develop a portfolio.
