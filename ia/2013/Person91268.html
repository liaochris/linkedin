Melissa Alicia Cavero's Summary

I am self taught.

In 1987 I was born in St. Joseph, Trinidad & Tobago to a family rich in multiple cultures and ethnicities. At 2 years of age we moved to Miami, Florida.

I've always been an art addict. I spent my adolescence covered in paints and charcoal. I was greatly influenced by the works of Vedova and Andy Warhol. I have always had a fascination with people and they would be the center of my pieces. My biggest problem was (still is) I can't draw people. It only happens once in a blue moon. I turned to photography after working on screen printing Warhol style using photographs of friends or people to create pieces.

I became a photographer because I can't draw people.

Photography has given me the ability to create the works I couldn't do with pencil and paper or brush and canvas.

Over the past few years I’ve worked with a few community organizations. I’ve been part of the Heart Gallery of America working with the Southwest Florida Children’s Network photographing foster children who are up for adoption help get adopted by capturing their personalities in a photograph. In 2010, started Southwest Florida’s Help-Portrait group with a couple friends and local photographers, make-up artists, and volunteers. We’ve worked with Nation’s Association, Dress for Success, and Cafe of Life to give portraits to families for the holiday season.

Melissa Alicia Cavero's Projects

  • "Winter is Coming" Editorial

    • October 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Melissa Alicia Cavero, Rebecca Oros, Helen Gerro, Lisette Villanueva, John "Dedo" Cristina

    Winter editorial featuring couture designs by Helen Gerro or Gerro Couture. Planning started in July of 2012. Editorial was based on the Japanese folklore Banchō Sarayashiki. We wanted the editorial to follow the flow of the story from start to end in which the servant girl's ghost came up from the well.

    The editorial features 4 of Helen's couture dresses, and jewelry from local designers John "Dedo" Cristina and Jewels by Zhia. Styling was a collaboration between Helen and I to keep with the descending order of vibrancy as the story.

    Make-up and hair was done by Lisette Villanueva of LaBrayJay Beauty.

    Our model was Rebecca Oros from Pulse Models Ink.

    Shooting was one full day on location with a full crew of assistants, as well as each member of the team, and the designer.
