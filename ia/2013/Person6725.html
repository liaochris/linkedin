Andres Moreno's Summary

Andres lived in the U.S. and 7 other countries on 3 continents while growing up. He began his entrepreneurial career in Venezuela, co-founding a company that provided Fortune 500 companies such as Procter and Gamble, Sun Microsystems, and Cargill with one-on-one English language instruction for their Latin American executives. After this company was unable to scale to meet demand, Andres spent the next four years developing innovative technologies and techniques that can scale online language training. Such proprietary technologies and content are at the core of his current venture, Open English, the leading online English school in Latin America. As CEO of Open English, he's raised over USD $50 million to expand the business to over 15 markets and 1000 team members.

Andres Moreno's Experience

Founder & CEO

Open English

Privately Held; 1001-5000 employees; E-Learning industry

January 2006 – Present (7 years 8 months)

Open English is an online school created to reinvent the English language learning experience. The company serves a large and rapidly growing global market, initially targeting Spanish speakers in the Americas. Through live instruction with native English speaking teachers and multi-media learning content, all provided over the internet, Open English empowers students on their customized path to English fluency.

Open English was originally founded in 2006, and its companies, with offices in Miami, Sao Paulo, Buenos Aires, Caracas, Bogotá, and Panamá, combine the best of US corporate governance practices with efficient Latin American operations and deep knowledge of the Spanish and Portuguese-speaking consumer/student.

Co-Founder & CEO

Optimal English

January 2002 – December 2005 (4 years)

Optimal English provided Fortune 500 companies such as Procter and Gamble, Sun Microsystems, and Cargill with one-on-one English language instruction for their Latin American executives.
