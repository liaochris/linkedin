Michael Page - Professional Recruitment Consultancy

Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; MPI.L; Staffing and Recruiting industry

February 1997 – July 2007 (10 years 6 months) UK

Before leaving the company, I was responsible for running Legal Recruitment teams in Reading, Guildford and London, working in both the Private Practice and In-house Markets.

I joined the business in the Leeds Finance and Accountancy practice, training under some truly talented people who took me from new boy, to one of the company's top billers.

In late 1999 I was asked to relocate to the South to develop and open a new greenfield office in Oxford. An exciting challenge involving all aspects of a start up operation, from building a profitable new client base, to hiring, developing and training both operational and support staff.

The office was opened in early 2000, and was taken to a strong profit position very quickly. By late 2003 I was looking for a new challenge and was again asked to relocate and this time change division to Legal Recruitment.

I was given the floundering Reading Legal business, and developed it from one person to a solid team of 7 consultants, support staff and a new manager making one of the highest profit streams in the business.

Next I opened a greenfield office site in Guildford, and was asked to also take over the London In-house business.

During my time at Michael Page I enjoyed many successes including 2 national annual high flyers awards (once as the MD's manager of the year), dozens of quarterly high flyers awards, and several office of the month awards. I promoted a number of managers and developed, trained and mentored dozens of consultants and up and coming managers.

At all stages of my career with Michael Page I was highly involved in developing and delivering training programmes for both new consultants, as well as experienced consultants in all facets of their professional development. I began to specialise in business development training and highly skilled consultant mentoring where I developed a love of training people to high performance.
