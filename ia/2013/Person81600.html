Lisa Martino, M.A.'s Summary

My professional experience is in adult career education both online and face-to-face since 2007 as an undergraduate online adjunct instructor and business instructor at a for-profit technical school. As a doctoral student in Career and Workforce Education (USF), my curriculum is the product of the most recent, innovative educational research in the field. I have a deeper understanding of curriculum design, assessment and evaluation techniques, educational philosophy, and higher education administration duties to include accreditation requirements. I developed and facilitated successful professional development workshops for faculty and staff.

I enjoy working with adults (both students and faculty) who want to advance their skills in order to seek a better future in the workforce. I am honored to be a part of my students' growth process. I will do anything I can to help my hard-working students change their circumstance for the better.

As a hobby, I write nonfiction and fiction. My fiction novel, Mercy Me, is a novel-in-progress. The novel was short listed for the prestigious William Faulkner Society Writing Competition 2012. My short story, "The Salon" won the finalist title in the Royal Palm Literary Competition in Florida 2011. My nonfiction article, Manufacturing Careers, Skilled Workers, and the Economy, appeared in Nov/Dec 2011 issue of Techniques Magazine (see link below). I am working on a nonfiction book as well, called "Land That Job"(details to follow).

Specialties: Strengths include research, technical writing, fiction writing, detailed writing style, grammar and punctuation, facilitation, presentation skills, curriculum design and lesson planning. Software: Microsoft Word, PowerPoint, Access, Outlook, Excel, and One Note. Web design using Claris and Front Page. Wiki for educational courses. Professional development workshop design and delivery.

Lisa Martino, M.A.'s Test Scores

  • ECW 7066 Foundations & Philosophy of Vocational, Technical & Adult Education

    • December 2012
    • Score: Final Grade 98% (A)

    Course Description: This course examines the concept of work and our nation’s system of Career and Workforce Education (CWE) including school-based and non-school based education and training with a closer look at the 9-14 career and technical education component. The historical development, philosophical perspectives, and social and economic foundations of education and training for work are considered.

  • EEX 7743 Philosophies of Inquiry

    • December 2012
    • Score: Final Grade: 105% A+

    The purpose of this course is to broaden the purview of the research preparation curriculum in education to ensure that the research leaders of tomorrow will be knowledgeable about, appreciative of, and committed to valuing the philosophical stances and moral/political values upon which the methodological canons of education inquiry are built. The course is decidedly not about teaching or learning about research designs or the technical methods and procedures adopted in various designs. It is designed explicitly to forge critical engagement with the epistemological stances and related moral and political values informing inquiry. All forms of inquiry make distinct assumptions about the nature of reality (ontology) and the nature of knowledge (epistemology) – even if such assumptions are not always explicitly communicated. The assumptions underlying different ontological and epistemological stances find expression not only in the construal of the nature of the relationship between the inquirer and what passes for knowledge, but also in the methodological tools employed in the inquiry process. Additionally, to the extent that inquiry takes place in context (historical, socio-cultural, economic, and political), it is by nature driven by prevailing value systems at any given point in time.
