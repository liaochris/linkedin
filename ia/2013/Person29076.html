Dana Oshiro's Summary

GOAL: To ensure that the best content, products and ideas bubble to the top regardless of the platform.

EDITORIAL STRATEGY: After writing for top-tier publications like ReadWriteWeb, Mashable and the NYTimes, I dove headfirst into the publishing business. I’ve since trained executive and content teams to build editorial calendars, manage newsrooms and boost their SEO and social engagement. In my most recent role I’ve offered strategy to 200+ publishers including 9to5Mac, Computer.org and Phandroid. In maintaining a steady flow of breaking news for those publishers, I’ve concurrently built relationships with CMOs at brands like Sony, Samsung and Toshiba .

BRANDING / CONTENT MARKETING: From board presentations, to press releases, to bylined articles, to executive keynotes -- I love shaping a brand story. Through messaging sessions, speaking opportunities and editorial, some of my best work has helped executives level up as thought leaders. In the past I co-managed a $20M communications plan to rebrand the province of British Columbia including working on events, speeches, billboards, print campaigns and broadcast ads for 22 government ministries. Today, I focus on media, influencer and analyst audiences to move the needle for brands.

STRATEGIC PLANNING: As a veteran PR pro and marketer I’ve not only planned PR, event and engagement strategies, but I’ve led teams to execute on them. With company-wide KPIs at the core of all planning, I use social analytics to map PR efforts and spending to customer conversion. This evolving process allows brands to tweak processes, forecast trends and develop a more user-centric approach.
