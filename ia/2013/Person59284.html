Jason Juliano's Summary

Jason Juliano is a open-minded leader with a passion to eliminate the injustice in his community. He has a background in technology management, project management, information security, and business continuity planning with over 20 years experience in the banking and financial industry. Most present, he held senior positions leading Enterprise, Operational & Information Risk Management; Project Management Office; Application Engineering; Technology Infrastructure and Network Operations. In his banking career, he had a major project to the implement of a strong authentication internet environment regulated under the new forming FFIEC (Federal Financial Institutions Examination Council). Prior to that, he was managing the Business Technology Analysts for Bank of NY focusing on global technology planning and project management.

In the past 9 years, Jason has been directly involved in establishing, building and directing Information Security and Privacy functions. He is the founder of Intellectual Property Security Review, an information security organization providing consulting and advisory services to major, startup and venture capital companies. He is a member Financial Services Information Sharing and Analysis Center (FS/ISAC) and a member of the Wall Street Technology Association. Jason currently has a seat on the Mortgage Banking Association Security Work Group. He has had speaking engagements at Network World, Black Hat & Technology Manager Forum.

Specialties: Project Managment, ITIL, Service Support and Service Delivery Management, Strategic planning, financing, budget planning, contract negotiation, information security, business recovery, application architecture, network operations, software development, helpdesk management, change management, vendor management, Banking, Financial Services, Healthcare , CISSP, CISM, Certified Mortgage Technologist. Community Activist: Political Empowerment, Youth Empowerment, Youth Anti-Violence.
