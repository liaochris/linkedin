Corey Luthringer's Summary

I'm an innovative social engineer, passionate about global health, and determined to inspire positive change in the world. I'm a firm believer in the power of creative cross-sector collaboration, mutual respect, and genuine understanding and I strive to leave this legacy everywhere I go. I am a proud alumna of Lehigh University, where I earned a B.S. in Chemical Engineering and built my foundation as a true global citizen. I was so inspired to have the opportunity to participate in a service project during a trip to the Czech Republic during my freshman year that I later was instrumental in creating the first international internship program at Lehigh focused on service learning and working with nonprofit organizations. This internship program is now a fully funded curricular option for students with opportunities in several countries.

My professional life began with work in research and development at a pharmaceutical giant. But, I've since segued into working with the nonprofit and public sector seeking a more active role in the health and development of communities by working with people instead of Erlenmeyer flasks. I am currently working in an international development firm and looking to see where I best fit in this realm. I try to find learning experiences and adventure in the little things that make up my daily life and I love empowering others to be engaged in the world around them.

I caught the travel bug in 2003 when I spent a month in the UK with the People to People Student Ambassador Program. Ever since, I've been struggling to find harmony between my nomadic, travel-loving, adventurous side and my proximity-to-family, stability side. What has resulted is a dream to work in international development, specifically in the areas of health systems strengthening and sustainable transitions out of government foreign aid.
