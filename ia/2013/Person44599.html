Daniel Fettinger's Experience

Sr. Manager, UI Engineering

TheFind, Inc.

Privately Held; 51-200 employees; Internet industry

July 2010 – Present (3 years 3 months)

TheFind crawls the entire Web and with its patented search technology to help shoppers discover everything that online shopping has to offer. Over 320 million products from more than 500,000 stores and online merchants are currently universally accessible.

As the leader of their UI team, I am responsible for all web engineering timelines, scoping exercises, inter-departmental communications, hirings, personnel evaluations and release deliveries.

Director of Technology

Salzinger LLC

October 2008 – July 2010 (1 year 10 months)

Salzinger LLC is a strategy and operations consulting firm in the media, technology and communications arena.

Salzinger LLC brought the Noozler.com technology and team into their fold in 2008 with eyes on digital marketing opportunities in the health and wellness industry. Products developed since include a business-to-consumer front end for OptumHealth Allies (A United Health Group Company), a health and wellness storefront, an online quiz management and delivery API and a customer survey engine.

As their Director of Technology, my duties include product design, technical and systems planning, technical client liaison and hands-on software implementation with an emphasis on SEO and font-end technologies.

Co-Founder

Noozler.com

March 2008 – July 2010 (2 years 5 months)

Noozler is a blog aggregation engine focused on the celebrity gossip space. As a co-founder, my responsibilities ranged from product and technical design to implementation, system maintenance and content management.

The technology and team behind the noozler.com website was soon acquired by Salzinger LLC (see above) and became core to many of their product offerings.

While Noozler.com remains online, it is no longer actively monitored or maintained.

Director of Technology

FanIQ.com

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Internet industry

August 2007 – November 2007 (4 months)

FanIQ is a leading sports community site with millions of users.

As their first technical manager, reporting directly to the CEO, I quickly doubled their capacity and engineering resources. Many of their earliest user acquisition features were directly in my purview.

Senior Manager, Software Development

StubHub

Privately Held; 1001-5000 employees; Internet industry

September 2001 – April 2007 (5 years 8 months)

StubHub is the world's largest ticket marketplace, enabling users to buy and sell tickets to tens of thousands of live entertainment events.

I was hired as their third engineer and 12th employee. During my tenure, the company grew from a tiny start up, to an enterprise of over 400, eventually being acquired by eBay for over $300 million dollars.

Over the course of my nearly six years there, I was involved in every major application development project. At the time of acquisition, I managed the software development department, leading a team of 17 engineers and managers responsible for all application work on the StubHub.com website.

Co-Founder/Director

zeroDEF Design Associates

September 1999 – September 2001 (2 years 1 month)

Founded a collective of freelance internet and multimedia development contractors.

Clients included BET.com (Black Entertainment Television), UC Berkeley Extension, Xircom, Detroit New Media Association and Dragonfly Mobile, Inc.

Web Operations Manager

Sigma6 Interactive Media

October 1997 – September 1999 (2 years)

Served as project development coordinator, lead developer and primary technical client liaison for an interactive media/web presence shop. In two years I oversaw the development of over 30 successful web rollouts varying from corporate identity and e-commerce storefronts to operational intranets. Sigma6 was acquired by Appnet (Commerce One) near the end of my tenure.

Project clients included General Motors, Plymouth, Worlddealer.net, Delphi Automotive, Harmony House and STS Systems.

Co-Founder/Vice President

Control Room Technologies

Privately Held; 11-50 employees; Entertainment industry

November 1994 – September 1997 (2 years 11 months)

Founded Internet software development company. CRT was covered in Web Week and Interactive Week for its work with then cutting edge remotely manageable web sites and real-time handheld data. CRT was also heralded for being among the very first to embrace streaming audio technology.

Clients included One-on-One Sports Radio Network, Nokia Sugar Bowl and Beatbox.com.
