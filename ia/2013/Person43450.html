Michael J. Evans' Summary

The subtitle of Mike Evans' blog is "How lawyers can use the Internet to attract clients, be more effective, make more money, and have more fun."

That's one thing Mike, who is primarily a plaintiffs' mass tort lawyer, has been doing the past twelve years.

During that time, Mike helped create a network of plaintiffs litigation firms, small law firms, and sole practitioners in 42 states. The goal was to let injury victims in rural America hire the same mass tort litigation firms that residents of California or N.Y could hire.

That goal was reached in Mike's first "Rezulin Legal Alliance" project which led Mike and his joint venture law firms to represent thousands of clients nationwide, with confidential settlement amounts that were, at the time, staggering. It has been repeated many times.

Mike is currently organizing other law firm alliances designed to bring top-notch legal representation to people across the U.S. These alliances will benefit participating law firms by enabling them to represent more people, more efficiently, using the latest technology.

Mike is involved in several current endeavors, but they all share the goal of using cutting-edge technology and marketing practices to connect lawyers with clients, as easily, simply and cost-effectively as possible.

In addition to Mike's legal marketing work, Mike is CEO of Digital Media Advocates, Inc., (DMA) a company formed in December 2012 to engage in innovative Internet technologies. marketing and public relations agency. DMA also engages in other innovative marketing strategies, including social media, online video, mobile marketing and the creation of mobile apps.

Mike believes we are lucky to be born in exciting times, with the opportunity to revolutionize, simplify and provide legal services nationally for the first time in the history of the world. As Guy Kawasaki (and countless others) have said, "Timing is everything." Mike believes the time for lawyers to fully embrace the Internet is now.

Michael J. Evans' Projects

  • Cutting-edge social media coverage of the 2010 Gulf oil spill.

    • April 2010 to Present
    Team Members: Michael J. Evans, Michael "bear" Praytor

    In April 2010, I began blogging and using social media to provide timely "citizen journalism" coverage of the BP oil spill in the Gulf of Mexico. I sent a videographer to the site of the spill, and he was prohibited by law enforcement officials, acting at the direction of BP employees, from videoing the aftermath of the disaster. My blogging about this and the violation of his First Amendment rights received substantial coverage by both mainstream media and scholarly publications. I also used BPOilNews.com to "crowdsource" possible solutions for stopping the oil leak. We received over 20,000 suggestions from members of the public, most of them before BP began allowing people to call and make suggestions. This experiment in using social media to cover a massive disaster resulted in my articles being cited in articles published by the American Bar Association, the Environmental Law Institute, the Tulane University Law School, the Boston College Law School, the New York Times, and numerous other publications, both in the U.S. and the U.K. At one point, my BPOilNews twitter account was one of the top 1,000 in the U.S. as measured by the number of "retweets." BPOilNews tweets were, for a time, "retweeted" more than those of celebrities and mainstream news anchors, such as Anderson Cooper. The BP Oil News Facebook Page had approximately 2,000 friends. After approximately a year of using social media to cover the oil spill, I decided to turn what began as a project in environmental advocacy into a legal cause because I lost faith in the BP Claims Fund and its administrator. At that time, I announced that I was no longer going to merely write about the oil spill, but that I would begin taking clients with BP claims. Although the social media coverage of the project is almost finished, I continue to represent individuals and businesses with BP claims. It was the BP oil spill project that taught me how to use social media.
