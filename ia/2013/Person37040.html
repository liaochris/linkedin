Dorothy Sander's Publications

  • Keeping Up Appearances: Who Would We Be If We Quit Talking About Aging?

    • Huffington Post
    • March 28, 2012
    Authors: Dorothy Sander

    What would happen to us if we stopped thinking and talking about "anti-aging"? If we quit working on, reshaping, modifying and enhancing our outer shell and altering our inner dialogue to fit some preconceived idea of the perfect "mature person"? What if we stopped trying to outsmart nature, stopped trying to prove that we are just as good, if not better than we were 20 years ago? Where might we put all that energy and creativity instead?

  • Caring for Mom: Midlife Reflections

    • LuLu
    • November 19, 2010
    Authors: Dorothy Sander

    In the pages of Caring for Mom, the reader will find understanding, compassion and food for thought. Dorothy Sander shares her experience and her reflections as she cares for her mother in the last days of her life. The book is a compilation of blogs and essays that delves into the complexities of aging, caregiving, loss and finding balance as we age. The author takes her readers on a very personal journey but it is one that those facing the challenges of midlife will recognize.

  • Aging Abundantly: A Little Book of Hope

    • Lulu
    • December 15, 2011
    Authors: Dorothy Sander, http://agingabundantly.com/downloads/aging-abundantly-a-little-book-of-hope/

    “A Little Book of Hope” is a pocketful of inspiration for the woman at midlife and beyond. Quotes and vignettes that inspire and uplift the reader, providing a few moments of relief and reflection any time. The paperback version is just the right size to put in your purse, the glove compartment of your car or beside your bed.
