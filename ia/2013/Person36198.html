Danielle Dayries' Summary

Danielle M. Dayries & Associates is an expert premier career consulting, outplacement, and resume writing firm that addresses and meets each clients individual goals and needs in an efficient manner. With 15 years of diversified experience, DMD & Associates is dedicated to the vision of our clients’ success. We specialize in virtual career consulting, resume writing and outplacement services, serving companies and individuals across the United States.

Taking a “non-cookie cutter” approach to consulting, we have maintained a 99% satisfaction record in the career transition of my clients. Our past clients have found new jobs in a median time of 3.2 months, and 85 to 90% find equivalent or better jobs.

The key services we provide include:

Coaching to assist in determining career direction
Developing resumes, cover letters and LinkedIn profiles
Utilizing multiple approaches, with an emphasis on networking, to uncover opportunities
Coaching candidates through interviewing and negotiating
Utilizing technology and social media methodologies as part of successful search strategies


CEO and Owner, Danielle Dayries, also enjoys a full speaking schedule with appearances including AmeriCorps, the New Orleans Business and Professional Women’s Association, and multiple Job Fairs.

New Orleans Outreach – Power Ties Educational Enrichment Coordinator (www.nooutreach.org)

New Orleans Outreach

Nonprofit; 11-50 employees; Nonprofit Organization Management industry

2009 – Present (4 years) Greater New Orleans Area

Power Ties is a multi-faceted program that connects local businesses and business people with public school partners and their students through job skills workshops, a career day, job site visits and internships. Power Ties is a grade level experience program, meaning that the partner school’s entire eighth-grade class participates fully in the program. The program consists of a series of four 1-hour workshops, held at the school site, in which volunteers teach students how to fill out a job application, write a resume, and present themselves for interviews. After the workshops are completed, a power luncheon with a motivational speaker is held at a special venue . The last event includes a job shadow experience for each student based on their unique career interests.

Board Member

WORKFORCE INVESTMENT BOARD

January 2008 – Present (5 years 9 months) Lafayette, Louisiana Area

Serve on board that addresses issues that face employers and job seekers in local communities. The WIB's provide a setting in which the private sector interacts with governmental agencies and non-profit organizations to identify the most effective steps towards improving the workforce.

The Lafayette Workforce Investment Board serves Lafayette Parish and receives federal money that is used to provide a variety of services to businesses and job seekers through the Lafayette Business and Career Solutions Center. The Lafayette WIB develops strategy for the federal money based on labor statistics and input from its business members. In addition, the board serves as a key intermediary between business, job seekers, and training providers. The Lafayette WIB host the Business Advisory Committee which allows the business community to network, voice concerns and gain vital information on an array of topics which affect employers from recruitment to workforce generation gaps. The board also has a youth committee which focuses on preparing youth for the workforce. Other WIB activities include:
•Strategic Planning
•Releasing Request for Proposals
•Selecting Service Providers
•Submitting Grant applications
•Financial Budgeting
•Compliance and Equal Opportunity Monitoring
