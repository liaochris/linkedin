Farooq Mughal's Summary

As the firm’s managing partner, Mr. Mughal maintains an active governmental affairs practice, oversees daily operations of the firm, and provides strategic direction to all the divisions. Farooq Mughal’s practice encompasses a broad range of political, legislative and governmental affairs matters. Farooq Mughal utilizes his distinguished background to provide strategic counsel to clients on public policy issues involving the White House, federal agencies and departments, Capitol Hill, and state and local governments.

Mr. Mughal’s practice is broad-ranging and includes policy matters in a number of different fields, including, diplomacy, international trade, foreign affairs, homeland security, defense and other matters. Mr. Mughal maintains contacts across a broad spectrum of elected and political leaders at both the federal and state levels. Mr. Mughal routinely advises elected officials on legislative priorities, governmental affairs, constituency outreach, foreign affairs, and political strategies. In addition to his expertise on public policy matters, Mr. Mughal is also a seasoned international negotiator, advising clients on international trade, cross cultural communication, political risk and U.S. foreign affairs.

In May 2013, Mr. Mughal was one of the few national leaders invited by the White House to meet President Obama and Vice President Joe Biden. In August 2012, Mr. Mughal was invited by President Obama to attend the White House Dinner to honor the leadership of Muslim Americans.
Mr. Mughal’s leadership in the political and legislative arena has been recognized by the White House, U.S. Congress, Georgia Governor Roy Barnes and Georgia state legislators.

Prior to his work in political arena, Mr. Mughal worked at King & Spalding LLP. At King & Spalding he got the opportunity to work under Judge Griffin Bell, who served as the 72nd Attorney General of the United States under President Jimmy Carter.
