Shirry Dolgin's Summary

Shirry Dolgin, a Southern California native, began her career in residential and commercial design after graduating Cum Laude with a degree in Interior Design at the highly prestigious "Fashion Institute for Design & Merchandising". Her natural artistic talent, stemming from an earlier dance career, made the transition into design a seamless one. Her warm personality and passion for the arts makes her a natural at her craft. She continues to design fantastic spaces for business owners, international developers, restaurateurs and residences setting her apart from the rest. Dolgin’s attention to detail is the foundation of her success and she prides herself on providing an unprecedented level of service to her clients. In 2004, Shirry officially opened ASD Interiors, serving clients from coast to coast.

Dolgin, who draws inspirations from her world travels, focuses on sharing her knowledge of design by creating custom spaces for each client. In addition to her award winning style, Shirry’s TV appearances include a design and remodel segment on KTLA, an appearance on HGTV's "Selling LA", and she was the winner on two episodes of HGTV's new design competition show "Design Wars". Her most recent TV appearances were on the new HGTV show "House Hunters Renovation" as well as HGTV's "You Live In What?". She also takes part in many speaking engagements at local universities and has been a guest speaker at the Kravet Showroom. In 2011 Shirry even took part in the charity design project "Good Shepherd Charity Design Project"

To find out more about current projects in the works, visit Shirry’s website shirrydolgin.com, follow her on Twitter @asdinteriors or on Facebook.

Specialties: Residential and commercial interior design including kitchen and bath remodeling.

Shirry Dolgin's Experience

Interior designer and owner of A.S.D. Interiors

ASD Interiors

January 2003 – Present (10 years 8 months)

Phase 1: It all starts with the first meeting! The consultation is where we gather all of the information on the overall project and what you are looking to achieve. Once we have a clear picture of your needs, a design plan can be developed. Having a design plan is critical in executing the project smoothly, no matter what size. This is what people miss when they are not working with a professional designer. In this phase we select color, materials, and lighting, design a space plan, and a budget starts to take shape. Once a design plan is completed, all bids from contractors are organized for final pricing. It is our belief that clients should have a full budget in place before making any decisions.
Phase 2: Once the design plan and budget have been approved by the client, it is now time to place all orders and coordinate all sub contractors and vendors. This takes much more time than it may sound. It involved countless meeting with contractors and vendors to ensure accuracy on all the fine details of the project. During this phase, there are regular visits to the job site to manage progress and answer any questions which may come up. This phase also involves tracking and coordinating the shipment and deliveries of all products that have been ordered.
Phase 3: Installation can finally begin! This is where the real transformation takes shape…and we are involved every step of the way. During this last phase, each area is completely “dressed” with all of the elements including furniture, lighting, accessories, plants, and art to give it you a completely designed space that is unique for your taste and designed just for you.
