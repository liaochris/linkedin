Charles Roop's Projects

  • Relationships Between Temperature and Precipitation in Florida and Atmospheric Teleconnection Indices

    • January 2010 to April 2010
    Team Members: Charles Roop, Chani Morris, Michelle Ruiz, Janna Rosenthal, Corene Matyas

    Participated in independent study research that was presented as a poster at the Florida Society of Geographers conference in 2011.

    Abstract:
    Florida weather conditions are affected on a seasonal scale by atmospheric teleconnections such as the El Nino Southern Oscillation (ENSO) and the Pacific/North American teleconnection (PNA). When certain phases of these teleconnections are occurring, Florida may experience drought, flooding or temperature extremes. To better understand which teleconnection indices exhibit strong seasonal relationships with Florida weather across the state, we examine the Atlantic Multidecadal Oscillation (AMO) and the North Atlantic Oscillation (NAO), in addition to ENSO and the PNA. Specifically, we chose 15 total sites from Florida’s seven climate divisions, split them into seasons, examined the monthly maximum and minimum temperatures and the monthly total rainfall, and then ran a Pearson’s Correlation Coefficient test between the teleconnections and the temperature and precipitation anomalies. Our results indicate that both the PNA and ENSO have a strong correlation with maximum temperature in the winter and spring, and we also developed a multivariate teleconnection index, which had the highest correlation with minimum temperatures in the summer in cities near the East coast.

    Keywords: Florida climate, climate forecasting, El Nino, teleconnections
