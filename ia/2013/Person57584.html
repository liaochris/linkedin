James Farrell's Publications

  • GNSS Aided Navigation and Tracking

    • American Literary Press
    • May 2007
    Authors: James Farrell, I am the sole author.

    All aspects of satellite and inertial integration are covered, all the way from raw measurement pre-processing to final 3-D position/velocity/attitude, with thorough backup and integrity provisions. Full uses is made of any measurement from any satellite in any GNSS constellation. Extensive test results illustrate the attainable accuracies (cm/s RMS velocities in three-dimensions) during flight under extreme vibration. The book provides several original flight-validated formulations and algorithms not yet in use. Considerable opportunity is therefore offered in multiple areas including
    * full use of highly intermittent ambiguous carrier phase
    * rigorous integrity for separate SVs
    * unprecedented robustness and situation awareness
    * high performance from low cost IMUs
    * “cookbook” steps
    * new interoperability features
    * new insights for easier implementation.

  • Integrated Aircraft Navigation

    • Academic Press
    • 1976
    Authors: James Farrell, I am the sole author.

    This book, written just as GPS was emerging from its experimental stage, supplies fundamental theory and development useful for those learning inertial navigation and/or Kalman filtering. After five hard cover printings it is now available in paperback.

  • GPS Made Simple

    • Amazon
    • April 2013
    Authors: James Farrell, Steve Wright

    This presentation provides a basic understanding of GPS for those trying to learn it for the first time. Although satellite navigation now includes constellations from Europe and Asia, all have enough in common to focus on GPS for introductory discussion. Material was selected and organized with a a learner's perspective in mind. for a "straight-to-the-point" exposition with little or no mathematics and, rather than an extensive bibliography, a few few URLs that can be followed towards a wealth of further sources for those interested.
