Logan Flatt, CFA's Experience

SVP, Strategic Planning

Ansira

Privately Held; 201-500 employees; Marketing and Advertising industry

2010 – Present (3 years) Dallas

With close to $100 million in revenue in 2013, Ansira is one of the leading data-driven marketing agencies in the United States.

Division: Strategy

Strategy consulting unit focused on providing one-to-one marketing planning services to major brands

Lead a team of 9 Strategic Planners working with clients’ senior marketing executives to develop and implement ROI-centered, one-to-one marketing plans to be executed across multiple addressable touch points including web, mobile, search, email, social media, display media, direct mail, print, and call centers. Plans range from 6 months to 3 years in scope. Budgets range from $0.5 MM to $50 MM. Example client results: deals closed up 23% YOY, average Cost Per Order down 64% YOY, and database acquisitions up 109% YOY.

Clients include Domino's Pizza, Rent-A-Center, Wendy's, Nestlé Purina, Carpet One, Culligan International, Panera Bread, Dave & Busters, Mitsubishi Motors, and Aruba Tourism Authority.

Division: Insight

Marketing performance measurement and analysis unit generating data-driven insights and intelligence

Hired in Q1 2010 to turn around unit and add digital marketing competency. While also leading Strategy unit, led a team of 12 Insight Analysts charged with delivering to clients each month a variety of KPI-driven insights on campaigns, customers, consumers, and markets in the form of business intelligence reports, dashboards, and presentations. Today, 16 analysts in thriving unit serve over 25 clients.

President/Owner

Vero Group, Inc.

Privately Held; 1-10 employees; Marketing and Advertising industry

1997 – 2012 (15 years) Dallas

Formed Vero Group, Inc. in the late 1990s to support independent consulting initiatives. Highlights:

Client: Leading U.S. private equity firm dedicated to investments in travel and leisure businesses

Client’s investment in a country club operating company with 150 clubs in 25 states declined significantly in value due to membership attrition following an economic downturn. Retained in Q4 2009 to lead client executives through a strategy development process to identify revenue growth opportunities centered on digital marketing and technology. After implementation, memberships grew 3.8% and revenue per member increased 4.3% per year. In September 2013, company successfully completed an IPO on the NYSE.

Client: Fifth-busiest international airport in the U.S.

Client sought to replace ‘pay-per-use’ Wi-Fi Internet service with free service as the airport’s city prepared to host a series of major international sporting events. However, an airport bond payment was funded partly by a major telecom’s annual fee to operate the Wi-Fi concession. Retained in Q1 2007 to assess feasibility of replacing concessions fee with Wi-Fi advertising revenue. Advised client to implement a hybrid approach – free for a limited time and then paid – until guest traffic and ad rates increased. Today, airport continues to make its bond payments while offering its guests both free and premium Wi-Fi services.

Client: Co-lead counsel in class action against managers of an imploded, fixed-income derivatives hedge fund

Plaintiff class lost $2.5 billion NAV despite manager reassurances that risk management practices would preclude deep losses. Retained in Q1 2009 to conduct a forensic analysis of published performance data and manager letters to offer pre-filing opinion regarding manager fraud and misrepresentation. As a result of findings, co-counsels filed class action in federal court.

Managing Principal

Makati Capital Management, LLC

2008 – 2009 (1 year) Dallas

While working as a financial analyst and operations assistant to the Managing Partner of Anson Investments Master Fund LP, a $79 million long‐short hedge fund, started from scratch the Texas Registered Investment Advisory firm, Makati Capital Management, LLC and began to raise funds for Makati Premier Fund LP, a long-short hedge fund seeking to profit by varying exposures (net long, net short, or neutral) to equity markets using equity and equity derivative securities, including options and futures.

Unfortunately, in mid-September 2008 worldwide financial markets collapsed, the aftermath of which created an extremely challenging environment for raising capital for a new fund with a new manager. After many months of making a go at capital raising, mothballed Makati in July 2009 and returned to Vero Group, Inc. to consult to attorneys on financial litigation increasingly underway at that time.

President, ROI Solutions

Tribal Worldwide

Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; OMC; Marketing and Advertising industry

2004 – 2008 (4 years) Dallas

Division: ROI Solutions

Global consulting unit providing marketing analytics and strategy services to corporate marketers

In Q4 2004, identified need for digital marketing analytics and strategy practice. Crafted business plan that won CEO’s approval to start new, global unit. Grew business to 17 FTEs generating over $5.0 MM in consulting and media fees by mid-2008. Clients: PepsiCo, Nokia, AT&T, ExxonMobil, KLM-Air France, U.S. Air Force, DISH Network, and others. Success featured in November/December 2007 issue of CFA Magazine.

Division: Dallas Operations

Largest operating division of a worldwide network of digital marketing agencies with 54 offices in 36 countries

As Vero Group, retained in Q1 2004 to help turn around unprofitable operations and achieve target levels (20%) for growth and profitability. Later joined management team full-time as head of two departments with 29 FTEs. Team achieved record performance results in 2006: 48.5% revenue growth and 37.1% PBT Margin.

Adweek named Tribal Worldwide the 2005 Digital Agency of the year.

On weekends while working at Tribal, studied for and successfully obtained the Chartered Financial Analyst (CFA) designation, confirming my value to the investment industry. Left the agency in July 2008 to work for a hedge fund while starting my own hedge fund.

Vice President, Business Strategy

Rare Medium

Public Company; 1001-5000 employees; RRRR; Internet industry

1999 – 2001 (2 years) Dallas

Rare Medium was a high-growth digital technology and e-commerce consulting firm during the "dot com" boom and bust. Developed Internet strategies for major corporations with executive teams largely unfamiliar with the Internet and its possibilities for business. Highlights:

Client: Global media and entertainment company specializing in radio and outdoor display advertising

Client acquired many radio companies, each with fledgling Internet radio units the client assigned no value. Retained in Q4 1999 to advise on each unit and all units collectively. Client followed recommendations to retain and merge all units to create one digital streaming radio property that today generates advertising and subscription revenues from 243 MM listeners each month.

Client: Large, privately held publishing company with world’s top-rated business news website

Prominent business magazine website struggled to deliver sufficient ad impressions, negatively impacting revenue growth. Retained in Q3 1999 by family owners to investigate. Team uncovered myriad organizational and technology challenges precluding success. Owners followed recommendations that increased ad impression volume. Today, the site’s 27 MM monthly visitors generate over $100 MM per year in revenue.

Rare Medium halted its professional services practice after the 09/11/2001 terrorist attacks on the U.S.

Senior Consultant, Petroleum & Chemicals

Towers Perrin General Management Consultants

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; TW; Management Consulting industry

1993 – 1997 (4 years) Dallas, Chicago, Singapore & Manila

Towers Perrin – now Towers Watson – is a leading global professional services company. Cut costs and clarified market opportunities through strategy and process reengineering engagements for clients. Highlights:

Client: Global joint venture with refining and marketing operations in Asia-Pacific, Africa, and Middle East

Client sought to cut operating costs by creating a shared services center for the Asia-Pacific region. Retained in Q1 1997 to lead functional managers in Singapore, the Philippines, Thailand, Hong Kong, and Malaysia through a restructuring process. Team cut costs by over 35% and, after a detailed assessment of alternatives, established Manila, Philippines, as the new center of operations.

Left the firm in late 1997 to pursue a new career involving my interest in the Internet and the World Wide Web, which were undergoing commercialization at that time.
