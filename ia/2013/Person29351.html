Global Voices is a community of more than 700 authors and 600 translators around the world who work together to bring you reports from blogs and citizen media everywhere, with emphasis on voices that are not ordinarily heard in international mainstream media.

Global Voices was founded in 2005 by former CNN Beijing and Tokyo Bureau Chief, Rebecca MacKinnon and technologist and Africa expert, Ethan Zuckerman while they were both fellows at the Berkman Center for Internet and Society at Harvard University. The idea for the project grew out of an international bloggers’ meeting held at Harvard in December 2004 and it began as a simple blog. (Here's a written report and podcast of that meeting).

Global Voices quickly expanded thanks to patronage of the Berkman Center, support from Reuters, the MacArthur Foundation, and the energy and creativity of our contributors.

Danica Radisic's Projects

  • Digital Voice of Serbia

    • May 2012 to May 2012
    Team Members: Danica Radisic, Vesna Opavsky, Ana Keglović Horvat, Bartol Simunic, Petar Vojinovic

    The focus of this project was to use online resources and tools to collect information, comments, voter sentiment and other indicators regarding the 2012 elections in Serbia from social networks (public comments only), discussion forums, blogs and comments on online media sites. While focusing on online voter sentiment and indicators as to which party or candidate they favor in this election, we also monitored and reported on any possible irregularities during the pre-election silence and election process in general. Users were happy to assist and offer information.
    This is the first time that any such undertaking has been carried out publicly during any European election.
    An Ushahidi Crowdmap was set up on izbori2012.crowdmap.com, as well as an email and mobile number to monitor and report on real-time information regarding elections directly from voters and other sources during the electoral process.
    Based on received and analyzed information, reports were regularly written and published on both mc.rs and digitalniglas.rs in periods May 4-6 and 14-20 during the 2012 elections in Serbia

  • Balkan Venture Forum - Belgrade

    • September 2012 to Present
    Team Members: Danica Radisic, Nenad Paunovic, Aleksandar Bijelic, Milos Dosen

    The Balkan Venture Forum is a leading regional investment and innovation forum. It showcases innovative companies to active investors and promotes innovation, entrepreneurship, investment readiness and access to finance in the Balkans. The first Belgrade edition of the Balkan Venture Forum will take place November 22-23 at Hotel Metropol Palace in Belgrade and bring together over 40 global and regional investors, a total of 70 renowned speakers, policy makers from several countries in the region and hundreds of guests.
