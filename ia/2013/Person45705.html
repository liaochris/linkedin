Fwd:Vault

Privately Held; Myself Only; Information Technology and Services industry

September 2008 – December 2011 (3 years 4 months) http://fwdvault.com

Fwd:Vault ("forward vault") is a cloud-based SaaS product that facilitates document collaboration through email. Users are provided a special email address, which project teams then use to share files. Fwd:Vault archives the attachment and retains the change history of the document. The website allows users to download any version of the file, and provides additional sharing tools.

As the sole Founder, Frank is responsible for all technical decisions and business direction.

For more details, visit http://fwdvault.com

ClassicWines was the social hub for wine enthusiasts. Frank was brought in to lead all technical tasks, including a rebuild of the original prototype website. He established formal software development practices, improving development output by 20% and reducing post-launch bugs by 40% in the process. His initial team included four direct reports, with plans to expand the team as business progressed.

Frank also provided technical direction to business decisions, delivering monthly development milestone updates to investors, managing the technology budget, and acting as the IT acquisition authority.

Director of Technology

Destination ImagiNation, Inc.

Nonprofit; 11-50 employees; Education Management industry

June 2004 – October 2007 (3 years 5 months)

Destination ImagiNation is an education non-profit serving all 50 states, over 30 countries, and over 30,000 volunteers. It operates with less than 20 staff. As the lone "technical" staff member, Frank wore a lot of hats.

He was responsible for all IT operations: hardware/software acquisition, configuration, networking, security, and direct user support. Locations included a networked central office and a multitude of remote staff.

Frank also led software development initiatives. He installed and administered the organization's first ecommerce platform. Then he integrated the company's paper-based membership process, building an accompanying management interface for staff, volunteers, and participants at all levels of the organization.

Between product and membership services, the platforms Frank developed processed more than 20k orders from 25k users, worth over $4M, just during his tenure. These systems are still in use today.

Frank's solitary position was replaced with two staff members upon his departure.
