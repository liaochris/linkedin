Richard Chen's Summary

I was last at Digital Pulp, an interactive agency where I code mostly frontend (PHP heavily, WordPress themes, many frameworks, mobile and responsive sites) for many of our clients, especially non-profits & education, both causes which inspire my care and natural motivation to be ever more technically astute. I also did backend including Radiant, sysadmin for various linux boxes & migrating drupal installs. Creative fun shop.

I was at Acadaca, a startup which put boutique clients onto their e-commerce platform of Magento (uses Zend, PHP). I coded e-commerce sites (andrewmarc.com, chromebagstore.com, l-r-g.com & girlsdressshop.com). I disliked its culture.

I liked Scholastic's simple, clear mission and balanced corporate culture. Its coworkers & organizational purpose gave daily satisfaction. I coded scholastic.com (touching 1000s of its Millions of pages) with ~80 inspiring coworkers. I love its morale & ethos.

I also loved interning for the US EPA Superfund division and the OSRTI (Office of Science, Research and Technology Initiatives) Data Standards Branch. Public policy and guiding technology are close to my heart's desire and my eventual career goal after gaining deep technical experience in the private sector.

I coded DB2 for Hewitt Associates's healthcare outsourcing for client Lucent. I was bored.
