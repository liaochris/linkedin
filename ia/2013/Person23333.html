Chris Habecker's Summary

As a member of the Nook team at Barnes & Noble, I help to imagine and create the future of reading and education.

Previously, I developed Android applications and associated web service components, leveraging my background in enterprise DB, embedded software, and hypermedia applications. See the BetterHabit LLC, Scanillion, and Eebo Plus sections, below, for more information.

My client-facing experience is extensive: pre-sales engineer, consultant, analyst, trainer, and trade show presenter.

My development experience ranges from designing enterprise DB applications to writing Java frameworks on set-top boxes to scripting interactive, data-rich, multimedia applications.

My management experience ranges from leading small teams of direct reports to medium teams (up to 8) of customer staff.

I am a veteran of Oracle, Sybase, three successful startups, and two independent consultancies.

Professional, pleasant, and productive under pressure.

Affiliate services (TenFold SOA, web services): Exposed an order-processing DB application as a service for the company’s affiliates. Integrated a third-party subscription web service into the order-processing application using TenFold’s SOA framework.

Senior engineer

Zentek

Public Company; 51-200 employees; Computer Software industry

April 2001 – April 2005 (4 years 1 month)

I refactored, fixed, and completed our implementation of the GEM/MHP API, a Java framework that ran Java apps (Xlets) on set-top boxes. I owned the multi-threaded Xlet manager, which loaded classes and XML configurations from the MPEG stream, managed Xlet lifecycles, and enabled inter-Xlet communication. I mastered GEM/MHP so well that I identified flaws in the compiled reference platform that disqualified thousands of the official certification tests.

Special projects: I ported an MPEG device driver from PiE-OS to Linux, managing the client relationship with Matsushita and delivering the code and porting guide on schedule. The success of that project led to a second: prototyping a web service-based consumer electronics network using SOAP and UDDI. I wrote the MHP test harness in Perl. And for Zentek’s mobile platform, I wrote a program to parse Java bytecode and verify that it adhered to the mobile specification.
