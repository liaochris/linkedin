Joshua Jeffryes' Summary

Blending startup technology and processes like DevOps, Lean Startup, Agile, and deeply integrated UX/Development to make any team, large or small, work at startup speeds is my specialty. I know how to hire the right people, build processes to make them work the right way, and implement the technologies that let them perform better than anyone else.

I've been building award-winning websites, apps, brands, organizations and designs for 18 years, along with the teams that built them.

My current focus is on modernizing College Board's web development team to embrace fully modern practices like Scrum/Agile, Mobile First design, Responsive Design, JavaScript MVC and Object Oriented CSS (if you're not using OOCSS, you're doing it wrong).

My day varies between 20% and 80% hands-on code, with the rest being filled with leading, mentoring, growing my team, and making technology and product decisions. My specialty is User Experience (UX) and Design, HTML5, CSS3, Mobile, and Accessibility (WCAG 2 AA+). My JavaScript isn't at the same level as my other skills, but I know enough to architect a web app, write most of the code, and hire a team that can do the rest.

If the above isn't sufficiently exotic, I am also reasonably good at public speaking, writing, concept art, and game design. I'm an award-winning political cartoonist, and I co-designed the helicopter that appeared on stage on NBC's World's Greatest Magic. I can sing too, though I'm rarely paid to.

(I'm very serious about HTML and CSS. I dare you to find someone in the DC area that knows more about it, and how it affects your scalability, SEO, accessibility, and site performance than me)
