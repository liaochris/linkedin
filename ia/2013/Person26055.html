Movement Coach

Coach Oscar

September 2007 – 2009 (2 years)

What is Movement Coaching?

Simply put, I help people move better.

By combing the BioPhysical, the BioChemical, and the Emotional/Cognitive aspects of movement, health, and wellness, we learn to develop a subconscious mastery over our own bodies. With improved movement, we are able to overcome injury and reduce the chance of recurrence. We are able to reduce or eliminate aches and pains often resulting from daily demands. We are able to excel in sports because we become masters of small movement and allow the body to perform gross movements more effectively and efficiently thus resulting in improved performance. We are able to enjoy our lives more because we are removing those physical limitations that lead to mental blocks and poor choices.

In doing all this, we lose weight, we get stronger and more athletic, and in its simplest form, WE GET BETTER! Getting better does not need to involve pain (i.e. foam rolls, stretching to your limit, "no pain, no gain" attitudes). When we experience pain, our body is telling us something is wrong. So if we stop performing a movement that hurts, doesn't it make sense to stop performing a "treatment" that hurts? So how do you move away from pain AND get better at the same time?

Some of my clients are people who have gone through recent surgery or are looking to return from a past injury. By teaching them to move all parts of their bodies better, they have been able to regain full or near-full range of motion and do so in a pain free manner.

I have had other clients who want to live healthy, active lives and who are doing so through goal-oriented training challenges. Runners and triathletes have benefited from these methods.

I have also had my clients receive full academic scholarships to major universities or acceptance in collegiate sports programs. Those who are experiencing success are those who are becoming masters of their sport. I train to develop that mastery.
