Chris Gabriel's Summary

Chris Gabriel has delivered extraordinary results in telecommunications and technology companies across five continents. His success arises from his inclusive leadership style, cross-cultural sensitivity, intuition for customer motivation, and a sense of humour that keeps everything in perspective. Chris is now an angel investor and holds various strategic roles including senior advisor to Macquarie Group and chairman of Alive Mobile Group.

Chris is:
• A renowned international telecommunications executive with a passion for operational excellence and over-achievement in high-growth start-up, turn-around and emerging market environments;
• Recognised as the pioneer of cross-border mobile commerce in Africa;
• A results-oriented and delivery-focussed entrepreneur with a proven track record;
• An engaging, charismatic and decisive leader who is self-motivated, with energy, drive and enthusiasm;
• A goal focussed team player who is proactive and creative, with a strong readiness to “challenge the norm”;
• Renowned for building, developing, leading and retaining high-performance teams; and
• An exceptional communicator, with strong interpersonal and presentation skills.

Chris has an exemplary array of qualifications in finance, law, marketing, strategy and information technology gained at London Business School, University of Technology, Sydney, University of New England and numerous other institutions.

Chris’s specialties:
• Start-ups, turn-arounds and high-growth emerging markets;
• Business development and revenue growth;
• Strategic planning and execution;
• Mergers, acquisitions, divestments, funding and IPOs;
• Financial control and performance optimisation;
• Information technology and strategic architecture;
• Project management and delivery;
• Business process engineering;
• Organisational and cultural transformation;
• Leadership, training, coaching and mentoring;
• Communication, public relations and public speaking; and
• Having Fun!

World E-Money Council

September 2010 – Present (3 years) London, United Kingdom

The World E-Money Council is a body focussed on helping E-Money providers worldwide to further develop their platforms in a coherent and orderly manner. The Council operates under the global patronage of Official Monetary & Financial Institutions Forum (OMFIF).

The decision to establish the World E-Money Council was taken following a ‘World Roundtable on Electronic Money Management’ held in May 2010. This provided an early opportunity for the industry to engage with monetary authorities to ensure the roadblocks for a faster adoption of electronic money and electronic payments are removed, while safeguards for the protection of consumers and participants in the payments chain are placed on sound foundations.

Members of the World E-Money Council comprise leading retail banks, cards and payments providers, telecommunication companies, technology vendors, central banks and other relevant operations.
