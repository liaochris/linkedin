Jeff Hoffman's Summary

Proven Public Affairs executive for the world’s premier media and entertainment company and currently leading his own firm. Experienced in setting an agenda informed by current and emerging issues in philanthropy, community relations, volunteerism, cause marketing and corporate citizenship. Partner with business, philanthropic, civic and creative leaders in designing and implementing initiatives to strategically advance the role of civil society to help solve some of the world’s most pressing issues. Adept at organizational, fiscal, creative, communications and brand management. Vice President, Disney Worldwide Outreach

The Walt Disney Company

Public Company; 10,001+ employees; DIS; Entertainment industry

February 2001 – February 2010 (9 years 1 month)

Global responsibility for Philanthropy, Community Relations, Volunteerism and Cause Related Marketing for The Walt Disney Company.

Responsible for a decentralized team of up to 30 and an additional 75 dotted line reports imbedded in the businesses supporting more than 144,000 employees. Developed and executed a global strategic vision for the company’s community relations building upon the unique Disney, ABC and ESPN brands. Reported to the Senior Vice President of Corporate Responsibility. Significant accomplishments included:

•Oversaw Disney’s charitable giving which in FY2009 was $230 Million in cash, in-kind support, product donations and public service air time. Championed the effort to better report Disney’s giving globally and manage charitable requests for both company donations and those from The Walt Disney Company Foundation.

•Created the Disney Legends program to honor individuals whose work made a significant impact on the Disney legacy. Produced high profile media events honoring such legendary luminaries as Elton John, Julie Andrews, Kurt Russell and Barbara Walters.

•Established the award winning employee Disney VoluntEARS program at corporate headquarters and then was tasked to lead its expansion globally. In FY 2009, was responsible for the VoluntEARS’ efforts that contributed more than 569,000 hours of service in more than 40 countries.

•Worked with Walt Disney International to integrate the outreach vision into local country business plans including the startup operations in India and China.

•Convened and then co-chaired the Corporate Social Responsibility Committee that led to the eventual creation of the Corporate Responsibility function.

•Collaborated with Disney Parks Marketing on the strategy and implementation of their very successful 2010 signature marketing initiative, “Give A Day, Get A Disney Day” where guests earn a ticket to a Disney Theme Park for their efforts volunteering in the community.
