Cy Gorman's Summary

Cy Gorman is an interdisciplinary performer, producer and director of hybrid performance and audiovisual arts and psychology.

Cy's work as a theatre and film director has appeared in Short and Sweet Festival, St Kilda Film Festival, MTV and Next Wave Festival.

His work as a music producer/composer and designer is extensive and functions underground, in the professional arts world and in the corporate world.

Cy has performed at the Melbourne international Jazz festival with the VCA Jazz ensemble 'Ensemble Max', the 2009 Electundra Festival and completed studying a scholarship funded Postgraduate Diploma in Performance Creation/Animateuring at the Victorian College of the Arts. He is currently experimenting with MRI software to generate 3D audiovisual work, using and abusing pre-recorded text to create sonic landscapes, producing his debut album and tracks for other artists and writing oceanic piano waltzes to be performed in public soon.

PostGrad, Hybrid Performance Creation/Music/Theatre

Animateuring involves the creation of new performances through the mobilisation of expressive, artistic and imaginative faculties in a variety of artistic and community contexts, the ability to work individually or in small groups, the capacity to work within and across art forms, community ownership of process and performance outcomes and collaboration within communities interested in developing and realising various forms of artistic performance. The animateur is a skilled performance artist (Dance and/or Theatre) with a central commitment to artistic leadership. He or she may be involved in various ways with the processes of creation, direction, facilitation, and performance. The role played is a pivotal one, with the success measured not only in artistic terms, but also by other indicators such as level of community support, participation, ownership and achievement of social and political goals.
