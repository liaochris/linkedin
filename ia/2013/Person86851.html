Mark McGregor's Summary

A highly experienced product manager and marketer, Mark currently serves as an Industry Analyst. Providing help and advice to both vendors and end user organisations.

Having held held senior positions in a number of software vendors, he has earned a strong reputation for helping companies “punch above their weight” with limited marketing budgets. This experience has been gained in both the EMEA and North American markets.

Getting the product and marketing messages right, and then getting those messages out to the market is crucial for success – history is littered with stories of great technology failing to achieve due to poor marketing.

A popular speaker, and frequent writer, Mark is a recognized as having specialist expertise in the areas of Business Process Management, Enterprise Architecture and Modelling Tools. He has been described by GartnerGroup as a "BPM Guru" and by IQPC as a "Master of Mindset".

Marks books include:-

"People Centric Process Management" (Co-Author)
"Winning with Enterprise Process Management" (Author)
"Thrive! How to Succeed in the Age of the Customer" (Co-Author)
"In Search of BPM Excellence" (Contributor and Co-Editor)
"Extreme Competition" (Contributor)
"El Linro del BPM 2011" (Contributor)

In addition to his work in product and strategy, Mark also called upon to assist venture capitalists with due diligence on potential investments and providing M&A advice to software vendors.

These days Mark is very focussed on the people side of change, especially innovation and communication and works with groups around the world, helping them to help themselves and their companies.

Mark McGregor's Publications

  • People Centric Process Management

    • Smart Questions Ltd
    • March 20, 2010
    Authors: Mark McGregor

    A catalyst for action aimed at leaders of process change or transformation projects. The book is not technical and is aimed at people like you and me. People who are looking to maximise organisational performance, drive up quality and conformance, and enable more agile response to change.

  • Thrive! How to Succeed in The Age of The Customer

    • Meghan-Kiffer Press
    • 2006
    Authors: Mark McGregor, Steve Towers

    How much time do you spend looking at ways to get done inside your business? It is a fair guess to say that a massive chunk of corporate resources goes into such efforts, whether it is in IT, finance, sales, marketing or operations. But seeking internal improvements is no longer enough. The world's leading companies now look outward to emphasise a deep understanding of their customers, and what it takes to make their customers successful. They have come to realise that only when their customers are successful, will they be successful. These leading companies are intent on achieving Successful Customer Outcomes - everywhere, all the time.

    Thrive! discloses how you can learn and deploy these new ideas immediately!

  • In Search of BPM Excellence

    • Meghan-Kiffer Press
    • 2005
    Authors: Mark McGregor, Roger Burlton, Steve Towers

    Business process innovation is on the minds of executives these days, and for good reason. In the 1990s, companies also had a focus on business processes. They used enterprise-wide networks to tear down walls between functional departments and reengineered their companies to remain competitive. Today, the universal connectivity of the Internet makes it possible to tear down walls between companies to reinvent entire value chains. The result? We are now witnessing a grand globalization of white collar work, outsourcing, offshoring and other new forms of extreme competition. Industry and national boundaries have become a blur. All is changed, and no industry is exempt. Pioneering companies have already disrupted incumbents and come from nowhere to dominate their industries. Their secret sauce? Business process management (BPM). This book brings together some of the best minds to explore the role and value of BPM, and what it portends. In its pages you will find the essential discussions and insights, straight from the thought leaders. In Search of BPM Excellence is for those who want to sustain the success of their businesses in the midst of the current sea of change. Is your company ready for extreme competition?

  • 7 Steps to BPM Success

    • El Libro del BPM 2011
    • 2011
    Authors: Mark McGregor

    An article (in Spanish) describing 7 steps that if taken together will ensure your BPM program is one of those that delivers the maximum benefit to your organisation, with involvement and engagement at all levels of the business.
