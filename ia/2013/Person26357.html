Colin Walcott's Publications

  • Mobile Applications in Urban Market Places

    • The Gaurdian, Toronto Spots, Latinamerica Press
    Authors: Colin Walcott, Charles Roach

    AP - Within an evolving Mobile Market Place, the biggest trends with mobile users and subscribers seems to the Afro and Latin Communities. Colin Walcott (mobile designer from Lavalife since 2006) noted trends and demographic usage with mobile apps, WAP and usage on both feature phone s and smart phones. "Using the default apps with the phone was one thing, reverse engineering them to develop a totally new application was another. I even remember the 1st generation iPhone sneaking its way to the Canadian side of the border; someone hacked it to run off the local providers and run original ‘web’ apps," laughed Colin Walcott, Mobile designer from Toronto, CANADA."The 1st thing that was done was modifying the device to do more than what it was designed to do. They would jailbreak the phone and over haul what the OS was doing on the device. Web apps originally built in C++ were dumbed-down to comply with the native code and rules of IOS then. Every one that reverse-engineered the apps, devices or mobile experience had done it before either in Brazil, Dubai, India, Venezuela, or out in West Vancouver, Canada.”
    When the Smartphone devices and apps were based on corporate blackberry users during 2007, the spike of people using and purchasing apps were between ages of 18 to 35: males from either the Afro or Latin communities. "Popularity with apps and social media sharing in South America seems 2 to 3 years ahead of the US," said Colin Walcott – See more on the AP Press.
