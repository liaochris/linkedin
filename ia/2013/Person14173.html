Bill Bowman's Summary

I've served as a weekly newspaper reporter and editor, newsletter editor, a magazine writer and a daily newspaper reporter.

I also have recorded, mixed and mastered two CDs for jazz vocalist PJ Parker: "Intimate" in 2006 and "It's Christmas" in 2008. I served as co-producer, with PJ, for her 2008 release, "Dreams Are Meant For Two."

My reporting has instigated change a number of times: creating a state-wide program through which all of New Jersey's fire departments and fire academies received free thermal imaging cameras; requiring the state's top judges to file yearly financial disclosure forms; stopping a massive residential development corrupted by backroom deals, and creating a Web-based, searchable Web directory of the state's nursing homes and adult day care centers, through which owners and violations can be found.

I also was part of a two-reporter team which discovered that the Department of Defense underestimated the costs and overestimated the savings involved in closing Fort Monmouth, NJ and moving most of its mission to Aberdeen Proving Ground, Md., as part of the 2005 Base Realignment and Closure round. Our reporting found that the cost to close the fort -- pegged at $780 million by DOD in 2005 -- had jumped to nearly $2 billion by the time the fort was closed in 2011. We found documents that showed the DOD knew in 2005 that its estimated costs were low, but ignored that information.

Our reporting led to a Congressional hearing, and investigations by the General Accountability Office -- which substantiated our findings -- and the DOD's Inspector General.

We also appeared on C-SPAN's "Washington Report," taking calls from viewers asking about the 2005 BRAC process.

Specialties: Conducting online and paper-based research; quickly learning new technologies/software; building communities; covering beats; simplifying complicated subjects.
