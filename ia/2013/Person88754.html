Matt Gray's Summary

A 20 year veteran of the Capitol, Matt has worked on every major public policy issue to come before the California State Legislature, including green energy, information technology, water, public safety, health care, taxation, education, and natural resources.

In 2004 Matt left the Capitol where he was Deputy Chief of Staff within the Legislature, to join Capital Alliance as a contract advocate for numerous HiTech and information technology clients as well as a venture capital firm on developing emergent technologies.

Formally educated in communications and public relations, as well as organic chemistry and nuclear physics, Matt has extensive technical experience which enables him to digest complex discussions and process them into actionable plans.

In 2010, Matt was elected by Sacramento voters to the City Council for the proposed formation of Arden Arcade as California's newest city. While cityhood for Arden Arcade was ultimately defeated, Matt remains an active member of his community on behalf of small businesses, and is known for being a strong proponent of improving California's small business climate.

One of the added benefits to having this experience is the ability to implement diverse strategies within the Legislature to increase successes on behalf of clients.
