Atsuo Kuki's Summary

Top research technology leader in Pfizer La Jolla, and global strategic leader in global Pfizer molecular design and chemical technologies (across all Pfizer R&D sites).

Strategic innovator and People leader who harnesses advanced technologies and spearheads successful impact on essential missions in biomedicine. Deployed and managed advanced technology resources to accelerate the Pfizer Oncology Research portfolio.

Over 15 years, launched new cross-disciplinary R&D departments, led the advancement and deployment of diverse biophysical, molecular design, and protein sciences technologies bringing integrated laboratory and computational results to empower dozens of programs. In addition, led drug discovery projects, and built teams of drug hunters.

Thrive in dynamic partnerships with valued colleagues pursuing top-shelf science in action-driven organizations.

Deep thinker. Keen focus on value-creation in the portfolio. Track record of strategic leadership, penetrating insight, and delivery of creative solutions to both technology and team challenges.

In La Jolla, launched and led a department of 50 technology aces advancing crystallography and SBDD, fragment screening and FBDD, protein biophysics, and computational biology and computational chemistry. Experienced research business leader (budgets, IP, talent development) as a department head since 1995.

Atsuo Kuki became a faculty member at age 28 in the Dept of Chemistry at Cornell University. Trained Ph.D. students and postdocs in the departments of Chemistry, Physics, and in the Cornell Theory Center.

Specialties

Penetrating strategist. Advances in guided lead optimization and incisive data to drive design feedback in novel therapeutics. Laboratory and computational dual fluency. Decisive problem solving based on creativity and deep analysis. Protein, peptide and heterocyclic architectures. Molecular design expert (MedChem, SBDD, FBDD). Leader of Protein Sciences & Biophysics labs. Cheminformatics, screening and project team informatics. Strategic and People leader who energizes advanced technology teams

Atsuo Kuki's Education

University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign

Theoretical Chemical Physics & Biophysics

1985 – 1986

NIH Postdoctoral Fellow with Prof. Peter Wolynes on Quantum Path Integral approaches to electronic tunneling in proteins.

Developed and ran the first simulation of a biomolecule on the NSF National Center for Supercomputer Applications supercomputer (NCSA Cray). The quantum behavior of electrons in redox-active proteins was revealed by a Path Integral Monte Carlo simulation to capture the moment of electron tunneling as an ensemble of instantons. This pioneering simulation applied quantum path integrals to protein function, advancing theoretical and computational methods in biophysics.

Stanford University

Ph.D., Physical Chemistry & Biophysical Chemistry

1979 – 1985

Dissertation titled "Electron and Excitation Transfer within Structured Chlorophyllide Dimers", with Professor Steve Boxer

Synthesized chlorophyll dimers and chlorophyll-protein hybrids. Specialty in chlorophyll chemistry and the photophysics of electronically excited states, in picosecond laser time-resolved fluorescence, in molecular design, and in protein biophysics. Thereafter, lured out of the lab and into quantum physics through Bloch equations, density matrices, and the Stochastic Liouville Eqn.

Yale University

B.S., Chemistry

1974 – 1978

Graduated with Honors, cum laude
