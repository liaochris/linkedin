Greg Swan's Summary

Greg's experience with the Internet, social marketing, emerging media and how specific audience groups interact online provides clients valuable strategy, insight and counsel as they consider an integrated marketing campaign, standalone viral campaign or expanding a brand or product launch using new media. A thought leader on the ever-changing marketing landscape and how technology is impacting traditional strategy and execution, Greg uses this expertise, passion and experience to create game-changing marketing programs for some of the largest CPG, B2C, B2B and service industry clients in the world.

An avid blogger, trend spotter, early adopter and active member of industry trade organizations, Greg is always looking for ways to take a more active role in defining and shaping the fields of public relations, advertising and marketing.

In his free time, Greg runs an AllTop-.com-accredited music blog, PerfectPorridge.com, which he founded more than eight years ago and has watched grow into a widely respected music taste-making platform.

Greg is a widely respected presenter at events both large and small, including South by Southwest Interactive (x3), BlogWorld & New Media Expo (x2), MIMA Summit (x2), PR News Next (x1), etc. etc.

Greg is a frequent guest lecturer at professional organization events (e.g., AMA, IABC, PRSA, IMA), corporate meetings and area colleges, presenting real-life applications of marketing and public relations, social marketing 101, 201 and thought leadership topics, such as "Influencer Engagement" and "The Brand of You in the Digital Age."

Accolades include: PR Week's 40 Under 40; MSP Business Journal's 40 Under 40; PR News Social Media Professional of the Year Honorable Mention (x2); Top 20 Minnesota Social Media Innovators; and "Daddy of the Year" by his three kids.

Greg Swan's Honors and Awards

  • PR Week 40 Under 40

    PR Week
    • August 2011

    Greg Swan started working at 17 and grasped the value of social media early. At Weber Shandwick, he leads a team of 16 people managing the social media strategy for the US Army recruiting account, among others. Swan helped the Army launch its first soldier blog, Army Strong Stories, which now boasts more than 600 soldiers and 2,000 posts.

    Based in Minneapolis, Swan's digital savvy has helped land new accounts such as Verizon and Polaroid. He has planned and executed media strategies for clients such as Chevrolet and DuPont.

    Outside the office, Swan started a statewide art newspaper, Art Scene, and a music blog, Perfect Porridge, with a national following.

  • Minnesota-St. Paul Business Journal 40 Under Forty

    Minnesota-St. Paul Business Journal
    • March 2011

    The 40 Under Forty awards recognize local business leaders under the age of 40 for their exceptional professional achievements and community contributions.

    He popularized the meme “snowmaggedon,” but Greg Swan’s influence goes well beyond meteorology catchphrases. As vice president and director of digital strategy for New York City-based Weber Shandwick, he manages high-risk media campaigns for clients including the U.S. Army. Swan designed the army’s first social media venture with a blog called Army Strong Stories, designed to offer a place for potential recruits to gain insight into life in the army.
    Swan is also the founder of the music blog PerfectPorridge.com, which covers local, national and international bands, and last year presented on the state of music blogs at the South by Southwest Interactive conference in Austin. Minnesota Public Radio frequently taps him for its weekly Art Hounds segment, and Swan is recognized as an advocate for local bands.

    Swan has the ability to incorporate almost anything into social media. He live tweeted the birth of his children, set up a live stream for his endangered office coffeemaker, and represents Punxsutawney Phil on Twitter each February.
