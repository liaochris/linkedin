Eric Brodeur's Summary

This is where I tell you about my keen ability to tell a story, shape performances and pacing, and how developing a character is more important than eye-popping visual effects. Etcetera.

We all know what an editor does and I'm not going to bore you with a long, flowery description in an attempt to one-up my peers.

Why did I change careers to become an editor? I love movies. It's the anticipation and thrill of what you're going to experience during the next two hours from the moment the lights dim to the closing credit roll. Take me to another town, time, or universe for an adventure the likes of which I've never seen. The best films have me thinking about them next week, next month, and twenty years later.

It's my goal to inspire the audience's imagination through my films...just like other films inspired me over the years.

* * *

I'm a member of the Motion Picture Editors Guild, IATSE Local 700.

My editing experience encompasses Feature Films and Commercials using Avid Media Composer and Final Cut Pro and a variety of camera formats including: ARRI ALEXA, RED EPIC, RED ONE, Canon, P2, XDCAM, and others.

There are many tools I use to get the job done: FileMaker Pro, Adobe Photoshop, Compressor, Cinema Tools, MPEG Streamclip. 5DtoRGB, DaVinci Resolve...and more.
