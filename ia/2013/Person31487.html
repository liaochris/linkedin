David Drake's Summary

*Twenty years training and consulting in support of systemic change, coaching and communication projects.
*Doctorate in Human and Organizational Systems; research on narratives, identity and transformation; involved in international coaching research community; author of 30 publications on coaching as an evidence-based practice, the use of stories in coaching, and the psychodynamics of the coaching process.
*Global thought leader on stories and coaching; developed narrative coaching as an integral approach to development and change; teach it internationally.
*Consult to and teach in advanced coaching and graduate programs on the use of narrative practices and the art of an interdisciplinary approach.

Founder and Executive Director

Center for Narrative Coaching

January 2006 – Present (8 years)

Dr. David Drake works internationally with clients on using coaching and narrative to drive culture change.

He uses his deep expertise in both human and organization development to help clients such as PricewaterhouseCoopers, BHP Billiton and Nike to (1) take a systemic, narrative approach to change and leadership and (2) develop the internal coaching capabilities to support their success.

The Center also trains advanced practitioners around the world and it will soon launch a laboratory on innovative uses for narrative-based practices.

A practical visionary, David:
• speaks internationally on the evolution of coaching and organizations from a narrative perspective
• serves on the editorial boards for three coaching journals
• has written over 30 publications on narratives and coaching
• edited "The Philosophy and Practice of Coaching: Insights and Issues for a New Era” by Jossey-Bass, 2008 (www.practiceofcoaching.com).

Retained Consultant on Coaching and Change

PricewaterhouseCoopers

Partnership; 10,001+ employees; Accounting industry

2007 – 2011 (4 years)

• Developed and delivered a customised program to train hundreds of internal coaching champions who are linked across a network to support culture change within their teams. Co-created an internal wiki and have written dozens of practical tools and resources for use by anyone within the organization who wants to improve their development and performance conversations.
• With the Human Capital Executive and the Chief Learning Officer, developed a firm-wide strategy to embed coaching approaches in every development and milestone program and team processes.
• Working with the firm strategy leaders to use the coaching work to change the way in which leaders and others engage with their clients.
• Seeing successes across many parts of the firm in terms of staff engagement, team effectiveness and client satisfaction.

President

Catalyst Communications (Portland, OR)

October 1993 – December 2006 (13 years 3 months)

My management consulting firm focused on organizational change, leadership development and coaching skills. I worked with over 60 clients in that period, largely in health care, education and high tech. I was an adjunct faculty in several Masters programs and taught coaching skills to nearly 4,000 professionals in organizations. I closed the firm in order to focus my work more explicitly on coaching and coaching culture development as well as my narrative work.
