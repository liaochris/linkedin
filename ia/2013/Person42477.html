At an exciting transition in Eric's career, he was fortunate to work for a "real" design build contractor. The designers were at the other end of the office, and the direct interaction provided speedy and efficient project expediting. He was responsible for a 200,000 square foot manufacturing facility that was designed "in house." The facility, a pre-engineered building, went from breaking ground to having a roof overhead in 67 days. The project schedule was driven by the expiration of the Owner's existing lease.

Eric's ability to work closely with the designers made it both fun and challenging. His team put the entire $4M facility in place and had the owner's business operational in 90 days. The design build delivery method and this experience provided Eric with the opportunity to learn, and to find new ways to complete a difficult project schedule.

In the course of this project Eric was also responsible for the company's implementation of a standard subcontractor agreement for use throughout the company.

The Lathrop Company, a division of Turner Construction, provided Eric with the opportunity to transition from engineering design into the world of commercial construction.

During his time with The Lathrop Company, Eric gained a great understanding of the project control systems and tools that are utilized to keep a construction project on schedule and running smoothly. In an effort to track and manage job progress, Eric brought his own computer to work - before the industry was utilizing such tools.

All of Eric's construction projects were negotiated design build projects that offered him the chance to see the project evolve from the design phase, to budgeting, contracting, and ultimately to project completion.

Eric's fundamental skills and knowledge in construction came from experiencing all types and phases of the construction management process: cast in place concrete, slab on deck concrete pours, structural steel erection, interior finishes, manufacturing, and commercial office building construction.
