Underwater Photographer

National Geographic Society

Nonprofit; 1001-5000 employees; Publishing industry

June 2012 – June 2012 (1 month) San Salvador, Bahamas

I was selected as an underwater photographer to visually document the health status of a reef system in San Salvador, Bahamas and also conduct crinoid population counts for a UC Geology research trip in conjunction with The National Geographic Society.

Dr. David Meyer led the trip and is currently working on establishing crinoids as an accepted indicator of the overall health status for reef environments. Crinoids, commonly known as feather stars, are filter filters that thrive in conditions that are also considered to be factors of a healthy reef. Dr. Meyer has conducted population surveys of crinoids at specific dive sites around Jamaica, Barbados, and the Bahamas over the past thirty years. He has a research grant from the National Geographic Society to revisit those sites to measure the population fluctuation of crinoids and how that corresponds with the change in the overall health of the reef.
