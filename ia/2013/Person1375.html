Adam Eisendrath's Publications

  • A Study of the Changing Membership Acquisition Strategies in Bay Area Reform Synagogues: A Focused Analysis of Interfaith, Lesbian, Gay, Bisexual, and Transgender Recruitment

    • University of San Francisco
    • September 2007
    Authors: Adam Eisendrath

    This study conducted an interview survey of six Reform synagogues in the Bay Area region of California. The goal of the research was to explore the relationship between membership recruitment strategies and the growing population of the interfaith, lesbian, gay, bisexual and transgender communities in the region. Further, it asked how their strategies have changed over a ten-year period in response to the growth of these constituent groups.

    The study found data that suggest a close connection between Reform synagogue growth and the development of membership outreach strategies focused on young families, particularly those in interfaith relationships. Furthermore, the data suggest that Reform synagogues that have or have had at least one clergy member associated with the LGBT community will be more attractive to the young liberal Jewish community. While most synagogues don’t specifically target the LGBT community, it has become a regional standard to incorporate such individuals as members of both congregation and clergy. Synagogue size and geographic region did not affect this outcome, however, web presence and targeted marketing strategies appeared to have a significant influence on membership profiles.

The Master of Nonprofit Administration (MNA) degree program prepares experienced adults for management and leadership roles in the nonprofit sector. The program trains its graduates to be reflective practitioners, capable of independent professional judgment that integrates theory, practical experience, and commitment to social values. The MNA program is designed for students already working in nonprofit corporations, foundations, voluntary associations, community organizations, or nongovernmental organizations.

The curriculum addresses issues and problems of nonprofit management, and helps students master relevant concepts, skills, and analytic tools. Courses investigate the political, economic, legal, and social environments of nonprofit organizations.
